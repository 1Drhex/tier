Guest Question.

Gravity Retaining Wall Analysis

Given: The concrete gravity wall and soil backfill shown in Fig. E8.1a.

Determine: The factor of safety against sliding; the factor of safety against overturning; the line of action of the resultant. Use the Rankine theory for soil pressure computations. Neglect the passive pressure acting on the toe.

Admin
Solution

For \(\varphi =30k_{a}=\frac{1-\sin \varphi }{1+\sin \varphi }=\frac{1}{3}\)

 

Then \(P_{a}=\frac{1}{2}(0.12)(12)^{2}\left ( \frac{1}{3} \right )(1\, ft)=2.88\) kip/ft of wall

 

Next, we compute the weight of the concrete wall segments per foot of wall. Noting Fig. E8.1b,

 

\(W_{1}=(0.150)(10)(1)(1\, ft)=1.5\) kip

 

\(W_{2}=(0.150)\left ( \frac{10}{2} \right )(3)(1\, ft)=2.25\) kip

 

\(W_{3}=(0.150)(4)(2)(1\, ft)=1.2\) kip

 

Applying vertical force equilibrium yields

 

\(N=W_{1}+W_{2}+W_{3}=1.5+2.25+1.2=4.95\) kip

 

The factor of safety with respect to sliding is defined as the ratio of the maximum available friction force \(F_{max}\) to the actual horizontal force.

 

\(F_{max}=\mu N=N\tan \phi =0.577(4.95)=2.86\) kip

 

\(F.S._{sliding}=\frac{\mu N}{P_{a}}=\frac{2.86}{2.88}=0.99\)

 

The line of action of N is determined by summing moments about the toe. The factor of safety with respect to overturning is defined as the ratio of the resisting moment to the overturning moment, both quantities with respect to the toe.

 

\(M_{B_{overturning}}=P_{a}\left ( \frac{H}{3} \right )=2.88(4)=11.52\) kip ft

 

\(M_{B_{resisting}}=W_{1}(3.5)+W_{2}(2)+W_{3}(2)\)

 

\(=1.5(3.5)+2.25(2)+1.2(2)=12.15\) kip ft

 

\(F.S._{overturning}=\frac{M_{B_{resisting}}}{M_{B_{overturning}}}=\frac{12.15}{11.52}=1.05\)

 

\(M_{net}=M_{B_{overturning}}-M_{B_{resisting}}=0.63\) kip ft clockwise

 

\(\bar{x}=\frac{M_{net}}{N}=\frac{0.63}{4.95}=0.13\) ft

 

In order to increase the factors of safety, the geometry needs to be modified.

 

The following procedure is useful for estimating appropriate values for \(b_{1}\) and \(b_{2}\). Given the wall height, one can derive expressions for the factors of safety. The details are listed below.

 

\(W=\frac{(b_{1}+b_{2})}{2}H_{y_{c}}=N\)

 

\(P_{a}=k_{a}\left \{ \frac{1}{2}y_{s}H^{2} \right \}\)

 

\(F.S._{sliding}=\frac{\mu N}{P_{s}}\)

 

\(\Downarrow\)

 

\(F.S._{sliding}=\left ( \frac{\mu y_{c}}{k_{a}y_{s}} \right )\left ( \frac{b_{2}}{H} \right )\left ( 1+\frac{b_{1}}{b_{2}} \right )\)

 

\(M_{overturning}=\frac{H}{3}P_{a}=\frac{1}{6}k_{a}y_{s}H^{3}\)

 

\(M_{resisting}=\frac{2}{3}(b_{2}-b_{1})W_{2}+\left ( b_{2}-\frac{b_{1}}{2} \right )W_{1}\)

 

\(=\frac{Hy_{c}b_{2}^{2}}{3}\left \{ 1-\frac{1}{2}\left ( \frac{b_{1}}{b_{2}} \right )^{2}+\frac{b_{1}}{b_{2}} \right \}\)

 

\(F.S._{overturning}=\frac{M_{B_{resisting}}}{M_{B_{overturning}}}=\frac{\frac{Hy_{c}b_{2}^{2}}{3}\left \{ 1-\frac{1}{2}\left ( \frac{b_{1}}{b_{2}} \right )^{2}+\frac{b_{1}}{b_{2}} \right \}}{\frac{1}{6}k_{a}y_{s}H^{3}}\)

 

\(\Downarrow\)

 

\(F.S._{overturning}=\frac{2y_{c}}{k_{a}y_{s}}\left ( \frac{b_{2}}{H} \right )^{2}\left \{ 1-\frac{1}{2}\left ( \frac{b_{1}}{b_{2}} \right )^{2}+\frac{b_{1}}{b_{2}} \right \}\)

 

One specifies the factor of safety with respect to overturning, and the ratio \(b_{1}/b_{2}\), and then computes the value for \(b_{2}/H\). With \(b_{2}/H\) known, one checks for sliding and if necessary modifies the value of \(b_{2}/H\).
