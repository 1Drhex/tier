Guest Question.

Consider the common-collector (CC) amplifier circuit shown in Fig. 9.10a. Assume that the transistor has a dc current gain hFE = 100 and an ac current gain hfe = 150.

(a) Determine VCEQ and r′e.

(b) Compute the overall voltage gain, input resistance, overall current gain and output resistance of the amplifier.

(c) Determine the minimum value of the capacitor Ce for a signal frequency of fs min = 1000 Hz .

Admin
Solution

(a) The dc equivalent circuit can be drawn in the same manner as that of Fig. 9.9c by simply replacing Rc = 2.2 K, Rc = 0, Re1 = 0 and Re2 by Re = 2.2 K which is redrawn in Fig. 9.10b where VBB = 2.79 V and Rb = 7.67 K.

The collector current can be obtained from Eq. (9.50), \(I_{CQ}=h_{FE}I_{BEQ}=\frac{h_{FE}(V_{BB}-V_{BEQ})}{R_{b}+h_{FE}(R_{e1}+R_{e2})} \) , as

\(I_{CQ}=\frac{100(2.79V-0.7V)}{7.67K+100(0+2.2K)} =0.92 \) mA

From Eq. (9.51), VCEQ = VCC – ICQ (Rc + Re1 + Re2 ) , the quiescent collector-to-emitter voltage is

VCEQ = 12 V – 0.92 mA × 2.2 K = 9.98 V

The value of r′e is obtained from Eq. (9.52) , \(r^{′}_{e}=\frac{26 mV}{I_{EQ}} \approx \frac{26 mV}{I_{CQ}} \) , as

\(r^{′}_{e}=\frac{26mV}{0.92 mA} =28.26\Omega \)

(b) Note that the generalised amplifier circuit of 9.7a reduces to the CC amplifier of Fig. 9.10a under Re1 = 0, Rc = 0, Re2 = Re and RL1 = ∞. Thus, the overall voltage gain of the amplifier can be obtained from Eq. (9.25) , \(A_{V2s} =\left[\frac{h_{fe}}{R_{th}+h_{fe}(r^{′}_{e}+R_{l2e})}\frac{R_{e2}R_{L2}}{R_{c2}+R_{L2} }\frac{(R_{1}||R_{2})}{R_{s}+(R_{1}||R_{2})}\right] \) as

\(A_{V2s} =\left[\frac{h_{fe}R_{l2e}}{R_{th}+h_{fe}(r^{′}_{e}+R_{l2e})}\frac{(R_{1}||R_{2})}{R_{s}+(R_{1}||R_{2})}\right] \)

\(=\frac{150\times 2.1K}{556\Omega +150(28.26\Omega +2.1K)}\times \frac{7.67K}{600\Omega +7.67K}=0.91 \)

where we have used

\(R_{l2e}=\frac{R_{e}R_{L}}{R_{e}+R_{L}}=\frac{2.2K\times 47K}{2.2K+47K}=2.1 \) K and

\(R_{th}=R_{s} ||R_{1}||R_{2}=\frac{1}{1/600\Omega +1/33K+1/10K}=556\Omega \)

Note that gain is always less than unity in the case of CC amplifier. The input resistance at the base is determined from Eq. (9.29) , \(R_{in}(base)=h_{fe} \left\lgroup r^{′}_{e}+ \frac{R_{e2}R_{L2}}{R_{e2}+R_{L2}} \right\rgroup \) , as

Rin (base) = 150 × 28.26 Ω ≈ 4.24 K

Now, the total input resistance is obtained from Eq. (9.30) , \(R_{in}(total)= R_{1}||R_{2}||R_{in}(base)=\frac{R_{1}R_{2}R_{in}(base)}{R_{2}R_{in}(base)+R_{1}R_{in}(base)+R_{1}R_{2}} \) , as

\( R_{in}(total)=\left\lgroup\frac{1}{33K}+\frac{1}{10K}+\frac{1}{4.24K} \right\rgroup ^{-1}=2.73 \) K

The overall current gain can be obtained from Eq. (9.33) , \(A_{Isc}\approx A_{Ise}=\frac{h_{fe}(R_{s}+R_{in}(total))}{r_{th}+h_{fe}(r^{′}_{e}+R_{l2e})}\frac{(R_{1}||R_{2})}{R_{s}+(R_{1}||R_{2})} \) , as

\(A_{Is}=\frac{150\times (600\Omega +2.73K)}{556\Omega +150\times (28.26\Omega +2.1K) }\times \frac{7.67K}{600\Omega+7.67 K}=1.45 \)

The output resistance is obtained from Eq. (9.36) , \(R_{out 2}=R_{e2}|| \left\lgroup \frac{R_{1}||R_{2}||R_{s}}{h_{fe}}+r^{′}_{e} \right\rgroup \) , as

\(R_{out}=R_{out2}=\frac{2.2K\times(556\Omega +150\times 28.26\Omega ) }{556\Omega +150\times (28.26\Omega +2.2K)}=31.5\Omega \)

It may be observed that the output resistance is very low in CC amplifier which makes the amplifier useful for driving low resistance loads.

(c) The minimum value of the coupling capacitor Ce min of Ce can be determined by substituting fe min = 1000 Hz,

RL2 = RL = 47 K and Rout2 = Rout = 31.5 Ω in Eq. (9.47) , \(C_{e2}\geq \frac{1.59}{f_{smin}(R_{out2}+R_{L2})}=C_{e2min} \) , Thus we obtain,

\(C_{e min}=C_{e2 min}=\frac{1.59}{1000 Hz\times (31.5\Omega +47K)}=33.8\) nF
