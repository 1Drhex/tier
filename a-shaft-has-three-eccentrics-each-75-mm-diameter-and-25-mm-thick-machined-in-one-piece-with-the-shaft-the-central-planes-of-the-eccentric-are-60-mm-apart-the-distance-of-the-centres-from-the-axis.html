Guest Question.

A shaft has three eccentrics, each 75 mm diameter and 25 mm thick, machined in one piece with the shaft. The central planes of the eccentric are 60 mm apart. The distance of the centres from the axis of rotation are 12 mm, 18 mm and 12 mm and their angular positions are \(120^{\circ}\) apart. The density of metal is \(7000 kg / m ^{3} \text { . }\). Find the amount of out-of-balance force and couple at 600 r.p.m. If the shaft is balanced by adding two masses at a radius 75 mm and at distances of 100 mm from the central plane of the middle eccentric, find the amount of the masses and their angular positions.

Admin
Solution

Given : D = 75 mm = 0.075 m ; t = 25 mm = 0.025 m ; \(r_{ A }=12 mm =0.012 m\) ; \(r_{ B }=18 mm =0.018 m ; r_{ C }=12 mm =0.012 mm ; \rho=7000 kg / m ^{3} ; N=600 r.p.m . \text { or }\) \(\omega=2 \pi \times 600 / 60=62.84 rad / s ; r_{ L }=r_{ M }=75 mm =0.075 m\)

We know that mass of each eccentric,

\(m_{ A }=m_{ B }=m_{ C }=\text { Volume } \times \text { Density }=\frac{\pi}{4} \times D^{2} \times t \times \rho\)

 

\(=\frac{\pi}{4}(0.075)^{2}(0.025) 7000=0.77 kg\)

Let L and M be the planes at distances of 100 mm from the central plane of middle eccentric. The position of the planes and the angular position of the three eccentrics is shown in Fig. 21.12 (a) and (b) respectively. Assuming L as the reference plane and mass of the eccentric A in the vertical direction, the data may be tabulated as below :

Out-of-balance force

The out-of-balance force is obtained by drawing the force polygon, as shown in Fig. 21.12 (c), from the data given in Table 21.6 (column 4). The resultant oc represents the out-of-balance force.

Since the centrifugal force is proportional to the product of mass and radius (i.e. m.r), therefore by measurement.

\(\text { Out-of-balance force }=\text { vector } o c=4.75 \times 10^{-3} kg – m\)

 

\(=4.75 \times 10^{-3} \times \omega^{2}=4.75 \times 10^{-3}(62.84)^{2}=18.76 N\)

 

Out-of-balance couple

The out-of-balance couple is obtained by drawing the couple polygon from the data given in Table 21.6 (column 6), as shown in Fig. 21.12 (d). The resultant o′ c′ represents the out-ofbalance couple. Since the couple is proportional to the product of force and distance (m.r.l), therefore by measurement,

\(\text { Out-of-balance couple }=\text { vector } o^{\prime} c^{\prime}=1.1 \times 10^{-3} kg – m ^{2}\)

 

\(=1.1 \times 10^{-3} \times \omega^{2}=1.1 \times 10^{-3}(62.84)^{2}=4.34 N – m\)

 

Amount of balancing masses and their angular positions

The vector c′ o′ (in the direction from c′ to o′ ), as shown in Fig. 21.12 (d) represents the balancing couple and is proportional to \(15 \times 10^{-3} m_{ M }, \text { i.e. }\)

\(15 \times 10^{-3} m_{ M }=\text { vector } c^{\prime} o^{\prime}=1.1 \times 10^{-3} kg – m ^{2}\)

 

or \(m_{ M }=0.073 kg\)

Draw OM in Fig. 21.12 (b) parallel to vector c′ o′ . By measurement, we find that the angular position of balancing mass \(\left(m_{ M }\right) \text { is } 5^{\circ}\) from mass A in the clockwise direction.

In order to find the balancing mass \(\left(m_{ L }\right)\), a force polygon as shown in Fig. 21.12 (e) is drawn. The closing side of the polygon i.e. vector do (in the direction from d to o) represents the balancing force and is proportional to \(75 \times 10^{-3} m_{ L }\). By measurement, we find that

\(75 \times 10^{-3} m_{L}=\text { vector } d o=5.2 \times 10^{-3} kg – m\)

 

or \(m_{ L }=0.0693 kg\)

Draw OL in Fig. 21.12 (b), parallel to vector do. By measurement, we find that the angular position of mass \(\left(m_{ L }\right) \text { is } 124^{\circ}\) from mass A in the clockwise direction
