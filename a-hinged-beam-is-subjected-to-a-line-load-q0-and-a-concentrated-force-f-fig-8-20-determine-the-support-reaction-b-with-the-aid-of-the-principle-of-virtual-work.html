Guest Question.

A hinged beam is subjected to a line load \(q_{0}\) and a concentrated force F (Fig. 8.20).

Determine the support reaction B with the aid of the principle of virtual work.

Admin
Solution

We remove the support B and replace it by the support reaction. Then the point of application of the support reaction is able to move in the vertical direction and the support reaction has to be treated as an external load. The line load is replaced by the resultant forces \(R_{1} = q_{0} a\) and \(R_{2} = q_{0} a\) , respectively,

to the left and to the right of the hinge G. We now impose a virtual displacement \(\delta w_{G}\) on the system. Since only the component \(F \sin \alpha\) of the force F in the direction of the displacement has to be taken into account, the principle of virtual work reads

\(\delta U =0 :R_{1} \delta w_{1} + R_{2} \delta w_{2} -B \delta w_{B}+F \sin \alpha \delta w_{F} =0\) .

The following geometrical relations between the various virtual displacements can be taken from the figure:

\(\delta w_{G} =a \delta \varphi =3a \delta\psi \rightarrow \delta \varphi =3 \delta\psi\) ,

\(\delta w_{1} =\frac{1}{2} a \delta \varphi , \delta w_{2}=\frac{5}{2} a \delta\psi , \delta w_{B}=2a \delta\psi , \delta w_{F}=a \delta\psi\) .

Introduction into the principle of virtual work yields

\(\left(\frac{3}{2} q_{0} a + \frac{5}{2} q_{0} a – 2B +F \sin \alpha \right) a\delta\psi =0 \rightarrow \underline{\underline{B =2q_{0} a +\frac{1}{2} F \sin \alpha } }\) .
