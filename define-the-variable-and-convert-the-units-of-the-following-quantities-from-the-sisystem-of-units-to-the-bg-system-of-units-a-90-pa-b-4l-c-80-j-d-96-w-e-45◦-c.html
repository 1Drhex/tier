Guest Question.

Define the variable and convert the units of the following quantities from the SIsystem of units to the BG system of units: (a) 90 Pa. (b) 4L. (c) 80 J. (d) 96 W. (e) \(45^{\circ } C\).

Admin
Solution

The conversions from the SI system of units to the BG system of units for some common variables are given at the front of this textbook (and some are also available in Mathcad).
(a) The pressure is converted from SI to BG as follows:
\(1Pa=1\frac{N}{m^{2} } \) \(1m = 3.281 ft\) \(N:=0.225 lb\)
\(90Pa1\frac{N}{m^{2} }\frac{1}{Pa}\frac{0.225lb}{1N}\frac{\left(1m\right) ^{2} }{\left(3.281 ft\right) ^{2} } =1.881 \frac{lb}{ft^{2} } \)
(b) The volume is converted from SI to BG as follows:
\(1L=1\times 10^{-3} m^{3} \) \(1m=3.28ft\) \(4L1.10^{-3} \frac{\left(1m\right) ^{3} }{1L} \frac{\left(x3.281ft\right) ^{3} }{\left(x1m\right) ^{3} } =0.141 ft^{3} \)
(c) The energy (or work or quantity of heat) is convened from SI to BG as follows:
\(J: 1N m\) \(N: =0.225 lb\) \(1m=3.281 ft\)
\(80J\frac{1Nm}{1J}\frac{0.225lb}{1N} \frac{3.281ft}{1m}=59.058 ftlb \)
(d) The power is converted from SI to BG as follows:
\(W:=1\frac{Nm}{sec} \) \(N:=0.225 lb \) \(1m=3.281ft \)
\(96W1\frac{Nm}{sec} \frac{1}{1W}\frac{0.225lb}{1N} \frac{3.281ft}{1m}=70.87\frac{ft lb}{sec} \)
The temperature is converted from SI to BG as follows. The conversion formula listed at the front of the textbook is as follows: \(^{\circ } F=32+\frac{9}{5}\left(^{\circ }C \right) \) The direct computation using Mathcad is as follows:
\(45^{\circ } C=113^{\circ } F\)
