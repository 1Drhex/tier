Guest Question.

A 27.50 lb soil sample has a volume of \(0.220 ft^{3}\) a moisture content of 10.2%, and a specific gravity of solids of 2.65. Compute the unit weight, dry unit weight, degree of saturation, void ratio, and porosity.

Admin
Solution

Solution using fundamental and derived equations

\(\gamma =\frac{W}{V} =\frac{27.50 lb}{0.220 ft^{3}} =125.0 lb/ft^{3} \rightarrow 125 lb/ft^{3}\)

 

\(\gamma _{d}=\frac{\gamma }{1+w} =\frac{125.0 lb/ft^{3}}{1+0.102} =113.4 lb/ft^{3} \rightarrow 113 lb/ft^{3}\)

 

\(S=\frac{w}{\frac{\gamma _{w}}{\gamma _{d}} -\frac{1}{G_{s}} } \times 100\)% \(=\frac{0.102}{\frac{62.4 lb/ft^{3}}{113.4 lb/ft^{3}} -\frac{1}{2.65} } \times 100\)% \(=59\)%

 

\(e=\frac{G_{s}\gamma _{w}}{\gamma _{d}} -1=\frac{\left(2.65\right) \left(62.4 lb/ft^{3}\right) }{113.4 lb/ft^{3}} -1=0.4582 \rightarrow 0.458\)

 

\(n=\frac{e}{1+e} \times 100\)% \( =\frac{0.4582}{1+0.4582} \times 100\)% \(=31.4\)%

Solution using a phase diagram

Although the fundamental and derived equations were sufficient to solve this problem, and would be the easiest method, we also will illustrate a solution using a phase diagram.

Step 1: Draw and annotate a phase diagram (see Figure 4.4).

Step 2: Assume dimension.

Not applicable to this problem, because weights and volumes have been given.

Step 3: Determine dimensions in phase diagram.

\(W_{s}=\frac{W}{1+w} =\frac{27.50 lb}{1+0.102} =24.95 lb\)

 

\(W_{w}=W – W_{s}=27.50 lb-24.95 lb=2.55 lb\)

 

\(G_{s}=\frac{W_{s}}{V_{s}\gamma _{w}} \)

 

\(2.65=\frac{24.95 lb}{V_{s}\left(62.4 lb/ft^{3}\right) } \rightarrow V_{s}=0.1509 ft^{3}\)

 

\(\gamma _{w}=\frac{W_{w}}{V_{w}}\)

 

\(62.4 lb/ft^{3}=\frac{2.55 lb}{V_{w}} \rightarrow V_{w}=0.0409 ft^{3}\)

 

\(V_{a}=V – V_{w} – V_{s}=0.220 ft^{3} – 0.0409ft^{3} – 0.1509 ft^{3}=0.0282 ft^{3}\)

Step 4: Compute parameters.

\(\gamma =\frac{W}{V} =\frac{27.50 lb}{0.220 ft^{3}} =125 lb/ft^{3}\)

 

\(n=\frac{V_{v}}{V} \times 100\)% \(=\frac{0.0282 ft^{3}+0.0409 ft^{3}}{0.220 ft^{3}} \times 100\)% \(=31.4\)%

 

\(e=\frac{V_{v}}{V_{s}} =\frac{0.0409 ft^{3}+0.0282 ft^{3}}{0.1509 ft^{3}} =0.458\)

 

\(\gamma _{d}=\frac{W_{s}}{V} =\frac{24.95 lb}{0.220 ft^{3}} =113 lb/ft^{3}\)

 

\(S=\frac{V_{w}}{V_{v}} \times 100\)% \(=\frac{0.0409 ft^{3}}{0.0409 ft^{3}+0.0282 ft^{3}} \times 100\)% \(=59\)%
