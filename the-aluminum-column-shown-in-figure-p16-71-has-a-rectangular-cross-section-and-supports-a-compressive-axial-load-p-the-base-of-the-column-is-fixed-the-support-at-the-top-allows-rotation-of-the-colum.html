Guest Question.

The aluminum column shown in Figure P16.71 has a rectangular cross section and supports a compressive axial load P. The base of the column is fixed. The support at the top allows rotation of the column in the x-y plane (i.e., bending about the strong axis) but prevents rotation in the x-z plane (i.e., bending about the weak axis). Determine the allowable axial load \(P _{\text {allow }} \) that may be applied to the column for the following parameters: L = 60 in., b = 1.25 in., and h = 2.00 in. Use the Aluminum Association column design formulas.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&A=(1.25 in .)(2.00 in .)=2.50 in .^{2} \\&I_{z}=\frac{(1.25 in .)(2.00 in .)^{3}}{12}=0.833333 in. ^{4} \quad r_{z}=\sqrt{\frac{0.833333 in .^{4}}{2.50 in. ^{2}}}=0.577350 in .\\&I_{y}=\frac{(2.00 in. )(1.25 in .)^{3}}{12}=0.325521 in .^{4} \quad r_{y}=\sqrt{\frac{0.325521 in .^{4}}{2.50 in .^{2}}}=0.360844 in .\end{aligned}\)

Effective-slenderness ratio:

\(\frac{K_{z} L}{r_{z}}=\frac{(0.7)(60 in .)}{0.577350 in .}=72.746 \quad \frac{K_{y} L}{r_{y}}=\frac{(0.5)(60 in .)}{0.360844 in. }=83.138\)

Aluminum Association column design formula:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{\text {allow }} &=\frac{51,000}{(K L / r)^{2}} \text { ksi } \quad \text { where } \frac{K L}{r}>66 \\&=\frac{51,000}{(83.138)^{2}} ksi \\&=7.378 ksi\end{aligned}\)

Allowable axial load \(P _{\text {allow }}:\)

\(P_{\text {allow }}=\sigma_{\text {allow }} A=(7.378 ksi )\left(2.50 in .^{2}\right)=18.45 kips\)
