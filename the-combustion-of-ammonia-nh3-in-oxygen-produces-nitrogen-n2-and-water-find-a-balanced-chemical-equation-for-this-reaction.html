Guest Question.

The combustion of ammonia \(\left(\mathrm{NH}_{3}\right)\) in oxygen produces nitrogen \(\left(\mathrm{N}_{2}\right)\) and water. Find a balanced chemical equation for this reaction.

Admin
Solution

If we denote the numbers of molecules of ammonia, oxygen, nitrogen, and water by \(w, x, y\), and \(z\), respectively, then we are seeking an equation of the form

\(w \mathrm{NH}_{3}+x \mathrm{O}_{2} \longrightarrow y \mathrm{~N}_{2}+z \mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}\)

Comparing the numbers of nitrogen, hydrogen, and oxygen atoms in the reactants and products, we obtain three linear equations:

Nitrogen: \(w=2 y\)

Hydrogen: \(3 w=2 z\)

Oxygen: \(\quad 2 x=z\)

Rewriting these equations in standard form gives us a homogeneous system of three linear equations in four variables. [Notice that Theorem 2.3 guarantees that such a

system will have (infinitely many) nontrivial solutions.] We reduce the corresponding augmented matrix by Gauss-Jordan elimination.

\(\begin{aligned}& w-2 y &=0 \\3 w &-2 z &=0 \\2 x &-z &=0\end{aligned} \longrightarrow\left[\begin{array}{rrrr|r}1 & 0 & -2 & 0 & 0 \\3 & 0 & 0 & -2 & 0 \\0 & 2 & 0 & -1 & 0\end{array}\right] \longrightarrow\left[\begin{array}{rrrr|r}1 & 0 & 0 & -\frac{2}{3} & 0 \\0 & 1 & 0 & -\frac{1}{2} & 0 \\0 & 0 & 1 & -\frac{1}{3} & 0\end{array}\right]\)

Thus, \(w=\frac{2}{3} z, x=\frac{1}{2} z\), and \(y=\frac{1}{3} z\). The smallest positive value of \(z\) that will produce integer values for all four variables is the least common denominator of the fractions \(\frac{2}{3}, \frac{1}{2}\), and \(\frac{1}{3}\)-namely, 6 – which gives \(w=4, x=3, y=2\), and \(z=6\). Therefore, the balanced chemical equation is

\(4 \mathrm{NH}_{3}+3 \mathrm{O}_{2} \longrightarrow 2 \mathrm{~N}_{2}+6 \mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}\)

Theorem 2.3 If \([A \mid 0]\) is a homogeneous system of \(m\) linear equations with \(n\) variables, where \(m<n\), then the system has infinitely many solutions.
