Guest Question.

A stream of water has velocity components of u = -2 m/s, v = 3 m/s for \(0 \leq t < 10 s \); and u = 5 m/s, v = -2 m/s for \( 10 s < t \leq 15 s. \) Plot the pathline and streamline for a particle released at point (0, 0) when t = 0 s.

Admin
Solution

Using the definition of velocity, for \(0 \leq t < 10 \)s

 

\(\frac{dx}{dt} = u; \frac{dx}{dt} = -2\)

 

\(\int_{0}^{x} dx = -2\int_{0}^{t} dt\)

x = (-2t) m (1)

When t = 10 s, x = -2(10) = -20 m

\(\frac{dy}{dt} = v; \frac{dy}{dt} = 3 \)

 

\(\int_{0}^{y} dy = 3\int_{0}^{t} dt \)

y = (3t) m (2)

When t = 10 s, y = 3(10) = 30 m

The equation of the streamline can be determined by eliminating t from Eq. (1) and (2).

\(y= -\frac{3}{2}x \)

For \(10 < t \leq 15 s.\)

 

\(\frac{dx}{dt} = u; \frac{dx}{dt}=5 \)

 

\(\int_{- 20 m}^{x} dx = 5\int_{10 s}^{t} dt\)

 

\(x – (-20) = 5(t – 10) \)

x = (5t – 70) m (3)

At t = 15 s, x = 5(15) – 70 = 5 m

\(\frac{dy}{dt}= v; \frac{dy}{dt} = -2 \)

 

\(\int_{30 m}^{y }dy = -2\int_{10 s}^{t}dt \)

y – 30 = -2(t – 10)

y = (- 2 t + 50) m (4)

When t = 15 s, y = -2(15) + 50 = 20 m

Eliminate t from Eqs. (3) and (4),

\(y = (- \frac{2}{5}x + 22) \)

The two streamlines intersect at (-20, 30), point B in Fig. (a). The pathline is the path ABC.

For 0≤t<10s , y=-\frac{3}{2}x

For 10s<t≤15s , y=-\frac{2}{5}x+22
