Guest Question.

(a) Consider a tunnel diode with \(N_{D}=N_{A} \) and with the impurity concentration corresponding to 1 atom per \(10^{3} \) germanium atoms. At room temperature calculate (i) the height of the potential energy barrier under open‑circuit conditions (the contact potential energy), and (ii) the width of the depletion region.

(b) Repeat part (a) for the silicon tunnel diode.

Admin
Solution

(a) The number of atoms per unit volume of any material can be given by

\(N\left\lgroup\frac{atoms}{cm^{3}} \right\rgroup =6.02\times 10^{23}\frac{atoms}{mole}\times \frac{1mole}{M(atomic weight in gram)} \times d\left\lgroup density in \frac{g}{cm^{3}} \right\rgroup \)

Putting M = 72.59 g and d = 5.32 \(g/cm^{3} \) in the above equation, the atomic concentration of germanium is obtained as

\(N=6.02\times 10^{23}\frac{atoms}{mole}\times \frac{1mole}{72.59 g}\times 5.32 \frac{g}{cm^{3}} =4.41\times 10^{22} atoms/cm^{3} \)

Clearly, the impurity concentrations \(N_{A} \) and \(N_{D} \) are \(N_{A} =N_{D}=\frac{N}{10^{3}} =4.41\times 10^{19}cm^{-3} \) .

Now, the height of the potential energy barrier under open‑circuit condition can be obtained from Eq. (5.8) as

\(E_{0}=kT1n\left\lgroup\frac{N_{A}N_{D}}{n^2_{i}} \right\rgroup =0.026\times 2\times 1n\left\lgroup\frac{4.41\times 10^{19}}{2.5\times 10^{13}} \right\rgroup eV = 0.75 eV \)

Thus, the contact potential barrier across the p‑n junction is V0 = 0.75 V.

The width of the depletion region can be obtained by using the permittivity of germanium \(\epsilon =16\times 8.85\times 10^{-14} F/cm \) ,

\(N_{A} =N_{D}=\frac{N}{10^{3}} =4.41\times 10^{19}cm^{-3} \) , V0 = 0.75 V and V = 0 in the expression of W derived in which can be calculated as

\(W=\sqrt{\frac{2\times 16\times 8.85\times 10^{-14}}{1.6\times 10^{-19}} \times \frac{2}{4.41\times 10^{19}}\times 0.75 } =77.7\times 10^{-8} cm \) = 77.7 Å

(b) Since M = 28.09 g and d = 2.33 \(g/cm^{3} \) for silicon, following the similar method of part (a), we obtain \(N=5\times 10^{22} atoms/cm^{3}\), \(N_{A}=N_{D}=\frac{N}{10^{3}}=5\times 10^{19} cm^{-3} \) , E0= 1.09 eV and V0 = 1.09 V for silicon semiconductor based tunnel diode.

The width of the depletion region can also be calculated by using \(\epsilon =11.7\times 8.85\times 10^{-14} \)F/cm , \(N_{A}=N_{D}=5\times 10^{19} cm^{-3} \) , V0 = 0.75 V and V = 0 in the expression of W as in part (a) and can be given by

\(W=\sqrt{\frac{2\times 11.7\times 8.85\times 10^{-14}}{1.6\times 10^{-19}} \times \frac{2}{5\times 10^{19}}\times 1.09 } =76\times 10^{-8} cm \) = 76 Å
