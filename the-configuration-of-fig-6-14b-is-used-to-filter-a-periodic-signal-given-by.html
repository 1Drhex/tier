Guest Question.

The configuration of Fig. 6.14b is used to filter a periodic signal given by

\(\tilde{x}(t)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} x\left(t+n \tau_{0}\right) \quad \text { where } \quad x(t)= \begin{cases}\sin \omega_{0} t & \text { for } 0 \leq t \leq \tau_{0} / 2 \\ 0 & \text { for }-\tau_{0} / 2 \leq t \leq 0\end{cases}\)

where \(\omega_{0}=2 \pi / \tau_{0}\). The lowpass filters are characterized by

\(H_{ LP }(j \omega)= \begin{cases}1 & \text { for } 0 \leq|\omega|<6 \omega_{0} \\ 0 & \text { otherwise }\end{cases}\)

and the digital filter has a baseband response

\(H_{D}\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)= \begin{cases}T & \text { for } 0.95 \omega_{0}<|\omega|<1.05 \omega_{0} \\ 0 & \text { otherwise }\end{cases}\)

Assuming that \(\omega_{s}=12 \omega_{0}\), find the time- and frequency-domain representations of the signals at nodes 1, 2, . . . , 7.

Admin
Solution

Node 1 From , part (b), the Fourier series of the signal can be expressed as

 

\(x_{1}(t)=\frac{1}{2} a_{0}+\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_{n} \cos n \omega_{0} t+\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_{n} \sin n \omega_{0} t\) (6.58)

 

where

 

\(a_{0}=\frac{2}{\pi} \quad a_{1}=0 \quad a_{2}=-\frac{2}{3 \pi} \quad a_{3}=0 \quad a_{4}=-\frac{2}{15 \pi}\)

 

\(a_{5}=0 \quad a_{6}=-\frac{2}{35 \pi} \quad a_{7}=0 \cdots\)

 

\(b_{1}=\frac{1}{2} \quad b_{2}=0 \quad b_{3}=0 \quad b_{4}=0 \quad b_{5}=0 \quad b_{6}=0, \cdots\)

 

and from Table 6.2, the Fourier transform of \(x_{1}(t)\) can be obtained as

 

\(X_{1}(j \omega)= F x_{1}(t)=a_{0} \pi \delta(\omega)+\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} a_{n} \pi\left[\delta\left(\omega+n \omega_{0}\right)+\delta\left(\omega-n \omega_{0}\right)\right]\)

 

\(+\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} j b_{n} \pi\left[\delta\left(\omega+n \omega_{0}\right)-\delta\left(\omega-n \omega_{0}\right)\right]\) (6.59)

 

Node 2 The bandlimiting filter will remove all frequencies equal to or greater than \(6 \omega_{0}\) and hence Eq. (6.58) gives

 

\(x_{2}(t)=\frac{1}{2} a_{0}+\sum_{n=1}^{4} a_{n} \cos n \omega_{0} t+\sum_{n=1}^{4} b_{n} \sin n \omega_{0} t\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{\pi}+\frac{1}{2} \sin \omega_{0} t-\frac{2}{3 \pi} \cos 2 \omega_{0} t-\frac{2}{15 \pi} \cos 4 \omega_{0} t\)

 

and

 

\(X_{2}(j \omega)= F x_{2}(t)\)

 

\(=2 \delta(\omega)+j \frac{\pi}{2}\left[\delta\left(\omega+\omega_{0}\right)-\delta\left(\omega-\omega_{0}\right)\right]-\frac{2}{3}\left[\delta\left(\omega+2 \omega_{0}\right)\right.\)

 

\(\left.+\delta\left(\omega-2 \omega_{0}\right)\right]-\frac{2}{15}\left[\delta\left(\omega+4 \omega_{0}\right)+\delta\left(\omega-4 \omega_{0}\right)\right]\)

 

Nodes 3 and 4 The output of the impulse modulator is obtained from Eq. (6.42d) as

 

\(\hat{x}(t)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} x(n T) \delta(t-n T)\) (6.42d)

 

\(\hat{x}_{3}(t)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} x(n T) \delta(t-n T)\)

 

\(=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}\left(\frac{1}{\pi}+\frac{1}{2} \sin \omega_{0} n T-\frac{2}{3 \pi} \cos 2 \omega_{0} n T-\frac{2}{15 \pi} \cos 4 \omega_{0} n T\right) \delta(t-n T)\)

 

Thus

 

\(x_{4}(n T)=\frac{1}{\pi}+\frac{1}{2} \sin \omega_{0} n T-\frac{2}{3 \pi} \cos 2 \omega_{0} n T-\frac{2}{15 \pi} \cos 4 \omega_{0} n T\)

 

and from Eq. (6.45a), we have

 

\(\hat{X}(j \omega)=X_{D}\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)=\frac{1}{T} \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} X\left(j \omega+j n \omega_{s}\right)\) (6.45a)

 

\(\hat{X}_{3}(j \omega)=X_{4}\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)=\frac{1}{T} \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} X_{2}\left(j \omega+j n \omega_{0}\right)\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{T} \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}\left\{2 \delta\left(\omega+n \omega_{s}\right)+j \frac{\pi}{2}\left[\delta\left(\omega+n \omega_{s}+\omega_{0}\right)-\delta\left(\omega+n \omega_{s}-\omega_{0}\right)\right]\right.\)

 

\(-\frac{2}{3}\left[\delta\left(\omega+n \omega_{s}+2 \omega_{0}\right)+\delta\left(\omega+n \omega_{s}-2 \omega_{0}\right)\right]\)

 

\(\left.-\frac{2}{15}\left[\delta\left(\omega+n \omega_{s}+4 \omega_{0}\right)+\delta\left(\omega+n \omega_{s}-4 \omega_{0}\right)\right]\right\}\)

 

Nodes 5 and 6 The bandpass digital filter will reject all components except those with frequencies \(\pm \omega_{0} \pm n \omega_{s}\) and it will also provide a gain of T , and so

 

\(X_{5}\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)=\hat{X}_{6}(j \omega)\)

 

\(=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} j \frac{\pi}{2}\left[\delta\left(\omega+n \omega_{s}+\omega_{0}\right)-\delta\left(\omega+n \omega_{s}-\omega_{0}\right)\right]\)

 

Thus, from Table 6.2, we get

 

\(x_{5}(n T)=\frac{1}{2} \sin \omega_{0} n T\)

 

and

 

\(\hat{x}_{6}(t)=\frac{1}{2} \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}\left(\sin \omega_{0} n T\right) \delta(t-n T)\)

 

Node 7 Finally, the lowpass filter at the output will reject all components with frequencies outside the baseband, and as a result

 

\(X_{7}(j \omega)=\left(\frac{j \pi}{2}\right)\left[\delta\left(\omega+\omega_{0}\right)-\delta\left(\omega-\omega_{0}\right)\right]\)

 

and

 

\(x_{7}(t)=\frac{1}{2} \sin \omega_{0} t\)

 

The various signal waveforms and amplitude spectrums are illustrated in Fig. 6.15a and b, respectively.
