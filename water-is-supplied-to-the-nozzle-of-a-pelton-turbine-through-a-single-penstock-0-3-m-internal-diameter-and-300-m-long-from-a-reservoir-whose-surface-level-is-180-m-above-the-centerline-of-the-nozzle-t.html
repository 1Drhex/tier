Guest Question.

Water is supplied to the nozzle of a Pelton turbine through a single penstock 0.3 m internal diameter and 300 m long from a reservoir whose surface level is 180 m above the centerline of the nozzle. The friction factor for the penstock is \(f = 0.04 \). The head loss of the flow in the nozzle is \(0.04c^{2}_{1} /\left(2g\right) \). Determine the jet diameter that will give the maximum power in the jet.

Admin
Solution

The energy equation for the pipe flow can be written as

 

\(H_{E} -\frac{flc^{2}_{p}}{2gd}-\frac{0.04c^{2}_{1}}{2g} =\frac{c^{2}_{1}}{2g} \)

 

where \(H_{E}\) is the effective head and \(c_{p}\) is the flow velocity in the pipeline.
Substituting values, we get

 

\(180-\frac{0.04\times 300\times c^{2}_{p}}{2\times 9.81\times 0.3} =\frac{1.04\times c^{2}_{1}}{2\times 9.81} \therefore 180-2.039\times c^{2}_{p} =0.053\times c^{2}_{1} \) (i)

 

From the continuity equation,

\(\therefore d^{2}c_{1}=d^{2}_{p}c_{p} \) so that \(c_{p}=c_{1}\left(\frac{d}{d_{p}} \right) ^{2} \) (ii)

Substituting Eq. (ii) into Eq. (i) and simplifying, we get

\(180=c^{2}_{1} \left(0.053+251.7 d^{4}\right) \) (iii)

Choose a range of values of d, calculate \(c_{1}\) using Eq. (iii), then Q and P as shown in the table below:

d (m) 0.053+251.7 \(d^{4}\) \(c_{1} \) (m/s) Q(\(m^{3}/s \)) P (kW)
0.06 0.06015 54.7 0.1547 23.59
0.08 0.06331 53.41 0.2685 39.04
0.10 0.07817 47.99 0.3769 44.24
0.12 0.1052 41.36 0.4677 40.78
0.14 0.1497 34.68 0.5339 32.73
0.16 0.2180 28.73 0.5777 24.30

The maximum power for the jet is 44.24 kW at d = 0.10 m.
Of course it is possible to obtain a solution for the jet diameter and maximum power by setting up an expression for the power and differentiating with respect to the diameter. The advantage of the tabular method used here is that of being able to observe the variations of the various factors with the change in diameter of the jet.
As an exercise, it would be useful to determine the nozzle efficiency, \(\eta _{N}\), as given in Eq. (9.9a). We must first calculate \(H_{E}=H_{g}-H_{f}\), where

 

\(\eta _{N}=\frac{ energy at nozzle exit}{energy at nozzle inlet}=\frac{ c^{2}_{1}}{2g H_{E}} \) (9.9a)

 

\(H_{f}=\left(\frac{8fl}{\pi ^{2}g } \right) \frac{Q^{2} }{d^{5} } =57.94 m\)

\(\therefore H_{E}=180-57.94=122.06 m\)

\(\therefore \eta _{N}=\frac{ c^{2}_{1}}{2g H_{E}} =\frac{47.99^{2}}{2\times 9.81\times 122.06} =0.9617\)
