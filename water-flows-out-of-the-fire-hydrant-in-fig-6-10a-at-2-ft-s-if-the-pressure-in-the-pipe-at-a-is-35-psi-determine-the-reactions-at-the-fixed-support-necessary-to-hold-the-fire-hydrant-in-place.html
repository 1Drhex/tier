Guest Question.

Water flows out of the fire hydrant in Fig. 6-10a at 2 ft/s. If the pressure in the pipe at A is 35 psi, determine the reactions at the fixed support necessary to hold the fire hydrant in place.

Admin
Solution

Fluid Description. This is a case of steady flow. The water will be assumed to be an ideal fluid, where \(\gamma = 62.4 lb/ft^3.\)

 

Control Volume and Free-Body Diagram. We will consider the entire fire hydrant and water contained within it as a fixed control volume. Since the support at A is fixed, three reactions act on its free bexty diagram. Fig. 6-10b. Also, the pressure force \( P_AA_A, \) acts on the open control surface at A. Since atmospheric pressure exists at B. there is no pressure force at B. Here we will neglect the weight of the fire hydrant and the water within it.

Bernoulli Equation. We must first determine the pressure at A. The velocities at A and B are

\(Q = V_AA_A ; (2 ft^3/s) = V_A \left[\pi \left(\frac{2}{12} ft \right)^2 \right] V_A =22.92 ft/s\)

 

\(Q = V_BA_B ; (2 ft^3/s) = V_B \left[\pi \left(\frac{1.5}{12} ft \right)^2 \right] V_B =40 ft/s\)

Thus, with the datum at A.

\(\frac{P_A}{\gamma } + \frac{V_A^2}{2g} + z_A = \frac{P_B}{\gamma } + \frac{V_B^2}{2g} + z_B \)

 

\(\frac{P_A}{62.4 Ib/ft^3} + \frac{(22.92 m/s)^2}{2( 32.2 ft/s^2)} + 0 = 0 + \frac{(40.74 m/s )^2}{2(32.2 ft/s^2)} + 2 ft\)

 

\(P_A = 1224.4 Ib/ft^2\)

Linear and Angular Momentum. The force reactions at the support are obtained from the linear momentum equation. For steady flow,

\(\sum{F} = \frac{\partial}{\partial t}\int_{cv}V\rho dV + \int_{cs} V\rho V\cdot dA\)

 

\(\sum{F} = 0 + V_B(\rho V_BA_B) + V_A(-\rho V_AA_A) \)

Considering the r and y components of the velocities, we have

\(\xrightarrow{+} \sum{F_x} = V_{Bx}(\rho V_BA_B) + 0 \)

 

\(F_X = (40.74 ft/s)(\frac{62.4}{32.2} slug/ft^3 )(40.74 ft/s)^2[\pi (\frac{1.5}{12} ft )^2]\)

 

\(F_x = 158 Ib\)

 

\(+\uparrow \sum{F_y} = 0 + (v_{Ay}) (-\rho V_AA_A)\)

 

\((122.4 Ib/ft^2)[\pi (\frac{2}{12} ft)^2 ] – F_y = -(22.92 ft/s)(\frac{62.4}{32.2} slug/ft^3 )(22.92 ft/s)[\pi (\frac{2}{12} ft)^2]\)

 

\(F_y = 196 Ib\)

We will apply the angular momentum equation about point, in order to eliminate the force reactions at this point.

\(\sum{M}_A = \frac{\partial}{\partial t}\int_{cv}(r \times V)\rho dV + \int_{cs}(r \times V)\rho V\cdot dA\)

 

\(\Rsh + \sum{M}_{A} = 0 + (rV_B)(\rho V_BA_B) \)

Here the vector cross product has been evaluated as a scalar moment of Vg about point A. Therefore,

\(M_A = [2ft(40.74 ft/s)](\frac{62.4}{32.2} slug/ft^3)(40.74 ft/s)[\pi (\frac{1.5}{12} ft )^2]\)

 

\(= 316 Ib\cdot ft \)
