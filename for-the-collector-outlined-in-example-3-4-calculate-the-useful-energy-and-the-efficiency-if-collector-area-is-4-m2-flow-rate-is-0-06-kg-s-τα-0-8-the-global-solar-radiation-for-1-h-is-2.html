Guest Question.

For the collector outlined in , calculate the useful energy and the efficiency if collector area is 4 \(m^2\), flow rate is 0.06 kg/s, \((\tau \alpha )\)= 0.8, the global solar radiation for 1h is 2.88 MJ/\(m^2\), and the collector operates at a temperature difference of 5°C.

Admin
Solution

The dimensionless collector capacitance rate is

\(\frac{\dot{m} c_{p}}{A_{c}U_{L}F^{′} } =\frac{0.06\times 4180}{4\times 6.9 \times 0.91} = 9.99\)

From Eq. (3.59), \(F^{′′}= \frac{F_{R}}{F^{′}}=\frac{\dot{m} c_{p}}{A_{c}U_{L}F^{′}} \left\lgroup1-Exp\left[-\frac{U_{L}F'A_{c}}{\dot{m}c_{p} }\right] \right\rgroup\)

\(F^{′′}=9.99(1-e^{-1/9.99})= 0.952\)

Therefore, the heat removal factor is

\(F_{R}=F^{′}\times F^{′′} = 0.91\times 0.952 = 0.886\)

From Eq. (3.60)

\(Q_{u}=A_{c}F_{R}[G_{t}(\tau \alpha )-U_{L}(T_{i}-T_{a})]\)

modified to use \(I_{t}\) instead of \(G_{t}\),

\(Q_{u}=A_{c}F_{R}[I_{t}(\tau \alpha )-U_{L}(T_{i}-T_{a})\times 3.6]=4\times 0.866[2.88\times 10^3\times 0.8 – 6.9 \times 5\times 3.6]= 7550 KJ = 7.55 MJ\)

and the collector efficiency is

\(\eta =Q_{u}/A_{c}I_{t}= 7.55/(4\times 2.88)= 0.655\), or 65.5%
