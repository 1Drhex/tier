Guest Question.

The pressure vessel in Figure P14.19 consists of spirally wrapped steel plates that are welded at the seams in the orientation shown where β = 35°. The cylinder has an inside diameter of 540 mm and a wall thickness of 10 mm. The ends of the cylinder are capped by two rigid end plates. The gage pressure inside the cylinder is 4.25 MPa, and compressive axial loads of P = 215 kN are applied to the rigid end caps. Determine:
(a) the normal stress perpendicular to the weld seams.
(b) the shear stress parallel to the weld seams.
(c) the absolute maximum shear stress in the cylinder.

Admin
Solution

(a) Normal stress perpendicular to the weld

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{\text {long }}=\frac{p d}{4 t}=\frac{(4.25 MPa )(540 mm )}{4(10 mm )}=57.375 MPa \\&\sigma_{\text {hoop }}=\frac{p d}{2 t}=\frac{(4.25 MPa )(540 mm )}{2(10 mm )}=114.750 MPa\end{aligned}\)

The compressive axial load also creates a normal stress in the x direction.

\(\begin{aligned}&A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[D^{2}-d^{2}\right]=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[(560 mm )^{2}-(540 mm )^{2}\right]=17,278.760 mm ^{2} \\&\sigma_{\text {axial }}=\frac{P}{A}=\frac{-215,000 N }{17,278.760 mm ^{2}}=-12.443 MPa\end{aligned}\)

The longitudinal axis of the cylinder is defined as the x axis and the circumferential direction is defined as the y axis; therefore, the normal and shear stresses on longitudinal and circumferential faces of a stress element are:

\(\sigma_{x}=44.932 MPa , \sigma_{y}=114.750 MPa , \tau_{x y}=0 MPa\)

The weld is oriented at 35° as shown. The angle θ required for the stress transformation equations is the angle normal to the weld, which is also 35°. Using this value of θ, the normal stress transformation equation [Eq. (12-3)] can be used to compute the normal stress perpendicular to the weld:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{n} &=\sigma_{x} \cos ^{2} \theta+\sigma_{y} \sin ^{2} \theta+2 \tau_{x y} \sin \theta \cos \theta \\&=(44.932 MPa ) \cos ^{2}\left(35^{\circ}\right)+(114.750 MPa ) \sin ^{2}\left(35^{\circ}\right)+2(0 MPa ) \sin \left(35^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(35^{\circ}\right) \\&=67.901 MPa =67.9 MPa ( T )\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Shear stress parallel to the weld
Similarly, the shear stress transformation equation [Eq. (12-4)] gives \(\tau_{n t}\):

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{n t} &=-\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) \sin \theta \cos \theta+\tau_{x y}\left(\cos ^{2} \theta-\sin ^{2} \theta\right) \\&=-[(44.932 MPa )-(114.750 MPa )] \sin \left(35^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(35^{\circ}\right)+(0 MPa )\left[\cos ^{2}\left(35^{\circ}\right)-\sin ^{2}\left(35^{\circ}\right)\right] \\&=32.804 MPa =32.8 MPa\end{aligned}\) \(\sigma_{p 1}=\sigma_{\text {hoop }}=114.750 MPa \quad \sigma_{p 2}=\sigma_{\text {long }}+\sigma_{\text {axial }}=44.932 MPa \quad \sigma_{p 3}=0\)

On the outside surface of the cylinder:

\(\tau_{ abs \max }=\frac{\sigma_{p 1}-0}{2}=\frac{114.750 MPa }{2}=57.375 MPa\)

Inside the cylinder, the third principal stress is equal in magnitude to the internal pressure:

\(\sigma_{p 3}=\sigma_{\text {radial }}=-p=-4.25 MPa\)

On the inside surface of the cylinder:

\(\tau_{\text {abs } \max }=\frac{\sigma_{p 1}-\sigma_{p 3}}{2}=\frac{114.750 MPa -(-4.25 MPa )}{2}=59.500 MPa\)

Thus, the absolute maximum shear stress in the cylinder is

\(\tau_{\text {abs } \max }=59.5 MPa\)
