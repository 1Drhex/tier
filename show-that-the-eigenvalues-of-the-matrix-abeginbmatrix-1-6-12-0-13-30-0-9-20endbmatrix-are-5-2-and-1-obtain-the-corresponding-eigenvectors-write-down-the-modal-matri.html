Guest Question.

Show that the eigenvalues of the matrix A=\(\begin{bmatrix}-1 & 6 & -12\\ 0 & -13 & 30\\ 0 & -9 & 20\end{bmatrix}\) are 5, 2 and .1. Obtain the corresponding

eigenvectors. Write down the modal matrix M and spectral matrix\( \Lambda\) . Evaluate \(M^{-1}\) and show that \(M^{-1}AM=\Lambda\).

Admin
Solution

Eigenvalues given by
\(\Delta =\begin{vmatrix}-1-\lambda & 6 & -12\\ 0 & -13-\lambda & 30\\ 0 & -9 & 20-\lambda \end{vmatrix}\)=0

NOW \(\Delta =(-1-\lambda )\begin{vmatrix}-13-\lambda & 30\\ -9 & 20-\lambda \end{vmatrix}=(-1-\lambda )(\lambda ^{2}-7\lambda +10)\)

=\((-1-\lambda )(\lambda -5)(\lambda -2) so \Delta =0, \lambda _{1}=5 ,\lambda _{2},\lambda _{3}\)=-1

Corresponding eigenvectors are given by the solutions of
\((A-\lambda _{i}I)e_{i}\)=0
When\( \lambda =\lambda _{1}\)=5 We have

\(\begin{bmatrix}-6 & 6 & -12\\ 0 & -18 & 30\\ 0 & -9 & 15\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}e_{11}\\ e_{12}\\ e_{13}\end{bmatrix}\)=0

leading to the solution
\(\frac{e_{11}}{-36}=\frac{-e_{12}}{-180}=\frac{e_{13}}{108}=\beta _{1}\)
so the eigenvector corresponding to
\(\lambda _{1}=5 is e_{12}=\beta _{1}\begin{bmatrix}1 & -5 & -3\end{bmatrix}^{T}\)
When\(\lambda =\lambda _{2}\)=2 , we have
\(\begin{bmatrix}-3 & 6 & -12\\ 0 & -15 & 30\\ 0 & -9 & 18\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}e_{21}\\ e_{22}\\ e_{23}\end{bmatrix}\)=0
leading to the solution
\(\frac{e_{21}}{0}=\frac{-e_{22}}{-90}=\frac{e_{23}}{45}=\beta _{2}\)
so the eigenvector corresponding to
\(\lambda _{2}=2 is e_{2}=\beta _{2}\begin{bmatrix}0 & 2 & 1\end{bmatrix}^{T}\)
When \(\lambda =\lambda _{3}\)=-1 we have
\(\begin{bmatrix}0 & 6 & -12\\ 0 & -15 & -12\\ 0 & -9 & 18\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}e_{21}\\ e_{22}\\ e_{33}\end{bmatrix}\)=0

leading to the solution
\(\frac{e_{31}}{18}=\frac{-e_{32}}{0}=\frac{e_{33}}{0}=\beta _{3}\)
so the eigenvector corresponding to
\(\lambda _{3}=-1 is e_{3}=\beta _{3}\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}^{T}\)
A modal matrix M and spectral matrix \(\Lambda\) are
M =\(\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 1\\ -5 & 2 & 0\\ -3 & 1 & 0\end{bmatrix}\)

\(\Lambda =\begin{bmatrix}5 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 2 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & -1\end{bmatrix}\) \(M^{-1}=\begin{bmatrix}0 & 1 &-2 \\ 2 & 0 & 1\\ 1 & -1 & 2\end{bmatrix}\)

and matrix multiplication confirms \(M^{-1}AM=\Lambda\) .
