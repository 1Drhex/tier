Guest Question.

An air flow 2 kg/s at 30째C, and relative humidity 80% is conditioned by taking half the flow, cooling it and mix it with the other half. Assume the outlet flow should have a water content that is 75% of the original flow. Find the temperature to cool to, the rate of cooling and the final exit flow temperature.

Admin
Solution

CV Total setup

Continuity water: \(0=\dot{ m }_{ a } \omega_{1}-\dot{ m }_{ liq }-\dot{ m }_{ a } \omega_{3} ; \quad \dot{ m }_{ liq }=\dot{ m }_{ a }\left(\omega_{1}-\omega_{3}\right)\)

State 1:

\(\begin{aligned}& P _{ g 1}=4.246 ; P _{ v 1}=0.8 \times 4.246=3.3968 kPa \\&\omega_{1}=0.622 \times 3.3968 /(100-3.3968)=0.02187\end{aligned}\)

CV Junction

Continuity water: \(0=0.5 \dot{ m }_{ a } \omega_{1}+0.5 \dot{ m }_{ a } \omega_{2}-\dot{ m }_{ a } \omega_{3} ; \quad \omega_{3}=0.75 \omega_{1}\)

Energy Eq.: \(0=0.5 \dot{ m }_{ a }\left( h _{ a 1}+\omega_{1} h _{ v 1}\right)+0.5 \dot{ m }_{ a }\left( h _{ a 2}+\omega_{2} h _{ v 2}\right)-\dot{ m }_{ a }\left( h _{ a 3}+\omega_{3} h _{ v 3}\right)\)

Solve for \(\omega_{2}: \quad \omega_{2}=2 \omega_{3}-\omega_{1}=1.5 \omega_{1}-\omega_{1}=0.5 \omega_{1}=0.010935\)

State 2:

\(\begin{aligned}&\phi_{2}=100 \%, \omega_{2}=0.010935 \Rightarrow P _{ v 2}=100 \frac{\omega_{2}}{0.622+\omega_{2}}=1.728 kPa \\& P _{ v 2}= P _{ g 2} \Rightarrow T _{2}= 1 5 . 2 ^{\circ} C ; \quad h _{ f }=63.74 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

CV Cooler

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ Q } &=0.5 \dot{ m }_{ a }\left( h _{2}- h _{1}\right)_{ a }+0.5 \dot{ m }_{ a }\left(\omega_{2} h _{ v 2}-\omega_{1} h _{ v 1}\right)+\dot{ m }_{ liq } h _{ f } \\&=0.5 \dot{ m }_{ a }\left[ C _{ p a }\left( T _{2}- T _{1}\right)+\omega_{2} h _{ v 2}-\omega_{1} h _{ v 1}+2\left(\omega_{1}-\omega_{3}\right) h _{ f }\right] \\=& 0.5 \frac{2}{1+\omega_{1}} kg / s [1.004(15.2-30)+0.010935 \times 2529.3\\&\quad-0.02187 \times 2556.25+2 \times 0.25 \times 0.02187 \times 63.74] kJ / kg \\=&- 4 1 . 5 k W\end{aligned}\)

From the energy equation for the junction

\(\begin{aligned}h _{ a 3}+\omega_{3} h _{ v 3}=& 0.5\left[ h _{ a 1}+\omega_{1} h _{ v 1}+ h _{ a 2}+\omega_{2} h _{ v 2}\right]=0.5[1.004(30+15.2)\\&+0.02187 \times 2556.25+0.010935 \times 2529.3]=64.472 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

This is now trial and error on \(T _{3}\).

\(\begin{aligned}& T _{3}=20 C : LHS =1.004 \times 20+0.016403 \times 2538.06=61.71 kJ / kg \\& T _{3}=25 C : LHS =1.004 \times 25+0.016403 \times 2547.17=66.88 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

Interpolate for final ans.: \(T _{3}=22.7^{\circ} C\)
