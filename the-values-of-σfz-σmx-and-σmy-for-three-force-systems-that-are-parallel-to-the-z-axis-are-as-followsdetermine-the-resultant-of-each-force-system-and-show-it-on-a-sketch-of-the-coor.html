Guest Question.

The values of \(\Sigma F_{z}\), \(\Sigma M_{x}\), and \(\Sigma M_{y}\) for three force systems that are parallel to the z-axis are as follows:

part \(\Sigma F_{z}\) \(\Sigma M_{x}\) \(\Sigma M_{y}\)
1 50 KN 60 KN.m -125 KN.m
2 -600 N 0 -1200 N.m
3 0 600 N.mm -800N.cm

Determine the resultant of each force system and show it on a sketch of the coordinate system.

Admin
Solution

Part 1

\(\Sigma F_{z}=50 KN\) \(\Sigma M_{x}=60 KN.m\) \(\Sigma M_{y}=-125 KN.m\)

The resultant is the force R=50k kN. With \(\Sigma M_{O}=\Sigma M_{x}i+\Sigma M_{y}j\) and r=xi + yj, Eq. (3.10) can be used to determine the line of action of R:

\(\Sigma M_{O}=r\times R\) \(60i-125j=\left|\begin{matrix} i & j & k \\ x & y & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 50 \end{matrix} \right|=50yi-50xj\)

Equating like components gives the following scalar equations:

60=50y and − 125= −50x

which gives x =2.5 m and y =1.2 m. The sketch of the resultant is shown in Fig. (a).Identical results for x and y are obtained if one uses Eqs. (3.11):

\(x=-\frac{\Sigma M_{y}}{R}=-\frac{-125}{50}=2.5 m\)

 

\(y=\frac{\Sigma M_{x}}{R}=\frac{60}{50}=1.2 m\)

Part 2

\(\Sigma F_{z}=-600 N\) \(\Sigma M_{x}=0\) \(\Sigma M_{y}=-1200 N.m\)

The resultant is the force R= −600k N. In this case, Eq. (3.10) yields

\(\Sigma M_{O}=r\times R\) \(-1200j=\left|\begin{matrix} i & j & k \\ x & y & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -600 \end{matrix} \right|=-600yi+600xj\)

Equating like components gives x= −2 m and y =0. The resultant is shown in Fig. (b).

Part 3

\(\Sigma F_{z}=0\) \(\Sigma M_{x}=600 N.m\) \(\Sigma M_{y}=-800 N.cm\)

Because the sum of the forces is zero and the sum of the moments is not zero, the resultant is the couple-vector \(C^{R}=\Sigma M_{x}i+\Sigma M_{y}j=600i − 800j N · cm\),shown in Fig. (c). The magnitude of this couple-vector is 1000 N · cm.
