ISENTROPIC FLOW IN A CONVERGING NOZZLE

A converging nozzle, with a throat area of 0.001 m\(^2\), is operated with air at a back pressure of 591 kPa (abs). The nozzle is fed from a large plenum chamber where the absolute stagnation pressure and temperature are 1.0 MPa and 60째C. The exit Mach number and mass flow rate are to be determined.

Admin
Solution

Given: Air flow through a converging nozzle at the conditions shown: Flow is isentropic.


Find:

(a) \(M_e\).
(b) \(\dot m\).
Solution:
The first step is to check for choking. The pressure ratio is

\(\frac{p_{b}}{p_{0}}=\frac{5.91 \times 10^{5}}{1.0 \times 10^{6}}=0.591>0.528\)

so the flow is not choked. Thus \(p_{b}=p_{e}\),

and the flow is isentropic, as sketched on the Ts diagram.
Since \(p_{0}=\) constant, \(M_{e}\) may be found from the pressure ratio,

\(\frac{p_{0}}{p_{e}}=\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{e}^{2}\right]^{k /(k-1)}\)

 

Solving for \(M_{e}\), since \(p_{e}=p_{b}\), we obtain

\(1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{e}^{2}=\left(\frac{p_{0}}{p_{b}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}\)

 

and

\(M_{e}=\left\{\left[\left(\frac{p_{0}}{p_{b}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}-1\right] \frac{2}{k-1}\right\}^{1 / 2}=\left\{\left[\left(\frac{1.0 \times 10^{6}}{5.91 \times 10^{5}}\right)^{0.286}-1\right] \frac{2}{1.4-1}\right\}^{1 / 2}=0.90\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad M_e}\)

The mass flow rate is

\(\dot{m}=\rho_{e} V_{e} \Lambda_{e}=\rho_{e} M_{e} c_{e} \Lambda_{e}\)

 

We need \(T_{e}\) to find \(\rho_{e}\) and \(c_{e}\). Since \(T_{0}=\) constant,

\(\frac{T_{0}}{T_{e}}=1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{e}^{2}\)

 

or

\(\begin{gathered}T_{e}=\frac{T_{0}}{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{e}^{2}}=\frac{(273+60) \mathrm{K}}{1+0.2(0.9)^{2}}=287 \mathrm{~K} \\\quad\\c_{e}=\sqrt{k R T_{e}}=\left[1.4 \times 287 \frac{N \cdot m}{k g \cdot K} \times 287 \mathrm{~K} \times \frac{k g \cdot m}{\mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}\right]^{1 / 2}=340 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\end{gathered}\)

and

\(\rho_{e}=\frac{p_{e}}{R T_{e}}=5.91 \times 10^{5} \frac{\mathrm{N}}{\mathrm{m}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}}{287 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m}} \times \frac{1}{287 \mathrm{~K}}=7.18 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\)

 

Finally,

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{m} &=\rho_{e} M_{e} c_{e} A_{e}=7.18 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}} \times 0.9 \times 340 \frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}} \times 0.001 \mathrm{~m}^{2} \\\quad\\&=2.20 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{s} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad \dot{m}}\end{aligned}\)

 

This problem illustrates use of the isentropic equations, Eqs. 13.7, for a flow that is not choked.

 

\(\begin{gathered}\frac{p_{0}}{p}=\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\right]^{k /(k-1)} \\\quad\\\frac{T_{0}}{T}=1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\\\quad\\\frac{\rho_{0}}{\rho}=\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\right]^{1 /(k-1)} \\\quad\\\frac{A}{A^{*}}=\frac{1}{M}\left[\frac{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}}{\frac{k+1}{2}}\right]^{(k+1) / 2(k-1)}\end{gathered}\) (13.7 a,b,c,d)

 

The Excel workbook for this Example is convenient for performing the calculations (using either the isentropic equations or the basic equations). (The Excel add-ins for isentropic flow, on the Web site, also make calculations much easier.)
