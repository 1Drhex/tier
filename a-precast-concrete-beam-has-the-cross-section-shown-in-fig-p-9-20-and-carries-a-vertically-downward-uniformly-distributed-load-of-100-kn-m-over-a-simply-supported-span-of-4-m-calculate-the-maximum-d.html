Guest Question.

A precast concrete beam has the cross section shown in Fig. P.9.20 and carries a vertically downward uniformly distributed load of 100 kN/m over a simply supported span of 4 m. Calculate the maximum direct stress in the cross section of the beam, indicating clearly the point at which it acts.

Admin
Solution

The maximum bending moment occurs at mid-span and is given by

\( M_{\max }=\frac{100 \times 4^{2}}{8}=200 kNm =M_{z} \)

The area of cross section is given by

\( A=300 \times 500-220 \times 400+100 \times 50=67000 mm ^{2}\)

Then,

\( 67000 \bar{y}=(500 \times 300-220 \times 400) \times 250+100 \times 50 \times 25 \)

i.e.

\( \bar{y}=233.2 mm \)

and

\( 67000 \bar{z}=(500 \times 300-220 \times 400) \times 150+100 \times 50 \times 350 \)

i.e.

\( \bar{z}=164.9 mm \)

The second moments of area are

\(\begin{aligned}I_{z}=&\left(\frac{300 \times 500^{3}}{12}+300 \times 500 \times 16.8^{2}\right)-\left(\frac{220 \times 400^{3}}{12}+220 \times 400 \times 16.8^{2}\right) \\&+\frac{100 \times 50^{3}}{12}+100 \times 50 \times 208.2^{2}=2.19 \times 10^{9} mm ^{4} \\I_{y}=&\left(\frac{500 \times 300^{3}}{12}+500 \times 300 \times 14.9^{2}\right)-\left(\frac{400 \times 220^{3}}{12}+400 \times 220 \times 14.9^{2}\right) \\&+\frac{50 \times 100^{3}}{12}+50 \times 100 \times 185.1^{2}=1.00 \times 10^{9} mm ^{4} \\I_{z y}=&(500 \times 300-400 \times 220)(14.9)(16.8)+100 \times 50(-185.1)(-208.2) \\=& 0.21 \times 10^{9} mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

The denominator in Eq. (9.31) is then equal to \( \left(2.19 \times 1.00-0.21^{2}\right) \times 10^{18}=2.15 \times 10^{18} \) and the direct stress is given by

\(\sigma_{x}=-\left(\frac{M_{y} I_{z}-M_{z} I_{z y}}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}}\right) z-\left(\frac{M_{z} I_{y}-M_{y} I_{z y}}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}}\right) y\) (9.31)

\( \sigma_{z}=-\left(\frac{-200 \times 10^{6} \times 0.21 \times 10^{9}}{2.15 \times 10^{18}}\right) z-\left(\frac{200 \times 10^{6} \times 1.00 \times 10^{9}}{2.15 \times 10^{18}}\right) y \)

i.e.

\( \sigma_{z}=0.0195 z-0.0930 y \) (i)

The maximum stress will occur at a point whose coordinates are of opposite sign. By inspection the critical point is B where z=-135.1 mm and y=266.8 mm. Then

\( \sigma_{\max }=-27.4 N / mm ^{2} \)
