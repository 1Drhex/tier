Guest Question.

This example repeats the calculations of using the same blade specification (i.e., the pitch angle β = β(r)) but now it includes the Prandtl correction factor. The results of the iterations to determine a, \(a^{\prime } \) , \(\phi \), and \(C_{L} \) and used as data for the summations are shown in Table 10.5. The details of the calculation for one mid-ordinate radius (r/R = 0.95) are shown first to clarify the process.

Admin
Solution

At r/R = 0.95, F = 0.522, using Eqs (10.44b) and (10.43). Thus, with Z = 3, l = 1.0,

\(\pi d/s=\frac{1}{2}Z\left(1-r/R\right)\left(1+J^{2}\right)^{0.5}\) (10.44b)

\(F=\left(2/\pi \right) \cos ^{-1}\left[\exp \left(-\pi d/s\right) \right] \) (10.43)

\(F/\lambda =62.32/C_{L}\)

In the BEM method, we start with \(a=a^{\prime }=0 \) so, initially, \(\tan \phi=\left(R/r\right)/J=\left(1/0.95\right)/5=0.2105 \). Thus, \(\phi=11.89° \) and \(C_{L} =\left(\phi -\beta \right)/10=\left(11.89-1.59\right)/10=1.03 \). Hence, \(F/\lambda =60.5\). With Eqs (10.47a) and (10.48a), we compute \(a=0.2759\) and \(a^{\prime } =0.0172\).

\(a/\left(1-a\right)=\lambda \left(\cos \phi+\varepsilon \sin \phi \right)/\left(F\sin^{2}\phi \right) \) (10.47a)

\(a^{\prime }/\left(1+a^{\prime }\right)=\lambda \left(s\sin \phi -\varepsilon \cos \phi \right)/\left(F\sin \phi \cos \phi \right) \) (10.48a)

The next cycle of iteration gives \(\phi=8.522 \) , \(C_{L} = 0.693 \) , F/λ = 89.9, a = 0.3338, and \(a^{\prime }= 0.0114\). Continuing the series of iterations we finally obtain

For the elements of force,

\(\Delta X=\frac{1}{2}\rho ZlRc^{2}_{x1}\left[\left(1-a\right)/\sin \phi \right]^{2}\cos \phi C_{L}\Delta \left(r/R\right) \)

 

where, in Table 10.5, \(Var.1 =\left[\left(1-a\right)/\sin \phi \right]^{2}\cos \phi C_{L}\Delta \left(r/R\right) \) ,

\(\sum{Var.1 } =6.3416\)

As in , \(\left(1/2\right)ZlRc^{2}_{x1}=1518.8 \) , then

\( X=1518.8 \times 6.3416 = 9631 N\)

Evaluating the elements of the torque using Eq. (10.42),

\(\Delta \tau=\frac{1}{2}\rho Zl\Omega^{2}R^{4} \left[\left(1+a^{\prime }\right)/\cos \phi \right]^{2}\left(r/R\right)^{3} C_{L}\sin \phi \Delta \left(r/R\right) \) (10.42)

where, in Table 10.6,\(Var.2 =\left[\left(1+a^{\prime }\right)/\cos \phi \right]^{2}\left(r/R\right)^{3} C_{L}\sin \phi \Delta \left(r/R\right) \)

 

\(\sum{Var.2}=40.707\times 10^{-3} \) and \(\frac{1}{2}\rho Zl\Omega^{2}R^{4}=05695\times 10^{6}\)

 

then

\(\tau =23.183\times 10^{3} Nm\)

Hence, \(P=\tau \Omega =57.962 kW, C_{P}=0.324\) , and \(\zeta=0.547\) .
