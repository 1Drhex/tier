Guest Question.

A slab milling operation similar to that shown in Fig. 7.14 is performed on a medium carbon steel. The width of the steel is 50 mm and a 5 mm layer is to be removed in one pass of the tool. The steel is delivered to the tool at 2 mm/s and the tool is 40 mm in diameter, has 10 teeth and rotates at 150 rpm. Estimate the power required for this operation and the metal removal rate.

Admin
Solution
\( d_{c}=5mm, v_{f}=2mm/s, D=40mm, n_{t}=10, N=150 rpm \)

Use equation 7.10

\( u_{c_{avg_{milling}}}=\frac{v_{f}}{Nn_{t}}\sqrt{\frac{d_{c}}{D} } \)

to calculate the undeformed chip thickness:

\( u_{c}= \frac{v_{f}}{Nn_{t}}\sqrt{\frac{d_{c}}{D} } =\frac{2mm/s}{10 \times 2.5 /s}\sqrt{\frac{5}{40} }=0.028mm \)

Calculate the metal removal rate using equation 7.9: \( MRR_{milling}= v_{f}d_{c}w\)

\( MRR=v_{f}d_{c}w=2mm/s\times 5mm\times 40mm= 400 mm^{3}/s\)

From Fig. 7.20 estimate the specific cutting energy as \( P_{s} = 8.5 GJ/m ^{3}\) , and use Î·= 0.5 from Table 7.1. Then calculate power required from equation 7.12 \( P=\frac{p_{s}\times MRR}{\eta } \)

\( p=\frac{p_{s}MRR}{\eta } =\frac{8.5 GJ/m^{3} \times 400 mm^{3}/s}{0.5} =6.8 kW\)
