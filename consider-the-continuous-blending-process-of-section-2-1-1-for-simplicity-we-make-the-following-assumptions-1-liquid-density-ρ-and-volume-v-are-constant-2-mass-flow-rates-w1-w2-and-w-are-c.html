Guest Question.

Consider the continuous blending process of Section 2.1.1. For simplicity, we make the following assumptions:
1. Liquid density ρ and volume V are constant.
2. Mass flow rates \(w_{1},w_{2}, \ and \ w\) are constant.
Then the component balance in Equation
\( \frac{d(V\rho x)}{dt}=w_{1}x_{1}+w_{2}x_{2}-wx \)
becomes
\(\rho V\frac{dx}{dt}=w_{1}x_{1}+w_{2}x_{2}-wx \)
Derive transfer function models for two cases:
(a) Inlet concentration \(x_{1}\) varies while \(x_{2}\) is constant.
(b) Inlet concentrations \(x_{1} \ and \ x_{2} \) vary.

Admin
Solution

Case (a): Inlet concentration \(x_{1}\) varies while \(x_{2}\) is constant We will derive a transfer function model between exit composition x (the output variable) and inlet composition \(x_{1}\) (the input variable), starting with Equation
\(\rho V\frac{dx}{dt}=w_{1}x_{1}+w_{2}x_{2}-wx\) (1)
The steady-state version of Equation (1) is
\(0=w_{1}\bar{x}_{1}+w_{2}x_{2}-w\bar{x}\) (2)
where the bar over a symbol denotes a nominal steady-state
value. Subtracting Equation (2) from Equation (1) gives
\(\rho V\frac{dx}{dt}=w_{1}x^{\prime}_{1}-wx^{\prime}\) (3)
where the two deviation variables are defined as
\(x^{\prime}_{1}\triangleq x_{1}-\bar{x}_{1}\) (4)
\(x^{\prime}\triangleq x-\bar{x}\)
Because \(\bar{x}\) is a constant, it follows that
\(\frac{dx}{dt}=\frac{d(x-\bar{x})}{dt}=\frac{dx^{\prime}}{dt} \) (5)
Substituting Equation (5) into Equation (3) gives the solute component balance in deviation variable form:
\(\rho V\frac{dx^{\prime}}{dt}=w_{1}x^{\prime}_{1}-wx^{\prime}\) (6)
Assume that the blending system is initially at the nominal steady state. Thus, \(x(0) = \bar{x}\space and\space x^{\prime}(0)=0\). Taking the Laplace transform of Equation (6) gives
\( \rho Vs[X^{\prime}(s)-\cancel{x^{\prime0}(0)}]=w_{1}X^{\prime}_{1}(s)-wX^{\prime}(s)\) (7)
where \( X^{\prime }(s)\triangleq L[x^{\prime }(t)] \) and \(X^{\prime }_{1}(s)\triangleq L[x^{\prime }_{1}(t)]. \) Rearranging gives the transfer function G(s) between the exit and inlet and compositions:

\( \frac{X^{\prime}(s)}{X^{\prime}_{1}(s)}= \frac{w_{1}}{\rho Vs+w} \)
It is useful to place the transfer function in a standard form by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by w:
\( \frac{X^{\prime}(s)}{X^{\prime}_{1}(s)} = G(s) \triangleq \frac{K_{1}}{\tau s+1} \) (*)
where constants \( K_{1} \) and τ are defined as
\( K_{1} ≜ \frac{w_{1}}{w} \)
\( τ≜ \frac{\rho V}{w} \)
Useful physical interpretations of K and τ are provided in Section 5.2.

Case (b): Both inlet concentrations, \( x_{1} \space and \space x_{2}, \) vary
For the case of two input variables, \( x_{1} \space and \space x_{2}, \) two transfer functions are needed to describe their effects on output variable x. Their derivation is analogous to the derivation for Case (a).
For this case, the steady-state version of Equation (1) can be written as
\( 0 = w_{1}\bar{x} _{1}+w_{2}\bar{x}_{2}-w\bar{x} \) (8)
Subtracting Equation (8) from Equation (1) and introducing deviation variables gives
\( \rho V\frac{dx^{\prime }}{dt} = w_{1}x^{\prime }_{1}+w_{2}x^{\prime }_{2}-wx^{\prime } \) (9)
where \( x^{′}_{2} ≜ x_{2}-\bar{x}_{2}. \) Again assuming that the blending system is initially at the nominal steady state, taking the Laplace transform of Equation (9) gives
\( \rho Vs [X^{\prime }(s)-0] = w_{1}X^{\prime }_{1}(s)+w_{2}X^{\prime }_{2}(s)-wX^{\prime }(s) \)
which can be rearranged as
\( X^{\prime }(s)=\frac{K_{1}}{\tau s+1}X^{\prime }_{1}(s)+\frac{K_{2}}{\tau s+1}X^{\prime }_{2}(s) \) (10)
where \( K_{2} \) is defined as
\( K_{2} ≜ \frac{w_{2}}{w} \)
In order to derive the transfer function between x and \( x_{1} \), assume that \( x_{2} \) is constant at its nominal steady-state value, \( x_{2} = \bar{x}_{2}. \) . Therefore, \( x^{\prime }_{2}(t)=0,\space X^{\prime }_{2}(s)=0 \), and Equation (10) reduces to the previous transfer function relating x and \( x_{1} \) (see Equation (*)).

\( \frac{X^{\prime }(s)}{X^{\prime }_{1}(s)} \triangleq G_{1}(s) \triangleq \frac{K_{1}}{\tau s+1} \)
Similarly, the transfer function between x and \( x_{2} \) can be derived from Equation (10) and the assumption that \( x_{1} \) is constant at its nominal steady-state value, \( x_{1} = \bar{x}_{1}: \)
\( \frac{X^{\prime }(s)}{X^{\prime }_{2}(s)}\triangleq G_{2}(s) \triangleq \frac{K_{2}}{\tau s+1} \) .
