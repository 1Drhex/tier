Guest Question.

A centrifugal pump delivers 50 kg of water per second. The inlet and outlet pressures are 1 bar and 4.2 bar respectively. The suction is 2.2 m below the centre of the pump and delivery is 8.5 m above the centre of the pump. The suction and delivery pipe diameters are 20 cm and 10 cm respectively.
Determine the capacity of the electric motor to run the pump.

Admin
Solution

Refer to Fig. 2.51.

 

Quantity of water delivered by the pump, \( m_{w}\) = 50 kg/s

 

Inlet pressure, \(p_{1} = 1 bar = 1 × 10^{5} N/m^{2}\)

 

Outlet pressure, \(p_{2} = 4.2 bar = 4.2 × 10^{5} N/m^{2}\)

 

Suction-below the centre of the pump = 2.2 m

 

Delivery-above the centre of the pump = 8.5 m

 

Diameter of suction pipe, \(d_{1} \)= 20 cm = 0.2 m

 

Diameter of delivery pipe, \(d_{2}\) = 10 cm = 0.1 m

 

Capacity of electric motor :

 

Steady flow energy equation is given by

 

\(m_{w}\left ( u_{1} +p_{1}v_{1}+\frac{C_{1}^{2}}{2}+Z_{1}g\right )+Q=m_{w}\left ( u_{2}+p_{2}v_{2} +\frac{C_{2}^{2}}{2}+Z_{2}g\right )+W \) …(i)

 

Considering the datum from suction 1, as shown

 

\(Z_{1} = 0, Z_{2}= 8.5 + 2.2 = 10.7 \) m

 

\(u_{2} – u_{1} = 0 \) ; Q = 0

 

Thus eqn. (i) reduces to

 

\(W=m_{w}\left [ \left ( p_{1}v_{1}-p_{2} v_{2}\right )+\left ( Z_{1}-Z_{2}g \right )+\left ( \frac{C_{1}^{2}-C_{2}^{2}}{2} \right ) \right ] \) …(ii)

 

As water is incompressible fluid.

 

\(\therefore\) \( v_{2}=v_{1}=v=\frac{1}{\rho }=\frac{1}{1000}\)

 

The mass flow through inlet and exit pipe is given by

 

\(m_{w}=\frac{\pi }{4}\times d_{1}^{2}\times C_{1}\times \rho =\frac{\pi }{4}\times d_{2}^{2}\times C_{2}\times \rho as \rho _{1}=\rho _{2}=\rho\) (for water)

 

\(\therefore\) \( 50= \frac{\pi }{4}\times \left ( 0.4 \right )^{2}\times C_{1}\times 1000\)

 

\(\therefore\) \( C_{1}=\frac{50\times 4}{\pi \times \left ( 0.2 \right )^{2}\times 1000}=1.59\) m/s

 

and \( C_{2}=\frac{50\times 4}{\pi \times \left ( 0.1 \right )^{2}\times 1000}= 6.37 \) m/s

 

Substituting the values in eqn. (ii) we get

 

\(W=50\left [ \left ( 1\times 10^{5}\times \frac{1}{1000}-4.2\times 10^{5}\times \frac{1}{1000} \right )+\left ( 0-10.7 \right )\times 9.81+\left ( \frac{1.59^{2}-6.37^{2}}{2} \right ) \right ]\)

 

\(= 50[– 320 – 104.96 – 19.02]\)

 

= 22199 J/s or 22.199 kJ/s \(\approx \) 22.2 kW.

 

Hence capacity of electric motor = 22.2 kW.
