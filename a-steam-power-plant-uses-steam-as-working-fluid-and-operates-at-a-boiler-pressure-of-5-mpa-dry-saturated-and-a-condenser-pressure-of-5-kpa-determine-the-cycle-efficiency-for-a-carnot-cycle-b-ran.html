Guest Question.

A steam power plant uses steam as working fluid and operates at a boiler pressure of 5 MPa, dry saturated and a condenser pressure of 5 kPa. Determine the cycle efficiency for (a) Carnot cycle (b) Rankine cycle. Also show the T-s representation for both the cycles.

Admin
Solution

From :\(\\\begin{aligned}\text{At 5 MPa}\qquad\qquad \qquad h_{\text{ƒ, 5MPa}}&= 1154.23 kJ/kg, s_{\text{ƒ, 5 MPa}} = 2.92 kJ/kg · K\\h_{\text{g, 5MPa}}&= 2794.3 kJ/kg, s_{\text{g, 5 MPa }}= 5.97 kJ/kg · K\\\text{At 5 kPa} \quad\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad &\\h_{\text{ƒ, 5kPa}}&= 137.82 kJ/kg, s_{\text{ƒ, 5kPa}} = 0.4764 kJ/kg · K\\h_{\text{g, 5kPa}}&= 2561.5 kJ/kg, s_{\text{g, 5kPa}} = 8.3951 kJ/kg · K\\v_{\text{ƒ, 5kPa}}&= 0.001005 m^{3}/kg\\\text{As process 2-3 is isentropic, so }s_{2}& = s_{3}\\\text{and} \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad &\\s_{3}&= s_{\text{ƒ, 5kPa}} + x_{3} · s_{\text{ƒg, 5kPa}} = s_{2} = s_{\text{g, 5MPa}}\\x_{3}&= 0.694\\\text{Hence enthalpy at 3,}\quad \qquad \qquad &=\\h_{3}&= h_{\text{ƒ, 5kPa}} + x_{3} · h_{\text{ƒg, 5kPa}}\\h_{3}&= 1819.85 kJ/kg\\\text{Enthalpy at 2,}\qquad\qquad \quad \qquad h_{2}&= h_{\text{g, 5MPa}} = 2794.3 kJ/kg\\\text{Process 1-4 is isentropic, so } \quad s_{1}& = s_{4} \\s_{1}&= 2.92 = 0.4764 + x_{4} · (8.3951 – 0.4764)\\x_{4}&= 0.308\\\text{Enthalpy at 4,}\qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad &\\h_{4}&=137.82 + (0.308 × (2561.5 – 137.82))\\h_{4}&= 884.3 kJ/kg\\\text{Enthalpy at 1,}\qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad &\\h_{1}&= h_{\text{ƒ at 5 MPa}}\\h_{1}&= 1154.23 kJ/kg\\\text{Carnot cycle (1-2-3-4-1) efficiency:}&\\η_{carnot}&= \frac{\text{Net work}}{\text{Heat added}}\\&= \frac{(h_{2} – h_{3}) – (h_{1} – h_{4})}{(h_{2} – h_{1})}\\&= \frac{\left\{(2794.3 – 1819.85) – (1154.23 – 884.3)\right\} }{(2794.3 – 1154.23)}\\η_{carnot}&= 0.4295\\\text{or}\quad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad &\\η_{carnot}&= \textbf{42.95 }\pmb{\%}\\\text{In Rankine cycle, 1-2-3-5-6-1} \quad&\\\text{Pump work, }h_{6} – h_{5}&= v_{ƒ, 5}(p_{6} – p_{5})\\&= 0.001005 (5000 – 5)\\h_{6} – h_{5}&= 5.02\\h_{5}&= h_{\text{ƒ at 5kPa}} = 137.82 kJ/kg\\\text{Hence }h_{6}&= 137.82 + 5.02 = 142.84 kJ/kg\\h_{6}&= 142.84 kJ/kg\\\text{Net work in Rankine cycle}&= (h_{2} – h_{3}) – (h_{6} – h_{5})\\&= 974.45 – 5.02\\&= 969.43 kJ/kg\\\text{Heat added}&= h_{2} – h_{6}\\&= 2794.3 – 142.84\\&= 2651.46 kJ/kg\\\text{Rankine cycle efficiency}&= \frac{969.43}{2651.46}\\η_{Rankine}&= 0.3656\\\text{or}\quad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad &\\η_{Rankine}&= \textbf{36.56 }\pmb{\%}\end{aligned}\)
