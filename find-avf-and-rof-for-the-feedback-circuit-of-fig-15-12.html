Guest Question.

Find Avf and Rof for the feedback circuit of Fig. 15.12.

Admin
Solution

Suppose that in the circuit of Fig. 15.12 we define the output terminals to be S and N, so that Vo = Vsn, and the input terminals to be G and S, so that Vi = Vgs. The external signal generator is connected to G and N, so that Vs = Vgn. The circuit may now be redrawn as in Fig. 15.13a, which corresponds to Fig. 15.11, with Rs = 0. Independently of whether the resistor Rs is considered a part of the amplifier or an external load, we have a case of voltage feedback in which β = +1, since Vf = Vo. Let us consider the Rs is an external load, and not a part of the amplifier. Then Vo = μVi. Now we write

\(A_{v}=\frac{V_{o}}{V_{i}}=\frac{V_{sn}}{V_{gs}}=\mu \)

and 1 + βAv = 1 + μ . The impedance without feedback seen looking to the left between terminals S and N is rd + Rd. The voltage gain and output impedance with feedback are found from Eqs (15.12) , \(A_{f}\equiv A_{vsf}=\frac{A_{vs}}{1+A_{vs}\beta } \) , and (15.13) , \(R_{of}=\frac{R_{o}}{1+A_{vs}\beta } \) , to be

\(A_{vf}= \frac{\mu }{\mu +1} \)

and

\(R_{of}=\frac{r_{d}+R_{d}}{\mu +1} \)

The equivalent circuit is as indicated in Fig. 15.13b and is the circuit “looking into the source” .
