Finding the Currents of a Step-up Dc Converter

The step-up converter in Figure 5.9a has \(V_s = 10 V, f = 1 kHz, R = 5 Ω\),\( L = 6.5 mH, E = 0 V\), and \(k = 0.5\). Find \(I_1, I_2\) and \(ΔI\). Use SPICE to find these values and plot the load, diode, and switch current.

Admin
Solution

Equations (5.43) and (5.44)

\(I_1=\frac{V_skz}{R}\frac{e^{-(1-k)z}}{1 -e^{-(1-k)z}}+\frac{V_s-E}{R}\) (5.43)

 

\(I_2=\frac{V_skz}{R}\frac{1}{1 -e^{-(1-k)z}}+\frac{V_s-E}{R}\) (5.44)

give \(I_1 = 3.64 A\) (3.36 A from SPICE) and \(I_2 = 4.4 A\) (4.15 A from SPICE). The plots of the load current I(L), the diode current \(I(D_m)\), and the switch current \(IC(Q_1)\) are shown in Figure 5.10.
