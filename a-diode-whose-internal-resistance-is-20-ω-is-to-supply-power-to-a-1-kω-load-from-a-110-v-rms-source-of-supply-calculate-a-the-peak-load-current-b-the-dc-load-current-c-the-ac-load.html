Guest Question.

A diode whose internal resistance is 20 Ω is to supply power to a 1 kΩ load from a 110 V (rms) source of supply. Calculate (a) the peak load current, (b) the dc load current, (c) the ac load current, (d ) the dc diode voltage, (e) the total input power to the circuit, ( f ) the peak inverse voltage, and (g) the percentage regulation from no load to the given load

Admin
Solution

The given problem is related to a half-wave rectifier which is shown in Fig. 6.1. In this case, \(v_{i}\) represents the ac source with rms voltage of 110 V, \(R_{f}\) = 20 Ω and \(R_{L}\) = 1 kΩ. Thus the sinusoidal input \(v_{i}\) in the Fig. 6.1b has maximum amplitude

\(V_{m}=\sqrt{2}\times 110 =155.56 \) V

(a) The peak load current \(I_{m}\) can be obtained from Eq. (6.2) ,\(\alpha \equiv \omega t \) \(I_{m}\equiv \frac{V_{m}}{R_{f}+R_{L}} \) :

\(I_{m}=\frac{155.56}{1020}=0.1525 A = 152.2\) mA

(b) The dc load current \(I_{dc}\) can be obtained by using Eq. (6.4) , \(I_{dc}=\frac{1}{2\pi }\int_{0}^{\pi }{I_{m} \sin \alpha d\alpha }=\frac{I_{m}}{\pi } \) , as

\(I_{dc}=\frac{152.5}{\pi }=48.54 \) mA

(c) The ac load current \(I_{rms}\) is determined from (6.6) , \(I_{rms}=\left\lgroup \frac{1}{2\pi }\int_{0}^{\pi }{I^{2}_{m}\sin ^{2} \alpha d\alpha } \right\rgroup ^{\frac{1}{2} } =\frac{I_{m}}{2} \) . as

\(I_{rms}=\frac{152.5}{2 }=76.25 \) mA

(d) The dc voltage across the diode can be calculated using Eq. (6.8) as

\(V_{dc}=-\frac{I_{m}R_{L}}{\pi }=-I_{dc}R_{L}=-(48.54\times 10^{-3} A)\times (1000 \Omega )=-48.54 \) V

Note that the negative sign indicates that the positive terminal of a dc voltmeter is to be connected to the cathode of the diode to read upscale of the meter.

(e) Substituting the result of Part (c) for \(I_{rms}\) = 76.25 mA in Eq. (6.10), \(P_{i}=I^{2}_{rms}(R_{f}+R_{L}) \) , the total input power \(P_{i}\) to the circuit can be obtained as

\(P_{i}=(76.25\times 10^{-3} A)^{2}\times (1020 \Omega )=5.33\) Watts

( f ) The peak inverse voltage is the maximum voltage that appears across the diode when the diode is not conducting. For a half-wave rectifier circuit, it is simply the maximum value \(V_{m}\) = 155.56 V of \(v_{i}\) which is the peak value of the transformer secondary voltage.

(g) The output dc voltage \(V_{no load}\) load and \(V_{full load}\) load of Eq. (6.11) , % regulation\(\equiv \frac{V_{no load}-V_{full load}}{V_{full load}}\times 100 \)% , at no load and given load can be determined by substituting the values of \(I_{dc}\) = 0 and \(I_{dc}\) = 48.54 mA for \(I_{dc}\) in Eq. (6.13) , \(V_{dc}=\frac{V_{m}}{\pi }-I_{dc}R_{f} \) , respectively. Thus we can get

\(V_{no load}=\frac{V_{m}}{\pi }=\frac{155.26}{\pi } =49.51 \) V

\(V_{full load}= 49.51 V – (48.54\times 10^{-3}A)\times (20\Omega )\)

\(= (49.51 -0.97 )\) V

\(=48.54\)

Using Eq. (6.11), the percentage regulation from no load to the given load is obtained as

% regulation \(=\frac{49.51-48.54}{48.54}\times 100 =\frac{97}{48.54}=1.94 \) %
