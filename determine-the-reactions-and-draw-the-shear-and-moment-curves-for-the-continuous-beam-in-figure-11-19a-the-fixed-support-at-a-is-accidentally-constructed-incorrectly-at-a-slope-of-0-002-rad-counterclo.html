Guest Question.

Determine the reactions and draw the shear and moment curves for the continuous beam in Figure 11.19a. The fixed support at A is accidentally constructed incorrectly at a slope of 0.002 rad counterclockwise from a vertical axis through A, and the support at C is accidentally constructed 1.5 in. below its intended position. Given: \( E=29000\frac{kips}{in.^{2}} \) and \( I=300 in.^{4}\) .

Admin
Solution

With the supports located in their as-built position (Figure 11.19b), the beam is connected to the supports. Since the unloaded beam is straight but the supports are no longer in a straight line and correctly aligned, external forces must be applied to the beam to bring it into contact with its supports. After the beam is connected to its supports, reactions must develop to hold the beam in its bent configuration. Also at both joints B and C we imagine that clamps are applied at these joints to hold the ends of the beam in a horizontal position; that is, \( \Theta _{B} \) and \( \Theta _{C} \) are zero. We now use the slopedeflection equation to compute the moments at each end of the restrained beams in Figure 11.19b.

 

\( M_{NF}=\frac{2EI}{L}\left ( 2\Theta _{N}+\Theta _{F}-3\psi \right )+FEM_{NF} \) (10.16)

 

Compute moments in span AB:\( \Theta _{A} = −0.002 \) rad,\( \Theta _{B} = 0\) , and \( \psi _{AB}=0\) .Since no transverse loads are applied to span AB, \( FEM_{AB}=FEM_{BA}=0\) .

 

\( M_{AB}=\frac{2\left ( 29000 \right )\left ( 300 \right )}{20\left ( 12 \right )}\left [ 2\left ( -0.002 \right ) \right ]=-290 \) kip.in. \( =-24.2 \) kip.ft

 

\( M_{BA}=\frac{2\left ( 29000 \right )\left ( 300 \right )}{20\left ( 12 \right )}\left ( -0.002 \right )=-145kip.in.=-12.1 \) kip.ft

 

Compute moments in span BC: \( \Theta _{B}=0\) , \( \Theta _{C}=0\) , \( \psi =1.5 \) in. \( \left [ 25\left ( 12 \right ) \right ]=0.005\) . \( FEM_{BC}=FEM_{CB}=0\) since no transverse loads applied to span BC.

 

\( FEM_{BC}=FEM_{CB}=\frac{2\left ( 29000 \right )\left ( 300 \right )}{12\left ( 25 \right )}\left [ 2\left ( 0 \right )+0-3\left ( 0.005 \right ) \right ]\)

 

\( =-870 \) kip.in. \( =-72.5 \) kip.ft

 

Compute the distribution factors at joint B.

 

\( K_{AB}=\frac{300}{20}=15\) \( K_{BC}=\frac{3}{4}\left ( \frac{300}{25} \right )=9 \) \( \sum K =24\)

 

\( DF_{BA}=\frac{K_{AB}}{\sum K}=\frac{15}{24}=0.625\) \( DF_{BC}=\frac{K_{BC}}{\sum K}=\frac{9}{24}=0.375\)

 

The moment distribution is carried out in Figure 11.20a, shears and reactions are computed in Figure 11.20b, and the moment curve is shown in Figure 11.20c.
