Guest Question.

The decomposition of \(H_{2}O_{2}\) is conducted with an enzyme E and the products \(H_{2}O\) and \(O_{2}\) are obtained, according to the following mechanism:
\(H_{2}O_{2}+E\overset{k_{1}}{\rightarrow}\left [ H_{2}O_{2}E^{*} \right ]\)
\(\left [ H_{2}O_{2}E^{*} \right ]\overset{k_{2}}{\rightarrow}H_{2}O_{2}+E\)
\(\left [ H_{2}O_{2}E^{*} \right ]+H_{2}O\overset{k_{3}}{\rightarrow}H_{2}O+O_{2}E\)

The experimental results that were obtained are displayed as follows:

100 50 20 10 0 t(min)
0.005 0.0106 0.0158 0.0177 0.02 \([H_{2}O_{2}]\) (mol/L)

(a) Calculate the Michaelis constant and the maximum rate.
(b) If the concentration of enzyme is triplicated, what is the time necessary to achieve a conversion of 95%?

Admin
Solution

With this mechanism, we obtain:
\(\left ( -r_{S} \right )=\frac{V_{max}\left [ H_{2}O_{2} \right ]}{\left [ H_{2}O_{2} \right ]+K_{M}}\)

Whose solution in a batch reactor:
\(-\frac{1}{t}\ln \left ( 1-X_{S} \right )=\frac{V_{max}}{K_{M}}-\frac{\left [ H_{2}O_{2} \right ]}{K_{M}}\frac{X_{S}}{t}\)

Considering that:
\(X_{S}=\frac{\left [ H_{2}O_{2} \right ]_{0}-\left [ H_{2}O_{2} \right ]}{\left [ H_{2}O_{2} \right ]_{0}}\)

where \(X_{S}\) is the conversion
Since \(\left [ H_{2}O_{2} \right ]_{0} =0.02\), we obtain:

The angular coefficient is –0.56. Therefore, since \(\left [ H_{2}O_{2} \right ]_{0} =0.02\), we have:
\(K_{M} =0.0354\)

The linear coefficient is equal to 0.018, therefore:
\(V_{max} =6.37 × 10^{-4} (mol/L) × min ^{-1}\)

If the enzymes are triplicated, we have:
\(V_{máx} =k_{3}E_{T} =3 × 6.37 × 10^{-4} =1.911 × 10^{-3} (mol/L min)\)

Thus, the time necessary to reach a conversion \((X_{S})\) of 0.95 will be:
t =15 min
