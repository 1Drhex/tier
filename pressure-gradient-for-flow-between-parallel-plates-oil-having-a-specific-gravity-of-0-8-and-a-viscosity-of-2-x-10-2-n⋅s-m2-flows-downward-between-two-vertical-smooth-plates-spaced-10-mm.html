PRESSURE GRADIENT FOR FLOW BETWEEN PARALLEL PLATES

Oil having a specific gravity of 0.8 and a viscosity of \(2 \times 10^{-2} N \cdot s / m ^{2}\) flows downward between two vertical smooth plates spaced 10 mm apart. If the discharge per meter of width is 0.01 \(m ^{2} / s\), what is the pressure gradient dp/ds for this flow?

Problem Definition

Situation: Oil flows downward between two vertical smooth plates spaced 10 mm apart. The discharge per meter of width is 0.01 \(\left( m ^{2} / s \right)\).

Find: Pressure gradient dp/ds (in Pa/m) for this flow.

Sketch:

Properties: \(S =0.8, \mu=2 \times 10^{-2} N \cdot s / m ^{2}\).

Plan

1. Check to see if the flow is laminar \(V B / \nu<1000\). If it is laminar, continue.

2. Calculate piezometric head gradient using Eq. (9.8).

\(q=\int_{0}^{B} u d y=-\left(\frac{B^{3}}{12 \mu}\right) \frac{d}{d s}(p+\gamma z)=-\left(\frac{B^{3} \gamma}{12 \mu}\right) \frac{d h}{d s}\) (9.8)

3. Subtract elevation gradient to obtain the pressure gradient.

Admin
Solution

1. Laminar flow condition is

 

\(Re =\frac{V B}{\nu}=\frac{V B \rho}{\mu}=\frac{q \rho}{\mu}\)

 

\(=\frac{\left(0.01 m ^{2} / s \right) \times 800 kg / m ^{3}}{0.02 N \cdot s / m ^{2}}=400\)

 

\(V B / v<1000\). Flow is laminar, equations apply.

 

2. Kinematic viscosity:

 

\(v=\mu / \rho=\frac{2 \times 10^{-2} N \cdot s / m ^{2}}{0.8 \times 1000 kg / m ^{3}}=2.5 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2} / s\)

 

Piezometric head gradient is

 

\(\frac{d h}{d s}=-\frac{12 \mu}{B^{3} \gamma} q=-\frac{12 v}{B^{3} g} q\)

 

\(\frac{d h}{d s}=-\frac{12 \times 2.5 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2} / s }{(0.01 m )^{3} \times 9.81 m ^{4} / s ^{2}} \times 0.01 m ^{2} / s =-0.306\)

 

3. Plates are oriented vertically, s is positive downward, so dz/ds = -1. Thus

 

\(\frac{d h}{d s}=\frac{d}{d s}\left(\frac{p}{\gamma}\right)+\frac{d z}{d s}\)

 

\(\frac{d}{d s}\left(\frac{p}{\gamma}\right)=\frac{d h}{d s}-\frac{d z}{d s}=-0.306+1=0.694\)

 

or

 

\(\frac{d p}{d s}=\left(0.8 \times 9810 N / m ^{3}\right) \times 0.694=5450 N / m ^{2}\) per meter

 

Review

Note that the pressure increases in the downward direction, which means that the pressure, in part, supports the weight of the fluid.
