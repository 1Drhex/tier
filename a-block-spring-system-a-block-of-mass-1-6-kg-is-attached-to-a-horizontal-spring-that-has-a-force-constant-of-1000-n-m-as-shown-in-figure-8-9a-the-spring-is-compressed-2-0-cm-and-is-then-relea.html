A Block–Spring System

A block of mass 1.6 kg is attached to a horizontal spring that has a force constant of 1000 N/m as shown in Figure 8.9a. The spring is compressed 2.0 cm and is then released from rest as in Figure 8.9b.

(A) Calculate the speed of the block as it passes through the equilibrium position x = 0 if the surface is frictionless.

(B) Calculate the speed of the block as it passes through the equilibrium position if a constant friction force of 4.0 N retards its motion from the moment it is released.

Admin
Solution

Conceptualize This situation has been discussed before, and it is easy to visualize the block being pushed to the right by the spring and moving with some speed at x = 0.

Categorize We identify the system as the block and model the block as a nonisolated system.

Analyze In this situation, the block starts with \(v_{i}=0\) at \(x_{i}=-2.0 \mathrm{~cm}\), and we want to find \(v_{f}\) at \(x_{f}=0\).

Use Equation \(7.11\) to find the work done by the spring on the system with \(x_{\max }=x_{i}\) :

\(W_{s}=\int \overrightarrow{F}_{s} \cdot d \overrightarrow{r}=\int_{x_{i}}^{x_{f}}(-k x \hat{i}) \cdot(d x \hat{i})=\int_{-x_{\max }}^{0}(-k x) d x=\frac{1}{2} k x_{\max }^{2}\) (7.11)

\(W_{s}=\frac{1}{2} k x_{\max }^{2}\)

Work is done on the block, and its speed changes. The conservation of energy equation, Equation \(8.2\), reduces to the work-kinetic energy theorem. Use that theorem to find the speed at \(x=0\) :

\(\Delta K+\Delta U+\Delta E_{\mathrm{int}}=W+Q+T_{\mathrm{MW}}+T_{\mathrm{MT}}+T_{\mathrm{ET}}+T_{\mathrm{ER}}\) (8.2)

\(W_{s}=\frac{1}{2} m {v_{f}}^{2}-\frac{1}{2} m{ v_{i}}^{2}\)

\(v_{f}=\sqrt{v_{i}^{2}+\frac{2}{m} W_{s}}=\sqrt{v_{i}^{2}+\frac{2}{m}\left(\frac{1}{2} k x_{\max }^{2}\right)}\)

Substitute numerical values:

\(v_{f}=\sqrt{0+\frac{2}{1.6 \mathrm{~kg}}\left[\frac{1}{2}(1000 \mathrm{~N} / \mathrm{m})(0.020 \mathrm{~m})^{2}\right]}=0.50 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\)

Finalize Although this problem could have been solved in Chapter 7, it is presented here to provide contrast with the following part (B), which requires the techniques of this chapter.

Conceptualize The correct answer must be less than that found in part (A) because the friction force retards the motion.

Categorize We identify the system as the block and the surface, a nonisolated system because of the work done by the spring. There is a nonconservative force acting within the system: the friction between the block and the surface.

Analyze Write Equation 8.15:

\(\sum W_{\text {other forces }}=W=\Delta K+\Delta E_{\text {int }}\) (8.15)

\(W_{s}=\Delta K+\Delta E_{\mathrm{int}}=\left(\frac{1}{2} m v_{f}^{2}-0\right)+f_{k} d\)

Solve for \(v_{f}\) :

\(v_{f}=\sqrt{\frac{2}{m}\left(W_{s}-f_{k} d\right)}\)

Substitute for the work done by the spring:

\(v_{f}=\sqrt{\frac{2}{m}\left(\frac{1}{2} k x_{\max }^{2}-f_{k} d\right)}\)

Substitute numerical values:

\(v_{f}=\sqrt{\frac{2}{1.6 \mathrm{~kg}}\left[\frac{1}{2}(1000 \mathrm{~N} / \mathrm{m})(0.020 \mathrm{~m})^{2}-(4.0 \mathrm{~N})(0.020 \mathrm{~m})\right]}=0.39 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\)

Finalize As expected, this value is less than the 0.50 m/s found in part (A).
