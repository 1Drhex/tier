Guest Question.

and \( R \equiv\left(m_{1} r _{1}+m_{2} r _{2}\right) /\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right) \)(the center of mass).

(a) Show that \( r _{1}= R +\left(\mu / m_{1}\right) r , \quad r _{2}= R -\left(\mu / m_{2}\right) r \) , and \( \nabla_{1}=\left(\mu / m_{2}\right) \nabla_{R}+\nabla_{r}, \nabla_{2}=\left(\mu / m_{1}\right) \nabla_{R}-\nabla_{r} \), where

\( \mu \equiv \frac{m_{1} m_{2}}{m_{1}+m_{2}} \) (5.15).

is the reduced mass of the system

(b) Show that the (time-independent) Schrödinger equation (5.7) becomes

\( -\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m_{1}} \nabla_{1}^{2} \psi-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m_{2}} \nabla_{2}^{2} \psi+V \psi=E \psi \) (5.7).

\( -\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right)} \nabla_{R}^{2} \psi-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 \mu} \nabla_{r}^{2} \psi+V( r ) \psi=E \psi \).

(c) Separate the variables, letting \( \psi( R , r )=\psi_{R}( R ) \psi_{r}( r ) \). Note that \( ψ_R \) satisfies the one-particle Schrödinger equation, with the total mass \( (m_1 + m_2) \) in place of m, potential zero, and energy \( E_R \) while \( ψ_r \) satisfies the one-particle Schrödinger equation with the reduced mass in place of m, potential V(r) , and energy \( E_r \) . The total energy is the sum: \( E=E_{R}+E_{r} \) . What this tells us is that the center of mass moves like a free particle, and the relative motion (that is, the motion of particle 2 with respect to particle 1) is the same as if we had a single particle with the reduced mass, subject to the potential V. Exactly the same decomposition occurs in classical mechanics; it reduces the two-body problem to an equivalent one-body problem.

Solution

(a)

\( \left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right) R =m_{1} r _{1}+m_{2} r _{2}=m_{1} r _{1}+m_{2}\left( r _{1}- r \right)=\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right) r _{1}-m_{2} r \quad \Rightarrow \).

\( r _{1}= R +\frac{m_{2}}{m_{1}+m_{2}} r = R +\frac{\mu}{m_{1}} r \).

\( \left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right) R =m_{1}\left( r _{2}+ r \right)+m_{2} r _{2}=\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right) r _{2}+m_{1} r \Rightarrow r _{2}= R -\frac{m_{1}}{m_{1}+m_{2}} r = R -\frac{\mu}{m_{2}} r \).

Let R = (X, Y,Z); r = (x,y, z).

\( \left(\nabla_{1}\right)_{x}=\frac{\partial}{\partial x_{1}}=\frac{\partial X}{\partial x_{1}} \frac{\partial}{\partial X}+\frac{\partial x}{\partial x_{1}} \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \).

\( =\left(\frac{m_{1}}{m_{1}+m_{2}}\right) \frac{\partial}{\partial X}+(1) \frac{\partial}{\partial x}=\frac{\mu}{m_{2}}\left(\nabla_{R}\right)_{x}+\left(\nabla_{r}\right)_{x}, \quad \text { so } \quad \nabla_{1}=\frac{\mu}{m_{2}} \nabla_{R}+\nabla_{r} \).

\( \left(\nabla_{2}\right)_{x}=\frac{\partial}{\partial x_{2}}=\frac{\partial X}{\partial x_{2}} \frac{\partial}{\partial X}+\frac{\partial x}{\partial x_{2}} \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \).

\( =\left(\frac{m_{2}}{m_{1}+m_{2}}\right) \frac{\partial}{\partial X}-(1) \frac{\partial}{\partial x}=\frac{\mu}{m_{1}}\left(\nabla_{R}\right)_{x}-\left(\nabla_{r}\right)_{x}, \quad \text { so } \quad \nabla_{2}=\frac{\mu}{m_{1}} \nabla_{R}-\nabla_{r} \).

(b)

\( \nabla_{1}^{2} \psi=\nabla_{1} \cdot\left(\nabla_{1} \psi\right)=\nabla_{1} \cdot\left[\frac{\mu}{m_{2}} \nabla_{R} \psi+\nabla_{r} \psi\right] \).

\( =\frac{\mu}{m_{2}} \nabla_{R} \cdot\left(\frac{\mu}{m_{2}} \nabla_{R} \psi+\nabla_{r} \psi\right)+\nabla_{r} \cdot\left(\frac{\mu}{m_{2}} \nabla_{R} \psi+\nabla_{r} \psi\right) \).

\( =\left(\frac{\mu}{m_{2}}\right)^{2} \nabla_{R}^{2} \psi+2 \frac{\mu}{m_{2}}\left(\nabla_{r} \cdot \nabla_{R}\right) \psi+\nabla_{r}^{2} \psi \).

Likewise, \( \nabla_{2}^{2} \psi=\left(\frac{\mu}{m_{1}}\right)^{2} \nabla_{R}^{2} \psi-2 \frac{\mu}{m_{1}}\left(\nabla_{r} \cdot \nabla_{R}\right)+\nabla_{r}^{2} \psi \).

\( \therefore H \psi=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m_{1}} \nabla_{1}^{2} \psi-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m_{2}} \nabla_{2}^{2} \psi+V\left( r _{1}, r _{2}\right) \psi \).

\( =-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2}\left(\frac{\mu^{2}}{m_{1} m_{2}^{2}} \nabla_{R}^{2}+\frac{2 \mu}{m_{1} m_{2}} \nabla_{r} \cdot \nabla_{R}+\frac{1}{m_{1}} \nabla_{r}^{2}+\frac{\mu^{2}}{m_{2} m_{1}^{2}} \nabla_{R}^{2}-\frac{2 \mu}{m_{2} m_{1}} \nabla_{r} \cdot \nabla_{R}+\frac{1}{m_{2}} \nabla_{r}^{2}\right) \psi \)

\( +V( r ) \psi=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2}\left[\frac{\mu^{2}}{m_{1} m_{2}}\left(\frac{1}{m_{2}}+\frac{1}{m_{1}}\right) \nabla_{R}^{2}+\left(\frac{1}{m_{1}}+\frac{1}{m_{2}}\right) \nabla_{r}^{2}\right] \psi+V( r ) \psi=E \psi \).

\( \text { But }\left(\frac{1}{m_{1}}+\frac{1}{m_{2}}\right)=\frac{m_{1}+m_{2}}{m_{1} m_{2}}=\frac{1}{\mu}, \text { so } \frac{\mu^{2}}{m_{1} m_{2}}\left(\frac{1}{m_{2}}+\frac{1}{m_{1}}\right)=\frac{\mu}{m_{1} m_{2}}=\frac{m_{1} m_{2}}{m_{1} m_{2}\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right)}=\frac{1}{m_{1}+m_{2}} \).

\( -\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right)} \nabla_{R}^{2} \psi-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 \mu} \nabla_{r}^{2} \psi+V( r ) \psi=E \psi \).

(c) Put in \( \psi=\psi_{r}( r ) \psi_{R}( R ), \text { and divide by } \psi_{r} \psi_{R} \):

\( \left[-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right)} \frac{1}{\psi_{R}} \nabla_{R}^{2} \psi_{R}\right]+\left[-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 \mu} \frac{1}{\psi_{r}} \nabla_{r}^{2} \psi_{r}+V( r )\right]=E \).

The first term depends only on R, the second only on r, so each must be a constant; call them \( E_R \) and \( E_r \), respectively. Then:

\( -\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right)} \nabla^{2} \psi_{R}=E_{R} \psi_{R} \); \( -\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 \mu} \nabla^{2} \psi_{r}+V( r ) \psi_{r}=E_{r} \psi_{r} \), with \( E_{R}+E_{r}=E \).
