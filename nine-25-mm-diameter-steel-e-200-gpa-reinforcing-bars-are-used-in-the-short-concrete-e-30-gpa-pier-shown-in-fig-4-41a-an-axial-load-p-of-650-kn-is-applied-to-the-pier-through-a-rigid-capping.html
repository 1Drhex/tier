Guest Question.

Nine 25-mm-diameter steel (E = 200 GPa) reinforcing bars are used in the short concrete (E = 30 GPa) pier shown in Fig. 4-41a. An axial load P of 650 kN is applied to the pier through a rigid capping plate. Determine
(a) The normal stresses in the concrete and in the steel bars.
(b) The shortening of the pier.

Admin
Solution

(a) A free-body diagram of the rigid capping plate is shown in Fig. 4-41b. The free-body diagram contains two unknown forces, the resultant force \(P_C\) exerted by the concrete and the resultant force \(P_R\) exerted by the rods. Both forces are drawn as pushing on the rigid capping plate. The capping plate will exert equal pushing forces back on the concrete pier and the steel reinforcing bars. Thus, the forces \(P_C\) and \(P_R\) are both compressive forces and they will lead to shortening of both the pier and the rods. Since only one equation of equilibrium is available,

\(+\uparrow \sum{F_y}=0:\quad\quad\quad P_R+P_C-P=0 \quad\quad\quad P_R+P_C=650kN\) (a)

the problem is statically indeterminate. The additional equation needed to obtain a solution to the problem is obtained from the deformation diagram shown in Fig. 4-41c.

As the load P is applied to the rigid capping plate, it moves downward an amount δ, which is the same as the shrink experienced by the steel rods \( δ_R\) and by the concrete \( δ_C\). The relationship between the loads and the deformations for axial loading is given by \(\delta =\frac{PL}{EA} \), \(δ_R = PL/EA)_R\) and \(δ_C=(PL/EA)_C\). (Note that the forces and deformations are compatible; that is, \(P_R\) and \(P_C\) are both pushing forces on the free-body diagram and in the equilibrium equation, and \(δ_R\) and \(δ_C\) are both shrinks on the deformation diagram and in the deformation equations.)
The total cross-sectional area \(A_R\) for the nine steel rods is

\(A_R=9[\frac{\pi}{4}(25^2) ]=4418mm^2\) (b)

The cross-sectional area \(A_C\) for the concrete is

\(A_C=(250)^2-4418=58,080mm^2\) (c)

Therefore, compatibility of the deformations \(δ_R =δ_C =δ\) requires that

\(\frac{P_R(0.600)}{(200)(10^9)(4418)(10^{-6})}=\frac{P_C(0.600)}{(30)(10^9)(58,080)(10^{-6})} \) (d)

from which

\(P_R=0.5071P_C\) (e)

Solving Eqs. (a) and (e) simultaneously yields

\(P_R=218.7kN\quad C\)

 

\(P_C=431.3kN \quad C\)

The normal stresses in the rods and in the concrete are obtained by using the definition of stress, \(\sigma _{avg}=\frac{F}{A} \) . Thus

\(\sigma _R=\frac{P_R}{A_R}=\frac{218.7(10^3)}{4418(10^{-6})}=49.50(10^6) N/m^2\cong 49.5MPa\quad C \)

 

\(\sigma _C=\frac{P_C}{A_C}=\frac{431.3(10^3)}{58,080(10^{-6})}=7.426(10^6) N/m^2\cong 7.43MPa\quad C \)

(b) The shortening of the pier is obtained from either the deformation of the rods or the deformation of the concrete since they are equal. Thus, from the deformation of the rods,

\(\delta =\delta _R=\frac{218.7(10^3)(0.600)}{(200)(10^9)(4418)(10^{-6})} \)

 

\(=0.1485(10^{-3})m=0.1485mm\)
