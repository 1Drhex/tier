Guest Question.

Assuming that the operational amplifier of Figure 7.18 is ideal:

(a) Find the z-parameters of Figure 7.18.

(b) If the network is connected by a voltage source with source resistance of 50 \(\Omega \) and a load resistance of 1 k\(\Omega \), find the voltage gain.

(c) Use MATLAB to plot the magnitude response.

Admin
Solution

Using KVL,

(7.78)

\(V_1 = R_1 I_1+\frac{ I_1}{sC} \)

(7.79)

\(V_2 = R_4 I_2 + R_3 I_3 + R_2 I_3\)

From the concept of virtual circuit discussed in Chapter 11,

(7.80)

\(R_2 I_3 =\frac{ I_1}{sC}\)

Substituting Equation (7.80) into Equation (7.79), we get

(7.81)

\(V_2=\frac{\left(R_1+R_2\right)I_1 }{sCR_2} +R_4I_2\)

Comparing Equation (7.79) and Equation (7.81) to Equation (7.1) and Equation (7.2), we have

(7.1)

\(V_1 = z_{11} I_1 + z_{12} I_2\)

(7.2)

\(V_2 = z_{21} I_1 + z_{22} I_2\)

(7.82)

\(z_{11}=R_1+\frac{1}{sC} \)

\(z_{12}=0\)

\(z_{21}=\left\lgroup1+\frac{R_3}{R_2} \right\rgroup \left\lgroup\frac{1}{sC} \right\rgroup \)

\(z_{22} = R_4\)

From Equation (7.63), we get the voltage gain for a terminated two-port network. It is repeated here.

\(\frac{V_2}{V_g} =\frac{z_{21} Z_L}{( z_{11} + Z_g )( z_{22} + Z_L ) – z_{12} z_{21}} \)

Substituting Equation (7.82) into Equation (7.75), we have

(7.75)

\(I_1=V_2\left[a_{21}+\frac{a_{22}}{Z_L} \right] \)

(7.83)

\(\frac{V_1}{V_g} =\frac{\left\lgroup1+\frac{R_3}{R_2} \right\rgroup Z_L }{( R_4 + Z_L )\left[1+sC\left(R_1+Z_g\right) \right] } \)

For\( Z_g = 50 \Omega , Z_L = 1 k\Omega , R 1 = 1 k\Omega , R_2 = 12 k\Omega , R_3 = 10 k\Omega , R_4 = 2 k\Omega \), and \(C = 0.1 \mu F\), Equation (7.83) becomes

(7.84)

\(\frac{V_2}{V_g} =\frac{2}{[ 1 + 1 . 05 * 10^{- 4} s ]} \)
