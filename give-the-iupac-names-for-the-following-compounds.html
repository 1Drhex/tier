Guest Question.

Give the IUPAC names for the following compounds:

Admin
Solution

(a) The alkoxy part contains an isopropyl group. The acyl part comes from propanoic acid (common name: propionic acid). The IUPAC name of the ester is isopropyl propanoate (common name: isopropyl propionate).

(b) Do not assume that the structure of an ester is always written with the acyl part on the left and the alkoxy part on the right. This ester is drawn in the opposite manner, acyl part on the right and alkoxy part on the left. Always remember that the acyl part does not contain an oxygen connected to the carbonyl carbon. The IUPAC name is ethyl 2-methylpropanoate.

(c) The alkoxy part contains the s-butyl group, and the acyl part comes from p-ethylbenzoic acid. The ester is named s-butyl p-ethylbenzoate.
