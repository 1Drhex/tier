Guest Question.

Select a structural tee for the bottom chord of the Warren roof truss shown in Figure. The trusses are welded and spaced at 20 feet. Assume that the bottom chord connection is made with 9-inch-long longitudinal welds at the flange. Use A992 steel and the following load data (wind is not considered in this example):
Purlins: M8\( \times \)6.5
Snow: 20 psf of horizontal projection
Metal deck: 2 psf
Roofing: 4 psf
Insulation: 3 psf

Admin
Solution

Calculate loads:
Snow = 20(40)(20) = 16,000 lb
Dead load (exclusive of purlins) = Deck 2 psf
Roof 4
Insulation 3
Total 9 psf
Total dead load = 9(40)(20) = 7200 lb
Total purlin weight = 6.5(20)(9) = 1170 lb
Estimate the truss weight as 10% of the other loads:
0.10(16,000 + 7200 + 1170) = 2437 lb
Loads at an interior joint are
\(D = \frac {7200}{8}+\frac {2437}{8}+6.5(20)=1335 lb\)
\(S=\frac{16,000}{8}=2000 lb\)
At an exterior joint, the tributary roof area is half of that at an interior joint.
The corresponding loads are
\(D =\frac{7200}{2(8)}+\frac{2437}{8}+6.5(20)=1335 lb\)
\(S=\frac{16,000}{2(8)}=2000 lb\)
Load combination 3 will control:
\(P_{u}\) = 1.2D + 1.6S
At an interior joint,
\(P_{u}\) = 1.2(1.335) + 1.6(2.0) = 4.802 kips
At an exterior joint,
\(P_{u}\) = 1.2(0.7323) + 1.6(1.0) = 2.479 kips
The loaded truss is shown in Figure 3.34a.
The bottom chord is designed by determining the force in each member of the bottom chord and selecting a cross section to resist the largest force. In this example, the force in member IJ will control. For the free body left of section a–a shown in Figure 3.34b,
\(\sum M_{E}\)= 19.29(20) – 2.479(20) – 4.802(15 + 10 + 5) – 4\(F_{IJ}\) = 0

\(F_{IJ}\) = 48.04 kips
For the gross section,
Required\( A_{g}=\frac{F_{IJ}}{0.90F_{y}}= \frac{48.04}{0.90(50)}=1.07 in.^{2}\)
For the net section,
Required \(A_{e}=\frac{F_{IJ}}{0.75F_{u}}= \frac{48.04}{0.75(65)}=0.985 in.^{2}\)

Try an MT5\( \times 3.75:\)

\(A_{g}= 1.11 in.^{2} > 1.07 in.^{2}\) (OK)
Since this is a connection with longitudinal welds only, the shear lag factor, U, is
\(U=\left ( \frac{3l^{2}}{3l^{2}+w^{2}} \right )\left ( 1-\frac{\bar{x}}{l} \right )\)
(category 3b, welded members; same as Case 4 in AISC Table D3.1).
For an MT5 \(\times\) 3.75,
w = flange width \(= b_{f} = 2.69 in.\)

\(\bar{x}= 1.51 in.\)
\(U=\left [ \frac{3(9)^{2}}{3(9)^{2}+(2.69)^{2}} \right ]\left ( 1 -\frac{1.51}{9} \right )=0.9711(0.8322)\)
\(A_{e} = A_{g}U =1.11(0.8081) = 0.8970 in.^{2} < 0.985 in.^{2} (N.G.)\)

Try an MT6 \(\times \)5:
\(A_{g} = 1.48 in.^{2} > 1.07 in.2^{2}\) (OK)

For an MT6 \(\times\) 5,
w = \(b_{f}\) = 3.25 in.
\(\bar{x}\) = 1.86 in.
\(U=\left [ \frac{3(9)^{2}}{3(9)^{2}+(3.25)^{2}} \right ]\left ( 1 -\frac{1.86}{9} \right )= 0.9583(0.7933)\)
=0.7602
\(A_{e} = A_{g}U = 1.148(0.7602) = 1.13 in.^{2} > 0.985 in.^{2}\) (OK)
If we assume that the bottom chord is braced at the panel points,
\(\frac{L}{r}=\frac{5(12)}{0.594}=101 < 300\)
Use an MT6 \(\times\) 5.
Load combination 3 will control. At an interior joint,
\(P_{a} = D + S = 1.335 + 2.0 = 3.335 kips\)
At an exterior joint,
\(P_{a}\) = 0.7323 + 1.0 = 1.732 kips
The loaded truss is shown in Figure 3.35a.
Member IJ is the bottom chord member with the largest force. For the free body shown in Figure 3.35b,
\(\sum M_{E}\) = 13.40(20) – 1.732(20) – 3.335(15 + 10 + 5) – \(4F_{IJ}\) = 0
\(F_{IJ}\) = 33.33 kips
For the gross section,\( F_{t} = 0.6F_{y} = 0.6(36) = 21.6 ksi\)
Required \(A_{g} =\frac{F_{IJ}}{F_{t}}=\frac{33.33}{21.6}=1.54 in.^{2}\)
For the net section, \(F_{t} = 0.5F_{u} =0.5(58) = 29.0 ksi\)
\(Required A_{e} = \frac{F_{IJ}}{F_{t}}=\frac{33.33}{29.0}=1.15in.^{2}\)
\(Try an MT6 \times 5.4:\)
\(A_{g} = 1.59 in.^{2} > 1.54 in.^{2}\) (OK)
Since this is a connection with longitudinal welds only, the shear lag factor, U, is
\(U= \left (\frac{3l^{2}}{3l^{2}+w^{2}}\right ) \left ( 1 -\frac{\bar{x}}{l} \right )\)
(category 3b, welded members; same as Case 4 in AISC Table D3.1).
For an MT6 \(\times\) 5.4,
w = flange width =\( b_{f}\) = 3.07 in.
\(\bar{x}= 1.86 in.\)
\(U=\left [ \frac{3(9)^{2}}{3(9)^{2}+(3.07)^{2}} \right ]\left ( 1 -\frac{1.86}{9} \right )= 0.9627(0.7933)\)
= 0.7637
\(A_{e} = A_{g}U = 1.59(0.7637) = 1.21 in.^{2} > 1.15 in.^{2}\) (OK)
Assuming that the bottom chord is braced at the panel points, we get
\(\frac{L}{r}=\frac{5(12)}{0.566}=106 < 300\) (OK)
Use an MT6 \(\times 5.4.\)
