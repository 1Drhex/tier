Guest Question.

Figure P17.78 shows a simply supported beam. Assume that \(E I=15 \times 10^{6} \text { kip-in. }^{2}\) for the beam. Apply the virtual-work method and calculate:
(a) the deflection at A.
(b) the slope at C.

Admin
Solution

(a) Deflection at A

Virtual Moment m: To determine the deflection of the beam, first remove the real load from the beam and apply a virtual unit load downward at A. The free-body diagram of the beam with this loading is shown.

 

Cut a free-body diagram through the beam at section a–a. Derive a moment equation m from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{array}{rl}\Sigma M_{a-a} & =(1 \text { kip }) x_{1}+m=0 \\& \therefore m=-(1 \text { kip }) x_{1} & 0 \leq x_{1}<8 ft\end{array}\)

 

 

Cut a second free-body diagram through the beam at section b–b. Derive a moment equation m from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{array}{rlrl}\Sigma M_{b-b} & =-(0.4 kips ) x_{2}-m=0 & \\& \therefore m=-(0.4 kips ) x_{2} & 0 \leq x_{2}<20 ft\end{array}\)

 

 

Real Moment M: Remove the virtual load and reapply the real load. A free-body of the beam is shown.

 

 

Draw a free-body diagram that cuts through the beam at section a–a and derive a moment equation M from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma M_{a-a}=\frac{3.5 kips / ft }{2} x_{1}^{2}+M=0 \\&\therefore M=-\frac{3.5 kips / ft }{2} x_{1}^{2} \quad 0 \leq x_{1}<8 ft\end{aligned}\)

 

 

Draw a second free-body diagram that cuts through the beam at section b–b and derive a moment equation M from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{b-b} &=-\frac{3.5 kips / ft }{2} x_{2}^{2}+(29.4 kips ) x_{2}-M=0 \\& \therefore M=-\frac{3.5 kips / ft }{2} x_{2}^{2}+(29.4 kips ) x_{2} \quad 0 ft \leq x_{2}<20 ft\end{aligned}\)

 

Virtual-Work Equation for Beam Deflection: From Equation (17.35), the beam deflection at A can now be determined.

\(\begin{aligned}(1 kip ) \cdot \Delta_{A} &=\int_{0}^{L} m\left(\frac{M}{E I}\right) d x \\&=\frac{1}{E I} \int_{0}^{8}\left(-1 x_{1}\right)\left(-\frac{3.5}{2} x_{1}^{2}\right) d x_{1}+\frac{1}{E I} \int_{0}^{20}\left(-0.4 x_{2}\right)\left(-\frac{3.5}{2} x_{2}^{2}+29.4 x_{2}\right) d x_{2} \\&=\frac{1.75}{4 E I}\left[x_{1}^{4}\right]_{0}^{8}+\frac{0.7}{4 E I}\left[x_{2}^{4}\right]_{0}^{20}-\frac{11.76}{3 E I}\left[x_{2}^{3}\right]_{0}^{20} \\&=\frac{1,792}{E I}+\frac{28,000}{E I}-\frac{31,360}{E I} \\&=-\frac{1,568}{E I}=-\frac{\left(1,568 kip ^{2}- ft ^{3}\right)(12 in . / ft )^{3}}{15 \times 10^{6} kip – in .^{2}}=-0.180634 kip \text {-in. } \\& \therefore \Delta_{A}=0.1806 in . \uparrow\end{aligned}\)

 

 

 

(b) Slope at C

Virtual Moment m: To determine the slope of the beam, first remove the real load from the beam and apply a virtual unit moment counterclockwise at C. The free-body diagram of the beam with this loading is shown.

 

 

Cut a free-body diagram through the beam at section a–a. Derive a moment equation m from this free-body diagram.

\(\Sigma M_{a-a}=m=0 \quad 0 \leq x_{1}<8 ft\)

 

 

Cut a second free-body diagram through the beam at section b–b. Derive a moment equation m from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{b-b} &=-(0.05 kips ) x_{2}+(1 kip – ft )-m=0 \\& \therefore m=-(0.05 kips ) x_{2}+(1 kip – ft ) & 0 \leq x_{2}<20 ft\end{aligned}\)

 

Virtual-Work Equation for Beam Slope: From Equation (17.36), the beam slope at C can now be determined.

\(\begin{aligned}(1 kip – ft ) \cdot \theta_{C} &=\int_{0}^{L} m\left(\frac{M}{E I}\right) d x \\&=\frac{1}{E I} \int_{0}^{8}(0)\left(-\frac{3.5}{2} x_{1}^{2}\right) d x_{1}+\frac{1}{E I} \int_{0}^{20}\left(-0.05 x_{2}+1\right)\left(-\frac{3.5}{2} x_{2}^{2}+29.4 x_{2}\right) d x_{2} \\&=\frac{1}{E I} \int_{0}^{20}\left(-0.05 x_{2}\right)\left(-\frac{3.5}{2} x_{2}^{2}+29.4 x_{2}\right) d x_{2}+\frac{1}{E I} \int_{0}^{20}(1)\left(-\frac{3.5}{2} x_{2}^{2}+29.4 x_{2}\right) d x_{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&=\frac{0.0875}{E I} \int_{0}^{20} x_{2}^{3} d x_{2}-\frac{1.47}{E I} \int_{0}^{20} x_{2}^{2} d x_{2}-\frac{1.75}{E I} \int_{0}^{20} x_{2}^{2} d x_{2}+\frac{29.4}{E I} \int_{0}^{20} x_{2} d x_{2} \\&=\frac{0.0875}{4 E I}\left[x_{2}^{4}\right]_{0}^{20}-\frac{1.47}{3 E I}\left[x_{2}^{3}\right]_{0}^{20}-\frac{1.75}{3 E I}\left[x_{2}^{3}\right]_{0}^{20}+\frac{29.4}{2 E I}\left[x_{2}^{2}\right]_{0}^{20} \\&=\frac{3,500}{E I}-\frac{3,920}{E I}-\frac{4,666.667}{E I}+\frac{5,880}{E I} \\&=\frac{793.333}{E I}=\frac{\left(793.333 kip ^{2}- ft ^{3}\right)(12 in . / ft )^{2}}{15 \times 10^{6} kip – in .{ }^{2}}=0.007616 kip – ft \\&\therefore \theta_{C}=0.00762 rad CCW\end{aligned}\)
