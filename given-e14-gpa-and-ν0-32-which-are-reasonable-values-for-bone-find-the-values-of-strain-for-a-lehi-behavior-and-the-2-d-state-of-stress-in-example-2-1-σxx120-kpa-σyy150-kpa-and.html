Guest Question.

Given E=14 GPa and \(\nu =0.32,\) which are reasonable values for bone, find the values of strain for a LEHI behavior and the 2-D state of stress in : \(σ_{xx}=120 kPa, σ_{yy}=150 kPa,\) and \(σ_{xy}=0.\) Note: \( 1 GPa=10^{3} MPa=10^{6} kPa=10^{9} Pa,\) where \(1 Pa=1 N/m^{2}.\)

Admin
Solution

From Eq. 2.69,

\( \varepsilon _{xx}=\frac{1}{E}\left[\sigma _{xx}-\nu (\sigma _{yy}+\sigma _{zz})\right]+\beta \Delta T, \varepsilon _{xy}=\frac{1}{2G}\sigma _{xy},\)

 

\( \varepsilon _{yy}=\frac{1}{E}\left[\sigma _{yy}-\nu (\sigma _{xx}+\sigma _{zz})\right]+\beta \Delta T, \varepsilon _{xz}=\frac{1}{2G}\sigma _{xz},\) (2.69)

 

\( \varepsilon _{zz}=\frac{1}{E}\left[\sigma _{zz}-\nu (\sigma _{xx}+\sigma _{yy})\right]+\beta \Delta T, \varepsilon _{yz}=\frac{1}{2G}\sigma _{yz},\)

 

\( \varepsilon _{xx}=\frac{1}{14\times 10^{6}}\left[120-0.32(150+0)\right]\frac{kPa}{kPa}=5.1\times 10^{-6},\)

 

\( \varepsilon _{yy}=\frac{1}{14\times 10^{6}}\left[150-0.32(120+0)\right]=8.0\times 10^{-6}\)

 

\( \varepsilon _{xy}=\frac{1}{2G}(0)=0,\)

where \(G=E/2(1+\nu ).\) Unless the problem is treated as purely two dimensional,

\( \varepsilon _{zz}=\frac{1}{14\times 10^{6}}\left[0-0.32(150+120)\right]=-6.2\times 10^{-6},\)

which is to say that the material will thin in the z direction due to the (inplane) stresses in the x and y directions. Note, too, that each value of strain is much less than unity, consistent with the small strain requirement for Hooke’s law, and positive values denote lengthening, whereas negative values denote shortening. Moreover, strain is unitless (it represents normalized changes in length and changes in angles) and a value of strain times \(10^{–6}\) is often called a microstrain \((\mu \varepsilon ),\) as, for example, \(5.1\times10^{–6}\equiv5.1 (\mu \varepsilon ).\)
