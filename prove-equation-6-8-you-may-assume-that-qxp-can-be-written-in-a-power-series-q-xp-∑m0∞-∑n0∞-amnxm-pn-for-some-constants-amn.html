Guest Question.

Prove Equation 6.8. You may assume that \( Q(\hat{x}, \hat{p}) \) can be written in a power series

\( \hat{Q}(\hat{x}, \hat{p})=\sum_{m=0}^{\infty} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_{m n} \hat{x}^{m} \hat{p}^{n} \)

for some constants \( a_{m n} \).

\( \hat{Q}^{\prime}(\hat{x}, \hat{p})=\hat{T}^{\dagger} \hat{Q}(\hat{x}, \hat{p}) \hat{T}=\hat{Q}\left(\hat{x}^{\prime}, \hat{p}^{\prime}\right)=\hat{Q}(\hat{x}+a, \hat{p}) \) (6.8).

Admin
Solution

We start by writing

\( \hat{T}^{\dagger} \hat{\Omega} \hat{T}=\sum_{m n} a_{m n} \hat{T}^{\dagger} \hat{x}^{m} \hat{p}^{n} \hat{T} \).

The idea is that we’re then free to insert copies of \( \hat{T} \hat{T}^{\dagger}=1 \) wherever necessary. For example, consider the term

\( \hat{T}^{\dagger} \hat{x}^{3} \hat{p}^{2} \hat{T}=\underbrace{\hat{T}^{\dagger} \hat{x} \hat{T}}_{\hat{x}^{\prime}} \hat{T}^{\dagger} \hat{x} \hat{T} \hat{T}^{\dagger} \hat{x} \hat{T} \hat{T}^{\dagger} \hat{p} \hat{T} \hat{T}^{\dagger} \hat{p} \hat{T}=\hat{x}^{\prime 3} \hat{p}^{\prime 3} \).

It is then clear that

\( \hat{T}^{\dagger} \hat{\Omega} \hat{T}=\sum_{m n} a_{m n}\left(\hat{T}^{\dagger} \hat{x} \hat{T}\right)^{m}\left(\hat{T}^{\dagger} \hat{p} \hat{T}\right)^{n}=\sum_{m n} a_{m n}\left(\hat{x}^{\prime}\right)^{m}\left(\hat{p}^{\prime}\right)^{n} \).

so that

\( \hat{\Omega}^{\prime}(\hat{x}, \hat{p})=\hat{T}^{\dagger} \hat{\Omega}(\hat{x}, \hat{p}) \hat{T}=\hat{\Omega}\left(\hat{x}^{\prime}, \hat{p}^{\prime}\right) \).
