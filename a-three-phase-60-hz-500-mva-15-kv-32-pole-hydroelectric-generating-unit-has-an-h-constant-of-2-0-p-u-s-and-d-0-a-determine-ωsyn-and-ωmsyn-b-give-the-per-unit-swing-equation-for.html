Guest Question.

Generator per-unit swing equation and power angle during a short circuit

A three-phase, 60-Hz, 500-MVA, 15-kV, 32-pole hydroelectric generating unit has an H constant of 2.0 p.u.-s and D = 0. (a) Determine \(\omega _{syn} \) and \(\omega _{msyn} \). Give the per-unit swing equation for this unit. (c) The unit is initially operating at \(P _{mp.u.} =P _{ep.u.}=1.0,\omega =\omega _{syn} \), and δ = 10° when a three-phase-to-ground bolted short circuit at the generator terminals causes \(P _{ep.u.}\) to drop to zero for t ≥ 0. Determine the power angle 3 cycles after the short circuit commences. Assume \(P _{mp.u.}\) remains constant at 1.0 per unit. Also assume \(\omega _{p.u.}(t)=1.0\) in the swing equation.

Admin
Solution

a. For a 60-Hz generator,

\(\omega _{syn} =2\pi 60=377\) rad/s

and, from (11.1.14), with P = 32 poles,

\(\omega _{syn} =\frac{P}{2}\omega _{msyn} \) (11.1.14)

\(\omega _{msyn} =\frac{2}{P}\omega _{syn}=\left(\frac{2}{32} \right)377=23.56 \) rad/s

b. From (11.1.16), with H = 2.0 p.u.-s,

\(\frac{2H}{\omega _{syn} } \omega _{p.u.}\left(t\right) \frac{d^{2}\delta \left(t\right) }{dt^{2} } =P_{mp.u. }\left(t\right)- P_{ep.u.}\left(t\right)=P_{ap.u.}\left(t\right)\) (11.1.16)

\(\frac{4}{2\pi 60 } \omega _{p.u.}\left(t\right) \frac{d^{2}\delta \left(t\right) }{dt^{2} } =P_{mp.u. }\left(t\right)- P_{ep.u.}\left(t\right)\)

c. The initial power angle is

δ(0) = 10° = 0.1745 radian

Also, from (11.1.17), at t = 0,

\(2H/\omega _{syn} \omega _{p.u}\left(t\right)\left(d^{2}\delta \left(t\right) /\left(dt^{2} \right) \right) =P_{mp.u.} \left(t\right) -P_{ep.u.}\left(t\right) -D/\omega _{syn}\left(d\delta \left(t\right) /\left(dt\right) \right) =P_{ap.u.}\left(t\right)\) (11.1.17)

\(\frac{d\delta \left(0\right) }{dt}=0 \)

Using \(P_{mp.u.}\left(t\right)=1.0,P_{ep.u.}=0, \) and \(\omega _{p.u.} \left(t\right)=1.0 \), the swing equation from (b) is

\(\left(\frac{4}{2\pi 60} \right) =\frac{d^{2}\delta \left(t\right) }{dt^{2} } =1.0\) t ≥ 0

Integrating twice and using the above initial conditions,

\(\frac{d\delta \left(t\right) }{dt } =\left(\frac{2\pi 60}{4} \right) t+0\)

 

\(\delta \left(t\right) =\left(\frac{2\pi 60}{8} \right) t^{2} +0.1745\)

At t = 3 cycles =\(\frac{3cycles}{60 cycles/second} \)= 0.05 second,

\(\delta \left(0.05\right) =\left(\frac{2\pi 60}{8} \right)\left(0.05\right)^{2} +0.1745=0.2923 radian=16.75°\)
