Guest Question.

Access the validity of Eqn. (12.7) \(\theta =\frac{\gamma +1}{2} \delta \) for \(δ = 5°\) and 35° for an infinite free-stream Mach number \(M_{1} → ∞\), and for \(δ = 30°\) and \(M_{1} = 10\).

Admin
Solution

For \(δ = 5° ≈ 0.0873\), \(θ ≈ 6° = 1.2δ\). In fact for \(δ = 35° ≈ 0.611\), \(θ ≈ 42.08° ≈ 1.202δ\), quite a pleasantly surprising result considering the 35° deflection is not quite a small angle. For other finite, but still large values of \(M_{1}\), the linear relation between \(θ\) and \(δ\) is limited o a smaller range of \(δ\), as can be seen from Fig. 4.12. For finite values of \(M_{1}\), the deviations from the prediction using Eqn. (12.7) \(\theta =\frac{\gamma +1}{2} \delta \) increase with decreasing \(M_{1}\). Such a result is expected, because of the underlying assumption that \(M_{1}\) be infinity, and can be clearly visualized as the intercept on the \(θ\) axis increases with decreasing value of \(M_{1}\). For example for \(M_{1} = 10\) and \(δ = 30°\), Fig. 4.12 gives, \(θ ≈ 39.25° ≈ 1.308δ\), with Eqn. (12.7) \(\theta =\frac{\gamma +1}{2} \delta \) under-predicting by approximately 9%.
