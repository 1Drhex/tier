Guest Question.

The roof of a house consists of a 3–cm-thick concrete slab (\(k=2 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\)) that is 15 m wide and 20 m long. The convection heat transfer coefficients on the inner and outer surfaces of the roof are 5 and \( 12 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\), respectively. On a clear winter night, the ambient air is reported to be at 10°C, while the night sky temperature is 100 K. The house and the interior surfaces of the wall are maintained at a constant temperature of 20°C. The emissivity of both surfaces of the concrete roof is 0.9. Considering both radiation and convection heat transfers, determine the rate of heat transfer through the roof, and the inner surface temperature of the roof. If the house is heated by a furnace burning natural gas with an efficiency of 80 percent, and the price of natural gas is $0.60/therm (1 therm = 105,500 kJ of energy content), determine the money lost through the roof that night during a 14-h period.

Admin
Solution

The roof of a house with a gas furnace consists of 3-cm thick concrete that is losing heat to the outdoors by radiation and convection. The rate of heat transfer through the roof and the money lost through the roof that night during a 14 hour period are to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Steady operating conditions exist. 2 The emissivity and thermal conductivity of the roof are constant.

Properties The thermal conductivity of the concrete is given to be \( k=2 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\). The emissivity of both surfaces of the roof is given to be 0.9.

Analysis When the surrounding surface temperature is different than the ambient temperature, the thermal resistances network approach becomes cumbersome in problems that involve radiation. Therefore, we will use a different but intuitive approach.

In steady operation, heat transfer from the room to the roof (by convection and radiation) must be equal to the heat transfer from the roof to the surroundings (by convection and radiation), that must be equal to the heat transfer through the roof by conduction. That is,

\( \dot{Q}=\dot{Q}_{\text {room to roof, conv }+ rad }=\dot{Q}_{\text {roof, cond }}=\dot{Q}_{\text {roof to surroundings, conv }+ rad }\)

 

Taking the inner and outer surface temperatures of the roof to be \(T_{s, \text { in }} \text { and } T_{s, \text { out }}\) , respectively, the quantities above can be expressed as

\( \dot{Q}_{\text {room to roof, conv }+ rad }=h_{i} A\left(T_{\text {room }}-T_{s, i n}\right)+\varepsilon A \sigma\left(T_{\text {room }}^{4}-T_{s, i n}^{4}\right) = \)

 

\( \left(5 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(300 m ^{2}\right)\left(20-T_{s, i n}\right)^{\circ} C +(0.9)\left(300 m ^{2}\right)\left(5.67 \times 10^{-8} W / m ^{2} \cdot K ^{4}\right)\)

 

\( \left[(20+273 K )^{4}-\left(T_{s, i n}+273 K \right)^{4}\right]\)

 

\( \dot{Q}_{\text {roof }, \text { cond }}=k A \frac{T_{s, \text { in }}-T_{s, \text { out }}}{L}=\left(2 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(300 m ^{2}\right) \frac{T_{s, i n}-T_{s, \text { out }}}{0.15 m } \)

 

\( \dot{Q}_{\text {roof to surr, conv }+ rad }=h_{o} A\left(T_{s, \text { out }}-T_{\text {surr }}\right)+\varepsilon A \sigma\left(T_{s, \text { out }}^{4}-T_{\text {surr }}{ }^{4}\right)= \)

 

\( \left(12 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(300 m ^{2}\right)\left(T_{s, \text { out }}-10\right)^{\circ} C +(0.9)\left(300 m ^{2}\right)\left(5.67 \times 10^{-8} W / m ^{2} \cdot K ^{4}\right) \)

 

\( \left[\left(T_{s, o u t}+273 K \right)^{4}-(100 K )^{4}\right]\)

 

Solving the equations above simultaneously gives

\( \dot{Q}= 3 7 , 4 4 0 W , T_{s, \text { in }}= 7 . 3 ^{\circ} C \text { , and } T_{s, \text { out }}=-2.1^{\circ} C \)

 

The total amount of natural gas consumption during a 14-hour period is

\( Q_{\text {gas }}=\frac{Q_{\text {total }}}{0.80}=\frac{\dot{Q} \Delta t}{0.80}=\frac{(37.440 kJ / s )(14 \times 3600 s )}{0.80}\left(\frac{1 \text { therm }}{105,500 kJ }\right)=22.36 \text { therms }\)

 

Finally, the money lost through the roof during that period is

\( \text { Money lost }=(22.36 \text { therms })(\$ 0.60 / \text { therm })=\$ 13.4 \)
