Guest Question.

Design an overhung crankshaft for a 300 × 350 mm single cylinder vertical engine using the following data:
Maximum gas pressure = 2.5 MPa
(L/r) ratio = 4.5
Weight of flywheel cum belt pulley = 10 kN
Total belt pull = 5 kN
Width of hub for flywheel cum belt pulley = 150 mm
The torque on the crankshaft is maximum when the
crank turns through 35° from the top dead centre and at this position the gas pressure inside the cylinder is 1 MPa. The belts are in the horizontal direction.
Assume suitable data and state the assumptions you make.

Admin
Solution

Given D = 300 mm

\( p_{\text {max. }}=2.5 MPa =2.5 N / mm ^{2} \quad(L / r)=4.5 \).

\( l=350 mm \quad W=10 kN =10000 N \quad \theta=35^{\circ} \).

\( \left(P_{1}+P_{2}\right)=5 kN =5000 N \).

\( p^{\prime}=1 MPa =1 N / mm ^{2} \).

The crank radius r is given by,

\( r=\left(\frac{l}{2}\right)=\frac{350}{2}=175 mm \).

Case I The crank is at the top dead centre position and subjected to maximum bending moment and no torsional moment.
At the top dead centre position, the thrust in the connecting rod will be equal to the force acting on piston.

\( P_{p}=\left(\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}\right) p_{\max }=\left(\frac{\pi(300)^{2}}{4}\right) \).

= 176714.59 N.

Step 1 Design of crank pin
Suppose

\( d_{c}=\text { diameter of crank pin }( mm ) \).

\( l_{c}=\text { length of crank pin }( mm ) \).

\( \sigma_{b}= \) allowable bending stress for crank pin (N/mm²)

\( p_{b}= \) allowable bearing pressure at the crank pin bush (MPa or N/mm²)

Assumption 1 The allowable bearing pressure at the crank pin is 10 MPa.
Assumption 2 The allowable bending stress for crank pin is 60 N/mm².
Assumption 3 The (l/d) ratio for the crank pin is 0.80.

\( \left(\frac{l_{c}}{d_{c}}\right)=0.8 \quad \text { or } \quad l_{c}=0.8 d_{c} \).

The bearing pressure at the crank pin is given by,

\( p_{b}=\frac{P_{p}}{d_{c} l_{c}} \quad \text { or } \quad 10=\frac{176714.59}{d_{c}\left(0.8 d_{c}\right)} \).

\( d_{c}^{2}=22089.32 \).

\( d_{c}=148.62 \text { or } 150 mm \).

\( l_{c}=0.8 d_{c}=0.8(150)=120 mm \).

After calculating the dimensions, it is necessary to check the bending stresses in the crank pin.
The crank pin acts as a cantilever and the maximum bending moment is given by

\( M_{b}=\left(\frac{3}{4}\right) P_{p} l_{c}=\left(\frac{3}{4}\right)(176714.59)(120) \).

\( =15904.31 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

The section modulus for the crank pin is given by,

\( Z=\frac{\pi d_{c}^{3}}{32}=\frac{\pi(150)^{3}}{32}=331.34 \times 10^{3} mm ^{3} \).

\( M_{b}=\sigma_{b}\left(\frac{\pi d_{c}^{3}}{32}\right) \).

\( \text { or } \quad 15904.31 \times 10^{3}=\sigma_{b}\left(331.34 \times 10^{3}\right) \).

\( \sigma_{b}=48 N / mm ^{2} \quad \sigma_{b}<60 N / mm ^{2} \).

The bending stress in the crank pin is within the permissible limit.

Step 2 Design of bearings
Refer to Fig. 25.26. Suppose,

\( d_{1}=\text { diameter of journal or shaft at the bearing } \)1 (mm).

\( l_{1}=\text { length of the bearing } 1( mm ) \).

\( \sigma_{b}=\text { allowable bending stress for shaft }\left( N / mm ^{2}\right) \).

t = thickness of web (mm)
The thickness of the web is calculated by the following empirical relationship:

\( t=0.60 d_{c}=0.60(150)=90 mm \).

The length of the bearing is calculated by the following empirical relationship:

\( l_{1}=1.75 d_{c}=1.75(150)=262.5 \text { or } 265 mm \).

Refer to Fig. 25.26. The bending moment at the bearing 1 is given by,

\( M_{b}=P_{p}\left[0.75 l_{c}+t+0.5 l_{1}\right] \).

\( =176714.59[0.75(120)+90+0.5(265)] \).

\( =55.22 \times 10^{6} N – mm \).

The bending stress in the shaft of the bearing 1 is given by,

\( M_{b}=\sigma_{b}\left(\frac{\pi d_{1}^{3}}{32}\right) \).

or \( 55.22 \times 10^{6}=60\left(\frac{\pi d_{1}^{3}}{32}\right) \).

\( d_{1}^{3}=9374.44 \times 10^{3} mm ^{3} \).

\( d_{1}=210.85 \text { or } 215 mm \).

Step 3 Dimensions of b and c
Refer to Fig. 25.26. The dimension b is given by,

\( b=\left[0.75 l_{c}+t+\frac{l_{1}}{2}\right]=\left[0.75(120)+90+\frac{(265)}{2}\right] \).

= 312.5 or 320 mm
The dimension c is given by,

\( c=\left\{\frac{l_{1}}{2}+\text { flywheel width }+\frac{l_{2}}{2}+\text { margin }\right\} \).

\( c=\left\{\frac{265}{2}+150+\frac{265}{2}+15\right\}=430 mm \).

We will assume,

\( c_{1}=c_{2}=\frac{c}{2}=\frac{430}{2}=215 mm \).

\( b=320 mm \quad c=430 mm \quad c_{1}=c_{2}=215 mm \).

Step 4 Bearing reactions

\( \left(R_{1}\right)_{v}=\frac{P_{p} \times(b+c)}{c} \).

\( =\frac{176714.59 \times(320+430)}{430} \).

\( =308.22 \times 10^{3} N \).

\( \left(R_{2}\right)_{v}=\frac{P_{p} \times b}{c} \).

\( =\frac{176714.59 \times 320}{430} \).

\( =131.51 \times 10^{3} N \).

\( \text { Since, } c_{1}=c_{2} \).

By symmetry,

\( \left(R_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{v}=\left(R_{2}^{\prime}\right)_{v}=\frac{W}{2}=\frac{10 \times 10^{3}}{2}=5000 N \).

\( \left(R_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{h}=\left(R_{2}^{\prime}\right)_{h}=\frac{\left(P_{1}+P_{2}\right)}{2}=\frac{5000}{2}=2500 N \).

Step 5 Check for bearing pressure at the bearing 1
The dimensions \( d_{1} \text { and } l_{1} \) are to be checked for bearing pressure.
The reaction \( R_{1} \) is given by,

\( R_{1}=\sqrt{\left[\left(R_{1}\right)_{v}+\left(R_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{v}\right]^{2}+\left[\left(R_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{h}\right]^{2}} \).

\( =\sqrt{\left[\left(308.22 \times 10^{3}\right)+\left(5 \times 10^{3}\right)\right]^{2}+\left(2.5 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2}} \).

\( =313.23 \times 10^{3} N \).

The bearing pressure is given by,

\( p_{b}=\frac{R_{1}}{d_{1} l_{1}}=\frac{313.23 \times 10^{3}}{(215)(265)} \).

\( =5.5 N / mm ^{2} \quad p_{b}<10 N / mm ^{2} \).

Bearings 1 and 2 are made identical.

Step 6 Design of crank web
Assumption 4 The allowable compressive stress is 60 N/mm².
Suppose,
w = width of crank web (mm)
As shown in Fig. 25.26, the crank web is subjected to the eccentric load \(P_{p}\). There are two types of stresses in the central plane of the crank web, viz., direct compressive stress and bending stress due to the reaction \(P_{p}\).
The direct compressive stress is given by,

\( \sigma_{c}=\frac{P_{P}}{w t}=\frac{176714.59}{w(90)}=\left(\frac{1963.5}{w}\right) N / mm ^{2} \).

The bending moment at the central plane is given by,

\( M_{b}=P_{P}\left[0.75 l_{c}+\frac{t}{2}\right] \).

\( =176714.59\left[0.75(120)+\frac{90}{2}\right] \).

\( =23856.47 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

\( Z=\left(\frac{w t^{2}}{6}\right)=\left(\frac{w(90)^{2}}{6}\right)=(1350 w) mm ^{3} \).

\( \sigma_{b}=\frac{M_{b}}{Z}=\frac{23856.47 \times 10^{3}}{(1350 w)}=\left(\frac{17671.46}{w}\right) N / mm ^{2} \).

The total compressive stress is given by,

\( \left(\sigma_{c}\right)_{t}=\sigma_{c}+\sigma_{b}=\left(\frac{1963.5}{w}\right)+\left(\frac{17671.46}{w}\right) \).

\( =\left(\frac{19634.96}{w}\right) \).

\( \left(\sigma_{c}\right)_{t}=\left(\frac{19634.96}{w}\right) \quad \text { or } \quad 60=\left(\frac{19634.96}{w}\right) \).

\( w=327.25 \text { or } 330 mm \).

Step 6 Design of shaft under flywheel
The forces acting on the shaft under the flywheel are shown in Fig. 25.26. The central plane of the shaft is subjected to maximum bending moment. Suppose,
\( d_{s}= \) diameter of shaft under flywheel (mm)
The bending moment in vertical plane is given by,

\( \left(M_{b}\right)_{v}=P_{p}\left(b+c_{1}\right)-\left[\left(R_{1}\right)_{v}+\left(R_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{v}\right]\left(c_{1}\right) \).

= 176 714.59(320 + 215) – [308.22 × 10³ + 5000] (215).

\( =27.2 \times 10^{6} N – mm \).

The bending moment in the horizontal plane is given by,

\( \left(M_{b}\right)_{h}=\left(R_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{h} c_{1}=2500(215)=0.54 \times 10^{6} N – mm \).

The resultant bending moment is given by,

\( M_{b}=\sqrt{\left(M_{b}\right)_{v}^{2}+\left(M_{b}\right)_{h}^{2}} \).

\( =\sqrt{\left(27.2 \times 10^{6}\right)^{2}+\left(0.54 \times 10^{6}\right)^{2}} \).

\( =27.2 \times 10^{6} \quad N – mm \).

Also,

\( M_{b}=\left(\frac{\pi d_{s}^{3}}{32}\right) \sigma_{b} \quad \text { or } \quad 27.2 \times 10^{6}=\left(\frac{\pi d_{s}^{3}}{32}\right)(60) \).

\( d_{s}^{3}=4617.62 \times 10^{3} \).

\( d_{s}=166.52 \text { or } 170 mm \).

The diameter \( \left(d_{s}\right) \) should be more than the diameter \( \left(d_{1}\right) \).

\( d_{s}>d_{1} \text { or } d_{s}>215 mm \).

Let us assume the diameter \( \left(d_{s}\right) \) as 250 mm.

Case II The crank is at an angle with the line of dead centre positions and subjected to maximum torsional moment.
Step 1 Components of force on crank pin

\( P_{p}=\left(\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}\right) p^{\prime}=\left(\frac{\pi(300)^{2}}{4}\right)(1.0) \).

\( =70685.83 N \).

\( \sin \varphi=\frac{\sin \theta}{(L / r)}=\frac{\sin (35)}{(4.5)}=0.1275 \).

\( \varphi=\sin -1(0.1275)=7.32^{\circ} \).

\( \text { The thrust on the connecting } \operatorname{rod}\left(P_{q}\right) \text { is given by, } \).

\( P_{q}=\frac{P_{p}}{\cos \varphi}=\frac{(70685.83)}{\cos (7.32)}=71266.65 N \).

\( P_{t} \text { and } P_{r} \text { are the tangential and radial components } \) \( \text { of } P_{q} \text { at the crank pin. } \)

\( P_{t}=P_{g} \sin (\theta+\varphi)=71266.65 \sin (35+7.32) \).

\( =47981.74 N \).

\( P_{r}=P_{q} \cos (\theta+\varphi)=71266.65 \cos (35+7.32) \).

\( =52694.28 N \).

Step 2 Bearing reactions
The forces acting on the side crankshaft at an angle of maximum torque are shown in Fig. 25.28. The crankshaft is supported on two bearings, 1 and 2. The forces acting on crankshaft in vertical and horizontal planes are shown in Fig. 25.29.
(a) Forces in vertical plane [Fig. 25.29(a)]

\( \text { (i) Reactions of } P_{r} \).

\( \left(R_{1}\right)_{v} \text { and }\left(R_{2}\right)_{v} \text { are the reactions at bearings due } \) \( \text { to radial component } P_{r} \text {. } \)

\( \left(R_{1}\right)_{v}=\frac{P_{r} \times(b+c)}{c}=\frac{52694.28 \times(320+430)}{430} \).

\( =91908.63 N \).

\( \left(R_{2}\right)_{v}=\frac{P_{r} \times b}{c}=\frac{52694.28 \times 320}{430}=39214.35 N \).

(ii) Reactions of W
By symmetry

\( \left(R_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{v}=\left(R_{2}^{\prime}\right)_{v}=\frac{W}{2}=\frac{10 \times 10^{3}}{2}=5000 N \).

(b) Forces in horizontal plane [Fig. 25.29(b)]

\( \text { (iii) Reactions of } P_{t} \).

\( \left(R_{1}\right)_{h}=\frac{P_{t} \times(b+c)}{c}=\frac{47981.74 \times(320+430)}{430} \).

\( =83689.08 N \).

\( \left(R_{2}\right)_{h}=\frac{P_{t} \times b}{c}=\frac{47981.74 \times 320}{430}=35707.34 N \).

\( \text { (iv) Reactions of }\left(P_{1}+P_{2}\right) \).

By symmetry,

\( \left(R_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{h}=\left(R_{2}^{\prime}\right)_{h}=\frac{\left(P_{1}+P_{2}\right)}{2}=\frac{5000}{2}=2500 N \).

Step 3 Design of crank pin
Check for bending stresses
The crank pin acts as a cantilever and subjected to maximum bending moment at the crank web as shown in Fig. 25.28. The bending moment in a vertical plane is given by,

\( \left(M_{b}\right)_{v}=P_{r} \times\left(0.75 l_{c}\right)=52694.28(0.75 \times 120) \).

\( =4742.49 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

The bending moment in horizontal plane is given by,

\( \left(M_{b}\right)_{h}=P_{t} \times\left(0.75 l_{c}\right)=47981.74(0.75 \times 120) \).

\( =4318.36 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

\( \text { The resultant bending moment } M_{b} \text { is given by, } \)

\( M_{b}=\sqrt{\left[\left(M_{b}\right)_{v}\right]^{2}+\left[\left(M_{b}\right)_{h}\right]^{2}} \).

\( =\sqrt{\left[4742.49 \times 10^{3}\right]^{2}+\left[4318.36 \times 10^{3}\right]^{2}} \).

\( =6414 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

The section modulus for the crank pin is given by,

\( Z=\frac{\pi d_{c}^{3}}{32}=\frac{\pi(150)^{3}}{32}=331.34 \times 10^{3} mm ^{3} \).

\( M_{b}=\sigma_{b} Z \quad \text { or } \quad 6414 \times 10^{3}=\sigma_{b}\left(331.34 \times 10^{3}\right) \).

\( \sigma_{b}=19.36 N / mm ^{2} \quad \sigma_{b}<60 N / mm ^{2} \).

The bending stresses in the crank pin are within the permissible limit.

Step 4 Design of crank web
Check for bending stresses
The bending moment due to radial component at the central plane is given by,

\( \left(M_{b}\right)_{r}=P_{r}\left[0.75 l_{c}+0.5 t\right] \).

= 52 694.28 [0.75(120) + 0.5(90)].

\( =7113.73 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

\( Z=\frac{1}{6} w t^{2}=\frac{1}{6}(330)(90)^{2}=445.5 \times 10^{3} mm ^{3} \).

\( \left(M_{b}\right)_{r}=\left(\sigma_{b}\right)_{r} \times Z \).

or \( 7113.73 \times 10^{3}=\left(\sigma_{b}\right)_{r}\left(445.5 \times 10^{3}\right) \).

\( \left(\sigma_{b}\right)_{r}=15.97 N / mm ^{2} \) (a).

The bending moment due to tangential component at the juncture of the crank web and shaft is given by,

\( \left(M_{b}\right)_{t}=P_{t}\left[r-\frac{d_{1}}{2}\right]=47981.74\left[175-\frac{215}{2}\right] \).

\( =3238.77 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

\( Z=\frac{1}{6} t w^{2}=\frac{1}{6}(90)(330)^{2}=1633.5 \times 10^{3} mm ^{3} \).

\( \left(M_{b}\right)_{t}=\left(\sigma_{b}\right)_{t} Z \quad \text { or }\).

\( 3238.77 \times 10^{3}=\left(\sigma_{b}\right)_{t} 1633.5 \times 10^{3} \).

\( \left(\sigma_{b}\right)_{t}=1.98 N / mm ^{2} \). (b).

The direct compressive stress due to radial component is given by,

\( \left(\sigma_{c}\right)_{d}=\frac{P_{r}}{w t}=\frac{52694.28}{(330)(90)}=1.77 N / mm ^{2} \) (c).

\( \text { The maximum compressive stress }\left(\sigma_{c}\right) \text { is given by, } \)

\( \sigma_{c}=\left(\sigma_{b}\right)_{r}+\left(\sigma_{b}\right)_{t}+\left(\sigma_{c}\right)_{d}=15.97+1.98+1.77 \).

\( =19.72 N / mm ^{2} \).

The torsional moment on the arm is given by,

\( M_{t}=P_{t}\left[0.75 l_{c}+0.5 t\right] \).

= 47 981.74[0.75(120) + 0.5(90)]

\( =6477.53 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

\( \tau=\frac{M_{t}}{Z_{p}}=\frac{4.5 M_{t}}{w t^{2}}=\frac{4.5\left(6477.53 \times 10^{3}\right)}{(330)(90)^{2}} \).

\( =10.9 N / mm ^{2} \).

The maximum compressive stress is given by,

\( \left(\sigma_{c}\right)_{\max }=\frac{\sigma_{c}}{2}+\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\left(\sigma_{c}\right)^{2}+4 \tau^{2}} \).

\( =\frac{(19.72)}{2}+\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{(19.72)^{2}+4(10.9)^{2}} \).

\( =24.56 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \left(\sigma_{c}\right)_{\max }<60 N / mm ^{2} \).

The value of \( \left(\sigma_{c}\right)_{\max } \). is less than the allowable
compressive stress and design is safe.

Step 5 Design of shaft at the juncture of crank web
Check for torsional shear stresses
Suppose,

\( d_{s 1}=\text { diameter of shaft at the juncture of the crank web (mm)} \).

\( d_{s 1}=d_{1}=215 mm \).

\( \left(M_{b}\right)_{v}=P_{r}\left[0.75 l_{c}+t\right] \).

\( =52694.28[0.75(120)+90] \).

\( =9484.97 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

\( \left(M_{b}\right)_{h}=P_{t}\left[0.75 l_{c}+t\right] \).

= 47 981.74[0.75(120) + 90]

\( =8636.71 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

\( \text { The resultant bending moment } M_{b} \text { is given by, } \)

\( M_{b}=\sqrt{\left[\left(M_{b}\right)_{v}\right]^{2}+\left[\left(M_{b}\right)_{h}\right]^{2}} \).

\( =\sqrt{\left(9484.97 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2}+\left(8636.71 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2}} \).

\( =12827.99 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

\( M_{t}=P_{t} \times r=47981.74 \times 175 \).

\( =8396.8 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

The torsional shear stress in shaft is calculated by the following expression,

\( \tau=\frac{16}{\pi d_{s 1}^{3}} \sqrt{\left(M_{b}\right)^{2}+\left(M_{t}\right)^{2}} \).

\( =\frac{16}{\pi(215)^{3}} \sqrt{\left(12827.99 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2}+\left(8396.8 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2}} \).

\( =7.86 N / mm ^{2} \quad \tau<40 N / mm ^{2} \).

The shear stress in the shaft is within permissible limits and the design is safe.

Step 6 Design of shaft under flywheel
Check for torsional shear stresses
Suppose,
\( d_{s}= \) diameter of shaft under flywheel (mm)
The central plane of the shaft is subjected to maximum bending moment. The bending moment in the vertical plane \( \left(M_{b}\right)_{v} \) is given by,

\( \left(M_{b}\right)_{v}=P_{r}\left(b+c_{1}\right)-\left[\left(R_{1}\right)_{v}+\left(R_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{v}\right] c_{1} \).

\( =52694.28(320+215) \) \( -[91908.63+5000](215) \).

\( =7.36 \times 10^{6} N – mm \).

The bending moment in the horizontal plane \( \left(M_{b}\right)_{h} \text { is given by, } \)

\( \left(M_{b}\right)_{h}=P_{t}\left(b+c_{1}\right)-\left[\left(R_{1}\right)_{h}+\left(R_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{h}\right] c_{1} \).

\( =47981.74(320+215) \) \( -[83689.08+2500](215) \)

\( =7.14 \times 10^{6} N – mm \).

\( \text { The resultant bending moment } M_{b} \text { is given by, } \)

\( M_{b}=\sqrt{\left[\left(M_{b}\right)_{v}\right]^{2}+\left[\left(M_{b}\right)_{h}\right]^{2}} \).

\( =\sqrt{\left[7.36 \times 10^{6}\right]^{2}+\left[7.14 \times 10^{6}\right]^{2}} \).

\( =10.25 \times 10^{6} N – mm \).

The shaft is also subjected to torsional moment \( M_{t} \text { due to tangential component } P_{t} \text {. } \)

\( M_{t}=P_{t} \times r=47981.74(175) \).

\( =8.4 \times 10^{6} N – mm \).

The torsional shear stress in the shaft is calculated by the following equation:

\( \tau=\frac{16}{\pi d_{s}^{3}} \sqrt{\left(M_{b}\right)^{2}+\left(M_{t}\right)^{2}} \).

\( =\frac{16}{\pi(250)^{3}} \sqrt{\left(10.25 \times 10^{6}\right)^{2}+\left(8.4 \times 10^{6}\right)^{2}} \).

\( =4.32 N / mm ^{2} \quad \tau<40 N / mm ^{2} \).

The shear stress in the shaft is within permissible limit and the design is safe.
