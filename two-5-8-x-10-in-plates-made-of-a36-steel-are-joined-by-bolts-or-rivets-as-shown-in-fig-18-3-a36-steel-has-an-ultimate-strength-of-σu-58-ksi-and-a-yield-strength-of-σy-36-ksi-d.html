Guest Question.

Two 5/8 × 10-in. plates made of A36 steel are joined by bolts or rivets as shown in Fig. 18.3. (A36 steel has an ultimate strength of \(σ_{u} = 58\ ksi\) and a yield strength of \(σ_{y} = 36\ ksi\).) Determine the maximum allowable load on the plates if the bolts or rivets are as follows:

a. 7/8-in. A502 Grade 1 rivets

b. 7/8-in. A325-N bolts

Admin
Solution

a. 7/8-in.-diameter A502 Grade 1 rivets

Tensile Capacity: Based on the gross area: The allowable tensile stress \(σ_{t} = 0.6σ_{y}\) = 0.6(36) = 21.6 ksi and the gross area \(A_{g} = (5/8)(10) = 6.25 in.^{2}\); thus

\(P_{all} = σ_{t}A_{g} = 21.6(6.25) = 135.0\ kip\)

Based on the net area: The allowable tensile stress \(σ_{t} = 0.5σ_{u}\)= 29.0 ksi and the net area

\(A_{n} = [10 – 3(7/8 + 1/8)4(5/8)] = 4.375 in.^{2}\)

Thus,

\(P_{all} = σ_{t}A_{n} = 29.0(4.375) = 126.9\ kip\)

Shear Capacity: One 7/8-in. A502 Grade 1 rivet in single shear has an allowable shear stress τ = 17.5 ksi (Table 18.1) and a shear area \(A_{rivet} = πd^{2}/4 = π(7/8)^{2}/4 = 0.601 in.^{2}\); therefore, the allowable load for twelve rivets in single shear

\(P_{all} = τA_{rivet}N = 17.5(0.601)(12) = 126.2\ kip\)

 

Bearing Capacity: From Eqs. (18.3), (18.4), and (18.5) the bearing stress for one 7/8-in. rivet is given by the smallest of the following values:

\(\sigma _{b}=\frac{\sigma_{u}l_{e}}{2d}=\frac{58(2)} {2(0.875)}=66.3\ ksi\)

 

\(\sigma_{b}=\frac{\sigma_{u}}{2}\left(\frac{s}{d}-0.5 \right)=\frac{58}{2}\left(\frac{3}{0.875}-0.5 \right)=84.9\ ksi\)

 

\(σ_{b} ≤ 1.5 σ_{u} = 1.5(58) = 87\ ksi\)

The area in bearing for one 7/8-in. rivet \(A_{b} = dt = (7/8)(5/8) = 0.547 in.^{2}\); therefore, the allowable load for 12 rivets in bearing

\(P_{all} = σ_{b}A_{b}N = 66.3(0.547)(12) = 434.2\ kip\)

The smallest allowable load is in shear. Accordingly,

\(P_{max} = P_{all}(shear) = 126.2\ kip\)

b. 7/8-in.-diameter A325-N bolts. The tensile and bearing capacity is unchanged from part (a).

Shear Capacity: One 7/8-in. A325-N bolt in single shear has an allowable shear stress τ = 21.0 ksi (Table 18.1) and the shear area \(A_{bolt} = πd^{2}/4 = 0.601 in.^{2}\); therefore, the allowable load for 12 bolts in single shear

\(P_{all} = τA_{bolt}N = 21.0(0.601)(12) = 151.4\ kip\)
