Guest Question.

A magnetic field strength of 5 μA/m is required at a point on θ = π/2, which is 2 km from an antenna in air. Neglecting ohmic loss, how much power must the antenna transmit if it is

(a) A Hertzian dipole of length λ/25?

(b) A half-wave dipole?

(c) A quarter-wave monopole?

(d) A 10-turn loop antenna of radius \(\rho _{o}=\lambda /20\)?

Admin
Solution

(a) For a Hertzian dipole

\(|H_{\phi s}|=\frac{I_{o}\beta dl\sin \theta }{4\pi r}\)

where \(dl=\lambda /25\) or

\(\beta dl=\frac{2\pi }{\lambda}\cdot \frac{\lambda}{25}=\frac{2\pi}{25}\)

Hence

\(5\times 10^{-6}=\frac{I_{o}\cdot\frac{2\pi}{25}(1)}{4\pi(2\times 10^{3})}=\frac{I_{o}}{10^{5}}\)

or

\(I_{o}=0.5A\)

\(P_{rad}=40\pi^{2}\left[\frac{dl^{2}}{\lambda}\right]I_{o}^{2}=\frac{40\pi^{2}(0.5)^{2}}{(25)^{2}}=158mW\)

(b) For a \(\lambda /2\) dipole,

\(|H_{\phi s}|=\frac{I_{o}\cos\left(\frac{\pi }{2}\cos\theta \right)}{2\pi r\sin\theta }\)

\(5\times 10^{-6}=\frac{I_{o}\cdot 1}{2\pi(2\times 10^{3})\cdot (1)}\)

or

\(I_{o}=20\pi mA\)

\(P_{rad}=\frac{1}{2}I_{o}^{2}R_{rad}=\frac{1}{2}(20\pi)^{2}\times 10^{-6}(73)=144mW\)

(c) For a \(\lambda /4\) monopole,

\(I_{o}=20\pi mA\)

as in part (b).

\(P_{rad}=\frac{1}{2}I_{o}^{2}R_{rad}=\frac{1}{2}(20\pi)^{2}\times 10^{-6}(36.56)=72mW\)

(d) For a loop antenna

\(|H_{\theta s}|=\frac{\pi I_{o}}{r}\frac{S}{\lambda ^{2}}\sin\theta\)

For a single turn \(S=\pi \rho _{o}^{2}\) For \(N-turn, S=N\pi \rho _{o}^{2}\).

Hence

\(5\times 10^{-6}=\frac{\pi I_{o}10\pi }{2\times 10^{3}} =\left[\frac{\rho _{o}}{\lambda } \right]^{2}\)

or

\(I_{o}=\frac{10}{10\pi ^{2}}\left[\frac{\lambda}{\rho _{o}}\right]^{2}\times10^{-3}=\frac{20^{2}}{\pi ^{2}}\times 10^{-3} =40.53mA\)

\(R_{rad}=\frac{320\pi ^{4}S^{2}}{\lambda ^{4}}=320\pi ^{6}N^{2}\left[\frac{\rho_{o}}{\lambda }\right]^{4}=320\pi ^{6}\times100\left[\frac{1}{20}\right]^{4}=192.3\Omega\)

\(P_{rad}=\frac{1}{2}I_{o}^{2}R_{rad}=\frac{1}{2}(40.53)^{2}\times10^{-6}(192.3)=158mW\)
