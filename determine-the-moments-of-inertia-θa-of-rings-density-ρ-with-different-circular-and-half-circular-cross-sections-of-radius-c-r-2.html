Guest Question.

Determine the moments of inertia \(\Theta_{a}\) of rings (density \(\rho \) ) with different circular and half-circular cross sections of radius \(c = R/2\) .

Admin
Solution

The moment of inertia is defined as \(\int{r^{2} dm}\) . We first choose an appropriate ring-shaped mass element of radius \(r\) and thickness \(dr\). From the sketch follows

\(dm=\rho 2\pi r2c \cos \varphi dr \) ,

\(r=R+c \sin \varphi\) ,

\(dr = c\cos \varphi d\varphi\) ,

\(\rightsquigarrow dm=4\pi \rho c^{2}\left(R+c \sin \varphi \right)\cos^{2} \varphi d\varphi \) .

Depending on the cross section, we must integrate over different domains:

\(\Theta_{a}=4\pi \rho c^{2}\int_{\varphi _{1} }^{\varphi _{2} }{\left(R+c\sin \varphi \right)^{3}\cos^{2} \varphi d\varphi }\)

 

\(=4\pi \rho c^{2}\int_{\varphi _{1} }^{\varphi _{2} }{\left[R^{3}\cos ^{2}\varphi +3R^{2}c\sin \varphi \cos ^{2} \varphi +3Rc^{2}\sin ^{2}\varphi \cos ^{2}\varphi +c^{3} \sin ^{3} \varphi \cos ^{2}\varphi \right]d\varphi }\)

 

=\(4\pi\rho c^{2}\left[R^{3}\frac{1}{4}\left(2\varphi +\sin 2\varphi \right)-R^{2}c\cos^{3}\varphi +3Rc^{2}\left(\frac{1}{8}\varphi -\frac{1}{32}\sin4\varphi\right)+c^{3}\left(-\frac{1}{3}\cos^{3}\varphi +\frac{1}{5} \cos ^{5}\varphi\right) \right]^{\varphi_{2} }_{\varphi _{1}}\).

In case \(A\) , we obtain with \(\varphi _{1}=-\pi /2\) , \(\varphi _{2} =+\pi /2\) , \(m_{A}=2\pi ^{2} \rho c^{2}R\)

 

\(\underline{\underline{\Theta^{A}_{a} } }=4\pi \rho c^{2}\left(\frac{\pi }{2}R^{3}+\frac{3\pi }{8}Rc^{2} \right)=\underline{\underline{m_{A}\left(R^{2}+\frac{3}{4}c^{2} \right)=1.19m_{A}R^{2} } } \) .

Case \(B\) with \( \varphi_{1}=0 \) , \(\varphi _{2}=+\pi /2 \) , \(m_{B}=\pi ^{2} \rho c^{2}\left(R+4c/\left(3\pi \right) \right) \) (note: \(m_{B}\neq m_{A}/2! \) ) leads to

\(\underline{\underline{\Theta ^{B}_{a} } } =4\pi \rho c^{2}\left(\frac{\pi }{4}R^{3}+R^{2}c+\frac{3\pi }{16}Rc^{2}+\frac{2 }{15}c^{3}\right) =\underline{\underline{1.52m_{B}R^{2} } } \) .

Finally, in case \(C\) follows for symmetry reasons (\( m_{C}=m_{A}/2 \) ))

\(\underline{\underline{\Theta ^{C}_{a}=\Theta ^{A}/2=m_{C}\left(R^{2}+\frac{3}{4}c^{2} \right)=1.19m_{C}R^{2} } } \) .
