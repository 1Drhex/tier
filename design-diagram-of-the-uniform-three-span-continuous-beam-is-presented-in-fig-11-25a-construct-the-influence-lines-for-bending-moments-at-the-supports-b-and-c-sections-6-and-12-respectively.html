Guest Question.

Design diagram of the uniform three-span continuous beam is presented in Fig. 11.25a. Construct the influence lines for bending moments at the supports B and C (sections 6 and 12, respectively).

Admin
Solution

Each span of the beam is divided in equal portions and specified sections are numerated (0–18). Next we need to show the displacement-load (Z-P) and S-e diagrams (Fig. 11.25a). Unknown moments \(S_1, S_2\) arise at support B and \(S_3, S_4\) at support C.

Static matrix The Z-P and S-e diagrams allow us to constructing the following equilibrium equations:

\( P_1 =S_1 +S_2\)

 

\( P_2 =S_3 +S_4\)

so the static matrix of the structure is

\( A_{(2\times 4)}= \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

Stiffness matrix Stiffness matrices for each finite element are

\(k_1= \frac{EI}{l}[3], k_2 =\frac{EI}{l}\left [ \begin{matrix} 4 & 2 \\ 2 & 4 \end{matrix} \right ], k_3= \frac{EI}{l} [3].\)

Stiffness matrix of all structure in local coordinates and intermediate complex \(\tilde{k}A^T\) are

\(\tilde{k}=\frac{EI}{l} \left [ \begin{matrix} 3 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 4 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 4 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 3 \end{matrix} \right ], \tilde{k}A^T=\frac{EI}{l} \left [ \begin{matrix} 3 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 4 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 4 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 3 \end{matrix} \right ]\cdot \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ]= \frac{EI}{l} \left [ \begin{matrix} 3& 0 \\ 4 & 2 \\ 2 & 4\\ 0 & 3 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

The stiffness matrix of all structure in global coordinates and inverse stiffness matrix are

\(K =A\cdot \tilde{k}A^T= \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\frac{EI}{l}\left [ \begin{matrix} 3 & 0 \\ 4 & 2 \\ 2 & 4 \\ 0 & 3 \end{matrix} \right ] = \frac{EI}{l} \left [ \begin{matrix} 7 & 2 \\ 2 & 7 \end{matrix} \right ],\)

 

\(K^{-1}= \frac{l}{45EI}\left [ \begin{matrix} 7 & -2 \\ – 2 & 7 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

To construct the matrices P and \(S_1\) we need to consider the unit moving load in all spans separately. Next for each loading we need to construct the bending moment diagram in primary system of displacement method and show the corresponding Joint-load diagram.

Load P =1 in the first span The bending moment diagram in the primary system and equivalent joint-load diagram are shown in Fig. 11.25b.

On the basis of the joint-load and Z-P diagrams, the vector P of the external joint loads for any position u of unit load P in the first span is

\(\vec{P}=l\left\lceil\begin{matrix} -0.5u (1 – u^2) \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right\rceil\)

On the basis of the \(M^0_P\) and S-e diagrams the vector \(S_1\) of unknown internal force \(S_1 – S_4\) in the first state for any position P becomes

\( \vec{S}_1= l \left\lceil \begin{matrix} 0.5u(1 -u^2) \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right\rceil\)

Now we can determine the entries of both of these matrices when moving load P is placed at the sections 2 and 4 and perform corresponding matrix procedures.

P = 1 at the section 2 (u = 0.333, υ = 0.667) P = 1 at the section 4 (u = 0.667, υ = 0.333)
\(\vec{P}= l \left\lceil \begin{matrix} -0.1480 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right\rceil,\vec{S}_1\left\lceil \begin{matrix} M_1 \\ M_2 \\ M_3 \\ M_4 \end{matrix} \right\rceil_1 =l\left\lceil \begin{matrix} 0.1480 \\ 0 \\ 0\\ 0 \end{matrix} \right\rceil\) \(\vec{P}= l \left\lceil \begin{matrix} -0.1851 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right\rceil,\vec{S}_1\left\lceil \begin{matrix} M_1 \\ M_2 \\ M_3 \\ M_4 \end{matrix} \right\rceil_1 =l\left\lceil \begin{matrix} 0.1851 \\ 0 \\ 0\\ 0 \end{matrix} \right\rceil \)
Matrix procedures
\(\vec{Z}= \left\lceil \begin{matrix} Z_1 \\ Z_2\end{matrix} \right\rceil= K^{-1}\vec{P}=\frac{l}{45EI} \left [ \begin{matrix} 7 & – 2 \\ – 2 & 7 \end{matrix} \right ]\times l\left\lceil\begin{matrix} -0.1480 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right\rceil= \frac{l^2}{304.05EI}\left\lceil\begin{matrix} -7 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right\rceil\) \(\vec{Z}= \left\lceil \begin{matrix} Z_1 \\ Z_2\end{matrix} \right\rceil= K^{-1}\vec{P}=\frac{l}{45EI} \left [ \begin{matrix} 7 & – 2 \\ – 2 & 7 \end{matrix} \right ]\times l\left\lceil\begin{matrix} -0.1851 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right\rceil= \frac{l^2}{243.11EI}\left\lceil\begin{matrix} -7 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right\rceil \)
\(\vec{S}_2=\left\lceil \begin{matrix} M_1 \\ M_2 \\ M_3 \\ M_4 \end{matrix} \right\rceil_2=\tilde{k}A^T.\vec{Z}=\frac{EI}{l} \left [ \begin{matrix} 3 & 0 \\ 4 & 2 \\ 2 & 4 \\ 0 & 3 \end{matrix} \right ]\times \frac{l^2}{304.05EI}\left\lceil\begin{matrix} -7 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right\rceil =l\left\lceil \begin{matrix} – 0.06907 \\ – 0.07893 \\ -0.01973 \\ 0.01973 \end{matrix} \right\rceil \) \(\vec{S}_2=\left\lceil \begin{matrix} M_1 \\ M_2 \\ M_3 \\ M_4 \end{matrix} \right\rceil_2=\tilde{k}A^T.\vec{Z}=\frac{EI}{l} \left [ \begin{matrix} 3 & 0 \\ 4 & 2 \\ 2 & 4 \\ 0 & 3 \end{matrix} \right ]\times \frac{l^2}{243.11EI}\left\lceil\begin{matrix} -7 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right\rceil =l\left\lceil \begin{matrix} – 0.08638 \\ – 0.09872 \\ -0.02468 \\ 0.02468 \end{matrix} \right\rceil\)
Bending moment at the sections 6 and 12 \( \vec{S}= \vec{S}_1 + \vec{S}_2\)
\(\vec{S}=l\left\lceil \begin{matrix} 0.1480 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{matrix} \right\rceil+l \left\lceil\begin{matrix} -0.06907 \\ -0.07893 \\ -0.01973 \\ 0.01973 \end{matrix}\right\rceil =l \left\lceil \begin{matrix}0.07893 \\ – 0.07893 \\ -0.01973 \\ 0.01973 \end{matrix} \right\rceil\) \(\vec{S}=l\left\lceil \begin{matrix} 0.1851 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{matrix} \right\rceil+l \left\lceil\begin{matrix} -0.08638 \\ -0.09872 \\ -0.02468 \\ 0.02468 \end{matrix}\right\rceil = l\left\lceil \begin{matrix}0.09872 \\ – 0.09872 \\ -0.02468 \\ 0.02468 \end{matrix} \right\rceil\)

The signs of the bending moments should be treated according to S-e diagram and general rules of the bending moments. If the load P = 1 is placed at point 2, then ordinates of influence lines for bending moments at sections 6(B) and 2(C) are given in Fig. 11.25c.

If the load P = 1 is placed at point 4, then ordinates of influence lines for bending moments at the same sections are following: \(M^B_4 =- 0.09872l; M^C_4= 0.02468l.\)

Load P = 1 in the second span The bending moment diagram in the primary system of displacement method and equivalent joint-load diagram are shown in Fig. 11.25d.

Now for any position u of unit load P in the second span, we can compile the vector P of the external joint loads (using the J-L and Z-P diagrams) and vector \(S_1\) of unknown internal forces in the first state (using the \(M^0_P\) and S-e diagrams).

They are

\( \vec{P}=l\left\lceil\begin{matrix} uυ^2 \\ -u^2υ \end{matrix} \right\rceil,\vec{S}_1=l\left\lceil \begin{matrix} 0 \\ -uυ^2 \\ u^2υ \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right\rceil\)

After that we will determine the entries of these both matrices when moving load P is placed at the sections 8 and 10 and perform corresponding matrix procedures.

Load P = 1 in the third span This case can be considered elementary by taking into account the symmetry of the beam and loading in the first span. The final influence lines for moments at the supports B and C are shown in Fig. 11.25e. If load P = 1 is placed at sections with odd numbers 1, 3, . . . then ordinates of influence lines \(M_B\) and \(M_C\) may be computed by similar way.

It is obvious that it is possible to expand the number of unknown forces at the ends of the element and consider the displacements at the ends. For example, we can consider four components for unknown forces (not only the bending moments, but the reactions also) and four components for end displacements (not only the angular displacements, but the linear displacements too). According Table A.4, the force–displacement relationships can be presented in the following form

\(\left\lceil \begin{matrix} R_1 \\ M_{1} \\ R_{2} \\ M_{2} \end{matrix} \right\rceil=\underbrace{\frac{2EI}{l} \left [ \begin{matrix} 6/l^2 & 3/l & -6/l^2 & 3/l \\ 3/l & 2 & -3/l & 1 \\ -6/l^2 & -3/l & 6/l^2 & -3/l \\ 3/l & 1 & -3/l & 2 \end{matrix} \right ] }_{K_{f -f}} \cdot \left\lceil \begin{matrix} \Delta _1 \\ \varphi _1 \\ \Delta _2 \\ \varphi _2 \end{matrix} \right\rceil\)

 

In this case we need to use for analysis the (4 × 4) – stiffness matrix. Similarly for pinned-fixed member the stiffness matrix becomes

\(K_{P -f} = \frac{3EI}{l} \left [ \begin{matrix} 1/l^2 & -1/l^2 & 1/l \\ -1/l^2 & 1/l^2 & -1/l \\ 1/l & -1/l & 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

It is possible to expand more the number of internal forces at the ends considering also the axial forces (and corresponding axial displacements). It is obvious that dimension of all initial and intermediate matrices become very large. So in this chapter the adopted stiffness matrix should be considered as a truncated matrix. Such form leads to the short and readily available for visual analysis of matrix procedures.
