Guest Question.

The single-threaded screw of the vise has a mean diameter of 1 in. and has 5 square threads per inch. The coefficient of static friction in the threads is 0.20. A 60-lb pull applied normal to the handle at A produces a clamping force of 1000 lb between the jaws of the vise. (a) Determine the frictional moment \(M_{B}\), developed at B, due to the thrust of the screw against the body of the jaw. (b) Determine the force Q applied normal to the handle at A required to loosen the vise.

Admin
Solution

From the free-body diagram of the jaw we first obtain the tension T in the screw

\([\sum{M_{c} }=0 ] 1000(16)-10T=0 T=1600 Ib\)

 

The helix angle \(\alpha\) and the friction angle \(\phi \) for the thread are given by

\(\alpha =\tan ^{-1} \frac{L}{2\pi r} =\tan ^{-1}\frac{1/5}{2\pi (0.5)} =3.64^{\circ } \)

 

\(\phi =\tan ^{-1}\mu =\tan ^{-1}0.20=11.31^{\circ } \)

where the mean radius of the thread is \(r = 0.5 in\).

(a) To tighten. The isolated screw is simulated by the free-body diagram shown where all of the forces acting on the threads of the screw are represented by a single force R inclined at the friction angle \(\phi \) from the normal to the thread.
The moment applied about the screw axis is \(60(8) = 480 lb-in\). in the clockwise direction as seen from the front of the vise. The frictional moment \(M_{B}\) due to the friction forces acting on the collar at B is in the counterclockwise direction to oppose the impending motion. From Eq. 6/3 \(M=W_{r} \tan (\alpha +\phi ) \) with T substituted for W the net moment acting on the screw is

 

\(M=Tr \tan (\alpha +\phi ) \)

\(480-M_{B} =1600(0.5)\tan (3.64^{\circ }+ 11.31^{\circ } ) \)

\(MB = 266 lb-in.\)

 

(b) To loosen. The free-body diagram of the screw on the verge of being loosened is shown with R acting at the friction angle from the normal in the direction to counteract the impending motion. Also shown is the frictional moment \(M_{B} = 266 lb-in\). acting in the clockwise direction to oppose the motion. The angle between R and the screw axis is now \(\phi -\alpha\) , and we use Eq. 6/3a with the net moment equal to the applied moment \(M^{\prime } \) minus \(M_{B} \). Thus

\(M=Tr \tan (\alpha +\phi ) \)

\(M^{\prime } -266=1600(0.5)\tan (11.31^{\circ }- 3.64^{\circ }) \)

\(M^{\prime }=374 Ib-in\)

Thus, the force on the handle required to loosen the vise is

\(Q=M^{\prime }/d=374/8=46.8 lb\)

 

Helpful Hints
1) Be careful to calculate the helix angle correctly. Its tangent is the lead L (advancement per revolution) divided by the mean circumference \(2πr\) and not by the diameter \(2r\).
2) Note that R swings to the opposite side of the normal as the impending motion reverses direction.
