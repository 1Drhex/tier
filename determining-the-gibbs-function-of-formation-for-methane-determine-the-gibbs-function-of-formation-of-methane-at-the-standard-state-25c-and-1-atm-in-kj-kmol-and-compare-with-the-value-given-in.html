Determining the Gibbs Function of Formation for Methane

Determine the Gibbs function of formation of methane at the standard state, 25°C and 1 atm, in kJ/kmol, and compare with the value given in .

Admin
Solution

Known The compound is methane.

Find Determine the Gibbs function of formation at the standard state, in kJ/kmol, and compare with the value.

Assumptions In the formation of methane from carbon and
hydrogen \(\left( H _{2}\right)\), the carbon and hydrogen are each initially at 25°C and 1 atm. The methane formed is also at 25°C and 1 atm.

Analysis Methane is formed from carbon and hydrogen according to \(C +2 H _{2} \rightarrow CH _{4}\). The change in the Gibbs function for this reaction is

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{g}_{ P }-\bar{g}_{ R } &=(\bar{h}-T \bar{s})_{ CH _{4}}-(\bar{h}-T \bar{s})_{ C }-2(\bar{h}-T \bar{s})_{ H _{2}} \\&=\left(\bar{h}_{ CH _{4}}-\bar{h}_{ C }-2 \bar{h}_{ H _{2}}\right)-T\left(\bar{s}_{ CH _{4}}-\bar{s}_{ C }-2 \bar{s}_{ H _{2}}\right)\end{aligned}\) (1)

 

where \(\bar{g}_{ P } \text { and } \bar{g}_{ R }\) denote, respectively, the Gibbs functions of the products and reactants, each per kmol of methane.

In the present case, all substances are at the same temperature and pressure, 25°C and 1 atm, which correspond to the standard reference state values. At the standard reference state, the enthalpies and Gibbs functions for carbon and hydrogen are zero by definition.

Thus, in Eq. (1), \(\bar{g}_{ R }=\bar{h}_{ C }=\bar{h}_{ H _{2}}=0 . \text { Also, } \bar{g}_{ P }=\left(\bar{g}_{ f }^{\circ}\right)_{ CH _{4}}\). Eq. (1) then reads

 

\(\left(\bar{g}_{ f }^{\circ}\right)_{ CH _{4}}=\left(\bar{h}_{ f }^{\circ}\right)_{ CH _{4}}-T_{ ref }\left(\bar{s}_{ CH _{4}}^{\circ}-\bar{s}_{ C }^{\circ}-2 \bar{s}_{ H _{2}}^{\circ}\right)\) (2)

 

where all properties are at \(T_{\text {ref }}, p_{\text {ref }}\). With enthalpy of formation and absolute entropy data from , Eq. (2) gives

 

\(\begin{aligned}\left(\bar{g}_{ f }^{\circ}\right)_{ CH _{4}} &=-74,850-298.15[186.16-5.74-2(130.57)] \\&=-50,783 kJ / kmol\end{aligned}\)

 

The slight difference between the calculated value for the Gibbs function of formation of methane and the value from can be attributed to round-off.
