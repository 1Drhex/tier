Guest Question.

Solve the problem given in by A.W. method.

Admin
Solution

Procedure 1: The N.P.W. has already been calculated in and its value is ₹2,20,160. Considering ₹2,20,160 as P, its A.W. is calculated by using Equation (5.3) as:

\(A.W.=P\left(A/P,i\% ,n\right)\)

 

\(A.W.=₹2,20,160\left(A/P,15\% ,5\right)\)

 

\(=₹2,20,160\left(0.2983\right)\)

 

\(A.W.=₹65,673.73\)

Since \(A.W. \gt 0\), this equipment is economically justified.

Procedure 2: The N.F.W. has already been calculated in and its value is ₹4,42,780. Considering ₹4,42,780 as F, its A.W. is calculated by using Equation (5.4) as:

\(A.W.=F\left(A/F,i\% ,n\right)\)

 

\(A.W.=₹4,42,780\left(A/F,15\% ,5\right)\)

 

\(=₹4,42,780\left(0.1483\right)\)

 

\(A.W.=₹65,664.27\)

Since \(A.W. \gt 0\), this equipment is economically justified.

Procedure 3:

Step 1: The cash flow diagram for the given problem is shown in Fig. 5.1.
Step 2: The P.W. of the given series of cash receipts has already been calculated in and its value is ₹7,20,160.

Step 3: Consider ₹7,20,160 as present worth P and determine its A.W. using Equation (5.3) as:

\(A.W.=P\left(A/P,i\% ,n\right)\)

 

\(A.W.=₹7,20,160\left(A/P,15\% ,5\right)\)

 

\(=₹7,20,160\left(0.2983\right)\)

 

\(A.W.=₹2,14,823.7\)

Step 4: The P.W. of the given series of cash disbursements has already been calculated in and its value is ₹5,00,000.
Step 5: Consider ₹5,00,000 as present worth P and determine its A.W. using Equation (5.3)

\(A.W.=P\left(A/P,i\% ,n\right)\)

 

\(A.W.=₹5,00,000\left(A/P,15\% ,5\right)\)

 

\(=₹5,00,000\left(0.2983\right)\)

 

\(A.W.=₹1,49,150\)

Step 6: Determine the annual worth A.W. as:

\(A.W.=A.W. at Step 3 – A.W. at Step 5\)

 

\(=₹2,14,823.7-₹1,49,150\)

 

\(A.W.=₹65,673.7\)

Since \(A.W. \gt 0\), this equipment is economically justified.

Procedure 4:

Step 1: The cash flow diagram for the given problem is shown in Fig. 5.1.
Step 2: The F.W. of the given series of cash receipts has already been calculated in and its value is ₹14,48,480.

Step 3: Consider ₹14,48,480 as future worth F and determine its A.W. using Equation (5.4) as:

\(A.W.=F\left(A/F,i\% ,n\right)\)

 

\(A.W.=₹14,48,480\left(A/F,15\% ,5\right)\)

 

\(=₹14,48,480\left(0.1483\right)\)

 

\(A.W.=₹2,14,809.6\)

Step 4: The F.W. of the given series of cash disbursements has already been calculated in and its value is ₹10,05,700.
Step 5: Consider ₹10,05,700 as future worth, F and determine its A.W. using Equation (5.4) as:

\(A.W.=F\left(A/F,i\% ,n\right)\)

 

\(A.W.=₹10,05,700\left(A/F,15\% ,5\right)\)

 

\(=₹10,05,700\left(0.1483\right)\)

 

\(A.W.=₹1,49,145.3\)

Step 6: Determine the annual worth A.W. as:

\(A.W.=A.W. at Step 3 – A.W. at Step 5\)

 

\(=₹2,14,809.6-₹1,49,145.3\)

 

\(A.W.=₹65,664.3\)

Since \(A.W. \gt 0\), this equipment is economically justified.

Procedure 5: The following steps are used to calculate A.W.:

Step 1: The cash flow diagram for the given problem is shown in Fig. 5.1.
Step 2: From the cash flow diagram shown in Fig. 5.1, it is clear that the equivalent annual receipts, \(R = ₹2,00,000\).

Step 3: From the cash flow diagram shown in Fig. 5.1, it is clear that the equivalent annual expenses, E = 0.

Step 4: From the cash flow diagram shown in Fig. 5.1, it is clear that the initial investment, P = ₹5,00,000 and salvage value, S = ₹1,00,000.
Step 5: The equivalent annual capital recovery amount (C.R.) is calculated by using the following formula:

\(C.R.=P\left(A/P,i\% ,n\right)-S\left(A/F,i\% ,n\right)\)

 

\(C.R.=₹5,00,000\left(A/P,15\% ,5\right)-₹1,00,000\left(A/F,15\% ,5\right)\)

 

\(=₹5,00,000\left(0.2983\right)-₹1,00,000\left(0.1483\right)\)

 

\(=₹1,49,150-₹14,830\)

 

\(C.R.=₹1,34,320\)

Step 6: A.W. is calculated as:

\(A.W.=R – E – C.R.\)

 

\(A.W.=₹2,00,000-₹0-₹1,34,320\)

 

\(A.W.=₹65,680\)

Since \(A.W. \gt 0\), this equipment is economically justified.

Note: It may be noted that the small difference in the values of A.W. obtained from the above procedures is due to rounding-off the values of factors in the interest table. However, the result by all methods is same.
