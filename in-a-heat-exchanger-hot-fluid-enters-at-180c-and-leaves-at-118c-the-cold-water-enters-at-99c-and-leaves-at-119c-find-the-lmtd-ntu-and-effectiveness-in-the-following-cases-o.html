Guest Question.

In a heat exchanger, hot fluid enters at 180°C and leaves at 118°C. The cold water enters at 99°C and leaves at 119°C. Find the LMTD, NTU and effectiveness in the following cases of heat exchanger :
(i) Counterflow,
(ii) One shell pass and multiple tube passes,
(iii) Two shell passes and multiple tube passes,
(iv) Cross flow both fluids unmixed, and
(v) Cross flow, the cold fluid unmixed.

Admin
Solution

Given : A heat exchanger in various modes with
\(T_{h, i} = 180°C T_{c, i} = 99°C\)
\(T_{h, 0} = 118°C T_{c, o}= 119°C\)
To find :
(a) LMTD,
(b) effectiveness and
(c) NTU, for
(i) Counterflow heat exchanger,
(ii) One shell pass and multiple tube passes,
(iii) Two shell passes and multiple tube passes
(iv) Cross flow heat exchanger with both fluids unmixed, and
(v) Cross flow heat exchanger with cold fluid unmixed.
Assumption : Hot fluid flows through shell side and cold fluid through tubes.
Analysis : (i) Counterflow heat exchanger
(a) LMTD : \(∆T_1 = T_{h, i} – T_{c, o} = 180 – 119 = 61°C\)
\(∆T_2 =T_{h, o} – T_{c, i} = 118 – 99 = 19°C\)
\(∆T_{lm, counter }= \frac{∆T_1 – ∆T_2}{ln\left(\frac{∆T_1}{∆T_2} \right)}= \frac{61 – 19}{ln\left(\frac{61}{19} \right)} = 47.45°C\)
= 36°C.
(b) Effectiveness : Making energy balance on heat exchanger
\(\dot{m}_c C_{p , c} (T_{c, o} – T_{c, i}) =\dot{m}_h C_{p, h} (T_{h, i} – T_{h, o})\)
or \(C_c (119 – 99) = C_h(180 – 118)\)
or \(\frac{C_h}{C_c}=\frac{ 20}{62}=0.322\)
It indicates that the hot fluid has lower heat capacity rate, the effectiveness can be obtained as :
\(ε_{εcounter} = \frac{T_{h, i} – T_{h, o}}{T_{h, i} – T_{c, i}} =\frac{180 – 118}{180 – 199}=0.765\)
(c) NTU :
For C = 0.322 and ε = 0.765 from Fig. 14.31
NTU = 1.72.
(ii) One shell pass and multiple tube pass heat exchanger:
(a) LMTD :\( ∆Tlm = F ∆T_{lm, counter}\) From Fig. 14.15(a)
\(P =\frac{t_2 − t_1}{ T_1 − t_1}=\frac{119 – 99}{ 180 − 99}=0.25\)
\(R =\frac{T_1 – T_2}{ t_2 − t_1}=\frac{180 – 118}{ 119 − 99}=3.1\)
It gives F = 0.88, thus
\(∆T_{lm} = 0.88 × 36 = 31.68°C. \)
(b) Effectiveness : Since hot fluid has lower heat capacity rate, thus the effectiveness can be obtained as :
\(ε = \frac{T_{h, i} – T_{h, o}}{T_{h, i} – T_{c, i}} =\frac{180 – 118}{180 – 199}=0.765\)
(c) NTU :
From Fig. 14.34 , for C = 0.322 and ε = 0.765,
NTU = 2.3.
(iii) Two shell passes and multiple tube passes heat exchanger :
(a) LMTD : \(∆T_{lm} = F ∆T_{lm, counter}\)
For P = 0.25, and R = 3.1, from Fig. 14.15(b)
F = 0.97
\(∆T_{lm} = 0.97 × 36 = 34.92°C.\)
(b) Effectiveness : Since operating temperatures and heat capacity rates are same, thus effectiveness will not change, i.e.,
ε = 0.765.
(c) NTU :
For C = 0.322 and ε = 0.765, from Fig. 14.35,
NTU = 1.7.
(iv) Cross flow heat exchanger with both fluid unmixed :
(a) LMTD \(: ∆T_{lm} = F ∆T_{lm, counter}\)
For P = 0.25, and R = 3.1, from Fig. 14.15 (c), the correction factor F = 0.93
\(∴ ∆T_{lm} = 0.93 × 36 = 33.5°C.\)
(b) Effectiveness : It remains same ε = 0.765.
(c) NTU : For C = 0.322 and ε = 0.765, from Fig. 14.32
NTU = 2.0.
(v) Cross flow heat exchanger with cold fluid unmixed :
(a) LMTD \(: ∆T_{lm} = F ∆T_{lm, counter}\)
For P = 0.25, R = 3.1, from Fig. 14.15(d), the correction factor
F = 0.92
\(∆T_{lm} = 0.92 × 36 = 33.12°C.\)
(b) Effectiveness : It remains same i.e., ε = 0.765.
(c) NTU : For C = 0.322 and ε = 0.765, from Fig. 14.33(d)
NTU = 2.4.
