Guest Question.

Calculation of a Phosphate Solution Cooler. 20,160 lb/hr of a 30% \(K_3PO_4\) solution, specific gravity at 120 °F = 1.30, is to be cooled from 150 to 90 °F using well water from 68 to 90 °F. Pressure drops of 10 psi are allowable on both streams, and a total dirt factor of 0.002 is required.

Available for this service is a 10.02 in. ID 1–2 exchanger having 52¾ in. OD, 16 BWG tubes 16’0” long laid out on 1-in. square pitch. The bundle is arranged for two passes, and the baffles are spaced 2 in. apart. Will the exchanger be suitable?

Admin
Solution

Shell-Side

ID = 10.02 in

Baffle space = 2 in

 

Passes = 1

 Tube-Side

Number and length = 52 tubes, 16’0”

OD, BWG, pitch = ¾ in, 18 BWG, 1-in square pitch

Passes = 2

 

(1) Heat Balance:

Hot \(K_3PO_4\) solution, \(T_{mean}=\frac{150+90}{2}\) = 120°F

\(C_{K_3PO_4}\) = 0.19 Btu/(lb · °F) at 120 °F

\(C_{H_2O}\) ≈ 1.0 Btu/(lb · °F) at 120 °F

Therefore, \(C = X_{k_3PO_4} ·C_{k_3PO_4} + X_{H_2O} ·C_{H_2O}\)

= (0.3)(0.19) + (0.7)(1.0)

= 0.757 Btu/(lb · °F)

Q = (20,160)(0.757)(150 – 90) = 915,000 Btu/hr

Cold Well Water, \(t_{mean}=\frac{90+68}{2}\) = 79°F

c ≈ 1.0 Btu/(lb · °F) at 79 °F

Q = (41,600)(1.0)(90 – 68) = 915,000 Btu/hr

(2) Δ\(T^*\):

\(LMTD=\frac{\Delta T_a-\Delta T_b}{\ln (\Delta T_a/\Delta T_b)}=\frac{60-22}{\ln (60/22)}=37.9°F\)

\(R=\frac{T_1-T_2}{t_2-t_1}=\frac{60}{22}=2.73\)

 

\(S=\frac{t_2-t_1}{T_1-t_1}=\frac{22}{150-68}=0.268\)

 

\(F_T=\left\lgroup\frac{\sqrt{R^2+1} }{R-1} \right\rgroup\frac{\ln \left[(1-P)/(1-RP)\right] }{\ln \left[(2-P(R+1-\sqrt{R^2+1}))/(2-P(R+1+\sqrt{R^2+1} ))\right] }\)

 

\(=\left\lgroup\frac{\sqrt{2.73^2+1} }{2.73-1} \right\rgroup ×\frac{\ln \left[(1-0.268)/(1-(2.73)(0.268))\right] }{\ln \left[(2-0.268(2.73+1-\sqrt{2.73^2+1} ))/(2-0.268(2.73+1+\sqrt{2.73^2+1}) )\right] }\)

= 0.81

Δ\(T^*\) = LMTD × \(F_T\) = 37.9 °F × 0.81 = 30.7 °F

(3) \(T_{avg} and t_{avg}\): Obtained in step (1).

 

Hot Fluid (Phosphate Solution) – Shell-Side (s)

(4) Flow Area, \(a_s\) :

\(a_s=\frac{ID\times C'B}{144P_T}\)

\(=\frac{(10.02)(0.25)(2)}{144(1)}=0.0347\ ft^2\)

 Cold Fluid (Water) – Tube-Side (t)

(4′) Flow Area, \(a_t\) :

\(a^{'}_t=0.302\ in^2\ \Leftarrow [Table AT.8]\)

 

\(a_t=\frac{N_ta^{'}_t}{144n}\)

\(=\frac{(52)(0.302)}{144(2)}=0.0545\ ft^2\)

 

(5) Mass Velocity, \(G_s\) :

\(G_s=\frac{\dot{M}}{a_s}=\frac{20,160}{0.0347}\)

\(=578,000lb/(hr\cdot ft^2)\)

 (5′) Mass Velocity, \(G_t\) :

\(G_t=\frac{\dot{m}}{a_t}=\frac{41,600}{0.0545} \)

\(=762,000lb/(hr\cdot ft^2)\)

 

(6) Compute \(D_e\) :

\(D_e=\frac{4P^2_T-\pi d^2_o}{\pi d_o}\)

 

\(=\frac{4(1^2)-\pi (0.75^2)}{\pi (0.75)}\)

 

\(=0.95\ in=\frac{0.95in}{12in/ft}=0.079\ ft\)

 

Obtain μ at \(T_{avg}\) :

μ = 2\(μ_{water}\) ⇐ for nonviscous salt sol.

\(μ_{water}\) = 0.60cP at 120°F

μ = 2(0.60) = 1.20cP

\(=1.20\ cP\left\lgroup\frac{2.42lb/(ft\cdot hr)}{1\ cP} \right\rgroup\)

= 2.90lb/(ft · hr)

 (6′) Obtain D from Table AT.8:

D = 0.62 in

\(=\frac{0.62\ in}{12\ in/ft}=0.0517\ ft\)

 

 

 

 

 

Obtain μ at \(t_{avg}:\)

μ = 0.91cP at 79°F

\(=0.91\ cP\left\lgroup\frac{2.42lb/(ft\cdot hr)}{1\ cP} \right\rgroup\)

= 2.20lb/(ft·hr)

 

(7) Reynolds Number, R\(e_s\) :

\(Re_s=\frac{D_eG_s}{\mu}\)

 

\(=\frac{(0.079)(578,000)}{2.90}=15,750\)

 (7′) Reynolds Number, \(Re_t\) :

\(Re_t=\frac{DG_t}{\mu}\)

 

\(=\frac{(0.0517)(762,000)}{2.20}=17,900\)

(\(Re_t\) is for pressure drop only)

 

(8) Obtain C and k at \(T_{avg}\) :

C = 0.757Btu/lb · °F at 120°F

k = 0.9\(k_{water}\) ⇐ for low conc.salt sol.

\(k_{water}\) = 0.37 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F/ft at 120°F

k = 0.9(0.37) = 0.33 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F/ft

 (8′) Obtain c and k at \(t_{avg}\) :

c = 1Btu/lb · °F at 79°F

k = 0.35Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F/ft at 79°F

 

(9) Solve for \(h_o\) and \(\phi_s\) at \(t_w\): (9′) Solve for \(h_i\) and \(\phi_t\) at \(t_w\) :
\(\phi_s and \phi_t\) can be assumed approximately equal to 1.0 as the viscosity of Newtonian fluids tend to be constant over a small temperature range. Therefore, the tube-wall temperature correction factor is not necessary in this case.
\(\phi _s=\left\lgroup\frac{\mu }{\mu _w} \right\rgroup^{0.14}\approx 1.0\) \(\phi _t=\left\lgroup\frac{\mu }{\mu _w} \right\rgroup^{0.14}\approx 1.0\)

(9) \(h_o=0.027\left\lgroup\frac{D_eG_s}{\mu}\right\rgroup^{0.8}\left\lgroup\frac{c\mu}{k}\right\rgroup^{¹⁄³}\left\lgroup\frac{\mu}{\mu _w}\right\rgroup^{0.14}\left\lgroup\frac{k}{D_e}\right\rgroup\)

 

\(=0.027\left\lgroup\frac{G_s}{\mu }\right\rgroup^{0.8}(c\mu k^2)^{¹⁄³}\left\lgroup\frac{\mu}{\mu _w}\right\rgroup^{0.14}\left\lgroup\frac{1}{D_e}\right\rgroup^{0.2}\)

 

\(=0.027\left\lgroup\frac{578,000}{2.90}\right\rgroup^{0.8}((0.757)(2.90)(0.33)^2)^{¹⁄³}(1.0)\left\lgroup\frac{1}{0.079}\right\rgroup^{0.2}\)

≈ 483 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F

(9′) \(h_i=0.027\left\lgroup\frac{DG_t}{\mu}\right\rgroup^{0.8}\left\lgroup\frac{c\mu}{k}\right\rgroup^{¹⁄³}\left\lgroup\frac{\mu}{\mu _w}\right\rgroup^{0.14}\left\lgroup\frac{k}{D}\right\rgroup\)

 

\(=0.027\left\lgroup\frac{G_t}{\mu }\right\rgroup^{0.8}(c\mu k^2)^{¹⁄³}\left\lgroup\frac{\mu}{\mu _w}\right\rgroup^{0.14}\left\lgroup\frac{1}{D}\right\rgroup^{0.2}\)

 

\(=0.027\left\lgroup\frac{762,000}{2.20}\right\rgroup^{0.8}((1)(2.20)(0.35)^2)^{¹⁄³}(1.0)\left\lgroup\frac{1}{0.0517}\right\rgroup^{0.2}\)

≈ 852 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F

(13) Clean overall coefficient, \(U_C\) :

\(U_C=\frac{h_ih_o}{h_i+h_o}=\frac{(483)(852)}{483+852}=308\ Btu/hr\cdot ft^2\cdot °F\)

(14) Design overall coefficient, \(U_D\) :

\(a^{"}\) = 0.1963 \(ft^2\) per line. ft ⇐ [Table AT.8]

A = \(a^{''}N_t\)L = (0.1963)(52)(16) = 163 \(ft^2\)

\(U_D=\frac{Q}{A\Delta T^*}=\frac{915,000}{(163)(30.7)}=183\ Btu/hr\cdot ft^2\cdot °F\)

(15) Dirt factor \(R_d\):

\(R_d=\frac{U_C-U_D}{U_CU_D}=\frac{308-183}{(308)(183)}=0.00222hr\cdot ft^2\cdot °F/Btu\)

Summary
Variable Value Units
\(h_o\) = 483 \(\frac{Btu}{hr·ft^2·°F/ft}\)
\(h_i\) = 852
\(U_C\) = 303 \(\frac{Btu}{hr·ft^2·°F}\)
\(U_D\) = 183
\(R_d\) (calculated) = 0.00222 \(\frac{hr·ft^2·°F}{Btu}\)
\(R_d\) (required) = 0.00200

Pressure Drop

(1) For \(Re_s\) and \(T_{mean}\) in (6’) obtain f:

f = 0.0019\(\frac{ft^2}{in^2}\)at \(Re_s\) = 15,750

s = 1.3 ⇐ given

\(D_s\) = 10.02in\(\left\lgroup\frac{1ft}{12in} \right\rgroup\) = 0.833 ft

 (1) for \(Re_t\) and \(t_c\) in (6) above:

f = 0.00023\(\frac{ft^2}{in^2}\) at \(Re_t\) = 17,900

s = 1.0 ⇐ water

 

(2) Number of crosses, N + 1:

\(N+1=\frac{12L}{B}=\frac{12(16)}{2}=96\)
(2′) \(\Delta P_t=\frac{fG^2_tLn}{(5.22\times 10^{10})D_es\phi _t}\)

\(=\frac{(0.00023)(762,000)^2(16)(2)}{(5.22\times 10^{10})(0.0517)(1.0)(1.0)}\)

= 1.6 psi

\(\frac{\overline{v}^2}{2g^{'}}\left\lgroup\frac{62.5}{144} \right\rgroup\) = 0.08 at \(G_t\) = 762,000

 

\(\Delta P_r=\left\lgroup\frac{4n}{s}\right\rgroup\left\lgroup\frac{\overline{v}^2}{2g^{'}}\right\rgroup\left\lgroup\frac{62.5}{144}\right\rgroup\)

 

\(=\left\lgroup\frac{4(2)}{1.0}\right\rgroup(0.08)=0.7\ psi\)

 

(3′) \(\Delta P_s=\frac{fG^2_sD_s(N+1)}{(5.22\times 10^{10})D_es\phi _s}\)

\(\Delta P_s=\frac{(0.0019)(578,000)^2(0.833)(96)}{(5.22\times 10^{10})(0.079)(1.3)(1.0)}\)

Δ\(P_s\) = 9.5 psi

Δ\(P_s\) < 10 psi as allowable ΔP

 (3) Δ\(P_T\) = Δ\(P_t\) + Δ\(P_r\)

Δ\(P_T\)= 1.6 + 0.7 = 2.3psi

Δ\(P_T\) < 10 psi as allowable ΔP

This exchanger is satisfactory for service.
