Guest Question.

A diffuse surface at 1600 K has the spectral, hemispherical emissivity shown as follows. Determine the total, hemispherical emissivity and the total emissive power. At what wavelength will the spectral emissive power be a maximum?

Admin
Solution

Known: Spectral, hemispherical emissivity of a diffuse surface at 1600 K.

Find:
1. Total, hemispherical emissivity.
2. Total emissive power.
3. Wavelength at which spectral emissive power will be a maximum.

Assumptions: Surface is a diffuse emitter.

Analysis:
1. The total, hemispherical emissivity is given by Equation 12.43, where the integration may be performed in parts as follows:

 

\(\varepsilon(T)=\frac{\int_{0}^{\infty} \varepsilon_{\lambda}(\lambda, T) E_{\lambda, b}(\lambda, T) d \lambda}{E_{b}(T)}\) (12.43)

 

\(\varepsilon=\frac{\int_{0}^{\infty} \varepsilon_{\lambda} E_{\lambda, b} d \lambda}{E_{b}}=\frac{\varepsilon_{1} \int_{0}^{2} E_{\lambda, b} d \lambda}{E_{b}}+\frac{\varepsilon_{2} \int_{2}^{5} E_{\lambda, b} d \lambda}{E_{b}}\)

 

or

\(\varepsilon=\varepsilon_{1} F_{(0 \rightarrow 2 \mu m )}+\varepsilon_{2}\left[F_{(0 \rightarrow 5 \mu m )}-F_{(0 \rightarrow 2 \mu m )}\right]\)

\(\lambda_{1} T=2 \mu m \times 1600 K =3200 \mu m \cdot K : \quad F_{(0 \rightarrow 2 \mu m )}=0.318\)

 

\(\lambda_{2} T=5 \mu m \times 1600 K =8000 \mu m \cdot K : \quad F_{(0 \rightarrow 5 \mu m )}=0.856\)

 

Hence

\(\varepsilon=0.4 \times 0.318+0.8[0.856-0.318]=0.558\)

 

2. From Equation 12.36 the total emissive power is

 

\(\varepsilon(T) \equiv \frac{E(T)}{E_{b}(T)}\) (12.36)

 

\(E=\varepsilon E_{b}=\varepsilon \sigma T^{4}\)

 

\(E=0.558\left(5.67 \times 10^{-8} W / m ^{2} \cdot K ^{4}\right)(1600 K )^{4}=207 kW / m ^{2}\)

 

3. If the surface emitted as a blackbody or if its emissivity were a constant, independent of λ, the wavelength corresponding to maximum spectral emission could be obtained from Wien’s displacement law. However, because \(\varepsilon_{1}\) varies with λ, it is not immediately obvious where peak emission occurs. From Equation 12.31 we know that

 

\(\lambda_{\max } T=C_{3}\) (12.31)

 

\(\lambda_{\max }=\frac{2898 \mu m \cdot K }{1600 K }=1.81 \mu m\)

 

The spectral emissive power at this wavelength may be obtained by using Equation 12.40 with Table 12.2. That is,

 

\(\varepsilon_{\lambda}(\lambda, T) \equiv \frac{E_{\lambda}(\lambda, T)}{E_{\lambda, b}(\lambda, T)}\) (12.40)

 

\(E_{\lambda}\left(\lambda_{\max }, T\right)=\varepsilon_{\lambda}\left(\lambda_{\max }\right) E_{\lambda, b}\left(\lambda_{\max }, T\right)\)

 

or, since the surface is a diffuse emitter,

 

\(E_{\lambda}\left(\lambda_{\max }, T\right)=\pi \varepsilon_{\lambda}\left(\lambda_{\max }\right) I_{\lambda, b}\left(\lambda_{\max }, T\right)\)

 

\(=\pi \varepsilon_{\lambda}\left(\lambda_{\max }\right) \frac{I_{\lambda, b}\left(\lambda_{\max }, T\right)}{\sigma T^{5}} \times \sigma T^{5}\)

 

\(E_{\lambda}(1.81 \mu m , 1600 K )=\pi \times 0.4 \times 0.722 \times 10^{-4}(\mu m \cdot K \cdot sr )^{-1} \times 5.67\)

 

\(\times 10^{-8} W / m ^{2} \cdot K ^{4} \times(1600 K )^{5}=54 kW / m ^{2} \cdot \mu m\)

 

Since \(\varepsilon_{\lambda}=0.4 \text { from } \lambda=0 \text { to } \lambda=2 \mu m\), the foregoing result provides the maximum spectral emissive power for the region \(\lambda<2 \mu m\). However, with the change in \(\varepsilon_{\lambda}\) that occurs at \(\lambda=2 \mu m\), the value of \(E_{\lambda} \text { at } \lambda=2 \mu m\) may be larger than that for \(\lambda=1.81 \mu m\). To determine whether this is, in fact, the case, we compute

 

\(E_{\lambda}\left(\lambda_{1}, T\right)=\pi \varepsilon_{\lambda}\left(\lambda_{1}\right) \frac{I_{\lambda, b}\left(\lambda_{1}, T\right)}{\sigma T^{5}} \times \sigma T^{5}\)

 

\(\text { where, for } \lambda_{1} T=3200 \mu m \cdot K ,\left[I_{\lambda, b}\left(\lambda_{1}, T\right) / \sigma T^{5}\right]=0.706 \times 10^{-4}(\mu m \cdot K \cdot sr )^{-1} \text { . Hence }\)

 

\(E_{\lambda}(2 \mu m , 1600 K )=\pi \times 0.80 \times 0.706 \times 10^{-4}(\mu m \cdot K \cdot sr )^{-1} \times 5.67\)

 

\(\times 10^{-8} W / m ^{2} \cdot K ^{4}(1600 K )^{5}\)

 

\(E_{\lambda}(2 \mu m , 1600 K )=105.5 kW / m ^{2} \cdot \mu m >E_{\lambda}(1.81 \mu m , 1600 K )\)

 

and peak emission occurs at

 

\(\lambda=\lambda_{1}=2 \mu m\)

 

Comments: For the prescribed spectral distribution of \(\varepsilon_{\lambda}\), the spectral emissive power will vary with wavelength as shown.
