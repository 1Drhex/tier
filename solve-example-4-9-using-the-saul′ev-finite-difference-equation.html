Guest Question.

Solve using the Saul′ev finite difference equation.

Admin
Solution

The same assumptions in apply here as well. The data are as follows:

 

\(\rho_{s}=0.5(1000)\,kg/m^{3}\)

 

\(c_{p}=837\,J/(kg\cdot K)\)

 

\(k=0.128\, W/(m\cdot K)\)

 

\(\alpha =0.049\times 10^{-5}\,m^{2}/s\)

 

For the Fourier number, we use

 

\(Fo_{\Delta }=\frac{\alpha \Delta t}{(\Delta x)^{2}}=1\)

 

compared to 1/2 in the earlier example. For purposes of comparison, we select Δx = 5 cm as before. Substituting

 

\(1=\frac{0.049 \times 10^{-5}\Delta t}{0.05^{2}}\)

 

and the time increment becomes

 

\(\Delta t=5.102\times 10^{3}\,s=1.42\,hr\)

 

With the Fourier number equal to 1, the finite difference equation for temperature is

 

\(T^{p+1}_{m}=\frac{T^{p}_{m+1}+T^{p+1}_{m-1}}{2}\)

 

The parameter (space coordinate) m varies from 1 to 6, whereas p starts at 0 and increases until \(T_{6}\) > 20°C. At m = 0, the temperature is constant at 200°C. Letting p = 0, and 0 ≥ m ≥ 6, we substitute into the preceding equation to get

 

p = 1
m = 1 \(\; \; \; \; \;T^{1}_{1}=(T^{0}_{2}+T^{1}_{0})/2=110\)
m = 2 \(\; \; \; \; \;T^{1}_{2}=(T^{0}_{3}+T^{1}_{1})/2=65\)
m = 3 \(\; \; \; \; \;T^{1}_{3}=(T^{0}_{4}+T^{1}_{2})/2=42.5\)
m = 4 \(\; \; \; \; \;T^{1}_{4}=(T^{0}_{5}+T^{1}_{3})/2=31.3\)
m = 5 \(\; \; \; \; \;T^{1}_{5}=(T^{0}_{6}+T^{1}_{4})/2=25.6\)
m = 6 \(\; \; \; \; \;T^{1}_{6}=(T^{0}_{5}+T^{1}_{5})/2=22.8\)

 

Note that we begin with the temperature at the boundary (200°C) and proceed by substituting into the differenced equation. Th is procedure seems simple enough, but can be quite confusing; thus, a chart summarizing the calculations may help to visualize the procedure. Such a chart is provided in Table 4.3. Note that for the insulated boundary, the temperature at node 5 is used. If there was a node 7 at this insulated surface, its temperature would equal that of node 5.

 

A graph of temperature versus distance is provided in Figure 4.21. Shown is the initial temperature profiles and the ones for the first two time intervals.
