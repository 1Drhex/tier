Guest Question.

Obtain the stoichiometric reaction equation for \(\textbf{CH}_{4}\) being burnt with
(i) 100% theoretical air
(ii) 200% excess air
(iii) 20% less than theoretical air requirement
Consider air to have 21% oxygen and 79% nitrogen by mass.

Admin
Solution

\(\text{Let number of air molecules required for one molecule of }CH_{4}\text{ be ‘n’. Combustion equation for CH}_{4},\\CH_{4} + n \left\lgroup O_{2} + \frac{79}{21} . N_{2} \right\rgroup → a . CO_{2} + b . N_{2} + d . H_{2}O\\\text{From above equation the C, H}_{2}\text{ and O}_{2}\text{ can be equated as under;}\\\begin{aligned}C, 1&= a, or a = 1\\O_{2}, n&= a + \frac{d}{2} , or 2n = 2a + d\\H_{2}, 2&= d, or d = 2\\N_{2}, n × \frac{79}{21}&= b, or b = 3.76 n \\\text{It gives}\qquad \qquad &\\n&= 2, a = 1\\b&= 7.52, d = 2\end{aligned}\\\text{Stoichiometric combustion equation shall be,}\\\text{For 100}\%\text{ theoretical air}\\CH_{4} + 2(O_{2} + 3.76 N_{2}) → CO_{2} + 7.52N_{2} + 2H_{2}O\\\)When 200% of excess air is added then total air supplied will be 300% i.e. (200% excess + 100% theoretical). Due to this excess \(O_{2}\) shall be there in combustion products.
Thus modified form of combustion equation shall be,\(\\CH_{4} + 3 × 2 (O_{2} + 3.76 N_{2}) → a . CO_{2} + b . N_{2} + d . H_{2}O + e . O_{2}\\\text{Equating C, H}_{2}, O_{2}\text{ and N}_{2}\text{ we get}\\\begin{aligned}C; a&= 1\\O_{2}; a + \frac{d}{2} + e&= 6\\H_{2}; d&= 2\\N_{2}; b&= 6 × 3.76\\\text{It yields,}\qquad \qquad &\\a&= 1\\b&= 22.56\\d&= 2\\e&= 4\end{aligned}\\\text{Now the combustion equation with 200}\%\text{ excess air shall be;}\\ \text{With 200}\%\text{ excess air;}\\CH_{4} + 6(O_{2} + 3.76 N_{2}) → CO_{2} + 22.56N_{2} + 2H_{2}O + 4O_{2}\\\)When there is 20% less air then actual air supplied will be 20% less than 100% theoretical air. Due to less than theoretical air supplied there will be incomplete combustion and CO will be present with combustion products.\(\\CH_{4} + 0.8 × 2 (O_{2} + 3.76 N_{2}) → aCO_{2} + bCO + dN_{2} + eH_{2}O\\\text{Equating C, H}_{2}, O_{2}\text{ and N}_{2}\text{ we get}\\\begin{aligned}C; a + b&= 1\\O_{2}; a + \frac{b}{2} + \frac{e}{2}&= 1.6\\H_{2}; e&= 2\\N_{2}; d&= 1.2 × 3.76 = 4.512\\\text{It yields,}\qquad \qquad&\\a&= 0.2\\b&= 0.8\\d&= 4.512\\e&= 2\end{aligned}\\\text{Hence for 20}\%\text{ less air the combustion equation shall be as under}\\ \text{For 20}\% \text{ less air.}\\CH_{4} + 1.6 (O_{2} + 3.76N_{2}) → 0.2CO_{2} + 0.8CO + 4.512N_{2} + 2H_{2}O\)
