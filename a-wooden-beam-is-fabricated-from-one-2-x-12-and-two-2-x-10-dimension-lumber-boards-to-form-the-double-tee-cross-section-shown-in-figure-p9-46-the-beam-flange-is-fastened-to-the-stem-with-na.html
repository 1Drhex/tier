Guest Question.

A wooden beam is fabricated from one 2 × 12 and two 2 × 10 dimension lumber boards to form the double-tee cross section shown in Figure P9.46. The beam flange is fastened to the stem with nails. Each nail can safely transmit a force of 175 lb in direct shear. The allowable shear stress of the wood is 70 psi.
(a) If the nails are uniformly spaced at an interval of s = 4 in. along the span, what is the maximum internal shear force V that can be supported by the double-tee cross section?
(b) What nail spacing s would be necessary to develop the full strength of the double-tee shape in shear? (Full strength means that the maximum horizontal shear stress in the double-tee shape equals the allowable shear stress of the wood.)

Admin
Solution

Centroid location in y direction:

\(\begin{array}{cccccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}\text { Width } b \\(\text { in. })\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Height } h \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left(\text { in. }{ }^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} \text { (from bottom) } \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} A_{i} \\\left(\text { in. }{ }^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 12 & 2 & 24 & 11 & 264 \\\text { left stem } & 2 & 10 & 20 & 5 & 100 \\\text { right stem } & 2 & 10 & 20 & 5 & 100 \\\hline & & & 64 & & 464\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{464 in. ^{3}}{64 in .^{2}} &=7.25 in . & & \text { (from bottom of shape to centroid) } \\&=4.75 in . & & \text { (from top of shape to centroid) }\end{aligned}\)

 

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

\(\begin{aligned}&\begin{array}{lcccc}{\text { Shape }} & I_{C} & d=y_{i}-\bar{y} & d^{2} A & I_{C}+d^{2} A \\& \left( in .{ }^{4}\right) & \text { (in.) } & \left( in .{ }^{4}\right) & \left( in .{ }^{4}\right) \\\hline \text { left board } & 8.000 & 3.750 & 337.500 & 345.500 \\\text { flange board } & 166.667 & -2.250 & 101.250 & 267.917 \\\text { right board } & 166.667 & -2.250 & 101.250 & 267.917 \\\hline\end{array}\\&\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left(\text { in } .^{4}\right)=\quad 881.333\end{aligned}\)

 

(a) Maximum shear force based on capacity of nails at s = 4 in.:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q=(12 \text { in. })(2 \text { in. })(4.75 \text { in. }-2 \text { in. } / 2)=90.000 \text { in. }^{3} \\&q=\frac{(2 \text { nails })(175 lb / \text { nail })}{4 \text { in. }}=87.5 lb / in . \\&q=\frac{V Q}{I} \quad \therefore V_{\max }=\frac{q I}{Q}=\frac{(87.5 lb / in .)\left(881.333 in. ^{4}\right)}{90.000 in .{ }^{3}}=856.852 lb\end{aligned}\)

 

Maximum shear force based on full shear strength of double tee shape:

At neutral axis:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q_{\max }=2(2 \text { in. })(7.25 in .)(7.25 in . / 2)=105.125 in .{ }^{3} \\&\tau_{\max }=\frac{V Q}{I t} \quad \therefore V_{\max } \leq \frac{\tau_{\max } I t}{Q}=\frac{(70 psi )\left(881.333 in .^{4}\right)(2 \times 2 in .)}{105.125 in .{ }^{3}}=2,347.428 lb\end{aligned}\)

Comparison of two \(V _{\max }\) values:
The smaller of the two values computed above for \(V _{\max }\) gives the limiting shear force:

\(V_{\max }=857 lb\)

(b) Nail spacing necessary to develop the full shear strength of the section:
Consider the nailed portion of the beam (i.e., only the top flange) to establish the minimum required nail spacing for \(V _{\max }\) = 2,347.428 lb, which is the shear force that produces a maximum horizontal shear stress of 70 psi:

\(\begin{aligned}&q=\frac{V Q}{I}=\frac{(2,347.428 lb )\left(90.000 in .{ }^{3}\right)}{881.333 in .{ }^{4}}=239.715 lb / in . \\&q s \leq n_{f} V_{f} \\&\therefore s_{\max } \leq \frac{n_{f} V_{f}}{q}=\frac{(2 \text { nails })(175 lb / nail )}{239.715 lb / in .}=1.460 in .\end{aligned}\)
