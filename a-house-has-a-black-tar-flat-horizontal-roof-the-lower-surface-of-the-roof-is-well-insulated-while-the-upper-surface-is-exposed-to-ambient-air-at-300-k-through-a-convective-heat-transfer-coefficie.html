Guest Question.

A house has a black tar, flat, horizontal roof. The lower surface of the roof is well insulated, while the upper surface is exposed to ambient air at 300 K through a convective heat transfer coefficient of \(10\space W/m^{2}K\). Calculate the roof equilibrium temperature for the following conditions: (a) a clear sunny day with an incident solar radiation flux of \(500\space W/m^{2}K\) and the ambient sky at an effective temperature of 50 K and (b) a clear night with an ambient sky temperature of 50 K.

Admin
Solution

A schematic sketch of the system is shown in Fig. 1.34. The control volume is the roof. Assume that there are no obstructions between the roof, called surface 1, and the sky, called surface 2, and that both surfaces are black. The sky behaves as a blackbody because it absorbs all the radiation emitted by the roof and reflects none.
Heat is transferred by convection between the ambient air and the roof and by radiation between the sun and roof and between the roof and the sky.

This is a closed system in thermal equilibrium. Since there is no generation, storage, or work output, we can express the energy conservation requirement by the conceptual relation

net rate of radiation rate of convection rate of solar
= heat transfer from heat transfer radiation heat transfer +
roof to ambient sky to roof to roof

Analytically, this relation can be cast in the form

\(A_{1} q_{r, \text { sun } \rightarrow \text { roof }}+\bar{h}_{c} A_{1}\left(T_{\text {air }}-T_{\text {roof }}\right)=A_{1} q_{r, \text { roof } \rightarrow \text { ambient sky }}\)

Canceling the roof area \(A_{1}\) and substituting the Stefan-Boltzmann relation [Eq.(1.17)] for the net radiation from the roof to the ambient sky gives :

\(q_{r}=A_{1} \varepsilon_{1} \sigma\left(T_{1}^{4}-T_{2}^{4}\right)\\q_{r, \text { sun } \rightarrow 1}+\bar{h}_{c}\left(300-T_{\text {roof }}\right)=\sigma\left(T_{\text {roof }}^{4}-T_{{sky}}^{4}\right)\)

(a) When the solar radiation to the roof,\( q_{r,sun→1}\) is \( 500\space W/m^{2}\) and \(T_{sky}\) is 50 k,we get :

\(\begin{array}{l}500 {~W} / {m}^{2}+\left(10 {~W} / {m}^{2} {~K}\right)\left(300-T_{\text {roof }}\right)({K}) \\ =\left(5.67 \times 10^{-8} {~W} / {m}^{2} {~K}^{4}\right)\left(T_{\text {roof }}^{4}-50^{4}\right)\left({K}^{4}\right)\end{array}\)

Solving by trial and error for the roof temperature, we get

\(T_{\text roof }=303 {~K}=30^{\circ} {C}\)

Note that the convection term is negative because the sun heats the roof to a temperature above the ambient air, so that the roof is not heated but is cooled by convection to the air.

(b) At night the term \(Q_{r,sun→1}=0\) and we get, upon substituting the numerical data in the conservation of energy relation,

\(\bar{h}_{c}\left(T_{\text {air }}-T_{\text {roof }}\right)=\sigma\left(T_{\text {roof }}^{4}-T_{\text {sky }}^{4}\right)\)

or

\(\left(10 {~W} / {m}^{2} {~K}\right)\left(300-T_{\text {roof }}\right)({K})=\left(5.67 \times 10^{-8} {~W} / {m}^{2} {~K}^{4}\right)\left(T_{\text {roof }}^{4}-50^{4}\right)\left({K}^{4}\right)\)

Solving this equation for \(T_{roof}\) gives :

\(T_{\text {roof }}=270 {~K}=-3^{\circ} {C}\)

At night the roof is cooler than the ambient air and convection occurs from the air to the roof, which is heated in the process. Observe also that the conditions at night and during the day are assumed to be steady and that the change from one steady condition to the other requires a period of transition in which the energy stored in the roof changes and the roof temperature also changes. The energy stored in the roof increases during the morning hours and decreases during the evening after the sun has set, but these periods are not considered in this example.
