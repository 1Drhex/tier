Guest Question.

The column shown in Figure P16.88 is fabricated from two C250 × 30 standard steel shapes (see Appendix B for cross-sectional properties) that are oriented back-toback with a gap of 25 mm between the two channels. The column is fixed at its base and free to translate in the y direction at its upper end. Translation in the z direction, however, is restrained at its upper end. A load P is applied at an offset distance of 500 mm from the channel flanges. Use the AISC equations given in Section 16.5 and assume that E = 200 GPa, \(\sigma_{Y}\) = 250 MPa, and \(\left(\sigma_{\text {allow }}\right)_{b}\) = 150 MPa. Using the interaction equation method,
determine:
(a) whether column is safe for a load of P = 75 kN. Report the value of the interaction equation.
(b) the magnitude of the largest load P that may be applied to the column.

Admin
Solution

The following section properties for a standard steel C250 × 30 shape are given in Appendix B:

\(A=3,790 mm ^{2}, d=254 mm , I_{x}=32.8 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}, r_{x}=93.0 mm , I_{x}=1.17 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}, \bar{x}=15.4 mm\)

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&I_{z}=2\left(32.8 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\right)=65.6 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4} \\&I_{y}=2\left[1.17 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}+(25 mm / 2+15.4 mm )^{2}\left(3,790 mm ^{2}\right)\right]=8.2403478 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\) \(\begin{aligned}&r_{z}=93.0 mm & r_{y}=\sqrt{\frac{8.2403478 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}}{2\left(3,790 mm ^{2}\right)}}=32.971 \\&\frac{K_{z} L}{r_{z}}=\frac{(2)(4,500 mm )}{93.0 mm }=96.774 & \frac{K_{y} L}{r_{y}}=\frac{(0.7)(4,500 mm )}{32.971 mm }=95.537\end{aligned}\)

Limiting slenderness ratio

\(4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{Y}}}=4.71 \sqrt{\frac{200,000 MPa }{250 MPa }}=133.219\)

Elastic critical buckling stress

\(\sigma_{e}=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{\left(\frac{K L}{r}\right)^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(200,000 MPa )}{(96.774)^{2}}=210.771 MPa\)

Critical and allowable buckling stresses

Since \(\frac{K L}{r} \leq 4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{y}}}\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{c r}=\left[0.658^{\left(\frac{\sigma_{Y}}{\sigma_{e}}\right)}\right] \sigma_{Y}=\left[0.658^{\left(\frac{250 MPa }{210.771 MPa }\right)}\right](250 MPa )=152.172 MPa \\&\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\sigma_{c r}}{1.67}=\frac{152.172 MPa }{1.67}=91.121 MPa\end{aligned}\)

(a) Interaction method for P = 75 kN:

\(\frac{\frac{P}{A}}{\left(\sigma_{\text {allow }}\right)_{a}}+\frac{\frac{M c}{I}}{\left(\sigma_{\text {allow }}\right)_{b}}=\frac{\frac{75,000 N }{2\left(3,790 mm ^{2}\right)}}{91.121 MPa }+\frac{\frac{(75,000 N )(500 mm +254 mm / 2)(254 mm / 2)}{65.6 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}}}{150 MPa }\)

\(=\frac{9.894 MPa }{91.121 MPa }+\frac{91.039 MPa }{150 MPa }=0.716\) ∴ Safe

(b) Determine largest P:

\(\frac{\frac{P}{A}}{\left(\sigma_{\text {allow }}\right)_{a}}+\frac{\frac{M c}{I}}{\left(\sigma_{\text {allow }}\right)_{b}}=P\left[\frac{\frac{1}{2\left(3,790 mm ^{2}\right)}}{91.121 MPa }+\frac{\frac{(500 mm +254 mm / 2)(254 mm / 2)}{65.6 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}}}{150 MPa }\right] \leq 1\) \(\begin{aligned}P\left[\frac{1}{690,695.24 N }+\frac{1}{123,573.07 N }\right] \leq & 1 \\& \therefore P \leq 104,820 N =104.8 kN\end{aligned}\)
