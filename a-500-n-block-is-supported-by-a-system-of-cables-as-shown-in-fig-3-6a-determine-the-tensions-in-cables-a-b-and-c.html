Guest Question.

A 500-N block is supported by a system of cables as shown in Fig. 3-6a. Determine the tensions in cables A, B, and C.

Admin
Solution

Since we are trying to find the forces in the cables, we must “cut” the cables at some point along their length to break the “equal/opposite” force pairs in the cable. The ring D is acted on by all three of the unknown tension forces and by the known weight force. The free-body diagram of the ring is drawn in Fig. 3-6b. The coordinates of the support points for each of the cables are shown on the free-body diagram in (x, y, z) format as an aid in writing vector equations for the cable tensions.
The tension forces in the cables and the weight of the traffic light are concurrent at the ring D. For a concurrent system of forces in three the vector equation of equilibrium \(R=\sum{F}=0\) has three independent scalar components. Before writing the equilibrium equation, it is necessary to express the cable tensions and the weight of the block in Cartesian vector form. The tension \(T_A\) can be expressed in terms of its unknown magnitude \(T_A\) and a unit vector \(e_{AD}\) in the direction of the line AD as

\(T_A=T_Ae_{AD}=T_A(\frac{1.2i-1.5j+2.4k}{\sqrt{1.2^2+1.5^2+2.4^2} } )\)

 

\(=0.3904T_Ai-0.4880T_Aj+0.7807T_Ak\) (a)

Similarly for the tensions \(T_B\) and \(T_C\) and for the weight W

\(T_B=T_Be_{BD}=T_B(\frac{-1.8i-2.1j+2.1k}{\sqrt{1.8^2+2.1^2+2.1^2} } )\)

 

\(=-0.5183T_Bi-0.6047T_Bj+0.6047T_Bk\) (b)

 

\(T_C=T_Ce_{CD}=T_C(\frac{-1.2i+1.8j+0.9k}{\sqrt{1.2^2+1.8^2+0.9^2} } )\)

 

\(=-0.5121T_Ci+0.7682T_Cj+0.3841T_Ck\) (c)

\(W=-500kN\) (d)

Using Eqs. (a)–(d) and the free-body diagram (Fig. 3-6b) to write out the vector equilibrium equation results in

\(\sum{F}=0: \quad \quad \quad 0.3904T_Ai-0.4880T_Aj+0.7807T_Ak\) \(-0.5183T_Bi-0.6047T_Bj+0.6047T_Bk\)

\(-0.5121T_Ci+0.7682T_Cj+0.3841T_Ck-500k =0\) (e)

Since each of the components of Eq. (e) must be zero if the resultant is to be zero, the following three scalar equations must be satisfied.

\(0.3904T_A-0.5183T_B-0.5121T_C=0\)

 

\(-0.4880T_A-0.6047T_B+0.7682T_C=0\)

 

\(0.7807T_A+0.6047T_B+0.3841T_C=500\) (f)

The simultaneous solution of these three linear equations gives

\(T_A=459N\)

 

\(T_B=32.4N\)

 

\(T_C=317N\)
