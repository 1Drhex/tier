Guest Question.

Consider an isothermal medium at temperature T , confined between two large, parallel black plates that are isothermal at the (same) temperature \(T_{w}\),. The medium is gray and absorbs and emits, but does not scatter. Determine an expression for the heat transfer rates within the medium using the \(S_{2}\) and \(S_{4}\) discrete ordinates approximations.

Admin
Solution

For this particularly simple case equations (16.18) \(\mu _{i}\frac{dI^{+}_{i} }{d\tau } + I^{+}_{i} = (1-w)I_{b}+\frac{w}{4\pi } (G+A_{1}q\mu _{i})\)
\(-\mu _{i}\frac{dI^{-}_{i} }{d\tau } + I^{-}_{i} = (1-w)I_{b}+\frac{w}{4\pi } (G+A_{1}q\mu _{i}) i=1,2,….,N/2; \mu _{i}\gt 0\) reduce to

\(\mu _{i}\frac{dI^{+}_{i}}{d\tau } +I_{i}=I_{b},\)
\(-\mu _{i}\frac{dI^{-}_{i}}{d\tau } +I_{i}=I_{b}.\)

Since \(I_{b}\) = const, these equations may be integrated right away, leading to

\(I^{+}_{i} = I_{b}+ C^{+}e^{-\tau /\mu _{i}},\)
\(I^{-}_{i} = I_{b}+ C^{-}e^{-\tau /\mu _{i}}.\)

The integration constants \( C^{+}\) and \( C^{-}\) may be found from boundary conditions (16.19) \(\tau =0 : I^{+}_{i}=J_{1}/\pi =I_{b1} – \frac{1-\epsilon _{1}}{\epsilon _{1}\pi } q_{1}\)
\(\tau =\tau _{L} : I^{-}_{i}=J_{2}/\pi =I_{b2} – \frac{1-\epsilon _{2}}{\epsilon _{2}\pi } q_{q} i=1,2,…,N/2 , \mu _{i}\gt 0.\) as

\(\tau =0 : I^{+}_{i}=I_{bw}=I_{b}+ C^{+}, or C^{+}= I_{bw}-I_{b};\)
\(\tau =\tau _{L} : I^{-}_{i}=I_{bw}=I_{b}+ C^{-}e^{\tau _{L}/\mu _{i}}, or C^{-}= (I_{bw}-I_{b})e^{-\tau _{L}/\mu _{i}}.\)

Thus,

\(I^{+}_{i}=I_{b}+(I_{bw}-I_{b})e^{-\tau /\mu _{i}},\)
\(I^{-}_{i}=I_{b}+(I_{bw}-I_{b})e^{-(\tau_{L}-\tau ) /\mu _{i}}.\)

The radiative heat flux follows then from equation (16.20)\(q=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N/2}{w^{\prime }_{i} \mu _{i} }(I^{+}_{i}-I^{-}_{i}) ,\)
\(G=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N/2}{w^{\prime }_{i} }(I^{+}_{i}+I^{-}_{i}) .\) as

\(q= \sum\limits_{i=1}^{N/2}{w^{\prime }_{i} \mu _{i}(I_{bw}-I_{b})\left(e^{-\tau /\mu _{i}}- e^{-(\tau_{L}-\tau ) /\mu _{i}} \right) } ,\)

or, in nondimensional form ,

\(\Psi \frac{q}{2^{2}\sigma (T^{4}_{w}- T^{4} )}=\frac{1}{\pi }\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N/2}{w^{\prime }_{i} \mu _{i}\left(e^{-\tau /\mu _{i}}- e^{-(\tau_{L}-\tau ) /\mu _{i}} \right) }\)

For the nonsymmetric \(S_{2}\) -approximation we have \(w^{\prime }_{i}=2\pi\) and \(\mu _{1}\) = 0.5, or

\(\Psi _{S_{2}} = e^{-2\tau }-e^{-2(\tau _{L}-\tau )}.\)

For the \(S_{4}\)-approximation, \(w^{\prime }_{1}=4\pi/3\), \(w^{\prime }_{2}=2\pi/3\), \(\mu _{1}\)= 0.2958759,\(\mu _{2}\)= 0.9082483, and \(\sum{w^{\prime }_{i} \mu _{i}=\pi }\) , so that

\(\Psi _{S_{4}} = 0.3945012(e^{-\tau /0.2958759}- e^{(\tau _{L}-\tau )/0.2958759})+0.6054088 (e^{-\tau /0.9082483}- e^{(\tau _{L}-\tau )/0.9082483}).\).

The results should be compared with those of and for the \(P_{1}\) and \(P_{3}\)-approximations.
Note that the \(S_{N}\)-method goes to the correct optically thick limit (\(\tau _{L} →\infty\) ) at the wall, i.e., Ψ→ 1 [if the half moment of equation (16.13) \(\int_{\overset{\wedge }{n} \cdot \overset{\wedge }{s}\lt 0}^{}{} \left|\overset{\wedge }{n}\cdot \overset{\wedge }{s}\right| d\Omega =\int_{\overset{\wedge }{n} \cdot \overset{\wedge }{s}\gt 0}^{}{} \overset{\wedge }{n} \overset{\wedge }{s} d\Omega=\pi =\sum\limits_{\overset{\wedge }{n} \cdot \overset{\wedge }{s_{i}}\gt 0}{w^{\prime }_{i} \overset{\wedge }{n} \cdot \overset{\wedge }{s_{i}}}\)] is satisfied]. The \(P_{N}\)-approximations, on the other hand, overpredict the optically thick limit for this particular example.
