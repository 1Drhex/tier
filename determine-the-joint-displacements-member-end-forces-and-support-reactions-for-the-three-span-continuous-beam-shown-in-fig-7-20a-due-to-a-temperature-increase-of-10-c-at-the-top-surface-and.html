Guest Question.

Determine the joint displacements, member end forces, and support reactions for the three-span continuous beam shown in Fig. 7.20(a), due to a temperature increase of 10° C at the top surface and 70° C at the bottom surface, of all spans. The temperature increase varies linearly over the depth d  = 600 mm of the beam cross-section. Use the matrix stiffness method.

Admin
Solution

This beam was analyzed in for member loads and support settlements.

Analytical Model: See Fig. 7.20(b). The analytical model used herein is the same as used in .

Structure Stiffness Matrix: As determined in , the structure stiffness matrix
for the beam, in units of kN and meters, is

\(S=\begin{bmatrix}7,140 &1,785\\1,785& 6,247.5 \end{bmatrix}\) (1)

Joint Load Vector:

P=0 (2)

Structure Fixed-Joint Force Vector Due to Temperature Change:

Members 1 and 2 (MT = 0) By substituting the numerical values of E, I, L, α , d = 0.6m, \(T_{t}\) = 10°C and \(T_{b}\) = 70°C into Eq. (7.33) \(FA_{b}=-FA_{e}=EA\alpha \left(\frac{T_{b}+T_{t}}{2} \right) , FM_{b}=-FM_{e}=EI\alpha \left(\frac{T_{b}-T_{t}}{d} \right)\) , we evaluate the member fixedend moments due to the given temperature change as

\(FM_{b}=-FM_{e}=70(10^{6})(102)(10^{-6})(2.36)(10^{-5})\left(\frac{70-10}{0.6} \right) =16.85 kN\cdot m\) (3a)

\(FS_{b}=FS_{e}=0\) (3b)

Thus,

(4)

Next, using the member code numbers, we store the pertinent elements of \(Q_{f1} and Q_{f2}\) in their proper positions in the structure fixed-joint force vector \(P_{f}\) , as shown in Fig. 7.20(c).

Member 3 (MT = 2) Because MT = 2 for this member, we substitute the values of
fixed-end moments and shears from Eqs. (3) into Eq. (7.19) \(Q_{f}=\left[\begin{matrix} FS_{b}-\frac{3}{2L}FM_{e} \\ FM_{b}-\frac{1}{2}FM_{e} \\ FS_{e}+\frac{3}{2L}FM_{e}\\0 \end{matrix} \right]\) to obtain the fixed-end force vector for the released member 3 as

\(Q_{f3}=\left[\begin{matrix}0-\frac{3(-16.85)}{2(8)} \\ 16.85-\frac{1}{2}(-16.85) \\ 0+\frac{3(-16.85)}{2(8)} \\0 \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix} 3.1595\\25.276\\−3.1595\\0 \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 6 \\ 2 \\ 7\\8 \end{matrix}\) (5)

The relevant elements of \(Q_{f3}\) are stored in \(P_{f}\) using the member code numbers. The completed structure fixed-joint force vector \(P_{f}\) due to the temperature change, is shown in Fig. 7.20(c).

Joint Displacements: The structure stiffness relationship P − \(P_{f}\) = Sd for the entire beam can be written as

\(\left[\begin{matrix}0 \\ -8.4252 \end{matrix} \right] =\begin{bmatrix} 7,140 &1,785\\1,785& 6,247.5 \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix}d_{1} \\ d_{2} \end{matrix} \right]\)

By solving these equations, we determine the joint displacements to be

\(d=\left[\begin{matrix} 3.6308 \\ -14.523 \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 1\\2 \end{matrix} \times 10^{-4}rad\)

Member 1

\(u_{1}=\left[\begin{matrix}0 \\0 \\ 0\\d_{1}\end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 3\\4\\5\\1 \end{matrix}= \left[\begin{matrix}0 \\0 \\ 0\\3.6308\end{matrix} \right] \times 10^{-4}\)

Using \(k_{1}\) from and \(Q_{f1}\) from Eq. (4),
