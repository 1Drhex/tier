Guest Question.

A condenser is employed in a steam power plant to handle 35,000 kg/h of dry and saturated steam at 50°C. The cooling water enters the condenser at 15°C and leaves at 25°C. The tubes are 22.5 mm inside diameter and 25 mm outside diameter. The water flows through the tubes at an average velocity of 2 m/s. The heat transfer coefficient on steam side is \(5000 W/m^2.K.\) Calculate :
(i) The mass flow rate of water.
(ii) Heat transfer surface area.
(iii) Number of tubes required for water flow.
(iv) Number of tube passes in condenser if the length of each tube per pass should not be more than 2.5 m.
Ignore thermal resistance of wall material and assume that the condensate coming out the condenser is saturated water i.e., steam loose only its latent heat.
Use relation ;\( Nu = 0.023 Re^{0.8} Pr^{0.4}\) for determination of waterside heat transfer coefficient. Take latent heat of steam as 2374 kJ/kg Following properties of water at mean bulk temperature of 20°C may be used :
\(ρ = 998.8 kg/m^3,\)
\(C_p = 4180 J/kg.K,\)
\(ν = 1.0006 × 10^{–6} m^2/s,\)
\(k_f = 0.59859 W/m.K.\)

 

 

Admin
Solution

Given : A shell and tube type steam condenser of a steam power plant ;
No. of shell = 1
Hot fluid Cold fluid
\(\dot{m}_h = 35,000 kg/h, u_m = 2 m/s\)
\(h_o = 5,000 W/m^2.K, T_{c, i} = 15°C\)
\(T_h = 50°C, T_{c, o} = 25°C,\)
\(d_o = 25 mm, d_i = 22.5 mm,\)
\(h_{fg} = 2374 kJ/kg.\)
and properties of water and relation.
To find :
(i) Mass flow rate of water.
(ii) Number of tubes required for given water flow rate.
(iii) Heat transfer surface area.
(iv) Number of tube passes for tube length per pass within 2.5 m.
Assumptions :
(i) Assuming no heat loss to the surroundings.
(ii) Fully developed flow through tubes.
(iii) Constant properties.
(iv) Negligible resistances of tube material and scale formation.
Analysis : The heat transfer rate through heat exchanger
\(Q = \dot{m}_h h_{fg}\)
= (35,000/3600)(kg/s) × (2374 kJ/kg)
\(= 23.08 × 10^3 kW\)
(i) The mass flow rate of water :
\(Q = \dot{m}_cC_{p, c} (T_{c, o} – T_{c, i})\)
\(23.08 × 10^3 kW = \dot{m}_c × (4.180 kJ/kg.K) × (25 – 15)\)(K or °C)
\(or \dot{m}_c = 552 kg/s.\)
(ii) The mass flow rate of water through each tube;
(ii) The mass flow rate of water through each tube;
\(\dot{m}_{tube} = ρ Ac u_m = ρ \left ( \frac{\pi }{4} d_i^2\right )u_m\)
\(= (998.8 kg/m^2) × \left \{ \frac{\pi }{4} \times (0.0225 m)^2 \right \} × (2 m/s) = 0.7942 kg/s\)
Hence, the number of tubes required for mass flow rate of 552 kg/s are
\(N = \frac{\dot{m}_c}{\dot{m}_{tube}}=\frac{552}{0.7942} = 695 tubes.\)
(iii) The heat transfer area :
The LMTD for counter flow arrangement
\(∆T_1 = T_{h, o} – T_{c, i} = 50 – 15 = 35°C\)
\(∆T_2 = T_{h, i} – T_{c, o} = 50 – 25 = 25°C\)
\(∆T_{lm, counter} =\frac{∆T_1 – ∆T_2}{ln\left(\frac{∆T_1}{∆T_2} \right)}= \frac{35 – 25}{ln\left(\frac{35}{25} \right)} = 29.72°C\)
The correction factor F for one fluid condensing through condenser is unity.
The corrected LMTD
\(∆T_{lm} = F ∆T_{lm, counter}\)
= 1 × 29.72 = 29.72°C.
Since the heat transfer coefficient on waterside is not given, thus, its calculation is required to determine overall heat transfer coefficient.
Reynolds number
\(Re =\frac{\dot{m}_{tube}}{\pi d_i ρν}= \frac{4 × 0.7942}{\pi × 0.0225 × 998.8 ×1.0006 × 10^{-6}}\)
= 44970
Prandtl number,
\(Pr = \frac{µC_p}{k_f} =\frac{ ρν C_p}{k_f}\)
\(= \frac{998.8 × 1.0006 × 10^{-6} × 4180}{0.59859} = 6.97\)
Using relation
\(Nu =\frac{h_i d_i}{k_f}= 0.023 Re^{0.8} Pr^{0.4}\)
or \(h_i = 0.023 × \frac{k_f}{ d_i}× Re^{0.8} Pr^{0.4}\)
\(= \frac{0.023 × 0.59859}{0.0225}× (44970)^{0.8} × (6.97)^{0.4}\)
\(= 7020 W/m^2.K\)
The overall heat transfer coefficient ;
\(U_o = \frac{1}{\frac{d_o}{d_ih_i} + \frac{1}{h_o}} = \frac{1}{\frac{0.025}{0.225 × 7020 } + \frac{1}{5000}}\)
or \(U_o = 2791 W/m^2.K\)
The heat transfer area of heat exchanger ;
\(Q = U_o A_o ∆T_{lm}\)
or \(A_o = \frac{23.08 × 10^6 W}{(2791 W/m .K) × (29.72 K)}\)
\(= 278.23 m^2. \)
(iv) The number of tube passes for each tube to be 2.5 m long
\(A_o = πd_oL pN\)
or \(p = \frac{278.23 m^2}{(π × 0.025 m × 2.5 m)×695 tubes}\)
= 2 passes.
The each tube will have two passes.
