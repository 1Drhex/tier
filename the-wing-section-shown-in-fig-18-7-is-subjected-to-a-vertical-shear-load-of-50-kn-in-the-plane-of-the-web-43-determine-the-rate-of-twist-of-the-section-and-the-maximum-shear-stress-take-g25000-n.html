Guest Question.

The wing section shown in Fig. 18.7 is subjected to a vertical shear load of 50 kN in the plane of the web 43. Determine the rate of twist of the section and the maximum shear stress. Take \(G=25,000 N / mm ^{2}\).

Admin
Solution

The 50 kN load acting in the plane of the web 43 must be replaced by a 50 kN load acting through the shear center of the section together with a torque. Initially, therefore, we must find the position of the shear center which will, however, lie on the horizontal axis of symmetry. From Section 15.4.5

 

\(I_{x x}=\left(\pi \times 50^{3} \times 2\right) / 2+\left(4 \times 100^{3} / 12\right)+2\left[2 \times 100 \times 50^{2}+\left(2 \times 25^{3} / 12\right)+2 \times 25 \times 37.5^{2}\right]\)

 

i.e.,

 

\(I_{x x}=1.87 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\)

 

To find the position of the shear center we apply an arbitrary shear load, \(S_{y}\), through the shear center S as shown in Fig. 18.7. Then, since \(S_{x}=0\) and \(I_{x y}=0\), Eq. (16.15) reduces to

 

\(q_{s}=-\left(\frac{S_{x} I_{x x}-S_{y} I_{x y}}{I_{x x} I_{y y}-I_{x y}^{2}}\right) \int_{0}^{s} t x d s-\left(\frac{S_{y} I_{y y}-S_{x} I_{x y}}{I_{x x} I_{y y}-I_{x y}^{2}}\right) \int_{0}^{s} t y d s+q_{s, 0}\) (16.15)

 

\(q_{s}=-\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right) \int_{0}^{s} t y d s+q_{s, 0}\) (i)

 

For the open part of the wing section \(q_{s, 0}=0\) at the open edges 1 and 6 while for the closed part of the section \(q_{s, 0}\) may be found using Eq. (16.27) since the rate of twist will be zero for a shear load applied through the shear center. Then

 

\(q_{s, 0}=-\frac{\oint\left(q_{ b } / G t\right) d s}{\oint d s / G t}\) (16.27)

 

\(q_{12}=-\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right) \int_{0}^{s_{1}} t y d s_{1}\)

 

i.e.,

 

\(q_{12}=-\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right) \int_{0}^{s_{1}} 2\left(25+s_{1}\right) d s_{1}\)

 

which gives

 

\(q_{12}=-\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right)\left(50 s_{1}+s_{1}^{2}\right)\) (ii)

 

At the point 2, when \(s_{1}=25 mm , q_{2}=-1,875\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right)\). In the wall 23

 

\(q_{23}=-\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right)\left(\int_{0}^{s_{2}} 2 \times 50 d s_{2}+1,875\right)\)

 

so that

 

\(q_{23}=-\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right)\left(100 s_{2}+1,875\right)\) (iii)

 

When \(s_{2}=100 mm , q_{3}=-11,875\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right)\). Now we “cut” the closed part of the section at 7. Then

 

\(q_{ b , 73}=-\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right) \int_{0}^{\theta} 2 \times 50 \sin \theta \times 50 d \theta\)

 

which gives

 

\(q_{ b , 73}=5,000\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right)(\cos \theta-1)\) (iv)

 

When \(\theta=\pi / 2 \text { at } 3, q_{ b , 3}=-5,000\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right)\). In the wall 34

 

\(q_{ b , 34}=-\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right)\left[\int_{0}^{s_{3}} 4\left(50-s_{3}\right) d s_{3}+5,000+11,875\right]\)

 

which gives

 

\(q_{ b , 34}=\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right)\left(2 s_{3}^{2}-200 s_{3}-16,875\right)\) (v)

 

From Eq. (16.27)

 

\(q_{s, 0}=-\left[\oint\left(q_{ b } / t\right) d s\right] /(\oint( d s / t)\) (vi)

 

where \(\oint d s / t=(\pi \times 50 / 2)+(100 / 4)=103.5\) and

 

\(\oint\left(q_{ b } / t\right) d s=2\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right)\left[\int_{0}^{\pi / 2}(5,000 / 2)(\cos \theta-1) 50 d \theta+\int_{0}^{50}(1 / 4)\left(2 s_{3}^{2}-200 s_{3}-16,875\right) d s_{3}\right]\)

 

Integrating and substituting the limits gives

 

\(\oint\left(q_{ b } / t\right) d s=-647,908\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right)\)

 

so that from Eq. (vi)

 

\(q_{s, 0}=6,260\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right)\)

 

Then

 

\(q_{73}=\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right)(5,000 \cos \theta+1,260)\) (vii)

 

and

 

\(q_{34}=\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right)\left(2 s_{3}^{2}-200 s_{3}-10,615\right)\) (viii)

 

Now taking moments about the center of the web 34

 

\(S_{y} \xi_{ S }=2\left[\int_{0}^{\pi / 2} q_{73} \times 50^{2} d \theta-\int_{0}^{25} q_{12} \times 100 d s_{1}-\int_{0}^{100} q_{23} \times 50 d s_{2}\right]\)

 

Substituting for \(q_{73}\) etc. from Eqs. (vii), (ii), and (iii) and carrying out the integration gives

 

\(\xi_{ S }=57.7 mm\)

 

The actual loading on the wing section of 50 kN in the plane of the web 43 is therefore equivalent to a 50 kN load acting through the shear center together with an anticlockwise torque of \(50 \times 10^{3} \times 57.7=2885 Nm\). The shear flow distribution due to the 50 kN load acting through the shear center is given by Eqs. (ii), (iii), (vii), and (viii) in which \(S_{y}=50 kN\). Then

 

\(q_{12}=-\left(50 \times 10^{3} / 1.87 \times 10^{6}\right)\left(50 s_{1}+s_{1}^{2}\right)=-0.027\left(50 s_{1}+s_{1}{ }^{2}\right)\) (ix)

 

\(q_{23}=-0.027\left(100 s_{2}+1,875\right)\) (x)

 

\(q_{73}=0.027(5,000 \cos \theta+1,260)\) (xi)

 

\(q_{34}=0.027\left(2 s_{3}^{2}-200 s_{3}-10,615\right)\) (xii)

 

The maximum shear flow in the wall 12 occurs, from Eq. (ix), at \(s_{1}=25 mm\) and is equal to –50.1 N/mm. From Eq. (x) the maximum shear flow in the wall 23 occurs when \(s_{2}=100 mm\) and is equal to -320.6 N/mm. From Eq. (xi) the maximum shear flow in the wall 73 occurs when \(\theta=0\) and is +169.0 N/mm. At 3 in the wall 73 the shear flow is 34.0 N/mm. Also, in the wall 34 at 3, the shear flow, from Eq. (xii), is 286.6 N/mm and the maximum value of the shear flow in the wall 34 occurs when \(s_{3}=50 mm\) and is equal to –421.6 N/mm. We shall assume, as in , that the torsional rigidity, GJ, of the complete section is the sum of the torsional rigidities of the closed and open sections. For the closed portion, from Eq. (17.4)

 

\(\frac{ d \theta}{ d z}=\frac{T}{4 A^{2}} \oint \frac{ d s}{G t}\) (17.4)

 

\((G J)_{ cl }=\left(4 A^{2} / G\right) /(\oint d s / t)=\left[4\left(\pi \times 50^{2} / 2\right)^{2} \times 25,000\right] / 103.5=1,487 \times 10^{7} Nmm ^{2}\)

 

From Eq. (17.11)

 

\(J=\sum \frac{s t^{3}}{3} \quad \text { or } \quad J=\frac{1}{3} \int_{\text {sect }} t^{3} d s\) (17.11)

 

\((G J)_{ op }=25,000 \times 2\left(100 \times 2^{3}+25 \times 2^{3}\right) / 3=1.7 \times 10^{7} Nmm ^{2}\)

 

The torsional rigidity of the complete section is then

 

\(G J=1,487 \times 10^{7}+1.7 \times 10^{7}=1,489 \times 10^{7} Nmm ^{2}\)

 

The rate of twist in the combined section is given by

 

\(( d \theta / d z)=(T / G J)=2,885 \times 10^{3} / 1,489 \times 10^{7}=1.94 \times 10^{-4} rad / mm\)

 

Substituting for the torque, T, in Eq. (17.1) from Eq. (17.4) we obtain the shear flow in the closed part of the section, i.e.,

 

\(T=2 A q\) (17.1)

 

\(q_{ cl }=\left[(G J)_{ cl } / 2 A\right]( d \theta / d z)=\left[1,487 \times 10^{7} / 2\left(\pi \times 50^{2} / 2\right)\right] \times 1.94 \times 10^{-4}=367.3 N / mm\)

 

which is due to torsion. In the open part of the section the maximum shear stress is obtained directly from Eq. (17.10) and is

 

\(\tau_{z s, \max }=\pm G t \frac{ d \theta}{ d z}\) (17.10)

 

\(\tau_{\max , \text { op }}=25,000 \times 2 \times 1.94 \times 10^{-4}=9.7 N / mm ^{2}\)

 

We shall now examine the complete shear stress distribution to determine its maximum value. The maximum shear flow due to shear in the open part of the section occurs at 3 in the wall 23 and is 320.6 N/mm so that the corre corresponding shear stress is 320.6/2=160.3 \(N / mm ^{2}\) giving a maximum shear stress of 160.3+9.7=170.0 \(N / mm ^{2}\). The maximum shear flow due to shear in the closed part of the section is 421.6 N/mm in the direction 43 while the shear flow due to torsion is 363.5 N/mm in the direction 34 giving a resultant shear flow of 421.6 – 363.5=58.1 N/mm in the direction 43; the corresponding shear stress is 58.1/4=14.5 \(N / mm ^{2}\). At 3 in the web 34 the combined shear flow due to shear and torsion is 363.5 – 286.6=76.9 N/mm in the direction 34; the corresponding shear stress is 76.9/4=19.2 \(N / mm ^{2}\). In the wall 73 the maximum shear flow due to shear and torsion is 169.0+363.5=532.5 N/mm giving a shear stress of 532.5/2=266.3 \(N / mm ^{2}\). We conclude, therefore, that the maximum shear stress in the combined section is 266.3 \(N / mm ^{2}\) and occurs at the point 7 in the wall 73.
