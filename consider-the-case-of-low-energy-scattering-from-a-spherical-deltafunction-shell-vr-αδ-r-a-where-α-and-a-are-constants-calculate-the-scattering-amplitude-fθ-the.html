Guest Question.

Consider the case of low-energy scattering from a spherical delta-function shell:

\( V(r)=\alpha \delta(r-a) \),

where α and a are constants. Calculate the scattering amplitude ,f(θ) , the differential cross-section, D(θ), and the total cross-section, σ. Assume \( k a \ll 1 \), so that only the \( \ell=0 \) term contributes significantly. (To simplify matters, throw out all \( \ell \neq 0 \) terms right from the start.) The main problem, of course, is to determine \( C_0\) . Express your answer in terms of the dimensionless quantity \( \beta \equiv 2 \operatorname{ma\alpha} / \hbar^{2} \cdot \text { Answer: } \sigma=4 \pi a^{2} \beta^{2} /(1+\beta)^{2} \).

Admin
Solution

Keeping only the \( \ell=0 \) terms, Eq. 10.29 says that in the exterior region:

\( \psi(r, \theta)=A \sum_{\ell=0}^{\infty} i^{\ell}(2 \ell+1)\left[j_{\ell}(k r)+i k a_{\ell} h_{\ell}^{(1)}(k r)\right] P_{\ell}(\cos \theta) \) (10.29)

\( \psi \approx A\left[j_{0}(k r)+i k a_{0} h_{0}^{(1)}(k r)\right] P_{0}(\cos \theta)=A\left[\frac{\sin (k r)}{k r}+i k a_{0}\left(-i \frac{e^{i k r}}{k r}\right)\right]=A\left[\frac{\sin (k r)}{k r}+a_{0} \frac{e^{i k r}}{r}\right] \quad(r>a) \).

In the internal region Eq. 10.18 (with \( n_{\ell} \) eliminated because it blows up at the origin) yields

\( u(r)=\operatorname{Arj}_{\ell}(k r)+\operatorname{Brn} \ell(k r) \) (10.18).

\( \psi(r) \approx b j_{0}(k r)=b \frac{\sin (k r)}{k r} \quad(r<a) \).

The boundary conditions hold independently for each \( \ell \), as you can check by keeping the summation over \( \ell \) and exploiting the orthogonality of the Legendre polynomials :

(1) ψ continuous at r = a: \( A\left[\frac{\sin k a}{k a}+a_{0} \frac{e^{i k a}}{a}\right]=b \frac{\sin k a}{k a} \).

(2) ψ′ discontinuous at r = a: Integrating the radial equation across the delta function gives

\( -\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \int \frac{d^{2} u}{d r^{2}} d r+\int\left[\alpha \delta(r-a)+\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \frac{\ell(\ell+1)}{r^{2}}\right] u d r=E \int u d r \Rightarrow-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \Delta u^{\prime}+\alpha u(a)=0, \text { or } \Delta u^{\prime}=\frac{2 m \alpha}{\hbar^{2}} u(a) \).

\( \text { Now } u=r R, \quad \text { so } u^{\prime}=R+r R^{\prime} ; \quad \Delta u^{\prime}=\Delta R+a \Delta R^{\prime}=a \Delta R^{\prime}=\frac{2 m \alpha}{\hbar^{2}} a R(a), \text { or } \Delta \psi^{\prime}=\frac{2 m \alpha}{\hbar^{2}} \psi(a)=\frac{\beta}{a} \psi(a) \).

\( \frac{A}{k a}\left[k \cos (k a)+a_{0} i k^{2} e^{i k a}\right]- \frac{A}{k a^{2}}\cancel {\left[\sin (k a)+a_{0} k e^{i k a}\right]}-\frac{b}{k a} k \cos (k a)+\cancel {\frac{b}{k a^{2}} \sin k a}=\frac{\beta}{a} b \frac{\sin (k a)}{k a} \).

The indicated terms cancel (by (1)), leaving \( A\left[\cos (k a)+i a_{0} k e^{i k a}\right]=b\left[\cos (k a)+\frac{\beta}{k a} \sin (k a)\right] \).

Using (1) to eliminate b: \( A\left[\cos (k a)+i a_{0} k e^{i k a}\right]=\left[\cot (k a)+\frac{\beta}{k a}\right]\left[\sin (k a)+a_{0} k e^{i k a}\right] A \).

\( \cancel {\cos (k a)}+i a_{0} k e^{i k a}=\cancel {\cos (k a)}+\frac{\beta}{k a} \sin (k a)+a_{0} k \cot (k a) e^{i k a}+\beta \frac{a_{0}}{a} e^{i k a} \).

\( i a_{0} k e^{i k a}\left[1+i \cot (k a)+i \frac{\beta}{k a}\right]=\frac{\beta}{k a} \sin (k a) . \quad \text { But } k a \ll 1, \text { so } \sin (k a) \approx k a, \text { and } \cot (k a)=\frac{\cos (k a)}{\sin (k a)} \approx \frac{1}{k a} \).

\( i a_{0} k(1+i k a)\left[1+\frac{i}{k a}(1+\beta)\right]=\beta ; \quad i a_{0} k\left[1+\frac{i}{k a}(1+\beta)+i k a-1-\beta\right] \approx i a_{0} k\left[\frac{i}{k a}(1+\beta)\right]=\beta \).

\( a_{0}=-\frac{a \beta}{1+\beta} \) Equation 10.25 ⇒\( f(\theta) \approx a_{0}=-\frac{a \beta}{1+\beta} \) . Equation 10.14 \( \Rightarrow D=|f|^{2}=\left(\frac{a \beta}{1+\beta}\right)^{2} \).

\( f(\theta)=\sum_{\ell=0}^{\infty}(2 \ell+1) a_{\ell} P_{\ell}(\cos \theta) \) (10.25).

\( D(\theta)=\frac{d \sigma}{d \Omega}=|f(\theta)|^{2} \) (10.14).

Equation 10.27 \( \Rightarrow \sigma=4 \pi D=4 \pi\left(\frac{a \beta}{1+\beta}\right)^{2} \).

\( \sigma=4 \pi \sum_{\ell=0}^{\infty}(2 \ell+1)\left|a_{\ell}\right|^{2} \).
