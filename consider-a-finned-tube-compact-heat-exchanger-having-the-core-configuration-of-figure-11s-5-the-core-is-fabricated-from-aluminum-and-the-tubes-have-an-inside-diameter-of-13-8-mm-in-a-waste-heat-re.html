Guest Question.

Consider a finned-tube, compact heat exchanger having the core configuration of Figure 11S.5. The core is fabricated from aluminum, and the tubes have an inside diameter of 13.8 mm. In a waste heat recovery application, water flow through the tubes provides an inside convection coefficient of \(h_{i}=1500 W / m ^{2} \cdot K,\) while combustion gases at 1atm and 825 K are in cross flow over the tubes. If the gas flow rate is 1.25 kg/s and the frontal area is \(0.20 m ^{2},\) what is the gas-side overall heat transfer coefficient? If a water flow rate of 1 kg/s is to be heated from 290 to 370 K, what is the required heat exchanger volume?

Admin
Solution

Known: Compact heat exchanger geometry, gas-side flow rate and temperature, and water-side convection coefficient. Water flow rate and inlet and outlet temperatures.

Find: Gas-side overall heat transfer coefficient. Heat exchanger volume.

Assumptions:
1. Gas has properties of atmospheric air at an assumed mean temperature of 700 K.
2. Fouling is negligible.

Analysis: Referring to Equation 11.1b, the combustion gas and the water are the hot and cold fluids, respectively. Hence, neglecting fouling effects and acknowledging that the tube inner surface is not finned \(\left(\eta_{o, c}=1\right),\) the overall heat transfer coefficient based on the gas-(hot) side surface area is given by

\(\frac{1}{UA} = \frac{1}{(\eta _{o}hA)_{c}} + \frac{R^{\prime \prime }_{f,c}}{(\eta _{o}A)_{c}} + R_{w} + \frac{R^{\prime \prime }_{f,h}}{(\eta _{o}A)_{h}} + \frac{1}{(\eta _{o}hA)_{h}} \) (11.1b)

\(\frac{1}{U_{h}}=\frac{1}{h_{c}\left(A_{c} / A_{h}\right)}+A_{h} R_{w}+\frac{1}{\eta_{o, h} h_{h}}\)

 

where \(A_{h}\) and \(A_{c}\) are the total gas-side (hot) and water-side (cold) surface areas, respectively. If the fin thickness is assumed to be negligible, it is readily shown that

\(\frac{A_{c}}{A_{h}} \approx \frac{D_{i}}{D_{o}}\left(1-\frac{A_{f, h}}{A_{h}}\right)\)

 

where \(A_{f, h}\) is that portion of the total gas-side area associated with the fins. The approximation is valid to within 10%, and for the heat exchanger core conditions (Figure 11S.5)

\(\frac{A_{c}}{A_{h}} \approx \frac{13.8}{16.4}(1-0.830)=0.143\)

 

Obtaining the wall conduction resistance from Equation 3.33, it follows that

\(R_{t, \text { cond }}=\frac{\ln \left(r_{2} / r_{1}\right)}{2 \pi L k}\) (3.33)

 

\(A_{h} R_{w}=\frac{\ln \left(D_{o} / D_{i}\right)}{2 \pi L k / A_{h}}=\frac{D_{i} \ln \left(D_{o} / D_{i}\right)}{2 k\left(A_{c} / A_{h}\right)}\)

 

Hence

\(A_{h} R_{w}=\frac{(0.0138 m ) \ln (16.4 / 13.8)}{2(237 W / m \cdot K )(0.143)}=3.51 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2} \cdot K / W\)

 

The gas-side convection coefficient may be obtained by first using Equation 11S.2 to evaluate the mass velocity:

\(G \equiv \rho V_{\max }=\frac{\rho V A_{ fr }}{A_{ ff }}=\frac{\dot{m}}{A_{ ff }}=\frac{\dot{m}}{\sigma A_{ fr }}\) (11S.2)

 

\(G=\frac{\dot{m}}{\sigma A_{ fr }}=\frac{1.25 kg / s }{0.449 \times 0.20 m ^{2}}=13.9 kg / s \cdot m ^{2}\)

 

Hence

\(R e=\frac{13.9 kg / s \cdot m ^{2} \times 6.68 \times 10^{-3} m }{338.8 \times 10^{-7} kg / s \cdot m }=2740\)

 

and from Figure 11S.5, \(j_{H} \approx 0.010 .\) Hence

\(h_{h} \approx 0.010 \frac{G c_{p}}{P r^{2 / 3}}=0.010 \frac{\left(13.9 kg / s \cdot m ^{2}\right)(1075 J / kg \cdot K )}{(0.695)^{2 / 3}}\)

 

\(=190 W / m ^{2} \cdot K\)

 

To obtain the hot-side temperature effectiveness from Equation 11.3, the fin efficiency must first be determined from Figure 3.20. With \(r_{2 c}=14.38 mm , r_{2 c} / r_{1}=1.75, L_{c}=6.18 mm, A_{p}=1.57 \times 10^{-6} m ^{2} \text { , and } L_{c}^{3 / 2}\left(h_{h} / k A_{p}\right)^{1 / 2}=0.34,\) it follows that \(\eta_{f} \approx 0.89.\) Hence

 

\(\eta_{o}=1-\frac{A_{f}}{A}\left(1-\eta_{f}\right)\) (11.3)

 

\(\eta_{o, h}=1-\frac{A_{f}}{A}\left(1-\eta_{f}\right)=1-0.830(1-0.89)=0.91\)

 

We then obtain

\(\frac{1}{U_{h}}=\left(\frac{1}{1500 \times 0.143}+3.51 \times 10^{-5}+\frac{1}{0.91 \times 190}\right) m ^{2} \cdot K / W\)

 

\(\frac{1}{U_{h}}=\left(4.66 \times 10^{-3}+3.51 \times 10^{-5}+5.78 \times 10^{-3}\right)=0.010 m ^{2} \cdot K / W\)

 

or

\(U_{h}=100 W / m ^{2} \cdot K\)

 

With \(C_{c}=\dot{m}_{c} c_{p, c}=1 kg / s \times 4184 J / kg \cdot K =4184 W / K,\) the heat exchanger must be large enough to transfer heat in the amount

\(q=C_{c}\left(T_{c, o}-T_{c, i}\right)=4184 W / K (370-290) K =3.35 \times 10^{5} W\)

 

With \(C_{h}=\dot{m}_{h} c_{p, h}=1.25 kg / s \times 1075 J / kg \cdot K =1344 W / K,\) the minimum heat capacity rate corresponds to the hot fluid and the maximum possible heat transfer rate is

\(q_{\max }=C_{\min }\left(T_{h, i}-T_{c, i}\right)=1344 W / K (825-290) K =7.19 \times 10^{5} W\)

 

It follows that

\(\varepsilon=\frac{q}{q_{\max }}=\frac{3.35 \times 10^{5} W }{7.19 \times 10^{5} W }=0.466\)

 

Hence, with \(\left(C_{\min } / C_{\max }\right)=0.321 \text { , }\) Figure 11.14 (cross- flow heat exchanger with both fluids unmixed) yields

\(NTU =\frac{U_{h} A_{h}}{C_{\min }} \approx 0.65\)

 

The required gas-side heat transfer surface area is then

\(A_{h}=\frac{0.65 \times 1344 W / K }{100 W / m ^{2} \cdot K }=8.7 m ^{2}\)

 

With the gas-side surface area per unit heat exchanger volume corresponding to \(\alpha=269 m ^{2} / m ^{3}\) (Figure 11S.5), the required heat exchanger volume is

\(V=\frac{A_{h}}{\alpha}=\frac{8.7 m ^{2}}{269 m ^{2} / m ^{3}}=0.032 m ^{3}\)

 

Comments:
1. The effect of the tube wall thermal conduction resistance is negligible, while contributions due to the cold- and hot-side convection resistances are comparable.

2. Knowledge of the heat exchanger volume yields the heat exchanger length in the gasflow direction, \(L=V / A_{ fr }=0.032 m ^{3} / 0.20 m ^{2}=0.16 m,\) from which the number of tube rows in the flow direction may be determined.

\(N_{L} \approx \frac{L-D_{f}}{S_{L}}+1=\frac{(160-28.5) mm }{34.3 mm }+1=4.8 \approx 5\)

 

3. The temperature of the gas leaving the heat exchanger is

\(T_{h, o}=T_{h, i}-\frac{q}{C_{h}}=825 K -\frac{3.35 \times 10^{5} W }{1344 W / K }=576 K\)

 

Hence the assumption of \(\overline{T_{h}}=700 K\) is excellent.

4. From Figure 11S.5, the friction factor is \(f \approx 0.033 . \text { With }\left(A / A_{ ff }\right)=\left(\alpha V / \sigma A_{ fr }\right)=(269 \times 0.032 / 0.449 \times 0.20)=96, v_{i}(825 K )=2.37 m ^{3} / kg , v_{o}(576 K )=1.65 m ^{3} / kg , \text { and } v_{m}= 2.01 m ^{3} / kg,\) Equation 11S.3 yields a pressure drop of

\(\Delta p=\frac{G^{2} v_{i}}{2}\left[\left(1+\sigma^{2}\right)\left(\frac{v_{o}}{v_{i}}-1\right)+f \frac{A}{A_{ ff }} \frac{v_{m}}{v_{i}}\right]\) (11S.3)

 

\(\Delta p=\frac{\left(13.9 kg / s \cdot m ^{2}\right)^{2}\left(2.37 m ^{3} / kg \right)}{2}[(1+0.202)(0.696-1)+0.033 \times 96 \times 0.848]\)

 

\(\Delta p=530 kg / s ^{2} \cdot m =530 N / m ^{2}\)
