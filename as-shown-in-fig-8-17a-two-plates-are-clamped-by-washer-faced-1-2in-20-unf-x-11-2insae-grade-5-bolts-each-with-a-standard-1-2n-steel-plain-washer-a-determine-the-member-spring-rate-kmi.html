Guest Question.

As shown in Fig.8-17 a, two plates are clamped by washer-faced \(\frac{1}{2} in-20 \ UNF \times 1 \frac{1}{2}\) in SAE grade 5 bolts each with a standard\(\frac{1}{2} \ N\) steel plain washer.
(a) Determine the member spring rate \(k_{m}\) if the top plate is steel and the bottom plate is gray cast iron.
(b) Using the method of conical frusta, determine the member spring rate \(k_{m}\) if both plates are steel.
(c) Using Eq. (8-23), determine the member spring rate \(k_{m}\) if both plates are steel. Compare the results with part \((b)\).
(d) Determine the bolt spring rate \(k_{b}\).

Admin
Solution

From

the thickness of a standard \(\frac {1}{2} N\) plain washer is 0.095 in.
(a) As shown in Fig. 8–17b, the frusta extend halfway into the joint the distance
\( \frac {1}{2} \left(0.5 + 0.75 + 0.095\right) = 0.6725 \) in

The distance between the joint line and the dotted frusta line is \(0.6725 − 0.5 −0.095 = 0.0775\) in.
Thus, the top frusta consist of the steel washer, steel plate, and 0.0775 in of the cast iron.
Since the washer and top plate are both steel with \( E = 30 \left(10^6\right) \) psi, they can be considered a single frustum of 0.595 in thick.
The outer diameter of the frustum of the steel member at the joint interface is \(0.75 + 2 \left(0.595\right) tan 30° = 1.437\) in. The outer diameter at the midpoint of the entire joint is \(0.75 +2(0.6725) \tan 30° = 1.527\) in.
Using Eq. (8–20),
\(k=\frac{0.5774\pi Ed}{\ln \frac{\left(1.155t+D-d\right)\left(D+d\right) }{\left(1.155t+D+d\right)\left(D-d\right) } } \)
the spring rate of the steel is
\(k_1 = \frac{0.5774\pi \left(30\right)\left(10^6\right)0.5 }{\ln \left\{\frac{\left[1.155\left(0.595\right) +0.75-0.5\right]\left(0.75+0.5\right) }{\left[1.155\left(0.595\right)+0.75+0.5\right]\left(0.75-0.5\right) }\right\} }= 30.80 \left(10^6\right) \ lbf/in \)

For the upper cast-iron frustum
\(k_2 = \frac{0.5774\pi \left(14.5\right)\left(10^6\right)0.5 }{\ln \left\{\frac{\left[1.155\left(0.595\right) +1.437-0.5\right]\left(1.437+0.5\right) }{\left[1.155\left(0.0775\right)+1.437+0.5\right]\left(1.437-0.5\right) }\right\} }= 285.5 \left(10^6\right) \ lbf/in \)
For the lower cast-iron frustum
\(k_3 = \frac{0.5774\pi \left(14.5\right)\left(10^6\right)0.5 }{\ln\left\{ \frac{\left[1.155\left(0.6725\right) +0.75-0.5\right]\left(0.75+0.5\right) }{\left[1.155\left(0.6725\right)+0.75+0.5\right]\left(0.75-0.5\right) }\right\} }= 14.15 \left(10^6\right) \ lbf/in \)
The three frusta are in series, so from Eq. (8–18)

\(\frac{1}{k_m} =\frac{1}{k_1} +\frac{1}{k_2} +\frac{1}{k_3} +\cdot \cdot \cdot \cdot +\frac{1}{k_i} \)

 

\(\frac {1}{k_m}= \frac {1}{30.80 \left(10^6\right)}+ \frac {1}{285.5 \left(10^6\right)}+\frac{1}{14.15 \left(10^6\right)}\)

 

This results in \(k_m= 9.378 \left(10^6\right) \ lbf/in\).
(b) If the entire joint is steel, Eq. (8–22)

 

\(k_m=\frac{0.5774 \pi Ed}{2 \ln\left(5\frac{0.5774l+0.5d}{0.5774l+2.5d} \right) } \)

 

with \(l = 2 \left(0.6725\right) = 1.345 \) in gives

 

\(k_m=\frac{0.5774 \pi \left(30.0\right) \left(10^6\right)0.5}{2 \ln\left\{5\left[\frac{0.5774\left(1.345\right)+0.5\left(0.5\right) }{0.5774\left(1.345\right)+2.5\left(0.5\right)} \right]\right\} }=14.64\left(10^6\right) \ lbf/in \)
