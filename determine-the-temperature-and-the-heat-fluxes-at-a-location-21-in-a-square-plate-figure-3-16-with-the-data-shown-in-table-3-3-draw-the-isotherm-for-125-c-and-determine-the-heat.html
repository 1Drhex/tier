Guest Question.

Determine the temperature and the heat fluxes at a location (2,1) in a square plate (Figure 3.16) with the data shown in Table 3.3. Draw the isotherm for \( 125^{\circ} C\) and determine the heat fluxes if \( k_{x}=k_{y}=2 W / m ^{\circ} C\)

Table 3.3 Data for Example 3.2.3
Node no. x(cm) y(cm) Temperature ◦C
1 0.0 0.0 100.0
2 5.0 0.0 150.0
3 5.0 5.0 200.0
4 0.0 5.0 50.0
Admin
Solution

Note that the origin is at node 1 . In order to use the shape functions already derived, we can give the coordinates of the nodes with the origin at the center of the square plate. Note that 2 a=2 b=5.0 .

The temperature at any point within the element can be expressed as

\(T=N_{1} T_{1}+N_{2} T_{2}+N_{3} T_{3}+N_{4} T_{4}\) (3.97)

The location of the point (2,1), using the nondimensional coordinates and new origin at the center, is (-0.5,-1.5) The shape functions at this point are calculated by substituting the new coordinates of point (2, I), that is,

\(\begin{array}{l}N_{1}=\frac{1}{4 a b}(b-x)(a-y)=\frac{12}{25} \\N_{2}=\frac{1}{4 a b}(b+x)(a-y)=\frac{8}{25} \\N_{3}=\frac{1}{4 a b}(b+x)(a+y)=\frac{2}{25} \\N_{4}=\frac{1}{4 a b}(b-x)(a+y)=\frac{3}{25}\end{array}\) (3.98)

\(\begin{aligned}&\text { Note that } N_{1}+N_{2}+N_{3}+N_{4}=I .\\&\text { Therefore, the temperature at the point }(-0.5,-1.5) \text { is }\end{aligned}\)

\(T_{(-0.5,1.5)}=\frac{12}{25}(100)+\frac{8}{25}(150)+\frac{2}{25}(200)+\frac{3}{25}(50)=118^{\circ} C\) (3.99)

Table 3.4 Nondimensional coordinates for Example 3.2.3
1 -2.5 -2.5
2 2.5 -2.5
3 2.5 2.5
4 -2.5 2.5

 

\(\text { The heat fuxes can be calculated from Equation }(3.95) \text { as follows: }\) \(\left\{\begin{array}{l}q_{x} \\q_{y}\end{array}\right\}=-\left\{\begin{array}{l}k_{x} \frac{\partial T}{\partial x} \\k_{y} \frac{\partial T}{\partial y}\end{array}\right\}\)

\(=-\frac{2}{25}\left[\begin{array}{cccc}-4.0 & 4.0 & 1.0 & -1.0 \\-3.0 & -2.0 & 2.0 & 3.0\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{c}100.0 \\150.0 \\200.0 \\50.0\end{array}\right\}\) (3.100)

\(=\left\{\begin{array}{c}-28.0 \\4.0\end{array}\right\} W / cm ^{2}\)

The isotherm of \( 125^{\circ} C\) will not normally be a straight-line due to the bilinear nature of the elements. Thus, we need more than two points to represent an isotherm. It is certain that one point on side 1-2 and one on 3-4 will contain a point with a temperature of \( 125^{\circ} C\). We know the y coordinates of both the sides 1-2 and 3-4. Thus, the x coordinate of the point on side 1-2 which has a temperature of \( 125^{\circ} C\) is calculated by substituting y=0.0 into the temperature distribution of Equation (3.97),

\(T=N_{1} T_{1}+N_{2} T_{2}+N_{3} T_{3}+N_{4} T_{4}\)

that is,

\(\begin{array}{r}125.0=\frac{1}{25}[(2.5-x)(2.5-0.0) 100.0+(2.5+x)(2.5-0.0) 150 \\+(2.5+x)(2.5+0.0) 200.0+(2.5-x)(2.5+0.0) 50.0 .\end{array}\) (3.101)

which gives x=2.5 and similarly, if we substitute a value of y=5.0 for the side 3-4 the results is x=2.5 . These coordinates can be written in a nondimensionalform as (0.0,-2.5) and (0.0,2.5). From the two points found, it is clear that the \(125^{\circ} C\) isotherm crosses all horizontal lines between the bottom and top sides. Therefore, to determine another point, we can assume a y value of 2.5(0.0, in nondimensional form ) and on substituting into Equation (3.97) results in an x coordinate of 2.5(0.0, in nondimensional form ) Connecting all three points will generate the \(125^{\circ} C\) isotherm.
