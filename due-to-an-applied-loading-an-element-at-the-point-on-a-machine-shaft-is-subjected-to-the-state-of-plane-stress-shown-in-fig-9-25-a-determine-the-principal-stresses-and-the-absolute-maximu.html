Guest Question.

Due to an applied loading, an element at the point on a machine shaft is subjected to the state of plane stress shown in Fig. 9 –25 a . Determine the principal stresses and the absolute maximum shear stress at the point.

Admin
Solution

Principal Stresses. The in-plane principal stresses can be determined from Mohr’s circle. The center of the circle is on the \(\sigma\) axis at \(\sigma_{avg} = (-20 + 0)/2 = -10 psi\). Plotting the reference point A(-20, -40), the radius CA is established and the circle is drawn as shown in Fig. 9–25 b . The radius is

\(R=\sqrt{(20-10)^2+(40)^2}=41.2psi\)

The principal stresses are at the points where the circle intersects the \(\sigma\) axis; i.e.,

\(\sigma_1=-10+41.2=31.2psi\)

 

\(\sigma_2=-10-41.2=-51.2psi\)

From the circle, the counterclockwise angle \(2\theta\), measured from CA to the \(-\sigma\) axis, is

\(2\theta = tan^{-1}(\frac{40}{20-10})=76.0°\)

Thus,

\(\theta=38.0°\)

This counterclockwise rotation defines the direction of the x′ axis and \(\sigma_2\) and its associated principal plane, Fig. 9–25 c. We have

\(\sigma_1=31.2psi \quad\quad \sigma_2=-51.2psi\)

Absolute Maximum Shear Stress. Since these stresses have opposite signs, applying

\(\tau_{\overset{abs}{_{max}} }=\frac{\sigma_1-\sigma_2}{2}\)

\(\sigma_1 \quad and \quad \sigma_2 \quad have \quad opposite \quad signs\) we have

\(\tau_{\overset{abs}{_{max}} }=\frac{\sigma_1-\sigma_2}{2}=\frac{31.2-(-51.2)}{2}=41.2psi \)

 

\(\sigma_{avg}=\frac{31.2-51.2}{2}=-10psi\)

NOTE: These same results can also be obtained by drawing Mohr’s circle for each orientation of an element about the x, y, and z axes, Fig. 9–25 d . Since \(\sigma_1\) and \(\sigma_2\) are of opposite signs , then the absolute maximum shear stress equals the maximum in-plane shear stress.
