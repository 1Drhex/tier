Guest Question.

Determine the joint displacements, member end forces, and support reactions for the plane frame shown in Fig. 7.21(a) due to the combined effect of the following: (a) a temperature increase of 75° F in the girder, and (b) the fabricated length of the left column being \(\frac{1}{4}\) in. too short. Use the matrix stiffness method.

Admin
Solution

This frame, subjected to joint and member loads, was analyzed in

Analytical Model: See Fig. 7.21(b). The analytical model used herein is the same as used in Example 7.1.

Structure Stiffness Matrix: As determined in , the structure stiffness matrix
for the frame, in units of kips and inches, is

\(S=\left[\begin{matrix}1,781.3& 0& −1,776.3& 0 &0\\ 0& 1,781.3& 0 &−5.0347& 1,208.3 \\−1,776.3 &0& 1,781.3& 0& 1,208.3\\0& −5.0347 &0& 1,781.3& −1,208.3\\0& 1,208.3& 1,208.3& −1,208.3& 580,000 \end{matrix} \right]\) (1)

Joint Load Vector:

P=0 (2)

Structure Fixed-Joint Force Vector Due to Temperature Changes and
Fabrication Errors:

Member 1 (MT = 2) By substituting the numerical values of E,A, L, and \(e_{a} =- \frac{1}{4} in\).
into Eq. (7.35) \(FA_{b}=-FA_{e}=\frac{EA}{L}e_{a}\), we obtain the member fixed-end forces, due to the specified fabrication error, as

\(FA_{b} = −FA_{e} = −444.06 k\)

\(FS_{b} = FS_{e} = FM_{b} = FM_{e} = 0\)

As MT = 2, we use Eq. (7.10) \(Q_{f}=\left[\begin{matrix}FA_{b} \\ FS_{b}-\frac{3}{2L}FM_{e} \\FM_{b}-\frac{1}{2}FM_{e}\\FA_{e}\\FS_{e}+\frac{3}{2L}FM_{e} \\0 \end{matrix} \right]\) to form the member local fixed-end force vector. Thus,

\(Q_{f1}=\left[\begin{matrix}-444.06\\0\\0\\444.06\\0\\0 \end{matrix} \right]\) (3)

Using the member transformation matrix, \(T_{1}\), from , we evaluate the global fixed-end force vector as

\(F_{f1}=T^{T}_{1}Q_{f1}\left[\begin{matrix}0\\-444.06\\\underset{…………}{0} \\0\\\underset{………….} {444.06}\\0 \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 6\\7\\8\\1\\2\\9 \end{matrix}\)

Next, using the member code numbers, we store the pertinent elements of \(F_{f1}\) in their proper positions in the 5 × 1 structure fixed-joint force vector \(P_{f}\) , as shown in Fig. 7.21(c).

Member 2 (MT = 1) Bysubstituting the numerical values of E,A, α, and \(T_{u}\) = 75° F into Eq. (7.34) \(FA_{b}=-FA_{e}=EA\alpha T_{u}\) , we evaluate the member fixed-end forces, due to the given temperature change:

\(FA_{b} = −FA_{e} = 207.82 k\)

\(FS_{b} = FS_{e} = FM_{b} = FM_{e} = 0\)

As MT = 1, we use Eq. (7.6) \(Q_{f}=\left[\begin{matrix} FA_{b}\\FS_{b}-\frac{3}{2L}FM_{b} \\0\\FA_{e} \\ FS_{e}+\frac{3}{2L}FM_{b}\\FM_{e}-\frac{1}{2}FM_{b} \end{matrix} \right]\) to form this horizontal member’s local and global fixedend force vectors:

\(F_{f2}=Q_{f2}\left[\begin{matrix}207.82\\\underset{…………}{0} \\\underset{………….} {0}\\-207.82\\0\\0 \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 1\\2\\9\\3\\4\\5 \end{matrix}\) (4)

The relevant elements of \(F_{f2}\) are stored in \(P_{f}\) using the member code numbers. The completed structure fixed-joint force vector \(P_{f}\), due to the temperature change and fabrication error, is given in Fig. 7.21(c).

Joint Displacements: Solving the structure stiffness relationship P − \(P_{f}\) = Sd, we obtain the following joint displacements.

\(d=\left[\begin{matrix} −0.12199 in.\\−0.24965 in.\\−0.0053343 in.\\−0.00034577 in.\\0.00053051 rad \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix}1\\2\\3\\4\\5 \end{matrix}\)

Member End Displacements and End Forces:

Member 1 (MT = 2)

\(v_{1}=\left[\begin{matrix} 0\\0\\0\\d_{1}\\d_{2}\\0 \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix}6\\7\\8\\1\\2\\9 \end{matrix} =\left[\begin{matrix} 0\\0\\0\\ -0.12199\\ 0.24965\\0 \end{matrix} \right]\)

\(u_{1}=T_{1}v_{1}=\left[\begin{matrix} 0\\0\\0\\ -0.24965\\ 0.12199\\0 \end{matrix} \right]\)

Using \(k_{1}\) from and \(Q_{f1}\) from Eq. (3),

\(Q_{1}=k_{1}u_{1}+Q_{f1}=\left[\begin{matrix}−0.60671 k\\−0.61418 k\\−147.4 k-in.\\0.60671 k\\0.61418 k\\0 \end{matrix} \right]\)

These end forces are depicted in Fig. 7.21(d).

\(F_{1}=T^{T}_{1}Q_{1}=\left[\begin{matrix} 0.61418\\−0.60671\\\underset{…………..} {−147.4}\\−0.61418\\\underset{…………} {0.60671}\\0 \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 6\\7\\8\\1\\2\\9 \end{matrix}\)

The pertinent elements of \(F_{1}\) are stored in R, as shown in Fig. 7.21(e).

Member 2 (MT = 1)

\(u_{2}=v_{2}=\left[\begin{matrix}d_{1}\\d_{2}\\0\\d_{3}\\d_{4}\\d_{5} \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix}1\\2\\9\\3\\4\\5 \end{matrix} =\left[\begin{matrix} −0.12199\\−0.24965\\0\\−0.0053343\\-0.00034577\\0.00053051 \end{matrix} \right]\)

Using \(k_{2}\) from and \(Q_{f2}\) from Eq. (4),

\(F_{2}=Q_{2}=k_{2}u_{2}+Q_{f2}=\left[\begin{matrix}0.60448 k\\\underset{…………} {−0.61416 k}\\\underset{………….}{0} \\-0.60448 k\\0.61416 k\\−147.4 k-in. \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 1\\2\\9\\3\\4\\5 \end{matrix}\)

Member 3 (MT = 1)

\(v_{3}=\left[\begin{matrix} 0\\0\\0\\d_{3}\\d_{4}\\d_{5} \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix}10\\11\\12\\3\\4\\5\end{matrix} =\left[\begin{matrix} 0\\0\\0\\ −0.0053343\\−0.00034577\\0.00053051 \end{matrix} \right]\)

Using \(T_{3}\) from ,

\(u_{3}=k_{3}v_{3}\left[\begin{matrix}0\\0\\0\\−0.00034577\\0.0053343\\0.00053051 \end{matrix} \right]\)

Using \(k_{3}\) from and \(Q_{f3}\) = 0, we calculate,

\(Q_{3}=k_{3}u_{3}=\left[\begin{matrix}0.61419 k\\0.61416 k\\0\\−0.61419 k\\−0.61416 k\\147.4 k-in. \end{matrix} \right]\)

The member local end forces are shown in Fig. 7.21(d).

\(F_{3}=T^{T}_{3}Q_{3}=\left[\begin{matrix} -0.61416\\0.61419\\\underset{…………..} {0}\\0.61416\\ -0.61419\\147.4 \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 10\\11\\12\\3\\4\\5 \end{matrix}\)

Support Reactions: See Figs. 7.21(e) and (f ).
