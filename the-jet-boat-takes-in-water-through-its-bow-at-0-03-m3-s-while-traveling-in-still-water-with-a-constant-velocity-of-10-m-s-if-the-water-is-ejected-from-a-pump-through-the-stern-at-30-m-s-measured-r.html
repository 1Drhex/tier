Guest Question.

The jet boat takes in water through its bow at \(0.03 m^3/s, \) while traveling in still water with a constant velocity of 10 m/s. If the water is ejected from a pump through the stern at 30 m/s, measured relative to the boat, determine the thrust developed by the engine. What would be the thrust if the \(0.03 m^3/s \)of water were taken in along the sides of the boat, perpendicular to the direction of motion? If the efficiency is defined as the work done per unit time divided by the energy supplied per unit time, then determine the efficiency for each case

Admin
Solution

The control volume considered is the entire boat and its contents, which moves with a constant velocity. The flow, measured relative to the control volume, is steady. Water is considered to be incompressible. Here, \(V_{cv} = 10 m/s, \dot{m}_f = 0, .\)

\(\dot{m}_w = \rho Q = (1000 kg/m^3)(0.03 m^3/s) = 30 kg/s. and V_e = 30 m/s\). The thrust is

\(T1 = – [\dot{m}_w V_{cv} – (\dot{m}_w + \dot{m}_f)Ve ] \)

 

\(= – [ (30 kg/s)(10 m/s) – (30 kg/s + 0)(30 m/s) ] \)

 

\(= 600 N \)

If the intake of water is perpendicular to the direction of motion, \(V_{cv} = 0.\) Then

\(T_2 = [\dot{m}_w V_{cv} – (\dot{m}_w + \dot{m}_f)Ve ] \)

 

\(= – [ (30 kg/s)(0) – (30 kg/s + 0)(30 m/s) ] \)

 

\(= 900 N \)

The power output for both cases can be determined from

\((\dot{W}_o)_1 = T_1V = (600 N)(10 m/s) = 6000 W \)

 

\((\dot{W}_o)_2 = T_2V = (900 N)(10 m/s) = 9000 W \)

Some of the power produces the kinetic energy per unit time of the ejected water. Its velocity relative to ground is \(V = V_e – V_{cv} = 30 m/s – 10 m/s = 20 m/s.\) For both cases, the power loss in the same and is

\(\dot{W}_{l} = \frac{1}{2}(\dot{m}_w+ \dot{m} _f)V^2 = \frac{1}{2}(30 kg/s + 0)(20 m/s)^2 = 6000 W \)

Thus, the efficiency for each case is

\(e_1 = \frac{(\dot{W}_0)_1}{(\dot{W}_0)_1 + (\dot{W}_0),} = \frac{6000 W}{6000 W + 6000 W} = 0.5 \)

 

\(e_2 = \frac{(\dot{W}_0)_2}{(\dot{W}_0)_2 + (\dot{W}_0),} = \frac{9000 W}{9000 W + 6000 W} = 0.6 \)
