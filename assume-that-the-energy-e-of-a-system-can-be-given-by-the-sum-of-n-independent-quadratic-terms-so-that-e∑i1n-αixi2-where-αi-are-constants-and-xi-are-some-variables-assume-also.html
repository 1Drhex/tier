Guest Question.

Assume that the energy E of a system can be given by the sum of n independent quadratic terms, so that \(E=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{\alpha _{i}x^{2}_{i}},\) (19.7) where \(\alpha_{i}\) are constants and \(x_{i}\) are some variables. Assume also that each \(x_{i}\) could in principle take any value with equal probability. Calculate the mean energy.

Admin
Solution

The mean energy \(〈E〉\) is given by

\( 〈E〉=\int_{-\infty }^{\infty }{\cdot \cdot \cdot }\int_{-\infty }^{\infty }{E P(x_{1} ,x_{2} ,…x_{n})}dx_{1} dx_{2}…dx_{n}.\) (19.8)

This now looks quite complicated when we substitute in the probability as follows

\( 〈E〉=\frac{\int_{-\infty }^{\infty }{\cdot \cdot \cdot }\int_{-\infty }^{\infty }{\left(\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{\alpha _{i}x^{2}_{i}} \right) }\exp \left(-\beta \Sigma ^{n}_{j=1}\alpha _{j}x^{2}_{j}\right)dx_{1}dx_{2}\cdot \cdot \cdot dx_{n} }{\int_{-\infty }^{\infty }{\cdot \cdot \cdot }\int_{-\infty }^{\infty }{\exp \left(-\beta \Sigma _{j=1}^{n}\alpha _{j}x^{2}_{j}\right) dx_{1}dx_{2}\cdot \cdot \cdot dx_{n} } },\) (19.9)

where \(i\) and \(j\) have been used to distinguish different sums. This ex-pression can be simplified by recognizing that it is the sum of n similar terms (write out the sums to convince yourself):

\(〈E〉=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{\frac{\int_{-\infty }^{\infty }{\cdot \cdot \cdot }\int_{-\infty }^{\infty }{\alpha _{i}x^{2}_{i} }\exp \left(-\beta \Sigma ^{n}_{j=1}\alpha _{j}x^{2}_{j}\right)dx_{1}dx_{2}\cdot \cdot \cdot dx_{n} }{\int_{-\infty }^{\infty }{\cdot \cdot \cdot }\int_{-\infty }^{\infty }{\exp \left(-\beta \Sigma _{j=1}^{n}\alpha _{j}x^{2}_{j}\right) dx_{1}dx_{2}\cdot \cdot \cdot dx_{n} } }},\) (19.10)

and then all but one integral cancels between the numerator and denom-inator of each term, so that

\( 〈E〉=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{\frac{\int_{-\infty }^{\infty }{\alpha _{i}x^{2}_{i}\exp }(-\beta \alpha _{i}x^{2}_{i}) dx_{i} }{\int_{-\infty }^{\infty }{\exp }(-\beta \alpha _{i}x^{2}_{i}) dx_{i} } }.\) (19.11)

Now each term in this sum is the same as the one treated above in eqn 19.6. Hence

\( 〈E〉 = \int_{-\infty }^{\infty }{EP(x)dx}\)

 

\( =\frac{\int_{-\infty }^{\infty }{\alpha x^{2}e^{-\beta \alpha x^{2}}dx} }{\int_{-\infty }^{\infty }{e^{-\beta \alpha x^{2}}dx} }\)

 

\( =\frac{1}{2\beta }\)

 

\( =\frac{1}{2}k_{B}T.\) (19.6)

 

\(〈E〉=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{\alpha _{i}〈x^{2}_{i}〉} = \sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{\frac{1}{2}k_{B}T }\)

 

\( =\frac{n}{2}k_{B}T.\) (19.12)
