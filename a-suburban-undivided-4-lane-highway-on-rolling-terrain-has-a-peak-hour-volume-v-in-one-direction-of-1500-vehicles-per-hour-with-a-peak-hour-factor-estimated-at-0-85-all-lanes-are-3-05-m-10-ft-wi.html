Guest Question.

A suburban undivided 4-lane highway on rolling terrain has a peak hour volume (V) in one direction of 1500 vehicles per hour, with a peak hour factor estimated at 0.85. All lanes are 3.05 m (10 ft) wide. There are no obstructions within 1.83 m (6 ft) of the kerb.

The percentages for the various heavy vehicle types are:
\(P_{T}\) – 12%
\(P_{B}\) – 6%
\(P_{R}\)– 2%

Determine the level of service of this section of highway.

Admin
Solution

The service flow is again calculated knowing the hourly volume during the peak hour and the peak hour factor:

\(SF = V \div PHF \)
\(= 1500 \div 0.85 = 1764.71\) vehicles per hour

\(C_{60}= 2000\) passenger cars per hour per lane N (the number of lanes in each
direction) = 2.

\(f_{w}= 0.91\) (3.05 m wide lanes, no roadside obstructions)

\(f_{HV} = \frac{1}{1 + \left\{P_{T}\left(E_{T} – 1\right) + P_{B}\left(E_{B} – 1\right)+ P_{R}\left(E_{R} – 1\right) \right\} }\)

\(= \frac{1}{1 + \left\{0.12\left(3\right) + 0.06\left(2\right)+ 0.02\left(2\right) \right\} }\)

\(f_{P}\)= 1.0 (ideal driver population)
\(f_{E}\)= 0.80 (suburban undivided)

Since:

\(SF_{i}= C_{j} \times \left\lgroup\frac{v}{c} \right\rgroup_{i} \times N \times f_{w} \times f_{hv} \times f_{p} \times f_{E}\)

Therefore:

\(\left\lgroup\frac{v}{c} \right\rgroup_{i} = SF_{i} \div (C_{j} \times N \times f_{w} \times f_{HV} \times f_{p} \times f_{E})\)

\(\left\lgroup\frac{v}{c} \right\rgroup_{i} = 1764.71 \div (2000 \times 2\times 0.91 \times 0.66 \times 1.0 \times 0.8)\)

Using the data from Table 4.2, the highway operates at level of service E.

speeds (Source: Highway Capacity Manual (TRB, 1985))
