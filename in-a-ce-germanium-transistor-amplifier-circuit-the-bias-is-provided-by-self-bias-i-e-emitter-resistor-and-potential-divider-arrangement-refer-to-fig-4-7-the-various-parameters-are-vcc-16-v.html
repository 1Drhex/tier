Guest Question.

In a CE germanium transistor amplifier circuit, the bias is provided by self bias, i.e. emitter resistor and potential divider arrangement (refer to Fig. 4.7). The various parameters are: \(V_{CC} =16V,R_{C} =3k\Omega ,R_{E} =2k\Omega ,R_{1} =20k\Omega ,R_{2} =20k\Omega \) and \(\alpha =0.985\). Determine (a) the coordinates of the operating point, and (b) the stability factor S.

Admin
Solution

For a germanium transistor, \(V_{BE}=0.3V\) As \(\alpha =0.985\),

\(\beta =\frac{\alpha }{1-\alpha }=\frac{0.985}{1-0.985} =66\)

 

(a) To find the coordinates of the operating point
Referring to Fig. 4.16, we have

Thevenin’s voltage, \(V_{T} =\frac{R_{2} }{R_{1}+R_{2} } V_{CC} =\frac{20\times 10^{3} }{76\times 10^{3} } \times 16=4.21V\)

 

Thevenin’s resistance, \(R_{B} =\frac{R_{1} R_{2} }{R_{1}+R_{2} } =\frac{20\times 10^{3}\times 56\times 10^{3} }{76\times 10^{3} } =14.737k\Omega \)

 

The loop equation around the base circuit is

\(V_{T} =I_{B} R_{B} +V_{BE} + (I_{B}+I_{C} )R_{E} \)

 

\(=\frac{I_{C} }{\beta } R_{B} +V_{BE} + (\frac{I_{C}}{\beta } +I_{C} )R_{E} \)

 

\(4.21=\frac{I_{C} }{66}\times 14.737\times 10^{3}+0.3 + I_{C}(\frac{1}{66} +1)\times 2\times 10^{3} \)

 

\(3.91= I_{C}\left[0.223+2.03\right] \times 10^{3} \)

 

Therefore, \( I_{C}=\frac{3.91}{2.253\times 10^{3} } =1.73mA\)

 

Since \(I_{B}\) is very small, \(I_{C}\approx I_{E} =1.73mA\)

 

Therefore, \(V_{CE} =V_{CC} -I_{C} R_{C} -I_{E} R_{E} \)

 

\(=V_{CC} -I_{C} \left[R_{C} + R_{E} \right] =16-1.73\times 10^{-3} \times 5\times 10^{3} =7.35V\)

 

Therefore, the coordinates of the operating point are \(I_{C} =1.73mA\) and \(V_{CE}=7.35V\).

 

(b) To find the stability factor S,

\(S=(1+\beta )\frac{1+ \frac{R_{B}}{R_{E} } }{1+\beta+ \frac{R_{B} }{R_{E} } } =(1+66 )\frac{1+ \frac{14.737}{2} }{1+66+ \frac{14.737}{2} }=67\times \frac{8.3685}{74.3685} =7.537\)
