Guest Question.

The nozzles of make an angle of 0° with the ground and 90° with the arms.The water is suddenly turned on at t = 0 with the sprinkler motionless. Determine the resulting Ω(t) if the arm diameter is 24 mm. Neglect friction.

Admin
Solution

The reference frame is again attached to the rotating arms, as sketched in . Referring to the control volume integral of Eq. 4.7.3, we observe that r × V = 0 since r is in the same direction as V along an arm.Thus Eq. 4.7.3, along with Eq. 4.7.2, takes the form

\(\Sigma \mathbf{M}-\mathbf{M}_{I}=\frac{d}{d t} \int_{\mathbf{c . v}} \mathbf{r} \times \mathbf{V} \rho d V+\int_{\mathrm{c.s.}} \mathbf{r} \times \mathbf{V}(\mathbf{V} \cdot \hat{\mathbf{n}}) \rho d A (4.7.3)\)

\(\mathbf{M}_{I}=\int \mathbf{r} \times\left[\frac{d^{2} \mathbf{S}}{d t^{2}}+2 \mathbf{\Omega} \times \mathbf{V}+\mathbf{\Omega} \times(\mathbf{\Omega} \times \mathbf{r})+\frac{d \mathbf{\Omega}}{d t} \times \mathbf{r}\right] \rho d V (4.7.2)\)

\(\begin{aligned}\sum {\mathbf{M}}-4 \int_{0}^{0.5} r \hat{\mathbf{i}} &\times\left[2 \Omega \hat{\mathbf{k}} \times V \hat{\mathbf{\imath}}+\Omega \hat{\mathbf{k}} \times(\Omega \hat{\mathbf{k}} \times r \hat{\mathbf{i}})+\frac{d \Omega}{d t} \hat{\mathbf{k}} \times r \hat{\mathbf{i}}\right] \rho A d r\\&=\frac{d}{d t} \int_{\mathbf{c . v .}} r \hat{\mathbf{i}} \times V \hat{\mathbf{\imath}} \rho d V+4 \int_{A_{\text {exit }}} 0.5 \hat{\mathbf{i}} \times V_{e}(-\hat{\mathbf{j}}) V_{e} \rho d A\end{aligned}\)

Perform the vector operations and divide by \(4 \rho\),

\(-2 A V \Omega \int_{0}^{0.5} r d r-\frac{d \Omega}{d t} A \int_{0}^{0.5} r^{2} d r=-0.5 V_{e}^{2} A_{e}\)

The required integration, using \(A V=A_{e} V_{e}=0.01 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{s}\) and \(V_{e}=2.21 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\) gives

\(\frac{d \Omega}{d t}+132.6 \Omega=5862\)

This linear, first-order differential equation is solved by adding the homogeneous solution(suppress the right-hand side) to the particular solution to obtain

\( \Omega (t) = Ce^{-132.6t} + 44.2 \)

Using the initial condition \( \Omega (t) = 0 \), we find that C = -44.2.Then

\( \Omega (t) =(1 – e^{-132.6t}) rad/s \)

Observe that as time becomes large, the angular velocity is limited to 44.2 rad/s. If friction were included, this value would be reduced. If 44.2 is multiplied by 0.707 to account for the 45° angle, we obtain the value of .
