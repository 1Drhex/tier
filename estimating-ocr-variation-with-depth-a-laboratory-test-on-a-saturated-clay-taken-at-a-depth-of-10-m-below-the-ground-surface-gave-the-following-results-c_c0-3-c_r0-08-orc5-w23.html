Guest Question.

Estimating OCR Variation with Depth.
A laboratory test on a saturated clay taken at a depth of 10 m below the ground surface gave the following results:
\(C_{c}=0.3,\, C_{r}=0.08,\, ORC=5,\, w=23\%,\, and\, G_{s}=2.7\). The groundwater level is at the surface. Determine and plot the variation of water content and over consolidation ratio with depth up to 50 m.

Strategy: you need to find an equation for this line using the data given. Identify what given data are relevant to finding the equation for the unloading/reloading line. Here you are given the slope,\(C_{r}\), so you need to use the other data to find the complete question. You can find the coordinate of one point on the unloading/reloading line from the water content and the depth, as shown in Step 1.

Admin
Solution

Step 1: Determine \(e_{o}\) and \({\sigma }'_{zo}\).

The soil is saturated, so S = 1.

\({\gamma }'=(\frac{G_{s}-1}{1+e})\gamma _{w}=(\frac{2.7-1}{1+0.621})9.8=10.3\, kN/m^{3}\) \(e_{o}=G_{s}w=2.7*0.23=0.621\) \({\sigma }'_{zo}={\gamma }'z=10.3*10=103\, kPa\)

Step 2: Determine the past maximum vertical effective stress.

\({\sigma }'_{zc}={\sigma }'_{zo}*OCR=103*5=515\, kPa\)

Step 3: Find the equation for the URL (slope BC in Figure E9.5a).

\(e_{B}=e_{o}-C_{r}\, log\, \frac{{\sigma }'_{zc}}{{\sigma }'_{zo}}\)

Therefore,

\(e_{B}=0.621-0.08\, log\, (5)=0.565\) (1)

Hence, the equation for the unloading/reloading line is

e = 0.565 + 0.08 log (OCR)

Substituting \(e=wG_{s}(G_{s}=2.7)\: and\: ORC=515/{\gamma }'z({\gamma }'=10.3\, kN/m^{3},z\: is\: depth)\) in Equation (1) gives

\(w=0.209+0.03\: log\: (\frac{50}{z})\)

You can now substitute values of z from 1 to 50 and fi nd w and then substitute \(e=wG_{s}\) in Equation (1) to find the OCR. The table below shows the calculated values and the results, which are plotted in Figure E9.5b.
