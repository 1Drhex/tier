Designing a TTL NAND gate

(a) Design the TTL NAND gate of the circuit in Fig. 15.43. It has two inputs and feeds four identical NAND gates. The output voltage at output high is \( V_{ OH }=3.5 V \text {, and } I_{ N 1}=72.5 \mu \text { A. Assume } V_{ CC }=5 V \), \( V_{ D }=0.7 V , V_{ CE \text { (sat) }}=0.2 V , V_{ BE }=0.7 V , \beta_{ F (\text { forced })}=10, \beta_{ R }=0.1, I_{ C 3 \text { (sat) }}=1 mA , I_{ C 4(\max )}=1 mA \), and \( I_{ BR }=750 \mu A \). Capacitance of each load is, \( C_{ i }=5 pF \).

(b) Use PSpice/SPICE to check your design by plotting the transfer characteristic. Determine \( NM _{ L } \text { and } NM _{ H } \). Model parameters for transistors are

BF=10 BR=0.1 TF=0.1NS TR=10NS VJC=0.85 VAF=50

Admin
Solution

(a) The design steps are similar to those in . After completing steps 1, 6, and 9 in to determine the values of \( R_{1}, R_{ B }, \text { and } R_{4} \), we follow these steps.

Step 1. Using Eq. (15.94), calculate the collector resistance of \( Q _{6} \):

\( R_{6}=\frac{V_{ BE 3}-V_{ CE 6( sat )}}{I_{ BR } /\left(1+1 / \beta_{ F (\text { forced })}\right)} \) (15.94)

 

\( R_{6}=\frac{V_{ BE 3}-V_{ CE 6 \text { (sat) }}}{I_{ BR } /\left(1+1 / \beta_{ F }\right)}=\frac{(0.7-0.2) V }{0.75 mA /(1+1 / 10)}=733 \Omega \)

 

Choose \( R_{3}=2 R_{6}=2 \times 733=1466 \Omega \).

Step 2. Choose a value of \( I_{ R 5}: \text { Let } I_{ R 5}=1130 \mu A \) (the same as the value for the TTL) ≈ 1.2 mA. For \( v_{ O }=V_{ OH } \), find the value of \( R_{5} \):

\( R_{5}=\frac{V_{ OH }+V_{ BE 4}}{I_{ R 5}}=\frac{(3.5+0.7) V }{1.2 mA }=3.5 k \Omega \)

Step 3. Calculate the base current of \( Q _{4} \) at output high and the base current of \( Q _{5} \):

\(\begin{aligned}&I_{ B 4}=\frac{N_{ H } I_{ N 1}}{1+\beta_{ F 4}}=\frac{4 \times 72.5 \mu A }{1+10}=26.4 \mu A \\& I _{ B 5}=\frac{I_{ B 4}+I_{ R 5}}{1+\beta_{ F 5}}=\frac{26.4 \mu A +1.2 mA }{1+10}=111.5 \mu A\end{aligned}\)

 

Step 4. Using Eq. (15.93) for \( v_{ O }=V_{ OH } \), find the value of \( R_{2} \) that meets the output requirement:

\( R_{2}=\frac{V_{ CC }-V_{ BE 4}-V_{ BE 5}-V_{ OH }}{I_{ B 5}}=\frac{(5-0.7-0.7-3.5) V }{111.5 \mu A }=897 \Omega \)

Step 5. Using Eq. (15.89), calculate the value of \( R_{1}\):

\( I_{ N 1}=\beta_{ R }\left[\frac{V_{ CC }-V_{ BE 2( sat )}-V_{ BE 3( sat )}-V_{ BCl }}{R_{1}}\right] \) (15.89)

 

\( R_{1}=\beta_{ R }\left[\frac{V_{ CC }-2 V_{ BE ( sat )}-V_{ BCl }}{I_{ N 1}}\right]=0.1\left[\frac{(5-2 \times 0.7-0.7) V }{72.5 \mu A }\right]=4 k \Omega \)

 

Step 6. Using Eq. (15.86), find the value of \( I_{ B 1} \) at output low, and calculate the high-state \( I_{ B 2} \):

\(\begin{aligned}&I_{ B 1}=\frac{V_{ CC }-V_{ BE 2( sat )}-V_{ BE 3( sat )}-V_{ BCl }}{R_{1}}=\frac{(5-0.7-0.7-0.7) V }{4 k \Omega}=725 \mu A \\&I_{ B 2}=-I_{ Cl }=\left(1+\beta_{ R }\right) I_{ B 1}=(1+0.1) \times 725 \mu A =798 \mu A\end{aligned}\)

Step 7. Calculate the high-state \( I_{ C 2}, I_{ E 2} \text {, and } I_{ B 3} \text {. The high-state } I_{ C 2} \) for low output is

\( I_{ C 2}=\frac{V_{ CC }-V_{ BE 3( sat )}-V_{ CE 2( sat )}}{R_{2}}=\frac{(5-0.7-0.2) V }{897}=4.57 mA \)

In the high state,

\( I_{ E 2}=I_{ B 2}+I_{ C 2}=798 \mu A +4.57 mA =5.37 mA \)

For the base current,

\( I_{ B 3}=I_{ E 2}-I_{ R }=5.37 mA -0.75 mA =4.62 mA \)

Step 8. Calculate the maximum permissible collector low-state current to drive \( Q _{3} \) in saturation:

\( I_{ C 3(\max )}=\beta_{ F (\text { forced })} I_{ B 3}=10 \times 4.62 mA =46.2 mA \)

which is higher than \( I_{ L 0}=410 \mu A \), from step 4 of . Thus, the design should be satisfactory. (Otherwise, steps 1 through 8 should be repeated with a lower value of N, a higher value of \( \beta_{ F ( sat )} \), or a lower value of \( I_{ N 1} \).) The overdrive factor is

\( k_{ ODF }=\frac{I_{ C 3( sat )}}{I_{ L 0}}=\frac{46.2 mA }{410 \mu A }=113 \)

which is high; that is, the fan-out can be much more than N = 4.

(b) To examine the propagation time, we will assume that a load resistance of

\( R_{ L }=\frac{V_{ OH }}{N I_{ L 0}}=\frac{3.5 V }{4 \times 72.5 \mu A }=12 k \Omega \)

and an equivalent capacitance of

\( C_{ L }=4 \times 5 pF =20 pF \)

is connected to the output.

The PSpice schematic is shown in Fig. 15.45. The PSpice plots, which are shown in Fig. 15.46, give \( V_{ OH }=3.3 V \) (expected value is 3.5 V), \( V_{ IL }=1.35 V \text { at } V_{ O }=3.3 V , V_{ OL }=62 mV \text { at } v_{ O }=3.3 V \text {, and } V_{ IH }=1.6 V \) at \( V_{ O }=62 mV \).

\(\begin{aligned}& NM _{ L }=V_{ IL }-V_{ OL }=(1.35-0.062) V \approx 1.29 V \\& NM _{ H }=V_{ OH }-V_{ IH }=3.3-1.6=1.7 V\end{aligned}\)

 

\( t_{ pHL }=1.93 ns , t_{ pLH }=(57.57 ns -51.77 ns )=5.8 ns , \text { and } t_{ pd }=\left(t_{ pHL }+t_{ pLH }\right) / 2=3.87 ns \)
