Guest Question.

To increase the heat dissipation from a 2.5-cm-OD tube,circumferential fins made of aluminum (k = 200 W/m K) are soldered to the outer surface.The fins are 0.1 cm thick and have an outer diameter of 5.5 cm as shown in Fig.2.23. If the tube temperature is 100°C, the environmental temperature is 25°, and the heat transfer coefficient between the fins and the environment is \(65 W/m^2 K\), calculate the rate of heat loss from a fin.

Admin
Solution

The geometry of the fin in this problem corresponds to that in Fig. 2.22, and we can therefore use the fin efficiency curve in Fig. 2.22. The parameters required to obtain the fin efficiency are

\( \left(r_{o}+\frac{t}{2}-r_{i} \right)^{{3}/{2}}\) \(=[(2.75+0.05-1.25)/100m]^{{3}/{2}}\) \(=0.00193 m^{{3}/{2}}\)

 

\([2\bar{h}/Kt(r_{o}- r_{i}) ]^{{1}/{2}}\) \(=\left[\frac{2(65 W/m^2 K)}{(200 W/m K)(0.001 m)(0.0275 – 0.0125)(m)} \right]^{{1}/{2}}\) \(=208 m^{3/2}\)

 

\(\left(r_{o} + \frac{t}{2} \right)/ r_{i} = \frac{(0.0275 + 0.001)(m)}{0.0125 m} = 2.24\)

 

\(\left(r_{o}+\frac{t}{2} – r_{i} \right)^{3/2} [2\bar{h}/Kt (r_{o}-r_{i}) ]^{1/2}=0.402\)

From Fig. 2.22 the fin efficiency is found to be 91%. The rate of heat loss from a single fin is

\(q_{fin} = \eta _{fin} \bar{h} A_{fin}(T_{S} -T_{\infty })\)

 

\(= \eta _{fin} \bar{h}2\pi\left[\left(r_{o} + \frac{t}{2} \right)^{2}-r^{2}_{i} \right] (T_{S} -T_{\infty })\)

 

\(=0.91(65 W/m^2 K)2\pi (7.84 – 1.56)\times 10^{- 4} m^2 (75K) =17.5 W\)
