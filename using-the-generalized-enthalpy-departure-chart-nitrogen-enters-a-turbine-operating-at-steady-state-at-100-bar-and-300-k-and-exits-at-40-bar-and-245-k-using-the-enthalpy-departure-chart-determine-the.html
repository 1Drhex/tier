Guest Question.

Using the Generalized Enthalpy Departure Chart

Nitrogen enters a turbine operating at steady state at 100 bar and 300 K and exits at 40 bar and 245 K. Using the enthalpy departure chart, determine the work developed, in kJ per kg of nitrogen flowing, if heat transfer with the surroundings can be ignored. Changes in kinetic and potential energy from inlet to exit also can be neglected.

Admin
Solution

Known A turbine operating at steady state has nitrogen entering at 100 bar and 300 K and exiting at 40 bar and 245 K.

Find Using the enthalpy departure chart, determine the work developed.

Schematic and Given Data:

Engineering Model

1. The control volume shown on the accompanying figure operates at steady state.

2. There is no significant heat transfer between the control volume and its surroundings.

3. Changes in kinetic and potential energy between inlet and exit can be neglected.

4. Equilibrium property relations apply at the inlet and exit.

Analysis The mass and energy rate balances reduce at steady state to give

 

\(0=\frac{\dot{Q}_{ cv }}{\dot{m}}-\frac{\dot{W}_{ cv }}{\dot{m}}+\left[h_{1}-h_{2}+\frac{ V _{1}^{2}- V _{2}^{2}}{2}+g\left(z_{1}-z_{2}\right)\right]\)

 

where \(\dot{m}\) is the mass flow rate. Dropping the heat transfer term by assumption 2 and the kinetic and potential energy terms by assumption 3 gives on rearrangement

 

\(\frac{\dot{W}_{ cv }}{\dot{m}}=h_{1}-h_{2}\)

 

The term \(h_{1}-h_{2}\) can be evaluated as follows:

 

\(h_{1}-h_{2}=\frac{1}{M}\left\{\bar{h}_{1}^{*}-\bar{h}_{2}^{*}-\bar{R} T_{ c }\left[\left(\frac{\bar{h}^{*}-\bar{h}}{\bar{R} T_{ c }}\right)_{1}-\left(\frac{\bar{h}^{*}-\bar{h}}{\bar{R} T_{ c }}\right)_{2}\right]\right\}\)

 

In this expression, M is the molecular weight of nitrogen and the other terms have the same significance as in Eq. 11.85.

 

\(\bar{h}_{2}-\bar{h}_{1}=\underline{\bar{h}_{2}^{*}-\bar{h}_{1}^{*}}-\underline{\bar{R} T_{ c }\left[\left(\frac{\bar{h}^{*}-\bar{h}}{\bar{R} T_{ c }}\right)_{2}-\left(\frac{\bar{h}^{*}-\bar{h}}{\bar{R} T_{ c }}\right)_{1}\right]}\) (11.85)

 

With specific enthalpy values from at \(T_{1}=300\) K and \(T_{2}=245\) K, respectively,

 

\(\bar{h}_{1}^{*}-\bar{h}_{2}^{*}=8723-7121=1602 kJ / mol\)

 

The terms \(\left(\bar{h}^{*}-\bar{h}\right) / \bar{R} T_{ c }\) at states 1 and 2 required by the above expression for \(h_{1}-h_{2}\) can be determined from Fig. A-4. First, the reduced temperature and reduced pressure at the inlet and exit must be determined. From , \(T_{ c }=126 K , p_{ c }=33.9\) bar. Thus, at the inlet

 

\(T_{ R 1}=\frac{300}{126}=2.38, \quad p_{ R 1}=\frac{100}{33.9}=2.95\)

 

At the exit

 

\(T_{ R 2}=\frac{245}{126}=1.94, \quad p_{ R 2}=\frac{40}{33.9}=1.18\)

 

By inspection of Fig. A-4

 

1 \(\left(\frac{\bar{h}^{*}-\bar{h}}{\bar{R} T_{ c }}\right)_{1} \approx 0.5, \quad\left(\frac{\bar{h}^{*}-\bar{h}}{\bar{R} T_{ c }}\right)_{2} \approx 0.31\)

 

Substituting values

 

\(\frac{\dot{W}_{ cv }}{\dot{m}}=\frac{1}{28 \frac{ kg }{ kmol }}\left[1602 \frac{ kJ }{ kmol }\right.\)

 

\(\left.-\left(8.314 \frac{ kJ }{ kmol \cdot K }\right)(126 K )(0.5-0.31)\right]\)

 

= 50.1 kJ/kg

 

1 Due to inaccuracy in reading values from a graph such as Fig. A-4, we cannot expect extreme accuracy in the final calculated result.
