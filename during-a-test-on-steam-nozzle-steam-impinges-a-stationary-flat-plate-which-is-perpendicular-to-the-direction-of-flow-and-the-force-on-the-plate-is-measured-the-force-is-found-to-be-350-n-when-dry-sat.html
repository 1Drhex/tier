Guest Question.

During a test on steam nozzle steam impinges a stationary flat plate which is perpendicular to the direction of flow and the force on the plate is measured. The force is found to be 350 N when dry saturated steam at 8 bar is expanded to 1 bar. Throat cross-section area is 5 cm\(^{2}\) and exit area is such that the complete expansion is achieved under these conditions.
Determine
(i) the discharge at throat.
(ii) the efficiency of nozzle assuming that all the losses occur after throat and that n = 1.13 for isentropic expansion.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{For isentropic expansion up to throat.}\\\begin{aligned}\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}&= \left\lgroup\frac{2}{n + 1} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{n}{(n – 1)} }\\P_{2}&= 8 \left\lgroup\frac{2}{1.13 + 1} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{1.13}{0.13} }\\P_{2}&= 4.62 bar\\\text{From steam table,}\qquad \qquad &\\\text{Enthalpy at 1, }\qquad \qquad h_{1}&= h_{\text{g at 8 bar}} = 2769.1 kJ/kg\\s_{1}&= s_{\text{g at 8 bar}} = 6.6628 kJ/kg.K\\\text{At throat section let the dryn}&\text{ess fraction be x}_{2},\text{ then}\\s_{1}&= s_{2} = 6.6628 = s_{\text{f at 4.62 bar}} + s_{\text{fg at 4.62 bar}} · x_{2}\\6.6628&= 1.7872 + x_{2} · 5.0178\\x_{2}&= 0.9717 or 97.17\%\\h_{2}&= h_{\text{f at 4.62 bar}} + x_{2} · h_{\text{fg at 4.62 bar}}\\&= 627.33 + (0.9717 × 2117.77)\\h_{2}&= 2685.17 kJ/kg\\\text{Specific volume at throat, }v_{2}&= v_{\text{f at 4.62 bar }}+ x_{2} · v_{\text{fg at 4.62 bar}}\\&= 0.001089 + (0.9717 × 0.4035)\\v_{2}&= 0.3932 m^{3}/kg\\\text{Enthalpy change up to throa}&\text{t }Δh_{12}= h_{1} – h_{2}\\Δh_{12}&= 83.93 kJ/kg\\\text{Velocity at throat, }C_{2}&= \sqrt{2 × Δh_{12}} = \sqrt{2 × 83.93 × 10^{3}} = 409.71 m/s\\\text{Discharge at throat, }m_{2}&= \frac{A_{2} · C_{2}}{v_{2}}\\&= \frac{5 × 10^{-4} × 409.71}{0.3932}\\m_{2}&= 0.521 kg/s\\\text{Discharge at throat}&= 0.521 kg/s\end{aligned}\\\text{Force acting on plate}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad F&= 350 N = m (C_{3}^{\prime} – C_{1})\\C_{1}&\approx 0\end{aligned}\\\text{Exit actual velocity }C_{3}^{\prime} = 671.78 m/s\\\text{For diverging section between states 1 and 3, }s_{1} = s_{2} = s_{3}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad s_{3}&= 6.6628 = s_{\text{f at 1 bar}} + x_{3} · s_{\text{fg at 1 bar}}\\6.6628&= 1.3026 + (x_{3} × 6.0568)\\x_{3}&= 0.8238 or 82.38\%\end{aligned}\\\text{Enthalpy at exit}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad h_{3}&= h_{\text{f at 1 bar}} + x_{3} · h_{\text{fg at 1 bar}}\\&= 417.46 + (0.8238 × 2258)\\h_{3}&= 2277.6 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Theoretical enthalpy drop, }Δh_{23} = h_{2} – h_{3} = 407.57 kJ/kg\\\text{Actual enthalpy drop, }Δh_{23^{\prime}} = η_{N} · Δh_{23}\\\text{Actual velocity}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad C_{3}^{\prime}&= \sqrt{2 · Δh_{23^{\prime}}}\\671.78&= \sqrt{2 × η_{N} × 407.5 × 10^{3}}\end{aligned}\\\qquad η_{N} = 0.5537 or 55.37\%\\\quad \text{Nozzle efficiency} = 55.37\%\)
