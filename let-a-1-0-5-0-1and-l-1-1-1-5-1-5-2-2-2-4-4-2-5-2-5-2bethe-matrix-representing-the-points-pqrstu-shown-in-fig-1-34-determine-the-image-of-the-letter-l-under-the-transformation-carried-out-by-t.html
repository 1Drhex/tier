Guest Question.

Let \(\mathrm{A}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}1 & 0.5 \\ 0 & 1\end{array}\right)\) and \(\mathrm{L}=\left(\begin{array}{llllll}1 & 1 & 1.5 & 1.5 & 2 & 2 \\ 2 & 4 & 4 & 2.5 & 2.5 & 2\end{array}\right)\) be the matrix representing the points pqrstu shown in Fig. 1.34. Determine the image of the letter L under the transformation carried out by the matrix multiplication \(\mathrm{AL}\) .

Admin
Solution

Let the image of the corners \(p, q, r, s, t\) and \(u\) be denoted by \(p^{\prime}, q^{\prime}, r^{\prime}, s^{\prime}, t^{\prime}\) and \(u^{\prime}\) respectively. The six vertices \(p, q, r, s, t\) and \(u\) of letter \(L\) are vectors, and the matrix \(\mathrm{A}\) transforms each of these to new vectors \(p^{\prime}, q^{\prime}, r^{\prime}, s^{\prime}, t^{\prime}\) and \(u^{\prime}\) respectively.

Carrying out the matrix multiplication \(\mathrm{AL}\):

\( p q r s t u p^{\prime} q^{\prime} r^{\prime} s^{\prime} t^{\prime} u^{\prime} \\\) \(\mathrm{AL}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}1 & 0.5 \\0 & 1\end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{llllll}1 & 1 & 1.5 & 1.5 & 2 & 2 \\2 & 4 & 4 & 2.5 & 2.5 & 2\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{cccccc}2 & 3 & 3.5 & 2.75 & 3.25 & 3 \\2 & 4 & 4 & 2.5 & 2.5 & 2\end{array}\right)\)

Plotting the transformed letter \(p^{\prime} q^{\prime} r^{\prime} s^{\prime} t^{\prime} u^{\prime}\) as shown in Fig. \(1.35\).

The transformation represented by matrix multiplication \(\mathrm{AL}\) italicizes the letter \(\mathrm{L}\). The columns of our transformed matrix are the coordinates of the italicized letter \(\mathrm{L}\).
