Guest Question.

Finding the Circuit Values of a UJT Triggering Circuit

Design the triggering circuit of Figure 9.47a. The parameters of the UJT are \(V_s = 30 V\),\(η= 0.51\), \(I_p = 10 \mu A, V_\upsilon = 3.5 V\), and \(I_v =10 mA\). The frequency of oscillation is \(f = 60 Hz\), and the width of the triggering pulse is \(t_g = 50 µs\). Assume \(V_D = 0.5\).

Admin
Solution

\(T = 1/f = 1/60 Hz = 16.67 ms\). From Eq. (9.26),

\(V_p = ηV_{BB} + V_D( = 0.5 V) ≈ηV_s + V_D( = 0.5 V_2)\) (9.26)

\(V_p = 0.51 × 30 + 0.5 = 15.8 V\). Let \(C =0.5 µF\). From Eqs. (9.24) and (9.25), the limiting values of R are

\(R \lt \frac{V_s-V_p}{I_p}\) (9.24)

 

\(R \gt \frac{V_s-V_\upsilon }{I_p}\) (9.25)

 

\(R \lt \frac{30-15.8}{10 µA}=1.42 M Ω\)

 

\(R \gt \frac{30-3.5 }{10 mA}= 2.65 k Ω\)

From Eq. (9.23),

\(T=\frac{1}{f}\approx RC \ln \frac{1}{1-\eta }\) (9.23)

\(16.67 ms = R × 0.5 µF × \ln [1/(1 – 0.51)]\), which gives \(R = 46.7 Ωk\), which falls within the limiting values. The peak gate voltage \(V_{B1} = V_p = 15.8 V\). From Eq. (9.27),

\(t_g = R_{B1} C\) (9.27)

\(R_{B1}=\frac{t_g}{C}=\frac{50 \mu s}{0.5 \mu F}= 100 \Omega\)

From Eq. (9.28),

\(R_{B2}=\frac{10^4}{ηV_s}\) (9.28)

\(R_{B2}=\frac{10^4}{0.51\times 30}=654 \Omega\)
