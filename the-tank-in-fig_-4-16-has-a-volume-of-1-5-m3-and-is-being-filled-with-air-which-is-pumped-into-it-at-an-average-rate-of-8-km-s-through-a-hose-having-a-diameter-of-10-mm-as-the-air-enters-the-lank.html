Guest Question.

The tank in Fig_ 4-16 has a volume of \(1.5 m^3 \) and is being filled with air, which is pumped into it at an average rate of 8 Km/s through a hose having a diameter of 10 mm . As the air enters the lank .its temperature is \(30^\circ \)and its absolute pressure is 500 kPa. Determine the rate at which the density of the air within the tank is changing at this instant.

Admin
Solution

Fluid Description .Due to mixing. we will assume the air has a uniform density within the tank. This density is changing because the air is compressible.The flow into the tank is steady.

Control Volume. We will consider the fixed control volume to be the air contained within the tank. Local changes occur within this control volume because the mass of air within the tank is changing with time. The average velocity of the air stream will be considered the open control surface at A.

Continuity Equation. Applying the continuity equation, realizing that the control volume (tank) has a constant volume, and assuming that the density within it is changing in a uniform manner, we have

\(\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\int_{cv} \rho dV + \int_{cs}\rho \cdot dA =0 \)

 

\(\frac{\partial \rho _a}{\partial t} – \rho _AV_AA_A = 0 \) (1)

Ideal Gas Law. The density of air flowing into the tank is determined using the ideal gas law. From Appendix A. R = 286.9 J/(kg .K), and

so

\(P_A = \rho_ART_A; 500(10^3)N/m^3 = \rho _A[286.9J/(kg\cdot K)](30 + 273)K\)

 

\(\rho _A = 5.752 kg/m^3\)

Therefore;

\(\frac{\partial \rho _a}{\partial t} (1.5 m^3) – [(5.752 kg/m^3)(8 m/s)][\pi (0.005 m)^2]\) =0

 

\(\frac{\partial \rho_a}{\partial t} = 2.41(10^{-3}) kg/(m^3 . s)\)

This positive result indicate.s that the density of this air within the tank is increasing which is to be expected.
