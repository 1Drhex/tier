Guest Question.

An exothermic reaction, \(A \rightarrow 2 B\), takes place adiabatically in a stirred-tank reactor. This liquid reaction occurs at constant volume in a 1000-gal reactor. The reaction can be considered to be first-order and irreversible with the rate constant given by

\(k=2.4 \times 10^{15} e^{-20,000 / T}\left( min ^{-1}\right)\)

where \(T\) is in \(R\).

(a) Using the information below, derive a transfer function relating the exit temperature \(T\) to the inlet concentration \(c_{A i}\). State all assumptions that you make.

(b) How sensitive is the transfer function gain \(K\) to the operating conditions? Find an expression for the gain in terms of \(\bar{q}, \bar{T}\), and \(\bar{c}_{A i}\), and evaluate the sensitivities (that is, \(\partial K / \partial \bar{q}\), etc. \()\)

Available Information

(i) Nominal steady-state conditions are

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{T}=150^{\circ} F , \quad \bar{c}_{A i}=0.8 mol / ft ^{3} \\&\bar{q}=20 gal / min =\text { flow in and out of the reactor }\end{aligned}\)

(ii) Physical property data for the mixture at the nominal steady state:

\(C=0.8 \frac{ Btu }{1 b ^{\circ} F }, \quad \rho=52 lb / ft ^{3}, \quad-\Delta H_{R}=500 kJ / mol\)

Admin
Solution

Assumptions

\(\begin{aligned}\quad\quad&\text{1. Perfectly mixed reactor}\\&\text{2. Constant fluid properties and heat of reaction.}\end{aligned}\)

a) Component balance for A,

\(V \frac{d c_{A}}{d t}=q\left(c_{A_{i}}-c_{A}\right)-V k(T) c_{A}\) (1)

Energy balance for the tank,

\(\rho V C \frac{d T}{d t}=\rho q C\left(T_{i}-T\right)+(-\Delta H) V k(T) c_{A}\) (2)

Since a transfer function with respect to \(c_{A i}\) is desired, assume the other inputs, namely \(q\) and \(T_{i}\), to be constant.

Linearize (1) and (2) and note that \(\frac{d c_{A}}{d t}=\frac{d c_{A}^{\prime}}{d t}, \frac{d T}{d t}=\frac{d T^{\prime}}{d t}\),

\(V \frac{d c_{A}^{\prime}}{d t}=q c_{A i}^{\prime}-(q+V k(\bar{T})) c_{A}^{\prime}-V{c}_{A} k(\bar{T}) \frac{20000}{\bar{T}^{2}} T^{\prime}\) (3)

\(\rho V C \frac{d T^{\prime}}{d t}=-\left(\rho q C+\Delta H V \bar{c}_{A} k(\bar{T}) \frac{20000}{\bar{T}^{2}}\right) T^{\prime}+(-\Delta H) V k(\bar{T}) c_{A}^{\prime}\) (4)

Taking the Laplace transforms and rearranging,

\([V s+q+V k(\bar{T})] C_{A}^{\prime}(s)=q C_{A i}^{\prime}(s)-V \bar{C}_{A} k(\bar{T}) \frac{20000}{\bar{T}^{2}} T^{\prime}(s)\) (5)

\(\left[\rho V C s+\rho q C-(-\Delta H) V \bar{C}_{A} k(\bar{T}) \frac{20000}{\bar{T}^{2}}\right] T^{\prime}(s)=(-\Delta H) V k(\bar{T}) C_{A}^{\prime}(s)\) (6)

\(\text { Substituting for } C_{A}^{\prime}(s) \text { from Eq. } 5 \text { into Eq. } 6 \text { and rearranging, }\)

\(\frac{T^{\prime}(s)}{C_{A i}^{\prime}(s)}=\frac{-\Delta H V k(\bar{T}) q}{[V s+q+V k(\bar{T})]\left[\rho V C s+\rho q C-(-\Delta H) V \bar{C}_{A} k(\bar{T}) \frac{20000}{\bar{T}^{2}}\right]+(-\Delta H) \bar{c}_{A} V^{2} k^{2}(\bar{T}) \frac{20000}{\bar{T}^{2}}}\) (7)

\(\bar{c}_{A}\) is obtained from the steady-state version of Eq.1,

\(\bar{c}_{A}=\frac{q \bar{c}_{A i}}{q+V k(\bar{T})}=0.001155 mol / cu . ft\)

Substituting the numerical values of \(\bar{T}, \rho, C,(-\Delta H), q, V, \bar{c}_{A}\) into Eq. 7 and simplifying gives,

\(\frac{T^{\prime}(s)}{C_{A i}^{\prime}(s)}=\frac{11.38}{(0.0722 s+1)(50 s+1)}\)

b) The gain K of the above transfer function is equal to \(\left[\frac{T^{\prime}(s)}{C_{A i}^{\prime}(s)}\right]_{s=0}\),

\(K=\frac{0.15766 \bar{q}}{\left(\frac{\bar{q}}{1000}-3.153 \times 10^{6} \frac{\bar{c}_{A}}{\bar{T}^{2}}\right)\left(\frac{\bar{q}}{1000}+13.84\right)+4.364 \cdot 10^{7} \frac{\bar{c}_{A}}{\bar{T}^{2}}}\) (8)

It is obtained by setting s=0 in Eq. 7 and substituting numerical values for ρ, C, ( -ΔH), V. Evaluating sensitivities gives,

\(\frac{d K}{d \bar{q}}=\frac{K}{\bar{q}}-\frac{K^{2}}{0.15766 \bar{q}}\left[2 \frac{\bar{q}}{10^{6}}+0.01384-3153 \frac{\bar{c}_{A}}{\bar{T}^{2}}\right]=-6.50 \times 10^{-4}\)

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{d K}{d \bar{T}} &=-\frac{K^{2}}{3.153}\left[\left(\frac{\bar{q}}{1000}+13.84\right)\left(\frac{3.153 \times 10^{6} \bar{c}_{A} \times 2}{\bar{T}^{3}}\right)-\frac{2 \times 4.364 \times 10^{7} \bar{c}_{A}}{\bar{T}^{3}}\right] \\&=-2.57 \times 10^{-5}\end{aligned}\)

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{d K}{d \bar{c}_{A i}}&=\frac{d K}{d \bar{c}_{A}} \times \frac{d \bar{c}_{A}}{d \bar{c}_{A i}}\\&=\frac{-K^{2}}{0.15766 \bar{q}}\left[-\left(\frac{\bar{q}}{1000}+13.84\right)\left(\frac{3.153 \times 10^{6}}{\bar{T}^{2}}\right)+\frac{4.364 \times 10^{7}}{\bar{T}^{2}}\right]\left(\frac{\bar{q}}{\bar{q}+13840}\right) \\&=8.87 \times 10^{-3}\end{aligned}\)
