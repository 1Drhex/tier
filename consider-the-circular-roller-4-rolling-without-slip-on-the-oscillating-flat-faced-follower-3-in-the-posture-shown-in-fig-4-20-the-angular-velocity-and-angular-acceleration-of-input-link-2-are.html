Guest Question.

Consider the circular roller, 4, rolling without slip on the oscillating flat-faced follower, 3, in the posture shown in Fig. 4.20. The angular velocity and angular acceleration of input link 2 are ω2 = 10 rad/s cw and α2 = 25 rad/s2 cw, respectively. Determine the angular accelerations of the roller and the follower.

Admin
Solution

Since this problem is an extension of , in which a roller (link 4) has been included, then the velocity polygon can be easily completed as in Fig. 4.20b. The rolling contact condition for velocity implies that \(V _{C_{4}}= V _{C_{3}}\), which allows drawing the velocity image of link 4 as indicated.

Next, we might be tempted to write the apparent-acceleration equation for point C4:

\(A _{C_{4}}= \overset{\sqrt{}\sqrt{}}{A _{B_{2} A_{2}}^{n}}+ \overset{\sqrt{}\sqrt{}}{A _{B_{2} A_{2}}^{t}}+ \overset{\sqrt{}\sqrt{}}{A _{C_{4} B_{4}}^{n}}+ \overset{?\sqrt{}}{A _{C_{4} B_{4}}^{t}}= \overset{\sqrt{}\sqrt{}}{A _{C_{3} D_{3}}^{n}}+ \overset{?\sqrt{}}{A _{C_{3} D_{3}}^{t}}+ \overset{?\sqrt{}}{A _{C_{4} / 3}^{r}}.\) (1)

Unfortunately, this equation cannot be solved, since it contains three unknowns— namely, the two angular accelerations, α3 and α4, and the rolling contact acceleration, \(A_{C_{4} / 3^{ }}^{r}.\) A different solution strategy must be sought.

We proceed as in , Fig. 4.18d, all the way to the solution for α3 and to finding \(A _{C_{3}}.\) From this, we will have constructed a good portion of the acceleration polygon of Fig. 4.20c.

Now we can relate the acceleration of point C4 to that of point B4; that is,

\(\overset{??}{A _{C_{4}}}= \overset{\sqrt{}\sqrt{}}{A _{B_{4}}}+ \overset{\sqrt{}\sqrt{}}{A _{C_{4} B_{4}}^{n}}+ \overset{?\sqrt{}}{A _{C_{4} B_{4}}^{t}},\) (2)

where

\(A_{C_{4} B_{4}}^{n}=\frac{V_{C_{4} B_{4}}^{2}}{R_{C B}}=\frac{(2.87 ft / s )^{2}}{(1.5 / 12 ft )}=65.9 ft / s ^{2}\) (3)

We can also write the rolling contact acceleration condition [Eq. (4.19)] as

\(A _{P_{3}}= A _{P_{2}}+ A _{P_{3} / 2}^{r}\) (4.19)

 

\(\overset{??}{A _{C_{4}}}= \overset{\sqrt{}\sqrt{}}{A _{C_{3}}}+ \overset{?\sqrt{}}{A _{C_{4} / 3}^{r}}.\) (4)

Remembering that \(A _{C_{4} / 3}^{r}\) is perpendicular to the surfaces at C, we graphically construct the simultaneous solution to Eqs. (2) and (4) as shown in Fig. 4.20c. Finally, the angular acceleration of roller 4 is obtained as follows:

\(\alpha_{4}=\frac{A_{C_{4} B_{4}}^{t}}{R_{C B}}=\frac{65.9 ft / s ^{2}}{(1.5 / 12 ft )}=527 rad / s ^{2} ccw.\)

The angular acceleration of the follower is identical to that found in ; namely,

\(\alpha_{3}=262 rad / s ^{2} cw.\)
