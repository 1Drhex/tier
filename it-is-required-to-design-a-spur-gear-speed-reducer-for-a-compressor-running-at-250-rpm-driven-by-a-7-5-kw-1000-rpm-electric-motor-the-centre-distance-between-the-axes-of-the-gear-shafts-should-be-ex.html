Guest Question.

It is required to design a spur gear speed reducer for a compressor running at 250 rpm driven by a 7.5 kW, 1000 rpm electric motor. The centre distance between the axes of the gear shafts should be exactly 250 mm. The starting torque of the motor can be assumed to be 150% of the rated torque. The gears are made of carbon steel 50C4 \( \left(S_{u t}=700 N / mm ^{2}\right) \). The pressure angle is 20°. The factor of safety is 2 for preliminary design based on the use of velocity factor.
(i) Design the gears and specify their dimensions.
(ii) Assume that the gears are manufactured to meet the requirements of Grade 6 and calculate the dynamic load by using Buckingham’s equation.
(iii) Calculate the effective load.
(iv) What is the actual factor of safety against bending failure?
(v) Using the same factor of safety against pitting failure, specify suitable surface hardness for the gears.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } \quad k W=7.5 \quad n_{p}=1000 rpm \).

\( n_{g}=250 rpm \quad a=250 mm \).

starting torque = 150% (rated torque)

\( S_{u t}=700 N / mm ^{2} \quad(f s)=2 \).

Grade of machining-6
Step I Estimation of module based on beam strength

\( a=\frac{1}{2}\left(d_{p}^{\prime}+d_{g}^{\prime}\right) \quad \text { or } \quad 250=\frac{1}{2}\left(d_{p}^{\prime}+d_{g}^{\prime}\right) \).

\( \left(d_{p}^{\prime}+d_{g}^{\prime}\right)=500 mm \) (a).

\( \text { Also, } \quad \frac{d_{g}^{\prime}}{d_{p}^{\prime}}=\frac{n_{p}}{n_{g}}=\frac{1000}{250}=4 \) (b).

From (a) and (b),

\( d_{p}^{\prime}=100 mm \quad \text { and } \quad d_{g}^{\prime}=400 mm \).

\( v=\frac{\pi d_{p}^{\prime} n_{p}}{60 \times 10^{3}}=\frac{\pi(100)(1000)}{60 \times 10^{3}}=5.236 m / s \).

\( C_{v}=\frac{3}{3+v}=\frac{3}{3+5.236}=0.3643 \).

The face width is assumed to be ten times that of the module. The Lewis form factor is unknown at this stage. It varies from 0.32 (for 20 teeth) to 0.358 (for 30 teeth). Assuming an intermediate value,
Y = 0.34.

\( S_{b}=m b \sigma_{b} Y=m(10 m )\left(\frac{700}{3}\right)(0.34) \),

\( =\left(793.33 m ^{2}\right) N \) (c).

\( M_{t}=\frac{60 \times 10^{6}( kW )}{2 \pi n_{p}}=\frac{60 \times 10^{6}(7.5)}{2 \pi(1000)} \).

\( =71619.93 N – mm \).

\( P=\frac{2 M_{t}}{d_{p}^{\prime}}=\frac{2(71619.93)}{100}=1432.39 N \).

\( P_{ eff }=\frac{C_{s}}{C_{v}} P_{t}=\frac{1.5(1432.39)}{0.3643}=5897.85 N \). (d).

From (c) and (d),

\( P_{\text {eff }}(f s)=S_{b} \text { or } \quad 5897.85(2)=793.33 m ^{2} \).

∴ m = 3.86 mm
Step II Gear dimensions
The first preference value of the module is 4 mm.

m = 4 mm
b = 10 m = 10(4) = 40 mm.

\( z_{p}=\frac{d_{p}^{\prime}}{m}=\frac{100}{4}=25 \).

\( z_{g}=\frac{d_{g}^{\prime}}{m}=\frac{400}{4}=100 \).

Step IV Dynamic load by Buckingham’s equation
For Grade 6,

\( e=8+0.63 \phi \).

For pinion,

\( \phi=m+0.25 \sqrt{d_{p}^{\prime}}=4+0.25 \sqrt{100} \).

\( e_{p}=8+0.63 \phi=12.095 \mu m \).

For gear,

\( \phi=m+0.25 \sqrt{d_{g}^{\prime}}=4+0.25 \sqrt{400} \).

\( e_{g}=8+0.63 \phi=13.67 \mu m \).

\( \therefore \quad e=e_{p}+e_{g}=12.095+13.67 \).

\( =25.765 \mu m \text { or }\left(25.765 \times 10^{-3}\right) mm \).

From Table 17.7, the value of deformation factor C is 11 400 N/mm². Also,

\( v=5.236 m / s \quad b=40 mm \quad P_{t}=1432.39 N \).

From Eq. (17.26),

\( P_{d}=\frac{21 v\left( Ceb +P_{t}\right)}{21 v+\sqrt{\left( Ceb +P_{t}\right)}} \) (17.26).

\( P_{d}=\frac{21 v\left( Ceb +P_{t}\right)}{21 v+\sqrt{\left( Ceb +P_{t}\right)}} \).

\( =\frac{21(5.236)\left[11400\left(25.765 \times 10^{-3}\right)(40)+1432.39\right]}{21(5.236)+\sqrt{\left[11400\left(25.765 \times 10^{-3}\right)(40)+1432.39\right]}} \).

= 6448.30 N
Step V Effective load

\( P_{\text {eff }}=\left(C_{s} P_{t}+P_{d}\right)=1.5(1432.39)+6448.30 \).

= 8596.89 N.
Step VI Actual factor of safety against bending failure

\( \therefore \quad\left(f_{s}\right)=\frac{S_{b}}{P_{ eff }}=\frac{12693.33}{8596.89}=1.48 \).

The design is satisfactory and the module should be 4 mm.
Step VII Surface hardness for gears

\( Q=\frac{2 z_{g}}{z_{g}+z_{p}}=\frac{2(100)}{100+25}=1.6 \).

\( P_{\text {eff }}(f s)=S_{w} \quad \text { or } \quad P_{\text {eff }}(f s)=b Q d_{p}^{\prime} K \).

\( \therefore \quad 8596.89(1.48)=40(1.6)(100)(0.16)\left(\frac{ BHN }{100}\right)^{2} \).

BHN = 352.49 or 360
Step VIII Dimensions of gears
(i) number of teeth on pinion = 25
(ii) number of teeth on gear = 100
(iii) module = 4 mm
(iv) face width = 40 mm
(v) pitch circle diameter of pinion = 100 mm
(vi) pitch circle diameter of gear = 400 mm
(vii) addendum (m) = 4 mm
(viii) dedendum (1.25 m) = 5 mm
(ix) clearance (0.25 m) = 1 mm
(x) tooth thickness (1.5708 m) = 6.2832 mm
(xi) fillet radius (0.4 m) = 1.6 mm
