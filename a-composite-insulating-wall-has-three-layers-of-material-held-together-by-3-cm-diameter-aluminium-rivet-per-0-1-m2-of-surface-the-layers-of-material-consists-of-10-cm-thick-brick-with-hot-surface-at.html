Guest Question.

A composite insulating wall has three layers of material held together by 3 cm diameter aluminium rivet per 0.1 \(m^2 \) of surface. The layers of material consists of 10 cm thick brick with hot surface at 200°C, 1 cm thick wood with cold surface at 10°C. These two layers are interposed by third layer of insulating material 25 cm thick. The conductivity of the materials are :
\(k_{brick} = 0.93 W/m.K, k_{insulation} = 0.12 W/m.K\)
\(k_{wood} = 0.175 W/m.K, k_{Aluminium} = 204 W/m.K\) Assuming one dimensional heat flow. Calculate the percentage increase in heat transfer rate due to rivets.

Admin
Solution

Given : The schematic is shown in Fig. 3.23 (a)
\(A_w = 0.1 m^2, d = 3 cm = 0.03 m,\)
\(L_1 = 10 cm = 0.1 m, T_i= 200°C\)
\(L_2 = 25 cm = 0.25 m, L_3 = 1 cm = 0.01 m,\)
\(k_1 = 0.93 W/m.K, T_o = 10°C\)
\(k_2 = 0.12 W/m.K, k_3 = 0.175 W/m.K,\)
\(k_4 = 204 W/m.K.\)
To find :
(i) Heat flow rate without rivet.
(ii) Heat flow rate with rivet.
(iii) Percentage increase in heat flow due to rivet.

Assumptions :
(i) Steady state heat flow in one direction only.
(ii) Constant thermal conductivity.
(iii) No contact thermal resistances at interfaces.
Analysis : (i) The resistances acting in path of heat flow without rivets as shown in 3.23 (b) :

\(ΣR_{series} = \frac{1}{A_w} \left [ \frac{L_1}{k_1} + \frac{L_2}{k_2} + \frac{L_3}{k_3}\right ]\)
\(=\frac{1}{0.1}\left [\frac{0.1}{0.93} + \frac{0.25}{0.12} + \frac{0.01}{0.175} \right ]= 22.48 K/W\)
Heat transfer rate without rivet,
\(Q_1 = \frac{∆T}{ΣR_{series}}=\frac{200 – 10}{22.48}= 8.45 W.\)
(ii) The heat transfer rate with rivet : Thermal
network in Fig. 3.23 (c)
The area of rivet through which heat is conducted
(i.e., cross-sectional area)
\(A_{Rivet} =(π/4) (d_{Rivet})^2 = (π/4) × (0.03)^2\)
\(= 7.068 × 10^{–4} m^2\)
\(R_{Rivet} = \frac{L_{Rivet}}{A_{Rivet} k_{Rivet}}=\frac{L_1 +L_2+L_3}{A_{Rivet} k_{4}}\)
\(=\frac{0.1 +0.25 +0.01}{7.068 \times 10^{–4} ×204} = 2.496 K/W\)
With consideration of rivet, the net effective area of the wall
\(A_e =A_w – A_{Rivet} = 0.1 – 7.068 × 10^{–4}\)
\(= 0.099293 m^2\)
Now the wall resistance ;
\(ΣR_w = \frac{1}{A_e} \left [ \frac{L_1}{k_1} + \frac{L_2}{k_2} + \frac{L_3}{k_3}\right ]\)
\(=\frac{1}{0.099293 }\left [\frac{0.1}{0.93} + \frac{0.25}{0.12} + \frac{0.01}{0.175} \right ]\)
= 22.64 K/W
and
\(\frac{1}{R_{eq}}=\frac{1}{R_{w}} +\frac{1}{R_{Rivet}}=\frac{1}{22.64} + \frac{1}{2.496}\)

\(R_{eq} = 2.245 K/W\)
Further, heat flow through composite wall
\(Q_2 = \frac{T_i – T_s}{R_{eq}} = 84.6 W. \)
(iii) The percentage increase in heat flow due to rivet
\(\frac{Q_2 – Q_1}{Q_1}=\frac{84.6 – 8.45}{8.45} ≈ 9 or 900%.\)
