Guest Question.

In a reaction turbine 6 kg/s steam is admitted at 15 bar dry saturated in the first stage. Turbine has eight pairs on mean diameter of 50 cm and run at 3000 rpm with mean blade speed to steam velocity ratio of 0.8. There occurs tip leakage of steam at all rows amounting to 10% of total and efficiency of working steam is 85%. Considering blade outlet angles for both fixed and moving blades to be 20°, determine the following analytically.
(i) the output from turbine in hp.
(ii) the pressure of steam leaving turbine,
(iii) the mean blade height.

Admin
Solution

\(\text{Let the degree of reaction be 50}\%\text{ for the reaction turbine.}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Mean blade speed }U&= \frac{π × 50 × 10^{-2} × 2 × 3000}{2 × 60} = 78.54 m/s\\\text{Steam velocity, }C_{1}&= \frac{U}{ρ} = \frac{78.54}{0.8} = 98.18 m/s\\\text{Angles, }α_{1}&= 20°, β_{2} = 20°\\ΔC_{w}&= 2C_{1} \cos α_{1} – U\\&= \left\{(2 × 98.18 × \cos 20) – 78.54)\right\} \\ΔC_{w}&= 105.98 m/s\\\text{Actual mass of steam }&\text{flowing through rows, }m_{actual} = 6 × 0.90 = 5.4 kg/s\\\text{Power output}&= U · ΔC_{w} × m_{actual} × \text{no. of pairs}\\&=\frac{78.54 × 105.98 × 5.4 × 8 × 10^{-3}}{0.7454}\\&= 482.4 hp\\\text{Output from turbine}&= 482.4 hp\end{aligned}\\\text{Theoretical heat drop in turbine can be obtained using efficiency of working steam,}\\\begin{aligned}Δh&= \frac{482.4 × 0.7454}{6 × 0.85} = 70.51 kJ/kg\\\text{At inlet to steam turb}&\text{ine the enthalpy may be seen from steam table as,}\\h_{1}&= h_{\text{at 15 bar, dry saturated}} = h_{\text{g at 15 bar}} = 2792.2 kJ/kg\\s_{1}&= s_{\text{g at 15 bar}} = 6.448 kJ/kg.K, v_{1} = 0.13177 m^{3}/kg\\\text{Hence, enthalpy at ex}&\text{it of turbine}\\h_{exit}&= h_{1} – Δh\\&= 2792.2 – 70.51\\h_{exit}&= 2721.69 kJ/kg\\\end{aligned}\\\)Pressure at exit of turbine can be approximated from the mollier diagram corresponding to \(h_{exit}\) and \(s_{exit} (= s_{1})\) vertically below state 1. Pressure at exit = 12 bar (approx.)\(\\\qquad\qquad \text{Pressure at exit = 12 bar}\\\text{Dryness fraction at exit of turbine, is obtained using entropy values,}\\\begin{aligned}s_{1}&= s_{exit}\\&= s_{\text{f at 12 bar}} + x_{exit} × s_{\text{fg at 12 bar}}\\6.448&= 2.2166 + x_{exit} × 4.3067\\x_{exit}&= 0.98\\v_{exit}&= v_{\text{f at 12 bar}} + x_{exit’} v_{\text{fg at 12 bar}}\\&= 0.001139 + 0.98 × (0.16333 – 0.001139)\\v_{exit}&= 0.16009 m^{3}/kg\\\text{Mean specific volume}&= \frac{v_{1} + v_{exit}}{2} = \frac{(0.13177 + 0.16009)}{2}\\&= 0.14593 m^{3}/kg\\\text{Steam mass flow rate}&= 6 × 0.92 × 0.14593 = \frac{\pi × 50 × h × C_{1a}}{100}\\\text{Axial component of velocity }C_{1a}&= C_{1}\sin α_{1}\\&= 98.18 \sin 20\\C_{1a}&= 33.58 m/s\\\text{This axial component of velocity}& C_{1a}\text{ is approximated as average flow velocity}\\\text{Substituting }C_{1a},\text{h is estimated as}&\text{ under}\\h&=\frac{6 × 0.92 × 0.14593 × 100}{\pi × 50 × h × 33.58} = 0.0153 m\end{aligned}\\\quad \text{Mean blade height = 1.53 cm}\)
