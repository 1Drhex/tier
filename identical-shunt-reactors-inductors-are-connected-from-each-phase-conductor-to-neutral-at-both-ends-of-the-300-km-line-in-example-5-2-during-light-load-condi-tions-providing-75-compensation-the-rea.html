Shunt reactive compensation to improve transmission-linevoltage regulation

Identical shunt reactors (inductors) are connected from each phase conductor to neutral at both ends of the 300 km line in during light load condi-tions, providing 75% compensation. The reactors are removed during heavy load conditions. Full load is 1.90 kA at unity p.f. and at 730 kV. Assuming that the sending-end voltage is constant, determine the following properties.

a. Percent voltage regulation of the uncompensated line

b. The equivalent shunt admittance and series impedance of the compensated line

c. Percent voltage regulation of the compensated line

Admin
Solution

a. From (5.1.1) with \(I_{RFL} =1.9\angle 0°\) kA, using the A and B parameters from ,

\(V_{S}=AV_{R}+BI_{R} volts\) (5.1.1)

\(V_{S}=AV_{RFL}+BI_{RFL}=\left(0.9313\angle 0.209°\right)\left(\frac{730}{\sqrt{3} } \angle 0°\right)+\left(97.0\angle 87.2°\right)\left(1.9\angle 0°\right)=392.5\angle 0.209°+184.3\angle 87.2°=401.5+j185.5=442.3\angle 24.8°kV_{LN}\)

 

\(V_{S}=442.3\sqrt{3} =766.0kV_{LL}\)

The no-load receiving-end voltage is, from (5.1.19),

\(V_{RNL} =\frac{V_{S} }{A}\) (5.1.19)

\(V_{RNL} =\frac{766.0 }{0.9313} =822.6kV_{LL}\)

and the percent voltage regulation for the uncompensated line is, from (5.1.18),

\(percent VR=\frac{\mid V_{RNL}\mid -\mid V_{RFL}\mid }{\mid V_{RFL}\mid} \times 100\) (5.1.18)

\(percent VR=\frac{822.6-730}{730}\times 100=12.68 \)%

b. From , the shunt admittance of the equivalent π circuit without compensation is

\(Y^{′} =2\left(3.7\times 10^{-7}+j7.094\times 10^{-4} \right) =7.4\times 10^{-7} +j14.188\times 10^{-4} \) S

With 75% shunt compensation, the equivalent shunt admittance is

\(Y_{eq}=7.4\times 10^{-7} +j14.188\times 10^{-4}\left(1-\frac{75}{100} \right) =3.547\times 10^{-4} \angle 89.88° \) S

Since there is no series compensation, the equivalent series impedance is the same as without compensation:

\(Z_{eq}=Z^{′} =97.0\angle 87.2°\Omega \)

c. The equivalent A parameter for the compensated line is

\(A_{eq}=1+\frac{Y_{eq}Z_{eq}}{2} =1+\frac{\left(3.547\times 10^{-4} \angle 89.88°\right)\left(97.0\angle 87.2°\right) }{2}=1+0.0172\angle 177.1°=0.9828\angle 0.05° \) per unit

Then, from (5.1.19),

\(V_{RNL} =\frac{V_{S} }{A}\) (5.1.19)

\(V_{RNL} =\frac{766 }{0.9828}=779.4kV_{LL}\)

Since the shunt reactors are removed during heavy load conditions \(V_{RNL} =730kV\) is the same as without compensation. Therefore

\(percent VR=\frac{779.4-730}{730} \times 100=6.77\)%

The use of shunt reactors at light loads improves the voltage regulation from
12.68% to 6.77% for this line.
