Guest Question.

THE MOST STRONGLY BOUND NUCLIDE

Find the mass defect, the total binding energy, and the binding energy per nucleon of \({ }_{28}^{62} \mathrm{Ni}\), which has the highest binding energy per nucleon of all nuclides (Fig. 43.2). The neutral atomic mass of \({ }_{28}^{62} \mathrm{Ni}\) is 61.928345 u.

Admin
Solution

IDENTIFY and SET UP:

The mass defect \(∆M\) is the difference between the mass of the nucleus and the combined mass of its constituent nucleons. The binding energy \(E_B\) is this quantity multiplied by \(c^2\), and the binding energy per nucleon is \(E_B\) divided by the mass number \(A\). We use Eq. (43.10), \(\Delta M=Z M_{\mathrm{H}}+N m_{\mathrm{n}}-{ }_{Z}^{A} M\), to determine both the mass defect and the binding energy.

\(E_{\mathrm{B}}=\left(Z M_{\mathrm{H}}+N m_{\mathrm{n}}-{ }_{Z}^{A} M\right) c^{2}\) (43.10)

EXECUTE:

With \(Z\) = 28, \(M_H\) = 1.007825 u, \(N = A – Z\) = 62 – 28 = 34, \(m_n\) = 1.008665 u, and \({ }_{Z}^{A} M\) = 61.928345 u,

Eq. (43.10) gives \(∆M\) = 0.585365 u. The binding energy is then

\(E_{\mathrm{B}}=(0.585365 \mathrm{u})(931.5 \mathrm{MeV} / \mathrm{u})=545.3 \mathrm{MeV}\)

The binding energy per nucleon is \(E_B/A\) = (545.3 MeV/62), or 8.795 MeV per nucleon.

 

EVALUATE: Our result means that it would take a minimum of 545.3 MeV to pull a \({ }_{28}^{62} \mathrm{Ni}\) completely apart into 28 protons and 34 neutrons. The mass defect of \({ }_{28}^{62} \mathrm{Ni}\) is about 1% of the atomic (or the nuclear) mass. The binding energy is therefore about 1% of the rest energy of the nucleus, and the binding energy per nucleon is about 1% of the rest energy of a nucleon. Note that the mass defect is more than half the mass of a nucleon, which suggests how tightly bound nuclei are.
