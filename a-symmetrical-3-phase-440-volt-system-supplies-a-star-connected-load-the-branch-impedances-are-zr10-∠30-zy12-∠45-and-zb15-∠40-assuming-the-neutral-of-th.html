Guest Question.

A symmetrical 3-phase 440 volt system supplies a star connected load. The branch impedances are \(Z_{R}=10\angle 30^{\circ}\),\(Z_{Y}=12\angle 45^{\circ}\) and \(Z_{B}=15\angle 40^{\circ}\). Assuming the neutral of the supply to be earthed, calculate the potential of the star point and the line currents, phase sequence R Y B.

Admin
Solution

Using Millman Theorem

 

\(V_{s}=\frac{\frac{254\angle 0}{10\angle 30}+\frac{254\angle -120}{12\angle 45^{\circ}}+\frac{254\angle 120^{\circ}}{15\angle 40}}{0.1\angle 30+\frac{1}{12}\angle -45^{\circ}+\frac{1}{15}\angle -40}\)

 

\(=\frac{25.4\angle -30^{\circ}+21.17\angle -165+16.9\angle 80}{0.0866-j0.05+0.0589-j0.0589+0.05-j0.0428}\)

 

\(=\frac{21.996-j12.702-20.45-j5.48+2.94+j16.68}{0.1966-j0.152}\)

 

\(=\frac{4.73\angle -18.5}{0.2485\angle -37.71}\)

 

\(=19.03< 19.21\)

 

\(I_{a}=\frac{254\angle 0-19.03\angle 19.21}{10\angle 30}\)

 

\(=\frac{236.1\angle -15}{10\angle 30}\)

 

\(I_{a}=23.61\angle -31.5\)

 

\(I_{b}=\frac{254\angle -120-19.03\angle 19.21}{12\angle 45^{\circ}}\)

 

\(=\frac{-127-j219.96-17.97-j6.26}{12\angle 45^{\circ}}\)

 

\(=\frac{268.68\angle -122.7}{12\angle 45^{\circ}}\)

 

\(=22.39\angle -167.7\)

 

\(I_{c}=\frac{254\angle 120-19.03\angle 19.21}{15\angle 40}\)

 

\(=\frac{-144.97-j213.70}{15\angle 40}=\frac{258.2\angle +124.2}{15\angle 40}\)

 

\(=17.21\angle 84.2\)

 

\(I_{a}+I_{b}+I_{c}=23.61\angle -31.5+22.39\angle -167.7+17.21\angle 84.2\)

 

\(=20.13-j12.336-21.87-j4.77+1.74+j17.12\)

 

\(=21.87-21.87-j17.10+j17.12=0\)

 

Hence it is found that for a 3-phase 3 wire system with unbalanced loading the sum of the three currents is zero (KCL at star point).
