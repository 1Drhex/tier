Guest Question.

In metal processing plants, workers often operate near hot metal surfaces.Exposed hot surfaces are hazards that can potentially cause thermal burns on human skin tissue. Metallic surface with a temperature above 70°C is considered extremely hot. Damage to skin tissue can occur instantaneously upon contact with metallic surface at that temperature. In a plant that processes metal plates, a plate is conveyed through a series of fans to cool its surface in an ambient temperature of 30°C, as shown in Figure 2-47. The plate is 25 mm thick and has a thermal conductivity of 13.5 W/m∙K. Temperature at the bottom surface of the plate is monitored by an infrared (IR) thermometer. Obtain an expression for the variation of temperature in the metal plate. The IR thermometer measures the bottom surface of the plate to be 60°C. Determine the minimum value of the convection heat transfer coefficient necessary to keep the top surface below 47°C to avoid instantaneous thermal burn upon accidental contact of hot metal surface with skin tissue.

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION In this example, the concepts of Prevention through Design (PtD) are applied in conjunction with the solution of steady one-dimensional heat conduction problem. The top surface of the plate is cooled by convection, and temperature at the bottom surface is measured by an IR thermometer. The variation of temperature in the metal plate and the convection heat transfer coefficient necessary to keep the top surface below 47°C are to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Heat conduction is steady and one-dimensional. 2 Thermal conductivity is constant. 3 There is no heat generation in the plate. 4 The bottom surface at x = 0 is at constant temperature while the top surface at x = L is subjected to convection.

Properties The thermal conductivity of the metal plate is given to be k = 13.5 W/m∙K.

Analysis Taking the direction normal to the surface of the wall to be the x direction with x = 0 at the lower surface, the mathematical formulation can be expressed as

\( \frac{d^{2} T}{d x^{2}}=0\)

with boundary conditions

\( T(0)=T_{0}\) \( -k \frac{d T(L)}{d x}=h\left[T(L)-T_{\infty}\right] \)

Integrating the differential equation twice with respect to x yields

\( \frac{d T}{d x}=C_{1}\)

 

\( T(x)=C_{1} x+C_{2}\)

where \( C_{1} \) and \( C_{2}\) are arbitrary constants. Applying the first boundary condition yields

\( T(0)=C_{1} \times 0+C_{2}=T_{0} \rightarrow C_{2}=T_{0}\)

The application of the second boundary condition gives

\( -k \frac{d T(L)}{d x}=h\left[T(L)-T_{\infty}\right] \rightarrow-k C_{1}=h\left(C_{1} L+C_{2}-T_{\infty}\right) \)

Solving for \( C_{1}\) yields

\( C_{1}=\frac{h\left(T_{\infty}-C_{2}\right)}{k+h L}=\frac{T_{\infty}-T_{0}}{(k / h)+L}\)

Now substituting \( C_{1}\) and \( C_{2}\) into the general solution, the variation of temperature becomes

\( T(x)=\frac{T_{\infty}-T_{0}}{(k / h)+L} x+T_{0}\)

The minimum convection heat transfer coefficient necessary to maintain the top surface below \( 47^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\) can be determined from the variation of temperature:

\( T(L)=T_{L}=\frac{T_{\infty}-T_{0}}{(k / h)+L} L+T_{0}\)

Solving for h gives

\( h=\frac{k}{L} \frac{T_{L}-T_{0}}{T_{\infty}-T_{L}}=\left(\frac{13.5 \mathrm{~W} / \mathrm{m} \cdot \mathrm{K}}{0.025 \mathrm{~m}}\right) \frac{(47-60)^{\circ} \mathrm{C}}{(30-47)^{\circ} \mathrm{C}}=413 \mathrm{~W} / \mathrm{m}^{2} \cdot \mathrm{K}\)

Discussion To keep the top surface of the metal plate below \( 47^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\), the convection heat transfer coefficient should be greater than \( 413 \mathrm{~W} / \mathrm{m}^{2} \cdot \mathrm{K}\). A convection heat transfer coefficient value of \( 413 \mathrm{~W} / \mathrm{m}^{2} \cdot \mathrm{K}\) is very high for forced convection of gases. The typical values for forced convection of gases are \( 25-250 \mathrm{~W} / \mathrm{m}^{2} \cdot \mathrm{K}\) (see Table 1-5 in Chapter 1 ). To protect workers from thermal burn, appropriate apparel should be worn when operating in an area where hot surfaces are present.

Free convection of gases 2–25
