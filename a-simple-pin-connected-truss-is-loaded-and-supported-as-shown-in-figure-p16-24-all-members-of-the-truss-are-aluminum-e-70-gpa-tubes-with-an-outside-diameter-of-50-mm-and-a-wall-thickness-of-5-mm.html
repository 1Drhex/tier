Guest Question.

A simple pin-connected truss is loaded and supported as shown in Figure P16.24. All members of the truss are aluminum [E = 70 GPa] tubes with an outside diameter of 50 mm and a wall thickness of 5 mm. The yield strength of the aluminum is 250 MPa. Determine the maximum load P that may be applied to the structure if a factor of safety of 2.0 with respect to failure by yielding and a factor of safety of 3.0 with respect to failure by buckling are specified.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&d=50 mm -2(5 mm )=40 mm \\&A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[(50 mm )^{2}-(40 mm )^{2}\right]=706.858 mm ^{2} \\&I=\frac{\pi}{64}\left[(50 mm )^{4}-(40 mm )^{4}\right]=181,132.45 mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Euler buckling load:

\(P_{c r}=\frac{\pi^{2} E I}{L^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}\left(70,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(181,132.45 mm ^{4}\right)}{L^{2}}=\frac{125.1394 \times 10^{6} kN – mm ^{2}}{L^{2}}\) (a)

Yield load:

\(P_{Y}=\left(250 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(706.858 mm ^{2}\right)=176,714.5 N =176.7145 kN\) (b)

Truss analysis results:

\(\begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c|c|}\hline \text { Member } & \begin{array}{c}\text { Member } \\\text { axial force }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Member } \\\text { length } L\end{array} & {P_{c r}} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Corresponding } \\\text { truss load P based } \\\text { on buckling } \\\text { (with FS =3.0) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Corresponding } \\\text { truss load P } \\\text { based on yielding } \\\text { (with FS = 2.0) }\end{array} \\& & ( mm ) & ( kN ) & ( kN ) & ( kN ) \\\hline A B & -0.600 P & 2,000 & 31.285 & 52.142 & 147.262 \\\hline A D & 1.16619 P & 1,943.65 & \text { N.A. } & \text { N.A. } & 75.766 \\\hline B C & -0.400 P & 3,000 & 13.904 & 11.587 & 220.893 \\\hline B D & -1.16619 P & 1,943.65 & 33.125 & 9.468 & 75.766 \\\hline B E & -1.04137 P & 2,603.416 & 18.463 & 5 . 9 1 0 & 84.847 \\\hline C E & 0.7746 P & 1,943.65 & \text { N.A. } & \text { N.A. } & 114.068 \\\hline D E & 1.200 P & 3,000 & \text { N.A. } & \text { N.A. } & 73.631 \\\hline\end{array}\)

Maximum allowable truss load P:

\(P_{\max }=5.91 kN\)
