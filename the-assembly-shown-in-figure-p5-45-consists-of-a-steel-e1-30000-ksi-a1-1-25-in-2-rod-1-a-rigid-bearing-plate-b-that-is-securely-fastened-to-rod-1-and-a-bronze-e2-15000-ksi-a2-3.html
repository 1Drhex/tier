Guest Question.

The assembly shown in Figure P5.45 consists of a steel [\(E_{1}\) = 30,000 ksi; \(A_{1}=1.25 \text { in. }^{2}\) ] rod (1), a rigid bearing plate B that is securely fastened to rod (1), and a bronze [\(E_{2}\)= 15,000 ksi; \(A_{2}=3.75 \text { in. }^{2}\) ] post (2). The yield strengths of the steel and bronze are 62 ksi and 75 ksi, respectively. A clearance of 0.125 in. exists between the bearing plate B and bronze post (2) before the assembly is loaded. After a load of P = 65 kips is applied to the bearing plate, determine:
(a) the normal stresses in bars (1) and (2).
(b) the factors of safety with respect to yield for each of the members.
(c) the vertical displacement of bearing plate B.

Admin
Solution

Deformation in rod (1) alone: Check to see if the bearing plate attached to rod (1) will contact the bronze post for the 65-kip load.

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}=\frac{(65 kips )(14 ft )(12 in. / ft )}{\left(1.25 in .^{2}\right)(30,000 ksi )}=0.2912 in .>0.125 in . \text { gap } \quad \therefore \text { contact will occur }\)

This calculation proves that the bearing plate attached to rod (1) will contact the bronze post when the 65-kip load is applied; therefore, this structure must be analyzed as a statically indeterminate structure.

Equilibrium: Consider a FBD of flange B after the gap at B has been closed.
Sum forces in the vertical direction to obtain:

\(\Sigma F_{y}=F_{1}-F_{2}-P=0\) (a)

Geometry of Deformations:

\(\delta_{1}+\delta_{2}=0.125 \text { in. }\) (b)

Force-Deformation Relationships:

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}} \quad \delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}\) (c)

Compatibility Equation: Substitute Eqs. (c) into Eq. (b) to derive the compatibility equation:

\(\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}=0.125 \text { in. }\) (d)

Solve the Equations: Solve Eq. (d) for \(F_{1}\):

\(F_{1}=\left[0.125 \text { in. }-\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}\right] \frac{A_{1} E_{1}}{L_{1}}=(0.125 \text { in. }) \frac{A_{1} E_{1}}{L_{1}}-F_{2} \frac{L_{2}}{L_{1}} \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \frac{E_{1}}{E_{2}}\)

and substitute the resulting expression into Eq. (a) to determine an expression for \(F_{2}\):

\(\begin{aligned}(0.125 \text { in. }) \frac{A_{1} E_{1}}{L_{1}}-F_{2} \frac{L_{2}}{L_{1}} \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \frac{E_{1}}{E_{2}}-F_{2}-65 \text { kips } &=0 \\F_{2}\left[1+\frac{L_{2}}{L_{1}} \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \frac{E_{1}}{E_{2}}\right]=(0.125 \text { in. }) \frac{A_{1} E_{1}}{L_{1}}-65 \text { kips } \\F_{2} &=\frac{(0.125 \text { in. }) \frac{A_{1} E_{1}}{L_{1}}-65 \text { kips }}{1+\frac{L_{2}}{L_{1}} \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \frac{E_{1}}{E_{2}}}\end{aligned}\)

The axial force in post (2) is thus:

\(F_{2}=\frac{(0.125 \text { in. }) \frac{\left(1.25 in .^{2}\right)(30,000 ksi )}{(168 \text { in. })}-65 kips}{1+\frac{36 \text { in. }}{168 \text { in. }} \frac{1.25 in .^{2}}{3.75 in .^{2}} \frac{30,000 ksi }{15,000 ksi }} =-32.4609 kips\)

and the axial force in rod (1) is:

\(F_{1}=F_{2}+P=-32.4609 kips +65 kips =32.5391 kips \)

 

(a) Normal stresses:

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{1}=\frac{F_{1}}{A_{1}}=\frac{32.5391 kips }{1.25 in .^{2}}=26.0313 ksi =26.0 ksi ( T ) \\&\sigma_{2}=\frac{F_{2}}{A_{2}}=\frac{-32.4609 kips }{3.75 in .^{2}}=-8.6563 ksi =8.66 ksi ( C )\end{aligned}\)

(b) Factors of safety:

\(FS _{1}=\frac{62 ksi }{26.0313 ksi }=2.38 \quad FS _{2}=\frac{75 ksi }{8.6563 ksi }=8.66\)

(c) Displacement of plate B: The displacement of plate B is equal to the deformation of rod (1).

\(\begin{aligned}\delta_{1}=& \frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}=\frac{(32.5391 kips )(168 in .)}{\left(1.25 in .^{2}\right)(30,000 ksi )}=0.145775 in . \downarrow \\& \therefore u_{B}=0.1458 in. \downarrow\end{aligned}\)
