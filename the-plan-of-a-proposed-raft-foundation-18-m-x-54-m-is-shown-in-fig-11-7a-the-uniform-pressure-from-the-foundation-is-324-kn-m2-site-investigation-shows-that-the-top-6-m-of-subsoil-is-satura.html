Guest Question.

The plan of a proposed raft foundation 18 m × 54 m is shown in Fig. 11.17(a). The uniform pressure from the foundation is 324 kN/\(m ^{2}\). Site investigation shows that the top 6 m of subsoil is saturated coarse sand with a unit weight of 18.0 kN/\(m ^{3}\). The ground water level occurs at 3.00 m from the top of sand. The standard penetration value of the sand taken at a depth of 4.50 m is 18. Below the sand there exists a clay layer of 30 m thickness (E = 16.2 MN/\(m ^{2}\), \(E_{\text {swelling }}\) = 63 MN/\(m ^{2}\)). The clay rests on hard rock. Determine the total settlement under the foundation.

Admin
Solution

Vertical stress increments:

Net pressure \(=324 kN / m ^{2}\)

Relief due to excavation of 1.5 m sand \(=18 \times 1.5=27 kN / m ^{2}\)

Gross pressure \(=(324+27)=351 kN / m ^{2}\).

The foundation is split into four rectangles as shown and Fadum’s’ chart is used:

\(\begin{array}{|c|c|l|c|c|c|}\hline \text { Depth }( m ) & B / z & L / z & I_{\sigma} & 4 I_{\sigma} & \Delta \sigma_{z}\left( kN / m ^{2}\right) \\\hline 3 & 3 & 9 & 0.247 & 0.988 & 320 \\9 & 1 & 3 & 0.203 & 0.812 & 263 \\15 & 0.60 & 1.80 & 0.152 & 0.608 & 197 \\21 & 0.43 & 1.30 & 0.113 & 0.452 & 146 \\27 & 0.33 & 1.00 & 0.086 & 0.344 & 112 \\33 & 0.27 & 0.82 & 0.067 & 0.268 & 87 \\\hline\end{array}\)

Immediate settlement (Sand):

\(\begin{aligned}&N=18 \bar{\sigma}_{0}=4.5 \times 18-1.5 \times 9.81=66.3 kN / m ^{2} \\&C_{s}=1.5 \frac{C_{r}}{\bar{\sigma}_{0}}\end{aligned}\)

But \(C_{r}=400 N =400 \times 18=7200 kN / m ^{2}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}C_{s} &=\frac{15 \times 7200}{66.3}=162.9 \\S_{1} &=\frac{H}{C_{s}} \cdot \log _{e}\left(\frac{\bar{\sigma}_{0}+\Delta \bar{\sigma}}{\bar{\sigma}_{0}}\right) \\&=\frac{(6-15)}{162.9} \log _{e}\left(\frac{66.3+320}{66.3}\right) m =0.0487 m =48.7 mm\end{aligned}\)

Since most part of the sand below the foundation is submerged customarily it is assumed that the settlement will be doubled.

\(\therefore \quad S_{i} \text { for sand }=2 \times 48.7=97.4 mm\)

Clay:
With reference to Fig. 11.3(b),

\(H_{1}=4.5 m ; H_{2}=34.5 m\)

For \(H _{2}\):

\(L / B=27 / 9=3 ; \frac{H_{2}}{B}=\frac{34.5}{9}=3.83 \quad \therefore \quad I_{s}=0.47\)

For \(H_{1}\):

\(\begin{aligned}L / B &=3 ; \frac{H_{1}}{B}=\frac{4.5}{9}=0.5, \quad \therefore \quad I_{s}=0.07 \\S_{i} &=q \cdot \frac{B}{E}\left(1-\nu ^{2}\right) \times 4 I_{s} \times(\text { rigidity factor })\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}S_{i}, \text { taking gross pressure } &=\frac{351}{16200} \times 9 \times 0.75 \times 4(0.47-0.07) \times 0.8 \\&=0.1872 m =187.2 mm\end{aligned}\)

Heave effect:
Relief pressure due to excavation \(=1.5 \times 18=27 kN / m ^{2}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\therefore \quad \text { Heave } &=\frac{27}{62000} \times 9 \times 0.75 \times 4(0.47-0.07) \times 0.8 \\&=0.0037 m =3.7 mm\end{aligned}\)

Net immediate settlement in clay = 187.2 – 3.7 = 183.5 mm
The heave effect is obviously insignificant except for great depth of excavation.
Consolidation Settlement:
The clay layer is divided into five layers of 6 m thickness.

\(m_{ \nu }\) \(\Delta \sigma_{z}\) \(m_{ \nu } \cdot \Delta \sigma_{z} \cdot H\)
0.000150 263 0.2367
0.000110 197 0.1300
0.000090 146 0.0788
0.000075 112 0.0504
0.000045 87 0.0235
0.5194 = 519.4 mm

Total settlement = (97.4 + 183.5 + 519.4) mm ≈ 800 mm.
