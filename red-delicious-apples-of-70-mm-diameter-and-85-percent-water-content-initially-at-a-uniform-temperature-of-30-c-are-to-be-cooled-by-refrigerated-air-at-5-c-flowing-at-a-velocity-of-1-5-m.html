Guest Question.

Red Delicious apples of 70 mm diameter and 85 percent water content initially at a uniform temperature of \( 30^{\circ} C\) are to be cooled by refrigerated air at \( -5^{\circ} C\) flowing at a velocity of 1.5 m / s Fig.17-11. The average heat transfer coefficient between the apples and the air is given in Table (17-2) to be \( 21 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\). Determine how long it will take for the center temperature of the apples to drop to \( 6^{\circ} C\). Also, determine if any part of the apples will freeze during this process.

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION The center temperature of apples drops to \( 6^{\circ} C\) during cooling. The cooling time and if any part of the apples will freeze are to be determined.

Assumptions 1 The apples are spherical in shape with a radius of \( r_{o}=3.5 cm\). 2 The thermal properties and the heat transfer coefficient are constant.

Properties The thermal conductivity and thermal diffusivity of apples are \( k=0.418 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\) and \( \alpha=0.13 \times 10^{-6} m ^{2 }/ s \) (Table A -7)

Analysis Noting that the initial and the ambient temperatures are \( T_{i}=30^{\circ} C\) and \( T_{\infty}=-5^{\circ} C\), the time required to cool the midsection of the apples to \( T_{o}=6^{\circ} C\) is determined from transient temperature charts for a sphere as follows:

\( \left.\begin{array}{c} \frac{1}{ Bi }=\frac{k}{h r_{o}}=\frac{0.418 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C }{\left(21 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)(0.035 m )}=0.57 \\ \frac{T_{0}-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}=\frac{6-(-5)}{30-(-5)}=0.314 \end{array}\right\} \tau=\frac{\alpha t}{r_{o}^{2}}=0.46 \)

Therefore,

\(t=\frac{\tau r_{o}^{2}}{\alpha}=\frac{(0.46)(0.035 m )^{2}}{0.13 \times 10^{-6} m ^{2} / s }=4335 s \cong 1.20 h\)

The lowest temperature during cooling will occur on the surface \( \left(r / r_{o}=1\right) \) of the apples that is in direct contact with refrigerated air and is determined from

\(\left.\begin{array}{l} \frac{1}{ Bi }=\frac{k}{h r_{0}}=0.57 \\ \frac{r}{r_{o}}=1 \end{array}\right\} \frac{T(r)-T_{\infty}}{T_{o} T_{\infty}}=0.50\)

It gives

\( T_{\text {surace }}=T(r)=T_{\infty}+0.50\left(T_{0}-T_{\infty}\right)=-5+0.5[6-(-5)]=0.5^{\circ} C\)

which is above \( -1.1^{\circ} C\), the highest freezing temperature of apples. Therefore, no part of the apples will freeze during this cooling process.
