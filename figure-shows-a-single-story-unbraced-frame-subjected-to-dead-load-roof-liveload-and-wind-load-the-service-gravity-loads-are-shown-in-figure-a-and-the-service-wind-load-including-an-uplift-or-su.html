Guest Question.

Figure shows a single-story, unbraced frame subjected to dead load, roof liveload, and wind load. The service gravity loads are shown in Figure a, and the service wind load (including an uplift, or suction, on the roof) is shown in Figure b. Use A992 steel and select a W12 shape for the columns (vertical members). Design for a drift index of 1⁄400 based on service wind load. Bending is about the strong axis, and each column is laterally braced at the top and bottom. Use LRFD.

Admin
Solution

Figure 6.26 shows the results of first-order analyses for dead load, roof live load,wind acting on the roof, and lateral wind load. The dead load results include the effects of an added notional load. Member stiffnesses were not reduced, and the effective length method is applicable. All vertical loads are symmetrically placed and contribute only to the Mnt moments. The lateral load produces an \( M_{ℓt}\) moment.
Load combinations involving dead load, D, roof live load, \( L_r\) , and wind, W, are as follows.
Combination 2: \( 1.2D + 0.5L_r\)
\( P_{nt} = 1.2(14) + 0.5(26) = 29.8 kips\)
\( M_{nt} = 1.2(50) + 0.5(94) = 107 ft-kips\)
\( M_{ℓt} = 0\)

Combination 3: \( 1.2D + 1.6L_{r} + 0.5W\)
\( P_{nt} = 1.2(14) + 1.6(26) = 58.4 kips, P_{ℓt} = 0.5(-9 + 1) = -4.0 kips (Use 0.)\)
\( M_{nt} = 1.2(50) + 1.6(94) = 210.4 ft-kips,\)
\( M_{ℓt} = 0.5(-32 + 20) = -6.0 ft-kips (Use 0.)\)

Combination 4: \( 1.2D + 1.0w + 0.5L_{r}\)
\( P_{nt} = 1.2(14) + 0.5(26) = 29.8 kips, P_{ℓt} = 1.0(-9 + 1) = -8.0 kips (Use 0.)\)
\( M_{nt} = 1.2(50) + 0.5(94) = 64.7 ft-kips\)
\( M_{ℓt} = 1.0(232 + 20) = -12.0 ft-kips (Use 0.)\)
Load combination 3 will obviously govern. It produces the largest axial load and the largest total moment.

For purposes of selecting a trial shape, assume that\( B_1 = 1.0.\) The value of \( B_2 \) can be computed using the design drift index and a value of Pe story from AISC Equation A-8-7:
\( P_{e story} = R_{M} \frac{HL}{\Delta_H}= R_{M} \frac{H}{\Delta_{H}/L}= 0.85\left ( \frac{2.7}{1/400} \right ) =918.0 kips\)
(The unfactored horizontal load, H = 2.7 kips, is used because the drift index is based on the maximum drift caused by service loads.)
\( B_{2} = = \frac{1}{1 – \frac{\alpha P_{story}}{P_{e story}}}\approx \frac{1}{1 -1\frac{1.0(58.4)}{918.0}}=1.068\)
The amplified axial load is
\( P_r = P_u = P_{nt} + B_{2}P_{ℓt} = 58.4 + 0 = 58.4 kips\)
and the amplified bending moment is
\( M_{u} = B_{1}M_{nt} + B_{2}M_{ℓt} = 1.0(210.4) + 0 = 210.4 ft-kips\)
Without knowing the frame member sizes, the alignment chart for the effective length factor cannot be used. Table C-A-7.1 in the Commentary to the Specification reveals that case (f) corresponds most closely to the end conditions for the sidesway case of this example and that \(K_{x} \)= 2.0.
For the braced condition, \(K_x \)= 1.0 will be used, and because the member is braced in the out-of-plane direction,\( K_y = 1.0\) will be used. Since the bending moment appears to be dominant in this member, use AISC Equation H1-1b.
Try a W12 \( \times\) 53: From Manual Table 6-2, with \( L_c = L_{b} = 15 ft, \phi _{c}P_{n} = 478 kips, and \phi _{b}M_{nx} = 258 ft-kips\)
Check to see which axis controls the axial compressive strength. From Table 6-2,
\( r_{x}/r_{y} = 2.11\)
and \( \frac{K_{x}L}{r_{x}/r_{y}}= \frac{2.0(15)}{2.11} = 14.2 ft < K_{y}L = 15 ft \therefore L_{c} = 15\) ft as assumed.
Verify that AISC Equation H1-1b is the correct one:
\( \frac{P_u}{\phi _{c}P_{n}}= \frac{58.4}{478} = 0.1222 < 0.2\)
Therefore, AISC Equation H1-1b controls as assumed.
For a bending moment that varies linearly from zero at one end to a maximum at the other, the value of \( C_{b}\) is 1.67 (see Figure g). Modify \(\phi _{b}M_{nx} \)to account for \( C_{b}\) .
\( \phi _{b} M_{nx} \times C_{b} = 258(1.67) = 431 ft-kips\)

For \( L_{b} = 0,\)
\( \phi _{b}M_{nx} =\phi _{b}M_{px} =292 ft-kips\)
Since 431 ft-kips > 292 ft-kips, the upper limit of 292 ft-kips controls, and
\( \phi _{b}M_{nx} = 292 ft-kips\)
\(P_{e1} =\frac{\pi ^{2}EI_{x}}{(K_{x}L)^{2}}= \frac{\pi ^{2}(29,000)(425)}{(1.0 \times 15 \times 12)^{2}} = 3754 kips\)[/latex]
\(C_{mx} = 0.6 – 0.4 \frac{M_1}{M2}= 0.6 – 0.4\frac{0}{M2} = 0.6\)
From AISC Equation A-8-3,
\(B_{1} =\frac {C_{m}}{1 – (\alpha P_{r}/P_{e1})}= \frac {C_{m}}{1 – \alpha[(1.00P_{nt}+P_{ℓt})/P_{e1})}\)
\( = \frac {0.6}{1- 1.0[(58.4 + 0)]/3754}= 0.610 < 1.0 \therefore Use B_1 = 1.0.\)
Since \( B_1\) is the value originally assumed and \(B_2\) will not change, the previously computed value of \( M_u = 210.4\) ft-kips is unchanged.
Check AISC Equation H1-1b:
\(\frac{P_u}{\phi _{c}P_{n}}+\left (\frac{M_{ux}}{\phi _{b} M_{nx}} + \frac{M_{uy}}{\phi _{b} M_{ny}} \right )=\frac{0.1222}{2}+\left (\frac{210.4}{292} +0 \right )\)
= 0.782 , 1.0 (OK)
This result is significantly smaller than 1.0, so try a shape two sizes smaller.
Try a W12 \( \times\) 45: From Table 6-2, \( \phi _{c}P_{n} = 316 kips, \phi _{b} M_{nx}\) = 194 ft-kips, and
\( r_{x}/r_{y} = 2.11.\)
\( \frac{K_{x}L}{r_{x}/r_{y}}= \frac{2.0(15)}{2.11}=14.2 ft < K_{y}L = 15 ft \therefore L_{c} \) = 15 ft as assumed.
\(\frac{P_u}{\phi _{c}P_{n}}=\frac{ 58.4}{316} = 0.1848 < 0.2\)
Therefore, AISC Equation H1-1b controls as assumed.
Modify \( \phi _{b} M_{nx}\) to account for \( C_{b}:\)
\( \phi _{b} M_{nx} \times C_b = 194(1.67) = 324 ft-kips\)
For \( L_b = 0,\)
\( \phi _{b} M_{nx} = \phi _{b} M_{px} = 241 ft-kips\)

Since 324 ft-kips > 241 ft-kips, the upper limit of 241 ft-kips controls, and
\( \phi _{b} M_{nx} = 241 ft-kips\)
For the braced condition,
\(P_{e1} =\frac{\pi ^{2}EI_{x}}{(L_{cx})^{2}}=\frac{\pi ^{2}EI_{x}}{(K_{x}L)^{2}} =\frac{\pi ^{2}(29,000)(348)}{(1.0 \times 15 \times 12)^{2}} = 3074 kips\)

From AISC Equation A-8-3,
\(B_{1} =\frac {C_{m}}{1 – (\alpha P_{r}/P_{e1})}= \frac {C_{m}}{1 – \alpha[(1.00P_{nt}+P_{ℓt})/P_{e1})}\)

\( = \frac {0.6}{1 – 1.0[(58.4 + 0)]/3074}= 0.612 < 1.0 \therefore Use B_1 = 1.0.\)
Check AISC Equation H1-1b:
\(\frac{P_u}{2\phi _{c}P_{n}}+\left (\frac{M_{ux}}{\phi _{b} M_{nx}} + \frac{M_{uy}}{\phi _{b} M_{ny}} \right )=\frac{0.1848}{2}\left (\frac{210.4}{241} +0 \right )\)
= 0.965 < 1.0 (OK)
Use a W12 \( \times\) 45.
