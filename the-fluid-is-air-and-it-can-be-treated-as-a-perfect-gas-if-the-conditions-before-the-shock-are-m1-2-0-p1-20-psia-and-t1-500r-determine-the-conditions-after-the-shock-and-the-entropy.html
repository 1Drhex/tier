Guest Question.

The fluid is air and it can be treated as a perfect gas. If the conditions before the shock are:
\(M_{1}\)=2.0, \(p_{1}\) = 20 psia, and \(T_{1}=500^{\circ}R\); determine the conditions after the shock and the entropy change across the shock.

Admin
Solution

First, we compute \(p_{t1}\):

\(p_{t1}=\frac{p_{t1}}{p_{1}}p_{1}=\left ( \frac{1}{0.1278} \right )\) (20) = 156.5 psia

Now \(M_{1}\) = 2.0, we find

\(M_{2}\) = 0.57735 \(\frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}\) = 4.5000 \(\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}\) = 1.6875 \(\frac{p_{t2}}{p_{t1}}\) = 0.72087

Thus

\(p_{2}=\frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}p_{1}\) = (4) (5) (20) = 90 psia (or 0.620MPa)

\(T_{2}=\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}T_{1}\) = (1.6875) (500) = \(844^{\circ}R\) (or 469 K)

\(p_{t2}=\frac{p_{t2}}{p_{t1}}p_{t1}\) = (0.72087) (156.5) = 112.8 psia (or 0.777MPa)

To compute the entropy change, we use equation (4.28):

\(\frac{p_{t2}}{p_{t1}}=0.72087=e^{-\Delta s/R}\) \(\frac{\Delta s}{R}=0.3273\)

 

\(\Delta s=\frac{(0.3273)(53.3)}{778}\) = 0.0224 Btu/lbm-\(^{\circ}R\) (or 93.79 J/kg-K)

 

equation (4.28):\(\frac{p_{t2}}{p_{t1}}=e^{-\Delta s/R}\)
