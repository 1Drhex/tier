Guest Question.

Design diagram of the frame is shown in Fig. 14.8a. Find eigenfre-quincies and mode shape vibration.

Admin
Solution

The system has two degrees of freedom. Generalized coordinate are \(q_1\) and \(q_2\). We need to apply unit forces in direction of \(q_1\) and \(q_2\), and construct the bending momens deagram. Unit displacements are

\(\delta _{11}= \frac{\overline{M}_1\times \overline{M}_1 }{EI}=\frac{1}{2EI}\cdot \frac{1}{2}\cdot 1\cdot l\cdot l\cdot \frac{2}{3}\cdot 1\cdot l+\frac{1}{EI}\cdot 1\cdot l\cdot h\cdot 1\cdot l = \frac{l^3}{6EI}+\frac{l^2h}{EI};\)

 

\(\delta _{22}= \frac{\overline{M}_2\times \overline{M}_2 }{EI}=\frac{1}{EI}\cdot \frac{1}{2}\cdot 1\cdot h\cdot h\cdot \frac{2}{3}\cdot 1\cdot h = \frac{h^3}{3EI};\)

 

\(\delta _{12}=\delta _{21}= \frac{\overline{M}_1\times \overline{M}_2 }{EI}=\frac{1}{EI}\cdot \frac{1}{2}\cdot 1\cdot h\cdot h\cdot1\cdot l = \frac{h^2l}{2EI};\)

Let h = 2l and \(\delta _0 =l^3/6EI\). In this case \(\delta _{11} =13\delta _0;\delta _{22}= 16\delta _0;\delta _{12} = \delta _{21} =12\delta _0.\)

Equation for calculation of amplitudes (14.4)

\( \begin{matrix} (m_1\delta _{11}ω^2 -1)A_1 +m_2\delta _{12}ω^2A_2 + . . . + m_n\delta _{1n}ω^2A_n=0 , \\m_1\delta _{21}ω^2 A_1 +(m_2\delta _{22}ω^2-1)A_2 + . . . + m_n\delta _{2n}ω^2A_n=0 , \\ . . . . . . . . . . .. . . . .. . … \\ m_1\delta _{31}ω^2A_1 +m_2\delta _{32}ω^2A_2 + . . . + (m_n\delta _{nn}ω^2-1)A_n=0 . \end{matrix} \) (14.4)

\((13\delta _{0}mω^2 -1)A_1 + 12\delta _{0}mω^2A_2 =0,\)

 

\(12\delta _{0}mω^2 A_1 + (16\delta _{0}mω^2-1)A_2 =0,\) (a)

Let

\(\lambda =\frac{1}{\delta _0mω^2}= \frac{6EI}{mω^2l^3}.\)

In this case equation (a) may be rewritten

\((13 -\lambda )A_1 +12A_2 =0,\)

 

\(12A_1 +(16 -\lambda )A_2 =0.\) (b)

Frequency equation becomes

\(D = \left [ \begin{matrix} 13 -\lambda & 12 \\ 12 & 16 -\lambda \end{matrix} \right ]= (13 -\lambda )(16 – \lambda ) – 144 =0.\)

Roots in descending order are \(\lambda _1 =26.593; \lambda _2 = 2.4066\)

Eigenfrequencies in increasing order are

\(ω_1 = \sqrt{\frac{6EI}{\lambda _1ml^3} }= 0.4750\sqrt{\frac{EI}{ml^3} }, ω_2 = \sqrt{\frac{6EI}{\lambda _2ml^3} }=1.5789\sqrt{\frac{EI}{ml^3} }.\)

Mode shape vibration may be determined on the basis of equations (b).

For first mode (\(\lambda_1 = 26.593\)) ratio of amplitudes are

\(\frac{A_2}{A_1} = -\frac{13 – \lambda }{12}= -\frac{13 -26.593}{12}= 1.1328,\)

 

\(\frac{A_2}{A_1} = -\frac{12 }{16- \lambda }= -\frac{12}{16 – 26.593}= 1.1328.\)

Assume that \(A_1 = 1\), so the first eigenvector φ becomes

\(\varphi =\left\lfloor\varphi _{11}\varphi _{21}\right\rfloor^T= \left\lfloor\begin{matrix} 1 & 1.1328 \end{matrix} \right\rfloor^T\)

For second mode \((\lambda _2 =2.4066)\) ratio of amplitudes are

\(\frac{A_2}{A_1}= – \frac{13 -\lambda }{12} = -\frac{13-2.4066 }{12}=- 0.8828\)

 

\(\frac{A_2}{A_1}= – \frac{12 }{16 -\lambda } = -\frac{12 }{16-2.4066}=- 0.8828\)

The modal matrix Φ is then defined as

\(\Phi =\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1.1328 & – 0.8828 \end{matrix} \right ].\)

Corresponding mode shapes of vibration are shown in Fig. 14.8b.
