Guest Question.

Verify whether an ASTM A992 W40 × 264 member, subjected to combined compression and bending about the x-axis only, is able to support the axial forces and bending moment listed here (second order effects included), using AISC 360-10 H1.1, H1.3 and H2 methods. The member is pinned and subjected to a uniformly distributed load along the x-axis. The unbraced length is 45 ft along both axes. The member is not braced along its height (lateral-torsional buckling not prevented).
Geometrical properties:
d = 40 in. (1016 mm) \(A_{g} = 77.6 in.^{2} (500.6 cm^{2})\)
\(b_{f}\) = 11.9 in. (302 mm) \(Z_{x} = 1130 in.^{3} (18 520 cm^{3})\)
\(t_{f}\)= 1.73 in. (43.9 mm) \(S_{x} = 971 in.^{3} (15 910 cm^{3})\)
\(t_{w}\)= 0.96 in. (24.4 mm) \(Z_{y} = 132 in.^{3} (2163 cm^{3})\)
k = 2.91 in. (73.9 mm) \(S_{y} = 82.6 in.^{3} (1354 cm^{3})\)
L = 45 ft (13.72 m) \(I_{x} = 19 400 in.^{4} (807 500 cm^{4})\)
\(L_{x}\)= 45 ft \(I_{y} = 493 in.^{4} (20 520 cm^{4})\)
\(L_{y}\)= 45 ft J = 56.1 \(in.^{4} (2335 cm^{4})\)
\(L_{b}\)= 45 ft \(C_{w}= 181 000 in.^{6}(4 860 000 cm^{6})\)
\(r_{x}\)= 15.8 in. (40.1 cm) \(r_{y}\)= 2.52 in. (6.4 cm)
Material properties:
Steel : ASTM A992 \(F_{y}\)= 50 ksi (345 MPa) \(F_{u}\)= 65 ksi (448 MPa)
Axial load and maximum moments acting at the midspan:

LFRD ASD
\(P_{u}\)=500kips(2224 kN) \(P_{a}\)= 325 kips (1446 kN)
\(M_{ux}\)= 1000kips-ft (1356 kNm) \(M_{ax}\)=660kips-ft(895 kNm)
\(M_{uy}\)= 0 \(M_{ay}\)=0

 

Admin
Solution

(a) Verification According to AISC 360-10 H1.1: ‘Doubly and Singly-Symmetric Members Subjected to Flexure and Compression’.
(a1) Axial Strength
Section classification for local buckling.
Flange:
\(b/t=(0.5\times 11.9)/1.73=3.44< 0.56\sqrt{E/F_{y}}=0.56\times \sqrt{29000/50}=13.49\rightarrow\) non-slender
Web:
\(h/t_{w}=(40-2\times 2.91)/0.96=35.6< 1.49\sqrt{E/F_{y}}=1.49\times \sqrt{29000/50}=35.88\rightarrow \)non-slender
ASTM A36 steel W40 × 264, subjected to axial load, is a non-slender section (Q = 1). Check slenderness ratio about both axes (assuming K = 1.0)
\(\frac{KL_{x}}{r_{x}}=\frac{1\times (45.12)}{15.8}=34.2\)
\(\frac{KL_{y}}{r_{y}}=\frac{1\times (45.12)}{2.52}=107.1 \)governs
Calculate the elastic critical buckling stress (\(F_{e}\)).
\(F_{e}=\frac{\pi ^{2}E}{\left ( \frac{KL_{y}}{r_{y}} \right )^{2}}=\frac{\pi ^{2}\times 29000}{(107.1)^{2}}=24.9 ksi(172 MPa)\)
Calculate flexural buckling stress (\(F_{cr}\)).
Check limit:\(4.71\sqrt{\frac{E}{QF_{y}}}=4.71\times \sqrt{\frac{29000}{1\times 50}}=113.4> 107.1\)
Because \(\frac{KL_{y}}{r_{y}}\leq 4.71\sqrt{\frac{E}{(QF_{y})}}\)then:
\(F_{cr}=\left [ 0.658^{\frac{(QF_{y})}{F_{e}}} \right ](QF_{y})=\left [ 0.658^{\frac{1\times 50}{24.9}} \right ]\times (1\times 50)=21.6 ksi(149 MPa)\)
Compute the nominal compressive strength (\(P_{n}\)).\(P_{n} = F_{cr}A_{g}\) =21.6×77.6 = 1676 kips (7455 kN)
Compute the available strength.
LFRD: \(\phi_{c}P_{n}\) =0.90 × 1676 = 1508.4 kips (6710 kN)
ASD: \(P_{n}/\Omega_{c}\)= 1676/1.67 = 1003.7 kips (4465 kN)
(a2) Flexural Strength
Section classification for local buckling.
Flange:
\(b/t=(0.5\times 11.9)/1.73=3.44< 0.38\sqrt{E/F_{y}}=0.38\times \sqrt{29000/50}=9.15\rightarrow\) compact
Web:
\(h/t_{w}=(10-2\times 2.91)/0.96=35.6< 3.76\sqrt{E/F_{y}}=3.76\times \sqrt{29000/50}=90.6\rightarrow \)compact
ASTM A36 steel W40 × 264, subjected to flexure, is a compact section.
Plastic moment:
\(M_{p} = F_{y} × Z_{x}\)= 50 × 1130/12 = 4708 kips-ft (6383 kNm)
Flexural strength corresponding to lateral torsional buckling limit state \(M_{LT}\):
\(C_{b}\)= 1.136 (uniformly distributed load)
\(L_{p}=1.76r_{y}\sqrt{\frac{E}{F_{y}}}=1.76\times 2.52\times \sqrt{\frac{29000}{50}}=106.8 in./12=8.9ft(2.71 m)\)
\(r_{ts}=\sqrt{\frac{\sqrt{I_{y}C_{w}}}{S_{x}}}=\sqrt{\frac{\sqrt{493\times 181000}}{971}}=3.12 in.(7.92 cm)\)
c = 1 (doubly symmetrical I-shape)
\(h_{o} = d – t_{f}\)=40 – 1.73 = 38.27 in.
\(L_{r}=1.95r_{ts}\frac{E}{0.7F_{y}}\sqrt{\frac{J.c}{S_{x}h_{0}}+\sqrt{\left ( \frac{J.c}{S_{x}h_{0}} \right )^{2}+6.76\left ( \frac{0.7F_{y}}{E} \right )^{2}}}\)
\(=1.95\times 3.12\times \frac{29000}{0.7\times 50}\sqrt{\frac{56.1\times 1}{971\times 38.27}+\sqrt{\left ( \frac{56.1\times 1}{971\times 38.27} \right )^{2}+6.76\times \left ( \frac{0.7\times 50}{29000} \right )^{2}}}\)
= 356.1 in./12 = 29.7 ft (9.04 m)
\(L_{b}= 45 ft > L_{r} \)= 29.7 ft; Hence:
\(F_{cr}=\frac{C_{b}\pi ^{2}E}{\left ( \frac{L_{b}}{r_{ts}} \right )^{2}}\sqrt{1+0.078\frac{J.c}{S_{x}h_{0}}\left ( \frac{L_{b}}{r_{ts}} \right )^{2}}\)
\(=\frac{1.136\times \pi ^{2}\times 29000}{\left ( \frac{45/12}{3.12} \right )^{2}}\sqrt{1+0.078\frac{56.1\times 1}{971\times 38.27}\left ( \frac{45/12}{3.12} \right )^{2}}=23.09 ksi(159.2 MPa)\)
\(M_{LT} = F_{cr}S_{x}\)=23.1 × 971/12 = 1868 kips−ft (2533 kNm)
Compute the nominal flexural strength (\(M_{n}\)).
\(M_{n} =min (M_{p};M_{LT}\)=min (4708; 1868) = 1868 kips-ft (2533 kNm)
Compute the available strength.
LFRD: \(\phi_{b}M_{n}\) =0.90 × 1868 = 1682 kips-ft (2280 kNm)
ASD: \(M_{n}/\Omega_{b}\)= 1868/1.67 = 1119 kips-ft (1517 kNm)
(a3) Verification for Axial Load and Bending Moments
LFRD:
\(P_{r} = P_{u}\)= 500 kips (2224 kN)
\(M_{rx} =M_{ux}\)= 1000 kips-ft (1356 kNm)
\(M_{ry} =M_{uy}\)=0
\(P_{c} =\phi_{c}P_{n}\)= 1508 kips (6710 kN)
\(M_{cx} = \phi_{b}M_{n}\)= 1682 kips (2280 kN)
\(\frac{P_{r}}{P_{c}}=\frac{500}{1508}=0.33\geq 0.20\)
\(\frac{P_{r}}{P_{c}}+\frac{8}{9}\left ( \frac{M_{rx}}{M_{cx}} \right )=\frac{500}{1508}+\frac{8}{9}\left ( \frac{1000}{1682} \right )=0.33+\frac{8}{9}(0.59)=0.86< 1.0\)
ASD:
\(P_{r} = P_{a}\)= 325 kips (1446 kN)
\(M_{rx} =M_{ax}\)= 660 kips-ft (895 kNm)
\(M_{ry} =M_{ay}\)=0
\(P_{c} = P_{n}/\Omega_{c}\)= 1004 kips-ft (4465 kN)
\(M_{cx} =M_{n}/\Omega_{b}\)= 1119 kips−ft (1517 kNm)
\(\frac{P_{r}}{P_{c}}=\frac{325}{1004} =0.32\geq 0.20\)
\(\frac{P_{r}}{P_{c}}+\frac{8}{9}\left ( \frac{M_{rx}}{M_{cx}} +\frac{M_{ry}}{M_{cy}}\right )=\frac{325}{1004}+\frac{8}{9}\left ( \frac{660}{1119} \right )=0.32+\frac{8}{9}(0.59)=0.85< 1.0\)
(b) Verification According to AISC 360-10 H1.3: ‘Doubly-Symmetric Rolled Compact Members Subjected to Single Flexure and Compression’.
(b1) Axial Strength for In-Plane Instability
Check slenderness ratio about the x-axis only (assuming K = 1.0)
\(\frac{KL_{x}}{r_{x}}=\frac{1\times (45.12)}{15.8}=34.2\)
Calculate the elastic critical buckling stress (\(F_{e}\)).
\(F_{e}=\frac{\pi ^{2}E}{\left ( \frac{KL_{x}}{r_{x}} \right )^{2}}=\frac{\pi ^{2}\times 29000}{(34.2)^{2}}=245 ksi(1689 MPa)\)
Calculate the flexural buckling stress (\(F_{cr}\)).
Check limit:\(4.71\sqrt{\frac{E}{QF_{y}}}=4.71\times \sqrt{\frac{29000}{1\times 50}}=113.4> 34.2\)
Because \(\frac{KL_{y}}{r_{y}}\leq 4.71\sqrt{\frac{E}{(QF_{y})}}\)then:
\(F_{cr}=\left [ 0.658^{\frac{(QF_{y})}{F_{e}}} \right ](QF_{y})=\left [ 0.658^{\frac{1\times 50}{245}} \right ]\times (1\times 50)=45.9 ksi(317 MPa)\)
Compute the nominal compressive strength (\(P_{n}\)).
\(P_{n} = F_{cr}A_{g}\)=45.9×77.6 = 3562 kips (15850 kN)
Compute the available strength.
LFRD: \(\phi_{c}P_{n}\) =0.90 × 3562 = 3206 kips (14260 kN)
ASD: \(P_{n}/\Omega_{c}\)= 3562/1.67 = 2133 kips (9489 kN)
(b2) Axial Strength for Out-of-Plane Instability
Check slenderness ratio about the y-axis only (assuming K = 1.0)
\(\frac{KL_{y}}{r_{y}}=\frac{1\times (45.12)}{2.52}=107.1\)
Calculate the elastic critical buckling stress (\(F_{e}\)).
\(F_{e}=\frac{\pi ^{2}E}{\left ( \frac{KL_{y}}{r_{y}} \right )^{2}}=\frac{\pi ^{2}\times 29000}{(107.1)^{2}}=24.9 ksi(172 MPa)\)
Calculate flexural buckling stress (\(F_{cr}\)).
Check limit:\(4.71\sqrt{\frac{E}{QF_{y}}}=4.71\times \sqrt{\frac{29000}{1\times 50}}=113.4>107.1\)
Because \(\frac{KL_{y}}{r_{y}}\leq 4.71\sqrt{\frac{E}{(QF_{y})}}\)then:
\(F_{cr}=\left [ 0.658^{\frac{(QF_{y})}{F_{e}}} \right ](QF_{y})=\left [ 0.658^{\frac{1\times 50}{24.9}} \right ]\times (1\times 50)=21.6 ksi(149 MPa)\)
Compute the nominal compressive strength (\(P_{n}\)).
\(P_{n} = F_{cr}A_{g}\)=21.6×77.6 = 1676 kips (7456 kN)
Compute the available strength.
LFRD: \(\phi_{c}P_{n}\) =0.90 × 1676 = 1509 kips (6710 kN)
ASD: \(P_{n}/\Omega_{c}\)= 1676/1.67 = 1004 kips (4465 kN)
(b3) Flexural Strength in the Plane of Bending
Plastic moment:
\(M_{n} = M_{p}=F_{y} \times Z_{x}\)= 50 × 1130/12 = 4708 kips-ft (6383 kNm)
Compute the available strength.
LFRD: \(\phi_{b}M_{n}\) =0.90 × 4708 = 4237 kips-ft (5745 kNm)
ASD: \(M_{n}/\Omega_{b}\)= 4708/1.67 = 2819 kips-ft (3923 kNm)
(b4) Flexural Strength Out of the Plane of Bending
Plastic moment:
\(M_{p} = F_{y} \times Z_{x}\)= 50 × 1130/12 = 4708 kips-ft (6383 kNm)
Flexural strength corresponding to lateral torsional buckling limit state \(M_{LT}\) determined using \(C_{b}\) = 1:
\(L_{p}\) = 8.9 ft (2.71 m)
\(L_{r}\)= 29.7 ft (9.04 m)
\(L_{b}= 45 ft > L_{r} \)= 29.7 ft; Hence:
\(F_{cr}=\frac{C_{b}\pi ^{2}E}{\left ( \frac{L_{b}}{r_{ts}} \right )^{2}}\sqrt{1+0.078\frac{J.c}{S_{x}h_{0}}\left ( \frac{L_{b}}{r_{ts}} \right )^{2}}\)
\(=\frac{1.0\times \pi ^{2}\times 29000}{\left ( \frac{45/12}{3.12} \right )^{2}}\sqrt{1+0.078\frac{56.1\times 1}{971\times 38.27}\left ( \frac{45/12}{3.12} \right )^{2}}=20.3 ksi(140.1 MPa)\)
Compute the nominal flexural strength (\(M_{n}\)).
\(M_{n} =M_{LT}=F_{cr} S_{x}\)=20.3 × 971/12 = 1643 kips-ft (2230 kNm)
Compute the available strength.
LFRD: \(\phi_{b}M_{n}\) =0.90 × 1643 = 1480 kips-ft (2007 kNm)
ASD: \(M_{n}/\Omega_{b}\)= 1643/1.67 = 985 kips-ft (1335 kNm)
(b5) Verification for In-Plane Instability
LFRD:
\(P_{r} = P_{u}\)= 500 kips (2224 kN)
\(M_{rx} =M_{ux}\)= 1000 kips-ft (1356 kNm)
\(P_{c} =\phi_{c}P_{n}\)= 3206 kips (14260 kN)
\(M_{cx} = \phi_{b}M_{n}\)= 4237 kips-ft (5745 kNm)
\(\frac{P_{r}}{P_{c}}=\frac{500}{3206}=0.16 < 0.20\)
\(\frac{P_{r}}{2P_{c}}+\left ( \frac{M_{rx}}{M_{cx}} \right )=\frac{500}{2\times 3206}+\left ( \frac{1000}{4237} \right )=0.08+(0.24)=0.32< 1.0\)
ASD:
\(P_{r} = P_{a}\)= 325 kips (1446 kN)
\(M_{rx} =M_{ax}\)= 660 kips-ft (895 kNm)
\(P_{c} = P_{n}/\Omega_{c}\)= 2133 kips( 9489 kN)
\(M_{cx} =M_{n}/\Omega_{b}\)= 2819 kips−ft (3934 kNm)
\(\frac{P_{r}}{P_{c}}=\frac{325}{2133} =0.15 < 0.20\)
\(\frac{P_{r}}{2P_{c}}+\left ( \frac{M_{rx}}{M_{cx}} \right )=\frac{325}{2\times 2133}+\left ( \frac{660}{2819} \right )=0.08+(0.23)=0.31< 1.0\)
(b6) Verification for Out-of-Plane Buckling and Lateral-Torsional Buckling
LFRD:
\(P_{r} = P_{u}\)= 500 kips (2224 kN)
\(M_{rx} =M_{ux}\)= 1000 kips-ft (1356 kNm)
\(P_{cy} = \phi_{c}P_{n}\)= 1509 kips(6710 kN)
\({M}'_{cx}=\phi_{b}M_{n}\)=1480 kips-ft(2007 kNm)
\(\frac{P_{r}}{P_{cy}}\left ( 1.5-0.5\frac{P_{r}}{P_{cy}} \right )+\left ( \frac{M_{rx}}{C_{b}{M}'_{cx}} \right )=\frac{500}{1509}\left ( 1.5-0.5\frac{500}{1509} \right )+\left ( \frac{1000}{1.136\times 1480} \right )^{2}=0.80< 1.0\)
ASD:
\(P_{r} = P_{a}\)= 325 kips (1446 kN)
\(M_{rx} =M_{ax}\)= 660 kips-ft (895 kNm)
\(P_{cy} =P_{n}/\Omega_{c}\)= 1004 kips(4465 kN)
\({M}'_{cx}=M_{n}/\Omega_{b}\)=985 kips-ft(1335 kNm)
\(\frac{P_{r}}{P_{cy}}\left ( 1.5-0.5\frac{P_{r}}{P_{cy}} \right )+\left ( \frac{M_{rx}}{C_{b}{M}'_{cx}} \right )=\frac{325}{1004}\left ( 1.5-0.5\frac{325}{1004} \right )+\left ( \frac{660}{1.136\times 985} \right )^{2}=0.78< 1.0\)
(c) Verification According to AISC 360-10 H2: ‘Unsymmetric and Other Members Subjected to Flexure and Axial Force’.
LFRD:
\(f_{ra}=\frac{P_{u}}{A_{g}}=\frac{500}{77.6}\)=6.44 ksi(44.4 MPa)
\(f_{rbw}=\frac{M_{ux}}{S_{x}}=\frac{1000.12}{971}\)=12.4 ksi(85.2 MPa)
\(F_{ca} = \phi_{c}F_{cr}\)=0.90 × 21.6=19.4 ksi (134 MPa)
\(f_{cbw}=\frac{\phi _{b}M_{n}}{S_{x}}=\frac{0.90\times 1869.12}{971}\)=20.8 ksi(143 MPa)
\(\left | \frac{f_{ra}}{F_{ca}}+\frac{f_{rbw}}{F_{cbw}} \right | =\left | \frac{6.44}{19.4}+\frac{12.4}{20.8} \right |=\left | 0.33+0.60 \right |=0.93< 1.0\)
ASD:
\(f_{ra}=\frac{P_{a}}{A_{g}}=\frac{325}{77.6}\)=4.19 ksi(28.9 MPa)
\(f_{rbw}=\frac{M_{ux}}{S_{x}}=\frac{660.12}{971}\)=8.16 ksi(56.2 MPa)
\(F_{ca} = \frac{F_{cr}}{\Omega_{c}}=\frac{21.6}{1.67}\)=12.9 ksi (89.2 MPa)
\(F_{cbw}=\frac{M_{n}}{\Omega_{b}S_{x}}=\frac{1869.12}{1.67\times 971}\)=13.8 ksi (95.3 MPa)
\(\left | \frac{f_{ra}}{F_{ca}} +\frac{f_{rbw}}{F_{cbw}}\right |=\left | \frac{4.19}{12.9}+\frac{8.16}{13.8} \right |=\left | 0.32+0.59 \right |=0.91< 1.0\)
Verification results are summarized in the following table in terms of values of the safety index (SI):

Verification according to: Safety index
LFRD ASD
(a) H1.1 0.86 0.85
(b) H1.3 0.80 0.78
(c) H2 0.93 0.91

In this case, verification according to paragraph H1.1 of AISC 360-10 produces slightly more conservative results than the one according to paragraph H1.3. Verifications according to paragraph H2 are much more conservative.
