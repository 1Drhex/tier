Guest Question.

Consider the simple pumping system shown in Fig. P11.4. Assume the following parameters to be known:

Reservoir elevations, \(z_1, z_2\)

Pipe length, L

Pipe roughness, e

Sum of minor loss coefficients, Σ K

Kinematic viscosity, ν

Discharge, Q

Develop a numerical solution to find the required diameter. Use an equivalent length to represent the minor losses. Determine the diameter for the following data:

(a) \(z_2 - z_1\) = 120 ft, L = 1500 ft, e = 0.003 ft , ΣK = 2.5, water at 50°F, Q = 15,000 gal/min, pump characteristic curve:

\(H_P\) (ft) 490 486 473 453 423 406
Q (gal/min) 0 4000 8000 12000 16000 18000
Admin
Solution

Write energy eqn. between locs. 1 and 2:

\(H_P +z_1 =z_2 +\frac{f}{D} (L+L_e)\frac{Q^2}{2g\left\lgroup\frac{\pi }{4}D^2 \right\rgroup^2 } \).

Solving for \(D_1, D=\left[\frac{Q^2 (L+L_e)f}{2g(\pi /4)^2(H_P-(z_2-z_1))} \right]^{1/5} \) .

Substitute the relations

\(f = 1.325 \left[\ln \left\lgroup0.27\frac{e}{D}+5.74 Re^{-0.9} \right\rgroup \right]^{-2} and Re =\frac{4Q}{π Dν}, L_e= \frac{ΣkD}{f}\) ,

and solve for D by successive substitution.

(a) Compute \(H_P\) from pump data using linear interpolation:

\(H_P = 453 -(453 -423) \frac{(12,000 – 15,000)}{(12,000 – 16,000)} = 430.5 ft\).

\(Q=15,000 gpm ×\frac{1 ft^3}{7.48 gal} × \frac{1 min}{60 sec} =33.4 ft^3/sec\) ,

∴ \(D =\left[\frac{33.4^2(1500+L_e)f}{2×32.2(\pi /4)^2 (430.5-120)} \right]^{1/5} =0.6184 [(1500+L_e)f]^{1/5}\) .
