Guest Question.

Determine the Jacobian matrix for a four-node, two-dimensional quadrilateral element having the parent element whose interpolation functions are given by Equation 6.56.

Equation (6.56a):

\(\ N_{1}(r,s)=\frac{1}{4}(1- r)(1- s) \)

 

 

\(\ N_{2}(r,s)=\frac{1}{4}(1+ r)(1- s) \)

 

 

\(\ N_{3}(r,s)=\frac{1}{4}(1+ r)(1+ s) \)

 

 

\(\ N_{4}(r,s)=\frac{1}{4}(1- r)(1+ s) \)

Equation (6.56b):

\(\ \phi (x,y)=\phi (r,s)=N_{1}(r,s)\phi _{1}+ N_{2}(r,s)\phi _{2}+ N_{3}(r,s)\phi _{3}+ N_{4}(r,s)\phi _{4} \)
Admin
Solution

The partial derivatives of x and y with respect to r and s per Equations 6.56 and 6.80 are

Equation (6.56a):

\(\ N_{1}(r,s)=\frac{1}{4}(1- r)(1- s) \)

 

 

\(\ N_{2}(r,s)=\frac{1}{4}(1+ r)(1- s) \)

 

 

\(\ N_{3}(r,s)=\frac{1}{4}(1+ r)(1+ s) \)

 

 

\(\ N_{4}(r,s)=\frac{1}{4}(1- r)(1+ s) \)

Equation (6.56b):

\(\ \phi (x,y)=\phi (r,s)=N_{1}(r,s)\phi _{1}+ N_{2}(r,s)\phi _{2}+ N_{3}(r,s)\phi _{3}+ N_{4}(r,s)\phi _{4} \)

Equation (6.80):

\(\ x=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{4}{N_{i} }(r,s)x_{i} \)

 

 

\(\ y=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{4}{N_{i} }(r,s)y_{i} \)

 

\(\ \frac{\partial x}{\partial r}=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{4}{\frac{\partial N_{i} }{\partial r}x_{i}}=\frac{1}{4}\left[- (1- s)x_{1}+ (1- s)x_{2}+ (1+ s)x_{3}- (1+ s)x_{4} \right] \)

 

 

\(\ \frac{\partial y}{\partial r}=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{4}{\frac{\partial N_{i} }{\partial r}y_{i}}=\frac{1}{4}\left[- (1- s)y_{1}+ (1- s)y_{2}+ (1+ s)y_{3}- (1+ s)y_{4} \right] \)

 

 

\(\ \frac{\partial x}{\partial s}=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{4}{\frac{\partial N_{i} }{\partial s}x_{i}}=\frac{1}{4}\left[- (1- r)x_{1}- (1+ r)x_{2}+ (1+ r)x_{3}+ (1- r)x_{4} \right] \)

 

 

\(\ \frac{\partial y}{\partial s}=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{4}{\frac{\partial N_{i} }{\partial s}y_{i}}=\frac{1}{4}\left[- (1- r)y_{1}- (1+ r)y_{2}+ (1+ r)y_{3}+ (1- r)y_{4} \right] \)

The Jacobian matrix is then

\(\ \left[J\right]=\frac{1}{4} \left [ \begin{matrix} (1- s)(x_{2}- x_{1})+ (1+ s)(x_{3}- x_{4}) & (1- s)(y_{2}- y_{1})+ (1+ s)(y_{3}- y_{4}) \\ (1- r)(x_{4}- x_{1})+ (1+ r)(x_{3}- x_{2}) & (1- r)(y_{4}- y_{1})+ (1+ r)(y_{3}- y_{2}) \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Note that ﬁnding the inverse of this Jacobian matrix in explicit form is not an enviable task. The task is impossible except in certain special cases. For this reason, isoparametric element formulation is carried out using numerical integration, as discussed in Section 6.10.
