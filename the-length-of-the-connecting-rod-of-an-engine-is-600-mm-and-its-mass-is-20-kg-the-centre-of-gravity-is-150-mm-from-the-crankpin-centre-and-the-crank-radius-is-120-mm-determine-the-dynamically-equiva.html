Guest Question.

The length of the connecting rod of an engine is 600 mm and its mass is 20 kg. The centre of gravity is 150 mm from the crankpin centre and the crank radius is 120 mm. Determine the dynamically equivalent system keeping one mass at the small end. The frequency of oscillations of the rod when suspended from the centre of the small end is 40 vibrations per minute.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given: } l=600 mm , m=20 kg , a=150 mm , r=120 mm , f_{n}=40 cycles / min \).

\( f_{n}=\frac{1}{2 \pi}\left(\frac{g l_{1}}{K^{2}+l_{1}^{2}}\right)^{0.5} \).

Here \( l_{1}=a=150 mm \).

\( \frac{40}{60}=\frac{1}{2 \pi}\left(\frac{9.81 \times 0.15}{K^{2}+0.15^{2}}\right)^{0.5} \).

\( K^{2}=0.06136 \).

\( K=0.2477 m \).

\( K^{2}=a b \).

\( b=\frac{(0.2477)^{2}}{0.15}=0.409 m \).

\( m_{a}=\frac{b m}{a+b}=\frac{0.409 \times 20}{0.15+0.409}=14.63 kg \).

\( m_{b}=20-14.63=5.37 kg \).
