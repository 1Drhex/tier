Guest Question.

Annular Fins. A circular tube has an outside diameter of 2.5 cm and a surface temperature, \(T_b\), of 170 °C. An annular aluminum fin of rectangular profile is attached to the tube. The fin has an outside radius, \(r_f\) , of 2.75 cm, a thickness, \(z_{f ,t}\) , of 1 mm, and a thermal conductivity, k, of 200 W/m·K. The surrounding fluid is at a temperature \(T_{\infty}\) = 25 °C and the associated heat transfer coefficient, h, is 130 W/\(m^2\)·K. Calculate the heat transfer rate without the fin, \(Q_{w/o, f}\) , the corrected length, \(z_{f ,c}\), the outer radius, \(r_{o,c}\), the maximum heat transfer rate from the fin, \(Q_{f ,max}\) , the fin efficiency, \(η_f\) , the fin heat transfer rate, \(Q_f\) , and the fin thermal resistance, \(R_{t , f}\).

Admin
Solution

Determine the area of the base of the fin:

\(r_o=\frac{D_o}{2}=\frac{0.025}{2}=0.0125\ m\)

\(A_b=2\pi r_oz_{f ,t}=2\pi(0.0125)(0.001)=7.854\times 10^{-5}m^2\)

Calculate the excess temperature at the base of the fin:

\(\theta _b\) = \(T_b\) – T = 170 – 25= 145 K

The total heat transfer rate without the fin, \(Q_{w/o, f}\) , is then (see Equation \(Q_{w/o, f}=hA_b\theta _b\)),

\(Q_{t,w/o, f}=Q_{w/o,f}=hA_b\theta_b=(130)(7.854\times 10^{-5})(145)\)

= 1.48 W = 5.0 Btu/hr

Calculate the Biot number. Employ Equation \(Bi_f=\frac{h(z_{f ,t}/2)}{k}\leq 0.25\). Determine if it is valid to use the fin efficiency figures provided in this section since the Biot number must be less than 0.25,

\(Bi_f=\frac{h(z_{f,t}/2)}{k_f}=\frac{(130)(0.001/2)}{200}=3.25\times 10^{-4}\lt 0.25\)

The fin efficiency figures may be used. Calculate the fin height, \(z_f\). Since,

\(r_f\) = 0.0275 m

\(z_f = r_f – r_o\) = 0.0275 – 0.0125 = 0.015 m

Calculate the corrected radius and height:

\(r_{f,c}=r_f+\frac{z_{f,t}}{2}=0.0275+\frac{0.001}{2}=0.028\ m\)

\(z_{f,c}=z_f+\frac{z_{f,t}}{2}=0.015+\frac{0.001}{2}=0.0155\ m\)

Calculate the profile area and the fin surface area:

\(A_p = z_{f,c}z_{f,t} = (0.0155)(0.001) = 1.55×10^{-5}m^2\)

\(A_p = 2π(r^2_{f,c} – r^2_o) = 2π(0.028^2-0.0125^2)=3.94×10^{-3}m^2\)

The maximum fin heat transfer rate, \(Q_{f ,max}\) , is therefore:

\(Q_{f,max} = hA_fθ_b\)

= (130)(3.94×\(10^{-3}\))(145) = 74.35 W

Calculate the curve parameter and the abscissa for the fin efficiency in Figure 8.8. Employ Equation (8.10) and the information provided in Figure 8.8:

\(Abscissa=z^{³⁄²}_{f,c}\left\lgroup\frac{h}{kA_p} \right\rgroup^{¹⁄²}=\sqrt{\frac{z^3_{f,c}h}{kA_p}}\) (8.10)

\(=\sqrt{\frac{(0.0155)^3(130)}{(200)(1.55\times 10^{-5})} }=0.395\)

From Figure 8.8 (interpolating),

\(η_f\) = 86% = 0.86

Therefore, the fin heat transfer rate, \(Q_f\) , as per Equation \(\eta_f=\frac{Q_f}{Q_{f,max}}=\frac{Q_f}{hA_f\theta_b }\) is,

\(Q_f = η_fQ_{f,max}\)

= (0.86)(74.35) = 64 W = 218 Btu/hr

The corresponding fin resistance, from Equations \(\eta_f=\frac{Q_f}{Q_{f,max}}=\frac{Q_f}{hA_f\theta_b }\) , \(R_f=fin\ thermal\ resistance=\frac{1}{\eta _fhA_f}\), is,

\(R_{t,f}=\frac{\theta_b}{Q_f};\ \ \theta_b=T-T_{\infty}\)

\(R_{t,f}=\frac{145}{64}=218\ K/W\)
