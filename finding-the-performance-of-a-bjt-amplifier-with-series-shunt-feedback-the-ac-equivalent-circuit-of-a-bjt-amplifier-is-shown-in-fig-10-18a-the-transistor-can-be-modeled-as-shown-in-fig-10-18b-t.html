Guest Question.

Finding the performance of a BJT amplifier with series-shunt feedback The AC equivalent circuit of a BJT amplifier is shown in Fig. 10.18(a). The transistor can be modeled as shown in Fig. 10.18(b). The DC bias currents of the transistors are \( I_{ C 1}=0.5 mA , I_{ C 2}=1 mA \text {, and } I_{ C 3}=5 mA \). The transistor parameters are \( h_{ fe }=h_{ fe 1}=h_{ fe 2}=h_{ fe 3}=100 \text { and } r_{\mu}=r_{ o }=\infty .\)

(a) Use the techniques of feedback analysis to calculate the input resistance \( R_{ if } \), the output resistance \( R_{\text {of }} \), and the closed-loop voltage gain \( A_{ f } \).
(b) Use PSpice/SPICE to check your results.

NOTE: The following analysis can also be applied to MOSFET amplifiers by substituting a very large
value of the B-E resistance \(r_\pi \) tending to infinity, \(1/r_\pi \simeq 0.\)

Admin
Solution

For \( I_{ C 1}=0.5 mA, \) Eqs. (8.40) and (8.42) give

\(\frac{1}{r_{\pi}}=\frac{i_{ b }}{v_{ be }}=\left.\frac{d i_{ B }}{d v_{ BE }}\right|_{ at Q \text {-point }}=\frac{I_{ B }}{V_{ T }}=\frac{I_{ B }}{25.8 mV }\) (8.40)

 

\(g_{ m }=\frac{i_{ c }}{v_{ be }}=\left.\frac{\beta_{ F } d i_{ B }}{d v_{ BE }}\right|_{\text {at } Q \text {-point }}=\frac{\beta_{ f } I_{ B }}{V_{ T }}=\frac{I_{ C }}{V_{ T }}=\frac{\beta_{ f }}{r_{\pi}}\) (8.42)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&r_{\pi 1}=\frac{h_{ fe } \times 25.8 mV }{I_{ C 1}}=\frac{100 \times 25.8 mV }{0.5 mA }=5.16 k \Omega \\&g_{ ml }=\frac{I_{ C 1}}{V_{ T }}=\frac{0.5 mA }{25.8 mV }=19.38 mA / V\end{aligned}\)

For \( I_{ C 2}=1 mA,\)

\(\begin{aligned}&r_{\pi 2}=\frac{h_{ fe } \times 25.8 mV }{I_{ C 2}}=\frac{100 \times 25.8 mV }{1 mA }=2.58 k \Omega \\&g_{ m 2}=\frac{I_{ C 2}}{V_{ T }}=\frac{1 mA }{25.8 mV }=38.76 mA / V\end{aligned}\)

 

For \( I_{ C 3}=5 mA , \)

\(\begin{aligned}r_{\pi 3} &=\frac{h_{ fe } \times 25.8 mV }{I_{ C 3}}=\frac{100 \times 25.8 mV }{5 mA }=516 \Omega \\g_{ m 3} &=\frac{I_{ C 3}}{V_{ T }}=\frac{5 mA }{25.8 mV }=193.8 mA / V\end{aligned}\)

 

(a) The steps in analyzing the feedback network are as follows:

Step 1. \( R_{ E } \text { and } R_{ F }\), which constitute the feedback network, produce a feedback voltage proportional to the output voltage. The input voltage \(v_{i}\) is compared with the feedback signal \( v_{ f } \). The error voltage \( v_{ e }=v_{ i }-v_{ f } \) is the B-E voltage of transistor \( Q _{1} \). The block diagram representing the feedback mechanism is shown in Fig. 10.19(a). The feedback network is shown in Fig. 10.19(b).

Step 2. The amplifier uses series-shunt feedback. Thus, the voltage gain A must be expressed in V⁄ V.

Step 3. The effect of the feedback network is taken into account at the input side by shorting \( R_{ F } \)at side 2 to the ground, as shown in Fig. 10.19(b), and at the output side by removing the feedback network from the emitter of \( Q _{1} \). These modifications are shown in the small-signal AC equivalent circuit in Fig. 10.19(c).

Step 4. If we represent the amplifier of Fig. 10.19(c) by an equivalent voltage amplifier, the input resistance at the base of \( Q _{1} \) is

\( R_{ i }=\frac{v_{ i }}{i_{ b 1}}=r_{\pi 1}+\left(1+g_{ m 1} r_{\pi 1}\right)\left(R_{ E } \| R_{ F }\right)=5.16 k +101 \times(100 \| 650)=13.91 k \Omega \)

The input resistance is

\( R_{ ie }=\frac{v_{ el }}{i_{ bl }}=R_{s}+R_{ i }=R_{ s }+r_{\pi 1}+\left(1+g_{ m 1} r_{\pi 1}\right)\left(R_{ E } \| R_{ F }\right)=150+13.91 k =14.06 k \Omega \)

The output resistance is

\( R_{ oe }=R_{ L }\left\|\left(R_{ F }+R_{ E }\right)\right\|\left(\frac{R_{2}+r_{\pi 3}}{1+g_{ m 1} r_{\pi 1}}\right)=100\|750\|\left(\frac{5 k +516}{101}\right)=33.74 \Omega \)

Thus,

\( V_{ bel }=\left(\frac{r_{\pi 1}}{R_{ i }}\right) v_{ i }=\left(\frac{5.16 k }{13.91 k }\right) v_{ i }=0.371 v_{ i } \)

 

\(\begin{aligned}v_{ b 2} &=v_{ be 2}=-\left(R_{1} \| r_{\pi 2}\right) g_{ m 1} v_{ be 1}=-(9 k \| 2.58 k ) \times 19.38 m \times 0.371 v_{ i }=-14.412 v_{ i } \\v_{ b 3} &=-\left\{R_{2} \|\left[r_{\pi 3}+\left(1+g_{ m 3} r_{\pi 3}\right)\left(R_{ L } \|\left(R_{ F }+R_{ E }\right)\right)\right]\right\} g_{ m 2} v_{ be 2} \\&=-\{5 k \|[516+(1+193.8 m \times 516)(100 \| 750)]\} \times 38.76 m \times\left(-14.412 v_{ i }\right)=1.825 \times 10^{3} v_{ i }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}v_{ o } &=\frac{v_{ b 3} \times\left(1+g_{ m 3} r_{\pi 3}\right)\left[R_{ L } \|\left(R_{ F }+R_{ E }\right)\right]}{\left\{r_{\pi 3}+\left(1+g_{ m 3} r_{\pi 3}\right)\left[R_{ L } \|\left(R_{ F }+R_{ E }\right)\right]\right\}} \\&=\frac{\left(1.825 \times 10^{3} \times v_{ i }\right) \times(1+193.8 m \times 516)(100 \| 750)}{[516+(1+193.8 m \times 516)(100 \| 750)]}=1725.7 v_{ i }\end{aligned}\)

 

Therefore, the open-loop voltage gain A is given by \( A=v_{ o } / v_{ i }=1725.7 V / V \)

and \( A_{ e }=\frac{v_{ o }}{v_{ el }}=\frac{A R_{ i }}{R_{ s }+R_{ i }}=\frac{1725.7 \times 13.91 k }{150+13.91 k }=1707.3 V / V \)

Step 5. From Fig. 10.19(b) and Eq. (10.33), the feedback factor β is given by

\( \beta=\left.\frac{v_{ f }}{v_{ o }}\right|_{i_{ f }=0} \) (open-circuited input side) (10.33)

 

\( \beta=\left.\frac{v_{ f }}{v_{ o }}\right|_{i_{ f }=0}=\frac{R_{ E }}{R_{ E }+R_{ F }}=\frac{100}{100+650}=0.1333 V / V \)

 

Step 6. The input resistance (seen by the source) with feedback is

\( R_{ if }=\frac{v_{ s }}{i_{ s }}=R_{ ie }\left(1+\beta A_{ e }\right)=14.06 k \Omega \times(1+0.1333 \times 1707.3)=3.21 M \Omega \)

Step 7. The output resistance with feedback is

\( R_{ of }=\frac{R_{ oe }}{1+\beta A_{ e }}=\frac{33.74}{1+0.1333 \times 1707.3}=0.147 \Omega \)

Step 8. The closed-loop voltage gain \( A_{ f } \) is

\( A_{ f }=\frac{v_{ o }}{v_{ s }}=\frac{A_{ e }}{1+\beta A_{ e }}=\frac{1707.3}{1+0.1333 \times 1707.3}=7.47 V / V \)

 

(b) The series-shunt feedback network for PSpice simulation is shown in Fig. 10.20. The results of PSpice simulation (.TF analysis) are shown below, with hand calculations to the right:

\(\begin{array}{lr}V (8) / VS =7.468 E +00=7.468 V / V & A_{ f }=7.47 V / V \\\text { INPUT RESISTANCE AT VS=3.214E+06=3.214 M }\Omega & R_{\text {if }}=3.21 M \Omega \\\text { OUTPUT RESISTANCE AT V(8)=1.460E-01=0.146 } \Omega & R_{\text {of }}=0.147 \Omega\end{array}\)

Note the close agreement between the results obtained by PSpice and the hand calculations.
