Guest Question.

Showing that the RHF wavefunction is an eigenfunction of \(S^{2}\)
Consider the RHF wavefunction \(\Psi _{0} =(2)^{-1/2} det \left|\psi ^{\alpha }_{a}(1)\psi ^{\beta }_{a}(2) \right| \) for the He atom. Show that it is an eigenfunction of \(S^{2}\) and evaluate its eigenvalue.
Method We need to expand the Slater determinant and consider the effect of the spin operator, which acts only on the spin states α and β and not on the spatial function \(\psi_{a}\). Because we are dealing with a two-electron system, \(S = s_{1} + s_{2}\), where \(s_{i}\) acts only on electron i. We use the relations

\(S^{2} =(s_{1}+s_{2} )\cdot (s_{1}+s_{2} )=s^{2}_{1} +s^{2}_{2} +2s_{1} \cdot s_{2} \)

 

\(s_{1} \cdot s_{2} =s_{1z} s_{2z} +s_{1x} s_{2x}+s_{1y} s_{2y}=s_{1z} s_{2z}+\frac{1}{2}(s_{1+} s_{2-}+s_{1-} s_{2+}) \)

The results of the operations of \(s^{2}, s_{z}, s_{+}\), and \(s_{−}\) on α and β are given in Section 4.8.

Admin
Solution

First, we expand the determinant:

\(\Psi _{0} =(2)^{-1/2} \left\{\psi _{a}(1)\alpha (1)\psi _{a}(2)\beta (2) – \psi _{a}(2)\alpha (2)\psi _{a}(1)\beta (1) \right\}\)

The effect of \(S^{2}\) on the first term in \(\Psi _{0}\) is

\(S^{2} \psi _{a} (1)\alpha (1)\psi _{a} (2)\beta (2)\)

 

\(= \psi _{a} (1)s^{2}_{1} \alpha (1)\psi _{a} (2)\beta (2)+ \psi _{a} (1) \alpha (1)\psi _{a} (2)s^{2}_{2}\beta (2)+2\psi _{a} (1)s_{1z} \alpha (1)\psi _{a} (2)s_{2z}\beta (2)+\psi _{a} (1)s_{1+} \alpha (1)\psi _{a} (2)s_{2-}\beta (2)+\psi _{a} (1)s_{1-} \alpha (1)\psi _{a} (2)s_{2+}\beta (2)\)

 

\(=\frac{3}{4} \hbar ^{2} \psi _{a} (1) \alpha (1)\psi _{a} (2)\beta (2)+\frac{3}{4} \hbar ^{2} \psi _{a} (1) \alpha (1)\psi _{a} (2)\beta (2)-\frac{1}{2} \hbar ^{2} \psi _{a} (1) \alpha (1)\psi _{a} (2)\beta (2)+0+ \hbar ^{2} \psi _{a} (1) \beta (1)\psi _{a} (2)\alpha (2)\)

 

\(=\hbar ^{2} \psi _{a} (1) \alpha (1)\psi _{a} (2)\beta (2)+\hbar ^{2} \psi _{a} (1) \beta (1)\psi _{a} (2)\alpha (2)\)

A similar analysis of the effect of \(S^{2}\) on the second term in \(\Psi _{0}\) yields

\(S ^{2} \psi _{a} (2) \alpha (2)\psi _{a} (1)\beta (1)=\hbar ^{2} \psi _{a} (2) \alpha (2)\psi _{a} (1)\beta (1)+\hbar ^{2} \psi _{a} (2) \beta (2)\psi _{a} (1)\alpha (1) \)

On collecting terms we obtain

\(S ^{2}\Psi _{0}=(2)^{-1/2} \left\{(\hbar^{2} -\hbar^{2} )\psi _{a} (1) \alpha (1)\psi _{a} (2)\beta (2)-(\hbar^{2} -\hbar^{2} )\psi _{a} (2) \alpha (2)\psi _{a} (1)\beta (1)\right\} =0\)

Therefore, \(\Psi _{0} \) is an eigenfunction of \(S^{2}\) with an eigenvalue of zero, as is to be expected because the ground state of the closed-shell helium atom is a singlet.
