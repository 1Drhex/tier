Guest Question.

A 500 m2 light-colored swimming pool is located in a normal suburban site, where the measured wind speed at 10 m height is 3 m/s. The water temperature is 25°C, the ambient air temperature is 17°C, and relative humidity is 60%. There are no swimmers in the pool, the temperature of the make-up water is 22°C, and the solar irradiation on a horizontal surface for the day is 20.2 MJ/m2-d. How much energy must the solar system supply (Qss) to the pool to keep its temperature to 25°C?

Admin
Solution

The energy balance of the pool is given by

\(q_{e}+q_{r}+q_{c}+q_{muw}-q_{s}=q_{ss}\)

The velocity at 0.3 m above the pool surface is 0.3 × 3 = 0.9 m/s. The partial pressures for air and water are given by Eqs. (5.20) and (5.21). The saturation water vapor pressure at air temperature, ta, is also given by Eq. (5.21); therefore,

\( P_{s} = 100(0.004516 +0.0007178 t_{a} –2.649 × 10^{-6} t^{2}_{a} + 6.944 × 10^{-7} t^{3}_{a} )\)

= \(100 ( 0.004516 + 0.0007178 × 17 – 2.649 × 10^{-6} × 17^2 + 6.944 × 10^{-7} × 17^3)\)

= 1.936 kPa

From Eq. (5.20),

\(P_{a}=\frac{P_{s}\times RH}{100} =\frac{1.936 \times 60}{100}\) = 1.162 kPa

Saturation water vapor pressure can also be obtained from Eq. (5.21) by using tw instead of ta. Therefore,

Pw = 3.166kPa

From Eq. (5.18), evaporation heat losses are

\(q_{e}= ( 5.64 + 5.96 \upsilon _{0.3})(P_{w}-P_{a})= (5.64+5.96 \times 0.9)(3.166 – 1.162) = 22.052\) MJ/m2-d

From Eq. (5.25),

\(\varepsilon _{s}= 0.711 + 0.56\left\lgroup\frac{t_{dp}}{100} \right\rgroup + 0.73\left\lgroup\frac{t_{dp}}{100} \right\rgroup ^2 = 0.711+0.56 \left\lgroup\frac{17}{100} \right\rgroup +0.73 \left\lgroup\frac{17}{100} \right\rgroup ^2 = 0.827\)

From Eq. (5.24),

\(T_{s}=T_{a}\sqrt{\varepsilon _{s}} =290\sqrt{0.827} = 263.7 K\)

 

From Eq. (5.22), radiation heat losses are

\(q_{r} = \frac{24 \times 3600}{10^6} \varepsilon _{w}\sigma (T^{4}_{w} – T^{4}_{s})\)

\(= 0.0864 \times 0.95\times 5.67\times 10^{-8}(298^4 – 263.7^4)= 14.198\)MJ/m2-d

From Eq. (5.26), convection heat losses are

\(q_{c} = 0.0864(3.1+4.1v)(t_{w}-t_{a})=0.0864(3.1+4.1\times 0.9)(25-17)=4.693\)MJ/m2-d

From steam tables, hfg, the latent heat of vaporization of water at 25°C is equal to 2441.8 MJ/kg. Therefore, the daily evaporation rate is given by Eq. (5.28):

\(m_{evp } = \frac{q_{c}}{h_{fg}} =\frac{4.694 \times 10^3}{2441.8} =1.922\)MJ/m2-d

From Eq. (5.27), the heat losses due to the make-up water are

\(q_{muw}= m_{evp}c_{p}(t_{muw}-t_{w})=1.922 \times 4.18(22-25)=24.10\)MJ/m2-d (the negative sign is not used, because all the values are losses)

From Eq. (5.29), solar radiation heat gain is

\(q_{s}=\alpha H_{t}=0.85\times 20.2 = 17.17\)MJ/m2-d

Therefore, the energy required by the solar system to keep the pool at 25°C is

\(q_{ss}=q_{e}+q_{r}+q_{c}+q_{muw}-q_{s}=22.052 +14.198 +4.693 +24.1 – 17.17 = 47.873\)MJ/m2-d or \(Q_{ss}=23.94\) GJ/d
