Guest Question.

Uniqueness of Basis Representation
Let \(u = \left\{u_1, u_2, u_3\right\} \) be any vector in \(R^3\).

Show that the equation \(u = c_{1}v_{1}+ c_{2}v_{2}+ c_{3}v_{3}\)
has a unique solution for the basis\( S =\left\{v1, v2, v3\right\}=\left\{\left(1,2,3\right),\left(0,1,2\right) ,\left(-2,1,0\right) \right\} \)

Admin
Solution

From the equation
\(\left(u_{1}, u_{2}, u_{3}\right) = c_1\left(1, 2, 3\right) + c_2\left(0, 1, 2\right) + c_3\left(−2, 0, 1\right)\\= (c_{1}− 2c_{3}, 2c_{1}+ c_{2}, 3c_{1}- 2c_{2}+ c_{3})\)
you obtain the system of linear equations below.

\(c_{1}-2c_{2}=u_{1}\) \(2c_{1}+c_{2}=u_{2}\) \(3c_{1}+2c_{2}+c_{3}=u_{3}\)

\(\underset{A \ \ C \ \ B}{\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & -2 \\ 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 3 & 2 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\left [ \begin{matrix} c_{1} \\ c_{2} \\ c_{3} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} u_{1} \\ u_{2} \\ u_{3} \end{matrix} \right ] } \)
The matrix A is invertible, so you know this system has a unique solution, \(c = A^{1}u\).
Verify by finding A^{1} that
\(c_{1}=−u_{1}+4u_{2}-2u_{3}\).

\(c_{2}=2u_{1}-7u_{2}+4u_{3}\)

\(c_{3}=−u_{1}+2u_{2}-u_{3}\).

For example, \(u = \left(1, 0, 0\right)\) can be represented uniquely as \(−v_{1}+ 2v_{2}− v_{3}\).
