Guest Question.

Find the mean and variance of the \( X(t) \) response for an SDF oscillator with \( m=100kg, k=10kN/m \) , and \( c=40N\cdot s/m \) . The excitation is a nonstationary delta-correlated process having a mean value of \( \mu _{F}(t)=\mu _{0}te^{-\alpha t} \) with \( \mu _{0}=1kN/s \) and \( \alpha =0.8s^{-1} \) and a covariance function of \( K_{FF}(t,s)=2\pi S_{0}te^{-\alpha t}\delta (t-s)U(t) \) with \( S_{0}=1000 N^{2}/rad \) . The system is initially at rest.

Admin
Solution

The state-space description of the system has the same form as in .

Thus, the equation for the mean value of the response is

\( A\dot{\vec{\mu }} _{Y}(t)+B\vec{\mu }_{Y}(t) =\vec{\mu }_{F}(t)=\mu _{0}te^{-\alpha t} U(t)\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right] \)

Or

\( \left(\begin{matrix} -10^{4} & 0 \\ 0 & 100 \end{matrix} \right) \dot{\vec{\mu } }_{Y}(t)+\left(\begin{matrix} 0 & 10^{4} \\ 10^{4} & 40 \end{matrix} \right) \vec{\mu }_{Y}(t)=10te^{-0.8t}U(t)\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right] \)

Its solution can be written as

\( \vec{\mu } _{Y}(t)=\frac{\mu _{0}}{m}\int_{0}^{t}{\exp [-(t-s)A^{-1}B]se^{-\alpha s}}ds\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right] \) \( =10\int_{0}^{t}{\theta \exp [-(t-s)}\lambda ]\theta ^{-1}se^{-0.8s} ds\left [\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right] \)

in which \( \theta \) is the \( 2\times 2 \) matrix with columns that are the eigenvectors of \( A^{-1}B \) , and \( \lambda =\theta ^{-1}A^{-1}B\theta \) is the diagonal matrix of eigenvalues. Eigenanalysis yields

\( \lambda_{11}=\zeta \omega _{0}-i\omega _{d}=0.2-9.998i \) , \( \lambda _{22}=\zeta \omega _{0}+i\omega _{d}=0.2+9.998i \) and

\( \theta =\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 1 \\ -\lambda _{11} & -\lambda _{22} \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 1 \\ -0.2+9.998i & -0.2-9.998i \end{matrix} \right] \)

The diagonal matrix \( \hat{A}=\theta ^{T}A\theta \) has elements \( \hat{A}_{11}=-19992-399.9i \) and \( \hat{A}_{22}=-19992+399.9i \) , and

\( \theta ^{-1}=\hat{A}^{-1}\theta ^{T}A=\left(\begin{matrix} 0.5-0.01002i & – 0.05001i\\ 0.5+0.01002i & 0.05001i \end{matrix} \right) \)

The first term of the \( \vec{\mu }_{Y} (t) \) vector is the mean of \( X(t) \) , and it is given by

\( \mu_{X} (t)=10\sum\limits_{r=1}^{2}{\theta _{1r}}\theta ^{-1}_{r2}\int_{0}^{t}{e^{-(t-s)\lambda _{rr}}se^{-\alpha s}} ds \)

which reduces to

\( \mu_{X} (t)=(0.001192+0.09968t)e^{-0.8t}-e^{-0.09899t}[0.001192\cos (9.9980t)+0.009899\sin (9.9980t)] \)

This result is shown here, along with \( \mu _{F}(t)/k \) , which is the static response that one would obtain by neglecting the dynamics of the system.

For the covariance analysis we can use the results derived in , for which our parameters give

\( S_{PP}=\frac{S_{0}te^{-\alpha t}}{4m^{2}\omega ^{2}_{d}} \left(\begin{matrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{matrix} \right) =te^{-0.8t}\left(\begin{matrix} -2.501\times 10^{-4} & 2.501\times 10^{-4} \\ 2.501\times 10^{-4} & -2.501\times 10^{-4} \end{matrix} \right) \)

 

Equation 9.31

\(K_{ZZ}(t,t)=\exp [-(t-t_{0})\lambda ]K_{ZZ}(t_{0},t{0})\exp [-(t-t_{0})\lambda ]+ 2\pi \int_{t_{0}}^{t}{\exp [-(t-s)\lambda ]S_{PP} (s)\exp [-(t-s)\lambda ] } ds \)

then gives the components of \( K_{ZZ}(t,t) \) , which can be written as

\( [K_{ZZ}(t,t)] _{jl}=\int_{0}^{t}{[S_{PP}(s,s)]_{jl}}e^{-(\lambda _{jj}+\lambda _{ll})(t-s)} ds \) \( =\pm 2.501\times 10^{-4}e^{-0.8t}\beta ^{-2}(1-e^{\beta t}+\beta t) \)

in which \( \beta =0.8-\lambda _{jj}-\lambda _{ll} \) . The covariance matrix for \( \vec{Y}(t) \) is then found from \( K_{YY}(t,t)=\theta K_{YY}(t,t)\theta ^{T} \) , and the (1,1) component is

\( \sigma ^{2}_{X}(t)=(-1.965\times 10^{-2}-7.854\times 10^{-3}t)e^{-0.8t}+e^{-0.4t}[1.964\times 10^{-2}+7.851\times 10^{-6}\cos (20.00t)-3.142\times 10^{-7}\sin (20.00t)] \)

The accompanying sketch compares this result with a “pseudostationary” approximation based only on stationary analysis. This approximation is simply \( \pi S_{0}(t)/(kc) \) , in which \( K_{FF}(t+\tau ,t)=2\pi S_{0}(t)\delta (\tau ) \) gives \( S_{0}(t)=S_{0}te^{-\alpha t}=1000te^{-0.8t} \) . The pseudostationary approximation is obtained by substituting the nonstationary autospectral density of the excitation into the formula giving the response variance for a stationary excitation. This approximation is good for problems in which \( S_{0}(t) \) varies slowly, but it is not surprising that it fails for the present problem.
