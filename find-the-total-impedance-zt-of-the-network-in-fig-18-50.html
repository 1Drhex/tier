Guest Question.

Find the total impedance \(Z_T\) of the network in Fig. 18.50.

Admin
Solution
\(Z_B = -j 4\qquad Z_A = -j 4 \qquad Z_C = 3 + j 4\\[0.5cm] Z_1=\frac{Z_BZ_C}{Z_A + Z_B + Z_C}=\frac{(-j 4 Ω)(3 Ω + j 4 Ω)}{(-j 4 Ω) + (-j 4 Ω) + (3 Ω + j 4 Ω)}\\[0.5cm] =\frac{(4 ∠-90°)(5 ∠53.13°)}{3 – j 4}=\frac{20 ∠-36.87°}{5 ∠-53.13°}\\[0.5cm] =4 Ω ∠16.13° = 3.84 Ω + j 1.11 Ω\\[0.5cm] Z_2=\frac{Z_AZ_C}{Z_A+Z_B+Z_C}=\frac{(-j 4 Ω)(3 Ω + j 4 Ω)}{5 Ω ∠-53.13°}\\[0.5cm] =4 Ω ∠16.13° = 3.84 Ω + j 1.11 Ω \)

Recall from the study of dc circuits that if two branches of the Y or ∆ are the same, the corresponding ∆ or Y, respectively, will also have two similar branches. In this example, \(Z_A = Z_B. Therefore, Z_1 = Z_2\), and

\(Z_3=\frac{Z_AZ_B}{Z_A+Z_B+Z_C}=\frac{(-j 4 Ω)(-j 4 Ω)}{5 Ω ∠-53.13°}\\[0.5cm] =\frac{16 Ω ∠-180°}{5 ∠-53.13°}=3.2 Ω ∠-126.87° = -1.92 Ω – j 2.56 Ω \)

Replace the ∆ by the Y (Fig. 18.51):

\(Z_1 = 3.84 Ω + j 1.11 Ω \qquad Z_2 = 3.84 Ω + j 1.11 Ω\\[0.5cm] Z_3 = -1.92 Ω – j 2.56 Ω \qquad Z4 = 2 Ω\\[0.5cm] Z_5 = 3 Ω\)

Impedances \(Z_1 and Z_4\) are in series:

\( Z_{T_1} = Z_1 + Z_4 = 3.84 Ω + j 1.11 Ω + 2 Ω = 5.84 Ω + j 1.11 Ω\\[0.5cm] =5.94 Ω ∠10.76°\)

Impedances \(Z_2 and Z_5\) are in series:

\( Z_{T2} = Z_2 + Z_5 = 3.84 Ω + j 1.11 Ω + 3 Ω = 6.84 Ω + j 1.11 Ω\\[0.5cm] =6.93 Ω ∠9.22°\)

Impedances \(Z_{T_1 } and Z_{T_2}\) are in parallel:

\(Z_{T_3}=\frac{Z_{T_1}Z_{T_2}}{Z_{T_1}+Z_{T_2}} =\frac{(5.94 Ω ∠10.76°)(6.93 Ω ∠9.22°)}{5.84 Ω + j 1.11 Ω + 6.84 Ω + j 1.11 Ω} \\[0.5cm] =\frac{41.16 Ω ∠19.98°}{12.68 + j 2.22}=\frac{41.16 Ω ∠19.98°}{12.87 ∠9.93°}=3.198 Ω ∠10.05°=3.15 Ω + j 0.56 Ω \)

Impedances \(Z_3 and Z_{T_3}\) are in series. Therefore,

\( Z_T = Z_3 + Z_{T_3} = -1.92 Ω – j 2.56 Ω + 3.15 Ω + j 0.56 Ω\\[0.5cm] =1.23 Ω – j 2.0 Ω =2.35Ω \angle -58.41°\)
