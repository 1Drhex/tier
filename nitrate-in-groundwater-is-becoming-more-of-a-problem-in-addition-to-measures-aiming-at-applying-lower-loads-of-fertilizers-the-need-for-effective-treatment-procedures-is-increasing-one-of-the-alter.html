Guest Question.

Nitrate in groundwater is becoming more of a problem. In addition to measures aiming at applying lower loads of fertilizers, the need for effective treatment procedures is increasing. One of the alternatives investigated is removal of nitrate by conversion to gaseous \(N _{2}\) using denitrifying bacteria immobilized in gel beads. Calculate the required size of an ideally mixed bioreactor treating 100 \(m ^{3}\)/h of ground water. The concentration of nitrate in the ground water is 50 g \(\cdot m ^{-3}\) and the treated water contains 1 g\(\cdot m ^{-3}\) nitrate. The load of gel beads in the reactor is 0.2 \(m^{3}-g e l / m^{3}\)-reaction mixture. The diameter of the gel beads is 1.5 mm, the diffusivity of nitrate inside the gel is \(10^{-10} m^{2}\)/s. Mass transfer coefficient of nitrate in the reactor is \(10^{-5}\) m/s. The kinetic constant are given by \(r_{\max }^{\prime}=0.4 g /\left( s \cdot m ^{3}- gel \right) \text { and } K_{ m }=2 g \cdot m ^{-3} \text { . }\)

Admin
Solution

The bioreactor is ideally well mixed (or CSTR, as shown in Fig. E17.5.1); thus the concentration of nitrate in the reactor is equal to the concentration in the effluent, that is \(C_{A b}=1 g \cdot m ^{-3}\). Mass balance of A (nitrate) over the CSTR gives

\(Q C_{ A 0}-Q C_{ Ab }+r_{ A } V=0 \). (E17.5.1)

The reaction occurs inside the gel (particle), and nitrate must diffuse from the bulk solution to the gel surface. Thus

\(-r_{A} V=\eta\left(-r_{A S}^{\prime}\right) V_{g e l}=k_{c} a_{c} V\left(C_{A b}-C_{A S}\right),\) (E17.5.2)

where \(V_{\text {gel }}\) is the total volume of gel particles, \(r_{A S}\) is the rate of reaction based on the gel volume and concentration of A on the external surface of gel particle, \(a_{c}\) is the specific area of gel external surface area (over the reactor volume), and η is the internal effectiveness factor.

The rate of reaction can be expressed as

\(-r_{A}^{\prime}=\frac{r_{m a x}^{\prime} C_{A}}{K_{m}+C_{A}}\), (E17.5.3)

when the reaction rate and concentration of A are evaluated based on the gel volume. The kinetic parameters are given by \(r_{\max }^{\prime}=0.4 g /\left( s \cdot m ^{-3} \text { -gel) and } K_{ m }=2 g \cdot m ^{-3}\right. \text { -gel }\). To compute the effectiveness factor, we need both \(K_{\beta} \text { and } \phi\). From the saturation constant,

\(K_{\beta}=\frac{K_{m}}{C_{A S}}\) (E17.5.4)

and the Thiele modulus can be computed by Eq. (17.51) \(\phi=\frac{1}{a_{p}} \sqrt{\frac{r_{\max }^{\prime}}{2 D_{e A} C_{A S}}} \frac{\left[1-K_{\beta} \ln \left(1+K_{\beta}^{-1}\right]^{-\frac{1}{2}}\right.}{1+K_{\beta}}\) or

\(\phi=\frac{d_{p}}{6} \sqrt{\frac{r_{\max }^{\prime}}{2 D_{e A} C_{A 5}}} \frac{\left[1-K_{\beta} \ln \left(1+K_{\beta}^{-1}\right]^{-\frac{1}{2}}\right.}{1+K_{\beta}}\) (E17.5.5)

Both \(K_{\beta} \text { and } \phi\) require \(C_{A S}\), which is an unknown that needs to be determined from Eq. (E17.4.2) \(-r_{A}^{\prime}=\frac{k^{\prime}\left(C_{A}-C_{B} / K_{C}\right)}{1+K_{A}^{\prime} C_{A}+K_{B}^{\prime} C_{B}}\). Therefore iterative scheme is needed to solve the problem.

Since \(C_{A S}<C_{A b}=1 g / m ^{3}\), we know from Eq. (E17.4.4) \(-r_{A}^{\prime}=\frac{k^{\prime}\left[C_{A}-\left(C_{0}-C_{A}\right) / K_{C}\right]}{1+K_{A}^{\prime} C_{A}+K_{B}^{\prime}\left(C_{0}-C_{A}\right)}\) that \( K_{\beta}>2 \). We can start the iterative solution by assuming \(K_{\beta} \rightarrow \infty\).

Eq. (E17.4.5) \(C_{A}^{\prime}=C_{A}-\frac{C_{0}}{1+K_{C}}\) leads to

\(\left.\phi\right|_{K_{\beta} \rightarrow \infty}=\frac{d_{p}}{6} \sqrt{\frac{r_{\max }^{\prime}}{D_{e A} K_{m}}}\) (E17.5.6)

\(\phi_{K \beta \rightarrow \infty}=11.18 \) From Eq. (17.72) \(\eta=\frac{\phi \operatorname{coth}(3 \phi)-1 / 3}{\phi^{2}}\),

\(\eta_{1}=\frac{\phi \operatorname{coth}(3 \phi)-1 / 3}{\phi^{2}}\) (E17.5.7)

η = 0.08678. With a value of η, we can solve for concentration of nitrate on the gel surface through Eq. (E17.5.2), or

\(\eta \frac{r_{m a x}^{\prime} C_{A S}}{K_{m}+C_{A S}} V_{g e l}=k_{c} a_{c} V\left(C_{A b}-C_{A S}\right)\). (E17.5.8)

Let

\(K_{r}=\frac{r_{m a x}^{\prime} V_{g e l}}{k_{c} a_{c} V}=\frac{0.4 \times 0.2}{10^{-5} \times \frac{6(1-0.2)}{1.5 \times 10^{-3}}} g / m ^{3}=2.5 g / m ^{3}\)

Eq. (E17.5.8) is reduced to

\(\eta \frac{C_{A S}}{K_{m}+C_{A S}} K_{r}=\left(C_{A b}-C_{A S}\right)\) (E17.5.9)

which can be solved to give (only the root that is physical is retained):

\(C_{A S}=\frac{C_{A b}-K_{m}-\eta K_{r}+\sqrt{\left(C_{A b}-K_{m}-\eta K_{r}\right)^{2}+4 C_{A b} K_{m}}}{2}\). (E17.5.10)

One obtains \(C_{A S}=0.93108 g / m ^{3}\).

Now if we assume \(C_{A S}=0.93108 g / m ^{3}\): Eq. (E17.5.4) gives \(K_{\beta}=2.148 ; \phi\) = 8.700; Eq. (E17.5.7) gives \(\eta_{1}\) = 0.11054; Eq. (17.67b) gives \(\eta_{0}\) = 0.1120; Eq. (17.87) gives η = 0.11078. Eq. (E17.5.9) gives \(C_{A S}=0.91318 g / m ^{3}\), which is slightly different from our first calculated value.

To get a converged solution, we use Excel solver continuing on the iterations (toward \(C_{A S}\) not changing), we obtain \(C_{A S}\) = 0.91349 g/\(m ^{3}\), η = 0.11036. This solution is very close to that from our second iteration.

Eqs. (E17.5.1) and (E17.5.2) gives

\(V=Q \frac{C_{A 0}-C_{A b}}{-r_{A}}=Q \frac{C_{A 0}-C_{A b}}{\eta\left(-r_{A S}^{\prime}\right) V_{\text {gel }} / V}=Q \frac{C_{A 0}-C_{A b}}{\eta \frac{r_{m a x}^{\prime} C_{A S}}{K_{m}+C_{A S}}(1-\varepsilon)}\). (E17.5.11)

Thus

\(V=Q \frac{C_{A 0}-C_{A b}}{\eta \frac{r_{\max }^{\prime} C_{A S}}{K_{m}+C_{A S}}(1-\varepsilon)}=\frac{100}{3600} \times \frac{50-1}{0.11036 \times \frac{0.4 \times 0.91249}{2+0.91249} \times 0.2} m ^{3}\)

 

\(=491.7 m ^{3}\)

The required working volume of the reactor is \(491.7 m ^{3}\).
