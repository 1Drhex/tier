Solution

a. As both webs and flanges are produced using an eight-ply stacking sequence, laminate thicknesses are

\(t_w = t_{bf }= t_{tf} = 8(0.125mm) = 1mm\)

The length of the bottom and top flange laminates equals the overall width of the beam (30 mm). The length of the web laminates are

\(h = 50 mm − t_{bf }− t_{tf} = 50 mm − 2(1mm) = 48 mm\)

We can now calculate the area properties of the web and flange laminates:

\(A_w=(h)(t_w)=(48mm)(1mm)=48×10^{-6}m^2\)

 

\(A_{bf}=A_{tf}=(b)(t_{bf})=(30mm)(1mm)=30×10^{-6}m^2\)

 

\(I_{bf}=I_{tf}=\frac{(b)(t_{bf})^3}{12}=\frac{(30mm)(1mm)^3}{12}=2.5\times 10^{-12}m^4\)

 

The effective moduli for the web and flange laminates are calculated using the process described in Section 6.9.1 and are found to be

\(\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_w=\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_{bf}=\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_{tf}=90.4Gpa\)

 

\(\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{fl}\right|_{bf}=\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{fl}\right|_{tf}=120 GPa\)

From Table 8.1, for a composite box beam the location of the centroid is given by

\(z_b=\frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{2A_w\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_w(h+2t_{bf})+t_{bf}A_{bf}\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_{bf}+A_{tf}\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_{tf}(2t_{bf}+2h+t_{tf})}{2A_w\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_w+A_{bf}\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_{bf}+A_{tf}\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_{tf}} \right]\)

Substituting all known values and completing the indicated calculations, we find

\(z_b=25mm\)

This result is as anticipated, as the bottom and top flange laminates are identical and the total depth of the beam is 50 mm.

From Table 8.2, for a composite box-beam the effective axial rigidity is

\((\overline{AE} )=2A_w\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_w+A_{bf}\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_{bf}+A_{tf}\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_{tf}\)

Substituting all known values and completing the indicated calculations, we find

\((\overline{AE} )=14.1\times 10^6 N\)

From Table 8.3, for a composite box-beam the effective flexural rigidity is

\((\overline{IE} )=\frac{2t_w\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_w}{3}\left[(z_b-t_{bf})^3-(z_b-h-t_{bf})^3\right]+\left[(z_b-t_{bf}/2)^2A_{bf}\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_{bf}+(z_b-t_{bf}-h-t_{tf}/2)^2A_{tf}\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_{tf}+I_{bf}\left. \overline{E}_{xx}^{fl}\right|_{bf}+I_{tf}\left. \overline{E}_{xx}^{fl}\right|_{tf} \right]\)

Substituting all known values and completing the indicated calculations, we find

\((\overline{IE} )=4.92\times 10^3N-m^2\)

b. The web and bottom flanges are produced using the same eight-ply stacking sequence considered in Part (a), but now the top flange is a 20-ply laminate. Adjusting our previous calculations, we find

\(t_w=t_{bf}=8(0.125 mm) =1 mm\)

 

\(t_{tf} =20(0.125 mm) =2.5 mm\)

 

\(h=50 mm -t_{bf} – t_{tf} = 50 mm -1 mm – 2.5 mm = 46.5 mm\)

 

\(A_w=(h)(t_w) = (46.5 mm)(1 mm) =46.5 × 10^{-6} m^2\)

 

\(A_{bf}=(b)(t_{bf}) = (30 mm)(1 mm) =30 × 10^{-6} m^2\)

 

\(A_{tf}=(b)(t_{tf}) = (30 mm)(2.5 mm) =74 × 10^{-6} m^2\)

 

\(I_{bf}=\frac{(b)(t_{bf})^3}{12}=\frac{(30 mm)(1 mm)^3}{12}=2.5\times 10^{-12} m^4\)

 

\(I_{tf}=\frac{(b)(t_{tf})^3}{12}=\frac{(30 mm)(2.5 mm)^3}{12}=39.1\times 10^{-12 }m^4\)

 

\(\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_w=\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_{bf}=90.4 GPa\)

 

\(\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{fl}\right|_{bf}=120 GPa\)

 

\(\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_{tf}=83.3 GPa\)

 

\(\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{fl}\right|_{tf}=94.0 GPa\)

On the basis of the appropriate expression taken from Table 8.1, we find that the centroid is located at

\(z_b =29.4 mm\)

Note that the increased thickness of the top flange laminate has had the effect of moving the centroid 4.4 mm toward the top flange. The new axial and flexural rigidities are

\((\overline{AE} )=17.4\times 10^6 N\)

 

\((\overline{IE} )=6.35\times 10^3 N-m^2\)

The increased thickness of the top flange laminate has increased the effective axial and flexural rigidities by 23% and 29%, respectively.

c. For this cross-section, the web laminates are produced using the same eight-ply stacking sequence considered in part (a), but now both the bottom and top flanges consist of a 20-ply laminate. We find

\(t_w =8(0.125 mm) =1 mm\)

 

\(t_{tf}=t_{bf} =20(0.125 mm) =2.5 mm\)

 

\(h=50 mm -t_{bf} -t_{tf} =50 mm – 2.5 mm – 2.5 mm =45 mm\)

 

\(A_w=(h)(t_w)=(45 mm)(1 mm) =45 × 10^{-6} m^2\)

 

\(A_{bf}=A_{tf}=(b)(t_{bf})=(30 mm)(2.5 mm) =75 × 10^{-6} m^2\)

 

\(I_{bf}=I_{tf}=\frac{(b)(t_{bf})^3}{12}=\frac{(30 mm)(2.5 mm)^3}{12}=39.1\times 10^{-12} m^4\)

 

\(\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_{w}=90.4 GPa\)

 

\(\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_{bf}=\left. {E} _{xx}^{ex}\right|_{tf}=83.3 GPa\)

 

\(\left. \overline{E} _{xx}^{fl}\right|_{bf}=\left. {E} _{xx}^{fl}\right|_{tf}=94.0 GPa\)

Employing the appropriate expressions from Tables 8.1 through 8.3, we find

\(z_b =25.0 mm\)

 

\((\overline{AE} )=20.6\times 10^6 N\)

 

\((\overline{IE} )=8.43\times 10^3 N-m^2\)

As the top and bottom flanges are identical, the centroid is centered in the overall beam cross-section, as was the case in part (a). There is now a further increase in effective rigidities. Compared with the values calculated in part (a) the increased thickness of the bottom and top flange laminates has increased the effective axial and flexural rigidities by 46% and 71%, respectively.
