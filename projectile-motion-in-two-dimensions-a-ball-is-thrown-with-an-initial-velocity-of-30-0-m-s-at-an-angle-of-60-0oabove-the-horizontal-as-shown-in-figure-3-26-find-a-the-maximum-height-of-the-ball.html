Guest Question.

Projectile motion in two dimensions. A ball is thrown with an initial velocity of 30.0 m/s at an angle of 60.0º above the horizontal, as shown in figure 3.26. Find (a) the maximum height of the ball, (b) the time to rise to the top of the trajectory, (c) the total time the ball is in the air, (d) the range of the ball, (e) the velocity of the ball as it strikes the ground, and (f) the position and velocity of the ball at t = 4 s.

Admin
Solution

The x-component of the initial velocity is

\(v_{0_x} = v_0 \cos θ = (30.0 m/s) \cos 60^\circ = 15.0 m/s\)

The y-component of the initial velocity is

\(v_{0_y} = v_0 \sin θ = (30.0 m/s) \sin 60.0^\circ = 26.0 m/s\)

a. The maximum height of the ball, found from equation 3.49, is

\(y_{max}=\frac{v_{0y}^2}{2g}\) (3.49)

\(y_{max}=\frac{v_{0y}^2}{2g}= \frac{(26.0 m/s)^2}{2(9.80 m/s^2)} = 34.5 m\)

b. To find the time to rise to the top of the trajectory, we use equation 3.43,

\(t_r =\frac{v_{0y}}{g}\) (3.43)

\(t_x =\frac{v_{0y}}{g}=\frac{26.0 m/s}{9.80 m/s^2}=2.65 s\)

c. To find the total time that the ball is in the air, we use equation 3.45,

\(t_t =\frac{2v_{0y}}{g}=2t_r\) (3.45)

\(t_t = 2t_r = 2(2.65 s) = 5.30 s\)

d. The range of the ball, found from equation 3.47, is

\(R = \frac{v_0 ^2\sin 2\theta }{g}\) (3.47)

\(R = \frac{v_0^2\sin 2\theta }{g}= \frac{(30.0 m/s)^2\sin 120^\circ }{9.80 m/s^2}=79.5 m\)

As a check, we can use equation 3.46 to get

\(range=R = x_{max} = v_{0x}t_t \) (3.46)

\(R = x_{max} = v_{0x}t_t = (15.0 m/s)(5.30 s) = 79.5 m\)

e. To find the magnitude of the velocity of the ball at the ground, we use equation 3.53,

\(v_g =\sqrt{(v_{xg})^2+(v_{yg})^2}\) (3.53)

where

\(v_{xg} = v_{0x} = 15.0 m/s\)

and

\(v_{yg }= v_{0y }− gt_t = 26.0 m/s − (9.80 m/s^2)(5.30 s)\)

 

\(= 26.0 m/s − 51.9 m/s = −25.9 m/s\)

Hence,

\(v_g =\sqrt{(15.0 m/s)^2+(-25.9 m/s)^2}\)

= 29.9 m/s ≅ 30.0 m/s because of round off errors

The direction that the velocity vector makes with the ground is

\(\theta = \tan^{-1}\frac{v_{yg}}{v_{xg}}= \tan^{-1}\frac{-25.9 m/s}{15.0 m/s}= -59.9^\circ \cong 60.0^\circ \)

f. To find the position and velocity of the ball at t = 4 s we use the kinematic equations 3.38 through 3.41.

\(x = v_{0x}t \) (3.38)

1. \(x = v_{0_x}t = (15.0 m/s)(4 s) = 60.0 m\)

2. \(y = v_{0y}t – \frac{1}{2}gt^2\) (3.39)

\(= (26.0 m/s)(4 s)- \frac{1}{2}(9.80 m/s^2)(4 s)^2\)

= 25.6 m

The ball is 60.0 m down range and is 25.6 m high

The components of the velocity at 4 s are

\(v_x = v_{0x} \) (3.40)

3. \(v_x = v_{0x} = 15.0 m/s\)

4. \(v_y = v_{0y} − gt\) (3.41)

\(= 26.0 m/s − (9.80 m/s^2)(4 s)\)

= −13.2 m/s

At the end of 4 s the x-component of the velocity is 15.0 m/s and the y-component is −13.2 m/s. To determine the magnitude of the velocity vector at 4 s we have

\(v = \sqrt{(v_x)^2+(v_y)^2}\)

 

\(v = \sqrt{(15.0 m/s)^2+(- 13.2 m/s)^2}\)

= 20.0 m/s

The direction of the velocity vector at 4 s is

\(\theta = \tan^{-1}\frac{v_{yg}}{v_{xg}}= \tan^{-1}\frac{-13.2 m/s}{15.0 m/s}= -41.3^\circ \)

The velocity vector makes an angle of \(41.3^\circ \) below the horizontal at 4 s.
