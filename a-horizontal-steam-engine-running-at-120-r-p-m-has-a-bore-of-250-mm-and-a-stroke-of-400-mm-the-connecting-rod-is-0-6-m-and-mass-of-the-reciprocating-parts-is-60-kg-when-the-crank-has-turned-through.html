Guest Question.

A horizontal steam engine running at 120 r.p.m. has a bore of 250 mm and a stroke of 400 mm. The connecting rod is 0.6 m and mass of the reciprocating parts is 60 kg. When the crank has turned through an angle of \(45^{\circ}\) from the inner dead centre, the steam pressure on the cover end side is 550 \(k N / m^{2}\) and that on the crank end side is 70 \(k N / m^{2}\). Considering the diameter of the piston rod equal to 50 mm, determine:
1. turning moment on the crank shaft, 2. thrust on the bearings, and 3. acceleration of the flywheel, if the power of the engine is 20 kW, mass of the flywheel 60 kg and radius of gyration 0.6 m.

Admin
Solution

Given : N = 120 r.p.m. or \(\omega=2 \pi \times 120 / 60=12.57 rad / s\) ; D = 250 mm = 0.25 m ; L = 400 mm = 0.4 m or r = L/2 = 0.2 m ; l = 0.6 m ; \(m_{ R }=60 kg ; \theta=45^{\circ}\) ; d = 50 mm = 0.05 m ; \(p_{1}=550 kN / m ^{2}=550 \times 10^{3} N / m ^{2} ; p_{2}=70 kN / m ^{2}=70 \times 10^{3} N / m ^{2}\)

1. Turning moment on the crankshaft

First of all, let us find the net load on the piston \(\left(F_{ P }\right)\).

We know that area of the piston on the cover end side,

\(A_{1}=\frac{\pi}{4} \times D^{2}=\frac{\pi}{4} \times(0.25)^{2}=0.049 m ^{2}\)

and area of piston rod, \(a=\frac{\pi}{4} \times d^{2}=\frac{\pi}{4} \times(0.05)^{2}=0.00196 m ^{2}\)

\(\therefore \) Net load on the piston,

\(F_{ L }=p_{1} \cdot A_{1}-p_{2} \cdot A_{2}=p_{1} \cdot A_{1}-p_{2}\left(A_{1}-a\right)\)

 

\(=550 \times 10^{3} \times 0.049-70 \times 10^{3}(0.049-0.00196)=23657 N\)

We know that ratio of lengths of the connecting rod and crank,

n = l/r = 0.6/0.2 = 3

and inertia force on the reciprocating parts,

\(F_{ I }=m_{ R } \cdot \omega^{2} \cdot r\left(\cos \theta+\frac{\cos 2 \theta}{n}\right)\)

 

\(=60 \times(12.57)^{2} \times 0.2\left(\cos 45^{\circ}+\frac{\cos 90^{\circ}}{3}\right)=1340 N\)

\(\therefore \) Net force on the piston or piston effort,

\(F_{ P }=F_{ L }-F_{ I }=23657-1340=22317 N =22.317 kN\)

Let \(\phi\) = Angle of inclination of the connecting rod to the line of stroke.

We know that, \(\sin \phi=\sin \theta / n=\sin 45^{\circ} / 3=0.2357\)

\(\therefore \) \(\phi=13.6^{\circ}\)

We know that turning moment on the crankshaft,

\(T=\frac{F_{ p } \sin (\theta+\phi)}{\cos \phi} \times r=\frac{22.317 \times \sin \left(45^{\circ}+13.6^{\circ}\right)}{\cos 13.6^{\circ}} \times 0.2 kN – m\)

 

= 3.92 kN-m = 3920 N-m

 

2. Thrust on the bearings

We know that thrust on the bearings,

\(F_{ B }=\frac{F_{ P } \cos (\theta+\phi)}{\cos \phi}=\frac{22.317 \times \cos \left(45^{\circ}+13.6^{\circ}\right)}{\cos 13.6^{\circ}}=11.96 kN\)

 

3. Acceleration of the flywheel

Given: P = 20 kW = 20 × \(10^{3}\) W; m = 60 kg ; k = 0.6 m

 

Let \(\alpha\) = Acceleration of the flywheel in \(rad / s ^{2}\).

We know that mass moment of inertia of the flywheel,

\(I=m \cdot k^{2}=60 \times(0.6)^{2}=21.6 kg – m ^{2}\)

 

\(\therefore \) Accelerating torque, \(T_{ A }=I \cdot \alpha=21.6 \alpha N – m\) …(i)

and resisting torque, \(T_{ R }=\frac{P \times 60}{2 \pi N}=\frac{20 \times 10^{3} \times 60}{2 \pi \times 120}=1591 N – m\) \(\left(\therefore P=\frac{2 \pi N T}{60}\right)\)

Since the accelerating torque is equal to the difference of torques on the crankshaft or turning moment (T) and the resisting torque \(\left(T_{ R }\right)\), therefore, accelerating torque,

\(T_{ A }=T-T_{ R }=3920-1591=2329 N – m\) …(ii)

From equation (i) and (ii),

\(\alpha=2329 / 21.6=107.8 rad / s ^{2}\)
