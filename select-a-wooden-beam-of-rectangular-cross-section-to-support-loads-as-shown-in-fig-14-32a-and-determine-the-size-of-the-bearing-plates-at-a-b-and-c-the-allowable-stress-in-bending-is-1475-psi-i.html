Guest Question.

Select a wooden beam of rectangular cross section to support loads as shown in Fig. 14.32(a) and determine the size of the bearing plates at A, B, and C. The allowable stress in bending is 1475 psi, in shear parallel to the grain 115 psi, and in bearing perpendicular to the grain of the wood 200 psi.

Admin
Solution

Solving for the reactions at A and C from the free-body diagram shown in Fig. 14.32(b), we have \(A_{y} = 2160\ lb\) and \(C_{y} = 1440\ lb.\) The shear force and bending-moment diagrams are plotted in Fig.
14.32(c) and (d). From Eq. (14.12), the required cross-sectional area

\(\tau_{a} \geq \frac{3 V_{\max }}{2 A} \text { or } A_{\text {req }} \geq \frac{3 V_{\max }}{2 \tau_{a}}\quad(14.12)\)

 

\(A_{req}\geq \frac{3V_{max}}{2\tau _{a}}=\frac{3(2160)}{2(115)}=23.2\ in.^{2}\)

The maximum bending moment \(M_{max} = 8640 lb-ft = 103,680\ lb-in.,\) and from Eq. (14.13), the required section modulus

\(\sigma_{a} \geq \frac{M_{\max }}{S} \text { or } \quad S_{\mathrm{req}} \geq \frac{M_{\max }}{\sigma_{a}}\quad(14.13)\)

\(S_{req}\geq \frac{M_{max}}{\sigma _{a}}=\frac{103,680}{1475}=70.3\ in.^{3}\)

We select two possible cross sections from Table A.9 of the Appendix.

4 in. × 12 in. A = 39.4 \(in.^{2}\) S = 73.8 \(in.^{3}\)

6 in. × 10 in. A = 52.2 \(in.^{2}\) S = 82.7 \(in.^{3}\)

Check of the 4 × 12-in. Wooden Beam: The 4 × 12-in. nominal size timber is the lightest section, and it weighs 10.9 lb/ft. It also has a depth three times the width. To check the section, we determine the additional shear force at A and the additional bending moment at B due to the weight of the beam. From Eqs. (b) and (c) of , the additional shear force at the reactions

\(V_{x}=\frac{w L}{2}-w x=\frac{w}{2}(L-2 x)\quad(a)\)

\(V_{\max }=\frac{w L}{2} \text { and } M_{\max }=\frac{w L^{2}}{8}\quad(c)\)

\(V_{max}=\frac{wL}{2}=\frac{10.9(16)}{2}=87\ lb\)

and the additional bending moment at x = 4 ft

\(M_{x-4\ ft}=\frac{10.9(4)}{2}(16-4)=262\ lb-ft\)

Therefore, the maximum bending moment and shear force including the weight of the beam \(M_{max} = 8640 + 262 = 8902\ lb-ft = 106,824\ lb-in.\) and \(V_{max} = 2160\ lb + 87\ lb = 2247\ lb.\) From Eq. (14.12), the required cross-sectional area including the weight of the member

\(\tau_{a} \geq \frac{3 V_{\max }}{2 A} \text { or } A_{\text {req }} \geq \frac{3 V_{\max }}{2 \tau_{a}}\)

 

\(A_{req}\geq \frac{3V_{max}}{2\tau _{a}}=\frac{3(2247)}{2(115)}=29.3\ in.^{2}\lt A=39.4\ in.^{2}\) OK

and from Eq. (14.13), the required section modulus including the weight of the member

\(S_{req}\geq \frac{M_{max}}{\sigma _{a}}=\frac{106.824}{1475}=72.4\ in.^{3}\lt S=73.8\ in.^{3}\) OK

Use a 4 × 12-in. nominal size beam.

Bearing plates are provided at the concentrated load and the supports. From Eq. (9.4), the required bearing area \((Ab)_{req} = P/σ_{ba}\), where P is the load on the plate and \(σ_{ba}\) is the allowable bearing stress. The bearing area is equal to the width of the beam w times the length of the plate L, that is, \(A_{b} = wL;\) therefore,

\(W=\Sigma \Delta w_{i}\quad(9.4)\)

 

\((wL)_{req}\geq \frac{P}{\sigma _{ba}}\) or \(L_{req}\geq \frac{P}{W\sigma _{ba}}\) (a)

The width of the beam is 3.5 in., and the allowable bearing stress perpendicular to the grain is 200 psi.

Bearing Plate at A : P = R = 2160 lb + 87 lb = 2247 lb (including the weight of the beam). From Eq. (a),

\(L_{req}\geq \frac{P}{W\sigma _{ba}} =\frac{2247}{3.5(200)}=3.21\ in.\) (use 3.5 in.)

Use PL3.5 in. × 3.5 in.

Bearing Plate at B: P = 2160 lb. From Eq. (a),

\(L_{req}\geq \frac{P}{W\sigma _{ba}} =\frac{2160}{3.5(200)}=3.09\ in.\) (use 3.5 in.)

Use PL3.5 in. × 3.5 in.

Bearing Plate at C: P = R = 1440 lb + 87 lb = 1527 lb (including weight of beam). From Eq. (a),

\(L_{req}\geq \frac{P}{W\sigma _{ba}} =\frac{1527}{3.5(200)}=2.18\ in.\) (use 2.5 in.)

Use PL3.5 in. × 2.5 in.
