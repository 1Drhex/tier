Guest Question.

The container with water in it has a mass of 30 kg. Block B has a density of \(8500 kg/m^{3}\) and a mass of 15 kg. If springs C and D have an unstretched length of 200 mm and 300 mm, respectively, determine the length of each spring when the block is submerged in the water.

Admin
Solution

The volume of block B is,
\(V_{B} =\frac{m_{B}}{\rho } = \frac{15 kg}{8500 kg/m^{3}}= 1.7647(10^{-3}) m^{3} \)

Thus, the bouyant force is
\(F_{b} = \rho _{w}gV_{sub} = (1000 kg/m^{3})(9.81 m/s^{2})(1.7647(10^{-3}) m^{3}) = 17.31 N\)

Referring to the FBD of block B, Fig. a,
\(+ \sum F_{y} = 0; (F_{sp})_{c} + 17.31 N – [ 15(9.81) N] = 0 (F_{sp})_{c} = 129.84 N\)

Referring to the FBD of the container, Fig. b,
\(+ \sum F_{y} = 0; (F_{sp})_{D} – 17.31 N – [30(9.81) N] = 0 (F_{sp})_{D} = 311.61 N \)

Thus, the deformations of springs C dand D are

\(\delta _{C} = \frac{(F_{sp})_{C}}{k_{C}} = \frac{129.84 N}{2000 N/m} = 0.06492 m = 64.92 mm\)

 

\(\delta _{D} = \frac{(F_{sp})_{D}}{k_{D}} = \frac{311.61 N}{3000 N/m}= 0.1039 m = 103.87 mm \)

Thus

\(l_{C} = (lo)_{C} + \delta _{c} = 200 mm + 64.92 mm = 264.92 mm = 265 mm \)

 

\(l_{D} = (lo)_{D} + \delta _{D} = 300 mm – 103.87 mm = 196.13 mm = 196 mm \)
