Guest Question.

Compute the overall voltage amplification for the two-stage \(RC\)-coupled amplifier shown in \(Fig. 61.8\). Express the answer in decibels. Neglect \(V_{BE}\) and use \(r_e = 50 mV/I_E\). Take \(\beta_1 =\beta_2 = 100\) .

Admin
Solution

For finding the overall gain, we will have to find each stage gain.

\((i)\) \(A_{v1}= \frac{r_{0.1} }{r_{e.1} }\) , Now, \(r_{0.1}= R_3 \left|\right| r_{i.2}\) and \(r_{i.2} = R_5 \left|\right| R_6 \left|\right| \beta_2.r_{e.2}\)

Now, \(r_{e.2} = 50/I_{E.2}\)

Drop across

\(R_{6}= 20 \times \frac{5}{5+45}=2V\)

It also represents the approximate drop across \(R_8\).

\( \therefore \) \(I_{E2} = 2/1 K = 2 mA\)

 

\( \therefore \) \(r_{e.2} = 50/2=25 \Omega\) .

\(\beta_2.r_{e.2} = 100 \times 25 = 2.5 K\)

 

\(r_{i.2} = 45 K \left|\right| 5 K \left|\right| 2.5 K=1.6 K\) ;

\(\therefore\) \(r_{0.1} = 5 K \left|\right| 1.6 K =1.2 K\)

 

\(\therefore\) \(r_{e.1} = 50/I_{E.1}= 50/2=25 \Omega\)

 

\(\therefore\) \(A_{v.1} = 1200/25=48\)

 

\((ii)\) \(A_{v2}= \frac{r_{0.2} }{r_{c.2} }\) ; \(r_{0.2}=R_{7}\left|\right| R_{9}= 5K\left|\right| 20K=4K \)

 

\(\therefore\) \(A_{v.2} = 4000/25 = 160\) \(\therefore\) \(A_v = 48 \times 160 = 7,680 \) ; \(G_v = 20 \log_{10} 7,680 = 77.6 dB\)
