Guest Question.

Finding the parameters of a common-gate amplifier The CG amplifier of Fig. 7.44(a) has \( R_{ s }=500 \Omega \), \( R_{ SR }=1 k \Omega, R_{ D }=5 k \Omega, \text { and } R_{ L }=10 k \Omega \). The transistor parameters are \( r_{ o1 }=100 k \Omega \text { and } \mu_{ g }=230 \). Assume that \( C_{1} \text { and } C_{2} \) are very large, tending to infinity. That is, \( C_{1}=C_{2} \approx \infty \). Calculate (a) the input resistance \( R_{ in }=v_{ s } / i_{ s } \), (b) the no-load voltage gain \( A_{ vo }=v_{ o } /\left(-v_{ gs }\right) \), (c) the output resistance \( R_{ o } \), and (d) the overall voltage gain \( A_{ V }=v_{ L } / v_{ s } \).

Admin
Solution
\( R_{ s }=500 \Omega, R_{ SR }=1 k \Omega, R_{ D }=5 k \Omega, R_{ L }=10 k \Omega, r_{ o1 }=100 k \Omega, \text { and } \mu_{ g }=230 .\)

 

(a) From Eq. (7.81),

 

\( R_{ i }=\frac{-v_{ gs }}{i_{ s }}=R_{ SR } \|\left(\frac{r_{ o 1}+R_{ D } \| R_{ L }}{1+\mu_{ g }}\right) \) (7.81)

 

\( R_{ i }=R_{ SR }\left\|\left(\frac{r_{ o 1}+R_{ D } \| R_{ L }}{1+\mu_{ g }}\right)=1 k \right\| \frac{100 k +5 k \| 10 k }{1+230}=309 \Omega \)

 

\( R_{ in }=R_{ i }+R_{ s }=309+500=809 \Omega \)

 

(b) From Eq. (7.82),

 

\( A_{ vo }=\frac{v_{ o }}{-v_{ gs }}=\frac{R_{ D }\left(1+\mu_{ g }\right)}{r_{ o 1}+R_{ D }} \) (7.82)

 

\(\begin{aligned}A_{ vo } &=\frac{v_{ o }}{-v_{ gs }}=\frac{R_{ D }\left(1+\mu_{ g }\right)}{r_{ o 1}+R_{ D }} \\&=5 k \times \frac{1+230}{100 k +5 k }=11\end{aligned}\)

 

(c) \( R_{ o }=R_{ D }=5 k \Omega .\)

(d) For the overall voltage gain \( A_{ V } \),

 

\( A_{ v }=\frac{v_{ L }}{v_{ s }}=\frac{A_{ vo } R_{ i } R_{ L }}{\left(R_{ i }+R_{ s }\right)\left(R_{ L }+R_{ o }\right)}=\frac{11 \times 309 \times 10 k }{(309+500) \times(10 k +5 k )}=2.83 \)
