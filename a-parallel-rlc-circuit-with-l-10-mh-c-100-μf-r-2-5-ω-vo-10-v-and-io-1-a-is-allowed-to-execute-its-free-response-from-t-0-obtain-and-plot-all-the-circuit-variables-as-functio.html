Guest Question.

A parallel \(RLC\) circuit with \(L = 10 mH\), \(C = 100 μF\), \(R = 2.5 Ω\), \(V_{o} = 10 V\) and \(I_{o} = -1 A\) is allowed to execute its free-response from \(t = 0.\) Obtain and plot all the circuit variables as functions of time.

Admin
Solution

Undamped natural frequency, \(\omega_{n}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{L C}}=1000 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{sec}\)

Critical resistance \(=\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\frac{L}{C}}=5 \Omega\)

\(\therefore\) Damping factor \(\xi=5 / 2.5=2\)

\(\therefore\) Natural frequencies \(=\left(-\xi \pm \sqrt{\xi^{2}-1}\right) \omega_{n}=-0.268 \times 10^{3} \text { and }-3.732 \times 10^{3}\)

Hence, this circuit is an over-damped one with two real negative natural frequencies corresponding to two time constants of \(3.732 ms\) and \(0.268 ms\). There is no forced response component. Therefore total response for all variables will contain sum of two decaying exponential functions with time constants calculated above.

\(\therefore i_{L}(t)=A_{1} e^{-0.268 \times 10^{3} t}+A_{2} e^{-3.732 \times 10^{3} t}(12-11-1)\)

 

\(A_{1}\) and \(A_{2}\) have to be evaluated from initial conditions. One of the initial conditions – inductor current – can be directly applied on this equation. The initial current given is specified at \(t = 0^{-} \). But since there is no impulse voltage in this circuit, the inductor current can not change instantaneously.

Therefore, the value of inductor current at \(t = 0^{+}\) and at \(t = 0^{-}\) will be the same. Applying this initial condition at \(t = 0^{+}\) on the assumed solution gives one equation on \(A_{1}\) and \(A_{2}.\)

\(A_{1}+A_{2}=I_{o}=-1\)

 

The second initial condition, the value of capacitor voltage at \(t = 0^{-}\), can be employed to obtain the second equation needed to solve for \(A_{1}\) and \(A_{2}\). We recognise that (i) in a parallel \(RLC\) circuit, voltage is the common variable and hence the initial value of voltage across inductor is same as \(V_{o}\) and (ii) the voltage across an inductor is proportional to first derivative of current.

\(\left.\therefore \frac{d i_{L}(t)}{d t}\right|_{0^{+}}=\left.\frac{v_{L}(t)}{L}\right|_{\left(0^{+}\right)}=\left.\frac{v_{C}(t)}{L}\right|_{\left(0^{+}\right)}\)

 

\(\text { But }\left.\frac{v_{C}(t)}{L}\right|_{\left(0^{+}\right)}=\left.\frac{v_{C}(t)}{L}\right|_{\left(0^{-}\right)}\) if there is no impulse current flow in the capacitor at \(t=0\)

\(\left.\therefore \frac{d i_{L}(t)}{d t}\right|_{0^{+}}=\frac{V_{o}}{L} \mathrm{~A} / \mathrm{sec}\)

 

Differentiate the assumed response in Eqn. 12.11-1 and apply this initial condition to get the second equation on \(A_{1}\) and \(A_{2}.\)

 

\(-268 A_{1}-3732 A_{2}=10 / 0.01=1000 \mathrm{~A} / \mathrm{sec}\)

 

Solving for \(A_{1}\) and \(A_{2},\)

\(\begin{array}{l} A_{1}=-0.789 \text { and } A_{2}=-0.211 \\ \therefore i_{L}(t)=-0.789 e^{-268 t}-0.211 e^{-3732 t} \text { A for } t \geq 0^{+} \\ v(t)=L \frac{d i_{L}(t)}{d t}=2.11 e^{-268 t}+7.89 e^{-3732 t} \mathrm{~V} \text { for } t \geq 0^{+} \\ i_{C}(t)=C \frac{d v(t)}{d t}=-0.0565 e^{-268 t}-2.945 e^{-3732 t} \text { A for } t \geq 0 \\ i_{R}(t)=\frac{v(t)}{R}=0.846 e^{-268 t}+3.156 e^{-3732 t} \text { A for } t \geq 0^{+} \end{array}\)

 

The sum of the three currents should be zero and it is verified.

Figure 12.11-2 shows the three currents and the common voltage waveforms. The two exponential functions that make the voltage waveform are also shown. In all cases, the exponential with lower time constant \((0.268 ms)\) dominates the initial behaviour, whereas the exponential with the higher time constant \((3.732 ms)\) affects the behaviour after 1ms.
