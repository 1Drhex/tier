Guest Question.

Although the Schrödinger equation for helium itself cannot be solved exactly, there exist “helium-like” systems that do admit exact solutions. A simple example is “rubber-band helium,” in which the Coulomb forces are replaced by Hooke’s law forces:

\( H=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m}\left(\nabla_{1}^{2}+\nabla_{2}^{2}\right)+\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2}\left(r_{1}^{2}+r_{2}^{2}\right)-\frac{\lambda}{4} m \omega^{2}\left| r _{1}- r _{2}\right|^{2} \) (8.78).

(a) Show that the change of variables from \( r _{1}, r _{2}, \text { to } \)

\( u \equiv \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left( r _{1}+ r _{2}\right), \quad v \equiv \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left( r _{1}- r _{2}\right) \) (8.79).

turns the Hamiltonian into two independent three-dimensional harmonic oscillators:

\( H=\left[-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \nabla_{u}^{2}+\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} u^{2}\right]+\left[-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \nabla_{v}^{2}+\frac{1}{2}(1-\lambda) m \omega^{2} v^{2}\right] \) (8.80).

(b) What is the exact ground state energy for this system?

(c) If we didn’t know the exact solution, we might be inclined to apply the method of Section 8.2 to the Hamiltonian in its original form (Equation 8.78). Do so (but don’t bother with shielding). How does your result compare with the exact answer? Answer: \( \langle H\rangle=3 \hbar \omega(1-\lambda / 4) \).

Admin
Solution

(a)

\( r _{1}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}( u + v ) ; r _{2}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}( u – v ) ; r_{1}^{2}+r_{2}^{2}=\frac{1}{2}\left(u^{2}+2 u \cdot v +v^{2}+u^{2}-2 u \cdot v +v^{2}\right)=u^{2}+v^{2} \).

\( \left(\nabla_{1}^{2}+\nabla_{2}^{2}\right) f\left( r _{1}, r _{2}\right)=\left(\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial x_{1}^{2}}+\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial y_{1}^{2}}+\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial z_{1}^{2}}+\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial x_{2}^{2}}+\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial y_{2}^{2}}+\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial z_{2}^{2}}\right) \).

\( \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_{1}}=\frac{\partial f}{\partial u_{x}} \frac{\partial u_{x}}{\partial x_{1}}+\frac{\partial f}{\partial v_{x}} \frac{\partial v_{x}}{\partial x_{1}}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\frac{\partial f}{\partial u_{x}}+\frac{\partial f}{\partial v_{x}}\right) ; \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_{2}}=\frac{\partial f}{\partial u_{x}} \frac{\partial u_{x}}{\partial x_{2}}+\frac{\partial f}{\partial v_{x}} \frac{\partial v_{x}}{\partial x_{2}}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\frac{\partial f}{\partial u_{x}}-\frac{\partial f}{\partial v_{x}}\right) \).

\( \frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial x_{1}^{2}}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \frac{\partial}{\partial x_{1}}\left(\frac{\partial f}{\partial u_{x}}+\frac{\partial f}{\partial v_{x}}\right)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial u_{x}^{2}} \frac{\partial u_{x}}{\partial x_{1}}+\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial u_{x} \partial v_{x}} \frac{\partial v_{x}}{\partial x_{1}}+\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial v_{x} \partial u_{x}} \frac{\partial u_{x}}{\partial x_{1}}+\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial v_{x}^{2}} \frac{\partial v_{x}}{\partial x_{1}}\right) \).

\( =\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial u_{x}^{2}}+2 \frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial u_{x} \partial v_{x}}+\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial v_{x}^{2}}\right) \).

\( \frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial x_{2}^{2}}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \frac{\partial}{\partial x_{2}}\left(\frac{\partial f}{\partial u_{x}}-\frac{\partial f}{\partial v_{x}}\right)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial u_{x}^{2}} \frac{\partial u_{x}}{\partial x_{2}}+\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial u_{x} \partial v_{x}} \frac{\partial v_{x}}{\partial x_{2}}-\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial v_{x} \partial u_{x}} \frac{\partial u_{x}}{\partial x_{2}}-\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial v_{x}^{2}} \frac{\partial v_{x}}{\partial x_{2}}\right) \).

\( =\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial u_{x}^{2}}-2 \frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial u_{x} \partial v_{x}}+\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial v_{x}^{2}}\right) \).

\( \text { So }\left(\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial x_{1}^{2}}+\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial x_{2}^{2}}\right)=\left(\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial u_{x}^{2}}+\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial v_{x}^{2}}\right), \text { and likewise for } y \text { and } z: \quad \nabla_{1}^{2}+\nabla_{2}^{2}=\nabla_{u}^{2}+\nabla_{v}^{2} \).

\( H=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m}\left(\nabla_{u}^{2}+\nabla_{v}^{2}\right)+\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2}\left(u^{2}+v^{2}\right)-\frac{\lambda}{4} m \omega^{2} 2 v^{2} \).

\( =\left[-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \nabla_{u}^{2}+\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} u^{2}\right]+\left[-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \nabla_{v}^{2}+\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} v^{2}-\frac{1}{2} \lambda m \omega^{2} v^{2}\right] \). QED

(b) The energy is \( \frac{3}{2} \hbar \omega \text { (for the } u \text { part) and } \frac{3}{2} \hbar \omega \sqrt{1-\lambda} \text { (for the } v \text { part): } E_{ gs }=\frac{3}{2} \hbar \omega(1+\sqrt{1-\lambda}) \text {. } \)

(c) The ground state for a one-dimensional oscillator is

\( \psi_{0}(x)=\left(\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}\right)^{1 / 4} e^{-m \omega x^{2} / 2 \hbar} \) (Eq. 2.60).

So, for a 3-D oscillator, the ground state is \( \psi_{0}( r )=\left(\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}\right)^{3 / 4} e^{-m \omega r^{2} / 2 \hbar} \) , and for two particles

\( \psi\left( r _{ 1 }, r _{ 2 }\right)=\left(\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}\right)^{3 / 2} e^{-\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar}\left(r_{1}^{2}+r_{2}^{2}\right)} \) (This is the analog to Eq. 8.18.)

\( \psi_{0}\left( r _{1}, r _{2}\right) \equiv \psi_{100}\left( r _{1}\right) \psi_{100}\left( r _{2}\right)=\frac{8}{\pi a^{3}} e^{-2\left(r_{1}+r_{2}\right) / a} \) (8.18).

\( \langle H\rangle=\frac{3}{2} \hbar \omega+\frac{3}{2} \hbar \omega+\left\langle V_{e e}\right\rangle=3 \hbar \omega+\left\langle V_{e e}\right\rangle \) (the analog to Eq. 8.20).

\( \langle H\rangle=8 E_{1}+\left\langle V_{e e}\right\rangle \) (8.20).

\( \left\langle V_{e e}\right\rangle=-\frac{\lambda}{4} m \omega^{2}\left(\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}\right)^{3} \int e^{-\frac{m \omega}{\hbar}\left(r_{1}^{2}+r_{2}^{2}\right)} \underbrace{\left( r _{1}- r _{2}\right)^{2}}_{r_{1}^{2}-2 r _{1} \cdot r _{2}+r_{2}^{2}} d^{3} r _{1} d^{3} r _{2} \) (the analog to Eq. 8.21).

\( \left\langle V_{e e}\right\rangle=\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)\left(\frac{8}{\pi a^{3}}\right)^{2} \int \frac{e^{-4\left(r_{1}+r_{2}\right) / a}}{\left| r _{1}- r _{2}\right|} d^{3} r _{1} d^{3} r _{2} \) (8.21).

\( \text { The } r_{1} \cdot r_{2} \text { term integrates to zero, by symmetry, and the } r_{2}^{2} \text { term is the same as the } r_{1}^{2} \text { term, so } \)

\( \left\langle V_{e e}\right\rangle=-\frac{\lambda}{4} m \omega^{2}\left(\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}\right)^{3} 2 \int e^{-\frac{m \omega}{\hbar}\left(r_{1}^{2}+r_{2}^{2}\right)} r_{1}^{2} d^{3} r _{1} d^{3} r _{2} \).

\( =-\frac{\lambda}{2} m \omega^{2}\left(\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}\right)^{3}(4 \pi)^{2} \int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-m \omega r_{2}^{2} / \hbar} r_{2}^{2} d r_{2} \int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-m \omega r_{1}^{2} / \hbar} r_{1}^{4} d r_{1} \).

\( =-\lambda \frac{8 m^{4} \omega^{5}}{\pi \hbar^{3}}\left[\frac{1}{4} \frac{\hbar}{m \omega} \sqrt{\frac{\pi \hbar}{m \omega}}\right]\left[\frac{3}{8}\left(\frac{\hbar}{m \omega}\right)^{2} \sqrt{\frac{\pi \hbar}{m \omega}}\right]=-\frac{3}{4} \lambda \hbar \omega \).

\( \langle H\rangle=3 \hbar \omega-\frac{3}{4} \lambda \hbar \omega=3 \hbar \omega\left(1-\frac{\lambda}{4}\right) \).

The variational principle says this must exceed the exact ground-state energy (b); let’s check it:

\( 3 \hbar \omega\left(1-\frac{\lambda}{4}\right)>\frac{3}{2} \hbar \omega(1+\sqrt{1-\lambda}) \Leftrightarrow 2-\frac{\lambda}{2}>1+\sqrt{1-\lambda} \Leftrightarrow 1-\frac{\lambda}{2}>\sqrt{1-\lambda} \Leftrightarrow 1-\lambda+\frac{\lambda^{2}}{4}>1-\lambda \).

It checks. In fact, expanding the exact answer in powers of \( \lambda, \quad E_{ gs } \approx \frac{3}{2} \hbar \omega\left(1+1-\frac{1}{2} \lambda\right)=3 \hbar \omega\left(1-\frac{\lambda}{4}\right) \), we recover the variational result.
