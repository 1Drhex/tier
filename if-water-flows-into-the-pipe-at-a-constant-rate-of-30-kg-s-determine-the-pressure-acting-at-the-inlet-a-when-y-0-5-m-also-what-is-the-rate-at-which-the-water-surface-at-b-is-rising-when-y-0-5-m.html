Guest Question.

If water flows into the pipe at a constant rate of 30 kg/s, determine the pressure acting at the inlet A when y = 0.5 m. Also, what is the rate at which the water surface at B is rising when y = 0.5 m? The container is circular

Admin
Solution

The volume of the control volume changes with time. Since it contains the water in the container. Referring to the geometry shown in Fig. a, the volume is

\(\forall = 2\pi \sum yA = 2\pi {(0.075m)(0.15)(y)+(0.15m+\frac{y}{3}\tan 30^{\circ})[\frac{1}{2}(y\tan 30^{\circ})]} \)

 

\( = 2\pi (0.05556y^{3} + 0.075 tan 30° y^{2} + 0.01125y) m^{3} \)

Then

 

\(\frac{\partial \forall }{\partial t} =2\pi (0.1667y^{2} \frac{\partial y}{\partial t} + 0.15\tan 30^{\circ}y\frac{\partial \partial y}{\partial t}+0.01125\frac{\partial y}{\partial t}) \)

 

\(= 2\pi (0.1667y^{2} + 0.15 tan 30° y+ 0.01125)\frac{\partial y}{\partial t} \)

Thus

\(\frac{\partial }{\partial t}\int_{cs} \rho _{w}d\forall +\int _{cs} \rho_{w}V\cdot dA = 0 \)

 

\(\rho _{w}\frac{\partial \forall }{\partial t} – \rho _{w}V_{A} A_{A} = 0 \) \((1000 kg)[2\pi (0.1667y^{2} + 0.15 tan 30°y + 0.01125) ] \frac{\partial y}{\partial t}-30kg/s = 0 \)

 

\(\frac{\partial y}{\partial t} = \frac{3}{200\pi (0.1667y^{2} + 0.15\tan 30^{\circ} y) + 0.01125)} \)

At the instant y = 0.5 m ,

\(V_{B} = \frac{\partial y}{\partial t} = \frac{3}{200\pi [0.1667(0.5^{2}) + 0.15\tan 30^{\circ}(0.5)+0.01125]} \)

 

\(= 0.04962 m/s = 0.0496 m/s .\)

Since water can be considered an ideal fluid (incompressible and inviscid) and the flow is steady, Bernoulli’s equation is applicable writing this equation between points A and B on the central streamline,

 

\(\frac{P_{A}}{\rho _{w}} + \frac{V^{2}_{A}}{2} + gz_{A} = \frac{P_ {B}}{\rho _{w}} +\frac{V^{2} _{B}}{2} + gz_{B} \)

Here,\( P_{B} = P_{atm} = 0 \)since point B is exposed to the atmosphere. With reference to

the datum set through point A,\( z_{A} = 0 and z_{A} = 0.5 m\). Also, from the mass flow rate

\(m = \rho _{w}V_{A}A_{A}; 30 kg/s = (1000 kg/m^{3})(V_{A}) [\pi (0.05 m)^{2}] \)

 

Thus

\(\frac{P_{A}}{1000kg/m^{3}} + \frac{(3.8197 m/s)^{2}}{2} + 0 = 0 + \frac{(0.04962 m/s)^{2}}{2} + (9.81 m/s^{2})(0.5 m) \)

 

\(P_{A} = -2.389(10^{3}) Pa = -2.39 kPa . \)

Note: Since \(V_{B} is very small, then the term \frac{V^{2}_{B}}{2} \)

 

can be neglected.

 

\(\frac{P_{A}}{1000 kg/m^{3}} + \frac{(3.8197 m/s)^{2}}{2} + 0 = 0 + 0 + (9.81 m/s^{2})(0.5 m) \) \(P_{A} = -2.390(10^{3} Pa = -2.39 kPa\)
