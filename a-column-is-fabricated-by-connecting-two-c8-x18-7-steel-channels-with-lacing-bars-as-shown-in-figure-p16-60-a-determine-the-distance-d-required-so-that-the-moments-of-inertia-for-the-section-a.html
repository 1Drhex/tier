Guest Question.

A column is fabricated by connecting two C8 ×18.7 steel channels with lacing bars as shown in Figure P16.60. (a) Determine the distance d required so that the moments of inertia for the section about the two principal axes are equal. (b) For a column with an effective length of KL = 32 ft, determine the allowable axial load \(P_{\text {allow }}\) that may be supported by the column using the value of d determined in part (a). Use the AISC equations and assume E = 29,000 ksi and \( \sigma_{Y}\) = 36 ksi.

Admin
Solution

The following section properties for a standard steel C8 ×18.7 shape are given in Appendix B:

\(A=5.51 \text { in. }^{2}, I_{x}=43.9 \text { in. }^{4}, r_{x}=2.82 \text { in., } I_{y}=1.97 \text { in. }^{4}, \bar{x}=0.565 \text { in. }\)

(a) Determine distance d:

\(\begin{aligned}&I_{x}=2\left(43.9 in. ^{4}\right)=87.8 in .^{4} \\&I_{y}=2\left[1.97 in. ^{4}+(d / 2+0.565 \text { in. })^{2}\left(5.51 in .^{2}\right)\right]\end{aligned}\)

Equate these two moment of inertia expressions and solve for d:

\(d=4.387 in . = 4 .39 in.\)

(b) Determine the allowable axial load:
Limiting slenderness ratio

\(4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{Y}}}=4.71 \sqrt{\frac{29,000 ksi }{36 ksi }}=133.681\)

Slenderness ratio:

\(r=\sqrt{\frac{87.8 in .{ }^{4}}{2\left(5.51 in .^{2}\right)}}=2.823 in . \quad \therefore \frac{K L}{r}=\frac{(32 ft )(12 in . / ft )}{2.823 in .}=136.043\)

Elastic critical buckling stress

\(\sigma_{e}=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{\left(\frac{K L}{r}\right)^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(29,000 ksi )}{(136.043)^{2}}=15.465 ksi\)

Critical and allowable buckling stresses

Since \(\frac{K L}{r} \leq 4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{y}}}\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{c r}=\left[0.658^{\left(\frac{\sigma_{y}}{\sigma_{e}}\right)}\right] \sigma_{Y}=\left[0.658^{\left(\frac{36 ksi }{15.465 ksi }\right)}\right](36 ksi )=13.563 ksi \\&\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\sigma_{c r}}{1.67}=\frac{13.563 ksi }{1.67}=8.121 ksi\end{aligned}\)

Allowable axial load:

\(P_{\text {allow }}=\sigma_{\text {allow }} A=(8.121 ksi )\left(2 \times 5.51 in .^{2}\right)=89.5 kips\)
