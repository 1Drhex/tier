An uninsulated piston/cylinder contains propene, \(C _{3} H _{6}\) , at ambient temperature, 19째C, with a quality of 50% and a volume of 10 L. The propene now expands very slowly until the pressure in the cylinder drops to 460 kPa. Calculate the mass of propene, the work, and heat transfer for this process.

Admin
Solution

Propene \(C _{3} H _{6}: \quad T _{1}=19^{\circ} C =292.2 K , \quad x _{1}=0.50, \quad V _{1}=10 L\)

From Fig. D.1: \(T _{ r1 }=292.2 / 364.9=0.80\) ,

\(P _{ r1 }= P _{ r \text { sat }}=0.25, \quad P _{1}=0.25 \times 4.6=1.15 MPa\)

From D.1: \(Z _{1}=0.5 \times 0.04+0.5 \times 0.805=0.4225\)

\(m =\frac{ P _{1} V _{1}}{ Z _{1} RT _{1}}=\frac{1150 \times 0.010}{0.4225 \times 0.19758 \times 292.2}= 0 . 4 7 1 kg\)

Assume reversible and isothermal process (slow, no friction, not insulated)

\({ }_{1} Q _{2}= m \left( u _{2}- u _{1}\right)+{ }_{1} W _{2}\)

\({ }_{1}W _{2}=\int_{1}^{2} PdV\) (cannot integrate); \({ }_{1} Q _{2}=\int_{1}^{2} TdS = Tm \left( s _{2}- s _{1}\right)\)

From Figs. D.2 and D.3:

\(\begin{aligned}& h _{1}^{*}- h _{1}=0.19758 \times 364.9(0.5 \times 4.51+0.5 \times 0.46)=179.2 kJ / kg \\&\left( s _{1}^{*}- s _{1}\right)=0.19758(0.5 \times 5.46+0.5 \times 0.39)=0.5779 kJ / kg K\end{aligned}\)

The ideal gas change in h and s are

\(\left( h _{2}^{*}- h _{1}^{*}\right)=0 \text { and } \quad\left( s _{2}^{*}- s _{1}^{*}\right)=0-0.19758 \ln \frac{460}{1161}=+0.1829 kJ / kg K\)

At \(T _{ r 2}=0.80, P _{ r 2}=0.10\) , from D.1, D.2 and D.3, \(Z_{2}=0.93\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\left( h _{2}^{*}- h _{2}\right)=0.19758 \times 364.9 \times 0.16=11.5 kJ / kg \\&\left( s _{2}^{*}- s _{2}\right)=0.19758 \times 0.13=0.0257 kJ / kg K\end{aligned}\)

Now we can do the change in s and h from state 1 to state 2

\(\begin{aligned}\left( s _{2}- s _{1}\right) &=-\left( s _{2}^{*}- s _{2}\right)+\left( s _{2}^{*}- s _{1}^{*}\right)+\left( s _{1}^{*}- s _{1}\right) \\&=-0.0257+0.1829+0.5779=0.7351 kJ / kg K\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\left( h _{2}- h _{1}\right) &=-\left( h _{2}^{*}- h _{2}\right)+\left( h _{2}^{*}- h _{1}^{*}\right)+ h _{1}^{*}- h _{1} \\=&-11.5+0+179.2=167.7 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

 

The heat transfer is found from the second law

\({ }_{1} q _{2}=292.2 \times 0.7351=214.8 kJ / kg \quad \Rightarrow \quad{ }_{1} Q _{2}= m {}_{1} q _{2}= 1 0 1 . 2 kJ\)

We need the internal energy in the energy equation

\(\begin{aligned}u _{2}- u _{1} &=\left( h _{2}- h _{1}\right)+ RT \left( Z _{1}- Z _{2}\right)=167.7+0.19758 \times 292.2(0.4225-0.93) \\&=138.4 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&{ }_{1} w _{2}={ }_{1} q _{2}-\left( u _{2}- u _{1}\right)=214.8-138.4=76.4 kJ / kg \\&{ }_{1} W _{2}= m {}_{1} w _{2}= 3 6 . 0 ~ k J\end{aligned}\)
