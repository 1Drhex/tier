Guest Question.

A safety valve, 50 mm in diameter, is to blow off at a pressure of 1.5 MPa. It is held on its seat by means of a helical compression spring, with an initial compression of 25 mm. The maximum
lift of the valve is 10 mm. The spring index can be taken as 6. The spring is made of patented and cold-drawn steel wire with ultimate tensile strength of 1500 N/mm² and modulus of rigidity of 81 370 N/mm². The permissible shear stress for the spring wire should be taken as 30% of the ultimate tensile strength. Design the spring and calculate:
(i) wire diameter;
(ii) mean coil diameter;
(iii) number of active turns;
(iv) total number of turns;
(v) solid length;
(vi) free length; and
(vii) pitch of the coil.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } \quad C=6 \quad S_{u t}=1500 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( G=81370 N / mm ^{2} \quad \tau=0.3 S_{u t} \).

Step I Wire diameter
Let \( P_{1} \text { and } \delta_{1} \) denote the initial spring force and deflection respectively when the valve just begins to blow off.

\( P_{1}=\frac{\pi}{4}(50)^{2}(1.5)=2945.24 N \).

Also,

\( P \propto \delta \),

Therefore,

\( \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}=\frac{\delta_{2}}{\delta_{1}} \quad \text { or } \quad \frac{P_{2}}{(2945.24)}=\frac{35}{25} \).

\( P_{2}=4123.34 N \text { (maximum force) } \).

The permissible shear stress for the spring wire is given by,

\( \tau=0.3 S_{u t}=0.3(1500)=450 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( K=\frac{4 C-1}{4 C-4}+\frac{0.615}{C}=\frac{4(6)-1}{4(6)-4}+\frac{0.615}{6} \).

= 1.2525.

From Eq. (10.13),

\( \tau=K\left(\frac{8 P C}{\pi d^{2}}\right) \) (10.13).

\( \tau=K\left(\frac{8 P C}{\pi d^{2}}\right) \text { or } 450=1.2525\left\{\frac{8(4123.34)(6)}{\pi d^{2}}\right\} \).

∴ d = 13.24 or 14 mm (i)
Step II Mean coil diameter
D = Cd = 6(14) = 84 mm (ii)
Step III Number of active turns
From Eq. (10.8),

\( \delta=\frac{8 P D^{3} N}{G d^{4}} \) (10.8)

\( \delta=\frac{8 P D^{3} N}{G d^{4}} \),

\( \text { Substituting values of } P_{1} \text { and } \delta_{1} \text {, } \)

\( 25=\frac{8(2945.24)(84)^{3} N}{(81370)(14)^{4}} \).

∴ N = 5.6 or 6 turns (iii)
Step IV Total number of turns
It is assumed that the spring has square and ground ends. The number of inactive coils is 2. Therefore,

\( N_{t}=N+2=6+2=8 \text { turns } \) (iv)
Step V Solid length of spring

\( \text { Solid length }=N_{t} d=8(14)=112 mm \) (v).

Step VI Free length of spring
The maximum deflection of the spring under the force of 4123.34 N is given by,

\( \delta=\frac{8 P D^{3} N}{G d^{4}}=\frac{8(4123.34)(84)^{3}(6)}{(81370)(14)^{4}}=37.53 mm \).

It is assumed that there will be a gap of 2 mm between the adjacent turns, when the spring is subjected to the maximum compression. This gap is essential to avoid clashing of the coils. The total number of turns is 8. Therefore, the total axial gap will be (8 – 1) × 2 = 14 mm.

\( \text { Free length }=\text { solid length }+\text { total axial gap }+\delta \).

= 112 + 14 + 37.53 = 163.53 mm
or free length = 165 mm (vi)
Step VII Pitch of coils

\( \text { Pitch of coil }=\frac{\text { free length }}{\left(N_{t}-1\right)}=\frac{165}{(8-1)}=23.57 mm \) (vii).
