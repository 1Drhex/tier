Guest Question.

An oil-operated hydraulic compensating valve is shown in Fig. 10.23. Initially when \( \left(p_{1}=p_{2}\right) \) there is a pre-load of 100 N in the spring. The plunger moves through 10 mm when,

\( p_{1}=0.35 N / mm ^{2} \),

\( p_{2}=0.25 N / mm ^{2} \)

This is the normal operating condition of the valve. The spring is to be fitted in a cylindrical space of 25 mm diameter. There is no restriction on the free length of the spring. The permissible shear stress in the spring wire is recommended as 30% of the ultimate tensile strength. Design the spring from static considerations and determine the factor of safety against fluctuating stresses. Neglect the effect of inertia forces.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } \quad D_{\max .}=25 mm \quad \tau=0.3 S_{u t} \).

Step I Maximum spring force
The spring is subjected to fluctuating stresses.
Therefore, an oil-hardened and tempered valve spring wire of Grade-VW is selected for this application. The spring index for such applications varies from 6 to 8. Since there is restriction on the maximum coil diameter, a low value of 5 is selected as the spring index.
The spring force is minimum when the plunger does not move through 10 mm.

\( P_{\min }=\text { initial pre-load }=100 N\).

The spring force is maximum when the plunger moves through 10 mm. The maximum spring force consists of initial pre-load and additional spring forces.

\( P_{\max .}=P_{\min .}+\text { additional force }\).

\( =100+\frac{\pi}{4}(40)^{2}(0.35-0.25) \).

= 100 + 125.66 = 225.66 N
Step II Design against static load
When the spring force varies from 100 N to 225.66 N, the spring is compressed by 10 mm.

\( k=\frac{225.66-100}{10}=12.57 N / mm \).

From Eq. (10.7) and (10.5),

\( K=\frac{4 C-1}{4 C-4}+\frac{0.615}{C} \) (10.7).

\( K_{s}=\left(1+\frac{0.5}{C}\right) \) (10.5).

\( K=\frac{4 C-1}{4 C-4}+\frac{0.615}{C}=\frac{4(5)-1}{4(5)-4}+\frac{0.615}{5} \).

= 1.3105.

\( K_{s}=1+\frac{0.5}{C}=1+\frac{0.5}{5}=1.1 \).

From Eq. (10.13),

\( \tau=K\left(\frac{8 P C}{\pi d^{2}}\right) \) (10.13).

\( \tau=K\left(\frac{8 P C}{\pi d^{2}}\right) \quad \text { or } \quad \tau=(1.3105)\left[\frac{8(225.66)(5)}{\pi d^{2}}\right] \).

or \( \tau=\frac{3765.32}{d^{2}} N / mm ^{2} \) (a).

The permissible shear stress is denoted by \( \tau_{d} \) in order to differentiate it from induced stress τ. It is given by,

\( \tau_{d}=0.3 S_{u t} \). (b)
Equations (a) and (b) are solved by the trial and error method.
Trial 1
d = 2.5 mm.

\( \tau=\frac{3765.32}{d^{2}}=\frac{3765.32}{(2.5)^{2}}=602.45 N / mm ^{2} \).

From Table 10.2,

\( S_{u t}=1470 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \tau_{d}=0.3 S_{u t}=0.3(1470)=441 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \text { Therefore, } \tau>\tau_{d} \).

The design is not safe.
Trial 2
d = 3 mm

\( \tau=\frac{3765.32}{d^{2}}=\frac{3765.32}{(3)^{2}}=418.37 N / mm ^{2} \).

From Table 10.2,

\( S_{u t}=1430 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \tau_{d}=0.3 S_{u t}=0.3(1430)=429 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \text { Therefore, } \tau<\tau_{d} \).

The design is satisfactory and the wire diameter should be 3 mm. However, the spring is subjected to fluctuating stresses and to account for these stresses, the wire diameter is increased to 3.6 mm.
d = 3.6 mm
D = Cd = 5(3.6) = 18 mm.

\( D_{\max .}=D+d=18+3.6=21.6 mm \).

The outer diameter of the spring is 21.6 mm, leaving sufficient margin for diametral expansion.
The spring can be easily fitted in a cylindrical space of 25 mm diameter.
From Eq. (10.9),

\( k=\frac{G d^{4}}{8 D^{3} N} \) (10.9).

\( N=\frac{G d^{4}}{8 D^{3} k}=\frac{81370(3.6)^{4}}{8(18)^{3}(12.57)}=23.3 \text { or } 24 \text { coils } \).

It is assumed that the spring has square and ground ends.

\( N_{t}=N+2=24+2=26 \text { coils } \).

From Eq. (10.8),

\( M_{t}=\frac{\mu P}{4}(D+d) \) (11.8).

\( \delta=\frac{8 P D^{3} N}{G d^{4}}=\frac{8(225.66)(18)^{3}(24)}{(81370)(3.6)^{4}}=18.49 mm \).

\( \text { Solid length of spring }=N_{t} d=26(3.6) \).

= 93.6 mm
It is assumed that there will be a gap of 0.5 mm between consecutive coils when the spring is subjected to the maximum force. The total number of coils is 26. The total axial gap between the coils will be (26 – 1) × 0.5 = 12.5 mm.

\( \text { Free length }=\text { solid length }+\text { total axial gap }+\delta \).

Free length = 93.6 + 12.5 + 18.49
= 124.59 or 125 mm
Step III Factor of safety against fluctuating stresses

\( P_{m}=\frac{1}{2}\left(P_{\max }+P_{\min .}\right)=\frac{1}{2}(225.66+100) \).

= 162.83

\( P_{a}=\frac{1}{2}\left(P_{\max .}-P_{\min .}\right)=\frac{1}{2}(225.66-100) \).

= 62.83.

From Eq. (10.18) and (10.19),

\( \tau_{m}=K_{s}\left(\frac{8 P_{m} D}{\pi d^{3}}\right) \) (10.18).

\( \tau_{a}=K\left(\frac{8 P_{a} D}{\pi d^{3}}\right) \) (10.19).

\( \tau_{m}=K_{s}\left(\frac{8 P_{m} D}{\pi d^{3}}\right)=(1.1)\left(\frac{8(162.83)(18)}{\pi(3.6)^{3}}\right) \).

\( =175.97 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \tau_{a}=K\left(\frac{8 P_{a} D}{\pi d^{3}}\right)=(1.3105)\left(\frac{8(62.83)(18)}{\pi(3.6)^{3}}\right) \).

\( =80.89 N / mm ^{2} \).

From Table No. 10.2, (d = 3.6 mm)

\( S_{u t}=1400 N / mm ^{2} \).

From Eq. (10.21), the relationships for oil-hardened and tempered steel wire are as follows:

\( S_{s y}=0.45 S_{u t} \) (10.21).

\( S_{s e}^{\prime}=0.22 S_{u t}=0.22(1400)=308 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( S_{s y}=0.45 S_{u t}=0.45(1400)=630 N / mm ^{2} \).

From Eq. (10.22),

\( \frac{\tau_{a}}{\frac{S_{s y}}{\left(f_{s}\right)}-\tau_{m}}=\frac{\frac{1}{2} S_{s e}^{\prime}}{S_{s y}-\frac{1}{2} S_{s e}^{\prime}} \) (10.22).

\( \frac{\tau_{a}}{\left(\frac{S_{s y}}{f_{s}}\right)-\tau_{m}}=\frac{\frac{1}{2} S_{s e}^{\prime}}{S_{s y}-\frac{1}{2} S_{s e}^{\prime}} \).

\( \text { or } \frac{80.89}{\left(\frac{630}{f s}\right)-175.97}=\frac{\frac{1}{2}(308)}{630-\frac{1}{2}(308)} \).

\( \frac{80.89}{\left(\frac{630}{f s}\right)-175.97}=\frac{154}{476} \).

\( \frac{630}{\left(f_{s}\right)}-175.97=\frac{80.89(476)}{154}=250.02 \),

(fs) = 1.48.

In Section 10.8 on ‘Design of Helical Springs’, it was explained that the factor of safety in spring design is usually 1.5 or less on account of four factors. Therefore, the factor of safety of 1.48 in the above application is reasonable.
Step IV Spring specifications
(i) Material = oil-hardened and tempered steel wire of Grade-VW
(ii) Wire diameter = 3.6 mm
(iii) Mean coil diameter = 18 mm
(iv) Free length = 125 mm
(v) Total number of turns = 26
(vi) Style of ends = square and ground.
