Guest Question.

A load PC is acting on the horizontal link 2 at point C of a simple truss, as shown in Fig. 11.36. Link 2 is pinned to the ground at point O2 and to the inclined link 3 at point B, whereas link 3 is pinned to the ground at point O3. The lengths are \(R_{C O_{2}}=5 ft , R_{B O_{2}}=4 ft, \text { and } R_{B O_{3}}=5 ft \text {. }\) The two links each have a solid circular cross section with a diameter of D = 2 in. Also, the two links are made from a steel alloy with a compressive yield strength Syc = 60 kpsi, a tensile yield strength Syt = 50 kpsi, and a modulus of elasticity E = 30 Mpsi.

Part 1: Using the theoretic value for the end-condition constants for the two links, determine: (a) the value of the slenderness ratio at the point of tangency between Euler’s column formula and Johnson’s parabolic formula for link 3; (b) the critical load and the critical unit load acting on link 3; and (c) the maximum load PC for the factor of safety to guard against buckling of link 3 to be N = 1.
Part 2: If the load acting at point C is specified as PC = 24 000 lb, determine: (a) the factor of safety for link 2; (b) the factor of safety for link 3; and (c) which link is most likely to fail first. Briefly explain the answer.

Admin
Solution

Part 1.

(a) Links 2 and 3 are in static equilibrium. The free-body diagram of link 2 is shown in Fig. 11.37. Note that the link is in tension due to the external load PC.

Taking moments about the pin O2 gives the y component of the load at pin B as

\(F_{32}^{y}=\frac{5 ft }{4 ft } P_{C}^{y} \quad \text { or } \quad F_{23}^{y}=-\frac{5}{4} P_{C}^{y}\)

The free-body diagram of link 3 is as shown in Fig. 11.38.
Taking moments about the pin O3 gives

\(F_{23}^{x}=-\frac{4 ft }{3 ft } F_{23}^{y}=-\frac{4}{3}\left(-\frac{5}{4} P_{C}^{y}\right)=\frac{5}{3} P_{C}^{y}\)

The magnitude of the force at pin B can be written as

\(F_{23}=\sqrt{\left(\frac{5}{3} P_{C}^{y}\right)^{2}+\left(-\frac{5}{4} P_{C}^{y}\right)^{2}}=5 P_{C}^{y} \sqrt{\frac{1}{9}+\frac{1}{16}}=5 P_{C}^{y} \sqrt{\frac{16+9}{144}}=\frac{25}{12} P_{C}^{y}\) (1)

The direction of this force at pin B, that is, the line of action, is

\(\angle F_{23}=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{F_{23}^{y}}{F_{23}^{x}}\right)=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{-5 / 4 P_{C}^{y}}{5 / 3 P_{C}^{y}}\right)=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{-3}{4}\right)=-36.87^{\circ}\)

Since link 3 is a two-force member, forces F23 and F13 must be equal, opposite, and collinear; that is, F23 = F13, and link 3 is in compression because of the internal load PB (Fig. 11.39). Therefore, it is possible that link 3 could fail because of buckling.

We must check link 3 for the factor of safety to guard against buckling to be N = 1. The cross-sectional area of link 3 is

\(A=\frac{\pi}{4} D^{2}=\frac{\pi}{4}(2 in )^{2}=3.1416 in ^{2}\)

The second moment of area of link 3 can be written as

\(I=\frac{\pi}{64} D^{4}=\frac{\pi}{64}(2 in )^{4}=0.7854 in ^{4}\).

The radius of gyration of link 3 is

\(k=\sqrt{\frac{I}{A}}=\sqrt{\frac{0.7854 in ^{4}}{3.1416 in ^{2}}}=0.500 in\)

The slenderness ratio of link 3 is

\(S_{ r }=\frac{L}{k}=\frac{5 ft \cdot 12 in / ft }{0.500 in }=120\) (2)

From the pinned-pinned end conditions, the end-condition constant for link 3 is C = 1.0. Therefore, the slenderness ratio of link 3 at the point of tangency is

\(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{D}=\pi \sqrt{\frac{2 C E}{S_{y c}}}=\pi \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot 1.0\left(30 \times 10^{6} psi \right)}{60 \times 10^{3} psi }}=99.35.\) (3)

(b) To determine the critical load on link 3, we must first determine whether this link is an Euler column or a Johnson column. The criterion for using Johnson’s parabolic equation is \(S_{ r }<\left(S_{ r }\right)_{D}.\) From Eqs. (2) and (3), we have 120 > 99.35; that is, \(S_{ r }<\left(S_{ r }\right)_{D}.\) Therefore, link 3 is an Euler column. The critical load on link 3 is

\(P_{ cr }=A\left[\frac{C \pi^{2} E}{S_{ r }^{2}}\right]=\left(3.1416 in ^{2}\right)\left[\frac{1.0 \pi^{2}\left(30 \times 10^{6} psi \right)}{120^{2}}\right]=64596 lb \text {. }\) (4)

The critical unit load is

\(\frac{P_{ cr }}{A}=\frac{64596 lb }{3.1416 in ^{2}}=20562 lb / in ^{2}\)

(c) If we identify \(P_B=F_{23}\) as the compressive load at point B on link 3 (Fig.11.40), then, from the given factor of safety of N=1 guarding against buckling for link 3, the applied load at pin B on link 3 can be written as

\(P_B=\frac{P_{cr}}{N} = \frac{P_{cr}}{1} = 64 596 lb.\) (5)

Therefore, load PB(= F23) is equal to the critical load Pcr, as shown in Fig. 11.40. Note that a compressive load PB = 64 596 lb is the limiting load on link 3 to avoid buckling, with a factor of safety of N = 1.

Rearranging Eq. (1), the vertical component of the load at point C is

\(P_{C}^{y}=\frac{12}{25} F_{23}=\frac{12}{25} P_{B}\) (6)

Substituting Eq. (5) into Eq. (6), the vertical component of the load at point C is

\(P_{C}^{y}=\frac{12}{25}(64596 lb )=31006 lb\)

Finally, the load acting at point C is

\(P_{C}=\frac{P_{C}^{y}}{\cos 30^{\circ}}=\frac{31006 lb }{0.866}=35803 lb\)

Note that the load PC = 35 803 lb is the load that ensures that the factor of safety to guard against buckling of link 3 is N = 1. A load PC > 35 803 lb would cause link 3 to buckle; that is, the factor of safety would be N < 1.

Part 2.

(a) The x and y components of the load PC = 24 000 lb are

\(\begin{aligned}&P_{C}^{x}=P_{C} \sin 30^{\circ}=(24000 lb ) 0.500=12000 lb \\&P_{C}^{y}=P_{C} \cos 30^{\circ}=(24000 lb ) 0.866=20785 lb\end{aligned}\) (7)

Link 2 is subject to the tensile load \(P_{C}^{x}\), which creates a tensile stress in the link. The factor of safety guarding against yielding of link 2 is defined as

\(N=\frac{S^{y t}}{\sigma}\)

where the normal stress due to the tensile load can be can be written as

\(\sigma=\frac{P_{C}^{x}}{A}=\frac{12000 lb }{3.1416 in ^{2}}=3819.7 lb / in ^{2}\)

Therefore, the factor of safety guarding against yielding of link 2 is

\(N=\frac{50000 psi }{3819.7 psi }=13.1\) (8)

Note that this relatively high factor of safety indicates safety against tensile failure.

(b) Substituting Eq. (7) into Eq. (6), the compressive force F23 exerted on link 3 is

\(F_{23}=\frac{25}{12} P_{C}^{y}=\frac{25}{12}(20785 lb )=43302 lb\) (9)

where force F23 is equal to force PB. For link 3, the factor of safety to guard against buckling can be calculated by substituting Eqs. (4) and (9) into Eq. (5); that is,

\(N=\frac{P_{ cr }}{P_{B}}=\frac{P_{ cr }}{F_{23}}=\frac{64596 lb }{43302 lb }=1.49.\) (10)

(c) Note that the factor of safety guarding against yielding of link 2 [the tensile member; Eq. (8)] is much higher than the factor of safety guarding against buckling of link 3 [the compressive member; Eq. (10)]. Therefore, the prediction is that link 3 will fail before link 2.
