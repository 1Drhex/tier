Guest Question.

In the circuit of Fig. 3.11, \(I_{L}(0)\) = 1 A. What is the equation of \(V_{ab}(t)\) for t > 0?

Admin
Solution

4.11. To simplify the problem, we can determine the Thevenin equivalent circuit seen by the inductor. The input resistance of the circuit can be calculated by using the series-parallel rule, as is shown in Fig. 4.11.2.

\(R_{Th}=R_{in}=1+(1+3)||(2+4)= 1+\frac{4×6}{4+6}=\) 3.4Ω……(1)

The Thevenin voltage of the circuit will be zero, since there is no power source in the circuit. Therefore, we have the Thevenin equivalent circuit shown in Fig. 4.11.3.

Based on the given information:

\(I_{L}(0)=\) 1 A…….(2)

Due to the current continuity of the inductor in lack of a power source with an infinite size (e.g., impulse power source):

\(I_{L}(0^{+})=I_{L}(0)=\) 1 A……(3)

The final current of the inductor will be 0 A, since there is no power source in the circuit. Thus:

\(I_{L}(∞)\) = 0 A …….(4)

The time constant of the RC circuit:

τ = \(\frac{L}{R_{Th}}=\frac{1}{3.4}\)……..(5)

Using the general form of the inductor current in a first-order circuit with DC power supply:

\(I_{L}(t)=I_{L}(∞)+(I_{L}(0^{+}-I_{L}(∞))e^{-\frac{t}{τ}} = 0 +(1-0)e^{-3.4t}= e^{-3.4t}\) ……(6)

Now, by using the current division formula and Ohm’s law, we can calculate \(V_{ab}(t)\) in the circuit of Fig. 4.11.4.

\(I_{1,3}=\frac{2+4}{2+4+1+3}×e^{-3.4t}= 0.6e^{-3.4t}\) A……..(7)

\(I_{2,4}=\frac{1+3}{2+4+1+3}×e^{-3.4t}= 0.4e^{-3.4t}\) A……..(8)

\(V_{ab}(t)=V_{a}(t)-V_{b}(t)=3I_{1,3}-4I_{2,4}=3×0.6e^{-3.4t}-4×0.4e^{-3.4t}= (1.8-1.6)e^{-3.4t}\)

\(\Rightarrow V_{ab}(t)\)= 0.2\(e^{-3.4t}\) V
