Guest Question.

A two-dimensional supersonic wing has the profile shown in Figure P7 .7. At zero angle of attack, determine the drag force on the wing per unit length of span at Mach 2 and at Mach 4. Repeat for the lift force. Take the maximum thickness of the airfoil to be 0.2m.

Admin
Solution

\(M_{l}=4.0\) computations

 

At this Mach number and the deflection angle of 9.4623º, θ = 21.7505° and \(M _{2}=3.3241, \quad \frac{ p _{2}}{ p _{1}}=2.3966\). Furthermore, at \(M _{2}, \frac{ p _{2}}{ p _{ o 2}}=0.016876 \text { and } v _{2}=\) 55.6341º. Therefore,

 

\(v_{3}=v_{2}+2 \delta=55.6341+2(9.4623)=74.5587^{\circ}\)

 

Hence, \(M_{3}=4.7575\). The static to total pressure ratio in region 3 is \(p _{3} / p _{ o 3}=0.00252\). The pressure on the rear side of the airfoil, i.e., \(p _{3}\) is computed as

 

\(\frac{p_{3}}{p_{2}}=\left(\frac{p_{3}}{p_{o3}}\right)\left(\frac{p_{o3}}{p_{o2}}\right)\left(\frac{p_{o2}}{p_{2}}\right)=(0.00252)(1)\left(\frac{1}{0.016876}\right)=0.14932\)

 

\(p _{1}= p _{\infty}=20 kPa\),

 

\(p _{2}=\left(\frac{ p _{2}}{ p _{1}}\right) p _{1}=(2.3966)(20)=47.932 kPa\),

 

\(p _{3}=\left(\frac{ p _{3}}{ p _{2}}\right) p _{2}=(0.14932)(33.208)=7.1572 kPa\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\text { Drag } &=\left(p_{2}\right)\left(\frac{t}{\sin \delta}\right) \sin \delta-\left(p_{3}\right)\left(\frac{t}{\sin \delta}\right) \sin \delta=\left(p_{2}-p_{3}\right) t=(47.932-7.1572) 0.2 \\&=8.155 kN / m\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\text { Lift } &=\left(p_{1}\right)(L)-\left(p_{2}\right)\left(\frac{t}{\sin \delta}\right) \cos \delta-\left(p_{3}\right)\left(\frac{t}{\sin \delta}\right) \cos \delta=p_{1} L-\left(p_{2}+p_{3}\right) \frac{t}{\cot \delta} \\&=p_{1} L-\left(p_{2}+p_{3}\right) \frac{L}{2}=\\&=(20)(2.4)-(47.932+7.1572)(1.2)=-18.107 k N / m\end{aligned}\)
