Guest Question.

A homogeneous square plate (weight W = mg) is suspended by a pin at A (Fig. 3.19a). It is displaced from the position of equilibrium and then released. Find the equation of motion.

Admin
Solution

The plate rotates about the fixed axis that is perpendicular to the plane of the plate and passes through A. We introduce the angle φ as shown in Fig. 3.19b and apply the principle of angular momentum:

\(\overset{\curvearrowleft }{A }:\Theta _{A}\ddot{\varphi }=M_{A}\) (a)

with

\(M_{A}=-mg\frac{\sqrt{2} }{2} a\sin \varphi\) . (b)

The symbol \(\overset{\curvearrowleft }{A }:\) characterizes the point of reference and the positive sense of rotation.

To find the moment of inertia we use (3.17) and the notation in Fig. 3.19c. With the thickness t of the plate and

\(\Theta _{a} =\int r^{2}dm \) (3.17)

dm = ρ dV = ρt dx dy, \( m=\rho a^{2}t\) , \( r^{2} =x^{2} +y^{2} \)

we obtain

\( \Theta _{A}=\int r^{2} dm=\rho t\int\limits_{0}^{a}\int\limits_{0}^{a}\left(x^{2} +y^{2} \right) dx dy=\frac{2}{3} \rho ta^{4}=\frac{2}{3} ma^{2}\) .

Inserting \(\Theta _{A} \) and \(M_{A} \) (see (b)) into (a), the equation of motion becomes

\(\frac{2}{3} ma^{2} \ddot{\varphi }=-mg\frac{\sqrt{2} }{2}a\sin \varphi \rightarrow \ddot{\varphi }+\frac{3\sqrt{2} }{4}\frac{g}{a} \sin \varphi =0 \) .
