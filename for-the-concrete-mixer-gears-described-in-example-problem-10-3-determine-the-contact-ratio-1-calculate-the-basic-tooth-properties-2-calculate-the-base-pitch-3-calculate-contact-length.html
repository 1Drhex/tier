Guest Question.

For the concrete mixer gears described in , determine the contact ratio. 1.Calculate the Basic Tooth Properties 2.Calculate the Base Pitch 3.Calculate Contact Length.

Admin
Solution

1: The pitch radii of both gears can be determined from the pitch diameters.

\(r_1=\frac{d_1}{2}=\frac{3.0\ in.}{2}=1.5\ in.\)

\(r_2=\frac{d_2}{2}=\frac{6.0\ in.}{2}=3.0\ in.\)

From Table 10.3, the addendum for 20° full-depth teeth is

\(a=\frac{1}{P_d}=\frac{1}{5}=0.20\ in.\)

2: The base pitch is calculated from equation \(P_b=\frac{\pi d_1cos\phi }{N_1}=\frac{\pi d_2cos\phi }{N_2}\).

\(p_b=\frac{\pi d_1\ cos\phi }{N_1}=\frac{\pi (3.0\ in.)cos(20^\circ )}{15}=0.6890\ in.\)

3: The length of the contact line is calculated from equation \(Z=\sqrt{(r_2+a_2)^2-(r_2cos\phi )^2}-r_2\ sin\phi +\sqrt{(r_1+a_1)^2-(r_1\ cos\phi )^2}-r_1\ sin\phi \).

 

\(Z=\sqrt{(r_2+a_2)^2-(r_2cos\phi )^2}-r_2\ sin\phi +\sqrt{(r_1+a_1)^2-(r_1\ cos\phi )^2}-r_1\ sin\phi \)

 

\(=\sqrt{(3.0+0.2)^2-(3.0cos20^\circ )^2}-3.0sin20^\circ\)

 

\(+\sqrt{(1.5+0.2)^2-(1.5cos20^\circ )^2}-1.5sin20^\circ\)

= 0.9255 in.

 

Then the contact ratio is determined from equation \(m_p=\frac{Z}{p_b}\).

 

\(m_p=\frac{Z}{pb}=\frac{0.9255\ in.}{0.6890\ in.}=1.3433\)

Although this ratio is acceptable, larger values (1.4–1.5) are desirable.
