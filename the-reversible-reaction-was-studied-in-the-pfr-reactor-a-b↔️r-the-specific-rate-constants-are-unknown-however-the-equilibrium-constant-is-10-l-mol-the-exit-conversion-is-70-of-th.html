Guest Question.

The reversible reaction was studied in the PFR reactor:
\(A + B\leftrightarrow R\)
The specific rate constants are unknown; however, the equilibrium constant is 10 L/mol. The exit conversion is 70% of the equilibrium conversion. The feed concentrations of A and B are each one equal to 2 mol/L, and the feed rate is 0.2 L/min. The reactor volume is 0.1 L. Calculate the direct and reverse specific velocities.

Admin
Solution

The resultant reaction rate is as follows:
\(r=kC_{A}C_{B} − k'CR\)

However, for equimolar concentrations, we obtain:
\(r=kC_{A}^{2}-k'C_{R}\)

In terms of conversion it becomes:
\(r=kC_{A0}^{2}[(1-X_{A}^{2})-\frac{1}{KC_{A0}}X_{A}]\) (11.15)

At equilibrium, r=0 and substituting K=10 and \(C_{A0} =2\), we obtain:
\(KC_{A0}=K_{c}=20\)
\(0=[(1-X_{Ae})^{2}-\frac{1}{20}X_{Ae}]\)
\(20X_{Ae}^{2}-41X_{Ae}+20=0\)

Finally, we get:
\(X_{Ae} =0.80\) and \(X'_{Ae} =1.25\)

Substituting:
\(KC_{A0}\frac{X_Ae}{(1-X_{Ae})^{2}}=K_{c}\) (11.16)

From the Equation 11.5, we get:
\(r=kC_{A0}^{2}[(1-X_A)^{2}-\frac{(1-X_{Ae})^{2}}{X_{Ae}}X_{A}]\)

or
\(r=kC_{A0}^{2}X_{Ae}[(1-\frac{X_{A}}{X_{Ae}})(\kappa -\frac{X_{A}}{X_{Ae}})]\) (11.17)

where:
\(\kappa =\frac{X'_{A}}{X_{A}}\)

It is the reaction rate as function of the conversion. Substituting Equation 11.17 in the PFR equation, we obtain:
\(\tau =C_{A0}\int_{0}^{X_{A}}\frac{dX_{A}}{r}\) (11.18)

After integration, we obtain the solution:
\(\ln [\frac{(\kappa -\frac{X_{A}}{X_{Ae}})}{\kappa (1-\frac{X_{A}}{X_{Ae}})}]=k^{*}\tau\) (11.19)

where:
\(k^{*}=(\kappa -1)kC_{A0}\frac{K_{c}-1}{K_{c}}X_{Ae}\) (11.20)

Since the final conversion is 70% of the equilibrium conversion, we have:
\(\kappa =\frac{X'_{Ae}}{X_{Ae}}=1.65\) and \(\frac{X_{A}}{X_{Ae}}=0.7\)

Thus,
\(\ln [\frac{(\kappa -\frac{X_{A}}{X_{Ae}})}{\kappa (1-\frac{X_{A}}{X_{Ae}})}]=0.608=k^{*}\tau\)

However,
\(\tau =\frac{V}{\nu _{0}}=2\)
\(0.608=k^{*}\tau\)

Then,
\(k^{*}0.304\)

From Equation 11.20, we get:
\(k^{*}=(\kappa -1)kC_{A0}\frac{K_{c}-1}{K_{c}}X_{Ae}\)

Substituting the values, we have:
k = 3.57 L/mol min
k’ = 0.178 \(min^{-1}\)
