Guest Question.

Consider the following investment opportunity by Knox County.

Initial cost = $600,000
Benefit per year at the end of Years 1 and 2 = $30,000
Benefit per year at the end of Years 3 to 30 = $50,000

If Knox County set the interest at 7% per year, would this be an economically feasible investment for the county?

Admin
Solution

For this problem, we will use both the PW and the AW approaches in order to show that both equations would give the same results.

Using Present Worth

\(PW_{benefits} = 30,000\left(P/ A,7\%,2\right) + 50,000\left(P/ A,7\%,28\right)\left(P/ F,7\%,2\right)\)

 

\(= \$584,267.16\)

 

\(PW_{costs} = 600,000\)

Using Annual Worth

\(AW_{benefits} = \left[ 30,000\left(P/ A,7\%,2\right) + 50,000\left(P/ A,7\%,28\right)\left(P/ F,7\%,2\right)\right]\left(A/ P,7\%,2\right)\)

 

\(= \$47,086.09\)

 

\(AW_{costs} = \$600,000\left(A/ P,7\%,30\right)\)

 

\(= \$48,354.00\)

 

\(B/ C = \frac{584,267.16}{600,000.00} = \frac{47,086.09}{48,354.00} = 0.97\)

 

The B/C ratio indicates that the investment is not economically feasible because B/C < 1.0. However, one can see that the PW and AW methods both produced the same result.
