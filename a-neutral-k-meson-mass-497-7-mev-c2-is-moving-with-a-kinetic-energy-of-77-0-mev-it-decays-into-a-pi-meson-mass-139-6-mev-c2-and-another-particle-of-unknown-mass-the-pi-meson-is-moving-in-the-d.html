Guest Question.

A neutral K meson (mass 497.7 MeV/\(c^{2}\)) is moving with a kinetic energy of 77.0 MeV. It decays into a pi meson (mass 139.6 MeV/\(c^{2}\)) and another particle of unknown mass. The pi meson is moving in the direction of the original K meson with a momentum of 381.6 MeV/c. (a) Find the momentum and total relativistic energy of the unknown particle. (b) Find the mass of the unknown particle.

Admin
Solution

(a) The total energy and momentum of the K meson are

\( E_{K} = K_{K} + m_{K}c^{2} = 77.0 MeV + 497.7 MeV = 574.7 MeV\)

 

\(p_{K} = \frac{1}{c} \sqrt{E_{K} ^{2} – (m_{K}c^{2})^{2}}\)

 

\(= \frac{1}{c} \sqrt{(574.7MeV)^{2} – (497.7MeV)^{2}}\)
= 287.4 MeV/c

and for the pi meson
\(E_{\pi } = \sqrt{(cp_{\pi })^{2}+ (m_{\pi }c^{2})^{2}}\)

 

\(=\sqrt{(381.6MeV)^{2} + (139.6MeV)^{2}}\)
= 406.3 MeV/c

Conservation of relativistic momentum (\(p_{initial} = p_{final}\)) gives \(p_{K} =p_{\pi } + p_{x}\) (where x represents the unknown particle), so

\(p_{x} = p_{K} – p_{\pi } = 287.4 MeV/c − 381.6 MeV/c\)

= −94.2 MeV/c

and conservation of total relativistic energy (\(E_{initial} = E_{final}\)) gives \(E_{K} =E_{\pi } + E_{x}\), so

\(E_{x} = E_{K} – E_{\pi } = 574.7 MeV − 406.3 MeV \)

= 168.4 MeV

(b) We can find the mass by solving Eq. 2.39 for \(mc^{2}\) :

\(m_{x }c^{2} = \sqrt{E_{x} ^{2} -(cp_{x })^{2}}\)

 

\(=\sqrt{(168.4 MeV)^{2} – (94.2 MeV)^{2}}\)

= 139.6 MeV

Thus the unknown particle has a mass of 139.6 MeV/\(c^{2}\), and its mass shows that it is another pi meson.
