Guest Question.

Calculate the value of \(V_{CE}\) in the collector stabilisation circuit of \(Fig. 57.34\) .

Admin
Solution

We will use \(\beta\)-rule to find \(I_{C}\) in the following two ways.

\((i)\) First Method
Here, we will transfer \(R_{L}\) to the base circuit.

\(I_{B}=\frac{V_{CC} }{R_{B}+\beta R_{L} }=\frac{20}{1000+100\left(10\right) } =10mA \)

 

\(I_{C} = \beta I_{B}= 100 \times 10 = 1000 mA = 1 A\)

 

\(V_{CE}\cong V_{CC} –I_{C} R_{L} = 20 – 1 \times 10 = 10 V\)

 

\((ii)\) Second Method
Now, we will refer \(R_{B}\) to collector circuit.

\(I_{C}\cong \frac{V_{CC} }{R_{L} +R_{B} /\beta } =\frac{20}{10+1000/100} =1mA\)

 

\(V_{CE} =V_{CC} – I_{C} R_{L} = 10 V\) — as above

It was a simple circuit because \(R_{E}=0 \)and \(R_{B} \) was connected to \(V_{CC}\) through \(R_{L} \) and not directly (in which case, \(R_{L} \) would not lie in the path of \(I_{B}\) ). Now, we will consider the case when \(R_{E} \) is present and \(R_{L} \) does not lie in the path of \(I_{B}\) .
