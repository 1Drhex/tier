Guest Question.

A steam engine has steam being supplied at 10 bar, 0.9 dry and exhausted at 1 bar. Cut-off occurs at 60% of stroke. Determine
(i) the fraction of work obtained by expansion working of engine.
(ii) the efficiency of cycle
(iii) the % increase in work and efficiency when engine works as non-expansive engine.
Neglect clearance volume and assume expansion to be hyperbolic.

Admin
Solution

\(\text{Let us analyze for 1 kg of steam.}\\\text{At inlet to engine cylinder,}\\h_{1} = h_{\text{f at 10 bar}} + 0.9 × h_{\text{fg at 10 bar}}\\h_{1} = 2576.58 kJ/kg, v_{1} = v_{\text{f at 10 bar}} + 0.9 × v_{\text{fg at 10 bar}}\\v_{1} = 0.1751 m^{3}/kg\\\begin{aligned}\text{At 1 bar, }h_{f}&= 417.46 kJ/kg\\\text{Heat added per}&\text{ kg of steam, q}_{add} = h_{1} – h_{\text{f at 1 bar}}\\q_{add}&= 2159.12 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{For given cut-off volume, v}_{1} = 0.6 × v_{2}\\\text{or v}_{2} = 0.2918 m^{3}/kg,\text{ Expansion ratio, r }= \frac{1}{0.6} = \frac{5}{3}\\\text{Non expansive work in cycle 1-2-3-4-5 can be given by area 1-3}^{\prime}\text{ -4-5.}\\\text{Non expansive work per kg of steam }w_{ne} = v_{1} × (10 – 1) × 10^{2} = 157.59 kJ/kg\\\text{Expansive work in cycle 1-2-3-4-5 can be given by area 1-2-3-3}^{\prime}.\\\text{Expansive work per kg of steam.}\\\begin{aligned}w_{e}&= \left[p_{1} v_{1} \ln r – p_{3} (v_{2} – v_{1})\right] \\w_{e}&= \left[10 × 10^{2} × 0.1751 × \ln\left\lgroup\frac{5}{3} \right\rgroup– 1 × 10^{2} (0.2918 – 0.1751)\right] \\w_{e}&= 77.77 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Total work per kg of steam }= w_{ne} + w_{e} = 235.36 kJ/kg\\\begin{aligned}\text{Fraction of work obtained by expansive working }&= \frac{w_{e}}{w_{ne} + w_{e}}\\= \frac{77.77}{235.36}&= \text{0.3304 or 33.04}\%\\\text{Thermal efficiency of cycle }= \frac{235.36}{q_{add}} = \frac{235.36}{2159.12} &=\text{0.1090 or 10.90}\%\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Fraction of expansive work}&= 33.04\%\text{ of total output}\\\text{Thermal efficiency}&= 10.90\%\end{aligned}\\\)For cycle having complete non expansive work: Such modified cycle is shown by 1′-3-4-5. This refers to the situation when cut-off shall become unity. Therefore, the mass of steam admitted shall be increased as compared to previous case in which 1 kg steam is admitted up to 60% cut-off point. Steam admitted per cycle when cut off is at 60% = 1 kg\(\\\text{Steam admitted per cycle when cut off becomes unity }= \frac{1}{0.6} = 1.66 kg\\\text{Total work per cycle = Area 1}^{′}\text{-3-4-5 = }(p_{1} – p_{3}) · v_{1} × 1.66 = 261.59 kJ\\\begin{aligned}\%\text{ increase in work}&=\left\lgroup\frac{261.59 – 235.36}{235.36} \right\rgroup × 100 = 11.14\% \\\text{Modified thermal efficiency}&= \frac{261.59}{1.66 × 2159.12} = \text{0.0729 or 7.29}\%\\\text{There is reduction in thermal}&\text{ efficiency.}\%\text{ decrease in efficiency}\\&= \left\lgroup\frac{10.9 – 7.29}{10.9} \right\rgroup × 100 = 33.12\% \\\%\text{ increase in work}&= 11.14\%\\\%\text{ decrease in efficiency}&= 33.12\% \end{aligned}\)
