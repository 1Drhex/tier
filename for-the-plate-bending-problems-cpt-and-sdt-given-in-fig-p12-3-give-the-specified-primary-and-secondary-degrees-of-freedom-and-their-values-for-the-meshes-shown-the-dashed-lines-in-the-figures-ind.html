Guest Question.

For the plate bending problems (CPT and SDT) given in Fig. P12.3, give the specified primary and secondary degrees of freedom and their values for the meshes shown. The dashed lines in the figures indicate simply supported boundary conditions Use E, \(\nu \), h, a, and b in formulating the data. You are required to give values of the loads for at least a couple of representative loads.

Admin
Solution

The specified displacement degrees of freedom are

\(w_{3}=w_{6}=w_{7}=w_{8}=w_{9}=0 ; \quad \theta_{x 1}=\theta_{x 4}=\theta_{x 7}=\theta_{x 8}=\theta_{x 9}=0\)

 

\(\theta_{y 1}=\theta_{y 2}=\theta_{y 3}=\theta_{y 6}=\theta_{y 9}=0, \quad \theta_{x y 1}=0\)

For SDT, use \(\phi _{x}\) in place of \(\theta_{x}=\frac{\partial w}{\partial x}\) and \(\phi _{y}\) in place of \(\theta_{y}=\frac{\partial w}{\partial y}\) in place of \(\phi_{y}\) .

In SDT, the load vector of uniformly loaded nine-node element can be obtained from the tensor product of the load vectors of 1-D quadratic elements. The load vector of a 1-D quadratic element of length hx with uniform load \(q_{0}\) is

\(\frac{q_{0} h_{x}}{6}\left\{\begin{array}{l}1 \\4 \\1\end{array}\right\}\)

Hence the load vector for a nine-node quadratic element with uniform load is

\(\frac{q_{0} h_{x}}{6}\left\{\begin{array}{l}1 \\4 \\1\end{array}\right\} \frac{h_{y}}{6}\left\{\begin{array}{l}1 \\4 \\1\end{array}\right\}^{ T }=\frac{q_{0} h_{x} h_{y}}{36}\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 4 & 1 \\4 & 16 & 4 \\1 & 4 & 1\end{array}\right]\)

Thus, for the SDT, the loads at different nodes are \(\left(h_{x}=a / 4 \text { and } h_{y}=b / 4\right)\)

\(F_{1}=\frac{q_{0} a b}{576}, \quad F_{2}=\frac{4 q_{0} a b}{576}, \quad F_{3}=\frac{2 q_{0} a b}{576}, \quad F_{4}=\frac{4 q_{0} a b}{576}, \quad F_{5}=\frac{q_{0} a b}{576}\)

 

\(F_{6}=\frac{4 q_{0} a b}{576}, \quad F_{7}=\frac{16 q_{0} a b}{576}, \quad F_{8}=\frac{8 q_{0} a b}{576}, \quad F_{9}=\frac{16 q_{0} a b}{576}, \quad F_{10}=\frac{4 q_{0} a b}{576}\)

 

\(F_{11}=\frac{2 q_{0} a b}{576}, \quad F_{12}=\frac{8 q_{0} a b}{576}, \quad F_{13}=\frac{4 q_{0} a b}{576}, \quad F_{14}=\frac{8 q_{0} a b}{576}, \quad F_{15}=\frac{2 q_{0} a b}{576}\)

etc.

For the CPT(C) element, the given mesh must be interpreted as a 2 × 2 mesh of four-node elements (a total of nine nodes). The load vector of uniformly loaded four-node element (of sides \(h_{x}\) and \(h_{y}\)) can be obtained from the tensor product of the load vectors of 1-D Euler—Bernoulli beam elements. The load vector of an Euler—Bernoulli beam element of length \(h_{x}\) with uniform load \(q_{0}\) is (the rotations used in plate bending do not include the negative sign)

\(\frac{q_{0} h_{x}}{12}\left\{\begin{array}{c}6 \\h_{x} \\6 \\-h_{x}\end{array}\right\}\)

Hence, the load vector for a four-node Hermite cubic element with uniform load is

\(\frac{q_{0} h_{x}}{12}\left\{\begin{array}{c}6 \\h_{x} \\6 \\-h_{x}\end{array}\right\} \frac{h_{y}}{12}\left\{\begin{array}{c}6 \\h_{y} \\6 \\-h_{y}\end{array}\right\}^{ T }\) = \(\frac{q_{0} h_{x} h_{y}}{144}\left[\begin{array}{cccc}36 & 6 h_{y} & 36 & -6 h_{y} \\6 h_{x} & h_{x} h_{y} & 6 h_{x} & -h_{x} h_{y} \\36 & 6 h_{y} & 36 & -6 h_{y} \\-6 h_{x} & -h_{x} h_{y} & -6 h_{x} & h_{x} h_{y}\end{array}\right]\)

The 2 × 2 submatrix of coefficients (there are four such submatrices) correspoind to the four degrees \(\left(Q_{n}, M_{x}, M_{y}, M_{x y}\right)\) of freedom at the node. Thus, at node 1 we have \(\left(h_{x}=a / 4 \text { and } h_{y}=b / 4\right)\)

\(Q_{1}=\frac{q_{0} a b}{64}, \quad M_{x 1}=\frac{q_{0} a^{2} b}{64 \times 24}, \quad M_{y 1}=\frac{q_{0} a b^{2}}{64 \times 24}, \quad M_{x y 1}=\frac{q_{0} a^{2} b^{2}}{256 \times 144}\)

At node 2 we have

\(Q_{2}=\frac{72 q_{0} a b}{16 \times 144}, \quad M_{x 2}=0, \quad M_{y 2}=-\frac{12 q_{0} a b^{2}}{64 \times 144}, \quad M_{x y 2}=0\)

At node 5, we have

\(Q_{5}=\frac{4 \times 36 q_{0} a b}{16 \times 144}, \quad M_{x 5}=0, \quad M_{y 5}=0, \quad M_{x y 5}=0\)

and so on.
