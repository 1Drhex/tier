Guest Question.

An air compressor receives atmospheric air at \(T _{0}=17^{\circ} C\), 100 kPa, and compresses it up to 1400 kPa. The compressor has an isentropic efficiency of 88% and it loses energy by heat transfer to the atmosphere as 10% of the isentropic work. Find the actual exit temperature and the reversible work.

Admin
Solution

C.V. Compressor

Isentropic: \(w_{ c , in , s }= h _{ e , s }- h _{ i } ; \quad s _{ e , s }= s _{ i }\)

From table A.7.1 and entropy equation we get

\(s _{ Te s }^{ o }= s _{ Ti }^{ o }+ R \ln \left( P _{ e } / P _{ i }\right)=6.83521+0.287 \ln (14)=7.59262 kJ / kgK\)

Back interpolate in Table A.7: ⇒ \(h _{ e , s }=617.23 kJ / kg\)

\(w _{ c , in , s }=617.23-290.43=326.8 kJ / kg\)

Actual: \(w_{c, \text { in,ac }}=w_{c, \text { in }, s} / \eta_{c}=371.36 kJ / kg ; \quad q_{\text {loss }}=32.68 kJ / kg\)

\(w _{ c , in , ac }+ h _{ i }= h _{ e , ac }+ q _{ loss }\)

 

\(\begin{array}{l}\Rightarrow h _{ e , ac }=290.43+371.36-32.68=629.1 kJ / kg \\\Rightarrow T _{ e , ac }= 6 2 1 K\end{array}\)

 

Reversible:

\(\begin{aligned}w ^{ rev } &= h _{ i }- h _{ e , ac }+ T _{0}\left( s _{ e , ac }- s _{ i }\right) \\&=290.43-629.1+290.15 \times(7.6120-6.8357) \\&=-338.67+225.38= – 1 1 3 . 3 k J / kg\end{aligned}\)

 

Since \(q _{\text {loss }}\) is also to the atmosphere it is the net q exchanged with the ambient that explains the change in s.
