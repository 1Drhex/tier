Guest Question.

At the Pickwick plant of TVA the propeller-type turbines are rated at 48,000 hp at 81.8 rpm under a 43-ft head. The discharge diameter is 292.3 in. For a geometrically similar turbine to develop 36,000 hp under a 36-ft head, what speed and diameter should be used? What percentage change in flow is probable?

Admin
Solution

The specific speed of geometrically similar turbines can be expressed as \(N_{S}=\frac{N\sqrt{P} }{H^{5/4}} \) .

Then

\(\frac{81.8\sqrt{48,000} }{(43)^{5/4}} =\frac{N\sqrt{36,000} }{(36)^{5/4}} \) and N = 75.6 rpm

The same result can be obtained by calculating Nu, then Pu and NS. Apply these values to the turbine to be designed. Thus,

\(N_{u}=\frac{D_{1}N}{\sqrt{H} } =\frac{(292.3)(81.8)}{\sqrt{43} }=3646 \) , \(P_{u}=\frac{P}{D^2_{1}H^{3/2}}=\frac{48,000}{(292.3)^2(43)^{3/2}}=0.0020 \)

\(N_{s}=N_{u}\sqrt{P_{u}}=3646\sqrt{0.0020}=163.1 \)

and \(N=\frac{N_{S}H^{5/4}}{\sqrt{P} }=\frac{(163.1)(36)^{5/4}}{\sqrt{36,000} }=75.8 \) rpm , as above

For the diameter of the new turbine, using \(N_{u}=\frac{D_{1}N}{\sqrt{H} } \) , \(D_{1}=\frac{N_{u}\sqrt{H} }{N}=\frac{3646\sqrt{36} }{75.8} =289 \) in .

For the percentage change in flow Q, the flow relation for Pickwick and new turbines is

new \(\frac{Q}{D^2_{1}H^{1/2}}= \) Pickwick \(\frac{Q}{D^2_{1}H^{1/2}} \) , \(\frac{Q_{Pick}}{(292.3)^2(43)^{1/2}}=\frac{Q_{new}}{(289)^2(36)^{1/2}} \)

and new \(Q=0.894 Q_{Pick} \) or about 11% decrease in Q.
