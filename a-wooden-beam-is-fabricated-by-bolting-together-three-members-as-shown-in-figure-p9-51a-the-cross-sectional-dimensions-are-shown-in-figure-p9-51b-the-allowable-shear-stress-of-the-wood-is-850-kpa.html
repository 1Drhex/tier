Guest Question.

A wooden beam is fabricated by bolting together three members, as shown in Figure P9.51a. The cross-sectional dimensions are shown in Figure P9.51b. The allowable shear stress of the wood is 850 kPa, and the allowable shear stress of the 10-mm-diameter bolts is 40 MPa. Determine:
(a) the maximum internal shear force V that the cross section can withstand based on the allowable shear stress in the wood.
(b) the maximum bolt spacing s required to develop the internal shear force computed in part (a).

Admin
Solution

Centroid location in y direction:

\(\begin{array}{lccccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Width } b \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Height } ~ \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} \text { (from bottom) } \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { left board } & 40 & 90 & 3,600 & 255 & 918,000 \\\text { center board } & 40 & 300 & 12,000 & 150 & 1,800,000 \\\text { right board } & 40 & 90 & 3,600 & 255 & 918,000 \\\hline & & & 19,200 & & 3,636,000\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{3,636,000 mm ^{3}}{19,200 mm ^{2}} &=189.375 mm \text{(from bottom of shape to centroid)}\\&=110.625 mm \text{(from top of shape to centroid)}\end{aligned}\)

 

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

\(\begin{aligned}&\begin{array}{lcccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { left board } & 2,430,000 & 65.625 & 15,503,906.25 & 17,933,906.25 \\\text { center board } & 90,000,000 & -39.375 & 18,604,687.50 & 108,604,687.50 \\\text { right board } & 2,430,000 & 65.625 & 15,503,906.25 & 17,933,906.25 \\\hline\end{array}\\&\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( mm ^{4}\right)=144,472,500.00\end{aligned}\)

 

Consider maximum horizontal shear stress:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q=(40 mm )(189.375 mm )(189.375 mm / 2)=717,257.813 mm ^{3} \\&\tau=\frac{V Q}{I t} \leq 850 kPa =0.850 MPa \\&\therefore V_{\max } \leq \frac{\left(0.850 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(144,472,500 mm ^{4}\right)(40 mm )}{717,257.813 mm ^{3}}=6,848.4 N =6.85 kN\end{aligned}\)

Maximum bolt spacing
Consider left board, which is held in place by the bolt:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q=(40 mm )(90 mm )(110.625 mm -90 mm / 2)=236,250 mm ^{3} \\&q=\frac{V Q}{I}=\frac{(6,848.4 N )\left(236,250 mm ^{3}\right)}{144,472,500 mm ^{4}}=11.1989 N / mm\end{aligned}\)

Note that this value of q is the shear flow that must be transmitted by one surface of the bolt cross section. The cross-sectional area of the bolt is:

\(A_{ bolt }=\frac{\pi}{4}(10 mm )^{2}=78.5398 mm ^{2}\)

Relate the shear flow and the bolt shear stress with Eq. (9.14):

\(\begin{aligned}&q s \leq n_{f} \tau_{f} A_{f} \\&\therefore s \leq \frac{n_{f} \tau_{f} A_{f}}{q}=\frac{(1 \text { bolt surface })\left(40 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(78.5398 mm ^{2}\right)}{11.1989 N / mm }=281 mm\end{aligned}\)
