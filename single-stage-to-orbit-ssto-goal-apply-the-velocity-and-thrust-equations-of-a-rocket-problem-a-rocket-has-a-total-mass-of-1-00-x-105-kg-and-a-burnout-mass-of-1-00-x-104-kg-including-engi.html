Single Stage to Orbit (SSTO)

GOAL Apply the velocity and thrust equations of a rocket.

PROBLEM A rocket has a total mass of \(1.00 \times 10^{5} kg\) and a burnout mass of \(1.00 \times 10^{4} kg\), including engines, shell, and payload. The rocket blasts off from Earth and exhausts all its fuel in 4.00 min, burning the fuel at a steady rate with an exhaust velocity of \(v_{e}=4.50 \times 10^{3} m / s\). (a) If air friction and gravity are neglected, what is the speed of the rocket at burnout? (b) What thrust does the engine develop at liftoff? (c) What is the initial acceleration of the rocket if gravity is not neglected? (d) Estimate the speed at burnout if gravity isn’t neglected.

STRATEGY Although it sounds sophisticated, this problem is mainly a matter of substituting values into the appropriate equations. Part (a) requires substituting values into Equation 6.19 for the velocity. For part (b), divide the change in the rocket’s mass by the total time, getting \(\Delta M / \Delta t\), then substitute into Equation 6.20 to find the thrust. (c) Using Newton’s second law, the force of gravity, and the result of (b), we can find the initial acceleration. For part (d), the acceleration of gravity is approximately constant over the few kilometers involved, so the velocity found in part (b) will be reduced by roughly \(\Delta v_{g}=-g t\). Add this loss to the result of part (a).

 

\(\text { Instantaneous thrust }=M a=M \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t}=\left|v_{e} \frac{\Delta M}{\Delta t}\right|\) [6.20]

Admin
Solution

(a) Calculate the velocity at burnout, ignoring gravity and air drag.

Substitute \(v_{i}=0, v_{e}=4.50 \times 10^{3} m / s , M_{i}=1.00 \times 10^{5} kg\), and \(M_{f}=1.00 \times 10^{4}\) kg into Equation 6.19: \(\begin{aligned}v_{f} &=v_{i}+v_{e} \ln \left(\frac{M_{i}}{M_{f}}\right) \\&=0+\left(4.5 \times 10^{3} m / s \right) \ln \left(\frac{1.00 \times 10^{5} kg }{1.00 \times 10^{4} kg }\right) \\v_{f} &=1.04 \times 10^{4} m / s\end{aligned}\)

 

\(v_{f}-v_{i}=v_{e} \ln \left(\frac{M_{i}}{M_{f}}\right)\) [6.19]

 

(b) Find the thrust at liftoff.

Compute the change in the rocket’s mass:

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta M &=M_{f}-M_{i}=1.00 \times 10^{4} kg -1.00 \times 10^{5} kg \\&=-9.00 \times 10^{4} kg\end{aligned}\)

 

Calculate the rate at which rocket mass changes by dividing the change in mass by the time (where the time interval equals \(\left.4.00 min =2.40 \times 10^{2} s \right)\) :

\(\frac{\Delta M}{\Delta t}=\frac{-9.00 \times 10^{4} kg }{2.40 \times 10^{2} s }=-3.75 \times 10^{2} kg / s\)

 

Substitute this rate into Equation 6.20, obtaining the thrust:

\(\begin{aligned}\text { Thrust } &=\left|v_{e} \frac{\Delta M}{\Delta t}\right|=\left(4.50 \times 10^{3} m / s \right)\left(3.75 \times 10^{2} kg / s \right) \\&=1.69 \times 10^{6} N\end{aligned}\)

 

(c) Find the initial acceleration, including the gravity force.

Write Newton’s second law, where T stands for thrust, and
solve for the acceleration a:

\(\begin{aligned}M a &=\sum F=T-M g \\a &=\frac{T}{M}-g=\frac{1.69 \times 10^{6} N }{1.00 \times 10^{5} kg }-9.80 m / s ^{2} \\&=7.10 m / s ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

(d) Estimate the speed at burnout when gravity is not neglected.

Find the approximate loss of speed due to gravity:

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta v_{g} &=-g \Delta t=-\left(9.80 m / s ^{2}\right)\left(2.40 \times 10^{2} s \right) \\&=-2.35 \times 10^{3} m / s\end{aligned}\)

 

Add this loss to the result of part (b):

\(\begin{aligned}v_{f} &=1.04 \times 10^{4} m / s -2.35 \times 10^{3} m / s \\&=8.05 \times 10^{3} m / s\end{aligned}\)

 

REMARKS Even taking gravity into account, the speed is sufficient to attain orbit. Some additional boost may be required to overcome air drag.
