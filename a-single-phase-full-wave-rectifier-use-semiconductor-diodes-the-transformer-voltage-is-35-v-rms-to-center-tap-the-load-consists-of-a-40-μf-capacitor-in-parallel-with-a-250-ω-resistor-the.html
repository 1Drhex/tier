Guest Question.

A single-phase full-wave rectifier use semiconductor diodes. The transformer voltage is 35 V rms to center tap. The load consists of a 40 μF capacitor in parallel with a 250 Ω resistor. The diode and transformer resistances and leakage reactance may be neglected. Assume that the power-line frequency is 50 Hz. Calculate

(a) The dc current \(I_{dc}\) in the circuit.

(b) Peak-to-peak amplitude of the ripple voltage, \(V_{r}\).

(c) The rms value of the ripple voltage, \(V^{'}_{rms}\).

(d ) Ripple factor of the rectifier-filter output, r.

(e) Repeat parts (a), (b), (c) and (d) for the case when the load consists of a 100 μF capacitor in parallel with a 250 Ω resistor.

( f ) Repeat parts (a), (b), (c) and (d) for the case when the load consists of a 40 μF capacitor in parallel with a 2000 Ω resistor.

(g) Compare the percentage regulation of the power supply for the values of \(I_{dc}\) of part (e) and ( f ).

Admin
Solution

(a) The peak output voltage of the transformer secondary which is applied as the input to the rectifier is \(V_{m}=35\sqrt{2}=49.5 \) V .

From Eq. (6.47) we can write

\(V_{dc}=I_{dc}R_{L}=V_{m}-\frac{I_{dc}}{4fC} \)

or \(I_{dc}=\frac{V_{m}}{R_{L}+1/4fC} =\frac{V_{m}}{R_{L}+R_{O}} \)

where \(R_{L} \) is the load resistance and \(R_{o}=\frac{1}{4fC}=\frac{1}{4\times 50\times 40\times 10^{-6}} =125 \Omega \) is the effective resistance of the power supply.

Using \(V_{m} \) = 49.5 V, \(R_{L}\) = 250 Ω, and \(R_{o}\) = 125 Ω in the above expression, the dc current in the circuit can be given by

\(I_{dc}=\frac{49.5}{250+125}=132 \) mA

(b) Using (6.45b), \(V_{r}=\frac{I_{dc}}{2fC} \) , the peak-to-peak ripple voltage \(V_{r} \) is determined as

\(V_{r} =\frac{0.132}{2\times 50\times 40\times 10^{-6}}=33 \) V

(c) Using the result of Part (b) in (6.44), \(V^{′}_{rms}=\frac{V_{r}}{2\sqrt{3} } \) , the rms value of the ripple is given by

\(V^{′}_{rms} =\frac{33}{2\sqrt{3} }=9.52 \) V

(d) The ripple factor can be determined by using Eq. (6.46) , \(r=\frac{1}{4\sqrt{3}fCR_{L} } \) , :

\(r=\frac{1}{4\sqrt{3}\times 50\times 40\times 10^{-6}\times 250 }=2.89 \)

(e) For C = 100 μF and \(R_{L} \) = 250 Ω, the value of the effective resistance is given by

\(R_{o}=\frac{1}{4\times 50\times 100\times 10^{-6}} =50\Omega \)

Following the similar method used in Parts (a), (b), (c), and (d), we can obtain

\(I_{dc}=\frac{49.5}{250+50} =165 \) mA

\(V_{r}=\frac{0.165}{2\times 50\times 100\times 10^{-6}}=16.5 \) V

\(V^{′}_{rms} =\frac{16.5}{2\sqrt{3} }=4.76 \) V

\(r=\frac{1}{4\sqrt{3}\times 50\times 100\times 10^{-6}\times 250 }=1.16\)

(f) Since C = 40 μF remains the same as in part a, \(R_{o}\) = 125 Ω. Following similar methods as used in Part (e), the values of \(I_{dc}\), \(V_{r}\), \(V^{′}_{rms}\) and r for \(R_{L}\) = 2000 Ω, and C = 40 μF are given by

\(I_{dc}=\frac{49.5}{2000+125} =23 \) mA

\(V_{r}=\frac{0.023}{2\times 50\times 40\times 10^{-6}}=5.82 \) V

\(V^{′}_{rms} =\frac{5.82}{2\sqrt{3} }=1.68 \) V

\(r=\frac{1}{4\sqrt{3}\times 50\times 40\times 10^{-6}\times 2000 }=0.036\)

From the results of Part (e) and ( f ), we may observe that by increasing the discharging time constant which is given by \(R_{L}\)C, the ripple in the output dc of the circuit can be reduced. This can be achieved by increasing either the resistance \(R_{L}\) or the capacitance C of the load circuit.

(g) Using Eq. (6.47), \(V_{dc}=V_{m}-\frac{I_{dc}}{4fC} \) , in Eq. (6.11), % regulation\(\equiv \frac{V_{no load}-V_{full load}}{V_{full load}}\times 100 \)% , the percentage regulation can be described as

% regulation \(=\frac{V_{m}-(V_{m}-R_{o}I_{dc})}{V_{m}-R_{o}I_{dc}}\times 100 =\frac{R_{o}I_{dc}}{I_{dc}R_{L}}\times 100=\frac{R_{o}}{R_{L}}\times 100 \)

Thus the percentage regulation for \(R_{o} \) = 50 Ω and \(R_{L} \) = 250 Ω is

% regulation (Part e) \(=\frac{50}{250}\times 100=20 \) %

Since \(R_{o} \) = 125 Ω and \(R_{L} \) = 2000 Ω in Part f, the percentage regulation in this case is given by

% regulation (Part f) \(=\frac{125}{250}\times 100=6.25 \) %

It is observed that by increasing the time constant from \(25\times 10^{-3} \) sec (in Part e) to \(80\times 10^{-3} \) sec (in Part f ), the regulation is improved by a factor of 3.2.
