Guest Question.

A generator is connected to an infinite bus through an external impedance of \(jx_{e}\) The generator is represented by a voltage source \(E_{g}\measuredangle \delta\) in series with a reactance \(x_{g}\).If \(E_{g}=E_{b}\) (infinite bus voltage) = 1.0,\(x_{e}=-0.5\), \(x_{g}=0.3\) (all in p.u.), for \(p_{b}\) =1.0 p.u. , find the equilibrium values of \(\delta\) , in the range of \(\left ( -\pi ,\pi \right )\). Test their stability ( \(p_{b}\) is the received power at the infinite bus). Assume infinite bus angle as zero.

Admin
Solution

The expression for the electrical power output, \(P_{e}\) is given by

\(P_{e}=\frac{E_{g}E_{b}\sin \delta }{\left ( x_{e}+ x_{g} \right )}= P_{b}\)

Substituting the values for \(E_{g}\), \(E_{b}\), \(x_{e}\) and \(x_{g}\),

\(P_{e} = -5\sin \delta\)

The power angle curve is shown if Fig. 1.. For \(P_{b} = P_{e} = 1.0\) pu., the equilibrium points are

\(\delta _{e}^{1} = -11.54^{\circ},\delta _{e}^{2} = -168.46^{\circ},\) Testing for stability,

 

\(\left.\begin{matrix} \frac{dP_{e}}{d\delta } \end{matrix}\right|_{\delta =\delta _{e}^{1}} = -5 \cos \delta _{e } < 0\)

 

\(\left.\begin{matrix} \frac{dP}{d\delta } \end{matrix}\right|_{\delta =\delta _{e}^{2}} = -5 \cos \delta _{e}^{2} > 0\)

 

Hence, \(\delta _{e}^{1}=-11.54^{\circ}\) is an unstable equilibrium point (UEP) and \(\delta _{e}^{2}=-168.46^{\circ}\) is a stable equilibrium point (SEP).
