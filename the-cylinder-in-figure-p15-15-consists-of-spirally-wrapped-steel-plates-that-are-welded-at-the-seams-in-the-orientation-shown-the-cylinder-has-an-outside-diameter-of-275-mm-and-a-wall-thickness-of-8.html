Guest Question.

The cylinder in Figure P15.15 consists of spirally wrapped steel plates that are welded at the seams in the orientation shown. The cylinder has an outside diameter of 275 mm and a wall thickness of 8 mm. The ends of the cylinder are capped by two rigid end plates. The cylinder is subjected to tension axial loads of P = 45 kN and torques of T = 60 kN-m, which are applied to the rigid end caps in the directions shown in Figure P15.15. Determine:
(a) the normal stress perpendicular to the weld seams.
(b) the shear stress parallel to the weld seams.
(c) the absolute maximum shear stress in the cylinder.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[(275 mm )^{2}-(259 mm )^{2}\right]=6,710.442 mm ^{2} \\&J=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[(275 mm )^{4}-(259 mm )^{4}\right]=119,702,540 mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Normal and shear stress magnitudes:

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma=\frac{P}{A}=\frac{(45 kN )(1,000 N / kN )}{6,710.442 mm ^{2}}=6.706 MPa ( T ) \\&\tau=\frac{T c}{J}=\frac{(60 kN – m )(275 mm / 2)(1,000 N / kN )(1,000 mm / m )}{119,702,540 mm ^{4}}=68.921 MPa\end{aligned}\)

(sense of shear stresses to be determined by inspection)

A stress element for a point on the weld seam is shown below.

 

(a) Normal stress perpendicular to the weld seams:
The normal stress perpendicular to the weld seam can be determined by Mohrâ€™s circle or the stress
transformation equations directly. From the normal stress transformation equation Eq. (12.3):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{n} &=\sigma_{x} \cos ^{2} \theta+\sigma_{y} \sin ^{2} \theta+2 \tau_{x y} \sin \theta \cos \theta \\&=(6.706 MPa ) \cos ^{2}\left(-35^{\circ}\right)+(0) \sin ^{2}\left(-35^{\circ}\right)+2(-68.921 MPa ) \sin \left(-35^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(-35^{\circ}\right) \\&=69.264 MPa ( T )=69.3 MPa ( T )\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Shear stress parallel to the weld seams:
The shear stress parallel to the weld seam can be determined from the shear stress transformation equation [Eq. (12.4)]:

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{n t} &=-\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) \sin \theta \cos \theta+\tau_{x y}\left(\cos ^{2} \theta-\sin ^{2} \theta\right) \\&=-[(6.706 MPa )-(0)] \sin \left(-35^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(-35^{\circ}\right)+(-68.921 MPa )\left[\cos ^{2}\left(-35^{\circ}\right)-\sin ^{2}\left(-35^{\circ}\right)\right] \\&=-20.422 MPa =-20.4 MPa\end{aligned}\)

 

(c) Absolute maximum shear stress:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq. (12.12):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}}{2}\right)^{2}+\tau_{x y}^{2}} \\&=\frac{(6.706)+(0)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(6.706)-(0)}{2}\right)^{2}+(-68.921)^{2}} \\&=3.353 \pm 69.002 \\\sigma_{p 1}=& 72.355 MPa \quad \text { and } \quad \sigma_{p 2}=-65.649 MPa\end{aligned}\)

The outer surface of the cylinder is in plane stress; therefore, \(\sigma_{p 3}=0\). Since \(\sigma_{p 1} \text { and } \sigma_{p 2}\) have opposite signs, the absolute maximum shear stress will be equal to the maximum in-plane shear stress:

\(\tau_{ abs \max }=\tau_{\max }=69.0 MPa\)
