Guest Question.

An electrical fitting manufacturer has found that the unit selling price P and the sales volume for one of its models of circuit breakers has the following relationship:

P = 1,00,000 – 200D.

It therefore, articulates the unit selling price to control the sales volume in accordance with the rate of production. The company’s monthly fixed costs and the unit variable costs are ₹75,000 and ₹250 respectively. How many units, the company should produce and sell to maximize the profit and what is the maximum profit?

Admin
Solution

Profit = Total revenue – Total cost

= P × D – (FC + VC)

= (1,00,000 – 200D) × D – (₹75,000 + ₹250D)

= –₹75,000 + (1,00,000 – 250) × D – 200D² for \(0 ≤ D ≤ \frac{c}{m}\)

We now take the first derivative with respect to D and equate it to zero:

\(\frac{d(Profit)}{dD} = (1,00,000 – 250) – 400D = 0 \)

The value of D that maximizes profit is

\(D^∗ = \frac{(1,00,000 – 250)}{400}\) = 249.38 \(≈\) 250 units

\((Profit)_{max}\) = -75,000 + (1,00,000 – 250)(250) – (200)(250)²

\((Profit)_{max}\) = ₹ 1,23,62,500

Figure 1.5 shows that the total revenue curve intersects the total cost line at two points. Thus at these points, total revenue is equal to the total cost and these points are called breakeven points. For a demand D, at the breakeven point

Total revenue = Total cost

cD – mD² = FC + vD

–mD² + (c – v) × D – FC = 0 (1.11)

Equation (1.11) is a quadratic equation with one unknown D, it can be solved for the breakeven points \(D_{1}\) and \(D_{2}\) which are, indeed, the two roots of the equation.

\(D_{1}= \frac{− (c − v) + \sqrt{(c − v)^{2} − 4(−m)(−FC)} }{2(−m)} \) (1.12)

and

\(D_{2}= \frac{− (c − v) – \sqrt{(c − v)^{2} − 4(−m)(−FC)} }{2(−m)} \) (1.13)
