Guest Question.

Determine the shear force and bending moment diagrams once for the depicted simply suppor-ted beam, carrying a linearly varying line load and additionally for a clam-ped support on the right and left side.

Admin
Solution

1. Simply supported beam.1. Simply supported beam.

The linearly varying load, which can be described by

\(q(x)=q_{0}\frac{x}{l},\)

yields by integration

\(V(x)=-\int{q}(x)dx=-q_{0}\frac{x^{2} }{2l}+C_{1},\)

 

\(M(x)=\int{V}(x)=-q_{0}\frac{x^{3}} {6l}+C_{1}x+C_{2}.\)

The constants follow from the support conditions:

\(M(0)=0 \rightsquigarrow C_{2}=0,\)

 

\(M(l)=0 \rightsquigarrow C_{1}=\frac{q_{0}l}{6}.\)

The shear force is obtained by

\(\underline{\underline{V(x)=\frac{q_{0}l}{6}\left[1-3\frac{x^{2} }{l^{2} } \right] } }.\)

The values of \(q_{0}l/6\) and \(q_{0}l/3\) corre-spond to the support reactions. Due to the sign convention, a negative shear force indicates a force into the upwars direction. The bending mo-ment diagram results in

\(\underline{\underline{M(x)=\frac{q_{0}lx}{6}\left[1-\frac{x^{2} }{l^{2} } \right] } }.\)

The maximum \(M_{max}\) appears at the root of the shear force: V = 0 for \( \sqrt{3} l/3=0.577 l.\)It follows:

\(M_{\max }=q_{0}\frac{\sqrt{3} }{3}l^{2}\frac{1}{6}(1-\frac{1}{3} )=\frac{\sqrt{3} }{27}q_{0}l^{2}.\)

2. Beam, clamped on the right side

\(q(x)=q_{0}\frac{x}{l},\)

 

\(V(x)=-q_{0}\frac{x^{2} }{2l}+C_{1},\)

 

\(M(x)=-q_{0}\frac{x^{3} }{6l}+C_{1}x+C_{2}.\)

Due to the left boundary conditions

\(V(0)=0 \rightsquigarrow C_{1}=0, M(0)=0 \rightsquigarrow C_{2}=0\)

we obtain

\(\underline{\underline{V(x)=-\frac{q_{0}x^{2} }{2l} } }, \underline{\underline{M(x)=-\frac{q_{0}x^{3} }{6l} } }.\)

The result can be checked by determination of the moment at the right edge by the equilibrium conditions for the complete beam as

\(\uparrow : B-\frac{1}{2}q_{0}l=0, \overset{\curvearrowleft }{B}: M_{B}+\frac{l}{3}\frac{q_{0}l}{2}=0.\)

3. Beam, clamped on the left side

\(q(x)=q_{0}\frac{x}{l}, \)

 

\(V(x)=-\frac{q_{0}x^{2} }{2l}+C_{1}, \)

 

\(M(x)=-\frac{q_{0}x^{3} }{6l}+C_{1}x+C_{2}. \)

Due to the right boundary conditions

\(V(l)=0\rightsquigarrow C_{1}=\frac{q_{0}l}{2}, \)

 

\(M(l)=0\rightsquigarrow C_{2}=\frac{q_{0}l^{2}}{6}-C_{1}l=-\frac{q_{0}l^{2}}{3},\)

we obtain

\(\underline{\underline{V(x)=\frac{q_{0}l}{2}\left[1-\frac{x^{2}}{l^{2}} \right] } }, \underline{\underline{M(x)=-\frac{q_{0}l^{2}}{6}\left[2-3\frac{x}{l}+\frac{x^{3}}{l^{3}} \right] } }.\)

To check the result, the clamping moment is obtained as

\(\overset{\curvearrowleft }{A} : -M_{A}-\frac{2l}{3}\frac{q_{0}l}{2}=0 \rightsquigarrow M_{A}=-\frac{q_{0}l^{2}}{3}.\)
