Guest Question.

Design a full hydrodynamic journal bearing with the following specification for machine tool application:
journal diameter = 75 mm
radial load = 10 kN
journal speed = 1440 rpm
minimum oil film thickness = 22.5 microns
inlet temperature = 40°C
bearing material = babbitt
Determine the length of the bearing and select a suitable oil for this application.

\( \left(\frac{l}{d}\right)=1 \quad \text { and } \quad\left(\frac{h_{o}}{c}\right)=\frac{0.0225}{0.0375}=0.6 \).

For the above mentioned values,

\( S=0.264\left(\frac{r}{c}\right) f=5.79 \quad \frac{Q}{r c n_{s} l}=3.99 \).

\( n_{s}=\frac{1440}{60}=24 rps \).

\( S=\left(\frac{r}{c}\right)^{2} \frac{\mu n_{s}}{p} \text { or } 0.264=(1000)^{2} \frac{\mu(24)}{(1.78)} \).

or \( \mu=(19.58)\left(10^{-9}\right) N – sec mm ^{2} \text { or } 19.58 cP \). (a).

From Eq. (16.23),

\( \Delta t=\frac{8.3 p( CFV )}{( FV )} \) (16.23).

\( \Delta t=\frac{8.3 p( CFV )}{( FV )}=\frac{8.3(1.78)(5.79)}{(3.99)}=21.44^{\circ} C \).

From Eq. (16.24),

\( T_{ av }=T_{i}+\left(\frac{\Delta t}{2}\right) \) (16.24).

\( T_{ av }=T_{i}+\frac{\Delta t}{2}=40+\frac{21.44}{2}=50.72^{\circ} C \) (b).

From (a) and (b), it is observed that the lubricating oil should have minimum viscosity of 19.58 cP at 50.72°C. Referring to Fig. 16.8, the viscosity of SAE-10 oil is 22 cP at 50°C. We will select SAE-10 oil for this application, which will satisfy the minimum viscosity of 19.58 cP.
