Guest Question.

CONTEXT Helium is the closest of any real gas to an ideal gas, because—as we know from trends in the periodic table—it has the smallest efective radius of any atom (smaller even than a hydrogen atom) and the highest ionization energy. As a result, it has the smallest excluded volume and the lowest polarizability because its electrons are so tightly bound to the nucleus. Argon is larger and more polarizable, which means that both its excluded volume and its inter-particle attractions are greater than for helium. Nonideality in argon is of particular interest in high-pressure electrical discharges through the gas, because argon does not require high electron energies to ionize and support an electric discharge, while at the same time it is a non-reactive gas and does not rapidly degrade in the discharge the way molecular gases such as nitrogen do. Pressures as high as 103 bar are used to generate these plasmas, extending well into the range of densities where non-ideality may be signiicant.

PROBLEM Estimate the van der Waals coeicients for helium and argon using their Lennard-Jones parameters.

Admin
Solution

The values for a are obtained by calculating the following:

\(a(L^{2}bar mol^{-2} )= N^{2}_{A}(\frac{16\pi \varepsilon R^{3}_{LJ} }{9} ) (1.381\cdot 10^{-23} JK^{-1} )(1000 L m^{-3} )^{2}\)

 

\(\times (10^{-10} m \mathring{A}^{-1} )^{3} (10^{-5} bar pa^{-1} )\)

 

\(= 2.797\cdot 10^{-4} \varepsilon (K)R_{LJ}(\mathring{A} )^{3}\)

and the values for b similarly:

\(b(L mol^{-1} )= N_{A}(\frac{2\pi \varepsilon R^{3}_{LJ} }{3} )(1000 L m^{-3} )(10^{-10} m \mathring{A} ^{-1} )^{3}\)

 

\(= 1.26\cdot 10^{-4} R_{LJ}(\mathring{A} )^{3}\)

Using \(\varepsilon /k_{B} = 10 K\) and \(R_{LJ}= 2.85 \mathring{A}\) for He, we predict a = 0.048 L² bar mol-² and b = 0.0217 L mol-¹.
These are comparable to the values 0.0346 L² bar mol-² and 0.0238 L mol-¹ reported in Table 4.2. Using \(\varepsilon /k_{B} = 120 K \) and \( R_{LJ}= 3.44 \mathring{A}\)RLJ = 3.44 Å for Ar, we predict a = 1.366 L² bar mol-² and b = 0.0513 L mol-¹, and the measured values are 1.355 L² bar mol-² and 0.03201 L mol-¹.
