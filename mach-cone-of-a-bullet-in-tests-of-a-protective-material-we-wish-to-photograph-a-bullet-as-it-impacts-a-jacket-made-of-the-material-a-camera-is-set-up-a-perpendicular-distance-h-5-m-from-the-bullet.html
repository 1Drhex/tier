MACH CONE OF A BULLET

In tests of a protective material, we wish to photograph a bullet as it impacts a jacket made of the material. A camera is set up a perpendicular distance \(h = 5\) m from the bullet trajectory. We wish to determine the perpendicular distance \(d\) from the target plane at which the camera must be placed such that the sound of the bullet will trigger the camera at the impact time. Note: The bullet speed is measured to be 550 m/s; the delay time of the camera is 0.005 s.

Admin
Solution

Find: Location of camera for capturing impact image.
Solution:
The correct value of \(d\) is that for which the bullet hits the target \(0.005 \mathrm{~s}\) before the Mach wave reaches the camera. We must first find the Mach number of the bullet; then we can find the Mach angle; finally, we can use basic trigonometry to find \(d\).
Assuming sea level conditions, from Table A.3 we have \(T=288 \mathrm{~K}\). Hence Eq. 12.18 yields

\(\begin{aligned}c &=\sqrt{k R T} \\\quad\\c &=\sqrt{1.4 \times 287 \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}} \times 288 \mathrm{~K} \times \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}}=340 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\end{aligned}\)

 

Then we can find the Mach number,

\(M=\frac{V}{c}=\frac{550 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}}{340 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}}=1.62\)

 

From Eq. 12.19 we can next find the Mach angle,

\(\alpha=\sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{M}\right)=\sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{1.62}\right)=38.2^{\circ}\)

 

The distance \(x\) traveled by the bullet while the Mach wave reaches the camera is then

\(x=\frac{h}{\tan (\alpha)}=\frac{5 \mathrm{~m}}{\tan \left(38.2^{\circ}\right)}=6.35 \mathrm{~m}\)

 

Finally, we must add to this the time traveled by the bullet while the camera is operating, which is \(0.005 \mathrm{~s} \times 550 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\),

\(\begin{aligned}&d=0.005 \mathrm{~s} \times \frac{550 \mathrm{~m}}{\mathrm{~s}}+6.35 \mathrm{~m}=2.75 \mathrm{~m}+6.35 \mathrm{~m} \\\quad\\&d=9.10 \mathrm{~m} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad d}\end{aligned}\)
