Guest Question.

ON A CONTRACTION IN A PIPE

A pipe 30 cm in diameter carries water (\(10^{\circ} C\), 250 kPa) at a rate of 0.707 \(m ^{3} / s\). The pipe contracts to a diameter of 20 cm. The head loss through the contraction is given by

\(h_{L}=0.1 \frac{V_{2}^{2}}{2 g}\)

where \(V_{2}\) is the velocity in the 20 cm pipe. What horizontal force is required to hold the transition in place? Assume \(\alpha_{1}=\alpha_{2}=1\).

Problem Definition

Situation: Water flows through a contraction in a round pipe.

Find: The horizontal force \(F_{x}\) (newtons) required to hold the contraction stationary.

Sketch:

Assumptions: \(\alpha_{1}=\alpha_{2}=1\).

Properties: Water (10°C), Table A.5: \(\gamma=9810 N / m ^{3}\).

Plan

1. Develop an equation for \(F_{x}\) by applying the momentum equation (6.6).

\(\sum F =\frac{d}{d t} \int_{ cv } \rho v d V+\sum_{ cs } \dot{m}_{o} v _{o}-\sum_{ cs } \dot{m}_{i} v _{i}\) (6.6)

2. Develop an equation for \(p_{2}\) by applying the energy equation (7.29).

\(\left(\frac{p_{1}}{\gamma}+\alpha_{1} \frac{\bar{V}_{1}^{2}}{2 g}+z_{1}\right)+h_{p}=\left(\frac{p_{2}}{\gamma}+\alpha_{2} \frac{\bar{V}_{2}^{2}}{2 g}+z_{2}\right)+h_{t}+h_{L}\) (7.29)

3. Calculate \(p_{2}\).

4. Calculate \(F_{x}\).

Admin
Solution

1. Momentum equation (horizontal direction)

• General equation

 

\(\sum F =\frac{d}{d t} \int_{ cv } \rho v d V +\sum_{ cs } \dot{m}_{o} v _{o}-\sum_{c s} \dot{m}_{i} v _{i}\)

 

• Force terms (see force diagram)

 

\(\sum F_{x}=p_{1} A_{1}+F_{x}-p_{2} A_{2}\).

 

• Momentum accumulation is zero because flow is steady.

• Momentum efflux is

 

\(\sum_{ cs } \dot{m}_{o} v _{o}-\sum_{c s} \dot{m}_{i} v _{i}=\left(\dot{m} V_{2}-\dot{m} V_{1}\right) \hat{i}\)

• Substitute force and momentum terms into the momentum equation.

 

\(p_{1} A_{1}-p_{2} A_{2}+F_{x}=\dot{m} V_{2}-\dot{m} V_{1}\)

 

\(F_{x}=\rho Q\left(V_{2}-V_{1}\right)+p_{2} A_{2}-p_{1} A_{1}\)

 

2. Energy equation (from section 1 to section 2)

• Since \(\alpha_{1}=\alpha_{2}=1, z_{1}=z_{2}\), and \(h_{p}=h_{t}=0\), Eq.
(7.29) simplifies to

 

\(\frac{p_{1}}{\gamma}+\frac{V_{1}^{2}}{2 g}=\frac{p_{2}}{\gamma}+\frac{V_{2}^{2}}{2 g}+h_{L}\)

 

\(p_{2}=p_{1}-\gamma\left(\frac{V_{2}^{2}}{2 g}-\frac{V_{1}^{2}}{2 g}+h_{L}\right)\)

 

3. Pressure at section 2

• Find velocities using the flow rate equation.

 

\(V_{1}=\frac{Q}{A_{1}}=\frac{0.707 m ^{3} / s }{(\pi / 4) \times(0.3 m )^{2}}=10\) m/s

 

\(V_{2}=\frac{Q}{A_{2}}=\frac{0.707 m ^{3} / s }{(\pi / 4) \times(0.2 m )^{2}}=22.5\) m/s

 

• Calculate head loss.

 

\(h_{L}=\frac{0.1 V_{2}^{2}}{2 g}=\frac{0.1 \times(22.5 m / s )^{2}}{2 \times\left(9.81 m / s ^{2}\right)}=2.58\) m

 

• Calculate pressure.

 

\(p_{2}=p_{1}-\gamma\left(\frac{V_{2}^{2}}{2 g}\right)-\frac{V_{1}^{2}}{2 g}+h_{L}\)

 

\(=250 kPa -9.81 kN / m ^{3}\)

 

\(\times\left(\frac{(22.5 m / s )^{2}}{2\left(9.81 m / s ^{2}\right)}-\frac{(10 m / s )^{2}}{2\left(9.81 m / s ^{2}\right)}+2.58 m \right)\)

 

= 21.6 kPa

 

4. Calculate \(F_{x}\).

 

\(F_{x}=\rho Q\left(V_{2}-V_{1}\right)+p_{2} A_{2}-p_{1} A_{1}\)

 

\(=\left(1000 kg / m ^{3}\right)\left(0.707 m ^{3} / s \right)(22.5-10)( m / s )\)

 

\(+(21,600 Pa )\left(\frac{\pi(0.2 m )^{2}}{4}\right)-(250,000 Pa )\)

 

\(\times\left(\frac{\pi(0.3 m )^{2}}{4}\right)\)

 

\(=(8837+677-17,670) N =-8.16\) KN

 

\(F_{x}=8,16\) kN applied in the negative x direction
