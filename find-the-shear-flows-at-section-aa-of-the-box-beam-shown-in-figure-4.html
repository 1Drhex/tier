Solution

The moment of inertia of the cross section at AA about the neutral axis is

\(I = 2 (2+1+1) \times 7.5^{2} = 450 in^{4}\)

The bending streasee at section AA are

\(f = \frac{M\cdot y}{I} = \frac{400,000 \times 7.5}{450} = 6666.67 psi\)

The horizontal components of the forces acting on the 2-sq in. stringers are 13333,33 lb, and the forces on the 1-sq in. stringers are 6666.67 lb, as shown in Fig. 6.36. The vertical components are obtained by multiplying the forces by the tangents of the angles between stringers and the horizontal, and are also shown in Fig. 6.36. The sum of the vertical components of forces on all

stringers \(V_{f}\) is 4,000 lb, and the remaining shear \(V_{w}\), of 4,000 lb is resisted by the shear flows in the webs. If one of the upper webs is cut, as shown in Fig. 6.37, the hear flows in the webs may be obtained from the equation

\(q= \frac{V_{w}}{I}\int y dA\)

where the integral represents the moment of the areas between the cut web and the web under consideration. The change in bending stress on a stringer between two cross sections 1 in. apart is \(V_{w}.y/I\) when the effect of taper is considered, and the change in axial load on a stringer of area \(A_{f}\) is

\(\Delta P = \frac{V_{w}}{I} y A_{f}\)
