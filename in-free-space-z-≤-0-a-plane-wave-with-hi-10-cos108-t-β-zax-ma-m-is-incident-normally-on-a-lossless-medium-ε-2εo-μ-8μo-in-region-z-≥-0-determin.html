Guest Question.

In free space \((z\leq 0)\), a plane wave with

\(H_{i}=10\cos(10^{8}t-\beta z)a_{x} mA/m\)

is incident normally on a lossless medium \((\varepsilon =2\varepsilon_{o}, \mu=8\mu_{o})\) in region \(z\geq 0\). Determine the reflected wave \(H_{r}, E_{r}\) and the transmitted wave \(H_{t}, E_{t}\).

Admin
Solution

This problem can be solved in two different ways.

Method 1: Consider the problem as illustrated in Figure 10.15. For free space

\(\beta_{1}=\frac{\omega}{c}=\frac{10^{8}}{3\times 10^{8}}=\frac{1}{3}\)

\(\eta_{1}=\eta_{o}=120\pi\)

For the lossless dielectric medium

\(\beta_{2}=\omega\sqrt{\mu\varepsilon}=\omega\sqrt{\mu_{o}\varepsilon_{o}}\sqrt{\mu_{r}\varepsilon_{r}}=\frac{\omega}{c}\cdot(4)=4\beta_{1}=\frac{4}{3}\)

\(\eta_{2}=\sqrt{\frac{\mu}{\varepsilon}}=\sqrt{\frac{\mu_{o}}{\varepsilon_{o}}}\sqrt{\frac{\mu_{r}}{\varepsilon_{r}}}=2\eta_{o}\)

Given that

\(H_{i}=10\cos(10^{8}t-\beta_{1}z)a_{x} mA/m\)

we expect that

\(E_{i}=E_{io}\cos(10^{8}t-\beta_{1}z)a_{E_{i}}\)

where

\(a_{E_{i}}=a_{H_{i}}\times a_{k_{i}}=a_{x}\times a_{z}=-a_{y}\)

and

\(E_{io}=\eta_{1}H_{io}=10\eta_{o}\)

Hence

\(E_{i}=-10\eta_{o}\cos(10^{8}t-\beta_{1}z)a_{y} mV/m\)

Now

\(\frac{E_{ro}}{E_{io}}=\Gamma=\frac{\eta_{2}-\eta_{1}}{\eta_{2}+\eta_{1}}=\frac{2\eta_{o}-\eta_{o}}{2\eta_{o}+\eta_{o}}=\frac{1}{3}\)

\(E_{ro}=\frac{1}{3}E_{io}\)

Thus

\(E_{r}=-\frac{10}{3}\eta_{o}\cos\left(10^{8}t+\frac{1}{3}z\right)a_{y} mV/m\)

from which we easily obtain \(H_{r}\) as

\(H_{r}=-\frac{10}{3}\cos\left(10^{8}t+\frac{1}{3}z\right)a_{x} mA/m\)

Similarly

\(\frac{E_{to}}{E_{io}}=\tau=1+\Gamma=\frac{4}{3}\) or \(E_{to}=\frac{4}{3}E_{io}\)

Thus

\(E_{t}=E_{to}\cos(10^{8}t-\beta_{2}z)a_{E_{t}}\)

where \(a_{E_{t}}=a_{E_{i}}=-a_{y}\). Hence

\(E_{t}=-\frac{40}{3}\eta_{o}\cos\left(10^{8}t-\frac{4}{3}z\right)a_{y} mV/m\)

from which we obtain

\(H_{t}=\frac{20}{3}\cos\left(10^{8}t-\frac{4}{3}z\right)a_{x} mA/m\)

Method 2: Alternatively, we can obtain \(H_{r}\) and \(H_{t}\) directly from \(H_{i}\) by using

\(\frac{H_{ro}}{H_{io}}=-\Gamma\) and \(\frac{H_{to}}{H_{io}}=\tau\frac{\eta_{1}}{\eta_{2}}\)

Thus

\(H_{ro}=-\frac{1}{3}H_{io}=-\frac{10}{3}\)

\(H_{to}=\frac{4}{3}\frac{\eta_{o}}{2\eta_{o}}\cdot H_{io}=\frac{2}{3}H_{io}=\frac{20}{3}\)

and

\(H_{r}=-\frac{10}{3}\cos(10^{8}t+\beta_{1}z)a_{x} mA/m\)

\(H_{t}=\frac{20}{3}\cos(10^{8}t-\beta_{2}z)a_{x} mA/m\)

as obtained by Method 1.

Notice that the boundary conditions at \(z=0\), namely

\(E_{i}(0)+E_{r}(0)=E_{t}(0)=-\frac{40}{3}\eta_{o}\cos(10^{8}t)a_{y}\)

and

\(H_{i}(0)+H_{r}(0)=H_{t}(0)=\frac{20}{3}\cos(10^{8}t)a_{x}\)

are satisfied. The boundary conditions can always be used to cross-check E and H.
