Consider the nozzle in . What is the second law efficiency for the nozzle?
A nozzle in a high pressure liquid water sprayer has an area of 0.5 \(cm ^{2}\). It receives water at 350 kPa, 20°C and the exit pressure is 100 kPa. Neglect the inlet kinetic energy and assume a nozzle isentropic efficiency of 85%. Find the ideal nozzle exit velocity and the actual nozzle mass flow rate.

Admin
Solution

C.V. Nozzle. Liquid water is incompressible v ≈ constant, no work, no heat transfer => Bernoulli Eq.7.17

\(\frac{1}{2} V _{ ex }^{2}-0= v \left( P _{ i }- P _{ e }\right)=0.001002(350-100)=0.2505 kJ / kg\)

 

\(V _{ ex }=\sqrt{2 \times 0.2505 \times 1000 J / kg }=22.38 m s ^{-1}\)

 

This was the ideal nozzle now we can do the actual nozzle, Eq. 7.30

\(\frac{1}{2} V _{\text {ex ac }}^{2}=\eta \frac{1}{2} V _{\text {ex }}^{2}=0.85 \times 0.2505=0.2129 kJ / kg\)

The second law efficiency is the actual nozzle compared to a reversible process between the inlet and actual exit state. However no work so the reversible exit state (with \(P _{ e }, T _{ ex ac }\)) then must have the reversible possible kinetic energy.

Energy actual nozzle: \(h _{ i }+0= h _{ e }+\frac{1}{2} V _{\text {ex ac }}^{2}\) same Z, no q and no w.

The reversible process has zero change in exergies from Eq.8.38 as

\(\psi_{i}=\psi_{ e }= h _{ i }+0- T _{ o } s _{ i }= h _{ e }+\frac{1}{2} V _{\text {ex rev }}^{2}- T _{o} s _{ e }\)

 

\(\frac{1}{2} V _{\text {ex rev }}^{2}= h _{ i }- h _{ e }+ T _{ o }\left( s _{ e }- s _{ i }\right)=\frac{1}{2} V _{ ex ac }^{2}+ T _{ o } s _{ gen }\)

 

We can not get properties for these states accurately enough by interpolation so use constant specific heat and incompressibility.

\(h _{ ex ac }= h _{ i }-\frac{1}{2} V _{ ex ac }^{2}= u _{ ex ac }+ P _{ e } v = u _{ i }+ P _{ i } v -\frac{1}{2} V _{ ex ac }^{2}\)

 

\(u _{ ex ac }- u _{ i }= P _{ i } v – P _{ e } v -\frac{1}{2} V _{ ex ac }^{2}= C \left( T _{ ex ac }- T _{ i }\right)\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}T _{ ex ac } &= T _{ i }+\left( P _{ i } v – P _{ e } v -\frac{1}{2} V _{ ex ac }^{2}\right) / C \\&=20+[(350-100) 0.001002-0.2129] / 4.18=20.01^{\circ} C\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{1}{2} V _{\text {ex rev }}^{2} &=\frac{1}{2} V _{\text {ex ac }}^{2}+ T _{o} s _{\text {gen }}=0.2129+293.15 \times 4.18 \ln \frac{293.16}{293.15} \\&=0.2547 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\eta_{I I}=0.2129 / 0.2547=0.84\)
