Guest Question.

Solve the flow field at the exit of an underexpanded two-dimensional nozzle with air flow, shown in Figure 9.18. At the nozzle exit, \(M_{A}=1.435 \text { and } \theta_{A}=0^{\circ}\).

Admin
Solution

Because of symmetry, the streamline along the axis of the nozzle must be straight, and may be replaced by a solid wall, as shown in the figure. Given \(M_{A}=1.435 \text { and } \theta_{A}=0 . \text { For } M_{A}=1.435\), from the isentropic table, we get \(ŒΩ_{A}=10^{\circ} \text { and } p_{A} / p_{0}=0.299 .\) In this example, it is assumed that the entire expansion is taking place through a single expansion wave.

From our discussion on the Prandtl‚ÄìMeyer function (Section 4.9), we know that v and ùúÉ are connected by the relation \(ŒΩ=\pm \theta\), where the plus sign holds for a right-running characteristic and the minus sign holds for a left-running characteristic.

Now, the entire expansion from region A to region B is taking place across a left-running characteristic and, therefore, \(\theta_{B}=-ŒΩ_{A}=-10^{\circ}\).

Also, by Eq. (4.49a), for the expansion

\(ŒΩ_{n}=ŒΩ_{n-1}+\left|\theta_{n}-\theta_{n-1}\right| \text { (expansion) }\) (4.49a)

\(ŒΩ_{B}=ŒΩ_{A}+\left|\theta_{B}-\theta_{A}\right|=10+10=20^{\circ}\)

 

With the value of the Prandtl‚ÄìMeyer function, we can get the flow properties. For \(ŒΩ_{B}=20^{\circ}\), from the isentropic table,

\(M_{B}=1.775, \quad p_{B} / p_{0}=0.181\)

After the expansion, the pressure ratio in region B is 0.181. At the free boundary, the pressure outside the boundary must be equal to \(p_{B}\). Therefore, the pressure ratio at the free boundary for the given exit pressure \(p_{A}\) is

\(\frac{p}{p_{A}}=\frac{p_{B} / p_{0}}{p_{A} / p_{0}}=\frac{0.181}{0.299}=0.605\)

 

Following the above procedure, we can get the flow field as given in Table 9.2. The above solution can yield the wave and deflection angles also. The mean Mach angle \(\bar{\mu}\) for an expansion fan with \(\mu_{1}\) and \(\mu_{2}\) as the Mach angles at the beginning and end of the fan is given by

\(\bar{\mu}=\frac{\mu_{1}+\mu_{2}}{2}\)

 

Similarly, the mean deflection angle \(\bar{\theta}\) is given by

\(\bar{\theta}=\frac{\theta_{1}+\theta_{2}}{2}\)

 

With the above relations, for the present flow field, we have

\(\begin{array}{lcr} \text { Wave } & \bar{\mu} & \bar{\theta} \\ 1-2 & 39.25^{\circ} & -5^{\circ} \\ 2-3 & 31.1^{\circ} & 5^{\circ} \\ 3-4 & 31.1^{\circ} & 5^{\circ} \\ 4-5 & 39.25^{\circ} & -5^{\circ} \end{array}\)

 

Note: in the above problem, all the regions are assumed to be simple regions. There is a \(10^{\circ}\) deflection of the jet boundary as it leaves the nozzle.
