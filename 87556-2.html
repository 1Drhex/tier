Guest Question.

The table below shows present worth calculations of the costs associated with using a presently owned machine (defender) and a possible replacement (challenger) for different numbers of years. Determine when the defender should be replaced using an interest rate of \(10\)% per year and a \(5\)-year study period. Show solutions \(( a )\) by hand and \(( b )\) by spreadsheet.

AW If Kept Stated
Number of Years, $ per Year
Number of Years Kept Used Defender Challenger
1 -36,000 -89,000
2 -75,000 -96,000
3 -125,000 -102,000
4 -166,000 -113,000
5 -217,000 -149,000
Admin
Solution

\((a)\) \(PW C\) for \(5\) years \(= \)$\(-149,000\)

 

\(PW D\) for \(1\) year, \(C\) for \(4\) years \(= -36,000 – 113,000(P/F,10\)%\(,1)\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad\quad = -36,000 – 113,000(0.9091)\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad =\) $\(-138,728\)

 

\(PW D\) for \(2\) years, \(C\) for \(3\) years \(= -75,000 – 102,000(P/F,10\)%\(,2)\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad = -75,000 – 102,000(0.8264)\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad =\) $\(-159,293\)

 

\(PW D\) for \(3\) years, \(C\) for \(2\) years \(= -125,000 – 96,000(P/F,10\)%\(,3)\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad = -125,000 – 96,000(0.7513)\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad = \)$\(-197,125\)

 

 

\(PW D\) for \(4\) years, \(C\) for \(1\) years \(= -166,000 – 89,000(P/F,10\)%\(,4)\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad = -166,000 – 89,000(0.6830)\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad =\) $\(-226,787\)

 

\(PW D\) for \(5 \) years \(=\) $\(-217,000\)

 

Lowest \(PW\) is $\(-138,728\). Therefore, keep the defender \(1\) year and then replace it with the challenger

 

\((b)\) The \(PW\) values are placed in the year cell prior to when the year starts for challenger. Lowest \(PW =\) $\(-138,727\) for option \(E\) (defender for \(1\) year, followed by challenger for \(4\) years)
