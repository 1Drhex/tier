Guest Question.

The temperature of the inner side of a furnace wall is 640°C and that of on other side is 240°C and it is exposed to an atmosphere at 40°C. In order to reduce the heat loss from the furnace, its wall thickness is increased by 100%.
Calculate the percentage decrease in the heat loss due to increase in wall thickness. Assume no change in properties except temperature.

Admin
Solution

Given : Furnace wall with
\(L_1 = L, L_2 = 2L_1\)

\(T_1 = 640°C, T_2 = 240°C\)

\(T_∞ = 40°C.\)
To find :
(i) Heat flow with original wall thickness.
(ii) Heat flow with change in the wall thickness.
(iii) Percentage change in heat flow.
Assumptions :
(i) One dimensional steady state heat flow.
(ii) No contact thermal resistance at interfaces.
(iii) Constant properties.

Analysis : From thermal network, Fig. 3.16 (b) we have
\(Q_1 =\frac {kA}{L_1}(T_1 – T_2) = hA(T_2 – T_∞)\)

or

\(\frac{k}{L_1} × (640 – 240) = h (240 – 40)\)

 

or \(400 × \frac{k}{L_1} = 200 h \) or \(L_1 = 2\frac{2k}{h} …(i)\)

Also \(Q_1 = \frac{T_1 – T_{∞}}{\frac{L_1}{kA} +\frac{1}{hA}}\)
\(=\frac{(T_1 – T_{∞})}{\frac{3}{hA}}= 0.333 h A(T_1 – T_∞) …(ii)\)

When wall thickness is increased by 100%, then refer Fig. 3.16 (c) thermal network, we have
\(Q_2 = \frac{T_1 – T_{∞}}{\frac{L_2}{kA} +\frac{1}{hA}}=\frac{T_1 – T_{∞}}{\frac{2L_1}{kA} +\frac{1}{hA}}\)

 

\(\frac{T_1 – T_{∞}}{\frac{2 \times 2k}{h kA} +\frac{1}{hA}} = 0.2 hA (T_1 – T_∞) …(iii)\)
Change in heat flow

 

\(Q_1 – Q_2 = 0.333 hA(T_1 – T_∞) – 0.2 hA(T_1 – T_∞)\)
\(= 0.1333 hA(T_1 – T_∞)\)
% decrease in heat flow
\(= \frac{0.1333 hA(T_1 – T_∞)}{0.333 hA(T_1 – T_∞)} × 100\)
= 40%.
