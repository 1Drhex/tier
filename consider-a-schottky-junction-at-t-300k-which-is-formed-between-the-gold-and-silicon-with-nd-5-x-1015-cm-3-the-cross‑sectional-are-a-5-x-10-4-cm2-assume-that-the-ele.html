Guest Question.

Consider a Schottky junction at T = 300°K which is formed between the gold and silicon with \(N_{D}=5\times 10^{15} cm^{-3}\) . The cross‑sectional are \(A=5\times 10^{-4} cm^{2}\) . Assume that the electron affinity and effective density of states at the conduction band of silicon are \(e\chi _{s}\) = 4.01 eV and \(N_{C}=2.8\times 10^{19} cm^{-3}\) respectively, and work function of the gold is e\(V_{m}\) = 5.1 eV.

(a) Find the energy barrier \(E_{Bn}\) and Schottky barrier height \(V_{Bn}\) at the junction.

(b) Determine the potential difference \(V_{n}\) between the conduction band and Fermi level of the silicon in the bulk region and contact potential \(V_{o}\) across the Schottky junction.

(c) Calculate the depletion width \(W_{s}\) and transition capacitance \(C_{Ts}\) for a reverse bias voltage of 3V. Also find the value of the electric field \(\varepsilon _{junc}\) at the metal‑semiconductor junction for the above voltage

Admin
Solution

(a) Using Eq. (5.84), \(r_{d}=\frac{dV}{dI}|_{I=I_{Q}} \approx \frac{2V_{T}}{I_{Q}} \) , the energy barrier seen by an electron in the metal side is given by

\(E_{Bn}=(5.1 -4.01)eV =1.09 eV=1.74\times 10^{-19}J\)

The Schottky barrier height \(V_{Bn}\) is given by

\(V_{Bn}=\frac{E_{Bn}}{e} =\frac{1.74\times 10^{-19}J}{1.60\times 10^{-19}C} =1.09\) V

Note that \(V_{Bn}\) can also be directly obtained from the numerical value of \(E_{Bn}\) = 1.09 eV.

(b) Using (5.88) , \(V_{n}=\left\lgroup\frac{E_{C}-E_{Fs}}{e} \right\rgroup =\frac{\overline{k}T }{e} 1n\left\lgroup\frac{N_{C}}{N_{D}} \right\rgroup \) , \(V_{n}\) is obtained as

\(V_{n}=(0.0259 V)1n\left\lgroup\frac{2.8\times 10^{19}}{5\times 10^{15}} \right\rgroup =0.22\) V

The contact potential \(V_{o}\) can be obtained from Eq. (5.89) , \(V_{o} =V_{Bn}-\frac{\overline{k}T }{e} 1n\left\lgroup\frac{N_{C}}{N_{D}} \right\rgroup \) , as

\(V_{o}\)= 1.09 – 0.22 = 0.87 V

(c) Using V = – 3V, \(\epsilon _{s}=1.04\times 10^{-12}\) F/cm for silicon and \(N_{D}=5\times 10^{15} cm^{-3}\) in Eq. (5.90), \(W_{s}=\sqrt{\frac{2\in _{s}(V_{0}-V)}{eN_{D}} } \) , the depletion width extended into the semiconductor side is given by

\(W_{s}=\sqrt{\frac{2\times (1.04\times 10^{12} F/cm)\times (3.87 V)}{(1.60\times10^{19}C)\times (5\times 10^{15}cm^{-3}) } }=1.0\times 10^{-4} \) cm

The transition capacitance is obtained using Eq. (5.92) , \(C_{Ts}=\frac{\in _{s}A}{W_{s}}=C_{Ts0}\left\lgroup1-\frac{V}{V_{o}} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{1}{2} } \) , as

\(C_{Ts} =\frac{(1.04\times 10^{12} F/cm)\times (5\times 10^{-4}cm^{2})}{1.0\times 10^{-4}cm}=5.2\times 10^{-12}F =5.2 pF \)

The electric field at the Schottky junction can be determined from Eq. (5.91) , \(\varepsilon _{junc}=\sqrt{\frac{2eN_{D}}{\in _{s}} (V_{0}-V)} \) , as

\(\left|\varepsilon _{junc}\right| =\sqrt{\frac{2\times (1.60\times 10^{-19}C)\times (5\times 10^{15}cm^{-3})\times (3.87 V)}{(1.04\times 10^{-12} F/cm)} } =7.72\times 10^{4} \) V/cm
