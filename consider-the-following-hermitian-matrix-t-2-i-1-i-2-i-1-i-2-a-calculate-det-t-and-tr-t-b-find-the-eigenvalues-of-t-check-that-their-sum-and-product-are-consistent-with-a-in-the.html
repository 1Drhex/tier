Guest Question.

Consider the following hermitian matrix:

\( T =\left(\begin{array}{ccc} 2 & i & 1 \\ -i & 2 & i \\ 1 & -i & 2 \end{array}\right) \).

(a) Calculate det (T) and Tr (T).

(b) Find the eigenvalues of T. Check that their sum and product are consistent with (a), in the sense of Equation A.93. Write down the diagonalized version of T.

(c) Find the eigenvectors of T. Within the degenerate sector, construct two linearly independent eigenvectors (it is this step that is always possible for a hermitian matrix, but not for an arbitrary matrixâ€”contrast ). Orthogonalize them, and check that both are orthogonal to the third. Normalize all three eigenvectors.

(d) Construct the unitary matrix S that diagonalizes T, and show explicitly that the similarity transformation using S reduces T to the appropriate diagonal form.

Admin
Solution

(a)

\( \operatorname{det}( T )=8-1-1-2-2-2=0 . \quad \operatorname{Tr}( T )=2+2+2=6 \).

(b)

\( \left|\begin{array}{ccc} (2-\lambda) & i & 1 \\ -i & (2-\lambda) & i \\ 1 & -i & (2-\lambda) \end{array}\right|=(2-\lambda)^{3}-1-1-(2-\lambda)-(2-\lambda)-(2-\lambda)=8-12 \lambda+6 \lambda^{2}-\lambda^{3}-8+3 \lambda=0 \).

\( -\lambda^{3}+6 \lambda^{2}-9 \lambda=-\lambda\left(\lambda^{2}-6 \lambda+9\right)=-\lambda(\lambda-3)^{2}=0 . \quad \lambda_{1}=0, \lambda_{2}=\lambda_{3}=3 . \)

\( \lambda_{1}+\lambda_{2}+\lambda_{3}=6=\operatorname{Tr}( T ) . \quad V \quad \lambda_{1} \lambda_{2} \lambda_{3}=0=\operatorname{det}( T ) . \quad V \quad \text { Diagonal form: }\left(\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 \end{array}\right) \).

(c)

\( \left(\begin{array}{ccc} 2 & i & 1 \\ -i & 2 & i \\ 1 & -i & 2 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} \alpha \\ \beta \\ \gamma \end{array}\right)=0 \Longrightarrow\left\{\begin{array}{l} 2 \alpha+i \beta+\gamma=0 \\ -i \alpha+2 \beta+i \gamma=0 \Longrightarrow \alpha+2 i \beta-\gamma=0 \end{array}\right\} \).

Add the two equations: \( 3 \alpha+3 i \beta=0 \Longrightarrow \beta=i \alpha ; \quad 2 \alpha-\alpha+\gamma=0 \Longrightarrow \gamma=-\alpha \).

\( a ^{(1)}=\left(\begin{array}{c} \alpha \\ i \alpha \\ -\alpha \end{array}\right) . \quad \text { Normalizing: }|\alpha|^{2}+|\alpha|^{2}+|\alpha|^{2}=1 \Longrightarrow \alpha=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} . \quad a ^{(1)}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\left(\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ i \\ -1 \end{array}\right) \).

\( \left(\begin{array}{ccc} 2 & i & 1 \\ -i & 2 & i \\ 1 & -i & 2 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} \alpha \\ \beta \\ \gamma \end{array}\right)=3\left(\begin{array}{l} \alpha \\ \beta \\ \gamma \end{array}\right) \Longrightarrow\left\{\begin{array}{l} 2 \alpha+i \beta+\gamma=3 \alpha \Rightarrow-\alpha+i \beta+\gamma=0 \\ -i \alpha+2 \beta+i \gamma=3 \beta \Longrightarrow \alpha-i \beta-\gamma=0 \\ \alpha-i \beta+2 \gamma=3 \gamma \quad \Longrightarrow \alpha-i \beta-\gamma=0 \end{array}\right. \).

The three equations are redundant – there is only one condition here: \( \alpha-i \beta-\gamma=0 \). We could pick \( \gamma=0, \beta=-i \alpha, \text { or } \beta=0, \gamma=\alpha \). Then

\( a _{0}^{(2)}=\left(\begin{array}{c} \alpha \\ -i \alpha \\ 0 \end{array}\right) ; \quad a _{0}^{(3)}=\left(\begin{array}{c} \alpha \\ 0 \\ \alpha \end{array}\right) \).

But these are not orthogonal, so we use the Gram-Schmidt procedure (); first normalize \( a _{0}^{(2)} \):

\( a ^{(2)}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -i \\ 0 \end{array}\right) \).

\( a ^{(2)^{\dagger}} a _{0}^{(3)}=\frac{\alpha}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{ll} 1 & i & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right)=\frac{\alpha}{\sqrt{2}} . \quad \text { So } \quad a _{0}^{(3)}-\left( a ^{(2)^{\dagger}} a _{0}^{(3)}\right) a ^{(2)}=\alpha\left(\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right)-\frac{\alpha}{2}\left(\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -i \\ 0 \end{array}\right)=\alpha\left(\begin{array}{c} 1 / 2 \\ i / 2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) \).

\( \text { Normalize: } \quad|\alpha|^{2}\left(\frac{1}{4}+\frac{1}{4}+1\right)=\frac{3}{2}|\alpha|^{2}=1 \quad \Longrightarrow \quad \alpha=\sqrt{\frac{2}{3}} \text {. } \)

\( a ^{(3)}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}\left(\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ i \\ 2 \end{array}\right) \).

Check orthogonality:

\( a ^{(1)^{\dagger}} a ^{(2)}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}(1-i-1)\left(\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -i \\ 0 \end{array}\right)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}(1-1+0)=0 \).

\( a ^{(1)^{\dagger}} a ^{(3)}=\frac{1}{3 \sqrt{2}}(1-i-1)\left(\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ i \\ 2 \end{array}\right)=\frac{1}{3 \sqrt{2}}(1+1-2)=0 \).

(d) \( S ^{-1} \) is the matrix whose columns are the eigenvectors of T (Eq. A.81):

\( \left( S ^{-1}\right)_{i j}=\left( a ^{(j)}\right)_{i} \) (A.81).

\( S ^{-1}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}\left(\begin{array}{crr} \sqrt{2} & \sqrt{3} & 1 \\ \sqrt{2} i & -\sqrt{3} & i i \\ -\sqrt{2} & 0 & 2 \end{array}\right) ; \quad S =\left( S ^{-1}\right)^{\dagger}= \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}\left(\begin{array}{ccc} \sqrt{2} & -\sqrt{2} i & -\sqrt{2} \\ \sqrt{3} & \sqrt{3} i & 0 \\ 1 & -i & 2 \end{array}\right) \).

\( STS ^{-1}=\frac{1}{6}\left(\begin{array}{ccc} \sqrt{2} & -\sqrt{2} i & -\sqrt{2} \\ \sqrt{3} & \sqrt{3} i & 0 \\ 1 & -i & 2 \end{array}\right) \underbrace{\left(\begin{array}{ccc} 2 & i & 1 \\ -i & 2 & i \\ 1 & -i & 2 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{ccc} \sqrt{2} & \sqrt{3} & 1 \\ \sqrt{2} i & -\sqrt{3} & i & i \\ -\sqrt{2} & 0 & 2 \end{array}\right)}_{\left(\begin{array}{ccc} 0 & 3 \sqrt{3} & 3 \\ 0 & -3 \sqrt{3} & i & 3 i \\ 0 & 0 & 6 \end{array}\right)}=\frac{1}{6}\left(\begin{array}{ccc} 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 18 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 18 \end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 \end{array}\right) \).
