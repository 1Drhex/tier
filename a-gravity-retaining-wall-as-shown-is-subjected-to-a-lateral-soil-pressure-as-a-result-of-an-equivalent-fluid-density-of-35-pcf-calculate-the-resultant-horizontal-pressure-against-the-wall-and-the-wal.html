Guest Question.

A gravity retaining wall as shown is subjected to a lateral soil pressure as a result of an equivalent fluid density of 35 pcf. Calculate the resultant horizontal pressure against the wall and the wall’s factor of safety against overturning. Assume that concrete has a density of 150 pcf. Check the bearing pressure under the footing.

Admin
Solution

W = (1′)(3′)(1′)(150#/ft3) = 450#

Pmax = ω × h = (35#/ft3)(3′) = 105#/ft2

P = (1/2)Pmax × h × 1′ = (1/2)(105#/ft2)(3′)(1′) = 157.5#

Moments about the toe @ A:

MOTMA = P × 1′ = (157.5#) × 1′ = 157.5#-ft.

MRMA = W × 0.5′ = (450#)(0.5′) = 225#-ft.

S.F. = \(\frac{M_{RM_A}}{M_{OTM_A}} = \frac{225\#-ft.}{157.5\#-ft.}\) = 1.43 < 1.5

Retaining wall does not meet the overturning requirement.

R = \(\sqrt{(W)^{2} + (P)^{2}} = \sqrt{(450)^{2} + (157.5)^{2}} \) = 477#

Bearing pressure check:

x = \(\frac{M_{RM_A} – M_{OTM_A}}{W} = \frac{(225\#-ft.) – (157.5\#-ft.)}{450\#}\) = 0.15′

x = 0.15′ < a/3 = 1’/3 = 0.33′

Maximum bearing pressure at the toe is:

Pmax = \(\frac{2W}{3x} = \frac{2(450\#)}{3(0.15^{'})}\) = 2000psf < 3000psf (allow able)

∴ OK
