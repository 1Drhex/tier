Guest Question.

A salt solution at 293 K is fed at the rate of 6.3 kg/s to a forward-feed triple-effect evaporator and is concentrated from 2 per cent to 10 per cent of solids. Saturated steam at 170 kN/\(m^{2}\) is introduced into the calandria of the first effect and a pressure of 34 kN/\(m^{2}\) is maintained in the last effect. If the heat transfer coefficients in the three effects are 1.7, 1.4 and 1.1 kW/\(m^{2}\) K respectively and the specific heat capacity of the liquid is approximately 4 kJ/kg K, what area is required if each effect is identical? Condensate may be assumed to leave at the vapour temperature at each stage, and the effects of boiling point rise may be neglected. The latent heat of vaporisation may be taken as constant throughout.

Admin
Solution

The mass balance is as follows:

Solids (kg/s) Liquor (kg/s) Total (kg/s)
Feed 0.126 6.174 6.3
Product 0.126 1.134 1.26
Evaporation __ 5.04 5.04

Thus: (\(D_{1}+D_{2}+D_{3}\)) = 5.04 kg/s

At 170 kN/\(m^{2}\): \(T_{0}\) = 388 K and \(\lambda _{0}\) = 2216 kJ/kg

At 34 kN/\(m^{2}\): \(T_{3}\) = 345 K and \(\lambda _{3}\) = 2328 kJ/kg

Thus the latent heat will be taken as 2270 kJ/kg throughout and:

\(\sum \Delta T\) = (388 – 345) = 43 deg K

From equation 14.8:

\(1.7\Delta T_{1}=1.4\Delta T_{2}=1.1\Delta T_{3}\)

and hence: \(\Delta T_{1}\) = 11.5 deg K, \(\Delta T_{2}\) = 14 deg K, and \(\Delta T_{3}\) = 17.5 deg K

Modifying these values for the cold feed, it will be assumed that:

\(\Delta T_{1}\) = 15 deg K, \(\Delta T_{2}\) = 12 deg K, and \(\Delta T_{3}\) = 16 deg K

Thus: \(T_{0}\) = 388 K, \(T_{1}\) = 373 K, \(T_{2}\) = 361 K, and \(T_{3}\) = 345 K

\(\lambda _{0}=\lambda _{1}=\lambda _{2}=\lambda _{3}\)= 2270 kJ/kg

The heat balance over each effect is now:

(1) \(D_{0}\lambda _{0}=WC_{p}(T_{1}-T_{f})+D_{1}\lambda _{1}\)

or 2270\(D_{0}\) = (6.3 × 4)(373 – 293) + 2270\(D_{1}\)

(2) \(D_{1}\lambda _{1}+(W-D_{1})C_{p}(T_{1}-T_{2})=D_{2}\lambda _{2}\)

or 2270\(D_{1}\) + (6.3 – \(D_{1}\))4(373 – 361) = 2270\(D_{2}\)

(3) \(D_{2}\lambda _{2}+(W-D_{1}-D_{2})C_{p}(T_{1}-T_{2})=D_{3}\lambda _{3}\)

or 2270\(D_{2}\) + (6.3 – \(D_{1}-D_{2}\))4(361 – 345) = 2270\(D_{3}\)

Solving:
\(D_{0}\) = 2.48 kg/s, \(D_{1}\) = 1.59 kg/s, \(D_{2}\) = 1.69 kg/s, \(D_{3}\) = 1.78 kg/s

The areas are given by:

\(A_{1}=D_{0}\lambda _{0}/U_{1}\Delta T_{1}\)=(2270 × 2.48)/(1.7 × 15) = 220.8 \(m^{2}\)

\(A_{2}=D_{1}\lambda _{1}/U_{2}\Delta T_{2}\)=(2270 × 1.59)/(1.4 × 12) = 214.8 \(m^{2}\)

\(A_{3}=D_{2}\lambda _{2}/U_{3}\Delta T_{3}\)=(2270 × 1.69)/(1.1 × 16) = 217.9 \(m^{2}\)

which are close enough for design purposes.
The area specified for each stage is therefore 218 \(m^{2}\).

 

equation 14.8: \(U_{1}\Delta T_{1}=U_{2}\Delta T_{2}=U_{3}\Delta T_{3}\)
