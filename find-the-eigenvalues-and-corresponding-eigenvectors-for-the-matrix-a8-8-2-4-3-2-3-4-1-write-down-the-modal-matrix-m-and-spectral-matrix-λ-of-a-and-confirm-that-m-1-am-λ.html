Guest Question.

Find the eigenvalues and corresponding eigenvectors for the matrix
A=\(\begin{bmatrix}8 & -8 & -2\\ 4 & -3 & -2\\ 3 & -4 & 1\end{bmatrix}\)

Write down the modal matrix M and spectral matrix \(\Lambda\) of A, and confirm that \(M^{-1}AM=\Lambda\) .

Admin
Solution

Eigenvalues are given by
\(\left | A-\lambda I \right |=\begin{bmatrix}8-\lambda & -8 & -2\\ 4 & -3-\lambda & -2\\ 3 & -4 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix}=0\)

Row 1 – (Row 2 + Row 3) gives \(\left | A-\lambda I \right |=\begin{vmatrix}1-\lambda & -1+\lambda &-1+\lambda \\ 4 & -3-\lambda & -2\\ 3 & -4 & 1-\lambda \end{vmatrix}\)

=\((1-\lambda )\begin{vmatrix}1 & -1 & -1\\ 4 & -3-\lambda & -2\\ 3 & -4 & 1-\lambda \end{vmatrix}\)
=\((1-\lambda )\begin{vmatrix}1 & 0 & 0\\ 4 & 1-\lambda & 2\\ 3 & -4 & 4-\lambda \end{vmatrix}=(1-\lambda )[(1-\lambda )(4-\lambda )+2]\)
=\((1-\lambda )(\lambda -2)(\lambda -3)\)
Thus, eigenvalues are \(\lambda _{1}=3 , \lambda _{2} , \lambda _{3}=1\) Corresponding eigenvectors are given by
\((8-\lambda )e_{i1}-8e_{i2}-2e_{i3}\)=0
\(4e_{i1}-(3+\lambda )e_{i2}-2e_{i3}\)=0
\(3e_{i1}-4e_{i2}+(1-\lambda )e_{i3}\)=0
When i = 1,\(\lambda _{i}=\lambda _{1}\)=3 and solution given by
\(\frac{e_{11}}{4}=\frac{-e_{12}}{-2}=\frac{e_{13}}{2}=\beta _{1}\)
so a corresponding eigenvector is
\(e_{1}=\begin{bmatrix}2 & 1 & 1\end{bmatrix}^{T}\)
When i = 2,\(\lambda _{i}=\lambda _{1}\)=2 and solution given by
\(\frac{e_{21}}{-3}=\frac{-e_{22}}{2}=\frac{e_{23}}{-1}=\beta _{2}\)
so a corresponding eigenvector is\( e_{2}=\begin{bmatrix}3 & 2 & 1\end{bmatrix}^{T}\)
When i = 3,\(\lambda _{i}=\lambda _{3}\)=1 and solution given by
\(\frac{e_{31}}{-8}=\frac{-e_{32}}{6}=\frac{e_{33}}{-4}=\beta _{3}\)
so a corresponding eigenvector is
\(e_{3}=\begin{bmatrix}4 & 3 & 2\end{bmatrix}^{T}\)
Corresponding modal and spectral matrices are
M=\(\begin{bmatrix}2 & 3 & 4\\ 1 & 2 & 3\\ 1 & 1 & 2\end{bmatrix} and \Lambda =\begin{bmatrix}3 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 2 &0 \\ 1 & 0 & 1\end{bmatrix}\)
\(M^{-1}=\begin{bmatrix}1 & -2 & 1\\ 1 & 0 & -2\\ -1 & 1 & 1\end{bmatrix}\) and matrix multiplication confirms \(M^{-1} A M = \Lambda\)
