Guest Question.

Applying Famularo’s Procedure. Solve the previous employing the above procedure provided by Famularo [7]. Also calculate the discharge temperature, the LMTD, and Q for the countercurrent case.

Admin
Solution

Equation (6.34) applies for cocurrent case,

\(T_2=\frac{\left[(b/B)+e^{-UA(B+b)}\right]T_1+\left[1-e^{-UA(B+b)}\right]t_1 }{1+(b/B)}\) (6.34)

Using the expressions for “B” and “b” developed from Equation \(Q_p=\dot{M}c(T_1-T_2)=\dot{m}c(t_2-t_1)\): B = 3.33 × \(10^{-5}\), b = 4.48 × \(10^{-5}\), and A = 108.2 and U = 482, one obtains:

\(T_2\) = 199.4°F ≈ 200°F

Apply an energy balance to both fluids.

(30,000)(300 – \(T_2\)) = (22,300)(\(t_2\) – 60)

For \(T_2\) = 200 °F , one obtains:

\(t_2\) = 195°F

The agreement between both methods is excellent. Note that the temperature \(t_2\) may also be calculated directly from Equation (6.34) with the temperature(s) appropriately reversed. Substitute into Equation (6.38) for the countercurrent case:

\(T_2=\frac{\left[(b/B)-1\right]T_1+\left[(B/b)e^{UA(B-b)}-(B/b)\right]t_1 }{(B/b)e^{UA(B-b)}-1}\) (6.38)

Once again, for B = 3.33 × \(10^{-5}\), b = 4.48 × \(10^{-5}\), A = 108.2 and U = 482, one obtains,

\(T_2\) = 164.20°F ≈ 164°F

Substitute into Equation \(T_2=\frac{\left[(b/B)-1\right]T_1+\left[(B/b)e^{UA(B-b)}-(B/b)\right]t_1 }{(B/b)e^{UA(B-b)}-1}\) with the temperature reversed to generate \(t_2\):

\(t_2=\frac{(b/B)\left[e^{UA(B-b)}-1\right]T_1+\left[(B/b)-1\right]t_1 }{(B/b)e^{UA(B-b)}-1}\)

Substituting gives,

\(t_2\) = 242.85°F ≈ 243°F

For LMTD (see Equation \(\Delta T_{LM}=\frac{\Delta T_1-\Delta T_2}{\ln (\Delta T_1/\Delta T_2)}\),

\(\Delta T_{LM}=\frac{(T_2-t_1)-(T_1-t_2)}{\ln \left\lgroup\frac{T_2-t_1}{T_1-t_2} \right\rgroup }\)

Substituting,

\(T_2 – t_1\) = 164-60 = 104

\(T_1 – t_2\) = 300-243 = 57

\(\Delta T_{LM}=\frac{104-57}{\ln (104/57)}=78.2°F\)

Apply the heat transfer equation to calculate Q.

\(Q = UAΔT_{LM}\)

= (482 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F)(108.2 \(ft^2\))(78.2°F)

= 4,078,000 Btu/hr

This result may be checked by employing the heat conservation equation.

\(Q = \dot{M}C(T_1 – T_2)\)

= (30,000 Btu/hr · °F)(300°F – 164.20°F)

= 4,074,000 Btu/hr

The two results are once again in reasonable agreement.
