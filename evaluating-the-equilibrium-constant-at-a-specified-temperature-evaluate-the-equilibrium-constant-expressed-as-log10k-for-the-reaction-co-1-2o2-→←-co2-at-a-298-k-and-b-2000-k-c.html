Evaluating the Equilibrium Constant at a Specified Temperature

Evaluate the equilibrium constant, expressed as \(\log _{10} K\), for the reaction \(CO +\frac{1}{2} O _{2} \rightleftarrows CO _{2}\) at (a) 298 K and (b) 2000 K. Compare with the value obtained from .

Admin
Solution

Known The reaction is \(CO +\frac{1}{2} O _{2} \rightleftarrows CO _{2}\).

Find Determine the equilibrium constant for T = 298 K (25°C) and T = 2000 K.

Engineering Model The ideal gas model applies.

Analysis The equilibrium constant requires the evaluation of \(\Delta G^{\circ}\) for the reaction. Invoking Eq. 14.29b for this purpose, we have

 

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta G^{\circ} &=v_{ C }\left(\bar{h}_{ C }-T \bar{s}_{ C }^{\circ}\right)+v_{ D }\left(\bar{h}_{ D }-T \bar{s}_{ D }^{\circ}\right)-v_{ A }\left(\bar{h}_{ A }-T \bar{s}_{ A }^{\circ}\right)-v_{ B }\left(\bar{h}_{ B }-T \bar{s}_{ B }^{\circ}\right) \\&=\left(v_{ C } \bar{h}_{ C }+v_{ D } \bar{h}_{ D }-v_{ A } \bar{h}_{ A }-v_{ B } \bar{h}_{ B }\right)-T\left(v_{ C } \bar{s}_{ C }^{\circ}+v_{ D } \bar{s}_{ D }^{\circ}-v_{ A } \bar{s}_{ A }^{\circ}-v_{ B } \bar{s}_{ B }^{\circ}\right)\end{aligned}\) (14.29b)

 

\(\Delta G^{\circ}=\left(\bar{h}_{ CO _{2}}-\bar{h}_{ CO }-\frac{1}{2} \bar{h}_{ O _{2}}\right)-T\left(\bar{s}_{ CO _{2}}^{\circ}-\bar{s}_{ CO }^{\circ}-\frac{1}{2} \bar{s}_{ O _{2}}^{\circ}\right)\)

 

where the enthalpies are evaluated at temperature T and the absolute entropies are evaluated at temperature T and a pressure of 1 atm. Using Eq. 13.9, the enthalpies are evaluated in terms of the respective enthalpies of formation, giving

 

\(\bar{h}(T, p)=\bar{h}_{ f }^{\circ}+\left[\bar{h}(T, p)-\bar{h}\left(T_{ ref }, p_{ ref }\right)\right]=\bar{h}_{ f }^{\circ}+\Delta \bar{h}\) (13.9)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta G^{\circ}=&\left[\left(\bar{h}_{ f }^{\circ}\right)_{ CO _{2}}-\left(\bar{h}_{ f }^{\circ}\right)_{ CO }-\frac{1}{2}\left(\bar{h}_{ f }^{\circ \nearrow0}\right)_{ O _{2}}\right]+\left[(\Delta \bar{h})_{ CO _{2}}-(\Delta \bar{h})_{ CO }\right.\\&\left.-\frac{1}{2}(\Delta \bar{h})_{ O _{2}}\right]-T\left(\bar{s}_{ CO _{2}}^{\circ}-\bar{s}_{ CO }^{\circ}-\frac{1}{2} \bar{s}_{ O _{2}}^{\circ}\right)\end{aligned}\)

 

where the \(\Delta \bar{h}\) terms account for the change in specific enthalpy from \(T_{\text {ref }}=298\) K to the specified temperature T. The enthalpy of formation of oxygen is zero by definition.

a. When T = 298 K, the \(\Delta \bar{h}\) terms of the above expression for \(\Delta G^{\circ}\) vanish. The required enthalpy of formation and absolute entropy values can be read from , giving

 

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta G^{\circ}=&\left[(-393,520)-(-110,530)-\frac{1}{2}(0)\right] \\&-298\left[213.69-197.54-\frac{1}{2}(205.03)\right]\end{aligned}\)

 

= -257,253 kJ/kmol

 

With this value for \(\Delta G^{\circ}\), Eq. 14.31 gives

 

\(-\frac{\Delta G^{\circ}}{\bar{R} T}=\ln K(T)\) (14.31)

 

\(\ln K=-\frac{(-257,253 kJ / kmol )}{(8.314 kJ / kmol \cdot K )(298 K )}=103.83\)

 

which corresponds to \(\log _{10} K=45.093\).

gives the logarithm to the base 10 of the equilibrium constant for the inverse reaction: \(CO +\frac{1}{2} O _{2} \rightleftarrows CO _{2}\). That is, \(\log _{10} K^{*}=-45.066\). Thus, with Eq. 14.34, \(\log _{10} K=45.066\), which agrees closely with the calculated value.

 

\(\log _{10} K^{*}=\log _{10} K\) (14.34)

 

b. When T = 2000 K, the \(\Delta \bar{h} \text { and } \bar{s}^{\circ} \text { terms for } O _{2}, CO , \text { and } CO _{2}\) required by the above expression for \(\Delta G^{\circ}\) are evaluated from . The enthalpy of formation values are the same as in part (a). Thus,

 

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta G^{\circ}=&\left[(-393,520)-(-110,530)-\frac{1}{2}(0)\right]+[(100,804-9364)\\&\left.-(65408-8669)-\frac{1}{2}(67,881-8682)\right]-2000[309.210\\&\left.-258.600-\frac{1}{2}(268.655)\right]\end{aligned}\)

 

\(=-282,990+5102+167,435=-110,453 kJ / kmol\)

 

With this value, Eq. 14.31 gives

 

\(\ln K=-\frac{(-110,453)}{(8.314)(2000)}=6.643\)

 

which corresponds to \(\log _{10} K=2.885\).

At 2000 K, gives \(\log _{10} K^{*}=-2.884\). With Eq. 14.34, \(\log _{10} K=-2.884\), which is in agreement with the calculated value.
