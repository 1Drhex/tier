Guest Question.

A tube of aluminium with a solid core of steel on the inside is shown in Figure E3.5.The member is subjected to a torque \(T\). The shear moduli for aluminium and steel are,respectively, \(G_{ a } \text { and } G_{\text {steel }}\). What are the maximum shear stresses in the aluminium and the steel?

Admin
Solution

This is also a statically indeterminate problem. Due to the composite section, the shear stress distribution over the steel and aluminium areas has to be considered separately. Apart from the equilibrium condition, the compatibility of deformation condition is that the angle of twist has the same value for both the steel and the aluminium.

Assume that the twist moments carried by the steel and the aluminium are, respectively, \(T_{\text {steel }}\) and \(T_{\text {a}}\). Thus, from equilibrium:

 

\(T_{ a }+T_{\text {steel }}=T\)

 

Since the steel and the aluminium have the same angle of twist, from Equation (3.5):

 

\(\theta=\frac{T L}{G J}\) (3.5)

 

\(\frac{T_{\text {steel }} L}{G_{\text {steel }} J_{\text {steel }}}=\frac{T_{ a } L}{G_{ a } J_{ a }}\)

 

From the above two equations

 

\(T_{ a }=\frac{G_{ a } J_{ a }}{G_{ a } J_{ a }+G_{\text {steel }} J_{\text {steel }}} T \)

 

\(T_{\text {steel }}=\frac{G_{\text {steel }} J_{\text {steel }}}{G_{ a } J_{ a }+G_{\text {steel }} J_{\text {steel }}} T\)

 

Then, the maximum shear stresses in the steel and the aluminium are, respectively:

 

\(\tau_{ a }^{\max }=\frac{T_{ a } \times D / 2}{J_{ a }}=\frac{T G_{ a } D}{2\left(G_{ a } J_{ a }+G_{\text {steel }} J_{\text {steel }}\right)}\)

 

\(\tau_{\text {steel }}^{\max }=\frac{T_{\text {steel }} \times d / 2}{J_{\text {steel }}}=\frac{T G_{\text {steel }} d}{2\left(G_{ a } J_{ a }+G_{\text {steel }} J_{\text {steel }}\right)}\)
