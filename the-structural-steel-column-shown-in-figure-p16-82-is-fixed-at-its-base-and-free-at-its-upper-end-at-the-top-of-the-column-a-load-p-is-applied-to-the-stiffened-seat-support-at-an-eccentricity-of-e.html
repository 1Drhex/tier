Guest Question.

The structural steel column shown in Figure P16.82 is fixed at its base and free at its upper end. At the top of the column, a load P is applied to the stiffened seat support at an eccentricity of e = 9 in. from the centroidal axis of the wide-flange shape. Use the AISC equations given in Section 16.5 and assume that E = 29,000 ksi and \(\sigma_{Y}\) = 36 ksi. Employ the allowable stress method to determine:
(a) whether the column is safe for a load of P = 15 kips. Report the results in the form of the stress ratio \(\sigma_{x} / \sigma_{\text {allow }}\).
(b) the magnitude of the largest eccentric load P that may be applied to the column.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&A=2(0.50 in .)(8 in .)+(7 in .)(0.35 in .)=10.450 in .^{2} \\&I_{z}=\frac{(8 in .)(8 in .)^{3}}{12}-\frac{(7.65 in .)(7 in .)^{3}}{12}=122.671 in .^{4} \quad r_{z}=\sqrt{\frac{122.671 in .^{4}}{10.450 in .^{2}}}=3.426 in . \\&I_{y}=2\left[\frac{(0.50 in .)(8 in .)^{3}}{12}\right]+\frac{(7 in .)(0.35 in .)^{3}}{12}=42.692 in. ^{4} \quad r_{y}=\sqrt{\frac{42.692 in .^{4}}{10.450 in .^{2}}}=2.021 in .\end{aligned}\)

Limiting slenderness ratio

\(\begin{aligned}&4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{Y}}}=4.71 \sqrt{\frac{29,000 ksi }{36 ksi }}=133.681 \\&\frac{K_{z} L}{r_{z}}=\frac{(2)(12 ft )(12 in . / ft )}{3.426 in .}=84.058 \quad \frac{K_{y} L}{r_{y}}=\frac{(2)(12 ft )(12 in . / ft )}{2.021 in .}=142.488\end{aligned}\)

Elastic critical buckling stress

\(\sigma_{e}=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{\left(\frac{K L}{r}\right)^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(29,000 ksi )}{(142.488)^{2}}=14.097 ksi\)

Critical and allowable buckling stresses

Since \(\frac{K L}{r}>4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{y}}}\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{c r}=0.877 \sigma_{e}=(0.877)(14.097 ksi )=12.363 ksi \\&\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\sigma_{c r}}{1.67}=\frac{12.363 ksi }{1.67}=7.403 ksi\end{aligned}\)

(a) Allowable stress method with P = 15 kips:

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{x}=\frac{P}{A}+\frac{M_{z} c}{I_{z}}=\frac{15 kips }{10.450 in .^{2}}+\frac{(15 kips )(9 in .)(8 in. / 2)}{122.671 in .^{4}}=1.435 ksi +4.402 ksi =5.837 ksi \\&\frac{\sigma_{x}}{\sigma_{\text {allow }}}=\frac{5.837 ksi }{7.403 ksi }=0.788 \quad \therefore \text { Safe }\end{aligned}\)

(b) Largest eccentric load P:

\(\begin{aligned}P\left[\frac{1}{10.450 in .^{2}}+\frac{(9 \text { in. })(8 in. / 2)}{122.671 in .^{4}}\right] & \leq 7.403 ksi \\& \therefore P \leq 19.02 kips\end{aligned}\)
