Guest Question.

Finding the frequency response of a two-stage CE-CB BJT amplifier A two-stage CE-CB amplifier is shown in Fig. 8.70. The circuit parameters are \( C_{\pi 1}=C_{\pi 2}=15 pF , C_{\mu 1}=C_{\mu 2}=1 pF , R_{ s }=200 \Omega \), \(R_{11}=22 k \Omega, R_{21}=47 k \Omega, R_{ C 1}=15 k \Omega, R_{ E 1}=9 k \Omega, R_{12}=22 k \Omega, R_{22}=47 k \Omega, R_{ C 2}=15 k \Omega, R_{ E 2}=9 k \Omega\), \( R_{ L }=10 k \Omega, r_{\pi 1}=r_{\pi 2}=1.4 k \Omega, \beta_{ f 1}=100, \beta_{ f 2}=150, g_{ m 1}=71.4 m \mho, g_{ m 2}=107.1 m \mho, C_{1}=1 \mu F \), \( C_{2}=10 \mu F , C_{3}=1 \mu F , C_{ E }=10 \mu F , \text { and } C_{ B }=10 \mu F \).

(a) Calculate the low 3-dB frequency \( f_{ L } \).
(b) Calculate the high 3-dB frequency \( f_{ H } \).

Admin
Solution

We have

\(\begin{aligned}&R_{ B 1}=R_{11}\left\|R_{21}=22 k \Omega\right\| 47 k \Omega=15 k \Omega \\&R_{ B 2}=R_{12}\left\|R_{22}=22 k \Omega\right\| 47 k \Omega=15 k \Omega\end{aligned}\)

 

(a) The low-frequency equivalent circuit is shown in Fig. 8.71(a).

The time constant \( \tau_{1} \text { due to } C_{1} \) only is

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{1} &=\left[R_{ s }+\left(R_{ B 1} \| r_{\pi 1}\right)\right] C_{1} \\&=[200 \Omega+(15 k \Omega \| 1.4 k \Omega)] \times 1 \mu F =1.48 ms\end{aligned}\)

 

The time constant \(\tau_{2} \text { due to } C_{2}\) only is

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{2} &=\left[R_{ C 1}+\left(R_{ E 2} \| r_{\pi 2}\right) \| \frac{1}{g_{ m 2}}\right] C_{2} \\&=\left[15 k \Omega+(9 k \Omega \| 1.4 k \Omega) \| \frac{1000}{107.1 \mho{0}}\right] \times 10 \mu F =150.1 ms\end{aligned}\)

 

The time constant \(\tau_{3} \text { due to } C_{3}\) only is

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{3} &=\left[R_{ C 2}+R_{ L }\right] C_{3} \\&=[15 k \Omega+10 k \Omega] \times 1 \mu F =25 ms\end{aligned}\)

 

The time constant \(\tau_{4} \text { due to } C_{ E 1}\) only is

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{4} &=\left[R_{ E 1} \| \frac{r_{\pi 1}+\left(R_{ s } \| R_{ B 1}\right)}{1+\beta_{ fl }}\right] C_{ E } \\&=\left[9 k \Omega \| \frac{1.4 k \Omega+(200 \Omega \| 15 k \Omega)}{1+100}\right] \times 10 \mu F =0.16 ms\end{aligned}\)

 

The time constant \(\tau_{5} \text { due to } C_{ B }\) only is

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{5} &=\left[R_{ B 2} \| r_{\pi 2}+\left(1+\beta_{ f 2}\right)\left(R_{ C 1} \| R_{ E 2}\right)\right] C_{ B 2} \\&=[15 k \Omega \| 1.4 k \Omega+(1+150)(15 k \Omega \| 9 k \Omega)] \times 10 \mu F =147.2 ms\end{aligned}\)

 

From Eq. (2.106), the low 3-dB frequency \( f_{ L } \) is

\(f_{ L }=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \sum\limits_{k=1}^{n} \frac{1}{\tau_{ Ck }}=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \sum\limits_{k=1}^{n} \frac{1}{C_{ k } R_{ Ck }}\) (2.106)

\(f_{ L }=\frac{1}{2 \pi}\left[\frac{1}{1.48 ms }+\frac{1}{150.1 ms }+\frac{1}{25 ms }+\frac{1}{0.16 ms }+\frac{1}{147.2 ms }\right]=1111 Hz\)

 

If we consider only the smallest time constant \( \tau_{4}=0.16 ms \), we get

\(f_{ L }=\frac{1}{2 \pi \tau_{4}}=\frac{1}{2 \pi \times 0.16 ms }=995 Hz\)

 

(b) Replacing the transistors by their high-frequency models gives the high-frequency equivalent circuit shown in Fig. 8.71(b). If \(R_{\pi 1}\) is Thevenin’s equivalent resistance faced by \(C_{\pi 1} \text { with } C_{\mu 1}, C_{\mu 2}, \text { and } C_{\pi 2}\) open-circuited, the time constant \(\tau_{\pi 1}\) is

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{\pi 1} &=R_{\pi 1} C_{\pi 1}=\left(r_{\pi 1}\left\|R_{ s }\right\| R_{ B 1}\right) C_{\pi 1} \\&=(1.4 k \Omega\|200 \Omega\| 15 k \Omega) \times 15 pF =2.6 ns\end{aligned}\) (8.207)

If \( R_{\pi 2} \) is Thevenin’s equivalent resistance faced by \( C_{\pi 2} \text { with } C_{\mu 1}, C_{\mu 2}, \text { and } C_{\pi 1} \) open-circuited, the time constant \( \tau_{\pi 2} \) can be found from

\(\tau_{\pi 2}=R_{\pi 2} C_{\pi 2}=\left[r_{\pi 2}\left\|R_{ E 2}\right\| R_{ C 1} \| \frac{1}{g_{ m 2}}\right] C_{\pi 2}\) (8.208)

\(=\left[1.4 k \Omega\|9 k \Omega\| 15 k \Omega \| \frac{1000}{107.1 \mho }\right] \times 15 pF =0.14 ns\)

 

With \( C_{\mu 2}, C_{\pi 1}, \text { and } C_{\pi 2} \) open-circuited, the effective load resistance of \(C_{\mu 1}\) is

\(R_{ L1 ( eff )}=r_{\pi 2}\left\|R_{ E 2}\right\| R_{ C1 } \| \frac{1}{g_{ m 2}}\) (8.209)

\(=1.4 k \Omega\|9 k \Omega\| 15 k \Omega \|\left(\frac{1000}{107.1 \mho }\right)=9.3 \Omega\)

 

and its effective input side resistance of \(C_{\mu 1}\) is

\(\begin{aligned}R_{\pi 1( eff )} &=r_{\pi 1}\left\|R_{ s }\right\| R_{ B 1} \\&=1.4 k \Omega\|200 \Omega\| 15 k \Omega=173 \Omega\end{aligned}\) (8.210)

From Eq. (2.116), the time constant presented to \( C_{\mu 1} \) is

\(R_{ eq }=\frac{v_{ X }}{i_{ x }}=R_{ L }+R_{ i }\left(1+g_{ m } R_{ L }\right)\) (2.116)

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{\mu 1} &=\left[R_{ L 1( eff )}+R_{\pi 1( eff )}\left(1+g_{ m 1} R_{ L 1( eff )}\right)\right] C_{\mu 1} \\&=[9.3 \Omega+173 \Omega \times(1+71.4 m\mho \times 9.3 \Omega)] \times 1 pF =0.3 ns\end{aligned}\) (8.211)

With \(C_{\mu 1}, C_{\pi 1}, \text { and } C_{\pi 2}\) open-circuited, the time constant presented to \(C_{\mu 2}\) is

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{\mu 2} &=\left(R_{ L } \| R_{ C 2}\right) C_{\mu 2} \\&=(10 k \Omega \| 15 k \Omega) \times 1 pF =6.0 ns\end{aligned}\) (8.212)

From Eq. (2.112), the high cutoff frequency is

\(f_{ H }=\frac{1}{2 \pi \sum\limits_{j=1}^{n} \tau_{ Cj }}=\frac{1}{2 \pi \sum\limits_{ j =1}^{n} C_{ j } R_{ Cj }}\) (2.112)

\(f_{ H }=\frac{1}{2 \pi}\left[\frac{1}{\tau_{\pi 1}+\tau_{\pi 2}+\tau_{\mu 1}+\tau_{\mu 2}}\right]=\frac{1}{2 \pi}\left[\frac{10^{9}}{2.6+.14+0.3+6.0}\right]=17.6 MHz\)

 

which is much higher than \(f_{ H }=694.4 kHz\) for the CE-CE BJT amplifier in .
