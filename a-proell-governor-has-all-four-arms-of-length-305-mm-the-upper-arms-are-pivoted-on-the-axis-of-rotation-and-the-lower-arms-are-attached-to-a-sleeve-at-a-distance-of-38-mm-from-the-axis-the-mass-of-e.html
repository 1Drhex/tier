Guest Question.

A Proell governor has all four arms of length 305 mm. The upper arms are pivoted on the axis of rotation and the lower arms are attached to a sleeve at a distance of 38 mm from the axis. The mass of each ball is 4.8 kg and are attached to the extension of the lower arms which are 102 mm long. The mass on the sleeve is 45 kg. The minimum and maximum radii of governor are 165 mm and 216 mm. Assuming that the extensions of the lower arms are parallel to the governor axis at the minimum radius, find the corresponding equilibrium speeds.

Admin
Solution

Given : PF = DF = 305 mm ; DH = 38 mm ; BF = 102 mm ; m = 4.8 kg ; M = 54 kg

Equilibrium speed at the minimum radius of governor

The radius of the governor is the distance of the point of intersection of the upper and lower arms from the governor axis. When the extensions of the lower arms are parallel to the governor axis, then the radius of the governor (FG) is equal to the radius of rotation \(\left(r_{1}\right)\).

The governor configuration at the minimum radius (i.e. when FG = 165 mm) is shown in Fig. 18.16

Let \(N_{1}\) = Equilibrium speed at the minimum radius i.e. when FG = \(r_{1}\) = 165 mm.

From Fig. 18.16, we find that

\(\sin \alpha=\frac{F G}{F P}=\frac{165}{305}=0.541\)

 

\(\therefore \quad \alpha=32.75^{\circ}\)

 

and \(\tan \alpha=\tan 32.75^{\circ}=0.6432\)

 

Also \(\sin \beta=\frac{F K}{D F}=\frac{F G-K G}{D F}\)

 

\(=\frac{165-38}{305}=0.4164\)

 

\(\therefore \quad \beta=24.6^{\circ}\)

 

and \(\tan \beta=\tan 24.6^{\circ}=0.4578\)

We know that

\(q=\frac{\tan \beta}{\tan \alpha}=\frac{0.4578}{0.6432}=0.712\)

From Fig. 18.16, we find that height of the governor,

\(h=P G=\sqrt{(P F)^{2}-(F G)^{2}}=\sqrt{(305)^{2}-(165)^{2}}=256.5 mm =0.2565 m\)

 

MD = FK = FG – KG = 165 – 38 = 127 mm

 

\(\therefore \quad F M=\sqrt{(D F)^{2}-(M D)^{2}}=\sqrt{(305)^{2}-(127)^{2}}=277 mm =0.277 m\)

 

and BM = BF + FM = 102 + 277 = 379 mm = 0.379 m

We know that

\(\left(N_{1}\right)^{2}=\frac{F M}{B M}\left[\frac{m+\frac{M}{2}(1+q)}{m}\right] \frac{895}{h}\)

 

\(=\frac{0.277}{0.379}\left[\frac{4.8+\frac{54}{2}(1+0.712)}{4.8}\right] \frac{895}{0.2565}=27109\)

 

\(\therefore \quad N_{1}=165 \text { r.p.m. }\)

Note : The valve of \(N_{1}\) may also be obtained by drawing the governor configuration to some suitable scale and measuring the distances BM, IM and ID. Now taking moments about point I,

\(F_{ C } \times B M=m \cdot g \times I M+\frac{M \cdot g}{2} \times I D\)

 

where \(F_{ C }=\text { Centrifugal force }=m\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2} r_{1}=m\left(\frac{2 \pi N_{1}}{60}\right)^{2} r_{1}\)

 

Equilibrium speed at the maximum radius of governor

Let \(N_{2}\) = Equilibrium speed at the maximum radius of governor, i.e. when \(F_{1} G_{1}\)

= \(r_{2}\) = 216 mm.

First of all, let us find the values of BD and \(\gamma\) in Fig. 18.16. We know that

\(B D=\sqrt{(B M)^{2}+(M D)^{2}}=\sqrt{(397)^{2}+(127)^{2}}=400 mm\)

 

and \(\tan \gamma=M D / B M=127 / 379=0.335 \quad \text { or } \quad \gamma=18.5^{\circ}\)

The governor configuration at the maximum radius of \(F_{1} G_{1}=216\) mm is shown in Fig. 18.17. From the geometry of the figure,

\(\sin \alpha_{1}=\frac{F_{1} G_{1}}{P_{1} F_{1}}=\frac{216}{305}=0.7082\)

 

\(\therefore \quad \alpha_{1}=45.1^{\circ}\)

 

\(\sin \beta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} K_{1}}{F_{1} D_{1}}=\frac{F_{1} G_{1}-K_{1} G_{1}}{F_{1} D_{1}}\)

 

\(=\frac{216-38}{305}=0.5836\)

 

\(\therefore \quad \beta_{1}=35.7^{\circ}\)

 

Since the extension is rigidly connected to the lower arm (i.e. DFB or \(D_{1} F_{1} B_{1}\) is one continuous link) therefore \(B_{1} D_{1}\) and angle \(B_{1} D_{1} F_{1}\) do not change. In other words,

\(B_{1} D_{1}=B D=400 mm\)

 

and \(\gamma-\beta=\gamma_{1}-\beta_{1} \text { or } \gamma_{1}=\gamma-\beta+\beta_{1}\)

 

\(=18.5^{\circ}-24.6^{\circ}+35.7^{\circ}=29.6^{\circ}\)

 

\(\therefore \) Radius of rotation,

 

\(r_{2}=M_{1} D_{1}+D_{1} H_{1}=B_{1} D_{1} \times \sin \gamma_{1}+38 mm\)

 

\(=400 \sin 29.6^{\circ}+38=235.6 mm =0.2356 m\)

From Fig. 18.17, we find that

\(B_{1} M_{1}=B_{1} D_{1} \times \cos \gamma_{1}=400 \times \cos 29.6^{\circ}=348 mm =0.348 m\)

 

\(F_{1} N_{1}=F_{1} D_{1} \times \cos \beta_{1}=305 \times \cos 35.7^{\circ}=248 mm =0.248 m\)

 

\(I_{1} N_{1}=F_{1} N_{1} \times \tan \alpha_{1}=0.248 \times \tan 45.1^{\circ}=0.249 m\)

 

\(N_{1} D_{1}=F_{1} D_{1} \times \sin \beta_{1}=305 \times \sin 35.7=178 mm =0.178 m\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(I_{1} D_{1}=I_{1} N_{1}+N_{1} D_{1}=0.249+0.178=0.427 m\)

 

\(M_{1} D_{1}=B_{1} D_{1} \sin \gamma_{1}=400 \sin 29.6^{\circ}=198 mm =0.198 m\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(I_{1} M_{1}=I_{1} D_{1}-M_{1} D_{1}=0.427-0.198=0.229 m\)

We know that centrifugal force,

\(F_{ C }=m\left(\omega_{2}\right)^{2} r_{2}=4.8\left(\frac{2 \pi N_{2}}{60}\right)^{2} 0.2356=0.0124\left(N_{2}\right)^{2}\)

Now taking moments about point \(I_{1}\)

\(F_{ C } \times B_{1} M_{1}=m \cdot g \times I_{1} M_{1}+\frac{M \cdot g}{2} \times I_{1} D_{1}\)

 

\(0.0124\left(N_{2}\right)^{2} \times 0.348=4.8 \times 9.81 \times 0.229+\frac{54 \times 9.81}{2} \times 0.427\)

 

\(0.0043\left(N_{2}\right)^{2}=10.873+113.1=123.883\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(\left(N_{2}\right)^{2}=\frac{123.883}{0.0043}=28810 \quad \text { or } \quad N_{2}=170 \text { r.p.m. }\)

 

Note : The value of \(N_{2}\) may also be obtained by drawing the governor configuration to some suitable scale and measuring the distances \(B_{1} M_{1}, I_{1} M_{1} \text { and } I_{1} D_{1^{-}}\).
