Guest Question.

Develop the weighted-residual finite element model (not weakform finite element model) of the following pair of equations:
\(-\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} w_{0}}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}-\frac{M}{EI}=0\) , \(-\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} M}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}=q\) (1)
Assume the following approximations of the form
\(w_{0}(x)\approx \sum_{i=1}^{4}\Delta _{i}\varphi _{i} ^{(1)}(x)\) , \(M_{0}(x)\approx \sum_{i=1}^{4}\Lambda _{i}\varphi _{i} ^{(2)}(x)\) (2)
The finite element equations should be in the form
\(0=\sum_{j=1}^{m}k_{ij}^{11}\Delta _{j}^{e}+\sum_{j=1}^{n}K_{ij}^{12}\Lambda _{j}^{e}-F_{i}^{1}\) (3a)
\(0=\sum_{j=1}^{m}k_{ij}^{21}\Delta _{j}^{e}+\sum_{j=1}^{n}K_{ij}^{22}\Lambda _{j}^{e}-F_{i}^{2}\) (3b)
(a) Define the coefficients \(K_{ij}^{11},K_{ij}^{12},K_{ij}^{21},K_{ij}^{22},F_{i}^{1}\) , \(F_{i}^{2}\) in terms of the interpolation functions, known data, and secondary variables, and (b) comment on the choice of the interpolation functions (what type, Lagrange or Hermite, and why).

 

Admin
Solution

The weighted-residual statements of Eqs. (1) are

\(0=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}v_{1}\left ( -\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} w_{0}}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}-\frac{M}{EI} \right )dx\) , \(0=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}v_{2}\left ( -\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} M}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}-q \right )dx\) (4)

where \((v_{1},v_{2})\) are the weight functions. A close examination of the above statements indicate that \(v_{1}\sim M\) and \(v_{2}\sim w_{0}\) (i.e., \(v_{2}q_{0}\) must be work done; therefore, \(v_{2}\) must be like \(w_{0}\)). Using approximations (1), we obtain the following Galerkin (i.e. \(v_{1}\sim \varphi _{i}(2)\) and \(v_{2}\sim \varphi _{i}(1)\) )finite element model:

\(\begin{bmatrix}[0] &[A^{e}] \\ [B^{e}] &[D^{e}] \end{bmatrix}\begin{Bmatrix}{\Delta ^{e}}\\ {\Lambda ^{e}}\end{Bmatrix}=\begin{Bmatrix}{f^{e}}\\ {0}\end{Bmatrix}\) (5)

where \(([K^{11}]=[0],[K^{12}]=[A],[K^{21}]=[B],[K^{22}]=[C],{F^{1}}={f},and {F^{2}}={0})\)

\(A_{ij}^{e}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\varphi _{i}^{(1)}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} \varphi _{j}^{(2)}}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}dx\) , \(f_{i}^{e}=-\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}q\varphi _{i}^{(1)}dx\)

\(B_{ij}^{e}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\varphi _{i}^{(2)}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} \varphi _{j}^{(1)}}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}dx\) , \(D_{ij}^{e}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\varphi _{i}^{(2)}\varphi _{j}^{(2)}dx\) (6)

Note that Hermite cubic interpolations of both \(w_{0}\) and M are implied by Eq. (4a,b), and \(\varphi _{i}^{(1)}=\varphi _{i}^{(2)}\) . The coefficient matrix in Eq. (5) is not symmetric.

 

(\(\sum_{j=1}^{n}A_{ij}^{e}v_{j}^{e}-F_{i}^{e}\) (4a))

(\(A_{ij}^{e}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{i}}{\mathrm{d} x}\frac{\mathrm{d} \varphi _{j}}{\mathrm{d} x}dx\) , \(F_{i}^{e}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\psi _{i}f dx + P_{a}\psi _{i}(x_{a})+P_{b}\psi _{i}(x_{b})\) (4b))
