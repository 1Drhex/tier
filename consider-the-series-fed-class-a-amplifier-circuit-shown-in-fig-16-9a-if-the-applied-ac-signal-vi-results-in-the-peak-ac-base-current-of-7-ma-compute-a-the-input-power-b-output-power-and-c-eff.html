Guest Question.

Consider the series-fed class-A amplifier circuit shown in Fig.16.9a. If the applied ac signal Vi results in the peak ac base current of 7 mA, compute (a) the input power, (b) output power and (c) efficiency of the amplifier. Assume that the transistor has hFE = hfe = 50.

Admin
Solution

Note that the dc quiescent base and collector currents of the transistor are respectively given by

\(I_{BQ}=\frac{V_{BB}-V_{BE}}{R_{b}}=\frac{10V-0.7V}{1K}=9.3 \) mA

and

\(I_{Y}=I_{CQ}=h_{FE}I_{BQ}=50\times 9.3 mA=465 mA \)

Thus, the quiescent collector-to-emitter voltage is

\(V_{Y}=V_{CEQ}=V_{YY}-I_{Y}R_{L} \)

\(=V_{CC}-I_{CQ}R_{L}\)

\(=12V -465 mA\times 10 \Omega \)

which gives VY = VCEQ = 7.35 V

The saturation current is given by

\(I_{C}(sat)=\frac{V_{CC}}{R_{L}}=\frac{12V}{10\Omega }=1.2 A=1200 mA \)

The total collector current is given by

\(i_{Y}=I_{CQ}+i_{b}h_{fe} \)

Clearly, the maximum and minimum values Imax and Imin of the collector current are respectively given by

\(I_{max}=i_{Y}(max)=I_{CQ}+i_{b}(peak)h_{fe}=I_{Y}+I_{m} \)

\(I_{max}=i_{Y}(min)=I_{CQ}-i_{b}(peak)h_{fe}=I_{Y}-I_{m} \)

where IY = ICQ and

\(I_{m}=i_{b}(max)h_{fe}=7 mA\times 50=350 mA \)

The minimum and maximum collector-to-emitter voltages are given by

\(V_{min}=V_{CE}(min)=V_{CC}-i_{Y}(max)R_{L}=V_{CC}-R_{L}(I_{CQ}+I_{m})=V_{Y}-V_{m} \)

\(V_{max}=V_{CE}(max)=V_{CC}-i_{Y}(min)R_{L}=V_{CC}-R_{L}(I_{CQ}-I_{m})=V_{Y}+V_{m} \)

where VY = VCEQ and

\(V_{m}=I_{m}R_{L}=350mA\times 10 \Omega =3.5 V \)

The dc load line and variations in the collector current and collector-to-emitter voltage are demonstrated in 16.9b for a sinusoidal input signal Vi .

(a) Since the dc current IY = ICQ = 465 mA is drawn from the power supply voltage VYY = VCC = 12 V, the dc power input to the circuit is

\(P_{i}(dc)=V_{YY}I_{Y}=12 V\times 0.465 A=5.58 W \)

Since, Im and Vm represent the peak or maximum values the ac collector current iy, and collector-to-emitter voltage vy, the ac output power is obtained as

\(P(ac)=i_{y}(rms)v_{y}(rms)=I_{Y}V_{Y}=\frac{I_{m}}{\sqrt{2} }\times \frac{V_{m}}{\sqrt{2} }=\frac{0.35 A}{\sqrt{2} }\times \frac{3.5 V}{\sqrt{2} } =612.5 mW \)

(c) The efficiency of the amplifier is given by

\(\eta =\frac{P(ac)}{P_{i}(dc)}=\frac{0.6125W}{5.58} \times 100\%=10.98\% \)
