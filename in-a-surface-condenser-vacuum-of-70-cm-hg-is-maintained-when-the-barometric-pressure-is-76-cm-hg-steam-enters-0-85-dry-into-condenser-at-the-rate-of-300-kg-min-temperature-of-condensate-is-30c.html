Guest Question.

In a surface condenser vacuum of 70 cm Hg is maintained when the barometric pressure is 76 cm Hg. Steam enters 0.85 dry into condenser at the rate of 300 kg/min. Temperature of condensate is 30°C and the rise in circulating water temperature is 20°C. For sending water through condenser and piping a pressure head of 5 m is required. For surface condenser determine,
(i) the flow surface area required when water flows at 50 m/min,
(ii) the cooling surface area required when heat transfer rate is 15 × 10\(^{5}\) kJ/m\(^{2}\) · hr °C.
(iii) the total head required to be developed by pump

Admin
Solution
\(\begin{aligned}\text{Absolute pressure in condenser = 76 – 70}&= \text{6 cm of Hg, or, } (6 × 0.0135951 × 10^{4} × 9.81) Pa\\p_{t}&= 8.002 kPa\\\text{Partial pressure of steam, }p_{s}&= \text{Saturation pressure corresponding to 30°C}\\&= 4.246 kPa\\\text{Applying heat balance on condenser, }\quad &\\m_{w} × 4.18 × 20 &= (h_{\text{f at 30°C}} + 0.85 · h_{\text{fg at 30°C}} – 4.18 × 30) × 300\\m_{w} &= 7415 kg/min\\\text{Volume flow of water = }\frac{7415}{1000} m^{3}/min&= 7.415 m^{3}/min\\\text{Flow surface area requirement}&= \frac{\text{Velocity flow rate of water}}{\text{Velocity of water flow}} = \frac{7.415}{50}\\&= 0.1483 m^{2}\\\text{Flow surface area required = 0.1}&\text{483 m}^{2}\\\text{Cooling surface area required}&= \frac{\text{Total heat given by steam}}{\text{Heat transfer rate}}\\&= \frac{300 × \left\lgroup \begin{matrix} h_{\text{fg at 30ºC}} + 0.85 \\ × h_{\text{fg at 30ºC}} – 4.18 × 30 \end{matrix} \right\rgroup × 60} {15 × 10^{5}}\\&=0.413 × 60\\&= 24.79 m^{2}\\\text{Cooling surface area requ}&\text{ired = 24.79 m}^{2}\\\text{Velocity head present}&= \frac{1}{2} × \left\lgroup\frac{50}{60} \right\rgroup ^{2} × \frac{1}{9.81} = 0.0354 m\\\text{Total head required}&= \text{Pressure head + Velocity head}\\&= 5 + 0.0354 = 5.0354 m\\\text{Head required = 5.03}&\text{54 m}\end{aligned}\)
