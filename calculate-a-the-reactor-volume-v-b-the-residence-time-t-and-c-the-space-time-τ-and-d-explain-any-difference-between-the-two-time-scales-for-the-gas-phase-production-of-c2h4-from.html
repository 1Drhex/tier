Guest Question.

Calculate (a) the reactor volume, V, (b) the residence time, \(\bar{t}\) , and (c) the space time, \( \tau<br /> \) and (d) explain any difference between the two-time scales, for the gas-phase production of \(C _{2} H _{4}\) from \(C _{2} H _{6}\) in a cylindrical PFR of constant diameter, based on the following data and assumptions:

\(C _{2} H _{6}( A ) \rightarrow C _{2} H _{4}( B )+ H _{2} \text { (C) } \quad r=k C_{ A }\)

1. The feed is pure \(C _{2} H _{6}\) at 1 kg/s, 1000 K, and 2 bar.

2. k = 0.254 \( min ^{-1}\) at 1000 K.

3. The reactor operates isothermally with negligible pressure drop.

4.\(f_{ A }\) = 0.20 at the outlet.

5. Side reactions are negligible.

6. Ideal gas law can be used.

Admin
Solution

Fig. E5.2 shows a schematic diagram of the PFR.

Mole balance for species A over a differential volume between V and V + dV at steady state leads to

In – Out + Generation = Accumulation

\(\left.F_{A}\right|_{V}-\left.F_{A}\right|_{V+d V}+r_{A} d V=0\) (E5.2.1)

or

\(-d F_{A}+r_{A} d V=0\) (E5.2.2)

which leads to

\(F_{A 0} \frac{d f_{A}}{-r_{A}}=d V=F_{A 0} \frac{d f_{A}}{k C_{A}}\) (E5.2.3)

The concentration of A at any where in the reactor can be calculated through

\(C_{A}=\frac{F_{A}}{Q}=F_{A 0} \frac{1-f_{A}}{Q}\) (E5.2.4)

The volumetric flow rate changes in the reactor as the number of moles of the reaction mixture changes. The volumetric flow rate is proportional to the total molar flow rate change. Stoichiometry is needed to find the total molar flow rate. Table E5.2 shows a summary of stoichiometry.

Based ideal gas

PQ = FRT (E5.2.5)

Since the pressure is constant, we have

\(\frac{Q}{Q_{0}}=\frac{F}{F_{0}}=1+f_{A}\) (E5.2.6)

Substituting Eq. (E5.2.6) \(\frac{Q}{Q_{0}}=\frac{F}{F_{0}}=1+f_{A}\) into Eq. (E5.2.4) \(C_{A}=\frac{F_{A}}{Q}=F_{A 0} \frac{1-f_{A}}{Q}\) , we obtain

\(C_{A}=\frac{F_{A 0}}{Q_{0}} \frac{1-f_{A}}{1+f_{A}}=C_{A 0} \frac{1-f_{A}}{1+f_{A}}\) (E5.2.7)

Substituting Eq. (E5.2.6) \(\frac{Q}{Q_{0}}=\frac{F}{F_{0}}=1+f_{A}\) into Eq. (5.2.2) \(-d F_{A}+r_{A} d V=0\) , we obtain

\(d V=\frac{F_{A 0}}{k C_{A}} d f_{A}=\frac{F_{A 0}}{k C_{A 0}} \frac{1+f_{A}}{1-f_{A}} d f_{A}=\frac{Q_{0}}{k} \frac{1+f_{A}}{1-f_{A}} d f_{A}\) (E5.2.8)

Thus, the volume of the reactor by integration.

\(V=\int_{0}^{f_{\text {Ae }}} \frac{Q_{0}}{k} \frac{1+f_{A}}{1-f_{A}} d f_{A}=\int_{0}^{f_{\text {Ae }}} \frac{Q_{0}}{k} \frac{2-\left(1-f_{A}\right)}{1-f_{A}} d f_{A} =\frac{Q_{0}}{k} \int_{0}^{f_{ A e }}\left(\frac{2}{1-f_{A}}-1\right) d f_{A}=-\frac{Q_{0}}{k}\left[2 \ln \left(1-f_{A e}\right)+f_{A e}\right]\) (E5.2.9)

 

TABLE E5.2 Stoichiometry of the reaction system.
Species Initial Change at V
A \(F_{ A0 }\) \(-F_{ A 0} f_{ A} \) \(F_{ A 0}\left(1-f_{ A }\right)\)
B 0 \( F_{ A 0} f_{ A} \) \(F_{ A 0} f_{ A }\)
C 0 \(F_{ A 0} f_{ A} \) \(F_{ A 0} f_{ A }\)
Total \(F_{ A0 }\) \(F_{ A 0} f_{ A} \) \(F_{ A 0}\left(1+f_{ A }\right)\)

Where the volumetric flow rate can be computed from the reactor inlet conditions based on ideal gas law,

\(Q_{0}=\frac{F_{A 0} R T_{0}}{P_{0}}=\frac{\dot{m}_{0} R T_{0}}{P_{0} M_{A}}\) (E5.2.10)

(a) The required volume of the PFR can thus be computed using Eq. (E5.2.8) \(d V=\frac{F_{A 0}}{k C_{A}} d f_{A}=\frac{F_{A 0}}{k C_{A 0}} \frac{1+f_{A}}{1-f_{A}} d f_{A}=\frac{Q_{0}}{k} \frac{1+f_{A}}{1-f_{A}} d f_{A}\) . First, we need to find the volumetric flow rate at the reactor inlet. Since the mass flow rate is given, as well as the pressure and temperature. Using Eq. (E5.2.9) \(V=\int_{0}^{f_{\text {Ae }}} \frac{Q_{0}}{k} \frac{1+f_{A}}{1-f_{A}} d f_{A}=\int_{0}^{f_{\text {Ae }}} \frac{Q_{0}}{k} \frac{2-\left(1-f_{A}\right)}{1-f_{A}} d f_{A} =\frac{Q_{0}}{k} \int_{0}^{f_{ A e }}\left(\frac{2}{1-f_{A}}-1\right) d f_{A}=-\frac{Q_{0}}{k}\left[2 \ln \left(1-f_{A e}\right)+f_{A e}\right]\)

 

\(Q_{0}=\frac{\dot{m}_{0} R T_{0}}{P_{0} M_{A}}=\frac{1 kg / s \times 8314 J /( kmol \cdot K ) \times 1000 K }{2 \times 10^{5} Pa \times(2 \times 12.011+6 \times 1.00794) kg / kmol }\)

 

\(=1.38246 m ^{3} / s\)

Using Eq. (E5.2.8) \(d V=\frac{F_{A 0}}{k C_{A}} d f_{A}=\frac{F_{A 0}}{k C_{A 0}} \frac{1+f_{A}}{1-f_{A}} d f_{A}=\frac{Q_{0}}{k} \frac{1+f_{A}}{1-f_{A}} d f_{A}\) , we obtain

\(V=-\frac{Q_{0}}{k}\left[2 \ln \left(1-f_{A e}\right)+f_{A e}\right]=-\frac{1.38246 m ^{3} / s }{0.254 min ^{-1} \div 60 s / min }[2 \ln (1-0.2)+0.2\)

 

\(=80.43 m ^{3}\)

(b) Residence time for the reaction mixture passing through the differential volume as shown in Fig. E5.2 can be determined by

\(d \bar{t}=\frac{d V}{Q}\) (E5.2.11)

Substituting Eqs. (E5.2.3) \(F_{A 0} \frac{d f_{A}}{-r_{A}}=d V=F_{A 0} \frac{d f_{A}}{k C_{A}}\) and (E5.2.4) \(C_{A}=\frac{F_{A}}{Q}=F_{A 0} \frac{1-f_{A}}{Q}\) into Eq. (E5.2.11) \(d \bar{t}=\frac{d V}{Q}\) , we obtain

\(d \bar{t}=\frac{d V}{Q}=\frac{F_{A 0}}{Q} \frac{d f_{A}}{k C_{A}}=F_{A 0} \frac{d f_{A}}{k F_{A}}\) (E5.2.12)

which leads to

\(d t=\frac{1}{k} \frac{d f_{A}}{1-f_{A}}\) (E5.2.13)

Thus,

\(\bar{t}=\frac{1}{k} \int_{0}^{f_{A e}} \frac{d f_{A}}{1-f_{A}}=-\frac{\ln \left(1-f_{A e}\right)}{k}\) (E5.2.14)

Plug in the numbers,

\(\bar{t}=-\frac{\ln \left(1-f_{A e}\right)}{k}=-\frac{\ln (1-0.2)}{0.254 min ^{-1}}=0.8785 min\)

(c) The space time is defined as the reactor volume divided by the inlet volumetric flow rate,

\(\tau=\frac{V}{Q_{0}}\) (E5.2.15)

In part (a) we have obtained the values for V and \(Q_{0}\). Substituting V and \( Q_{0}\) into the Eq. (E5.2.15), we obtain

\(\tau=\frac{V}{Q_{0}}=\frac{80.429 m ^{3}}{1.38246 m ^{3} / s }=58.18 s =0.9696 min\)

(d) One can observe that \(\tau \neq \bar{t}\) . This is due to the change of flow rate or density of the reaction mixture in the reactor. As the reaction mixture density decreases in the reactor, the volumetric flow rate (or flow velocity) increases and the residence time decreases. The space time, however, is defined based on the inlet flow rate.
