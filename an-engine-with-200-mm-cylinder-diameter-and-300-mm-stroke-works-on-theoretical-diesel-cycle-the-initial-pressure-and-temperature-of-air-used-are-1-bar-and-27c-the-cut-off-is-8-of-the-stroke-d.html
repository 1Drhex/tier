Guest Question.

An engine with 200 mm cylinder diameter and 300 mm stroke works on theoretical Diesel cycle. The initial pressure and temperature of air used are 1 bar and 27°C. The cut off is 8% of the stroke. Determine :
(i) Pressures and temperatures at all salient points.
(ii) Theoretical air standard efficiency.
(iii) Mean effective pressure.
(iv) Power of the engine if the working cycles per minute are 380.
Assume that compression ratio is 15 and working fluid is air.
Consider all conditions to be ideal.

Admin
Solution

Refer to Fig. 4.17.

 

Cylinder diameter, D = 200 mm or 0.2 m

 

Stroke length, L = 300 mm or 0.3 m

 

Initial pressure, \(p_{1}=1.0 \text { bar }\)

 

Initial temperature, \(T_{1}=27+273=300 K\)

 

Cut off \(=\frac{8}{100} V_{s}=0.08 V_{s}\)

 

(i) Pressures and temperatures at salient points :


Now, stroke volume, \(V_{s}=\pi / 4 D^{2} L=\pi / 4 \times 0.2^{2} \times 0.3=0.00942 m ^{3}\)

 

\(V_{1}=V_{s}+V_{c}=V_{s}+\frac{V_{s}}{r-1} \quad\left[\because V_{c}=\frac{V_{s}}{r-1}\right]\)

 

\(=V_{s}\left(1+\frac{1}{r-1}\right)=\frac{r}{r-1} \times V_{s}\)

 

i.e., \(V_{1}=\frac{15}{15-1} \times V_{s}=\frac{15}{14} \times 0.00942= 0 . 0 1 0 1 m ^{3}\)

 

Mass of the air in the cylinder can be calculated by using the gas equation

 

\(p_{1} V_{1}=m R T_{1}\)

 

\(m=\frac{p_{1} V_{1}}{R T_{1}}=\frac{1 \times 10^{5} \times 0.0101}{287 \times 300}=0.0117 kg / \text { cycle }\)

 

For the adiabatic (or isentropic) process 1-2

 

\(p_{1} V_{1}^{\gamma}=p_{2} V_{2}^{\gamma}\)

 

or \(\frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}=\left(\frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}}\right)^{\gamma}=(r)^{\gamma}\)

 

\(\therefore \quad p_{2}=p_{1} \cdot(r)^{\gamma}=1 \times(15)^{1.4}= 4 4 . 3 1 \text { bar. }\)

 

\(\text { Also, } \quad \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}=\left(\frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}}\right)^{\gamma-1}=(r)^{\gamma-1}=(15)^{1.4-1}=2.954\)

 

\(\therefore \quad T_{2}=T_{1} \times 2.954=300 \times 2.954= 8 8 6 . 2 K .\)

 

\(V_{2}=V_{c}=\frac{V_{s}}{r-1}=\frac{0.00942}{15-1}= 0 . 0 0 0 6 7 2 8 m ^{3}\)

 

\(p_{2}=p_{3}= 4 4 . 3 1 \text { bar. }\)

 

% cut off ratio \(=\frac{\rho-1}{r-1}\)

 

\(\frac{8}{100}=\frac{\rho-1}{15-1}\)

 

i.e., ρ = 0.08 × 14 + 1 = 2.12

 

\(\therefore \quad V_{3}=\rho V_{2}=2.12 \times 0.0006728= 0 . 0 0 1 4 2 6 ~ m ^{3}\)

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{l}V_{3} \text { can also be calculated as follows : } \\V_{3}=0.08 V _{s}+ V _{c}=0.08\times0.00942+0.0006728=0.001426 m ^{3}\end{array}\right]\)

 

For the constant pressure process 2-3

 

\(\frac{V_{3}}{T_{3}}=\frac{V_{2}}{T_{2}}\)

 

\(\therefore \quad T_{3}=T_{2} \times \frac{V_{3}}{V_{2}}=886.2 \times \frac{0.001426}{0.0006728}= 1 8 7 8 . 3 K\)

 

For the isentropic process 3-4

 

\(p_{3} V_{3}^{\gamma}=p_{4} V_{4}^{\gamma}\)

 

\(p_{4}=p_{3} \times\left(\frac{V_{3}}{V_{4}}\right)^{\gamma}=p_{3} \times \frac{1}{(7.07)^{1.4}}\)

 

\(=\frac{44.31}{(7.07)^{1.4}}\)

 

= 2.866 bar.

 

\(\text { Also, } \quad \frac{T_{4}}{T_{3}}=\left(\frac{V_{3}}{V_{4}}\right)^{\gamma-1}=\left(\frac{1}{7.07}\right)^{1.4-1}=0.457\)

 

\(\therefore \quad T_{4}=T_{3} \times 0.457=1878.3 \times 0.457= 8 5 8 . 3 8 K .\)

 

\(V_{4}=V_{1}= 0 . 0 1 0 1 m ^{ 3 }\)

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\because \frac{V_{4}}{V_{3}}=\frac{V_{4}}{V_{2}} \times\frac{V_{2}}{V_{3}} \\=\frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}} \times\frac{V_{2}}{V_{3}} \\=\frac{r}{\rho}, \because V_{4}=V_{1} \\=\frac{15}{2.12}=7.07\end{array}\right]\)

 

(ii) Theoretical air standard efficiency :

 

\(\eta_{\text {diesel }}=1-\frac{1}{\gamma(r)^{\gamma-1}}\left[\frac{\rho^{\gamma}-1}{\rho-1}\right]=1-\frac{1}{1.4(15)^{1.4-1}}\left[\frac{(2.12)^{1.4}-1}{2.12-1}\right]\)

 

= 1 – 0.2418 × 1.663 = 0.598 or 59.8%.

(iii) Mean effective pressure, \(p _{ m } \text { : }\)

 

Mean effective pressure of Diesel cycle is given by

 

\(p_{m}=\frac{p_{1}(r)^{\gamma}\left[\gamma(\rho-1)-r^{1-\gamma}\left(\rho^{\gamma}-1\right)\right]}{(\gamma-1)(r-1)}\)

 

\(=\frac{1 \times(15)^{1.4}\left[1.4(2.12-1)-(15)^{1-1.4}\left(2.12^{1.4}-1\right)\right]}{(1.4-1)(15-1)}\)

 

\(=\frac{44.31[1.568-0.338 \times 1.863]}{0.4 \times 14}=7.424 \text { bar. }\)

 

(iv) Power of the engine, P:


Work done per cycle

 

\(=p_{m} V_{s}=\frac{7.424 \times 10^{5} \times 0.00942}{10^{3}}=6.99 kJ / \text { cycle }\)

 

Work done per second


= work done per cycle × no. of cycles per second

 

= 6.99 × 380/60 = 44.27 kJ/s = 44.27 kW

Hence, power of the engine = 44.27 kW.
