Guest Question.

Work out a good design for \(80 \%\) conversion of a feed consisting of \(1 \mathrm{~mol} \mathrm{~A}\) and 7 mol inert.

Admin
Solution

First determine the slope of the adiabatic line. For this note that 8 moles enter/ mole of \(A\). Thus

\(\mathbf{C}_{p}=(40 \mathrm{~J} / \mathrm{mol} \cdot \mathrm{K})(8)=320 \mathrm{~J} /(\mathrm{mol} \text { of } \mathrm{A}+\text { inerts }) \cdot \mathrm{K}\)

Thus the slope of the adiabatic is

\(\frac{\mathbf{C}_{p}}{-\Delta H_{r}}=\frac{320}{80000}=0.004=\frac{1}{250}\)

Drawing various adiabatics in Fig. 19.11 it seems that the one shown on Fig. \(\mathrm{E} 19.1 a\) looks best. So now tabulate \(X_{\mathrm{A}}\) versus \(1 /\left(-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime}\right)\) from Fig. \(19.11 .\) This gives

Plotting \(1 /\left(-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime}\right)\) versus \(X_{\mathrm{A}}\) gives Fig. E19.1b. This tells right away that a recycle reactor should be used. Thus

\(\frac{W}{F_{\mathrm{A} 0}}=\frac{X_{\mathrm{A}}}{-\bar{r}_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime}}=0.8(6 \times 8)=38.4 \mathrm{~kg} \cdot \mathrm{s} / \mathrm{mol}\)

Or

\(W=F_{A 0}(38.4)=(100)(38.4)=3840 \mathrm{~kg}\)

The recycle ratio, from Fig. E19.1b is \(\boldsymbol{R}=1\).

The feed is available at \(300 \mathrm{~K},\) but enters the reactor at \(600 \mathrm{~K}\) (from Fig. E19.1a), so it must be heated. Thus

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{1}=n C_{p} \Delta T &=(800 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{s})(40 \mathrm{~J} / \mathrm{mol} \cdot \mathrm{K})(600-300 \mathrm{~K})=9.6 \times 10^{6} \mathrm{~J} / \mathrm{s} \\&=\underline{9.6 \mathrm{MW}}\end{aligned}\)

The product stream leaves the reactor at \(800 \mathrm{~K}\) and must be cooled to \(300 \mathrm{~K},\) thus

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{2}=n C_{p} \Delta T &=(800 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{s})(40 \mathrm{~J} / \mathrm{mol} \cdot \mathrm{K})(300-800 \mathrm{~K})=-16 \times 10^{6} \mathrm{~J} / \mathrm{s} \\&=\underline{-16 \mathrm{MW}}\end{aligned}\)

We show our recommended design in Fig. E19.1c.
