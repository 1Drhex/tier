Guest Question.

Fink trusses spaced at 20 feet on centers support W6 \(\times \)12 purlins, as shown in Figure a. The purlins are supported at their midpoints by sag rods. Use A36 steel and design the sag rods and the tie rod at the ridge for the following service loads.
Built-up roof: 5 psf
Snow: 18 psf of horizontal projection of the roof surface
Purlin weight: 12 pounds per foot (lbyft) of length

Admin
Solution

Tributary width for each sag rod = 20⁄2 = 10 ft
Tributary area for deck and built-up roof = 10(46.6) = 466 \(ft^{2}\)
Dead load (deck and roof) = (2 + 5)(466) = 3262 lb
Total purlin weight = 12(10)(9) = 1080 lb
Total dead load = 3262 + 1080 = 4342 lb
Tributary area for snow load = 10(45) = 450 \(ft^{2}\)
Total snow load = 18(450) = 8100 lb

Check load combinations.
Combination 2: 1.2D + 0.5S = 1.2(4342) + 0.5(8100) = 9260 lb
Combination 3: 1.2D + 1.6S = 1.2(4342) + 1.6(8100) = 18,170 lb
Combination 3 controls. (By inspection, the remaining combinations will not govern.)
For the component parallel to the roof (Figure b),
\(T=(18.17) \frac{12}{46.6}=4.679 kips\)
Required \(A_{b}=\frac{T}{\phi(0.75F_{u})}=\frac{4.679}{0.75(0.75)(58)}=0.1434 in.^{2}\)
Use a 5⁄8-inch-diameter threaded rod \((A_{b} = 0.3068 in.^{2}).\)
Tie rod at the ridge (Figure c):
\(P = (4.679)\frac{46.6}{45}=4.845 kips\)
Required \(A_{b}= \frac{4.845}{0.75(0.75)(58)}=0.1485 in.^{2}\)
Use a 5⁄8-inch-diameter threaded rod \((A_{b} = 0.3068 in.^{2}).\)
By inspection, load combination 3 will control.
D + S = 4342 + 8100 = 12,440 lb
The component parallel to the roof is
\(T = 12.44\left ( \frac{12}{46.6} \right )=3.203 kips\)
The allowable tensile stress is \(F_{t} = 0.375F_{u} = 0.375(58) = 21.75 ksi.\)
Required \(A_{b}= \frac{T}{F_{t}}=\frac{3.203}{21.75}=0.1473 in.^{2}\)
Use a 5⁄8-inch-diameter threaded rod \((A_{b} = 0.3068 in.^{2})\) for the sag rods.
Tie rod at the ridge:
\(P = (3.2031)\frac{46.6}{45}=4.845 kips=3.317 kips\)
Required \(A_{b}= \frac{3.317}{21.75}=0.525 in.^{2}\)
Use a 5⁄8-inch-diameter threaded rod \((A_{b} = 0.3068 in.^{2})\) for the tie rod at the ridge.
