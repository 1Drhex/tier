Guest Question.

Suppose that your rich uncle has $1,000,000 that he wishes to distribute to his heirs at the rate of $100,000 per year. If the $1,000,000 is deposited in a bank account that earns 6% interest per year, how many years will it take to completely deplete the account? How long will it take if the account earns 8% interest per year instead of 6%? (4.7)

Admin
Solution

$100,000 = $1,000,000 (A/P, 6%, N), so (A/P, 6%, N) = 0.10.
From Table C-9, 15≤ N ≤ 16. If i = 8%, Table C-11 gives us 20 ≤ N ≤ 21.
