Guest Question.

Applying the Clapeyron Equation

Using \(p-v-T\) data for saturated water, calculate at 100°C (a) \(h_{ g }-h_{ f }\), (b) \(u_{ g }-u_{ f }\), (c) \(s_{ g }-s_{ f }\). Compare with the respective steam table value.

Admin
Solution

Known The system consists of a unit mass of saturated water at 100°C.

Find Using saturation data, determine at 100°C the change on vaporization of the specific enthalpy, specific internal energy, and specific entropy, and compare with the respective steam table value.

Analysis For comparison, gives at 100°C, \(h_{ g }-h_{ f }=2257.0 kJ / kg , u_{ g }-u_{ f }=2087.6 kJ / kg , s_{ g }-s_{ f }=6.048 kJ / kg \cdot K\).

 

a. The value of \(h_{ g }-h_{ f }\) can be determined from the Clapeyron equation, Eq. 11.40, expressed as

 

\(\left(\frac{d p}{d T}\right)_{ sat }=\frac{h_{ g }-h_{ f }}{T\left(v_{ g }-v_{ f }\right)}\) (11.40)

 

\(h_{ g }-h_{ f }=T\left(v_{ g }-v_{ f }\right)\left(\frac{d p}{d T}\right)_{ sat }\)

 

This equation requires a value for the slope \((d p / d T)_{ sat }\) of the saturation pressure–temperature curve at the specified temperature.

The required value for \((d p / d T)_{ sat }\) at 100°C can be estimated graphically as follows. Using saturation pressure–temperature data from the steam tables, the accompanying plot can be prepared. Note that a line drawn tangent to the curve at 100°C is shown on the plot. The slope of this tangent line is about 3570 \(N / m ^{2} \cdot K\). Accordingly, at 100°C

 

\(\left(\frac{d p}{d T}\right)_{ sat } \approx 3570 \frac{ N }{ m ^{2} \cdot K }\)

 

Inserting property data from into the equation for \(h_{ g }-h_{ f }\) gives

 

\(h_{ g }-h_{ f }=(373.15 K )\left(1.673-1.0435 \times 10^{-3}\right)\left(\frac{ m ^{3}}{ kg }\right)\)

 

\(\times\left(3570 \frac{ N }{ m ^{2} \cdot K }\right)\left|\frac{1 kJ }{10^{3} N \cdot m }\right|\)

 

= 2227 kJ/kg

 

This value is about 1% less than the value read from the steam tables.

 

1 Alternatively, the derivative \((d p / d T)_{ sat }\) can be estimated using numerical methods and computer-generated data. The following IT code illustrates one way the derivative, denoted dpdT, can be estimated:

 

Using the Explore button, sweep dT from 0.001 to 0.01 in steps of 0.001. Then, reading the limiting value from the computer data

 

\(\left(\frac{d p}{d T}\right)_{ sat } \approx 3616 \frac{ N }{ m ^{2} \cdot K }\)

 

When this value is used in the above expression for \(h_{ g }-h_{ f }\), the result is \(h_{ g }-h_{ f }=2256\) kJ/kg, which agrees closely with the value read from the steam tables.

b. With Eq. 11.39

 

\(u_{ g }-u_{ f }=h_{ g }-h_{ f }-p\left(v_{ g }-v_{ f }\right)\) (11.39)

 

\(u_{ g }-u_{ f }=h_{ g }-h_{ f }-p_{ sat }\left(v_{ g }-v_{ f }\right)\)

 

Inserting the IT result for \(\left(h_{ g }-h_{ f }\right)\) from part (a) together with saturation data at 100°C from

 

\(u_{ g }-u_{ f }=2256 \frac{ kJ }{ kg }-\left(1.014 \times 10^{5} \frac{ N }{ m ^{2}}\right)\left(1.672 \frac{ m ^{3}}{ kg }\right)\left|\frac{1 kJ }{10^{3} N \cdot m }\right|\)

 

\(=2086.5 \frac{ kJ }{ kg }\)

 

which also agrees closely with the value from the steam tables.

 

c. With Eq. 11.38 and the IT result for \(\left(h_{ g }-h_{ f }\right)\) from part (a)

 

\(s_{ g }-s_{ f }=\frac{h_{ g }-h_{ f }}{T}\) (11.38)

 

\(s_{ g }-s_{ f }=\frac{h_{ g }-h_{ f }}{T}=\frac{2256 kJ / kg }{373.15 K }=6.046 \frac{ kJ }{ kg \cdot K }\)

 

which again agrees closely with the steam table value.

1 Also, \((d p / d T)_{ sat }\) might be obtained by differentiating an analytical expression for the vapor pressure curve, as discussed immediately above the introduction of this example.

• use graphical and computer-based methods to evaluate thermodynamic property data and relations.
