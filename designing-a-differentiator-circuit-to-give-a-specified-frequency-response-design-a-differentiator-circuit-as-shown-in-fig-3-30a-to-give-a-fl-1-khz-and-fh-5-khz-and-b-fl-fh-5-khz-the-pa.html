Guest Question.

Designing a differentiator circuit to give a specified frequency response Design a differentiator circuit as shown in Fig. 3.30(a) to give (a) \( f_{ L }=1 kHz \text { and } f_{ H }=5 kHz \text { and (b) } f_{ L }=f_{ H }=5 kHz \). The pass-band gain is \( A_{ PB }=-20 \). The op-amp parameters are \( C_{ i }=1.5 pF , R_{ i }=2 M \Omega, R_{ o }=75 \Omega \), and open-loop voltage gain \( A_{0}=2 \times 10^{5} \).

Admin
Solution

If we assume that \( C_{1} \) is short-circuited and the other capacitors are open-circuited, the pass-band voltage gain is given by \( A_{ PB } \approx-R_{ F } / R_{1} \text {. If we let } R_{1}=5 k \Omega \),

 

\( R_{ F }=\left|A_{ PB }\right| R_{1}=20 \times 5=100 k \Omega \)

 

(a) We will first consider \( f_{ L }=1 kHz \text { and } f_{ H }=5 kHz \). The low-frequency equivalent circuit is shown in Fig. 3.31.

We can see from Eq. (3.65) that the effective resistance \( R_{ X } \text { due to } R_{ F } \) is very small because the voltage gain \( \left.A_{0} \text { (i.e., } g_{ m }\left[=A_{ o } / R_{ o }\right]\right) \) is very large and the op-amp input voltage \( v_{ d } \) is very small. Thus, Thevenin’s equivalent resistance seen by \( C_{1} \) becomes

\(R_{x}=\frac{v_x}{i_x} = \frac{R_F+R_o\parallel R_L}{1+g_m(R_o\parallel R_L)} \) (3.65)

\( R_{ C1 }=R_{1}+\left(R_{ i } \| R_{ x }\right) \approx R_{1} \)

 

and Thevenin’s equivalent resistance seen by \( C_{ F } \) becomes

\( R_{ CF }=R_{ F } \| R_{ y } \approx R_{ F } \)

 

The low 3-dB frequency is given by

 

\( f_{ L }=\frac{1}{2 \pi R_{ C1 } C_{1}} \) (3.76)

 

which gives

\( C_{1}=\frac{1}{2 \pi R_{1} f_{ L }}=\frac{1}{2 \pi \times 5 k \times 1 kHz }=31.83 nF \)

 

The high 3-dB frequency is given by

 

\( f_{ H }=\frac{1}{2 \pi R_{ F } C_{ F }} \) (3.77)

 

which gives

 

\( C_{ F }=\frac{1}{2 \pi R_{ F } f_{ H }}=\frac{1}{2 \pi \times 100 k \times 5 k \times}=318.3 pF \)

 

(b) For \( f_{ L }=f_{ H }=5 kHz , C_{ F }=318.3 pF \), and Eq. (3.76) gives

 

\( C_{1}=\frac{1}{2 \pi R_{1} f_{ L }}=\frac{1}{2 \pi \times 5 k \times 5 k \times}=6.37 pF \)
