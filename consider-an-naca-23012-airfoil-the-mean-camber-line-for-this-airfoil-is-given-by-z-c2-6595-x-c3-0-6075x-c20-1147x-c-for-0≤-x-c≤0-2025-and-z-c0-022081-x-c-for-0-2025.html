Guest Question.

Consider an NACA 23012 airfoil. The mean camber line for this airfoil is given by

\(\frac{z}{c}=2.6595\left [ (\frac{x}{c})^{3}-0.6075(\frac{x}{c})^{2}+0.1147(\frac{x}{c}) \right ]\) for \(0≤ \frac{x}{c}≤0.2025\)

and \(\frac{z}{c}=0.02208(1-\frac{x}{c})\) for \(0.2025≤\frac{x}{c}≤ 1.0\)

Calculate (a) the angle of attack at zero lift, (b) the lift coefficient when α = 4°, (c) the moment coefficient about the quarter chord, and (d) the location of the center of pressure in terms of xcp/c, when α = 4°. Compare the results with experimental data.

Admin
Solution

We will need dz/dx. From the given shape of the mean camber line, this is

\(\frac{dz}{dx}=2.6595\left [ 3(\frac{x}{c})^{2}-1.215(\frac{x}{c})+0.1147 \right ]\) for\(0\leq \frac{x}{c}\leq 0.2025\)

and \(\frac{dz}{dx}=-2.02208\) for \(0.2025\leq \frac{x}{c}\leq 1.0\)

Transforming from x to θ, where x \(= (c/2)(1 − cos θ)\), we have

\(\frac{dz}{dx}=2.6595\left [ \frac{3}{4}(1-2\cos \Theta +\cos ^{2})0.6075(1-\cos \Theta )+0.1147 \right ]\)

or \(=0.6840-2.3736\cos \Theta +1.995\cos ^{2}\Theta\) for\(0\leq \Theta \leq 0.9335\) red

and \( =-0.02208\) for \(0.9335\leq \Theta \leq \pi\)

(a) From Equation (4.61),

\(\alpha _{L}=0=-\frac{1}{\pi }\int_{0}^{\pi }\frac{dz}{dx}(\cos \Theta _{0}-1)d\Theta _{0} (4.61)\)

\(\alpha _{L}=0=-\frac{1}{\pi }\int_{0}^{\pi }\frac{dz}{dx}(\cos \Theta -1)d\Theta\)

(Note: For simplicity, we have dropped the subscript zero from θ; in Equation (4.61), \(\Theta _{0}\) is the variable of integration—it can just as well be symbolized as θ for the variable of integration.) Substituting the equation for dz/dx into Equation (4.61), we have

\(\alpha _{L}=0=-\frac{1}{\pi }\int_{0}^{0.9335}(-0.6840+3.0576\cos \Theta -4.3686\cos ^{2}\Theta +1.995\cos ^{3}\Theta )d\Theta\)

\(-\frac{1}{\pi }\int_{0.9335}^{\pi }(0.02208-0.02208\cos \Theta )d\Theta (E.1)\)

From a table of integrals, we see that

\(\int \cos \Theta d\Theta =\sin \Theta\)

\(\int \cos ^{2}\Theta d\Theta =\frac{1}{2}\sin \Theta \cos \Theta +\frac{1}{2}\Theta\)

\(\int \cos ^{3}\Theta d\Theta =\frac{1}{3}=\sin \Theta (\cos ^{2}\Theta +2)\)

Hence, Equation (E.1) becomes

\(\alpha _{L}=0=-\frac{1}{\pi }[-2.8683\Theta +3.0576\sin \Theta -2.1843\sin \Theta \cos \Theta +0.665\sin \Theta (\cos ^{2}\Theta +2)]_{0}^{0.9335}\)

\(-\frac{1}{\pi }[0.022080\Theta -0.002208\sin \Theta ]_{0.9335}^{\pi }\)

Hence, \(\alpha _{L}=0=-\frac{1}{\pi }(-0.0065+0.0665)=-0.0191\) rad

or \( \alpha _{L}=0=-1.09^{\circ}\)

(b) \(α = 4° = 0.0698\) rad

From Equation (4.60),

\(c_{l}=2\pi (\alpha -\alpha _{L}=0) (4.60)\)

\(c_{l}=2\pi (\alpha -\alpha _{L}=0)=2\pi (0.0698+0.0191)=0.559\)

(c) The value of \(c_{m,c/4}\) is obtained from Equation (4.64). For this, we need the two Fouriercoefficients \(A_{1}\) and \( A_{2}\). From Equation (4.51),

\(c_{m,c/4}=\frac{\pi }{4}(A_{2}-A_{1}) (4.64)\)

\(A_{1}=\frac{2}{\pi }\int_{0}^{\pi }\frac{dz}{dx}\cos \Theta _{0}d\Theta_{0} (4.51)\)

\(A_{1}=\frac{2}{\pi }\int_{0}^{\pi }\frac{dz}{dx}\cos \Theta d\Theta\)

\(A_{1}=\frac{2}{\pi }\int_{0}^{0.9335}(0.6840\cos \Theta -2.3736\cos ^{2}\Theta +1.995\cos ^{3}\Theta )d\Theta\)

\(+\frac{2}{\pi }\int_{0.9335}^{\pi }(-0.02208\cos \Theta )d\Theta\)

\(=\frac{2}{\pi }[0.6840\sin \Theta -1.1868\sin \Theta \cos \Theta -1.1868\Theta +0.665\sin \Theta (\cos ^{2}+2)]_{0}^{0.9335}\)

\(+\frac{2}{\pi }[-0.02208\sin \Theta ]_{0.09335}^{\pi }\)

\(=\frac{2}{\pi }(0.1322+0.0177)=0.0954\)

From Equation (4.51),

\(A_{2}=\frac{2}{\pi }\int_{0}^{\pi }\frac{dz}{dx}\cos 2\Theta d\Theta =\frac{2}{\pi }\int_{0}^{\pi }\frac{dz}{dx}(2\cos ^{2}\Theta -1)d\Theta\)

\(=\frac{2}{\pi }\int_{0}^{0.9335}(-0.6840+2.3736\cos \Theta -0.627\cos ^{-2}\Theta -4.747\cos ^{3}\Theta +3.99\cos ^{4}\Theta )d\Theta\)

\(+\frac{2}{\pi }\int_{0.9335}^{\pi }(0.02208-0.0446\cos ^{2}\Theta )\)

Note:

\(\int \cos ^{4}\Theta d\Theta =\frac{1}{4}\cos ^{3}\Theta \sin \Theta +\frac{3}{8}(\sin \Theta \cos \Theta +\Theta )\)

Thus,

\(A_{2}=\frac{2}{\pi }\left \{ -0.6840\Theta +2.3736\sin \Theta -0.628(\frac{1}{2})(\sin \Theta \cos \Theta +\Theta )-4.747(\frac{1}{3})\sin \Theta (\cos ^{2}\Theta +2)+3.99[\frac{1}{4}\cos ^{3}\sin \Theta +\frac{3}{8}(\sin \Theta \cos \Theta +\Theta )] \right \}_{0}^{0.9335}\)

\(+\frac{2}{\pi }[0.02208\Theta -0.0446(\frac{1}{2})(\sin \Theta \cos \Theta +\Theta )]_{0.9335}^{\pi }\)

\(=\frac{2}{\pi }(0.11384+0.01056)=0.0792\)

From Equation (4.64),

\(c_{m,c/4}=\frac{\pi }{4}(A_{2}-A_{1}) =\frac{\pi }{4}(0.0792-0.0954)\)

\(c_{m,c/4}=-.0127\)

(d) From Equation (4.66),

\(x_{cp}=\frac{c}{4}[1+\frac{\pi }{c_{l}}(A_{1}-A_{2})] (4.66)\)

Hence, \(\frac{x_{ep}}{c}=\frac{1}{4}[1+\frac{\pi }{0.559}(0.0954-0.0792)]=0.273\)
