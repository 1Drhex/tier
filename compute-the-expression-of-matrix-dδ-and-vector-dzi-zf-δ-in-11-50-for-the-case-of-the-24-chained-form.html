Guest Question.

Compute the expression of matrix D(Δ) and vector \( d\left(z_{i},z_{f},\Delta \right) \) in (11.50) for the case of the (2,4) chained form.

\( D\left(\Delta \right)\left[\begin{matrix} c_{0}\\ c_{1}\\ . \\ . \\. \\ c_{n - 2} \end{matrix} \right]\) = \( d\left(z_{i},z_{f},\Delta \right)\) (11.50)

Admin
Solution

The geometric version of the (2,4) chained form is

\( z^{\prime }_{1}\) =\(\tilde{\upsilon } _{1} \)

\( z^{\prime }_{2}\) =\(\tilde{\upsilon } _{2} \)

\( z^{\prime }_{3}\) =\( z_{2} \tilde{\upsilon } _{1} \)

\( z^{\prime }_{4}\) =\( z_{3} \tilde{\upsilon } _{1} \)

Choose the geometric inputs \(\tilde{\upsilon } _{1} \) , \(\tilde{\upsilon } _{2} \) in the parameterized form (11.49),

\(\tilde{\upsilon } _{2} = c_{0} + c_{1}s + . . .+ c_{n – 2} s^{n – 2}\) (11.49)

with \(\Delta \) = \(z_{1,f} – z_{1,i}\) and \(s\in \left[s_{i} , s_{f}\right] \) = \(\left[0,\left|\Delta \right| \right]\) Integrating in a recursive fashion the second, third and fourth equations and setting \( z _{2}\left(s_{f}\right) \) = \( z_{2,f }\) , \( z _{3}\left(s_{f}\right) \) = \( z_{3,f }\) and\( z _{4}\left(s_{f}\right) \) = \( z_{4,f }\) leads to a linear system in the form (11.50) where

 

\( D \) = \(\left[\begin{matrix} \left|\Delta \right| & \frac{\Delta^{2} }{2} & \frac{\left|\Delta \right|^{3} }{3} \\ sgn \left(\Delta \right) \frac{\Delta^{2} }{2} & \frac{\Delta^{3} }{6} & sgn \left(\Delta \right) \frac{\Delta^{4} }{12}\\ \frac{\left|\Delta \right|^{3} }{6} & \frac{\Delta^{4} }{24} & \frac{\left|\Delta \right|^{5} }{60}\end{matrix} \right]\)

\( d \) = \(\left[\begin{matrix} z_{2,f} – z_{2,i} \\ z_{3,f} – z_{3,i} – z_{2,i}\Delta \\ z_{4,f} – z_{4,i} – z_{3,i}\Delta – z_{2,i} \left|\Delta \right|\end{matrix} \right] \)
