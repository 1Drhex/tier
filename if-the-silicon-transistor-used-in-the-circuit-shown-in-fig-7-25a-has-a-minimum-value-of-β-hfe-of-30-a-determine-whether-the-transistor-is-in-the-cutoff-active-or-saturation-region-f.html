Guest Question.

If the silicon transistor used in the circuit shown in Fig. 7.25a has a minimum value of \(\beta =h_{FE}\) of 30,

(a) Determine whether the transistor is in the cutoff, active or saturation region for V1=12 V,V2= –12 V,

\(V_{CC}\) = 12 V, \(R_{1}\) = 15 K, \(R_{2}\) = 100 K, and \(R_{C}\) = 2.2 K. Also find \(V_{0}\).

(b) Find the minimum value of \(R_{1}\) for which the transistor in part (a) is in the active Compute \(V_{0}\) for \(R_{1}\) = 50 K.

(c) If \(R_{1}\)=15 K and V1 = 10 V in part (a), find the minimum value of V2 required to drive the transistor into the cutoff.

(d) Show that the transistor in part (a) will be in cutoff for V1 = 1 V and V2 = –12 V

Admin
Solution

(a) An equivalent form of the circuit of Fig. 7.25a can be drawn as shown in Fig. 7.25b where \(V_{BB}\) and \(R_{b}\) are the Thevenin voltage and resistance given respectively by

\(V_{BB}=\left\lgroup\frac{R_{2}}{R_{1}+R_{2}} \right\rgroup V_{1}+\left\lgroup\frac{R_{1}}{R_{1}+R_{2}} \right\rgroup V_{2}\) and \(R_{b}=\frac{R_{1}R_{2}}{R_{1}+R_{2}} \)

Putting V1 = 12V, V2 = –12 V, \(R_{1}\) = 15 K, \(R_{2}\) = 100 K, and \(R_{c}\)= 2.2 K in the above equation, we obtain \(V_{BB} \) and \(R_{b}\) as follows:

\(V_{BB}=\left\lgroup\frac{100K}{15K+100K} \right\rgroup\times (12V)+\left\lgroup\frac{15K}{15K+100K} \right\rgroup \times (-12V) =8.87 \) V

\(R_{b}=\frac{15K\times 100K}{15K+100K}=13.04 \) K

Since, the equivalent circuit of Fig. 7.25b of the given circuit is similar to that of Fig. 7.22 with \(R_{e} \) = 0, we can apply all the equations derived in Sec. 7.17 with \(R_{e} \) = 0. Clearly, \(V_{BB}=8.87V>>V_{BE} \) ensures that the transistor can’t be in the cutoff region

In order to determine whether the transistor is in active or saturation, we can assume first that the transistor is in the active region. Now, using \(V_{BB} \) = 8.87V, \(R_{b} \) = 13.04 K, \(V_{BE}\) = 0.7V, \(V_{CC}\) = 12V, \( V_{CE}(sat)\) = 0.2V,\(R_{c}\) = 2.2 K , \(R_{e}\) = 0 Ω and \(h_{FE}\) = 30 in Eq. (7.68) , \(I_{C}=h_{FE}I_{B}=\frac{h_{FE}(V_{BB}-V_{BE})}{R_{b}+(h_{FE}+1)R_{e}} \) , and Eq. (7.76),\(I_{C}(sat)=\frac{V_{CC}-V_{CE}(sat)}{\left\lgroup R_{c}+R_{e}+\frac{R_{e}}{h_{FE}} \right\rgroup } \) , the respective collector and saturation currents can be obtained as follows:

\(I_{C}=\frac{30\times (8.87V-0.7V)}{13.04K}=18.8 mA \) and \(I_{C}(sat)=\frac{12V-0.2V}{2.2K} =5.36 mA \)

Clearly, \(I_{C}>I_{C}(sat) \) and thus the condition for saturation in Eq. (7.75) , \(I_{C}=\frac{h_{FE}(V_{BB}-V_{BE})}{R_{b}+(h_{FE}+1)R_{e}} \geq \frac{V_{CC}-V_{CE}(sat)}{\left\lgroup R_{c}+R_{e}+\frac{R_{e}}{h_{FE}} \right\rgroup } =I_{C}(sat) \) , is satisfied. This implies that the assumption of the transistor in the active region is invalid and hence the transistor should be in the saturation region.

Since, the transistor is in saturation in this case, the output voltage \(V_{0}=V_{CE}(sat)=0.2 \) V

(b) If the transistor needs to be in the active region, the collector current \(I_{C} \) must be smaller than the saturation current \(I_{C} (sat)\). Thus, using \(R_{e}\) = 0 in Eq. (7.75), we can say that the transistor will be in the active region if

\(\frac{h_{FE}(V_{BB}-V_BE)}{R_{b}}<\frac{V_{CC}-V_{CE}(sat)}{R_{c}} \)

which gives

\(\frac{(V_{BB}-V_{BE})}{R_{b}}<\frac{V_{CC}-V_{CE}(sat)}{h_{FE}R_{c}}=a \)

where \(a=\frac{V_{CC}-V_{CE}(sat)}{h_{FE}R_{c}}=\frac{I_{C}(sat)}{h_{FE}}=\frac{5.36 mA}{30} =178.7 \mu A \)

Since \(V_{BB}=(12V)\times \left\lgroup\frac{100K-R_{1}}{100K+R_{1}} \right\rgroup \) and \(R_{b}=\frac{R_{1}\times 100K}{R_{1}+100K} \) in this case, we get

\((12V)\times \left\lgroup\frac{100K-R_{1}}{100K+R_{1}} \right\rgroup V < (178.7\mu A) \left\lgroup\frac{R_{1}\times 100K}{100K+R_{1}} \right\rgroup +V_{BE} \)

or, \(R_{1} > \frac{(12V-0.7V)}{178.7\mu A\times 100K+0.7V+12V}=36.97K \)

Clearly, the transistor will be in the active region for \(R_{1} \) > 36.96 K.

Clearly, for \(R_{1}\approx 50K \), the transistor is in the active region. Now \(V_{BB}\) and \(R_{b}\) can be obtained as

\(V_{BB}=\left\lgroup\frac{100K}{50K+100K} \right\rgroup \times (12V)+\left\lgroup\frac{50K}{50K+100K} \right\rgroup \times (-12V)=4.0\) V

\(R_{b}=\frac{50K\times 100K}{50K+100K} =33.33\) K

Thus, the collector current is obtained as

\(I_{C}=\frac{30\times (4.0V-0.7V)}{33.33K}=2.97 \) mA

\(V_{0}=V_{CE}=V_{CC}-I_{C}R_{C}=12V-2.97mA\times 2.2K=5.46 \) V

(c) The transistor will be in the cutoff region if the base-emitter junction is reverse-biased which implies that \(V_{BB}\leq 0 \). Thus, we write

\(V_{BB}=\left\lgroup\frac{100K}{50K+100K} \right\rgroup \times (12V)+\left\lgroup\frac{15K}{15K+100K} \right\rgroup V_{2}\leq 0\)

which gives V2 ≤ – 6.67 V. Clearly, the minimum value of V2 for which the transistor will be in the cutoff is V2 = – 6.67 V.

(d ) For V1 = 1 V, V2 = –12 V, \(R_{1}\) = 15 K and \(R_{2}\) = 100 K

\(V_{BB}=\left\lgroup\frac{100K}{50K+100K} \right\rgroup \times (1V)+\left\lgroup\frac{15K}{15K+100K} \right\rgroup (-12V)=-0.7\) V

Since \(V_{BB}\leq 0\) , the base-emitter junction is reverse-biased and hence the transistor is in the cutoff condition.
