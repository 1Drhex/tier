Guest Question.

Two particles (masses \( m_1 \) and \( m_2 \)) are attached to the ends of a massless rigid rod of length a. The system is free to rotate in three dimensions about the (fixed) center of mass.

(a) Show that the allowed energies of this rigid rotor are

\( E_{n}=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 I} n(n+1), \quad(n=0,1,2, \ldots), \quad \text { where } \quad I=\frac{m_{1} m_{2}}{\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right)} a^{2} \).

is the moment of inertia of the system. Hint: First express the (classical) energy in terms of the angular momentum.

(b) What are the normalized eigenfunctions for this system? (Let θ and ϕ define the orientation of the rotor axis.) What is the degeneracy of the nth energy level?

(c) What spectrum would you expect for this system? (Give a formula for the frequencies of the spectral lines.) Answer

\( v_{j}=\hbar j / 2 \pi I, j=1,2,3, \ldots \).

(d) Figure 4.13 shows a portion of the rotational spectrum of carbon monoxide (CO). What is the frequency separation \( (\Delta v) \) between adjacent lines? Look up the masses of \( { }^{12} C \text { and }{ }^{16} O \), and from \( m_{1}, m_{2} \) and \( (\Delta v) \) determine the distance between the atoms.

Admin
Solution

(a) Classically, \( H=\frac{1}{2} I \omega^{2} \text { and } L=I \omega \text {; so } H=\frac{L^{2}}{2 I} \). But we know the eigenvalues of \( L^{2}: \hbar^{2} \ell(\ell+1) \). Or, since we usually label energies with n:

\( E_{n}=\frac{\hbar^{2} n(n+1)}{2 I}(n=0,1,2, \ldots) \).

(b) \( \psi_{n m}(\theta, \phi)=Y_{n}^{m}(\theta, \phi) \), the ordinary spherical harmonics. The degeneracy of the nth energy level is the number of m-values for given n: 2n + 1.

(c) As in Equation 4.92, the frequency of an emitted photon, in a transition from \( n_{i} \text { to } n_{f} \) is given by

\( E_{\gamma}=h v \) (4.92).

\( E_{\gamma}=h \nu=2 \pi \hbar \nu=E_{i}-E_{f}=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 I}\left[n_{i}\left(n_{i}+1\right)-n_{f}\left(n_{j}+1\right)\right] \).

The term in square brackets is the difference of two even numbers, so it is necessary even. It cannot be zero, but it does hit every positive even integer, since if \( n_{i}=n_{f}+1 \text {, then }\left[n_{i}\left(n_{i}+1\right)-n_{j}\left(n_{j}+1\right)\right]= 2\left(n_{f}+1\right)=2,4,6, \ldots \) Calling this number 2j , \( \nu_{j}=\frac{\hbar j}{2 \pi I}, j=1,2,3, \ldots \).

(d) \( \Delta \nu=\frac{68-27}{11}=3.73 cm ^{-1}=3.73 \times\left(3 \times 10^{10}\right) Hz =11 \times 10^{10} / s \).

\( m_{ C }=12 u =2.00 \times 10^{-26} kg , \quad m_{ O }=16 u =(4 / 3) m_{ C } ; \quad \frac{m_{1} m_{2}}{m_{1}+m_{2}}=1.14 \times 10^{-26} kg \).

\( I=\frac{m_{1} m_{2}}{m_{1}+m_{2}} a^{2}=\frac{\hbar}{2 \pi(\Delta \nu)} \Rightarrow a=\left(\frac{1.05 \times 10^{-34}}{2 \pi\left(11 \times 10^{10}\right)\left(1.14 \times 10^{-26}\right)}\right)^{1 / 2} m =1.15 \times 10^{-10} m \).

(The accepted value is \( 1.128 \times 10^{-10} \)).
