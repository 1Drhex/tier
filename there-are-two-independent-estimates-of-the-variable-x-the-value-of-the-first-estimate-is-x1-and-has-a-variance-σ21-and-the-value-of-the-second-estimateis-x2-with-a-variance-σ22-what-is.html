Guest Question.

There are two independent estimates of the variable x. The value of the first estimate is \(x_{1}\)and has a variance \(\sigma_{1}^{2}\) , and the value of the second estimate is \(x_{2}\) with a variance \(\sigma_{2}^{2}\) . What is the optimal linear combination of these two estimates that represent the state estimate \(\hat{x}\)with minimal variance?

Admin
Solution

The estimation of optimal value of the variable x is assumed to be a linear
combination of two measurements:

\(\hat{x}=\omega_{1} x_{1}+\omega_{2} x_{2}\)

where the parameters \(\omega_{1}\) and \(\omega_{2}\) are the unknown weights that satisfy the following condition: \(\omega_{1}\) + \(\omega_{2}\) = 1. The optimum values of the weights should minimize the variance \(\sigma^{2}\) of the optimal estimate \(\hat{x}\). Hence, the variance is as follows:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma^{2} &= E \left\{(\hat{x}- E \{\hat{x}\})^{2}\right\} \\&= E \left\{\left(\omega_{1} x_{1}+\omega_{2} x_{2}- E \left\{\omega_{1} x_{1}+\omega_{2} x_{2}\right\}\right)^{2}\right\} \\&= E \left\{\left(\omega_{1} x_{1}+\omega_{2} x_{2}-\omega_{1} E \left\{x_{1}\right\}-\omega_{2} E \left\{x_{2}\right\}\right)^{2}\right\} \\&= E \left\{\left(\omega_{1}\left(x_{1}- E \left\{x_{1}\right\}\right)+\omega_{2}\left(x_{2}- E \left\{x_{2}\right\}\right)\right)^{2}\right\}\end{aligned}\) \(\begin{aligned}&= E \left\{\omega_{1}^{2}\left(x_{1}- E \left\{x_{1}\right\}\right)^{2}+\omega_{2}^{2}\left(x_{2}- E \left\{x_{2}\right\}\right)^{2}+2 \omega_{1} \omega_{2}\left(x_{1}- E \left\{x_{1}\right\}\right)\left(x_{2}- E \left\{x_{2}\right\}\right)\right\} \\&=\omega_{1}^{2} E \left\{\left(x_{1}- E \left\{x_{1}\right\}\right)^{2}\right\}+\omega_{2}^{2} E \left\{\left(x_{2}- E \left\{x_{2}\right\}\right)^{2}\right\}+2 \omega_{1} \omega_{2} E \left\{\left(x_{1}- E \left\{x_{1}\right\}\right)\left(x_{2}- E \left\{x_{2}\right\}\right)\right\} \\&=\omega_{1}^{2} \sigma_{1}^{2}+\omega_{2}^{2} \sigma_{2}^{2}+2 \omega_{1} \omega_{2} E \left\{\left(x_{1}- E \left\{x_{1}\right\}\right)\left(x_{2}- E \left\{x_{2}\right\}\right)\right\}\end{aligned}\)

Since the variables \(x_{1}\) and \(x_{2}\) are independent, the differences \(x_{1}- E \left\{x_{1}\right\} \text { and } x_{2}- E \left\{x_{2}\right\}\) are also independent, and therefore \(E \left\{\left(x_{1}- E \left\{x_{1}\right\}\right)\left(x_{2}- E \left\{x_{2}\right\}\right)\right\}=0\). Hence,

\(\sigma^{2}=\omega_{1}^{2} \sigma_{1}^{2}+\omega_{2}^{2} \sigma_{2}^{2}\)

or after introducing\(\omega_{2}\) = ω and \(\omega_{1}\) = 1 − ω,

\(\sigma^{2}=(1-\omega)^{2} \sigma_{1}^{2}+\omega^{2} \sigma_{2}^{2}\)

We are seeking the value of the weight ω that minimizes the variance that can be obtained from variance derivative:

\(\frac{\partial}{\partial \omega} \sigma^{2}=-2(1-\omega) \sigma_{1}^{2}+2 \omega \sigma_{2}^{2}=0\)

which yields the solution

\(\omega=\frac{\sigma_{1}^{2}}{\sigma_{1}^{2}+\sigma_{2}^{2}}\)

The minimum-variance estimate is therefore

\(\hat{x}=\frac{\sigma_{2}^{2} x_{1}+\sigma_{1}^{2} x_{2}}{\sigma_{1}^{2}+\sigma_{2}^{2}}\)

and the minimum variance is

\(\sigma^{2}=\frac{\sigma_{1}^{2} \sigma_{2}^{2}}{\sigma_{1}^{2}+\sigma_{2}^{2}}=\left(\frac{1}{\sigma_{1}^{2}}+\frac{1}{\sigma_{2}^{2}}\right)^{-1}\)

The obtained results confirm that the source with lower variance (higher belief) contributes more to the final estimate, and vice-versa.
