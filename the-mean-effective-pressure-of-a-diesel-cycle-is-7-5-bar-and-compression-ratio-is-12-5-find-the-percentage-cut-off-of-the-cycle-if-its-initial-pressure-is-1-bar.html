Guest Question.

The mean effective pressure of a Diesel cycle is 7.5 bar and compression ratio is 12.5. Find the percentage cut off of the cycle if its initial pressure is 1 bar.

Admin
Solution

Mean effective pressure, \(p_{m}=7.5 \text { bar }\)

 

Compression ratio, r = 12.5

 

Initial pressure, \(p_{1}=1 \text { bar }\)

 

Refer Fig. 4.15.

 

The mean effective pressure is given by

 

\(p_{m}=\frac{p_{1} r^{\gamma}\left[\gamma(\rho-1)-r^{1-\gamma}\left(\rho^{\gamma}-1\right)\right.}{(\gamma-1)(r-1)}\)

 

\(7.5=\frac{1 \times(12.5)^{1.4}\left[1.4(\rho-1)-(12.5)^{1-1.4}\left(\rho^{1.4}-1\right)\right.}{(1.4-1)(12.5-1)}\)

 

\(7.5=\frac{34.33\left[1.4 \rho-1.4-0.364 \rho^{1.4}+0.364\right]}{4.6}\)

 

\(7.5=7.46\left(1.4\rho-1.036-0.364 \rho^{1.4}\right)\)

 

\(1.005=1.4 \rho-1.036-0.364 \rho^{1.4}\)

 

or \(2.04=1.4 \rho-0.364 \rho^{1.4}\)

 

or \(0.346 \rho^{1.4}-1.4 \rho+2.04=0\)

 

Solving by trial and error method, we get


ρ = 2.24

 

\(\therefore\) \(\% \text { cut off }=\frac{\rho-1}{r-1} \times 100=\frac{2.24-1}{12.5-1} \times 100= 1 0 . 7 8 \%\)
