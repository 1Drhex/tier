Guest Question.

Determine the moments at each joint of the frame shown in Fig. \(12-20(\mathrm{a})\). \(EI\) is constant.

Admin
Solution

First sidesway is prevented by the restraining force \(\mathbf{R}\), Fig. 12-20b. The FEMs for member \(B C\) are

\((\mathrm{FEM})_{B C}=-\frac{8(10)}{8}=-10 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \quad(\mathrm{FEM})_{\mathrm{CB}}=\frac{8(10)}{8}=10 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft}\)

Since spans \(A B\) and \(D C\) are pinned at their ends, the stiffness factor is computed using \(3 E I / L\). The moment distribution is shown in Fig. 12-20d.

Using these results, the horizontal reactions at \(A\) and \(D\) must be determined. This is done using an equilibrium analysis of each member, Fig. 12-20e. Summing moments about points \(B\) and \(C\) on each leg, we have

\(\begin{array}{lrlrl} \curvearrowleft +\Sigma M_{B} & =0 ; & & -5.97+A_{x}(8)-4(6)=0 & & A_{x}=3.75 \mathrm{k} \\ \curvearrowleft +\Sigma M_{C} & =0 ; & & 5.97-D_{x}(8)+4(6)=0 & & D_{x}=3.75 \mathrm{k} \end{array}\)

Thus, for the entire frame,

\( \Sigma F_{x}=0 ; \quad R=3.75-3.75+20=20 \mathrm{k} \)

The opposite force \(\mathbf{R}\) is now applied to the frame as shown in Fig. 12-20c. In order to determine the internal moments developed by \(\mathbf{R}\) we will first consider the force \(\mathbf{R}^{\prime}\) acting as shown in Fig. 12-20f. Here the dashed lines do not represent the distortion of the frame members; instead, they are constructed as straight lines extended to the final positions \(B^{\prime}\) and \(C^{\prime}\) of points \(B\) and \(C\), respectively. Due to the symmetry of the frame, the displacement \(B B^{\prime}=C C^{\prime}=\Delta^{\prime}\). Furthermore, these displacements cause \(B C\) to rotate. The vertical distance between \(B^{\prime}\) and \(C^{\prime}\) is \(1.2 \Delta^{\prime}\), as shown on the displacement diagram, Fig. 12-20g. Since each span undergoes end-point displacements that cause the spans to rotate, fixed-end moments are induced in the spans. These are: \((\mathrm{FEM})_{B A}=(\mathrm{FEM})_{C D}=-3 E I \Delta^{\prime} /(10)^{2}, \quad(\mathrm{FEM})_{B C}=(\mathrm{FEM})_{C B}=\) \(6 E I\left(1.2 \Delta^{\prime}\right) /(10)^{2} .\)

Notice that for \(B A\) and \(C D\) the moments are negative since clockwise rotation of the span causes a counterclockwise FEM.

If we arbitrarily assign a value of \((\mathrm{FEM})_{B A}=(\mathrm{FEM})_{C D}=\) \(-100 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft}\), then equating \(\Delta^{\prime}\) in the above formulas yields \((\mathrm{FEM})_{B C}=(\mathrm{FEM})_{C B}=240 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft}\). These moments are applied to the frame and distributed, Fig. 12-20h. Using these results, the equilibrium analysis is shown in Fig. 12-20i. For each leg, we have

\( \begin{array}{llll} \curvearrowleft +\Sigma M_{B} & =0 ; & -A_{x}^{\prime}(8)+29.36(6)+146.80 & =0 & A_{x}^{\prime} & =40.37 \mathrm{k} \\ \curvearrowleft +\Sigma M_{C} & =0 ; & -D_{x}^{\prime}(8)+29.36(6)+146.80 & =0 & D_{x}^{\prime} & =40.37 \mathrm{k} \end{array}\)

Thus, for the entire frame,

\( \Sigma F_{x}=0 ; \quad R^{\prime}=40.37+40.37=80.74 \mathrm{k} \)

The resultant moments in the frame are therefore

\( \begin{array}{l}M_{B A}=5.97+\left(\frac{20}{80.74}\right)(-146.80)=-30.4 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\M_{B C}=-5.97+\left(\frac{20}{80.74}\right)(146.80)=30.4 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\M_{C B}=5.97+\left(\frac{20}{80.74}\right)(146.80)=42.3 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\M_{C D}=-5.97+\left(\frac{20}{80.74}\right)(-146.80)=-42.3 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft}\end{array}\)
