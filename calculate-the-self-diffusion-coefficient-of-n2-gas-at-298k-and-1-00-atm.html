Guest Question.

Calculate the self-diffusion coefficient of \(N_{2}\) gas at 298K and 1.00 atm.

Admin
Solution

We use the ideal gas law to calculate the number density:

\( \mathscr{N}_{tot}=\frac{N}{V} =\frac{P}{k_{B}T} =\frac{101325 N m^{−2}}{\bigl(1.3807×10^{−23} J K^{−1}\bigr)(298 K)} =2.46×10^{25} m^{−3}\)

 

From Table A.15, \(d= 3.7×10^{−10} m. \)

 

\( D_{1}=\frac{3}{8d^{2}\mathscr{N}_{tot}}\Bigl(\frac{k_{B}T}{πm} \Bigr) ^{1/2} =\frac{3}{8d^{2}\mathscr{N}_{tot}}\Bigl(\frac{RT}{πM} \Bigr) ^{1/2} \)

 

\(=\frac{3}{8(3.7×10^{−10} m)^{2}(2.46×10^{25} m^{−3})} \Biggl(\frac{\bigl(8.3145 J K^{−1} mo1^{−1}\bigr)(298 K) \bigr)}{ π\bigl(0.028 kg mol^{−1} \bigr)} \Biggr) ^{1/2} \)

 

\(=1.9×10^{−5} m^{2} s^{−1}\)
