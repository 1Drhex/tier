As part of a paper fabrication process, after sheet forming of the pulp, the sheet, while extended and rolling between an upper and a lower roller, is heated and partially dried by surface convection (and in general also by surface radiation, but neglected here) using the hot gaseous products of a combustion. This is shown in Figure (a). This heat transfer to the pulp results in the pulp drying. Consider a pulp surface temperature \(T_{s}\), and treat the combustion products as air at temperature \(T_{f,∞}\) flowing parallel to the surface at a far-field velocity \(u_{f,∞}\). Assume that the pulp sheet is stationary relative to the flowing gas and that a flat, semi-infinite approximation is valid for the pulp sheet, with the leading edge, x = 0, marked as in Figure.
(a) Draw the thermal circuit diagram.
(b) Determine the rate of surface-convection heat transfer \(\left\langle Q_{ku}\right\rangle _{L}\).
(c) Determine the boundary-layer thickness at x = L, \(\delta _{\alpha }(L)\).
\(T_{s} = 35^{\circ }C, T_{f,∞} = 900^{\circ }C, u_{f,∞} = 0.5 m/s , L = 2 m, w = 1 m\).

Solution

(a) The thermal circuit diagram is shown in Figure (b).
(b) The surface-convection heat transfer rate, from the hot gas to the pulp \(\left\langle Q_{ku}\right\rangle _{L}\)., is given by \(A_{ku}=Lw, \left\langle Q_{ku}\right\rangle _{L}=\frac{T_{s}-T_{f,\infty }}{\left\langle R_{ku}\right\rangle_{L} } =A_{ku}\left\langle Nu\right\rangle _{L} \frac{k_{f}}{L} (T_{s}-T_{f,\infty })\), i.e.,

\(\left\langle Q_{ku}\right\rangle _{L} =A_{ku}\left\langle Nu\right\rangle _{L} \frac{k_{f}}{L} (T_{s}-T_{f,\infty })\)

The Nusselt number depends on the Reynolds number ReL, defined by \(Re_{L}\equiv \frac{F_{u}}{F_{\mu }} \equiv \frac{u_{f,\infty }L}{v_{f}} , v_{f}=(\mu /\rho )_{f}\) as

\( Re_{L}= \frac{u_{f,\infty }L}{v_{f}} \)

From Table, and at T = 700 K, we have

\( ν_{f} = 6.515 × 10^{5 }m^{2}/s\) Table

where we note that \( (Pa-s) = (N-s/m^{2}) = (kg-m-s/m^{2}-s) = (kg/m-s)\) . Then

\(Re_{L}= \frac{ 0.5(m/s) × 2(m)}{6.515 × 10^{-5} (m^{2}/s)} = 1.535 × 10^{4} < Re_{L,t} = 5 × 10^{5} \) laminar flow

Then we can use \(\left\langle Nu\right\rangle _{L}=0.332Pr^{1/3}\left(\frac{u_{f,\infty }}{v_{f}} \right) ^{1/2}\int_{0}^{L}{x^{-1/2}dx} =2Nu_{L}=0.664Re_{L}^{1/2}Pr^{1/3}=\frac{L}{A_{ku}\left\langle R_{ku}\right\rangle_{L}k_{f} } , Re_{L}\leq Re_{L,t}=5\times 10^{5}\) for \( \left\langle Nu\right\rangle _{L}\) , i.e.,

\( \left\langle Nu\right\rangle _{L}= 0.664Re^{1/2} _{L} Pr^{1/3}.\)

From Table and at T = 700 K, we have

Pr = 0.70 Table

\( k_{f}\) = 0.0513 W/m-K Table

Then
\(\left\langle Nu\right\rangle _{L} = 0.664 × (1.535 × 10^{4})^{1/2} × (0.7)^{1/3} = 73.05.\)

With \(A_{ku} = Lw\), the heat transfer rate \(\left\langle Q_{ku}\right\rangle _{L}\) becomes

\(\left\langle Q_{ku}\right\rangle _{L}= 2(m) × 1(m) × 73.05 ×\frac{0.0513(W/m-K)}{2(m)} × (900 − 35)(K)\) \( = 3.242 × 10^{3} W\)
