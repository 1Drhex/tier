Guest Question.

In the circuit of Fig. 8.67, find:

(a) \(v_{R}\left(0^{+}\right)\) and \(v_{L}\left(0^{+}\right)\)

(b) \(d v_{R}\left(0^{+}\right) / d t\) and \(d v_{L}\left(0^{+}\right) / d t\)

(c) \(v_{R}(\infty)\) and \(v_{L}(\infty)\)

Admin
Solution

(a) Let i the inductor current. For t <0, u(t)=0 so that

\(\mathrm{i}(0)=0 \text { and } \mathrm{v}(0)=0\)

For \(\mathrm{t}>0, \mathrm{u}(\mathrm{t})=1 .\) since , \(\mathrm{v}(0+)=\mathrm{v}(0-)=0,\) and \(\mathrm{i}(0+)=\mathrm{i}(0-)=0\)

\(\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{R}}(0+)=\mathrm{Ri}(0+)={{0} {V}}\)

Also, since \(\mathrm{v}(0+)=\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{R}}(0+)+\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{L}}(0+)=0=0+\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{L}}(0+)\) or \(\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{L}}(0+)={{0} \mathrm{V}}\) (1)

(b) since \(\mathrm{i}(0+)=0, \quad \mathrm{i}_{\mathrm{C}}(0+)=\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{S}} / \mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{S}}\)

But,
\(\mathrm{i}_{\mathrm{C}}=\mathrm{Cdv} / \mathrm{dt} \text { which leads to } \mathrm{d} \mathrm{v}(0+) / \mathrm{dt}=\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{S}} /\left(\mathrm{CR}_{\mathrm{S}}\right)\) (2)

\(\begin{array}{l}\text { From }(1), \quad \mathrm{d} v(0+) / d t=d v_{R}(0+) / d t+d v_{L}(0+) / d t (3)\\\\\quad v_{R}=i R \text { or } d v_{R} / d t=R d i / d t (4)\\\\\text { But, } \quad v_{L}=L d i / d t, \quad v_{L}(0+)=0=\operatorname{Ldi}(0+) / d t \text { and } \operatorname{di}(0+) / d t=0 (5)\end{array}\)

From (4) and (5)

\(\mathrm{d} v_{\mathrm{R}}(0+) / \mathrm{dt}={{0} {V} / {s}}\)

From (2) and (3)

\(\mathrm{d} v_{\mathrm{L}}(0+) / \mathrm{dt}=\mathrm{d} \mathrm{v}(0+) / \mathrm{dt}={\mathrm{V}}_{\mathrm{s}} /\left(\mathrm{CR}_{\mathrm{s}}\right)\)

(c) As t approaches infinity, the capacitor acts like an open circuit, while the inductor acts like a short circuit.

\(\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{R}}(\infty)=\left[{R} /\left({R}+{R}_{\mathrm{s}}\right) \mid {V}_{{s}}\right.\)

\(v_{L}(\infty ) \)= 0 V
