Guest Question.

A cylinder contains 3.00 mol of helium gas at a temperature of 300 K. (a) If the gas is heated at constant volume, how much energy must be transferred by heat to the gas for its temperature to increase to 500 K ?

Admin
Solution

For the constant-volume process, we have

\(Q_{1}=n C_{V} \Delta T\)

Because \(C_{V}=12.5 \mathrm{~J} / \mathrm{mol} \cdot \mathrm{K}\) for helium and \(\Delta T=200 \mathrm{~K}\), we obtain

\(Q_{1}=(3.00 \mathrm{~mol})(12.5 \mathrm{~J} / \mathrm{mol} \cdot \mathrm{K})(200 \mathrm{~K})=7.50 \times 10^{3} \mathrm{~J}\)

(b) How much energy must be transferred by heat to the gas at constant pressure to raise the temperature to \(500 \mathrm{~K}\) ?

Making use of Table 21.2, we obtain

\(Q_{2} =n C_{p} \Delta T=(9.00 \mathrm{~mol})(20.8 \mathrm{~J} / \mathrm{mol} \cdot \mathrm{K})(200 \mathrm{~K}) \) \(=12.5 \times 10^{3} \mathrm{~J}\)
