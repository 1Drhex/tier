Potential Energy Stored in a U-Bolt Calculate the elastic potential energy stored in the two straight sections of the U-bolt examined in and We are tasked with finding the potential energy stored in the straight sections of the U-bolt as a result of the elongation of these sections. With the dimensions and material properties given in those examples, the spring stiffness for one straight section can be found by applying Equation (5.7);

 

\(k=\frac{E A}{L}\) (Equation 5.7)

 

Each straight section of the U-bolt, having length L 5325 mm and crosssectional area \(A=7.854 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2}\), was found to stretch by \(\Delta L=\) \(78.82 \mu m =7.882 \times 10^{-5} m\) The elastic potential energy is found by using Equation (7.2).

 

\(U_{e}=\frac{1}{2} k \Delta L^{2}\) (Equation 7.2)

Admin
Solution

By using the rule-of-thumb value for the elastic modulus of steel

 

\(\begin{gathered}E_{\text {steel }} \approx 210 GPa \approx 30 Mpsi \\E_{\text {aluminum }} \approx 70 GPa \approx 10 Mpsi\end{gathered}\)

 

the stiffness of one of the U-boltâ€™s straight sections is

 

\(\begin{aligned}k &=\frac{\left(210 \times 10^{9} Pa \right)\left(7.854 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2}\right)}{0.325 m } \leftarrow\left[k=\frac{E A}{L}\right] \\&=5.075 \times 10^{7}\left(\frac{ N }{ m ^{2}}\right)\left( m ^{2}\right)\left(\frac{1}{ m }\right) \\&=5.075 \times 10^{7} \frac{ N }{ m }\end{aligned}\)

 

Here we used the definition of the derived unit pascal \(\left(1 Pa =1 N / m ^{2}\right) \text { in }\) manipulating the dimensions for the U-boltâ€™s elastic modulus. The elastic potential energy becomes

 

\(U_{e}=\frac{1}{2}\left(5.075 \times 10^{7} \frac{ N }{ m }\right)\left(7.882 \times 10^{-5} m \right)^{2} \leftarrow\left[U_{e}=\frac{1}{2} k \Delta L^{2}\right]\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&=0.1576\left(\frac{ N }{ m }\right)( m )^{2} \\&=0.1576 N \cdot m \\&=0.1576 J\end{aligned}\)
