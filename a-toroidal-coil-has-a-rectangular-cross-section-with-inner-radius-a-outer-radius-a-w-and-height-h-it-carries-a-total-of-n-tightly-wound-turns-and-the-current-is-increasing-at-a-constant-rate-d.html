Guest Question.

A toroidal coil has a rectangular cross section, with inner radius a, outer radius a + w, and height h. It carries a total of N tightly wound turns, and the current is increasing at a constant rate (d I/dt = k). If w and h are both much less than a, find the electric field at a point z above the center of the toroid. [Hint: Exploit the analogy between Faraday fields and magnetostatic fields, and refer to .]

Admin
Solution

In the quasistatic approximation, \(B = \begin{cases}\frac{\mu_{0} N I}{2 \pi s} \hat{\phi}, & \text { (inside toroid) } \\ 0, & \text { (outside toroid) }\end{cases}\)

(Eq. 5.60). The flux around the toroid is therefore

\(B ( r )=\left\{\begin{array}{cl}\frac{\mu_{0} N I}{2 \pi s} \hat{ \phi }, & \text { for points inside the coil, } \\0 , & \text { for points outside the coil ,}\end{array}\right.\)

 

\(\Phi=\frac{\mu_{0} N I}{2 \pi} \int_{a}^{a+w} \frac{1}{s} h d s=\frac{\mu_{0} N I h}{2 \pi} \ln \left(1+\frac{w}{a}\right) \approx \frac{\mu_{0} N h w}{2 \pi a} I . \quad \frac{d \Phi}{d t}=\frac{\mu_{0} N h w}{2 \pi a} \frac{d I}{d t}=\frac{\mu_{0} N h w k}{2 \pi a}\) .

The electric field is the same as the magnetic field of a circular current (Eq. 5.41):

\(B(z)=\frac{\mu_{0} I}{4 \pi}\left(\frac{\cos \theta}{ᴫ^{2}}\right) 2 \pi R=\frac{\mu_{0} I}{2} \frac{R^{2}}{\left(R^{2}+z^{2}\right)^{3 / 2}}\) (5.41)

\(B =\frac{\mu_{0} I}{2} \frac{a^{2}}{\left(a^{2}+z^{2}\right)^{3 / 2}} \hat{ z }\) ,

with (Eq. 7.19)

\(\oint E \cdot d l =-\frac{d \Phi}{d t}\) (7.19)

\(I \rightarrow-\frac{1}{\mu_{0}} \frac{d \Phi}{d t}=-\frac{N h w k}{2 \pi a} . \quad \text { So } E =\frac{\mu_{0}}{2}\left(-\frac{N h w k}{2 \pi a}\right) \frac{a^{2}}{\left(a^{2}+z^{2}\right)^{3 / 2}} \hat{ z }=-\frac{\mu_{0}}{4 \pi} \frac{N h w k a}{\left(a^{2}+z^{2}\right)^{3 / 2}} \hat{ z }\) .
