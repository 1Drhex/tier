Guest Question.

A plane composite slab with unit crosssectional area is made up material A, (100 mm thick, \(k_A \)= 60 W/m.K) and material B (10 mm thick, \(k_B\) = 2 W/m.K). Thermal contact resistance at the interface is 0.003 \(m^2.K/W\). The temperature of open side of slab A is 300°C and that of open side of slab B is 50°C. Calculate:
(i) Rate of heat flow through the slab.
(ii) Temperature on both sides of interface.

Admin
Solution

Given : A composite wall with contact resistance as shown in Fig.
\(L_A = 100 mm = 0.1 m L_B = 10 mm = 0.01 m\)
\(k_A = 60 W/m.K k_B = 2 W/m.K\)
\(T_1 = 300°C T4 = 50°C\)
\(R_{cont} =0.003 m^2.K/W\)

To find :
(a) Heat transfer rate through slab.
(b) Temperature on both sides interface.
Analysis: Assuming \(1 m^2 \)slab area, and calculating material resistance.
Thermal resistance of material A
\(R_A = \frac{L_A}{k_A}=\frac{0.1}{60}\)
\(= 1.667 × 10^{–3} m^2.K/W\)
Thermal resistance of material B ;
\(R_B = \frac{L_B}{k_B}=\frac{0.01}{2}\)
\(= 0.005 m^2.K/W\)
Total thermal resistance in the circuit
\(∑ R_{th} =R_A + R_{cont} + R_B\)
\(= 1.667 × 10^{–3} + 0.003 + 0.005\)
\(= 9.667 × 10^{–3} m^2.K/W\)
(a) Heat flow rate
\(Q = \frac{∆Τ}{∑R_{th}}\)
\(=\frac{300 – 50}{9.667 × 10^{–3} }\)
= 25,862 W.

(b) Temperature on interfaces.
The heat flow rate in material A is given by
\(Q = \frac{T_1 − T_2}{R_A}\)

or \(T_2 =T_1 – Q R_A\)
\(= 300 – 25,862 × 1.667 × 10^{–3}\)
= 256.9°C.
Heat transfer rate in material B
\(Q = \frac{T_3 − T_4}{R_B}\)
or \(T_3 = Q R_B + T_4\)
\(= 25,862 × 0.005 + 50 = 179.3°C.\)
