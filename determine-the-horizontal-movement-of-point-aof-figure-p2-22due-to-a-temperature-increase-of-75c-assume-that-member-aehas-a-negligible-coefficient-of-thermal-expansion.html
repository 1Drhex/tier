Guest Question.

Determine the horizontal movement of point A of Figure P2.22 due to a temperature increase of 75°C. Assume that member AE has a negligible coefficient of thermal expansion. The coefficients of thermal expansion are \(11.9\times { 10 }^{ -6 }/°C\)\(22.5\times { 10 }^{ -6 }/°C\)for the aluminum alloy.

Admin
Solution

In response to the 75°C temperature increase, the steel piece elongates by the amount
\({ \delta }_{ Steel }={ \alpha }_{ steel }\Delta T\quad { L }_{ steel }=\left( 11.9\times { 10 }^{ -6 }/°C \right) \left( 75°C \right) \left( 300mm \right) =0.267750\quad mm.\)
Thus, joint C moves to the right by 0.267750 mm.
Next, calculate the deformation of the aluminum piece:
\({ \delta }_{ Alum }={ \alpha }_{ Alum }\Delta T{ L }_{ Alum }=(22.5\times { 10 }^{ -6 }/°C)(75°C)(300mm)=0.50625mm\)
Joint E moves to the right by 0.50625 mm.
Take the initial position of Eas the origin. The coordinates of E in the deflected position are (0.50625 mm, 0) and the coordinates of C are (0.267750 mm, 25 mm). Use the deflected-position coordinates of Eand C to determine the slope of the pointer.
\(slope=\frac { { y }_{ c }-{ y }_{ E } }{ { \chi }_{ C }-{ \chi }_{ E } } =\frac { 25mm-0 }{ 0.267750mm-0.50625mm } =\frac { 25mm }{ -0.2385mm } =-104.821803\)
A general equation for the deflected pointer can be expressed as
\(y=m\chi +b=-104.821803\chi +b\)
Use the known coordinates of point Eto determine b:
\(0=-104.821803(0.50625mm)+b\)
\(\therefore b=53.066038mm\)
Thus, the deflected pointer can be described by the line
\(y=-104.821803\chi +53.066038mm\)
or rearranging to solve for x
\(\chi =\frac { (53.066038mm)-y }{ 104.821803 } \)
At pointer tip A, y = 275 mm; therefore, the x coordinate of the pointer tip is
\(\chi =\frac { (53.066038mm-275mm) }{ 104.821803 } =\frac { -221.933962mm }{ 104.821803 } =-2,11725mm=-2.12mm\)
The x coordinate is the same as the horizontal movement since we took the initial position of joint Eas the origin.
