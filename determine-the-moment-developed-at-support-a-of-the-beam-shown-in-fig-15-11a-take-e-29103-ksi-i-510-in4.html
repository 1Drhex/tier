Guest Question.

Determine the moment developed at support \(A\) of the beam shown in Fig. \(15-11 (\mathrm{a})\). Take \(E=29\left(10^{3}\right) \mathrm{ksi}, I=510 \mathrm{in}^{4}\).

Admin
Solution

Notation. Here the beam has two unconstrained degrees of freedom, identified by the code numbers 1 and 2 .

The matrix analysis requires that the external loading be applied at the nodes, and therefore the distributed and concentrated loads are replaced by their equivalent fixed-end moments, which are determined from the table on the inside back cover. (See .) Note that no external loads are placed at ① and no external vertical forces are placed at ② since the reactions at code numbers 3, 4 and 5 are to be unknowns in the load matrix. Using superposition, the results of the matrix analysis for the loading in Fig. 15-11b will later be modified by the loads shown in Fig. 15-11c. From Fig. 15-11b, the known displacement and load matrices are

\(\mathbf{D}_{k}=\left[\begin{array}{l}0 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{array}\right] \begin{array}{l}4 \\ 5 \\6 \end{array} \quad \mathbf{Q}_{k}=\left[\begin{array}{c}144 \\ 1008\end{array}\right] \begin{array}{l}1 \\ 2\end{array}\)

Member Stiffness Matrices. Each of the two member stiffness matrices is determined from Eq. 15-1.

Member 1:

\( \begin{array}{l} \frac{12 E I}{L^{3}}=\frac{12(29)\left(10^{3}\right)(510)}{[24(12)]^{3}}=7.430 \\ \frac{6 E I}{L^{2}}=\frac{6(29)\left(10^{3}\right)(510)}{[24(12)]^{2}}=1069.9 \\ \frac{4 E I}{L}=\frac{4(29)\left(10^{3}\right)(510)}{24(12)}=205417 \\ \frac{2 E I}{L}=\frac{2(29)\left(10^{3}\right)(510)}{24(12)}=102708 \end{array} \)

Member 2:

\( \begin{array}{l} \frac{12 E I}{L^{3}}=\frac{12(29)\left(10^{3}\right)(510)}{[8(12)]^{3}}=200.602 \\ \frac{6 E I}{L^{2}}=\frac{6(29)\left(10^{3}\right)(510)}{[8(12)]^{2}}=9628.91 \\ \frac{4 E I}{L}=\frac{4(29)\left(10^{3}\right)(510)}{8(12)}=616250 \\ \frac{2 E I}{L}=\frac{2(29)\left(10^{3}\right)(510)}{8(12)}=308125 \end{array} \)

Displacements and Loads. We require

\( \mathbf{Q} = \mathbf{KD} \)

Solving in the usual manner,

\( \begin{aligned} 144 &=616250 D_{1}+308125 D_{2} \\ 1008 &=308125 D_{1}+821667 D_{2} \\ D_{1} &=-0.4673\left(10^{-3}\right) \text { in. } \\ D_{2} &=1.40203\left(10^{-3}\right) \text { in. } \end{aligned} \)

Thus,

\( Q_{3}=0+102708(1.40203)\left(10^{-3}\right)=144 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{in.}=12 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \)

The actual moment at \(A\) must include the fixed-supported reaction of \(+96 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft}\) shown in Fig. 15-11c, along with the calculated result for \(Q_{3}\). Thus,

\( M_{A B}=12 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft}+96 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft}=108 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \curvearrowleft \)

This result compares with that determined in .

Although not required here, we can determine the internal moment and shear at \(B\) by considering, for example, member 1 , node 2 , Fig. 15-11b. The result requires expanding

\( \mathbf{q}_{1}=\mathbf{k}_{1} \mathbf{d}+\left(\mathbf{q}_{0}\right)_{1} \)
