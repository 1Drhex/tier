Guest Question.

A hydraulic jump is situated in a 4-m-wide rectangular channel. The discharge in the channel is \(7.5 m^3/s\), and the depth upstream of the jump is \(0.20 m\). Determine the depth downstream of the jump, the upstream and downstream Froude numbers, and the rate of energy dissipated by the jump.

Admin
Solution

Find the unit discharge and upstream Froude number:

\(\begin{aligned}q &=\frac{Q}{b} \\&=\frac{7.5}{4}=1.88 \mathrm{~m}^{2} / \mathrm{s} \\\operatorname{Fr}_{1} &=\frac{q}{\sqrt{g y_{1}^{3}}} \\&=\frac{1.88}{\sqrt{9.81 \times 0.20^{3}}}=6.71\end{aligned}\)

The downstream depth is computed, using Eq. \(10.5 .9\), to be

\(\frac{y_{2}}{y_{1}}=\frac{1}{2}\left(\sqrt{1+8 \mathrm{Fr}_{1}^{2}}-1\right) (10.5.9)\)

\(\begin{aligned}y_{2} &=\frac{y_{1}}{2}\left(\sqrt{1+8 \mathrm{Fr}_{1}^{2}}-1\right) \\&=\frac{0.20}{2}\left(\sqrt{1+8 \times 6.71^{2}}-1\right)=1.80 \mathrm{~m}\end{aligned}\)

The downstream Froude number is

\(\begin{aligned}\operatorname{Fr}_{2} &=\frac{q}{\sqrt{g y_{2}^{3}}} \\&=\frac{1.88}{\sqrt{9.81 \times 1.80^{3}}}=0.25\end{aligned}\)

The head loss in the jump is given by Eq. 10.5.11:

\(h_{j}=\frac{\left(y_{2}-y_{1}\right)^{3}}{4 y_{1} y_{2}} (10.5.11)\)

\(\begin{aligned}h_{j} &=\frac{\left(y_{2}-y_{1}\right)^{3}}{4 y_{1} y_{2}} \\&=\frac{(1.80-0.20)^{3}}{4 \times 0.20 \times 1.80}=2.84 \mathrm{~m}\end{aligned}\)

Hence, the rate of energy dissipation in the jump is (see Eq. 4.5.25)

\(\dot{W}_{T}=\dot{m g} H_{T} \eta_{T}=\gamma Q H_{T} \eta_{T} (4.5.25)\)

\(\gamma Q h_{j}=9800 \times 7.5 \times 2.84=2.09 \times 10^{5} \mathrm{~W} \quad \text { or } \quad 209 \mathrm{~kW}\)
