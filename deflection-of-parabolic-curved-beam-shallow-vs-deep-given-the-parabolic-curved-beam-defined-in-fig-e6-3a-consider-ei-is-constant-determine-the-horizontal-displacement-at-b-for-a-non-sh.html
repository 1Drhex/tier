Deflection of Parabolic Curved Beam—Shallow vs. Deep

Given: The parabolic curved beam defined in Fig. E6.3a. Consider EI is constant.

Determine: The horizontal displacement at B for (a) non-shallow beam and (b) shallow beam.

Admin
Solution

The internal forces for this loading are

 

\(F_{x}=0\; \; \; \;\Rightarrow \; \; \; F=\left ( -\frac{wL}{2}+wx \right )\sin \theta\)

 

\(F_{y}=-\frac{wL}{2}+wx\; \; \; \Rightarrow \; \; \; V=\left ( -\frac{wL}{2}+wx \right )\cos \theta\)

 

\(M=\frac{wL}{2}x-\frac{wx^{2}}{2}\)

 

In order to determine the horizontal displacement at support B, we apply the virtual force system shown in Fig. E6.3c.

 

The internal virtual forces are

 

\(\delta F_{x}=1\; \; \Rightarrow \; \; \delta F=\delta F_{y}\sin \theta +\delta F_{x}\cos \theta =\cos \theta\)

 

\(\delta F_{y}=0\; \; \Rightarrow \; \; \delta V=\delta F_{y}\cos \theta -\delta F_{x}\sin \theta =-\sin \theta\)

 

\(\delta M=y(x)\)

 

\(\tan \theta =\frac{dy}{dx}\)

 

(a) Non-shallow curved member:

 

We use the approximate form defined by (6.10) for a non-shallow curved member.

 

\(u_{B}=\int_{0}^{L}\frac{M\: \delta M}{EI\cos \theta }dx\)

 

Substituting for M, \(\delta M\), and \(\cos \theta \), this expression expands to

 

\(u_{B}=\int_{0}^{L}\left ( \frac{wL}{2}x-\frac{wx^{2}}{2} \right )\left \{ 4h\left ( \frac{x}{L}-\left ( \frac{x}{L} \right )^{2} \right ) \right \}\sqrt{1+(\tan \theta )^{2}\frac{dx}{EI}}\)

 

where

 

\(\tan \theta =4\frac{h}{L}\left ( 1-2\frac{x}{L} \right )\)

 

For EI constant, the solution is expressed as

 

\(u_{B}=\frac{wL^{4}}{EI}(\alpha )\)

 

where \(\alpha\) is a function of h/L. We evaluate \(\alpha\) using numerical integration. The result is plotted below.

 

(b) Shallow curved member:

 

When the parabola is shallow \((\cos \theta \approx 1)\), we need to include the axial deformation term as well as the bending deformation term. Starting with the form specified for a shallow member, (6.11),

 

\(u_{B}=\int_{x}^{}\left ( \frac{F}{AE}\delta F+\frac{M}{EI}\delta M \right )dx\)

 

and noting that

 

\(F\approx \left ( -\frac{wL}{2}+wx \right )\frac{dx}{dy}=-\frac{2wh}{L^{2}}(L-2x)^{2}\)

 

\(M=\frac{wL}{2}x-\frac{wx^{2}}{2}\)

 

\(\delta F_{x}=1\Rightarrow \delta F=\delta F_{x}\cos \approx 1\)

 

\(\delta M=y=4h\left \{ \frac{x}{L}-\left ( \frac{x}{L} \right )^{2} \right \}\)

 

leads to

 

\(u_{B}=-\frac{2}{3}\frac{wLh}{AE}+\frac{1}{15}\frac{whL^{3}}{EI}\)

 

Note that the axial deformation causes the ends to move together, whereas the bending deformation causes the ends to move apart.
