Guest Question.

Design diagram of a nonuniform continuous beam is presented in Fig. 11.24a. The angle of rotation of the clamped support is \(φ_0 = φ = 0.01\) rad and the vertical displacement of the support 2 is \(Δ_2 =Δ= 0.04\) m. Construct the bending moment diagram.

Admin
Solution

Degree of kinematical indeterminacy equals two. Both joints at supports B and C have angular possible displacements. Now we need to present the effect of the settlements of supports in form of moments at the joints B and C. The fixed end moments are

\(M_{AB}= \frac{4EI}{l_{AB}}\varphi = \frac{4EI_0}{6} \times 0.01 =0.00667EI_0\)

 

\(M_{BC}= M_{CB}=\frac{6EI}{l_{BC}^2}\Delta = \frac{6\cdot 2EI_0}{4^2} \times 0.04 =0.03EI_0\)

 

\(M_{BA}=\frac{2EI}{l_{AB}}\varphi = \frac{2EI_0}{6} \times 0.01 =0.00333EI_0\)

 

\(M_{CD}=\frac{3EI}{l_{CD}^2}\Delta = \frac{3\cdot 2EI_0}{4^2} \times 0.04 =0.015EI_0.\)

Corresponding bending moment diagram \(M^0_Δ\) in the primary system of the displacement method and the joint-load diagram are shown in Fig. 11.24b, c.

The positive possible angular displacements of the joints and corresponding possible external forces are presented on the Z-P diagram (Fig. 11.24d). The S-e diagram and positive bending moments diagram are shown in Fig. 11.24e. The vector of external joint loads is

\(\vec{P}= EI_0\left\lfloor\begin{matrix} 0.02667 & 0.015 \end{matrix} \right\rfloor^T.\)

Vector of fixed end moments in the first state is

\(\vec{S}_1= EI_0\left\lfloor\begin{matrix} 0.00667 & 0.00333 – 0.03 -\begin{matrix} 0.03 & 0.015 \end{matrix} \end{matrix} \right\rfloor ^T\)

Static matrix Considering the Z-P and S-e diagrams we get

\( P_1= S_2 +S_3\)

 

\( P_2 = S_4 + S_5\)

so the static matrix becomes

\(A_{(2\times 5)}=\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & 1 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

Stiffness matrix Stiffness matrices for finite elements AB, BC, and CD in local coordinates are

\(K_1 =\frac{EI_1}{l_1} \left[\begin{matrix} 4 & 2 \\ 2 & 4 \end{matrix} \right] = \frac{EI_0}{6} \left[\begin{matrix} 4 & 2 \\ 2 & 4 \end{matrix} \right]= EI_0\left[\begin{matrix} 0.667 & 0.333 \\ 0.333 & 0.667 \end{matrix} \right] ,\)

 

\(K_2 =\frac{EI_2}{l_2} \left[\begin{matrix} 4 & 2 \\ 2 & 4 \end{matrix} \right] = \frac{2EI_0}{4} \left[\begin{matrix} 4 & 2 \\ 2 & 4 \end{matrix} \right]= EI_0\left[\begin{matrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{matrix} \right] ,\)

 

\(K_3 =\frac{EI_3}{l_3}[3] = \frac{2EI_0}{4}[3]=EI_0[1.5]\)

Stiffness matrix for structure in whole in local coordinates is

\(\tilde{K}_{(5\times 5)}= \left [ \begin{matrix} 0.667 & 0.333 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0.333 & 0.667 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 1.5 \end{matrix} \right ] EI_0\)

Matrix procedures Intermediate matrix complex

\(\tilde{K}A^T= EI_0 \left [ \begin{matrix} 0.667 & 0.333 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0.333 & 0.667 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 1.5 \end{matrix} \right ] \times \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ] = EI_0 \left [ \begin{matrix} 0.333 & 0 \\ 0.667 & 0 \\ 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1.5 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

Stiffness matrix for structure in whole in global coordinates and inverse stiffness matrix are

\(K = A\cdot \bar{K}A^T = \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & 1 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\times EI_0\left [ \begin{matrix} 0.333 & 0 \\ 0.667 & 0 \\ 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1.5 \end{matrix} \right ]= EI_0\left [ \begin{matrix} 2.667 & 1 \\ 1 & 3.5 \end{matrix} \right ] ,\)

 

\(K^{-1}= \frac{1}{EI_0}\left [ \begin{matrix} 0.42 & -0.12 \\ -0.12 & 0.32 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

Vector of joint displacements

\(\vec{Z}=K^{-1}\vec{P} =\frac{1}{EI_0} \left [ \begin{matrix} 0.42 & -0.12 \\ -0.12 & 0.32 \end{matrix} \right ]\times EI_0\left\lceil\begin{matrix} 0.02667 \\ 0.015 \end{matrix} \right\rceil= \left\lceil\begin{matrix} 0.0094 \\ 0.0016 \end{matrix} \right\rceil\)

Vector of unknown bending moments of the second state is

\(\vec{S}_2= \tilde{K}A^T\cdot Z = EI_0\left [ \begin{matrix} 0.333 & 0 \\ 0.667 & 0 \\ 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1.5 \end{matrix} \right ] \times \left\lceil\begin{matrix} 0.0094 \\ 0.0016 \end{matrix} \right\rceil = EI_0\left\lceil \begin{matrix} 0.00313 \\ 0.00627 \\ 0.0204 \\ 0.0126 \\ 0.0024 \end{matrix} \right\rceil\)

The final vector of required bending moments (kNm) is

\(\vec{S}= \vec{S}_1+ \tilde{K}A^TZ= EI_0\left\lceil \begin{matrix} 0.00667 \\ 0.00333 \\ -0.3 \\ -0.3 \\ 0.015 \end{matrix} \right\rceil + EI_0\left\lceil \begin{matrix} 0.00313 \\ 0.00627 \\ 0.0204 \\ 0.0126 \\ 0.0024 \end{matrix} \right\rceil = EI_0\left\lceil \begin{matrix} 0.0098 \\ 0.0096 \\ – 0.0096 \\ – 0.0174 \\ 0.0174 \end{matrix} \right\rceil\)

Corresponding final bending moment diagram is presented in Fig. 11.24f.
