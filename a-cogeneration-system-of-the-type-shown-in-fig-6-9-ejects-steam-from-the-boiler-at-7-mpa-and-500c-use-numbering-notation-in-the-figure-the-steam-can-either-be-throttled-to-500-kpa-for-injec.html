Guest Question.

A cogeneration system of the type shown in Fig. 6-9 ejects steam from the boiler at 7 MPa and 500°C. (Use numbering notation in the figure.) The steam can either be throttled to 500 kPa for injection into the process heater, or expanded in a turbine, where it can be partially expanded to 500 kPa for the process heater, or fully expanded and condensed at 5 kPa. The function of the process heater is controlled so that the fluid leaves saturated liquid at 500 kPa. Separate pumps compress the fluid to 7 MPa and mix it in a mixing chamber before returning it to the boiler. Calculated process heat output, net electrical output, and utilization for the following two cases:

1. All steam is fed to the turbine and then to the process heater (e.g., m\(_2\) = m\(_6\) = 0).

2. Ten percent of the steam is fed to the expansion valve, 70% partially expanded and fed to the process heater, and 20% fully expanded.

Admin
Solution

First, calculate all known values of enthalpy that apply to both (1) and (2)—refer to Fig. 6-10.

There is no change in enthalpy across the expansion valve, so from the steam tables we have

\(h_{1}=h_{2}=h_{3}=h_{4}=3411.4 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}\)

 

Using the isentropic relationship s\(_{1}\) = s\(_{5}\) = s\(_{6}\) and evaluating the quality of the steam at states 5 and 6, we obtain

\(\begin{aligned} &h_{5}=2739.3 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \\ &h_{6}=2073.0 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \end{aligned}\)

 

Enthalpies h\(_7\) and h\(_8\) are saturated fluids at 500 kPa and 5 kPa, respectively, so from the tables h\(_7\) = 640.1 and h\(_8\) = 137.8 kJ/kg. In order to evaluate h\(_9\) and h\(_{10}\), we need to know the pump work per unit of mass. Using specific volumes of v\(_{@ 5kPa}\) = 1.005 × 10\(^{−3}\) m\(^3\)/kg and v\(_{@ 5kPa}\) = 1.093 × 10\(^{−3}\) m\(^{3}\)/kg, we calculate the corresponding values of pump work as 7.0 and 7.1 kJ/kg for the two pumps, respectively.

We then obtain

\(\begin{aligned} &h_{9}=137.8+7.0=144.8 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \\ &h_{10}=640.1+7.1=647.2 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \end{aligned}\)

 

The remaining value of h\(_{11}\) depends on the specific circumstances of the mixing chamber.

Case 1. Since m\(_{9}\) = 0, m\(_{11}\) = m\(_{10}\) and h\(_{11}\) = h\(_{10}\) = 647.2 kJ/kg.

Therefore, heat input is

Q\(_{in}\) = 15 kg/s × (3411.4 − 647.2) = 41, 463 kW

Pump work for pump 1 is calculated as follows:

W\(_{P1}\) = (7.1 kJ/kg)(15 kg/s) = 105.5 kW

Given the enthalpy change for the partially expanded steam and the flow rate m\(_3\) = m\(_5\) = 15 kg/s, power output and net work from the turbine are respectively

\(\begin{aligned} &W_{\text {out }}=15(3411.4-2739.3)=10081 \mathrm{~kW} \\ &W_{\text {net }}=W_{\text {out }}-W_{\text {pump }}=10081-105.5=9975 \mathrm{~kW} \end{aligned}\)

 

From Eq. (6-10), process heat is

\(m_{v \text {.in }} h_{v \text {.in }}+m_{t \text {.in }} h_{t \text {.in }}=m_{\text {out }} h_{\text {out }}+Q_{p}\) (6-10)

\(Q_{p}=15(2739.3-640.2)=31,488 \mathrm{~kW}\)

 

It can now be confirmed that utilization is 100%:

\(\varepsilon_{u}=\frac{W_{\text {net }}+Q_{p}}{Q_{\text {In }}}=\frac{9975+31488}{41,463}=1.00\)

 

Case 2. Based on the allocation of input steam from the boiler, we have m\(_2 \) = 1.5, m\(_5 \)= 10.5, and m\(_6\) = 3 kg/s. Thus m\(_9 \)= 3 and m\(_{10}\) = 10.5 + 1.5 = 12 kg/s. Solving for h\(_{11}\) using Eq. (6-12) gives

\(h_{\text {out }}=\frac{m_{1 . \text { in }} h_{1 . \text { in }}+m_{2 \text { in }} h_{2 \text { in }}}{m_{\text {out }}}\) (6-12)

\(h_{11}=\frac{m_{9} h_{9}+m_{10} h_{10}}{m_{11}}=\frac{3(144.8)+12(647.2)}{15}=546.8 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}\)

 

Heat input is then

Q\(_{in}\) = 15 kg/s(3410.3 − 546.8) = 42, 952 kW

Turbine work now consists of two stages, so we account for work done by m\(_3\) = m\(_1\) – m\(_2 \) = 13.5 and by m\(_6\) = 3 kg/s:

W\(_{out}\)= 13.5(3411.4 − 2739.3) + 3(2739.3 − 2073.0) = 11072 kW

 

Subtracting pump work for both pumps 1 and 2 gives the net work:

\(W_{\text {ret }}=W_{\text {out }}-W_{\text {pumpl } 1}-W_{\text {pump2 }}=11,072-21.1-85.3=10,966 \mathrm{~kW}\)

 

On the process heat side, input to the heater comes both from the expansion valve and the turbine so

Q\(_p\)= 1.5(3411.4) + 10.5(2739.3) − 12(640.1) = 26199 kW

Finally, utilization in this case is

\(\varepsilon_{u}=\frac{10966+26199}{42952}=0.865\)

 

Discussion Case 1 illustrates how the operator might vary allocation between the turbine and process heater to match process heat output to demand, with remaining energy allocated to electricity production. Also, although utilization decreases in case 1 compared to case 2, electricity production increases, so that, with the value of electricity often two or three times that of process heat on a per-kW basis, it may be preferable to operate using the allocation in case 2.
