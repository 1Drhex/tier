Guest Question.

The plan of a uniformly loaded rectangular area is shown in Figure 6.23a. Determine the vertical stress increase, Δσ, below point A′ at a depth z = 4 m.

Admin
Solution

The stress increase, \(\Delta\sigma\) can be written as

\(\Delta\sigma = \Delta\sigma_{1} – \Delta\sigma_{2}\)

where \(\Delta\sigma_{1} = \text{stress increase due to the loaded area shown in Figure 6.23b}\\ \Delta\sigma_{2} =\text{stress increase due to the loaded area shown in Figure 6.23c} \)

For the loaded area shown in Figure 6.23b:

\( m^{\prime} = \frac{B}{z} = \frac{2}{4} = 0.5\\n^{\prime} = \frac{L}{z} = \frac{4}{4} = 1\)

From Figure 6.21 for m′ = 0.5 and n′ = 1, the value of \(I_{5}\) = 0.1225. So \(\Delta\sigma_{1} = qI_{5} =(150)(0.1225) = 18.38 kN/m^{2}\)
Similarly, for the loaded area shown in Figure 6.23c:

\(m^{\prime} = \frac{B}{z} = \frac{1}{4} = 0.25\\n^{\prime} = \frac{L}{z} = \frac{2}{4} = 0.5\\\text{Thus, }I_{5} = 0.0473., \text{Hence}\\\Delta\sigma_{2} = (150)(0.0473) = 7.1\,kN/m^{2}\\So\\\Delta\sigma = \Delta\sigma_{1} – \Delta\sigma_{2} = 18.38 – 7.1 = \textbf{11.28 kN/}\textbf{m}^{\textbf{2}}\)
