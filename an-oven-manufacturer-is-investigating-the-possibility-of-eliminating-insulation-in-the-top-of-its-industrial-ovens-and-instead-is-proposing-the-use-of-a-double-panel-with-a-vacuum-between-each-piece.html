Guest Question.

An oven manufacturer is investigating the possibility of eliminating insulation in the top of its industrial ovens and instead is proposing the use of a double panel with a vacuum between each piece. Figure 12.18 shows the top consisting of two sheet-steel surfaces (oxidized) separated by a 3 cm gap.In the worst case the temperature of the inside surface is 260°C, and correspondingly, an exterior-surface temperature of 50°C is acceptable (for safety reasons). Determine the heat lost by the oven through its top surface.

Admin
Solution

With an evacuated gap the heat lost by conduction and convection through the double panel is eliminated or at least considered negligible compared to the heat transferred by radiation. The energy transferred from the warmer panel is radiated in all directions, including the edges.

Assumptions
1. The sheet metal is thin enough that the temperatures given are the inside-surface
temperatures
2. Both sheet metal pieces are isothermal
3. The plates are very large and spaced close together, so they can be considered infinite (i.e., edge effects are neglected)
4. The surfaces emit energy diffusely and can be considered gray
As indicated in Figure 12.18, the warmer surface is \(A_{1}\). A consequence of the third assumption is that all energy leaving \(A_{1}\) is intercepted by \(A_{2}\), and vice versa. Th erefore, the view factors become

\(F_{1- 2} =F_{2- 1} =1\)

 

Because the surfaces are flat

\(F_{1- 1} =F_{2- 2} =0\)

 

Equation 12.30 applies and, using it, we get

\(\frac{q_{k}}{A_{k}\epsilon _{k}}-\sum\limits_{m=1}^{N}{F_{k-m}\frac{q_{m}}{A_{m}}\frac{(1-\epsilon _{m})}{\epsilon _{m}} } =\sigma T^{4}_{k}-\sum\limits_{m=1}^{N}{F_{k-m}\sigma T^{4}_{m}} \) (12.30)

 

\( k=1: \frac{q_{1}^{\prime \prime}}{\varepsilon_{1}}-F_{1-1} q_{1}^{\prime \prime} \frac{\left(1-\varepsilon_{1}\right)}{\varepsilon_{1}}-F_{1-2} q_{2}^{\prime \prime} \frac{\left(1-\varepsilon_{2}\right)}{\varepsilon_{2}}=\sigma T_{1}^{4}-F_{1-1} \sigma T_{1}^{4}-F_{1-2} \sigma T_{2}^{4}\)

 

\(k=2: \frac{q_{2}^{\prime \prime}}{\varepsilon_{2}}-F_{2-1} q_{1}^{\prime \prime} \frac{\left(1-\varepsilon_{1}\right)}{\varepsilon_{1}}-F_{2-2} q_{2}^{\prime \prime \prime} \frac{\left(1-\varepsilon_{2}\right)}{\varepsilon_{2}}=\sigma T_{2}^{4}-F_{2-1} \sigma T_{1}^{4}-F_{2-2} \sigma T_{2}^{4}\)

 

Substituting for the view factors, we reduce these equations to

\(\frac{q_{1}^{\prime \prime}}{\varepsilon_{1}}-q_{2}^{\prime \prime} \frac{\left(1-\varepsilon_{2}\right)}{\varepsilon_{2}}=\sigma T_{1}^{4}-\sigma T_{2}^{4}\)

 

\(\frac{q_{2}^{\prime \prime}}{\varepsilon_{2}}-q_{1}^{\prime \prime} \frac{\left(1-\varepsilon_{1}\right)}{\varepsilon_{1}}=\sigma T_{2}^{4}-\sigma T_{1}^{4}\)

 

We now solve by eliminating \(q_{2}^{\prime \prime}\) from the equations. Solving the first equation for \(q_{2}^{\prime \prime}\) gives

 

\(q_{2}^{\prime \prime}=\frac{\varepsilon_{2}}{1-\varepsilon_{2}}\left[\frac{q_{1}^{\prime \prime}}{\varepsilon_{1}}+\sigma T_{2}^{4}-\sigma T_{1}^{4}\right]\) (i)

 

Substituting into the second equation, we get

 

\(\frac{1}{1-\varepsilon_{2}}\left(\frac{q_{1}^{\prime \prime}}{\varepsilon_{1}}+\sigma T_{2}^{4}-\sigma T_{1}^{4}\right)-q_{1}^{\prime \prime} \frac{\left(1-\varepsilon_{1}\right)}{\varepsilon_{1}}=\sigma T_{2}^{4}-\sigma T_{1}^{4}\)

 

After considerable manipulation, we finally obtain

\(q_{1}^{\prime \prime}=\frac{\sigma\left(T_{1}^{4}-T_{2}^{4}\right)}{1 / \varepsilon_{1}+1 / \varepsilon_{2}-1}\) (ii)

 

From Appendix Table E.1 for oxidized steel, \(\varepsilon=0.94.\) Both surfaces are the same, so \(\varepsilon_{1}=\varepsilon_{2}=0.94. \)With \(T_{1}=260^{\circ} {C}=533 {~K} and T_{2}=50^{\circ} {C}=323 {~K},\) we substitute into Equation ii to get

 

\(q^{\prime \prime }_{1} =\frac{5.67\times 10^{-8}\left(533^{4}-323^{4} \right) }{1/0.94+1/0.94-1}\)

 

or

 

\(q^{\prime \prime }_{1} =3501W/m^{2}\)

 

If we substitute Equation ii into Equation i, we will find that

\(q^{\prime \prime }_{2}=-q^{\prime \prime }_{1}=-3510W/m^{2}\)

 

The result that \(q^{\prime \prime }_{1}=-q^{\prime \prime }_{2}\) is evident also from Figure 12.18.
