Guest Question.

Aluminum pin fins of parabolic profile with blunt tips are attached on a plane wall with surface temperature of 200°C (Fig. 3–49). Each fin has a length of 20 mm and a base diameter of 5 mm. The fins are exposed to an ambient air condition of 25°C and the convection heat transfer coefficient is \( 50 W / m ^{2} \cdot K \). If the thermal conductivity of the fins is \( 240 W / m ^{2} \cdot K \) determine the efficiency, heat transfer rate, and effectiveness of each fin.

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION The efficiency, heat transfer rate, and effectiveness of a pin fin of parabolic profile with blunt tips are to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Heat conduction is steady and one-dimensional. 2 Thermal properties are constant. 3 Heat transfer by radiation is negligible.

Properties The thermal conductivity of the fin is given as \( 240 W / m ^{2} \cdot K \)

Analysis From Table 3–3, for pin fins of parabolic profile (blunt tip), we have

\( m L=\sqrt{\frac{4 h}{k D}} L=\sqrt{\frac{4\left(50 W / m ^{2} \cdot K \right)}{(240 W / m \cdot K )(0.005 m )}}(0.020 m )=0.2582 \)

 

\( A_{\text {fin }}=\frac{\pi D^{4}}{96 L^{2}}\left\{\left[16\left(\frac{L}{D}\right)^{2}+1\right]^{3/2}-1\right\}=\frac{\pi(0.005 m )^{4}}{96(0.020 m )^{2}}\left\{\left[16\left(\frac{0.020 m }{0.005 m }\right)^{2}+1\right]^{3 / 2}-1\right\}\)

 

\( =2.106 \times 10^{-4} m ^{2} \)

 

\( \eta_{ fin }=\frac{3}{2 m L} \frac{I_{1}(4 mL / 3)}{I_{0}(4 mL / 3)}=\frac{3}{2(0.2582)} \frac{I_{1}[4(0.2582) / 3]}{I_{0}[4(0.2582) / 3]}=5.8095 \frac{I_{1}[0.3443]}{I_{0}[0.3443]} \)

 

The values of the Bessel functions corresponding to x = 0.3443 are determined from Table 3–4 to be

\( \mid l_{0}=1.0350 \) and \( i_{1}=0.1716 \) .Substituting, the fin efficiency is determined to be \( \eta_{ fin }=5.8095 \frac{0.1716}{1.0350}=0.9632 \)

The heat transfer rate for a single fin is

\( \dot{Q}_{ fin }=\eta_{ fin } h A_{ fin }\left(T_{b}-T_{\infty}\right) \)

 

\( =(0.9632)\left(50 W / m ^{2} \cdot K \right)\left(2.106 \times 10^{-4} m ^{2}\right)(200-25)^{\circ} C =1.77 W \)

 

The fin effectiveness is

\( \varepsilon_{ fin }=\frac{\dot{Q}_{ fin }}{h A_{b}\left(T_{b}-T_{\infty}\right)}=\frac{\dot{Q}_{ fin }}{h\left(\pi D^{2} / 4\right)\left(T_{b}-T_{\infty}\right)} \)

 

\( =\frac{1.77 W }{\left(50 W / m ^{2} \cdot K \right)\left[\pi(0.005 m )^{2} / 4\right](200-25)^{\circ} C } \) =10.3

 

That is, over a 10-fold increase in heat transfer is achieved by using a pin fin in this case.

Discussion The fin efficiency can be determined more accurately by avoiding the interpolation error by using an equation solver with built-in mathematical functions such as EES. Copying the line

eta_fin=3/(2*0.2582)*Bessel_|1(4*0.2582/3)/Bessel_|0 (4*0.2582/3)

on a blank EES screen and hitting the ‘solve’ button gives the fin efficiency to be \( \eta_{ fin }=0.9855 \) which is about 2 percent higher than the result obtained above using the tables.
