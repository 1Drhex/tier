Guest Question.

In a steam nozzle steam expands from 16 bar to 5 bar with initial temperature of 300°C and mass flow of 1 kg/s. Determine the throat and exit areas considering (i) expansion to be frictionless and, (ii) friction loss of 10% throughout the nozzle.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{Let us assume velocity at inlet to nozzle to be negligible. From steam table, At inlet to the nozzle, enthalpy,}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad h_{1}&= \text{3034.8 kJ/kg at 16 bar, 300°C}\\s_{1}&= 6.8844 kJ/kg·K\\v_{1}&= 0.15862 m^{3}/kg\end{aligned}\\\textit{(i) Considering expansion to be frictionless}\\\text{Pressure at throat of nozzle, }p_{2} = p_{1} \left\lgroup\frac{2}{n + 1} \right\rgroup ^{n/n – 1}\text{, for n = 1.3, }p_{2} = 8.73 bar\\\text{Enthalpy at throat and exit of nozzle can be looked from steam table as,}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad h_{2}&= 2891.39 kJ/kg\\h_{3}&= 2777.0 kJ/kg\\v_{2}&= 0.2559 m^{3}/kg\\v_{3}&= 0.3882 m^{3}/kg\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Heat drop up to throat section}&= h_{1} – h_{2}\\Δq_{12}&= 143.41 kJ/kg\\\text{Velocity at throat,} \qquad\quad C_{2}&= \sqrt{2(h_{1} – h_{2})} = 535.56 m/s\\\text{Heat drop from throat to exit}&= h_{2} – h_{3}\\Δq_{23}&= 114.39 kJ/kg\\\text{Velocity at exit,}\qquad \qquad C_{3}&= \sqrt{2(h_{2} – h_{3}) + C_{2}^{2}}\\C_{3}&= 718.06 m/s\\\text{Throat area, A}_{2}&= \frac{m_{2} × v_{2}}{C_{2}} = \frac{1 × 0.2559}{535.56} = 4.78 × 10^{–4} m^{2}\\\text{Exit area, A}_{3}&= \frac{m_{3} × v_{3}}{C_{3}} = \frac{1 × 0.3882}{718.06} = 5.41 × 10^{–4} m^{2}\end{aligned}\\\text{For frictionless expansion,}\\\text{Throat area = }4.78 cm^{2}\\\text{Exit area = }5.41 cm^{2}\\\textit{(ii) Considering expansion to have 10}\%\textit{ friction loss:}\\\text{Due to frictional heat loss actual heat drop shall get reduced.}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Actual heat drop upto throat,} Δq^{\prime}_{12}&= 0.9 × q_{12}\\Δq^{\prime}_{12} &= 129.07 kJ/kg\\\text{Thus, enthalpy at throat, }h_{\text{2 , actual}}&= 2905.73 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{from steam table, }v_{\text{2, actual}} = 0.2598 m^{3}/kg\text{ corresponding to the modified state as indicated by enthalpy, } h_{\text{2, actual}}.\text{ This new state at throat shall have temperature}\\\text{of 231.35°C, which can be calculated from known enthalpy }h_{\text{2, actual}}\text{ at respective pressure.}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Actual velocity at throat,} C_{\text{2, actual}}&= \sqrt{2 × 129.07 × 10^{3}}\\C_{\text{2, actual}}&= 508.1 m/s\\\text{Actual throat area, }\qquad A_{\text{2, actual}}&= \frac{1 × 0.2598}{508.1}\end{aligned}\\\qquad \qquad \text{Actual throat area} = 5.11 × 10^{–4} m^{2} or, 5.11 cm^{2}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Actual heat drop from throat to exit of nozzle, }Δq^{\prime}_{23}&= 0.9 × 114.39\\Δq^{\prime}_{23}&= 102.95 kJ/kg\\\text{Thus, enthalpy at exit,} \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad h_{\text{3, actual}}&= 2905.73 – 102.95\\&= 2802.78 kJ/kg\\\text{From steam table, corresponding to modified state }&\text{as given by }h_{\text{3, actual}}\text{ the specific volume can be given as, }v_{\text{3, actual}} = 0.40023 m^{3}/kg\\\text{Actual velocity at exit,}\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad C_{\text{3, actual}}&= \sqrt{(2 × 102.95 × 10^{3}) + (508.1)^{2}}\\C_{\text{3, actual}}&= 681.22 m/s\\\text{Actual area at exit}\qquad\quad \qquad\qquad \qquad ⇒A_{\text{3, actual}}&= \frac{1 × 0.40023}{681.22}\\A_{\text{3, actual}}&= 5.875 × 10^{–4} m^{2}\end{aligned}\\\qquad \text{Actual area at exit, }= 5.875 × 10^{–4} m^{2} or 5.875 cm^{2}\)
