Guest Question.

Two infinitely long vertical plates are pamllel to each other. Both the plates are black and are at tempemtures \(T_{1}=1500 K\) and \(T_{2}=900 K\) respectively. The spacing between the two plates is 1.5m and is filled with a gmy gas at 1200K with an absorption coefficient of \(0.08 m^{-1}\). Determine the heat tmnsfer rate at each of the two boundaries.

Admin
Solution

We can use superposition solving, for the left and right walls separately. For one wall, from left to right we consider in the positive x direction and for the other we consider the negative x direction. We need to take the algebraic sum of the heat fluxes. The heat flux expression must be asymptotically correct, such that when \(\kappa=0\), we must get the original parallel plate formula for

\(q=\sigma\left(T_{1}^{4}-T_{2}^{4}\right) \text { when } \epsilon_{1}=\epsilon_{2}=1\)

Left wall:

Heat flux is transferred from left wall to the right wall.

\(q_{0}=2 \sigma T_{1}^{4} E_{3}\left(\tau_{x}\right)+\sigma T_{g}^{4}\left[1-2 E_{3}\left(\tau_{x}\right)\right]\) (5.72)

Right wall:

\(q_{L}=2 \sigma T_{2}^{A} E_{3}\left(\tau_{L}-\tau_{x}\right)+\sigma T_{g}^{4}\left[1-2 E_{3}\left(\tau_{L}-\tau_{x}\right)\right]\) (5.73)

Heat flux is transferred from the right wall to the left wall.

\(\text { Net radiative flux at } x =q_{0}+q_{L}\) (5.74)

\(\text { Special case: } \kappa=0\) (5.75)

\(q_{0}=\sigma T_{1}^{4}\) (5.76)

\(q_{L}=\sigma T_{2}^{A}\) (5.77)

\(\text { Net radiative flux at } x =q_{0}-q_{L}=\sigma\left(T_{1}^{4}-T_{2}^{4}\right)\) (5.78)

The contrihution for the gas volume term is 0 from both the left and the right. This is consistent with our understanding of the problem. Now, substituting the numerical values in the equations for the problem under consideration, we get \(q_{0} \text { and } q_{L} . \text { At } x =0\)

\(E_{3}(0)=0.5\) (5.79)

\(q_{0}=2 \sigma T_{1}^{4} E_{3}\left(\tau_{x}\right)+\sigma T_{g}^{4}\left[1-2 E_{3}\left(\tau_{x}\right)\right]\)

 

\(=2 \times 5.67 \times 10^{-8} \times 1500^{4} \times 0.5+0\)

\(=287.043 kW / m ^{2}\) (5.80)

\(E_{3}\left(\tau_{L}-\tau_{x}\right)=E_{3}(0.12)=0.40\) (5.81)

\(q_{L}=2 \sigma T_{2}^{4} \times 0.40+\sigma T_{g}^{4}[12 \times 0.40]\)

 

\(=29961.5807+22879.7291\)

\(=52.84 kW / m ^{2}\) (5.82)

\(q_{n e t}=q_{0}-q_{L}=234.20 k W / m^{2}\) (5.83)
