Guest Question.

Motion of the particle along a straight line is defined by \( ν^3 = 64s^2 \) where ν is in m/s and s is in m. Determine the

(i) velocity when distance covered is 8 m,

(ii) acceleration when distance covered is 27 m and,

(iii) acceleration when the velocity is 9 m/s.

Admin
Solution

(i) \( ν^3 = 64 s^2 \) At s = 8 m; ν = ?.

∴ \( ν^3 = 64 (8)^2 \) ∴ ν = 16 m/s Ans.

(ii) At s = 27 m ; a = ?.

\( ν^3 = 64 s^2 \).

\( 3ν^2 . \frac{dν}{dt} = 64(2s) \bigl(\frac{ds}{dt}\bigr) \).

\( 3ν^2 . (a) = 128 . s (ν) \).

∴ \( a = \frac{128 . s }{3ν} \) .

At s = 27 m.

\( ν^3 = 64 (27)^2 \).

\( ν = 36 \) m/s.

∴ \( a = \frac{128 × 27 }{3× 36} \) ∴ a = 32 m/s² Ans.

OR

\( ν^3 = 64 s^2 \).

\( 3ν^2 . \frac{dν}{ds} = 64(2s) \).

\( 3 \bigl[ν .\frac{dν}{ds}\bigr] ν = 128 s \).

3(a) . ν = 128 s.

\( a = \frac{128 s}{3ν} \) .

∴ \( a = \frac{128 × 27}{3 × 36} \) ∴ a = 32 m/s² Ans.

(iii) At ν = 9 m/s ; a = ?

\( ν^3 = 64 s^2 \).

\( (9)^3 = 64 s^2 \) ∴ s = 3.375 m.

∴ \( a = \frac{128 . s}{3 ν} = \frac{128 × 3.375}{3 × 9}\)

∴ a = 16 m/s² Ans.
