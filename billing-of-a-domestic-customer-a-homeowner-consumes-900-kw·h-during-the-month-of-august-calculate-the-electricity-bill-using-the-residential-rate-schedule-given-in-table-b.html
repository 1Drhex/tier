Guest Question.

Billing of a domestic customer
A homeowner consumes 900 kW·h during the month of August. Calculate the electricity bill using the residential rate schedule given in Table B.

 

TABLE B TYPICAL RATE STRUCTURES
Rate schedule
Demand charge: First 75 000 kW of demand per month at $2.50 per kW
Excess over 75 000 kW of demand per month at $2.00 per kW
Extra charge for any demand in excess of customer’s contract demand at $2.20 per month per kW
Energy charge: First 20 million kW⋅h per month at 6.1 mills per kW⋅h*
Next 30 million kW⋅h per month at 6.0 mills per kW⋅h
Additional energy at 5.9 mills per kW⋅h
*1 mill = one thousandth of a dollar, or one tenth of a cent.
Admin
Solution
\(\begin{array}{lr}\text { Minimum charge } & =\$ 5.00 \\\text { First } 100 kW \cdot h @ 5 \text { cents/kW} \cdot h & =5.00 \\\text { Next } 200 kW \cdot h @ 3 cents / kW \cdot h & =6.00 \\\text { Remaining energy consumed } & \\(900-300)=600 kW \cdot h & =12.00 \\600 kW \cdot h @ 2 \text { cents/kW} \cdot h & \$ 28.00\end{array}\)

 

This represents an average cost of 2800/900 = 3.11 cent/kW·h.

 

Demand meters are not usually installed in homes because the maximum demand seldom exceeds 10 kW.
