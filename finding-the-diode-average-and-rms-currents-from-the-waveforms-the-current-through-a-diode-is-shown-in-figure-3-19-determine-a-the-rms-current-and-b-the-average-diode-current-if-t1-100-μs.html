Guest Question.

Finding the Diode Average and rms Currents from the Waveforms

The current through a diode is shown in Figure 3.19. Determine (a) the rms current and (b) the average diode current if \(t_1 = 100 μs, t_2 = 350 μs, t_3 = 500 μs, f = 250 Hz, f_s = 5 kHz, I_m = 450 A, and I_a = 150 A\).

Admin
Solution

a. The rms value is defined as

\(I_{D(rms)}=\left[\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^{t_1}{(I_m \sin ω_s t)^2 dt}+\frac{1}{T}\int_{t_2}^{t_3}{I_a^2 dt} \right]^{1/2} \) (3.45)

 

\(= (I_{t1}^2 + I_{t2}^2 )^{1/2}\)

where \(ω_s = 2πf_s = 31,415.93 rad/s, t_1 = π/ω_s = 100 μs\), and \(T = 1/f\).

 

\(I_{D(rms)}=\left[\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^{t_1}{(I_m \sin ω_s t)^2 dt} \right]^{1/2}=I_m\sqrt{\frac{ft_1}{2} }\) (3.46)

= 50.31 A

and

\(I_{D(rms)}=\left(\frac{1}{T}\int_{t_2}^{t_3}{I_a dt} \right)^2=I_a\sqrt{f(t_3 -t_2)}\) (3.47)

= 29.05 A

Substituting Eqs. (3.46) and (3.47) in Eq. (3.45), the rms value is

\(I_{D(rms)}=\left[\frac{I_m^2 ft_1}{2}+ I_a^2 f(t_3 – t_2) \right]^{1/2}\) (3.48)

\(= (50.31^2 + 29.05^2)^{1/2} = 58.09 A\)

b. The average current is found from

\(I_{D(av)}=\left[\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^{t_1}{(I_m \sin ω_s t) dt}+\frac{1}{T}\int_{t_2}^{t_3}{I_adt} \right]^{1/2}\)

 

\(= I_{D1(av)} + I_{D2(av)}\)

where

\(I_{d1}=\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^{t_1}{(I_m \sin ω_s t) dt}=\frac{I_mf}{\pi f_s}\) (3.49)

 

\(I_{d2}=\frac{1}{T}\int_{t_2}^{t_3}{I_a dt}=I_a f(t_3 – t_2)\) (3.50)

Therefore, the average current becomes

\(I_{dc}=\frac{I_m f}{\pi f_s}+ I_a f(t_3 – t_2) = 7.16 + 5.63 = 12.79 A\)
