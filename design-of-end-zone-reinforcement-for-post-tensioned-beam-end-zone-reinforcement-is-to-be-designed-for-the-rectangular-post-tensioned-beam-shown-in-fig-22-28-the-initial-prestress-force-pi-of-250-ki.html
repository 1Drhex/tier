Guest Question.

Design of end-zone reinforcement for post-tensioned beam. End-zone reinforcement is to be designed for the rectangular post-tensioned beam shown in Fig. 22.28. The initial prestress force \(P_i\) of 250 kips is applied by two closely spaced tendons having a combined eccentricity of 8.0 in. Material properties are \(f_{ci}^\prime\) = 4250 psi and \(f_y\) = 60,000 psi.

Admin
Solution

The rectangular section and the closely spaced anchorage devices allow the use of the simplified ACI equations.
\(d_{burst} = 0.5( h − 2 e_{anc} ) = 0.5(30 − 2 \times 8)\) = 7 in.

The initial prestressing force is 250 kips, which corresponds to a tendon stress level of \(0.82f_{py}\). The maximum jacking stress level in the tendons is \(0.94f_{py}\), or \(0.80f_{pu}\). In this example, only the initial prestress is provided. Hence, the factored tendon force is calculated as

\(P_{pu}=1.2\left(\frac{0.94}{0.82} \right)250=344\) kips

for which

\(T_{burst}=0.25\sum{P_{pu}\left(1-\frac{h_{anc}}{h} \right) }=0.25\times344\left(1-\frac{6}{30} \right)=68.8\) kips

The area of steel needed to resist \(T_{burst}\) is

\(A_s=\frac{T_{burst}}{\phi f_y}=\frac{68.8}{0.85\times60}=1.35 in^2\)

Using No. 4 (No. 13) closed stirrups with an area of \( 2 \times 0.20 in^2\) gives

\(n=\frac{1.35}{2\times0.20}=3.4\) stirrups

Four No. 4 (No. 13) closed stirrups will be used. The first stirrup will be placed \(2\frac{1}{2}\) in. from the anchor plate, and the other three stirrups will be placed 3 in. on center, as shown in Fig. 22.28b, centering the stirrups a distance \(d_{burst}\) from the anchor plate. The closed stirrups ensure that anchorage requirements are satisfied. Details of the reinforcement in the local zone are not shown.
