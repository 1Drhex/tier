Guest Question.

Find the \(V_{\text {av }}\) of the combined voltage half-wave if the maximum value of the source voltage is \(50 \mathrm{~V}\).

Admin
Solution

\( \begin{aligned} \mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{av}} &=\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{m}} / \pi \\ &=50 / 3.14 \\ &=15.9 \mathrm{~V} . \end{aligned} \)

Formula derivative:

\(V_{a v}\) or \(V_{d c}\) or \(V_{\text {lmean }}=\frac{1}{T} \int_{0}^{T} f(t) d t\)

\(=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} V_{s \max } \sin \theta d \theta\)

\(=\frac{V_{s \max }}{2 \pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} \sin \theta d \theta\)

\(=-\frac{V_{s \max }}{2 \pi}[\cos \pi-\cos 0]\)

\(v_{s rms} =\frac{V_{s \max }}{\sqrt{2}}\)

\(\therefore V_{\text {lmean }}=\frac{V_{s \max }}{\pi}\)

\(\therefore I_{\text {Imean }}=\frac{V_{\text {lmean }}}{R}\) or \(I_{\text {mean }}=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} I_{s \max } \sin \theta d \theta\)

\(v_{0 rms}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{T} \int_{0}^{\pi} V^{2}(t) \mathrm{dt}}\)

\( \begin{aligned} v_{0 rms}&=\sqrt{\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} V_{\operatorname{smax}}{}^{2} \sin ^{2} \theta d \theta} \\ &=V_{s \max } \sqrt{\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} \frac{1}{2}(1-\cos 2 \theta) d \theta} \\ &=V_{s \max } \sqrt{\frac{1}{4 \pi} \int_{0}^{\pi}(1-\cos 2 \theta) d \theta} \\ &=V_{s \max } \sqrt{\frac{1}{4 \pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} 1 d \theta-\frac{1}{2} \int_{0}^{\pi} \cos 2 \theta d \theta} \\ &=V_{s \max } \sqrt{\frac{1}{4 \pi}\left[\theta-\frac{\sin 2 \theta}{2}\right]_{0}^{\pi}}\\ &=V_{\operatorname{smax}} \sqrt{\frac{1}{4 \pi}\left[\pi-\frac{\sin 2 \pi}{2}-0+\frac{\sin 2 \times 0}{2}\right]}\\ &=V_{\text {smax }} \sqrt{\frac{1}{4 \pi}[\pi]} \end{aligned} \)

\( \begin{array}{c} \therefore i_{0 rms}=\frac{v_{0 rms}}{R} \text { or } i_{0 rms}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} I_{s \max }{}^{2} \sin ^{2} \theta d \theta} \\ P_{d c}=\frac{\left(\frac{V m}{\pi}\right)^{2}}{R}=\frac{V_{m}{ }^{2}}{\pi^{2} R} \\ P_{\text {total }}=\frac{V_{r m s}{ }^{2}}{R}=\frac{\left(V_{m} / 2\right)^{2}}{R}=\frac{1}{4} \frac{V_{m}{ }^{2}}{R} . \end{array} \)

  1. \(\frac{P_{d c}}{P_{\text {total }}}=\frac{1}{\pi^{2} R} 4 R=0.405(40.5 \%)\).
  2. \(F F=\frac{V_{r m s}}{V_{d c}}=\frac{\frac{V m}{2}}{V m / \pi}=\frac{\pi}{2}=1.57(157 \%)\).
  3. \(\begin{aligned} R F &=\sqrt{F F^{2}-1} \\ &=\sqrt{1.57^{2}-1} \\ &=1.21(121 \%) . \end{aligned}\)
  4. TUF \(=\frac{P_{d c}}{V_{S} \cdot I_{S}}=\frac{\frac{V_{m}{ }^{2}}{\pi^{2} R}}{\left(\frac{V_{m}}{\sqrt{2}}\right) *\left(\frac{V_{m}}{2 R}\right)}=\frac{2 \sqrt{2}}{\pi^{2}}=0.287\) #.
