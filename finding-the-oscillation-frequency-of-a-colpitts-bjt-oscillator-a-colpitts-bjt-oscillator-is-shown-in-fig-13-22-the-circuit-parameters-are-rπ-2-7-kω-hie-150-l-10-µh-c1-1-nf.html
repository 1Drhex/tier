Guest Question.

Finding the oscillation frequency of a Colpitts BJT oscillator A Colpitts BJT oscillator is shown in Fig. 13.22. The circuit parameters are \( r_{\pi}=2.7 k \Omega, h_{ ie }=150, L=10 \mu H , C_{1}=1 nF , C_{2}=100 nF \text {, and } R_{ L }= 10 kΩ \). The BJT acts like a normal amplifier, and the LC resonant signal is fed back to the input base in order to sustain the oscillation. Therefore, the configuration is different from the oscillator circuit in Fig. 13.20.

(a) Find the approximate value of the frequency of oscillation \( f_{\text {o }} \).
(b) Use PSpice/SPICE to plot the transient response of the output voltage \( v_{o}(t) \) from 20 ms to 2 ms.

Admin
Solution

(a) From Eq. (13.52), the frequency of oscillation is

\( \omega_{ o }=\left[\frac{C_{1}+C_{2}}{C_{1} C_{2} L}\right]^{1 / 2} \quad(\text { in } rad / s ) \) (13.52)

 

\( f_{ o }=\frac{1}{2 \pi}\left[\frac{C_{1}+C_{2}}{C_{1} C_{2} L}\right]^{1 / 2}=\frac{1}{2 \pi}\left[\frac{1 nF +100 nF }{1 nF \times 100 nF \times 10 \mu H }\right]^{1 / 2}=1.6 MHz \)

 

(b) The PSpice plot of the output voltage \( v_{ o } \equiv V ( RL : 2) \) is shown in Fig. 13.23, which gives \( f_{o}=1 /(49.149 \mu s – 48.505 \mu s )=1.5 MHz \) (expected value is 1.6 MHz).

NOTE: An initial voltage of 1 V has been assigned to the capacitor \( C _{2} \) in order to start the oscillator, and the UIC (use initial condition) is used in transient analysis.
