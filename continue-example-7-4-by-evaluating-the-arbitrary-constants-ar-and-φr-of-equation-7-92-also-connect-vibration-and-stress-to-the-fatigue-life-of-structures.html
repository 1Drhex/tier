Guest Question.

Continue by evaluating the arbitrary constants \(A_{r}\) and \(\phi_{r}\) of Equation 7.92. Also, connect vibration and stress to the fatigue life of structures.

 

\(y(x, t)=\sum_{r=1}^{\infty}\left[A_{r} \sqrt{\frac{2}{m L}} \cos \left(\omega_{r} t-\phi_{r}\right) \sin \left(\frac{r \pi x}{L}\right)\right]\)
Admin
Solution

Solution: We use the following form for the solution,

 

\(\begin{aligned}&y(x, t) \\&=\sum_{p=1}^{\infty} \sqrt{\frac{2}{m L}}\left[B_{p} \cos \omega_{p} t+D_{p} \sin \omega_{p} t\right] \sin \left(\frac{p \pi x}{L}\right),\end{aligned}\)

 

and evaluate the constants \(B_{p}\) and \(D_{p}\). The initial conditions are assumed to be

 

\(\begin{aligned}&y(x, 0)=C x\left(1-\frac{x}{L}\right) \\&\dot{y}(x, 0)=0\end{aligned}\)

 

where \(C\) is dimensionless.
To satisfy the initial displacement,

 

\(\begin{aligned}y(x, 0) &=C x\left(1-\frac{x}{L}\right) \\&=\sum_{p=1}^{\infty} \sqrt{\frac{2}{m L}} B_{p} \sin \frac{p \pi x}{L},\end{aligned}\) (7.114)

 

and to satisfy the initial velocity,

 

\(\dot{y}(x, 0)=0=\sum_{p=1}^{\infty} \sqrt{\frac{2}{m L}} D_{p} \omega_{p} \sin \frac{p \pi x}{L}\)

 

From the velocity condition, \(D_{p}\) = 0 for all \(p\).

 

We multiply Equation 7.114 by \(\sin r \pi x / L\), where \(r\) is another index, and integrate over the length of the beam,

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\int_{0}^{L} C x\left(1-\frac{x}{L}\right) \sin \frac{r \pi x}{L} d x \\&\quad=\sum_{p=1}^{\infty} \sqrt{\frac{2}{m L}} \int_{0}^{L} B_{p} \sin \frac{p \pi x}{L} \sin \frac{r \pi x}{L} d x\end{aligned}\)

 

in order to take advantage of the orthogonality properties. The integral on the right-hand side equals zero except for \(p\) = \(r\). Performing the integrations leads to

 

\(\frac{2 C L^{2}}{(r \pi)^{3}}(1-\cos r \pi)=\sqrt{\frac{2}{m L}} B_{r} \frac{L}{2}\)

 

The term (\(1-\cos r \pi\)) equals zero for even \(r\) and equals two for odd \(r\). Therefore,

 

\(B_{r}=\frac{8 C}{(r \pi)^{3}} \sqrt{\frac{m L^{3}}{2}}, \quad r=1,3, \ldots\)

 

and the response is given by

 

\(y(x, t)=\sum_{r=1,3, \ldots}^{\infty} \frac{8 L C}{(r \pi)^{3}} \cos \omega_{r} t \sin \frac{r \pi x}{L}\) (7.115)

 

The displacement of the beam as a function of space and time can be related to the stresses that develop in a vibrating beam. Stress oscillations are related directly
to the fatigue life and reliability of a structure and its components. Mechanics of materials relates the bending moment \(M\) to the second derivative of the slope by the equation

 

\(M=E I \frac{\partial^{2} y}{\partial x^{2}}\)

 

The stress \(\sigma_{M}\) at an element of beam a distance \(e\) from the neutral axis of the beam is

 

\(\sigma_{M}=\frac{M e}{I}=E e \frac{\partial^{2} y}{\partial x^{2}}\)

 

Then, using Equation 7.115,

 

\(\sigma_{M}(x, t)=E e \sum_{r=1,3, \ldots}^{\infty} \frac{-8 C}{r \pi L} \sin \frac{r \pi x}{L} \cos \omega_{r} t\)

 

The stress can be evaluated for particular values of \(x\) and \(t\). For a simply supported beam, the maximum stress occurs at the midspan, \(x=L / 2\). Then,

 

\(\sin \frac{r \pi x}{L}=\sin \frac{r \pi}{2}\)

 

which is nonzero for odd \(r\),

 

\(\sigma_{M}\left(\frac{L}{2}, t\right)=-E e \sum_{r=1,3, \ldots}^{\infty} \frac{8 C}{r \pi L} \sin \frac{r \pi}{2} \cos \omega_{r} t\)

 

The argument \(\omega_{r} t\) can be written in terms of the fundamental frequency \(\omega_{1}\). Since

 

\(\omega_{r}=r^{2}\left(\frac{\pi}{L}\right)^{2} \sqrt{\frac{E I}{m}}=r^{2} \omega_{1}\)

 

then

 

\(\omega_{r} t=r^{2} \omega_{1} t\)

 

The fundamental frequency is related to the fundamental period \(T_{1}\) by \(\omega_{1}=2 \pi / T_{1}\). Therefore, at \(t=T_{1} / 2\), we have \(\omega_{r} t=r^{2} \pi\), and \(\cos \omega_{r} t=\cos r^{2} \pi=-1\) for odd \(r\). The stress is then given37 by

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{M}\left(\frac{L}{2}, \frac{T_{1}}{2}\right)=E e \sum_{r=1,3}^{\infty} \frac{8 C}{r \pi L} \sin \frac{r \pi}{2} \\&\sigma_{M}\left(\frac{L}{2}, \frac{T_{1}}{2}\right)=\frac{2 E e C}{L},\end{aligned}\) (7.116)

 

with the same units as the modulus of elasticity \(E\).
We conclude with a specific case. Assuming that the beam is a pipe of length \(L\) = 200 in, the modulus of elasticity \(E=30 \times 10^{6} psi\), the initial displacement constant \(C\) = 1, and the distance from the neutral axis \(e\) = 2 in, the stress calculation using Equation 7.116 yields

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{M}\left(\frac{L}{2}, \frac{T_{1}}{2}\right) &=\frac{2 \times\left(30 \times 10^{6}\right) \times 2 \times 1}{200} psi \\&=0.6 \times 10^{6} psi .\end{aligned}\)

 

We note that the stress is linearly related to the value of \(C\), which was given as part of the initial displacement.
