Guest Question.

The positions of two points are known to be \(\bar{r}_{A}\) = −250\(\bar{i}\) + 400\(\bar{j}\) − 500\(\bar{k}\) mm relative to the xyz coordinate system described in the sketch and \(\bar{r}_{B}\) = 400\(\bar{I}\) − 600\(\bar{J}\) + 200\(\bar{K}\) mm relative to the XYZ coordinate system. Determine the position coordinates of each point relative to the coordinate system not given, and also determine the distance between the points.

 

 

 

Admin
Solution

This example illustrates most of the basic operations associated with rotation transformations. According to the sketch the y axis lies in the XZ plane such that

\(\bar{j}=\cos 30^{\circ} \bar{I}+\sin 30^{\circ} \bar{K}.\)

The only other piece of information conveyed by the sketch is that the z axis is depressed 20◦ below the XY plane. Thus the Z component of \(\bar{k}\) is − sin 20◦. Let θ denote the angle between the Y axis and the projection of the z axis onto the XY plane. Because \(\bar{k}\) has unit magnitude, the length of its projection onto this plane is cos 20◦, from which it follows that

\(\bar{k}=\cos 20^{\circ}(\sin \theta \bar{I}+\cos \theta J)-\sin 20^{\circ} \bar{K} \text { . }\)

The angle θ is set by the condition that \(\bar{j}\) and \(\bar{k}\) are orthogonal, so that their dot product is zero,

\(\bar{j} \cdot \bar{k}=\cos 30^{\circ} \cos 20^{\circ} \sin \theta-\sin 30^{\circ} \sin 20^{\circ}=0,\)

from which we find

\(\theta=\sin ^{-1}\left(\tan 30^{\circ} \tan 20^{\circ}\right)=0.2117 \mathrm{rad}=12.131^{\circ}.\)

The preceding description of \(\bar{k}\) correspondingly gives

\(\bar{k}=0.19747 \bar{I}+0.91871 \bar{J}-0.34202 \bar{k}.\)

The fact that \(\bar{i}\), \(\bar{j}\), and \(\bar{k}\) are a set of orthonormal unit vectors enables us to directly determine \(\bar{i}\) from a cross product:

\(\bar{i}=\bar{j} \times \bar{k}=-0.45936 \bar{I}+0.39493 \bar{J}+0.79563 \bar{K} .\)

The rows of the transformation from XYZ to xyz are the components of the unit vectors of xyz relative to XYZ, so we have found that

\([R]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc} -0.45936 & 0.39493 & 0.79563 \\0.86603 & 0 & 0.500 \\0.19747 & 0.91871 & -0.34202 \end{array}\right].\)

We use \([R]^{T}\) to transform the xyz coordinates of point A, and [R] to transform the XYZ coordinates of point B:

\(\begin{array}{c}\left\{\begin{array}{c}X_{A} \\ Y_{A} \\ Z_{A}\end{array}\right\}=[R]^{\mathrm{T}}\left\{\begin{array}{c}-250 \\400 \\-500 \end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{c} 362.52 \\-558.09 \\172.10 \end{array}\right\} \mathrm{mm}, \\\\\left\{\begin{array}{c}x_{B} \\ y_{B} \\ z_{B}\end{array}\right\}=[R]\left\{\begin{array}{c}400 \\ -600 \\200 \end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{c} -261.58 \\ 446.41 \\-540.64\end{array}\right\} \mathrm{mm} .\end{array}\)

The distance between the points is the magnitude of the position vector between them, which may be constructed from \(\bar{r}_{B / A}=\bar{r}_{B / O}-\bar{r}_{A / O}.\) We calculate this difference by taking the difference of components with respect to the XYZ coordinate system:

\(\left\{r_{B / A}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{c}X_{B}-X_{A} \\ Y_{B}-Y_{A} \\ Z_{B}-Z_{A}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{c}37.48 \\-41.91 \\27.90 \end{array}\right\}.\)

The distance |\(\bar{r}_{B/A}\)| may be determined from the matrix implementation of a dot product, which gives

\(\begin{array}{c} \left|\bar{r}_{B / A}\right|^{2}=\left[\begin{array}{lll}37.48 & -41.91 & 27.90\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{c}37.48 \\-41.91 \\27.90,\end{array}\right\}, \\\\\left|\bar{r}_{B / A}\right|=62.77 \mathrm{~mm} .\end{array}\)

This result should be the same as what would be obtained if we described \(\bar{r}_{B/A}\) in terms of xyz components. Doing so would have given

\(\left\{r_{B / A}^{\prime}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{l}x_{B}-x_{A} \\y_{B}-y_{A} \\z_{B}-z_{A}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{c}-11.58 \\46.41 \\-40.64\end{array}\right\},\)

 

\(\left|\bar{r}_{B/A}^{\prime}\right|^{2}=\left[\begin{array}{lll}-11.58 & 46.41 & -40.64\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{c}-11.58 \\46.41 \\-40.64 \end{array}\right\},\)

 

\(\left|\bar{r}_{B / A}^{\prime}\right|=62.77 \mathrm{~mm}.\)
