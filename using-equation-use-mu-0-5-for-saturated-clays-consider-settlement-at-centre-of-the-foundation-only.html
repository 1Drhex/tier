Guest Question.

Using equation. Use \( \mu = 0.5 \) for saturated clays. Consider settlement at centre of the foundation only.

Admin
Solution

Equation : \( S_{i} = q \times B \times\frac{1 – \mu^{2}}{E_{s}} \times m \times I_{S} \times I_{F} \)

(a) Consider the upper clay layer, with \( E_{u} = 40 MN/m^{2} \):

For settlement at centre of the foundation:

\( m = 4; B = \frac{2}{2} = 1 m; L = \frac{4}{2} = 2 m; M = \frac{\frac{L}{2}}{\left ( B/2 \right )} = \frac{\frac{4}{2}}{\left ( 2/2 \right )} = 2; N = \frac{H}{\left ( B/2 \right )} = \frac{4}{\left ( 2/2 \right )} = 4 \)

 

From Table 3.8 obtain \( I_{1} = 0.476 \) and \( I_{2} = 0.069 \). Hence,

\( I_{S} = I_{1} + \frac{1 – 2 \mu }{1 – \mu } I_{2} = 0.476 + \frac{1 – 2 \times 0.5}{1 – 0.5} 0.069 = 0.476 \)

 

\( \frac{D}{B} = \frac{1}{2} = 0.5; \frac{B}{L} = \frac{2}{4} = 0.5; \mu = 0.5. \) From

Table 3.7 obtain \( I_{F} = 0.90 \)

\( S_{i \left ( a \right )} = 150 \times \frac{2}{2} \times \frac{1 – \left ( 0.5 \right )^{2}}{40000} \times 4 \times 0.476 \times 0.9 = 0.0048 m = 4.8 mm \)

 

(b) Consider the two clay layers combined, with \( E_{u} = 75 MN/m^{2}: \)

For settlement at centre of the foundation:

\( m = 4; B = \frac{2}{2} = 1 m; L = \frac{4}{2} = 2 m; M = \frac{\frac{L}{2}}{\left ( B/2 \right )} = \frac{\frac{4}{2}}{\left ( 2/2 \right )} = 2; N = \frac{H}{\left ( B/2 \right )} = \frac{12}{\left ( 2/2 \right )} = 12 \)

 

From Table 3.8 obtain \( I_{1} = 0.653 \) and \( I_{2} = 0.028. \) Hence,

\( I_{S} = I_{1} + \frac{1 – 2 \mu }{1 – \mu } I_{2} = 0.653 + \frac{1 – 2 \times 0.5}{1 – 0.5} 0.069 = 0.0.653 \)

 

\( \frac{D}{B} = \frac{1}{2} = 0.5; \frac{B}{L} = \frac{2}{4} = 0.5; \mu = 0.5. \) From

Table 3.7 obtain \( I_{F} = 0.90 \)

\( S_{i \left ( b \right )} = 150 \times \frac{2}{2} \times \frac{1 – \left ( 0.5 \right )^{2}}{75 000} \times 4 \times 0.653 \times 0.9 = 0.0035 m = 3.5 mm \)

 

(c) Consider the upper clay layer, with \( E_{u} = 75 MN/m^{2}: \)

For settlement at centre of the foundation:

\( m = 4; B = \frac{2}{2} = 1 m; L = \frac{4}{2} = 2 m; M = \frac{\frac{L}{2}}{\left ( B/2 \right )} = \frac{\frac{4}{2}}{\left ( 2/2 \right )} = 2; N = \frac{H}{\left ( B/2 \right )} = \frac{4}{\left ( 2/2 \right )} = 4 \)

 

From Table 3.8 obtain \( I_{1} = 0.476 \) and \( I_{2} = 0.069 \). Hence,

\( I_{S} = I_{1} + \frac{1 – 2 \mu }{1 – \mu } I_{2} = 0.476 + \frac{1 – 2 \times 0.5}{1 – 0.5} 0.069 = 0.476 \)

 

\( \frac{D}{B} = \frac{1}{2} = 0.5; \frac{B}{L} = \frac{2}{4} = 0.5; \mu = 0.5. \) From

Table 3.7 obtain \( I_{F} = 0.90 \)

\( S_{i \left ( c \right )} = 150 \times \frac{2}{2} \times \frac{1 – \left ( 0.5 \right )^{2}}{75 000} \times 4 \times 0.476 \times 0.9 = 0.0026 m = 32.6 mm \)

 

Hence, using the principle of superposition, settlement at centre of the foundation, considering flexible loading area, is given by:

\( S_{i} = S_{i \left ( a \right )} + S_{i \left ( b \right )} – S_{i \left ( c \right )} = 4.8 + 3.5 – 2.6 = 5.7 mm\)

 

Equation : \( S_{i\left ( rigid \right )} = 0.93 S_{i\left ( flexible \right )} \)

\( S_{i} = 0.93 \times 5.7 = 5.3 mm \)

Another procedure is to calculate settlement of each layer alone and then adding them to obtain the total settlement, as follows:

(a) Settlement of the upper clay layer was calculated = 4 8 mm.

(b) Settlement of the lower clay layer:

Assume using the simple 2 V : 1 H ratio method of stress distribution At top of the layer: \( q = 150 \left [ \left ( 2\times 4 \right)/ \left ( 2 + 4 \right)\left ( 4 + 4 \right ) \right ] = 25 kN/m^{2} \)

For settlement at the centre of the foundation:

\( m = 4; B = \frac{6}{2} = 3 m; L = \frac{8}{2} = 4 m; M = \frac{\frac{L}{2}}{\left ( B/2 \right )} = \frac{4}{3} = 1.33; N = \frac{H}{\left ( B/2 \right )} = \frac{8}{3} = 2.67 \)

 

From Table 3.8 obtain \( I_{1} = 0.36 \) and \( I_{2} = 0.074. \) Hence,

\( I_{S} = I_{1} + \frac{1 – 2 \mu }{1 – \mu } I_{2} = 0.36 + \frac{1 – 2 \times 0.5}{1 – 0.5} 0.074 = 0.36 \)

 

\( \frac{D}{B} = \frac{5}{6} = 0.83; \frac{B}{L} = \frac{6}{8} = 0.75; \mu = 0.5. \) From

Table 3.7 obtain \( I_{F} = 0.90 \)

\( S_{i \left ( b \right )} = 25 \times \frac{6}{2} \times \frac{1 – \left ( 0.5 \right )^{2}}{75 000} \times 4 \times 0.36 \times 0.79 = 0.0009 m = 0.9 mm \)

 

Hence, using the principle of superposition, settlement at centre of the foundation, considering flexible loading area, is given by

\( S_{i} = S_{i \left ( a \right )} + S_{i \left ( b \right )} = 4.8 + 0.9 = 5.7 mm \)

 

Equation : \( S_{i \left ( rigid \right )} = 0.93 S_{i \left ( flexible \right )} \)

\( S_{i} = 0.93 \times 5.7 = 5.3 mm \)
