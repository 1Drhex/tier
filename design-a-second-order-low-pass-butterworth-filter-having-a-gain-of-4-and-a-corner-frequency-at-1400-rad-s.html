Guest Question.

Design a second-order low-pass Butterworth filter having a gain of 4 and a corner frequency at 1400 rad/s.

Admin
Solution

We begin by selecting the Sallen-Key prototype shown in Fig. 16.48, and opt for the simplification which arises when we set \(R_{1}=R_{2}=R\) and \(C_{1}=C_{2}=C\). With a second-order Butterworth filter we expect from Table 16.2 to have a denominator polynomial

and comparing to Eq. [42]

 

\(a_{1}=\frac{3-G}{R C}\) [42]

 

\(R C=1\)

 

and

 

\(\frac{2}{R C}+\frac{1-G}{R C}=1.414\)

 

hence

 

\(G=\frac{R_{A}+R_{B}}{R_{B}}=1.586\)

 

We first set values for the two resistors in our gain network (which do not need to undergo scaling) by arbitrarily choosing \(R_{B}=1 k \Omega\), so that \(R_{A}=586 \Omega\).

Next, we note that if C = 1 F, then R = 1 Ω, neither of which is a particularly conventional value. Instead we select \(C^{\prime}=1 \mu F\); this requires scaling the resistor by \(10^{6}\). We also need to frequency-scale to 1400 rad/s. Thus,

 

\(10^{-6} F =\frac{1 F }{k_{m} k_{f}}=\frac{1 F }{1400 k_{m}}\)

 

and \(k_{m}=714 \Omega\). Consequently, \(R^{\prime}=k_{m} R=714 \Omega\).

 

Unfortunately, our design is not quite done. We were constrained to an amplifier gain of 1.586, or 4 dB, but the specifications called for a gain of 4, or 12 dB. The only option available to us is to feed the output of our circuit into a noninverting amplifier such as the one in Fig. 6.6a. Choosing 1 kΩ and 1.52 kΩ for \(R_{1}\) (output stage) and \(R_{f}\) completes the design.

PRACTICE

16.19 Design a second-order Butterworth low-pass filter having a gain of 10 dB and a cutoff frequency of 1000 Hz.

Ans: A two-stage circuit, with the output of the circuit of Fig. 16.48 fed into the input of a noninverting amplifier, with component values \(C_{1}=C_{2}=1 \mu F\), \(R_{1}=R_{2}=159 \Omega, R_{A}=586 \Omega, R_{B}=1 k \Omega \text { (stage 1) and } R_{1}=1 k \Omega, R_{f}=994 \Omega\) (stage 2).
