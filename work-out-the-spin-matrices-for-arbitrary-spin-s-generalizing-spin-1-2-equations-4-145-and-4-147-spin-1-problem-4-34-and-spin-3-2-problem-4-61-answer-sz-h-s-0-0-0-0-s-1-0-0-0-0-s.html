Guest Question.

Work out the spin matrices for arbitrary spin s, generalizing spin 1/2 (Equations 4.145 and 4.147), spin 1 (), and spin 3/2 (). Answer:

\( S _{z}=\frac{\hbar}{2}\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{array}\right) \) (4.145).

\( S _{x}=\frac{\hbar}{2}\left(\begin{array}{ll} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{array}\right), \quad S _{y}=\frac{\hbar}{2}\left(\begin{array}{cc} 0 & -i \\ i & 0 \end{array}\right) \) (4.147).

\( S _{z}=\hbar\left(\begin{array}{ccccc} s & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ 0 & s-1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & s-2 & \cdots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \cdots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & -s \end{array}\right) \) ;

\( S _{x}=\frac{\hbar}{2}\left(\begin{array}{ccccccc} 0 & b_{s} & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ b_{s} & 0 & b_{s-1} & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & b_{s-1} & 0 & b_{s-2} & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & b_{s-2} & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \cdots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & b_{-s+1} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & b_{-s+1} & 0 \end{array}\right) \);

\( S _{y}=\frac{\hbar}{2}\left(\begin{array}{cccccc} 0 & -i b_{s} & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ i b_{s} & 0 & -i b_{s-1} & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & i b_{s-1} & 0 & -i b_{s}-2 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & i b_{s-2} & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \cdots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & -i b_{-s+1} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & i b_{-s+1} & 0 \end{array}\right) \).

where

\( b_{j} \equiv \sqrt{(s+j)(s+1-j)} \).

Admin
Solution

From Eq. 4.135, \( S_{z}|s m\rangle=\hbar m|s m\rangle \). Since s is fixed, here, let’s just identify the states by the value of m (which runs from \( -s \text { to }+s \). The matrix elements of \( S_z \) are

\( S^{2}|s m\rangle=\hbar^{2} s(s+1)|s m\rangle ; \quad S_{z}|s m\rangle=\hbar m|s m\rangle \). (4.135).

\( S _{n m}=\left\langle n\left|S_{z}\right| m\right\rangle=\hbar m\langle n \mid m\rangle=\hbar m \delta_{n m} \).

It’s a diagonal matrix, with elements \( m \hbar \), ranging from m = s in the upper left corner to m = -s in the lower right corner

\( S _{z}=\hbar\left(\begin{array}{ccccc} s & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ 0 & s-1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & s-2 & \cdots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & -s \end{array}\right) \).

From Eq. 4.136,

\( S_{\pm}|s m\rangle=\hbar \sqrt{s(s+1)-m(m \pm 1)}|s(m \pm 1)\rangle \) (4.136).

\( S_{\pm}|s m\rangle=\hbar \sqrt{s(s+1)-m(m \pm 1)}|s(m \pm 1)\rangle=\hbar \sqrt{(s \mp m)(s \pm m+1)}|s(m \pm 1)\rangle \).

\( \left( S _{+}\right)_{n m}=\left\langle n\left|S_{+}\right| m\right\rangle=\hbar \sqrt{(s-m)(s+m+1)}\langle n \mid m+1\rangle=\hbar b_{m+1} \delta_{n(m+1)}=\hbar b_{n} \delta_{n(m+1)} \).

All nonzero elements have row index (n) one greater than the column index (m), so they are on the diagonal just above the main diagonal (note that the indices go down, here: s, s – 1, s – 2. . . , -s):

\( S _{+}=\hbar\left(\begin{array}{cccccc} 0 & b_{s} & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & b_{s-1} & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & b_{s-2} & \cdots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & b_{-s+1} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \end{array}\right) \).

Similarly

\( \left( S _{-}\right)_{n m}=\left\langle n\left|S_{-}\right| m\right\rangle=\hbar \sqrt{(s+m)(s-m+1)}\langle n \mid m-1\rangle=\hbar b_{m} \delta_{n(m-1)} \).

This time the nonzero elements are on the diagonal just below the main diagonal:

\( S _{-}=\hbar\left(\begin{array}{cccccc} 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ b_{s} & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & b_{s-1} & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & b_{-s+1} & 0 \end{array}\right) \).

To construct \( S _{x}=\frac{1}{2}\left( S _{+}+ S _{-}\right) \text {and } S _{y}=\frac{1}{2 i}\left( S _{+}- S _{-}\right) \) ), simply add and subtract the matrices \( S _{+} \text {and } S _{-} \):

\( S _{x}=\frac{\hbar}{2}\left(\begin{array}{ccccccc} 0 & b_{s} & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ b_{s} & 0 & b_{s-1} & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & b_{s-1} & 0 & b_{s-2} & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & b_{s-2} & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & b_{-s+1} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & b_{-s+1} & 0 \end{array}\right) ; \quad S _{y}=\frac{\hbar}{2 i}\left(\begin{array}{ccccccc} 0 & b_{s} & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ -b_{s} & 0 & b_{s-1} & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -b_{s-1} & 0 & b_{s-2} & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -b_{s-2} & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & b_{-s+1} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & -b_{-s+1} & 0 \end{array}\right) \).
