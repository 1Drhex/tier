Design of a CD amplifier with a MOS current source

(a) Use the sinking current in Fig. 7.29(b) to bias the source follower in Fig. 7.39(a) at a drain current of \( I_{ D }=3.25 mA \). The DC supply voltage is \( V_{ DD }=15 V , \text { and } R_{ ref }=4 k \Omega \). The MOS parameters are \( V_{ t }=1 V \), \( K_{ n }=3.25 mA / V ^{2}, K_{ p }=6.5 mA / V ^{2}, \text { to or } W = L , \text { and }\left|V_{ M }\right|=1 / \lambda=100 V .\)

(b) Find the small-signal voltage \( A_{\text {vo }} \) and the output resistance \( R_{0} \).

(c) Use SPICE to plot the small-signal output voltage \( v_{ o } \) for a sinusoidal input signal \( v_{ S } \) of 1 mV at 1 kHz.

Solution

(a) From , we get \( V_{ GS }=2 V , R_{ ref }=4 k \Omega, r_{ o }=r_{ ol }=r_{ o 2}=30.77 k \Omega, g_{ m 1}=6.5 mA / V \), \( R_{2}=100 k \Omega \text {, and } R_{1}=650 k \Omega \).

(b) From Eq. (7.77),

 

\( A_{ vo }=\frac{v_{ o }}{v_{ g }}=\frac{g_{ m1 }\left(r_{ o1} \| r_{ o 2}\right)}{1+g_{ m 1}\left(r_{ o 1} \| r_{ o 2}\right)} \) (7.77)

 

\( A_{ vo }=\frac{g_{ m 1}\left(r_{ o 1} \| r_{ o 2}\right)}{1+g_{ m 1}\left(r_{ o 1} \| r_{ o 2}\right)}=\frac{6.5 m \times(30.77 k \| 30.77 k )}{1+6.5 m \times(30.77 k \| 30.77 k )}=0.99 \)

 

From Eq. (7.78),

 

\( R_{ o }=\frac{v_{ x }}{i_{ x }}=\frac{1}{g_{ m 1}}\left\|r_{ o 1}\right\| r_{ o 2} \) (7.78)

 

\( R_{ o }=\frac{1}{g_{ m 1}}\left\|r_{ o1}\right\| r_{ o 2}=\frac{1}{6.5 m }\|30.77 k \| 30.77 k =152.3 \Omega \)

 

(c) The PSpice schematic is shown in Fig. 7.40. The plot of the output voltage is shown in Fig. 7.41, which gives a voltage gain 0.988 V⁄ V, which is close to the calculated value of 0.99 V⁄ V. Note there is no phase shift.
