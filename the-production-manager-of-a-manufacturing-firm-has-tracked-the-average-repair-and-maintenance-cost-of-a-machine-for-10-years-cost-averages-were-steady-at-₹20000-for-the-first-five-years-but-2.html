Guest Question.

The production manager of a manufacturing firm has tracked the average repair and maintenance cost of a machine for \(10\) years. Cost averages were steady at \(₹20,000\) for the first five years, but have increased consistently by \(₹5,000\) for each of the last \(5\) years. Calculate the equivalent present worth and annual worth of the costs, if the rate of interest is \(10\%\) per year.

Admin
Solution

The cash flow diagram is shown in Fig. 4.41.

It can be seen from Fig. 4.41 that the base amount is \(₹20,000\) and the arithmetic gradient \(G = ₹5,000\) starting from the end of year \(6\). Fig. 4.42 (a) and (b) partitions these two series.

The present worth of the cash flow diagram shown in Fig. 4.42(a) i.e. \(P_{A}\) is obtained as:

\(P_{A} =A\left(P/A,i\%,n \right)\)

 

\(=₹20,000\left(P/A,6\%,10 \right)\)

 

\(=₹20,000\left(7.3601\right)\)

 

\(=₹1,47,202\)

In order to calculate the present worth of the shifted arithmetic gradient series shown in Fig. 4.42(b), first present worth of the arithmetic gradient \(G = ₹5,000\) is calculated as:

\(P_{G} =G\left(P/G,i\%,6 \right)\)

 

\(=₹5,000\left(P/G,6\%,6 \right)\)

 

\(=₹5,000\left(11.4594\right)\)

 

\(=₹57,297\)

The \(P_{G}\) will be located two periods before gradient starts i.e. at the end of period 4 (Fig. 4.42(b)). Considering \(P_{G}\) calculated above as future worth, the present worth \(P^{\prime }_{G}\) is calculated as;

\(P^{\prime }_{G}=F\left(P/F,i\%,n \right)\)

 

\(=₹57,297\left(P/F,6\%,4 \right)\)

 

\(=₹57,297\left(0.7921\right)\)

 

\(=₹45,384.95\)

The equivalent present worth of the cash flow diagram shown in Fig. 4.41 i.e. \(P_{T}\) is obtained as:

\(P_{T} =P_{A} +P^{\prime }_{G}\)

 

\(=₹1,47,202+₹45,384.95\)

 

\(=₹1,92,586.95\)

The equivalent annual worth \(A_{T}\) for the above calculated \(P_{T}\) is obtained as:

\(A_{T} =P_{T} \left(A/P,i\%,n \right)\)

 

\(=₹1,92,586.95\left(A/P,6\%,10 \right)\)

 

\(=₹1,92,586.95\left(0.13587\right)\)

 

\(=₹26,166.79\)
