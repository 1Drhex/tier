Guest Question.

Determine the shear and tension resistance of the web fin plate shown in Figure 9.22a.

Admin
Solution

If the yield criterion of equation 9.15, \(σ^2_{y} + σ^2_{z} − σ_{y}σ_{z} + 3τ^2_{yz} ≤ f^2_{y}\) is used with the elastic stresses on the gross cross-section of the plate, then the elastic bending stress may be determined using an elastic section modulus of \(bd^2/6\), so that

\(σ_{y} = 0.2 Q × 10^6 × 6/(10 × 280^2) N/mm^2 = 1.531 Q N/mm^2\)

and the average shear stress is

\(τ_{yz} = Q × 10^3/(280 × 10) N/mm^2 = 0.357 Q N/mm^2.\)

 

Substituting into equation 9.15, \(σ^2_{y} + σ^2_{z} − σ_{y}σ_{z} + 3τ^2_{yz} ≤ f^2_{y}\)

\((1.531 Q)^2 + 3 × (0.357Q)^2 ≤ 355^2\)

 

so that \(Q_{vty} ≤ 215.0 < 254.0(≥ Q_{v}).\)

If the equivalent fracture criterion derived from equation 9.15 \(σ^2_{y} + σ^2_{z} − σ_{y}σ_{z} + 3τ^2_{yz} ≤ f^2_{y}\)

is used with the elastic stresses on the net cross-section of the plate, then

\(I_{p} = 280^3 × 10/12 − 2 × 22 × 10 × 105^2 − 2 × 22 × 10 × 35^2 = 12.90 × 10^6 mm^4\)

so that

\(W_{el,}p = 12.90 × 10^6/140 = 92.2 × 10^3mm^3,\) and

\(σ_{y} = 0.2 Q × 10^6/(92.2 × 10^3) N/mm^2 = 2.170 Q N/mm^2\)

 

and the average shear stress is
\(τ_{yz} = Q × 10^3/{\left\{(280 − 4 × 22) × 10\right\} } N/mm^2 = 0.521 Q N/mm^2.\)

 

Using the equivalent of equation 9.15 \(σ^2_{y} + σ^2_{z} − σ_{y}σ_{z} + 3τ^2_{yz} ≤ f^2_{y}\)

for fracture with \(f_{u} = 510 N/mm^2\) (EN10025-2),

\((2.170 Q)^2 + 3 × (0.521 Q)^2 ≤ 510^2\)

so that \(Q_{vtu} ≤ 228.5 > 215.0(≥ Q_{vty}).\)

However, these calculations are conservative, since the maximum bending stresses occur at the top and bottom of the plate, where the shear stresses are zero.
If the shear stresses are ignored, then \(Q_{ty} ≤ 355/1.531 = 231.9 < 254.3(≥ Q_{v})\)
and

\(Q_{tu} ≤ 510/2.170 = 235.0 > 231.9(≥ Q_{ty}).\)
