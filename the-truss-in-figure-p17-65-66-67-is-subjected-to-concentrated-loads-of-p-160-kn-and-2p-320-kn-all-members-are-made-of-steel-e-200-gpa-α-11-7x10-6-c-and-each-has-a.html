Guest Question.

The truss in Figure P17.65/66/67 is subjected to concentrated loads of P = 160 kN and 2P = 320 kN. All members are made of steel [E = 200 GPa; α = \(11.7 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} C\)], and each has a cross-sectional area of A = 3,500 \(mm ^{2}\) . Utilizing the virtual-work method, calculate:
(a) the vertical displacement of joint D.
(b) the vertical displacement of joint D if the temperature of the truss decreases by 40°C.

Admin
Solution

Real Truss Reaction Forces

\(B_{x}=0.000 kN \quad B_{y}=640.000 kN \uparrow \quad F_{y}=640.000 kN \uparrow\)

 

(a) Vertical Displacement of Joint D
Virtual Load Assume that a 1-kN virtual load is applied in the –y direction at D. The reaction forces for the virtual load are:

\(B_{x}=0.00 kN \quad B_{y}=0.500 kN \uparrow \quad F_{y}=0.500 kN \uparrow\)

 

\(\begin{array}{ccccc}\hline \text { Member } & \begin{array}{c}L \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}F \\( kN )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}f \\( kN )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}f(F L) \\\left( kN ^{2}- mm \right)\end{array} \\\hline A B & 10,000 & -266.67 & 0.0000 & 0.000 \\A C & 8,000 & 213.33 & 0.0000 & 0.000 \\B C & 6,000 & -480.00 & -0.5000 & 1,440,000.000 \\B D & 8,000 & -213.33 & 0.0000 & 0.000 \\C D & 10,000 & 266.67 & 0.8333 & 2,222,213.361 \\C E & 8,000 & 0.00 & -0.6667 & 0.000 \\D E & 6,000 & -320.00 & 0.0000 & 0.000 \\D F & 8,000 & -213.33 & 0.0000 & 0.000 \\D G & 10,000 & 266.67 & 0.8333 & 2,222,213.361 \\E G & 8,000 & 0.00 & -0.6667 & 0.000 \\F G & 6,000 & -480.00 & -0.5000 & 1,440,000.000 \\F H & 10,000 & -266.67 & 0.0000 & 0.000 \\G H & 8,000 & 213.33 & 0.0000 & 0.000 \\\hline & & & \sum f(F L)= & 7,324,426.722 \\\hline\end{array}\)

 

Equation (17.30) can now be applied.

\(1 \cdot \Delta=\sum_{j} f_{j}\left(\frac{F_{j} L_{j}}{A_{j} E_{j}}\right)=\frac{1}{A E} \sum_{j} f_{j}\left(F_{j} L_{j}\right)\)

From the tabulated results:

\(\begin{aligned}(1 kN ) \cdot \Delta_{D} &=\frac{\left(7,324,426.722 kN ^{2}- mm \right)(1,000 N / kN )}{\left(3,500 mm ^{2}\right)\left(200,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)} \\\Delta_{D} &=10.46 mm \downarrow\end{aligned}\)

Since the virtual load was applied in the –y direction at D, the positive value of the result confirms that joint D does displace downward.

 

(b) Vertical Displacement of Joint D for ΔT = –40°C

\(\begin{array}{ccccccc}\hline \text { Member } & L & F & \frac{F L}{A E} & \alpha \Delta T L & f & f\left(\frac{F L}{A E}+\alpha \Delta T L\right) \\& ( mm ) & ( kN ) & ( mm ) & ( mm ) & ( kN ) & ( kN – mm ) \\\hline AB & 10,000 & -266.67 & -3.8095 & -4.6800 & 0.0000 & 0.0000 \\A C & 8,000 & 213.33 & 2.4381 & -3.7440 & 0.0000 & 0.0000 \\B C & 6,000 & -480.00 & -4.1143 & 0.0000 & -0.5000 & 2.0571 \\B D & 8,000 & -213.33 & -2.4381 & -3.7440 & 0.0000 & 0.0000 \\C D & 10,000 & 266.67 & 3.8095 & -4.6800 & 0.8333 & -0.7254 \\C E & 8,000 & 0.00 & 0.0000 & 0.0000 & -0.6667 & 0.0000 \\D E & 6,000 & -320.00 & -2.7429 & -2.8080 & 0.0000 & 0.0000 \\D F & 8,000 & -213.33 & -2.4381 & -3.7440 & 0.0000 & 0.0000 \\D G & 10,000 & 266.67 & 3.8095 & -4.6800 & 0.8333 & -0.7254 \\E G & 8,000 & 0.00 & 0.0000 & -3.7440 & -0.6667 & 2.4960 \\F G & 6,000 & -480.00 & -4.1143 & -2.8080 & -0.5000 & 3.4611 \\F H & 10,000 & -266.67 & -3.8095 & -4.6800 & 0.0000 & 0.0000 \\G H & 8,000 & 213.33 & 2.4381 & -3.7440 & 0.0000 & 0.0000 \\\hline & & & & & \sum f\left(\frac{F L}{A E}+\alpha \Delta T L\right)= & 6.5635\end{array}\)

 

Apply Equation (17.33)

\(1 \cdot \Delta=\sum_{j} f_{j}\left(\frac{F_{j} L_{j}}{A_{j} E_{j}}+\alpha_{j} \Delta T_{j} L_{j}+\Delta L_{j}\right)\)

to get the deflection at A from the tabulated results:

\(\begin{aligned}(1 kN ) \cdot \Delta_{D} &=6.5635 kN – mm \\\Delta_{D} &=6.56 mm \downarrow\end{aligned}\)

Since the virtual load was applied in the –y direction at D, the positive value of the result confirms that joint D does displace downward.
