Guest Question.

For the Darlington pair shown in \(Fig. 61.27\) , calculate the value of \(1\).\( \beta , 2\).input impedance , \(3\). voltage amplification.

Assume \(\beta_1 = \beta_2 = 100\) and \(R_L \gg (r_{e.1} + r_{e.2} )\) .

Admin
Solution

The approximate values are as under :

1.\(\beta \) of Darlington pair

=\( \beta_1 \times \beta_2 \)

=\(100 \times 100 = 10,000\)

2. \(r_E = R_E \left|\right| R_L\)

=\(10 K \left|\right| 500 \Omega = 475 \Omega\)

 

\(r_{in(base)}\) of \(Q_1 \cong β^2 r_E\)

=\(10,000 \times 475=4.75 M\)

 

\(r_i\) = input impedance of the pair

=\(R_B \left|\right| r_{in(base)}\) of \(Q_1\)

=\(1 M \left|\right| 4.75 M = 0.826 M = 826 K\)

3. \(A_v \cong 1\)
