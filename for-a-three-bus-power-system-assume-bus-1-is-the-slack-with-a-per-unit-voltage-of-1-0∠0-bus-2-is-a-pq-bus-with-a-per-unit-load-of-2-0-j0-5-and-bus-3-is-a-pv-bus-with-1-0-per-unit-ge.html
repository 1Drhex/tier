Guest Question.

For a three-bus power system, assume bus 1 is the slack with a per unit voltage of 1.0∠0°, bus 2 is a PQ bus with a per unit load of 2.0 + j0.5, and bus 3 is a PV bus with 1.0 per unit generation and a 1.0 voltage setpoint. The per unit line impedances are j0.1 between buses 1 and 2, j0.4 between buses 1 and 3, and j0.2 between buses 2 and 3. Using a flat start, use the Newton-Raphson approach to determine the first iteration phasor voltages at buses 2 and 3.

Admin
Solution

Step 1
By inspection:

\(\bar{Y}_{\text {Bus }}=\left[\begin{array}{l|l|l}-j 12.5 & +j 10.0 & +j 2.5 \\\hline+j 10.0 & -j 15.0 & +j 5.0 \\\hline+j 2.5 & +j 5.0 & -j 7.5\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(\delta_{2}(0)=\delta_{3}(0)=0^{\circ} \quad V_{2}(0)=1.0\)

Compute \(\Delta y(0)\)

\(\begin{aligned}&P_{2}(X)=1.0\left[10 \cos \left(-90^{\circ}\right)+5 \cos \left(-90^{\circ}\right)\right]=0 \\&P_{3}(X)=2.5 \cos \left(-90^{\circ}\right)+5 \cos \left(-90^{\circ}\right)=0 \\&Q_{2}(X)=1.0\left[10 \sin \left(-90^{\circ}\right)+15+5 \sin \left(-90^{\circ}\right)\right]=0\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\Delta y(0)=\left[\begin{array}{c}P_{2}-P_{2}(X) \\P_{3}-P_{3}(X) \\Q_{2}-Q_{2}(X)\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}-2.0-0 \\1.0-0 \\-0.5-0\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}-2.0 \\1.0 \\-0.5\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 2 Compute \(\underline{J}(0)\) (see Table 6.5 text)

\(\begin{aligned}J 1_{22}=\frac{\partial P_{2}}{\partial \delta_{2}} &=-V_{2}\left[Y_{21} V_{1} \sin \left(\delta_{2}-\delta_{1}-\theta_{21}\right)+Y_{23} V_{3} \sin \left(\delta_{2}-\delta_{3}-\theta_{23}\right)\right] \\&=-1.0\left[10(1) \sin \left(-90^{\circ}\right)+5(1) \sin \left(-90^{\circ}\right)\right]=15 .\end{aligned}\)

 

\(J 1_{23}=\frac{\partial P_{2}}{\partial \delta_{3}}=V_{2} Y_{23} V_{3} \sin \left(\delta_{2}-\delta_{3}-\theta_{23}\right)=(1.0)(5) \sin \left(-90^{\circ}\right)=-5 .\)

 

\(J 1_{32}=\frac{\partial P_{3}}{\partial \delta_{2}}=V_{3} Y_{32} V_{2} \sin \left(\delta_{3}-\delta_{2}-\theta_{32}\right)=(1)(5)(1) \sin \left(-90^{\circ}\right)=-5\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}J 1_{33}=\frac{\partial P_{3}}{\partial \delta_{3}} &=-V_{3}\left[Y_{31} V_{1} \sin \left(\delta_{3}-\delta_{1}-\theta_{31}\right)+Y_{32} V_{2} \sin \left(\delta_{3}-\delta_{2}-\theta_{32}\right)\right] \\&=-1.0\left[(2.5)(1) \sin \left(-90^{\circ}\right)+(5)(1) \sin \left(-90^{\circ}\right)\right]=7.5\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{gathered}J 2_{22}=\frac{\partial P_{2}}{\partial V_{2}}=V_{2} Y_{22} \cos \left(\theta_{22}\right)+\left[Y_{21} V_{1} \cos \left(\delta_{2}-\delta_{1}-\theta_{21}\right)+Y_{22} V_{2} \cos \left(-\theta_{22}\right)\right. \\\left.+Y_{23} V_{3} \cos \left(\delta_{2}-\delta_{3}-\theta_{23}\right)\right]=0\end{gathered}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&J 2_{32}=\frac{\partial P_{3}}{\partial V_{2}}=V_{3} Y_{32} \cos \left(\delta_{3}-\delta_{2}-\theta_{32}\right)=0 \\&J 3_{22}=\frac{\partial Q_{2}}{\partial \delta_{2}}=V_{2}\left[Y_{21} V_{1} \cos \left(\delta_{2}-\delta_{1}-\theta_{21}\right)\right]+Y_{23} V_{3} \cos \left(\delta_{2}-\delta_{3}-\theta_{23}\right)=0 \\&J 3_{23}=\frac{\partial Q_{2}}{\partial \delta_{3}}=-V_{2} Y_{23} V_{3} \cos \left(\delta_{2}-\delta_{3}-\theta_{23}\right)=0\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}J 4_{22} &=\frac{\partial Q_{2}}{\partial V_{2}}=-V_{2} Y_{22} \sin \theta_{22}+\left[\begin{array}{l}Y_{21} V_{1} \sin \left(\delta_{2}-\delta_{1}-\theta_{21}\right)+Y_{22} V_{2} \sin \left(-\theta_{22}\right) \\+Y_{23} V_{3} \sin \left(\delta_{2}-\delta_{3}-\theta_{23}\right)\end{array}\right] \\J 4_{22} &=(-1)(15) \sin \left(-90^{\circ}\right)+\left[(10)(1) \sin \left(-90^{\circ}\right)+15(1) \sin \left(90^{\circ}\right)+5(1) \sin \left(-90^{\circ}\right)\right] \\&=15\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\underline{J}(0)=\left[\begin{array}{c|c}\underline{\underline{J}} 1 & \underline{J} 2 \\\underline{\underline{J}} 3 & \underline{J} 4\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}15 & -5 & 0 \\-5 & 7.5 & 0 \\\hline 0 & 0 & 15\end{array}\right] \text { per unit }\)

 

Step 3 Solve \(J \Delta x=\Delta y\)

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc}15 & -5 & 0 \\-5 & 7.5 & 0 \\0 & 0 & 15\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}\Delta \delta_{2} \\\Delta \delta_{3} \\\Delta V_{2}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}-2.0 \\1.0 \\-0.5\end{array}\right]\)

 

Using Gauss elimination, multiply the first equation by (−5/15) and subtract from the second equation:

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc}15 & -5 & 0 \\0 & 5.833333 & 0 \\0 & 0 & 15\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}\Delta \delta_{2} \\\Delta \delta_{3} \\\Delta V_{2}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}-2.0 \\0.33333 \\-0.5\end{array}\right]\)

Back substitution:

\(\begin{aligned}&\Delta V_{2}=-0.5 / 15=-0.033333 \\&\Delta \delta_{3}=0.33333 / 5.833333=0.05714285 \\&\Delta \delta_{2}=[-2.0+5(0.05714285)] / 15=-0.1142857\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\Delta x=\left[\begin{array}{c}\Delta \delta_{2} \\\Delta \delta_{3} \\\Delta V_{2}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}-0.1142857 \\0.05714285 \\-0.033333\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 4 Compute x(1)

\(\underline{x}(1)=\left[\begin{array}{l}\delta_{2}(1) \\\delta_{3}(1) \\V_{2}(1)\end{array}\right]=x(0)+\Delta x=\left[\begin{array}{l}0 \\0 \\1\end{array}\right]+\left[\begin{array}{c}-.1142857 \\.05714285 \\-.0333333\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}-0.1142857 \\0.05714285 \\0.96666667\end{array}\right] \begin{aligned}&\text { radians } \\&\text { radians } \\&\text { per unit }\end{aligned}\)

Check \(Q_{G 3}\) using Eq. (6.5.3)

\(\begin{array}{r}Q_{3}=V_{3}\left[Y_{31} V_{1} \sin \left(\delta_{3}-\delta_{1}-\theta_{31}\right)+Y_{32} V_{2} \sin \left(\delta_{3}-\delta_{2}-\theta_{32}\right)+Y_{33} V_{3} \sin \left(-\theta_{33}\right)\right] \\=1\left[(2.5)(1) \sin (\underbrace{0.05714}_{\text {radians }}-\frac{\pi}{2})+5(0.966666) \sin \left(0.05714+0.11429-\frac{\pi}{2}\right)\right. \\\left.+7.5(1) \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right)\right]\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{3} &=1[-2.4959-4.7625+7.5]=0.2416 \text { per unit } \\Q _{ G 3} &=Q_{3}+Q_{L 3}=0.2416+0=0.2416 \text { per unit }\end{aligned}\)

 

Since \(Q_{G 3}\) = 0.2416 is within the limits [−5.0, +5.0], bus 3 remains a voltage-controlled bus. This completes the first Newton-Raphson iteration.

\(n \neq k\)

\(n=k\)

\(\begin{aligned}& J 1_{k k}=\frac{\partial P _{k}}{\partial \delta_{k}}=- V _{k} \sum_{n=1 \atop n \neq k}^{N} Y _{ kn } V _{n} \sin \left(\delta_{k}-\delta_{n}-\theta_{k n}\right) \\& J 2_{k k}=\frac{\partial P _{k}}{\partial V _{k}}= V _{k} Y _{ kk } \cos \theta_{k k}+\sum_{n=1}^{N} Y _{ kn } V _{n} \cos \left(\delta_{k}-\delta_{n}-\theta_{k n}\right) \\& J 3_{k k}=\frac{\partial Q _{k}}{\partial \delta_{k}}= V _{k} \sum_{n=1 \atop n \neq k}^{N} Y _{ kn } V _{n} \cos \left(\delta_{k}-\delta_{n}-\theta_{k n}\right) \\& J 4 _{k k}=\frac{\partial Q _{k}}{\partial V _{k}}=- V _{k} Y _{ kk } \sin \theta_{k k}+\sum_{n=1}^{N} Y _{ kn } V _{n} \sin \left(\delta_{k}-\delta_{n}-\theta_{k n}\right)\end{aligned}\)
