Guest Question.

Determine the natural frequencies and mode shapes for the system of Figure 8.1. Use \(\theta\) and x as generalized coordinates.

Admin
Solution

The kinetic energy of the system at an arbitrary instant is

\(T=\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{1}{12}mL^{2}\dot{\theta }^{2} \right) +\frac{1}{2}(2m)\dot{x}^{2} \) (a)

The potential energy of the system at an arbitrary instant is

\(V=\frac{1}{2}k \left(x-\frac{L}{2}\theta \right)^{2}+\frac{1}{2}kx^{2} \) (b)

Application of Lagrange’s equations leads to

\( \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{12}mL^{2} & 0 \\ 0 &2m \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} \ddot{\theta } \\ \ddot{x} \end{matrix}\right]+\begin{bmatrix} 4\frac{L^{2}}{4} & -k\frac{L}{2} \\ -k\frac{L}{2} & 2k \end{bmatrix}\left[\begin{matrix} \theta \\ x \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix}0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right] \) (c)

Since the mass matrix is a diagonal matrix, its inverse is also a diagonal matrix with the reciprocals of the diagonal elements of \(\pmb{M}\) along its diagonal. The matrix \(\pmb{M}^{-1} \pmb{K}\) is

\(\pmb{M}^{-1} \pmb{K}=\begin{bmatrix} \frac{12}{mL^{2}} & 0 \\ \\0& \frac{1}{2m} \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}k\frac{L^{2}}{4} & -k\frac{L}{2} \\ \\-\frac{L}{2} & 2k \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{3k}{m} & -\frac{6k}{mL} \\ \\-\frac{kL}{4m} & \frac{k}{m} \end{bmatrix} =\phi \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -\frac{6}{L} \\ \\-\frac{L}{4} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) (d)

where \( \phi =\frac{k}{m} \). Calculating the eigenvalues of \(\pmb{M}^{-1} \pmb{K} \)

\(\det (\pmb{M}^{-1} \pmb{K}-\lambda \pmb{I})=\begin{vmatrix} 3\phi -\lambda & \frac{-6\phi }{L} \\ \\ \frac{-\phi L}{4} & \phi -\lambda \end{vmatrix} =(3\phi -\lambda )(\phi -\lambda ) -\left(\frac{-6\phi }{L} \right)\left(\frac{-\phi L}{4} \right) \)

\(=\lambda ^{2}-4\phi \lambda +\frac{3}{2}\phi ^{2} \) (e)

The eigenvalues are obtained by solving

\( \beta ^{2}-4\beta +\frac{3}{2}=0 \) (f)

where \( \beta =\lambda /\phi \). The solutions are

\(\beta =\frac{4\pm \sqrt{(-4)^{2}-4 \left(\frac{3}{2} \right)} }{2}=\frac{1}{2}(4\pm \sqrt{10} )=0.419,3.58 \) (g)

The natural frequencies are the square roots of the eigenvalues

\(\omega _{1}=\sqrt{0.419\frac{k}{m} }=0.647\sqrt{\frac{k}{m} } \) \( \omega _{2}=\sqrt{3.58\frac{k}{m} }=1.89\sqrt{\frac{k}{m} } \) (h)

The mode-shape vectors are obtained from

\(\begin{vmatrix} 3\phi -\lambda _{i}& \frac{-6\phi }{L} \\ \\\frac{-\phi L}{4} & \phi -\lambda _{i} \end{vmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix}X_{i,1}\\ \\ X_{i,2} \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right] \) (i)

for i = 1,2. The two equations are linearly dependent when evaluated for the eigenvalues. The first equation gives

\((3\phi -\lambda _{i})X_{i1}-\frac{6\phi }{L} X_{i2}=0 \) (j)

or

\( X_{i2}=\frac{L(3\phi -\lambda _{i})}{6\phi }X_{i1} \) (k)

Recalling that \(\lambda _{1}=0.419\phi \),

\(X_{12}=\frac{L(3\phi -0.419\phi )}{6\phi } X_{11}=0.430 LX_{11} \) (l)

and given that \(\lambda _{2}=3.58\phi \),

\(X_{22}=\frac{L(3\phi -3.58\phi )}{6\phi }X_{21}=-0.0977LX_{21} \) (m)

Arbitrarily taking \(X_{i1}-1 \), the mode-shape vectors are

\(\pmb{X_{1}}=\left[\begin{matrix}1 \\ 0.430L \end{matrix} \right] \) \(\pmb{X_{2}}=\left[\begin{matrix}1 \\ -0.0977L \end{matrix} \right] \) (n)

In the first mode, when x is 1, the value of \(\theta\) is 0.430L. The bar and the block are moving in the same direction for the first mode. In the second mode, when x is 1, the value of \(\theta\) is -0.977L, which is a counter-clockwise rotation. The bar and the block move in opposite directions for the second mode. A point of zero displacement must exist in the spring connecting the bar to the block.
