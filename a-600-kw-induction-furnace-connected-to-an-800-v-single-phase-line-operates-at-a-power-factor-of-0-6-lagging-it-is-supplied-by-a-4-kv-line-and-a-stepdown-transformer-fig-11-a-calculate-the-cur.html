Guest Question.

A 600 kW induction furnace connected to an 800 V single-phase line operates at a power factor of 0.6 lagging. It is supplied by a 4 kV line and a stepdown transformer (Fig. 11).

a. Calculate the current in the 4000 V line.
b. If a 500 kvar capacitor is installed on the HV side of the transformer, calculate the new powera factor and the new line current.

Admin
Solution

This is an interesting example where individual power factor correction must be applied. The reason is that the induction furnace is a single-phase device whereas the plant is certainly energized by a 3-phase line. We cannot correct the power factor of singlephase equipment by adding balanced 3-phase capacitors at the service entrance.

a. Active power absorbed by the furnace is

 

\(P=600 kW\)

 

Apparent power absorbed by the furnace is

 

\(\begin{aligned}S &=P / \cos \theta=600 / 0.6 \\&=1000 kVA\end{aligned}\)

 

Current in the 4 kV line is

 

\(\begin{aligned}I &=S / E=1000 / 4 \\&=250 A\end{aligned}\)

 

b. Reactive power absorbed by the furnace is

 

\(\begin{aligned}Q &=\sqrt{S^{2}-P^{2}} \\&=\sqrt{1000^{2}-600^{2}} \\&=800 kvar\end{aligned}\)

 

Reactive power supplied by the capacitor is

 

\(Q_{ C }=500 kvar\)

 

Reactive power that the line must supply is

 

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{ L } &=Q-Q_{ C } \\&=800-500=300 kvar\end{aligned}\)

 

Active power drawn from the line is

 

\(P_{ L }=600 kW\)

 

Apparent power drawn from the line is

 

\(\begin{aligned}S_{ L } &=\sqrt{P_{ L }^{2}+Q_{ L }^{2}} \\&=\sqrt{600^{2}+300^{2}} \\&=671 kVA\end{aligned}\)

 

New power factor is of the line

 

\(\cos \theta=P_{ L } / S_{ L }=600 / 671=0.89\)

 

The new line current is

 

\(\begin{aligned}I &=S_{ L } / E=671000 / 4000 \\&=168 A\end{aligned}\)

 

By installing a single-phase capacitor bank, the line current drops from 250 A to 168 A, which represents a decrease of 33 percent. It follows that the \(I^{2} R\) loss and voltage drop on the supply line will be greatly reduced. Furthermore, the power factor rises from 60% to 89% which will significantly reduce the monthly power bill. Finally, the 3-phase line currents are more likely to be reasonably balanced at the service entrance despite the presence of this large single-phase load.

A single-phase load can be made to appear as a balanced, unity power-factor 3-phase load by using capacitors and inductors.
