Guest Question.

The following reactions occur during the formation of ethyl bromide:
\(C_{2}H_{5}Br\overset{k_{1}}{\rightarrow}C_{2}H_{5}^{*}+Br^{*}\)
\(Br^{*}+C_{2}H_{5}Br\overset{k_{2}}{\rightarrow}HBr+C_{2}H_{4}Br^{*}\)
\(C_{2}H_{4}Br^{*}\overset{k_{3}}{\rightarrow}C_{2}H_{4}+Br^{*}\)
\(Br^{*}+C_{2}H_{4}Br^{*}\overset{k_{4}}{\rightarrow}C_{2}H_{4}Br_{2}\)

The activation energies for each step are \(E_{1}\) =20 kcal/mol, \(E_{2}\) =15 kcal/mol, \(E_{3}\) =20 kcal/mol, and \(E_{4}\) =10 kcal/mol, respectively. Determine the decomposition rate of \(C_{2}H_{5}Br\) and the apparent activation energy.

Admin
Solution

Rename
\(M=C_{2}H_{5}Br, R_{1}^{\bullet }=C_{2}H_{5}^{*}\), \(R_{2}^{\bullet }=Br^{*}\), \(R_{3}^{\bullet }=C_{2}H_{4}Br^{*}\)

\(M\overset{k_{1}}{\rightarrow}R_{1}+R_{2}\)
\(R_{1}+M\overset{k_{2}}{\rightarrow}HBr+R_{3}\)
\(R_{3}\overset{k_{3}}{\rightarrow}C_{2}H_{4}+R_{1}\)
\(R_{1}+R_{3}\overset{k_{4}}{\rightarrow}P_{2}\)

Thus, the reaction rates are as follows:
\((-r_{M})=k_{1}[M]+k_{2}[R_{\dot{1}}][M]\approx k_{2}[R_{\dot{1}}][M]\) (11.27)
\(r_{\underset{R_{\dot{1}}}{\rightarrow}}=K_{1}[M]-K_{2}[R_{\dot{1}}][M]+k_{3}[R_{\dot{3}}]-k_[4][R_{\dot{1}}][R_{\dot{3}}]=0\) (11.28)
\(r_{R_{\dot{3}}}=-k_{3}[R_{\dot{3}}]+k_{2}[R_{\dot{1}}][M]-k_{4}[R_{\dot{1}}][R_{\dot{3}}]=0\) (11.29)

Add Equations 11.28 and 11.29, we obtain:
\(R_{\dot{3}}=\frac{k_{1}[M]}{2k_{4}[R_{\dot{1}}]}\) (11.30)

Substitute Equation 11.30 in Equation 11.28:
\([R_{\dot{1}}]^{2}=\frac{k_{1}k_{3}}{2k_{2}k_{4}}\) (11.31)

Substitute Equation 11.31 in Equation 11.27:
\((-r_{M})=k_{2}[R_{\dot{1}}][M]=\sqrt{\frac{k_{1}k_{2}k_{3}}{2k_{4}}}[C_{2}H_{5}Br]\)

Thus, the decomposition rate is a first-order reaction. Considering that:
\(k_{a}=k_{0}e^{-(Ea/RT)}\)

or
\(k_{i}=k_{0}e^{-(Ei/RT)}\)

Using logarithm and equaling, we obtain:
\(E_{a}=\frac{E_{1}+E_{2}+E_{3}-E_{4}}{2}=27.5 kcal/mol\)
\(E_{a}=27.5 kcal/mol\)
