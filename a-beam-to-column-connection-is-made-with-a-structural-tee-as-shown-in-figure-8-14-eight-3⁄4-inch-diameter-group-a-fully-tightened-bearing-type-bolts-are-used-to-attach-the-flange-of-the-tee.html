Guest Question.

A beam-to-column connection is made with a structural tee as shown in Figure 8.14 .Eight 3⁄4-inch-diameter, Group A, fully tightened bearing-type bolts are used to attach the flange of the tee to the column flange. Investigate the adequacy of this connection (the tee-to-column connection) if it is subjected to a service dead load of 20 kips and a service live load of 40 kips at an eccentricity of 2.75 inches. Assume that the bolt threads are in the plane of shear. All structural steel is A992.

Admin
Solution

Determine the shear and bearing strengths. The tables in Part 7 of the Manual will be used. From Table 7-1, the shear strength is
\(\phi r_n = 17.9 kips/bolt\)
For the inner bolts with a spacing of 3 inches, the bearing strength from Table 7-4 is
\(\phi r_n = 87.8t = 87.8(0.560) = 49.2 kips/bolt\)
For the edge bolts, use Table 7-5 and a conservative edge distance of 11⁄4 inches.The bearing strength for these bolts is
\(\phi r_n = 49.4t = 49.4(0.560) = 27.7 kips/bolt\)
Since the shear strength is less than the bearing strength of any bolt, the shear strength controls.
The factored load is
\(P_u = 1.2D + 1.6L = 1.2(20) + 1.6(40) = 88 kips\)
and the shearybearing load per bolt is 88/8 = 11 kips. The shear design strength per bolt is
\(\phi r_n = 17.9 kips > 11 kips (OK)\)
Compute the tensile force per bolt and then check the tension–shear interaction.
Because of symmetry, the centroid of the connection is at middepth. Figure 8.15 shows the bolt areas and the distribution of bolt tensile forces. From Equation 8.6, the resisting moment is
\(M = nr_{t} d = 4r_{t}(3 – 1.5 + 3 + 3 – 1.5) = 24r_{t}\)
The applied moment is
\(M_u = P_{u}e = 88(2.75) = 242 in.-kips\)
Equating the resisting and applied moments, we get
\(24r_{t} = 242, or r_{t} = 10.08 kips\)
The factored load shear stress is
\(f_{rv} =\frac{ 11}{0.4418} = 24.90 ksi\)

and from AISC Equation J3-3a, the nominal tensile stress is
\(F'_{nt}=1.3F_{nt} – \frac{ F_{nt}}{\phi F_{nv} }f_{rv}\leq F_{nt}\)
\(= 1.3(90) – \frac{90}{0.75(54)}(24.90) = 61.67 ksi < 90 ksi\)
The design tensile strength is
\(\phi R_n = 0.75F'\)
\(n_{t}A_{b} = 0.75(61.67)(0.4418) = 20.4 kips > 10.08 kips (OK)\)
The connection is satisfactory.
Determine the shear and bearing strengths. The tables in Part 7 of the Manual will be used. From Table 7-1, the shear strength is
\(\frac{r_n}{\Omega} = 11.9 kips/bolt\)
For the inner bolts with a spacing of 3 inches, the bearing strength from Table 7-4 is
\(\frac{r_n}{\Omega} = 58.5t = 58.5(0.560) = 32.8 kips/bolt\)
For the edge bolts, use Table 7-5 and a conservative edge distance of 11⁄4 inches. The bearing strength for these bolts is
\(\frac{r_n}{\Omega} \)=32.9t = 32.9(0.560) = 18.4 kips/bolt
The shear strength controls.
The total applied load is
\(P_a = D + L = 20 + 40 = 60 kips\)
and the shearybearing load per bolt is 60/8 = 7.5 kips. The allowable shear strength per bolt is
\(\frac{r_n}{\Omega} = 11.9 kips > 7.5 kips (OK)\)
Compute the tensile force per bolt, then check the tension–shear interactions. The applied moment is
\(M_a = P_{a}e = 60(2.75) = 165 in.-kips\)
From Equation 8.6, the resisting moment is
\(M = nr_{t}d = 4rt(3 – 1.5 + 3 + 3 – 1.5) = 24r_{t}\)
Equating the resisting and applied moments, we get \(24r_t = 165 \)or\( r_t = 6.875 kips\)
The shearing stress is
\(f_{rv} =\frac {7.5}{0.4418} = 16.98 ksi\)
and from AISC Equation J3-3b, the nominal tensile stress is
\(F'_{nt}=1.3F_{nt} – \frac{\Omega F_{nt}}{F_{nv} }f_{rv}\leq F_{nt}\)
\(= 1.3(90) -\frac{ 2.00(90)}{54} (16.98) = 60.40 ksi < 90 ksi\)
The allowable tensile strength is
\(\frac{R_n}{\Omega}= \frac{F'_{nt}A_b}{\Omega}\)
\(= \frac{60.40(0.4418)}{2.00} = 13.3 kips > 6.875 kips (OK)\)

The connection is satisfactory.
