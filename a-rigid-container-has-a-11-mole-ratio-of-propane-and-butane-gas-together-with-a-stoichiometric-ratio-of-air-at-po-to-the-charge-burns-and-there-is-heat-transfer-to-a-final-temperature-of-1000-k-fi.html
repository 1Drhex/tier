Guest Question.

A rigid container has a 1:1 mole ratio of propane and butane gas together with a stoichiometric ratio of air at \(P _{ o }, T _{ o }\). The charge burns and there is heat transfer to a final temperature of 1000 K. Find the final pressure and the heat transfer per kmol of fuel mixture.

Admin
Solution
\(C _{3} H _{8}+ C _{4} H _{10}+ v _{ O _{2}}\left( O _{2}+3.76 N _{2}\right) \rightarrow 7 CO _{2}+9 H _{2} O +3.76 v _{ O _{2}} N _{2}\)

The carbon and hydrogen balance has been used so oxygen gives

\(v_{ O _{2}}=7+9 / 2=11.5 ; \quad v_{ N _{2}}=43.24\)

The energy equation is

\(\begin{aligned}U _{ P }- U _{ R } &= Q -0=\left( H _{ P }- H _{ R }\right)- n _{ P } \overline{ R } T _{ P }+ n _{ R } \overline{ R } T _{ R } \\&= H _{ P }^{ o }+\Delta H _{ P }- H _{ R }^{ o }- n _{ P } \overline{ R } T _{ P }+ n _{ R } \overline{ R } T _{ R }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(n _{ R }=1+1+11.5 \times 4.76=56.74, \quad n _{ P }=7+9+43.24=59.24\)

 

From Table A.9

\(\Delta H _{ P }=7(33397)+9(26000)+43.24(21463)=1395839 kJ\)

From Table 13.3 (convert kJ/kg to kJ/kmol with M)

\(H _{ P }^{ o }- H _{ R }^{ o }=44.094(-46352)+58.124(-45714)=-4700926 kJ\)

Substitute into energy equation

Q = – 4 700 926 + 1 395 839 – 8.3145(59.24 × 1000 – 56.74 × 298.15)
= – 3 656 981 kJ/2 kmol fuel
= – 1 828 490 kJ/kmol fuel
