Guest Question.

Determine the member end moments, the support reactions, and the horizontal deflection of joint \(F\) of the two-story frame shown in Fig. \( 15.19 (\mathrm{a})\) by the slope-deflection method.

Admin
Solution

Degrees of Freedom. From Fig. 15.19(a), we can see that the joints \(C, D, E\), and \(F\) of the frame are free to rotate and translate in the horizontal direction. As shown in Fig. 15.19(b), the horizontal displacement of the first-story joints \(C\) and \(D\) is designated as \(\Delta_{1}\), whereas the horizontal displacement of the second-story joints \(E\) and \(F\) is expressed as \(\Delta_{1}+\Delta_{2}\), with \(\Delta_{2}\) representing the displacement of the second-story joints relative to the first-story joints. Thus, the frame has six degrees of freedom—that is, \(\theta_{c}\), \(\theta_{D}, \theta_{E}, \theta_{F}, \Delta_{1}\), and \(\Delta_{2}\).

Fixed-End Moments. The nonzero fixed-end moments are

\( \begin{array}{l} \mathrm{FEM}_{C D}=\mathrm{FEM}_{E F}=200 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \\ \mathrm{FEM}_{D C}=\mathrm{FEM}_{F E}=-200 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \end{array} \)

Chord Rotations. See Fig. 15.19(b).

\( \begin{array}{l} \psi_{A C}=\psi_{B D}=-\frac{\Delta_{1}}{20} \\ \psi_{C E}=\psi_{D F}=-\frac{\Delta_{2}}{20} \\ \psi_{C D}=\psi_{E F}=0 \end{array} \)

Slope-Deflection Equations. Using \(I_{\text {column }}=I\) and \(I_{\text {girder }}=2 I\), we write

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{A C}=0.1 E I \theta_{C}+0.015 E I \Delta_{1} \\\end{array} \) (1)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{C A}=0.2 E I \theta_{C}+0.015 E I \Delta_{1} \\\end{array} \) (2)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{B D}=0.1 E I \theta_{D}+0.015 E I \Delta_{1} \\\end{array} \) (3)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{D B}=0.2 E I \theta_{D}+0.015 E I \Delta_{1} \\\end{array} \) (4)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{C E}=0.2 E I \theta_{C}+0.1 E I \theta_{E}+0.015 E I \Delta_{2} \\\end{array} \) (5)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{E C}=0.2 E I \theta_{E}+0.1 E I \theta_{C}+0.015 E I \Delta_{2} \\\end{array} \) (6)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{D F}=0.2 E I \theta_{D}+0.1 E I \theta_{F}+0.015 E I \Delta_{2} \\\end{array} \) (7)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{F D}=0.2 E I \theta_{F}+0.1 E I \theta_{D}+0.015 E I \Delta_{2} \\\end{array} \) (8)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{C D}=0.2 E I \theta_{C}+0.1 E I \theta_{D}+200 \\\end{array} \) (9)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{D C}=0.2 E I \theta_{D}+0.1 E I \theta_{C}-200 \\\end{array} \) (10)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{E F}=0.2 E I \theta_{E}+0.1 E I \theta_{F}+200 \\\end{array} \) (11)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{F E}=0.2 E I \theta_{F}+0.1 E I \theta_{E}-200 \end{array} \) (12)

Equilibrium Equations. By considering the moment equilibrium of joints \(C, D, E\), and \(F\), we obtain

\( \begin{array}{r} M_{C A}+M_{C D}+M_{C E}=0 \\\end{array} \) (13)

\( \begin{array}{r} M_{D B}+M_{D C}+M_{D F}=0 \\\end{array} \) (14)

\( \begin{array}{r} M_{E C}+M_{E F}=0 \\\end{array} \) (15)

\( \begin{array}{r} M_{F D}+M_{F E}=0 \end{array} \) (16)

To establish the remaining two equilibrium equations, we successively pass a horizontal section just above the lower ends of the columns of each story of the frame and apply the equation of horizontal equilibrium \(\left(\sum F_{X}=0\right)\) to the free body of the portion of the frame above the section. The free-body diagrams thus obtained are shown in Fig 15.19(c) and (d). By applying the equilibrium equation \(\sum F_{X}=0\) to the top story of the frame (Fig. 15.19(c)), we obtain

\( S_{C E}+S_{D F}=10 \)

Similarly, by applying \(\sum F_{X}=0\) to the entire frame (Fig. 15.19(d)), we write

\( S_{A C}+S_{B D}=30 \)

By expressing column end shears in terms of column end moments as

\( \begin{array}{ll} S_{A C}=\frac{M_{A C}+M_{C A}}{20} & S_{B D}=\frac{M_{B D}+M_{D B}}{20} \\ S_{C E}=\frac{M_{C E}+M_{E C}}{20} & S_{D F}=\frac{M_{D F}+M_{F D}}{20} \end{array} \)

and by substituting these expressions into the force equilibrium equations, we obtain

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{C E}+M_{E C}+M_{D F}+M_{F D}=200 \\\end{array} \) (17)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{A C}+M_{C A}+M_{B D}+M_{D B}=600 \end{array} \) (18)

Joint Displacements. Substitution of the slope-deflection equations (Eqs. (1) through (12)) into the equilibrium equations (Eqs. (13) through (18)) yields

\( \begin{aligned} 0.6 E I \theta_{C}+0.1 E I \theta_{D}+0.1 E I \theta_{E}+0.015 E I \Delta_{1}+0.015 E I \Delta_{2} &=-200 \\\end{aligned} \) (19)

\( \begin{aligned} 0.1 E I \theta_{C}+0.6 E I \theta_{D}+0.1 E I \theta_{F}+0.015 E I \Delta_{1}+0.015 E I \Delta_{2} &=200 \\\end{aligned} \) (20)

\( \begin{aligned} 0.1 E I \theta_{C}+0.4 E I \theta_{E}+0.1 E I \theta_{F}+0.015 E I \Delta_{2} &=-200 \\\end{aligned} \) (21)

\( \begin{aligned} 0.1 E I \theta_{D}+0.1 E I \theta_{E}+0.4 E I \theta_{F}+0.015 E I \Delta_{2} &=200 \\\end{aligned} \) (22)

\( \begin{aligned} 0.3 E I \theta_{C}+0.3 E I \theta_{D}+0.3 E I \theta_{E}+0.3 E I \theta_{F}+0.06 E I \Delta_{2} &=200 \\\end{aligned} \) (23)

\( \begin{aligned} 0.1 E I \theta_{C}+0.1 E I \theta_{D}+0.02 E I \Delta_{1} &=200 \end{aligned} \) (24)

By solving Eqs. (19) through (24) by the Gauss-Jordan elimination method (Appendix B), we determine

\( \begin{array}{l} E I \theta_{C}=-812.988 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}^{2} \\ E I \theta_{D}=-241.566 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}^{2} \\ E I \theta_{E}=-789.612 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}^{2} \\ E I \theta_{F}=353.248 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}^{2} \\ E I \Delta_{1}=15,272.728 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}^{3} \quad \text { or } \quad \Delta_{1}=0.0758 \mathrm{ft}=0.91 \mathrm{in} \rightarrow \\ E I \Delta_{2}=10,787.878 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}^{3} \quad \text { or } \quad \Delta_{2}=0.0536 \mathrm{ft}=0.643 \mathrm{in} . \rightarrow \end{array} \)

Thus, the horizontal deflection of joint \(F\) of the frame is as follows:

\( \Delta_{F}=\Delta_{1}+\Delta_{2}=0.91+0.643=1.553 \mathrm{in} . \rightarrow \)

Member End Moments. By substituting the numerical values of the joint displacements into the slope-deflection equations (Eqs. (1) through (12)), we obtain

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{A C}=147.8 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowleft \\ M_{C A}=66.5 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowleft \\ M_{B D}=204.9 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowleft \\ M_{D B}=180.8 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowleft \\ M_{C E}=-79.7 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \quad \text { or } \quad 79.7 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowright \\ M_{E C}=-77.4 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \quad \text { or } \quad 77.4 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowright \\ M_{D F}=148.8 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowleft \\ M_{F D}=208.3 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowleft \\ M_{C D}=13.2 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowleft \\ M_{D C}=-329.6 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \quad \text { or } \quad 329.6 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowright \\ M_{E F}=77.4 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowleft \\ M_{F E}=-208.3 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \quad \text { or } \quad 208.3 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowright \end{array} \)

Back substitution of the numerical values of member end moments into the equilibrium equations yields

\( \begin{array}{c} M_{C A}+M_{C D}+M_{C E}=66.5+13.2-79.7=0 \\ M_{D B}+M_{D C}+M_{D F}=180.8-329.6+148.8=0 \\ M_{E C}+M_{E F}=-77.4+77.4=0 \\ M_{F D}+M_{F E}=208.3-208.3=0 \\ M_{C E}+M_{E C}+M_{D F}+M_{F D}=-79.7-77.4+148.8+208.3=200 \\ M_{A C}+M_{C A}+M_{B D}+M_{D B}=147.8+66.5+204.9+180.8=600 \end{array} \)

Member End Shears and Axial Forces. See Fig. 15.19(e).

Support Reactions. See Fig. 15.19(f).

Equilibrium Check. The equilibrium equations check.
