Guest Question.

The scheme shown below represents geometry of a heavily loaded column footing which has a total depth (thickness) h = 0.8 m. The footing is centrally loaded by the vertical imposed actions. The gross design soil pressure \( q_{Ed} \) on the underside of the footing was calculated equals 1445 kPa. The design requires using class C25/30 concrete and type B500B steel. It is required to design the bottom reinforcement using the approach described in EN 1992-1-1§9.8.2.2.

Admin
Solution

The EN 1992-1-1 § 9.8.2.2 design approach for anchorage of steel bars provides the maximum force in the reinforcement. Hence, the required bottom reinforcement may be designed.
Actions and effects:

Characteristic self-weight of footing \( W_{Gk} = y_{c, k} \times L \times B \times h \)

\( = 25 \times 2.0 \times 2.0 \times 0.8 = 80 kN \)

 

Table 6.6 gives partial factor \( y_{G} = 1.35.\) Hence,

\( W_{d} = 1.35 \times 80 = 108 kN \)

 

The effective design soil pressure on the underside of the footing is

\( {q}'_{Ed} = q_{Ed} – \left ( \frac{W_{d}}{BL} \right ) = 1445 – \left ( \frac{108}{2 \times 2} \right ) = 1418 kPa \)

 

The tensile force to be anchored is given by:

\( F_{s} = R_{d} \times \frac{Z_{e}}{Z_{i}} \)

 

where:
\( R_{d} = \) design resultant of ground pressure within distance X
\( Z_{e} = \) External lever arm
\( Z_{i} = \) Internal lever arm

EN 1992-1-1 §9.8.2.2(3) suggests, as simplifications, \( Z_{e} \) may be determined assuming \( e = 0.15 b \) and \( Z_{i} \) may be taken as 0.9 d.

Assume using bar diameter \( \varnothing = 16 mm \), and nominal concrete cover to the reinforcing bars \( c_{nom} = K_{2} = 75 mm \) [EN 1992-1-1 §4.4.1.3(4)]

 

\( e = 0.15 \times 0.5 = 0.075 m \)

 

\( Z_{e} = \frac{B}{2} – \frac{X}{2} – \frac{b}{2} + e \)

 

Maximum tensile force \( F_{s, max} \)

Assuming \( X = \frac{B}{2} – 0.35 b \) (the distance between sections at A and B), the maximum tensile force \( F_{s,max} \) on the reinforcement is obtained using

\( F_{s,max} = R_{d} \times \frac{Z_{e}}{Z_{i}} \)

 

\( X = \frac{B}{2} – 0.35 b = \frac{2}{2} – 0.35 \times 0.5 = 0.825 m \)

 

\( d = h – c_{nom} – \left ( \frac{\varnothing }{2} \right ) = 0.800 – 0.075 – 0.008 = 0.717 m \)

 

\( Z_{i} = 0.9 d = 0.9 \times 0.717 = 0.645 m \)

 

\( Z_{e} = \frac{B}{2} – \frac{X}{2} – \frac{b}{2} + e = \frac{2}{2} – \frac{0.825}{2} – \frac{0.5}{2} + 0.075 = 0.413 m \)

 

\( R_{d} = {q}'_{Ed} \times L \times X = 1418 \times 2 \times 0.825 = 2340 kN \)

 

\( F_{s,max} = 2340 \times \frac{0.413}{0.645} = 1498 kN \)

 

Required reinforcement area \( A_{s} \)
Partial factor for reinforcing steel \( y_{s} = 1.15 \)

Required reinforcement area

\( A_{s} = \frac{F_{s, max}}{f_{yk} /y_{s}} = \frac{1498/1000}{500/1.15} \)

 

\( = 3.445 \times 10^{-3} m^{2} = 3445 mm^{2} \)

 

Provide 18\( \varnothing \) 16 both ways with 91 mm clear bar spacing.
Verifications and reinforcement arrangement

• The recommended minimum bar diameter is 8 mm [EN 1992-1-1 §9.8.2.1(1)]. The provided bar diameter is \( 16 mm > \varnothing_{min}\) ( OK.)

 

\( A_{s, provided} = 18 \times 200 = 3600 mm^{2} > A_{s,required} \) ( OK.)

 

• The minimum clear spacing of reinforcing bars \( s_{min} = 25 mm \) (EN 1992-1-1 §8.2). The provided clear bar spacing is \( 91 mm > s_{min} \) ( OK.)

 

\( A_{s, max} = 0.04 A_{c} \) (EN 1992-1-1 §9.2.1.1)

 

\( = 0.04 \times 800 \times 2000 = 64000 mm^{2} \gg A_{s,provided} \) ( OK.)

 

• From Table 6.16: minimum percentage of reinforcement is

 

\( A_{s,min} = 0.13 \% = 0.0013 \times 717 \times 2000 \)

 

\( = 1864 mm^{2} < A_{s, provided} \) ( OK.)

 

• The provided concrete cover to the external reinforcing bars is

 

\( c_{provided} = \frac{2000 – 18 \times 16 – 17 \times 91}{2} = 82.5 mm > 75 mm \) ( OK.)

 

• Verification of straight bar anchorage:
For straight bars without end anchorage the minimum value of X is the most critical. As a simplification, \( X_{min} = h/2 \) may be assumed [EN 1992-1-1 §9.8.2.2(5)]

 

\( X_{min} = \frac{h}{2} = \frac{0.8}{2} = 0.4 m \)

 

\( Z_{e} = \frac{B}{2} – \frac{X_{min}}{2} – \frac{b}{2} + e = \frac{2}{2} – \frac{0.4}{2} – \frac{0.5}{2} + 0.075 = 0.625 m \)

 

\( R_{d} = {q}'_{Ed} \times L \times X_{min} = 1418 \times 2 \times 0.4 = 1134.4 kN \)

 

\( F_{s} = 1134.4 \times \frac{0.625}{0.645} = 1099.2 kN \)

 

for the purpose of this verification assume \( c_{nom} = 0.040 m . \)
From Table 6.20: the design anchorage length in case of tensile force and good bond conditions is \( I_{bd} = 500 mm = 0.5 m. \)

 

\( I_{b} = \frac{F_{s}}{A_{s}f_{yd}} \times I_{bd} = \frac{1099.2}{ \frac{3600}{10^{6}} \times \frac{500 \times 1000}{1.15} } \times 0.5 = 0.351 m \)

 

Straight bar anchorage will be verified satisfactory when

\( I_{b} + c_{nom} < X_{min} \)

 

\( 0.351 + 0.040 = 0.391 m < \left ( X_{min} = 0.4 m; OK. \right ) \)
