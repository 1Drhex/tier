Guest Question.

Consider hypersonic flow past a sharp cone where \(-θ_c ≤ α ≤ θ_c\), as shown in Fig. 12.20 . Neglecting the effects of skin friction and using the modified Newtonian flow model to describe the pressure distribution, derive expressions for the lift coefficient, the drag coefficient, and the pitch moment coefficient.

Admin
Solution

For simplicity, let us first calculate the forces in a body-fixed coordinate system. As shown in Fig. 12.21 , the forces in the body-fixed coordinate system are A , the force along the axis of the body, and N , the force normal to the body axis. Once A and N have been calculated, the lift and the drag can be calculated since:

\(L=N \cos α – A \sin α\)

and

\(D=N \sin α -A\cos α\).

Applying equations (12.24) through (12.28) specifically for the sharp cone flow depicted in Fig. 12.20 ,

\(\cos \eta =\frac{\vec{V_\infty.\hat{n} } }{\left|\vec{V_\infty } \right|\left|\hat{n} \right| }\). \(\vec{V_\infty}=U_\infty \cos \alpha \hat{i}- U_\infty \sin \alpha \hat{j}\). \(\hat{n} =\hat{i}\sin \theta – \hat{j}\cos \theta \cos \beta -\hat{k}\cos \theta \sin \beta\).

\(\cos \eta =\cos \alpha \sin \theta +\sin \alpha \cos \theta \cos \beta\).

\(C_p=C_{p,t2}\cos ^2\eta\).

\(C_p= C_{p,t2}(\cos α \sinθ_c +\sin α \cos θ_c cos β)^2\).

since the deflection angle is the cone semivertex angle, \(θ_c\), which is a constant.

The axial force coefficient is found by integrating the pressure force over the entire (closed) surface of the cone:

\(C_A=\frac{1}{(\frac{1}{2}\rho _\infty U^{2}_{\infty } )(\pi R^{2}_{b} )} \oiint_{S} {(p-p_\infty )(\hat{n}dS ).\hat{i} }\).

Recall that, since we are integrating over a closed surface, the net force in any direction due to a constant pressure acting on that closed surface is zero, so

subtracting \(p_\infty\), as is done in equation (12.32), does not affect the value of the axial force. A differential element for the surface of the cone ( dS ) is:

\(dS=r dβ ds=(x\tanθ_c)dβ \frac{dx}{\cos \theta _c}\).

Combining equations (12.31) through (12.33) with equation (12.26) and noting that \(C_{p, base}\) = 0, the limits of integration are 0≤ β ≤ π (if we multiply by 2) and 0≤ x ≤ \(x_L \), the axial force coefficient is:

\(C_p= C_{p,t2}(\cos α \sinθ_c +\sin α \cos θ_c cos β)^2\).

\(C_A=\frac{1}{(\frac{1}{2}\rho _\infty U^{2}_{\infty } )(\pi R^{2}_{b} )}\oiint_{S}{(p-p_\infty )(\hat{n}dS ).\hat{i} }\).

\(dS=r dβ ds=(x\tanθ_c)dβ \frac{dx}{\cos \theta _c}\).

\(\hat{n} =\hat{i}\sin \theta – \hat{j}\cos \theta \cos \beta -\hat{k}\cos \theta \sin \beta\). \(C_A=\frac{2C_{p,t2}}{\pi R^{2}_{b} }\int_{0}^{x_L}{[\int_{0}^{\pi }{(\cos^2 \alpha \sin ^2\theta _c+2\sin \alpha \cos \alpha \sin \theta _c\cos \theta _c\cos \beta +\sin ^2\alpha \cos ^2\theta _c\cos ^2\beta )(x\tan \theta _c)d\beta \frac{dx}{\cos \theta _c}\sin \theta _c } ]}\).

Integrating first over β yields

\(C_A=\frac{2C_{p,t2}}{\pi R^{2}_{b} }(\cos ^2\alpha \sin^2 \theta _c\pi +\sin ^2\alpha \cos ^2\theta _c\frac{\pi }{2} )\tan ^2\theta _cxdx\).

Integrating with respect to x gives us

\(C_A=\frac{2C_{p,t2}}{ R^{2}_{b} }(\cos ^2\alpha \sin^2 \theta _c+\frac{1}{2} \sin ^2\alpha \cos ^2\theta _c )\tan ^2\theta _c\frac{x^{2}_{L} }{2}\).

Since \(R_b = x_L \tan θ_c\).

\(C_A=C_{p,t2}[\sin ^2\theta _c+\frac{1}{2}\sin ^2\alpha (1-3\sin ^2\theta _c) ]\). We will calculate the normal force coefficient due to the pressures acting over the closed surface of the cone. Since the normal force is positive in the negative y direction,
\(C_N=\frac{1}{(\frac{1}{2}\rho _\infty U^{2}_{\infty } )(\pi R^{2}_{b} )}\oiint_{S}{(p-p_\infty )(\hat{n}dS ).(-\hat{i} )}\).

Combining equations (12.26), (12.31), (12.33), and (12.35) and using the limits of integration, \(0 ≤ β ≤ π\) and \(0 ≤ x ≤ x_L\) (as discussed previously), we have:

\(\hat{n} =\hat{i}\sin \theta – \hat{j}\cos \theta \cos \beta -\hat{k}\cos \theta \sin \beta\).

\(C_p= C_{p,t2}(\cos α \sinθ_c +\sin α \cos θ_c cos β)^2\).

\(dS=r dβ ds=(x\tanθ_c)dβ \frac{dx}{\cos \theta _c}\).

\(C_N=\frac{1}{(\frac{1}{2}\rho _\infty U^{2}_{\infty } )(\pi R^{2}_{b} )}\oiint_{S} {(p-p_\infty )(\hat{n}dS ).(-\hat{i} )}\). \(C_N=\frac{2C_{p,t2}}{\pi R^{2}_{b} }\int_{0}^{x_L}{[\int_{0}^{\pi }{(\cos ^2\alpha \sin ^2\theta _c+2\sin \alpha \cos \alpha \sin \theta _c\cos \theta _c\cos \beta +\sin ^2\alpha \cos ^2\theta _c\cos ^2\beta )(x\tan \theta _c)d\beta \frac{dx}{\cos \theta _c}(\cos \theta _c\cos \beta ) } ]}\).

Integrating first with respect to β gives us:

\(C_N=\frac{2C_{p,t2}}{\pi R^{2}_{b} }\int_{0}^{x_L}{(2\sin \alpha \cos \alpha \sin \theta _c\cos \theta _c\frac{\pi }{2} )\tan \theta _cxdx}\).

Integrating with respect to x yields:

\(C_N=\frac{2C_{p,t2}}{ R^{2}_{b} }(\sin \alpha \cos \alpha \sin \theta _c\cos \theta _c)\tan \theta _c\frac{x^{2}_{L} }{2}\).

so that

\(C_N=\frac{C_{p,t2}}{2} \sin 2\alpha \cos ^2\theta _c\).

Referring to equation (12.29), the lift coefficient is

\(L=N \cos α – A \sin α\).

\(C_L=C_N\cos \alpha -C_A\sin \alpha\).

Using the coefficients presented in equations (12.34) and (12.36), we have

\(C_A=C_{p,t2}[\sin ^2\theta _c+\frac{1}{2}\sin ^2\alpha (1-3\sin ^2\theta _c) ]\).

\(C_N=\frac{C_{p,t2}}{2} \sin 2\alpha \cos ^2\theta _c\).

\(C_L=C_{p,t2}\sin \alpha [\cos ^2\alpha \cos ^2\theta _c-\sin ^2\theta _c-\frac{1}{2}\sin ^2\alpha (1-3\sin ^2\theta _c) ]\).

Correspondingly,

\(C_D=C_{p,t2}\cos \alpha [\sin ^2\alpha \cos ^2\theta _c+\sin ^2\theta _c+\frac{1}{2}\sin ^2\alpha (1-3\sin ^2\theta _c) ]\).

To calculate the pitch moment, notice that the moment due to the incremental pressure force acting at a radial distance \(\vec{r}\) from the origin is

\(\vec{dM} =\vec{r} \times \vec{dF} =\vec{r} \times p\hat{n} dS\).

Also notice that the incremental moment \(\vec{dM}\) is a vector, which can be written in terms of its components:

\(\vec{dM} =d \mathscr{L} \hat{i} +d \mathscr{N} \hat{j} +dM\hat{k}\). (12.39)

In equation (12.39), \(\mathscr{L}\) is the roll moment (which is positive when causing the right wing to move down), N is the yaw moment (which is positive when causing the nose to move to the right), and M is the pitch moment (which is positive when causing a nose-up rotation). If we want to take the moments about the apex of the cone, the moment arm is

\(\vec{r}=x \hat{i}+x \tan \theta_{c} \cos \beta \hat{j}+x \tan \theta_{c} \sin \beta \hat{k}\)

Now, examine the \(\hat{r} \times \hat{n}\) term of the moment expression:

\(\vec{r} \times \hat{n}=\left|\begin{array}{ccc}\hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\x & x \tan \theta_{c} \cos \beta & x \tan \theta_{c} \sin \beta \\\sin \theta_{c} & -\cos \theta_{c} \cos \beta & -\cos \theta_{c} \sin \beta \end{array}\right|\)

and we can obtain,

\(\vec{r} \times \hat{n} =\hat{i} [0]+\hat{j}[x\sin \beta (\tan \theta _c\sin \theta _c+\cos \theta _c)] -\hat{k}[x\cos \beta (\tan \theta _c\sin \theta _c+\cos \theta _c)]\).

The first term (i.e., the contribution to the roll moment) is always zero, since, in the absence of viscous forces, the only forces are the pressure forces that act normal to the surface and therefore act through the axis of symmetry for a body of revolution. The negative sign for the kn term (i.e., the pitch moment) results because a pressure force acting in the first and fourth quadrants \((\pi /2\geq \beta \geq -\pi /2)\) produces a nose-down (negative) pitch moment.

For this example we are interested in the pitch moment about the apex, which is considered positive when nose up,

\(M_0=\oiint_{S} {\vec{r} } \times (p-p_\infty )\hat{n} dS.\hat{k}\).

Taking the resultant cross product of the moment arm from the apex \((\vec{r})\) and the inward-directed unit normal to the surface area \(\vec{n}\) with the dot product with \(\vec{k}\), we obtain:

\((\vec{r}\times \hat{n} ).\hat{k} =-x\cos \theta _c\cos \beta -x\sin \theta _c\tan \theta _c\cos \beta\).

Therefore, the pitch moment coefficient is:

\(C_{M_0}=\frac{-2}{\pi R^{2}_{b}R_b } \int_{0}^{x_L}{[\int_{0}^{\pi }{C_px\tan \theta _cd\beta \frac{dx}{\cos \theta _c}(x\cos \theta _c\cos \beta +x\sin \theta _c\tan \theta _c\cos \beta ) } ]}\).

We can simplify this expression if we focus on the terms containing the conehalf-
angle \((θ_c)\), so that

\(\tan \theta _c\frac{1}{\cos \theta _c} (\cos \theta _c+\sin \theta _c\tan \theta _c)=\tan \theta _c(1+\tan ^2\theta _c)=\frac{\tan \theta _c}{\cos ^2\theta _c}\).

Substituting the modified-Newtonian-flow expression for the pressure coefficient and integrating with respect to β yields

\(C_{M_0}=\frac{2C_{P,t2}}{\pi R^{2}_{b}R_b }\frac{\tan \theta _c}{\cos ^2\theta _c} \int_{0}^{x_L}{x^2(\sin \theta \cos \alpha \sin \theta _c\cos \theta _c\pi )dx}\).

Integrating with respect to x and using the fact that \(R_b = x_L\ tan θ_c\), we obtain

\(C_{M_0}= -\frac{C_{p,t2}\sin 2\alpha }{3\tan \theta _c}\).

Although the resultant moment is produced by the integration of the distributed aerodynamic forces over the vehicle surface, it can be represented as composed of two components—one effectively due to the normalforce component and the other due to the axial-force component. Using this concept, we can isolate the two terms of equation (12.43):

\(C_{M_0}=\frac{-2}{\pi R^{2}_{b}R_b } \int_{0}^{x_L}{[\int_{0}^{\pi }{C_px\tan \theta _cd\beta \frac{dx}{\cos \theta _c}(x\cos \theta _c\cos \beta +x\sin \theta _c\tan \theta _c\cos \beta ) } ]}\). \(C_{M_0}= -\frac{2}{\pi R^{2}_{b}R_b } \int_{0}^{x_L}{(\int_{0}^{\pi }{C_{p}x\tan \theta _cd\beta \frac{dx}{\cos \theta _c}x\cos \theta _c\cos \beta } )}-\frac{2}{\pi R^{2}_{bb} } \int_{0}^{x_L}{(\int_{0}^{\pi }{C_{p}x\tan \theta _cd\beta \frac{dx}{\cos \theta _c}x\sin \theta _c\tan \theta _c\cos \beta } )}\).

Writing the total pitch moment as the sum of the two components, we have

\(C_{M_0}=C_{M_{0,N}}+C_{M_{0,N}}\).

where

\(C_{M_{0,N}}=-\frac{2\tan \theta _c}{\pi R^{2}_{b}R_b }\int_{0}^{x_L}{(\int_{0}^{\pi }{C_p\cos \beta d\beta } )x^2dx}\)

and

\(C_{M_{0,A}}=-\frac{2\tan^3 \theta _c}{\pi R^{2}_{b}R_b }\int_{0}^{x_L}{(\int_{0}^{\pi }{C_p\cos \beta d\beta } )x^2dx}\).

Substituting the modified Newtonian-flow expression for the pressure coefficient yields

\(C_{M_{0,N}}=-\frac{2\tan \theta _cC_{p,t2}}{\pi R^{2}_{b}R_b } \int_{0}^{x_L}{[\int_{0}^{\pi }{(\cos ^2\alpha \sin ^2\theta _c\cos \beta +2\sin \alpha \cos \alpha \sin \theta _c\cos \theta _c\cos ^2\beta +\sin ^2\alpha \cos ^2\theta _c\cos ^3\beta )} ]}\).

Integrating gives us

\(C_{M_{0,N}}=-\frac{C_{p,t2}\sin 2\alpha }{R^{2}_{b}R_b } \tan \theta _c\sin \theta _c\cos \theta _c\frac{x^{3}_{L} }{3}\).

\(C_{M_{0,N}}=-\frac{C_{p,t2\sin 2\alpha \cos ^2\theta _c}}{3} \frac{x_L}{R_b}\).

Using equation (12.36) for the expression for \(C_N\), we can rewrite

\(C_N=\frac{C_{p,t2}}{2} \sin 2\alpha \cos ^2\theta _c\).

\(C_{M_{0,N}}=C_N(-\frac{2}{3}\frac{x_L}{R_b} )\).

Similarly,

\(C_{M_{0,A}}=-\frac{-2\tan^3 \theta _cC_{p,t2}}{\pi R^{2}_{b}R_b } \int_{0}^{x_L}{[\int_{0}^{\pi }{(\cos ^2\alpha \sin ^2\theta _c\cos \beta +2\sin \alpha \cos \alpha \sin \theta _c\cos \theta _c\cos ^2\beta +\sin ^2\alpha \cos ^2\theta _c\cos ^3\beta )} ]x^2dx}\).

Integrating yields

\(C_{M_{0,A}}=-\frac{C_{p,t2}\sin 2\alpha \sin ^2\theta _c}{3} \frac{x_L}{R_b}\).

Using equation (12.34) for the expression for \(C_A\), we can rewrite this as:

\(C_A=C_{p,t2}[\sin ^2\theta _c+\frac{1}{2}\sin ^2\alpha (1-3\sin ^2\theta _c) ]\).

\(C_{M_{0,A}}=C_A\frac{-\sin 2\alpha \sin^2\theta _c}{3[\sin ^2\theta _c+0.5\sin \alpha (1-3\sin ^2\theta _c)]} \frac{x_L}{R_b}\).

Following this division of the pitch moment into components, we can represent it as an effective net force acting at a “center of pressure,” as shown in Fig. 12.22 . The pitch moment may be represented by

\(M_0=M_{0.N}+M_{0.A}=-x_{cp}N-y_{cp}A\).

The two terms represent the effects of the normal force and of the axial force, respectively. In terms of the coefficients, we can write this as:

\(C_{M_0}=\frac{M_0}{q_\infty SR_b} =-C_N\frac{x_{cp}}{R_b} -C_A\frac{y_{cp}}{R_b}\).

Comparing the formulations presented in equations (12.48), (12.49), and (12.51), we find that

\(C_{M_{0,N}}=C_N(-\frac{2}{3}\frac{x_L}{R_b} )\).

\(C_{M_{0,A}}=C_A\frac{-\sin 2\alpha \sin^2\theta _c}{3[\sin ^2\theta _c+0.5\sin \alpha (1-3\sin ^2\theta _c)]} \frac{x_L}{R_b}\). \(C_{M_0}=\frac{M_0}{q_\infty SR_b} =-C_N\frac{x_{cp}}{R_b} -C_A\frac{y_{cp}}{R_b}\).

\(x_{cp}=\frac{2}{3} x_L\).

and

\(y_{cp}=\frac{\sin 2\alpha \sin ^2\theta _cx_L}{3[\sin ^2\theta _c+0.5\sin ^2\alpha (1-3\sin ^2\theta _c)]}\)

If the vehicle is to be statically stable, the center of pressure should be located such that the aerodynamic forces produce a restoring moment when the configuration is perturbed from its “stable orientation.” Therefore, if the vehicle pitches up (i.e., α increases), the net pitch moment should be negative (causing a nose-down moment), which decreases α.
The parameter

\(S.M.=\frac{x_{cp}-x_{cg}}{x_L}\).

is the static margin, which must be positive for uncontrolled vehicles. For high-performance, hypersonic vehicles, the static margin is usually 3% to 5% of the length of the vehicle. Note, however, as illustrated in Fig. 12.22 , both the axial force and the normal force contribute to the pitch moment.
Therefore, it is possible that the vehicle is statically stable when \(x_{cp} = x_{cg}\), if \(y_{cp}\) is below \(y_{cg}\) (as is the case in the sketch of Fig. 12.22 ). In this case, the axial force will produce the required restoring moment.
