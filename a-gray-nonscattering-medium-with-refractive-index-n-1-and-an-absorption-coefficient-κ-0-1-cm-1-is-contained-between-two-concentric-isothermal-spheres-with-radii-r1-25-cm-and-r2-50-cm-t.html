Guest Question.

A gray, nonscattering medium with refractive index n = 1 and an absorption coefficient \(κ = 0.1 cm^{-1}\) is contained between two concentric isothermal spheres with radii \(R_{1}\) = 25 cm and \(R_{2}\) =50 cm. The inner sphere is hot (\(T_{1}\)= 2000 K) and highly reflective (\(ε_{1}\) = 0.1); the outer sphere is a strong absorber (\(α_{2}\) = \(ε_{2}\) = 0.9), which must be kept relatively cool (\(T_{2}\) = 400 K). Assuming that conductive and convective heat transfer can be neglected as compared with radiation, determine the necessary cooling rate.

Admin
Solution

We have the situation of one-dimensional radiative equilibrium between concentric spheres, and equation (13.72) \(\tau ^{2}q(\tau )=(J_{1}-J_{2})\tau ^{2}_{1}\Psi (\tau )+\frac{Q^{\prime \prime \prime }}{\kappa }\left[\frac{\tau ^{3}}{3} – \tau ^{2}_{1}\Psi _{s}(\tau )\right]\) applies with \(Q^{\prime \prime \prime }\) = 0. \(q_{1}=0.0930\sigma (T^{4}_{1}-T^{4}_{2})\)

\(= 0.0930\times 5.670\times 10^{-12}(2000^{4}-400^{4})W/cm^{2}=8.42W/cm^{2}\) .
