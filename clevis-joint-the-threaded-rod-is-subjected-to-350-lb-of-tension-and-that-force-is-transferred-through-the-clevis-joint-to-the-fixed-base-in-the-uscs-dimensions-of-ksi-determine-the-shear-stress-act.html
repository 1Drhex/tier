Guest Question.

Clevis Joint The threaded rod is subjected to 350 lb of tension, and that force is transferred through the clevis joint to the fixed base. In the USCS dimensions of ksi, determine the shear stress acting within the 38in. diameter hinge pin. (See Figure 5.19.)

The threaded rod and the bolts attached to the fixed base are loaded along their length in tension. The clevis joint’s hinge pin carries forces perpendicular to its length, and it is loaded in shear. To determine the magnitude of the shear force, draw free body diagrams of the clevis joint to show how forces are carried within it. (See Figure 5.20.)

 

Admin
Solution

The 350 lb force is transmitted from the pin to the base through two shear planes, and V =175 lb. The pin’s cross-sectional area is

 

\(\begin{aligned}A &=\frac{\pi(0.375 in .)^{2}}{4} \leftarrow\left[A=\frac{\pi d^{2}}{4}\right] \\&=0.1104 in ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

The shear stress is

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tau &=\frac{175 lb }{0.1104 in ^{2}} \leftarrow\left[\tau=\frac{V}{A}\right] \\&=1.584 \times 10^{3} \frac{ lb }{ in ^{2}} \\&=1.584 \times 10^{3} psi\end{aligned}\)

 

where we have used the abbreviation psi to denote \(lb / in ^{2}\). To place the numerical value in conventional form, we convert the stress to the dimension ksi by using Table 5.1:

 

Table 5.1 Conversion Factors between USCS and SI Units for Stress

103 1 6.895 × 106 6.895 × 103 6.895
1450 × 10-4 1.450 × 10-7 1 10-3 10-6
0.1450 1.450 × 10-4 103 1 10-3

\(\begin{aligned}\tau &=\left(1.584 \times 10^{3} psi \right)\left(10^{-3} \frac{ ksi }{ psi }\right) \\&=1.584( psi )\left(\frac{ ksi }{ psi }\right) \\&=1.584 ksi\end{aligned}\)

Discussion

The forces acting on the clevis joint tend to cleave or cut through the pin at two locations. The connection is loaded in double shear at the intensity of 1.584 ksi, which again is much less than the yield strength of steel, a common material for hinge pins.

τ = 1.584 ksi
