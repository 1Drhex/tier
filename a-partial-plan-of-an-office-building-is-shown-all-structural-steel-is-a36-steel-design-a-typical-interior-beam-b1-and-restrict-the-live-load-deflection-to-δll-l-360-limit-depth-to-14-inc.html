Guest Question.

A partial plan of an office building is shown. All structural steel is A36 steel. Design a typical interior beam B1, and restrict the live load deflection to \(\Delta_{ LL } \) < L/360. Limit depth to 14 inches. Also design the spandrel beam, restricting its total deflection to \(\Delta_{ LL } \) < L/240. Limit depth to 18 inches.

Loads:

Concrete floor: 150 pcf

1″ finish wood floor: 2.5 psf

Suspended fire-resistant ceiling: 3.0 psf

LL: 70 psf*

Curtain wall: 400 #/ft.

*Occupancy for office building with moveable partitions

A36 steel:

\(F_{ b }\) = 22 ksi

\(F_{ v }\) = 14.5 ksi

E = 29 × 10³ ksi

Admin
Solution

Beam B1 Design

5″ concrete floor: 62.5 psf

1″ finish wood floor: 2.5 psf

Suspended ceiling: 3.0 psf

\(\space\) \(_____\)

Total DL 68 psf

Total LL = 70 psf × 8′ (tributary width) = 560 #/ft.

Total DL + LL = 138 psf × 8′ (trib. width) = 1,104 #/ft.

\(M_{\max }=\frac{\omega L^{2}}{8}=\frac{1.1 k / ft \cdot\left(28^{\prime}\right)^{2}}{8}=108 k – ft.\)

 

\(S_{\text {required }}=\frac{M_{\max }}{F_{ b }}=\frac{108 k – ft . \times 12 in . / ft. }{22 ksi }=59 in .^{3}\)

Try W14 ×43 (S = 62.7 in.³; I = 428 in.\(^{4} \) ). No check is necessary for \(S_{\text {add. }}\)

Deflection

\(\Delta_{\text {allow }_{( LL )}}=\frac{L}{360}=\frac{28^{\prime} \times 12 in . / ft .}{22 ksi }=0.93^{\prime \prime}\)

 

\(\Delta_{\text {actual }_{( LL )}}=\frac{5 \omega_{ LL } L^{4}}{384 EI }=\frac{5(0.56 k / ft .)\left(28^{\prime}\right)\left(1,728 in \cdot^{3} / ft .^{3}\right)}{384\left(29 \times 10^{3} ksi \right)\left(428 in .^{4}\right)}=0.62^{\prime \prime}\)

 

\(\Delta_{\text {actual }_{( LL )}}=0.62^{\prime \prime}<\Delta_{\text {allow }_{( LL )}}=0.93^{\prime \prime} \therefore OK \)

Use W14 × 43.

Beam Reaction onto Spandrel Beam

\(R=\frac{\omega_{\text {total }} L}{2}=\frac{(1.1+0.043 k / ft .)\left(28^{\prime}\right)}{2}=16.1 k\)

 

Spandrel Design

Curtain wall = 400 plf

Span length =32′

\(\Delta_{\text {allow }_{( D + L )}}=\frac{L}{240}=\frac{32^{\prime} \times 12 in . / ft .}{240}=1.6^{\prime \prime}\)

 

\(M_{\max }\) = 309 k-ft.

\(S_{\text {required }}=\frac{309 k \text { -ft. } \times 12 \text { in. } / ft .}{22 ksi }=168.5 in .{ }^{3}\)

Try W18 × 97 \(\left(S_{x}=188 \text { in. }^{3} ; I_{x}=1,750 \text { in. }^{4}\right) \).

 

\(M_{\text {add. }}=\frac{0.097 k / ft \cdot\left(32^{\prime}\right)^{2}}{8}=12.4 k – ft.\)

 

\(S_{\text {add. }}=\frac{12.4 k – ft . \times 12 in . / ft .}{22 ksi }=6.8 in .^{3}\)

 

\(S_{\text {total }}=168.5+6.8=175.3 \text { in. }^{3}<188 \text { in. }^{3} \therefore \text { OK }\)

 

Deflection

\(\Delta_{\text {total }_{( DL + LL )}}=\frac{5 \omega L^{4}}{384 E I}+\frac{P L^{3}}{20.1 E I}\)

 

\(\Delta_{\text {total }_{( DL + LL )}}=\frac{5(.5)\left(32^{\prime}\right)^{4}(1,728)}{384\left(29 \times 10^{3}\right)(1,750)}+\frac{16.1\left(32^{\prime}\right)^{3}(1,728)}{20.1\left(29 \times 10^{3}\right)(1,750)}\)

 

\(\Delta_{\text {actual }}=0.23^{\prime \prime}+0.91^{\prime \prime}=1.14^{\prime \prime}<\frac{L}{240}=1.6^{\prime \prime} \therefore OK\)

 

Shear Check

\(f_{ vaverage }=\frac{V}{t_{ w } h}=\frac{30.6 k +1.6 k }{\left(0.535^{\prime \prime}\right)\left(18.59^{\prime \prime}\right)}=3.2 ksi <14.5 ksi \therefore OK \)

Use W18 × 97.
