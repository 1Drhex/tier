Guest Question.

A hollow structural steel flexural member (Figure P15.75b) is subjected to the load shown in Figure P15.75a. The yield strength of the steel is \(\sigma_{Y}\) = 320 MPa.
(a) Determine the factors of safety predicted at points H and K by the maximum-shear-stress theory of failure.
(b) Determine the Mises equivalent stresses at points H and K.
(c) Determine the factors of safety at points H and K predicted by the maximum-distortion-energy theory.

Admin
Solution

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

\(\begin{array}{ccccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { outer rectangle } & 195,312,500 & 0.000 & 0.000 & 195,312,500 \\\text { inner rectangle } & -143,077,428 & 0.000 & 0.000 & -143,077,428 \\\hline & {\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( mm ^{4}\right)=} & 52,235,072\end{array}\)

Bending stress at point H:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{y} &=\frac{(225 kN – m )(75 mm )(1,000 N / kN )(1,000 mm / m )}{52,235,072 mm ^{4}} \\&=323.059 MPa \quad \text { (tension, by inspection) }\end{aligned}\)

Transverse and horizontal shear stress at point H:

\(\begin{aligned}Q &=(150 mm )(8 mm )(121 mm )+2(8 mm )(42 mm )(96 mm ) \\&=209,712 mm ^{3} \\\tau &=\frac{V Q}{I t}=\frac{(225 kN )\left(209,712 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(52,235,072 mm ^{4}\right)(2)(8 mm )}=56.458 MPa\end{aligned}\)

(Sense to be determined by inspection)

Principal stress calculations for point H:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{(0 MPa )+(232.059 MPa )}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(0 MPa )-(232.059 MPa )}{2}\right)^{2}+(56.458 MPa )^{2}} \\&=151.529 MPa \pm 171.112 MPa\end{aligned}\)

therefore, \(\sigma_{p 1}=332.641 MPa \text { and } \quad \sigma_{p 2}=-9.582 MPa\)

Bending stress at point K:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{y} &=\frac{(225 kN – m )(50.0 mm )(1,000 N / kN )(1,000 mm / m )}{52,235,072 mm ^{4}} \\&=215.373 MPa \quad \text { (compression, by inspection) }\end{aligned}\)

Transverse and horizontal shear stress at point K:

\(\begin{aligned}Q &=(150 mm )(8 mm )(121 mm )+2(8 mm )(67 mm )(83.5 mm ) \\&=234,712 mm ^{3} \\\tau &=\frac{V Q}{I t}=\frac{(225 kN )\left(234,712 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(52,235,072 mm ^{4}\right)(2)(8 mm )}=63.188 MPa\end{aligned}\)

(Sense to be determined by inspection)

Principal stress calculations for point K:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{(0 MPa )+(-215.373 MPa )}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(0 MPa )-(-215.373 MPa )}{2}\right)^{2}+(63.188 MPa )^{2}} \\&=-107.686 MPa \pm 124.856 MPa\end{aligned}\)

therefore, \(\sigma_{p 1}=17.170 MPa\) and \(\sigma_{p 2}=-232.542 MPa\)

 

(a) Maximum-Shear-Stress Theory
Element H:

\(\left|\sigma_{p 1}-\sigma_{p 2}\right|=|332.641 MPa -(-9.582 MPa )|=342.223 MPa\)

The factor of safety associated with this state of stress is:

\(FS _{H}=\frac{320 MPa }{342.223 MPa }=0.935\)

Element K:

\(\left|\sigma_{p 1}-\sigma_{p 2}\right|=|17.170 MPa -(-232.542 MPa )|=249.712 MPa\)

The factor of safety associated with this state of stress is:

\(FS _{K}=\frac{320 MPa }{249.712 MPa }=1.281\)

 

(b) Mises equivalent stresses at points H and K:
Element H:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{M, H} &=\left[\sigma_{p 1}^{2}-\sigma_{p 1} \sigma_{p 2}+\sigma_{p 2}^{2}\right]^{1 / 2} \\&=\left[(332.641 MPa )^{2}-(332.641 MPa )(-9.582 MPa )+(-9.582 MPa )^{2}\right]^{1 / 2} \\&=337.534 MPa =338 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Element K:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{M, K} &=\left[\sigma_{p 1}^{2}-\sigma_{p 1} \sigma_{p 2}+\sigma_{p 2}^{2}\right]^{1 / 2} \\&=\left[(17.170 MPa )^{2}-(17.170 MPa )(-232.542 MPa )+(-232.542 MPa )^{2}\right]^{1 / 2} \\&=241.586 MPa =242 MPa\end{aligned}\)

 

(c) Maximum-Distortion-Energy Theory:
The factor of safety for the maximum-distortion-energy theory can be calculated from the Mises equivalent stress:

\(\begin{aligned}& FS _{H}=\frac{320 MPa }{337.534 MPa }=0.948 \\& FS _{K}=\frac{320 MPa }{241.586 MPa }=1.325\end{aligned}\)
