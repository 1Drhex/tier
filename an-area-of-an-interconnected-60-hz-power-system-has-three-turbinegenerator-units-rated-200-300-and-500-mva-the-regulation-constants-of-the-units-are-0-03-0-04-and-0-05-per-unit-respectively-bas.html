Guest Question.

An area of an interconnected 60-Hz power system has three turbinegenerator units rated 200, 300, and 500 MVA. The regulation constants of the units are 0.03, 0.04, and 0.05 per unit, respectively, based on their ratings. Each unit is initially operating at one-half its own rating when the load suddenly decreases by 150 MW. Determine (a) the unit area frequency response characteristic Î² on a 100-MVA base, (b) the steady-state increase in area frequency, and (c) the MW decrease in mechanical power output of each turbine. Assume that the reference power setting of each turbine-governor remains constant. Neglect losses and the dependence of load on frequency.

Admin
Solution

(a) Converting the regulation constants to a 100 MVA system base:

\(\begin{gathered}R_{1 \text { new }}=0.03\left(\frac{100}{200}\right)=0.015 \\R_{2 \text { new }}=0.04\left(\frac{100}{300}\right)=0.0133 \\R_{3 \text { new }}=0.05\left(\frac{100}{500}\right)=0.01\end{gathered}\)

Using (12.2.3):

\(\beta=\left(\frac{1}{0.015}+\frac{1}{0.0133}+\frac{1}{0.01}\right)=\underline{\underline{241.86}} \text { per unit }\)

 

(b) Using (12.2.4) with \(\Delta P_{r e f}=0 \text { and } \Delta P_{m}=\frac{-150}{100} p . u .=-1.5 p . u \text {. }\)

\(-1.5=-241.86 \Delta f\)

 

\(\Delta f=\frac{-1.5}{-241.86} p . u .=6.20 \times 10^{-3} \text { per unit }=\left(6.20 \times 10^{-3}\right)(60)=\underline{\underline{0.372}} Hz\)

 

(c) Using (12.2.1) with \(\Delta P_{r e f}=0\):

\(\begin{aligned}&\Delta P_{m 1}=-\left(\frac{1}{0.015}\right)\left(6.20 \times 10^{-3}\right)=-0.4133 \text { per unit }=\underline{\underline{-41.33}} MW \\&\Delta P_{m 2}=-\left(\frac{1}{0.0133}\right)\left(6.20 \times 10^{-3}\right)=-0.4662 \text { per unit }=\underline{\underline{-46.62}} MW \\&\Delta P_{m 3}=-\left(\frac{1}{0.01}\right)\left(6.20 \times 10^{-3}\right)=-0.62 \text { per unit }=\underline{\underline{-62}} MW\end{aligned}\)
