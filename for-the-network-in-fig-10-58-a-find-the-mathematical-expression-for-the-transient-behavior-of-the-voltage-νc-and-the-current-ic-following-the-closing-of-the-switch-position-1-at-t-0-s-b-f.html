Guest Question.

For the network in Fig. 10.58:

a. Find the mathematical expression for the transient behavior of the voltage \(ν_C\) and the current \(i_C\) following the closing of the switch (position 1 at t = 0 s).

b. Find the mathematical expression for the voltage \(ν_C\) and the current \(i_C\) as a function of time if the switch is thrown into position 2 at t = 9 ms.

c. Draw the resultant waveforms of parts (a) and (b) on the same time axis.

Admin
Solution

a. Applying Thévenin’s theorem to the 0.2 μF capacitor, we obtain Fig. 10.59. We have

\(R_{Th}=R_1\parallel R_2+R_3=\frac{(60 kΩ)(30 kΩ)}{90 kΩ} + 10 kΩ\\[0.5cm] = 20 kΩ + 10 kΩ = 30 kΩ\\[0.5cm] E_{Th }=\frac{R_2E}{R_2+R_1}=\frac{(30 kΩ)(21 V)}{30 kΩ + 60 kΩ}=\frac{1}{3}(21 V) = 7 V \)

The resultant Thévenin equivalent circuit with the capacitor replaced is shown in Fig. 10.60.

Using Eq. (10.21) with \(V_f = E_{Th} and V_t = 0 V\), we find that

\(\nu_C=V_f+(V_i-V_f)e^{-t/\tau}\\[0.5cm] becomes\quad \nu_C=E_{Th}+(0V-E_{Th})e^{-t/\tau}\\[0.5cm] or\quad \nu_C=E_{Th}(1-e^{-t/\tau})\\[0.5cm] with\quad \tau=RC = (30 kΩ)(0.2 \mu F) = 6 ms\\[0.5cm] Therefore,\quad \nu_C=7V(1-e^{-t/6ms})\)

For the current \(i_C\):

\(i_C=\frac{E_{Th}}{R}e^{-t/RC}=\frac{7V}{30 kΩ}e^{-t/6ms}=0.23 mAe^{-t/6ms} \)

 

b. At t = 9 ms,

\(\nu_C=E_{Th}(1-e^{-t/\tau})=7V(1-e^{-(9ms/6ms)})\\[0.5cm] =(7V)(1-e^{-1.5})=(7 V)(1 – 0.223)\\[0.5cm] =(7 V)(0.777) = 5.44 V\)

 

and \(i_C=\frac{E_{Th}}{R}e^{-t/\tau}=0.23 mAe^{-1.5} =(0.23 \times 10^{-3})(0.233) =0.052 \times 10^{-3}=0.05 mA\)

Using Eq. (10.21) with \(V_f = 0 V and V_i = 5.44 V\), we find that

\(\nu_C=V_f+(V_i-V_f)e^{-t/\tau^{\prime}}\)

 

becomes \(\nu_C=0V+(5.44V-0V)e^{-t/\tau^{\prime}}=5.44 Ve^{-t/\tau^{\prime}}\\[0.5cm] with\quad \tau^{\prime}=R_4C = (10 kΩ)(0.2 \mu F) = 2 ms\\[0.5cm] and\quad \nu_C=5.44 Ve^{-t/2ms}\)

 

By Eq. (10.19),

\(i_C=\frac{E}{R}e^{-t/\tau} \) (10.19)

 

\(I_i=\frac{5.44V}{10 kΩ}=0.54 mA\\[0.5cm] and\quad i_C=I_ie^{-t/\tau} =0.54mAe^{-t/2ms}\)

 

c. See Fig. 10.61.
