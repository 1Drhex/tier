Guest Question.

The present drug capsule consists of a 0.652-cm-diameter spherical bead (radius of 0.326 cm) containing a uniform initial concentration of \( 68.9 mg / cm ^{3} \) Dramamine. (a) What is the residual concentration of Dramamine at the center of the spherical bead after 48 h ? (b) Consider now that the capsule is now a cube 0.652 cm on a side. Recalculate part (a) above. (c) Consider that the capsule is now a cylindrical tablet of diameter 0.652 cm and thickness 0:3 cm. Recalculate part (a) above. The diffusion coefficient of Dramamine (species A) in the gel matrix (species B) is \( 3 \times 10^{-7} cm ^{2} / s \) at a body temperature of \( 37^{\circ} C \). The three capsule configurations are presented in Figure.

Admin
Solution

This problem is readily solved using the charts given in Appendix F.
(a) For the spherical capsule, calculate the relative time \( \left(X_{D}\right) \), relative position (n), and relative resistance (m) based on the spherical coordinate system:

 

\( X_{D} = \frac{D_{A B} t}{R^{2}} = \frac{\left(3 \times 10^{-7} \frac{ cm ^{2}}{ s }\right)\left(48 h \frac{3600 s }{1 h }\right)}{(0.326 cm )^{2}} = 0.488 \)

 

\( n = \frac{r}{R} = \frac{0 cm }{0.326 cm } = 0(\text { center of sphere }) \)

 

\( m = \frac{D_{A B}}{k_{c} R} \approx 0 \)

 

From Appendix F, Figure, the value for Y, which in this case is the unaccomplished concentration change at the center of the spherical bead, is about 0.018. We can now calculate \( c_{A} : \)

 

\( Y = 0.018 = \frac{c_{A s} – c_{A}}{c_{A s} – c_{A o}} = \frac{0 – c_{A}}{0 – 68.9 mg / cm ^{3}} \)

 

The residual Dramamine concentration at the center of the bead after 48 h \( \left(c_{A}\right) \) is \( 1.24 mg / cm ^{3}. \)
(b) For the cube-shaped capsule, the distance from the midpoint of the cube to any of the six faces is 0.652 cm/2, or 0.326 cm. The relative time \( X_{D} \) is now defined as

 

\( X_{D} = \frac{D_{A B} t}{x_{1}^{2}} = \frac{\left(3 \times 10^{-7} \frac{ cm ^{2}}{ s }\right)\left(48 h \frac{3600 s }{1 h }\right)}{(0.326 cm )^{2}} = 0.488 \)

 

Values for n and m are unchanged, with n = 0 and m = 0. As all of the faces of the cube are of equal dimension, let

\( Y = Y_{a} Y_{b} Y_{c} = Y_{a}^{3} \)

 

From the Appendix F, Figure, given \( X_{D} = 0.488, m = 0 \) and \( n = 0, Y_{a} \) is 0.4 for a flat plate of semi-thickness \( x_{1} = a = 0.326 cm. \) Extending this value to a three-dimensional cube using the above relationship, we have

 

\( Y = Y_{a}^{3} = (0.4)^{3} = 0.064 \)

 

Finally,

\( Y = 0.064 = \frac{c_{A s} – c_{A}}{c_{A s} – c_{A o}} = \frac{0 – c_{A}}{0 – 68.9 mg / cm ^{3}} \)

 

with \( C_{A} = 4.41 mg / cm ^{3} \) after 48 h.
(c) For a cylindrical capsule with exposed ends, R = 0.326 cm for the radial coordinate, and \( x_{1} = a = 0.15 cm \) or the axial coordinate. The relative times are

 

\( X_{D} = \frac{D_{A B} t}{R^{2}} = \frac{\left(3.0 \times 10^{-7} \frac{ cm ^{2}}{ s }\right)\left(48 h \frac{3600 s }{1 h }\right)}{(0.326 cm )^{2}} = 0.488 \)

 

for the cylindrical dimension and

\( X_{D} = \frac{D_{A B} t}{x_{1}^{2}} = \frac{\left(3.0 \times 10^{-7} \frac{ cm ^{2}}{ s }\right)\left(48 h \frac{3600 s }{1 h }\right)}{(0.15 cm )^{2}} = 2.30 \)

 

for the axial dimension. Values for n and m are unchanged, with n = 0 and m = 0. From Appendix F, Figure F.1 and Figure F.2, respectively, \( Y_{\text {cylinder }} = 0.1 \) for the cylindrical dimension and \( Y_{a} = 0.006 \) for the axial dimension. Therefore,

 

\( Y = Y_{\text {cylinder }} Y_{a} = (0.1)(0.006) = 0.0006 \)

 

and finally

\( Y = 0.006 = \frac{c_{A s} – c_{A}}{c_{A s} – c_{A o}} = \frac{0 – c_{A}}{0 – 68.9 mg / cm ^{3}} \)

 

with \( c_{A} = 0.413 mg / cm ^{3} \) after 48 h. As \( Y_{a} \ll Y_{\text {cylinder }} \) the flux directed out of the exposed ends of the cylindrical tablet along the axial dimension dominates.
The above calculations assume that convective mass-transfer resistances associated with external fluid flow over the surface of the capsule are negligible. The drug release for unsteady-state diffusion and convection in series.
