Guest Question.

A heat pump using R134a is proposed for heating a home that requires a maximum heating load of
300 kW. The evaporator operates at -12째C and the condenser at 900 kPa. Assume an ideal cycle.
(a) Determine the COP.
(b) Determine the cost of electricity at $0.08/kWh.
(c) Compare the R134a system with the cost of operating a furnace using natural gas at $0.45/therm if there are 100 000 kJ/therm of natural gas.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { (a) The } T-s \text { diagram (Fig. 10.7) is sketched for the ideal heat pump. From Appendix D, we find } h_{1}=240.2 kJ / kg , s_{1}=s_{2}= \) \( 0.9267 kJ / kg \cdot K \text {, and } h_{3}=h_{4}=99.56 kJ / kg \text {. Interpolating, the results are } \)

\( h_{2}=\left(\frac{0.9267-0.9217}{0.9566-0.9217}\right)(282.3-271.2)+271.2=272.8 kJ / kg \).

The heat rejected by the condenser is

\( Q_{ c }=\dot{m}\left(h_{2}-h_{3}\right) \quad 300=\dot{m}(272.8-99.56) \).

\( \text { This gives the refrigerant mass flux as } \dot{m}=1.732 kg / s \text {. The required power by the compressor is then } \dot{W}_{\text {in }}=\dot{m}\left(h_{2}-h_{1}\right)= \) (1.732)(272.8 – 240.2) = 56.5 kW.
This results in a coefficient of performance of

\( COP _{H P}=\frac{\dot{Q}_{C}}{\dot{W}_{\text {in }}}=\frac{300}{56.5}=5.31 \).

(b) The cost of electricity (56.5 kW)($0.08/kWh) = $4.52/h.
(c) Assuming the furnace to be ideal, that is, it converts all of the energy of the gas into usable heat, we have

\( \text { Cost of gas }=\left[\frac{(300)(3600)}{100000}\right](0.45)=\$ 4.86 / h \).
