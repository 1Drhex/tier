Guest Question.

A water stream flowing at 100 L/min. at \(25 ^{◦}C\) contaminated with acetone at 5 g/L must be reduced to an acetone concentration of 2 g/L. TCE at \(25^{ ◦}C\) is available for this process. A technically feasible design is to be proposed following the design procedure previously outlined.

Admin
Solution

1- The process stream (water) is specified and the feed and desired outlet concentrations given. Given our previous experience with TCE–water systems, the water will be the dispersed phase (II) and TCE the continuous phase (I), and a mixed–mixed tank type contactor will be designed.

2- The load is calculated with Eq. (4.5.1) as

\(m_{load}=(q^I C^I_A-q^I_FC_{AF}^I)=-(q^{II}C^{II}_A-q^{II}_FC_{AF}^{II})\) (4.5.1)

\(m_{load }= (−100 L/min)(2 − 5 g/L) = 300 g/min. \)

Note the load is positive as Phase I is to be enriched with acetone.

3- The solvent TCE is available and sufficient thermodynamic data are available (Figure 4.2). Furthermore, the concentrations are low enough such that M =2. As noted in , TCE has environmental and health effects such that it should be handled carefully. However, TCE is largely insoluble in water and has a stronger affinity for acetone, both being advantageous for separation.

4- The minimum TCE flow rate is given by the second of Eqs. (4.5.2) for a tank-type contactor as

\(q_{min}^{I}=q^{II}\left(\frac{C_{AF}^{II}-C_{A}^{II}}{MC_{A}^{II}-C_{AF}^{I}} \right) \) (4.5.2)

\(q_{min}^{I}=(100 L/min)\left(\frac{5-2}{2\times 2-0} g/L\right)\) =75L/min

We will assume a 90% stage efficiency, which yields the estimated flow rate of TCE for the technically feasible design to be

\(q^{I}=\frac{M^{-1}q^{II}}{\chi (\frac{M^{-1}C_{AF}^{I}-C_{AF}^{II}}{C_{A}^{II}-C_{AF}^{II}} )}\)\(=\frac{2^{-1}\times 100 L/min}{[0.9(\frac{0\times 2^{-1}-5}{2-5} )-1]}\)=100L/min

Note that this choice results in λ=\( q^{II}/q{I} \)= 1, which is appropriate for the mixed–mixed mass contactor under design.

5- The mass transfer coefficient is, in the absence of other experimental data, taken to be \(K_{m} ≈ 3 × 10^{−5}\) m/s. This estimate should be checked and its uncertainty recognized in the final recommendation.

6- From the Level I balance, we find the exit concentration of acetone in the TCE to be \(C^{I}_{A}\)= 3 g/L. The area is determined from Eq. (4.5.5), with the value of

\(m_{Load}=K_{m}a\left\langle\Delta C_{A}^{I-II} \right\rangle \) (4.5.5)

\(\left\langle\Delta C^{I-II} \right\rangle =-(C_{A}^{I}-MC_{A}^{II})\)\(=-(3-2\times 2g/L)\)=1g/L

\(a=\frac{m_{Load}}{\left\langle\Delta C^{I-II} \right\rangle }\)\(=\frac{(300 g/min)(1/60 min/s)}{(3×10^{-5} m/s)(1 g/L)(10^{3} L/m^{3})}\)\(=167 m^{2}.\)

7- The volume of the tank will depend on how the area is created. A reasonable bubble size is 2 mm. Therefore the volume of the dispersed phase in the tank can be calculated from the definition of the sauter mean drop size as

\(V^{II}=\frac{1}{6}(167 m^{2})(2 × 10^{−3} m) \)\(= 0.056 m^{3} \)= 56 L.

Because the residence times in the tank are equal, the volume of the continuous phase is given by

\(\frac{V^{I}}{q^{I}}=\frac{V^{II}}{q^{II}} \therefore V^{I}\)\(=V^{I}\frac{q^{I}}{q^{II}}=56L.\)

Hence, the total volume of the tank is 112 L, with a residence time of 33 s. A reasonable geometry would be a tank of 0.78 m in diameter and height.
To complete this design, the power required for generating the specified area must be specified and, based on the mixing in the tank, a better estimate of the mass transfer coefficient calculated. These topics will be covered in Part II of this text.
