Guest Question.

In a piston-cylinder arrangement the steam at 1.0 MPa, 80% dryness fraction, and 0.05 \(\textbf{m}^{3}\) volume is heated to increase its volume to 0.2 \(\textbf{m}^{3}\). Determine the heat added.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{Given;}\\\quad\text{Initial states, 1: }P_{1} = 1.0 MPa, V_{1} = 0.05 m^{3}, x_{1} = 0.80\\\quad\text{Final state, 2: }V_{2} = 0.2 m^{3}, P_{2} = 1 MPa\\\quad\text{Work done during constant pressure process, }W = P_{1}(V_{2} – V_{1})\\\begin{aligned}W&= 1000 (0.2 – 0.05)\\W&= 150 kJ\\\text{Mass of steam}&= \frac{V_{1}}{v_{1}}\\\text{From steam table at }P_{1} ; v_{f}&= 0.001127 m^{3}/kg\\v_{g}&= 0.19444 m^{3}/kg\\u_{f}&= 761.68 kJ/kg\\u_{fg}&= 1822 kJ/kg\\\text{so}\qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad v_{1}&= v_{f} + x_{1} v_{fg}\\v_{1}&= 0.15578 m^{3}/kg\\\text{Hence, mass of steam}&= \frac{0.05}{0.15578} = 0.32097 kg\\\text{Specific volume at final state}&= \frac{V_{2}}{\text{mass of steam}}\\v_{2}&= 0.62311 m^{3}/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Corresponding to this specific volume the final state is to be located for getting the internal energy at final state at 1 MPa}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad v_{2} > v_{\text{g1 MPa}}\\\text{hence state lies in superheated region, from the steam table by interpolation we get temperature as;}\\\quad\text{State lies between temperature of 1000°C and 1100°C}\\\text{so exact temperature at final state}\\\begin{aligned}&= 1000 + \frac{100 × (0.62311 – 0.5871)}{(0.6335 – 0.5871)}\\&= 1077.61°C\\\text{Thus internal energy at final state,} & 1 MPa, 1077.61°C;\\u_{2}&= 4209.6 kJ/kg\\\text{Internal energy at initial state,}\qquad& \\u_{1}&= u_{f} + x_{1} . u_{fg} = 761.68 + 0.8 × 1822\\u_{1}&= 2219.28 kJ/kg\\\text{From first law of thermodynamics;} &\\Q – W&= ΔU\\Q&= (U_{2} – U_{1}) + W\\&= m(u_{2} – u_{1}) + W\\&= 0.32097 (4209.6 – 2219.28) + 150\\Q&= 788.83 kJ\\\text{Heat added}&= \textbf{788.83 kJ}\end{aligned}\)
