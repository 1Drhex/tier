Guest Question.

The switch is closed at t = 0 in the circuit of Fig. 3.17. Determine I(t) for t ≥ 0.

Admin
Solution

4.17. Before closing the switch at t = 0, the circuit is like the one shown in Fig. 4.17.2. As can be seen, the inductor is modeled by an open circuit branch, since the circuit is in its steady state condition.

\(I_{L}(0^{-})\)…….(1)

After the switching operation, the circuit is changed to the one illustrated in Fig. 4.17.3. Due to current continuity of the inductor:

\(I_{L}(0^{+})=I_{L}(0^{-})=\) 5A …….(2)

To calculate the Thevenin resistance seen by the inductor, we need to turn off the independent current source, as is shown in Fig. 4.17.4.

\(R_{Th}=R_{in}\)=100Ω…….(3)

The time constant of the circuit:

\(τ=\frac{L}{R_{Th}}=\frac{\frac{1}{10}}{100}=\frac{1}{1000}\) sec …….(4)

Figure 4.17.5 shows how to calculate the final current of the inductor. As can be seen, the final current will be zero, since the whole current will pass through the short-circuit branch.

\(I_{L}(∞)\) = 0 A…….(5)

Using the general form of the current of an inductive first-order circuit with DC power supply:

\(I_{L}(t)=I_{L}(∞)+(I_{L}(0^{+})-I_{L}(∞))e^{-\frac{t}{τ}}= 0+(5-0)e^{-\frac{-\frac{t}{1}}{1000}}=5e^{-1000t}\)…….(6)

Now, by applying KCL in the indicated node of the circuit of Fig. 4.17.3, we have:

\(-5+I(t)+I_{L}(t)\Rightarrow 5-I_{L}(t)=5-5e^{-1000t}=5(1-e^{-1000t} )A\)
