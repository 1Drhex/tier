Guest Question.

Plot to scale the equilibrium composition of nitrogen at 10 kPa over the temperature range 5000 K to 15 000 K, assuming that \(N _{2}, N , N ^{+}, \text {and } e^{-}\) are present. For the ionization reaction \(N \Leftrightarrow N ^{+}+e^{-}\), the ionization equilibrium constant K has been calculated from spectroscopic data as

\(\begin{array}{ccccc}T[ K ] & 10000 & 12000 & 14000 & 16000 \\100 K & 0.0626 & 1.51 & 15.1 & 92\end{array}\)
Admin
Solution

1) \(N _{2} \Leftrightarrow 2 N\) 2) \(N \Leftrightarrow N ^{+}+ e ^{-}\)

change -a +2a change -b +b +b

Equil. Comp.: \(n _{ N 2}=1- a , \quad n _{ N }=2 a – b , \quad n _{ N^{ +}}= b , \quad n _{ e ^{-}}= b\)

EQ1: \(K _{1}=\frac{ y _{ N }^{2}}{ y _{ N 2}}\left(\frac{ P }{ P ^{0}}\right)=\frac{(2 a – b )^{2}}{(1- a )(1+ a + b )}\left(\frac{ P }{ P ^{0}}\right)\)

EQ2: \(K _{2}=\frac{ y _{ N^{ +}} y _{ e ^{-}}}{ y _{ N }}\left(\frac{ P }{ P ^{0}}\right)=\frac{ b ^{2}}{(2 a – b )(1+ a + b )}\left(\frac{ P }{ P ^{0}}\right)\)

For T < 10 000 K: b ~ 0 so neglect EQ2: ⇒ \(K _{1}=\frac{4 a ^{2}}{\left(1- a ^{2}\right)}\left(\frac{10}{100}\right)\)

To extrapolate \(K _{1}\) above 6000 K: \(\ln K _{1} \approx 16.845-\frac{118260}{ T }\)

(from values at 5000 K & 6000 K)

\(\begin{array}{ccccc}T ( K ) & K _{1} & a & y _{ N } & y _{ N 2} \\5000 & 0.0011 & 0.0524 & 0.0996 & 0.9004 \\6000 & 0.0570 & 0.3532 & 0.5220 & 0.4780 \\7000 & 0.9519 & 0.8391 & 0.9125 & 0.0875 \\8000 & 7.866 & 0.9755 & 0.9876 & 0.0124 \\10000 & 151.26 & 0.9987 & 0.9993 & 0.0007\end{array}\)

For T > 10 000 K: a ≈ 1.0 ⇒ \(K _{2}=\frac{ b ^{2}}{(2- b )(2+ b )}\left(\frac{10}{100}\right)=\frac{ b ^{2}}{\left(4- b ^{2}\right)} 0.1\)

\(\begin{array}{ccccc}T ( K ) & K _{2} & b & y _{ N } & y _{ N ^{+}} \\10000 & 6.26 \times 10^{-4} & 0.1577 & 0.8538 & 0.0731 \\12000 & 1.51 \times 10^{-2} & 0.7244 & 0.4862 & 0.2659 \\ 14000 & 0.151 & 1.5512 & 0.1264 & 0.4368 \\ 16000 & 0.92 & 1.8994 & 0.0258 & 0.4871 \end{array}\)

Note that b ≈ 0 is not a very good approximation in the vicinity of 10 000 K. In this region, it would be better to solve the original set simultaneously for a & b. The answer would be approximately the same.
