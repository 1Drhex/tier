Guest Question.

The following data refer to the decay of \(^{222}Rn\):

Time / s 0 2.0 5.0 12.0 20.0
umoles of \(^{222}Rn\) 4.40 3.0 1.77 0.50 0.12

 

Determine the half-life of \(^{222}Rn \)and the rate constant for its decay

\((1/mu mol = 10^{-6} mol).\)
Admin
Solution

Two methods could be used to tackle this problem. Method 1: Since radioactive decay is first order with respect to the parent nuclide, use the integrated form of the rate equation and plot ln (moles of

\(^{222}Rn\) against time. Data to be plotted:

Time / s 0 2.0 5.0 12.0 20.0
In moles \(^{222}Rn\) -12.33 -12.70 -13.24 -14.51 -15.94

These data give the following plot:

From equation 15.36 the gradient of this graph gives the rate constant, k:

\(K= 0.180 day^{-1}\)

To find the half-life:

\(t_{\frac{1}{2}}=\frac{In}{K}=\frac{In2}{(0.180 day^{-1})}=3.85 days\)
