Social Security Income Calculations

An engineer has the option of retiring and collecting social security when he is 62, 66.5, or 70 years old. If he decides to start his social security income at age 62 or 66.5, he will only be entitled to a reduced amount rather than the entire amount he would receive if he retires at age 70. Table 4.1 contains the amounts the engineer would receive on a monthly basis for each of the three different starting ages.

Part A—If the engineer estimates he will live to be 85 years old, what would be the present worth for each of the social security age alternatives using an interest rate of 4%? Figures 4.9 through 4.11 are the cash flow diagrams for each of the social security options for 85 years.

Part B—What would be the present worth of the three options if the engineer only lives to 77 years old? Figures 4.12 through 4.14 are the cash flow diagrams for each of the social security options for 77 years.

TABLE 4.1
Social Security Monthly Payments at Ages 62, 66.5, and 70 Years

Age Monthly Income Number of Years to Age 85 Number of Years to Age 77
62 $1,799.00 23 15
66.5 $2,412.00 18.5 10.5
70 $3,151.00 15 7

Solution

SOLUTION TO PART A—SOCIAL SECURITY MONTHLY
WITHDRAWALS UNTIL AGE 85

First, solve for the total number of monthly periods and the monthly interest rate for age 62 until 85:

\(n =\frac{Number of compounding periods}{Year}\times Total number of years\)

 

\(n =\frac{12 periods}{Year}\times 23 years\)

 

= 276 periods

\(i = \frac{i_n}{m}\)

 

\(i = \frac{4 \%/ year }{12 periods/year}\)

 

= 0.33% per period

Second, calculate the present worth of the social security income for age 62 until 85 using Equation 4.6:

\(P_0 =A\left[\frac{(1 +i)^n -1}{i(1 +i)^n} \right]\) (4.6)

\(=\$1,799.00\left[\frac{(1 +0.0033)^{276} -1}{0.0033(1 +0.0033)^{276}} \right]\)

 

\(=\$1,799.00\left\lgroup\frac{1.482585}{0.008193} \right\rgroup\)

 

\(=\$ 1,799.00(180.9575)\)

 

\(= \$ 325,542.54 age 62 until 85\)

 

Third, calculate the total number of periods and the period interest rate for age 66.4 until 85:

\(n =\frac{Number of compounding periods}{Year}\times Total number of years\)

 

\(n =\frac{12 periods}{Year}\times 23 years\)

 

n = 222 period

\(i = \frac{i_n}{m}\)

 

\(i = \frac{4 \%/ year }{12 periods/year}\)

 

= 0.33% per period

Fourth, calculate the present worth of the social security income for age 66.5 until 85:

\(P_0 =A\left[\frac{(1 +i)^n -1}{i(1 +i)^n} \right]\)

 

\(=\$2,412.00\left[\frac{(1 +0.0033)^{222} -1}{0.0033(1 +0.0033)^{222}} \right]\)

 

\(=\$2,412.00\left\lgroup\frac{1.077975}{0.006857} \right\rgroup\)

 

\(= \$ 2,412.00(157.2080)\)

 

= $ 379,185.70 age 66.5 until 85

Fifth, calculate the total number of periods and the period interest rate for age 70 until 85:

\(n =\frac{Number of compounding periods}{Year}\times Total number of years\)

 

\( =\frac{12 periods}{Year}\times 15 years\)

 

=180 periods

\(i = \frac{i_n}{m}\)

 

\(i = \frac{4 \%/ year }{12 periods/year}\)

 

= 0.33% per period

Sixth, calculate the present worth of the social security income for age 70 until 85:

\(P_0 =A\left[\frac{(1 +i)^n -1}{i(1 +i)^n} \right]\)

 

\(=\$3,151.00\left[\frac{(1 +0.0033)^{180} -1}{0.0033(1 +0.0033)^{180}} \right]\)

 

\(=\$3,151.00\left\lgroup\frac{0.809448}{0.005971} \right\rgroup\)

 

\(= \$3,151.00(135.5632)\)

 

= $427,259.64 age 70 until 85
