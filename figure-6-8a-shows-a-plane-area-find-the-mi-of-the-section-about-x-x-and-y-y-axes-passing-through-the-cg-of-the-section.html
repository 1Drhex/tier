Guest Question.

Figure 6.8(a) shows a plane area. Find the MI of the section about x-x and y-y axes passing through the CG of the section.

Admin
Solution

(i) Shaded area = Big Δ – Small Δ shown in Fig. 6.8(a).

Height of big triangle,

\( \tan 60° = \frac{H}{17.5} \) \( \therefore H = 30.31 \) cm.

Height of small triangle,

\( \tan 60° = \frac{h}{12.5} \) \( \therefore h = 21.65 \) cm.

We should first find distance of \( \overline{y} \) from x-x axis.

\( \overline{y} = \frac{\bigl(\frac{1}{2} \times 35\times 30.31\times \frac{30.31}{3} \bigr) – \bigl(\frac{1}{2} \times 25\times 21.65\times \frac{21.65}{3} \bigr)}{\bigl(\frac{1}{2} \times 35\times 30.31\bigr) – \bigl(\frac{1}{2} \times 25\times 21.65\bigr)} \) .

\( \overline{y} = \frac{3406.05}{259.8} \) \( \therefore \overline{y} = 13.11 \) cm.

Shaded area \( A = \bigl(\frac{1}{2} \times 35\times 30.31\bigr) – \bigl(\frac{1}{2} \times 25\times 21.65\bigr) \) .

A = 259.8 \( cm^2 \) .

(ii) MI about y-y axis

Since the given figure is symmetric about y-axis, finding \( I_{yy} \) is easy. Consider the MI valued twice

\( I_{yy} = 2 \times \Bigl(\frac{30.31 \times 17.5^3}{12}\Bigr) -2 \times \Bigl(\frac{21.65 \times 12.5^3}{12}\Bigr) \).

\( I_{yy} = 27073.78 – 7047.53 \).

\( I_{yy} \)= 20026.25 \(cm^4 \) Ans.

(iii) MI about AB axis

First find MI about the baseline, say AB

\( I_{AB} = \frac{35×30.31^3}{12} – \frac{25 ×21.65^3}{12} \) .

\( I_{AB} \)= 60075.23 \( cm^4\) Ans.

By parallel axis theorem, we have

\( I_{AB} = I_{xx_G} + A \overline{y}^2\) (A = Shaded area)

\( \therefore I_{xx_G} = I_{AB}- A \overline{y}^2 = 60075.23 – (259.8 × 13.11^2)\).

\( I_{xx_G} \)= 15422.86 \( cm^4\) Ans.
