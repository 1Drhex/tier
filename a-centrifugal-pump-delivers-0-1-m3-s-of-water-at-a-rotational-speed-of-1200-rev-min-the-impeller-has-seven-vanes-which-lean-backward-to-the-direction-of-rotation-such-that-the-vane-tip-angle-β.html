Guest Question.

A centrifugal pump delivers \(0.1 m^{3}/s \) of water at a rotational speed of 1200 rev/min. The impeller has seven vanes, which lean backward to the direction of rotation such that the vane tip angle \(\beta^{\prime} _{2}\) is 50°. The impeller has an external diameter of 0.4 m, an internal diameter of 0.2 m, and an axial width of 31.7 mm. Assuming that the diffuser efficiency is 51.5%, that the impeller head losses are 10% of the ideal head rise, and that the diffuser exit is 0.15 m in diameter, estimate the slip factor, the manometric head, and the hydraulic efficiency.

Admin
Solution

The criterion given as Eq. (7.24c)

\(\varepsilon =\left(\frac{r_{1}}{r_{2}}\right)_{\lim}=\exp \left(\frac{-8.16\cos \beta^{\prime }_{2}}{Z}\right) \) (7.24c)

is employed prior to estimating the slip factor. As \(\exp \left(2\pi \cos \beta^{\prime }_{2}/Z\right) =\exp \left(2\pi \times 0.643/7\right)=1.78\) (which is less than \(r_{2}/r_{1}=2\)), then B = 1 and A ≈ 0.77. Note: This value of A is obtained by replotting values of A given in Figure 7.17 for \(\beta^{\prime }_{2}=50°\) and interpolating.
The vane tip speed

\(U_{2}=\pi ND_{2}/60=\pi \times 1200\times 0.4/6=25.13 m/s \)

The radial velocity

\(c_{r2}=Q/\left(\pi D_{2} b_{2}\right)=0.1/\left(\pi \times 0.4\times 0.0317\right)=2.51 m/s \)

Hence, the Busemann slip factor is

\(\sigma =\left(0.77-0.1\times 1.192\right) /\left(1-0.1\times 1.192\right)=0.739\)

Hydraulic losses occur in the impeller, in the diffuser, and in the volute. The loss in head in the diffuser is

\(\Delta H_{D}=\left(p_{02}-p_{03}\right)/\left(\rho g\right)=\left(p_{2}-p_{3}\right)/\left(\rho g\right)+\left(c^{2}_{2}-c^{2}_{3}\right)/\left(2g\right)\)

and, from Eq. (7.52) for incompressible flow,

\(\eta _{D} =\left(h_{2s} -h_{1}\right)/\left(h_{2} -h_{1}\right)=\left(c^{2}_{1}-c^{2}_{2s}\right)/\left(c^{2}_{1}-c^{2}_{2}\right)\) (7.52)

\(p_{3}-p_{2}=\frac{1}{2}\eta _{D} \rho \left(c^{2}_{2}-c^{2}_{3}\right)\)

Substituting in the previous equation, we find

\(\Delta H_{D}=\left(1-\eta _{D} \right) \left(c^{2}_{2}-c^{2}_{3}\right)/\left(2g\right)\)

The kinetic energy leaving the diffuser is only partly recovered. Watson and Janota (1982) ascribe the total loss in the volute as about half the dynamic head leaving the diffuser, and this is also assumed in this calculation. The exit head loss is \( 0.5\times c^{2}_{3}/\left(2g\right)\) and the head loss in the impeller is \(0.1\times U_{2} c_{\theta 2}/g\).
Summing all the losses,

\(H_{L} =0.485\times \left(c^{2}_{2}-c^{2}_{3}\right)/\left(2g\right)+0.1\times U_{2} c_{\theta 2}/g+0.5\times c^{2}_{3}/\left(2g\right) \)

Determining the velocities and heads needed,

\( c_{\theta 2}= \sigma U_{2}\left(1-\phi _{2} \tan \beta _{2^{\prime} } \right) =0.739\times 25.13\times 0.881=16.35 m/s\)

\(H_{i} = U_{2} c_{\theta 2}/g=25.13\times 16.35/9.81=41.8 m\)

\(c^{2}_{2}/\left(2g\right)=\left(16.35^{2}+2.51^{2}\right)/19.62=13.96 m \)

\(c_{3}=4Q/\left(\pi d^{2} \right)=0.4/\left(\pi \times 0.15^{2}\right)=5.65 m/s \)

\(c^{2}_{3}/\left(2g\right)=1.63 m \)

Therefore,

\(H_{L}=4.18+0.485\left(13.96-1.63\right)+1.63/2=10.98 m \)

The manometric head is

\(H=H_{i}-H_{L}=41.8-10.98=30.82 m\)

and the hydraulic efficiency is

\(\eta _{h}=H/H_{i}=73.7\% \)
