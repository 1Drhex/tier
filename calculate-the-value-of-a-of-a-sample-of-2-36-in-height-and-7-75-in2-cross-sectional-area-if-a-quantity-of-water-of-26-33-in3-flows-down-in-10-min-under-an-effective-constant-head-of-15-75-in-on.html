Guest Question.

Calculate the value of A: of a sample of 2.36 in. height and 7.75\(\text { in }^{2}\) cross-sectional area, if a quantity of water of 26.33 \(\text { in }^{3}\) flows down in 10 min under an effective constant head of 15.75 in. On oven drying, the test specimen weighed 1.1 Ib. Assuming \(G_{s}=2.65\), calculate the seepage velocity of water during the test.

Admin
Solution

From Eq. (4.9), \(k=\frac{Q L}{h A t}=\frac{26.33 \times 2.36}{15.75 \times 7.75 \times 10 \times 60}=0.8 \times 10^{-3} \text { in./sec }\)

 

\(k=\frac{Q L}{h A t}\) (4.9)

 

Discharge velocity, \(v=k i=k \frac{h}{L}=0.8 \times 10^{-3} \times \frac{15.75}{2.36}=5.34 \times 10^{-3} \mathrm{in} . / \mathrm{sec}\)

 

\(\gamma_{d}=\frac{W_{s}}{V}=\frac{1.1}{7.75 \times 2.36}=0.0601 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{in}^{3}=103.9 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{ft}^{3}\)

 

FromEq. (3.18a), \(e=\frac{\gamma_{w} G_{s}}{\gamma_{d}}-1\)

 

\(\gamma_{d}=\frac{\gamma_{w} G_{s}}{1+e}\) (3.18a)

 

or \(e=\frac{62.4 \times 2.65}{103.9}-1=0.5915\)

 

\(n=\frac{e}{1+e}=\frac{0.5915}{1+0.5915}=0.372\)

 

Seepage velocity , \(v_{s}=\frac{v}{n}=\frac{5.34 \times 10^{-3}}{0.372}=14.35 \times 10^{-3} \mathrm{in} . / \mathrm{sec}\)
