Guest Question.

Find the allowable load for a pin-ended round steel compression member that has a diameter of 30 mm and a length of 0.75 m. Use a factor of safety of 2.5, a yield strength of \(σ_{y} = 290 × 10^{3}\ kN/m^{2}\) and a modulus of \(E = 209 × 10^{6}\ kN/m^{2}\). Use Eq. (17.14) \(\sigma_{a}=\frac{\left[1-\frac{\sigma _{y}(kL/r)^{2}}{4\pi^{2}E } \right]\sigma_{y} }{F.S.}\) where \(0\leq \frac{kL}{r}\leq C_{c}\) or (17.15) \(\sigma _{a}=\frac{\frac{\pi ^{2}E}{(kL/r)^{2}}}{F.S.}\) where \(\frac{kL}{r}\geq C_{c}\) .

Admin
Solution

To determine the appropriate equation we calculate \(C_{c}\).

\(C_{c}=\sqrt{\frac{2\pi ^{2}E}{\sigma_{y}}}=\sqrt{\frac{2\pi^{2}209\times 10^{6}\ kN/m^{2} }{290\times 10^{3}\ kN/m^{2}} }=119.3\)

The area of a round bar of radius R is \(πR^{2}\) and the moment of inertia is \(πR^{4}/4\) from Table A.2 of the Appendix. Therefore, the radius of gyration

\(r=\sqrt{\frac{1}{A}}=\sqrt{\frac{\pi R^{4}}{4\pi R^{2}}}=\frac{R}{2}=\frac{15}{2}=7.5\ mm\)

For a pin-ended column, k = 1, and the slenderness ratio

\(\frac{kL}{r}=\frac{1(750\ mm)}{7.5\ mm}=100\)

The slenderness ratio \(kL/r = 100 < C_{c} = 119.3\), so we use the J. B. Johnson formula, Eq. (17.14).

\(\sigma_{a}=\frac{\left[1-\frac{\sigma _{y}(kL/r)^{2}}{4\pi^{2}E } \right]\sigma_{y} }{F.S.}\)

 

\(\sigma_{a}=\frac{\left[1-\frac{290\times10^{3}kN/m^{2}(100)^{2} }{4\pi^{2}209\times 10^{6}\ kN/m^{2} } \right] }{2.5}(290\times 10^{3}\ kN/m^{2})\)

 

\(σ_{a} = 75,230\ kN/m^{2} = 75.23\ MN/m^{2}\)

The cross-sectional area of the column is \(A = πR^{2} = π(15\ mm)^{2} = 703.9\ mm^{2}\), and the allowable load

\(P_{a}=\sigma _{a}A=\left[\frac{75,230\ kN(703.9\ mm^{2})}{m^{2}} \right]\left(\frac{m^{2}}{1\times 10^{6}mm^{2}} \right)\)

 

\(P_{a} = 52.9\ kN\)
