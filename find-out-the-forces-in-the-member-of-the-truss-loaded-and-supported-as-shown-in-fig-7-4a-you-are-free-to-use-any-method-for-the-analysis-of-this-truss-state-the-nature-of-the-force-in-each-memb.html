Guest Question.

Find out the forces in the member of the truss, loaded and supported, as shown in Fig. 7.4(a). You are free to use any method for the analysis of this truss. State the nature of the force in each member.

Admin
Solution

For finding forces in all members, it is preferable to use joint method. First let us find support reaction and some geometrical distance for simplicity.

(i) Consider the FBD of the entire truss

In Δ ABE

\( \sin 30° = \frac{AB}{AE} \) .

AB = 2.5 m.

AB = AC = CE = 2.5 m.

Drop perpendicular line from B and D and mark P and Q respectively as shown in Fig. 7.4(b).

In Δ ABP

\( \cos 60° = \frac{AP}{AB} \) .

∴ AP = 1.25 m.

In Δ CDE

\( \sin 30° = \frac{CD}{CE} \) ∴ CD = 1.25 m.

In Δ CQD

\( \cos 60° = \frac{CQ}{CD} \) ∴ CQ = 0.625 m.

\( ∑ M_A = 0 \).

\( R_E × 5 – 5 × 1.25 – 10 × (2.5 + 0.625) = 0 \).

\( R_E = 7.5\) kN (↑).

\( ∑ F_x = 0 \).

\( H_A = 0\).

\( ∑ F_y = 0 \).

\( V_A + R_E – 5 – 10 = 0 \).

\( V_A = 7.5 \) kN (↑).

(ii) Consider the FBD of Joint A

\( ∑ F_y = 0 \).

\( V_A – F_{AB} \sin 60° = 0 \).

\( F_{AB} = 8.66 \) kN (C).

\( ∑ F_x = 0 \).

\( F_{AC} – F_{AB} \cos 60° = 0 \).

\( F_{AC}= 4.33 \) kN (T).

(iii) Consider the FBD of Joint E

\( ∑ F_y = 0 \).

\( R_E – F_{DE} \sin 30° = 0 \).

\( F_{DE} = 15 \) kN (C).

\( ∑ F_x = 0 \).

\( F_{DE} \cos 30° – F_{CE} = 0 \).

\( F_{CE} = 12.99 \) kN (T).

(iv) Consider the FBD of Joint D

Note : Orientation of axes are changed for easy solution.

\( ∑ F_y = 0 \).

\( F_{CD} – 10 \sin 60° = 0 \).

\( F_{CD} = 8.66 \) kN (C).

\( ∑ F_x = 0 \).

\( F_{DE} – F_{BD} – 10 \cos 60° = 0 \).

\( F_{BD} = 15 – 10 \cos 60° \).

\( F_{BD} = 10 \) kN (C).

(v) Consider the FBD of Joint C

\( ∑ F_y = 0 \).

\( F_{BC} \sin 60° – F_{CD} \sin 60° = 0 \).

\( F_{BC} = 8.66 \) kN (T).

The results are tabulated as follows :

Member AB AC CE DE BD CD BC
Force (kN) 8.66 4.33 12.99 15 10 8.66 8.66
Type (T/C) C T T C C C T
