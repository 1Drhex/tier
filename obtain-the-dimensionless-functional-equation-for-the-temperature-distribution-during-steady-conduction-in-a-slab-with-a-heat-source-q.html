Guest Question.

Obtain the dimensionless functional equation for the temperature distribution during steady conduction in a slab with a heat source , \(\dot{q}\) .

Admin
Solution

In such a case, there might be one or two specified temperatures in the problem: \(T_{1} or T_{2}\) . Thus the dimensional functional equation is

 

\(\underbrace{T-T_{1} }_{K}=fn[(\underbrace{T_{2}-T_{1} }_{K} ),\underbrace{X,L}_{m},\underbrace{\dot{q} }_{W/m^3},\underbrace{K}_{W/m.K},\underbrace{\bar{h} }_{W/m^2K} ]\)

where we presume that a convective b.c. is involved and we identify a characteristic length, L, in the x-direction. There are seven variables
in three dimensions, or 7 – 3 = 4 pi-groups. Three of these groups are ones we have dealt with in the past in one form or another:

\(\Pi _{1}=\frac{T-T_{1} }{T_{2}-T_{1} }\) dimensionless temperature, which we shall give the name Θ

\(\Pi _{2}=\frac{X}{L }\) dimensionless length, which we call ξ

\(\Pi _{3}=\frac{\bar{h}L }{K }\) which we recognize as the Biot number, Bi

The fourth group is new to us:

\(\Pi _{4}=\frac{\dot{q} L^2 }{K(T_{2}-T_{1} ) }\) which compares the heat generation rate to the rate of heat loss; we call it Γ

Thus, the solution is

Θ = fn (ξ ,Bi , Γ )
