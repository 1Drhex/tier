Guest Question.

A long 10-cm-diameter steam pipe whose external surface temperature is \( 110^{\circ} C\) passes through some open area that is not protected against the winds (Fig 7-23 ). Determine the rate of heat loss from the pipe per unit of its length when the air is at 1 atm pressure and \( 10^{\circ} C\) and the wind is blowing across the pipe at a velocity of 8 m / s.

 

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION A steam pipe is exposed to windy air. The rate of heat loss from the steam is to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Steady operating conditions exist. 2 Radiation effects are negligible. 3 Air is an ideal gas.

Properties The properties of air at the average film temperature of \( T_{f}=\left(T_{s}+\right.) (\left.T_{\infty}\right) / 2=(110+10) / 2=60^{\circ} C\) and 1 atm pressure are (Table A -15)

\( k=0.02808 W / m \cdot K \operatorname{Pr}=0.7202 \quad v=1.896 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2} / s\)

Analysis The Reynolds number is

\( Re =\frac{V D}{\nu}=\frac{(8 m / s )(0.1 m )}{1.896 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2} / s }=4.219 \times 10^{4}\)

The Nusselt number can be determined from

\( Nu =\frac{h D}{k}=0.3+\frac{0.62 Re ^{1 / 2} Pr ^{1 / 3}}{\left[1+(0.4 / Pr )^{2 / 3}\right]^{1 / 4}}\left[1+\left(\frac{ Re }{282,000}\right)^{5 / 8}\right]^{4 / 5}=0.3+\frac{0.62\left(4.219 \times 10^{4}\right)^{1 / 2}(0.7202)^{1 / 3}}{\left[1+(0.4 / 0.7202)^{2 / 3}\right]^{1 / 4}}\left[1+\left(\frac{4.219 \times 10^{4}}{282,000}\right)^{5 / 8}\right]^{4 / 5}=124\) (7.35)

And

\( h=\frac{k}{D} Nu =\frac{0.02808 W / m \cdot K }{0.1 m }(124)=34.8 W / m ^{2} \cdot K\)

Then, the rate of heat transfer from the pipe per unit of its length becomes

\( A_{s}=p L=\pi D L=\pi(0.1 m )(1 m )=0.314 m ^{2} \dot{Q}=h A_{s}\left(T_{s}-T_{\infty}\right)=\left(34.8 W / m ^{2} \cdot K \right)\left(0.314 m ^{2}\right)(110-10)^{\circ} C =1093 W\)

The rate of heat loss from the entire pipe can be obtained by multiplying the value above by the length of the pipe in m.

Discussion The simpler Nusselt number relation in Table (7-1) in this case would give N u=128, which is 3 percent higher than the value obtained above using Eq. (7-35)

Laminar:

\( Nu _{x}=\frac{ Nu _{x(\text { for } \xi=0)}}{\left[1-(\xi / x)^{3 / 4}\right]^{1 / 3}}=\frac{0.332 Re _{x}^{0.5} \operatorname{Pr}^{1 / 3}}{\left[1-(\xi / x)^{3 / 4}\right]^{1 / 3}} \)
