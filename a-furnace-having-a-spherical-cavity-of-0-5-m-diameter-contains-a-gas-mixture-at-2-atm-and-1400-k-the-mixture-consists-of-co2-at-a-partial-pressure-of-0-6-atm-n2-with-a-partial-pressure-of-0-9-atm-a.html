Guest Question.

A furnace having a spherical cavity of 0.5 m diameter, contains a gas mixture at 2 atm and 1400 K. The mixture consists of\(CO _{2}\) at a partial pressure of 0.6 atm, \(N_{2}\) with a partial pressure of 0.9 atm and the remaining is water vapour. If the cavity wall is black, what is the cooling mte required to maintain its tempemture at 500 K?

Admin
Solution

\(L_{m}=\text { Mean beam length }=0.65 D=0.325 m\) (5.138)

\(P_{c} L_{m}=0.6 \times 0.325=0.195 a t m m\) (5.139)

\(\text { From the charts, } \epsilon_{c}=0.13\) (5.140)

\(\text { Correction factor for carbon dioxide } c_{c}=1.2\) (5.141)

\(\text { Correction factor for water vapour } c_{w}=1.57\) (5.142)

\(P_{w} L_{m}=0.5 \times 0.325=0.163 \text { atm } m\) (5.143)

\(\text { From the charts, } \epsilon_{w}=0.14\) (5.144)

\(\left(P_{ c }+P_{w}\right) L_{m}=1.1 \times 0.325=0.35 \text { atm } m\) (5.145)

\(\frac{P_{w}}{P_{w}+P_{c}}=\frac{0.5}{1.1}=0.45\) (5.146)

 

\(\Delta \epsilon\left(T_{g}=1400 K\right)=0.03\) (5.147)

\(\epsilon_{g}=c_{c} \epsilon_{c}+c_{w} \epsilon_{w}-\Delta \epsilon\) (5.148)

\(\epsilon_{g}=0.13 \times 1.2+0.14 \times 1.57-0.03\) (5.149)

\(\epsilon_{g}=0.36\) (5.150)

We repeat the procedure for calculation of \(\alpha_{g}\). But remember that we have to apply the correction in this case as

\(\alpha_{c} \text { (from the charts) }=0.09\) (5.151)

\(\alpha_{w} \text { (from the charts) }=0.17\) (5.152)

\(c_{c}=1.2\) (5.153)

\(c_{w}=1.57\) (5.154)

\(\alpha_{c}=c_{c} \alpha_{c}\left(\frac{T_{g}}{T_{s}}\right)^{0.65}=0.211\) (5.155)

\(\alpha_{w}=c_{w} \alpha_{w}\left(\frac{T_{g}}{T_{s}}\right)^{0.45}=0.432\) (5.156)

\(\Delta \alpha=f_{8}\left(\frac{P_{w}}{P_{w}+P_{c}},\left(P_{c}+P_{w}\right) L_{m}, T_{s}\right)\) (5.157)

\(\Delta \alpha=0.005\) (5.158)

\(\alpha_{g}=\alpha_{c}+\alpha_{w}-\Delta \alpha=0.64\) (5.159)

The most important thing we see here is that fg of \(\epsilon_{g} \neq \alpha_{g} .\) A mixture of carbon dioxide and water vapour is a non gray gas.

\(q_{1}=J_{1}-G_{1}\) (5.160)

\(Q_{1}=\left(J_{1}-G_{1}\right) 4 \pi R^{2}\) (5.161)

\(\epsilon_{1}=1\) (5.162)

\(J_{1}=\epsilon_{1} \sigma T_{1}^{4}+\left(1-\epsilon_{1}\right)=\sigma T_{1}^{4}\) (5.163)

\(G_{1}=\epsilon_{g} \sigma T_{g}^{4}+\tau_{g}\left(\sigma T_{1}^{4}\right)\) (5.164)

\(\alpha_{g}+\tau_{g}+\rho_{g}=1 ; \rho_{g}=0\) (5.165)

\(\alpha_{g}+\tau_{g}=1 ; \tau_{g}=1-\alpha_{g}\) (5.166)

\(G_{1}=\epsilon_{g} \sigma T_{g}^{4}+\left(1-\alpha_{g}\right)\left(\sigma T_{1}^{4}\right)\) (5.167)

\(q_{1}=J_{1}-G_{1}=\sigma T_{1}^{4}-\epsilon_{g} \sigma T_{g}^{4}-\sigma T_{1}^{4}+\alpha_{g} \sigma T_{1}^{4}\) (5.168)

\(q_{1}=\alpha_{g} \sigma T_{1}^{4}-\epsilon_{g} \sigma T_{g}^{4}\) (5.169)

For this problem under consideration,

\(\begin{aligned}q_{1} &=0.64 \times 5.67 \times 10^{-8} \times 500^{4} \\&-0.36 \times 5.67 \times 10^{-8} \times 1500^{4}\end{aligned}\) (5.170)

\(q_{1}=-76.2 \frac{k W}{m^{2}}\) (5.171)

\(Q_{1}=q_{1} 4 \pi R^{2}=-59.8 k W\) (5.172)

The solution tells us that we need to cool the cavity, which is very evident. Most importantly, in this spherical cavity, if we have ethane, propane, butane or LPG, burn it, allow it to reach a temperature of 1500 K and we get a mixture of \(CO _{2}+ H _{2} O\), it is possible to have tubes on the outside of the spherical cavity, send water through them which will get heated at the rate of 60 kW. This is the radiative heat transfer power that the gas is capable of transferring to the wall. This will be very important in furnace calculations, design of radiant super heaters and so on. It has several engineering applications!
