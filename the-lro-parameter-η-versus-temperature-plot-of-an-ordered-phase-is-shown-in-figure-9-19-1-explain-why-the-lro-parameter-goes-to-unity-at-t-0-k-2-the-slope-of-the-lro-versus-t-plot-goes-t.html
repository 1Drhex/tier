Guest Question.

The LRO parameter, η , versus temperature plot of an ordered phase is shown in Figure 9.19.

1. Explain why the LRO parameter goes to unity at T = 0 K.
2. The slope of the LRO versus T plot goes to 0 as T approaches 0. Show why this is true.

Hint : Start by writing G = G (η ,T̤ and take the full differential dG . By the time you finish, you will have to use a form of L’Hôpital’s rule:

\(\lim of \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} as x \rightarrow 0=\frac{f^\prime(x) }{g^\prime (x)} \)
Admin
Solution

1. From the Third Law of Thermodynamics, we expect that the configurational
entropy of a system in equilibrium goes to zero as the temperature goes to zero.

2. First we write G = G (T , η ),

\(dG=\left\lgroup\frac{\partial G}{\partial T} \right\rgroup_\eta dT+\left\lgroup\frac{\partial G}{\partial \eta } \right\rgroup _Td\eta =0 \)

 

\(\frac{d\eta }{dT}=-\frac{\left\lgroup\frac{\partial G}{\partial T} \right\rgroup_\eta }{{\left\lgroup\frac{\partial G}{\partial \eta } \right\rgroup_T }} =\frac{S}{\left\lgroup\frac{\partial G}{\partial \eta } \right\rgroup_T } \)

Since both terms go to zero in the quotient (at equilibrium), we need to apply L’Hôpital’s rule:

\(\lim of \left\lgroup\frac{d\eta }{dT} \right\rgroup \) as T → 0 =\(\frac{\left\lgroup\frac{\partial S}{\partial \eta } \right\rgroup }{\left\lgroup\frac{\partial^2G }{\partial \eta ^2} \right\rgroup }_T=\frac{0}{+} =0 \)
