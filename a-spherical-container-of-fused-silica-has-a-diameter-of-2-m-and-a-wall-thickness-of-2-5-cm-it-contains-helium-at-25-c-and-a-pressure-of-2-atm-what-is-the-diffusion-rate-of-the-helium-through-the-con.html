Guest Question.

A spherical container of fused silica has a diameter of 2 m and a wall thickness of 2.5 cm. It contains helium at 25 C and a pressure of 2 atm. What is the diffusion rate of the helium through the container wall?

Admin
Solution

Let us assume that the diameter given is the inside diameter of the container. Then \(r_{i}= 1m\) and \(r_{0} =1.025m\) . The molar rate of flow of the helium through the container wall is given by Eq .(11.45) :

For spherical walls : \(\dot{n} _{A}=\frac{4\pi D_{AB} }{\left(\frac{1}{r_{i} }-\frac{1}{r_{0} } \right) } \left(C_{Ai}-C_{A0} \right)\) (11.45)

 

, \(\dot{n} _{A}=\frac{4\pi D_{AB} }{\left(\frac{1}{r_{i} }-\frac{1}{r_{0} } \right) } \left(C_{Ai}-C_{A0} \right)\) (11.48)

 

We need the diffusion coefficient \( D_{AB}\) for the helium-fused silica system and the concentrations \(C_{Ai}\) and \(C_{A0}\) of the helium at the inner and outer surfaces of the container .

, the solubility for the helium-fused silica system \(S=4.5\times 10^{-4} kmol/m^{3} bar\) .

The helium pressure is 2 atm. Converting this to bars, we have

\(P=2atm\times \left(101.325kPa/1atm\right) \times \left(1bar/100kPa\right)=2.0265 bar\) .

The container has only helium, so the partial pressure of the helium is 2.0265 bar.

From Eq. (11.46)

\(C_{A,solid- side} =SP_{A,gas -side}\) (11.46)

, the concentration of the helium on the silica side of the inner surface is

\(C_{A,solid- side} =SP_{A,gas -side} = \left(4.5\times 10^{-4} \right) \left(2.0265\right) =0.000912kmol/m^{3}\)

On the silica side of the outer surface, the concentration of helium is essentially zero as there is negligible helium in the adjacent atmospheric air. Putting values into Eq. (11.48)

\(\dot{n} _{A} =\frac{4\pi D_{AB} }{\left(\frac{1}{r_{i} } -\frac{1}{r_{0} } \right) } \left(C_{Ai} -C_{A0} \right)\) (11.48)

 

, we have, for the molar diffusion of helium through the container wall ,

\(\dot{n} _{A} =\frac{4\pi D_{AB} }{\left(\frac{1}{r_{i} } -\frac{1}{r_{0} } \right) } \left(C_{Ai} -C_{A0} \right) =\frac{4\pi \left(4\times 10^{-14} \right) }{\left(1-\frac{1}{1.025} \right) } \left(0.000912-0\right) =1.88\times 10^{-14} kmol/s\)

 

On a mass basis, the diffusion is

\(\dot{m} _{A} = \dot{n} _{A}M_{A} =\left(1.88\times 10^{-14} kmol/s\right)\left(4kg/kmol\right) =7.52\times 10^{-14} kg/s=0.0024g/year\)

 

Yes, there will be leakage of helium through container wall, but the leakage will be very, very small!
