Guest Question.

Sometimes it is possible to solve Equation 7.10 directly, without having to expand \( \psi_{n}^{1} \) in terms of the unperturbed wave functions (Equation 7.11). Here are two particularly nice examples.

\( \left(H^{0}-E_{n}^{0}\right) \psi_{n}^{1}=-\left(H^{\prime}-E_{n}^{1}\right) \psi_{n}^{0} \) (7.10).

\( \psi_{n}^{1}=\sum_{m \neq n} c_{m}^{(n)} \psi_{m}^{0} \) (7.11).

(a) Stark effect in the ground state of hydrogen.

(i) Find the first-order correction to the ground state of hydrogen in the presence of a uniform external electric field \( E_{\text {ext }} \) (see ). Hint: Try a solution of the form

\( \left(A+B r+C r^{2}\right) e^{-r / a} \cos \theta \);

your problem is to find the constants A, B, and C that solve Equation 7.10.

\( \left(H^{0}-E_{n}^{0}\right) \psi_{n}^{1}=-\left(H^{\prime}-E_{n}^{1}\right) \psi_{n}^{0} \) (7.10).

(ii) Use Equation 7.14 to determine the second-order correction to the ground state energy (the first-order correction is zero, as you found in (a)). \( \text { Answer: }-m\left(3 a^{2} e E_{\text {ext }} / 2 \hbar\right)^{2} \).

\( E_{n}^{2}=\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle-E_{n}^{1}\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0} \mid \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle \) (7.14).

(b) If the proton had an electric dipole moment p, the potential energy of the electron in hydrogen would be perturbed in the amount

\( H^{\prime}=-\frac{e p \cos \theta}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0} r^{2}} \).

(i) Solve Equation 7.10 for the first-order correction to the ground state wave function.

\( \left(H^{0}-E_{n}^{0}\right) \psi_{n}^{1}=-\left(H^{\prime}-E_{n}^{1}\right) \psi_{n}^{0} \) (7.10).

(ii) Show that the total electric dipole moment of the atom is (surprisingly) zero, to this order.

(iii) Use Equation 7.14 to determine the second-order correction to the ground state energy. What is the first-order correction?

\( E_{n}^{2}=\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle-E_{n}^{1}\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0} \mid \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle \) (7.14).

Admin
Solution

(a) (i) Equation 7.10:

\( \left(H^{0}-E_{n}^{0}\right) \psi_{n}^{1}=-\left(H^{\prime}-E_{n}^{1}\right) \psi_{n}^{0} \) (7.10).

\( \left(H^{0}-E_{0}^{0}\right) \psi_{0}^{1}=-\left(H^{\prime}-E_{0}^{1}\right) \psi_{0}^{0} \).

\( H^{0}=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \nabla^{2}-\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \frac{1}{r}=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m}\left(\nabla^{2}+\frac{2}{a r}\right), \quad \text { since } \quad a=\frac{4 \pi \epsilon_{0} \hbar^{2}}{m e^{2}} \).

\( E_{0}^{0}=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m a^{2}} \).

\( H^{\prime}=e E_{\text {ext }} r \cos \theta ; \quad E_{0}^{1}=0 \) ((a)).

\( \psi_{0}^{0}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi a^{3}}} e^{-r / a} ; \quad \psi_{0}^{1}=f(r) e^{-r / a} \cos \theta \).

Equation 4.13 ⇒

\( \nabla^{2} \psi_{0}^{1}=\frac{\cos \theta}{r^{2}} \frac{d}{d r}\left[r^{2} \frac{d}{d r}\left(f e^{-r / a}\right)\right]+\frac{f e^{-r / a}}{r^{2} \sin \theta} \frac{d}{d \theta}\left[\sin \theta \frac{d}{d \theta}(\cos \theta)\right] \).

\( \nabla^{2}=\frac{1}{r^{2}} \frac{\partial}{\partial r}\left(r^{2} \frac{\partial}{\partial r}\right)+\frac{1}{r^{2} \sin \theta} \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta}\left(\sin \theta \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta}\right)+\frac{1}{r^{2} \sin ^{2} \theta}\left(\frac{\partial^{2}}{\partial \phi^{2}}\right) \) (4.13).

\( =\frac{\cos \theta}{r^{2}} \frac{d}{d r}\left[r^{2}\left(f^{\prime}-\frac{1}{a} f\right) e^{-r / a}\right]+\frac{f e^{-r / a}}{r^{2} \sin \theta} \frac{d}{d \theta}\left[-\sin ^{2} \theta\right] \).

\( =\frac{\cos \theta}{r^{2}}\left[2 r\left(f^{\prime}-\frac{1}{a} f\right) e^{-r / a}+r^{2}\left(f^{\prime \prime}-\frac{2}{a} f^{\prime}+\frac{1}{a^{2}} f\right) e^{-r / a}\right]-\frac{2 \cos \theta}{r^{2}} f e^{-r / a} \).

\( =\cos \theta e^{-r / a}\left[\left(f^{\prime \prime}-\frac{2}{a} f^{\prime}+\frac{1}{a^{2}} f\right)+2\left(f^{\prime}-\frac{1}{a} f\right) \frac{1}{r}-2 f \frac{1}{r^{2}}\right] \).

Plug this into Eq. 7.10:

\( \left(H^{0}-E_{n}^{0}\right) \psi_{n}^{1}=-\left(H^{\prime}-E_{n}^{1}\right) \psi_{n}^{0} \) (7.10).

\( -\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \cos \theta e^{-r / a}\left[\left(f^{\prime \prime}-\frac{2}{a} f^{\prime}+\frac{1}{a^{2}} f\right)+2\left(f^{\prime}-\frac{1}{a} f\right) \frac{1}{r}-2 f \frac{1}{r^{2}}+2 f \frac{1}{a} \frac{1}{r}-f \frac{1}{a^{2}}\right]=-e E_{ ext } r \cos \theta \frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi a^{3}}} e^{-r / a} \).

\(\blacklozenge \) \( \left(f^{\prime \prime}-\frac{2}{a} f^{\prime}\right)+2 f^{\prime} \frac{1}{r}-2 f \frac{1}{r^{2}}=\left(\frac{2 m e E_{\text {ext }}}{\hbar^{2} \sqrt{\pi a^{3}}}\right) r=\frac{4 \gamma}{a} r, \quad \text { where } \quad \gamma \equiv \frac{m e E_{\text {ext }}}{2 \hbar^{2} \sqrt{\pi a}} \).

Now let \( f(r)=A+B r+C r^{2}, \quad \text { so } \quad f^{\prime}=B+2 C r \quad \text { and } \quad f^{\prime \prime}=2 C \). Then

\( 2 C-\frac{2}{a}(B+2 C r)+\frac{2}{r}(B+2 C r)-\frac{2}{r^{2}}\left(A+B r+C r^{2}\right)=\frac{4 \gamma}{a} r \).

Collecting like powers of r:

\( r^{-2}: \quad A=0 \).

\( r^{-1}: \quad 2 B-2 B=0 \text { (automatic) } \).

\( r^{0}: \quad 2 C-2 B / a+4 C-2 C=0 \Rightarrow B=2 a C . \)

\( r^{1}: \quad-4 C / a=4 \gamma / a \Rightarrow C=-\gamma \).

Evidently the function suggested does satisfy Eq. 7.10, with the coefficients \( A=0, B=-2 a \gamma, C=-\gamma \); the first-order correction to the wave function is

\( \psi_{0}^{1}=-\gamma r(r+2 a) e^{-r / a} \cos \theta \).

\( \left(H^{0}-E_{n}^{0}\right) \psi_{n}^{1}=-\left(H^{\prime}-E_{n}^{1}\right) \psi_{n}^{0} \) (7.10).

(ii) Equation 7.14 says, in this case:

\( E_{n}^{2}=\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle-E_{n}^{1}\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0} \mid \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle \) (7.14).

\( E_{0}^{2}=\left\langle\psi_{0}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{0}^{1}\right\rangle=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi a^{3}}} \frac{m e E_{\text {ext }}}{2 \hbar^{2} \sqrt{\pi a}} e E_{\text {ext }} \int e^{-r / a}(r \cos \theta) r(r+2 a) e^{-r / a} \cos \theta r^{2} \sin \theta d r d \theta d \phi \).

\( =-\frac{m\left(e E_{ ext }\right)^{2}}{2 \pi a^{2} \hbar^{2}} 2 \pi \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{4}(r+2 a) e^{-2 r / a} d r \int_{0}^{\pi} \cos ^{2} \theta \sin \theta d \theta \).

\( =-\left.m\left(\frac{e E_{ ext }}{a \hbar}\right)^{2}\left[5 !\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^{6}+2 a 4 !\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^{5}\right]\left(-\frac{\cos ^{3} \theta}{3}\right)\right|_{0} ^{\pi} \).

\( =-m\left(\frac{e E_{\text {ext }}}{a \hbar}\right)^{2}\left(\frac{27}{8} a^{6}\right) \frac{2}{3}=-m\left(\frac{3 e E_{\operatorname{ext}} a^{2}}{2 \hbar}\right)^{2} \).

(b) (i) This is the same as (a) [note that \( E_{0}^{1}=0 \), as before, since \( \psi_{0}^{0} \) is spherically symmetric, so \( \langle\cos \theta\rangle=0] \) except for the r-dependence of \( H^{\prime} \) .

So Eq. \(\blacklozenge \) ⇒

\( f^{\prime \prime}+2 f^{\prime}\left(\frac{1}{r}-\frac{1}{a}\right)-2 f \frac{1}{r^{2}}=-\left(\frac{2 m e p}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0} \hbar^{2} \sqrt{\pi a^{3}}}\right) \frac{1}{r^{2}}=-\frac{2 \beta}{r^{2}} \), where \( \beta \equiv \frac{m e p}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0} \hbar^{2} \sqrt{\pi a^{3}}} \).

The solution this time it obvious: f(r) = β (constant). [For the general solution we would add the general solution to the homogeneous equation (right side set equal to zero), but this would simply reproduce the unperturbed ground state, \( \psi_{0}^{0} \), which we exclude-see p. 282.] So

\( \psi_{0}^{1}=\beta e^{-r / a} \cos \theta \).

(ii) The electric dipole moment of the electron is

\( \left\langle p_{e}\right\rangle=\langle-e r \cos \theta\rangle=-e\left\langle\psi_{0}^{0}+\psi_{0}^{1}|r \cos \theta| \psi_{0}^{0}+\psi_{0}^{1}\right\rangle=-e\left(\left\langle\psi_{0}^{0}|r \cos \theta| \psi_{0}^{0}\right\rangle+2\left\langle\psi_{0}^{0}|r \cos \theta| \psi_{0}^{1}\right\rangle+\left\langle\psi_{0}^{1}|r \cos \theta| \psi_{0}^{1}\right\rangle\right) \).

But the first term is zero, and the third is higher order, so

\( \left\langle p_{e}\right\rangle=-2 e \frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi a^{3}}} \beta \int e^{-r / a}(r \cos \theta) e^{-r / a} \cos \theta r^{2} \sin \theta d r d \theta d \phi \).

\( =-2 e\left(\frac{m e p}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0} \hbar^{2} \pi a^{3}}\right) 2 \pi \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{3} e^{-2 r / a} d r \int_{0}^{\pi} \cos ^{2} \theta \sin \theta d \theta=-\left(\frac{m e^{2} p}{\epsilon_{0} \hbar^{2} \pi a^{3}}\right)\left[3 !\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^{4}\right]\left(\frac{2}{3}\right) \).

\( =-\left(\frac{m e^{2} p}{\epsilon_{0} \hbar^{2} \pi a^{3}}\right)\left(\frac{3 a^{4}}{8}\right)\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)=-\left(\frac{m e^{2} p a}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0} \hbar^{2}}\right)=-p . \)

Evidently the dipole moment associated with the perturbation of the electron cloud cancels the dipole moment of the nucleus, and the total dipole moment of the atom is zero.

(iii) The first-order correction is zero (as noted in (i)). The second-order correction is

\( E_{0}^{2}=\left\langle\psi_{0}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{0}^{1}\right\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi a^{3}}}\left(-\frac{e p}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)\left(\frac{m e p}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0} \hbar^{2} \sqrt{\pi a^{3}}}\right) \int e^{-r / a}\left(\frac{\cos \theta}{r^{2}}\right) e^{-r / a} \cos \theta r^{2} \sin \theta d r d \theta d \phi \).

\( =-m \frac{(e p)^{2}}{\left(4 \pi \epsilon_{0}\right)^{2} \hbar^{2} \pi a^{3}} 2 \pi \int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-2 r / a} d r \int_{0}^{\pi} \cos ^{2} \theta \sin \theta d \theta=-2 m \frac{(e p)^{2}}{\left(4 \pi \epsilon_{0}\right)^{2} \hbar^{2} a^{3}}\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)\left(\frac{2}{3}\right) \).

\( =\frac{4}{3}\left(-\frac{m e^{4}}{2\left(4 \pi \epsilon_{0}\right)^{2} \hbar^{2}}\right) \frac{p^{2}}{e^{2} a^{2}}=\frac{4}{3}\left(\frac{p}{e a}\right)^{2} E_{1} \).
