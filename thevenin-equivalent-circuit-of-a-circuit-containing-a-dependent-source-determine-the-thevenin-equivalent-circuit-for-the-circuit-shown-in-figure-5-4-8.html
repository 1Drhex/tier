Guest Question.

Thevenin Equivalent Circuit of a Circuit Containing a Dependent Source. Determine the Thevenin equivalent circuit for the circuit shown in Figure 5.4-8.

Admin
Solution

We will determine the values of \(v_{oc}, R_t, and i_{sc}\) and use Eq. 5.4-2 to check that our values are correct.

\(v_{oc} = R_t i_{sc}\) (5.4-2)

To determine the open-circuit voltage of the circuit shown in Figure 5.4-8, we connect an open circuit between terminals a and b and label the voltage across that open circuit as \(v_{oc}\). Figure 5.4-9 shows the resulting circuit after using KCL to label the element currents.

The open circuit causes the current in the 5-Ω resistor to be zero. The voltage across that resistor is also zero, so the voltage across the 40-Ω resistor is \(v_{oc}\) as labeled in Figure 5.4-9.

Using Ohm’s law \(i_a = \frac{v_{oc}}{40}\)

Applying KVL to the loop consisting the 12-V source, 10-Ω resistor, and 40-Ω resistor gives

\(0 = -12 + v_{oc} – 10(3.5i_a)\)

Solving these equations for \(v_{oc}\) gives \(v_{oc} = 96 V\)

To determine the short-circuit current of the circuit shown in Figure 5.4-8, we connect a short circuit between terminals a and b and label the current across that short circuit as \(i_{sc}\). Figure 5.4-10 shows the resulting circuit after using KCL to label the element currents.

Applying KVL to the loop consisting of the 5-Ω and 40-Ω resistors gives

\(5 i_{sc} – 40 i_a = 0 ⇒ i_a = \frac{i_{sc}}{8}\)

Apply KCL at the top node of the 10-Ω resistor to write

\(4.5i_a = i_b + (i_a + i_{sc}) ⇒ i_b = 3.5i_a – i_{sc} = – \frac{9}{16} i_{sc}\)

Apply KVL to the loop consisting of the voltage source and the 5-Ω and 10-Ω resistors to write

\(-12 + 5 i_{sc} – 10 \left(- \frac{9}{16} i_{sc}\right) = 0\)

Solving this equation for \(i_{sc}\) gives \(i_{sc} = \frac{12}{5 + \frac{90}{16}} = 1.1294 A\)

Referring to Figure 5.4-4, we’ll determine the Thevenin resistance of the circuit by replacing the independent voltage source by a short circuit and connecting a current source to terminal a-b as shown in Figure 5.4-11. (Circuit A* in Figure 5.4-4 is obtained from Circuit A by replacing the independent voltage sources by short circuits and the independent current sources by open circuits.)

Apply KCL at the top node of the 10-Ω resistor to write

\(4.5i_a + (i_t – i_a) = i_b ⇒ i_b = 3.5 i_a + i_t\)

Applying KVL to the loop consisting of the 10-Ω and 40-Ω resistors gives

\(40i_a = 10i_b = 10 (3.5i_a + i_t) ⇒ i_a = 2i_t\)

Applying KVL to the loop consisting of the independent current source and the 10-Ω and 5-Ω resistors gives

\(v_t = 5i_t + 10i_b = 5i_t + 10 (3.5i_a + i_t) = 15i_t + 35i_a = 15i_t + 35 (2 i_t) = 85i_t\)

The Thevenin resistance is \(R_t = \frac{v_t}{i_t} = 85 Ω\)

Our values of \(v_{oc}, R_t, and i_{sc}\) satisfy Eq. 5.4-2, so we’re confident that they are correct. Finally, the Thevenin equivalent circuit is shown in Figure 5.4-12.
