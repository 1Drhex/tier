Guest Question.

Consider natural gas D which are listed in Table 13.2. Calculate the enthalpy of combustion of each gas at 25°C, assuming that the products include vapor water. Repeat the answer for liquid water in the products.

Admin
Solution

Natural Gas D:

\(\begin{gathered}0.543 CH _{4}+0.163 C _{2} H _{6}+0.162 C _{3} H _{8}+0.074 C _{4} H _{10} \\+0.058 N _{2}+ v _{ O 2}\left( O _{2}+3.76 N _{2}\right)\end{gathered}\)

→ \(1.651 CO _{2}+2.593 H _{2} O + v _{ O 2} \times 3.76 N _{2}\)

\(H _{ R }=0.543(-74873)+0.163(-84740)+0.162(-130900)+ 0.074(-126 200) = -80 639 kJ \)

 

a) vapor \(H _{2} O\)

\(\begin{aligned}& H _{ P }=1.651(-393522)+2.593(-241826)=-1276760 kJ \\&\overline{ h }_{ RP }= – 1 196 1 2 1 kJ / kmol\end{aligned}\)

b) Liq. \(H _{2} O\)

\(\begin{aligned}& H _{ P }=1.651(-393522)+2.593(-285830)=-1390862 kJ \\&\overline{ h }_{ RP }=-1310223 kJ / kmol\end{aligned}\)
