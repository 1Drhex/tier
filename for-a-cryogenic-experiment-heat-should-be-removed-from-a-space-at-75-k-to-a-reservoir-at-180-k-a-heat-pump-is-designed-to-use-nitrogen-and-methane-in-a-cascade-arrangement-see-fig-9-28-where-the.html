Guest Question.

For a cryogenic experiment heat should be removed from a space at 75 K to a reservoir at 180 K. A heat pump is designed to use nitrogen and methane in a cascade arrangement (see Fig. 9.28), where the high temperature of the nitrogen condensation is at 10 K higher than the low-temperature evaporation of the methane. The two other phase changes take place at the listed reservoir temperatures. Find the saturation temperatures in the heat exchanger between the two cycles that gives the best coefficient of performance for the overall system.

Admin
Solution

The nitrogen cycle is the bottom cycle and the methane cycle is the top cycle. Both are standard refrigeration cycles.

\(\begin{aligned}&T _{ Hm }=180 K = T _{3 m }, \quad T _{ LN }=75 K = T _{4 N }= T _{1 N }\\&T _{ Lm }= T _{4 m }= T _{1 m }= T _{3 N }-10, \text { Trial and error on } T _{3 N } \text { or } T _{ Lm } \text { . }\end{aligned}\)

For each cycle we have,

\(– w _{ C }= h _{2}- h _{1}, s _{2}= s _{1}, \quad- q _{ H }= h _{2}- h _{3}, \quad q _{ L }= h _{1}- h _{4}= h _{1}- h _{3}\)

Nitrogen: \(T _{4}= T _{1}=75 K \Rightarrow h _{1}=74.867 kJ / kg , s _{1}=5.4609 kJ / kg K\)

\(\begin{array}{lccccccc}N _{2} & T _{3} & h _{3} & P _{2} & h _{2} & – w _{ c } & – q _{ H } & q _{ L } \\\text { a) } & 120 & -17.605 & 2.5125 & 202.96 & 128.1 & 220.57 & 92.47 \\\text { b) } & 115 & -34.308 & 1.9388 & 188.35 & 113.5 & 222.66 & 109.18 \\\text { c) } & 110 & -48.446 & 1.4672 & 173.88 & 99.0 & 222.33 & 123.31\end{array}\)

 

Methane: \(T _{3}=180 K \Rightarrow h _{3}=-0.5 kJ / kg , P _{2}=3.28655 MPa\)

\(\begin{array}{lllccccc}CH _{4} & T _{4} & h _{1} & s _{1} & h _{2} & – w _{ c } & – q _{ H } & q _{ L } \\\text { a) } & 110 & 221 & 9.548 & 540.3 & 319.3 & 540.8 & 221.5 \\\text { b) } & 105 & 212.2 & 9.691 & 581.1 & 368.9 & 581.6 & 212.7 \\\text { c) } & 100 & 202.9 & 9.851 & 629.7 & 426.8 & 630.2 & 203.4\end{array}\)

The heat exchanger that connects the cycles transfers a Q

\(\dot{ Q }_{ Hn }= q _{ Hn } \dot{ m }_{ n }=\dot{ Q }_{ Lm }= q _{ Lm } \dot{ m }_{ m } \Rightarrow \dot{ m }_{ m } / \dot{ m }_{ n }= q _{ Hn } / q _{ Lm }\)

The overall unit then has

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{ Q }_{ L 75 K }=\dot{ m }_{ n } q _{ Ln } ; \quad \dot{ W }_{\text {tot in }}=-\left(\dot{ m }_{ n } w _{ cn }+\dot{ m }_{ m } w _{ cm }\right) \\&\beta=\dot{ Q }_{ L 75 K } / \dot{ W }_{\text {tot in }}= q _{ Ln } /\left[- w _{ cn }-\left(\dot{ m }_{ m } / \dot{ m }_{ n }\right) w _{ cm }\right]\end{aligned}\) \(\begin{array}{llcc}\text { Case } & \dot{ m }_{ m } / \dot{ m }_{ n } & w _{ cn }+\left(\dot{ m }_{ m } / \dot{ m }_{ n }\right) w _{ cm } & \beta \\\text { a) } & 0.996 & 446.06 & 0.207 \\\text { b) } & 1.047 & 499.65 & 0.219 \\\text { c) } & 1.093 & 565.49 & 0.218\end{array}\)

A maximum coefficient of performance is between case b) and c).
