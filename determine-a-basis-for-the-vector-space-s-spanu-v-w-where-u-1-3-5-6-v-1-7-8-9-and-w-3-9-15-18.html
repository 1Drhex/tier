Guest Question.

Determine a basis for the vector space \(S=\operatorname{span}\{\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}, \mathbf{w}\}\) where

\(\mathbf{u}=\left(\begin{array}{l}1 \\3 \\5 \\6\end{array}\right), \mathbf{v}=\left(\begin{array}{l}1 \\7 \\8 \\9\end{array}\right) \text { and } \mathbf{w}=\left(\begin{array}{l}3 \\9 \\15 \\18\end{array}\right)\)
Admin
Solution

By applying the above procedure we have:

Step 1 and 2: Writing the matrix A and evaluating its reduced row echelon form:

A=\(\left(\begin{array}{c}\mathbf{u} \\\mathrm{v} \\\mathbf{w}\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{cccc}1 & 3 & 5 & 6 \\1 & 7 & 8 & 9 \\3 & 9 & 15 & 18\end{array}\right) \quad â‡’\quad \mathrm{R}=\left(\begin{array}{cccc}1 & 0 & 2.75 & 3.75 \\0 & 1 & 0.75 & 0.75 \\0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right)\)

\(S=\operatorname{span}\{\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}, \mathbf{w}\}=\) Row space of matrix \(\mathrm{A}\)

Step 3:

What is a basis for \(S=\operatorname{span}\{\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}, \mathbf{w}\} ?\)

It is the non-zero rows of the reduced row echelon form matrix \(\mathrm{R}\). We have

\(\mathbf{r}_{1}=\left(\begin{array}{c}1 \\0 \\2.75 \\3.75\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{c}1 \\0 \\11 / 4 \\15 / 4\end{array}\right)=\frac{1}{4}\left(\begin{array}{c}4 \\0 \\11 \\15\end{array}\right) \text { and } \mathbf{r}_{2}=\left(\begin{array}{c}0 \\1 \\0.75 \\0.75\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{c}0 \\1 \\3 / 4 \\3 / 4\end{array}\right)=\frac{1}{4}\left(\begin{array}{l}0 \\4 \\3 \\3\end{array}\right)\)

Since the row space is the linear combination of vectors \(\mathbf{r}_{1}\) and \(\mathbf{r}_{2}\) so any non – zero scalar multiple of \(\mathbf{r}_{1}\) and \(\mathbf{r}_{2}\) is also a basis for the row space. (The basis gives the directions of the two axes for the space.) Hence, multiplying vectors \(\mathbf{r}_{1}\) and \(\mathbf{r}_{2}\) by 4 gives vectors:

\( \left\{\left(\begin{array}{llll} 4 & 0 & 11 & 15 \end{array}\right)^{T},\left(\begin{array}{llll} 0 & 4 & 3 & 3 \end{array}\right)^{T}\right\} \)

which form a basis for \(S=\operatorname{span}\{\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}, \mathbf{w}\}\) and this is a subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^{4}\). This \(S\) is a plane in \(\mathbb{R}^{4}\) because we have two axes or two basis vectors for this space.
