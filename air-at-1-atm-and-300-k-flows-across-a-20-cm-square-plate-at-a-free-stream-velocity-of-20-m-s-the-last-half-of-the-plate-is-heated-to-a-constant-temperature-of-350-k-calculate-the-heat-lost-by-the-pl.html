Guest Question.

Air at 1 atm and 300 K flows across a 20-cm-square plate at a free-stream velocity of 20 m/s. The
last half of the plate is heated to a constant temperature of 350 K. Calculate the heat lost by the
plate.

Admin
Solution

First we evaluate the air properties at the film temperature:

\(T_{f} =(T_{w} +T_{∞} ) /2=325\; K \)

and obtain

\(v=18.23×10^{-6}m^{2} /s \qquad k =0.02814\enspace W/m· ^{◦} C\qquad Pr=0.7\)

At the trailing edge of the plate the Reynolds number is:

\(Re_{L} =u_{∞} L/v=(20)(0.2)/18.23×10^{-6}=2.194×10^{5} \)

 

or, laminar flow over the length of the plate.
Heating does not start until the last half of the plate, or at a position x0 =0.1 m. The local
heat-transfer coefficient for this condition is given by Equation (5-41):

\(h_{x}=0.332k\; Pr^{1/3}(\frac{u_{∞} }{νx} )^{1/2} \left[1-(\frac{x_{0}}{x} )^{3/4} \right]^{-1/3} \)

 

\(h_{x}=0.332k\; Pr^{1/3} (u∞/vx)^{1/2}\left[1-(x_{0}/x)^{0.75} \right]^{-1/3} \hspace{30 pt} [a] \)

Inserting the property values along with x0 =0.1 gives

\(h_{x}=8.6883x^{-1/2}(1-0.17783x^{-0.75} )^{-1/3}\hspace{30 pt} [b] \)

The plate is 0.2 m wide so the heat transfer is obtained by integrating over the heated length
x0 <x<L

\(q=(0.2)(T_{w} −T_{∞} ) \int_{x_{0}=0.1 }^{L=0.2}{h_{x}dx\hspace{30 pt} [c] } \)

Inserting Equation (b) in Equation (c) and performing the numerical integration gives

\(q=(0.2)(8.6883)(0.4845)(350−300)=421\; W \hspace{30 pt} [d] \)

The average value of the heat-transfer coefficient over the heated length is given by

\(h=q/(T_{w} −T_{∞} )(L-x_{0} )W= 421/(350−300)(0.2−0.1)(0.2)=421\enspace W/m^{2} · ^{◦} C \)

where W is the width of the plate.
An easier calculation can be made by applying Equation (5-45b) \(\frac{\bar{h}_{x0-L}}{h_{x=L} }=2L\frac{1-(x_{0}/L )^{3/4} }{L-x_{0} } \) to determine the average
heat transfer coefficient over the heated portion of the plate. The result is

\(h=425.66\enspace W/m^{2} · ^{◦} C \quad and\quad q=425.66W\)

which indicates, of course, only a small error in the numerical integeration.
