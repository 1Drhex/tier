Guest Question.

Consider the stationary response of an SDF oscillator with \( m = 100kg, k = 10,000N/m, and c = 40N\cdot s/m \) . The excitation is a stationary delta-correlated process having a mean value of \( \mu _{F}(t)=\mu _{0}=1000N \) and a covariance function of \( K_{FF}(t,s)=2\pi S_{0}\delta (t,s)=2\pi (1000N^{2}\cdot s/rad)\delta (t-s) \) .

Find the conditional mean and variance of the \( X(t)\) and \( \dot{X}(t) \) responses for \( t\gt 5s \) given that \( X(5) = 0.05 m \) and \( \dot{X}(5)=0 \) .

Admin
Solution

This oscillator has the same parameters as the one considered in , so we can use basically the same state-space equations as were developed there -only the excitation and the initial conditions are different. Thus, the conditional mean value of the response is found from the solution of

\( \frac{d}{dt}E\left(\vec{Y}(t)\mid\vec{Y} (5)=[0.05,0]^{T} \right) +A^{-1}BE\left(\vec{Y}(t)\mid \vec{Y}(5)=[0.05,0]^{T} \right) =A^{-1}\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ \mu _{0} \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 10 \end{matrix} \right] \)

The result for \( t\gt 5s \) can be written as

\( E\left(\vec{Y}(t)\mid\vec{Y} (5)=[0.05,0]^{T} \right) =\exp [-(t-5)A^{-1}B]\left[\begin{matrix} 0.05 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right]+\int_{5}^{t}{\exp [-(t-s)A^{-1}B]} ds\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 10 \end{matrix} \right] \)

Or

\( E\left(\vec{Y}(t)\mid\vec{Y} (5)=[0.05,0]^{T} \right) =\exp [-(t-5)A^{-1}B]\left[\begin{matrix} 0.05 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right]+(A^{-1}B)^{-1}(I_{2}-\exp [-(t-5)A])\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 10 \end{matrix} \right] \) \( =\theta \exp [-(t-5)\lambda ]\theta ^{-1}\left[\begin{matrix} 0.05 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right] +\theta \lambda ^{-1}\theta ^{-1}\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 10 \end{matrix} \right] -\theta \lambda ^{-1}\exp [-(t-5)\lambda ]\theta ^{-1}\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\10 \end{matrix} \right] \)

Using the \( 2\times 2\theta \) and \( \lambda \) . matrices from gives

\( E[X(t)\mid X(5)=0.05,\dot{X}(5)=0] \) \( =0.1-e^{-0.2(t-5)}\left(0.05\cos [9.998(t-5)]+10^{-3}\sin [9.998(t-5)]\right) \)

And

\( E[\dot{X}(t)\mid X(5)=0.05,\dot{X}(5)=0]=0.5001e^{-0.2(t-5)}\sin [9.998(t-5)] \)

The values are shown in the following sketches.

For the conditional analysis of covariance, we first note that the given deterministic initial conditions at \( t = 5 \) imply that the covariance matrix is zero at that time. This gives the solution as \( K_{YY}(t,t)=\theta K_{ZZ}(t,t)\theta ^{T} \) with

\( K_{ZZ}(t,t)=2\pi \int_{5}^{t}{\exp [-(t-s)\lambda ]}S_{PP}\exp [-(t-s)\lambda ]ds \)

In which

\( S_{PP}=\theta ^{-1}A^{-1}S_{QQ}(\theta ^{-1}A^{-1})^{T}=\theta ^{-1}A^{-1}\begin{pmatrix}0 & 0 \\ 0 & S_{0} \end{pmatrix} (\theta ^{-1}A^{-})^{T} \)

Thus,

\( [K_{ZZ}(t,t)]_{jl}=\frac{2\pi [S_{PP}]_{jl}}{\lambda _{j}+\lambda _{l}} \left(1-e^{-(\lambda _{j}+\lambda _{l})(t-5)}\right) \)

The numerical result for the (1,1) and (2,2)elements of \( K_{YY}(t,t) \) give

\( Var[X(t)\mid X(5)=0.05,\dot{X}(5)=0]=[K_{YY}(t,t)]_{1,1}= \) \( \frac{\pi}{1000}\left[2.5-e^{-0.4(t-5)}(2.501-0.001\cos [20.00(t-5)]+0.05001\sin [20.00(t-5)])\right] \) \( Var[\dot{X}(t)\mid X(5)=0.05,\dot{X}(5)=0]=[K_{YY}(t,t)]_{22} \) \( \frac{\pi}{10}\left[2.5-e^{-0.4(t-5)}(2.501-0.001\cos [20.00(t-5)]-0.05001\sin [20.00(t-5)])\right] \)

These results are not sketched here because they have exactly the same form as the zero-start variances shown in Fig. 5.7.
