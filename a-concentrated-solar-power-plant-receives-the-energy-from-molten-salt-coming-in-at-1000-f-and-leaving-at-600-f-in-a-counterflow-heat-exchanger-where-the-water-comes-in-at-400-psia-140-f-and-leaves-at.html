Guest Question.

A concentrated solar power plant receives the energy from molten salt coming in at 1000 F and leaving at 600 F in a counterflow heat exchanger where the water comes in at 400 psia, 140 F and leaves at 800 F, 400 psia. The molten salt has 10 lbm/s flow with \(\text { Cp }\) = 0.36 Btu/lbm-R. What is the possible water flow rate, the rate of energy transfer and rate of entropy generation.

Admin
Solution

Continuity Eqs.: Each line has a constant flow rate through it.

Energy Eq.4.10: \(0=\dot{ m }_{ H _{2} O }\left( h _{1}- h _{2}\right)+\dot{ m }_{\text {salt }}\left( h _{3}- h _{4}\right)\)

Entropy Eq.7.7: \(0=\dot{ m }_{ H _{2} O }\left( s _{1}- s _{2}\right)+\dot{ m }_{\text {salt }}\left( s _{3}- s _{4}\right)+\dot{ S }_{ gen }\)

Process: Each line has a constant pressure.

Table F.7: \(h _{2}=1416.59 Btu / lbm , s _{1}=0.1985, s _{2}=1.6844 Btu / lbm – R\)

\(h _{1}= h _{ f }+\left( P _{3}- P _{\text {sat }}\right) v _{ f }=107.96+(400-2.9) 0.01629 \times 144 / 778=109.15 Btu / lbm\)

From the energy leaving the salt we get

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ Q } &=\dot{ m }_{\text {salt }}\left( h _{3}- h _{4}\right)=\dot{ m }_{\text {salt }} C _{ P \text { salt }}\left( T _{3}- T _{4}\right) \\&=10 \times 0.36[1000-600]= 1 4 4 0 Btu / s\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\dot{ m }_{ H _{2} O }=\dot{ m }_{\text {salt }} \frac{ h _{3}- h _{4}}{ h _{2}- h _{1}}=\frac{\dot{ Q }}{ h _{2}- h _{1}}=\frac{1440 Btu / s }{(1416.59-109.15) Btu / lbm }=1.1 lbm / s\)

 

\(s _{4}- s _{3}= C _{ P \text { salt }} \ln \left( T _{4} / T _{3}\right)=0.36 \ln (1059.7 / 1459.7)=-0.115 Btu / lbm – R\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ S }_{ gen } &=\dot{ m }_{ H _{2} O }\left( s _{2}- s _{1}\right)+\dot{ m }_{\text {salt }}\left( s _{4}- s _{3}\right) \\&=1.1 lbm / s (1.6844-0.1985) \frac{ B t u }{ lbm – R }-10 lbm / s \times 0.115 \frac{ Btu }{ lbm – R } \\&= 0 . 4 8 Btu / s – R\end{aligned}\)

 

C.V. Heat exchanger, steady flow 1 inlet and 1 exit for salt and water each. The two flows exchange energy with no heat transfer to/from the outside
