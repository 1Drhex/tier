Guest Question.

In a Brayton cycle gas turbine power plant the minimum and maximum temperature of the cycle are 300 K and 1200 K. The compression is carried out in two stages of equal pressure ratio with intercooling of the working fluid to the minimum temperature of the cycle after the first-stage of compression. The entire expansion is carried out in one stage only. The isentropic efficiency of both compressors is 0.85 and that of the turbine is 0.9. Determine the overall pressure ratio that would give the maximum net work per kg working fluid. Take \(\pmb{\gamma}\) = 1.4. [U.P.S.C. 1995]

Admin
Solution
\(\begin{aligned}\text{Given:} \qquad \qquad \qquad T_{5}&= 1200 K, T_{1} = 300 K \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} = \frac{P_{4}}{P_{3}}, T_{3} = 300 K, \eta_{isen, c} = 0.85\\\eta_{isen, T} &= 0.9, As \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} = \frac{P_{4}}{P_{3}} , so \frac{P_{4}}{P_{3}} = \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} = \sqrt{\frac{P_{4}}{P_{1}}} = \sqrt{r_{p}}\\\text{For process, 1-2,} \qquad \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}&= \left\lgroup\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}\right\rgroup ^{\frac{\gamma – 1}{\gamma} }\\\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}&= (r_{p})^{\frac{\gamma – 1}{2\gamma} }\\\text{For process, 3-4,} \qquad \frac{T_{4}}{T_{3}}&= \left\lgroup\frac{P_{4}}{P_{3}}\right\rgroup ^{\frac{\gamma – 1}{\gamma} } = (r_{p})^{\frac{\gamma – 1}{2\gamma}}\\\frac{T_{4}}{T_{1}}&= (r_{p})^{\frac{\gamma – 1}{2\gamma} }\\\text{For process, 5-6,} \qquad \frac{T_{5}}{T_{6}}&= \left\lgroup\frac{P_{4}}{P_{1}}\right\rgroup ^{\frac{\gamma – 1}{\gamma} }\\\frac{T_{5}}{T_{6}}&= (r_{p})^{\frac{\gamma – 1}{\gamma} } or, T_{6} = \frac{T_{5}}{(r_{p})^{\frac{\gamma – 1}{\gamma} }}\\\text{Compressor work,} \quad W_{C}&= W_{C1} + W_{C2}\\&= c_{p} (T_{2^{\prime}} – T_{1}) + c_{p} (T_{4^{\prime}} – T_{3})\\&= c_{p} (T_{2^{\prime}} – T_{1}) + c_{p} (T_{4^{\prime}} – T_{1})\\\text{Using Isentropic efficiency }&\text{of compressor,}\\η_{isen, c}&= \frac{T_{2} – T_{1}}{T_{2^{\prime}} – T_{1}} = \frac{T_{4} – T_{3}}{T_{4^{\prime}} – T_{3}} = \frac{T_{4} – T_{1}}{T_{4^{\prime}} – T_{1}}\\\text{Thus,} \qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad &\\W_{C}&= c_{p} . \frac{(T_{2} – T_{1})}{η_{isen, c}} + c_{p} \frac{(T_{4} – T_{1})}{η_{isen, c}}\\&= \frac{c_{p}}{η_{isen, c}}\left\{\left\lgroup r_{p}^{\frac{(\gamma – 1)}{2\gamma} } . T_{1} – T_{2}\right\rgroup + \left\lgroup r_{p}^{\frac{(\gamma – 1)}{2\gamma} } . T_{1} – T_{2}\right\rgroup \right\} \\W_{C}&= \frac{2c_{p} . T_{1} \left\lgroup r_{p}^{\frac{(\gamma – 1)}{2\gamma} } – 1\right\rgroup}{η_{isen, c}}\\\text{Turbine work,} \quad \quad W_{T}&= c_{p} · (T_{5} – T_{6^{\prime}})\\\text{Using isentropic efficiency }& \text{of turbine }η_{isen, T} = \frac{T_{5} – T_{6^{\prime}}}{T_{5} – T_{6}}\\W_{T} = c_{p} · (T_{5} – T_{6}) η_{isen, T}&= c_{p} · T_{5} · η_{isen, T} \left\lgroup 1 – \frac{1}{r_{p}^{\frac{\gamma – 1}{\gamma} }} \right\rgroup \\\text{Net work}\qquad \qquad\quad W_{net}&= W_{T} – T_{C}\\&= c_{p} · T_{5} · η_{isen, T} \left\lgroup 1 – \frac{1}{r_{p}^{\frac{\gamma – 1}{\gamma} }} \right\rgroup – \frac{2c_{p} . T_{1}\left\lgroup r_{p}^{\frac{\gamma – 1}{2\gamma}} – 1\right\rgroup }{η_{isen, c}}\\\text{Differentiating new work }&\text{with respect to }r_{p}\\\frac{d W_{net}}{d r_{p}}&= c_{p} · T_{5} · η_{isen, T} . \left\lgroup\frac{\gamma – 1}{\gamma} \right\rgroup . r_{p}^{\frac{1 – 2\gamma}{\gamma} } – \frac{2c_{p} . T_{1}}{\eta_{isen, c}} \left\lgroup\frac{\gamma – 1}{2\gamma} \right\rgroup . r_{p}^{\frac{- 1 – \gamma}{2\gamma} }\\\text{Putting,} \qquad \qquad \frac{d W_{net}}{d r_{p}}&= 0\\c_{p} · T_{5} · η_{isen, T} r_{p}^{\frac{1 – 2\gamma}{\gamma} }&= \frac{2c_{p} . T_{1}}{\eta_{isen, c}} . r_{p}^{\frac{(- 1 – \gamma)}{2\gamma} }\\r_{p}^{\frac{3(1 – \gamma)}{2\gamma} } &= \frac{T_{1}}{T_{5} . \eta_{isen, c} \eta_{isen, T}} ⇒ r_{p} = \left\lgroup\frac{T_{1}}{T_{5} . \eta_{isen, c} \eta_{isen, T}}\right\rgroup ^{\frac{2\gamma}{3(1 – \gamma)} }\\r_{p, opt}&= \left\lgroup\frac{T_{1}}{T_{5} . \eta_{isen, c} \eta_{isen, T}}\right\rgroup ^{\frac{2\gamma}{3(1 – \gamma)} }\\\text{Substituting known values,}&\\r_{p, opt}&= \left\lgroup\frac{300}{1200 × 0.85 × 0.90} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{2 × 1.4}{3(1 – 1.4)} } = 13.6\end{aligned}\\\text{Overall optimum pressure ratio}= \textbf{13.6}\)
