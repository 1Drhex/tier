Guest Question.

A 415 V, three-phase, 50 Hz, 4 pole, star-connected induction motor runs at 24 rev/s on full load. The rotor resistance and reactance per phase are 0.35 \(\Omega\) and 3.5 \(\Omega\) respectively, and the effective rotor-stator turns ratio is 0.85:1. Determine for the induction motor at full load, (a) the rotor current, (b) the rotor copper loss, and (c) the starting current.

Admin
Solution

(a) \(\textbf{Rotor current}\),

\(I_{r} =\frac{s\, (\frac{N_{2}}{N_{1}})\, E_{1}}{\sqrt{R_{2}^{2}+(sX_{2})^{2}}}\)

 

= \( \frac{(0.04)(0.85)(239.6)}{\sqrt{0.35^{2}+(0.04\times 3.5)^{2}}}\)

= \(\frac{8.1464}{0.37696} = 21.61\, A\)

(b) Rotor copper

loss per phase \(= I_{r}^{2}R_{2}\)
\(= (21.61)^{2}(0.35)\)
\(= 163.45\, W\)

\(\textbf{Total copper loss}\) (for 3 phases)
\(= 3 \times 163.45\)
\(= 490.35\, W\)

(c) Starting current,

\(I_{2} =\frac{(\frac{N_{2}}{N_{1}})\, E_{1}}{\sqrt{R_{2}^{2}+X_{2}^{2}}}=\frac{(0.85)(239.5)}{\sqrt{0.35^{2}+0.35^{2}}}\)

 

= \( 57.90\, A\)

(Note that the starting current of \(57.90\, A\) is considerably higher than the full load current of \(21.61\, A\))
