Guest Question.

Dilute A diffuses through a stagnant liquid film onto a plane surface consisting of \(\mathrm{B}\), reacts there to produce \(\mathrm{R}\) which diffuses back into the mainstream. Develop the overall rate expression for the L/S reaction

\(\mathrm{A}(l)+\mathrm{B}(s) \rightarrow \mathrm{R}(l)\)

which takes place on this flat surface, see Fig. \(\mathrm{E} 17.3 .\)

Admin
Solution

By diffusion, the flux of \(\mathrm{A}\) to the surface is

\(r_{\mathrm{A} 1}^{\prime}=\frac{1}{S} \frac{d N_{\mathrm{A}}}{d t}=-\frac{\mathscr{D}}{\Delta x}\left(C_{\mathrm{A} l}-C_{\mathrm{A} \mathrm{J}}\right)=k_{\mathrm{l}}\left(C_{\mathrm{N}}-C_{\mathrm{A}}\right) (i)\)

Reaction is first order with respect to \(\mathrm{A}\), so based on unit surface

\(r_{A 2}^{\prime}=\frac{1}{S} \frac{d N_{A}}{d t}=k^{\prime \prime} C_{\mathrm{A}} (ii)\)

At steady state the flow rate to the surface is equal to the reaction rate at the surface (steps in series). \(S o\)

\(r_{\mathrm{Al}}^{\prime}=r_{\mathrm{A}^{2}}^{\prime}\) \(k_{l}\left(C_{A}-C_{A t}\right)=k^{\prime \prime} C_{A S}\)

from which

\(C_{\mathrm{As}}=\frac{k_{l}}{k_{\mathrm{I}}+k^{\prime \prime}} C_{\mathrm{A}} (iii)\)

Replacing Eq. (iii) into either Eq. (i) or Eq. (ii) then eliminates \(C_{\mathrm{As}}\) which cannot be measured, giving

 

\({r}''_{A1}={r}''_{A2}={r}''_{A}=-\frac{1}{\frac{1}{K_{1}}+\frac{1}{{K}''}}C_{Al}=-k_{overall}C_{Al},\: \: [\frac{mol}{m^{2}.s}]\)

 

Comment This result shows that \(1 / k_{1}\) and \(1 / k^{\prime \prime}\) are additive resistances. It so happens that the addition of resistances to obtain on overall resistance is permissible only when the rate is a linear function of the driving force and when the processes occur in series.
