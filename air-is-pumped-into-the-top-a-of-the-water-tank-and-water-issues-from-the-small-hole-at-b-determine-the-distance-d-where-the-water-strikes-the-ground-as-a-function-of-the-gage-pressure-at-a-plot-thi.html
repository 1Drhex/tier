Guest Question.

Air is pumped into the top A of the water tank, and water issues from the small hole at B. Determine the distance d where the water strikes the ground as a function of the gage pressure at A. Plot this distance (vertical axis) versus the pressure\( P_A for 0 \leq P_A \leq 100 psi \). Give values for increments of \(\Delta P_A = 20 psi.\)

Admin
Solution

Since water can be considered as an ideal fluid (incompressible and inviscid) and the flow is steady, Bernoulli’s equation is applicable. Writing this equation between points A and B,

\(\frac{P_A}{\rho _w} + \frac{V_A^2}{2} + gz_A = \frac{P_B}{\rho _w} + \frac{V_B^2}{2} + gz_A \)

Since the water is discharged into the atmosphere at B, \(P_B = 0 \). Also, it is discharged from a large reservoir,\( V_A = 0.\) If we set the datum at B, \(Z_A = 4 ft and Z_B = 0 \).

\(\frac{P_A(\frac{12 in}{1 ft})^2}{(\frac{62.4 Ib/ft^3}{32.2 ft/s^2})} 0 + (32.2 ft/s^2)(4 ft) = 0 + \frac{V_B^2}{2} + 0 \)

 

\(V_B = 12148.62 p_A + 257.62 ft/s \)

Vertical Motion,

\(( + ) (s_C)y = (s_B)y + (V_B)_yt + \frac{1}{2}a_Ct^2 \)

 

\(2 ft = 0 + 0 + \frac{1}{2}(32.2 ft/s^2)t^2 \)

t = 0.3525 s

Horizontal Motion,

\((\overset{+}{\rightarrow}) 2 (s_C)_x = (s_B)_x + (V_B)_xt \)

 

\(d = 0 + (\sqrt{148.62 p_A + 257.62)} (0.3525 s) \)

 

\(d = (0.352 \sqrt{149.6P_A + 258.}) ft \) where \(P_A\) is in psi .
