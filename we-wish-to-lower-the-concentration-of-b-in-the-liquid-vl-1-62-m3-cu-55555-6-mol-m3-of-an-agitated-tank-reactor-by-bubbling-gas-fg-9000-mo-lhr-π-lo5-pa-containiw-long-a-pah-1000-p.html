Guest Question.

We wish to lower the concentration of \(\mathrm{B}\) in the liquid \(\left(V_{l}=1.62 \mathrm{~m}^{3}, C_{\mathrm{U}}=\right.\) \(55555.6 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\) ) of an agitated tank reactor by bubbling gas \(\left(F_{g}=9000 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{hr}\right.\), \(\pi=10^{5} \mathrm{~Pa}\) ) containing \(\mathrm{A}\left(p_{\mathrm{Ain}}=1000 \mathrm{~Pa}\right)\) through it. \(\mathrm{A}\) and \(\mathrm{B}\) react as follows:

\(\mathrm{A}(g \rightarrow l)+\mathrm{B}(l) \rightarrow \text { product }(l),-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime \prime \prime \prime}=k C_{\mathrm{A}} C_{\mathrm{B}}\)

(a) How long must we bubble gas through the vessel to lower the concentration

\(\text { from } C_{\mathrm{B} 0}=555.6 \text { to } C_{\mathrm{B} f}=55.6 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{m}^{3} ?\)

(b) What percent of entering A passes through the vessel unreacted?

Additional Data

\(\begin{array}{rlrl}k_{\mathrm{Ag}} a & =0.72 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{hr} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} \cdot \mathrm{Pa} & & f_{l} =0.9 \mathrm{~m}^{3} \text { liquid } / \mathrm{m}^{3} \text { total } \\k_{\mathrm{Al}} a & =144 \mathrm{hr}^{-1} & \mathscr{D}_{\mathrm{A}} & =\mathscr{D}_{\mathrm{B}}=3.6 \times 10^{-6} \mathrm{~m}^{2} / \mathrm{hr}, \quad a=100 \mathrm{~m}^{2} / \mathrm{m}^{3} \\H_{\mathrm{A}} & =10^{3} \mathrm{~Pa} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{mol} & k & =2.6 \times 10^{5} \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{mol} \cdot \mathrm{hr}\end{array}\)
Admin
Solution

Let us sketch what is known in Fig. E24.6.

At the start

Since \(p_{\mathrm{A}} \ll \pi\) and \(E=M_{H}\) the text says we can use the shortcut outlined above Eq. 19. Let us do it.

\(\begin{aligned}-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime \prime \prime \prime} &=p_{\mathrm{Ain}} /\left(\frac{\pi V_{r}}{F_{g}}+\frac{1}{k_{\mathrm{Ag}} a}+\frac{H_{\mathrm{A}}}{k_{\mathrm{A} l} a E}+\frac{H_{\mathrm{A}}}{k C_{\mathrm{B}} f_{l}}\right) \\&=1000 /\left(\frac{10^{5}(1.62)}{9000}+\frac{1}{0.72}+\frac{10^{3}}{144(15.84)}+\frac{10^{3}}{2.6 \times 10^{5}(555.6) 0.9}\right) \\&=50.44 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{m}^{3} \cdot \mathrm{hr}\end{aligned}\)

At the end, following a similar treatment, we find

\(\left.\begin{array}{c}M_{H}=5 \\E_{i}=55.6, \text { or higher }\end{array}\right\} E=M_{H}=5.0\)

The rate of reaction at the beginning and at the end of the run is just about the same, \(\mathrm{SO}\)

\(-r_{\mathrm{A}, \text { ave }}^{m \prime}=\frac{50.44+48.13}{2}=49.28\)

Thus the run time needed is

\(t=\frac{f_{l}}{b} \int_{C_{\mathrm{B} f}}^{C_{\mathrm{B} 0}} \frac{d C_{\mathrm{B}}}{-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime \prime \prime \prime}}=\frac{0.9(555.6-55.6)}{49.28}=\underline{\underline{9.13 \mathrm{hr}}}\)

The minimum time required is

\(t_{\min }=\frac{V_{l}\left(C_{\mathrm{B} 0}-C_{\mathrm{Bf}}\right)}{F_{g}\left(p_{\mathrm{Ain}} /\left(\pi-p_{\mathrm{Ain}}\right)\right)}=\frac{1.62(555.6-55.6)}{9000\left(1000 /\left(10^{5}-100\right)\right)}=\underline{\underline{8.91 \mathrm{hr}}}\)

Thus the fraction of reactant which passes through the tank untreated is

\(\text { Fraction }=\frac{9.13-8.91}{8.19}=0.025=\underline{\underline{2.5 \%}}\)
