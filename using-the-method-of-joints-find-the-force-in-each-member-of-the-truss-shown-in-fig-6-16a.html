Guest Question.

Using the method of joints, find the force in each member of the truss shown in Fig. 6.16(a).

Admin
Solution

The member DF is a two-force member, and the reaction at D is directed along the member. Members EG and EC lie along a straight line; therefore, the force in member ED is zero, and the forces in EG and EC are equal. The free-body diagram of the entire truss is shown in Fig. 6.16(b). For equilibrium of the entire truss, we have

\(ΣM_{G} = D_{x}(6.928) – 5(8) – 10(12) = 0\)

 

\(D_{x} = 23.09 \ kip \longrightarrow\)

 

\(ΣF_{x} = -G_{x} + D_{x} = 0\)

 

\(G_{x} = 23.09 \ kip \longleftarrow\)

 

\(ΣF_{y} = G_{y} – 5 – 10 = 0\)

 

\(G_{y} = 15 \ kip \uparrow\)

Check:

\(ΣM_{D} = G_{x}(6.928) – G_{y}(4) – 10(8) – 5(4)\)

= 160 – 60 – 80 – 20 =0 OK

In Fig. 6.16(c), the x and y components of a force with a slope of \(60^{\circ}\) are indicated, and in Fig. 6.17(a)–(e), the free-body diagrams of the joints are drawn so that each new free-body diagram contains no more than two unknown forces. The internal forces are all assumed to be tensile. Starting with joint A, we write the equilibrium equations for each new joint in the sequence.

Joint A [Fig. 6.17(a)]:

\(ΣF_{y} = 0.866F_{AC} – 10 = 0\)

 

\(F_{AC} = 11.55 \ kip\) (tension)

 

\(ΣF_{x} = -0.5F_{AC} – F_{AB} = 0\)

 

\(F_{AB} = -5.77 \ kip\) (compression)

Joint B [Fig. 6.17(b)]:

\(ΣF_{x}= F_{BA} – F_{BD} = 5.77 – F_{BD} = 0\)

 

\(F_{BD} = 5.77 \ kip\) (tension)

 

\(ΣF_{y} = F_{BC} – 5 = 0\)

 

\(F_{BC} = 5 \ kip\) (tension)

Joint C [Fig. 6.17(c)]:

\(ΣF_{y} = -0.866F_{CA} – F_{CB} – 0.866F_{CD}\)

 

\(= -0.866(11.55) – 5 – 0.866F_{CD} = 0\)

 

\(F_{CD} = -17.32 \ kip\) (compression)

 

\(ΣF_{x} = 0.5F_{CA} – 0.5F_{CD} – F_{CE}\)

 

\(= 0.5(11.55) – 0.5(-17.32) – F_{CE} = 0\)

 

\(F_{CE} = 14.44 \ kip\) (tension)

Joint E: Member ED is a zero-force member, and CE and EG are collinear; therefore,

\(F_{ED} = 0\)

and

\(F_{CE} = F_{EG} = 14.44 \ kip\) (tension)

Joint D [Fig. 6.17(d)]:

\(ΣF_{y} = 0.866F_{DC} + 0.866F_{GD} = 0\)

 

\(F_{GD} = -F_{DC} = -(-17.32) = 17.32 \ kip\) (tension)

First check:

\(ΣF_{x} = F_{DB} + 0.5F_{DC} + 23.09 – 0.5F_{GD}\)

= -5.78 + 0.5(-17.32) + 23.09 – 0.5(17.32) =0 OK

Joint G [Fig. 6.17(e)]:

Second check:

\(ΣF_{y} = 15 – 0.866F_{GD} = 15 – 0.866(17.32) =0\) OK

Third check:

\(ΣF_{x} = F_{GE} + 0.5F_{GD} – 23.09\)

= 14.44 + 0.5(17.32) – 23.09 = 0 OK

The results are shown on the truss in Fig. 6.18. Positive values indicate tension and negative values compression.
