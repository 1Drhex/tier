Guest Question.

The column shown in Figure 4.13 is subjected to a service dead load of 140 kips and a service live load of 420 kips. Use A992 steel and select a W-shape.

Admin
Solution

\(K_{x}L = 20\) ft and maximum \(K_{y}L = 8\) ft. The effective length \(K_{x}L\) will control whenever

\(\frac{K_{x}L}{r_{x}/r_{y}}\gt K_{y}L\)

or

\(r_{x}/r_{y}\lt \frac{K_{x}L}{K_{y}L}\)

In this example,

\(\frac{K_{x}L}{K_{y}L}=\frac{20}{8} =2.5\)

so \(K_{x}L\) will control if \(r_{x}/r_{y} < 2.5\). Since this is true for almost every shape in the column load tables, \(K_{x}L\) probably controls in this example.

Assume \(r_{x}/r_{y}=1.7:\)

\(\frac{K_{x}L}{r_{x}/r_{y}}=\frac{20}{1.7}=11.76\gt K_{y}L\)

\(P_{u} = 1.2D + 1.6L = 1.2(140) + 1.6(420) = 840\) kips

Enter the column load tables with KL = 12 feet. There are no W8 shapes with enough load capacity.

Try a W10 × 88 \((\phi _{c} P_{n} = 940\) kips):

Actual \(\frac{K_{x}L}{r_{x}/r_{y}}=\frac{20}{1.73}=11.56\) \(ft \lt 12\) ft

\(\therefore \phi _{c} P_{n} >\) required 840 kips

(By interpolation, \(\phi _{c} P_{n} = 955\) kips.)

Check a W12 × 79:

\(\frac{K_{x}L}{r_{x}/r_{y}}=\frac{20}{1.75}=11.43\) ft

\(\phi _{c} P_{n} =900\) kips \(> 840\) kips (OK)

Investigate W14 shapes. For \(r_{x}/r_{y} = 2.44\) (the approximate ratio for all likely possibilities),

\(\frac{K_{x}L}{r_{x}/r_{y}}=\frac{20}{2.44}=8.197\) ft \(> K_{y}L = 8\) ft

For KL = 9 ft, a W14 × 74, with a capacity of 854 kips, is the lightest W14-shape.
Since 9 feet is a conservative approximation of the actual effective length, this shape is satisfactory.

Use a W14 × 74 (lightest of the three possibilities).

\(P_{a} = D + L = 140 + 420 = 560\) kips

Enter the column load tables with KL = 12 feet. There are no W8 shapes with enough load capacity. Investigate a W10 × 88 (for KL = 12 ft, \(P_{n}/Ω_{c} = 625\) kips):

Actual \(\frac{K_{x}L}{r_{x}/r_{y}}=\frac{20}{1.73}=11.56\) ft \(< 12\) ft

\(\therefore \phi _{c} P_{n} >\) required 560 kips

(By interpolation, \(P_{n}/Ω_{c} = 635\) kips.)

Check a W12 × 79:

\(\frac{K_{x}L}{r_{x}/r_{y}}=\frac{20}{1.75}=11.43\) ft \(> K_{y}L = 8\) ft

\(\frac{P_{n}}{Ω_{c}}=599\) kips \(>560\) kips (OK)

Investigate W14 shapes. Try a W14 × 74:

\(\frac{K_{x}L}{r_{x}/r_{y}}=\frac{20}{2.44}=8.20 > K_{y}L = 8\) ft

For KL = 8.20 ft,

\(\frac{P_{n}}{Ω_{c}}=582\) kips \(>560\) kips (OK)

Use a W14 × 74 (lightest of the three possibilities).
