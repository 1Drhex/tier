Guest Question.

Forced convection. A hot air heating system is rated at \(8.40 × 10^7 J/hr\). If the heated air in the furnace reaches a temperature of 120 ºC, the room temperature is 15.6 ºC, and the fan can deliver \(7.00 m^3/min\), what is the thermal energy transfer per hour from the furnace to the room, and the efficiency of this system? The specific heat of air at constant pressure is \(c_{air} = 1009 J/kg ºC\) and the density of air is \(ρ = 1.29 kg/m^3\).

Admin
Solution

We find the thermal energy transfer per hour from equation 16.8 as

\(\frac{ΔQ}{Δt}= ρc\frac{ΔV}{Δt}(T_h -T_c)\) (16.8)

\(=\left\lgroup1.29\frac{kg}{m^3} \right\rgroup \left\lgroup 1009 \frac{J}{kg ºC} \right\rgroup \left\lgroup 7.00\frac{m^3}{min} \right\rgroup (120 ºC -15.6 ºC)\left\lgroup60\frac{ min}{hr} \right\rgroup\)

 

\(= 5.71 × 10^7 J/hr\)

We determine the efficiency, or rated value, of the heater as the ratio of the thermal energy out of the system to the thermal energy in, times 100%. Therefore,

\(E{ff}=\left\lgroup\frac{5.71\times 10^7 J/hr}{8.40 \times 10^7 J/hr} \right\rgroup(100\%)\)

= 67.9%
