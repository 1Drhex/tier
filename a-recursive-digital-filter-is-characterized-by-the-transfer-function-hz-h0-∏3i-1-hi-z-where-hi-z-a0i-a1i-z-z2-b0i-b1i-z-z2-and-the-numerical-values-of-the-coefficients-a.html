Guest Question.

A recursive digital filter is characterized by the transfer function

\(H(z)=H_{0} \prod_{i=1}^{3} H_{i}(z)\)

where

\(H_{i}(z)=\frac{a_{0 i}+a_{1 i} z+z^{2}}{b_{0 i}+b_{1 i} z+z^{2}}\)

and the numerical values of the coefficients are given in Table 5.4. The sampling frequency is 20 rad/s. (a) Construct the zero-pole plot of the filter. (b) Plot the surface |H(z)| as a function of \(z=\Re e z+j \Im m z\). (c) Obtain expressions for the amplitude and phase responses. (d) Plot the amplitude and phase responses first in terms of 3-D plots and then in terms of 2-D plots.

 

Table 5.4 Transfer-function coefficients for Example 5.11
i \(a_{0 i}\) \(a_{1i}\) \(b_{0 i}\) \(b_{1 i}\)
1 -1 0 8.131800E−1 7.870090E−8
2 1 -1.27526 9.211099E−1 5.484026E−1
3 1 1.275258 9.211097E−1 −5.484024E−1
\(H_{0}=1.763161 E-2\)

Solution

(a) The zeros and poles of the transfer function can be readily obtained as

 

\(z_{1}, z_{2}=\pm 1 \quad z_{3}, z_{4}=0.6376 \pm j 0.7703\)

 

\(z_{5}, z_{6}=-0.6376 \pm j 0.7703\)

 

and

 

\(p_{1}, p_{2}=\pm j 0.9018 \quad p_{3}, p_{4}=0.2742 \pm j 0.7703\)

 

\(p_{5}, p_{6}=-0.2742 \pm j 0.7703\)

 

respectively. Hence the zero-pole plot depicted in Fig. 5.13a can be readily constructed. Since there is a cluster of poles close to the unit circle at ωT ≈ π/2 and zeros at (1, 0) and (−1, 0), the recursive filter must be a bandpass filter which will select frequencies closed to ω = π/2T .

 

(b) The 3-D plot of 20 log |H(z)| versus z depicted in Fig. 5.13b demonstrates clearly that this is a bandpass filter.

 

(c) The frequency response of the filter can be obtained as

 

\(\left.H(z)\right|_{z \rightarrow e^{j \omega T}}=H\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)=M(\omega) e^{j \theta(\omega)}\)

 

with

 

\(M(\omega)=\left|H_{0}\right| \prod_{i=1}^{3}\left|H_{i}\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)\right|=\left|H_{0}\right| \prod_{i=1}^{3} M_{i}(\omega)\)

 

and

 

\(\theta(\omega)=\arg H_{0}+\sum_{i=1}^{3} \arg H_{i}\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)=\sum_{i=1}^{3} \theta_{i}(\omega)\)

 

where

 

\(M_{i}(\omega)=\left|H_{i}\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)\right|=\left|\frac{a_{0 i}+a_{1 i} e^{j \omega T}+e^{j 2 \omega T}}{b_{0 i}+b_{1 i} e^{j \omega T}+e^{j 2 \omega T}}\right|\)

 

\(=\left|\frac{\left(a_{0 i}+a_{1 i} \cos \omega T+\cos 2 \omega T\right)+j\left(a_{1 i} \sin \omega T+\sin 2 \omega T\right)}{\left(b_{0 i}+b_{1 i} \cos \omega T+\cos 2 \omega T\right)+j\left(b_{1 i} \sin \omega T+\sin 2 \omega T\right)}\right|\)

 

\(=\left[\frac{\left(a_{0 i}+a_{1 i} \cos \omega T+\cos 2 \omega T\right)^{2}+\left(a_{1 i} \sin \omega T+\sin 2 \omega T\right)^{2}}{\left(b_{0 i}+b_{1 i} \cos \omega T+\cos 2 \omega T\right)^{2}+\left(b_{1 i} \sin \omega T+\sin 2 \omega T\right)^{2}}\right]^{\frac{1}{2}}\)

 

\(=\left[\frac{1+a_{0 i}^{2}+a_{1 i}^{2}+2\left(1+a_{0 i}\right) a_{1 i} \cos \omega T+2 a_{0 i} \cos 2 \omega T}{1+b_{0 i}^{2}+b_{1 i}^{2}+2\left(1+b_{0 i}\right) b_{1 i} \cos \omega T+2 b_{0 i} \cos 2 \omega T}\right]^{\frac{1}{2}}\)

 

and

 

\(\theta_{i}(\omega)=\arg H_{i}\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)\)

 

\(=\arg \frac{a_{0 i}+a_{1 i} e^{j \omega T}+e^{j 2 \omega T}}{b_{0 i}+b_{1 i} e^{j \omega T}+e^{j 2 \omega T}}\)

 

\(=\arg \frac{\left(a_{0 i}+a_{1 i} \cos \omega T+\cos 2 \omega T\right)+j\left(a_{1 i} \sin \omega T+\sin 2 \omega T\right)}{\left(b_{0 i}+b_{1 i} \cos \omega T+\cos 2 \omega T\right)+j\left(b_{1 i} \sin \omega T+\sin 2 \omega T\right)}\)

 

\(=\tan ^{-1} \frac{a_{1 i} \sin \omega T+\sin 2 \omega T}{a_{0 i}+a_{1 i} \cos \omega T+\cos 2 \omega T}\)

 

\(-\tan ^{-1} \frac{b_{1 i} \sin \omega T+\sin 2 \omega T}{b_{0 i}+b_{1 i} \cos \omega T+\cos 2 \omega T}\)

 

The 3-D plots for the amplitude and phase responses are depicted in Fig. 5.13c and d and the corresponding 2-D plots can be readily obtained from the above expressions as shown in Fig. 5.13e and f . As can be seen from these plots, the system being analyzed is definitely a bandpass filter.
