Guest Question.

Find \(v_{o}\) of the circuit of Fig. 10.13 using the superposition theorem.

Admin
Solution

Since the circuit operates at three different frequencies (ω = 0 for the dc voltage source), one way to obtain a solution is to use superposition, which breaks the problem into single-frequency problems. So we let

\(v_{o}=v_{1}+v_{2}+v_{3}\) (10.6.1)

where \(v_{1}\) is due to the 5-V dc voltage source, \(v_{2}\) is due to the \(10 \cos 2t V\) voltage source, and \(v_{3}\) is due to the \(2 \sin 5t A\) current source.
To find \(v_{1}\) , we set to zero all sources except the 5 -V dc source. We recall that at steady state, a capacitor is an open circuit to dc while an inductor is a short circuit to dc. There is an alternative way of looking at this. Since ω = 0, jωL= 0,1 / jωC = ∞ . Either way, the equivalent circuit is as shown in Fig. 10.14(a) . By voltage division,

\(-v_{1}=\frac{1}{1 + 4}(5)=1 V\) (10.6.2)

To find \(v_{2}\) , we set to zero both the 5 – V source and the \(2 \sin 5t\) current source and transform the circuit to the frequency domain.

\(\begin{aligned}10 \cos 2 t & \Rightarrow \quad 10 \angle 0^{\circ}, \quad \omega= 2 rad / s \\2 H & \Rightarrow \quad j \omega L = j 4 \Omega \\0.1 F & \Rightarrow \quad \frac{1}{j \omega C} = -j 5 \Omega\end{aligned}\)

The equivalent circuit is now as shown in Fig. 10.14(b). Let

\(\mathbf{Z}=-j 5 \| 4=\frac{-j 5 \times 4}{4 – j 5}=2.439 – j 1.951\)

By voltage division,

\(\mathbf{V}_{2}=\frac{1}{1 + j 4 + \mathbf{Z} }\left(10 \angle 0^{\circ}\right)=\frac{10}{3.439+j 2.049}=2.498 \angle -30.79^{\circ}\)

In the time domain,

\(v_{2}=2.498 \cos \left(2 t – 30.79^{\circ}\right)\) (10.6.3)

To obtain \(v_{3}\), we set the voltage sources to zero and transform what is left to the frequency domain.

\(\begin{aligned}2 \sin 5 t & \Rightarrow 2 \angle -90^{\circ}, \quad \omega= 5 rad / s \\2 II & \Rightarrow j \omega L = j 10 \Omega \\0.1 F & \Rightarrow \quad \frac{1}{j \omega C}= -j 2 \Omega\end{aligned}\)

The equivalent circuit is in Fig. 10.14(c). Let

\(\mathbf{Z} _{1}= -j 2 \| 4=\frac{-j 2 \times 4}{4 – j 2}=0.8 – j 1.6 \Omega\)

By current division,

 

\(\mathbf{I}_{1}=\frac{j 10}{j 10 + 1 + \mathbf{Z}_{1}}\left(2 \angle-90^{\circ}\right) A\)

 

\(\mathbf{V}_{3}= \mathbf{I}_{1} \times 1=\frac{j 10}{1.8 + j 8.4}(-j 2)= 2.328 \angle-80^{\circ} V\)

 

In the time domain,

\(v_{3}=2.33 \cos \left(5 t-80^{\circ}\right)=2.33 \sin \left(5 t + 10^{\circ}\right) V\) (10.6.4)

Substituting Eqs. (10.6.2) to (10.6.4) into Eq. (10.6.1), we have

\( v_{o}(t)=-1 + 2.498 \cos \left(2 t – 30.79^{\circ}\right) + 2.33 \sin \left(5 t + 10^{\circ}\right) V\)
