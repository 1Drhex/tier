Guest Question.

An 8-inch-long WT10.5 \(\times\) 66 is attached to the bottom flange of a beam as shown in Figure . This hanger must support a service dead load of 20 kips and a service live load of 60 kips. Determine the number of 7⁄8-inch-diameter, Group A bolts required and investigate the adequacy of the tee. A992 steel is used.

Admin
Solution

Compute the constants that are based on the geometry of the connection.
\(b = \frac{(5.5 – 0.650)}{2}= 2.425 in.\)

\(a = \frac{(12.4 2 5.5)}{2}= 3.450 in.\)

Since 1.25b = 1.25(2.425) = 3.031 in. < 3.450 in., use a = 3.031 in.

 

\({b}'=b – \frac{d}{2}= 2.425 – \frac{7/8}{2} = 1.988 in.\)

 

\({a}'=a + \frac{d}{2}= 3.031 +\frac{7/8}{2}=3.469 in.\)

 

\({d}'=d + \frac{1}{8}= \frac{1}{8}+\frac{7}{8}= 1 in.\)

 

\(\delta =1-\frac{{d}'}{p}=1-\frac{1}{4}= 0.75\)
The bolt cross-sectional area will also be needed in subsequent calculations:
\(A_b = \frac{\pi (7/8)^{2}}{4}= 0.6013 in.^{2}\)
The design strength of one bolt is

 

\(B = \phi r_n =\phi F_{t}A_{b} = 0.75(90.0)(0.6013) = 40.59 kips\)
The total factored load is
1.2D + 1.6L = 1.2(20) + 1.6(60) = 120 kips
and the number of bolts required (without considering prying action) is 120/40.59 = 2.96. A minimum of four bolts will be needed to maintain symmetry, so try four bolts. The factored external load per bolt, excluding prying force, is T = 120/4 = 30 kips.
\(s = \frac{8}{2}= 4 in.\)

\(p = 3.5b = 3.5(2.425) = 8.488 in. > 4 in. \therefore \)Use p = 4 in.

\(2b = 2(2.425) = 4.85 in. > 4 in. \therefore\) Use p = 4 in.
Compute \alpha :

\(\frac{B}{T} – 1 = \frac{40.59}{30} – 1 = 0.353\)
From Equation 7.15,

 

\(\alpha =\frac{[(B/T-1)]({a}'/{b}')}{\delta \left \{ 1-[(B/T-1)]({a}'/{b}') \right \}} =\frac{0.353(3.469/1.988)}{0.75[1 – 0.353(3.469/1.988)]}= 2.139\)
Because \(| \alpha | > 1.0, use \alpha \)= 1.0. From Equation 7.18,
Required \(t_f = \sqrt{\frac{4T{b}'}{\Phi _{b}pF_{u}(1+\delta \alpha )}}=\sqrt{\frac{4(30)(1.988)}{0.90(4)(65)[1 + 0.75(1.0)]}}\)
= 0.763 in. < 1.04 in. (OK)
Both the number of bolts selected and the flange thickness are adequate, and no further computations are required. To illustrate the procedure, however, we compute the prying force, using Equations 7.20 and 7.14. From Equation 7.20,
\(\alpha =\frac{1}{\delta }\left ( \frac{4T{b}'}{\Phi _{b}pt_{f}^{2}F_{u}(1+\delta \alpha )} – 1 \right )=\frac{1}{0.75}\left ( \frac{4(30)(1.988)}{0.90(4)(1.04)^{2}(65)} -1\right )=-0.07657\)
Since a must be between 0 and 1 inclusive, use a = 0.
From Equation 7.14, the total bolt force, including prying, is
\(B_{c}=T\left [ 1+\frac{\delta \alpha }{(1+\delta \alpha )}\frac{{b}'}{{a}'}\right ]=30\left [ 1 + \frac{0.75(0)}{(1 + 0.75 \times 0)}\left ( \frac{1.988}{3.469} \right ) \right ]= 30 kips\)
The prying force is
\(q = B_c – T = 30 – 30 = 0 kips\)
This indicates that the tee section is not flexible enough to produce prying action.
A WT10.5 \(\times\) 66 is satisfactory. Use four 7⁄8-inch-diameter Group A bolts.
The allowable tensile strength of one bolt is
\(B = \frac{R_{n}}{\Omega} = \frac{F_{t}A_{b}}{\Omega}= \frac{90.0(0.6013)}{2.00} = 27.06 kips\)
The total applied load is
D + L = 20 + 60 = 80 kips
and the number of bolts required (without considering prying action) is 80/27.06 =2.96. A minimum of four bolts will be needed to maintain symmetry, so try four bolts. The external load per bolt, excluding prying force, is T = 80/4 = 20 kips.
Compute \(\alpha:\)
\(\frac{B}{T} – 1 = \frac{27.06}{20} – 1 =0.353\)

 

\(\alpha =\frac{[(B/T-1)]({a}'/{b}')}{\delta \left \{ 1-[(B/T-1)]({a}'/{b}') \right \}}\) \(=\frac{0.353(3.469/1.988)}{0.75[1 – 0.353(3.469/1.988)]}=2.139\)
Because \(| \alpha | > \)1.0, use

 

\(\alpha = 1.0\). From Equation 7.19,
Required \(t_f = \sqrt{\frac{\Omega _{b}4T{b}'}{pF_{u}(1+\delta \alpha )}}=\sqrt{\frac{1.67(4)(30)(1.988)}{4(65)[1 + 0.75(1.0)]}}\)
= 0.936 in. < 1.04 in. (OK)
Determine the prying force (this is not required). From Equation 7.21,
\(\alpha =\frac{1}{\delta }\left ( \frac{\Omega _{b}4T{b}'}{pt_{f}^{2}F_{u})} -1 \right )=\frac{1}{ 0.75}\left ( \frac{1.67(4)(20)(1.988)}{4(1.04)^{2}(65) } – 1 \right )=-0.07406\)
Since a must be between 0 and 1 inclusive, use a = 0.
\(B_{c}=T\left [ 1+\frac{\delta \alpha }{(1+\delta \alpha )}\frac{{b}'}{{a}'}\right ]=20\left [ 1 + \frac{0.75(0)}{(1 + 0.75 \times 0)}\left ( \frac{1.988}{3.469} \right ) \right ]= 20 kips\)
The prying force is

\(q = B_{c} – T = 20 – 20 = 0 kips\)
This indicates that the tee section is not flexible enough to produce prying action.
A WT10.5 \(\times\) 66 is satisfactory. Use four 7/8-inch-diameter Group A bolts.
