Guest Question.

A mixture of 60% carbon dioxide and 40% water by mass is flowing at 400 K, 100 kPa into a reversible adiabatic compressor, where it is compressed to 1000 kPa. Find the exit temperature using (a) constant specific heats and (b) variable specific heats.

Admin
Solution

Energy Eq.: \(0=h_{i}-h_{e}+w_{c \text { in }}\)

Entropy Eq.: \(0=s_{i}-s_{e}+0+0\)

The exit states is therefore determined as

Exit state: \(P_{e}, s_{e}=s_{i}\)

The mixture gas constant \(R_{\operatorname{mix}}\) and specific heat \(C_{p \text { mix }}\) become

\(R_{\operatorname{mix}}=0.6 \times 0.1889+0.4 \times 0.4615=0.29794 kJ / kg – K\)

\(C_{p \text { mix }}=0.6 \times 0.842+0.4 \times 1.872=1.254 kJ / kg – K\)

\(k_{\operatorname{mix}}=1.254 /(1.254-0.29794)=1.3116\)

From Eq. 6.23 we get

\(\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}=\left(\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}\right)^{(k-1) / k} (6.23)\)

\(T_{e}=T_{i}\left(P_{e} / P_{i}\right)^{(k-1) / k}=400 K \left(\frac{1000}{100}\right)^{0.3116 / 1.3116}=691.25 K\)

For variable specific heatswe must use the tabulated standard entropy functions to evaluate the change in entropy.

\(s_{i}-s_{e}=\sum c_{j}\left(s_{T i}^{0}-s_{T e}^{0}\right)_{j}-R_{\operatorname{mix}} \ln \left(P_{e} / P_{i}\right)=0\)

\(=0.6\left(s_{T e CO _{2}}^{0}-5.1196\right)+0.4\left(s_{ Te H _{2} O }^{0}-11.0345\right)-0.29794 \ln \left(\frac{1000}{100}\right)\)

Arrange the entropy equation with knowns on the right-hand side as

\(0.6 s_{T e CO _{2}}^{0}+0.4 s_{T e H _{2} O }^{0}=0.6 \times 5.1196+0.4 \times 11.0345+0.29794 \ln \left(\frac{1000}{100}\right)\)

\(=8.17159 kJ / kg – K\)

The left-hand-side \(T_{e}\) is found by trial and error as

\(\begin{array}{ll}\text { LHS at } 650 K =8.16466 kJ / kg – K ; & \text { LHS at } 700 K =8.2754 kJ / kg – K\end{array}\)

Now interpolate for the final result:

\(T_{e}=650 K +50 K \frac{8.17159-8.16466}{8.2754-8.16466}=653.1 K\)
