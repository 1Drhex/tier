Guest Question.

A long rectangular bar 3 × 3 cm made of stainless steel (type 304) is initially at 95°C. It is submerged in water whose temperature is 17°C. Determine the time it takes for the center temperature 4 cm from the bottom to reach 50°C. Assume a convection coefficient of 50 W/(\(m^{2}\) · K).

Admin
Solution

A “long” bar suggests that the length is infinite. Although the bar in reality is not infinite in length, we can obtain a solution (an estimate of the temperature) by modeling it as such. Referring to Table 4.2, we apply the semi-infinite rectangular bar solution with \(2L_{1} = 2L_{2} = 2L\), because the bar is square in cross section.

 

Assumptions

 

1. The convection coefficient is constant.
2. Material properties are constant.
3. The semi-infinite bar model applies. For stainless steel, we read from Appendix Table B.2

 

\(\rho =7817\, kg/m^{3}\)

 

\(k=14.4\, W/(m\cdot K)\)

 

\(c=461\, J/(kg\cdot K)\)

 

\(\alpha =0.387\times 10^{-5}\, m^{2}/s\)

 

We will use the infinite plate solution \(P(\alpha t/L^{2})\), and the semi-infinite slab solution \(S(L_{3}, t)\) in which \(L_{3} = 4 cm\). We thus calculate

 

Infinite plate

 

\(L=\frac{0.03}{2}=0.015\, m\)

 

\(\frac{1}{Bi}=\frac{k}{\bar{h_{c}}L}=\frac{14.4}{50(0.015)}=19.2\)

 

\(Fo=\frac{\alpha t}{L^{2}}=\frac{0.387\times 10^{-5}t}{(0.015)^{2}}=0.017\,t\)

 

Semi-infinite solid

 

\(Bi\sqrt{Fo}=\frac{\bar{h_{c}}\sqrt{\alpha t}}{k}=\frac{50 \sqrt{(0.387\times 10^{-5}t)} }{14.4}=6.83\times10^{-3}t^{1/2}\)

 

\(\frac{x}{2\sqrt{\alpha t}}=\frac{0.04}{2\sqrt{(0.387\times 10^{-5}t)}}=\frac{10.16}{t^{1/2}}\)

 

The time at this point is unknown. Consequently, we will have to resort to a trial-and-error procedure that sounds unappealing but is immensely rewarding when a solution is obtained. The following serves as an example of the procedure:

 

First trial—assume a time of 1500 s, then

 

Infinite plate (Figure 4.6a)

\(\left.\begin{array}{rl} 1 / \mathrm{Bi} & =19.2 \\ \mathrm{Fo} & =0.017 t=25.8 \end{array}\right\} \frac{T_{\mathrm{c}}-T_{\infty}}{T_{\mathrm{i}}-T_{\infty}} \approx 0.34\)

 

Semiinfinite solid (Figure 4.12 ) \(\left.\begin{array}{l} \mathrm{Bi} \sqrt{\mathrm{Fo}}=0.374 \\ \frac{x}{2 \sqrt{\alpha t}}=0.186 \end{array}\right\} \frac{T-T_{\mathrm{i}}}{T_{\infty}-T_{\mathrm{i}}} \approx 0.14\)

 

The temperature at a depth of 4 cm then is calculated as

 

\((P_{L=3\,cm})\cdot(P_{L=3\,cm})\cdot(S_{L=4\,cm})=\frac{T_{c}-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}\cdot\frac{T_{c}-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}\cdot\left ( 1-\frac{T-T_{i}}{T_{\infty}-T_{i}} \right )\)

 

or

 

\(\frac{T-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}=(0.34)(0.34)(1-0.14)=0.099\)

 

Solving for temperature, we find

 

\(T_{L=4\, cm}=0.099(95-17)+17=17.4^{\circ}C\)

 

Thus, 1500 s is too long a time; we are seeking the time it takes for the point of interest to reach 50°C. The result for other times is provided in the following table:

In using the graphs, it quickly becomes apparent that obtaining an accurate result from Figure 4.6a is extremely difficult because of the region we are working in. In view of this shortcoming, we remember that 350 s is an estimate of the time required for the temperature at the point of interest to be within 10% of 50°C.
