Guest Question.

The cross-section of an earth dam on an impermeable base is shown in Fig. 9.29. The stability of the downstream slope is to be investigated using the slip circle shown. Given:

\(\begin{aligned}\gamma_{\text {sat }} &=19.5 kN / m ^{3} \\c^{\prime} &=9 kN / m ^{2} \\\phi^{\prime} &=27^{\circ} \\r &=9 m . \\\theta &=88^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

For this circle determine the factor of safety by the conventional approach, as well as the rigorous one.

Admin
Solution

The following \(r_{u}\)values obtained from a flow net and weights of slices are given:

\(\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|}\hline \text { Slice No. } & 1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 \\\hline r_{u} & 0.360 & 0.420 & 0.375 & 0.300 & 0.075 \\\hline W(k N) & 42 & 114 & 150 & 162 & 75 \\\hline\end{array}\)

(b) Rigorous approach:
The calculations for the rigorous approach are set out in the tabular form (Table 9.3).

F with the first approximation \(=\frac{294.45}{209.08}=1.41\)

Columns (2) and (3) are recalculated with an F value of 1.4.

F with the second approximation \(=\frac{290.55}{209.08}=1.39\)

which is very near the assumed value of F, i.e. 1.4 Thus, the factor of safety may be taken as 1.4 by the rigorous approach.
