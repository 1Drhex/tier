Guest Question.

A jet plane uses a diverging passage as a diffuser (Figure P4.11). For a flight Mach number of 1.8, determine the range of back pressures over which a normal shock will appear in the diffuser. Ambient pressure and temperature are 25 kPa and 220 K. Find the mass flow range handled by the diffuser for the determined back pressure range. Also, the inlet and exit area are \(A_{i}=250 cm ^{2}, A_{e}=500 cm ^{2}\). Assume isentropic flow except for the shocks. Take Î³ = 1.4.

Admin
Solution

For a shock at the inlet, with \(M _{1}=1.8 \text { and } \gamma=1.4\), from the normal shock relations

 

\(\frac{ p _{ o 2}}{ p _{ o 1}}=0.8127=\frac{ A _{1}^{*}}{ A _{2}^{*}}\)

 

\(\frac{ A _{ e }}{ A _{2}^{*}}=\frac{ A _{ e }}{ A _{ i }} \frac{ A _{ i }}{ A _{1}^{*}} \frac{ A _{1}^{*}}{ A _{2}^{*}}=\left(\frac{500}{250}\right)(1.4390)(0.8127)=2.3390\)

 

From this area ratio, we can determine the exit Mach number and therefore the exit static to total pressure ratio

 

\(M _{ e }=0.2574, \quad \frac{ p _{ e }}{ p _{ o 2}}=0.9550\)

 

The following pressure ratio string may be readily formed

 

\(p _{ e }=\frac{ p _{ e }}{ p _{ o 2}} \frac{ p _{ o 2}}{ p _{ o 1}} \frac{ p _{ o1 }}{ p _{ i }} p _{ i }=(0.9550)(0.8127) \frac{1}{0.1740}(25)=111.5127 kPa\)

 

Next the mass flow rate is computed

 

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ m }=\frac{ p }{ RT } AV &=\frac{25}{0.287(220)}\left(250 \times 10^{-4}\right)(1.8 \sqrt{1.4(287) 220}) \\&=\left(0.3959 kg / m ^{3}\right)\left(0.0250 m ^{2}\right)(535.1664 m / s ) \\&=5.2974 kg / s\end{aligned}\)

 

For a shock at the exit,

 

\(\frac{A_{1}}{A_{1}^{*}}=\frac{A_{ e }}{A_{ i }} \frac{A_{ i }}{A_{ i }^{*}}=\frac{500}{250}(1.4390)=2.8780\)

 

\(M _{1}=2.5934, \frac{ p _{1}}{ p _{ o 1}}=0.0506, \frac{ p _{2}}{ p _{1}}=7.6799\)

 

\(p _{ e }=\frac{ p _{2}}{ p _{1}} \frac{ p _{1}}{ p _{ o 1}} \frac{ p _{ o 1}}{ p _{\infty}} p _{\infty}=(7.6799)(0.0506)\left(\frac{1}{0.1740}\right)(25)=55.8338 kPa\)

 

The diffuser is choked so it passes the same mass flow for the back pressure range,

 

\(\dot{ m }=5.2974 kg / s\)

 

A shock appears in the diffuser for: 55.8338 kPa \(< p _{ b }<111.5127\) kPa
