Guest Question.

A W530 × 66 structural steel [E = 200 GPa] wide-flange shape is loaded and supported as shown in Fig. P11.67. A uniformly distributed load of 70 kN/m is applied to the beam, causing the roller support at B to settle downward (i.e., displace downward) by 10 mm. Determine:
(a) the reactions at supports A and B.
(b) the maximum bending stress in the beam.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:
\(\begin{array}{ccc}\text { W530 } \times 66: & I=351 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4} & d=526 mm & S=1,340 \times 10^{3} mm ^{3}\end{array}\)

 

(a) Reactions at supports A and B.
The reaction force at B will be taken as the redundant, leaving a cantilever beam between A and C as the released beam.

 

Downward deflection of beam at B due to 70 kN/m uniformly distributed load.
[Appendix C, Cantilever beam with uniformly distributed load.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{B}=-\frac{w x^{2}}{24 E I}\left(6 L^{2}-4 L x+x^{2}\right) \text { (elastic curve) }\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&w=70 kN / m , L=6 m , x=4.5 m, \\&E I=70,200 kN – m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Calculation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{B} &=-\frac{w x^{2}}{24 E I}\left(6 L^{2}-4 L x+x^{2}\right) \\&=-\frac{(70 kN / m )(4.5 m )^{2}}{24\left(70,200 kN – m ^{2}\right)}\left[6(6 m )^{2}-4(6 m )(4.5 m )+(4.5 m )^{2}\right]=-0.107903 m\end{aligned}\)

 

 

Upward deflection of beam at B due to reaction force \(B_{y}\).
[Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated load at tip.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{B}=-\frac{P L^{3}}{3 E I}\)

Values:

\(P=-B_{y}, L=4.5 m , E I=70,200 kN – m ^{2}\)

Calculation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{B} &=-\frac{P L^{3}}{3 E I} \\&=-\frac{\left(-B_{y}\right)(4.5 m )^{3}}{3\left(70,200 kN – m ^{2}\right)}=\left(432.6923 \times 10^{-6} m / kN \right) B_{y}\end{aligned}\)

 

Compatibility equation at B:
The sum of the downward deflection caused by the uniformly distributed load and the upward deflection caused by the reaction force By must equal the support settlement:

\(\begin{aligned}&-0.107903 m +\left(432.6923 \times 10^{-6} m / kN \right) B_{y}=-0.010 m \\&\therefore B_{y}=\frac{97.90264 \times 10^{-3} m }{432.6923 \times 10^{-6} m / kN }=226.2639 kN =226 kN \uparrow\end{aligned}\)

 

Equilibrium equations for entire beam:

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{A}=-M_{A} &+B_{y}(4.5 m )-(70 kN / m )(6 m )(3 m )=0 \\\therefore M_{A} &=(226.2639 kN )(4.5 m )-(70 kN / m )(6 m )(3 m ) \\&=-241.8125 kN – m =242 kN – m ( ccw )\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma F_{y}=& A_{y}+B_{y}-(70 kN / m )(6 m )=0 \\& \therefore A_{y}=(70 kN / m )(6 m )-226.2639 kN =193.7361 kN =193.7 kN \uparrow\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Determine maximum bending stress in beam:

Maximum bending moment magnitude

\(M_{\max }=241.8125 kN – m (\text { at } A)\)

Bending stress at maximum moment

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\frac{(241.8125 kN – m )(526 mm / 2)(1,000)^{2}}{351 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}} \\&=181.187 MPa =181.2 MPa\end{aligned}\)

or using the tabulated value for the section modulus:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\frac{(241.8125 kN – m )(1,000)^{2}}{1,340 \times 10^{3} mm ^{3}} \\&=180.457 MPa =180.5 MPa\end{aligned}\)
