Guest Question.

Consider the Stark effect () for the n = 3 states of hydrogen. There are initially nine degenerate states, \( \psi_{3 \ell m} \)(neglecting spin, as before), and we turn on an electric field in the z direction.

(a) Construct the 9 × 9 matrix representing the perturbing Hamiltonian. Partial answer: \( \langle 300|z| 310\rangle=-3 \sqrt{6} a,\langle 310|z| 320\rangle=-3 \sqrt{3} a \) , \( \langle 31 \pm 1|z| 32 \pm 1\rangle=-(9 / 2) a \).

(b) Find the eigenvalues, and their degeneracies.

Admin
Solution

(a) The nine states are:

\( \left\{\begin{aligned} \underline{\ell=0}:|300\rangle &=R_{30} Y_{0}^{0} \\ \underline{\ell=1}:|311\rangle &=R_{31} Y_{1}^{1} \\ |310\rangle &=R_{31} Y_{1}^{0} \\ |31-1\rangle &=R_{31} Y_{1}^{-1} \\ \underline{\ell=2}:|322\rangle &=R_{32} Y_{2}^{2} \\ |321\rangle &=R_{32} Y_{2}^{1} \\ |320\rangle &=R_{32} Y_{2}^{0} \\ |32-1\rangle &=R_{32} Y_{2}^{-1} \\ |32-2\rangle &=R_{32} Y_{2}^{-2} \end{aligned}\right. \) \( H_{s}^{\prime} \text { contains no } \phi \text { dependence, so the } \phi \text { integral will be: } \)

\( \left\langle n \ell m\left|H_{s}^{\prime}\right| n^{\prime} \ell^{\prime} m^{\prime}\right\rangle=\{\cdots\} \int_{0}^{2 \pi} e^{-i m \phi} e^{i m^{\prime} \phi} d \phi, \quad \text { which is zero unless } \quad m^{\prime}=m \).

For diagonal elements: \( \left\langle n \ell m\left|H_{s}^{\prime}\right| n \ell m\right\rangle=\{\cdots\} \int_{0}^{\pi}\left[P_{\ell}^{m}(\cos \theta)\right]^{2} \cos \theta \sin \theta d \theta \) But (p. 136 in the text) \( P_{\ell}^{m} \) is a polynomial (even or odd) in \( \cos θ \), multiplied (if m is odd) by \( \sin θ \). Since \( \sin ^{2} \theta=1-\cos ^{2} \theta \), \( \left[P_{\ell}^{m}(\cos \theta)\right]^{2} \) is a polynomial in even powers of \(\cos θ\). So the θ integral is of the form \( \int_{0}^{\pi}(\cos \theta)^{2 j+1} \sin \theta d \theta=-\left.\frac{(\cos \theta)^{2 j+2}}{(2 j+2)}\right|_{0} ^{\pi}=0 \). All diagonal elements are zero.

There remain just 4 elements to calculate:

\( m=m^{\prime}=0:\left\langle 300\left|H_{s}^{\prime}\right| 310\right\rangle,\left\langle 300\left|H_{s}^{\prime}\right| 320\right\rangle,\left\langle 310\left|H_{s}^{\prime}\right| 320\right\rangle ; m=m^{\prime}=\pm 1:\left\langle 31 \pm 1\left|H_{s}^{\prime}\right| 32 \pm 1\right\rangle \).

\( \left\langle 300\left|H_{s}^{\prime}\right| 310\right\rangle=e E_{\text {ext }} \int R_{30} R_{31} r^{3} d r \int Y_{0}^{0} Y_{1}^{0} \cos \theta \sin \theta d \theta d \phi \). From Table 4.7 :

Table 4.7: The first few radial wave functions for hydrogen \( R_{n \ell}(r) \).
\( R_{10}=2 a^{-3 / 2} \exp (-r / a) \).
\( R_{20}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} a^{-3 / 2}\left(1-\frac{1}{2} \frac{r}{a}\right) \exp (-r / 2 a) \).

\( R_{21}=\frac{1}{2 \sqrt{6}} a^{-3 / 2}\left(\frac{r}{a}\right) \exp (-r / 2 a) \).
\( R_{30}=\frac{2}{3 \sqrt{3}} a^{-3 / 2}\left(1-\frac{2}{3} \frac{r}{a}+\frac{2}{27}\left(\frac{r}{a}\right)^{2}\right) \exp (-r / 3 a) \).

\( R_{31}=\frac{8}{27 \sqrt{6}} a^{-3 / 2}\left(1-\frac{1}{6} \frac{r}{a}\right)\left(\frac{r}{a}\right) \exp (-r / 3 a) \).

\( R_{32}=\frac{4}{81 \sqrt{30}} a^{-3 / 2}\left(\frac{r}{a}\right)^{2} \exp (-r / 3 a) \).
\( R_{40}=\frac{1}{4} a^{-3 / 2}\left(1-\frac{3}{4} \frac{r}{a}+\frac{1}{8}\left(\frac{r}{a}\right)^{2}-\frac{1}{192}\left(\frac{r}{a}\right)^{3}\right) \exp (-r / 4 a) \).

\( R_{41}=\frac{5}{16 \sqrt{15}} a^{-3 / 2}\left(1-\frac{1}{4} \frac{r}{a}+\frac{1}{80}\left(\frac{r}{a}\right)^{2}\right)\left(\frac{r}{a}\right) \exp (-r / 4 a) \).

\( R_{42}=\frac{1}{64 \sqrt{5}} a^{-3 / 2}\left(1-\frac{1}{12} \frac{r}{a}\right)\left(\frac{r}{a}\right)^{2} \exp (-r / 4 a) \).

\( R_{43}=\frac{1}{768 \sqrt{35}} a^{-3 / 2}\left(\frac{r}{a}\right)^{3} \exp (-r / 4 a) \).

\( \int R_{30} R_{31} r^{3} d r=\frac{2}{\sqrt{27}} \frac{1}{a^{3 / 2}} \frac{8}{27 \sqrt{6}} \frac{1}{a^{3 / 2}} \frac{1}{a} \int\left(1-\frac{2 r}{3 a}+\frac{2 r^{2}}{27 a^{2}}\right) e^{-r / 3 a}\left(1-\frac{r}{6 a}\right) r e^{-r / 3 a} r^{3} d r \).

\( \text { Let } \quad x \equiv 2 r / 3 a \text { : } \)

\( \int R_{30} R_{31} r^{3} d r=\frac{2^{4}}{3^{5} \sqrt{2} a^{4}}\left(\frac{3 a}{2}\right)^{5} \int_{0}^{\infty}\left(1-x+\frac{x^{2}}{6}\right)\left(1-\frac{x}{4}\right) x^{4} e^{-x} d x \).

\( =\frac{a}{2 \sqrt{2}} \int_{0}^{\infty}\left(1-\frac{5}{4} x+\frac{5}{12} x^{2}-\frac{1}{24} x^{3}\right) x^{4} e^{-x} d x=\frac{a}{2 \sqrt{2}}\left(4 !-\frac{5}{4} 5 !+\frac{5}{12} 6 !-\frac{1}{24} 7 !\right) \).

\( =-9 \sqrt{2} a \).

\( \int Y_{0}^{0} Y_{1}^{0} \cos \theta \sin \theta d \theta d \phi=\frac{1}{\sqrt{4 \pi}} \sqrt{\frac{3}{4 \pi}} \int \cos \theta \cos \theta \sin \theta d \theta d \phi=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4 \pi} 2 \pi \int_{0}^{\pi} \cos ^{2} \theta \sin \theta d \theta=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \frac{2}{3}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \).

\( \left\langle 300\left|H_{s}^{\prime}\right| 310\right\rangle=e E_{\text {ext }}(-9 \sqrt{2} a)\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}\right)=-3 \sqrt{6} a e E_{\text {ext }} \).

\( \left\langle 300\left|H_{s}^{\prime}\right| 320\right\rangle=e E_{\text {ext }} \int R_{30} R_{32} r^{3} d r \int Y_{0}^{0} Y_{2}^{0} \cos \theta \sin \theta d \theta d \phi \).

\( \int Y_{0}^{0} Y_{2}^{0} \cos \theta \sin \theta d \theta d \phi=\frac{1}{\sqrt{4 \pi}} \sqrt{\frac{5}{16 \pi}} \int\left(3 \cos ^{2} \theta-1\right) \cos \theta \sin \theta d \theta d \phi=0 . \quad\left\langle 300\left|H_{s}^{\prime}\right| 320\right\rangle=0 \).

\( \left\langle 310\left|H_{s}^{\prime}\right| 320\right\rangle=e E_{\text {ext }} \int R_{31} R_{32} r^{3} d r \int Y_{1}^{0} Y_{2}^{0} \cos \theta \sin \theta d \theta d \phi \).

\( \int R_{31} R_{32} r^{3} d r=\frac{8}{27 \sqrt{6}} \frac{1}{a^{3 / 2}} \frac{1}{a} \frac{4}{81 \sqrt{30}} \frac{1}{a^{3 / 2}} \frac{1}{a^{2}} \int\left(1-\frac{r}{6 a}\right) r e^{-r / 3 a} r^{2} e^{-r / 3 a} r^{3} d r \).

\( =\frac{2^{4}}{3^{8} \sqrt{5} a^{6}}\left(\frac{3 a}{2}\right)^{7} \int_{0}^{\infty}\left(1-\frac{x}{4}\right) x^{6} e^{-x} d x=\frac{a}{24 \sqrt{5}}\left(6 !-\frac{1}{4} 7 !\right)=-\frac{9 \sqrt{5}}{2} a \).

\( \int Y_{1}^{0} Y_{2}^{0} \sin \theta \cos \theta d \theta d \phi=\sqrt{\frac{3}{4 \pi}} \sqrt{\frac{5}{16 \pi}} \int \cos \theta\left(3 \cos ^{2} \theta-1\right) \cos \theta \sin \theta d \theta d \phi \).

\( =\frac{\sqrt{15}}{8 \pi} 2 \pi \int_{0}^{\pi}\left(3 \cos ^{4} \theta-\cos ^{2} \theta\right) \sin \theta d \theta=\left.\frac{\sqrt{15}}{4}\left[-\frac{3}{5} \cos ^{5} \theta+\frac{1}{3} \cos ^{3} \theta\right]\right|_{0} ^{\pi}=\frac{2}{\sqrt{15}} \).

\( \left\langle 310\left|H_{s}^{\prime}\right| 320\right\rangle=e E_{\text {ext }}\left(-\frac{9 \sqrt{5}}{2} a\right)\left(\frac{2}{\sqrt{15}}\right)=-3 \sqrt{3} a e E_{\text {ext }} . \)

\( \left\langle 31 \pm 1\left|H_{s}^{\prime}\right| 32 \pm 1\right\rangle=e E_{\text {ext }} \int R_{31} R_{32} r^{3} d r \int\left(Y_{1}^{\pm 1}\right)^{*} Y_{2}^{\pm 1} \cos \theta \sin \theta d \theta d \phi \).

\( \int\left(Y_{1}^{\pm 1}\right)^{*} Y_{2}^{\pm 1} \cos \theta \sin \theta d \theta d \phi=\left(\mp \sqrt{\frac{3}{8 \pi}}\right)\left(\mp \sqrt{\frac{15}{8 \pi}}\right) \int \sin \theta e^{\mp i \phi} \sin \theta \cos \theta e^{\pm i \phi} \cos \theta \sin \theta d \theta d \phi \).

\( =\frac{3 \sqrt{5}}{8 \pi} 2 \pi \int_{0}^{\pi} \cos ^{2} \theta\left(1-\cos ^{2} \theta\right) \sin \theta d \theta=\left.\frac{3}{4} \sqrt{5}\left(-\frac{\cos ^{3} \theta}{3}+\frac{\cos ^{5} \theta}{5}\right)\right|_{0} ^{\pi} \).

\( =\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} \).

\( \left\langle 31 \pm 1\left|H_{s}^{\prime}\right| 32 \pm 1\right\rangle=e E_{\text {ext }}\left(-9 \frac{\sqrt{5}}{2} a\right)\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\right)=-\frac{9}{2} a e E_{\text {ext }} . \)

Thus the matrix representing \( H_{s}^{\prime} \) is (all empty boxes are zero; all numbers multiplied by \( \left.-a e E_{\text {ext }}\right) \):

(b) The perturbing matrix (below) breaks into a 3 × 3 block, two 2 × 2 blocks, and two 1 × 1 blocks, so we can work out the eigenvalues in each block separately.

\( \underline{3 \times 3}: \quad 3 \sqrt{3}\left(\begin{array}{ccc} 0 & \sqrt{2} & 0 \\ \sqrt{2} & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right) ; \quad\left|\begin{array}{ccc} -\lambda & \sqrt{2} & 0 \\ \sqrt{2} & -\lambda & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & -\lambda \end{array}\right|=-\lambda^{3}+\lambda+2 \lambda=-\lambda\left(\lambda^{2}-3\right)=0 \).

\( \lambda=0, \pm \sqrt{3} \Rightarrow E_{1}^{1}=0, E_{2}^{1}=9 a e E_{\text {ext }}, E_{3}^{1}=-9 a e E_{\text {ext }} \).

\( \underline{2 \times 2}: \quad \frac{9}{2}\left(\begin{array}{cc} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{array}\right) ; \quad\left|\begin{array}{cc} -\lambda & 1 \\ 1 & -\lambda \end{array}\right|=\lambda^{2}-1=0 \Rightarrow \lambda=\pm 1 \).

\( E_{4}^{1}=\frac{9}{2} a e E_{ ext }, E_{5}^{1}=-\frac{9}{2} a e E_{ ext } \).

From the other \( 2 \times 2 \text { we get } E_{6}^{1}=E_{4}^{1}, E_{7}^{1}=E_{5}^{1}, \text { and from the } 1 \times 1 \text { 's we get } \) \( E_{8}^{1}=E_{9}^{1}=0 \). Thus the perturbations to the energy \( \left(E_{3}\right) \) are:

\( \begin{array}{ll} 0 & (\text { degeneracy 3) } \\ (9 / 2) a e E_{\text {ext }} & \text { (degeneracy 2) } \\ -(9 / 2) a e E_{\text {ext }} & \text { (degeneracy 2) } \\ 9 a e E_{\text {ext }} & (\text { degeneracy 1) } \\ -9 a e E_{\text {ext }} & (\text { degeneracy 1) } \end{array} \)
