Guest Question.

Making a turn on a banked road. The car from is to manipulate a turn with a radius of curvature of 60.0 m at a speed of 90.0 km/hr = 25.0 m/s. At what angle should the road be banked for the car to make the turn?

Admin
Solution

To have the necessary centripetal force, the road should be banked at the angle θ given by equation 6.33 as

\(\theta = \tan^{-1}\frac{v^2}{rg}= \tan ^{-1}\left[\frac{(25.0 m/s)^2}{(60.0 m)(9.80 m/s^2)} \right] \)

= 46.7º

This angle is a little large for practical purposes. A reasonable compromise might be to increase the radius of curvature r, to a higher value, say r = 180 m, then,

\(\theta = \tan^{-1}\frac{v^2}{rg}= \tan ^{-1}\left[\frac{(25.0 m/s)^2}{(180 m)(9.80 m/s^2)} \right] \)

=19.5º

a more reasonable angle of bank.

The design of the road becomes a trade-off between the angle of bank and the radius of curvature, but the necessary centripetal force is supplied by the horizontal component of the reaction force of the road on the car.
