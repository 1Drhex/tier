Guest Question.

It is required to design a cotter joint to connect two steel rods of equal diameter. Each rod is subjected to an axial tensile force of 50 kN.
Design the joint and specify its main dimensions.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } P=\left(50 \times 10^{3}\right) N\).

Part I Selection of material
The rods are subjected to tensile force and strength is the criterion for the selection of the rod material.
The cotter is subjected to direct shear stress and bending stresses. Therefore, strength is also the criterion of material selection for the cotter. On the basis of strength, the material of the two rods and the cotter is selected as plain carbon steel of Grade \( 30 C 8\left(S_{y t}=400 N / mm ^{2}\right) \).

Part II Selection of factor of safety
In stress analysis of the cotter joint, the following factors are neglected:
(i) initial stresses due to tightening of the cotter; and
(ii) stress concentration due to slot in the socket and the spigot ends.
To account for these factors, a higher factor of safety is used in the present design. The factor of safety for the rods, spigot end and socket end is assumed as 6, while for the cotter, it is taken as 4.
There are two reasons for assuming a lower factor of safety for the cotter. They are as follows:
(i) There is no stress concentration in the cotter.
(ii) The cost of the cotter is small compared with the socket end or spigot end. If at all, a failure is going to occur, it should occur in the cotter rather than in the spigot or socket end. This is ensured by assuming a higher factor of safety for the spigot and socket ends, compared with the cotter.

It is assumed that the yield strength in compression is twice the yield strength in tension.
Part III Calculation of permissible stresses
The permissible stresses for rods, spigot end and socket end are as follows:

\( \sigma_{t}=\frac{S_{y t}}{(f s)}=\frac{400}{6}=66.67 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \sigma_{c}=\frac{S_{y c}}{(f s)}=\frac{2 S_{y t}}{(f s)}=\frac{2(400)}{6}=133.33 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \tau=\frac{S_{s y}}{(f s)}=\frac{0.5 S_{y t}}{(f s)}=\frac{0.5(400)}{6} \).

= 33.33 N/mmÂ².

Permissible stresses for the cotter are as follows:

\( \sigma_{t}=\frac{S_{y t}}{(f s)}=\frac{400}{4}=100 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \tau=\frac{S_{s y}}{(f s)}=\frac{0.5 S_{y t}}{(f s)}=\frac{0.5(400)}{4}=50 N / mm ^{2} \).

Part IV Calculation of dimensions
The dimensions of the cotter joint are determined by the procedure outlined in Section 4.10.
Step I Diameter of rods

\( d=\sqrt{\frac{4 P}{\pi \sigma_{t}}}=\sqrt{\frac{4\left(50 \times 10^{3}\right)}{\pi(66.67)}}=30.90 \text { or } 32 mm \).

Step II Thickness of cotter
t = 0.31 d = 0.31(32) = 9.92 or 10 mm.

\( \text { Step III Diameter }\left(d_{2}\right) \text { of spigot } \)

\( P=\left[\frac{\pi}{4} d_{2}^{2}-d_{2} t\right] \sigma_{t} \).

\( 50 \times 10^{3}=\left[\frac{\pi}{4} d_{2}^{2}-d_{2}(10)\right] \).

or \( d_{2}^{2}-12.73 d_{2}-954.88=0 \).

Solving the above quadratic equation,

\( d_{2}=\frac{12.73 \pm \sqrt{12.73^{2}-4(-954.88)}}{2} \).

\( \therefore \quad d_{2}=37.91 \text { or } 40 mm \).

\( \text { Step IV Outer diameter }\left(d_{1}\right) \text { of socket } \).

\( P=\left[\frac{\pi}{4}\left(d_{1}^{2}-d_{2}^{2}\right)-\left(d_{1}-d_{2}\right) t\right] \sigma_{t} \).

\( 50 \times 10^{3}=\left[\frac{\pi}{4}\left(d_{1}^{2}-40^{2}\right)-\left(d_{1}-40\right)(10)\right. \).

or \( d_{1}^{2}-12.73 d_{1}-2045.59=0 \).

Solving the above quadratic equation,

\( d_{1}=\frac{12.73 \pm \sqrt{12.73^{2}-4(-2045.59)}}{2} \).

\( \therefore \quad d_{1}=52.04 \text { or } 55 mm \).

\( \text { Step } V \text { Diameters of spigot collar }\left(d_{3}\right) \text { and socket collar }(d_4) \).

\( d_{3}=1.5 d=1.5(32)=48 mm \).

\( d_{4}=2.4 d=2.4(32)=76.8 \text { or } 80 mm \).

Step VI Dimensions a and c

a = c = 0.75d = 0.75(32) = 24 mm
Step VII Width of cotter

\( b=\frac{P}{2 \tau t}=\frac{50 \times 10^{3}}{2(50)(10)}=50 mm \) (a).

or

\( b=\sqrt{\frac{3 P}{t \sigma_{b}}\left[\frac{d_{2}}{4}+\frac{d_{4}-d_{2}}{6}\right]} \).

\( =\sqrt{\frac{3\left(50 \times 10^{3}\right)}{(10)(100)}\left[\frac{40}{4}+\frac{80-40}{6}\right]} \)

= 50 mm (b).

From (a) and (b),
b = 50 mm
Step VIII Check for crushing and shear stresses in spigot end

\( \sigma_{c}=\frac{P}{t d_{2}}=\frac{50 \times 10^{3}}{(10)(40)}=125 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \tau=\frac{P}{2 a d_{2}}=\frac{50 \times 10^{3}}{2(24)(40)}=26.04 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \therefore \quad \sigma_{c}<133.33 N / mm ^{2} \quad \text { and } \quad \tau<33.33 N / mm ^{2} \).

Step IX Check for crushing and shear stresses in socket end

\( \sigma_{c}=\frac{P}{\left(d_{4}-d_{2}\right) t} \).

\( =\frac{50 \times 10^{3}}{(80-40)(10)}=125 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \tau=\frac{P}{2\left(d_{4}-d_{2}\right) c} \).

\( =\frac{50 \times 10^{3}}{2(80-40)(24)}=26.04 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \therefore \quad \sigma_{ c }<133.33 N / mm ^{2} \quad \text { and } \quad \tau<33.33 N / mm ^{2} \).

The stresses induced in the spigot and the socket ends are within limits.

Step X Thickness of spigot collar

\( t_{1}=0.45 d=0.45(32)=14.4 \text { or } 15 mm \).

The taper for the cotter is 1 in 32.
