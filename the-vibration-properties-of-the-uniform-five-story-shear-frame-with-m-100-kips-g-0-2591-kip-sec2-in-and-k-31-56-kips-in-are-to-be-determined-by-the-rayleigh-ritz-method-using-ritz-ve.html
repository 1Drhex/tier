Guest Question.

The vibration properties of the uniform five-story shear frame with \(m = 100 kips/g = 0.2591 kip-sec^{2}/in.\) and k = 31.56 kips/in. are to be determined by the Rayleigh–Ritz method using Ritz vectors determined from a force distribution \(\mathbf{s}=\left \langle \begin{matrix} m & m & m & m & m \end{matrix} \right \rangle^{T}\) . Using two force-dependent vectors, determine the first two natural frequencies and modes of vibration.

Admin
Solution

1. The stiffness and mass matrices, \(\mathbf{k}\) and \(\mathbf{m}\), are given with \(k = 31.56 kips/in. and m = 0.2591 kip-sec^{2}/in\).

2. Determine the first Ritz vector, \(\psi _{1}.\)
• Solve \(\mathbf{ky_{1}} = m_{1}\) to obtain

\(\mathbf{y_{1}} = \left \langle \begin{matrix} 0.0410 & 0.0793 & 0.0985 & 0.1149 & 0.1231 \end{matrix} \right \rangle^{T}\)

 

• Divide \(y_{1}\) by \(\left ( \mathbf{y_{1}^{T} my_{1}} \right )^{1/2} = 0.1082\) to obtain the normalized vector:

\(\psi _{1} = \left \langle \begin{matrix} 0.3792 & 0.6826 & 0.9102 & 1.062 & 1.138 \end{matrix} \right \rangle^{T}\)

3. Determine the second Ritz vector \(\psi _{2}.\)
• Solve\(\mathbf{ky_{2}} = \mathbf{m} \psi _{1}\) to obtain

\(\mathbf{y_{2}} = \left \langle \begin{matrix} 0.0342 & 0.0654 & 0.0909 & 0.1090 & 0.1183 \end{matrix} \right \rangle^{T}\)

• Orthogonalize \(y_{2}\) with respect to ψ1:
\(a_{12} = \psi _{1}^{T} \mathbf{my_{2}} = 0.1012\)

\(\hat {\psi _{2}} = \mathbf{y_{2}}− 0.1012\psi _{1} = 10^{−2}\left \langle \begin{matrix}−0.4134 & − 0.3705 & − 0.1204 & 0.1500 & 0.3164\end{matrix} \right \rangle^{T}\)

• Divide \(\hat{\psi _{2}} \) by \(\left ( \hat{\psi _{2}^{T}}\mathbf{m}\hat{\psi _{2}} \right )^{1/2} = 0.3396 × 10^{−2}\) to get the normalized vector:

\(\psi _{2} =\left \langle \begin{matrix} −1.217 & − 1.091 & − 0.3546 & 0.4418 & 0.9316 \end{matrix} \right \rangle^{T}\)

 

4. Compute \(\tilde{\mathbf{k}}\)and\( \tilde{\mathbf{m}} .\)

\(\mathbf{\Psi } =\left [ \psi _{1}\psi _{2} \right ]\) \(\mathbf{\tilde{k} =\Psi ^{T}k\Psi }=\begin{bmatrix} 9.986 &−3.086 \\ −3.086 & 91.95 \end{bmatrix}\) \(\mathbf{\tilde{m} =\Psi ^{T}m\Psi } =\begin{bmatrix} 1.0 & \\ & 1.0 \end{bmatrix}\)

5. Solve the reduced eigenvalue problem, Eq. (15.3.11).
\(\tilde{\omega _{1}}= 3.142\)

\(\tilde{\omega _{2}} = 9.595\) \(\chi _{1}=\begin{Bmatrix}0.9993\\ 0.0376 \end{Bmatrix}\) \(\chi _{2}=\begin{Bmatrix} −0.0376\\ 0.9993 \end{Bmatrix}\)

6. Determine the natural modes. Substituting \(Ψ_{n}\) and \(χ_{n}\) in Eq. (15.3.14) gives

\(\tilde{\phi _{1}} = \left \langle \begin{matrix} 0.3332 & 0.6412 & 0.8962 &1.078 &1.172 \end{matrix} \right \rangle^{T}\)

 

\(\tilde{\phi _{2}} = \left \langle \begin{matrix} −1.230 & − 1.116 & − 0.3886 & 0.4016 & 0.8882 \end{matrix} \right \rangle^{T}\)

7. Compare with the exact results. Table E15.1 gives the exact modes and frequencies; the latter, after substituting for k and m, are \(\omega _{1}= 3.142 \omega _{2} = 9.170 rad/sec\) The approximate frequencies \(\tilde{\omega _{n}}\) and modes from this example, using force-dependent Ritz vectors, are better than those determined from assumed vectors.
