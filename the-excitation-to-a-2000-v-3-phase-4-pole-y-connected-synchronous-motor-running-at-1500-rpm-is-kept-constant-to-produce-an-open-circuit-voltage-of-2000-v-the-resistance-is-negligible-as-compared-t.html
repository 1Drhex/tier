Guest Question.

The excitation to a 2000 V, 3-phase, 4-pole, Y-connected synchronous motor running at 1500 rpm is kept constant to produce an open-circuit voltage of 2000 V. The resistance is negligible as compared to synchronous reactance of 3 Ω/phase. Determine the power input, power factor and torque developed for an armature current of 200 A.

Admin
Solution

Here, \(V_{L} = 2000 V; E_{L} = 2000 V; I = 200 A\)

Supply voltage per phase, \( V=\frac{2000}{\sqrt{3} }=1154.7 V \)

Induced emf per phase, \( E=\frac{2,000}{\sqrt{3} }=1154.7 V \)

Impedance drop per phase, \( E_{r} = IZ_{s} = 200 \times 3 = 600 V \)

Internal angle, \( \theta = 90^{\circ }\) ∴ resistance is negligible

Assuming armature current lagging behind the supply voltage by an angle \( \phi \), as shown in phasor diagram (Fig. 8.17).

In ΔAOB we have \( E^{2}= V^{2} + E^{2}_{r} – 2VE_{r} \cos \left(90^{\circ }- \phi \right) \)

or \( 1154.7^{2}= 1154.7^{2} + 600^{2} – 2\times 1154.7\times 600 \sin \phi \)

or \( \sin \phi = \frac{600^{2}}{2\times 1154.7\times 600} = 0.26\)

\( \phi = \sin^{-1}0.26 = 15.06^{\circ }\)

 

\( Power factor = \cos 15.06^{\circ }= \mathbf{0.966 lagging} \)

 

\( Power input = \sqrt{3} V_{L} I_{L} \cos \phi = \sqrt{3} \times 2000 \times 200 \times 0.966= 669029 W \)

 

or \(Torque developed = \frac{Power input – copper losses in armature}{2\pi N_{s}/60} \)

Since armature resistance is neglected, copper losses in the armature are considered to be zero

\(\therefore T= \frac{669029}{2\pi \times 1500/ 60} = \mathbf{4259 Nm} \)
