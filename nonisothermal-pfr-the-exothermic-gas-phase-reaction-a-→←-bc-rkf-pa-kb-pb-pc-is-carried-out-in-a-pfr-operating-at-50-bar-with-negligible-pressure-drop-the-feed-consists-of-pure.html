Guest Question.

Nonisothermal PFR. The exothermic gas phase reaction:

\(A \rightleftarrows B + C , \quad r=k_{ f } p_{ A }-k_{ b } p_{ B } p_{ C }\)

is carried out in a PFR operating at 50 bar with negligible pressure drop. The feed consists of pure A and the required conversion is 70%. The rate data is given by

\(k_{f}=0.435 \exp \left\lgroup -\frac{E_{f}}{R T} \right\rgroup mol \cdot s ^{-1} \cdot m ^{3} \cdot bar ^{-1}, \quad E_{f}=20 kJ / mol\)

 

\(k_{b}=147 \exp \left\lgroup -\frac{E_{b}}{R T} \right\rgroup mol \cdot s ^{-1} \cdot m ^{3} \cdot bar ^{-2}, \quad E_{b}=60 k J / mol\)

a. Find the optimum temperature progression in the reactor with the maximum allowed temperature of 350°C.

b. What is the reactor space time required if the optimum temperature progression is taken?

c. What is the heat to be removed if \(C_{ P } \approx 0\)?

 

Admin
Solution

The stoichiometry:

\(A \rightleftharpoons B+C\)

 

\(V=0 \quad F_{A 0} \quad 0 \quad 0\)

 

\(\left.V=V \quad F_{A} \quad F_{B} \quad F_{C} \quad+\right)=F_{T}\)

 

\(\left.F_{A 0}\left(1-f_{A}\right) \quad F_{A 0} f_{A} \quad F_{A 0} f_{A}+\right)=F_{A 0}\left(1+f_{A}\right)\)

The partial pressures in the reaction mixture are given by

\(p_{A}=\frac{F_{A}}{F_{T}} P=\frac{1-f_{A}}{1+f_{A}} P\) (E5.10.1)

 

\(p_{B}=\frac{F_{B}}{F_{T}} P=\frac{f_{A}}{1+f_{A}} P=p_{C}\) (E5.10.2)

Thus, the reaction rate is given by

\(r=k_{f} P \frac{1-f_{A}}{1+f_{A}}-k_{b} P^{2} \frac{f_{A}^{2}}{\left(1+f_{A}\right)^{2}}\) (E5.10.3)

a. The optimum temperature is the temperature that gives rise to the maximum reaction rate, that is

\(0=\frac{d r}{d T}=P \frac{1-f_{A}}{1+f_{A}} \frac{d k_{f}}{d T}-P^{2} \frac{f_{A}^{2}}{\left(1+f_{A}\right)^{2}} \frac{d k_{b}}{d T}\) (E5.10.4)

Since the final pressures are not functions of temperature for the required conversion of A. Since based on the rate law,

\(\frac{d k_{f}}{d T}=k_{f} \frac{E_{f}}{R T^{2}} \frac{d k_{b}}{d T}=k_{b} \frac{E_{b}}{R T^{2}}\) (E5.10.5)

We obtain by substituting Eq. (E5.10.5) \(\frac{d k_{f}}{d T}=k_{f} \frac{E_{f}}{R T^{2}} \frac{d k_{b}}{d T}=k_{b} \frac{E_{b}}{R T^{2}}\) into (E5.10.4) \(0=\frac{d r}{d T}=P \frac{1-f_{A}}{1+f_{A}} \frac{d k_{f}}{d T}-P^{2} \frac{f_{A}^{2}}{\left(1+f_{A}\right)^{2}} \frac{d k_{b}}{d T}\) ,

\(0=P k_{f} \frac{1-f_{A}}{1+f_{A}} \frac{E_{f}}{R T^{2}}-P^{2} k_{b} \frac{f_{A}^{2}}{\left(1+f_{A}\right)^{2}} \frac{E_{b}}{R T^{2}}\) (E5.10.6)

Therefore

\(\frac{k_{f}}{k_{b}}=P \frac{f_{A}^{2}}{\left(1-f_{A}\right)\left(1+f_{A}\right)} \frac{E_{b}}{E_{f}}\) (E5.10.7)

Also

\(\frac{k_{f}}{k_{b}}=\frac{k_{f 0} \exp \left\lgroup -\frac{E_{f}}{R T} \right\rgroup }{k_{b 0} \exp \left\lgroup -\frac{E_{b}}{R T} \right\rgroup } \text { bar }=\frac{k_{f 0}}{k_{b 0}} \exp \frac{E_{b}-E_{f}}{R T}\) (E5.10.8)

Therefore, the optimum temperature in the PFR is given by

\(T=\frac{E_{b}-E_{f}}{R \ln \left\lgroup \frac{k_{f}}{k_{b}} \times \frac{k_{b 0}}{k_{f 0}} \right\rgroup }=\frac{E_{b}-E_{f}}{R \ln \left\lgroup \frac{f_{A}^{2} P}{1-f_{A}^{2}} \times \frac{E_{b}}{E_{f}} \times \frac{k_{b 0}}{k_{f 0}} \right\rgroup }\) (E5.10.9)

Substituting in the known numbers, we obtain

\(T=\frac{4811.16 K}{\ln\left\lgroup \frac{f_{A}^{2}}{1-f_{A}^{2}} \right\rgroup +10.833477}\) (E5.10.10)

From the above equation, we observe that as the conversion (\(f_{ A }\)) is increased, the required optimum temperature decreases.
Therefore, at low conversions, the optimum temperature calculated could exceed the maximum allowed temperature. At the maximum temperature is T = 350°C = 623.15 K, its corresponding conversion can be determined from. Eq. (E5-10.10) \(T=\frac{4811.16 K}{\ln\left\lgroup \frac{f_{A}^{2}}{1-f_{A}^{2}} \right\rgroup +10.833477}\) to be \(f_{ A }\) = 0.206357793. Therefore, the optimum temperature progression in the PFR is given by

\(T= \begin{cases}623.15 K & f_{A} \leq 0.20636 \\ \frac{4811.16 K}{\ln \left(\frac{f_{A}^{2}}{1-f_{A}^{2}}\right)+10.833477} & f_{A}>0.20636\end{cases}\) (E5.10.11)

The temperature progression is plotted in Fig. E5.10. One can observe that temperature decreases along the length of the reactor (or space time). The maximum temperature (at which the reaction stops) is also shown as it progresses through the reactor.

b. To determine the space time (reactor size) requirement, we must perform a mole balance to the PFR. At steady state, the mole balance of A in the differential volume is given by

\(-d F_{ A }+r_{ A } d V=0\) (E5.10.12)

Noting that \(F_{ A }=F_{ A 0}\left(1-f_{ A }\right)=Q_{0} C_{ A 0}\left(1-f_{ A }\right)\), Eq. (E5.10.12) \(-d F_{ A }+r_{ A } d V=0\) can be rendered to give

\(r d \tau=C_{ A 0} d f_{ A }=0\) (E5.10.13)

At reactor inlet, \(f_{ A }=0, T=T_{0}=523.15 K :\)

 

\(C_{A 0}=\frac{P}{R T_{0}}=\frac{50 \times 10^{5}}{8.314 \times 623.15} mol / m ^{3}=965.089 mol / m ^{3}\)

Integrating Eq. (5.10.13) \(r d \tau=C_{ A 0} d f_{ A }=0\) yield,

\(\tau=C_{A 0} \int_{0}^{f_{Ae}} \frac{d f_{A}}{r}=C_{A 0} \int_{0}^{f_{Ae}} \frac{d f_{A}}{k_{f} P \frac{1-f_{A}}{1+f_{A}}-k_{b} P^{2} \frac{f_{A}^{2}}{\left(1+f_{A}\right)^{2}}}\) (E5.10.14)

Normally, one needs to integrate numerically as \(k_{ f }\) and \(k_{ b }\) are functions of temperature, which varies along the reactor or with mixture composition. Numerical techniques such as the Simpson’s rule, integrator such as OdexLims can be applied for this purpose. In this particular case, integration can be carried out analytically. Substituting the temperature (E5.10.10) \(T=\frac{4811.16 K}{\ln\left\lgroup \frac{f_{A}^{2}}{1-f_{A}^{2}} \right\rgroup +10.833477}\) , the rate constants, initial concentration and the pressure into Eq. (E5.10.13) \(r d \tau=C_{ A 0} d f_{ A }=0\) , we obtain

\(\tau=965.089 \int_{0}^{0.20636} \frac{d f_{A}}{21.75 \frac{1-f_{A}}{1+f_{A}} \exp \left\lgroup -\frac{20000}{8.314 \times 623.15} \right\rgroup -367500 \frac{f_{A}^{2}}{\left(1+f_{A}\right)^{2}} \exp \left\lgroup -\frac{60000}{8.314 \times 623.15} \right\rgroup }+\)

 

\(965.089 \int_{0.20636}^{0.8} \frac{d f_{A}}{21.75 \frac{1-f_{A}}{1+f_{A}}\left\lgroup \frac{1-f_{A}^{2}}{f_{A}^{2}} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{1}{2}} \times 4.4416 \times 10^{-3}-367500 \frac{f_{A}^{2}}{\left(1+f_{A}\right)^{2}}\left\lgroup \frac{1-f_{A}^{2}}{f_{A}^{2}} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{3}{2}} \times 8.7623 \times 10^{-8}}\)

 

\(\tau=965.089 \int_{0}^{0.20636} \frac{d f_{A}}{21.75 \frac{1-f_{A}}{1+f_{A}} \exp \left\lgroup -\frac{20000}{8.314 \times 623.15} \right\rgroup -367500 \frac{f_{A}^{2}}{\left(1+f_{A}\right)^{2}} \exp\left\lgroup -\frac{60000}{8.314 \times 623.15} \right\rgroup }+\)

 

\(965.089 \int_{0.20636}^{0.8} \frac{d f_{A}}{21.75 \frac{1-f_{A}}{1+f_{A}}\left\lgroup \frac{f_{A}^{2}}{1-f_{A}^{2}} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{1}{2}} \times 4.4416 \times 10^{-3}-367500 \frac{f_{A}^{2}}{\left(1+f_{A}\right)^{2}}\left\lgroup \frac{f_{A}^{2}}{1-f_{A}^{2}} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{3}{2}} \times 8.7623 \times 10^{-8}}\)

which is integrated to yield

\(\tau=248.03\left[-f_{A}+3.25762 \ln \frac{0.3431108+f_{A}}{0.3431108-f_{A}}\right]_{0}^{0.20636}\) +

 

\(14985.09\left[\left(1+f_{A}\right)\left(1-f_{A}^{2}\right)^{-\frac{1}{2}}-\operatorname{arcsinf}_{A}\right]_{0.20636}^{0.8} s\)

 

\(=16771.86 s=4.659 h\)

Plotted in Fig. E5.10 is also the variation of conversion with reactor space time. This is achieved by integrating for the space time as a function of conversion.

c. Since the heat capacity is negligible, temperature change can be achieved with negligible thermal energy. Therefore, the heat generated during reaction must be removed. Energy balance gives

\(\sum_{j=1}^{N_{S}} F_{j 0} d H_{j}+\sum_{i=1}^{N_{R}} r_{i} \Delta H_{R i} d V=\delta \dot{Q}-\delta \dot{W}_{s}\) (5.94)

There is no work input or output. When heat capacity is negligible, the energy balance equation reduces to

\(\frac{\delta \dot{Q}}{\delta V}=r \Delta H_{R}=r\left(E_{f}-E_{b}\right)\) (E5.10.15)

which gives the heat required for the reactor. It is the negative of what needs to be removed.

Fig. E5.10D shows the heat generated (or removal requirement) along the PFR. One can observe that more heat is being generated at the front-end of the reactor.
