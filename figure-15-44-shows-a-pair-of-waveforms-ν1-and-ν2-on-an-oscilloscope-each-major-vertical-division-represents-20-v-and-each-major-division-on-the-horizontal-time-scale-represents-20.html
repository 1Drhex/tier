Guest Question.

Figure 15–44 shows a pair of waveforms \(\nu_{1}\) and \(\nu_{2}\) on an oscilloscope. Each major vertical division represents 20 V and each major division on the horizontal (time) scale represents 20 μs. Voltage \(\nu_{1}\) leads. Prepare a phasor diagram using \(\nu_{1}\) as reference. Determine equations for both voltages.

Admin
Solution

From the photograph, the magnitude of \(\nu_{1}\) is \(V_{m_{1}}=3 div \times 20 V/div=60 V\). Similarly, \(V_{m_{2}}=40 V\). Cycle length is \(T=6\times 20 \mu s=120 \mu s\), and the displacement between waveforms is 20 μs, which is 1⁄6 of a cycle (i.e., 60°). Selecting \(\nu_{1}\) as reference and noting that \(\nu_{2}\) lags yields the phasors shown in (b). Angular frequency \(\omega =2\pi /T=2\pi /(120\times 10^{-6} s)=52.36\times 10^{3} rad/s\). Thus, \(\nu _{1}=V_{m_{1}}\sin\omega t=60\sin(52.36\times 10^{3}t) V\) and \(\nu _{2}=40\sin(52.36\times 10^{3}t-60^{\circ}) V\).
