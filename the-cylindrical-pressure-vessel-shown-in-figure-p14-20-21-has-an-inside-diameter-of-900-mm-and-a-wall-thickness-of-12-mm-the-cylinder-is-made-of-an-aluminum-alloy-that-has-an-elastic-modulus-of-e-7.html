Guest Question.

The cylindrical pressure vessel shown in Figure P14.20/21 has an inside diameter of 900 mm and a wall thickness of 12 mm. The cylinder is made of an aluminum alloy that has an elastic modulus of E = 70 GPa and a shear modulus of G = 26.3 GPa. Two strain gages are mounted on the exterior surface of the cylinder at right angles to each other. The angle θ is 25°. If the pressure in the vessel is 1.75 MPa, determine
(a) the strains that act in the x and y directions.
(b) the strains expected in gages a and b.
(c) the normal stresses \(\sigma_{n} \text { and } \sigma_{t}\).
(d) the shear stress \(\tau_{n t}\).

Admin
Solution

(b) Strains in the x and y directions

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{x}=\sigma_{\text {long }}=\frac{p d}{4 t}=\frac{(1.75 MPa )(900 mm )}{4(12 mm )}=32.813 MPa \\&\sigma_{y}=\sigma_{ hoop }=\frac{p d}{2 t}=\frac{(1.75 MPa )(900 mm )}{2(12 mm )}=65.625 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Before proceeding, we need to derive an expression for ν from Eq. (13.18):

\(G=\frac{E}{2(1+v)} \quad \therefore v=\frac{E}{2 G}-1\)

Determine Poisson’s ratio from this expression:

\(v=\frac{E}{2 G}-1=\frac{70 GPa }{2(26.3 GPa )}-1=0.331\)

From the generalized Hooke’s law, the strain in the x direction is:

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{x} &=\frac{1}{E}\left(\sigma_{x}-v \sigma_{y}\right) \\&=\frac{1}{70,000 MPa }[32.813 MPa -(0.331)(65.625 MPa )] \\&=158.626 \times 10^{-6} mm / mm =158.6 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)

Similarly, the strain in the y direction is:

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{y} &=\frac{1}{E}\left(\sigma_{y}-v \sigma_{x}\right) \\&=\frac{1}{70,000 MPa }[65.625 MPa -(0.331)(32.813 MPa )] \\&=782.438 \times 10^{-6} mm / mm =782 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Expected strain gage readings in gages a and b
A strain transformation equation [Eq. (13.3)]

\(\varepsilon_{n}=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2} \theta+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2} \theta+\gamma_{x y} \sin \theta \cos \theta\)

can be written for the normal strain in the direction of strain gage a:

\(\varepsilon_{a}=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2}\left(25^{\circ}\right)+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2}\left(25^{\circ}\right)+\gamma_{x y} \sin \left(25^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(25^{\circ}\right)\)

Since this is a cylindrical pressure vessel, the shear stress \(\tau_{x y}\) must equal zero, and hence, the shear strain \(\gamma_{x y}\) must also equal zero. The strain transformation equation reduces to:

\(\varepsilon_{a}=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2}\left(25^{\circ}\right)+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2}\left(25^{\circ}\right)\)

The expected strain reading in gage a is thus:

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{a} &=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2}\left(25^{\circ}\right)+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2}\left(25^{\circ}\right) \\&=(158.626 \mu \varepsilon) \cos ^{2}\left(25^{\circ}\right)+(782.438 \mu \varepsilon) \sin ^{2}\left(25^{\circ}\right)=270 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)

Use a value of θ = 25° + 90° = 115° to obtain the strain reading expected in gage b:

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{b} &=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2}\left(115^{\circ}\right)+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2}\left(115^{\circ}\right) \\&=(158.626 \mu \varepsilon) \cos ^{2}\left(115^{\circ}\right)+(782.438 \mu \varepsilon) \sin ^{2}\left(115^{\circ}\right)=671 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)

(c) Normal stresses \(\sigma_{n} \text { and } \sigma_{t} \text {. }\)
Use the normal stress transformation equation [Eq. (12.3)] to calculate \(\sigma_{n}\):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{n} &=\sigma_{x} \cos ^{2} \theta+\sigma_{y} \sin ^{2} \theta+2 \tau_{x y} \sin \theta \cos \theta \\&=(32.813 MPa ) \cos ^{2}\left(25^{\circ}\right)+(65.625 MPa ) \sin ^{2}\left(25^{\circ}\right)+2(0 MPa ) \sin \left(25^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(25^{\circ}\right) \\&=38.673 MPa =38.7 MPa ( T )\end{aligned}\)

and \(\sigma_{t}\):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{t} &=\sigma_{x} \cos ^{2}\left(\theta+90^{\circ}\right)+\sigma_{y} \sin ^{2}\left(\theta+90^{\circ}\right)+2 \tau_{x y} \sin \left(\theta+90^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(\theta+90^{\circ}\right) \\&=(32.813 MPa ) \cos ^{2}\left(115^{\circ}\right)+(65.625 MPa ) \sin ^{2}\left(115^{\circ}\right)+2(0 MPa ) \sin \left(115^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(115^{\circ}\right) \\&=59.764 MPa =59.8 MPa ( T )\end{aligned}\)

 

(d) Shear stress \(\tau_{n t}\).
The shear stress transformation equation [Eq. (12.4)] gives \(\tau_{n t}\):

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{n t} &=-\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) \sin \theta \cos \theta+\tau_{x y}\left(\cos ^{2} \theta-\sin ^{2} \theta\right) \\&=-[(32.813 MPa )-(65.625 MPa )] \sin \left(25^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(25^{\circ}\right)+(0 MPa )\left[\cos ^{2}\left(25^{\circ}\right)-\sin ^{2}\left(25^{\circ}\right)\right] \\&=12.568 MPa =12.57 MPa\end{aligned}\)
