Guest Question.

Derive the equation of motion for the homogeneous circular cylinder, which rolls without slipping. If the cylinder mass is 50 kg, the cylinder radius 0.5 m, the spring constant 75 N /m, and the damping coefficient 10 N∙s/m, determine

(a) the undamped natural frequency

(b) the damping ratio

(c) the damped natural frequency

(d) the period of the damped system.

In addition, determine x as a function of time if the cylinder is released from rest at the position x = −0.2 m when t = 0.

Admin
Solution

We have a choice of motion variables in that either x or the angular 1 displacement θ of the cylinder may be used. Since the problem statement involves x, we draw the free-body diagram for an arbitrary, positive value of x and write the two motion equations for the cylinder as

 

2 \(\left[\Sigma F_{x}=m \ddot{x}\right] \quad-c \dot{x}-k x+F=m \ddot{x}\)

 

\(\left[\Sigma M_{G}=\bar{I} \ddot{\theta}\right] \quad-F r=\frac{1}{2} m r^{2} \ddot{\theta}\)

 

The condition of rolling with no slip is \(\ddot{x}=r \ddot{\theta}\). Substitution of this condition into the moment equation gives \(F=-\frac{1}{2} m \ddot{x}\). Inserting this expression for the friction force into the force equation for the x-direction yields

 

\(-c \dot{x}-k x-\frac{1}{2} m \ddot{x}=m \ddot{x} \quad \text { or } \quad \ddot{x}+\frac{2}{3} \frac{c}{m} \dot{x}+\frac{2}{3} \frac{k}{m} x=0\)

 

Comparing the above equation with that for the standard damped oscillator, Eq. 8 /9, allows us to state directly

 

\(\ddot{x}+2 \zeta \omega_{n} \dot{x}+\omega_{n}{ }^{2} x=0\) (8/9)

 

(a) \(\omega_{n}{ }^{2}=\frac{2}{3} \frac{k}{m} \quad \omega_{n}=\sqrt{\frac{2}{3} \frac{k}{m}}=\sqrt{\frac{2}{3} \frac{75}{50}}=1 rad / s\)

 

(b) \(2 \zeta \omega_{n}=\frac{2}{3} \frac{c}{m} \quad \zeta=\frac{1}{3} \frac{c}{m \omega_{n}}=\frac{10}{3(50)(1)}=0.0667\)

 

Hence, the damped natural frequency and the damped period are

 

(c) \(\omega_{d}=\omega_{n} \sqrt{1-\zeta^{2}}=(1) \sqrt{1-(0.0667)^{2}}=0.998 rad / s\)

 

(d) \(\tau_{d}=2 \pi / \omega_{d}=2 \pi / 0.998=6.30 s\)

 

From Eq. 8 /12, the underdamped solution to the equation of motion is

 

\(x=C e^{-\zeta \omega_{n} t} \sin \left(\omega_{d} t+\psi\right)\) (8/12)

 

\(x=C e^{-\zeta \omega_{n} t} \sin \left(\omega_{d} t+\psi\right)=C e^{-(0.0667)(1) t} \sin (0.998 t+\psi)\)

 

The velocity is \(\dot{x}=-0.0667 Ce ^{-0.0667 t} \sin (0.998 t+\psi)\)

 

\(+0.998 C e^{-0.0667 t} \cos (0.998 t+\psi)\)

 

At time t = 0, x and \(\dot{x}\) become

 

\(x_{0}=C \sin \psi=-0.2\)

 

\(\dot{x}_{0}=-0.0667 C \sin \psi+0.998 C \cos \psi=0\)

 

The solution to the two equations in C and \(\psi\) gives

 

\(C=-0.200 m \quad \psi=1.504 rad\)

 

Thus, the motion is given by

 

\(x=-0.200 e^{-0.0667 t} \sin (0.998 t+1.504) m\)

 

Helpful Hints

1 The angle θ is taken positive clockwise to be kinematically consistent with x.

2 The friction force F may be assumed in either direction. We will find that the actual direction is to the right for x > 0 and to the left for x < 0; F = 0 when x = 0.
