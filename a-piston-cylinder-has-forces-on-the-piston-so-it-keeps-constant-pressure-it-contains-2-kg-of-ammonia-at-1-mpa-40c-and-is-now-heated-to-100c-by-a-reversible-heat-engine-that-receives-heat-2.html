Guest Question.

A piston/cylinder has forces on the piston so it keeps constant pressure. It contains 2 kg of ammonia at 1 MPa, 40°C and is now heated to 100°C by a reversible heat engine that receives heat from a 200°C source. Find the work out of the heat engine using the exergy balance equation.

Admin
Solution

To evaluate it we need the change in exergy Eq.8.28

\(\Phi_{2}-\Phi_{1}=m_{a m}\left(u_{2}-u_{1}\right)+P_{o}\left(V_{2}-V_{1}\right)-m_{a m} T_{o}\left(s_{2}-s_{1}\right)\)

The work \(\left( W = W _{ H.E .}+{ }_{1} W _{2, pist }\right)\) is from the integrated exergy Eq.8.38

for a reversible process with no flow terms and use of Eq.8.28

\(\begin{aligned}W &= P _{o}\left( V _{2}- V _{1}\right)+\left(1-\frac{ T _{ o }}{ T _{ H }}\right) {}_{1} Q _{2}-\left(\Phi_{2}-\Phi_{1}\right)-0 \\&=\left(1-\frac{ T _{ o }}{ T _{ H }}\right) {}_{1} Q _{2}- m _{ am }\left( u _{2}- u _{1}\right)+ m _{ am } T _{o}\left( s _{2}- s _{1}\right)\end{aligned}\)

Now we must evaluate the three terms on the RHS and the work \(_{1}W _{2, pist }\) .

State 1: \(u _{1}=1369.8 kJ / kg , v _{1}=0.13868 m ^{3} / kg , s _{1}=5.1778 kJ / kg K\)

State 2: \(u _{2}=1490.5 kJ / kg , v _{2}=0.17389 m ^{3} / kg , s _{2}=5.6342 kJ / kg K\)

\({ }_{1} W _{2, pist }= m _{ am } P \left( v _{2}- v _{1}\right)=2 \times 1000(0.17389-0.13868)=70.42 kJ\)

C.V. Heat engine and ammonia (otherwise we involve
another Q)

Entropy: \(m _{ am }\left( s _{2}- s _{1}\right)={ }_{1} Q _{2} / T _{ H }+0\)

\(\begin{aligned}\Rightarrow \quad{ }_{1} Q _{2} &= T _{ H } m _{ am }\left( s _{2}- s _{1}\right) \\&=473.15 \times 2(5.6342-5.1778) \\&=431.89 kJ\end{aligned}\)

Substitute this heat transfer into the work term

\(\begin{aligned}W &=\left(1-\frac{298.15}{473.15}\right) 431.89-2(1490.5-1369.8)+2 \times 298.15(5.6342-5.1778) \\&=159.74-241.4+272.15=190.49 kJ\end{aligned}\)

 

\(W _{ H.E .}= W -{ }_{1} W _{2, pist }=190.49-70.42= 1 2 0 . 0 kJ\)

Eq.8.38 :

\(-\dot{W}_{ c.v. }+P_{0} \frac{d V}{d t}\) Transfer by shaft/boundary work
\(+\sum \dot{m}_{i} \psi_{i}-\sum \dot{m}_{e} \psi_{e}\) Transfer by flow
