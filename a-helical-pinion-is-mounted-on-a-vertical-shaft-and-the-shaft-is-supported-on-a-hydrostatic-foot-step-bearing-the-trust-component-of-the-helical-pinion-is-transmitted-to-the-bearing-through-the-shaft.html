Guest Question.

A helical pinion is mounted on a vertical shaft and the shaft is supported on a hydrostatic foot step bearing. The trust component of the helical pinion is transmitted to the bearing through the shaft. The pinion transmits 75 kW power at 500 rpm. The normal module and the transverse module are 6 and 8 mm respectively. The pinion has 14 teeth. The nominal bearing pressure is 0.6 MPa. The ratio of recess diameter to shaft diameter is 0.70. Calculate
(i) the diameter of the shaft;
(ii) the diameter of the recess; and
(iii) the supply pressure.
Select the viscosity of lubricating oil so that the minimum oil film thickness is 20 microns and the power lost in pumping and friction taken together is minimum.

Admin
Solution

Given kW = 75 n = 500 rpm.

\( \text { For helical pinion, } \quad m_{n}=6 mm \quad m=8 mm \quad z=14 \).

\( \text { For bearing, } \quad R_{i} / R_{o}=0.7 \quad h_{o}=20 \text { microns } \).

\( p_{b}=0.6 MPa \).

Step I Thrust load on bearing
The analysis of forces acting on helical gear is discussed in Chapter 18 on helical gears. The torque transmitted by the pinion is given by,

\( M_{t}=\frac{\left(60 \times 10^{6}\right) kW }{2 \pi n_{p}}=\frac{\left(600 \times 10^{6}\right)(75)}{2 \pi(500)} \).

\( =1432394.49 \text { N-mm } \) (a)
From Eq. (18.6),

\( d=m z=\frac{z m_{n}}{\cos \psi} \) (18.6).

\( d=m z=\frac{z m_{n}}{\cos \psi} \)

where,
m = transverse module (mm)

\( m_{n}=\text { normal module }( mm ) \)

d = pitch circle diameter of gear (mm)
z = number of teeth
Therefore,

d = m z = 8 (14) = 112 mm (b).

From Eq. (18.17),

\( P_{t}=\frac{2 M_{t}}{d} \) (18.17)

\( P_{t}=\frac{2 M_{t}}{d} \)

where \( \left(P_{t}\right) \) is the tangential component of gear force. Substituting values from (a) and (b),

\( P_{t}=\frac{2 M_{t}}{d}=\frac{2(1432394.49)}{(112)}=25578.47 N \) (c).

From Eq. (18.3),

\(m_{n}=m \cos \psi \) (18.3)

\(m_{n}=m \cos \psi \)

where (ψ) is helix angle. Therefore

\( \cos \psi=\frac{m_{n}}{m}=\frac{6}{8}=0.75 \quad \text { or } \quad \psi=41.41^{\circ} \). (d).

From Eq. (18.15),

\( P_{a}=P_{t} \tan \psi \) (18.15).

\( P_{a}=P_{t} \tan \psi \)

where \( P_{a}\) is the thrust component or load (W) acting on the bearing. Substituting values from (c) and (d),

\( W=P_{a}=P_{t} \tan \psi=(25578.47) \tan \left(41.41^{\circ}\right) \).

= 22 558.39 N (e)
Step II Diameter of shaft and recess
The ratio of recess diameter to shaft diameter is given as 0.70.

\( \therefore \quad \frac{R_{i}}{R_{o}}=\frac{D_{i}}{D_{o}}=0.70 \) (f).

The bearing pressure \( \left(p_{b}\right) \) is given as 0.6 MPa or 0.6 N/mm². The bearing area is the annular area between the shaft circumference and recess circumference. Therefore,

\( W=\pi\left(R_{o}^{2}-R_{i}^{2}\right) p_{b} \).

Substituting values from (e) and (f),

\( 22558.39=\pi\left[R_{o}^{2}-\left(0.70 R_{o}\right)^{2}\right](0.6) \).

\( \therefore \quad R_{o}=153.19 mm \).

\( \text { and } \quad R_{i}=0.70(153.19)=107.23 mm \).

Rounding the values,

\( R_{o}=155 mm \text { and } R_{i}=107.5 mm \).

Shaft diameter = 2 (155) = 310 mm (i)
Recess diameter = 2 (107.5) = 215 mm (ii)
Step III Supply pressure
From Eq. (16.10),

\( P_{i}=\frac{2 W \log _{e}\left(\frac{R_{o}}{R_{i}}\right)}{\pi\left(R_{o}^{2}-R_{i}^{2}\right)} \) (16.10)

\( P_{i}=\frac{2 W \log _{e}\left(\frac{R_{o}}{R_{i}}\right)}{\pi\left(R_{o}^{2}-R_{i}^{2}\right)} \)

\( =\frac{2(22558.39) \log _{e}\left(\frac{155}{107.5}\right)}{\pi\left(155^{2}-107.5^{2}\right)} \).

\( =0.42 N / mm ^{2} \)

\( \text { or } \quad P_{i}=0.42 MPa \) (iii)
Step IV Optimum viscosity for minimum power loss
From Eq. (16.9),

\( Q=\frac{\pi P_{i} h_{o}^{3}}{6 \mu \log _{e}\left(\frac{R_{o}}{R_{i}}\right)} \) (16.9),

\( Q=\frac{\pi P_{i} h_{o}^{3}}{6 \mu \log _{e}\left(\frac{R_{o}}{R_{i}}\right)} \)

\( =\frac{\pi(0.42)\left(20 \times 10^{-3}\right)^{3}}{6 \mu \log _{e}\left(\frac{155}{107.5}\right)}=\left[\frac{4.8077\left(10^{-6}\right)}{\mu}\right] mm ^{3} / s \).

From Eq. (16.11),

\( ( kW )_{p}=Q\left(P_{i}-P_{o}\right)\left(10^{-6}\right) \) (16.11).

\( ( kW )_{p}=Q\left(P_{i}-P_{o}\right)\left(10^{-6}\right) \)

\( =\left[\frac{4.8077\left(10^{-6}\right)}{\mu}\right](0.42-0.0)\left(10^{-6}\right) \).

\( =\left[\frac{2.019\left(10^{-12}\right)}{\mu}\right] \). (a).

From Eq. (16.12),

\( ( kW )_{f}=\left(\frac{1}{58.05 \times 10^{6}}\right) \frac{\mu n^{2}\left(R_{o}^{4}-R_{i}^{4}\right)}{h_{o}} \) (16.12)

\( =\left(\frac{1}{58.05 \times 10^{6}}\right) \frac{\mu(500)^{2}\left[(155)^{4}-(107.5)^{4}\right]}{\left(20 \times 10^{-3}\right)} \).

\( =\left[95.53\left(10^{6}\right) \mu\right] \) (b)
The total energy loss is given by

\( ( kW )_{t}=( kW )_{p}+( kW )_{f} \)

\( \text { or } \quad( kW )_{t}=\left[\frac{2.019\left(10^{-12}\right)}{\mu}\right]+\left[95.53\left(10^{6}\right) \mu\right] \) (c).

\( \text { Differentiating }( kW )_{t} \text { with respect to }(\mu) \text { and } \) setting the result to zero

\( \frac{d}{d \mu}( kW )_{t}=0 \).

\( \text { or } \quad 0=2.019\left(10^{-12}\right)\left(-\frac{1}{\mu^{2}}\right)+95.53\left(10^{6}\right) \).

\( \therefore \quad \mu=0.145\left(10^{-9}\right) N – s / mm ^{2}=0.145 cP \).
