Guest Question.

Use the WKB approximation to find the allowed energies \( (E_n)\) of an infinite square well with a “shelf,” of height \( V_0\) , extending half-way across (Figure 7.3):

\( V(x)= \begin{cases}V_{0}, & (0<x<a / 2) \\ 0, & (a / 2<x<a) \\ \infty, & (\text { otherwise })\end{cases} \).

Express your answer in terms of \( V_{0} \text { and } E_{n}^{0} \equiv(n \pi \hbar)^{2} / 2 m a^{2} \) (the nth allowed energy for the infinite square well with no shelf). Assume that \( E_{1}^{0}>V_{0} \), but do not assume that \( E_{n} \gg V_{0} \). Compare your result with what we got in Section 7.1.2, using first-order perturbation theory. Note that they are in agreement if either \( V_0\) is very small (the perturbation theory regime) or n is very large (the WKB—semi-classical—regime).

Admin
Solution

\( \int_{0}^{a} p(x) d x=n \pi \hbar, \quad \text { with } n=1,2,3, \ldots \text { and } \quad p(x)=\sqrt{2 m[E-V(x)]} \) (Eq. 9.17).

\( \int_{0}^{a} p(x) d x=n \pi \hbar \). (9.17).

Here \( \int_{0}^{a} p(x) d x=\sqrt{2 m E}\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)+\sqrt{2 m\left(E-V_{0}\right)}\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)=\sqrt{2 m}\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)\left(\sqrt{E}+\sqrt{E-V_{0}}\right)=n \pi \hbar \).

\( \Rightarrow E+E-V_{0}+2 \sqrt{E\left(E-V_{0}\right)}=\frac{4}{2 m}\left(\frac{n \pi \hbar}{a}\right)^{2}=4 E_{n}^{0} ; \quad 2 \sqrt{E\left(E-V_{0}\right)}=\left(4 E_{n}^{0}-2 E+V_{0}\right) \).

Square again: \( 4 E\left(E-V_{0}\right)=4 E^{2}-4 E V_{0}=16 E_{n}^{0^{2}}+4 E^{2}+V_{0}^{2}-16 E E_{n}^{0}+8 E_{n}^{0} V_{0}-4 E V_{0} \).

\( \Rightarrow 16 E E_{n}^{0}=16 E_{n}^{0^{2}}+8 E_{n}^{0} V_{0}+V_{0}^{2} \Rightarrow E_{n}=E_{n}^{0}+\frac{V_{0}}{2}+\frac{V_{0}^{2}}{16 E_{n}^{0}} \).

Perturbation theory gave \( E_{n}=E_{n}^{0}+\frac{V_{0}}{2} \); the extra term goes to zero for very small \( \left.V_{0} \text {, or (since } E_{n}^{0} \sim n^{2}\right) \), for large n.
