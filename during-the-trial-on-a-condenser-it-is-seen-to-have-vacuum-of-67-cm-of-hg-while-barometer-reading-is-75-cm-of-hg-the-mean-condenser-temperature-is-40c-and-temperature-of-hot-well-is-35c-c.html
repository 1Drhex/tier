Guest Question.

During the trial on a condenser it is seen to have vacuum of 67 cm of Hg while barometer reading is 75 cm of Hg. The mean condenser temperature is 40°C and temperature of hot well is 35°C. Circulating water flows at 1000 kg/min for giving condensate at the rate of 50 kg/min. Temperature of cooling water at inlet and exit are 10°C and 25°C. Determine, (i) the vacuum corrected to standard barometer reading of 76 cm. (ii) the vacuum efficiency of condenser, (iii) the undercooling of condensate, (iv) the condenser efficiency, (v) the state of steam entering condenser, (vi) the mass of air per m\(^{3}\) of condenser volume, and (vii) the mass of air per kg of uncondensed steam.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{Vacuum corrected to standard barometer reading of 76 cm}\\\begin{aligned}&= 76 – (75 – 67) = \text{68 cm Hg}\\\text{Corrected vacuum}&= \text{68 cm Hg}\\\text{Absolute pressure in condenser}&= \text{75 – 67 = 8 cm of Hg or }(8 × 0.0135951 × 10^{4} × 9.81 = 10.67 kPa)\\\text{or, }\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\quad p_{t}&= 10.67 kPa\\\text{Partial pressure of steam, }p_{s}&= \text{Saturation pressure at 40°C from steam table}\\p_{s}&= 7.384 kPa\\\text{Partial pressure of air, }p_{a}&= p_{t} – p_{s} = 3.286 kPa\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Vacuum efficiency}&= \frac{\text{Actual vacuum}}{\text{Theoretical vacuum}} = \frac{(75 – 8) × 0.0135951 × 10^{4} × 9.81}{= (75 × 0.0145951 × 10^{4} × 9.81) – 7.384 × 10^{3}}\\&= 0.9645 or 96.45\%\\\text{Vacuum efficiency}&= 96.45\%\\\text{Undercooling of condensate}&= 40 – 35 = 5°C\\\text{Undercooling}&= 5°C\\\text{Condenser efficiency}&= \frac{(T_{\text{w out}} – T_{\text{w in}} )}{ (T_{\text{sat corresponding to p}_{t}} – T_{\text{w in}} )}\end{aligned}\\\text{Saturation temperature corresponding to absolute pressure in condenser, 40.01 kPa.}\\\begin{aligned}T_{\text{sat corresponding to p}_{t}}&= \text{46.9°C, from steam table.}\\T_{\text{w out}}&= 25°C, T_{\text{w in}} = 10°C\\\text{Condenser efficiency}&= \frac{25 – 10}{46.9 – 10} = 0.4065 or 40.65\%\end{aligned}\\\text{Let us consider enthalpy of steam entering be h kJ/kg and dryness fraction be x. By applying heat balance,}\\\begin{aligned}m_{s} · (h – c_{p_{w}} · T_{c})&= m_{w} · c_{p_{w}} · (T_{w_{o}} – T_{w_{i}})\\m_{s}&= 50 kg/min, m_{w} = 1000 kg/min\\50(h – 4.18 × 40)&= 1000 × 4.18 (25 – 10)\\h&= 1421.2 kJ/kg = h_{\text{f at 40°C}} + x · h_{\text{fg at 40°C}}\\1421.2&= 167.57 + x · 2406.7\\x&= 0.5209\\\text{Dryness fraction of}&\text{ steam entering = 0.5209}\\\text{Mass of air per m}^{3}\text{ of cond}&\text{enser volume}\\m&= \frac{p_{a} · V}{RT_{c}} = \frac{3.286 × 1}{0.287 × (273 + 40)} = \textbf{0.0366 kg/m}^{\textbf{3}}\end{aligned}\\\text{Volume of per kg of uncondensed steam = }v_{\text{g at 40°C}} = 19.52 m^{3}/kg\\\text{Mass of air in one kg of uncondensed steam = Mass of air in 19.52 m}^{3}\text{ volume}\\\qquad\qquad\qquad = \frac{3.286 × 19.52}{0.287 × (40 + 273)} = 0.714 kg\\\quad \text{Mass of air/m}^{3}\text{ of condenser volume = 0.0366 kg/m}^{3}\\\quad \text{Mass of air in per kg of uncondensed steam = 0.714 kg}\)
