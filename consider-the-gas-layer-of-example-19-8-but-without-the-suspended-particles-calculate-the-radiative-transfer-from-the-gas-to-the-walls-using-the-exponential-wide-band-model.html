Guest Question.

Consider the gas layer of , but without the suspended particles. Calculate the radiative transfer from the gas to the walls using the exponential wide band model.

Admin
Solution

For a slab 0 < z < L the intensity as given by equation (19.92) \(I(s)=[1-\alpha (T_{w},T_{m},s)]I_{bw}+\epsilon (T_{m},s)I_{bm}\) may emanate from either of the two bounding surfaces. Splitting intensity into \(^{+}\) and \(^{-}\) (“positive” and “negative” directions), as was done in Section 13.2, leads to s=z/μ for “positive” directions (0 < μ < 1) and to s = -(L – z)/μ for “negative” directions (-1 <μ < 0), as shown in Fig. 13-1. Substituting into equation (19.92) \(I(s)=[1-\alpha (T_{w},T_{m},s)]I_{bw} + \epsilon (T_{m},s)I_{bm}\) results in

\(I^{+}(z)=[1-\alpha (T_{w},T_{m},z/\mu )]I_{bw}+\epsilon (T_{m},z/\mu)I_{bm}, 0\lt \mu \lt 1,\)
\(I^{-}(z)=[1-\alpha (T_{w},T_{m},-(L-z)/\mu )]I_{bw}+\epsilon (T_{m},-(L-z)/\mu)I_{bm}, -1\lt \mu \lt 0.\)

The local heat flux is then determined from

\(q(z)=2\pi \left[\int_{-1}^{0}{}I^{-1}\mu d\mu +\int_{0}^{+1}{}I^{+}\mu d\mu \right]\)
\(=2\pi \left\{\int_{0}^{1}{} \left[\epsilon \left(T_{m},\frac{z}{\mu } \right)-\epsilon \left(T_{m},\frac{L-z}{\mu } \right) \right]\mu d\mu I_{b}(T_{m})-\int_{0}^{1}{} \left[\alpha \left(T_{w},T_{m},\frac{z}{\mu } \right)-\alpha \left(T_{w},T_{m},\frac{L-z}{\mu } \right) \right]\mu d\mu I_{b}(T_{w}) \right\}\) .

If the medium is a molecular gas, equation (19.82) \(\alpha (T,s^{\prime}\rightarrow s)\simeq \sum\limits_{n=1}^{N}{}\frac{w_{n}I_{b\eta_{n} }}{I_{b}}(T)\frac{1}{w_{n}}\int_{0}^{\infty }{} [1-exp(\int_{s^{\prime }}^{s}{\kappa _{n\eta }}ds^{\prime \prime } )] d\eta\)
\(=\sum\limits_{n=1}^{N}{}\frac{w_{n}I_{b\eta_{n} }}{I_{b}}(T) A^{*}_{n}(s^{\prime}\rightarrow s)\) is substituted into this expression. Using emissive powers in favor of intensities,\(E-{b\eta }=\pi I_{b\eta }\) , then leads to

\(q(z)=\sum\limits_{n=1}^{N}{}w_{n}\left[E_{b\eta _{n}}(T_{m})- E_{b\eta _{n}}(T_{w})\right][A_{s}(\kappa _{n}z,\beta _{n})-A_{s}(\kappa _{n}(L-z),\beta _{n})]\)

where \(A_{s}\) termed slab band absorptance by Edwards and Balakrishnan [57], is defined as

\(A_{s}(\tau ,\beta )=2\int_{0}^{1}{}A^{*}\left\lgroup\frac{\tau }{\mu },\beta \right\rgroup \mu d\mu\) (19.93)

The slab band absorptance may be evaluated explicitly for a number of band absorptance correlations, such as the Edwards and Menard correlation of Table 10.2

\(\beta \leq 1\) \(0\leq \tau _{0}\leq \beta\) \(A^{*}=\tau _{0}\) Linear regime
\(\beta \leq \tau _{0}\leq 1/\beta\) \(A^{*}=2\sqrt{\tau _{0}\beta }-\beta\) Square root regime
\(1/\beta \leq \tau _{0}\lt \infty\) \(A^{*}= \ln (\tau _{0}\beta )+2-\beta\) Logarithmic regime
\(\beta \geq 1\) \(0\leq \tau _{0} \leq 1\) \(A^{*}=\tau _{0}\) Linear regime
\(1\leq \tau _{0}\lt \infty\) \(A^{*}=\ln \tau _{0}+1\) Logarithmic regime
\(\alpha ,\beta \), and w from Table 10.3 and equations (10.111) through (10.118) \(\tau _{0}=\alpha X/w\)

and the high-pressure limit given by equation (10.123) \(A^{*}=E_{1}(\tau _{0})+\ln \tau _{0}+\gamma _{E}= Ein (\tau _{0}), \beta \rightarrow \infty\) , and must be evaluated numerically for more involved correlations such as the ones by Felske and Tien, equation (10.124) \(A^{*}=2E_{1}\left\lgroup\sqrt{\frac{\tau _{0}\beta }{1+\beta /\tau _{0}} } \right\rgroup+ E_{1}\left\lgroup \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\frac{\tau _{0}/\beta }{1+\beta /\tau _{0}} } \right\rgroup -E_{1} \left\lgroup \frac{1+2\beta }{2} \sqrt{\frac{\tau _{0}/\beta }{1+\beta /\tau _{0}} } \right\rgroup +\ln \left\lgroup\frac{\tau _{0}\beta }{(1+\beta /\tau _{0})(1+2\beta )} \right\rgroup +2\gamma _{E}\) , and Wang, equation (10.126) \(A^{*}=e^{\beta }[E_{1}(\beta +w)-E_{1}(\beta )]+\ln (1+w/B)+ Ein (w), w=\left\lgroup\frac{\overline{W} }{d} \right\rgroup_{0, Malkmus} = \frac{\beta }{2}[\sqrt{1+4\tau _{0}/\beta }-1 ]\) . We shall consider here only the high-pressure limit (β → ∞),

\(A^{*}(\tau ,\infty )=E_{1}(\tau )+\ln \tau +\gamma _{E}\)

or

\(A_{s}(\tau ,\infty )=2\int_{0}^{1}{} \left[E_{1}\left(\frac{\tau }{\mu } \right) +\ln \frac{\tau }{\mu }+\gamma _{E} \right] \mu d\mu\)

Integrating by parts we obtain, with \(E^{\prime }_{1}(x)= -E_{0}(x)= e^{-x}/x\),

\(A_{s}(\tau ,\infty )=\mu ^{2} \left[E_{1}\left(\frac{\tau }{\mu } \right) +\ln \frac{\tau }{\mu }+\gamma _{E} \right]|^{1}_{0}-\int_{0}^{1}{\mu ^{2}}\left(\frac{\tau }{\mu ^{2}}\frac{e^{-\tau /\mu }}{\tau /\mu }-\frac{1}{\mu } \right) d\mu\)

or

\(A_{s}(\tau ,\infty )= E_{1}(\tau )+\ln \tau +\gamma _{E}+\frac{1}{2}-E_{3}(\tau )\) . (19.94)

The line overlap parameter β has already been calculated for all three bands in (\(\beta _{4.3}=7.55\)) and \(\beta _{2.7}=5.47\),\(\beta _{15}=1.83\)), and is substantially larger than unity for all bands. Thus, the high-pressure limit may be used. Calculating q at z = L for the three important \(CO_{2}\) bands, we obtain [with\(T_{w}\) = 0 and \(A_{s}\)(0,β) = 0]

\(q_{n}L=w_{n}E_{b\eta _{n}}(T_{m})A_{s}\left\lgroup\frac{\alpha _{n}\rho _{CO_{2}}}{w_{n}}L,\beta \right\rgroup .\)

All required terms have already been calculated in the , and we find the following:

2.7 μm band:

\(\alpha _{2.7}\rho _{CO_{2}}L/w_{2.7}=2005\times 1/96.9=20.69\)

\(A_{s2.7}(20.69,\infty )=E_{1}(20.69)+\ln 20.69+0.5772+0.5 – E_{3}(20.69)= 4.107\)

\(q_{2.7}(L)= 1.7649\times 10^{-8}\times 1700^{3}\times 96.9\times 4.107=34,508 W/m^{2}:\)

4.3 μm band:

\(\alpha _{4.3}\rho _{CO_{2}}L/w_{4.3}=34,254\times 1/46.2=741.43\)

\(\alpha _{s4.3}(741.43,\infty )=E_{1}(741.43)+\ln 741.34+0.5772+0.5 – E_{3}(741.43)= 7.686\)

\(q_{4.3}(L)=1.5548\times 10^{-8}\times 1700^{3}\times 46.2\times7.686=27,125 W/m^{2}:\)

15 μm band:

\(\alpha _{15}\rho _{CO_{2}}L/w_{15}=5917\times 1/52.4=112.92\)

\(\alpha _{s15}(112.92,\infty )=E_{1}(112.92)+\ln 112.92+0.5772+0.5 – E_{3}(112.92)= 5.804\)

\(q_{15}(L)=0.2979\times 10^{-8}\times 1700^{3}\times 52.4\times 5.804=4805 W/m^{2}\).

Finally, the total heat loss from the gas is

\(q=2\times [34,508+27,125+4805]=13.29 W/cm^{2}\)

Comparing with results from we find that the present values are ~15% lower for each band. Since the gas bands are essentially opaque, the particle absorption coefficient has no effect on the \(q_{n}\)(as opposed to \(q_{w}\))- Roughly one half of the discrepancy is due to the fact that we used the \(P_{1}\)-approximation in the previous example. Therefore, we conclude that the results from the crude step wise-gray model differ by only -10% from the much more sophisticated exponential wide band model.
Comparison with shows that the present results agree excellently with those of the mean beam length method.
