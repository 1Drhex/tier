Guest Question.

Determine the power output from a mixed pressure turbine fed with 2 kg/s of HP steam at 16 bar, 300°C and 1.5 kg/s of LP steam at 2 bar dry saturated. The expansion efficiency is 90% for both and the exhaust pressure is 0.075 bar. Consider that low pressure steam is admitted through a throttle value and there is no throttling of high pressure steam. This mixed pressure turbine develops 3 MW power full output when dry and saturated steam at 2 bar is supplied and leaves at 0.075 bar. Neglect the throttling losses.

Admin
Solution

\(\text{Let us assume that pressure after mixing of high pressure and low pressure streams is }p_{2}\text{ bar.}\\\begin{aligned}m_{HP}&= 2 kg/s, m_{LP} = 1.5 kg/s\\\text{From steam tables;} \quad\qquad \qquad \qquad &\\h_{1}&= h_{\text{at 15 bar, 300°C}} = 3034.8 kJ/kg\\s_{1}&= s_{\text{at 10 bar, 300°C}} = 6.8844 kJ/kg.K = s_{3}\\\text{Using expansion efficiency;}\qquad \qquad &\\0.9&= \frac{h_{1} – h_{3^{\prime}}}{h_{1} – h_{3}}\\\text{Let dryness fraction at state 3 be }x_{3} & so.\\s_{3}&= 6.8844 = s_{\text{fat 2bar}} + x_{3} . s_{\text{fg at 2 bar}}\\6.8844 &= 1.5301 + (x_{3} × 5.597)\\x_{3}&= 0.9566\\h_{3}&= h_{\text{f at 2 bar}} + x_{3} . h_{\text{fg at 2 bar}}\\&= 504.7 + (0.9566 × 2201.9)\\h_{3}&= 2611.04 kJ/kg\\h_{3}^{\prime}&= h_{1} – (0.9 × (h_{1} – h_{3})) = 2653.42 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{In this case it is given that this turbine develops 3 MW when LP steam is supplied at 2 bar, dry saturated and expanded upto }\\\text{0.075 bar.}\\\text{Let mass flow rate of steam in this case be }m_{s} kg/s.\\\begin{aligned}\text{In this case the enthalpy, } \qquad\quad h_{in}&= h_{\text{g at 2 bar}} = 2706.7 kJ/kg = h_{2} = h_{2}\\s_{in}&= s_{\text{g at 2 bar}} = 7.1271 kJ/kg.K.\\\text{Let dryness fraction at exit state be }&x_{out}.\\s_{out} = s_{in}&= s_{\text{f at 0.075 bar}} + x_{out} s_{\text{fg at 0.075 bar}}\\7.1271&= 0.5764 + x_{out} . 7.6750.\\x_{out}&= 0.8535\\h_{out}&= h_{\text{f at 0.075 bar}} + x_{out} . h_{\text{fg at 0.075 bar}}\\&= 168.79 + (0.8535 × 2406)\\h_{out}&= 2222.31 kJ/kg\\\text{Considering expansion efficiency } &\\0.9&= \frac{h_{in} – h_{out^{\prime}}}{h_{in} – h_{out}} = \frac{(2706.7 – h_{out^{\prime}})}{(2706.7 – 2222.31)}\\h_{out^{\prime}}&= 2270.75 kJ/kg\\m_{s} ×(h_{in} – h_{out^{\prime}})&= 3 × 10^{3}\\m_{s}&= \frac{3000}{(2706.7 − 2270.75)} = 6.88 kg/s.\end{aligned}\\\)Mixed pressure turbine is being fed with HP steam too along with LP steam. This is normally done when power available cannot be met by LP steam. During this mixed pressure operation it is quite possible that pressure of steam at exit of HP turbine may not be same as LP steam pressure of 2 bar and let us assume that the steam pressure at exit from HP steam be \(p_{2}.\) In this case LP steam has to be throttled up to pressure \(p_{2}.\) while maintaining speed constant. Thus, for throttle governing considerations.\(\\\begin{aligned}\frac{p_{2}}{(m_{HP} + m_{LP})}&= \frac{2 × 10^{2}}{m_{s}}\\p_{2}&= \frac{2 × 10^{2} × (2 + 1.5)}{6.88} = 1.017 × 10^{2} kPa\\\text{For state after mixing;}\qquad\quad \qquad \qquad &\\h_{4}&= \frac{(m_{HP} × h_{3^{\prime}}) + (m_{LP} × h_{2^{\prime}})}{(m_{HP} × m_{LP})} = 2676.25 kJ/kg\\\text{Power output from mixed pressure tur}&\text{bine}\\\text{Power}&= m_{HP}(h_{1} – h_{3^{\prime}}) + (m_{HP} + m_{LP})(h_{4} – h_{5^{\prime}})\\\text{For pressure of 1.017 bar and enthalpy }&\text{of 2676.25 kJ/kg the iseutropic expansion upto 0.075 bar can be seen.} \\h_{5}&= 2290 kJ/kg\\\text{Considering expansion efficiency, 0.9}&= \frac{h_{4} – h_{5^{\prime}}}{h_{4} – h_{5}}\\h_{5^{\prime}}&= 2328.63 kJ/kg\\\text{Power}&= 2(3034.8 – 2653.42) + (2 + 1.5)(2676.25 – 2328.63)\\&= 1979.43 kW.\end{aligned}\)
