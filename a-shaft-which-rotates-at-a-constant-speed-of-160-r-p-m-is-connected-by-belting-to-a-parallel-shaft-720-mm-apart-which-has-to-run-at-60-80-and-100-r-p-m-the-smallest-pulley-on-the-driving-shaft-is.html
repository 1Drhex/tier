Guest Question.

A shaft which rotates at a constant speed of 160 r.p.m. is connected by belting to a parallel shaft 720 mm apart, which has to run at 60, 80 and 100 r.p.m. The smallest pulley on the driving shaft is 40 mm in radius. Determine the remaining radii of the two stepped pulleys for 1. a crossed belt, and 2. an open belt. Neglect belt thickness and slip.

Admin
Solution

Given : \(N_{1}=N_{3}=N_{5}=160 r . p . m . ; x=720 mm\) ; \(N_{2}=60 \text { r.p.m.; } N_{4}=80 \text { r.p.m.; } N_{6}=100 \text { r.p.m. } ; r_{1}=40 mm\)

Let \(r_{2}, r_{3}, r_{4}, r_{5} \text { and } r_{6}\) be the radii of the pulleys 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6 respectively, as shown in Fig. 11.13.

 

1. For a crossed belt

We know that for pulleys 1 and 2,

\(\frac{N_{2}}{N_{1}}=\frac{r_{1}}{r_{2}}\)

or

\(r_{2}=r_{1} \times \frac{N_{1}}{N_{2}}=40 \times \frac{160}{60}=106.7 mm \)

and for pulleys 3 and 4,

\(\frac{N_{4}}{N_{3}}=\frac{r_{3}}{r_{4}} \text { or } r_{4}=r_{3} \times \frac{N_{3}}{N_{4}}=r_{3} \times \frac{160}{80}=2 r_{3}\)

We know that for a crossed belt drive,

\(r_{1}+r_{2}=r_{3}+r_{4}=r_{5}+r_{6}=40+106.7=146.7 mm\) …(i)

\(\therefore \) \(r_{3}+2 r_{3}=146.7 \text { or } \quad r_{3}=146.7 / 3=48.9 mm\)

and

\(r_{4}=2 r_{3}=2 \times 48.9=97.8 mm\)

Now for pulleys 5 and 6,

\(\frac{N_{6}}{N_{5}}=\frac{r_{5}}{r_{6}} \text { or } r_{6}=r_{5} \times \frac{N_{5}}{N_{6}}=r_{5} \times \frac{160}{100}=1.6 r_{5}\)

From equation (i),

\(r_{5}+1.6 r_{5}=146.7 \text { or } r_{5}=146.7 / 2.6=56.4 mm\)

and

\(r_{6}=1.6 r_{5}=1.6 \times 56.4=90.2 mm\)

 

2. For an open belt

We know that for pulleys 1 and 2,

\(\frac{N_{2}}{N_{1}}=\frac{r_{1}}{r_{2}} \text { or } r_{2}=r_{1} \times \frac{N_{1}}{N_{2}}=40 \times \frac{160}{60}=106.7 mm\)

and for pulleys 3 and 4,

\(\frac{N_{4}}{N_{3}}=\frac{r_{3}}{r_{4}} \text { or } r_{4}=r_{3} \times \frac{N_{3}}{N_{4}}=r_{3} \times \frac{160}{80}=2 r_{3}\)

We know that length of belt for an open belt drive,

\(L=\pi\left(r_{1}+r_{2}\right)+\frac{\left(r_{2}-r_{1}\right)^{2}}{x}+2 x\)

 

\(=\pi(40+106.7)+\frac{(106.7-40)^{2}}{720}+2 \times 720=1907 mm\)

Since the length of the belt in an open belt drive is constant, therefore for pulleys 3 and 4, length of the belt (L),

\(1907=\pi\left(r_{3}+r_{4}\right)+\frac{\left(r_{4}-r_{3}\right)^{2}}{x}+2 x\)

 

\(=\pi\left(r_{3}+2 r_{3}\right)+\frac{\left(2 r_{3}-r_{3}\right)^{2}}{720}+2 \times 720\)

 

\(=9.426 r_{3}+0.0014\left(r_{3}\right)^{2}+1440\)

or

\(0.0014\left(r_{3}\right)^{2}+9.426 r_{3}-467=0\)

 

\(\therefore \quad r_{3}=\frac{-9.426 \pm \sqrt{(9.426)^{2}+4 \times 0.0014 \times 467}}{2 \times 0.0014}\)

 

\(=\frac{-9.426 \pm 9.564}{0.0028}=49.3 mm\) …(Taking +ve sign)

and

\(r_{4}=2 r_{3}=2 \times 49.3=98.6 mm\)

Now for pulleys 5 and 6,

\(\frac{N_{6}}{N_{5}}=\frac{r_{5}}{r_{6}} \text { or }\)

 

\(r_{6}=\frac{N_{5}}{N_{6}} \times r_{5}=\frac{160}{100} \times r_{5}=1.6 r_{5}\)

and length of the belt (L),

\(1907=\pi\left(r_{5}+r_{6}\right)+\frac{\left(r_{6}-r_{5}\right)^{2}}{x}+2 x\)

 

\(=\pi\left(r_{5}+1.6 r_{5}\right)+\frac{\left(1.6 r_{5}-r_{5}\right)^{2}}{720}+2 \times 720\)

 

\(=8.17 r_{5}+0.0005\left(r_{5}\right)^{2}+1440\)

 

or \(0.0005\left(r_{5}\right)^{2}+8.17 r_{5}-467=0\)

 

\(\therefore \quad r_{5}=\frac{-8.17 \pm \sqrt{(8.17)^{2}+4 \times 0.0005 \times 467}}{2 \times 0.0005}\)

 

\(=\frac{-8.17 \pm 8.23}{0.001}=60 mm\) …(Taking +ve sign)

and

\(r_{6}=1.6 r_{5}=1.6 \times 60=96 mm\)
