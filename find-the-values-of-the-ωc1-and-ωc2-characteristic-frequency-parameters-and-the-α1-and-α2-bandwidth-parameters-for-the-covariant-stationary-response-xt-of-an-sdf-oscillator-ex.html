Guest Question.

Find the values of the \( \omega _{c1} \) and \( \omega _{c2} \) characteristic frequency parameters and the \( \alpha _{1} \) and \( \alpha _{2} \) bandwidth parameters for the covariant stationary response \( \left\{X(t)\right\} \) of an SDF oscillator excited by white noise

Admin
Solution

Two of the four spectral moments of interest can be obtained almost trivially. In particular, \( \lambda _{0} \) is the response variance, so Eq. 6.45

\(\sigma _{X}^{2}\approx \frac{\pi S_{FF}(\omega _{0})}{2m^{2}\zeta \omega _{0}^{3}} =\frac{\pi S_{FF}(\omega _{0})}{ck} \)

gives

\( \lambda _{0}=\sigma ^{2}_{X}=\frac{\pi S_{0}}{2m^{2}\zeta \omega ^{3}_{0} } =\frac{\pi S_{0}}{ck} \)

in which \( S_{0} \) is the autospectral density of the excitation. Similarly, \( \lambda _{2} \) is the variance of the derivative of the response, and this can be found from Eq. 5.63

\( \sigma _{\dot{X} }^{2}=\frac{G_{0}}{4m^{2}\zeta \omega _{0}} \)

as

\( \lambda _{2}=\sigma ^{2}_{\dot{X}}=\frac{\pi S_{0}}{2m^{2}\zeta \omega _{0} } =\frac{\pi S_{0}}{mc} \)

The \( \lambda _{1} \) spectral moment is not a variance quantity, but it can be evaluated as

\( \lambda _{1}=2\int_{0}^{\infty }{\omega S_{XX}(\omega )} d\omega =2S_{0}\int_{0}^{\infty }{\omega \left|H_{x}(\omega )\right|^{2} } d\omega \) \( =\frac{2S_{0}}{m^{2}} \int_{0}^{\infty }{\frac{\omega d\omega }{(\omega ^{2}-\omega ^{2}_{0})^{2}(2\zeta \omega _{0}\omega )^{2}} } \)

This integral over \( \omega \) from zero to infinity is not appropriate for evaluation by the calculus of residues, which is useful for most of our frequency-domain integrals, but it can be shown that the integrand is the differential of an arctangent function. The result can be written as

\( \lambda _{1}=\frac{S_{0}}{2m^{2}\zeta \omega _{0}\omega _{d}}\left(\frac{\pi}{2}+\tan ^{-1}\left[\frac{1-2\zeta ^{2}}{2\zeta (1-\zeta ^{2})^{1/2}} \right] \right) \) \( =\frac{S_{0}}{2m^{2}\zeta \omega _{0}\omega _{d}}\left(\pi-2\tan ^{-1}\left[\frac{\zeta}{ (1-\zeta ^{2})^{1/2}} \right] \right) \)

in which the slightly simpler final form has been derived by use of trigonometric identities. The \( \lambda _{4} \) spectral moment is somewhat more of a problem. In particular, \( \left|H_{x}(\omega )\right| ^{2} \) behaves like \( \omega ^{-4} \) for \( \left|\omega \right| \) tending to infinity, and this shows that \( \omega ^{4}\left|H_{x}(\omega )\right| ^{2} \) is not integrable. Actually, this is not surprising inasmuch as \( \lambda _{4}=\sigma ^{2}_{X} \) , and we noted in Section 5.6 that the variance of . \( \ddot {X}(t) \) is infinite for the SDF system excited by a delta-correlated process. Thus, we have \( \lambda _{4}=\infty \) . From these spectral moments, we can write the characteristic frequency parameters as

\( \omega _{c1}=\frac{\omega _{0}}{(1-\zeta ^{2})^{1/2}} \left(1-\frac{2}{\pi}\tan ^{-1}\left[\frac{\zeta }{(1-\zeta ^{2})^{1/2}} \right] \right) , \omega _{c2=\omega _{0}} \)

One may note that, in general, \( \omega _{c1} \) is less than the resonant frequency \( \omega _{0} \) for the oscillator, and for \( \zeta \lt \lt 1 \) it tends to \( \omega _{0} \) like \( \omega _{c1}\approx \omega _{0}(1-2\zeta /\pi) \) , while \( \omega _{c2} \) is identical to the resonant frequency. Similarly, the bandwidth parameters are

\( \alpha _{1}=\frac{1}{(1-\zeta ^{2})^{1/2}}\left(1-\frac{2}{\pi}\tan ^{-1}\left[\frac{\zeta }{(1-\zeta ^{2})^{1/2}} \right] \right) , \alpha _{2}=0 \)

Clearly, this is a problem for which the two bandwidth parameters give very different results. In fact, the \( \alpha _{2} \) parameter fails to give any useful information, because it gives \( \alpha _{2}=0 \) , suggesting a broadband \( \left\{X\left(t\right) \right\} \) process regardless of the value of \( \zeta \) . We anticipate, though, that \( \zeta \lt \lt 1 \) will be a narrowband situation, based on the shape of the \( S_{XX}(\omega )=S_{0}\left|H_{x}(\omega) \right| ^{2} \) function. The \( \alpha _{1} \) parameter

does behave as we might expect. In particular, it tends to unity as \( \zeta \) tends to zero, indicating that the response process is narrowband in this situation. For \( \zeta \lt \lt 1 \) , the limiting behavior is linear in \( \zeta \) with \( \alpha _{1}\approx 1-2\zeta /\pi \) .

According to Eq. 7.13

\(\rho _{X\ddot{X} }\equiv \frac{K_{X\ddot{X}}(t,t)}{\sigma _{X}\sigma _{\ddot{X} }} =-\frac{\sigma _{\dot{X} }^{2}}{\sigma _{X}\sigma _{\ddot{X} }} =-\alpha _{2}\)

\( \alpha _{2} \) is also the negative of the correlation coefficient \( \rho _{X\ddot{X}} \) . The fact that \( \alpha _{2}=0 \) for the SDF response to white noise then means that \( X(t) \) and ,\( \ddot{X}(t) \) , at the same instant of time \( t \) , are uncorrelated for this system. This follows directly, though, from the fact that the cross-covariance \( K_{X\ddot{X}} \) is finite , while the variance of \( \ddot{X}(t) \) is infinite.
