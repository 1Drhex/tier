Guest Question.

A single-stage transonic compressor operates with axial flow at inlet. The inlet absolute stagnation temperature is 288 K and the inlet absolute stagnation pressure is 101 kPa. The relative flow angle at inlet to the rotor is 45° and the inlet relative Mach number is 0.9.
a. Calculate the rotor blade speed and the inlet relative stagnation pressure.
b. The mean radius and the mass flow rate per unit annulus area are constant through the rotor. If the rotor loss coefficient is 0.068 and the rotor exit relative Mach number is 0.5, find the rotor exit relative flow angle and determine the static pressure ratio across the rotor.
c. Show that the absolute stagnation temperature and pressure at entry to the stator are 322 K and 145 kPa, respectively. Determine the total-to-total isentropic efficiency of the compressor stage if the stagnation pressure loss coefficient for the stator is 0.04.

Admin
Solution

a. \(T_{01}=288 K, p_{01}=101 kPa\). Given that the flow is axial at inlet, the absolute inlet Mach number can be calculated (using the Mach number triangles shown in Figure 5.4):

\( M_{1} =M_{1,rel}\cos 45°=0.9/\sqrt{2}=0.6364 \)

The inlet static temperature can be calculated from the inlet Mach number and inlet stagnation temperature as follows:

\( T_{1}=T_{01}\left(1+\left(\gamma -1\right) M^{2}_{1} /2\right) ^{-1} =266.4 K \)

The blade speed can then be determined from the inlet Mach number triangle and the fact that the relative inflow angle is 45°:

\( U=M_{1}\sqrt{\gamma R T_{1}} =0.634\times \sqrt{1.4\times 287.15\times 266.4} =208.3 m/s\)

The blade relative stagnation pressure can be found from compressible flow tables:

\( p_{01,rel}=\frac{p_{01}\times p_{1}/p_{01}}{p_{1}/ p_{01,rel}}=\frac{101\times 0.7614}{0.5913}=130 kPa \)

Note that \( p_{1}=101\times 0.7614=76.9 kPa .\)

b. To relate the conditions across the rotor, first calculate the ratio of relative stagnation pressures:

\( Y_{p} =\frac{1-p_{02,rel}/p_{01,rel}}{1-p_{1}/ p_{01,rel}} \) , which implies that \(\frac{p_{02,rel}}{ p_{01,rel}} =1-Y_{p}\left(1-p_{1}/ p_{01,rel}\right) \)

Therefore,

\(\frac{p_{02,rel}}{ p_{01,rel}} =1-0.068\times \left(1-0.5913\right)=0.9722 \)

Applying continuity across the rotor,

\(\frac{\dot{m}\sqrt{C_{p}T_{01,rel}}}{A_{x}\cos \beta _{1} p_{01,rel} } =Q\left(M_{1,rel}\right)= \frac{\dot{m}\sqrt{C_{p}T_{02,rel}}}{A_{x}\cos \beta _{2} p_{02,rel} }\times \frac{\cos \beta _{2}}{\cos \beta _{1}}\times \frac{p_{02,rel}}{ p_{01,rel}} \)

Therefore,

\(\cos \beta _{2}=\frac{Q\left(M_{1,rel}\right)}{Q\left(M_{2,rel}\right)} \times \cos \beta _{1}\times \frac{p_{01,rel}}{ p_{02,rel}} \)

 

This is true since \( T_{02,rel}=T_{01,rel}\) (constant radius) and \(\dot{m}/A_{x}\) is constant. Putting in the values from the question and using the compressible flow tables:

 

\(\cos \beta _{2}=\frac{Q\left(0.9\right)}{Q\left(0.5\right)} \times \cos 45°\times \frac{1}{0.9722}=\frac{1.2698}{0.9561} \times \frac{1}{\sqrt{2} } \times \frac{1}{0.9722} =0.9659 \)

 

which implies that \(\beta _{2}=15°\).

The static pressure ratio is then determined from the various ratios just derived:

\( \frac{p_{2} }{p_{1} } =\frac{p_{2}/p_{02,rel}\times p_{02,rel}/p_{01,rel}}{p_{1}/p_{01,rel}}=\frac{0.8430\times 0.9722}{0.5913}=1.386 \)

Note that \(p_{2}=0.8430 \times0.9722 \times 130 =106.6 kPa.\)

c. To calculate the conditions at stator inlet, apply the exit Mach number triangle from the rotor (as shown in Figure 5.4) to convert the properties from the relative to absolute frame of reference. Using the compressible flow tables, the static temperature and relative velocity at rotor exit can be found:

 

\(T_{2} =\frac{T_{1}\times T_{2} /T_{02,rel}}{T_{1}/T_{01,rel}} =\frac{266.4\times 0.9524}{0.8606}=294.8 K \left(since T_{02,rel}=T_{01,rel}\right) \)

 

\( W_{2}= M_{2,rel}\sqrt{\gamma R T_{2}} =0.5\times \sqrt{1.4\times 287.15\times 294.8}=172.1 m/s \)

The velocity triangle can be used to find the absolute flow Mach number, which is needed to determine the absolute stagnation quantities:

\(M_{2}=\frac{c_{2}}{\sqrt{\gamma R T_{2}}} =\frac{\sqrt{\left( W_{2}\cos 15°\right) ^{2} + \left(U- W_{2} \sin 15°\right) ^{2}}}{\sqrt{\gamma R T_{2}}} =0.6778\)

It then follows that

\( T_{02}=T_{2} \left[1+\left(\gamma -1\right)M^{2}_{2} /2 \right] =321.9 K\)

\( p_{02}=p_{2}\left(1+\left(\gamma -1\right)M^{2}_{2} /2\right)^{\gamma /\left(\gamma -1\right)} =145 kPa \)

The overall stage efficiency can now be calculated, using Eqs (5.4) and (5.8):

\(\eta _{tt}=\frac{\Delta W_{\min } }{\Delta W} \cong 1-\frac{T_{03} \Delta s_{stage}}{h_{03}-h_{01}} \) (5.4)

 

\( \Delta s_{rotor}\cong \frac{R\Delta p_{0,rotor} }{p_{01,rel}} =RY_{p,rotor} \left(1-p_{1}/ p_{01,rel}\right) \) (5.8)

 

\(\eta _{tt} =\frac{T_{02s}-T_{01}}{T_{02}-T_{01}} =1-\frac{T_{02}-T_{02s}}{T_{02}-T_{01}}=1-\frac{T_{02}\left(\Delta s_{rotor} + \Delta s_{stator}\right)/C_{p}}{T_{02}-T_{01}} \)

which implies that

\( \Delta s_{rotor}=R Y_{p}\left(1-\frac{p_{1}}{p_{01,rel}} \right) =287.15\times 0.068\times \left(1-76.9/130\right)=7.98 J/kg K \)

and

\( \Delta s_{stator}=R Y_{p}\left(1-\frac{p_{2}}{p_{02}} \right) =287.15\times 0.04\times \left(1-106.6/145\right)=3.04 J/kg K \)

Thus,

\(\eta _{tt}=1-\frac{321.9\times \left(7.98+3.04\right)/1005 }{321.9-288}=0.896 \)

This is a realistic efficiency value for a single-stage transonic compressor.
