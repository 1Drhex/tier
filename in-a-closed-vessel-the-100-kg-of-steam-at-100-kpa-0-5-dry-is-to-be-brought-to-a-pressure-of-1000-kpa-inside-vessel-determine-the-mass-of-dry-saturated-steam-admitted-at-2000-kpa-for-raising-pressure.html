Guest Question.

In a closed vessel the 100 kg of steam at 100 kPa, 0.5 dry is to be brought to a pressure of 1000 kPa inside vessel. Determine the mass of dry saturated steam admitted at 2000 kPa for raising pressure. Also determine the final quality.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{It is a constant volume process.}\\\quad\text{Volume of vessel V = (Mass of vapour) × (Specific volume of vapour)}\\\quad\text{Initial specific volume, v}_{1}\\\begin{aligned}v_{1}&= v_{\text{f, 100kPa}} + x_{1} . v_{\text{fg, 100kPa}}\\\text{at 100 kPa from steam table;}\quad\qquad \qquad &\\h_{\text{f, 100 kPa}}&= 417.46 kJ/kg\\u_{\text{f, 100 kPa}}&= 417.36 kJ/kg\\v_{\text{f, 100 kPa}}&= 0.001043 m^{3}/kg\\h_{\text{fg, 100 kPa}}&= 2258 kJ/kg,\\u_{\text{fg, 100 kPa}}&= 2088.7 kJ/kg,\\v_{\text{g, 100 kPa}}&= 1.6940 m^{3}/kg\\\text{given}\qquad \qquad\qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad x_{1}&= 0.5, v_{1} = 0.8475 m^{3}/kg\\\text{Enthalpy at 1, }h_{1} = 417.46 + 0.5 × 2258 &= 1546.46 kJ/kg\\\text{Thus, volume of vessel}&= (100 × 0.5) × (0.8475)\\V&= 42.375 m^{3}\\\text{Internal energy in the beginning}&= U_{1} = m_{1} × u_{1}\\&= 100 (417.36 + 0.5 × 2088.7)\\U_{1}&= 146171 kJ\end{aligned}\\\text{Let the mass of dry steam added be ‘m’, Final specific volume inside vessel, v}_{2}\\\begin{aligned}v_{2}&= v_{\text{f, 1000 kPa}} + x_{2} . v_{\text{fg, 1000 kPa}}\\\text{At 2000 kPa, from steam table,}\qquad\qquad &\\v_{\text{g, 2000 kPa}}&= 0.09963 m^{3}/kg\\u_{\text{g, 2000 kPa}}&= 2600.3 kJ/kg\\h_{\text{g, 2000 kPa}}&= 2799.5 kJ/kg\\\text{Total mass inside vessel}&= \text{Mass of steam at 2000 kPa + Mass of mixture at 100 kPa}\\\frac{V}{v_{2}}&= \frac{V}{v_{\text{g, 2000 kPa}}} + \frac{V}{v_{1}}\\\frac{42.375}{v_{2}}&= \frac{42.375}{0.09963} + \frac{42.375}{0.8475}\\v_{2}&= 0.089149\\\text{substituting} \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad v_{2} &= v_{\text{f, 1000 kPa}} + x_{2}.v_{\text{fg, 1000 kPa}}\\\text{At 1000 kPa from steam table,} \quad\qquad \qquad &\\h_{\text{f, 1000 kPa}}&= 762.81 kJ/kg,\\h_{\text{fg, 1000 kPa}}&= 2015.3 kJ/kg v_{\text{f, 1000 kPa}} = 0.001127 m^{3}/kg\\v_{\text{g, 1000 kPa}}&= 0.19444 m^{3}/kg\\\text{it gives}\qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad x_{2}&= 0.455\\\text{For adiabatic mixing}\qquad \quad\qquad \qquad \qquad &\\(100 + m) · h_{2}&= 100 × h_{1} + m × h_{\text{g, 2000 kPa}}\\(100 + m) · (762.81 + 0.455 × 2015.3)&= \left\{100 × (1546.46)\right\} + \left\{m × 2799.5\right\} \\\text{It gives upon solving;}\quad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad&\\(100 + m) (1679.77)&= 154646 + (2799.5)m\\m&= 11.91 kg\\\text{Mass of dry steam at 2000 kPa to be added}&= \textbf{11.91 kg}\\\text{Quality of final mixture}&= \textbf{0.455}\end{aligned}\)
