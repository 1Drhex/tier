Guest Question.

The reaction \(A \overset{k}{\rightarrow} R + S\) is irreversible and first order. It is conducted in a PFR with 50 tubes, each with “½’’ in diameter and 1.0m of height. 200 kg/h of reactant A (MW=80 g/gmol) with 30% inert is introduced at a pressure of 50 bar at 500◦C. The output conversion is 80%. Calculate the average residence time.

Admin
Solution

The reaction rate will be:
\(-r_{A}=kC_{A}=C_{A0}\frac{(1-X_{A})}{1+\varepsilon _{A}X_{A}}\)

Substituting that rate in PFR equation, we get:
\(\tau =-\frac{1}{k}[(1+\varepsilon _{A})\ln (1-X_{A})+\varepsilon _{A}X_{A}]\) (20.6)

To determine \(\tau\), we need to calculate both the volume and flow.

As the total mass flow of G is given, we have for each tube:
\(G_{1}=\frac{G}{50}=4 kg/h\)

The molar flow would be:
\(F_{A0}=\frac{G_{1}}{M_{A}}=\frac{4}{80}=0.05 kmol/h=50 mol/h\)

Calculation of initial concentration:
\(C_{A0}=\frac{P_{A0}}{RT}=\frac{0.7\times 50}{0.082\times 773}=5.52 \times 10^{-1}mol/L\)

Hence:
\(F_{A0}=C_{A0}\nu _{0}\)

With the values of flow and concentration, we have:
\(\nu _{0}=\frac{F_{A0}}{C_{A0}}=90.5 L/h=1.5 L/min\)

Calculating the volume of the tube:
\(V=\frac{\pi d^{2}}{4}.L=126 cm^{3}\)

Hence:
\(\tau =\frac{V}{\nu _{0}}=0.084 min =5.04 s\)

Calculating \(\varepsilon _{A}\):

A R S Inert Total
Initial 0.7 0 0 0.3 1
Final 0 0.7 0.7 0.3 1.7
\(\varepsilon _{A}=0.7\)

With these values, we calculate the reaction rate constant using Equation 20.6 :
\(k=35 min^{-1}\)

The average residence time can be determined by Equation 20.3 (PFR equation):
\(\bar{t}=-\frac{1}{k\ln (1-X_{A})}\)
\(\bar{t}=2.76 s\)
