Guest Question.

The temperature on a cold winter day in the mountains of Wyoming is \(-22^{\circ} \mathrm{F}\) at an elevation of \(10,000 \mathrm{ft}\). Calculate the density of the air assuming the same pressure as in the local atmosphere; also find the speed of sound.

Admin
Solution

From Table \(\mathrm{B} .3\) we find the atmospheric pressure at an elevation of \(10,000 \mathrm{ft}\) to be \(10.1\) psi. The absolute temperature is found to be

\(T=-22+460=438^{\circ} \mathrm{R}\)

Using the ideal-gas law, the density is calculated as

\(\begin{aligned}\rho &=\frac{p}{R T} \\&=\frac{10.1 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{in}^{2} \times 144 \mathrm{in}^{2} / \mathrm{ft}^{2}}{\left(1716 \mathrm{ft}-\mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{slug}-{ }^{\circ} \mathrm{R}\right) \times 438^{\circ} \mathrm{R}}=0.00194 \mathrm{slug} / \mathrm{ft}^{3}\end{aligned}\)

The speed of sound, using Eq. 1.7.17, is determined to be

\(c=\sqrt{\left.\frac{d p}{d \rho}\right|_{s}}=\sqrt{k R T} (1.7.17)\)

\(\begin{aligned}c &=\sqrt{k R T} \\&=\sqrt{1.4 \times\left(1716 \mathrm{ft}-\mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{slug}-{ }^{\circ} \mathrm{R}\right) \times 438^{\circ} \mathrm{R}}=1026 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{sec}\end{aligned}\)

Note: The gas constant in the foregoing equations has units of \(\mathrm{ft}-\mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{slug}-{ }^{\circ} \mathrm{R}\) so that the appropriate units result. Express slug \(=1 \mathrm{~b}-\mathrm{sec}^{2} / \mathrm{ft} (\) from \(m=F / a)\) to observe that this is true.
