Guest Question.

Figure 7.12a shows fl ow over a fl at wall, where the wall itself consists of 10 strip heaters (Figure 7.12b) placed side by side. Each strip heater is 1-1/2 in. wide and has an effective heating length (into the page) of 10 in. Each heater provides 10 W of energy to the fluid. The fluid itself is oxygen traveling at 20 ft /s with a free-stream temperature of 300°R. According to the manufacturer, the heater- surface temperature should not exceed 800°R. Determine the maximum heater-surface temperature for the conditions given.

Admin
Solution

As the oxygen passes over the plate, heat is transferred from the heaters to the oxygen. Th e greatest temperature difference will therefore exist at the front of the plate. Th e maximum surface temperature correspondingly will exist at plate end.

Assumptions

1. Th e system is at steady state.
2. Oxygen properties are constant.
3. All heat generated by the heaters is transferred to the oxygen through the upper surface; the insulation makes the heat loss through the lower surface negligible.

Without knowing the film temperature at which we evaluate properties, we therefore assume that fluid properties can be taken at \(\left(300^{\circ }R+800^{\circ } R\right) /2=550^{\circ } R\approx 540^{\circ } R\) as a first estimate. From Appendix Table D.6 for oxygen at\( 540^{\circ }R\)

 

\(\rho =0.0812lbm/ft^{3}\)

 

\(c_{p} =0.2918 BTU/\left(lbm.R^{\circ } \right)\)

 

\(\nu =17.07\times 10^{-5} ft^{2} /s\)

 

\(k_{f} =0.01546 BTU/\left(hr.ft.R^{\circ } \right)\)

 

 

\(\alpha =0.8862ft^{2}/hr\)

 

Pr=0.709

The wall flux for any number of heaters is found to be

\(q^{\prime \prime }_{w} =\frac{n\left(10.0\right) }{n\left(1.5\right)\left(10.125\right) }=0.658W/in^{2} \left\lgroup\frac{1}{0.2928}\frac{BTU}{W}\frac{144in{2} }{ft^{2} } \right\rgroup =324BTU/\left(hr.ft^{2} \right) \)

 

Anywhere along the plate we can write

\(q^{''}_{w} =h_{x} \left(T_{w}-T_{\infty } \right) \)

 

At plate end, x = L and

\(\frac{q^{''}_{w} L }{k_{f} } =\frac{h_{x}L }{k_{f} } \left(T_{w}-T_{\infty } \right)=Nu_{L} \left(T_{w}-T_{\infty } \right) \mid _{L} \)

 

Rearranging and substituting from Equation 7.23 for the Nusselt number gives

 

\(T_{w} \mid _{L} =T_{\infty }+\frac{q^{''}_{w}L }{k_{f} } \frac{1}{Nu_{L} }= T_{\infty }+\frac{q^{''}_{w}L }{k_{f} } \frac{1}{0.453Re^{1/2}_{L}Pr^{1/3} } \)

 

The Reynolds number at the end of 10 heaters (plate end) is

 

\(Re_{L}=\frac{V_{\infty }L }{\nu } =\frac{20\left(10\right)\left(\frac{1.5}{12} \right) }{17.07\times 10^{-5} } =1.46\times 10^{5}\)

 

So the flow is laminar, and we are justified in using Equation 7.23. Substituting, we find for the wall temperature at plate end

 

\(T_{w} \mid _{L} =300+\frac{324\left(10\right)\left\lgroup\frac{1.5}{12} \right\rgroup }{0.01546} \frac{1}{0.453\left(1.46\times 10^{5} \right) ^{1/2}\left(0.709\right) ^{1/3} } =470^{\circ } R\)

 

This result is dependent on the properties of oxygen being evaluated at \(540^{\circ } R\). A refined estimate can be obtained by evaluating properties at (300 + 470)12 =\( 385^{\circ } R\). Th is is reserved as an exercise.
