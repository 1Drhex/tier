Guest Question.

Single line-to-ground short-circuit calculations using \(Z_{bus 0} \), \(Z_{bus 1} \), and \(Z_{bus 2} \)

Faults at buses 1 and 2 for the three-phase power system given in are of interest. The prefault voltage is 1.05 per unit. Prefault load current is neglected. (a) Determine the per-unit zero-, positive-, and negative-sequence bus impedance matrices. (b) Find the subtransient fault current in per-unit for a bolted single line-to-ground fault current from phase a to ground at bus 1, and (c) at bus 2. Find the per-unit line-to-ground voltages at (d) bus 1, and (e) bus 2 during the single line-to ground fault at bus 1.

Admin
Solution

a. Referring to Figure 9.4(a), the zero-sequence bus admittance matrix is

\(Y_{bus 0} =-j\left [ \begin{matrix} 20 & 0 \\ 0 & 4 \end{matrix} \right ]\) per unit

Inverting \(Y_{bus 0}\),

\(Z_{bus 0} =j\left [ \begin{matrix} 0.05 & 0 \\ 0 & 0.25 \end{matrix} \right ] \) per unit

Note that the transformer leakage reactances and the zero-sequence transmissionline reactance in Figure 9.4(a) have no effect on \(Z_{bus 0}\). The transformer D connections block the flow of zero-sequence current from the transformers to bus 1 and 2. The positive-sequence bus admittance matrix, from Figure 9.4(b), is

\(Y_{bus 1} =-j\left [ \begin{matrix} 9.9454& -3.2787\\ -3.2787& 8.2787 \end{matrix} \right ]\) per unit

Inverting \(Y_{bus 1}\),

\(Z_{bus 1} =j\left [ \begin{matrix} 0.11565& 0.04580\\ 0.04580& 0.13893 \end{matrix} \right ] \) per unit

Similarly, from Figure 9.4(c)

\(Y_{bus 2} =-j\left [ \begin{matrix} 9.1611& -32787\\ -3.2787& 8.0406 \end{matrix} \right ] \) per unit

Inverting \(Z_{bus 2}\),

\(Z_{bus 2} =j\left [ \begin{matrix} 0.12781& 0.05212\\ 0.05212& 0.14562 \end{matrix} \right ] \) per unit

b. From (9.5.3), with n51 and \(Z_{F} =0\), the sequence fault currents are

\(I_{n-0} =I_{n-1}=I_{n-2}=\frac{V_{F} }{Z_{nn-0}+Z_{nn-1}+Z_{nn-2}+3Z_{F} }\) (9.5.3)

\(I_{1-0} =I_{1-1}=I_{1-2}=\frac{V_{F} }{Z_{11-0}+Z_{11-1}+Z_{11-2}} =\frac{1.05\angle 0°}{j\left(0.05+0.11565+0.12781\right) }=\frac{1.05}{j0.29346 } =-j3.578\) per unit

The subtransient fault currents at bus 1 are, from (8.1.16),

\(I_{P} =AI_{S} \) (8.1.16)

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} I^{\prime \prime }_{1a} \\ I^{\prime \prime }_{1b} \\ I^{\prime \prime }_{1c} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & a^{2} & a \\ 1 & a & a^{2} \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} -j3.578 \\ -j3.578 \\ -j3.578 \end{matrix} \right ]= \left [ \begin{matrix} -j10.73 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \) per unit

c. Again from (9.5.3), with n = 2 and \(Z_{F}=0\),

\(I_{2-0} =I_{2-1}=I_{2-2}=\frac{V_{F} }{Z_{22-0}+Z_{22-1}+Z_{22-2}} =\frac{1.05\angle 0°}{j\left(0.25+0.13893+0.14562\right) }=\frac{1.05}{j0.53455} =-j1.96427\) per unit

and

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} I^{\prime \prime }_{2a} \\ I^{\prime \prime }_{2b} \\ I^{\prime \prime }_{2c} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & a^{2} & a \\ 1 & a & a^{2} \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} -j1.96427 \\ -j1.96427 \\ -j1.96427 \end{matrix} \right ]= \left [ \begin{matrix} -j5.8928\\ 0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ]\) per unit

This is the same result as obtained in .

d. The sequence components of the line-to-ground voltages at bus 1 during the fault at bus 1 are, from (9.5.9), with k=1 and n = 1,

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} V_{k-0} \\ V_{k-1} \\ V_{k-2} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ V_{F} \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ] -\left [ \begin{matrix} Z_{kn-0} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & Z_{kn-1} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & Z_{kn-2} \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} I_{n-0} \\ I_{n-1} \\ I_{n-2} \end{matrix} \right ] \) (9.5.9)

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} V_{1-0} \\ V_{1-1} \\ V_{1-2} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1.05\angle 0° \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ] -\left [ \begin{matrix} j0.05 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & j0.11565 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & j0.12781 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} -j3.578 \\ -j3.578 \\ -j3.578 \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} -0.1789 \\ 0.6362 \\ -0.4573 \end{matrix} \right ]\) per unit

and the line-to-ground voltages at bus 1 during the fault at bus 1 are

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} V_{1-ag} \\ V_{1-bg} \\ V_{1-cg} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & a^{2} & a \\ 1 & a & a^{2} \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} -0.1789 \\ +0.6362\\ -0.4573\end{matrix} \right ]= \left [ \begin{matrix} 0\\ 0.9843\angle 254.2° \\ 0.9843\angle 105.8° \end{matrix} \right ]\) per unit

e. The sequence components of the line-to-ground voltages at bus 2 during the fault at bus 1 are, from (9.5.9), with k = 2 and n = 1,

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} V_{2-0} \\ V_{2-1} \\ V_{2-2} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1.05\angle 0° \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ] -\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & j0.04580 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & j0.05212 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} -j3.578 \\ -j3.578 \\ -j3.578 \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0.8861 \\ -0.18649 \end{matrix} \right ]\) per unit

Note that since both bus 1 and 2 are on the low-voltage side of the Δ–Y transformers in Figure 9.3, there is no shift in the phase angles of these sequence voltages. From the above, the line-to-ground voltages at bus 2 during the fault at bus 1 are

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} V_{2-ag} \\ V_{2-bg} \\ V_{2-cg} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & a^{2} & a \\ 1 & a & a^{2} \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0.8861 \\ -0.18649 \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 0.70 \\ 0.9926\angle 249.4° \\ 0.9926\angle 110.6° \end{matrix} \right ] \) per unit
