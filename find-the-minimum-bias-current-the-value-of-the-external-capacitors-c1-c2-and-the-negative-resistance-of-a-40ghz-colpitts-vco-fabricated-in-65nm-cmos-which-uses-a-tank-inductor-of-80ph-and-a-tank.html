Guest Question.

Find the minimum bias current, the value of the external capacitors \(C_{1}, C_{2}\), and the negative resistance of a \(40 \mathrm{GHz}\) Colpitts VCO fabricated in \(65 \mathrm{~nm}\) CMOS which uses a tank inductor of 80 pH and a tank Q (dominated by the inductor) of 5. Assume that \(I_{D S}=0.15 \mathrm{~mA} / \mu \mathrm{m}, g_{m}^{\prime}=0.8 \mathrm{mS} / \mu \mathrm{m}, C_{g s}^{\prime}=0.7 \mathrm{fF} / \mu \mathrm{m}, C_{s d}^{\prime}=0.35 \mathrm{fF} / \mu \mathrm{m}\), and \(C_{s b}^{\prime}=0.6 \mathrm{fF} / \mu \mathrm{m}\) and that the tank voltage amplitude \(V_{O S C}\) is \(0.6 \mathrm{~V}\).

Admin
Solution

According to (10.41), the minimum transconductance and bias current needed for oscillation are obtained when \( C_{1}=C_{2}\) and \(G_{m} R_{P}=4\) where \(R_{P}=Q \omega_{O S C} L=100.5 \Omega . \) This implies that \(G_{m}=40 \mathrm{mS}\) and

\(I_{B I A S}=G_{m} \frac{V_{O S C}}{2}=\frac{0.04 \times 0.6}{2}=12 \mathrm{~mA}\)

The required MOSFET size is \(W=I_{B I A S} /(0.15 \mathrm{~mA} / \mu \mathrm{m})=80 \mu \mathrm{m}\) and \(g_{m}=64 \mathrm{mS}, C_{g s}=56 \mathrm{fF}\), \(C_{g d}=28 \mathrm{fF}, C_{s b}=48 \mathrm{fF}\).
The equivalent capacitance needed for oscillation at \(40 \mathrm{GHz}\) is calculated next
\(C_{e q}=\frac{1}{\omega_{O S C}^{2} L}=\frac{1}{\left(6.28 \times 4 \times 10^{10}\right)^{2} \times 80 \times 10^{-12}}=198 f F\) .

From (10.36) and accounting for } \(C_{g d}\), \(C_{g s}\), and \( C_{s b}\)

\(C_{e q}-C_{g d}=198 \mathrm{fF}-28 \mathrm{fF}=170 \mathrm{fF}=\frac{C_{1}^{\prime}+C_{g s}}{2}=\frac{C_{2}^{\prime}+C_{s b}}{2}\);

\(C_{1}^{\prime}=340 \mathrm{fF}-C_{g s}=284 \mathrm{fF} ; C_{2}^{\prime}=340 \mathrm{fF}-C_{s b}=292 \mathrm{fF}\)

where \(C_{1}=C_{1}^{\prime}+C_{g s}\) and \(C_{2}=C_{2}^{\prime}+C_{s b}\).
The small signal negative resistance is
\(R=\frac{-g_{m}}{\omega^{2} C_{1} C_{2}}=\frac{0.064}{\left(6.28 \times 4 \times 10^{10} \times 340 \times 10^{-15}\right)^{2}}=-7 \Omega \text {. }\)

In a practical implementation, MOSFET, varactor, inductor, and capacitor layout parasitic capacitance will lead to smaller values for \(C_{1}^{\prime}\) and \(C_{2}^{\prime}\).

We conclude this example by noting that \(g_{m} / G_{m}\) is only 1.6, and not 2-3, as discussed in Section 10.1.2. When biased at relatively high current densities, MOSFETs exhibit a flat \(g_{m^{-}}\) \(V_{G S}\) characteristics which leads to only a small degradation of the transconductance under large signal operation. In CMOS Colpitts VCOs, it is thus possible to use a smaller \(g_{m} / G_{m}\) ratio.

*equation(10:36) \(R=\frac{-g_{m}}{\omega^{2} C_{1} C_{2}}\)

*equation(10:41)\(\frac{G_{m}}{\omega_{O S C}^{2} C_{1} C_{2}}=\frac{R_{P}}{Q^{2}}=>G_{m} R_{P}=\frac{\left(C_{1}+C_{2}\right)^{2}}{C_{1} C_{2}} \geq 4\)
