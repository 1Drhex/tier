Guest Question.

The five-story shear building is subjected to a full sinusoidal cycle of ground acceleration \(\ddot{u}_{g}(t) = \ddot{u}_{go} sin 2\pi t\) (Fig. E16.1b) with td = 1 sec and \(\ddot{u}_{go} = 0.5g\). Solve the static equilibrium equations at every time step \(\Delta t = 0.05 sec.\)

Admin
Solution

The static equilibrium equations are

\(\mathbf{f}_{S}(\mathbf{u}) =\mathbf{m\iota }\ddot{u}_{go}\sin 2\pi t\)

The reference force distribution \(p_{ref} = \mathbf{m\iota }\ddot{u}_{go}\) and the load factor is \(\lambda _{i} = \sin 2\pi t _{i}\), where \( t _{i} = i\Delta t.\) Thus ,\(p_{ref} = −50\left \langle \begin{matrix}1 & 1& 1& 1 &1\end{matrix} \right \rangle^{T}\) and \(\lambda =\left \langle \begin{matrix}0 &0.309 &0.588& 0.809& 0.951 &1.0\end{matrix} \right \rangle^{T}\) . Computational step 1.0 is identical . Computational steps 2.0 and 3.0 are implemented for i = 0, 1, 2, 3, . . . to obtain the displacements \(u_{1}, u_{2}, u_{3}, u_{4}\), and \(u_{5}\) presented in Table E16.3 and plotted in Figure E16.3a at i = 0, 2, 4, . . . . The base shear is plotted as a function of the roof displacement \(u_{5}\) in Fig. E16.3b. Story shear–story drift relationships are presented in Fig. E16.3c–g for the five stories of the building. The computations in step 3.5 must recognize that the force–deformation relation of inelastic systems is path dependent; see Section 16.3.3.
