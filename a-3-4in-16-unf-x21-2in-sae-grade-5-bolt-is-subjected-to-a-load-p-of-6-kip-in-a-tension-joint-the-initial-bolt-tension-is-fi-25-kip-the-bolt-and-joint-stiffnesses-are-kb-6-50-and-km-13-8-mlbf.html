Guest Question.

A \(\frac{3}{4} \ in-16 \ UNF \times 2\frac{1}{2} \ in \) SAE grade 5 bolt is subjected to a load P of 6 kip in a tension joint.
The initial bolt tension is \(F_i= 25\) kip.
The bolt and joint stiffnesses are \(k_b= 6.50 \ and \ k_m= 13.8 \ Mlbf/in\), respectively.
(a) Determine the preload and service load stresses in the bolt. Compare these to the SAE minimum proof strength of the bolt.
(b) Specify the torque necessary to develop the preload, using Eq. (8–27).
(c) Specify the torque necessary to develop the preload, using Eq. (8–26) with \(f = f_c= 0.15\).

\(T=K F_{i} d\). (8-27)

\(K=\left(\frac{d_{m}}{2 d}\right)\left(\frac{\tan \lambda+f \sec \alpha}{1-f \tan \lambda \sec \alpha}\right)+0.625 f_{c}\) (8-26)

(a) The preload stress is

\(\sigma_{i}=\frac{F_{i}}{A_{t}}=\frac{25}{0.373}=67.02 \mathrm{kpsi}\)

The stiffness constant is

\(C=\frac{k_b}{k_b+k_m}=\frac{6.5}{6.5+13.8}=0.320 \)

\(F_b=P_p+F_i=CP+F_i \ \ \ F_m\lt 0\)

\(\sigma_b= \frac{F_b}{A_t}=\frac{CP+F_i}{A_i}= C \frac{P}{A_t}+\sigma_i \\ =0.320 ]\frac{6}{0.373}+67.02=72.17 \ kpsi\)

The preload and service load stresses are respectively 21 and 15 percent less than the proof strength.
(b) From Eq. (8–27),

\(T=KF_id\)

the torque necessary to achieve the preload is
\(T=KF_id=0.2 \left(25\right) \left(10^3\right) \left(0.75\right)=3750 \ lbf.in \)
c) The minor diameter can be determined from the minor area in Table 8–2.Thus
\(d_r=\sqrt{{4A_r}/{\pi }} =\sqrt{{4\left(0.351\right) }/{\pi } } =0.6685 \ in\)
Thus, the mean diameter is

\(d_m= {\left(0.75 +0.6685\right)}/{2} = 0.7093 \ in\).

The lead angle is

\(\lambda =\tan^{-1}\frac{l}{\pi d_m} =\tan^{-1}\frac{1}{\pi d_m N}=\tan^{-1}\frac{1}{\pi \left(0.7093\right)\left(16\right) }= 1.6066◦\)
For \( \alpha = 30◦ \) , Eq. (8–26)

\(K=\left(\frac{d_m}{2d} \right) \left(\frac{\tan \lambda +fsec\alpha }{1-f \tan \lambda sec\alpha } \right) +0.625 f_c\)

gives
\(T=\left\{\left[\frac{0.7093}{2\left(0.75\right) } \right]\left[\frac{\tan 1.6066◦ + 0.15\left(sec 30◦\right) }{1-0.15\left(\tan 1.6066◦\right)\left(sec 30◦\right) } \right]+0.625\left(0.15\right) \right\} 25\left(10^3\right) \left(0.75\right) =3551 \ lbf.in\)
which is 5.3 percent less than the value found in part (b).
