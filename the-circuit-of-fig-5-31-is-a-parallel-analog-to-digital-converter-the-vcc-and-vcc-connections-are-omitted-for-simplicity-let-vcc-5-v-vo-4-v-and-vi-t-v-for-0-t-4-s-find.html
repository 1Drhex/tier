Guest Question.

The circuit of Fig. 5-31 is a parallel analog-to-digital converter. The \( +V_{c c} \text { and }-V_{c c} \) connections are omitted for simplicity. Let \( V_{c c}=5 V , v_{o}=4 V \text {, and } v_{i}=t( V ) \text { for } 0<t<4 s \text {. } \). Find outputs \( v_{3}, v_{2} \text {, and } v_{1} \).

Admin
Solution

Interpret the answer.
The op amps have no feedback, and they function as comparators. The outputs with values at +5 or -5 V are given in Table 5-2.

Table 5-2

time, s input, V input, V
0 < t < 1 \(0<v_{i}<1\) \( v_{3}=-5 \quad v_{2}=-5 \quad v_{1}=-5\)
1 < t < 2 \(0<v_{i}<2\) \( v_{3}=-5 \quad v_{2}=-5 \quad v_{1}=+5\)
2 < t < 3 \(0<v_{i}<3\) \( v_{3}=-5 \quad v_{2}=+5 \quad v_{1}=+5\)
3 < t < 4 \(0<v_{i}<4\) \( v_{3}=+5 \quad v_{2}=+5 \quad v_{1}=+5\)

The binary sequences \( \left\{v_{3}, v_{2}, v_{1}\right\} \) in Table 5-2 uniquely specify the input voltage in discrete domain. However, in their present form they are not the binary numbers representing input amplitudes. Yet, by using a coder we could transform the above sequences into the binary numbers corresponding to the values of analog inputs.
