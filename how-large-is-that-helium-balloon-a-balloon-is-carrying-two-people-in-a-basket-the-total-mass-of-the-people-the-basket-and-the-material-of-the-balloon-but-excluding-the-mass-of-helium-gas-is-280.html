How Large Is That Helium Balloon?

A balloon is carrying two people in a basket. The total mass of the people, the basket, and the material of the balloon, but excluding the mass of helium gas, is 280 kg. What volume of helium is needed to lift the balloon (Figure 12-30)? The density of the helium is 0.18 \(kg / m ^{3}\), and the density of the air is 1.30 \(kg / m ^{3}\).

Admin
Solution

When a balloon is filled with helium gas it displaces air and, the surrounding air exerts a buoyant force on the balloon and the gas inside it. As more helium is added, the volume of the balloon
increases, so more air is displaced and the buoyant force on the balloon increases. When the buoyant force equals the total weight of the balloon (including the helium), the balloon will begin to float; adding any more helium will cause the balloon to accelerate upward.

The total weight of the balloon is

 

\(\begin{aligned}W=& \text { weight of balloon and its contents } \\&+\text { weight of the helium gas filling the balloon }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(W=(280 kg ) g+m_{ He } g=(280 kg ) g+\left(V_{ He } \rho_{ He }\right) g\) (12-30)

 

In Equation 12-30, \(V_{ He }\) is the volume of the helium-filled balloon, and \(\rho_{ He }\) is the density of helium gas. The buoyant force exerted by the air on the balloon is

 

\(\begin{aligned}F_{ B } &=\text { weight of the air displaced by the balloon } \\&=m_{ air } g=\left(V_{ He } \rho_{\text {air }}\right) g\end{aligned}\) (12-31)

 

At liftoff, the buoyant force equals the total weight of the balloon, so

 

\((280 kg ) g+V_{ He } \rho_{ He } g=\left(V_{ He } \rho_{ air }\right) g\)

 

and

 

\(V_{ He }=\frac{280 kg }{\left(\rho_{ air }-\rho_{ He }\right)}=\frac{280 kg }{(1.30-0.18) kg / m ^{3}}=2.5 \times 10^{2} m ^{3}\) (12-32)

 

With \(250 m ^{3}\) of helium, the balloon is ready to lift off. To initiate an upward velocity, the amount of helium pumped into the balloon must be greater than the above calculated value.

 

Making sense of the result

 

A volume of \(250 m ^{3}\) of air will exert a buoyant force of approximately \(<br /> (250 \times 1.3 \times 10) N =3250 N\), which is sufficient to lift a mass of about 320 kg against the force of gravity. Since the mass of the people plus the balloon is 280 kg and we have to add to it the mass of helium gas, the above-calculated value of the required volume of helium gas makes sense.
