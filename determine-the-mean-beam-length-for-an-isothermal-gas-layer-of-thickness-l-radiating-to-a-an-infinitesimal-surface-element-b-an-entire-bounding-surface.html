Guest Question.

Determine the mean beam length for an isothermal gas layer of thickness L radiating to (a) an infinitesimal surface element, (b) an entire bounding surface

Admin
Solution

The mean beam length may be evaluated by first finding from equation (13.38) \(q=n^{2}\sigma T^{4}_{1} -2n^{2}\sigma T^{4}_{2}E_{3}(\tau _{L}) -2\int_{0}^{\tau _{L}}{}n^{2}\sigma T^{4}(\tau ^{\prime })E_{2}(\tau ^{\prime })d\tau ^{\prime }\) the radiative heat flux hitting a surface element, or by integrating equation (19.3) \(\frac{q_{\eta }}{\pi I_{b\eta }}=1-e^{-\kappa _{\eta }L_{c}} =\frac{1}{\pi }\int_{V}^{}{} e^{-\kappa _{\eta }S} \frac{\kappa _{\eta }\cos \theta dV}{S^{2}}\) directly. Choosing the latter for illustrative purposes, we express V in terms of a cylindrical coordinate system with its origin at dA. Thus, dV = 2πr dr dz , \(S=\sqrt{r^{2}+z^{2}}\) , and cos θ = z/S, leading to

\(\frac{q_{\eta }}{E_{b\eta }}=\frac{1}{\pi } \int_{z=0}^{L}{} \int_{r=0}^{\infty }{} e^{-\kappa _{\eta }S}\frac{\kappa _{\eta }z 2\pi r dr dz}{S^{3}}\).

By replacing the integration variable r by S, this expression becomes, with rdr = SdS,

\(\frac{q_{\eta }}{E_{b\eta }}=2\int_{z=0}^{L}{} \int_{S=z}^{\infty }{} e^{-\kappa _{\eta }S}\frac{\kappa _{\eta }z dS dz}{S^{2}} =2\kappa _{\eta }\int_{z=0}^{L}{}E_{2}(\kappa _{\eta }z)dz,\)

where the definition for the exponential integral has been employed [see equation (13.31) \(E_{n}(x)=\int_{1}^{\infty }{} e^{-xt}\frac{dt}{t^{n}}=\int_{0}^{1}{}\mu ^{n-2}e^{-x/\mu } d\mu\) or Appendix E]. Integrating, we obtain

\(\frac{q_{\eta }}{E_{b\eta }}=-2E_{3}(\kappa _{\eta }z)|^{L}_{0} =1- 2E_{3}(\kappa _{\eta }L),\)

which, of course, would also have followed immediately from equation (13.38) \(q=n^{2}\sigma T^{4}_{1} -2n^{2}\sigma T^{4}_{2}E_{3}(\tau _{L}) -2\int_{0}^{\tau _{L}}{}n^{2}\sigma T^{4}(\tau ^{\prime })E_{2}(\tau ^{\prime })d\tau ^{\prime }\), if only emission from the medium had been considered (\(T_{1}=T_{2}=0\)).

Thus, from equation (19.3) \(\frac{q_{\eta }}{\pi I_{b\eta }}=1-e^{-\kappa _{\eta }L_{c}} =\frac{1}{\pi }\int_{V}^{}{} e^{-\kappa _{\eta }S} \frac{\kappa _{\eta }\cos \theta dV}{S^{2}}\), the mean beam length from the gas layer to a surface element dA is

\(L_{e}=\frac{1}{\kappa _{\eta }}\ln \frac{1}{2E_{3}(\kappa _{\mu }L)}\)

The mean beam length for the entire surface, equation (19.4) \(\frac{q_{\eta , av}}{\pi I_{b\eta }}=1-e^{\kappa _{\eta }L_{c}}=\frac{1}{A} \int_{A}^{}{}\int_{V}^{}{} e^{\kappa _{\eta }S}\frac{\kappa _{\eta }\cos \theta dV}{S^{2}}dA\) , is the same since, for this one-dimensional problem, local and average heat flux are identical.
