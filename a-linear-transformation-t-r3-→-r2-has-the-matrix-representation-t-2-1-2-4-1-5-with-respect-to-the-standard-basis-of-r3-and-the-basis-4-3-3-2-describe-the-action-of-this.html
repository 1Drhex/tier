Guest Question.

A linear transformation \(T : R^3 → R^2\) has the matrix representation

\(T = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 & - 1 & 2 \\ 4 & 1 & 5 \end{matrix} \right]\)

with respect to the standard basis of \(R^3\) and the basis \([(4, 3) , (3, 2)]\). Describe the action of this linear transformation.

Admin
Solution

The first column of the matrix gives us the action of the transformation on \((1 , 0 , 0)\) and so on, in the form

\(T (v_{1}) = a_{11}w_{1} + a_{21}w_{2} +···+ a_{m1}w_{m}\)

Therefore we have

\(T (1 , 0 , 0) = 2 (4 , 3 ) + 4 (3 , 2) = (8 , 6) + (12 , 8 ) = (20 , 14)\)

 

\(T (0, 1, 0) = −1 (4 , 3) + 1 (3 , 2) = (−4 , −3) + (3 , 2) = (−1 , −1)\)

 

\(T (0 , 0, 1) = 2 (4 , 3) + 5 (3 , 2) = (8 , 6) + (15 , 10) = (23 , 16)\)

 

We can use this information to find the action on an arbitrary vector. Since we can write

\((a, b, c) = a (1 , 0 , 0) + b (0 , 1 , 0) + c (0 , 0 , 1 )\)

and for a linear transformation \(L\) we have

\(L (\alpha v) = \alpha L (v)\)

where \(\alpha\) is a scalar and \(v\) is a vector. Therefore the action of the transformation in this problem on an arbitrary vector is

\(T (a , b , c) = aT (1 , 0 , 0) + bT (0 , 1 , 0) + cT (0 , 0 , 1)\)

\(= a (20, 14) + b (−1, −1) + c (23, 16)\)

\(= (20a − b + 23c, 14a − b + 16c)\)
