Guest Question.

Refer to Figure 14.20b. Here, \(\textbf{H}_{f}\) = 2 m, pile diameter = 0.305 m, \(\pmb{\gamma}_{f}\) = 16.5 kN/\(\textbf{m}^{\textbf{3}}, \pmb{\phi}^{\prime}_{clay}\) = 34°, \(\pmb{\gamma}_{sat(clay)}\) = 17.2 kN/\(\textbf{m}^{\textbf{3}}\), and L = 20 m. The water table coincides with the top of the clay layer. Determine the downward drag force.

Admin
Solution

The depth of the neutral plane is given in Eq. (14.55)

\(L_{1} = \frac{L – H_{f}}{L_{1}}\left(\frac{L – H_{f}}{2} + \frac{\gamma ^{\prime}_{f}H_{f}}{\gamma ^{\prime}} \right) – \frac{2\gamma^{\prime} _{f}H_{f}}{\gamma ^{\prime}}\)

as

\(L_{1} = \frac{L – H_{f}}{L_{1}}\left(\frac{L – H_{f}}{2} + \frac{\gamma _{f}H_{f}}{\gamma ^{\prime}} \right) – \frac{2\gamma _{f}H_{f}}{\gamma ^{\prime}}\)

Note that \(\gamma^{\prime}_{f}\) in Eq. (14.55) has been replaced by \(\gamma_{f}\) because the fill is above the water table. So

\(L_{1} = \frac{20 – 2}{L_{1}}\left[\frac{\left(20 – 2\right) }{2} + \frac{\left(16.5\right) \left(2\right) }{\left(17.2 – 9.81\right) } \right] – \frac{(2)(16.5)(2)}{(17.2 – 9.81)} \\\quad =\frac{242.4}{L_{1}} – 8.93\\L = 11.75 m\)

Now, referring to Eq. (14.57),

\(Q_{n} =\int_{0}^{L_{1}}{p f_{n}dz} = \int_{0}^{L_{1}}{pK^{\prime }}(\gamma ^{\prime }_{f}H_{f} + \gamma^{\prime } z)\tan \delta^{\prime } dz\\\quad = (pK^{\prime }\gamma ^{\prime }_{f}H_{f}\tan \delta^{\prime })L_{1} + \frac{L_{1}^{2}pK^{\prime }\gamma^{\prime }\tan \delta^{\prime }}{2}\)

we have

\(Q_{n} = (pK^{\prime }\gamma _{f}H_{f}\tan \delta^{\prime })L_{1} + \frac{L_{1}^{2}pK^{\prime }\gamma^{\prime }\tan \delta^{\prime }}{2}\\p = \pi(0.305) = 0.958 m\\K^{\prime} = 1 – \sin 34^{\circ} = 0.44\\Q_{n} = (0.958)(0.44)(16.5)(2)[\tan(0.6 \times 34)](11.75)\\ +\frac{(11.75)^{2}(0.958)(0.44)(17.2 – 9.81)[\tan(0.6 \times 34)]}{2}\\ = 60.78 + 79.97 = \textbf{140.75 kN}\)
