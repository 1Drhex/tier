Guest Question.

The stoichiometry of the reaction of nitric oxide with hydrogen is
\(2NO+2H_{2}\rightarrow N_{2}+2H_{2}O\) (A)
The overall rate expression is third order:
\(r_{N_{2}}=k\left [ NO \right ]^{2}\left [ H_{2} \right ]\) (B)
Two different mechanisms have been proposed for this reaction.
Mechanism A:
\(2NO+H_{2}\overset{k_{1}}{\rightarrow}N_{2}+H_{2}O_{2}\) (slow) (C)
\(H_{2}O_{2}+H_{2}\overset{k_{2}}{\rightarrow}2H_{2}O\) (fast) (D)

Mechanism B:
\(2NO\overset{k_{3}}{\underset{k_{4}}{\leftrightarrow}}N_{2}O_{2}\) (fast) (E)
\(N_{2}O_{2}+H_{2}\overset{k_{5}}{\rightarrow}N_{2}+H_{2}O_{2}\) (slow) (F)
\(H_{2}O_{2}+H_{2}\overset{k_{2}}{\rightarrow}2H_{2}O\) (fast) (G)
Show that these two mechanisms are each consistent with both the rate expression observed and the stoichiometry of the reaction.

Admin
Solution

First consider mechanism A. Addition of reactions (C) and (D) gives reaction (A), so the stoichiometry of the mechanism is consistent. Reaction (C) is the rate-limiting step; hence, the overall reaction rate is given by
\(r_{N_{2}}=k_{1}\left [ NO \right ]^{2}\left [ H_{2} \right ]\) (H)
This rate expression is consistent with the kinetics observed, so this combination of a slow termolecular step with a rapid bimolecular step is a plausible mechanism based on the information we have been given. Now consider mechanism B. Addition of reactions (E), (F), and (G) again yields equation (A). Thus, the stoichiometry is consistent. The second step in the mechanism is the rate-controlling step. Thus,
\(r_{N_{2}}=k_{5}\left [ N_{2}O_{2} \right ]\left [ H_{2} \right ]\) (I)
This expression contains the concentration of an intermediate species \(\left (N_{2}O_{2} \right )\).This species may be eliminated by
recognizing that reaction (E) is essentially at equilibrium.
Hence,
\(k_{3}\left [ NO \right ]^{2}=k_{4}\left [ N_{2}O_{2} \right ]\) (J)
The concentration of the intermediate is given by
\(\left [ N_{2}O2 \right ]=\frac{k_{3}}{k_{4}}\left [ NO \right ]^{2}=K_{E}\left [ NO \right ]^{2}\) (K)
where \(K_{E}\) is an equilibrium constant for reaction (E). Substitution of this expression for the \(N_{2}O_{2}\) concentration in equation (I) gives
\(r_{N_{2}}=\frac{k_{3}k_{5}}{k_{4}}\left [ NO \right ]^{2}\left [ H_{2} \right ]=k_{5}K_{E}\left [ NO \right ]^{2}\left [ H_{2} \right ]\) (L)
These equations are consistent with the kinetics observed if we identify k as
\(k=\frac{k_{3}k_{5}}{k_{4}}=k_{5}K_{E}\) (M)
Thus, mechanism B, which consists solely of bimolecular and unimolecular steps, is also consistent with the information that we have been given. This mechanism is somewhat simpler than the first in that it does not requite a termolecular step.
This illustration points out that the fact that a mechanism gives rise to the experimentally observed rate expression is by no means an indication that the mechanism is a unique solution to the problem being studied.
