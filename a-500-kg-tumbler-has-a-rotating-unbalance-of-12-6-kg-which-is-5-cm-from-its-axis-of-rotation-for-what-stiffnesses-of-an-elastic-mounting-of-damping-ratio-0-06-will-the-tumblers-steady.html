Guest Question.

A 500 kg tumbler has a rotating unbalance of 12.6 kg, which is 5 cm from its axis of rotation. For what stiffnesses of an elastic mounting of damping ratio 0.06 will the tumbler’s steady–state amplitude be less than 2 mm for all speeds of operation between 200 rpm and 600 rpm?

Admin
Solution

From the given information, the allowable value of the nondimensional parameter \(\Lambda\) is

\( \Lambda _{all}=\frac{mX_{all}}{m_{0}e}=\frac{(500kg)(0.002 m)}{(12.6 kg)(0.05m)}=1.587\) (a)

The curve of \( \Lambda (r,0.06) \) versus r is shown in Figure 4.56. Since \( \Lambda_{all}\gt 1 \) there are two values of r for which \( \Lambda (r,0.06)=\Lambda_{all} \) These can be found by solving

\( \frac{r^{2}}{\sqrt{(1-r^{2})^{2}+\left[2(0.06)r\right]^{2} } } =1.587 \) (b)

The solutions are r = 0.788, 1.635. Consider first the lower value of \( r,\Lambda \lt 1.587 \) for \( r\lt 0.788.\) Thus, if r = 0.788 corresponds to \( \omega =600 rpm \), the steady state amplitude is less than 2 mm for all speeds less than 600 rpm. Thus, requiring \( r\lt 0.788 \) or equivalently \( \frac{600 rpm}{\omega _{n}}\lt0.788,\) this implies \( \omega _{n }\gt761.4 rpm \) or \( \omega _{n}\lt \left(761.4\frac{rev}{\min}\right)\left(\frac{2\pi rad}{rev} \right)\left(\frac{1\min}{60s} \right) =79.73 rad/s.\) This leads to

\( k\gt (500 kg)\left(79.73\frac{r}{s} \right)^{2}=3.18 \times 10^{6} N/m \) (c)

If r = 1.635 corresponds to \( \omega =200 rpm \) then \( \Lambda \lt 1.537 \) or \( X\lt 2 mm \) for all \( \omega \gt 200 rpm \) thus, \( r\gt 1.635 \) implies that \( \frac{200rpm}{\omega _{n}}\gt 1.635 \), which leads to \( \omega _{n}\lt 122.3 rpm.\) or \( \omega _{n}\lt 12.81 \). The allowable stiffnesses are

\( k\gt (500kg)(12.81 rad/s)^{2}= 8.21\times 10^{4} N/m \) (d)

Thus, the steady-state amplitude of the machine is less than 2 mm at all speeds between 200 rpm and 600 rpm if \( k\gt 3.18\times 10^{6} N/m \) or \( k\lt 8.21\times 10^{4} N/m \)
