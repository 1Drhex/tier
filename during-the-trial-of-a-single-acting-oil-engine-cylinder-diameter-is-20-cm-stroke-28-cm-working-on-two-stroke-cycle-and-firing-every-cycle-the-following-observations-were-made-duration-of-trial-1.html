Guest Question.

During the trial of a single acting oil engine, cylinder diameter is 20 cm, stroke 28 cm, working on two stroke cycle and firing every cycle, the following observations were made:
Duration of trial :1 hour
Total fuel used :4.22 kg
Calorific value :44670 kJ/kg
Proportion of hydrogen in fuel : 15%
Total number of revolutions : 21000
Mean effective pressure : 2.74 bar
Net brake load applied to a drum of 100 cm diameter : 600 N
Total mass of cooling water circulated : 495 kg
Total mass of cooling water : inlet 13ºC, outlet 38ºC
Air used : 135 kg
Temperature of air in test room : 20ºC
Temperature of exhaust gases : 370ºC
Assume C\(_{p, gases}\) = 1.005 kJ/kg K,
C\(_{p, steam}\) at atmospheric pressure = 2.093 kJ/kg K
Calculate thermal efficiency and draw up the heat balance. [U.P.S.C. 1997]

Admin
Solution
\(\begin{aligned}\text{Brake power}&= 2π NT\\N&= \frac{21,000}{60} rpm = 350 rpm\\T&= 600 × 0.5 = 300 N . m\\\text{Brake power}&= \frac{2π ×350×300}{60×1000} = 10.996 kW\\\text{Indicated power}&= \frac{mep × L ⋅ A ⋅ N}{60 × 1000} kW\\&= \frac{2.74 × 10^{5} × 0.28 × \pi × (0.2)^{2} × 300}{60 × 1000 × 4} = 14.06 kW\\\text{(A) Heat added}&= \frac{4.22}{3600} × 44670 = 52.36 kJ/s or 3141.79 kJ/min\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Thermal efficiency, η}&= \frac{\text{Indicated power}}{\text{Heat added}}\\&= \frac{14.06}{\frac{4.22}{3600} × 44670}\\&= 0.2685 or 26.85\%\\\text{(B) Heat equivalent of brake power}&= 10.996 × 60 = 659.76 kJ/min\\\text{(C) Heat lost to cooling water}&= m_{w} × C_{pw} × ΔT\\&= \frac{495}{60} × 4.18 × (38 – 13)\\&= 862.13 kJ/min\\\text{Heat carried by exhaust gases}&= \text{Heat carried by steam in exhaust gases + Heat carried by fuel gases (dry gases) in exhaust gases}\\\text{Mass of exhaust gases}&= \text{mass of air + mass of fuel}\\&= \frac{(135 + 4.22)}{60} kg/min\\&= 2.32 kg/min\\\text{Mass of steam in exhaust gases}&= 9 × \left\lgroup 0.15 × \frac{4.22}{60}\right\rgroup \\&= 0.095 kg/min\\\text{Mass of dry exhaust gases}&= 2.32 – 0.095\\&= 2.225 kg/min\\\text{(D) Heat carried by steam in exhaust}&= 0.095 × \left\{4.18 (100 – 20) + 2256.9 + 2.093 × (370 – 100)\right\} \\&= 299.86 kJ/min\\\text{(E) Heat carried by fuel gases (dry ga}&\text{ses) in exhaust gases}\\&= 2.225 × 1.005 (370 – 20)\\&= 782.64 kJ/min\\(F)\qquad\qquad\qquad\quad\text{Unaccounted loss}&= \text{A – B – C – D – E}\\&= \text{(3141.79) – (659.76) – (862.13) – (299.86) – (782.64)}\\&= 537.4 kJ/min\end{aligned}\\\text{Heat balance sheet (on per minute basis)}\)
