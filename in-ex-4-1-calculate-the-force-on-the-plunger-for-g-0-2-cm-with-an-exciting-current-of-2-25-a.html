Guest Question.

In calculate the force on the plunger for g = 0.2 cm with an exciting current of 2.25 A.

Admin
Solution

\(F_{f} =\frac{\partial \acute{W_{f} } \left(i,g\right) }{\partial g} \)
where g is the air-gap. Given g \(= 0.2\times 10^{–2} \) m.
Case a: Reluctance of the iron path accounted for.
This is the nonlinear case where the derivative desired can be found numerically.
Assume \(\Delta g= –0.05\times 10^{–2}\) m (a decrease)
\(g+\Delta g= 0.15\times 10^{–2} \) m
Air-gap reluctance\(= 810.5\times 10^{3} \times \frac{0.15}{0.2} + 84.4\times 10^{3} =692\times 10^{3}\)
For this air-gap

This is plotted in Fig. 4.10 from which increase in co energy for i = 2.25 A is the area oab, i.e.
\(\Delta \acute{W_{f} } = 0.718\) J
\(F_{f} =\frac{\partial \acute{W_{f} } }{\partial g}=\frac{0.718}{-0.05\times 10^{-2} } = –1436\) N
Since a decrease in g causes an increase in \(W_{f}\)
, the force on plunger acts in negative direction (positive direction is in the increasing direction of g). Hence the force is attractive (tending to reduce g).
It may be noted that better results will be obtained by choosing a smaller value of \(\Delta g\) .
Case b: The magnetization curve of iron is assumed linear (corresponding to the initial slope).
This is the linear case so that we can proceed analytically.
Total reluctance R\(=\frac{60}{0.001} +84.4\times 10^{3} +\frac{g}{4\pi \times 10^{-7}\times \frac{\pi }{4} \left(0.05\right) ^{2} } =144.4\times 10^{3} +4053g\times 10^{5} = 144.4\left(1+28.1\times 10^{2}g \right) \times 10^{3}\)
\(\phi =\frac{1200i}{ 144.4\left(1+28.1\times 10^{2}g \right) \times 10^{3}}\) Wb
\(\lambda =N\phi =\frac{\left(1200\right) ^{2} i}{144.4\left(1+28.1\times 10^{2}g \right) \times 10^{3}}\) WbT
\(\acute{W_{f} } \left(i,g\right) =\frac{1}{2}i\lambda =\frac{\left(1200\right) ^{2} i^{2} }{2\times 144.4\left(1+28.1\times 10^{2}g \right) \times 10^{3}}\)
\(F_{f} =\frac{\partial \acute{W_{f} } }{\partial g} =-\frac{\left(1200\right) ^{2} i^{2}\times 28.1\times 10^{2} }{2\times 144.4\left(1+28.1\times 10^{2}g \right)^{2} \times 10^{3}}\)
For g \(= 0.2\times 10^{-2}\) m
\(F_{f} =-\frac{\left(1200\right) ^{2}\times \left(2.25\right) ^{2}\times 28.1\times 10^{2} }{2\times 144.4\left(1+28.1\times 0.2 \right)^{2} \times 10^{3}}= –1619\) N
The linearity assumption causes the values of force to differ by about \(13\% \)from that obtained by the nonlinear method. The actual difference will be still less as in the nonlinear case the derivative is obtained by approximation ( \(\Delta g = 0.05\times 10^{2} \)m). It may be noted here that the linearity assumption renders great computational saving and is hence commonly employed.
