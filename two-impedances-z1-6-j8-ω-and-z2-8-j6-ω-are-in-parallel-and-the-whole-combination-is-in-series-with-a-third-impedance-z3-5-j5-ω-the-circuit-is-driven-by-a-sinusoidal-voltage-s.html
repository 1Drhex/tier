Guest Question.

Two impedances \(Z_{1} = 6 + j8 Ω\) and \(Z_{2} = 8-j6 Ω\) are in parallel and the whole combination is in series with a third impedance \(Z_{3} = 5 + j5 Ω\). The circuit is driven by a sinusoidal voltage source \(v_{S}(t) = 50\) \(sin 100\) \(πt V\). Solve the circuit by phasor diagram method.

Admin
Solution

The circuit and phasor diagrams are shown in Fig. 7.8-8.

We choose the current phasor \(I\) as the reference phasor.
The impedance values are converted to polar form as \(Z_{1} = 10\) \(∠5 3.1° Ω\), \(Z_{2} = 10\) \(∠-36.9° Ω\) and \(Z_{3} = 7.07\) \(∠45° Ω\). The parallel combination, \(Z_{1}//Z_{2}\) is \(= 7 + j1 Ω = 7.07\) \(∠ 8.1° Ω\).

\(\begin{array}{l} I_{1}=\frac{Z_{2}}{Z_{1}+Z_{2}} I=(0.5-j 0.5) I=0.707 \angle-45^{\circ} I \\ I_{2}=\frac{Z_{1}}{Z_{1}+Z_{2}} I=(0.5+j 0.5) I=0.707 \angle 45^{\circ} I \end{array}\)

 

We use a length \(d\) for \(I\). Then, the length to be used for \(I_{1}\) and \(I_{2}\) are \(0.707d\) and they are oriented at \(–45°\) and \(45°\) respectively. Next we draw the \(V = 7.07∠8.1° I\) phasor at \(8.1°\) with respect to horizontal and use a convenient length \(d_{1}\) if the length \(7.07d\) is not suitable. The \(V _{3}\) phasor is also of the same length since \(V_{3} = Z_{3} I = 7.07\) \(∠45° I\). But it is to be drawn at \(45°\) position.

The phasors \(V\) and \(V _{3}\) on addition as per parallelogram law should result in \(V_{S}\). The length of \(V_{S}\) must be \(2 \times d_{1} \times \cos \left[\left(45^{\circ}-8.1^{\circ}\right) / 2\right]=1.9 d_{1}\) But this length must stand for \(50 V\) and hence \(d_{1}\) must stand for \(26.3 V\). Therefore, magnitudes of \(V\) and \(V _{3}\) are \(26.3 V\). Since \(I = V _{3}/ Z _{3} \), magnitude of \(I\) will be \(3.72 A\).

Now, magnitudes of \(I _{1}\) and \(I_{2}\) are \(0.707\) times the magnitude of \(I\). Hence they are of \(2.63 A\) magnitude.

The angle of \(V_{S}\) as per the phasor diagram is \(8.1° + (45°-8.1°)/2 = 26.55°\). But since \(v_{S}(t) = 50\) \(sin 100\) \(π t\), the actual phase angle of \(V_{S}\) is \(–90°\) with respect standard cosine wave. Therefore, an angle of \(-116.55°\) has to be added to the angle of all phasors in the phasor diagram shown in Fig. 7.8-8.

Therefore, the sinusoidal steady-state solution of the circuit is obtained as:

\(\begin{aligned} &V_{\mathrm{s}}=50 \angle-90^{\circ}, \text { and }\\ &V=26.3 \angle-108.45^{\circ} \mathrm{V} \text { and } V_{3}=26.3 \angle-71.55^{\circ} \mathrm{V}\\ &I=3.72 \angle-116.55^{\circ} \mathrm{A}, I_{I}=2.63 \angle-151.55^{\circ} \mathrm{A}, I_{2}=2.63 \angle-71.55^{\circ} \mathrm{A}\\ &\text { The time-domain functions are: }\\ &v_{\mathrm{S}}(t)=50 \cos \left(100 \pi t-90^{\circ}\right)=50 \sin 100 \pi t \mathrm{~V}\\ &v(t)=26.3 \cos \left(100 \pi t 108.45^{\circ}\right)=26.3 \sin \left(100 \pi t-18.45^{\circ}\right) \mathrm{V}\\ &v_{3}(t)=26.3 \cos \left(100 \pi t-71.55^{\circ}\right)=26.3 \sin \left(100 \pi t+18.45^{\circ}\right) \mathrm{V}\\ &i(t)=3.72 \cos \left(100 \pi t-116.55^{\circ}\right)=3.72 \sin \left(100 \pi t-26.55^{\circ}\right) \mathrm{A}\\ &i_{1}(t)=2.63 \cos \left(100 \pi t-151.55^{\circ}\right)=2.63 \sin \left(100 \pi t-61.55^{\circ}\right) \mathrm{A}\\ &i_{2}(t)=2.63 \cos \left(100 \pi t-71.55^{\circ}\right)=2.63 \sin \left(100 \pi t+18.45^{\circ}\right) \mathrm{A} \end{aligned}\)
