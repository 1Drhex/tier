Guest Question.

In a hot and dry climate, air enters an air-conditioner unit at 100 kPa, 40°C, and 5% relative humidity, at the steady rate of 1.0 \(m ^{3} / s\). Liquid water at 20°C is sprayed into the air in the AC unit at the rate 20 kg/hour, and heat is rejected from the unit at at the rate 20 kW. The exit pressure is 100 kPa. What are the exit temperature and relative humidity?

Admin
Solution

State 1:

\(\begin{aligned}& T _{1}=40^{\circ} C , P _{1}=100 kPa , \quad \phi_{1}=5 \%, \dot{ V }_{ a1 }=1 m ^{3} / s \\& P _{ g 1}=7.3837 kPa , P _{ v 1}=\phi_{1} P _{ g 1}=0.369 kPa , P _{ a1 }= P – P _{ v 1}=99.63 kPa\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\omega_{1}=0.622 \frac{ P _{ v 1}}{ P _{ a1 }}=0.0023, \dot{ m }_{ a1 }=\frac{ P _{ a1 } \dot{ V }_{ a1 }}{ RT _{ a1 }}=1.108 kg / s , h _{ v1 }=2574.3 kJ / kg\)

 

State 2 : Liq. Water. 20°C, \(\dot{ m }_{ f 2}=20 kg / hr =0.00556 kg / s , h _{ f2 }=83.9 kJ / kg\)

Conservation of Mass: \(\dot{ m }_{ a1 }=\dot{ m }_{ a 3}, \quad \dot{ m }_{ v1 }+\dot{ m }_{12}=\dot{ m }_{ v 3}\)

\(\omega_{3}=\left(\dot{ m }_{ f 2} / \dot{ m }_{ a1 }\right)+\omega_{1}=(0.00556 / 1.108)+0.0023=0.0073\)

State 3 : \(P _{3}=100 kPa \text { and } \quad P _{ v 3}= P _{3} \omega_{3} /\left(0.622+\omega_{3}\right)=1.16 kPa\)

Energy Eq. with \(\dot{ Q }=-20 kW :\)

\(\dot{ Q }+\dot{ m }_{ a 1} h _{ a1 }+\dot{ m }_{ v1 } h _{ v1 }+\dot{ m }_{ f 2} h _{ f 2}=\dot{ m }_{ a 3} h _{ a 3}+\dot{ m }_{ v 3} h _{ v 3}\) ;

 

\(\begin{aligned}\left( h _{ a 3}- h _{ a 1}\right.&)+\omega_{3} h _{ v 3}=\omega_{1} h _{ v1 }+\left(\dot{ m }_{ f 2} h _{ f 2}+\dot{ Q }\right) / \dot{ m }_{ a1 } \\&=0.0023 \times 2574.3+(0.00556 \times 83.9-20) / 1.108=-11.7\end{aligned}\)

 

Unknowns: \(h _{ a 3}, h _{ v 3}\) implicitly given by a single unknown: \(T _{3}\)

Trial and Error for \(T _{3} ; T _{3}= 1 0 ^{\circ} C , \quad P _{ g 3}=1.23 kPa , \phi_{3}=\frac{ P _{ v 3}}{ P _{ g 3}}= 0 . 9 4\)

If we solved with the psychrometric chart we would get:

State 1: \(\dot{ m }_{ v 1} / \dot{ m }_{ a }=\omega_{1}=0.002, \quad \widetilde{ h }_{1}=65 kJ / kg \text { dry air }\),

State 3: \(\omega_{3}=\left(\dot{ m }_{ f2 } / \dot{ m }_{ a1 }\right)+\omega_{1}=(0.00556 / 1.108)+0.002=0.007\)

Now the energy equation becomes

\(\tilde{ h }_{3}=\widetilde{ h }_{1}+\left(\dot{ m }_{ f 2} h _{ f 2}+\dot{ Q }\right) / \dot{ m }_{ a1 }=65+(0.00556 \times 83.9-20) / 1.108=47.4\)

Given \(\omega_{3}\) we find the state around 10°C and \(\phi_{3}=90 \%\)
