Guest Question.

ALPHA DECAY OF RADIUM

Show that the α-emission process \({ }_{88}^{226} \mathrm{Ra} \rightarrow{ }_{86}^{222} \mathrm{Rn}+{ }_{2}^{4} \mathrm{He}\) (Fig. 43.5a) is energetically possible, and calculate the kinetic energy of the emitted α particle. The neutral atomic masses are 226.025410 u for \({ }_{88}^{226} \mathrm{Ra}\), 222.017578 u for \({ }_{88}^{226} \mathrm{Rn}\), and 4.002603 u for \({ }_{2}^{4} \mathrm{He}\).

Admin
Solution

IDENTIFY and SET UP:

Alpha emission is possible if the mass of the \({ }_{88}^{226} \mathrm{Ra}\) atom is greater than the sum of the atomic masses of \({ }_{88}^{226} \mathrm{Rn}\) and \({ }_{2}^{4} \mathrm{He}\). The mass difference between the initial radium atom and the final radon and helium atoms corresponds (through \(E = mc^2\)) to the energy \(E\) released in the decay. Because momentum is conserved as well as energy, both the alpha particle and the \({ }_{88}^{226} \mathrm{Rn}\) atom are in motion after the decay; we will have to account for this in determining the kinetic energy of the alpha particle.

EXECUTE:

The difference in mass between the original nucleus and the decay products is

226.025410 u – (222.017578 u + 4.002603 u) = +0.005229 u

Since this is positive, α decay is energetically possible. The energy equivalent of this mass difference is

\(E=(0.005229 \mathrm{u})(931.5 \mathrm{MeV} / \mathrm{u})=4.871 \mathrm{MeV}\)

In this process the \({ }_{88}^{226} \mathrm{Rn}\) nucleus is produced in its ground level (Fig. 43.5c). Thus we expect the decay products to emerge with total kinetic energy 4.871 MeV. Momentum is also conserved; if the parent \({ }_{88}^{226} \mathrm{Ra}\) nucleus is at rest, the daughter \({ }_{88}^{226} \mathrm{Rn}\) nucleus and the α particle have momenta of equal magnitude \(p\) but opposite direction. Kinetic energy is \(K=\frac{1}{2} m v^{2}=p^{2} / 2 m\): Since \(p\) is the same for the two particles, the kinetic energy divides inversely as their masses. Hence the a particle gets 222/(222 + 4) of the total, or 4.78 MeV.

 

EVALUATE: Experiment shows that \({ }_{88}^{226} \mathrm{Ra}\) does emit α particles with a kinetic energy of 4.78 MeV. Check your results by verifying that the alpha particle and the \({ }_{88}^{226} \mathrm{Rn}\) nucleus produced in the decay have the same magnitude of momentum \(p = mv\). You can calculate the speed \(v\) of each of the decay products from its respective kinetic energy [note that the \({ }_{88}^{226} \mathrm{Rn}\) nucleus gets 4/(222 + 4) of the 4.871 MeV released]. You’ll find that the alpha particle moves at 0.0506c = 1.52 × \(10^7\)m/s; if momentum is conserved, you should find that the \({ }_{88}^{226} \mathrm{Rn}\) nucleus moves \(\frac{4}{222}\) as fast. Does it?
