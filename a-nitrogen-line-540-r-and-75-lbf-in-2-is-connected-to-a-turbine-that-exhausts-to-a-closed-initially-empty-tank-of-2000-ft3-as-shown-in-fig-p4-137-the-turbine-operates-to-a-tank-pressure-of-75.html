Guest Question.

A nitrogen line, 540 R, and 75 \(lbf / in .^{2}\), is connected to a turbine that exhausts to a closed initially empty tank of 2000 \(ft ^{3}\), as shown in Fig. P4.137. The turbine operates to a tank pressure of 75 \(lbf / in .^{2}\), at which point the temperature is 450 R. Assuming the entire process is adiabatic, determine the turbine work.

Admin
Solution

C.V. turbine & tank ⇒ Transient problem

Conservation of mass: \(m _{ i }= m _{2}= m\)

Energy Eq.: \(m _{ i } h _{ i }= m _{2} u _{2}+ W _{ CV } ; W _{ CV }= m \left( h _{ i }- u _{2}\right)\)

Inlet state: \(P _{ i }=75 lbf / in ^{2}, T _{ i }=540 R\)

Final state 2: \(P _{2}=75 lbf / in ^{2}, T _{2}=450 R\)

\(v _{2}= RT _{2} / P _{2}=55.15 \times 450 /(75 \times 144)=2.298 ft ^{3} / lbm\)

 

\(m _{2}= V / v _{2}=2000 / 2.298=870.32 lbm\)

 

\(h _{ i }- u _{2}= u _{ i }+ RT _{ i }- u _{2}= RT _{ i }+ C _{ v }\left( T _{ i }- T _{2}\right)\)

 

\(=\frac{55.15}{778.17} 540+0.178(540-450)=38.27+16.02=54.29 \frac{ Btu }{ lbm }\)

 

\(W _{ CV }=870.32 \times 54.29= 4 7 2 5 0 ~ B t u\)
