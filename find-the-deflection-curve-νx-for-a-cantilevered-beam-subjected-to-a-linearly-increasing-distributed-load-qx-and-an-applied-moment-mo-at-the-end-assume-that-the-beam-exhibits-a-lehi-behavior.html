Guest Question.

Find the deflection curve \(\nu (x)\) for a cantilevered beam subjected to a linearly increasing distributed load \(q(x)\) and an applied moment \(M_{\omicron }\) at the end. Assume that the beam exhibits a LEHI behavior, is of length L, is initially straight, and has a constant rectangular cross section.

Admin
Solution

Let us divide this “complex” problem into two simpler problems: a cantilever subjected to a uniformly increasing load q(x) and an identical cantilever subjected to an end moment \(M_{\omicron }.\) For “beam 1,” our governing differential equation is

\( EI_{zz}\frac{d^{4}\nu _{1}}{dx^{4}}=-q(x)=-q_{\omicron }\left(\frac{x}{L} \right),\)

where \(q_{\omicron }\) is the value of \(q(x)\) at the end. Integrating this equation four times yields

\( EI_{zz}\frac{d^{3}\nu _{1}}{dx^{3}}=-\frac{q_{\omicron }}{L}\left(\frac{x^{2}}{2} \right)+c_{1},\)

 

\( EI_{zz}\frac{d^{2}\nu _{1}}{dx^{2}}=-\frac{q_{\omicron }}{L}\left(\frac{x^{3}}{6} \right)+c_{1}x+c_{2},\)

 

\( EI_{zz}\frac{d\nu _{1}}{dx}=-\frac{q_{\omicron }}{L}\left(\frac{x^{4}}{24} \right)+c_{1}\left(\frac{x^{2}}{2} \right)+c_{2}x+c_{3} ,\)

 

\( EI_{zz}\nu _{1}(x)=-\frac{q_{\omicron }}{L}\left(\frac{x^{5}}{120} \right)+c_{1}\left(\frac{x^{3}}{6} \right)+c_{2}\left(\frac{x^{2}}{2} \right)+c_{3}x+c_{4}\)

for which we need four boundary conditions:

\((a) \nu _{1}(x=0)=0,\)

 

\((b) \frac{d\nu _{1}}{dx}(x=0)=0,\)

 

\((c) M_{z}(x=L)=0=EI_{zz}\frac{d^{2}\nu _{1}(x=L)}{dx^{2}},\)

 

\((d) V(x=L)=0=EI_{zz}\frac{d^{3}\nu _{1}(x=L)}{dx^{3}}.\)

Hence, from (a), we have \(c_{4}=0,\) and from (b), we have \(c_{3}=0.\) Similarly, from (d), we have \(c_{1}=q_{\omicron }L/2,\) and thus from (c), we have \(c_{2}=-q_{\omicron }L^{2}/3.\) Our first solution is

\( EI_{zz}\nu _{1}(x)=-\frac{q_{\omicron }}{L}\left(\frac{x^{5}}{120} \right)+\frac{q_{\omicron }L}{2}\left(\frac{x^{3}}{6} \right)-\frac{q_{\omicron }L^{2}}{3}\left(\frac{x^{2}}{2} \right),\)
or
\( \nu _{1}(x)=\frac{1}{EI_{zz}}\left(\frac{-q_{\omicron }}{120L} \right)(x^{5}-10L^{2}x^{3}+20L^{3}x^{2}).\)

Next, for “beam 2,” we have

\( EI_{zz}\frac{d^{2}\nu _{2}}{dx^{2}}=M_{\omicron },\)

which can be integrated twice to yield

\( EI_{zz}\frac{d\nu _{2}}{dx}=M_{\omicron }x+c_{5},\)

 

\( EI_{zz}\nu _{2}(x)=M_{\omicron }\left(\frac{x^{2}}{2} \right)+c_{5}x+c_{6},\)

for which we need but two boundary conditions:

\( (e) \nu _{2}(x=0)=0,\)

 

\( (f) \frac{d\nu _{2}(x=0)}{dx}=0.\)

Hence, \(c_{5}=0\) and \(c_{6}=0\) and

\( \nu _{2}(x)=\frac{1}{2EI_{zz}}\left(M_{\omicron }x^{2}\right).\)

Beam Bending and Column Buckling The solution for our original problem is thus

\( \nu (x)=\nu _{1}(x)+\nu _{2}(x)\)

by superposition.
