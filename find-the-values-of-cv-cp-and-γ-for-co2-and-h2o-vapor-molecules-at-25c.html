Guest Question.

Find the values of \(c_v\), \(c_p\), and γ for \(CO_{2} \)and \(H_{2} O\) vapor molecules at 25°C.

Admin
Solution

At this ordinary temperature, vibrational energy is negligible. The \(CO_{2}\) molecule (N = 3) is a linear molecule, similar to a diatomic molecule. It therefore has three terms of kinetic energy, i.e., \(\xi _{tr} =3\). Therefore, \(\xi _{CO_{2} } =\xi _{tr}+\xi _{int}=3+\left(3\times 3-5\right)=7 \). Using Eqn. (1.21) \(e_{tr}=\frac{\tilde{e} _{tr}}{\hat{M}/\hat{N} } =\frac{3}{2} \cdot \frac{\hat{N}kT}{\hat{M}} =\frac{3}{2} RT\),

\(c_{v,CO_{2}}=\frac{\xi _{CO_{2}}}{2} R=\frac{\xi _{CO_{2}}}{2}\frac{\hat{R} }{\hat{M}_{CO_{2}} } =\frac{7}{2} \frac{8.3143kJ/kmol\cdot K}{44kg/kmol} =0.6614\frac{kJ}{kg\cdot K} \)

 

\(c_{v,CO_{2}}=\frac{\xi _{CO_{2} }+2}{2} R=\frac{\xi _{CO_{2} }+2}{2}\frac{\hat{R}}{\hat{M}_{CO_{2}}} =\frac{9}{2} \frac{8.3143kJ/kmol\cdot K}{44kg/kmol} =0.8503\frac{kJ}{kg\cdot K}\)

 

\(\gamma =\frac{\xi +2}{\xi } =\frac{9}{7} =1.2857\)

For comparison, the published values* of \(c_v\), \(c_p\), and γ for \(CO_{2} \) at 25°C are, respectively, 0.6529 kJ/kg·K, 0.8418 kJ/kg·K, and 1.2890.

The \(H_{2} O\) vapor molecule (N = 3) is not a linear molecule with \(\xi _{int}=\left(3\times 3-6\right) =3\) and \(\xi _{tr}=3\). Therefore, \(\xi _{H_{2}O}=6\). Using Eqn. (1.21) \(e_{tr}=\frac{\tilde{e} _{tr}}{\hat{M}/\hat{N} } =\frac{3}{2} \cdot \frac{\hat{N}kT}{\hat{M}} =\frac{3}{2} RT\) and noting that \(\hat{M} _{H_{2}O}=18 kg/kmol\),

\(c_{v,H_{2}O}=1.3857 kJ/kg\cdot K\)

 

\(c_{p,H_{2}O}=1.8476 kJ/kg\cdot K\)

 

\(\gamma =1.333\).

The published values* of \(c_v\), \(c_p\) and γ for \(CO_{2} \) at 25°C are, respectively, 1.4108 kJ/kg·K, 1.8723 kJ/kg·K, and 1.3270.

*These values are taken from R.E. Sonntag and G.J. Van Wylen,Introduction to Thermodynamics, Classical and Statistical, 3rd ed., John Wiley and Sons, 1991.
