Guest Question.

The U.S. domestic output of petroleum for the years 1910, 1940, 1970, and 2000 are measured at 210, 1503, 3517, and 2131 million barrels, respectively, according to the U.S. Energy Information Agency. If the predicted ultimate recovery of oil is estimated at 223 billion barrels, predict the point in time at which the oil output will peak, to the nearest year. What is the predicted output in that year?

Admin
Solution

As a starting point, suppose that we guess at the values of the missing parameters S and t\(_m\) to calculate an initial value of RMSD. Let S = 30 years and t\(_m\) = 1970. Then for each year t, the value of P\(_{est}\) can be calculated using Eq. (5-1). The appropriate values for calculating RMSD that result are given in the following table, leading to a value of (αP\(_{actual}\) – P\(_{est}\))\(^2\) = 5.38 × 10\(^{17}\):

\(P=\frac{Q_{\text {inf }}}{S \sqrt{2 \pi}} \exp \left[-\left(t_{m}-t\right)^{2} /\left(2 S^{2}\right)\right]\) (5-1)

P\(_{actual}\) P\(_{est}\)
t (10\(^6\) barrel) (10\(^6\) barrel) (P\(_{actual}\) – P\(_{est}\))\(^2\)
1910 210 401 3.665E + 16
1940 1,503 1,799 8.772E + 16
1970 3,517 2,966 3.032E + 17
2000 2,131 1,799 1.101E + 17
Total 5.377E + 17

 

The value of the RMSD is then calculated using Eq. (5-2):

\(\mathrm{RMSD}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n}\left(P_{\text {actual }}-P_{\text {est }}\right)^{2}} \) (5-2)

\(\text { RMSD }=\sqrt{\frac{1}{4}\left(5.377 \times 10^{17}\right)}=3.67 \times 10^{8}\)

 

The values of S and t\(_m\) can then be systematically changed by hand in order to reduce RMSD. Here we have used a solver to find values S = 25.3 years and t\(_m\) = 1974 that minimize RMSD:

P\(_{actual}\) P\(_{est}\)
t (10\(^6\) barrel) (10\(^6\) barrel) (P\(_{actual}\) – P\(_{est}\))\(^2\)
1910 210 146 4.028E + 15
1940 1,503 1,442 3.671E + 15
1970 3,517 3,479 1.456E + 15
2000 2,131 2,056 5.659E + 15
Total 1.481E + 16

 

Based on these values, the RMSD is reduced as follows:

\(\mathrm{RMSD}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{4}\left(1.481 \times 10^{16}\right)}=6.086 \times 10^{7}\)

 

Thus the minimum value is RMSD = 6.09 × 10\(^7\). Substituting Q\(_{inf}\) = 223 billion, S = 25.3 years, and t\(_m\) = 1974 into Eq. (5-1) gives

\(P=\frac{2.23 \times 10^{11}}{25.3 \sqrt{2 \pi}} \exp (0)=\frac{2.23 \times 10^{11}}{25.3 \sqrt{2 \pi}}=3.518 \times 10^{9} \mathrm{bbl}\)

 

This value is quite close to the observed maximum for U.S. petroleum output of 3.52 billion barrel in 1972.
