Guest Question.

The following data refer to an outside cylinder uncoupled locomotive:
Mass of rotating parts per cylinder = 350 kg
Mass of reciprocating parts per cylinder = 300 kg
Angle between cranks = 90°
Crank radius = 0.3 m
Cylinder centers = 1.8 m
Radius of balance masses = 0.8 m
Wheel centers = 1.5 m
If whole of the rotating and 2/3rd of the reciprocating parts are to be balanced in planes of the driving wheels, find (a) magnitude and angular positions of balance masses, (b) speed in km/h at which the wheel will lift off the rails when the load on each driving wheel is 30 kN, and the diameter of tread of driving wheels is 1.8 m, and (c) swaying couple at speed found in (b) above.

Admin

Balancing mass of reciprocating parts, \( B=\left(\frac{c R}{M}\right) M_{B}=\left[\frac{2 \times 300}{3 \times 550}\right] \times 227.8=82.83 kg \).

\( v=\omega \times \frac{D}{2}=21.28 \times 0.9 \times \frac{3600}{1000}=68.94 km / h \).
