Guest Question.

An electric field strength of \(10 \mu V/m\) is to be measured at an observation point \(\theta =\pi /2, 500 km\) from a half-wave (resonant) dipole antenna operating in air at \(50 MHz\).

(a) What is the length of the dipole?

(b) Calculate the current that must be fed to the antenna.

(c) Find the average power radiated by the antenna.

(d) If a transmission line with \(Z_{o}=75\Omega V\) is connected to the antenna, determine the standing wave ratio.

Admin
Solution

(a) The wavelength

\(\lambda =\frac{c}{f}=\frac{3\times 10^{8}}{50\times 10^{6}}=6m\)

Hence, the length of the half-dipole is

\(\ell=\frac{\lambda }{2}=3m\)

(b) From eq. (13.19):

\(H_{\phi s}=\frac{jI_{o}e^{-j\beta r}\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\cos\theta \right)}{2\pi r\sin\theta}, E_{\theta s}=\eta H_{\phi s}\)

\(|E_{\theta s}|=\frac{\eta _{o}I_{o}\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\cos\theta \right)}{2\pi r\sin\theta }\)

or

\(I_{o}=\frac{|E_{\theta s}|2\pi r\sin\theta }{\eta _{o}\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\cos\theta \right)}\)

\(=\frac{10\times10^{-6}2\pi (500\times10^{3})\cdot(1)}{120\pi (1)}=83.33mA\)

(c)

\(R_{rad}\simeq 73\Omega\)

\(P_{rad}=\frac{1}{2}I_{o}^{2}R_{rad}=\frac{1}{2}(83.33)^{2}\times10^{-6}\times73=253.5mW\)

(d)

\(\Gamma =\frac{Z_{L}-Z_{o}}{Z_{L}+Z_{o}}(Z_{L}=Z_{in} \) in this case)

\(=\frac{73+j42.5-75}{73+j42.5+75}=\frac{-2+j42.5}{148+j42.5}\)

\(=\frac{42.55\angle 92.69^{\circ}}{153.98\angle 16.02^{\circ}}=0.2763\angle 76.67^{\circ}\)

\(s=\frac{1+|\Gamma |}{1-|\Gamma |}=\frac{1+0.2763}{1-0.2763}=1.763\)
