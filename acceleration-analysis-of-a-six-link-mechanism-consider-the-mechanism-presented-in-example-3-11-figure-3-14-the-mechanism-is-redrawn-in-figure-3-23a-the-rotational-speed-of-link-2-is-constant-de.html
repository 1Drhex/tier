ACCELERATION ANALYSIS OF A SIX-LINK MECHANISM

Consider the mechanism presented in (Figure 3.14). The mechanism is redrawn in Figure 3.23(a). The rotational speed of link 2 is constant. Determine the acceleration polygon of the mechanism and the angular acceleration of link 5 for the given configuration.

Admin
Solution

For this problem, there can be a sliding component of acceleration of the slider (link 6) with respect to the slide (link 5). However, since the slide rotates, a component of Coriolis acceleration can be present.
The following procedure is employed:
(a) Calculate the normal acceleration components:

\(a^N_{BO_2}=-r_{BO_2}\dot{\theta^2_2}=-1.0 \times 41.9^2\)=-1760 in/sec²

\(a^N_{DB}=-r_{DB}\dot{\theta^2_3}=-3.5 \times 12.9^2=-582 \) in/sec²

\(a^N_{DO_4}=-r_{DO_4}\dot{\theta^2_4}=-2.0 \times 24.0^2= -1150 \) in/sec²

\(a^N_{E_5C}=-r_{E_5C}\dot{\theta^2_5}=-1.5 \times 26.0^2 \)=-1010 in/sec²

(b) Calculate the magnitude of the Coriolis acceleration:

\(a^C_{E_5E_1}=2\dot r_{E_5E_1}\dot{\theta}_5=2\times 10\times 26.0\)=520 in /sec²

(c) Construct the acceleration polygon as follows:
• Draw a\(_{B}^N\) and locate B.
• Draw a\(_{D}^N\) and a\(_{DB}^N\)
. • Draw the lines of action of a\(_{D}^T\) and a\(_{DB}^T\) and locate D.
• Locate C by using the acceleration image.
• Draw a\(^N_{E_5C}\) and a\(^C_{E_5E_1}\) (refer to Figure 3.24 for the direction of the Coriolis acceleration).
• Draw the lines of action of a\(_{E_5C}^T\) and a\(_{E_5E_1}^S\) and locate E\(_5\).
• Label all vectors. The completed acceleration polygon is shown in Figure 3.23(b).
(d) Determine the angular acceleration of link 5.

\(\ddot{\theta}_5=\frac{a^T_{E_5C}}{r_{E_5C}}=\frac{1550}{1.5}\)=1030 rad /sec² CCW
