Guest Question.

Again consider the stirred-tank blending system in Equations
\( \frac{dV}{dt} = \frac{1}{\rho }(w_{1} +w_{2} −w) \)
\( \frac{dx}{dt} = \frac{w_{1}}{V\rho }(x_{1}-x)+\frac{w_{2}}{V\rho }(x_{2}-x) \)
written as
\( \rho \frac {dV}{dt} = w_{1} +w_{2} −w \) (1)
\( \rho V\frac{dx}{dt} = w_{1}(x_{1}-x)+w_{2}(x_{2}-x) \) (2)
Assume that volume V remains constant (due to an overflow line that is not shown) and consequently, \( w = w_{1} + w_{2} \).
Inlet composition \( x_{1} \) and inlet flow rates \( w_{1} \space and \space w_{2} \) can vary, but stream 2 is pure solute so that \( x_{2} = 1 \).
Derive transfer functions that relate the exit composition to the three input variables \( (w_{1},w_{2},\space and \space x_{1}) \) using the steps shown in the flow chart of Fig. 4.6.

Admin
Solution

The nonlinearities in Equation (2) are due to the product terms, \( w_{1}x_{1} \), and so forth. The right side of Equation (2) has the functional form \( f(x,x_{1},w_{1},w_{2}). \) For Step 1 of Fig. 4.6, calculate the nominal steady-state value, \( \overline{w} \), from Equation (1) and \( \overline{x} \) from Equation (2), after setting the derivatives equal to zero. For Step 2, linearize Equation (2) about the nominal steady-state values to obtain
\( \rho V\frac{dx}{dt} = \rho V\frac{dx^{\prime }}{dt} = (\frac{∂ f}{∂ x} )_{s}(x-\overline{x} )+ (\frac{∂f}{∂x_{1}} )_{s}(x_{1}-\overline{x}_{1}) + (\frac{∂f}{∂w_{1}} )_{s}(w_{1}-\overline{w}_{1} )+ (\frac{∂f}{∂w_{2}} )_{s}(w_{2}-\overline{w}_{2} ) \) (3)

The partial derivatives are as follow:
\( (\frac{∂ f}{∂ x} )_{s} = -\overline{w}_{1} -\overline{w}_{2} \)
\( (\frac{∂f}{x_{1}} )_{s} = \overline{w}_{1} \) (4)
\( (\frac{∂f}{w_{1}} )_{s} = \overline{x}_{1} – \overline{x} \)
\( (\frac{∂f}{w_{2}} )_{s} = \overline{x}_{2} – \overline{x} = 1 – \overline{x} \)

Substitute Equation (4) into Equation (3) and introduce deviation variables (Step 3):
\( \rho V\frac{dx^{\prime }}{dt} = -\overline{w}x^{\prime }+ \overline{w}_{1}x^{\prime }_{1}+(\overline{x}_{1}-\overline{x})w^{\prime }_{1}+(1-\overline{x})w^{\prime }_{2} \) (5)
The above equation is general in that it applies to any specified operating point.

For Step 4 take the Laplace transform of both sides of Equation (5) with the initial condition, \( x^{\prime }(0) = 0: \)
\( \rho VsX^{\prime }(s) = – \overline{w}X^{\prime }(s) + \overline{w}_{1}X^{\prime }_{1}(s)+ ( \overline{x}_{1}-\overline{x})W^{\prime }_{1}(s)+(1-\overline{x})W^{\prime }_{2}(s) \)
Rearranging and dividing by \( \overline{w} \) yields
\( (\frac{V\rho }{ \overline{w}}s+1 )X^{\prime }(s) = \frac{\overline{w}_{1}}{\overline{w}}X^{\prime }_{1}(s)+\frac{\overline{x}_{1}-\overline{x}}{\overline{w}}W^{\prime }_{1}(s)+\frac{1-\overline{x}}{\overline{w}}W^{\prime }_{2}(s) \)

Define
\( τ≜ \frac{V\rho }{\overline{w}},\quad K_{1} ≜ \frac{\overline{w}_{1}}{\overline{w}} \)
\( K_{2} ≜ \frac{1-\overline{x}}{\overline{w}},\quad and \space K_{3} ≜ \frac{\overline{x}_{1}-\overline{x}}{\overline{w}} \)

Applying Step 5 gives the relationship for the single output and three inputs:

\( X^{\prime }(s) = \frac{K_{1}}{\tau s+1}X^{\prime }_{1}(s)+\frac{K_{2}}{\tau s+1}W^{\prime }_{2}(s)+\frac{K_{3}}{\tau s+1}W^{\prime }_{1}(s) \)

Three input–output transfer functions can be derived from Steps 6 and 7:

\( G_{1}(s) = \frac{X^{\prime }(s)}{X^{\prime }_{1}(s)} = \frac{K_{1}}{\tau s+1} \)
\( G_{2}(s) = \frac{X^{\prime }(s)}{W^{\prime }_{2}(s)} = \frac{K_{2}}{\tau s+1} \)
\( G_{3}(s) = \frac{X^{\prime }(s)}{W^{\prime }_{1}(s)} = \frac{K_{3}}{\tau s+1} \).
