Guest Question.

Consider a cylindrical electric immersion heater with a rating of 1000W. The heater is 16 mm in diameter and 350 mm long. The heater is placed horizontally in a pool of water in a bucket at 37 \(^\circ C\).
(a) Estimate the temperature of the heater under steady state. The properties of the water at temperatures 37 \(^\circ C\) and 67 \(^\circ C\) are tabulated below in Table 6.1 (A more comprehensive Table of properties for water was given in chapter 5). Please note that only β has to be evaluated at \(T_{_\infty }\) and all other properties need to be evaluated at \(T_{_f}=(T_{_w}+T_{_\infty })/2\), where \(T_{_w}\) is the heater temperature. Since \(T_{_w}\) is unknown, assume \(T_{_w}\) initially to be 67 \(^\circ C\) .
(b) What will be the heater temperature be if we forget to add water in the bucket and the heater is left to operate in air?

S. No. T(\(^\circ C\)) ρ(kg/\(m^3\)) \(C_p\)(J/kg.K) k(W/m.K) α(\(m^2\)/s) ν, (\(m^2\)/s) Pr
1 37 993.3 4179 0.626 1.508 × \(10^{-7}\) 6.98 × \(10^{-7}\) 4.63
2 67 979.5 4189 0.6605 1.610 × \(10^{-7}\) 4.31 × \(10^{-7}\) 2.68

Solution

(a) \(T_{_w}\) is assumed to be 67 \(^\circ C\) as given in the problem. Film temperature, \(T_{_f}=(T_{_w}+T_{_\infty })/2\)= (340 + 310)/2 = 650/2 = 325K.

Properties of water at \(T_{_f}\)= 325K:

\(v=5.65\times 10^{-7}m^2/s\), \(\alpha =1.56\times 10^{-7}m^2/s\), k = 0.64325 W/mK, Pr = 3.655. \(\beta =3.61\times 10^{-4}K^{-1}(atT_{_\infty })\)

\(Ra_{_D}=\frac{g\beta (T_w-T_\infty )L^3}{v \alpha } \)

 

\(Ra_{_D}=\frac{9.81\times (340-310)\times 0.016^3\times 3.61\times 10^{-4}}{5.65\times 1.56\times 10^{-14}} \)

 

\(Ra_{_D}=4.94\times 10^6\)

From Eq. (6.103)

\(\overline{Nu_{_D}} =\left\lgroup 0.60+0.387\left\lgroup\frac{Ra_{_D}}{\left\lgroup1+\left\lgroup\frac{0.559}{Pr}\right\rgroup ^{9/16}\right\rgroup^{16/9} }\right\rgroup ^{1/6}\right\rgroup ^2\)

 

\(\overline{Nu_{_D}} =\left\lgroup0.60+0.387\left\lgroup\frac{4.94\times 10^6}{\left\lgroup1+\left\lgroup\frac{0.559}{Pr}\right\rgroup ^{9/16}\right\rgroup^{16/9} }\right\rgroup ^{1/6}\right\rgroup ^2\)

 

\(\overline{Nu_{_D}} =27.286\)

 

\(h=\overline{Nu_{_D}} \times \frac{k}{D} \)

 

\(h=27.286\times \frac{0.64325}{0.016} \)

 

\(h=1097W/m^2K\) .

The estimated wall temperature

\(T_w=T_\infty +\frac{1000}{h\times \pi DL} =361.8K\)

Iteration 2
Update wall temperature from 340K to 361.8K

Film temperature,

\(T_f =(T_w+T_\infty )/2=(361.8+310)/2=335.9K\)

Properties of water at \(T_f =325K\):

\(v=4.67\times 10^{-7}m^2/s\), \(\alpha =1.6\times 10^{-7}m^2/s\), k = 0:66 W/mK, Pr = 2.95

\(Ra_{_D}=\frac{9.81\times (361.8-310)\times 0.016^3\times 3.61\times 10^{-4}}{4.67\times 1.6\times 10^{-14}} \)

 

\(Ra_{_D}=1\times 10^7\)

 

\(\overline{Nu_{_D}} =\left\lgroup 0.60+0.387\left\lgroup\frac{1\times 10^7}{\left\lgroup1+\left\lgroup\frac{0.559}{2.95}\right\rgroup ^{9/16}\right\rgroup^{16/9} }\right\rgroup ^{1/6}\right\rgroup ^2\)

 

\(\overline{Nu_{_D}} =33.135\)

 

\(h= \overline{Nu_{_D}} \times \frac{k}{D} \)

 

\(h=33.135\times \frac{0.66}{0.016} \)

 

\(h=1358.15W/m^2K\)

Estimated wall temperature

\(T_w=T_\infty +\frac{1000}{h\times \pi DL}=351.9K \)

After seven iterations (see Table 6.2),

(b) Now with air as the operating ﬂuid and assuming \(T_w\) = 360 K

Film temperature,

\(T_f =(T_w+T_\infty )/2=(360+310)/2=335K\)

Properties of air at \(T_f =335K\):

\(v=1.9\times 10^{-5}m^2/s\), \(\alpha =2.72\times 10^{-5}m^2/s\), k = 0.0285 W/mK, Pr = 0.7075

 

\(\beta =\frac{1}{T_\infty } =3.2\times 10^{-3}K^{-1}\)

 

\(Ra_{_D}=\frac{9.81\times (360-310)\times 0.016^3\times 3.2\times 10^{-3}}{1.9\times 2.72\times 10^{-10}} \)

 

\(Ra_{_L}=12540.8 \)

 

\(\overline{Nu_{_D}} =\left\lgroup 0.60+0.387\left\lgroup\frac{12540.8}{\left\lgroup1+\left\lgroup\frac{0.559}{0.7075}\right\rgroup ^{9/16}\right\rgroup^{16/9} }\right\rgroup ^{1/6}\right\rgroup ^2\)

 

\(\overline{Nu_{_D}} =4.6\)

 

\(h=\overline{Nu_{_D}} \times \frac{k}{D} \)

 

\(h=4.6\times \frac{0.028545}{0.016} \)

 

\(h=8.232W/m^2K\).

Estimated wall temperature,

\(T_w=T_\infty +\frac{1000}{h\times \pi DL}=7217.8K \)

As we can see, at the end of the frst iteration itself, the temperature of the heater has reached an incredibly high value far beyond the melting point of the material of the heater (what it is made of is immaterial). Hence, even by inadvertence, we can ill-afford to operate an immersion heater designed for water, with air as the medium.
