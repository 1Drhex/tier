Guest Question.

Determining the Step Response of an RC Circuit

The switch in the circuit shown in Fig. 7.22 has been in position 1 for a long time. At t = 0, the switch moves to position 2. Find

a)\( v_{o}\)(t) for t ≥ 0 and

b) \(i_{o}(t) for t ≥ 0^{+}\).

Admin
Solution

a) The switch has been in position 1 for a long time, so the initial value of \(v_{0}\) is 40(60/80), or 30 V. To take advantage of Eqs. 7.51 \(v_{C} = I_{s}R + (V_{0} – I_{s}R)e^{-t/RC}, t ≥ 0\). and 7.53 \(i = \left(I_{s }-\frac{V_{0}}{R}\right) e^{-t/RC}, t ≥ 0^{+}\), , we find the Norton equivalent with respect to the terminals of the capacitor for t ≥ 0. To do this, we begin by computing the open-circuit voltage, which is given by the -75 V source divided across the 40 kΩ and 160 kΩ resistors:

\(V_{oc} = \frac {160 × 10^{3}}{(40 + 160) × 10^{3}} (-75)\) = -60 V.

Next, we calculate the Thévenin resistance, as seen to the right of the capacitor, by shorting the -75 V source and making series and parallel combinations of the resistors:

\(R_{Th} = 8000 + 40,000 || 160,000 = 40 kΩ\)

The value of the Norton current source is the ratio of the open-circuit voltage to the Thévenin resistance,or -60/(40 × \(10^{3}\)) = -1.5 mA. The resulting Norton equivalent circuit is shown in Fig. 7.23. From Fig. 7.23, \(I_{s}R\) = -60 V and RC = 10 ms. We have already noted that \(v_{o}(0) = 30 V\), so the solution for \(v_{o}\) is

\(v_{o} = -60 + [30 – (-60)]e^{-100t}\)

= \(-60 + 90e^{-100t}\) V, t ≥ 0.

b) We write the solution for \(i_{0}\) directly from Eq. 7.53 \(i = \left(I_{s }-\frac{V_{0}}{R}\right) e^{-t/RC}, t ≥ 0^{+}\), by noting that \(I_{s} = -1.5 mA\) and \(V_{o}/R = (30/40) × 10^{-3}\), or 0.75 mA:

\(i_{o} = -2.25e^{-100t} mA\), t ≥ \(0^{+}\)

We check the consistency of the solutions for \(v_{o} and i_{0}\) by noting that

\(i_{o} = C \frac {dv_{o}}{dt} = (0.25 × 10^{-6})(-9000e^{-100t})\)

= \(-2.25e^{-100t} mA\).

Because \(dv_{o}(0^{-})/dt = 0\), the expression for \(i_{o}\) clearly is valid only for t ≥\( 0^{+}\).
