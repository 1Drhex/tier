Guest Question.

The mass density of a graded shaft is given by \(\rho =\rho _{0}\left(1+\kappa r^{2} \right) \) .

Determine the moments of inertia \(\Theta _{x}\) and \(\Theta _{y}\) .

Admin
Solution

We first consider a thin circular disk element of thickness \(dx\) . With the mass

\(dm= \rho 2\pi rdrdx=2\pi \rho _{0}\left(r+\kappa r^{3} \right)drdx \)

of a chosen ring, we obtain for its mass and its moment of inertia

\(dm^{*} =\int\limits_{0}^{R}{dm}=\frac{\pi }{2}\rho _{0}R^{2}dx \left(2+\kappa R^{2} \right) \) ,

\(d\Theta^{*}_{x} =\int\limits_{0}^{R}{r^{2} dm}=2\pi \rho _{0}dx \int\limits_{0}^{R}{\left(r^{3}+\kappa r^{5} \right)dr } \)

 

\(=2\pi \rho _{0} dx \left(\frac{R^{4} }{4}+\kappa \frac{R^{6} }{6} \right)=\frac{\pi }{6}\rho _{0}dx R^{4}\left(3+2\kappa R^{2} \right) \) .

Because of the radial symmetry of the disk, its axial moments of inertia with respect to \(x\) , \(y\) and \(z\) (axes through the center of mass of the disk) are simply related by \(d\Theta ^{*}_{x}=d\Theta ^{*}_{y}+d\Theta ^{*}_{z}=2d\Theta ^{*}_{y} \) , i.e.,

\(d\Theta ^{*}_{y}= \frac{\pi }{12}\rho _{0}dx R^{4}\left(3+2\kappa R^{2} \right) \) .

To calculate \(\Theta _{x} \) and \(\Theta _{y}\) , we now integrate over the length \(l\) , where we use the parallel-axis theorem to obtain \(\Theta _{y}\) :

\(\underline{\underline{ \Theta _{x}} }=\int\limits_{-l/2}^{l/2}{d\Theta ^{*}_{x} }=\frac{\pi }{6}\rho _{0}R^{4}\left(3+2\kappa R^{2} \right) \int\limits_{-l/2}^{l/2}{dx } =\underline{\underline{\frac{\pi }{6}\rho _{0}lR^{4}\left(3+2\kappa R^{2} \right) } } \) ,

\(\underline{\underline{ \Theta _{y}} }=\int\limits_{-l/2}^{l/2}{\left(d\Theta ^{*}_{y}+x^{2}dm^{*} \right) } \)

 

\(=\frac{\pi }{12}\rho _{0}R^{4}\left(3+2\kappa R^{2} \right) \int\limits_{-l/2}^{l/2}{dx } + \frac{\pi }{2}\rho _{0}R^{2}\left(2+\kappa R^{2} \right) \int\limits_{-l/2}^{l/2}{x^{2} dx } \)

 

\(=\underline{\underline{\frac{\pi }{24}\rho _{0}lR^{2}\left[2R^{2}\left(3+2\kappa R^{2} \right)+l^{2}\left(2+\kappa R^{2} \right) \right]} } \) .

Remark: For \(\kappa =0\) follow with \(m=\rho _{0} \pi R^{2}l \) the results of the homogeneous shaft: \(\Theta _{x}=mR^{2}/2 \) , \(\Theta _{y}=m\left(3R^{2}+l^{2} \right) /12 \) .
