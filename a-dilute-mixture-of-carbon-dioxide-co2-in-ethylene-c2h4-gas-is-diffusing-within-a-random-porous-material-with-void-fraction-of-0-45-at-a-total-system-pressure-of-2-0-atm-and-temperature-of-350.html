Guest Question.

A dilute mixture of carbon dioxide ( \( CO_{2} \)) in ethylene ( \( C_{2}H_{4} \)) gas is diffusing within a random porous material with void fraction of 0.45 at a total system pressure of 2.0 atm and temperature of 350 K. Estimate the effective diffusion coefficient of \( CO_{2} \) and ethylene within the porous material if the mean pore diameter is \( 0.20 \mu m \).

Admin
Solution

Let species A represent \( CO_{2} \) with molecular weight of 44 g/mole, and species B represent \( C_{2}H_{4} \) with a molecular weight of 28 g/mole. The gas-phase binary molecular diffusion coefficient, \( D_{AB} \), is \( 0.077 cm^{2}/s \) at 2.0 atm total system pressure and 350 K absolute temperature. Given that the diffusion process is within a mesoporous material, which has pore diameters less than \( 1.0 \mu m \left (1.0 \mu m = 1.0 \times 10^{-6} m \right ), \) Knudsen diffusion may contribute to the molecular mass-transfer process. The Knudsen diffusion coefficient for \( CO_{2} \) is estimated by equation ( \( D_{KA} = 4850 d_{pore} \sqrt{\frac{T}{M_{A}}} \) ), with \( d_{pore} = 2.0 \times 10^{-5} cm \) and \( M_{A} = 44 g/gmole \) :

 

\( D_{KA} = 4850 d_{pore} \sqrt{\frac{T}{M_{A}}} = 4850 \left ( 2.0 \times 10^{-5} \right )\sqrt{\frac{350}{44}} = 0.274 cm^{2}/s \)

 

Consequently, from equation ( \( \frac{1}{D_{Ae}} = \frac{1}{D_{AB}} + \frac{1}{D_{KA}} \) ), for \( CO_{2} \) diluted in ethylene, effective diffusion coefficient of species A in a dilute gas mixture within the pore is

 

\( D_{Ae} = \frac{D_{AB} D_{KA}}{D_{AB} + D_{KA}} = \frac{\left ( 0.077 cm^{2}/s \right ) \left ( 0.274 cm^{2} /s \right )}{\left ( 0.077 cm^{2}/s \right ) + \left ( 0.274 cm^{2} /s \right )} = 0.060 cm^{2} /s \)

 

We note that \( D_{Ae} < D_{AB} \), showing the effect of Knudsen diffusion. To this point, the mean-free path and Knudsen number for \( CO_{2} \) inside the pore are

 

\( \lambda = \frac{\kappa T}{\sqrt{2} \pi \sigma_{A}^{2} P} = \frac{\left ( 1.38 \times 10^{-16} \frac{erg}{K} \frac{1 Nm}{10^{7} erg} \right ) \left ( 350 K \right )}{\sqrt{2} \pi \left ( 0.3996 nm \frac{1 m}{10^{9} nm} \right )^{2} \left ( 2.0 atm \frac{101.300 N/m^{2}}{atm} \right )} \)

 

\( = 3.36 \times 10^{-8} m = 0.0336 \mu m \)

 

and

\( Kn = \frac{\lambda }{d_{pore}} = \frac{0.0336 \mu m}{0.200 \mu m} = 0.17 \)

 

From this analysis, Knudsen diffusion plays only a moderate role at the conditions of the process even though the pore diameter is only \( 0.2 \mu m \). Finally, the effective diffusion coefficient for \( CO_{2} \), as corrected for the void fraction within the random porous material, is

\( {D}' _{Ae}= \varepsilon^{2} D_{Ae} = \left ( 0.45 \right )^{2} \left ( 0.060 cm^{2}/s \right ) = 0.012 cm^{2} /s \)

 

The effect of total system pressure on the ratio \( D_{Ae} / D_{AB} \) is shown in Figure. At high total system pressures, the effective diffusion coefficient ( \( D_{Ae} \)) approaches the molecular diffusion coefficient, \( D_{AB} \). At low total system pressures, Knudsen diffusion becomes important.
