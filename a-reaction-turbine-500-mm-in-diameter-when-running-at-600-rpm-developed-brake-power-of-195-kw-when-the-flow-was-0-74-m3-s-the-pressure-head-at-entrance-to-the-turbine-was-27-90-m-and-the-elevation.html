Guest Question.

A reaction turbine 500 mm in diameter, when running at 600 rpm, developed brake power of 195 kW when the flow was 0.74 m3/s. The pressure head at entrance to the turbine was 27.90 m, and the elevation of the turbine casing above tailwater level was 1.91 m. The water enters the turbine with a velocity of 3.66 m/s. Calculate (a) the effective head, (b) the efficiency, (c) the speed expected under a head of 68.6 m, and (d) the brake power and discharge under the 68.6-m head.

Admin
Solution

(a) Effective head \(H =\frac{P}{\gamma }+\frac{V^2}{2g}+z =27.90+\frac{(3.66)^2}{2g}+1.91=30.5 \) m .

(b) Power supplied by water \(=\gamma QH=(9.79)(0.74)(30.5) =221 \) kW .

Efficiency \(=\frac{power from shaft}{power supplied}=\frac{195}{221}=88.2 \)%

(c) For the same turbine, the \(\frac{ND_{1}}{\sqrt{H} } \) ratio is constant. Then \(\frac{N\times 500}{\sqrt{68.6} }=\frac{600\times 500}{\sqrt{30.5} } \) or N = 900 rpm .

(d) For the same turbine, the \(\frac{P}{D^2_{1}H^3/2} \) and \(\frac{Q}{D^2_{1}\sqrt{H} } \) ratios are also constant. Then

\(\frac{P}{(500)^2(68.6)^{3/2}}=\frac{195}{(500)^2(30.5)^{3/2}} \) , P = 658 kW and \(\frac{Q}{(500)^2\sqrt{68.6} }=\frac{0.74}{(500)^2\sqrt{30.5} } \) , Q = 1.11 m3/s .
