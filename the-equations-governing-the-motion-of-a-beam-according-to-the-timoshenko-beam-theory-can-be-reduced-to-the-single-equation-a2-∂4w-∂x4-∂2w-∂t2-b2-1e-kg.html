Guest Question.

The equations governing the motion of a beam according to the Timoshenko beam theory can be reduced to the single equation
\(a^{2}\frac{\partial^{4} w}{\partial x^{4}}+\frac{\partial ^{2}w}{\partial t^{2}}-b^{2}(1+\frac{E}{kG})\frac{\partial^{4}w }{\partial x^{2}\partial t^{2}}+\frac{b^{2}m}{kG}\frac{\partial^{4} }{\partial t^{4}}=0\)
where \(a^{2}=EI/mA\) and \(b^{2}=I/A\). Here E is the Young’s modulus, G is the shear modulus, m is the mass per unit length, A is the area of cross sectin, and I is the moment of inertia. Assuming that \((b^{2}m/kG)< < 1\) (i.e., neglect the last term in the governing equation), formulate the finite element model of the (a) eigenvalue problem for the determination of natural frequencies, and (b) fully discretized problem for the determination of the transient response.

Admin
Solution

(a) This is a fourth—order hyperbolic differential equation. Let

\(w(x,t)=W(x)e^{-iwt}\)

and reduce the given equation to

\(a^{2}\frac{\mathrm{d^{4}} W}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}-\omega ^{2}W+\omega ^{2}b^{2}\left ( 1+\frac{E}{kG} \right )\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} W}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}=0\)

The weak form of the equation is given by

\(0=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}v\left [ a^{2}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} W}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}-\omega ^{2}\left ( W-c^{2}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} W}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}} \right ) \right ]dx\)

 

= \(\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\left [ a^{2}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} v}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}}W }{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}-\omega ^{2}\left ( vW+c^{2}\frac{\mathrm{d} v}{\mathrm{d} x}\frac{\mathrm{d} W}{\mathrm{d} x} \right ) \right ]dx\)

\(-v(x_{a})Q_{1}-v(x_{b})Q_{3}-\left ( -\frac{\mathrm{d} v}{\mathrm{d} x} \right )_{A}Q_{2}-\left ( -\frac{\mathrm{d} v}{\mathrm{d} x} \right )_{B}Q_{4}\)

where v is the weight function, \(c^{2}=b^{2}\left ( 1+\frac{E}{kG} \right )\), and

\(Q_{1}=\left [ a^{2}\frac{\mathrm{d^{3}} W}{\mathrm{d} x^{3}}+\omega ^{2}c^{2}\frac{\mathrm{d} W}{\mathrm{d} x} \right ]_{A}\), \(Q_{3}=-\left [ a^{2}\frac{\mathrm{d^{3}} W}{\mathrm{d} x^{3}}+\omega ^{2}c^{2}\frac{\mathrm{d} W}{\mathrm{d} x} \right ]_{B}\)

\(Q_{2}=\left [ a^{2}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} W}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}} \right ]_{A}\) , \(Q_{4}=-\left [ a^{2}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} W}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}} \right ]_{B}\)

The finite element model is given by

\(([K^{e}]-\omega ^{2}[M^{e}])\left \{ \Delta ^{e} \right \}=\left \{ Q^{e} \right \}\)

where

\(W(x)\approx \sum_{j=1}^{4}\Delta _{j}^{e}\varphi _{j}^{e}(x)\)

and

\(K_{ij}^{e}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}a^{2}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} \varphi _{i}}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} \varphi _{j}}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}dx\)

 

\(M_{ij}^{e}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\left [ \varphi _{i}\varphi _{j}+b^{2} \left ( 1+\frac{E}{kG} \right )\frac{\mathrm{d} \varphi _{i}}{\mathrm{d} x}\frac{\mathrm{d} \varphi _{j}}{\mathrm{d} x}\right ]dx\)

and \(\varphi _{i}\) are the Hermite family of interpolation functions.

 

(b) The semidiscrete weak form (neglecting the term involving the fourth-order derivative with respect to t) is given by

0 = \(\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}v\left [ a^{2}\frac{\partial^{4} w}{\partial x^{4}}+\frac{\partial^{2} w}{\partial t^{2}}-b^{2}\left ( 1+\frac{E}{kG} \right )\frac{\partial^{4} w}{\partial x^{2} \partial t^{2}} \right ]dx\)

= \(\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\left [ a^{2}\frac{\partial^{2} v}{\partial x^{2}}\frac{\partial^{2} w}{\partial x^{2}}+v\frac{\partial^{2} w}{\partial t^{2}}+c^{2}\frac{\mathrm{d} v}{\mathrm{d} x}\frac{\partial^{3} w}{\partial x \partial t^{2}} \right ]dx\)

\(-v(x_{a})Q_{1}-v(x_{b})Q_{3}-\left ( -\frac{\mathrm{d} v}{\mathrm{d} x} \right )_{A}Q_{2}-\left ( -\frac{\mathrm{d} v}{\mathrm{d} x} \right )_{B}Q_{4}\)

wehere

\(Q_{1}=\left [ a^{2}\frac{\partial3 w}{\partial x^{3}}-c^{2}\frac{\partial^{3} w}{\partial x \partial t^{2}} \right ]_{A}\), \(Q_{3}=-\left [ a^{2}\frac{\partial3 w}{\partial x^{3}}-c^{2}\frac{\partial^{3} w}{\partial x \partial t^{2}} \right ]_{B}\)

\(Q_{2}=\left [ a^{2}\frac{\partial^2 w}{\partial x^2} \right ]_{A}\), \(Q_{4}=-\left [ a^{2}\frac{\partial^2 w}{\partial x^2} \right ]_{B}\)

The finite element model is given by

\([K^{e}]\left \{ \Delta ^{e} \right \}+[M^{e}]\left \{ \ddot{\Delta }^{e} \right \}=\left \{ Q^{e} \right \}\)

where \(K_{ij}^{e}\) and \(M_{ij}^{e}\) are the same as defined earlier.

The fully discretized finite element model can be obtained .
