Solve with variable specific heats using Table A.7.

A Brayton cycle inlet is at 300 K, 100 kPa and the combustion adds 800 kJ/kg. The maximum temperature is 1400 K due to material considerations. What is the maximum allowed compression ratio? For this compression ratio calculate the net work and cycle efficiency using cold air properties.

Solution

Combustion: \(h _{3}= h _{2}+ q _{ H } ; \quad {}_{2} w _{3}=0 \text { and } T _{\max }= T _{3}=1400 K\)

\(h _{2}= h _{3}- q _{ H }=1515.27-800=715.27 kJ / kg\)

From Table A.7.1

\(T _{2} \approx 701.6 K ; \quad s _{ T 2}^{ o }=7.74251 ; \quad T _{1}=300 K ; \quad s _{ T1 }^{ o }=6.86926 kJ / kg K\)

Reversible adiabatic compression leads to constant s, from Eq.6.19:

\(P _{2} / P _{1}=\exp \left[\left( s _{ T 2}^{ o }- s _{ T 1}^{ o }\right) / R \right]=\exp (3.04268)= 2 0 . 9 6\)

Reversible adiabatic expansion leads to constant s, from Eq.6.19

\(s _{ T 4}^{ o }= s _{ T 3}^{ o }+ R \ln \left( P _{4} / P _{3}\right)=8.52891+0.287 \ln (1 / 20.96)=7.65568 kJ / kgK\)

From Table A.7.1 by linear interpolation \(T _{4} \approx 646.85 K , h _{4}=656.81 kJ / kg\)
