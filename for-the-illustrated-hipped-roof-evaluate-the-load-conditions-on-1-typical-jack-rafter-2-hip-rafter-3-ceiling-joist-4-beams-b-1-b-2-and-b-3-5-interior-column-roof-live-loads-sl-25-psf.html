Guest Question.

For the illustrated hipped roof, evaluate the load conditions on:

1. Typical jack rafter.

2. Hip rafter.

3. Ceiling joist.

4. Beams B-1, B-2, and B-3.

5. Interior column.

Roof Live Loads:

SL= 25 psf

Roof Dead Loads:

Roofing = 6 psf

Plywood roof sheathing = 1.5 psf

Joist framing = 4#/ft.

Ceiling Loads:

DL = 7 psf

LL = 20 psf

Admin
Solution

Jack Rafter (Typical span):

Roof DL:
ωDL = (12 lb./ft2) × (2′) = 24 lb./ft.

\(\underset{(horiz proj)}{\omega ^{'}_{DL} }\) = (15/12)(24lb./ft.) = 30 lb./ft.

Snow:

ωSL = (25 lb./ft2) × (2′) = 50 lb./ft.

\(\underset{(horiz proj)}{\omega_{total} } = \omega_{SL} + \omega ^{'}_{DL} \) = 50 lb./ft. + 30 lb./ft. = 80 lb./ft.

This rafter represents the maximum load condition onto the hip rafter since other rafters diminish in length.

The spacing of the jack rafter along the length of the hip rafter is: 2′ × \(\sqrt{2}\) = 2.82′

∴ ω = \( \frac{2}{2.82} × (400 lb.) = 284 \) lb./ft.

Ceiling Joists: (2′ o.c. spacing)

ωDL = (7 lb./ft.2) × (2′) = 14 lb./ft.

ωLL = (20 lb./ft.2) × (2′) = 40 lb./ft.

ωtotal = 14 lb./ft. + 40 lb./ft. = 54 lb./ft

Beam B-1:

ωD+L = \(\frac{540 lb.}{2 ft.}\) = 270 lb./ft.

Beam B-2:
Joist load: (span = 8′)

ω = (27 lb./ft.2) × \(\underset{(trib width)}{(4^{'})} \) = 108 lb./ft.

Beam B-3:
The load condition on beam B-3 is identical to beam B-2.

ωD+L = 135lb./ft.

Loads to the column –
Ceiling joist: 1081b.
Beam B-1: 10801b.
Beam B-2: 8101b.
Beam B-3: 540 lb.

P = 2538 lb.

In addition to the vertically applied loads on both the jack and hip rafters, truss action develops due to the ceiling tie condition. An examination of the truss action for each rafter case will be performed. The three dimensional truss solution for the hip rafter was not covered in the text but can be done relatively easily using readily available structural software.
