Guest Question.

During the flow of air at \(T_{\infty}=20^{\circ} C\) over a plate surface maintained at a constant temperature of \(T_{s}=160^{\circ} C\), the dimensionless temperature profile within the air layer over the plate is determined to be \( \frac{T(y)-T_{\infty}}{T_{s}-T_{\infty}}=e^{-a y}\) where \( a=3200 m ^{-1}\) and y is the vertical distance measured from the plate surface in m (Fig. 6-8). Determine the heat flux on the plate surface and the convection heat transfer coefficient.

 

 

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION Airflow over a flat plate has a given temperature profile. The heat flux on the plate surface and the convection heat transfer coefficient are to be determined.

Assumptions 1 The given non dimensional temperature profile is representative of the variation of temperature over the entire plate. 2 Heat transfer by radiation is negligible.

Properties The thermal conductivity of air at the film temperature of \( T_{f}=\left(T_{s}+T_{\infty}\right) / 2=\left(160^{\circ} C +20^{\circ} C \right) / 2=90^{\circ} C\) is \( k=0.03024 W / m \cdot K \) Table (A -15)

Analysis Noting that heat transfer from the plate to air at the surface is by conduction, heat flux from the solid surface to the fluid layer adjacent to the surface is determined from

\(\dot{q}=\dot{q}_{ cond }=-\left.k_{ fluid } \frac{\partial T}{\partial y}\right|_{y=0} \)

where the temperature gradient at the plate surface is

\( \left.\frac{\partial T}{\partial y}\right|_{y=0} =\left(T_{s}-T_{\infty}\right) a\left[e^{-a y}\right]_{y=o}=\left(T_{s}-T_{\infty}\right)(-\alpha)=\left(160^{\circ} C -20^{\circ} C \right)\left(-3200 m ^{-1}\right)=-4.48 \times 10^{5}{ }^{\circ} C / m \)

Substituting, the heat flux is determined to be

\( \dot{q}=-(0.03024 W / m \cdot K )\left(-4.48 \times 10^{5}{ }^{\circ} C / m \right)=1.35 \times 10^{4} W / m ^{2}\)

Then the convection heat transfer coefficient becomes

\(h =\frac{-k_{\text {fluid }}(\partial T / \partial y)_{y=0}}{T_{s}-T_{\infty}} =\frac{-(0.03024 W / m \cdot K )\left(-4.48 \times 10^{5}{ }^{\circ} C / m \right)}{\left(160^{\circ} C -20^{\circ} C \right)}=96.8 W / m ^{2} . K \)

Discussion The convection heat transfer coefficient could also be determined from Newton’s law of cooling, \(\dot{q}=h\left(T_{s}-T_{\infty}\right) .\)
