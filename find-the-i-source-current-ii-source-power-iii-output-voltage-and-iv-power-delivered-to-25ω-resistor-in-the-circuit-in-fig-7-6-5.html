Guest Question.

Find the (i) source current, (ii) source power, (iii) output voltage and (iv) power delivered to \(25Ω\) resistor in the circuit in Fig. 7.6-5.

Admin
Solution

The source voltage phasor is \(300∠0° V\).
The dependent sources involve first derivative of controlling currents. But differentiation in timedomain is to be replaced by multiplication by \(jω\) in the phasor equivalent circuit. The value of \(ω\) in this example is \(100\) rad/s. The phasor equivalent circuit of the circuit is given in Fig. 7.6-6. The mesh equations are written as follows:

\(\begin{aligned} -300 \angle 0^{\circ}+I_{1}+j 100 I_{1}+j 50\left(-I_{2}\right) &=0 \\ -j 50 I_{1}+0.25 I_{2}+j 25 I_{2}+25 I_{2} &=0 \end{aligned}\)

 

Recasting these equations in matrix form,

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc} 1+j 100 & -j 50 \\-j 50 & 25.25+j 25 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c} I_{1} \\ I_{2} \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c} 300 \angle 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]\)

 

Solving for \(I_{1}\) and \(I_{2}\), we get

\(\begin{aligned} I_{1} &=\frac{300 \angle 0 \times(25.25+j 25)}{(1+j 100)(25.25+j 25)-(j 50)(j 50)}=\frac{300 \angle 0 \times(25.25+j 25)}{(25.25+j 2550)} \mathrm{A} \\ &=\frac{300 \angle 0 \times 35.53 \angle 44.71^{\circ}}{2550.13 \angle 89.43^{\circ}}=4.18 \angle-44.72^{\circ} \mathrm{A} \\ I_{2} &=\frac{300 \angle 0 \times j 50}{2550.13 \angle 89.43^{\circ}}=5.88 \angle 0.57^{\circ} \mathrm{A} \\ \therefore V_{o} &=25 \times 5.88 \angle 0.57^{\circ}=147 \angle 0.57^{\circ} \mathrm{V} \\ \therefore i_{1}(t) &=4.18 \cos \left(100 t-44.72^{\circ}\right) \mathrm{A} \\ i_{2}(t) &=5.88 \cos \left(100 t+0.57^{\circ}\right) \mathrm{A} \\ v_{o}(t) &=147 \cos \left(100 t+0.57^{\circ}\right) \mathrm{V} \end{aligned}\)

 

Source power \(=0.5 \times 300 \times 4.18 \times \cos \left(0-\left(-44.72^{\circ}\right)\right)=445.5 \mathrm{~W}\)

Average power delivered to \(25 \Omega=0.5 \times 5.88 \times 5.88 \times 25=432.2 \mathrm{~W}\)

[This is an equivalent circuit for a \(2:1\) two-winding transformer using dependent sources to model the mutual coupling between the windings. Note that the output voltage is almost equal to half of the input voltage and almost in phase with it. The presence of the \(1Ω\) and \(0.25Ω\) resistors make the output amplitude slightly less than \(\)150 V and phase of output slightly different from zero. These resistors model the inevitably present winding resistances.]
