Guest Question.

Find the linearization parameters and the mean-squared response levels predicted by Eqs. 10.80 \( \hat{g}[X(t), \dot{X}(t), Z(t)]\thickapprox a_{0} +a_{1} X(t)+ a_{2} \dot{X} (t)+a_{3}Z(t) \) , 10.84 \( a_{3}=E \left(\frac{\partial \hat{g} [X(t), \dot{X}(t), Z(t)]}{\partial Z(t)} \right) = kE\left(1-U[Z(t)-x_{y}] U[\dot{X}(t)] -U[-Z(t)- x_{y}] U[-\dot{X}(t) ]\right) \) for stationary response of the bilinear hysteretic oscillator shown in Fig. 10.2 with an \( \left\{F(t)\right\} \) excitation that is a stationary, mean-zero, Gaussian white noise with autospectral density \( S_{0} \) .

Admin
Solution

The procedure is the same as in . In addition to the \( b_{2} \) and \( b_{3} \) parameters evaluated there, the linearization now also uses \( a_{3}=k_{1}b_{2} \) and the parameter \( a_{2} \) from Eq. 10.83

 

\( a_{2}= E\left(\frac{\partial \hat{g}[X(t), \dot{X}(t), Z(t)]}{\partial \dot{X}(t)} \right) \) \( =kE\left[Z(t) \delta [\dot{X} (t)] \left(-U[Z(t)-x_{y}] + U[-Z(t) -x_{y}]\right) \right] + kx_{y}E \left[\delta [\dot{X}(t)] \left(U[Z(t)-x_{y}] +U[-Z(t) -x_{y}]\right) \right] \) \( =-2k\int_{x_{y}}^{\infty }{(w-x_{y})p_{\dot{X}(t)Z(t)}(0,w)dw} \)

which can be rewritten as \( a_{2}=-2k_{1}(\sigma _{Z}/\sigma _{\dot{X}})\hat{a} _{2} \) with

\( \hat{a} _{2}=\frac{(1-\rho ^{2}_{\dot{X}Z})^{1/2}}{2\pi}\exp \left(\frac{-x^{2}_{y}}{2\sigma ^{2}_{Z}(1-\rho ^{2}_{\dot{X}Z})} \right) -\frac{x_{y}}{(2\pi)^{1/2}\sigma _{Z}} \Phi \left(\frac{-x_{y}}{\sigma _{Z}(1-\rho ^{2}_{\dot{X}Z})^{1/2}} \right) \)

Note that \( \hat{a} _{2} \) , like \( b_{2} \) and \( \hat{b} _{3} \) , is a function only of \( \rho _{\dot{X}Z} \) and \( x_{y}/\sigma _{Z} \) , so it can be evaluated independently from the analysis of the dynamics of the oscillator.

Substituting Eq. 10.80 \( \hat{g}[X(t), \dot{X}(t), Z(t)]\thickapprox a_{0} +a_{1} X(t)+ a_{2} \dot{X} (t)+a_{3}Z(t) \), with \( a_{0}=0,a_{1},=0, \) , and \( a_{3}=k_{1}b_{2} \) into Eq. 10.56 \( m\ddot{X} (t)+c\dot{X} (t)+k_{2}X(t)+g[\left\{X(t)\right\} ]=F(t) \) , gives the equation of motion for the linearized system as

\( m\ddot{X}(t)+(c+a_{2})\dot{X}(t)+k_{2}X(t)+k_{1}b_{2}Z(t)=F(t) \)

This equation must be solved in conjunction with Eq. 10.78 \( \dot{Z} (t)=b_{2}\dot{X} (t)+b_{3}Z(t) \) , giving the rate of change of \( Z(t) \) . Note that the new equation of motion has the same form as Eq. 10.77 \( m\ddot{X} (t)+c\dot{X} (t)+k_{2}X(t)+ k_{1}Z(t)= F(t) \), but it has different stiffness and damping values. In particular, the presence of \( b_{2} \) , which is a number between zero and unity, results in a reduced stiffness for this linearization of the problem. Similarly, \( a_{2} \) is a negative quantity, so it causes a reduction in the damping of the system. Thus, we can anticipate that the response of this linear model will be greater than that for Eq. 10.70 \( \hat{g}[X(t), \dot{X}(t), Z(t) ]=kZ(t) \). The details of the state-space analysis to find the response quantities as functions of the linearization parameters will be omitted, because the procedure is fundamentally the same as in .
