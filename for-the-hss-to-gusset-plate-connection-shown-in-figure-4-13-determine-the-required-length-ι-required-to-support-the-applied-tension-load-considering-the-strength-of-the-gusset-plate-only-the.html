Guest Question.

For the \(HSS\)-to-gusset plate connection shown in Figure 4-13, determine the required length, \(ι\) , required to support the applied tension load considering the strength of the gusset plate only. The plate is ASTM A529, grade \(50.\)

Admin
Solution

From the AISCM, Table 2-4:

\(F_{y}= 50 ksi \).

\(F_{u} = 70 ksi\) to \(100 ksi\) (use \(F_{u} = 70 ksi\))

From equation (4-1), the strength based on the gross area of the plate is

\(\begin{aligned} \phi P_{n} &=\phi F_{y} A_{g}\quad (4-1) \\ &=(0.9)(50)(1.5+6+1.5)(0.375)=151 \mathrm{kips}>150 \mathrm{kips} . \mathrm{OK} \end{aligned}\)

 

From equation (4-2), the strength based on effective area is

\(\phi P_{n}=\phi F_{u} A_{e}\quad (4-2) \) (\(A_{e} = A_{g}\), load is concentric on the plate and bolts are not used)

\(=(0.75)(70)(1.5+6+1.5)(0.375)=177 \text { kips }>150 \text { kips. OK }\)

 

The available block shear strength is found from equation (4-11):

\(\begin{aligned} \phi P_{n} &=\phi\left(0.60 F_{u} A_{n v}+U_{b s} F_{u} A_{n t}\right) \leq \phi\left(0.60 F_{y} A_{g v}+U_{b s} F_{u} A_{n t}\right)\quad (4-11) \\ U_{b s} &=1.0 \text { (tension stress is uniform) } \end{aligned}\)

 

Since \(A_{n v} = A_{g v}\), the right-hand side of the equation will control. Solving for \(A_{g v},\)

\(\begin{aligned} \phi P_{n} &=\phi\left(0.60 F_{y} A_{g v}+U_{b s} F_{u} A_{n t}\right) \\ 150 &=(0.75)\left[(0.60)(50)\left(A_{g v}\right)+(1.0)(70)(6)(0.375)\right] \end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{array}{l} A_{g v}=1.42 \mathrm{in}^{2} \\ \ell_{\min }=\frac{1.42}{(2)(0.375)}=1.89 \text { in. } \end{array}\)

 

The minimum length of engagement is \(1.89 in\). in order to develop adequate strength in the gusset plate. Note that from Table 4-1, the minimum length,\(ι \), is the height of the connecting \(HSS\) member, or \(6 in\). \((ι> H) \). Therefore, the minimum length,\(ι \), is actually \(6 in\). From Table 4-1, the shear lag factor for the \(HSS\) member is

\(\begin{aligned} \bar{x} &=\frac{B^{2}+2 B H}{4(B+H)} \\ &=\frac{(4)^{2}+(2)(4)(6)}{4(4+6)}=1.6 \\ U &=1-\frac{\bar{x}}{\ell} \\ &=1-\frac{1.6}{6}=0.733 \end{aligned}\)

 

This value would then be used to determine the strength of the \(HSS\) member in tension.
