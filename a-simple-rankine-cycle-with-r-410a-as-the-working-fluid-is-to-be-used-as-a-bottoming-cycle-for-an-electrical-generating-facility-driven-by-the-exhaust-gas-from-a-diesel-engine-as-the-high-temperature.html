Guest Question.

A simple Rankine cycle with R-410A as the working fluid is to be used as a bottoming cycle for an electrical generating facility driven by the exhaust gas from a Diesel engine as the high temperature energy source in the R-410A boiler. Diesel inlet conditions are 100 kPa, 20°C, the compression ratio is 20, and the maximum temperature in the cycle is 2800 K. The R-410A leaves the bottoming cycle boiler at 80°C, 4 MPa and the condenser pressure is 1800 kPa. The power output of the Diesel engine is 1 MW. Assuming ideal cycles throughout, determine
a. The flow rate required in the diesel engine.
b. The power output of the bottoming cycle, assuming that the diesel exhaust is cooled to 200°C in the R-410A boiler.

Admin
Solution

Diesel cycle information given means: \(\dot{ W }_{ DIESEL }=1 MW\)

\(P _{1}=100 kPa , T _{1}=20{ }^{\circ} C , CR = v _{1} / v _{2}=20, T _{3}=2800 K\)

Consider the Diesel cycle

\(\begin{aligned}& T _{2}= T _{1}\left( v _{1} / v _{2}\right)^{ k -1}=293.2 K (20)^{0.4}=971.8 K \\& P _{2}= P _{1}\left( v _{1} / v _{2}\right)^{ k }=100 kPa (20)^{1.4}=6629 kPa \\& q _{ H }= C _{ P 0}\left( T _{3}- T _{2}\right)=1.004 kJ / kg – K (2800-971.8) K =1835.5 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}& v _{1}=\frac{0.287 \times 293.2}{100}=0.8415 m ^{3} / kg , \quad v _{2}=\frac{0.8415}{20}=0.04208 m ^{3} / kg \\& v _{3}= v _{2}\left( T _{3} / T _{2}\right)=0.04208 m ^{3} / kg (2800 / 971.8)=0.12124 m ^{3} / kg \\& T _{4}= T _{3}\left(\frac{ v _{3}}{ v _{4}}\right)^{ k -1}=2800 K \left(\frac{0.12124}{0.8415}\right)^{0.4}=1290 K\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}& q _{ L }=0.717(293.15-1290)=-714.7 kJ / kg \\& w _{ NET }=1835.5-714.7=1120.76 kJ / kg \\&\dot{ m }_{ AIR }=\dot{ W }_{ NET } / w _{ NET }=1000 kW / 1120.76 kJ / kg = 0 . 8 9 2 kg / s\end{aligned}\)

 

Rankine cycle information given means:

Boiler exit state: \(T _{7}=80^{\circ} C , P _{7}=4 MPa\),

Condenser: \(P _{8}= P _{5}=1800 kPa , T _{5}=28.22^{\circ} C , h _{5}= h _{ f }=103.1 kJ / kg\)

Consider the Rankine cycle

\(\begin{aligned}& s _{8}= s _{7}=1.0028=>\text { Sup. vapor } T _{8}=32.92^{\circ} C , h _{8}=290.6 kJ / kg \\& w _{ T }= h _{7}- h _{8}=311.48-290.6=20.88 kJ / kg \\&- w _{ P }= v _{5}\left( P _{6}- P _{5}\right)=0.0009595 m ^{3} / kg (4000-1800) kPa =2.11 kJ / kg \\& h _{6}= h _{5}- w _{ P }=103.1+2.11=105.21 kJ / kg \\& q _{ H }= h _{7}- h _{6}=311.48-105.21=206.27 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

Connecting the two cycles.

\(\dot{ Q }_{ H }\) available from Diesel exhaust flow cooled to 200 °C:

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{ Q }_{ H }=0.892 kg / s \times 1.004 kJ / kg – K (1290-473.2) K =731 kW \\&\Rightarrow \dot{ m }_{ R -410 a }=\dot{ Q }_{ H } / q _{ H }=731 kW / 206.27 kJ / kg =3.544 kg / s \\&\dot{ W }_{ R -410 a }=3.544 kg / s (20.88-2.11) kJ / kg = 6 6 . 5 k W\end{aligned}\)
