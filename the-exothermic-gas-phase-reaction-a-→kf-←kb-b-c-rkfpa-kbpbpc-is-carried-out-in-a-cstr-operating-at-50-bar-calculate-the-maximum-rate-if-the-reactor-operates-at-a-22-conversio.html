Guest Question.

The exothermic gas phase reaction:

\(A \underset{k_{ b }}{\stackrel{k_{ f }}{\rightleftarrows}} B + C , \quad r=k_{ f } p_{ A }-k_{ b } p_{ B } p_{ C }\)

is carried out in a CSTR operating at 50 bar. Calculate the maximum rate if the reactor operates at a 22% conversion of A with a pure A in the feed. Additional data are given below

\(k_{f}=0.435 \exp \left\lgroup -\frac{E_{f}}{R T} \right\rgroup mol \cdot s ^{-1} \cdot m ^{3} \cdot bar ^{-1}, \quad E_{f}=20 kJ / mol\)

 

\(k_{b}=147 \exp\left\lgroup -\frac{E_{b}}{R T} \right\rgroup mol \cdot s ^{-1} \cdot m ^{3} \cdot bar ^{-2}, \quad E_{b}=60 kJ / mol\)
Admin
Solution

For non-isothermal reactors, temperature plays an important role in reactor performance. In a CSTR, the temperature is uniform in the reactor and thus is the same as the final (output) temperature. To find the maximum reaction rate, we perform:

\(0=\frac{d r}{d T}=p_{A} \frac{d k_{f}}{d T}-p_{B} p_{C} \frac{d k_{b}}{d T}\) (E5.9.1)

Since the final pressures are not functions of temperature for the required conversion of A. Since based on the rate law,

\(\frac{d k_{f}}{d T}=k_{f} \frac{E_{f}}{R T^{2}} \quad \frac{d k_{b}}{d T}=k_{b} \frac{E_{b}}{R T^{2}}\) (E5.9.2)

We obtain by substituting Eq. (E5.9.2) \(\frac{d k_{f}}{d T}=k_{f} \frac{E_{f}}{R T^{2}} \quad \frac{d k_{b}}{d T}=k_{b} \frac{E_{b}}{R T^{2}}\) into (E5.9.1) \(0=\frac{d r}{d T}=p_{A} \frac{d k_{f}}{d T}-p_{B} p_{C} \frac{d k_{b}}{d T}\) ,

\(0=p_{A} k_{f} \frac{E_{f}}{R T^{2}}-p_{B} p_{C} k_{b} \frac{E_{b}}{R T^{2}}\) (E5.9.3)

Therefore

\(\frac{k_{f}}{k_{b}}=\frac{p_{B} p_{C}}{p_{A}} \frac{E_{b}}{E_{f}}\) (E5.9.4)

For a given conversion \(f_{ A }\), the flow rates of the reaction mixture are given by

\(F_{ A }=F_{ A 0}\left(1-f_{ A }\right) ; F_{ B }=F_{ A 0} f_{ A }=F_{ C } ; \text { and } F_{ T }=F_{ A }+F_{ B }+F_{ C }=F_{ A 0}\left(1+f_{ A }\right)\).

Therefore, the partial pressures in the final reaction mixture are given by

\(p_{A}=\frac{F_{A}}{F_{T}} P=\frac{1-f_{A}}{1+f_{A}} P\) (E5.9.5)

 

\(p_{B}=\frac{F_{B}}{F_{T}} P=\frac{f_{A}}{1+f_{A}} P=p_{C}\) (E5.9.6)

Substitute (E5.9.5) \(p_{A}=\frac{F_{A}}{F_{T}} P=\frac{1-f_{A}}{1+f_{A}} P\) and (E5.9.6) \(p_{B}=\frac{F_{B}}{F_{T}} P=\frac{f_{A}}{1+f_{A}} P=p_{C}\) into Eq. (E5.9.4) \(\frac{k_{f}}{k_{b}}=\frac{p_{B} p_{C}}{p_{A}} \frac{E_{b}}{E_{f}}\) , we obtain

\(\frac{k_{f}}{k_{b}}=\frac{f_{A}^{2}}{\left(1-f_{A}\right)\left(1+f_{A}\right)} \frac{E_{b}}{E_{f}} P=\frac{0.22^{2}}{(1+0.22)(1-0.22)} \times \frac{60}{20} \times 50 bar =7.63 bar\)

Also

\(\frac{k_{f}}{k_{b}}=\frac{0.435 \exp\left\lgroup -\frac{E_{f}}{R T} \right\rgroup }{147 \exp \left\lgroup -\frac{E_{b}}{R T} \right\rgroup } \operatorname{bar}=\frac{0.435}{147} \exp \frac{E_{b}-E_{f}}{R T} \text { bar }\)

Therefore,

\(T=\frac{E_{b}-E_{f}}{R \ln \left\lgroup \frac{k_{f}}{k_{b}} \times \frac{147}{0.435} \right\rgroup }=\frac{60000-20000}{8.314 \times \ln \left\lgroup 7.63 \times \frac{147}{0.435} \right\rgroup } K =614.8 K\)

Now we have obtained the optimum temperature (for maximum reaction rate). Substitute the temperature and partial pressures into the rate expression, we obtain the maximum reaction rate

\(r=0.183 mol \cdot s ^{-1} \cdot m ^{-3}\).
