Guest Question.

Let the real signal x[n], n = 0, 1, 2, …, 7:

x[n] = {2,0,1,0,−1,1,−2,1}

Compute the DFT of the signal using the FFT. How many complex multiplications are required for the computation of the DFT and of the FFT, respectively?

Admin
Solution

x[n] = {2, 0, 1, 0, −1, 1, −2, 1}, N = 8 ⇒ v = 3, so three stages of segmentation are required.

2nd stage 1st stage
etc. \(x_{oe}[n]\)={0,1} \(x_{ee}[n]\)={2,-1} \(x[n]=x_{e}[n]+x_{o}[n]\)
\(x_{oo}\)={0,1} \(x_{eo}[n]\)={1,-2} \(x_{e}[n]\)={2,1,-1,-2}
\(x_{o}[n]\)={0,0,1,1}

For the first segmentation stage, the FFT equations are:

\(X[k]=X_{e}[k]+W_{N}^{k}X_{o}[k], k=0,1,2,…,\frac{N}{2}-1 \) (1)
\(X[k+\frac{N}{2}]=X_{e}[k]-W_{N}^{k}X_{o}[k], k=0,1,2,…,\frac{N}{2}-1 \) (2)
\((1)\Rightarrow X[k]=X_{e}[k]+W_{8}^{k}X_{o}[k], k=0,1,2,3\) (1)’
\((2)\Rightarrow X[k+4]=X_{e}[k]+W_{8}^{k}X_{o}[k], k=0,1,2,3 \) (2)’

\((1)'\Rightarrow X[0]=X_{e}[0]+X_{o}[0]\)
\(X[1]=X_{e}[1]+e^{-j\frac{2\pi }{8} }X_{o}[1]\)
\(X[2]=X_{e}[2]+e^{-j\frac{2\pi }{8}2 }X_{o}[2]\)
\(X[3]=X_{e}[3]+e^{-j\frac{2\pi }{8}3 }X_{o}[3]\)

The DFT of \(x_{e}[n]\) is:

\(X_{e}[k]=\sum\limits_{n=0}^{3}{x_{e}[n]W_{4}^{kn}} =x_{e}[0]+x_{e}[1]W_{4}^{k}+x_{e}[2]W_{4}^{2k}+x_{e}[3]W_{4}^{3k}\)
\(\begin{bmatrix} \begin{matrix} X_{e}[0] \\ X_{e}[1] \\ X_{e}[2] \\ X_{e}[3] \end{matrix} \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix} \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 1& 1 \\ 1 & W_{4}^{1} & W_{4}^{3} & W_{4}^{3} \\ 1 &W_{4}^{2} & W_{4}^{0} & W_{4}^{2} \\ 1 & W_{4}^{3} &W_{4}^{2} & W_{4}^{1} \end{matrix} \end{bmatrix}.\begin{bmatrix} \begin{matrix} x_{e}[0] \\ x_{e}[1] \\ x_{e}[2] \\ x_{e}[3] \end{matrix} \end{bmatrix} \Rightarrow \)
\(\begin{bmatrix} \begin{matrix} X_{e}[0] \\ X_{e}[1] \\ X_{e}[2] \\ X_{e}[3] \end{matrix} \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix} \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 1& 1 \\ 1 &-j & -1 & j \\ 1 &-1 & 1 & -1 \\ 1 & j &-1 & -j \end{matrix} \end{bmatrix}.\begin{bmatrix} \begin{matrix} 2 \\ 1\\ -1 \\ -2 \end{matrix} \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix} \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 3-3j \\ 2 \\ 3+3j \end{matrix} \end{bmatrix}\)

Likewise, for\( X_{o}(k)\), it is:

\(\begin{bmatrix} \begin{matrix} X_{o}[0] \\ X_{o}[1] \\ X_{o}[2] \\ X_{o}[3] \end{matrix} \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix} \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 1& 1 \\ 1 &-j & -1 & j \\ 1 &-1 & 1 & -1 \\ 1 & j &-1 & -j \end{matrix} \end{bmatrix}.\begin{bmatrix} \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0\\ 1 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix} \begin{matrix} 2 \\ -1+j \\ 0 \\ -1-j \end{matrix} \end{bmatrix} \)
\((3)\Rightarrow X[0]=2 \)
\(X[1]=3-3j+e^{-j\frac{\pi }{4} }(-1+j)=3-j1.586\)
\(X[2]=2+e^{-j\frac{\pi }{2} }.0=2\)
\(X[3]=3+3j+e^{-j\frac{3\pi }{4} }(-1-j)=3+j4.414\)

For the remaining part of the DFT, it is:

\((2)'\Rightarrow X[4]=X_{e}[0]-X_{o}[0]=-2\)
\(X[5]=X_{e}[1]-W_{8}^{1}X_{o}[1]=3-3j+e^{-j\frac{2\pi }{8} }(-1+j)=3-j4.414\)
\(X[6]=X_{e}[2]-W_{8}^{2}X_{o}[2]=2\)
\(X[7]=X_{e}[3]-W_{8}^{3}X_{o}[3]=3+j1.585\)

Note that if we used the second segmentation stage, the FFT equations would be:

\( \begin{matrix} X_{e}[k]=X_{ee}[k]+W_{N}^{2k}X_{eo}[k] \\ X_{e}[k+\frac{N}{4} ]=X_{ee}[k]-W_{N}^{2k}X_{eo}[k] \\ X_{o}[k]=X_{oe}[k]+W_{N}^{2k}X_{oo}[k] \\ X_{o}[k+\frac{N}{4} ]=X_{oe}[k]-W_{N}^{2k}X_{oo}[k] \end{matrix} k=0,1,2,…,\frac{N}{4}-1 \)

The computation of the DFT requires \(8^{2}=64\) complex multiplications, whereas the FFT requires \(\frac{N.v}{2}=\frac{8.3}{2} =12\) complex multiplications
