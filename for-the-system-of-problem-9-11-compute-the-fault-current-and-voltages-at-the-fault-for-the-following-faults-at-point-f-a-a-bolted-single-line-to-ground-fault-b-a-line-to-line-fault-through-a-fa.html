Guest Question.

For the system of , compute the fault current and voltages at the fault for the following faults at point F: (a) a bolted single line-to-ground fault; (b) a line-to-line fault through a fault impedance \(Z_{ F }=j 0.05\) per unit; (c) a double line-to-ground fault from phase B to C to ground, where phase B has a fault impedance \(Z_{ F }=j 0.05\) per unit, phase C also has a fault impedance \(Z_{ F }=j 0.05\) per unit, and the common line-to-ground fault impedance is \(Z_{ G }=j 0.033\) per unit.

Admin
Solution

(a) For a single line-to-ground fault, the sequence networks from the solution of are to be connected in series.

The sequence currents are given by

\(\bar{I}_{0}=\bar{I}_{1}=\bar{I}_{2}=\frac{1}{j(0.26+0.2085+0.14)}=1.65 \angle-90^{\circ} pu\)

The subtransient fault current is

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{I}_{a}=3\left(1.65 \angle-90^{\circ}\right)=4.95 \angle-90^{\circ} pu \\&\bar{I}_{b}=\bar{I}_{c}=0\end{aligned}\)

The sequence voltages are given by Eq. (9.1.1):

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{V}_{1}=1 \angle 0^{\circ}-\bar{I}_{1} \bar{Z}_{1}=1 \angle 0^{\circ}-\left(1.65 \angle-90^{\circ}\right)\left(0.26 \angle 90^{\circ}\right)=0.57 pu \\&\bar{V}_{2}=-\bar{I}_{2} \bar{Z}_{2}=-\left(1.65 \angle-90^{\circ}\right)\left(0.2085 \angle 90^{\circ}\right)=-0.34 pu \\&\bar{V}_{0}=-\bar{I}_{0} \bar{Z}_{0}=-\left(1.65 \angle-90^{\circ}\right)\left(0.14 \angle 90^{\circ}\right)=-0.23 pu\end{aligned}\)

The line-to-ground (phase) voltages at the faulted bus are

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{V}_{a g} \\\bar{V}_{b g} \\\bar{V}_{c g}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a^{2} & a \\1 & a & a^{2}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}-0.23 \\0.57 \\-0.34\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}0 \\0.86 \angle-113.64^{\circ} \\0.86 \angle 113.64^{\circ}\end{array}\right] pu\)

 

(b) For a line-to-line fault through a fault impedance \(\bar{Z}_{F}=j 0.05\), the sequence network connection is shown below:

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{I}_{1}=-\bar{I}_{2}=\frac{1 \angle 0^{\circ}}{0.5185 \angle 90^{\circ}}=1.93 \angle-90^{\circ} pu \\&\bar{I}_{0}=0\end{aligned}\)

The phase currents are given by (Eq. 8.1.20 ∼ 8.1.22)

\(\bar{I}_{a}=0 ; \bar{I}_{b}=-\bar{I}_{c}=\left(a^{2}-a\right) \bar{I}_{1}=3.34 \angle-180^{\circ} pu\)

The sequence voltages are

\(\begin{gathered}\bar{V}_{1}=1 \angle 0^{\circ}-\bar{I}_{1} \bar{Z}_{1}=1 \angle 0^{\circ}-\left(1.93 \angle-90^{\circ}\right)\left(0.26 \angle 90^{\circ}\right) \\=0.5 pu\end{gathered}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{V}_{2}=-\bar{I}_{2} \bar{Z}_{2} &=-\left(-1.93 \angle-90^{\circ}\right)\left(0.2085 \angle 90^{\circ}\right) \\&=0.4 pu\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\bar{V}_{0}=-\bar{I}_{0} \bar{Z}_{0}=0\)

The phase voltages are then given by

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{V}_{a}=\bar{V}_{1}+\bar{V}_{2}+\bar{V}_{0}=0.9 pu \\&\bar{V}_{b}=a^{2} \bar{V}_{1}+a \bar{V}_{2}+\bar{V}_{0}=0.46 \angle-169.11^{\circ} pu \\&\bar{V}_{c}=a \bar{V}_{1}+a^{2} \bar{V}_{2}+\bar{V}_{0}=0.46 \angle 169.11^{\circ} pu\end{aligned}\)

Check: \(\bar{V}_{b}-\bar{V}_{c}=\bar{I}_{b} \bar{Z}_{F}=0.17 \angle-90^{\circ}\)

 

(c) For a double line-to-ground fault with given conditions, the sequence network connection is shown below:

The reductions are shown below:

\(\begin{aligned}&\therefore \bar{I}_{1}=1 \angle 0^{\circ} / 0.45 \angle 90^{\circ}=2.24 \angle-90^{\circ} \\&\bar{I}_{2}=-\bar{I}_{1}\left(\frac{0.29}{0.29+0.2585}\right)=-1.18 \angle-90^{\circ} \\&\bar{I}_{0}=-1.06 \angle-90^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

The sequence voltages are given by

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{V}_{1}=1 \angle 0^{\circ}-\bar{I}_{1} \bar{z}_{1}=1 \angle 0^{\circ}-\left(2.24 \angle-90^{\circ}\right)\left(0.26 \angle 90^{\circ}\right)=0.42 \\&\bar{V}_{2}=-\bar{I}_{2} \bar{Z}_{2}=-\left(-1.18 \angle-90^{\circ}\right)\left(0.2085 \angle 90^{\circ}\right)=0.25 \\&\bar{V}_{0}=-\bar{I}_{0} \bar{Z}_{0}=-\left(-1.06 \angle-90^{\circ}\right)\left(0.14 \angle 90^{\circ}\right)=0.15\end{aligned}\)

The phase currents are calculated as

\(\begin{gathered}\bar{I}_{a}=0 ; \bar{I}_{b}=a^{2} \bar{I}_{1}+a \bar{I}_{2}+\bar{I}_{0}=3.36 \angle 151.77^{\circ}; \\\bar{I}_{c}=a \bar{I}_{1}+a^{2} \bar{I}_{2}+\bar{I}_{0}=3.36 \angle 28.23^{\circ}.\end{gathered}\)

The neutral fault current is \(\bar{I}_{b}+\bar{I}_{c}=3 \bar{I}_{0}=-3.18 \angle-90^{\circ}\).

The phase voltages are obtained as

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{V}_{a}=\bar{V}_{1}+\bar{V}_{2}+\bar{V}_{0}=0.82 \\&\bar{V}_{b}=a^{2} V_{1}+a \bar{V}_{2}+\bar{V}_{0}=0.24 \angle-141.49^{\circ} \\&\bar{V}_{c}=a \bar{V}_{1}+a^{2} \bar{V}_{2}+\bar{V}_{0}=0.24 \angle 141.49^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)
