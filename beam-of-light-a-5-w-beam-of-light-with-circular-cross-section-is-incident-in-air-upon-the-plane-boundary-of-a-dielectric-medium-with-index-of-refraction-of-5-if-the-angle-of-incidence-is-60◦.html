Guest Question.

Beam of Light

A 5 W beam of light with circular cross section is incident in air upon the plane boundary of a dielectric medium with index of refraction of 5. If the angle of incidence is \(60^◦\) and the incident wave is parallel polarized, determine the transmission angle and the powers contained in the reflected and transmitted beams.

Admin
Solution

From Eq. (8.56)

\( \frac{sinθ_t}{sinθ_i} = \frac{k_1}{k_2} = \frac{w \sqrt{μ_1 ϵ_1}}{w \sqrt{μ_2 ϵ_2}} = \frac{n_1}{n_2}. \\ \text{(Snell’s law of refraction)} \) (8.56)

\(sin θ_t=\frac{n_1}{n_2} sin θ_i=\frac{1}{5} sin 60^◦ = 0.17 \)

or

\(θ_t = 10^◦\)

With \(\epsilon_2/\epsilon_1 = n^2 _{2}/n^2_{1} = (5)^2 = 25\), the reflection coefficient for parallel polarization follows from Eq. (8.68) as

\(\Gamma _{\parallel }=\frac{−(\epsilon_2/\epsilon_1) cos θ_i + \sqrt{ (\epsilon_2/\epsilon_1) − sin^2 θ_i}}{(\epsilon_2/\epsilon_1) cos θ_i + \sqrt{ (\epsilon_2/\epsilon_1) − sin^2 θ_i}} \)

 

\(=\frac{−25 cos 60^◦ + \sqrt{ 25 − sin^2 60^◦}}{25 cos 60^◦ + \sqrt{ 25 − sin^2 60^◦}} = −0.435\)

 

The reflected and transmitted powers therefore are

 

\(P^r_{\parallel }=P^i_{\parallel }\left|\Gamma _{\parallel }\right|^2 = 5(0.435)^2 = 0.95 W,\)

 

\(P^t_{\parallel }=P^i_{\parallel } – P^r_{\parallel }= 5 − 0.95 = 4.05 W.\)
