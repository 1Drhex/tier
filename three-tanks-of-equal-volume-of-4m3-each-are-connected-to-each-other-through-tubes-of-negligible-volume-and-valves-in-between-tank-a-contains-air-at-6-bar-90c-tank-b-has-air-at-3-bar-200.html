Guest Question.

Three tanks of equal volume of 4\(\textbf{m}^{3}\) each are connected to each other through tubes of negligible volume and valves in between. Tank A contains air at 6 bar, 90°C, tank B has air at 3 bar, 200°C and tank C contains nitrogen at 12 bar, 50°C. Considering adiabatic mixing determine (i) the entropy change when valve between tank A and B is opened until equilibrium, (ii) the entropy change when valves between tank C; tank A and tank B are opened until equilibrium. Consider \(\textbf{R}_{Air}\) = 0.287 kJ/kg.K, \(\textbf{g}_{Air}\) = 1.4, \(\textbf{R}_{Nitrogen}\) = 0.297 kJ/kg. K and \(\textbf{g}_{Nitrogen}\) = 1.4.

Admin
Solution

Let states in tanks A, B C be denoted by subscripts A, B C respectively.

(i)When tank A and B are connected;

 

\(\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad C_{\text{v,Air}} = \frac{R_{Air}}{( r_{Air} – 1)} = 0.718 kJ/kg.K\)

After adiabatic mixing let the states be denoted by subscript ‘D’.

Internal energy before mixing = Internal energy after mixing

 

\(\begin{aligned}m_{A} . C_{v_{Air}} . T_{A} + m_{B} . C_{v_{, Air}} . T_{B}&= (m_{A} + m_{B}) . C_{V_{, Air}} . T_{D}\\⇒ \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad T_{D}&= \frac{(m_{A}.T_{A} + m_{B}.T_{B})}{(m_{A} + m_{B})}\end{aligned}\)

Using gas laws,

 

\(\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad m_{A}&= \frac{p_{A}.V_{A}}{R_{Air}T_{A}} = \frac{6 × 10^{2} × 4}{0.287 × 363} = 23.04 kg\\m_{B}&= \frac{p_{B}.V_{B}}{R_{Air}T_{B}} = \frac{3 × 10^{2} × 4}{0.287 × 573} = 7.29 kg\end{aligned}\)

Final temperature ⇒

 

\(\begin{aligned}T_{D}&= \left\{\frac{(23.04 × 363) + (7.29 × 573)}{(23.04 + 7.29)} \right\} = 413.47 K\end{aligned}\)

Final pressure,

 

\(\begin{aligned} p_{D} = \frac{R_{Air}T_{D}.m_{D}}{V_{D}}&= \frac{R_{Air}T_{D}. (m_{A} + m_{B})}{(V_{A} + V_{B})}\\p_{D}&= \frac{0.287 × 413.47 × (23.04 + 7.29)}{(4 + 4)} = 449.89 kPa\\p_{D}&= 4.4989 bar\end{aligned}\)

Entropy change,

 

\(\begin{aligned}Δ_{ΔS}&= (S_{D} – S_{A}) + (S_{D} – S_{B})\\ΔS&= \left\{(m_{A}. C_{p ,Air} \ln \frac{T_{D}}{T_{A}} – m_{A} .R_{Air} \ln \frac{p_{D}}{p_{A}}) + (m_{B}. C_{p ,Air} \ln \frac{T_{D}}{T_{B}} – m_{B} .R_{Air} \ln \frac{p_{D}}{p_{B}})\right\}\end{aligned}\) \(\begin{aligned}ΔS&= \left\{\left\lgroup23.04× 1.005 \ln \left\lgroup\frac{413.47}{363} \right\rgroup \right\rgroup – \left\lgroup23.04 × 0.287 \ln\left\lgroup \frac{449.89}{600}\right\rgroup \right\rgroup + \left\lgroup7.29 × 1.005 \ln \left\lgroup\frac{413.47}{573} \right\rgroup \right\rgroup – \left\lgroup7.29 × 0.287 \ln \left\lgroup\frac{449.89}{300} \right\rgroup \right\rgroup \right\} \\&= \left\{3.014 + 1.904 + (– 2.391) – 0.848\right\}\end{aligned}\) Entropy chnage, ΔS = 1.679 kJ/K

 

(ii) After the three tanks A, B, and C are interconnected then the equilibrium will be attained amongst three.

Equilibrium between A

B will result in state D as estimated in part (i) above.

Thus it may be considered as the mixing of state D and nitrogen in tank C. Let the final state attained be ‘F’.

After adiabatic mixing the final gas properties (as a result of mixing of air, state D and nitrogen, state C) may be estimated as under

 

\(\begin{aligned}m_{c}&=\frac{p_{c}. V_{c}}{R_{\text{Nitrogen}}. T_{c}} = \frac{12 × 10^{2} × 4}{0.297 × 323}\\m_{c}&= 50.04 kg ; m_{D} = m_{A} + m_{B} = 30.33 kg\end{aligned}\) \(\begin{aligned}C_{\text{v, Nitrogen}}&= \frac{R_{\text{Nitrogen}}}{γ_{\text{Nitrogen}} – 1} = \frac{0.297}{(1.4 – 1)} = 0.7425 kJ/kgK\\C_{\text{p, Nitrogen}}&= γ_{\text{Nitrogen}} .C_{\text{v, Nitrogen}} = 1.4 × 0.7425\\&= 1.0395 kJ/kg.K\\m_{F}&= (m_{D} + m_{c}) = (23.04 + 7.29 + 50.04) = 80.37 kg\\C_{v,F}&= \left\{(m_{D}.C_{\text{v, Air}} + m_{c}.C_{\text{v, Nitrogen}})/(m_{D} + m_{C})\right\}\end{aligned}\) \(\begin{aligned}&= 0.733 kJ/kg.K\\R_{F}&= \frac{m_{D}.R_{Air} + m_{c}.R_{\text{Nitrogen}}}{(m_{D} + m_{C})}\\&= \left\{\frac{30.33 × 0.287 + 50.04 × 0.297}{80.37} \right\} \\R_{F}&= 0.293 kJ/kg.K\end{aligned}\)

By first law of thermodynamics,

 

\(\begin{aligned}& \\ΔQ&= ΔW + ΔU; \quad \text{here ΔQ = 0, ΔW = 0}\end{aligned}\)

Internal energy before mixing

Internal energy after mixing

 

\(\begin{aligned}m_{D}.C_{\text{v,Air}}.T_{D} + m_{c}.C_{\text{v,Nitrogen}} .T_{c}&= (m_{D} + m_{C}) C_{v_{F}}. T_{F}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}⇒ (30.33 × 0.718 × 413.47)+ (50.04 × 0.7425 × 323) = 80.37 × 0.733 × T_{F}\\⇒ T_{F} =\end{aligned}\)

356.55 K; Final temperature after mixing = 356.55 K

Final pressure after mixing;

 

\(\begin{aligned}p_{F}&= \frac{m_{F}. R_{F}. T_{F}}{V_{F}} = \frac{(80.37 × 0.293 × 356.55)}{(4 + 4 + 4)}\\p_{F}\end{aligned}\) = 699.68 kPa; Final pressure. Entropy change after mixing, \(\begin{aligned} ΔS &= (S_{F} – S_{D}) + (S_{F} – S_{C})\end{aligned}\\ΔS = m_{D} \left\{C_{\text{p, Air}}\ln \frac{T_{F}}{T_{D}} – R_{Air}\ln \frac{p_{F}}{p_{D}} \right\} + m_{C} \left\{C_{\text{p, Nitrogen}}\ln \frac{T_{F}}{T_{C}} – R_{Nitrogen}\ln \frac{p_{F}}{p_{C}} \right\}\) \(= 30.33\left\{1.005 \ln \frac{356.55}{413.47} – 0.287 \ln \frac{699.68}{449.89} \right\} + 50.04\left\{1.0395\ln \frac{356.55}{323} – 0.297 \ln \frac{699.68}{1200} \right\}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad = – 8.359 + 13.158\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad ΔS = \textbf{4.799 kJ/kg.K}\)
