Guest Question.

The sun deck is to be framed using Hem-Fir No. 2 grade timber. Joists are spaced at two feet o.c. with a span length of 10 feet. One end of the joist is supported by a concrete foundation wall and the other end by a beam. The supporting beam is actually made of two beams, spliced at the center of the span. Joists and beams are to be considered as simply supported.

Loads:

2″ plank deck = 5 psf

LL = 60 psf

For Joists:

\(Δ_{\text {total }} \) < L/240

\(F_{ b } \)= 1,150 psi

\(F_{ v }\)=75 psi

E = 1.4 × \(10^{6} \) psi

For Beams:

\(Δ_{\text {total }} \) < L/240

\(F_{ b } \) = 1,000 psi

\(F_{ v } \)=75 psi

E = 1.4 × \(10^{6} \) psi

Admin
Solution

Joist Design

DL—2″ deck =5 psf

LL = 60 psf

\(\space\) \(_____\)

Total DL+ LL = 65 psf

\(ω_{ DL + LL }\) = 65 psf × 2’= 130 #/ft.

\(V_{\max }=\frac{ω L}{2}=\frac{130 \# / ft.\left(10^{\prime}\right)}{2}=650 \#\)

 

\(M_{\max }=\frac{ω L^{2}}{8}=\frac{130 \# / ft.\left(10^{\prime}\right)^{2}}{8}=1,625 \# \text { -ft. }\)

 

\(A_{\text {required }}=\frac{1.5 V }{F_{ v }}=\frac{1.5(650 \#)}{75 \# / in .^{2}}=13 in .{ }^{2}\)

 

\(S_{\text {required }}=\frac{M}{F_{ b }}=\frac{1,625 \# \text { -ft. } \times 12 in . / ft. }{1,150 psi }=17 in .{ }^{3}\)

Try 2 × 10.

\(\left(A=13.88 in. ^{2} ; S_{x}=21.4 in.^{3} ; I_{x}=98.9 in.^{4} ; \omega_{\text {beam }}=3.5 \# / ft .\right)\)

Note: Hem-Fir has a density of 30 pcf; hence, the conversion factor is \(ω_{\text {beam }}\) = 0.22 × cross-sectional area of the beam.

Bending and Shear

\(V_{\text {add. }}=\frac{3.5 \# / \text { ft. }\left(10^{\prime}\right)}{2}=17.5 \#\)

 

\(A_{\text {add. }}=\frac{1.5\left(17.5 in.^{3}\right)}{75 \text { psi }}=0.35 in.^{2}\)

 

\(A_{\text {total }}=13.4 \text { in. }^{2} \therefore O K\)

 

\(M_{\text {add. }}=\frac{3.5 \# \text { -ft. }\left(10^{\prime}\right)^{2}}{8}=43.75 \# \text { -ft. }\)

 

\(S_{\text {add. }}=\frac{43.75 \#- ft . \times 12 \text { in./ft. }}{1,150 \text { psi }}=0.5 in.^{3} \)

 

\(S_{\text {total }}=17.5 in .^{3} \therefore OK\)

Deflection

\(\Delta_{\text {allow }_{(D L+L L)}}=\frac{L}{240}=\frac{10^{\prime} \times 12 \text { in. } / \text { ft }}{240}=0.5^{\prime \prime}\)

 

\(\Delta_{\text {actual }_{( DL + LL )}}=\frac{5 \omega L^{4}}{384 E I}=\frac{5(130+3.5)\left(10^{\prime}\right)^{4}(1,728)}{384\left(1.4 \times 10^{6}\right)(98.9)}\)

 

\(\Delta_{\text {actual }_{( DL + LL )}}=0.22^{\prime \prime}<0.5^{\prime \prime} \therefore OK\)

Use 2 × 10 joists at two feet o.c. (equivalent to 2 psf)

Beam Design

Because joists are spaced uniformly and occur at a relatively close spacing, assume loads to be uniformly distributed on the beam.

Loads:

2″ plank deck = 5 psf

2 × 10 @ 2′ o.c. = 2 psf

LL = 60 psf

ω = 67 psf × 5′ = 335 #/ft.

\(V_{\max }=\frac{ω L}{2}=\frac{335 \# / ft \cdot\left(8^{\prime}\right)}{2}=1,340 \#\)

 

\(M_{\max }=\frac{ω L^{2}}{8}=\frac{335 \# / ft .\left(8^{\prime}\right)^{2}}{8}=2,680 \#- ft\)

 

\(A_{\text {required }}=\frac{1.5 V }{F_{ v }}=\frac{1.5(1,340 \#)}{75 psi }=26.8 in .^{2}\)

 

\(S_{\text {required }}=\frac{M}{F_{ b }}=\frac{2,680 \#- ft . \times 12 in \cdot / ft .}{1,000 psi }=32.2 in .^{3}\)

 

\(\text { Try } 4 \times 10 .\left(A=32.38 in .^{2} ; S_{x}=49.91 in .{ }^{3} ; I_{x}=230.84 in .^{4} ; \omega_{\text {beam }}=8 \# / ft .\right)\)

 

\(A_{\text {add. }} \text { and } S_{\text {add. }}\)should not be critical here.

\(\Delta_{\text {allow }}=\frac{L}{240}=\frac{8^{\prime} \times 12 \text { in. } / ft .}{240}=0.4^{\prime \prime}\)

 

\(\Delta_{\text {actual }}=\frac{5 \omega L^{4}}{384 E I}=\frac{5(335 \# / ft .)\left(8^{\prime}\right)^{4}(1,728)}{384\left(1.4 \times 10^{6}\right)\left(231 in .^{4}\right)}\)

 

\(\Delta_{\text {actual }}=0.1^{\prime \prime}<0.4^{\prime \prime} \therefore OK\)

 

Use 4 × 10 S4S beam.
