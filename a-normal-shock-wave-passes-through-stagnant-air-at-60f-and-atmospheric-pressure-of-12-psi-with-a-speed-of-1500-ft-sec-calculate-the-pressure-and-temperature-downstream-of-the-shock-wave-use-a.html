Guest Question.

A normal shock wave passes through stagnant air at 60°F and atmospheric pressure of 12 psi with a speed of 1500 ft/sec. Calculate the pressure and temperature downstream of the shock wave. Use (a) the equations and (b) the gas tables.

Admin
Solution

We consider the shock wave to be stationary with \(V_{1}=1500 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{sec}\) and \(p_{1}=12\) psia.(a) To use the simplified equations (9.4.14) we must know the upstream Mach number. It is

\(\begin{aligned} \mathrm{M}_{2}^{2} &=\frac{\mathrm{M}_{1}^{2}+5}{7 \mathrm{M}_{1}^{2}-1} \\ \frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}} &=\frac{7 \mathrm{M}_{1}^{2}-1}{6} \\ \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}} &=\frac{\left(\mathrm{M}_{1}^{2}+5\right)\left(7 \mathrm{M}_{1}^{2}-1\right)}{36 \mathrm{M}_{1}^{2}} \end{aligned} (9.4.14)\)

\(\begin{aligned}\mathrm{M}_{1} &=\frac{V_{1}}{c_{1}}=\frac{V_{1}}{\sqrt{k R T_{1}}} \\&=\frac{1500 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{sec}}{\sqrt{1.4 \times 1716 \mathrm{ft}-\mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{slug}-{ }^{\circ} \mathrm{R} \times 520^{\circ} \mathrm{R}}}=1.342\end{aligned}\)

The pressure and temperature are then found to be

\(\begin{aligned}p_{2} &=\frac{p_{1}\left(7 \mathrm{M}_{1}^{2}-1\right)}{6} \\&=\frac{12\left(7 \times 1.342^{2}-1\right)}{6}=23.21 \mathrm{psia} \\T_{2} &=\frac{T_{1}\left(\mathrm{M}_{1}^{2}+5\right)\left(7 \mathrm{M}_{1}^{2}-1\right)}{36 \mathrm{M}_{1}^{2}} \\&=\frac{520\left(1.342^{2}+5\right)\left(7 \times 1.342^{2}-1\right)}{36 \times 1.342^{2}}=633.1^{\circ} \mathrm{R}\end{aligned}\)

(b) From part (a), we use \(\mathrm{M}_{1}=1.342 .\) Interpolation in Table D.2 yields

\(\begin{array}{l}\frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}=\frac{1.342-1.34}{1.36-1.34}(1.991-1.928)+1.928=1.934 \\\\\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}=\frac{1.342-1.34}{1.36-1.34}(1.229-1.216)+1.216=1.217\end{array}\)

Using the information given, we have

\(\begin{array}{l}p_{2}=12 \times 1.934=23.21 \mathrm{psia} \\T_{2}=520 \times 1.217=632.8^{\circ} \mathrm{R}\end{array}\)
