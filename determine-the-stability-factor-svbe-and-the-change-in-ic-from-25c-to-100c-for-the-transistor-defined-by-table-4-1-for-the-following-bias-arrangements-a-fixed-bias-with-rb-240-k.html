Guest Question.

Determine the stability factor \(S(V_{BE})\)and the change in \(I_{C}\) from 25°C to 100°C for the transistor defined by Table 4.1 for the following bias arrangements.
(a) Fixed-bias with \(R_{B} = 240 k \Omega\) , and \(\beta = 100\).
(b) Emitter-bias with\(R_{B} = 240 k \Omega , RE = 1 k \Omega\), and \( \beta = 100\).
(c) Emitter-bias with \(R_{B} = 47 k \Omega , R_{E} = 4.7 k \Omega \), and \( \beta = 100\).

TABLE 4.1 Variation of silicon Transistor
Parameters with Temperature
\(T(°C)\) \(I_{CO}(n A)\) \(\beta\) \(V_{BE}(V)\)
-65 0.2×\(10^{-3}\) 20 0.85
25 0.1 50 0.65
100 20 80 0.48
175 3.3 × 10³ 120 0.3
Admin
Solution

(a) Eq. (4.65): \(S(V_{BE}) = – \frac{\beta}{R_{B}} = – \frac{100}{240 k \Omega} = – 0.417 × 10^{-3}\)

and \(\Delta I_{C} = \left[S(V_{BE})\right](\Delta V_{BE}) = ( – 0.417 × 10^{-3}) (0.48 V – 0.65 V) = ( – 0.417 × 10^{-3})( – 0.17 V) = 70.9 \mu A \)

(b) In this case, \((\beta + 1) = 101 \) and \( R_{B}/R_{E} = 240 \). The condition \((\beta + 1) \gg R_{B}/R_{E} \) is not satisfied, negating the use of Eq (4.67) and requiring the use of Eq. (4.64).

Eq. (4.64): \(S(V_{BE}) = \frac { – \beta}{R_{B} + (\beta + 1)R_{E}} =\frac{ – 100}{240 k \Omega + (101) 1 k \Omega} = – \frac{100}{341 k \Omega} = – 0.293 × 10^{-3}\)

which is about 30% less than the fixed-bias value due to the additional \((\beta + 1) R_{E}\) term in the denominator of the \(S(V_{BE})\) equation.

\(\Delta I_{C} = \left[S(V_{BE})\right](\Delta V_{BE}) = ( – 0.293 × 10^{-3}) ( – 0.17 V) \cong 50 \mu A\)

(c) In this case,

\((\beta + 1) = 101 \gg \frac{R_{B}}{R_{E}} = \frac{47 k \Omega}{4.7 k \Omega} = 10 \) (satisfied)

Eq. (4.67): \(S(V_{BE}) = – \frac{1}{R_{E}} = – \frac{1}{4.7 k \Omega} = – 0.212 × 10^{-3} \)

and \(\Delta I_{C} = \left[S(V_{BE})\right](\Delta V_{BE})= ( – 0.212 × 10^{-3})( – 0.17 V) = 36.04 \mu A\)
