Guest Question.

A thin-walled metal tank containing fluid at 40°C cools in air at 14°C; \(\overline{h}\) is very large inside the tank. If the sides are 0.4 m high, compute \(\overline{h}, \overline{q}\) , and δ at the top. Are the b.l. assumptions reasonable?

Admin
Solution

\(\beta _{air}=1/T_{\infty }=1/(273+14)=0.00348 K^{-1}\). Then

\(Ra_{L}=\frac{g\beta \Delta TL^3}{v\alpha }=\frac{9.8(0.00348)(40-14)(0.4)^3}{(1.566\times 10^{-5})(2.203\times 10^{-5})}=1.645\times 10^8\)

and Pr = 0.711, where the properties are evaluated at 300 K = 27°C. Then, from eqn. (8.26),

\(\overline{Nu_{L}} =0.678 Ra^{1/4}_{L} (\frac{Pr}{0.952+Pr} )^{1/4}\)

 

\(\overline{Nu_{L}} =0.678 (1.645\times 10^8)^{1/4} (\frac{0.711}{0.952+0.711} )^{1/4}=62.1\)

so

\(\overline{h}=\frac{62.1 k}{L}=\frac{62.1(0.02614)}{0.4}=4.06 W/m^2 K\)

and

\(\overline{q}=\overline{h}\Delta T=4.06(40-14)=105.5 W/m^2\)

The b.l. thickness at the top of the tank is given by eqn. (8.23)

\(\frac{\delta }{x}=3.936(\frac{0.952+Pr}{Pr^2} )^{1/4}\frac{1}{Gr^{1/4}_{x} }\)

 

at x=L:

 

\(\frac{\delta }{L}=3.936(\frac{0.952+0.711}{0.711^2} )^{1/4}\frac{1}{(Ra_{L}/Pr)^{1/4} } =0.0430\)

 

Thus , the b.l. thickness at the end of the plate is only 4% of the height, or 1.72 cm thick. This is thicker than typical forced convection b.l.’s, but it is still reasonably thin.
