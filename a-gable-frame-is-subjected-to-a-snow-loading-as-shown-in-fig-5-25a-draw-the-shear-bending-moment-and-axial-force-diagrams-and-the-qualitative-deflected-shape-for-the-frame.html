Guest Question.

A gable frame is subjected to a snow loading, as shown in Fig. \( 5.25 (\mathrm{a})\).Draw the shear, bending moment, and axial force diagrams and the qualitative deflected shape for the frame.

Admin
Solution

Static Determinacy. \(m=4, j=5, r=4\), and \(e_{c}=1\). Because \(3 m+r=3 j+e_{c}\) and the frame is geometrically stable, it is statically determinate.

Reactions. (See Fig. 5.25(b).)

\( \begin{aligned} +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{E} &=0 & & \\ -A_{Y}(8)+12(8)(4) &=0 & A_{Y}=48 \mathrm{kN} \uparrow \\ +\uparrow \sum F_{Y} &=0 & \\ 48-12(8)+E_{Y} &=0 & E_{Y}=48 \mathrm{kN} \uparrow \\ +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{C}^{A C} &=0 & \\ A_{X}(8)-48(4)+12(4)(2) &=0 & A_{X}=12 \mathrm{kN} \rightarrow \\ +\rightarrow \sum F_{X} &=0 & \\ 12+E_{X} &=0 & \\ E_{X} &=-12 \mathrm{kN} & E_{X}=12 \mathrm{kN} & \end{aligned} \)

Member End Forces. (See Fig. 5.25(c).)

Joint \(\boldsymbol{A}\). By applying the equations of equilibrium \(\sum F_{X}=0\) and \(\sum F_{Y}=0\), we obtain

\( A_{X}^{A B}=12 \mathrm{kN} \quad A_{Y}^{A B}=48 \mathrm{kN} \)

Member \(\boldsymbol{A B}\). Considering the equilibrium of member \(A B\), we obtain

\( B_{X}^{A B}=-12 \mathrm{kN} \quad B_{Y}^{A B}=-48 \mathrm{kN} \quad M_{B}^{A B}=-60 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \)

Joint \(\boldsymbol{B}\). Applying the three equilibrium equations, we obtain

\( B_{X}^{B C}=12 \mathrm{kN} \quad B_{Y}^{B C}=48 \mathrm{kN} \quad M_{B}^{B C}=60 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \)

Member \(\boldsymbol{B C}\).

\( \begin{aligned} +\rightarrow \sum F_{X} &=0 \quad C_{X}^{B C}=-12 \mathrm{kN} \\ +\uparrow \sum F_{Y} &=0 \\ 48-12(4)+C_{Y}^{B C} &=0 \quad C_{Y}^{B C}=0 \\ +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{B} &=0 \\ 60-12(4)(2)+12(3) &=0 \end{aligned} \)

Joint \(\boldsymbol{C}\). Considering the equilibrium of joint \(C\), we determine

\( C_{X}^{C D}=12 \mathrm{kN} \quad C_{Y}^{C D}=0 \)

Member \(\boldsymbol{C D}\).

\( \begin{aligned} +\rightarrow \sum F_{X} &=0 \quad D_{X}^{C D}=-12 \mathrm{kN} \\ +\uparrow \sum F_{Y} &=0 \\ -12(4)+D_{Y}^{C D} &=0 \quad D_{Y}^{C D}=48 \mathrm{kN} \\ +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{D} &=0 \\ -12(3)+12(4)(2)+M_{D}^{C D} &=0 \quad M_{D}^{C D}=-60 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \end{aligned} \)

Joint \(\boldsymbol{D}\). Applying the three equilibrium equations, we obtain

\( D_{X}^{D E}=12 \mathrm{kN} \quad D_{Y}^{D E}=-48 \mathrm{kN} \quad M_{D}^{D E}=60 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \)

Member \(\boldsymbol{D E}\).

\( \begin{aligned} +\rightarrow \sum F_{X} &=0 & & E_{X}^{D E}=-12 \mathrm{kN} \\ +\uparrow \sum F_{Y} &=0 & & E_{Y}^{D E}=48 \mathrm{kN} \\ +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{E} &=0 & & \\ 60-12(5) &=0 & & \end{aligned} \)

Joint \(\boldsymbol{E}\).

\( \begin{array}{rr} +\rightarrow \sum F_{X}=0 & -12+12=0 \\ +\uparrow \sum F_{Y}=0 & 48-48=0 \end{array} \)

Distributed Loads on Inclined Members \(\boldsymbol{B C}\) and \(\boldsymbol{C D} \). As the \(12 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}\) snow loading is specified per horizontal meter, it is necessary to resolve it into components parallel and perpendicular to the directions of members \(B C\) and \(C D .\) Consider, for example, member \(B C\), as shown in Fig. \(5.25\) (d). The total vertical load acting on this member is \((12 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m})(4 \mathrm{~m})=48 \mathrm{kN}\). Dividing this total vertical load by the length of the member, we obtain the intensity of the vertical distributed load per meter along the inclined length of the member as \(48 / 5=9.6 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m} .\) The components of this vertical distributed load in the directions parallel and perpendicular to the axis of the member are \((3 / 5)(9.6)=5.76 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}\) and \((4 / 5)(9.6)=7.68 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}\), respectively, as shown in Fig. \(5.25 (\mathrm{~d}) .\) The distributed loading for member \(C D\) is computed similarly and is shown in Fig. \(5.25 (\mathrm{e})\).

Shear and Bending Moment Diagrams. See Fig. 5.25(f) and (g).

Axial Force Diagrams. The equations for axial force in the members of the frame are:

\(\begin{array}{ll}\text { Member } A B & Q=-48 \\ \text { Member BC } & Q=-38.4+5.76 x \\ \text { Member CD } & Q=-9.6-5.76 x \\ \text { Member DE } & Q=-48\end{array}\)

The axial force diagrams are shown in Fig. 5.25(h).

Qualitative Deflected Shape. See Fig. 5.25(i).
