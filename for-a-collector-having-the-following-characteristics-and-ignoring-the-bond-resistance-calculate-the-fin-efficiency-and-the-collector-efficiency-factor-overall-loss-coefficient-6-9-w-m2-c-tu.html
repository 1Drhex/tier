Guest Question.

For a collector having the following characteristics and ignoring the bond resistance, calculate the fin efficiency and the collector efficiency factor:
Overall loss coefficient =6.9 W/\(m^2\)-°C.
Tube spacing = 120 mm.
Tube outside diameter = 15 mm.
Tube inside diameter = 13.5 mm.
Plate thickness = 0.4 mm.
Plate material = copper.
Heat transfer coefficient inside the tubes = 320 W/\(m^2\)-°C.

Admin
Solution

From Appendix 5, Table A5.3,

for copper, k = 385 W/m-°C.

From Eq. (3.34),

\(m=\sqrt{\frac{U_{L}}{k\delta } } =\sqrt{\frac{6.9}{385 \times 0.0004} }=6.69 m^{-1}\)

From Eq. (3.42),

\(F=\frac{\tanh[m(W-D)/2]}{m(W-D)/2} =\frac{\tanh[6.69(012-0.015)/2]}{6.69[(0.12-0.015)/2]} =0.961\)

Finally, from Eq. (3.48) and ignoring bond conductance,

\(\acute{F} =\frac{\frac{1}{U_{L}} }{W[\frac{1}{U_{L}[D+(W-D)F]}+\frac{1}{C_{b}}+\frac{1}{\pi D_{i}h_{fi}} ]}\)

\(=\frac{{1}/{6.9} }{0.12[\frac{1}{6.9[0.015+(0.12-0.015)\times 0.961]}+\frac{1}{\pi \times 0.0135\times 320} ]}=0.912\)
