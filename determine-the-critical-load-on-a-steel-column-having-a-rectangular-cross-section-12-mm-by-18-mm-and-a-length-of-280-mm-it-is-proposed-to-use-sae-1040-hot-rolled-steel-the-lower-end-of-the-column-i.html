Guest Question.

Determine the critical load on a steel column having a rectangular cross section, 12 mm by 18 mm, and a length of 280 mm. It is proposed to use SAE 1040 hot-rolled steel. The lower end of the column is inserted into a close-fitting socket and is welded securely. The upper end is pinned (see Figure 6–8).

Admin
Solution

Objective: Compute the critical load for the column.
Given: Solid rectangular cross section: \(B\) = 12 mm; \(H\) = 18 mm; \(L\)= 280 mm.
Cross sectional area = \(A\) = \(BH\) = (12)(18) = 216 \(mm^2\)
The bottom of column is fixed; the top is pinned (see Figure 6–8).
Material: SAE 1040 hot-rolled steel.

Analysis: Use the procedure in Figure 6–7.
Results:

Step 1. Compute the slenderness ratio. The radius of gyration must be computed about the axis that gives the least value. This is the Y-Y axis, for which

\(r=\frac{B}{\sqrt{12}}=\frac{12 \mathrm{~mm}}{\sqrt{12}}=3.46 \mathrm{~mm}\)
The column has a fixed-pinned end fixity for which \(K=0.8\). Then
\(K L / r=[(0.8)(280)] / 3.46=64.7\)
Step 2. Compute the transition slenderness ratio. For the SAE 1040 hot-rolled steel, \(E=207 \mathrm{GPa}\) and \(s_{y}=290 \mathrm{MPa}\). Then, from Equation (6-8) (\(\frac{s_{y}}{2}=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{S_{r T}^{2}} \Rightarrow C_{c}=S_{r T}=\sqrt{\frac{2 \pi^{2} E}{s_{y}}}\)),
\(C_{c}=\sqrt{\frac{2 \pi^{2}\left(207 \times 10^{9} \mathrm{~Pa}\right)}{290 \times 10^{6} \mathrm{~Pa}}}=119\)

Step 3. Then \(K L/ r<C_{c}\); thus the column is short. Use the J. B. Johnson formula (Eqn. 6-9) (\(P_{c r}=A s_{y}\left[1-\frac{s_{y} S_{r}^{2}}{4 \pi^{2} E}\right]\)) to compute the critical load.

\(\begin{aligned}&P_{c r}=A s_{y}\left[1-\frac{s_{y}(K L/ r)^{2}}{4 \pi^{2} E}\right] \\&P_{c r}=\left(216 \mathrm{~mm}^{2}\right)\left(290 \mathrm{~N} / \mathrm{mm}^{2}\right)\left[1-\frac{\left(290 \times 10^{6} \mathrm{~Pa}\right)(64.7)^{2}}{4 \pi^{2}\left(207\times 10^{9} \mathrm{~Pa}\right)}\right] \\&P_{c r}=53.3 \times 10^{3}\mathrm{~N}=53.3 \mathrm{kN}\end{aligned}\)
Comments: This is the critical buckling load. We would have to apply a design factor to determine the allowable load. Specifying \(N=3\) results in \(P_{a}=17.8 \mathrm{kN}\).
