Measuring \( Z_L \)

A slotted-line probe is an instrument used to measure the unknown impedance of a load, \( Z_L. \) A coaxial slotted line contains a narrow longitudinal slit in the outer conductor. A small probe inserted in the slit can be used to sample the magnitude of the electric field and, hence, the magnitude \( |\tilde{V} (d) |\) of the voltage on the line (Fig. 2-16). By moving the probe along the length of the slotted line, it is possible to measure \( |\tilde{V}|_{max} \text{ and } |\tilde{V}|_{min} \) and the distances from the load at which they occur. Use
of Eq. (2.73), namely \( S = |\tilde{V}|_{max}/|\tilde{V}|_{min}, \) provides the voltage standing-wave ratio S. Measurements with a Z = 50 Ω slotted line terminated in an unknown load impedance determined that S = 3. The distance between successive voltage minima was found to be 30 cm, and the first voltage minimum was located at 12 cm from the load. Determine the load impedance \( Z_L.\)

\( S= \frac{|\tilde{V}|_{max}}{|\tilde{V}|_{min}} = \frac{1+ | \Gamma |}{1- | \Gamma |} \) (dimensionless). (2.73)

Admin
Solution

The following quantities are given:

\( Z_0 = 50 Ω, \quad \quad S = 3, \quad \quad d_{min} = 12 cm. \)

Since the distance between successive voltage minima is λ/2,

\( λ = 2 × 0.3 = 0.6 m, \)

and

\( β = \frac{2π}{λ} = \frac{2π}{0.6} = \frac{10π}{3} \quad \text{(rad/m)}. \)

From Eq. (2.73), solving for\( | \Gamma | \) in terms of S gives

\( S= \frac{|\tilde{V}|_{max}}{|\ِtilde{V}|_{min}} = \frac{1+ | \Gamma |}{1- | \Gamma |} \) (dimensionless). (2.73)

\( |\Gamma| = \frac{S-1}{S+1} = \frac{3-1}{3+1} = 0.5 . \)

Next, we use the condition given by Eq. (2.71) to find \( θ_r: \)

\( |\tilde{V}|_{min} = |V_0^+|\left[1- | \Gamma |\right] , \text{when} (2\beta d_{min} – \theta _r) = (2n +1)\pi \) (2.71)

\( 2βd_{min} − θ_r = π, \quad \quad \quad \text{ for }n = 0 \text{ (first minimum)}, \)

which gives

\( θ_r = 2βd_{min} − π \)

\( = 2 × \frac{10π}{3} × 0.12 − π = −0.2π (rad) = −36^◦. \)

Hence,

\( \Gamma = |\Gamma|e^{j\theta _r} = 0.5 e^{-j36^\circ } = 0.405 – j 0.294. \)

Solving Eq. (2.59) for \( Z_L, \) we have

\( \Gamma = \frac{V_0^-}{V_0^+} = \frac{Z_L – Z_0}{Z_L + Z_0} \\ \quad \quad \quad \quad = \frac{Z_L/Z_0 -1}{Z_L /Z_0 +1} \\ \quad \quad \quad \quad = \frac{z_L -1}{z_L +1} \quad \quad \quad \text{(dimensionless),} \) (2.59)

\( Z_L = Z_0 \left[\frac{1+\Gamma }{1-\Gamma } \right] \\ \quad \quad =50 \left[\frac{1+0.405-j0.294}{1-0.405+j0.294} \right] = (85-j67)\Omega . \)
