Guest Question.

Applying the first law of thermodynamics. Two moles of an ideal gas are carried around the thermodynamic path ABCDA in figure 17.6. Here \(T_D = 150 K, T_{AC} = 300 K, T_B = 600 K\), and \(p_A = 2.00 × 10^4 Pa\), while \(p_D = 1.00 × 10^4 Pa\). The volume \(V_A = 0.250 m^3\), while \(V_B = 0.500 m^3\). Find the work done, the heat lost or absorbed, and the internal energy of the system for the thermodynamic paths (a) AB, (b) BC, (c) CD, (d) DA, and (e) ABCDA.

Admin
Solution

a. The work done by the expanding gas along the path AB is

\(W = pΔV\)

 

\(W_{AB} = p_A(V_B − V_A)\)

 

\(=\left\lgroup2.00\times 10^4\frac{N}{m^2} \right\rgroup (0.500 m^3- 0.250 m^3)\)

 

\(= 5.00 × 10^3 J\)

The heat absorbed by the gas along path AB is

\(Q = nC_pΔT\)

 

\(Q_{AB} = nC_p(T_B − T_{AC})\)

 

\(=(2 moles)\left\lgroup20.8\frac{J}{mole K} \right\rgroup(600 K – 300 K)\)

 

\(= 1.25 × 10^4 J\)

The change in internal energy along path AB, found from the first law equation 17.31, is

\(ΔU = Q − W\) (17.31)

\(ΔU_{AB} = Q_{AB} − W_{AB}\)

 

\(= 1.25 × 10^4 J − 5.00 × 10^3 J\)

 

\(= 7.50 × 10^3 J\)

Thus, there is a gain of internal energy along the path AB.

b. The work done along path BC is

\(W = pΔV\)

 

\(W_{BC} = p(V_B − V_B) = 0\)

= 0

The heat lost along path BC is

\(Q_{BC} = nC_vΔT = nC_v(T_{AC} − T_B)\)

 

\(=(2 moles)\left\lgroup12.5\frac{J}{mole K} \right\rgroup(300 K – 600 K)\)

 

\(= −7.50 × 10^3 J\)

The loss of internal energy in dropping from 600 K at B to 300 K at C is found from the first law as

\(ΔU_{BC} = Q_{BC }− W_{BC}\)

 

\(= −7.50 × 10^3 J − 0\)

 

\(= −7.50 × 10^3 J \)

c. The work done during the compression along the path CD is

\(W_{CD }= pΔV = p_D(V_A − V_B)\)

 

\(=\left\lgroup1.00\times 10^4\frac{N}{m^2} \right\rgroup (0.250 m^3- 0.500 m^3)\)

 

\(= −2.50 × 10^3 J\)

The heat lost along the path CD is

\(Q_{CD }= nC_pΔT = nC_p(T_D − T_{AC})\)

 

\(=(2 moles)\left\lgroup20.8\frac{J}{mole K} \right\rgroup(150 K – 300 K)\)

 

\(= −6.24 × 10^3 J\)

The change in internal energy along the path CD, found from the first law, is

\(ΔU_{CD} = Q_{CD} − W_{CD}\)

 

\(= − 6.24 × 10^3 J − (−2.50 × 10^3 J)\)

 

\(= −3.74 × 10^3 J\)

Note that the internal energy decreased, as expected, since the temperature decreased from 300 K to 150 K.

d. The work done along the path DA is

\(W_{DA }= pΔV = p(V_A − V_A) = 0\)

The heat added along the path DA is

\(Q_{DA} = nC_vΔT = nC_v(T_{AC }− T_D)\)

 

\(=(2 moles)\left\lgroup12.5\frac{J}{mole K} \right\rgroup(300 K – 150 K)\)

 

\(= 3.74 × 10^3 J\)

The change in internal energy along DA is

\(ΔU_{DA }= Q_{DA} − W_{DA}\)

 

\(= 3.74 × 10^3 J\)

e. The net work done throughout the cycle ABCDA is

\(W_{ABCDA }= W_{AB} + W_{BC }+ W_{CD} + W_{DA}\) \(= 5.00 × 10^3 J + 0 − 2.50 × 10^3 J + 0\)

 

\(= 2.50 × 10^3 J\)

The net heat added throughout the cycle ABCDA is

\(Q_{ABCDA} = Q_{AB} + Q_{BC} + Q_{CD }+ Q_{DA}\)

 

\(= 1.25 × 10^4 J − 7.50 × 10^3 J − 6.24 × 10^3 J + 3.74 × 10^3 J\)

 

\(= 2.50 × 10^3 J\)

Note that \(Q_{AB }\)and \(Q_{DA}\) are positive quantities, which means that heat is being added to the system along these two paths. Also note that \(Q_{BC}\) and \(Q_{CD}\) are negative quantities, which means that heat is being taken away from the system along these two paths. In general, Q is always positive when heat is added to the system and negative when heat is removed from the system.
