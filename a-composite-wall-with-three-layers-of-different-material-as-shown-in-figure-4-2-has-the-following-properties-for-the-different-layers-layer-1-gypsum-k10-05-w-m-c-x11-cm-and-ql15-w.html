Guest Question.

A composite wall, with three layers of different material as shown in Figure 4.2, has the following properties for the different layers.

\(\text { Layer-1: Gypsum, } k_{1}=0.05 W / m ^{\circ} C , x_{1}=1 cm \text { and } q_{l}=15 W / m ^{2} .\)

 

\(\text { Layer-2: Fiber-glass, } k_{2}=0.0332 W / m ^{\circ} C \text { and } x_{2}=5 cm .\)

 

\(\text { Layer-3: Concrete, } k_{3}=1.2 W / m ^{\circ} C \text { , } x_{3}=15 cm , h =15 W / m ^{2}{ }^{\circ} C \text { and } T_{a}=25^{\circ} C \text { . }\)

 

Calculate the temperatures \( T_{1}, T_{2}, T_{3}\) and \( T_{4}\) assuming a heat flow area of \( 1 m ^{2} . \)

Admin
Solution

On substituting the given parameter values into Equation (4.23),

\(\left[\begin{array}{cccc}\frac{k_{1} A}{x_{1}} & -\frac{k_{1} A}{x_{1}} & 0 & 0 \\-\frac{k_{1} A}{x_{1}} & \left(\frac{k_{1} A}{x_{1}}+\frac{k_{2} A}{x_{2}}\right) & -\frac{k_{2} A}{x_{2}} & 0 \\0 & -\frac{k_{2} A}{x_{2}} & \left(\frac{k_{2} A}{x_{2}}+\frac{k_{3} A}{x_{3}}\right) & \frac{k_{3} A}{x_{3}} \\0 & 0 & -\frac{k_{3} A}{x_{3}} & \frac{k_{3} A}{x_{3}}+h A\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{c}T_{1} \\T_{2} \\T_{3} \\T_{4}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{c}q_{l} A \\0 \\0 \\h A T_{a}\end{array}\right\} .\)

we obtain

\(\left[\begin{array}{rrcr}5.0 & -5.0 & 0.0 & 0.0 \\-5.0 & 5.66 & -0.66 & 0.0 \\0.0 & -0.66 & 8.66 & -8 \\0.0 & 0.0 & -8.0 & 23.0\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}T_{1} \\T_{2} \\T_{3} \\T_{4}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{c}15 \\0.0 \\0.0 \\375\end{array}\right\}\) (4.27)

The solution of the above simultaneous equations results in \( T_{1}=53.6^{\circ} C , T_{2}=50.60^{\circ} C\), \( T_{3}=27.875^{\circ} C\), and \( T_{4}=26^{\circ} C . \) The balance of heat may be verified by computing the total heat transfer at the right face as \( Q_{r}=A_{r} h\left(T_{4}-T_{a}\right)=15 W . \) This should be equal to the heat supplied at the left side \( Q_{l}=A_{l} q_{l}=15 W . \) Thus, the system satisfies the equilibrium conditions. In the above relationships, \( A_{r}\) and \( A_{l}\) are respectively the surface areas at the right and left face of the composite wall.
