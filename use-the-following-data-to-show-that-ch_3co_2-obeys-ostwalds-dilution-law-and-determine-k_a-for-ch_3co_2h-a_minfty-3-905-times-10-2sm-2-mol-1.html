Guest Question.

Use the following data to show that \(CH_{3}CO_{2}\) obeys Ostwald’s Dilution Law, and determine \(K_{a} for CH_{3}CO_{2}H\).

\((A_{m}^{\infty }=3.905 \times 10^{-2}Sm^{-2} mol^{-1})\)
Admin
Solution
\([CH_{3}CO_{2}H] / mold m^{-3}\) \(A_{m} /Sm^{2} mol^{-1}\)
0.0752 6.021 \(\times 10^{-4}\)
0.0354 8.750 \(\times 10^{-4}\)
0.0180 1.221 \(\times 10^{-4}\)
0.0067 1.987 \(\times 10^{-4}\)

Ostwald’s Dilution Law states:

\(K=\frac{cA_{m}^{2}}{A_{m}^{\infty }(A_{m}^{\infty }-A_{m})}\)

where c and \(A_{m} \)are variables, and K and\( A_{m}^{\infty } \)are constants

Rearrange the equation into the form of a linear relationship, y = mx ‏+ c:

\(cA_{m}^{2}=K(A_{m}^{\infty })^{2}-KA_{m}^{\infty }A_{m}\).

\(cA_{m}=\frac{K(A_{m}^{\infty })^{2}}{A_{m}}- KA_{m}^{\infty }\).

Now plot\( cA_{m} against \frac{1}{A_{m}}\)

The linear plot confirms that \(CH_{3}CO_{2}H\) obeys Ostwald’s Dilution Law over the range of concentrations studied. The acid dissociation constant, Ka, can be found from the gradient of the graph. The equation for the

straight line is:

\(cA_{m}=\frac{K(A_{m}^{\infty })^{2}}{A_{m}}- KA_{m}^{\infty }\)

and therefore:

Gradient=\( K(A_{m}^{\infty })^{2}\).

\(2.77\times 10^{-8}S^{2}m^{4} mol ^{-1}dm^{-3}\).

\((A_{m}^{\infty })^{2}\) can also be found from the graph, but is given in the problem:

\((A_{m}^{\infty })^{2} = 3.905\times 10^{-2}Sm^{2} mol^{-1}\).

\(K_{a}=\frac{(2.77\times 10^{-8}S^{2}m^{4} mol^{-1}dm^{-3})}{(A_{m}^{\infty })^{2} }\)

=\(1.82\times 10^{-5} moldm^{-3}\)
