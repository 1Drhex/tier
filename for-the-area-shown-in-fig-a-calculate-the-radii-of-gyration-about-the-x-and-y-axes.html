Guest Question.

For the area shown in Fig. (a), calculate the radii of gyration about the x- and y-axes.

Admin
Solution

We consider the area to be composed of the three parts shown in Figs. (b)–(d): a triangle, plus a semicircle, minus a circle. The moments of inertia of each part are obtained in two steps. First, the moments of inertia about the centroidal axes of the part are found from Table 9.1. The parallel-axis theorem is then used to calculate the moments of inertia about the x- and y-axes.

Triangle

\(A=\frac{bh}{2}=\frac{90(100)}{2}=4500 mm^{2}\)

 

\(\bar{I}_{x} =\frac{bh^{3}}{36}=\frac{90(100)^{3}}{36}=2.50\times 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

 

\(I_{x}=\bar{I}_{x}+A\bar{y}^{2}=(2.50\times 10^{6})+(4500)(66.7)^{2}=22.52\times 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

 

\(\bar{I}_{y} =\frac{hb^{3}}{36}=\frac{100(90)^{3}}{36}=2.025\times 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

 

\(I_{y}=\bar{I}_{y}+A\bar{x}^{2}=(2.025\times 10^{6})+(4500)(66)^{2}=18.23\times 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

Semicircle

\(A=\frac{\pi R^{2}}{2}=\frac{\pi (45)^{2}}{2}=3181 mm^{2}\)

 

\(\bar{I}_{x} = 0.1098R^{4} = 0.1098(45)^{4} = 0.450 × 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

 

\(I_{x} = \bar{I}_{x} + A \bar{y}^{-2} = (0.450 × 10^{6}) + (3181)(119.1)^{2} = 45.57 × 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

 

\(\bar{I}_{y}=\frac{\pi R^{4}}{8}=\frac{\pi (45)^{4}}{8}=1.61× 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

 

\(I_{y}=\bar{I}_{y}+A\bar{x}^{2}=(1.61\times 10^{6})+(3181)(45)^{2}=8.05\times 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

Circle

\(A = π R^{2} = π(20)^{2} = 1257 mm^{2}\)

 

\(\bar{I}_{x}=\frac{\pi R^{4}}{4}=\frac{\pi (20)^{4}}{4}=0.1257× 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

 

\(I_{x} = \bar{I}_{x} + A \bar{y}^{-2} = (0.1257 × 10^{6}) + (1257)(100)^{2} = 12.70 × 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

 

\(\bar{I}_{y}=\frac{\pi R^{4}}{4}=\frac{\pi (20)^{4}}{4}=0.1257× 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

 

\(I_{y}=\bar{I}_{y}+A\bar{x}^{2}= (0.1257 × 10^{6})+(1257)(45)^{2}=2.67\times 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

Composite Area

To determine the properties for the composite area, we superimpose the foregoing results (taking care to subtract the quantities for the circle) and obtain

\(A = ΣA = 4500 + 3181 − 1257 = 6424 mm^{2}\)

 

\(I_{x} = ΣI_{x} = (22.52 + 45.57 − 12.70) × 10^{6} = 55.39 × 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

 

\(I_{y} = ΣI_{y} = (18.23 + 8.05 − 2.67) × 10^{6} = 23.61 × 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

Therefore, for the radii of gyration we have

\(k_{x}=\sqrt{\frac{I_{x}}{A} }=\sqrt{\frac{55.39\times 10^{6}}{6424} }=92.9 mm\)

 

\(k_{y}=\sqrt{\frac{I_{y}}{A} }=\sqrt{\frac{23.61\times 10^{6}}{6424} }=60.6 mm\)
