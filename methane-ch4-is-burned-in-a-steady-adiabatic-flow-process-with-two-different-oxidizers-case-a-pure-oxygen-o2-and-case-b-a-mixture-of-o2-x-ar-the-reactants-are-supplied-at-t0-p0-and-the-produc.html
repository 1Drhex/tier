Guest Question.

Methane, \(CH _{4}\), is burned in a steady adiabatic flow process with two different oxidizers: Case A: Pure oxygen, \(O _{2}\) and case B: A mixture of \(O _{2}\) + x Ar. The reactants are supplied at \(T_{0}, P_{0}\) and the products for both cases should be at 2100 K. Find the required equivalence ratio in case (A) and the amount of Argon, x, for a stoichiometric ratio in case (B).

Admin
Solution

a) Stoichiometric has ν = 2, actual has:

\(CH _{4}+ vO _{2} \rightarrow CO _{2}+2 H _{2} O +( v -2) O _{2}\)

Energy eq.: \(H _{ R }^{\circ}= H _{ P }^{\circ}+\Delta H _{ P 2100}\)

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta H _{ P 2100} &= H _{ R }^{\circ}- H _{ P }^{\circ}=\overline{ h }_{ f \text { fuel }}^{ o }+0-\overline{ h }_{ f CO _{2}}^{ o }-2 \overline{ h }_{ f H _{2} O}^{ o } -0 \\RHS &=-74873-(-393522)-2(-241826)=802301 kJ / kmol\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\Delta \overline{ h }_{ CO _{2}}=97500, \quad \Delta \overline{ h }_{ H _{2} O }=77970, \Delta \overline{ h }_{ O _{2}}=62973 \text { all in } kJ / kmol\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta H _{ P 2100} &=\Delta \overline{ h }_{ CO _{2}}+2 \Delta \overline{ h }_{ H _{2} O }+( v -2) \Delta \overline{ h }_{ O _{2}} \\&=97500+2 \times 77970+( v -2) \times 62973 \\&=127494+ v 62973=802301 kJ / kmol \text { fuel }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\Rightarrow \quad v = 1 0 . 7 2 , \quad \Phi=\frac{ AF _{ S }}{ AF }=\frac{2}{10.72}= 0 . 1 8 6 6\)

 

b) \(CH _{4}+2 O _{2}+2 x Ar \rightarrow CO _{2}+2 H _{2} O +2 x Ar\)

\(\Delta \overline{ h }_{ Ar }=\overline{ C }_{ P Ar } \Delta T = C _{ P Ar } M _{ Ar } \Delta T\) from table A.5

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta H _{ P 2100} &=97500+2 \times 77970 \\&+2 x \times 0.52 \times 39.948 \times(2100-298)=253440+ x 74866\end{aligned}\)

Now the energy equation becomes

802 301 = 253 440 + x 74 866 ⇒ x = 7.33
