Guest Question.

Consider a stone having a mass of 10 kg and a bucket containing 100 kg of liquid water. Initially the stone is 10.2 m above the water, and the stone and the water are at the same temperature, state 1. The stone then falls into the water.

Determine U, KE, PE, Q, and W for the following changes of state, assuming standard gravitational acceleration of \(9.80665 m / s ^{2}\).

a. The stone is about to enter the water, state 2.
b. The stone has just come to rest in the bucket, state 3.
c. Heat has been transferred to the surroundings in such an amount that the stone and water are at the same temperature, \(T_{1}\), state 4.

Admin
Solution

The energy equation for any of the steps is

\(\Delta U+\Delta KE +\Delta PE =Q-W\)

and each term can be identified for each of the changes of state.

a. The stone has fallen from \(Z_{1}\) to \(Z_{2}\), and we assume no heat transfer as it falls. The water has not changed state; thus

\(\Delta U=0, \quad{ }_{1} Q_{2}=0, \quad{ }_{1} W_{2}=0\)

and the first law reduces to

\(\Delta KE +\Delta PE =0\)

\(\Delta KE =-\Delta PE =-m g\left(Z_{2}-Z_{1}\right)\)

\(=-10 kg \times 9.80665 m / s ^{2} \times(-10.2 m )\)

\(=1000 J =1 kJ\)

That is, for the process from state 1 to state 2,

\(\Delta KE =1 kJ \text { and } \Delta PE =-1 kJ\)

b. For the process from state 2 to state 3 with zero kinetic energy, we have

\(\Delta PE =0, \quad{ }_{2} Q_{3}=0, \quad{ }_{2} W_{3}=0\)

Then

\(\Delta U+\Delta KE =0\)

\(\Delta U=-\Delta KE =1 kJ\)

c. In the final state, there is no kinetic or potential energy, and the internal energy is the same as in state 1.

\(\Delta U=-1 kJ , \quad \Delta KE =0, \quad \Delta PE =0, \quad{ }_{3} W_{4}=0\)

\({ }_{3} Q_{4}=\Delta U=-1 kJ\)
