Finding the Peak Value, the di/dt, and the dv/dt Values of the Snubber Circuit

An RC-snubber circuit, as shown in Figure 17.16c, has \(C = 0.75 µF, R = 6.6 Ω\), and input voltage \(V_s = 220 V\). The circuit inductance is L = 50 μH. Determine (a) the peak forward voltage \(V_p\), (b) the initial \(dv/dt\), and (c) the maximum \(dv/dt\).

Admin
Solution

\(R = 6.6 \Omega , C = 0.75 \mu F, L = 50 \mu H\), and \(V_s = 220 V\). By setting \(I_R = 0\), the forward voltage across the device can be determined from Eq. (17.12),

\(\upsilon (t) = V_s – (V_s – RI_R)\left(\cos\omega t-\frac{\alpha }{\omega }\sin\omega t \right)e^{-\alpha t}+\frac{I_R}{\omega C}e^{-\alpha t}\sin\omega t \) (17.12)

\(\upsilon (t) = V_s – (V_s – RI_R)\left(\cos\omega t-\frac{\alpha }{\omega }\sin\omega t \right)e^{-\alpha t}\) (17.26)

From Eq. (17.17)

\(\frac{dv}{dt}=(V_s – RI_R)\left(2\alpha \cos \omega t+\frac{\omega ^2-\alpha ^2}{\omega }\sin\omega t \right)e^{-\alpha t}+\frac{I_R}{C}\left(\cos\omega t -\frac{\alpha }{\omega }\sin\omega t \right)e^{- \alpha t} \) (17.17)

for \(I_R = 0\),

\(\frac{dv}{dt}=V_s \left(2\alpha \cos \omega t+\frac{\omega ^2-\alpha ^2}{\omega }\sin\omega t \right)e^{-\alpha t}\) (17.27)

The initial dv/dt can be found either from Eq. (17.27) by setting t = 0, or from Eq. (17.19)

\(\left.\frac{dv}{dt}\right| _{t=0}=V_s\omega _0(2\delta – 4d\delta ^2 + d)\) (17.19)

by setting \(I_R = 0\)

\(\frac{dv}{dt}\mid _{t=0}= V_s2\alpha =\frac{V_s R}{L}\) (17.28)

The forward voltage is maximum at \(t = t_1\). The time \(t_1\), which can be obtained either by setting
Eq. (17.27) equal to zero, or by setting \(I_R = 0\) in Eq. (17.22),

\(\tan \omega t_1=\frac{\omega[(V_s – RI_R)2\alpha + I_R/C] }{(V_s – RI_R)(\omega ^2 – \alpha ^2) – \alpha I_R/C}\) (17.22)

is given by

\(\tan \omega t_1=-\frac{2\alpha \omega }{\omega ^2-\alpha ^2} \) (17.29)

\(\cos \omega t_1=-\frac{\omega^2-\alpha ^2 }{\omega ^2+\alpha ^2}\) (17.30)

\(\sin \omega t_1=-\frac{2\alpha \omega }{\omega ^2+\alpha ^2}\) (17.31)

Substituting Eqs. (17.30) and (17.31) into Eq. (17.26), the peak voltage is found as

\(V_p = v(t = t_1) = V_s(1 + e^{-t_1}) \) (17.32)

where

\( \omega t_1=\left(\pi -\tan^{-1}\frac{2\delta \sqrt{1-\delta ^2} }{1-2\delta ^2} \right)\) (17.33)

Differentiating Eq. (17.27) with respect to t, and setting equal to zero, dv/dt is maximum at \( t = t_m\) when

\(-\frac{\alpha (3\omega ^2-\alpha ^2)}{\alpha }\sin \omega t_m+(\omega ^2-3\alpha ^2)\cos \omega t_m = 0 \)

or

\(\tan \omega t_m=\frac{\omega (\omega ^2-3\alpha ^2)}{\alpha (3\omega ^2-\alpha ^2)}\) (17.34)

Substituting the value of tm in Eq. (17.27) and simplifying the sine and cosine terms yield the maximum value of dv/dt,

\(\frac{d_v}{dt}\mid _ {\max}=V_s\sqrt{\omega ^2+\alpha ^2}e^{-\alpha t_m} \) \(\delta \leq 0.5 \) (17.35)

For a maximum to occur, d( dv/dt)/dt must be positive if \(t \leq t_m \); and Eq. (17.34) gives the necessary condition as

\(\omega ^2-3\alpha ^2\geq 0 or \frac{\alpha }{\omega } \leq \frac{1}{\sqrt{3} } or \delta \leq 0.5\)

Equation (17.35) is valid for \(\delta \leq 0.5\) For \(\delta \gt 0.5\), dv/dt becomes maximum when \(t = 0\) is obtained from Eq. (17.27),

\(\frac{d_v}{dt}\mid _{\max}=\frac{d_v}{dt}\mid _{t=0} =V_s2\alpha =\frac{V_s R}{L}\) for \(\delta \gt 0.5 \) (17.36)

a. From Eq. (17.13),

\(\alpha =\frac{R}{2L}\) (17.13)

\(\alpha =6.6/(2\times 50\times 10^{-6})=66,000\) and from Eq. (17.14),

\(\omega _0=\frac{1}{\sqrt{LC} } \) (17.14)

\(\omega _0=\frac{10^6}{\sqrt{50\times 0.75} }=163,299 rad/s\)

From Eq. (17.15),

\(\delta =\frac{\alpha }{\omega _0}=\frac{R}{2}\sqrt{\frac{C}{L} }\) (17.15)

\(\delta =(6.6/2)\sqrt{0.75/50}=0.404\), and from Eq. (17.16),

\(\omega =\sqrt{\omega _0^2-\alpha ^2}=\omega _0\sqrt{1-\delta ^2}\) (17.16)

\(\omega =163,299\sqrt{1-0.404^2}=149,379 rad/s\)

From Eq. (17.33), \(t_1 = 15.46 µs\); therefore, Eq. (17.32) yields the peak voltage, \(V_p = 220(1 + 0.36)= 299.3 V\).

b. Equation (17.28) gives the initial dv/dt of \((220 × 6.6/50) = 29 V/ µs\).

c. Because \(\delta \lt 0.5\), Eq. (17.35) should be used to calculate the maximum dv/dt. From Eq. (17.34), \(t_m = 2.16 µs \) and Eq. (17.35) yields the maximum dv/dt as 31.2 V/ µs.
