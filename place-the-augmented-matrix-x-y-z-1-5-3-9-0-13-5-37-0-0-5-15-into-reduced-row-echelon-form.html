Guest Question.

\(~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~\begin{matrix} _{} & _{~x} & _{~y} & _{~~~z} \end{matrix}\)

\( \\Place ~the ~augmented ~matrix\left(\begin{matrix} _{1} & _{5} & _{- 3} & _{|} & _{- 9} \\ _{0} & _{- 13} & _{5} & _{|} & _{37} \\ _{0} & _{0} & _{5} & _{|} & _{- 15} \end{matrix} \right) into ~reduced ~row ~echelon ~form.\)

Admin
Solution

Why should we want to place this matrix into reduced row echelon form?
In a nutshell, it’s to avoid back substitution. If we look at the bottom row of the given augmented matrix we have \(5 z = – 15\).

We need to divide by 5 in order to find the \(z\) value.

The reduced row echelon form, rref, gives us the values of the unknowns directly, and we do not need to carry out further manipulation or elimination.
What does reduced row echelon form mean in this case?
It means convert the given augmented matrix

\(\begin{matrix} _{R_{1} } \\ _{R_{2} } \\ _{R_{3} } \end{matrix} \left(\begin{matrix} _{1} & _{5} & _{- 3} & _{|} & _{- 9} \\ _{0} & _{- 13} & _{5} & _{|} & _{37} \\ _{0} & _{0} & _{5} & _{|} & _{- 15} \end{matrix} \right)\) into something like \(\left(\begin{matrix} _{1} & _{0} & _{0} & _{|} & _{\ast } \\ _{0} & _{1} & _{0} & _{|} & _{\ast } \\ _{0} & _{0} & _{1} & _{|} & _{\ast } \end{matrix} \right)\)

This means that we need to get 0 in place of the 5 in the second row, and \(0^{\prime }\)s in place of the 5 and −3 in the first row. We also need a 1 in place of −13 in the middle row and 1 in place of the 5 in the bottom row.
How do we convert the 5 in the bottom row into 1?
Divide the last row by 5 (remember, this is the same as multiplying by 1/5):

\(\begin{matrix} _{R_{1} } \\ _{R_{2} } \\ _{R^{\prime }_{3} = R_{3} /5} \end{matrix} \left(\begin{matrix} _{1} & _{5} & _{- 3} & _{|} & _{- 9} \\ _{0} & _{- 13} & _{5} & _{|} & _{37} \\ _{0} & _{0} & _{1} & _{|} & _{- 3} \end{matrix} \right)\)

How do we get 0 in place of −3 in the first row and the 5 in the second row?

We execute the row operations \(R_{1} + 3R^{\prime }_{3}\) and \(R_{2} – 5R^{\prime }_{3}\):

\(\begin{matrix} _{R^{\ast }_{1} = R_{1} + 3R^{\prime }_{3} } \\ _{R^{\ast }_{2} = R_{2} – 5R^{\prime }_{3} } \\ _{R^{~\prime }_{3} } \end{matrix} \left(\begin{matrix} _{1 + 3\left(0\right) } & _{5+ 3\left(0\right) } & _{- 3 + 3\left(1\right) } & _{|} & _{- 9 + 3\left(- 3\right) } \\ _{0- 5\left(0\right) } & _{- 13 – 5\left(0\right) } & _{5 – 5\left(1\right) } & _{|} & _{37 – 5\left(- 3\right) } \\ _{0} & _{0} & _{1} & _{|} & _{- 3} \end{matrix} \right)\)

Simplifying the entries gives

\(\begin{matrix} _{R^{\ast }_{1} } \\ _{R^{\ast }_{2} } \\ _{R^{\prime }_{3} } \end{matrix} \left(\begin{matrix} _{1} & _{5} & _{0} & _{|} & _{- 18} \\ _{0} & _{- 13} & _{0} & _{|} & _{52} \\ _{0} & _{0} & _{1} & _{|} & _{- 3} \end{matrix} \right)\)

How do we get a 1 in place of −13?
Divide the middle row by −13 (remember, this is the same as multiplying by −1/13):

\(\begin{matrix} _{R^{\ast }_{1} } \\ _{R^{\ast \ast }_{2} = R^{\ast }_{2} /\left(- 13\right) } \\ _{R^{\prime }_{3} } \end{matrix} \left(\begin{matrix} _{1} & _{5} & _{0} & _{|} & _{- 18} \\ _{0} & _{- 13 /\left(- 13\right) } & _{0} & _{|} & _{52 /\left(- 13\right) } \\ _{0} & _{0} & _{1} & _{|} & _{- 3} \end{matrix} \right)\)

Simplifying the second row gives

\(\begin{matrix} _{R^{\ast }_{1} } \\ _{R^{\ast \ast }_{2} } \\ _{R^{\prime }_{3} } \end{matrix} \left(\begin{matrix} _{1} & _{5} & _{0} & _{|} & _{- 18} \\ _{0} & _{1} & _{0} & _{|} & _{- 4} \\ _{0} & _{0} & _{1} & _{|} & _{- 3} \end{matrix} \right)\)

This matrix is now in row echelon form but not in reduced row echelon form.We need to convert the 5 in the top row into 0 to get it into reduced row echelon form.
How?
We carry out the row operation, \(R^{\ast }_{1} – 5R^{\ast \ast }_{2}\):

\(\begin{matrix} _{R^{\ast \ast }_{1} = R^{\ast }_{1} – 5R^{\ast \ast }_{2} } \\ _{R^{\ast \ast }_{2} } \\ _{R^{\prime }_{3} } \end{matrix} \left(\begin{matrix} _{1 – 5\left(0\right) } & _{5 – 5\left(1\right) } & _{0 – 5\left(0\right) } & _{|} & _{- 18 – 5\left(- 4\right) } \\ _{0} & _{1} & _{0} & _{|} & _{- 4} \\ _{0} & _{0} & _{1} & _{|} & _{- 3} \end{matrix} \right)\)

Simplifying the top row entries gives

\(\begin{matrix} _{} & _{x} & _{y} & _{z}~~~~~~~~~~~~\end{matrix}\)

\(\begin{matrix} _{R^{\ast \ast }_{1} } \\ _{R^{\ast \ast }_{2} } \\ _{R^{\prime }_{3} } \end{matrix} \left(\begin{matrix} _{1} & _{0} & _{0} & _{|} & _{2} \\ _{0} & _{1} & _{0} & _{|} & _{- 4} \\ _{0} & _{0} & _{1} & _{|} & _{- 3} \end{matrix} \right)\)

Hence we have placed the given augmented matrix into reduced row echelon form.
