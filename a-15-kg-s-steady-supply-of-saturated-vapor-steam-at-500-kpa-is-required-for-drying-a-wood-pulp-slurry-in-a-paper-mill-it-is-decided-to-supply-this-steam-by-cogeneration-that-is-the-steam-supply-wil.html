Guest Question.

A 15 kg/s steady supply of saturated-vapor steam at 500 kPa is required for drying a wood pulp slurry in a paper mill. It is decided to supply this steam by cogeneration, that is, the steam supply will be the exhaust from a steam turbine. Water at 20°C, 100 kPa, is pumped to a pressure of 5 MPa and then fed to a steam generator with an exit at 400°C. What is the additional heat transfer rate to the steam generator beyond what would have been required to produce only the desired steam supply? What is the difference in net power?

Admin
Solution

Desired exit State 4: \(P _{4}=500 kPa , \text { sat. vap. } \Rightarrow x _{4}=1.0, T _{4}=151.9^{\circ} C\)

\(h _{4}= h _{ g }=2748.7 kJ / kg , \quad s _{4}= s _{ g }=6.8212 kJ / kg – K\)

Inlet State: 20°C, 100 kPa \(h _{1}= h _{ f }=83.94 kJ / kg , v _{1}= v _{ f }=0.001002 m ^{3} / kg\)

Without Cogeneration; The water is pumped up to 500 kPa and then heated in the steam generator to the desired exit T.

C.V. Pump:

\(\begin{aligned}& w _{ Pw / o }= v _{1}\left( P _{4}- P _{1}\right)=0.4 kJ / kg \\& h _{2}= h _{1}+ w _{ Pw / o }=84.3 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

C.V. Steam Generator: \(q _{ w / o }= h _{4}- h _{2}=2664.4 kJ / kg\)

With Cogeneration; The water is pumped to 5 MPa, heated in the steam generator to 400°C and then flows through the turbine with desired exit state.

C.V. Pump:

\(\begin{aligned}& w _{ Pw }=\int vdP = v _{1}\left( P _{2^{-}} P _{1}\right)=4.91 kJ / kg \\& h _{2}= h _{1}+ w _{ Pw }=88.85 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

C.V. Steam Generator: Exit 400°C, 5 MPa \(\Rightarrow h _{3}=3195.64 kJ / kg\)

\(q _{ w }= h _{3}- h _{2}=3195.64-88.85=3106.8 kJ / kg\)

C.V.: Turbine, Inlet and exit states given

\(w _{ t }= h _{3}- h _{4}=3195.64-2748.7=446.94 kJ / kg\)

Comparison
Additional Heat Transfer: \(q _{ w }- q _{ w / o }=3106.8-2664.4=442.4 kJ / kg\)

\(\dot{ Q }_{\text {extra }}=\dot{ m }\left( q _{ w }- q _{ w / o }\right)= 6 6 3 6 k W\)

Difference in Net Power: \(w _{ diff }=\left( w _{ t }- w _{ Pw }\right)+ w _{ Pw / o }\),

\(w _{ diff }=446.94-4.91+0.4=442.4 kJ / kg\)

 

\(\dot{ W }_{ diff }=\dot{ m } w _{ diff }= 6 6 3 6 k W\)

By adding the extra heat transfer at the higher pressure and a turbine all the extra heat transfer can come out as work (it appears as a 100% efficiency)
