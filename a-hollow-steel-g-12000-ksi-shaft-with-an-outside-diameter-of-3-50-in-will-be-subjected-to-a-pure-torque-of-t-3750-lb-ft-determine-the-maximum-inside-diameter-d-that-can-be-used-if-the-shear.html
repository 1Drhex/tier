Guest Question.

A hollow steel [G = 12,000 ksi] shaft with an outside diameter of 3.50 in. will be subjected to a pure torque of T = 3,750 lb-ft. Determine the maximum inside diameter d that can be used if the shear stress must not exceed 8,000 psi and the angle of twist must not exceed 3° in an 8-ft length of shaft. Report both the maximum shear stress τ and the angle of twist \(\phi\) for this maximum inside diameter.

Admin
Solution

Consider shear stress: The polar moment of inertia for a hollow shaft can be expressed as

\(J=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[D^{4}-d^{4}\right]\)

The elastic torsion formula can be rearranged to gather terms with D:

\(\frac{\pi}{32} \frac{\left[D^{4}-d^{4}\right]}{D / 2}=\frac{T}{\tau}\)

Rearrange this equation to isolate the inside diameter d term:

\(D^{4}-d^{4} \geq \frac{32 T D}{2 \pi \tau} \quad \therefore d^{4} \leq D^{4}-\frac{32 T D}{2 \pi \tau}\)

From this equation, the unknown inside diameter of the hollow shaft can be expressed as

\(d \leq \sqrt[4]{D^{4}-\frac{32 T D}{2 \pi \tau}}\)

For the hollow steel shaft, the maximum diameter that will satisfy the allowable shear stress is:

\(d \leq \sqrt[4]{(3.50 in .)^{4}-\frac{32(3,750 lb – ft )(3.50 in .)(12 in . / ft )}{2 \pi(8,000 psi )}}=2.6564 in.\)

Consider angle of twist: Rearrange the angle of twist equation:

\(\phi=\frac{T L}{J G} \quad \therefore J=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[D^{4}-d^{4}\right] \geq \frac{T L}{\phi G}\)

Rearrange this equation to isolate the inside diameter d term:

\(d^{4} \leq D^{4}-\frac{32 T L}{\pi \phi G}\)

and solve for the maximum inside diameter that will satisfy the angle of twist limitation:

\(d \leq \sqrt[4]{D^{4}-\frac{32 T L}{\pi \phi G}}=\sqrt[4]{(3.50 in .)^{4}-\frac{32(3,750 lb – ft )(8 ft )(12 in. / ft )^{2}}{\pi\left(3^{\circ}\right)\left(\pi / 180^{\circ}\right)(12,000,000 psi )}}=2.991 in.\)

Therefore, the maximum inside diameter that could be used for the shaft is

\(d_{\max }=2.66 in.\)

The shear stress for this shaft is τ = 8,000 psi. To compute the angle of twist in an 8-ft length of the hollow shaft, first compute the polar moment of inertia:

\(J=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[D^{4}-d^{4}\right]=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[(3.50 in .)^{4}-(2.66 in .)^{4}\right]=9.817318 in .{ }^{4}\)

The angle of twist in the shaft is thus:

\(\phi=\frac{T L}{J G}=\frac{(3,750 lb – ft )(8 ft )(12 in. / ft )^{2}}{\left(9.817318 in .{ }^{4}\right)(12,000,000 psi )}=0.0367 rad =2.10^{\circ}\)
