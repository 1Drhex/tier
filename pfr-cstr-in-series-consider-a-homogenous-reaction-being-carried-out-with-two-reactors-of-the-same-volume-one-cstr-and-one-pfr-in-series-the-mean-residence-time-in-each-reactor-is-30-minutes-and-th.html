Guest Question.

PFR-CSTR in series
Consider a homogenous reaction being carried out with two reactors of the same volume, one CSTR and one PFR, in series. The mean residence time in each reactor is 30 minutes, and the reaction rate is given by

\(-r_{A}=k_{c} C_{A}^{3 / 2}\) (E19.2.1)

with \(k_{ c }=0.05 mol ^{-1 / 2} \cdot L ^{1 / 2} \cdot min ^{-1}\). The feed consists of \(C_{ A 0}\) = 2 mol/L.

a. Determine the fractional conversion can be achieved from the reactor system if CSTR is placed in front of the PFR;
b. Determine the fractional conversion if PFR is placed in front of the CSTR;
c. Determine the residence-time distribution and comment on the two reactor systems as given by (a) and (b);
d. Determine the fractional conversion from the residence time distribution of part (c) using a segregation model. Comment on your findings.

Admin
Solution

The reactor systems are shown in Fig. E19.2.1. The reactors are of equal volumes and the mean residence times are both of 30 minutes.

(a) We solve for the conversion of A from the CSTR first. Mole balance of species A around the CSTR gives,

In − out +Generation = Accumulation

\(Q C_{ A 0}-Q C_{ A 1}+r_{ A 1} V_{ CSTR }=0\) (E19.2.2)

Substituting in the rate law, Eq. (E19.2.1), one obtains

\(\frac{C_{A 0}-C_{A 1}}{\bar{t}}-k_{c} C_{A 1}^{3 / 2}=0\) (E19.2.3)

And the solution is given by

\(C_{A 1}+k_{c} \bar{t} C_{A 1}^{3 / 2}-C_{A 0}=0\) (E19.2.4)

Which can be solved after substituting in the values of \(k_{c}=0.05 mol ^{-1 / 2} \cdot L ^{1 / 2} \cdot min ^{-1} \text { and } C_{ A 0}=2 mol / L , C_{ A 1}=0.841695 mol / L \text { . }\)

We next examine the PFR. Mole balance on a differential volume gives,

\(-Q d C_{A}-k_{c} C_{A}^{3 / 2} d V=0\) (E19.2.5)

Let,

\(d t=\frac{d V}{Q}\) (E19.2.6)

Substituting into Eq. (E19.2.5) and separating the variables,

\(-C_{A}^{-3 / 2} d C_{A}=k_{c} d t \) (E19.2.7)

Integrating over the PFR, we obtain

\(2\left(C_{A e}^{-1 / 2}-C_{A 1}^{-1 / 2}\right)=k_{c} t\) (E19.2.8)

or

\(C_{A e}=\left\lgroup \frac{k_{c} t}{2}+C_{A 1}^{-1 / 2} \right\rgroup ^{-2}\) (E19.2.9)

The fractional conversion,

\(f_{A e}=1-\frac{\left\lgroup \frac{k_{c} t}{2}+C_{A 1}^{-1 / 2} \right\rgroup ^{-2}}{C_{A 0}}\) (E19.2.10)

Substituting in the numbers, \(t=\bar{t}_{P F R}=30 min , k_{ c } 0.05 mol ^{-1 / 2} \cdot L ^{1 / 2} \cdot min ^{-1}, C_{ A 0}=2 mol / L \text { and } C_{ A 1}\) = 0.841695 mol/L, we obtain,

\(C_{ Ae }=0.295372 mol / L \text { and } f_{ Ae }=0.852314 .\)

(b) PFR-CSTR in series.

We start with the PFR. Mole balance on a differential volume gives,

\(-Q d C_{A}-k_{c} C_{A}^{3 / 2} d V=0\) (E19.2.5)

Let,

\(d t=\frac{d V}{Q}\) (E19.2.6)

Substituting into Eq. (E19.2.5) and separating the variables,

\(-C_{A}^{-3 / 2} d C_{A}=k_{c} d t\) (E19.2.7)

Integrating over the PFR, we obtain

\(2\left(C_{A 1}^{-1 / 2}-C_{A 0}^{-1 / 2}\right)=k_{c} t\) (E19.2.11)

or

\(C_{A 1}=\left\lgroup \frac{k_{c} t}{2}+C_{A 0}^{-1 / 2} \right\rgroup ^{-2}\) (E19.2.12)

Substituting in the numbers, \(t=\bar{t}_{P F R}=30 min , k_{ c } 0.05 mol ^{-1 / 2} \cdot L ^{1 / 2} \cdot min ^{-1}, C_{ A 0}=2 mol / L , \text { we obtain, } C_{ A 1}\) = 0.470996 mol/L.

We then examine the second reactor, CSTR. Mole balance of species A over the entire reactor leads to

\(Q C_{ A 1}-Q C_{ Ae }+r_{ Ae } V_{ CSTR }=0\) (E19.2.13)

Substituting in the rate law, Eq. (E19.2.1), one obtains

\(\frac{C_{A 1}-C_{A e}}{\bar{t}}-k_{c} C_{A e}^{3 / 2}=0\) (E19.2.14)

And the solution is given by

\(C_{A e}+k_{c} \bar{t} C_{A e}^{3 / 2}-C_{A 1}=0\) (E19.2.15)

Which can be solved after substituting in the values of \(k_{ c } 0.05 mol ^{-1 / 2} \cdot L ^{1 / 2} \cdot min ^{-1} \text { and } C_{ A 1}=0.470996 mol / L , C_{ Ae }\) = 0.265635 mol/L.

The conversion can be obtained as

\(f_{A e}=1-\frac{C_{A e}}{C_{A 0}}=1-\frac{0.265635}{2}=0.867183\)

(c) The residence-time distribution for the two reactors in series can be determined rather easily. The PFR has one single residence-
time point, \(t=\bar{t}_{P F R}\) = 30min, it would only display as a delay of the output of the CSTR. The residence-time distribution for a CSTR alone is,

\(E(t)=\frac{1}{\bar{t}_{C S T R}} \exp \left\lgroup -\frac{t}{\bar{t}_{C S T R}} \right\rgroup\) (E19.2.16)

And the PFR-CSTR in series is thus,

\(E(t)= \begin{cases}0, & t<\bar{t}_{P F R} \\ \frac{1}{\bar{t}_{C S T R}} \exp \left(-\frac{t-\bar{t}_{P F R}}{\bar{t}_{C S T R}}\right), & t \geq \bar{t}_{P F R}\end{cases}\) (E19.2.17)

There is no difference on the residence-time distribution whether CSTR or PFR is in the front.

(d) As we have solved in parts (a) and (b), the conversion from a PFR is given by Eq. (E19.1.10) or

\(f_{A}=1-\frac{\left\lgroup \frac{k_{c} t}{2}+C_{A 0}^{-1 / 2} \right\rgroup ^{-2}}{C_{A 0}}\) (E19.2.18)

If we treat the residence-time distribution as a normal probability distribution function, we can compute the conversion using the conversion as a function of time from the PFR. That is,

\(f_{A e}=\int_{0}^{\infty} E(t) f_{A e, P F R}(t) d t\)

 

\(=\int_{\bar{T}_{P R}}^{\infty} \frac{1}{\bar{t}_{C S T R}} \exp \left\lgroup -\frac{t-\bar{t}_{P F R}}{\bar{t}_{C T R}} \right\rgroup \left[1-\frac{\left\lgroup \frac{k_{c} t}{2}+C_{A 0}^{-1 / 2} \right\rgroup ^{-2}}{C_{A 0}}\right] d t\) (E19.2.19)

 

\(=1-\frac{1}{\bar{t}_{C S T R}} \exp\left\lgroup \frac{\bar{t}_{P F R}}{\bar{t}_{C S T R}} \right\rgroup \int_{\bar{t}_{P F R}}^{\infty} \exp \left\lgroup -\frac{t}{\bar{t}_{C S T R}} \right\rgroup \left\lgroup 1+\frac{k_{c} t}{2} C_{A 0}^{1 / 2} \right\rgroup ^{-2} d t\)

which can be rendered to an expression using the exponential integral function.

\(f_{A e}=1-\frac{1}{\bar{t}_{C S T R}} \exp \left\lgroup \frac{\bar{t}_{P F R}}{\bar{t}_{C S T R}} \right\rgroup \int_{\bar{t}_{P r R}}^{\infty} \exp \left\lgroup -\frac{t}{\bar{t}_{C S T R}} \right\rgroup \left\lgroup 1+\frac{k_{c} t}{2} C_{A 0}^{1 / 2} \right\rgroup ^{-2} d t\)

 

\(=1-\frac{2}{\bar{t}_{C S T R} k_{c} C_{A 0}^{1 / 2}} \exp\left\lgroup \frac{\bar{t}_{P F R}}{\bar{t}_{C S T R}} \right\rgroup \int_{1+\frac{k_{c} \bar{t}_{R R}}{2} C_{A 0}^{y_{2}}} ^{\infty }\exp \left\lgroup -2 \frac{x-1}{\bar{t}_{C S T R} k_{c} C_{A 0}^{1 / 2}} \right\rgroup \frac{d x}{x^{2}}\) (E19.2.20)

 

\(=1-2 \frac{\exp \left\lgroup \frac{\bar{t}_{P F R} k_{c} C_{A 0}^{1 / 2}+2}{\bar{t}_{C S T R} k_{c} C_{A 0}^{1 / 2}} \right\rgroup }{\bar{t}_{C S T R} k_{c} C_{A 0}^{1 / 2}} \int_{1+\frac{k_{c} \bar{t}_{R R R}}{2} C_{A 0}^{1 / 2}} ^{\infty } \exp \left\lgroup -\frac{2 x}{\bar{t}_{C S T R} k_{c} C_{A 0}^{1 / 2}} \right\rgroup \frac{d x}{x^{2}}\)

The integral can be rearranged to yield,

\(\int_{1+\frac{k_{c} \bar{t}_{P R}}{2}}^{\infty} \exp _{A 0}^{1 / 2}\left\lgroup -\frac{2 x}{\bar{t}_{C S T R} k_{c} C_{A 0}^{1 / 2}} \right\rgroup \frac{d x}{x^{2}}=\)\(\int_{\frac{2+k_{c} t_{PFR}} C_{A 0}} ^{\infty} {\bar{t}_{C T R R} R_{c} C_{A 0}^{2}} \exp (-y) \frac{2}{\bar{t}_{C S T R} k_{c} C_{A 0}^{1 / 2}} \frac{d y}{y^{2}}\)
