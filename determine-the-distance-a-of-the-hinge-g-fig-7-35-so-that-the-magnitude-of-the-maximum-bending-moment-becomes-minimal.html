Guest Question.

Determine the distance a of the hinge G (Fig. 7.35) so that the magnitude of the maximum bending moment becomes minimal.

Admin
Solution

The structure is a three-hinged arch. In order to calculate the support reactions we separate the two bodies. The equilibrium conditions for the structure as a whole and for the right-hand portion

\(\uparrow :A_{V} + B_{V} =0\) ,

\(\rightarrow : q_{0}l -A_{H} – B_{H} =0\) ,

\(\overset{\curvearrowright }{A} :\frac{1}{2} q_{0} l^{2} – l B_{V} =0\) ,

\(\overset{\curvearrowright }{G} : l B_{H}-(l-a)B_{V} =0\)

lead to

\(B_{V} =- A_{V} =\frac{1}{2} q_{0} l , B_{H}= \frac{1}{2} q_{0} (l-a) , A_{H}= \frac{1}{2} q_{0} (l+a)\) .

The bending moment is linear in the regions ② and ③. At the points C and D it has the values

\(M_{C} =l A_{H} -\frac{1}{2} q_{0} l^{2} = \frac{1}{2} q_{0} l a , M_{D} =-\frac{1}{2} q_{0} l (l-a)\) .

In region ① the bending moment is given by

\(M(x)=x A_{H} – \frac{1}{2} q_{0} x^{2} =\frac{1}{2} q_{0} [(l+a)x-x^{2}]\) .

Its maximum value follows through differentiation:

\(\frac{dM}{dx} =0 : l+a -2x =0 \rightarrow x^{*} =\frac{l+a}{2}\)

\(\rightarrow M^{*} =M(x^{*}) =\frac{1}{8} q_{0}(l+a)^{2}\) .

The largest bending moments are found at C, D and \(x^{*}\) .If, in turn, we equate the magnitudes of two moments, we obtain

\(\left|M_{C}\right| =\left|M_{D}\right| : a=l/2\)

\(\rightarrow \left|M_{C}\right| =\left|M_{D}\right| =q_{0} l^{2} /4 =0.25 q_{0} l^{2}\) ,

\(M^{*}=9 q_{0} l^{2}/32 =0.28 q_{0} l^{2}\) ,

\(\left|M_{C}\right| = \left|M^{*}\right|:4la =(l+a)^{2} \rightarrow a=l\)

\(\rightarrow \left|M_{C}\right| = \left|M^{*}\right| = 0.5 q_{0} l^{2}\) ,

\(M_{D} =0\) ,

\(\left|M^{*}\right|= \left|M_{D}\right|: (l+a)^{2} =4l(l-a) \rightarrow a= l(\sqrt{12}-3 )\)

\(\rightarrow \left|M^{*}\right|= \left|M_{D}\right| = (4-\sqrt{12} ) q_{0} l^{2}/2 =0.268 q_{0} l^{2}\),

\(M_{C} =(-3 +\sqrt{12} ) q_{0} l^{2} /2 =0.232 q_{0} l^{2}\).

We see that the magnitude of the largest moment is a minimum if

\(\underline{\underline{a=l(\sqrt{12}-3 )=0.464l} }\) .

Then the support reactions\(B_{H}\) and \(A_{H}\) are

\(B_{H} =\frac{4-\sqrt{12} }{2} q_{0} l =0.268 q_{0} l , A_{H}=\frac{\sqrt{12} -2}{2} q_{0} l =0.732q_{0} l\)
