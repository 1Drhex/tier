Guest Question.

In a boiler unit forced draught fan delivers ambient air at 20°C with velocity of 20 m/s. The draught lost through grate is 30 mm of water column. Determine the power required to drive the fan if fan’s mechanical efficiency is 80% and coal is burnt at the rate of 1000 kg per hour and air is supplied at the rate of 16 kg per kg of coal. Ambient pressure and density of air may be taken as 1.01325 bar and 1.29 kg/\(\textbf{m}^{3}\).

Admin
Solution
\(\text{Total draught loss = Pressure equivalent to velocity head + Draught loss through grate.}\\\text{Pressure equivalent to velocity head}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad = \frac{1}{2}ρV^{2} = \frac{1}{2} × 1.29 × (20)^{2} = 258 N/m^{2}\\\text{Since 1 mm of water column is equal to 9.81 N/m}^{2};\\\text{so pressure equivalent to velocity head }= \frac{258}{9.81} = \text{26.29 mm of water}\\\text{Hence total draught loss = 26.29 + 30 = 56.29 mm of water column}\\\text{Pressure required, p = 56.29 × 9.81, N/m}^{2} = 552.21 N/m^{2}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Forced draught fan power requirement}&= \frac{p · m_{f} · m_{a} · T_{a}}{ρ_{a} ·T_{o} ·η_{mech}}\\&= \frac{552.21 × 1000 × 16 × 293}{1.29 × 273 × 0.80 × 3600}, W\end{aligned}\\\text{F.D. fan, power = 2552.39 W or 2.55 kW}\)
