Guest Question.

Consider a single tank liquid-level system where the out-flow passes through a valve. Recalling Equation \(q=\frac{1}{R_{v}}h\), assume now that the valve discharge rate is related to the square root of liquid level: \(q=C_{v}\sqrt{h} \) where \(C_{v}\) depends on the size of the valve (see Chapter 9). Derive an approximate dynamic model for this process by linearization and compare with the results in .

Admin
Solution

The material balance for the process (Equation
\(A\frac{dh}{dt}=q_{i}-q \))
after substituting Equation
\(q=C_{v}\sqrt{h} \)
is
\( A\frac{dh}{dt}=q_{i}-C_{v}\sqrt{h} \) (1)
To obtain the system transfer function, linearize Equation (1) about the steady-state conditions (\(\bar{h},\bar{q}_{i})\). The deviation
variables are
\(h^{\prime}=h-\bar{h}\)
\(q^{\prime}_{i}=q_{i}-\bar{q}_{i} \)
Applying Equation
\(\frac{dy^{\prime}}{dt}=\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\mid _{s}y^{\prime}+ \frac{\partial f}{\partial u} \mid _{s}u^{\prime} \)
where y = h and x= \(q_{i}\), and \(f(h,q_{i})\) is the right side of Equation (1), the linearized differential equation is
\(A\frac{dh^{\prime}}{dt}=q^{\prime}_{i}-\frac{C_{v}}{2\sqrt{\bar{h} }}h^{\prime} \) (2)
If we define the valve resistance R using the relation
\(\frac{1}{R}= \frac{C_{v}}{2\sqrt{\bar{h} }}\) (3)
the resulting dynamic equation is analogous to the linear model presented earlier in Equation
\(A_{1}\frac{dh^{\prime}_{1}}{dt}=q^{\prime}_{i}-\frac{1}{R_{1}}h^{\prime}_{1} \):
\(A\frac{dh^{\prime}}{dt}=q^{\prime}_{i}-\frac{1}{R}h^{\prime} \) (4)
The transfer function corresponding to Equation (4) was derived earlier as Equation
\(\frac{H^{\prime}_{1}(s)}{Q^{\prime}_{1}(s)}=\frac{R_{1}}{A_{1}R_{1}s+1}=\frac{K_{1}}{\tau _{1}s+1}\).
Note that the linearized model in Equation (4) has the
same form as the corresponding linear model in Equation
\(A\frac{dh^{\prime}}{dt}=q^{\prime}_{i}-\frac{1}{R_{v}}h^{\prime} \)
of . But the valve resistance parameter \( R_{1}\) for the linearized model depends on the nominal steady-state liquid level \(\bar{h}\), while the corresponding parameter R for the linear model was a constant.
