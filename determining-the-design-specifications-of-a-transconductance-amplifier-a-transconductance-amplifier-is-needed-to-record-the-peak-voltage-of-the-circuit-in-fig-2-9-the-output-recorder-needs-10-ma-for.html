Guest Question.

Determining the design specifications of a transconductance amplifier A transconductance amplifier is needed to record the peak voltage of the circuit in Fig. 2.9. The output recorder needs 10 mA for a reading of 1 cm, and it should read 10 cm ±2% for a peak input voltage of 100 V. The internal resistance of the recorder varies from \( R_{ L }=100 \Omega \text { to } R_{ L }=500 \Omega \). The frequency of the input voltage is \( f_{ s }=1 kHz \).

(a) Determine the value of capacitance C.

(b) Determine the design specifications of the transconductance amplifier.

Admin
Solution

(a) The capacitor C will charge to the peak input voltage when the diode conducts, and it will discharge through the amplifier when the diode is off. Let us assume that the discharging time constant \( \tau\left(=C R_{ i }\right) \) is related to the input frequency by \( \tau=10 / f_{ s } . \text { For } f_{ s }=1 kHz , C R_{ i }=10 /(1 kHz )=10 ms . \). Let us choose C = 0.01 µF. Then \( R_{ i }=10 ms / 0.01 \mu F =1 M \Omega \).

(b) Since the output variation should be kept within ±2%, the variation in the effective transconductance \( G_{ m } \) should also be limited to ±2%. According to Eq. (2.34), the variation in \( G_{ m } \) will be contributed by \( G_{ ms } \text { and } R_{ L } \). Let us assume that each of them contributes equally to the variation—that is, each contributes ±1%. Note that there is no source resistance: \( R_{ s }=0 \). The nominal transconductance gain is

\( G_{ m }=\frac{i_{ o }}{v_{ s }}=\frac{G_{ ms } R_{ o } R_{ i }}{\left(R_{ o }+R_{ L }\right)\left(R_{ i }+R_{ s }\right)}=\frac{G_{ ms }}{\left(1+R_{ L } / R_{ o }\right)\left(1+R_{ s } / R_{ i }\right)} \) (2.34)

 

\( G_{ ms }=\frac{i_{ o }}{v_{ s }}=\left(\frac{10 cm }{100 V }\right)\left(\frac{10 mA }{1 cm }\right)=1 mA / V \pm 1 \% \)

 

Thus, the value of \( R_{ o } \) that will keep the gain variation within 1% for variation in \( R_{ L } \) from 100 Ω to 500 Ω can be found from

 

\( 0.99 \frac{R_{ o }}{R_{ o }+100}=\frac{R_{ o }}{R_{ o }+500} \)

 

which gives \( R_{ o } \geq 39.5 k \Omega \text { when solved for } R_{ o } \text {. } \) Thus, the amplifier specifications are \( G_{ ms }=1 mA / V \pm 1 \% , R_{ i } \approx 1 M \Omega, \text { and } R_{ o } \geq 39.5 k \Omega .\)
