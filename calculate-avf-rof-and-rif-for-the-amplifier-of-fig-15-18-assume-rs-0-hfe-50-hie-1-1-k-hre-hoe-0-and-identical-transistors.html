Guest Question.

Calculate AVf, Rof, and Rif for the amplifier of Fig. 15.18. Assume Rs = 0, hfe = 50, hie = 1.1 K, hre = hoe = 0, and identical transistors.

Admin
Solution

The effective load R′L1 of transistor Q1 is

R′L1 = 10 || 47 || 33 || 1.1 K = 942 Ω

Since the voltage across R1 is negligible compared with that across R2, the effective load R′L2 of transistor Q2 is

R′L2 = 4.7 || 4.7 = 2.35 K

The voltage gain AV1 of Q1 is independent of the emitter impedance and is, from Eq. (10.28), \(A_{V}=A_{I}\frac{R_{L}}{R_{i}}=-\frac{h_{fe}R_{L}}{h_{ie}} \) ,

\(A_{V1}\equiv \frac{V_{1}}{V_{i}}=\frac{-h_{fe}R^{′}_{L1}}{h_{ie}}=\frac{-50\times 0.942}{1.1}=-42.8 \)

The voltage gain AV2 of Q2 is, from Eq. (10.28),

\(A_{V2}\frac{V_{o}}{V_{1}}=-h_{fe}\frac{R^{′}_{L2}}{h_{ie}}=-50\times \frac{2.35}{1.1}=-107 \)

Hence the voltage gain AV of the two stages in cascade is

\(A_{v}\equiv \frac{V_{o}}{V_{i}}=A_{V1}A_{V2}=42.8\times 107=4,580 \)

If again we assume I′ << I, the feedback factor β is given by

\(\beta =\frac{R_{1}}{R_{1}+R_{2}}=\frac{100}{4,800}=\frac{1}{48} \) and \(A_{V}\beta =\frac{4,580}{48}=95.4 \)

Using Eq. (15.12), \(A_{f}\equiv A_{vsf}=\frac{A_{vs}}{1+A_{vs}\beta } \) , we obtain

\(A_{Vf}=\frac{4,580}{1+95.4}=47.5 \)

This value is to be compared with the approximate solution (based upon AV → ∞ ) given by Eq. (15.25), \(A_{Vf}\equiv \frac{V_{o}}{V_{s}} \approx \frac{R_{1}+R_{2}}{R_{1}}=\frac{1}{\beta } \) , namely, AVf = 1/β = 48.

The output resistance without feedback is Ro ≈ 4.7 || 4.7 = 2.35 K. Hence, from Eq. (15.13), \(R_{of}=\frac{R_{o}}{1+A_{vs}\beta } \) ,

\(R_{of}=\frac{2.35}{1+95,4}K=24.4\Omega \)

The input resistance without feedback is

Ri ≈ hie = 1.1 K

Hence, from Eq. (15.18), \(R_{if}=R_{i}+\frac{V_{f}}{I_{i}}=R_{i}(1+\beta A_{V}) \) ,

Rif = (1.1) (1 + 95.4) = 106 K

If we take the biasing resistances at the base of Q1 into account, the input impedance R′if seen by the signal source is

R′if = 106 || 150 || 47 = 26.8 K

We now justify the assumption that I′ << I. We have already emphasized that Vf ≈ Vs , and hence the voltage across R1 is approximately equal to Vs, or

\(I+I^{′}=\frac{V_{s}}{R_{1}} \) and \(I=\frac{V_{o}-V_{s}}{R_{2}}=\frac{A_{Vf}V_{s}-V_{s}}{R_{2}} \)

Dividing these two equations gives

\(1+\frac{I^{′}}{I}=\frac{R_{2}}{R_{1}(A_{Vf}-1)}=\frac{4.7}{(0.1)(16.5)}=1.01 \)

Hence I′ is only 1 percent of I.
