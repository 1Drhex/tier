Guest Question.

Rigid bar ABC is supported by two identical solid bronze [E = 100 GPa, α = 16.9 × \(10^{-6}\)/°C] rods, and a solid steel [E = 200 GPa, α = 11.9×\(10^{-6}\)/°C] rod, as shown in Figure P5.59. The bronze rods (1) each have a diameter of 16 mm and they are symmetrically positioned relative to the center rod (2) and the applied load P. Steel rod (2) has a diameter of 20 mm. The bars are unstressed when the structure is assembled at 30°C. When the temperature decreases to –20°C, determine:
(a) the normal stresses in the bronze and steel rods.
(b) the normal strains in the bronze and steel rods.

Admin
Solution

Equilibrium: By virtue of symmetry, the forces in the two bronze rods (1) are identical. Consider a FBD of the rigid bar. Sum forces in the vertical direction to obtain:

\(\Sigma F_{y}=2 F_{1}+F_{2}=0\) (a)

Geometry of Deformations: For this configuration, the deflections of joints A, B, and C are equal:

\(v_{A}=v_{B}=v_{C}\) (b)

All pin connections are ideal; therefore, the deflection of joints A and C will cause an identical deformation of rods (1):

\(v_{A}=\delta_{1}\) (c)

and the rigid bar deflection \(v_{B}\) will cause an identical deformation of rod (2):

\(v_{B}=\delta_{2}\) (d)

Substitute Eqs. (c) and (d) into Eq. (b) to obtain the geometry of deformation equation:

\(\delta_{1}=\delta_{2}\) (e)

Force-Temperature-Deformation Relationships: The relationship between internal force, temperature change, and deformation can be stated for members (1) and (2):

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\alpha_{1} \Delta T L_{1} \quad \delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}+\alpha_{2} \Delta T L_{2}\) (f)

Compatibility Equation: Substitute the force-deformation relationships (f) into the geometry of deformation relationship (e) to derive the compatibility equation:

\(\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\alpha_{1} \Delta T_{1} L_{1}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}+\alpha_{2} \Delta T_{2} L_{2}\) (g)

Solve the Equations: For this situation, \(\Delta T_{1}=\Delta T_{2}=\Delta T\) . Solve Eq. (g) for \(F_{2}\) :

\(\begin{aligned}F_{2} &=\left[\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\alpha_{1} \Delta T_{1} L_{1}-\alpha_{2} \Delta T_{2} L_{2}\right] \frac{A_{2} E_{2}}{L_{2}} \\&=F_{1} \frac{L_{1}}{L_{2}} \frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}} \frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}+\Delta T\left[\alpha_{1} L_{1}-\alpha_{2} L_{2}\right] \frac{A_{2} E_{2}}{L_{2}}\end{aligned}\)

Substitute this expression into Eq. (a)

\(2 F_{1}+F_{1} \frac{L_{1}}{L_{2}} \frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}} \frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}+\Delta T\left[\alpha_{1} L_{1}-\alpha_{2} L_{2}\right] \frac{A_{2} E_{2}}{L_{2}}=0\)

and derive an expression for \(F_{1}\), noting that \(L_{1}=L_{2}\):

\(\begin{aligned}F_{1}\left[2+\frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}} \frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}\right] &=-\Delta T\left[\alpha_{1}-\alpha_{2}\right] A_{2} E_{2} \\F_{1} &=-\frac{\Delta T\left[\alpha_{1}-\alpha_{2}\right] A_{2} E_{2}}{2+\frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}} \frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}} (h)\end{aligned}\)

For this structure, the areas, coefficients of thermal expansion, and elastic moduli are given below:

\(\begin{array}{ll}A_{1}=\frac{\pi}{4}(16 mm )^{2}=201.0619 mm ^{2} & A_{2}=\frac{\pi}{4}(20 mm )^{2}=314.1593 mm ^{2} \\E_{1}=100,000 MPa & E_{2}=200,000 MPa \\\alpha_{1}=16.9 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} C & \alpha_{2}=11.9 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} C\end{array}\)

Substitute these values along with \(\Delta T=-50^{\circ} C\) into Eq. (h) and calculate \(F_{1}\):

\(\begin{aligned}F_{1} &=-\frac{\Delta T\left[\alpha_{1}-\alpha_{2}\right] A_{2} E_{2}}{2+\frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}} \frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}} \\&=-\frac{\left(-50^{\circ} C \right)\left[16.9 \times 10^{-6}-11.9 \times 10^{-6}\right]\left(452.3893 mm ^{2}\right)\left(200,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}{2+\left(\frac{314.1593 mm ^{2}}{201.0619 mm ^{2}}\right)\left(\frac{200,000 MPa }{100,000 MPa }\right)} \\&=3,064.97 N\end{aligned}\)

Backsubstitute this result into Eq. (a) to calculate \(F_{2}=-6,129.94 N\) .

(a) Normal Stresses: The normal stresses in each rod can now be calculated:

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{1}=\frac{F_{1}}{A_{1}}=\frac{3,064.97 N }{201.0619 mm ^{2}}=15.24 MPa ( T ) \\&\sigma_{2}=\frac{F_{2}}{A_{2}}=\frac{-6,129.94 N }{314.1593 mm ^{2}}=19.51 MPa ( C )\end{aligned}\)

(b) Normal Strains: The force-temperature-deformation relationships were expressed in Eq. (f). By definition, \(\varepsilon=\delta /L\). Therefore, the normal strain for each axial member can be determined by dividing the relationships in Eq. (f) by the respective member lengths:

\(\varepsilon_{1}=\frac{F_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\alpha_{1} \Delta T_{1}=\frac{\sigma_{1}}{E_{1}}+\alpha_{1} \Delta T_{1} \quad \varepsilon_{2}=\frac{F_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}+\alpha_{2} \Delta T_{2}=\frac{\sigma_{2}}{E_{2}}+\alpha_{2} \Delta T_{2}\) (f)

Substitute the appropriate values to calculate the normal strains in each member:

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{1} &=\frac{\sigma_{1}}{E_{1}}+\alpha_{1} \Delta T_{1} \\&=\frac{15.2439 MPa }{100,000 MPa }+\left(16.9 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(-50^{\circ} C \right) \\&=-0.00069256 mm / mm =-693 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{2} &=\frac{F_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}+\alpha_{2} \Delta T_{2} \\&=\frac{-19.5122 MPa }{200,000 MPa }+\left(11.9 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(-50^{\circ} C \right) \\&=-0.00069256 mm / mm =-693 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)
