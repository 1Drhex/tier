Guest Question.

The chlorination of propene:
\(C_{3}H_{6} + Cl_{2}→CH_{2} =CH − CH_{2}Cl + HCl\)
takes place in gas phase inside a PFR reactor of 5 \(m^{3}\) at 300◦C and 20 atm. One introduces 30% \(C_{3}H_{6}\), 40% \(Cl_{2}\), and balance in \(N_{2}\). The total molar flow is 0.45 kmol/h. The rate constant is given:
\(k=4.12 × 10^{10} exp (−27, 200/(RT)) (m^{3}/(kmol min))\)

Calculate the final conversion and the mean residence time.

Admin
Solution

The reactant \(C_{3}H_{6} (A)\) is the limiting one and even in gas phase there is no volume variation, \(\varepsilon _{A}\) =0 (A + B→R + S). Based on the unit of rate constant, we have a second-order rate expression. One assumes an elementary reaction. The rate expression will be:
\((−r_{A})=kC_{A}C_{B} =kC^{2}_{A0}(1 − X_{A})(M − X_{A})\)

Substituting into PFR balance equation:
\(\tau =\frac{V}{\nu _{0}}=C_{A0}\int_{0}^{X_{A}}\frac{dX_{A}}{(-r_{A})}\)

We obtain the following solution:
\(\tau =\frac{1}{kC_{A0}(M-1)}\ln \frac{(M-X_{A})}{M(1-X_{A})}\) (14.49)

where:
\(M=\frac{C_{B0}}{C_{A0}}\)

The rate constant is calculated at 300◦C (573 K):
\(k=2.02m^{3}/(kmol min)\)

The initial concentration can be calculated:
\(C_{A0} =0.3(P/(RT))=0.127 mol/L=kmol/m^{3}\)

The ratio \(M=\frac{C_{B0}}{C_{A0}}\)

Thus,
M=1.33

Therefore, the molar flow of A:
\(F_{A0} =y_{A0}F_{0} =0.3 × 0.45=0.135 kmol/h\)

and the volumetric flow will be:
\(F_{A0} =C_{A0}.\nu _{0} \nu _{0} =1.063m^{3}/h\)

Then:
\(\tau =4.7 min\)

Substituting the values into expression 14.49, we obtain the conversion:
\(X_{A} =0.66\)
