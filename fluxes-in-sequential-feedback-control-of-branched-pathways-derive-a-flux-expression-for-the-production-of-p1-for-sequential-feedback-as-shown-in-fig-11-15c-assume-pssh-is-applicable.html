Guest Question.

Fluxes in sequential feedback control of branched pathways.

Derive a flux expression for the production of \(P _{1}\) for sequential feedback as shown in Fig. 11.15C. Assume PSSH is applicable.

Admin
Solution

In , we have shown how the kinetics can be derived from metabolic pathway via reaction rate approach. In this example, we examine the flux approach to the kinetics. To derive a reasonable flux expression from the simplified pathway given in Fig. 11.15C, we first identify all the nodes and connections as shown in Fig. E11.3.1. We next identify all the fluxes:

\(J_{1^{\prime}}=J_{1}=k_{1}\left[M_{1}\right]\left[ E _{2}\right]-k_{-1}\left[M_{1} E _{2}\right]\) (E11.3.1)

\(J _{2^{\prime}}=J_{2}=k_{1 c }\left[ M _{1} E _{2}\right]\) (E11.3.2)

\(J _{3^{\prime}}=I_{3}=k_{2}\left[ M _{2}\right]\left[ E _{3}\right]-k_{-2}\left[ M _{2} E _{3}\right]\) (E11.3.3)

\(J _{4^{\prime}}=J_{4}=k_{2 c }\left[ M _{2} E _{3}\right]\) (E11.3.4)

\(J_{5}=I_{6}=k_{ f 1}\left[ M _{3}\right]\left[ E _{2}\right]-k_{-f 1}\left[ M _{3} E _{2}\right]\) (E11.3.5)

\(J_{7^{\prime}}=J_{7}=k_{3}\left[M_{3}\right]\left[E_{4}\right]-k_{-3}\left[M_{3} E_{4}\right]\) (E11.3.6)

\(J_{8^{\prime}}=J_{8}=k_{3 c}\left[M_{3} E _{4}\right]\) (E11.3.7)

\(J_{9}=k_{4}\left[M_{4}\right]\) (E11.3.8)

\(J_{10}=J_{11}=k_{ f 2}\left[ P _{1}\right]\left[ E _{4}\right]-k_{-f 2}\left[ P _{1} E _{4}\right]\) (E11.3.9)

\(J_{12^{\prime}}=J_{12}=k_{5}\left[M_{3}\right]\left[ E _{5}\right]-k_{-5}\left[ M _{3} E _{5}\right]\) (E11.3.10)

\(J_{13^{\prime}}=J_{13}=k_{5 c }\left[ M _{3} E _{5}\right]\) (E11.3.11)

\(J_{14}=k_{6}\left[M_{5}\right]\) (E11.3.12)

\(J_{15}=J_{16}=k_{f 3}\left[P_{2}\right]\left[E_{5}\right]-k_{-f 3}\left[P_{2} E_{5}\right]\) (E11.3.13)

With PSSH, all the net fluxes passing through the internal notes (i.e., \(E _{2}, M_{1} E _{2}, M_{2}, E _{3}, M _{2} E _{3}, M_{3} E _{2}, E _{4}, M _{3} E _{4}, M _{4}, P _{1} E _{4}, E _{5}, M _{3} E _{5},\) \(M_{5}, P_{2} E_{5}\)) are zero. That is

\(E _{2} \Rightarrow 0=-J_{1^{\prime}}+J_{2^{\prime}}- J _{5}\) (E11.3.14)

\(M _{1} E _{2} \Rightarrow 0=J_{1}-J_{2}\) (E11.3.15)

\(M_{2} \Rightarrow 0=J_{2}-J_{3}\) (E11.3.16)

\(E _{3} \Rightarrow 0=-J_{3^{\prime}}+J_{4^{\prime}}\) (E11.3.17)

\(M _{2} E _{3} \Rightarrow 0=J_{3}-J_{4}\) (E11.3.18)

\(M _{3} E _{2} \Rightarrow 0=J_{5}=J_{6}\) (E11.3.19)

\(E _{4} \Rightarrow 0=-J_{7^{\prime}}+J_{8^{\prime}}-J_{11}\) (E11.3.20)

\(M _{3} E _{4} \Rightarrow 0=J_{7}-J_{8}\) (E11.3.21)

\(M_{4} \Rightarrow 0=J_{8}-J_{9}\) (E11.3.22)

\(P _{1} E _{4} \Rightarrow 0=J_{10}=J_{11}\) (E11.3.23)

\(E _{5} \Rightarrow 0=-J_{12^{\prime}}+J_{13^{\prime}}-J_{16}\) (E11.3.24)

\(M_{3} E_{5} \Rightarrow 0=J_{12}-J_{13}\) (E11.3.25)

\(M_{5} \Rightarrow 0=J_{13}-J_{14}\) (E11.3.26)

\(P _{2} E _{5} \Rightarrow 0=J_{15}-J_{16}\) (E11.3.27)

The species and enzyme balances apply

\(\left[M_{1}\right]_{T}=\left[M_{1}\right]+\left[M_{1} E_{2}\right]\) (E11.3.28)

\(\left[M_{2}\right]_{T}=\left[M_{2}\right]+\left[M_{2} E_{3}\right]\) (E11.3.29)

\(\left[ M _{3}\right]_{T}=\left[ M _{3}\right]+\left[ M _{3} E _{2}\right]+\left[ M _{3} E _{4}\right]+\left[ M _{3} E _{5}\right]\) (E11.3.30)

\(\left[M_{4}\right]_{T}=\left[M_{4}\right]\) (E11.3.31)

\(\left[M_{5}\right]_{T}=\left[M_{5}\right]\) (E11.3.32)

\(\left[ P _{1}\right]_{T}=\left[ P _{1}\right]+\left[ P _{1} E _{4}\right]\) (E11.3.33)

\(\left[ P _{2}\right]_{ T }=\left[ P _{2}\right]+\left[ P _{2} E _{5}\right]\) (E11.3.34)

\(\left[ E _{2}\right]_{ T }=\left[ E _{2}\right]+\left[ M _{1} E _{2}\right]+\left[ M _{3} E _{2}\right]\) (E11.3.35)

\(\left[E_{3}\right]_{T}=\left[E_{3}\right]+\left[M_{2} E_{3}\right]\) (E11.3.36)

\(\left[E_{4}\right]_{T}=\left[E_{4}\right]+\left[M_{4} E_{4}\right]+\left[P_{1} E_{4}\right]\) (E11.3.37)

\(\left[ E _{5}\right]_{ T }=\left[ E _{5}\right]+\left[M_{5} E _{5}\right]+\left[ P _{2} E _{5}\right]\) (E11.3.38)

Further solution of these Eqs. render identical solutions to the problem (for the intermediates) as compared with that from the reaction rate approach as shown in . The fluxes can be obtained as

\(J_{1}=J_{2}=k_{1}\left[M_{1}\right]\left[E_{2}\right]-k_{-1}\left[M_{1} E_{2}\right]=k_{1 c}\left[M_{1} E_{2}\right]=k_{1 c} K_{1}^{-1}\left[M_{1}\right]\left[E_{2}\right] =\frac{k_{1 c}\left[M_{1}\right]\left[E_{2}\right]_{T}}{K_{1}+K_{1} K_{f 1}^{-1}\left[M_{3}\right]+\left[M_{1}\right]}\) (E11.3.39)

\(J_{4}=J_{3}=k_{2}\left[M_{2}\right]\left[E_{3}\right]-k_{-2}\left[M_{2} E_{3}\right]=k_{2 c}\left[M_{2} E_{3}\right]=k_{2 c} K_{2}^{-1}\left[M_{2}\right]\left[E_{3}\right] =\frac{k_{2 c}\left[M_{2}\right]\left[E_{3}\right]_{T}}{K_{2}+\left[M_{2}\right]}\) (E11.3.40)

\(J_{8}=J_{7}=k_{3 c}\left[M_{3} E_{4}\right]=k_{3 c} K_{3}^{-1}\left[M_{3}\right]\left[E_{4}\right] =\frac{k_{3 c}\left[M_{3}\right]\left[E_{4}\right]_{T}}{K_{3}+K_{3} K_{f 2}^{-1}\left[P_{1}\right]+\left[M_{3}\right]}\) (E11.3.41)

\(J_{9}=k_{4 c}\left[M_{4}\right]=J_{8}=J_{7}\) (E11.3.42)

\(J_{13}=J_{12}=\frac{k_{5 c}\left[M_{3}\right]\left[E_{5}\right]_{T}}{K_{5}+K_{5} K_{f 3}^{-1}\left[P_{2}\right]+\left[M_{3}\right]}\) (E11.3.43)

\(J_{14}=k_{6 c }\left[M_{5}\right]=J_{13}=J_{12}\) (E11.3.44)

where the upper case K’s are defined the same by Eqs. (E11.2.51) and (E11.2.52). That is,

\(K_{i}=\frac{k_{-i}+k_{i c}}{k_{i}} \quad i=1,2,3, \text { and } 5\) (E11.3.45)

and

\(K_{f i}=\frac{k_{-f i}}{k_{f i}} \quad i=1,2,3\) (E11.3.46)

Keeping the same notation with reaction rate expressions allows us to see the connections between reaction expressions and the flux expressions.

Therefore, the fluxes can be written based on simplified pathways as one usually does. The simplified pathway is showing in Fig. E11.3.2, which is identical to Fig. 11.15C, with each path numbered. The fluxes for each path can be written quite easily based on this example.

From Eq. (E11.3.39),

\(J_{I}=\frac{k_{1 c}\left[M_{1}\right]\left[E_{2}\right]_{T}}{K_{1}+K_{1} K_{f 1}^{-1}\left[M_{3}\right]+\left[M_{1}\right]}\) (E11.3.47)

From Eq. (E11.3.40),

\(J_{I I}=\frac{k_{2 c}\left[M_{2}\right]\left[E_{3}\right]_{T}}{K_{2}+\left[M_{2}\right]}\) (E11.3.48)

From Eq. (E11.3.41),

\(J_{I I I}=\frac{k_{3 c}\left[M_{3}\right]\left[E_{4}\right]_{T}}{K_{3}+K_{3} K_{f 2}^{-1}\left[P_{1}\right]+\left[M_{3}\right]}\) (E11.3.49)

From Eq. (E11.3.42),

\(J_{ IV }=k_{4 c }\left[ M _{4}\right]\) (E11.3.50)

From Eq. (E11.3.43),

\(J_{V}=\frac{k_{5 c}\left[M_{3}\right]\left[E_{5}\right]_{T}}{K_{5}+K_{5} K_{f 3}^{-1}\left[P_{2}\right]+\left[M_{3}\right]}\) (E11.3.51)

From Eq. (E11.3.44),

\(J_{ VI }=k_{6 c }\left[ M _{5}\right]\) (E11.3.52)
