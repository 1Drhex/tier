Guest Question.

Beam design initiating with load balancing. A post-tensioned beam is to be designed to carry a uniformly distributed load over a 30 ft span, as shown in Fig. 22.21. In addition to its own weight, it must carry a dead load of 150 lb/ft and a service live load of 600 lb/ft. Concrete strength of 4000 psi will be attained at 28 days; at the time of transfer of the prestress force, the strength will be 3000 psi. Prestress loss may be assumed at 20 percent of \(P_i\). On the basis that about one-quarter of the live load will be sustained over a substantial time period, \(k_b\) of 0.25 will be used in determining the balanced load.

Admin
Solution

On the basis of an arbitrarily chosen span-depth ratio of 18, a 20 in. deep, 10 in. wide trial section is selected. The calculated self-weight of the beam is 208 lb/ft, and the selected load to be balanced is

\(w_{bal} = w_o + w_d + k_b w_l\) = 208 + 150 + 150 = 508 lb/ft

Based on a minimum concrete cover from the steel centroid to the bottom face of the beam of 4 in., the maximum eccentricity that can be used for the 20 in. trial section is 6 in. A parabolic tendon will be used to produce a uniformly distributed upward tendon load. To equilibrate the sustained downward loading, the prestress force \(P_e\) after losses, from Eq. (22.33), should be

\(w_p =\frac{8 P y}{ℓ^2}\)

\(P_e = \frac{w_{bal} ℓ^2}{8 y} = \frac{508 \times 900}{8 \times 0.5} = 114,000\) lb

and the corresponding initial prestress force is
\(P_i = \frac{P_e}{R} = \frac{114,000}{0.8} = 143,000\) lb

For the balanced load stage, the concrete will be subjected to a uniform compressive stress of
\(f_{bal} = \frac{114,000}{200} = − 570\) psi

as shown in Fig. 22.21b. Should the partial live load of 150 lb/ft be removed, the stresses to be superimposed on \(f_{bal}\) result from a net upward load of 150 lb/ft. The section modulus for the trial beam is 667 \(in^3\) and

\(M_{unbal} = 150 \times \frac{900}{8} = 16,900\) ft-lb

Hence, the unbalanced bending stresses at the top and bottom faces are
\(f_{unbal} = 16,900 \times \frac{12}{667} = 304\) psi

Thus, the net stresses are
\(f_1\) = − 570 + 304 = − 266 psi
\(f_2\) = − 570 − 304 = − 874 psi

Similarly, if the full live load should act, the stresses to be superimposed are those resulting from a net downward load of 450 lb/ft. The resulting stresses in the concrete at full service load are
\(f_1\) = − 570 − 910 = − 1480 psi
\(f_2\) = − 570 + 910 = + 340 psi

Stresses in the concrete with live load absent and live load fully in place are shown in Fig. 22.21b.
It is also necessary to investigate the stresses in the initial unloaded stage, when the member is subjected to \(P_i\) plus moment due to its own weight.

\(M_o = 208 \times \frac{900}{8} = 23,400\) ft-lb

Hence, in the initial stage:

\(f_1=-\frac{143,000}{200}\left(1-\frac{6\times10}{33.35} \right)-\frac{23,400\times12}{667}=+150\) psi

\(f_2=-\frac{143,000}{200}\left(1+\frac{6\times10}{33.35} \right)+\frac{23,400\times12}{667}=−1580\) psi

The stresses in the unloaded and full service load stages must be checked against these permitted by the ACI Code. With \(f_c^\prime\) = 4000 psi and \(f_{ci}^\prime\) = 3000 psi, the stresses permitted for a Class U member are
\(f_{ti} = + 165 psi f_{ts} = + 474 psi\)

\(f_{ci} = − 1800 psi f_{cs} = − 2400 psi\)

The actual stresses, shown in Fig. 22.21b, are within these limits and acceptably close, and no revision will be made in the trial \(10 \times 20\) in. cross section on the basis of stress limits.

The flexural strength of the members must now be checked, to ensure that an adequate margin of safety against collapse has been provided. The required \(P_i\) of 143,000 lb will be provided using Grade 270 strand, with \(f_{pu}\) = 270,000 psi and \(f_{py}\) = 243,000 psi. Referring to Section 22.4, the initial stress immediately after transfer must not exceed \(0.74 \times 270,000\) = 200,000 psi, or \(0.82 \times 243,000\) = 199,000 psi, which controls in this case. Accordingly, the required area of tendon steel is

\(A_{ps} = 143,000/199,000 = 0.72 in^2\)

This will be provided using five \(\frac{1}{2}\)-in. strands, giving an actual area of 0.765 \(in^2\) (Table A.15). The resulting stresses at the initial and final stages are
\(f_{pi} = \frac{143,000}{0.765} = 187,000\) psi

\(f_{pe} = \frac{114,000}{0.765} = 149,000\) psi

Using the ACI approximate equation for steel stress at failure [see Eq. (22.6)], with \(ρ_p\) = 0.765/160 = 0.0048 and \(\gamma_p\) = 0.40 for the ordinary Grade 270 tendons, the stress \(f_{ps}\) is given by

\(f_{ps}=f_{pu}\left(1-\frac{\gamma_p}{\beta_1}\frac{\rho_p f_{pu}}{f_c^\prime} \right) \)

 

\(=270\left(1-\frac{0.40}{0.85}\frac{0.0048\times270}{4} \right) \)

= 229 ksi

Then
\(a = \frac{A_{ps} f_{ps}}{0.85 f_c^\prime b}\)

\(= \frac{0.765 \times 229}{0.85 \times 4 \times 10} = 5.15\) in.

c = \( \frac{5.15}{0.85}\) = 6.06
\(\frac{c}{d_t} = \frac{6.06}{16}\) = 0.379

This exceeds \(c/d_t\) = 0.375, reducing ϕ to 0.89. The nominal flexural strength is

\(M_n=A_{ps}f_{ps}\left(d-\frac{a}{2}\right)=0.765 \times 229,000 \left(16-\frac{5.15}{2}\right)\frac{1}{12} \)

= 196,000 ft-lb
and the design strength with ϕ = 0.89 is
\(ϕ M_n = 0.89 \times 196,000\) = 174,000 ft-lb

It will be recalled that the ACI load factors with respect to dead and live loads are, respectively, 1.2 and 1.6. Calculating the factored load,
\(w_u = 1.2(208 + 150) + 1.6(600)\) = 1390 lb/ft
\(M_u = \frac{1390(900)}{8}\) = 156,000 ft-lb
Thus, \(ϕ M_n \gt M_u\), and the design is judged satisfactory.
