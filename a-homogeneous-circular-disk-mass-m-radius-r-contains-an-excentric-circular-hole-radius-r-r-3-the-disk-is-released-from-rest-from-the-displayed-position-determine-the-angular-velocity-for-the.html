Guest Question.

A homogeneous circular disk (mass \(m\), radius \(R\)) contains an excentric circular hole (radius \(r = R/3\) ). The disk is released from rest from the displayed position.

Determine the angular velocity for the instant, when \(B\) reaches its highest position

a) if the base is ideally frictionless,

b) if the disk rolls on the rough base.

Admin
Solution

The solution is found by using the energy conservation law. For that purpose, we need the position of the center of mass and and the mass moment of inertia. It is useful to determine them by calculating the difference of two circular disks:

① : \(A_{1}=\pi R^{2} \) , \(m_{1}=\frac{R^{2}\pi }{R^{2}\pi-r^{2}\pi}m=\frac{9}{8}m \) , \(\Theta _{1}=\frac{1}{2}m_{1}R^{2} \) ,

② : \(A_{2}=\pi r^{2} \) , \(m_{2}=\frac{r^{2}\pi }{R^{2}\pi-r^{2}\pi}m=\frac{1}{8}m \) , \(\Theta _{2}=\frac{1}{2}m_{2}r^{2}\) .

This leads to

\(x_{c}=\frac{0\cdot \pi R^{2}-r\cdot \pi r^{2} }{\pi R^{2}-\pi r^{2} } =-\frac{R}{24} \) , \(z_{c}=0 \) ,

\(\Theta _{c}= \left[\Theta _{1}+m_{1}x^{2}_{c} \right]-\left[\Theta _{2}+m_{2}\left(x_{c}+r \right)^{2} \right] \)

 

\(=\frac{9}{16}mR^{2}+\frac{9}{8\left(24\right)^{2} } mR^{2}-\frac{1}{16\cdot 9}mR^{2} \)

 

\(-\frac{m}{8} \left(\frac{R}{24}+\frac{R}{3} \right)^{2} \)

 

\(=\frac{311}{576}mR^{2}\) .

a) In the initial state, the disk is at rest and, if the zero level of potential energy is chosen at level of A, we have

\(V_{0}=0\) , \(T_{0}=0\) .

Because the base is frictionless, there act no forces in horizontal direction. Therefore, the center of mass \(C\) experiences no velocity change in this direction, i.e. \(\dot{x}_{c}=0 \) . When B reaches its highest position, \(C\) just reaches its lowest position. In vertical direction, the velocities of these points change at that instant their sign, and consequently, \(\dot{z}_{c}=0 \) holds. Thus, the energies at that instant are

\(V_{1}=-mg\left|x_{c} \right| \) , \(T_{1}=\frac{1}{2}\Theta _{c}\omega ^{2} \)

and from the energy conservation law

\(V_{1}+T_{1}=V_{0}+T_{0} \) ,

it follows

\(\frac{1}{2}\Theta _{c}\omega ^{2}=mg\left|x_{s} \right| \rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{\omega =\pm \sqrt{\frac{2mg\left|x_{c} \right| }{\Theta _{c} } }=\pm \sqrt{\frac{48g}{311R} } } } \) .

b) At the initial state, we have as before

\(V_{0}=0 \) , \(T_{0}=0\) .

When the disk is rolling, the instantaneous center of rotation Π is located at the contact point between disk and base. When the center of mass reaches its lowest position (and \(B\) its highest position), its velocity has the horizontal direction and the magnitude \(\dot{x}_{s}=\omega \left(R-\left|x_{s} \right| \right) \) . Therewith, the energies at this moment are given by

\(V_{1}=-mg\left|x_{c} \right| \) , \(T_{1}=\frac{1}{2} m\dot{x}_{c} +\frac{1}{2}\Theta _{c}\omega ^{2} \) ,

and the energy conservation law leads to

\(\frac{1}{2}\omega ^{2}\left[m\left(R-\left|x_{c} \right| \right)^{2}+\Theta _{c} \right] =mg\left|x_{c} \right| \)

and finally to

\(\underline{\underline{\omega =\pm \sqrt{\frac{2mg\left|x_{c} \right| }{m\left(R-\left|x_{c} \right| \right)^{2}+\Theta _{c} } }=\pm \sqrt{\frac{2g}{35R} } } } \) .

Remark: The angular velocity in case a) is higher than in case b). The reason for that lies in the fact that the kinetic energy in case b) is split into translational and the rotational parts, while in case a), there exists only a rotational part.
