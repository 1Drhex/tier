Guest Question.

Assume the condenser of a large power plant to be a shell and tube type heat exchanger consisting of a single shell and 30,000 tubes, each executing two passes. The tubes are of thin wall constructed with 25 mm in diameter, and steam condenses on their outer surface with an associated convection coefficient of \(11,000 W/m^2.K.\) The heat transfer rate that must be effected by exchanger is 2000 MW and this is accomplished by cooling water through the tubes at the rate of 30,000 kg/s. The water enters at 20°C, while the steam condenses at 50°C. What is the temperature of cooling water coming out the condenser ? What is the required tube length per pass ?

 

Admin
Solution

Given : A shell and tube type condenser of a power plant ;
No. of shell = 1,
No. of tubes, N = 30,000
No. of passes, p = 2,
d = 25 mm, Q = 2000 MW
Cold fluid Hot fluid
\(\dot{m}_c = 30,000 kg/s, h_o = 11,000 W/m^2.K\)
\(T_{c, i} = 20°C, T_h = 50°C.\)
To find :
(i) Outlet temperature of the cooling water.
(ii) Tube length per pass.
Schematic :
Assumptions :
(i) Assuming no heat loss to the surroundings.
(ii) Negligible thermal resistance of tube material and fouling effects.
(iii) Constant properties.
(iv) Fully developed flow through tubes.
(v) Specific heat of water as 4180 J/kg.K.
Properties : Properties of water at Tm ≈ 300 K
\(C_p = 4180 J/kg.K,\)
\(µ = 855 × 10^{–6} Ns/m^2,\)
\(k_f = 0.613 W/m.K,\)
Pr = 5.83.
Analysis : (i) The heat transfer rate through heat exchanger
\(Q =\dot{m}_c C_{p, c} (T_{c, o} – T_{c, i})\)
\(2000 × 10^6 W = (30,000 kg/s) × (4180 J/kg.K) × (T_{c, o} – 20)\)(K or °C)
or \(T_{c, o} = 36.0°C.\)
(ii) The LMTD for counter flow arrangement
\(∆T_1 =T_{h, i} – T_{c, o} = 50 – 36 = 14°C\)
\(∆T_2 =T_{h, o} – T_{c, i} = 50 – 20 = 30°C\)
\(∆T_{lm , counter} = \frac{∆T_1 – ∆T_2}{ln\left(\frac{∆T_1}{∆T_2} \right)}= \frac{14 – 30}{ln\left(\frac{14}{30} \right)} = 21°C\)
The correction factor F can be obtained from Fig. (a), where
\(P =\frac{t_2 – t_1}{T_2 – t_1}=\frac{36 – 20}{50 – 20} = 0.466\)
and \(R =\frac{T_1 – T_2}{t_2 – t_1}=\frac{50 – 50}{36 – 20} = 0\)
Hence the correction factor F = 1, and the corrected LMTD
\(∆T_{lm} = F ∆T_{lm, counter} = 1 × 21 = 21°C\)
Since the heat transfer coefficient on waterside is not given, thus its calculation is required to determine overall heat transfer coefficient.
The mass flow rate per tube,
\(\dot{m}_{tube} = \frac{30,000 (kg/s)}{30,000 tubes} = 1 kg/s \)per tube
Reynolds number
\(Re =\frac{\dot{m}_{tube}}{\pi d µ}= \frac{4 × 1}{\pi × 0.025 × 855 × 10^{-6}}\)
= 59,567
Using relation,
\(Nu = 0.023 Re^{0.8} Pr^{0.4}\)
or \(h_i = 0.023 × \frac{k_f}{d} × Re^{0.8} Pr^{0.4}\)
\(= \frac{0.023 × 0.613}{0.025}× (59,567)^{0.8} × (5.83)^{0.4}\)
\(= 7552 W/m^2.K\)
The overall heat transfer coefficient ;
\(U_o = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{h_i} + \frac{1}{h_o}} = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{7552} + \frac{1}{11000}}\)
\(= 4478 W/m^2.K\)
The length of heat exchanger ;
\(Q = U_o A_o ∆T_{lm}\)
or \(A_o = \frac{2000 × 10^6}{4478 × 21}= 21,268 m^2\)
The length of each pipe ;
\(A_o = π d L p N\)
\(L =\frac{21,268}{ π × 0.025 × 2 × 30,000} = 4.51 m. \)
The length of each tube per pass is 4.51 m.
