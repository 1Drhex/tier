Guest Question.

A solid 1.50-in.-diameter shaft is subjected to a torque of T = 225 lb-ft and an axial load of P = 5,500 lb, as shown in Figure P15.1/2.
(a) Determine the principal stresses and the maximum shear stress at point H on the surface of the shaft.
(b) Show the stresses of part (a) and their directions on an appropriate sketch.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&A=\frac{\pi}{4}(1.5 in .)^{2}=1.76715 in .^{2} \\&J=\frac{\pi}{32}(1.5 in .)^{4}=0.49701 in .^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Normal and shear stress magnitudes:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma &=\frac{P}{A}=\frac{5,500 lb }{1.76715 in .^{2}}=3.112 .363 ksi ( C ) \\\tau &=\frac{T c}{J}=\frac{(225 lb – ft )(1.5 in . / 2)(12 in. / ft )}{0.49701 in. ^{4}}=4,074.367 psi \quad \text { (sense determined by inspection) }\end{aligned}\)

Principal stress calculations:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq. (12.12):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}}{2}\right)^{2}+\tau_{x y}^{2}} \\&=\frac{(-3,112.363)+(0)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(-3,112.363)-(0)}{2}\right)^{2}+(-4,074.367)^{2}} \\&=-1,556.182 \pm 4,361.441\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{p 1}=2,810 psi \text { and } \sigma_{p 2}=-5,920 psi \\&\tau_{\max }=4,360 psi \quad \text { (maximum in-plane shear stress) } \\&\sigma_{\text {avg }}=1,556 psi ( C ) \quad \text { (normal stress on planes of maximum in-plane shear stress) }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan 2 \theta_{p} &=\frac{\tau_{x y}}{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) / 2}=\frac{-4,074.367}{[(-3,112.363)-(0)] / 2}=\frac{-4,074.367}{-1,556.182}=2.6182 \\&\left.\therefore \theta_{p}=34.55^{\circ} \quad \text { (counterclockwise from the } x \text { axis to the direction of } \sigma_{p 2}\right)\end{aligned}\)
