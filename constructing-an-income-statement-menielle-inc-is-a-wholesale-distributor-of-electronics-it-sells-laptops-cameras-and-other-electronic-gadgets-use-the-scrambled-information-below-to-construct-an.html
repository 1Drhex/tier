Constructing an Income Statement
Menielle, Inc. is a wholesale distributor of electronics. It sells laptops, cameras, and other electronic gadgets. Use the scrambled information below to construct an income statement, along with a common-sized income statement. Also calculate the firm’s earnings per share and dividends per share.

 

Interest expense
Cost of goods sold
Selling and marketing expenses
Administrative expenses
Number of shares outstanding 		$ 35,000
$150,000
$ 40,000
$ 30,000
20,000		 Sales
Common stock dividends
Income taxes
Depreciation expense 		$400,000
$ 15,000
$ 40,000
$ 20,000

Solution

Step 1: Formulate a Solution Strategy
The following template provides the format of the income statement:

 

Sales
Less: Cost of goods sold
Equals: Gross profit
Less: Operating expenses (Sellingand marketing expenses + General and administrative expenses + Depreciation expense)
Equals: Operating income
Less: Interest expense
Equals: Earnings before taxes
Less: Income taxes
Equals: Net income

 

Step 2: Crunch the numbers

Your results should be as follows:

 

Dollars Percentage of Sales
Sales $400,000 100.0%
Cost ofgoods sold (150,000) (37.5%)
Gross profit $250,000 62.5%
Operating expenses:
Selling and marketing expenses ($ 40,000) (10.0%)
Administrative expense (30,000) (7.5%)
Depreciation expenses (20,000) (5.0%)
Total operating expenses ($ 90,000) (22.5%)
Operating income $ 160,000 40.0%
Interest expense (35,000) (8.8%)
Earnings before tax $125,000 31.3%
Income taxes (40,000) (10.0%)
Net income $ 85,000 21.3%
Earnings per share ($85,000 net income ÷ 20,000 shares) $4.25
Dividendes per share ($15,000 dividends ÷ 20,000 shares) $0.75

 

Step 3: analyze Your results

We can make some important observations about Menielle’s income statement. First, the firm is profitable, earning a net income of $85,000, or $4.25 on a per share basis and then paying $15,000 in dividends to the shareholders, or $0.75 per share. Also, for every $100 of sales, Menielle earned $62.5 in gross profits, $40 in operating income, and $21.3 in net income. Finally, the dividends/earnings ratio is $15,000 , $85,000 = 17.6%, indicating that the firm is retaining most of its earnings to grow the firm.
