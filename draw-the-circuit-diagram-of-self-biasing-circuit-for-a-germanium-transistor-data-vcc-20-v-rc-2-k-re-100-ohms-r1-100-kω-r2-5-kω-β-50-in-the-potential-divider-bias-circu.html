Guest Question.

Draw the circuit diagram of self-biasing circuit for a germanium transistor. Data: \(V_{CC} = 20 V; R_C = 2 K; R_E =100 ohms. R_1= 100 K\Omega ; R_2 = 5 K\Omega ; \beta = 50\).

In the potential divider bias circuit of Fig. 1, \(V_{CC} \) in association with \(R_1\) and \(R_2\) provide the bias and \(R_E\) provides bias stability as is explained.

Determination of \(I_{CQ}\) and \(I_{CE.Q}\) and stability factor S

Admin
Solution

Stability factor \(S=\frac{(1+\beta )\left[1+\frac{R_B}{R_E} \right] }{(1+\beta )+\frac{R_B}{R_E}} \) (1)
\(\beta = 50; R_B = R_1\parallel R_2 = 100 K\parallel 5 K = 4.76 K \Omega \)
∴ S=\((51)\frac{\left[1+\frac{4.76\times 10^3}{10^2} \right] }{51+\frac{4.76\times 10^3}{10^2}}=\frac{(51)\times 48.6}{98.6}=25.13\cong 25 \)

Voltage across \(R_2=V_{R2}=\frac{V_{CC}\times R_2}{\left[R_1+R_2\right] }=\frac{20\times 5\times 10^3}{\left[100+5\right]\times 10^3 } \cong 0.95 \)volt.
∴ voltage \(V_{RE}\) across \(R_E = V_{R2} – V_{BE}= 0.95 – 0.3 = 0.65 V (using V_{BE} = 0.3 V)\)
∴ \(I_E \cong I_C =\frac{V_{RE}}{R_E} = 6.5 m A\) ∴ \( I_{CQ} = 6.5 m A\)
Collector to Emitter Voltage \(V_{CEQ}=\left[V_{CC}-I_{C.Q}.(R_C+R_E)\right] \)

\(V_{CEQ}=\left[20-6.5\times 10^{-3}(2\times 10^3+0.1\times 10^3)\right]=\left[20-13.65\right]=6.53 \) volts
