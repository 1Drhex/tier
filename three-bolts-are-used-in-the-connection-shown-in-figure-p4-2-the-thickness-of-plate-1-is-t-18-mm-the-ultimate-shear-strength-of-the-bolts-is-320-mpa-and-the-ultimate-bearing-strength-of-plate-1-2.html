Guest Question.

Three bolts are used in the connection shown in Figure P4.2. The thickness of plate (1) is t = 18 mm. The ultimate shear strength of the bolts is 320 MPa and the ultimate bearing strength of plate (1) is 350 MPa. Determine the minimum bolt diameter required to support an applied load of P = 180 kN if a minimum factor of safety of 2.5 is required with respect to both bolt shear and plate bearing failure.

Admin
Solution

Three bolts support the load; therefore, the force acting on each bolt is

\(F_{\text {bolt }}=\frac{180 kN }{3}=60 kN / bolt\)

Bolt Shear: The allowable shear stress for the bolts is

\(\tau_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\tau_{U}}{ FS }=\frac{320 MPa }{25}=128 MPa\)

Each bolt acts in double shear; therefore, two cross-sectional surfaces on each bolt are subjected to shear stress. The shear force on each surface is thus

\(V_{\text {bolt }}=\frac{F_{\text {bolt }}}{2 \text { surfaces }}=\frac{60 kN / bolt }{2 \text { surfaces/bolt }}=30 kN / surface\)

The bolt cross-sectional area required to satisfy the allowable shear stress limit must equal or exceed:

\(A_{\text {bolt }} \geq \frac{V_{\text {bolt }}}{\tau_{\text {allow }}}=\frac{30,000 N }{128 N / mm ^{2}}=234.375 mm ^{2}\)

The minimum bolt diameter is thus:

\(\frac{\pi}{4} d_{\text {bolt }}^{2} \geq 234.375 mm ^{2} \quad \therefore d_{\text {bolt }} \geq 17.275 mm\)

Plate Bearing: The allowable bearing stress of the plate is:

\(\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{350 MPa }{2.5}=140 MPa\)

Since the force on each bolt is 60 kN, the projected area (i.e., the product of the plate thickness and the bolt diameter) required to satisfy the allowable bearing stress limit is

\(A_{ b }=\frac{60,000 N }{140 N / mm ^{2}}=428.571 mm ^{2}\)

The plate has a thickness of t = 18 mm; thus, the bolt diameter must equal or exceed:

\(d_{\text {bolt }} \geq \frac{428.571 mm ^{2}}{18 mm }=23.810 mm\)

The minimum bolt diameter required to satisfy both bolt shear and plate bearing failure is thus

\(d_{\text {bolt }} \geq 23.8 mm\)
