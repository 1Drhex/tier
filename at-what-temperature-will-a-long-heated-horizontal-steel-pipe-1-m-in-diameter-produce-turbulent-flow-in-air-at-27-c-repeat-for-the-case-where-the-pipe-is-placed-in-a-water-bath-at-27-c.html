Guest Question.

At what temperature will a long, heated, horizontal steel pipe 1 m in diameter produce turbulent flow in air at 27 °C ? Repeat for the case where the pipe is placed in a water bath at 27 °C. Use property values at 27 °C.

Admin
Solution

The criterion for transition is \(Ra_D = 10^9\). For air at 27 °C, this gives

\(Ra_{D}=\frac{(9.8 m/s^2)(300 K)^{-1}(\Delta T)(1 m)^3(0.71)}{(1.64 \times 10^{-5}m^2/s)^2} = 10^{9}\)

Therefore,

ΔT = 12°C

\(T_{pipe} = 12 + 27 = 39°C\)
