Guest Question.

Consider the following form of the governing equations of the classical plate theory:
\(-\left(\frac{\partial^{2} M_{x x}}{\partial x^{2}}-4 D_{66} \frac{\partial^{4} w}{\partial x^{2} \partial y^{2}}+\frac{\partial^{2} M_{y y}}{\partial y^{2}}\right)=q\) (a)
\(\frac{\partial^{2} w}{\partial x^{2}}=-\left(\bar{D}_{22} M_{x x}+\bar{D}_{12} M_{y y}\right)\),
\(\frac{\partial^{2} w}{\partial y^{2}}=-\left(\bar{D}_{12} M_{x x}+\bar{D}_{11} M_{y y}\right)\) (b)
where \(M_{xx}\) and \(M_{yy}\) are the bending moments, w is the transverse deflection, q is the distributed load, ν is the Poisson ratio, and
\(\bar{D}_{i j}=\frac{D_{i j}}{D_{0}}, \quad D_{0}=D_{11} D_{22}-D_{12}^{2}\)
(a) Gove the weak form of the equations, and (b) assume approximation of the form
\(w=\sum_{i=1}^{4} w_{i} \psi_{i}^{1}, \quad M_{x x}=\sum_{i=1}^{2} M_{x i} \psi_{i}^{2}, \quad M_{y y}=\sum_{i=1}^{2} M_{y i} \psi_{j}^{3}\)
to develop the (mixed) finite element model in the form
\(\left[\begin{array}{cc}{\left[K^{11}\right]} & {\left[K^{12}\right]} & {\left[K^{13}\right]} \\& {\left[K^{22}\right]} & {\left[K^{23}\right]} \\\operatorname{symm} . & {\left[K^{33}\right]}\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{c}\{w\} \\\left\{M_{x x}\right\} \\\left\{M_{y y}\right\}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{l}\left\{F^{1}\right\} \\\left\{F^{2}\right\} \\\left\{F^{3}\right\}\end{array}\right\}\)
Comment on the choice of the functions \(\psi_{i}^{\alpha} \text { for } \alpha=1,2,3\).

Admin
Solution

The weak forms of Eqs. (a) and (b) over a typical element \(\Omega _{e}\) are

\(0=\int_{\Omega_{e}}\left(\frac{\partial \delta w}{\partial x} \frac{\partial M_{x x}}{\partial x}+\frac{\partial \delta w}{\partial y} \frac{\partial M_{y y}}{\partial y}+4 D_{66} \frac{\partial^{2} \delta w}{\partial x \partial y} \frac{\partial^{2} w}{\partial x \partial y}-q \delta w\right) d x d y\)

\(-\oint_{\Gamma_{e}}\left[\delta w \bar{Q}_{n}-2 D_{66} \frac{\partial^{2} w}{\partial x \partial y}\left(\delta \theta_{x} n_{y}+\delta \theta_{y} n_{x}\right)\right] d s\) (c)

\(0=\int_{\Omega_{e}}\left[\frac{\partial w}{\partial x} \frac{\partial \delta M_{x x}}{\partial x}-\delta M_{x x}\left(\bar{D}_{22} M_{x x}-\bar{D}_{12} M_{y y}\right)\right] d x d y\)

\(-\oint_{\Gamma_{e}} \delta M_{x x} \theta_{x} n_{x} d s\) (d)

\(0=\int_{\Omega_{e}}\left[\frac{\partial w}{\partial y} \frac{\partial \delta M_{y y}}{\partial y}-\delta M_{y y}\left(\bar{D}_{11} M_{y y}-\bar{D}_{12} M_{x x}\right)\right] d x d y\)

\(-\oint_{\Gamma_{e}} \delta M_{y y} \theta_{y} n_{y} d s\) (e)

The primary and secondary variables of the formulation are

\(w, M_{x x}, M_{y y}\) (f)

\(V_{n}, \quad \theta_{x} n_{x} \equiv \frac{\partial w}{\partial x} n_{x}, \quad \theta_{y} n_{y} \equiv \frac{\partial w}{\partial y} n_{y}\) (g)

where \(V_{n}\) is the effective shear force (Kirchhoff free edge condition)

\(V_{n}=Q_{n}+\frac{\partial M_{n s}}{\partial s}, \quad Q_{n}=Q_{x} n_{x}+Q_{y} n_{y}\) (h)

The finite element model of Eq. (d) and (e) is obtained by substituting the approximations of the form

\(w=\sum_{i=1}^{r} w_{i} \psi_{i}^{(1)}, \quad M_{x x}=\sum_{i=1}^{s} M_{x i} \psi_{i}^{(2)}, \quad M_{y y}=\sum_{i=1}^{p} M_{y i} \psi_{i}^{(3)}\) (i)

where \(\psi_{i}^{(\alpha)},(\alpha=1,2,3,4)\) are appropriate interpolation functions. We obtain

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc}{\left[K^{11}\right]} & {\left[K^{12}\right]} & {\left[K^{13}\right]} \\& {\left[K^{22}\right]} & {\left[K^{23}\right]} \\\operatorname{symm} . & {\left[K^{33}\right]}\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{c}\{w\} \\\left\{M_{x}\right\} \\\left\{M_{y}\right\}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{l}\left\{F^{1}\right\} \\\left\{F^{2}\right\} \\\left\{F^{3}\right\}\end{array}\right\}\) (j)

where

\(K_{i j}^{11}=4 D_{66} \int_{\Omega_{e}} \frac{\partial^{2} \psi_{i}^{1}}{\partial x \partial y} \frac{\partial^{2} \psi_{i}^{1}}{\partial x \partial y} d x d y, \quad i, j=1,2, \ldots, r\)

 

\(K_{i j}^{12}=\int_{\Omega_{e}} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{1}}{\partial x} \frac{\partial \psi_{j}^{2}}{\partial x} d x d y, \quad i, j=1,2, \ldots, r ; \quad j=1,2, \ldots, s\)

 

\(K_{i j}^{13}=\int_{\Omega_{e}} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{1}}{\partial y} \frac{\partial \psi_{j}^{3}}{\partial y} d x d y, \quad i, j=1,2, \ldots, r ; \quad j=1,2, \ldots, p\)

 

\(K_{i j}^{22}=\int_{\Omega_{e}}\left(-\bar{D}_{22}\right) \psi_{i}^{2} \psi_{j}^{2} d x d y, \quad i, j=1,2, \ldots, s\)

 

\(K_{i j}^{23}=\int_{\Omega_{e}}\left(-\bar{D}_{12}\right) \psi_{i}^{2} \psi_{j}^{3} d x d y, \quad i=1,2, \ldots, s ; \quad j=1,2, \ldots, p\)

 

\(F_{i}^{1}=\int_{\Omega_{e}} q \psi_{i}^{1} d x d y+\oint_{\Gamma_{e}} V_{n} \psi_{i}^{1} d s, \quad i=1,2, \ldots, r\)

 

\(F_{i}^{2}=\oint_{\Gamma} \theta_{x} n_{x} \psi_{i}^{2} d s, \quad i=1,2, \ldots, s\)

 

\(F_{i}^{3}=\oint_{\Gamma_{e}} \theta_{y} n_{y} \psi_{i}^{3} d s, \quad i=1,2, \ldots, p\)

 

An examination of the weak forms (d) and (e) show that the minimum continuity conditions of the interpolation functions \(\psi_{i}^{\alpha}(\alpha=1,2,3)\) are

\(\psi_{i}^{1}\) = linear in x and linear in y

\(\psi_{i}^{2}\) = linear in x and constant in y

\(\psi_{i}^{3}\) = linear in y and constant in x

\(\psi_{i}^{4}\) = linear in x and linear in y (i)
