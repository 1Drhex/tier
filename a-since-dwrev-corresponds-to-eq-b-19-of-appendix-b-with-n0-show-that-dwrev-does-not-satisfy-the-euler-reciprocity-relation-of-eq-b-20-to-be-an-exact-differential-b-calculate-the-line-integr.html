Guest Question.

a. Since \(dw_{rev} \) corresponds to Eq. (B-19): du=L(x,y,z)dx+M(x,y,z)dy+N(x,y,z)dz , of Appendix B with N=0, show that \(dw_{rev} \) does not satisfy the Euler reciprocity relation of Eq. (B-20):

\((B-20a)\Bigl(\frac{\partial L}{\partial y} \Bigr) _{x,z}=\Bigl(\frac{\partial M}{\partial x} \Bigr) _{y,z} ,\\ (B-20b):\Bigl(\frac{\partial L}{\partial z} \Bigr) _{x,y}=\Bigl(\frac{\partial N}{\partial x} \Bigr) _{y,z} , \\ (B-20c):\Bigl(\frac{\partial M}{\partial z} \Bigr) _{x,y}=\Bigl(\frac{\partial N}{\partial y} \Bigr) _{x,z}\) to be an exact differential.

b. Calculate the line integral of dP for the process of . Show that the integral is path-independent for the two paths of and . For the second path the integral will have to be done in three sections, but in each section only one term of the line integral will be nonzero. For the heating and cooling processes, dV=0 but dT≠0. For the isothermal process, dV≠0 but dT=0.

Admin
Solution

a. \(dw=P_{ext}dV+0=PdV+0\) (reversible processes)

\(\Bigl(\frac{\partial P}{\partial T} \Bigr) _{V}\neq \Bigl(\frac{\partial 0}{\partial V} \Bigr) _{T}=0\)

b. Let 5.000 L be called \(V_{1}\) and 10.00 L be called \(V_{2}\). Let 298.15K be called \(T_{1}\) and 200.00K be called \(T_{2}\).

\(dP=\Bigl(\frac{\partial P}{\partial T} \Bigr) _{V}dT+ \Bigl(\frac{\partial P}{\partial V} \Bigr) _{T}dV=\frac{nR}{V} dT-\frac{nRT}{V^{2}} dV\)

We carry out the three integration steps. For the first step, the second term vanishes. For the second step, the first term vanishes, and for the third step, the second term vanishes.

\(n=(50.00g)(\frac{1mol}{39.948g} )=1.2516mol\)

 

\(\int{dP} =\frac{nR}{V_{1}} \int_{T_{1}}^{T_{2}}{dT} – nRT_{2}\int_{V_{1}}^{V_{2}}{\frac{1}{V^{2}} } +\frac{nR}{V_{2}} \int_{T_{2}}^{T_{1}}{dT}\)

 

\(=\frac{nR}{V_{1}} (T_{2}-T_{1})+nRT_{2}(\frac{1}{V_{2}}-\frac{1}{V_{1}} ) +\frac{nR}{V_{2}} (T_{1}-T_{2})\)

 

\(=(1.2516 mol)(8.3145 JK^{−1} mol^{−1})(\frac{-98.15K}{0.00500m^{3}} )+ (1.2516 mol)(8.3145 JK^{−1} mol^{−1})(200.00 K)(\frac{1}{0.0100m^{3}} -\frac{1}{0.0050m^{3}} )+ (1.2516 mol)(8.3145 JK^{−1} mol^{−1})(\frac{98.15K}{0.01000m^{3}} )\)

 

\(=−2.0428×10^{5}Jm^{3}-2.0813×10^{5}Jm^{3}+1.0214×10^{5}Jm^{3}\)

 

\(=−3.103×10^{5}Jm^{3}=3.062atm\)

This value is the same as in the previous example, showing that the integral of dP is path-independent so far as these two paths are concerned.
