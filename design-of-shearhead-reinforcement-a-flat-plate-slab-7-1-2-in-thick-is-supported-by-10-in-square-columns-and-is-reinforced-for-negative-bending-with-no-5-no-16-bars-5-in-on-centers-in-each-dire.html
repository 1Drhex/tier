Guest Question.

Design of shearhead reinforcement. A flat plate slab \(7\frac{1}{2}\) in. thick is supported by 10 in. square columns and is reinforced for negative bending with No. 5 (No. 16) bars 5 in. on centers in each direction, with an average effective depth d of 6 in. The concrete strength \(f_c^\prime\) is 3000 psi. The slab must transfer a factored shear \(V_u\) of 107,000 lb to the column. What special slab reinforcement is required, if any, at the column to transfer the factored shear?

Admin
Solution

The nominal shear strength at the critical section d/2 from the face of the column is found from Eq. (13.11a) to be

\(V_c=4\lambda \sqrt{f_c^\prime}b_o d\)

\(V_c = 4 \sqrt{3000} \times 64 \times 6\) = 84.1 kips

and \(ϕ V_c = 0.75 \times 84.1\) = 63.1 kips. This is less than \(V_u\) = 107 kips, indicating that shear reinforcement is necessary. A shearhead similar to Fig. 13.20a will be used, fabricated from I-beam sections with \(f_y\) = 50 ksi. Maintaining \(\frac{3}{4}\) in. clearance below such steel, bar clearance at the top of the slab permits use of an I beam with a \(4\frac{5}{8}\) in. depth; a nominal 4 in. section will be used. With such reinforcement, the upper limit of shear \(V_n\) on the critical section is \(7 \sqrt{3000} (64 \times 6)\) = 147 kips, and \(ϕ V_n = 0.75 \times 147\) = 110 kips, above the value of \(V_u\) to be resisted. The required perimeter \(b_o\) can be found by setting \(V_u = ϕ V_c\), where \(V_c\) is given by Eq. (13.11a):

\(b_o=\frac{V_u}{4\phi \sqrt{f_c^\prime}d}=\frac{107,000}{4\times0.75\sqrt{3000}\times6}=109 in. \)

(Note that the actual shear force to be transferred at the critical section is slightly less than 107 kips because a part of the floor load is within the effective perimeter \(b_o\); however, the difference is small except for very large shearheads.) The required projecting length \(ℓ_v\) of the shearhead arm is found from geometry, with \(b_o\) expressed in terms of \(ℓ_v\).

\(b_o=4\sqrt{2}\left[\frac{c_1}{2}+\frac{3}{4}\left(ℓ_v-\frac{c_1}{2}\right)\right]=109 in.\)

from which \(ℓ_v\) = 24.0 in. To determine the required plastic section modulus for the shear arm, it is necessary to assume a trial value of the relative stiffness \(α_v\). After selecting 0.25 for trial, the required moment capacity is found from Eq. (13.14):

\(M_p=\frac{V_u}{2\phi n}\left[h_v+\alpha _v\left(ℓ_v-\frac{c_1}{2} \right) \right]\)

 

\(M_p=\frac{107,000}{8\times0.90}[4+0.25(24.0-5)]=130,000 in-lb\)

A standard I beam \(S4 \times 7.7\), with yield stress of 50 ksi, provides 176,000 in-lb resistance and will tentatively be adopted. The \(E_s I_s\) value provided by the beam is \(174 \times 10^6 in^2-lb\).

The effective cross section of the slab strip is shown in Fig. 13.28. Taking moments of the composite cracked section about the bottom surface to locate the neutral axis gives

\(y=\frac{8.90\times6+19.9\times2.75+8y^2}{8.90+19.9+16y} \)

from which y = 2.29 in. The moment of inertia of the composite section is
\(I_c = \frac{1}{3} \times 16 \times 2.29^3 + 8.90 \times 3.71^2 + 6 \times 9 + 19.9 \times 0.46^2 = 244 in^4\)

the flexural stiffness of the effective composite slab strip is
\(E_c I_c = 3.1 \times 10^6 \times 244 = 756 \times 10^6 in^2 -lb\)

and, from Eq. (13.13),

\(\alpha _v=\frac{E_s I_s}{E_c I_c}\)

 

\(\alpha _v=\frac{174}{756}=0.23 \)

This is greater than the specified minimum of 0.15 and close to the 0.25 value assumed earlier. The revised value of \(M_p\) is

\(M_p=\frac{107,000}{8\times0.90}[4+0.23(24.0-5)]=124,400 in-lb\)

The 4 in. beam is adequate. The calculated length \(ℓ_v\) of 24.0 in. will be retained. The reduction in column-strip moment in the slab may be based on this actual length. From Eq. (13.15),

\(M_v=\frac{\phi \alpha_vV_u}{2n}\left(ℓ_v-\frac{c_1}{2} \right)\)

 

\(M_v=\frac{0.90\times0.23\times107,000}{8}(24-5)=52,600 in-lb\)

This value is less than \(M_p\), as required by specification, and must also be less than 30 percent of the design negative moment in the column strip and less than the change in the column-strip moment in the distance \(ℓ_v\).
