Guest Question.

A coolant (specific gravity \(=1.2\) ) is pumped through a heat exchanger and control valve at a nominal (desired) flow rate of \(q_{d}=0.6 m ^{3} / min\). The total pressure drop over this heat exchanger system (including piping and fittings) is constant at \(450 kPa\). The pressure drop over the system without the control valve, \(\Delta P_{s}\), is proportional to the flow rate squared. Its nominal value at the desired flow rate is \(\Delta P_{s d}=200 kPa\). The control valve is to be designed so that the nominal flow rate is \(2 / 3\) of the maximum flow rate when the valve is completely open. Based on this information, what value of the valve coefficient \(C_{v}\) should be specified?

Admin
Solution

Starting from Eq. 9-7:

\(C_{v}=\frac{q}{N f(\ell) \sqrt{\frac{\Delta P_{v}^{2}}{g_{s}}}}\) (9-7)

\(C_{v}=\frac{q}{N f(l) \sqrt{\frac{\Delta P_{v}}{g_{s}}}}\) (1)

The pressure drop in the valve is:

\(\Delta P_{v}=\Delta P-\Delta P_{s}\) (2)

where

\(\Delta P_{s}=K q^{2}\) (3)

Solve for \(K\) by plugging in the nominal values of \(q\) and \(\Delta P_{s}\). First, convert the nominal value of \(q\) into units of \(m ^{3} / h\) to match the metric units version of \(N\) (the parameter \(N=0.0865 m ^{3} / h ( Kpa )^{1 / 2}\) when \(q\) has units of \(m ^{3} / h\) and pressure has units of \(KPa\) ).

\(\begin{aligned}&q_{d}=0.6 m ^{3} / min =36 m ^{3} / h \\&\Delta P_{s d}=200 kPa \\&K=\frac{\Delta P_{s d}}{q_{d}^{2}}=\frac{200 kPa }{36^{2}\left( m ^{3} / h \right)^{2}}=0.154 kPa /\left( m ^{3} / h \right)^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Now substitute (3) into (2) to get an expression for \(\Delta P_{v}\) in terms of \(q\).

\(\Delta P_{v}=\Delta P-K q^{2}\) (4)

Substitute (4) into (1) to get:

\(C_{v}=\frac{q}{N f(l) \sqrt{\frac{\Delta P-K q^{2}}{g_{s}}}}\) (5)

The problem specifies that \(q_{d}\) should be \(2 / 3\) of \(q_{\max }\) (where \(q_{\max }\) is the flow rate through the valve when the valve is fully open).

\(\begin{aligned}&q_{d}=\frac{2}{3} q_{\max } \\&q_{\max }=\frac{3}{2} q_{d}=\frac{3}{2} 36 m ^{3} / h \\&q_{\max }=54 m ^{3} / h\end{aligned}\)

Now find the \(C_{v}\) that will give \(q_{\max }=54 m ^{3} / h\). Substitute \(q=q_{\max }\) and \(f(l)=1\) (valve fully open) into (5).

\(C_{v}=\frac{q_{\max }}{N \sqrt{\frac{\Delta P-K q_{\max }^{2}}{g_{s}}}}\)

Now that all of the variables on the right hand side of the equation are known, plug in to solve for \(C_{v}\).

\(\begin{aligned}&\Delta P=450 kPa , \quad K=0.154 \frac{ kPa }{\left( m ^{3} / h\right)^{2}}, \quad N=0.0865 \frac{ m ^{3}}{ h ( kPa )^{1 / 2}} \\&g_{s}=1.2, \quad q_{\max }=54 m ^{3} / h\end{aligned}\)

\(\begin{aligned}C_{v} &=\frac{54 \frac{ m ^{3}}{ h }}{0.0865 \frac{ m ^{3}}{ h ( kPa )^{1 / 2}} \sqrt{\frac{450 kPa -0.154 \frac{ kPa }{\left( m ^{3} / h \right)^{2}} 54^{2}\left( m ^{3} / h \right)^{2}}{1.2}}} \\&=\frac{54 \frac{ m ^{3}}{ h }}{0.0865 \frac{ m ^{3}}{ h ( kPa )^{1 / 2}}\left(0.88( kPa )^{1 / 2}\right)}=\frac{54 \frac{ m ^{3}}{ h }}{0.076 \frac{ m ^{3}}{ h }} \\C_{v} &=710.5\end{aligned}\)
