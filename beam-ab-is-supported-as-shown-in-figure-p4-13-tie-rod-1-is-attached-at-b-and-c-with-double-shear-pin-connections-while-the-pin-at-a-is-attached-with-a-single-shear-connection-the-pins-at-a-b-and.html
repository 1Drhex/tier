Guest Question.

Beam AB is supported as shown in Figure P4.13. Tie rod (1) is attached at B and C with double shear pin connections while the pin at A is attached with a single shear connection. The pins at A, B, and C each have an ultimate shear strength of 54 ksi, and tie rod (1) has a yield strength of 36 ksi. A concentrated load of P = 16 kips is applied to the beam as shown. A factor of safety of 3.0 is required for all components. Determine:
(a) the minimum required diameter for tie rod (1).
(b) the minimum required diameter for the double shear pins at B and C.
(c) the minimum required diameter for the single shear pin at A.

Admin
Solution

Tie rod (1) is a two-force member that is oriented at θ with respect to the horizontal axis:

\(\tan \theta=\frac{8.5 ft }{12 ft }=0.7083 \quad \therefore \theta=35.3112^{\circ}\)

From a FBD of beam AB, the following equilibrium equations can be written:

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma F_{x}=& A_{x}+F_{1} \cos 35.3112^{\circ}=0 (a) \\\Sigma F_{y}=& A_{y}+F_{1} \sin 35.3112^{\circ}-P=0 (b) \\\Sigma M_{A}=&-\left(F_{1} \cos 35.3112^{\circ}\right)\left(\frac{10 in .}{12 in . / ft }\right) \\&-\left(F_{1} \sin 35.3112^{\circ}\right)(12 ft )+P(8 ft )=0 (c)\end{aligned}\)

From Eq. (c):

\(F_{1}=\frac{(16 kips )(8 ft )}{\left(\cos 35.3112^{\circ}\right)(0.833333 ft )+\left(\sin 35.3112^{\circ}\right)(12 ft )}=16.8062 kips\)

Substitute \(F_{1}\) into Eq. (a) to obtain \(A_{x}\):

\(A_{x}=-F_{1} \cos 35.3112^{\circ}=-(16.8062 kips ) \cos 35.3112^{\circ}=-13.7143 kips\)

and substitute \(F_{1}\) into Eq. (b) to obtain \(A_{y} \text { : }\)

\(A_{y}=P-F_{1} \sin 35.3112^{\circ}=16 kips -(16.8062 kips ) \sin 35.3112^{\circ}=6.2857 \text { kips }\)

The resultant pin force at A is found from \(A_{x} \text { and } A_{y} \text { : }\)

\(|A|=\sqrt{A_{x}^{2}+A_{y}^{2}}=\sqrt{(13.7143 kips )^{2}+(6.2857 kips )^{2}}=15.0861 kips\)

 

(a) The allowable normal stress for tie rod (1) is

\(\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\sigma_{Y}}{ FS }=\frac{36 ksi }{3.0}=12.0 ksi\)

The minimum cross-sectional area required for the tie rod is

\(A_{\min } \geq \frac{F_{1}}{\sigma_{\text {allow }}}=\frac{16.8062 kips }{12 ksi }=1.400518 in .{ }^{2}\)

Therefore, the minimum tie rod diameter is

\(\frac{\pi}{4} d_{1}^{2} \geq 1.400518 \text { in. }^{2} \quad \therefore d_{1} \geq 1.335363 in .=1.335 in .\)

(b) The allowable shear stress for the pins at A, B, and C is

\(\tau_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\tau_{U}}{ FS }=\frac{54 ksi }{3.0}=18 ksi\)

The double-shear pin connection at B and C must support a load of \(F_{1}\) = 16.8062 kips. The shear area \(A_{V}\) required for these pins is

\(A_{V} \geq \frac{F_{1}}{\tau_{\text {allow }}}=\frac{16.8062 kips }{18 ksi }=0.933679 in .{ }^{2}\)

The pin diameter can be computed from

\(\text { (2 surfaces) } \frac{\pi}{4} d_{ pin }^{2} \geq 0.933679 in .^{2} \quad \therefore d_{\text {pin }} \geq 0.7710 \text { in. }=0.771 in.\)

(c) The pin at A is a single shear connection; therefore, \(A_{V}=A_{\text {pin }}\). The shear area \(A_{V}\) required for this pin is

\(A_{V} \geq \frac{|A|}{\tau_{\text {allow }}}=\frac{15.0861 kips }{18 ksi }=0.838119 in .{ }^{2}\)

The pin diameter can be computed from

\((1 \text { surface }) \frac{\pi}{4} d_{\text {pin }}^{2} \geq 0.838119 \text { in. }^{2} \quad \therefore d_{\text {pin }} \geq 1.0330 \text { in. }=1.033 \text { in. }\)
