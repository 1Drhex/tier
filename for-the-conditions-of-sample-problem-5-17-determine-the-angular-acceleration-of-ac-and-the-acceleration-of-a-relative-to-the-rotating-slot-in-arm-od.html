Guest Question.

For the conditions of , determine the angular acceleration of AC and the acceleration of A relative to the rotating slot in arm OD.

Admin
Solution

We attach the rotating coordinate system x-y to arm OD and use Eq. 5 /14. With the origin at the fixed point O, the term \(a_{B}\) becomes zero so that

 

\(a _{A}= a _{B}+\dot{\omega} \times r +\omega \times(\omega \times r )+2 \omega \times v _{ rel }+ a _{ rel }\) (5/14)

 

\(a _{A}=\dot{\omega} \times r +\omega \times(\omega \times r )+2 \omega \times v _{ rel }+ a _{ rel }\)

 

From the solution to , we make use of the values \(\omega=2 k\) rad /s, \(\omega_{C A}=-4 k\) rad /s, and \(v _{\text {rel }}=-450 \sqrt{2} i\) mm /s and write

 

\(\begin{aligned}a _{A} &=\dot{\omega}_{C A} \times r _{C A}+\omega_{C A} \times\left(\omega_{C A} \times r _{C A}\right) \\&=\dot{\omega}_{C A} k \times \frac{225}{\sqrt{2}}(- i – j )-4 k \times\left(-4 k \times \frac{225}{\sqrt{2}}[- i – j ]\right)\end{aligned}\)

 

1 \(\begin{aligned}\dot{\omega} \times r &= 0 \text { since } \omega =\text { constant } \\\omega \times(\omega \times r ) &=2 k \times(2 k \times 225 \sqrt{2} i )=-900 \sqrt{2} i mm / s ^{2} \\2 \omega \times v _{\text {rel }} &=2(2 k ) \times(-450 \sqrt{2} i )=-1800 \sqrt{2} j mm / s ^{2} \\a _{\text {rel }} &=\ddot{x} i \end{aligned}\)

 

Substitution into the relative-acceleration equation yields

 

\(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(225 \dot{\omega}_{C A}+3600\right) i +\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(-225 \dot{\omega}_{C A}+3600\right) j =-900 \sqrt{2} i -1800 \sqrt{2} j +\ddot{x} i\)

 

Equating separately the i and j terms gives

 

\(\left(225 \dot{\omega}_{C A}+3600\right) / \sqrt{2}=-900 \sqrt{2}+\ddot{x}\)

 

and \(\left(-225 \dot{\omega}_{C A}+3600\right) / \sqrt{2}=-1800 \sqrt{2}\)

 

Solving for the two unknowns gives

 

\(\dot{\omega}_{C A}=32 rad / s ^{2} \quad \text { and } \quad \ddot{x}=a_{ rel }=8910 mm / s ^{2}\) Ans.

 

If desired, the acceleration of A may also be written as

 

\(a _{A}=(225 / \sqrt{2})(32)( i – j )+(3600 / \sqrt{2})( i + j )=7640 i -2550 j \quad mm / s ^{2}\)

 

We make use here of the geometric representation of the relative-acceleration equation to further clarify the problem. The geometric approach may be used as an alternative solution. Again, we introduce point P on OD coincident with A. The equivalent scalar terms are

 

\(\begin{aligned}\left(a_{A}\right)_{t} &=\left|\dot{\omega}_{C A} \times r _{C A}\right|=r \dot{\omega}_{C A}=r \alpha_{C A} \text { normal to } C A, \text { sense unknown } \\\left(a_{A}\right)_{n} &=\left|\omega_{C A} \times\left(\omega_{C A} \times r _{C A}\right)\right|=r \omega_{C A}^{2} \text { from } A \text { to } C \\\left(a_{P}\right)_{n} &=|\omega \times(\omega \times r )|=\overline{O P} \omega^{2} \text { from } P \text { to } O \\\left(a_{P}\right)_{t} &=|\dot{\omega} \times r |=r \dot{\omega}=0 \text { since } \omega=\text { constant } \\\left|2 \omega \times v _{\text {rel }}\right| &=2 \omega v_{\text {rel }} \text { directed as shown } \\a_{\text {rel }} &=\ddot{x} \text { along } O D, \text { sense unknown }\end{aligned}\)

 

We start with the known vectors and add them head-to-tail for each side of the equation beginning at R and ending at S, where the intersection of the known directions of \(\left( a _{A}\right)_{t} \text { and } a _{ rel }\) 2 establishes the solution. Closure of the polygon determines the sense of each of the two unknown vectors, and their magnitudes are easily calculated from the figure geometry.

 

Helpful Hints

1 If the slot had been curved with a radius of curvature ρ, the term \(a _{\text {rel }}\) would have had a component \(v_{ rel }^{2} / \rho\) normal to the slot and directed toward the center of curvature in addition to its component along the slot.

2 It is always possible to avoid a simultaneous solution by projecting the vectors onto the perpendicular to one of the unknowns.
