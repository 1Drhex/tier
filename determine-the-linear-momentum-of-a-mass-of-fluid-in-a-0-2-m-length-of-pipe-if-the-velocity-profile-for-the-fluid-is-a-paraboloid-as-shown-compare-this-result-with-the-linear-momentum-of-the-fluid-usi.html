Guest Question.

Determine the linear momentum of a mass of fluid in a 0.2-m length of pipe if the velocity profile for the fluid is a paraboloid as shown. Compare this result with the linear momentum of the fluid using the average velocity of flow. Take \( \rho = 800 kg/m^3. \)

Admin
Solution

The shell differential element that has a thickness dr and length 0.2 m shown shaded in Fig. a has a volume of \(dV = (2\pi rdr)(0.2 m) = 0.4\pi rdr. \)Thus, the mass of this element is \( dm = \rho dV = (800 kg/m^3)(0.4rdr) = 320\pi rdr. \) The linear momentum of the fluid is

\(L = \int_{m} v dm\)

 

\(= \int_{0}^{0.1 m}4(1 – 100r^2)(320\pi r dr)\)

 

\(= 1280 \pi \int_{0}^{0.1 m}(r – 100r^3)dr\)

 

\( = 1280\pi (\frac{r^2}{2} – 25 r^4)|_{0}^{0.1 m}\)

 

\(= 10.05 kg \cdot m/s = 10.1 kg \cdot m/s.\)
The ring differential element shown shaded in Fig. a has an area of\( dA = 2 \pi rdr.\)

Therefore

\(V_{avg} = \int \frac{vdA}{A} \)

 

\( = \frac{\int_{0}^{0.1 m}4(1 – 100r^2)(2\pi r dr)}{\pi (0.1 m)^2}\)

 

\( = \frac{8 \pi \int_{0}^{0.1 m}(r – 100r^3)dr}{\pi (0.1 m)^2}\)

 

\( = \frac{8\pi (\frac{r^2}{2} – 25 r^4)|_{0}^{0.1 m}}{\pi (0.1 m)^2} = 2 m/s\)

 

The mass of the fluid is \(m\rho V = (800 kg/m^3) [\pi (0.1 m)^2](0.2 m) = 1.6\pi kg. \)Thus,

 

\(L = mV_{avg} = \rho VV_{avg} = (1.6p kg)(2 m/s) \)

 

\(= 10.05 kg \cdot m/s = 10.1 kg \cdot m/s \)
