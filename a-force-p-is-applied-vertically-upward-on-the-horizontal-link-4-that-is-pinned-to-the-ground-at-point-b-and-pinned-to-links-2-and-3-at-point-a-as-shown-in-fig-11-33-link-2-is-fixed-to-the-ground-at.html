Guest Question.

A force P is applied vertically upward on the horizontal link 4 that is pinned to the ground at point B and pinned to links 2 and 3 at point A, as shown in Fig. 11.33. Link 2 is fixed to the ground at point D. Assume that links 2 and 4 are in static equilibrium. The distances are RBA =1 m and RCA =3 m, and links 2 and 4 are made from a steel alloy with a compressive yield strength Syc = 415 MPa and a modulus of elasticity E = 207 GPa. The length of the vertical link 2 is RDA = 2.75 m, and this link has a hollow circular cross section with an outside diameter of 50 mm and a wall thickness of 6 mm. Using the theoretic value for the end-condition constant for link 2, determine: (a) the value of the slenderness ratio at the point of tangency between Euler’s column formula and Johnson’s parabolic formula; (b) the critical load acting on link 2 and the critical unit load on link 2; and (c) the force P for the factor of safety of link 2 to guard against buckling to be N = 2. (d) If link 2 is replaced with a solid circular cross section of the same material with a diameter of 40 mm and force P = 70 kN, then determine the maximum length of link 2 for the factor of safety to guard against buckling of the link to be N = 2.

Admin
Solution

(a) From the fixed-pinned end conditions of link 2, the end-condition constant for this link is C = 2. Therefore, the slenderness ratio for link 2 at the point of tangency is

\(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{D}=\pi \sqrt{\frac{2 C E}{S_{y c}}}=\pi \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot 2\left(207 \times 10^{9} Pa \right)}{415 \times 10^{6} Pa }}=140.3\) (1)

 

(b) The cross-sectional area of link 2 can be written as

\(A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left(D^{2}-d^{2}\right)=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[(0.050 m )^{2}-(0.038 m )^{2}\right]=0.000829 m ^{2}\)

The second moment of area of link 2 can be written as (Appendix A, Table 4)

\(I=\frac{\pi}{64}\left(D^{4}-d^{4}\right)=\frac{\pi}{64}\left[(0.050 m )^{4}-(0.038 m )^{4}\right]=0.2044 \times 10^{-6} m ^{4}\)

The radius of gyration of link 2 can be written as

\(k=\sqrt{\frac{I}{A}}=\sqrt{\frac{0.2044 \times 10^{-6} m ^{4}}{0.000829 m ^{2}}}=0.0157 m\)

The slenderness ratio of link 2 is

\(S_{ r }=\frac{L}{k}=\frac{2.75 m }{0.0157 m }=175.2\) (2)

To determine the critical load on link 2, we must first determine whether this link is an Euler column or a Johnson column. The criterion for using Johnson’s parabolic equation is \( S_{ r }<\left(S_{ r }\right)_{D} .\) From Eqs. (1) and (2), we have \( S_{ r }<\left(S_{ r }\right)_{D} ,\) that is, 175.2 > 140.3. Therefore, link 2 is an Euler column.
The critical load on link 2, from the Euler column equation, is

\(P_{c r}=A\left[\frac{C \pi^{2} E}{S_{r}^{2}}\right]=\left(0.000829 m ^{2}\right)\left[\frac{2 \pi^{2}\left(207 \times 10^{9} Pa \right)}{175.2^{2}}\right]=110.35 kN\)

The critical unit load on link 2 is defined as

\(\frac{P_{c r}}{A}=\frac{110.35 kN }{0.000829 m ^{2}}=133.1 MPa\)

(c) Links 2 and 4 are in static equilibrium. The free-body diagram of link 4 is shown in Fig. 11.34.

Taking moments about pin B gives

\(R _{C B} \times P = R _{A B} \times F _{24}^{y}\)

Substituting the given data into this equation gives

\(P=\frac{(1 m ) F_{24}^{y}}{2 m }=\frac{F_{24}^{y}}{2}\) (3)

Link 2 is in compression as shown in Fig. 11.35.

The reaction force \(P_{A}^{y}=F_{42}^{y}\) onto link 2 is opposite to the reaction force \(F_{24}^{y}\); therefore, the magnitude of \(P_{A}^{y}\) is equal to the magnitude of \(F_{24}^{y}\). Equation (3) can be written as

\(P=\frac{P_{A}^{y}}{2}\) (4)

The factor of safety guarding against buckling for link 2 is defined as

\(N=\frac{P_{ cr }}{P_{A}^{y}}\) (5)

where \(P_{A}^{y}=F_{42}^{y}\) is the compressive load at point D on link 2, as shown in Fig. 11.35. Therefore, rearranging Eq. (5), the force, \(P_{A}^{y}\), is

\(P_{A}^{y}=\frac{P_{ cr }}{N}=\frac{110.35 kN }{2}=55.175 kN\) (6)

Finally, substituting Eq. (6) into Eq. (4) gives

\(P=\frac{P_{A}^{y}}{2}=\frac{55.175 kN }{2}=27.588 kN\)

(d) Link 2 is a solid circular column with a factor of safety N = 2 and P = 70 kN. Rearranging Eq. (4), the compressive force is

\(P_{A}^{y}=2 P=2(70 kN )=140 kN\)

Rearranging Eq. (5), the critical load applied on link 2 is

\(P_{ cr }=P_{A}^{y} \cdot N=140 kN \cdot 2=280 kN\)

First, assume the link is an Euler column. Then from Euler’s column formula, Eq. (11.34), the critical load applied on link 2 is

\(\frac{P_{ cr }}{A}=C \frac{\pi^{2} E}{S_{ r }^{2}}\) (11.34)

 

\(P_{ cr }=A\left[\frac{C \pi^{2} E}{S_{ r }^{2}}\right]\) (7)

The cross-sectional area of link 2 is

\(A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left(D^{2}\right)=\frac{\pi}{4}(0.040 m )^{2}=0.00126 m ^{2}\)

The second moment of area of the cross section of link 2 is

\(I=\frac{\pi}{64}\left(D^{4}\right)=\frac{\pi}{64}(0.040 m )^{4}=0.126 \times 10^{-6} m ^{4}\)

The radius of gyration of the cross section of link 2 is

\(k=\sqrt{\frac{I}{A}}=\sqrt{\frac{0.126 \times 10^{-6} m ^{4}}{0.00126 m ^{2}}}=0.010 m\)

Rearranging Eq. (7), the slenderness ratio of link 2 is

\(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{\text {Euler }}=\sqrt{\frac{C \pi^{2} E A}{P_{ cr }}}=\sqrt{\frac{2 \pi^{2}\left(207 \times 10^{9} Pa \right)\left(0.00126 m ^{2}\right)}{280 \times 10^{3} N }}=135.6\)

Next, assume the link is a Johnson column. The critical unit load can be written from Johnson’s parabolic equation, Eq. (11.39), as

\(\frac{P_{ cr }}{A}=S_{y c}-\frac{1}{C E}\left(\frac{S_{y c} S_{ r }}{2 \pi}\right)^{2}\) (11.39)

 

\(\frac{P_{ cr }}{A}=\left[S_{y c}-\frac{1}{C E}\left(\frac{S_{y c} S_{ r }}{2 \pi}\right)^{2}\right]\) (8)

Rearranging Eq. (8), the slenderness ratio can be written as

\(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{ Johnson }=\frac{2 \pi}{S_{y c}} \sqrt{\left(S_{y c}-\frac{P_{ cr }}{A}\right) C E}\)

Substituting the known data into this equation gives

\(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{ Johnson }=\frac{2 \pi}{\left(415 \times 10^{6} Pa \right)} \sqrt{\left[\left(415 \times 10^{6} Pa \right)-\frac{280000 N }{0.00126 m ^{2}}\right] 2\left(207 \times 10^{9} Pa \right)} = 135.3.\)

The slenderness ratio at the point of tangency can be written as

\(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{D}=\pi \sqrt{\frac{2 C E}{S_{y c}}}\)

Since the material properties are the same, this gives the same result as Eq. (1), that is \(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{D}=140.33.\) Therefore, since \( \left(S_{ r }\right)_{ Johnson }<\left(S_{ r }\right)_{ Euler }<\left(S_{ r }\right)_{D}\), the link is a Johnson column. Using the slenderness ratio for the case of a Johnson column; that is, \(S_{ r }=\left(S_{ r }\right)_{ Johnson }=135.3\) and rearranging Eq. (2), the maximum possible length of link 2 is

\(L=k S_{ r }=(0.010 m ) 135.3=1.353 m\)
