Guest Question.

Suppose you want to calculate the expectation value of some observable Ω, in the nth energy eigenstate of a system that is perturbed by H′:

\( \langle\Omega\rangle=\left\langle\psi_{n}|\hat{\Omega}| \psi_{n}\right\rangle \).

Replacing ψn by its perturbation expansion, Equation 7.5,

\( \psi_{n}=\psi_{n}^{0}+\lambda \psi_{n}^{1}+\lambda^{2} \psi_{n}^{2}+\cdots \) (7.5)

\( \langle\Omega\rangle=\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0}|\hat{\Omega}| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle+\lambda\left[\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1}|\hat{\Omega}| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle+\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0}|\hat{\Omega}| \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle\right]+\lambda^{2}(\cdots)+\cdots \).

The first-order correction to 〈Ω〉 is therefore

\( \langle\Omega\rangle^{1}=2 \operatorname{Re}\left[\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0}|\hat{\Omega}| \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle\right] \).

or, using Equation 7.13,

\( \psi_{n}^{\prime}=\sum_{m \neq n} \frac{\left\langle\psi_{m}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle}{\left(E_{n}^{0}-E_{m}^{0}\right)} \psi_{m}^{0} \) (7.13).

\( \langle\Omega\rangle^{1}=2 \operatorname{Re} \sum_{m \neq n} \frac{\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0}|\hat{\Omega}| \psi_{m}^{0}\right\rangle\left\langle\psi_{m}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle}{E_{n}^{0}-E_{m}^{0}} \) (7.118).

(assuming the unperturbed energies are nondegenerate, or that we are using the “good” basis states).

(a) Suppose \( \Omega=H^{\prime} \) (the perturbation itself). What does Equation 7.118 tell us in this case? Explain (carefully) why this is consistent with Equation 7.15.

\( E_{n}^{2}=\sum_{m \neq n} \frac{\left|\left(\psi_{m}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle\right|^{2}}{E_{n}^{0}-E_{m}^{0}} \) (7.15).

(b) Consider a particle of charge q (maybe an electron in a hydrogen atom, or a pith ball connected to a spring), that is placed in a weak electric field \( E_{ ext } \) pointing in the x direction, so that

\( H^{\prime}=-q E_{\text {ext }} x \),

The field will induce an electric dipole moment, \( p_{e}=q x\), in the “atom.” The expectation value of \( p_{e}\) is proportional to the applied field, and the proportionality factor is called the polarizability, α. Show that

\( \alpha=-2 q^{2} \sum_{m \neq n} \frac{\left|\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0}|x| \psi_{m}^{0}\right\rangle\right|^{2}}{E_{n}^{0}-E_{m}^{0}} \), (7.119)

Find the polarizability of the ground state of a one-dimensional harmonic oscillator. Compare the classical answer.

(c) Now imagine a particle of mass m in a one-dimensional harmonic oscillator with a small anharmonic perturbation.

\( H^{\prime}=-\frac{1}{6} \kappa x^{3} \) (7.120).

Find \( \langle x\rangle \) (to first order), in the nth energy eigenstate. Answer: \( \left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right) \hbar \kappa /\left(2 m^{2} \omega^{3}\right) \). Comment: As the temperature increases, higherenergy states are populated, and the particles move farther (on average) from their equilibrium positions; that’s why most solids expand with rising temperature.

Solution

(a) \( \left\langle H^{\prime}\right\rangle=2 \operatorname{Re} \sum_{m \neq n} \frac{\left|\left\langle\psi_{m}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle\right|^{2}}{E_{n}^{0}-E_{m}^{0}} \).

The sum is manifestly real (so we can drop the Re), and in fact it is precisely \( E_{n}^{2} \text { (Equation 7.15): }\left\langle H^{\prime}\right\rangle=2 E_{n}^{2} \text {. } \).

\( E_{n}^{2}=\sum_{m \neq n} \frac{\left|\left(\psi_{m}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle\right|^{2}}{E_{n}^{0}-E_{m}^{0}} \) (7.15).

Explanation: The second-order correction to the energy is the term of order λ² in

\( \frac{\left\langle\psi_{n}\left|H_{0}+\lambda H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{n}\right\rangle}{\left\langle\psi_{n} \mid \psi_{n}\right\rangle}=\frac{\left\langle\psi_{n}\left|H_{0}\right| \psi_{n}\right\rangle}{\left\langle\psi_{n} \mid \psi_{n}\right\rangle}+\lambda \frac{\left\langle\psi_{n}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{n}\right\rangle}{\left\langle\psi_{n} \mid \psi_{n}\right\rangle} \).

where \( \psi_{n} \) is the exact nth eigenstate. We just calculated the second term; we need to evaluate the first term, to order λ².

\( \left\langle\psi_{n}\left|H_{0}\right| \psi_{n}\right\rangle=\left\langle\left(\psi_{n}^{0}+\lambda \psi_{0}^{1}+\lambda^{2} \psi_{n}^{2}+\cdots\right)\left|H_{0}\right|\left(\psi_{n}^{0}+\lambda \psi_{0}^{1}+\lambda^{2} \psi_{n}^{2}+\cdots\right)\right\rangle \).

\( =\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0}\left|H_{0}\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle+\lambda\left[\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0}\left|H_{0}\right| \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle+\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1}\left|H_{0}\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle\right] \)

\( +\lambda^{2}\left[\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0}\left|H_{0}\right| \psi_{n}^{2}\right\rangle+\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1}\left|H_{0}\right| \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle+\left\langle\psi_{n}^{2}\left|H_{0}\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle\right]+\cdots \).

\( \approx E_{n}^{0}+\lambda\left[E_{n}^{0}\left(\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0} \mid \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle+\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1} \mid \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle\right)\right]+\lambda^{2}\left[E_{n}^{0}\left(\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0} \mid \psi_{n}^{2}\right\rangle+\left\langle\psi_{n}^{2} \mid \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle\right)+\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1}\left|H_{0}\right| \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle\right] \).

\( =E_{n}^{0}+\lambda^{2}\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1}\left|H_{0}\right| \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle \).

(As always, we choose \( \left\langle\psi_{n}^{i} \mid \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle=0 \)-see comments after Equation 7.11.) Similarly

\( \psi_{n}^{1}=\sum_{m \neq n} c_{m}^{(n)} \psi_{m}^{0} \) (7.11).

\( \left\langle\psi_{n} \mid \psi_{n}\right\rangle=\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0} \mid \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle+\lambda\left[\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0} \mid \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle+\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1} \mid \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle\right]+\lambda^{2}\left[\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0} \mid \psi_{n}^{2}\right\rangle+\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1} \mid \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle+\left\langle\psi_{n}^{2} \mid \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle\right]+\cdots \).

\( \approx 1+\lambda^{2}\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1} \mid \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle \).

So

\( \frac{\left\langle\psi_{n}\left|H_{0}\right| \psi_{n}\right\rangle}{\left\langle\psi_{n} \mid \psi_{n}\right\rangle}=\left[E_{n}^{0}+\lambda^{2}\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1}\left|H_{0}\right| \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle\right]\left(1+\lambda^{2}\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1} \mid \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle\right)^{-1} \approx\left[E_{n}^{0}+\lambda^{2}\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1}\left|H_{0}\right| \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle\right]\left(1-\lambda^{2}\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1} \mid \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle\right) \).

\( =E_{n}^{0}+\lambda^{2}\left[\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1}\left|H_{0}\right| \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle-E_{n}^{0}\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1} \mid \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle\right]=E_{n}^{0}+\lambda^{2}\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1}\left|\left(H_{0}-E_{n}^{0}\right)\right| \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle \).

Finally, using Equation 7.13:

\( \psi_{n}^{\prime}=\sum_{m \neq n} \frac{\left\langle\psi_{m}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle}{\left(E_{n}^{0}-E_{m}^{0}\right)} \psi_{m}^{0} \) (7.13).

\( \left\langle\psi_{n}^{1}\left|\left(H_{0}-E_{n}^{0}\right)\right| \psi_{n}^{1}\right\rangle=\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1}\left|\left(H_{0}-E_{n}^{0}\right) \sum_{m \neq n} \frac{H_{m n}^{\prime}}{\left(E_{n}^{0}-E_{m}^{0}\right)}\right| \psi_{m}^{0}\right\rangle=-\left\langle\psi_{n}^{1}\left|\sum_{m \neq n} H_{m n}^{\prime}\right| \psi_{m}^{0}\right\rangle \).

\( =-\sum_{p \neq n} \sum_{m \neq n} \frac{H_{p n}^{\prime}{ }^{*}}{E_{n}^{0}-E_{p}^{0}} H_{m n}^{\prime}\left\langle\psi_{p}^{0} \mid \psi_{m}^{0}\right\rangle=-\sum_{m \neq n} \frac{\left|H_{m n}^{\prime}\right|^{2}}{E_{n}^{0}-E_{m}^{0}}=-E_{n}^{2} \).

(Equation 7.15). So the second-order correction the nth energy is \( -E_{n}^{2}+2 E_{n}^{2}=E_{n}^{2} \) , as it should be.

\( E_{n}^{2}=\sum_{m \neq n} \frac{\left|\left(\psi_{m}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle\right|^{2}}{E_{n}^{0}-E_{m}^{0}} \) (7.15).

(b) To first order, the expectation value of \( p_e \) in the nth state is

\( \left\langle p_{e}\right\rangle=2 \operatorname{Re} \sum_{m \neq n} \frac{\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0}|q x| \psi_{m}^{0}\right\rangle\left\langle\psi_{m}^{0}\left|\left(-q E_{\text {ext }} x\right)\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle}{E_{n}^{0}-E_{m}^{0}}=-2 q^{2} E_{\operatorname{ext}} \sum_{m \neq n} \frac{\left|\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0}|x| \psi_{m}^{0}\right\rangle\right|^{2}}{E_{n}^{0}-E_{m}^{0}} \).

so

\( \alpha=-2 q^{2} \sum_{m \neq n} \frac{\left|\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0}|x| \psi_{m}^{0}\right\rangle\right|^{2}}{E_{n}^{0}-E_{m}^{0}} \).

For the one-dimensional oscillator (Equation 3.114)

\( \left\langle n|x| n^{\prime}\right\rangle=\sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega}}\left(\sqrt{n^{\prime}} \delta_{n, n^{\prime}-1}+\sqrt{n} \delta_{n^{\prime}, n-1}\right) \) (3.114).

\( \left\langle\psi_{n}^{0}|x| \psi_{m}^{0}\right\rangle=\sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega}}\left(\sqrt{m} \delta_{n, m-1}+\sqrt{n} \delta_{m, n-1}\right) \Rightarrow\left\langle\psi_{0}^{0}|x| \psi_{m}^{0}\right\rangle=\sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega}} \sqrt{m} \delta_{0, m-1}=\sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega}} \delta_{m, 1} \).

(don’t confuse the mass m, in the factor out front, with the index m), so

\( \alpha=-2 q^{2} \frac{\hbar / 2 m \omega}{(\hbar \omega / 2)-(3 \hbar \omega / 2)}=\frac{q^{2}}{m \omega^{2}} \).

Classically, the electric force \( q E_{\text {ext }} \) balances the spring force kd, so \( p_{e}=q d=q^{2} E_{\text {ext }} / k \), and hence \( \alpha=q^{2} / k=q^{2} /\left(m \omega^{2}\right) \), in agreement with the quantum result.

(c)

\( \langle x\rangle^{1}=2 \operatorname{Re}\left(\sum_{m \neq n} \frac{\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0}|x| \psi_{m}^{0}\right\rangle\left\langle\psi_{m}^{0}\left|\left(-\frac{1}{6} \kappa x^{3}\right)\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle}{\left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right) \hbar \omega-\left(m+\frac{1}{2}\right) \hbar \omega}\right)=-\frac{\kappa}{3 \hbar \omega} \operatorname{Re}\left(\sum_{m \neq n} \frac{\left\langle\psi_{n}^{0}|x| \psi_{m}^{0}\right\rangle\left\langle\psi_{m}^{0}\left|x^{3}\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle}{(n-m)}\right) \).

\( =-\frac{\kappa}{3 \hbar \omega} \sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega}} \operatorname{Re}\left(\sum_{m \neq n} \frac{\left(\sqrt{m} \delta_{n, m-1}+\sqrt{n} \delta_{m, n-1}\right)}{(n-m)}\left\langle\psi_{m}^{0}\left|x^{3}\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle\right) \).

\( =-\frac{\kappa}{3 \hbar \omega} \sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega}} \operatorname{Re}\left[-\sqrt{n+1}\left\langle\psi_{n+1}^{0}\left|x^{3}\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle+\sqrt{n}\left\langle\psi_{n-1}^{0}\left|x^{3}\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle\right] \).

Now, from (Equation 2.70),

\( x=\sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega}}\left(\hat{a}_{+}+\hat{a}_{-}\right) ; \quad \hat{p}=i \sqrt{\frac{\hbar m \omega}{2}}\left(\hat{a}_{+}-\hat{a}_{-}\right) \) (2.70).

\( x^{3}=\left(\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega}\right)^{3 / 2}\left(a_{+}+a_{-}\right)^{3} \).

\( =\left(\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega}\right)^{3 / 2}\left(a_{+}^{3}+a_{+}^{2} a_{-}+a_{+} a_{-} a_{+}+a_{+} a_{-}^{2}+a_{-} a_{+}^{2}+a_{-} a_{+} a_{-}+a_{-}^{2} a_{+}+a_{-}^{3}\right) \).

We need

\( \left\langle n+1\left|x^{3}\right| n\right\rangle=\left(\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega}\right)^{3 / 2}\left\langle n+1\left|\left(a_{+}^{2} a_{-}+a_{+} a_{-} a_{+}+a_{-} a_{+}^{2}\right)\right| n\right\rangle \).

\( \left\langle n-1\left|x^{3}\right| n\right\rangle=\left(\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega}\right)^{3 / 2}\left\langle n-1\left|\left(a_{+} a_{-}^{2}+a_{-} a_{+} a_{-}+a_{-}^{2} a_{+}\right)\right| n\right\rangle \).

(In the first line we need two \( a_{+} \text {'s and one } a_{-}, \text {to raise }|n\rangle \text { up to }|n+1\rangle \) in the second line we need \( a_{-} \text {'s and one } a_{+}, \text {to lower }|n\rangle \text { to }|n-1\rangle \).) Using Equation 2.67 repeatedly:

\( \hat{a}_{+} \psi_{n}=\sqrt{n+1} \psi_{n+1}, \quad \hat{a}_{-} \psi_{n}=\sqrt{n} \psi_{n-1} \) (2.67).

\( a_{+}^{2} a_{-}|n\rangle=\sqrt{n} a_{+}^{2}|n-1\rangle=\sqrt{n} \sqrt{n} a_{+}|n\rangle=n \sqrt{n+1}|n+1\rangle \).

\( a_{+} a_{-} a_{+}|n\rangle=\sqrt{n+1} a_{+} a_{-}|n+1\rangle=\sqrt{n+1} \sqrt{n+1} a_{+}|n\rangle=(n+1) \sqrt{n+1}|n+1\rangle, \).

\( a_{-} a_{+}^{2}|n\rangle=\sqrt{n+1} a_{-} a_{+}|n+1\rangle=\sqrt{n+1} \sqrt{n+2} a_{-}|n+2\rangle=(n+2) \sqrt{n+1}|n+1\rangle \).

so

\( \left\langle n+1\left|\left(a_{+}^{2} a_{-}+a_{+} a_{-} a_{+}+a_{-} a_{+}^{2}\right)\right| n\right\rangle=\langle n+1|(n \sqrt{n+1}+(n+1) \sqrt{n+1}+(n+2) \sqrt{n+1})| n+1\rangle \).

\( =3(n+1) \sqrt{n+1} \).

In the same way,

\( a_{+} a_{-}^{2}|n\rangle=\sqrt{n} a_{+} a_{-}|n-1\rangle=\sqrt{n} \sqrt{n-1} a_{+}|n-2\rangle=(n-1) \sqrt{n}|n-1\rangle \).

\( a_{-} a_{+} a_{-}|n\rangle=\sqrt{n} a_{-} a_{+}|n-1\rangle=\sqrt{n} \sqrt{n} a_{-}|n\rangle=n \sqrt{n}|n-1\rangle \).

\( a_{-}^{2} a_{+}|n\rangle=\sqrt{n+1} a_{-}^{2}|n+1\rangle=\sqrt{n+1} \sqrt{n+1} a_{-}|n\rangle=(n+1) \sqrt{n}|n-1\rangle \).

so

\( \left\langle n-1\left|\left(a_{+} a_{-}^{2}+a_{-} a_{+} a_{-}+a_{-}^{2} a_{+}\right)\right| n\right\rangle=\langle n-1|((n-1) \sqrt{n}+n \sqrt{n}+(n+1) \sqrt{n})| n-1\rangle \).

\( =3 n \sqrt{n} \).

Thus

\( \left\langle n+1\left|x^{3}\right| n\right\rangle=\left(\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega}\right)^{3 / 2} 3(n+1) \sqrt{n+1}, \quad\left\langle n-1\left|x^{3}\right| n\right\rangle=\left(\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega}\right)^{3 / 2} 3 n \sqrt{n} \).

Finally,

\( \langle x\rangle^{1}=-\frac{\kappa}{3 \hbar \omega} \sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega}}\left(\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega}\right)^{3 / 2}[-\sqrt{n+1} 3(n+1) \sqrt{n+1}+\sqrt{n} 3 n \sqrt{n}] \).

\( =-\frac{\kappa}{\hbar \omega}\left(\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega}\right)^{2}\left[-(n+1)^{2}+n^{2}\right]=(2 n+1) \frac{\hbar \kappa}{4 m^{2} \omega^{3}} \).
