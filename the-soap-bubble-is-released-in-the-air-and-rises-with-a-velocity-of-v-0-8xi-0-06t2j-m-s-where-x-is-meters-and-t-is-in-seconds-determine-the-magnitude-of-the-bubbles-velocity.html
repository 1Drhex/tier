Guest Question.

The soap bubble is released in the air and rises with a velocity of \(V = [ (0.8x)i + (0.06t^{2})j ] m/s \) where x is meters and t is in seconds. Determine the magnitude of the bubble’s velocity, and its directions measured counterclockwise from the x axis, when t = 5 s, at which time x = 2 m and y = 3 m. Draw its streamline at this instant

Admin
Solution

The velocity vector of a particle at x = 2 m and the corresponding time t = 5 s is

\(V = { (0.8x)i + (0.06t^{2})}j m/s \)

 

\(= [0.8(2)i + 0.06(5)^{2} j] \)

 

\(= {1.6i + 1.5j} m/s \)

The magnitude of V is

\(V = \sqrt{V_{x}^{2} + V_{y}^{2}} =\sqrt{(1.6 m/s)^{2} + (1.5 m/s)^{2}} = 2.19 m/s \)

As indicated in Fig. a, the direction of V is defined by

\(θ = tan^{-1} (\frac{V_y}{V_x}) = tan^{-1} (\frac{1.5 m/s}{1.6 m/s})= 43.2° \)

 

Using the definition of the slope of the streamline and initial condition at x = 2 m, y = 3 m.

\(\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{v}{u^{,}} \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{0.06 t^{2}}{0.8x} \)

Note that since we are finding the streamline, which represents a single instant in time, t = 5 s, t is a constant.

\(\int ^{y} _{3 m} \frac{dy}{t^{2}} = \int ^{x} _{2 m} \frac{0.075 dx}{x}\)

 

\(\frac{1}{t^{2}} (y – 3) = 0.075 \ln \frac{x}{2} \)

 

\(y = (0.075t^{2} \ln \frac{x}{2} + 3 ) \)

When t = 5 s,

\(y = 0.075(5^{2}) \ln \frac{x}{2} + 3 \)

 

\(y = [ 1.875 \ln \frac{x}{2} + 3 ] m \)
