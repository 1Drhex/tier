Guest Question.

Determine the total heat ﬂow rate of convection for element 3 of .

Admin
Solution

First we note that, for element 3, the element-to-global correspondence relation for nodal temperatures is

\(\ \left [ \begin{matrix} T^{(3)}_{1} & T^{(3)}_{2} & T^{(3)}_{3} & T^{(3)}_{4} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} T_{5} & T_{8} & T_{9} & T_{6} \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Second, element 3 is subjected to convection on both lateral surfaces as well as the two edges deﬁned by nodes 8-9 and 6-9. Consequently, three integrations are required as follows:

\(\dot{H}^{(e)}_{h} =2\int\limits_{A^{(e)} }^{}{\int\limits_{}^{}{h(\left[N\right]\left\{T\right\}-T_{a} )dA^{(e)} } } +\int\limits_{A_{8-9} }^{}{\int\limits_{}^{}{h(\left[N\right]\left\{T\right\}-T_{a} )dA_{8-9} } } +\int\limits_{A_{6-9} }^{}{\int\limits_{}^{}{h(\left[N\right]\left\{T\right\}-T_{a} )dA_{6-9} } } \)

where \(A^{(e)} \) is element area in the xy plane and the multiplier in the ﬁrst term (2) accounts for both lateral surfaces.

Transforming the ﬁrst integral to normalized coordinates results in

\(\ I_{1}=2hab\int\limits_{-1}^{1}{\int\limits_{-1}^{1}{(\left[N\right]\left\{T\right\} -T_{a} )dr ds} } =2hab\int\limits_{-1}^{1}{\int\limits_{-1}^{1}{\left[N\right]dr ds \left\{T\right\} } }- 2habT_{a} \int\limits_{-1}^{1}{dr ds}=\frac{2hA}{4}\int\limits_{-1}^{1}{\int\limits_{-1}^{1}{{\left[N\right]dr ds \left\{T\right\} } }- 2hAT_{a} } \)

Therefore, we need integrate the interpolation functions only over the area of the element, as all other terms are known constants. For example,

\(\ \int\limits_{-1}^{1}{\int\limits_{-1}^{1}{N_{1}dr ds } } =\int\limits_{-1}^{1}{\int\limits_{-1}^{1}{\frac{1}{4}(1-r)(1-s)dr ds } }=\frac{1}{4}\frac{(1-r)^{2} }{2}\mid ^{1}_{-1}\frac{(1-s)^{2} }{2} \mid ^{1}_{-1} =1 \)

An identical result is obtained when the other three functions are integrated. The integral corresponding to convection from the element lateral surfaces is then

\(\ I_{1}=2hA\left(\frac{T^{(3)}_{1}+T^{(3)}_{2}+T^{(3)}_{3}+T^{(3)}_{4} }{4}-T_{a} \right) \)

The ﬁrst term in the parentheses is the average of the nodal temperatures, and this is a general result for the rectangular element. Substituting numerical values

\(\ I_{1}=\frac{2(50)(1)^{2} }{144}\left(\frac{111.982+90.966+89.041+106.507}{4}-68 \right)=21.96 Btu/ hr \)

Next, we consider the edge convection terms. Along edge 8-9,

\(\ I_{2}=\int\limits_{A_{8-9} }^{}{\int\limits_{}^{}{h(\left[N\right]\left\{T\right\}-T_{a} )dA_{8-9} } } \)

and, since r = 1 along that edge, \(\ dA_{8-9}=t b ds \) , and the integral becomes

\(\ I_{2}=htb\int\limits_{-1}^{1}{(\left[N_{r=1} \right]\left\{T\right\}-T_{a} ds )} =htb\int\limits_{-1}^{1}{\frac{1}{4}\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & 1-s & 1+s & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] } -htbT_{a} \int\limits_{-1}^{1}{ds} \)

 

\(\ =h(2tb)\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & \frac{1}{2} & \frac{1}{2} & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \left\{T\right\} -h(2tb)T_{a}=hA_{edge}\left(\frac{T^{(3)}_{2} +T^{(3)}_{3} }{2}-T_{a} \right) \)

Again, we observe that the average temperature of the nodes associated with the area of the edge appears. Stated another way, the convection area is allocated equally to the two nodes, and this is another general result for the rectangular element. Inserting numerical values,

\(\ I_{2} =\frac{50(0.5)(1)}{144} \left(\frac{90.966 +89.041 }{2}-68 \right)=3.82 Btu/hr \)

By analogy, the edge convection along edge 6-9 is

\(\ I_{3} =hA_{edge}\left(\frac{T^{(3)}_{3} +T^{(3)}_{4} }{2}-T_{a} \right)=\frac{50(0.5)(1)}{144}\left(\frac{89.041+106.507}{2}-86 \right)= 5.17 Btu/hr \)

The total convective heat ﬂow rate for element 3 is then

\(\dot{H}^{(e)}_{h} =I_{1} +I_{2}+I_{3}=30.95 Btu/hr \)
