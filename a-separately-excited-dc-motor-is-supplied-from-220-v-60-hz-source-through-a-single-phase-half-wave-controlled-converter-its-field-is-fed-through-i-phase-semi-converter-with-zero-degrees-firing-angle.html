Guest Question.

A separately excited DC motor is supplied from \(220 \mathrm{~V}, 60 \mathrm{~Hz}\) source through a single-phase half-wave controlled converter. Its field is fed through i-phase semi converter with zero degrees firing-angle delay. Motor resistance \(R_{a}=0.1 \Omega\), and motor constant \(=0.6 \mathrm{~V}\)-sec/rad. For rated load torque of \(15 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m}\) at \(1200 \mathrm{rpm}\) and for continuous ripple free currents, determine:

  1. The firing angle delay of the armature converter
  2. The RMS value of thyristor and freewheeling diode currents
  3. The input power factor of the armature converter.
Admin
Solution
  1. The average current \(=15 / 0.5=30 \mathrm{~A}\)

\( \begin{array}{l} \omega_{m}=\frac{2 \pi \cdot n_{m}}{60} \\ \omega_{m}=\frac{2 \pi \cdot 1200}{60}=125.66 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{sec} . \end{array} \)

Motor EMF \(E_{a}=K_{m} \cdot \omega_{m}=0.6 \times 125.66=75.4 \mathrm{~V}\).

For 1-phase half-wave converter feeding a DC motor,

\( \begin{array}{c} V_{0}=V_{t}=\frac{V_{m}}{2 \pi}(1+\cos \alpha)=E_{a}+I_{a} r_{a} \\ V_{0}=V_{t}=\frac{220 \cdot \sqrt{2}}{2 \pi}(1+\cos \alpha)=75.4+30 \times 0.1 . \end{array} \)

Solving for \(\alpha\) to get \(\alpha=54.32^{\circ}=0.948 \mathrm{rad}\).

  1. RMS value of thyristor current is:

\( \begin{array}{l} I_{s r}=\sqrt{I_{a}^{2} \frac{\pi-\alpha}{2 \pi}}=I_{a}\left(\frac{\pi-\alpha}{2 \pi}\right)^{1 / 2} \\ I_{s r}=30\left(\frac{\pi-0.948}{2 \pi}\right)^{1 / 2}=17.725 \mathrm{~A}, \end{array} \)

the RMS value of freewheeling diode current is:

\( \begin{array}{l} I_{s r}=\sqrt{I_{a}^{2} \frac{\pi+\alpha}{2 \pi}}=I_{a}\left(\frac{\pi+\alpha}{2 \pi}\right)^{1 / 2} \\ I_{s r}=30\left(\frac{\pi+0.948}{2 \pi}\right)^{1 / 2}=24.2 \mathrm{~A} . \end{array} \)

  1. The input power factor of armature converter:

\( \begin{array}{c} p f=\frac{V_{t} \cdot I_{a}}{V_{s} \cdot I_{s r}} \\ p f=\frac{78.4 \times 30}{220 \times 24.2}=0.441 \text { lagging. } \end{array} \)
