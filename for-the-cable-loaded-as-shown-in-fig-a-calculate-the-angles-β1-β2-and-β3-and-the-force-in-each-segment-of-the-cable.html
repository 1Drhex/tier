Guest Question.

For the cable loaded as shown in Fig. (a), calculate the angles \(β_{1}, β_{2}\), and \(β_{3}\) and the force in each segment of the cable.

Admin
Solution

Method of Analysis

The free-body diagram of the entire cable is shown in Fig. (b). Its main function is to identify the variables and to enforce the sign conventions defined in Fig. 6.12 (recall that the positive directions for the θ’s are counterclockwise and that positive h is measured downward from end B).

Observe that the length of each cable segment is given. As pointed out in Art. 6.6, in problems of this type (and this problem is no exception) it is seldom possible to obtain a solution without having to solve nonlinear, simultaneous equations. An inspection of Fig. (b) reveals that two or more unknown angles would appear in each equilibrium equation for the entire cable (this would also be the situation if the equilibrium of any portion of the cable is analyzed). Therefore, the most direct method of solution is to write and solve Eqs. (6.19) \(T_{0}(\tan \theta _{i+1}-\tan \theta _{i})=W_{i}\) and (6.21) \(h=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{s_{i}\sin \theta _{i}}\) \(L=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{s_{i}\cos \theta _{i}}\) , giving us a total of four equations containing the unknowns \(T_{0}\), \(θ_{1}, θ_{2}\), and \(θ_{3}\). Because the equations are nonlinear, the solution must be obtained numerically by a computer program capable of solving nonlinear, simultaneous equations. Afterthe solution has been found, the force in each segment can be calculated from Eqs. (6.18) \(T_{i}\cos \theta _{i}=T_{0}\).

Mathematical Details

On substituting the given values, Eqs. (6.19) yield

(i = 1) \(T_{0}(\tan θ_{2} − \tan θ_{1}) = W_{1}\)

 

\(T_{0}(\tan θ_{2} − \tan θ_{1}) = 1600\) (a)

and

(i = 2) \(T_{0}(\tan θ_{3} − \tan θ_{2}) = W_{2}\)

 

\(T_{0}(\tan θ_{3} − \tan θ_{2}) = 2000\) (b)

and Eqs. (6.21) become

\(s_{1}\sin \theta _{1}+s_{2}\sin \theta _{2}+s_{3}\sin \theta _{3}=L\)

 

\(8 \sin \theta _{1} + 12 \sin \theta _{2} + 10 \sin \theta _{3} = −6\) (c)

and

\(s_{1} \cos \theta _{1} + s_{2} \cos \theta _{2} + s_{3} \cos \theta _{3} = L\)

 

\(8 \cos \theta _{1} + 12 \cos \theta _{2} + 10 \cos \theta _{3} = 24\) (d)

The solution of Eqs. (a)–(d), which can be verified by substitution, is

\(T_{0}\) = 1789 lb

 

\(θ_{1} = −53.62^{\circ }(= −β_{1})\)

 

\(θ_{2} = −24.83^{\circ }(= −β_{2})\)

 

\(θ_{3} = 33.23^{\circ } (= β_{3})\)

Using Eqs. (6.18), the tensions in the cable segments are

\(T_{1}=\frac{T_{0}}{\cos \theta _{1}}=\frac{1789}{\cos (-53.62^{\circ })}=3020 N\)

 

\(T_{2}=\frac{T_{0}}{\cos \theta _{2}}=\frac{1789}{\cos (-24.83^{\circ })}=1971 N\)

 

\(T_{3}=\frac{T_{0}}{\cos \theta _{3}}=\frac{1789}{\cos (-33.23^{\circ })}=2140 N\)
