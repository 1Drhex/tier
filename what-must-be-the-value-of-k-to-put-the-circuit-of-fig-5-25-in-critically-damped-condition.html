Guest Question.

What must be the value of k to put the circuit of Fig. 5.25 in critically damped condition?

Admin
Solution

6.25. We need to determine the characteristic equation of the circuit based on the preferred variable (I(t)).

Defining the voltage of the independent voltage source based on the node voltages in the circuit of Fig. 6.25.2:

\(KI(t)=V_{L}(t)-V_{C}(t)\Rightarrow V_{L}(t)=kI(t)+V_{C}(t) \)…….(1)

Applying Ohm’s law for the 2 Ω resistor:

\(V_{C}(t)=2I(t)\) …….(2)

\(I_{L}(t)+\frac{V_{L}(t)}{\frac{4}{7}}+I_{C}(t)+I(t)=0\Rightarrow I_{L}(t)+\frac{7}{4}V_{L}(t)+I_{C}(t)+I(t)=0\) ……(3)

The voltage-current relation of the capacitor:

\(I_{C}(t)=C\frac{d}{dt}V_{C}(t)=2×\frac{d}{dt}V_{C}(t)\) …….(4)

The voltage-current relation of the inductor:

\(I_{L}(t)=I_{L}(0^{-})+\frac{1}{L}\int_{0^{-}}^{t}V_{L}(t).dt=0+\frac{1}{1}×\int_{0^{-}}^{t}V_{L}(t).dt=\int_{0^{-}}^{t}V_{L}(t).dt\) …….(5)

In (5), \(I_{L}(t)(0^{-})\) is assumed to be zero, since the general response of a circuit does not depend on its primary condition.

Using (4) and (5) in (3):

\(\int_{0^{-}}^{t}V_{L}(t).dt+\frac{7}{4}V_{L}(t)+2\frac{d}{dt}V_{C}(t)+I(t)=0 \) \(\frac{d}{dt}\Rightarrow V_{L}(t)+\frac{7}{4}\frac{d}{dt}V_{L}(t)+2\frac{d²}{dt²}V_{C}(t)+\frac{d}{dt}I(t)=0\)

Using (1)\( \Rightarrow kI(t)+V_{C}(t)+\frac{7}{4}\frac{d}{dt}(kI(t)+V_{C}(t))+2\frac{d²}{dt²}V_{C}(t)+\frac{d}{dt}I(t)=0\)

\(\Rightarrow kI(t)+V_{C}(t)+(k\frac{7}{4}+1)\frac{d}{dt}I_{L}(t)+\frac{7}{4}\frac{d}{dt}V_{C}(t)+2\frac{d²}{dt²}V_{C}(t)=0\)

Using (2)\(\Rightarrow kI(t)+2I(t)+(k\frac{7}{4}+1)\frac{d}{dt}I_{L}(t)+\frac{7}{4}\frac{d}{dt}(2I(t))+2\frac{d²}{dt²}(2I(t))=0\)

\(\Rightarrow 4\frac{d²}{dt²}(I(t)) + (k\frac{7}{4}+\frac{9}{2})\frac{d}{dt}I(t)+(k+2)I(t)\) =0

\(\Rightarrow \frac{d²}{dt²}(I(t)) + (k\frac{7}{16}+\frac{9}{8})\frac{d}{dt}I(t)+\frac{(k+2)}{4}I(t) =0 \)…….(6)

The characteristic equation of (6) is:

\(s²+(k\frac{7}{16}+\frac{9}{8})s+\frac{(k+2)}{4}=0 \)……(7)

To adjust the circuit in the critically damped condition, the delta (discriminant) of the quadratic characteristic equation must be zero (Δ = 0). Therefore:

\(Δ=(k\frac{7}{16}+\frac{9}{8})^{2}-4(1)(\frac{(k+2)}{4})=0\Rightarrow \frac{49}{256}k^{2}+\frac{63}{64}k+\frac{81}{64}-k-2=0\) \(\Rightarrow \frac{49}{256}k^{2}-\frac{1}{64}k-\frac{47}{64}-k-2=0 \Rightarrow k=\frac{\frac{1}{64}± \sqrt{(\frac{1}{64})^{2}+4\frac{49}{256}\frac{47}{64}}}{\frac{98}{256}}=\frac{\frac{1}{64}± \sqrt{\frac{9}{16}}}{\frac{98}{256}}\) \(\Rightarrow \frac{\frac{1}{64}±\frac{1}{4}}{\frac{98}{256}}=\frac{4±192}{98}=-\frac{94}{49},2\)

Therefore, k = 2 is one of the answers.
