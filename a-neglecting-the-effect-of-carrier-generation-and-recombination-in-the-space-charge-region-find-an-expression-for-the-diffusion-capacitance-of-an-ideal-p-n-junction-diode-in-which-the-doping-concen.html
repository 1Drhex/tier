Guest Question.

(a) Neglecting the effect of carrier generation and recombination in the space-charge region,
find an expression for the diffusion capacitance of an ideal p-n junction diode in which the doping concentrations of the p- and n-sides are comparable to each other.
(b) Express the diffusion capacitance of part (a) in terms of the electron and hole currents \(I_{np}(O) \)and \(I_{pn}(O) \) respectively.

(c) Verify the validity of Eq. (5.26), \(I=I_{o}[exp(V/V_{T})-1] \) , using the charge control model of the p-n junction diode of part (a).
(d) How can you modify the results of parts (a) and (b) to obtain the diffusion capacitance of a practical diode where the current-voltage relation is described by Eq. (5.33) , \(I=I_{o}[exp(V/\eta V_{T})-1] \) ?

Admin
Solution

Consider the coordinate system for the p-n junction as shown in Fig. 5.5b. Further, assume that \(\Delta p_{n}(x) \) and \(\Delta n_{p}(x) \) be the excess hole and electron densities in the n- and p- materials, respectively. Using
Eqs (5.16), \( p_{n}(x)=p_{no}+P_{n}(0)exp(-x/L_{p})\), (5.23), \( P_{n}(0)=p_{no}[exp(V/V_{T})-1]\) , and (5.26), \( I=I_{o}[exp(V/V_{T})-1]\) , \(\Delta p_{n}(x) \) can be given by

\( \Delta p_{n}(x)=p_{n}(x)-p_{no}=P_{n}(0)exp(-x/L_{p})=p_{no}exp(-x/L_{p})[exp(V/V_{T}-1)]\)

Following the similar reason, \( \Delta n_{p}(x)\) can also be expressed as

\( \Delta n_{p}(x)=n_{po}exp(-x/L_{n})[exp(V/V_{T}-1)]\)

where x ≤ 0 is the distance measured from the junction along the –x axis.
If the excess charge in the n- and p- sides of the junction are \(Q_{pn}\) and \(Q_{np}\) due to the diffusion of holes from the p- to n- side and electron from n- to p- side of the junction at x = 0 respectively then we can write

\(Q_{pn}=Ae\int_{0}^{\infty }{\Delta p_{n}(x)dx}=Ae p_{no} L_{p}[exp(V/V_{T}-1)]=\left\lgroup\frac{Ae p_{no} L_{p}}{I_{o}} \right\rgroup I \)

and

\(Q_{np}=Ae\int_{-\infty }^{0 }{\Delta n_{p}(x)dx}=\left\lgroup\frac{Ae n_{po} L_{n}}{I_{o}} \right\rgroup I \)

where A is the cross-sectional area of the junction and I is the diode current described by Eq. (5.26). Note that, we have considered only the magnitude of the charge in the above charge expressions. Using Eq. (5.51), \(C_{D}=\frac{dQ}{dV} =AeL_{p}\frac{dP_{n}(0)}{dV} \) , the diffusion capacitance due to hole in th n-side is given by

\(C_{Dp}=\frac{dQ_{pn}}{dV}=\left\lgroup\frac{Ae p_{no}L_{p}}{I_{o}} \right\rgroup \frac{dI}{dV} \)

Similarly, the diffusion capacitance due to electron in the p-material is given by

\(C_{Dn}=\frac{dQ_{np}}{dV}=\left\lgroup\frac{Ae n_{po}L_{n}}{I_{o}} \right\rgroup \frac{dI}{dV} \)

Thus, the total diffusion capacitance is obtained as

\(C_{D}= C_{Dp}+C_{Dn} \)

\(C_{D}= \left\{\frac{Ae}{I_{o}}(p_{no}L_{p}+n_{po}L_{n}) \right\} \frac{dI}{dV} \)

\(C_{D}= \left\{\frac{Ae}{V_{T}}(p_{no}L_{p}+n_{po}L_{n}) \right\} \left\lgroup1+\frac{I}{I_{o}}\right\rgroup \)

\(C_{D}= \left\{\frac{Ae}{V_{T} I_{o}}(p_{no}L_{p}+n_{po}L_{n}) \right\} I \)

Hence, the diffusion capacitance is approximately proportional to the current passing through the diode.
(b) The diffusion capacitance can be also expressed in terms of the electron and hole currents at the junction as follows.
Using Eqs (5.24) , \(I_{pn}(0) =\frac{Ae D_{p} p_{no}}{L_{p}} [exp(V/V_{T})-1] \) , and (5.27), \( I_{o \equiv }\frac{Ae D_{p} p_{no}}{L_{p}} +\frac{Ae D_{n} n_{po}}{L_{n}} \) , we can write

\(I=\left\lgroup\frac{I_{o}L_{p}}{Ae D_{p}p_{no}} \right\rgroup I_{pn}(0) = \left\lgroup\frac{I_{o}L_{n}}{Ae D_{n}n_{po}} \right\rgroup I_{np}(0)\)

Substituting the above expression of I in the expression of CD of part (a), the diffusion capacitance can be expressed as

\(C_{D}=\frac{1}{V_{T}}\left\lgroup\frac{L^2_{p}}{D_{p}}I_{pn}(0)+ \frac{L^2_{n}}{D_{n}}I_{np}(0) \right\rgroup \)

\(=\frac{1}{V_{T}} (\tau _{p}I_{pn}(0)+\tau _{n}I_{np}(0)) \)

where \(\tau _{p} \) and \(\tau _{n} \) are the mean lifetime of holes and electrons in the n- and p- materials.

(c) Using the charge control model, the hole and electron currents at the junction are given by

\(I_{pn}(0)=\frac{Q_{pn}}{\tau _{p}} =\frac{Ae n_{po} D_{n}}{L_{n}} [exp(V/V_{T})-1] \)

and

\(I_{pn}(0)=\frac{Q_{np}}{\tau _{n}} =\frac{Ae n_{po} D_{n}}{L_{n}} [exp(V/V_{T})-1] \)

Hence, the total current passing through the junction I is obtained as

\( I=I_{pn}(0)+I_{np}(0)=I_{o}[exp(V/V_{T})-1] \)

where \( I_{o} \) is same as given by Eq. (5.27). This shows the validity of the charge control model in determining the current of an ideal p-n junction diode where the generation and recombination of thermally generated electron-hole
pairs in the space-charge region is neglected.

(d) Comparing the current-voltage relation described by Eqs (5.26) and (5.33), \(I=I_{o}[exp(V/\eta V_{T})-1] \) , we can simply replace \( V_{T} \) by \(\eta V_{T} \) in the expressions for the diffusion capacitance of part (a) and (b) to modify the same for a practical diode where the generation and recombination of carriers in the space-charge region can not be neglected. Thus, the diffusion capacitance in this case can be obtained from the result of Part (a) as

\(C_{D}=\left\{\frac{Ae}{\eta V_{T}}(p_{no}L_{p}+n_{po}L_{n}) \right\} I \)

Similarly, the result of part (b) can be given in this case as

\(C_{D}=\frac{1}{\eta V_{T}} (\tau _{p}I_{pn}(0)+\tau _{n}I_{np}(0)) \)

For the special case of \(\tau _{p}=\tau _{n}=\tau \), the diffusion capacitance \(C_{D}=\frac{\tau I}{\eta V_{T}} \) which is exactly identical to Eq. (5.57).
