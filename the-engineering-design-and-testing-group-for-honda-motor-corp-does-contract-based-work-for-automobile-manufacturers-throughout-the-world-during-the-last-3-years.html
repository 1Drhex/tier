The engineering design and testing group for Honda Motor Corp. does contract-based work for automobile manufacturers throughout the world. During the last 3 years, the net cash flows for contract payments have varied widely, as shown below, primarily due to a large manufacturerâ€™s inability to pay its contract fee.

Year 0 1 2 3
Cash Flow ($1000) +2000 -500 -8100 +6800

Solution

a. Table 6.11 shows the annual cash flows and cumulative cash flows. Since there are two sign changes in the cash flow sequence, the rule of signs indicates a maximum of two \(i^{*}\) values. The cumulative cash flow sequence has two sign changes, indicating there is not just one nonnegative root. The conclusion is that as many as two \(i^{*}\) values can be found.

b. The PW relation is
PW =2000- 500(P/F,i,1)- 8100(P/F,i,2) + 6800(P/F,i,3)

Select values of \(i\) to find the two \(i^{*}\) values, and plot PW versus \(i\).

The PW values are shown below and plotted in Figure 6.3 (using a smooth approximation) for \(i\) values of 0, 5, 10, 20, 30, 40, and 50%. The characteristic parabolic shape for a second-degree polynomial is obtained, with PW crossing the \(i\) axis at approximately \(i_{1}^{*}\) =8 and \(i_{2}^{*} \)=41%.
