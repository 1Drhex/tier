Guest Question.

Let W be a subspace of \( R^{n} \). Exercise 30 establishes that \( W^{\bot } \) is also a subspace of \( R^{n} \). Prove that \( \text{dim} W + \text{dim} W^{\bot } = n \).

Admin
Solution

If W ≠ {0}, let {\( b_{1} , . . . , b_{p} \)} be a basis for W , where 1 ≤ p ≤ n. Let A be the p × n matrix having rows \( b^{T}_{1} , . . . , b^{T}_{p} \) . It follows that W is the row space of A. Theorem 3 implies that \( W^{\bot } = (\text{Row }A)^{\bot} = \text{Nul }A \) and hence \( \text{dim }W^{\bot } = \text{dim Nul }A \). Thus, \( \text{dim }W + \text{dim }W^{\bot } = \text{dim Row }A + \text{dim Nul }A = \text{rank }A + \text{dim Nul }A = n \), by the Rank Theorem. If W = {0}, then \( W^{\bot } = \mathbb{R} ^{n} \), and the result follows.

THEOREM 3 :

Let A be an m × n matrix. The orthogonal complement of the row space of A is the null space of A, and the orthogonal complement of the column space of A is the null space of \( A^{T} \) :

\( (\text{Row }A)^{\bot} = \text{Nul }A \text{and} (\text{Col }A)^{\bot} = \text{Nul }A^{T} \)
