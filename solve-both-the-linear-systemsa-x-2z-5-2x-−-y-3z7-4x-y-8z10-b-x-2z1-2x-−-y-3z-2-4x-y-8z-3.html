Guest Question.

Solve both the linear systems:

\(\begin{aligned}x+2 z &=5 \\\text { (a) } 2 x-y+3 z &=7 \\4 x+y+8 z &=10\end{aligned}\) \(\begin{aligned}x+2 z &=1 \\\text { (b) } 2 x-y+3 z &=2 \\4 x+y+8 z &=3\end{aligned}\)

Admin
Solution

(a) Writing this in matrix form \(\mathrm{Ax}=\mathrm{b}\) where

\(\mathrm{A}=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}1 & 0 & 2 \\2 & -1 & 3 \\4 & 1 & 8\end{array}\right), \quad \mathrm{x}=\left(\begin{array}{l}x \\y \\z\end{array}\right) \text { and } \mathbf{b}=\left(\begin{array}{r}5 \\7 \\10\end{array}\right)\)

By the above result \((1.36)\) we have \(\mathrm{x}=\mathrm{A}^{-1} \mathbf{b}\), provided \(\mathrm{A}\) is invertible. What is the inverse matrix \(\mathrm{A}^{-1}\) equal to?

The matrix \(\mathrm{A}\) is the same matrix as the previous example, therefore the inverse matrix is already evaluated:

\(\mathbf{A}^{-1}=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}-11 & 2 & 2 \\-4 & 0 & 1 \\6 & -1 & -1\end{array}\right)\)

Hence

\(\begin{aligned}\mathrm{x}=\left(\begin{array}{l}x \\y \\z\end{array}\right) &=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}-11 & 2 & 2 \\-4 & 0 & 1 \\6 & -1 & -1\end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{r}5 \\7 \\10\end{array}\right) \quad\left[\mathrm{x}=\mathrm{A}^{-1} \mathbf{b}\right] \\&=\left(\begin{array}{r}-(11 \times 5)+(2 \times 7)+(2 \times 10) \\-(4 \times 5)+(0 \times 7)+(1 \times 10) \\(6 \times 5)-(1 \times 7)-(1 \times 10)\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{r}-21 \\-10 \\13\end{array}\right)\end{aligned}\)

We have \(x=-21, y=-10\) and \(z=13\). You can check this is the solution to the given linear system by substituting these values into the system.

(b) The coefficient matrix is the same and we have only changed the vector b. Hence

\(\mathbf{x}=\left(\begin{array}{c}x \\y \\z\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}-11 & 2 & 2 \\-4 & 0 & 1 \\6 & -1 & -1\end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l}1 \\2 \\3\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{r}-1 \\-1 \\1\end{array}\right)\left[\text { because } \mathrm{x}=\mathrm{A}^{-1} \mathbf{b}\right]\)

We have \(x=-1, y=-1\) and \(z=1\)
