Guest Question.

A hollow steel shaft with an internal diameter of \(d\) = 8 in. and an outside diameter of D=12 in. is to be replaced by a solid alloy shaft. If the maximum shear stress has the same value in both shafts, calculate the diameter of the latter and the ratio of the torsional rigidities \(G J \cdot G_{\text {steel }}=2.4 G_{\text {alloy }}\) .

Admin
Solution

Because the maximum shear stress must be the same in both shafts under the same
torque, from Equation (3.2), \(\left[r_{\max } / J\right]_{\text {steel }}\) must be equal to\(\left[r_{\max } / J\right]_{\text {alloy }}\).

 

\(G J \cdot G_{\text {steel }}=2.4 G_{\text {alloy }}\) (3.2)

 

Thus:

 

\(\frac{D_{\text {steel }} / 2}{J_{\text {steel }}}=\frac{D_{\text {alloy }} / 2}{J_{\text {alloy }}}\) (i)

 

For the shafts:

 

\(J_{\text {steel }}=\frac{\pi}{32}\left(12^{4}-8^{4}\right)=520 \pi \text { in. }^{4}\)

 

\(J_{\text {alloy }}=\frac{\pi}{32} D_{\text {alloy }}^{4}\)

 

From (i):

 

\(\frac{6}{520 \pi}=\frac{D_{\text {alloy }} / 2}{\frac{\pi}{32} D_{\text {alloy }}^{4}}\)

 

Hence,\(D_{\text {alloy }}^{3}=1386 \text { in. }^{3} \text { and } D_{\text {alloy }}=11.15 \text { in. }\)

 

Ratio of torsional rigidity:

 

\(\frac{G_{\text {steel }} J_{\text {steel }}}{G_{\text {alloy }} J_{\text {alloy }}}=\frac{G_{\text {steel }}}{G_{\text {alloy }}} \times \frac{D_{\text {steel }}}{D_{\text {alloy }}}\) \([\text { from (i)] }\)

 

\(=2.4 \times \frac{12}{11.15}=2.58\)

 

That is, the torsional rigidity of the steel is 2.58 times that of the alloy shaft. This ratio means that, though replacing the steel shaft by the alloy one meets the strength requirement, the angle of twist of the alloy shaft will be 2.58 times larger than that of the steel.
