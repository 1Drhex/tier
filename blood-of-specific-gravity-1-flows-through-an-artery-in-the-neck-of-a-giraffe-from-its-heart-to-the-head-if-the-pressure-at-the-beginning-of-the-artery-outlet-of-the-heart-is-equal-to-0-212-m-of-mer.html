Guest Question.

Blood of specific gravity 1 flows through an artery in the neck of a giraffe from its heart to the head. If the pressure at the beginning of the artery (outlet of the heart) is equal to 0.212 m of mercury, determine the pressure at the end of the artery when the head is:

(a) 2.4 m above the heart
(b) 1.8 m below the heart

Admin
Solution

Fig. 2.18 shows the position of heart 1 and neck 2 of the giraffe

(a) Applying Bemoullis equation between 1 and 2

\( \frac{P_{1}}{\gamma } + \frac{V^{2}_{1}}{2g} + z_{1}=\frac{P_{2}}{\gamma } + \frac{V^{2}_{2}}{2g} + z_{2}\)

 

\(\therefore \frac{P_{2}}{\gamma }=\left\lgroup\frac{P_{1}}{\gamma } \right\rgroup_{Hg} + z_{1} – z_{2}\)

Equating pressure developed by column of mercury to column of blood, thus

.\( P=\rho g h\)

 

\(\therefore \left(\rho gh\right)_{Hg} = \left(\rho gh\right)_{Blood}\)

 

\( 13.6\times 1000 \times 0.212 = 1\times 1000\times h_{Blood}\)

 

\( h_{Blood} = 2.88 m\)

 

\(\therefore \frac{P_{2}}{\gamma } =\left\lgroup\frac{P_{1}}{\gamma } \right\rgroup_{Blood} + \left(z_{1} – z_{2}\right)\)

 

\(\therefore \frac{P_{2}}{\gamma } = 2.88 + 0 – 2.4 = 0.48\)

 

\( P_{2} = 0.48 \times 1000 \times 9.8 = 4.7 kPa\)

(b) When the head is 1.8 m below the heart

\( \frac{P_{2}}{\gamma } = \left\lgroup\frac{P_{1}}{\gamma } \right\rgroup_{Blood} + z_{1} – z_{2}\)

 

\( \frac{P_{2}}{\gamma } = 2.88 + 0 – \left( -1.8\right)\)

 

\( = 4.68\)

 

\( P_{2} = 4.68\times 1000\times 9.8 Pa\)

 

\( P_{2} = 45.8 kPa\)
