Guest Question.

Find the deflection at C for the beam in Figure P17.82/83. Use the virtual-work method and assume that \(E I=11.4 \times 10^{6} \text { kip-in. }^{2}\) for the beam.

Admin
Solution

Virtual Moment m: To determine the deflection of the beam, first remove the real load from the beam and apply a virtual unit load downward at A. The beam with this loading is shown.

 

 

Cut a free-body diagram through the beam at section a–a between A and B.
Derive a moment equation m from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{array}{rl}\Sigma M_{a-a} & =-(0.5 kip ) x_{1}+m=0 \\& \therefore m=(0.5 \text { kip }) x_{1} & 0 \leq x_{1}<6 ft\end{array}\)

 

 

Cut a second free-body diagram through the beam at section b–b between C and D. Derive a moment equation m from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{array}{rlr}\Sigma M_{b-b} & =(0.5 kips ) x_{2}-m=0 \\& \therefore m=(0.5 \text { kips }) x_{2} & 0 \leq x_{2}<9 ft\end{array}\)

 

 

Cut a third free-body diagram through the beam at section c–c between B and C. Derive a moment equation m from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{c-c}=(0.5 kips ) x_{3}-(1 kips )\left(x_{3}-9 ft \right)-m=0 \\\therefore m=(0.5 kips ) x_{3}-(1 kips )\left(x_{3}-9 ft \right) \\\quad=-(0.5 kips ) x_{3}+9 kip – ft \quad 9 ft \leq x_{3}<12 ft\end{aligned}\)

 

 

 

Real Moment M: Remove the virtual load and reapply the real loading. The free-body diagram of the beam is shown.

 

 

 

Draw a free-body diagram that cuts through the beam at section a–a and derive a moment equation M from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{a-a} &=90 \text { kip-ft }-(29 \text { kips }) x_{1}+M=0 & \\& \therefore M=(29 kips ) x_{1}-90 \text { kip-ft } & 0 \leq x_{1}<6 ft\end{aligned}\)

 

 

Draw a second free-body diagram that cuts through the beam at section b–b and derive a moment equation M from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma M_{b-b}=-\frac{6 kips / ft }{2} x_{2}^{2}+(43 kips ) x_{2}-M=0 \\&\therefore M=-\frac{6 kips / ft }{2} x_{2}^{2}+(43 kips ) x_{2} \quad 0 \leq x_{2}<12 ft\end{aligned}\)

 

 

 

Virtual-Work Equation for Beam Deflection: From Equation (17.35), the beam deflection at C can now be determined.

\(\begin{aligned}&(1 kip ) \cdot \Delta_{C}=\int_{0}^{L} m\left(\frac{M}{E I}\right) d x\\&=\frac{1}{E I} \int_{0}^{6}\left(0.5 x_{1}\right)\left(29 x_{1}-90\right) d x_{1}+\frac{1}{E I} \int_{0}^{9}\left(0.5 x_{2}\right)\left(-\frac{6}{2} x_{2}^{2}+43 x_{2}\right) d x_{2}\\&+\frac{1}{E I} \int_{9}^{12}\left(-0.5 x_{3}+9\right)\left(-\frac{6}{2} x_{3}^{2}+43 x_{3}\right) d x_{3}\\&=\frac{14.5}{E I} \int_{0}^{6} x_{1}^{2} d x_{1}-\frac{45}{E I} \int_{0}^{6} x_{1} d x_{1}-\frac{1.5}{E I} \int_{0}^{9} x_{2}^{3} d x_{2}+\frac{21.5}{E I} \int_{0}^{9} x_{2}^{2} d x_{2}\\&+\frac{1.5}{E I} \int_{9}^{12} x_{3}^{3} d x_{3}-\frac{21.5}{E I} \int_{9}^{12} x_{3}^{2} d x_{3}-\frac{27}{E I} \int_{9}^{12} x_{3}^{2} d x_{3}+\frac{387}{E I} \int_{9}^{12} x_{3} d x_{3}\\&=\frac{14.5}{3 E I}\left[x_{1}^{3}\right]_{0}^{6}-\frac{45}{2 E I}\left[x_{1}^{2}\right]_{0}^{6}-\frac{1.5}{4 E I}\left[x_{2}^{4}\right]_{0}^{9}+\frac{21.5}{3 E I}\left[x_{2}^{3}\right]_{0}^{9}\\&+\frac{1.5}{4 E I}\left[x_{3}^{4}\right]_{9}^{12}-\frac{21.5}{3 E I}\left[x_{3}^{3}\right]_{9}^{12}-\frac{27}{3 E I}\left[x_{3}^{3}\right]_{9}^{12}+\frac{387}{2 E I}\left[x_{3}^{2}\right]_{9}^{12}\\&=\frac{1,044}{E I}-\frac{810}{E I}-\frac{2,460.3750}{E I}+\frac{5,224.5}{E I}+\frac{5,315.625}{E I}-\frac{7,159.5}{E I}-\frac{8,991}{E I}+\frac{12,190.5}{E I}\\&=\frac{4,353.75}{E I}=\frac{\left(4,353.75 kip ^{2}- ft ^{3}\right)(12 in . / ft )^{3}}{11.4 \times 10^{6} kip – in. ^{2}}=0.659937 \text { kip-in. }\\&\therefore \Delta_{C}=0.660 in. \downarrow\end{aligned}\)
