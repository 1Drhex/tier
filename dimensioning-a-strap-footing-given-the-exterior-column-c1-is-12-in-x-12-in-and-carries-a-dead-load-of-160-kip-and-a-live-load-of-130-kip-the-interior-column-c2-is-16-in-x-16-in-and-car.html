Guest Question.

Dimensioning a Strap Footing

Given: The exterior column \(C_{1}\) is 12 in. \(\times\) 12 in. and carries a dead load of 160 kip and a live load of 130 kip. The interior column \(C_{2}\) is 16 in. \(\times\) 16 in. and carries a dead load of 200 kip and a live load of 185 kip. The property line is at the edge of column #1 and the distance between the center lines of the columns #1 and #2 is 20 ft. The effective soil pressure is \(q_{e}=4.625\: kip/ft^{2}\) (Figs. E7.6a and E7.6b).

Determine: The dimensions of the footings for both columns using the two methods described above.

Admin
Solution

Procedure #1: The individual column loads are:

 

\(P_{1}=160+130=290\) kip

 

\(P_{2}=200+185=385\) kip

 

Next, we locate the resultant of the column loads.

 

\(R=P_{1}+P_{2}=675\) kip

 

\(d_{1}=x_{1}=\frac{385}{675}(20)=11.407\) ft

 

\(d_{1}=x_{1}=20-11.407=8.593\) ft

 

Noting (7.16), we obtain:

(7.16)= \(A_{1}+A_{2}\geq \frac{P_{1}+P_{2}}{q_{e}} \)

 

\(A_{1}+A_{2}=\frac{675}{4.625}\geq 146\, ft^{2}\)

 

We estimate \(A_{2}\) knowing \(R_{2}\), the resultant of the pressure distribution acting on footing # 2, is less than \(P_{2}\).

 

\(A_{2}\lesssim \frac{385}{4.625}=83\, ft^{2}\)

 

We take \(L_{2}=B_{2}=8.5\, ft(A_{2}=72.25\, ft^{2})\).

 

Then \(A_{1}\geq 73.75\, ft^{2}\)

 

Noting (7.17),

(7.17)= \(A_{1}\overline{d_{1}}=A_{2}\overline{d_{2}} \)

 

\(73.75\bar{d}_{1}=72.25(8.593)\Rightarrow \bar{d}_{1}=8.418\) ft

 

Then

 

\(\frac{L_{1}}{2}\approx e_{1}+(d_{1}-\bar{d})=0.5+(11.407-8.418)=3.49\) ft

 

Take \(L_{1}=7 ft and B_{1}=10.75\, ft(A_{1}=75.25\, ft^{2})\)

 

The final dimensions are shown below.

 

Procedure #2: We illustrate the second design approach here. We estimate \(A_{1}\) by requiring the pressure under the footing #1 to be equal to \(q_{e}\).

 

\(A_{1}> \frac{P_{1}}{q_{e}}=\frac{290}{4.625}=62.7\, ft^{2}\)

 

We take \(L_{1}=6\) ft as a first estimate. Then, noting Fig. E7.6b

 

\(e_{1}=\frac{L_{1}}{2}-0.5\approx 2.5\) ft

 

The remaining steps are listed below

 

\(V=\frac{P_{1}e_{1}}{d-e_{1}}=\frac{290(2.5)}{20-2.5}=41.43\) kip

 

\(R_{1}=P_{1}+V=290+41.43=331.43\) kip

 

\(R_{2}=P_{2}-V=385-41.43=343.57\) kip

 

\(A_{1_{required}}=\frac{331.43}{4.625}=71.66\, ft^{2}\Rightarrow B_{1}=\frac{71.66}{6}=11.94\Rightarrow L_{1}=6\, ft\; \; \; B_{1}=12\) ft

 

\(A_{2_{required}}=\frac{343.57}{4.625}=74.29\, ft^{2}\Rightarrow L_{2}=B_{2}=\sqrt{74.29}=8.62\Rightarrow L_{2}=B_{2}=8.75\)

 

The final dimensions are shown below.

 

Repeating this computation for the ultimate loading case,

 

\(P_{1u}=1.2P_{D}+1.6P_{L}=1.2(160)+1.6(130)=400\) kip

 

\(P_{2u}=1.2P_{D}+1.6P_{L}=1.2(200)+1.6(185)=536\) kip

 

and assuming the same value for \(e_{1}\) leads to

 

\(V_{u}=\frac{P_{u1}e_{1}}{d-e_{1}}=\frac{400(2.5)}{17.5}=57.14\) kip

 

\(R_{1u}=P_{1u}+V_{u}=400+57.14=457.14\) kip

 

\(q_{1u}=\frac{R_{1u}}{B_{1}L_{1}}=\frac{457.14}{6(12)}=6.35\, kip/ft^{2}\)

 

\(R_{2u}=P_{2u}-V_{u}=536-57.14=478.86\) kip

 

\(q_{2u}=\frac{R_{2u}}{B_{2}L_{2}}=\frac{478.86}{8.75(8.75)}=6.25\, kip/ft^{2}\)
