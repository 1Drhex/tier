Study of Maturity Variances (Hypothesis Tests for the Equality of Two Variances)

The research staff of Investors Now, an online financial trading firm, was interested in determining if there is a difference in the variance of the maturities of AAA-rated industrial bonds compared to CCC-rated industrial bonds.

Admin
Solution

This question requires that we design a study that compares the population variances of maturities for the two different bonds. We will test the null hypothesis

 

\(H_{0}:\sigma ^{2}_{x}=\sigma ^{2}_{y}\)

 

against the alternative hypothesis

 

\(H_{1}:\sigma ^{2}_{x}\neq \sigma ^{2}_{y}\)

 

where \(\sigma ^{2}_{x}\) is the variance in maturities for AAA-rated bonds and \(\sigma ^{2}_{y}\) is the variance in maturities for CCC-rated bonds. The significance level of the test was chosen as α = 0.02.

 

The decision rule is to reject \(H_{0}\) in favor of \(H_{1}\) if

 

\(\frac{S^{2}_{x} }{S^{2}_{y} } \gt F_{n_{x}-1,n_{y}-1,\alpha /2}\)

 

Note here that either sample variance could be larger, and we place the larger sample variance in the numerator. Hence, the probability for this upper tail is α>2. A random sample of 17 AAA-rated bonds resulted in a sample variance \(S^{2}_{x}\)= 123.35, and an independent random sample of 11 CCC-rated bonds resulted in a sample variance \(S^{2}_{y}\)= 8.02. The test statistic is as follows:

 

\(\frac{S^{2}_{x}}{S^{2}_{y}}=\frac{123.35}{8.02}=15.380\)

 

Given a significance level of α = 0.02, we find that the critical value of F, from interpolation in Appendix Table 9, is as follows:

 

\(F_{16,10,0.01}\) = 4.520

 

Clearly, the computed value of F (15.380) exceeds the critical value (4.520), and we reject \(H_{0}\) in favor of \(H_{1}\). Thus, there is strong evidence that variances in maturities are different for these two types of bonds.
