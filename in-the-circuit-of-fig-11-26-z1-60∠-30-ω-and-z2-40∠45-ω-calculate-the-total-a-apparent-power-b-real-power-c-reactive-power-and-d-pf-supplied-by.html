Guest Question.

In the circuit of Fig. 11.26, \( Z _{1}=60 \angle -30^{\circ} \Omega \) and \( Z _{2}=40 \angle 45^{\circ} \Omega \). Calculate the total: (a) apparent power, (b) real power, (c) reactive power, and (d) pf, supplied by the source and seen by the source.

Admin
Solution

The current through \(Z_{1}\) is

\( I _{1}=\frac{ V }{ Z _{1}}=\frac{120 \angle 10^{\circ}}{60 \angle -30^{\circ}}=2 \angle 40^{\circ} A rms \)

while the current through \(Z_{2}\) is

\(I _{2}=\frac{ V }{ Z _{2}}=\frac{120 \angle 10^{\circ}}{40 \angle 45^{\circ}}=3 \angle-35^{\circ} A rms\)

The complex powers absorbed by the impedances are

\(S _{1}=\frac{V_{ rms }^{2}}{ Z _{1}^{*}}=\frac{(120)^{2}}{60 \angle 30^{\circ}}=240\angle-30^{\circ}=207.85 – j 120 VA\)

\(S _{2}=\frac{V_{ rms }^{2}}{ Z _{2}^{*}}=\frac{(120)^{2}}{40 \angle-45^{\circ}}=360 \angle45^{\circ}=254.6 + j 254.6 VA\)

The total complex power is

\(S _{t}= S _{1} + S _{2}=462.4 + j 134.6 VA\)

(a) The total apparent power is

\(\left| S _{t}\right|=\sqrt{462.4^{2} + 134.6^{2}}=481.6 VA\).

(b) The total real power is

\(P_{t}=\operatorname{Re}\left( S _{t}\right)=462.4 W \text { or }P_{t}=P_{1} + P_{2}\).

(c) The total reactive power is

\(Q_{t}=\operatorname{Im}\left( S _{t}\right)=134.6 \text { VAR or } Q_{t}=Q_{1} + Q_{2}\).

(d) The \( pf =P_{t} /\left| S _{t}\right|=462.4 / 481.6=0.96\) (lagging).
We may cross check the result by finding the complex power \(S_{s}\) supplied by the source.

\(\begin{aligned}I _{t}= I _{1} + I _{2} &=(1.532 + j 1.286) + (2.457 – j 1.721) \\&=4 – j 0.435=4.024 \angle-6.21^{\circ} A rms\end{aligned}\)

\(\begin{aligned}S _{s}= V I _{t}^{*} &=\left(120 \angle 10^{\circ}\right)\left(4.024 \angle 6.21^{\circ}\right) \\&=482.88 \angle 16.21^{\circ}=463 + j 135 VA\end{aligned}\)

which is the same as before.
