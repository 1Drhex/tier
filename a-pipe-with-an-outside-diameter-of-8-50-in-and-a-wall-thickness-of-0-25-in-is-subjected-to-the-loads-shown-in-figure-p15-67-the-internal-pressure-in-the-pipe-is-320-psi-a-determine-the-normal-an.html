Guest Question.

A pipe with an outside diameter of 8.50 in. and a wall thickness of 0.25 in. is subjected to the loads shown in Figure P15.67. The internal pressure in the pipe is 320 psi.
(a) Determine the normal and shear stresses on the outer surface of the pipe at point K.
(b) Determine the principal stresses and maximum in-plane shear stress at point K and show the orientation of these stresses on an appropriate sketch.
(c) Compute the absolute maximum shear stress at K.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&d=8.50 in .-2(0.25 in .)=8.00 in . \\&A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[(8.50 in .)^{2}-(8.00 in .)^{2}\right]=6.479535 in. ^{2} \\&J=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[(8.50 in .)^{4}-(8.00 in .)^{4}\right]=110.354578 in .^{4} \\&I_{x}=I_{z}=\frac{\pi}{64}\left[(8.50 \text { in. })^{4}-(8.00 in .)^{4}\right]=55.177289 in. ^{4} \\&Q=\frac{1}{12}\left[(8.50 in .)^{3}-(8.00 \text { in. })^{3}\right]=8.510417 in .{ }^{3}\end{aligned}\)

Vector expression for the applied loads:
The loads applied to the structure can be expressed in vector form as:

\(F =(2.75 kips ) i -(1.9 kips ) k\)

The position vector from the section of interest to a point on the line of action of F is:

\(r =-(36 in .) i +(72 in .) j -(60 in .) k\)

The equivalent moments are found from the cross-product r Ã— F.

\(\begin{aligned}r \times F &=\left|\begin{array}{ccc} i & j & k \\-36 & 72 & -60 \\2.75 & 0 & -1.9\end{array}\right| \\&=-136.8 \text { kip-in. } i -233.4 \text { kip-in. } j -198.0 \text { kip-in. } k\end{aligned}\)

 

Equivalent forces at H and K: Equivalent moments at H and K:

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{x}=2.75 kips \\&F_{y}=0 kips \\&F_{z}=-1.90 kips\end{aligned}\) \(\begin{aligned}M_{x} &=-136.8 \text { kip-in. } \\M_{y} &=-233.4 \text { kip-in. } \\M_{z} &=-198.0 \text { kip-in. }\end{aligned}\)

Each of the non-zero forces and moments will be evaluated to determine whether stresses are created at the point of interest.

 

 

(a) Consider point K.
Force \(F _{x}\) creates a transverse shear stress in the xy plane at K. The magnitude of this shear stress is:

\(\tau_{x y}=\frac{(2.75 kips )\left(8.510417 in .^{3}\right)}{\left(55.177289 in .^{4}\right)[(8.50 in .)-(8.00 in .)]}=0.848 ksi\)

Force \(F _{z}\) does not cause either a normal stress or a shear stress at K.

Moment \(M _{x}\) creates bending stress at K. The magnitude of this stress is:

\(\sigma_{y}=\frac{M_{x} z}{I_{x}}=\frac{(136.8 kip – in .)(8.50 in . / 2)}{55.177289 in .^{4}}=10.537 ksi\)

Moment \(M _{y}\), which is a torque, creates a torsion shear stress in the xy plane at K. The magnitude of this shear stress is:

\(\tau_{x y}=\frac{M_{x} c}{J}=\frac{(233.4 kip – in .)(8.50 in . / 2)}{110.354578 in. ^{4}}=8.989 ksi\)

Moment \(M _{z}\) does not create bending stress at K because K is located on the neutral axis for bending about the z axis.

Stresses due to internal pressure:
The 320-psi internal fluid pressure creates tension normal stresses in the 0.25-in.-thick wall of the pipe.
The longitudinal stress (which acts in the y direction) in the pipe wall is:

\(\sigma_{\text {long }}=\frac{p d}{4 t}=\frac{(320 psi )(8.00 in .)}{4(0.25 \text { in. })}=2,560 psi =2.56 ksi ( T )\)

and the circumferential stress is:

\(\sigma_{\text {hoop }}=\frac{p d}{2 t}=\frac{(320 psi )(8.00 in .)}{2(0.25 in .)}=5,120 psi =5.12 ksi ( T )\)

The hoop stress acts in the x direction at K.

Summary of stresses at K:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=5.12 ksi ( T ) \\\sigma_{y} &=10.537 ksi +2.56 ksi \\&=13.097 ksi =13.10 ksi ( T ) \\\tau_{x y} &=0.848 ksi -8.989 ksi \\&=-8.140 ksi =-8.14 ksi\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Principal stress calculations:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq. (12.12).

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{(5.12)+(13.097)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(5.12)-(13.097)}{2}\right)^{2}+(-8.140)^{2}} \\&=9.108 \pm 9.065\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{p 1}=18.17 ksi \text { and } \quad \sigma_{p 2}=0.0434 ksi \\&\tau_{\max }=9.07 ksi \text { (maximum in-plane shear stress) } \\&\sigma_{\text {avg }}=9.11 ksi ( T ) \quad(\text { normal stress on planes of maximum in-plane shear stress) }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan 2 \theta_{p} &=\frac{-8.140}{[(5.12)-(13.097)] / 2}=\frac{-8.140}{-3.988}=2.040993 \\&\left.\therefore \theta_{p}=31.95^{\circ} \quad \text { (counterclockwise from the } x \text { axis to the direction of } \sigma_{p 2}\right)\end{aligned}\)

 

(c) Absolute maximum shear stress at K:

\(\tau_{\text {abs } \max }=\frac{18.174 ksi }{2}=9.09 ksi\)
