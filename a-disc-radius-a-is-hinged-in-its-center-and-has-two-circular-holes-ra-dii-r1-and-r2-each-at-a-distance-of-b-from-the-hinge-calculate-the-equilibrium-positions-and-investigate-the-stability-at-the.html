Guest Question.

A disc (radius a) is hinged in its center and has two circular holes (ra-dii \(r_{1}\) and \(r_{2}\)) each at a distance of b from the hinge. Calculate the equilibrium positions and investigate the stability at these equilibri-um positions for \(r_{1}=\sqrt{2} r_{2}.\)

Admin
Solution

Since the disc without the holes is in equilibrium in any posi-tion, only the influence of the two holes needs to be taken into account. Therefore, the holes are considered as “negative” weights which must be “added” to the disc. Specifying the hinge as zero level, the potential energy of the system can be formulated as

\(V=W_{1} b \sin \alpha +W_{2} \sin (180^{\omicron }-120^{\omicron }-\alpha )=V(\alpha ).\)

The states of equilibrium result from

\(V^{\prime }=W_{1}b\cos \alpha -W_{2}b \cos (60^{\omicron -}\alpha )=0.\)

With the weights

\(W_{1}=\pi r^{2}_{1}\rho g=2\pi r^{2}_{2}\rho g, W_{2}=\pi r^{2}_{2}\rho g\)

one obtains

\(V^{\prime }=\pi r^{2}_{2}\rho g\left[2 \cos \alpha -\cos (60^{\omicron }-\alpha )\right]=0\)

and thus

\(2\cos \alpha -\frac{1}{2}\cos \alpha -\frac{\sqrt{3} }{2}\sin \alpha =0 \rightsquigarrow \tan \alpha =\sqrt{3}\)

 

\(\rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{\alpha _{1}=60^{\omicron }} }, \underline{\underline{\alpha _{2}=240^{\omicron }} }.\)

The second derivative

\(V^{\prime \prime }=-W_{1}b\sin \alpha -W_{2}b\sin (60^{\omicron }-\alpha )\)

specifies the stability at the equilibrium position yielding

\(\underline{\underline{V^{\prime \prime }(\alpha _{1})} }=-W_{1}b\frac{\sqrt{3} }{2}\lt 0 \rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{unstable} },\)

 

\(\underline{\underline{V^{\prime \prime }(\alpha _{2})} }=+W_{1}b\frac{\sqrt{3} }{2}\lt 0 \rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{stable} }.\)

Note: Since the center of gravity of the perforated disc is located above the support for \(α_{1}\) and below the support for \(α_{2},\) the statement about the stability is well illustrated.
