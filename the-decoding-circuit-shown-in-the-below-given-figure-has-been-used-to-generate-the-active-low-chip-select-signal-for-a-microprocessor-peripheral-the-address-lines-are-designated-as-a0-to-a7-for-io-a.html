Guest Question.

The decoding circuit shown in the below-given figure has been used to generate the active low chip select signal for a microprocessor peripheral. (The address lines are designated as A0 to A7 for IO addresses.) The peripheral will correspond to IO addresses in the range:

(a) 60 H to 63H (b) A4H to A7H

(c) 50 H to AFH (d) 70H to 73H

Admin
Solution

For active low chip signal, NAND gate output should be ‘0’.
NAND gate output will be ‘0’, when all the inputs are ‘1’. So, possible combinations given in options are

\(\begin{array}{cccccccc|c}A_{7} & A_{6} & A_{5} & A_{4} & A_{3} & A_{2} & A_{1} & A_{0} & \\\hline 0 & 1 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 60 H \text { to } \\0 & 1 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 & 63 H \\\hline 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 & A 4 H \text { to } \\1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 & 1 & A 7 H \\\hline 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 50 H \text { to } \\1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 & A F \\\hline 0 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 70 H \text { to } \\0 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 & 73 H\end{array}\)

 

\(A_{2} \text { and } A_{3}\)should be the same. So, only options (a), (c) and (d) satisfy this.
Option (c) cannot be correct, because all chip select signals are not the same.
Option (d) cannot be correct because it gives logic ‘1’ output. So, the correct option is (a).
Hence, the correct option is (a).
