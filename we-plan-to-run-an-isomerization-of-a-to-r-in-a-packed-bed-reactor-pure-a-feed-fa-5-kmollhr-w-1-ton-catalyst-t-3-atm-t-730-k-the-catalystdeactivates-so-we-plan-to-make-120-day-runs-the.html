Guest Question.

We plan to run an isomerization of \(A\) to \(R\) in a packed bed reactor (pure \(A\) feed, \(F_{\mathrm{A} 0}=5 \mathrm{kmol} / \mathrm{hr}, W=1\) -ton catalyst, \(\left.\pi=3 \mathrm{~atm}, T=730 \mathrm{~K}\right) .\) The catalyst

deactivates so we plan to make 120 day runs, then regenerate the catalyst.

(a) Plot conversion and activity versus time for the run

(b) find \(\bar{X}_{\mathrm{A}}\) for the 120 day run

The rate of reaction with \(C_{\mathrm{A}}\) in \(\mathrm{mol} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\) is described by

\(-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime}=0.2 C_{\mathrm{A}}^{2} \mathbf{a} \frac{\mathrm{mol} \mathrm{A}}{\mathrm{kg} \mathrm{cat} \cdot \mathrm{hr}}\)

and the rate of deactivation is given by

\(\text { (a) .... }-\frac{d \mathbf{a}}{d t}=8.3125 \times 10^{-3}, \quad \text { day }^{-1}\)

This expression represents poisoning by a feed impurity.

\(\text { (b).... }-\frac{d \mathbf{a}}{d t}=10^{-3}\left(C_{\mathrm{A}}+C_{\mathrm{R}}\right) \mathbf{a}, \quad \text { day }^{-1}\)

This represents poisoning by both reactant and by product, thus pore diffusion resistance does not influence the deactivation rate.

\(\text { (c) ... }-\frac{d \mathbf{a}}{d t}=3.325 \mathbf{a}^{2}, \quad \text { day }^{-1}\)

This represents fairly strong pore diffusion resistance.

\(\text { (d) .... }-\frac{d \mathbf{a}}{d t}=666.5 \mathbf{a}^{3}, \quad \text { day }^{-1}\)

This represents very strong pore diffusion resistance.

Admin
Solution

In general for parts (a), (b), (c), and (d) we can write

\(\begin{aligned}-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime} &=0.2 C_{\mathrm{A}}^{2} \mathbf{a} \frac{\mathrm{mol} \mathrm{A}}{\mathrm{kg} \mathrm{cat} \cdot \mathrm{hr}} \\C_{\mathrm{A} 0} &=\frac{p_{\mathrm{A} 0}}{\mathbf{R} T}=\frac{3 \mid \mathrm{atm}}{\left(82.06 \times 10^{-6} \frac{\mathrm{m}^{3} \cdot \mathrm{atm}}{\mathrm{mol} \cdot \mathrm{K}}\right)(730 \mathrm{~K})}=50 \frac{\mathrm{mol}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}} \\\tau^{\prime} &=\frac{W C_{\mathrm{A} 0}}{F_{\mathrm{A} 0}}=\frac{(1000 \mathrm{~kg})\left(50 \frac{\mathrm{mol}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}}\right)}{\left(5000 \frac{\mathrm{mol}}{\mathrm{hr}}\right)}=10 \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{hr}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}} \\&=\int_{C_{\mathrm{A} 0}}^{C_{\mathrm{A}}} \frac{d C_{\mathrm{A}}}{0.2 C_{\mathrm{A}}^{2} \mathbf{a}}=\frac{1}{0.2 \mathrm{a}}\left(\frac{1}{C_{\mathrm{A}}}-\frac{1}{C_{\mathrm{A} 0}}\right)\end{aligned}\)

or on rearranging

\(X_{\mathrm{A}}=1-\frac{C_{\mathrm{A}}}{C_{\mathrm{A} 0}}=\frac{100 \mathrm{a}}{1+100 \mathrm{a}} (i)\)

Let us now replace the activity term in Eq. (i).

Part \((a)\)

or

\(\begin{array}{c}-\int_{1}^{\mathrm{a}} d \mathbf{a}=8.3125 \times 10^{-3} \int_{0}^{t} d t \\\mathbf{a}=1-8.3125 \times 10^{-3} t\end{array} (ii)\)

Replacing Eq. (ii) in Eq. (i) and evaluating \(X_{\mathrm{A}}\) at various values of \(t\) gives the topmost curve shown in Fig. E21.2.

Part (b) Separating and integrating \(-\frac{d \mathbf{a}}{d t}=10^{-3}\left(C_{\mathrm{A}}+C_{\mathrm{R}}\right)\) a gives

or

\(\begin{array}{c}\int_{\mathrm{a}}^{1} \frac{d \mathbf{a}}{\mathbf{a}}=0.05 \int_{0}^{t} d t \\\mathbf{a}=e^{-0.05 \mathrm{t}}\end{array} (iii)\)

Replacing Eq. (iii) in Eq. (i) and evaluating \(X_{\mathrm{A}}\) at various \(t\) values gives the corresponding curve in Fig. E21.2.

Part (c) Separating and integrating \(-\frac{d \mathbf{a}}{d t}=3.325 \mathbf{a}^{2}\) gives

or

\(\begin{array}{c}\int_{1}^{a} \frac{d \mathbf{a}}{\mathbf{a}^{2}}=3.325 \int_{0}^{t} d t \\\mathbf{a}=\frac{1}{1+3.325 t}\end{array} (iv)\)

Again, replacing Eq. (iv) in (i) gives the corresponding curve in Fig. E21.2.

Part

(d) Separating and integrating \(-\frac{d \mathbf{a}}{d t}=666.5 \mathbf{a}^{3}\) gives

\(\mathbf{a}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+1333 t}} (v)\)

Combining Eq. (v) in (i) gives the lowest curve shown in Fig. E21.2.

From Fig. E21.2 we find by graphical integration that for the 120 -day period, \(X_{\mathrm{A}, \text { start }}=0.99, X_{\mathrm{A}, \text { end }}=0.20\) for

\(d=0\) the mean conversion & \(\underline{\bar{X}_{\mathrm{A}}=0.96}\)

\(d=1\) the mean conversion & \(\underline{\bar{X}_{\mathrm{A}}=0.73}\)

\(d=2\) the mean conversion & \(\underline{\bar{X}_{\mathrm{A}}=0.40}\)

\(d=3\) the mean conversion & \(\underline{\bar{X}_{\mathrm{A}}=0.30}\)

It also clearly shows how different is the progress of the reaction with the different orders of deactivation.
