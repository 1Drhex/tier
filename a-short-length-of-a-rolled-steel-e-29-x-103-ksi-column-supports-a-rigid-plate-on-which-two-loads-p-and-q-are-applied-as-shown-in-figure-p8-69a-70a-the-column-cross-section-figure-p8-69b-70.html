Guest Question.

A short length of a rolled-steel [E = 29 × \(10^{3}\) ksi] column supports a rigid plate on which two loads P and Q are applied as shown in Figure P8.69a/70a. The column cross section (Figure P8.69b/70b) has a depth of d = 8.0 in., an area of A = 5.40 \(\text { in. }^{2}\) , and a moment of inertia of \(I_{z}\) = 57.5 \(\text { in. }^{4}\) . Normal strains are measured with strain gages H and K, which are attached on the centerline of the outer faces of the flanges. The strains measured in the two gages are \(\varepsilon_{H}\) = –530 × \(10^{-6}\) in./in. and \(\varepsilon_{K}\) = –310 × \(10^{-6}\)in./in. Using a = 6.0 in., determine the magnitudes of loads P and Q.

Admin
Solution

The axial normal stress caused at H by both P and Q will be compression. By inspection, the bending stress at H caused by load Q will be compression, and the bending stress at H caused by load P will be tension. The normal stress at gage H can be expressed by:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{H} &=-\frac{P}{5.40 in. ^{2}}-\frac{Q}{5.40 in. ^{2}}+\frac{P(6 in. )(8 in . / 2)}{57.5 in .{ }^{4}}-\frac{Q(6 in. )(8 in . / 2)}{57.5 in .{ }^{4}} \\&=-\frac{P}{5.40 in .^{2}}+\frac{P\left(24 in. ^{2}\right)}{57.5 in .{ }^{4}}-\frac{Q}{5.40 in .{ }^{2}}-\frac{Q\left(24 in. ^{2}\right)}{57.5 in .{ }^{4}} \\&=\left(0.2322061 in. ^{-2}\right) P-\left(0.6025765 in. ^{-2}\right) Q (a)\end{aligned}\)

 

The axial normal stress caused at K by both P and Q will be compression. By inspection, the bending stress at K caused by load Q will be tension, and the bending stress at K caused by load P will be compression.

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{K} &=-\frac{Q}{5.40 in .^{2}}-\frac{P}{5.40 in .^{2}}+\frac{Q(6 in. )(8 in. / 2)}{57.5 in .{ }^{4}}-\frac{P(6 in. )(8 in. / 2)}{57.5 in .{ }^{4}} \\&=-\frac{Q}{5.40 in .{ }^{2}}+\frac{Q\left(24 in .{ }^{2}\right)}{57.5 in .{ }^{4}}-\frac{P}{5.40 in. ^{2}}-\frac{P\left(24 in. ^{2}\right)}{57.5 in .{ }^{4}} \\&=\left(-0.6025765 in .^{-2}\right) P+\left(0.2322061 in. ^{-2}\right) Q (b)\end{aligned}\)

 

Normal stresses from measured strains
The normal strains at H and K were measured as \(\varepsilon_{H}=-530 \times 10^{-6} \text { in./in. and } \varepsilon_{K}=-310 \times 10^{-6} \text { in./in. }\)

From these values, the stresses at H and K can be calculated from Hooke’s Law:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{H} &=E \varepsilon_{H}=(29,000 ksi )\left(-530 \times 10^{-6} \text { in./in. }\right)=-15.3700 ksi \\\sigma_{K} &=E \varepsilon_{K}=(29,000 ksi )\left(-310 \times 10^{-6} \text { in./in. }\right)=-8.9900 ksi\end{aligned}\)

Substitute these values in Eqs. (a) and (b), respectively, to obtain the following equations.

\(\begin{aligned}&-15.3700 ksi =\left(0.2322061 in. ^{-2}\right) P-\left(0.6025765 in. ^{-2}\right) Q (c)\\&-8.9900 ksi =\left(-0.6025765 in. ^{-2}\right) P+\left(0.2322061 in. ^{-2}\right) Q (d)\end{aligned}\)

Solve Eqs. (c) and (d) simultaneously to obtain the values for P and Q:

Load P

\(P=\frac{\left|\begin{array}{cc}-15.3700 & -0.6025765 \\-8.9900 & 0.2322061\end{array}\right|}{\left|\begin{array}{cc}0.2322061 & -0.6025765 \\-0.6025765 & 0.2322061\end{array}\right|}=\frac{(-15.3700)(0.2322061)-(-8.9900)(-0.60257565)}{(0.2322061)^{2}-(-0.6025765)^{2}}\)

 

\(=\frac{-8.9861629}{-0.3091788}=29.0645 kips =29.1 kips\)

 

Load Q

\(Q=\frac{\left|\begin{array}{cc}0.2322061 & -15.3700 \\-0.6025765 & -8.9900\end{array}\right|}{\left|\begin{array}{cc}0.2322061 & -0.6025765 \\-0.6025765 & 0.2322061\end{array}\right|}\frac{(0.2322061)(-8.9900)-(-0.60257565)(-15.3700)}{(0.2322061)^{2}-(-0.6025765)^{2}}\)

 

\(=\frac{-11.3491206}{-0.3091788}=36.7074 kips =36.7 kips\)
