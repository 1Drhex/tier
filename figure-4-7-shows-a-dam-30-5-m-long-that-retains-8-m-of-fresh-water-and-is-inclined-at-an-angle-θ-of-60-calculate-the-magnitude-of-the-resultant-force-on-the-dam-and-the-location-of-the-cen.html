Guest Question.

figure 4.7 shows a dam 30.5 m long that retains 8 m of fresh water and is inclined at an angle θ of 60°. Calculate the magnitude of the resultant force on the dam and the location of the center of pressure.

Admin
Solution

Step 1.

\(F_R = \gamma \left({h}/{2}\right) A\)

To calculate the area of the dam we need the length of its face, called ∠ in Fig. 4.7:

\(sin\Theta ={h}/{L}\)

\(L= {h}/{sin\Theta }=8{m}/{sin 60°}=9.24m\)

Then, the area of the dam is

\(A=\left(9.24m\right) \left(30.5m\right) =281.8m^2\)

Now we can calculate the resultant force:

\(F_R = \gamma \left({h}/{2}\right)A=\frac{9.81kN}{m^3} \times \frac{8m}{2} \times 281.8m^2 \)

\( = 11060kN =11.06MN\)

 

Step 2. The center of pressure is at a vertical distance of

\({h}/{3}=8{m}/{3}=2.67m\)

from the bottom of the dam, or measured from the bottom of the dam along the face of the dam, the center of pressure is at

\({L}/{3} = 9.24 {m}/{3}=3.08m\)

Measured along the face of the dam we define

\(L_p =\) Distance from the free surface of the fluid to the center of pressure

\(L_p = L-{L}/{3}\)

\(L_p = 9.24m-3.08m=6.16m\)

We show \( F_R\) acting at the center of pressure perpendicular to the wall.
