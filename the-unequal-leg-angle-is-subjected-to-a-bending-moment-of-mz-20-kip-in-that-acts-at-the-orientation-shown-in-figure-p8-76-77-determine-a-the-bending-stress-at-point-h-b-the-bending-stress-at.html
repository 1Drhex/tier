Guest Question.

The unequal-leg angle is subjected to a bending moment of \(M_{z}\) = 20 kip-in. that acts at the orientation shown in Figure P8.76/77. Determine:
(a) the bending stress at point H.
(b) the bending stress at point K.
(c) the maximum tension and the maximum compression bending stresses in the cross section.
(d) the orientation of the neutral axis relative to the +z axis. Show its location on a sketch of the cross section.

Admin
Solution

Section properties
Centroid location in y direction:

\(\begin{array}{lccccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}\text { Width } b \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Height } h \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\text { (in. } \left.^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} \text { (from bottom) } \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} A_{i} \\\left(\operatorname{in} .^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { upright leg } & 0.375 & 4.000 & 1.5000 & 2.00 & 3.00 \\\text { bottom leg } & 2.625 & 0.375 & 0.9844 & 0.1875 & 0.18457 \\\hline & & & 2.4844 & & 3.18457\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{3.18457 in. ^{3}}{2.4844 in. ^{2}} &=1.2818 in . \text{(from bottom of shape to centroid)} \\&=2.7182 in . \text{(from top of shape to centroid) } \end{aligned}\)

 

Centroid location in z direction:

\(\begin{array}{cccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left(\text { in. }^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}z_{i} \text { (from right edge) } \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}z_{i} A_{i} \\\left(\text { in. }{ }^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { upright leg } & 1.5000 & 0.1875 & 0.2813 \\\text { bottom leg } & 0.9844 & 1.6875 & 1.6612 \\\hline & 2.4844 & & 1.94243\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{z}=\frac{\Sigma z_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{1.94243 in .{ }^{3}}{2.4844 in .^{2}} &=0.7818 in . \text{(from right edge of shape to centroid) }\\&=2.2182 in . \text{(from left edge of shape to centroid) }\end{aligned}\)

 

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

\(\begin{array}{lcccc}\text { Shape } & I_{C} & d=y_{i}-\bar{y} & d^{2} A & I_{C}+d^{2} A \\& \left( in .{ }^{4}\right) & (\text { in. }) & \left( in .{ }^{2}\right) & \left( in .{ }^{4}\right) \\\hline \text { upright leg } & 2.000 & 0.7182 & 0.77372 & 2.7737 \\\text { bottom leg } & 0.011536 & -1.0943 & 1.17881 & 1.1903 \\\hline {\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)=} &&&& 3.9640\end{array}\)

 

Moment of inertia about the y axis:

\(\begin{array}{lcccc}\text { Shape } & I_{C} & d=z_{i}-\bar{z} & d^{2} A & I_{C}+d^{2} A \\& \left( in. ^{4}\right) & (\text { in. }) & \left( in. ^{4}\right) & \left( in. ^{4}\right) \\\hline \text { upright leg } & 0.017578 & -0.5943 & 0.52979 & 0.5474 \\\text { bottom leg } & 0.565247 & 0.9057 & 0.80750 & 1.3727 \\\hline {\text { Moment of inertia about the } y \text { axis }\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)=} &&&& 1.9201\end{array}\)

 

Product of inertia about the centroidal axes:

\(\begin{array}{ccccccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}I_{y^{\prime} z^{\prime}} \\\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{c} \\( in .)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}z_{c} \\( in .)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left( in .{ }^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{c} z_{c} A_{i} \\\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{y z} \\\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { upright leg } & 0 & 0.7182 & -0.5943 & 1.5000 & -0.6402 & -0.6402 \\\text { bottom leg } & 0 & -1.0943 & 0.9057 & 0.9844 & -0.9757 & -0.9757 \\\hline {\text { Product of inertia }\left( in .{}^{4}\right)=}&&&&& & -1.6159\end{array}\)

 

(a) Bending stress at H
Since the angle shape has no axis of symmetry, Eq. (8.21) or Eq. (8.22) must be used to determine the bending stresses. Equation (8.22) will be used here. Note that the bending moment component about the y axis is zero (i.e., \(M_{y}\) = 0); therefore, the first term in Eq. (8.22) is eliminated. To compute the normal stress at H, use (y, z) coordinates of y = 2.7182 in. and z = −0.4068 in.:

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{x}=\left[\frac{-I_{y} y+I_{y z} z}{I_{y} I_{z}-I_{y z}^{2}}\right] M_{z}\\&=\left[\frac{-\left(1.9201 in .{ }^{4}\right)(2.7182 in .)+\left(-1.6159 in .{ }^{4}\right)(-0.4068 in .)}{\left(1.9201 in. ^{4}\right)\left(3.9640 in. ^{4}\right)-\left(-1.6159 in .{ }^{4}\right)^{2}}\right](20 kip – in .)\\&=\left[\frac{-4.5619 in. ^{5}}{5.0001 in. ^{8}}\right](20 kip – in. )\\&=-18.2469 ksi =18.25 ksi ( C )\end{aligned}\)

(b) Bending stress at K
To compute the normal stress at K, use (y, z) coordinates of y = −0.9068 in. and z = 2.2182 in.:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\left[\frac{-I_{y} y+I_{y z} z}{I_{y} I_{z}-I_{y z}^{2}}\right] M_{z} \\&=\left[\frac{-\left(1.9201 in. ^{4}\right)(-0.9068 in .)+\left(-1.6159 in .^{4}\right)(2.2182 in .)}{\left(1.9201 in. ^{4}\right)\left(3.9640 in .^{4}\right)-\left(-1.6159 in .{ }^{4}\right)^{2}}\right](20 kip – in .) \\&=\left[\frac{-1.8432 in. ^{5}}{5.0001 in. ^{8}}\right](20 kip – in. ) \\&=-7.3728 ksi =7.37 ksi ( C )\end{aligned}\)

(d) Orientation of neutral axis
Since the angle shape has no axis of symmetry, Eq. (8.23) must be used to determine the orientation of the neutral axis:

\(\begin{aligned}\tan \beta &=\frac{M_{y} I_{z}+M_{z} I_{y z}}{M_{z} I_{y}+M_{y} I_{y z}}=\frac{(20 kip – in .)\left(-1.6159 in.^{4}\right)}{(20 kip – in .)\left(1.9201 in .^{4}\right)}=-0.8416 \\& \therefore \beta=-40.08^{\circ} &\text { (i.e., } \left.40.08^{\circ} CCW \text { from }+z \text { axis }\right)\end{aligned}\)

(c) Maximum bending stresses
Sketch the orientation of the neutral axis. By inspection, the points on the angle cross section that are farthest from the neutral axis are point H and the corner of the angle. The bending stress at H has already been computed. To compute the normal stress at the corner of the angle, use (y, z) coordinates of y = −1.2818 in. and z = −0.7818 in.

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\left[\frac{-I_{y} y+I_{y z} z}{I_{y} I_{z}-I_{y z}^{2}}\right] M_{z} \\&=\left[\frac{-\left(1.9201 in .^{4}\right)(-1.2818 in .)+\left(-1.6159 in .{ }^{4}\right)(-0.7818 \text { in. })}{\left(1.9201 in. ^{4}\right)\left(3.9640 in. ^{4}\right)-\left(-1.6159 in .{ }^{4}\right)^{2}}\right](20 kip \text {-in. }) \\&=\left[\frac{3.7245 in. ^{5}}{5.0001 in. ^{8}}\right](20 kip – in .) \\&=14.8977 ksi =14.90 ksi ( T )\end{aligned}\)

Therefore, the maximum compression bending stress is:

\(\sigma_{x}=18.25 ksi ( C )\)

and the maximum tension bending stress is:

\(\sigma_{x}=14.90 ksi ( T )\)
