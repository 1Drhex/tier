Guest Question.

A 2 cm diameter aluminum rod with k = 205 W/m·K, 8 cm in length, protrudes from a 150◦C wall. Air at 26◦C flows by it, and \(\bar{h}\) = 120 W/m²K. Determine whether or not tip conduction is important in this problem. To do this, make the very crude assumption that \(\bar{h}\simeq \bar{h} _{L}\) . Then compare the tip temperatures as calculated with and without considering heat transfer from the tip.

Admin
Solution
\(mL=\sqrt{\frac{\bar{h}PL^2 }{KA} }=\sqrt{\frac{120(0.08)^2}{205(0.01/2)} }=0,8656\)

 

\(Bi_{ax}=\frac{\bar{h}L }{K}=\frac{120(0.08)}{205}=0.0468\)

Therefore, eqn. becomes

\(\Theta =\frac{\cos hmL(1-\xi )+(Bi_{ax}/mL )\sin hmL(1-\xi )}{\cos hmL+(Bi_{ax}/mL )\sin hmL}\)

becomes

\(\Theta (\xi =1)=\Theta _{tip} =\frac{\cos h0+(0.0468/0.8656)\sin h0}{\cos h(0.8656)+(0.0468/0.8656)\sin h(0.8656)}=\frac{1}{1.3986+0.0529}=0.6886\)

so the exact tip temperature is

\(T_{tip}=T_{\infty }+0.6886(T_{0}-T_{\infty } ) =26+0.6886(150-26)=111.43^\circ C\)

Equation \(\Theta =\frac{\cos hmL(1-\xi )}{\cos hmL}\)

or Fig , on the other hand, gives

\(Θ_{tip} =\frac{1}{1.3986} =0.7150\)

so the approximate tip temperature is

\(T_{tip}=26+0.715(150-26)=114.66^\circ C\)

Thus the insulated-tip approximation is adequate for the computation in this case.
