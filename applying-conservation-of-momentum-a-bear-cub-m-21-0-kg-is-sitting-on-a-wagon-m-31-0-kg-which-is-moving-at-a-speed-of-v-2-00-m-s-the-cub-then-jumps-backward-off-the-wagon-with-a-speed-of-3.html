Applying Conservation of Momentum

A bear cub (m = 21.0 kg) is sitting on a wagon (M = 31.0 kg), which is moving at a speed of v = 2.00 m/s. The cub then jumps backward off the wagon with a speed of 3.00 m/s with respect to the wagon. Ignoring the effects of friction, find the speed of the cub and the wagon with respect to the ground after the cub jumps.

Admin
Solution

We will consider the system consisting of the bear cub and the wagon, as shown in Figure 7-17.

When the bear cub jumps off the wagon, the force that the cub exerts on the wagon and the force that the wagon exerts on the cub are internal to the cub–wagon system. Since we are ignoring the effects of friction and there are no other net external forces acting on the system at this time, the linear momentum of the cub–wagon system is conserved as the bear cub jumps off the wagon:

 

\(\vec{p}_{ i }=\vec{p}_{ f }\) (1)

 

We substitute (3) into (2) and solve for \(\vec{v}_{w}\) :

 

\((m+M) \vec{v}=m \vec{v}_{ b }+M \vec{v}_{ w }\)

 

\(=m\left(\overrightarrow{ v }_{ w }-3 m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{ i }\right)+M \overrightarrow{ v }_{ w }\)

 

\(=m \overrightarrow{ v }_{ w }-m \cdot 3 m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{ i }+M \overrightarrow{ v }_{ w }\)

 

\(\overrightarrow{ v }_{ w }=\frac{(m+M) \overrightarrow{ v }+m \cdot 3 m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{ i }}{m+M}\)

 

\(=3.21 m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{ i }\)

 

Here we have used \(\overrightarrow{ v }=2 m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{ i }\) . We can substitute our result for the velocity of the wagon into either (2) or (3) to find the final velocity of the bear cub. We will use (3):

 

\(\overrightarrow{ v }_{ b }=\overrightarrow{ v }_{ w }-3 m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{ i }\)

 

\(=3.21 m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{ i }-3 m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{ i }\)

 

\(=0.21 m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{ i }\)

 

Making sense of the result

 

We can check that momentum is conserved. The momentum before the jump is \((m+M) \overrightarrow{ v }=(21 kg +31 kg )\left(2 m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{ i }\right)=\) \(104 kg \cdot m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{ i }\) . The momentum after the jump is \(m \overrightarrow{ v }_{ b }+M \overrightarrow{ v }_{ w }=(21 kg )\left(0.21 m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{ i }\right)+(31 kg )\left(3.21 m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{ i }\right)=\) \(104 kg \cdot m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{i}\) , where we have rounded appropriately. Thus, momentum is conserved.
