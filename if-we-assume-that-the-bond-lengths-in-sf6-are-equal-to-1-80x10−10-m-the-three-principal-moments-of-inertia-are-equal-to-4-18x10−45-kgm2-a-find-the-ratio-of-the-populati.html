Guest Question.

If we assume that the bond lengths in \( SF_{6}\) are equal to \( 1.80×10^{−10} m\), the three principal moments of inertia are equal to \( 4.18×10^{−45} kgm^{2}. \)

a. Find the ratio of the population of one of the state of the J=2 level to the population of the J=0 level at 298 K.

b. Find the ratio of the population of the J=2 level to the population of the J=0 level at 298 K.

Admin
Solution

a. Ratio= \( \frac{e^{−E_{2}/k_{B}T}}{e^{−E_{0}/k_{B}T}}=e^{−(E_{2}−E_{0})/k_{B}T}\)

 

\( E_{2} – E_{0}=\frac{\hbar ^{2}}{2I_{A}}2(2+1) =\frac{3\hbar ^{2}}{I_{A}}=\frac{3h^{2}}{4π^{2}I_{A}}=\frac{3\bigl(6.6261 × 10^{−34} J s \bigr) ^{2}}{4π^{2}\bigl(4.18 × 10^{−45} kgm^{2}\bigr)}\)

 

\(=7.98 × 10^{−24} J \)

 

Ratio= \(\exp \Bigl(\frac{ −7.98×10^{−24} J}{ \bigl(1.3807×10^{−23} JK^{−1}\bigr)(298K)} \Bigr)=e^{−0.00194}= 0.9981\)

 

b. Ratio= \( \frac{ g_{2}e^{−E_{2}/k_{B}T}}{ g_{0}e^{−E_{0}/k_{B}T}}=(25)e^{−0.00194}=24.95\)
