Guest Question.

Derive the DFT of a 4-point sequence using the radix-2 FFT algorithm when:

a. x[n] = {x[0], x[1], x[2], x[3]} and

b. x[n] = {1, ,1 1, 1}.

Admin
Solution

a. \(N = 4 = 2^{2}\) ⇒ v = 2. Thus, there are two segmentation stages. The DFT is:

\(X[k]=DFT[x[n]]=\sum\limits_{n=0}^{3}{x[n]W_{4}^{kn}} , k=0,1,2,3\Rightarrow \)
\(X[k]=x[0]W_{4}^{0}+x[1]W_{4}^{k}+x[2]W_{4}^{2k}+x[3]W_{4}^{3k} ,k-0,1,2,3\) (1)

Then, from Eq. (1) we can conclude that in order to calculate X[k], \(4^{2}=16\) complex multiplications are needed.

By partition of x[n] we get: \(\begin{matrix} x_{e}[n]=\left\{x[0], x[2]\right\} \\ x_{0}[n]=\left\{x[1], x[3]\right\}\end{matrix}\) (2)

The DFT of \(x_{e}[n]\) and \(x_{o}[n]\) is:

\(X_{e}[k]=DFT[x_{e}[n]]=\sum\limits_{n=0}^{1}{x_{e}[n]W_{2}^{kn}}=x_{e}[0]W_{2}^{0}+x_{e}[1]W_{2}^{k}\Rightarrow \)
\(X_{e}[k]=x[0]W_{2}^{0}+x[1]W_{2}^{k} ,k=0,1\) (3)
Likewise, \(X_{o}[k]=DFT[x_{0}[n]]=x[1]W_{2}^{0}+x[3]W_{2}^{k}, k=0,1\) (4)

By writing equations (3) and (4) in a general form, we obtain:

\(X_{e}[k]=x[0]W_{\frac{N}{2} }^{0}+x[2]W_{\frac{N}{2}}^{k}=x[0]W_{N}^{0}+x[2]W_{N}^{2k} ,k=0,1,2,…,\frac{N}{2}-1\) (5)
and \(X_{o}[k]=x[1]W_{\frac{N}{2} }^{0}+x[3]W_{\frac{N}{2}}^{k}=x[1]W_{N}^{0}+x[3]W_{N}^{2k} ,k=0,1,2,…,\frac{N}{2}-1\) (6)

From (1), obviously it is:

\(X[k]=X_{e}[k]+W_{N}^{k}X_{o}[k], k=0,1,2,…,N-1\) (7)

And for the remaining part of the DFT, it is:

\(X[k+\frac{N}{2}]=X[k+2]=X_{e}[k]+W_{N}^{k}X_{o}[k], k=0,1\) (8)

where

\(X_{e}[0]=x[0]+x[2] \) (9)
\(X_{e}[1]=x[0]+x[2]W_{2}=x[0]+x[2]e^{-j\frac{2\pi }{2} }\Rightarrow \) (10)
\(X_{e}[1]=x[0]+x[2]e^{-j\pi }=x[0]-x[2]\)
\(X_{o}[0]=x[1]+x[3] \) (11)
\(X_{o}[0]=x[1]-x[3]\) (12)

b. x[n] = {1, 1, 1, 1}, N = 4
In this case, it is: x[0] = x[1] = x[2] = x[3] = 1

\(\begin{matrix} (9)\Rightarrow X_{e}[0]=2 \\ (10)\Rightarrow X_{e}[1]=0 \\ (11)\Rightarrow X_{o}[0]=2 \\ (12)\Rightarrow X_{o}[1]=0 \end{matrix} (13) \)
\((7)\overset{k=0}{\Rightarrow }X[0]=X_{e}[0]+X_{o}[0]=4 \)
\((7)\overset{k=1}{\Rightarrow }X[1]=X_{e}[1]+W_{4}^{1}X_{o}[0]\Rightarrow \)
\(X[1]=X_{e}[1]+e^{-j\frac{2\pi }{4} }X_{o}[1]=X_{e}[1]+e^{-j\frac{\pi }{2} }X_{o}[1]\Rightarrow \)
\(X[1]=X_{e}[1]+jX_{o}[1]=0\)
\((8)\overset{k=0}{\Rightarrow }X[2]=X_{e}[0]+W_{4}^{0}X_{o}[0]=X_{e}[0]-X_{o}[0]=0\)
\((8)\overset{k=1}{\Rightarrow }X[3]=X_{e}[1]+W_{4}^{1}X_{o}[1]=X_{e}[1]-jX_{o}[1]=0\)

Hence, the DFT of x[n], using radix-2 FFT, is X{k} = {4, 0, 0, 0}
