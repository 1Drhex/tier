Guest Question.

A beam having a T section with the dimensions shown in Fig. 3–15 is subjected to a bending moment of 1600 N · m, about the negative z axis, that causes tension at the top surface.
Locate the neutral axis and find the maximum tensile and compressive bending stresses

Admin
Solution

Dividing the T section into two rectangles, numbered 1 and 2, the total area is
\( A = 12\left(75\right) + 12\left(88\right) = 1956 \ mm^2 \).

Summing the area moments of these rectangles about the top edge, where the moment arms of areas 1 and 2 are 6 mm and\( \left(12+{88}/{2}\right) =56 \ mm \) respectively, we have
\( 1956c_1= 12\left(75\right) \left(6\right) + 12\left(88\right) \left(56\right) \)
and hence \( c_1= 32.99 \ mm\). Therefore \( c_2= 100 − 32.99 = 67.01 mm \).
Next we calculate the second moment of area of each rectangle about its own cen-troidal axis. Using Table A–18

, we find for the top rectangle
\(I_1=\frac{1}{12}bh^3=\frac{1}{12} \left(75\right) 12^3=1.080\times 10^4 \ mm^4\).

For the bottom rectangle, we have
\(I_2=\frac{1}{12} \left(12\right) 88^3=6.815\times 10^5 \ mm^4\).

We now employ the parallel-axis theorem to obtain the second moment of area of the composite figure about its own centroidal axis.

This theorem states

\(I_z=I_{ca}+Ad^2\)

where \(I_{ca}\) is the second moment of area about its own centroidal axis and \(I_{z}\) is the sec-
ond moment of area about any parallel axis a distance d removed. For the top rectan-gle, the distance is
\( d_1= 32.99 − 6 = 26.99 \ mm\)
and for the bottom rectangle,
\(d_2= 67.01− \frac{88}{2} = 23.01 \ mm \)
Using the parallel-axis theorem for both rectangles, we now find that
\(I=\left[1.080\times 10^4+12\left(75\right)20.99^2 \right]+\left[6.815\times 10^5+12\left(88\right)23.01^2 \right]\\ =1.907\times 10^6 \ mm^4\).

 

Finally, the maximum tensile stress, which occurs at the top surface, is found to be
\(\sigma =\frac{M_{C1}}{I} =\frac{1600\left(32.99\right)^{-3} }{1.907\left(10^{-6}\right) } =27.68(10^{6}) = 27.68 \ MPa\)

Similarly, the maximum compressive stress at the lower surface is found to be

\(\sigma =-\frac{M_{C2}}{I} =\frac{1600\left(67.01\right)^{-3} }{1.907\left(10^{-6}\right) }=-56.22\left(10^6\right)\ Pa=-56.22 \ MPa\).
