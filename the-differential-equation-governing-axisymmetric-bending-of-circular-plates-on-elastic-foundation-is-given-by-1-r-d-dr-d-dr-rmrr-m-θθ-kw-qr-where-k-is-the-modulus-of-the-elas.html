Guest Question.

The differential equation governing axisymmetric bending of circular plates on elastic foundation is given by
\(-\frac{1}{r}\frac{\mathrm{d} }{\mathrm{d} r}\left [ \frac{\mathrm{d} }{\mathrm{d} r}(rM_{rr})-M_{\theta \theta } \right ]+kw = q(r)\)
where k is the modulus of the elastic foundation, q is the transverse distributed load, and
\(M_{rr}=-D\left ( \frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} w}{\mathrm{d} r^{2}}+\nu \frac{1}{r}\frac{\mathrm{d} w}{\mathrm{d} r} \right )\) , \(M_{\theta \theta }=-D\left (\nu\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} w}{\mathrm{d} r^{2}}+\frac{1}{r}\frac{\mathrm{d} w}{\mathrm{d} r} \right )\)
Develop (a) the weak form and identify the primary and secondary variables, and (b) the finite element model. Note that the shear force is defined by
\(Q_{r}=\frac{1}{r}\left [ \frac{\mathrm{d} }{\mathrm{d} r}(rM_{rr})-M_{\theta \theta } \right ]\)

 

Admin
Solution

Since this is an axisymmetric problem associated with a circular plate, the elemental volume is \(dV=dr\cdot r d\theta\cdot dz \). The integration with respect to \(\theta\) and z yields (because all quantities are independent of \(\theta\) and z) a factor \(2\pi t\), where t is the thickness of the plate. Dividing out by this factor, we have

0 = \(\int_{r_{a}}^{r_{b}}v\left [ -\frac{1}{r}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} }{\mathrm{d} r^{2}}(rM_{rr})+\frac{1}{r}\frac{\mathrm{d} M_{\theta \theta }}{\mathrm{d} r}+kvw-q\right ]r dr\)

= \(\int_{r_{a}}^{r_{b}}\left [ -\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}}v }{\mathrm{d} r^{2}}M_{rr}-\frac{1}{r}\frac{\mathrm{d} v}{\mathrm{d} r}M_{\theta \theta }+kvw-vq \right ]r dr\)

\(-v(r_{a})Q_{1}-v(r_{b})Q_{3}-\left ( -\frac{\mathrm{d} v}{\mathrm{d} r} \right )_{a}Q_{2}-\left ( -\frac{\mathrm{d} v}{\mathrm{d} r} \right )_{b}Q_{4}\) (1)

where v is the weight function and

\(Q_{1}=-\left [ \frac{\mathrm{d} }{\mathrm{d} r}(rM_{rr})-M_{\theta \theta } \right ]_{r_{a}}\) , \(Q_{3}=-\left [ \frac{\mathrm{d} }{\mathrm{d} r}(rM_{rr})-M_{\theta \theta } \right ]_{r_{b}}\)

\(Q_{2}=-[rM_{rr}]_{r_{a}}\) , \(Q_{4}=-[rM_{rr}]_{r_{b}}\) (2)

To develop the finite element model, we assume finite element interpolation of w(r) (like in the beam bending)

\(w(r)\approx w_{h}^{e}(r)=\sum_{j=1}^{n=4}\Delta _{j}^{e}\phi _{j}^{e}(r)\) (3)

Substituting for v = \(\phi _{i}\) (to obtain the ith algebraic equation of the system) and w = \(w_{h}^{e}\) from Eq. (3), we arrive at the result

\([K^{e}]\left \{ \Delta ^{e} \right \}=\left \{ q^{e} \right \}+\left \{ Q^{e} \right \}\) (4)

where

\(K_{ij}^{e}=\int_{r_{a}}^{r_{b}}D\left [ \frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} \phi _{i}}{\mathrm{d} r^{2}}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} \phi _{j}}{\mathrm{d} r^{2}}+\frac{\nu }{r}\left ( \frac{\mathrm{d} \phi _{i}}{\mathrm{d} r}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} \phi _{j}}{\mathrm{d} r^{2}}+\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} \phi _{i}}{\mathrm{d} r}\frac{\mathrm{d} \phi _{j}}{\mathrm{d} r} \right )+\frac{1}{r^{2}}\frac{\mathrm{d} \phi _{i}}{\mathrm{d} r}\frac{\mathrm{d} \phi _{j}}{\mathrm{d} r} \right ]r dr\)

 

\(q_{i}^{e}=\int_{r_{a}}^{r_{b}}q\phi _{i}r dr\) (5)
