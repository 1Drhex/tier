CONSTANT-PROPERTY LINES ON A Ts DIAGRAM

For an ideal gas, find the equations for lines of (a) constant volume and (b) constant pressure in the \(Ts\) plane.

Admin
Solution

Find: Equations for lines of (a) constant volume and
(b) constant pressure in the Ts plane for an ideal gas.
Solution:
(a) We are interested in the relation between \(T\) and \(s\) with the volume \(v\) held constant. This suggests use of Eq. 12.11a (\(s_{2}-s_{1}=c_{v} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}+R \ln \frac{v_{2}}{v_{1}}\)),

\(s_{2}-s_{1}=c_{v} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}+R \ln \cancel{\frac{v_2}{v_{1}}}^{=0}\) (12.8)

 

We relabel this equation so that state 1 is now reference state 0 , and state 2 is an arbitrary state,

\(s-s_{0}=c_{v} \ln \frac{T}{T_{0}} \quad\quad \text { or } \quad T=T_{0} e^{\left(s-s_{0}\right) / c_{v}}\) (1)

 

Hence, we conclude that constant volume lines in the Ts plane are exponential.
(b) We are interested in the relation between \(T\) and \(s\) with the pressure \(p\) held constant. This suggests use of Eq. 12.11b (\(s_{2}-s_{1}=c_{p} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-R \ln \frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}\)), and using a similar approach to case (a), we find

 

\(T=T_{0} e^{\left(s-s_{0}\right) / c_{p}}\) (2)

 

Hence, we conclude that constant pressure lines in the Ts plane are also exponential.
What about the slope of these curves? Because \(c_p > c_v\) for all gases, we can see that the exponential, and therefore the slope, of the constant pressure curve, Eq. 2, is smaller than that for the constant volume curve, Eq. 1.
