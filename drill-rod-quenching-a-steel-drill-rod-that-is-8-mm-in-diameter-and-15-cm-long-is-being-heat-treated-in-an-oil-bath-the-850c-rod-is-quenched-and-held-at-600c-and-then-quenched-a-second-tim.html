Drill Rod Quenching

A steel drill rod that is 8 mm in diameter and 15 cm long is being heat treated in an oil bath. The 850°C rod is quenched and held at \( 600°C \)and then quenched a second time to \(20°C\). Calculate the quantities of heat that must be removed at the two quenching stages.

Approach

We will calculate the quantities of heat that fl ow from the rod during the two quenching stages by applying Equation (7.7).

\(Q = mc(T - T _{0})\) (7.7)

Solution

The volume V of the rod is calculated based on its length L and cross sectional area A

\(A=\pi \frac{(0.008m)^2}{4} \longleftarrow \left[A=\pi \frac{d^2}{4} \right] \)

\(= 5.027 \times 10^{-5} m^2\)

The volume becomes

\(V=(5.027 \times 10^{-5} m^2)(0.15m) \longleftarrow \left[V=AL\right] \)

\(= 7.540 \times 10^{-6} m^3 \)

The rod’s weight is therefore

\(w=\left(76\times 10^3\frac{N}{m^3} \right) (7.540 \times 10^{-6} m^3) \longleftarrow \left[w=\rho _{w}V\right] \)

\(=0.5730\left(\frac{N}{\cancel{m^3}} \right) (\cancel{m^3})\)

\(=0.5730 N \)

and its mass is

\(m=\frac{0.5730N}{9.81m/s^2} \longleftarrow \left[w=mg\right] \)

\(= 5.841 \times 10^{-2} (N)\left(\frac{s^2}{m} \right) \)

\(= 5.841 \times 10^{-2}\left(\frac{kg.\cancel{m}}{\cancel{s^2}} \right) \left(\frac{\cancel{s^2}}{\cancel{m}} \right) \)

\(= 5.841 \times 10^{-2} kg \)

In the last step, we expanded the derived unit newton in terms of the base units meter, kilogram, and second. The quantities of heat removed during the two quenching stages become

\(Q_1=( 5.841\times 10^{-2} kg )\left(0.50\frac{kJ}{kg.^\omicron C } \right) (850^\omicron C – 600^\omicron C) \longleftarrow \left[Q=mc(T-T_0)\right] \)

\(= 7.301(\cancel{kg})\left(\frac{kJ}{\cancel{kg}.^\omicron \cancel{C}} \right)( \cancel{^ºC }) \)

\(= 7.301 kJ\)

and

\(Q_2=( 5.841\times 10^{-3} kg )\left(0.50\frac{kJ}{kg.^\omicron C } \right) (600^\omicron C – 20^\omicron C)) \longleftarrow \left[Q=mc(T-T_0)\right] \)

\(= 16.94(\cancel{kg})\left(\frac{kJ}{\cancel{kg}. \cancel{^\omicron C}} \right)( \cancel{^\omicron C }) \)

\(= 16.94 kJ\)

Discussion
The heat flows from the drill rod into the oil bath. Following the two quenching stages, the temperature of the oil bath rises by amounts that can be calculated by using Equation (7.7). That the second quenching removes more heat makes sense because the second temperature reduction is significantly more than the reduction in the first quenching.

\(Q_1= 7.301 kJ\)
\(Q_2= 16.94 kJ\)
