Guest Question.

Calculate the efficiency of a transformer-coupled class A amplifier for a supply of 12 V and outputs of:

(a) V(p) = 12 V.

(b) V(p) = 6 V.

(c) V(p) = 2 V.

Admin
Solution

Since \(V_{CE_{Q}} = V_{CC} = 12 V\), the maximum and minimum of the voltage swing are
(a) \(V_{CE_{max}} = V_{CE_{Q}} + V(p) = 12 V + 12 V = 24 V\)

 

\(V_{CE_{min}} = V_{CE_{Q}} – V(p) = 12 V – 12 V = 0 V\)
resulting in

\(\% \eta = 50 \left(\frac{24 V – 0 V}{24 V + 0 V}\right)^{2}\% = 50 \%\)

(b) \(V_{CE_{max}} = V_{CE_{Q}} + V(p) = 12 V + 6 V = 18 V\)

 

\(V_{CE_{min}} = V_{CE_{Q}} – V(p) 12 V – 6 V = 6 V\)

resulting in

\(\% \eta = 50 \left(\frac{18 V – 6 V}{18 V + 6 V}\right)^{2}\% = 12.5 \%\)

(c) \(V_{CE_{max}} = V_{CE_{Q}} + V(p) = 12 V + 2 V = 14 V\)

 

\(V_{CE_{min}} = V_{CE_{Q}} – V(p) = 12 V – 2 V = 10 V\)

resulting in

\(\% \eta = 50 \left(\frac{14 V – 10 V}{14 V + 10 V}\right)^{2}\% = 1.39 \%\)

Notice how dramatically the amplifier efficiency drops from a maximum of \(50\%\) for \(V(p) = V_{CC}\) to slightly over \(1\%\) for V(p) = 2 V.
