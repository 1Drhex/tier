Guest Question.

Solution

(a) The results for the individual 6 × 12 in cylinders are given in columns 3 and 4 of Table 2.4. A minimum of two 6 × 12 in cylinders are required per ACI 5.6.2.4. The average of the two cylinder tests is given in column 5, which is the strength test result. The sample standard deviation is determined by Eq. (2.1):

\(s_{s}=[\frac{\sum{(x_{i}-\overset{-}{x} )^{2} } }{(n-1)} ]^{1/2} \)

In this equation, \(x_{i} \) is the result from an individual strength test and \(\overset{-}{x} \) is the average (mean) strength value of n strength tests. The mean strength for 30 tests is equal to the sum of the values in column 5 of Table 2.4 (or column 2 of Table 2.5) divided by 30:

\(\overset{-}{x}=\frac{4,845+…+4,990}{30}=\frac{156,320}{30}=5,211 psi \)

The data in column 3 of Table 2.5 are obtained by subtracting the 28-day average strength by the mean strength. For example, for test 1, \(x_{i}-\overset{-}{x} \) = 4,845 − 5,211 = −366 psi. Given the data in Table 2.5, the sample standard deviation is

\(s_{s}=[\frac{10,425,290}{30-1} ]=600 psi \)

On the basis of the data in Table 2.4, the concrete satisfies the acceptance criteria of ACI 5.6.3:
1. The arithmetic average of each set of three consecutive strength tests, which is given in column 6 of Table 2.4, exceeds \(f^{'}_{c} \)= 4,000 psi.
2. No single strength test is less than 4,000 − 500 = 3,500 psi.
