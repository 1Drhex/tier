Guest Question.

Water flowing at the rate of 0.05 \(m^{3}\)/s has a velocity of 40 m/s. The jet strikes a vane and is deflected \(120^{\circ}\)(Figure E3.7). Friction along the vane is negligible and the entire system is exposed to the atmosphere. Potential changes can also be neglected. Determine the force necessary to hold the vane stationary.

Admin
Solution

\(p_{1}=p_{2}=p_{atmos}\) \(h_{\iota }=0\)

\(z_{1}=z_{2}\) \(w_{s}\)=0

Energy:

\(\frac{p_{1}}{\rho }+\frac{V_{1}^{2}}{2g_{c}}+\frac{g}{g_{c}}z_{1}=\frac{p_{2}}{\rho }+\frac{V_{2}^{2}}{2g_{c}}+\frac{g}{g_{c}}z_{2}+h_{\iota }+w_{s}\)

 

Thus

We indicate the force components of the vane on the fluid as \(R_{x}\) and \(R_{y}\) and put them on the diagram in assumed directions. (If we have guessed wrong, our answer will turn out to be negative.) For the x-component:

\(\sum F_{x}=\frac{\dot{m}}{g_{c}}(V_{2x}-V_{1x})\)

 

\(-R_{x}=\frac{\dot{m}}{g_{c}}\left [ (-V_{2}\sin 30)-V_{1} \right ]=\frac{\dot{m}}{g_{c}}(-\sin 30-1)\)

 

\(-R_{x}=\frac{(10^{3})(0.05)(40)}{1}(-0.5-1)\)

 

\(R_{x}\)=3.0 kN (or 674.4 lbf)

For the y-component:

\(\sum F_{y}=\frac{\dot{m}}{g_{c}}(V_{2y}-V_{1y})\)

 

\(R_{y}=\frac{\dot{m}}{g_{c}}\left [ (V_{2}\cos 30)-0 \right ]\)

 

\(R_{y}=\frac{(10^{3})(0.05)(40)}{1}(0.866)\)

 

\(R_{y}\) = 1.732 kN (or 389.4 lbf)

Note that the assumed directions for \(R_{x}\) and \(R_{y}\) were correct since the answers came out positive.
