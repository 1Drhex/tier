Guest Question.

A clothesline with a maximum tension of 150# is anchored to a wall by means of an eye screw. If the eye screw is capable of carrying a horizontal pulling force (withdrawal force) of 40# per inch of penetration, how many inches \(L\) should the threads be embedded into the wall?

Admin
Solution

For this problem, notice that the direction of the force \(F\) is given in terms of a slope relationship. The small-slope triangle has the same angle \(\theta \) from the horizontal as the force \(F\) makes with the x axis. Therefore, we may conclude that both the small-slope triangle and the large triangle (as shown in the tip-to-tail diagram) are similar.

By similar triangles,

\(\frac{F_{x}}{4} =\frac{ F_{y}}{3} =\frac{F}{5} \)

\(\therefore \) solving for the component forces,

\(\cos \theta =\frac{4}{5} =\frac{F_{x}}{F}\)

 

\(\sin \theta =\frac{3}{5} =\frac{F_{y}}{F}\)

then,

\(\frac{F_{x}}{4}=\frac{F }{5}\)

 

\(F_{x}=\frac{4}{5} (F)=\frac{4}{5}(150\# )=120 \# \)

or

\(F_{x}=F\cos \theta\) ,but \(\cos \theta =\frac{4}{5} \) (per slope triangle)

 

\(F_{x}=150\# \left(\frac{4}{5} \right) =120\# \Longleftarrow CHECKS\)

Continuing with the similar triangle relationship,

\(\frac{F_{y}}{3}=\frac{F }{5}\)

 

\(F_{y}=\frac{3}{5} (F)=\frac{3}{5}(150\# )=90 \#\)

 

\(F_{y}=F\sin \theta =150\# \left(\frac{3}{5} \right) =90\# \Longleftarrow CHECKS\)

If the eye screw is capable of resisting a 40 #/in. penetration in the horizontal direction, the length of embedment required may be calculated as

\(F_{x}=120\# =(40 \space \#-in.)\times (L)\)

 

\(L=\frac{120\#}{40 \space \#-in.} =3^{\prime \prime }\)embedment

Aword of caution concerning the equations for the x and y force components: The components of a force depend on how the reference angle is measured, as shown in Figure 2.25.

In Figure 2.25(a), the components \(F_{x}\) and \(F_{y}\) may be stated as

\(F_{x}= F\cos \theta \)

 

\(F_{y}=F \sin \theta\)

where the direction of force \(F\) is defined by the angle \(\theta \) measured from the horizontal x axis. In the case of Figure 2.25(b), the direction of \(F\) is given in terms of an angle \(\phi \) measured from the y reference axis. This therefore changes the trigonometric notation, where

\(F_{x}= F \sin \phi \)

 

\(F_{y}=F \cos \phi\)

Note: The reversal of sine and cosine depends on how the reference angle is measured.
