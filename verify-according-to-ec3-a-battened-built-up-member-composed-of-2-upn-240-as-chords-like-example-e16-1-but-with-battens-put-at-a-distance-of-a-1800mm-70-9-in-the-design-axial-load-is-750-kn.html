Guest Question.

Verify, according to EC3, a battened built-up member composed of 2 UPN 240 as chords, like Example E16.1, but with battens put at a distance of a = 1800mm (70.9 in.). The design axial load is 750 kN (169 kips). All other parameters are equal to those in Example E16.1.

Chords 2 UPN 240
Steel S275 \(f_{y}\)= 275 MPa (40 ksi)
Area of 1 chord \(A_{ch}=42.3cm^{2}(6.56in.^{2})\)
Moment of inertia about the y–y axis \(I_{y}=3599cm^{4}(86.5in.^{4})\)
Moment of inertia about the z–z axis \(I_{z}=I_{ch}=274cm^{4}(6.58in.^{4})\)
Radius of inertia about the y–y axis \(i_{y}\)= 9.22 cm (3.63 in.)
Radius of inertia about the z–z axis \(i_{z}\)= 2.42 cm (0.953 in.)
Distance between chord centroids \(h_{0}\)= 355mm (14 in.)
Distance between battens a = 1800mm (70.9 in.)
Battens 350 × 150 × 12 plate (13.8 × 5.91 × 0.472 in.)
Built-up member length L = 2000 cm (65.6 ft)
Design axial load \(N_{Ed}\)= 750 kN (169 kips)

Example 16.1

Chords 2 UPN 240
Steel S275 \(f_{y}\)= 275 MPa (40 ksi)
Area of 1 chord \(A_{ch}=42.3cm^{2}(6.56in.^{2})\)
Moment of inertia about the y–y axis \(I_{y}=3599cm^{4}(86.5in.^{4})\)
Moment of inertia about the z–z axis \(I_{z}=I_{ch}=274cm^{4}(6.58in.^{4})\)
Radius of inertia about the y–y axis \(i_{y}\)= 9.22 cm (3.63 in.)
Radius of inertia about the z–z axis \(i_{z}\)= 2.42 cm (0.953 in.)
Distance between chord centroids \(h_{0}\)= 355mm (14 in.)
Distance between battens a = 1200mm (47 in.)
Battens 350 × 150 × 12 plate (13.8 × 5.91 × 0.472 in.)
Built-up member length L = 2000 cm (65.6 ft)
Design axial load \(N_{Ed}\)= 800 kN (180 kips)
Admin
Solution

Verify each chord instability between two battens:
Moment of inertia of the whole member:
\(I_{1}=0/5h^{2}_{0}A_{ch}+2I_{ch} =0.5\times 35.5^{2} \times 42.3 + 2 \times 274 = 27 202 cm^{4} (654 in.^{4})\)
Radius of inertia of the whole section:
\(i_{0}=\sqrt{\frac{I}{2A_{ch}}}=\sqrt{\frac{27202}{2\times 42.3}}=1793 cm(7.06 in.)\)
Critical length of built-up member in the plane containing battens:
\(\lambda =\frac{L_{cr}}{i_{0}}=\frac{2000}{17.93}=112;75\leq \lambda \leq 150 then:\mu =2-\frac{\lambda }{75}=2-\frac{112}{75}=0.51\)
Effective moment of inertia:
\(I_{eff} =0.5h^{2}_{0}A_{ch} +2\mu I_{ch} =0.5 \times 35.5^{2} \times 42.3+ 2 \times 0.51 \times 274 = 26934 cm^{4} (647 in.^{4})\)
Radius of inertia of whole section computed using effective moment of inertia:
\(i_{0,eff}=\sqrt{\frac{I_{eff}}{2A_{ch}}}=\sqrt{\frac{26934}{2\times 42.3}}=17.84 cm(7.02 in.)\)
Moment of inertia of batten :\(I_{b}=\frac{1}{12}\times 1.2\times 15^{3}=337.5cm^{4}(8.11 in.^{4}).\)
Shear stiffness:
\(S_{v}=\frac{24EI_{ch}}{a^{2}\left ( 1+\frac{2I_{ch}}{nI_{b}}\cdot \frac{h_{0}}{a} \right )}=\frac{24\times 21000\times 274}{180^{2}\times \left ( 1+\frac{2\times 274}{2\times 337.5 }\times \frac{35.5}{180} \right )}=3674 kN\)
\(\leq \frac{2\pi ^{2}EI_{ch}}{a^{2}}=\frac{2\times \pi ^{2}\times 21000\times 274}{180^{2}}\)= 3506 kN; Assume \(S_{v}\)= 3506 kN(788 kips)
Let’s consider :\(e_{0}=\frac{L_{cr}}{500}=\frac{2000}{500}\)=4 cm (1.58 in.) (eccentricity).
Built-up member critical load:
\(N_{cr}= \pi ^{2}EI_{eff}/L^{2}=3.14^{2} \times 21.000 \times 26934/2000^{2}\)= 1396 kN (314 kips)
Second order moment:
\(M_{Ed}=\frac{N_{Ed}e_{0}}{1-\frac{N_{Ed}}{N_{cr}}-\frac{N_{Ed}}{S_{v}}}=\frac{750\times 4}{1-\frac{750}{1396}-\frac{750}{3506}}=12056 kNcm(88.9 kip-ft)\)
Maximum axial load in a chord considering second order effects:
\(N_{ch,Ed} =0.5 N_{Ed} +\frac{M_{Ed}h_{0}A_{ch}}{2I_{eff}}=0.5\times 750+\frac{12056\times 35.5\times 42.3}{2\times 26934}\)=375+336=711kN(160 kips)
Verify a chord under compression load \(N_{ch,Ed}\) using as unbraced length the distance between chord and using minimum radius of inertia.
\(\lambda _{1}=93.9\varepsilon =93.9\sqrt{\frac{235}{f_{y}}}=93.9\sqrt{\frac{235}{275}}=86.8;\lambda =\frac{a}{i_{z}}=\frac{180}{2.42}=74\)
\(\bar{\lambda }=\lambda /\lambda _{1}=74/86.8=0.853 ;\Phi=0.5\left [ 1+a(\bar{\lambda }-0.2)+\bar{\lambda}^{2} \right ]=0.5\times [1+0.49\times (0.853-0.2)+0.853^{2}]\)
\(\chi =\frac{1}{\Phi +\sqrt{\Phi ^{2}-\bar{\lambda}^{2}}}=\frac{1}{1.024+\sqrt{1.024^{2}-0.853^{2}}}=0.629\)
Compute the design compressive strength of a single chord \(N_{ch,Rd}\) and compare it with maximum compression load \(N_{ch,Ed}\).
\(N_{ch,Rd}=\chi \frac{A_{ch}f_{y}}{\gamma _{M1}}=0.629\times \frac{42.3\times 27.50}{1.00}=732kN> N_{ch,Ed}=711 kN Ok\)
(Stress ratio :711/732 = 0,97).
Verify the whole built-up member (two chords) for buckling in the plane of battens:
Use the already computed radius of inertia of the whole section, \(i_{0}\).
\(\lambda =\frac{L_{cr}}{i_{0,eff}}=\frac{2000}{17.84}=112;\bar{\lambda }=\lambda /\lambda _{1}=112/86.8=1.29\)
\(\Phi =0.5\left [ 1+a(\bar{\lambda }-0.2)+\bar{\lambda}^{2} \right ]=0.5\times \left [ 1+0.49(1.29-0.2)+1.29^{2} \right ]=1.599\)
\(\chi =\frac{1}{ \Phi +\sqrt{\Phi ^{2}-\bar{\lambda }^{2}}}=\frac{1}{1.559+\sqrt{1.559^{2}-1.29^{2}}}=0.393\)
\(N_{Rd}=\chi \frac{2A_{ch}f_{y}}{\gamma _{M1}}=0.393\times\frac{2\times 42.3\times 27.50}{1.00}=914 kN> N_{Ed}=750kN\)
In this case, different from Example E16.1, the verification of a single chord for buckling on the distance between battens is dominant on the verification of the whole built-up member for buckling on the length of 20 m. Other verifications are equivalent to those in Example E16.1. In Table E16.2.1 a comparison between stress ratios, for Examples E16.1 and E16.2, is shown.

Example Chord local buckling (between two battens) Built-up member global buckling(over the entire member length) Dominant buckling mode
E16.1 0.73 0.88 Global
E16.2 0.97 0.82 Local

It can be outlined that, by increasing batten distance from 1200 to 1800 mm, and as a consequence increasing slenderness of a single chord between two battens from 50 to 74, the dimensioning buckling mode switches from the global buckling of the built-up member to the buckling of a chord between two battens.
