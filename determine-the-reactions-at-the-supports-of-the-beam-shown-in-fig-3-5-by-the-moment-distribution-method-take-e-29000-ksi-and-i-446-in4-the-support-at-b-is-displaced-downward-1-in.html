Guest Question.

Determine the reactions at the supports of the beam shown in Fig. 3.5 by the moment distribution method. Take E = 29,000 ksi and I=\(446in^{4}\).The support at B is displaced downward 1 in.

Admin
Solution

Method 1

Step 1

Stiffness Factors are not needed here because the distribution factors are known.

Step 2

\(DF_{AB}=0\) (fixed support)

 

\(DF_{BA}=1\) (End-pin support)

 

\(DF_{BC}=0\) (No moment is transferred from B to C)

Step 3

Find\(M_{FAB}=0\) due to settlement downward of support B \(\rightarrow M_{FAB}=-\frac{3EI\Delta}{L^{2} }=-\frac{3\left(4176000\right)\left(0.0215\right)\left(\frac{1}{12} \right) }{\left(10\right) ^{2} }=-224.46^{k-ft} \)

 

\(M_{FBC}=-36\left(12\right)=-432^{k-ft} \)

Note:\(M_{FBC}\) is negative because the internal moment caused by the loading, acts in the counterclockwise direction (opposite to the external moment at that point).

In this moment distribution table, the unbalanced moments are equal to the fixed-end moments at each joint. Just as was done in the first problem, the unbalanced moment is multiplied by the respected DF and the Bal is found. Only one carry over takes place, which is from BA to AB. After only two cycles the moment distribution table is finished because all Bal values are zero

The answers found here,\(M_{AB}=-8.46^{k-ft}\circlearrowleft ,M_{BA}=432^{k-ft}\circlearrowright,\)and\( M_{BC}=-432^{k-ft}\circlearrowleft \)are the same as found by the force method in Chapter One () and by the slope-deflection method in Chapter Two (), but with less work.

Method 2

Step 1

Stiffness Factors are not needed because the distribution factors are known.

Step 2

\(DF_{AB}=0\) (fixed support)

 

\(DF_{BA}=1\) (End-pin support)

 

\(DF_{BC}=0\) (No moment is transferred from B to C)

Step 3

\(M_{FAB}=M_{FBA}=-\frac{6EI\Delta}{L^{2} }=-\frac{6\left(4176000\right)\left(0.0215\right)\left(\frac{1}{12} \right) }{\left(10\right) ^{2} }=-488.92^{k-ft}\)

Step 4

Since the left column fixed-end moments are used, member BA now has the same moment as member AB.The unbalanced moment for joint B is found by summing up\(M_{FBA} \)and\(M_{FBC} \).This value is then multiplied by the distribution factor of members BA and BC as seen below:\(Bal=-M_{\left(unbalanced\right) }*DF\)

 

\(Bal_{BA}=\left(-M_{BC}+M_{BA} \right)*1\)

 

\(Bal_{BA}=-\left(-432+\left(-448.92\right) \right)*1=880.92^{ft-k}\)

When comparing the two methods, the final moments are exactly the same,and found in only two cycles. This proves that both methods can be used for a given problem based on preference. The authors of this book prefer method 2 due to the simplicity of finding the fixed-end moments. It should also be noted that the results are identical to those obtained using the slope-deflection method in which can be seen in the chapter summary in Section 3.7.
