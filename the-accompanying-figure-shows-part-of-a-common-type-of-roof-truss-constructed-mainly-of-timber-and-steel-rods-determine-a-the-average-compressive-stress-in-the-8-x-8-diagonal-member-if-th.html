Guest Question.

The accompanying figure shows part of a common type of roof truss, constructed mainly of timber and steel rods. Determine:

a. The average compressive stress in the 8″ × 8″ diagonal member if the load in it is 20 k.

b. The tensile stress in the \(\frac{3}{4}\)” diameter threaded steel rod if the load in it is 4 k.

c. The bearing stress between the timber and the 4″ × 4″ square steel washer if the hole in it is \(\frac{7}{8}\)” diameter.

d. The bearing stress between the brick wall column and the 8″ × 10″ timber if the load in the column is 15 k.

e. The length L required to keep the dashed portion of the 8″ × 10″ member from shearing off due to the horizontal thrust of 16 k against the steel shoe. The Fv = 120psi allowable.

Admin
Solution

a) P = 20,000#; A = 64 in.2

\(f_{c} = \frac{P}{A} = \frac{20,000\#}{64 in.^{2}} = 312.5 \#/in.^{2}\)

 

b) P = 40,000#; \(A \neq \frac{\pi D^{2}}{4} \) since rod is threaded

A =0.302 in.2

\(\therefore f_{t} = \frac{P}{A} = \frac{4000\#}{0.302 in.^{2}} = 13,250 psi\)

 

c) P = 40,000#; \( A = 4^{"} \times 4^{"} – \frac{\pi ({7}/{8}^{"})^{2}}{4} = 15.4 in.^{2}\)

 

\(f_{brg} = \frac{4000\#}{15.4 in.^{2}} = 259.7 \#/in.^{2}\)

 

d) P = 15,000#; A = 8″×12’= 96 in.2

 

\(f = \frac{15,000\#}{96 in.^{2}} = 156.3 psi\)

 

e) P = 16,000#; A = 8″×L; Fv = 120psi

 

\(A_{req'd} = \frac{P}{F_{v}} = \frac{16,000\#}{120\#/in.^{2}} = 133.3 in.^{2}\)

 

133.3 in.2 = 8″×L; L = 16.7″
