Guest Question.

A sloping circular gate with a diameter of 3 ft is submerged at a depth of 7ft in water open to the atmosphere, making an \(20^{\circ } \) angle with the horizontal, as illustrated in Figure EP 2.34. (a) Determine the magnitude of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the sloping circular gate. (b) Determine the location of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the sloping circular gate.

Admin
Solution

(a) The magnitude of the hydrostatic resultant force acting on the plane gate is determined by applying Equation 2.205 \(F= \int{pdA} = \int_{0}^{L}{(\gamma h(y))(w(y)dy)} = \int_{0}^{L}{(\gamma } (y_{o}+ y )\sin \alpha)(w(y)dy)\), integrating over the length of the plane gate using the top of the plane as a reference point for integration. Furthermore, because there is no analytical solution for the integral given in Equation 2.205 \(F= \int{pdA} = \int_{0}^{L}{(\gamma h(y))(w(y)dy)} = \int_{0}^{L}{(\gamma } (y_{o}+ y )\sin \alpha)(w(y)dy)\) for the hydrostatic force, it must be numerically integrated. As such, in order to successfully numerically integrate Equation 2.205 \(F= \int{pdA} = \int_{0}^{L}{(\gamma h(y))(w(y)dy)} = \int_{0}^{L}{(\gamma } (y_{o}+ y )\sin \alpha)(w(y)dy)\) using Mathcad, the units for all the variables have been temporarily eliminated throughout the entire example problem solution as follows:

\( D:= 3 ft\) \(A:= \frac{\pi .D^{2} }{4} =7.069\) \(L:= D = 3\) \(r:= \frac{D}{2} =1.5\) \(h_{o} :=7\)

\(\gamma :=62.417\) \(\alpha :=20 deg\) \(\sin (\alpha )=0.342\) \(\sin \alpha := \sin (\alpha ) = 0.342\)

\(y_{o}:= \frac{h_{o}}{\sin \alpha } =20.467\) \(h_{y}:= (y_{o}+ y) \sin \alpha\) \(w(y):=2 \sqrt{-y(y-2r)} \)

\(F:= \int_{0}^{L}{\gamma h(y)w(y)dy} \longrightarrow 3314.747\)

Alternatively, the magnitude of the hydrostatic force acting on the plane gate may be determined by applying Equation 2.207 \(F= p_{ca} A= \gamma h_{ca}A= \gamma (y_{o}+y_{ca})\left(\frac{\pi D^{2} }{4} \right) = \gamma (y_{o}+ D/2) \sin \alpha \left(\frac{\pi D^{2} }{4} \right)\) as follows:

\(y_{ca}:= \frac{L}{2} = 1.5\) \(h_{ca}:= (y_{0}+ y_{ca})\sin \alpha=7.513\)
\(F:= \gamma h_{ca}A=3314.747\)

Thus, the magnitude of the hydrostatic force acting on the plane gate is 3,314.747 lb.
(b) The location of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the sloping plane gate is determined by applying Equation 2.210 \(y_{F} = \frac{M}{F} \). Furthermore, because there is no analytical solution for the integral given in Equation 2.210 \(y_{F} = \frac{M}{F} \) for the location of the hydro- static force, it must be numerically integrated. As such, in order to successfully numerically integrate Equation 2.210 using Mathcad, the units for all the variables have been temporarily eliminated throughout the entire example problem solution as follows:

\(y_{F}:= \frac{\int_{0}^{L}{y(\gamma h(y))w(y)dy} }{\int_{0}^{L}{(\gamma h(y))w(y)dy} } \longrightarrow 1.526\) \(h_{F}:= (y_{o}+y_{F} ) \sin \alpha = 7.522\)

Alternatively, the location of the resultant force acting on the sloping plane gate may be determined by applying Equation 2.213 \(z_{F} = \frac{I_{x-ca}+A z^{2}_{ca} }{z_{ca}A} =\frac{I_{x-ca} }{z_{ca}A} +z_{ca}= \frac{\pi D^{4}/64 }{(y_{o} +D/2)(\pi D^{4}/4)} + (y_{o} +D/2)\) as follows:

\(I_{x_{xca} } := \frac{\pi .D^{4} }{64} =3.976\) \(z_{ca}:= (y_{o}+y_{ca} )= 21.967\)
\(z_{F}:= \frac{I_{x_{xca} }+ A x^{2}_{ca} }{z_{ca}A} =21.992\) \(y_{F}:= y_{F}- y_{o}=1.526\)
\(h_{F}:= z_{F} \sin \alpha =7.522 \)

Thus, the location of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the vertical plane gate is 7.522 ft below the water surface.
