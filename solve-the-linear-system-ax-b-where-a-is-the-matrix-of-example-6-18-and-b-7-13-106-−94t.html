Guest Question.

Solve the linear system \(\mathrm{Ax}=\mathbf{b}\) where \(\mathrm{A}\) is the matrix of and \(\mathbf{b}=\left(\begin{array}{cccc}7 & 13 & 106 & -94\end{array}\right)^{T}\).

 

Admin
Solution

We first need to find the vector \(y\) such that \(\mathrm{Ly}=\mathrm{b}\) where \(\mathrm{L}\) is the lower triangular matrix that we evaluated in the previous example. Substituting this \(\mathrm{L}\) and the given \(\mathbf{b}\) into \(\mathrm{Ly}=\mathbf{b}\) :

\( \mathbf{L y}=\left(\begin{array}{rrrr} 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 5 & 2 & 0 & 0 \\ 6 & -5 & 1 & 0 \\ 5 & 8 & 1 & 1 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} y_{1} \\ y_{2} \\ y_{3} \\ y_{4} \end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{c} 7 \\ 13 \\ 106 \\ -94 \end{array}\right) \) (†)

From the first row we have \(y_{1}=7\). Substituting this \(y_{1}=7\) into the second row gives

\(\begin{aligned}5 y_{1}+2 y_{2} &=13 \\5(7)+2 y_{2} &=13 \text { gives } y_{2}=-11\end{aligned}\)

Using these values \(y_{1}=7\) and \(y_{2}=-11\) in the third row of \((\dagger)\) yields

\(\begin{aligned}&6 y_{1}-5 y_{2}+y_{3}=106 \\&6(7)-5(-11)+y_{3}=106 \Rightarrow y_{3}=9\end{aligned}\)

Repeating this process with \(y_{1}=7, y_{2}=-11\) and \(y_{3}=9\) on the bottom row of \((\dagger)\) gives

\(\begin{aligned}&5 y_{1}+8 y_{2}+y_{3}+y_{4}=-94 \\&5(7)+8(-11)+9+y_{4}=-94 \Rightarrow y_{4}=-50\end{aligned}\)

We have \(y_{1}=7, y_{2}=-11, y_{3}=9\) and \(y_{4}=-50\), or in vector form:

\(\mathbf{y}=\left(\begin{array}{llll}7 & -11 & 9 & -50\end{array}\right)^{T}\)

Now we use our upper triangular matrix \(\mathrm{U}\) to find the required unknown \(x^{\prime}\) s from \(\mathrm{Ux}=\mathrm{y}\). We found the upper triangular matrix \(\mathrm{U}\) in the above . Substituting that matrix \(\mathrm{U}\) and the above vector \(\mathbf{y}\) into \(\mathrm{Ux}=\mathrm{y}\) gives

\(\left(\begin{array}{cccc}1 & 4 & 5 & 3 \\0 & 1 & 1 & -2 \\0 & 0 & 2 & 3 \\0 & 0 & 0 & -10\end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l}x_{1} \\x_{2} \\x_{3} \\x_{4}\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{c}7 \\-11 \\9 \\-50\end{array}\right)\) (*)

From the bottom row of \(\left(^{*}\right)\) we have \(x_{4}=5\). Substituting this \(x_{4}=5\) into the penultimate row of \(\left({ }^{*}\right)\) gives

\(\begin{aligned}&2 x_{3}+3 x_{4}=9 \\&2 x_{3}+3(5)=9 \Rightarrow x_{3}=-3\end{aligned}\)

Using the second row and \(x\) values already found, \(x_{3}=-3\) and \(x_{4}=5\) gives

\(\begin{aligned}&x_{2}+x_{3}-2 x_{4}=-11 \\&x_{2}-3-2(5)=-11 \Rightarrow x_{2}=2\end{aligned}\)

Using these values \(x_{2}=2, x_{3}=-3\) and \(x_{4}=5\) in the top row of \(\left({ }^{*}\right)\) yields

\(\begin{aligned} &x_{1}+4 x_{2}+5 x_{3}+3 x_{4}=7 \\ &x_{1}+4(2)+5(-3)+3(5)=7 \Rightarrow x_{1}=-1 \end{aligned}\)

Hence our unknowns are \(x_{1}=-1, x_{2}=2, x_{3}=-3\) and \(x_{4}=5\)
