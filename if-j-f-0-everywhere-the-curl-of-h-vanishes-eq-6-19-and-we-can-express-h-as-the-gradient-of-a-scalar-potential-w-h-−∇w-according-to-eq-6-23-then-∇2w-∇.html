Guest Question.

If \(J _{f}= 0\) everywhere, the curl of H vanishes (Eq. 6.19), and we can express H as the gradient of a scalar potential W :

\(\nabla \times H = J _{f}\) (6.19)

H = –∇W.

According to Eq. 6.23, then,

∇ · H = –∇ · M. (6.23)

\(\nabla^{2} W=( \nabla \cdot M )\) .

so W obeys Poisson’s equation, with ∇ · M as the “source.” This opens up all the machinery of Chapter 3. As an example, find the field inside a uniformly magnetized sphere () by separation of variables. [Hint: ∇ · M = 0 everywhere except at the surface (r = R), so W satisfies Laplace’s equation in the regions r < R and r > R; use Eq. 3.65, and from Eq. 6.24 figure out the appropriate boundary condition on W .]

\(V(r, \theta)=\sum_{l=0}^{\infty}\left(A_{l} r^{l}+\frac{B_{l}}{r^{l+1}}\right) P_{l}(\cos \theta)\) (3.65)

\(H_{\text {above }}^{\perp}-H_{\text {below }}^{\perp}=-\left(M_{\text {above }}^{\perp}-M_{\text {below }}^{\perp}\right)\) (6.24)

Admin
Solution

“Potentials”:

\(\left\{\begin{array}{l}W_{ in }(r, \theta)=\sum A_{l} r^{l} P_{l}(\cos \theta),(r<R); \\W_{ out }(r, \theta)=\sum \frac{B_{l}}{r^{l+1}} P_{l}(\cos \theta),(r>R).\end{array}\right.\)

Boundary Conditions:

\(\left\{\begin{array}{l}\text { (i) } W_{\text {in }}(R, \theta)=W_{\text {out }}(R, \theta), \\\text { (ii) }-\left.\frac{\partial W_{\text {out }}}{\partial r}\right|_{R}+\left.\frac{\partial W_{\text {in }}}{\partial r}\right|_{R}=M^{\perp}=M \hat{ z } \cdot \hat{ r }=M \cos \theta.\end{array}\right.\)

(The continuity of W follows from the gradient theorem: \( W( b )-W( a )=\int_{ a }^{ b } \nabla W \cdot d l =-\int_{ a }^{ b } H \cdot d l \) ; if the two points are infinitesimally separated, this last integral → 0.)

\(\left\{\begin{array}{l}( i ) \Rightarrow A_{l} R^{l}=\frac{B_{l}}{R^{l}+1} \Rightarrow B_{l}=R^{2 l+1} A_{l} \\(i i) \Rightarrow \sum(l+1) \frac{B_{l}}{R^{l+2}} P_{l}(\cos \theta)+\sum l A_{l} R^{l-2} P_{l}(\cos \theta)=M \cos \theta\end{array}\right.\)

Combining these:

\(\sum(2 l+1) R^{l-1} A_{l} P_{l}(\cos \theta)=M \cos \theta, \text { so } A_{l}=0(l \neq 1), \text { and } 3 A_{1}=M \Rightarrow A_{1}=\frac{M}{3}\) .

Thus \(W_{\text {in }}(r, \theta)=\frac{M}{3} r \cos \theta=\frac{M}{3} z, \text { and hence } H _{\text {in }}=-\nabla W_{\text {in }}=-\frac{M}{3} \hat{ z }=-\frac{1}{3} M\) , so

\(B =\mu_{0}( H + M )=\mu_{0}\left(-\frac{1}{3} M + M \right)=\frac{2}{3} \mu_{0} M\) .
