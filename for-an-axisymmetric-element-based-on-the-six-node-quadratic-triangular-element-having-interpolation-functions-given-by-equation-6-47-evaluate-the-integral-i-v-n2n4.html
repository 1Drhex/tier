Guest Question.

For an axisymmetric element based on the six-node, quadratic triangular element having interpolation functions given by Equation 6.47, evaluate the integral

\(\ I=\int\limits_{V}^{}{N_{2}N_{4} dV } \)
Admin
Solution

Using Equation 6.93,

\(\ \left \{ \begin{matrix} \frac{\partial}{\partial r} \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial s} \end{matrix} \right \}\left[N\right]=\left [ \begin{matrix} \frac{\partial x}{\partial r} & \frac{\partial x}{\partial s} \\ \frac{\partial y}{\partial r} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial s} \end{matrix} \right ]\left \{ \begin{matrix} \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial y} \end{matrix} \right \} \left[N\right] \) (6.93)

 

\(\ I=\int\limits_{V}^{}{N_{2}N_{4} dV }=2\pi \int\limits_{A}^{}{N_{2}N_{4} r dr dz}=2\pi \int\limits_{A}^{}{L_{2}(2L_{2}- 1 )(4L_{1}L_{2} )r dr dz} \)

Now observe that, even though the interpolation functions vary quadratically over the element area, the area coordinates, by deﬁnition, vary linearly. Since the element sides are straight, the radial coordinate can still be expressed as

\(\ r=L_{1}r_{1}+ L_{2}r_{2}+ L_{3}r_{3} \)

Therefore, we have

\(\ I=2\pi \int\limits_{A}^{}{L_{2}(2L_{2}- 1 )(4L_{1}L_{2} )(L_{1}r_{1}+ L_{2}r_{2}+ L_{3}r_{3})dA} \)

or

\(\ I=16\pi \int\limits_{A}^{}{(L^{2}_{1}L^{3}_{2}r_{1}+ L_{1}L^{4}_{2}r_{2}+L_{1}L^{3}_{2}L_{3}r_{3})dA}- 8\pi \int\limits_{A}^{}{(L^{2}_{1}L^{2}_{2}r_{1}+ L_{1}L^{3}_{2}r_{2}+ L_{1}L^{2}_{2}L_{3}r_{3})dA} \)

Application of the integration formula, Equation 6.49, to each of the six integrals represented here (left as an exercise), we ﬁnd

\(\ \int\limits_{A}^{}{\int\limits_{}^{}{L^{a}_{1}L^{b}_{2}L^{c}_{3} }dA}=(2A)\frac{a!b!c!}{(a+ b+ c+ 2)!} \) (6.49)

 

\(\ I=\frac{\pi A}{315}(6r_{2}- 4r_{1}- 2r_{3} ) \)
