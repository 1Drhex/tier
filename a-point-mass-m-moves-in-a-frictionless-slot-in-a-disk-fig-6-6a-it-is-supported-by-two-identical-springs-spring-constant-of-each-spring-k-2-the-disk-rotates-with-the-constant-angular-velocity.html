Guest Question.

A point mass m moves in a frictionless slot in a disk (Fig. 6.6a). It is supported by two identical springs (spring constant of each spring k/2). The disk rotates with the constant angular velocity ω about point A.

Describe the motion of the mass relative to the rotating disk.
Determine the force that is exerted from the slot on the mass.
Neglect the weight of the mass.

Admin
Solution

The mass can only move in the slot: it undergoes a rectilinear motion relative to the disk. We introduce the rotating coordinate system ξ, η shown in Fig. 6.6b. Then the motion takes place in the direction of the η-axis. Hence, ξ = 0, \(\dot{\xi } =0, \ddot{\xi }=0 \) The equation of motion (6.20) in the rotating system is given by

\(ma_{r}=F+F_{f} +F _{c}\) . (6.20) (a)

The forces acting on the mass are the contact force N and the force \(2\frac{k}{2} \eta \) exerted by the springs (see Fig. 6.6b). Therefore the external force follows as

\(F=Ne_{\xi }-k\eta e_{\eta }\) . (b)

In order to determine the fictitious forces \(F_{f} \) and \(F_{c} \) we first have to find the acceleration of the frame of reference and the Coriolis acceleration. The origin 0 of the rotating system is located at a distance h from the center A of the disk. Its acceleration is therefore given by \(a_{0}=-h\omega ^{2}e_{\xi}\) (circular motion with \( \dot{\omega }=0 \) With r = η and \( e_{r}=e_{\eta } \) we obtain from (6.18)

\(\upsilon _{f} =\upsilon _{0} +r\omega e_{\varphi } , a_{f} =a_{0} +r\dot{\omega }e_{\varphi }-r\omega ^{2}e_{r}\) (6.18)

\(a _{f} =-h\omega ^{2}e_{\xi }-\eta \omega ^{2}e_{\eta } \rightarrow F_{f}=m\left(h\omega ^{2}e_{\xi }+\eta \omega ^{2}e_{\eta } \right)\) . (c)

The vector of the angular velocity ω is orthogonal to the ξ, η- plane. Therefore, the Coriolis acceleration and the Coriolis force follow from (6.17b) with \(\upsilon _{r}=\dot{\eta }e_{\eta } \) as

\(a_{f}=a_{0}+\dot{\omega }\times r_{0P} \times \omega \times \left(\omega \times r_{0P} \right) , a_{r}=\frac{d^{*}\upsilon _{r} }{dt}=\frac{d^{2*}r_{0P} }{dt^{2} } , a_{c}=2\omega \times \upsilon _{r}\) . (6.17b)

\(a_{c}=-2\dot{\eta }\omega e_{\xi }\rightarrow F_{c}=2m\dot{\eta }\omega e_{\xi } \) . (d)

Inserting the relative acceleration

\(a_{r} =\ddot{\eta }e_{\eta }\)

and the forces (b) – (d) into (a) leads to

\(0=N+mh\omega ^{2} +2m\dot{\eta }\omega , m\ddot{\eta }=-k\eta +m\eta \omega ^{2}\) . (e)

The second equation yields

\(\ddot{\eta }+\left(\frac{k}{m} -\omega ^{2} \right)\eta =0 \) .

Thus, the point mass undergoes a harmonic vibration η = A cos ω*t+B sin ω*t relative to the rotating disk if \(\omega ^{2} \)< k/m. The circular frequency \( \omega ^{*} =\sqrt{k/m-\omega ^{2} } \) is smaller than the circular frequency \(\sqrt{k/m} \) in the case of a non-rotating disk (ω = 0). The first equation in (e) yields the contact force

\(N=-m\left(h\omega ^{2}+2\dot{\eta }\omega \right)\) .
