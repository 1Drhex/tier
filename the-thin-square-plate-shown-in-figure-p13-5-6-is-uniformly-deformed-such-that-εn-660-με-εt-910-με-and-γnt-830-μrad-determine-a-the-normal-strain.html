Guest Question.

The thin square plate shown in Figure P13.5/6 is uniformly deformed such that \(\varepsilon_{n}=660 \mu \varepsilon, \varepsilon_{t}=910 \mu \varepsilon\), and \(\gamma_{n t}=830 \mu rad\). Determine
(a) the normal strain \(\varepsilon_{x}\) in the plate.
(b) the normal strain \(\varepsilon_{y}\) in the plate.
(c) the shear strain \(\gamma_{x y}\) in the plate.

Admin
Solution

The given strain values are:

\(\varepsilon_{n}=660 \mu \varepsilon \quad \varepsilon_{t}=910 \mu \varepsilon \quad \gamma_{n t}=830 \mu rad\)

The x axis is rotated 45° counterclockwise from the x axis; therefore, we can use θ = +45° (from the n to the x axis) with the strains associated with the n and t axes to determine the strains in the x and y directions.

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{x} &=\varepsilon_{n} \cos ^{2} \theta+\varepsilon_{t} \sin ^{2} \theta+\gamma_{n t} \sin \theta \cos \theta \\&=(660 \mu \varepsilon) \cos ^{2}\left(45^{\circ}\right)+(910 \mu \varepsilon) \sin ^{2}\left(45^{\circ}\right)+(830 \mu \operatorname{rad}) \sin \left(45^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(45^{\circ}\right) \\&=1,200 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)

The normal strain in the y direction is found by setting θ = 45° + 90° = 135° in the normal strain transformation equation:

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{y} &=(660 \mu \varepsilon) \cos ^{2}\left(135^{\circ}\right)+(910 \mu \varepsilon) \sin ^{2}\left(135^{\circ}\right)+(830 \mu rad ) \sin \left(135^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(135^{\circ}\right) \\&=370 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)

The shear strain \(\gamma_{n t}\) is found from the shear strain transformation equation [Eq. (13.5)]:

\(\begin{aligned}\gamma_{x y} &=-2\left(\varepsilon_{n}-\varepsilon_{t}\right) \sin \theta \cos \theta+\gamma_{n t}\left(\cos ^{2} \theta-\sin ^{2} \theta\right) \\&=-2[(660 \mu \varepsilon)-(910 \mu \varepsilon)] \sin \left(45^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(45^{\circ}\right)+(830 \mu \operatorname{rad})\left[\cos ^{2}\left(45^{\circ}\right)-\sin ^{2}\left(45^{\circ}\right)\right] \\&=250 \mu rad\end{aligned}\)
