Guest Question.

Obtain the stiffness matrix and the equivalent nodal force vector for a 4-noded axisymmetric rectangle.

Admin
Solution

Eq.(4.80) for the shape functions in cartesian coordinates will be used, i.e.

 

\(\begin{array}{lll}N_{1}=\frac{1}{4}\left(1-\frac{r}{a}\right)\left(1-\frac{s}{b}\right) & ; & N_{2}=\frac{1}{4}\left(1+\frac{r}{a}\right)\left(1-\frac{s}{b}\right) \\N_{3} =\frac{1}{4}\left(1+\frac{r}{a}\right)\left(1+\frac{s}{b}\right) & ; & N_{4}=\frac{1}{4}\left(1-\frac{r}{a}\right)\left(1+\frac{s}{b}\right)\end{array}\) (4.80)

 

\(\begin{array}{lll}N_{1} & =\frac{1}{4}\left(1-\frac{r}{a}\right)\left(1-\frac{z}{b}\right) \quad ; & N_{2}=\frac{1}{4}\left(1+\frac{r}{a}\right)\left(1-\frac{z}{b}\right) \\N_{3} & =\frac{1}{4}\left(1+\frac{r}{a}\right)\left(1+\frac{z}{b}\right) \quad ;& N_{4}=\frac{1}{4}\left(1-\frac{r}{a}\right)\left(1+\frac{z}{b}\right)\end{array}\)

 

The strain matrix of Eq.(7.20) is

 

\(\varepsilon=\sum_{i=1}^{3}\left[\begin{array}{cc}\frac{\partial N_{i}}{\partial r} & 0 \\0 & \frac{\partial N_{i}}{\partial z} \\\frac{N_{i}}{r} & 0 \\\frac{\partial N_{i}}{\partial z} & \frac{\partial N_{i}}{\partial r}\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}u_{i} \\w_{i}\end{array}\right\}=\left[ B _{1}, B _{2}, B _{3}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l} a _{1}^{(e)} \\a _{2}^{(e)} \\a _{3}^{(e)}\end{array}\right\}= B a ^{(e)}\) (7.20)

 

\(B =\left[ B _{1}, B _{2}, B _{3}, B _{4}\right]\)

 

where, for instance,

 

\(B _{1}=\left[\begin{array}{cc}-\frac{1}{4 a}\left(1-\frac{z}{b}\right) & 0 \\0 & -\frac{1}{4 b}\left(1-\frac{r}{a}\right) \\\frac{\left(1-\frac{r}{a}\right)\left(1-\frac{z}{b}\right)}{4 r} & 0 \\-\frac{1}{4 b}\left(1-\frac{r}{a}\right) & -\frac{1}{4 a}\left(1-\frac{z}{b}\right)\end{array}\right]\)

 

with similar expressions for \(B _{2}, B _{3} \text { and } B _{4}\).

 

The element stiffness matrix is obtained from Eq.(7.28). The expression of \(B _{i}\) shows that the stiffness matrix integrals contain terms like \(r, z, z^{2}, \frac{1}{r}, \frac{z}{r}, \frac{z^{2}}{r}\). The computation of the integrals over the element area can be performed analytically as explained in Section 5.6. The expressions from Box 7.2 are also applicable after splitting the element into two triangular regions. The equivalent nodal force vector due to body forces is deduced from Eq.(7.33a) as

 

\(\underset{2 n \times 2 n}{ K ^{(e)}}=2 \pi \iint_{A^{(e)}} B ^{T} D B r d A\) (7.28a)

 

\(\underset{2 \times 2}{ K _{i j}^{(e)}}=2 \pi \iint_{A^{(e)}} \underset{2 \times 4}{ B _{i}^{T}} \underset{4 \times 4}{ D } \underset{4 \times 2}{ B _{j}} r d r d z\) (7.28b)

 

\(f _{b}=2 \pi \iint_{A^{(e)}} N ^{T} b r d A\) (7.33a)

 

\(f _{b}^{(e)}=2 \pi \iint_{A^{(\varepsilon)}}\left[ N _{1}, N _{2}, N _{3}, N _{4}\right]^{T} b r d r d z\)

 

with

 

\(f _{b_{i}}^{(e)}=2 \pi \iint_{A^{(e)}} N _{i}^{T} b r d r d z\)

 

The integration of the terms in \(r, r^{2} \text { and } r z\) in above expression can be obtained analytically as explained in Section 5.6.
