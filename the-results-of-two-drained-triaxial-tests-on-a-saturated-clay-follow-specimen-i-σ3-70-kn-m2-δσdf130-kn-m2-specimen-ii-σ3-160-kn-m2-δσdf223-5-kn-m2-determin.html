Guest Question.

The results of two drained triaxial tests on a saturated clay follow:

Specimen I:

\( \sigma_{3}=70 kN/m^{2}\)

 

\( (\Delta \sigma _{d} )_{f}=130 kN/m^{2} \)

Specimen II:

\( \sigma_{3}=160 kN/m^{2}\)

 

\( (\Delta \sigma _{d} )_{f}=223.5 kN/m^{2} \)

Determine the shear strength parameters.

Admin
Solution

Refer to Figure 12.27. For Specimen I, the principal stresses at failure are

\( \sigma ^{\prime }_{3}=\sigma_{3}=70 kN/m^{2}\)

and

\( \sigma ^{\prime }_{1}=\sigma_{1}=\sigma_{3}+(\Delta \sigma _{d} )_{f}=70+130=200kN/m^{2}\)

Similarly, the principal stresses at failure for Specimen II are

\( \sigma ^{\prime }_{3}=\sigma_{3}=160 kN/m^{2}\)

and

\( \sigma ^{\prime }_{1}=\sigma_{1}=\sigma_{3}+(\Delta \sigma _{d} )_{f}=160+223.5=383.5kN/m^{2}\)

Now, from Eq. (12.23),

\( \phi ^{\prime }_{1} =2\left\{tan^{-1} [\frac{\sigma ^{\prime }_{1(1)}- \sigma ^{\prime }_{1(2)} }{\sigma ^{\prime }_{3(1)} -\sigma ^{\prime }_{3(2)} } ]^{0.5}-45° \right\} \) \(=2\left\{tan^{-1}\left[\frac{200-383.5}{70-160} \right] ^{0.5} -45° \right\} =20°\)

Again, from Eq. (12.24),

\( c^{\prime } =\frac{\sigma ^{\prime }_{1(1)} -\sigma ^{\prime }_{3(1)} \tan ^{2}(45+\frac{\phi ^{\prime }_{1}}{2} ) }{2\tan (45+\frac{\phi ^{\prime }_{1}}{2})} =\frac{200-70\tan ^{2}(45+\frac{20}{2} ) }{2\tan(45 +\frac{20}{2} )}= 20 kN/m^{2} \)
