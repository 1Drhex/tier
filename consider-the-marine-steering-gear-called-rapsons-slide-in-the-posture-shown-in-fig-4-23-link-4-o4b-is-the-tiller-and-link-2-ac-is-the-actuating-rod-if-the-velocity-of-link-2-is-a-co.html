Guest Question.

Consider the marine steering gear, called Rapson’s slide, in the posture shown in Fig. 4.23. Link 4, O4B, is the tiller, and link 2, AC, is the actuating rod. If the velocity of link 2 is a constant 15 ft/s to the left, determine the angular acceleration of the tiller and the acceleration of point B.

Admin
Solution

The vectors that are chosen for the kinematic analysis of the mechanism are shown in Fig. 4.23. Note that point D is a point of the ground link, 1, such that it lies vertically below point O4; thus, the input is the magnitude of vector R2 from point A of link 2 to point D.
Also, note that the change in the input is negative; that is, the magnitude of the input is decreasing.

With the vectors shown in Fig. 4.23, the vector loop equation for the linkage is

\(\overset{I\sqrt{}}{R_{2}} + \overset{\sqrt{}\sqrt{}}{R_{1}} + \overset{??}{R_{3}} = 0,\) (1)

where θ1 = 90°, θ2 = 180°, θ3 = 300°, and, from trigonometry, R2 = R1 tan 30° = 4.618 8 ft. The x and y components of Eq. (1) are

\(R_{2} \cos \theta_{2}+R_{1} \cos \theta_{1}+R_{3} \cos \theta_{3}=0,\) (2a)

 

\(R_{2} \sin \theta_{2}+R_{1} \sin \theta_{1}+R_{3} \sin \theta_{3}=0.\) (2b)

Differentiating Eqs. (2) with respect to the input position R2 gives

\(\cos \theta_{2}-R_{3} \sin \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}+R_{3}^{\prime} \cos \theta_{3}=0,\) (3a)

 

\(\sin \theta_{2}+R_{3} \cos \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}+R_{3}^{\prime} \sin \theta_{3}=0.\) (3b)

In matrix form, Eqs. (3) can be written as

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc}-R_{3} \sin \theta_{3} & \cos \theta_{3} \\R_{3} \cos \theta_{3} & \sin \theta_{3}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}\theta_{3}^{\prime} \\R_{3}^{\prime}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}-\cos \theta_{2} \\-\sin \theta_{2}\end{array}\right].\) (4)

 

The determinant of the coefficient matrix in Eq. (4) is

 

\(\Delta=\left|\begin{array}{cc}-R_{3} \sin \theta_{3} & \cos \theta_{3} \\R_{3} \cos \theta_{3} & \sin \theta_{3}\end{array}\right|=-R_{3} \sin ^{2} \theta_{3}-R_{3} \cos^{2} \theta_{3}=-R_{3}\) (5)

 

where R3 = (8 ft)/cos 30° = 9.237 6 ft. Therefore, Δ = −9.237 6 ft.

Differentiating Eqs. (3) with respect to the input position, R2 , gives

\(-R_{3} \cos \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime 2}-R_{3} \sin \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime \prime}-2 R_{3}^{\prime} \sin \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}+R_{3}^{\prime \prime} \cos \theta_{3}=0\) (6a)

 

\(-R_{3} \sin \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime 2}+R_{3} \cos \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime \prime}+2 R_{3}^{\prime} \cos \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}+R_{3}^{\prime \prime} \sin \theta_{3}=0\) (6b)

 

In matrix form, Eqs. (6) can be written as

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc}-R_{3} \sin \theta_{3} & \cos \theta_{3} \\R_{3} \cos \theta_{3} & \sin \theta_{3}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}\theta_{3}^{\prime \prime} \\R_{3}^{\prime \prime}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}2 R_{3}^{\prime}\sin \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}+R_{3} \cos\theta_{3}\theta_{3}^{\prime 2} \\-2 R_{3}^{\prime} \cos\theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}+R_{3} \sin \theta_{3}\theta_{3}^{\prime 2}\end{array}\right] .\) (7)

 

Note that the coefficient matrix in Eq. (7) is the same as the coefficient matrix in Eq. (4). The coefficient matrix does not change with differentiation of the unknown variables.

Substituting the known data into Eq. (4) and using Cramer’s rule, the first-order kinematic coefficients are

 

\(\theta_{3}^{\prime}=\frac{\left|\begin{array}{cc}-\cos \theta_{2} & \cos \theta_{3} \\-\sin \theta_{2} & \sin \theta_{3}\end{array}\right|}{\Delta}=\frac{\sin \left(\theta_{2}-\theta_{3}\right)}{-R_{3}}=+0.09375 rad / ft\) (8a)

 

and

 

\(R_{3}^{\prime}=\frac{\left|\begin{array}{cc}-R_{3} \sin \theta_{3} & -\cos \theta_{2} \\R_{3} \cos \theta_{3} & -\sin \theta_{2}\end{array}\right|}{\Delta}=\frac{R_{3} \cos\left(\theta_{2}-\theta_{3}\right)}{-R_{3}}=+0.500 ft / ft\) (8b)

Substituting the known data into Eqs. (7) and using Cramer’s rule, the second-order kinematic coefficients are

 

\(\theta_{3}^{\prime \prime}=\frac{\left|\begin{array}{cc}2 R_{3}^{\prime} \sin \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}+R_{3} \cos\theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime 2} & \cos \theta_{3} \\-2 R_{3}^{\prime} \cos \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}+R_{3} \sin\theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{2} & \sin \theta_{3}\end{array}\right|}{\Delta}=\frac{2 R_{3}^{\prime} \theta_{3}^{\prime}}{-R_{3}}=-0.0101 rad / ft ^{2}\) (9a)

 

and

 

\(R_{3}^{\prime \prime}=\frac{\left|\begin{array}{cc}-R_{3} \sin \theta_{3} & 2 R_{3}^{\prime} \sin \theta_{3}\theta_{3}^{\prime}+R_{3} \cos \theta_{3}\theta_{3}^{\prime 2} \\R_{3} \cos \theta_{3} & -2 R_{3}^{\prime} \cos \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}+R_{3} \sin \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime 2}\end{array}\right|}{\Delta}=\frac{-\left(R_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}\right)^{2}}{-R_{3}}=+0.0812 ft / ft^{2}\) (9b)

 

The angular velocity of link 3 is

\(\omega_{3}=\theta_{3}^{\prime} \dot{R}_{2}=(+0.09375 rad / ft )(-15 ft / s )=-1.41 rad / s.\) (10)

The negative sign indicates that the direction of the angular velocity of link 3 is clockwise. Note that \(\dot{R}_{2}=-15 ft / s,\) since link 2 is moving to the left (the input magnitude, R2, is decreasing). Also, note that link 4 is constrained to rotate with the same angular velocity as link 3. Therefore, the angular velocity of link 4 is

ω4 = ω3 = −1.41 rad/s (cw). (11)

The angular acceleration of link 3 is

\(\alpha_{3}=\theta_{3}^{\prime} \ddot{R}_{2}+\theta_{3}^{\prime \prime} \dot{R}_{2}^{2}\)

 

\(=(+0.09375 rad / ft )(0)+\left(-0.0101 rad / ft ^{2}\right)(-15 ft / s )^{2}=-2.27 rad / s ^{2}\) (12)

The negative sign indicates that link 3 is accelerating in the clockwise direction. Since link 4 is constrained to rotate with link 3, the angular acceleration of link 4 is

\(\alpha_{4}=\alpha_{3}=-2.27 rad / s ^{2}( cw ).\) (13)

The sliding velocity of link 3 with respect to link 4 is

\(V_{A_{3} / 4}=R_{3}^{\prime} \dot{R}_{2}=(+0.5 ft / ft )(-15 ft / s )=-7.5 ft / s.\) (14)

The negative sign indicates that the direction of the apparent velocity is along link 4, pointing toward the pin, O4; that is, the direction is \(V _{A_{3} / 4}=7.5 ft / s \angle 120^{\circ}.\)

The sliding acceleration of link 3 with respect to link 4 can be written as

\(A_{A_{3} / 4}=R_{3}^{\prime} \ddot{R}_{2}+R_{3}^{\prime \prime} \dot{R}_{2}^{2}\)

 

\(=(+0.5 ft / ft )(0)+\left(+0.0812 ft / ft ^{2}\right)(-15 ft / s )^{2}=+18.27 ft / s ^{2}.\) (15)

The positive sign indicates that the direction of the apparent acceleration is along link 4, pointing away from pin O4 ; that is, the direction is \(A _{A_{3} / 4}=18.27 ft / s ^{2} \angle-60^{\circ}.\)

To determine the acceleration of point B, we draw vector RB, as shown in Fig. 4.24.

The x and y components of the position of point B are

\(x_{B}=R_{B} \cos \theta_{3}=5.50 ft,\) (16a)

\(y_{B}=R_{B} \sin \theta_{3}=-9.53 ft.\) (16b)

Differentiating Eqs. (16) with respect to the input variable, R2, the first-order kinematic coefficients of point B are

\(x_{B}^{\prime}=-R_{B} \sin \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}=+0.89309 ft / ft,\) (17a)

 

\(y_{B}^{\prime}=R_{B} \cos \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}=+0.51563 ft / ft.\) (17b)

Then, differentiating Eqs. (17) with respect to the input, R2, gives the second-order kinematic coefficients of point B:

\(x_{B}^{\prime \prime}=-R_{B} \cos \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime 2}-R_{B} \sin \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime \prime}=-0.145 ft / ft ^{2},\) (18a)

 

\(y_{B}^{\prime \prime}=-R_{B} \sin \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime 2}+R_{B} \cos \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime \prime}=+0.028 ft / ft ^{2}.\) (18b)

 

The velocity of point B can be written as

\(V _{B}=\left(x_{B}^{\prime} \hat{ i }+y_{B}^{\prime} \hat{ j }\right) \dot{R}_{2}\)

 

\(=(0.89309 \hat{ i }+0.51563 \hat{ j })(-15 ft / s )=-13.40 \hat{ i }-7.73 \hat{ j } ft / s\)

 

\(=15.47 ft / s \angle 210^{\circ}.\) (19)

As a check, the velocity of point B can also be written as

\(V_{B}=\omega_{4} R_{B O_{4}}=(1.41 rad / s )(11 ft )=15.51 ft / s,\)

where the difference is a result of truncation in the value of ω4 in Eq. (10) and (11), (ω4 = 1.406 25 rad/s).

The acceleration of point B can be written as

\(A _{B}=\left(x_{B}^{\prime} \hat{ i }+y_{B}^{\prime} \hat{ j }\right) \ddot{R}_{2}+\left(x_{B}^{\prime \prime} \hat{ i }+y_{B}^{\prime \prime} \hat{ j }\right) \dot{R}_{2}^{2}\)

 

\(=(0.893 \hat{ i }+0.516 \hat{ j } ft / ft )(0)+\left(-0.145 \hat{ i }+0.028 \hat{ j } ft / ft ^{2}\right)(-15 ft / s )^{2}\)

 

\(=-32.6 \hat{ i }+6.3 \hat{ j } ft / s ^{2}=33.2 ft / s ^{2} \angle 169.1^{\circ}.\) (20)

Note that the inverse tangent function on some calculators initially indicates that the angle is −10.9°. However, this angle must be transposed into the second quadrant; that is, ∠AB = −10.9° +180° = 169.1°.
