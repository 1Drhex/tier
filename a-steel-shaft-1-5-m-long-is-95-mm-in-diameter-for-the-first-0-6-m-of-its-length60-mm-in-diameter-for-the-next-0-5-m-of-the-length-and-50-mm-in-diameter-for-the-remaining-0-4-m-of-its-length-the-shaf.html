Guest Question.

A steel shaft 1.5 m long is 95 mm in diameter for the first 0.6 m of its length, 60 mm in diameter for the next 0.5 m of the length and 50 mm in diameter for the remaining 0.4 m of its length. The shaft carries two flywheels at two ends, the first having a mass of 900 kg and 0.85 m radius of gyration located at the 95 mm diameter end and the second having a mass of 700 kg and 0.55 m radius of gyration located at the other end. Determine the location of the node and the natural frequency of free torsional vibration of the system. The modulus of rigidity of shaft material may be taken as \(80 GN / m ^{2}\).

Admin
Solution

Given :\( L=1.5 m ; d_{1}=95 mm =0.095 m ; l_{1}=0.6 m ; d_{2}=60 mm =0.06 m \text { ; }\) \(l_{2}=0.5 m ; d_{3}=50 mm =0.05 m ; l_{3}=0.4 m ; m_{ A }=900 kg ; k_{ A }=0.85 m ; m_{ B }=700 kg ; k_{ B }=0.55 m\) \(; C=80 GN / m ^{2}=80 \times 10^{9} N / m ^{2}\)

The actual shaft is shown in Fig. 24.9(a). First of all, let us find the length of the equivalent shaft, assuming its diameter as \(d_{1}=95 mm\) as shown in Fig 24.9(b).

We know that length of the equivalent shaft,

\(\begin{array}{llllllllll}l & l_{1} & l_{2} & \frac{d_{1}}{d_{2}} & l_{3} & \frac{d_{1}}{d_{3}} & 0.6 & 0.5 & \frac{0.095}{0.06}^{4} & 0.4 \frac{0.095}{0.05}\end{array}^{4}\) \(=0.6+3.14+5.21=8.95 m\)

Location of the node

Suppose the node of the equivalent shaft lies at $N$ as shown in Fig. 24.9(c).

Let \(l_{ A }\) Distance of the node from flywheel A, and

\(l_{ B }=\text { Distance of the node from flywheel } B\)

We know that mass moment of inertia of flywheel A,

\(\begin{array}{lllll}I_{ A } & m_{ A }\left(k_{ A }\right)^{2} & 900(0.85)^{2} & 650 & kg – m ^{2}\end{array}\)

and mass moment of inertia of flywheel B,

\(\begin{array}{lllll}I_{ B } & m_{ B }\left(k_{ B }\right)^{2} & 700(0.55)^{2} & 212 & kg – m ^{2}\end{array}\)

We know that \(l_{ A } \cdot I_{ A } \quad l_{ B } \cdot I_{ B } \quad \text { or } \quad l_{ A } \quad \frac{l_{ B } \cdot I_{ B }}{I_{ A }} \quad \frac{l_{ B } 212}{650} \quad 0.326 l_{ B }\)

Also , \(\begin{array}{llllllll}l_{ A } & l_{ B } & l & 8.95 m & \text { or } & 0.326 l_{ B } & l_{ B } & 8.95 & \text { or } & l_{ B }=6.75 m\end{array}\)

and \(l_{ A }=8.95-6.75=2.2 m\)

Hence the node lies at 2.2 m from flywheel A or 6.75 m from flywheel B on the equivalent shaft.

\(\therefore \) Position of node on the original shaft from flywheel $A$

\(\begin{aligned}&l_{1}\left(l_{ A } \quad l_{1}\right) \frac{d_{2}}{d_{1}}^{4}\\&\begin{array}{lll}0.6 & (2.2 & 0.6) & \frac{0.06}{0.095}^{4} & =0.855 m\end{array}\end{aligned}\)

Natural frequency of free torsional vibrations

We know that polar moment of inertia of the equivalent shaft,

\(J \quad \frac{-}{32}\left(d_{1}\right)^{4} \quad \frac{-}{32}(0.095)^{4} \quad 810^{6} m ^{4}\)

Natural frequency of free torsional vibrations,

\(f_{n} \quad f_{n A } \text { or } f_{n B }\) \(\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\frac{C . J}{l_{ A } \cdot I_{ A }}} \quad \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\frac{80 \quad 10^{9} 810^{6}}{2.2} 650}=3.37 Hz\)
