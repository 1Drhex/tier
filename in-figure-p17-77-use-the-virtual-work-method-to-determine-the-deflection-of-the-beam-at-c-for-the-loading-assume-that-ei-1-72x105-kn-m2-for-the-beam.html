Guest Question.

In Figure P17.77, use the virtual-work method to determine the deflection of the beam at C for the loading. Assume that \(E I=1.72 \times 10^{5} kN - m ^{2}\) for the beam.

Admin
Solution

Virtual Moment m: To determine the deflection of the beam at C, first remove the real loads from the beam and apply a virtual unit load downward at C. The free-body diagram of the beam with this loading is shown.

 

 

Cut a free-body diagram through the beam at section a–a. Derive a moment equation m from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{array}{rl}\Sigma M_{a-a} & =-(0.4 kN ) x_{1}+m=0 \\& \therefore m=(0.4 kN ) x_{1} & 0 \leq x_{1}<6 m\end{array}\)

 

 

A second free-body diagram through the beam at section b–b is not necessary in this instance; however, it is included here for completeness.

\(\begin{array}{rl}\Sigma M_{b-b} & =-(0.4 kN ) x_{2}+m=0 \\& \therefore m=(0.4 kN ) x_{2} & 0 \leq x_{2}<6 m\end{array}\)

 

 

Cut a third free-body diagram through the beam at section c–c. Derive a moment equation m from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{array}{rl}\Sigma M_{c-c} & =(0.6 kN ) x_{3}-m=0 \\& \therefore m=(0.6 kN ) x_{3} & 0 \leq x_{3}<4 m\end{array}\)

 

 

 

 

Real Moment M: Remove the virtual load and reapply the real loads. A free-body of the beam is shown.

 

 

Draw a free-body diagram that cuts through the beam at section a–a and derive a moment equation M from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{array}{rlr}\Sigma M_{a-a} & =-(156 kN ) x_{1}+M=0 & \\& \therefore M=(156 kN ) x_{1} & 0 \leq x_{1}<3 m\end{array}\)

 

 

Draw a second free-body diagram that cuts through the beam at section b–b and derive a moment equation M from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{b-b} &=-(156 kN ) x_{2}+(120 kN )\left(x_{2}-3 m \right)+M=0 \\& \therefore M=(156 kN ) x_{2}-(120 kN )\left(x_{2}-3 m \right) & 3 m \leq x_{2}<6 m\end{aligned}\)

 

 

Draw a third free-body diagram that cuts through the beam at section c–c and derive a moment equation M from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{array}{rl}\Sigma M_{c-c} & =(144 kN ) x_{3}-M=0 \\& \therefore M=(144 kN ) x_{3} & 0 \leq x_{3}<4 m\end{array}\)

 

Virtual-Work Equation for Beam Deflection: From Equation (17.35), the beam deflection at C can now be determined.

\(\begin{aligned}(1 kN ) \cdot \Delta_{C} &=\int_{0}^{L} m\left(\frac{M}{E I}\right) d x \\&=\frac{1}{E I} \int_{0}^{3}\left(0.4 x_{1}\right)\left(156 x_{1}\right) d x_{1}+\frac{1}{E I} \int_{3}^{6}\left(0.4 x_{2}\right)\left[156 x_{2}-120\left(x_{2}-3\right)\right] d x_{2}+\frac{1}{E I} \int_{0}^{4}\left(0.6 x_{3}\right)\left(144 x_{3}\right) d x_{3} \\&=\frac{20.8}{E I}\left[x_{1}^{3}\right]_{0}^{3}+\frac{20.8}{E I}\left[x_{2}^{3}\right]_{3}^{6}-\frac{8}{E I}\left[2\left(x_{2}-3\right)^{3}+9\left(x_{2}-3\right)^{2}\right]_{3}^{6}+\frac{28.8}{E I}\left[x_{3}^{3}\right]_{0}^{4} \\&=\frac{561.6}{E I}+\frac{3,931.2}{E I}-\frac{1,080}{E I}+\frac{1,843.2}{E I} \\&=\frac{5,256}{E I}=\frac{5,256 kN ^{2}- m ^{3}}{1.72 \times 10^{5} kN – m ^{2}}=0.03055814 kN – m \\& \therefore \Delta_{C}=30.6 mm \downarrow\end{aligned}\)
