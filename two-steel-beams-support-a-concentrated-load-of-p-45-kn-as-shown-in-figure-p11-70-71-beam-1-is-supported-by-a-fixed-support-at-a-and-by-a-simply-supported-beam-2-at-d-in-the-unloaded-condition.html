Guest Question.

Two steel beams support a concentrated load of P = 45 kN, as shown in Figure P11.70/71. Beam (1) is supported by a fixed support at A and by a simply supported beam (2) at D. In the unloaded condition, beam (1) touches but exerts no force on beam (2). The beam lengths are a = 4.0 m, b = 1.5 m, and \(L_{2}\) = 6 m. The flexural rigidities of the beams are \(E I_{1} =40,000 kN - m ^{2} \) and \(E I_{2}=14,000 kN - m ^{2}\) . Determine the deflection of beam (1) (a) at D and (b) at B.

Admin
Solution

Case 1—Cantilever Beam with Downward Concentrated Load at Tip
Remove beam (2) and consider cantilever beam (1) subjected to a concentrated load of P = 45 kN at its tip. The deflection of this beam must be determined at point D. From Appendix C, the elastic curve equation can be adapted to give the deflection of beam (1) at D:

\(v_{D}=-\frac{P a^{2}}{6 E_{1} I_{1}}(3(a+b)-a)=-\frac{P a^{2}}{6 E_{1} I_{1}}(2 a+3 b)\) (a)

Case 2—Cantilever Beam with Upward Concentrated Load at D
Beam (2) exerts an upward reaction force on the beam (1) at D. Consider beam (1) subjected to this upward reaction force \(D_{y}\). Using the appropriate beam deflection formula from Appendix C, the deflection of the cantilever beam at the point of application of the upward reaction force Dy is given \(D_{y}\):

\(v_{D}=-\frac{\left(-D_{y}\right) a^{3}}{3 E_{1} I_{1}}=\frac{D_{y} a^{3}}{3 E_{1} I_{1}}\) (b)

Case 3—Simply Supported Beam with Downward Concentrated Load at Midspan
Beam (2) supplies an upward force to beam (1) at D. Conversely, beam (1) exerts an equal magnitude force on beam (2), causing it to deflect downward. The downward deflection of beam (2) that is produced by reaction force \(D_{y}\) is given by:

\(v_{D}=-\frac{D_{y} L_{2}^{3}}{48 E_{2} I_{2}}\) (c)

Compatibility Equation
The sum of the downward deflection of beam (1) due to the concentrated load [Equation (a)] and the upward deflection produced by the reaction force supplied by beam (2) [Equation (b)] must equal the downward deflection of beam (2) [Equation (c)]. These three equations for the deflection at D are combined in a compatibility equation:

\(-\frac{P a^{2}}{6 E_{1} I_{1}}(2 a+3 b)+\frac{D_{y} a^{3}}{3 E_{1} I_{1}}=-\frac{D_{y} L_{2}^{3}}{48 E_{2} I_{2}}\) (d)

The only unknown term in this equation is the reaction force \(D_{y}\). Rearrange this equation to obtain:

\(D_{y}\left[\frac{a^{3}}{3 E_{1} I_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}^{3}}{48 E_{2} I_{2}}\right]=\frac{P a^{2}}{6 E_{1} I_{1}}(2 a+3 b)\) (e)

Before beginning the calculation, pay special attention to the distinction between those properties that apply to beam (1) (i.e., \(L_{1}, I_{1}, \text { and } E_{1}\)) and those that apply to beam (2) (i.e., \(L_{2}, I_{2}, \text { and } E_{2}\)). For instance, the flexural stiffness term EI appears in each term, but EI for beam (2) is much different than EI for beam (1).

Calculate the reaction force exerted on beam (1) using the following values:

Beam (1) Properties Beam (2) Properties

\(\begin{aligned}&P=45 kN \\&a=4.0 m \\&b=1.5 m \\&E I_{1}=40,000 kN – m ^{2}\end{aligned}\) \(\begin{aligned}&L_{2}=6 m \\&E I_{2}=14,000 kN – m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Substitute these values into Equation (e) and compute \(D_{y}=43.782 kN\).

 

(a) Determine the deflection of beam (1) at D. The deflection of beam (1) at D is equal to the deflection of beam (2) at D; therefore, calculate the deflection from:

\(v_{D}=-\frac{D_{y} L_{2}^{3}}{48 E_{2} I_{2}}=-\frac{(43.872 kN )(6 m )^{3}}{48\left(14,000 kN – m ^{2}\right)}=-0.01410 m =14.10 mm \downarrow\)

 

(b) Determine the deflection of beam (1) at B.
Consider downward deflection of cantilever beam at B due to P.
[Appendix C, Cantilever beam with concentrated load at tip.]

\(v_{B}=-\frac{P(a+b)^{3}}{3 E_{1} I_{1}}=-\frac{(45 kN )(4.0 m +1.5 m )^{3}}{3\left(40,000 kN – m ^{2}\right)}=-0.062391 m\)

 

Consider upward deflection of cantilever beam at B due to concentrated load \(D_{y}\).
[Appendix C, Cantilever beam with concentrated load at tip.]

\(\begin{aligned}v_{D} &=\frac{D_{y} a^{3}}{3 E_{1} I_{1}}=\frac{(43.872 kN )(4.0 m )^{3}}{3\left(40,000 kN – m ^{2}\right)}=0.023398 m \\\theta_{D} &=\frac{D_{y} a^{2}}{2 E_{1} I_{1}}=\frac{(43.872 kN )(4.0 m )^{2}}{2\left(40,000 kN – m ^{2}\right)}=0.00877440 rad \\v_{B} &=v_{D}+\theta_{D} b \\&=0.023398 m +(0.00877440 rad )(1.5 m )=0.036560 m\end{aligned}\)

 

Deflection at B.

\(v_{B}=-0.062391 m +0.036560 m =-0.025831 m =25.8 mm \downarrow\)
