Guest Question.

A projectile is fired horizontally from the roof of a building. A projectile is fired horizontally from the roof of a building 30.0 m high at an initial velocity of 20.0 m/s, as shown in figure 3.27. Find (a) the total time the projectile is in the air, (b) where the projectile will hit the ground, and (c) the velocity of the projectile as it hits the ground.

Admin
Solution

The x- and y-components of the velocity are

\(v_{0x} =v_0 =20.0 m/s\)

 

\(v_{0y}= 0\)

a. To find the total time that the projectile is in the air, we use equation 3.39,

\(y=v_{0y}t – \frac{1}{2}gt^2\) (3.39)

However, the initial conditions are that \(v_{0y} = 0\). Therefore,

\(y= – \frac{1}{2}gt^2\)

Solving for t,

\(t = \sqrt{-\frac{2y}{g} }\)

However, when \(t = t_t\), y = −30.0 m. Hence,

\(t_t = \sqrt{-\frac{2y}{g} }=\sqrt{- \frac{2(-30.0 m)}{9.80 m/s^2} }\)

= 2.47 s

b. To find where the projectile hits the ground, we use equation 3.38,

\( x =v_{0x}t\) (3.38)

Now the projectile hits the ground when \(t = t_t\), therefore,

\(x = v_{0x}t_t = (20.0 m/s)(2.47 s) = 49.4 m\)

The projectile hits the ground at the location y = −30.0 m and x = 49.4 m.

c. To find the velocity of the projectile at the ground we use equations 3.50, 3.51, and 3.53:

\(v_{xg }= v_x = v_{0x} \) (3.50)

\(v_{yg} = v_{0y}-g\frac{(2v_{0y})}{g} \) (3.51)

\(v_{xg }= v_{0x} = v_0 = 20.0 m/s\)

 

\(v_{yg} = v_{0y} − gt_t = 0 − (9.80 m/s^2)(2.47 s) = −24.2 m/s\) (3.53)

\(v_g =\sqrt{\left(v_{xg}\right)^2 +\left(v_{yg}\right)^2 }\)

 

\(v= \sqrt{(20.0 m/s)^2+(-24.2 m/s)^2}\)

= 31.4 m/s

The direction that the velocity vector makes with the ground is

\(\theta =\tan^{-1}\frac{v_{yg}}{v_{xg}}= \tan^{-1}\frac{-24.2 m/s}{20.0 m/s}= -50.4º\)

The velocity vector makes an angle of 50.4º below the horizontal when the projectile hits the ground.
