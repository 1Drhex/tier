Guest Question.

Find the approximate time taken for \(100\%\) consolidation in the clay layer shown in Figure 17.23.

Admin
Solution

Step 1: Time taken for consolidation is given by

\(t=\frac{H^{2}T_{v}}{c_{v}}\)

t, time taken for the consolidation process; H, thickness of the drainage layer; \(T_{v},\) time coefficient.

The approximate \(T_{v}\) value for \(100\%\) consolidation is 1.0. (Table 17.1) \(c_{v}\) is given to be \(0.011 m^{2}\)/day.

\(T_{v}\) \(U\%\) (percent consolidation)
0.00 0
0.048 10
0.090 20
0.115 30
0.207 40
0.281 50
0.371 60
0.488 70
0.652 80
0.933 90
1.0 (approximately) 100

In Figure 17.23, the clay layer can drain from top and bottom. From the top it can drain to the surface and from bottom it can drain to the sand layer below. Hence, “H” should be half the thick-ness of the clay layer.

\(H = 2m.\)

\(t=\frac{2^{2}\times 1.0}{0.011}=363\) days

We can reasonably assume that all the settlement due to clay consolidation has occurred after 363 days.
