Guest Question.

The following data refers to a steam turbine power plant employing one stage of regenerative feed heating:
State of steam entering HP stage : 10 MPa, 600ºC
State of steam entering LP stage: 2 MPa, 400ºC
Condenser pressure: 10 KPa
The correct amount of steam is bled for feed heating at exit from the HP stage. Calculate the mass of steam bled per kg of steam passing through the HP stage and the amount of heat supplied in the boiler per second for an output of 10 MW. Neglect pump work, [U.P.S.C. 1993]

Admin
Solution
\(\text{From steam tables:}\\h_{1}= 3625.3 kJ/kg, s_{1} = 6.9029 kJ/kg · K\\\text{Due to isentropic expansion, }s_{1} = s_{2} = s_{3} = 6.9029 kJ/kg · K\\\text{At state 2, i.e. at pressure of 2 MPa and entropy 6.9029 kJ/kg . K.}\\\text{By interpolating state for }s_{2}\text{ between 2 MPa, 300ºC and 2 MPa, 350ºC from steam tables,}\\\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad h_{2} = 3105.08 kJ/kg\\\text{For state 3, i.e. at pressure of 0.01 MPa entropy, }s_{3}\text{ lies in wet region as }s_{3} < s_{g}\text{ at 0.01 MPa.}\\\text{Let dryness fraction be }x_{3}\text{ at this state}\\\begin{aligned}s_{3}&= s_{\text{ƒ at 0.01 MPa}} + x_{3} · s_{\text{ƒg at 0.01 MPa}}\\6.9029&= 0.6493 + x_{3} × 7.5009\\x_{3}&= 0.834\\\text{Enthalpy at state 3, }\quad h_{3}&= h_{\text{f at 0.01 MPa}} + x_{3} · h_{\text{ƒg at 0.01 MPa}}\\&= 191.83 + (0.834 × 2392.8)\\h_{3}&= 2187.43 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\ \text{Let the mass of steam bled be }m_{b}\text{ per kg of steam from exit of HP for regenerative feed heating.}\\ \text{Considering state at exit from feed heater being saturated liquid the enthalpy at exit of feed heater will be, }h_{\text{ƒ at 2 MPa}}.\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad\quad h_{6} = h_{\text{ƒ at 2 MPa}} = 908.79 kJ/kg\\\text{For adiabatic mixing in feed heater, for one kg of steam leaving boiler, the heat balance yields,}\\\begin{aligned}(1 – m_{b}) · h_{5} + m_{b} · h_{2}&= h_{6}\\\text{While neglecting pump work,}\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad &\\h_{5}&= h_{4} = h_{\text{ƒ at 0.01 MPa}} = 191.83 kJ/kg\\\text{Substituting in heat balance on the feed heater, }&\\(1 – m_{b}) · 191.83 + m_{b} · 3105.08&= 908.79\\m_{b}&= \text{0.246 kg per kg of steam entering HP turbine}\\\text{Steam bled per kg of steam passing through HP }&\text{stage = }\textbf{0.246 kg} \\\text{Let mass of steam leaving boiler be m kg/s.}\qquad&\\\text{Output}&= 10 × 10^{3} = m(h_{1} – h_{2}) + m(1 – m_{b}) (h_{2} – h_{3})\\10 × 10^{3}&=m \left\{(3625.3 – 3105.08) + (1 – 0.246) (3105.08 – 2187.43)\right\} \\m&= 8.25 kg/s\\\text{Neglecting pump work, } \qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad h_{7}&= h_{6} = 908.79 kJ/kg\\\text{Heat supplied to boiler,}\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad Q_{7–1}&= m(h_{1} – h_{7})\\Q_{7–1}&= 8.25 (3625.3 – 908.79)\\&= 22411.208 kJ/s\\\text{Heat added}&= \textbf{22411.21 kJ/s}\end{aligned}\)
