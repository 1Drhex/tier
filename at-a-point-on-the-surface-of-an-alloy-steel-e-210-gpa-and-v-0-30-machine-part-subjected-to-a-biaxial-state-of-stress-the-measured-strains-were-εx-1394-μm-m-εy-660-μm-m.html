Guest Question.

At a point on the surface of an alloy steel (E = 210 GPa and v = 0.30) machine part subjected to a biaxial state of stress, the measured strains were \(ε_x =+1394 μm/m, ε_y =-660 μm/m\), and \( γ_{xy} = 2054 μrad\). Determine
(a) The stress components \(σ_x, σ_y\), and \(τ_{xy}\) at the point.
(b) The principal stresses and the maximum shear stress at the point. Locate the planes on which these stresses act and show the stresses on a complete sketch.

Admin
Solution

(a) The normal stresses \(σ_x\) and \(σ_y\) are obtained by using \(\sigma _x=\frac{E}{1-v^2}(\epsilon _x+v\epsilon _y)\) ,

\(\sigma _y=\frac{E}{1-v^2}(\epsilon _y+v\epsilon _x)\). Thus,

\( \sigma _x=\frac{E}{1-v^2}(\epsilon _x+v\epsilon _y)\)

 

\(=\frac{210(10^9)}{1-(0.30)^2}[1394+0.30(-660)](10^{-6})=+276.0(10^6)N/m^2=276MPa \quad T \)

 

\(\sigma _y=\frac{E}{1-v^2}(\epsilon _y+v\epsilon _x)\)

 

\(=\frac{210(10^9)}{1-(0.30)^2}[-660+0.30(1394)](10^{-6})=-55.80(10^6)N/m^2=55.8MPa \quad C \)

The shear stress \(τ_{xy}\) is obtained by using \(\tau _{xy}=G\gamma _{xy}=\frac{E}{2(1+v)}\gamma _{xy} \)

\(\tau _{yz}=G\gamma _{yz}=\frac{E}{2(1+v)}\gamma _{yz} \)

\(\tau _{zx}=G\gamma _{zx}=\frac{E}{2(1+v)}\gamma _{zx} \). Thus,

 

\(\tau _{xy}=G\gamma _{xy}=\frac{E}{2(1+v)}\gamma _{xy} \)

 

\(=\frac{210(10^9)}{2(1+0.30)}(2054)(10^{-6})=165.90(10^6)N/m^2=165.9MPa \)

(b) When the values for \(σ_x, σ_y\), and \(τ_{xy}\) are substituted in

\(\sigma _{p1,p2}=\frac{\sigma _x+\sigma _y}{2}\pm \sqrt{(\frac{\sigma _x-\sigma _y}{2} )^2+\tau _{xy}^2} \) , the principal stresses are found to be

\(\sigma _{p1,p2}=\frac{\sigma _x+\sigma _y}{2}\pm \sqrt{(\frac{\sigma _x-\sigma _y}{2} )^2+\tau _{xy}^2} \)

 

\(=\frac{276.0-55.80}{2}\pm \sqrt{(\frac{276.0+55.80}{2} )^2+(165.90)^2}=110.10\pm 234.62 \)

 

\(\sigma _{p1}=110.10+234.62=344.72MPa\cong 345MPa \quad T\)

 

\(\sigma _{p1}=110.10-234.62=-124.52MPa\cong 124.5MPa \quad C\)

A state of plane stress exists on the surface of the machine part; therefore,

\(\sigma_{p3}=\sigma_z=0\)

Since \(σ_{p1}\) and \(σ_{p2}\) have opposite signs, the maximum shearing stress is given by \(\tau _p=(\sigma _{p1}-\sigma _{p2})/2\) as

\(\tau _{max}=\tau _p=\frac{1}{2}(\sigma _{p1}-\sigma _{p2}) \)

 

\(=\frac{1}{2}(344.72+124.52)=234.62MPa\cong 235 Mpa \)

 

The in-plane principal stress directions are given by \(tan2\theta _p=\frac{2\tau _{xy}}{\sigma _x-\sigma _y}\) as

\(tan2\theta _p=\frac{2\tau _{xy}}{\sigma _x-\sigma _y} =\frac{2(165.90)}{276.0+55.80}=1.0000 \)

 

\(2θ_p=45.00° \quad\quad θ_p=22.5°\)

The required sketch is shown in Fig. 10-28. The direction of \(τ_{max} = 235 MPa\) on Fig. 10-28 is to oppose the larger of the principal stresses \(σ_{p1} = 345 MPa\) and \(σ_{p2} = 124.5 MPa\). As observed in and , the normal stress on the surface of maximum shear stress is the average of the principal stresses

\(σ_n=\frac{344.72+(-124.52)}{2}=110.1MPa\).
