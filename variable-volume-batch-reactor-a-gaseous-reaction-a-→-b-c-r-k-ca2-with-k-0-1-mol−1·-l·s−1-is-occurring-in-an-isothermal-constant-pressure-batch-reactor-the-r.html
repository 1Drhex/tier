Guest Question.

Variable volume batch reactor

A gaseous reaction

\(A \rightarrow B + C \quad r=k C_{ A }^{2}\)

with \(k=0.1 mol ^{-1} . L \cdot s ^{-1}\) is occurring in an isothermal constant pressure batch reactor. The reactor is charged initially with pure A at 1 bar and 373 K. Determine the time required for the reaction volume to expand by 50%, that is V/V0 = 1.5.

Admin
Solution

A sketch of the batch reactor is shown in Fig. E4.2. The stoichiometry of the reaction system is given by

\(A \rightarrow B + C\)

 

\(\left.t=0: \quad n_{ A 0} \quad 0 \quad 0 \quad+\right)=n_{ A 0}\)

 

\(\left.t=t \begin{array}{lllll}: & n_{ A } & n_{ B } & n_{ C } & +\end{array}\right)=n_{ T }\)

 

\(\left.\begin{array}{llll}n_{ A } & n_{ A 0}-n_{ A } & n_{ A 0}-n_{ A } & +\end{array}\right)=2 n_{ A 0}-n\)

Assume ideal gas law applies. The volume of the reaction mixture and concentration of A in the reactor are given by

\(V=\frac{n_{T} R T}{P}=\frac{2 n_{A 0}-n_{A}}{n_{A 0}} V_{0}\), (E4.2.1)

 

\(C_{A}=\frac{p_{A}}{R T}=\frac{n_{A}}{n_{T}} \frac{P}{R T}=\frac{n_{A}}{2 n_{A 0}-n_{A}} C_{A 0}\). (E4.2.2)

Based on Eq. (E4.2.1) \(V=\frac{n_{T} R T}{P}=\frac{2 n_{A 0}-n_{A}}{n_{A 0}} V_{0}\), we obtain

\(\frac{n_{A}}{n_{A 0}}=2-\frac{V}{V_{0}}\). (E4.2.3)

Thus the extent of reaction is dependent on the volume expansion. Mole balance of A in the reactor gives

\(\overset{\overset{0}{\nwarrow } }{In} – \overset{\overset{0}{ \nearrow } }{Out}+Generation=Accumulation\)

\(0-0+r_{A} V=\frac{d n_{A}}{d t}\). (E4.2.4)

Substituting the rate expression into the mole balance equation, we obtain

\(\frac{d n_{A}}{d t}=r_{A} V=-k C_{A}^{2} V=-k \frac{n_{A}^{2}}{V} .\) (E4.2.5)

Substituting Eq. (E4.2.2) \(C_{A}=\frac{p_{A}}{R T}=\frac{n_{A}}{n_{T}} \frac{P}{R T}=\frac{n_{A}}{2 n_{A 0}-n_{A}} C_{A 0}\). into Eq. (E4.2.4) \(0-0+r_{A} V=\frac{d n_{A}}{d t}\)., we obtain

\(\frac{d n_{A}}{d t}=-k C_{A 0} \frac{n_{A}^{2}}{2 n_{A 0}-n_{A}}\). (E4.2.6)

Separation of variables,

\(\frac{2 n_{A 0}-n_{A}}{n_{A}^{2}} d n_{A}=-k C_{A 0} d t.\) (E4.2.7)

Integration between t = 0 and t = t, we obtain

\(-\frac{2 n_{A 0}}{n_{A}}+\frac{2 n_{A 0}}{n_{A 0}}-\ln \frac{n_{A}}{n_{A 0}}=-k C_{A 0} t.\) (E4.2.8)

which is reduced to

\(k C_{A 0} t=2 \frac{n_{A 0}}{n_{A}}-2-\ln \frac{n_{A 0}}{n_{A}}\). (E4.2.9)

Substituting Eq. (E4.2.3) \(\frac{n_{A}}{n_{A 0}}=2-\frac{V}{V_{0}}\). into Eq. (E4.2.9) \(k C_{A 0} t=2 \frac{n_{A 0}}{n_{A}}-2-\ln \frac{n_{A 0}}{n_{A}}\). , we obtain

\(t=\frac{\frac{2}{2-V / V_{0}}-2-\ln \left\lgroup 2-\frac{V}{V_{0}}\right\rgroup }{k C_{A 0}} \text { . }\) (E4.2.10)

Thus the time required for 50% expansion of the reactor volume is given by

\(t=\frac{\frac{2}{2-V / V_{0}}-2-\ln \left\lgroup 2-\frac{V}{V_{0}} \right\rgroup }{k C_{A 0}}=\frac{\frac{2}{2-V / V_{0}}-2-\ln \left\lgroup2-\frac{V}{V_{0}}\right\rgroup }{k \frac{P}{R T}}\)

\(=\frac{\frac{2}{2-1.5}-2+\ln (2-1.5)}{0.1 \times 10^{-3} \times \frac{1 \times 10^{5}}{8.314 \times 373}} s=405.3 s\).

Therefore it takes 405.3 s for the reactor volume to expand 50%.
