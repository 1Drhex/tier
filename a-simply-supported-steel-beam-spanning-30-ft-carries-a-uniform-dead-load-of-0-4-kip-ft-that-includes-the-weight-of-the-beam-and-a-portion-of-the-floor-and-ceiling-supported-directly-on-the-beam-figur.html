Guest Question.

A simply supported steel beam spanning 30 ft carries a uniform dead load of 0.4 kip/ft that includes the weight of the beam and a portion of the floor and ceiling supported directly on the beam (Figure 7.32). The beam is also loaded at its third points by two equal concentrated loads that consist of 14.4 kips of dead load and 8.2 kips of live load. To support these loads, the designer selects a 16-in.-deep steel wide-flange beam with a modulus of elasticity E = 29,000 ksi and a moment of inertia \( I=758 in.^{4}\) . (a) Specify the required camber of the beam to compensate for the total dead load deflection and 10 percent of the live load deflection. (b) Verify that under live load only, the beam does not deflect more than \( \frac{1}{360}\) of its span length. (This provision ensures the beam will not be excessively flexible and vibrate when the live load acts.)

Admin
Solution

We first compute the required camber for dead load, using equations for deflection given by cases (a) and (c) in Appendix Table A.3.
(a) Dead load deflection produced by uniform load is

 

\( \Delta _{D1}=\frac{5wL^{4}}{384EI}=\frac{5\left ( 0.4 \right )\left ( 30 \right )^{4}\left ( 1728 \right )}{384\left ( 29000 \right )\left ( 758 \right )}=0.33 \) in.

 

Dead load deflection produced by concentrated loads is

 

\( \Delta _{D2}=\frac{Pa\left ( 3L^{2}-4a^{2} \right )}{24EI}=\frac{14.4\left ( 10 \right )\left [ 3\left ( 30 \right )^{2}-4\left ( 10 \right )^{2} \right ]\left ( 1728 \right )}{24\left ( 29000 \right )\left ( 758 \right )}\)

 

\( \Delta _{D2}=1.08 \) in.

 

Total dead load deflection, \( \Delta _{D2}=\Delta _{D1}+\Delta _{D2}=0.33+1.08=1.41\) in.

 

Live load deflection, \( \Delta _{L}=\frac{Pa\left ( 3L^{2}-4a^{2} \right )}{24EI}=\frac{8.2\left ( 10 \right )\left [ 3\left ( 30 \right )^{2}-4\left ( 10 \right )^{2} \right ]\left ( 1728 \right )}{24\left ( 29000 \right )\left ( 758 \right )}\)

 

\( \Delta _{L}=0.62 \) in.

 

Required camber \( = \Delta _{DT}+0.1\Delta _{L}=1.41+0.1\left ( 0.62 \right )=1.47\) in.

 

Since real connections are not theoretical pins and have some fixity to them, some designers use 80 percent of the theoretical beam deflections when specifying camber.

 

Camber \( = 0.8\left ( 1.47 \right ) = 1.18 in.,\) round to \( 1\frac{1}{4}\) in.

 

(b) Allowable live load deflection is

 

\( \frac{L}{360}=\frac{30\times 12}{360}=1\) in.\( > 0.62 \) in.

 

Therefore, the beam is sufficiently stiff to meet the live load deflection limit.
