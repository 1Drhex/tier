Guest Question.

Finding the high-frequency model parameters of a BJT Use the DC-biasing values of the transistor circuit in Fig. 8.20(a): \( I_{ C }=10 mA , V_{ CE }=5 V , V_{ BE }=0.7 V \text {, and } V_{ CS }=V_{ C }=10 V \). This circuit is shown in Fig. 8.53. The parameters of the transistor are as follows: \( C_{ je 0}=29.6 pF , V_{ je }=0.8 V \) for determining \( C_{ je }, C_{\mu 0}= 19.4 pF , V_{ jc }=0.8 V \) for determining \( C_{\mu}, C_{ cs 0}=3 pF , V_{ js }=0.8 V \) for determining \( C_{ cs }, \text { and } \beta_{ f }=100 \). Assume that \( V_{ T }=25.8 mV \) and that the substrate is connected to the ground. The transition frequency is \( f_{ T }=300 MHz \) at \( V_{ CE }=20 V \text { and } I_{ C }=20 mA \).

(a) Find transition time \( \tau_{ F } \).
(b) Calculate the small-signal capacitances of the high-frequency model in Fig. 8.50(a).
(c) Use PSpice/SPICE to generate the model parameters.

Admin
Solution

(a) The transition period is \( \tau_{ T }=1 / 2 \pi f_{ T }=1 /(2 \pi \times 300 MHz )=530.5 ps \). The transition frequency \( f_{ T }= 300 MHz \) is specified at \( I_{ C }=20 mA \). The transconductance \( \left.g_{ m } \text { (at } I_{ C }=20 mA \right) \) becomes

\( g_{ m }=\frac{I_{ C }}{V_{ T }}=\frac{20 mA }{25.8 mV }=775.2 mA / V \)

 

We know that

 

\( V_{ CB }=V_{ CE }-V_{ BE }=20-0.7=19.3 V \)

Equation (8.161) gives

 

\(C_{\mu}=\frac{C_{\mu 0}}{\left[1+V_{ CB } / V_{ jc }\right]^{1 / 3}}\) (8.161)

 

\(C_{\mu}=\frac{19.4 pF }{(1+19.3 / 0.8)^{1 / 3}}=6.62 pF\)

 

From Eq. (8.170) we can find the transit time \( \tau_{ F }\):

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{ T } &=\frac{1}{\omega_{ T }}=\frac{C_{\pi}+C_{\mu}}{g_{ m }}=\frac{C_{ b }+C_{ je }+C_{\mu}}{g_{ m }}=\frac{C_{ b }}{g_{ m }}+\frac{C_{ je }}{g_{ m }}+\frac{C_{\mu}}{g_{ m }} \\&=\tau_{ F }+\frac{C_{ je }}{g_{ m }}+\frac{C_{\mu}}{g_{ m }}\end{aligned}\) (8.170)

 

\(530.5 ps =\tau_{ F }+\frac{25 pF }{0.7752}+\frac{6.62 pF }{0.7752}\)

 

which gives \( \tau_{ F }= TF =489.7 ps \).

(b) We know that

\( V_{ CB }=V_{ CE }+V_{ EB }=V_{ CE }-V_{ BE }=5-0.7=4.3 V \)

From Eq. (8.159),

 

\(C_{ je }=\frac{C_{ je 0}}{\left[1-V_{ BE } / V_{ je }\right]^{1 / 3}}\) (8.159)

 

\(C_{ je }=\frac{C_{ je 0}}{\left(1-V_{ BE } / V_{ j }\right)^{1 / 3}}=\frac{29.6 pF }{(1-0.7 / 0.8)^{1 / 3}}=59.2 pF\)

 

From Eq. (8.161),

\(C_{\pi}=\frac{C_{\pi 0}}{\left(1+V_{ CB } / V_{ jc }\right)^{1 / 3}}=\frac{19.4 pF }{(1+4.3 / 0.8)^{1 / 3}}=10.47 pF\)

 

From Eq. (8.162),

 

\(C_{ cs }=\frac{C_{ cs 0}}{\left[1+V_{ CS } / V_{ js }\right]^{1 / 3}}\) (8.162)

 

\(C_{ cs }=\frac{C_{ cs 0}}{\left(1+V_{ CS } / V_{ js }\right)^{1 / 3}}=\frac{3 pF }{(1+10 / 0.8)^{1 / 3}}=1.26 pF\)

 

From Eq. (8.158),

 

\(C_{ b }=\tau_{ F } g_{ m }=\tau_{ F } \frac{I_{ C }}{V_{ T }}\) (8.158)

 

\(C_{ b }=\frac{\tau_{ F } I_{ C }}{V_{ T }}=\frac{489.7 \times 10^{-12} \times 10 mA }{25.8 mV }=189.8 pF\)

 

Then

\(C_{\pi}=C_{ b }+C_{ je }=189.8 pF +59.2 pF =249 pF\)

 

(c) Since the capacitances of a BJT depend on the junction voltages, PSpice/SPICE parameters for BJTs are specified at the zero-biased conditions. PSpice/SPICE first calculates the biasing voltages by finding the Q-point and then adjusts the values of junction capacitances accordingly. We will add the PSpice zero-biased parameters affecting the capacitances. That is, \( CJE =C_{ je 0}=29.6 pF , CJC =C_{ JC }=19.4 pF , CJS =C_{ cs }= 1.26 pF , VJE =V_{ je }=0.8 V , VJC =V_{ jc }=0.8 V , VJS =V_{ js }=0.8 V , \text { and } TF =\tau_{ F }=489.7 ps\). The model statement for the transistor Q2N2222 in is as follows for \( V_{ cs }=0 \):

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\text { MODEL Q2N2222 NPN }(B F=100 \text { IS }=3.295 E -14 \quad VA =200 CJE =29.6 pF \\&+\quad CJC =19.4 pF CJS =1.3 pF VJE =0.8 VJC =0.8 VJS =0.8 TF =489.7 ps )\end{aligned}\)

 

The results of .OP analysis, which are obtained from the output file, are as follows: (The values obtained from hand calculations are shown in parentheses; the results obtained from the PSpice model are shown in the left-hand column.)

 

\(\begin{array}{lll}GM & 3.54 E -01 & g_{ m }=0.354 A / V (0.3876 A / V ) \\RPI & 2.90 E +02 & r_{\pi}=290 \Omega(258 \Omega) \\RO & 2.24 E +04 & r_{ o }=22.4 k \Omega(20 k \Omega) \\CBE & 2.19 E -10 & C_{\pi}=219 pF (243.6 pF ) \\CBC & 1.00 E -11 & C_{\mu}=10 pF (10.47 pF ) \\CJS & 1.26 E -12 & C_{ cs }=1.26 pF (1.26 pF ) \\FT & 2.46 E +08 & f_{ T }=246 MHz (308.49 MHz )\end{array}\)

 

NOTE: There are many factors that affect the parameters of BJTs. The hand calculations are expected to give approximate values only. Even the PSpice results will differ from results of measurements on practical BJTs.
