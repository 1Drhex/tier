Guest Question.

A 12-in-long strip of steel is 18 in thick and 1 in wide, as shown in Figure 3–28. If the allowable shear stress is 11 500 psi and the shear modulus is 11.5(\(10^{6}\) ) psi, find the torque corresponding to the allowable shear stress and the angle of twist, in degrees, (a) using Equation (3–47) and (b) using Equations (3–40) and (3–41).

Figure 3–28 The cross section of a thin strip of steel subjected to a torsional moment T.

Admin
Solution

(a) The length of the median line is 1 in. From Equation (3–47),

\( T =\frac{Lc^{2} τ}{3}= \frac{(1)(1∕8)^{2} 11 500}{3}= 59.90 lbf · in\)

θ =\( θ_{1}l =\frac{τl}{Gc}= \frac{11 500(12)} {11.5(10^{6} )(1∕8)} = 0.0960 rad = 5.5° \)

A torsional spring rate kt can be expressed as T∕θ:

\( k_{t} = 59.90∕0.0960 = 624 lbf · in∕rad\)

(b) From Equation (3–40),

\( T =\frac{τ_{max}bc^{2}}{ 3+ 1.8∕(b∕c)}= \frac{11 500(1)(0.125)^{2}}{3 + 1.8∕(1∕0.125)}= 55.72 lbf · in\)

From Equation (3–41), with b∕c = 1∕0.125 = 8,

\( θ =\frac{Tl}{βbc^{3} G}=\frac{55.72(12)} {0.307(1)0.125^{3} (11.5)10^{6} }= 0.0970 rad = 5.6°\)

 

\(k_{t} = 55.72∕0.0970 = 574 lbf · in/rad\)
The cross section is not thin, where b should be greater than c by at least a factor of 10. In estimating the torque, Equation (3–47) provided a value of 7.5 percent higher than Equation (3–40), and 8.5 percent higher than the table after Equation (3–41).

 

Equation (3–40)

\(\tau_{\max }=\frac{T}{a b c^{2}} \approx \frac{T}{b c^{2}}\left(3+\frac{1.8}{b / c}\right)\)
