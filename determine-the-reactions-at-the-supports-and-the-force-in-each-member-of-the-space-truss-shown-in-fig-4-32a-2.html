Guest Question.

Determine the reactions at the supports and the force in each member of the space truss shown in \(Fig. 4.32(a)\).

Admin
Solution

Static Determinacy The truss contains \(9\) members and \(5\) joints and is supported by \(6\) reactions. Because \(m + r = 3j\) and the reactions and the members of the truss are properly arranged, it is statically determinate.

Member Projections The projections of the truss members in the \(x, y\), and \(z\) directions, as obtained from Fig. 4.32(a), as well as their lengths computed from these projections, are tabulated in Table 4.1.

Zero-Force Members It can be seen from Fig. 4.32(a) that at joint \(D\), three members, \(AD,CD\), and \(DE\), are connected. Of these members, \(AD\) and \(CD\) lie in the same \((xz)\) plane, whereas \(DE\) does not. Since no external loads or reactions are applied at the joint, member \(DE\) is a zero-force member.

\(F_{DE} = 0\) Ans.

Having identified \(DE\) as a zero-force member, we can see that since the two remaining members \(AD\) and \(CD\) are not collinear, they must also be zero-force members.

\(F_{AD} = 0\) Ans.

\(F_{CD} = 0\) Ans.

Reactions See Fig. 4.32(a).

\(\swarrow \sum{F_z}=0\)

 

\(B_z + 15 = 0\)

 

\(B_z = -15 k\)

 

\(B_z = 15 k \nearrow \) Ans.

\(+\circlearrowleft \sum{M_y} = 0\)

 

\(B_x(6) + 15(12) – 15(6) = 0\)

 

\(B_x = -15 k\)

 

\(B_x = 15 k \longrightarrow \) Ans.

\(+\longrightarrow \sum{F_x} = 0\)

 

\(-15 + C_x = 0\)

 

\(C_x = 15 k \longrightarrow\) Ans.

\(+\circlearrowleft \sum{M_x} = 0\)

 

\(-A_y(6) – B_y(6) + 25(3) + 15(12) = 0\)

 

\(A_y + B_y = 42.5\) (1)

\(+\uparrow \sum{F_y} = 0\)

 

\(A_y + B_y + C_y – 25 = 0\) (2)

By substituting Eq. (1) into Eq. (2), we obtain

\(C_y = -17.5 k\)

 

\(C_y = 17.5 k \downarrow\) Ans.

\(+\circlearrowleft \sum{M_z} = 0\)

 

\(B_y(12) – 17.5(12) – 25(6) = 0\)

 

\(B_y = 30 k \uparrow\) Ans.

By substituting \(B_y = 30\) into Eq. (1), we obtain \(A_y\).

\(A_y = 12.5 k \uparrow\) Ans.

Joint A See Fig. 4.32(b).

\(+\uparrow \sum{F_y}=0\) \(12.5 +(\frac{y_{AE}}{L_{AE}}) F_{AE} =0\)

in which the second term on the left-hand side represents the y component of \(F_{AE}\). Substituting the values of y and L for member AE from Table 4.1, we write

\(12.5 +(\frac{12}{13.75}) F_{AE} =0\)

 

\(F_{AE} = -14.32 k\)

 

\(F_{AE} = 14.32 k (C)\) Ans.

Similarly, we apply the remaining equilibrium equations:

\(+\swarrow \sum{F_z}=0\) \(-(\frac{6}{13.42}) F_{AC} +(\frac{3}{13.75})(14.32)=0\)

 

\(F_{AC} = 7.0 k (T)\)

 

\(+\longrightarrow \sum{F_x}=0\) \(F_{AB} + (\frac{12}{13.42})(7) – (\frac{6}{13.75})(14.32)=0\)

 

\(F_{AB} = 0\) Ans.

Joint B (See Fig. 4.32(c).)

\(+\longrightarrow \sum{F_x}=0\) \(-(\frac{6}{13.75})F_{BE} – 15 = 0\)

 

\(F_{BE} = -34.38 k\)

 

\(F_{BE} = 34.38 k (C)\) Ans.

\(+\swarrow \sum{F_z}=0\) \(-15 – F_{BC} + (\frac{3}{13.75})(34.38)=0\)

 

\(F_{BC} = -7.5 k\)

 

\(F_{BC} = 7.5 k (C)\) Ans.

As all the unknown forces at joint B have been determined, we will use the remaining equilibrium equation to check our computations:

\(+\uparrow \sum{F_y}=30 -(\frac{12}{13.75})(34.38) = 0\) Checks

Joint C See Fig. 4.32(d).

\(+\uparrow \sum{F_y}=0\) \(-17.5 +(\frac{12}{13.75})F_{CE} = 0\)

 

\(F_{CE} = 20.05 k (T)\) Ans.

Checking Computations At joint C (Fig. 4.32(d)),

\(+\longrightarrow \sum{F_x}=15 – (\frac{6}{13.75})(20.05) -(\frac{12}{13.42})(7)=0 \) Checks

\(+\swarrow \sum{F_z}=-7.5 + (\frac{6}{13.42})(7) -(\frac{3}{13.75})(20.05)=0 \) Checks

At joint E (Fig. 4.32(e)),

\(+\longrightarrow \sum{F_x}=\frac{6}{13.75}(14.32 – 34.38 + 20.05) = 0\) Checks

\(+\uparrow \sum{F_y}=-25 + (\frac{12}{13.75})(14.32 + 34.38 – 20.05) = 0\) Checks

\(+\swarrow \sum{F_z}=15 – (\frac{3}{13.75})(14.32 + 34.38 + 20.05) = 0\) Checks
