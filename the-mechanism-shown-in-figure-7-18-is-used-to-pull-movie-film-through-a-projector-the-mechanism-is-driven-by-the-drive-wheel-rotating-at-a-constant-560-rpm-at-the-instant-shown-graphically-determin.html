Guest Question.

The mechanism shown in Figure 7.18 is used to pull movie film through a projector. The mechanism is driven by the drive wheel rotating at a constant 560 rpm. At the instant shown, graphically determine the acceleration of the claw, which engages with the film. 1.Draw a Kinematic Diagram 2.Calculate the Acceleration Components 3.Construct an Acceleration Diagram 4.Measure the Unknown Components 5.Continue the Acceleration Diagram 6.Measure the Desired Components.

Admin
Solution

1: A scaled kinematic diagram of this mechanism is shown in Figure 7.19a. Notice that this is the basic slider-crank mechanism with a point of interest, point X, located at the claw.

The first step is to construct a velocity diagram that includes points B, C, and X. Calculate the magnitude of the velocity of point B with the following:

\(\omega =\frac{\pi }{30}(560\ rpm)=58.6\ rad/s, counterclockwise\)

 

\(V_B=\omega _2r_{AB}=258.6\ rad/s(18\ mm)=1055\ mm/s=1.055\ mm/s\ 30^\circ \angle\)

The direction of \(V_{B2}\) is perpendicular to link 2 and consistent with the direction of \(Ï‰_2\), down and to the right. Therefore, a vector can be drawn to scale from the velocity diagram origin to represent this velocity. The relative velocity equation for points B and C can be written as

\(V_C = V_B + > V_{C/B}\)

The velocity of C is constrained to translation in the vertical direction. Of course, the relative velocity of C with respect to B is perpendicular to the line that connects C and B. The velocity diagram shown in Figure 7.19b was drawn and the vector magnitudes were measured as

\(V_C=1.087\ m/s\downarrow\)

\(V_{C/B}=1.072\ m/s\ 31.5^\circ \angle\)

Because it is a general point on a floating link, the velocity of point X must be determined from solving the simultaneous vector equations.

\(V_X = V_B +>V_{X/B}\)

\(V_X = V_C +>V_{X/C}\)

The velocities of points B and C are already known and the directions of \(V_{X/B}\) and \(V_{X/C}\) are perpendicular to the lines that connect points X and B and X and C, respectively. These velocities were drawn to scale and added to the velocity polygon. The completed velocity diagram is shown in Figure 7.19c. The magnitudes of the unknown velocities were found as

\(V_{X/C}=0.625\ m/s\ 35.2^\circ \angle\)

\(V_{X/B}=1.046\ m/s\ 66.4^\circ \angle\)

2: The next step is to construct an acceleration diagram that includes points A and B and, eventually, X. Calculate the magnitudes of the known accelerations with the following:

\(A^n_B=\frac{(v_B)^2}{r_{AB}}=\frac{(1055\ mm/s)^2}{18\ mm}=61,834\ mm/s^2=61.8\ m/s^2\ 60^\circ \angle\)

(directed toward the center of rotation, point A)

\(A^t_B=0\ (because\ a_2=0)\)

\(A^n_{C/B}=\frac{(v_{C/B})^2}{r_{C/B}} =\frac{(1072\ mm/s)^2}{48\ mm}=23,941\ mm/s^2=23.9\ m/s^2\ 58.5^\circ \angle\)

(directed from C toward B measured from CAD)

Note that point C does not have normal acceleration because the motion is strictly translational.

\(A^n_{X/B}=\frac{(v_{X/B})^2}{r_{BX}}=\frac{(1046\ mm/s)^2}{45\ mm}=24,313\ mm/s^2=24.3\ m/s^2\ 23.6^\circ \angle\)

(directed from X toward B measured from CAD)

\(A^n_{X/C}=\frac{(v_{X/C})^2}{r_{CX}}=\frac{(62.5\ mm/s)^2}{28\ mm}=13,950\ mm/s^2=13.9\ m/s^2\ 54.8^\circ \angle\)

(directed from X toward C measured from CAD)

3: Understanding that there are no \(A^t_B\) and \(A^n_C\) components of acceleration, the relative acceleration equation for points B and C can be written as

\(A_C=A^t_C=A^n_B+\gt A^t_B+\gt A^n_{C/B}+\gt A^t_{C/B}\)

An acceleration diagram drawn to scale is shown in Figure 7.19d.

4: Scaling the vector magnitudes from the diagram gives the following results.

\(A^t_{C/B}=50.9\ m/s^2\ 31.5^\circ \angle\)

\(A_C=A^t_C=6.5\ m/s^2\uparrow\)

5: As with velocities, because point X is a general point on a floating link, its acceleration must be determined from solving the simultaneous vector equations.

\(A_X=A^n_B+\gt A^t_B+\gt A^n_{X/B}+\gt A^t_{X/B}\)

\(A_X=A^n_C+\gt A^t_C+\gt A^n_{X/C}+\gt A^t_{X/C}\)

As determined, the accelerations \(A^t_B and A^n_C\) are zero. Also, \(A^n_B, A^t_C, A^n_{X/B} and A^n_{X/C}\) have been determined.

Again, in a similar fashion to the velocity analysis, the two acceleration equations are superimposed onto the original acceleration polygon. The accelerations were drawn to scale, and the completed acceleration diagram is shown in Figure 7.19e.

6: The magnitudes of the unknown accelerations were measured as

\(A^t_{X/C}=31.6\ m/s^2\ 35.2^\circ \angle\)

\(A^t_{X/B}=48.1\ m/s^2\ 66.4^\circ \angle\)

and finally,

\(A_X=33.8\ m/s^2\ 0.3^\circ \angle\)
