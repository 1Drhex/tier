Guest Question.

A homogeneous plate with weight G is supported by six bars and loaded by a force F (Fig. 3.26a). Calculate the forces in the bars.

Admin
Solution

First, the free-body diagram is sketched (Fig. 3.26b). It displays the weight G (acting at the center of the plate, compare Chapter 4), the load F and the internal forces \(S_{1}\) to \(S_{6}\) in the bars (these are assumed to be tensile forces). In addition, the auxiliary angles α and β are introduced. Choosing the coordinate system such that as many moments as possible about its origin are zero, the following equilibrium conditions are obtained:

\(\sum F_{ix}=0: -S_{3}cos\beta -S_{6}cos\beta =0,\)

 

\(\sum F_{iy}=0: S_{4}cos\alpha -S_{5}cos\alpha+F =0,\)

 

\(\sum F_{iz}=0: -S_{1}-S_{2}-S_{3}sin\beta-S_{6}sin\beta -S_{4}sin\alpha -S_{5}sin\alpha -G =0\)

 

\(\sum M_{ix}^{0}=0: aS_{1}-aS_{2}+aS_{6}sin\beta -aS_{3}sin\beta =0,\)

 

\(\sum M_{iy}^{0}=0: \frac{b}{2}G+bS_{1}+bS_{2}+bS_{6}sin\beta +bS_{3}sin\beta =0,\)

 

\(\sum M_{iz}^{0}=0: bF+aS_{3}cos\beta -aS_{6}cos\beta=0.\)

 

Using the trigonometrical relations

\(cos\alpha =sin\alpha =\frac{a}{\sqrt{2a^{2}}}=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\)

 

\(cos\beta =\frac{b}{\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}}}, sin\beta =\frac{a}{\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}}}\)

 

the first and sixth equilibrium condition lead to

\(S_{3}=-S_{6}=-\frac{\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}}}{2a}F.\)

Then the fourth and fifth equations yield

\(S_{1}=-\frac{G}{4}-\frac{F}{2},\) \(S_{2}=-\frac{G}{4}+\frac{F}{2}.\)

Finally, from the second and third equations

\(S_{4}=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(\frac{G}{2}+F),\) \(S_{5}=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(\frac{G}{2}-F)\)

are obtained. As a check, it is verified that the equilibrium of moments is satisfied if an axis is chosen that is parallel to the y-axis and passes through point A:

\(\sum M_{iy}^{(A)}=-\frac{b}{2}G-bS_{4}sin\alpha -bS_{5}sin\alpha\)

 

\(=-b[\frac{G}{2}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(\frac{G}{2}+F)\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(\frac{G}{2}-F)\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}]=0\)
