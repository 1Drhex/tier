Guest Question.

On the free surface of an aluminum [E = 70 GPa; ν = 0.35] component, the strain rosette shown in Figure P13.60 was used to obtain the following normal strain data: \(\varepsilon_{a}=-300 \mu \varepsilon, \varepsilon_{b}=735 \mu \varepsilon\), and \(\varepsilon_{c}=410 \mu \varepsilon\). Determine
(a) the normal stress \(\sigma_{x}\).
(b) the normal stress \(\sigma_{y}\).
(c) the shear stress \(\tau_{x y}\).

Admin
Solution

Write three normal strain transformation equations [Eq. (13.3)], one for each strain gage, where \(\varepsilon_{n}\) is the measured normal strain. In each equation, the angle θ associated with each strain gage will be referenced from the positive x axis.

\(\begin{aligned}&-300 \mu \varepsilon=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2}\left(30^{\circ}\right)+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2}\left(30^{\circ}\right)+\gamma_{x y} \sin \left(30^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(30^{\circ}\right) (a)\\&735 \mu \varepsilon=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2}\left(90^{\circ}\right)+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2}\left(90^{\circ}\right)+\gamma_{x y} \sin \left(90^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(90^{\circ}\right) (b)\\&410 \mu \varepsilon=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2}\left(150^{\circ}\right)+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2}\left(150^{\circ}\right)+\gamma_{x y} \sin \left(150^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(150^{\circ}\right) (c)\end{aligned}\)

From Eq. (b):

\(\varepsilon_{y}=735 \mu \varepsilon\)

Using this result, solve Eqs. (a) and (c) simultaneously to obtain:

\(\varepsilon_{x}=-171.667 \mu \varepsilon \text { and } \quad \gamma_{x y}=-819.837 \mu rad\)

 

(a) From Eqs. (13.23), compute \(\sigma_{x}\):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\frac{E}{1-v^{2}}\left(\varepsilon_{x}+v \varepsilon_{y}\right)=\frac{70,000 MPa }{1-(0.35)^{2}}\left[\left(-171.667 \times 10^{-6}\right)+(0.35)\left(735 \times 10^{-6}\right)\right] \\&=6.8272 MPa =6.83 MPa ( T )\end{aligned}\)

(b) From Eqs. (13.23), compute \(\sigma_{y}\):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{y} &=\frac{E}{1-v^{2}}\left(\varepsilon_{y}+v \varepsilon_{x}\right)=\frac{70,000 MPa }{1-(0.35)^{2}}\left[\left(735 \times 10^{-6}\right)+(0.35)\left(-171.667 \times 10^{-6}\right)\right] \\&=53.8395 MPa =53.8 MPa ( T )\end{aligned}\)

(c) From Eq. (13.18), determine the shear modulus G:

\(G=\frac{E}{2(1+v)}=\frac{70,000 MPa }{2(1+0.35)}=25,925.926 MPa\)

and compute the shear stress \(\tau_{x y}\) from Eq. (13.20):

\(\tau_{x y}=G \gamma_{x y}=(25,925.926 MPa )\left(-819.8374 \times 10^{-6} rad \right)=-21.2550 MPa =-21.3 MPa\)
