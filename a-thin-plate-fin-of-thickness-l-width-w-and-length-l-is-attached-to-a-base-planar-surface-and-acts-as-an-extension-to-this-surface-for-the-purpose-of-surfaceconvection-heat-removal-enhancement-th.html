Guest Question.

A thin plate (fin) of thickness l, width \(\omega \), and length L is attached to a base planar surface and acts as an extension to this surface for the purpose of surfaceconvection heat removal enhancement. The heat is removed from the fin by surface convection at a rate \(A_{ku}q_{ku}\), where \(q_{ku}\) is assumed constant as shown in Figure. The heat is conducted,\( q_{k}\), within the fin plate and \(q_{k} = q_{k}(x)\) varies only along the x direction (a justifiable assumption for \( l\ll L\)). Derive the combined integral- (for other than the x direction) and differential-length (along the x direction) energy equation for the thin plate, for a steady-state (i.e., \({∂ }/{∂ t}=0\)) intramedium conduction and surface convection, with no energy conversion (i.e.,\(\dot{s}=0 \)).

Admin
Solution

Figure shows the mechanisms of heat transfer and the integraldifferential control volume and control surface for this problem. We only have two terms in the energy equation; these are the surface convection \(A_{ku}q_{ku} \) and the intramedium conduction \(A_{k}q_{k}\), where \(A_{ku}\) is the surface area for surface convection and \(A_{k}\) is the surface area for conduction. The combined-length energy equation is developed from \( \triangledown .q\equiv \lim _{\Delta V \xrightarrow[]{} 0 }\frac{\int_{\Delta A}{(q.s_{n})dA} }{\Delta V}=\lim _{\Delta V \xrightarrow[]{} 0}\frac{Q\mid _{\Delta A}}{\Delta V} \)

and is \( \lim _{\Delta x \xrightarrow[]{} 0 }\frac{\int_{\Delta A}{(q.s_{n})dA} }{\Delta V}=\lim _{\Delta x \xrightarrow[]{} 0}\left( \frac{\int_{\Delta A_{ku}}{(q_{ku}.s_{n})dA_{ku}}}{\Delta V} +\frac{\int_{ A_{k}}{(q_{k}.s_{n})dA_{k}}}{\Delta V} \right)=0\) energy equation

where

\( \Delta A_{ku}=2l\Delta x+2\omega \Delta x \)

 

\(A_{k}=l\omega \)

 

\(\Delta V=l\omega \Delta x \)

Substituting for these in the above energy equation, we have

\(\lim _{\Delta x \xrightarrow[]{} 0 }\left[\frac{q_{ku}(2l+2\omega )\Delta x}{l\omega \Delta x}+\frac{(q_{k}\mid _{x+\Delta x}-q_{k}\mid _{x})l\omega }{l\omega \Delta x} \right]=0 \)

 

\(\lim _{\Delta x \xrightarrow[]{} 0 }\left[\frac{q_{ku}(2l+2\omega )}{l\omega }+\frac{(q_{k}\mid _{x+\Delta x}-q_{k}\mid _{x}) }{ \Delta x} \right]=0 \)

or

\( q_{ku}\frac{2l+2\omega }{l\omega }+\frac{dq_{k}}{dx}=0 \) combined integral- and differential-length energy equation.

Note that at location x = x, \(q_{k}\) and\( s_{n}\) are in opposite directions, while at \(x = x + \Delta x\) they are in the same direction.
