Guest Question.

A fully developed flow of air at 27°C moves at 2 m/s in a 1 cm I.D. pipe. An electric resistance heater surrounds the last 20 cm of the pipe and supplies a constant heat flux to bring the air out at \(T_{b}\) = 40°C. What power input is needed to do this? What will be the wall temperature at the exit?

Admin
Solution

This is a case in which the wall heat flux is uniform along the pipe. We first must compute \(Gz_{20 cm}\) , evaluating properties at \((27+40)/2\simeq 34^\circ C\).

 

\(Gz_{20 cm} =\frac{Re_{D}Pr_{D}}{x} =\frac{\frac{(2 m/s)(0.01 m)}{16.4\times 10^{-6}m^2/s}(0.711)(0.01 m) }{0.2 m} =43.38\)

From eqn. 7.30

 

\(NU_{D}=\begin{cases}1.302 Gz^{1/3}-1 for 2\times 10^4 \leq Gz \\ 1.302 Gz^{1/3}-0.5 for 667\leq Gz \leq 2\times 10^4 \\ 4.364+0.263 Gz^{0.506}e^{-41/Gz} for 0\leq Gz \leq 667\end{cases} \)

 

, we compute NuD = 5.05, so

 

\(Tw_{exit}-T_{b}=\frac{q_{w}D}{5.05 k}\)

Notice that we still have two unknowns, \(q_{w}\) and \(T_{w}\). The bulk temperature is specified as 40°C,and \(q_{w}\) is obtained from this number by a simple energy balance:

\(q_{w}(2\pi Rx)=\rho c_{p}u_{av}(T_b-T_{entry})\pi R^2\)

so

\(q_{w}=1.159\frac{kg}{m^3}\cdot 1004\frac{J}{kg.K}\cdot 2\frac{m}{s}\cdot (40-27)^\circ C\cdot \underbrace{\frac{R}{2x} }_{1/80}=378 W /m^2\)

Then

\(Tw_{exit}=40^\circ C+\frac{(378 W/m^2)(0.01m)}{5.05(0.0266 W/m.K)}=68.1^\circ C\)
