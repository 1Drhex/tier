Guest Question.

Consider a quantum system with just three linearly independent states. Suppose the Hamiltonian, in matrix form, is

\( H =V_{0}\left(\begin{array}{ccc} (1-\epsilon) & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & \epsilon \\ 0 & \epsilon & 2 \end{array}\right) \),

where V0 is a constant, and ϵ is some small number \( (\epsilon \ll 1) \) .

(a) Write down the eigenvectors and eigenvalues of the unperturbed Hamiltonian ( ε = 0).

(b) Solve for the exact eigenvalues of H. Expand each of them as a power series in ϵ, up to second order.

(c) Use first- and second-order non-degenerate perturbation theory to find the approximate eigenvalue for the state that grows out of the nondegenerate eigenvector of \( H^0 \) . Compare the exact result, from (b).

(d) Use degenerate perturbation theory to find the first-order correction to the two initially degenerate eigenvalues. Compare the exact results.

Admin
Solution

(a) \( \chi_{1}=\left(\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right) \), eigenvalue \( V_0 \) ; \( \chi_{2}=\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right) \), eigenvalue \( V_0 \); \( \chi_{3}=\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) \), eigenvalue \( 2V_0 \).

(b) Characteristic equation: \( \operatorname{det}( H -\lambda)=\left|\begin{array}{ccc} {\left[V_{0}(1-\epsilon)-\lambda\right]} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & {\left[V_{0}-\lambda\right]} & \epsilon V_{0} \\ 0 & \epsilon V_{0} & {\left[2 V_{0}-\lambda\right]} \end{array}\right|=0 \),

\( \left[V_{0}(1-\epsilon)-\lambda\right]\left[\left(V_{0}-\lambda\right)\left(2 V_{0}-\lambda\right)-\left(\epsilon V_{0}\right)^{2}\right]=0 \Rightarrow \lambda_{1}=V_{0}(1-\epsilon) \).

\( \left(V_{0}-\lambda\right)\left(2 V_{0}-\lambda\right)-\left(\epsilon V_{0}\right)^{2}=0 \Rightarrow \lambda^{2}-3 V_{0} \lambda+\left(2 V_{0}^{2}-\epsilon^{2} V_{0}^{2}\right)=0 \Rightarrow \)

\( \lambda=\frac{3 V_{0} \pm \sqrt{9 V_{0}^{2}-4\left(2 V_{0}^{2}-\epsilon^{2} V_{0}^{2}\right)}}{2}=\frac{V_{0}}{2}\left[3 \pm \sqrt{1+4 \epsilon^{2}}\right] \approx \frac{V_{0}}{2}\left[3 \pm\left(1+2 \epsilon^{2}\right)\right] \).

\( \lambda_{2}=\frac{V_{0}}{2}\left(3-\sqrt{1+4 \epsilon^{2}}\right) \approx V_{0}\left(1-\epsilon^{2}\right) ; \quad \lambda_{3}=\frac{V_{0}}{2}\left(3+\sqrt{1+4 \epsilon^{2}}\right) \approx V_{0}\left(2+\epsilon^{2}\right) \).

(c)

\( H ^{\prime}=\epsilon V_{0}\left(\begin{array}{ccc} -1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right) ; \quad E_{3}^{1}=\left\langle\chi_{3}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \chi_{3}\right\rangle=\epsilon V_{0}\left(\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{ccc} -1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) \).

\( =\epsilon V_{0}\left(\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right)=0 \) (no first-order correction).

\( E_{3}^{2}=\sum_{m=1,2} \frac{\left|\left\langle\chi_{m}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \chi_{3}\right\rangle\right|^{2}}{E_{3}^{0}-E_{m}^{0}} ; \quad\left\langle\chi_{1}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \chi_{3}\right\rangle=\epsilon V_{0}\left(\begin{array}{lll} 1 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{ccc} -1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right)=\epsilon V_{0}\left(\begin{array}{lll} 1 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right)=0 \).

\( \left\langle\chi_{2}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \chi_{3}\right\rangle=\epsilon V_{0}\left(\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{ccc} -1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right)=\epsilon V_{0} \).

\( E_{3}^{0}-E_{2}^{0}=2 V_{0}-V_{0}=V_{0} . \quad \text { So } \quad E_{3}^{2}=\left(\epsilon V_{0}\right)^{2} / V_{0}=\epsilon^{2} V_{0} \). Through second-order, then,

\( E_{3}=E_{3}^{0}+E_{3}^{1}+E_{3}^{2}=2 V_{0}+0+\epsilon^{2} V_{0}=V_{0}\left(2+\epsilon^{2}\right) \). (same as we got for \( λ_3\) in (b)):

(d)

\( W_{a a}=\left\langle\chi_{1}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \chi_{1}\right\rangle=\epsilon V_{0}\left(\begin{array}{lll} 1 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{ccc} -1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right)=\epsilon V_{0}\left(\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right)=-\epsilon V_{0} \).

\( W_{b b}=\left\langle\chi_{2}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \chi_{2}\right\rangle=\epsilon V_{0}\left(\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{ccc} -1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right)=\epsilon V_{0}\left(\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right)=0 \).

\( W_{a b}=\left\langle\chi_{1}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \chi_{2}\right\rangle=\epsilon V_{0}\left(\begin{array}{lll} 1 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{ccc} -1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right)=\epsilon V_{0}\left(\begin{array}{lll} 1 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right)=0 \).

Plug the expressions for \( W_{a a}, W_{b b}, \text { and } W_{a b} \) into Eq. 7.33:

\( E_{\pm}^{1}=\frac{1}{2}\left[W_{a a}+W_{b b} \pm \sqrt{\left(W_{a a}-W_{b b}\right)^{2}+4\left|W_{a b}\right|^{2}}\right] \) (7.33).

\( E_{\pm}^{1}=\frac{1}{2}\left[-\epsilon V_{0}+0 \pm \sqrt{\epsilon^{2} V_{0}^{2}+0}\right]=\frac{1}{2}\left(-\epsilon V_{0} \pm \epsilon V_{0}\right)=\left\{0,-\epsilon V_{0}\right\} \).

To first-order, then, \( E_{1}=V_{0}-\epsilon V_{0}, \quad E_{2}=V_{0} \), and these are consistent (to first order in ε) with what we got in (b).
