Guest Question.

The tensile reinforcing steel bars at bottom of a column footing are of the type H16 and Grade B500B. The footing depth h is 500 mm. Assume the concrete strength class is C25/30, nominal concrete cover \( c_{nom} \) is 40 mm and bar spacing is 200 mm. Calculate:

(a) Bond stress, \( f_{bd} \)
(b) Basic anchorage length, \( I_{b,req} \)
(c) Design tension anchorage length \( I_{bd} \) for straight bars
(d) Design tension anchorage length \( I_{bd} \) for \( 90^{\circ} \)– bent bars
(e) Design lap length in tension, \( I_{0} \)

Admin
Solution

(a) Bond stress, \( f_{bd} \)

\( f_{bd} = 2.25 \eta_{1} \eta_{2} f_{ctd} \) [EN 1992-1-1 § 8.4.2 (2)]

• \( \eta_{1} = 1.0 \) for “Good” bond conditions

• \( \eta_{2} = 1.0 \) for bar \( \varnothing \leq 32 mm \)

 

\( f_{ctd} = \) design value of concrete tensile strength according to
EN 1992-1-1 §3.1.6(2)

 

\( = \frac{\alpha_{ct} f_{ctk, 0.05} }{y_{c}}, \) where \( \alpha_{ct} = 1 \) and \( y_{c} = 1.5 \)

 

\( f_{ctk, 0.05} = 0.7 f_{ctm} \) (Table 3.1 of EN 1992-1-1)

 

\( f_{ctm} = 0.3 \times f_{ck}^{2/3} \leq \) C50 60 (Table 3.1 of EN 1992-1-1)

 

\( f_{ctk, 0.05} = 0.7 \times 0.3 \times f_{ck}^{2/3} = 0.21 \times 25^{2/3} = 1.8 MPa \)

 

\( f_{ctd} = \frac{1 \times 1.8}{1.5} = 1.2 MPa \)

 

\( f_{bd} = 2.25 \times 1 \times 1 \times 1.2 = 2.7 MPa \)

 

(b) Basic anchorage length, \( I_{b,req} \)
The \( I_{b,req} \) for anchoring the force \( \sigma_{sd} A_{s} \) in a straight bar assuming constant bond stress \( f_{bd} \) is

\( I_{b,req} = \left ( \varnothing /4 \right )\left ( \sigma_{sd} / f_{bd} \right ) \) [EN 1992-1-1 §8.4.3(2)]

Where \( \sigma_{sd} \) is the design stress of the bar at the position the anchorage is measured from.
For bent bars, the length \( I_{b,req} \) and design length \( I_{bd} \) should be measured along the centre-line of the bar [EN 1992-1-1 §8.4.3(3)].

Maximum stress in the bar \( \sigma_{sd,max} = \frac{f_{yk}}{y_{s}} = \frac{500}{1.15} = 435 MPa. \)

 

\( I_{b, req} = \left ( \varnothing /4 \right ) \left ( 435 / 2.7 \right ) = 40.3 \varnothing \) (For concrete class C25/30)

 

(c) Design tension anchorage length \( I_{bd} \) for straight bars

\( I_{bd} = \alpha_{1}\alpha_{2}\alpha_{3}\alpha_{4}\alpha_{5} I_{b, req} \geq I_{b, min}\) [EN 1992-1-1 §8.4.4(1)]

 

Where:
\( \alpha_{1}, \alpha_{2}, \alpha_{3}, \alpha_{4} \) and \( \alpha_{5} \) are coefficients given in Table 8.2 of EN 1992-1-1

 

\( \alpha_{1} = 1.0 \)

 

\( \alpha_{2} = 1 – 0.15 \left ( c_{d} – \varnothing \right ) / \varnothing \)

 

\( \geq 0.7 \)

 

\( \leq 1.0 \)

 

Let \( c = c_{1} = c_{nom} = 40 mm \)

 

\( a = 200 mm; \varnothing = 16 mm \)

 

\( c_{d} = min \left ( a/2, c_{1}, c \right ) \) [EN 1992-1-1 §8.4.4(1)]

 

\( \alpha_{2} = 1 – \frac{0.15 \left ( 40 – 16 \right )}{16} = 0.775 \)

 

\( \alpha_{3} = 1 – K \lambda \)

 

\( \geq 0.7 \)

 

\( \leq 1.0 \)

 

Assume using conservative value for \( \alpha_{3} \), that is K = 0. Hence,

\( \alpha_{3} = 1 \)

Also, assume using conservative values for both \( \alpha_{4} \) and \( \alpha_{5} \), but not greater than 1.0. Hence,

\( \alpha_{4} = \alpha_{5} = 1 \)

 

\( I_{bd} = 1 \times 0.775 \times 1 \times 1 \times 1 \times 40.3 \varnothing = 31.23 \times 16 = 500 mm \)

 

\( I_{b, min} \geq max \left ( 0.3 I_{b, req}, 10 \varnothing, 100 mm \right ) \) [EN 1992-1-1 §8.4.4(1)]

 

\( I_{b, min} = 0.3 I_{b, req} = 0.3 \times 40.3 \times 16 = 193.4 mm < \left ( I_{bd} = 500 mm \right ) \)

 

\( I_{bd} = 500 mm \) (For concrete class C25/30)

 

(d) Design tension anchorage length \( I_{bd} \) for \( 90^{\circ} \) – bent bars

\( I_{bd} = \alpha_{1}\alpha_{2}\alpha_{3}\alpha_{4}\alpha_{5} I_{b, req} \geq I_{b, min} \) [EN 1992-1-1 §8.4.4(1)]

 

\( \alpha_{1} = 0.7 \) if \( c_{d} > 3 \varnothing; \) otherwise \( \alpha_{1} = 1.0 \) (Table 8.2 of EN 1992-1-1)

 

\( c_{d} = min \left ( a/2, c_{1} \right ) \) (Figure 8.6 of EN 1992-1-1)

 

Let \( c_{1} = c_{nom} = 40 mm. \)

 

\( a = 200 mm; \varnothing = 16 mm \)

 

\( c_{d} = 40 mm < 3 \varnothing \). Hence,

 

\( \alpha_{1} = 1.0 \)

 

\( \alpha_{2} = 1 – 0.15 \left ( c_{d} – 3 \varnothing \right ) /\varnothing \) (Table 8.2 of EN 1992-1-1)

 

\( \geq 0.7 \)

 

\( \leq 1.0 \)

 

\( \alpha_{2} = 1 – \frac{0.15 \left ( 40 – 3 \times 16 \right )}{16} = 1.075 > 1.0.\) Hence,

 

\( \alpha_{2} = 1 \)

 

\( \alpha_{3} = 1 – K \lambda \)

 

\( \geq 0.7 \)

 

\( \leq 1.0 \)

 

Assume using conservative value for, \( \alpha_{3} \), that is K = 0. Hence,

\( \alpha_{3} = 1 \)

Also, assume using conservative values for both \( \alpha_{4} \) and \( \alpha_{5} \), but not greater than1.0. Hence,

\( \alpha_{4} = \alpha_{5} = 1 \)

 

\( I_{bd} = 1 \times 1 \times 1 \times 1 \times 1 \times 40.3 \varnothing = 40.3 \times 16 = 645 mm \)

 

\( I_{b, min} \geq max \left ( 0.3 I_{b, req}, 10 \varnothing , 100 mm \right ) \) [EN 1992-1-1 §8.4.4(1)]

 

\( I_{b, min} = 0.3 I_{b, req} = 0.3 \times 40.3 \times 16 \)

 

\( = 193.4 mm < \left ( I_{bd} = 500 mm \right ) \)

 

\( I_{bd} = 645 mm \) (For concrete class C25/30)

 

(e) Design lap length in tension, \( I_{0} \)

\( I_{0} = \alpha_{1}\alpha_{2}\alpha_{3}\alpha_{5}\alpha_{6} I_{b, req} \geq I_{0, min} \) [EN 1992-1-1 §8.7.3(1)]

Values of \(\alpha_{1}\alpha_{2}\alpha_{3} \) and \(\alpha_{5} \) may be taken from Table 8.2 of EN 1992-1-1; however, for the calculation of \(\alpha_{3}, \sum A_{st, min} \) should be taken as \( 1.0 A_{s} \left ( \frac{\sigma_{sd}}{f_{yd}} \right ), \) with \( A_{s} = \) area of one lapped bar.

From Table 8.2 of EN 1992-1-1:

\( \alpha_{1} = 1.0 \)

 

\( \alpha_{2} = 0.775 \) [As calculated in (c)]

 

\( \alpha_{3} = 1.0 \) [Conservative value as calculated in (c)]

 

\( \alpha_{5} = 1.0 \) [Conservative value]

 

\( \alpha_{6} = \left ( \frac{\rho_{1}}{25} \right )^{0.5} \) with \( 1.5 \geq a_{6} \geq 1.0. \) The ratio \( \rho_{1} \) is the percentage of reinforcement lapped within \( 0.65 I_{0} \) from centre of the lap length considered (see Figure 8.8 of EN 1992-1-1). Values of \( \alpha_{6} \) are given in Table 8.3 of EN 1992-1-1.

Percentage of reinforcement lapped within a bar spacing of 200 mm is conservatively calculated as

\( \rho_{1} = \frac{A_{st}}{A_{c}} = \frac{2 \left ( \pi \varnothing^{2} /4 \right )}{A_{c}} = \frac{2 \left ( \pi \times 16^{2} / 4 \right )}{200 \times 500} = 0.004 = 0.4 \% < 25 \% \). Hence,

 

Table 8.3 of EN 1992-1-1 gives \( \alpha_{6} = 1.0 \)

\( I_{0, min} \geq max \left \{ 0.3 \alpha_{6} I_{b, req}; 15 \varnothing ; 200 mm \right \} \) [EN 1992-1-1 §8.7.3(1)]

 

\( \geq max \left \{ 0.3 \times 1 \times 40.3 \times 16;15 \times 16; 200 mm \right \} \)

 

\( \geq max \left \{ 193.4 mm; 240 mm; 200 mm \right \} \)

 

\( \geq 240 mm \)

 

\( I_{0} = 1 \times 0.775 \times 1 \times 1 \times 1 \times 40.3 \times 16 = 500 mm > l_{0, min} \). Hence,

 

\( I_{0} = 500 mm \) (For concrete class C25/30)
