Guest Question.

A free particle has the initial wave function

\( \Psi(x, 0)=A e^{-a|x|} \),

where A and a are positive real constants.

(a) Normalize Ψ(x,0).

(b) Find Φ(k).

(c) Construct Ψ(x,t), in the form of an integral.

(d) Discuss the limiting cases very large, and a very small).

Admin
Solution

(a)

\( 1=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}|\Psi(x, 0)|^{2} d x=2|A|^{2} \int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-2 a x} d x=\left.2|A|^{2} \frac{e^{-2 a x}}{-2 a}\right|_{0} ^{\infty}=\frac{|A|^{2}}{a} \Rightarrow A= \sqrt{a} \).

(b)

\( \phi(k)=\frac{A}{\sqrt{2 \pi}} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-a|x|} e^{-i k x} d x=\frac{A}{\sqrt{2 \pi}} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-a|x|}(\cos k x-i \sin k x) d x \).

The cosine integrand is even, and the sine is odd, so the latter vanishes and

\( \phi(k)=2 \frac{A}{\sqrt{2 \pi}} \int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-a x} \cos k x d x=\frac{A}{\sqrt{2 \pi}} \int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-a x}\left(e^{i k x}+e^{-i k x}\right) d x \).

\( =\frac{A}{\sqrt{2 \pi}} \int_{0}^{\infty}\left(e^{(i k-a) x}+e^{-(i k+a) x}\right) d x=\left.\frac{A}{\sqrt{2 \pi}}\left[\frac{e^{(i k-a) x}}{i k-a}+\frac{e^{-(i k+a) x}}{-(i k+a)}\right]\right|_{0} ^{\infty} \).

\( =\frac{A}{\sqrt{2 \pi}}\left(\frac{-1}{i k-a}+\frac{1}{i k+a}\right)=\frac{A}{\sqrt{2 \pi}} \frac{-i k-a+i k-a}{-k^{2}-a^{2}}= \sqrt{\frac{a}{2 \pi}} \frac{2 a}{k^{2}+a^{2}} \).

(c)

\( \Psi(x, t)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi}} 2 \sqrt{\frac{a^{3}}{2 \pi}} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{k^{2}+a^{2}} e^{i\left(k x-\frac{h k^{2}}{2 m} t\right)} d k =<br /> \frac{a^{3 / 2}}{\pi} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{k^{2}+a^{2}} e^{i\left(k x-\frac{h k^{2}}{2 m} t\right)} d k \).

(d) For large a, Ψ(x,0) is a sharp narrow spike whereas \( \phi(k) \cong \sqrt{2 / \pi a} \) is broad and at; position is well-de fined but momentum is ill-defined. For small a, Ψ(x,0) is a broad and at whereas \( \phi(k) \cong\left(\sqrt{2 a^{3} / \pi}\right) / k^{2} \) is a sharp narrow spike; position is ill-defined but momentum is well-defined.
