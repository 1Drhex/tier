Guest Question.

Calculation of a Double Pipe Extended-surface Gas Oil Cooler [2]. It is desired to cool 18,000 lb/hr of 28 ° API gas oil from 250 to 200 °F in double pipe exchangers consisting of 3 in. IPS shells with 1½ in. IPS inner pipes on which are mounted 24 fins ½ in. high by 0.035 in. wide (20 BWG). Water from 80 to 120°F will serve as the cooling medium. Pressure drops of 10.0 psi are allowable on both streams, and fouling factors of 0.002 for the gas oil and 0.003 for the water are required. Assuming the pipe and fins are cast iron, how many 20-ft hairpins will be required?

Admin
Solution

(1) Heat Balance:

Hot Gas Oil,

\(T_{avg}=\frac{250+200}{2}=225°F\)

C = 0.53Btu/(lb · °F) for 28° API at 225°F

Q = (18,000)(0.53)(250 – 200) = 477,000Btu/hr

Cold Water,

\(t_{avg}=\frac{80+120}{2}=100°F\)

c = 1.0Btu/(lb · °F) at 100°F

Q = (11,925)(1.0)(120 – 80) = 477,000Btu/hr

(2) Δ\(T^*\):

Δ\(T^*=LMTD=\frac{\Delta T_a-\Delta T_b}{\ln(\Delta T_a/\Delta T_b)}=\frac{130-120}{\ln (130/120)}=125°F\)

(3) \(T_{avg} and t_{avg}\): obtained in step (1).

Hot Fluid (Gas Oil) –
Annulus (a)		 Cold Fluid (Water) –
Inner Pipe (p)
(4′) Flow Area, \(a_a\)

\(d_2\) = 3.068in ⇐[Table AT.8]

\(d_1\) = 1.90in ⇐[Table AT.8]

\(z_{f,t}\) = 0.035in⇐[table AT.7]

\(a_f = N_fz_{f,t}\) L⇐for longitudinal
fins

= (24)(0.035)(0.5) = \(0.42in^2\)

\(a_a=\frac{\pi}{4}(d^2_2-d^2_1)-a_f\)

 

 

\(=\left[\frac{\pi}{4}(3.068^2-1.90^2)-0.42\right]\)

 

\(=4.14in^2\left\lgroup\frac{1\ ft^2}{144in^2} \right\rgroup\)

= 0.0287 \(ft^2\)

Wetted Perimeter*, \(z_{wp}\)

\(z_{wp}=d_1π+2N_fz_f\)

= 1.90π + 2(24)(0.5)

= 29.97 in · \(\frac{1\ ft}{12\ in}\) = 2.498 ft

Equivalent Diameter, \(D_e\)

\(D_e=\frac{4a_a}{z_{wp}}=\frac{4(0.0287)}{2.498}=0.0460\ f\)

 (4) Flow Area, \(a_p\)

D = 1.61 in\(\left\lgroup\frac{1\ ft}{12in}\right\rgroup\) ⇐ [Table AT.8]

= 0.134 ft

 

 

 

 

\(a_p=\frac{D^2\pi }{4}\)

 

\(=\frac{(0.134)^2\pi}{4}\)

= 0.0141 \(ft^2\)
(5′) Mass Velocity, \(G_a\)

\(G_a=\frac{\dot{M}}{a_a}=\frac{18,000}{0.0287}\)

= 627,000lb/(hr · \(ft^2\))

 (5) Mass Velocity, \(G_p\)

\(G_p=\frac{\dot{m}}{a_p}=\frac{11,925}{0.0141}\)

= 846,000lb/(hr · \(ft^2\))
(6′) Obtain at \(T_{avg}\)

μ = 2.50 cP at 225°F

= 2.50 cP\(\left\lgroup\frac{2.42lb/(ft\ \cdot\ hr )}{1cP}\right\rgroup\)

= 6.05lb/(ft · hr)

 (6) Obtain μ at \(t_{avg}\)

μ = 0.72 cP at 100°F

= 0.72 cP\(\left\lgroup\frac{2.42lb/(ft\ \cdot\ hr )}{1cP}\right\rgroup\)

= 1.74lb/(ft · hr)
(7′) Reynolds Number, \(Re_a\)

\(Re_a=\frac{D_eG_a}{\mu}\)

 

\(=\frac{(0.0460)(627,000)}{6.05}\)

= 4,770

 (7) Reynolds Number, \(Re_p\)

\(Re_p=\frac{DG_p}{\mu}\)

 

\(=\frac{(0.134)(846,000)}{1.74}\)

= 65,200
(8′) Obtain k at \(T_{avg}\)

k = 0.072 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F/ft at 225°F

 (8) Obtain k at \(t_{avg}\)

k = 0.36Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F/ft at 100°F

*In the derivation, the outermost edge of the fins was assumed to have zero heat transfer.

(9’) Solve for \(h_o\), \(\phi _a\), and \(t_w\) of turbulent viscous stream flowing through annulus:

\(\frac{h_oD_e}{k}=0.027\left\lgroup\frac{D_eG_a}{\mu}\right\rgroup^{0.8}\left\lgroup\frac{C\mu}{k}\right\rgroup^{1/3}\left\lgroup\frac{\mu}{\mu_w}\right\rgroup^{0.14}\) ⇐ Sieder-Tate Equation

\(h_o=0.027\left\lgroup\frac{D_eG_a}{\mu}\right\rgroup^{0.8}\left\lgroup\frac{C\mu}{k}\right\rgroup^{1/3}\left\lgroup\frac{\mu}{\mu_w}\right\rgroup^{0.14}\left\lgroup\frac{k}{D_e}\right\rgroup\)

where,

\(μ_w=μ\ at\ t_w\)

 

\(t_w=t_c+\left\lgroup\frac{h_i}{h_i+h_o}\right\rgroup(T_c-t_c)\)

\(h_o\) = 129Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F at \(t_w\) = 204.7°F ⇐ from iterative calculation

\(\phi _a=\left\lgroup\frac{\mu}{\mu_w}\right\rgroup^{0.14}=0.862\)

(9) Solve for \(h_i\) of non-viscous water stream flowing through inner pipe:

\(\frac{h_iD}{k}=0.023\left\lgroup\frac{DG_p}{\mu}\right\rgroup^{0.8}\left\lgroup\frac{C\mu}{k}\right\rgroup^{0.3}\) ⇐ Dittus-Boelter eq. for cooling fluid

\(h_i=0.023\left\lgroup\frac{DG_p}{\mu}\right\rgroup^{0.8}\left\lgroup\frac{C\mu}{k}\right\rgroup^{0.3}\left\lgroup\frac{k}{D}\right\rgroup\)

 

\(=0.023\left[\frac{(0.134)(846,000)}{1.74}\right]^{0.8}\left[\frac{(1.0)(1.74)}{0.36}\right]^{0.3}\left\lgroup\frac{0.36}{0.134} \right\rgroup\)

= 704Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F

\(\phi_p\) ≈ 1. Assume non-viscous fluid

(10) Surface area calculation of inner pipe and fins (per 1 lin. ft length of exchanger):

Fin total surface, \(A_{f,t}=\frac{2N_fz_{f,c}L}{L}=\frac{2N_f(z_f+0.5z_{f,t})L}{L}\)

\(=2N_f(z_f+0.5z_{f,t})\)

= (2)(24)[ 0.5 + (0.5)(0.035)] · \(\frac{1\ ft}{12\ in}\)

= 2.07 \(ft^2\)/Lin.ft

Bare exposed pipe surface, \(A_{be}=\frac{d_1\pi L-N_fz_{f,t}L}{L}=d_1\pi -N_fz_{f,t}\)

= [1.90π – (24)(0.035)] · \(\frac{1\ ft}{12\ in}\)

= 0.567 \(ft^2\)/lin.ft

Total outer surface, \(A_t=A_{f,total}+A_{be}=2.07+0.567=2.64\ ft^2/lin.ft\)

Total inside surface, \(A_i\) = 0.422 \(ft^2\)/lin.ft ⇐ [Table AT.8]

Pipe outer area, \(A_{i(o)}\) = 0.498 \(ft^2\)/lin.ft ⇐ [Table AT.7]

Log mean average of Pipe surface area, \(A_{LM}=\frac{A_{i{(o)}}-A_i}{\ln (A_{i(o)}/A_i)}=\frac{0.498-0.422}{\ln(0.498/0.422)}\)

= 0.459 \(ft^2\)/lin.ft

(11) Fin efficiency, \(η_f\) and \(η_{o,f}\) (for a rectangular fin):

Refer to Table 8.3.

Table 8.3 Efficiency of straight rectangular fins, triangular fins, and parabolic profile fins.
For rectangular fins:
\(Y=(4.5128)X^3-(10.079)X^2-(31.413)X+101.47\) (a)
For triangular and parabolic profile fins:
\(Y=(3.1453)X^3-(7.5664)X^2-(25.536)X+101.18\) (b)
Note:
Y = \(η_f\),%
X = \(z^{³⁄²}_{f,c}\left\lgroup\frac{h}{kA_p}\right\rgroup^{½}=\sqrt{\frac{z^3_{f,c}h}{kA_p}}\)

\(z_{f,c}=z_f+\frac{z_{f,t}}{2}=0.5+\frac{0.035}{2}=0.5175\ in=0.0431\ ft\)

\(A_p=(z_{f,c})(z_{f,t})=(0.5175)(0.0175)=0.00906\ in^2=6.29\times 10^{-5}ft^2\)

\(k_w\) = 25Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F/ft

Generate the X-coordinate (abscissa).

\(X=\sqrt{\frac{z^3_{f,c}h_o}{k_wA_p}}=\sqrt{\frac{(0.0431)^3(129)}{(25)(6.29\times 10^{-5})}}=2.56\)

Calculate \(η_f\) and \(η_{o,f}\) (employ Equation (a) in Table 8.3).

\(\eta _f\) = (4.5128) \(X^3\) – (10.079) \(X^2\) – (31.413) X + 101.47

= (4.5128)\((2.56)^3\) – (10.079)\((2.56)^2\) – (31.413)(2.56) + 101.47

= 30.7% = 0.307

\(\eta_{o,f}=1-\left\lgroup\frac{N_fA_f}{A_t}\right\rgroup(1-\eta_f)=1-\left\lgroup\frac{A_{f,t}}{A_t}\right\rgroup(1-\eta _f)\)

\(=1-\frac{2.07}{2.64}(0.307-1)\)

= 1.54

(12) Clean overall coefficient, \(U_C\) :

\(\frac{1}{U_iA_i}=\frac{1}{h_iA_i}+\frac{z_{f,t}}{k_wA_{LM}}+\frac{1}{\eta_oh_oA}\)

\(U_C=\left[\left\lgroup\frac{1}{h_iA_i}+\frac{z_{f,t}}{k_wA_{LM}}+\frac{1}{\eta_oh_oA_t}A_i\right\rgroup \right]^{-1}\)

\(=\left[\left\lgroup\frac{1}{(704)(0.422)}+\frac{0.035}{(25)(0.459)}+\frac{1}{(1.54)(129)(2.64)}(0.422)\right\rgroup \right]^{-1}\)

= 285 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F

(13) Design overall coefficient, \(U_D\):

\(\frac{1}{U_D}=\frac{1}{U_C}+R_d=\frac{1}{U_C}+R_{d,i}+R_{d,o}\)

\(U_D=\left[\frac{1}{U_C}+R_{d,i}+R_{d,o}\right]^{-1}\)

\(=\left[\frac{1}{285}+0.002+0.003\right]^{-1}\)

= 118Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F

(14) Length of exchanger, L:

Q = \(U_D(A_i\)L)Δt

Note that L is the length of the whole exchanger. \(A_i\) is in terms of \(ft^2\) per ft, so by multiplying it by L which is in terms of ft, provides the total inside surface area of exchanger.

\(L=\frac{Q}{U_DA_i\Delta t}=\frac{477,000}{(118)(0.422)(125)}=76.6\ ft\)

In order to cover 76.6 ft of piping, a total of four 20-ft hairpins will be necessary. The new length will be 80 ft total.

Pressure Drop

(1′) Obtain \(Re^{'}_a\): (1) \(Re_p\) = 65,200 from (7) above.
\(D^{'}_e=\frac{4a_a}{z_{wp}+D_2\pi}\)

\(=\frac{4(0.0287)}{2.498+\frac{3.068\pi}{12\ in./ft}}\)

= 0.0348 ft

\(Re^{'}_a=\frac{D^{'}_eG_a}{\mu}\)

 

\(=\frac{(0.0348)(627,000)}{6.05}\)

= 3610

f = 0.00037\(\frac{ft^2}{in.^2}\) at \(Re^{'}_a\) = 3610

S = 0.82 for 28° API at 225°F.

  

 

 

 

 

 

f = 0.000192\(\frac{ft^2}{in.^2}\) at \(Re_p\) = 65,200

s ≈ 1.0 ⇐ water.
(2′) Calculate Δ\(P_a\):

\(\Delta P_a=\frac{fG^2_aL}{(5.22\times 10^{10})D^{'}_eS\phi_a}\)

 

\(=\frac{(0.00037)(627,000)^2(80)}{(5.22\times 10^{10})(0.0348)(0.82)(0.86)}\)

= 9.08 psi

Δ\(P_a\) < 10 psi as the allowable ΔP

 (2) Calculate Δ\(P_p\):

\(\Delta P_p=\frac{fG^2_pL}{(5.22\times 10^{10})Ds\phi_p}\)

 

\(=\frac{(0.000192)(846,000)^2(80)}{(5.22\times 10^{10})(0.134)(1.0)(1.0)}\)
