Guest Question.

Draw the influence lines for the reactions at supports for the beam shown in Fig. \( 14.5 (\mathrm{a})\).

Admin
Solution

The beam is indeterminate to the second degree. We select the vertical reactions \(D_{y}\) and \(G_{y}\) at the roller supports \(D\) and \(G\), respectively, to be the redundants. The influence line ordinates will be evaluated at \(5 \mathrm{~m}\) intervals at points \(A\) through \(G\) shown in Fig. 14.5(a).

Influence Lines for Redundants \( \boldsymbol D_{y}\) and \( \boldsymbol G_{y} .\) The values of the redundants \(D_{y}\) and \(G_{y}\) for an arbitrary position \(X\) of the unit load can be determined by solving the compatibility equations (see Fig. 14.5(b) through (d)):

\( \begin{array}{l} f_{D X}+\bar{f}_{D D} D_{y}+\bar{f}_{D G} G_{y}=0 \\\end{array} \) (1)

\( \begin{array}{l} f_{G X}+\bar{f}_{G D} D_{y}+\bar{f}_{G G} G_{y}=0 \end{array} \) (2)

Since by Maxwell’s law, \(f_{D X}=f_{X D}\), we place the unit load at \(D\) on the primary beam (Fig. 14.5(e)) and compute the deflections at points \(A\) through \(G\) by using the beam-deflection formulas given inside the front cover of the book. Thus,

\( \begin{array}{l} f_{D A}=f_{A D}=0 \\ f_{D B}=f_{B D}=-\frac{166.667 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kN}}{E I} \\ f_{D C}=f_{C D}=-\frac{583.333 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kN}}{E I} \\ f_{D D}=-\frac{1,125 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kN}}{E I} \\ f_{D E}=f_{E D}=-\frac{1,687.5 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kN}}{E I} \\ f_{D F}=f_{F D}=-\frac{2,250 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kN}}{E I} \\ f_{D G}=f_{G D}=-\frac{2,812.5 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kN}}{E I} \end{array} \)

Similarly, the deflections \(f_{G X}=f_{X G}\) are computed by placing the unit load at \(G\) (Fig. 14.5(f)):

\( \begin{aligned} f_{G A} &=f_{A G}=0 \\ f_{G B} &=f_{B G}=-\frac{354.167 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kN}}{E I} \\ f_{G C} &=f_{C G}=-\frac{1,333.333 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kN}}{E I} \\ f_{G E} &=f_{E G}=-\frac{4,666.667 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kN}}{E I} \\ f_{G F} &=f_{F G}=-\frac{6,770.833 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kN}}{E I} \\ f_{G G} &=-\frac{9,000 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kN}}{E I} \end{aligned} \)

In these equations the negative signs indicate that these deflections are in the downward direction.

The upward deflections due to the unit values of the redundants (Fig. \(14.5(\mathrm{c})\) and (d)) are given by

\( \begin{array}{l} \bar{f}_{D D}=+\frac{1,125 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kN}}{E I} \\ \bar{f}_{D G}=\bar{f}_{G D}=+\frac{2,812.5 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kN}}{E I} \\ \bar{f}_{G G}=+\frac{9,000 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kN}}{E I} \end{array} \)

By substituting the numerical values of these flexibility coefficients into the compatibility equations (Eqs. (1) and (2)) and solving for \(D_{y}\) and \(G_{y}\), we obtain

\( \begin{aligned} D_{y} &=\frac{E I}{1,968.75}\left(-8 f_{D X}+2.5 f_{C X}\right) \\\end{aligned} \) (3)

\( \begin{aligned} G_{y} &=\frac{E I}{1,968.75}\left(2.5 f_{D X}-f_{C X}\right) \end{aligned} \) (4)

The values of the redundants \(D_{y}\) and \(G_{y}\) for each position of the unit load can now be determined by substituting the corresponding values of the deflections \(f_{D X}\) and \(f_{G X}\) into Eqs. (3) and (4). For example, the ordinates of the influence lines for \(D_{y}\) and \(G_{y}\) for the position of the unit load at \(B\) can be computed by substituting \(f_{D X}=f_{D B}=-166.667 / E I\) and \(f_{G X}=f_{G B}=-354.167 / E I\) into Eqs. (3) and (4):

\( \begin{aligned} D_{y} =\frac{E I}{1,968.75}\left[-8\left(-\frac{166.667}{E I}\right)+2.5\left(-\frac{354.167}{E I}\right)\right]&=0.228 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{kN} \uparrow \\ G_{y} =\frac{E I}{1,968.75}\left[2.5\left(-\frac{166.667}{E I}\right)+\frac{354.167}{E I}\right]&=-0.032 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{kN} \\ &=0.032 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{kN} \downarrow \end{aligned} \)

The remaining ordinates of the influence lines for the redundants are computed in a similar manner. These ordinates are tabulated in Table 14.3, and the influence lines for \(D_{y}\) and \(G_{y}\) are shown in Fig. 14.5(g) and (h), respectively.

Influence Lines for \( \boldsymbol A_{y}\) and \( \boldsymbol M_{A}\). The ordinates of the influence lines for the remaining reactions can now be determined by placing the unit load successively at points \(A\) through \(G\) on the indeterminate beam and by applying the equations of equilibrium. For example, for the position of the unit load at \(B\) (Fig. 14.5(i)), the values of the reactions \(D_{y}\) and \(G_{y}\) have been found to be \(0.228 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{kN}\) and \(-0.032 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{kN}\), respectively. By considering the equilibrium of the beam, we determine the values of the reactions \(A_{y}\) and \(M_{A}\) to be as follows:

\(\begin{aligned}+\uparrow \sum F_{y}=0 \quad A_{y}-1+0.228-0.032 &=0 \\ A_{y} &=0.804 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{kN} \uparrow \end{aligned}\)

\( \begin{aligned} +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{A}=0 \quad M_{A}-1(5)+0.228(15)-0.032(30) &=0 \\ M_{A} &=2.54 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} / \mathrm{kN} \curvearrowleft \end{aligned} \)

The values of the remaining influence line ordinates are computed in a similar manner. These ordinates are listed in Table \(14.3\), and the influence lines for \(A_{y}\) and \(M_{A}\) are shown in Fig. 14.5(j) and (k), respectively.
