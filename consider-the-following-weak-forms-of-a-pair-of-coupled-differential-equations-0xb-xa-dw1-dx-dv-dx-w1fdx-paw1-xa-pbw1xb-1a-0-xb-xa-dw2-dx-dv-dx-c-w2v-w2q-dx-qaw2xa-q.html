Guest Question.

Consider the following weak forms of a pair of coupled differential equations:
\(0=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\left ( \frac{\mathrm{d} w_{1}}{\mathrm{d} x}\frac{\mathrm{d} v}{\mathrm{d} x}-w_{1} f\right )dx-P_{a}w_{1}(x_{a})-P_{b}w_{1}(x_{b})\) (1a)
\(0=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\left ( \frac{\mathrm{d} w_{2}}{\mathrm{d} x}\frac{\mathrm{d} u}{\mathrm{d} x}+c w_{2} v -w_{2}q\right )dx -Q_{a}w_{2}(x_{a})-Q_{b}w_{2}(x_{b})\) (1b)
where c(x) is a known function, \(w_{1}\) and \(w_{2}\) are weight functions, u and v are dependent variables (primary variables), and \(P_{a},P_{b},Q_{a}\), and \(Q_{b}\) are the secondary variables of the formulation. Use the finite element approximations of the form
\(u(x)=\sum_{j=1}^{m}u_{j}^{e}\psi _{j}^{e}(x)\) , \(v(x)=\sum_{j=1}^{n}v_{j}^{e}\varphi _{j}^{e}(x)\) (2)
and \(w_{1}=\psi _{i}\) and \(w_{2}=\varphi _{i}\) and derive the finite element equations from the weak forms. The finite element equations should be in the form
\(0=\sum_{j=1}^{m}K_{ij}^{11}u_{j}^{e}+\sum_{j=1}^{n}K_{ij}^{12}v_{j}^{e}-F_{i}^{1}\) (3a)
\(0=\sum_{j=1}^{m}K_{ij}^{21}u_{j}^{e}+\sum_{j=1}^{n}K_{ij}^{22}v_{j}^{e}-F_{i}^{2}\) (3b)
Define the coefficients \(K_{ij}^{11},K_{ij}^{12},K_{ij}^{21},K_{ij}^{22},F_{i}^{1}\) and \(F_{i}^{2}\) in terms of the interpolation functions, known data, and secondary variables.
For this Problem carry out the following tasks:
(a) Develop the weak forms of the given differential equation(s) over a typical finite element, which is a geometric subdomain located between x = \(x_{a}\) and x = \(x_{b}\). Note that there are no “specified” boundary conditions at the element level. Therefore, in going from Step 2 to Step 3 of the weak-form development, one must identify the secondary variable(s) at the two ends of the domain by some symbols (like \(Q_{1}^{e}\) and \(Q_{2}^{e}\)) and complete the weak form.
(b) Assume an approximation(s) of the form
\(u(x)=\sum_{j=1}^{n}u_{j}^{e}\psi _{j}^{e}(x)\) (i)
where u is a primary variable of the formulation and \(\psi _{j}^{e}(x)\) are the interpolation functions, and \(u _{j}^{e}\) are the values of the primary variable(s) at the jth node of the element. Substitute the expression in (i) for the primary variable and \(\psi _{i}^{e}\) for the weight function into the weak form(s) and derive the finite element model. Be sure to define all coefficients of the model in terms of the problem data and \(\psi _{i}^{e}\).

Solution

Substitution of the finite element approximation (2) into the weak forms gives

\(0=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\left [ \frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{i}}{\mathrm{d} x}\left ( \sum_{j=1}^{n} v_{j}^{e}\frac{\mathrm{d} \varphi _{j}}{\mathrm{d} x}\right )-\psi _{i}f \right ]dx – P_{a}\psi _{i}(x_{a})-P_{b}\psi _{i}(x_{b})\)

\(=\sum_{j=1}^{n}A_{ij}^{e}v_{j}^{e}-F_{i}^{e}\) (4a)

where

\(A_{ij}^{e}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{i}}{\mathrm{d} x}\frac{\mathrm{d} \varphi _{j}}{\mathrm{d} x}dx\) , \(F_{i}^{e}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\psi _{i}f dx + P_{a}\psi _{i}(x_{a})+P_{b}\psi _{i}(x_{b})\) (4b)

and

\(0=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\left [ \frac{\mathrm{d} \varphi _{i}}{\mathrm{d} x} \left ( \sum_{j=1}^{m}u_{j}^{e}\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{j}}{\mathrm{d} x} \right )+c\varphi _{i}\left ( \sum_{j=1}^{n}v_{j}^{e}\varphi _{j} \right )-q\varphi _{i}\right ]dx\)

–\(Q_{a}\varphi _{i}(x_{a})-Q_{b}\varphi _{i}(x_{b})\)

\(=\sum_{j=1}^{m}B_{ij}^{e}u_{j}^{e}+\sum_{j=1}^{n}C_{ij}^{e}v_{j}^{e}-G_{i}^{e}\) (5a)

where

\(B_{ij}^{e}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\frac{\mathrm{d} \varphi _{i}}{\mathrm{d} x}\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{i}}{\mathrm{d} x}dx =A_{ji}^{e}\)

\(C_{ij}^{e}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}c\varphi _{i}\varphi _{j}dx\) (5b)

\(G_{i}^{e}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}q\varphi _{i}dx+Q_{a}\varphi _{i}(x_{a})+Q_{b}\varphi _{i}(x_{b})\)

Comparing with the given expressions in (3a,b), it is clear that

\(K_{ij}^{11}=0\) , \(A_{ij}^{e}=K_{ij}^{12}\) , \(B_{ij}^{e}=K_{ij}^{21}\) , \(C_{ij}^{e}=K_{ij}^{22}\) , \(F_{i}^{e}=F_{i}^{1}\) , \(G_{i}^{e}=F_{i}^{1}\) (6)
