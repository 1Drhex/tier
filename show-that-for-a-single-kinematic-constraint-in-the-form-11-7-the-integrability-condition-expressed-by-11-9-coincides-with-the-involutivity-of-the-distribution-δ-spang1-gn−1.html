Guest Question.

Show that, for a single kinematic constraint in the form (11.7), the integrability condition expressed by (11.9) coincides with the involutivity of the distribution Δ = span{ \( g_{1}\) , … , \(g_{n - 1}\) }associated with the input vector fields of the corresponding kinematic model.

\(a^{T} \left(q\right) \dot{q}\) = \(\sum\limits_{j = 1}^{n}{a_{j}\left(q\right)\dot{qa}_{j} }\) = 0 (11.7)

\(\frac{\partial \left(\gamma a_{k}\right) }{\partial q_{j} }\) = \(\frac{\partial \left(\gamma a_{j}\right) }{\partial q_{k} }\) j, k = 1, . . . , n, j ≠ k, (11.9)

Admin
Solution

Consider a single Pfaffian constraint

\( a^{T} \left(q\right) \dot{q} \) = \(\sum\limits_{j = 1}^{n}{a_{j}\left(q\right)\dot{q}_{j} } \) = 0

The corresponding kinematic model is

\(\dot{q}\) = \( \sum\limits_{j = 1}^{n – 1}{g_{j}\left(q\right) u_{j} } \)

where \( \left\{g_{1} \left(q\right), . . . g_{n – 1} \left(q\right)\right\} \) is a basis of \( N \left(a^{T} \left(q\right)\right) \). Without loss of generality, assume that \(a_{1} \left(q\right) \) ≠ 0, and consider the following choice of input vector fields

\( g_{1} \) = \(\left[\begin{matrix} -\gamma \left(q\right) a_{2}\left(q\right) \\ \gamma \left(q\right) a_{2}\left(q\right) \\ 0\\ 0\\ \vdots \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right] \) \( g_{2} \) = \(\left[\begin{matrix} -\gamma \left(q\right) a_{3}\left(q\right) \\ 0 \\ \gamma \left(q\right) a_{1}\left(q\right) \\ 0\\ \vdots \\ 0\\ 0\end{matrix} \right] \) \( g_{n – 1} \) = \(\left[\begin{matrix} -\gamma \left(q\right) a_{n}\left(q\right) \\0 \\ 0\\ 0\\ \vdots \\ 0\\ \gamma \left(q\right) a_{1}\left(q\right) \end{matrix} \right] \)

where \(\gamma \left(q\right) \) ≠ 0 The distribution Δ = span{ \( g_{ 1} \), …, \( g_{n – 1} \) } Lie bracket \( \left[g_{i} , g_{j}\right]\) can be written as a linear combination of \( g_{ 1} \) , ……\( g_{ n – 1} \) For example, consider \( \left[g_{1} , g_{2}\right]\) A simple computation gives

\( \left[g_{1} , g_{2}\right]\) = \( \left [ \begin{matrix} \frac{\partial \left(\gamma a_{2}\right) }{\partial q_{1}} \gamma a_{3} – \frac{\partial \left(\gamma a_{3}\right) }{\partial q_{1}} \gamma a_{2} + \left(\frac{\partial \left(\gamma a_{3}\right) }{\partial q_{2}} – \frac{\partial \left(\gamma a_{2}\right) }{\partial q_{3}} \right)\gamma a_{1} \\ \frac{\partial \left(\gamma a_{1}\right) }{\partial q_{3}} \gamma a_{1} – \frac{\partial \left(\gamma a_{1}\right) }{\partial q_{1}} \gamma a_{3}\\ \frac{\partial \left(\gamma a_{1}\right) }{\partial q_{1}} \gamma a_{2} – \frac{\partial \left(\gamma a_{1}\right) }{\partial q_{2}} \gamma a_{1}\\ 0 \\ \vdots \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Using the integrability condition expressed by (11.9), one easily verifies that
\( \left[g_{1} , g_{2}\right]\) = \( \frac{\partial \left(\gamma a_{3}\right) }{\partial q_{1}} g_{1} – \frac{\partial \left(\gamma a_{3}\right) }{\partial q_{1}} g_{2} + \frac{\partial \left(\gamma a_{1}\right) }{\partial q_{1}} \frac{a_{3} g_{1} + a_{2} g_{2} }{a_{1}}\)

i.e., \( \left[g_{1} , g_{2}\right]\) is a linear combination of \( g_{1} \) , \( g_{2}\)

A similar construction can be repeated for any Lie bracket \( \left[g_{i} , g_{j}\right]\) ; hence, the distribution Δ is involutive under the integrability condition (11.9).
