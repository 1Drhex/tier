LOCAL ISENTROPIC STAGNATION CONDITIONS IN CHANNEL FLOW

Air flows steadily through the duct shown from 350 kPa (abs), 60째C, and 183 m/s at the inlet state to \(M = 1.3\) at the outlet, where local isentropic stagnation conditions are known to be 385 kPa (abs) and 350 K. Compute the local isentropic stagnation pressure and temperature at the inlet and the static pressure and temperature at the duct outlet. Locate the inlet and outlet static state points on a \(Ts\) diagram, and indicate the stagnation processes.

Admin
Solution

Given: Steady flow of air through a duct as shown in the sketch.

Find:

(a) \(p_{0_{1}}\).
(b) \(T_{0_{1}}\).
(c) \(p_{2}\).
(d) \(T_{2}\).
(e) State points (1) and (2) on a Ts diagram; indicate the stagnation processes.
Solution: To evaluate local isentropic stagnation conditions at section (1), we must calculate the Mach number, \(M_{1}=V_{1} / c_{1}\). For an ideal gas, \(c=\sqrt{k R T}\). Then

\(c_{1}=\sqrt{k R T_{1}}=\left[1.4 \times 287 \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}} \times(273+60) \mathrm{K} \times \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}\right]^{1 / 2}=366 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\)

 

and

\(M_{1}=\frac{V_{1}}{c_{1}}=\frac{183}{366}=0.5\)

 

Local isentropic stagnation properties can be evaluated from Eqs. 12.21. Thus

\(\begin{aligned}&\frac{p_{0}}{p}=\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\right]^{k /(k-1)} \\&\frac{T_{0}}{T}=1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2} \\&\frac{\rho_{0}}{\rho}=\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\right]^{1 /(k-1)}\end{aligned}\) (12.21 a,b,c)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&p_{0_{1}}=p_{1}\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{1}^{2}\right]^{k(k-1)}=350 \mathrm{kPa}\left[1+0.2(0.5)^{2}\right]^{3.5}=415 \mathrm{kPa}(\mathrm{abs})\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad p_{0_{1}}} \\\quad\\&T_{0_{1}}=T_{1}\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{1}^{2}\right]=333 \mathrm{~K}\left[1+0.2(0.5)^{2}\right]=350 \mathrm{~K}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad T_{0_{1}}}\end{aligned}\)

 

 

At section (2), Eqs. 12.21 can be applied again. Thus from Eq. 12.21 a,

\(p_{2}=\frac{p_{0_{2}}}{\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{2}^{2}\right]^{k /(k-1)}}=\frac{385 \mathrm{kPa}}{\left[1+0.2(1.3)^{2}\right]^{3.5}}=139 \mathrm{kPa}(\mathrm{abs})\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad {p_{2}}}\)

From Eq. 12.21b,

\(T_{2}=\frac{T_{0_{2}}}{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{2}^{2}}=\frac{350 \mathrm{~K}}{1+0.2(1.3)^{2}}=262 \mathrm{~K} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad {T_{2}}}\)

 

To locate states (1) and (2) in relation to one another, and sketch the stagnation processes on the Ts diagram, we need to find the change in entropy \(s_{2}-s_{1}\). At each state we have \(p\) and \(T\), so it is convenient to use Eq. 12.11 b,

\(\begin{aligned}s_{2}-s_{1} &=c_{p} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-R \ln \frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}} \\\quad\\&=1.00 \frac{\mathrm{kJ}}{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}} \times \ln \left(\frac{262}{333}\right)-0.287 \frac{\mathrm{kJ}}{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}} \times \ln \left(\frac{139}{350}\right) \\\quad\\s_{2}-s_{1} &=0.0252 \mathrm{~kJ} /(\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K})\end{aligned}\)

 

Hence in this flow we have an increase in entropy. Perhaps there is irreversibility (e.g., friction), or heat is being added, or both. (We will see in Chapter 13 that the fact that \(T_{0_{1}}=T_{0_{2}}\) for this particular flow means that actually we

 

have an adiabatic flow.) We also found that \(T_2 < T_1\) and that \(p_2 < p_1\). We can now sketch the Ts diagram (and recall we saw in that isobars (lines of constant pressure) in \(Ts\) space are exponential),

This problem illustrates use of the local isentropic stagnation properties (Eqs. 12.21) to relate different points in a flow.

The Excel workbook Isentropic Relations, available on the Web site, can be used for computing property ratios from the Mach number \(M\), as well as for computing \(M\) from property ratios.
