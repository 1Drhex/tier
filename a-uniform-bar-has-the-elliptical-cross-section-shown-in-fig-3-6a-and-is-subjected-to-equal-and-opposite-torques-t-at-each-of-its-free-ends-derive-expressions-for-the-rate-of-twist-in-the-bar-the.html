Guest Question.

A uniform bar has the elliptical cross-section shown in Fig. 3.6(a) and is subjected to equal and opposite torques T at each of its free ends. Derive expressions for the rate of twist in the bar, the shear stress distribution, and the warping displacement of its cross-section.

Calculate the warping displacement (w) along the boundary of the cross-section illustrated in Fig. 3.6(a) using the equation for w derived Plot the resulting values of w using the plot3 function in MATLAB. Assume the following variable values: \(a =200 mm ; b =300 mm ; G =25,000 N / mm ^{2} ; T =15 kNm \text { . }\)

Admin
Solution

The semi-major and semi-minor axes are a and b, respectively, so that the equation of its boundary is

\(\frac{x^{2}}{a^{2}}+\frac{y^{2}}{b^{2}}=1\)

 

If we choose a stress function of the form

\(\phi=C\left(\frac{x^{2}}{a^{2}}+\frac{y^{2}}{b^{2}}-1\right)\) (i)

then the boundary condition \(\phi=0\) is satisfied at every point on the boundary and the constant C may be chosen to fulfill the remaining requirement of compatibility. Thus, from Eqs. (3.11) and (i),

\(-2 G \frac{ d \theta}{ d z}=\nabla^{2} \phi=F(\text { constant })\) (3.11)

\(2 C\left(\frac{1}{a^{2}}+\frac{1}{b^{2}}\right)=-2 G \frac{ d \theta}{ d z}\)

 

or

\(C=-G \frac{ d \theta}{ d z} \frac{a^{2} b^{2}}{\left(a^{2}+b^{2}\right)}\) (ii)

giving

\(\phi=-G \frac{ d \theta}{ d z} \frac{a^{2} b^{2}}{\left(a^{2}+b^{2}\right)}\left(\frac{x^{2}}{a^{2}}+\frac{y^{2}}{b^{2}}-1\right)\) (iii)

Substituting this expression for \(\phi\) in Eq. (3.8) establishes the relationship between the torque T and the rate of twist:

\(T=2\iint{\phi dx dy} \) (3.8)

\(T=-2 G \frac{ d \theta}{ d z} \frac{a^{2} b^{2}}{\left(a^{2}+b^{2}\right)}\left(\frac{1}{a^{2}} \iint x^{2} d x d y+\frac{1}{b^{2}} \iint y^{2} d x d y-\iint d x d y\right)\)

 

The first and second integrals in this equation are the second moments of area \(I_{y y}=\pi a^{3} b / 4\) and \(I_{x x}=\pi a b^{3} / 4\), while the third integral is the area of the cross-section \(A=\pi a b\). Replacing the integrals by these values gives

\(T=G \frac{ d \theta}{ d z} \frac{\pi a^{3} b^{3}}{\left(a^{2}+b^{2}\right)}\) (iv)

from which (see Eq. (3.12))

\(T=G J \frac{ d \theta}{ d z}\) (3.12)

\(J=\frac{\pi a^{3} b^{3}}{\left(a^{2}+b^{2}\right)}\) (v)

The shear stress distribution is obtained in terms of the torque by substituting for the product \(G( d \theta / d z)\) in Eq. (iii) from (iv) and then differentiating as indicated by the relationships of Eqs. (3.2). Thus,

\(\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial x}=-\tau_{z y}, \quad \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial y}=\tau_{z x}\) (3.2)

\(\tau_{z x}=-\frac{2 T y}{\pi a b^{3}}, \quad \tau_{z y}=\frac{2 T x}{\pi a^{3} b}\) (vi)

So far we have solved for the stress distribution, Eqs. (vi), and the rate of twist, Eq. (iv). It remains to determine the warping distribution w over the cross-section. For this, we return to Eqs. (3.10), which become, on substituting from the preceding for \(\tau_{z x}, \tau_{z y}\), and \(d \theta / d z\),

\(\frac{\partial w}{\partial x}=\frac{\tau_{z x}}{G}+\frac{ d \theta}{ d z} y, \quad \frac{\partial w}{\partial y}=\frac{\tau_{z y}}{G}-\frac{ d \theta}{ dz } x\) (3.10)

\(\frac{\partial w}{\partial x}=-\frac{2 T y}{\pi a b^{3} G}+\frac{T}{G} \frac{\left(a^{2}+b^{2}\right)}{\pi a^{3} b^{3}} y, \quad \frac{\partial w}{\partial y}=\frac{2 T x}{\pi a^{3} b G}-\frac{T}{G} \frac{\left(a^{2}+b^{2}\right)}{\pi a^{3} b^{3}} x\)

 

or

\(\frac{\partial w}{\partial x}=\frac{T}{\pi a^{3} b^{3} G}\left(b^{2}-a^{2}\right) y, \quad \frac{\partial w}{\partial y}=\frac{T}{\pi a^{3} b^{3} G}\left(b^{2}-a^{2}\right) x\) (vii)

Integrating both of Eqs. (vii),

\(w=\frac{T\left(b^{2}-a^{2}\right)}{\pi a^{3} b^{3} G} y x+f_{1}(y) \quad w=\frac{T\left(b^{2}-a^{2}\right)}{\pi a^{3} b^{3} G} x y+f_{2}(x)\)

 

The warping displacement given by each of these equations must have the same value at identical points (x, y). It follows that \(f_{1}(y)=f_{2}(x)=0\). Hence,

\(w=\frac{T\left(b^{2}-a^{2}\right)}{\pi a^{3} b^{3} G} x y\) (viii)

Lines of constant w therefore describe hyperbolas with the major and minor axes of the elliptical cross-section as asymptotes. Further, for a positive (counterclockwise) torque, the warping is negative in the first and third quadrants (a > b) and positive in the second and fourth.
