Guest Question.

Consider the problem of finding the fundamental frequency of a circular membrane of radius a, fixed at its edge. The governing equation for axisymmetric vibration is
\(-\frac{1}{r}\frac{\mathrm{d} }{\mathrm{d} r}-\lambda u=0\) \(0< r< a\)
where \(\lambda \) is the frequency parameter and u is the deflection of the membrane. (a) Determine the trigonometric approximation functions for the Galerkin method, (b) use one-parameter Galerkin approximation to determine \(\lambda \), and (c) use two-parameter Galerkin approximation to determine \(\lambda \).

Admin
Solution

(a) The approximation functions that satisfy the boundary condition u = 0 at r = a (and du/dr = 0 at r = 0) are

\(\phi _{1}(r)=\cos \frac{\pi r}{2a}\) , \(\phi _{2}(r)=\cos \frac{3\pi r}{2a}\) , \(\phi _{3}(r)=\cos \frac{5\pi r}{2a}\) …

(b) For one-parameter approximation \(u(r)\approx U_{1}(r)=c_{1}\cos (\pi r/2a)\), the Galerkin integral is

\(\int_{0}^{a}\left \{ \frac{1}{r}\frac{\mathrm{d} }{\mathrm{d} r}\left [ r\frac{\pi }{2a}\left ( -\sin \frac{\pi r}{2a} \right ) \right ]c_{1}+\lambda c_{1} \cos \frac{\pi r}{2a}\right \}\cos \frac{\pi r}{2a}r\mathrm{d} r=0 \)

from which we obtain

\(\frac{\pi ^{2}}{4}\left ( \frac{1}{2}+\frac{2}{\pi ^{2}} \right )-\lambda \left ( \frac{1}{2}-\frac{2}{\pi ^{2}} \right )=0\)

It follows that \(\lambda =5.832/a^{2}\).

(c) For a two-parameter Ritz approximation \(U_{2}(r)=c_{1}\cos (\pi r/2a)+c_{2}\cos (3\pi r/2a)\), we obtain

\((1.7337-0.29736\lambda a^{2})c_{1}+(0.20264\lambda a^{2}-1.5)c_{2}=0\)

 

\((0.20264\lambda a^{2}-1.5)c_{1}+(11.603-0.47748\lambda a^{2})c_{2}=0\)

Setting the determinant of the above equations to zero, we obtain a quadratic equation in \(\lambda \)

\(0.10092\bar{\lambda }^{2}-3.6701\bar{\lambda }+17.866=0\) , \(\bar{\lambda }=\lambda a^{2}\)

The smaller root of the equation is \(\lambda = 5.792/a^{2}\) .The exact value is \(\lambda = 5.779/a^{2}\).
