Guest Question.

Consider two springs of stiffness \(K_{1} \) and \(K_{2} \) in the configurations shown in Figure 2.18. (a) Determine the effective stiffness of a system with the two springs in series and of a system with the two springs in parallel.(b) Assuming that a mass m is suspended from these springs, find the general relations for the respective frequencies of oscillation. (c) For the case where \(k_{1} = 200 N/m, k_{2} = 400 N/m\), and m= 50 kg, calculate the values of the frequencies.

Admin
Solution

(a) Extend the series configuration of the two springs by applying a static force \(F_{2}\)at the end. Let \(x_{1}\) be the deflection of a point in between the two springs, and \(x_{2}\) be the deflection of the end. Then

\(x_{1} =\frac{F_{s} }{K_{1}} \), \(x_{2} =\frac{F_{s} }{K_{2}} \)

since the same \(F_{s}\) acts on each spring. The total deflection is the sum,

\(x=x_{1} + x_{2} =F_{s} \left(\frac{1}{K_{1}} +\frac{1}{K_{2}} \right) =F_{s}\frac{1}{K_{s}} \) ,

where \(k_{s}\) is the equivalent or effective series spring constant,

\(k_{s}=\frac{k_{1} k_{2}}{k_{1}+k_{2}}\)

For the parallel configuration, a static force\(F_{p}\) is applied at the end resulting in the same deflection x of each spring. Each spring resists a part of the force,

\(F_{p1}=K_{1} x\) , \(F_{p2}=K_{2} x\)

The total force equals the sum of the two forces,

\(F_{p}=F_{p1} + F_{p2}=\left(K_{1}+K_{2}\right) x=k_{p} x\),

where \(k_{p}\) is the equivalent or effective parallel spring constant, \( k_{p} =K_{1} +K_{2}\).

(b) The frequencies of oscillation are obtained from the
equation, \(\omega _{n}=\sqrt{k/m}\) . For the series arrangement,

\(\omega _{ns}=\sqrt{\frac{k_{s}}{m} } =\sqrt{\frac{k_{1}k_{2}}{m\left(k_{1} + k_{2}\right) } }\)

and for the parallel arrangement,

\(\omega _{np}=\sqrt{\frac{k_{p}}{m} }=\sqrt{\frac{k_{1} +k_{2}}{m} }\)

(c) For the parameter values given,

\(k_{s}=\frac{200\cdot 400}{200 +400} =133 N/m\)

 

\(k_{p}=200 + 400= 600 N/m\)

 

\(\omega _{ns}=\sqrt{\frac{133}{50} }=1.63 rad/s\)

 

\(\omega _{np}\sqrt{\frac{600}{50} }=3.46 rad/s\).
