Guest Question.

Consider a particle of mass m in a two-dimensional infinite square well with sides of length L. With the origin placed at the center of the well, the stationary states can be written as

\( \psi_{n_{x} n_{y}}(x, y)=\frac{2}{L} \sin \left[\frac{n_{x} \pi}{L}\left(x-\frac{L}{2}\right)\right] \sin \left[\frac{n_{y} \pi}{L}\left(y-\frac{L}{2}\right)\right] \),

with energies

\( E_{n_{x} n_{y}}=\frac{\pi^{2} \hbar^{2}}{2 m L^{2}}\left(n_{x}^{2}+n_{y}^{2}\right) \),

for positive integers \( n_{x} \text { and } n_{y} \).

(a) The two states \( \psi_{a b} \text { and } \psi_{b a} \text { for } a \neq b \) are clearly degenerate. Show that a rotation by 90 counterclockwise about the center of the square carries one into the other,

\( \hat{R} \psi_{a b} \propto \psi_{b a} \),

and determine the constant of proportionality. Hint: write \( \psi_{a b} \) in polar
coordinates.

(b) Suppose that instead of \( \psi_{a b} \text { and } \psi_{b a} \) we choose the basis \( \psi_{+} \text {and } \psi_{-} \) for our two degenerate states:

\( \psi_{\pm}=\frac{\psi_{a b} \pm \psi_{b a}}{\sqrt{2}} \),

Show that if a and b are both even or both odd, then \( \psi_{+} \text {and } \psi \) are eigenstates of the rotation operator.

(c) Make a contour plot of the state \( \psi_{-} \text {for } a=5 \text { and } b=7 \) and verify (visually) that it is an eigenstate of every symmetry operation of the square (rotation by an integer multiple of π/2, reflection across a diagonal, or reflection along a line bisecting two sides). The fact that \( \psi_{+} \text {and } \psi_{-} \) are not connected to each other by any symmetry of the square means that there must be additional symmetry explaining the degeneracy of these two states.

Admin
Solution

(a) In polar coordinates \( x=r \cos \phi \text { and } y=r \sin \phi \) the states are

\( \psi_{a b}(r, \phi)=\frac{2}{L} \sin \left[\frac{a \pi}{L}\left(r \cos \phi-\frac{L}{2}\right)\right] \sin \left[\frac{b \pi}{L}\left(r \sin \phi-\frac{L}{2}\right)\right] \).

Rotating the state counter-clockwise produces

\( \hat{R} \psi_{a b}(r, \phi)=\psi_{a b}(r, \phi-\pi / 2) \).

\( =\frac{2}{L} \sin \left\{\frac{a \pi}{L}\left[r \cos \left(\phi-\frac{\pi}{2}\right)-\frac{L}{2}\right]\right\} \sin \left\{\frac{b \pi}{L}\left[r \sin \left(\phi-\frac{\pi}{2}\right)-\frac{L}{2}\right]\right\} \).

\( =\frac{2}{L} \sin \left[\frac{a \pi}{L}\left(r \sin \phi-\frac{L}{2}\right)\right] \sin \left[\frac{b \pi}{L}\left(-r \cos \phi-\frac{L}{2}\right)\right] \).

\( =\frac{2}{L} \sin \left[\frac{a \pi}{L}\left(y-\frac{L}{2}\right)\right] \sin \left[-\frac{b \pi}{L}\left(x-\frac{L}{2}+L\right)\right] \).

\( =-\frac{2}{L} \sin \left[\frac{b \pi}{L}\left(x-\frac{L}{2}\right)+b \pi\right] \sin \left[\frac{a \pi}{L}\left(y-\frac{L}{2}\right)\right] \).

\( =(-1)^{b+1} \frac{2}{L} \sin \left[\frac{b \pi}{L}\left(x-\frac{L}{2}\right)\right] \sin \left[\frac{a \pi}{L}\left(y-\frac{L}{2}\right)\right] \).

so that

\( \hat{R} \psi_{a b}=(-1)^{b+1} \psi_{b a} \).

(b) Using the result from part (a),

\( \hat{R} \psi_{\pm}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left[\hat{R} \psi_{a b} \pm \hat{R} \psi_{b a}\right] \).

\( =\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left[(-1)^{b+1} \psi_{b a} \pm(-1)^{a+1} \psi_{a b}\right] \).

\( =\pm(-1)^{a+1} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left[\psi_{a b} \pm(-1)^{a+b} \psi_{b a}\right] \).

Now if a and b are both even or both odd then \( (-1)^{a+b}=1 \) and \( \psi_{\pm} \text {is an eigenstate of } \hat{R} \) with eigenvalue \( \mp(-1)^{a} \).

(c) Plot is shown below. All of the listed symmetries are clearly present.

\( ln [*]=\psi\left[a_{-}, b_{-}, x_{-}, y_{-}\right]:=\frac{2}{L} \sin \left[\frac{a \pi}{L}\left(x-\frac{L}{2}\right)\right] \sin \left[\frac{b \pi}{L}\left(y-\frac{L}{2}\right)\right] \).

\( ln [*]=\psi M \left[ x _{-}, y _{-}\right]:=\frac{\psi[5,7, x , y ]-\psi[7,5, x , y ]}{\sqrt{2}} \).

In[*]:= L = 1;

In[*]:=\( \text { ContourP } \operatorname{lot}\left[\psi M [ x , y ],\left\{ x ,-\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}\right\},\left\{ y ,-\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}\right\}\right] \).
