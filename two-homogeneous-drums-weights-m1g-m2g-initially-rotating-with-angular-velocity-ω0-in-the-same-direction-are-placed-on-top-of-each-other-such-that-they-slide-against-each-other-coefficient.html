Guest Question.

Two homogeneous drums (weights \(m_{1}g \) , \(m_{2}g \) ), initially rotating with angular velocity \(\omega _{0} \) in the same direction, are placed on top of each other, such that they slide against each other (coefficient of dynamic friction \(\mu \) ).

After which time \(t_{R} \) , the drums roll from one another and what are then their angular velocities?

Admin
Solution

During sliding the equations of motion read

① \(\overset{\curvearrowleft }{B} :\Theta _{1}\ddot{\varphi }_{1}=-r_{1}R\) ,

② \(\overset{\curvearrowleft }{A} :\Theta _{2}\ddot{\varphi }_{2}=-r_{2}R\) ,

\(\uparrow :0=N-m_{2}g \) .

With the friction law \(R=\mu N\) and \(\Theta _{1}=m_{1}r^{2}_{1} /2\) , \(\Theta _{2}=m_{2}r^{2}_{2} /2\) and considering the initial conditions, it follows

\(\ddot{\varphi }_{1}=-\frac{2\mu g}{r_{1} }\frac{m_{2} }{m_{1} } \rightsquigarrow \dot{\varphi }_{1}=-\frac{2 \mu g}{r_{1} }\frac{m_{2} }{m_{1} }t+\omega _{0}\) ,

\(\ddot{\varphi }_{2}=-\frac{2\mu g}{r_{2} } \rightsquigarrow \dot{\varphi }_{2}=-\frac{2 \mu g}{r_{2} }\frac{m_{2} }{m_{2} }t+\omega _{0}\) .

The drums stop sliding and start rolling from each other when their velocities at the contact point are the same at time \(t=t_{R} \) :

\(r_{1}\dot{\varphi }_{1}=-r_{2}\dot{\varphi }_{2} \) .

Note that positive angular velocities of the drums ① and ② lead to opposite directed velocities at the contact point! Thus, we obtain

\(\underline{\underline{t_{R}=\frac{\omega _{0}\left(r_{1}+r_{2} \right) }{2\mu g\left(1+\frac{m_{2} }{1} \right) } } }\)

and for the angular velocities

\(\underline{\underline{\dot{\varphi }_{1}\left(t_{R} \right)=\omega _{0}\frac{\frac{m_{1} }{m_{2} }-\frac{r_{2} }{r_{1} } }{1+\frac{m_{1} }{m_{2} } } } } \) , \(\underline{\underline{\dot{\varphi }_{2}\left(t_{R} \right)=-\omega _{0}\frac{\frac{r_{1} m_{1} }{r_{2} m_{2} }-1 }{1+\frac{m_{1} }{m_{2} } } } } \) .

Remark: For \( m_{1}/m_{2}=r_{2}/r_{1} \) both drums come to standstill.
