Guest Question.

In a Hackworth radial valve gear, as shown in Fig. 17.37, the dimensions of various link are as follows :
OC = 225 mm; CP = 800 mm; DE = 625 mm and AE = 300 mm
If the lead is constant at 3 mm, the steam lap is 18 mm and the angle \(\beta \text { is } 20^{\circ}\), find the length of eccentric OE, the distance OF, the effective valve travel and the effective angle of advance.

Admin
Solution

Given : l = 3 mm ; s = 18 mm ; \(\beta = 20^{\circ}\)

Length of eccentric OE

We know that in case of a Hackworth radial valve gear, the virtual eccentric OV may be assumed to be equivalent to two eccentrics, i.e. \(180^{\circ}\) component eccentric and \(90^{\circ}\) component eccentric as shown in Fig. 17.38. The throw of the \(180^{\circ}\) component eccentric is given by

\(O X=\frac{D A}{D E} \times O E=\frac{D E-A E}{D E} \times O E=\frac{625-300}{625} \times O E\)

= 0.52 OE …(i)

Also OX = s + l = 18 + 3 = 21 mm …(ii)

From equations (i) and (ii),

OE = 21/ 0.52 = 40.4 mm

Distance OF

When the eccentric OE is along the line of stroke, the point D coincides with F and the angle FOE = \(90^{\circ}\).

\(\therefore \quad(O F)^{2}+(O E)^{2}=(D E)^{2}\)

or \(O F=\sqrt{(D E)^{2}-(O E)^{2}}=\sqrt{(625)^{2}-(40.4)^{2}}=623.7 mm\)

Effective valve travel

We know that the \(90^{\circ}\) component eccentric,

\(O Y=\frac{E A}{E D} \times O E \tan \beta=\frac{300}{625} \times 40.4 \tan 20^{\circ}=7.06 mm\)

\(\therefore \) Throw of the virtual eccentric,

\(O V=\sqrt{(O X)^{2}+(O Y)^{2}}=\sqrt{(21)^{2}+(7.06)^{2}}=22.15 mm\)

and effective valve travel \(=2 \times O V=2 \times 22.15=44.3 mm\)

Effective angle of advance

We know that effective angle of advance,

\(\alpha=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{O X}{O Y}\right)=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{21}{7.06}\right)=\tan ^{-1} 2.9745=71.4^{\circ}\)
