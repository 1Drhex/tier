Equal-area criterion: critical clearing angle for a cleared three-phase fault

The synchronous generator in Figure 11.4 is initially operating in the steady-state condition given in when a permanent three-phase-to-ground bolted short circuit occurs on line 1–3 at bus 3. The fault is cleared by opening the circuit breakers at the ends of line 1–3 and line 2–3. These circuit breakers then remain open. Calculate the critical clearing angle. As in previous examples, H = 3.0 p.u.-s, \(p_{m} =1.0\) per unit and \(\omega _{b.u.}=1.0 \) in the swing equation.

Admin
Solution

From , the equation for the prefault electrical power, denoted \(p_{e1} \) here, is \(p_{e1} \) = 2.4638 sin δ per unit. The faulted network is shown in Figure 11.12 (a), and the Thévenin equivalent of the faulted network, as viewed from the generator internal voltage source, is shown in Figure 11.12(b). The Thévenin reactance is

\(X_{Th} =0.40+0.20\parallel 0.10=0.46666\) per unit

and the Thévenin voltage source is

\(V_{Th} =1.0\angle 0°\left[\frac{X_{13} }{X_{13} +X_{12} } \right] =1.0\angle 0°\frac{0.10}{0.30}=0.33333\angle 0° \) per unit

From Figure 11.12(b), the equation for the electrical power delivered by the generator to the infinite bus during the fault, denoted \(p_{e2} \), is

\(p_{e2} =\frac{E^{\prime }V_{Th} }{X_{Th} } \sin \delta =\frac{\left(1.2812\right) \left(0.3333\right) }{0.46666} \sin \delta =0.1952\sin \delta\) per unit

The postfault network is shown in Figure 11.12(c), where circuit breakers have opened and removed lines 1–3 and 2–3. From this figure, the postfault electrical power delivered, denoted \(p_{e3} \), is

\(p_{e3} =\frac{\left(1.2812\right) \left(1.0\right) }{0.60} \sin \delta =2.1353\sin \delta \) per unit

The p–δ curves as well as the accelerating area \(A_{1} \) and decelerating area \(A_{2} \) corresponding to critical clearing are shown in Figure 11.12(d). Equating \(A_{1} \) and \(A_{2} \),

\(A_{1} =\int_{\delta _{0} }^{\delta _{cr} }\left(p_{m}-P_{2\max } \sin \delta \right)d\delta = A_{2}=\int_{\delta _{cr} }^{\delta _{3} }\left(P_{3\max }\sin \delta -p_{m} \right)d\delta \int_{0.4179}^{\delta _{cr} }\left(1.0-0.9152\sin \delta \right)d\delta =\int_{\delta _{cr} }^{2.6542}\left(2.1353\sin \delta -1.0\right) d\delta \)

Solving for \(\delta _{cr} \),

\(\left(\delta _{cr}-0.4179\right) +0.9152\left(\cos\delta _{cr}-\cos 0.4179 \right)=2.1353\left(\cos \delta _{cr}-\cos 2.6542\right) -\left(2.6542-\delta _{cr}\right)-1.2201\cos\delta _{cr}=0.4868 \)

 

\(\delta _{cr} =1.9812radians=111.5°\)

If the fault is cleared before \(\delta =\delta _{cr} =111.5°\), stability is maintained. Otherwise, stability is lost. To see this case in PowerWorld Simulator open case Example 11_6.
