Guest Question.

a. A Visa credit card issued through Chase Bank carries an interest rate of 1% per month on the unpaid balance. Calculate the effective rate per semiannual period.

b. If the card’s interest rate is stated as 3.5% per quarter, find the effective semiannual and annual rates.

Admin
Solution

a. The compounding period is monthly. Since the effective interest rate per semiannual period is desired, the r in Equation [3.2] must be the nominal rate per 6 months.

\(i per period = (1+r/m)^m -1\) [3.2]

\(r = 1\% per month \times 6 months per semiannual period\) \(= 6\% per semiannual period\)

The m in Equation [3.2] is equal to 6, since interest is compounded 6 times in 6 months. The effective semiannual rate is

\(i per 6 months = \left(1+\frac{0.06}{6} \right) ^6 -1 = 0.0615 (6.15\%)\)

b. For an interest rate of 3.5% per quarter, the compounding period is quarterly. In a semiannual period, \(m = 2\) and \(r = 7%\).

\(i per 6 months = \left(1+\frac{0.07}{2} \right) ^2 -1 = 0.0712 (7.12\%)\)

The effective interest rate per year is determined using \(r = 14%\) and \(m = 4\).

\(i per year = \left(1+\frac{0.14}{4} \right) ^4 -1 = 0.1475 (14.75\%)\)

Comment: Note that the term r/m in Equation [3.2] is always the effective interest rate per compounding period. In part (a) this is 1% per month, while in part (b) it is 3.5% per quarter.
