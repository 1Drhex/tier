Guest Question.

A hollow steel [E = 30,000 ksi] tube (1) with an outside diameter of 3.50 in. and a wall thickness of 0.216 in. is fastened to a solid 2-in.- diameter aluminum [E = 10,000 ksi] rod. The assembly is attached to unyielding supports at the left and right ends and is loaded as shown in Figure P5.48. Determine:
(a) the stresses in all parts of the axial structure.
(b) the deflections of joints B and C.

Admin
Solution

Section properties: The steel tube cross-sectional area is:

\(\begin{aligned}&D_{1}=3.50 \text { in. } \quad d_{1}=3.50 \text { in. }-2(0.216 in .)=3.068 in . \\&A_{1}=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[(3.50 in .)^{2}-(3.068 in .)^{2}\right]=2.2285 in .{ }^{2}\end{aligned}\)

and the aluminum rod has a cross-sectional area of:

\(\begin{aligned}&D_{2}=D_{3}=2.00 in . \\&A_{2}=A_{3}=\frac{\pi}{4}(2.00 in .)^{2}=3.1416 in .^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Equilibrium: Consider a FBD of flange B. Sum forces in the horizontal direction to obtain:

\(\Sigma F_{x}=-F_{1}+F_{2}-34 \text { kips }=0\) (a)

Consider a FBD of flange C. Sum forces in the horizontal direction to obtain:

\(\Sigma F_{x}=-F_{2}+F_{3}+26 kips =0\) (b)

 

Geometry of Deformations:

\(\delta_{1}+\delta_{2}+\delta_{3}=0\) (c)

Force-Deformation Relationships:

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}} \quad \delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}} \quad \delta_{3}=\frac{F_{3} L_{3}}{A_{3} E_{3}}\) (d)

Compatibility Equation: Substitute Eqs. (d) into Eq. (c) to derive the compatibility equation:

\(\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}+\frac{F_{3} L_{3}}{A_{3} E_{3}}=0\) (e)

Solve the Equations: Solve Eq. (a) for \(F_{1} \):

\(F_{1}=F_{2}-34 kips\) (f)

Solve Eq. (b) for \(F_{3} \):

\(F_{3}=F_{2}-26 kips\) (g)

Substitute Eqs. (f) and (g) into compatibility equation (e):

\(\frac{\left(F_{2}-34 \text { kips }\right) L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}+\frac{\left(F_{2}-26 kips \right) L_{3}}{A_{3} E_{3}}=0\)

and expand terms:

\(\frac{F_{2} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}-\frac{(34 kips ) L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}+\frac{F_{2} L_{3}}{A_{3} E_{3}}-\frac{(26 kips ) L_{3}}{A_{3} E_{3}}=0\)

Regroup terms:

\(F_{2}\left[\frac{L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}+\frac{L_{3}}{A_{3} E_{3}}\right]=\frac{(34 kips ) L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\frac{(26 kips ) L_{3}}{A_{3} E_{3}}\)

and solve for \(F_{2}\):

\(F_{2}=\frac{\frac{(34 kips ) L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\frac{(26 kips ) L_{3}}{A_{3} E_{3}}}{\frac{L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}+\frac{L_{3}}{A_{3} E_{3}}}\)

 

\(=\frac{\frac{(34 kips )(48 in .)}{\left(2.2285 in .^{2}\right)(30,000 ksi )}+\frac{(26 kips )(60 in .)}{\left(3.1416 in.^{2}\right)(10,000 ksi )}}{\frac{48 in .}{\left(2.2285 in .^{2}\right)(30,000 ksi )}+\frac{60 in .}{\left(3.1416 in. ^{2}\right)(10,000 ksi )}+\frac{60 in .}{\left(3.1416 in .^{2}\right)(10,000 ksi )}}\)

= 16.3227 kips

 

Backsubstitute into Eqs. (f) and (g) to obtain \(F_{1} \text { and } F_{3}\):

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{1}=F_{2}-34 \text { kips }=16.3227 \text { kips }-34 \text { kips }=-17.6773 \text { kips } \\&F_{3}=F_{2}-26 \text { kips }=16.3227 \text { kips }-26 \text { kips }=-9.6773 \text { kips }\end{aligned}\)

 

(a) Normal stresses: The normal stresses in each axial member can now be calculated:

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{1}=\frac{F_{1}}{A_{1}}=\frac{-17.6773 kips }{2.2285 in .^{2}}=-7.9325 ksi =7.93 ksi ( C ) \\&\sigma_{2}=\frac{F_{2}}{A_{2}}=\frac{16.3227 kips }{3.1416 in .^{2}}=5.1957 ksi =5.20 ksi ( T ) \\&\sigma_{3}=\frac{F_{3}}{A_{3}}=\frac{-9.6773 kips }{3.1416 in .^{2}}=-3.0834 ksi =3.08 ksi ( C )\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Joint deflections: The deflection of flange B is equal to the deformation (i.e., contraction in this instance) of member (1):

\(u_{B}=\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}=\frac{(-17.6773 kips )(48 in .)}{\left(2.2285 in. ^{2}\right)(30,000 ksi )}=-0.012692 in .=0.01269 in . \leftarrow\)

The deflection of flange C is equal to the sum of the deformations in members (1) and (2). The deformation of member (2) is:

\(\delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}=\frac{(16.3227 kips )(60 in .)}{\left(3.1416 in .^{2}\right)(10,000 ksi )}=0.031174 in.\)

And thus, the deflection of flange C is:

\(u_{C}=\delta_{1}+\delta_{2}=-0.012692 in .+0.031174 in .=0.018482 in .=0.01848 in . \rightarrow\)
