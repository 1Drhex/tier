Guest Question.

Let \(\mathrm{A}=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}-1 & 3 & 4 \\ 9 & 6 & 1\end{array}\right), \quad \mathbf{B}=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}1 & 5 & 8 \\ -2 & 3 & 2\end{array}\right), c=-2, k=5\) and \(\mathrm{x}=\left(\begin{array}{l}x \\ y \\ z\end{array}\right) .\) Determine:

(a) \((c+k) \mathbf{B}\) (b) \(c \mathrm{~B}+k \mathrm{~B}\) (c) \(-10 \mathrm{~A}\) (d) \(c(k \mathrm{~A})\) (e) \(k(\mathrm{~A}+\mathrm{B}) \mathrm{x}\)

(f) Write the following linear system in terms of \(\mathbf{A}, \mathrm{B}, c, k, \mathbf{x}\) and \(\mathbf{O}\) :

\(\begin{array}{r}14 x+14 y+28 z=0 \\-77 x-21 y+7 z=0\end{array}\)
Admin
Solution

(a) We first evaluate \((c+k)\) and then scalar multiply this result by matrix \(\mathrm{B}\) :

\(\begin{aligned}(c+k) \mathbf{B} &=(-2+5) \mathbf{B} \quad[\text { Substituting } c=-2 \text { and } k=5] \\&=3 \mathrm{~B}=3\left(\begin{array}{rrr}1 & 5 & 8 \\-2 & 3 & 2\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}3 & 15 & 24 \\-6 & 9 & 6\end{array}\right) \quad\left[\begin{array}{l}\text { Multiplying each } \\\text { entry by } 3\end{array}\right]\end{aligned}\)

(b) By Theorem (1.13) (c) we have \((c+k) \mathbf{B}=c \mathbf{B}+k \mathbf{B}\), therefore we have the same solution as part (a).

(c) How do we evaluate \(-10 \mathrm{~A}\) ?

Multiply each entry of matrix \(\mathbf{A}\) by \(-10\) :

\(-10 \mathrm{~A}=-10\left(\begin{array}{rrr}-1 & 3 & 4 \\9 & 6 & 1\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}10 & -30 & -40 \\-90 & -60 & -10\end{array}\right)\)

(d) What is \(c(k \mathrm{~A})\) equal to?

By Theorem (1.13) (a) we have \((c k) \mathrm{A}=c(k \mathbf{A})\) and \(c k=-2 \times 5=-10\), therefore \(c(k \mathbf{A})=(c k) \mathbf{A}=-10 \mathrm{~A}\). The evaluation of \(-10 \mathrm{~A}\) was done in part (c) above.

(e) Adding the matrices \(\mathrm{A}\) and \(\mathrm{B}\) and multiplying the result by \(k=5\) and \(\mathrm{x}\) we have

\(\begin{aligned}k(\mathbf{A}+\mathbf{B}) \mathbf{x} &=5\left[\left(\begin{array}{rrr}-1 & 3 & 4 \\9 & 6 & 1\end{array}\right)+\left(\begin{array}{rrr}1 & 5 & 8 \\-2 & 3 & 2\end{array}\right)\right] \mathbf{x} \\&=5\left(\begin{array}{lll}0 & 8 & 12 \\7 & 9 & 3\end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l}x \\y \\z\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{ccc}0 & 40 & 60 \\35 & 45 & 15\end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l}x \\y \\z\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{c}40 y+60 z \\35 x+45 y+15 z\end{array}\right)\end{aligned}\)

(f) We can write the linear system as

\(\begin{array}{r}14 x+14 y+28 z=0 \\-77 x-21 y+7 z=0\end{array} \quad \Rightarrow \quad\left(\begin{array}{rrr}14 & 14 & 28 \\-77 & -21 & 7\end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l}x \\y \\z\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{l}0 \\0\end{array}\right)\)

The 2 by 3 matrix has a common factor of 7 . Taking this out, we have

\(7\left(\begin{array}{rrr}2 & 2 & 4 \\-11 & -3 & 1\end{array}\right)\) (*)

Since we are given \(c=-2, k=5\), we can write \(7=5-(-2)=k-c\).

Can we find any relationship between the matrix in \(\left(^{*}\right)\) and the given matrices \(\mathrm{A}=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}-1 & 3 & 4 \\ 9 & 6 & 1\end{array}\right)\) and \(\mathrm{B}=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}1 & 5 & 8 \\ -2 & 3 & 2\end{array}\right) ?\)

Adding matrices \(\mathbf{A}\) and \(\mathrm{B}\) does not give us the coefficients in \(\left({ }^{*}\right) .\) However, \(\mathrm{B}-\mathrm{A}\) works:

\(\mathrm{B}-\mathbf{A}=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}1 & 5 & 8 \\-2 & 3 & 2\end{array}\right)-\left(\begin{array}{rrr}-1 & 3 & 4 \\9 & 6 & 1\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}2 & 2 & 4 \\-11 & -3 & 1\end{array}\right) \quad\left[\begin{array}{l}\text { Subtracting the } \\\text { corresponding entries }\end{array}\right]\)

Hence we can write the given linear system as

\((k-c)(\mathbf{B}-\mathbf{A}) \mathbf{x}=\mathbf{O}\)

Theorem (1.13){

(a) \((c k) \mathbf{A}=c(k \mathbf{A})\)
(c) \((c+k) \mathbf{A}=c \mathrm{~A}+k \mathbf{A}\)}
