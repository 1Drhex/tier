Guest Question.

Assume the equilibrium mole fractions of oxygen and nitrogen are close to those in air, find the equilibrium mole fraction for NO at 5400 R, 75 psia disregarding dissocoiations.

Admin
Solution

Assume the simple reaction to make NO as:

\(\begin{array}{cccc|l}N _{2} & + O _{2} \Leftrightarrow & 2 NO & Ar & n _{ N 2}=0.78- x \\0.78 & 0.21 & 0 & 0.01 & n _{ O 2}=0.21- x \\- x & – x & +2 x & & n _{ NO }=2 x , \quad n _{ ar }=0.01 \\\hline 0.78- x & 0.21- x & 2 x & 0.01 & n _{ tot }=1.0\end{array}\)

 

From A.11 at 5400 R = 3000 K, ln K = -4.205, K = 0.014921

\(\begin{aligned}&K=\frac{4 x^{2}}{(0.78-x)(0.21-x)}\left(\frac{P}{P^{0}}\right)^{0} \quad \Rightarrow \\&\frac{x^{2}}{(0.78-x)(0.21-x)}=\frac{0.014921}{4}=0.00373 \quad \text { and } \quad 0<x<0.21\end{aligned}\)

 

Solve for x: x = 0.0230 \(\Rightarrow \quad y_{N O}=\frac{2 x}{1.0}=0.046\)

Comment: Since the reaction is pressure insensitive P does not matter.
