Guest Question.

A centrifugal clutch is to transmit 15 kW at 900 r.p.m. The shoes are four in number. The speed at which the engagement begins is 3/4th of the running speed. The inside radius of the pulley rim is 150 mm and the centre of gravity of the shoe lies at 120 mm from the centre of the spider. The shoes are lined with Ferrodo for which the coefficient of friction may be taken as 0.25.
Determine : 1. Mass of the shoes, and 2. Size of the shoes, if angle subtended by the shoes at the centre of the spider is \(60^{\circ}\) and the pressure exerted on the shoes is \(0.1 N / mm ^{2}\).

Admin
Solution

Given : \(P=15 kW =15 \times 10^{3} W ; N=900 r.p.m . \text { or } \omega=25 \times 900 / 60=94.26 rad / s\) ; n = 4 ; R = 150 mm = 0.15 m ; r = 120 mm = 0.12 m ; \(\mu=0.25\)

Since the speed at which the engagement begins (i.e. \(\omega_{1}\)) is 3/4th of the running speed (i.e.\(\omega\)), therefore

\(\omega_{1}=\frac{3}{4} \omega=\frac{3}{4} \times 94.26=70.7 rad / s\)

Let T = Torque transmitted at the running speed.

We know that power transmitted (P),

\(15 \times 10^{3}=T . \omega=T \times 94.26 \text { or } \quad T=15 \times 10^{3} / 94.26=159 N – m\)

 

1. Mass of the shoes

Let m = Mass of the shoes in kg.

We know that the centrifugal force acting on each shoe,

\(P_{c}=m \cdot \omega^{2} \cdot r=m(94.26)^{2} \times 0.12=1066 m N\)

and the inward force on each shoe exerted by the spring i.e. the centrifugal force at the engagement speed \(\omega_{1}\),

\(P_{s}=m\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2} r=m(70.7)^{2} \times 0.12=600 m N\)

\(\therefore \) Frictional force acting tangentially on each shoe,

\(F=\mu\left(P_{c}-P_{s}\right)=0.25(1066 m-600 m)=116.5 m N\)

We know that the torque transmitted (T ),

\(159=n \cdot F \cdot R=4 \times 116.5 m \times 0.15=70 m \quad \text { or } \quad m=2.27 kg\)

 

2. Size of the shoes

Let l = Contact length of shoes in mm,

b = Width of the shoes in mm,

\(\theta\) = Angle subtended by the shoes at the centre of the spider in radians

\(=60^{\circ}=\pi / 3 rad , \text { and }\) …(Given)

p = Pressure exerted on the shoes in \(N / mm ^{2}=0.1 N / mm ^{2}\) …(Given)

We know that \(l=\theta . R=\frac{\pi}{3} \times 150=157.1 mm\)

and \(\text { l.b. } p=P_{c}-P_{s}=1066 m -600 m =466 m\)

\(\therefore \) \(157.1 \times b \times 0.1=466 \times 2.27=1058\)

or b = 1058/157.1 × 0.1 = 67.3 mm
