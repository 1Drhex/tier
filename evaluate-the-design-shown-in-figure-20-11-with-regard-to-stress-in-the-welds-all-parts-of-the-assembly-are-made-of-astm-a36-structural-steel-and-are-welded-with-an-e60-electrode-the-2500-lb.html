Guest Question.

Evaluate the design shown in Figure 20–11 with regard to stress in the welds. All parts of the assembly are made of ASTM A36 structural steel and are welded with an E60 electrode. The 2500-lb load is a dead load.

Admin
Solution

The critical point would be the weld at the top of the tube where it is joined to the vertical surface. At this point, there is a three-dimensional force system acting on the weld, as shown in Figure 20–12. The offset location of the load causes a twisting on the weld that produces a force \(f_t\) on the weld toward the left in the y-direction. The bending produces a force \(f_b\) acting outward along the x-axis. The vertical shear force \(f_s\) acts downward along the z-axis.
From statics, the resultant of the three force components would be

\(f_{R}=\sqrt{f_{t}^{2}+f_{b}^{2}+f_{s}^{2}}\)
Now each component force on the weld will be computed.
Twisting Force, \(f_{t}\)
\(\begin{aligned}f_{t} &=\frac{T C}{J_{w}} \\T &=(2500 \mathrm{lb})(8.00\mathrm{in})=20000 \mathrm{lb} \cdot \mathrm{in} \\c &=O D / 2=4.500 \mathrm{in} /2=2.25 \mathrm{in} \\J_{w} &=(\pi)(O D)^{3} / 4=(\pi)(4.500 \mathrm{in})^{3} / 4=71.57\mathrm{in}^{3}\end{aligned}\)
Then
\(f_{t}=\frac{T c}{J_{w}}=\frac{(20000 \mathrm{lb} \cdot \mathrm{in})(2.25 \mathrm{in})}{71.57 \mathrm{in}^{3}}=629 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{in}\)
Bending Force, \(f_{b}\)
\(\begin{aligned}f_{b} &=\frac{M}{S_{w}} \\M &=(2500 \mathrm{lb})(14.00\mathrm{in})=35000\mathrm{lb} \cdot \mathrm{in} \\S_{W} &=(\pi)(O D)^{2} / 4=(\pi)(4.500 \mathrm{in})^{2} / 4=15.90 \mathrm{in}^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Then
\(f_{b}=\frac{M}{S_{W}}=\frac{35000 \mathrm{lb} \cdot \mathrm{in}}{15.90 \mathrm{in}^{2}}=2201 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{in}\)
Vertical Shear Force, \(f_{s}\)

\(\begin{aligned}f_{S} &=\frac{P}{A_{W}} \\A_{W} &=(\pi)(O D)=(\pi)(4.500\mathrm{in})=14.14 \mathrm{in} \\f_{S} &=\frac{P}{A_{W}}=\frac{2500 \mathrm{lb}}{14.14 \mathrm{in}}=177 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{in}\end{aligned}\)
Now the resultant can be computed: (Note: All terms have the unit of \(\mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{in}\).)
\(\begin{aligned}f_{R} &=\sqrt{f_{t}{ }^{2}+f_{b}{ }^{2}+f_{s}{ }^{2}} \\f_{R}&=\sqrt{629^{2}+2201^{2}+177^{2}}=2296 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{in}\end{aligned}\)
Comparing this with the allowable force on a \(1.0\)-in weld gives minimum size for the weld leg to be
\(w=\frac{2296 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{in}}{9600 \mathrm{Ib} / \mathrm{in} \text { per inch of leg size }}=0.239 \text { in }\)
The \(1 / 4\)-in fillet specified in Figure 20-11 is satisfactory.
