Guest Question.

A 2-\( m ^{3}\) storage tank contains 95% liquid and 5% vapor by volume of liquified natural gas (LNG) at 160 K, as shown in Fig. P4.155. It may be assumed that LNG has the same properties as pure methane. Heat is transferred to the tank and saturated vapor at 160 K flows into a steady flow heater which it leaves at 300 K. The process continues until all the liquid in the storage tank is gone. Calculate the total amount of heat transfer to the tank and the total amount of heat transferred to the heater.

Admin
Solution

CV: Tank, flow out, transient.
Continuity Eq.: \(m_{2}-m_{1}=-m_{e}\)

Energy Eq.: \( Q_{Tank }=m_{2} u _{2}- m _{1} u _{1}+ m _{ e } h _{ e }\)

At 160 K, from Table B.7:

 

\(m _{ f }= V _{ f } / v _{ f }=\frac{0.95 \times 2}{0.00297}=639.73 kg , \quad m _{ g }= V _{ g } / v _{ g }=\frac{0.05 \times 2}{0.03935}=2.541 kg\)

 

\(m _{1}=642.271 kg , \quad m _{2}= V / v _{ g 2}=2 / 0.03935=50.826 kg\)

 

\(m _{1} u _{1}=639.73(-106.35)+2.541(207.7)=-67507 kJ\)

 

\(m_{e}=m_{1}-m_{2}=591.445 kg\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}Q _{\text {Tank }} &=50.826 \times 207.7-(-67507)+591.445 \times 270.3 \\&=+ 2 3 7 9 3 1 kJ\end{aligned}\)

 

CV: Heater, steady flow, \(P = P _{ G 160 K }=1593 kPa\)

 

\( Q_{Heater}= m _{ e Tank } \left( h _{ e }- h _{ i }\right)_{Heater}\)

 

= 591.445(612.9 – 270.3) = 202 629 kJ
