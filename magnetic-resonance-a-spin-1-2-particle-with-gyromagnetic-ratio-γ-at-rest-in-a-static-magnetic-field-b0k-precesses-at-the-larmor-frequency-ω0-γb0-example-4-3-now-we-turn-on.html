Guest Question.

Magnetic resonance. A spin-1/2 particle with gyromagnetic ratio γ, at rest in a static magnetic field \( B_{0} \hat{k} \), precesses at the Larmor frequency \( \omega_{0}=\gamma B_{0} \) (Example 4.3). Now we turn on a small transverse radiofrequency (rf) field , \( B_{ rf }[\cos (\omega t) \hat{\imath}-\sin (\omega t) \hat{\jmath}] \), so that the total field is

\( B =B_{ rf } \cos (\omega t) \hat{\imath}-B_{ rf } \sin (\omega t) \hat{\jmath}+B_{0} \hat{k} \) (11.123).

(a) Construct the 2 ×2 Hamiltonian matrix (Equation 4.158) for this system.

\( H =-\gamma B \cdot S \) (4.158).

(b) If \( \chi(t)=\left(\begin{array}{l} a(t) \\ b(t) \end{array}\right) \) is the spin state at time t, show that

\( \dot{a}=\frac{i}{2}\left(\Omega e^{i \omega t} b+\omega_{0} a\right) ; \quad \dot{b}=\frac{i}{2}\left(\Omega e^{-i \omega t} a-\omega_{0} b\right) \) (11.124).

where \( \Omega \equiv \gamma B_{ rf } \) is related to the strength of the rf field.

(c) Check that the general solution for a(t) and b(t) , in terms of their initial values \( a_{0} \text { and } b_{0}, \text { is } \)

\( a(t)=\left\{a_{0} \cos \left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right)+\frac{i}{\omega^{\prime}}\left[a_{0}\left(\omega_{0}-\omega\right)+b_{0} \Omega\right] \sin \left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right)\right\} e^{i \omega t / 2} \),

\( b(t)=\left\{b_{0} \cos \left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right)+\frac{i}{\omega^{\prime}}\left[b_{0}\left(\omega-\omega_{0}\right)+a_{0} \Omega\right] \sin \left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right)\right\} e^{-i \omega t / 2} \).

where

\( \omega^{\prime} \equiv \sqrt{\left(\omega-\omega_{0}\right)^{2}+\Omega^{2}} \). (11.125).

(d) If the particle starts out with spin up (i.e. \( a_{0}=1, b_{0}=0 \)), find the probability of a transition to spin down, as a function of time. Answer:

\( P(t)=\left\{\Omega^{2} /\left[\left(\omega-\omega_{0}\right)^{2}+\Omega^{2}\right]\right\} \sin ^{2}\left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right) \).

(e) Sketch the resonance curve,

\( P(\omega)=\frac{\Omega^{2}}{\left(\omega-\omega_{0}\right)^{2}+\Omega^{2}} \) (11.126).

as a function of the driving frequency ω (for fixed \( \omega_{0} \text { and } Ω\)). Note that the maximum occurs at \( \omega=\omega_{0} . \) . Find the “full width at half maximum,” Δω.

(f) Since \( \omega_{0}=\gamma B_{0} \), we can use the experimentally observed resonance to determine the magnetic dipole moment of the particle. In a nuclear magnetic resonance (nmr) experiment the g-factor of the proton is to be measured, using a static field of 10,000 gauss and an rf field of amplitude 0.01 gauss. What will the resonant frequency be? (See Section 7.5 for the
magnetic moment of the proton.) Find the width of the resonance curve. (Give your answers in Hz.)

Admin
Solution

(a)

\( H=-\gamma B \cdot S =-\gamma\left(B_{x} S_{x}+B_{y} S_{y}+B_{z} S_{z}\right) \);

\( H =-\gamma \frac{\hbar}{2}\left(B_{x} \sigma_{x}+B_{y} \sigma_{y}+B_{z} \sigma_{z}\right)=-\frac{\gamma \hbar}{2}\left[B_{x}\left(\begin{array}{ll} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{array}\right)+B_{y}\left(\begin{array}{cc} 0 & -i \\ i & 0 \end{array}\right)+B_{z}\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{array}\right)\right] \).

\( =-\frac{\gamma \hbar}{2}\left(\begin{array}{cc} B_{z} & B_{x}-i B_{y} \\ B_{x}+i B_{y} & -B_{z} \end{array}\right)=-\frac{\gamma \hbar}{2}\left(\begin{array}{cc} B_{0} & B_{ rf }(\cos \omega t+i \sin \omega t) \\ B_{ rf }(\cos \omega t-i \sin \omega t) & -B_{0} \end{array}\right) \).

\( =-\frac{\gamma \hbar}{2}\left(\begin{array}{cc} B_{0} & B_{ rf } e^{i \omega t} \\ B_{ rf } e^{-i \omega t} & -B_{0} \end{array}\right) . \).

(b) \( i \hbar \dot{\chi}= H \chi \Rightarrow \)

\( i \hbar\left(\begin{array}{c} \dot{a} \\ \dot{b} \end{array}\right)=-\frac{\gamma \hbar}{2}\left(\begin{array}{cc} B_{0} & B_{ rf } e^{i \omega t} \\ B_{ rf } e^{-i \omega t} & -B_{0} \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} a \\ b \end{array}\right)=- \frac{\gamma \hbar}{2}\left(\begin{array}{c} B_{0} a+B_{ rf } e^{i \omega t} b \\ B_{ rf } e^{-i \omega t} a-B_{0} b \end{array}\right) \quad \Rightarrow \)

\( \left\{\begin{array}{l} \dot{a}=i \frac{\gamma}{2}\left(B_{0} a+B_{ rf } e^{i \omega t} b\right)=\frac{i}{2}\left(\Omega e^{i \omega t} b+\omega_{0} a\right) \\ \dot{b}=-i \frac{\gamma}{2}\left(B_{0} b-B_{ rf } e^{-i \omega t} a\right)=\frac{i}{2}\left(\Omega e^{-i \omega t} a-\omega_{0} b\right) \end{array}\right. \).

(c) You can decouple the equations by differentiating with respect to t, but it is simpler just to check the quoted results. First of all, they clearly satisfy the initial conditions: \( a(0)=a_{0} \text { and } b(0)=b_{0} \). Differentiating a:

\( \dot{a}=\frac{i \omega}{2} a+\left\{-a_{0} \frac{\omega^{\prime}}{2} \sin \left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right)+\frac{i}{\omega^{\prime}}\left[a_{0}\left(\omega_{0}-\omega\right)+b_{0} \Omega\right] \frac{\omega^{\prime}}{2} \cos \left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right)\right\} e^{i \omega t / 2} \).

\( =\frac{i}{2} e^{i \omega t / 2}\left\{\omega a_{0} \cos \left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right)+i \frac{\omega}{\omega^{\prime}}\left[a_{0}\left(\omega_{0}-\omega\right)+b_{0} \Omega\right] \sin \left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right)\right. \)

\( \left.+i \omega^{\prime} a_{0} \sin \left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right)+\left[a_{0}\left(\omega_{0}-\omega\right)+b_{0} \Omega\right] \cos \left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right)\right\} \).

Equation 11.124 says this should be equal to

\( \frac{i}{2}\left(\Omega e^{i \omega t} b+\omega_{0} a\right)=\frac{i}{2} e^{i \omega t / 2}\left\{\Omega b_{0} \cos \left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right)+i \frac{\Omega}{\omega^{\prime}}\left[b_{0}\left(\omega-\omega_{0}\right)+a_{0} \Omega\right] \sin \left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right)\right. \)

\( \left.+\omega_{0} a_{0} \cos \left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right)+i \frac{\omega_{0}}{\omega^{\prime}}\left[a_{0}\left(\omega_{0}-\omega\right)+b_{0} \Omega\right] \sin \left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right)\right\} \).

By inspection the \( \cos \left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right) \) terms in the two expressions are equal; it remains to check that

\( i \frac{\omega}{\omega^{\prime}}\left[a_{0}\left(\omega_{0}-\omega\right)+b_{0} \Omega\right]+i \omega^{\prime} a_{0}=i \frac{\Omega}{\omega^{\prime}}\left[b_{0}\left(\omega-\omega_{0}\right)+a_{0} \Omega\right]+i \frac{\omega_{0}}{\omega^{\prime}}\left[a_{0}\left(\omega_{0}-\omega\right)+b_{0} \Omega\right] \).

which is to say

\( a_{0} \omega\left(\omega_{0}-\omega\right)+b_{0} \omega \Omega+a_{0}\left(\omega^{\prime}\right)^{2}=b_{0} \Omega\left(\omega-\omega_{0}\right)+a_{0} \Omega^{2}+a_{0} \omega_{0}\left(\omega_{0}-\omega\right)+b_{0} \omega_{0} \Omega \),

or

\( a_{0}\left[\omega \omega_{0}-\omega^{2}+\left(\omega^{\prime}\right)^{2}-\Omega^{2}-\omega_{0}^{2}+\omega_{0} \omega\right]=b_{0}\left[\Omega \omega-\omega_{0} \Omega+\omega_{0} \Omega-\omega \Omega\right]=0 \).

Substituting Eq. 11.125 for \( \omega^{\prime} \text {, the coefficient of } a_{0} \text { on the left becomes } \)

\(2 \omega \omega_{0}-\omega^{2}+\left(\omega-\omega_{0}\right)^{2}+\Omega^{2}-\Omega^{2}-\omega_{0}^{2}=0 \).

The check of b(t) is identical, with \( a \leftrightarrow b, \omega_{0} \rightarrow-\omega_{0}, \text { and } \omega \rightarrow-\omega \).

(d)

\( b(t)=i \frac{\Omega}{\omega^{\prime}} \sin \left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right) e^{-i \omega t / 2} ; \quad P(t)=|b(t)|^{2}=\left(\frac{\Omega}{\omega^{\prime}}\right)^{2} \sin ^{2}\left(\omega^{\prime} t / 2\right) \).

(e)

The maximum \( \left(P_{\max }=1\right) \text { occurs (obviously) at } \omega=\omega_{0} \).

\( P=\frac{1}{2} \Rightarrow\left(\omega-\omega_{0}\right)^{2}=\Omega^{2} \Rightarrow \omega=\omega_{0} \pm \Omega, \quad \text { so } \quad \Delta \omega=\omega_{+}-\omega_{-}=2 \Omega \).

(f) \( B_{0}=10,000 \text { gauss }=1 T ; \quad B_{ rf }=0.01 \text { gauss }=1 \times 10^{-6} T . \quad \omega_{0}=\gamma B_{0} \). Comparing Eqs. 4.156 and 7.89, \( \gamma=\frac{g_{p} e}{2 m_{n}}, \quad \text { where } \quad g_{p}=5.59 . \) . So

\( \mu=\gamma S \) (4.156).

\( \mu _{p}=\frac{g_{p} e}{2 m_{p}} S _{p}, \quad \mu _{e}=-\frac{e}{m_{e}} S _{e} \) (7.89).

\( \nu_{\text {res }}=\frac{\omega_{0}}{2 \pi}=\frac{g_{p} e}{4 \pi m_{p}} B_{0}=\frac{(5.59)\left(1.6 \times 10^{-19}\right)}{4 \pi\left(1.67 \times 10^{-27}\right)}(1)=4.26 \times 10^{7} Hz \).

\( \Delta \nu=\frac{\Delta \omega}{2 \pi}=\frac{\Omega}{\pi}=\frac{\gamma}{2 \pi} 2 B_{ rf }=\nu_{\text {res }} \frac{2 B_{ rf }}{B_{0}}=\left(4.26 \times 10^{7}\right)\left(2 \times 10^{-6}\right)=85.2 Hz \).
