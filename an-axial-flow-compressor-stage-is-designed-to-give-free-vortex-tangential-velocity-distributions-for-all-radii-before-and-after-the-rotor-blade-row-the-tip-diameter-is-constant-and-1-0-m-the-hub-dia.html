Guest Question.

An axial-flow compressor stage is designed to give free-vortex tangential velocity distributions for all radii before and after the rotor blade row. The tip diameter is constant and 1.0 m; the hub diameter is 0.9 m and constant for the stage. At the rotor tip, the flow angles are as follows:
absolute inlet angle, \(\alpha _{1} =30°\);
relative inlet angle, \(\beta _{1} =60°\);
absolute outlet angle, \(\alpha _{2} =60°\);
relative outlet angle, \(\beta _{2} =30°\).
Determine
a. the axial velocity;
b. the mass flow rate;
c. the power absorbed by the stage;
d. the flow angles at the hub;
e. the reaction ratio of the stage at the hub;
given that the rotational speed of the rotor is 6000 rev/min and the gas density is \(1.5 kg/m^{3} \) , which can be assumed constant for the stage. It can be further assumed that stagnation enthalpy and entropy are constant before and after the rotor row for the purpose of simplifying the calculations.

Admin
Solution

a. The rotational speed, Ω = 2πN/60 = 628.4 rad/s. Therefore, blade tip speed, \(U_{t} =\Omega r_{t}=314.2 m/s \) , and blade speed at hub, \(U_{h} =\Omega r_{h}=282.5 m/s \). From the velocity diagram for the stage (e.g., Figure 5.2), the blade tip speed is

\(U_{t}=c_{x}\left(\tan 60°+\tan 30°\right) =2.309 c_{x}\)

Therefore, \(c_{x}=136 m/s\), constant at all radii because the flow is a free vortex.

b. The rate of mass flow, \(\dot{m}=\pi \left(r^{2}_{t} -r^{2}_{h}\right) \rho c_{x}=\pi \left(0.5^{2}-0.45^{2} \right) 1.5\times 136=30.4 kg/s\)

c. The power absorbed by the stage,

\(\dot{W}e=\dot{m}U_{t}\left(c_{\theta 2t} -c_{\theta 1t}\right) \)

\(=\dot{m}U_{t}c_{x}\left(\tan \alpha _{2t} -\tan \alpha _{1t} \right)\)

\(=30.4\times 314.2\times 136\left(\sqrt{3}-1/\sqrt{3} \right) \)

\(=1.5 MW\)

d. At inlet to the rotor tip,

\(c_{\theta 1t}=c_{x}\tan \alpha _{1}=136/\sqrt{3}=78.6 m/s \)

The absolute flow is a free vortex, \(rc_{\theta } \)= constant. Therefore,

\(c_{\theta 1h}=c_{\theta 1t}\left(r_{t}/r_{h}\right) =78.6\times 0.5/0.45=87.3 m/s\).
At outlet to the rotor tip,

\(c_{\theta 2t}=c_{x}\tan \alpha _{2}=136\times \sqrt{3}=235.6 m/s \)

Therefore, \(c_{\theta 2h}=c_{\theta 2t}\left(r_{t}/r_{h}\right) =235.6\times 0.5/0.45=262 m/s\). The flow angles at the hub are

\(\tan \alpha _{1}=c_{\theta 1h}/c_{x}=87.3/136=0.642\)

\(\tan\beta _{1} =U_{h}/c_{x}-\tan \alpha _{1}=1.436\)

\(\tan \alpha _{2}=c_{\theta 2h}/c_{x}=262/136=1.928\)

\(\tan\beta _{2} =U_{h}/c_{x}-\tan \alpha _{2}=0.152\)

Thus, \(\alpha _{1} =32.75°\), \(\beta_{1} =55.15°\), \(\alpha _{2} =62.6°\), \(\beta_{2} =8.64°\) at the hub.

e. The reaction at the hub can be found by several methods. With Eq. (6.9),

\(R=1-\frac{k}{r^{2}} \) (6.9)

\(R=1-k/r^{2} \)

and noticing that, from symmetry of the velocity triangles,

R = 0.5 at \(r=r_{t}\) , then \(k=0.5r^{2}_{t} \)

Therefore,

\(R_{h} =1-0.5\left(0.5/0.45\right) ^{2}=0.382 \)

The velocity triangles will be asymmetric and similar to those in Figure 5.5(b).
