ROTATION WITH CONSTANT ANGULAR ACCELERATION

You have finished watching a movie on Blu-ray and the disc is slowing to a stop. The disc’s angular velocity at t = 0 is 27.5 rad/s, and its angular acceleration is a constant -10.0 rad/\(s^2\). A line PQ on the disc’s surface lies along the +x-axis at t = 0 (Fig. 9.8). (a) What is the disc’s angular velocity at t = 0.300 s? (b) What angle does the line PQ make with the +x-axis at this time?

Admin
Solution

IDENTIFY and SET UP:

The angular acceleration of the disc is constant, so we can use any of the equations derived in this section (Table 9.1).

comparison of linear and Angular Motions
with constant Acceleration

Constant Linear Acceleration		 Fixed-Axis Rotation with
Constant Angular Acceleration
\(a_{x}=\text { constant }\) \(\alpha_{z}=\text { constant }\)
\(v_{x}=v_{0 x}+a_{x} t\) (2.8) \(\omega_{z}=\omega_{0 z}+\alpha_{z} t\) (9.7)
\(x=x_{0}+v_{0 x} t+\frac{1}{2} a_{x} t^{2}\) (2.12) \(\theta=\theta_{0}+\omega_{0 z} t+\frac{1}{2} \alpha_{z} t^{2}\) (9.11)
\(v_{x}^{2}=v_{0 x}^{2}+2 a_{x}\left(x-x_{0}\right)\) (2.13) \(\omega_{z}^{2}=\omega_{0 z}^{2}+2 \alpha_{z}\left(\theta-\theta_{0}\right)\) (9.12)
\(x-x_{0}=\frac{1}{2}\left(v_{0 x}+v_{x}\right) t\) (2.14) \(\theta-\theta_{0}=\frac{1}{2}\left(\omega_{0 z}+\omega_{z}\right) t\) (9.10)

Our target variables are the angular velocity \(w_z\) and the angular displacement θ at t = 0.300 s. Given \(w_{0z}\) = 27.5 rad/s, \(\theta_{0}=0, \text { and } \alpha_{z}=-10.0 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}^{2}\) it’s easiest to use Eqs. (9.7) (\(\omega_{z}=\omega_{0 z}+\alpha_{z} t\)) and (9.11) (\(\theta=\theta_{0}+\omega_{0 z} t+\frac{1}{2} \alpha_{z} t^{2}\)) to find the target variables.

EXECUTE:

(a) From Eq. (9.7) (\(\omega_{z}=\omega_{0 z}+\alpha_{z} t\)) , at t = 0.300 s we have

\(\omega_{z}=\omega_{0 z}+\alpha_{z} t=27.5 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}+\left(-10.0 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}^{2}\right)(0.300 \mathrm{~s})\) = 24.5 rad/s
(b) From Eq. (9.11) (\(\theta=\theta_{0}+\omega_{0 z} t+\frac{1}{2} \alpha_{z} t^{2}\)),

\(\begin{aligned}\theta &=\theta_{0}+\omega_{0 z} t+\frac{1}{2} \alpha_{z} t^{2} \\&=0+(27.5\mathrm{rad}/ \mathrm{s})(0.300 \mathrm{~s})+\frac{1}{2}\left(-10.0 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}^{2}\right)(0.300 \mathrm{~s})^{2} \\&=7.80 \mathrm{rad}=7.80 \mathrm{rad}\left(\frac{1 \mathrm{rev}}{2 \pi \mathrm{rad}}\right)=1.24 \mathrm{rev}\end{aligned}\)

The disc has made one complete revolution plus an additional 0.24 revolution—that is, 360° plus (0.24 rev)(360°/rev) = 87°.
Hence the line PQ makes an angle of 87° with the +x-axis.

 

EVALUATE: Our answer to part (a) tells us that the disc’s angular velocity has decreased, as it should since \(a_z\) < 0. We can use our result for \(w_z\) from part (a) with Eq. (9.12) (\(\omega_{z}^{2}=\omega_{0 z}^{2}+2 \alpha_{z}\left(\theta-\theta_{0}\right)\)) to check our result for θ from part (b). To do so, we solve Eq. (9.12) for θ:

\(\begin{aligned}\omega_{z}^{2} &=\omega_{0 z}^{2}+2 \alpha_{z}\left(\theta-\theta_{0}\right) \\\theta&=\theta_{0}+\left(\frac{\omega_{z}^{2}-\omega_{0 z}^{2}}{2 \alpha_{z}}\right) \\&=0+\frac{(24.5\mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s})^{2}-(27.5 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s})^{2}}{2\left(-10.0\mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}^{2}\right)}=7.80 \mathrm{rad}\end{aligned}\)

This agrees with our previous result from part (b).
