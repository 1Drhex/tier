Because we are considering a low-speed flight condition, the terms related to the Mach number can be ignored; for example, \(\partial C_m/\partial M\) and \(C_{D_u}\), The stability coefficient for the STOL transport are calculated next.

The change in the X force coefficient, \(C_x\) with respect to a change in the forward speed is given by

\(C_{x_{u}}=-(C_{D_u}+2C_{D_0})+C_{T_u}\)

\(C_{D_u}\) is set to 0 and \(C_{T_u}\) is assumed to be equal to \(-C_{D_0}\), as explained in Section 3.6:

\(C_{x_{u}}=-3C_{D_0}=-3(0.057)=-0.171\)

The change in the X-force coefficient, \(C_x\) with respect to a change in angle of attack can be estimated from the following formula:

\(C_{x_\alpha }=C_{L_0}(1-\frac{2 C_{L_\alpha }}{\pi e AR} )\)

 

\(=(0.77)[1-\frac{(2.0)(5.2/rad)}{\pi (0.75) (9.75)} ]= 0.42 /rad\)

The Z-force coefficient, \(C_z\) with respect to a change in forward speed is given by

\(C_{z_{u}}=-(\frac{M^2}{1-M^2} )C_{L_0}-2C_{L_0}\)

where the first term can be neglected due to the low flight speed:

\(C_{z_{u}}=— 2(0.77) = — 1.54\)

The Z-force coefficient,\(C_z\) with respect to a change in angle of attack is given by the expression

\(C_{z_{\alpha }}=-(C_{L_\alpha }+C_{D_0})\) \(=- [5.2 + 0.057]=— 5.26/rad\)

The Z-force coefficient, \(C_z\), with respect to a change time rate of angle of attack \(\dot{\alpha } \), is given by

\(C_{z_{\dot{\alpha }}}=-2C_{L_{\alpha _t} }\eta V_H \frac{d\varepsilon }{d\alpha }\)

The rate of change of the downwash angle with respect to the angle of attack can be estimated using the relationship presented in Section 2.3

\(\frac{d\varepsilon }{d\alpha }=\frac{2C_{L_{\alpha _w}}}{\pi AR_w}=\frac{2(5.2/rad)}{ \pi (9 .75)} = 0.34 \)

and the horizontal tail volume ratio, \(V_H\), is defined as

\(V_H=\frac{l_t S_t}{S\bar{c} }=\frac{( 46 ft)(233 ft^2)}{(965 ft^2)(10.1 ft)} =1.1 \)

The tail efficiency factor, \(\eta \), is assumed to be equal to unity. With this information we can now calculate \(C_{z_{\dot{ \alpha }}}\) :

\(C_{z_{\dot{ \alpha }}}= — 2(3.5/rad)(1.0)(1.1)(0.34)\)

or

\(C_{z_{\dot{ \alpha }}}= — 2.62/rad.\)

The change in the Z-force coefficient, \(C_z\), with respect to a nondimensional pitch rate \(q\bar{c}/2u_0\) is given by

\(C_{z_{q}}= \frac{\partial C_z }{\partial (\frac{q\bar{c}}{2u_0} ) } =-2C_{L_{\alpha_ t }}\eta V_H\)

or

\(C_{z_{q}}=— (2.0)(3.5/rad)(1.1) = — 7.7/rad\)

The Z-force coefficient, \(C_z\) with respect to a change in the elevator angle, \(\delta _e\) is given by

\(C_{z_{\delta _e }}=-C_{L_{\alpha_ t} }\tau \eta \frac{S_t}{S} \)

The flap effectiveness parameter, \(\tau \), can be estimated from Figure 2.21. For the ratio of elevator area to tail plane area, \(S_e/S_t=81.5 ft^2 /233 ft^2 = 0.35\) the flap effectiveness parameter is estimated to be \(\tau =0.55\):

\(C_{z_{\delta _e }}=— (3.5/rad)(0.55)(1.0)(\frac{233 ft^2}{965 ft^2 })= — 0.46/rad \)

The rate of change of the pitch moment coefficient, \(C_m\) with respect to a change speed, u, is given by

\(C_{m_{u}}=\frac{\partial C_m }{\partial M }M_0 \)

For low-speed flight \(\partial C_m /partial M\) can be assumed to be 0 ;therefore,\(C_{m_{u}}=0\).
The rate of change of the pitching moment coefficient, \(C_m\) with respect to a change in angle of attack, \(\alpha \), is given by

\(C_{m_{\alpha }}=C_{L_{\alpha _w} }(\frac{X_{cg}}{\bar{c}}-\frac{X_{ac}}{\bar{c}} )+C_{m_{\alpha _{fus}}}-\eta V_H C_{L_{\alpha_ t}}(1- \frac{d\varepsilon }{d\alpha })\)

The fuselage contribution to \(C_{m_{\alpha }}\) including power effects was given as \(C_{m\alpha _{fus}} =0.93/rad\). The wing and tail contribution are added to the fuselage contribution:

\(C_{m_{\alpha }}= (5.2/rad)(0.4 — 0.25) + 0.93 — (1.0)(1.1)(3.5/rad)(1 — 0.34)= — 0.83/rad\)

The stability coefficients \(C_{m_{\dot{ \alpha }}}, C_{m_{q }}, and C_{m_{\delta _e }}\) are related to the corresponding Z-force coefficients times the ratio of the tail moment over the wing mean chord. For example,

\(C_{m_{\dot{ \alpha }}}=C_{z_{\dot{ \alpha } }}\frac{l_t}{\bar{c} }= ( — 2.62/rad)(4.55) = — 11.92/rad \)

 

\(C_{m_{q }}=C_{z_{q }}\frac{l_t}{\bar{c} } = ( — 7.7/rad)(4.55)= — 35/rad\)

 

\(C_{m_{\delta _e }}=C_{z_{\delta _e }}\frac{l_t}{\bar{c} }= ( — 0.46/rad)(4.55) = — 2.09/rad\)

The dimensional derivatives \(X_u , X_\alpha \) and the like can be estimated from the formulas in Tables 3.5 and 3.6.

To complete this problem we need to multiply each stability coefficient by the appropriate parameter. The parameters included in the dimensional derivatives are \(QS/m, QS/(mu_0), (\bar{c} /2_{ u_0})QS/m, QS\bar{c}/I_y or (\bar{c}/2_{u_0}) QS\bar{c}/I_y\) These quantities are calculated next:

\(m=W/g = 40,000 lb/32.2 ft/s^2 = 1242 slugs\)

 

\(Q=\frac{1}{2}\rho u_0^2 = (0.5)(0.0238 slug/ft^3)(215 ft/s)^2 =5-5 lb/ft^2 \)

 

\(QS/m = (55 lb/ft^2)(975 ft^2)/(1242 slugs) = 43 ft/s^2\)

 

\(QS/(mu_0)= (43 ft/s)/(215 ft/s) = 0.2/s\)

 

\(\bar{c} /(2 u_0)=(10.1 ft)/[2(215 ft/s)] = 0.023 s\)

 

\(QS\bar{c}/I_y = (55 lb/ft^2)(975 ft^2)(10.1 ft)/(215,000 slug-ft^2)\) \(QS\bar{c}/I_y = 2.52/s^2\)

 

\((\frac{\bar{c}}{2 u_0} )QS\bar{c}/I_y= (0.023 s) (2.52/s^2) = 0.058/s\)
