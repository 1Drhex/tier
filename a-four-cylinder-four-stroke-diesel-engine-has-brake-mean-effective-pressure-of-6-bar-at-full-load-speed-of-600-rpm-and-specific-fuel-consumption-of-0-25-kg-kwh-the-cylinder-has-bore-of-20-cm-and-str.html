Guest Question.

A four cylinder, four stroke diesel engine has brake mean effective pressure of 6 bar at full load speed of 600 rpm and specific fuel consumption of 0.25 kg/kWh. The cylinder has bore of 20 cm and stroke length of 30 cm. The air fuel ratio is measured as 26 from the exhaust gas analysis. The ambient conditions are 1 bar, 27ºC. Assuming the calorific value of fuel as 43 MJ/kg determine the brake thermal efficiency and the volumetric efficiency. Also find out brake power.

Admin
Solution
\(\begin{aligned}\text{Brake thermal efficiency}&= \frac{3600}{\text{(brake specific fuel consumption × Calorific value)}}\\&= \frac{3600}{(0.25× 43×10^{3})} = \text{0.3349 or 33.49}\%\\\text{Brake thermal efficiency}&= \textbf{33.49}\pmb{\%}\\\text{Brake power}&= \frac{4 × 6 × 10^{2} × 0.30 × \left\lgroup\frac{\pi}{4} × (0.20)^{2} \right\rgroup × 600}{60} = 226.19 kW\\\text{Brake power}&= \textbf{226.19 kW}\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Brake specific fuel consumption}&= \frac{m_{f}}{\text{brake power}}\\0.25&= \frac{m_{f}}{226.19} ⇒ m_{f} = 56.55 kg/hr\\\text{Air consumption from given air fuel ratio}&= 26 × 56.55 = 1470.3 kg/hr\\\text{or }\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad&\\m_{a}&= 24.505 kg/min\\\text{Using perfect gas equation,}\qquad\qquad\qquad&\\P · V_{a}&= m_{a} RT\\1 × 10^{2} × V_{a}&= 24.505 × 0.287 × 300\\V_{a}&= 21.098 m^{3}/min\\\text{Swept volume, }V_{s}&=\frac{\pi}{4} D^{2} L = \frac{\pi}{4} × (0.30)^{2} × 0.4 = 0.02827 m^{3}\\\text{Volumetric efficiency, η}_{vol}&= \frac{V_{a}}{V_{s} × \frac{N}{2} × \text{number or cylinders}}\\&= \frac{21.09 × 2}{0.02827 × 600 × 4}\\&=\text{0.6217 or 62.17}\\\text{Volumetric efficiency}&=\textbf{62.17}\pmb{\%}\end{aligned}\)
