Guest Question.

In a Hartnell governor, the lengths of ball and sleeve arms of a bell crank lever are 120 mm and 100 mm respectively. The distance of the fulcrum of the bell crank lever from the governor axis is 140 mm. Each governor ball has a mass of 4 kg. The governor runs at a mean speed of 300 r.p.m. with the ball arms vertical and sleeve arms horizontal. For an increase of speed of 4 per cent, the sleeve moves 10 mm upwards. Neglecting friction, find :
1. the minimum equilibrium speed if the total sleeve movement is limited to 20 mm, 2. the spring stiffness, 3. the sensitiveness of the governor, and 4. the spring stiffness if the governor is to be isochronous at 300 r.p.m.

Admin
Solution

Given : x = 120 mm = 0.12 m ; y = 100 mm = 0.1 m ; r = 140 mm = 0.14 m ;m = 4 kg ; N = 300 r.p.m. or \(\omega=2 \pi \times 300 / 60=31.42 rad / s ; h_{1}=10 mm =0.01 m\) ; h = 20 mm = 0.02 m

1. Minimum equilibrium speed

Let \(N_{1}\)= Minimum equilibrium speed,

\(r_{1}\) = Radius of rotation in the minimum position, i.e. when the sleeve moves downward, and

\(r_{2}\) = Radius of rotation in the maximum position, i.e. when the sleeve moves upward.

Since the increase in speed is 4%, therefore maximum speed,

\(N_{2}=N+0.04 N=1.04 N=1.04 \times 300=312 \text { r.p.m. }\)

 

or \(\omega_{2}=2 \pi \times 312 / 60=32.7 rad / s\)

We know that lift of the sleeve for the maximum position,

\(h_{2}=h-h_{1}=0.02-0.01=0.01 m\)

Now for the minimum position,

\(\frac{h_{1}}{y}=\frac{r-r_{1}}{x} \quad \text { or } \quad r_{1}=r-h_{1} \times \frac{x}{y}=0.14-0.01 \times \frac{0.12}{0.1}=0.128 m\)

Similarly for the maximum position,

\(\frac{h_{2}}{y}=\frac{r_{2}-r}{x} \quad \text { or } \quad r_{2}=r+h_{2} \times \frac{x}{y}=0.14+0.01 \times \frac{0.12}{0.1}=0.152 m\)

We know that centrifugal force in the mean position,

\(F_{ C }=m \cdot \omega^{2} \cdot r=4(31.42)^{2} 0.14=553 N\)

Centrifugal force in the minimum position,

\(F_{ Cl }=m\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2} r_{2}=4\left(\frac{2 \pi N_{1}}{60}\right)^{2} 0.128=0.0056\left(N_{1}\right)^{2}\) …(i)

and centrifugal force in the maximum position,

\(F_{ C 2}=m \cdot\left(\omega_{2}\right)^{2} r_{2}=4(32.7)^{2} 0.152=650 N\)

We know that centrifugal force at any instant,

\(F_{ C }=F_{ Cl }+\left(F_{ C 2}-F_{ Cl }\right)\left(\frac{r-r_{1}}{r_{2}-r_{1}}\right)\)

 

\(553=F_{ Cl }+\left(650-F_{ Cl }\right)\left(\frac{0.14-0.128}{0.152-0.128}\right)=0.5 F_{C 1}+325\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(F_{ Cl }=\frac{553-325}{0.5}=456 N\) …(ii)

 

From equations (i) and (ii),

\(\left(N_{1}\right)^{2}=\frac{456}{0.0056}=81428 \quad \text { or } \quad N_{1}=285.4 \text { r.p.m. }\)

 

2. Spring stiffness

Let \(S_{1} \text { and } S_{2}\) = Spring force at the minimum and maximum position.

Neglecting the effect of obliquity of arms, we have for the minimum position,

\(\frac{M \cdot g+S_{1}}{2} \times y=F_{ Cl } \times x \quad \text { or } \quad S_{1}=2 F_{ Cl } \times \frac{x}{y}=2 \times 456 \times \frac{0.12}{0.1}=1094.4 N\) \(\cdots(\because M=0)\)

and for the maximum position,

\(\frac{M \cdot g+S_{2}}{2} \times y=F_{ C 2} \times x \quad \text { or } \quad S_{2}=2 F_{ C 2} \times \frac{x}{y}=2 \times 650 \times \frac{0.12}{0.1}=1560 N\)

We know that spring stiffness,

\(s=\frac{S_{2}-S_{1}}{h}=\frac{1560-1064.4}{20}=23.28 N / mm\)

 

3. Sensitiveness of the governor

We know that sensitiveness of the governor

\(=\frac{2\left(N_{2}-N_{1}\right)}{N_{1}+N_{2}}=\frac{2(312-285.4)}{285.4+312}=0.089 \text { or } 8.9 \%\)

 

4. Spring stiffness for the governor to be isochronous at 300 r.p.m.

The governor is isochronous, when \(N=N_{1}=N_{2}=300 \text { r.p.m. or } \omega=\omega_{1}=\omega_{2}=31.42 rad / s\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(F_{C 1}=m \cdot \omega^{2} \cdot r_{1}=4(31.42)^{2} 0.128=505.5 N\)

 

and \(F_{ C 2}=m \cdot \omega^{2} \cdot r_{2}=4(31.42)^{2} 0.152=600 N\)

 

We know that \(S_{1}=2 F_{ Cl } \times \frac{x}{y}=2 \times 505.5 \times \frac{0.12}{0.1}=1213 N\)

 

and \(S_{2}=2 F_{ C 2} \times \frac{x}{y}=2 \times 600 \times \frac{0.12}{0.1}=1440 N\)

 

\(\therefore \) Spring stiffness, \(s=\frac{S_{2}-S_{1}}{h}=\frac{1440-1213}{20}=11.35 N / mm\)
