Guest Question.

The single-phase full-bridge fully controlled thyristor rectifier of Fig. 5.1(a) is
used for charging a battery bank of 95 V which has an internal resistance of
0.25 Ω. The rms input voltage is 220 V and the load inductance is large enough
so that the load current can be considered pure dc of 10 A. Calculate the following:
a) The firing angle α for the particular application.
b) The power factor.
c) The energy stored in the battery bank.

Admin
Solution

Solution

a) Using Eq. (5.16) the firing angle can be calculated as follows:

 

\(\overline{ V }_{ o }=\frac{1}{\pi} \int_{\alpha}^{\pi+\alpha} \sqrt{2} \widetilde{ V }_{ i } \sin (\omega t ) d (\omega t )=\frac{2 \sqrt{2} \widetilde{ V }_{ i }}{\pi} \cos \alpha=0.9 \widetilde{ V }_{ i } \cos \alpha\) (5.16)

 

\(\overline{ V }_{ o }=\frac{2 \sqrt{2}}{\pi} 220 \cos \alpha= V _{ bat }+ R \overline{ I }_{ o }=95+(0.25)(10)=97.5 V\)

 

and solving for \(\alpha=60.5^{\circ}\).

b) Output active power \(= V _{ bat } \overline{ I }_{ o }+ R \overline{ I }_{ o }^{2}=(95)(10)+(0.25)(100)=975 W\) Taking into consideration that the output current is pure dc of 10 A value, the input current is a square wave with 10 A amplitude and \(180^{\circ}\) pulse width. Therefore, the rms input current is:

 

\(\widetilde{I}_{i}=\left[\frac{1}{2 \pi}\left(\int_{0^{\circ}}^{180^{\circ}} \bar{I}_{o}^{2} d (\omega t)+\int_{180^{\circ}}^{360^{\circ}}\left(-\bar{I}_{o}\right)^{2} d (\omega t)\right)\right]^{1 / 2}=10 A\)

 

Assuming that the rectifier is ideal (i.e., it has zero power losses):

Input active power = Output active power = 975W.

Therefore, the power factor is: \(\lambda=\frac{ P _{ i }}{\widetilde{ V }_{ i } \widetilde{ I }_{ i }}=\frac{975}{(220)(10)}=0.44\)

c) The energy stored in the battery bank is:

\(975 W -\text { power losses in the } 0.25 \Omega \text { resistance }=975 W -0.25 \times 100=950 W \text {. }\)
