Guest Question.

Consider the CE amplifier shown in Fig. 9.9. Assume that the transistor has a dc current gain hFE =100 and an ac current gain hfe =150.

(a) Determine the quiescent collector current ICQ and collector-to-emitter voltage VCEQ for Re2 = 0 and Re2 = 470 Ω. Also compute the values of r′e corresponding to Re2 = 0 and Re2 = 470 Ω.

(b) Using the result of part-(a) for r′e with Re2 = 0, determine the output voltage V0 , overall gain of the amplifier, gain by the transistor, input resistance at the base, total input resistance, overall current gain and output resistance of the circuit under consideration.

(c) For Re2 ≠ 0, find the minimum value of Re2 required to be connected in the circuit so that Re2 ≥ 10 r′e = Re2 (min). Also compare the overall gains of the circuit corresponding to Re2 = 0 and Re2 = Re2 (min) = 10 r′e .

(d) If amplifier is to operate over a frequency range from 2 kHz to 10 kHz of an input signal, determine the minimum values of the capacitors Cb, Cc and Ce for part (b).

Admin
Solution

(a) Since the capacitors are open-circuited under dc conditions, the dc equivalent circuit can be drawn as shown in 9.9b which can also be drawn in the simplified form as shown in Fig. 9.9c where

\(V_{BB}=\frac{R_{2}V_{CC}}{R_{1}+R_{2}}=\frac{10K\times 12V}{10K+33K}=2.79 \) V

and

\(R_{b}=\frac{R_{1}R_{2}}{R_{1}+R_{2}}=\frac{33K\times 10V}{10K+33K}=7.67 \) K

Note that the base current can be given by

\(I_{BEQ}=\frac{V_{BB}-V_{BEQ}}{R_{b}+h_{FE}(R_{e1}+R_{e2})} \) ( 9.49 )

Thus, the collector current is given by

\(I_{CQ}=h_{FE}I_{BEQ}=\frac{h_{FE}(V_{BB}-V_{BEQ})}{R_{b}+h_{FE}(R_{e1}+R_{e2})} \) ( 9.50 )

For Re2 = 0, the collector current is obtained from Eq. (9.50) as

\(I_{CQ}=\frac{100(2.79V-0.7V)}{7.67K+100(1K+0)}=1.94 \) mA

Similarly, the collector current for Re2 = 470 Ω is obtained as

\(I_{CQ}=\frac{100(2.79V-0.7V)}{7.67K+100(1K+470\Omega )}=1.35 \) mA

The quiescent collector-to-emitter voltage VCEQ can be obtained by applying the KVL in the collector-emitter circuit of Fig. 9.9c which gives

VCEQ = VCC – ICQ (Rc + Re1 + Re2 ) ( 9.51 )

where we have assumed IEQ ≈ ICQ to obtain the above equation.

Using ICQ =1.94 mA, Rc = 2.2 K, Re2 = 1 K, Re2 = 0 , and VCC = 12 V in Eq. (9.51), we obtain VCEQ for Re2 = 0 as

VCEQ = 12 V – 1.94 mA × (2.2 K + 1 K + 0) = 5.79 V

Similarly, ICQ = 1.35 mA, Rc = 2.2 K, Re2 = 1 K, Re2 = 470 Ω , and VCC = 12 V in Eq. (9.51), we obtain VCEQ for Re2 = 470 Ω as

VCEQ = 12 V – 1.35 mA × (2.2 K + 1 K + 470 Ω) = 7.05 V

Since, IEQ ≈ ICQ , we obtain

\(r^{′}_{e}=\frac{26 mV}{I_{EQ}} \approx \frac{26 mV}{I_{CQ}} \) ( 9.52 )

Using, ICQ = 1.94 mA for Re2 = 0, the value of r′e for Re2 = 0 is obtained from Eq. (9.52) as

\(r^{′}_{e}=\frac{26 mV}{1.94 mA} =13.40 \Omega \)

Similarly, r′e for Re2 = 470 Ω can be obtained by putting ICQ = 1.35 mA in Eq. (9.52) as

\(r^{′}_{e}=\frac{26 mV}{1.35 mA} =19.250 \Omega \)

(b) Comparing the given circuit with the generalized amplifier circuit of 9.7a, we can observe that the generalized circuit will represent the given circuit if we consider RL2 ∞ in Fig. 9.7a. Thus, we can use R1 = 33 K, R2 = 10 K, Rs = 600 Ω , Rc = 2.2 K, Re1 = 1 K, Re2 = 0, RL = RL1 = 47 K, RL2 = ∞ and Vs = 10 mV (rms) in the equations presented in Sec. 9.3 to obtain the amplifier parameters for CE configuration from the generalized amplifier circuit equations as follows.

From Eq. (9.20), \(R_{l1c}=R_{c} || R_{L1}=\frac{R_{c}R_{L1}}{R_{c}+R_{L1}} \) and \(R_{l2e}=R_{e2} || R_{L2}=\frac{R_{e2}R_{L2}}{R_{e2}+R_{L2}} \) we get

\(R_{l1c}=\frac{2.2K\times 47K}{2.2K+47K} =2.1 K \) and \(R_{l2e} =R_{e2}=0 \)

Using r′e =13.4 Ω , hfe = 150, \(R_{l2e} = 0 \), Vs = 10 mV,

R1 || R2 = \(\frac{33K\times 10K}{33K+10K} =7.67 \) K , Rth = Rs || R1 || R2 = \(\frac{1}{1/600\Omega +1/33K+1/10K} =556 \Omega \) , and

\(V_{th}=\frac{R_{1}||R_{2}}{R_{s}+R_{1}||R_{2}} V_{s}=\frac{7.67K}{600\Omega +7.67K}\times (10mV)=9.27 \) mV in Eq. (9.21), \(I_{b}=\frac{V_{th}}{R_{th}+h_{fe}(r^{′}_{e}+R_{l2e})}=\frac{1}{R_{th}+h_{fe}(r^{′}_{e}+R_{l2e})}\frac{(R_{1}||R_{2})}{R_{s}+(R_{1}||R_{2})} V_{s} \) , the ac base current is obtained as

\(I_{b}=\frac{9.27 mV}{556\Omega +150\times (13.4\Omega +0)}=3.61 \mu A \)

From Eq. (9.22), \(V_{01}=-\left[\frac{h_{fe}}{R_{th}+h_{fe}(r^{′}_{e}+R_{l2e})}\frac{R_{c}R_{L1}}{R_{c}+R_{L1} }\frac{(R_{1}||R_{2})}{R_{s}+(R_{1}||R_{2})} \right] V_{s}\) , the output voltage of the circuit is determined as

V0 = V01 = – 2.1 K × 150 × 3.61 μA = – 1.13 V

Thus, the overall voltage gain of the circuit is

\(\left|A_{Vs}\right| = \left| \frac{V_{0}}{V_{s}} \right| =\frac{1.13 V}{10mV} =113\)

The gain of the transistor can be obtained from Eq. (9.27) , \(A_{v1} =-\frac{R_{l1c}}{(r^{′}_{e}+R_{l2e})} \) , as

\(\left|A_{v}\right| =\left|A_{v1}\right| =\frac{R_{l1c}}{r^{′}_{e}+0} =\frac{2.1K}{13.4\Omega }\approx 156 \)

Note that AVs < Av. This implies that the input signal Vs is attenuated by the input circuit in between the source and base of the transistor.

The input resistance at the base is determined from Eq. (9.29) , \(R_{in}(base)=h_{fe} \left\lgroup r^{′}_{e}+ \frac{R_{e2}R_{L2}}{R_{e2}+R_{L2}} \right\rgroup \) , as

Rin (base) = 150 × 13.4 Ω ≈ 2 K

Now, the total input resistance is obtained from Eq. (9.30) , \(R_{in}(total)= R_{1}||R_{2}||R_{in}(base)=\frac{R_{1}R_{2}R_{in}(base)}{R_{2}R_{in}(base)+R_{1}R_{in}(base)+R_{1}R_{2}} \) , as

\( R_{in}(total)=\left\lgroup\frac{1}{33K}+\frac{1}{10K}+\frac{1}{2K} \right\rgroup ^{-1}=1.58 \) K

The overall current gain can be obtained from Eq. (9.33) , \(A_{Isc}\approx A_{Ise}=\frac{h_{fe}(R_{s}+R_{in}(total))}{r_{th}+h_{fe}(r^{′}_{e}+R_{l2e})}\frac{(R_{1}||R_{2})}{R_{s}+(R_{1}||R_{2})} \) , as

\(A_{Is}=\frac{150(600\Omega +1.58K)}{556\Omega +150(13.4\Omega +0) }\frac{7.67K}{600\Omega+7.67 K}\approx 118 \)

From Eq. (9.35), the output resistance is the same as the collector resistance Rc and hence we get

Rout = Rout1 = Rc = 2.2 K

(c) Since Re2 ≥ 10 r′e = \(\frac{10\times 26mV}{I_{EQ}}\approx \frac{260mV}{I_{CQ}} \) , from Eq. (9.50) we obtain

\(R_{e2}\geq \left[\frac{260mV}{h_{FE}(V_{BB}-V_{BEQ})} \right] \left(R_{b}+h_{FE}(R_{e1}+R_{e2})\right)=aR_{b}+a h_{FE}(R_{e1}+R_{e2}) \)

or \((1-ah_{FE})R_{e2}\geq a(R_{b}+h_{FE}R_{e1}) \)

which gives

\(R_{e2} \geq \left\lgroup\frac{a}{1-ah_{FE}} \right\rgroup \left(R_{b}+h_{FE}R_{e1}\right) =R_{e2 min}\) ( 9.53 )

where

\(a=\frac{260mV}{h_{FE}(V_{BB}-V_{BEQ})} \) ( 9.54 )

Eq. (9.54) gives \(a=\frac{260mV}{100(2.79V-0.7V)}=0.0012 \)

Putting a = 0.0012 in Eq. (9.53), the minimum value of Re2 is obtained as

\(R_{e2 min}=\left\lgroup\frac{0.0012}{1-0.0012\times 100} \right\rgroup(7.67K+100\times 1K)=153 \Omega \)

Clearly , \(r^{′}_{e}=\frac{R_{e2min}}{10}\frac{153\Omega }{10}\approx 15.3\Omega \)

Using Re2 = 153 Ω and RL2 = ∞ in Eq. (9.20) we get

Rl2e = Re2 = Re2 min = 153 Ω

The overall voltage gain of the amplifier is calculated from Eq. (9.24) , \(A_{V1s} =-\frac{h_{fe}}{R_{th}+h_{fe}(r^{′}_{e}+R_{l2e})}\frac{R_{c}R_{L1}}{R_{c}+R_{L1} }\frac{(R_{1}||R_{2})}{R_{s}+(R_{1}||R_{2})}\) ,as

\(\left|A^{′}_{Vs}\right| =\left|A_{V1s}\right| =\frac{150}{556\Omega +150(15.3\Omega +153\Omega )}\times 4.27K\times \frac{7.67K}{600\Omega +7.67K}\approx 23 \)

It is interesting to note that the overall voltage gain is drastically reduced from 113 to 23 when Re2 = 153 Ω is connected in series with the emitter. Note that by neglecting r′e in Eq. (9.24), the overall gain becomes

\(A^{′}_{Vs} =A_{V1s} =\frac{150}{556\Omega +150\times 153\Omega }\times 4.27K\times \frac{7.67K}{600\Omega +7.67K}\approx 25 \)

which is very close to 23 .

Since \(r^{′}_{e} =\frac{V_{T}}{I_{EQ}}=\frac{T}{11600 I_{EQ}} \) where VT is the thermal voltage, the value of r′e is not only dependent on the quiescent

emitter current but also on the operating temperature of the transistor. As a matter of fact, the gain of the amplifier becomes unstable due to the temperature variation caused by the heat power dissipation in the device. However, by connecting a resistor Re2 ≥ 10 r′e in series with the emitter, the effect of r′e on the gain can be minimized. Such a CE amplifier is commonly called a swamped amplifier as discussed earlier.

(d) Using Rs = 600 Ω, fs min = 2 × 103 Hz and Rin (total) = 1.58 K in Eq. (9.39), \(C_{b}\geq \frac{1.59}{f_{s min}(R_{s}+R_{in}(total))}=C_{b min} \) , the minimum value Cb min of the blocking capacitor Cb is obtained as

\(C_{b min}=\frac{1.59}{2\times 10^3Hz\times (600\Omega +1.58K)}=0.36 \mu F \)

Substituting Rc = 2.2 K, fs min = 2 × 103 Hz and RL1 = RL = 47 K in Eq. (9.43), \(C_{c}\geq \frac{1.59}{f_{s min}(R_{c}+R_{L1})}=C_{c min} \) , the minimum value Cc min of the coupling capacitor Cc is obtained as

\(C_{c min}=\frac{1.59}{2\times 10^3Hz\times (2.2K +47K)}=16.15 \) nF

The minimum value Ce1 min of the bypass capacitor Ce1 is obtained from Eq. (9.48) \(C_{e1}\geq \frac{1.59}{f_{s min}R_{e1}}=C_{e1 min} \) ,as

\(C_{e1 min}=\frac{1.59}{2\times 10^3Hz\times 1K}=0.8 \mu F \)
