Guest Question.

Consider a heat-generating chip that is 12 × 12 \(mm^{2} \)in cross-section. The chip is very thin and can be considered to be spatially isothermal. It is mounted on a substrate made of aluminum (k = 205 W/mK) that is 10 mm thick. The dimensions of the substrate are the same as the chip. A joint made of resin with a thickness of 0.04 mm holds the chip and the substrate together. The joint has a thermal conductivity of 2 W/mK on both ends there is a convective heat transfer for coeffcient of 100 \(W/m^{2}K \)with an ambient at \(30^{\circ }C \).

1. If the heat ﬂux from the chip is 10,000 \(W/m^{2} \) , what is the maximum temperature in the assembly, and where does it occur?
2. If the joint offers an additional resistance (known as thermal contact resistance)of

\(0.8\times 10^{-4}m^{2}K/W \) , what is the maximum temperature in the assembly?

3. If a designer says that one can power the chip all the way up to a maximum of \(85^{\circ}C \), what is the maximum possible heat ﬂux or power?

Solution

Schematic representation, as given in the problem is shown in Fig. 2.16

1. When there is no thermal contact resistance, the heat ﬂux \(q_{1} \) shown in the fgure is given by

\(q_{1} =\frac{T_{chip}-30 }{\frac{1}{100} } \)

Similarly, \(q_{2} =\frac{T_{chip}-30 }{\frac{10\times 10^{-3} }{205} +\frac{0.04\times 10^{-3} }{2}+\frac{1}{100} } \approx \frac{T_{chip}-30}{0.01} \)

 

\(q=q_{1} +q_{2} = \frac{T_{chip}-30}{0.01}+\frac{T_{chip}-30}{0.01}\)

 

\(10000=2\times \left\lgroup\frac{T_{chip}-30}{0.01}\right\rgroup \)

 

\(T_{chip} =\left\lgroup\frac{10000}{2} \right\rgroup \times 0.01+30=80^{\circ } C\)

 

\(q_{2} =\frac{80-30}{0.01} =5000=\frac{T_{chip}-30}{\frac{0.04\times 10^{-3} }{2} } \)

 

\(T_{int} =79.9^{\circ }C \)

Thus the maximum temperature in the assembly is \(80^{\circ } C\) at the interface between chip and joint.

2. When considering a fnite thermal contact resistance

\(q_{2} =\frac{T_{chip}-30 }{\frac{10\times 10^{-3} }{205} +\frac{0.04\times 10^{-3} }{2}+0.8\times 10^{-4}+\frac{1}{100} } \)

 

\(q_{2} =\frac{T_{chip}-30 }{0.0101}\)

 

\(q=q_{1} +q_{2} = \frac{T_{chip}-30}{0.01}+\frac{T_{chip}-30}{0.0101}\)

 

\(10000=10T_{chip} -3000+99T_{chip} -2970.3\)

 

\(T_{chip}=80.25^{\circ } C\)

As expected, the addition of contact resistance increases the temperature, but its effect in this case is negligibly small.

3. Maximum allowable heat ﬂux

\(q_{1} =\frac{T_{chip}-30 }{\frac{1}{100} }\)

 

\(q_{1} =100\times (85-20)=5500W/m^{2} \)

 

\(q_{2} =\frac{T_{chip}- 30}{0.0101}=\frac{85-30}{0.0101}=5445.54 W/m^{2} \)

Maximum allowable heat ﬂux , \(q=q_{1} +q_{2} =10945 W/m^{2} \)

 

\(q\approx 10.95kW/m^{2} \).
