Guest Question.

Due to the applied loading, the element at point A on the solid shaft in Fig. 9–18 a is subjected to the state of stress shown. Determine the principal stresses acting at this point.

Admin
Solution

Construction of the Circle. From Fig. 9–18 a ,

\(\sigma_x=-12ksi \quad\quad \sigma_y=0 \quad\quad \tau_{xy}=-6ksi\)

The center of the circle is at

\(\sigma_{avg}=\frac{-12+0}{2}=-6ksi\)

The reference point A(-12, -6) and the center C(-6, 0) are plotted in Fig. 9–18 b . The circle is constructed having a radius of

\(R=\sqrt{(12-6)^2+(6)^2}=8.49ksi \)

Principal Stress. The principal stresses are indicated by the coordinates of points B and D. We have, for \(\sigma_1 > \sigma_2\),

\(\sigma_1=8.49-6=2.49ksi \\ \sigma_2=-6-8.49=-14.5ksi\)

The orientation of the element can be determined by calculating the angle \(2\theta_{p_2}\) in Fig. 9–18 b , which here is measured counterclockwise from CA to CD. It defines the direction \(\theta_{p_2}\) of \(\sigma_2\) and its associated principal plane. We have

\(2\theta_{p_2}=tan^{-1} \frac{6}{12-6}=45.0°\\ \theta_{p_2}=22.5°\)

The element is oriented such that the x′ axis or \(\sigma_2\) is directed 22.5° counterclockwise from the horizontal ( x axis) as shown in Fig. 9 –18 c .
