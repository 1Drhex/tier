Guest Question.

Information about Forces from Collisions

Suppose we drop a basketball (m = 0.63 kg) from a height of 1.0 m above the floor. It falls and rebounds off the floor to a height of 0.90 m. We estimate that the ball is in contact with the floor for 0.10 s based on the length of the sound produced by the bounce.

(a) What is the change in momentum of the ball during the bounce?

(b) What average force is exerted on the ball by the floor?

Admin
Solution

(a) To determine the change in momentum of the basketball, we need to know its velocity just before the bounce and just after the bounce. We can use conservation of energy to find the speed of the ball just before the bounce:

 

\(m g h_{ i }=\frac{1}{2} m v_{ i }^{2}\)

 

\(v_{ i }=\sqrt{2 g h_{ i }}=\sqrt{2\left(9.81 m \cdot s ^{-2}\right)(1.0 m )}=4.43 m \cdot s ^{-1}\)

 

The ball only rebounds to a height of 0.90 m, and the kinetic energy it has just as it leaves the floor gets converted back into gravitational potential energy. Thus,

 

\(m g h_{ f }=\frac{1}{2} m v_{ f }^{2}\)

 

\(v_{ f }=\sqrt{2 g h_{ f }}=\sqrt{2\left(9.81 m \cdot s ^{-2}\right)(0.90 m )}=4.20 m \cdot s ^{-1}\)

 

The velocity before the collision is directed downward, and after the collision it is directed upward. Choosing the y-direction to be up, the change in momentum is thus

 

\(\Delta \vec{p}=\vec{p}_{ f }-\vec{p}_{ i }\)

 

\(=m \vec{v}_{ f }-m \vec{v}_{ i }\)

 

\(=(0.63 kg )\left[4.20 m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{ j }-\left(-4.43 m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{ j }\right)\right]\)

 

\(=2.65 kg \cdot m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{j}+2.79 kg \cdot m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{j}\)

 

\(=5.44 kg \cdot m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{j} \approx 5.4 kg \cdot m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{j}\)

 

(b) It is a straightforward use of Equation 7-5 to find the average force exerted on the ball:

 

\(\overrightarrow{ F }_{ net }^{ avg }=\frac{\Delta \overrightarrow{ p }}{\Delta t}\) {7-5}

 

\(\overrightarrow{ F }_{ net }^{ avg }=\frac{\Delta \vec{p}}{\Delta t}=\frac{5.44 kg \cdot m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{j}}{0.10 s }\)

 

\(=\frac{5.44 kg \cdot m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{j}}{0.10 s }=54.4 kg \cdot m \cdot s ^{-2} \hat{j}=54 N \hat{j}\)

 

Making sense of the result

 

The floor has to push upward on the ball to make it bounce, and we can see that both the change in momentum and the force point upward, as we would expect. The force of gravity on the ball is \(m g=(0.63 kg )\left(9.81 m \cdot s ^{2}\right)=6.18 N\) , and it takes \(t_{\text {fall }}=\sqrt{2 h / g}=\sqrt{2 \cdot 1.0 m / 9.81 m \cdot s ^{-2}}=0.45 s\) for gravity to change the magnitude of the momentum of the ball by \(2.79 kg \cdot m \cdot s ^{-1}\) as the ball falls toward the floor. The floor has to change the magnitude of the momentum of the ball by a little less than twice this amount and it has to do it in a much shorter period of time. The average force we obtained seems quite reasonable in this context.
