Guest Question.

A voltage divider bias is provided to an N-channel JFET circuit as shown in Fig. 5.28. To establish \(I_{DSS} =10mA,V_{P} =-3.5V,R_{1} +R_{2} =120k\Omega , I_{D} =5mA\) and \(V_{DS} =5V\), determine the values of \(R_{1} ,R_{2}\) and \(R_{D}\).

Admin
Solution

Let us assume that the JFET is biased in the saturation region. Then the dc drain current is given by

\(I_{D} =I_{DSS} \left(1-\frac{V_{GS} }{V_{P} } \right) ^{2} \)

 

Therefore, \(5 =10 \left(1-\frac{V_{GS} }{(-3.5)} \right) ^{2} \)

 

By solving, we get \(V_{GS}=-1.008V\)

 

The voltage at the source terminal is

\(V_{S} =I_{D} R_{S} -5=(5)(0.5)-5=-2.5V\)

 

The gate voltage is

\(V_{G} =V_{GS}+V_{S} =-1.008-2.5=-3.508V\)

 

The gate voltage can be written as

\(V_{G} =\left(\frac{R_{2} }{R_{1}+R_{2} } \right) (10)-5\)

 

Therefore, \(-3.508 =\frac{R_{2} }{120 } (10)-5\)

 

i.e. , \( R_{2}=2.984k\Omega \)

 

and \(R_{1} =17.016k\Omega \)

 

The drain-to-source voltage is

\(V_{DS} =5-I_{D} R_{D} -I_{D} R_{S} -(-5)\)

 

Substituting the specified values, we get

\(R_{D} =\frac{10-V_{DS} -I_{D} R_{S}}{I_{D}} =\frac{10-5-(5)(0.5)}{5} =0.5k\Omega \)

 

\(V_{GS} -V_{P} =-1.24-(-3.5)=2.26V\)

 

Here, since \(V_{DS} \gt (V_{GS} -V_{P} )\), the JFET is biased in the saturation region, which satisfies the initial assumption
