Guest Question.

A rail car has a total mass of 4000 kg. The moment of inertia of each wheel together with its gearing is 20 kg · m². The centre distance between the two wheels on an axle is 1.5 m and each wheel is 400 mm radius. Each axle is driven by a motor, the speed ratio between the two being 1:14. Each motor with its gear has a moment of inertia of 15 kg · m² and runs in a direction opposite to that of its axle. The centre of gravity of the car is 1 m above the rails.
Determine the limiting speed for the car when moving on a curve of 250 m radius such that no wheel leaves the rails.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given: } W=1000 N , I_{w}=20 kg \cdot m ^{2}, a=1.5 m , r_{w}=0.4 m , i=3, I_{m}=15 kg \cdot m ^{2}, R=250 m , h=1 m \).

\( \text { Let } N_{w}=\text { rpm of wheel, then speed of motor, } N_{m}=3 N_{w} \).

\( \omega_{w}=\frac{2 \pi \times N_{w}}{60}=\frac{\pi N_{w}}{30} rad / s , \omega_{m}=3 \omega_{w} \).

\( v_{w}=\omega_{w} r_{w}=\frac{\pi N_{w} \times 0.4}{30}=0.041888 N_{w} m / s \).

\( \omega_{p}=\frac{v_{w}}{R}=\frac{\pi N_{w} \times 0.4}{30 \times 250}=1.6755 \times 10^{-4} \times N_{w} rad / s \).

Gyroscopic couple due to four wheels:

\( C_{w}=4 I_{w} \omega_{w} \omega_{p} \).

\( =4 \times 20\left(\frac{\pi N_{w}}{30}\right) \times 1.6755 \times 10^{-4} \times N_{w} \).

\( =14.037 \times 10^{-4} \times N_{w}^{2} Nm \).

Gyroscopic couple due to motor:

\( C_{m}=I_{m} \omega_{m} \omega_{p} \).

\( =15 \times\left(\frac{3 \pi N_{w}}{30}\right) \times 1.6755 \times 10^{-4} \times N_{w} \).

\( =8.8956 \times 10^{-4} \times N_{w}^{2} Nm \).

Total gyroscopic couple:

\( C=C_{w}-C_{m}=6.1414 \times 10^{-4} \times N_{w}^{2} Nm \).

Negative sign has been taken because the speed of motor is opposite to that of wheels. Vertical reaction at each of the outer or inner wheels due to gyroscopic effect,

\( \frac{P}{2}=\pm \frac{C}{2 a} \).

\( =\frac{\pm 6.1414 \times 10^{-4} \times N_{w}^{2}}{2 \times 1.5} \).

\( =\pm 2.04713 \times 10^{-4} \times N_{w}^{2} N \).

\( \text { Centrifugal couple, } C_{c}=\frac{W v_{w}^{2} h}{g R} \).

\( =1000 \times \frac{\left(0.041888 N_{w}\right)^{2} \times 1}{9.81 \times 250} \).

\( =0.7154 \times 10^{-3} \times N_{w}^{2} Nm \).

Vertical reaction at each outer or inner wheel due to centrifugal effect,

\( \frac{Q}{2}=\pm \frac{C_{c}}{2 a} \).

\( =\frac{0.7154 \times 10^{-3} \times N_{w}^{2}}{3} \).

\( =2.3848 \times 10^{-4} \times N_{w}^{2} N \).

For no wheel to leave the rails,

\( W=2(P+Q) \).

\( 1000=4\left(2.04713 \times 10^{-4} \times N_{w}^{2}+2.3848 \times 10^{-4} \times N _{w}^{2}\right) \).

\( =18.72772 \times 10^{-4} \times N_{w}^{2} \).

\( N_{w}=751 rpm \).

\( v_{w}=113.26 km / h \).
