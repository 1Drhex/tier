Guest Question.

A liquor containing 15 per cent solids is concentrated to 55 per cent solids in a doubleeffect evaporator operating at a pressure of 18 kN/\(m^{2}\) in the second effect. No crystals are formed. The feedrate is 2.5 kg/s at a temperature of 375 K with a specific heat capacity of 3.75 kJ/kg K. The boiling-point rise of the concentrated liquor is 6 deg K and the pressure of the steam fed to the first effect is 240 kN/\(m^{2}\). The overall heat transfer coefficients in the first and second effects are 1.8 and 0.63 kW/\(m^{2}\) K, respectively. If the heat transfer area is to be the same in each effect, what areas should be specified?

 

Admin
Solution

Making a mass balance based on a flow of feed of 2.5 kg/s, gives:

Solids (kg/s) Liquor (kg/s) Total (kg/s)
Feed 0.375 2.125 2.5
Product 0.375 0.307 0.682
Evaporation __ 1.818 1.18

Thus: \((D_{1}+D_{2})\) = 1.818 kg/s (i)

At 18 kN/\(m^{2}\) pressure, \(T_{2}\)=331 K and \(T'_{2}\),the temperature of the liquor in the second effect, allowing for the boiling-point rise, = (331 + 6) = 337 K.

At 240 kN/\(m^{2}\) pressure, \(T_{0}\) = 399 K, then:

\(\Delta T_{1}+\Delta T_{2}\) = (399 – 337) = 62 deg K (ii)

From equation 14.8: \(U_{1}\Delta T_{1}=U_{2}\Delta T_{2}\)

Substituting \(U_{1}\) = 1.8 and \(U_{2}\) = 0.63 kW/\(m^{2}\) K, and combining with equation (ii), gives:

\(\Delta T_{1}\)= 16 deg K and \(\Delta T_{2}\)=46 deg K

Thus \(T_{1}\) = (399 – 16) = 383 K and hence the feed enters slightly cooler than the temperature of the liquor in the first effect. Hence \(\Delta T_{1}\) will be slightly greater and the following values will be assumed:

\(\Delta T_{1}\)=17 deg K \(\Delta T_{2}\)=45 deg K

Thus, for steam to 1: \(T_{0}\) = 399 K, \(\lambda _{0}\) = 2185 kJ/kg

For steam to 2: \(T_{1}\) = 382 K, \(\lambda _{1}\) = 2232 kJ/kg

and: \(T_{2}\) = 331 K, \(\lambda _{2}\) = 2363 kJ/kg

Making a heat balance around each effect:

(1) \(D_{0}\lambda _{0}=WC_{p}(T_{1}-T_{f})+D_{1}\lambda _{1}\)

or 2185\(D_{0}\) = (2.5 × 3.75)(382 – 375) + 2232\(D_{1}\) (iii)

(2) \(D_{1}\lambda _{1}+(W-D_{1})C_{p}(T_{1}-T'_{2})=D_{2}\lambda _{2}\)

or 2232\(D_{1}\) + (2.5 – D1)3.75(382 – 337) = 2363\(D_{2}\) (iv)

Solving equations (i), (iii), and (iv) simultaneously:

\(D_{0}\) = 0.924 kg/s, \(D_{1}\) = 0.875 kg/s, and \(D_{2}\) = 0.943 kg/s.

The areas are given by:

\(A_{1}=D_{0}\lambda _{0}/U_{1}\Delta T_{1}\)=(0.924 × 2185)/(1.8 × 17) = 66.1 \(m^{2}\)

\(A_{2}=D_{1}\lambda _{1}/U_{2}\Delta T_{2}\)= (0.875 × 2232)/(0.63 × 45) = 68.8 \(m^{2}\)

which are sufficiently close to justify the assumed values of \(\Delta T_{1}\) and \(\Delta T_{2}\). A total area of 134.9 \(m^{2}\) is required and hence an area of, say, 67.5 \(m^{2}\) would be specified for each effect.
