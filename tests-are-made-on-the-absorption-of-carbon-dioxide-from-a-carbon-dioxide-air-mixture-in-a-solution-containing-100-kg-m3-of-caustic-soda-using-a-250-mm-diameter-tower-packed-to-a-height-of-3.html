Guest Question.

Tests are made on the absorption of carbon dioxide from a carbon dioxide–air mixture in a solution containing 100 kg/\(m^{3}\) of caustic soda, using a 250 mm diameter tower packed to a height of 3 m with 19 mm Raschig rings.
The results obtained at atmospheric pressure were:
Gas rate, G’= 0.34 kg/\(m^{2}\)s.Liquid rate, L’= 3.94 kg/\(m^{2}\)s.
The carbon dioxide in the inlet gas was 315 parts per million and the carbon dioxide in the exit gas was 31 parts per million.
What is the value of the overall gas transfer coefficient \(K_{G}a\)?

Admin
Solution

At the bottom of the tower:

\(y_{1}=315\times 10^{-6}\) , G’= 0.34 kg/\(m^{2}\)s

and: \(G_{m}^{'}\) = (0.34/29) = 0.0117 kmol/\(m^{2}\)s

At the top of the tower: \(y_{2}=31\times 10^{-6}\) , \(x_{2}=0\)

and: L’= 3.94 kg/\(m^{2}\)s

The NaOH, solution contains 100 kg/\(m^{3}\) NaOH.

The mean molecular mass of liquid is:

\(\mathbf{\frac{(100\times 40)+(900\times 18)}{1000}}\)=20.2 kg/kmol

Thus: \(L_{m}^{'}\)=(3.94/20.2) = 0.195 kmol/\(m^{2}\)s

For dilute gases, y = Y and a mass balance over the tower gives:

\(G_{m}^{'}(y_{1}-y_{2})A=K_{G}aP(y-y_{e})_{lm} ZA\)

It may be assumed that as the solution of NaOH is fairly concentrated, there will be a negligible vapour pressure of \(CO_{2}\) over the solution, that is all the resistance to transfer lies in the gas phase.

Therefore the driving force at the top of the tower = \((y_{2}-0)=31\times 10^{-6}\)

and: at the bottom of the tower =\((y_{1}-0)=315\times 10^{-6}\)

The log mean driving force, \((y-y_{e})_{lm}=\mathbf{\frac{(315-31)\times 10^{6}}{\ln (315/31)}}=122.5\times 10^{-6}\)

Therefore: \(0.0117(315-31)10^{-6}=K_{G}a(101.3\times 122.5\times 10^{-6}\times 3)\)

from which: \(K_{G}a=8.93\times 10^{-5}\) kmol/\(m^{3}\)s\((kN/m^{2})\)
