Guest Question.

For the network in Fig. 10.47:
a. Find the mathematical expression for the transient behavior of the voltage across the capacitor if the switch is thrown into position 1 at t = 0 s.

b. Find the mathematical expression for the transient behavior of the voltage across the capacitor if the switch is moved to position 2 at \(t = 1\tau\).

c. Plot the resulting waveform for the voltage \(ν_C\) as determined by parts (a) and (b).

d. Repeat parts (a)–(c) for the current \(i_C\).

Admin
Solution

a. Converting the current source to a voltage source results in the configuration in Fig. 10.48 for the charging phase.

For the source conversion

\(E = IR = (4 mA)(5 kΩ) = 20 V\)

 

and \(R_s = R_p = 5 kΩ\\[0.5cm] \tau=RC = (R_1 + R_3)C = (5 kΩ + 3 kΩ)(10 \mu F) = 80 ms\\[0.5cm] \nu_C=E(1-e^{-t/\tau})=20V(1-e^{-t/80ms})\)

 

b. With the switch in position 2, the network appears as shown in Fig. 10.49. The voltage at 1t can be found by using the fact that the voltage is 63.2% of its final value of 20 V, so that 0.632(20 V) = 12.64 V. Alternatively, you can substitute into the derived equation as follows:

\(e^{-t/\tau}=e^{-\tau/\tau}=e^{-1}=0.368\)

 

and \(\nu_C=20V(1-e^{-t/80ms})=20V(1-0.368)=(20 V)(0.632) = 12.64 V\)

Using this voltage as the starting point and substituting into the discharge equation results in

\(\tau^{\prime }=RC = (R_2 + R_3)C = (1 kΩ + 3 kΩ)(10 \mu F) = 40 ms\\[0.5cm] \nu_C=Ee^{-t/\tau^{\prime }}=12.64e^{-t/40ms}\)

 

c. See Fig. 10.50.

d. The charging equation for the current is

\(i_C=\frac{E}{R}e^{-t/\tau}=\frac{E}{R_1+R_3} e^{-t/\tau}=\frac{20V}{8 kΩ}e^{-t/80ms}=2.5mAe^{-t/80s} \)

which, at t = 80 ms, results in

\(i_C=2.5mAe^{-80s/80s}=2.5mAe^{-1}=(2.5 mA)(0.368) = 0.92 mA \)

When the switch is moved to position 2, the 12.64 V across the capacitor appears across the resistor to establish a current of 12.64 V/4 kΩ = 3.16 mA. Substituting into the discharge equation with \(V_i = 12.64 V and \tau^{\prime}= 40 ms\) yields

\(i_C=-\frac{V_i}{R_2+R_3}e^{-t/\tau^{\prime}}=-\frac{12.64V}{1 kΩ + 3 kΩ}e^{-t/40ms}=-\frac{12.64 V}{4 kΩ} e^{-t/40ms}=-3.16 mAe^{-t/40ms}\)

The equation has a minus sign because the direction of the discharge current is opposite to that defined for the current in Fig. 10.49. The resulting plot appears in Fig. 10.51.
