Single Amounts: Find N, Given P, F, and i

You have just purchased 100 shares of Facebook stock at $60 per share. You will sell the stock when its market price has doubled. If you expect the stock price to increase 20% per year, how long do you anticipate waiting before selling the stock (Figure 3.16)?

Admin
Solution

Given: P = $6,000, F = $12,000, and i = 20% per year.
Find: N (years).
Using the single-payment compound-amount factor, we write

\(F=P\left(1+i\right) ^{N} = P\left(F/P , i , N\right)\)

\(\$12,000 = \$6,000 \left(1+0.20\right) ^{N} = \$6,000 \left(F/P , 20\% , N\right)\)

\(2= \left(1.20\right) ^{N} = \left(F/P , 20\% , N\right)\)

Again, we could use a calculator or Excel to find N.

1. Using a Calculator. Solving for N gives

log 2 = N log 1.20

or

\(N=\frac{\log 2}{\log 1.20}\)

\(= 3.80 \approx 4 years\)

2. Using Excel. Within Excel, the financial function NPER(i,0,P,F) computes the number of compounding periods it will take an investment (P) to grow to a future value (F), earning a fixed interest rate (i) per compounding period. In our example, the Excel command would look like Figure 3.17.

Comments: A very handy rule of thumb, called the Rule of 72, estimates approximately how long it will take for a sum of money to double. The rule states that, to find the time it takes for a present sum of money to grow by a factor of two, we divide 72 by the interest rate. In our example, the interest rate is 20%. Therefore, the Rule of 72 indicates 72/20 = 3.60, or roughly four years, for a sum to double. This is, in fact, relatively close to our exact solution.
