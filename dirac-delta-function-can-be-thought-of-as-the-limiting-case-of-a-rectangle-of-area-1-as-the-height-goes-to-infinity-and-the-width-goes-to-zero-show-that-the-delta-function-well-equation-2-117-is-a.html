Guest Question.

Dirac delta function can be thought of as the limiting case of a rectangle of area 1, as the height goes to infinity and the width goes to zero. Show that the delta-function well (Equation 2.117) is a “weak” potential (even though it is infinitely deep), in the sense that \( z_0→0 \). Determine the bound state energy for the delta-function potential, by treating it as the limit of a finite square well. Check that your answer is consistent with Equation 2.132. Also show that Equation 2.172 reduces to Equation 2.144 in the appropriate limit.

\( V(x)=-\alpha \delta(x) \) (2.117).

\( \psi(x)=\frac{\sqrt{m \alpha}}{\hbar} e^{-m \alpha|x| / \hbar^{2}} ; \quad E=-\frac{m \alpha^{2}}{2 \hbar^{2}} \) (2.132).

\( T^{-1}=1+\frac{V_{0}^{2}}{4 E\left(E+V_{0}\right)} \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{2 a}{\hbar} \sqrt{2 m\left(E+V_{0}\right)}\right) \) (2.172).

\( R=\frac{1}{1+\left(2 \hbar^{2} E / m \alpha^{2}\right)}, \quad T=\frac{1}{1+\left(m \alpha^{2} / 2 \hbar^{2} E\right)} \) (2.144).

Admin
Solution

Equation 2.158

\( z \equiv l a, \quad \text { and } z_{0} \equiv \frac{a}{\hbar} \sqrt{2 m V_{0}} \) (2.158).

\( \Rightarrow z_{0}=\frac{a}{\hbar} \sqrt{2 m V_{0}} \). We want α = area of potential = \( 2aV_0 \) held constant as a → 0. Therefore \( V_{0}=\frac{\alpha}{2 a} ; z_{0}=\frac{a}{\hbar} \sqrt{2 m \frac{\alpha}{2 a}}=\frac{1} {\hbar} \sqrt{m \alpha a} \rightarrow 0 \). So \( z_0 \) is small, and the intersection in Fig. 2.17 occurs at very small z. Solve Eq. 2.159 for very small z, by expanding tan z:

\( \tan z=\sqrt{\left(z_{0} / z\right)^{2}-1} \) (2.159).

\( \tan z \cong z=\sqrt{\left(z_{0} / k\right)^{2}-1}=(1 / z) \sqrt{z_{0}^{2}-z^{2}} \).

Now (from Eqs. 2.149, 2.151 and 2.158)

\( \kappa \equiv \frac{\sqrt{-2 m E}}{\hbar} \) (2.149).

\( l \equiv \frac{\sqrt{2 m\left(E+V_{0}\right)}}{\hbar} \) (2.151).

\( z \equiv l a, \quad \text { and } z_{0} \equiv \frac{a}{\hbar} \sqrt{2 m V_{0}} \) (2.158).

\( z_{0}^{2}-z^{2}=\kappa^{2} a^{2}, \text { so } z^{2}=\kappa a . \text { But } z_{0}^{2}-z^{2}=z^{4} \ll 1 \Rightarrow z \cong z_{0}, \text { so } \kappa a \cong z_{0}^{2} \), But we found that \( z_{0} \cong \frac{1}{\hbar} \sqrt{m \alpha a} \), here, so \( \kappa a=\frac{1}{\hbar^{2}} m \alpha a, \text { or } \kappa=\frac{m \alpha}{\hbar^{2}} \). (At this point the a’s have canceled, and
we can go to the limit a → 0.)

\( \frac{\sqrt{-2 m E}}{\hbar}=\frac{m \alpha}{\hbar^{2}} \Rightarrow-2 m E=\frac{m^{2} \alpha^{2}}{\hbar^{2}} \). \( E=-\frac{m \alpha^{2}}{2 \hbar^{2}} \) (which agrees with Eq. 2.132).

\( \psi(x)=\frac{\sqrt{m \alpha}}{\hbar} e^{-m \alpha|x| / \hbar^{2}} ; \quad E=-\frac{m \alpha^{2}}{2 \hbar^{2}} \)

In Eq. 2.172

\( T^{-1}=1+\frac{V_{0}^{2}}{4 E\left(E+V_{0}\right)} \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{2 a}{\hbar} \sqrt{2 m\left(E+V_{0}\right)}\right) \) (2.172).

\( V_{0} \gg E \Rightarrow T^{-1} \cong 1+\frac{V_{0}^{2}}{4 E V_{0}} \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{2 a}{\hbar} \sqrt{2 m V_{0}}\right) \) . But \( V_{0}=\frac{\alpha}{2 a} \), so the argument of the sine is small, and we can replace sin ε by ε: \( T^{-1} \cong 1+\frac{V_{0}}{4 E}\left(\frac{2 a}{\hbar}\right)^{2} 2 m V_{0}=1+\left(2 a V_{0}\right)^{2} \frac{m}{2 \hbar^{2} E} \). But \( 2 a V_{0}=\alpha, \text { so } T^{-1}=1+\frac{m \alpha^{2}}{2 \hbar^{2} E} \), in agreement with Eq. 2.144.

\( R=\frac{1}{1+\left(2 \hbar^{2} E / m \alpha^{2}\right)}, \quad T=\frac{1}{1+\left(m \alpha^{2} / 2 \hbar^{2} E\right)} \) (2.144).
