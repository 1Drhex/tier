Guest Question.

Determination of Mean Heat Capacity for Air

Use the data available in Appendix A.2 to calculate the mean heat capacity, \(\bar{c}_{P}\), for air between \(T_{1}=298\) K and \(T_{2}=300\) to 1000 K, in intervals of 100 K.

Admin
Solution

Using the defi nition of \(\bar{c}_{P}\) from Equation (2.33):

 

\(\bar{c}_{P}=\frac{\int_{298}^{T} c_{P} d T}{T-298}\) (2.33)

 

\(\bar{c}_{P}=\frac{\Delta h}{(T-298)}=\frac{\int_{298}^{T} c_{P} d T}{T-298}\) (E2.8A)

 

The heat capacity can be integrated with respect to temperature using the parameters in Appendix A.2:

 

\(\int_{T_{1}}^{T_{2}} c_{P} d T=R \int_{298}^{T}\left[A+B T+D T^{-2}\right] d T=R\left[A T+\frac{B}{2} T^{2}-\frac{D}{T}\right]_{298}^{T}\) (E2.8B)

 

where for air:

 

\(A=3.355, \quad B=0.575 \times 10^{-3}, \quad \text { and } D=-0.016 \times 10^{5}\) (E2.8C)

 

The solution to Equation (E2.8A), using (E2.8B) and (E2.8C), is presented at intervals of 100 K in Table E2.8.
