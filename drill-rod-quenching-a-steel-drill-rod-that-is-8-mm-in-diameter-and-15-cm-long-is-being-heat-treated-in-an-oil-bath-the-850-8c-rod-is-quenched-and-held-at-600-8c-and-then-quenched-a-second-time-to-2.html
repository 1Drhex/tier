Drill Rod Quenching A steel drill rod that is 8 mm in diameter and 15 cm long is being heat treated in an oil bath. The 850 ºC rod is quenched and held at 600 ºC and then quenched a second time to 20 ºC. Calculate the quantities of heat that must be removed at the two quenching stages.

We will calculate the quantities of heat that flow from the rod during the two quenching stages by applying Equation (7.7).

 

\(Q=m c\left(T-T_{0}\right)\) (Equation 7.7)

 

Table 5.2 lists the weight density of steel as \(\rho_{w}=76 kN / m ^{3}\), which we will use in calculating the rod’s mass. The specific heat of steel is listed in Table \(7.4 \text { as } 0.50 kJ /\left( kg \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\).

Solution

The volume Vof the rod is calculated based on its length Land crosssectional area A

 

\(\begin{aligned}A &=\pi \frac{(0.008 m )^{2}}{4} \quad \leftarrow\left[A=\pi \frac{d^{2}}{4}\right] \\&=5.027 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

The volume becomes

 

\(\begin{aligned}V &=\left(5.027 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2}\right)(0.15 m ) \leftarrow[V=A L] \\&=7.540 \times 10^{-6} m ^{3}\end{aligned}\)

 

The rod’s weight is therefore

 

\(\begin{aligned}w &=\left(76 \times 10^{3} \frac{ N }{ m ^{3}}\right)\left(7.540 \times 10^{-6} m ^{3}\right) \leftarrow\left[w=\rho_{w} V\right] \\&=0.5730\left(\frac{ N }{ m ^{3}}\right)\left( m ^{3}\right) \\&=0.5730 N\end{aligned}\)

 

and its mass is

 

\(m=\frac{0.5730 N }{9.81 m / s ^{2}} \quad \leftarrow[w=m g]\) \(\begin{aligned}&=5.841 \times 10^{-2}( N )\left(\frac{ s ^{2}}{ m }\right) \\&=5.841 \times 10^{-2}\left(\frac{ kg \cdot m }{ s ^{2}}\right)\left(\frac{ s ^{2}}{ m }\right) \\&=5.841 \times 10^{-2} kg\end{aligned}\)

 

In the last step, we expanded the derived unit newton in terms of the base units meter, kilogram, and second. The quantities of heat removed during the two quenching stages become

 

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{1} &=\left(5.841 \times 10^{-2} kg \right)\left(0.50 \frac{ kJ }{ kg \cdot{ }^{\circ} C }\right)\left(850{ }^{\circ} C -600{ }^{\circ} C \right) \leftarrow\left[Q=m c\left(T-T_{0}\right)\right] \\&=7.301( kg )\left(\frac{ kJ }{ kg \cdot{ }^{\circ} C }\right)\left({ }^{\circ} C \right) \\&=7.301 kJ\end{aligned}\)

 

and

 

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{2} &=\left(5.841 \times 10^{-3} kg \right)\left(0.50 \frac{ kJ }{ kg \cdot{ }^{\circ} C }\right)\left(600^{ p } C -20{ }^{\circ} C \right) \leftarrow\left[Q=m c\left(T-T_{0}\right)\right] \\&=16.94( kg )\left(\frac{ kJ }{ kg \cdot{ }^{\circ} C }\right)\left({ }^{\circ} C \right) \\&=16.94 kJ\end{aligned}\)
