Guest Question.

The 30-cm-diameter hemisphere in Figure Example 8-8 is maintained at a constant temperatureof \( 500^{◦} C \) and insulated on its back side. The surface emissivity is 0.4. The opening exchanges radiant energy with a large enclosure at \(30^{◦} C \). Calculate the net radiant exchange.

Admin
Solution

This is an object completely surrounded by a large enclosure but the inside surface of the sphere is not convex; that is, it sees itself, and therefore we are not permitted to use Equation (8-43a)

\(q=σA_{1} \epsilon _{1} (T ^{4} _{1} −T ^{4} _{2} )\hspace{30 pt} [8-43a] \)

. In the figure we take the inside of the sphere as surface 1 and the enclosure as surface 2.We also create an imaginary surface 3 covering the opening.We actually have a two-surface problem (surfaces 1 and 2) and therefore may use Equation (8-40)

\(q_{net}=\frac{E_{b1} −E_{b2} }{(1−\epsilon _{1} )/\epsilon _{1} A_{1} +1/A_{1} F_{12} +(1−\epsilon _{2} )/\epsilon _{2} A_{2} }= \frac{σ(T ^{4} _{1} −T ^{4} _{2} ) }{(1−\epsilon _{1} )/\epsilon _{1} A_{1} +1/A_{1} F_{12} +(1−\epsilon _{2} )/\epsilon _{2} A_{2} } \hspace{30 pt} [8-40] \)

to calculate the heat transfer. Thus,

\( E_{b1} = σT ^{4} _{1} =σ(773)^{4} =20,241 \; W/m^{2} \)

 

\(E_{b2} = σT^{ 4} _{2} =σ(303)^{4} =478\enspace W/m^{2} \)

 

\(A_{1} = 2πr^{2} =(2)π(0.15)^{2} =0.1414\; m^{2} \)

 

\(\frac{1−\epsilon _{1} }{\epsilon _{1} A_{1} } =\frac{0.6}{(0.4)(0.1414)}=10.61 \)

 

\(A_{2} →∞ \)

so that

\(\frac{1−\epsilon _{2} }{\epsilon _{2} A_{2} }→0 \)

Now, at this point we recognize that all of the radiation leaving surface 1 that will eventually arrive at enclosure 2 will also hit the imaginary surface 3 (i.e., \(F_{12} =F_{13} \)).We also recognize that

\(A_{1} F_{13} =A_{3} F_{31} \)

But,\( F_{31} =1.0\) so that

\(F_{13} =F_{12}=\frac{A_{3} }{A_{1} }=\frac{πr^{2} }{2πr^{2} } =0.5 \)

Then \( 1/A_{1} F_{12} =1/(0.1414)(0.5)=14.14 \) and we can calculate the heat transfer by inserting the quantities in Equation (8-40):

\( q=\frac{20,241−478}{10.61+14.14+0} =799 \; W \)
