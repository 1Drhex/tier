Guest Question.

For the beam with overhang shown in Fig. 13.9(a), find the shear forces and bending moments at sections to the left and right of B (x = 2 m), to the left and right of C (x = 6 m), halfway between C and D (x = 7.5 m), and at the free end D (x = 9 m).

Admin
Solution

The free-body diagram for the entire beam is shown in Fig. 13.9(b). The resultant of the distributed load has been calculated and is shown in the figure. Equilibrium requires that

\(ΣF_{x} = 0\) \(A_{x} = 0\)

\(ΣM_{A} = 0\) \(-300(2) + C_{y}6 – 200(7.5) = 0\)

\(C_{y}\) = 350 kN

\(ΣM_{C} = 0\) \(-A_{y}\)6 + 300(4) – 200(1.5) = 0

\(A_{y}\) = 150 kN

Check:

\(ΣF_{y} = 0\) \(A_{y} – 300 + C_{y} – 200 = 0\) OK

In Fig. 13.9(c) and (d), free-body diagrams are shown for sections immediately to the left and right of point B. Point B must be excluded from the first free-body diagram because the 300-kN load acts at point B. The primed value of shear and bending moment \((\acute{V}\ and\ \acute{M} )\) are reserved for a section to the left of a point, and the double-primed values \((V''\ and\ M'')\) for a section to the right.

From Eqs. (13.1) and (13.3),

\(\acute{V}_{2}\) = 150 kN

\(\acute{M}_{2}\) = 150(2) = 300 kN.m

\(V''_{2}\) = 150 – 300 = -150 kN

\(M''_{2}\) = 150(2) = 300 kN.m

The free-body diagrams are shown in Fig. 13.9(e) and (f) for portions of the beam to the left and right of point C. (Note that \(C_{y}\) does not appear on either diagram.)

From Eqs. (13.1) and (13.3),

\(\acute{V}_{6}\) = 150 – 300 = -150 kN

\(\acute{M}_{6}\) = 150(6) – 300(4) = -300 kN.m

From Eqs. (13.2) and (13.4),

\(V''_{6}\) = 200 kN

\(M''_{6}\) = -200(1.5) = -300 kN.m

For the section at x = 7.5 m [Fig. 13.9(g)], we use Eqs. (13.2) and (13.4) to find the shear and bending moment.

\(V_{7.5}\) = 100 kN

\(M_{7.5}\) = -100(0.75) = -75 kN.m

For the free end of the beam, point D, x = 9 m [Fig. 13.9(h)], we use Eqs. (13.2) and (13.4) to find the shear and bending moment.

\(V_{9}\) = 0

\(M_{9}\) = 0
