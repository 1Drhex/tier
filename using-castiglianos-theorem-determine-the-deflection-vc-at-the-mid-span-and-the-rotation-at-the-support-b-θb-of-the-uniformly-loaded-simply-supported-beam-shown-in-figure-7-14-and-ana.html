Guest Question.

Using Castigliano’s theorem, determine the deflection \( v_C \) at the mid-span and the rotation at the support \( B (θ_B) \) of the uniformly loaded, simply-supported beam shown in Figure 7.14 and analysed previously in .

Admin
Solution

(i) Calculate \( v_C \)

Applying a downward load P at mid-span, the bending moment M caused by the actual uniformly distributed load \( w_1 \) and the load P at any point x from support A is:

\( M = 0.5 w_1 Lx + (Px/2) − 0.5 w_1 x^2 \) for x ≤ L/2.

\( M = 0.5 w_1Lx − P(x − L)/2 − 0.5 w_1x^2 \) for x > L/2.

The partial derivative ∂M/∂P is therefore:

∂M/∂P = x/2 for x ≤ L/2.

∂M/∂P = −(x − L)/2 for x > L/2.

The vertical deflection at mid-span caused by the applied load is obtained from Equation 7.18a:

\( \theta = \int\limits_{0}^{L}{\Bigl(\frac{\partial M }{\partial P }\Bigr) } \frac{M}{EI} dx = \int\limits_{0}^{L}{\Bigl(\frac{\partial M }{\partial P }\Bigr) } κ dx \) (7.18a) .

\( u_C = \int\limits_{0}^{L}{\Bigl(\frac{\partial M }{\partial P }\Bigr) } κ dx = \frac{1}{4EI}\int\limits_{0}^{L/2}{(w_1 L x^2 – w_1 x^3)}dx + \frac{1}{4EI}\int\limits_{L/2}^{L/2}{(- w_1 L x^2 + w_1L^2 x + w_1 x^3 – w_1 L x^2)}dx \)

\( = \frac{5}{768} \frac{w_1 L^4}{EI} + \frac{5}{768} \frac{w_1 L^4}{EI} = \frac{5}{384} \frac{w_1 L^4}{EI} \).

(ii) Calculate \( θ_B \)

Applying a counterclockwise couple \( M^* \) at B, the bending moment caused by the uniformly distributed load \( w_1 \) and the couple \( M^* \) at any point x from A is:

\( M = 0.5 w_1Lx + xM^*/L − 0.5 w_1x^2 \) for 0 ≤ x ≤ L.

The partial derivative \( ∂M/∂M^* \) is therefore x/L and the rotation at support B caused by the applied load can be obtained using Equation 7.18b as:

\( \theta = \int\limits_{0}^{L}{\Bigl(\frac{\partial M }{\partial M^* }\Bigr) } \frac{M}{EI} dx = \int\limits_{0}^{L}{\Bigl(\frac{\partial M }{\partial M^* }\Bigr) } κ dx \) (7.18b) .

\( \theta_B = \int\limits_{0}^{L}{\Bigl(\frac{\partial M }{\partial M^* }\Bigr) } κ dx = \frac{1}{2EI} \int\limits_{0}^{L}{\Bigl(w_1 x^2 – \frac{\partial w_1x^3 }{\partial L }\Bigr) } dx = \frac{1}{2EI}\Bigl( \frac{\partial w_1L^3 }{3 } – \frac{w_1 L^3}{4}\Bigr) = \frac{w_1 L^3}{24EI} \).

The expressions for the deflection and the rotation determined here are identical to those derived using virtual work in .
