Guest Question.

For the simple truss shown in Fig. 11.45, a vertical load P = 10 kN is acting at point A in the negative y direction on the horizontal member 2, which is pinned to the vertical wall at point D. Assume that member 3 is pinned to member 2 at point B and also to the vertical wall at point C. Use the theoretic value for the end-condition constant of member 3, and assume that this member is steel with a yield strength Syc = 370 MPa and a modulus of elasticity E = 207 GPa.

(a) Assume that member 3 has a solid circular cross section with a constant diameter d. Determine the diameter d to ensure that the member has a factor of safety guarding against buckling of N = 2. Does Euler’s column formula or Johnson’s parabolic equation give the correct diameter?
(b) Assume that member 3 has a square cross section with a constant width t. Determine the width, t, to ensure that the member has a factor of safety guarding against buckling of N = 2. Does Euler’s column formula or Johnson’s parabolic equation give the correct width?

Admin
Solution

First, determine the magnitude of the compressive load that is acting on member 3. The unknown lengths can be determined from the given geometry. The length of member 3 can be obtained from the trigonometric relation

\(L_{3}=5.0 m / \cos 40^{\circ}=6.527 m\)

The distance from point B to point D is

\(R_{B D}=(5.0 m ) \tan 40^{\circ}=4.196 m\)

The force acting on member 3 can be obtained by summing moments about pin D; that is,

\((7.0 m )(10000 N )-(4.196 m ) F_{B}^{y}=0\)

Therefore, the vertical force acting on member 3 is

\(F_{B}^{y}=16683 N\)

The total compressive force P acting on member 3 can then be obtained from the geometry; that is,

\(P=\frac{16683 N }{\cos 40^{\circ}}=21778 N\)

(a) Member 3 has a circular cross section with constant diameter d. Therefore, from Table 4 of Appendix A, the cross-sectional area and second moment of area of the member are

\(A=\frac{\pi d^{2}}{4} \quad \text { and } \quad I=\frac{\pi d^{4}}{64}\)

The radius of gyration of the member is

\(k=\sqrt{\frac{I}{A}}=\sqrt{\frac{\pi d^{4} / 64}{\pi d^{2} / 4}}=\frac{d}{4}\)

The end conditions of member 3 are pinned-pinned. Using the theoretic value for the end-condition constant (that is, C = 1 or α = 1) gives Leff = L. Therefore, the slenderness ratio of member 3 is

\(S_{ r }=\frac{L_{ eff }}{k}=\frac{6.527 m }{d / 4}=\frac{26.108 m }{d}\)

 

The factor of safety guarding against buckling is defined as

\(N=\frac{P_{ cr }}{P}\)

Rearranging this equation gives the critical load as

\(P_{ cr }=N P=2(21778 N )=43556 N\)

The slenderness ratio at the point of tangency is

\(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{D}=\pi \sqrt{\frac{2 E}{S_{y c}}}=\pi \sqrt{\frac{2\left(207 \times 10^{9} Pa \right)}{370 \times 10^{6} Pa }}=105.1\)

The diameter d can be obtained from Euler’s column formula; that is,

\(\frac{P_{ cr }}{A}=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{S_{ r }^{2}}\)

Substituting known values gives

\(\frac{43556 N }{\pi d^{2} / 4}=\frac{\pi^{2}\left(207 \times 10^{9} Pa \right)}{(26.108 m / d)^{2}}\)

Solving for diameter d gives

d = 0.066 m. (1)

Check: Diameter d can also be obtained from Johnson’s parabolic equation; that is,

\(\frac{P_{ cr }}{A}=S_{y c}-\frac{1}{E}\left(\frac{S_{y c} S_{ r }}{2 \pi}\right)^{2}\) (2)

Substituting the known values into Eq. (2) gives

\(\frac{43556 N }{\left(\pi d^{2} / 4\right)}=\left(370 \times 10^{6} Pa \right)-\frac{1}{207 \times 10^{9} Pa }\left(\frac{\left(370 \times 10^{6} Pa \right)(26.108 m / d)}{2 \pi}\right)^{2}\)

Solving this for diameter d gives

d = 0.176 m. (3)

Now we must check which answer is valid; that is, Eq. (1) or (3). We recall that the slenderness ratio is

\(S_{ r }=\frac{26.108 m }{d}\)

First, we check to determine whether Euler’s column result, Eq. (1), is valid. The slenderness ratio is

\(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{\text {Euler }}=\frac{26.108 m }{0.066 m }=395.6\)

Since the slenderness ratio \(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{\text {Euler }}\) is greater than \(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{D}\), that is, since 395.6 is greater than 105.1, Euler’s column formula gives a valid result. The diameter of the member is

d = 0.066 m.

Next we check to make sure that Johnson’s parabolic equation result is not valid. The slenderness ratio from this result is

\(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{ Johnson }=\frac{26.108 m }{0.176 m }=148.3\) (11.42)

Since the slenderness ratio, \(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{\text {Johnson }}\), is greater than \(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{D}\), that is, since 148.3 is greater than 105.1, Johnson’s parabolic equation result is not valid.

(b) Member 3 has a square cross section with constant width t. Therefore, from Table 4, Appendix A, the cross-sectional area and second moment of area of the member are

\(A=t^{2} \quad \text { and } \quad I=t^{4} / 12\)

The radius of gyration of the member is

\(k=\sqrt{\frac{I}{A}}=\sqrt{\frac{t^{4} / 12}{t^{2}}}=0.289 t\)

The slenderness ratio of member 3 is

\(S_{ r }=\frac{L_{ eff }}{k}=\frac{6.527 m }{0.289 t}=\frac{22.6 m }{t}\)

First, t can be found using the Euler column formula; that is,

\(\frac{P_{ cr }}{A}=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{S_{ r }^{2}}\)

Substituting the known values into this equation gives

\(\frac{43556 N }{t^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}\left(207 \times 10^{9} Pa \right)}{(22.6 m / t)^{2}}\)

Solving for the width, t, gives

t = 0.057 m.

The width, t, can also be obtained from the Johnson parabolic equation; that is,

\(\frac{P_{ cr }}{A}=S_{y c}-\frac{1}{E}\left(\frac{S_{y c} S_{ r }}{2 \pi}\right)^{2}\)

Substituting the known values into this equation gives

\(\frac{43556 N }{t^{2}}=\left(370 \times 10^{6} Pa \right)-\frac{1}{\left(207 \times 10^{9} Pa \right)}\left(\frac{\left(370 \times 10^{6} Pa \right) 22.6 m / t}{2 \pi}\right)^{2}\)

Therefore, the width, t, is

t = 0.152 m.

To check which answer is valid, we recall that the slenderness ratio is

\(S_{ r }=\frac{22.6 m }{t}\)

First, check to see whether the use of Euler’s column formula is valid:

\(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{\text {Euler }}=\frac{22.6 m }{0.057 m }=396.5\)

Since the slenderness ratio, \(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{\text {Euler }}\), is greater than \(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{D}\), that is, since 396.5 is greater than 105.1, Euler’s column formula gives a valid result. The correct width of the member is

t = 0.057 m.

Next we check to make sure that Johnson’s parabolic equation result is not valid. The slenderness ratio from this result is

\(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{ Johnson }=\frac{22.6 m }{0.152 m }=148.7\)

Since the slenderness ratio, \(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{\text {Johnson }},\) is greater than \(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{D}\), that is, since 148.7 is greater than 105.1, use of Johnson’s parabolic equation is not valid.
