Guest Question.

Saturated liquid nitrogen at 600 kPa enters a boiler at a rate of 0.005 kg/s and exits as saturated vapor. It then flows into a super heater also at 600 kPa where it exits at 600 kPa, 280 K. Assume the heat transfer comes from a 300 K source and find the rates of entropy generation in the boiler and the super heater.

Admin
Solution

C.V.: boiler steady single inlet and exit flow, neglect KE, PE energies in flow

Continuity Eq.: \(\dot{ m }_{1}=\dot{ m }_{2}=\dot{ m }_{3}\)

Table B.6.1:

\(\begin{array}{l}h _{1}=-81.53 kJ / kg , s _{1}=3.294 kJ / kgK, \\h _{2}=86.85 kJ / kg , s _{2}=5.041 kJ / kgK\end{array}\)

Table B.6.2: \(h _{3}=289.05 kJ / kg , s _{3}=6.238 kJ / kgK\)

Energy Eq.4.13: \(q_{\text {boiler }}=h_{2}-h_{1}=86.85-(-81.53)=168.38 kJ / kg\)

\(\dot{ Q }_{\text {boiler }}=\dot{ m }_{1} q _{\text {boiler }}=0.005 \times 168.38=0.842 kW\)

Entropy Eq.:

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ S }_{\text {gen }} &=\dot{ m }_{1}\left( s _{2}- s _{1}\right)-\dot{ Q }_{\text {boiler }} / T _{\text {source }} \\&=0.005(5.041-3.294)-0.842 / 300= 0 . 0 0 5 9 k W / K\end{aligned}\)

C.V. Superheater (same approximations as for boiler)

Energy Eq.4.13: \(q _{\text {sup heater }}= h _{3}- h _{2}=289.05-86.85=202.2 kJ / kg\)

\(\dot{ Q }_{\text {sup heater }}=\dot{ m }_{2} q _{\text {sup heater }}=0.005 kg / s \times 202.2 kJ / kg =1.01 kW\)

Entropy Eq.:

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ S }_{ gen } &=\dot{ m }_{1}\left( s _{3}- s _{2}\right)-\dot{ Q }_{\text {sup heater }} / T _{\text {source }} \\&=0.005 kg / s (6.238-5.041) kJ / kgK -1.01 kW / 300 K \\&= 0 . 0 0 2 6 2 k W / K\end{aligned}\)
