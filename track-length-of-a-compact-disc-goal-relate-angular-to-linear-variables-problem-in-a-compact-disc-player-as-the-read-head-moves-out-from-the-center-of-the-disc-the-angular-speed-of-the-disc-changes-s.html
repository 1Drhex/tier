Track Length of a Compact Disc

GOAL Relate angular to linear variables.

PROBLEM In a compact disc player, as the read head moves out from the center of the disc, the angular speed of the disc changes so that the linear speed at the position of the head remains at a constant value of about 1.3 m/s. (a) Find the angular speed of a compact disc of radius 6.00 cm when the read head is at r = 2.0 cm and again at r = 5.6 cm. (b) An old-fashioned record player rotates at a constant angular speed, so the linear speed of the record groove moving under the detector (the stylus) changes. Find the linear speed of a 45.0-rpm record at points 2.0 and 5.6 cm from the center. (c) In both the CDs and phonograph records, information is recorded in a continuous spiral track. Calculate the total length of the track for a CD designed to play for 1.0 h.

STRATEGY This problem is just a matter of substituting numbers into the appropriate equations. Part (a) requires relating angular and linear speed with Equation 7.10, \(v_{t}=r \omega, \text { solving for } \omega\) and substituting given values. In part (b), convert from rev/min to rad/s and substitute straight into Equation 7.10 to obtain the linear speeds. In part (c), linear speed multiplied by time gives the total distance.

 

\(v_{t}=r \omega\) [7.10]

Admin
Solution

(a) Find the angular speed of the disc when the read head is at r = 2.0 cm and r = 5.6 cm.

Solve \(v_{t}=r \omega, \text { for } \omega\) and calculate the angular speed at r = 2.0 cm: \(\omega=\frac{v_{t}}{r}=\frac{1.3 m / s }{2.0 \times 10^{-2} m }=65 rad / s\)

 

Likewise, find the angular speed at r = 5.6 cm: \(\omega=\frac{v_{t}}{r}=\frac{1.3 m / s }{5.6 \times 10^{-2} m }=23 rad / s\)

 

(b) Find the linear speed in m/s of a 45.0-rpm record at points 2.0 cm and 5.6 cm from the center.

Convert rev/min to rad/s: \(45.0 \frac{ rev }{ min }=45.0 \frac{ rev^{\nearrow0} }{\min^{\nearrow0} }\left(\frac{2 \pi rad }{ rev^{\nearrow0} }\right)\left(\frac{1.00 min^{\nearrow0} }{60.0 s }\right)=4.71 \frac{ rad }{ s }\)

 

Calculate the linear speed at r = 2.0 cm: \(v_{t}=r \omega=\left(2.0 \times 10^{-2} m \right)(4.71 rad / s )=0.094 m / s\)

 

Calculate the linear speed at r = 5.6 cm: \(v_{t}=r \omega=\left(5.6 \times 10^{-2} m \right)(4.71 rad / s )=0.26 m / s\)

 

(c) Calculate the total length of the track for a CD designed to play for 1.0 h.

Multiply the linear speed of the read head by the time in seconds: \(d=v_{t} t=(1.3 m / s )(3600 s )=4700 m\)

 

REMARKS Notice that for the record player in part (b), even though the angular speed is constant at all points along
a radial line, the tangential speed steadily increases with increasing r. The calculation for a CD in part (c) is easy only
because the linear (tangential) speed is constant. It would be considerably more difficult for a record player, where the
tangential speed depends on the distance from the center.
