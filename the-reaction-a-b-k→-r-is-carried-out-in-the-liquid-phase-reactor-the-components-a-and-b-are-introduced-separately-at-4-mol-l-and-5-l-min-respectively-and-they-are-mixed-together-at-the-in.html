Guest Question.

The reaction \(A + B \overset{k}{\rightarrow} R\) is carried out in the liquid-phase reactor. The components A and B are introduced separately at 4 mol/L and 5 L/min, respectively, and they are mixed together at the inlet to the reactor. This mixture goes into the reactor at 27◦C. There are two types of reactors to choose from:
1 A CSTR with 200 L, which can operate at 77◦C or 0◦C.
2 A PFR with 800 L, which can only operate at 27◦C.
It is known that the reaction is elementary and k=0.07 L/(mol min) at 27◦C and the activation energy is equal to 20 kcal/mol.
What reactor would you recommend and under what conditions? Justify.
If the reaction was performed in a batch reactor with 200 L and the final conversion was equal to the PFR, calculate the time at 77◦C and 0◦C, knowing that the initial concentrations are equal to 1 mol/L.

Admin
Solution

Since the initial concentrations are equal at the inlet, we have a second-order irreversible reaction, where the rate is given by the equation:
\((-r_{A})=kC_{A}^{2}=kC_{A0}^{2}(1-X_{A})^{2}\) (20.8)

For the CSTR:
\(\frac{V}{\nu _{0}}=\tau=C_{A0}\frac{X_{A}}{(-r_{A})}\) (20.9)

Substituting Equation 20.8 into Equation 20.9, we get:
\(\frac{V}{\nu _{0}}=\tau=C_{A0}\frac{X_{A}}{kC_{A0}^{2}(1-X_{A})^{2}}\) (20.10)

The value of k is solved at 77◦C and 0◦C. But at 27◦C, we have k and, therefore, \(k_{0}\) will be:
\(k=k_{0}e^{−(E/RT)}\)
\(0.07=k_{0}e^{−(20,000/1.98×300)}\)
\(k_{0} =8.51 × 1014\)

At 77◦C:
\(k_{350} =13.8 (L/mol min )\)

At 0◦C:
\(k_{273} =1.79 × 10^{−3}(L/mol min )\)

But the space-time will be:
\(\tau =\frac{V}{\nu _{0}}=\frac{200}{10}=20 min\)

Substituting these values into Equation 20.10:
\(20=\frac{X_{A}}{kC_{A0}(1-X_{A})^{2}}\)

At 77◦C, we get:
\(k_{350} =13.8 (L/mol min )\)

The solution is:
\(2X^{2}_{A}− 2X_{A} + 1=0\)

Therefore:
\(X_{A} >1\) is impossible.

At 0◦C:
\(k_{273} =1.79 × 10^{−3}(L/mol min )\)

We find:
\(X_{A} =0.1\)

For the PFR:
Calculating:
\(\tau =\frac{V}{\nu _{0}}=\frac{800}{10}=80 min\)

With k=0.07 L/(mol min) at 27◦C, we get:
\(\tau =\frac{1}{kC_{A0}}\frac{X_{A}}{(1-X_{A})}\) (20.12)

In the condition of 27.C, we obtain:
\(X_{A} =0.9\)

This is the best solution.
In the Batch reactor:
The time required would be calculated with a conversion of 90%. With \(C_{A0} =C_{B0} =1 mol/L\) and k at 77.C and 0.C, we have:
\(\tau =C_{A0}\int_{0}^{X_{A}}\frac{dX_{A}}{(-r_{A})}\) (20.13)

Substituting Equation 20.8 into Equation 20.13, we have (Equation 20.12):
\(\tau =\frac{1}{kC_{A0}}\frac{X_{A}}{(1-X_{A})}\)

Therefore, at 77◦C will be:
\(k_{350} =13.8 (L/mol min )\)

We get:
t =0.65 min

At 0◦C:
\(k_{273} =1.79 × 10^{−3}(L/mol min )\)
t =5000 min=83 h=3 days
