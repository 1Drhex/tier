Guest Question.

For each of the response functions in Eqs. (4.86) and (4.87), determine whether there is cancellation between the zero and the pole closest to the zero. For any function for which pole-zero cancellation is valid, find the approximate response.

\(C_{1}(s) = \frac{26.25(s+4)}{s(s+3.5)(s+5)(s+6)}\) (4:86)

\(C_{2}(s) = \frac{26.25(s+4)}{s(s+4.01)(s+5)(s+6)}\) (4:87)

Admin
Solution

The partial-fraction expansion of Eq. (4.86) is

\(C_{1}(s) =\frac{1}{s} – \frac{3.5}{s+5} + \frac{3.5}{s+6} – \frac{1}{s+3.5}\) (4:88)

The residue of the pole at – 3.5, which is closest to the zero at -4,is equal to 1 and is not negligible compared to the other residues. Thus, a second-order step response approximation cannot be made for \(C_{1}(s)\). The partial-fraction expansion for \(C_{2}(s)\) is

\(C_{2}(s) = \frac{0.87}{s} – \frac{5.3}{s+5} + \frac{4.4}{s+6} + \frac{0.033}{s+4.01}\) (4:89)

The residue of the pole at -4.01, which is closest to the zero at -4, is equal to 0.033, about two orders of magnitude below any of the other residues. Hence, we make a second-order approximation by neglecting the response generated by the pole at – 4.01:

\(C_{2}(s) \approx \frac{0.87}{s} – \frac{5.3}{s+5} + \frac{4.4}{s+6}\) (4:90)

and the response \(c_{2}(t)\) is approximately

\(c_{2}(t) \approx 0.87 – 5.3e^{-5t} + 4.4e^{-6t}\) (4:91)
