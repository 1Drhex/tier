CALCULATING A VECTOR PRODUCT

Vector A has magnitude 6 units and is in the direction of the +x-axis. Vector B has magnitude 4 units and lies in the xy-plane, making an angle of 30° with the +x-axis (Fig. 1.33). Find the vector product A= C × B.

Admin
Solution

IDENTIFY and SET UP: We’ll find the vector product in two ways, which will provide a check of our calculations.

First we’ll use

Eq. (1.20):\(C=A B \sin \phi\)

and the right-hand rule; then we’ll use

Eqs. (1.25): \(C_{x}=A_{y} B_{z}-A_{z} B_{y} \quad C_{y}=A_{z} B_{x}-A_{x} B_{z} \quad C_{z}=A_{x} B_{y}-A_{y} B_{x}\)

to find the vector product by using components.

EXECUTE: From Eq. (1.20) the magnitude of the vector product is
AB sin Φ = (6)(4)(sin 30°) = 12

By the right-hand rule, the direction of \(\vec{A} \times \vec{B}\) is along the +z-axis (the direction of the unit vector \(\hat{\boldsymbol{k}}\) so \(\vec{C}=\vec{A} \times \vec{B}=12 \hat{k}\).

To use Eqs. (1.25), we first determine the components of \(\overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{A}}\) and \(\overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{B}}\). Note that \(\overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{A}}\) points along the x-axis, so its only nonzero component is \(A_{x}\). For \(\overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{B}}\), Fig. 1.33 shows that Φ = 30° is measured from the +x-axis toward the +y-axis, so we can use

Eqs. (1.5): \(\frac{A_{x}}{A}=\cos \theta \quad \text { and } \quad \frac{A_{y}}{A}=\sin \theta\), \(A_{x}=A \cos \theta \quad \text { and } \quad A_{y}=A \sin \theta\).

\(A_{x}\) = 6 \(A_{y}\) = 0 \(A_{z}\) = 0

\(B_{x}=4 \cos 30^{\circ}=2 \sqrt{3}\) \(B_{y}=4 \sin 30^{\circ}=2 \quad B_{z}=0\)

Then Eqs. (1.25) yield

\(C_{x}=(0)(0)-(0)(2)=0\)

 

\(C_{y}=(0)(2 \sqrt{3})-(6)(0)=0\)

 

\(C_{z}=(6)(2)-(0)(2 \sqrt{3})=12\)

Thus again we have \(\overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{C}}=12 \hat{k}\).

EVALUATE: Both methods give the same result. Depending on the situation, one or the other of the two approaches may be the more convenient one to use.
