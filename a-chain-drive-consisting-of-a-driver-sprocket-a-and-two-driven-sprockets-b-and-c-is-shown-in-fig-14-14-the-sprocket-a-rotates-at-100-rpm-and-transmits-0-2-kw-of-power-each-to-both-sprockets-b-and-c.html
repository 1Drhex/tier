Guest Question.

A chain drive consisting of a driver sprocket A and two driven sprockets B and C is shown in Fig. 14.14. The sprocket A rotates at 100 rpm and transmits 0.2 kW of power each to both sprockets B and C. The chain used is simple roller chain 08B (pitch = 12.7 mm). The centre distances given in the figure are in terms of pitches.
Calculate the tensions in various parts of the chain and determine the shaft reactions for the sprocket B.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } \quad z_{A}=17 \quad z_{B}=34 \quad z_{C}=51 \quad n_{A}=100 rpm \).

p = 12.7 mm
power to each sprocket B and C = 0.2 kW
Chain-08B
Step I Chain tensions
The chain velocity is given by

\( v=\frac{z_{A} p n_{A}}{60 \times 10^{3}}=\frac{17(12.7)(100)}{60 \times 10^{3}}=0.36 m / s \).

The total power transmitted by the sprocket A is (0.2 + 0.2) or 0.4 kW and the chain tension is given by

\( P_{A}=\frac{1000 kW }{v}=\frac{1000(0.4)}{0.36}=1111 N \).

The power transmitted to each of the sprockets B and C is 0.2 kW. Therefore, the net tension is given by

\( P_{B}=P_{C}=\frac{1000(0.2)}{0.36}=555.5 N \).

The free-body diagram showing the tensions in various parts of the chain is shown in Fig. 14.15(a).

Step II Shaft reactions at the sprocket B
The pitch circle diameters of the sprockets are as follows:

\( D_{A}=\frac{p}{\sin \left(\frac{180}{z_{A}}\right)}=\frac{12.7}{\sin \left(\frac{180}{17}\right)}=69.12 mm \).

\( D_{B}=\frac{p}{\sin \left(\frac{180}{z_{B}}\right)}=\frac{12.7}{\sin \left(\frac{180}{34}\right)}=137.64 mm \).

\( D_{C}=\frac{p}{\sin \left(\frac{180}{z_{C}}\right)}=\frac{12.7}{\sin \left(\frac{180}{51}\right)}=206.30 mm \).

and 40p = 40(12.7) = 508 mm
Refer to Fig.14.15(a). a, b and c are the centres of circles A, B and C respectively. Construct a line ad perpendicular to the line bc.

\( \sin \beta=\frac{b d}{a b}=\frac{20 p}{40 p}=0.5 \quad \therefore \beta=30^{\circ} \).

Referring to Fig. 14.15(a),

\( \sin \alpha_{1}=\frac{D_{B}-D_{A}}{2 a}=\left[\frac{137.64-69.12}{2(508)}\right] \).

\( \text { or } \quad \alpha_{1}=3.87^{\circ} \).

\( \sin \alpha_{2}=\frac{D_{C}-D_{B}}{2 a}=\left[\frac{206.3-137.64}{2(508)}\right] \).

\( \text { or } \quad \alpha_{2}=3.87^{\circ} \).

The forces acting on the shaft of the sprocket B are shown in Fig.14.15(b). Considering equilibrium of vertical and horizontal forces,

\( R_{Y}=1111 \sin \left(30+\alpha_{1}\right)+555.5 \cos \alpha_{2} \).

\( =1111 \sin (30+3.87)+555.5 \cos (3.87) \).

= 1173.41 N.

\( R_{X}=1111 \cos \left(30+\alpha_{1}\right)-555.5 \sin \alpha_{2} \).

\( =1111 \cos (30+3.87)-555.5 \sin (3.87) \).

= 884.98 N.

\( \text { The resultant reaction } R_{B} \text { is given by } \).

\( R_{B}=\sqrt{(1173.41)^{2}+(884.98)^{2}}=1469.72 N \).
