PROBLEM Contrary to the apparent results of the previous example, the \([HCl]/[HCl^{+} ]\) ratio in the Orion Molecular Cloud is estimated at\(2\cdot 10^{9}\). There is a more eicient way to produce the HCl than by reaction 2 in the mechanism given above, and the \([HCl^{+} ]\) is itself consumed in one of those reactions:

\(Cl + H^{+}_{3} \xrightarrow[]{k_{3} } H Cl^{+}+ H^{2}\) \(k_{3} = 1.0\cdot 10^{-9} cm^{3}s^{-1}\)

 

\(H Cl^{+}+ H_{2} \xrightarrow[]{k_{4} } H_{2} Cl^{+}+ H\) \(k_{4} = 1.3\cdot 10^{-9} cm^{3}s^{-1}\)

 

\(H_{2} Cl^{+}+ e^{-} \xrightarrow[]{k_{5} } HCl+ H\) \(k_{5} = 1.5\cdot 10^{-7} cm^{3}s^{-1}\)

Reaction 3 is actually about four times more eicient at forming \([HCl^{+} ]\) than reaction 1 (but doesn’t ofer such a straightforward comparison with neutral-neutral kinetics). The net reaction is

\(Cl+ H^{+}_{3}+ e^{-} \xrightarrow[]{} HCl + 2H\)

Find the steady-state number density of \([HCl^{+} ]\) in this mechanism, using the parameters in Table 14.7.

Solution

Treating \([HCl^{+} ]\) as an intermediate in the mechanism, we apply the steady-state approximation:

\(\frac{d[HCl^{+}]}{dt} \approx k_{3}[Cl][H^{+}_{3} ]- k_{4}[HCl^{+} ]_{ss} [H_{2} ]= 0\)

 

\([HCl^{+} ]_{ss}= \frac{ k_{3}[Cl][H^{+}_{3} ]}{ k_{4}[H_{2} ]}\)

 

\(= \frac{( 1.0\cdot 10^{-9} )( 4\cdot 10^{-3} )( 1\cdot 10^{-9} )}{( 1.3\cdot 10^{-9} )( 1\cdot 10^{5} )}\)

 

\(= 3\cdot 10^{-12} cm^{-3} \)

The steady-state concentration is roughly the measured value of \( 1\cdot 10^{-12} cm^{-3}\)
