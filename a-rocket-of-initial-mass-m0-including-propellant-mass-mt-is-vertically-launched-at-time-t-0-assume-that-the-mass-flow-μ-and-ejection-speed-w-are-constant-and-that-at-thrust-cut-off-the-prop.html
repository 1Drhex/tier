Guest Question.

A rocket of initial mass \(m_{0}\) (including propellant mass \(m_{T}\) ) is vertically launched at time \(t = 0\). Assume that the mass flow \(\mu \) and ejection speed w are constant and that at thrust cut-off the propellant is fully consumed.

a) Determine the velocity \(v\left(t\right) \) of the rocket, assuming that there is no air drag and the gravity \(g\) is \(constant\).

b) What is the velocity at thrust cut-off for \(m_{T}=0.8m_{0} \) , thrust duration \(t_{T}=2 min \)and \(w=2000 m/ s\) ?

c) What are the accelerations at lift-off and just before thrust cut-off ?

Admin
Solution

a) The rocket motion is described by

\(\uparrow :m\left(t\right)\frac{dv}{dt}=-m\left(t\right)g+T \) .

With

\(T=\mu w\) , \(m\left(t\right)=m_{0}-\mu t\)

follows the acceleration

\(a=\frac{dv}{dt}=\frac{\mu w}{m_{0}-\mu t }-g \) .

Integration and considering the initial condition \(v\left(0\right)=0 \) leads to the velocity

\(\underline{\underline{v\left(t\right)=w\ln \frac{1}{1-\frac{\mu}{m_{0} }t }-gt } } \) .

b) The mass at thrust cut-off is \( m\left(t_{T} \right)=m_{0}-m_{T} \) , resulting in

\(\mu =\frac{m_{0}-m\left(t_{T} \right) }{t_{T} }=\frac{m_{T} }{t_{T} } \) ,

Thus, the velocity at thrust cut-off is given by

\(\underline{\underline{v\left(t_{T} \right) } }=w\ln \frac{1}{1-\frac{m_{T} }{m_{0} } }-g t_{T}=2000\ln \frac{1}{1-0.8}-9.81\cdot 120=2042 \frac{m}{s}=\underline{\underline{7350\frac{km}{h} } }\) .

c) The accelerations at lift-off \(\left(t=0\right)\) and just before thrust-off \(\left(t=t_{T} \right)\) follow as

\(\underline{\underline{a\left(0\right) } }=\frac{\mu w}{m_{0} }-g=\frac{m_{T} w }{m_{0} t_{T} }-g=\frac{0,8 \cdot 2000}{120}-9.81=\underline{\underline{3.52 \frac{m}{s^{2} } } } \)

 

\(\underline{\underline{a\left(t_{T} \right) } }=\frac{\mu w}{m_{0}-m_{T} }-g=\frac{m_{T}w }{0.2m_{0}t_{T} }-g= \underline{\underline{56.84 \frac{m}{s^{2} } } } \) .
