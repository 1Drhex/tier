Guest Question.

Using the method of Faddeev, obtain the characteristic polynomials of the matrices.

Admin
Solution

a) \(\begin{bmatrix} 3& 2 &1 \\ 4&5 &-1 \\2 & 3 & 4\end{bmatrix}\)

Characteristic polynomial is\( \lambda ^{3}-p_{1}\lambda ^{2}-p_{2}\lambda- p_{3}\) with \( p_{1}\)= trace A = 12

\(B_{1}\)=\(A-12I\)=\(\begin{bmatrix}-9 &2 &1 \\4 & -7 &-1 \\2 & 3 & -8\end{bmatrix}\)

\( A_{2}\)=\(AB_{1}\)=\(\begin{bmatrix}-17 & -5 &-7 \\-18 &-30 &7 \\2 & -5 &-33\end{bmatrix}\)

\(P_{2}\)=\(\frac{1}{2}\) trace \(A_{2}\)=-40

\(B_{2}\)=\(A_{2}+40I\)=\(\begin{bmatrix}23 & -5 &-7 \\ -18& -30 & 7\\2 &-5 & -33\end{bmatrix}\)

\(A_{3}\)=\(AB_{2}\)=\(\begin{bmatrix}35 &0 &0 \\0 &35 &0 \\0 &0 & 35\end{bmatrix}\)

\(P_{3}\)=\(\frac{1}{3}\) trace \(A_{3}\)= 35

Thus, characteristic polynomial is

\(\lambda ^{3}-12\lambda ^{2}+40\lambda -35\)

Note that\( B_{3}\)=\(A_{3}-35I=0\) confirming check.
