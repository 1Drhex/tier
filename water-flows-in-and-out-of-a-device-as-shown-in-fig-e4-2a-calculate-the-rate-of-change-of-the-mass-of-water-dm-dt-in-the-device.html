Guest Question.

Water flows in and out of a device as shown in Fig. E4.2a. Calculate the rate of change of the mass of water (dm/dt) in the device.

Admin
Solution

The control surface of the control volume selected is shown in Fig. E4.2b.The continuity equation (4.4.2), with three surfaces across which water flows, takes the following form:

\(0 = \frac{d}{d t} \int_{\text {c.v. }} \rho d V +\int_{\text {c.s. }} \rho \hat{\mathbf{n}} \cdot \mathbf{V} d A (4.4.2)\)

\(\begin{aligned}0 &=\frac{d}{d t} \int_{\text {c.v. }} \rho d V+\int_{\text {c.s. }} \rho \hat{\mathbf{n}} \cdot \mathbf{V} d A \\&=\frac{d m}{d t}-\rho_{1} A_{1} V_{1}+\rho_{2} A_{2} V_{2}+\rho_{3} A_{3} V_{3}\end{aligned}\)

where we have assumed the density to be constant over the volume and we have used \(\mathbf{V}_{1} \cdot \hat{\mathbf{n}}=-V_{1}\), since \(\hat{\mathbf{n}}_{1}\) points out of the volume, opposite to the direction of \(\mathbf{V}_{1}\). The last three terms come from the area integral. In terms of the quantities given, the above can be expressed as

\(\begin{aligned}0=& \frac{d m}{d t}-\rho_{1} A_{1} V_{1}+\dot{m}_{2}+\rho_{3} Q_{3} \\=\frac{d m}{d t} &-1.94 \mathrm{slug} / \mathrm{ft}^{3} \times\left(\pi \times \frac{1.5^{2}}{144}\right) \mathrm{ft}^{2} \times 30 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{sec}+0.3 \mathrm{slug} / \mathrm{sec} \\&+1.94 \mathrm{slug} / \mathrm{ft}^{3} \times 0.3 \mathrm{ft}^{3} / \mathrm{sec}\end{aligned}\)

This is solved to yield

\(\frac{d m}{d t}=1.975 \mathrm{slug} / \mathrm{sec}\)

Hence the mass is increasing at the rate of 1.975 slug/sec.To accomplish this, the device could have a spongelike material that absorbs water.
