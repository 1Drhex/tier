Guest Question.

You have just washed your hair and now blow dry it in a room with 23°C, \(\phi\) = 60%, (1). The dryer, 500 W, heats the air to 49°C, (2), blows it through your hair where the air becomes saturated (3), and then flows on to hit a window where it cools to 15°C (4). Find the relative humidity at state 2, the heat transfer per kilogram of dry air in the dryer, the air flow rate, and the amount of water condensed on the window, if any.
The blowdryer heats the air at constant specific humidity to 2 and it then goes through an adiabatic saturation process to state 3, finally cooling to 4.

Admin
Solution

1: \(23^{\circ} C , 60 \% \text { rel hum } \Rightarrow w _{1}=0.0104, \quad \tilde{ h }_{1}=69 kJ / kg \text { dry air }\)

2: \(w _{2}= w _{1}, T _{2} \Rightarrow \phi_{2}= 1 5 \% , \quad \tilde{ h }_{2}=95 kJ / kg \text { dry air }\)

CV. 1 to 2:

\(\begin{gathered}w _{2}= w _{1} ; \quad q =\tilde{ h }_{2}-\tilde{ h }_{1}=95-69= 2 6 k J / kg \text { dry air } \\\dot{ m }_{ a }= Q / q =0.5 / 26= 0 . 0 1 9 2 3 kg / s\end{gathered}\)

CV. 2 to 3: \(w _{3}- w _{2}=\dot{ m }_{ li q} / \dot{ m }_{ a } ; \quad \dot{ m }_{ a } \tilde{ h }_{2}+\dot{ m }_{ liq } h _{ f }=\dot{ m }_{ a } \tilde{ h }_{3}\)

3: \(\phi=100 \%=> T _{3}= T _{\text {wet }, 2}= 2 4 . 8 ^{\circ} C , w _{3}= 0 . 0 1 9 8\)

4: \(\phi=100 \%, T _{4}=> w _{4}=0.01065\)

\(\dot{ m }_{ liq }=\left( w _{3}- w _{4}\right) \dot{ m }_{ a }=(0.0198-0.01065) \times 0.01923= 0 . 1 7 6 g / s\)

If the steam tables and formula’s are used then we get

\(\begin{aligned}& h _{ g 1}=2543.5, h _{ g 2}=2590.3, P _{ g 1}=2.837 kPa , P _{ v 1}=1.7022 kPa, \\& P _{ g 2}=11.8 kPa , w _{1}=0.01077, w _{2}= w _{1}, P _{ v 2}= P _{ v1 } \\&\phi_{2}= P _{ v 2} / P _{ g 2}=14.4 \%, h _{ f 3}=114 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

Trial and error for adiabatic saturation temperature.

\(\begin{aligned}& T _{3}=25^{\circ} C , w _{3}=0.02, P _{ v 4}= P _{ g 4}=1.705 kPa \\& w _{4}=0.622 \times 1.705 /(100-1.705)=0.0108\end{aligned}\)
