Guest Question.

A gas occupies a volume of 200. mL at 400. torr pressure. To what pressure must the gas be subjected in order to change the volume to 75.0 mL?

Admin
Solution

READ Knowns \( P_1\) = 400. torr \(V_1\) = 200. L
\(V_2\) = 75.0 mL
Solving for: new pressure \((P_2)\)

PLAN Since the problem is concerned only with pressure and volume, Boyle’s law can be used: \(P_1V_1 = P_2V_2\)

Solution map: volume decreases → pressure increases

SETUP Since \(P_1V = P_2V_2\) solving for \(P_2\) gives \(P_2=\frac{P_1V_1}{V_2}\)

CALCULATE \( P_2=\frac{(400. torr) (200. \cancel{mL})}{75.0 \cancel{mL}} = 1.07 \times 10^3 torr \)

Another way to approach this is to use the solution map to multiply the original pressure by a ratio of volumes that will result in an increase in pressure.

\(V_2 = (400. torr) (\frac{200. \cancel{mL}}{75.0 \cancel{mL}} )= 1.07 \times 10^3 torr \)

CHECK The final pressure is larger than the original as predicted by Boyle’s law and the solution map.
