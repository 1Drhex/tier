Guest Question.

What is the deflection at E for the simply supported beam shown in Figure P17.118/119? Utilize Castigliano’s second theorem and assume that \(E I=37.7 \times 10^{6} kip \text {-in. }^{2}\) .

Admin
Solution

To determine the deflection of the simply-supported beam, first apply a dummy concentrated load P downward at E. A free-body diagram of the beam is shown.

 

 

Draw a free-body diagram that cuts through the beam at section a–a between A and B. Derive a moment equation M from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{array}{rlrl}\Sigma M_{a-a} & =-(38 \text { kips }-0.28 P) x_{1}+M=0 & \\& \therefore M=(38 \text { kips }-0.28 P) x_{1} & 0 \leq x_{1}<10 ft\end{array}\)

Differentiate this expression to obtain ∂M/∂P.

\(\frac{\partial M}{\partial P}=-0.28 x_{1}\)

Substitute P = 0 into the bending-moment equation to obtain

\(M=(38 kips ) x_{1}\)

 

Draw a second free-body diagram that cuts through the beam at section b–b between B and C and derive a moment equation M.

\(\begin{gathered}\Sigma M_{b-b}=-(38 \text { kips }-0.28 P) x_{2}+(34 \text { kips })\left(x_{2}-10 ft \right) \\+\frac{5 \text { kips } / ft }{2}\left(x_{2}-10 ft \right)^{2}+M=0 \\\therefore M=-\frac{5 kips / ft }{2}\left(x_{2}-10 ft \right)^{2}-(34 kips )\left(x_{2}-10 ft \right) \\+(38 kips -0.28 P) x_{2} \\10 ft \leq x_{2}<12.5 ft\end{gathered}\)

Differentiate this expression to obtain ∂M/∂P.

\(\frac{\partial M}{\partial P}=-0.28 x_{2}\)

Substitute P = 0 into the bending-moment equation to obtain

\(M=-\frac{5 kips / ft }{2}\left(x_{2}-10 ft \right)^{2}-(34 kips )\left(x_{2}-10 ft \right)+(38 kips ) x_{2}\)

 

Draw a third free-body diagram that cuts through the beam at section c–c between C and D and derive a moment equation M.

\(\begin{gathered}\Sigma M_{c-c}=(106 \text { kips }+1.28 P) x_{3}-\frac{5 kips / ft }{2}\left(x_{3}+7 ft \right)^{2} \\-P\left(x_{3}+7 ft \right)-M=0 \\\therefore M=-\frac{5 kips / ft }{2}\left(x_{3}+7 ft \right)^{2}+(106 kips +1.28 P) x_{3} \\-P\left(x_{3}+7 ft \right) \\0 ft \leq x_{3}<12.5 ft\end{gathered}\)

Differentiate this expression to obtain ∂M/∂P.

\(\frac{\partial M}{\partial P}=1.28 x_{3}-\left(x_{3}+7\right) x_{3}=0.28 x_{3}-7\)

Substitute P = 0 into the bending-moment equation to obtain

\(M=-\frac{5 kips / ft }{2}\left(x_{3}+7 ft \right)^{2}+(106 kips ) x_{3}\)

 

Draw a fourth free-body diagram that cuts through the beam at section d–d between D and E and derive a moment equation M.

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma M_{d-d}=-\frac{5 kips / ft }{2} x_{4}^{2}-P x_{4}-M=0 \\&\therefore M=-\frac{5 kips / ft }{2} x_{4}^{2}-P x_{4} \quad 0 ft \leq x_{4}<7 ft\end{aligned}\)

Differentiate this expression to obtain ∂M/∂P.

\(\frac{\partial M}{\partial P}=-x_{4}\)

Substitute P = 0 into the bending-moment equation to obtain

\(M=-\frac{5 kips / ft }{2} x_{4}^{2}\)

 

Castigliano’s second theorem applied to beam deflections can be expressed by Equation (17.40).

\(\Delta=\int_{0}^{L}\left(\frac{\partial M}{\partial P}\right) \frac{M}{E I} d x\)

The beam deflection at E can now be determined.

\(\begin{gathered}\Delta_{E}=\frac{1}{E I} \int_{0}^{10}\left(-0.28 x_{1}\right)\left(38 x_{1}\right) d x_{1}+\frac{1}{E I} \int_{10}^{12.5}\left(-0.28 x_{2}\right)\left(-\frac{5}{2}\left(x_{2}-10\right)^{2}-34\left(x_{2}-10\right)+38 x_{2}\right) d x_{2} \\+\frac{1}{E I} \int_{0}^{12.5}\left(0.28 x_{3}-7\right)\left(-\frac{5}{2}\left(x_{3}+7\right)^{2}+106 x_{3}\right) d x_{3}+\frac{1}{E I} \int_{0}^{7}\left(-1 x_{4}\right)\left(-\frac{5}{2} x_{4}^{2}\right) d x_{4} \\=-\frac{10.64}{E I} \int_{0}^{10} x_{1}^{2} d x_{1}+\frac{0.7}{E I} \int_{10}^{12.5} x_{2}\left(x_{2}-10\right)^{2} d x_{2}+\frac{9.52}{E I} \int_{10}^{12.5} x_{2}\left(x_{2}-10\right) d x_{2} \\-\frac{10.64}{E I} \int_{10}^{12.5} x_{2}^{2} d x_{2}-\frac{0.7}{E I} \int_{0}^{12.5} x_{3}\left(x_{3}+7\right)^{2} d x_{3}+\frac{29.68}{E I} \int_{0}^{12.5} x_{3}^{2} d x_{3} \\+\frac{17.5}{E I} \int_{0}^{12.5}\left(x_{3}+7\right)^{2} d x_{3}-\frac{742}{E I} \int_{0}^{12.5} x_{3} d x_{3}+\frac{2.5}{E I} \int_{0}^{7} x_{4}^{3} d x_{4}\end{gathered}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&=-\frac{10.64}{3 E I}\left[x_{1}^{3}\right]_{0}^{10}+\frac{0.7}{E I}\left[\frac{1}{4}\left(x_{2}-10\right)^{4}+\frac{10}{3}\left(x_{2}-10\right)^{3}\right]_{10}^{12.5}+\frac{9.52}{E I}\left[\frac{1}{3}\left(x_{2}-10\right)^{3}+\frac{10}{2}\left(x_{2}-10\right)^{2}\right]_{10}^{12.5} \\&-\frac{10.64}{3 E I}\left[x_{2}^{3}\right]_{10}^{12.5}-\frac{0.7}{E I}\left[\frac{1}{4}\left(x_{3}+7\right)^{4}-\frac{7}{3}\left(x_{3}+7\right)^{3}\right]_{0}^{12.5}+\frac{29.68}{3 E I}\left[x_{3}^{3}\right]_{0}^{12.5} \\&+\frac{17.5}{E I}\left[\frac{1}{3}\left(x_{3}+7\right)^{3}\right]_{0}^{12.5}-\frac{742}{2 E I}\left[x_{3}^{2}\right]_{0}^{12.5}+\frac{2.5}{4 E I}\left[x_{4}^{4}\right]_{0}^{7}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&=-\frac{3,546.6667}{E I}+\frac{43.2943}{E I}+\frac{347.0833}{E I}-\frac{3,380.4167}{E I}-\frac{13,333.3568}{E I}+\frac{19,322.9167}{E I} \\&+\frac{41,252.6042}{E I}-\frac{57,968.7500}{E I}+\frac{1,500.6250}{E I} \\&=-\frac{15,762.6667}{E I}=-\frac{\left(15,762.6667 \text { kip-ft }^{3}\right)(12 in . / ft )^{3}}{37.7 \times 10^{6} \text { kip-in. }^{2}}=-0.722490 \text { in. } \\&\therefore \Delta_{E}=0.722 in . \uparrow\end{aligned}\)
