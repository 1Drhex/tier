Guest Question.

Determine the joint displacements, member axial forces, and support reactions for the plane truss shown in Fig. 7.19(a) on the next page, due to the combined effect of the following: (a) the joint loads shown in the figure, (b) a temperature drop of 30° F in member 1, and (c) the fabricated length of member 3 being \(\frac{1}{8}\) in. too short. Use the matrix stiffness method.

Admin
Solution

This truss was analyzed in for joint loads only, and in for a support displacement.

Analytical Model: See Fig. 7.19(b). The analytical model used herein is the same as used in and .

Structure Stiffness Matrix: From ,

S=\(\left[\begin{matrix} 696 & 0 \\ 0 & 2,143.6 \end{matrix} \right]\) k/in (1)

Joint Load Vector: From ,

P=\(\left[\begin{matrix} 150 \\ -300 \end{matrix} \right]\) k (2)

Structure Fixed-Joint Force Vector Due to Temperature Changes and Fabrication Errors:

Member 1 By substituting E = 29,000 ksi, A = 8 \(in^{2}\), α = 6.5\((10^{−6})\)/° F, and Tu = −30° F into Eqs. (7.34) \(FA_{b}=-FA_{e}=EAαT_{u}\) , we evaluate the member fixed-end forces, due to the specified temperature change, as

\(FA_{b}=-FA_{e}=-45.24 k\)

Thus, the local fixed-end force vector for member 1 can be expressed as

\(Q_{f1}=\left[\begin{matrix} FA_{b} \\ FS_{b} \\ FA_{e}\\FS_{e} \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} -45.24 \\ 0 \\ 45.24 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right]\) k (3)

Next, we obtain the global fixed-end force vector for this member by applying the transformation relationship \(F_{f} = T^{T}Q_{f}\), while using the transformation matrix \(T_{1}\) from . Thus,

\(F_{f1}=T^{T}_{1}Q_{f1}=\left[\begin{matrix} 0.6 & -0.8 & 0 &0\\0.8 & 0.6 & 0&0 \\ 0&0 & 0.6 & -0.8\\0&0&0.8&0.6 \end{matrix} \right]\left[\begin{matrix} -45.24 \\ 0 \\ 45.24\\0 \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} -27.144 \\ -36.192 \\ \overset{…………..}{27.144}\\36.192 \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 3 \\ 4 \\ 1\\2 \end{matrix} k\)

From the member code numbers 3, 4, 1, 2, which are written on the right side of \(F_{f1}\), we realize that the elements in the third and fourth rows of \(F_{f1}\) should be stored in rows 1 and 2, respectively, of the 2 × 1 structure fixed-joint force vector \(P_{f}\), as shown in Fig. 7.19(c).

Member 3 By substituting \(e_{a}=-\frac{1}{8}\) in. into Eq. (7.35) \(FA_{b}=-FA_{e}=\frac{EA}{L}e_{a}\) , we obtain

\(FA_{b}=-FA_{e}=\frac{29,00(8)}{20(12)}\left(-\frac{1}{8} \right)=-120.83k\)

Thus,

\(Q_{f3}=\left[\begin{matrix} FA_{b} \\ FS_{b} \\ FA_{e}\\FS_{e} \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix} -120.83 \\ 0 \\ 120.83\\0 \end{matrix} \right] k\) (4)

Using \(T_{3}\) from , we calculate

\(F_{f3}=T^{T}_{3}Q_{f3}=\left[\begin{matrix} -0.6 & -0.8 & 0 &0\\0.8 & -0.6 & 0&0 \\ 0&0 & -0.6 & -0.8\\0&0&0.8&-0.6 \end{matrix} \right]\left[\begin{matrix}-120.83 \\ 0 \\ 120.83\\0 \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} 72.5 \\ \underset{…………} {-96.667}\\-72.5\\96.667 \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 7 \\ 8 \\ 1\\2 \end{matrix} k\)

The relevant elements of \(F_{f3}\) are stored in \(P_{f}\) using the member code numbers. The completed structure fixed-joint force vector \(P_{f}\) , due to temperature change and fabrication error, is given in Fig. 7.19(c).

Joint Displacements: By substituting P (Eq. (2)), \(P_{f}\) (Fig. 7.19(c)), and S (Eq. (1)) into the structure stiffness relationship, we write

P-\(P_{f}\) = SD

 

\(\left[\begin{matrix}150 \\ -300 \end{matrix} \right]-\left[\begin{matrix}-45.356 \\ 132.86 \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix} 195.36 \\ -432.86 \end{matrix} \right]=\begin{bmatrix}696 & 0 \\0 &2,143.6 \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} d_{1} \\ d_{2} \end{matrix} \right]\)

Solving the foregoing equations,

\(d=\left[\begin{matrix}0.28068 \\ -0.20193 \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{matrix} in\)
