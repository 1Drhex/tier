Guest Question.

Determine the reactions and draw the shear and bending moment diagrams for the continuous beam shown in Fig. \( 15.9 (\mathrm{a})\) due to a settlement of 20 \(mm\) at support \(B\). Use the slope-deflection method.

Admin
Solution

Degrees of Freedom. \( \theta_{B}\) and \( \theta_{C}\)

Fixed-End Moments. Since no external loads act on the beam, the fixed-end moments are zero.

Chord Rotations. The specified support settlement is depicted in Fig. 15.9(b), using an exaggerated scale. The inclined dashed lines in this figure indicate the chords (not the elastic curves) of the members in the deformed positions. Because the length of member \( A B\) is \( 8 \mathrm{~m}\) , the rotation of its chord is

\( \psi_{A B}=-\frac{0.02}{8}=-0.0025 \)

in which the negative sign has been assigned to the value of \( \psi_{A B}\) to indicate that its direction is clockwise, as shown in Fig. 15.9(b). Similarly, the chord rotation for member \( B C\) is

\( \psi_{B C}=\frac{0.02}{8}=0.0025 \)

From Fig. 15.9(b), we can see that

\( \psi_{C D}=0 \)

Slope-Deflection Equations. Applying Eq. (15.9) to members \( A B, B C\) , and \( C D\) , we write

\( M_{n f}=\frac{2 E I}{L}\left(2 \theta_{n}+\theta_{f}-3 \psi\right)+\mathrm{FEM}_{n f} \) (15.9)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{A B}=\frac{2 E I}{8}\left(\theta_{B}+0.0075\right) \\\end{array} \) (1)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{B A}=\frac{2 E I}{8}\left(2 \theta_{B}+0.0075\right) \\\end{array} \) (2)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{B C}=\frac{2 E I}{8}\left(2 \theta_{B}+\theta_{C}-0.0075\right) \\\end{array} \) (3)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{C B}=\frac{2 E I}{8}\left(\theta_{B}+2 \theta_{C}-0.0075\right) \\\end{array} \) (4)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{C D}=\frac{2 E I}{8}\left(2 \theta_{C}\right) \\\end{array} \) (5)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{D C}=\frac{2 E I}{8}\left(\theta_{C}\right) \end{array} \) (6)

Equilibrium Equations. See Fig. 15.9(c).

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{B A}+M_{B C}=0 \\\end{array} \) (7)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{C B}+M_{C D}=0 \end{array} \) (8)

Joint Rotations. Substitution of the slope-deflection equations (Eqs. (1) through (6)) into the equilibrium equations (Eqs. (7) and (8)) yields

\( \begin{array}{l} 4 \theta_{B}+\theta_{C}=0 \\\end{array} \) (9)

\( \begin{array}{l} \theta_{B}+4 \theta_{C}=0.0075 \end{array} \) (10)

By solving Eqs. (9) and (10) simultaneously, we determine

\( \begin{array}{l} \theta_{B}=-0.0005 \mathrm{rad} \\ \theta_{C}=0.002 \mathrm{rad} \end{array} \)

Member End Moments. To compute the member end moments, we substitute the numerical values of \( \theta_{B}, \theta_{C}\) , and \( E I=(70)(800)=56,000 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{2}\) into the right sides of the slope-deflection equations (Eqs. (1) through (6)) to obtain

\( \begin{array}{lll} M_{A B}=98 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}\curvearrowleft \ & & \\ M_{B A}=91 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}\curvearrowleft & & \\ M_{B C}=-91 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} & \text { or } & 91 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}\curvearrowright \\ M_{C B}=-56 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} & \text { or } & 56 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}\curvearrowright \\ M_{C D}=56 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}\curvearrowleft & & \\ M_{D C}=28 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}\curvearrowleft & & \end{array} \)

Member End Shears and Support Reactions. See Fig. 15.9(d) and (e).

Equilibrium Check. See Fig. 15.9(e).

\( \begin{array}{c} +\uparrow \sum F_{y}=0 \quad 23.63-42+28.87-10.5=0 \\ +\curvearrowleft M_{A}=0 \\ 98-42(8)+28.87(16)-10.5(24)+28=-0.08 \approx 0 \end{array} \)

Shear and Bending Moment Diagrams. See Fig. 15.9(f) and (g).
