Guest Question.

A structure composed of three rigid frames must be stabilized by diagonal bracing in one of the frames. The braced frame is shown in Figure 6.23. The loading is the same for all frames. Use A36 steel and determine the required cross-sectional area of the bracing. Use load and resistance factor design.

Admin
Solution

The total vertical load to be stabilized by the bracing is

\(P_{r} = Σ{P_{u} }= 0.9(45) × 3 = 121.5\) kips

From AISC Equation A-6-1, the lateral shear to be resisted is

\(P_{rb }= 0.004P_{r} = 0.004(121.5) = 0.4860\) kips

If we consider both braces to be tension-only members, then the entire force must be resisted by one brace. Because \(P_{rb}\) is the horizontal component of the brace force (see Figure 6.24a), the brace force is

\(F=\frac{P_{rb}}{\cos }\)

where

\( =\tan^{–1}(\frac{18}{45})=21.80^{\circ}\)

Then,

\(F=\frac{0.4860}{\cos(21.81)}=0.5234\) kips

Based on the limit state of tension yielding, the required area is

\(A=\frac{F}{0.9F_{y}}=\frac{0.5234}{0.9(36)}=0.0162\) \(in.^{2}\)

From AISC Equation A-6-2, the required lateral stiffness is

\(β_{br}=\frac{1}{\phi}(\frac{2P_{r}}{L_{b}})=\frac{1}{0.75}[\frac{2(121.5)}{(18×12)}]=1.500\) kips/in.

The axial stiffness of the brace is given by \(F/δ\), where \(δ\) is the axial deformation of the brace. From Figure 6.24b,

\(δ\) \(\cos\theta\)

where is the lateral displacement. The axial stiffness of the brace can be written as

\(\frac{F}{}=\frac{P_{rb}/\cos}{\cos}=\frac{P_{rb}}{}\bullet\frac{1}{\cos^{2} }\)

or

\(\frac{F}{}\cos^{2} =\frac{P_{rb}}{ }\)

 

That is,

Axial stiffness \(× \cos^{2} \theta=\) lateral stiffness

From elementary mechanics of materials, the axial stiffness is AE/L, where

\(L =\) length of brace \(= (18 × 12)/\sin(21.80) = 581.6\) in.

Let

\(\frac{AE}{L} \cos^{2} =\frac{1}{\phi}(\frac{2P_{r}}{L_{b}})=1.500\)

 

\(A=\frac{1.500 L}{E \cos^{2} }=\frac{1.500(581.6)}{29,000cos^{2}(21.8)}=0.0349\) \(in.^{2}\)

The stiffness requirement controls. The results are typical in that the requirements are minimal.

Use a tension brace with a cross-sectional area of at least \(0.0349\) \(in.^{2}\)
