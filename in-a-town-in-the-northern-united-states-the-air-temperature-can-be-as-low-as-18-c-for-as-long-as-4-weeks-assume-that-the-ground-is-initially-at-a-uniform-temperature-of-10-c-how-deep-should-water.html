Guest Question.

In a town in the Northern United States, the air temperature can be as low as -18 C for as long as 4 weeks. Assume that the ground is initially at a uniform temperature of 10 C. How deep should water pipes be buried to prevent freezing? Assume that the combined convective and radiative coefficient at the ground’s surface is an average 30 W/\(m^{2}\) C. The ground has a thermal conductivity of 2 W/m C, a density of 1800 kg/\(m^{3}\), and a specific heat of 2100 J/kg C.

Admin
Solution

We want to find the depth at which the temperature is the freezing temperature of water (0 C).

\(\alpha = \frac{k}{\rho c} = \frac{2}{(1800)(1200)} = 5.291\times 10^{-7} m^{2} /s\)

where t = 4 weeks ×7 days/week ×24 h/day ×3600 s/h =2.42 ×\(10^{6}\) s.

We will use Eq. (4.111):

\(\frac{T(x,t)-T_{i} }{T_{\infty }-T_{i} } = erfc(\frac{x}{2\sqrt{\alpha t} } )-e^{(\frac{hx}{k}+\frac{h^{2}\alpha t }{k^{2} } )} erfc(\frac{x}{2\sqrt{\alpha t} }+\frac{h\sqrt{\alpha t} }{k} )\)

 

\(\alpha t= (5.291 \times 10^{-7} )(2.420\times 10^{6} )= 1.280\)

Putting values into Eq. (4.111), we have

\(\frac{0-10 }{-18-10 } = erfc(\frac{x}{2\sqrt{1.280} } )-e^{(\frac{30x}{2}+\frac{30^{2}(1.280) }{2^{2} } )} erfc(\frac{x}{2\sqrt{1.280} }+\frac{30\sqrt{1.280} }{2} )\)

0.3571=erfc(0.4419x)- \(e ^{(15x+288)}\) erfc(0.4419x+16.971)

This equation can be solved by trial and error, that is, guessing a value of x, calculating the right side of the equation, and seeing if it equals 0.3571. If not, choose another x and try again. Of course, it is much more efficient to use software. Solving the equation for x using Excel’s Goal Seek gives x =1.474 m = 4.84 ft.
Being conservative, we will round this answer upwards and conclude that the water pipe should be buried at a depth of 5 feet or greater.

Another way to solve this problem is to use Fig. 4.9. The left axis is \(\frac{T(x,t)-T_{i} }{T_{\infty }-T_{i} } = 0.3571\) and the lines on the figure are for different \(\frac{h\sqrt{\alpha t} }{k}\)=16.971. For our problem, the left axis is 0.3571 and the line should be the one for 16.971, which is close to the one for ∞. Reading the figure, we get

\(\eta =\frac{x}{2\sqrt{\alpha t} } = 0.64 and x= (0.64)(2)\sqrt{1.280}= 1.45 m= 4.76 ft \)

This result agrees with the above result from Eq. (4.111).
