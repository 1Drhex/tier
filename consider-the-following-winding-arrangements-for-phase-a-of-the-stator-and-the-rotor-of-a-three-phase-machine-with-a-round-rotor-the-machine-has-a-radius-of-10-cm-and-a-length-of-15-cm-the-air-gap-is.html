Guest Question.

Consider the following winding arrangements for phase a of the stator and the rotor of a three-phase machine with a round rotor. The machine has a radius of 10 cm and a length of 15 cm. The air gap is uniform and has a length of 1 mm.

Determine the following:
1. The mutual inductance between phase-a windings of stator
and rotor.
2. The mutual inductance if the windings are replaced with their
equivalent sinusoidally distributed windings.

Plot the inductance variations versus the position of the rotor.

Admin
Solution

Let us first consider equivalent sinusoidal windings that produce mmf waveforms with equal peaks to the fundamental component of the mmf of the aforementioned two windings. This can be done using a proce-dure similar to that in. It can be shown that the equivalent sinusoidal windings to the given stator and rotor winding arrangements have peak turns densities of 59.4 and 55.1, respectively. The turns density expressions for the equivalent windings will, therefore, begiven as follows:

\(n_{s}\left(\phi _{s} \right)= \frac{N_{s} }{2}\mid \sin \left(\phi _{s} \right)\mid, N_{s} = 118.8\)
\(n_{r}\left(\phi _{r} \right)= \frac{N_{r} }{2}\mid \sin \left(\phi _{r} \right)\mid, N_{r} = 110.2\)

Calculation of inductances for the equivalent sinusoidal windings is straight forward as shown in Equations 2.24

\(L_{aa}= \frac{L_{1}i_{a}+ \lambda_{aa} }{i_{a}}= L_{1}+ L_{m}= L_{1}+ \mu _{0} \left(\frac{N_{s}}{2} \right)^2\frac{\pi rl}{g }\)

and 2.26.

\(L_{sr}=\mu _{0}\frac{N_{r}}{2}\frac{N_{s}}{2}\frac{rl\pi }{g}\cos \theta _{r}\)

To calculate mutual inductance between the actual distributed wind-ings, we note that the mmf established by the stator winding has the following shape (assuming a 1 A current through the winding).

If we consider a single turn of wire located at \(\phi_{s} \), the flux passing through the surface of the turn due to the current in the stator winding is obtained using the following expression:

\(\Phi _{dist}(\phi _{s})=-\mu _{0}\frac{rl}{g}\int\limits_{\phi _{s} }^{\phi _{s}+\pi}{mmf(\alpha )d\alpha } \)

Evaluation of the aforementioned equation with the mmf expressions of the actual and the equivalent windings yields the following expres-sions (shown for the first quarter of the waveform).

\(\Phi _{dist}(\phi _{s})=\begin{Bmatrix} 1.885e-3\phi _{s} & 0\leq \phi _{s}\leq \frac{\pi }{3} \\ 0.9425e-3\left\lgroup\phi _{s}+\frac{\pi }{4} \right\rgroup &\frac{\pi }{3}\leq \phi _{s}\leq \frac{\pi }{2} \\ 0.9425e-3\left\lgroup-\phi _{s}+\frac{4\pi }{3} \right\rgroup & \frac{\pi }{2}\leq \phi _{s}\leq \frac{2\pi }{3} \\ 1.885e-3(\pi-\phi _{s}) & \frac{2\pi }{3}\leq \phi _{s}\leq \pi \end{Bmatrix}\)

For the equivalent sinusoidally distributed winding (with unity current),

\(\Phi _{sin}(\phi _{s})=\mu _{0}\frac{N_{s}rl}{g}\sin (\phi _{s})\)

For the original winding (with unity current), the result will be a piecewise linear waveform. The following figure shows the variation of the flux through the single turn located at \(\phi_{s} \) for the original and the equivalent winding.

As shown, the difference between the flux of the actual winding and the one estimated by an equivalent sinusoidal winding is quite small. We already have an expression (Equation 2.26)

\(L_{sr}=\mu _{0}\frac{N_{r}}{2}\frac{N_{s}}{2}\frac{rl\pi }{g}\cos \theta _{r}\)

to calculate the mutual inductance between the stator and rotor windings if sinusoidal distribu-tion is assumed. To calculate the winding inductance for the original windings, we note that the rotor winding has 50 turns located at \(θ_{r} + π/3 (and θ_{r }+ π/3 + π)\) and another 50 turns at \(θ_{r} + 2π/3 (and θ_{r} + 2π/3 + π)\). The expression for the flux linkage of the rotor winding due to the stator winding’s mmf will, therefore, be as follows:

\(L(\theta _{r})=\frac{50\left\lgroup\Phi _{dist}\left\lgroup\theta _{r}+\frac{\pi }{3} \right\rgroup +\Phi _{dist}\left\lgroup\theta _{r}+\frac{2\pi }{3} \right\rgroup \right\rgroup }{i_{a}=1 A}\)

The following figure shows the variation of the mutual inductances for the original and the equivalent windings as a function rotor posi-tion. The dark curve shows the sinusoidal approximation and the white trace shows the piecewise linear inductance for the original windings. As seen, the sinusoidally distributed approximation produces a highly accurate estimation of the actual inductance variations.
