Solution

This problem is analogous to , except that we are now considering the behavior of an off-axis composite. The magnitude of each stress component is indicated in Figure 5.9. Based on the sign conventions reviewed in Section 2.5, the algebraic sign of each stress component is

\(\sigma _{xx}=200 MPa \sigma _{yy}=-30 MPa \tau _{xy}=-50 MPa\)

Fiber angles are measured from the +x-axis to the +1-axis (or, equivalently, from the +y-axis to the +2-axis). In accordance with the right-hand rule, the fiber angle in Figure 5.9 is algebraically positive: θ = +30°. Strains are calculated using Equation 5.29. Since ΔT = ΔM = 0, we have

\(\left \{ \begin{matrix} \varepsilon _{xx} \\\varepsilon _{yy} \\ \gamma _{xy} \end{matrix} \right \}=\left [ \begin{matrix} \overline{S}_{11} & \overline{S}_{12} & \overline{S}_{16} \\ \overline{S}_{12} & \overline{S}_{22} & \overline{S}_{26} \\ \overline{S}_{16} & \overline{S}_{26} & \overline{S}_{66}\end{matrix} \right ]\left \{ \begin{matrix} \sigma _{xx} \\ \sigma _{yy} \\ \tau _{xy} \end{matrix} \right \}\)

Recall that elements of the reduced compliance matrix, the [S] matrix, were calculated as a part of . Each term within the transformed reduced compliance matrix, the \([\overline{S}]\) matrix, can therefore be calculated via a straightforward application of Equations 5.22. Calculation of \(\overline{S}_{11}\), for example, proceeds as follows:

\(\overline{S}_{11}=S_{11}\cos^4\theta +(2S_{12}+S_{66})\cos^2\theta \sin^2\theta +S_{22}\sin^4\theta\) (5.22)

 

\(\overline{S}_{11}=(5.88 × 10^{-12})\cos^4(30°)+\left\{2(-1.76×10^{-12})+76.9×10^{-12}\right\} \cos^2(30°)\sin^2(30°)+(100.0×10^{-12})\sin^4(30°)\)

 

\(\overline{S}_{11}=23.32×10^{-12}(Pa^{-1})\)

 

Calculating the remaining elements of the \([\overline{S}]\) matrix in similar fashion and applying Equation 5.29, we find:

\(\left \{ \begin{matrix} \varepsilon _{xx} \\\varepsilon _{yy} \\ \gamma _{xy} \end{matrix} \right \}=\left [ \begin{matrix} \overline{S}_{11} & \overline{S}_{12} & \overline{S}_{16}\\ \overline{S}_{12} & \overline{S}_{22} & \overline{S}_{26} \\ \overline{S}_{16} & \overline{S}_{26} & \overline{S}_{66}\end{matrix} \right ]\left \{ \begin{matrix} \sigma _{xx} \\ \sigma _{yy} \\ \tau _{xy}\end{matrix} \right \}+\Delta T\begin{Bmatrix} \alpha _{xx} \\ \alpha _{yy} \\ \alpha _{xy}\end{Bmatrix} +\Delta M\begin{Bmatrix} \beta _{xx} \\ \beta _{yy} \\ \beta _{xy} \end{Bmatrix} \) (5.29)

\(\left \{ \begin{matrix} \varepsilon _{xx} \\\varepsilon _{yy} \\ \gamma _{xy} \end{matrix} \right \}=\left [ \begin{matrix} 23.32\times 10^{-12} & 4.327\times 10^{-12} & -33.72\times 10^{-12} \\ 4.327\times 10^{-12} & 70.38\times 10^{-12} & -47.79\times 10^{-12} \\ -33.72\times 10^{-12} & -47.79\times 10^{-12} & 101.3\times 10^{-12}\end{matrix} \right ]\left\lgroup\frac{1}{Pa} \right\rgroup\left \{ \begin{matrix} 200\times 10^6 \\ -30\times 10^6 \\ -50\times 10^6 \end{matrix} \right \}(Pa) \)

Completing the matrix multiplication indicated, we obtain:

\(\left \{ \begin{matrix} \varepsilon _{xx} \\\varepsilon _{yy} \\ \gamma _{xy} \end{matrix} \right \}=\left \{ \begin{matrix} 6220 μm/m \\ 1144 μm/m \\ -10375 μ rad \end{matrix} \right \}\)
