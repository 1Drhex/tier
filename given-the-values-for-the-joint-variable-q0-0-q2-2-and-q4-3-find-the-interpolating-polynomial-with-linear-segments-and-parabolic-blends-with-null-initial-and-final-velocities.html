Guest Question.

Given the values for the joint variable: q(0) = 0, q(2) = 2, and q(4) = 3,find the interpolating polynomial with linear segments and parabolic blends with null initial and final velocities.

Admin
Solution

The interpolating trajectory given by a sequence of linear polynomials with parabolic blends can be analytically described as

\(q(t) = \begin{cases}a_{k1} \left(t – t_{k}\right) + a_{k0} & t_{k} + \frac{\Delta t^\prime _{k} }{2} \leq t \lt t_{k + 1}- \frac{\Delta t^\prime _{k + 1}}{2} \\b_{k2}\left(t – t_{k}\right)^{2} + b_{k1}\left(t – t_{k}\right) + b_{k0} & t_{k} – \frac{\Delta t_{k}^\prime }{2} \leq t \lt t_{k}-\frac{\Delta t^\prime _{k} }{2}\end{cases} \)

 

The velocity in the linear segments is \(\dot{q}_{k-1,k} \) =\(\begin{cases}\dot{q}_{i}& k = 1 \quad \\\frac{q_{k} – q_{k -1} }{\Delta \acute{t_{k – 1}}} & K = 2, …..,N \quad \\ \dot{q}_{f} & k = N + 1\end{cases} \)

and then the coefficients of the linear polynomials are

\(a_{k0}\) = \(q_{k} \) \( a_{k1} \) = \( \dot{q}_{k, k+1} \) k = 1,…,Nâˆ’1.

Imposing continuity of velocity between the linear and parabolic polynomials at time instants \( \left( t_{k} – {\Delta t^{\prime }_{k} }/{2}\right) \) and \( \left( t_{k} + {\Delta t^{\prime }_{k} }/{2}\right) \) allows computing the coefficients

\( b_{k1} \) = \( \frac{ \dot{q}_{k, k + 1} + \dot{q}_{k – 1, k} }{2} \)

\( b_{k2} \) = \( \frac{ \dot{q}_{k, k+1} – \dot{q}_{k – 1, k} }{2 \Delta \acute{t}_{k }} \) k = 1 ,… , N.

Then, imposing continuity of position at \( \left( t_{k} + {\Delta t^{\prime }_{k} }/{2}\right) \) yields

\( b_{k0} = q_{k} + \left(\dot{q}_{k, k+1} – \dot{q}_{k – 1,k} \right)\frac{ \Delta t^{\prime }_{k} }{8} \) k = 1, . . ., N;

it can be verified that this choice guarantees continuity of position also at \( \left( t_{k} – {\Delta t^{\prime }_{k} }/{2}\right) \) .

The duration of the blend times is chosen as \( \Delta t^{\prime }_{k} \) = 0.2 for k = 1, 2, 3. With the given data, the files used to compute the above coefficients, and to generate the time behaviour of position, velocity and acceleration can be found in Folder 4-6.

The coefficients are:

\(a_{10} \) = 0 \(a_{11} \) = 1 \(a_{20} \) = 2 \(a_{21} \) = 0.5

\(b_{10} \) = 0.025 \(b_{11} \) = 0.5

\(b_{12} \) = 2.5 \(b_{20} \) = 1.9875

\(b_{21} \) = 0.75 \(a_{22} \) = -1.25

\(b_{30} \) = 2.9875 \(b_{31} \) = 0.25

\(b_{32} \) = -1.25
