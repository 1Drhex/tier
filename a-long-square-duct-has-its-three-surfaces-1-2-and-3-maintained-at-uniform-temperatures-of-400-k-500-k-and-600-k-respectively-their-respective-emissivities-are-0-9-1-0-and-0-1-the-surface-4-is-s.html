Guest Question.

A long square duct has its three surfaces 1, 2, and 3 maintained at uniform temperatures of 400 K, 500 K and 600 K, respectively, their respective emissivities are 0.9, 1.0 and 0.1. The surface 4 is subjected to a uniform heat flux of \(5000 W/m^2\) and emissivity of 0.8. Determine the net radiative heat fluxes from surfaces 1, 2, and 3 and temperature of surface 4. Assume all surfaces are gray and diffuse.

Admin
Solution

Given : A long square duct
\(T_1 = 400 K, ε_1 = 0.9\)
\(T_2 = 500 K, ε_2 = 1.0\)
\(T_3 = 600 K, ε_3 = 0.1\)
\(q_4 = 5000 W/m^2, ε_4 = 0.8.\)
To find :
(i) Radiation heat flux \(q_1,\)
(ii) Radiation heat flux \(q_2,\)
(iii) Radiation heat flux \(q_3,\)
(iv) Temperature of surface 4.

Analysis : The energy balance For surface 1:
\(\frac{E_{b1} -J_1}{\frac{1 – ε_1}{ε_1 A_1}} + \frac{J_2 – J_1}{ \frac{1}{A_1 F_{1 – 2}}}+ \frac{J_2 – J_1}{ \frac{1}{A_1 F_{1 – 3}}}+\frac{J_4 -J_1}{\frac{1}{A_1 F_{1 – 4}}} =0\)…(i)
For surface 2:
\(\frac{E_{b2} -J_2}{\frac{1 – ε_2}{ε_2 A_2}} + \frac{J_1 – J_2}{ \frac{1}{A_2 F_{2 – 1}}}+ \frac{J_3 – J_2}{ \frac{1}{A_2 F_{2 – 3}}}+\frac{J_4 -J_2}{\frac{1}{A_2 F_{2 – 4}}} =0\)…(ii)
For surface 3:
\(\frac{E_{b3} -J_3}{\frac{1 – ε_3}{ε_3 A_3}} + \frac{J_1 – J_3}{ \frac{1}{A_3 F_{3 – 1}}}+ \frac{J_2 – J_3}{ \frac{1}{A_3 F_{3 – 2}}}+\frac{J_4 -J_3}{\frac{1}{A_3 F_{3 – 4}}} =0\)…(iii)
For surface 4:
\(q_4 + \frac{J_1 – J_4}{ \frac{1}{A_4 F_{4 – 1}}}+ \frac{J_2 – J_4}{ \frac{1}{A_4 F_{4 – 2}}}+\frac{J_3 -J_4}{\frac{1}{A_4 F_{4 – 3}}} =0\)…(iv)
where,
\(E_{b1} = σ T_1^4 = 5.67 × 10^{–8} × (400)^4 = 1451.52 W/m^2\)
\(E_{b2} = σ T_2^4 = 5.67 × 10^{–8} × (500)^4 = 3543.75 W/m^2\)
\(E_{b3} = σ T_3^4 = 5.67 × 10^{–8} × (600)^4 = 7348.32 W/m^2\)
The view factors
We assume diagonally imaginary surface 5 as shown in Fig.
\(F_{5–1} =F_{5–2}= F_{5–4} = F_{5–3} = 0.5\)
\(F_{1–5} = \frac{A_5}{A_1} F_{5–1} = \sqrt{2} × 0.5 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}=0.7071\)

\(F_{1–1} +F_{1–2} + F_{1–5} = 1\)
\(→ F_{1–2} = 1 – F_{1–5} = 0.2928\)
\(F_{1–4} =F_{1–2} = 0.2928\)
\(F_{2–1} = \frac{A_1}{A_2}F_{1–2} = 0.2928, F_{2–3} = 0.2928\)
(By symmetry)
\(F_{2–4} =F_{1–3} = 1 – F_{1–2} – F_{1–4}\)
= 1 – 0.2928 – 0.2928 = 0.4142

Areas :\( A_1 =A_2 = A_3 = A_4 = A\)
Solution of above simultaneous equations gives
\(J_1 = 1843.1 W/m^2\)
\(J_3 = 4555.2 W/m^2\)
\(J_4 = 8342.1 W/m^2\)
\(J_2 =E_{b2} = 3543.75 W/m^2(since black ε_2 = 1)\)
The heat fluxes :
\(q_1 =\frac{E_{b1} -J_1}{\frac{1 – ε_1}{ε_1 A_1}}=\frac{ε_1}{1 – ε_1}(E_{b1} – J_1)\)
\(= \frac{0.9}{1 -0.9} × (1451.2 – 1843.1)\)
\(= – 3527.1 W/m^2. \)
Since surface 2 is black, its surface resistance will be zero, the emissive power equals the heat flux.
\(q_2 =F_{2–1} (E_{b2} – J_1) + F_{2–3} (E_{b2} – J_3)+ F_{2–4} (E_{b2} – J_4)\)
\(= 0.2928 × (3543.75 – 1843.1) + 0.2928 × (3543.75 – 4555.2) + 0.4142 × [13543.75 – 8342.1]\)
\(= – 1785.6 W/m^2. \)
\(q_3 = \frac{ε_3}{1 – ε_3}(E_{b3} – J_3)\)
\(= \frac{0.1}{1 – 0.1} × (7348.32 – 4555.2)\)
\(= 310.34 W/m^2\)
\(q_4 =\frac{ε_4}{1 – ε_4}(E_{b4} – J_4)\)
\(E_{b4}= J_4 +\frac{1 – ε_4}{ε_4}q_4\)

\( =8342.1×\frac{1 – 0.8}{0.8} 5000 = 9592.1\)
Further \(E_{b4} = σ T_4^4\)

or \(T_4^4 = \frac{9592.1}{5.67 × 10^{-8}} → T_4 \)
= 641.3 K.
