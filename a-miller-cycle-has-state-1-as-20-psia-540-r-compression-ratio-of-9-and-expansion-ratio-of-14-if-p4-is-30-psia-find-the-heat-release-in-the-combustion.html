Guest Question.

A Miller cycle has state 1 as 20 psia, 540 R, compression ratio of 9 and expansion ratio of 14. If \(P _{4}\) is 30 psia find the heat release in the combustion.

Admin
Solution

Solve the problem with constant heat capacity.

Compression 1 to 2: \(s _{2}= s _{1} \Rightarrow\) From Eq.6.24 and Eq.6.25

\(\begin{aligned}& T _{2}= T _{1}\left( v _{1} / v _{2}\right)^{ k -1}=540 \times 9^{0.4}=1300.4 R \\& P _{2}= P _{1} \times\left( v _{1} / v _{2}\right)^{ k }=20 \times 9^{1.4}=433.5 psia\end{aligned}\)

The expansion backwards from \(P _{4}\) gives

\(P_{3}=P_{4} \times\left(v_{4} / v_{3}\right)^{k}=P_{4} \times C R^{k}=30 \times 14^{1.4}=1207 \text { psia }\)

Combustion 2 to 3 at constant volume: \(u _{3}= u _{2}+ q _{ H }\)

\(\begin{gathered}T _{3}= T _{2}+ q _{ H } / C _{ v } \text { and } P _{3}= P _{2} \times\left( T _{3} / T _{2}\right) \\T _{3}= T _{2} \times\left( P _{3} / P _{2}\right)=1300.4 \times(1207 / 433.5)=3620.7 R \\q _{ H }= C _{ v }\left( T _{3}- T _{2}\right)=0.171(3620.7-1300.4)= 3 9 6 . 8 Btu / lbm\end{gathered}\)

……………………………………

Eq.6.24 : \(\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}=\left(\frac{v_{1}}{v_{2}}\right)^{k-1}\)

Eq.6.25 : \(\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}=\left(\frac{v_{1}}{v_{2}}\right)^{k}\)
