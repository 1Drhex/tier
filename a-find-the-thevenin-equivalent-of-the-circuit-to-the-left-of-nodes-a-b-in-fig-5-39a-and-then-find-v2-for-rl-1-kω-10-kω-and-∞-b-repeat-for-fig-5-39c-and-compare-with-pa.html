Guest Question.

(a) Find the Thevenin equivalent of the circuit to the left of nodes A-B in Fig. 5-39(a) and then find \(v_{2} \text { for } R_{l}=1 k \Omega, 10 k \Omega, \text { and } \infty\). (b) Repeat for Fig. 5-39(c) and compare with part (a).

Admin
Solution

(a) The Thevenin equivalent of the circuit in Fig. 5-39(a) is shown in Fig. 5-39(b).

 

\(v_{ Th }=\frac{6}{6+3}(15)=10 V \quad \text { and } \quad R_{ Th }=\frac{(3)(6)}{3+6}=2 k \Omega\)

 

By dividing \(v_{ Th } \text { between } R_{ Th } \text { and } R_{l}\) we get

\(v_{2}=\frac{R_{l}}{R_{l}+2}(10)\)

 

\(\begin{array}{ll}\text { For } R_{l}=1 k \Omega, & v_{2}=3.33 V \\\text { For } R_{l}=10 k \Omega, & v_{2}=8.33 V \\\text { For } R_{l}=\infty & v_{2}=10 V\end{array}\)

The output v2 depends on Rl. The operation of the voltage divider is also affected by Rl.

(b) The Thevenin equivalent of the circuit in Fig. 5-39(c) is shown in Fig. 5-12(d). Here we have

\(v_{ Th }=10 V \quad \text { and } \quad R_{ Th }=0\)

and \(v_{2}=v_{ Th }=10 V\) for all values of Rl, that is, the output v2 depends on R1, R2, and vs only and is independent of Rl.
