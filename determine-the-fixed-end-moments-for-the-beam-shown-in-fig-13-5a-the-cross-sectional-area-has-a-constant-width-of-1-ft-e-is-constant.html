Guest Question.

Determine the fixed-end moments for the beam shown in Fig. \(13-5 (\mathrm{a})\). The cross-sectional area has a constant width of \(1 \mathrm{ft} . E\) is constant.

Admin
Solution

Since the real beam supports at \(A\) and \(B\) are fixed, the conjugate beam is free at its ends \(A^{\prime}\) and \(B^{\prime}\). The moment diagrams will be plotted in parts, using the method of superposition discussed in Sec. 4-5. These parts shown on the right in Figs. 13-5b through 13-5d represent the moment diagrams for a simply supported beam loaded with a concentrated force of \(3 \mathrm{k}\) and each of the end moments \(\mathbf{M}_{A}\) and \(\mathbf{M}_{B}\). For span \(A C, \quad I_{A C}=\frac{1}{12}(1)(2)^{3}=0.667 \mathrm{ft}^{4}\), and for span \(C B\), \(I_{C B}=\frac{1}{12}(1)(1)^{3}=0.0833 \mathrm{ft}^{4}\). Using these values, the \(M / E I\) diagrams (loadings) on the conjugate beam are shown on the left in Figs. 13-5b through 13-5d. The resultant forces caused by these “distributed loads” have been computed and are also shown in the figure. Since the slope and deflection at the end \(A\) (or \(B\) ) of the real beam are equal to zero, we require

\(\begin{array}{c} +\uparrow \Sigma F_{y}=0 ; \quad \frac{45.56}{E}+\frac{121.5}{E}-\frac{5.062 M_{A}}{E}-\frac{3.375 M_{A}}{E}-\frac{4.5 M_{A}}{E}-\frac{5.062 M_{B}}{E}-\frac{27 M_{B}}{E}-\frac{4.5 M_{B}}{E}=0 \\167.06-12.94 M_{A}-36.56 M_{B}=0 \\ \end{array}\) (1)

\(\begin{array}{c}\curvearrowleft +\Sigma M_{A^{\prime}}=0 ; \quad \frac{45.56}{E}(6)+\frac{121.5}{E}(10)-\frac{5.062 M_{A}}{E}(3)-\frac{3.375 M_{A}}{E}(4.5)-\frac{4.5 M_{A}}{E}(10)-\frac{5.062 M_{B}}{E}(6) \\ -\frac{27 M_{B}}{E}(10.5)-\frac{4.5 M_{B}}{E}(11)=0 \\ 1488.36-75.37 M_{A}-363.37 M_{B}=0 \end{array}\) (2)

Solving Eqs. (1) and (2) simultaneously, we have

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{A}=(\mathrm{FEM})_{A B}=3.23 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ M_{B}=(\mathrm{FEM})_{B A}=3.43 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \end{array} \)
