Guest Question.

Water at \(40^{\circ }\) F flows at 1.2 ft /s over a metal plate that is maintained at a constant temperature of \(100^{\circ }\) F. Th e plate is 2 ft long (in the fl ow direction) and 18 in. wide. Calculate the heat transferred to the water over the first foot and over the entire length of the plate. Graph the temperature profile at x = 1 ft .

Admin
Solution

The water increases in temperature over the plate length and we expect that more heat is transferred to the water over the first foot than over the last foot.

Assumptions

1. Steady-state conditions exist.
2. Water properties evaluated at the film temperature are constant.

From Appendix Table C.11 for water at \(\left(40+100\right)/2=70^{\circ }F\approx 68^{\circ }F \)

\(\rho=62.4 lbm/ft^{3}\)

 

\(c_{p}=0.9988 BTU/\left(lbm.R^{\circ } \right) \)

 

\(\nu =1.083\times 10^{-5} ft^{2}/s \)

 

\(k_{f}=0.345 BTU/\left(hr.ft.R^{\circ } \right) \)

 

\(\alpha =5.54\times 10^{-3} ft^{2} /hr\)

 

Pr=7.02

 

At x = 1 ft the Reynolds number is

 

\(Re_{x} =\frac{V_{\infty }x }{\nu }=\frac{1.2\left(1\right) }{1.083\times 10^{-5} } =1.108\times 10^{5} \lt 5\times 10^{5} \)

 

Th e flow is laminar, and Equation 7.19 applies

 

\(\frac{\overline{h_{L}L } }{k_{f} } =0.664Pr^{1/3} Re^{1/2}_{L} \)

 

where L = x = 1 ft . Substituting and solving for the average convection coefficient gives

 

\(\overline{h_{L} } =\frac{0.345}{1}\left(0.664\right)\left(7.02\right) ^{1/3} \left(1.108\times 10^{5} \right) ^{1/2}=146 BTU/\left(hr.ft^{2}.R^{\circ } \right)\)

 

The heat transferred to the water over the first foot then is

\(q=\overline{h_{L} } A\left(T_{w}-T_{\infty } \right) =146\left(1\right)\left(1.5\right)\left(100-40\right)=1.314\times 10^{4}BTU/hr\)

Repeating the calculations for x = 2 ft , we write

\(Re_{x}=\frac{V_{\infty }x }{\nu } =\frac{1.2\left(2\right) }{1.083\times x^{-5} } =2.216\times 10^{5}\)

 

\(\overline{h_{L} } =\frac{0.345}{2} \left(0.664\right) \left(7.02\right) ^{1/3}\left(2.216\times 10^{5} \right) ^{1/2}=103.2 BTU/\left(hr.ft^{2}.R^{\circ } \right)\)

 

Th e heat transferred over the entire plate then is

\(q=103.2\left(2\right)\left(1.5\right)\left(100-40\right)=1.858\times 10^{4} BTU/hr\)

 

To graph the temperature profile at x = 1 ft , it is necessary to obtain values for dimensionless temperature
from Figure 7.9. By interpolating values from that graph at Pr = 7, we obtain the values in the first two columns of Table 7.3. At x = 1 ft , y is found with

 

\(\eta =y \sqrt{\frac{V_{\infty } }{\nu _{x} } }=y \sqrt{\frac{1.2}{1.083\times 10^{-5}\left(1\right) } }\)

or

\(y =3.00\times 10^{-3} \eta\)

 

Calculated values of y are provided in the third column of Table 7.2. Corresponding temperatures are found with

 

\(\frac{T-T_{\infty } }{T_{w} -T_{\infty } } =\theta ^{'}\)

 

\(T=\theta ^{'} \left(100-40\right) +40\)

or

\(\underline{T=600^{'}+40} \)

 

Calculations made with the preceding equation are given in the fourth column of Table 7.3. A graph of temperature versus y appears in Figure 7.11.
