Guest Question.

The connecting rod of an oil engine has a mass of 60 kg, the distance between the bearing centres is 1 metre. The diameter of the big end bearing is 120 mm and of the small end bearing is 75 mm. When suspended vertically with a knife-edge through the small end, it makes 100 oscillations in 190 seconds and with knife-edge through the big end it makes 100 oscillations in 165 seconds. Find the moment of inertia of the rod in kg-\(m^{2}\) and the distance of C.G. from the small end centre.

Admin
Solution

Given : m = 60 kg ; \(h_{1}+h_{2}=1 m ; d_{2}\) = 102 mm; \(d_{1}\) = 75 mm

Moment of inertia of the rod

First of all, let us find the radius of gyration of the connecting rod about the centre of gravity (i.e. \(k_{ G }\)).

Let \(h_{1} \text { and } h_{2}\) = Distance of centre of gravity from the small and big end centres respectively,

\(L_{1} \text { and } L_{2} \) = Equivalent length of simple perdulum when the axis of oscillation coincides with the small and big end centres respectively

When the axis of oscillation coincides with the small end centre, then frequency of oscillation,

\(n_{1}\) = 100/190 = 0.526 Hz

When the axis of oscillation coincides with the big end centre, the frequency of oscillation,

\(n_{2}\) = 100/165 = 0.606 Hz

We know that for a simple pendulum,

\(n_{1}=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \sqrt{\frac{g}{L_{1}}} Hz\)

\(\therefore \) \(L_{1}=\frac{g}{\left(2 \pi n_{1}\right)^{2}}=\frac{9.81}{(2 \pi \times 0.526)^{2}}=0.9 m\)

Similarly \(L_{2}=\frac{g}{\left(2 \pi n_{2}\right)^{2}}=\frac{9.81}{(2 \pi \times 0.606)^{2}}=0.67 m\)

We know that \(L_{1}=\frac{k_{ G }^{2}+\left(h_{1}\right)^{2}}{h_{1}} \text { or } k_{ G }^{2}=L_{1} \cdot h_{1}-\left(h_{1}\right)^{2}\) …(i)

Similarly \(k_{ G }^{2}=L_{2} \cdot h_{2}-\left(h_{2}\right)^{2}\) …(ii)

From equations (i) and (ii), we have

\(L_{1} \cdot h_{1}-\left(h_{1}\right)^{2}=L_{2} \cdot h_{2}-\left(h_{2}\right)^{2}\)

 

\(0.9 \times h_{1}-\left(h_{1}\right)^{2}=0.67\left(1-h_{1}\right)-\left(1-h_{1}\right)^{2}\) \(\ldots\left(\because h_{1}+h_{2}=1 m \right)\)

 

\(=0.67-0.67 h_{1}-1-\left(h_{1}\right)^{2}+2 h_{1}\)

 

\(0.9 h_{1}+0.67 h_{1}-2 h_{1}=-0.33 \quad \text { or } \quad-0.43 h_{1}=-0.33\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(h_{1}=0.33 / 0.43=0.767 m\)

Substituting the value of \(h_{1}\) in equation (i), we have

\(k_{ G }^{2}=0.9 \times 0.767-(0.767)^{2}=0.69-0.59=0.1 m ^{2}\)

We know that mass moment of inertia of the rod,

\(I=m \cdot k_{ G }^{2}=60 \times 0.1=6 kg – m ^{2}\)

Distance of C.G. from the small end centre

We have calculated above that the distance of C.G. from the small end centre,

\(h_{1}=0.767 m\)
