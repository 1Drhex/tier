Guest Question.

Derive the differential equations of the vehicle damping, as illustrated in Figure 7.7. Note this system was used in without damping.

Admin
Solution

The forms of the kinetic energy and potential energy are as in . The form of Rayleigh’s dissipation functions for this example is

\( \widetilde{S} =-\frac{1}{2}c_{1}[\dot{y}_{1}-(\dot{x}_{1}+a\dot{\theta })]^{2}-\frac{1}{2} c_{2}[\dot{y}_{2}-(\dot{x}_{1}-b\dot{\theta })]^{2}- \frac{1}{2}c_{3}[(\dot{x}-{1}-c\dot{\theta })-\dot{x}_{2}]^{2} \) (a)

Using the Lagrangian of Equation (c) of , application of the nonconservative form of Lagrange’s equations Equation (7.19) yields

\(\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{x}}\right) -\frac{\partial L}{\partial {x}_{1}}-\frac{\partial \widetilde{S} }{\partial \dot{x}_{1}}=0 \)

 

\(\frac{d}{dt}\left[\frac{1}{2}m_{1}(2\dot{x}_{1}) \right] -\left\{\frac{1}{2}k_{1}(2)[y_{1}(t)-(x_{1}+a\theta )](-1)-\frac{1}{2}k_{2}(2)[y_{2}(t)-(x_{1}-b\theta )](-1) \right.\\ \\ \left. -\frac{1}{2}k_{3}(2)[x_{1}+c\theta -x_{2}](1) \right\} \)

\( -\left\{-\frac{1}{2}c_{1}(2)[\dot{y}_{1}-(\dot{x}_{1}+a\dot{\theta })](-1)-\frac{1}{2}c_{2}(2)[\dot{y}_{2}-(\dot{x}_{1}-b\dot{\theta })](-1) \right.\\ \\ \left. -\frac{1}{2}c_{3}(2)[(\dot{x}_{1}+c\dot{\theta })-\dot{x}_{2}] \right\}=0 \) (b)

\( Q_{i}=\frac{\partial \widetilde{S} }{\partial \dot{x}_{\iota }}+Q_{i,nv} \) (7.19)

Application of Lagrange’s equations for \(\theta\) leads to

\(\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\theta }} \right)-\frac{\partial L}{\partial \theta }-\frac{\partial \widetilde{S}}{\partial \dot{\theta }} = 0 \)

 

\( \frac{d}{dt}\left[\frac{1}{2}I(2\dot{\theta }) \right]-\left\{-\frac{1}{2}k_{1}(2)[y_{1}(t)-(x_{1}+a\theta )] (-a)-\frac{1}{2}k_{2}(2)[y_{2}(t)-(x_{1}-b\theta )](b)\right. \\ \left.-\frac{1}{2}k_{3}(2)x_{1}+c\theta -x_{2}](c) \right\}-\left\{-\frac{1}{2} c_{1}(2)[\dot{y}_{1}-(\dot{x}_{1}+a\dot{\theta })](-a)\right. \\ \left.-\frac{1}{2}c_{2}(2)[\dot{y}_{2}-(\dot{x}_{1}-b\dot{\theta })](b) \right\}=0 \) (c)

Application of Lagrange’s equations for \(x_2\) leads to

\(\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{x}_{2}}\right)-\frac{\partial L}{\partial{x}_{2}}-\frac{\partial\widetilde{S} }{\partial \dot{x}_{2}}=0 \)

 

\(\frac{d}{dt}\left[\frac{1}{2}m_{2}(2\dot{x}_{2}) \right] -\left\{-\frac{1}{2}k_{3}(2)[x_{1}+c\theta -x_{2}](1) \right\} \)

\(-\left[-\frac{1}{2}c_{3}(2)(\dot{x}_{1}+c\dot{\theta }-\dot{x}_{2}(-1) \right] =0 \) (d)

Equations (b) through (d) are rearranged and written in a matrix form leading to

\(\begin{bmatrix} m_{1} &0 & 0\\ 0 & I & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & m_{2} \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} \ddot{x}_{1} \\ \ddot{\theta } \\ \ddot{x}_{2} \end{matrix} \right] +\begin{bmatrix} c_{1}+c_{2}+c_{3} & c_{1}a- c_{2}b+c_{3}c & -c_{3}\\ c_{1}a -c_{2}b+c_{3}c & c_{1}a^{2}+c_{2}b^{2}+c_{3}c^{2} & -c_{3}c \\ -c_{3 } & -c_{3}c & c_{3} \end{bmatrix}\left[\begin{matrix}\dot{x}_{1} \\ \dot{\theta } \\ \dot{x}_{2} \end{matrix} \right] \)

 

\( +\begin{bmatrix} k_{1}+k_{2}+k_{3} & k_{1}a -k_{2}b+k_{3}c & -k_{3}\\ k_{1}a -k_{2}b +k_{3}c & k_{1}a^{2}+k_{2}b^{2}+k_{3}c^{2} & -k_{3}c \\-k_{3} & -k_{3}c &k_{3}\end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ \theta \\ x_{2} \end{matrix} \right] \)

 

\( =\begin{bmatrix}k_{1}y_{1}(t)+k_{2}y_{2}(t)+c_{1}\dot{y}_{1}(t)+c_{2}\dot{y}_{2}(t) \\ k_{1}ay_{1}(t)-k_{2}by_{2}(t)+c_{1}a\dot{y}_{1}(t)+c_{2}b\dot{y}_{2}(t) \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \) (e)
