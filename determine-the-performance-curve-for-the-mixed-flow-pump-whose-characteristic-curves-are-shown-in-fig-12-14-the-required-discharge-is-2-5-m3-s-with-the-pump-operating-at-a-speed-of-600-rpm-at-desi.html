Guest Question.

Determine the performance curve for the mixed-flow pump whose characteristic curves are shown in Fig. 12.14. The required discharge is \(2.5 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{s}\) with the pump operating at a speed of \(600 \mathrm{~rpm}\). At design efficiency, what are the power and the NPSH requirements? Water is being pumped.

Admin
Solution

From Fig. \(12.14\) the design efficiency is \(\eta_{P}=0.85\) and the corresponding design values for the coefficients are

\(C_{Q}=0.15 \quad C_{H}=0.067 \quad C_{W}=0.0115 \quad C_{\text {NPSH }}=0.035\)

The rotational speed in radians per second is

\(\omega=600 \times \frac{\pi}{30}=62.8 \mathrm{~rad} / \mathrm{s}\)

The pump diameter is computed with Eq. 12.3.4:

\(C_{Q}=\frac{Q}{\omega D^{3}} \) flow rate coefficient (12.3.4)

\(\begin{aligned}D &=\left(\frac{Q}{\omega C_{Q}}\right)^{1 / 3} \\\\&=\left(\frac{2.5}{62.8 \times 0.15}\right)^{1 / 3}=0.64 \mathrm{~m}\end{aligned}\)

With Eqs. 12.3.2 and 12.3.6, the required power and \(\mathrm{NPSH}\) are computed:

\(C_{\dot{W}}=\frac{\dot{W}}{\rho \omega^{3} D^{5}} \quad \text { power coefficient }\) (12.3.2)

\(C_{H}=\frac{g H}{\omega^{2} D^{2}} \quad \) head coefficient (12.3.6)

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{W}_{P} &=\rho \omega^{3} D^{5} C_{W} \\\\ &=1000 \times 62.8^{3} \times 0.64^{5} \times 0.0115=3.1 \times 10^{5} \mathrm{~W} \\\\N P S H &=\frac{\omega^{2} D^{2}}{g} C_{N P S H} \\\\&=\frac{62.8^{2} \times 0.64^{2}}{9.81} \times 0.035=5.8 \mathrm{~m}\end{aligned}\)

Hence, the required power is \(310 \mathrm{~kW}\) and the required \(\mathrm{NPSH}\) is \(5.8 \mathrm{~m}\). The performance curve is constructed using Eqs. 12.3 .10 and 12.3.11:

\(\left(C_{H}\right)_{1}=\left(C_{H}\right)_{2}\) or \(\frac{H_{2}}{H_{1}}=\frac{g_{1}}{g_{2}}\left(\frac{\omega_{2}}{\omega_{1}}\right)^{2}\left(\frac{D_{2}}{D_{1}}\right)^{2}\) (12.3.10)

\(\left(C_{Q}\right)_{1}=\left(C_{Q}\right)_{2}\) or \(\frac{Q_{2}}{Q_{1}}=\frac{\omega_{2}}{\omega_{1}}\left(\frac{D_{2}}{D_{1}}\right)^{3}\) (12.3.11)

\(\begin{aligned}H_{2} &=\frac{\omega_{2}^{2} D_{2}^{2}}{g}\left(C_{H}\right)_{1} \\\\&=\frac{62.8^{2} \times 0.64^{2}}{9.81}\left(C_{H}\right)_{1}=165\left(C_{H}\right)_{1} \\\\Q_{2} &=\omega_{2} D_{2}^{3}\left(C_{Q}\right)_{1} \\\\&=62.8 \times 0.64^{3}\left(C_{Q}\right)_{1}=16.5\left(C_{Q}\right)_{1}\end{aligned}\)

 

Values of \(\left(C_{H}\right)_{1}\) and \(\left(C_{Q}\right)_{1}\) are read from Fig. \(12.14\), along with \(\eta_{P}\) and placed in the table shown below. Columns 4 and 5 are \(Q_{2}\) and \(H_{2}\) computed from the similarity equations. At best efficiency, \(H_{2}=10.4 \mathrm{~m}\) and \(Q_{2}=2.54 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{s}\). The characteristic curve and efficiency curve are plotted in Fig. E12.5.
