Guest Question.

Refer to the circuit of Figure 21–13.

a. Calculate the values of \(R_{L}\) and C for the circuit to have a resonant frequency of 200 kHz and a bandwidth of 16 kHz.
b. Use the designed component values to determine the power dissipated by the circuit at resonance.
c. Solve for \(\nu_{out}(t)\) at resonance.

Admin
Solution

a. Because the circuit is at resonance, we must have the following conditions:

\(Q_{S}=\frac{f_{S}}{BW} \)

 

\(=\frac{200kHz}{16kHz} \)

 

\(=12.5\)

 

\(X_{L}=2\pi fL\)

 

\(=2\pi (200kHz)(200\mu H)\)

 

\(=251.3 \Omega\)

 

\(R=R_{L}+R_{coil}=\frac{X_{L}}{Q_{s}}\)

 

\(=20.1 \Omega\)

and so \(R_{L}\) must be

\(R_{L} =20.1\Omega -5\Omega =15.1\Omega \)

Since \(X_{C} = X_{L}\), we determine the capacitance as

\(C=\frac{1}{2\pi fX_{C}} \)

 

\(=\frac{1}{2\pi (200kHz)(251.3\Omega )} \)

 

\(=3.17nF(\equiv 0.00317\mu F)\)

b. The power at resonance is found from Equation 21–20 as

\(P_{max}=I^{2}_{max}R=\frac{E^{2}}{R} \) (21-20)

\(P_{max}=\frac{E^{2}}{R} =\frac{\left(\frac{30V}{\sqrt{2} } \right)^{2} }{20.1\Omega } \)

 

\(=22.4 W\)

c. We see from the circuit of Figure 21–13 that the voltage \(\nu_{out}(t)\) may be determined by applying the voltage divider rule to the circuit. However, we must first convert the source voltage from time domain into phasor domain as follows:

\(e(t)=30\sin\omega t\Leftrightarrow E=21.21V\angle0^{\circ}\)

Now, applying the voltage divider rule to the circuit, we have

\(V_{out}=\frac{(R_{1}+j\omega L)}{R} E\)

 

\(=\frac{(5\Omega +j251.3\Omega )}{20.1\Omega } 21.21V\angle0^{\circ}\)

 

\(=(251.4\Omega \angle88.86^{\circ})(1.056A\angle0^{\circ})\)

 

\(=265.5 V\angle88.86^{\circ}\)

which in time domain is given as

\(\nu _{out}(t)=375 \sin(\omega t+88.86^{\circ})\)
