Laplace Transform Solution; Eigenvalues and Poles

PROBLEM: Given the system represented in state space by Eqs. (4.99),

\(\dot{x} =\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ -24 & -26 & -9 \end{matrix} \right] x+\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right] e^{-t}\)

 

\(y=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 0\end{matrix} \right] x\)

 

\(x(0)=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right] \)

do the following:

a. Solve the preceding state equation and obtain the output for the given exponential input.

b. Find the eigenvalues and the system poles.

Admin
Solution

a. We will solve the problem by finding the component parts of Eq. (4.96),

\(X(s)=(sI-A)^{-1}x(0)+(sI-A)^{-1}BU(s)\)

 

\(=\frac{adj(sI-A)}{\det (sI-A)} \left[x(0)+BU(s)\right]\)

followed by substitution into Eq. (4.97).

\(Y(s)=CX(s)+DU(s)\)

First obtain A and B by comparing Eq. (4.99a) to Eq. (4.92).

\(\dot{x} =Ax+Bu\)

Since

\(sI=\left[\begin{matrix} s & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & s & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & s \end {matrix} \right]\)

then

\((sI-A)=\left[\begin{matrix} s & -1 & 0 \\ 0 & s & -1 \\ 24 & 26 & s+9 \end{matrix} \right]\)

and

\((sI-A)^{-1}= \frac{\left[\begin{matrix} (s^{2}+9s+26 ) & (s+9) & 1 \\ -24 & s^{2}+9s & s \\ -24s & -(26s+24) & s^{2} \end{matrix} \right]}{s^{3}+9s^{2}+26s+24 }\)

Since U(s) is 1/(s + 1) (the Laplace transform for \(e^{-t}\)), X(s) can be calculated. Rewriting Eq. (4.96) as

\(X(s)=(sI-A)^{-1} \left[x(s+BU(s))\right]\)

and using B and x(0) from Eqs. (4.99a) and (4.99c), respectively, we get

\(X_{1}(s) =\frac{(s^{3}+10s^{2}+37s+29 )}{(s+1)(s+2)(s+3)(s+4)}\)

 

\(X_{2}(s) =\frac{(2s^{2}-21s-24)}{(s+1)(s+2)(s+3)(s+4)}\)

 

\(X_{3}(s) =\frac{s(2s^{2}-21s-24 )}{(s+1)(s+2)(s+3)(s+4)}\)

The output equation is found from Eq. (4.99b). Performing the indicated addition yields

\(Y(s)\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 0\end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} X_{1}(s) \\ X_{2}(s) \\ X_{3}(s) \end{matrix} \right] =X_{1}(s)+X_{2}(s)\)

or

\(Y(s)=\frac{(s^{3}+12s^{2}+16s+5)}{(s+1)(s+2)(s+3)(s+4)}\)

 

\(=\frac{-6.5}{s+2}+\frac{19}{s+3} -\frac{11.5}{s+4}\)

where the pole at -1 canceled a zero at -1. Taking the inverse Laplace transform,

\(y(t)=-6.5e^{-2t} +19e^{-3t}-11.5e^{-4t}\)

b. The denominator of Eq. (4.102),

\((sI-A)^{-1}= \frac{\left[\begin{matrix} (s^{2}+9s+26 ) & (s+9) & 1 \\ -24 & s^{2}+9s & s \\ -24s & -(26s+24) & s^{2} \end{matrix} \right]}{s^{3}+9s^{2}+26s+24 }\)

which is \(\det (sI-A)\), is also the denominator of the system’s transfer function. Thus, \(\det (sI-A)=0\) furnishes both the poles of the system and the eigenvalues -2, – 3, and -4.
