Guest Question.

Aircraft B has a constant speed of 150 m /s as it passes the bottom of a circular loop of 400-m radius. Aircraft A flying horizontally in the plane of the loop passes 100 m directly below B at a constant speed of 100 m /s. (a) Determine the instantaneous velocity and acceleration which A appears to have to the pilot of B, who is fixed to his rotating aircraft. (b) Compare your results for part (a) with the case of erroneously treating the pilot of aircraft B as nonrotating.

Admin
Solution

(a). We begin by clearly defining the rotating coordinate system x-y-z which best helps us to answer the questions. With x-y-z attached to aircraft B as shown, the terms \(v _{\text {rel }} \text { and } a _{\text {rel }}\) in Eqs. 5 /12 and 5 /14 will be the desired results. The terms in Eq. 5 /12 are

 

\(v _{A}= V _{B}+\omega \times r + v _{ rel }\) (5/12)

 

\(a _{A}= a _{B}+\dot{\omega} \times r +\omega \times(\omega \times r )+2 \omega \times v _{ rel }+ a _{ rel }\) (5/14)

 

\(\begin{aligned}& v _{A}=100 i m / s \\& v _{B}=150 i m / s\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\omega=\frac{v_{B}}{\rho} k =\frac{150}{400} k =0.375 k\) rad/s

 

\(r = r _{A / B}=-100 j\) m

 

Eq. 5/12: \(v _{A}= v _{B}+\omega \times r + v _{\text {rel }}\)

 

\(100 i =150 i +0.375 k \times(-100 j )+ v _{\text {rel }}\)

 

Solving for \(v _{\text {rel }}\) gives \(v _{\text {rel }}=-87.5 i\) m/s Ans.

 

The terms in Eq. 5 /14, in addition to those listed above, are

 

\(a _{A}= 0\)

 

\(a _{B}=\frac{v_{B}^{2}}{\rho} j =\frac{150^{2}}{400} j =56.2 j m / s ^{2}\)

 

\(\dot{\omega}=0\)

 

Eq. 5/14: \(a _{A}= a _{B}+\dot{\omega} \times r +\omega \times(\omega \times r )+2 \omega \times v _{ rel }+ a _{ rel }\)

 

\(0 =56.2 j + 0 \times(-100 j )+0.375 k \times[0.375 k \times(-100 j )]\)

 

\(+2[0.375 k \times(-87.5 i )]+ a _{\text {rel }}\)

 

Solving for \(a _{ \text {rel }}\) gives \(a _{ rel }=-4.69 k m / s ^{2}\) Ans.

 

(b) For motion relative to translating frames, we use Eqs. 2 /20 and 2 /21 of Chapter 2:

 

\(v _{A}= v _{B}+ v _{A / B}\) (2/20)

 

\(a _{A}= a _{B}+ a _{A / B}\) (2/21)

 

\(\begin{aligned}& v _{A / B}= v _{A}- v _{B}=100 i -150 i =-50 i m / s \\& a _{A / B}= a _{A}- a _{B}= 0 -56.2 j =-56.2 j m / s ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

Again, we see that \(v _{ rel } \neq v _{A / B} \text { and } a _{ rel } \neq a _{A / B}\). The rotation of pilot B makes a difference in what he observes!

 

The scalar result \(\omega=\frac{v_{B}}{\rho}\) can be obtained by considering a complete circular

 

motion of aircraft B, during which it rotates 2π radians in a time \(t=\frac{2 \pi \rho}{v_{B}}\):

 

\(\omega=\frac{2 \pi}{2 \pi \rho / v_{B}}=\frac{v_{B}}{\rho}\)

 

Because the speed of aircraft B is constant, there is no tangential acceleration and thus the angular acceleration \(\alpha=\dot{\omega}\) of this aircraft is zero.

 

Helpful Hint

1 Because we choose the rotating frame x-y-z to be fixed to aircraft B, the angular velocity of the aircraft and the term ω in Eqs. 5/12 and 5/14 are identical.
