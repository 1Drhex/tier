Guest Question.

A thin brass [E = 14,500 ksi; G = 5,500 ksi] plate is subjected to biaxial stress (Figure P13.57/58). The normal stress in the x direction is known to be twice as large as the normal stress in the y direction. The strain gage measures a normal strain of 775 με at an orientation of θ = 50° in the indicated direction. Determine the magnitudes of the normal stresses \(\sigma_{x} \text { and } \sigma_{y}\) acting on the plate.

Admin
Solution

Derive an expression for ν from Eq. (13.18):

\(G=\frac{E}{2(1+v)} \quad \therefore v=\frac{E}{2 G}-1\)

Determine Poisson’s ratio from this expression:

\(v=\frac{E}{2 G}-1=\frac{14,500 ksi }{2(5,500 ksi )}-1=0.3182\)

 

(a) A strain transformation equation [Eq. (13.3)]

\(\varepsilon_{n}=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2} \theta+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2} \theta+\gamma_{x y} \sin \theta \cos \theta\)

can be written for the normal strain in the direction of strain gage:

\(775 \mu \varepsilon=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)+\gamma_{x y} \sin \left(50^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(50^{\circ}\right)\)

Note that the shear strain \(\gamma_{x y}\) is related to the shear stress \(\tau_{x y}\) by Eq. (13.22):

\(\gamma_{x y}=\frac{1}{G} \tau_{x y}\)

Since \(\tau_{x y}\) = 0, the shear strain \(\gamma_{x y}\) must also equal zero, and the strain transformation equation reduces to:

\(775 \mu \varepsilon=775 \times 10^{-6} \text { in. } / in .=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)\)

Substitute Eqs. (13.21) for \(\varepsilon_{x} \text { and } \varepsilon_{y}\) to obtain an expression in terms of \(\sigma_{x} \text { and } \sigma_{y}\):

\(\begin{aligned}775 \times 10^{-6} &=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right) \\&=\frac{1}{E}\left(\sigma_{x}-v \sigma_{y}\right) \cos ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)+\frac{1}{E}\left(\sigma_{y}-v \sigma_{x}\right) \sin ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)\end{aligned}\)

It is known that \(\sigma_{x}=2 \sigma_{y}\). Make this substitution to obtain the following expression:

\(\begin{aligned}\left(775 \times 10^{-6}\right) E &=\left(2 \sigma_{y}-v \sigma_{y}\right) \cos ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)+\left(\sigma_{y}-2 v \sigma_{y}\right) \sin ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right) \\&=\sigma_{y}\left[(2-v) \cos ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)+(1-2 v) \sin ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)\right]\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\therefore \sigma_{y}=\frac{\left(775 \times 10^{-6}\right) E}{(2-v) \cos ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)+(1-2 v) \sin ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)}\)

 

Compute \(\sigma_{y}\):

\(\sigma_{y}=\frac{\left(775 \times 10^{-6}\right)(14,500 ksi )}{(2-0.3182) \cos ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)+[1-2(0.3182)] \sin ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)}=12.3723 ksi =12.37 ksi\)

and \(\sigma_{x}\):

\(\sigma_{x}=2 \sigma_{y}=2(12.3723 ksi )=24.7446 ksi =24.7 ksi\)
