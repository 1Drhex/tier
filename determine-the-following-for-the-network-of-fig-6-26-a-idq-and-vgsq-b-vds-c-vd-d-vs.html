Guest Question.

Determine the following for the network of Fig. 6.26.

(a) \( I_{DQ} and V_{GSQ}\).
(b) \(V_{DS}\).
(c) \(V_{D}\).
(d) \(V_{S}\).

Admin
Solution

(a) An equation for \(V_{GS}\) in terms of \(I_{D}\) is obtained by applying Kirchhoff’s voltage law to the input section of the network as redrawn in Fig. 6.27.

\(– V_{GS} – I_{S}R_{S} + V_{SS} = 0\)

or \(V_{GS} = V_{SS} – I_{S}R_{S}\)

but \(I_{S} = I_{D}\)

and \(V_{GS} = V_{SS} – I_{D}R_{S}\) (6.23)

The result is an equation very similar in format to Eq. (6.16) \(V_{GS} = V_{G} – I_{D}R_{S}\) that can be superimposed on the transfer characteristics using the procedure described for Eq. (6.16). That is, for this example,

\(V_{GS} = 10 V – I_{D}(1.5 k \Omega)\)

For \(I_{D} = 0 mA\),

\(V_{GS} = V_{SS} = 10 V\)

For \(V_{GS} = 0 V\),

\(0 = 10 V – I_{D}(1.5 k \Omega)\)

and \(I_{D} = \frac{10 V}{1.5 k \Omega} = 6.67 mA\)

The resulting plot points are identified on Fig. 6.28.

The transfer characteristics are sketched using the plot point established by \(V_{GS} = V_{P}/2 = – 3 V/2 = – 1.5 V\)and \(I_{D} = I_{DSS}/4 = 9 mA/4 = 2.25 mA\), as also appearing on Fig. 6.28. The resulting operating point establishes the following quiescent levels:

\(I_{DQ} = 6.9 mA\)

 

\(V_{GSQ} = – 0.35 V\)

(b) Applying Kirchhoff’s voltage law to the output side of Fig. 6.26 will result in

\(– V_{SS} + I_{S}R_{S} + V_{DS} + I_{D}R_{D} – V_{DD} = 0\)

Substituting \(I_{S} = I_{D}\) and rearranging gives

\(V_{DS} = V_{DD} + V_{SS} – I_{D}(R_{D} + R_{S})\)

which for this example results in

\(V_{DS} = 20 V + 10 V – ( 6.9 mA)(1.8 k \Omega + 1.5 k \Omega) = 30 V – 22.77 V = 7.23 V\)

(c) \(V_{D} = V_{DD} – I_{D}R_{D} = 20 V – (6.9 mA)(1.8 k \Omega) = 20 V – 12.42 V = 7.58 V\)

(d) \(V_{DS} = V_{D} – V_{S}\)

or \(V_{S} = V_{D} – V_{DS} = 7.58 V – 7.23 V = 0.35 V\)
