Guest Question.

In the circuit of Fig. 3.37, switch S1 is closed at t = 0. Additionally, switch S2 is closed when its voltage reaches 9 V. Determine \(V_{C}(t)\) after the second switching.

Admin
Solution

4.37. Before closing the first switch, the voltage of the capacitor is zero, since it is not connected to any power source. Thus:

\(V_{C}(0^{-}) \)= 0 V …….(1)

After closing the first switch and before closing the second switch, the circuit is like the one shown in Fig. 4.37.2. The voltage of the capacitor is continuous in lack of a power source with an infinite size (e.g., impulse power source); therefore:

\(V_{C}(0^{+})=V_{C}(0^{-}) = \)0 V ……..(2)

As is illustrated in Fig. 4.37.3, the final value of the capacitor voltage can be calculated by using the voltage division formula, since the capacitor will be open circuit after a long time. Herein, the capacitor voltage will be the same as the 6 Ω resistor’s voltage, since there is no voltage drop across the 2 Ω resistor.

\(V_{C}(∞) = V_{6Ω}(∞) = \frac{6}{6+2}×12\) = 9 V …….(3)

Figure 4.37.4 illustrates how to calculate the input resistance seen by the capacitor. As can be seen, the independent
voltage source is turned off. Figure 4.37.5 shows the simplified circuit of Fig. 4.37.4.

\(R_{Th}=R_{in}= 2+6||2 = 2+\frac{2×6}{2+6}\) = 3.5Ω …….(4)

The time constant of the RC circuit:

τ = \(R_{Th}C \)= 3.5×1 = 3.5 sec …….(5)

Now, we can use the general form of the capacitor voltage in a first-order circuit with DC power supply, as follows:

\(V_{C}(t)=V_{C}(∞)+(V_{C}(0^{+})-V_{C}(∞))e^{-\frac{t}{τ}}= 9+(0-9)e^{-\frac{t}{3.5}}=9(1-e^{-\frac{t}{3.5}}) \)…….(6)

But we need to calculate the voltage of the second switch. Thus, as can be seen in Fig. 4.37.6, we need to write a KVL, as follows:

\(-V_{S}(t) – 2I_{C}(t) +12 =0 \Rightarrow V_{S}(t) = -2I_{C}(t) +12\) …….(7)

From the voltage-current relation of the capacitor, we know that:

\(I_{C}(t) =C\frac{d}{dt}V_{C}(t)\) …….(8)

Using (6) and (8):

\(I_{C}(t) =C\frac{d}{dt}(9(1-e^{-\frac{t}{3.5}}))= \frac{9}{3.5}e^{-\frac{t}{3.5}}\) ……(9)

Solving (7) and (9):

\(V_{S}(t) = – 2×\frac{9}{3.5}e^{-\frac{t}{3.5}}+12 =-\frac{18}{3.5}e^{-\frac{t}{3.5}}+12 \)……(10)

Now, we can determine the time that the voltage of the second switch is 9 V.

\(-\frac{18}{3.5}e^{-\frac{t}{3.5}}+12 =9\Rightarrow -\frac{18}{3.5}e^{-\frac{t}{3.5}}=3\Rightarrow e^{-\frac{t}{3.5}} = \frac{3.5}{6} \Rightarrow -\frac{t}{3.5}=-0.539\Rightarrow \) t= 1.89 sec ……(11)

After t = 1.89 sec, the second switch is closed, and we have the circuit shown in Fig. 4.37.7. For the new circuit, we need to know the primary voltage of the capacitor that can be calculated by using the capacitor voltage continuity rule
and Equation (6).

\(V_{C}(1.89^{+}) = V_{C}(1.89^{-}) = 9(1-e^{-\frac{1.89}{3.5}}) \)= 3.75 V ……(12)

To calculate the final voltage of the capacitor, we can use the voltage division formula, as is shown in Fig. 4.37.8.

\(V_{C}(∞)=V_{+}(∞)-V_{-}(∞) = \frac{6}{6+2}×12 -\frac{2}{2+6}×12=6\) V …….(13)

To calculate the input resistance seen by the capacitor, we need to turn off the independent voltage source, as is shown in Fig. 4.37.9. The simplified circuit of Fig. 4.37.9 is shown in Fig. 4.37.10.

\(R_{Th}=R_{in}=6||2+6||2=\frac{6×2}{6+2}+\frac{6×2}{6+2}=\) 3 Ω …….(14)

The time constant of the RC circuit:

\(τ=R_{Th}C=3×1\)= 3 sec ……(15)

Now, by using the general form of the capacitor voltage in a first-order circuit with DC power l supply, we have:

\(V_{C}(t)=V_{C}(∞)+(V_{C}(1.89^{+})-V_{C}(∞))e^{-\frac{t-1.89}{τ}}= 6+(3.75-6)e^{-\frac{t-1.89}{3}}\) \(\Rightarrow V_{C}(t)=6-2.25e^{-\frac{t-1.89}{3}}\)
