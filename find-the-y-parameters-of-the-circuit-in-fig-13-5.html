Guest Question.

Find the Y-parameters of the circuit in Fig. 13-5.

Admin
Solution

We apply KCL to the input and output nodes (for convenience, we designate the admittances of the three branches of the circuit by \( Y _{a}, Y _{b}, \text { and } Y _{c} \) as shown in Fig. 13-6). Thus,

 

\(\begin{aligned}& Y _{a}=\frac{1}{2+5 s / 3}=\frac{3}{5 s +6} \\& Y _{b}=\frac{1}{3+5 s / 2}=\frac{2}{5 s +6} \\& Y _{c}=\frac{1}{5+6 / s }=\frac{ s }{5 s +6}\end{aligned}\) (11)

 

The node equations are

\(\begin{aligned}& I _{1}= V _{1} Y _{a}+\left( V _{1}- V _{2}\right) Y _{c}=\left( Y _{a}+ Y _{c}\right) V _{1}- Y _{c} V _{2} \\& I _{2}= V _{2} Y _{b}+\left( V _{2}- V _{1}\right) Y _{c}=- Y _{c} V _{1}+\left( Y _{b}+ Y _{c}\right) V _{2}\end{aligned}\) (12)

By comparing (9) with (12), we get

\(\begin{aligned}I_{1}= Y _{11}V_1 + Y _{12}V_2 \\ I_{2}= Y_{21}V_1 + Y_{22}V_2\end{aligned}\) (9)

\(\begin{aligned}& Y _{11}= Y _{a}+ Y _{c} \\& Y _{12}= Y _{21}=- Y _{c} \\& Y _{22}= Y _{b}+ Y _{c}\end{aligned}\) (13)

Substituting \( Y _{a}, Y _{b}, \text { and } Y _{c} \) in (11) into (13), we find

\(\begin{aligned}& Y _{11}=\frac{ s +3}{5 s +6} \\& Y _{12}= Y _{21}=\frac{- s }{5 s +6} \\& Y _{22}=\frac{ s +2}{5 s +6}\end{aligned}\) (14)

Since \( Y _{12}= Y _{21} \), the two-port circuit is reciprocal.
