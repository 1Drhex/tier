Guest Question.

A kiln has a 6 cm diameter window in one of its walls. The kiln environment is at 1200 C and can be assumed to be black.
The window has the following spectral transmissivity to radiation from a black environment at 1200 C:

\(\tau _{\lambda } = 0.6 for 0 < λ < 2 μm \\ 0.75 for 2 μm < λ < 4 μm \\ 0 for λ > 4 μm\)
(a) What is the total transmissivity of the glass window for black radiation at 1200 C?
(b) What is the rate of radiant energy transmitted through the window to the outside of the kiln?

 

Admin
Solution

The total transmissivity for incident black radiation is

 

\(\tau = \frac{\int_{0}^{\infty }{\tau _{\lambda }E_{b\lambda }d\lambda } }{\int_{0}^{\infty }{E_{b\lambda }d\lambda }} = \tau = \frac{(0.6) \int_{0}^{2 \mu m}{E_{b\lambda }d\lambda } + (0.75) \int_{2 \mu m}^{4 \mu m}{E_{b\lambda }d\lambda } }{\sigma T^4}\)

 

\(T = 1200 C + 273.15 = 1473.15 K \\ \lambda _{1} T = (2) (1473.15) = 2946 \mu m K \\ \lambda _{2} T = (4) (1473.15) = 5893 \mu m K\)

 

From Table 9.1 , \(F_{0 \rightarrow \lambda _{1}T} = 0.2510 and F_{0 \rightarrow \lambda _{2}T} = 0.7284\)
