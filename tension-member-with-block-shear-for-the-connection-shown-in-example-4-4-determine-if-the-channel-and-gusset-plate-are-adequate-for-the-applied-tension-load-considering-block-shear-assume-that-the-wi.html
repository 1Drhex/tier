\(F_{y}=36 Ksi\)

 

\(F_{μ}=58 Ksi\) to \( 80 ksi \)(use \(F_{μ} = 58 ksi\))

Plate Dimensions:

\(A_{gv}=(2)(4+1.5)(0.375)=4.12in^{2}\)

 

\(A_{nt}=A_{gt}-A_{holes}\)

 

\( = 4.12 -\left[\left(2 holes \right) \left(1.5\right) \left(\frac{5}{8} + \frac{1}{8} \right) \left(0.375\right) \right] = 3.28 in^2 \).

 

\( =[(4)(0.375)]-\left[\left(\frac{5}{8}+\frac{1}{8} \right)(0.375) \right]=1.21 in^{2}.\)

 

Channel Dimensions:

\(A_{gv}=(2)(4+1.5)(0.220)=2.42 in^{2}\).

 

\(A_{nv}=A_{gv}-A_{holes}\)

 

\(=2.42-\left[(2 holes)(1.5)\left(\frac{5}{8}+\frac{1}{8} \right)(0.220) \right] =1.92 in^{2}\)

 

\(A_{nt}=A_{gt}-A_{holes}\)

 

\( =[(4)(0.220)]-\left[\left(\frac{5}{8}+\frac{1}{8} \right)(0.220) \right]=0.715in^{2}\)

 

\(U_{bs}=1.0\)(tension stress is uniform)

The available block shear strength is found from equation (4-11):

\(\phi P_{n}=\phi (0.60F_{u}A_{nv}+U_{bs}F_{u}A_{nt} )\leq \phi (0.60F_{y}A_{gv}+U_{bs}F_{u}A_{nt} )\).

For the plate,

\(\phi P_{n}=0.75[(0.60)(58)(3.28) + (1.0)(58)(1.21)] \\ \leq 0.75[(0.60)(36)(4.12) + (1.0)(58)(1.21)] \\ =138 kips \gt 119 kips\)

 

The smaller value controls, so the available strength of the plate in block shear is 119 kips, which is greater than the applied load of \(P_{u}=75 kips\).

For the channel,

\(\phi P_{n}=0.75[(0.60)(58)(1.92) + (1.0)(58)(0.715)] \\ \leq 0.75[(0.60)(36)(2.42) + (1.0)(58)(0.715)] \\ =81.2 kips \gt 70.3 kips\)

 

The smaller value controls, so the available strength of the channel in block shear is 70.3 kips, which is less than the applied load of \(P_{u}=75kips\),so the channel is not adequate in block shear. (Note: The bolts also should be checked for shear and bearing, but bolt strength is covered later in this text.)
