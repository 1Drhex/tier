Guest Question.

Three network functions \( H _{1}, H _{2}, \text { and } H _{3} \) are given by

(a) \( H _{1}=\frac{1}{ s +1} \) (b) \( H _{2}=\frac{1}{ s ^{2}+\sqrt{2} s +1} \) (c) \( H _{3}=\frac{1}{ s ^{3}+2 s ^{2}+ 2 s + 1 }=\frac{1}{( s +1)\left( s ^{2}+ s +1\right)} \)

Find the magnitudes of their frequency responses. Show that all three functions are low-pass with half-power frequency at \( \omega_{0}=1 \).

Admin
Solution

(a) \( \left| H _{1}\right|^{2}=\frac{1}{(1+j \omega)(1-j \omega)}=\frac{1}{1+\omega^{2}} \)

(b) \( \left| H _{2}\right|^{2}=\frac{1}{\left(1-\omega^{2}+j \sqrt{2} \omega\right)\left(1-\omega^{2}-j \sqrt{2} \omega\right)}=\frac{1}{1+\omega^{4}} \)

(c) \( \left| H _{3}\right|^{2}=\frac{1}{\left(1+\omega^{2}\right)\left(1-\omega^{2}+j \omega\right)\left(1-\omega^{2}-j \omega\right)}=\frac{1}{1+\omega^{6}} \)

For all three functions, at \( \omega=0,1, \text { and } \infty \text {, we have }| H |^{2}=1,1 / 2 \text {, and } 0 \), respectively. Therefore, the three network functions are low-pass with the same half-power frequency of \( \omega_{0}=1 \). They are first-, second-, and third-order Butterworth filters, respectively. The higher the order of the filter, the sharper is the cutoff region in the frequency response.
