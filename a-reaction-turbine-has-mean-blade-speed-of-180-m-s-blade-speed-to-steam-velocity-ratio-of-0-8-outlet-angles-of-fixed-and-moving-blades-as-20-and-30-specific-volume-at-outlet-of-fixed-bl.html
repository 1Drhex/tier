Guest Question.

A reaction turbine has mean blade speed of 180 m/s, blade speed to steam velocity ratio of 0.8, outlet angles of fixed and moving blades as 20° and 30°, specific volume at outlet of fixed blade as 0.5 m\(^{3}\) and at moving blade outlet as 0.6 m\(^{3}\). Areas at exit of fixed blade and moving blades are same, Consider the efficiency of blades as 90% when considered as nozzles and K\(^{2}\) = 0.88 where K is blade velocity coefficient. Determine
(i) the degree of reaction,
(ii) adiabatic heat drop,
(iii) the overall stage efficiency.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{Given: }α_{1} = 20°, β_{2} = 30°, ρ = 0.8, U = 180 m/s.\\\begin{aligned}\text{Steam velocity }\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad C_{1}&= \frac{\text{Mean blade speed}}{\text{Blade speed to steam velocity ratio}}\\&= \frac{180}{0.8}\\C_{1}&= 225 m/s\\\text{Axial velocity component, at inlet }C_{1a}&= C_{1} \sin α_{1}\\&= 225 \sin 20\\C_{1a}&= 76.95 m/s\end{aligned}\\\text{From continuity equation, the steam mass flow rate at exit of fixed blades and moving blades shall be same, so}\\\begin{aligned}\frac{C_{1a}}{v_{\text{at exit of fixed blades}}}&= \frac{C_{2a}}{v_{\text{at exit of moving blades}}}\\\frac{76.95}{0.5}&= \frac{C_{2a}}{0.6}\\C_{2a}&= 92.34 m/s\\\text{From geometry of velocity diagrams,}\qquad&\\C_{2a}&= V_{2} \sin β_{2}\\V_{2}&= \frac{C_{2a}}{\sin β_{2}} = \frac{92.34}{\sin 30}\\V_{2}&= 184.68 m/s\\V_{1}^{2}&= C_{1}^{2} + U^{2} – 2 C_{1} U \cos α_{1}\\&= (225)^{2} + (180)^{2} – (2 × 225 × 180 × \cos 20)\\&= 6309.9\\\text{Heat drop in fixed blades}&= \left\{\frac{C_{1}^{2} – K^{2}C_{2}^{2}}{2 × η_{N}} \right\} \\\text{Heat drop in moving blades}&= \left\{\frac{V_{2}^{2} – K^{2}V_{1}^{2}}{2 × η_{N}} \right\} \\\text{Here nozzle efficiency is given as 0.9 for b}&\text{oth fixed and moving blades.}\\C_{2}^{2}&= V_{2}^{2} + U^{2} – 2UV_{2} \cos β_{2}\\&= (184.68)^{2} + (180)^{2} – (2 × 180 × 184.68 × \cos 30)\\C_{2}^{2}&= 8929.18\\C_{2}&= 94.49 m/s\\\text{Heat drop in fixed blades}&= \left\{\frac{(225)^{2} – (0.88 × 8929.18)}{2 × 0.9} \right\} \\&= 23.76 kJ/kg\\\text{Heat drop in moving blades}&= \left\{\frac{(184.68)^{2} – (0.88 × 6309.9)}{2 × 0.9} \right\} \\&= 15.86 kJ/kg\\\text{Therefore, degree of reaction}&= \frac{\text{Heat drop in moving blades}}{\text{Total heat drop}}\\&= \frac{15.86}{(23.76 + 15.86)}\\&= 0.4003 or 40.03\%\\\text{Degree of reaction}&= 40.03\%\\\text{Adiabatic heat drop in one pair of blades}&= (23.76 + 15.86)\\&= 39.62 kJ/kg.\\\text{Heat drop in a pair of blades}&= 39.62 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Work done}&= U · ΔC_{w}\\&= U · (V_{2} \cos β_{2} + C_{1} \cos α_{1} – U)\\&= 180 × (184.68 \cos 30 + 225 \cos 20 – 180)\\&= 34446.31 J/kg = 34.45 kJ/kg\\\text{Stage efficiency}&=\frac{34.45}{39.62} = 0.8695 or 86.95\%\\\end{aligned}\\\qquad \text{Stage efficiency = 86.95}\%\)
