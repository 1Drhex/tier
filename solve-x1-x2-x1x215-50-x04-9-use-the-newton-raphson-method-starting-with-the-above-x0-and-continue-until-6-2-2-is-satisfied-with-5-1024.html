Newton-Raphson method: solution to nonlinear algebraic equations

Solve

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} x_{1} & + & x_{2} \\ & x_{1}x_{2} & \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 15 \\ 50 \end{matrix} \right ]\) X(0) = \(\left [ \begin{matrix} 4 \\ 9 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Use the Newton-Raphson method starting with the above x(0) and continue until (6.2.2) is satisfied with \(\varepsilon =10^{-4} \) .

\(\left|\frac{x_{k}\left(i+1\right)-x_{k}\left(i\right) }{x_{k} \left(i\right) } \right| \lt \varepsilon \) for all k 5 1, 2, …, N (6.2.2)

 

Admin
Solution

Using (6.3.10) with \(f_{1}=\left(x_{1}+x_{2} \right) and f_{2} =x_{1} x_{2} \),

\(J\left(i\right) =\frac{df}{dX}\mid _{X=X\left(i\right) }= \left [ \begin{matrix} \frac{\partial f_{1} }{\partial x_{1} } & \frac{\partial f_{1} }{\partial x_{2} } & … & \frac{\partial f_{1} }{\partial x_{N} } \\ \frac{\partial f_{2} }{\partial x_{1} } & \frac{\partial f_{2} }{\partial x_{2} } & … & \frac{\partial f_{2} }{\partial x_{N} } \\ \vdots & \vdots & & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial f_{N} }{\partial x_{1} } & \frac{\partial f_{N} }{\partial x_{2} } & … & \frac{\partial f_{N} }{\partial x_{N} } \end{matrix} \right ] _{X=X\left(i\right) } \) (6.3.10)

\(J\left(i\right)^{-1}=\left [ \begin{matrix} \frac{\partial f_{1} }{\partial x_{1} } & \frac{\partial f_{1} }{\partial x_{2} } \\ \frac{\partial f_{2} }{\partial x_{1} } & \frac{\partial f_{2} }{\partial x_{2} } \end{matrix} \right ] ^{-1}_{X=X\left(i\right) } = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 \\ x_{2}(i) & x_{1}(i) \end{matrix} \right ] ^{-1} = \frac{\left [ \begin{matrix} x_{1}(i) & -1 \\ -x_{2}(i) & 1 \end{matrix} \right ]}{x_{1}\left(i\right)-x_{2} \left(i\right) } \)

Using \(J\left(i\right)^{-1} \) in (6.3.9),

\(X\left(i+1\right)=X\left(i\right)+J^{-1} \left(i\right)\left\{y-f\left[X\left(i\right) \right] \right\} \) (6.3.9)

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} x_{1}(i+1) \\ x_{2}(i+1) \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} x_{1}(i) \\ x_{2}(i) \end{matrix} \right ] +\frac{\left [ \begin{matrix} x_{1}(i) & -1 \\- x_{2}(i) & 1 \end{matrix} \right ] }{x_{1} \left(i\right)-x_{2}\left(i\right) } \left [ \begin{matrix} 15-x_{1} \left(i\right)-x_{2} \left(i\right) \\ 50-x_{1}\left(i\right)x_{2}\left(i\right) \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Writing the preceding as two separate equations,

\(x_{1}\left(i+1\right)=x_{1}\left(i\right)+\frac{x_{1}\left(i\right)\left[15-x_{1} \left(i\right)-x_{2}\left(i\right) \right]-\left[50-x_{1}\left(i\right)x_{2}\left(i\right) \right] }{x_{1}\left(i\right)-x_{2}\left(i\right) }\)

 

\(x_{2}\left(i+1\right)=x_{2}\left(i\right)+\frac{-x_{2}\left(i\right)\left[15-x_{1} \left(i\right)-x_{2}\left(i\right) \right]-\left[50-x_{1}\left(i\right)x_{2}\left(i\right) \right] }{x_{1}\left(i\right)-x_{2}\left(i\right) } \)

Successive calculations of these equations are shown in the following table.

Newton-Raphson

i 0 1 2 3 4
\(x_{1}\left(i\right)\) 4 5.20000 4.99130 4.99998 5.00000
\(x_{2}\left(i\right)\) 9 9.80000 10.00870 10.00002 10.00000

Newton-Raphson converges in four iterations for this example.
