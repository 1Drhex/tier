Guest Question.

Prove that the equation relating the angular velocity to the time derivative of the quaternion is given by

\(\omega\) = \( 2 S \left(\epsilon \right)\dot{\epsilon } + 2\eta \dot{\epsilon } - 2 \dot{\eta } \epsilon \)

[Hint: Start by showing that (2.33) can be rewritten as \(R\left(\eta ,\epsilon \right) \) = \(\left(2\eta^{2} - 1 \right) I + 2\epsilon \epsilon^{T} + 2\eta S\left(\epsilon \right) \) ]

\(R\left(\eta ,\epsilon \right)\) = \(\left[\begin{matrix} 2\left(\eta ^{2} + \epsilon^{2} _{x} \right) - 1 & 2 \left(\epsilon _{x}\epsilon _{y} - \eta \epsilon _{z} \right) & 2 \left(\epsilon _{x}\epsilon _{z} + \eta \epsilon _{y} \right) \\ 2 \left(\epsilon _{x}\epsilon _{y} + \eta \epsilon _{z} \right) & 2\left(\eta ^{2} + \epsilon^{2} _{y} \right) - 1 & 2 \left(\epsilon _{y}\epsilon _{z} - \eta \epsilon _{x} \right) \\ 2 \left(\epsilon _{x}\epsilon _{z} - \eta \epsilon _{y} \right) & 2 \left(\epsilon _{y}\epsilon _{z} + \eta \epsilon _{x} \right) & 2\left(\eta ^{2} + \epsilon^{2} _{z} \right) - 1 \end{matrix} \right] \) (2.33)

Admin
Solution

It is not difficult to see that, by using the skew-symmetric operator S(·), (2.33) can be rewritten in compact form as

\(R\left(\eta ,\epsilon \right)\) = \(\left(2\eta^{2} – 1 \right) I + 2\epsilon \epsilon^{T} + 2\eta S\left(\epsilon \right) \) (S3.9)

Computing the time derivative of (S3.9) gives \(\dot{R}\left(\eta ,\epsilon \right) \)= \(4\dot{\eta }\eta I + 2 \dot{\epsilon } \epsilon^{T} + 2 \epsilon \dot{\epsilon }^{T} + 2\dot{\eta } S\left(\epsilon \right) + 2\eta S\left(\dot{\epsilon } \right) \) (S3.10)

Substituting (S3.9) and (S3.10) into (3.6) gives:

\(S(t)= \dot{R} (t) R^{T}(t)\) (3.6)

\(S\left(\omega \right) \) = \(\dot{R } R^{T}\) = \( 4\dot{\eta}\eta \left(2\eta ^{2} – 1 \right) I + 8 \dot{\eta }\eta \epsilon \epsilon^{T} – 8 \dot{\eta }\eta ^{2} S\left(\epsilon \right) + 2\left(2\eta ^{2} – 1 \right) \dot{\epsilon} \epsilon^{T} + 4\dot{\epsilon} \epsilon^{T} \epsilon\epsilon^{T} – 4\eta \dot{\epsilon} \epsilon^{T} S\left(\epsilon \right) + 2\left(2\eta ^{2} – 1 \right) \epsilon \dot{\epsilon}^{T} + 4\epsilon\dot{\epsilon}^{T} \epsilon\epsilon^{T} – 4{\epsilon} \dot{\epsilon}^{T} S\left(\epsilon \right) + 2 \dot{\eta} \left(2\eta ^{2} – 1 \right) S\left(\epsilon \right) + 4\dot{\eta}S\left(\epsilon \right) \epsilon \epsilon^{T} – 4 \dot{\eta} \eta S\left(\epsilon \right) S\left(\epsilon \right) + 2 \dot{\eta} \left(2\eta ^{2} – 1 \right) S\left(\dot{\epsilon}\right) + 4 \eta S\left(\dot{\epsilon}\right) \epsilon \epsilon^{T} – 4 \eta ^{2}S\left(\dot{\epsilon}\right) S\left(\epsilon \right) \) (S3.11)

Next, taking the time derivative of the constraint in (2.32) gives

\(\eta ^{2} + \epsilon _{x}^{2} + \epsilon _{y}^{2} + \epsilon _{z}^{2} = 1, \) (2.32)

\(\epsilon^{T} \dot{\epsilon}\) = \(\dot{\epsilon}^{T} \) = \( – \eta \dot{\eta }\) (S3.12)

Then, using (2.32), (S3.12) and the property \(S\left(x\right) S\left(y \right) \) = \(y {x}^{T} – {x}^{T} {y}I\) yields the following equalities:

\(\dot{\epsilon} \epsilon^{T} \epsilon \epsilon^{T}\) = \( \left(1 – \eta ^{2} \right) \dot{\epsilon} \epsilon^{T}\)

\(\epsilon \dot{\epsilon}^{T} \epsilon \epsilon ^{T}\) = \(-\dot{\eta} \eta \epsilon \epsilon^{T}\)

\(S\left(\epsilon\right) S\left(\epsilon\right) \) = \(\epsilon \epsilon^{T} – \left(1 – \eta ^{2} \right) I\)

\(S\left(\dot{\epsilon}\right) S\left(\epsilon\right) \) = \(\epsilon \dot{\epsilon}^{T} + \dot{\eta} \eta I\)

\(\epsilon \dot{\epsilon}^{T} S\left(\epsilon\right)\) = \( \left(\left(S\dot{\epsilon}\right) S\left(\epsilon\right) – \dot{\eta} \eta I \right) S\left(\epsilon\right)\)

\(\left(S\dot{\epsilon}\right) \epsilon \epsilon^{T} \) = \( \left(S\dot{\epsilon}\right) \left(S\left(\epsilon\right) S\left(\epsilon\right) + \left(1 – \eta ^{2} \right) I\right) \)

By virtue of the above equalities and observing that \(S\left(\epsilon\right) \epsilon\) = 0 (S3.11) becomes

\(S\left(\omega\right) \) = \( 2 \left(\dot{\epsilon} \epsilon^{T } – \epsilon \dot{\epsilon}^{T } \right) + 2\eta \left(S\dot{\epsilon}\right) – 2 \dot{\eta} S\left(\epsilon\right)\)

which, in view of the property \(S\left(S\left(x \right)y \right) \) = \( y {x}^{T} – x {y}^{T}\) can be written in the form

\(S\left(\omega\right) \) =\( 2S\left(S\left(\epsilon \right)\dot{\epsilon} \right) + 2\eta S\left(\dot{\epsilon}\right) – 2 \dot{\eta} S\left(\epsilon\right)\)

Finally, the angular velocity can be computed as

\(\omega \) = \(2S \left(\epsilon\right)\dot{\epsilon} + 2\eta \dot{\epsilon} – 2\dot{\eta}\epsilon \) (S3.13)
