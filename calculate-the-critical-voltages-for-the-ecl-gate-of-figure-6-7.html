Guest Question.

Calculate the critical voltages for the ECL gate of Figure 6.7

Admin
Solution

Using the 1/10 V rule of thumb, the critical input voltages are \(V_{IL} \approx V_{REF} – 0.05\) V = –1.225 V and \(V_{IH} \approx V_{REF} + 0.05\) V = –1.125 V.
Now consider the noninverting output levels. With \(Q_{R}\) cut off, a negligible voltage drop in \(R_{CR}\) occurs so that \(V_{OH} = –V_{BEA} = –0.75\) V. With \(Q_{R}\) conducting, the emitter current is

\(I_{RE}=\frac{V_{REF}-V_{BEA}-(-V _{EE})} {R_E}=\frac{-1.175 V-0.75 V-(-5.2 V)}{1.24 k\Omega } =2.64 mA\)

Sympol Noninverting Output inverting Output
\(V_{IL}\) -1.225 V -1.225 V
\(V_{IH}\) -1.125 V -1.225 V
\(V_{OL}\) -1.527 V -1.538 V
\(V_{OH}\) -0.75 V -0.75 V

and the collector current is

\(I_{RCR}=\left\lgroup\frac{\beta _{F}}{1+\beta _{F}} \right\rgroup I_{RE}=\left\lgroup \frac{50}{51} \right\rgroup 2.64 mA=2.59\) mA

therefore, the low output level is \(V_{OL} = –I_{RCR}R_{CR} – V_{BEA} = –(2.59 mA)(300 W) –0.75 V = –1.527\) V.
Now consider the inverting output. With \(Q_I\) cut off, the only current flowing through RCI is the base current for \(Q_{INV}\). To the extent that this current can be neglected, the output high level is the same as for the noninverting output: \(V_{OH} = –V_{BEA} = –0.75\) V. With \(Q_{I}\) conducting, the emitter current is

\(I_{RE}=\frac{V_{OH}-V_{BEA}-(-V_{EE})}{R_{E}} =\frac{-0.75 V-0.75 V-(-5.2 V)}{1.24 k\Omega } =2.98 mA,\)

the collector current is

\(I_{RCI}=\left\lgroup\frac{\beta _{F}}{1+\beta _{F}} \right\rgroup I_{RE}=\left\lgroup \frac{50}{51} \right\rgroup 2.98 mA=2.92\) mA ,

and the low output level is therefore \(V_{OL} = –I_{RCI}R_{CI} – V_{BEA} = –(2.92 mA) (270 Ω) – 0.75 V = –1.538 V.\) The critical voltages are summarized in Table 6.1. Notice that the circuit is designed with different values for the two collector resistors so that the output levels are nearly equal in the low output states.
