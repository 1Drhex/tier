Guest Question.

A 16 kV dc source having an internal resistance of 1 Ω supplies 900 A to a 12 kV, 3-phase, 6-pulse, 60 Hz inverter (Fig. 48).

 

Calculate

 

a. The dc current carried by each SCR
b. The dc voltage generated by the inverter
c. The required firing angle α
d. The effective value of the ac line currents
e. The reactive power absorbed by the inverter

Admin
Solution

a. Each SCR carries the load current for one-third of the time. The dc current is, therefore,

 

\(\begin{aligned}I &=I_{ d } / 3=900 / 3 \\&=300 A\end{aligned}\)

 

b. The voltage \(E_{ d }\) generated by the inverter is equal to \(E_{ 0 }\) less the IR drop. Thus,

 

\(\begin{aligned}E_{ d } &=E_{0}-I_{ d } R \\&=16000-900 \times 1 \\&=15100 V\end{aligned}\)

 

c. Knowing that the effective ac line voltage is 12 000 V, the firing angle can be found from Eq. 13:

 

\(E_{ d }=1.35 E \cos \alpha\) (Equation 13)

where

\(E_{ d }= dc\) voltage produced by the 3-phase, 6-pulse converter [V]

 

\(\begin{aligned}E_{ d } &=1.35 E \cos \alpha \\15100 &=1.35 \times 12000 \cos \alpha \\\cos \alpha &=0.932 \\\alpha &=21.2^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

 

This is the firing angle that would be required if the converter operated as a rectifier. However, because it is in the inverter mode, the actual firing angle is

 

\(\alpha=180-21.2=158.8^{\circ}\)

 

d. The effective value of the ac line current is

 

\(\begin{aligned}I &=0.816 I_{ d } \\&=0.816 \times 900 \\&=734 A\end{aligned}\)

 

e. The dc power absorbed by the inverter is

 

\(\begin{aligned}P &=E_{ d } I_{ d } \\&=15100 \times 900 \\&=13.6 MW\end{aligned}\)

 

P is actually negative because the inverter absorbs dc power; hence,

 

\(P=-13.6 MW\)

 

The reactive power absorbed by the inverter is

 

\(\begin{aligned}Q &=P \tan \alpha \\&=-13.6 \tan 158.8 \\&=5.27 Mvar\end{aligned}\)

 

In practice, the actual reactive power is higher than the calculated value, due to commutation overlap.
