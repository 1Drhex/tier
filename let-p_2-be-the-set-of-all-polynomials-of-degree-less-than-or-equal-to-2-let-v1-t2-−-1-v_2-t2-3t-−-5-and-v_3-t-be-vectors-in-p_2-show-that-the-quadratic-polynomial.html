Guest Question.

Let \(P_{2}\) be the set of all polynomials of degree less than or equal to 2 .

Let \(\mathbf{v}_{1}=t^{2}-1, \mathbf{v}_{2}=t^{2}+3 t-5\) and \(\mathbf{v}_{3}=t\) be vectors in \(P_{2}\)

Show that the quadratic polynomial

\(\mathrm{x}=7 t^{2}-15\)

is a linear combination of \(\left\{\mathbf{v}_{1}, \mathbf{v}_{2}, \mathbf{v}_{3}\right\}\).

Admin
Solution

How do we show \(\boldsymbol{x}\) is a linear combination of vectors \(\mathbf{v}_{1}, \mathbf{v}_{2}\) and \(\mathbf{v}_{3} ?\)

We need to find the values of the scalars \(k_{1}, k_{2}\) and \(k_{3}\) which satisfy

\(k_{1} \mathbf{v}_{1}+k_{2} \mathbf{v}_{2}+k_{3} \mathbf{v}_{3}=\mathbf{x} \quad\left(^{*}\right)\)

How can we determine these scalars?

By substituting \(\mathbf{v}_{1}=t^{2}-1, \mathbf{v}_{2}=t^{2}+3 t-5, \mathbf{v}_{3}=t\) and \(\mathbf{x}=7 t^{2}-15\) into \(\left({ }^{*}\right)\) :

\(\begin{aligned} k_{1} \mathbf{v}_{1}+k_{2} \mathbf{v}_{2}+k_{3} \mathbf{v}_{3} &=k_{1}\left(t^{2}-1\right)+k_{2}\left(t^{2}+3 t-5\right)+k_{3} t \\ &=k_{1} t^{2}-k_{1}+k_{2} t^{2}+3 k_{2} t-5 k_{2}+k_{3} t \quad \quad[\text { expanding }] \\ &=\left(k_{1}+k_{2}\right) t^{2}+\left(3 k_{2}+k_{3}\right) t-\left(k_{1}+5 k_{2}\right) \quad[\text { factorizing }] \\ &=7 t^{2}-15 \quad\left[\text { remember } \mathrm{x}=7 t^{2}-15\right] \end{aligned}\)

By equating coefficients of the last two lines

\(\left(k_{1}+k_{2}\right) t^{2}+\left(3 k_{2}+k_{3}\right) t-\left(k_{1}+5 k_{2}\right)=7 t^{2}-15\)

gives

\(\begin{aligned} k_{1}+k_{2} &=7 & & \left.\text { [equating } t^{2}\right] \\ 3 k_{2}+k_{3} &=0 & &\text { [equating } t] \\ k_{1}+5 k_{2} &=15 & &\text { [equating constants }] \end{aligned}\)

Solving these equations gives the values of the scalars: \(k_{1}=5, k_{2}=2\) and \(k_{3}=-6\) Substituting these into \(k_{1} \mathbf{v}_{1}+k_{2} \mathbf{v}_{2}+k_{3} \mathbf{v}_{3}=\mathbf{x}\) :

\(5 \mathbf{v}_{1}+2 \mathbf{v}_{2}-6 \mathbf{v}_{3}=\mathbf{x}\)

This means that adding 5 lots of \(\mathbf{v}_{1}, 2\) lots of \(\mathbf{v}_{2}\) and \(-6\) lots of \(\mathbf{v}_{3}\) gives the vector \(\mathbf{x}\) :

\(5\left(t^{2}-1\right)+2\left(t^{2}+3 t-5\right)-6 t=7 t^{2}-15\)

You may like to check the algebra.

We conclude that \(\mathbf{x}\) is a linear combination of \(\left\{\mathbf{v}_{1}, \mathbf{v}_{2}, \mathbf{v}_{3}\right\}\)
