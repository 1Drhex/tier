Guest Question.

A hollow shaft is subjected to an axial load P and a torque T, acting in the directions shown in Figure P15.18. The shaft is made of bronze [E = 15,200 ksi; ν = 0.34], and it has an outside diameter of 2.50 in. and an inside diameter of 2.00 in. Strain gages a and b are mounted on the shaft at the orientations shown in Figure P15.18 where θ has a magnitude of 25°.
(a) If P = 6 kips and T = 17 kip-in., determine the strain readings that would be expected from the gages.
(b) If the strain gage readings are \(\varepsilon_{a}\) = –1,100 με and \(\varepsilon_{b}\) = 720 με, determine the axial force P and the torque T applied to the shaft.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[(2.5 in .)^{2}-(2.0 in .)^{2}\right]=1.767146 in .^{2} \\&J=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[(2.5 in .)^{4}-(2.0 \text { in. })^{4}\right]=2.264156 in .^{4}\end{aligned}\)

 

(a) Normal and shear stress magnitudes:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma &=\frac{P}{A}=\frac{6 kips }{1.767146 in .^{2}}=3.395 ksi ( C ) \\\tau &=\frac{T c}{J}=\frac{(17 kip – in .)(2.5 in . / 2)}{2.264156 in .^{4}}=9.385 \text { ksi (sense of shear stress determined by inspection) }\end{aligned}\)

A stress element showing the normal and shear stresses is shown below.

 

Generalized Hooke’s Law:
Since the stresses are known, the normal strains in the x and y directions can be computed from Eqs. (13.21):

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{x} &=\frac{1}{E}\left(\sigma_{x}-v \sigma_{y}\right)=\frac{1}{15,200 ksi }[-3.395 ksi -(0.34)(0)] \\&=-223.375 \times 10^{-6} \text { in./in. }=-223.375 \mu \varepsilon \\\varepsilon_{y} &=\frac{1}{E}\left(\sigma_{y}-v \sigma_{x}\right)=\frac{1}{15,200 ksi }[0-(0.34)(-3.395 ksi )] \\&=75.948 \times 10^{-6} in . / in .=75.948 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)

From Eq. (13.18), determine the shear modulus G:

\(G=\frac{E}{2(1+v)}=\frac{15,200 ksi }{2(1+0.34)}=5,671.624 ksi\)

and compute the shear stress \(\tau_{x y}\) from Eq. (13.20):

\(\gamma_{x y}=\frac{\tau_{x y}}{G}=\frac{9.385 ksi }{5,671.624 ksi }=1,654.794 \times 10^{-6} rad\)

Strain transformation equation:
Determine the strain that should be measured by strain gage a, which is oriented at \(\theta_{a}\) = –25°:

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{a} &=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2} \theta_{a}+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2} \theta_{a}+\gamma_{x y} \sin \theta_{a} \cos \theta_{a} \\&=(-223.375 \mu \varepsilon) \cos ^{2}\left(-25^{\circ}\right)+(75.948 \mu \varepsilon) \sin ^{2}\left(-25^{\circ}\right)+(1,654.794 \mu \operatorname{rad}) \sin \left(-25^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(-25^{\circ}\right) \\&=-803.737 \mu \varepsilon=-804 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)

Determine the strain that should be measured by strain gage b, which is oriented at \(\theta_{b}\) = +65°:

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{b} &=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2} \theta_{b}+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2} \theta_{b}+\gamma_{x y} \sin \theta_{b} \cos \theta_{b} \\&=(-223.375 \mu \varepsilon) \cos ^{2}\left(65^{\circ}\right)+(75.948 \mu \varepsilon) \sin ^{2}\left(65^{\circ}\right)+(1,654.794 \mu \operatorname{rad}) \sin \left(65^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(65^{\circ}\right) \\&=656.309 \mu \varepsilon=656 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Generalized Hooke’s Law:
Using the generalized Hooke’s Law and recognizing that the normal stress in the y direction is \(\sigma_{y}\) = 0, the normal strains in the x and y directions can be expressed as:

\(\varepsilon_{x}=\frac{1}{E}\left(\sigma_{x}-v \sigma_{y}\right)=\frac{\sigma_{x}}{E} \quad \text { and } \quad \varepsilon_{y}=\frac{1}{E}\left(\sigma_{y}-v \sigma_{x}\right)=-\frac{v \sigma_{x}}{E}\)

Write a strain transformation equation for each strain gage. For gage a:

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{a} &=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2} \theta_{a}+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2} \theta_{a}+\gamma_{x y} \sin \theta_{a} \cos \theta_{a} \\&=\frac{\sigma_{x} \cos ^{2}\left(-25^{\circ}\right)}{E}-\frac{v \sigma_{x} \sin ^{2}\left(-25^{\circ}\right)}{E}+\frac{\tau_{x y} \sin \left(-25^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(-25^{\circ}\right)}{G}\end{aligned}\)

which can be further simplified with Eq. (13.18):

\(G=\frac{E}{2(1+v)}\)

to

\(\varepsilon_{a}=\frac{\sigma_{x} \cos ^{2}\left(-25^{\circ}\right)}{E}-\frac{v \sigma_{x} \sin ^{2}\left(-25^{\circ}\right)}{E}+\frac{2(1+v) \tau_{x y} \sin \left(-25^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(-25^{\circ}\right)}{E}\)

A similar expression can be written for gage b:

\(\varepsilon_{b}=\frac{\sigma_{x} \cos ^{2}\left(65^{\circ}\right)}{E}-\frac{v \sigma_{x} \sin ^{2}\left(65^{\circ}\right)}{E}+\frac{2(1+v) \tau_{x y} \sin \left(65^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(65^{\circ}\right)}{E}\)

We now have two equations for the two unknowns \(\sigma_{x} \text { and } \tau_{x y}\):

\(\begin{aligned}(15,200 ksi )\left(-1,100 \times 10^{-6}\right) &=\sigma_{x}\left[\cos ^{2}\left(-25^{\circ}\right)-(0.34) \sin ^{2}\left(-25^{\circ}\right)\right]+\tau_{x y}\left[2(1+0.34) \sin \left(-25^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(-25^{\circ}\right)\right] \\(15,200 ksi )\left(720 \times 10^{-6}\right) &=\sigma_{x}\left[\cos ^{2}\left(65^{\circ}\right)-(0.34) \sin ^{2}\left(65^{\circ}\right)\right]+\tau_{x y}\left[2(1+0.34) \sin \left(65^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(65^{\circ}\right)\right]\end{aligned}\)

Solve simultaneously for \(\sigma_{x} \text { and } \tau_{x y}\):

\(\sigma_{x}=-8.752 ksi \quad \tau_{ xy }=9.803 ksi\)

Axial load:

\(P=\sigma A=(-8.752 ksi )\left(1.767146 in .^{2}\right)=15.465 kips ( C )=15.47 kips ( C )\)

Torque:

\(T=\frac{\tau J}{c}=\frac{(9.803 ksi )\left(2.264156 in .{ }^{4}\right)}{2.5 in . / 2}=17.757 \text { kip-in. }=17.76 \text { kip-in. }\)
