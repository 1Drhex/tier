Guest Question.

A body at a temperature of 50° F is placed outdoors where the temperature is 100° F. If after 5 minutes the temperature of the body is 60° F, find (a) how long it will take the body to reach a temperature of 75° F and (b) the temperature of the body after 20 minutes.

Admin
Solution

Using (7.2) with \(T_{m}=100\) (the surrounding medium is the outside air), we have

\(\frac{d T}{d t}+k T=100 k\)

This differential equation is linear. Its solution is given in as

\(T=c e^{-k t}+100\) (1)

Since T = 50 when t = 0,it follows from (1) that \(50=c e^{-k(0)}+100, \text { or } c=-50\) . Substituting this value into (1), we obtain

\(T=-50 e^{-k t}+100\) (2)

At t =5, we are given that T= 60; hence, from (2), \(60=-50 e^{-5 k}+100\) . Solving for k, we obtain

\(-40=-50 e^{-5 k} \quad \text { or } \quad k=\frac{-1}{5} \ln \frac{40}{50}=\frac{-1}{5}(-0.223)=0.045\)

Substituting this value into (2), we obtain the temperature of the body at any time t as

\(T=-50 e^{-0.045 t}+100\) (3)

(a) We require t when T = 75. Substituting T=75 into (3), we have

\(75=-50 e^{-0.045 t}+100 \quad \text { or } \quad e^{-0.045 t}=\frac{1}{2}\)

Solving for t, we find

\(-0.045 t=\ln \frac{1}{2} \quad \text { or } \quad t=15.4 min\)

(b) We require T when t = 20. Substituting t = 20 into (3) and then solving for T, we find

\(T=-50 e^{(-0.045)(20)}+100=-50(0.41)+100=79.5^{\circ} F\)
