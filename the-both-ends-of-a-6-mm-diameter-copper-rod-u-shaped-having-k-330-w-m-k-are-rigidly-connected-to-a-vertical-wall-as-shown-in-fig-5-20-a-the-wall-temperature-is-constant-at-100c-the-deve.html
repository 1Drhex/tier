Guest Question.

The both ends of a 6 mm diameter copper rod (U-shaped) having k = 330 W/m.K are rigidly connected to a vertical wall as shown in Fig. 5.20 (a). The wall temperature is constant at 100°C. The developed length of the rod is 50 cm and is exposed to air at 30°C. The combined convective and radiative heat transfer coefficient is 30 \(W/m^2.K.\) Calculate :
(i) The temperature at the centre of the rod.
(ii) Heat transfer by the rod.

 

Admin
Solution

Given : U-shaped circular fin.
d = 6 mm, L = 50 cm,
\(k = 330 W/m.K, T_0 = 100°C,\)
\(T_∞ = 30°C, T_L = 100°C,\)
\(h = 30 W/m^2.K\)
To find :
(i) The temperature at the centre of the fin.
(ii) The heat transfer rate.

Assumption : Steady state heat conduction along the rod.
Analysis : Considering finite long fin with specified temperature at its free end as shown in Fig. 5.20 (a).
\(A_c =\frac{\pi}{ 4} d^2 = \frac{\pi}{ 4} \times (6 × 10^{–3})^2\)
\(= 2.827 × 10^{–5} m^2\)
\(P = πd = π × 6 × 10^{–3} = 0.0188 m\)
\(m = \sqrt{\frac{hP}{k A_{c}}} = \sqrt{\frac{30 \times 0.0188}{330 × 2.827 × 10^{-5} }}\)

= 7.785 m

(i) The temperature distribution in the fin
\(\frac{T(x ) – T_∞}{T_0 – T_∞}= \frac{\left [ \frac{\theta_{L}}{\theta _{0}} sinh (mx) +sinh {m(L – x)}\right ]}{sinh (mL)}\)
The temperature at the centre of fin i.e., At x = 25 cm,
Due to symmetry \(θ_L = θ\)
\(\frac{T_{c} – 30}{100 – 30}= \frac{\left [ sinh (7.785 \times 0.25) +sinh {7.785(0.5 – 0.25)}\right ]}{sinh (7.785 \times 0.25)}\)
= 0.28
or\( T_c = 30 + 70 × 0.28\)
= 49.6°C.

Alternatively : At the centre of fin, temperature would be constant due to symmetry. The temperature of U-shaped fin can also be obtained by considering two insulated tip fins, 25 cm long each, as shown in Fig. 5.20 (b).

\(\frac{T_{c} – T_∞}{T_0 – T_∞}= \frac{ 1}{cosh (mL)}\)

\(T_c= 49.6°C.\)
(ii) The heat transfer rate from the fin : Considering two fins of 25 cm long each with insulated tip.
\(Q = 2 \sqrt{hPP A_c} (T_0 – T_∞) tanh (mL)\)
\(= 2 \sqrt{30 × 0.0188 × 330 × 2.827 × 10^{-5}} × (100 – 30) × tanh (7.785 × 0.25)\)
= 9.76 W.
