Guest Question.

A load of P = 1,800 N acts on the machine part shown in Fig. P15.32a. The machine part has a uniform thickness of 6 mm (i.e., 6-mm thickness in the z direction). Determine the principal stresses and the maximum shear stress acting at points H and K, which are shown in detail in Fig. P15.32b. Show these stresses on an appropriate sketch for each point.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&A=(6 mm )(40 mm )=240 mm ^{2} \\&I_{z}=\frac{(6 mm )(40 mm )^{3}}{12}=32,000 mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Equilibrium:

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma F_{x} &=B_{x}-(1,800 N ) \cos 30^{\circ}=0 \\\Sigma F_{y} &=A_{y}+B_{y}+(1,800 N ) \sin 30^{\circ}=0 \\\Sigma M_{B} &=(1,800 N )(125 mm ) \cos 30^{\circ}-A_{y}(185 mm )=0\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}A_{y} &=1,053.274 N \\B_{x} &=1,558.846 N \quad B_{y}=-1,953.274 N\end{aligned}\)

 

 

Internal forces at H:

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma F_{x} &=F_{H}=0 \\\Sigma F_{y} &=A_{y}-V_{H}=0 \quad \therefore V_{H}=1,053.274 N \\\Sigma M_{H} &=M_{H}-A_{y}(135 mm )=0 \\& \therefore M_{H}=142,192.009 N – mm\end{aligned}\)

Axial stress at H:

\(\sigma_{\text {axial }}=0 MPa\)

Shear stress at H:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q_{H}=(6 mm )(12 mm )(20 mm -6 mm )=1,008 mm ^{3} \\&\tau_{H}=\frac{(1,053.274 N )\left(1,008 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(32,000 mm ^{4}\right)(6 mm )}=5.530 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Bending stress at H:

\(\sigma_{\text {bend }}=\frac{(142,192.009 N – mm )(8 mm )}{32,000 mm ^{4}}=35.548 MPa ( T ) \quad \text { (by inspection) }\)

Summary of stresses at H:

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{x}=0 MPa +35.548 MPa =35.548 MPa \\&\sigma_{y}=0 MPa \tau_{x y}=-5.530 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Principal stress calculations:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq. (12.12):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}}{2}\right)^{2}+\tau_{x y}^{2}}=\frac{(35.548)+(0)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(35.548)-(0)}{2}\right)^{2}+(-5.530)^{2}} \\&=17.774 \pm 18.614\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1} &=36.4 MPa \text { and } \quad \sigma_{p 2}=-0.840 MPa \\\tau_{\max } &=18.61 MPa \text { (maximum in-plane shear stress) } \\\sigma_{\text {avg }} &=17.77 MPa ( T ) \text { (normal stress on planes of maximum in-plane shear stress) }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan 2 \theta_{p} &=\frac{\tau_{x y}}{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) / 2}=\frac{-5.530}{[(35.548)-(0)] / 2}=\frac{-5.530}{17.774}=-0.311111 \\&\left.\therefore \theta_{p}=-8.64^{\circ} \quad \text { (clockwise from the } x \text { axis to the direction of } \sigma_{p 1}\right)\end{aligned}\)

 

 

 

 

Internal forces at K:

\(\begin{array}{ll}\Sigma F_{x}=B_{x}-V_{K}=0 & \therefore V_{K}=1,558.846 N \\\Sigma F_{y}=A_{y}+\bar{B}_{y}+\bar{F}_{K}=0 & \therefore F_{K}=900 N \\\Sigma M_{K}=M_{K}-A_{y}(185 mm )+B_{x}(50 mm )=0 \\\therefore M_{K}=116,913.390 N – mm\end{array}\)

Axial stress at K:

\(\sigma_{\text {axial }}=\frac{900 N }{240 mm ^{2}}=3.750 MPa ( T )\)

Shear stress at K:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q_{K}=(6 mm )(15 mm )(20 mm -7.5 mm )=1,125 mm ^{3} \\&\tau_{K}=\frac{(1,558.846 N )\left(1,125 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(32,000 mm ^{4}\right)(6 mm )}=9.134 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Bending stress at K:

\(\sigma_{\text {bend }}=\frac{(116,913.390 N – mm )(5 mm )}{32,000 mm ^{4}}=18.268 MPa ( C )\) (by inspection)

Summary of stresses at K:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=0 MPa & \sigma_{y}=3.750 MPa -18.268 MPa =-14.518 MPa \\\tau_{x y} &=-9.134 MPa\end{aligned}\)

 

 

Principal stress calculations:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq. (12.12):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}}{2}\right)^{2}+\tau_{x y}^{2}}=\frac{(0)+(-14.518)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(0)-(-14.518)}{2}\right)^{2}+(-9.134)^{2}} \\&=-7.259 \pm 11.667\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1} &=4.41 MPa \text { and } \sigma_{p 2}=-18.93 MPa \\\tau_{\max } &=11.67 MPa \quad \text { (maximum in-plane shear stress) } \\\sigma_{\text {avg }} &=7.26 MPa ( C ) \quad \text { (normal stress on planes of maximum in-plane shear stress) }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan 2 \theta_{p} &=\frac{\tau_{x y}}{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) / 2}=\frac{-9.134}{[(0)-(-14.518)] / 2}=\frac{-9.134}{7.259}=-1.258305 \\&\left.\therefore \theta_{p}=-25.76^{\circ} \quad \text { (clockwise from the } x \text { axis to the direction of } \sigma_{p 1}\right)\end{aligned}\)
