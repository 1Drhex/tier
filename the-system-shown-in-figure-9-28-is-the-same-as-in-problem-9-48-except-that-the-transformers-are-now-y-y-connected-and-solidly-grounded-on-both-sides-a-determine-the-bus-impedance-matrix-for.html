Guest Question.

The system shown in Figure 9.28 is the same as in except that the transformers are now Y–Y connected and solidly grounded on both sides. (a) Determine the bus impedance matrix for each of the three sequence networks. (b) Assume the system to be operating at nominal system voltage without prefault currents when a bolted single-line-toground fault occurs on phase A at bus 3. Compute the fault current, the current out of phase C of machine 2 during the fault, and the line-toground voltages at the terminals of machine 2 during the fault.

Admin
Solution

(a) \(\bar{Z}_{B U S 1} \text { and } \bar{Z}_{B U S 2}\) are same as in the solution of . However, because the transformers are solidly grounded on both sides, the zero-sequence network is changed as shown below:

For the single line-to-ground fault, series connection of the Thévenin equivalents of the sequence networks is shown below:

\(\bar{Z}_{B U S 0}= \begin{matrix}(1)\\ (2)\\ (3)\\ (4)\end{matrix}\overset{\begin{matrix} (1) & (2) & (3) & (4) \end{matrix}}{\left[\begin{array}{llll}j 0.1553 & j 0.1407 & j 0.0493 & j 0.0347 \\j 0.01407 & j 0.1999 & j 0.0701 & j 0.0493 \\j 0.0493 & j 0.0701 & j 0.1999 & j 0.1407 \\j 0.0347 & j 0.0493 & j 0.1407 & j 0.1553\end{array}\right]}\)

 

(b)

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{I}_{f A 0} &=\bar{I}_{f A 1}=\bar{I}_{f A 2} \\&=\frac{1 \angle 0^{\circ}}{j(0.1696+0.1696+0.1999)} \\&=-j 1.8549 \\\bar{I}_{f A} &=3 \bar{I}_{f A 0}=-j 5.5648=931 \angle 270^{\circ} A\end{aligned}\)

∵ Base current in HV trans. line is

\(\frac{100,000}{\sqrt{3} \times 345}=167.35 A\)

Phase-a sequence voltages at bus (4), terminals of machine 2, are

\(\begin{gathered}\bar{V}_{4 a 0}=-\bar{Z}_{430} \bar{I}_{f A 0}=-(j 0.1407)(-j 1.8549)=-0.2610 \\\bar{V}_{4 a 1}=1-(j 0.1211)(-j 1.8549)=0.7754\left[=\bar{V}_{f}-\bar{Z}_{431} \bar{I}_{f A 1}\right] \\\bar{V}_{4 a 2}=-(j 0.1211)(-j 1.8549)=-0.2246\left[=-\bar{Z}_{432} \bar{I}_{f A 2}\right]\end{gathered}\)

Note: Subscripts A and a denote HV and LV circuits, respectively, of the Y-Y connected transformer. No phase shift is involved.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{V}_{4 a} \\\bar{V}_{4 b} \\\bar{V}_{4 c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a^{2} & a \\1 & a & a^{2}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}-0.2610 \\+0.7754 \\-0.2246\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}0.2898+j 0 \\-0.5364-j 0.866 \\-0.5364+j 0.866\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}0.2898 \angle 0^{\circ} \\1.0187 \angle-121.8^{\circ} \\1.0187 \angle 121.8^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\)

Line-to-ground voltages of machine 2 in kV are: (Multiply by \( 20 / \sqrt{3}\) )

\(\bar{V}_{4 a}=3.346 \angle 0^{\circ} kV ; \bar{V}_{4 b}=11.763 \angle-121.8 kV ; \bar{V}_{4 c}=11.763 \angle 121.8^{\circ} kV\)

Symmetrical components of phase-a current are

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{I}_{a 0}=-\frac{\bar{V}_{4 a 0}}{j X_{0}}=\frac{0.2610}{j 0.04}=-j 6.525 \\&\bar{I}_{a 1}=\frac{\bar{V}_{f}-\bar{V}_{4 a 1}}{j X^{\prime \prime}}=\frac{1.0-0.7754}{j 0.2}=-j 1.123 \\&\bar{I}_{a 2}=-\frac{\bar{V}_{4 a 2}}{j X_{2}}=\frac{0.2246}{j 0.2}=-j 1.123\end{aligned}\)

The phase-c currents in machine 2 are calculated as

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{I}_{c} &=\bar{I}_{a 0}+a \bar{I}_{a 1}+a^{2} \bar{I}_{a 2} \\&=-j 6.525+a(-j 1.123)+a^{2}(-j 1.123) \\&=-j 5.402\end{aligned}\)

Base current in the machine circuit is \(\frac{100 \times 10^{3}}{\sqrt{3}(20)}=2886.751 A\)

\(\therefore I_{c}=15,594 A\)
