Guest Question.

The following data were obtained during a tensile test on a mild steel specimen having an initial diameter of 0.505 inch. At failure, the reduced diameter of the specimen was 0.305 inch. Plot the data, and determine the following:
a. Modulus of elasticity.
b. Proportional limit.
c. Ultimate strength.
d. Percentage reduction in area.
e. Percentage elongation.
f. Indicated strength at rupture.
g. True rupture strength.

Solution

Initial diameter \(=0.505^{\prime \prime } \)

 

\(L_{orig} =2.00^{\prime \prime } \)

 

\(A_{orig} =0.20 in.^{2}\)

 

Reduced diameter at failure \(=0.305^{\prime \prime } \)

 

\(A_{fail} =0.073 \space in.^{2}\)

 

a. Modulus of elasticity:

 

\(E=\frac{\Delta f}{\Delta \varepsilon} =\frac{(30,000 \space \# /in.^{2})}{0.0010} =30,000,000 \# /in.^{2}\)

 

b. Proportional limit is the last point of the linear part of the diagram.

 

\(\therefore f_{prop}=30,000 \space psi\)

 

c. Ultimate strength is the absolute highest stress magnitude.

 

\(\therefore f_{ult}=67,900 \space psi\)

 

d. Percentage area reduction equals

 

\(\frac{A_{orig}-A_{fail}}{A_{orig}} =\frac{0.20-0.073}{0.20} \times 100 \%=63.5 \%\)

 

e. Percentage elongation equals

 

\(\frac{\delta _{total} }{L_{original}} =\frac{0.625^{\prime \prime }}{2.00^{\prime \prime } } \times 100 \%=31.25 \%\)

 

f. Indicated strength of rupture:

 

\(f_{rupture}=49,300 \space psi\)

 

g. True rupture strength:

 

\(f_{rupture}=\frac{9,860\# }{0.073 in.^{2}} =135,068 \space psi\)
