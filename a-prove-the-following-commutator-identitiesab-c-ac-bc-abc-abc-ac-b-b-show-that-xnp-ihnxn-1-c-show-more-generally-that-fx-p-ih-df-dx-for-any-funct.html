Guest Question.

(a) Prove the following commutator identities:

\( [\hat{A}+\hat{B}, \hat{C}]=[\hat{A}, \hat{C}]+[\hat{B}, \hat{C}] \), (3.64).

\( [\hat{A} \hat{B}, \hat{C}]=\hat{A}[\hat{B}, \hat{C}]+[\hat{A}, \hat{C}] \hat{B} \). (3.65)

(b) Show that

\( \left[x^{n}, \hat{p}\right]=i \hbar n x^{n-1} \).

(c) Show more generally that

\( [f(x), \hat{p}]=i \hbar \frac{d f}{d x} \) (3.66)

for any function f(x) that admits a Taylor series expansion.

(d) Show that for the simple harmonic oscillator

\( \left[\hat{H}, \hat{a}_{\pm}\right]=\pm \hbar \omega \hat{a}_{\pm} \) (3.67).

Hint: Use Equation 2.54.

\( \hat{H}=\hbar \omega\left(\hat{a}_{-} \hat{a}_{+}-\frac{1}{2}\right) \) (2.54).

Admin
Solution

(a)

\( [\hat{A}+\hat{B}, \hat{C}]=(\hat{A}+\hat{B}) \hat{C}-\hat{C}(\hat{A}+\hat{B})=\hat{A} \hat{C}+\hat{B} \hat{C}-\hat{C} \hat{A}-\hat{C} \hat{B}=[\hat{A}, \hat{C}]+\hat{B}, \hat{C}] \).

\( [\hat{A} \hat{B}, \hat{C}]=\hat{A} \hat{B} \hat{C}-\hat{C} \hat{A} \hat{B}=\hat{A} \hat{B} \hat{C}-\hat{A} \hat{C} \hat{B}+\hat{A} \hat{C} \hat{B}-\hat{C} \hat{A} \hat{B}=\hat{A}[\hat{B}, \hat{C}]+[\hat{A}, \hat{C}] \hat{B} \).

(b) Introducing a test function g(x), as in Eq. 2.51:

\( [x, \hat{p}] f(x)=\left[x(-i \hbar) \frac{d}{d x}(f)-(-i \hbar) \frac{d}{d x}(x f)\right]=-i \hbar\left(x \frac{d f}{d x}-x \frac{d f}{d x}-f\right) =i \hbar f(x) \) (2.51).
\( \left[x^{n}, \hat{p}\right] g=x^{n} \frac{\hbar}{i} \frac{d g}{d x}-\frac{\hbar}{i} \frac{d}{d x}\left(x^{n} g\right)=x^{n} \frac{\hbar}{i} \frac{d g}{d x}-\frac{\hbar}{i}\left(n x^{n-1} g+x^{n} \frac{d g}{d x}\right)=i \hbar n x^{n-1} g \).

So, dropping the test function, \( \left[x^{n}, \hat{p}\right]=i \hbar n x^{n-1} \).

(c) \( [f, \hat{p}] g=f \frac{\hbar}{i} \frac{d g}{d x}-\frac{\hbar}{i} \frac{d}{d x}(f g)=f \frac{\hbar}{i} \frac{d g}{d x}-\frac{\hbar}{i}\left(\frac{d f}{d x} g+f \frac{d g}{d x}\right)=i \hbar \frac{d f}{d x} g \Rightarrow[f, \hat{p}]=i \hbar \frac{d f}{d x} \).

(d)

\( \left[\hat{H}, \hat{a}_{\pm}\right]=\left[\hbar \omega\left(\hat{a}_{-} \hat{a}_{+}-\frac{1}{2}\right), \hat{a}_{\pm}\right]=\hbar \omega\left\{\left[\hat{a}_{-} \hat{a}_{+}, \hat{a}_{\pm}\right]- \cancel{\left[\frac{1}{2}, \hat{a}_{\pm}\right]}\right\}=\hbar \omega\left\{\hat{a}_{-}\left[\hat{a}_{+}, \hat{a}_{\pm}\right]+\left[\hat{a}_{-}, \hat{a}_{\pm}\right] \hat{a}_{+}\right\} \).

So, using \( \left[\hat{a}_{-}, \hat{a}_{+}\right]=1 \) (Equation 2.56),

\( \left[\hat{a}_{-}, \hat{a}_{+}\right]=1 \) (2.56).

\( \left[\hat{H}, \hat{a}_{+}\right]=\hbar \omega\left\{\hat{a}_{-} \cancel {\left[\hat{a}_{+}, \hat{a}_{+}\right]}+\left[\hat{a}_{-}, \hat{a}_{+}\right] \hat{a}_{+}\right\}=\hbar \omega \hat{a}_{+} \), and

\( \left[\hat{H}, \hat{a}_{-}\right]=\hbar \omega\left\{\hat{a}_{-}\left[\hat{a}_{+}, \hat{a}_{-}\right]+ \cancel{\left[\hat{a}_{-}, \hat{a}_{-}\right]} \hat{a}_{+}\right\}=\hbar \omega\left(-\hat{a}_{-}\right) \), or, combining them

\( \left[\hat{H}, \hat{a}_{\pm}\right]=\pm \hbar \omega \hat{a}_{\pm} \).
