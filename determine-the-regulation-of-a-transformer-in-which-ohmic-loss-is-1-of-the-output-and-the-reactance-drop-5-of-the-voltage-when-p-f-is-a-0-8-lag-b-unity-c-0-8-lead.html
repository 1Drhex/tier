Guest Question.

Determine the regulation of a transformer in which ohmic loss is 1% of the output and the reactance drop 5% of the voltage when p.f. is

(a) 0.8 lag (b) unity (c) 0.8 lead.

Admin
Solution

Since ohmic loss is 1 % of the output

 

i.e. \(\frac{I^{2}R}{VI}=0.01\) or \(\frac{IR}{V}=0.01\)

 

and reactance drop is 5% of the voltage

 

i.e. \(IX=0.05 V\) or \(\frac{IX}{V}=0.05\)

 

(a) Using equation 5.25 for percent regulation we have

 

% reguation \(=\left ( \frac{IR}{V}\cos \theta \pm \frac{IX}{V} \right )100\)

 

For 0.8 lag p.f. taking plus sign we have

 

\((0.01\times 0.8+0.05\times 0.6)100=3.8\%\)

 

(b) For unity p.f. \(\sin \theta =0\) Hence

 

% regulation \(\frac{IR}{V}100=0.01\times 1\times 100\)

 

=1%

 

(c) For 0.8 p.f. lead\( \sin \theta =-0.6\) Hence

 

\((0.01\times 0.8-0.05\times 0.6)100=-2.2\%\)
