Guest Question.

Evaluate the stiffness matrix for the isoparametric quadrilateral element shown in Figure 9.7 for plane stress with \(\ E=30(10)^{6} \) psi, v = 0.3, t = 1 in . Note that the properties are those of steel.

Admin
Solution

The mapping functions are

\(\ x(r,s)=\frac{1}{4}\left[(1-r)(1-s)(1)+(1+r)(1-s)(2)+(1+r)(1+s)(2.25)+(1-r)(1+s)(1.25) \right] \)

 

\(\ y(r,s)=\frac{1}{4}\left[(1-r)(1-s)(0)+(1+r)(1-s)(0)+(1+r)(1+s)(1.5)+(1-r)(1+s)(1) \right] \)

and the terms of the Jacobian matrix are

\(\ J_{11}=\frac{dx}{dr}=\frac{1}{2} \)

\(\ J_{12}=\frac{dy}{dr}=\frac{1}{4}(0.5-0.5s) \)

\(\ J_{21}=\frac{dx}{ds}=\frac{1}{2} \)

\(\ J_{22}=\frac{dy}{ds}=\frac{1}{4}(2.5-0.5r) \)

and the determinant is

\(\ \left|J\right| = J_{11}J_{22} -J_{12}J_{21}=\frac{1}{16} (4-r+s) \)

Therefore, the geometric matrix [G] of Equation 9.79 is known in terms of ratios of monomials in r and s as

\(\ \left[G\right] =\frac{1}{\left|J\right| }\left [ \begin{matrix} J_{22} & -J_{12} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & a0 & -J_{21} & J_{11} \\ –J_{21} & J_{11} & J_{22} & -J_{12} \end{matrix} \right ] \) (9.79)

 

\(\ \left[G\right] =\frac{4}{4-r+s}\left [ \begin{matrix} 2.5-0.5r & -(0.5-0.5s) & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -2 & 2 \\ -2 & 2 & 2.5-0.5r & -(0.5-0.5s) \end{matrix} \right ] \)

For plane stress with the values given, the material property matrix is

\(\ \left[D\right] =32.97(10)^{6} \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0.3 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0.35 \end{matrix} \right ] psi \)

Next, we note that, since the matrix of partial derivatives [P] as deﬁned in Equation 9.81 is also composed of monomials in r and s,

\(\ \left \{ \begin{matrix} \frac{\partial u}{\partial r} \\ \frac{\partial u}{\partial s} \\ \frac{\partial v}{\partial r} \\ \frac{\partial v}{\partial s} \end{matrix} \right \} \left[P\right]\left\{\delta \right\} \) (9.81)

 

\(\ \left[P\right]=\frac{1}{4} \left [ \begin{matrix} s-1 & 1-s & 1+s & -(1+s) & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ r-1 & -(1+r) & 1+r & 1-r & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & s-1 & 1-s & 1+s & -(1+s) \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & r-1 & -(1+r) & 1+r & 1-r \end{matrix} \right ] \)

the stiffness matrix of Equation 9.86 is no more than quadratic in the natural coordinates. Hence, we select four integration points given by

\(\ \left[K^{(e)} \right]=t\int\limits_{A}^{}{\left[B\right] ^{T}\left[D\right]\left[B\right] \left|J\right| dr ds }=t\int\limits_{-1}^{1}{\int\limits_{-1}^{1}{\left[B\right] ^{T}\left[D\right]\left[B\right] \left|J\right| dr ds} } \) (9.86)

 

\(\ r_{i} =s_{j}=\pm \frac{\sqrt{3} }{3} \)

and weighting factors

\(\ W_{i} =W_{j}=1.0 \)

per Table 6.1.

The element stiffness matrix is then given by

\(\ \left[k^{(e)} \right]=t\sum\limits_{i=1}^{2}{\sum\limits_{j=1}^{2}{W_{i}W_{j}\left[B(r_{i},s_{j})\right] ^{T}\left[D\right]\left[B(r_{i},s_{j})\right]\left|J(r_{i},s_{j})\right| } } \)

The numerical results for this example are obtained via a computer program written in MATLAB using the built-in matrix functions of that software package. The stiffness matrix is calculated to be

\(\ \left[K^{(e)} \right]=\left [ \begin{matrix} 2305 & -1759 & -617 & 72 & 798 & -152 & -214 & -432 \\ -1759 & 1957 & 471 & -669 & -52 & -522 & 14 & 560 \\ -617 & 471 & 166 & -19 & -214 & 41 & 57 & 116 \\ 72 & -669 & -19 & 616 & -533 & 633 & 143 & -244 \\ 798 & -52 & -214 & -533 & 1453 & -169 & -389 & -895 \\ -152 & -522 & -41 & 633 & -169 & 933 & 45 & -869 \\ -214 & 14 & 57 & 143 & -389 & 45 & 104 & 240 \\ -432 & 560 & 116 & -244 & -895 & -869 & 240 & 1524 \end{matrix} \right ] 10^{3} Ib/ in. \)
