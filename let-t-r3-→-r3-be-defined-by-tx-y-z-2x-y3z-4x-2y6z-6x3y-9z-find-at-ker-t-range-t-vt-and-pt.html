Guest Question.

Let \(T: \mathbb{R}^{3} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{3}\) be defined by \(T\left(\begin{array}{l}x \\ y \\ z\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{r}2 x-y+3 z \\ 4 x-2 y+6 z \\ -6 x+3 y-9 z\end{array}\right)\). Find \(A_{T}\), ker \(T\), range \(T\), \(\nu(T)\), and \(\rho(T)\).

Admin
Solution

Since \(T\left(\begin{array}{l}1 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{r}2 \\ 4 \\ -6\end{array}\right), T\left(\begin{array}{l}0 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{r}-1 \\ -2 \\ 3\end{array}\right)\), and \(T\left(\begin{array}{l}0 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{r}3 \\ 6 \\ -9\end{array}\right)\), we have

\(A_{T}=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}2 & -1 & 3 \\4 & -2 & 6 \\-6 & 3 & -9\end{array}\right)\)

From we see that \(\rho(A)=\rho(T)=1\) and range \(T=\operatorname{span}\left\{\left(\begin{array}{r}2 \\ 4 \\ -6\end{array}\right)\right\}\). Then
\(\nu(T)= 2\). To find \(N_{A} = ker T\), we row-reduce to solve the system

\(A x=0\) : \(\left(\begin{array}{rrr|l}2 & -1 & 3 & 0 \\ 4 & -2 & 6 & 0 \\ -6 & 3 & -9 & 0\end{array}\right) \begin{array}{r}A_{2,1}(-2) \\A_{2,3}(3) \\{\longrightarrow}\end{array}\left(\begin{array}{rrr|r}2 & -1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right)\). This means that \(\left(\begin{array}{l}x \\ y \\ z\end{array}\right) \in N_{A}\) if \(2 x-y+3 z=0\) or \(y=2 x+3 z\). First setting \(x=1, z=0\) and then \(x=0, z=1\),
we obtain a basis for \(N_{A}: \operatorname{ker} T=N_{A}=\operatorname{span}\left\{\left(\begin{array}{l}1 \\ 2 \\ 0\end{array}\right),\left(\begin{array}{l}0 \\ 3 \\ 1\end{array}\right)\right\}.\)
