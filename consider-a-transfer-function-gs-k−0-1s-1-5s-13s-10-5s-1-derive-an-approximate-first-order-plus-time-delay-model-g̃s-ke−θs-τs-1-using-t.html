Guest Question.

Consider a transfer function:
\(G(s)=\frac{K(-0.1s+1)}{(5s+1)(3s+1)(0.5s+1)} \) (a)
Derive an approximate first-order-plus-time-delay model,
\(\tilde{G} (s)=\frac{Ke^{-\theta s}}{\tau s+1} \) (b)
using two methods:
(a) The Taylor series expansions of Equations:
\(e^{-\theta s}\thickapprox 1-\theta s\) (c)
and
\(e^{-\theta s}=\frac{1}{e^{\theta s}} \thickapprox \frac{1}{1+\theta s} \) (d)

(b) Skogestad’s half rule.
Compare the normalized responses of G(s) and the approximate models for a unit step input.

Admin
Solution

(a) The dominant time constant (5) is retained. Applying
the approximations in Equations (c) and (d) gives
\(-0.1s+1\approx e^{-0.1s}\) (e)
and
\(\frac{1}{3s+1}\approx e^{-3s} \quad \frac{1}{0.5s+1}\approx e^{-0.5s} \) (f)
Substitution into Equation (a) gives the Taylor series approximation, \(\tilde{G}_{TS}(s) \):

\(\tilde{G}_{TS}(s) =\frac{Ke^{-0.1s}e^{-3s}e^{-0.5s}}{5s+1} =\frac{Ke^{-3.6s}}{5s+1} \) (g)

(b) To apply Skogestad’s method, we note that the largest neglected time constant in Equation (a) has a value of 3. According to his “half rule,” half of this value is added to the next largest time constant to generate a new time constant, \(\tau\) = 5 + 0.5(3) = 6.5. The other half provides a new time delay of 0.5(3) = 1.5. The approximation of the RHP zero in Equation (e) provides an additional time delay of 0.1. Approximating the smallest time constant of 0.5 in Equation (a) by Equation (d) produces an additional time delay of 0.5. Thus, the total time delay in Equation (b) is
θ = 1.5 + 0.1 + 0.5 = 2.1
and G(s) can be approximated as
\(\tilde{G}_{SK}(s) =\frac{Ke^{-2.1s}}{6.5s+1} \)
