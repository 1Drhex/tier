Guest Question.

In a chemical plant, a 3-m-diameter spherical tank contains a hot fluid that causes the surface temperature to be \( 150^{\circ} C\) (Fig. 9-33). For safety purposes, the tank is enclosed by a 3.15-m-diameter concentric outer spherical cover. The concentric enclosure provides an air gap that serves as an insulation layer. To prevent workplace hazards such as thermal burn on the skin, the outer cover surface temperature should be kept below \( 45^{\circ} C\). The temperature of ambient air outside the tank is \( 20^{\circ} C\) and the outer cover surface of the tank is covered with paint that gives an emissivity of 0.9. Determine whether the air gap is sufficient to keep the outer cover surface temperature below \( 45^{\circ} C\) to prevent thermal burns. Assume that the properties of air in the enclosure and at the outer surface can be evaluated at \( 95^{\circ} C\) and \( 30^{\circ} C\) and 1 atm pressure, respectively.

 

 

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION In this example, the concepts of PtD are applied in conjunction with the concepts of natural convection over a surface and in an enclosure.

Assumptions 1 Steady operating conditions exist. 2 Surface temperatures are constant. 3 Air is an ideal gas with constant properties. 4 Heat loss by radiation is negligible. 5 The air pressure is at 1 atm. 6 The surrounding air temperature is \( T_{\text {surr }}=T_{\infty}\).

Properties The properties of air in the enclosure at the assumed average temperature

\( T_{\text {avg }}=\left(T_{i}+T_{0}\right) / 2=95^{\circ} C\)

 

and 1 atm pressure are

\( k=0.03060 W / m \cdot K\)

 

\( v=2.254 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2} / s \)

 

Pr =0.7122 Table A-15

 

and \( \beta=1 / T_{ avg }=1 / 368 K\)

 

The properties of air surrounding the outer surface at the assumed film tempera ture

\( T_{f}=\left(T_{0}+T_{\infty}\right) / 2=30^{\circ} C\)

 

and 1 atm pressure are

 

\( k=0.02588 W / m \cdot K v=1.608 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2} / s \)

 

\( \operatorname{Pr}=0.7282\) Table (A -15)

 

and \( \beta=1 / T_{f}=1 / 303 K\).

 

The emissivity of the outer surface is given as

\( \varepsilon=0.9\).

 

Analysis For natural convection in the concentric enclosure, with the assumed \( T_{\text {avg }}=95^{\circ} C\), the outer surface temperature is estimated as

\( T_{o}=2 T_{\text {avg }}-T_{i}=2(95)-150=40^{\circ} C\)

The Rayleigh number is

\( Ra _{L}=\frac{g \beta\left(T_{i}-T_{o}\right) L_{c}^{3}}{\nu^{2}} \operatorname{Pr} \)

 

\( =\frac{\left(9.81 m / s ^{2}\right)(95+273 K )^{-1}(150-40) K (0.075 m )^{3}}{\left(2.254 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2} / s \right)^{2}}(0.7122)=1.7342 \times 10^{6} \)

 

\( \text { where } L_{c}=\left(D_{o}-D_{i}\right) / 2=(3.15-3.0) / 2=0.075 m\)

 

The effective thermal conductivity is

\(F_{ sph }=\frac{L_{c}}{\left(D_{i} D_{o}\right)^{4}\left(D_{i}^{-7 / 5}+D_{o}^{-7 / 5}\right)^{5}} \)

 

\(=\frac{0.075 m }{[(3.0 m )(3.15 m )]^{4}\left[(3.0 m )^{-7 / 5}+(3.15 m )^{-7 / 5}\right]^{5}}=0.0007602\)

 

\(k_{ eff }=0.74 k\left(\frac{ Pr }{0.861+ Pr }\right)^{1 / 4}\left(F_{ sph } Ra \right)^{1 / 4}\)

 

\(=0.74(0.03060 W / m \cdot K )\)

 

\(\left(\frac{0.7122}{0.861+0.7122}\right)^{1 / 4}\left[(0.0007602)\left(1.7342 \times 10^{6}\right)\right]^{1 / 4}\)

 

\(=0.1119 W / m \cdot K\)

 

For natural convection at the outer surface with the surrounding air, the Rayleigh number is

\(Ra _{D}=\frac{g \beta\left(T_{o}-T_{\infty}\right) D_{o}^{3}}{\nu^{2}} Pr\)

 

\(=\frac{\left(9.81 m / s ^{2}\right)(30+273 K )^{-1}(40-20) K (3.15 m )^{3}}{\left(1.608 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2} / s \right)^{2}}(0.7282)=5.6999 \times 10^{10}\)

 

The Nusselt number and the natural convection heat transfer coefficient at the outer surface are

\(Nu =2+\frac{0.589 Ra _{D}^{1 / 4}}{\left[1+\left(\frac{0.469}{ Pr }\right)^{9 / 16}\right]^{4 / 9}}=2+\frac{0.589\left(5.6999 \times 10^{10}\right)^{1 / 4}}{\left[1+\left(\frac{0.469}{0.7282}\right)^{9 / 16}\right]^{4 / 9}}=224.69 \)

 

\(h=\frac{k}{D_{o}} Nu =\frac{0.02588 W / m \cdot K }{3.15 m }(224.69)=1.846 W / m ^{2} \cdot K\)

 

The outer surface temperature can be solved from rate of heat balance at the outer surface as

\(\dot{Q}_{\text {encl }}=\dot{Q}_{\text {conv }}+\dot{Q}_{\text {rad }}\)

 

\(k_{ eff } \pi\left(\frac{D_{i} D_{o}}{L_{c}}\right)\left(T_{i}-T_{o}\right)=h\left(\pi D_{o}^{2}\right)\left(T_{o}-T_{\infty}\right)+\varepsilon \sigma\left(\pi D_{o}^{2}\right)\left(T_{o}^{4}-T_{ surr }^{4}\right) \rightarrow T_{o}=40.6^{\circ} C \)

 

Discussion The air gap between the concentric spheres and the high emissivity of the painted outer surface are sufficient to keep the outer cover surface temperature below \( 45^{\circ} C\) to alleviate thermal burn hazards. The assumed average temperature \( T_{\text {avg }}=95^{\circ} C\) and film temperature \( T_{f}=30^{\circ} C\) are appropriate for evaluating the air properties in the enclosure and at the outer surface, respectively. The determined \( T_{0}=40.6^{\circ} C\) would give \( T_{\text {avg }}=95.3^{\circ} C\) and \( T_{f}=30.3^{\circ} C\)
