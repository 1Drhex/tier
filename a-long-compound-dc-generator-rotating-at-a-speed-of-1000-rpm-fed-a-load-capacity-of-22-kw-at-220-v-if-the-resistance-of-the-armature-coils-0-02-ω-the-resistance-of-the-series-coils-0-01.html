Guest Question.

A long compound DC generator, rotating at a speed of \(1000 \mathrm{rpm}\), fed a load capacity of \(22 \mathrm{~kW}\) at \(220 \mathrm{~V}\), if the resistance of the armature coils \(0.02 \Omega\), the resistance of the series coils \(0.01 \Omega\), and the resistance of the shunt coils \(110 \Omega_{\Omega}\), calculate generator efficiency if the iron and mechanical losses are \(2500 \mathrm{~W}\).

Admin
Solution

\(P_{i}+P_{\text {mec }}=2500 \mathrm{~W}\)

\(I_{L}=P_{\text {out }} / V L=\frac{22 \times 1000}{220}=100 \mathrm{~A}\)

\(I_{s h}=V_{s h} / R_{s h}=V_{L} / R_{s h}=220 / 110=2 \mathrm{~A}\)

\(I_{a}=I_{l}+i_{s h}=100+2=102 \mathrm{~A}\)

\(P_{c u}=I_{a}{ }^{2} R_{a}+I^{2}{ }_{a} R_{s e}+I^{2}{ }_{s h} R_{s h}\)

\(P_{c u}=(102)^{2} \times 0.02+(102)^{2} \times 0.01+(2)^{2} \times 110\)

\(P_{c u}=752.12 \mathrm{~W}\)

the power losses \(=P_{\text {cu }}+\left(P_{i}+P_{mech^{.}}\right)\)

so, power losses \(=752.1+2500=3252.1 \mathrm{~W}\)

\(\eta=\frac{P_{\text {out }}}{P_{\text {out }}+\text { losses }}=\frac{22000}{22000+3252.1}=\frac{22000}{25252.1}=\eta=0.87=87 \%\).
