Guest Question.

To rescue shipwrecked persons, a rescue package of mass m is dropped from an airplane, flying with speed \(v_{0}=200 km/ h\) in a height of \( h = 150 m\) .

a) Determine the distance \(s_{B} \) from launching the package until it impacts on sea surface.

b) Calculate the impact velocity \(v_{B} \) Assuming a high horizontal velocity component \(v_{h} \) , the air drag shall be taken into account by a horizontal drag force \(D=\kappa mv^{2}_{h} \) with \(\kappa=0.003 m^{-1} \) .

Admin
Solution

a)We introduce an appropriate coordinate system and sketch the free body diagram with the acting drag force \(D\) and the weight \(mg\) . With \(v_{h}=\dot{x} \) , the equations of motion in \(x\)– and in \(z\) direction read

\(\longleftarrow :m\ddot{x}=-m\kappa \dot{x}^{2} \) , \(\uparrow :m\ddot{z}=-mg \) .

Integration yields with the initial conditions \( x\left(0\right)=0\) , \( z\left(0\right)=h \) , \(\dot{x}\left(0\right)=v_{0} \) , \(\dot{z}\left(0\right)=0\)

 

\(\int_{v_{0} }^{\dot{x} }{\frac{d\dot{\bar{x} } }{\dot{\bar{x}}^{2} }}=-\kappa \int_{0}^{t}{d\bar{t} } \rightsquigarrow \dot{x}=\frac{1}{\frac{1}{v_{0} } +\kappa t} \) ,

\(\int_{0 }^{x }d\bar{x} =\int_{0}^{t}\frac{1}{\frac{1}{v_{0} } +\kappa \bar{t} } {d\bar{t} } \rightsquigarrow x=\frac{1}{c_{0} }\ln \left(1+\kappa v_{0}t \right) \)

 

\(\dot{z}=-gt\) , \(z=-\frac{g}{2}t^{2}+h \) .

The impact time \(t_{B}\) follows from \(z_{B}=0\) as

\(t_{B}=\sqrt{2h/ g} \) ,

and thus, we obtain for the distance

\(\underline{\underline{s_{B}=x\left(t_{B} \right)=\frac{1}{\kappa } \ln \left(1+\kappa v_{0}\sqrt{2h/ g} \right) =218 m } } \) .

b) From the velocity components at impact,

\(\dot{x}\left(t_{B} \right)=\frac{v_{0} }{1+\kappa v_{0}t_{B}}=104km/ h \) ,

\(\dot{z}\left(t_{B} \right)=-gt_{B}=-54.2m/s=-195km/h \) ,

results the velocity as

\(\underline{\underline{v_{B}=\sqrt{\dot{x}^{2} \left(t_{B} \right)+\dot{z}^2\left(t_{B} \right)}=221km/h } } \) .
