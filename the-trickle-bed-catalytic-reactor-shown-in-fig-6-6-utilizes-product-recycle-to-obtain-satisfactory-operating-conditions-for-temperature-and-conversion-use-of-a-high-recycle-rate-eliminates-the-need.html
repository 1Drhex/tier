Guest Question.

The trickle-bed catalytic reactor shown in Figure below utilizes product recycle to obtain satisfactory operating conditions for temperature and conversion. Use of a high recycle rate eliminates the need for mechanical agitation. The concentration of the reactant is measured at a point in the recycle line where the product stream is removed. A liquid phase first-order reaction is assumed.
Under normal operating conditions, the following assumptions may be made:
(i) The reactor operates isothermally with a reaction rate given by r=kc, where −r denotes the rate of disappearance of reactant per unit volume, c is the concentration of reactant, and k is the rate constant.
(ii) All flow rates and the liquid volume V are constant.
(iii) No reaction occurs in the piping. The dynamics of the exit and recycle lines can be approximated as constant time delays, \( θ_{1} \space and \space θ_{2}, \) as indicated in the figure. Let \( c_{1} \) denote the reactant concentration at the measurement point.
(iv) Because of the high recycle flow rate, the reactor is completely mixed.

(a) Derive an expression for the transfer function \( C^{\prime }_{1}(s)/C^{\prime }_{i}(s). \)
(b) Using the following information, calculate \( C^{\prime }_{1}(t) \) for a step change in \( C^{\prime }_{i}(t) \) of 2000 \( kg/m^{3} \).

Parameter Values

\( V = 5m^{3} \) α=12
\( q = 0.05 m^{3} /min \) \( \theta _{1} = 0.9 min \)
\( k = 0.04 min^{−1} \) \( \theta _{2} = 1.1 min \)

Admin
Solution

(a) In analogy with Equation
\(V\frac{dc_{A}}{dt}=q(c_{Ai}-c_{A})-Vkc_{A}\);
the component balance around the reactor is,
\(V\frac{dc}{dt}=qc_{i}+\alpha qc_{2}-(1+\alpha )qc-Vkc \) (a)
where the concentration of the reactant is denoted by c. Equation (a) is a linear ODE with constant coefficients. Subtracting the steady-state equation and substituting deviation variables yields
\(V\frac{dc^{\prime}}{dt}=qc^{\prime}_{i}+\alpha qc^{\prime}_{2}-(1+\alpha )qc^{\prime}-Vkc^{\prime} \) (b)
Additional relations are needed for \( c^{\prime}_{2}(t) \space and \space c^{\prime}_{1}(t) \). Because the exit and recycle lines can be modeled as time delays,
\(c^{\prime}_{1}(t)=c^{\prime}(t-\theta _{1})\) (c)
\(c^{\prime}_{2}(t)=c_{1}^{\prime}(t-\theta _{2})\) (d)
Equations (b) through (d) provide the process model for the isothermal reactor with recycle. Taking the Laplace transform of each equation yields
\(sVC^{\prime}(s)=qC^{\prime}_{i}(s)+\alpha qC^{\prime}_{2}(s)-(1+\alpha )qC^{\prime}(s)-VkC^{\prime}(s)\) (e)

\(C^{\prime}_{1}(s)=e^{-\theta _{1}s}C^{\prime}(s)\) (f)

\(C^{\prime}_{2}(s)=e^{-\theta _{2}s}C^{\prime}_{1}(s)=e^{-(\theta _{1}+\theta _{2})s}C^{\prime}(s)=e^{-\theta _{3}s}C^{\prime}(s)\) (g)

where \(\theta _{3}\triangleq \theta _{1}+\theta _{2}\). Substitute Equation (g) into Equation (e) and solve for \(C^{\prime}(s)\):

\(C^{\prime}(s)=\frac{q}{sV-\alpha qe^{-\theta _{3}s}+(1+\alpha )q+Vk}C^{\prime}_{i}(s)\) (h)

Equation (h) can be rearranged to the following form:
\(C^{\prime}(s)=\frac{K}{\tau s+1+\alpha K(1-e^{-\theta _{3}s})} C^{\prime}_{i}(s)\) (i)
where
\(K=\frac{q}{q+Vk} \) (j)
\(\tau =\frac{V}{q+Vk} \) (k)
Note that, in the limit as \(\theta _{3}\rightarrow 0, e^{-\theta _{3}s}\rightarrow1 \) and
\(C^{\prime}(s)=\frac{K}{\tau s+1} C^{\prime}_{i}(s)\) (l)
So K and \(\tau\) can be interpreted as the process gain and time constant, respectively, of a recycle reactor with no time delay in the recycle line, which is equivalent to a stirred isothermal reactor with no recycle.
The desired transfer function \(C^{\prime}_{1}(s)/C^{\prime}_{i}(s)\) is obtained by combining Equations (i) and (f) to obtain
\(\frac{C^{\prime}_{1}(s)}{C^{\prime}_{i}(s)} =\frac{Ke^{-\theta _{1}s}}{\tau s+1+\alpha K(1-e^{-\theta _{3}s})} \) (m)
The numerical parameters in Equation (m) are
\(K=\frac{q}{q+Vk}=\frac{0.05}{0.05+(5)(0.04)}=0.2 \)
\(\tau =\frac{V}{q+Vk}=20min \)

(b) The easiest way to simulate the step response is to construct a Simulink diagram (see Appendix C) corresponding to Figure above, after substituting the numerical values into the transfer functions and transforms. The recycle loop can be treated graphically in Simulink instead of solving the model Equation (a).
