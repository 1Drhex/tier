Alternative Form of the Gram-Schmidt Orthonormalization Process
Find an orthonormal basis for the solution space of the homogeneous linear system.
\(x_{1}+x_{2}+7x_{4}=0\)

\(2x_{1}+x_{2}+2x_{3}+6x_{4}=0\).

Admin
Solution

The augmented matrix for this system reduces as shown below.

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 7 & 0 \\ 2 & 1 & 2 & 6 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \longrightarrow \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 2 & -1 & 0 \\ 2 & 1 & 2 & 6 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

If you let \(x_3= s and x_4= t\) , then each solution of the system has the form

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{matrix} \right]=\left [ \begin{matrix} -2s+t \\ 2s-8t \\ s \\ t \end{matrix} \right ] =s\left [ \begin{matrix} -2 \\ 2 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ] +t\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ -8 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ] \).

So, one basis for the solution space is \(B=\left\{v_1,v_2\right\} =\left\{\left(-2,2,1,0\right) ,\left(1,-8,0,1\right)\right\} \)

To find an orthonormal basis\( B′ = \left\{u_1,u_2\right\} \), use the alternative form of the Gram-Schmidt orthonormalization process, as shown below.

\(u_1=\frac{v_1}{\left\|v_1\right\| } \\=\left(-\frac{2}{3} ,\frac{2}{3} ,\frac{1}{3} ,0\right) \)

 

\(w_2=v_2- 〈v_{2}, u_{1}〉u_{1}\\=\left(1,-8,0,1\right)\left[ \left(-1,-8,0,1\right) .\left(-\frac{2}{3},\frac{2}{3} ,\frac{1}{3} ,0\right) \right]\left( -\frac{2}{3},\frac{2}{3} ,\frac{1}{3} ,0\right) =\left(-3,-4,2,1\right) \)

\(u_2=\frac{w_2}{\left\|w_2\right\| } =\left(-\frac{3}{\sqrt{30} } ,-\frac{4}{\sqrt{30} } ,\frac{2}{\sqrt{30} } ,\frac{1}{\sqrt{30} }\right) \).
