Guest Question.

A triple expansion steam engine has HP, IP and LP cylinder diameters of 25 cm, 40 cm and 85 cm with actual mean effective pressures of 0.5 MPa, 0.3 MPa and 0.1 MPa. Steam is supplied at 1.5 MPa and the exhaust finally occurs at 25 kPa. The cut-off in HP cylinder occurs at 60% of the stroke. Considering all the cylinders to have some stroke length determine,
(i) the actual and hypothetical mep referred to LP cylinder,
(ii) the overall diagram factor
(iii) the contribution of each cylinder in overall output. Neglect clearance volume.

Admin
Solution

\(\begin{aligned}\text{In HP cylinder, mep of HP referred to LP}&= 0.5 × 10^{3} × \frac{A_{HP}}{A_{LP}}\\&= 0.5 × 10^{3} × \frac{\frac{π}{4} × (0.25)^{2}}{\frac{π}{4} × (0.85)^{2}}\\&= 43.25 kPa\\\text{In IP cylinder, mep of IP referred to LP}&= 0.3 × 10^{3} × \frac{A_{IP}}{A_{LP}}\\&= 0.3 × 10^{3} × \frac{\frac{π}{4} × (0.40)^{2}}{\frac{π}{4} × (0.85)^{2}}\\&= 66.44 kPa\end{aligned}\\\text{For complete engine the overall mep referred to LP cylinder}\\\begin{aligned}&= 43.25 + 66.44 + 0.1 × 10^{3}\\&= 209.69 kPa\\\text{Volume of LP cylinder}&= \frac{π}{4} × (0.85)^{2} × L = 0.567 L\\\text{Volume of HP cylinder}&= \frac{π}{4} × (0.25)^{2} × L = 0.049 L\\\text{Cut-off volume of HP c}&\text{ylinder = 0.6 × Volume of HP cylinder = 0.0294 L} \\\text{For zero clearance volu}&\text{me,}\\\text{Overall expansion ratio}&= \frac{\text{Volume of LP cylinder}}{\text{Cut off volume of HP cylinder}} = \frac{0.567 L}{0.049 L}\\r&= 19.28\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Hypothetical m.e.p overall}&= \frac{1.5 × 10^{3}}{19.28}(1 + \ln 19.28) – 25 = 283.02 kPa\\\text{Overall diagram factor}&= \frac{\text{Actual m.e.p.}}{\text{Hypothetical m.e. p.}}\\&= \frac{209.69}{283.02} = 0.741\end{aligned}\\\)Percentage of total indicated power developed in HP, IP and LP cylinder. Since stroke length for each cylinder is same so the ratio of indicated power output of each cylinder to total output shall be the ratio of m.e.p of specific cylinder referred to LP cylinder and overall mep referred to LP cylinder\(\\\begin{aligned}\%\text{ of HP cylinder output}&= \frac{43.25}{209.69} × 100 = 20.63\%\\\%\text{ of IP cylinder output}&= \frac{66.44}{209.69} × 100 = 31.68\%\\\%\text{ of LP cylinder output}&= \frac{0.1 × 10^{3}}{209.69} × 100 = 47.69\%\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Actual mep referred to LP}&= 209.69 kPa\\\text{Hypothetical mep referred to LP}&= 283.02 kPa\\\text{Overall diagram factor}&= 0.741\\\%\text{ of HP, IP and LP cylinder out}&\text{puts = }20.63\%, 31.68\%, 47.69\%\end{aligned}\)
