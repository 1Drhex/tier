Calculation of v by the Redlich–Kwong Equation of State and the Generalized Compressibility Charts

Calculate the volume occupied by 10 kg of butane at 50 bar and 60°C using the Redlich–Kwong equation and the generalized compressibility charts.

Admin
Solution

Using the Redlich-Kwong equation of state

We first find the Redlich–Kwong parameters a and b using critical properties:

 

\(a=\frac{0.42748 R^{2} T_{c}^{2.5}}{P_{c}}=29.08\left[\frac{ JK ^{1 / 2} m ^{3}}{ mol ^{2}}\right] \text { and } b=\frac{0.08664 R T_{c}}{P_{c}}=8.09 \times 10^{-5}\left[\frac{ m ^{3}}{ mol }\right]\)

 

We can use these with the Redlich–Kwong equation:

 

\(P=\frac{R T}{v-b}-\frac{a}{T^{1 / 2} v(v+b)}\)

 

Solving by trial and error, we get:

 

\(v=1.2 \times 10^{-4}\left[ m ^{3} / mol \right]\)

 

and,

 

\(V=\frac{m}{M W} \times v=\frac{10}{.05812} \times 1.20 \times 10^{-4}=0.021\left[ m ^{3}\right]\)

 

Using the compressibility charts

We first find \(P_{r}, T_{r}, \text { and } \omega\):

 

\(P_{r}=\frac{P}{P_{c}}=\frac{50 bar }{37.9 bar }=1.32, \quad T_{r}=\frac{T}{T_{c}}=\frac{333.2 K }{425.2 K }=0.78, \quad \text { and } \omega=0.193\)

 

From Tables C.1 and C.2, we get:

 

\(z^{0}\) \(z^{1}\)
\(P_{r}\) \(P_{r}\)
\(T_{r}\) 1.3 1.4 \(T_{r}\) 1.3 1.4
0.75 0.2142 0.2303 0.75 -0.0871 -0.0934
0.78 (interpolated) 0.2116 0.2274 -0.0843 -0.0903
0.8 0.2099 0.2255 0.8 -0.0825 -0.0883

 

By double linear interpolation, where the first interpolation is performed in the table, we get:

 

\(z^{(0)}=0.2116+\frac{0.02}{0.1}(0.2274-0.2116)=0.2148\)

 

\(z^{(1)}=-0.0843+\frac{0.02}{0.1}(-0.0903-(-0.0843))=-0.0855\)

 

Thus,

 

\(z=z^{(0)}+\omega z^{(1)}=0.198\)

 

The low value for the compressibility factor indicates that butane is a liquid. Now solving for volume:

 

\(v=\frac{z R T}{P}=\frac{0.198 \times 8.314 \times 333.15}{50 \times 10^{5}}=1.1 \times 10^{-4}\left[\frac{ m ^{3}}{ mol }\right]\)

 

and,

 

\(V=\frac{m}{M W} \times v=\frac{10}{.05812} \times 1.1 \times 10^{-4}=0.019\left[ m ^{3}\right]\)

 

The compressibility charts and the Redlich–Kwong equation give similar values for liquid butane at 50 bar and 60°C.
