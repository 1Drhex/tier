Guest Question.

Find the scattering amplitude for low-energy soft-sphere scattering in the second Born approximation. Answer:

\( -\left(2 m V_{0} a^{3} / 3 \hbar^{2}\right)\left[1-\left(4 m V_{0} a^{2} / 5 \hbar^{2}\right)\right] \).

Admin
Solution

First let’s set up the general formalism. From Eq. 10.101:

\( \psi=\psi_{0}+\int g V \psi_{0}+\iint g V g V \psi_{0}+\iiint g V g V g V \psi_{0}+\cdots \) (10.101).

\( \psi( r )=\psi_{0}( r )+\int g\left( r – r _{0}\right) V\left( r _{0}\right) \psi_{0}\left( r _{0}\right) d^{3} r _{0}+\int g\left( r – r _{0}\right) V\left( r _{0}\right)\left[\int g\left( r _{0}- r _{1}\right) V\left( r _{1}\right) \psi_{0}\left( r _{1}\right) d^{3} r _{1}\right] d^{3} r _{0}+\cdots \).

\( \text { Put in } \psi_{0}( r )=A e^{i k z}, \quad g( r )=-\frac{m}{2 \pi \hbar^{2}} \frac{e^{i k r}}{r} \):

\( \psi( r )=A e^{i k z}-\frac{m A}{2 \pi \hbar^{2}} \int \frac{e^{i k\left| r – r _{0}\right|}}{\left| r – r _{0}\right|} V\left( r _{0}\right) e^{i k z_{0}} d^{3} r _{0} \)

\( +\left(\frac{m}{2 \pi \hbar^{2}}\right)^{2} A \int \frac{e^{i k\left| r – r _{0}\right|}}{\left| r – r _{0}\right|} V\left( r _{0}\right)\left[\int \frac{e^{i k\left| r _{0}- r _{1}\right|}}{\left| r _{0}- r _{1}\right|} V\left( r _{1}\right) e^{i k z_{1}} d^{3} r _{1}\right] d^{3} r _{0} \).

In the scattering region \( r \gg r_{0}, \text { Eq. } 10.73 \Longrightarrow \frac{e^{i k\left| r – r _{0}\right|}}{\left| r – r _{0}\right|} \approx \frac{e^{i k r}}{r} e^{-i k \cdot r _{0}}, \quad \text { with } \quad k \equiv k \hat{r} \), so

\( \frac{e^{i k\left| r – r _{0}\right|}}{\left| r – r _{0}\right|} \approx \frac{e^{i k r}}{r} e^{-i k \cdot r _{0}} \) (10.7).

\( \psi( r )=A\left\{e^{i k z}-\frac{m}{2 \pi \hbar^{2}} \frac{e^{i k r}}{r} \int e^{-i k \cdot r 0} V\left( r _{0}\right) e^{i k z_{0}} d^{3} r _{0}\right. \)

\( \left.+\left(\frac{m}{2 \pi \hbar^{2}}\right)^{2} \frac{e^{i k r}}{r} \int e^{-i k \cdot r _{0}} V\left( r _{0}\right)\left[\int \frac{e^{i k\left| r _{0}- r _{1}\right|}}{\left| r _{0}- r _{1}\right|} V\left( r _{1}\right) e^{i k z_{1}} d^{3} r _{1}\right] d^{3} r _{0}\right\} \).

\( f(\theta, \phi)=-\frac{m}{2 \pi \hbar^{2}} \int e^{i\left( k ^{\prime}- k \right) \cdot r } V( r ) d^{3} r +\left(\frac{m}{2 \pi \hbar^{2}}\right)^{2} \int e^{-i k \cdot r } V( r )\left[\int \frac{e^{i k| r – r |}}{\left| r – r _{0}\right|} V\left( r _{0}\right) e^{i k z_{0}} d^{3} r _{0}\right] d^{3} r \).

I simplified the subscripts, since there is no longer any possible ambiguity. For low-energy scattering we drop the exponentials (see p. 393):

\( f(\theta, \phi) \approx-\frac{m}{2 \pi \hbar^{2}} \int V( r ) d^{3} r +\left(\frac{m}{2 \pi \hbar^{2}}\right)^{2} \int V( r )\left[\int \frac{1}{\left| r – r _{0}\right|} V\left( r _{0}\right) d^{3} r _{0}\right] d^{3} r \).

Now apply this to the potential in Eq. 10.81:

\( V( r )= \begin{cases}V_{0}, & (r \leq a) \\ 0, & (r>a)\end{cases} \) (10.81).

\( \int \frac{1}{\left| r – r _{0}\right|} V\left( r _{0}\right) d^{3} r _{0}=V_{0} \int_{0}^{a} \frac{1}{\left| r – r _{0}\right|} r_{0}^{2} \sin \theta_{0} d r_{0} d \theta_{0} d \phi_{0} \).

\( \text { Orient the } z_{0} \text { axis along } r \text {, so }\left| r – r _{0}\right|=r^{2}+r_{0}^{2}-2 r r_{0} \cos \theta_{0} \text {. } \)

\( \int \frac{1}{\left| r – r _{0}\right|} V\left( r _{0}\right) d^{3} r _{0}=V_{0} 2 \pi \int_{0}^{a} r_{0}^{2}\left[\int_{0}^{\pi} \frac{1}{\sqrt{r^{2}+r_{0}^{2}-2 r r_{0} \cos \theta_{0}}} \sin \theta_{0} d \theta_{0}\right] d r_{0} . \quad \text { But } \).

\( \int_{0}^{\pi} \frac{1}{\sqrt{r^{2}+r_{0}^{2}-2 r r_{0} \cos \theta_{0}}} \sin \theta_{0} d \theta_{0}=\left.\frac{1}{r r_{0}} \sqrt{r^{2}+r_{0}^{2}-2 r r_{0} \cos \theta_{0}}\right|_{0} ^{\pi}=\frac{1}{r r_{0}}\left[\left(r_{0}+r\right)-\left|r_{0}-r\right|\right]= \begin{cases}2 / r, & r_{0}<r \\ 2 / r_{0}, & r_{0}>r\end{cases} \).

Here r < a (from the \outer” integral), so

\( \int \frac{1}{\left| r – r _{0}\right|} V\left( r _{0}\right) d^{3} r _{0}=4 \pi V_{0}\left[\frac{1}{r} \int_{0}^{r} r_{0}^{2} d r_{0}+\int_{r}^{a} r_{0} d r_{0}\right]=4 \pi V_{0}\left[\frac{1}{r} \frac{r^{3}}{3}+\frac{1}{2}\left(a^{2}-r^{2}\right)\right]=2 \pi V_{0}\left(a^{2}-\frac{1}{3} r^{2}\right) \).

\( \int V( r )\left[\int \frac{1}{\left| r – r _{0}\right|} V\left( r _{0}\right) d^{3} r _{0}\right] d^{3} r =V_{0}\left(2 \pi V_{0}\right) 4 \pi \int_{0}^{a}\left(a^{2}-\frac{1}{3} r^{2}\right) r^{2} d r=8 \pi^{2} V_{0}^{2}\left[a^{2} \frac{a^{3}}{3}-\frac{1}{3} \frac{a^{5}}{5}\right]=\frac{32}{15} \pi^{2} V_{0}^{2} a^{5} \).

\( f(\theta)=-\frac{m}{2 \pi \hbar^{2}} V_{0} \frac{4}{3} \pi a^{3}+\left(\frac{m}{2 \pi \hbar^{2}}\right)^{2} \frac{32}{15} \pi^{2} V_{0}^{2} a^{5}=-\left(\frac{2 m V_{0} a^{3}}{3 \hbar^{2}}\right)\left[1-\frac{4}{5}\left(\frac{m V_{0} a^{2}}{\hbar^{2}}\right)\right] \).
