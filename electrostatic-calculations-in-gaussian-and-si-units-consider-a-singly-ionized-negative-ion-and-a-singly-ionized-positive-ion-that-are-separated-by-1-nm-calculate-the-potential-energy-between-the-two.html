Electrostatic calculations in Gaussian and SI units

Consider a singly ionized negative ion and a singly ionized positive ion that are separated by 1 nm. Calculate the potential energy between the two ions using SI units and CGS (Gaussian) units

Admin
Solution

In SI units, the charge of a singly ionized ion is approximately \(1.60 \times 10^{-19}[ C ]\). The permittivity of free space has the value, \(\varepsilon_{0}=8.85 \times 10^{-12}\left[ C ^{2} /( Jm )\right]\). Thus, Equation D.11

 

\(F_{12}=\frac{Q_{1} Q_{2}}{4 \pi \varepsilon_{0} r^{2}} \quad \text { SI units }\) (D.11)

 

\(\Gamma_{12}=\frac{Q_{1} Q_{2}}{4 \pi \varepsilon_{0} r}=\frac{\left(1.60 \times 10^{-19}[ C ]\right)\left(1.60 \times 10^{-19}[ C ]\right)}{(4 \pi)\left(8.85 \times 10^{-12}\left[ C ^{2} /( Jm )\right]\right)\left(10^{-9}[ m ]\right)}=-2.30 \times 10^{-19}[ J ]\) (ED.1)

 

The charge of a singly ionized ion in CGS units can be found from Table D.2:

 

\(\left(1.60 \times 10^{-19}[ C ]\right)\left(\frac{[ esu ]}{3.34 \times 10^{-10}[ C ]}\right)=4.80 \times 10^{-10}[ esu ]\)

 

Thus, Equation D.7 becomes:

 

\(F_{12}=\frac{Q_{1} Q_{2}}{r^{2}} \quad \text { CGS units }\) (D.7)

 

\(\Gamma_{12}=\frac{Q_{1} Q_{2}}{r}=\frac{\left(-4.80 \times 10^{-10}[ esu ]\right)\left(4.80 \times 10^{-10}[ esu ]\right)}{10^{-7}[ cm ]}=-2.30 \times 10^{-12}[ erg ]\) (ED.2)

 

Since \(1[ erg ]=1 g cm ^{2} / s ^{2}=1 \times 10^{-7}[ J ]\), Expressions ED.l and ED.2 are equivalent.
