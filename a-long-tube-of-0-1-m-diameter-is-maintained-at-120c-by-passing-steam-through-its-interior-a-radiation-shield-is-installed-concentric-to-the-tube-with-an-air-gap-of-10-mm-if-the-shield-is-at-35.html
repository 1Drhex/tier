Guest Question.

A long tube of 0.1-m diameter is maintained at 120°C by passing steam through its interior. A radiation shield is installed concentric to the tube with an air gap of 10 mm. If the shield is at 35°C, estimate the rate of heat transfer by free convection from the tube per unit length. What is the rate of heat loss if the space between the tube and the shield is filled with glass-fiber blanket insulation?

Admin
Solution

Known: Temperatures and diameters of a steam tube and a concentric radiation shield.

Find:

1. Rate of heat loss per unit length of tube.

2. Rate of heat loss if air space is filled with glass-fiber blanket insulation.

Schematic:

Assumptions:

1. Radiation heat transfer may be neglected.

2. Contact resistance with insulation is negligible.

3. Ideal gas.

4. Constant properties.

Properties: Table A.4, air \(\left[T=\left(T_{i}+T_{o}\right) / 2=350 K \right]: k=0.030 W / m \cdot K , v=20.92 \times10^{-6} m ^{2} / s , \alpha=29.9 \times 10^{-6} m ^{2} / s , \operatorname{Pr}=0.70, \beta=0.00285 K ^{-1}\). Table A.3, insulation, glass-fiber \((T \approx 300 K ): k=0.038 W / m \cdot K\).

Analysis:

1. From Equation 9.58, the rate of heat loss per unit length by free convection is

 

\(q=\frac{2 \pi L k_{ eff }\left(T_{i}-T_{o}\right)}{\ln \left(r_{o} / r_{i}\right)}\) (9.58)

 

\(q^{\prime}=\frac{2 \pi k_{\text {eff }}\left(T_{i}-T_{o}\right)}{\ln \left(r_{o} / r_{i}\right)}\)

 

 

where \(k_{\text {eff }}\) may be obtained from Equations 9.59 and 9.60. With

 

\(\frac{k_{\text {eff }}}{k}=0.386\left(\frac{P r}{0.861+P r}\right)^{1 / 4} R a_{c}^{1 / 4}\) (9.59)

 

\(L_{c}=\frac{2\left[\ln \left(r_{o} / r_{i}\right)\right]^{4 / 3}}{\left(r_{i}^{-3 / 5}+r_{o}^{-3 / 5}\right)^{5 / 3}}\) (9.60)

 

\(L_{c}=\frac{2\left[\ln \left(r_{o} / r_{i}\right)\right]^{4 / 3}}{\left(r_{i}^{-3 / 5}+r_{o}^{-3 / 5}\right)^{5 / 3}}=\frac{2[\ln (0.06 m / 0.05 m )]^{4 / 3}}{\left(0.05^{-3 / 5}+0.06^{-3 / 5}\right)^{5 / 3} m ^{-1}}=0.00117 m\)

 

we find

 

\(R a_{c}=\frac{g \beta\left(T_{i}-T_{o}\right) L_{c}^{3}}{v \alpha}=\frac{9.8 m / s ^{2} \times 0.00285 K ^{-1} \times(120-35)^{\circ} C \times(0.00117 m )^{3}}{20.92 \times 10^{-6} m ^{2} / s \times 29.9 \times 10^{-6} m ^{2} / s }\)

 

\(=171\)

 

The effective thermal conductivity is then

 

\(k_{\text {eff }}=0.386 k\left(\frac{P r}{0.861+P r}\right)^{1 / 4} R a_{c}^{1 / 4}\)

 

\(=0.386 \times 0.030 W / m \cdot K \left(\frac{0.70}{0.861+0.70}\right)^{1 / 4}(171)^{1 / 4}=0.0343 W / m \cdot K\)

 

and the rate of heat loss is

 

\(q^{\prime}=\frac{2 \pi k_{\text {eff }}\left(T_{i}-T_{o}\right)}{\ln \left(r_{o} / r_{i}\right)}=\frac{2 \pi(0.0343 W / m \cdot K )}{\ln (0.06 m / 0.05 m )}(120-35)^{\circ} C =100 W / m\)

 

2. With insulation in the space between the tube and the shield, heat loss is by conduction; comparing Equation 3.32 and Equation 9.58,

 

\(q_{r}=\frac{2 \pi L k\left(T_{s, 1}-T_{s, 2}\right)}{\ln \left(r_{2} / r_{1}\right)}\) (3.32)

 

\(q_{\text {ins }}^{\prime}=q^{\prime} \frac{k_{\text {ins }}}{k_{\text {eff }}}=100 W / m \frac{0.038 W / m \cdot K }{0.0343 W / m \cdot K }=111 W / m\)

 

Comments:

Although there is slightly more heat loss by conduction through the insulation than by free convection across the air space, the total heat loss across the air space may exceed that through the insulation because of the effects of radiation. The heat loss due to radiation may be minimized by using a radiation shield of low emissivity, and the means for calculating the loss will be developed in Chapter 13.
