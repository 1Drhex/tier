Guest Question.

In the design of weldments in torsion it is helpful to have a hierarchical perception of the relative efficiency of common patterns. For example, the weld-bead patterns shown in Table 9–1 can be ranked for desirability. Assume the space available is an a × a square. Use a formal figure of merit that is directly proportional to J and inversely proportional to the volume of weld metal laid down:

\(fom =\frac{J}{ vol }=\frac{0.707 h J_{u}}{\left(h^{2} / 2\right) l}=1.414 \frac{J_{u}}{h l}\)

A tactical figure of merit could omit the constant, that is, \(\text { fom }^{\prime}=J_{u} /(h l)\) . Rank the six patterns of Table 9–1 from most to least efficient.

Table 9–1
Torsional Properties of Fillet Welds*		 Weld Throat Area Location of G Unit Second Polar Moment of Area
A = 0.707 hd \(\begin{aligned}&\bar{x}=0 \\&\bar{y}=d / 2\end{aligned}\) \(J_{u}=d^{3} / 12\)
A = 1.414 hd \(\begin{aligned}&\bar{x}=b / 2 \\&\bar{y}=d / 2\end{aligned}\) \(J_{u}=\frac{d\left(3 b^{2}+d^{2}\right)}{6}\)
A = 0.707h(b+d) \(\begin{aligned}\bar{x} &=\frac{b^{2}}{2(b+d)} \\\bar{y} &=\frac{d^{2}}{2(b+d)}\end{aligned}\) \(J_{u}=\frac{(b+d)^{4}-6 b^{2} d^{2}}{12(b+d)}\)
A = 0.707h(2b+d) \(\begin{aligned}\bar{x} &=\frac{b^{2}}{2 b+d} \\\bar{y} &=d / 2\end{aligned}\) \(J_{u}=\frac{8 b^{3}+6 b d^{2}+d^{3}}{12}-\frac{b^{4}}{2 b+d}\)
A = 1.414h(b+d) \(\begin{aligned}&\bar{x}=b / 2 \\&\bar{y}=d / 2\end{aligned}\) \(J_{u}=\frac{(b+d)^{3}}{6}\)
A = 1.414 πhr \(J_{u}=2 \pi r^{3}\)
*G is centroid of weld group; h is weld size; plane of torque couple is in the plane of the paper; all welds are of unit width.
