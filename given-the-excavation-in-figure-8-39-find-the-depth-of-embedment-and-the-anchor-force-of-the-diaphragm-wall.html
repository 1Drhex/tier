Guest Question.

Given the excavation in Figure 8.39, find the depth of embedment and the anchor force of the diaphragm wall.

Admin
Solution
  1. The active earth pressure \(K_{a}\), by making use of Coulomb critical wedge solution and assuming a wall friction \( \delta =2\varphi´/3\approxeq 22°\), is equal to 0.254. The horizontal component of the active pressure is then given by \( \acute{\sigma_{h}}=K_{a}\acute{\sigma_{vo}}\cos \delta =0.24\acute{\sigma_{vo}}\). The passive earth resistance coefficient KPH, based on the lower bound solution (equation 8.27): \(K_{p}=\left[\frac{\cos \delta }{1-\sin \varphi´ }\left(\cos \delta +\sqrt{\sin^2\varphi´-\sin^2\delta }\right) \right] e^{2\vartheta \tan\varphi´}\) , is equal to 5.66, assuming \( {\delta }/{\varphi´}=0.5\). Note that in this case the solution already provides the horizontal component.
  1. In order to compute the depth of embedment, reference is made to the free earth support method, so that the required depth is obtained by considering the equilibrium of rotation around the anchor point: \( \frac{1}{2}=\frac{K_{PH}}{F}\gamma d^2\cdot\left(\frac{2}{3}d+h\right)-\frac{1}{2}\gamma K_{AH}(a+h+d)^2\cdot\left[\frac{2}{3}(a+h+d)-a\right]=0\). If the passive resistance is factored by a factor of safety equal to 2, the above equation gives d = 2.50 m.
  1. The equilibrium of translation gives \( T_{h}=P_{a}-P_{p}=251.37-168.31=83.06KN/m\). If an inclined anchor (10° from the horizontal) is used, the required force is 84.34 kN/m and a load factor of 1.25 must be applied when designing the anchor. It must also be considered that, in order for the anchor be fully effective, its foundation must be behind the critical active wedge, as shown in Figure 8.39c. Where an anchor plate should be used, it must be checked that critical active and passive wedges do not intersect (see Figure 8.39d).
  1. Note that we did not consider in this illustrative Example unplanned events, such as surcharges on the retained side and unexpected additional excavation, as discussed in Section 8.9.
