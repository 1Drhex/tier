Guest Question.

A shell and tube oil to water heat exchanger has tubes of internal diameter 12mm and length 2m in a single shell. Cold water (\(C_{p c}=4180 J / kg ^{\circ} C\)) enters the tubes at \(33^{\circ} C\) with a flow rate of 5 kg/s and leaves at \(55^{\circ} C\). Oil (\(C_{p h}=2150 J / kg ^{\circ} C\) ) flows through the shell and is cooled from \(120^{\circ} C\) to \(75^{\circ} C\). The overall heat transfer coefficient is \(U_{i}=885 W / m ^{2}{ }^{\circ} C\) based on the internal area of the tubes. Determine the number of tubes required in this heat exchanger.

Admin
Solution

All temperatures are specified in this problem. Hence, the LMT D-correction factor approach is used to solve the problem. The given data is listed first:

Hot fluid inlet temperature: \(T_{h, i}=125^{\circ} C\)

Hot fluid outlet temperature: \(T_{h, o}=75^{\circ} C\)

Specific heat of the hot fluid: \(C_{p c}=2150 J / kg ^{\circ} C\)

Cold fluid inlet temperature: \(T_{c, i}=33^{\circ} C\)

Cold fluid outlet temperature: \(T_{c, o}=55^{\circ} C\)

Specific heat of the cold fluid: \(C_{p c}=4180 J / kg ^{\circ} C\)

Mass flow rate of cold fluid: \(\dot{m}_{c}=5 kg / s\)

Total heat exchange: \(Q=190 kW =190,000 W\)

Overall heat transfer coefficient: \(\bar{U}_{i}=885 W / m ^{2}{ }^{\circ} C\)

Geometric data:

\(D_{p i}=12 mm =0.012 m\), L = 2m

The total heat transferred in the heat exchanger is calculated as

\(Q=\dot{m}_{c} C_{p c}\left(T_{c, o}-T_{c, i}\right)=5 \times 4180(55-33)=4.598 \times 10^{5} W\)

The terminal temperature differences assuming counter current operation are

\(\Delta T_{0}=T_{h, i}-T_{c, o}=120-55=65^{\circ} C\)

 

\(\Delta T_{L}=T_{h, o}-T_{c, i}=75-33=42{ }^{\circ} C\)

The LMTD based on counter-current assumption is

\(L M T D=\frac{65-42}{\ln \left(\frac{65}{42}\right)}=52.67^{\circ} C\)

The parameters \(P_{1}\) and \(P_{2}\) are now calculated:

\(P_{1}=\frac{55-33}{120-33}=0.253\) , \(P_{2}=\frac{120-75}{55-33}=2.045\)

From Fig. 15.14, the correction factor is read off as F = 0.94. Based on the total heat transferred, the area required may be calculated as

\(S=\frac{Q}{U_{i} \times L M T D \times F}=\frac{4.598 \times 10^{5}}{885 \times 52.67 \times 0.94}=10.494 m ^{2}\)

Heat transfer area per tube is obtained as (based on tube ID)

\(A_{p i}=\pi D_{p i} L=\pi \times 0.12 \times 2=0.0754 m ^{2}\)

The number of tubes N required is then calculated as

\(N=\frac{\text { Total area required }}{\text { Area per tube }}=\frac{10.494}{0.0754}=139.2\)

The number may be rounded to nearest whole number N = 140.

It is also possible to use the \(N T U-\epsilon\) method for the analysis of shell and tube heat exchangers. For details the reader should consult specialized books on heat exchangers.
