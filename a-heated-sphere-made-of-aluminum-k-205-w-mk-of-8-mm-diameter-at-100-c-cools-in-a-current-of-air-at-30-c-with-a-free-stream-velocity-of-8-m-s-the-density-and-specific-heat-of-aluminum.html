Guest Question.

A heated sphere made of aluminum (k = 205 W/mK) of 8 mm diameter at 100 \(^\circ C\) cools in a current of air at 30 \(^\circ C\) , with a free stream velocity of 8 m/s. The density and specific heat of aluminum are 2700 kg/\(m^3\) and 900 J /kgK, respectively.
a. Determine the heat transfer coefficient from the sphere.
b. Determine the time constant of the sphere.
c. How much time will it take for the sphere to cool to 50 \(^\circ C\).

Neglect radiation from the sphere.

Admin
Solution

a. The Reynolds number \(Re_{_D}\) , is given by

\(Re_{_D}=\frac{U_{_\infty }D}{v}=\frac{8\times 8\times 10^{-3}}{19.51\times 10^{-6}} =3280 \)

\(Re_{_D}\) , is the same as in part (a) of.

\(\overline{Nu_{_D}} =2+[0.4(3280)^{0.5}+0.06(3280)^{0.667}](0.707)^{0.4}\left\lgroup\frac{1.857\times 10^{-5}}{2.025\times 10^{-5}} \right\rgroup^{0.25} \)

 

\(\overline{Nu_{_D}} =2+[22.91+13.28]\times 0.87\times 0.979=32.83\)

 

\(h=\frac{\overline{Nu_{_D}}k}{D}=\frac{32.83\times 0.0289}{8\times 10^{-3}}=118.58W/m^2K \)

b. Time constant

\(\tau =\frac{mc_p}{hA} \)

 

\(\tau =\frac{\rho Vc_p}{hA}=\frac{2700\times \frac{4}{3}\pi (4\times 10^{-3})^3\times 900 }{118.58\times 4\pi (4\times 10^{-3})^2} \)

 

\(\tau =27.33s\)

c. Let us now calculate the Biot number.

\(Bi=\frac{hr}{3k}=\frac{118.58\times 4\times 10^{-3}}{3\times 205} \)

 

\(Bi=0.00077\)

Since Bi < 0.1, the lumped capacitance method is valid. Recalling the solution for a spatially lumped object undergoing cooling

\(\therefore \frac{(T-T_\infty )}{(T_i-T_\infty )} =e^{-t/T}\)

 

\(\frac{(50-30)}{(100-30)}=e^{-t/27.33} \)

 

\(0.2857=e^{-t/27.33} \)

 

\(\therefore t=34.24s\)
