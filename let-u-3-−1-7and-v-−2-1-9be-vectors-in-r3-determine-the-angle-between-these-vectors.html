Guest Question.

Let \(\mathbf{u}=\left(\begin{array}{r}3 \\ -1 \\ 7\end{array}\right)\) and \(\mathbf{v}=\left(\begin{array}{r}-2 \\ 1 \\ 9\end{array}\right)\) be vectors in \(\mathbb{R}^{3}\). Determine the angle between these vectors.

Admin
Solution

The procedure is very similar to . We need to use the above formula ( \(2.13\) ):

\(\cos (\theta)=\frac{\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v}}{\|\mathbf{u}\|\|\mathbf{v}\|}\)

What are the dot product \(\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v}\) and the norms \(\|\mathbf{u}\|\) and \(\|\mathbf{v}\|\) equal to?

Applying dot product formula ( \(2.4)\) we have

\(\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v}=\left(\begin{array}{r}3 \\-1 \\7\end{array}\right) \cdot\left(\begin{array}{r}-2 \\1 \\9\end{array}\right)=(3 \times(-2))+(-1 \times 1)+(7 \times 9)=56\)

To evaluate the lengths we use Pythagoras’ theorem ( \(2.7\) ):

\(\|\mathbf{u}\|=\sqrt{3^{2}+(-1)^{2}+7^{2}}=\sqrt{59} \text { and }\|\mathbf{v}\|=\sqrt{(-2)^{2}+1^{2}+9^{2}}=\sqrt{86}\)

Substituting \(\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v}=56,\|\mathbf{u}\|=\sqrt{59}\) and \(\|\mathbf{v}\|=\sqrt{86}\) into \(\cos (\theta)=\frac{\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v}}{\|\mathbf{u}\|\|\mathbf{v}\|}\) gives

\(\cos (\theta)=\frac{56}{\sqrt{59} \sqrt{86}}=\frac{56}{\sqrt{59 \times 86}}=0.786\)

How do we find the angle \(\theta\) ?

\(\left.\theta=\cos ^{-1}(0.786)=38.19^{\circ} \quad \text { [inverse cos }\right]\)

(2.4): \(\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v}=\mathbf{u}^{T} \mathbf{v}=u_{1} v_{1}+u_{2} v_{2}+u_{3} v_{3}+\cdots+u_{n} v_{n}\)

Pythagoras’ Theorem (2.7). Let \(\mathbf{u}\) be a vector in \(\mathbb{R}^{n}\) then the length of \(\mathbf{u}=\left(\begin{array}{c}u_{1} \\ \vdots \\ u_{n}\end{array}\right)\) is given by:

\(\|\mathbf{u}\|=\sqrt{\left(u_{1}\right)^{2}+\left(u_{2}\right)^{2}+\left(u_{3}\right)^{2}+\cdots+\left(u_{n}\right)^{2}}\)

The norm of a vector \(\mathbf{u}\) is a real number which gives the length of the vector \(\mathbf{u}\).
