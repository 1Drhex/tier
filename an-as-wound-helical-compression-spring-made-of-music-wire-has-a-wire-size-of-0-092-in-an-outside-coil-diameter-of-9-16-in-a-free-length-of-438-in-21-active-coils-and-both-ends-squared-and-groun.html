Guest Question.

An as-wound helical compression spring, made of music wire, has a wire size of 0.092 in, an outside coil diameter of \(\frac{9}{16}\) in, a free length of \(4\frac{3}{8} \) in, 21 active coils, and both ends squared and ground. The spring is unpeened. This spring is to be assembled with a preload of 5 lbf and will operate with a maximum load of 35 lbf during use.
(a) Estimate the factor of safety guarding against fatigue failure using a torsional Gerber fatigue failure criterion with Zimmerli data.
(b) Repeat part (a) using the Sines torsional fatigue criterion (steady stress component has no effect), with Zimmerli data.
(c) Repeat using a torsional Goodman failure criterion with Zimmerli data.
(d) Estimate the critical frequency of the spring.

Admin
Solution

The mean coil diameter is \(D = 0.5625 − 0.092 = 0.4705\) in. The spring index is \(C = {D}/{d} = {0.4705}/{0.092} = 5.11\). Then
\(K_B = \frac{4C + 2}{4C − 3}= \frac{4\left(5.11\right) + 2}{4\left(5.11\right)− 3}= 1.287\)
From Eqs. (10–31),

\(F_a=\frac{F_{max}-F_{min}}{2} \\ F_m=\frac{F_{max}+F_{min}}{2}\)

\(F_a = \frac{35 − 5}{2}= 15 \ lbf \\ F_m =\frac{35 + 5}{2}= 20 \ lbf\)
The alternating shear-stress component is found from Eq. (10–32)
\( \tau_a = K_B \frac{8F_a D}{\pi d^3}\)
to be
\( \tau_a = K_B \frac{8F_m D}{\pi d^3}=\left(1.287\right) \frac{8 \left(15\right)0.4705}{\pi \left(0.092\right)^3} \left(10^{−3}\right) = 29.7 \ kpsi\)
Equation (10–33)
\( \tau_m = K_B \frac{8 F_m D}{\pi d^3}\)
gives the midrange shear-stress component
\( \tau_m = K_B \frac{8 F_m D}{\pi d^3}= 1.287 \frac{8\left(20\right)0.4705}{\pi \left(0.092\right)^3} \left(10^{−3}\right) = 39.6 \ kpsi\)

we find \(A = 201 \ kpsi · in^m \ and \ m = 0.145\). The ultimate tensile strength is estimated from Eq. (10–14)
\(S_{ut} = \frac{A}{d_m}\)
as
\(S_{ut} = \frac{A}{d_m}= \frac{201}{0.092^{0.145}}= 284.1 \ kpsi\)
Also the shearing ultimate strength is estimated from
\(S_{su} = 0.67 S_{ut} = 0.67\left(284.1\right) = 190.3 \ kpsi\)
The load-line slope \(r = {\tau_a}/{\tau_m} = {29.7}/{39.6} = 0.75\).
(a) The Gerber ordinate intercept for the Zimmerli data, Eq. (10–28),
\(S_{sa} = 35 \ kpsi (241 MPa)\\ S_{sm} = 55 \ kpsi (379 MPa)\)
is
\(S_{se} = \frac{S_{sa}}{1-\left({S_{sm}}/{S_su}\right) } =\frac{35}{1-\left({55}/{190.3}\right)^2 } =38.2\) kpsi

(b) The Sines failure criterion ignores Ssm so that, for the Zimmerli data with \( S_{sa} =35\) kpsi,

\( n_f =\frac{S_{sa}}{\tau_a}=\frac{35}{29.7}=1.18\)

\( S_{se}=\frac{S_{se}}{1-\left({S_{sm}}/{S_{su}}\right) }=\frac{35}{1-\left({55}/{190.3}\right) } =49.2\)

we estimate the spring rate as

\(k= \frac{d^4G}{8D^3N_a}=\frac{0.092^4 \left[11.75\left(10^6\right) \right] }{8\left(0.4705\right)^3 21 }=48.1 \ lbf/in \)

From Eq. (10–27)

\( W=AL\gamma=\frac{\pi d^2}{4} \left(\pi D N_a\right) \left(\gamma\right)=\frac{\pi^2d^2DN_a \gamma}{4} \)

we estimate the spring weight as

\(W=\frac{\pi^2 \left(0.092^2\right) 0.4705 \left(21\right) 0.248}{4}=0.0586\) lbf

and from Eq. (10–25)

\( f=\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\frac{kg}{W}}\)

the frequency of the fundamental wave is
\(f_n =\frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{48.1\left(386\right)}{0.0586}\right]^{{1}/{2}}= 281\) Hz
If the operating or exciting frequency is more than \({281}/{20} = 14.1\) Hz, the spring may have to be redesigned.
