Guest Question.

Find an upper bound on the energy of the first excited state of the hydrogen atom. A trial function with \( \ell=1 \) will automatically be orthogonal to the ground state (see footnote 6); for the radial part of ψ you can use the same function as in .

Admin
Solution

Adopting the variational wave function

\( \psi(r, \theta, \phi)=A e^{-b r^{2}} Y_{1}^{0}(\theta, \phi)=\sqrt{\frac{3}{4 \pi}} A e^{-b r^{2}} \cos \theta \).

\( 1=|A|^{2} \int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-2 b r^{2}} r^{2} d r \int\left|Y_{1}^{0}\right|^{2} d \Omega=|A|^{2}\left(\frac{1}{8 b} \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{2 b}}\right)(1) \Rightarrow A=2\left(\frac{8 b^{3}}{\pi}\right)^{1 / 4} \).

\( \langle V\rangle=-\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}|A|^{2} \int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-2 b r^{2}} \frac{1}{r} r^{2} d r=-\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\left(4 \sqrt{\frac{8 b^{3}}{\pi}}\right) \frac{1}{4 b}=-\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \sqrt{\frac{8 b}{\pi}} \).

\( \nabla^{2} \psi=\left\{\frac{1}{r^{2}} \frac{\partial}{\partial r}\left(r^{2} \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial r}\right)+\frac{1}{r^{2} \sin \theta} \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta}\left(\sin \theta \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial \theta}\right)\right\} \).

\( =\sqrt{\frac{3}{4 \pi}} A\left\{\frac{\cos \theta}{r^{2}} \frac{d}{d r}\left[r^{2}(-2 b r) e^{-b r^{2}}\right]+\frac{e^{-b r^{2}}}{r^{2} \sin \theta} \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta}\left(-\sin ^{2} \theta\right)\right\} \).

\( =\sqrt{\frac{3}{4 \pi}} A\left\{-\frac{2 b \cos \theta}{r^{2}}\left(3 r^{2} e^{-b r^{2}}-2 b r^{4} e^{-b r^{2}}\right)-\frac{e^{-b r^{2}}}{r^{2} \sin \theta}(2 \sin \theta \cos \theta)\right\} \).

\( =\sqrt{\frac{3}{\pi}} A e^{-b r^{2}} \frac{\cos \theta}{r^{2}}\left(2 b^{2} r^{4}-3 b r^{2}-1\right) \).

\( \langle T\rangle=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \int \psi^{*} \nabla^{2} \psi d^{3} r =-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \sqrt{\frac{3}{\pi}} \sqrt{\frac{3}{4 \pi}}|A|^{2} \int e^{-b r^{2}} \cos \theta e^{-b r^{2}} \frac{\cos \theta}{r^{2}}\left(2 b^{2} r^{4}-3 b r^{2}-1\right) r^{2} \sin \theta d r d \theta d \phi \).

\( =-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \frac{3}{2 \pi}|A|^{2} 2 \pi \int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-2 b r^{2}}\left(2 b^{2} r^{4}-3 b r^{2}-1\right) d r \int_{0}^{\pi} \cos ^{2} \theta \sin \theta d \theta \).

\( =-\frac{3 \hbar^{2}}{2 m}|A|^{2}\left\{2 b^{2}\left(12 \sqrt{\pi} \frac{1}{(2 \sqrt{2 b})^{5}}\right)-3 b\left(2 \sqrt{\pi} \frac{1}{(2 \sqrt{2 b})^{3}}\right)-\left(\sqrt{\pi} \frac{1}{(2 \sqrt{2 b})}\right)\right\}\left(\frac{2}{3}\right) \).

\( =-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{m}|A|^{2} \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2 \sqrt{2 b}}\left[\frac{24 b^{2}}{(2 \sqrt{2 b})^{4}}-\frac{6 b}{(2 \sqrt{2 b})^{2}}-1\right]=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{m}|A|^{2} \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{2 b}} \frac{11}{16}=\frac{11 b \hbar^{2}}{2 m} \).

\( \langle H\rangle=\frac{11 b \hbar^{2}}{2 m}-\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \sqrt{\frac{8 b}{\pi}} ; \quad \frac{\partial\langle H\rangle}{\partial b}=\frac{11 \hbar^{2}}{2 m}-\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi b}}=0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \sqrt{b}=\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi}} \frac{2 m}{11 \hbar^{2}} \).

\( \langle H\rangle_{\min }=\frac{11 \hbar^{2}}{2 m} \frac{e^{4}}{16 \pi^{2} \epsilon_{0}^{2}} \frac{2}{\pi} \frac{4 m^{2}}{121 \hbar^{4}}-\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \sqrt{\frac{8}{\pi}} \frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi}} \frac{2 m}{11 \hbar^{2}}=-\left(\frac{4}{11 \pi}\right) \frac{m e^{4}}{\hbar^{2}\left(4 \pi \epsilon_{0}\right)^{2}} \).

\( =\frac{32}{11 \pi} E_{2}=0.926 E_{2} \).

Since \( E_2\) is negative this is indeed an upper bound, within about 7% of the exact answer.

 

(6) You can extend this trick to other symmetries. Suppose there is a Hermitian operator A such that [A,H]=0. The ground state (assuming it is nondegenerate) must be an eigenstate of A; call the eigenvalue \( \lambda: A \psi_{g s}=\lambda \psi_{g s} \). If you choose a variational function ψ that is an eigenstate of A with a different eigenvalue: \( A \psi=v \psi \text { with } \lambda \neq v, \text { you can be certain that } \psi \text { and } \psi_{g} \) are orthogonal (see Section 3.3). For an application see Problem 8.20.
