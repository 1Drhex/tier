Guest Question.

In the circuit of Fig. 5.27, if \(I(0^{-})\) = 2 A, \(V_{C1}(0^{-})\) = 3 V, and \(V_{C2}(0^{-})\) = 1 V, calculate the value of \(\frac{dI(0^{+})}{dt}\).

Admin
Solution

6.27. First, we should note that the circuit is not in the steady state condition for t = \(0^{-}.\) Now, by using the current division formula in node “B” in the circuit of Fig. 6.27.2, we can write:

\(I(0^{-})=\frac{1}{1+2}×I_{L}(0^{-})\) …….(1)

Based on the given information:

\(I(0^{-}) =\) 2 A …….(2)

Solving (1) and (2):

\(2=\frac{1}{1+2}×I_{L}(0^{-}) \Rightarrow I_{L}(0^{-})=6\) A …….(3)

Based on the given information:

\(V_{C1}(0^{-})=\) 3 V …….(4)

\(V_{C2}(0^{-}) =\) 1 V ……..(5)

Due to the voltage continuity of the capacitor in lack of a power source with an infinite size (e.g., impulse power source):

\(V_{C1}(0^{+})=V_{C1}(0^{-})=\) 3 V …….(6)

\(V_{C2}(0^{+})= V_{C2}(0^{-}) =\) 1 V ……..(7)

Due to the current continuity of the inductor in lack of a power source with an infinite size (e.g., impulse power source):

\(I_{L}(0^{+})= I_{L}(0^{-})=\) 6 A …….(8)

Figure 6.27.3 shows the circuit for t = \(0^{+}\). As can be seen, the capacitors have been modeled by the voltage sources with the sizes of 3 V and 1 V. Additionally, the inductor has been modeled by a current source of 6 A.

Applying KCL in node “A”:

\(\frac{V_{A}(0^{+})-(-3) }{2}+\frac{V_{A}(0^{+})-1 }{6}+6=0\Rightarrow \frac{3V_{A}(0^{+}) +9 +V_{A}(0^{+}) -1+36}{6}=0\)

\(\Rightarrow 4V_{A}(0^{+})+44=0\Rightarrow V_{A}(0^{+})=-11V\) …….(9)

Using the current division formula in node “B”:

\(I(0^{+})=\frac{1}{1+2}×I_{L}(0^{+})\)

Using (8)\(\Rightarrow I(0^{+})=\frac{1}{3}×6\) = 2 A …….(10)

Using Ohm’s law for the right-side 2 Ω resistor:

\(V_{B}(0^{+})=2I(0^{+})=\) 2×2 = 4 V …….(11)

Defining the voltage of the inductor based on the node voltages:

\(V_{L}(0^{+})=V_{A}(0^{+})-V_{B}(0^{+})\)

Using (9),(11)\(\Rightarrow V_{L}(0^{+})=\) -11-4 = -15 V ………(12)

Using the current-voltage relation of the inductor for t =\(0^{+}\) :

\(V_{L}(0^{+})=L\frac{d}{dt}I_{L}(0^{+})=4\frac{d}{dt}I_{L}(0^{+})\Rightarrow \frac{d}{dt}I_{L}(0^{+})\frac{V_{L}(0^{+})}{4}\)

Usnig (12)\(\Rightarrow \frac{d}{dt}I_{L}(0^{+})=\frac{-15}{4}\) …….(13)

Using the current division formula in node “B”:

\(I(t)=\frac{1}{1+2}×I_{L}(t)\) \(\frac{d}{dt}\Rightarrow \frac{d}{dt}I(t)=\frac{1}{3}×\frac{d}{dt}I_{L}(t)\) \(for t=0^{+}\Rightarrow \frac{d}{dt}I(0^{+})=\frac{1}{3}×\frac{d}{dt}I_{L}(0^{+})\)

\(Using (13)\Rightarrow \frac{d}{dt}I(0^{+})=\frac{1}{3}×(\frac{-15}{4})=\frac{-5}{4} \) A/sec
