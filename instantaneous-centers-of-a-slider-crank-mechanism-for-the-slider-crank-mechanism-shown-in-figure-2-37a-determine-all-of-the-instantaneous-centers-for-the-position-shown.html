INSTANTANEOUS CENTERS OF A SLIDER CRANK MECHANISM

For the slider crank mechanism shown in Figure 2.37(a), determine all of the instantaneous centers for the position shown.

 

Admin
Solution

The mechanism has four links (n = 4) and, using Equation (2.10-4)\([p=\frac{n(n-1)}{n}]\), six instantaneous centers (p = 6). They are: \(P_{1,2}, P_{1,3}, P_{1,4}, P_{2,3}, P_{2,4}\), and \(P_{3,4}\).
The following instantaneous centers are located by examining the kinematic pairs (Table 2.1): \(P_{1,2}, P_{2,3}, P_{3,4}\), and \(P_{1,4}\).
To locate \(P_{1,3}\), we employ the auxiliary polygon. As shown in Figure 2.37(c), the diagonal between vertex 1 and vertex 3 is part of triangles 1-2-3 and 1-3-4. This indicates that \(P_{1,3}\) lies on the same straight lines as \(P_{1,2} P_{2,3}\) and \(P_{1,4} P_{3,4}\). It must therefore lie at the intersection of these two lines. Instantaneous center \(P_{2,4}\) is found in a similar manner. All six instantaneous centers are shown in Figure 2.37(b).

 

Type of Kinematic Pair Location of Instantaneous Center
Turning pair
The instantaneous center coincides
with the turning pair.
Sliding pair (curved slide)
The slider is in curvilinear motion
with respect to the slide. The
instantaneous center is at the center of curvature of the slide.
Sliding pair (curved slide)
The slider is in rectilinear motion with respect to the slide. A straight slide is equivalent to a curved slide of infinite radius. Thus, the instantaneous center is at an infinite distance in a direction perpendicular to the sliding motion.
Rolling pair
The instantaneous center is at the
point of contact, this being the only
point where the two links have the
same velocity.
Two degree of freedom pair

(P\(_{i,j}\) on common normal)
Relative motion takes place along the
common tangent between the two links at the point of contact. Consequently, the instantaneous center lies on the common normal. Its position on the common normal depends on the ratio of the sliding and angular velocities.
