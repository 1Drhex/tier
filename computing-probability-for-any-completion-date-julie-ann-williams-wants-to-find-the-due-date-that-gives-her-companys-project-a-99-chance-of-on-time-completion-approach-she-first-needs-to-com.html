COMPUTING PROBABILITY FOR ANY COMPLETION DATE. Julie Ann Williams wants to find the due date that gives her company’s project a 99% chance of on-time completion.
APPROACH \(\blacktriangleright\) She first needs to compute the Z-value corresponding to 99%, as shown in Figure 3.14. Mathematically, this is similar to , except the unknown is now the due date rather than Z.

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION \(\blacktriangleright\) Referring again to the Normal Table in Appendix I (alternatively using the Excel formula =NORMSINV(0.99)), we identify a Z-value of 2.33 as being closest to the probability of 0.99. That is, Julie Ann Williams’s due date should be 2.33 standard deviations above the mean project completion time. Starting with the standard normal equation [see Equation (3-9)], we can solve for the due date and rewrite the equation as:

Z = (Due date – Expected date of completion)/\(\sigma_p\)
= (16 weeks – 15 weeks)/1.76 weeks = 0.57 (3-9)

Due date = Expected completion time + (Z × \(\sigma_p\))
= 15 + (2.33 × 1.76) = 19.1 weeks (3-10)
