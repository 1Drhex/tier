Guest Question.

Referring to the single-phase two-wire lossless line shown in Figure 13.3, the receiving end is terminated by an inductor with 2LR henries. The source voltage at the sending end is a step, \(e_{ G }(t)= E u_{-1}(t) \text { with } Z _{ G }=Z_{c}\). Both the line and inductor are initially unenergized. Determine and plot the voltage at the center of the line Î½(l/2, t) versus time t.

 

 

Admin
Solution

From Eq (13.2.12) with \(Z_{R}=S\left(2 L_{R}\right) \text { and } Z_{G}=Z_{c}:\)

\(\Gamma_{R}(S)=\frac{\frac{S\left(2 L_{R}\right)}{Z_{c}}-1}{\frac{S\left(2 L_{R}\right)}{Z_{c}}+1}=\frac{S-\frac{Z_{c}}{2 L_{R}}}{S+\frac{Z_{c}}{2 L_{R}}} \quad \Gamma_{S}(S)=0\)

Then from Eq (13.2.10) with \(E_{G}(S)=\frac{E}{S}\)

\(V(x, S)=\frac{E}{S}\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)\left[e^{\frac{-S x}{v}}+\left(\frac{S-\frac{Z_{c}}{2 L_{R}}}{S+\frac{Z_{c}}{2 L_{R}}}\right) e^{S\left(\frac{x}{v}-2 \tau\right)}\right]\)

Using partial-fraction expansion

\(V(x, S)=\frac{E}{2}\left[\frac{e^{-\frac{S x}{v}}}{S}+\left(\frac{-1}{S}+\frac{2}{S+\frac{Z e}{2 L_{R}}}\right) e^{S\left(\frac{x}{v}-2 \tau\right)}\right]\)

Taking the inverse Laplace transform:

\(V(x, t)=\frac{E}{2} U_{-1}\left(t-\frac{x}{v}\right)+\frac{E}{2}\left[-1+2 e^{\frac{-1}{L_{R} \mid Z_{c}}\left(t+\frac{x}{v}-2 \tau\right)}\right] U_{-1}\left(t+\frac{x}{v}-2 \tau\right)\)

At the center of the line, where \(x=l / 2:\)

\(V\left(\frac{l}{2}, t\right)=\frac{E}{2} U_{-1}\left(t-\frac{\tau}{2}\right)+\frac{E}{2}\left[-1+2 e^{-\left(\frac{t-\frac{3}{2} \tau}{2 L_{R} \mid Z_{c}}\right)}\right] U_{-1}\left(t-\frac{3 \tau}{2}\right)\)

Eq (13.2.12) :

\(\Gamma_{ R }(s)=\frac{\frac{Z_{ R }(s)}{Z_{c}}-1}{\frac{Z_{ R }(s)}{Z_{c}}+1} \text { per unit }\)

 

\(\Gamma_{ S }(s)=\frac{\frac{Z_{ G }(s)}{Z_{c}}-1}{\frac{Z_{ G }(s)}{Z_{c}}+1} \text { per unit }\)

 

Eq (13.2.10) : \(V (x, s)= E _{ G }(s)\left[\frac{ Z _{c}}{ Z _{ G }(s)+Z_{c}}\right]\left[\frac{e^{-s x / v}+\Gamma_{ R }(s) e^{ s (x / v)-2 \tau]}}{1-\Gamma_{ R }(s) \Gamma_{ S }(s) e^{-2 s \tau}}\right]\)
