Guest Question.

A steel bar is attached to a wood support beam with four 22-mm-diameter lag screws as shown in Figure P4.4. The steel bar is 70-mm-wide by 6-mm-thick. For the steel bar, the yield strength is 250 MPa, and the ultimate bearing strength is 350 MPa. The ultimate shear strength of the lag screws is 165 MPa. Factors of safety of 1.67 with respect to yield strength and 3.0 with respect to bearing strength are required for the bar. A factor of safety of 3.0 with respect to the ultimate shear strength is required for the lag screws. Determine the allowable load P that can be supported by this connection. (Note: Consider only the gross cross-sectional area of the barâ€”not the net area.)

Admin
Solution

P based on normal stress in bar: The allowable normal stress is

\(\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\sigma_{Y}}{ FS }=\frac{250 MPa }{1.67}=149.7006 MPa\)

The full strength of the plate (based on the gross cross-sectional area) is therefore:

\(P_{\text {allow }}=\sigma_{\text {allow }} A=(149.7006 MPa )(70 mm )(6 mm )=62,874 N\)

P based on shear stress in lag screws: The allowable shear stress of the lag screws is

\(\tau_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\tau_{U}}{ FS }=\frac{165 MPa }{3.0}=55.0 MPa\)

Each lag screw has a cross-sectional area of

\(A_{\text {screw }}=\frac{\pi}{4} d_{\text {screw }}^{2}=\frac{\pi}{4}(22 mm )^{2}=380.1327 mm ^{2}\)

Based on the allowable shear stress, one lag screw can support

\(V_{\text {screw }}=\left(55 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(380.1327 mm ^{2}\right)=20,907 N\)

Consequently, four lag screws can support an allowable load of

\(P_{\text {allow }}=(4 \text { screws })(20,907 N / screw )=83,629 N\)

P based on bearing stress in bar: The allowable bearing stress is

\(\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\sigma_{Y}}{ FS }=\frac{350 MPa }{3.0}=116.6667 MPa\)

Use the projected area of a 22-mm-diameter lag screw bearing on a 6-mm-thick bar to calculate the allowable bearing force for one lag screw:

\(F_{b}=\left(116.6667 N / mm ^{2}\right)(22 mm )(6 mm )=15,400 N\)

Accordingly, four lag screws can support

\(P_{\text {allow }}=4 F_{b}=4(15,400 N )=61,600 N\)

Allowable load P: Based on normal and bearing stress in the bar and shear stress in the lag screws, the strength of this connection is

\(P_{\text {allow }}=61,600 N =61.6 kN\)
