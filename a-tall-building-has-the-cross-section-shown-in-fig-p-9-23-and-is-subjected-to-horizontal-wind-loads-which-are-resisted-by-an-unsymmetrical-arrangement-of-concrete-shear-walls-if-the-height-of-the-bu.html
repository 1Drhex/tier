Guest Question.

A tall building has the cross section shown in Fig. P.9.23 and is subjected to horizontal wind loads which are resisted by an unsymmetrical arrangement of concrete shear walls. If the height of the building is 60 m determine the maximum direct stress in the cross section produced by a uniform wind pressure of 750 N/mÂ² acting as shown. Specify the point at which the maximum direct stress acts and assume, for the purposes of calculation, that the shear walls, all of thickness 200 mm, are thin.

Admin
Solution

Referring to Fig. S.9.23 and taking moments of areas about AH

\(\begin{aligned}200(2+4+8+4+3) \times 10^{3} \bar{y}=&(2 \times 200 \times 1+4 \times 200 \times 6+8 \times 200 \times 4+4 \times 200 \times 8\\&+3 \times 200 \times 1.5) \times 10^{6}\end{aligned}\)

which gives

\(\bar{y}=4.5 \times 10^{3} mm\)

Now taking moments of areas about EF

\(\begin{aligned}200(2+4+8+4+3) \times 10^{3} \bar{z}=&(-2 \times 200 \times 8-4 \times 200 \times 6+3 \times 200\\&\times 8+4 \times 200 \times 6) \times 10^{6}\end{aligned}\)

from which

\( \bar{z}=0.4 \times 10^{3} mm\)

Then

\(\begin{aligned}I_{z}=& \frac{200 \times\left(2 \times 10^{3}\right)^{3}}{12}+200 \times 2 \times 10^{3} \times\left(3.5 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2}+200 \times 4 \times 10^{3} \times\left(-1.5 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2} \\&+\frac{200 \times\left(8 \times 10^{3}\right)^{3}}{12}+200 \times 8 \times 10^{3} \times\left(0.5 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2}+\frac{200 \times\left(3 \times 10^{3}\right)^{3}}{12} \\&+200 \times 3 \times 10^{3} \times\left(3 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2}+200 \times 4 \times 10^{3} \times\left(-3.5 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2}\end{aligned}\)

which gives

\(\begin{aligned}I_{z} &=3142 \times 10^{10} mm ^{4} \\I_{y}=& 200 \times 2 \times 10^{3} \times\left(8.4 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2}+\frac{200 \times\left(4 \times 10^{3}\right)^{3}}{12}+200 \times 4 \times 10^{3} \times\left(6.4 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2} \\&+200 \times\left(8 \times 10^{3}\right) \times\left(0.4 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2}+200 \times\left(3 \times 10^{3}\right) \times\left(-7.6 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2} \\&+\frac{200 \times\left(4 \times 10^{3}\right)^{3}}{12}+200 \times\left(4 \times 10^{3}\right) \times\left(-5.6 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2}\end{aligned}\)

which gives

\(\begin{gathered}I_{y}=12312.5 \times 10^{10} mm ^{4} \\I_{z y}=200[2 \times(3.5)(8.4)+4(-1.5)(6.4)+8(0.4)(0.5)+4(-5.6)(-3.5)+3(3)(-7.6)] \times 10^{9}\end{gathered}\)

from which

\(I_{z y}=640 \times 10^{10} mm ^{4}\)

Also \( M_{z}=-750 \times 16 \times 60 \times 30 \times 10^{3}=-2.16 \times 10^{10} Nmm \text { and } M_{y}=0 \). Substituting these values in Eq. (9.31) and simplifying

\(\sigma_{x}=-\left(\frac{M_{y} I_{z}-M_{z} I_{z y}}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}}\right) z-\left(\frac{M_{z} I_{y}-M_{y} I_{z y}}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}}\right) y\) (9.31)

 

\(\sigma_{z}=-0.36 \times 10^{-4} z+6.95 \times 10^{-4} y\) (i)

The maximum value will occur at H where \( z=-7.6 \times 10^{3} mm \text { and } y =+4.5 \times 10^{3} mm \) Then, from Eq.(i)

\( \sigma_{z}=3.4 N / mm ^{2} \text { (tension) } \)
