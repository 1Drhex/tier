Guest Question.

Calculation of a Distilled-water-Raw-water Exchanger. 175,000 lb/hr of distilled water enters an exchanger at 93 °F and leaves at 85 °F. The heat will be transferred to 280,000 lb/hr of raw water coming from supply at 75 °F and leaving the exchanger at 80 °F. A 10 psi pressure drop may be expended on both streams while providing a fouling factor of 0.0005 for distilled water and 0.0015 for raw water when the tube velocity exceeds 6 fps.

Available for this service is a 15¼ in. ID exchanger having 160¾ in. OD, 18 BWG tubes 16’0” long and laid out on 15/16-in. triangular pitch. The bundle is arranged for two passes, and baffles are spaced 12 in. apart. Will the exchanger be suitable?

Admin
Solution

Shell-Side

ID = 15¼ in

Baffle space = 12 in

 

Passes = 1

 Tube-Side

Number and length = 160 tubes, 16’0”

OD, BWG, pitch = ¾ in, 18 BWG, 15/16-in triangular pitch

Passes = 2

Heat Balance:

Hot Distilled Water, \(T_{avg}\) = \(\frac{93+85}{2}=89°F\)

C ≈ 1 Btu/(lb· °F) at 89 °F

Q = (175,000)(1)(93 – 85) = 1,400,000 Btu/hr

Cold Raw Water, \(t_{avg}\) = \(\frac{80+75}{2}=77.5°F\)

c ≈ 1 Btu/(lb· °F) at 77.5 °F

Q = (280,000)(1)(80 – 75) = 1,400,000 Btu/hr

Note: C and c are equal to each other because (i) both fluids are water, and (ii) stream temperatures are similar.

(2) Δ\(T^*\):

\(LMTD=\frac{\Delta T_a-\Delta T_b}{\ln (\Delta T_a/\Delta T_b)}=\frac{13-10}{\ln (13/10)}=11.4°F\)

 

\(R=\frac{T_1-T_2}{t_2-t_1}=\frac{8}{5}=1.6\)

 

\(P=\frac{t_2-t_1}{T_1-t_1}=\frac{5}{93-75}=0.278\)

 

\(F_T=\left\lgroup\frac{\sqrt{R^2+1} }{R-1} \right\rgroup\frac{\ln \left[(1-P)/(1-RP)\right] }{\ln \left[(2-P(R+1-\sqrt{R^2+1}))/(2-P(R+1+\sqrt{R^2+1} ))\right] }\)

 

\(=\left\lgroup\frac{\sqrt{1.6^2+1} }{1.6-1} \right\rgroup\frac{\ln \left[(1-0.278)/(1-(1.6)(0.278))\right] }{\ln \left[(2-0.278(1.6+1-\sqrt{1.6^2+1} ))/(2-0.278(1.6+1+\sqrt{1.6^2+1}) )\right] }\)

= 0.946

Δ\(T^*\) = LMTD × \(F_T\) = 11.4 °F × 0.946 = 10.8 °F

(3) \(T_{avg} and t_{avg}\) : Obtained in step (1).

 

Hot Fluid (Distilled Water) – Shell-Side (s)

(4 ‘) Flow Area, \(a_s\) :

\(a_s=\frac{ID\times C'B}{144P_T}\)

\(=\frac{(15.25)(0.1875)(5)}{144(0.9375)}=0.254\ ft^2\)

 Cold Fluid (Raw Water) – Tube-Side (t)

(4) Flow Area, \(a_t\) :

\(a^{'}_t=0.334\ in^2\ \Leftarrow [Table AT.8]\)

 

\(a_t=\frac{N_ta^{'}_t}{144n}\)

\(=\frac{(160)(0.334)}{144(2)}=0.186\ ft^2\)

 

(5′) Mass Velocity, \(G_s\) :

\(G_s=\frac{\dot{M}}{a_s}=\frac{175,000}{0.254}\)

\(=690,000lb/(hr\cdot ft^2)\)

 (5) Mass Velocity, \(G_t\) :

\(G_t=\frac{\dot{m}}{a_t}=\frac{280,000}{0.186} \)

\(=1,505,000lb/(hr\cdot ft^2)\)

 

(6′) Compute \(D_e\) :

\(D_e=\frac{3.44P^2_T-\pi d^2_o}{\pi d_o}\)

 

\(=\frac{3.44(0.9375^2)-\pi (0.75^2)}{\pi (0.75)}\)

 

\(=53\ in=\frac{0.53\ in}{12\ in/ft}=0.0442\ ft\)

 

Obtain μ at \(T_{avg}\) :

μ = 0.81 cP at 89 °F

\(=0.81\ cP\left\lgroup\frac{2.42lb/(ft\cdot hr)}{1\ cP} \right\rgroup\)

= 1.96lb/(ft·hr)

 (6) Obtain D from Table 10:

D = 0.65 in ⇐[Table AT.8]

\(=\frac{0.65\ in}{12\ in/ft}=0.054\ ft\)

 

 

 

 

 

Obtain μ at \(t_{avg}:\)

μ = 0.92 cP at 77.5 °F

\(=0.92\ cP\left\lgroup\frac{2.42lb/(ft\cdot hr)}{1\ cP} \right\rgroup\)

= 2.23lb/(ft·hr)

 

(7′) Reynolds Number, R\(e_s\) :

\(Re_s=\frac{D_eG_s}{\mu}\)

 

\(=\frac{(0.0442)(690,000)}{1.96}=16,200\)

 (7) Reynolds Number, \(Re_t\) :

\(Re_t=\frac{DG_t}{\mu}\)

 

\(=\frac{(0.054)(1,505,000)}{2.23}=36,400\)

(\(Re_t\) is for pressure drop only)

 

(8′) Obtain C and k at \(T_{avg}\) :

C ≈ 1 Btu/lb · °F at 89°F

k = 0.36 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F/ft at 89°F

 (8) Obtain c and k at \(t_{avg}\) :

c ≈ 1 Btu/lb · °F at 77.5°F

k = 0.36 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F/ft at 77.5°F

 

(9′) Solve for \(h_o\) and \(\phi_s\) at \(t_w\):

The small difference in the average temperatures
eliminates the need for a tube-wall correction, and therefore \(\phi_s\) can be approximated as 1.0 (as mentioned in step (2)).

\(\phi _s=\left\lgroup\frac{\mu }{\mu _w} \right\rgroup^{0.14}\approx 1.0\)

 (9) Solve for \(h_i\) and \(\phi_t\) at \(t_w\) :

The small difference in the average temperatures eliminates the need for a tube-wall correction, and therefore \(\phi_t\) can be approximated as 1.0 (as mentioned in step (2)).

\(\phi _t=\left\lgroup\frac{\mu }{\mu _w} \right\rgroup^{0.14}\approx 1.0\)

(9′) \(h_o=0.027\left\lgroup\frac{D_eG_s}{\mu}\right\rgroup^{0.8}\left\lgroup\frac{c\mu}{k}\right\rgroup^{¹⁄³}\left\lgroup\frac{\mu}{\mu _w}\right\rgroup^{0.14}\left\lgroup\frac{k}{D_e}\right\rgroup\)

 

\(=0.027\left\lgroup\frac{G_s}{\mu }\right\rgroup^{0.8}(c\mu k^2)^{¹⁄³}\left\lgroup\frac{\mu}{\mu _w}\right\rgroup^{0.14}\left\lgroup\frac{1}{D_e}\right\rgroup^{0.2}\)

 

\(=0.027\left\lgroup\frac{690,000}{1.96}\right\rgroup^{0.8}((1)(1.96)(0.36)^2)^{¹⁄³}(1.0)\left\lgroup\frac{1}{0.0442}\right\rgroup^{0.2}\)

≈ 873 \(\frac{Btu}{hr·ft^2·°F}\)

(9) \(h_i=0.027\left\lgroup\frac{DG_t}{\mu}\right\rgroup^{0.8}\left\lgroup\frac{c\mu}{k}\right\rgroup^{¹⁄³}\left\lgroup\frac{\mu}{\mu _w}\right\rgroup^{0.14}\left\lgroup\frac{k}{D}\right\rgroup\)

 

\(=0.027\left\lgroup\frac{G_t}{\mu }\right\rgroup^{0.8}(c\mu k^2)^{¹⁄³}\left\lgroup\frac{\mu}{\mu _w}\right\rgroup^{0.14}\left\lgroup\frac{1}{D}\right\rgroup^{0.2}\)

 

\(=0.027\left\lgroup\frac{1,505,000}{2.23}\right\rgroup^{0.8}((1)(2.23)(0.36)^2)^{¹⁄³}(1.0)\left\lgroup\frac{1}{0.054}\right\rgroup^{0.2}\)

≈ 1474 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F

(10) Clean overall coefficient, \(U_C\) :

\(U_C=\frac{h_ih_o}{h_i+h_o}=\frac{(873)(1474)}{873+1474}=548\ Btu/hr\cdot ft^2\cdot °F\)

When both film coefficients are high, the thermal resistance of the tube metal is not necessarily insignificant as assumed in the derivation of Eq. \(T_2=\frac{[(b/B)-1]T_1+[(B/b)e^{UA(B-b)}-(B/b)]t_1}{(B/b)e^{UA(B-b)}-1}\). For a steel 18 BWG tube \(R_m\) = 0.00017 and for copper \(R_m\) = 0.000017.

(11) Design overall coefficient, \(U_D\) :

\(a^{"}\) = 0.1963 \(ft^2\) per line. ft ⇐ [Table AT.8]

A = \(a^{''}N_t\)L = (0.1963)(160)(16) = 502 \(ft^2\)

\(U_D=\frac{Q}{A\Delta T^*}=\frac{1,400,000}{(502)(10.8)}=258\ Btu/hr\cdot ft^2\cdot °F\)

(12) Dirt factor \(R_d\):

\(R_d=\frac{U_C-U_D}{U_CU_D}=\frac{548-258}{(548)(258)}=0.00205hr\cdot ft^2\cdot °F/Btu\)

Summary
Variable Value Units
\(h_o\) = 873 \(\frac{Btu}{hr·ft^2·°F/ft}\)
\(h_i\) = 1474
\(U_C\) = 548 \(\frac{Btu}{hr·ft^2·°F}\)
\(U_D\) = 258
\(R_d\) (calculated) = 0.00205 \(\frac{hr·ft^2·°F}{Btu}\)
\(R_d\) (required) = 0.00200

Pressure Drop

(1) Using \(Re_s\) and \(T_{avg}\) in (6’) obtain f:

f = 0.0019\(\frac{ft^2}{in^2}\)at \(Re_s\) = 16,200

s = 1.0 ⇐ water

\(D_s\) = 15.25 in\(\left\lgroup\frac{1\ ft}{12\ in} \right\rgroup\) = 1.27 ft

 (1′) Using \(Re_t\) and \(t_{avg}\) in (6) above:

f = 0.00019\(\frac{ft^2}{in^2}\) at \(Re_t\) = 36,400

s = 1.0 ⇐ water

 

(2) Number of crosses, N + 1:

\(N+1=\frac{12L}{B}=\frac{12(16)}{12}=16\)

 (2′) \(\Delta P_t=\frac{fG^2_tLn}{(5.22\times 10^{10})D_es\phi _t}\)

\(=\frac{(0.00019)(1,505,000)^2(16)(2)}{(5.22\times 10^{10})(0.054)(1.0)(1.0)}\)

= 4.9 psi

\(\frac{\overline{v}^2}{2g^{'}}\left\lgroup\frac{62.5}{144} \right\rgroup\) = 0.33 at \(G_t\) = 1,505,000

 

\(\Delta P_r=\left\lgroup\frac{4n}{s}\right\rgroup\left\lgroup\frac{\overline{v}^2}{2g^{'}}\right\rgroup\left\lgroup\frac{62.5}{144}\right\rgroup\)

 

\(=\left\lgroup\frac{4(2)}{1.0}\right\rgroup(0.33)=2.6\ psi\)

 

(3′) \(\Delta P_s=\frac{fG^2_sD_s(N+1)}{(5.22\times 10^{10})D_es\phi _s}\)

\(\Delta P_s=\frac{(0.0019)(690,000)^2(1.27)(16)}{(5.22\times 10^{10})(0.0458)(1.0)(1.0)}\)

Δ\(P_s\) = 7.7 psi

Δ\(P_s\) < 10 psi as allowable ΔP

 (3′) Δ\(P_T\) = Δ\(P_t\) + Δ\(P_r\)

Δ\(P_T\)= 4.9 + 2.6 = 7.5psi

Δ\(P_T\) < 10 psi as allowable ΔP

It is seen that the overall design coefficient, \(U_D\), for Example 7.22 is five times that of the oil-to-oil exchanger in . The principal reason for the difference is the excellent thermal properties of water. This exchanger is satisfactory for this service.
