FRICTIONAL ADIABATIC FLOW IN A CONSTANT-AREA CHANNEL

Air flow is induced in an insulated tube of 7.16 mm diameter by a vacuum pump. The air is drawn from a room, where \(p_0 = 101\) kPa (abs) and \(T_0 = 23\)°C, through a smoothly contoured converging nozzle. At section (1), where the nozzle joins the constant-area tube, the static pressure is 98.5 kPa (abs). At section (2), located some distance downstream in the constant-area tube, the air temperature is 14°C. Determine the mass flow rate, the local isentropic stagnation pressure at section (2), and the friction force on the duct wall between sections (1) and (2).

Admin
Solution

Given: Air flow in insulated tube


Find:

(a) \(\dot{m}\).
(b) Stagnation pressure at section (2).
(c) Force on duct wall.
Solution:
The mass flow rate can be obtained from properties at section (1). For isentropic flow through the converging nozzle, local isentropic stagnation properties remain constant. Thus,

\(\frac{p_{0_{1}}}{p_{1}}=\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{1}^{2}\right)^{k /(k-1)}\)

 

and

 

\(\begin{aligned}&M_{1}=\left\{\frac{2}{k-1}\left[\left(\frac{p_{0_{1}}}{p_{1}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}-1\right]\right\}^{1 / 2}=\left\{\frac{2}{0.4}\left[\left(\frac{1.01 \times 10^{5}}{9.85 \times 10^{4}}\right)^{0.286}-1\right]^{1 / 2}=0.190\right. \\\quad\\&T_{1}=\frac{T_{0_{1}}}{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{1}^{2}}=\frac{(273+23) \mathrm{K}}{1+0.2(0.190)^{2}}=294 \mathrm{~K}\end{aligned}\)

 

For an ideal gas,

\(\begin{aligned}&\rho_{1}=\frac{p_{1}}{R T_{1}}=9.85 \times 10^{4} \frac{\mathrm{N}}{\mathrm{m}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}}{287 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m}} \times \frac{1}{294 \mathrm{~K}}=1.17 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3} \\\quad\\&V_{1}=M_{1} c_{1}=M_{1} \sqrt{k R T_{1}}=(0.190)\left[1.4 \times 287 \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}} \times 294 \mathrm{~K} \times \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}\right]^{1 / 2} \\\quad\\&V_{1}=65.3 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\end{aligned}\)

The area, \(A_{1}\), is

\(A_{1}=A=\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}=\frac{\pi}{4}\left(7.16 \times 10^{-3}\right)^{2} \mathrm{~m}^{2}=4.03 \times 10^{-5} \mathrm{~m}^{2}\)

From continuity,

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{m} &=\rho_{1} V_{1} A_{1}=1.17 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}} \times 65.3 \frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}} \times 4.03 \times 10^{-5} \mathrm{~m}^{2} \\\quad\\\dot{m} &=3.08 \times 10^{-3} \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{s}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad \dot{m}}\end{aligned}\)

Flow is adiabatic, so \(T_{0}\) is constant, and

\(T_{0_{2}}=T_{0_{1}}=296 \mathrm{~K}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad T_{0_{2}}}\)

 

Then

\(\frac{T_{0_{2}}}{T_{2}}=1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{2}^{2}\)

Solving for \(M_{2}\) gives

\(\begin{aligned}&M_{2}=\left[\frac{2}{k-1}\left(\frac{T_{0_{2}}}{T_{2}}-1\right)\right]^{1 / 2}=\left[\frac{2}{0.4}\left(\frac{296}{287}-1\right)\right]^{1 / 2}=0.396 \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad M_{2}}\\\quad\\&V_{2}=M_{2} c_{2}=M_{2} \sqrt{k R T_{2}}=(0.396)\left[1.4 \times 287 \frac{N \cdot m}{k g \cdot K} \times 287 \mathrm{~K} \times \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}\right]^{1 / 2} \\\quad\\&V_{2}=134 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad V_{2}}\end{aligned}\)

From continuity, Eq. 13.24a, \(\rho_{1} V_{1}=\rho_{2} V_{2}\), so

\(\rho_{2}=\rho_{1} \frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}}=1.17 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}} \times \frac{65.3}{134}=0.570 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad \rho_{2}}\)

and

\(p_{2}=\rho_{2} R T_{2}=0.570 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}} \times 287 \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}} \times 287 \mathrm{~K}=47.0 \mathrm{kPa}(\mathrm{abs})\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad p_{2}}\)

 

The local isentropic stagnation pressure is

\(\begin{aligned}&p_{0_{2}}=p_{2}\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{2}^{2}\right)^{k /(k-1)}=4.70 \times 10^{4} \mathrm{~Pa}\left[1+0.2(0.396)^{2}\right]^{3.5} \\\quad\\&p_{0_{2}}=52.4 \mathrm{kPa}(\mathrm{abs})\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad p_{0_{2}}}\end{aligned}\)

 

The friction force may be obtained using the momentum equation (Eq.13.24b),

\(R_{x}+p_{1} A-p_{2} A=\dot{m} V_{2}-\dot{m} V_{2}\) (13.18b)

which we apply to the control volume shown above (except we replace \(R_{x}\) from Fig. 13.18 with \(-F_{f}\) because we know the friction force \(F_{f}\) on the fluid acts in the negative \(x\) direction).

\(\begin{aligned}&-F_{f}=\left(p_{2}-p_{1}\right) A+\dot{m}\left(V_{2}-V_{1}\right) \\\quad\\&-F_{f}=(4.70-9.85) \times 10^{4} \frac{\mathrm{N}}{\mathrm{m}^{2}} \times 4.03 \times 10^{-5} \mathrm{~m}^{2}+3.08 \times 10^{-3} \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{s}} \times(134-65.3) \frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}} \times \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}{\mathrm{~kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}}\end{aligned}\)

 

or

\(F_f = 1.86\) N (to the left, as shown)
This is the force exerted on the control volume by the duct wall. The force of the fluid on the duct is

\(K_x = -F_f = 1.86 \text {N} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\text{(to the right)}\quad\quad\quad\quad K_x}\)

 

This problem illustrates use of some of the basic equations, Eqs. 13.24, for flow in a duct with friction.

\(\begin{gathered}\rho_{1} V_{1}=\rho_{2} V_{2}=\rho V \equiv G=\frac{\dot{m}}{A}=\text { constant } \\\quad\\R_{x}+p_{1} A-p_{2} A=\dot{m} V_{2}-\dot{m} V_{1}\\\quad\\h_{0_{1}}=h_{1}+\frac{V_{1}^{2}}{2}=h_{2}+\frac{V_{2}^{2}}{2}=h_{0_{2}}=h_{0} \\\quad\\s_{2}>s_{1} \\\quad\\p=\rho R T \\\quad\\\Delta h=h_{2}-h_{1}=c_{p} \Delta T=c_{p}\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right) \\\quad\\\Delta s=s_{2}-s_{1}=c_{p} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-R \ln \frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}\end{gathered}\) (13.24 a, b, c, d, e, f, g)
