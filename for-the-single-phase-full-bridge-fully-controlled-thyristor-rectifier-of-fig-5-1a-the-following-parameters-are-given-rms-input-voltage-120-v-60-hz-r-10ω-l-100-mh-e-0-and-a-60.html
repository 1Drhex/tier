Guest Question.

For the single-phase full-bridge fully controlled thyristor rectifier of Fig. 5.1(a) the following parameters are given: rms input voltage \(=120 V 60 Hz , \quad R =10 \Omega, L =100 mH , E =0\), and \(\alpha=60^{\circ}\).Perform the following: (a) Determine the output current function and (b) calculate the power consumed by the load.

 

Admin
Solution

Solution

\(\omega=2 \pi f =2 \pi(60)=377 rad / s\)

 

\(\left|Z_{ o }\right|=\sqrt{ R ^{2}+(\omega L )^{2}}=\sqrt{10^{2}+(377 \times 0.1)^{2}}=39 \Omega\)

 

\(\varphi=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\omega L }{ R }\right)=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{377 \times 0.1}{10}\right)=75^{\circ} \text { or } 1.3 rads\)

 

Since the angle \(\varphi\) is greater than the firing angle \(\alpha=60^{\circ}\), according to relation (5.13), the output current of the rectifier is continuous.

 

\(\alpha \leq \varphi\) (5.13)

 

a) The output current of the rectifier is:

 

\(\begin{aligned}i _{o}(\omega t )=& \overline{ I }_{o}+\widehat{ I }_{0,2} \cos \left(2 \omega t -\theta_{2}-\varphi_{2}\right)+\widehat{ I }_{o,4} \cos \left(4 \omega t -\theta_{4}-\varphi_{4}\right) \\&+\widehat{ I }_{o,6} \cos \left(6 \omega t -\theta_{6}-\varphi_{6}\right)+\ldots\end{aligned}\)

 

where \(\overline{ I }_{ o }=\frac{\overline{ V }_{ o }}{ R } ; \quad \overline{ V }_{ o }=\frac{2 \sqrt{2} \times 120}{\pi} \cos 60^{\circ}=54 V ; \quad \overline{ I }_{ o }=\frac{54}{10}=5.4 A ; \hat{ I }_{ o , 2}=\frac{\hat{ V }_{ o , 2}}{\left| Z _{ o , 2}\right|}\)

 

\(\widehat{ I }_{o,4}=\frac{\hat{ V }_{ o , 4}}{\left| Z _{ o , 4}\right|} ; \quad \widehat{ I }_{ o , 6}=\frac{\hat{ V }_{ o , 6}}{\left| Z _{ o , 6}\right|}\)

 

\(\left| Z _{ o , 2}\right|=\sqrt{ R ^{2}+(2 \omega L )^{2}}=\sqrt{10^{2}+(2 \times 377 \times 0.1)^{2}}=76 \Omega\)

 

\(\left| Z _{0,4}\right|=\sqrt{ R ^{2}+(4 \omega L )^{2}}=\sqrt{10^{2}+(4 \times 377 \times 0.1)^{2}}=151.13 \Omega\)

 

\(\left| Z _{o,6}\right|=\sqrt{ R ^{2}+(6 \omega L )^{2}}=\sqrt{10^{2}+(6 \times 377 \times 0.1)^{2}}=226.42 \Omega\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\phi_{2}=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 \omega L }{ R }\right)=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 \times 377 \times 0.1}{10}\right)=82.45^{\circ} \\&\phi_{4}=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{4 \omega L }{ R }\right)=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{4 \times 377 \times 0.1}{10}\right)=86.2^{\circ} \\&\phi_{6}=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{6 \omega L }{ R }\right)=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{6 \times 377 \times 0.1}{10}\right)=87.47^{\circ} \\&\theta_{2}=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\widehat{ V }_{} 2,2}{\widehat{ V }_{ o 1,2}}\right) \quad \theta_{4}=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\widehat{ V }_{ o 2,4}}{\widehat{ V }_{ ol , 4}}\right) \quad \theta_{6}=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\widehat{ V }_{ o 2,6}}{\widehat{ V }_{ o 1,6}}\right) .\end{aligned}\)

 

Using Fig. 5.5 for \(\alpha=60^{\circ}\) amplitudes of the output voltage harmonic components are:

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\widehat{ V }_{ o , 2}=0.75 \sqrt{2} \times 120=127.28 V \\&\widehat{ V }_{ o , 4}=0.3 \sqrt{2} \times 120=50.91 V \\&\widehat{ V }_{o,6}=0.15 \sqrt{2} \times 120=25.46 V\end{aligned}\)

 

Therefore, the respective amplitudes of the output current harmonics are:

 

\(\widehat{I}_{o,2}=\frac{127.28}{76}=1.67 \text { A } \quad \widehat{I}_{o,4}=\frac{50.91}{151.13}=0.337 \text { A } \quad \widehat{I}_{o,6}=\frac{25.46}{226.42}=0.112 A\)

 

Consequently, the output current is given by the following function:

 

\(\begin{aligned}i _{o}(\omega t )=& 5.4+1.67 \cos \left(2 \omega t -\theta_{2}-82.45^{\circ}\right)+0.337 \cos \left(4 \omega t -\theta_{4}-86.2^{\circ}\right) \\&+0.112 \cos \left(6 \omega t -\theta_{6}-87.47^{\circ}\right)+\cdots\end{aligned}\)

 

b) The power consumed by the load is:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\widetilde{ I }_{ o } &=\sqrt{\overline{ I }_{ o }^{2}+\widetilde{ I }_{ o , 2}^{2}+\widetilde{ I }_{ o , 4}^{2}+\widetilde{ I }_{ o , 6}^{2}+\cdots} \approx \sqrt{\overline{ I }_{ o }^{2}+\widetilde{ I }_{ o , 2}^{2}+\widetilde{ I }_{ o , 4}^{2}} \\&=\sqrt{(5.4)^{2}+\left(\frac{1.67}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{0.337}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^{2}}=5.53 A\end{aligned}\)

 

Therefore \(P _{ R }=(5.53)^{2}(10)=306 W\).
