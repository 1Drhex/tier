Guest Question.

Calculate the capacitance of a parallel-plate capacitor having a mica dielectric, \(\epsilon _{r} \)= 6, a plate area of 10 in.\(^{2} \), and a separation of 0.01 in.

Admin
Solution

We may find that

\(S=10 \times 0.0254^{2}=6.45\times 10^{-3}m^{2}\)

 

\(d=0.01\times 0.0254=2.54\times 10^{-4}m\)

and therefore

\(C=\frac{6\times 8.854\times 10^{-12}\times 6.45\times 10^{-3} }{2.54\times 10^{-4}} =1.349 \space nF\)

A large plate area is obtained in capacitors of small physical dimensions by stacking smaller plates in 50- or 100-decker sandwiches, or by rolling up foil plates separated by a flexible dielectric.

Finally, the total energy stored in the capacitor is

\(W_{E} =\frac{1}{2} \int_{vol}^{}{\epsilon E^{2} dv} =\frac{1}{2}\int_{0}^{S}{\int_{0}^{d}{\frac{\epsilon \rho ^{2}_{S} }{\epsilon ^{2} }dz \space dS } } =\frac{1}{2}\frac{ \rho ^{2}_{S} }{\epsilon }Sd=\frac{1}{2}\frac{\epsilon S}{d} \frac{ \rho ^{2}_{S} d^{2} }{\epsilon ^{2} }\)

or

\(W_{E} =\frac{1}{2}CV^{2}_{0} =\frac{1}{2}QV_{0} =\frac{1}{2}\frac{Q^{2} }{C} \) (4)

which are all familiar expressions. Equation (4) \(W_{E} =\frac{1}{2}CV^{2}_{0} =\frac{1}{2}QV_{0} =\frac{1}{2}\frac{Q^{2} }{C} \) also indicates that the energy stored in a capacitor with a fixed potential difference across it increases as the dielectric constant of the medium increases.
