Guest Question.

Calculate the ellipsometric parameters a, b and \(\gamma\) for the wave considered in

Admin
Solution

From equation

\(E=\Re \left\{E_{c} \right\} =\Re \left\{(A-i B)e^{-2 \pi i\eta n(z-ct)} \right\} =A \cos 2\pi \eta n(z-ct)-B \sin 2\pi \eta n(z-ct)\),

we find

\(A=E_{0} (6\hat{\imath} +2\hat{\jmath} -8\widehat{k} )/\sqrt{154} , B=-E_{0} (3\hat{\imath} -5\hat{\jmath} -4\widehat{k})/\sqrt{154}\) ,

and at any given location, say z = 0, the electric field vector may be written as

\(E=E_{0}[(6\cos 2\pi \nu t – 3\sin 2\pi \nu t)\hat{\imath} +(2\cos 2\pi \nu t+5\sin 2\pi \nu t )\hat{\jmath} -(8\cos 2\pi \nu t-4\sin 2\pi \nu t )\widehat{k}]/\sqrt{154}\) .

The time-varying magnitude |E| at this location then is

\(\left|E\right| ^{2} =E.E =\frac{E^{2}_{0} }{154} (36\cos ^{2}2\pi \nu t -36 \cos 2\pi \nu t \sin 2\pi \nu t +9\sin ^{2} 2\pi \nu t\)

\(+4\cos ^{2}2\pi \nu t +20 \cos^{2} 2\pi \nu t \sin 2\pi \nu t +25\sin ^{2} 2\pi \nu t\)

\(+64\cos ^{2}2\pi \nu t -64 \cos^{2} 2\pi \nu t \sin 2\pi \nu t +16\sin ^{2} 2\pi \nu t)\)

\(=E^{2}_{0}(50-80\cos 2\pi \nu t \sin 2\pi \nu t +54\cos ^{2}2\pi \nu t)/154\) .

The maximum (a) and minimum (b) of |E| may be found by differentiating the last expression with respect to t and setting the resuh equal to zero. This operation leads to

\(-80(\cos ^{2}2\pi \nu t-\sin ^{2} 2\pi \nu t)=108\sin 2\pi \nu t \cos 2\pi \nu t\)

\(-80\cos 4\pi \nu t =54 \sin 4\pi \nu t\)

or

\(2\pi \nu t=0.5 \tan ^{-1} \left(-\frac{80}{54} \right)\) .

This function is double-valued, leading to \((2\pi \nu t)_{1} =-27.99^{\circ }\) and \((2\pi \nu t)_{2} =62.01^{\circ }\) . Substituting these values into the expression for E gives

\(E_{1} =E_{0} (0.5404\hat{\imath} -0.0468\hat{\jmath} -0.720\widehat{k} ), \left|E\right| =a=0.9009E_{0}\)

and

\(E_{2} =E_{0}(0.0134\hat{\imath} +0.4314\hat{\jmath} -0.0179\widehat{k}) , \left|E\right| =b=0.4339E_{0}\) .

The evaluation of the azimuth depends on the choice of a reference axis in the plane of the vibration ellipse. In the present problem the y-axis lies in this plane and is, therefore, the natural choice. Thus,

\(\cos \gamma =\frac{E.\hat{\jmath} }{\left|E\right|} =-\frac{0.0468}{0.9009} =-0.0519 , \gamma =92.97^{\circ }\) .
