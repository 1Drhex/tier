Guest Question.

Calculate current gain; voltage gain; \(R_{IN} and R_{OUT}\) using the following data .

Data: \(h_{fe}= 36; R_L = 2 × 10^3 and h_{o.e} = 2 × 10^{-6}; h_{ie} = 1200 Ω . h_{r.e}= 0. And R_S = 500 Ω\).

Admin
Solution

Determination of current gain; \(A_I\)

\(A_I =-\frac{h_{f.e}}{\left[1+h_{o.e}.Z_L\right] }=\frac{36}{1 + 2 \times 10^{-6} \times 2.5 \times 10^3}=\frac{36}{1+5\times 10^{-3}} \equiv 36 \)

Determination of voltage gain; \(A_V\)

\(A_V =\frac{A_I.Z_L}{Z_{IN} }=\frac{36\times 2.5 \times 10^3}{1.2 \times 10^{3} }=\frac{90}{1.2} =75 \)

Determination of input resistance; \(R_{IN}\)

\(R_{IN}= Z_{IN}= h_{ie} + A_I.h_{re}.Z_L \) is approximately \(= h_{ie} as h_{re} = 0\). ; Therefore \( R_{IN}= h_{ie}=1200 ohms\).

Determination of output resistance;\( R_O\)

\(R_0 =\frac{R_S+h_{ie}}{h_{oe}(R_S+h_{ie}) }= \frac{500+1200}{2 \times 10^{6} (500 +1200) }=\frac{1700}{3.4\times 10^{-3}}=500\times 10^3 \).
