Guest Question.

The vector \(x_{1}\) is in the null space of A,

x1=[−1−52−4],A=[−33−6−6260−80−84−14−3−7−19210−2−14]

Find a basis for the null space that includes \(x_{1}\).

Admin
Solution

In , we were able to exploit the fact that we knew a basis for R³. Here we do not know a basis for the null space, so we start by determining the vector form of the general solution to Ax = 0 and use the vectors to form the initial basis. We skip the details and just report the news that

null(A)=span{[−1302],[−3120]} (1)

From this point we follow the procedure in , by forming the matrix with our given vector \(x_{1}\) and the two basis vectors in (1), and then finding an echelon form.

[−1−1−3−531202−420]∼[−1−1−3012000000]

Since the pivots are in the first two columns, it follows that

{[−1−52−4],[−1302]} (2)

forms a basis for the null space of A that contains \(x_{1}\). Note that (2) is not the only basis containing \(x_{1}\). For instance, if we reverse the order of the two vectors in (1) and follow the same procedure, we end up with the basis

{[−1−52−4],[−3120]}
