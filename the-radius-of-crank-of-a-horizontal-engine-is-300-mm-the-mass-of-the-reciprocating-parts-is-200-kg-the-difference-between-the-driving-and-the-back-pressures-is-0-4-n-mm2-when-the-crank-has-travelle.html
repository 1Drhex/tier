Guest Question.

The radius of crank of a horizontal engine is 300 mm. The mass of the reciprocating parts is 200 kg. The difference between the driving and the back pressures is 0.4 N/mm² when the crank has travelled 60° from I.D.C. The length of connecting rod is 1.2 m and the cylinder bore is 0.5 m. The engine runs at 240 rpm. Neglecting the effect of the piston rod, find (a) pressure on the slide bar, (b) thrust in the connecting rod, (c) tangential force, and (d) turning moment on the crankshaft.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given }: r=300 mm , M_{r}=200 kg , l=1.2 m , D=0.5 m , N=240 rpm , p_{1}-p_{2}=0.4 MPa \).

\( \omega=2 \pi \times 240 / 60=25.13 rad / s \).

\( n=l / r=1.2 / 0.3=4 \).

\( \text { Force on the piston, } F_{p}=\left(p_{1}-p_{2}\right) \times \pi D^{2} / 4 \).

\( =0.40 \times \pi \times 500^{2} / 4 \).

= 78540 N.

\( \text { Inertia force due to reciprocating parts, } F_{i}=M_{r} \omega^{2} r[\cos \theta+\cos 2 \theta / . n] \).

\( =200 \times(25.13)^{2} \times 0.3\left[\cos 60^{\circ}+\cos 120^{\circ} / 4\right] \).

= 14209 N.

\( \text { Piston effort, } PE =F_{p}-F_{i}=78540-14209=64331 N \).

(a) Pressure on the slide bar:

\( \sin \phi=\sin \theta / n =\sin 60^{\circ} / 4=0.2165 \).

\( \phi=12.5^{\circ} \).

\( \text { Pressure on the slide bars, } F_{n}=\text { PE } \tan \phi \).

\( =64331 \tan 12.5^{\circ}=14266 N \).

\( \text { (b) Thrust in the connecting rod, } F_{n}= PE / \cos \phi \).

\( =64331 / \cos 12.5^{\circ}=65893 N \).

\( \text { (c) Tangential force on the crankpin, } F_{t}=F_{c} \sin (\theta+\phi) \).

\( =65893 \sin 72.5^{\circ}=62843 N \).

\( \text { (d) Turning moment on the crankshaft }=F_{t} r \).

\( =62843 \times 0.3=18853 Nm \).
