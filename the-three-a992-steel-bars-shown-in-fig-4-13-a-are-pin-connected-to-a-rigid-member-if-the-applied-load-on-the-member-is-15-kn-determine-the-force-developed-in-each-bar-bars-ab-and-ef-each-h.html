Guest Question.

The three A992 steel bars shown in Fig. 4–13 a are pin connected to a rigid member. If the applied load on the member is 15 kN, determine the force developed in each bar. Bars AB and EF each have a cross-sectional area of \(50 mm^2\) , and bar CD has a cross-sectional area of \(30 mm^2\).

Admin
Solution

Equilibrium. The free-body diagram of the rigid member is shown in Fig. 4–13 b . This problem is statically indeterminate since there are three unknowns and only two available equilibrium equations.

\(+\uparrow \sum{F_y}=0; \quad\quad F_A+F_C+F_E-15kN=0 \) (1)

 

\(\searrow +\sum{M_C}=0; \quad\quad -F_A(0.4m)+15kN(0.2m)+F_E(0.4m)=0 \) (2)

Compatibility. The applied load will cause the horizontal line ACE shown in Fig. 4–13 c to move to the inclined line A′C′E′. The displacements of points A , C , and E can be related by similar triangles.
Thus the compatibility equation that relates these displacements is

\(\frac{\delta _A-\delta _E}{0.8m}=\frac{\delta _C-\delta _E}{0.4m} \)

 

\(\delta _C=\frac{1}{2}\delta _A+\frac{1}{2}\delta _E \)

Load-Displacement. Using the load–displacement relationship, \(\delta =\frac{PL}{AE}\) , we have

\(\frac{F_CL}{(30mm^2)E_{st}}=\frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{F_AL}{(50mm^2)E_{st}} \right]+\frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{F_EL}{(50mm^2)E_{st}} \right] \)

 

\(F_C=0.3F_A+0.3F_E\) (3)

Solving Eqs. 1 – 3 simultaneously yields

\(F_A=9.52kN\)

 

\(F_C=3.46kN\)

 

\(F_E=2.02kN\)
