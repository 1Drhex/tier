Guest Question.

A test on a 1/4 hp, 120 V, 60 Hz, 1725 r/min, singlephase motor reveals the following results:
stator resistance: 2 Ω
rotor resistance referred to the stator: 4 Ω
stator leakage reactance: 3 Ω
rotor leakage reactance referred to the stator: 3 Ω
resistance corresponding to the windage,
friction, and iron losses: 600 Ω
magnetizing reactance: 60 Ω
Draw the equivalent circuit diagram and determine the power output, efficiency, and power factor of the motor when it turns at 1725 r/min.

Admin
Solution

The equivalent circuit diagram (Fig. 32) shows the values of the listed impedances divided by two. The slip is \(s=(1800-1725) / 1800=0.0417\)

 

We first determine the impedance of the forward circuit between points 1, 3:

 

\(\begin{aligned}Z_{ F } &=1+j 1.5+\frac{1}{\frac{1}{j 30}+\frac{1}{300}+\frac{1}{48+j 1.5}} \\&=1+j 1.5+13.89+j 19.53 \\&=14.89+j 21.03\end{aligned}\)

 

The impedance of the backward circuit between points 3, 2 is

 

\(\begin{aligned}Z_{ B } &=1+j 1.5+\frac{1}{\frac{1}{j 30}+\frac{1}{300}+\frac{1}{1.02+j 1.5}} \\&=1+j 1.5+0.93+j 1.45 \\&=1.93+j 2.95\end{aligned}\)

 

The current in the stator is

 

\(\begin{aligned}I=E /\left(Z_{ F }+Z_{ B }\right) &=120 /(16.82+j 23.98) \\&=120 /(29.29 \angle 54.95)\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\therefore I=4.097 \angle-54.95\)

 

The forward voltage between points 1, 3 is

 

\(\begin{aligned}E_{ B } &=I Z_{ F }=4.097 \angle-54.95 \times(14.89+j 21.03) \\&=4.097 \angle-54.95 \times 25.77 \angle 54.7 \\&=105.6 \angle-0.25\end{aligned}\)

 

The backward voltage between points 3, 2 is

 

\(\begin{aligned}E_{ B } &=I Z_{ B }=4.097 \angle-54.95 \times(1.93+j 2.95) \\&=4.097 \angle-54.95 \times 3.52 \angle 56.8 \\&=14.42 \angle 1.85\end{aligned}\)

 

Forward rotor current

 

\(\begin{aligned}I_{ F } &=I \frac{1}{\frac{1}{j 30}+\frac{1}{300}+\frac{1}{48+j 1.5}} \\&=\frac{4.097 \angle-54.95(13.89+j 19.53)}{48.02 \angle 1.79} \\&=\frac{4.097 \angle-54.95 \times 23.96 \angle 54.58}{48.02 \angle 1.79} \\&=2.044 \angle-2.16\end{aligned}\)

 

Backward rotor current

 

\(\begin{aligned}I_{ B } &=I \frac{1}{\frac{1}{j 30}+\frac{1}{300}+\frac{1}{1.02+j 1.5}} \\&=\frac{4.097 \angle-54.95(0.93+j 1.45)}{1.81 \angle 55.78} \\&=\frac{4.097 \angle-54.95 \times 1.72 \angle 57.32}{1.81 \angle 55.78} \\&=3.89 \angle-53.4\end{aligned}\)

 

Forward power to rotor

 

\(P_{ F }=I_{ F }^{2} \times 48=2.044^{2} \times 48=200.5 W\)

 

Forward torque \(T_{ F }:\)

 

\(\frac{9.55 P_{ F }}{n_{ s }}=\frac{9.55 \times 200.5}{1800}=1.064 N \cdot m\)

 

Backward power to rotor \(P_{ B }\)

 

\(I_{ B }^{2} \times 1.02=3.89^{2} \times 1.02=15.4 W\)

 

Backward torque \(T_{ B }\)

 

\(\frac{9.55 P_{ B }}{n_{ s }}=\frac{9.55 \times 15.4}{1800}=0.082 N \cdot m\)

 

Net torque

 

\(T_{ F }-T_{ B }=1.064-0.082=0.982 N \cdot m\)

 

Mechanical power output P

 

\(\frac{n T}{9.55}=\frac{1725 \times 0.982}{9.55}=177 W\)

 

Horsepower

 

\(\frac{177}{746}=0.24 hp\)

 

Active power input to stator:

 

\(E I \cos \theta=120 \times 4.097 \cos 54.95=282.3 W\)

 

Power factor

 

\(\cos 54.95=0.57=57 \%\)

 

Efficiency

 

\(\frac{177}{282}=0.627=62.7 \%\)
