Guest Question.

A converging–diverging nozzle, with an exit area of \(40 cm^2\) and a throat area of \(10 cm^2\), is attached to a reservoir with T = 20°C and p = 500 kPa absolute. Determine the two exit pressures that result in M = 1 at the throat for an isentropic flow. Also, determine the associated exit temperatures and velocities. See Fig. 9.7.

Admin
Solution

The exit pressures we seek are associated with curves \(C\) and \(D\) of Fig. \(9.7\). The area ratio is

\(\frac{A}{A^{*}}=\frac{40}{10}=4\)

We could solve Eq. 9.3.19 for M using a trial-and-error technique; however, let us use the isentropic flow table, Table D.1. There are two entries for \(A / A^{*}=4 .\) Interpolation gives

\(\frac{A}{A^{*}}=\frac{1}{\mathrm{M}}\left[\frac{2+(k-1) \mathrm{M}^{2}}{k+1}\right]^{(k+1) / 2(k-1)} (9.3.19)\)

\(\begin{array}{l}\left(\frac{p}{p_{0}}\right)_{C}=\frac{4.182-4.0}{4.182-3.673}(0.9823-0.9864)+0.9864=0.9849 \\\\\left(\frac{p}{p_{0}}\right)_{D}=\frac{4.0-3.999}{4.076-3.999}(0.02891-0.02980)+0.02980=0.02979\end{array}\)

Hence the two exit pressures that will result in isentropic flow are

\(p_{C}=492.4 \mathrm{kPa} \quad \text { and } \quad p_{D}=14.9 \mathrm{kPa}\)

Note the very small pressure difference \((7.6 \mathrm{kPa})\) between receiver and reservoir necessary to create the flow condition of curve \(C\) of Fig. \(9.7\).

The exit temperature ratios and Mach numbers are interpolated to be

\(\begin{aligned}\left(\frac{T}{T_{0}}\right)_{C} &=0.3576(0.9949-0.9961)+0.9961=0.9957 \\\left(\frac{T}{T_{0}}\right)_{D} &=0.01299(0.3633-0.3665)+0.3665=0.3665 \\\mathrm{M}_{C} &=0.3576 \times 0.02+0.14=0.147 \\\mathrm{M}_{D} &=0.01299 \times 0.02+2.94=2.94\end{aligned}\)

The exit temperatures associated with curves \(C\) and \(D\) are thus

\(T_{C}=0.9957 \times 293=291.7 \mathrm{~K}\)\(T_{D}=0.3665 \times 293=107.4 \mathrm{~K}\)

The exit velocities are found from \(V=\mathrm{M} c\) to be

\(\begin{array}{l}V_{C}=0.147 \sqrt{1.4 \times 287 \times 291.7}=50.3 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s} \\V_{D}=2.94 \sqrt{1.4 \times 287 \times 107.4}=611 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\end{array}\)
