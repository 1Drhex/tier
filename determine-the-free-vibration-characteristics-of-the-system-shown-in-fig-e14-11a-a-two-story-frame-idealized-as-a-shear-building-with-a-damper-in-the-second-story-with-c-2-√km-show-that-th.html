Guest Question.

Determine the free vibration characteristics of the system shown in Fig. E14.11a, a two-story frame idealized as a shear building with a damper in the second story with \(c = 2 \sqrt{km}\). Show that the eigenvectors satisfy the orthogonality properties.

Admin
Solution

The mass, damping, and stiffness matrices of the system are

\(\mathbf{m} =\begin{bmatrix}2m & \\ & m \end{bmatrix}\)
\(\mathbf{c} =\begin{bmatrix}c &-c \\ -c & c \end{bmatrix}\)

\(\mathbf{k} =\begin{bmatrix}3k &-k \\ -k & k \end{bmatrix}\) (a)

The eigenvalue problem to be solved is defined by Eq. (A14.2.8), repeated here for convenience:

\(\lambda \mathbf{a \kappa } + \mathbf{b \kappa } = \mathbf{ O } \)(b)

where the matrices a and b, defined in Eq. (A14.2.5), for this system are

\(\mathbf{a} = \begin{bmatrix}\mathbf{ O } &\mathbf{m} \\ \mathbf{m} &\mathbf{c} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 &0&2m &0 \\ 0 &0 & 0 m\\ 2m &0 &c & -c\\ 0 & m & -c& c \end{bmatrix}\) (c)

\(\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix}\mathbf{-m } &\mathbf{O} \\ \mathbf{O} &\mathbf{k} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}-2m &0&0 &0 \\ 0 &-m & 0 &0\\ 0 &0 &3k & -k\\ 0 & 0 & -k & k \end{bmatrix}\) (d)

The eigenvalue problem can be solved numerically using an appropriate algorithm, e.g., the Matlab function eig(b, −a), resulting in the eigenvalues

\(\lambda _{1}. \bar { \lambda _{1}} = \frac{k}{m} (-0.0501 \pm 0.7955 i)\) (e.1)

\(\lambda _{2} =- 0.7232 \frac{k}{m}<br /> \lambda _{3} = -2.1766 \frac{k}{m} \)(e.2)

Note that two of the eigenvalues of the system are a complex-conjugate pair, whereas two are real and negative valued, with |λ3| > |λ2| From the eigenvalues λ1 and \(\bar { \lambda _{1}}\), ω1 and ζ1 can be determined using Eq. (14.5.6):

\(\omega _{1} = \left | \lambda _{1} \right | = 0.7971 \frac{k}{m}\)

\(\zeta _{1} =- \frac{Re( \lambda _{1})}{\left | \lambda _{1} \right |} = 0.0629\) (f)

Substituting eigenvalues λ2 and λ3 in Eqs. (14.10.3) and (14.10.4) gives

\(\omega _{2} = \sqrt { \lambda _{2} \lambda _{3}} = 1.2546 \frac{k}{m}\)

\(\zeta _{2} =- \frac{\lambda _{2} + \lambda _{3} }{2 \omega _{2}} =1.1556 \) (g)

From the eigenvalue λ1 of Eq. (e.1), the corresponding frequency ω1D of the damped system is determined from its definition in Eq. (14.5.5):

\(\omega _{1D}= Im (\lambda _{1}) = 0.7955 \frac{k}{m}\) (h)

However, ω2D should be determined from Eq. (14.10.5):

\(\omega _{2D}= \frac{\lambda _{2} – \lambda _{3}}{2} = 0.7267 \frac{k}{m}\) (i)

Solution of the eigenvalue problem [Eq. (b)] also provides the 4 × 1 eigenvectors, but only the third and fourth components are relevant and shown below:

\(\psi _{1}= \begin{Bmatrix}0.7923+0.2787i \\1 \end{Bmatrix}\)
\(\psi _{2}= \begin{Bmatrix}-0.1717 \\1 \end{Bmatrix}\)
\(\psi _{3}= \begin{Bmatrix} -0.4129 \\1 \end{Bmatrix}\) (j)

Note that \(\psi _{1}\) is complex valued, but \(\psi _{2}\) and \(\psi _{3}\) are real valued (Fig. E14.11b). To verify that the eigenvectors \(\psi _{1}\) and \(\psi _{2}\) are orthogonal, we compute the individual terms in the left side of Eqs. (14.6.1) and (14.6.2):

\(\psi _{1}^{T} \mathbf{m} \psi _{2} = \begin{Bmatrix} 0.7923 + 0.2787i \\1 \end{Bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}2m & \\ & m \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} -0.1717 \\1 \end{Bmatrix} = m (0.7279- 0.0957i)\) \(\psi _{1}^{T} \mathbf{k} \psi _{2} = \begin{Bmatrix} 0.7923 + 0.2787i \\1 \end{Bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}3k &-k \\ -k & k \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} -0.1717 \\1 \end{Bmatrix} = k (- 0.0287- 0.4222i )\) \(\psi _{1}^{T} \mathbf{c} \psi _{2} = \begin{Bmatrix} 0.7923 + 0.2787i \\1 \end{Bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}c &-c \\ -c & c \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} -0.1717 \\1 \end{Bmatrix} = -\sqrt{km} (- 0.0287- 0.4222i )\)

Substituting these individual terms in the left side of Eqs. (14.6.1) and (14.6.2) gives

\(( \lambda _{1}+ \lambda _{2}) \psi _{1}^{T} \mathbf{m} \psi _{2} + \psi _{1}^{T} \mathbf{c} \psi _{2} = \sqrt{km} + \sqrt{km}=0\) \(\psi _{1}^{T} \mathbf{k} \psi _{2} – \lambda _{1} \lambda _{2} \psi _{1}^{T} \mathbf{m} \psi _{2} = k (−0.0287 − 0.4222i) − k (0.0362 − 0.5753i) (0.7279 − 0.0957i) = 0\)

This verifies that the eigenvectors\( \psi _{1} \)and \(\psi _{2}\) computed for the system are orthogonal; similar calculations show that other pairs of eigenvectors are also orthogonal. In passing, we rewrite Eqs. (h) and (i) to facilitate an interesting observation:

\(\omega _{1D} = 0.9743 \frac{2k}{3m}\)

\(\omega _{2D} = 0.8900 \frac{2k}{3m}\) (k)

If the second story were rigid, the system of Fig. E14.11a would reduce to an SDF system with natural vibration frequency \(= \sqrt {2k/3m}\). Note that the frequencies of the damped system [Eq. (k)] are close to this reference frequency because the damper coefficient is so large that the damper provides much resistance to deformation in the second story.
