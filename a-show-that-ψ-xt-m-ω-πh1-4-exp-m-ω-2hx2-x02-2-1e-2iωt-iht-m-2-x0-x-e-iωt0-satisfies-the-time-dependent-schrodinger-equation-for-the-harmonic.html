Guest Question.

(a) Show that

\( \Psi(x, t)=\left(\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}\right)^{1 / 4} \exp \left[-\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar}\left(x^{2}+\frac{x_{0}^{2}}{2}\left(1+e^{-2 i \omega t}\right)+\frac{i \hbar t}{m}-2 x_{0} x e^{-i \omega t}\right)\right] \).

satisfies the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for the harmonic oscillator potential (Equation 2.44). Here \( x_0 \) is any real constant with the dimensions of length

\( V(x)=\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} x^{2} \) (2.44).

(b) Find \( |\Psi(x, t)|^{2} \), and describe the motion of the wave packet.

(c) Compute \( \left\langle x \right\rangle \) , and \( \left\langle p \right\rangle \), and check that Ehrenfest’s theorem (Equation 1.38) is satisfied.

\( \frac{d\langle p\rangle}{d t}=\left\langle-\frac{\partial V}{\partial x}\right\rangle \) (1.38).

Admin
Solution

(a)

\( \frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial t}=\left(-\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar}\right)\left[\frac{x_{0}^{2}}{2}\left(-2 i \omega e^{-2 i \omega t}\right)+\frac{i \hbar}{m}-2 x_{0} x(-i \omega) e^{-i \omega t}\right] \Psi \), so

\( i \hbar \frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial t}=\left[-\frac{1}{2} m x_{0}^{2} \omega^{2} e^{-2 i \omega t}+\frac{1}{2} \hbar \omega+m x_{0} x \omega^{2} e^{-i \omega t}\right] \Psi \).

\( \frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial x}=\left[\left(-\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar}\right)\left(2 x-2 x_{0} e^{-i \omega t}\right)\right] \Psi=-\frac{m \omega}{\hbar}\left(x-x_{0} e^{-i \omega t}\right) \Psi \);

\( \frac{\partial^{2} \Psi}{\partial x^{2}}=-\frac{m \omega}{\hbar} \Psi-\frac{m \omega}{\hbar}\left(x-x_{0} e^{-i \omega t}\right) \frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial x}=\left[-\frac{m \omega}{\hbar}+\left(\frac{m \omega}{\hbar}\right)^{2}\left(x-x_{0} e^{-i \omega t}\right)^{2}\right] \Psi \).

\( -\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \frac{\partial^{2} \Psi}{\partial x^{2}}+\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} x^{2} \Psi=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m}\left[-\frac{m \omega}{\hbar}+\left(\frac{m \omega}{\hbar}\right)^{2}\left(x-x_{0} e^{-i \omega t}\right)^{2}\right] \Psi+\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} x^{2} \Psi \).

\( =\left[\frac{1}{2} \hbar \omega-\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2}\left(x^{2}-2 x_{0} x e^{-i \omega t}+x_{0}^{2} e^{-2 i \omega t}\right)+\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} x^{2}\right] \Psi \).

\( =\left[\frac{1}{2} \hbar \omega+m x_{0} x \omega^{2} e^{-i \omega t}-\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} x_{0}^{2} e^{-2 i \omega t}\right] \Psi \).

\( =i \hbar \frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial t} \) (comparing second line above).

(b)

\( |\Psi|^{2}=\sqrt{\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}} e^{-\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar}}\left[\left(x^{2}+\frac{x_{0}^{2}}{2}\left(1+e^{2 i \omega t}\right)-\frac{i \hbar t}{m}-2 x_{0} x e^{i \omega t}\right)+\left(x^{2}+\frac{x_{0}^{2}}{2}\left(1+e^{-2 i \omega t}\right)+\frac{i \hbar t}{m}-2 x_{0} x e^{-i \omega t}\right)\right] \).

\( =\sqrt{\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}} e^{-\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar}\left[2 x^{2}+x_{0}^{2}+x_{0}^{2} \cos (2 \omega t)-4 x_{0} x \cos (\omega t)\right]} \). But \( x_{0}^{2}[1+\cos (2 \omega t)]=2 x_{0}^{2} \cos ^{2} \omega t \).

\( =\sqrt{\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}} e^{-\frac{m \omega}{\hbar}\left[x^{2}-2 x_{0} x \cos (\omega t)+x_{0}^{2} \cos ^{2}(\omega t)\right]} = \sqrt{\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}} e^{-\frac{m \omega}{\hbar}\left(x-x_{0} \cos \omega t\right)^{2}} \).

The wave packet is a Gaussian of fixed shape, whose center oscillates back and forth sinusoidally, with amplitude \( x_0 \) and angular frequency ω.

(c) Note that this wave function is correctly normalized (compare Eq. 2.60). Let \( y \equiv x-x_{0} \cos \omega t \):

\( \psi_{0}(x)=\left(\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}\right)^{1 / 4} e^{-\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar} x^{2}} \) (2.60).

\( \langle x\rangle=\int x|\Psi|^{2} d x=\int\left(y+x_{0} \cos \omega t\right)|\Psi|^{2} d y=0+x_{0} a \cos \omega t \int|\Psi|^{2} d y= x_{0} \cos \omega t \).

\( \langle p\rangle=m \frac{d\langle x\rangle}{d t}=-m x_{0} \omega \sin \omega t \). \( \frac{d\langle p\rangle}{d t}=-m x_{0} \omega^{2} \cos \omega t . \quad V=\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} x^{2} \Longrightarrow \frac{d V}{d x}=m \omega^{2} x \).

\( \left\langle-\frac{d V}{d x}\right\rangle=-m \omega^{2}\langle x\rangle=-m \omega^{2} x_{0} \cos \omega t=\frac{d\langle p\rangle}{d t} \), so Ehrenfest’s theorem is satisfied.
