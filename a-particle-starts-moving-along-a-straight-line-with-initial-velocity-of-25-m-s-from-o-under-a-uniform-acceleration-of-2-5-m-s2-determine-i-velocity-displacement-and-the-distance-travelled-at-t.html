Guest Question.

A particle starts moving along a straight line with initial velocity of 25 m/s, from O under a uniform acceleration of \( -2.5 m/s^2 \) . Determine
(i) velocity, displacement and the distance travelled at t = 5 sec,
(ii) how long the particle moves in the same direction ? What are its velocity, displacement and distance covered then ?
(iii) the instantaneous velocity, displacement and the distance covered at t = 15 sec,
(iv) the time required to come back to O, velocity displacement and distance covered then and
(v) instantaneous velocity, displacement and distance covered at t = 25 sec.

Admin
Solution

Given : u = 25 m/s, a = -2.5 m/s^2 , O is the origin.

(i) t = 5 sec. Refer to Fig. 11.1(a)

ν = u + at .

ν = 25 + (-2.5) × 5.

ν = 12.5 m/s (→).

Now, \( s = ut + \frac{1}{2} at^2 \).

\( s = 25 ×5 + \frac{1}{2}×(-2.5)×5^2 \).

s = 93.75 m (→).

Since velocity is positive, the particle is moving in the same direction and therefore displacement is equal to the distance travelled.

∴ d = s = 93.75 m. Ans.

(ii) The particle moves in the same direction till it comes to rest because of negative acceleration and then its direction will reverse.
At the above-said instant velocity of particle will be zero ν = 0 (Point of reversal).

Let t be the time taken by the particle to move in the same direction [Refer to Fig. 11.1(b)], we have

ν= u + at.

0 = 25 + (-2.5)t.

t = 10 sec Ans.

For displacement, we have

\( s = ut + \frac{1}{2} at^2 \).

\( s = 25 ×10 + \frac{1}{2}×(-2.5)×5^2 \).

s = 218.75 m (→).

As particle has not reversed its direction, we have Displacement = Distance travelled.

s = d = 218.75 m. Ans.

(iii) t = 15 sec. Refer to Fig. 11.1(c).

ν = u + at.

ν = 25 + (-2.5) × 15 = -12.5 m/s.

ν = 12.5 m/s (←).

Now, \( s = ut + \frac{1}{2} at^2 \).

\( s = 25 ×15 + \frac{1}{2}×(-2.5)×15^2 = 93.75 m \) (→).

Particle is moving from O to B and then from B to C in t = 15 sec.

∴ Distance travelled d = OB + BC (BC = OB – OC).

d = 218.75 + (218.75 – 93.75).

d = 343.75 m Ans.

(iv) Let t be the time taken by particle to reach origin. Refer to Fig. 11.1(d).

∴ Displacement = 0.

\( s = ut + \frac{1}{2} at^2 \).

\( 0 = 25 ×t + \frac{1}{2}×(-2.5)×t^2\).

t = 20 sec.

ν = u + at.

ν = 25 + (-2.5) × 20 = -25 m/s.

ν = 25 m/s (←).

Distance covered d = OB + BO = 218.75 + 218.75.

d = 437.5 m Ans.

(v) At t = 25 sec. Refer to Fig. 11.1(e).

ν = u + at.

ν = 25 + (-2.5) × 25.

ν = -37.5 m/s.

ν = 37.5 m/s (←) Ans.

Now, \( s = ut + \frac{1}{2} at^2 \).

\( s = 25 ×25 + \frac{1}{2}×(-2.5)×25^2 -156.25 \) m.

s = 156.25 m (←) Ans.

Distance covered d = OB + BO + OD = 218.75 + 218.75 + 156.25.

d = 593.75 m Ans.
