Guest Question.

A flanged-shaped flexural member is subjected to an internal axial force of P = 11.8 kN, an internal shear force of V = 21.3 kN, and an internal bending moment of M = 4.7 kN-m, as shown Figure P15.19a. The cross-sectional dimensions of the shape as shown in Figure P15.19b are \(b_{1}\) = 42 mm, \(b_{2}\) = 80 mm, \(t_{f}\) = 6 mm, d = 90 mm, \(t_{w}\) = 6 mm, and a = 20 mm. Determine the principal stresses and the maximum shear stress acting at points H and K. Show these stresses on an appropriate sketch for each point.

Admin
Solution

Centroid location in y direction: (reference axis at bottom of shape)

\(\begin{array}{lccccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Width } b \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Height } h \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} \text { (from bottom) } \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 42 & 6 & 252.0 & 87.0 & 21,924.0 \\\text { web } & 6 & 78 & 468.0 & 45 & 21,060.0 \\\text { bottom flange } & 80 & 6 & 480.0 & 3.0 & 1,440.0 \\\hline & & & 1,200.0 mm ^{2} && 44,424.0 mm ^{3}\end{array}\)

 

\(\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{44,424.0 mm ^{3}}{1,200.0 mm ^{2}}=37.02 mm\) (measured upward from bottom edge of bottom flange)

 

Moment of inertia about the z axis: (i.e., horizontal axis)

\(\begin{array}{lcccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 756.000 & 49.980 & 629,496.101 & 630,252.101 \\\text { web } & 237,276.000 & 7.980 & 29,802.427 & 267,078.427 \\\text { bottom flange } & 1,440.000 & -34.020 & 555,532.992 & 556,972.992 \\\hline \text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( mm ^{4}\right)= 1,454,303.520\end{array}\)

 

Axial stress at point H:

\(\sigma_{x}=\frac{F}{A}=\frac{11,800 N }{1,200.0 mm ^{2}}=9.833 MPa\)

Bending stress at point H: \((y=90 mm -20 mm -37.02 mm =32.98 mm )\)

\(\sigma_{x}=-\frac{M y}{I_{z}}=-\frac{(-4.700 kN – m )(32.980 mm )(1,000 N / kN )(1,000 mm / m )}{1,454,303.520 mm ^{4}}=106.584 MPa\)

Transverse and horizontal shear stress at point H:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q=(42 mm )(6 mm )(49.980 mm )+(6 mm )(14 mm )(39.980 mm )=15,953.280 mm ^{3} \\&\tau=\frac{V Q}{I t}=\frac{(21,300 N )\left(15,953.280 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(1,454,303.520 mm ^{4}\right)(6 mm )}=38.942 MPa \text { (Sense to be determined by inspection) }\end{aligned}\)

Stress element at H:

 

Principal stress calculations:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq. (12.12):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}}{2}\right)^{2}+\tau_{x y}^{2}} \\&=\frac{(116.418)+(0)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(116.418)-(0)}{2}\right)^{2}+(38.942)^{2}} \\&=58.209 \pm 70.034\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1} &=128.2 MPa \text { and } \quad \sigma_{p 2}=-11.83 MPa \\\tau_{\max } &=70.0 MPa \text { (maximum in-plane shear stress) } \\\sigma_{\text {avg }} &=58.2 MPa ( T ) \quad \text { (normal stress on planes of maximum in-plane shear stress) }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan 2 \theta_{p} &=\frac{\tau_{x y}}{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) / 2}=\frac{38.942}{[(116.418)-(0)] / 2}=\frac{38.942}{58.209}=0.6690 \\&\left.\therefore \theta_{p}=16.89^{\circ} \quad \text { (counterclockwise from the } x \text { axis to the direction of } \sigma_{p 1}\right)\end{aligned}\)

 

 

Axial stress at point K:

\(\sigma_{x}=\frac{F}{A}=\frac{11,800 N }{1,200 mm ^{2}}=9.833 MPa\)

Bending stress at point K: \((y=-37.02 mm +20 mm =-17.020 mm )\)

\(\sigma_{x}=-\frac{M y}{I_{z}}=-\frac{(-4.700 kN – m )(-17.020 mm )(1,000 N / kN )(1,000 mm / m )}{1,454,303.520 mm ^{4}}=-55.005 MPa\)

Transverse and horizontal shear stress at point K:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q=(80 mm )(6 mm )(34.020 mm )+(6 mm )(14 mm )(24.020 mm )=18,347.280 mm ^{3} \\&\tau=\frac{V Q}{I t}=\frac{(21,300 N )\left(18,347.280 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(1,454,303.520 mm ^{4}\right)(6 mm )}=44.786 MPa \text { (Sense to be determined by inspection) }\end{aligned}\)

Stress element at K:

 

 

Principal stress calculations:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq. (12.12):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}}{2}\right)^{2}+\tau_{x y}^{2}} \\&=\frac{(-45.172)+(0)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(-45.172)-(0)}{2}\right)^{2}+(44.786)^{2}} \\&=-22.586 \pm 50.159\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1} &=27.6 MPa \text { and } \quad \sigma_{p 2}=-72.7 MPa \\\tau_{\max } &=50.2 MPa \text { (maximum in-plane shear stress) } \\\sigma_{\text {avg }} &=22.6 MPa ( C ) \text { (normal stress on planes of maximum in-plane shear stress) }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan 2 \theta_{p} &=\frac{\tau_{x y}}{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) / 2}=\frac{44.786}{[(-45.172)-(0)] / 2}=\frac{44.786}{-22.586}=-1.9829 \\&\left.\therefore \theta_{p}=-31.62^{\circ} \quad \text { (clockwise from the } x \text { axis to the direction of } \sigma_{p 2}\right)\end{aligned}\)
