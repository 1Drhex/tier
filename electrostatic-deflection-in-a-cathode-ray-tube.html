Guest Question.

Electrostatic deflection in a Cathode Ray Tube

Admin
Solution

Electrons from an electron gun are accelerated by a potential \(V_{ax}\) in the X direction and enter the transverse field of \(V_{d}\) with an initial velocity of \(v_{ox}=\sqrt{\frac{2qV_a}{m} } \) (1)

Due to \(V_{d}\) , the electrons get deflected, reach the end of the plates at P and since there is no accelerating field beyond P, they continue in a linear path and touch the screen at P’ as shown in Fig. 1.38.

The equation of motion up to P between the plates is as given by the equation

\(y=\frac{1}{2}\left\lgroup\frac{a_y}{V^2_ox} \right\rgroup x^2 \)

So, the electrons will move in a parabolic path up to point P .

From there, the equations of the straight line P’ decides the motion of the electron and can be found from the equation of the straight line as per the geometry of the Fig. 1.38. The straight-line path from the edge of the deflecting plates to the screen is a tangent to the parabolic path between the plates at that point.

\((y-y_1)=(\tan \theta )(x-x_1)=\frac{dy}{dx}(x-x_1) \) (2)

\(y=\tan \theta\left\lgroup x-x_1+\frac{y_1}{\tan \theta} \right\rgroup \) (3)
At P, \(x_1=l\) and \(y_1=y\) at, \(l\frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{a_y}{V^2_ax} \right] l^2=y_1 \)
But \(\frac{dy}{dx}= \tan \theta=\frac{a_y.l}{v^2_{ox}} \) at \(y_1 =y_{atl}\)
Therefore, \(y=\tan \theta\left\lgroup x-l+\frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{a_y}{v^2_{ox}}\right]l^2\frac{v^2_{ox}}{a_y l} \right\rgroup =\tan \theta\left\lgroup x-\frac{l}{2} \right\rgroup\)
On simplification, \(Y=\tan \theta \left\lgroup x-\frac{1}{2} \right\rgroup\) (4)

when, x = l/2; y = 0 or the straight line PP′ when projected backwards intersects the X-axis at o’. At x = l/2; that is o’ bisects the line.
The deflection D can be found from the geometry again.

D is y at \(X=L+\frac{l}{2}\)

So , \(D=\frac{a_yl}{V^2_{ox}}\left\lgroup L+\frac{l}{2}-\frac{l}{2} \right\rgroup =\frac{a_ylL}{V^2_{ox}}\)
But \(a_y=\frac{V_d}{d}.\frac{q}{m} \) and \(V^2_{ox}=\frac{2qV_a}{m} \)

Substituting these values,

\(D=\frac{V_d}{d}.\frac{q}{m}.\frac{m}{2q}.\frac{lL}{V_a}=\frac{V_dlL}{2V_ad} \) (5)
D is proportional to \(V_d \)
∴ The deflection Sensitivity \(S\overset{\sim }{= }\frac{D}{V_d}=\frac{l.L}{2.V_a.d} \) (6)

From the equation (6) for deflection sensitivity, the deflection sensitivity S is independent of the charge and mass of the electrons. Reducing d and \(V_a \) increases the sensitivity. L and l have limitations in view of the construction of the equipment.
