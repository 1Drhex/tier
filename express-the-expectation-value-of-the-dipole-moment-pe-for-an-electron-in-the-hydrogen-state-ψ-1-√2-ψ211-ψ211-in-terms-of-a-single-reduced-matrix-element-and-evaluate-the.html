Guest Question.

Express the expectation value of the dipole moment \( P_e \) for an electron in the hydrogen state

\( \psi=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\psi_{211}+\psi_{210}\right) \).

in terms of a single reduced matrix element, and evaluate the expectation value.

Note: this is the expectation value of a vector so you need to compute all three components. Don’t forget Laporte’s rule!

Admin
Solution

The expectation value of the dipole moment \( p_e \) is

\( \left\langle\psi\left| p _{e}\right| \psi\right\rangle=\frac{1}{2}\left(\left\langle211|+\langle 200|) p _{e}(|211\rangle+|200\rangle)\right.\right. \).

\( =\frac{1}{2}\left(\left\langle 211\left| p _{e}\right| 211\right\rangle+2 \operatorname{Re}\left[\left\langle 211\left| p _{e}\right| 200\right\rangle\right]+\left\langle 210\left| p _{e}\right| 200\right\rangle\right) \).

Now because of Laporte’s rule, the first and last matrix elements vanish and

\( \left\langle\psi\left| p _{e}\right| \psi\right\rangle=\operatorname{Re}\left[\left\langle 211\left| p _{e}\right| 200\right\rangle\right] \).

We can now write out the operator

\( \left\langle\psi\left| p _{e}\right| \psi\right\rangle=-e(\operatorname{Re}[\langle 211|x| 200\rangle \hat{\imath}+\langle 211|y| 200\rangle \hat{\jmath}+\langle 211|z| 200\rangle \hat{k}]) \).

Now, according to Equation 6.57 the last term vanishes and we may write \( r_{\pm}=x \pm i y \) so that

\( \left\langle n^{\prime} \ell^{\prime} m^{\prime}\left|\hat{V}_{z}\right| n \ell m\right\rangle=0 \quad \text { unless } m^{\prime}=m \) (6.57).

\( \left\langle\psi\left| p _{e}\right| \psi\right\rangle=-e\left(\operatorname{Re}\left[\left\langle 211\left|\frac{r_{+}+r_{-}}{2}\right| 200\right\rangle \hat{\imath}+\left\langle 211\left|\frac{r_{+}-r_{-}}{2 i}\right| 200\right\rangle \hat{\jmath}\right]\right) \).

Now according to Equation 6.58, the matrix elements of \( r_-\) vanish and

\( \left\langle n^{\prime} \ell^{\prime} m^{\prime}\left|\hat{V}_{-}\right| n \ell m\right\rangle=0 \quad \text { unless } m^{\prime}=m-1 \).

\( \left\langle\psi\left| p _{e}\right| \psi\right\rangle=-e \operatorname{Re}\left[\left\langle 211\left|r_{+}\right| 200\right\rangle \frac{\hat{\imath}-i \hat{\jmath}}{2}\right] \).

We can then use Equation 6.59 to write

\( \left\langle n^{\prime} \ell^{\prime} m^{\prime}\left|\hat{V}_{+}\right| n \ell m\right\rangle=-\sqrt{2} C_{m 1 m^{\prime}}^{\ell 1 \ell^{\prime}}\left|n \ell^{\prime}\|V\| n \ell\right\rangle \).

\( \left\langle\psi\left| p _{e}\right| \psi\right\rangle=-e \operatorname{Re}\left[-\sqrt{2} C_{011}^{011}\langle 21\|r\| 21\rangle \frac{\hat{\imath}-i \hat{\jmath}}{2}\right] \)

\( =e \operatorname{Re}\left[\langle 21\|r\| 20\rangle \frac{\hat{\imath}-i \hat{\jmath}}{\sqrt{2}}\right]=\frac{e}{\sqrt{2}}\langle 21\|r\| 21\rangle \hat{\imath} \).

since, as we’ll prove in just a second, the reduced matrix element is real in this case. To evaluate the reduced matrix element, we choose the simplest instance:

\( \langle 210|z| 100\rangle=C_{000}^{011}\langle 21\|r\| 21\rangle=\langle 21\|r\| 21\rangle \).

or

\( \langle 21\|r\| 20\rangle=\sqrt{2}\langle 210|z| 200\rangle \)

\( =\sqrt{2} \int \psi_{210}^{*}( r ) r \cos \theta \psi_{200}( r ) d^{3} r =-3 a \).

and

\( \left\langle\psi\left| p _{e}\right| \psi\right\rangle=-\frac{3 e a}{\sqrt{2}} \).
