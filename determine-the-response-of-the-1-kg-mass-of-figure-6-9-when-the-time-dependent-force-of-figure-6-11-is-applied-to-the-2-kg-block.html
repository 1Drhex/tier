Guest Question.

Determine the response of the 1 kg mass of Figure 6.9 when the time-dependent force of Figure 6.11 is applied to the 2 kg block.

Admin
Solution

The mathematical form of the force shown in Figure 6.11 is

\(F(t)=10(1-10t)[u(t)-u(t-0.1)] \) (a)

The impulsive response of the 1 kg block due to a unit impulse applied to the 2 kg block is calculated in . The convolution integral of Equation (6.53) is used to determine the response of the system of Figure 6.10 as

\( x_{1}(t)=\int_{0}^{t}{10(1-10\tau )}\left[u(\tau ) -u(\tau -0.1)\right] \left[0.0162 \sin17.8(t-\tau ) \right. \\ -\left.0.0084\sin34.4(t-\tau ) \right] d\tau\) (b)

\( x_{i}(s)=\int_{0}^{t}{F_{j}(\tau )h_{i,j}(t-\tau )}d\tau \) (6.53)

Equation (b) is written as

\( \begin{matrix} x_{1}(t)=10\left\{0.0162\left[\int_{0}^{t}{(1-10\tau )\sin17.8(t-\tau )u(\tau )d\tau }\right.\right. \\ -\left.\int_{0}^{t}{(1-10\tau )\sin 17.8(t-\tau )u(\tau -0.1)d\tau } \right] \\ -0.0084 \left[\int_{0}^{t}{(1-10\tau )\sin34.4(t-\tau )u(\tau )d\tau } \right. \\ \left. \left. -\int_{0}^{t}{(1-10\tau )\sin34.4(t-\tau )u(\tau -0.1)d\tau } \right] \right\} \end{matrix}\) (c)

The integrals of Equation (c) are evaluated using the entries of Table 5.1. Use the table for the delayed ramp excitation with A = 10 and B = 1 with \(\omega_{n}= 17.8\) for the first two integrals. Use \(t_{0}= 0 \) for the first integral and \(t_{0}=0.1\) for the second. The third and fourth integrals are evaluated using \(\omega_{n}= 34.4\). Use \(m_{eq}=1\) when evaluating the integrals. For example, the second integral is evaluated as

\( \int_{0}^{t}{(1-10\tau )\sin17.8}(t-\tau )u(\tau -0.1)d\tau \)

\(=\frac{-10}{317}\left[t+\frac{1}{-10}-(0.1+\frac{1}{-10} )\cos17.8(t-0.1) \right. \\ \left. -\frac{1}{17.8}\sin17.8(t-0.1) \right]u(t-0.1) \)

\(=0.0315[t-0.1-0.0562\sin17.8(t-0.1)]u(t-0.1) \) (d)

TABLE 5.1
Response of an undamped SDOF to common forms of
excitation
Delayed impulse
Excitation:\(F(t)=A\delta (t-t_{0})\)
Response: \(m_{eq}\omega ^{2}_{n}x(t)/ A=\omega _{n}\sin \omega _{n}(t-t_{0})u(t-t_{0})\)
Delayed step function
Excitation: \(F(t ) = Au(t – t_{0})\)
Response: \( m_{eq}\omega^{2}_{n}x(t)/A=[1-\cos \omega_{n} (t-t_{0})] u (t-t_{0})\)
Delayed ramp function
Excitation: \(F(t)=(At+B)u(t-t_{0})\)
Response: \(m_{eq}\omega^{2}_{0}x(t)/A=[t+B/A – (t_{0}+B/A)\cos \omega_{n} (t-t_{0})- \frac{1}{\omega _{n}}\sin \omega _{n}(t – t_{0})]u(t – t_{0})\)
Delayed exponential function
Excitation: \(F(t) = Ae ^{-\alpha (t – t_{0})}u(t – t_{0})\)
Response: \(m_{eq} \omega^{2}_{n}x(t)/A=[e^{-\alpha(t -t_{0})}+\alpha/\omega_{n}\sin \omega_{n}(t-t_{0})\\ -\cos\omega_{n}(t-t_{0}]/(1+\alpha^{2}/\omega^{2}_{n}) u (t-t_{0})\)
Delayed sine function:
Excitation:\(F(t) = A \sin[\omega (t – t_{0})]u(t – t_{0})\)
Response: \(\frac{m_{eq}\omega ^{2}_{n}x(t)}{A} =\frac{1}{2} \left\{\left(\frac{1}{\omega /\omega _{n}-1} \right)[\sin\omega (t-t_{0})-\sin\omega _{n}(t-t_{0})] -\left(\frac{1}{\omega /\omega _{n}+1} \right)[\sin \omega (t-t_{0})+\sin\omega _{n}(t-t_{0})] \right\}u(t-t_{0}) \)
This table provides the response of an undamped SDOF system to common forms of excitation. Man forms of excitation can be written as combinations of the excitations whose system responses are provided in the table. Superposition can be used to determine the response due to these excitations. In other cases, excitations can be approximated by combinations of excitations in this table. Then this table and superposition is used to approximate the response of an undamped SDOF system. The table provides the mathematical form of the excitation and response as well as graphical representations. In all cases, values of \(\omega_{n} = 10 rad/s\) and \(r_{0} = 0.5 s\) were used to generate the graphs. The values of specific parameters used for specific excitations are given.

The resulting solution is

\(x_{1}(t)=10\left\{(0.0162)(0.0315)\left[t-0.1-0.1\cos17.8t-0.0562\sin17.8t\right]u(t)\right. \\ \left. -(0.0162)(0.0315)\left[t-0.1-0.0562\sin17.8(t-0.1)\right] u(t-0.1)\right. \\ \left.-(0.0084)(0.0085)\left[t-0.1-0.1\cos34.8t-0.0287\sin17.8t\right]u(t)\right. \\ \left. +(0.0084)(0.0085)\left[t-0.1-0.0287\sin34.88(t-0.1)\right] u(t-0.1)\right\}\) (e)

Simplification results in

\(\begin{matrix}x_{1}(t)=(0.0044t-0.00044-0.0051\cos17.8t+7.14\times10^{-4}\cos34.8t \\ -2.87\times 10^{-4}\sin 17.8t +2.05 \times 10^{-5}\sin34.8t)u(t) \\ \left[0.0044t-0.00044-2.87\times10^{-4}\sin17.8(t-0.1) \right. \\ \left. +2.05\times10^{-5}\sin34.8(t-0.1)\right]u(t-0.1) \end{matrix} \) (f)
