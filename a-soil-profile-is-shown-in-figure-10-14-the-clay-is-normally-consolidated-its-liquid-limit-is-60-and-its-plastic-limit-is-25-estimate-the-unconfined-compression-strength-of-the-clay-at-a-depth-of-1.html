Guest Question.

A soil profile is shown in Figure 10.14. The clay is normally consolidated. Its liquid limit is 60 and its plastic limit is 25. Estimate the unconfined compression strength of the clay at a depth of 10 m measured from the ground surface. Use Skempton’s relationship from Eqs. (10.19),\(\frac{c_{u(vst)}}{\sigma^{\prime}_{o}} = 0.11 + 0.0037(PI)\)

(10.20),\(c_{u(design)} = \lambda c_{u(\text{vane shear})}\)

and (10.21).\(\lambda = \text{correction factor} = 1.7 - 0.54\,\log(PI)\\ PI = \text{plasticity index}\)

Admin
Solution

For the saturated clay layer, the void ratio is

\(e = w G_{s} = (2.68)(0.3) = 0.8\)

The effective unit weight is

\(\gamma^{\prime}_{clay} = \left(\frac{G_{s} – 1}{1 + e}\right)\gamma_{w} = \frac{(2.68 – 1)(9.81)}{1 + 0.8} = 9.16\,kN/m^{3}\)

The effective stress at a depth of 10 m from the ground surface is

\(\sigma^{\prime}_{o} = 3\gamma_{sand} + 7\gamma^{\prime}_{clay} = (3)(15.5) + (7)(9.16)\\ =110.62\,kN/m^{2}\)

From Eq. (10.19),

\(\frac{c_{u(vst)}}{\sigma^{\prime}_{o}} = 0.11 + 0.0037(PI)\\\frac{c_{u(vst)}}{110.62} = 0.11 + 0.0037(60 – 25)\)

Hence

\(c_{u(vst)} = 26.49\,kN/m^{2}\)

From Eqs. (10.20) and (10.21), we get

\(c_{u} = \lambda c_{u(vst)}\\\quad= [1.7 – 0.54 \log(PI)]c_{u(vst)}\\\quad= [1.7 – 0.54 \log(60 – 25)]26.49 = 22.95\,kN/m^{2}\)

So the unconfined compression strength is

\(q_{u} = 2c_{u} = (2)(22.95) = \textbf{45.9 kN/}\textbf{m}^{\textbf{2}}\)
