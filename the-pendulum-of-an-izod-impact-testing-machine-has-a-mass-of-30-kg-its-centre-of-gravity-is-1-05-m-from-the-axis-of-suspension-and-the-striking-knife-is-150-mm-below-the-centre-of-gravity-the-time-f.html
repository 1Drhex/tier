Guest Question.

The pendulum of an Izod impact testing machine has a mass of 30 kg. Its centre of gravity is 1.05 m from the axis of suspension and the striking knife is 150 mm below the centre of gravity. The time for 20 small free oscillations is 43.5 seconds. In making a test the pendulum is released from an angle of \(60^{\circ}\) to the vertical. Determine :
1. the position of the centre of percussion relative to the striking knife and the striking velocity of the pendulum, and 2. the impulse on the pendulum and the sudden change of axis reaction when a specimen giving an impact value of 55 N-m is broken.

Admin
Solution

Given : m = 30 kg ; OG = h = 1.05 m ; AG = 0.15 m

Since the time for 20 small free oscillations is 43.5 s, therefore frequency of oscillation,

\(n=\frac{20}{43.5}=0.46 Hz\)

1. The position of centre of percussion relative to the striking knife and the striking velocity of the pendulum

Let L = Equivalent length of simple pendulum,

\(k_{ G }\) = Radius of gyration of the pendulum about the centre of gravity, and

\(k_{ O }\) = Radius of gyration of the pendulum about O.

We know that the frequency of oscillation,

\(n=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \sqrt{\frac{g}{L}}\)

or

\(L=\frac{g}{(2 \pi n)^{2}}=\frac{9.81}{(2 \pi \times 0.46)^{2}}\)

= 1.174 m

\(\therefore \) Distance of centre of percussion (C) from the centre of gravity (G),

CG = OC – OG = L – OG

= 1.174 – 1.05 = 0.124 m

and distance of centre of percussion (C) from knife edge A,

AC = AG – CG = 0.15 – 0.124 = 0.026 m

We know that \(k_{ O }^{2}=(h+l) h=L . h=1.174 \times 1.05=1.233 m ^{2}\)

A little consideration will show that the potential energy of the pendulum is converted into kinetic energy of the pendulum before it strikes the test piece. Let v and \(\omega\) be the linear and angular velocity of the pendulum before it strikes the test piece.

\(\therefore \) \(m \cdot g \cdot h_{1}=\frac{1}{2} m \cdot v^{2}=\frac{1}{2} m \cdot k_{ O }^{2} \cdot \omega^{2}\) \(\ldots\left(\because v=k_{ O } \cdot \omega\right)\)

 

\(30 \times 9.81 \times 1.05\left(1-\cos 60^{\circ}\right)=\frac{1}{2} \times 30 \times 1.233 \omega^{2} \quad \text { or } \quad 154.5=18.5 \omega^{2}\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(\omega^{2}=154.5 / 18.5=8.35 \quad \text { or } \quad \omega=2.9 rad / s\)

 

\(\therefore \) Velocity of striking \(=\omega \times O A=2.9(1.05+0.15)=3.48 m / s\)

2. Impulse on the pendulum and sudden change of axis reaction

It is given that the impact value of the specimen (i.e. the energy used for breaking the specimen) is 55 N-m. Let \(\omega_{1}\) be the angular velocity of the pendulum immediately after impact. We know that

Loss of kinetic energy \(=\frac{1}{2} I\left(\omega^{2}-\omega_{1}^{2}\right)=\frac{1}{2} m \cdot k_{ O }^{2}\left(\omega^{2}-\omega_{1}^{2}\right)=55 N – m\)

 

\(\therefore \quad \frac{1}{2} \times 30 \times 1.233\left(2.9^{2}-\omega_{1}^{2}\right)=55\)

 

\(\begin{array}{ll}18.5\left(8.41-\omega_{1}^{2}\right)=55 & \text { or } \quad \omega_{1}^{2}=8.41-55 / 18.5=5.44\end{array}\)

 

\(\therefore \quad \omega_{1}=2.33 rad / s\)

Let P and Q be the impulses at the knife edge A and at the pivot O respectively as shown in Fig. 4.8.

\(\therefore\) P + Q = Change of linear momentum

\(=m \cdot h\left(\omega-\omega_{1}\right)=30 \times 1.05(2.9-2.33)=17.95\) …(i)

Taking moments about G,

0.15 P – 1.05 Q = Change of angular momentum

\(=m \cdot k_{ G }^{2}\left(\omega-\omega_{1}\right)=m\left(k_{ O }^{2}-h^{2}\right)\left(\omega-\omega_{1}\right)\)

 

\(=30\left(1.233-1.05^{2}\right)(2.9-2.33)=2.27\) …(ii)

 

From equations (i) and (ii),

P = 17.6 N-s; and Q = 0.35 N-s

\(\therefore \) Change in axis reaction when pendulum is vertical

= Change in centrifugal force

\(=m\left(\omega^{2}-\omega_{1}^{2}\right) h=30\left(2.9^{2}-2.33^{2}\right) 1.05=94 N\)
