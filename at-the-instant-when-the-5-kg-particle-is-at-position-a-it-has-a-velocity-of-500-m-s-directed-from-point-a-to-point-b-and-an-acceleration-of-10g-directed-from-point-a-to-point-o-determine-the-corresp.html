Guest Question.

At the instant when the 5-kg particle is at position A, it has a velocity of 500 m/s directed from point A to point B and an acceleration of 10g directed from point A to point O. Determine the corresponding rate of change of the speed, the radius of curvature of the path, and the location of the center of curvature of the path. Also determine the tangent, normal, and binormal components of the resultant force acting on the particle.

Admin
Solution

This example demonstrates how one can extract path variable information when the velocity and acceleration are known. The idea is to make the velocity and acceleration fit the fundamental formulas for path variables. The given vectors are

\( \large\bar{v}=500 \bar{e}_{B / A} \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}, \quad \bar{a}=10(9.807) \bar{e}_{O / A} \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}^{2}.\)

The unit vectors are defined by the positions of the end points, according to

\( \large\begin{array}{c} \bar{r}_{A / O}=4 \cos 60^{\circ}\left(\cos 75^{\circ} \bar{i}+\sin 75^{\circ} \bar{j}\right)+4 \sin 60^{\circ} \bar{k} \\\\ =0.5176 \bar{i}+1.9319 \bar{j}+3.464 k \mathrm{~m}, \\\\ \bar{r}_{B / O}=4 \bar{i} ,\\\\ \bar{e}_{B / A}=\frac{\bar{r}_{B / A}}{\left|\bar{r}_{B / A}\right|}=\frac{\bar{r}_{B / O}-\bar{r}_{A / O}}{\left|\bar{r}_{B / O}-\bar{r}_{A / O}\right|}=0.6598 \bar{i}-0.3660 \bar{j}-0.6563 \bar{k}, \\\\ \bar{e}_{O / A}=-\frac{\bar{r}_{A / O}}{\left|\bar{r}_{A / O}\right|}=-0.1294 \bar{i}-0.4830 \bar{j}-0.8660 \bar{k} . \end{array}\)

In general, \( \bar{v}=v \bar{e}_{t}\), from which it follows that

\( \large\bar{e}_{t}=\bar{e}_{B / A}=0.6598 \bar{i}-0.3660 \bar{j}-0.6563 \bar{k}\) .

Then, because \(\dot{v}\) is the tangential component of acceleration, we find

\( \large\dot{v}=\bar{a} \cdot \bar{e}_{t}=98.07 \bar{e}_{O / A} \cdot \bar{e}_{B / A}=64.70 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}^{2} \) .

We may evaluate the normal acceleration by forming the difference between \(\bar{a}\) and \(\dot{v} \bar{e}_{t}\) , specifically

\( \large\begin{aligned} \frac{v^{2}}{\rho} \bar{e}_{n} &=\bar{a}-\dot{v} \bar{e}_{t}=98.07 \bar{e}_{O / A}-64.70 \bar{e}_{B / A} \\\\ &=-55.38 \bar{i}-23.68 \bar{j}-42.47 \bar{k} \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}^{2} . \end{aligned} \)

The values of ρ and \(\bar{e}_{n}\) come from the magnitude and direction of the normal acceleration component,

\(\large\frac{v^{2}}{\rho}=\left(55.38^{2}+23.69^{2}+42.47^{2}\right)^{1 / 2}=73.70 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}^{2},\)

 

\(\large\rho=\frac{v^{2}}{73.70}=3392 \mathrm{~m},\)

 

\(\large\bar{e}_{n}=\frac{-55.38 \bar{i}-23.69 \bar{j}-42.47 \bar{k}}{73.70}=-0.7515 \bar{i}-0.3213 \bar{j}-0.5762 \bar{k} . \)

To locate the center of curvature C we recall Eq. (2.1.9):

\( \large\bar{r}_{\text {center }}=\bar{r}_{P / O}(s)+\rho \bar{e}_{n} \)

 

\( \large\bar{r}_{C / O}=\bar{r}_{A / O}+\rho \bar{e}_{n}=-2549 \bar{i}-1088 \bar{j}-1951 \bar{k} \mathrm{~m} . \)

In general, if a problem involves forces we should draw a free-body diagram. However, the forces in the present case are straightforward. The gravity force is mg in the negative \(\bar{k}\) direction. In addition, there is a force tangent to the path, which changes the speed, and there may be normal and binormal components of a contact force that prevent the particle from moving perpendicularly to the path. Thus Newton’s Second Law is

\( \large\Sigma \bar{F}=-5(9.807) \bar{k}+F_{t} \bar{e}_{t}+F_{n} \bar{e}_{n}+F_{b} \bar{e}_{b}=5\left(\dot{v} \bar{e}_{t}+\frac{v^{2}}{\rho} \bar{e}_{n}\right) . \)

Expressions for \(\large\bar{e}_{t}\) and \(\bar{e}_{n}\) have been found. We evaluate \(\bar{e}_{b}\) from the cross product:

\(\large\bar{e}_{b}=\bar{e}_{t} \times \bar{e}_{n}=0.8734 \bar{j}-0.4871 \bar{k}\) .

To find the force components we use a dot product of the resultant force and each of the unit vectors,

\( \large\begin{array}{c} \Sigma \bar{F} \cdot \bar{e}_{t}=-49.035(-0.6563)+F_{t}=5 \dot{v} ,\\\\ \Sigma \bar{F} \cdot \bar{e}_{n}=-49.035(-0.5763)+F_{n}=5 \frac{v^{2}}{\rho} , \\\\ \Sigma \bar{F} \cdot \bar{e}_{b}=-49.035(-0.4871)+F_{b}=0, \end{array}\)

from which we obtain

\(\large F_{t}=291.3, \quad F_{n}=340.2, \quad F_{b}=-23.9 \mathrm{~N}\) .
