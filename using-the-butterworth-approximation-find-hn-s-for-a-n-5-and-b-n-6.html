Guest Question.

Using the Butterworth approximation, find \(H_{N}(s)\) for (a) n = 5 and (b) n = 6.

Admin
Solution

(a) For n = 5, Eq. (10.19) gives

 

\(z_{i}= \begin{cases}e^{j(2 i-1) \pi / 2 n} & \text { for even } n \\ e^{j(i-1) \pi / n} & \text { for odd } n\end{cases}\) (10.19)

 

\(z_{i}=e^{j(i-1) \pi / 5}\)

 

\(=\cos \frac{(i-1) \pi}{5}+j \sin \frac{(i-1) \pi}{5}\)

 

Hence, the zeros of the loss function are as follows:

 

\(\begin{array}{ll}z_{1}=1.0 \quad &z_{2}=0.809017+j 0.587785 \\z_{3}=0.309017+j 0.951057 & z_{4}=-0.309017+j 0.951057 \\z_{5}=-0.809017+j 0.587785 & z_{6}=-1.0 \\z_{7}=-0.809017-j 0.587785 & \quad z_{8}=-0.309017-j 0.951057 \\z_{9}=0.309017-j 0.951057 & z_{10}=0.809017-j 0.587785\end{array}\)

 

Dropping the right-hand s plane zeros of the loss function, we get

 

\(\begin{aligned}&z_{4}=-0.309017+j 0.951057 \quad z_{5}=-0.809017+j 0.587785 \\&z_{6}=-1.0 \quad z_{7}=-0.809017-j 0.587785 \\&z_{8}=-0.309017-j 0.951057\end{aligned}\)

 

Now if we combine complex conjugate pairs of poles into factors, we obtain

 

\(\begin{aligned}H(s)=& \frac{1}{(s+1)} \cdot \frac{1}{(s+0.309017-j 0.951057)(s+0.309017+j 0.951057)} \\& \cdot \frac{1}{(s+0.809017-j 0.587785)(s+0.809017+j 0.587785)} \\&=\frac{1}{(s+1)} \cdot \frac{1}{\left(s^{2}+0.618034 s+1\right)} \cdot \frac{1}{\left(s^{2}+1.618034 s+1\right)}\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Similarly, for n = 6, we have

 

\(z_{i}=e^{j(2 i-1) \pi / 2 n}\)

 

\(=\cos \frac{(2 i-1) \pi}{12}+j \sin \frac{(2 i-1) \pi}{12}\)

 

Hence

 

\(\begin{array}{ll}z_{1}=0.965928+j 0.258819 & z_{2}=0.707107+j 0.707107 \\z_{3}=0.258819+j 0.965926 & z_{4}=-0.258819+j 0.965926\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{array}{rlr}z_{5} =-0.707107+j 0.707107 & z_{6}=-0.965928+j 0.258819 \\z_{7} =-0.965928-j 0.258819 & z_{8}=-0.707107-j 0.707107 \\z_{9} =-0.258819-j 0.965926 & z_{10}=0.258819-j 0.965926 \\z_{11} =0.707107-j 0.707107 & z_{12}=0.965928-j 0.258819\end{array}\)

 

Dropping the right-hand s plane zeros of the loss function, we get

 

\(\begin{array}{ll}z_{4}=-0.258819+j 0.965926 & z_{5}=-0.707107+j 0.707107 \\z_{6}=-0.965928+j 0.258819 & z_{7}=-0.965928-j 0.258819 \\z_{8}=-0.707107-j 0.707107 & z_{9}=-0.258819-j 0.965926\end{array}\)

 

Now if we combine complex conjugate pairs of poles into factors, we obtain

 

\(H(s)=\frac{1}{(s+0.258819-j 0.965926)(s+0.258819+j 0.965926)}\)

 

\(\frac{1}{(s+0.707107-j 0.707107)(s+0.707107+j 0.707107)}\)

 

\(\frac{1}{(s+0.965928-j 0.258819)(s+0.965928+j 0.258819)}\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{\left(s^{2}+0.517638 s+1\right)} \cdot \frac{1}{\left(s^{2}+1.414214 s+1\right)}\)

 

\(\frac{1}{\left(s^{2}+1.931852 s+1\right)}\)

 

The zero-pole plots of the loss function for the two examples are shown in Fig. 10.8.
