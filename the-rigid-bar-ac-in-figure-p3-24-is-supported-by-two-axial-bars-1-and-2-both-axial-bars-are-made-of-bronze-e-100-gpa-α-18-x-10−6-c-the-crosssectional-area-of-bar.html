Guest Question.

The rigid bar AC in Figure P3.24 is supported by two axial bars (1) and (2). Both axial bars are made of bronze [E = 100 GPa; α = 18 × \(10^{-6}\) /°C]. The crosssectional area of bar (1) is \(A_{1}= 240 mm ^{2}\) and the cross-sectional area of bar (2) is \(A_{2} = 360 mm ^{2}\) . After load P has been applied and the temperature of the entire assembly has increased by 30°C, the total strain in bar (2) is measured as 1,220 με
(elongation). Determine:
(a) the magnitude of load P.
(b) the vertical displacement of pin A.

Admin
Solution

(a) The total strain in bar (2) is caused partly by the axial force in the bar and partly by the increase in temperature. The strain caused by the 30°C temperature increase is:

\(\varepsilon_{T}=\alpha \Delta T=\left(18 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(30^{\circ} C \right)=0.000540 mm / mm\)

The strain caused by the axial force in the bar is thus:

\(\varepsilon_{2, \sigma}=\varepsilon_{2}-\varepsilon_{2, T}=0.001220 mm / mm -0.000540 mm / mm =0.000680 mm / mm\)

The stress in bar (2) is

\(\sigma_{2}=E_{2} \varepsilon_{2, \sigma}=(100,000 MPa )(0.000680 mm / mm )=68 MPa\)

and the force in bar (2) is

\(F_{2}=\sigma_{2} A_{2}=\left(68 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(360 mm ^{2}\right)=24,480 N\)

Next, consider a FBD of the rigid bar AC. Equilibrium equations for this FBD are:

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma F_{y}=F_{1}+F_{2}-P=0 \\&\Sigma M_{A}=(1,400 mm ) F_{2}-(500 mm ) P=0\end{aligned}\)

which can be solve simultaneously to give:

\(F_{2}=\frac{500 mm }{1,400 mm } P=0.357143 P\)

and

\(F_{1}=0.642857 P\)

The applied load P can be expressed in terms of \(F_{2}\) as

\(P=\frac{1}{0.357143} F_{2}=2.8 F_{2}\)

and so the magnitude of load P is

\(P=2.8 F_{2}=2.8(24,480 N )=68,544 N =68.5 kN\)

 

(b) The force in bar (1) is

\(F_{1}=0.642857 P=(0.642857)(68,544 N )=44,064 N\)

Thus, the stress in bar (1) is

\(\sigma_{1}=\frac{F_{1}}{A_{1}}=\frac{44,064 N }{240 mm ^{2}}=183.60 MPa\)

The normal strain due to the axial force in bar (1) is

\(\varepsilon_{1, \sigma}=\frac{\sigma_{1}}{E_{1}}=\frac{183.60 MPa }{100,000 MPa }=0.001836 mm / mm\)

The normal strain caused by the 30°C temperature increase is:

\(\varepsilon_{1, T}=\alpha \Delta T=\left(18 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(30^{\circ} C \right)=0.000540 mm / mm\)

Therefore, the total strain in bar (1) is

\(\varepsilon_{1}=\varepsilon_{1, \sigma}+\varepsilon_{1, T}=0.001836 mm / mm +0.000540 mm / mm =0.002376 mm / mm\)

and the elongation in bar (1) is

\(\delta_{1}=\varepsilon_{1, \sigma} L_{1}=(0.002376 mm / mm )(1,300 mm )=3.0888 mm\)

Since rigid bar ABC is connected to bar (1) (with a perfect connection), joint A displaces downward by an amount equal to the elongation of bar (1); therefore,

\(v_{A}=\delta_{1}=3.09 mm \downarrow\)
