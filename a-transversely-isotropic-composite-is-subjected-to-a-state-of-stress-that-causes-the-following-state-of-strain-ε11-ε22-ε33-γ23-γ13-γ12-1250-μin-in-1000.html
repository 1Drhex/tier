Guest Question.

A transversely isotropic composite is subjected to a state of stress that causes the following state of strain:

\(\left\{\begin{matrix} \varepsilon _{11} \\ \varepsilon _{22} \\ \varepsilon _{33} \\ \gamma _{23} \\ \gamma _{13} \\ \gamma _{12} \end{matrix} \right\}=\left \{ \begin{matrix} -1250μin./in. \\-1000μin./in. \\ -500μin./in. \\ -2500μrad\\ -1000μrad\\ 2000μrad \end{matrix} \right \}\)

Determine the stresses that caused these strains (use material properties listed in ).

Admin
Solution

As the composite is transversely isotropic, stresses are calculated using Equation 4.23.

\(\left\{\begin{matrix} \sigma _{11} \\ \sigma _{22} \\ \sigma _{33} \\ \tau _{23} \\ \tau _{13} \\ \tau _{12} \end{matrix} \right\}=\left [ \begin{matrix} C_{11} & C_{12}& C_{12} & 0 &0 & 0 \\C_{12} &C_{22} &C_{23} & 0 & 0 & 0 \\C_{12} & C_{23}&C_{22}&0 &0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & (C_{22}-C_{23})/2 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 &C_{66} & 0 \\0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 &C_{66} \end{matrix} \right ] \left\{\begin{matrix}\varepsilon _{11} \\ \varepsilon _{22}\\\varepsilon _{33} \\ \gamma _{23}\\ \gamma _{13}\\ \gamma _{12} \end{matrix} \right\} \) (4.23)

The stiffness matrix can be obtained by (a) inverting the compliance matrix determined as a part of , (b) through the use of Equation 4.24,

\(\begin{matrix} C_{11}=\frac{S_{22}+S_{33}}{\Omega } \quad C_{12}=\frac{-S_{12}}{\Omega } \quad C_{22}=\frac{S_{11}S_{22}- S_{12}^2}{\Omega (S_{22}-S_{23})} \\ C_{23}=\frac{S_{12}^2-S_{11}S_{23}}{\Omega (S_{22}-S_{23})} \quad C_{66}= \frac{1}{S_{66}} \end{matrix}\) (4.24)

or (c) through the use of Equation 4.25.

\(C_{11} = \frac{E_{11}^2(1 -\nu _{23})}{\Omega } C_{12}=\frac{\nu _{12}E_{11}E_{22}}{\Omega } C_{22}=\frac{E_{22}(E_{11}-\nu _{12}^2E_{22})}{\Omega (1 +\nu _{23})} C_{23}= \frac{E_{22}(\nu _{23}E_{11}+\nu _{12}^2E_{22})}{\Omega (1+\nu _{23})} C_{44}= \frac{C_{22}-C_{23}}{2}=\frac{E_{22}}{2(1 +\nu _{23})} C_{66}= G_{12} \) (4.25)

All three methods are entirely equivalent, and which procedure is selected for use is simply a matter of convenience. Equation 4.25 will be used in this example:

\(\Omega =E_{11}(1 – \nu _{23})-2\nu^2_{12}E_{22}\)

 

\(\Omega =(25Msi)(1 − 0.40) − 2(0.30)^2(1.5Msi) = 14.73 Msi\)

 

\(C_{11} = \frac{E_{11}^2(1 -\nu _{23})}{\Omega }=\frac{(25Msi)^2(1 – 0.40)}{14.73 Msi}= 25.46 Msi\)

 

\(C_{12}=\frac{\nu _{12}E_{11}E_{22}}{\Omega }=\frac{(0.30)(25 Msi)(1.5 Msi)}{14.73 Msi}= 0.7637 Msi\)

 

\(C_{22}=\frac{E_{22}(E_{11}-\nu _{12}^2E_{22})}{\Omega (1 +\nu _{23})}=\frac{(1.5 Msi)\left\{(25 Msi)-(0.30)^2(1.5 Msi)\right\} }{14.73 Msi(1 + 0.40)}= 1.809 Msi\)

 

\(C_{23}= \frac{E_{22}(\nu _{23}E_{11}+\nu _{12}^2E_{22})}{\Omega (1+\nu _{23})}= \frac{(1.5Msi)\left\{(0.40)(25 Msi)+ (0.30)^2(1.5 Msi)\right\} }{14.73 Msi(1 + 0.40)} = 0.7372 Msi\)

 

\(C_{44}=\frac{E_{22}}{2(1 +\nu _{23})}=\frac{(1.5 Msi)}{2(1 +0.40)}=0.5357 Msi\)

 

\(C_{66} = G_{12}=2.0 Msi\)

Applying Equation 4.23, the stresses are

\(\left\{\begin{matrix} \sigma _{11} \\ \sigma _{22} \\ \sigma _{33} \\ \tau _{23} \\ \tau _{13} \\ \tau _{12} \end{matrix} \right\}=\left [ \begin{matrix} 25.46 & 0.7637& 0.7637 & 0 &0 & 0 \\0.7637 &1.809 &0.7372 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\0.7637 &0.7372&1.809&0 &0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0.5357 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 &2.0 & 0 \\0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 &2.0 \end{matrix} \right ](Msi) \left\{\begin{matrix}-1250μin./in \\ -1000μin./n\\-500μin./in \\ -2500μrad\\ -1000μrad\\ 2000μrad \end{matrix} \right\}\)

 

\(\left\{\begin{matrix} \sigma _{11} \\ \sigma _{22} \\ \sigma _{33} \\\tau _{23} \\ \tau _{13} \\ \tau _{12} \end{matrix} \right\}=\left \{ \begin{matrix} -32.97Ksi \\-3.13Ksi \\ -2.60Ksi \\ -1.34Ksi\\ -2.00Ksi\\ 4.00Ksi\end{matrix} \right \}\)
