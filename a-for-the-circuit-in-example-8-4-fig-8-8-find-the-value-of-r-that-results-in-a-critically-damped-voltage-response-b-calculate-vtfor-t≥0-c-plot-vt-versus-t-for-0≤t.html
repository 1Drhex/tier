Guest Question.

a) For the circuit in (Fig. 8.8), find the value of R that results in a critically damped voltage response

b) Calculate \( v\left(t\right)\) for t≥0.

c) Plot \( v\left(t\right)\) versus t for 0≤t≤7 ms.

Admin
Solution

a) From , we know that \(w^{2}_{0}=10^{6}\).Therefore for critical damping

 

\(\alpha=10^{3} =\frac{1}{2RC}\),

 

or

 

\(R =\frac{10^{6}}{\left(2000\right) \left(0.125\right) } =4000\Omega \).

 

b) From the solution of Example 8.4, we know that \(v\left(0^{+}\right)=0\) and \(\frac{dv\left(0^{+}\right)}{dt}=98,000\frac{V}{s}\)From Eqs. 8.35 \(v\left(0^{+}\right)=V_{0}=D_{2}\) and 8.36\(\frac{dv\left(0^{+}\right)}{dt}=\frac{i_{C}\left(0^{+}\right)}{C}=D_{1}-\alpha D_{2}\),\(D_{2} =0\) and \( D_{1} = 98,000\frac{V}{s}\) .

 

Substituting these values for a,\( D_{1}\) and \(D_{2} \) into Eq. 8.34 \(v\left(t\right) = D_{1}t e^{-\alpha t}+D_{2} e^{-\alpha t}\) gives

 

\(v\left(t\right) =98,000 t e^{-1000t}\) V,t≥0.

 

c) Figure 8.10 shows a plot of \(v\left(t\right)\) versus t in the interval 0≤t≤7 ms.
