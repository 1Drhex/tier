Guest Question.

A ratio control scheme is to be used to maintain a stoichiometric ratio of \(H_{2} \space and \space N_{2}\) as the feed to an ammonia synthesis reactor. Individual flow controllers will be used for both the \(H_{2} \space and \space N_{2}\) streams. Using the information given below,
(a) Draw a schematic diagram for the ratio control scheme.
(b) Specify the appropriate gain for the ratio station, \(K_{R}\).

Available information:
(i) The electronic flow transmitters have built-in square root extractors. The spans of the flow transmitters are 30 L/min for \(H_{2}\) and 15 L/min for \(N_{2}\).
(ii) The control valves have pneumatic actuators.
(iii) Each required current-to-pressure (I/P) transducer has a gain of 0.75 psi/mA.
(iv) The ratio station is an electronic instrument with 4–20 mA input and output signals.

Admin
Solution

The stoichiometric equation for the ammonia synthesis reaction is
\(3H_{2} + N_{2} ⇄ 2NH_{3}\)
In order to introduce a feed mixture in toichiometric proportions, the ratio of the molar flow rates (\(H_{2}/N_{2}\)) should be 3:1. For the sake of simplicity, we assume that the ratio of the molar flow rates is equal to the ratio of the volumetric flow rates. But, in general, the volumetric flow rates also depend on the temperature and pressure of each stream (cf. the ideal gas law).

(a) The schematic diagram for the ammonia synthesis reaction is shown in Figure below. The \(H_{2}\) flow rate is considered to be the disturbance variable, although this choice is arbitrary, because both the \(H_{2} \space and \space N_{2}\) flow rates are controlled. Note that the ratio station is merely a device with an adjustable gain. The input signal to the ratio station is \(d_{m}\), the measured \(H_{2}\) flow rate. Its output signal \(u_{sp}\) serves as the set point for the \(N_{2}\) flow control loop. It is calculated as \(u_{sp} = K_{R}d_{m}\).

(b) From the stoichiometric equation, it follows that the desired ratio is \(R_{d} = u/d = 1/3\). Substitution into Equation
\(K_{R}=R_{d}\frac{S_{d}}{S_{u}} \).
gives
\(K_{R}=(\frac{1}{3} )(\frac{30L/min}{15L/min} )=\frac{2}{3} \)
