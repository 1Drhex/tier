Guest Question.

If the flow rate through a 10-cm-diameter wrought iron pipe (Fig. E7.15) is \(0.04 m^3/s\), find the difference in elevation H of the two reservoirs.

Admin
Solution

The energy equation written for a control volume that contains the two reservoir surfaces (see Eq. 4.5.17), where \(V_{1}=V_{2}=0\) and \(p_{1}=p_{2}=0\), is

\(-\frac{\dot{W}_{S}}{\dot{m} g}=\frac{V_{2}^{2}-V_{1}^{2}}{2 g}+\frac{p_{2}}{\gamma_{2}}-\frac{p_{1}}{\gamma_{1}}+z_{2}-z_{1}+h_{L} (4.5.17)\)

\(0=z_{2}-z_{1}+h_{L}\)

Thus, letting \(z_{1}-z_{2}=H\), we have

\(H=\left(K_{\text {entrance }}+K_{\text {valve }}+2 K_{\text {elbow }}+K_{\text {exit }}\right) \frac{V^{2}}{2 g}+f \frac{L}{D} \frac{V^{2}}{2 g}\)

The average velocity, Reynolds number, and relative roughness are

\(\begin{array}{c}V=\frac{Q}{A}=\frac{0.04}{\pi \times 0.05^{2}}=5.09 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s} \\\mathrm{Re}=\frac{V D}{\nu}=\frac{5.09 \times 0.1}{10^{-6}}=5.09 \times 10^{5} \\\frac{e}{D}=\frac{0.046}{100}=0.00046\end{array}\)

From the Moody diagram we find that

\(f=0.0173\)

Using the loss coefficients from Table \(7.2\) for an entrance, a globe valve, screwed \(10-\mathrm{cm}-\) diameter standard elbows, and an exit there results

\(\begin{aligned}H &=(0.5+5.7+2 \times 0.64+1.0) \frac{5.09^{2}}{2 \times 9.8}+0.0173 \frac{50}{0.1} \frac{5.09^{2}}{2 \times 9.8} \\&=11.2+11.4=22.6 \mathrm{~m}\end{aligned}\)

Note: The minor losses are about equal to the frictional losses as expected, since there are five minor loss elements in 500 diameters of pipe length.
