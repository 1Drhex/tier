Guest Question.

Consider an ideal Stirling-cycle engine in which the pressure and temperature at the beginning of the isothermal compression process are 14.7 \(lbf / in .^{2}\), 80 F, the compression ratio is 6, and the maximum temperature in the cycle is 2000 F. Calculate the maximum pressure in the cycle and the thermal efficiency of the cycle with and without regenerators.

Admin
Solution

Ideal Stirling cycle

\(\begin{aligned}& T _{1}= T _{2}=80 F \\& P _{1}=14.7 lbf / in ^{2} \\&\frac{ v _{1}}{ v _{2}}=6 \\& T _{3}= T _{4}=2000 F\end{aligned}\)

Isothermal compression (heat goes out)

\(\begin{aligned}& T _{1}= T _{2} \rightarrow P _{2}= P _{1} \times v _{1} / v _{2}=14.7 \times 6=88.2 \\& w _{12}= q _{12}=- RT _{1} \ln \frac{ v _{1}}{ v _{2}}=-\frac{53.34}{778} \times 540 \ln 6=-66.3 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\)

Constant volume heat addition

\(\begin{aligned}& V _{2}= V _{3} \rightarrow P _{3}= P _{2} \times T _{3} / T _{2}=88.2 \times \frac{2460}{540}= 4 0 1 . 8 lbf / in ^{2} \\& q _{23}= C _{ V 0}\left( T _{3}- T _{2}\right)=0.171(2460-540)=328.3 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\)

Isothermal expansion (heat comes in)

\(\begin{aligned}&\begin{aligned}w _{34} &= q _{34}= R T _{3} \ln \left( v _{4} / v _{3}\right) \\&=(53.34 / 778) \times 2460 ln 6=302.2 Btu / lbm \\w _{ NET } &=302.2-66.3=235.9 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\end{aligned}\)

Efficiency without regenerator, (\(q _{23} \text { and } q _{34}\) are coming in from source)

\(\eta_{\text {NO REGEN }}=\frac{235.9}{302.2+328.3}=0.374\),

Efficiency with regenerator, (Now only \(q _{34}\) is coming in from source)

\(\eta_{\text {WITH REGEN }}=\frac{235.9}{302.2}= 0 . 7 8 1\)
