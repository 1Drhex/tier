Guest Question.

Consider the dynamical system:

\(\vec{x}(t+1)=\left[\begin{array}{ll} 3 & -5 \\ 1 & -1 \end{array}\right] \vec{x}(t)\) with initial state \(\vec{x}_{0}=\left[\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] \).

Use Theorem 7.6.3 to find a closed formula for \(\vec{x}(t)\) and sketch the trajectory.

Admin
Solution

In of Section 7.5, we found the eigenvalues:

\(\lambda_{1,2}=1 \pm i\)

The polar coordinates of eigenvalue \( 1+i \) are \(r=\sqrt{2} \text { and } \theta=\frac{\pi}{4}\) .Furthermore, we found that:

\(S=\left[\begin{array}{ll} \vec{w} & \vec{v} \end{array}\right] =\left[\begin{array}{ll} 0 & -5 \\ 1 & -2 \end{array}\right]\)

Since:

\(S^{-1} \vec{x}_{0}=\left[\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]\)

Theorem 7.6.3 gives:

\(\vec{x}(t) =(\sqrt{2})^{t}\left[\begin{array}{ll} 0 & -5 \\ 1 & -2 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{ll} \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) & -\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) \\ \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) & \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]\)

 

\(\qquad =(\sqrt{2})^{t}\left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) \\ \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{4} t\right)-2 \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) \end{array}\right]\)

We leave it to the reader to work out the details of this computation.
Next, letâ€™s think about the trajectory. We will develop the trajectory step by step:

  • The points:
\(\left[\begin{array}{rr} \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) & -\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) \\ \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) & \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] \quad \text { (for } t=0,1,2, \ldots)\)

are located on the unit circle, as shown in Figure 3a. Note that at \(t = 8\) the system returns to its initial position, \(\left[\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]\) ; the period of this system is 8 .

  • In Exercise 2.2.50, we saw that an invertible linear transformation maps the unit circle into an ellipse. Thus, the points:
\(\left[\begin{array}{ll} 0 & -5 \\ 1 & -2 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{lr} \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) & -\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) \\ \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) & \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) \end{array}\right]\left[\begin {array}{l} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]\)

are located on an ellipse, as shown in Figure 3b. The two column vectors of:

\( S=\left[\begin{array}{ll} 0 & -5 \\ 1 & -2 \end{array}\right] =\left[\begin{array}{ll} \vec{w} & \vec{v} \end{array}\right]\)

are shown in that figure as well. Again, the period of this system is 8.

  • The exponential growth factor \((\sqrt{2}) ^{t}\) will produce longer and longer vectors:
\(\vec{x}(t)=(\sqrt{2})^{t}\left [\begin{array}{ll} 0 & -5 \\ 1 & -2 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin {array}{lr} \cos \left (\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) & -\sin \left (\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) \\ \sin \left (\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) & \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{4} t\right) \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]\)

Thus, the trajectory spirals outward, as shown in Figure 3c. (We are using different scales in Figures 3a, b, and c.) Note that \(\vec{x}(8)=(\sqrt{2})^{8} \vec{x}(0)=16 \vec{x}(0)\) .

Theorem 7.6.3

\( \vec{x}(t)=r^{t} S\left[\begin{array}{cc} \cos (\theta t) & -\sin (\theta t) \\ \sin (\theta t) & \cos (\theta t) \end{array}\right] S^{-1} \vec{x}_{0}, \quad \text { where } \quad S=\left[\begin{array}{ll} \vec{w} & \vec{v} \end{array}\right] \)
