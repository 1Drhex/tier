Guest Question.

A motor supplies 60 kW at 5 Hz to flange A of the shaft shown in Figure P6.49/50. Gear B transfers 40 kW of power to operating machinery in the factory, and the remaining power in the shaft is transferred by gear D. Shafts (1) and (2) are solid 65-mm-diameter aluminum [G = 28 GPa] shafts, and shaft (3) is a solid 40-mm-diameter steel [G = 80 GPa] shaft. Calculate:
(a) the maximum shear stress in the aluminum shafts.
(b) the maximum shear stress in the steel shaft.
(c) the rotation angle of gear D with respect to flange A.

Admin
Solution

Power transmission and torques: The torque acting on flange A is:

\(T_{A}=\frac{P}{\omega}=\frac{60,000 N – m / s }{\left(\frac{5 rev }{ s }\right)\left(\frac{2 \pi rad }{1 rev }\right)}=1,909.859 N – m\)

The torque on shaft segment (1) is equal to the torque applied to flange A.

\(T_{1}=T_{A}=1,909.859 N – m\)

After gear B transfers 40 kW of power, the remaining power in the system is 20 kW. Therefore, the torque available at gear D is:

\(T_{D}=\frac{P}{\omega}=\frac{20,000 N – m / s }{\left(\frac{5 rev }{ s }\right)\left(\frac{2 \pi rad }{1 rev }\right)}=636.620 N – m\)

The torque in segments (2) and (3) is equal to the torque at gear D:

\(T_{2}=T_{3}=T_{D}=636.620 N – m\)

Section properties: The polar moments of inertia of aluminum shafts (1) and (2) and of steel shaft (3) are:

\(\begin{aligned}&J_{1}=\frac{\pi}{32} d_{1}^{4}=\frac{\pi}{32}(65 mm )^{4}=1,752,481.011 mm ^{4}=J_{2} \\&J_{3}=\frac{\pi}{32} d_{3}^{4}=\frac{\pi}{32}(40 mm )^{4}=251,327.412 mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

(a) Maximum shear stress in aluminum shafts:

\(\tau_{1}=\frac{T_{1} c_{1}}{J_{1}}=\frac{(1,909.859 N – m )(65 mm / 2)(1,000 mm / m )}{1,752,481.011 mm ^{4}}=35.419 MPa =35.4 MPa\)

(b) Maximum shear stress in steel shaft:

\(\tau_{3}=\frac{T_{3} c_{3}}{J_{3}}=\frac{(636.620 N – m )(40 mm / 2)(1,000 mm / m )}{251,327.412 mm ^{4}}=50.661 MPa =50.7 MPa\)

(c) Angles of twist: The angles of twist in the three shaft segments are:

\(\begin{aligned}&\phi_{1}=\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}=\frac{(1,909.859 N – m )(800 mm )(1,000 mm / m )}{\left(1,752,481.011 mm ^{4}\right)\left(28,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=0.031137 rad \\&\phi_{2}=\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}=\frac{(636.620 N – m )(400 mm )(1,000 mm / m )}{\left(1,752,481.011 mm ^{4}\right)\left(28,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=0.005190 rad \\&\phi_{3}=\frac{T_{3} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}=\frac{(636.620 N – m )(800 mm )(1,000 mm / m )}{\left(251,327.412 mm ^{4}\right)\left(80,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=0.025330 rad\end{aligned}\)

Rotation angle of D relative to flange A:

\(\begin{aligned}\phi_{D}-\phi_{A} &=\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}+\phi_{3} \\&=0.031137 rad +0.005190 rad +0.025330 rad \\&=0.061657 rad =0.0617 rad\end{aligned}\)
