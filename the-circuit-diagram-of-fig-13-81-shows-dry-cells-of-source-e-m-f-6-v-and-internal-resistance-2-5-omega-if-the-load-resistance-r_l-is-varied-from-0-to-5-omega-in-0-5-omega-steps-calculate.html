Guest Question.

The circuit diagram of Fig. 13.81 shows dry cells of source e.m.f. 6 V, and internal resistance 2.5 \(\Omega\). If the load resistance \(R_{L}\) is varied from 0 to 5 \(\Omega\) in 0.5 \(\Omega\) steps, calculate the power dissipated by the load in each case. Plot a graph of \(R_{L}\) (horizontally) against power (vertically) and determine the maximum power dissipated.

Admin
Solution

When \(R_{L} = 0\), current \(I = E/(r + R_{L}) = 6/2.5 = 2.4\, A\) and power dissipated in \(R_{L},\, P = I^{2}R_{L}\) i.e. \(P = (2.4)^{2}(0) = 0\, W\).
When \(R_{L} = 0.5 \, \Omega\), current \(I = E/(r + R_{L}) = 6/(2.5 + 0.5) = 2\, A\) and \(P = I^{2}R_{L} = (2)^{2}(0.5) = 2.00\, W\).
When \(R_{L} = 1.0\, \Omega\), current \(I = 6/(2.5 + 1.0) = 1.714\, A\) and \(P = (1.714)^{2}(1.0) = 2.94\, W\).
With similar calculations the following table is
produced:

 

2.5 2.0 1.5 1.0 0.5 0 \(R_{L}\, (\Omega)\)
1.2 1.333 1.5 1.714 2.0 2.4 \(I=\frac{E}{r+R_{L}}\)
3.60 3.56 3.38 2.94 2.00 0 \(P=I^{2}R_{L}\: (W)\)
5.0 4.5 4.0 3.5 3.0 \(R_{L}\, (\Omega)\)
0.8 0.857 0.923 1.0 1.091 \(I=\frac{E}{r+R_{L}}\)
3.20 3.31 3.41 3.50 3.57 \(P=I^{2}R_{L}\: (W)\)

 

A graph of \(R_{L}\) against \(P\) is shown in Fig. 13.82.
\(\textbf{The maximum value of power is 3.60 W}\) which occurs when \(R_{L}\) is \(2.5\, \Omega\), i.e. \(\textbf{maximum power occurs when } R_{L} = r\), which is what the maximum power transfer theorem states.
