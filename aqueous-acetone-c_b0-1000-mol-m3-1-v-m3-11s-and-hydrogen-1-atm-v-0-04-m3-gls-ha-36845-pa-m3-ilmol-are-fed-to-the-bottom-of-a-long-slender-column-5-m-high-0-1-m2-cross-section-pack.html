Guest Question.

Aqueous acetone \(\left(C_{\mathrm{B} 0}=1000 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{m}^{3} l, v_{l}=10^{-4} \mathrm{~m}^{3} l / \mathrm{s}\right)\) and hydrogen (1 atm, \(\left.v_{g}=0.04 \mathrm{~m}^{3} \mathrm{~g} / \mathrm{s}, H_{\mathrm{A}}=36845 \mathrm{~Pa} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} l / \mathrm{mol}\right)\) are fed to the bottom of a long,slender column (5-m high, \(0.1-\mathrm{m}^{2}\) cross section) packed with porous Raney nickel \(\text { catalyst }\left(d_{p}=5 \times 10^{-3} \mathrm{~m} \text { cat, } \rho_{s}=4500 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\right. \text { cat, } f_{s}=0.6, \mathscr{D}_{e}=8 \times 10^{-10} \mathrm{~m}^{3}\) \(l / \mathrm{m}\) cat \(\cdot \mathrm{s}\) ) and kept at \(14^{\circ} \mathrm{C},\) as shown in Fig. E22.1. At these conditions acetone is hydrogenated to propanol according to the reaction

\(\begin{array}{l}\mathrm{H}_{2}(g \rightarrow l)+\mathrm{CH}_{3} \mathrm{COCH}_{3}(l) \underset{\text { catalyst }}{\longrightarrow} \mathrm{CH}_{3} \mathrm{CHOHCH}_{3} \\\begin{array}{l}-- \\\text { (A) }\end{array}\end{array}\)

with rate given by

\(-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime}=-r_{\mathrm{B}}^{\prime}=k^{\prime} C_{\mathrm{A}}^{1 / 2} C_{\mathrm{B}}^{0} \quad \text { and } . . k^{\prime}=2.35 \times 10^{-3} \frac{\mathrm{m}^{3} l}{\mathrm{~kg} \cdot \mathrm{s}}\left(\frac{\mathrm{mol}}{\mathrm{m}^{3} l}\right)^{1 / 2}\)

What will be the conversion of acetone in this unit?

Additional Data

The mass transfer rate constants are estimated to be

\(\left(k_{\mathrm{A} i} \mathrm{a}_{i) g+l}=0.02 \frac{\mathrm{m}^{3} l}{\mathrm{~m}^{3} r \cdot \mathrm{s}} \quad k_{\mathrm{Ac}} a_{c}=0.05 \frac{\mathrm{m}^{3} l}{\mathrm{~m}^{3} \mathrm{r} \cdot \mathrm{s}})\right.\)
Admin
Solution

Before rushing to our equations to do the appropriate integrations needed for plug flow, let us consider the situation

– \(C_{\mathrm{B} 0}=1000\) while \(C_{\mathrm{A}}\) is given by Henry’s law as

\(C_{\mathrm{A}}=\frac{p_{\mathrm{A}}}{H_{\mathrm{A}}}=\frac{101325}{36845}=2.75 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{m}^{3} l\)

Comparing values shows that \(C_{\mathrm{B}} \gg C_{\mathrm{A}} ;\) hence, we are in extreme 1 (excess of \(\mathrm{B}\) ).

– Next, we are dealing with pure hydrogen; thus, \(p_{\mathrm{A}}\) is constant throughout the packed column. And since the rate is only dependent on \(C_{\mathrm{A}}\) and not on \(C_{\mathrm{B}},\) this means that the rate of reaction is constant throughout the column.

Let us next look at the rate. From Eq. 18.28 we have for a half order reaction

\(\begin{aligned}M_{T} &=L \sqrt{\frac{n+1}{2} \cdot \frac{k^{\prime} C_{\mathrm{A}}^{n-1} \rho_{s}}{\mathscr{D}_{e}}} \\&=\frac{5 \times 10^{-3}}{6} \sqrt{\frac{1.5}{2} \cdot \frac{\left(2.35 \times 10^{-3}\right)(2.75)^{-1 / 2}(4500)}{8 \times 10^{-10}}}=64.4 \\\therefore \mathscr{E} &=\frac{1}{64.4}=0.0155\end{aligned}\)

Replacing all known values into Eq. 18 gives

\({{-r}''}''_{A}=\frac{1}{\frac{1}{H_{A}k_{Ag}a_{i}}+\frac{1}{k_{Al}a_{i}}+\frac{1}{k_{Ac}a_{c}}+\frac{1}{{({k}''}'_{A}\overline{C}^{m}_{B}\overline{C}^{n-1}_{A})\varepsilon _{A}.f_{s}}}\frac{P_{A}}{H_{A}}\) (Eq 18)

 

\(-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime \prime \prime \prime}=\frac{1}{\frac{1}{0.02}+\frac{1}{0.05}+\frac{1}{\left(2.35 \times 10^{-3}\right)(1)(2.75)^{-1 / 2}(0.0155)(0.6)(4500)}} \frac{101325}{36845}\)

 

\(=0.0317 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{m}^{3} \mathrm{r} \cdot \mathrm{s}\)

Next, to the material balance of Eq. 11. With constant rate this becomes

\(F_{A0}X_{A,exit}=\frac{Vl}{b}(-\frac{dC_{B}}{dt})=({{-r}''}''_{A})V_{r}\) (Eq 11)

\(F_{\mathrm{A} 0} X_{\mathrm{A}}=\frac{F_{\mathrm{B} 0} X_{\mathrm{B}}}{b}=\left(-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime \prime \prime}\right) V_{r}\)

Thus on rearranging

\(X_{\mathrm{B}}=\frac{b\left(-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime \prime \prime}\right) V_{r}}{F_{\mathrm{B} 0}}=\frac{(1)(0.0317)(5 \times 0.1)}{0.1}\)

\(=0.158,\) or \(16 \%\) conversion
