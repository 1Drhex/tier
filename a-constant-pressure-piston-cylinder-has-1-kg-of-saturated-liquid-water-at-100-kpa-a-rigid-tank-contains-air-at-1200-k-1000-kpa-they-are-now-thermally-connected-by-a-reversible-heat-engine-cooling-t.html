Guest Question.

A constant pressure piston/cylinder has 1 kg of saturated liquid water at 100 kPa. A rigid tank contains air at 1200 K, 1000 kPa. They are now thermally connected by a reversible heat engine cooling the air tank and boiling the water to saturated vapor. Find the required amount of air and the work out of the heat engine.

 

Admin
Solution

CV. Tank, piston/cyl. and the heat engine.
The minimum amount of air is when \(T _{2}\) of air equals water T = 99.62 C.

State: 1 = initial air
2 = final air
3 = initial water
4 = final water

 

For this CV only \(W _{ HE } \text { and } W _{\text {pist }}\) cross the control surface no heat transfer. The entropy equation Eq.6.3 and Eq.6.37 for a reversible process becomes

\(\begin{array}{l}\left( S _{2}- S _{1}\right)_{ tot }= m _{ air }\left( s _{2}- s _{1}\right)+ m _{ H 2 O }\left( s _{4}- s _{3}\right)=0+0 \\m _{ H 2 O }\left( s _{4}- s _{3}\right)= m _{ H 2 O } s _{ fg }=1 kg \times 6.0568 kJ / kg – K =6.0568 kJ / K \\s _{2}- s _{1}= C _{ v } \ln \frac{ T _{2}}{ T _{1}}+ R \ln \frac{ v _{2}}{ v _{1}}= C _{ v } \ln \frac{ T _{2}}{ T _{1}}=0.717 \ln \frac{372.77}{1200} \\=-0.838256 kJ / kg – K\end{array}\)

 

Now solve for the air mass from entropy equation

\(m _{ air }=- m _{ H 2 O }\left( s _{4}- s _{3}\right) /\left( s _{2}- s _{1}\right)=-\frac{6.0568}{-0.838256}=7.2255 kg\)

 

Let us find the two heat transfers

Energy Eq, water: \(m_{ H 2 O }\left( u _{4}- u _{3}\right)= Q _{ H 2 O }- W _{ H 2 O } ; \quad W _{ H 2 O }=\operatorname{Pm}\left( v _{4}- v _{3}\right)\)

\(Q _{ H 2 O }= m _{ H 2 O }\left( u _{4}- u _{3}\right)+ W _{ H 2 O }= m _{ H 2 O }\left( h _{4}- h _{3}\right)= m _{ H 2 O } h _{ fg }=2258.02 kJ\)

Energy Eq. air: \(m _{ air }\left( u _{2}- u _{1}\right)=- Q _{ air }-0= m _{ air } C _{ v }\left( T _{2}- T _{1}\right)\)

\(Q _{ air }=7.2255 kg 0.717(1200-372.77)=4285.6 kJ\)

Now the work out of the heat engine is

\(W _{ HE }= Q _{ air }- Q _{ H 2 O }=4285.6-2258.02= 2 0 2 7 . 6 k J\)
