Guest Question.

Finding the performance of an inverting amplifier with shunt-series feedback The parameters of the shunt-series feedback amplifier shown in Fig. 10.10(b) are \( R_{ L }=5 \Omega, R_{ s }=2.5 k \Omega, R_{ F }=200 \Omega, \text { and } R_{1}=5 \Omega \). The op-amp parameters are \( R_{ i }=2 M \Omega, R_{ o }=75 \Omega \text {, and } \mu_{ g }=2 \times 10^{5} \text {. } \)

(a) Determine the input resistance at the op-amp input \( R_{ if }=v_{ i } / i_{ i } \), the output resistance \( R_{\text {of }} \), and the closed-loop current gain \( A_{ f }=i_{ o } / i_{ i } \).
(b) Use PSpice/SPICE to check your results.

Admin
Solution

\( R_{ s }=2.5 k \Omega, R_{ F }=200 \Omega, R_{1}=5 \Omega, R_{ L }=5 \Omega, R_{ i }=2 M \Omega, R_{ o }=75 \Omega, \text { and } \mu_{ g }=2 \times 10^{5} \).Replacing the op-amp by its equivalent circuit gives the amplifier shown in Fig. 10.45(a).

(a) Converting the voltage-controlled voltage source to a current-controlled current source, we get

\( i_{ o }=\frac{\mu_{ g } v_{ i }}{R_{ o }}=\frac{\mu_{ g } R_{ i } i_{ e }}{R_{ o }}=A i_{ e } \)

which gives

\( A=\frac{\mu_{ g } R_{ i }}{R_{ o }}=\frac{2 \times 10^{5} \times 2 \times 10^{6}}{75}=53.33 \times 10^{8} A / A \)

The effect of the feedback network is taken into account by severing \( R_{1} \) from the load at side 2 and shorting \( R_{ F } \) to the ground at side 1, as shown in Fig. 10.45(b). Then

\(\begin{aligned}&R_{ x }=R_{ F }+R_{1}=200+5=205 \Omega \\&R_{ y }=R_{ F }\left\|R_{1}=200\right\| 5=4.88 \Omega \\&R_{ ie }=R_{ i }\left\|\left(R_{ F }+R_{1}\right)=R_{ i }\right\| R_{ x }=2 M \| 205=204.98 \Omega \\&R_{ oe }=R_{ o }+R_{ y }+R_{ L }= R _{ o }+\left(R_{ F } \| R_{1}\right)+R_{L}=75+4.88+5=84.88 \Omega\end{aligned}\)

Equation (10.89) gives the feedback factor β :

\( \beta=\left.\frac{i_{ f }}{i_{ y }}\right|_{v_{ f }=0} \) (short-circuited input side) (10.89)

 

\(\beta=\left.\frac{i_{ f }}{i_{ y }}\right|_{v_{ f }=0}=\frac{R_{1}}{R_{ F }+R_{1}}=\frac{5}{200+5}=24.39 \times 10^{-3} A / A\)

Equation (10.97) gives the modified open-loop current gain \( A_{ e } \):

\(A_{ e }=\frac{i_{ o }}{i_{ e 1}}=\frac{R_{ o } R_{ x }}{\left(R_{ y }+R_{ L }+R_{ o }\right)\left(R_{ i }+R_{ x }\right)} A\) (10.97)

\( A_{ e }=\frac{75 \times 205 \times 53.33 \times 10^{8}}{(4.88+5+75)(2 M +205)}=483 \times 10^{3} A / A \)

The loop gain is

\( T_{ L }=\beta A_{ e }=24.39 \times 10^{-3} \times 483 \times 10^{3}=11.76 \times 10^{3} \)

The resistances are

\(\begin{aligned}&R_{ if }=\frac{R_{ ie }}{1+T_{ L }}=\frac{204.98}{1+11.76 \times 10^{3}}=17.4 m \Omega \\&R_{ in }=\frac{v_{ s }}{i_{ s }}=R_{ if }+R_{ s }=17.4 m +2.5 k \approx 2.5 k \Omega \\&R_{ of }=R_{ oe } \times\left(1+T_{ L }\right)=84.88 \times\left(1+11.76 \times 10^{3}\right)=999.6 k \Omega\end{aligned}\)

Then

\( A_{ f }=\frac{i_{ o }}{i_{ i }}=\frac{483 \times 10^{3}}{1+11.763 \times 10^{3}} \approx 41 \)

(b) The shunt-series feedback circuit for PSpice simulation is shown in Fig. 10.46. The results of PSpice simulation (.TF analysis) are shown below, with hand calculations to the right:

\(\begin{array}{ll}I ( VX ) / Is =4 \cdot 100 E +01=41 & A_{ f }=41 \\\text { INPUT RESISTANCE AT Is=1.740E-02=17.4 } m \Omega & R_{\text {if }}=17.4 m \Omega \\\text { OUTPUT RESISTANCE AT I }( VX )=1.000 E +06=1 M \Omega & R_{\text {of }}=999.6 k \Omega\end{array}\)

The PSpice results are very close to the hand-calculated values.
