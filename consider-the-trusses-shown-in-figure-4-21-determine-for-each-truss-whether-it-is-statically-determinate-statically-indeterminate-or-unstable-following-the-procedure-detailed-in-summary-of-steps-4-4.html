Guest Question.

Consider the trusses shown in Figure 4.21. Determine for each truss whether it is statically determinate, statically indeterminate or unstable following the procedure detailed in Summary of Steps 4.4.

Admin
Solution

The free-body diagram of each truss is presented in Figure 4.22, highlighting the coordinate system, freedom numbering and location vectors adopted for each member.

Truss A (Figure 4.22a):

b = 5; r = 3; j = 4 from which D = b + r − 2j = 5 + 3 − 2 × 4 = 0.

The direction cosines related to the adopted location vectors are tabulated as:

From geometric considerations, the direction cosines related to the reactions are:

\( l_{H_A} = 1 \) and \( m_{H_A} = 1 \) for \( H_A \) ;

\( l_{H_C} = 1 \) and \( m_{H_C} = 0 \) for \( H_C \) ;

\( l_{V_C} = 0 \) and \( m_{V_C} = 1 \) for \( V_C \) ;

Truss C (Figure 4.22c):

b = 4; r = 3; j = 4 ∴ D = b + r − 2j = 4 + 3 − 2 × 4 = −1.

As D < 0, Truss C is classified as unstable.
