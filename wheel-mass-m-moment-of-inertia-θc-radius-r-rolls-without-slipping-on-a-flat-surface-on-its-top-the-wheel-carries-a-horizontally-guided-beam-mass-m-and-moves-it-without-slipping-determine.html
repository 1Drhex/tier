Guest Question.

wheel (mass \(m\), moment of inertia \(\Theta _{C} \) , radius \(r\)) rolls without slipping on a flat surface. On its top, the wheel carries a horizontally guided beam (mass \(M\) ) and moves it without slipping.

Determine the eigenfrequency of the system. Neglect the mass of the guiding roll \(B\) .

Admin
Solution

We separate the system and introduce the coordinates \(x_{1} \) , \(x_{2} \) and \(\varphi \) , measured from the equilibrium position. Then the equations of motion are

① \(\longrightarrow :m\ddot{x}_{1}=-k_{1}x_{1}-H_{A}+H_{D}\) ,

\(\overset{\curvearrowright }{C} :\Theta _{C}\ddot{\varphi } =-rH_{A}-rH_{D}\) ,

② \(\longrightarrow :M\ddot{x}_{2} =-k_{2}x_{2}+H_{A} \) .

With the kinematic relations

\(x_{1}=r\varphi\) , \(x_{2}=2x_{1} \rightsquigarrow \ddot{x}_{1}=r\ddot{\varphi }\) , \(\ddot{x}_{2}=2\ddot{x}_{1}\)

we now have five equations for the five unknowns \( x_{1} , x_{2} , \varphi , H_{A} \) and
\(H_{D}\) . Solving for \(x_{1}\left(t\right)\) yields the equation of motion

\(\ddot{x}_{1}\left(m+4M+\frac{\Theta _{C} }{r^{2} } \right)+\left(k_{1}+4k_{2} \right)x_{1}=0 \)

or in standard form

\(\ddot{x}_{1}+\omega ^{2}x_{1}=0 \)

with the eigenfrequency

\( \underline{\underline{\omega=\sqrt{\frac{k_{1}+4k_{2} }{m+4M+\frac{\Theta _{C} }{r^{2} } } } } } \) .
