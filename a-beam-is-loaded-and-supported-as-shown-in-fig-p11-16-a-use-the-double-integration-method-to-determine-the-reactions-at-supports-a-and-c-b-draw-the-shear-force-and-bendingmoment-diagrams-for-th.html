Guest Question.

A beam is loaded and supported as shown in Fig. P11.16.
(a) Use the double-integration method to determine the reactions at supports A and C.
(b) Draw the shear-force and bendingmoment diagrams for the beam.

Admin
Solution

Beam FBD:

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma F_{y}=A_{y}+C_{y}=0 \quad \therefore C_{y}=-A_{y} \\&\Sigma M_{A}=-M_{A}+C_{y} L-M_{0}=0\end{aligned}\)

 

Moment equation:

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{a-a} &=M(x)-A_{y} x-M_{A}=0 \\& \therefore M(x)=A_{y} x+M_{A} \quad\left(0 \leq x \leq \frac{L}{2}\right)\end{aligned}\)

 

 

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{b-b} &=M(x)-A_{y} x-M_{A}-M_{0}=0 \\& \therefore M(x)=A_{y} x+M_{A}+M_{0} \quad\left(\frac{L}{2} \leq x \leq L\right)\end{aligned}\)

 

Integration:
For beam segment AB: For beam segment BC:

\(\begin{array}{ll}E I \frac{d^{2} v}{d x^{2}}=M(x)=A_{y} x+M_{A} & E I \frac{d^{2} v}{d x^{2}}=M(x)=A_{y} x+M_{A}+M_{0} \\E I \frac{d v}{d x}=\frac{A_{y} x^{2}}{2}+M_{A} x+C_{1} & E I \frac{d v}{d x}=\frac{A_{y} x^{2}}{2}+M_{A} x+M_{0} x+C_{3} \\E I v=\frac{A_{y} x^{3}}{6}+\frac{M_{A} x^{2}}{2}+C_{1} x+C_{2} & E I v=\frac{A_{y} x^{3}}{6}+\frac{M_{A} x^{2}}{2}+\frac{M_{0} x^{2}}{2}+C_{3} x+C_{4}\end{array}\)

 

Boundary conditions and evaluate constants for segment AB:

\(\text { at } x=0, v=0 \quad \frac{A_{y}(0)^{3}}{6}+\frac{M_{A}(0)^{2}}{2}+C_{1}(0)+C_{2}=0 \quad \therefore C_{2}=0\)

 

\(\text { at } x=0, \frac{d v}{d x}=0 \quad \frac{A_{y}(0)^{2}}{2}+M_{A}(0)+C_{1}=0 \quad \therefore C_{1}=0\)

 

Slope continuity condition at x = L/2:

\(\begin{aligned}\text { at } x=\frac{L}{2},\left.\frac{d v}{d x}\right|_{A B}=&\left.\frac{d v}{d x}\right|_{B C} \\\frac{A_{y} x^{2}}{2}+M_{A} x=& \frac{A_{y} x^{2}}{2}+M_{A} x+M_{0} x+C_{3} \\& \therefore C_{3}=-\frac{M_{0} L}{2}\end{aligned}\)

Deflection continuity condition at x = L/2:

\(\begin{aligned}&\text { at } x=\frac{L}{2},\left.v_{B}\right|_{A B}=\left.v_{B}\right|_{B C} \\&\frac{A_{y} x^{3}}{6}+\frac{M_{A} x^{2}}{2}=\frac{A_{y} x^{3}}{6}+\frac{M_{A} x^{2}}{2}+\frac{M_{0} x^{2}}{2}-\frac{M_{0} L x}{2}+C_{4} \\&\therefore C_{4}=\frac{M_{0} L^{2}}{8}\end{aligned}\)

Boundary condition for segment BC:

\(\text { at } x=L, v=0 \quad \frac{A_{y}(L)^{3}}{6}+\frac{M_{A}(L)^{2}}{2}+\frac{M_{0}(L)^{2}}{2}-\frac{M_{0} L}{2}(L)+\frac{M_{0} L^{2}}{8}=0 \quad \therefore A_{y} L+3 M_{A}=-\frac{3 M_{0}}{4}\)

Also, the beam moment equilibrium equation can be written as:

\(A_{y} L+M_{A}=-M_{0}\)

(a) Beam Reactions: Solve these two equations simultaneously to obtain:

\(M_{A}=\frac{M_{0}}{8}=\frac{M_{0}}{8}( cw )\) \(A_{y}=-\frac{9 M_{0}}{8 L}=\frac{9 M_{0}}{8 L} \downarrow\) \(C_{y}=\frac{9 M_{0}}{8 L} \uparrow\)
