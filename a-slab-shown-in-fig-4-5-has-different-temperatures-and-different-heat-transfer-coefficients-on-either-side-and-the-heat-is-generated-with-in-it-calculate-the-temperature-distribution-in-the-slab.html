Guest Question.

A slab shown in Fig. 4.5 has different temperatures and different heat transfer coefficients on either side and the heat is generated with in it. Calculate the temperature distribution in the slab.

Admin
Solution

The differential equation is
\(\frac{d^2T}{dX^2}=-\frac{\dot{q} }{k}\)

and the general solution is

\(T=-\frac{\dot{q}x^2 }{2k}+C_{1}X +C_{2}\) (4.19)

with b .c . ’s

\(\bar{h} _{1} (T_{1}-T )_{X=0} =-K\frac{dT}{dX}\mid _{x=0}\) , \(\bar{h} _{2} (T-T_{2} )_{X=L} =-K\frac{dT}{dX}\mid _{X=L}\) (4.20)

There are eight variables involved in the problem: \((T-T_{2} ) ,(T_{1}-T_{2} ) ,X ,L ,K ,\bar{{h}_{1} },\bar{{h}_{2} } and \dot{q}\) ; and there are three dimensions: K, W, and m. This results in 8-3 = 5 pi-groups. For these we choose

\(\Pi _{1}\equiv \Theta =\frac{T-T_{2} }{T_{1}-T_{2} }\) , \(\Pi _{2} \equiv \xi =\frac{X}{L}\) , \(\Pi _{3}\equiv Bi_{1}=\frac{\bar{h} _{1} L}{K}\) , \(\Pi _{4}\equiv Bi_{2}=\frac{\bar{h} _{2} L}{K}\) and \(\Pi _{5} \equiv \Gamma =\frac{\dot{q}L^2 }{2K(T_{1}-T_{2} )}\)

where as a comparison of the heat generated in
the slab to that which could flow through it.
Under this nondimensionalization, eqn. (4.19) becomes

\(\Theta =-\Gamma \xi ^2 +C_{3}\xi +C_{4}\) (4.21)

and b.c.’s become

\(Bi_{1}(1-\Theta _{\xi=0 } )=-\acute{\Theta } _{\xi=0}\) , \(Bi_{2}\Theta _{\xi=1 } =- \acute{\Theta } _{\xi=1}\) (4.22)

where the primes denote differentiation with respect to ξ. Substituting eqn. (4.21) in eqn. (4.22), we obtain

Substituting the first of eqns. (4.23) in the second we get

\(C_{4}=1+\frac{-Bi_{1}+2(Bi_{1}/Bi_{2} )\Gamma +Bi_{1}\Gamma }{Bi_{1}+Bi^{2}_{1}/Bi_{2}+Bi^{2}_{1} }\)

 

\(C_{3}= Bi_{1}(C_{4}-1 )\)

Thus, eqn. (4.21) becomes

\(\Theta =1+\Gamma [\frac{2(Bi_{1}/Bi_{2}) +Bi_{1} }{1+Bi_{1}/Bi_{2}+{Bi_{1} } } \xi -\xi ^2+\frac{2(Bi_{1}/Bi_{2})+Bi_{1} }{Bi_{1}+Bi^{2}_{1}/Bi_{2}+B^{2}_{1} } ]-\frac{Bi_{1} }{1+Bi_{1}/Bi_{2}+Bi_{1} }\xi -\frac{Bi_{1} }{Bi_{1}+Bi^{2}_{1}/Bi_{2}+Bi^{2}_{1} }\) (4.24)
