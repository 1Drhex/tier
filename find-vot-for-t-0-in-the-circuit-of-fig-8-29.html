Guest Question.

Find \(v_o(t)\) for t > 0 in the circuit of Fig. 8.29.

Admin
Solution

This is an example of a second-order circuit with two inductors. We first obtain the mesh currents \(i_1\) and \(i_2,\) which happen to be the currents through the inductors. We need to obtain the initial and final values of these currents.

For t < 0, 7u(t) = 0, so that \(i_1\)(0¯) = 0 = \(i_2\)(0¯). For t > 0, 7u(t) = 7, so that the equivalent circuit is as shown in Fig. 8.30(a). Due to the continuity of inductor current,

\(i_1(0^+)=i_1(0¯)=0,\) \( i_2(0^+)=i_2(0¯)=0\) (8.10.1)

\(v_{L_2}(0^+)=v_o(0^+)=1[(i_1(0^+)-i_2(0^+)]=0\) (8.10.2)

Applying KVL to the left loop in Fig. 8.30(a) at t = \(0^+,\)

\(7=3i_1(0^+)+v_{L_1}(0^+)+v_o(0^+)\)

or

\(v_{L_1}(0^+)\) = 7 V

Since \(L_1\ di_1/dt=v_{L_1},\)

\(\frac{di_1(0^+)}{dt}=\frac{v_{L_1}}{L_1}=\frac{7}{\frac{1}{2}}=14\ V/s\) (8.10.3)

Similarly, since \(L_2\ di_2/dt=v_{L_2},\)

\(\frac{di_2(0^+)}{dt}=\frac{v_{L_2}}{L_2}=0\) (8.10.4)

As t → ∞, the circuit reaches steady state, and the inductors can be replaced by short circuits, as shown in Fig. 8.30(b). From this figure,

\(i_1(∞)=i_2(∞)=\frac{7}{3}A\) (8.10.5)

Next, we obtain the form of the transient responses by removing the voltage source, as shown in Fig. 8.31. Applying KVL to the two meshes yields

\(4i_1-i_2+\frac{1}{2}\frac{di_1}{dt}=0\) (8.10.6)

and

\(i_2+\frac{1}{5}\frac{di_2}{dt}-i_1=0\) (8.10.7)

From Eq. (8.10.6),

\(i_2=4i_1+\frac{1}{2}\frac{di_1}{dt}\) (8.10.8)

Substituting Eq. (8.10.8) into Eq. (8.10.7) gives

\(4i_1+\frac{1}{2}\frac{di_1}{dt}+\frac{4}{5}\frac{di_1}{dt}+\frac{1}{10}\frac{d^2i_1}{dt^2}-i_1=0\)

\(\frac{d^2i_1}{dt^2}+13\frac{di_1}{dt}+30i_1=0\)

From this we obtain the characteristic equation as

\(s^2\) + 13s + 30 = 0

which has roots s = -3 and s = -10. Hence, the form of the transient response is

\(i_{1n}=Ae^{-3t}+Be^{-10t}\) (8.10.9)

where A and B are constants. The steady-state response is

\(i_{1ss}=i_1(∞)=\frac{7}{3}A\) (8.10.10)

From Eqs. (8.10.9) and (8.10.10), we obtain the complete response as

\(i_1(t)=\frac{7}{3}+Ae^{-3t}+Be^{-10t}\) (8.10.11)

We finally obtain A and B from the initial values. From Eqs. (8.10.1) and (8.10.11),

0 = \(\frac{7}{3}\) + A + B (8.10.12)

Taking the derivative of Eq. (8.10.11), setting t = 0 in the derivative, and enforcing Eq. (8.10.3), we obtain

14 = -3A – 10B (8.10.13)

From Eqs. (8.10.12) and (8.10.13), A = -4/3 and B = -1. Thus,

\(i_1(t)=\frac{7}{3}-\frac{4}{3}e^{-3t}-e^{-10t}\) (8.10.14)

We now obtain \(i_2\) from \(i_1\). Applying KVL to the left loop in Fig. 8.30(a) gives

\(7=4i_1-i_2+\frac{1}{2}\frac{di_1}{dt}\) ⇒ \(i_2=-7+4i_1+\frac{1}{2}\frac{di_1}{dt}\)

Substituting for \(i_1\) in Eq. (8.10.14) gives

\(i_2(t)=-7+\frac{28}{3}-\frac{16}{3}e^{-3t}-4e^{-10t}+2e^{-3t}+5e^{-10t}\)

(8.10.15)

\(=\frac{7}{3}-\frac{10}{3}e^{-3t}+e^{-10t}\)

From Fig. 8.29,

\(v_o(t)=1[i_1(t)-i_2(t)]\) (8.10.16)

Substituting Eqs. (8.10.14) and (8.10.15) into Eq (8.10.16) yields

\(v_o(t)=2(e^{-3t}-e^{-10t})\) (8.10.17)

Note that \(v_o\)(0) = 0, as expected from Eq. (8.10.2).
