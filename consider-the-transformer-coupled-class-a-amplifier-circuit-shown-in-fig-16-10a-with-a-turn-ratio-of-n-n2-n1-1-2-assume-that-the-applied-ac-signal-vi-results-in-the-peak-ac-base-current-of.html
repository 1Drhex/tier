Guest Question.

Consider the transformer coupled class-A amplifier circuit shown in Fig. 16.10a with a turn ratio of \( n=\frac{N_{2}}{N_{1}}=\frac{1}{2} \) . Assume that the applied ac signal Vi results in the peak ac base current of 7 mA and the transistor has hFE = hfe = 50.

(a) Draw the dc and ac load lines .

(b) Find the input dc power .

(c) Compute the ac output power .

(d) Find Imax, Imin, Vmaxand Vmin.

(e) Compute the power dissipated by the transistor .

(f ) Calculate the efficiency of the amplifier.

(g) What is the maximum theoretical efficiency of the circuit? Find the required peak base current to produce the maximum efficiency.

Admin
Solution

(a) The quiescent base and collector currents are already obtained in which are respectively given by

IBQ = 9.3 mA and ICQ = 465 mA

Since there is no resistive load in the collector and emitter circuits under the assumption that the coupling transformer has zero dc resistance, the dc load line is given by

\( I_{C}=\frac{V_{CC}-V_{CE}}{0\Omega } \)

which represents a line with infinite slope as shown in Fig. 16.11.

Since, the dc load line must pass through the Q-point, we can put VCE = VCEQ = VY and IC = ICQ = IY in the dc load line equation to obtain the quiescent collector-to-emitter voltage as

VCEQ = VY = VCC = VYY = 12 V

Note that the quiescent collector-to-emitter voltage VY = VCEQ =12 V is independent of the quiescent collector current IY = ICQ.

Since the load RL = 4 Ω is connected between the secondary terminals of the transistor, the effective load resistance seen at the primary is obtained from Eq. (16.24) as

\( R^{′}_{L}=\frac{R_{L}}{n^2} =\frac{4\Omega }{(1/2)^2}=16 \Omega \)

Now, replacing Rc by R’L = 16 Ω and RL = ∞ in Eq. (9.14) , \( i_{C}(sat)=I_{CQ}+\frac{V_{CEQ}}{R_{c}||R_{L}} \) , we get

\( i_{C}(sat)=I_{CQ}+\frac{V_{CEQ}}{R^{′}_{L}} =0.465A+\frac{12V}{16\Omega }=0.465 A+0.750 A =1.215 A \)

Thus, the ac load line can be drawn as shown in Fig. 16.10b. From Eq. (9.13), \( v_{CE}(cutoff)=V_{CEQ}+v_{ce}(cutoff)=V_{CEQ} +I_{CQ}(R_{c}||R_{L}) \) , we can find that the ac load line cuts the vCE -axis at

vCE = vCE (cutoff ) = VCEQ + ICQR’L = 12 V + 0.465 A × 16 Ω = 12 V + 7.44 V = 19.44 V

(b) The dc input power supplied to the circuit is obtained as

Pi (dc) = VYY IY = VCC ICQ = 12 V × 0.465 A = 5.58 W

(c) The peak value of the ac collector current Im has already determined in as

Im = 7 mA × 50 = 350 mA

Note that the output current has a full peak-to-peak swing without any clipping. Thus, the peak ac voltage output is

Vm = ImR’L = 0.35A × 16 Ω = 5.6 V

Now, the ac output power is determined as

\(P(ac)=\frac{V_{m}}{\sqrt{2} }\times \frac{I_{m}}{\sqrt{2} }=\frac{5.6V}{\sqrt{2} }\times \frac{0.35A}{\sqrt{2} } =0.98 W \)

(d) Since the ac current and voltage components are superimposed on their respective quiescent dc components, we obtain

Imax = ICQ + Im = 465 mA + 350 mA = 0.815 A

Imin = ICQ − Im = 465 mA − 350 mA = 0.115 A

Vmax = VCEQ + Vm = 12 V + 5.6 V = 17.6 V

Vmin = VCEQ − Vm = 12 V − 5.6 V = 6.4 V

(e) Note that the total input dc power by the power supply to the circuit is Pi(dc) and P(ac) is the amount of dc power converted into ac power that appears as output Now, the difference PC = Pi(dc) − P(ac) is called the collector dissipation power which is dissipated by the transistor normally as heat. Thus, power dissipated by the transistor is obtained as

PC = Pi (dc) − P (ac) = 5.58 W − 0.98 W = 4.6 W

(f) The efficiency of the amplifier is computed as

\(\eta =\frac{P(ac)}{P_{i}(dc)}\times 100\% \)

\(=\frac{0.98W}{5.58W} \)

= 17.56 %

(g) Note that the maximum theoretical efficiency of the given circuit is obtained for a suitable base current for which Im = IY = ICQ = 0.465A and hence Vm = ImR’L = 0.465A × 16 Ω = 7.44 V. In this case, Vmax and Vmin are obtained as

Vmax = VCEQ + Vm = 12 V +7.44 V = 19.44 V

Vmin = VCEQ − Vm = 12 V −7.44 V = 4.56 V

Thus, the theoretical maximum efficiency of the given transformer coupled circuit is obtained from Eq. (16.32) , \(\eta =50\frac{V_{max}-V_{min}}{V_{max}+V_{min}}\% \) , as

\(\eta (max)=50\times \frac{V_{max}-V_{min}}{V_{max}+V_{min}}\% \)

\(=50\times\frac{19.44V-4.56V}{19.44V+4.56V}\% \)

= 31 %

Note that the ac output power \(P(ac)=\frac{V^2_{m}}{2R^{′}_{L}}=\frac{(7.44V)^2}{2\times 16\Omega }=1.73 W \) in this case also gives \(\eta =\frac{P(ac)}{P_{dc}}=\frac{1.73W}{5.58W}\times 100\% =31\% \) which is same as obtained earlier from Eq. (16.32).

Clearly, the required peak base current to produce the maximum theoretical efficiency is

\(I_{b}(peak)=\frac{I_{m}}{h_{fe}}=\frac{465 mA}{50}=9.3 mA \)

which is same as the quiescent dc base current IBQ.
