Guest Question.

A bowling ball dropped off the Golden Gate bridge has height H (in meters) above the water at time t (in seconds) given by H(t) = at² + bt + c, where a, b, and c are constants. From calculus it follows that the velocity is V(t) = 2at + b and the acceleration is A(t) = 2a. At t = 2, it is known that the ball has height 47.4 m, velocity −19.6 m/s, and acceleration −9.8 m/s². (The velocity and acceleration are negative because the ball is moving downward.) What is the height of the bridge and when does the ball hit the water?

Our model ignores forces other than gravity.

Admin
Solution

We need to find the values of a, b, and c in order to answer these questions. At time t = 2, we have

47.4=H(2)=4a+2b+c,−19.6=V(2)=4a+b,−9.8=A(2)=2a

This gives us the linear system

4a+2b+c=47.44a+ b =−19.62a =−9.8 (14)

Back substituting as usual, we find that

a=−4.9,b=0,c=67

so that the height function is H(t) = −4.9t² + 67. At time t = 0 the ball is just starting its descent, so the bridge has height H(0) = 67
meters. The ball strikes the water when H(t) = 0, which leads to the equation

−4.9t2+67=0

The solution is t64/4.9≈3.7 seconds after the ball is released.
