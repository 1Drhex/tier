Guest Question.

A disk rolls without slipping on the \(X Y\) plane. At the instant shown, the horizontal diameter \(A C B\) is parallel to the \(X\) axis. Also, at this instant, the horizontal components of the velocity of the center \(C\) are known to be \(5 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\) in the \(X\) direction and \(3 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\) in the \(Y\) direction, and the \(Y\) component of the velocity of point \(B\) is \(6 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\). Determine the precession, nutation, and spin rates for the Eulerian angles in Fig. (4.15)

Admin
Solution

We begin with a sketch that is like Fig. 4.15, except that the nutation angle exceeds \(\pi / 2\) because the disk is tilted to the right. The \(y^{\prime}\) axis coincides with the horizontal diameter \(A B\), which is the line of nodes. Because an increase of \(\theta\) is defined to correspond to rotation about the positive \(y^{\prime}\) axis, we have \(\bar{j}^{\prime}=-\bar{I}\) at the given instant.

The axes and angle defined here match those in Fig. 4.15, so we may directly employ the description of \(\bar{\omega}\) in Eqs. (4.2.12). We set \(\theta=110^{\circ}\) for the instant of interest, which yields

\(\large\begin{aligned} \bar{\omega}=(&-\dot{\psi} \sin \theta \cos \phi+\dot{\theta} \sin \phi) \bar{i}+(\dot{\psi} \sin \theta \sin \phi+\dot{\theta} \cos \phi) \bar{j}+(\dot{\psi} \cos \theta+\dot{\phi}) \bar{k}, \\\\ \bar{\alpha}=&(-\ddot{\psi} \sin \theta \cos \phi+\ddot{\theta} \sin \phi-\dot{\psi} \dot{\theta} \cos \theta \cos \phi+\dot{\psi} \dot{\phi} \sin \theta \sin \phi+\dot{\theta} \dot{\phi} \cos \phi) \bar{i} \\\\ &+(\ddot{\psi} \sin \theta \sin \phi+\ddot{\theta} \cos \phi+\dot{\psi} \dot{\theta} \cos \theta \sin \phi+\dot{\psi} \dot{\phi} \sin \theta \cos \phi-\dot{\theta} \dot{\phi} \sin \phi) \bar{j} \\\\ &+(\ddot{\psi} \cos \theta+\ddot{\phi}-\dot{\psi} \dot{\theta} \sin \theta) \bar{k}. (4.2.12)\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\large\bar{\omega}=-0.9397 \dot{\psi} \bar{i}^{\prime}+\dot{\theta} \bar{j}^{\prime}+(\dot{\phi}-0.3420 \dot{\psi}) \bar{k}^{\prime} .\)

There is no slipping at the contact point \(D\), so \(\bar{v}_{D}=\overline{0}\). When we refer the velocities of points \(B\) and \(C\) to this point, we find that

\(\large\begin{aligned}\bar{v}_{C} &=\bar{\omega} \times\left(-0.08 \bar{i}^{\prime}\right)=-0.08(\dot{\phi}-0.3420 \dot{\psi}) \bar{j}^{\prime}+0.08 \dot{\theta} \bar{k}^{\prime}, \\\\ \bar{v}_{B} &=\bar{\omega} \times\left(-0.08 \bar{i}^{\prime}+0.08 \bar{j}^{\prime}\right) \\\\ &=-0.08(\dot{\phi}-0.3420 \dot{\psi})\left(\bar{i}^{\prime}+\bar{j}^{\prime}\right)+0.08(\dot{\theta}-0.9397 \dot{\psi}) \bar{k}^{\prime}. \end{aligned} \)

These velocities must match the given components. The fact that \(\bar{j}^{\prime}=-\bar{I}\) at this instant substantially expedites the evaluation of dot products, which we find to be

\(\large\begin{aligned} \bar{v}_{C} \cdot \bar{I}=5 &=-0.08(\dot{\phi}-0.3420 \dot{\psi})\left(\bar{j}^{\prime} \cdot \bar{I}\right)+0.08 \dot{\theta}\left(\bar{k}^{\prime} \cdot \bar{I}\right)=0.08(\dot{\phi}-0.3420 \dot{\psi}) \\\\ \bar{v}_{C} \cdot \bar{J}=3 &=-0.08(\dot{\phi}-0.3420 \psi)\left(\bar{j}^{\prime} \cdot \bar{J}\right)+0.08 \dot{\theta}\left(\bar{k}^{\prime} \cdot \bar{J}\right)=0.08 \dot{\theta} \cos 20^{\circ} \\\\ \bar{v}_{B} \cdot \bar{J}=6 &=-0.08(\dot{\phi}-0.3420 \dot{\psi})\left(\bar{i}^{\prime} \cdot \bar{J}+\bar{j}^{\prime} \cdot \bar{J}\right)+0.08(\dot{\theta}-0.9397 \dot{\psi}) \bar{k}^{\prime} \cdot \bar{J} \\\\ &=-0.08(\dot{\phi}-0.3420 \dot{\psi})\left(-\sin 20^{\circ}\right)+0.08(\dot{\theta}-0.9397 \dot{\psi}) \cos 20^{\circ} \end{aligned} \)

The solution of these simultaneous equations is

\(\large\dot{\psi}=-18.260, \quad \dot{\theta}=39.907 \dot{\phi}=56.255 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s} \)
