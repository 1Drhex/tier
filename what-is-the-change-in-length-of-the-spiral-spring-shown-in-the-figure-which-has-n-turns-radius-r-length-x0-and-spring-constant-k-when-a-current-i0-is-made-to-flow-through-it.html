Guest Question.

What is the change in length of the spiral spring shown in the figure, which has \( N \) turns, radius \( R, \) length \( x_0 \) and spring constant \( k, \) when a current \( I_0 \) is made to flow through it?

Admin
Solution

Imagine that the current \( I_0 \) flows in a superconducting, short-circuited coil of \( N \) turns, with cross-section \( A = πR^2 \) and length \( x_0.\) The magnetic field strength inside the coil is then \( B = μ_0 I N / x_0 \) and the total magnetic flux \( \Phi = BAN = μ_0 I_0 N^2 A/ x_0.\) Even if the length of the coil changed for some reason, the magnetic flux would not change, as to do so would induce a voltage and hence an ‘infinitely’ large current in the coil of zero resistance. Thus the current must vary with the length \( x \) as \( I(x) = I_0 x /x_0.\) The inductance of a coil of length \( x \) is \( L(x) = μ_0 N^2 A/x, \) and the magnetic energy of a coil with current \( I \) flowing through it is

\( W_m= \frac{1}{2}LI^2 =\mu _0 \frac{N^2I_0^2A}{2x_0^2}x. \)

Thus the energy of the coil is proportional to \( x, \) i.e. \( W_m(x) \propto x.\) The proportionality constant \( F_0 \) is the magnetic force of contraction, since work \( W =F_0x \) is needed to re-stretch the coil by length \( x.\)
The coil is in equilibrium when the magnetic force of contraction balances the elastic force \( F(x) = k(x_0- x), \) i.e. when the change of spring length is

\( \Delta x=x_0-x=\frac{F_0}{k} \approx \mu _0\frac{\pi I_0^2 N^2 R^2}{2kx^2_0} . \)
