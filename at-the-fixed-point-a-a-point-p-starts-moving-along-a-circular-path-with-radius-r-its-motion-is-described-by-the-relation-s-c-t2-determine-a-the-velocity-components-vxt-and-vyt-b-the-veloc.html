Guest Question.

At the fixed point \(A\) a point \(P\) starts moving along a circular path with radius r. Its motion is described by the relation \(s = ct^{2}\).

Determine:

a) the velocity components \(v_{x}\left(t\right)\) and \(v_{y}\left(t\right)\),

b) the velocity at point \(B\) ,

c) the tangential acceleration \(a_{t}\left(s\right)\) and the normal acceleration \(a_{n}\left(s\right)\).

Admin
Solution

From \(s = ct^{2}\) the speed follows as \(v = \dot{s} = 2ct\) .

a) Because the velocity is always tangential to the path, its cartesian components at an arbitrary point are

\(v_{x} = -v \sin \varphi \) ,\( v_{y} = v \cos \varphi\) .

Hence, with

\(\varphi = \frac{s}{r} = \frac{ct^{2} }{r}\)

we obtain

\(\underline{\underline{v_{x} = -2c t \sin \frac{c t^{2} }{r} } } \) , \(\underline{\underline{v_{y} = 2c t \cos \frac{c t^{2} }{r} } } \) .

b) In point B we have

\(s\left(t_{B} \right) = \frac{\pi r}{2} = c t^{2}_{B} \rightsquigarrow t_{B} = \sqrt{\frac{\pi r}{2c} }\) ,

 

\(\underline{\underline{v\left(t_{B} \right) } } = 2c t_{B} = 2c\sqrt{\frac{\pi r}{2c} } = \underline{\underline{\sqrt{2\pi rc} } }\) .

c) From

\(a_{t} = \dot{v}\) , \( a_{n} = \frac{v^{2} }{r} = \frac{4c^{2}t^{2} }{r}\) ,

with \(\dot{v} = 2c\) and \(c t^{2} = s\) , follow the results

\(\underline{\underline{a_{t} = 2c } } \) , \( \underline{\underline{a_{n} = \frac{4cs}{r} } }\) .

Remark: While the tangential acceleration remains constant, the normal acceleration increases linearly with \(s\) .
