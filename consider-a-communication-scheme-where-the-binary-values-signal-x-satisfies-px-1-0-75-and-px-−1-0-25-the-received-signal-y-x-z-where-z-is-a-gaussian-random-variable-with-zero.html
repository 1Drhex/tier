Guest Question.

Consider a communication scheme where the binary values signal X satisfies P{X = 1} = 0.75 and P{X = −1} = 0.25. The received signal Y = X + Z, where Z is a Gaussian random variable with zero mean and variance \(\sigma^{2}\). The received signal Y is fed to the threshold detector. The output of the threshold detector X is

\(\widehat{X}= \begin{cases}+1 & Y>\tau \\ -1 & Y \leq \tau\end{cases}\)

To achieve a minimum probability of error \(P\{\widehat{X} \neq X\}\), the threshold \(\tau\) should be
(a) strictly positive
(b) zero
(c) strictly negative
(d) strictly positive, zero, or strictly negative depending on the nonzero value of \(\sigma^{2}\)

Admin
Solution

Assume hypothesis \(H _{1} \text { and } H _{0}\) are given as \(H_{1}: X=+1 ; H_{0}: X=-1\) and their probabilities \(P\left(H_{1}\right)=0.75 ; P\left(H_{0}\right)=0.25\)

Let received sigma \(\gamma=X+Z\) where Z follow normal distribution as N (0, – 2); with \(f_{z}(Z)=\frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2 \pi}} e^{-z^{2} / 2 \sigma^{2}}\)

Then received signal \(\gamma=\left\{\begin{array}{cl}1+Z & i f X=1 \\-1+Z & i f X=-1\end{array}\right.\)

 

\(f_{\gamma}\left(y / H_{1}\right)=\frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2 \pi}} e^{-(\gamma-1)^{2} / 2 \sigma^{2}}\)

and \(f_{\gamma}\left(y / H_{0}\right)=\frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2 \pi}} e^{-(\gamma+1)^{2} / 2 \sigma^{2}}\)

Let \(y_{\text {opt }}\) denote an optimal threshold, then probability of error for optimum threshold will be calculated as

\(\begin{array}{r}\left.\frac{f_{\gamma}\left(y / H_{1}\right)}{f_{\gamma}\left(y / H_{0}\right)}\right|_{y=y_{ opt }}=\frac{P\left(H_{0}\right)}{P\left(H_{1}\right)} \\-[(\gamma-1)-(\gamma+1)]^{2} /\left.2 \sigma^{2}\right|_{y_{ opt }}=\frac{P\left(H_{0}\right)}{P\left(H_{1}\right)} \\e^{+2 y_{o p t} /^{2} _{\sigma} }=\frac{P\left(H_{0}\right)}{P\left(H_{1}\right)}\end{array}\)

So, \(y_{ opt }\) can be given as

\(y_{ opt }=\frac{\sigma^{2}}{2} \ln \frac{P\left(H_{0}\right)}{P\left(H_{1}\right)}=\frac{-\sigma^{2}}{2}=-0.55 \sigma^{2}\) , here,\(y_{ opt }\) is less than zero, so negative.
Hence, the correct option is (c)
