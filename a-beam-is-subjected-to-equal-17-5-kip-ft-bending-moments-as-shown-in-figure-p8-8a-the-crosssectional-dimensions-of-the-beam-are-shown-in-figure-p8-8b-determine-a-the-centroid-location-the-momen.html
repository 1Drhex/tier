Guest Question.

A beam is subjected to equal 17.5 kip-ft bending moments, as shown in Figure P8.8a. The crosssectional dimensions of the beam are shown in Figure P8.8b. Determine:
(a) the centroid location, the moment of inertia about the z axis, and the controlling section modulus about the z axis.
(b) the bending stress at point H. State whether the normal stress at H is tension or compression.
(c) the bending stress at point K. State whether the normal stress at K is tension or compression.
(d) the maximum bending stress produced in the cross section. State whether the stress is tension or compression.

Admin
Solution

(a) Centroid location in y direction: (reference axis at bottom of shape)

Shape \(\begin{gathered}\text{Area} A_{i} \\\left(\text { in. }^{2}\right)\end{gathered}\) \(\begin{gathered}y_{i} \\(\text { from bottom) } \\\text { (in.) }\end{gathered}\) \(\begin{gathered}y_{i} A_{i} \\\left(\text { in. }^{3}\right)\end{gathered}\)
top flange 12.0000 13.0000 156.0000
web 20.0000 7.0000 140.0000
bottom flange 20.0000 1.0000 20.0000
52.0000 \(\text { in. }^{2}\) 316.0000 \(\text { in. }^{3}\)

 

\(\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{316.0 in .{ }^{3}}{52.0 in .^{2}}=6.077 in .=6.08 in.\) (measured upward from bottom edge of bottom flange)

 

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

Shape \(\begin{gathered}I_{C} \\\left( in. ^{4}\right)\end{gathered}\) \(\begin{gathered}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\\text { (in.) }\end{gathered}\) \(\begin{gathered}d^{2} A \\\left(\text { in. }^{4}\right)\end{gathered}\) \(\begin{aligned}&I_{C}+d^{2} A \\&\left(\text { in. }^{4}\right)\end{aligned}\)
top flange 4.000 6.923 575.148 579.148
web 166.667 0.923 17.041 183.708
bottom flange 6.667 -5.077 515.503 522.170
Moment of inertia about the z axis \(\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)=\) 1,285.026

Section Moduli

\(\begin{aligned}&c_{b o t}=6.0769 in \text {. }\\&c_{\text {top }}=14 \text { in. }-6.0769 \text { in. }=7.9231 \text { in. }\\&S_{b o t}=\frac{I_{z}}{c_{b o t}}=\frac{1,285.026 in .{ }^{4}}{6.0769 in .}=211.460 in .{ }^{3}\\&S_{t o p}=\frac{I_{z}}{c_{\text {top }}}=\frac{1,285.026 in.{ }^{4}}{7.9231 in .}=162.188 in .{ }^{3}\end{aligned}\)

The controlling section modulus is the smaller of the two values; therefore,

\(S=162.2 in .{ }^{3}\)

Bending stress at point H:
From the flexure formula:

\(\sigma_{x}=-\frac{M y}{I_{z}}=-\frac{(-17.5 kip – ft )(7.9231 in .-2 in .)(12 in . / ft )}{1,285.0256 in .{ }^{4}}=967.9544 psi =968 psi ( T )\)

Bending stress at point K:
From the flexure formula:

\(\sigma_{x}=-\frac{M y}{I_{z}}=-\frac{(-17.5 kip – ft )(-6.0769 in .+2 in. )(12 in . / ft )}{1,285.026 in .{ }^{4}}=-666.2543 psi =666 psi ( C )\)

Maximum bending stress
Since \(c_{\text {top }}>c_{\text {bot }}\), the maximum bending stress occurs at the top of the flanged shape. From the flexure formula:

\(\sigma_{x}=-\frac{M y}{I_{z}}=-\frac{(-17.5 kip – ft )(7.9231 in .)(12 in . / ft )}{1,285.026 in .^{4}}=1,294.8 psi =1,295 psi ( T )\)

Also, note that the same maximum bending stress magnitude can be calculated with the section modulus:

\(\sigma_{x}=\frac{M}{S}=\frac{(17.5 kip – ft )(12 in. / ft )}{162.1877 in .{ }^{3}}=1,294.8 psi =1,295 psi\)

The sense of the stress (either tension or compression) would be determined by inspection.
