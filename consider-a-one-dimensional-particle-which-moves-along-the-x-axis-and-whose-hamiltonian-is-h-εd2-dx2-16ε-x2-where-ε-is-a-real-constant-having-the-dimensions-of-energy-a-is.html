Guest Question.

Consider a one-dimensional particle which moves along the x-axis and whose Hamiltonian is \(\hat{H}=-\varepsilon d^{2} /dx^{2}+16\varepsilon \hat{X}^{2}\), where ε is a real constant having the dimensions of energy.

(a) Is \(\psi (x)=Ae^{-2x^{2}}\) , where A is a normalization constant that needs to be found, an eigenfunction of \(\hat{H}\) ? If yes, find the energy eigenvalue.

(b) Calculate the probability of finding the particle anywhere along the negative x-axis.

(c) Find the energy eigenvalue corresponding to the wave function \(\phi (x)=2x\psi (x)\).

(d) Specify the parities of \(\phi (x)\) and \(\psi (x)\). Are \(\phi (x)\) and \(\psi (x)\) orthogonal?

Admin
Solution

(a) The integral \(\int_{-\infty }^{+\infty }e^{-4x^{2}}dx=\sqrt{\pi }/2\) allows us to find the normalization constant:

\(1=\int_{-\infty }^{+\infty }\left|\psi (x)\right| ^{2} dx=A^{2} \int_{-\infty }^{+\infty }e^{-4x^{2}}dx=A^{2}\frac{\sqrt{\pi } }{2}\); (2.486)

this leads to \(A=\sqrt{2/\sqrt{\pi } }\) and hence \(\psi (x)=\sqrt{2/\sqrt{\pi } } e^{-2x^{2}}\). nce the first and second
derivatives of \(\psi (x)\) are given by

\(\psi ^{′}(x)=\frac{d\psi (x)}{dx} =-4x\psi (x), \psi ^{′′}(x)= \frac{d^{2}\psi (x) }{dx^{2} } =(16x^{2}-4 )\psi (x)\), (2.487)

we see that \(\psi (x)\) is an eigenfunction of \(\hat{H}\) with an energy eigenvalue equal to 4ε:

\(\hat{H}\psi (x)=-\varepsilon \frac{d^{2}\psi (x)}{dx^{2} }+16\varepsilon x^{2}\psi (x)=-\varepsilon (16x^{2}-4)\psi (x)+16 \varepsilon x^{2} \psi (x)=4\varepsilon \psi (x)\). (2.488)

(b) Since \(\int_{-\infty }^{0}e^{-4x^{2}}dx=\sqrt{\pi }/4\) ,he probability of finding the particle anywhere along the negative x-axis is equal to \(\frac{1}{2}\):

\(\int_{-\infty }^{0}\left|\psi (x)\right| ^{2}dx=\frac{2}{\sqrt {\pi } } \int_{-\infty }^{0}e^{-4x^{2}}dx=\frac{1}{2}\). (2.489)

This is expected, since this probability is half the total probability, which in turn is equal to one.

(c) Since the second derivative of \(\phi (x)=2x\psi (x) is \phi ^{′′}(x)=4\psi ^{′}(x)+2x\psi ^{′′}(x)=8x(-3+4x^{2} )\psi (x)=4(-3+4x^{2} )\phi (x)\), we see that \(\phi (x)\) is an eigenfunction of \(\hat{H}\) with an energy eigenvalue equal to 12ε:

\(\hat{H}\phi (x)=-\varepsilon \frac{d^{2}\phi (x)}{dx^{2} } +16\varepsilon x^{2} \phi (x)=-4\varepsilon (-3+4x^{2} )\phi (x) +16\varepsilon x^{2} \phi (x)=12\varepsilon \phi (x)\). (2.490)

(d) The wave functions \(\psi (x)\) and \(\phi (x)\) are even and odd, respectively, since \(\psi (-x)=\psi (x)\) and \(\phi (-x)=-\phi (x)\); hence their product is an odd function. Therefore, they are orthogonal, since the symmetric integration of an odd function is zero:

\(〈\phi \mid \psi〉= \int_{-\infty }^{+\infty }\phi ^{*}(x)\psi (x)dx=\int_{-\infty }^{+\infty }\phi (x)\psi (x)dx= \int_{-\infty }^{+\infty }\phi (-x)\psi (-x)(-dx)\)

 

\(=-\int_{-\infty }^{+\infty }\phi (x)\psi (x)dx=0\). (2.491)
