Guest Question.

Show that Eq. (15.47) is satisfied by the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution function

\(\ln f_{0} \left(v^{\prime }_{2} \right) + \ln f_{0} \left(v^{\prime }_{1} \right) = \ln f_{0} \left(v_{2} \right) + \ln f_{0} \left(v_{1} \right),\) (15.47)

\(M\left(v - v_{r}\right) = A exp\left[- \left(m/2k_{B}T\right) \left(v - v_{r}\right)^{2} \right] ; A = \left[m/\left(2\pi k_{B}T \right) \right]^{3/2} ,\) (15.48)

where \(v_{r}\) is some reference velocity. This is a slight generalization of Eq. (19.70) that we derive later for the case \(v_{r} = 0.\)

\(M\left(v\right) = \left(\frac{m}{2\pi k_{B}T} \right)^{3/2} exp\left(- \frac{mv^{2}}{2 k_{B}T} \right). \) (19.70)

Admin
Solution

We substitute Eq. (15.48) into Eq. (15.47).

The quantity 2 lnA appears on both sides and can be canceled.

Then we divide both sides by \( – (m/2k_{B}T)\) to obtain \(\left(v^{\prime }_{2} – v_{r} \right)^{2} + \left(v^{\prime }_{1} – v_{r} \right)^{2} = \left(v_{2} – v_{r} \right)^{2} + \left(v_{1} – v_{r} \right)^{2}.\)

Multiplying out the squares and canceling \(v^{2}_{r}\) gives

\(\left(v^{\prime }_{1}\right)^{2} + \left(v^{\prime }_{2}\right)^{2} + 2v_{r} \cdot \left(v^{\prime }_{2} + v^{\prime }_{1}\right) = \left(v_{1}\right)^{2} + \left(v_{2}\right)^{2} + 2v_{r} \cdot \left(v_{2} + v_{1}\right) . \) (15.49)

In Eq. (15.49), the squared terms cancel by conservation of energy, Eq. (15.25), and the terms dotted into \(v_{r}\) cancel by conservation of momentum, Eq. (15.26).

\(v^{2}_{1} + v^{2}_{2} = v^{ \prime 2}_{1} + v^{ \prime 2}_{2}\) (15.25)

\(v_{1} + v_{2} = v^{\prime}_{1} + v^{\prime}_{2}.\) (15.26)
