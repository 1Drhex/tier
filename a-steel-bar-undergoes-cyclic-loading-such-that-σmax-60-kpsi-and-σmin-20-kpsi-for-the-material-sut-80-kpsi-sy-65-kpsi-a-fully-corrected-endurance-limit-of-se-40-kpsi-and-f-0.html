Guest Question.

A steel bar undergoes cyclic loading such that \(\sigma_{\max }=60 \text { kpsi and } \sigma_{\min }=-20 \text { kpsi. }\) For the material, \(S_{u t}=80 kpsi , S_{y}=65 kpsi\), a fully corrected endurance limit of \(S_{e}=40 kpsi , \text { and } f=0.9\). Estimate the number of cycles to a fatigue failure using:
(a) Modified Goodman criterion.
(b) Gerber criterion.

Admin
Solution

From the given stresses,

 

\(\sigma_{a}=\frac{60-(-20)}{2}=40 kpsi \quad \sigma_{m}=\frac{60+(-20)}{2}=20 kpsi\)

 

(a) For the modified Goodman criterion, Eq. (6–46), the fatigue factor of safety based on infinite life is

 

\(\frac{\sigma_{a}}{S_{e}}+\frac{\sigma_{m}}{S_{u t}}=\frac{1}{n}\) (6–46)

 

\(n_{f}=\frac{1}{\frac{\sigma_{a}}{S_{e}}+\frac{\sigma_{m}}{S_{u t}}}=\frac{1}{\frac{40}{40}+\frac{20}{80}}=0.8\)

 

This indicates a finite life is predicted. The S-N diagram is only applicable for completely reversed stresses. To estimate the finite life for a fluctuating stress, we will obtain an equivalent completely reversed stress that is expected to be as damaging as the fluctuating stress. A commonly used approach is to assume that since the modified Goodman line represents all stress situations with a constant life of \(10^{6}\) cycles, other constant-life lines can be generated by passing a line through \(\left(S_{u t}, 0\right)\) and a fluctuating stress point \(\left(\sigma_{m}, \sigma_{a}\right)\). The point where this line intersects the \(\sigma_{a}\) axis represents a completely reversed stress (since at this point \(\sigma_{m}=0\)), which predicts the same life as the fluctuating stress. This completely reversed stress can be obtained by replacing \(S_{e} \text { with } \sigma_{\text {rev }}\) in Eq. (6–46) for the modified Goodman line resulting in

 

\(\sigma_{ rev }=\frac{\sigma_{a}}{1-\frac{\sigma_{m}}{S_{u t}}}=\frac{40}{1-\frac{20}{80}}=53.3 kpsi\)

 

From the material properties, Eqs. (6–14) to (6–16), give

 

\(a=\frac{\left(f S_{u t}\right)^{2}}{S_{e}}\) (6–14)

 

\(N=\left(\frac{\sigma_{ rev }}{a}\right)^{1 / b}\) (6–16)

 

\(a=\frac{\left(f S_{u t}\right)^{2}}{S_{e}}=\frac{[0.9(80)]^{2}}{40}=129.6 kpsi\)

 

\(b=-\frac{1}{3} \log \left(\frac{f S_{u t}}{S_{e}}\right)=-\frac{1}{3} \log \left[\frac{0.9(80)}{40}\right]=-0.0851\) (1)

 

\(N=\left(\frac{\sigma_{ rev }}{a}\right)^{1 / b}=\left(\frac{\sigma_{ rev }}{129.6}\right)^{-1 / 0.0851}\)

 

Substituting \(\sigma_{\text {rev }}\) into Eq. (1) yields

 

\(N=\left(\frac{53.3}{129.6}\right)^{-1 / 0.0851}=3.4\left(10^{4}\right) \text { cycles }\)

 

(b) For Gerber, similar to part (a), from Eq. (6–47),

 

\(\frac{n \sigma_{a}}{S_{e}}+\left(\frac{n \sigma_{m}}{S_{u t}}\right)^{2}=1\) (6–47)

 

\(\sigma_{ rev }=\frac{\sigma_{a}}{1-\left(\frac{\sigma_{m}}{S_{u t}}\right)^{2}}=\frac{40}{1-\left(\frac{20}{80}\right)^{2}}=42.7 kpsi\)

 

Again, from Eq. (1),

 

\(N=\left(\frac{42.7}{129.6}\right)^{-1 / 0.0851}=4.6\left(10^{5}\right) \text { cycles }\)

 

Comparing the answers, we see a large difference in the results. Again, the modified Goodman criterion is conservative as compared to Gerber for which the moderate difference in \(S_{f}\) is then magnified by a logarithmic S, N relationship.
