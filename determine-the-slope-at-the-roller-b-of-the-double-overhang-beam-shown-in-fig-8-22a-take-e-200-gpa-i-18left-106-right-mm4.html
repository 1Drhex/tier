Guest Question.

Determine the slope at the roller \(B\) of the double overhang beam shown in Fig. 8–22a.

Take \(E = 200 GPa, I = 18\left ( 10^{6} \right )mm^{4}\)

Admin
Solution

\(M/EI\) Diagram. The \(M/EI\) diagram can be simplified by drawing it in parts and considering the \(M/EI\) diagrams for the three loadings each acting on a cantilever beam fixed at \(D\), Fig. 8–22b. (The \(10-kN\) load is not considered since it produces no moment about \(D\).)

Elastic Curve. If tangents are drawn at \(B\) and \(C\), Fig. 8–22c, the slope \(B\) can be determined by finding \(t_{C/B}\) and for small angles,

\(\theta _{B}=\frac{t_{C/B}}{2 m} \quad \left ( 1 \right )\)

 

Moment Area Theorem. To determine \( t_{C/B}\) we apply the moment area theorem by finding the moment of the \(M/EI\) diagram between \(BC\) about point \(C\).This only involves the shaded area under two of the diagrams in Fig. 8–22b.Thus,

\(t_{C/B}=\left ( 1m \right )\left [ \left ( 2m \right )\left ( \frac{-30 kN . m}{EI} \right ) \right ]+\left ( \frac{2m}{3} \right )\left [ \frac{1}{2}\left ( 2m \right )\left ( \frac{10kN.m}{EI} \right )\right ]\)

 

\(\quad\quad=\frac{53.33 kN . m^{3}}{EI}\)

 

Substituting into Eq. \((1),\)

\(\theta _{B}=\frac{53.33 kN . m^{3}}{\left (2m \right )\left [ 200\left ( 10^{6} \right )kN/m^{3} \right ]\left [ 18\left ( 10^{6} \right ) \left ( 10^{-12} \right )m^{4}\right ]}\)

 

\(\quad\quad = 0.00741 rad\)
