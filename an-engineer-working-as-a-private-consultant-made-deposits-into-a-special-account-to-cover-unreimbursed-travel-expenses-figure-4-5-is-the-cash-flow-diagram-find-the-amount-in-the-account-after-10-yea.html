we see that the future worth by Equation [4.10]

F = P(F/P, effective i% per CP, total number of periods n) [4.10]

is \(F = 1000(F/P,6\%,20) + 3000(F/P,6\%, 12) + 1500(F/P,6\%,8)\)
\(=1000(3.2071) + 3000(2.0122) + 1500(1.5938)\)
\(= \$11 ,634\)

Method 2: Express the effective annual rate, based on semiannual compounding.

Effective \(i\%\) per year =\(\left(1+\frac{0.12}{2} \right) ^2 \ -1=12.36\%\)

The n value is the actual number of years. Use the factor formula\( (F/ P,i ,n) = (1.1236)^{n}\) and Equation [4.10] to obtain the same answer as with method \(1.\)

\(F = 1000(F/ P,12.36\%,10) + 3000(F/ P,12.36\%,6) + 1500(F/P,12.36\%,4)\)
\(= 1000(3.2071) + 3000(2.0122) + 1500(1.5938)\)
\(= \$11 ,634\)
