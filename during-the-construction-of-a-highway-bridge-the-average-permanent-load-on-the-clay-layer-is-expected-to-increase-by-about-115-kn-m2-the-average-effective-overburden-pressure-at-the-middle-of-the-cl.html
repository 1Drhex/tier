Guest Question.

During the construction of a highway bridge, the average permanent load on the clay layer is expected to increase by about 115 kN/\(\textbf{m}^{\textbf{2}}\). The average effective overburden pressure at the middle of the clay layer is 210 kN/\(\textbf{m}^{\textbf{2}}\). Here, H = 6 m, \(\textbf{C}_{\textbf{c}}\) = 0.28, \(\textbf{e}_{\textbf{0}}\) = 0.9, and \(\textbf{c}_{\textbf{v}}\) = 0.36\( \textbf{m}^{\textbf{2}}\)/mo. The clay is normally consolidated.

a. Determine the total primary consolidation settlement of the bridge without precompression.
b. What is the surcharge, \(\pmb{\Delta} \pmb{\sigma} _{\textbf{(f)}}\), needed to eliminate by precompression the entire primary consolidation settlement in 9 months?
c. Redo part b with the addition of some sand drains. Assume that \(\textbf{r}_{\textbf{w}}\) = 0.1 m, \(\textbf{d}_{\textbf{e}}\) = 3 m, and \(\textbf{c}_{\textbf{v}} = \textbf{c}_{\textbf{vr}}\).

Admin
Solution
Part a.

The total primary consolidation settlement will be calculated from Eq. (7.56):

\(S_{p}=\frac{C_{c} H}{1 +e _{0}} \log \left[\frac{\sigma_{o}^{\prime}+\Delta \sigma_{(p)}}{\sigma_{o}^{\prime}}\right]=\frac{(0.28)(6)}{1 + 0.9} \log \left[\frac{210 + 115}{210}\right]\\ =0.1677 = \textbf{167.7 mm}\)
Part b.
\(T_{v} = \frac{c_{v}t_{2}}{H^{2}_{dr}}\\c_{v} = 0.36\,m^{2}/mo.\\H_{dr} = 3\,m\,\text{(two-way drainage)}\\t_{2} = 9\,mo.\\\text{Hence,}\\T_{v} = \frac{(0.36)(9)}{3^{2}} = 0.36\)

According to Figure 7.31, for \(T_{v} = 0.36,\) the value of U is 47%. Now

\(\Delta\sigma_{(p)} = 115\,kN/m^{2}\\\sigma^{\prime}_{o} = 210\,kN/m^{2}\\\text{So}\\\frac{\Delta\sigma_{(p)}}{\sigma^{\prime}_{o}} = \frac{115}{210} = 0.548\)

According to Figure 7.29, for U = 47% and \(\Delta\sigma_{(p)}\sigma^{\prime}_{o} = 0.548,\,\Delta\sigma_{(f)}/\Delta\sigma_{(p)} \approx 1.8.\\\text{So}\\\Delta\sigma_{(f)} = (1.8)(115) = \textbf{207 kN/}\textbf{m}^{\textbf{2}}\)

Part c.

From Part b, \(T_{v}\)= 0.36.The value of \(U_{v}\) from Table 7.3 is about 67%. From Eq. (7.65),

\(n = \frac{d_{e}}{2r_{w}} = \frac{3}{2 \times 0.1} = 15\\\text{Again,}\\T_{r} = \frac{c_{vr}t_{2}}{d^{2}_{e}} = \frac{(0.36)(9)}{(3)^{2}} = 0.36\)

From Table 7.5 for n = 15 and \(T_{r}\) = 0.36,the value of \(U_{r}\) is about 77%. Hence,

\(U_{ v,r} = 1 – (1 – U_{v}) (1 – U_{r}) = 1 – (1 – 0.67)(1 – 0.77)\\ =0.924 =92.4\%\)

Now, from Figure 7.29 for \(\Delta\sigma_{(p)}/\sigma^{\prime}_{o} = 0.548\text{ and }U_{ v,r} =92.4\%,\text{ the value of } \Delta\sigma_{(f)}/\Delta\sigma_{(p)}\approx 0.12.\text{ Hence, we have}\\\Delta\sigma_{(f)} = (115)(0.12) = \textbf{13.8 kN/}\textbf{m}^{\textbf{2}}\)
