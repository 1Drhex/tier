ISENTROPIC FLOW IN A CONVERGING CHANNEL

Air flows isentropically in a channel. At section (1), the Mach number is 0.3, the area is 0.001m\(^2\), and the absolute pressure and the temperature are 650 kPa and 62째C, respectively. At section (2), the Mach number is 0.8. Sketch the channel shape, plot a Ts diagram for the process, and evaluate properties at section (2). Verify that the results agree with the basic equations, Eqs. 13.2.

\(\begin{gathered} \rho_{1} V_{1} A_{1}=\rho_{2} V_{2} A_{2}=\rho V A=\dot{m}=\text { constant } \\\quad\\R_{x}+p_{1} A_{1}-p_{2} A_{2}=\dot{m} V_{2}-\dot{m} V_{1} \\\quad\\h_{0_{1}}=h_{1}+\frac{V_{1}^{2}}{2}=h_{2}+\frac{V_{2}^{2}}{2}=h_{0_{2}}=h_{0} \\\quad\\s_{2}=s_{1}=s \\\quad\\p=\rho R T \\\quad\\\Delta h=h_{2}-h_{1}=c_{p} \Delta T=c_{p}\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right) \\\quad\\\frac{p_{1}}{\rho_{1}^{k}}=\frac{p_{2}}{\rho_{2}^{k}}=\frac{p}{\rho^{k}}=\text { constant }\end{gathered}\) (13.2 a,b,c,d,e,f,g)

Admin
Solution

Given: Isentropic flow of air in a channel. At sections (1) and (2), the following data are given: \(M_{1}=0.3, T_{1}=62^{\circ} \mathrm{C}, p_{1}=650 \mathrm{kPa}(\mathrm{abs}), A_{1}=0.001 \mathrm{~m}^{2}\), and \(M_{2}=0.8\).
Find:

(a) The channel shape.
(b) A Ts diagram for the process.
(c) Properties at section (2).
(d) Show that the results satisfy the basic equations.
Solution:
To accelerate a subsonic flow requires a converging nozzle. The channel shape must be as shown.
On the Ts plane, the process follows an \(s=\) constant line. Stagnation conditions remain fixed for isentropic flow. Consequently, the stagnation temperature at section (2) can be calculated (for air, \(k=1.4\) ) from Eq. 13.7b.

\(\begin{aligned}T_{0_{2}} &=T_{0_{1}}=T_{1}\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{1}^{2}\right] \\\quad\\&=(62+273) \mathrm{K}\left[1+0.2(0.3)^{2}\right] \\\quad\\T_{0_{2}} &=T_{0_{1}}=341 \mathrm{~K}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad T_{0_{2}},T_{0_{1}}}\end{aligned}\)

 

For \(p_{0_{2}}\), from Eq. 13.7a,

\(\begin{aligned}&p_{0_{2}}=p_{0_{1}}=p_{1}\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{1}^{2}\right]^{k /(k-1)}=650 \mathrm{kPa}\left[1+0.2(0.3)^{2}\right]^{3.5} \\\quad\\&p_{0_{2}}=692 \mathrm{kPa}(\mathrm{abs})\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad p_{0_{2}}}\end{aligned}\)

 

For \(T_{2}\), from Eq. 13.7 b,

\(T_{2}=T_{0_{2}} /\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{2}^{2}\right]=341 \mathrm{~K} /\left[1+0.2(0.8)^{2}\right]\) \(T_{2}=302 \mathrm{~K}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad T_2}\)

 

For \(p_{2}\), from Eq. 13.7 a,

\(\begin{aligned}&p_{2}=p_{02} /\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{2}^{2}\right]^{k / k-1}=692 \mathrm{kPa} /\left[1+0.2(0.8)^{2}\right]^{3.5} \\\quad\\&p_{2}=454 \mathrm{kPa}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad p_{2}}\end{aligned}\)

 

Note that we could have directly computed \(T_{2}\) from \(T_{1}\) because \(T_{0}=\) constant:

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}=\frac{T_{2}}{T_{0}} / \frac{T_{0}}{T_{1}}=\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{1}^{2}\right] /\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{2}^{2}\right]=\left[1+0.2(0.3)^{2}\right] /\left[1+0.2(0.8)^{2}\right] \\\quad\\&\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}=\frac{0.8865}{0.9823}=0.9025\end{aligned}\)

Hence,

\(T_{2}=0.9025 T_{1}=0.9025(273+62) \mathrm{K}=302 \mathrm{~K}\)

 

Similarly, for \(p_{2}\),

\(\frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}=\frac{p_{2}}{p_{0}} / \frac{p_{0}}{p_{1}}=0.8865^{3.5} / 0.9823^{3.5}=0.6982\)

Hence,

\(p_{2}=0.6982 p_{1}=0.6982(650 \mathrm{kPa})=454 \mathrm{kPa}\)

 

The density \(\rho_{2}\) at section (2) can be found from Eq. 13.7c using the same procedure we used for \(T_{2}\) and \(p_{2}\), or we can use the ideal gas equation of state, Eq. 13.2e,

\(\rho_{2}=\frac{p_{2}}{R T_{2}}=4.54 \times 10^{5} \frac{\mathrm{N}}{\mathrm{m}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}}{287 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m}} \times \frac{1}{302 \mathrm{~K}}=5.24 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad \rho_{2}}\)

 

and the velocity at section (2) is

\(V_{2}=M_{2} c_{2}=M_{2} \sqrt{k R T_{2}}=0.8 \times \sqrt{1.4 \times 287 \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}} \times 302 \mathrm{~K} \times \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}^{2} \cdot \mathrm{N}}}=279 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad V_{2}}\)

 

The area \(A_{2}\) can be computed from Eq. 13.7 d, noting that \(A^{*}\) is constant for this flow,

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}} &\left.=\frac{A_{2}}{A^{*}} \frac{A^{*}}{A_{1}}=\frac{1}{M_{2}}\left[\frac{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{2}^{2}}{\frac{k+1}{2}}\right]^{(k+1) / 2(k-1)}\right] \frac{1}{M_{1}}\left[\frac{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{1}^{2}}{\frac{k+1}{2}}\right]^{(k+1) / 2(k-1)} \\\quad\\&=\frac{1}{0.8}\left[\frac{1+0.2(0.8)^{2}}{1.2}\right]^{3} / \frac{1}{0.3}\left[\frac{1+0.2(0.3)^{2}}{1.2}\right]^{3}=\frac{1.038}{2.035}=0.5101\end{aligned}\)

 

Hence,

\(A_{2}=0.5101 A_{1}=0.5101\left(0.001 \mathrm{~m}^{2}\right)=5.10 \times 10^{-4} \mathrm{~m}^{2}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad A_2}\)

 

Note that \(A_{2}<A_{1}\) as expected.
Let us verify that these results satisfy the basic equations.
We first need to obtain \(\rho_{1}\) and \(V_{1}\) :

\(\rho_{1}=\frac{p_{1}}{R T_{1}}=6.5 \times 10^{5} \frac{\mathrm{N}}{\mathrm{m}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}}{287 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m}} \times \frac{1}{335 \mathrm{~K}}=6.76 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\)

 

and

\(V_{1}=M_{1} c_{1}=M_{1} \sqrt{k R T_{1}}=0.3 \times \sqrt{1.4 \times 287 \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}} \times 335 \mathrm{~K} \times \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}^{2} \cdot \mathrm{N}}}=110 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\)

 

The mass conservation equation is

\(\rho_{1} V_{1} A_{1}=\rho_{2} V_{2} A_{2}=\rho V A=\dot{m}=\text { constant }\) (13.2 a)

 

\(\dot{m}=6.76 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}} \times 110 \frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}} \times 0.001 \mathrm{~m}^{2}=5.24 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}} \times 279 \frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}} \times 0.00051 \mathrm{~m}^{2}=0.744 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{s}\) (Check!)

 

We cannot check the momentum equation (Eq. 13.2b) because we do not know the force \(R_{x}\) produced by the walls of the device (we could use Eq. 13.2b to compute this if we wished). The energy equation is

\(h_{0_{1}}=h_{1}+\frac{V_{1}^{2}}{2}=h_{2}+\frac{V_{2}^{2}}{2}=h_{0_{2}}=h_{0}\) (13.2c)

 

We will check this by replacing enthalpy with temperature using Eq. 13.2f,

\(\Delta h=h_{2}-h_{1}=c_{p} \Delta T=c_{p}\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right)\) (13.2f)

 

so the energy equation becomes

\(c_{p} T_{1}+\frac{V_{1}^{2}}{2}=c_{p} T_{2}+\frac{V_{2}^{2}}{2}=c_{p} T_{0}\)

 

Using \(c_{p}\) for air from Table A.6,

\(\begin{aligned}c_{p} T_{1}+\frac{V_{1}^{2}}{2} &=1004 \frac{\mathrm{J}}{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}} \times 335 \mathrm{~K}+\frac{(110)^{2}}{2}\left(\frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}}\right)^{2} \times \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}{\mathrm{~kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}} \times \frac{\mathrm{J}}{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{m}}=342 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \\\quad\\c_{p} T_{2}+\frac{V_{2}^{2}}{2} &=1004 \frac{\mathrm{J}}{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}} \times 302 \mathrm{~K}+\frac{(278)^{2}}{2}\left(\frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}}\right)^{2} \times \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}{\mathrm{~kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}} \times \frac{\mathrm{J}}{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{m}}=342 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \\\quad\\c_{p} T_{0} &=1004 \frac{\mathrm{J}}{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}} \times 341 \mathrm{~K}=342 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}\quad\quad\quad \text{(Check!)}\end{aligned}\)

 

The final equation we can check is the relation between pressure and density for an isentropic process (Eq. 13.2g),

\(\begin{gathered}\frac{p_{1}}{\rho_{1}^{k}}=\frac{p_{2}}{\rho_{2}^{k}}=\frac{p}{\rho^{k}}=\text { constant }\quad\quad\quad \text{(Check!)} \\\quad\\\frac{p_{1}}{\rho_{1}^{1.4}}=\frac{650 \mathrm{kPa}}{\left(6.76 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}}\right)^{1.4}}=\frac{p_{2}}{\rho_{2}^{1.4}}=\frac{454 \mathrm{kPa}}{\left(5.24 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}}\right)^{1.4}}=44.7 \frac{\mathrm{kPa}}{\left(\frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}}\right)^{1.4}}\quad\quad \text{(Check!)}\end{gathered}\)

 

The basic equations are satisfied by our solution.

 

This Example illustrates:

  • Use of the isentropic equations, Eqs.13.7.
  • That the isentropic equations are consistent with the basic equations, Eqs. 13.2.
  • That the computations can be quite laborious without using preprogrammed isentropic relations (available, for example, in the Excel add-ins on the Web site)!

The Excel workbook for this Example is convenient for performing the calculations, using either the isentropic equations or the basic equations.
