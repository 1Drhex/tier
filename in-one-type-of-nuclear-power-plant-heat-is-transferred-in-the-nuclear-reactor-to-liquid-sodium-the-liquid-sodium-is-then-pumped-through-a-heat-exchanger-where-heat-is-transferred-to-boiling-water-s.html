Guest Question.

In one type of nuclear power plant, heat is transferred in the nuclear reactor to liquid sodium. The liquid sodium is then pumped through a heat exchanger where heat is transferred to boiling water. Saturated vapor steam at 5 MPa exits this heat exchanger and is then superheated to 600째C in an external gas-fired superheater. The steam enters the turbine, which has one (open-type) feedwater extraction at 0.4 MPa. The condenser pressure is 10 kPa. Determine the heat transfer in the reactor and in the superheater to produce a net power output of 5 MW.

Admin
Solution

The complete cycle diagram is similar to Figure 9.12 except the boiler is separated into a section heated by the reactor and a super heater section.

CV. Pump P1

\(\begin{aligned}& w _{ P 1}=0.001008(400-7.5)=0.4 kJ / kg; \\& h _{2}= h _{1}+ w _{ P 1}=168.8+0.4=169.2 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

CV. Pump P2

\(\begin{aligned}& w _{ P 2}=0.001084(5000-400)=5.0 kJ / kg \\& h _{4}= h _{3}+ w _{ P 2}=604.7+5.0=609.7 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

C.V. Turbine (to get exit state properties)

\(\begin{aligned}& s _{7}= s _{6}=7.2589, P _{7}=0.4 MPa \Rightarrow T _{7}=221.2^{\circ} C , h _{7}=2904.5 kJ / kg \\& s _{8}= s _{6}=7.2589=0.6492+ x _{8} \times 7.501 \quad x _{8}=0.88118 \\& h _{8}=191.81+0.88118 \times 2392.82=2300.3 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

CV: Feedwater heater FWH (to get the extraction fraction \(x _{7}\) )

Divide the equations with the total mass flow rate \(\dot{ m }_{3}=\dot{ m }_{4}=\dot{ m }_{5}=\dot{ m }_{6}\)

Continuity: \(x _{2}+ x _{7}= x _{3}=1.0\) , Energy Eq.: \(x _{2} h _{2}+ x _{7} h _{7}= h _{3}\)

\(x _{7}=(604.7-169.2) /(2904.5-169.2)=0.1592\)

CV: Turbine (to get the total specific work)
Full flow from 6 to 7 and the fraction \(\left(1-x_{7}\right)\) from 7 to 8.

\(\begin{aligned}w _{ T } &=\left( h _{6}- h _{7}\right)+\left(1- x _{7}\right)\left( h _{7}- h _{8}\right) \\&=3666.5-2904.5+0.8408(2904.5-2300.3)=1270.0 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

CV: Pumps \(\left( P 1 \text { has } x _{1}=1- x _{7}, \quad P 2 \text { has the full flow } x _{3}=1\right. \text { ) }\)

\(\begin{aligned}& w _{ P }= x _{1} w _{ P 1}+ x _{3} w _{ P 2}=0.8408 \times 0.4+1 \times 5.0=5.3 kJ / kg \\& w _{ NET }=1270.0-5.3=1264.7 \Rightarrow \dot{ m }=5000 / 1264.7=3.954 kg / s\end{aligned}\)

CV: Reactor (this has the full flow)

\(\dot{ Q }_{ REACT }=\dot{ m }\left( h _{5}- h _{4}\right)=3.954(2794.3-609.7)= 8 6 3 8 k W\)

CV: Superheater (this has the full flow)

\(\dot{ Q }_{ SUP }=\dot{ m }\left( h _{6}- h _{5}\right)=3.954(3666.5-2794.3)= 3 4 4 9 kW\)
