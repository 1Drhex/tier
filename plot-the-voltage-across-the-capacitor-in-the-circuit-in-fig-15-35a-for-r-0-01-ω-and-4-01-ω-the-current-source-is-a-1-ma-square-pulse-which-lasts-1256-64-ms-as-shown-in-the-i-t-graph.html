Guest Question.

Plot the voltage across the capacitor in the circuit in Fig. 15-35(a) for R = 0.01 Ω and 4.01 Ω. The current source is a 1 mA square pulse which lasts 1256.64 ms as shown in the i – t graph.

Admin
Solution

Model the resistor as a single-parameter resistor element with a single parameter R and change the value of R from 0.01 to 4.01 in step of 4. We use the .AC command to sweep the frequency from 500 Hz to 3 kHz in 100 steps. The source file is

Pulse response of RLC with variable R

\(\begin{array}{llllllllll}\text { Is } & 0 & 1 & \text { Pulse }(0 & 1 m & 100 u & 0.01 u & 0.01 u & 1256.64 u & 5000 u ) \\R & 1 & 2 & \text { LOSS } & 1 & & & & \\C & 1 & 0 & 2000 n & IC =0 & & & & \\L & 2 & 0 & 5 m & IC =0 & & & &\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{array}{llllll}\text { MODEL } & \text { LOSS } & \text { RES(R }=1) \\\text {.STEP } & \text { RES } & \text { LOSS }( R ) & .01 & 4.01 & 4\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\text {.TRAN } \quad 10 u \quad 3500 u & 0 & 1 u & \text { UIC } \\&\text {.PROBE } \\&\text {.END }\end{aligned}\)

The result is shown in Fig. 15-35(b).
The transient response is almost zero for R = 0.01 Ω. This is because pulse width is a multiple of the period of natural oscillations of the circuit.
