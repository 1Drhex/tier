Guest Question.

he following reaction mechanism was proposed to explain the formation of polyurethane and polyesters.
Initiation:
\(RSH\overset{k_{1}}{\rightarrow}RS^{\bullet }+H^{\bullet }\)

Transfer:
\(H^{\bullet }+RSH\overset{k_{2}}{\rightarrow}H_{2}+RS^{\bullet }\)

Propagation:
\(RS^{\bullet }+CH_{2}=CHR'\overset{k_{3}}{\rightarrow}RSCH_{2}-CH^{*}R^{'}\)
\(RSCH_{2}-CH^{*}R'+RSH\overset{k_{4}}{\rightarrow}RSCH_{2}CH_{2}R'\)

Termination:
\(2RS^{\bullet }\overset{k_5}{\rightarrow}RSSR\)

Determine the rate of disappearance of thiol (RSH). The stoichiometry of the global reaction is:
\(RSH+CH_{2}CHR'\overset{k}{\rightarrow}RSCH_{2}-CH^{*}R'\)

Check the experimental results and determine the specific kinetic constant, assuming an irreversible reaction. Compare it with the global reaction rate.

Admin
Solution

Naming:\( A=RSH, R_{1} =RS\);\( MR_{1} =RSCH_{2}–C^{∗}HR'\) , \(M=CH_{2} =CHR\) , and P=RSSR.
Then, the mechanism can be written simply by the following equations:
\(A\overset{k_{1}}{\rightarrow}R_{1}^{\bullet }+H^{\bullet }\)
\(H^{\bullet }+A \overset{k_{2}}{\rightarrow}H_{2}+R_{1}^{\bullet }\)
\(R_{1}^{\bullet }+M \overset{k_{3}}{\rightarrow}MR_{1}^{\bullet }\)
\(MR_{1}^{\bullet }+A\overset{k_{4}}{\rightarrow}R_{1}^{\bullet }+R_{2}\)
\(2R_{1}^{\bullet }\overset{k_{5}}{\rightarrow}P\)

The rate of disappearance of A is:
\(-r_{A}k_{1}[A]+k_{2}[A][H^{\bullet }]+k_{4}[MR_{1}^{\bullet }][A]\) (11.1)
\(r_{H^{\bullet }}=k_{1}[A]-k_{2}[A][H^{\bullet }]=0\) (11.2)
\([H^{\bullet }]=\frac{k_{1}}{k_{2}}\) (11.3)
\(r_{MR_{1}^{\bullet }}=k_{3}[R_{1}^{\bullet }][M]-k_{4}[MR_{1}^{\bullet }][A]=0\) (11.4)
\(r_{R_{1}^{\bullet }}=k_{1}[A]+\underset{k_{1}[A]}{\underbrace{k_{2}[A][H^{\bullet }]}}-k_{3}[R_{1}^{\bullet }][M]+k_{4}[MR_{1}^{\bullet }][A]-k_{5}[R_{1}^{\bullet }]^{2}\) (11.5)

Thus,
\(2k_{1}[A]=k_{5}[R_{1}^{\bullet }]^{2}\)
or:
\([R_{1}]=\sqrt{\frac{2k_{1}[A]}{k_{5}}}\) (11.6)

From Equation 11.4 we get:
\(k_{3}[R_{1}][M]=k_{4}[MR_{1}^{\bullet }][A]\)
\([MR_{1}]=\frac{k_{3}[R_{1}^{\bullet }][M]}{k_{4}[A]}\) (11.7)

Substituting Equation 11.6 in Equation 11.7, we obtain:
\([MR_{1}^{\bullet }]=\frac{k_{3}[R_{1}][M]}{k_{4}[A]}\sqrt{\frac{2k_{1}[A]}{k_{5}}}\) (11.8)

Substituting Equation 11.8 in Equation 11.1, we obtain:
\(-r_{A}=k_{1}[A]+k_{2}[A][H^{\bullet }]+k_{4}[MR_{1}][A]\)
\(-r_{A}=2k_{1}[A]+k_{3}[M]\sqrt{\frac{2k_{1}[A]}{k_{5}}}\)

Neglect the first term of this equation, which represents the initial rate, when compared to step 3. Therefore, the rate expression of disappearance of A is as follows:
\(-r_{A}=k_{3}[M]\sqrt{\frac{2k_{1}[A]}{k_{5}}}\) (11.9)

The concentration of the monomer M in the rate equation indicates that it is a first-order reaction in relation to the monomer.
Note that the concentration of reactant [A] varies with time.
Table below presents data of reactant [A] varying with time.

t(s) 0 350 400 500 640 750 830
[A] 0.874 0.223 0.51 0.478 0.432 0.382 0.343

For the global reaction, the rate of disappearance of [A] is second order:
\(RSH + CH_{2}=CHR'\rightarrow k RSCH_{2} − C^{·}HR'\)
\(A+M \rightarrow MR\)

Thus, the rate equation is:
\(−r_{A} =k[A][M]=k[A]^{2}\) (11.10)

Assuming equal initial concentrations of A and M: \([A_{0}]=[M_{0}]\)
Therefore, comparing these equations, we conclude that the rate expressions are different:
\(−r_{A} =k[A]^{1/2}[M]\) (11.11)

Verifying if the rate of the global reaction 11.10 is correct, assuming a second-order and irreversible reaction.
The expression as function of conversion is:
\((-r_{A})=kC_{A}^{2}=kC_{A0}^{2}(1-X_{A})^{2}\)

Substituting in the batch reactor equation, we get:
\(\frac{X_{A}}{(1-X_{A})}=kC_{A0}t\) (11.12)

Verifying Equation 11.11, assuming proportionality we get:
\(-r_{A}=k[A]^{1/2}[M]\)
\((-r_{A})=kC_{A}^{1/2}CM=kC_{A0}^{3/2}(1-X_{A})^{3/2}\) (11.13)

Substituting this equation in the batch reactor equation and further integration results in:
\(2\times \left [ \frac{1}{\sqrt{\left ( 1-X_{A} \right )}}-1 \right ]=kC_{A0}.t\) (11.14)

We calculate the specific constants from the following table 11.4 (last two columns).

Note that the specific kinetic constant for the global reaction is 2.34× \(10^{−3}\) L/mol min, while for the real model it is likely different, or 1.77×\(10^{−3}\) L/mol min.
