Guest Question.

For the circuit in Fig. 15.46,

a. Calculate \(I, V_R, V_L, and V_C\) in phasor form.

b. Calculate the total power factor.

c. Calculate the average power delivered to the circuit.

d. Draw the phasor diagram.

e. Obtain the phasor sum of \(V_R, V_L, and V_C\), and show that it equals the input voltage E.

f. Find \(V_R and V_C\) using the voltage divider rule.

Admin
Solution

a. Combining common elements and finding the reactance of the inductor and capacitor, we obtain

\(R_T=6 Ω + 4 Ω = 10 Ω\\[0.5cm] L_T = 0.05 H + 0.05 H = 0.1 H\\[0.5cm] C_T=\frac{200\mu F}{2}=100\mu F\\[0.5cm] X_L = \omega L = (377 rad/s)(0.1 H) = 37.70 Ω\\[0.5cm] X_C=\frac{1}{\omega C}=\frac{1}{(377rad/s)(100\times10^{-6}F)}=\frac{10^6\Omega}{37,700}=26.53 Ω \)

 

Redrawing the circuit using phasor notation results in Fig. 15.47.

For the circuit in Fig. 15.47,

\(Z_T=R ∠0° + X_L ∠90° + X_C ∠-90°\\[0.5cm] = 10 Ω + j 37.70 Ω – j 26.53 Ω=10 Ω + j 11.17 Ω = 15\Omega\angle 48.16°\)

The current I is

\(I=\frac{E}{Z_T}= \frac{20 V ∠0°}{15 Ω ∠48.16°}=1.33A\angle -48.16° \)

The voltage across the resistor, inductor, and capacitor can be found using Ohm’s law:

\(V_R=IZ_R=(I ∠\theta)(R ∠0°) = (1.33 A ∠-48.16°)(10 Ω ∠0°)=13.30V\angle -48.16°\\[0.5cm] V_L=IZ_L=(I ∠\theta)(X_L ∠90°) = (1.33 A ∠-48.16°)(37.70 Ω ∠90°)=50.14V\angle 41.84°\\[0.5cm] V_C=IZ_C=(I ∠\theta)(X_C ∠-90°) = (1.33 A ∠-48.16°)(26.53 Ω ∠-90°)=35.28V\angle -138.16°\)

 

b. The total power factor, determined by the angle between the applied voltage E and the resulting current I, is 48.16°:

\(F_p=cos\theta=cos48.16° =0.667\; lagging\\[0.5cm] or\qquad F_p=cos\theta=\frac{R}{Z_T}=\frac{10 Ω}{15 Ω}=0.667\; lagging \)

 

c. The total power in watts delivered to the circuit is

\(P_T = EIcos \theta = (20 V)(1.33 A)(0.667) = 17.74 W\)

 

d. The phasor diagram appears in Fig. 15.48.

e. The phasor sum of \(V_R, V_L, and V_C\) is

\(E=V_R+V_L+V_C\\[0.5cm] =13.30 V ∠-48.16° + 50.14 V ∠41.84° + 35.28 V ∠-138.16°\\[0.5cm] E=13.30 V ∠-48.16° + 14.86 V ∠41.84°\)

Therefore,

\(E=\sqrt{(13.30V)^2+(14.86V)^2} =20V\\[0.5cm] and\quad \theta_E=0°\hspace{30 pt} (from \> phasor \> diagram)\\[0.5cm] and\hspace{30 pt} E = 20 V ∠0°\)

 

f. \(V_R=\frac{Z_RE}{Z_T}=\frac{(10 Ω ∠0°)(20 V ∠0°)}{15 Ω ∠48.16°}=\frac{200 V ∠0°}{15 ∠48.16°}=13.3V\angle -48.16°\\[0.5cm] V_C=\frac{Z_CE}{Z_T}=\frac{(26.5 Ω ∠-90°)(20 V ∠0°)}{15 Ω ∠48.16°}=\frac{530.6 V ∠-90°}{15 ∠48.16°}=35.37V\angle -138.16° \)
