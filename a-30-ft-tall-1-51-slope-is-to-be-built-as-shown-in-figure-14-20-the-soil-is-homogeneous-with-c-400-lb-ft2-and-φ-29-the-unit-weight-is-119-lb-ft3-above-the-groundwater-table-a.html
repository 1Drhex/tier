Guest Question.

A 30 ft tall, 1.5:1 slope is to be built as shown in Figure 14.20. The soil is homogeneous, with \(c^{\prime }= 400 lb/ft^{2}\) and \(Φ^{\prime } = 29°\). The unit weight is \(119 lb/ft^{3}\) above the groundwater table, and \(123 lb/ft^{3}\) below. Using the ordinary method of slices and an effective stress analysis, compute the factor of safety along the trial circle shown in Figure 14.22.

Admin
Solution

Weights:

\(W_{1}/b=10.8\left\lgroup\frac{10.3}{2} \right\rgroup 119=6,620 lb/ft\)

 

\(W_{2}/b=9.4\left\lgroup\frac{10.3 + 12.5}{2} \right\rgroup 119 + 9.4\left\lgroup\frac{5.2}{2} \right\rgroup 123=15,800 lb/ft\)

 

\(W_{3}/b=12.1\left\lgroup\frac{12.5 + 14.6}{2} \right\rgroup 119 + 12.1\left\lgroup\frac{5.2 + 10.0}{2} \right\rgroup 123=30,800 lb/ft\)

 

\(W_{4}/b=12.7\left\lgroup\frac{14.6 + 16.8}{2} \right\rgroup 119 + 12.7\left\lgroup\frac{10.0 + 10.7}{2} \right\rgroup 123=39,900 lb/ft\)

 

\(W_{5}/b=9.3\left\lgroup\frac{16.8 + 12.8}{2} \right\rgroup 119 + 9.3\left\lgroup\frac{10.7 + 7.3}{2} \right\rgroup 123=26,700 lb/ft\)

 

\(W_{6}/b=7.6\left\lgroup\frac{12.8 + 9.9}{2} \right\rgroup 119 + 7.6\left\lgroup\frac{7.3}{2} \right\rgroup 123=13,700 lb/ft\)

 

\(W_{7}/b=4.0\left\lgroup\frac{9.9}{2} \right\rgroup 119=2,400 lb/ft\)

Average pore water pressure at base of each slice:

\(u_{1}=0\)

 

\(u_{2}=\left\lgroup\frac{5.2}{2} \right\rgroup 62.4=160 lb/ft^{2}\)

 

\(u_{3}=\left\lgroup\frac{5.2 + 10.0}{2} \right\rgroup 62.4=470 lb/ft^{2}\)

 

\(u_{4}=\left\lgroup\frac{10.0 + 10.7}{2} \right\rgroup 62.4=650 lb/ft^{2}\)

 

\(u_{5}=\left\lgroup\frac{10.7 + 7.3}{2} \right\rgroup 62.4=560 lb/ft^{2}\)

 

\(u_{6}=\left\lgroup\frac{7.3}{2} \right\rgroup 62.4=230 lb/ft^{2}\)

 

\(u_{7}=0\)

\(F=\frac{84,400}{46,100} =\) 1.83

Note how slices 1 and 2 have a negative a because they are inclined backwards.

Comments

Further trials with other circles will demonstrate that this is the critical circle (i.e., it is the one with the lowest factor of safety). Therefore, according to the ordinary method of slices, the factor of safety of this slope is 1.83.
