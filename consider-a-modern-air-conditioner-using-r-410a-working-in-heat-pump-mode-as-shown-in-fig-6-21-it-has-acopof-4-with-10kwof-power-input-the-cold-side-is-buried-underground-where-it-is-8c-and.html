Guest Question.

Consider a modern air conditioner using R-410a working in heat pump mode, as shown in Fig. 6.21. It has aCOPof 4 with 10kWof power input. The cold side is buried underground, where it is 8°C, and the hot side is a house kept at 21°C. For simplicity, assume that the cycle has a high temperature of 50°C and a low temperature of −10°C . We would like to know where entropy is generated associated with the heat pump, assuming steady-state operation.

Admin
Solution

Let us look first at the heat pump itself, as in CVHP, so from the COP

\(\dot{Q}_{H}=\beta_{H P} \times \dot{W}=4 \times 10 kW =40 kW\)

Energy Eq.: \(Q_{L}=Q_{H}-\dot{W}=40 kW -10 kW =30 kW\)

Entropy Eq.: \(0=\frac{Q_{L}}{T_{\text {low }}}-\frac{\dot{Q}_{H}}{T_{\text {high }}}+\dot{S}_{\text {gen } H P}\)

\(\dot{S}_{ gen H P}=\frac{\dot{Q}_{H}}{T_{ high }}-\frac{\dot{Q}_{L}}{T_{ low }}=\frac{40 kW }{323 K }-\frac{30 kW }{263 K }=9.8 W / K\)

Now consider \(C V_{1}\) from the underground 8°C to the cycle −10°C.

Entropy Eq.: \(0=\frac{\dot{Q}_{L}}{T_{L}}-\frac{\dot{Q}_{L}}{T_{\text {low }}}+\dot{S}_{ gen C V_{1}}\)

\(\dot{S}_{\operatorname{gen} C V_{1}}=\frac{Q_{L}}{T_{\text {low }}}-\frac{Q_{L}}{T_{L}}=\frac{30 kW }{263 K }-\frac{30 kW }{281 K }=7.3 W / K\)

And finally, consider \(C V_{2}\) from the heat pump at 50°C to the house at 21°C.

Entropy Eq.: \(0=\frac{\dot{Q}_{H}}{T_{\text {high }}}-\frac{\dot{Q}_{H}}{T_{H}}+\dot{S}_{ gen C V_{2}}\)

\(\dot{S}_{ genCV _{2}}=\frac{\dot{Q}_{H}}{T_{H}}-\frac{\dot{Q}_{H}}{T_{ high }}=\frac{40 kW }{294 K }-\frac{40 kW }{323 K }=12.2 W / K\)

The total entropy generation rate becomes

\(\dot{S}_{\text {genTOT }}=\dot{S}_{ gen C V_{1}}+\dot{S}_{ gen C V_{2}}+\dot{S}_{ gen H P}\)

\(=\frac{\dot{Q}_{L}}{T_{\text {low }}}-\frac{\dot{Q}_{L}}{T_{L}}+\frac{\dot{Q}_{H}}{T_{H}}-\frac{\dot{Q}_{H}}{T_{\text {high }}}+\frac{\dot{Q}_{H}}{T_{\text {high }}}-\frac{\dot{Q}_{L}}{T_{\text {low }}}\)

\(=\frac{\dot{Q}_{H}}{T_{H}}-\frac{\dot{Q}_{L}}{T_{L}}=\frac{40 kW }{294 K }-\frac{30 kW }{281 K }=29.3 W / K\)

This last result is also obtained with a total control volume of the heat pump out to the 8°C and 21°C reservoirs that is the sum of the three control volumes shown. However, such an analysis would not be able to specify where the entropy is made; only the more detailed, smaller control volumes can provide this information.
