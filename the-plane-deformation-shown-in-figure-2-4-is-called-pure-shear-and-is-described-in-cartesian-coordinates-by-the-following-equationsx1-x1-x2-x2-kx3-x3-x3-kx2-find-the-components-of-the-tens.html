Guest Question.

The (plane) deformation shown in Figure 2.4 is called pure shear and is described in
Cartesian coordinates by the following equations:

\(\begin{matrix} x_{1} = X_{1}\\ x_{2}= X_{2}+kX_{3}\\ x_{3} = X_{3}+kX_{2} \end{matrix}\).

Find the components of the tensors F, C and E, the stretch ratios of the diagonal elements and the angle \(ϑ_{23}\) in the deformed configuration.

Admin
Solution

The matrix representation of the required tensors is the following:

\(\begin{bmatrix} F_{ij} \end{bmatrix} =\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & k \\ 0 & k & 1 \end{bmatrix} ; \begin{bmatrix} C_{ij} \end{bmatrix} =\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & k \\ 0 & k & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & k \\ 0 & k & 1 \end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1+k^2 & k \\ 0 & 2k & 1+k^2 \end{bmatrix}\)

\(\begin{bmatrix} E_{ij} \end{bmatrix}=\frac{1}{2}(\begin{bmatrix} C_{ij} \end{bmatrix}-\begin{bmatrix} \delta _{ij} \end{bmatrix})=\begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & {k^2}/{2} & k \\ 0 & k & {k^2}/{2} \end{bmatrix}\).

Since the stretch ratio \(\Lambda _{N}\) is obtained by using the relation: \(\Lambda _{N}^2=\pmb{N}\cdot \pmb{CN}\), when considering the unit vector N oriented as the diagonal \(\overline{OB}\) we have:

\(\Lambda_{OB}^2=[0,{1}/{\sqrt{2}}, {1}/{\sqrt{2}}]\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1+{k^2} & 2k \\ 0 & 2k & 1+{k^2} \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ {1}/{\sqrt{2}} \\{1}/{\sqrt{2}} \end{bmatrix} =(1+k)^2\).

Similarly, along the diagonal \(\overline{CA}\)

\(\Lambda_{CA}^2=[0,{-1}/{\sqrt{2}}, {1}/{\sqrt{2}}]\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1+{k^2} & 2k \\ 0 & 2k & 1+{k^2} \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ {-1}/{\sqrt{2}} \\{1}/{\sqrt{2}} \end{bmatrix} =(1-k)^2\).

Finally, the angle \(ϑ_{23}\) in the deformed configuration is given by: \(\cos ϑ_{23}=\frac{C_{23}}{\sqrt{C_{22}}\sqrt{C_{33}}} =\frac{2k}{1+k^2}\)
