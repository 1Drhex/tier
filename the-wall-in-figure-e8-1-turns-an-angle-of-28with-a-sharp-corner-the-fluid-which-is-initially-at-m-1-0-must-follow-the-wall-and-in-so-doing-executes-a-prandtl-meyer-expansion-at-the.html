Guest Question.

The wall in Figure E8.1 turns an angle of \(28^{\circ} \)with a sharp corner. The fluid, which is initially at M = 1.0, must follow the wall and in so doing executes a Prandtl–Meyer expansion at the corner. Recall that v always represents the angle (measured from the flow direction where M = 1.0) through which the flow has turned. Since \(M_{1}\) is unity, then \(v_{2}=28^{\circ} \) .
