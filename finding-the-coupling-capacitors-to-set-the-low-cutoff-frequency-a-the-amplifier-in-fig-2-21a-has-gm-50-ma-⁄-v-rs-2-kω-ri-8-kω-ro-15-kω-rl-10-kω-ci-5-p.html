Guest Question.

Finding the coupling capacitors to set the low cutoff frequency

(a) The amplifier in Fig. 2.21(a) has \( g_{ m }=50 mA / V , R_{ s }=2 k \Omega, R_{ i }=8 k \Omega, R_{ o }=15 k \Omega, R_{ L }=10 k \Omega , C_{ i }=5 pF \text {, and } C_{ o }=1 pF \). Calculate the coupling capacitances \( C_{1} \text { and } C_{2} \) in order to set the low 3-dB frequency at \( f_{ L }=1.5 kHz \), the pass-band gain \( \left(A_{ PB }\right) \), and the feedback capacitance C so that the frequency of the dominant pole is \( f_{ H }=100 kHz \).

(b) Use Miller’s method to find the high cutoff frequencies.

(c) Use PSpice/SPICE to plot the voltage gain against the frequency.

Admin
Solution

We have

\(\begin{aligned}&R_{ s }+R_{ i }=2 k +8 k =10 k \Omega \\&R_{ o }+R_{ L }=15 k +10 k =25 k \Omega \\&R_{1}=R_{ s }\left\|R_{ i }=2 k \right\| 8 k =1.6 k \Omega \\&\text { and } \quad R_{2}=R_{ o }\left\|R_{ L }=15 k \right\| 10 k =6 k \Omega\end{aligned}\)

 

(a) \( f_{ L }=1.5 kHz \text { and } f_{ H }=100 kHz \). Since

\( \left(R_{ s }+R_{ i }\right)=10 k \Omega<\left(R_{ o }+R_{ L }\right)=25 k \Omega \)

let us set \( f_{ Cl } \) equal to the low 3-dB frequency. That is, \( f_{ Cl }=f_{ L }=1.5 kHz \). The capacitance can be found from Eq. (2.83):

 

\( f_{ Cl }=\frac{1}{2 \pi C_{1}\left(R_{ s }+R_{ i }\right)} \) (2.83)

 

\( C_{1}=\frac{1}{\left.2 \pi f_{ L } R_{ s }+R_{ i }\right)}=\frac{1}{2 \pi \times 1.5 k \times(2 k +8 k )}=0.01 \mu F \)

 

Let \( f_{ C 2}=f_{ Cl } / 10=1.5 k / 10=150 Hz \). We get \( C_{2} \) from Eq. (2.84):

 

\( f_{ C 2}=\frac{1}{2 \pi C_{2}\left(R_{ o }+R_{ L }\right)} \) (2.84)

 

\( C_{2}=\frac{1}{2 \pi f_{ C 2}\left(R_{ o }+R_{ L }\right)}=\frac{1}{2 \pi \times 150 \times(15 k +10 k )}=0.04 \mu F \)

 

The pass-band gain is

\( A_{ PB }=-\frac{R_{ i } R_{ L } R_{ o } g_{ m }}{\left(R_{ s }+R_{ i }\right)\left(R_{ o }+R_{ L }\right)}=-\frac{8 k \times 10 k \times 15 k \times 50 mA / V }{(2 k +8 k )(15 k +10 k )}=-240 \)

 

From Eq. (2.93), we get the capacitance C for the dominant pole:

 

\( p_{1} \approx \frac{1}{g_{ m } C R_{1} R_{2}} \) (2.93)

 

\( C \approx \frac{1}{2 \pi f_{ H } g _{ m } R_{1} R_{2}}=\frac{1}{2 \pi \times 100 k \times 50 mA / V \times 1.6 k \times 6 k }=3.32 pF \)

 

From Eq. (2.91), we get

\( p_{1}=\frac{1}{R_{1}\left(C_{ i }+C\right)+R_{2}\left(C_{ o }+C\right)+g_{ m } C R_{1} R_{2}} \) (2.91)

 

\(\begin{aligned}f_{ H 1} &=\frac{10^{12}}{2 \pi[1.6 k \times(5+3.32)+6 k \times(1+3.32)+50 mA / V \times 3.32 \times 1.6 k \times 6 k ]} \\&=97.47 kHz\end{aligned}\)

 

From Eq. (2.92), we get

\( p_{2}=\frac{R_{1}\left(C_{ i }+C\right)+R_{2}\left(C_{ o }+C\right)+g_{ m } C R_{1} R_{2}}{R_{1} R_{2}\left(C_{ i } C_{ o }+C_{ i } C+C_{ o } C\right)} \) (2.92)

 

\(\begin{aligned}f_{ H 2} &=\frac{[1.6 k \times(5+3.32)+6 k \times(1+3.32)+50 mA / V \times 3.32 \times 1.6 k \times 6 k ] \times 10^{12}}{2 \pi[1.6 k \times 6 k \times(5 \times 1+5 \times 3.32+1 \times 3.32)]} \\&=1.09 GHz\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) From Eq. (2.98),

\( C_{ m }=C\left[1+g_{ m }\left(R_{ o } \| R_{ L }\right)\right] \) (2.98)

 

\( C_{ m }=C\left[1+g_{ m }\left(R_{ o } \| R_{ L }\right)\right]=(3.32 pF )(1+50 mA / V \times 6 k )=999.3 pF \)

 

From Eq. (2.99),

\( C_{ n }=C\left[1+\frac{1}{g_{ m }\left(R_{ o } \| R_{ L }\right)}\right] \) (2.99)

 

\( C_{ n }=C\left[1+\frac{1}{g_{ m }\left(R_{ o } \| R_{ L }\right)}\right]=(3.32 pF )\left(1+\frac{1}{300}\right)=3.33 pF \)

 

From Eqs. (2.100) and (2.101), we get

 

\( f_{ H 1}=\frac{1}{2 \pi\left(C_{ i }+C_{ m }\right)\left(R_{ s } \| R_{ i }\right)} \) (2.100)

 

\( f_{ H 2}=\frac{1}{2 \pi\left(C_{ o }+C_{ n }\right)\left(R_{ o } \| R_{ L }\right)} \) (2.101)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&f_{ H 1}=\frac{1}{2 \pi\left(C_{1}+C_{ m }\right)\left(R_{ s } \| R_{ i }\right)}=\frac{1}{2 \pi \times(5 pF +999.3 pF ) \times 1.6 k }=99.05 kHz \\&f_{ H 2}=\frac{1}{2 \pi\left(C_{ o }+C_{ n }\right)\left(R_{ o } \| R_{ L }\right)}=\frac{1}{2 \pi \times(1 pF +3.33 pF ) \times 6 k }=6.13 MHz\end{aligned}\)

 

Thus, Miller’s capacitor method gives \( f_{ H 1}=99.05 kHz \), compared to 97.47 kHz calculated by s-domain analysis.
However, \( f_{ H 2}=6.13 MHz \), compared to 1.09 GHz. The error is due to the fact that Miller’s method does not take into account the effect of pole splitting.

(c) The circuit for PSpice simulation is shown in Fig. 2.25. Let us assume an input voltage of \( v_{ s }=10 mV \).

The results of the simulation are shown in Fig. 2.26, which gives \( A_{\text {mid }}=221.7 \) (expected value is 240), \( f_{ L }=1.376 kHz \) (expected value is 1.5 kHz) at \( |A(j \omega)|=0.707 \times 221.7=156.7, \text { and } f_{ H }=107.23 kHz \) (expected value is 100 kHz) at \( |A(j \omega)|=0.707 \times 221.7=156.7 \).
