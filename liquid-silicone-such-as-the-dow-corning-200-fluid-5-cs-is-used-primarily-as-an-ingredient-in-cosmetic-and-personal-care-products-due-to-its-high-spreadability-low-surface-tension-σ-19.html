Guest Question.

Liquid silicone such as the Dow Corning \( 200^{\circledast}\) Fluid, 5 cs, is used primarily as an ingredient in cosmetic and personal care products due to its high spreadability, low surface tension \( \left(\sigma=19.7 \times 10^{-3} N / m \right) \), non-greasy, soft feel, and subtle skin lubricity characteristics. A two-phase flow of air and silicone (Dow Corning \( 200^{\circledast}\) Fluid, 5 cs is being transported in an 11.7 -mm-internaldiameter vertical tube. The liquid phase consists of liquid silicone with dynamic viscosity of \( 45.7 \times 10^{-4} kg / m \cdot s\), density of \( 913 kg / m ^{3}\), thermal conductivity of \( 0.117 W / m \cdot K\), and Prandtl number of 64 . The gas phase consists of air with dynamic viscosity of \( 18.4 \times 10^{-6} kg / m \cdot s\) density of \( 1.19 kg / m ^{3}\), and Prandtl number of 0.71. If the two-phase flow has flow quality of \( x=2.08 \times 10^{-5}\), void fraction of \( \alpha=0.011\), and liquid mass flow rate of \( \dot{m}=0.907 kg / s\), estimate the two-phase heat transfer coefficient \( h_{t p}\) .Assume the dynamic viscosity of liquid silicone evaluated at the tube surface temperature to be \( 39.8 \times 10^{-4} kg / m \cdot s\).

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION A two-phase flow of air and silicone (Dow Corning \( 200^{\odot}\) Fluid,5 cs is being transported in an 11.7- mm-diameter vertical tube. Using the non-boiling two-phase heat transfer correlation, the two-phase heat transfer coefficient \( h_{t p}\) is to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Steady operating condition exists. 2 Two-phase flow is nonboiling and it does not involve phase change. 3 Fluid properties are constant.

Properties The properties of liquid silicone (liquid) are given to be \( \mu_{l}=45.7 \times 10^{-4} kg / m \cdot s , \mu_{s}=39.8 \times 10^{-4} kg / m \cdot s , \rho_{l}=913 kg / m ^{3} , k_{l}=0.117 W / m \cdot K\)

\( \sigma=19.7 \times 10^{-3} N / m\) , and \( \operatorname{Pr}_{/}=64\). The properties of air (gas) are given to be \( \mu_{g}=18.4 \times 10^{-6} kg / m \cdot s , \rho_{g}=1.19 kg / m ^{3}\), and \( \operatorname{Pr}_{g}=0.71\).

Analysis With known liquid mass flow rate \( \dot{m}_{1}\) and quality x, the gas mass flow rate \( \dot{m}_{g}\) is determined using

\(\dot{m}_{g}=\frac{x}{1-x} \dot{m}_{1}=\frac{2.08 \times 10^{-5}}{1-2.08 \times 10^{-5}}(0.907 kg / s )=1.89 \times 10^{-5} kg / s\)

From the gas and liquid mass flow rates, the superficial gas and liquid velocities can be calculated:

\( V_{s g}=\frac{\dot{m}_{g}}{\rho_{g} A}=\frac{4 \dot{m}_{g}}{\rho_{g} \pi D^{2}}=\frac{4\left(1.89 \times 10^{-5} kg / s \right)}{\left(1.19 kg / m ^{3}\right) \pi(0.0117 m )^{2}}=0.148 m / s \)

 

\( V_{s l}=\frac{\dot{m}_{l}}{\rho_{l} A}=\frac{4 \dot{m}_{l}}{\rho_{l} \pi D^{2}}=\frac{4(0.907 kg / s )}{\left(913 kg / m ^{3}\right) \pi(0.0117 m )^{2}}=9.24 m / s\)

 

Using the superficial velocities and void fraction, the gas and liquid velocities are found to be

\( V_{g}=\frac{V_{s g}}{\alpha}=\frac{0.148 m / s }{0.011}=13.5 m / s \)

 

\( V_{l}=\frac{V_{s l}}{1-\alpha}=\frac{9.24 m / s }{1-0.011}=9.34 m / s\)

 

The flow pattern factor \( \left(F_{p}\right) \) can be calculated using

\( F_{p} =(1-\alpha)+\alpha\left[\frac{2}{\pi} \tan ^{-1}\left(\sqrt{\frac{\rho_{g}\left(V_{g}-V_{l}\right)^{2}}{g D\left(\rho_{l}-\rho_{g}\right)}}\right)\right]^{2} \)

 

\( =(1-0.011)+(0.011)\left[\frac{2}{\pi} \tan ^{-1}\left(\sqrt{\frac{\left(1.19 kg / m ^{3}\right)(13.5 m / s -9.34 m / s )^{2}}{\left(9.81 m / s ^{2}\right)(0.0177 m )\left(913 kg / m ^{3}-1.19 kg / m ^{3}\right)}}\right)\right]^{2} \) \( =0.9898\)

The inclination factor \(\left(I^{*}\right)\) for vertical tube \( \left(\theta=90^{\circ}\right) \) is calculated to be

\( I^{*} =1+\frac{\left(\rho_{l}-\rho_{g}\right) g D^{2}}{\sigma}|\sin \theta|\)

 

\( =1+\frac{\left(913 kg / m ^{3}-1.19 kg / m ^{3}\right)\left(9.81 m / s ^{2}\right)(0.0117 m )^{2}}{19.7 \times 10^{-3} N / m }\left|\sin 90^{\circ}\right| \) \( =63.16\)

 

The liquid phase heat transfer coefficient is calculated using:

\( h_{l} =0.027 \operatorname{Re}_{l}^{4 / 5} \operatorname{Pr}_{l}^{1 / 3}\left(\frac{k_{l}}{D}\right)\left(\frac{\mu_{l}}{\mu_{s}}\right)^{0.14} \)

 

\( =0.027(21718)^{4 / 5}(64)^{1 / 3}\left(\frac{0.117 W / m \cdot K }{0.0117 m }\right)\left(\frac{45.7 \times 10^{-4} kg / m \cdot s }{39.8 \times 10^{-4} kg / m \cdot s }\right)^{0.14} \) \( =3246 W / m ^{2} \cdot K\)

where the in situ liquid Reynolds number is

\( \operatorname{Re}_{l} =\frac{4 \dot{m}_{l}}{\pi \sqrt{1-\alpha} \mu_{1} D}=\frac{4(0.907 kg / s )}{\pi \sqrt{1-0.011}\left(45.7 \times 10^{-4} kg / m \cdot s \right)(0.0117 m )} \) \( =21,718\)

Using the general two-phase flow heat transfer correlation, Eq. 10-38,

\( h_{t p}=F_{p} h_{l}\left[1+0.55\left(\frac{x}{1-x}\right)^{0.1}\left(\frac{1 F_{p}}{F_{p}}\right)^{0.4}\left(\frac{\operatorname{Pr}_{g}}{\operatorname{Pr}_{l}}\right)^{0.25}\left(\frac{\mu_{l}}{\mu_{g}}\right)^{0.25}\left(I^{*}\right)^{0.25}\right], W / m ^{2} \cdot K \)

the two-phase heat transfer coefficient is estimated to be

\( \frac{h_{t p}}{h_{l}}= F_{p}\left[1+0.55\left(\frac{x}{1-x}\right)^{0.1}\left(\frac{1-F_{p}}{F_{p}}\right)^{0.4}\left(\frac{\operatorname{Pr}_{g}}{\operatorname{Pr}_{l}}\right)^{0.25}\left(\frac{\mu_{l}}{\mu_{g}}\right)^{0.25}\left(I^{*}\right)^{0.25}\right] \) \( =(0.9898)\left[1+0.55\left(\frac{2.08 \times 10^{-5}}{1-2.08 \times 10^{-5}}\right)^{0.1}\left(\frac{1-0.9898}{0.9898}\right)^{0.4}\left(\frac{0.71}{64}\right)^{0.25}\right.\left.\times\left(\frac{45.7 \times 10^{-4} kg / m \cdot s }{18.4 \times 10^{-6} kg / m \cdot s }\right)^{0.25}(63.16)^{0.25}\right] \) \( = 1.098\)

Or

\(h_{t p}=1.098 h_{l}=1.098\left(3246 W / m ^{2} \cdot K \right)=3560 W / m ^{2} \cdot K\)

Discussion When compared with the measured two-phase heat transfer coefficient of \( 3480 W / m ^{2} \cdot K\) by Rezkallah (1987) in similar flow conditions, the general two-phase flow heat transfer correlation, Eq. 10-38, over-predicted the measured value by \( 2.3 \%\).
