Guest Question.

A beam has the cross section shown in Fig. 8-18a. On a section where the resisting moment is \(M_r =-75 kN · m\), determine
(a) The maximum tensile flexural stress.
(b) The maximum compressive flexural stress.

Admin
Solution

The neutral axis is horizontal and passes through the centroid of the cross section. The centroid is located using the principle of moments as applied to areas.
The total area A of the cross section is

\(A=200(25)+150(25)+50(75)=12,500mm^2\)

The moment of the area \(M_A\) about the bottom edge of the cross section is

\(M_A=12.5[200(25)]+100[150(25)]+200[50(75)]=1,187,500mm^3\)

The distance d from the bottom edge of the cross section to the centroid is

\(d=\frac{M_A}{A}=\frac{1,187,500}{12,500}=95mm\)

Since the centroid of the cross section is not located at the centroid of any of the individual parts (three rectangles), the second moment of the cross section with respect to the horizontal centroidal axis (neutral axis) is found using the parallel-axis theorem. For part A shown in Fig. 8-18b,

\(I_{x'A}=I_{xCA}+A_Ay_{CA}^2=\frac{75(50)^3}{12}+75(50)(105)^2=42.13(10^6)mm^4 \)

Similarly, for parts B and C of Fig. 8-18b,

\(I_{x'B}=I_{xCB}+A_By_{CB}^2=\frac{25(150)^3}{12}+25(150)(5)^2=7.13(10^6)mm^4 \)

 

\(I_{x'C}=I_{xCC}+A_Cy_{CC}^2=\frac{200(25)^3}{12}+200(25)(-82.5)^2=34.29(10^6)mm^4 \)

Adding the second moments of area for the three parts gives the second moment of area for the cross section as

\(I_{x'}=I_{x'A}+I_{x'B}+I_{x'C}=42.13(10^6)+7.13(10^6)+34.29(10^9)\)

 

\(=83.55(10^6)mm^4=83.55(10^{-6})m^4\)

(a) Since the resisting moment \(M_r\) is negative, the top portion of the beam is in tension (σ positive) and the bottom portion of the beam is in compression ( σ negative). The flexural stress varies linearly with distance from the neutral axis. Therefore, the maximum tensile flexural stress occurs at the top surface of the beam

\(\sigma _{maxT}=-\frac{M_ry_t}{I}=-\frac{(-75)(10^3)(130)(10^{-3})}{83.55(10^{-6})}=+116.7(10^6)N/m^2=116.7MPa \quad T \)

(b) The maximum compressive flexural stress occurs at the bottom surface of the beam

\(\sigma _{maxC}=-\frac{M_ry_b}{I}=-\frac{(-75)(10^3)(-95)(10^{-3})}{83.55(10^{-6})}=-85.3(10^6)N/m^2=85.3MPa \quad C \)

The linear variation of flexural stress over the depth of the beam is shown in Fig. 8-18c.
