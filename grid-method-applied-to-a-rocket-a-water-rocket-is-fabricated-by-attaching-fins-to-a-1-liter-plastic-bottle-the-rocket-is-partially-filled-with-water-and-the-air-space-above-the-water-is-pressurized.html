GRID METHOD APPLIED TO A ROCKET

A water rocket is fabricated by attaching fins to a 1-liter plastic bottle. The rocket is partially filled with water and the air space above the water is pressurized, causing water to jet out of the rocket and propel the rocket upward. The thrust force T from the water jet is given by \(T=\dot{m} V\),where \(\dot{m}\) is the rate at which the water flows out of the rocket in units of mass per time and V is the speed of the water jet. (a) Estimate the thrust force in newtons for a jet velocity of V = 30 m/s (98.4 ft/s) where the mass flow rate is \(\dot{m}=9 kg / s\) kg/s (19.8 lbm/s). (b) Estimate the thrust force in units of pounds-force (lbf). Apply the grid method during your calculations.

Problem Definition

Situation:

1. A rocket is propelled by a water jet.

2. The thrust force is given by \(T=\dot{m} V\).

Find: Thrust force supplied by the water jet. Present the answer in N and lbf.

Sketch:

Plan

Find the thrust force by using the process given in Table 1.3. When traditional units are used, apply Eq. (1.9).

\(1.0=\frac{32.2 lbm \cdot ft }{ lbf \cdot s ^{2}}\) (1.9)

Admin
Solution

1. Thrust force (SI units)

 

\(T=\dot{m} V\)

 

• Insert numbers and units:

 

\(T( N )=\dot{m} V=(9 kg / s )(30 m / s )\)

 

Insert conversion ratios and cancel units:

 

\(T( N )=\left[\frac{9 kg }{ s }\right]\left[\frac{30 m }{s}\right]\left[\frac{ N \cdot s ^{2}}{ kg \cdot m }\right]\)

 

\(T=270 N\)

 

2. Thrust force (traditional units)

 

\(T=\dot{m} V\)

 

• Insert numbers and units:

 

\(T( lbf )=\dot{m} V=(19.8 lbm / s )(98.4 ft / s )\)

 

• Insert conversion ratios and cancel units:

 

\(T( lbf )=\left[\frac{19.8 lbm }{ s }\right]\left[\frac{98.4 ft }{ s }\right]\left[\frac{ lbf \cdot s ^{2}}{32.2 lb m \cdot ft }\right]\)

 

\(T=60.5 lbf\)

 

Review

 

1. Validation. Since 1.0 lbf = 4.45 N, answer (a) is the same as answer (b).

 

2. Tip! To validate calculations in traditional units, repeat the calculation in SI units.
