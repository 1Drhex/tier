Guest Question.

Production of Ammonia at 1 Bar

Consider the production of ammonia from the catalytic reaction of a stoichiometric feed of nitrogen and hydrogen. The reaction temperature is 500°C and the reactor pressure is 1 bar.

\(N _{2}+3 H _{2} \rightleftarrows 2 NH _{3}\)

What is the maximum possible conversion?

(a) Take \(\Delta h^{o}=\) constant.

(b) Take \(\Delta h^{o}=\Delta h^{o}(T)\).

You may consider the reaction to occur under ideal gas conditions.

Admin
Solution

From Appendices A.2 and A.3, we can construct the Table E9.7. The bottom row of Table E9.7 provides the sum of the species’ values times the stoichiometric coefficient for each quantity listed. Since the feed is stoichiometric, there are three times the number of moles of \(H _{2} \text { than of } N _{2}\). Thus, the initial mole fraction can be written as follows:

 

\(n_{ H _{2}}^{o}=3, n_{ N _{2}}^{o}=1\)

 

TABLE E9.7 Summary of Thermochemical Data from Appendices A.2 and A.3
Species \(v_{i}\) \(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\) \(\Delta g_{f}^{o}\) \(A_{i}\) \(B_{i}\) \(D_{i}\)
\(NH _{3}\) 2 \(-46.11 kJ / mol\) \(-16.45 kJ / mol\) 3.578 \(3.020 \times 10^{-3}\) \(-0.186 \times 10^{5}\)
\(N _{2}\) -1 0 0 3.28 \(0.593 \times 10^{-3}\) \(0.040 \times 10^{5}\)
\(H _{2}\) -3 0 0 3.249 \(0.422 \times 10^{-3}\) \(0.083 \times 10^{5}\)
\(\sum v_{i}()_{i}\) -2 -92.22 -32.9 -5.871 \(4.180 \times 10^{-3}\) \(-0.661 \times 10^{5}\)

 

We can express the number of moles and mole fractions in terms of the extent of reaction according to Equations (9.8) and (9.10), respectively:

 

\(n_{i}=n_{i}^{o}+v_{i} \xi\) (9.8)

 

\(y_{i}=\frac{n_{i}}{n^{v}}=\frac{n_{i}^{o}+v_{i} \xi}{n^{o}+v \xi}\) (9.10)

 

\(\begin{aligned}n_{ N _{2}} &=1-1 \xi \\n_{ H _{2}} &=3-3 \xi \\n_{ NH _{3}} &=2 \xi \\\hline n &=4-2 \xi\end{aligned}\) \(\begin{aligned}y_{ N _{2}} &=\frac{n_{ N _{2}}}{n}=\frac{1-\xi}{4-2 \xi} \\y_{ H _{2}} &=\frac{n_{ H _{2}}}{n}=\frac{3-3 \xi}{4-2 \xi} \\y_{ NH _{3}} &=\frac{n_{ NH _{3}}}{n}=\frac{2 \xi}{4-2 \xi}\end{aligned}\)

 

Since we are at low pressure, ideal gas behavior is assumed. Thus, the equilibrium constant can be written as:

 

\(K=\Pi\left(y_{i}\right)^{v_{i}} \Pi\left(\varphi_{i}\right)^{v_{i}} P^{\nu}=\frac{y_{ NH _{3}}^{2}}{y_{ N _{2}}y_{ H _{2}}^{3}} P^{-2}\)

 

or, in terms of extent of reaction,

 

\(K=\frac{\left(\frac{2 \xi}{4-2 \xi}\right)^{2}}{\left(\frac{1-\xi}{4-2 \xi}\right)\left(\frac{3-3 \xi}{4-2 \xi}\right)^{3}} P^{-2}=\frac{(2 \xi)^{2}(4-2 \xi)^{2}}{(1-\xi)(3-3 \xi)^{3}} P^{-2}\) (E9.7)

 

The equilibrium constant is calculated in the usual manner. First, we use the Gibbs energy of reaction to calculate K at 25ºC:

 

\(K_{298}=\exp \left(-\frac{\Delta g_{ rxn }^{o}}{R T}\right)=\exp \left(-\frac{-32,900}{[8.314][298.2]}\right)=5.81 \times 10^{5}\)

 

(a) For \(\Delta h_{ rxn }^{o}=\) constant, we again use Equation (9.21):

 

\(\ln \frac{K_{T}}{K_{298}}=-\frac{\Delta h_{ rxn }^{o}}{R}\left(\frac{1}{T}-\frac{1}{298}\right)\) (9.21)

 

\(\ln \frac{K_{T}}{K_{298}}=\frac{\Delta h_{ rxn }^{o}}{R}\left(\frac{1}{T}-\frac{1}{298}\right)=\frac{92,220}{8.314}\left(\frac{1}{773}-\frac{1}{298}\right)=-22.88\)

 

and solving:

 

\(K_{T}=6.754 \times 10^{-5}\)

 

Plugging this into Equation (E9.7) with P = 1 bar and solving for the extent of reaction gives:

 

\(\xi=0.005\)

 

(b) For \(\Delta h_{ rxn }^{o}=\Delta_{ rxn }^{o}(T)\), we can use Equation (9.24):

 

\(\ln \left(\frac{K_{T}}{K_{298}}\right)=\left\{\begin{array}{l}{\begin{array}{l}[-\frac{\Delta h_{ rxn , 298}^{o}}{R}+\Delta A(298)+\frac{\Delta B}{2}\left(298^{2}\right)+\frac{\Delta C}{3}\left(298^{3}\right) \\\left.-\frac{\Delta D}{298}+\frac{\Delta E}{4}\left(298^{4}\right)\right]\left(\frac{1}{T}-\frac{1}{298}\right) \\+\Delta A \ln \left(\frac{T}{298}\right)+\frac{\Delta B}{2}(T-298)+\frac{\Delta C}{6}\left(T^{2}-298^{2}\right) \\+\frac{\Delta D}{2}\left(\frac{1}{T^{2}}-\frac{1}{298^{2}}\right)+\frac{\Delta E}{12}\left(T^{3}-298^{3}\right)\end{array}}\end{array}\right\}\) (9.24)

 

\(\ln \left(\frac{K_{T}}{K_{298}}\right)=\left\{\begin{array}{l}{\begin{array}{l}[-\frac{\Delta h_{ rxn , 298}^{o}}{R}+\Delta A(298)+\frac{\Delta B}{2}\left(298^{2}\right)+\frac{\Delta C}{3}\left(298^{3}\right) \\\left.-\frac{\Delta D}{298}\right]\left(\frac{1}{T}-\frac{1}{298}\right) \\+\Delta A \ln \left(\frac{T}{298}\right)+\frac{\Delta B}{2}(T-298)+\frac{\Delta C}{6}\left(T^{2}-298^{2}\right) \\+\frac{\Delta D}{2}\left(\frac{1}{T^{2}}-\frac{1}{298^{2}}\right)\end{array}}\end{array}\right\}=-24.37\)

 

and solving:

 

\(K_{T}=1.51 \times 10^{-5}\)

 

Plugging this into Equation (E9.7) with P = 1 bar and solving for the extent of reaction gives

 

\(\xi=0.003\)

 

In summary, under these conditions we do not expect to produce an appreciable amount of ammonia.
