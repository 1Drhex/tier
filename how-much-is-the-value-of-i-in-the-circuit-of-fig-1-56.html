Guest Question.

How much is the value of I in the circuit of Fig. 1.56?

Admin
Solution

2.56. Note: In this circuit, it is convenient to consider the units of kΩ, mA, and V for the resistances, the currents, and the voltages, respectively.

First Method (Nodal Analysis): As can be seen in Fig. 2.56.2, there are four nodes in the circuit; however, one of them is dependent (ground zone) and one of them has a known voltage (1 V). The voltages of other nodes are indicated by \(e_{1}\) and\( e_{2}\), as can be seen in the circuit. To solve the problem by using nodal analysis, we need to apply KCL in each independent node.

Note: Since there is a voltage source between two nodes, we must apply KCL for the supernode including these two nodes.

KCL in the supernode of the circuit of Fig. 2.56.2:

\(\frac{1-e_{1}}{1}-3I_{x}+\frac{e_{2}}{0.5}-2=0\Rightarrow \)…..(1)

Also, we need to identify the relation between the voltage of the voltage source and these node voltages\( (e_{1} and e_{2})\).

\(e_{1}-e_{2}=3V_{x}\)…..(2)

As can be seen in the equations, other variables have appeared in them; thus, we need to define them based on the node voltages.

Using Ohm’s laws for the 1kΩ resistor, to define \(I_{x}\) based on the node voltages:

\(I_{x}=\frac{1-e_{1}}{1}.\)…..(3)

Also, it is clear that:

\(V_{x}=e_{2}.\)….(4)

Put (3) in (1) and simplify it:

\(\frac{e_{1}-1}{1}-3\frac{1-e_{1}}{1}+\frac{e_{2}}{0.5}-2=0\Rightarrow e_{1}-1-3+3e_{1}+2e_{2}-2=0\Rightarrow 4e_{1}+2e_{2}=6\)…..(5)

Put (4) in (2) and simplify it:

\(e_{1}-e_{2}=3e_{2}\Rightarrow e_{1}=4e_{2}\)…..(6)

\(4(4e_{2})+2e_{2}=0\Rightarrow e_{2}=\frac{1}{3}V\) \(I=\frac{e_{2}}{0.5}\Rightarrow \frac{\frac{1}{3}V}{0.5kΩ}=\frac{2}{3}mA\)

Second Method (Mesh Analysis): As can be seen in Fig. 2.56.3, although the circuit includes three meshes, the current of the right-side mesh is known (2 mA).

\(i_{2}= 2 mA.\)….(7)

To solve the problem by using mesh analysis, we need to apply KVL in each independent mesh. However, since there is a current source between the two meshes, we must apply KVL for the supermesh including these two meshes.

KVL for the supermesh of the circuit of Fig. 2.56.3:

\(-1+I_{x}×1+3V_{x}+V_{x}=0\Rightarrow -1+I_{x}+4V_{x}=0\)…..(8)

Moreover, we need to determine the relation between the current of the current source and these mesh currents \((i_{1}and i_{2})\).

\(i_{2}-i_{1}=3I_{x}\)…..(9)

As can be seen in equations (8) and (9), they are not purely based on the independent variables \((i_{1} and i_{2})\). Therefore, we need to define \(V_{x}\) and \(I_{x}\) based on them.

Using Ohm’s laws for the 500 Ω resistor to define \(V_{x}\) based on the mesh currents:

\(V_{x}=0.5(i_{2}+i_{3})\)

 

\(Using (7) \Rightarrow V_{x}=0.5(i_{2}+2)\)……(10)

Additionally, it is clear that:

\(I_{x}=i_{1}\)…..(11)

Put (10) and (11) in (8) and simplify it:

\(-1+i_{1}+4(0.5(i_{2}+2))=0\Rightarrow i_{1}+2i_{2}+3=0\)……(12)

Put (11) in (9) and simplify it:

\(i_{2}-i_{1}=3i_{1}\Rightarrow i_{1}=\frac{i_{2}}{4}\)…..(13)

Solving (13) and (12):

\(\frac{i_{2}}{4}+2i_{2}+3=0\Rightarrow \frac{9}{4}i_{2}=-3 \Rightarrow i_{2}=-\frac{4}{3} mA\)

As can be seen in the circuit:

\(I=i_{2}+i_{3}=-\frac{4}{3}+2= \frac{2}{3}mA\)
