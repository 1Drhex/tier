Guest Question.

Consider the nozzle in flowing helium. What should the backpressure be for a normal shock to stand at the exit plane? This is case g in Fig.15.17. What is the exit velocity after the shock?

Admin
Solution

Reversible flow up to the shock with M = 1 at the throat.

\(P _{ x o}= P _{ o }, \quad T _{ x o }= T _{ o }, \quad A _{ E } / A ^{*}=175 / 100=1.75\)

As we do not have a Table 12 made for helium ( k = 5/3 = 1.667) we need to use the formulas. The area to M relation is given in Eq.15.43 so

\(A / A ^{*}=1.75=\left(1 / M _{ x }\right)\left[\frac{2}{ k +1}\left(1+(1 / 3) M _{ x }^{2}\right)\right]^{2}=\left[3+ M _{ x }^{2}\right]^{2} / 16 M _{ x }\)

There are two solutions and we need the one with \(M _{ x }>1\), see fig 15.10 we should expect a solution around \(M _{ x }=2\). With trial and error on

\(3+ M _{ x }^{2}=\sqrt{16 \times 1.75 M _{ x }} \text { which gives } M _{ x }=2.2033\)

Now we can do the normal shock from Eq.15.53

\(M _{ x }=2.2033 \Rightarrow M _{ y }^{2}=\frac{2.2033^{2}+3}{5 \times 2.2033^{2}-1}=0.3375\)

The temperature ratio from Eq. 15.49 and the \(T _{ x }\) from Eq. 15.34:

\(T_{ y }= T _{ x } \frac{1+0.5( k -1) M _{ x }^{2}}{1+0.5( k -1) M _{ y }^{2}}=\frac{ T _{ xo }}{1+0.5( k -1) M _{ y }^{2}}=\frac{375}{1+0.333 \times 0.3375}=337.1 \) \(\begin{aligned}V _{ y } &= M _{ y } c _{ y }= M _{ y } \sqrt{ kRT _{ y }}=\sqrt{0.3375} \times \sqrt{1.667 \times 2.0771 \times 337.1 \times 1000} \\&=0.58095 \times 1080.4= 6 2 7 . 6 m / s\end{aligned}\)

Eq.15.49 : \(\frac{T_{y}}{T_{x}}=\frac{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{x}^{2}}{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{y}^{2}}\)

Eq.15.34 : \(\frac{T_{0}}{T}=1+\frac{(k-1)}{2} M^{2}\)

Eq.15.53 : \(M_{y}^{2}=\frac{M_{x}^{2}+\frac{2}{k-1}}{\frac{2 k}{k-1} M_{x}^{2}-1}\)
