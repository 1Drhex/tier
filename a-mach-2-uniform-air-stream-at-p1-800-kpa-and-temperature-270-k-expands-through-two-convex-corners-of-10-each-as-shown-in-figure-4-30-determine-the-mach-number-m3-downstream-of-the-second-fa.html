Guest Question.

A Mach 2 uniform air stream at \(p_{1}=800\) kPa and temperature 270K expands through two convex corners of \(10^{\circ}\) each, as shown in Figure 4.30. Determine the Mach number \(M_{3}\) downstream of the second fan and \(p_{2}, T_{2}\), and the angle of the second expansion fan.

Admin
Solution

From the isentropic table, for \(M_{1}=2.0,\) we get

\(\nu _{1}=26.38^{\circ}\)

 

Therefore, the Prandtl–Meyer function after the first fan is

\(\nu _{2}=\nu _{1}+10^{\circ}=36.38^{\circ}\)

 

From the isentropic table, for \(\nu _{2}=36.38^{\circ}, M_{2}=2.38.\) The Prandtl–Meyer function after the second fan is

\(\nu _{3}=\nu _{2}+10=46.38^{\circ}\)

 

For \(\nu _{3}=46.38^{\circ},\) from the isentropic table, we get \(M_{3}=2.83\) and

\(M_{3}=2.83\)

 

Also,

\(\frac{p_{1}}{p_{01}}=0.1278, \frac{T_{1}}{T_{01}}=0.5556 \text { at } M_{1}=2.0\)

 

\(\frac{p_{2}}{p_{02}}=0.0706, \frac{T_{2}}{T_{02}}=0.4688 \text { at } M_{2}=2.38\)

 

But for isentropic flow, \(p_{02}=p_{01}, T_{02}=T_{01}.\) Therefore,

\(p_{2}=\frac{0.0706}{0.1278} \times p_{1}\)

 

\(=\frac{0.0706}{0.1278} \times 800\)

 

\(=441.94 kPa\)

 

\(T_{2}=\frac{0.4688}{0.5556} \times 270\)

 

\(= 227.82 K\)

 

After the second fan, following the same procedure as above and using the isentropic table, we get

\(p_{3}=22.03 kPa\)

 

\(T_{3}=186.8 K\)

 

The fan angle for the second fan is

 

\(\mu_{23}=\theta+\mu_{2}-\mu_{3}\)

 

\(= 10 + 24.85 − 20.69\)

 

\(=14.16^{\circ}\)
