Guest Question.

A lubricating oil must be delivered through a horizontal DN 150 Schedule 40 steel pipe with a maximum pressure drop of 60 kPa per 100 m of pipe. The oil has a specific gravity of 0.88 and a dynamic viscosity of \( 9.5 \times 10^{-3}\) Pa·s. Determine the maximum allowable volume flow rate of oil.

Admin
Solution

Figure 11.4 shows the system. This is a Class II series pipeline problem because the volume flow rate is unknown and, therefore, the velocity of flow is unknown. Method II-A is used here because only pipe friction losses exist in the system.

Step 1 Write the energy equation for the system.

Step 2 Solve for the limiting energy loss \( h_L\).

Step 3 Determine the following values for the system:

Pipe flow diameter D

Relative roughness \( D/\epsilon \)

Length of pipe L

Kinematic viscosity of the fluid \( \nu \); may require using \( \nu = \eta /\rho \)

Step 4 Use the following equation to compute the limiting volume flow rate, ensuring that all data are in the coherent units of the given system:

\( Q = -2.22D^2\sqrt{\frac{gDh_L}{L} } \log{\left(\frac{1}{3.7D/\epsilon} +\frac{1.784\nu}{D\sqrt{gDh_L/L} } \right)} \) (11–3)

Results We use points 1 and 2 shown in Fig. 11.3 to write the energy equation:

\( \frac{p_1}{\gamma} + z_1 + \frac{v^2_1}{2g} -h_L = \frac{p_2}{\gamma} + z_2 + \frac{v^2_2}{2g}\)

We can cancel some terms because in this problem, \( z_1 = z_2 \) and \( v_1 = v_2 \). The equation then becomes

\( \frac{p_1}{\gamma} -h_L = \frac{p_2}{\gamma}\)

Then we solve algebraically for \( h_L\) and evaluate the result:

\( h_L = \frac{p_1 – p_2}{\gamma} = \frac{60kN}{m^2} \times \frac{m^3}{\left(0.88\right)\left(9.81kN\right) } = 6.95 m\)

Other data needed are:

Pipe flow diameter, D = 0.1541 m [Appendix F]

Pipe wall roughness, \( \epsilon = 4.6 \times 10^{-5}m\) [Table 8.2]

Relative roughness, \( D/\epsilon = \left(0.1541m\right)/ \left(4.6 \times 10^{-5}m\right) =3350 \)

Length of pipe, L = 100 m

Kinematic viscosity of the fluid; use

\( \rho =\left(0.88\right) \left(1000 kg/m^3\right) = 880 kg/m^3 \)

Then

\( \nu = \eta/\rho = \left(9.5 \times 10^{-3}Pa·s\right) /\left(880kg/m^3\right) =1.08 \times 10^{-5} m^2/s\)

We place these values into Eq. (11–3), ensuring that all data are in coherent SI units for this problem.

\( Q = -2.22\left(0.1541\right)^2 \sqrt{\frac{\left(9.81\right)\left(0.1541\right) \left(6.95\right) }{100} } \times \log {\left[\frac{1}{\left(3.7\right)\left(3350\right) } +\frac{\left(1.784\right) \left(1.08 \times 10^{-5}\right) }{\left(0.1541\right) \sqrt{\left(9.81\right)\left(0.1541\right) \left(6.95\right)/100 } } \right] }\)

\( Q = 0.057 m^3/s\)

Comment Thus, if the volume flow rate of oil through this pipe is no greater than\( 0.057 m^3/s\), the pressure drop over a 100-m length of the pipe will be no greater than 60 kPa.
