Guest Question.

Determine the force in members 1 and 2 of the pin-connected assembly of Fig. 14-15 if member 2 was made \(0.01 \mathrm{~m}\) too short before it was fitted into place. Take \(A E=8\left(10^{3}\right) \mathrm{kN}\).

Admin
Solution

Since the member is short, then \(\Delta L=-0.01 \mathrm{~m}\), and therefore applying Eq. 14-26 to member 2, with \(\lambda_{x}=-0.8, \lambda_{y}=-0.6\), we have

\(\left[\begin{array}{l}\left(Q_{N x}\right)_{0} \\ \left(Q_{N y}\right)_{0} \\ \left(Q_{F x}\right)_{0} \\ \left(Q_{F y}\right)_{0}\end{array}\right]=\frac{A E \Delta L}{L}\left[\begin{array}{r}\lambda_{x} \\ \lambda_{y} \\ -\lambda_{x} \\ -\lambda_{y}\end{array}\right] \quad(14-26)\)

\(\left[\begin{array}{l}\left(Q_{1}\right)_{0} \\ \left(Q_{2}\right)_{0} \\ \left(Q_{5}\right)_{0} \\ \left(Q_{6}\right)_{0}\end{array}\right]=\frac{A E(-0.01)}{5}\left[\begin{array}{r}-0.8 \\ -0.6 \\ 0.8 \\ 0.6\end{array}\right]=A E\left[\begin{array}{r}0.0016 \\ 0.0012 \\ -0.0016 \\ -0.0012\end{array}\right] \begin{array}{l}1 \\ 2 \\ 5 \\ 6\end{array}\)

The structure stiffness matrix for this assembly has been established in . Applying Eq. 14-27, we have

\( \mathbf{Q}=\mathbf{K D}+\mathbf{Q}_{0} \) (14-27)

Partitioning the matrices as shown and carrying out the multiplication to obtain the equations for the unknown displacements yields

\( \left[\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]=A E\left[\begin{array}{ll} 0.378 & 0.096 \\ 0.096 & 0.405 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} D_{1} \\ D_{2} \end{array}\right]+A E\left[\begin{array}{cccccc} 0 & 0 & -0.128 & -0.096 & -0.25 & 0 \\ 0 & -0.333 & -0.096 & -0.072 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} 0 \\0 \\ 0 \\0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]+A E\left[\begin{array}{l} 0.0016 \\ 0.0012 \end{array}\right] \)

which gives

\( \begin{array}{l} 0=A E\left[0.378 D_{1}+0.096 D_{2}\right]+A E[0]+A E[0.0016] \\ 0=A E\left[0.096 D_{1}+0.405 D_{2}\right]+A E[0]+A E[0.0012] \end{array} \)

Solving these equations simultaneously,

\( \begin{array}{l} D_{1}=-0.003704 \mathrm{~m} \\ D_{2}=-0.002084 \mathrm{~m} \end{array} \)

Although not needed, the reactions \(\mathbf{Q}\) can be found from the expansion of Eq. (1) following the format of Eq. 14-29.

\( \mathbf{Q}_{u}=\mathbf{K}_{21} \mathbf{D}_{u}+\mathbf{K}_{22} \mathbf{D}_{k}+\left(\mathbf{Q}_{u}\right)_{0} \) (14-29)

In order to determine the force in members 1 and 2 we must apply Eq. 14-30, in which case we have

\( q_{F}=\frac{A E}{L}\left[\begin{array}{llll} -\lambda_{x}-\lambda_{y} & \lambda_{x} & \lambda_{y} \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c} D_{N x} \\ D_{N y} \\ D_{F x} \\ D_{F y} \end{array}\right]+\left(q_{F}\right)_{0} \) (14-30)

Member 1. \(\lambda_{x}=0, \lambda_{y}=1, L=3 \mathrm{~m}, A E=8\left(10^{3}\right) \mathrm{kN}\), so that

\( \begin{array}{l} q_{1}=\frac{8\left(10^{3}\right)}{3}\left[\begin{array}{llll} 0 & -1 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ -0.003704 \\ -0.002084 \end{array}\right]+[0] \\ q_{1}=-5.56 \mathrm{kN} \end{array} \)

Member 2. \(\quad \lambda_{x}=-0.8, \lambda_{y}=-0.6, L=5 \mathrm{~m}, A E=8\left(10^{3}\right) \mathrm{kN}\), so

\( \begin{array}{l}q_{2}=\frac{8\left(10^{3}\right)}{5}\left[\begin{array}{llll} 0.8 & 0.6 & -0.8 & -0.6 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c} -0.003704 \\ -0.002084 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]-\frac{8\left(10^{3}\right)(-0.01)}{5} \\ q_{2}=9.26 \mathrm{kN} \end{array} \)
