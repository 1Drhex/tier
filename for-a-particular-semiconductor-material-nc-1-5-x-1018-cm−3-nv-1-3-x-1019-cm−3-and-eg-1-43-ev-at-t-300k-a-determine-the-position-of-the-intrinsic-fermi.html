Guest Question.

For a particular semiconductor material, \(N_{C}=1.5 \times 10^{18} cm^{-3} \) , \(N_{V}=1.3 \times 10^{19} cm^{-3} \) and \(E_{G}=1.43 eV \) at T = 300°K.

(a) Determine the position of the intrinsic Fermi level with respect to the center of the What is the position of the Fermi level with respect to the top of the valence band \( E_{V}\)?

(b) Find the intrinsic carrier concentration of the semiconductor at T = 300°K.

(c) Determine effective masses mn and mp of electron and hole

Admin
Solution

(a) The position of the Fermi level with respect to centre of the bandgap \(E_{midgap} \) is obtained from Eq. (4.16),

\(E_{F}=\frac{E_{C}+E_{V}}{2}-\frac{kT}{2}1n\frac{N_{C}}{N_{V}} \) , as

\(E_{F}-E_{midgap}=-\frac{0.0259}{2}1n\left\lgroup\frac{1.5\times 10^{18}}{1.3\times 10^{19}} \right\rgroup \) = 0.028 eV

Thus the Fermi level is located at 0.028 eV above the center of the bandgap. The position of the Fermi level with respect to \(E_{V} \) is determined as follows.

From Eq. (4.16) we can write

\(E_{F}=\frac{E_{C}-E_{V}+2E_{V}}{2}-\frac{kT}{2}1n\left\lgroup\frac{N_{C}}{N_{V}} \right\rgroup \)

\(=\frac{E_{C}-E_{V}}{2}+E_{V}-\frac{kT}{2}1n\left\lgroup\frac{N_{C}}{N_{V}} \right\rgroup \)

Substituting \(E_{G}=E_{C}-E_{V} \) in the above equation, we can get

\(E_{F}-E_{V}=\frac{E_{G}}{2} -\frac{kT}{2}1n\left\lgroup\frac{N_{C}}{N_{V}} \right\rgroup \)

\(=0.715+0.028=0.743\) eV

Hence the Fermi level is located at 0.743 eV above the valence band edge \(E_{V}\). Note that the position of the Fermi level with respect to the conduction band edge \(E_{C}\) can be obtained as

\(E_{F}=\frac{2E_{C}-E_{G}}{2} -\frac{kT}{2}1n\left\lgroup\frac{N_{C}}{N_{V}} \right\rgroup \)

or

\(E_{F}-E_{C}=-\frac{E_{G}}{2} -\frac{kT}{2}1n\left\lgroup\frac{N_{C}}{N_{V}} \right\rgroup =-0.687 eV\)

which implies that the Fermi level is located at 0.687 eV below \(E_{C}\).

(b) Combining Eqs (4.18),

\( np = N_{C}N_{V}exp[-(E_{C}-E_{V})/kT]=N_{C}N_{V}exp(-(E_{G}/kT))\)

and (4.19), \( np=n^2_{i}\) , we can get

\(n^2_{i} =N_{C}N_{V}exp(-E_{G}/KT)\)

\( = (1.5\times 10^{18}cm^{-3})(1.3\times 10^{19}cm^{-3})exp\left\lgroup-\frac{1.43 eV}{0.0259 eV} \right\rgroup \)

\( = 2.05 \times 10^{13} cm^{-6} \)

Thus the intrinsic carrier concentration is given by

\( n_{i}=p_{i}=4.53\times 10^6 cm^{-3} \)

(c) Using \( N_{C}=1.5\times 10^{18}cm^{-3}=1.5\times 10^{24}m^{-3} \) in Eq. (4.20), the effective mass of electron is obtained as

\( m_{n}=\left\{\left\lgroup\frac{N_{C}}{4.82\times 10^{21}} \right\rgroup^{\frac{2}{3} }\frac{1}{T} \right\}m \)

\( =\left\{\left\lgroup\frac{1.5\times 10^{24}}{4.82\times 10^{21}} \right\rgroup^{\frac{2}{3} }\frac{1}{300} \right\}m \)

\( =0.135 m = 0.135\times 9.1\times 10^{-31}=1.23\times 10^{-31}\) kg

Similarly, the effective mass of hole can be given as

\( m_{p}=\left\{\left\lgroup\frac{N_{V}}{4.82\times 10^{21}} \right\rgroup^{\frac{2}{3} }\frac{1}{T} \right\}m \)

\( =\left\{\left\lgroup\frac{1.3\times 10^{25}}{4.82\times 10^{21}} \right\rgroup^{\frac{2}{3} }\frac{1}{300} \right\}m \)

\( =0.646 m = 5.88\times 10^{-31}\) kg
