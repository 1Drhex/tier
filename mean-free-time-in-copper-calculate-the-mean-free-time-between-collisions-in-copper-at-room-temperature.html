MEAN FREE TIME IN COPPER

Calculate the mean free time between collisions in copper at room temperature.

Admin
Solution

IDENTIFY and SET UP:

We can obtain an expression for mean free time \(\tau\) in terms of \(n, \rho, e\), and m by rearranging Eq. (25.24). From and Table 25.1, for copper \(n=8.5 \times 10^{28} \mathrm{~m}^{-3}\) and \(\rho=1.72 \times 10^{-8} \Omega \cdot \mathrm{m}\). In addition, \(e=1.60 \times 10^{-19} \mathrm{C}\) and \(m=9.11 \times 10^{-31} \mathrm{~kg}\) for electrons.

\(\rho=\frac{m}{n e^{2} \tau}\) (25.24)

EXECUTE:

From Eq. (25.24), we get

\(\begin{aligned}\tau &=\frac{m}{n e^{2} \rho} \\&=\frac{9.11 \times 10^{-31} \mathrm{~kg}}{\left(8.5 \times 10^{28} \mathrm{~m}^{-3}\right)\left(1.60 \times 10^{-19} \mathrm{C}\right)^{2}\left(1.72 \times 10^{-8} \Omega \cdot \mathrm{m}\right)} \\&=2.4 \times10^{-14}\mathrm{~s}\end{aligned}\)

 

EVALUATE: The mean free time is the average time between collisions for a given electron. Taking the reciprocal, we find that each electron averages \(1 / \tau=4.2 \times 10^{13}\) collisions per second!
