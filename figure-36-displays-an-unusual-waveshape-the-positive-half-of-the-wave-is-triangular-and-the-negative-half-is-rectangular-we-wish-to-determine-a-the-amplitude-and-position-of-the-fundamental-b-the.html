Guest Question.

Figure 36 displays an unusual waveshape. The positive half of the wave is triangular and the negative half is rectangular. We wish to determine:

 

a. The amplitude and position of the fundamental

b. The amplitude and position of the \(4^{\text {th }}\) harmonic

Admin
Solution

a. We want to analyze the fundamental; consequently, H = 1 and 10 H = 10 × 1 = 10. Thus, at least 10 readings must be taken during one cycle of the distorted wave. The interval D is therefore 360°/10 = 36°. Let us make D = 30°, which will improve the accuracy slightly. We must therefore take 360°/30° = 12 readings during one cycle.

 

Note that the value of the wave changes abruptly at 160°, 240°, and 320°. In such cases, we take the average of the maximum and minimum values. Thus, at 160° the average value is (40 + 0)/2 = +20. The average value at 240° (and at 320°) is –20.

 

Table C is the four-column table mentioned previously. Column [1] shows the angles from 0° to 360° in intervals D of 30°. By tracking the waveshape, the value A corresponding to each angle is determined and listed in column [2]. The necessary calculations are then made to complete columns [3] and [4].

The sum \(S_{ 1 }\) in column [3] gives 175.9. Consequently, we obtain

 

\(X = S _{1} D / 180=175.9 \times 30 / 180=29.3\)

 

Similarly, the sum of the values in column [4] yields \(S_{ 1 }\) = – 43.5 Consequently, we obtain

 

\(Y = S _{2} D / 180=-43.5 \times 30 / 180=-7.3\)

 

Applying Eqs. 16 and 17, we obtain the amplitude \(A_{ 1 }\) and phase angle α of the fundamental

 

\(A _{ H }=\sqrt{ X ^{2}+ Y ^{2}}\) (Equal 16)

 

\(\alpha=\arctan Y / X\) (Equal 17)

 

\(\begin{aligned}A_{1} &=\sqrt{X^{2}+Y^{2}}=\sqrt{29.3^{2}+(-7.3)^{2}}=30.2 \\\alpha &=\arctan \left(\frac{Y}{X}\right)=\arctan \left(\frac{-7.3}{29.3}\right) \\&=\arctan (-0.249)=-14^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

 

The fundamental can therefore be expressed by the equation:

 

\(H _{1}= A _{1} \sin ( H \theta+\alpha)=30.2 \sin \left(\theta-14^{\circ}\right)\)

 

The effective value of the fundamental is

 

\(30.2 / \sqrt{2}=21.3\)

 

Figure 37 shows the fundamental superposed on the original wave.

\(\begin{aligned}& X = S _{1} D / 180=-29.11 \times\left(9^{\circ}\right) / 180=-1.46 \\& Y = S _{2} D / 180=-101.96 \times\left(9^{\circ}\right) / 180=-5.10\end{aligned}\)

 

The amplitude of the \(4^{\text {th }}\) harmonic is therefore

 

\(A _{4}=\sqrt{ X ^{2}+ Y ^{2}}=\sqrt{(-1.46)^{2}+(-5.1)^{2}}=5.30\)

 

and the phase angle is

 

\(\begin{aligned}\alpha &=\arctan \left(\frac{ Y }{ X }\right)=\arctan \left(\frac{-5.10}{-1.46}\right) \\&=\arctan 3.49=74^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

 

However, since X is negative, we must add 180° to the calculated value of 74°. The true angle is therefore \(\alpha=74^{\circ}+180^{\circ}=254^{\circ}\)

 

The \(4^{\text {th }}\) harmonic can be expressed by the equation:

 

\(H _{4}=5.3 \sin \left(4 \theta+254^{\circ}\right)\)

 

Figure 37 shows the \(4^{\text {th }}\) harmonic superposed on both the original waveshape and the fundamental wave.
