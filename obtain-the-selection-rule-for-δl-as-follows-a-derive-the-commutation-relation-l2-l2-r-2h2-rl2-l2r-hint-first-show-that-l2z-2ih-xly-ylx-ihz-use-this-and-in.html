Guest Question.

Obtain the selection rule for \( \Delta \ell \) as follows:

(a) Derive the commutation relation

\( \left[L^{2},\left[L^{2}, r \right]\right]=2 \hbar^{2}\left( r L^{2}+L^{2} r \right) \). (11.78).

Hint: First show that

\( \left[L^{2}, z\right]=2 i \hbar\left(x L_{y}-y L_{x}-i \hbar z\right) \).

Use this, and (in the final step) the fact that r.L = r. (r×p) = 0 , to demonstrate that

\( \left[L^{2},\left[L^{2}, z\right]\right]=2 \hbar^{2}\left(z L^{2}+L^{2} z\right) \).

The generalization from z to r is trivial.

(b) Sandwich this commutator between \( \left\langle n^{\prime} \ell^{\prime} m^{\prime}\right| \text { and }|n \ell m\rangle \), and work out the implications.

Admin
Solution

(a)

\( \left[L^{2}, z\right]=\left[L_{x}^{2}, z\right]+\left[L_{y}^{2}, z\right]+\left[L_{z}^{2}, z\right]=L_{x}\left[L_{x}, z\right]+\left[L_{x}, z\right] L_{x}+L_{y}\left[L_{y}, z\right]+\left[L_{y}, z\right] L_{y}+L_{z}\left[L_{z}, z\right]+\left[L_{z}, z\right] L_{z} \).

\( \text { But }\left\{\begin{array}{l} {\left[L_{x}, z\right]=\left[y p_{z}-z p_{y}, z\right]=\left[y p_{z}, z\right]-\left[z p_{y}, z\right]=y\left[p_{z}, z\right]=-i \hbar y} \\ {\left[L_{y}, z\right]=\left[z p_{x}-x p_{z}, z\right]=\left[z p_{x}, z\right]-\left[x p_{z}, z\right]=-x\left[p_{z}, z\right]=i \hbar x} \\ {\left[L_{z}, z\right]=\left[x p_{y}-y p_{x}, z\right]=\left[x p_{y}, z\right]-\left[y p_{x}, z\right]=0} \end{array}\right. \).

\( \text { So: } \quad\left[L^{2}, z\right]=L_{x}(-i \hbar y)+(-i \hbar y) L_{x}+L_{y}(i \hbar x)+(i \hbar x) L_{y}=i \hbar\left(-L_{x} y-y L_{x}+L_{y} x+x L_{y}\right) \).

\( \text { But }\left\{\begin{array}{l} L_{x} y=L_{x} y-y L_{x}+y L_{x}=\left[L_{x}, y\right]+y L_{x}=i \hbar z+y L_{x} \\ L_{y} x=L_{y} x-x L_{y}+x L_{y}=\left[L_{y}, x\right]+x L_{y}=-i \hbar z+x L_{y} \end{array}\right. \).

\( \text { So: }\left[L^{2}, z\right]=i \hbar\left(2 x L_{y}-i \hbar z-2 y L_{x}-i \hbar z\right) \Longrightarrow\left[L^{2}, z\right]=2 i \hbar\left(x L_{y}-y L_{x}-i \hbar z\right) \text {. } \)

\( \left[L^{2},\left[L^{2}, z\right]\right]=2 i \hbar\left\{\left[L^{2}, x L_{y}\right]-\left[L^{2}, y L_{x}\right]-i \hbar\left[L^{2}, z\right]\right\} \).

\( =2 i \hbar\left\{\left[L^{2}, x\right] L_{y}+x\left[L^{2}, L_{y}\right]-\left[L^{2}, y\right] L_{x}-y\left[L^{2}, L_{x}\right]-i \hbar\left(L^{2} z-z L^{2}\right)\right\} \).

\( \text { But }\left[L^{2}, L_{y}\right]=\left[L^{2}, L_{x}\right]=0 \text { (Eq. 4.102), so } \),

\( \left[L^{2}, L_{x}\right]=0, \quad\left[L^{2}, L_{y}\right]=0, \quad\left[L^{2}, L_{z}\right]=0 \) (4.102).

\( \left[L^{2},\left[L^{2}, z\right]\right]=2 i \hbar\left\{2 i \hbar\left(y L_{z}-z L_{y}-i \hbar x\right) L_{y}-2 i \hbar\left(z L_{x}-x L_{z}-i \hbar y\right) L_{x}-i \hbar\left(L^{2} z-z L^{2}\right)\right\} \), or

\( \left[L^{2},\left[L^{2}, z\right]\right]=-2 \hbar^{2}(2 y L_{z} L_{y} \underbrace{-2 z L_{y}^{2}-2 z L_{x}^{2}}_{-2 z\left(L_{x}^{2}+L_{y}^{2}+L_{z}^{2}\right)+2 z L_{z}^{2}}-2 i \hbar x L_{y}+2 x L_{z} L_{x}+2 i \hbar y L_{x}-L^{2} z+z L^{2}) \).

\( =-2 \hbar^{2}\left(2 y L_{z} L_{y}-2 i \hbar x L_{y}+2 x L_{z} L_{x}+2 i \hbar y L_{x}+2 z L_{z}^{2}-2 z L^{2}-L^{2} z+z L^{2}\right) \).

\( =2 \hbar^{2}\left(z L^{2}+L^{2} z\right)-4 \hbar^{2}[\underbrace{\left(y L_{z}-i \hbar x\right)}_{L_{z} y} L_{y}+\underbrace{\left(x L_{z}+i \hbar y\right)}_{L_{z} x} L_{x}+z L_{z} L_{z}] \).

\( =2 \hbar^{2}\left(z L^{2}+L^{2} z\right)-4 \hbar^{2} \underbrace{\left(L_{z} y L_{y}+L_{z} x L_{x}+L_{z} z L_{z}\right)}_{L_{z}( r \cdot L )=0}=2 \hbar^{2}\left(z L^{2}+L^{2} z\right) \). QED

(b) Making the obvious generalization of part (a):

\( \left[L^{2},\left[L^{2}, r \right]\right]=2 \hbar^{2}\left( r L^{2}+L^{2} r \right) \).

As before, we sandwich this commutator between \( \left\langle n^{\prime} \ell^{\prime} m^{\prime}\right| \text { and }|n \ell m\rangle \) to derive the selection rule:

\( \left\langle n^{\prime} \ell^{\prime} m^{\prime}\left|\left[L^{2},\left[L^{2}, r \right]\right]\right| n \ell m\right\rangle=2 \hbar^{2}\left\langle n^{\prime} \ell^{\prime} m^{\prime}\left|\left( r L^{2}+L^{2} r \right)\right| n \ell m\right\rangle \).

\( =2 \hbar^{4}\left[\ell(\ell+1)+\ell^{\prime}\left(\ell^{\prime}+1\right)\right]\left\langle n^{\prime} \ell^{\prime} m^{\prime}| r | n \ell m\right\rangle=\left\langle n^{\prime} \ell^{\prime} m^{\prime}\left|\left(L^{2}\left[L^{2}, r \right]-\left[L^{2}, r \right] L^{2}\right)\right| n \ell m\right\rangle \).

\( =\hbar^{2}\left[\ell^{\prime}\left(\ell^{\prime}+1\right)-\ell(\ell+1)\right]\left\langle n^{\prime} \ell^{\prime} m^{\prime}\left|\left[L^{2}, r \right]\right| n \ell m\right\rangle=\hbar^{2}\left[\ell^{\prime}\left(\ell^{\prime}+1\right)-\ell(\ell+1)\right]\left\langle n^{\prime} \ell^{\prime} m^{\prime}\left|\left(L^{2} r – r L^{2}\right)\right| n \ell m\right\rangle \).

\( =\hbar^{4}\left[\ell^{\prime}\left(\ell^{\prime}+1\right)-\ell(\ell+1)\right]^{2}\left\langle n^{\prime} \ell^{\prime} m^{\prime}| r | n \ell m\right\rangle \).

Conclusion: Either

\( 2\left[\ell(\ell+1)+\ell^{\prime}\left(\ell^{\prime}+1\right)\right]=\left[\ell^{\prime}\left(\ell^{\prime}+1\right)-\ell(\ell+1)\right]^{2}, \quad \text { or else } \quad\left\langle n^{\prime} \ell^{\prime} m^{\prime}| r | n \ell m\right\rangle=0 \).

But

\( \left[\ell^{\prime}\left(\ell^{\prime}+1\right)-\ell(\ell+1)\right]=\left(\ell^{\prime}+\ell+1\right)\left(\ell^{\prime}-\ell\right) \).

and

\( 2\left[\ell(\ell+1)+\ell^{\prime}\left(\ell^{\prime}+1\right)\right]=\left(\ell^{\prime}+\ell+1\right)^{2}+\left(\ell^{\prime}-\ell\right)^{2}-1 \).

so the first possibility can be written in the form

\( \left[\left(\ell^{\prime}+\ell+1\right)^{2}-1\right]\left[\left(\ell^{\prime}-\ell\right)^{2}-1\right]=0 \).

The first factor cannot be zero (unless \( \ell^{\prime}=\ell=0, \text { but }\left\langle n^{\prime} 00| r | n 00\right\rangle \) is trivially zero, since the integrand is odd), so the condition simplifies to \( \ell^{\prime}=\ell \pm 1 \) . Thus we obtain the selection rule for \( \ell: \)

\( \text { No transitions occur unless } \Delta \ell=\pm 1 \text {. } \)
