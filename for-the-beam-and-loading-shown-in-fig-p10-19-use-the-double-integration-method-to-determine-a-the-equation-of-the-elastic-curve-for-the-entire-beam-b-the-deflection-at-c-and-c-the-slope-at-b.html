Guest Question.

For the beam and loading shown in Fig.P10.19, use the double-integration method to determine (a) the equation of the elastic curve for the entire beam, (b) the deflection at C, and (c) the slope at B. Assume that EI is constant for the beam.

Admin
Solution

Beam FBD:

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{A} &=B_{y}(3 L)-w L\left(3 L+\frac{L}{2}\right)=0 \\& \therefore B_{y}=\frac{7 w L}{6} \\\Sigma F_{y} &=A_{y}+B_{y}-w L=0 \\& \therefore A_{y}=-\frac{w L}{6}\end{aligned}\)

Consider beam segment AB (0 ≤ x ≤ 3L)
Moment equation:

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{a-a} &=M(x)-A_{y} x=M(x)+\left(\frac{w L}{6}\right) x=0 \\& \therefore M(x)=-\frac{w L x}{6}\end{aligned}\)

Integration of moment equation:

\(\begin{aligned}&E I \frac{d^{2} v}{d x^{2}}=M(x)=-\frac{w L x}{6} \\&E I \frac{d v}{d x}=-\frac{w L x^{2}}{12}+C_{1} (a)\\&E I v=-\frac{w L x^{3}}{36}+C_{1} x+C_{2} (b)\end{aligned}\)

Boundary conditions:

\(\begin{array}{lllll}v=0 & \text { at } & x=0 & \text { and } & v=0 \quad \text { at } & x=3 L\end{array}\)

Evaluate constants:
Substitute x = 0 and v = 0 into Eq. (b) to determine \(C_{2}=0 \text {. }\) Next, substitute x = 3L and v = 0 into Eq. (b) and solve for \(C_{1}:\)

\(E I(0)=-\frac{w L(3 L)^{3}}{36}+C_{1}(3 L) \quad \therefore C_{1}=\frac{9 w L^{3}}{36}=\frac{w L^{3}}{4}\)

Slope at B: Let x = 3L in Eq. (a).

\(\left.E I \frac{d v}{d x}\right|_{B}=E I \theta_{B}=-\frac{w L(3 L)^{2}}{12}+\frac{w L^{3}}{4}=-\frac{w L^{3}}{2}\) (c)

Consider beam segment BC (3L ≤ x ≤ 4L)
Moment equation:

\(\begin{gathered}\Sigma M_{b-b}=-M(x)-\frac{w}{2}(4 L-x)^{2}=0 \\\therefore M(x)=-\frac{w(4 L-x)^{2}}{2}\end{gathered}\)

Integration of moment equation:

\(\begin{aligned}&E I \frac{d^{2} v}{d x^{2}}=M(x)=-\frac{w(4 L-x)^{2}}{2} \\&E I \frac{d v}{d x}=\frac{w(4 L-x)^{3}}{6}+C_{3} (e) \\&E I v=-\frac{w(4 L-x)^{4}}{24}+C_{3} x+C_{4} (f)\end{aligned}\)

Boundary conditions:

\(v=0 \quad \text { at } \quad x=3 L\)

Substitute x = 3L and v = 0 into Eq. (f) to find

\(\begin{aligned}E I(0) &=-\frac{w(4 L-3 L)^{4}}{24}+C_{3}(3 L)+C_{4}=-\frac{w L^{4}}{24}+C_{3}(3 L)+C_{4} \\& \therefore C_{4}=\frac{w L^{4}}{24}-(3 L) C_{3} (g)\end{aligned}\)

Slope at B: Let x = 3L in Eq. (e).

\(\left.E I \frac{d v}{d x}\right|_{B}=E I \theta_{B}=\frac{w(4 L-3 L)^{3}}{6}+C_{3}=\frac{w L^{3}}{6}+C_{3}\) (h)

Continuity conditions:
Since the slope at B must be the same for both beam segments, equate Eqs. (c) and (h):

\(-\frac{w L^{3}}{2}=\frac{w L^{3}}{6}+C_{3} \quad C_{3}=-\frac{2 w L^{3}}{3}\) (i)

Backsubstitute this result into Eq. (g) to determine \(C_{4}:\)

\(C_{4}=\frac{w L^{4}}{24}-(3 L) C_{3}=\frac{w L^{4}}{24}-(3 L)\left(\frac{-2 w L^{3}}{3}\right)=\frac{49 w L^{4}}{24}\)

(a) Elastic curve equation for beam segment AB:

\(\begin{aligned}E I v &=-\frac{w L x^{3}}{36}+\frac{9 w L^{3} x}{36} \\& \therefore v=-\frac{w L x}{36 E I}\left[x^{2}-9 L^{2}\right] \quad(0 \leq x \leq 3 L)\end{aligned}\)

Elastic curve equation for beam segment BC:

\(\begin{aligned}E I v &=-\frac{w(4 L-x)^{4}}{24}-\frac{2 w L^{3} x}{3}+\frac{49 w L^{4}}{24} \\& \therefore v=-\frac{w}{24 E I}\left[(4 L-x)^{4}+16 L^{3} x-49 L^{4}\right] \quad(3 L \leq x \leq 4 L)\end{aligned}\)

(b) Deflection at C:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{C} &=-\frac{w}{24 E I}\left[(4 L-4 L)^{4}+16 L^{3}(4 L)-49 L^{4}\right]=-\frac{w}{24 E I}\left[64 L^{4}-49 L^{4}\right]=-\frac{15 w L^{4}}{24 E I} \\& \therefore v_{C}=-\frac{5 w L^{4}}{8 E I}\end{aligned}\)

(c) Slope at B: Let x = 3L in Eq. (a).

\(\left.E I \frac{d v}{d x}\right|_{B}=E I \theta_{B}=-\left.\frac{w L^{3}}{2} \quad \therefore \frac{d v}{d x}\right|_{B}=\theta_{B}=-\frac{w L^{3}}{2 E I}\)
