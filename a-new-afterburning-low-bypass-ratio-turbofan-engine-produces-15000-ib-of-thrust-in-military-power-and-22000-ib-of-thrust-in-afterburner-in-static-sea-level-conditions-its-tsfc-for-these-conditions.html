Guest Question.

A new afterburning low-bypass-ratio turbofan engine produces 15,000 Ib of thrust in military power and 22,000 Ib of thrust in afterburner in static, sea-level conditions. Its TSFC for these conditions is 0.8/hr in military power and 2.2/h in afterburner. What are its military and afterburner thrust and TSFC at h = 20,000 ft and M = 0.8?

Admin
Solution

Because only an altitude is specified, standard atmosphere conditions will be assumed. From the standard atmosphere table, for an altitude of 20,000 ft, \(\rho=0.001267 slug / ft ^{3}, \text { and } a=1036.9 ft / s\) . For military power, the thrust at 20,000 ft is given by Eq. (5.11):

\(\begin{aligned}T_{A} &=T_{ SL }\left(\frac{\rho}{\rho_{ SL }}\right)=15,000 lb \left(\frac{0.001267 slug / ft ^{3}}{0.002377 slug / ft ^{3}}\right) \\&=7995 lb \text { in military power }\end{aligned}\)

The military power TSFC is given by Eq. (5.16):

\(c_{t}=c_{t_{ SL }}\left(\frac{a}{a_{ SL }}\right)=(0.8 / h )\left(\frac{1036.9 ft / s }{1116.2 ft / s }\right)=0.74 / h \text { in military power }\)

Similarly, the thrust in afterburner is predicted by Eq. (5.12):

\(\begin{aligned}T_{A} &=T_{ SL }\left(\frac{\rho}{\rho_{ SL }}\right)\left(1+0.7 M_{\infty}\right)=22,000 lb \left(\frac{0.001267 slug / ft ^{3}}{0.002377 slug / ft ^{3}}\right)[1+0.7(0.8)] \\&=18,293 lb\end{aligned}\)

and the afterburning TSFC is

\(c_{t}=c_{t_{ SL }}\left(\frac{a}{a_{ SL }}\right)=(2.2 / h )\left(\frac{1036.9 ft / s }{1116.2 ft / s }\right)=2.04 / h \text { in afterburner }\)
