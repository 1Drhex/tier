Guest Question.

A point \(P\) moves on a disk with constant relative velocity \(v_{r}\) along a circular path. The disk rotates with constant angular velocity \(\omega \)about A.

Determine the absolute velocity and the absolute acceleration of \(P\) .

Admin
Solution

We introduce the moving coordinate system \(\xi , \eta , \zeta \) with its origin in the center \(0\) of the disk. Relative to this system, point \(P\) undergoes a circular motion. With the relative velocity \(v_{r}\) and the magnitude \( a_{r}=v^{2}_{r}/ r \) of the relative acceleration and its direction (from \(P\) to \(0\) ) we can write

\(v_{r}=v_{r}\left(-e_{\xi }\sin \varphi +e_{\eta }\cos \varphi \right) \) ,

\(a_{r}=-\frac{v^{2}_{r} }{r}\left(e_{\xi }\cos \varphi +e_{\eta }\sin \varphi \right) \) .

With

\(\omega = \omega e_{\zeta }\) , \(\dot{\omega }=0\) , \(r_{0P} =e_{\xi }r\cos \varphi +e_{\eta }r\sin \varphi\) ,

\(r_{0}=e e_{\eta }\) , \(\dot{r}_{0}=-e\omega e_{\xi } \) , \(\ddot{r}_{0}=a_{0}=-e\omega ^{2}e_{\eta } \)

we obtain

\(v_{f}=\dot{r}_{0} +\omega \times r_{0P}=-\left(e+r\omega \sin \varphi \right)e_{\xi }+r\omega \cos \varphi e_{\eta } \) ,

\(a_{f}=a_{0}+\omega \times \left(\omega \times r_{0P} \right) \)

 

\(=-e\omega ^{2}e_{\xi }+r\omega ^{2} \left[e_{\zeta }\times \left(e_{\zeta }\times e_{\zeta } \cos \varphi \right) +e_{\zeta }\times \left(e_{\zeta }\times e_{\eta }\sin \varphi \right) \right]\)

 

\(=-\left(e+r\cos \varphi \right)\omega ^{2}e_{\xi }-r\omega ^{2} \sin \varphi e_{\eta } \) ,

\(a_{c}=2\omega \times v_{r}=2\omega v_{r}\left[e_{\zeta }\times \left(-e_{\xi }\sin \varphi \right)+e_{\zeta }\times e_{\eta }\varphi \right] \)

 

\(=-2\omega v_{r}\left(e_{\xi }\cos \varphi +e_{\eta }\sin \varphi \right) \) .

Thus, the absolute velocity and acceleration are found as

\(\underline{\underline{v} }=v_{f}+v_{r}=\underline{\underline{-\left[e+\left(v_{r}+r\omega \right)\sin \varphi \right]e_{\xi }+\left(v_{r}+r\omega \right)\cos \varphi e_{\eta } } } \) ,

\(\underline{\underline{a} }=a_{f}+a_{r}+a_{c} \)

 

\(=-\left[e\omega ^{2}+\left(r\omega ^{2}+\frac{v^{2}_{r} }{r}+2\omega v_{r} \right)\cos \varphi \right]e_{\xi } -\left[r\omega ^{2}+\frac{v^{2}_{r} }{r}+2\omega v_{r} \right] \sin \varphi e_{\eta } \)

 

\(=\underline{\underline{-\left[e\omega ^{2}+r\left(\omega +\frac{v_{r} }{r} \right)^{2}\cos \varphi \right] e_{\xi } -r\left(\omega +\frac{v_{r} }{r} \right)^{2}\sin \varphi e_{\eta } } } \) .
