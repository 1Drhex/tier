Guest Question.

The floor system of a building consists of a 5 in. thick reinforced concrete slab resting on four steel floor beams, which in turn are supported by two steel girders, as shown in Fig. 2.7(a). The cross-sectional areas of the floor beams and the girders are 14.7 in. \({ }^{2}\) and \(52.3 \mathrm{in} .^{2}\), respectively. Determine the dead loads acting on the beams \(C G\) and \(D H\) and the girder \(A D .\)

Admin
Solution

Beam CG. As shown in Fig. \(2.7\) (a), the tributary area for beam \(C G\) has a width of \(10 \mathrm{ft}\) (i.e., half the distance between beams \(C G\) and \(B F\) plus half the distance between beams \(C G\) and \(D H\) ) and a length of \(24 \mathrm{ft}\). We use the unit weights of reinforced concrete and structural steel from Table \(2.1\) to compute the dead load per foot of length of beam \(C G\) as follows:

Concrete slab: \(\left(150 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{ft}^{3}\right)(10 \mathrm{ft})(1 \mathrm{ft})\left(\frac{5}{12}\right) \mathrm{ft}=625 \mathrm{lb}\)

Steel beam: \(\quad\left(490 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{ft}^{3}\right)\left(\frac{14.7}{144} \mathrm{ft}^{2}\right)(1 \mathrm{ft})=50 \mathrm{lb}\)

Total load \(=\overline{675 \mathrm{lb}}\)

This \(675 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{ft}\) load is uniformly distributed on the beam, as shown in Fig. \(2.7\) (b). This figure also shows the reactions exerted by the supporting girders at the ends of the beam. As the beam is symmetrically loaded, the magnitudes of the reactions are:

\( R_{C}=R_{G}=\frac{1}{2}(675 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{ft})(24 \mathrm{ft})=8100 \mathrm{lb} \)

Note that the magnitudes of these end reactions represent the downward loads being transmitted to the supporting girders \(A D\) and \(E H\) at points \(C\) and \(G\), respectively.

Beam DH. The tributary area for beam \(D H\) is \(5 \mathrm{ft}\) wide and \(24 \mathrm{ft}\) long. The dead load per foot of length of this beam is computed as follows:

Concrete slab: \(\left(150 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{ft}^{3}\right)(5 \mathrm{ft})(1 \mathrm{ft})\left(\frac{5}{12} \mathrm{ft}\right)=312.5 \mathrm{lb}\)

Steel beam: (same as for beam \(C G\) ) \(=50.0 \mathrm{lb}\)

Total load \(=\overline{362.5 \mathrm{lb}}\)

As shown in Fig. 2.7(c), the end reactions are

\( R_{D}=R_{H}=\frac{1}{2}(362.5 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{ft})(24 \mathrm{ft})=4350 \mathrm{lb} \)

Girder AD. Because of the symmetry of the framing system and loading, the loads acting on beams \(B F\) and \(A E\) are the same as those on beams \(C G\) and \(D H\), respectively. The load on girder \(A D\) consists of the uniformly distributed load due to its own weight, which has a magnitude of

\( \left(490 \mathrm{lb} /\mathrm{ft}^{3}\right)\left(\frac{52.3}{144} \mathrm{ft}^{2}\right)(1 \mathrm{ft})=178 \mathrm{lb} \)

and the concentrated loads transmitted to it by the beams at points \(A, B, C\), and \(D\), as shown in Fig. \(2.7(\mathrm{~d})\).
