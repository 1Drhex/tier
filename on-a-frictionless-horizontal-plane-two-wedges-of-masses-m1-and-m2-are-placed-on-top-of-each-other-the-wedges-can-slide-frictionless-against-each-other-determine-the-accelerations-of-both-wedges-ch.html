Guest Question.

On a frictionless horizontal plane, two wedges of masses \(m_{1} \) and \(m_{2}\) are placed on top of each other. The wedges can slide frictionless against each other.

Determine the accelerations of both wedges. Check the result by considering the limit cases \(m_{1}\rightarrow \infty \) and \(\alpha \rightarrow \pi / 2 \) .

Admin
Solution

We separate the two bodies and formulate the equations of motion in \(x-\) and in \(y-\)direction:

①\( \leftarrow :m_{1}\ddot{x}_{1}=N_{1}\sin \alpha \) ,

\(\downarrow :m_{1}\ddot{y}_{1}=m_{1}g-N_{2}+N_{1}\cos \alpha \) ,

②\(\leftarrow :m_{2}\ddot{x}_{2}=-N_{1} \sin \alpha \) ,

\(\downarrow :m_{2}\ddot{y}_{2}=m_{2}g-N_{1}\cos \alpha \) .

Since wedge ① moves horizontally and at its top side wedge ② slides downwards, the kinematic relations read

\(\ddot{y}_{1}=0\) ,

\(y_{2}=\left(x_{1}-x_{2} \right)\tan \alpha \rightsquigarrow \ddot{y}_{2}=\left(\ddot{x}_{1}-\ddot{x}_{2} \right)\tan \alpha \) .

Thus, we have six equations for the six unknowns (\(\ddot{x}_{1} , \ddot{y}_{1} , \ddot{x}_{2} , \ddot{y}_{2} , N_{1} , N_{2} \)) . By eliminating \( N_{1}\) and \( N_{2}\) it follows

\(\underline{\underline{\ddot{x}_{1}=\frac{\frac{m_{2} }{m_{1} }g\tan \alpha }{1+\left(1+\frac{m_{2} }{m_{1} } \right)\tan ^{2} \alpha } } } \) , \(\underline{\underline{ \ddot{y}_{1}=0 } }\) ,

\(\underline{\underline{\ddot{x}_{2}=-\frac{g\tan \alpha }{1+\left(1+\frac{m_{2} }{m_{1} } \right)\tan ^{2}\alpha } } } \) , \(\underline{\underline{\ddot{y}_{2}=\frac{\left(1+\frac{m_{2} }{m_{1} } \right)g\tan ^{2}\alpha }{1+\left(1+\frac{m_{2} }{m_{1} } \right)\tan ^{2}\alpha } } }\) .

For the two limit cases we obtain:

a) \(m_{1}\rightarrow \infty :\ddot{x}_{1}\rightarrow 0 \) and \(\left|\ddot{y}_{2}/ \ddot{x}_{2} \right|\rightarrow \tan \alpha \) (inclined plane),

b) \(\alpha \rightarrow \frac{\pi }{2} : \ddot{x}_{1}=\ddot{x}_{2}\rightarrow 0 \) and \(\ddot{y}_{2} \rightarrow g \) (free fall).

Remark: Adding the equations of motion in x-direction and time-integration confirms conservation of linear momentum: \(m_{1}\ddot{x}_{1}+m_{2}\ddot{x}_{2}=0 \rightsquigarrow m_{1}\dot{x}_{1}+m_{2}\dot{x}_{2}=C \) , i.e. the total linear momentum is constant!
