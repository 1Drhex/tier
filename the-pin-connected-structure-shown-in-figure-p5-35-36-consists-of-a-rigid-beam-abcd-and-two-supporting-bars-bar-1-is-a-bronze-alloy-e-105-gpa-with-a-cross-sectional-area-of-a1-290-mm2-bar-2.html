Guest Question.

The pin-connected structure shown in Figure P5.35/36 consists of a rigid beam ABCD and two supporting bars. Bar (1) is a bronze alloy [E = 105 GPa] with a cross-sectional area of \(A_{1}=290 mm ^{2}\). Bar (2) is an aluminum alloy [E = 70 GPa] with a cross-sectional area of \(A_{2}=650 mm ^{2}\). All bars are unstressed before the load P is applied; however, there is a 3-mm clearance in the pin connection at A. If a load of P = 85 kN is applied at B, determine:
(a) the normal stresses in both bars (1) and (2).
(b) the downward deflection of point A on the rigid bar

Admin
Solution

Equilibrium
Consider a FBD of the rigid bar. Assume tension forces in members (1) and (2). A moment equation about pin C gives the best information for this situation:

\(\Sigma M_{C}=-(1,150 mm ) F_{1}-(650 mm ) F_{2}+(480 mm ) P=0\) (a)

Geometry-of-deformation relationship
Draw a deformation diagram of the rigid bar. The deflections of the rigid bar are related by similar triangles:

\(\frac{v_{A}}{1,150 mm }=\frac{v_{D}}{650 mm }\) (b)

Since there is no clearance at pin D, the deformation of member (2) will equal the deflection of the rigid bar at D. However, there is a 3-mm clearance at A.
Consequently, not all of the rigid beam deflection at A will go toward elongating bar (1). The relationship between rigid beam deflection and axial member deformation can be expressed:

\(v_{A}=\delta_{1}+3 mm\) (c)

Equation (b) can be rewritten in terms of the member deformations as:

\(\frac{\delta_{1}+3 mm }{1,150 mm }=\frac{\delta_{2}}{650 mm }\) (d)

Force-Deformation Relationships
The relationship between the internal force and the deformation of an axial member can be stated for members (1) and (2):

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}} \quad \delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}\) (e)

Compatibility Equation
Substitute the force-deformation relationships (e) into the geometry-of-deformation relationship (d) to derive the compatibility equation:

\(\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+3 mm =\frac{1,150 mm }{650 mm } \frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}=1.769231 \frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}\) (f)

Solve the Equations
Solve Eq. (f) for \(F_{2}\):

\(F_{2}=\frac{A_{2} E_{2}}{1.769231 L_{2}}\left[\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+3 mm \right]=F_{1} \frac{L_{1}}{1.769231 L_{2}} \frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}} \frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}+\frac{(3 mm ) A_{2} E_{2}}{1.769231 L_{2}}\)

and substitute into Eq. (a) to solve for \(F_{1}\):

\((1,150 mm ) F_{1}+(650 mm ) F_{2}=(480 mm )(85 kN )\)

 

\((1,150 mm ) F_{1}+(650 mm )\left[F_{1} \frac{L_{1}}{1.769231 L_{2}} \frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}} \frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}+\frac{(3 mm ) A_{2} E_{2}}{1.769231 L_{2}}\right]=(480 mm )(85 kN )\)

 

\((1,150 mm ) F_{1}+(650 mm ) F_{1} \frac{L_{1}}{1.769231 L_{2}} \frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}} \frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}=(480 mm )(85 kN )-(650 mm ) \frac{(3 mm ) A_{2} E_{2}}{1.769231 L_{2}}\)

 

\(F_{1}=\frac{(480 mm )(85 kN )-(650 mm ) \frac{(3 mm ) A_{2} E_{2}}{1.769231 L_{2}}}{(1,150 mm )+(650 mm ) \frac{L_{1}}{1.769231 L_{2}} \frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}} \frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}}\)

 

The value of \(F_{1}\) is thus calculated as:

\(F_{1}=\frac{(480 mm )(85,000 N )-(650 mm ) \frac{(3 mm )\left(650 mm ^{2}\right)\left(70,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}{(1.769231)(1,600 mm )}}{(1,150 mm )+(650 mm ) \frac{2,250 mm }{(1.769231)(1,600 mm )}\left(\frac{650 mm ^{2}}{290 mm ^{2}}\right)\left(\frac{70 GPa }{105 GPa }\right)}\)

 

\(=4,920.4 N =4.9204 kN\)

and backsubstituting into Eq. (a) gives \(F_{2}\):

\(\begin{aligned}F_{2} &=\frac{(480 mm )(85 kN )-(1,150 mm ) F_{1}}{650 mm } \\&=\frac{(480 mm )(85 kN )-(1,150 mm )(4.9204 kN )}{650 mm } \\&=54.0640 kN\end{aligned}\)

 

(a) Normal stresses in bars (1) and (2):

\(\sigma_{1}=\frac{F_{1}}{A_{1}}=\frac{4,920.4 N }{290 mm ^{2}}=16.97 MPa ( T )\)

 

\(\sigma_{2}=\frac{F_{2}}{A_{2}}=\frac{54,064.0 N }{650 mm ^{2}}=83.2 MPa ( T )\)

 

(b) Deflections of the rigid bar at point A
Calculate the deformation of member (1):

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}=\frac{(4,920.4 N )(2,250 mm )}{\left(290 mm ^{2}\right)\left(105,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=0.3636 mm\)

The relationship between the rigid bar deflection at A and the deformation of member (1) was expressed in Eq. (c). Therefore, the rigid bar deflection at A is:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{A} &=\delta_{1}+3 mm \\&=0.3636 mm +3 mm \\&=3.36 mm \downarrow\end{aligned}\)
