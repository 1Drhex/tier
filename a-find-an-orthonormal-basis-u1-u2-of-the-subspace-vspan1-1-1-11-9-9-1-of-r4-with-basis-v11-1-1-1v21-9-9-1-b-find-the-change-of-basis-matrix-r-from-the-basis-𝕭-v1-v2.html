Guest Question.

a. Find an orthonormal basis \(\vec{u}_{1}, \vec{u}_{2}\) of the subspace \(V=\operatorname{span}\left(\left[\begin{array}{l}1 \\1 \\1 \\1\end{array}\right],\left[\begin{array}{l}1 \\9 \\9 \\1\end{array}\right]\right) \text { of } \mathbb{R}^{4} \text {, with basis } \vec{v}_{1}=\left[\begin{array}{l}1 \\1 \\1 \\1\end{array}\right], \vec{v}_{2}=\left[\begin{array}{l}1 \\9 \\9 \\1\end{array}\right]\).

b. Find the change of basis matrix R from the basis \(\mathfrak{B}=\left(\vec{v}_{1}, \vec{v}_{2}\right)\) to the basis \(\mathfrak{A} =\left(\vec{u}_{1}, \vec{u}_{2}\right)\) you constructed in part a.

Solution

a. Following the three steps illustrated in Figures 1, 2, and 3, we will compute Solution first \(\vec{u}_{1} \text {, then } \vec{v}_{2}^{\perp} \text {, and finally } \vec{u}_{2} \text {. }\)

\(\vec{u}_{1}=\frac{1}{\left\|\vec{v}_{1}\right\|}\vec{v}_{1}=\frac{1}{2}\left[\begin{array}{l}1 \\1 \\1 \\1\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}1 / 2 \\1 / 2 \\1 / 2 \\1 / 2\end{array}\right]\).

 

\(\vec{v}_{2}{ }^{\perp}=\bar{v}_{2}-\vec{v}_{2}^{||}=\vec{v}_{2}-\left(\vec{u}_{1} \cdot \vec{v}_{2}\right) \vec{u}_{1}=\left[\begin{array}{l}1 \\9 \\9 \\1\end{array}\right]-10\left[\begin{array}{l}1 / 2 \\1 / 2 \\1 / 2 \\1 / 2\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{r}-4 \\4 \\4 \\-4\end{array}\right]\),

\(\vec{u}_{2}^{\perp}=\frac{1}{\left\|\vec{v}_{2}{ }^{\perp}\right\|} \vec{v}_{2}{ }^{\perp}=\frac{1}{8}\left[\begin{array}{r}-4 \\4 \\4 \\-4\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{r}-1 / 2 \\1 / 2 \\1 / 2 \\-1 / 2\end{array}\right]\)

We have found an orthonormal basis \(\mathfrak{A}\) of V :

\(\vec{u}_{1}=\left[\begin{array}{l}1 / 2 \\1 / 2 \\1 / 2 \\1 / 2\end{array}\right], \quad \vec{u}_{2}=\left[\begin{array}{r}-1 / 2 \\1 / 2 \\1 / 2 \\-1 / 2\end{array}\right]\)

b. Recall that the columns of the change of basis matrix R from ùï≠ to ùï¨ are the coordinate vectors of \(\vec{v}_{1} \text { and } \vec{v}_{2}\) with respect to basis ùï¨ (see Definition 4.3.3):

\(R=\left[\left[\vec{v}_{1}\right]_{\mathfrak{A}} \quad\left[\vec{v}_{2}\right]_{\mathfrak{A}}\right]\).

A straightforward computation shows that

\(\left[\vec{v}_{1}\right]_{\mathfrak{A}}=\left[\begin{array}{l}2 \\0\end{array}\right] \quad \text { and } \quad\left[\vec{v}_{2}\right]_{\mathfrak{A}}=\left[\begin{array}{r}10 \\8\end{array}\right], \quad \text { so that } \quad R=\left[\begin{array}{rr}2 & 10 \\0 & 8\end{array}\right]\).

(Later in this section we will develop more efficient methods for finding the entries of R.)
To express the relationship between the bases ùï¨ and ùï≠ in matrix form,
we can use Theorem 4.3.4 and write

\(\left[\begin{array}{ll}\vec{v}_{1} & \vec{v}_{2}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ll}\vec{u}_{1} & \vec{u}_{2}\end{array}\right] R, \quad \text { or, } \quad \underbrace{\left[\begin{array}{ll}1 & 1 \\1 & 9 \\1 & 9 \\1 & 1\end{array}\right]}_{M}=\underbrace{\left[\begin{array}{rr}1 / 2 & -1 / 2 \\1 / 2 & 1 / 2 \\1 / 2 & 1 / 2 \\1 / 2 & -1 / 2\end{array}\right]}_{Q} \underbrace{\left[\begin{array}{cc}2 & 10 \\0 & 8\end{array}\right]}_{R} .\)

In this context, it is customary to denote the matrices on the right-hand side by Q and R. Note that we have written the 4 x 2 matrix M with columns \(\vec{v}_{1} \text { and } \vec{v}_{2}\) as the product of the 4 x 2 matrix Q with orthonormal columns and the upper triangular 2 x 2 matrix R with positive entries on the diagonal. This is referred to as the QR factorization of matrix M. Matrix Q stores the orthonormal basis \(\vec{u}_{1}, \vec{u}_{2}\) we constructed, and matrix R gives the relationship between the ‚Äúold‚Äù basis \(\vec{v}_{1}, \vec{v}_{2}\), and the ‚Äúnew‚Äù basis \(\vec{u}_{1}, \vec{u}_{2}\) of V.
