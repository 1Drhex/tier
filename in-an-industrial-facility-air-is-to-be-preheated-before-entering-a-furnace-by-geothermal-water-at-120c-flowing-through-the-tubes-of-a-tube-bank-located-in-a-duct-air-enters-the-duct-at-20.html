Guest Question.

In an industrial facility, air is to be preheated before entering a furnace by geothermal water at \( 120^{\circ} C\) flowing through the tubes of a tube bank located in a duct. Air enters the duct at \( 20^{\circ} C\) and 1 atm with a mean velocity of \( 4.5 m / s \) and flows over the tubes in normal direction. The outer diameter of the tubes is 1.5 cm, and the tubes are arranged in-line with longitudinal and transverse pitches of \( S_{L}=S_{T}=5 cm\). There are 6 rows in the flow direction with 10 tubes in each row, as shown in Fig.7-28. Determine the rate of heat transfer per unit length of the tubes, and the pressure drop across the tube bank.

 

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION Air is heated by geothermal water in a tube bank. The rate of heat transfer to air and the pressure drop of air are to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Steady operating conditions exist. 2 The surface temperature of the tubes is equal to the temperature of geothermal water.

Propenties The exit temperature of air, and thus the mean temperature, is not known. We evaluate the air properties at the assumed mean temperature of \( 60^{\circ} C\) (will be checked later) and 1 atm (Table.A -15).

\(k=0.02808 W / m \cdot K\)

 

\( \rho=1.059 kg / m ^{3} \)

 

\( c_{p}=1.007 kJ / kg \cdot K \)

 

\( Pr =0.7202 \)

 

\( \mu=2.008 \times 10^{-5} kg / m \cdot s \)

 

\( Pr _{s}=\operatorname{Pr}_{120^{\circ} C }=0.7073\)

 

Also, the density of air at the inlet temperature of 20°C (for use in the mass flow rate calculation at the inlet) is \( \rho_{1}=1.204 kg / m ^{3} \)

Analysis It is given that D=0.015 m ,\( S _{L}= S _{T}=0.05 m \) and V =4.5 m / s.

Then the maximum velocity and the Reynolds number based on the maximum velocity become

\(V_{\max }=\frac{S_{T}}{S_{T}-D} V=\frac{0.05}{0.05-0.015}(4.5 m / s )=6.43 m / s \)

 

\(\operatorname{Re}_{D}=\frac{\rho V_{\max } D}{\mu}=\frac{\left(1.059 kg / m ^{3}\right)(6.43 m / s )(0.015 m )}{2.008 \times 10^{-5} kg / m \cdot s }=5086 \)

 

The average Nusselt number is determined using the proper relation from Table 7–2 to be

\(Nu _{D} =0.27 Re _{D}^{0.63} Pr ^{0.36}\left( Pr / Pr _{ s }\right)^{0.25} =0.27(5086)^{0.63}(0.7202)^{0.36}(0.7202 / 0.7073)^{0.25}=52.1 \)

 

This Nusselt number is applicable to tube banks with \( N_{L}>16\) . In our case, the number of rows is \( N_{L}=6\) , and the corresponding correction factor from Table (7-3) is F=0.945. Then the average Nusselt number and heat transfer coefficient for all the tubes in the tube bank become

\( Nu _{D, N_{L<16}}=F Nu _{D}=(0.945)(52.1)=49.3\)

 

\( h=\frac{ Nu _{D, N_{L<16}} k}{D}=\frac{49.3(0.02808 W / m \cdot K )}{0.015 m }=92.2 W / m ^{2} \cdot K\)

 

The total number of tubes is \( N=N_{L} \times N_{T}=6 \times 10=60\) . For a unit tube length (L=1 m ), the heat transfer surface area and the mass flow rate of air (evaluated at the inlet) are

\( A_{s}=N \pi D L=60 \pi(0.015 m )(1 m )=2.827 m ^{2} \)

 

\( \dot{m}=\dot{m}_{1}=\rho_{1} V\left(N_{T} S_{T} L\right) \)

 

\( \quad=\left(1.204 kg / m ^{3}\right)(4.5 m / s )(10)(0.05 m )(1 m )=2.709 kg / s\)

 

Then the fluid exit temperature, the log mean temperature difference, and the rate of heat transfer become

\(T_{e} =T_{s}-\left(T_{s}-T_{i}\right) \exp \left(-\frac{A_{s} h}{\dot{m} c_{p}}\right) =120-(120-20) \exp \left(-\frac{\left(2.827 m ^{2}\right)\left(92.2 W / m ^{2} \cdot K \right)}{(2.709 kg / s )(1007 J / kg \cdot K )}\right)=29.11^{\circ} C \)

 

\(\Delta T_{1 m }=\frac{\left(T_{s}-T_{e}\right)-\left(T_{s}-T_{i}\right)}{\ln \left[\left(T_{s}-T_{e}\right) /\left(T_{s}-T_{i}\right)\right]}=\frac{(120-29.11)-(120-20)}{\ln [(120-29.11) /(120-20)]}=95.4^{\circ} C \)

 

\(\dot{Q}=h A_{s} \Delta T_{1 m }=\left(92.2 W / m ^{2} \cdot K \right)\left(2.827 m ^{2}\right)\left(95.4^{\circ} C \right)=2.49 \times 10^{4} W\)

The rate of heat transfer can also be determined in a simpler way from

\(\dot{Q} =h A_{s} \Delta T_{\operatorname{lm}}=\dot{m} c_{p}\left(T_{e}-T_{i}\right) =(2.709 kg / s )(1007 J / kg \cdot K )(29.11-20)^{\circ} C =2.49 \times 10^{4} W \)

For this square in-line tube bank, the friction coefficient corresponding to \( \operatorname{Re}_{D}=5086\) and \( S_{1} / D=5 / 1.5=3.33\) is, from Fig.7-27 a, f=0.16. Also, \( \chi=1\) for the square arrangements. Then the pressure drop across the tube bank becomes

\(\Delta P =N_{L} f \chi \frac{\rho V_{\max }^{2}}{2} =6(0.16)(1) \frac{\left(1.059 kg / m ^{3}\right)(6.43 m / s )^{2}}{2}\left(\frac{1 N }{1 kg \cdot m / s ^{2}}\right)=21 Pa \)

Discussion The arithmetic mean fluid temperature is \( \left(T_{i}+T_{e}\right) / 2=(20+29.11) / 2=24.6^{\circ} C\) , which is not close to the assumed value of \(60^{\circ} C .\) Repeating calculations for \( 25^{\circ} C\) gives \( 2.57 \times 10^{4} W\) for the rate of heat transfer and 23.5 Pa for the pressure drop.
