Guest Question.

A light airplane weighs 10 000 N, its wingspan measures 12 m, its chord measures 1.8 m, and a payload of 2000 N is anticipated. Predict (a) the takeoff speed if an angle of attack of 8째 is desired, (b) the stall speed of the conventional airfoil, and (c) the power required by the airfoil during cruise at 50 m/s.

Admin
Solution

(a) The lift on an airplane is equal to its weight. With the payload the total weight is \(12000 \mathrm{~N} ;\) hence the lift-coefficient equation (8.4.1) gives the following:

\(C_{D}=\frac{F_{D}}{\frac{1}{2} \rho V^{2} c L} \quad C_{L}=\frac{F_{L}}{\frac{1}{2} \rho V^{2} c L} (8.4.1)\)

\(\begin{aligned}V &=\left(\frac{F_{L}}{\frac{1}{2} \rho C_{L} c L}\right)^{1 / 2} \\&=\left(\frac{12000 \mathrm{~N}}{\frac{1}{2} \times 1.20 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3} \times 1.0 \times 1.8 \mathrm{~m} \times 12 \mathrm{~m}}\right)^{1 / 2}=30.4 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\end{aligned}\)

where we have used \(C_{L}=1.0\) at \(\alpha=8^{\circ}\) from Fig. 8.13, and \(\rho=1.2 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\) since light air craft take off at ground level, and the elevation is not given.

(b) The stall speed is found using a maximum lift coefficient of \(1.72\) from Fig. 8.13:

\(\begin{aligned}V_{\text {stall }} &=\left(\frac{F_{L}}{\frac{1}{2} \rho C_{L} c L}\right)^{1 / 2} \\&=\left(\frac{12000}{\frac{1}{2} \times 1.20 \times 1.72 \times 1.8 \times 12}\right)^{1 / 2}=23.2 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\end{aligned}\)

(c) The power demanded by the airfoil during cruise is equal to the drag force times the velocity. The design lift coefficient is assumed equal to \(0.3\), and thus from Fig. 8.13, assuming a conventional airfoil, \(C_{D}=0.0063\). This gives

\(\begin{aligned}F_{D} &=\frac{1}{2} \rho V^{2} c L C_{D} \\&=\frac{1}{2} \times 1.20 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3} \times 50^{2} \mathrm{~m}^{2} / \mathrm{s}^{2} \times 1.8 \mathrm{~m} \times 12 \mathrm{~m} \times 0.0063=204 \mathrm{~N}\end{aligned}\)

The power is then

\(\begin{aligned}\text { power } &=F_{D} \times V \\&=204 \mathrm{~N} \times 50 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}=10200 \mathrm{~W}\quad or \quad 13.7 hp\end{aligned}\)

The total power would be significantly larger since the drag on the fuselage and tail section must be included.
