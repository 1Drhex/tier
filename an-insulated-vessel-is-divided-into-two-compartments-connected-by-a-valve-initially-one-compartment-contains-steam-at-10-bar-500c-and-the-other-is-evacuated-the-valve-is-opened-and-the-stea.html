Guest Question.

An insulated vessel is divided into two compartments connected by a valve. Initially, one compartment contains steam at 10 bar, 500°C, and the other is evacuated. The valve is opened and the steam is allowed to fill the entire volume, achieving a final pressure of 1 bar. Determine the final temperature, in °C, the percentage of the vessel volume initially occupied by steam and the amount of entropy produced, in kJ/kg. K. [U.P.S.C. 1993]

Admin
Solution

Here throttling process is occurring therefore enthalpy before and after expansion remains same. Let initial and final states be given by 1 and 2. Initial enthalpy, from steam table.\(\\\begin{aligned} \qquad \qquad h_{\text{1 at 10 bar and 500°C}}&= 3478.5 kJ/kg\\s_{\text{1 at 10 bar and 500°C}}&= 7.7622 kJ/kg.K\\v_{\text{1 at 10 bar and 500°C}}&= 0.3541 m^{3}/kg\end{aligned}\\\)Finally pressure becomes 1 bar so the final enthalpy at this pressure (of 1 bar) is also 3478.5 kJ/kg which lies between superheat temperature of 400°C and 500°C at 1 bar. Let temperature be \(T_{2},\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad \qquad h_{\text{at 1 bar, 400°C}}& = 3278.2 kJ/kg\\h_{\text{at 1 bar, 500°C}}& = 3488.1 kJ/kg\\h_{2}&= 3478.5 = h_{\text{at 1 bar, 400°C}} + \frac{(h_{\text{at 1 bar, 500°C}} – h_{\text{at 1 bar, 400°C}})}{(500 – 400)}(T_{2} – 400)\\3478.5& = 3278.2 + \left\{\frac{3488.1 – 3278.2}{100} \right\} (T_{2} – 400)\\T_{2} &= 495.43°C,\\\text{Final temperature}&= \textbf{495.43°C}\end{aligned}\\\text{Entropy for final state,}\\\begin{aligned}s_{2}&= s_{\text{at 1 bar, 400°C}} + \frac{(s_{\text{at 1 bar, 500°C}} – s_{\text{at 1 bar, 400°C}})}{(500 – 400)}(495.43 – 400)\\s_{2}&= 8.5435 + \left\{\frac{8.8342 – 8.5435}{100} \right\} × (95.43)\\s_{2}&= 8.8209 kJ/kg. K\\\text{Change in entropy, Δs}&= 8.8209 – 7.7622\\&= 1.0587 kJ/kg . K\\\text{Change in entropy}&= \textbf{1.0587 kJ/kg K}\\\text{Final specific volume, }v_{2}&= v_{\text{at 1 bar, 400°C}} + \frac{(v_{\text{at 1 bar, 500°C}} – v_{\text{at 1 bar, 400°C}})}{(500 – 400)} × (95.43)\\&= 3.103 + \left\{\frac{3.565 – 3.103}{100} \right\} × 95.43\\v_{2}&= 3.544 m^{3}/kg\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Percentage volume occupied by steam} = \frac{0.3541}{3.544} × 100& = 9.99\%\\\text{Percentage of vessel volume initially occupied by steam }&=\textbf{9.99}\pmb{\%}\end{aligned}\)
