Guest Question.

A simply supported wooden beam with a cross section 4 in. by 6 in. and a span of 10 ft is used to support a uniformly distributed load of 0.1 kip/ft [Fig. 16.7(a)]. The beam cross section is inclined at an angle of \(30^{\circ}\) with the vertical plane [Fig. 16.7(b)]. Find the maximum bending stress and the neutral axis.

Admin
Solution

The maximum moment occurs at the middle of the beam and can be found from the free-body diagram [Fig. 16.7(c)] and the rotational equation of equilibrium. That is,

\(\Sigma M_{c}=-W\left(\frac{L}{2} \right)\left(\frac{L}{2} \right)+W\left(\frac{L}{2} \right)\left(\frac{L}{4} \right)+M_{max}=0\)

or \(M_{max} = wL^{2}/8.\) From Fig. 16.7(d), we see that the components of the load parallel and perpendicular to the y axis are

\(w_{y} = w \cos θ = 0.1 \cos 30^{\circ} = 0.0866\ kip/ft\)

 

\(w_{z} = w \sin θ = 0.1 \sin 30^{\circ} = 0.0500\ kip/ft\)

Bending About the z Axis: The maximum bending moment and moment of inertia about the z axis are given by

\(M_{z}=\frac{W_{y}L^{2}}{8}=\frac{0.0866(10^{2})}{8}=1.082\) kip-ft

 

\(I_{z}=\frac{bh^{3}}{12}=\frac{4(6)^{3}}{12}=72\ in.^{4}\)

Therefore, the maximum bending stress

\(\sigma _{1}=\pm \frac{M_{z}y_{max}}{I_{z}}=\pm \frac{1082(12)(3)}{72}=\pm 541\ psi\)

At A and B, the stress is compression, and at C and D, the stress is tension, as shown in Fig. 16.7(e).

Bending Moment About the y Axis: The maximum bending moment and moment of inertia about the y axis are given by

\(M_{y}=\frac{W_{z}L^{2}}{8}=\frac{0.05(10^{2})}{8}\) or \(M_{y} = 0.625\ kip-ft\)

 

\(I_{y}=\frac{bh^{3}}{12}=\frac{6(4)^{3}}{12}=32\ in.^{4}\)

Therefore, the bending stress

\(\sigma _{2}=\pm \frac{M_{y}z_{max}}{I_{y}}=\pm \frac{625(12)(2)}{32}=\pm 469\ psi\)

At A and D, the stress is compression, and at B and C, the stress is tension, as shown in Fig. 16.7(e).

Combined Stresses: From superposition, the stresses at A, B, C, and D are given by

\(σ_{A} = -541 – 469 = -1010\) psi

\(σ_{B} = -541 + 469 = -72\) psi

\(σ_{C} = +541 + 469 = 1010\) psi

and

\(σ_{D} = +541 – 469 = +72\) psi

The combined stresses are shown in Fig. 16.7(e).

Neutral Axis: The neutral axis can be located by finding the points of zero stress on the sides AD and BC of the beam. The stress distribution on the two sides of the beam is shown in Fig. 16.7(f). From similar triangles, we write a/72 = (6 – a)/1010 or a = 0.399 in. The slope of the neutral axis θ = arctan (6 – 2a)/4 = \(52.4^{\circ}\), as shown in Fig. 16.7(f).
