Guest Question.

Development of hooked bars in tension. Referring to the beam-column joint shown in Fig. 6.8, the No. 11 (No. 36) negative bars are to be extended into the column and terminated in a standard 90° hook, keeping 2 in. clear to the outside face of the column. The column width in the direction of beam width is 16 in. Find the minimum length of embedment of the hook past the column face, and specify the hook details.

Admin
Solution

The development length for hooked bars, measured from the critical section along the bar to the far side of the vertical hook, is given by Eq. (6.6).

\(ℓ_{dh}=\left(\frac{f_y \psi _e \psi _c \psi _r}{50\lambda \sqrt{f_c^\prime} } \right)d_b \)

In this case, the bars are uncoated, giving an epoxy coating factor of \(ψ_e\) = 1.0, the side cover for the No. 11 (No. 36) bars exceeds 2.5 in. and cover beyond the bent bar is adequate, so the cover factor \(ψ_c\) = 0.7, and the confining reinforcement is such that \(ψ_r\) = 1.0. This gives

\(ℓ_{dh}=\left(\frac{f_y \psi _e \psi _c \psi _r}{50\lambda \sqrt{f_c^\prime} } \right)d_b=\left(\frac{60,000\times1.0\times0.7\times1.0}{50\times1.0\times\sqrt{4000} } \right) 1.41=13.28\times1.41=19 in.\)

The only other factor applicable is for excess reinforcement, which is 0.93 as for . Accordingly, the minimum development length for the hooked bars is

\(ℓ_{dh} = 19 \times 0.93\) = 18 in.

The available length within the column is 21 − 2 = 19 in. and can thus accommodate \(ℓ_{dh}\). Note that although \(ℓ_{dh}\) is less that the available length, the hook should be placed to the far side of the column to use the full 19 in. available. The hook will be bent to a minimum diameter of \(8 \times 1.41\) = 11.28 in. The bar will continue for 12 bar diameters, or 17 in. past the end of the bend in the vertical direction.
