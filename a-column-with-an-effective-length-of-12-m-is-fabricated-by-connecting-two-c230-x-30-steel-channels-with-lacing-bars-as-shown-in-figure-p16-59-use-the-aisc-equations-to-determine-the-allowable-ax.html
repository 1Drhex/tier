Guest Question.

A column with an effective length of 12 m is fabricated by connecting two C230 × 30 steel channels with lacing bars as shown in Figure P16.59. Use the AISC equations to determine the allowable axial load \(P_{\text {allow }}\) that may be supported by the column if d = 100 mm. Assume E = 200 GPa and \(\sigma_{Y}\) = 250 MPa.

Admin
Solution

The following section properties for a standard steel C230 × 30 shape are given in Appendix B:

\(\begin{aligned}&A=3,790 mm ^{2}, I_{x}=25.3 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}, r_{x}=81.8 mm \\&I_{y}=1.00 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}, \bar{x}=14.8 mm\end{aligned}\)

Limiting slenderness ratio

\(4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{Y}}}=4.71 \sqrt{\frac{200,000 MPa }{250 MPa }}=133.219\)

Slenderness ratio about horizontal cross-sectional axis:

\(\frac{K L}{r_{x}}=\frac{12,000 mm }{81.8 mm }=146.699\)

Slenderness ratio about vertical cross-sectional axis:

\(\begin{aligned}&I_{y}=2\left[1.00 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}+(100 mm / 2+14.8 mm )^{2}\left(3,790 mm ^{2}\right)\right]=33.8287 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4} \\&r_{y}=\sqrt{\frac{33.8287 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}}{2\left(3,790 mm ^{2}\right)}}=66.805 mm \\&\frac{K L}{r_{y}}=\frac{12,000 mm }{66.805 mm }=179.628\end{aligned}\)

Controlling slenderness ratio: \(K L / r_{y}=179.628\)

Elastic critical buckling stress

\(\sigma_{e}=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{\left(\frac{K L}{r}\right)^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(200,000 MPa )}{(179.628)^{2}}=61.176 MPa\)

Critical and allowable buckling stresses

Since \(\frac{K L}{r}>4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{y}}}\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{c r}=0.877 \sigma_{e}=(0.877)(61.176 MPa )=53.652 MPa \\&\sigma_{ allow }=\frac{\sigma_{c r}}{1.67}=\frac{53.652 MPa }{1.67}=32.127 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Allowable axial load:

\(P_{\text {allow }}=\sigma_{\text {allow }} A=\left(32.127 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(2 \times 3,790 mm ^{2}\right)=244 kN\)
