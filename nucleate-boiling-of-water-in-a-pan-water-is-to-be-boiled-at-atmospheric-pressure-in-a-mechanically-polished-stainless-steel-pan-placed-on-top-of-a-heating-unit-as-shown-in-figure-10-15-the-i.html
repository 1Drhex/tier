Nucleate Boiling of Water in a Pan

Water is to be boiled at atmospheric pressure in a mechanically polished stainless steel pan placed on top of a heating unit, as shown in Figure 10–15. The inner surface of the bottom of the pan is maintained at \( 108^{\circ}C \). If the diameter of the bottom of the pan is 30 cm, determine (a) the rate of heat transfer to the water and (b) the rate of evaporation of water.

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION Water is boiled at 1 atm pressure on a stainless steel surface. The rate of heat transfer to the water and the rate of evaporation of water are to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Steady operating conditions exist. 2 Heat losses from the heater and the pan are negligible.

Properties The properties of water at the saturation temperature of \( 100^{\circ}C \) are \( \sigma = 0.0589 N/m \) (Table 10–1) and, from Table A-9,

\( \rho_{l} = 957.9 kg/m^{3} h_{fg} = 2257.0 \times 10^{3} J/kg \)

 

\( \rho_{v} = 0.6 kg/m^{3} \mu_{l} = 0.282 \times 10^{-3} kg \cdot m/s \)

 

\( Pr_{l} = 1.75 C_{pl} = 4217 J/kg \cdot ^{\circ}C \)

 

Also, \( C_{sf} = 0.0130 \) and n = 1.0 for the boiling of water on a mechanically polished stainless steel surface (Table 10–3). Note that we expressed the properties in units specified under Eq. \( \dot{q}_{nucleate} = \mu _{l} h_{fg} \left [ \frac{g \left ( \rho_{l} – \rho_{v}\right )}{\sigma } \right ]^{1/2} \left [ \frac{C_{p} \left ( T_{s} – T_{sat} \right )}{C_{sf} h_{fg} Pr_{l}^{n}} \right ]^{3} \) in connection with their definitions in order to avoid unit manipulations.

Analysis (a) The excess temperature in this case is \( \Delta T = T_{s} – T_{sat} = 108 – 100 = 8^{\circ}C \) which is relatively low (less than \( 30^{\circ}C \)). Therefore, nucleate boiling will occur. The heat flux in this case can be determined from the Rohsenow relation to be

\( \dot{q}_{nucleate} = \mu _{l} h_{fg} \left [ \frac{g \left ( \rho_{l} – \rho_{v}\right )}{\sigma } \right ]^{1/2} \left [ \frac{C_{pl} \left ( T_{s} – T_{sat} \right )}{C_{sf}}h_{fg} Pr_{l}^{n} \right ]^{3} \)

 

\( = \left ( 0.282 \times 10^{-3} \right )\left ( 2257 \times 10^{3} \right ) \left [ \frac{9.81 \times \left ( 957.9 – 0.6 \right )}{0.0589} \right ]^{1/2}\)

 

\( \times \left ( \frac{4217 \left ( 108 – 100 \right )}{0.0130 \left ( 2257 \times 10^{3} \right )1.75} \right )^{3} = 7.20 \times 10^{4} W/m^{2} \)

 

The surface area of the bottom of the pan is

\( A = \pi D^{2}/4 = \pi \left ( 0.3 m \right )^{2} /4 = 0.07069 m^{2} \)

 

Then the rate of heat transfer during nucleate boiling becomes

\( \dot{Q}_{boiling} = A \dot{q}_{nucleate} = \left ( 0.07069 m^{2} \right )\left ( 7.20 \times 10^{4} W/m^{2} \right )\)

 

\( = 5093 W \)

 

(b) The rate of evaporation of water is determined from

\( \dot{m}_{evaporation} = \frac{\dot{Q}_{boiling}}{h_{fg}} = \frac{5093 J/s}{2257 \times 10^{3} J/kg} = 2.26 \times 10^{-3} kg/s\)

 

That is, water in the pan will boil at a rate of more than 2 grams per second.
