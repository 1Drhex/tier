Guest Question.

A 50 kg machine has a rotating unbalance. The machine is mounted on an elastic foundation with a stiffness of \(1.3 \times10^{5} N/m\), and damping ratio of 0.04 and operates at 1500 rpm. An accelerometer is mounted on the machine to monitor its steady-state vibrations. (a) What is the minimum natural frequency of an accelerometer of damping ratio 0.2 such that it measures the vibrations of the machine with no more than 2 percent error?

(b) When the accelerometer of part

(a) is used, it measures a steady-state amplitude of \(14.8 m/s^2\). What is the magnitude of the rotating unbalance?

(c) What is the accelerometer output if the machine operates at 1200 rpm?

Admin
Solution

(a) The percent error in the accelerometer measurement is \( E =100\left|1- M(r,\zeta )\right|\) where the frequency ratio refers to the ratio of the frequency of excitation to the natural frequency of the accelerometer. The accelerometer works in the range of small r and \( \zeta \lt \frac{1}{\sqrt{2} }\). Thus, \( M(r,\zeta )\gt 1.\) In order for the error to be less than 2 percent

\( 100\left[M(r, 0.2)-1\right]\lt 2\) (a)

Or \( M(r, 0.2)\lt1.02\), which implies that

\( \frac{1}{\sqrt{(1-r^{2})^{2}+ \left[2(0.2)r\right]^{2} } }\lt 1.02\) (b)

The solutions of Equation (a) are \( r\lt0.146\) and \( r \gt1.35\) However, requiring \( r \gt1.35\) will lead to the error being greater than 2 percent for when \( 100\left[1- M(r,0.2)\right] \lt 0.98\)

Thus, the minimum natural frequency for the error to be less than 2 percent requires that r 1.46 corresponds to \(\omega = 1500 rpm.\) To this end

\( \frac{\left(1500 \frac{rev}{\min} \right)\left(2\pi\frac{rad}{rev} \right)\left(\frac{1\min}{60 \sec } \right) }{\omega _{n}}=0.146\) (c)

which leads to\(\omega_{v} = 1076 rad/s.\)

(b) The error in the measurement is 2 percent. Thus, if A is the actual acceleration and B is the measurement, then B = 1.02A. With \(B = 14.8 m/s^{2}\) , this gives \(A = 14.5 m/s^{2}\) . Then the amplitude of the steady-state vibration is related to the acceleration amplitude by \(A \omega^{2} X\). With \(\omega = 1500 rpm = 157.1 rad/s\), the steady-state amplitude is \(X = 5.87 \times 10^{-4} m. \) For the machine with a rotating unbalance,

\( \frac{mX}{m_{0}e} =\Lambda (r_{m}, 0.04)\)

where \(r_{m}\) is the ratio of the excitation frequency to the natural frequency of the machine. Performing the necessary calculations, the natural frequency of the machine is

\( \omega _{n}=\sqrt{\frac{k}{m_{m}} }=\sqrt{\frac{1.3\times 10^{5}\frac{N}{m} }{50kg} }=51.0 rad/s\)

The frequency ratio is

\( r\frac{\omega }{\omega _{n}}=\frac{157.1 rad/s}{51.0 rad/s} = 3.08\) (e)

Then

\( \Lambda (3.08,0.04)=\frac{(3.08)^{2}}{\sqrt{\left[1-(3.08)^{2}\right]^{2} +\left[2(0.04)(3.08)\right]^{2} } }=1.12 \) (f)

and the magnitude of the rotating unbalance is

\( m_{0}e=\frac{mX}{\Lambda (3.08, 0.04)}=\frac{(50 kg)(5.9\times 10^{-4}m)}{1.12} =0.0264 kg\cdot m\) (g)

(c) The machine now rotates at \(\omega= 1200 rpm = 125.7 rad/s.\) Thus, \( r=\frac{125.7 rad/s}{51.0 rad/s}=2.46\) and \( \Lambda (2.46, 0.4)= 1.197\). The steady-state response of the machine is \( x(t)=X\sin (\omega t -\phi )\)

 

\( X =\frac{m_{0}e}{m_{m}}\Lambda (r, 0.04) =\frac{0.0264 kg\cdot m}{50kg}(1.197)=6.32 \times 10^{-4}m\)

(h)

and

\( \phi =\tan^{-1}\left[\frac{2(0.04)r}{1-r^{2}} \right]=\tan^{-1}\left[\frac{(0.08)(2.46)}{1-(2.46)^{2}} \right] =-0.0389 rad\) (i)

Thus, the steady-state response of the machine is

\( x(t)= 6.32 \times 10^{-4}\sin (125.7t+0.0389)m\) (j)

The accelerometer output is \(-\frac{\omega ^{2}_{n}}{M(r_{a},0.2)}z(t)\) where \( r_{a} = \frac{125.7 rad/s}{1076 rad/s} = 0.117\) and M(0.117,0.2) = 1.013. The error in the accelerometer measurement is 1.3 percent. z(t) is the displacement of the seismic mass relative to the machine and is given as

\( z(t)=Z_{a}\sin(125.7t +0.0389-\phi _{a})\) (k)

where

\( Z_{a}=X\Lambda (0.117, 0.2)=(6.32 \times 10^{-4}m)(0.013)=8.78 \times^{-6}m\) (l)

and

\( \phi _{a}=\tan^{-1}\left[\frac{2(0.2)(0.117)}{1-(0.117)^{2}} \right]=0.0461 rad\) (m)

Thus, the accelerometer output is

\( a(t)= -\frac{(1076 rad/s)^{2}}{1.013}(8.78\times^{-6}m) \sin(125.7t+0.0389-0.0461)\)

\( = 10.03 \sin(125.7t -0.0072)m/s^{2}\) (n)
