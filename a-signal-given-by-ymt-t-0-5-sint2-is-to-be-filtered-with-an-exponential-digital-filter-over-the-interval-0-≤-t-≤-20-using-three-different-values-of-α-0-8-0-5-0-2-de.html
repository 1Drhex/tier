Guest Question.

A signal given by

\(y_{m}(t)=t+0.5 \sin \left(t^{2}\right)\)

is to be filtered with an exponential digital filter over the interval \(0 \leq t \leq 20\). Using three different values of \(\alpha(0.8,0.5,0.2)\), determine the output of the filter at each sampling time. Do this for sampling periods of \(1.0\) and \(0.1\). Compare the three filters for each value of \(\Delta t\).

Admin
Solution

Table \(S 17.3\) lists the unfiltered output and, from Eq. \(17-9\), the filtered data for sampling periods of \(1.0\) and \(0.1\). Notice that for sampling period of \(0.1\), the unfiltered and filtered outputs were obtained at \(0.1\) time increments, but they are reported only at intervals of \(1.0\) to preserve conciseness and facilitate comparison.

\(y_{F}(k)=\alpha y_{m}(k)+(1-\alpha) y_{F}(k-1)\) (17-9)

The results show that for each value of \(\Delta t\), the data become smoother as \(\alpha\) decreases, but at the expense of lagging behind the mean output \(y(t)=t\). Moreover, lower sampling period improves filtering by giving smoother data and less lagg for the same value of \(\alpha\).
