Guest Question.

Determine the bending moment for a cantilever subjected to a sinusoidal line load (Fig. 7.28).

Admin
Solution

Since the shear force and the bending moment are zero at the free end, it is convenient to use a coordinate system where the x-axis points to the left. The load is given by

\(q(x)=q_{0} \sin \frac{\pi x}{l}\) .

Integration leads to

\(V(x)=-\int{q_{0} \sin \frac{\pi x}{l} dx} = q_{0}\frac{l}{\pi } \cos \frac{\pi x}{l}+C_{1}\) ,

\(M(x)=q_{0} \left(\frac{l}{\pi } \right) ^{2} \sin \frac{\pi x}{l} + C_{1}x +C_{2}\) .

The constants of integration follow from the boundary conditions:

\(V(0)=0 \rightarrow C_{1}=-\frac{q_{0} l}{\pi } , M(0)=0 \rightarrow C_{2}=0\) .

Thus, the stress resultants are obtained as

\(V(x)= \frac{q_{0} l}{\pi }\left(\cos \frac{\pi x}{l} -1 \right) , \underline{\underline{M(x)=-\frac{q_{0}l^{ 2} }{\pi }\left(\frac{x}{l} -\sin \frac{\pi x}{l} \right) } }\) .

Their maximum values are found at the clamping (x = l):

\(V(l)=-\frac{2}{\pi } q_{0} l , M(l)=- \frac{1}{\pi } q_{0}l^{ 2}\) .

Note that the shear force is negative since the x-axis points to the left (positive face!).
