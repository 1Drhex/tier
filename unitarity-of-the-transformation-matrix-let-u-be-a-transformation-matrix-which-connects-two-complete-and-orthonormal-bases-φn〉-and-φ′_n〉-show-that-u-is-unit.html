Guest Question.

(Unitarity of the transformation matrix)

Let U be a transformation matrix which connects two complete and orthonormal bases \(\left\{| \phi _n〉\right\} \) and \(\left\{| \phi^{\prime }_n〉\right\}. \)Show that U is unitary.

 

Admin
Solution

For this we need to prove that \(\hat{U} \hat{U}^†=\hat{I}, \) which reduces to showing that \(〈\phi _m |\hat{U} \hat{U}^†| \phi _n〉=\delta _{mn}. \) This goes as follows:

\(〈\phi _m|\hat{U} \hat{U}^†|\phi _n〉=〈\phi _m|\hat{U}\left(\sum\limits_{l}{|\phi _l〉〈\phi _l|} \right) \hat{U}^†|\phi _n〉=\sum\limits_{l}{U_{ml}U^*_{nl}}, \) (2.233)

where \(U_{ml}= 〈\phi _m | \hat{U} | \phi _l〉 \) and \(U^*_{nl}= 〈\phi _l | \hat{U}^† | \phi _n〉 =〈\phi _n | \hat{U} | \phi _l〉^* \). According to (2.231) \(U_{mn}= 〈\phi^\prime _m | \phi _n〉. \), \(U_{ml}= 〈\phi^\prime _m | \phi _l〉 \) and \(U^*_{nl}= 〈\phi _l | \phi^\prime_n〉; \) we can thus rewrite (2.233) as

\(\sum\limits_{l}{U_{ml}U^*_{nl}}=\sum\limits_{l}{〈\phi^\prime _m | \phi _l〉}〈\phi_l | \phi^\prime _n〉= 〈\phi^\prime _m | \phi^\prime _n 〉=\delta _{mn}. \) (2.234)

Combining (2.233) and (2.234), we infer \({〈\phi_m | \hat{U} \hat{U}^† | \phi _n〉}=\delta _{mn}, or \hat{U} \hat{U}^†=\hat{I} \).
