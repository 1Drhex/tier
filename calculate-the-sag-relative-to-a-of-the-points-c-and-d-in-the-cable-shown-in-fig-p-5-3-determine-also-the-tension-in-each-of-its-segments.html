Guest Question.

Calculate the sag, relative to A, of the points C and D in the cable shown in Fig. P.5.3. Determine also the tension in each of its segments.

Admin
Solution

Suppose the horizontal and vertical components of reaction at A and E are \(R_{ A , H }, R_{ A , V }\) and \(R_{ E , H }, R_{ E , V }\), respectively. Then, taking moments about B,

\( R_{ A , V } \times 4-R_{ A , H } \times 2.6=0\)

i.e.

\(1.54 R_{ A , V }-R_{ A , H }=0\) (i)

Now taking moments about E

\( R_{ A , V } \times 18-R_{ A , H } \times 0.5-3 \times 14-5 \times 9-4 \times 4=0 \)

i.e.

\( 36 R_{ A , V }-R_{ A , H }-206=0\) (ii)

Eq. (i)–Eq. (ii)

\( -34.46 R_{ A , V }+206=0\)

i.e.

\(R_{ A , V }=5.98 kN\)

From Eq. (i)

\(R_{ A , H }=9.21 kN\)

Then

\( T_{ AB }=\sqrt{5.98^{2}+9.21^{2}}=10.98 kN \)

Suppose α is the angle AB makes with the horizontal, then

\( \alpha=\tan ^{-1} \frac{2.6}{4}=33.02^{\circ} \)

Also let β be the angle BC makes with the horizontal. Then, resolving horizontally at B

\( T_{ BC } \cos \beta=T_{ AB } \cos \alpha=10.98 \cos 33.02^{\circ}=9.21 kN \)

Resolving vertically at B

\( T_{ BC } \sin \beta+3=T_{ AB } \sin \alpha=10.98 \sin 33.02^{\circ}=5.98 kN \)

i.e.

\( T_{ BC } \sin \beta=2.98 \)

Then

\(\tan \beta=\frac{2.98}{9.21}=0.324\)

i.e.

\(\beta=17.93^{\circ}\)

and

\(T_{ BC }=9.68 kN\)

The sag of C below A is equal to 2.6 + 5 tan β=4.22 m.
Let γ be the angle CD makes with the horizontal. Then, resolving horizontally at C,

\( T_{ CD } \cos \gamma=T_{ CB } \cos \beta=9.68 \cos 17.93^{\circ}=9.21 kN \)

Resolving vertically

\( T_{ CD } \sin \gamma=5-T_{ CB } \sin \beta=2.02 \)

Then

\( \gamma=\tan ^{-1} \frac{2.02}{9.21}=12.37^{\circ} \)

and

\( T_{ CD }=\frac{9.21}{\cos \gamma}=9.431 kN \)

The sag of D below A is equal to 4.22-5 tan γ =3.13 m.
Resolving horizontally

\( R_{ E , H }=R_{ A , H }=9.21 kN \)

and resolving vertically

\( R_{ E , V }=12-R_{ A , V }=6.02 kN \)

Then

\( T_{ ED }=\sqrt{6.02^{2}+9.21^{2}}=11.0 kN . \)
