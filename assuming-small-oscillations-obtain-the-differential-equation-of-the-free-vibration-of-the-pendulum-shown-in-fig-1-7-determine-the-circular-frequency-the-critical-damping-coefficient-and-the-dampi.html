Guest Question.

Assuming small oscillations, obtain the differential equation of the free vibration of the pendulum shown in Fig. 1.7. Determine the circular frequency, the critical damping coefficient, and the damping factor of this system, assuming that the rod is massless.

Admin
Solution

As shown in the figure, let \(R_x and R_y\) be the components of the reaction force at the pin joint. The moments of the externally applied forces about O are

\( M_a=-(kl\sin \theta )l\cos \theta -(cl\theta \cos \theta )l\cos \theta -mgl\sin \theta \)

For small oscillations, sin θ≈θ and cos θ≈1. In this case, \(M_a \)reduces to

\( M_a = -kl^2\theta -cl^2\dot{\theta} -mgl\theta \)

One can show that the moment of the inertia (effective) forces about O is given by

\( M_{eff}= ml^2\ddot{\theta } \)

Therefore, the second-order differential equation of motion of the free vibration is given by

\( -kl^2\theta -cl^2\dot{\theta} -mgl\theta= ml^2\ddot{\theta } \)

or

\( ml^2\ddot{\theta } +cl^2\dot{\theta} + (kl + mg)l\theta = 0\) (1.75)

which can be written in the general form of Eq. 1.73 as

\( m_e\ddot{x} + c_e\dot{x} + k_ex = 0 \)

 

\( m_e\ddot{\theta } + c_e\dot{\theta } + k_e\theta = 0 \)

where

\(m_e=ml^2, c_e=cl^2, k_e (kl + mg)l\)

where the units of \(m_e\) are \(kg \cdot m^2\) or, equivalently, \(N\cdot m \cdot s^2\), the units of the
equivalent damping coefficient \(c_e\) are \(N\cdot m \cdot s\), and the units of the equivalent stiffness coefficient \(k_e\) are \(N\cdot m\). The natural frequency ω is

\(\omega =\sqrt{\frac{k_e}{m_e} } = \sqrt{\frac{(kl + mg)l}{ml^2} } = \sqrt{\frac{kl + mg}{ml} } \) rad∕s

The critical damping coefficient \( C_c\) is

\( C_c = 2m_e\omega =2ml^2\sqrt{\frac{kl + mg}{ml} } = 2\sqrt{ml^3(kl+mg)} \)

The damping factor ξ of this system is

\(\xi = \frac{c_e}{C_c} = \frac{cl^2}{2\sqrt{ml^3(kl+mg)} } \)
