Guest Question.

For Figure 24–31, \(V_{an} = 120 V\angle 0^{\circ}\). Compute the readings of each meter, then sum to determine total power. Compare \(P_{T}\) to the \(P_{T}\) found in .

Admin
Solution

\(V_{an} = 120 V\angle 0^{\circ}\). Thus, \(V_{ab} = 207.8 V\angle 30^{\circ}\) and \(V_{bc} = 207.8 V \angle -90^{\circ}\)—see Note. \(I_{a} = V_{an}/Z_{an} = 120 V\angle 0^{\circ}/(9 – j12) \Omega=8 A\angle 53.13^{\circ}\). Thus, \(I_{c} = 8 A\angle 173.13^{\circ}\).

First consider wattmeter 1, Figure 24–32. Note that \(W_{1}\) is connected to terminals a-b; thus it has voltage \(V_{ab}\) across it and current \(I_{a}\) through it. Its reading is therefore \(P_{1} = V_{ab}I_{a}\cos \theta_{1}\), where \(\theta_{1}\) is the angle between \(V_{ab}\) and \(I_{a}. V_{ab}\) has an angle of 30° and \(I_{a}\) has an angle of 53.13°. Thus, \(\theta_{1} = 53.13^{\circ} – 30^{\circ} = 23.13^{\circ}\) and \(P_{1} = (207.8)(8)\cos 23.13^{\circ} = 1529 W\).

Now consider wattmeter 2, Figure 24–33. Since \(W_{2}\) is connected to terminals c-b, the voltage across it is \(V_{cb}\) and the current through it is \(I_{c}\). But \(V_{cb} = -V_{bc} = 207.8 V\angle 90^{\circ}\) and \(I_{c} = 8 A\angle 173.13^{\circ}\). The angle between \(V_{cb}\) and \(I_{c}\) is thus \(173.13^{\circ} – 90^{\circ} = 83.13^{\circ}\). Therefore, \(P_{2}=V_{cb}I_{c}\cos\theta _{2}=(207.8)(8)\cos83.13^{\circ}=199 W\) and \(P_{T}=P_{1}+P_{2}=1529+199=1728 W\). (This agrees well with the answer we obtained in .) Note that one of the wattmeters reads lower than the other. (This is generally the case for the twowattmeter method.)
