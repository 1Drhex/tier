Guest Question.

Determine the joint displacements, member local end forces, and support reactions for the plane frame of Fig. 7.10(a), due to the combined effect of the loading shown and a settlement of 1 in. of the left support. Use the matrix stiffness method.

Admin
Solution

This frame was analyzed in for external loading. In this example, we use the same analytical model of the frame, so that the various member and structure matrices calculated previously can be reused in the present example.

Analytical Model: See Fig. 7.10(b). The frame has three degrees of freedom and six restrained coordinates.

Structure Stiffness Matrix: As determined in , the structure stiffness matrix for the frame, in units of kips and inches, is given by

S=\(\left[\begin{matrix} 1,685.3 & 507.89 & 670.08 \\ 507.89 & 1,029.2 & 601.42 \\ 670.08 & 601.42 & 2,838.48 \end{matrix} \right]\) (1)

Structure Fixed-Joint Forces Due to Member Loads: From ,

\(P_{f}=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 60 \\ -750 \end{matrix}\right]\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{matrix}\) (2)

Joint Load Vector: From ,

P=\(\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ -1,500 \end{matrix}\right]\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{matrix}\) (3)

Structure Fixed-Joint Forces Due to Support Displacement: From Fig. 7.10(b), we observe that the given 1 in. downward displacement of support 1 can be expressed as

\(d_{s5}\)=-1 in.

As member 1 is the only member attached to support 1, we form its global fixed-end displacement vector due to support displacement, using the member code numbers 4, 5, 6, 1, 2, 3, as

\(v_{fs1}=\left[\begin{matrix} v_{fs1} \\ v_{fs2} \\ v_{fs3}\\ v_{fs4}\\v_{fs5}\\v_{fs6} \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 4 \\ 5 \\ 6 \\1\\2\\3 \end{matrix}=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ d_{s2} \\ 0\\ 0\\0\\0 \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ -1 \\ 0\\ 0\\0\\0 \end{matrix} \right]\) in

Next, we substitute the member global stiffness matrix \(K_{1}\) (given in ) and the foregoing \(v_{fs1}\) vector into Eq. (7.26) \(F_{fs}=Kv_{fs}\), to evaluate the member global fixed-end force vector, \(F_{fs1}\) , due to support settlement:

\(F_{fs1}=K_{1}v_{fs1}=\left[\begin{matrix} -507.89 \\ -1,021.4 \\ -335.04 \\\overset{…………}{507.89}\\1,021.4\\-335.04 \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 4 \\ 5 \\ 6\\1\\2\\3 \end{matrix}\)

Based on the member code numbers, we add the elements in the fourth, fifth, and sixth rows of \(F_{fs1}\) into rows 1, 2, and 3, respectively, of the previously formed \(P_{f}\) vector (Eq. (2))

\(P_{f}=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 60 \\ -750 \end{matrix}\right]\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{matrix}\) , to obtain the structure fixed-joint force vector due to the combined effect of the member loads and support displacement, as

\(P_{f}=\left[\begin{matrix} 0+507.89 \\ 60+1,021.4 \\ -750-335.04 \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{matrix}=\left[\begin{matrix} 507.89 \\ 1,081.4 \\ -1,085.04 \end{matrix} \right]\) (4)

Joint Displacements: By substituting P (Eq. (3)) , Pf (Eq. (4)) , and S (Eq. (1)) into Eq. (6.42) \(P-P_{f}=Sd\) , we write the stiffness relations for the entire frame as

\(P-P_{f}=Sd\)

 

\(\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ -1,500 \end{matrix} \right]-\left[\begin{matrix} 507.89 \\ 1,081.4 \\ -1,085.04 \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} -507.89 \\ -1,081.4 \\ -414.96 \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} 1,685.3 & 507.89 & 670.08 \\ 507.89 & 1,029.2 & 601.42 \\ 670.08 & 601.42 & 283,848 \end{matrix} \right]\left[\begin{matrix} d_{1} \\ d_{2} \\ d_{3} \end{matrix} \right]\)

Solving these equations, we determine the joint displacements to be

d=\(\left[\begin{matrix} 0.017762 in \\ -1.0599 in \\ 0.00074192 rad \end{matrix} \right]\)

Member End Displacements and End Forces:

Member 1

\(v_{1}=\left[\begin{matrix} v_{1} \\ v_{2} \\ v_{3}\\v_{4}\\v_{5}\\v_{6} \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 4 \\ 5 \\ 6\\1\\2\\3 \end{matrix}=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ d_{s5} \\ 0\\d_{1}\\d_{2}\\d_{3} \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ -1 \\ 0\\0.017762 \\-1.0599\\0.00074192 \end{matrix} \right]\)

Using the member transformation matrix \(T_{1}\) from , and Eq. (6.20) u=Tv, we calculate

\(u_{1}=T_{1}v_{1}=\left[\begin{matrix} -0.89443 \\ -0.44721 \\ 0\\-0.94006 \\-0.48988\\0.00074192 \end{matrix} \right]\)

Next, we use the member local stiffness matrix \(k_{1}\) and fixed-end force vector \(Q_{f1}\) from , and Eq. (6.4) \(Q=ku+Q_{f}\) , to compute the local end forces as

\(Q_{1}=k_{1}u_{1}+Q_{f1}=\left[\begin{matrix} 98.441 k \\ 20.919 k \\ 1,431.7 k-in\\-17.943 k \\19.331 k\\-1,218.6 k-in \end{matrix} \right]\)

The local member end forces are depicted in Fig. 7.10(c).

The member global end forces F can nowbe determined by applying Eq. (6.23) \(T^{T}Q\) , as

\(F_{1}=T^{T}Q_{1}=\left[\begin{matrix} 25.313 \\ 97.404 \\ 1,431.7 \\\overset{…………}{-25.314}\\-7.404\\-1,218.6 \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 4 \\ 5 \\ 6\\1\\2\\3 \end{matrix}\)

Using the member code numbers, the pertinent elements of \(F_{1}\) are stored in the reaction vector R (see Fig. 7.10(d)).

Member 2 The global and local end displacements for this horizontal member are

\(u_{2}=v_{2}=\left[\begin{matrix} v_{1} \\ v_{2} \\ v_{3}\\v_{4}\\v_{5}\\v_{6} \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3\\7\\8\\9 \end{matrix}=\left[\begin{matrix} d_{1} \\ d_{1} \\ d_{3}\\0\\0\\0 \end{matrix}\right]=\left[\begin{matrix} 0.017762 \\ -1.0599 \\ 0.00074192\\0 \\0\\0 \end{matrix} \right]\)

Using \(k_{2}\) and \(Q_{f2}\) from , we compute the member local and global end forces to be

\(F_{2}=Q_{2}=k_{2}u_{2}+Q_{f2}=\left[\begin{matrix} 25.325 k \\ 7.4235 k \\ -281.39 k-in \\\overset{………………}{-25.325 k}\\22.576 k\\-1,537 k-in \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3\\7\\8\\9 \end{matrix}\)

The pertinent elements of \(F_{2}\) are stored in R.

Support Reactions: See Figs. 7.10(d) and (e).
