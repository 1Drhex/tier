Guest Question.

A 480–120 V, 20 kVA, two-winding transformer with a leakage impedance of (0.02 + j0.05) per unit has an efficiency of 0.96 when it delivers rated current at 0.90 power factor lagging. This transformer is to be connected as an autotransforme to a 600 V source to supply a load at either 480 or 120 V. For each type of autotransformer connection determine:
a. The kVA rating of the autotransformer. What percent of this kVA passes through the autotransformer untransformed?
b. The efficiency at full-load and 0.90 power factor lagging.
c. The input current when the load is shorted. Compare the results with those of the two-winding operation.

Admin
Solution

This problem compares the essential parameters of a two-winding transformer with those of its autotransformer connections.

Type (A) Autotransformer Connection (Load across the 120 V Winding)

a. Referring to Fig. 2-46(b), the magnitude of the current through the input will be the current rating of the 480 V winding. That is,

\(I=\frac{20,000}{480}=41.67A\)

Therefore, the input apparent power to the autotransformer is

S = 41.67(600)
=\(\underline{ \underline{25 kVA}}\)

Of these 25 kVA, 5 kVA pass unaltered, or untransformed, from input to output; the remaining 20 kVA are transformed, as in the case of the two-winding transformer connection. Thus,

percentage of untransformed kVA=\(\frac{5}{25}\left(100\right)\)

= \(\underline{\underline{20\%}}\)

b. The losses of the two-winding transformer are

losses = power input – power output =\(\frac{P_{out}}{\eta } -P_{out}\)

=\(20,000\left(0.9\right)\left(\frac{1}{0.96}-1 \right) = 750W\)

For the autotransformer connection, the losses remain constant. Thus, the efficiency is

\( \eta =\frac{25\left(0.90\right) }{25\left(0.90\right)+0.750 }=0.97\)

c. For the two-winding transformer, the magnitude of the short-circuit current is

\(I_{sc}=\left|\frac{1}{0.02+j0.05} \right|\)

= 18.57 pu

For the HV winding, the current in amperes is

\(I_{sc}=18.57\left(41.66\right)\)

=\(\underline{\underline{ 773.73 A} }\)

The leakage impedance, in ohms, is
HV side

\(Z_{bH}=\frac{\left(480\right)^{2} }{20,000}=11.52\Omega\)

 

\(Z_{H}=(0.02 + j0.05)(11.52)=0.62\angle 68.2°\Omega \)

LV side

\(Z_{bL}=\frac{\left(120\right)^{2} }{20,000}=0.72\Omega\)

 

\(Z_{L}=\left(0.02+j 0.05\right)\left(0.72\right) =0.04\angle 68.2^{°} \Omega\)

In the case of the autotransformer, when the 120 V winding is shorted, the source of 600 V is applied across the 480 V winding. Thus, the magnitude of the short-circuit current is

\(I_{sc}=\frac{600}{0.62}=\underline{\underline{967.16A} }\)

or, by using the appropriate relationship from Table 2-1.

\(I_{sc}=773.73\frac{\left(1+a\right) }{a}=773.73\left(\frac{5}{4} \right) \)

\(=\underline{\underline{967.16A} }\)
Type (B) Autotransformer Connection (Load across the 480 V Winding)

a. Referring to Fig. 2-46(d), the current through the 600 V source will be the rated current of the 120 V winding. That is,

\(I=\frac{20\times 10^{3}}{120}=166.67A\)

This current must pass through the input terminals. Therefore, the apparent input power for this type of autotransformer connection is
S = 166.67(600)
= \(\underline{\underline{100} } kVA\)

Of these 100 kVA, 80 kVA pass unaltered from the input to the output, while the remaining 20 kVA are transformed as in the case of the two-winding connection.
Expressing the untransformed kVA in percentage of the total output kVA, we have

\(\left(kVA\right) _{untr^{.}}=\frac{80}{100}\left(100\right)=\underline{80\% } \)

b. Since the losses are constant at 0.75 kW, the efficiency is

\(\eta =\frac{100\left(0.9\right) }{100\left(0.9\right)+0.75}\)

\(=\underline{\underline{0.99} }\)
c. By shorting the load, the 600 V are applied across the 120 V winding. Thus, the magnitude of the short-circuit current is

\(I_{sc}=\frac{600}{0.04}=\underline{\underline{15,474.6 A} } \)
