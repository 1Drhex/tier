Guest Question.

A shell-and-tube heat exchanger must be designed to heat 2.5 kg/s of water from 15 to \(85^{\circ} C .\) The heating is to be accomplished by passing hot engine oil, which is available at ] \(160^{\circ} C ,\)] through the shell side of the exchanger. The oil is known to provide an average convection coefficient of ] \(h_{o}=400 W / m ^{2} \cdot K\) ] on the outside of the tubes. Ten tubes pass the water through the shell. Each tube is thin walled, of diameter D = 25 mm, and makes eight passes through the shell. If the oil leaves the exchanger at] \(100^{\circ} C ,\) ] what is its flow rate? How long must the tubes be to accomplish the desired heating?

Admin
Solution

Known: Fluid inlet and outlet temperatures for a shell-and-tube heat exchanger with 10 tubes making eight passes.

Find:
1. Oil flow rate required to achieve specified outlet temperature.
2. Tube length required to achieve specified water heating.

Assumptions:
1. Negligible heat loss to the surroundings and kinetic and potential energy changes.
2. Constant properties.
3. Negligible tube wall thermal resistance and fouling effects.
4. Fully developed water flow in tubes.

Properties: Table A.5, unused engine oil \(\left(\bar{T}_{h}=130^{\circ} C \right): c_{p}=2350 J / kg \cdot K.\) Table A.6, water
\(\left(\bar{T}_{c}=50^{\circ} C \right): c_{p}=4181 J / kg \cdot K , \mu=548 \times 10^{-6} N \cdot s / m ^{2}, k=0.643 W / m \cdot K, Pr = 3.56.\)

Analysis:
1. From the overall energy balance, Equation 11.7b, the heat transfer required of the exchanger is

 

\(q=\dot{m}_{c} c_{p, c}\left(T_{c, o}-T_{c, i}\right)\) (11.7b)

 

\(q=\dot{m}_{c} c_{p, c}\left(T_{c, o}-T_{c, i}\right)=2.5 kg / s \times 4181 J / kg \cdot K (85-15){ }^{\circ} C\)

 

\(q=7.317 \times 10^{5} W\)

 

Hence, from Equation 11.6b,

 

\(q=\dot{m}_{h} c_{p, h}\left(T_{h, i}-T_{h, o}\right)\) (11.6b)

 

\(\dot{m}_{h}=\frac{q}{c_{p, h}\left(T_{h, i}-T_{h, o}\right)}=\frac{7.317 \times 10^{5} W }{2350 J / kg \cdot K \times(160-100)^{\circ} C }=5.19 kg / s\)

 

2. The required tube length may be obtained from Equations 11.14 and 11S.1, where

 

\(q=U A \Delta T_{ lm }\) (11.14)

 

\(\Delta T_{ lm }=F \Delta T_{ lm , CF }\) (11S.1)

 

\(q=U A F \Delta T_{ lm , CF }\)

 

From Equation 11.5,

 

\(\frac{1}{U A}=\frac{1}{U_{i} A_{i}}=\frac{1}{U_{o} A_{o}}\)

\(=\frac{1}{h_{i} A_{i}}+\frac{R_{f, i}^{\prime \prime}}{A_{i}}+\frac{\ln \left(D_{o} / D_{i}\right)}{2 \pi k L}+\frac{R_{f, o}^{\prime \prime}}{A_{o}}+\frac{1}{h_{o} A_{o}}\) (11.5)

 

\(U=\frac{1}{\left(1 / h_{i}\right)+\left(1 / h_{o}\right)}\)

 

where \(h_{i}\) may be obtained by first calculating \(R e_{D}\) With \(\dot{m}_{1} \equiv \dot{m}_{c} / N=0.25 kg / s\) defined as the water flow rate per tube, Equation 8.6 yields

 

\(R e_{D}=\frac{4 \dot{m}}{\pi D \mu}\) (8.6)

 

\(\operatorname{Re}_{D}=\frac{4 \dot{m}_{1}}{\pi D \mu}=\frac{4 \times 0.25 kg / s }{\pi(0.025 m ) 548 \times 10^{-6} kg / s \cdot m }=23,234\)

 

Hence the water flow is turbulent, and from Equation 8.60

 

\(N u_{D}=0.023 R e_{D}^{4 / 5} P r^{n}\) (8.60)

 

\(N u_{D}=0.023 R e_{D}^{4 / 5} P r^{0.4}=0.023(23,234)^{4 / 5}(3.56)^{0.4}=119\)

 

\(h_{i}=\frac{k}{D} N u_{D}=\frac{0.643 W / m \cdot K }{0.025 m } 119=3061 W / m ^{2} \cdot K\)

Hence

\(U=\frac{1}{(1 / 400)+(1 / 3061)}=354 W / m ^{2} \cdot K\)

 

The correction factor F may be obtained from Figure 11S.1, where

 

\(R=\frac{160-100}{85-15}=0.86 \quad P=\frac{85-15}{160-15}=0.48\)

 

Hence \(F \approx 0.87. \) From Equations 11.15 and 11.17, it follows that

 

\(\Delta T_{ lm }=\frac{\Delta T_{2}-\Delta T_{1}}{\ln \left(\Delta T_{2} / \Delta T_{1}\right)}=\frac{\Delta T_{1}-\Delta T_{2}}{\ln \left(\Delta T_{1} / \Delta T_{2}\right)}\) (11.15)

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} \Delta T_{1} \equiv T_{h, 1}-T_{c, 1}=T_{h, i}-T_{c, o} \\ \Delta T_{2} \equiv T_{h, 2}-T_{c, 2}=T_{h, o}-T_{c, i} \end{array}\right]\) (11.17)

 

\(\Delta T_{ lm , CF }=\frac{\left(T_{h, i}-T_{c, o}\right)-\left(T_{h, o}-T_{c, i}\right)}{\ln \left[\left(T_{h, i}-T_{c, o}\right) /\left(T_{h, o}-T_{c, i}\right)\right]}=\frac{75-85}{\ln (75 / 85)}=79.9^{\circ} C\)

 

Hence, since \(A=N \pi D L,\) where N = 10 is the number of tubes,

 

\(L=\frac{q}{U N \pi D F \Delta T_{ lm , CF }}=\frac{7.317 \times 10^{5} W }{354 W / m ^{2} \cdot K \times 10 \pi(0.025 m ) 0.87\left(79.9^{\circ} C \right)}\)

 

L = 37.9 m

 

Comments:
1. With (L/D) = 37.9 m/0.025 m = 1516, the assumption of fully developed conditions throughout the tube is justified.

2. With eight passes, the shell length is approximately L/M = 4.7 m.
