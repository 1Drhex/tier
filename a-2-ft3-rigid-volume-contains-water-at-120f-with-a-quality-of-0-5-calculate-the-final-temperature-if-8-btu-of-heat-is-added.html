Guest Question.

A 2-ft³ rigid volume contains water at 120°F with a quality of 0.5. Calculate the final temperature if 8 Btu of heat is added.

Admin
Solution

The first law for a process demands that Q − W = m Δu. To find the mass, we must use the specific volume as follows:

\( v_{1}=v_{f}+x\left(v_{g}-v_{f}\right)=0.016+(0.5)(203.0-0.016)=101.5 ft ^{3} / lbm \).

\( \therefore m=\frac{V}{v}=\frac{2}{101.5}=0.0197 lbm \).

For a rigid volume, the work is zero since the volume does not change. Hence, Q = m Δu. The value of the initial internal energy is

\( u_{1}=u_{f}+x u_{f g}=87.99+(0.5)(961.9)=568.9 Btu / lbm \).

The final internal energy is then calculated from the first law:

\( 8=0.0197\left(u_{2}-568.9\right) \quad \therefore u_{2}=975 Btu / lbm \).

This is less than \( u_{g} ; \text { consequently, state } 2 \text { is in the wet region with } v_{2}=101.5 ft ^{3} / lbm \). This requires a trial-and-error procedure to find state 2:

At T = 140°F:

\( 101.5=0.016+x_{2}(122.9-0.016) \quad \therefore x_{2}=0.826 \).

\( 975=108+948.2 x_{2} \quad \therefore x_{2}=0.914 \).

At T = 150°F:

\( v_{g}=96.99 \) ∴ slightly superheat

\( 975=118+941.3 x_{2} \quad \therefore x_{2}=0.912 \).

Obviously, state 2 lies between 140°F and 150°F. Since the quality is insensitive to the internal energy, we find \( T_{2} \) such that \( v_{g}=101.5 ft ^{3} / lbm \):

\( T_{2}=150-\left(\frac{101.5-96.99}{122.88-96.99}\right)(10)=148^{\circ} F \).

A temperature slightly less than this provides us with \( T_{2}=147^{\circ} F \).

\( \text { The IRC Fluid Property Calculator with } u_{2}=9.75 Btu / lbm \text { and } \rho_{2}=1 / v_{2}=1 / 101.5=0.00985 lbm / ft ^{3} \text { gives } T_{2}=144^{\circ} F \).
