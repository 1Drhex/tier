Frequency-Response Characteristics of a Lead Compensator
Recall from Chapter 5 [Eq. (5.70)] the transfer function of the lead compensation, which is equivalent to

\( D(s) = K \frac{Ts + 1}{αTs + 1}, \ \ \ \ \ α < 1. \) (6.8)

\( D(s) = K \frac{s + z}{s + p} \) (5.70)

1. Analytically determine its frequency-response characteristics and discuss what you would expect from the result.
2. Use MATLAB \( ^\circledR \) to plot \( D(jω) \) with \( K = 1, T = 1, \) and \( α = 0.1 \) for \( 0.1 ≤ ω ≤ 100, \) and verify the features predicted from the analysis in \( 1, \) above.

Admin
Solution

1. Analytical evaluation: Substituting \( s = jω \) into Eq. (6.8), we get

\( D(jω) = K \frac{Tjω + 1}{αTjω + 1}. \)

\( D(s) = K \frac{Ts + 1}{αTs + 1}, \ \ \ \ \ α < 1. \) (6.8)

From Eqs. (6.5) and (6.6) the amplitude is

\( M = |D|=|K| \frac{ \sqrt{1 + (ωT)^2}}{\sqrt{1 + (αωT)^2}}, \)

\( M = |G(jω_o)|=|G(s)|_{s=jω_o} =\sqrt{ \left\{Re[G(jω_o)]\right\}^ 2 + \left\{Im[G(jω_o)]\right\} ^2} , \) (6.5)

\( \phi = \tan^{−1} \left[ \frac{Im[G(jω_o)]}{Re[G(jω_o)]} \right] = ∠G(jω_o). \) (6.6)

and the phase is given by

\( \phi = ∠(1 + jωT) − ∠(1 + jαωT) \\ = \tan^{−1}(ωT) − \tan^{−1}(αωT). \)

At very low frequencies the amplitude is just \( |K|, \) and at very high frequencies it is \( |K/α|. \) Therefore, the amplitude is higher at very high frequency. The phase is zero at very low frequencies and goes back to zero at very high frequencies. At intermediate frequencies, evaluation of the \( \tan^{−1}(·) \) functions would reveal that \( \phi \) becomes positive. These are the general characteristics of lead compensation.
