Guest Question.

The pressure tank in Figure P14.10/11 is fabricated from spirally wrapped metal plates that are welded at the seams in the orientation shown where β = 40°. The tank has an inside diameter of 720 mm and a wall thickness of 8 mm. For a gage pressure of 2.15 MPa, determine (a) the normal stress perpendicular to the weld and (b) the shear stress parallel to the weld.

Admin
Solution

(a) Normal stress perpendicular to the weld

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{\text {long }}=\frac{p d}{4 t}=\frac{(2.15 MPa )(720 mm )}{4(8 mm )}=48.375 MPa \\&\sigma_{\text {hoop }}=\frac{p d}{2 t}=\frac{(2.15 MPa )(720 mm )}{2(8 mm )}=96.750 MPa\end{aligned}\)

The longitudinal axis of the cylinder is defined as the x axis and the circumferential direction is defined as the y axis; therefore, the normal and shear stresses on longitudinal and circumferential faces of a stress element are:

\(\sigma_{x}=48.375 MPa , \sigma_{y}=96.750 MPa , \tau_{x y}=0 MPa\)

The weld is oriented at 40° as shown; however, the angle θ required for the stress transformation equations is the angle normal to the weld. Thus, θ = 40° + 90° = 130° (or θ = 40° − 90° = −50°). Using this value of θ, the normal stress transformation equation [Eq. (12-3)] can be used to compute the normal stress perpendicular to the weld:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{n} &=\sigma_{x} \cos ^{2} \theta+\sigma_{y} \sin ^{2} \theta+2 \tau_{x y} \sin \theta \cos \theta \\&=(48.375 MPa ) \cos ^{2}\left(130^{\circ}\right)+(96.750 MPa ) \sin ^{2}\left(130^{\circ}\right)+2(0 MPa ) \sin \left(130^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(130^{\circ}\right) \\&=76.763 MPa =76.8 MPa ( T )\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Shear stress parallel to the weld
Similarly, the shear stress transformation equation [Eq. (12-4)] gives \(\tau_{n t}\):

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{n t} &=-\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) \sin \theta \cos \theta+\tau_{x y}\left(\cos ^{2} \theta-\sin ^{2} \theta\right) \\&=-[(48.375 MPa )-(96.750 MPa )] \sin \left(130^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(130^{\circ}\right)+(0 MPa )\left[\cos ^{2}\left(130^{\circ}\right)-\sin ^{2}\left(130^{\circ}\right)\right] \\&=-23.820 MPa =-23.8 MPa\end{aligned}\)
