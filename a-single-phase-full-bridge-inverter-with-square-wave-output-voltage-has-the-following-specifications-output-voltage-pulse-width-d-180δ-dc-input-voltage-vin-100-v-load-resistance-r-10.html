Guest Question.

A single-phase full-bridge inverter with square-wave output voltage has the following specifications:

Output voltage pulse width: δ = 180°;

dc input voltage: Vin = 100 V;

Load resistance: R = 10 Ω;

Load self-inductance: L = 25 mH;

Output frequency: f =50 Hz.

Calculate the following:

a) The amplitude of the load current harmonic components.
b) The active power consumed by the load.
c) The TH\(D _{ V _{ a }}\)% and TH\(D _{ i _{ a }}\)% factors

Admin
Solution

Solution

a) The amplitudes of the output voltage harmonics of a quasi square-wave inverter (i.e., pulses of 180° duration), are given by the following equation:

 

\(\widehat{ V }_{ o , n }=\frac{4 V _{ in }}{ n \pi}=\frac{4(100)}{ n \pi} \text { where } n =1,3,5,7, \ldots\)

 

Therefore, the amplitudes of the output current harmonic components are given by the
following relationship

 

\(\widehat{ I }_{o, n }=\frac{\widehat{ V }_{o, n }}{\left|z_{o, n }\right|}=\frac{\widehat{ V }_{o, n }}{\sqrt{ R ^{2}+( n \omega L )^{2}}}=\frac{\frac{4(100)}{ n \pi}}{\sqrt{10^{2}+[ n (2 \pi \times 50)(0.025)]^{2}}}\)

 

b) The active power absorbed by the load is given by the following equation:

 

\(\begin{aligned} P_{o} &=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} P_{n}=P_{1}+P_{2}+P_{3}+\ldots=\tilde{I}_{o, 1}^{2} R+\tilde{I}_{o, 3}^{2} R+\tilde{I}_{o, 5}^{2} R+\ldots \\ &=R\left(\frac{\widehat{I}_{o, 1}}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^{2} R+\left(\frac{\widehat{I}_{o, 3}}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^{2} R+\ldots=R\left(\tilde{I}_{o, 1}^{2}+\tilde{I}_{o, 3}^{2}+\tilde{I}_{o, 5}^{2}+\ldots\right) \end{aligned}\)

 

where

 

\(\begin{gathered} \widehat{ I }_{o, 1}=\frac{\widehat{ V }_{ o , 1}}{\left|z_{0,1}\right|}=\frac{\frac{4(100)}{\pi}}{\sqrt{10^{2}+[(2 \pi \times 50)(0.025)]^{2}}}=10 A \\ \widehat{ I }_{0,3}=\frac{\widehat{ V }_{ o , 3}}{\left|z_{ o , 3}\right|}=\frac{\frac{4(100)}{3 \pi}}{\sqrt{10^{2}+[3(2 \pi \times 50)(0.025)]^{2}}}=1.66 A \\ \widehat{ I }_{0,5}=\frac{\widehat{ V }_{ o , 5}}{\left|z_{ o , 5}\right|}=\frac{\frac{4(100)}{5 \pi}}{\sqrt{10^{2}+[5(2 \pi \times 50)(0.025)]^{2}}}=0.63 A \end{gathered}\)

 

As can be verified from the above output voltage and current harmonic components, when the order of the harmonic component increases the amplitude of the respective component is considerably reduced. Therefore, the output active power is given by:

 

\(\begin{aligned} P _{ o } \approx \tilde{ I }_{ o , 1}^{2} R +\tilde{ I }_{ o , 3}^{2} R +\tilde{ I }_{ o , 5}^{2} R &=\left(\frac{10}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^{2} 10+\left(\frac{1.66}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^{2} 10+\left(\frac{0.63}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^{2} 10 \\ &=490+13.7+2.03=505.73 W \end{aligned}\)

 

c)

\(THD _{ V _{0}} \%=\frac{\sqrt{\widehat{ v }_{ o , 3}^{2}+\widehat{ v }_{ o , 5}^{2}+\widehat{ v }_{ o , 7}^{2}+\ldots}}{\widehat{ v }_{ o , 1}} \times 100\)

 

\(\begin{aligned} =& \frac{\widehat{ V }_{ o , \text { ripple }}}{\widehat{ V }_{ o , 1}} \times 100 \approx \frac{\sqrt{\left(\frac{4(100)}{3 \pi}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{4(100)}{5 \pi}\right)^{2}}}{\frac{4(100)}{\pi}} \times 100 \\ =& \frac{\sqrt{1803.7+649.23}}{127.4} \times 100=38.87 \% \end{aligned}\)

 

\(THD _{ i _{ o }} \%=\frac{\sqrt{\widehat{ I }_{ o , 3}+\widehat{ I }_{0,5}^{2}}}{\widehat{ I }_{ o , 1}} \times 100=\frac{\widehat{ I }_{ o , \text { ripple }}}{\widehat{ I }_{ o , 1}} \times 100 \approx \frac{\sqrt{1.66^{2}+0.63^{2}}}{10} \times 100=16.6 \%\)
