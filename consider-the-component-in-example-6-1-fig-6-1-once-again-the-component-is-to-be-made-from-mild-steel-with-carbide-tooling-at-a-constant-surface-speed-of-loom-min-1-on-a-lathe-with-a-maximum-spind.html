Guest Question.

Consider the component in (Fig. 6.1) once again. The component is to be made from mild steel with carbide tooling at a constant surface speed of \(100 m min^{-1}\) on a lathe with a maximum spindle speed of \(1500 rev min^{-1}\). The machining allowance is \(2 mm\). Determine:

(i) if the lathe is capable of turning the component at the required surface speed;
(ii) the total machining time for the component if the lathe is capable.

Admin
Solution

(i) 1. Facing\(Ø 50 mm\):

 

\(D=? D=\frac{d_1+d_2}{2}\)

 

\(d_1=50 mm d_2=0 mm D=\frac{50+0}{2}=25 mm\)

 

\(V_c=100 m min^{-1} N=\frac{1000V_c}{\pi D}\)

 

\(D=25 mm N=? N=\frac{1000\times 100}{\pi \times 25}=1273.24 rev min^{-1}\)

2. Tuming \(Ø50 mm\):

 

\(V_c=100 m min^{-1} N=\frac{1000V_c}{\pi D}\)

 

\(D=50 mm N=? N=\frac{1000\times 100}{\pi \times 50}=636.62 rev min^{-1}\)

3. Turning taper:

\(D=? D=\frac{d_1+d_2}{2}\)

 

\(d_1=50 mm d_2=75 mm D=\frac{50+75}{2}=62.5 mm\)

 

\(V_c=100 m min^{-1} N=\frac{1000V_c}{\pi D}\)

 

\(D=62.5 mm N=? N=\frac{1000\times 100}{\pi \times 62.5}=509.3 rev min^{-1}\)

4. Turning \(Ø75 mm\):

\(V_c=100 m min^{-1} N=\frac{1000V_c}{\pi D}\)

 

\(D=75 mm N=? N=\frac{1000\times 100}{\pi \times 75}=424.41 rev min^{-1}\)

5. Parting off:

\(D=? D=\frac{d_1+d_2}{2}\)

 

\(d_1=75 mm d_2=0 mm D=\frac{75+0}{2}=25 mm\)

 

\(V_c=100 m min^{-1} N=\frac{1000V_c}{\pi D}\)

 

\(D=37.5 mm N=? N=\frac{1000\times 100}{\pi \times 37.5}=848.83 rev min^{-1}\)

According to the above spindle speed calculations, the lathe is capable of machining the component at the required surface speed.

(ii) From Table 6.3, the feed rate fr to be used is \(0.8 mm rev^{-1}\).
1. Facing \(Ø50 mm\):

 

\(T=? T=\frac{(D/2)+A}{f_rN}\)

 

\(D=50 mm^* A=2 mm T=\frac{(50/2)+2}{0.80\times 1273.24}=0.027 min\)

 

\(f_r=0.80 mm rev^{-1} N=1273.24 rev min^{-1}\)

2. Turn \(Ø50 mm\):

\(T=? T+\frac{L+A}{f_rN}\)

* The actual diameter is used as the distance travelled is half of the diameter, that
is, \(D/2\) as stated in the equation.

\(L=75 mm A=2 mm T=\frac{75+2}{0.80\times 636.62}=0.151 min\)

 

\(f_r=0.80 mm rev^{-1} N=636.62 rpm\)

Turn taper:
We must find the distance L travelled using Pythagoras’ theorem

\(L=\sqrt{25^2+12.5^2}=27.95 mm T=? T=\frac{L+A}{f_rN}\)

 

\(L=27.95 mm A=2 mm T=\frac{27.95+2}{0.80\times 509.83}=0.073 min\)
\(f_r=0.80 mm rev^{-1} N=509.3 rpm^*\)

4. Tum \(Ø50 mm\):

 

\(T=? T=\frac{L+A}{f_rN}\)

 

\(L=150 mm A=2 mm T=\frac{150+2}{0.80\times 424.21}=0.448 min\)
\(f_r=0.80 mm rev^{-1} N=424.21 rpm^*\)

5. Parting off:

\(T=? T=\frac{(D/2)+A}{f_rN}\)

 

\(D=75 mm A=2 mm T=\frac{(75/2)+2}{0.80\times 848.83}=0.058 min\)
\(f_r=0.80 mm rev^{-1} N=848.83\)

Total machining time \(= 0.027+0.151+0.073+0.448+0.058=0.757 min\)

The reason for such a short machining time is due to the speeds used for such a small component.

* This is the average spindle speed according to the calculations in the first part of the problem9 Obviously, the spindle speed will vary across the taper to maintain the constant surface speed.
♦ The actual diameter is used as the distance travelled is half of the diameter, that is, \(D/2\) as stated in the equation.
