Guest Question.

Lateral Bracing for a Column

A 32-ft.-long W8 × 31 column requires a lateral brace at midheight of the weak axis. Determine whether an L3 × 3 × 1⁄4 nodal brace provides adequate lateral bracing if the factored axial load in the column is \( P_u\) = 200 kips. kips. The L3 × 3 × 1⁄4 is ASTM A36 and has a length of 6 ft. from the column web to an isolated support.

Admin
Solution

From equation 14-7, the required strength of the brace is

\(P_{br} = 0.01P_u \) (14-7)

\(P_{br} = 0.01P_u = (0.01)(200) = 2\) kips

From AISCM, Table 4-11, the design strength of the L3× 3 ×1⁄4 at an unbraced length of 6 ft. is \(\phi P_n =20.9 kips \gt 2\) kips, OK.

From equation 14-8, the required stiffness of the brace is

\(\beta _{br}=\frac{8P_u}{\phi L_b}\) (14-8)

\(\beta _{br}=\frac{8P_u}{\phi L_b} =\frac{(8)(200)}{(0.75)(16)}=134 kips/ft. = 11.1\) kips/in.,

where\( L_b \)= 32 ft./2 = 16 ft. (midspan brace).

From AISCM, Table 1-7 for an L3 × 3 × 1⁄4,

\(A =1.44 in.^2\).

The stiffness of the brace is

\(\beta _{br}=\frac{EA}{ L} =\frac{(29,000)(1.44)}{(6)(12)}=580 kips/ft. \gt 11.1\) kips/in.

The L3 × 3 × 1⁄4 provides adequate strength and stiffness as lateral bracing.
