Guest Question.

The simply supported beam shown in Figure P15.25a/26a supports a uniformly distributed load of w = 300 lb/ft between supports A and B and a concentrated load of P = 750 lb at end C. The crosssectional dimensions of the beam shown in Figure P15.25b/26b are \(b_{f}\) = 10 in., \(t_{f}\) = 2 in., d = 12 in., and \(t_{w}\) = 2 in. Using L = 12 ft and \(x_{H}\) = 3 ft, determine the principal stresses and the maximum shear stress acting at point H, which is located at a distance of a = 5 in. above the bottom edge of the tee stem. Show these stresses on an appropriate sketch.

Admin
Solution

Centroid location in y direction: (reference axis at bottom of tee shape)

\(\begin{array}{lccccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Width } b \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Height } h \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left( in .^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} \text { (from bottom) } \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} A_{i} \\\left( in .^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 10 & 2 & 20.000 & 11.000 & 220.000 \\\text { stem } & 2 & 10 & 20.000 & 5.000 & 100.000 \\\hline & & & 40.000 \text { in. }^{2} & & 320.000 in .{ }^{3}\end{array}\)

 

\(\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{320.00 in. ^{3}}{40.00 in .^{2}}=8.00 in.\) (measured upward from bottom edge of stem)

 

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

\(\begin{array}{lcccc}\text { Shape } & I_{C} & d=y_{i}-\bar{y} & d^{2} A & I_{C}+d^{2} A \\& \left( in .^{4}\right) & \text { (in.) } & \left( in. ^{4}\right) & \left( in .^{4}\right) \\\hline \text { top flange } & 6.667 & 3.000 & 180.000 & 186.667 \\\text { stem } & 166.667 & -3.000 & 180.000 & 346.667 \\\hline \text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)= 533.333\end{array}\)

 

Shear-force and bending-moment diagram:

Shear force at H:
V = 525 lb
Bending moment at H:
M = 2,925 lb-ft

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Bending stress at point H: (y = −3.0 in.)

\(\sigma_{x}=-\frac{M y}{I_{z}}=-\frac{(2,925 lb – ft )(-3.00 in. )(12 in. / ft )}{533.333 in .^{4}}=197.438 psi\)

Transverse and horizontal shear stress at point H:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q=(2 \text { in. })(5.0 \text { in. })(5.5 \text { in. })=55.000 in .^{3} \\&\tau=\frac{V Q}{I t}=\frac{(525 lb )\left(55.000 in .^{3}\right)}{\left(533.333 in. ^{4}\right)(2 in .)}=27.070 psi \text{(Sense to be determined by inspection)}\end{aligned}\)

Stress element at H:

Principal stress calculations:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq. (12.12):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}}{2}\right)^{2}+\tau_{x y}^{2}} \\&=\frac{(197.438)+(0)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(197.438)-(0)}{2}\right)^{2}+(-27.070)^{2}} \\&=98.719 \pm 102.363\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{p 1}=201 psi \text{and} \sigma_{p 2}=-3.64 psi\\&\tau_{\max }=102.4 psi \text{(maximum in-plane shear stress)}\\&\sigma_{\text {avg }}=98.7 psi ( T ) \text{ (normal stress on planes of maximum in-plane shear str s es )}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan 2 \theta_{p} &=\frac{\tau_{x y}}{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) / 2}=\frac{-27.070}{[(197.438)-(0)] / 2}=\frac{-27.070}{98.719}=-0.2742 \\&\left.\therefore \theta_{p}=-7.67^{\circ} \quad \text { (clockwise from the } x \text { axis to the direction of } \sigma_{p 1}\right)\end{aligned}\)
