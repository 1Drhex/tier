Guest Question.

A very long electrical conductor lOmm diameter is concentric with a cooled cylindrical tube 50mm in diameter whose surface is gray and diffuse with an emissivity of 0.9 and a temperature of 300K. The electrical conductor has a diffuse gray surface with an emissivity of 0.6 and dissipates 7W 1m length. Assuming that the space between the conductor and the tube is evacuated, determine the surface temperature of the conductor.

Admin
Solution

We can say that this is an inverse problem where we know the heat flux but don’t know the temperature.We need to find the temperature to see if the wire can withstand it or not. Inverse problems are more practical from an engineer’s perspective than a direct problem. Many a time, we have to infer as we can not straightaway solve this without first writing the radiosity relations and getting the formula. It is better that we keep it as surface 1 and surface 2 with areas \(A_{1} \text { and } A_{2}\) , start with view factors and get expressions for \(J_{1} \text { and } J_{2}\). Next we get an expression for \(Q_{1}\) and then substitute the values to get the answer. We call these areas \(A_{1} \text { and } A_{2}\) so that in the exam, if a problem of sphere within a sphere is given, the area formulae alone need be substituted in that case.

\(F_{11}=0, F_{12}=1\) (4.264)

\(A_{1} F_{12}=A_{2} F_{21}\) (4.265)

\(F_{21}=A_{1} / A_{2}\) (4.266)

\(F_{22}=1-A_{1} / A_{2}\) (4.267)

\(\text { View factor matrix }=\left[\begin{array}{cc}0 & 1 \\A_{1} / A_{2} & 1-A_{1} / A_{2}\end{array}\right]\)

Radiosity relations for the 2 surface enclosure

\(J_{1}=\varepsilon_{1} \sigma T_{1}^{4}+\left(1-\varepsilon_{1}\right) J_{2}\) (4.268)

\(J_{2}=\varepsilon_{2} \sigma T_{2}^{4}+\left(1-\varepsilon_{2}\right)\left[\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} J_{1}+\left(1-\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\right) J_{2}\right]\) (4.269)

Substituting for \(J_{1}\) in Eq. (4.269)

\(J_{2}=\varepsilon_{2} \sigma T_{2}^{4}+\left(1-\varepsilon_{2}\right) \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} J_{1}+\left(1-\varepsilon_{2}\right)\left(1-\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\right) J_{2}\) (4.270)

\(J_{2}=\varepsilon_{2} \sigma T_{2}^{4}+\left(1-\varepsilon_{2}\right) \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\left[\varepsilon_{1} \sigma T_{1}^{4}+\left(1-\varepsilon_{1}\right) J_{2}\right]+\left(1-\varepsilon_{2}\right)\left(1-\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\right) J_{2}\) (4.271)

\(\begin{array}{c}J_{2}\left[1-\left(\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\right)\left(1-\varepsilon_{1}\right)\left(1-\varepsilon_{2}\right)-\left(1-\varepsilon_{2}\right)\left(1-\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\right)\right] \\=\varepsilon_{2} \sigma T_{2}^{4}+\left(1-\varepsilon_{2}\right)\left(\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\right) \varepsilon_{1} \sigma T_{1}^{4}\end{array}\) (4.272)

\(J_{2}=\frac{\left[\varepsilon_{2} \sigma T_{2}^{4}+\left(\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\right) \varepsilon_{1} \sigma T_{1}^{4}-\varepsilon_{1} \varepsilon_{2}\left(\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\right) \sigma T_{1}^{4}\right]}{\left[\varepsilon_{2}+\varepsilon_{1}\left(\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\right)-\varepsilon_{1} \varepsilon_{2}\left(\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\right)\right]}\) (4.273)

Substituting in Eq. (4.268), we can get \(J_{1}\) or

\(q_{12}=J_{1}-J_{2}\) (4.274)

\(q_{12}=\varepsilon_{1} \sigma T_{1}^{4}+\left(1-\varepsilon_{1}\right) J_{2}-J_{2}\) (4.275)

\(q_{12}=\varepsilon _{1}\sigma T_{1}^{4} +J_{2}-\varepsilon _{1}J_{2}-J_{2}\) (4.276)

\(q_{12}=\varepsilon_{1} \sigma T_{1}^{4}-\varepsilon_{1} J_{2}\) (4.277)

\(q_{12}=\varepsilon_{1} \sigma T_{1}^{4}-\varepsilon_{1}\left[\frac{\left[\varepsilon_{2} \sigma T_{2}^{4}+\left(\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\right) \varepsilon_{1} \sigma T_{1}^{4}-\varepsilon_{1} \varepsilon_{2}\left(\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\right) \sigma T_{1}^{4}\right]}{\left[\varepsilon_{2}+\varepsilon_{1}\left(\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\right)-\varepsilon_{1} \varepsilon_{2}\left(\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\right)\right]}\right]\) (4.278)

\(q_{12}= \frac{\varepsilon_{1} \varepsilon_{2} \sigma\left[T_{1}^{4}-T_{2}^{4}\right]}{\varepsilon _{2}+\varepsilon _{1}\left ( \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \right )-\varepsilon _{1}\varepsilon _{2}\left ( \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \right )}\) (4.279)

\(q_{12}= \frac{\sigma\left[T_{1}^{4}-T_{2}^{4}\right]}{[\frac{1}{\varepsilon _{1}}+\frac{1}{\varepsilon _{2}}\left ( \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \right )-\left ( \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \right ) ]}\) (4.280)

\(Q_{12}=q_{12} 2 \pi r_{1} L\)

A good check for this formula is that if \(A_{1} / A_{2}=1\), Eqn.4.280 will reduce to the parallel plate formula.

So, this formula displays asymptotic correctness. Substituting for \(\varepsilon_{1}, \varepsilon_{2}, A_{1}, A_{2} \text { and } T_{2}\)

\(\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}=\frac{2 \pi r_{1} L}{2 \pi r_{2} L}=\frac{r_{1}}{r_{2}}=0.2\) (4.281)

\(\frac{7}{2 \pi r_{1}(1)}=\frac{6.7 \times 10^{-8}\left[T_{1}^{4}-300^{4}\right]}{\frac{1}{0.6}+\frac{1}{0.9}(0.2)-0.2}\) (4.282)

\(T_{1}=348.4 K\)

The wire can withstand this temperature, roughly \(75^{\circ} C .\). Whenever the view factors involve the fundamental dimensions such as \(r_{1} \text { and } r_{2}\) in this case, the analysis becomes complicated but towards the end, we get a simplified answer.
