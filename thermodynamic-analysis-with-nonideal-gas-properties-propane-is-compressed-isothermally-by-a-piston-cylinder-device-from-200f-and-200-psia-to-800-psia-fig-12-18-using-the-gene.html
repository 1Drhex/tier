Guest Question.

Thermodynamic Analysis with Nonideal Gas Properties

Propane is compressed isothermally by a piston–cylinder device from 200°F and 200 psia to 800 psia (Fig. 12–18). Using the generalized charts, determine the work done and the heat transfer per unit mass of propane.

Admin
Solution

Propane is compressed isothermally by a piston–cylinder device. The work done and the heat transfer are to be determined using the generalized charts.

Assumptions 1 The compression process is quasi equilibrium. 2 Kinetic and potential energy changes are negligible.

Analysis The critical temperature and pressure of propane are \(T_{cr} = 665.9 R\) and \(P_{cr} = 617 psia\) , respectively. Propane remains close to its critical temperature and is compressed to a pressure above its critical value. Therefore, propane is expected to deviate from the ideal-gas behavior, and thus it should be treated as a real gas.

The enthalpy departure and the compressibility factors of propane at the initial and the final states are determined from the generalized charts to be (Figs. A–29 and A–15)

\(\left.\begin{array}{l}T_{R_{1}}=\frac{T_{1}}{T_{\mathrm{cr}}}=\frac{660 \mathrm{R}}{665.9 \mathrm{R}}=0.991 \\\\P_{R_{1}}=\frac{P_{1}}{P_{\mathrm{cr}}}=\frac{200 \mathrm{psia}}{617 \mathrm{psia}}=0.324\end{array}\right\} Z_{h_{1}}=0.37 \text { and } Z_{1}=0.88\)

and

\(\left.\begin{array}{l}T_{R_{2}}=\frac{T_{2}}{T_{\mathrm{cr}}}=\frac{660 \mathrm{R}}{665.9 \mathrm{R}}=0.991 \\\\P_{R_{2}}=\frac{P_{2}}{P_{\mathrm{cr}}}=\frac{800 \mathrm{psia}}{617 \mathrm{psia}}=1.297\end{array}\right\} Z_{h_{2}}=4.2 \text { and } Z_{2}=0.22\)

As an approximation, treating propane as a real gas with \(Z_{\text {avg }}=\left(Z_{1}+Z_{2}\right) / 2=(0.88+\) \(0.22) / 2=0.55\) gives

\(P v=Z R T \cong Z_{\text {avg }} R T=C=\text { constant }\)

Then the boundary work becomes

\(\begin{aligned}w_{b, \text { in }} &=-\int_{1}^{2} P d v=-\int_{1}^{2} \frac{C}{v} d v=-C \ln \frac{v_{2}}{v_{1}}=-Z_{\text {avg }} R T \ln \frac{Z_{2} R T / P_{2}}{Z_{1} R T / P_{1}} \\&=-Z_{\text {avg }} R T \ln \frac{Z_{2} P_{1}}{Z_{1} P_{2}} \\&=-(0.55)(0.04504 \mathrm{Btu} / \mathrm{lbm} \cdot \mathrm{R})(660 \mathrm{R}) \ln \frac{(0.22)(200 \mathrm{psia})}{(0.88)(800 \mathrm{psia})}\\&=\mathbf{4 5 . 3} \mathbf{~ B t u} / \mathbf{l b m}\end{aligned}\)

Also,

\(\begin{aligned}h_{2}-h_{1}=& R T_{\mathrm{cr}}\left(Z_{h_{1}}-Z_{h_{2}}\right)+\left(h_{2}-h_{1}\right)_{\mathrm{ideal}}^{\nearrow 0} \\=&(0.04504 \mathrm{Btu} / \mathrm{lbm} \cdot \mathrm{R})(665.9 \mathrm{R})(0.37-4.2)+0 \\=&-114.9 \mathrm{Btu} / \mathrm{lbm} \\u_{2}-u_{1}=&\left(h_{2}-h_{1}\right)-R\left(Z_{2} T_{2}-Z_{1} T_{1}\right) \\=&(-114.9 \mathrm{Btu} / \mathrm{lbm})-(0.04504 \mathrm{Btu} / \mathrm{lbm} \cdot \mathrm{R}) \\& \times[(0.22)(660 \mathrm{R})-(0.88)(660 \mathrm{R})] \\=&-95.3 \mathrm{Btu} / \mathrm{lbm}\end{aligned}\)

Then the heat transfer during this process is determined from the closed-system energy balance equation for the piston-cylinder device to be

\(\begin{aligned}E_{\text {in }}-E_{\text {out }} &=\Delta E_{\text {system }} \\q_{\text {in }}+w_{b, \text { in }} &=\Delta u=u_{2}-u_{1} \\q_{\text {in }} &=\left(u_{2}-u_{1}\right)-w_{b, \text { in }}=-95.3-45.3=-140.6 \mathrm{Btu} / \mathrm{lbm}\end{aligned}\)

The negative sign indicates heat rejection. Therefore, heat transfer out of the system during this process is

\(q_{\text {out }}=140.6 \mathrm{Btu} / \mathrm{lbm}\)

Discussion Note that if the ideal-gas assumption were used for propane, the magnitudes of boundary work and heat transfer would have been the same (41.2 Btu/lbm). Therefore, the ideal-gas approximation would underestimate boundary work by 9 percent and the heat transfer by 71 percent.
