Guest Question.

Let \(M_{22}\) be the vector space of size 2 by 2 matrices. Consider the matrices:

\(\mathbf{A}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}1 & 0 \\0 & 1\end{array}\right) \text { and } \mathrm{B}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}0 & 2 \\2 & 0\end{array}\right)\)

Show that the matrices \(\mathrm{A}\) and \(\mathrm{B}\) are linearly independent.

Admin
Solution

How do we show that matrices \(\boldsymbol{A}\) and \(\mathbf{B}\) are linearly independent?

Required to show that \(k \mathbf{A}+c \mathbf{B}=\mathbf{O}\) is true only when the scalars \(k=c=0\)

Let \(k\) and \(c\) be scalars, then by applying the above definition \((3.10)\) to these matrices with the zero matrix \(\mathbf{O}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0\end{array}\right)\) we have

\(\begin{aligned}k \mathbf{A}+c \mathbf{B} &=k\left(\begin{array}{ll}1 & 0 \\0 & 1\end{array}\right)+c\left(\begin{array}{cc}0 & 2 \\2 & 0\end{array}\right) \\&=\left(\begin{array}{ll}k & 0 \\0 & k\end{array}\right)+\left(\begin{array}{cc}0 & 2 c \\2 c & 0\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{cc}k & 2 c \\2 c & k\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{ll}0 & 0 \\0 & 0\end{array}\right)=\mathbf{O}\end{aligned}\)

Equating entries gives \(k=0\) and \(2 c=0\). Since both (all) our scalars, \(k=0\) and \(c=0\), are zero, then by definition (3.10) we conclude that the given matrices \(\mathbf{A}\) and \(\mathrm{B}\) are linearly independent.

Definition (3.10) :. We say vectors \(\mathbf{v}_{1}, \mathbf{v}_{2}, \mathbf{v}_{3}, \ldots\) and \(\mathbf{v}_{n}\) in \(V\) are linearly independent \(\Leftrightarrow\) the only real scalars \(k_{1}, k_{2}, k_{3}, \ldots\) and \(k_{n}\) which satisfy:

\(k_{1} \mathbf{v}_{1}+k_{2} \mathbf{v}_{2}+k_{3} \mathbf{v}_{3}+\cdots+k_{n} \mathbf{v}_{n}=\mathbf{O} \quad \text { are } \quad k_{1}=k_{2}=k_{3}=\cdots=k_{n}=0\)
