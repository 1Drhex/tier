Guest Question.

A single ~achine is connected to a load centre through a transmission line as shown in Fig. 1. The load centre is represented by a reactance connected to an infinite bus. The generator is initially operating with \(P_{e}=1.0 \) pu. and the magnitude of voltages \(V_{1} \) and \(V_{2} \) are 1.0 p.u. each. Find the maximum step increase in the mechanical power that will not cause transient instability. Use equal area criterion. Assume \(x_{g}=0.3, x_{t}=0.1, x=0.4, x_{2}=0.1 \).

Admin
Solution

At the initial operating point,

\(P_{e}=\frac{V_{1}V_{2}}{\left ( x_{t}+x \right )}\sin \left ( \theta _{1}-\theta _{2} \right )\)

where \(\theta _{1}\) and \(\theta _{2}\) are angles of the voltages \(\hat{V_{1}}\) and \(\hat{V_{2}}\). Substituting values of

\(P_{e}=1.0,V_{1}=V_{2}=1.0,x_{t}+x=0.5\), we get

 

\(\theta _{1}-\theta _{2}=30^{\circ}\)

The current \(\hat{I}\) in the line, referred to \(\hat{V_{2}}\) is

\(\hat{I}e^{-j\theta _{2}}=\frac{1.0\angle 30-1.0\angle 0}{j0.5}=1.0+j0.27\)

 

\(E_{b}e^{-j\theta _{2}}=V_{2}jx_{2}\hat{I}e^{-j\theta _{2}}=1.0j0.1\left ( 1.0+j0.27 \right )=1.032\angle-5.56^{\circ}\)

Hence,\(\theta _{2}=5.56^{\circ}\) and \(\theta _{1}=35.56^{\circ}\)

\(E_{g}\angle \delta =\hat{V_{1}}+jx_{g}\hat{I}=1.0\angle 35.56+j0.3\left ( 1.0+j0.27 \right )e^{j5.56}\)
\(=1.121\angle 51.1^{\circ}\)

The power \(P_{e}\) is given by the expression

\(P_{e}=P_{max}\sin \delta\) where \(P_{max}=1.121 . 1.032/0.9 = 1.285\)

The initial value of \(P_{m}=P_{mo}=1.0\). If \(P_{m}\) is increased from \(P_{mo}\) to \(P_{m1}\), the stable equilibrium value of \(\delta\) changes from \(\delta _{0}\left ( 51.1^{\circ} \right )\) to \(\delta _{1}\) (see Fig. 2.).

When there is a step increase in \(P_{m}\) the machine accelerates and overshoots the equilibrium angle \(\delta _{1}\). Let the maximum angle reached be \(\delta _{max}\). For critically stable case, \(\delta _{max}=\pi -\delta _{1}\) (see Fig. 2.). From equal area criterion, the ares \(A_{1}\) and \(A_{2}\) are equal

\(A_{1}=\int_{\delta _{0}}^{\delta _{1}}\left ( P_{m1}-P_{max}\sin \delta \right )d\delta\)

 

\(=-P_{max}\left [ \cos \delta _{0}-\cos \delta _{1} \right ]+P_{m1}\left ( \delta _{1}-\delta _{0} \right )\)

 

\(A_{2}=\int_{\delta _{1}}^{\pi -\delta _{1}}\left ( P_{max}\sin \delta -P_{m1}\ \right )d\delta\)

 

\(=2P_{max}\cos\delta _{1}-P_{m1}\left ( \pi -2\delta _{1} \right )\)

Equating \(A_{1}\) and \(A_{2}\), we get
\(P_{m1}\left ( \pi -\delta _{1}-\delta _{0} \right )=P_{max}\left [ \cos \delta _{1}+\cos \delta _{0} \right ]\)

Since,

\(P_{m1}=P_{max}\sin \delta _{1},\)

 

\(\sin \delta _{1}\left ( \pi -\delta _{1}-\delta _{0} \right )=\left ( \cos \delta _{1}+\cos \delta _{0} \right )\)

The solution of this nonlinear equation is

\(\delta _{1}=71^{\circ}, P_{m1}=1.215\)

The maximum step increase = 0.215 p.u.
