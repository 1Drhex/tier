Guest Question.

Consider the flow depicted in Fig. 4.18a with for \(M_{1} = 4.0\) with \(T_{1} = 216.7 K\) and \(p_{1} = 19.1 kPa\) and \(δ_{1} = 10°\). Find the air speed, loss in stagnation pressure, shock strength of the reflection, and the reflected shock angle.

Admin
Solution

From Fig. 4.12, for \(M_{1} = 4.0\) with \(T_{1} = 216.7 K\) and \(p_{1} = 19.1 kPa\) and \(δ_{1} = 10°\), \(θ_{i} ≈ 23°\) . Therefore, \(M_{1n} = M_{1} \sin23° ≈ 1.56\). Therefore, using shock table or Eqn. (4.18) \(\frac{p_{2} }{p_{1} } =\frac{\left(\frac{\gamma +1}{\gamma -1} \right)\left(\frac{\rho _{2} }{\rho _{1} }\right) -1 }{\left(\frac{\gamma +1}{\gamma -1} \right)-\left(\frac{\rho _{2} }{\rho _{1} }\right) } \) yields

\(\frac{p_{2} }{p_{1} }=2.673\) and \(\beta =\frac{p_{2}- p_{1}}{p_{1} }=1.673\)

\(\frac{p_{t2} }{p_{t1} }=0.910\) or \(\frac{p_{t2}- p_{t1}}{p_{t1} }=9\)%

\(\frac{T_{2} }{T_{1} }=1.361\)

 

\(M_{2n}=0.681\)

 

\(M_{2t}= M_{1t}\sqrt{\frac{T_{1} }{T_{2} }} =M_{1} \cos \theta _{1} \sqrt{\frac{T_{1} }{T_{2} }}=4\cos 23° \sqrt{\frac{1}{1.361} } =3.156\)

Therefore \(M_{2}=\sqrt{M^{2}_{2t} +M^{2}_{2n}} =3.229\)

So far the solution is the same as that shown in part (1) of .

For the flow to be parallel to the lower wall, it must be turned through an angle of \(δ_{2} = 10°\). By again using Fig 4.12 for \(M_{2} = 3.229\) and \(δ_{2} = 10°\), \(θ_{r} ≈ 25.5°\).

Therefore, \(M_{2n} = M_{2} \sin 25.5° ≈ 1.39\). Using the shock table or Eqn. (4.18) \(\frac{p_{2} }{p_{1} } =\frac{\left(\frac{\gamma +1}{\gamma -1} \right)\left(\frac{\rho _{2} }{\rho _{1} }\right) -1 }{\left(\frac{\gamma +1}{\gamma -1} \right)-\left(\frac{\rho _{2} }{\rho _{1} }\right) } \), one finds

\(\frac{p_{3} }{p_{2} }≈2.088 \) and \(\beta =\frac{p_{3}- p_{2}}{p_{2} }≈1.088\)

\(\frac{p_{t3} }{p_{t2} }≈0.961\) or \(\frac{p_{t3}- p_{t2}}{p_{t2} }≈3.9\)%

\(\frac{T_{3} }{T_{2} }≈1.248\)

 

\(M_{3n}≈0.681\)

 

\(M_{3t} =M_{2t} \sqrt{\frac{T_{2} }{T_{3} }} =M_{2} \cos \theta _{r} \sqrt{\frac{T_{2} }{T_{3} }}=3.229 \times \cos 25.5° \sqrt{\frac{1}{1.248} }≈ 2.609\)

Therefore \(M_{3}=\sqrt{M^{2}_{3t} +M^{2}_{3n}} ≈ 2.696 \)

\(\frac{p_{t3} }{p_{t1} }=\frac{p_{t3} }{p_{t2} } \times \frac{p_{t2} }{p_{t1} }≈ 0.961 \times 0.910=0.875\)

The stagnation pressure loss due to the incident and the reflected shocks is 12.5%, which translates into

\(\Delta p_{t}=p_{t3}- p_{t1}=p_{t1}\times \left(\frac{p_{t3}}{p_{t1}} -1\right) =p_{1}\times \frac{p_{t1}}{p_{1}} \times \left(\frac{p_{t3}}{p_{t1}} -1\right) =19.1\times \frac{1}{0.0066}\times \left(0.875-1\right) -361.7 kPa\)

 

\(\Delta p_{t}=-361.7 kPa\)

 

\(T_{3} =\frac{T_{3} } {T_{2} }\times \frac{T_{2} }{T_{1} }\times T_{1}=1.248\times 1.361\times 216.7 K=368.1 K\)

The speed in Region 3 is \(V_{3}=M_{3} \sqrt{\gamma RT_{3}} =2.696\times \sqrt{1.4\times 287\times 368.1} =1,036.8 m/s\)

The overall shock strength due to the reflection is \(p_{3}/p_{1} = (p_{3}/p_{2}) (p_{2}/p_{1} )=2.088 \times 2.673 = 5.581\)

The reflected shock angle is \(θ_{r} – δ_{2} ≈ 15.5°\).

Comments –

The reflection is not specular, i.e., \(\left(\theta _{r}-\delta _{2}\right) \neq \theta _{i}\); here \(\theta _{i}\gt \left(\theta _{r}-\delta _{2}\right)\). This is mainly because \(M_{2} ≠ M_{1}\) and that the wave angle depends on the Mach number and the deflection angle.
