Guest Question.

Determine the torque, T2, that must be applied to input link 2 of the parallelogram four-bar linkage in the posture shown in Fig. 12.23. The angular velocity and angular acceleration of link 2 are ω2 = 10 rad/s ccw and α2 = 10 rad/s2 cw, respectively. The masses of the links are m2 = 2.5 kg, m3 = 5 kg, and m4 = 2.5 kg, and the mass moments of inertia of the links are \(I_{G_{2}}=I_{G_{4}}=0.25 kg \cdot m ^{2} \text { and } I_{G_{3}}=4.5 kg \cdot m ^{2}\), and the center of mass of each link is coincident with its geometric center. The free length of the spring is r0 = 0.6 m, the spring rate is k = 500 N/m, and the damping constant is C = 10 N ·s/m.

ASSUMPTIONS

Gravity acts vertically downward, and friction can be ignored.

Admin
Solution

From the kinematic analysis of the four-bar linkage presented in Secs. 3.12 and 4.12, the first- and second-order kinematic coefficients of links 3 and 4 are

\(\theta_{3}^{\prime}=0, \quad \theta_{4}^{\prime}=1 rad / rad , \quad \theta_{3}^{\prime \prime}=0, \quad \text { and } \quad \theta_{4}^{\prime \prime}=0\) (1)

 

Recall that the angular velocities and angular accelerations of links 3 and 4 can be written as

\(\begin{aligned}&\omega_{3}=\theta_{3}^{\prime} \omega_{2} \quad \text { and } \quad \omega_{4}=\theta_{4}^{\prime} \omega_{2} \\&\alpha_{3}=\theta_{3}^{\prime \prime} \omega_{2}^{2}+\theta_{3}^{\prime} \alpha_{2} \quad \text { and } \quad \alpha_{4}=\theta_{4}^{\prime \prime} \omega_{2}^{2}+\theta_{4}^{\prime} \alpha_{2}\end{aligned}\) (2)

Substituting Eqs. (1) and the input angular velocity and angular acceleration into Eqs. (2) gives

\(\omega_{3}=0, \quad \omega_{4}=10 rad / s ccw , \quad \alpha_{3}=0, \quad \text { and } \quad \alpha_{4}=10 rad / s ^{2} cw\) (3)

The x and y components of the position of mass center G2 with respect to ground pivot O2 are

\(x_{G_{2}}=\frac{1}{2} r_{2} \cos \theta_{2} \quad \text { and } \quad y_{G_{2}}=\frac{1}{2} r_{2} \sin \theta_{2}\) (4)

Differentiating these equations twice with respect to θ2 and substituting θ2 = 270°, the first- and second-order kinematic coefficients of the mass center of link 2 are

\(x_{G_{2}}^{\prime}=0.2 m / rad , \quad y_{G_{2}}^{\prime}=0, \quad x_{G_{2}}^{\prime \prime}=0, \quad y_{G_{2}}^{\prime \prime}=0.2 m / rad ^{2}\) (5)

The x and y components of the position of mass center G3 with respect to ground pivot O2 can be written as

\(x_{G_{3}}=r_{2} \cos \theta_{2}+\frac{1}{2} r_{3} \cos \theta_{3} \quad \text { and } \quad y_{G_{3}}=r_{2} \sin \theta_{2}+\frac{1}{2} r_{3} \sin \theta_{3}\) (6)

Differentiating these equations twice with respect to θ2 and substituting θ2 = 270° and3θ2 = 0°, the first- and second-order kinematic coefficients of the mass center of link 3 are

\(x_{G_{3}}^{\prime}=0.4 m / rad , \quad y_{G_{3}}^{\prime}=0, \quad x_{G_{3}}^{\prime \prime}=0, \quad y_{G_{3}}^{\prime \prime}=0.4 m / rad ^{2}\) (7)

The first- and second-order kinematic coefficients of the mass center of link 4 are the same as for the mass center of link 2, that is,

\(x_{G_{4}}^{\prime}=x_{G_{2}}^{\prime}=0.2 m / rad , \quad y_{G_{4}}^{\prime}=y_{G_{2}}^{\prime}=0, \quad x_{G_{4}}^{\prime \prime}=x_{G_{2}}^{\prime \prime}=0, \quad y_{G_{4}}^{\prime \prime}=y_{G_{2}}^{\prime \prime}=0.2 m / rad ^{2}\) (8)

To obtain the first-order kinematic coefficient for the spring, the vector loop-closure equation, shown in Fig. 12.24, can be written as

\(r _{4}+ r _{s}- r _{6}= 0\) (9)

The two scalar equations are

\(\begin{aligned}r_{4} \cos \theta_{4}+r_{s} \cos \theta_{s}-r_{6} \cos \theta_{6} &=0 \\r_{4} \sin \theta_{4}+r_{s} \sin \theta_{s}-r_{6} \sin \theta_{6} &=0\end{aligned}\) (10)

Differentiating Eqs. (10) with respect to input θ2 gives

\(-r_{4} \sin \theta_{4} \theta_{4}^{\prime}-r_{s} \sin \theta_{s} \theta_{s}^{\prime}+r_{s}^{\prime} \cos \theta_{s}=0\) (11a)

 

\(r_{4} \cos \theta_{4} \theta_{4}^{\prime}+r_{s} \cos \theta_{s} \theta_{s}^{\prime}+r_{s}^{\prime} \sin \theta_{s}=0\) (11b)

 

Substituting \(\theta_{s}=0^{\circ}, \theta_{4}=270^{\circ}, \text { and } \theta_{4}^{\prime}=1 rad / rad\) into Eq. (11a), the first-order kinematic coefficient for the spring is

\(r_{s}^{\prime}=-r_{4} \theta_{4}^{\prime}=-0.4 m / rad\) (12)

To obtain the first-order kinematic coefficient for the damper, the vector loop-closure equation, shown in Fig. 12.25, can be written as

\(r _{2}+ r _{c}- r _{8}= 0\) (13)

The two scalar equations are

\(r_{2} \cos \theta_{2}+r_{c} \cos \theta_{c}-r_{8} \cos \theta_{8}=0\) (14a)

 

\(r_{2} \sin \theta_{2}+r_{c} \sin \theta_{c}-r_{8} \sin \theta_{8}=0\) (14b)

 

Differentiating Eqs. (14) with respect to input θ2 gives

\(-r_{2} \sin \theta_{2}-r_{c} \sin \theta_{c} \theta_{c}^{\prime}+r_{c}^{\prime} \cos \theta_{c}=0\) (15a)

 

\(r_{2} \cos \theta_{2}+r_{c} \cos \theta_{c} \theta_{c}^{\prime}+r_{c}^{\prime} \sin \theta_{c}=0\) (15b)

 

Substituting \(\theta_{2}=270^{\circ} \text { and } \theta_{c}=180^{\circ}\) into Eqs. (15a), the first-order kinematic coefficient for the damper is

\(r_{c}^{\prime}=r_{2}=0.4 m / rad\) (16)

The equivalent mass moment of inertia for the four-bar linkage is

\(I_{E Q}=\sum\limits_{j=2}^{4} A_{j}\)

 

From Eq. (12.79a), the equivalent mass moment of inertia can be written as

\(A_{j}=m_{j}\left(x_{G_{j}}^{\prime 2}+y_{G_{j}}^{\prime 2}\right)+I_{G_{j}} \theta_{j}^{\prime 2}\) (12.79a)

 

\(I_{E Q}=m_{2}\left(x_{G_{2}}^{\prime 2}+y_{G_{2}}^{\prime 2}\right)+I_{G_{2}}+m_{3}\left(x_{G_{3}}^{\prime 2}+y_{G_{3}}^{\prime 2}\right)+I_{G_{3}} \theta_{3}^{\prime 2}+m_{4}\left(x_{G_{4}}^{\prime 2}+y_{G_{4}}^{\prime 2}\right)+I_{G_{4}} \theta_{4}^{\prime 2}\)

Substituting the known data and the kinematic coefficients into this equation gives

\(I_{E Q}=0.1+0.25+0.8+0.1+0.25=1.5 kg \cdot m ^{2}\) (17)

From Eq. (12.79b), we can write

\(B_{j}=m_{j}\left(x_{G_{j}}^{\prime} x_{G_{j}}^{\prime \prime}+y_{G_{j}}^{\prime} y_{G_{j}}^{\prime \prime}\right)+I_{G_{j}} \theta_{j}^{\prime} \theta_{j}^{\prime \prime}\) (12.79b)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\sum\limits_{j=2}^{4} B_{j}=m_{2}\left(x_{G_{2}}^{\prime} x_{G_{2}}^{\prime \prime}+y_{G_{2}}^{\prime} y_{G_{2}}^{\prime\prime}\right)+m_{3}\left(x_{G_{3}}^{\prime}x_{G_{3}}^{\prime\prime}+y_{G_{3}}^{\prime} y_{G_{3}}^{\prime \prime}\right)+I_{G_{3}} \theta_{3}^{\prime} \theta_{3}^{\prime \prime} \\&+m_{4}\left(x_{G_{4}}^{\prime} x_{G_{4}}^{\prime \prime}+y_{G_{4}}^{\prime} y_{G_{4}}^{\prime \prime}\right)+I_{G_{4}} \theta_{4}^{\prime} \theta_{4}^{\prime \prime}\end{aligned}\) (18)

 

For the given posture, the kinematic coefficients are given by Eqs. (1), (5), (7), and (8). Substituting these kinematic coefficients into Eq. (18) gives

\(\sum\limits_{j=2}^{n} B_{j}=0\) (19)

Then, substituting the known data, the kinematic coefficients, and Eqs. (17) and (19) into Eq. (12.97), the equation of motion can be written

\(T_{2}=\left(1.5 kg \cdot m ^{2}\right) \ddot{\theta}_{2}+k\left(r_{s}-r_{0}\right) r_{s}^{\prime}+C r_{c}^{\prime 2} \dot{\theta}_{2}\)

Finally, substituting the known data and Eqs. (12) and (16) into this equation, the input torque is

\(\begin{aligned}T_{2}=&-15 N \cdot m +(500 N / m )(0.8 m -0.6 m )(-0.4 m / rad ) \\&+(10 N \cdot s / m )(0.4 m / rad )^{2}(10 rad / s ) \\T_{2}=&-39 N \cdot m =39 N \cdot m cw\end{aligned}\)

Since the result is negative, the input torque acts in the direction opposite to the input angular velocity, that is, clockwise. Note that this is necessary to achieve the clockwise input angular acceleration specified. Also note that a clockwise input torque of T2 = 24 N ·m would yield a zero input acceleration at the instant under study.
