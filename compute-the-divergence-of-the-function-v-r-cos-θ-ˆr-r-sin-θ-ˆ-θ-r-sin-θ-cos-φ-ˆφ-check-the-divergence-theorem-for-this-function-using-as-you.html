Guest Question.

Compute the divergence of the function

\(v=\left(r\cos \theta \right)\hat{r}\left(r\sin \theta \right)\hat{\pmb{\theta} } +\left(r\sin \theta \cos \phi \right) \hat{\pmb{\phi} } \) .

Check the divergence theorem for this function, using as your volume the inverted hemispherical bowl of radius R, resting on the xy plane and centered at the origin (Fig. 1.40).

Admin
Solution
\(∇\cdot v=\frac{1}{r^{2} }\frac{\partial}{\partial r}\left(r^{2} r\cos \theta \right)+\frac{1}{r\sin \theta }\frac{\partial }{\partial \theta } \left(\sin \theta r\sin \theta \right)+\frac{1}{r\sin \theta } \frac{\partial}{\partial \phi }\left(r\sin \theta \cos \phi \right)\)

=\(\frac{1}{r^{2} }3r^{2} \cos \theta +\frac{1}{r\sin \theta }r2\sin \theta \cos \theta +\frac{1}{r\sin \theta }r\sin \theta \left(-\sin \phi \right) \)

=\(3\cos \theta +2\cos \theta -\sin \phi =5\cos \theta -\sin \phi \)

 

\(\int{} \left(∇\cdot v\right)d\tau =\int{}\left(5\cos \theta -\sin \phi \right)r^{2}\sin \theta drd\theta d\phi =\int_{0}^{R}{}r^{2}dr\int_{0}^{\frac{\theta }{2} }{}\left[\int_{0}^{2\pi }{}\left(5\cos \theta -\sin \phi \right)d\phi \right] d\theta \sin \theta \)

 

\(\hookrightarrow2\pi \left(5\cos \theta \right) \)

=\(\left(\frac{R^{3} }{3} \right) \left(10\pi \right)\int_{0}^{\frac{\pi }{2} }{}\sin \theta \cos \theta d\theta \)

 

\(\hookrightarrow \frac{\sin ^{2}\theta }{2}\mid ^{\frac{\pi }{2} }_{0}=\frac{1}{2} \)

=\(\frac{5\pi }{3} R^{3} \) .

Two surfaces—one the hemisphere: \(da=R^{2}\sin \theta d\theta d\phi \hat{r};r=R;\phi :0\rightarrow 2\pi ,\theta :0\rightarrow \frac{\pi }{2} .\int{}v\cdot da=\int{} \left(r\cos \theta \right)R^{2}\sin \theta d\theta d\phi =R^{3}\int_{0}^{\frac{\pi }{2} }{} \sin \theta \cos \theta d\theta\int_{0}^{2\pi }{}d\phi =R^{3}\left(\frac{1}{2} \right)\left(2\pi \right)=\pi R^{3} \) .

other the flat bottom: \(da=(dr)\left(r\sin \theta d\phi \right)\left(+\hat{\pmb{\theta} } \right)=r dr d\phi \hat{\pmb{\theta} } \left(here \theta =\frac{\pi }{2} \right).r:0\rightarrow R ,\phi :0\rightarrow 2\pi \) .

\(\int{}v\cdot da=\int{}\left(r\sin \theta \right)\left(r dr d\phi \right)=\int_{0}^{R}{}r^{2}dr\int_{0}^{2\pi }{} d\phi =2\pi \frac{R^{3} }{3} \) .

Total: \(\int{} v\cdot da=\pi R^{3}+\frac{2}{3}\pi R^{3} =\frac{5}{3}\pi R^{3} \) .
