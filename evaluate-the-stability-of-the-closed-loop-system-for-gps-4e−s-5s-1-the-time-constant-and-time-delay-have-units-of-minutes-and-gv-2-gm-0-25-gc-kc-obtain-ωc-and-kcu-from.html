Guest Question.

Evaluate the stability of the closed-loop system for:
\( G_{p}(s)=\frac{4e^{-s}}{5s+1} \)
The time constant and time delay have units of minutes and,
\(G_{v} = 2, \quad G_{m} = 0.25,\quad G_{c} = K_{c}\)
Obtain \(\omega_{c} \space and \space K_{cu}\) from a Bode plot. Use an initial value of \(K_{c} = 1\).

Admin
Solution

The Bode plot for \(G_{OL} \space and \space K_{c} = 1 \) is shown in Figure on the right.
For \(\omega_{c} = 1.69 rad/min, \phi_{OL} = −180^∘, \space and \space AR_{OL} = 0.235\).
For \(K_{c} = 1, AR_{OL} = AR_{G} \space and \space K_{cu}\) can be calculated from Equation
\(K_{cu}=\frac{1}{AR_{G}(\omega_{c})} \) .
Thus, \(K_{cu} = 1/0.235 = 4.25\). Setting \(K_{c} = 1.5 K_{cu}\) gives \(K_{c} = 6.38\). A larger value of \(K_{c}\) causes the closed-loop system to become unstable. Only values of \(K_{c}\) less than \(K_{cu}\) result in a stable closed-loop system.
