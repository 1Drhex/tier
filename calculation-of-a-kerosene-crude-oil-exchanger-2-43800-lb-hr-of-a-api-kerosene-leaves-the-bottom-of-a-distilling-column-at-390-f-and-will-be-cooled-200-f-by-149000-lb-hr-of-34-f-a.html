Guest Question.

Calculation of a Kerosene-Crude Oil Exchanger.[2] 43,800 lb/hr of a API kerosene leaves the bottom of a distilling column at 390 °F and will be cooled 200 °F by 149,000 lb/hr of 34 °F API Midcontinent crude coming from storage at 100 °F and heated to 170 °F. A 10 psi pressure drop is permissible on both streams, and in accordance with Table AT.12, a combined dirt factor of 0.003 should be provided. Available for this service is a 21¼-in ID exchanger having 158 1-in OD, 13 BWG tubes 16 ft long and laid out on a 1¼-in square pitch. The bundle is arranged for four passes, and baffles are spaced 5 in. apart.

Admin
Solution

Shell-Side

ID = 21¼ in

Baffle space = 5 in

 

Passes = 1

 Tube-Side

Number and length = 158 tubes, 16’0”

OD, BWG, pitch = 1 in, 13 BWG, 1¼-in square pitch

Passes = 4

(1) Heat Balance:

Hot Kerosene,

\(T_{avg}=\frac{390+200}{2}=295°F\)

C = 0.61 Btu/(lb °F) for 42° API at 295 °F

Q = (43,800)(0.61)(390 – 200) = 5,100,000 Btu/hr

Cold Crude Oil,

\(t_{avg}=\frac{100+170}{2}=135°F\)

c = 0.49 Btu/(lb · °F) for 34° API at 135°F

Q = (149,000)(0.49)(170 – 100) = 5,100,000 Btu/hr

(2) Δ\(T^*\):

\(LMTD=\frac{\Delta T_a-\Delta T_b}{\ln (\Delta T_a/\Delta T_b)}=\frac{220-100}{\ln (220/100)}=152.2°F\)

 

\(R=\frac{T_1-T_2}{t_2-t_1}=\frac{190}{70}=2.71\)

 

\(P=\frac{t_2-t_1}{T_1-t_1}=\frac{70}{390-100}=0.241\)

 

\(F_T=\left\lgroup\frac{\sqrt{R^2+1} }{R-1} \right\rgroup\frac{\ln \left[(1-P)/(1-RP)\right] }{\ln \left[(2-P(R+1-\sqrt{R^2+1}))/(2-P(R+1+\sqrt{R^2+1} ))\right] }\)

 

\(=\left\lgroup\frac{\sqrt{2.71^2+1} }{2.71-1} \right\rgroup\frac{\ln \left[(1-0.241)/(1-(2.71)(0.241))\right] }{\ln \left[(2-0.241(2.71+1-\sqrt{2.71^2+1} ))/(2-0.241(2.71+1+\sqrt{2.71^2+1}) )\right] }\)

= 0.893

Δ\(T^*\) = LMTD × \(F_T\) = 152.2 °F × 0.893 = 136 °F

(3) \(T_{avg} and t_{avg}\) : Obtained in step (1).

Since the flow areas of the shell-side and tube-side are nearly equal, assume the larger stream flows in the tubes:

Hot Fluid (Kerosene) – Shell-Side (s)

(4 ‘) Flow Area, \(a_s\) :

\(a_s=\frac{ID\times C'B}{144P_T}\)

\(=\frac{(21.25)(0.25)(5)}{144(1.25)}=0.1475\ ft^2\)

 Cold Fluid (Crude Oil) – Tube-Side (t)

(4) Flow Area, \(a_t\) :

\(a^{'}_t=0.515\ in^2\ \Leftarrow [Table AT.8]\)

 

\(a_t=\frac{N_ta^{'}_t}{144n}\)

\(=\frac{(158)(0.515)}{144(4)}=0.141\ ft^2\)

 

(5′) Mass Velocity, \(G_s\) :

\(G_s=\frac{\dot{M}}{a_s}=\frac{43,800}{0.1475}\)

\(=297,000lb/(hr\cdot ft^2)\)

 (5) Mass Velocity, \(G_t\) :

\(G_t=\frac{\dot{m}}{a_t}=\frac{149,000}{0.141} \)

\(=1,060,000lb/(hr\cdot ft^2)\)

 

(6′) Compute \(D_e\) :

\(D_e=\frac{4\times (P^2_T-\pi d^2_o/4)}{\pi d_o}\)

 

\(=\frac{4\times ((1.25^2)-\pi (1^2)/4)}{\pi (1)}\)

 

\(=0.99\ in=\frac{0.99\ in}{12\ in/ft}=0.0825\ ft\)

 

Obtain μ at \(T_{avg}\) :

μ = 0.40 cP at 280°F

\(=0.40\ cP\left\lgroup\frac{2.42lb/(ft\cdot hr)}{1\ cP} \right\rgroup\)

= 0.97lb/(ft·hr)

 (6) Obtain D from Table AT.8:

D = 0.81 in

\(=\frac{0.81\ in}{12\ in/ft}=0.0675\ ft\)

 

 

 

 

 

Obtain μ at \(t_{avg}:\)

μ = 3.6 cP at 129

\(=3.6\ cP\left\lgroup\frac{2.42/(ft\cdot hr)}{1\ cP} \right\rgroup\)

= 8.7lb/(ft·hr)

 

(7′) Reynolds Number, R\(e_s\) :

\(Re_s=\frac{D_eG_s}{\mu}\)

 

\(=\frac{(0.0825)(297,000)}{0.97}=25,300\)

 (7) Reynolds Number, \(Re_t\) :

\(Re_t=\frac{DG_t}{\mu}\)

 

\(=\frac{(0.0675)(1,060,000)}{8.7}=8,220\)

 

 

(8′) Obtain C and k at \(T_{avg}\) :

C = 0.59 Btu/lb · °F at 295°F

k = 0.0765 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F/ft at 295°F

 (8) Obtain c and k at \(t_{avg}\) :

c = 0.49 Btu/lb · °F at 135°F

k = 0.077 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F/ft at 135°F

 

(9′) Solve for \(h_o\) and \(\phi_s\) at \(t_w\):

\(t_w\) = 202°F \(\Longleftarrow\) from Iterative calc.

\(μ_w\) = 0.66 cP at 202°F

\(=0.66\ cP\left\lgroup\frac{2.42lb/(ft\cdot hr)}{1\ cP} \right\rgroup \)

= 1.6lb/(ft · hr)

\(\phi _s=\left\lgroup\frac{\mu }{\mu _w} \right\rgroup^{0.14}=\left\lgroup\frac{0.97}{1.6} \right\rgroup^{0.14}=0.93\)

 

\(h_o\) = 164 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F/ft at 202°F

 (9) Solve for \(h_i\) and \(\phi_t\) at \(t_w\) :

\(t_w\) = 202°F \(\Longleftarrow\) from Iterative calc.

\(μ_w\) = 1.81 cP at 202°F

\(=1.81\ cP\left\lgroup\frac{2.42lb/(ft\cdot hr)}{1\ cP} \right\rgroup\)

= 4.4lb/(ft · hr)

\(\phi _t=\left\lgroup\frac{\mu }{\mu _w} \right\rgroup^{0.14}=\left\lgroup\frac{8.7}{4.4} \right\rgroup^{0.14}=1.1\)

 

\(h_i\) = 154 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F/ft at 202°F

 

(10) Clean overall coefficient, \(U_C\):

\(U_C=\frac{h_ih_o}{h_i+h_o}=\frac{(154)(164)}{154+164}=79.4\ Btu/hr\cdot ft^2\cdot °F\)

(11) Design overall coefficient, \(U_D\):

a” = 0.2618 \(ft^2\) per lin. ft ⇐[Table AT.8]

A = a”\(N_t\)L = (0.2618)(158)(16) = 662 \(ft^2\)

\(U_D=\frac{Q}{A\Delta T^*}=\frac{5,100,000}{(662)(136)}=56.6\ Btu/hr\cdot ft^2\cdot °F\)

(12) Dirt factor \(R_d\):

\(R_d=\frac{U_C-U_D}{U_CU_D}=\frac{79.4-56.6}{(79.4)(56.6)}=0.00507hr\cdot ft^2\cdot °F/Btu\)

Summary
Variable Value Units
\(h_o\) = 164 \(\frac{Btu}{hr·ft^2·°F/ft}\)
\(h_i\) = 154
\(U_C\) = 79.4 \(\frac{Btu}{hr·ft^2·°F}\)
\(U_D\) = 56.6
\(R_d\) (calculated) = 0.00507 \(\frac{hr·ft^2·°F}{Btu}\)
\(R_d\) (required) = 0.00300

Pressure Drop

(1′) For \(Re_s\) and \(T_{avg}\) in (6’) above:

f = 0.00175\(\frac{ft^2}{in^2}\)at \(Re_s\) = 25,300

s = 0.73 for 42° API at 280°F

\(D_s\) = 21.25 in\(\left\lgroup\frac{1\ ft}{12\ in} \right\rgroup\) = 1.77 ft

 (1) For \(Re_t\) and \(t_{avg}\) in (6) above:

f = 0.000285\(\frac{ft^2}{in^2}\) at \(Re_t\) = 8,220

s = 0.83 for 34° API at 129°F

 

(2′) Number of crosses, N + 1:

\(N+1=\frac{12L}{B}=\frac{12(16)}{5}=39\)

 (2) \(\Delta P_t=\frac{fG^2_tLN}{(5.22\times 10^{10})D_es\phi _t}\)

\(=\frac{(0.000285)(1,060,000)^2(16)(4)}{(5.22\times 10^{10})(0.0675)(0.83)(1.1)}\)

= 6.3 psi

\(\frac{\overline{v}^2}{2g^{'}}\left\lgroup\frac{62.5}{144} \right\rgroup\)

= 0.15 at \(G_t\) = 1,060,000

 

\(\Delta P_r=\left\lgroup\frac{4n}{s}\right\rgroup\left\lgroup\frac{\overline{v}^2}{2g^{'}}\right\rgroup\left\lgroup\frac{62.5}{144}\right\rgroup\)

 

\(=\left\lgroup\frac{4(4)}{0.83}\right\rgroup(0.15)=2.9\ psi\)

 

(3′) \(\Delta P_s=\frac{fG^2_sD_s(N+1)}{(5.22\times 10^{10})D_es\phi _s}\)

\(\Delta P_s=\frac{(0.00175)(297,000)^2(1.77)(39)}{(5.22\times 10^{10})(0.0825)(0.73)(0.93)}\)

Δ\(P_s\) = 3.6 psi

Δ\(P_s\) < 10 psi as allowable ΔP

 (3) Δ\(P_T\) = Δ\(P_t\) + Δ\(P_r\)

Δ\(P_T\)= 6.3 + 2.9 = 9.2psi

Δ\(P_T\) < 10 psi as allowable ΔP

 

It is seen that a dirt factor of 0.00507 will be obtained although only 0.00300 will be required to provide a reasonable maintenance period. The pressure drops have not been exceeded and the exchanger will be satisfactory for service.
