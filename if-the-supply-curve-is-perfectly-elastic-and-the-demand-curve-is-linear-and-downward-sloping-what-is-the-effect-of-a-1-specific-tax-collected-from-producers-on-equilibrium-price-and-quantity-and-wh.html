Guest Question.

If the supply curve is perfectly elastic and the demand curve is linear and downward sloping, what is the effect of a $1 specific tax collected from producers on equilibrium price and quantity, and what is the incidence on consumers? Why?

Admin
Solution
  1. Determine the equilibrium in the absence of a tax. Before the tax, the perfectly elastic supply curve, \(S^{1}\) in the graph, is horizontal at \(p_{1} .\) The downward-sloping linear demand curve, \(D\), intersects \(S^{1}\) at the pre-tax equilibrium, \(e_{1}\), where the price is \(p_{1}\) and the quantity is \(Q_{1}\).
  2. Show how the tax shifts the supply curve and determine the new equilibrium. A specific tax of \(\$ 1\) shifts the pre-tax supply curve, \(S^{1}\), upward by \(\$ 1\) to \(S^{2}\) which is horizontal at \(p_{1}+1\). The intersection of \(D\) and \(S^{2}\) determines the aftertax equilibrium, \(e_{2}\), where the price consumers pay is \(p_{2}=p_{1}+1\), the price firms receive is \(p_{2}-1=p_{1}\), and the quantity is \(Q_{2}\).
  3. Compare the before- and after-tax equilibria. The specific tax causes the equilibrium quantity to fall from \(Q_{1}\) to \(Q_{2}\), the price firms receive to remain at \(p_{1}\), and the equilibrium price consumers pay to rise from \(p_{1}\) to \(p_{2}=p_{1}+1\). The entire incidence of the tax falls on consumers:

\(\frac{\Delta p}{\Delta t}=\frac{p_{2}-p_{1}}{\Delta t}=\frac{\$ 1}{\$ 1}=1\)

(We can use Equation \(2.30\) to draw the same conclusion.)

\(\frac{\mathrm{d} p}{\mathrm{~d} t}=\frac{\frac{\mathrm{d} S}{\mathrm{~d} p} \frac{p}{Q}}{\frac{\mathrm{d} S}{\mathrm{~d} p} \frac{p}{Q}-\frac{\mathrm{d} D}{\mathrm{~d} p} \frac{p}{Q}}=\frac{\eta}{\eta-\varepsilon}\) (2.30)

  1. Explain why. The reason consumers must absorb the entire tax is that firms will not supply the good at a price that is any lower than they received before the \(\operatorname{tax}, p_{1} .\) Thus, the price must rise enough that the price suppliers receive after tax is unchanged. As consumers do not want to consume as much at a higher price, the equilibrium quantity falls.
