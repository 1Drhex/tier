Guest Question.

A rope passes over an ideal pulley as shown in Fig. 3.23a. It carries a crate with weight G and is held at point C. The radius of the pulley may be neglected.

Determine the resulting moment of the forces in the rope about point A.

Admin
Solution

The internal forces \(S_{1}\) and \(S_{2}\) in the rope are made visible by cuts through the rope. Their action on point B of the structure is shown in Fig. 3.23b. Since the bearing friction of the pulley is negligible (ideal pulley) both forces are equal, and equilibrium at the crate yields \(S_{1}\) = \(S_{2}\) = G.

To represent the moments of the forces, a coordinate system is introduced. Moment \(M_{1}^{(A)} \)of force S1 about A (represented as a column vector) has a component only in the x-direction:

\(M_{1}^{(A)}=\begin{pmatrix} -2\\ 0\\ 0\end{pmatrix}aG\) (a)

Moment \(M_{2}^{(A)}\) of force \(S_{2}\) may be obtained with the aid of the

cross-product (see (3.23))

\(M_{2}^{(A)}=r_{AB}\times S_{2}.\) (b)

The vector \(r_{AB}\) from reference point A to the point of application B of force\( S_{2}\) is given by

\(r_{AB}=\begin{pmatrix} 0\\ 2\\ 3\end{pmatrix}a.\) (c)

The force \(S_{2}\) may be represented by its magnitude \(S_{2} = G\) and the unit vector \(e_{2}\), which points in the direction of \(S_{2}\), i.e.,

\( S_{2} = S_{2} e_{2}\). To obtain vector \(e_{2}\), we first give vector \(r_{BC}\), which points from B to C (see Fig. 3.23c):

\(r_{BC}=r_{AC}-r_{AB}=\begin{pmatrix}-1\\4\\1\end{pmatrix}a-\begin{pmatrix}0\\2\\3\end{pmatrix}a=\begin{pmatrix}-1\\2\\-2\end{pmatrix}a.\)

If this vector is divided by its magnitude \(\left | r_{BC} \right |\) the unit vector \(e_{2}\) is obtained:

\(e_{2}=\frac{r_{BC}}{\left | r_{BC} \right |}=\frac{1} {a\sqrt{1+4+4}}\begin{pmatrix}-1\\2\\-2\end{pmatrix}a=\frac{1}{3}\begin{pmatrix}-1\\2\\-2\end{pmatrix}\)

Therefore,

\(S_{2}=S_{2}e_{2}=\frac{1}{3}\begin{pmatrix}-1\\2\\-2\end{pmatrix}G.\) (d)

With (c) and (d) the vector product (b) yields (compare Appendix (A.31))

\(M_{2}^{(A)}=r_{AB}\times S_{2}=\begin{vmatrix}e_{x} &e_{y} &e_{z} \\0 &2a &3a \\-G/3 &2G/3 &-2G/3\end{vmatrix}\) \(=(-4/3-2)aG e_{x}-aGe_{y}+(2/3)aGe_{z}.\)

This vector may be written as a column vector:

\(M_{2}^{(A)}=\frac{1}{3}\begin{pmatrix}-10\\-3\\2\end{pmatrix}aG.\) (e)

The resulting moment \(M_{R}^{(A)}\) of the forces \(S_{1}\) and \(S_{2}\) about point A is the sum of the moments (a) and (e), (compare (3.32)):

\(R=\sum{F_i} , M^{(A)}_{R} =\sum{M^{(A)}_{i}}\) (3.32)

 

\( M_{R}^{(A)}=M_{1}^{(A)}+M_{2}^{(A)}=\frac{1}{3}\begin{pmatrix}-16\\-3\\2\end{pmatrix}aG.\)
