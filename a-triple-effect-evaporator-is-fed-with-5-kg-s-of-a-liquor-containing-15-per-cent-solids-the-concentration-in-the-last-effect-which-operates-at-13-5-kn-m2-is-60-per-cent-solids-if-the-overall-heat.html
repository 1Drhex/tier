Guest Question.

A triple-effect evaporator is fed with 5 kg/s of a liquor containing 15 per cent solids. The concentration in the last effect, which operates at 13.5 kN/\(m^{2}\), is 60 per cent solids. If the overall heat transfer coefficients in the three effects are 2.5, 2.0, and 1.1 kW/\(m^{2}\) K,respectively, and the steam is fed at 388 K to the first effect, determine the temperature distribution and the area of heating surface required in each effect? The calandrias are identical. What is the economy and what is the heat load on the condenser? The feed temperature is 294 K and the specific heat capacity of all liquors is 4.18 kJ/kg K.
If the unit is run as a backward-feed system, the coefficients are 2.3, 2.0, and 1.6 kW/\(m^{2}\) K respectively. Determine the new temperatures, the heat economy, and the heating surface required under these conditions.

Admin
Solution

(a) Forward feed A mass balance gives:

Solids (kg/s) Liquor (kg/s) Total (kg/s)
Feed 0.75 4.25 5
Product 0.75 0.5 1.25
Evaporation __ 3.75 3.75

For steam saturated at 13.5 kN/\(m^{2}\)

\(T_{3}\) = 325 K and \(\lambda _{3}\) = 2375 kJ/kg

\(T_{0}\) = 388 K and \(\lambda _{0}\) = 2216 kJ/kg

Thus: \(\sum \Delta T\) = (388 – 325) = 63 deg K

For equal heat transfer rates in each effect:

\(U_{1}\Delta T_{1}=U_{2}\Delta T_{2}=U_{3}\Delta T_{3}\) (equation 14.8)

Thus: \(2.5\Delta T_{1}=2.0\Delta T_{2}=1.1\Delta T_{3}\)

and: \(\Delta T_{1}\) = 14 deg K, \(\Delta T_{2}\) = 17.5 deg K, and \(\Delta T_{3}\) = 31.5 deg K

Allowing for the cold feed it will be assumed that:

\(\Delta T_{1}\) = 18 deg K, \(\Delta T_{2}\) = 16 deg K, and \(\Delta T_{3}\) = 29 deg K

and hence: \(T_{0}\) = 388 K, \(\lambda _{0}\) = 2216 kJ/kg

\(T_{1}\) = 370 K, \(\lambda _{1}\) = 2266 kJ/kg

\(T_{2}\) = 354 K, \(\lambda _{2}\) = 2305 kJ/kg

\(T_{3}\) = 325 K, \(\lambda _{3}\) = 2375 kJ/kg

Making heat balances over each effect:

(1) \(D_{0}\lambda _{0}=WC_{p}(T_{1}-T_{f})+D_{1}\lambda _{1}\)

or 2216\(D_{0}\) = (5.0 × 4.18)(370 – 294) + 2266\(D_{1}\)

(2) \(D_{1}\lambda _{1}+(W-D_{1})C_{p}(T_{1}-T_{2})=D_{2}\lambda _{2}\)

or 2266\(D_{1}\) + (5.0 – \(D_{1}\))4.18(370 – 354) = 2305\(D_{2}\)

(3) \(D_{2}\lambda _{2}+(W-D_{1}-D_{2})C_{p}(T_{2}-T_{3})=D_{3}\lambda _{3}\)

or 2305\(D_{2}\) + (5.0 – \(D_{1}-D_{2}\))4.18(354 – 325) = 2375\(D_{3}\)

Also: (\(D_{1}+D_{2}+D_{3}\)) = 3.75 kg/s

Solving simultaneously:

\(D_{0}\) = 1.90 kg/s, \(D_{1}\) = 1.16 kg/s, \(D_{2}\) = 1.25 kg/s, and \(D_{3}\) = 1.35 kg/s

The areas are now given by:

\(A_{1}=D_{0}\lambda _{0}/U_{1}\Delta T_{1}\) = (1.90 × 2216)/(2.5 × 18) = 93.6 \(m^{2}\)

\(A_{2}=D_{1}\lambda _{1}/U_{1}\Delta T_{2}\) = (1.16 × 2266)/(2.0 × 16) = 82.1 \(m^{2}\)

\(A_{3}=D_{2}\lambda _{2}/U_{3}\Delta T_{3}\) = (1.25 × 2305)/(1.1 × 29) = 90.3 \(m^{2}\)

It is apparent that the modification to the temperature difference made to take account of the effect of the cold feed has been incorrect and it is now assumed that:

\(\Delta T_{1}\) = 19 deg K, \(\Delta T_{2}\) = 15 deg K, and \(\Delta T_{3}\) = 29 deg K

Thus: \(T_{0}\) = 388 K and \(\lambda _{0}\) = 2216 kJ/kg

\(T_{1}\) = 369 K and \(\lambda _{1}\) = 2267 kJ/kg

\(T_{2}\) = 354 K and \(\lambda _{2}\) = 2305 kJ/kg

\(T_{3}\) = 325 K and \(\lambda _{3}\) = 2375 kJ/kg

The heat balances now become:

(1) 2216\(D_{0}\) = (5.0 × 4.18)(369 – 294) + 2267\(D_{1}\)

(2) 2267\(D_{1}\) + (5.0 – \(D_{1}\))4.18(369 – 354) = 2305\(D_{2}\)

(3) 2305\(D_{2}\) + (5.0 – \(D_{1}-D_{2}\))4.18(354 – 325) = 2375\(D_{3}\)

and solving:

\(D_{0}\) = 1.89 kg/s, \(D_{1}\) = 1.16 kg/s, \(D_{2}\) = 1.25 kg/s, and \(D_{3}\) = 1.34 kg/s

Hence: \(A_{1}\) = (1.89 × 2216)/(2.5 × 19) = 88.2 \(m^{2}\)

\(A_{2}\) = (1.16 × 2267)/(2.0 × 15) = 87.8 \(m^{2}\)

\(A_{3}\) = (1.25 × 2305)/(1.1 × 29) = 90.3 \(m^{2}\)

giving much closer values for the three areas.
The temperature distribution is now:

(1) 369 K, (2) 354 K, (3) 325 K

The area in each effect should be about 89 \(m^{2}\).
The economy is (3.75/1.89) = 2.0 and the heat load on the condenser is:

\(D_{3}\lambda _{3}\)= (1.34 × 2375) = 31.8 kW

(b) Backward feed

As before: (\(D_{1}+D_{2}+D_{3}\)) = 3.75 kg/s

and: \(\sum \Delta T\) = (388 – 325) = 63 deg k

In this case: 2.3\(\Delta T_{1}\) = 2.0\(\Delta T_{2}\) = 1.6\(\Delta T_{3}\)

and: \(\Delta T_{1}\) = 17.5 deg K, \(\Delta T_{2}\) = 20.0 deg K, and \(\Delta T_{3}\) = 25.5 deg K

Modifying for the effect of the cold feed, it is assumed that:

\(\Delta T_{1}\) = 18.5 deg K, \(\Delta T_{2}\) = 20.5 deg K, and \(\Delta T_{3}\) = 24 deg K

Thus: \(T_{0}\) = 388 K, \(\lambda _{0}\) = 2216 kJ/kg

\(T_{1}\) = 369.5 K, \(\lambda _{1}\) = 2266 kJ/kg

\(T_{2}\) = 349 K, \(\lambda _{2}\) = 2318 kJ/kg

\(T_{3}\) = 325 K, \(\lambda _{3}\) = 2375 kJ/kg

The heat balances become:

(1) \(D_{0}\lambda _{0}=(W-D_{3}-D_{2})C_{p}(T_{1}-T_{2})+D_{1}\lambda _{1}\)

or 2216\(D_{0}\) = (5.0 – \(D+{3}-D_{2}\))4.18(369.5 – 349) + 2266\(D_{1}\)

(2) \(D_{1}\lambda _{1}=(W-D_{3})C_{p}(T_{2}-T_{3})+D_{2}\lambda _{2}\)

or 2266\(D_{1}\) = (5.0 – \(D_{3}\))4.18(349 – 325) + 2318\(D_{2}\)

(3) \(D_{2}\lambda _{2}=WC_{p}(T_{3}-T_{f})+D_{3}\lambda _{3}\)

or 2318\(D_{2}\) = (5.0 × 4.18)(325 – 294) + 2375\(D_{3}\)

The areas are therefore:

\(A_{1}\) = (1.62 × 2216)/(2.3 × 18.5) = 84.4 \(m^{2}\)

\(A_{2}\) = (1.49 × 2266)/(2.0 × 20.5) = 82.4 \(m^{2}\)

\(A_{3}\) = (1.28 × 2318)/(1.6 × 24) = 77.3 \(m^{2}\)

which are probably close enough for design purposes.
Thus, the temperatures in each effect are now:
(1) 369.5 K, (2) 349 K, and (3) 325 K

The economy is (3.75/1.62) = 2.3, and the area required in each effect is approximately 81 \(m^{2}\).
