Guest Question.

A flat plate solar collector L = 2m long and W = 1m wide is inclined at \(\theta=13^{\circ}\) to the horizontal. The cover plate is separated from the absorber plate by an air gap of s = 2.5 cm. The average temperature of the cover plate is \(T_{c}=305 K\), while the collector is at \(T_{h}=330 K\). Estimate the convective heat loss assuming air to be at 1 atm. Compare this with the solar energy input to the system at \(S=800 W / m ^{2}\).

Admin
Solution

Air properties are taken at the mean air temperature of \(T_{m}=\frac{T_{h}+T_{c}}{2}=\frac{330+305}{2}= 317.5 K\) and pressure of 1 atm.

Kinematic viscosity: \(v_{m}=17.46 \times 10^{-6} m ^{2} / s\)

Thermal conductivity: \(k_{m}=0.0276 W / m ^{\circ} C\)

Prandtl number: \(P r_{m}=0.704\)

Isobaric expansion coefficient: \(\beta_{m}=\frac{1}{317.5}=0.0032 K ^{-1}\)

The Rayleigh number based on air layer thickness is calculated.

\(R a_{s}=\frac{9.81 \times 0.0032(330-305) 0.025^{3}}{\left(17.46 \times 10^{-6}\right)^{2}} \times 0.704=27872\)

The factor \(\psi\) used in Hollands et al. correlation is then given by

\(\psi=\frac{1708}{17872 \times \cos 13}=0.0629\)

Using the correlating equation n16.69, we have

\(\overline{N u}_{s}=1+\underbrace{1.44(1-\psi)\left[1-\psi \sin ^{1.6}(1.8 \theta)\right]}_{0 \text { if negative }}+\underbrace{\left[\frac{0.664}{\psi^{\frac{1}{3}}}-1\right]}_{0 \text { if negative }}\) (16.69)

\(\overline{N u}_{s}=1+1.44(1-0.0629)\left[1-\psi \sin ^{1.6}(1.8 \theta)\right]+\left[\frac{0.664}{0.0629^{\frac{1}{3}}}-1\right]=3.00\)

The convective heat transfer coefficient is then calculated as

\(\bar{h}=\frac{3 \times 0.0276}{0.025}=3.312 W / m ^{2 \circ} C\)

The heat loss through the air layer is then given by

\(Q_{\text {loss }}=\bar{h} L W\left(T_{h}-T_{c}\right)=3.312 \times 2 \times 1(330-305)=165.6 W\)

Incident solar energy intercepted by the collector area is given by

\(Q_{\text {Solar }}=L W S=2 \times 1 \times 800=1600 W\)

The loss as a percentage of the incident solar flux is then given by

\(\frac{Q_{\text {loss }} \times 100}{Q_{\text {solar }}}=\frac{165.6 \times 100}{1600}=10.35 \%\)
