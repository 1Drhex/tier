Exergy Analysis of Heating a Room with a Radiator

A 50-L electrical radiator containing heating oil is placed in a well-sealed \(75-m^3\) room (Fig. 8-39). Both the air in the room and the oil in the radiator are initially at the environment temperature of 6°C. Electricity with a rating of 2.4 kW is now turned on. Heat is also lost from the room at an average rate of 0.75 kW. The heater is turned off after some time when the temperatures of the room air and oil are measured to be 20°C and 60°C, respectively. Taking the density and the specific heat of oil to be \(950 kg/m^3\) and 2.2 kJ/kg⋅°C, determine (a) how long the heater is kept on, (b) the exergy destruction, and (c) the second-law efficiency for this process.

Admin
Solution

An electrical radiator is placed in a room and it is turned on for a period of time. The time period for which the heater was on, the exergy destruction, and the second-law efficiency are to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Kinetic and potential energy changes are negligible. 2 Air is an ideal gas with constant specific heats. 3 The room is well sealed. 4 Standard atmospheric pressure of \(101.3 \mathrm{kPa}\) is assumed.

Properties The properties of air at room temperature are \(R=0.287 \mathrm{kPa} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}\), \(c_{p}=1.005 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}, c_{v}=0.718 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}(\) . The properties of oil are given to be \(\rho=950 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}, c=2.2 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}\).

Analysis \(\quad(a)\) The masses of air and oil are

\(\begin{array}{c}m_{a}=\frac{P_{1} \mathrm{~V}}{R T_{1}}=\frac{(101.3 \mathrm{kPa})\left(75 \mathrm{~m}^{3}\right)}{\left(0.287 \mathrm{kPa} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}\right)(6+273 \mathrm{~K})}=94.88 \mathrm{~kg} \\\\m_{\mathrm{oil}}=\rho V_{\mathrm{oil}}=\left(950 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\right)\left(0.050 \mathrm{~m}^{3}\right)=47.50 \mathrm{~kg}\end{array}\)

An energy balance on the system can be used to determine time period for which the heater was kept on.

\(\begin{aligned}\left(\dot{W}_{\text {in }}-\dot{Q}_{\text {out }}\right) \Delta t=&\left[m c_{v}\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right)\right]_{a}+\left[m c\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right)\right]_{\text {oil }} \\(2.4-0.75 \mathrm{~kW}) \Delta t=&\left[(94.88 \mathrm{~kg})\left(0.718 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot{ }^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\right)(20-6)^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\right] \\&+\left[(47.50 \mathrm{~kg})\left(2.2 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot{ }^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\right)(60-6)^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\right] \\\Delta t=& 3988 \mathrm{~s}=\mathbf{6 6 . 6} \mathrm{min}\end{aligned}\)

(b) The pressure of the air at the final state is

\(P_{a 2}=\frac{m_{a} R T_{a 2}}{V}=\frac{(94.88 \mathrm{~kg})\left(0.287 \mathrm{kPa} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}\right)(20+273 \mathrm{~K})}{75 \mathrm{~m}^{3}}=106.4 \mathrm{kPa}\)

The amount of heat transfer to the surroundings is

\(Q_{\text {out }}=\dot{Q}_{\text {out }} \Delta t=(0.75 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{s})(3988 \mathrm{~s})=2999 \mathrm{~kJ}\)

The entropy generation is the sum of the entropy changes of air, oil, and the surroundings.

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta S_{a}&=m\left(c_{p} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-R \ln \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}\right)\\&=(94.88 \mathrm{~kg})\left[(1.005 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}) \ln \frac{(20+273) \mathrm{K}}{(6+273) \mathrm{K}}\right.\\&\left.\quad-(0.287 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}) \ln \frac{106.4 \mathrm{kPa}}{101.3 \mathrm{kPa}}\right]\\&=3.335 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{K}\\\Delta S_{\text {oil }}&=m c \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}=(47.50 \mathrm{~kg})(2.2 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}) \ln \frac{(60+273) \mathrm{K}}{(6+273) \mathrm{K}}=18.49 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{K}\\&\Delta S_{\mathrm{surr}}=\frac{Q_{\mathrm{out}}}{T_{\mathrm{surr}}}=\frac{2999 \mathrm{~kJ}}{(6+273) \mathrm{K}}=10.75 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{K}\\&S_{\mathrm{gen}}=\Delta S_{\mathrm{a}}+\Delta S_{\mathrm{oil}}+\Delta S_{\mathrm{surr}}=3.335+18.49+10.75=32.57 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{K}\end{aligned}\)

The exergy destruction is determined from

\(X_{\text {dest }}=T_{0} S_{\text {gen }}=(6+273 \mathrm{~K})(32.57 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{K})=9088 \mathrm{~kJ} \cong 9.09 \mathrm{M} \mathrm{J}\)

(c) The second-law efficiency may be defined in this case as the ratio of the exergy recovered to the exergy expended. That is,

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta X_{a} &=m\left[c_{v}\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right)\right]-T_{0} \Delta S_{a} \\&=(94.88 \mathrm{~kg})\left[\left(0.718 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot{ }^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\right)(20-6)^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\right]-(6+273 \mathrm{~K})(3.335 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{K}) \\&=23.16 \mathrm{~kJ} \\\Delta X_{\text {oil }} &=m\left[c\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right)\right]-T_{0} \Delta S_{\text {oil }} \\&=(47.50 \mathrm{~kg})\left[\left(2.2 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot{ }^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\right)(60-6)^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\right]-(6+273 \mathrm{~K})(18.49 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{K}) \\&=484.5 \mathrm{~kJ} \\\eta_{\mathrm{II}} &=\frac{X_{\text {recovered }}}{X_{\text {expended }}}=\frac{\Delta X_{a}+\Delta X_{\mathrm{oil}}}{\dot{W}_{\mathrm{in}} \Delta t}=\frac{(23.16+484.5) \mathrm{kJ}}{(2.4 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{s})(3998 \mathrm{~s})}=0.0529 \text { or } 5.3 \%\end{aligned}\)

Discussion This is a highly irreversible process since the most valuable form of energy, work, is used to heat the room air. Out of \(9571 \mathrm{~kJ}\) of exergy expended by electrical work, \(9088 \mathrm{~kJ}\) is destroyed, with a corresponding second-law efficiency of \(5.3\) percent.
