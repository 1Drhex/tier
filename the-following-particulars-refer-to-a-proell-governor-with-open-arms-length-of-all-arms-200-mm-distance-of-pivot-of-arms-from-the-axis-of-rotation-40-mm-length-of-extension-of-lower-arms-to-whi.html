Guest Question.

The following particulars refer to a Proell governor with open arms :
Length of all arms = 200 mm, distance of pivot of arms from the axis of rotation = 40 mm, length of extension of lower arms to which the ball is attached = 100 mm, mass of each ball = 6 kg and mass of the central load = 150 kg. If the radius of rotation of the balls is 180 mm when the arms are inclined at \(40^{\circ}\) to the axis of rotation, find :
1. the equilibrium speed for the above configuration, 2. the coefficient of insensitiveness if the friction of the governor mechanism is equivalent to a force of 20 N at the sleeve, and 3. the range of speed between which the governor is inoperative.

Admin
Solution

Given : PF = FD = 200 mm = 0.2 m ; DK = 40 mm = 0.04 m ; BF = 100 mm = 0.1 m ; m = 6 kg ; M = 150 kg ; r = JG = 180 mm = 0.18 m ; F = 20 N

1. Equilibrium speed

Let N = Equilibrium speed.

From the equilibrium position, as shown in Fig 18.47, we find that the height of the governor

\(h=P H=P F \cos 40^{\circ}\)

 

= 0.2 × 0.766 = 0.1532 m

 

and \(F H=P F \sin 40^{\circ}\)

 

= 0.2 × 0.643 = 0.1286 m

 

\(\therefore \) JF = JG – HG – FH

 

= 0.18 – 0.04 – 0.1286

 

= 0.0114 m

 

and \(B J=\sqrt{(B F)^{2}-(J F)^{2}}\)

 

\(=\sqrt{(0.1)^{2}-(0.0114)^{2}}\)

 

= 0.0993 m

 

BM = BJ + JM

 

= 0.0993 + 0.1532 = 0.2525 m \(\cdots(\because J M=M D=P H)\)

 

IM = IN – MN = FH – JF = 0.1286 – 0.0114 = 0.1172 m

 

ID = 2 × IN = 2 × FH = 2 × 0.1286 = 0.2572 m

 

We know that centrifugal force,

\(F_{ C }=m \cdot \omega^{2} \cdot r=6\left(\frac{2 \pi N}{60}\right)^{2} 0.18=0.012 N ^{2}\)

Now taking moments about I,

\(F_{ C } \times B M=m \cdot g \times I M+\frac{M \cdot g}{2} \times I D\)

 

\(0.012 N ^{2} \times 0.2525=6 \times 9.81 \times 0.1172+\frac{150 \times 9.81}{2} \times 0.2572\)

 

or \(0.00303 N^{2}=6.9+189.2=196.1\)

\(\therefore \) \(N^{2}=196.1 / 0.00303=64720 \text { or } \quad N=254.4 \text { r.p.m. }\)

 

Coefficient of insensitiveness

Let N’ and N” = Minimum and maximum speed considering friction.

We know that centrifugal force at the minimum speed,

\(F_{ C }^{\prime}=m\left(\omega^{\prime}\right)^{2} r=6\left(\frac{2 \pi N^{\prime}}{60}\right)^{2} 0.18=0.012\left(N^{\prime}\right)^{2}\)

and centrifugal force at the maximum speed,

\(F_{ C }^{\prime \prime}=m\left(\omega^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} r=6\left(\frac{2 \pi N^{\prime \prime}}{60}\right)^{2} 0.18=0.012\left(N^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2}\)

Taking moments about I, when sleeve moves downwards,

\(F_{ C }^{\prime} \times B M=m \cdot g \times I M+\frac{M \cdot g-F}{2} \times I D\)

 

\(0.012\left(N^{\prime}\right)^{2} 0.2525=6 \times 9.81 \times 0.1172+\frac{150 \times 9.81-20}{2} \times 0.2572\)

 

\(0.00303\left(N^{\prime}\right)^{2}=6.9+186.7=193.6\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(\left(N^{\prime}\right)^{2}=193.6 / 0.00303=63894 \quad \text { or } \quad N^{\prime}=252.8 r . p . m .\)

Again taking moments about I, when the sleeve moves upwards,

\(F_{ C }^{\prime \prime} \times B M=m \cdot g \times I M+\frac{M \cdot g+F}{2} \times I D\)

 

\(0.012\left(N^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} 0.2525=6 \times 9.81 \times 0.1172+\frac{150 \times 9.81+20}{2} \times 0.2572\)

 

\(0.00303\left(N^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2}=6.9+191.8=198.7\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(\left(N^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2}=198.7 / 0.00303=65578 \quad \text { or } \quad N^{\prime \prime}=256 \text { r.p.m. }\)

We know that coefficient of insensitiveness,

\(\frac{N^{\prime \prime}-N^{\prime}}{N}=\frac{256-252.8}{254.4}=0.0126 \text { or } 1.26 \%\)

 

3. Range of speed

We know that range of speed

\(=N^{\prime \prime}-N^{\prime}=256-252.8=3.2 \text { r.p.m. }\)
