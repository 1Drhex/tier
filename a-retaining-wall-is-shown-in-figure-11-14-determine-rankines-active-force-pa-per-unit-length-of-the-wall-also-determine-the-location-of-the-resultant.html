Guest Question.

A retaining wall is shown in Figure 11.14. Determine Rankine’s active force, \(\textbf{P}_{\textbf{a}}\), per unit length of the wall. Also determine the location of the resultant.

Admin
Solution

Given \(c^{\prime}\) = 0, we know that \(\sigma^{\prime}_{a} = K_{a}\sigma^{\prime}_{o}.\) For the upper layer of the soil, the Rankine
active earth pressure coefficient is

\(K_{a} = K_{a(1)} = \frac{1 – \sin 30^{\circ}}{1 + \sin 30^{\circ}} = \frac{1}{3}\)

For the lower layer,

\(K_{a} = K_{a(2)} = \frac{1 – \sin 35^{\circ}}{1 + \sin 35^{\circ}} = \frac{0.4264}{1.5736} = 0.271\)

At z = 0, \(\sigma^{\prime}_{o}\)= 0. At z = 1.2 m (just inside the bottom of the upper layer),\(\sigma^{\prime}_{o} = (1.2)(16.5) = 19.8\,kN/m^{2}\). So

\(\sigma^{\prime}_{a} = K_{a(1)}\sigma^{\prime}_{o} = \frac{1}{3}(19.8) = 6.6\,kN/m^{2}\)

Again, at z = 1.2 m (in the lower layer), \(\sigma^{\prime}_{o} = (1.2)(16.5) = 19.8 \,kN/m^{2},\text{ and }\\\sigma^{\prime}_{a} = K_{a(2)}\sigma^{\prime}_{o} = (0.271)(19.8) = 5.37\,kN/m^{2}\\\text{At z = 6 m,}\\\sigma^{\prime}_{o} = (1.2)(16.5) + (4.8)(19.2 – 9.81) = 64.87 \,kN/m^{2}\\ \underset{\gamma _{w} }{\uparrow } \\and\\\sigma^{\prime}_{a} = K_{a(2)}\sigma^{\prime}_{o} = (0.271)(64.87) = 17.58\,kN/m^{2}\)

The variation of \(\sigma^{\prime}_{a}\) with depth is shown in Figure 11.14b. The lateral pressures from the pore water are as follows:

\(\bullet\) At z = 0, u = 0

\(\bullet\) At z = 1.2 m, u = 0

\(\bullet\) At z = 6 m, u = (4.8)(\(\gamma_{w}\)) = (4.8)(9.81) = 47.1 kN/\(m^{2}\)

The variation of u with depth is shown in Figure 11.14c, and the variation for \(\sigma_{a}\) (total active pressure) is shown in Figure 11.14d. Thus,

\(P_{a} = (\frac{1}{2})(6.6)(1.2) + (4.8)(5.37) + (\frac{1}{2})(4.8)(64.68 – 5.37)\\ =3.96 + 25.78 + 142.34 = \textbf{172.08 kN/m}\)

The location of the resultant can be found by taking the moment about the bottom of the wall. Thus,

\(\bar{z}=\frac{3.96\left(4.8+\frac{1.2}{3}\right)+(25.78)(2.4)+(142.34)\left(\frac{4.8}{3}\right)}{172.08}= \textbf{1.8 m}\)
