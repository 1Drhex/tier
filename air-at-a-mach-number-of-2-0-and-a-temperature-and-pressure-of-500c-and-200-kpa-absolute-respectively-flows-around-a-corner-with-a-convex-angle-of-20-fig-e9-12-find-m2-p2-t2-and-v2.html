Guest Question.

Air at a Mach number of 2.0 and a temperature and pressure of 500°C and 200 kPa absolute, respectively, flows around a corner with a convex angle of 20° (Fig. E9.12). Find \(M_{2}\), \(p_{2}\), \(T_{2}\), and \(V_{2}\).

Admin
Solution

Table \(\mathrm{D} .3\) uses \(\mathrm{M}=1\) as a reference condition; thus we visualize the flow as originating from a flow with \(\mathrm{M}=1\) and turning through the angle \(\theta_{1}\) to \(\mathrm{M}_{1}=2\), as shown in the sketch. From the table, adding an additional \(20^{\circ}\) to the deflection angle, we find that \(\theta_{2}=46.4^{\circ} .\) This would be equivalent to the flow at \(\mathrm{M}=1\) turning a convex corner with \(\theta=46.4^{\circ} .\) Since the flow is isentropic, we can simply superimpose in this manner. Now, for an angle of \(\theta=46.4^{\circ}\) from the table, we find that

\(\mathrm{M}_{2}=2.83\)

From the isentropic flow table (Table D.1) we find that

\(\begin{aligned}p_{2} &=p_{1} \frac{p_{0}}{p_{1}} \frac{p_{2}}{p_{0}} \\&=200 \times \frac{1}{0.1278} \times 0.0352=55.1 \mathrm{kPa} \\T_{2} &=T_{1} \frac{T_{0}}{T_{1}} \frac{T_{2}}{T_{0}} \\&=773 \times \frac{1}{0.5556} \times 0.3844=534.8 \mathrm{~K} \quad \text { or } \quad 261.8^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\end{aligned}\)

The velocity \(V_{2}\) is found to be

\(\begin{aligned}V_{2} &=\mathrm{M}_{2} \sqrt{k R T_{2}} \\&=2.83 \sqrt{1.4 \times 287 \times 534.8}=1312 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\end{aligned}\)
