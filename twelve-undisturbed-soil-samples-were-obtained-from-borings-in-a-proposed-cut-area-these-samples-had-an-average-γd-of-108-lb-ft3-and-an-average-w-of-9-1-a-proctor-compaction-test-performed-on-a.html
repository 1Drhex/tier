Guest Question.

Twelve undisturbed soil samples were obtained from borings in a proposed cut area. These samples had an average \(γ_{d}\) of \(108 lb/ft^{3}\) and an average \(w\) of 9.1%. A Proctor compaction test performed on a representative bulk sample produced \((γ_{d})_{\max} = 124 lb/ft^{3}\) and \(w_{o}= 12.8\)%. A proposed grading plan calls for \(12,000 yd^{3}\) of cut and \(11,500 yd^{3}\) of fill, and the specifications call for a relative compaction of at least 90%.

a. Compute the shrinkage factor

b. Compute the required quantity of import or export soils based on the unit weight of the cut.

c. Compute the weight of import or export in tons using the moisture content of the cut.

d. Compute the required quantity of water in gallons to bring the fill soils to the optimum moisture content.

Admin
Solution

a. Using an average relative compaction of 92%:

\(\left(\bar{\gamma }_{d} \right) _{f}=\left(\gamma _{d}\right) _{\max }\bar{C}_{R} =\left(124 lb/ft^{3}\right) \left(0.92\right) =114 lb/ft^{3}\)

 

\(\frac{\Delta V}{V_{f}} =\left[\frac{\left(\bar{\gamma }_{d} \right) _{f}}{\left(\bar{\gamma }_{d} \right) _{c}} -1\right] \times 100\)% \(=\left[\frac{114}{108} -1\right] \times 100\)% \(=6\)% (Shrinkage)

b. \(\Delta V=\frac{\Delta V}{V_{f}} V_{f}=\) (\(6\)%)\(\left(11,500 yd^{3}\right) =690 yd^{3}\)

Cut required to produce \(11,500 yd^{3}\) of fill \(= 11,500 yd^{3} + 690 yd^{3} = 12,190 yd^{3}\)

Required import \(= 12,190 yd^{3} – 12,000 yd^{3} = 190 yd^{3}\)

c. \(W_{s}=V\gamma _{d}=\left(190 yd^{3}\right) \left\lgroup\frac{3ft}{1yd} \right\rgroup ^{3}\left(108lb/ft^{3}\right) \left\lgroup\frac{1T}{2000lb} \right\rgroup =277 T\)

 

\(W=W_{s}\left(1 + W\right) =\left(277 T\right) \left(1 + 0.091\right) =302 T\)

 

\(\left(W_{s}\right) _{fill}=\left(\gamma _{d}\right) _{fill}\left(V_{fill}\right) =\left(114 lb/ft^{3}\right) \left(11,500 yd^{3}\right) \left\lgroup\frac{3ft}{1yd} \right\rgroup ^{3}=35.4\times 10^{6} lb\)

Based on Equation 4.1 \(w=\frac{W_{w}}{W_{s}} \times 100\)%, the weight of water to be added is the required increase in the moisture content, \(Δw\) times the dry weight:

\(\left(W_{w}\right) _{added}=\Delta w W_{s}=\left(0.128 – 0.091\right) \left(35.4\times 10^{6} lb\right) =1.31\times 10^{6} lb\)

Water has a unit weight of 8.34 lb/gal, so:

\(V_{w}=\frac{W_{w}}{\gamma _{w}} =\frac{1.31\times 10^{6} lb}{8.34 lb/gal} =157,000 gal\)

Comments

If our estimate of the shrinkage factor was in error, perhaps because the undisturbed samples were not truly representative, and the true shrinkage factor was 10%, the required import volume becomes \(650 yd^{3}\) instead of the computed \(190 yd^{3}\). Such a situation would not be unusual in practice. Although this difference represents an 340 percent increase in the required import quantity, it is only 4 percent of the total fill volume. Therefore, it is important to keep in mind the potential effects of small errors in the computed shrinkage factor.

Sometimes the weight of fills cause significant settlement in the underlying natural soils. Some or all of this settlement may occur during construction, but much of it often occurs after construction. In either case, the earthwork quantity computations will be affected. We will discuss methods of predicting these settlements in Chapters 11 and 12.
