Guest Question.

A pin-connected truss is loaded and supported as shown in Fig. 6-38a. The body W has a mass of 100 kg. Determine the components of the reactions at supports A and B. Neglect the masses of the members of the truss.

Admin
Solution

The tension T in the cable between the body W and the truss is

\(T=mg=100(9.81)=981N\)

A free-body diagram of the truss is shown in Fig. 6-38b. The action of the pin support at A is represented by force components \(A_x\) and Ay. The action of the roller support at B is represented by the force \( B_x\), which acts perpendicular to the vertical surface at B. Since the truss is subjected to a general coplanar force system, three independent equilibrium equations are available to solve for the unknown magnitudes of the three forces \(A_x, A_y\), and \(B_x\).

Solution by Scalar Analysis

Determination of \(B_x\):

\(+\circlearrowleft \sum{M_A}=0: \quad\quad B_x(4)-800(2)-750(4)-981(3)=0 \)

 

\(B_x=+1885.8N \quad\quad B_x=1886N\leftarrow \)

Determination of \(A_x\):

\(+\rightarrow \sum {F_x}=0: \quad\quad A_x+750-B_x=A_x+750-1885.8=0 \)

 

\(A_x=+1135.8N \quad\quad A_x=1136N\rightarrow \)

Determination of \(A_y\):

\(+\uparrow \sum {F_y}=0: \quad\quad A_y-800-981=0 \)

 

\(A_y=+1781N \quad\quad A_y=1781N\uparrow \)

The component Ax can also be determined directly by summing moments about point C.

\(+\searrow \sum {M_C}=0: \quad\quad A_x(4)-800(2)-981(3)=0\)

 

\(A_x=+1135.8N \quad\quad A_x=1136N\rightarrow \)

Solution by Vector Analysis

From \(R=\sum{F_xi}+\sum{F_yj}=0 \quad\quad C=\sum{M_zk}=0\)

The applied loads can be written in Cartesian vector form as

\(F_1=750iN \quad\quad F_2=-800jN \quad \quad F_3=-981j N\)

The reactions can be written as

\(A=A_xi+A_yj \quad\quad B=-B_xi\)

Therefore, from \(R=\sum{F_xi}+\sum{F_yj}=0 \quad\quad C=\sum{M_zk}=0\):

\(R=\sum{F_xi}+\sum{F_yj}\)

 

\(=(A_x-B_x+750)i+(A_y-800-981)j=0\)

Summing moments about point A to minimize the number of unknowns:

\(C= \sum{M_A k}\)

 

\(=r_1\times F_1+r_2\times F_2+r_3\times F_3+r_B\times B\)

 

\(=[(2i+4j)\times (750i)]+[(2i+4j)\times (-800j)]+[(3i+2j)\times (-981j)]+[(6i+4j)\times (-B_xi)]\)

 

\(=-3000k-1600k-2943k+4B_xk=(-7543+4B_x)k=0\)

Equating the coefficients of i, j, and k to zero gives

\(A_x-B_x+750=0\)

 

\(A_y-800-981=0\)

 

\(-7543+4B_x=0\)

Solving simultaneously yields:

\(A_x=1136N \quad \quad A_y=1781N \quad\quad B_x=1886N\)

Therefore:

\(A=1136i+1781jN \quad\quad B=-1886iN\)

The results are obviously identical to those obtained using a scalar analysis. For two-dimensional problems, scalar methods are usually preferred.
