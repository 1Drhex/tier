Guest Question.

(a) Derive an expression for the electron current \(I_{nn}(x) \) in the n‑type material (see Fig. 5.5) of a p‑n junction diode.

(b) Consider a silicon p‑n junction with cross‑sectional area of \(10^{-4} cm^2 \) . The doping concentration, mean lifetime and mobility of an electron in the p‑type silicon at 300°K are given as: \(N_{A}=2.5\times 10^{15} cm^{-3} \) , \(\tau _{n}=10^{-6} \) sec,\(\mu _{n}=1350 cm^2/V \) sec respectively. Similarly, the donor concentration, mean lifetime and mobility a hole in the n‑ type silicon at 300°K are given as: \(N_{D}=5\times 10^{16} cm^{-3} \) , \(\tau _{p}=10^{-7} \) sec, and \(\mu _{p}=325 cm^2/V \) sec respectively. Assume that the intrinsic car‑ rier concentration of silicon \(n_{i}=1.5\times 10^{10}cm^{-3}\) at 300°K. Starting from Eq. (5.27), \(I_{o}\equiv \frac{AeD_{p}p_{no}}{L_{p}}+\frac{AeD_{n}n_{po}}{L_{n}} \) , express the reverse saturation current \(I_{o}\) in terms of the parameters described above for the diode. Also calculate the reverse saturation current for the above specified values of the diode parameters. Calculate the total current (\(I\)) for a forward bias voltage of 0.6 V.

Admin
Solution

(a) Since the total current remains constant in both the n and p materials for all values of x, the total current in the n‑region can be written as

\(I=I_{nn}(x)+I_{pn}(x)\)

Using Eqs (5.19), \(I_{pn}(x)=\frac{AeD_{p}p_{n}(0)}{L_{p}}exp(-x/L_{p}) \) , (5.23), \(P_{n}(0)=p_{no}[exp(V/V_{T})-1] \), (5.26) , \(I=I_{o}[exp(V/V_{T})-1] \) , and (5.27) , \(I_{o}\equiv \frac{AeD_{p}p_{no}}{L_{p}}+\frac{AeD_{n}n_{po}}{L_{n}}\) in the above equation we can get

\(I_{nn}(x)=I-I_{pn}(x)\)

\(=\left\lgroup\frac{AeD_{p}p_{no}}{L_{p}}+\frac{AeD_{n}n_{po}}{L_{n}} \right\rgroup [exp(V/V_{T})-1]-\frac{AeD_{p}p_{no}\varepsilon ^{-x/L_{p}}}{L_{p}}[exp(V/V_{T})-1] \)

\(=\frac{AeD_{p}p_{no}}{L_{p}}[1-exp(-x/L_{p})][exp(V/V_{T})-1]+\frac{AeD_{p}p_{no}}{L_{p}}[exp(V/V_{T})-1] \)

Note that the electron current in the n‑side at the junction is given by

\(I_{nn}(O)=\frac{AeD_{n}n_{po}}{L_{n}}[exp(V/V_{T})-1]\)

(b) Substituting \(L_{p}=\sqrt{D_{p}\tau _{p}} \) and \(L_{n}=\sqrt{D_{n}\tau _{p}} \) in Eq. (5.27), the reverse saturation current can be written as

\(I_{o}=Ae\left\lgroup p_{no}\sqrt{\frac{D_{p}}{\tau _{p}} }+n_{po}\sqrt{\frac{D_{n}}{\tau _{n}} } \right\rgroup \)

Using the Einstein’s equation from Eq. (4.33), \(\frac{D_{p}}{\mu _{p}}=\frac{D_{n}}{\mu _{n}}=V_{T} \) , the diffusion constants for electrons and holes can be given by

\(D_{p}=\left\lgroup \frac{\overline{k}T }{e} \right\rgroup \mu _{p} \) and \(D_{n}=\left\lgroup \frac{\overline{k}T }{e} \right\rgroup \mu _{n} \)

respectively, where \(\overline{k}=1.381\times 10^{-23} J/^{\circ }K \) is the Boltzmann constant. Note that \(\frac{\overline{k}T }{e}=\frac{T}{11586}\approx \frac{T}{11600} \) as discussed in Sec. 4.7. Thus at T = 300°K, the diffusion constants of holes and electrons in the given n‑ and p‑type silicon materials are obtained as

\(D_{p}=(0.0259 V)\times (325 cm^2/V sec) = 8.41 cm^2/sec \)

and

\(D_{n}=(0.0259 V)\times (1350 cm^2/V sec) = 34.97 cm^2/sec \)

respectively. However, using the above relations of \(D_{p} \) and \(D_{n} \) in the exprssion of \(I_{o} \), we get

\(I_{o}= \left(A\sqrt{e\overline{k}T }\right) \left\lgroup p_{no}\sqrt{\frac{\mu _{p}}{\tau _{p}} } +n_{po}\sqrt{\frac{\mu _{n}}{\tau _{n}} } \right\rgroup \)

Now,substituting \(p_{no}=\frac{n^2_{i}}{N_{D}} \) and \(n_{po}=\frac{n^2_{i}}{N_{A}} \) [see Eq. (4.28)] in the above equation, we can finally write the desired expression for \(I_{o} \) as

\(I_{o}= \left(A\sqrt{e\overline{k}T }\right) \left\lgroup \frac{1}{N_{D}} \sqrt{\frac{\mu _{p}}{\tau _{p}} } + \frac{1}{N_{A}} \sqrt{\frac{\mu _{n}}{\tau _{n}} } \right\rgroup n^2_{i}\)

Putting the values of \(A=10^{-4} cm^2\) , T = 300°K , \(e=1.60\times 10^{-19} C\) , \(N_{A}=2.5\times 10^{15} cm^{-3}\) , \(\tau _{n}=10^{-6}\) sec , \(\mu _{n}=1350 cm^2/V\) sec , \(N_{D}=5\times 10^{16} cm^{-3}\) , \(\tau _{p}=10^{-7}\) sec , \(\mu _{p}=235 cm^2/V\) sec and \(n_{i}=1.5\times 10^{10} cm^{-3}\) in the above equation, the reverse saturation current can be given by

\(I_{o}=\left(10^{-4}\times \sqrt{(1.60\times 10^{-19})\times (1.381\times 10^{-23})\times 300} \right)\times \left\lgroup \frac{1}{5\times 10^{16}}\sqrt{\frac{325}{10^{-7}} }+\frac{1}{2.5\times 10^{15}} \sqrt{\frac{1350}{10^{-6}} } \right\rgroup \times \left(2.25\times 10^{20} \right) \)

\(=9.17\times 10^{-15} \) A

The forward bias current at V = 0.6 V can be obtained from Eq. (5.26):

\(I=I_{o}exp\left\lgroup\frac{0.6}{0.0259} \right\rgroup -1 \)

\(=1.05\times 10^{-4}A =10.5 mA\)

Note that we have used \(V_{T}=0.0259\) V in the above calculation.
