Guest Question.

A solid aluminum member is subjected to concentric axial load P = 3 kN, as shown in Fig. 11.44. The length of the member is L = 180 mm, and the cross section is rectangular with dimensions b = 20 mm and h = 4 mm. The aluminum has a modulus of elasticity E = 75 GPa and a compressive yield strength Syc = 150 MPa. Use the AISC recommended end-condition constant for fixed-pinned end conditions.

(a) Is the member an Euler column or a Johnson column?
(b) Determine the maximum load, Pcr, that can be applied before buckling occurs.
(c) Determine the factor of safety guarding against buckling of the member.

Admin
Solution

The cross-sectional area of the member is

\(A=b h=(0.020 m )(0.004 m )=0.000080 m ^{2}\)

The second moment of area (Table 4, Appendix A) is

\(I=\frac{b h^{3}}{12}=\frac{(0.020 m )(0.004 m )^{3}}{12}=1.067 \times 10^{-10} m ^{4}\)

Note here that the second moment of area has the smallest value allowable, since the member will buckle in the weakest plane. For this example, the member will buckle in the plane of Fig. 11.44.

The radius of gyration of the member is

\(k=\sqrt{\frac{I}{A}}=\sqrt{\frac{1.067 \times 10^{-10} m ^{4}}{0.000080 m ^{2}}}=1.155 mm\)

Using the AISC recommended end-condition effective length factor for fixed-pinned end conditions (that is, Î± = Leff/L = 0.707, Fig. 11.29), the end-condition constant is

\(C=\frac{1}{\alpha^{2}}=\frac{1}{0.707^{2}}=2\)

(a) The slenderness ratio of the member is

\(S_{ r }=\frac{L}{k}=\frac{0.180 m }{0.001155 m }=155.84\)

The slenderness ratio at the point of tangency is

\(\left(S_{ r }\right)_{D}=\pi \sqrt{\frac{2 E C}{S_{y c}}}=\pi \sqrt{\frac{2\left(75 \times 10^{9} Pa \right) 2}{150 \times 10^{6} Pa }}=140.5\)

Since the slenderness ratio \(S_{ r } \geq\left(S_{ r }\right)_{D},\) the member is an Euler column.

(b) Using Eulerâs column formula, the critical load is

\(P_{ cr }=A\left[\frac{C \pi^{2} E}{S_{ r }^{2}}\right]=\left(0.000080 m ^{2}\right)\left[\frac{2 \pi^{2}\left(75 \times 10^{9} Pa \right)}{155.84^{2}}\right]=4877 N\)

(c) The factor of safety guarding against buckling is

\(N=\frac{P_{ cr }}{P}=\frac{4877 N }{3000 N } 1.63\)

Therefore, the member should not fail in buckling.
