Guest Question.

A vertical hanger supporting the deck of a suspension bridge is formed from a steel cable 25 m long and having a diameter of 7.5 mm. If the density of the steel is 7850 kg/m³ and the load at the lower end of the hanger is 5 kN, determine the maximum stress in the cable and its elongation. Young’s modulus E = 200 000 N/mm².

Admin
Solution

The weight of the cable is \( =\left(\pi \times 7.5^{2} / 4\right) \times 25 \times 10^{3} \times 7850 \times 9.81 / 10^{9}=85.05 N \) and the total load on the cable is therefore \( =85.05+5 \times 10^{3}=5085.05 N \).

The tensile stress in the cable at its point of support is then, from Eq. (7.1)

\( \sigma=\frac{P}{A} \) (7.1)

 

\( \sigma=\frac{5085.05}{\left(\pi \times \frac{7.5^{2}}{4}\right)}=115.1 N / mm ^{2} \)

Consider the cable shown in Fig. S.7.8. The weight of a length h is equal to ρAh where ρ is the density of the cable and A its area of cross section. The stress in the cable at the section at the top of the length h is then ρh from Eq. (7.1).

The extension of the elemental length δh due to the self-weight of the cable is then, from Eqs (7.4) and (7.7), given by

\(\varepsilon=\frac{\delta}{L_{0}}\) (7.4)

 

\( \sigma=E \varepsilon \) (7.7)

 

\(ext =\left(\frac{\rho h}{E}\right) \delta h\)

The extension of the complete cable due to self-weight is therefore

\( ext =\int_{0}^{L}\left(\frac{\rho h}{E}\right) d h=\frac{\rho L^{2}}{2 E}=\frac{7850 \times 9.81\left(25 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2}}{2 \times 200000 \times 10^{9}}=0.12 mm \)

The extension due to the load is, from Eq. (7.28), given by

\( \Delta=\frac{P L_{0}}{A E}\) (7.28)

 

\( \operatorname{ext}( load )=\frac{5 \times 10^{3} \times 25 \times 10^{3}}{\left(\pi \times \frac{7.5^{2}}{4}\right) \times 200000}=14.15 mm \)

The total extension is then 14.15 + 0.12=14.27 mm.
