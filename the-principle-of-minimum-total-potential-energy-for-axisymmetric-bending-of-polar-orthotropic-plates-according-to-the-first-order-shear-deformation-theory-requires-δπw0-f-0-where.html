Guest Question.

The principle of minimum total potential energy for axisymmetric bending of polar orthotropic plates according to the first-order shear deformation theory requires \(\delta\Pi (w_{0},\phi )\) = 0, where
\(\delta \Pi (w,\Psi )=2\int_{b}^{a}\left [ \left ( D_{11}\frac{\mathrm{d} \Psi }{\mathrm{d} r}+D_{12}\frac{\Psi }{r} \right )\frac{\mathrm{d}\delta \Psi }{\mathrm{d} r}+\frac{1}{r}\left ( D_{12}\frac{\mathrm{d} \Psi }{\mathrm{d} r}+D_{22}\frac{\Psi }{r} \right )\delta \Psi +A_{55}\left ( \Psi +\frac{\mathrm{d} w}{\mathrm{d} r} \right )\left ( \delta \Psi +\frac{\mathrm{d} \delta w}{\mathrm{d} r} \right )-q\delta w \right ]r dr\) (1)
where b is the inner radius and a the outer radius of the radial element. Derive the displacement finite element model of the equations. In particular, show that the finite element model is of the form (i.e., define the matrix coefficients of the following equation)
\(\begin{bmatrix}[K^{11}] &[K^{12}] \\ [K^{12}]^{T}& [K^{22}]\end{bmatrix}\begin{Bmatrix}\left \{ w \right \}\\ \left \{ \Psi \right \}\end{Bmatrix}=\begin{Bmatrix}\left \{ F^{1} \right \}\\ \left \{ F^{2} \right \}\end{Bmatrix}\) (2)

 

Admin
Solution

Clearly, the given variational statement is equivalent to the following weak forms:

0 = \(\int_{b}^{a}A_{55}\left [ \frac{\mathrm{d} \delta w}{\mathrm{d} r}\left ( \Psi +\frac{\mathrm{d} w}{\mathrm{d} r} \right )-q\delta w \right ]r dr\) (3)

0 = \(\int_{b}^{a}\left [ \frac{\mathrm{d} \delta \Psi }{\mathrm{d} r} \left ( D_{11}\frac{\mathrm{d} \Psi }{\mathrm{d} r}+D_{12}\frac{\Psi }{r} \right )+\frac{1}{r}\delta \Psi \left ( D_{12}\frac{\mathrm{d} \Psi }{\mathrm{d} r}+D_{22}\frac{\Psi }{r} \right )+A_{55}\delta \Psi \left ( \Psi +\frac{\mathrm{d} w}{\mathrm{d} r} \right )\right ]r dr\) (4)

The variations \(\delta w=v_{1}\) and \(\delta \Psi =v_{2}\) are the weight functions of the weak forms.

Assuming approximation of the form

\(w(r)\approx w_{h}^{e}=\sum_{i=1}^{m}w_{i}^{(1)}(r)\) , \(\Psi (r)\approx \Psi _{h}^{e}=\sum_{i=1}^{m}\Psi _{i}^{(2)}(r)\) (5)

in (3) and (4), we obtain the finite element model in Eq. (2), with the matrix coefficients

\(K_{ij}^{11}=\int_{r_{a}}^{r_{b}}A_{55}\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{i}^{(1)}}{\mathrm{d} r}\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{j}^{(1)}}{\mathrm{d} r}r dr\) , \(K_{ij}^{12}=\int_{r_{a}}^{r_{b}}A_{55}\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{i}^{(1)}}{\mathrm{d} r}\psi _{j}^{(2)}r dr\)

\(K_{ij}^{22}=\int_{r_{a}}^{r_{b}}\left [ D_{11}\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{i}^{(2)}}{\mathrm{d} r}\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{j}^{(2)}}{\mathrm{d} r}+\frac{1}{r}D_{12}\left ( \psi _{i}^{(2)}\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{j}^{(2)}}{\mathrm{d} r}+\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{i}^{(2)}}{\mathrm{d} r}\psi _{j}^{(2)} \right )+\frac{1}{r^{2}}D_{22}\psi _{i}^{(2)}\psi _{j}^{(2)}+A_{55}\psi _{i}^{(2)}\psi _{j}^{(2)} \right ]r dr\) \(F_{i}^{1}=\int_{r_{a}}^{r_{b}}q\psi _{i}^{(1)}r dr +\psi _{i}^{(1)}(r_{a})Q_{1}+\psi _{i}^{(1)}(r_{b})Q_{3}\)

\(F_{i}^{2}=\psi _{i}^{(2)}(r_{a})Q_{2}+\psi _{i}^{(2)}(r_{b})Q_{4}\) (6)
