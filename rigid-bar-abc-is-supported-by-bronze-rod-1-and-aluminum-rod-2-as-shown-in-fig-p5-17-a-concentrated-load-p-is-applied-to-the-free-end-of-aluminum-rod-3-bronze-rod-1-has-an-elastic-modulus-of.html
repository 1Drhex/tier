Guest Question.

Rigid bar ABC is supported by bronze rod (1) and aluminum rod (2), as shown in Fig P5-17. A concentrated load P is applied to the free end of aluminum rod (3). Bronze rod (1) has an elastic modulus of \(E_{1}\) = 15,000 ksi and a diameter of \(d_{1}\) = 0.50 in. Aluminum rod (2) has an elastic modulus of \(E_{2}\) = 10,000 ksi and a diameter of \(d_{2}\) = 0.75 in. Aluminum rod (3) has a diameter of \(d_{3}\) = 1.0 in. The yield strength of the bronze is 48 ksi and the yield strength of the aluminum is 40 ksi.
(a) Determine the magnitude of load P that can safely be applied to the structure if a minimum factor of safety of 1.67 is required.
(b) Determine the deflection of point D for the load determined in part (a).
(c) The pin used at B has an ultimate shear strength of 54 ksi. If a factor of safety of 3.0 is required for this double shear pin connection, determine the minimum pin diameter that can be used at B.

Admin
Solution

Before beginning, the cross-sectional areas of the three rods can be calculated from the specified diameters:

\(A_{1}=0.196350 in .{ }^{2} \quad A_{2}=0.441786 in .{ }^{2} \quad A_{3}=0.785398 in .^{2}\)

The allowable stress of the bronze is

\(\sigma_{\text {allow }, 1}=\frac{\sigma_{Y, \text { bronze }}}{ FS }=\frac{48 ksi }{1.67}=28.742515 ksi\)

and the allowable stress of the aluminum is

\(\sigma_{\text {allow }, 2}=\sigma_{\text {allow }, 3}=\frac{\sigma_{Y, a l u m}}{F S}=\frac{40 ksi }{1.67}=23.952096 ksi\)

 

(a) From a FBD cut through rod (3), equilibrium requires that the internal force in rod (3) is \(F_{3}=P\).

From a FBD of the rigid bar, write two equilibrium equations:

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma F_{y}=F_{1}+F_{2}-F_{3}=F_{1}+F_{2}-P=0 (a)\\&\Sigma M_{A}=(4 ft ) F_{2}-(2.5 ft ) F_{3}=(4 ft ) F_{2}-(2.5 ft ) P=0 (b)\end{aligned}\)

Solve Eq. (b) for \(F_{2}\) in terms of the unknown load P:

\(F_{2}=\frac{2.5 ft }{4 ft } P=0.6250 P (c)\)

Backsubstitute this expression into Eq. (a) to obtain \(F_{1}\) in terms of the unknown load P:

\(F_{1}=P-F_{2}=P-0.6250 P=0.3750 P (d)\)

Rearrange Eqs. (c) and (d) to express P in terms of \(F_{2} \text { and } F_{1}\), respectively:

\(\begin{aligned}&P=1.6000 F_{2} (e) \\&P=2.6667 F_{1} (f)\end{aligned}\)

Based on the allowable stress for the bronze, the maximum allowable force that can be supported by rod (1) is:

\(F_{1} \leq \sigma_{\text {allow }, 1} A_{1}=(28.742515 ksi )\left(0.196350 in .^{2}\right)=5.643579 kips\)

Thus, from Eq. (f), the corresponding maximum load P is:

\(P \leq 2.6667 F_{1}=2.6667(5.643579 kips )=15.049545 kips (g)\)

Next, the maximum allowable force that can be supported by rod (2) based on the allowable stress for the aluminum is:

\(F_{2} \leq \sigma_{\text {allow }, 2} A_{2}=(23.952096 ksi )\left(0.441786 in .^{2}\right)=10.581711 \text { kips }\)

which leads to a corresponding maximum load P from Eq. (e):

\(P \leq 1.6000 F_{2}=1.6000(10.581711 kips )=16.930738 \text { kips } (h)\)

Finally, we should also check the capacity of rod (3):

\(F_{3} \leq \sigma_{\text {allow }, 3} A_{3}=(23.952096 ksi )\left(0.785398 in .^{2}\right)=18.811931 kips\)

which means that

\(P \leq 18.811931 \text { kips } (i)\)

From a comparison of Eqs. (g), (h), and (i), the maximum load that may be applied to the structure is

\(P_{\max }=15.049545 kips =15.05 kips\)

Based on this maximum load P, the internal forces in the three rods are:

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{1}=0.3750 P=0.3750(15.049545 kips )=5.643579 kips \\&F_{2}=0.6250 P=0.6250(15.049545 kips )=9.405966 kips \\&F_{3}=P=15.049545 kips\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Next, determine the deformations in rods (1), (2), and (3):

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}=\frac{(5.643579 kips )(6 ft )(12 in. / ft )}{\left(0.196350 in .^{2}\right)(15,000 ksi )}=0.137964 in .\)

 

\(\delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}=\frac{(9.405966 kips )(8 ft )(12 in. / ft )}{\left(0.441786 in .^{2}\right)(10,000 ksi )}=0.204391 in.\)

 

\(\delta_{3}=\frac{F_{3} L_{3}}{A_{3} E_{3}}=\frac{(15.049545 kips )(3 ft )(12 in. / ft )}{\left(0.785398 in .^{2}\right)(10,000 ksi )}=0.068982 in.\)

 

Since the connections at A and C are perfect, the rigid bar deflections at these joints are equal to the deformations of rods (1) and (2), respectively:

\(v_{A}=\delta_{1}=0.137964 \text { in. } \quad \text { and } \quad v_{C}=\delta_{2}=0.204391 \text { in. }\)

The rigid bar deflection at B can be determined from similar triangles:

\(\frac{v_{C}-v_{A}}{4 ft }=\frac{v_{B}-v_{A}}{2.5 ft }\)

Solve this expression for \(v_{B}\):

\(\begin{aligned}v_{B} &=\frac{2.5 ft }{4 ft }\left(v_{C}-v_{A}\right)+v_{A} \\&=\frac{2.5 ft }{4 ft }(0.204391 in .-0.137964 in .)+0.137964 in . \\&=(0.6250)(0.066427 in .)+0.137964 in . \\&=0.179481 in .\end{aligned}\)

The deflection of joint D is equal to the deflection of the rigid bar at B plus the deformation in rod (3):

\(v_{D}=v_{B}+\delta_{3}=0.179481 in .+0.068982 in .=0.248463 in .=0.248 in . \downarrow\)

 

(c) The pin at B has an allowable shear stress of

\(\tau_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\tau_{U}}{ FS }=\frac{54 ksi }{3.0}=18 ksi\)

The force tending to shear this pin is equal to the load P = 15.049545 kips. The shear area \(A_{V}\) required for this pin is thus

\(A_{V} \geq \frac{V}{\tau_{\text {allow }}}=\frac{15.049545 kips }{18 ksi }=0.836086 in .^{2}\)

Since the pin is used in a double shear connection, the shear area is equal to twice the cross-sectional area of the pin:

\(A_{V}=2 A_{\text {pin }}=2 \times \frac{\pi}{4} d_{\text {pin }}^{2}\)

and so the minimum pin diameter is

\(2 \times \frac{\pi}{4} d_{\text {pin }}^{2} \geq 0.836086 \text { in. }^{2} \quad \therefore d_{\text {pin }} \geq 0.729568 \text { in. }=0.730 \text { in. }\)
