Guest Question.

Mode Properties

A TM wave propagating in a dielectric-filled waveguide of unknown permittivity has a magnetic field with y component given by

\(H_y= 6 cos(25πx)sin(100πy) × sin(1.5π × 10^{10}t − 10^9πz) (mA/m).\)

If the guide dimensions are a = 2b = 4 cm, determine: (a) the mode numbers,(b) the relative permittivity of the material in the guide, (c) the phase velocity, and (d) obtain an expression for \(E_x\).

Admin
Solution

(a) By comparison with the expression for \(\tilde{H_y} \) given by Eq. (8.104d), we deduce that the argument of x is (mπ/a) and the argument of y is (nπ/b). Hence,

\( \tilde{H}_y = \frac{-jw\epsilon }{k_c^2} \left(\frac{m\pi }{a} \right) E_0 \cos \left( \frac{m\pi x}{a} \right) \sin \left(\frac{n\pi \pi y}{b} \right) e^{-j\beta z} . \) (8.104d)

\(25π =\frac{mπ}{4 × 10^{−2}}, 100π =\frac{nπ}{2 × 10^{−2}} \)

which yield m = 1 and n = 2. Therefore, the mode is \(TM_{12}\).

(b) The second sine function in the expression for \(H_y \) represents sin(ωt – βz), which means that

\(ω = 1.5π × 10^{10} (rad/s), or f = 7.5 GHz,\)

 

\(β = 109π (rad/m)\)

By rewriting Eq. (8.105) so as to obtain an expression for \(\epsilon _r = \epsilon /\epsilon_0\) in terms of the other quantities, we have

\( \beta =\sqrt{k^2 -k^2_c} \\ \ \ = \sqrt{w^2\mu \epsilon – \left( \frac{m\pi }{a}\right)^2 – \left( \frac{n\pi }{b} \right)^2 } . \) (8.105)

\(\epsilon _r = \frac{c^2}{ω^2} \left[\beta ^2 +(\frac{mπ}{a} )^2 +(\frac{nπ}{b} )^2\right]\)

 

where c is the speed of light. Inserting the available values, we obtain

\(\epsilon _r =\frac{(3 × 10^8)^2}{(1.5π × 10^{10})^2} . [(109π)^2 +(\frac{\pi }{4\times 10^{-2}} )^2 +(\frac{2\pi }{2\times 10^{-2}} )^2]=9\)

(c)

\(u_p =\frac{\omega }{\beta } = \frac{1.5π × 10^{10}}{109π} 1.38 × 10^8 m/s\)

 

which is slower than the speed of light. However, as explained later in Section 8-10, the phase velocity in a waveguide may exceed c, but the velocity with which energy is carried down the guide is the group velocity \(u_g\), which is never greater than c.
(d) From Eq. (8.109),

\( Z_{TM}= \frac{\tilde{E_x} }{\tilde{H_y} } = – \frac{\tilde{E_y} }{\tilde{H_x} } =\frac{\beta \eta }{k} =\eta \sqrt{1- \left(\frac{f_{mn}}{f} \right)^2 } \) (8.109)

\(Z_{TM} = η\sqrt{ 1 − (f_{12}/f )^2}\)

Application of Eq. (8.106) yields \(f_{12} = 5.15 GHz\) for the \(TM_{12}\) mode. Using that in the expression for \( Z_{TM}\), in addition to \(f = 7.5 GHz and η = \sqrt{ μ/\epsilon }= (\sqrt{ μ_0/\epsilon _0)}/\sqrt{\epsilon_r} = 377/\sqrt{ 9} = 125.67 \Omega \), gives

\(Z_{TM} = 91.3 \Omega .\)

\( f_{m n } = \frac{u_{p_0}}{2} \sqrt{\left( \frac{m}{a} \right)^2 +\left(\frac{n}{b} \right)^2 } , \\ \quad \quad \text{(TE and TM)}\) (8.106)

Hence,

\(E_x = Z_{TM}H_y\)

 

\(= 91.3 × 6 cos(25πx) sin(100πy) × sin (1.5π × 10^{10}t − 109πz) (mV/m)\)

 

\(= 0.55 cos(25πx)sin(100πy) × sin (1.5π × 10^{10}t − 109πz) (V/m).\)
