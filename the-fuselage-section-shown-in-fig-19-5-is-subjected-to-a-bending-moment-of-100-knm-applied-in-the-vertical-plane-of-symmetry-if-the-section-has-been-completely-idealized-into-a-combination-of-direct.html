Guest Question.

The fuselage section shown in Fig. 19.5 is subjected to a bending moment of 100 kNm applied in the vertical plane of symmetry. If the section has been completely idealized into a combination of direct stress carrying booms and shear stress only carrying panels, determine the direct stress in each boom.

Admin
Solution

The section has Cy as an axis of symmetry and resists a bending moment \(M_{x}=100 kNm\). Equation (15.17) therefore reduces to

 

\(\sigma_{z}=\left(\frac{M_{y} I_{x x}-M_{x} I_{x y}}{I_{x x} I_{y y}-I_{x y}^{2}}\right) x+\left(\frac{M_{x} I_{y y}-M_{y} I_{x y}}{I_{x x} I_{y y}-I_{x y}^{2}}\right) y\) (15.17)

The origin of axes Cxy coincides with the position of the centroid of the direct stress carrying area, which, in this case, is the centroid of the boom areas. Thus, taking moments of area about boom 9,

 

\((6 \times 640+6 \times 600+2 \times 620+2 \times 850) \bar{y}\) \(=640 \times 1,200+2 \times 600 \times 1140+2 \times 600 \times 960+2 \times 600 \times 768\) \(+2 \times 620 \times 565+2 \times 640 \times 336+2 \times 640 \times 144+2 \times 850 \times 38\)

\(\bar{y}=540 mm\)
