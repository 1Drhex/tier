Guest Question.

The inside temperature of a furnace wall (k = 1.35 W/m.K), 200 mm thick, is 1400°C. The heat transfer coefficient at the outside surface is a function of temperature difference and is given by \(h = 7.85 + 0.08 ∆T (W/m^{2}.K)\) where ∆T is the temperature difference between outside wall surface and surroundings. Determine the rate of heat transfer per unit area, if the surrounding temperature is 40°C.

Admin
Solution

Given : A furnace wall exposed to convection environment on one side.
k = 1.35 W/m.K
L = 200 mm = 0.2 m
\(T_1 = 1400°C\)
\(T_∞ = 40°C\)
\(h = 7.85 + 0.08 (∆T) (W/m^{2}.K)\)

To find : Heat flux.
Analysis : Steady state heat transfer rate per unit area
\(q = \frac{k (T_1 – T_2)}{L} = h(T_2 – T_∞)\)

 

or \(\frac{1.35\times (1400 – T_2 )}{0.2} = [7.85 + 0.08 (T_2 – 40)] × (T_2 – 40)\)

 

or \(9450 – 6.75 T_2 = 7.85 T_2 – 314 + 0.08 (T_2 – 40)^2\)

 

\(= 7.85 T_2 – 314 + 0.08 × (T_2^{2} – 80 T_2 + 1600)\)

 

\(= 7.85 T_2 – 314 + 0.08 T_2^{2} – 6.4 T_2 + 128\)

 

Rearranging, \(0.08 T_2^{2} + 8.2 T_2 – 9636 = 0\)

 

or \(T_2 = \frac{- 8.2 + \sqrt{(8.2 )^{2}+ 4\times 0.08 \times 9636}}{2\times 0.08}\)
= 299.57°C
The heat flow rate per unit area
\(q = \frac{k}{L }(T_1 – T_2)\)

 

\(= \frac{1.35}{0.2 }× (1400 – 299.57)\)
= 7427.88 W.
