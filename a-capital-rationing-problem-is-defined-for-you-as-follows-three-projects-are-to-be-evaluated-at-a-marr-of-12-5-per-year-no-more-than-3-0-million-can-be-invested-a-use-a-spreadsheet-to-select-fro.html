Guest Question.

A capital rationing problem is defined for you as follows: Three projects are to be evaluated at a \(MARR\) of \(12.5\)% per year. No more than $\(3.0\) million can be invested.

\((a)\) Use a spreadsheet to select from the independent projects.

\((b)\) If the life of project \(3\) can be increased from \(5\) to \(10\) years for the same $\(1\) million investment, use Goal Seek to determine the \(NCF\) in year \(1\) for project \(3\) alone to have the same \(PW\) as the best bundle in part \(( a )\). All other estimates remain the same. With these new \(NCF\) and life estimates, what are the best projects for investment?

Estimated NCF, $ per Year
Project Investment,$ M Life,Years Year 1 Gradient after Year 1
1 -0.9 6 250,000 -5000
2 -2.1 10 485,000 +5000
3 -1 5 485,000 20%
Admin
Solution

\((a)\) Spreadsheet shows the solution. Select projects \(1\) and \(2\) for an investment of $\(3.0\) million and \(PW = \)$\(753,139.\)

 

\((b)\) The Goal Seek target cell is \(D17\) to equal $\(753,139\). Result is a reduced year-one \(NCF\) for project \(3\) of $\(145,012\). However, with these changes for project \(3\), the best selection is now projects \(1\) and \(3\) with \(PW =\) $\(822,830.\)
