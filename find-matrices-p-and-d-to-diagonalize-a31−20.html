Guest Question.

Find matrices P and D to diagonalize A=[31−20].

Admin
Solution

We start by finding the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of A. Beginning with the eigenvalues, we have

det(A−λI2)=|3−λ1−2−λ| =(3−λ)(−λ)−(−2)=λ2−3λ+2=(λ−2)(λ−1)

Thus we have eigenvalues \(λ_{1}=1 \) and \(λ_{2}=2 \). Starting with \(λ_{1}=1 \), the homogeneous system \((A − I_{2})u = 0\) has augmented matrix and echelon form

[21−2−1|00]R1+R2→R2∼[2100|00]

Back substitution gives us the associated eigenvector

u1=[1−2]

For \(λ_{2}=2 \), the system \((A −2 I_{2})u = 0\) has augmented matrix and echelon form

[11−2−2|00]2R1+R2→R2∼[1100|00]

This time back substitution gives us the eigenvector

u2=[1−1]

Now we define P and D, with

P=[u1u2]=[11−2−1]andD=[λ100λ2]=[1002]

To check our work, we find \(P^{-1} \) using the Quick Formula, and then compute the product

PDP−1=[11−2−1] [1002] [−1−121]=[31−20]=A
