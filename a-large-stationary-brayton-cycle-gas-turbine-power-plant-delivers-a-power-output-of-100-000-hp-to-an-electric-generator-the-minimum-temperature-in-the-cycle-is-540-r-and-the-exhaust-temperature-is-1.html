Guest Question.

A large stationary Brayton cycle gas-turbine power plant delivers a power output of 100 000 hp to an electric generator. The minimum temperature in the cycle is 540 R, and the exhaust temperature is 1350 R. The minimum pressure in the cycle is 1 atm, and the compressor pressure ratio is 14 to 1. Calculate the power output of the turbine, the fraction of the turbine output required to drive the compressor and the thermal efficiency of the cycle?

 

Admin
Solution

Brayton:

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{w}_{ NET }=100000 hp \\& P _{1}=1 atm , T _{1}=540 R \\& P _{2} / P _{1}=14, T _{3}=2900 R\end{aligned}\)

Solve using constant \(C _{ P 0}\):

Compression in compressor: \(s _{2}= s _{1} \Rightarrow\) Implemented in Eq.6.23

\(\begin{aligned}&\rightarrow T _{2}= T _{1}\left(\frac{ P _{2}}{ P _{1}}\right)^{\frac{ k -1}{ k }}=540(14)^{0.2857}=1148.6 R \\& w _{ C }= h _{2}- h _{1}= C _{ P 0}\left( T _{2}- T _{1}\right)=0.24(1148.6-540)=146.1 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\)

Expansion in turbine: \(s _{4}= s _{3} \quad \Rightarrow\) Implemented in Eq.6.23

\(T _{3}= T _{4}\left(\frac{ P _{3}}{ P _{4}}\right)^{\frac{ k -1}{ k }}=1350(14)^{0.2857}=2869.3 R\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}& w _{ T }= h _{3}- h _{4}= C _{ P 0}\left( T _{3}- T _{4}\right)=0.24(2869.3-1350)=364.6 Btu / lbm \\& w _{ NET }= w _{ T }- w _{ C }=364.6-146.1=218.5 Btu / lbm \\&\dot{ m }=\dot{ W }_{ NET } / w _{ NET }=100000 \times 2544 / 218.5=1164302 lbm / h \\&\dot{ W }_{ T }=\dot{ m } w _{ T }= 1 6 6 8 6 5 hp , \quad w _{ C } / w _{ T }= 0 . 4 0\end{aligned}\)

Energy input is from the combustor

\(\begin{aligned}& q _{ H }= C _{ P 0}\left( T _{3}- T _{2}\right)=0.24(2869.3-1148.6)=413 Btu / lbm \\&\eta_{ TH }= w _{ NET } / q _{ H }=218.5 / 413= 0 . 5 2 9\end{aligned}\)
