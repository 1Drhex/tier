Guest Question.

A fluid has velocity components of \(u = (6y + t) ft/s\) and \(v = (2tx) ft/s \)where x and y are in feet and t is in seconds. Determine the magnitude of acceleration of a particle passing through the point (1 ft, 2 ft), when t = 1 s.

Admin
Solution

For two dimensional flow, the Eulerian description gives
\(a = \frac{\partial V}{\partial t} + u \frac{\partial V}{\partial x} + v \frac{\partial V}{\partial y} \)

Writing the scalar component of this equation along the x and y axes,

\(a_x = \frac{\partial u}{\partial t} + u \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} + v\frac{\partial u}{\partial y} \)

= 1+ (6y + t) (0) + (2tx) (6)

\(= (1+ 12tx) ft/s^2 \)

 

\(a_y =\frac{\partial v}{\partial t} + u \frac{\partial v}{\partial x} + v\frac{\partial v}{\partial y} \)

= 2x + (6y + t) (2t) + (2tx) (0)

\(= (2x + 12ty + 2t^2) ft/s^2 \)

When t = 1 s, x = 1 ft and y = 2 ft, then

\(a_x = [1 + 12(1)(1)] = 13 ft/s^2 \)

 

\(a_{y} = 2(1) + 12(1)(2) + 2(1^2) = 28 ft/s^{2}\)

Thus, the magnitude of the acceleration is

\(a = \sqrt{a_x^2 + a_y^2} = \sqrt{(13 ft/s^2)^2 + (28 ft/s^2)^2} = 30.9 ft /s^2 \)
