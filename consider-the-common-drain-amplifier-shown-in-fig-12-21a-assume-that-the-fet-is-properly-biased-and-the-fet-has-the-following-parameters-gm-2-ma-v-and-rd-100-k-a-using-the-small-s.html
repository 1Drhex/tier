Guest Question.

Consider the common-drain amplifier shown in Fig.12.21a. Assume that the FET is properly biased and the FET has the following parameters: gm = 2 mA/V and rd = 100 K.

(a) Using the small-signal low-frequency model shown in 12.9a, derive an expression for the gain of the amplifier.

(b) Compute the ac drain current for Vi = 2 V(rms), Rs = 4.7 K and Rs = 10 K

(c) Compute the output voltage and gain of the amplifier for part-(b).

Admin
Solution

(a) Assuming that all the externally connected capacitors are short-circuited under ac condition, the ac small-signal low-frequency model drain-source circuit can be obtained in the similar manner as in Fig. 12.20c and can be shown as in Fig. 12.21b where

\(R^{′}_{s}=R_{s}||R_{L}=\frac{4.7K\times 10K}{4.7K+10K}=3.2 \) K

Applying KVL in the circuit we get

gmrd = Vgs = Id(rd + Rs)

Since, Vgs = Vi − Id R′s , we get from the above equation

gmrd ( Vi − Id R′s ) = I = (rd + R=)

or

Id (rd+gmrdR′s+R′s) = gmrdVi

which gives

\(I_{d}=\frac{g_{m}r_{d}V_{i}}{r_{d}+g_{m}r_{d}R^{′}_{s}+R^{′}_{s}}=\frac{g_{m}V_{i}}{1+(g_{m}+g_{d})R^{′}_{s}} \)

Now, the gain of the amplifier can be obtained as

\(A_{V}=\frac{V_{0}}{V_{i}}=\frac{I_{d}R^{′}_{s}}{V_{i}}=\frac{g_{m}R^{′}_{s}}{1+(g_{m}+g_{d})R^{′}_{s}} \)

Note that the gain can be obtained from Eq. (12.34) , \(A_{V}=\frac{g_{m}R_{s}}{1+(g_{m}+g_{d})R_{s}} \) , by simply replacing Rs by R′s .

(b) Using Vi= 2 V, gm = 2 mA/V, rd = 100 K and Rs =3.2 K in the expression of the drain current of part-(a), we get

\(I_{d}=\frac{2mA/V\times 2V}{1+\left\lgroup2mA/V+\frac{1}{100K} \right\rgroup \times 3.2K}=0.54 \) mA

(c) The output voltage of the circuit is obtained as

V0= Id R′s = 0.54 mA×3.2 K = 1.73 V

Thus, the gain is obtained as

\(A_{V}=\frac{V_{0}}{V_{i}}=\frac{1.73V}{2V}=0.87 \)

Note that the gain of a common-drain amplifier is always less than unity.
