Guest Question.

We suspect that the gas reaction between \(\mathrm{A}, \mathrm{B},\) and \(\mathrm{R}\) is an elementary reversible reaction

\(\mathrm{A}+\mathrm{B} \underset{k_{2}}{\stackrel{k_{1}}{\rightleftarrows}} \mathrm{R}\)

and we plan to test this with experiments in an isothermal plug flow reactor.

(a) Develop the isothermal performance equation for these kinetics for a feed of \(\mathrm{A}, \mathrm{B}, \mathrm{R},\) and inerts.

(b) Show how to test this equation for an equimolar feed of \(\mathrm{A}\) and \(\mathrm{B}\).

Admin
Solution

(a) Feed of \(A, B, R,\) and inerts. For this elementary reaction the rate is

\(-r_{\mathrm{A}}=k_{1} C_{\mathrm{A}} C_{\mathrm{B}}-k_{2} C_{\mathrm{R}}=k_{1} \frac{N_{\mathrm{A}}}{V} \frac{N_{\mathrm{B}}}{V}-k_{2} \frac{N_{\mathrm{R}}}{V}\)

At constant pressure, basing expansion and conversion on substance \(\mathrm{A}\),

\(-r_{\mathrm{A}}=k_{1} \frac{N_{\mathrm{A} 0}-N_{\mathrm{A} 0} X_{\mathrm{A}}}{V_{0}\left(1+\varepsilon_{\mathrm{A}} X_{\mathrm{A}}\right)} \frac{N_{\mathrm{B} 0}-N_{\mathrm{A} 0} X_{\mathrm{A}}}{V_{0}\left(1+\varepsilon_{\mathrm{A}} X_{\mathrm{A}}\right)}-k_{2} \frac{N_{\mathrm{R} 0}+N_{\mathrm{A} 0} X_{\mathrm{A}}}{V_{0}\left(1+\varepsilon_{\mathrm{A}} X_{\mathrm{A}}\right)}\)

Letting \(M=C_{\mathrm{B} 0} / C_{\mathrm{A} 0}, M^{\prime}=C_{\mathrm{R} 0} / C_{\mathrm{A} 0},\) we obtain

\(-r_{\mathrm{A}}=k_{1} C_{\mathrm{A} 0}^{2} \frac{\left(1-X_{\mathrm{A}}\right)\left(M-X_{\mathrm{A}}\right)}{\left(1+\varepsilon_{\mathrm{A}} X_{\mathrm{A}}\right)^{2}}-k_{2} C_{\mathrm{A} 0} \frac{M^{\prime}+X_{\mathrm{A}}}{1+\varepsilon_{\mathrm{A}} X_{\mathrm{A}}}\)

Hence, the design equation for plug flow, Eq. \(17,\) becomes

\(\tau=C_{\mathrm{A} 0} \int_{0}^{X_{\mathrm{A}}} \frac{d X_{\mathrm{A}}}{-r_{\mathrm{A}}}=\int_{0}^{X_{\mathrm{Af}}} \frac{\left(1+\varepsilon_{\mathrm{A}} X_{\mathrm{A}}\right)^{2} d X_{\mathrm{A}}}{k_{1} C_{\mathrm{A} 0}\left(1-X_{\mathrm{A}}\right)\left(M-X_{\mathrm{A}}\right)-k_{2}\left(M^{\prime}+X_{\mathrm{A}}\right)\left(1+\varepsilon_{\mathrm{A}} X_{\mathrm{A}}\right)}\)

In this expression \(\varepsilon_{\mathrm{A}}\) accounts for the stoichiometry and for inerts present in the feed.

(b) Equimolar feed of \(A\) and \(B\). For \(C_{\mathrm{A} 0}=C_{\mathrm{B} 0}, C_{\mathrm{R} 0}=0,\) and no inerts, we have \(M=1, M^{\prime}=0, \varepsilon_{\mathrm{A}}=-0.5 ;\) hence the expression for part a reduces to

\(\tau=\int_{0}^{X_{\mathrm{A}}} \frac{\left(1-0.5 X_{\mathrm{A}}\right)^{2} d X_{\mathrm{A}}}{k_{1} C_{\mathrm{A} 0}\left(1-X_{\mathrm{A}}\right)^{2}-k_{2} X_{\mathrm{A}}\left(1-0.5 X_{\mathrm{A}}\right)} \stackrel{\text { call }}{\text { this }} \int_{0}^{X_{\mathrm{Af}}} f\left(X_{\mathrm{A}}\right) d X_{\mathrm{A}}\) …(i)

Having \(V, v_{0},\) and \(X_{\mathrm{A}}\) data from a series of experiments, separately evaluate the left side and the right side of Eq.(i). For the right side, at various \(X_{\mathrm{A}}\)evaluate \(f\left(X_{\mathrm{A}}\right),\) then integrate graphically to give \(\int f\left(X_{\mathrm{A}}\right) d X_{\mathrm{A}}\) and then make the plot of Fig. E5.6. If the data fall on a reasonably straight line, then the suggested kinetic scheme can be said to be satisfactory in that it fits the data.
