Guest Question.

A flat plate solar collector has 8 cm high and 1 m wide and 1.6 m depth is tilted at 40° to the horizontal. The inner wall is at 70°C and the outer wall at 10°C and the enclosure is filled with air at 1 atm. Estimate the heat loss.

Admin
Solution

Given : An inclined flat plate solar collector with air as working fluid
\(L_c = 8 cm = 0.08 m, w = 1 m\)
H = 1.6 m, θ = 40° (with horizontal)
\(T_1 = 70°C, T_2 = 10°C.\)

To find : Heat loss from the solar flat plate collector.

Assumptions :
(i) The bottom plate and sides of solar collector are well insulated.
(ii) Heat transfer by natural convection only.
(iii) Steady state conditions.
(iv) Constant properties.
Analysis : The film temperature
\(T_f= \frac{70 +10}{2}=40°C\)
The properties of air at 40°C,
\(ρ = 1.128 kg/m^3, Cp = 1005 J/kg.K\)
\(µ = 1.91 × 10^{–5} kg/ms, ν = 16.96 × 10^{–6} m^2/s\)
\(k_f = 0.0276 W/m.K Pr = 0.699, β = \frac{1}{313} K^{–1}\)
The Rayleigh number
\(Ra_L = \frac{g β (T_1 -T_2)L_c^3}{ν^2}Pr\)
\(=\frac{9.81 × \frac{1}{313} ×(70 – 10 ) × (0.08)^3}{(16.96 × 10^{-6})^2}\)
\(= 2.34 × 10^6\)
\(Ra_θ = Ra_L sin (40°) = 1.504 × 10^6\)
The average Nusselt number (Table 10.6)
\(Nu = 0.073 Ra_θ^{1/3} \left ( \frac{H}{L_c} \right )^{-1/9}\)

\(= 0.073 × (1.504 × 10^6)^{1/3} ×\left ( \frac{1.6}{0.08} \right )^{-1/9} = 5.97\)
The average heat transfer coefficient
\(h = Nu\frac{k_f}{L_c}= × 5.97× \frac{0.0276}{0.08} = 2.05\)

The heat dissipation rate
\(Q = h(wH) (T_1 – T_2)\)
= 2.05 × (1 × 1.6) × (70 – 10)
= 197.7 W.
