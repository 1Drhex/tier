Guest Question.

Figure 3.7 shows the drive mechanism for an engine system. Use trigonometry to determine the crank angle as shown in the figure. 1.Determine Angle BAC 2.Determine the Crank Angle 3.Determine the Other Interior Angles.

Admin
Solution

1: By focusing on the triangle created by points A, B, and C, it is apparent that this is a Case 4:

the law of cosines can be used to find an angle. Equation \(c^{2}=a^{2}+b^{2}-2ab\ cos\angle C\) is rewritten as

\(\angle C=cos^{-1}(\frac{a^{2}+b^{2}-c^{2}}{2ab} )\)

The law of sines can be used to find a second angle. Equation \(\frac{a}{sin\angle A} =\frac{b}{sin\angle B} =\frac{c}{sin\angle C}\) is rewritten as

\(\angle A=sin^{-1}\left\{(\frac{a}{c} )sin\angle C\right\}\)

Equation \(\angle A+\angle B+\angle C=180^{\circ }\) can be used to find the third angle:

\(\angle B=180^{\circ }-\angle A-\angle C\)

Once familiarity in solving problems involving general
triangles is gained, referring to the specific cases will be
unnecessary.

Angle BAC can be determined by redefining the variables in the law of cosines:

\(\angle BAC=cos^{-1} \left\{\frac{AC^{2}\ +\ AB^{2}\ -\ BC^{2} }{2(AC)(AB)} \right\}\)

 

\(=cos^{-1} \left\{\frac{(5.3\ in.)^{2}\ +\ (1\ in.)^{2}\ -\ (5in.)^{2} }{2(5.3\ in.)(1\ in.)} \right\} =67.3^{\circ }\)

 

2: Angle BAC is defined between side AC (the vertical side) and leg AB. Because the crank angle is defined from a horizontal axis, the crank angle can be determined by the following:

\(Crank\ angle=90^{\circ }-67.3^{\circ}=22.7^{\circ }\)

3: Although not required in this problem, angle ACB can be determined by

\(\angle ACB=\ sin^{-1}\left\{(\frac{AB}{BC})\ sin\angle BAC\right\}\)

 

\(=sin^{-1}\left\{(\frac{1\ in.}{5\ in.} )\ sin\ 67.3^{\circ }\right\} =\ 10.6^{\circ }\)

Finally, angle ABC can be found by

\(\angle ABC=\ 180^{\circ }-67.3^{\circ }-10.6^{\circ }= 102.1^{\circ }\)
