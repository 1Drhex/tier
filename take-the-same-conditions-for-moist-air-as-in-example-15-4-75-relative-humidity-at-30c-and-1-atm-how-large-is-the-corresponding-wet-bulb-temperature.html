Guest Question.

Take the same conditions for moist air as in (\(75\)% relative humidity at \(30°C\) and \(1\) \(atm\)). How large is the corresponding wet bulb temperature?

Admin
Solution

In addition to the definition of relative humidity in terms of the vapor pressure and the saturation vapor pressure, we will need to simultaneously solve Equ.(15.21), Equ.(15.33) and Equ.(15.34). For the stated problem, namely finding the wet bulb temperature, the equations are nonlinear. We will have to find the values of enthalpies and pressures for the quantities appearing in the equations, partly for the still unknown value \(T_{wb}\) . Below you will find the relations to be set up if the thermodynamic property data are given in the form of programmed functions. Compare the solution of these equations with a solution attempted using tabulated values.

Given data:\( phi = 0.75\)

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad T1 = 30\).

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad P1 = 101.3\).

Properties: \(ha\)_\(T =\) Enthalpy(Air,\(T=T1)\)

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad ha\)_\(Twb\) = Enthalpy(Air,\(T=Twb')\)

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad hg\)_\(T =\) Enthalpy(Steam,\(T=T1,X=1),\)

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad hg\)_\(Twb =\) Enthalpy(Steam,\(T=Twb,X=1)\)

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad hl\)_\(Twb = \)Enthalpy(Steam,\(T=Twb,X=0)\)

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad Pg\)_\(Twb = \)Pressure(Steam\(,T=Twb,X=1)\)

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad Pg\)_\(T =\) Pressure(Steam,\(T=T1,X=1)\)

Relations: \(w =((ha\)_\(Twb–ha\)_\(T)+w'*(hg\)_\(Twb–hl\)_\(Twb))/(hg\)_\(T–hl\)_\(Twb)\)

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad w' = 0.622*Pg\)_\(Twb/(P1–Pg _ Twb)\).

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad w = 0.622*Pv \)_\(T/(P1–Pv \)_\( T)\).

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad phi = Pv \)_\( T/Pg\)_\(T\).

Solution: \(ha_T = 303.6 kJ/kg \quad\quad ha\)_\(Twb = 299.9\)

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad hg \)_\( T = 2555 \quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad hg \)_\( Twb = 2549\).

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad hl \)_\( Twb = 110.2\).

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad P1 = 101.3 kPa \quad\quad\quad\quad Pg\)_\(T = 4.246\).

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad Pg\)_\(Twb = 3.425 P\quad\quad v\)_\(T = 3.184\).

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad phi = 0.750\).

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad T1 = 30.0°C \quad\quad\quad\quad\quad Twb = 26.3\).

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad w = 0.0202 \quad\quad\quad\quad\quad w' = 0.0218\).

The solution of the problem was performed completely within the program EES (Klein et al., 1991). The form of the property functions is pretty much self explanatory. Air stands for dry air, while Steam denotes water (liquid and vapor). The temperature and its wet bulb counterpart are denoted by \(T\) and \(Twb\), respectively, while w is used for the humidity ratio. \(X\) is the quality (defined in Equ.(15.59); \(X=0\) is for saturated liquid, while \(X=1\) is for saturated vapor).

 

\(\omega=\frac{M_{0 v} P_{v}}{M_{0 a} P_{a}}=0.622 \frac{P_{v}}{P-P_{v}} \quad\quad (15.21)\)

 

\(\omega=\frac{h_{a}\left(T_{w b}\right)-h_{a}(T)+\omega^{\prime}\left[h_{g}\left(T_{w b}\right)-h_{l}\left(T_{w b}\right)\right]}{h_{g}(T)-h_{l}\left(T_{w b}\right)} \quad\quad (15.33)\)

 

\(\omega^{\prime}=0.622 \frac{P_{g}\left(T_{w b}\right)}{P-P_{g}\left(T_{w b}\right)}\quad\quad (15.34)\)

 

\(x=\frac{n_{g}}{n_{l}+n_{g}}=\frac{m_{g}}{m_{l}+m_{g}}\quad\quad(15.59)\)
