Guest Question.

Consider the planetary gear train shown in the schematic diagram in Fig. 7.42. The radius of the fixed sun gear is R1 = 100 mm, the radius of input gear 2 is R2 = 100 mm, the radius of ring gear 4 is R4 = 200 mm, and the radii of gears 3 and 5 are R3 = R5 = 50 mm. The angular velocity of input shaft 2 is ω2 = 10 rad/s ccw (looking from the left). Determine the angular velocities of gears 3, and 4, and 5.

Admin
Solution

The rolling contact constraint between input gear 2 and gear 5 can be written as

\(-\frac{R_{2}}{R_{5}}=-\frac{100 mm }{50 mm }=-2=\frac{\theta_{ 5 2}^{\prime}}{\theta_{22}^{\prime}}\) (1)

Since the kinematic coefficient of input shaft 2 is \(\theta_{22}^{\prime}=1\) rad/rad, the kinematic coefficient of gear 5 is

\(\theta_{52}^{\prime}=-2 rad / rad,\) (2)

where the negative sign implies that gear 5 is rotating in the opposite direction to input gear 2—that is, clockwise. Therefore, the angular velocity of gear 5 is

\(\omega_{5}=\theta_{52}^{\prime} \omega_{2}=-20 rad / s ( cw ).\)

The rolling contact constraint between planet gear 3 and fixed sun gear 1 can be written as

\(-\frac{R_{3}}{R_{1}}=\frac{\theta_{12}^{\prime}-\theta_{5 A 2}^{\prime}}{\theta_{32}^{\prime}-\theta_{5 A 2}^{\prime}}\) (3)

which, for the given conditions, becomes

\(-\frac{50 mm }{100 mm }=\frac{0+2 rad / rad }{\theta_{32}^{\prime}+2 rad / rad }\)

Therefore, the kinematic coefficient of gear 3 with respect to fixed sun gear 1 is

\(\theta_{32}^{\prime}=-6 rad / rad\) (4)

and the angular velocity of gear 3 is

\(\omega_{3}=\theta_{32}^{\prime} \omega_{2}=-60 rad / s\)

where the negative sign indicates that the direction of ω3 is clockwise.
The rolling contact constraint between ring gear 4 and planet gear 3 can be written as

\(\frac{R_{3}}{R_{4}}=\frac{\theta_{42}^{\prime}-\theta_{5 A 2}^{\prime}}{\theta_{32}^{\prime}-\theta_{5 A 2}^{\prime}}\) (5)

which, using Eqs. (2) and (4) and the given dimensions, can be written as

\(\frac{50 mm }{200 mm }=\frac{\theta_{42}^{\prime}+2 rad / rad }{-6 rad / rad +2 rad / rad }\)

Therefore, the kinematic coefficient of gear 4 with respect to fixed sun gear 1 is

\(\theta_{42}^{\prime}=-3 rad / rad\) (6)

and the angular velocity of gear 4 is

\(\omega_{4}=\theta_{42}^{\prime} \omega_{2}=-30 rad / s\)

where the negative sign indicates that the direction of ω4 is clockwise.
The kinematic coefficients in Eqs. (2), (4), and (6) define the geometry of the gear train and provide the capability for obtaining the angular velocities (magnitudes and directions) in terms of the input angular velocity, ω2.
