Guest Question.

Derive the transfer functions relating displacements \( x_{1} (t) \).and \( x_{2} (t) \).to the external forces \( F_{1} (t) \).and \( F_{2} (t) \).in the system shown in Fig. 11.12.

Admin
Solution

The following equations of motion were derived for this system in :

\( m_{1} \frac{d^{2}x_{1}}{dt^{2}}+ b_{1} \frac{dx_{1}}{dt}+\left(k_{1}+k_{2}\right)x_{1}-k_{2}x_{2}=F_{1} \),

\( m_{2} \frac{d^{2}x_{2}}{dt^{2}}+k_{2}x_{2}-k_{2}x_{1}=F_{2} \).

conditions to zero and taking the Laplace transform of the basic equations of motion yields

\( m_{1} s^{2} X_{1} (s) + b_{1} sX_{1} (s) + (k_{1} + k_{2} )X_{1} (s) − k_{2} X_{2} (s) = F_{1} (s) \),

\( m_{2} s^{2} X_{2} (s) + k_{2} X_{2} (s)-k_{2} X_{1} (s) = F_{2} (s) \).

The two preceding equations can be put in a matrix form:

\( \left [ \begin{matrix} m_{1} s^{2} + b_{1} s + (k_{1} + k_{2} ) & −k_{2} \\ −k_{2} & m_{2} s^{2} + k_{2} \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} X_{1} (s) \\ X_{2} (s) \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} F_{1} (s) \\ F_{2} (s) \end{matrix} \right ] \).

This matrix equation can be solved for \( X_{1} (s) \) and \( X_{2} (s) \) by use of Cramer’s rule. According to Cramer’s rule, the two unknowns can be calculated as the ratios of determinants:

\( X_{1} (s)=\frac{\left | \begin{matrix} F_{1} (s) & −k_{2} \\ F_{2} (s) & m_{2} s^{2} + k_{2} \end{matrix} \right | }{\left | \begin{matrix} m_{1} s^{2} + b_{1} s + k_{1} + k_{2} & −k_{2} \\ −k_{2} & m_{2} s^{2} + k_{2} \end{matrix} \right | } \),

\( X_{2} (s)=\frac{\left | \begin{matrix} m_{1} s^{2} + b_{1} s + k_{1} + k_{2} & F_{1} (s) \\ −k_2 & F_{2} (s) \end{matrix} \right | }{\left | \begin{matrix} m_{1} s^{2} + b_{1} s + k_{1} + k_{2} & −k_{2} \\ −k_{2} & m_{2} s^{2} + k_{2} \end{matrix} \right | } \).

 

Calculating the values of the determinants gives

\( X_{1} (s)=\frac{F_{1} (s)\left(m_{2} s^{2} + k_{2}\right)+k_{2}F_{2} (s) }{\left(m_{1} s^{2} + b_{1} s + k_{1} + k_{2}\right)\left(m_{2} s^{2} + k_{2}\right)-k^{2}_{2}} \),

\( X_{2} (s)=\frac{\left(m_{1} s^{2} + b_{1} s + k_{1} + k_{2}\right)F_{2} (s)+k_{2}F_{1} (s) }{\left(m_{1} s^{2} + b_{1} s + k_{1} + k_{2}\right)\left(m_{2} s^{2} + k_{2}\right)-k^{2}_{2}} \).

From these two equations, all four transfer functions of the two-input, two-output system can be obtained:

\( T_{11} (s)=\frac{X_{1}(s)}{F_{1}(s)}\Biggm\vert_{F_{2}(s)=0} =\frac{m_{2} s^{2} + k_{2}}{m_{1}m_{2}s^{4} +m_{2}b_{1}s^{3}+\left(m_{1}k_{2} +m_{2}k_{1} + m_{2}k_{2}\right) s^{2}+ b_{1}k_{2} s + k_{1}k_{2}} \),

\( T_{12} (s)=\frac{X_{1}(s)}{F_{2}(s)}\Biggm\vert_{F_{1}(s)=0} =\frac{k_{2}}{m_{1}m_{2}s^{4} +m_{2}b_{1}s^{3}+\left(m_{1}k_{2} +m_{2}k_{1} + m_{2}k_{2}\right) s^{2}+ b_{1}k_{2} s + k_{1}k_{2}} \),

\( T_{21} (s)=\frac{X_{2}(s)}{F_{1}(s)}\Biggm\vert_{F_{2}(s)=0} =\frac{k_{2}}{m_{1}m_{2}s^{4} +m_{2}b_{1}s^{3}+\left(m_{1}k_{2} +m_{2}k_{1} + m_{2}k_{2}\right) s^{2}+ b_{1}k_{2} s + k_{1}k_{2}} \),

\( T_{22} (s)=\frac{X_{2}(s)}{F_{2}(s)}\Biggm\vert_{F_{1}(s)=0} =\frac{m_{1}s^{2}+ b_{1}s+k_{1}+k_{2}}{m_{1}m_{2}s^{4} +m_{2}b_{1}s^{3}+\left(m_{1}k_{2} +m_{2}k_{1} + m_{2}k_{2}\right) s^{2}+ b_{1}k_{2} s + k_{1}k_{2}} \).

Recall from Section 11.4 that the denominator of the transfer function is the same as the characteristic polynomial (the left-hand side of the system characteristic equation). The four transfer functions derived for this system represent the relations between different pairs of output and input variables within the same system, and it can be seen that the denominator in each transfer function is the same because it represents the inherent dynamic characteristics of the same system. The characteristic equation is

\( m_{1}m_{2}s^{4} +m_{2}b_{1}s^{3}+\left(m_{1}k_{2} +m_{2}k_{1} + m_{2}k_{2}\right) s^{2}+ b_{1}k_{2} s + k_{1}k_{2}=0 \).

Furthermore, from the equations previously derived for \( X_{1} (s) \) and \( X_{2} (s) \), one can obtain the system input–output differential equations by multiplying both sides of those equations by the denominator of the expression on the right-hand side and taking the inverse Laplace transform to obtain

\( m_{1}m_{2}\frac{d^{4}x_{1} }{dt^{4}} +m_{2}b_{1}\frac{d^{3}x_{1} }{dt^{3}}+\left(m_{1}k_{2} +m_{2}k_{1} + m_{2}k_{2}\right) \frac{d^{2}x_{1} }{dt^{2}}+ b_{1}k_{2} \frac{dx_{1} }{dt} \)

\( + k_{1}k_{2}x_{1}=m_{2}\frac{d^{2}F_{1}}{dt}+k_{2}F_{1}+k_{2}F_{2} \).

for \( x_{1} (s) \) as the output variable and

\( m_{1}m_{2}\frac{d^{4}x_{2} }{dt^{4}} +m_{2}b_{1}\frac{d^{3}x_{2} }{dt^{3}}+\left(m_{1}k_{2} +m_{2}k_{1} + m_{2}k_{2}\right) \frac{d^{2}x_{2} }{dt^{2}}+ b_{1}k_{2} \frac{dx_{2} }{dt} + k_{1}k_{2}x_{2} \)

\( = k_{2}F_{1}+ m_{1}\frac{d^{2}F_{2}}{dt^{2}}+b_{1}\frac{dF_{2}}{d}+ \left(k_{1}+ k_{2}\right) F_{2} \).

for \( x_{1} (s) \) .as the output variable. It can be verified that the input–output equations derived here with the system transfer functions are the same as the input–output equations for the same system obtained in by combining the system basic equations of motion in the time domain.
