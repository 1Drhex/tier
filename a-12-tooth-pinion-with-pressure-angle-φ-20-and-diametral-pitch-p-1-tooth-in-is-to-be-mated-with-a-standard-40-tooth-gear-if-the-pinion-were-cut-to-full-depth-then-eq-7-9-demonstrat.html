Guest Question.

A 12-tooth pinion with pressure angle Φ = 20° and diametral pitch P = 1 tooth/in is to be mated with a standard 40-tooth gear. If the pinion were cut to full depth, then Eq. (7.9) demonstrates that the contact ratio would be 1.41, but there would be undercutting, as indicated in Fig. 7.25. Instead, let the 12-tooth pinion be cut from a larger blank using center-distance modifications. Determine the cutter offset, the modified pressure angle, the modified pitch radii of the pinion and the gear, the modified center distance, the modified pitch radii of the pinion and gear, the modified center distance, the modified outside radii of the pinion and gear, and the contact ratio. Has the contact ratio increased significantly?

\(m_{c}=\frac{C D}{p_{b}}\) (7.9)

 

Admin
Solution

Designating the pinion as subscript 2 and the gear as 3, then with P = 1 tooth/in and Φ = 20°, the following values are determined:

\(\begin{aligned}p &=3.142 in / \text { tooth, } R_{2}=6 in , R_{3}=20 in , N_{2}=12 \text { teeth, } \\N_{3} &=40 \text { teeth, } \quad \text { and } \quad t_{3}=1.571 in .\end{aligned}\)

For a standard rack cutter, from Table 7.2, the addendum is a = 1/P = 1.0 in. From Eq. (7.21), the rack cutter will be offset by

\(e=a+R_{2} \cos ^{2} \phi-R_{2}=a-R_{2} \sin ^{2} \phi\) (7.21)

 

\(e=1.0-6.0 \sin ^{2} 20^{\circ}=0.298 \text { in. }\)

Then, the thickness of the pinion tooth at the 6-in pitch circle, using Eq. (7.17), is

\(t=2 e \tan \phi+\frac{p}{2}\) (7.17)

 

\(t_{2}^{\prime}=2 e \tan \phi+\frac{p}{2}=2(0.298 \text { in }) \tan 20^{\circ}+\frac{3.142 \text { in }}{2}=1.788 in.\)

The pressure angle at which this (and only this) gearset will operate is determined from Eq. (7.18); that is,

\(\operatorname{inv} \phi^{\prime}=\frac{N_{2}\left(t_{2}^{\prime}+t_{3}^{\prime}\right)-2 \pi R_{2}}{2 R_{2}\left(N_{2}+N_{3}\right)}+\operatorname{inv} \phi\) (7.18)

 

\(\operatorname{inv} \phi^{\prime}=\frac{N_{2}\left(t_{2}^{\prime}+t_{3}^{\prime}\right)-2 \pi R_{2}}{2 R_{2}\left(N_{2}+N_{3}\right)}+\operatorname{inv} \phi\)

 

\(=\frac{12(1.788 in +1.571 in )-2 \pi(6.0 in )}{2(6.0 in )(12+40)}+ inv 20^{\circ}=0.01908 rad.\)

 

From Appendix A, Table 6, we find that the new pressure angle is

\(\phi^{\prime}=21.65^{\circ}.\)

Using Eqs. (7.19) and (7.20), the modified pitch radii are found to be

\(R_{2}^{\prime}=\frac{R_{2} \cos \phi}{\cos \phi^{\prime}}\) (7.19)

 

\(R_{3}^{\prime}=\frac{R_{3} \cos \phi}{\cos \phi^{\prime}}\) (7.20)

 

\(R_{2}^{\prime}=\frac{R_{2} \cos \phi}{\cos \phi^{\prime}}=\frac{(6.0 \text { in }) \cos 20^{\circ}}{\cos 21.65^{\circ}}=6.066 in,\)

 

\(R_{3}^{\prime}=\frac{R_{3} \cos \phi}{\cos \phi^{\prime}}=\frac{(20.0 \text { in }) \cos 20^{\circ}}{\cos 21.65^{\circ}}=20.220 \text { in. }\)

So, the modified center distance is

\(R_{2}^{\prime}+R_{3}^{\prime}=6.066+20.220=26.286 \text { in. }\)

Note that the center distance has not increased as much as the offset of the rack cutter. Standard clearance of 0.25/P results from the standard dedendums equal to 1.25/P, as indicated in Table 7.2. So, the root radii of the two gears are

Root radius of pinion = 6.298−1.250 = 5.048 in,
Root radius of gear = 20.000−1.250 = 18.750 in,
Sum of root radii = 23.798 in.

The difference between this sum and the center distance is the working depth plus twice the clearance. Since the clearance is 0.25 in for each gear, the working depth is

Working depth = 26.286−23.798−2(0.250) = 1.988 in.

The outside radius of each gear is the sum of the root radius, the clearance, and the working depth; that is,

Outside radius of pinion = 5.048+0.250+1.988 = 7.286 in;

Outside radius of gear = 18.750+0.250+1.988 = 20.988 in.

The result is shown in Fig. 7.27, and the pinion is seen to have a stronger-looking form than the one of Fig. 7.25. Undercutting has been completely eliminated. The contact ratio can be obtained from Eqs. (7.9) through (7.11). The following quantities are needed:

\(P D=\sqrt{\left(R_{2}+a\right)^{2}-\left(R_{2} \cos \phi\right)^{2}}-R_{2} \sin \phi\) (7.11)

\(\text { Outside radius of pinion }=R_{2}^{\prime}+a=7.286 in \text {, }\)

 

\(\text { Outside radius of gear }=R_{3}^{\prime}+a=20.988 \text { in, }\)

 

\(r_{2}=R_{2} \cos \phi=(6.000 \text { in }) \cos 20^{\circ}=5.638 in,\)

\(r_{3}=R_{3} \cos \phi=(20.000 \text { in }) \cos 20^{\circ}=18.794 \text { in },\)

 

\(p_{b}=p \cos \phi=(3.1416 in / \text { tooth }) \cos 20^{\circ}=2.952 in / tooth .\)

Therefore, for Eqs. (7.10) and (7.11), we have

\(CP =\sqrt{\left(R_{3}+a\right)^{2}-\left(R_{3} \cos \phi\right)^{2}}-R_{3} \sin \phi\) (7.10)

\(\begin{aligned}C P &=\sqrt{\left(R_{3}^{\prime}+a\right)^{2}-r_{3}^{2}}-R_{3}^{\prime} \sin \phi \\&=\sqrt{(20.988 in )^{2}-(18.794 in )^{2}}-(20.220 \text { in }) \sin21.65^{\circ}=1.883 in\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}P D &=\sqrt{\left(R_{2}^{\prime}+a\right)^{2}-r_{2}^{2}}-R_{2}^{\prime} \sin \phi \\&=\sqrt{(7.286 in )^{2}-(5.638 in )^{2}}-(6.066 \text { in }) \sin 21.65^{\circ} \\&=2.377 in\end{aligned}.\)

 

Finally, from Eq. (7.9), the contact ratio is

\(m_{c}=\frac{C P+P D}{p_{b}}=\frac{1.883 in +2.377 in }{2.952 in / tooth }=1.443 \text { teeth avg. }\)

Therefore, the contact ratio has increased only slightly (approximately 2% increase). The modification, however, is justified because of the elimination of undercutting, which results in a substantial improvement in the strength of the teeth.
