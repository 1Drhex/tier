Guest Question.

Pressurized water is often available at elevated temperatures and may be used for space heating or industrial process applications. In such cases it is customary to use a tube bundle in which the water is passed through the tubes, while air is passed in cross flow over the tubes. Consider a staggered arrangement for which the tube outside diameter is 16.4 mm and the longitudinal and transverse pitches are \(S_{L} = 34.3 mm\) and \(S_{T} = 31.3 mm.\) There are seven rows of tubes in the airflow direction and eight tubes per row. Under typical operating conditions the cylinder surface temperature is at 70°C, while the air upstream temperature and velocity are 15°C and 6 m/s, respectively. Determine the air-side convection coefficient and the rate of heat transfer for the tube bundle. What is the air-side pressure drop?

Admin
Solution

Known: Geometry and operating conditions of a tube bank.

Find:
1. Air-side convection coefficient and heat rate.
2. Pressure drop.

Assumptions:
1. Steady-state, incompressible flow conditions.
2. Negligible radiation effects.
3. Negligible effect of change in air temperature across tube bank on air properties.

Analysis:
1. From Equations 7.58 and 7.59, the air-side Nusselt number is

 

\(Nu_{D}=C_{1}Re_{D,max}^{m}Pr^{0.36}\left ( \frac{Pr}{Pr_{s}} \right )^{1/4}\)

\(\begin{bmatrix} N_{L}\geq 20\\ 0.7\lesssim Pr \lesssim 500\\ 10 \lesssim Re_{D,max} \lesssim 2\times 10^{6} \end{bmatrix}\) (7.58)

 

\(\overline{Nu}_{D}\mid _{(N_{L}< 20)}=C_{2}\overline{Nu}_{D}\mid _{(N_{L}\ge 20)} \) (7.59)

 

\(Nu_{D}=C_{2}C_{1}Re_{D,max}^{m}Pr^{0.36}\left ( \frac{Pr}{Pr_{s}} \right )^{1/4}\)

 

Since \(S_{D}=[S_{L}^{2}+(S_{T}/2)^{2}]^{1/2}=37.7 mm (S_{T}+D)/2\) the maximum velocity occurs on the transverse plane, \(A_{1},\) of Figure 7.12. Hence from Equation 7.60

 

\(V_{max}=\frac{S_{T}}{S_{T}-D}\) (7.60)

 

\(V_{max}=\frac{S_{T}}{S_{T}-D}V= \frac{31.3mm}{(31.3-16.4 )mm}6m/s=12.6m/s\)

 

With

 

\(Re_{D,max}=\frac{V_{max}D}{\nu }=\frac{12.6 m/s\times 0.0164 m}{14.82\times 10^{-6}m^{2}/s}=13,943\)

 

and

 

\(\frac{S_{T}}{S_{L}}=\frac{31.3mm}{34.3mm}=0.91 < 2\)

 

it follows from Tables 7.5 and 7.6 that

and

From Equation 7.63

 

\(\frac{T_{s}-T_{o}}{T_{s}-T_{i}}=\exp \left ( -\frac{\pi DN\bar{h}}{\rho VN_{T}S_{T}c_{p}} \right )\) (7.63)

 

\({T_{s}-T_{o}}=({T_{s}-T_{i}})\exp \left ( -\frac{\pi DN\bar{h}}{\rho VN_{T}S_{T}c_{p}} \right )\)

 

\(T_{s}-T_{o}=(55^{\circ}C)\exp \left (- \frac{\pi (0.0164 m)56 (135.6 W/m^{2}\cdot K)}{1.217 kg/m^{3}(6 m/s)8(0.0313 m)1007 J/kg\cdot K} \right )\)

 

\(T_{s}-T_{o}=44.5^{\circ}C\)

 

Hence from Equations 7.62 and 7.64

 

\(\Delta T_{1m}=\frac{(T_{s}-T_{i})-(T_{s}-T_{o})}{\ln \left ( \frac{T_{s}-T_{i}}{T_{s}-T_{o}} \right )}\) (7.62)

 

\({q}'=N(\bar{h}\pi D\Delta T_{1m})\) (7.64)

 

\(\Delta T_{1m}=\frac{(T_{s}-T_{i})-(T_{s}-T_{o})}{\ln \left ( \frac{T_{s}-T_{i}}{T_{s}-T_{o}} \right )}=\frac{(55-44.5)^{\circ}C}{\ln \left ( \frac{55}{44.5} \right )}=49.6^{\circ}C\)

 

and

 

\({q}'=N(\bar{h}\pi D\Delta T_{1m})=56\pi \times 135.6 W/m^{2}\cdot K\times 0.0164 m\times 49.6^{\circ}C\)

 

\({q}'=19.4 kW/m\)

 

2. The pressure drop may be obtained from Equation 7.65.

 

\(\Delta p=N_{L}X\left ( \frac{\rho V^{2}_{max}}{2} \right )f\) (7.65)

 

With \(Re_{D,max}=13,943, P_{T}=(S_{T}/D)=1.91, P_{L}=(S_{L}/D)=2.09,\) and \((P_{T}/P_{L})=0.91,\) it follows from Figure 7.15 that \(X\approx 1.04\) and \(f\approx 0.35.\) Hence with \(N_{L}=7\)

 

\(\Delta p=7\times 1.04\left [ \frac{1.217kg/m^{3}(12.6 m/s)^{2}}{2} \right ]0.35\)

 

\(\Delta p=246 N/m^{2}=2.46\times 10^{-3}\) bars

 

 

Comments:
1. Had \(\Delta T_{i}\equiv T_{s}-T_{i}\) been used in lieu of \(\Delta T_{1m}\) in Equation 7.64, the heat rate would have been overpredicted by 11%.

2. Since the air temperature is predicted to increase by only 10.5°C, evaluation of the air properties at \(T_{i} = 15°C\) is a reasonable approximation. However, if improved accuracy is desired, the calculations could be repeated with the properties reevaluated at \((T_{i}+T_{o})/2=20.25^{\circ}C.\) An exception pertains to the density \(\rho\) in the exponential term of Equation 7.63. As it appears in the denominator of this term, \(\rho\) is matched with the inlet velocity to provide a product \((\rho V)\) that is linked to the mass flow rate of air entering the tube bank. Hence, in this term, \(\rho\) should be evaluated at \(T_{i}.\)

3. The air outlet temperature and heat rate may be increased by increasing the number of tube rows, and for a fixed number of rows, they may be varied by adjusting the air velocity. For \(5\leq N_{L}\leq 25\) and V=6 m/s, parametric calculations based on Equations 7.58, 7.59, and 7.62 through 7.64 yield the following results:

The air outlet temperature would asymptotically approach the surface temperature with increasing \(N_{L},\) at which point the heat rate approaches a constant value and there is no advantage to adding more tube rows.
