Guest Question.

The simply supported beam shown in Fig. P10.35 consists of a W410 × 85 structural steel wide-flange shape [E = 200 GPa; I = 316 × \(10^{6} mm ^{4}\) ]. For the loading shown, use discontinuity functions to compute (a) the slope of the beam at A and (b) the deflection of the beam at midspan.

Admin
Solution

 

Support reactions: A FBD of the beam is shown to the right.

 

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{A}=&-(75 kN / m )(2.5 m )(1.25 m ) \\&-(75 kN / m )(2.5 m )(6.75 m )+D_{y}(8 m )=0 \\& \therefore D_{y}=187.5 kN\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{gathered}\Sigma F_{y}=A_{y}+D_{y}-(75 kN / m )(2.5 m )-(75 kN / m )(2.5 m )=0 \\\therefore A_{y}=187.5 kN\end{gathered}\)

Load function w(x):

 

\(\begin{aligned}w(x)=& 187.5 kN \langle x-0 m \rangle^{-1}-75 kN / m \langle x-0 m \rangle^{0}+75 kN / m \langle x-2.5 m \rangle^{0} \\&-75 kN / m \langle x-5.5 m \rangle^{0}+75 kN / m \langle x-8 m \rangle^{0}+187.5 kN \langle x-8 m \rangle^{-1}\end{aligned}\)

Shear-force function V(x) and bending-moment function M(x):

 

\(\begin{aligned}V(x)=& 187.5 kN \langle x-0 m \rangle^{0}-75 kN / m \langle x-0 m \rangle^{1}+75 kN / m \langle x-2.5 m \rangle^{1} \\&-75 kN / m \langle x-5.5 m \rangle^{1}+75 kN / m \langle x-8 m \rangle^{1}+187.5 kN \langle x-8 m \rangle^{0} \\M(x)=& 187.5 kN \langle x-0 m \rangle^{1}-\frac{75 kN / m }{2}\langle x-0 m \rangle^{2}+\frac{75 kN / m }{2}\langle x-2.5 m \rangle^{2} \\&-\frac{75 kN / m }{2}\langle x-5.5 m \rangle^{2}+\frac{75 kN / m }{2}\langle x-8 m \rangle^{2}+187.5 kN \langle x-8 m \rangle^{1}\end{aligned}\)

Equations for beam slope and beam deflection:
From Eq. (10.1), we can write:

 

\(\begin{array}{r}E I \frac{d^{2} v}{d x^{2}}=M(x)=187.5 kN \langle x-0 m \rangle^{1}-\frac{75 kN / m }{2}\langle x-0 m \rangle^{2}+\frac{75 kN / m }{2}\langle x-2.5 m \rangle^{2} \\-\frac{75 kN / m }{2}\langle x-5.5 m \rangle^{2}+\frac{75 kN / m }{2}\langle x-8 m \rangle^{2}+187.5 kN \langle x-8 m \rangle^{1}\end{array}\)

Integrate the moment function to obtain an expression for the beam slope:

 

\(\begin{aligned}E I \frac{d v}{d x}=& \frac{187.5 kN }{2}\langle x-0 m \rangle^{2}-\frac{75 kN / m }{6}\langle x-0 m \rangle^{3}+\frac{75 kN / m }{6}\langle x-2.5 m \rangle^{3} \\&-\frac{75 kN / m }{6}\langle x-5.5 m \rangle^{3}+\frac{75 kN / m }{6}\langle x-8 m \rangle^{3}+\frac{187.5 kN }{2}\langle x-8 m \rangle^{2}+C_{1} (a)\end{aligned}\)

Integrate again to obtain the beam deflection function:

 

\(\begin{aligned}E I v=& \frac{187.5 kN }{6}\langle x-0 m \rangle^{3}-\frac{75 kN / m }{24}\langle x-0 m \rangle^{4}+\frac{75 kN / m }{24}\langle x-2.5 m \rangle^{4} \\&-\frac{75 kN / m }{24}\langle x-5.5 m \rangle^{4}+\frac{75 kN / m }{24}\langle x-8 m \rangle^{4}+\frac{187.5 kN }{6}\langle x-8 m \rangle^{3}+C_{1} x+C_{2} (b)\end{aligned}\)

Evaluate constants using boundary conditions: Boundary conditions are specific values of deflection v or slope dv/dx that are known at particular locations along the beam span. For this beam, the deflection v is known at the pin support (x = 0 m) and at the roller support (x = 8 m). Substitute the boundary condition v = 0 at x = 0 m into Eq. (b) to obtain:

 

\(C_{2}=0\)

 

Next, substitute the boundary condition v = 0 at x = 8 m into Eq. (b) to obtain:

 

\(\begin{aligned}0 &=\frac{187.5 kN }{6}(8 m )^{3}-\frac{75 kN / m }{24}(8 m )^{4}+\frac{75 kN / m }{24}(5.5 m )^{4}-\frac{75 kN / m }{24}(2.5 m )^{4}+C_{1}(8 m ) \\& \therefore C_{1}=-742.1875 kN – m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

The beam slope and elastic curve equations are now complete:

 

\(\begin{aligned}E I \frac{d v}{d x}=& \frac{187.5 kN }{2}\langle x-0 m \rangle^{2}-\frac{75 kN / m }{6}\langle x-0 m \rangle^{3}+\frac{75 kN / m }{6}\langle x-2.5 m \rangle^{3} \\&-\frac{75 kN / m }{6}\langle x-5.5 m \rangle^{3}+\frac{75 kN / m }{6}\langle x-8 m \rangle^{3}+\frac{187.5 kN }{2}\langle x-8 m \rangle^{2}-742.1875 kN – m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}E I v &=\frac{187.5 kN }{6}\langle x-0 m \rangle^{3}-\frac{75 kN / m }{24}\langle x-0 m \rangle^{4}+\frac{75 kN / m }{24}\langle x-2.5 m \rangle^{4} \\&-\frac{75 kN / m }{24}\langle x-5.5 m \rangle^{4}+\frac{75 kN / m }{24}\langle x-8 m \rangle^{4}+\frac{187.5 kN }{6}\langle x-8 m \rangle^{3}-\left(742.1875 kN – m ^{2}\right) x\end{aligned}\)

 

(a) Beam slope at A: For the W410 × 85 structural steel wide-flange shape, EI = 63,200 \(kN – m ^{2}\). The beam slope at A is:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\left.E I \frac{d v}{d x}\right|_{A} &=-742.1875 kN – m ^{2} \\&\left.\therefore \frac{d v}{d x}\right|_{A}=-\frac{742.1875 kN – m ^{2}}{63,200 kN – m ^{2}}=-0.011743 rad =-0.01174 rad\end{aligned}\)

(b) Beam deflection at midspan: At midspan where x = 4 m, the beam deflection is:

 

\(\begin{aligned}E I v_{\text {midspan }} &=\frac{187.5 kN }{6}(4 m )^{3}-\frac{75 kN / m }{24}(4 m )^{4}+\frac{75 kN / m }{24}(1.5 m )^{4}-\left(742.1875 kN – m ^{2}\right)(4 m ) \\&=-1,752.929687 kN – m ^{3} \\& \therefore v_{\text {midspan }}=-\frac{1,752.929687 kN – m ^{3}}{63,200 kN – m ^{2}}=-0.027736 m =27.7 mm \downarrow\end{aligned}\)
