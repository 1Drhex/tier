Guest Question.

Where must a circular hole of 1 metre radius be punched out of a circular disc of 3 metres radius so that the centre of gravity of the remainder be 2 cm from the centre of the disc ?

Admin
Solution

Refer to Fig. 7.29. Since, the remaining body is symmetrical about XX-axis, therefore \(\bar{y}\) = 0.

 

Let ‘c’ be the distance of the centre of the circular hole from the centre of the circular disc. For finding out the value of ‘c’ we have the following table :

 

ax(\(m^{3}\)) Centroidal distance ‘x’ from MM (m) Area ‘a’ (\(m^{2}\)) Components
\(27\pi\) 3 \(\pi × 3^{2} = 9\pi\) Circular disc (1)
\(-\pi (3+c)\) (3+c) \(– \pi × 1^{2} = – \pi\) Circular hole (2)
\(\sum ax = 27\pi – \pi (3 + c)\) _ \(\sum a = 8\pi\)

 

 

\(\bar{x}=\frac{\sum ax}{\sum a}=\frac{27\pi -\pi (3+c)}{8\pi }=\frac{24-c}{8}\)

 

But \(\bar{x} = 2.98\) m = (given) from MM

 

\(\therefore\) \( 2.98=\frac{24-c}{8}\)

 

or 24 – c = 23.84

 

or c = 0.16 m.
