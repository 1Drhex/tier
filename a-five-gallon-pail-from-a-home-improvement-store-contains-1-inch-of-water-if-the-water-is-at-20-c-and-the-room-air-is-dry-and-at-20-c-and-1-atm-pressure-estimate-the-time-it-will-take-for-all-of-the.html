Guest Question.

A five-gallon pail from a home improvement store contains 1 inch of water. If the water is at 20 C and the room air is dry and at 20 C and 1 atm pressure, estimate the time it will take for all of the water in the pail to evaporate.

Admin
Solution

From the website of a home improvement store, a 5-gallon pail has an inside diameter of 12 inches and a height of 14.5 inches.

Diameter of pail \(=D=12 in \times(0.0254 m / inch )=0.3048 m\)

 

Cross – sectional area of pail \(=A=\frac{\pi}{4} D^{2}=\frac{\pi}{4}(0.3048)^{2}=0.0730 m ^{2}\)

 

Volume of water in pail \(=(A)(\text { water height })=\left(0.0730 m ^{2}\right)(1 \text { inch } )\left(\frac{0.0254 m }{\text { inch }}\right)=0.001853 m ^{3}\)

 

Mass of water in pail \(1=\rho_{\text {water }} V=\left(998 kg / m ^{3}\right)\left(0.001853 m ^{3}\right)=1.85 kg\)

 

Evaporation rate from Eq. (11.29):

 

\(\dot{m}_{A}=D_{A B} A\left(\frac{M_{A}}{\bar{R} T}\right)\left(\frac{P}{L}\right) \ln \left[\frac{\left(P-P_{A}\right)_{x=L}}{\left(P-P_{A}\right)_{x=0}}\right]\) (11.29)

 

\(\dot{m}_{A}=D_{A B} A\left(\frac{M_{A}}{\bar{R} T}\right)\left(\frac{P}{L}\right) \ln \left[\frac{\left(P-P_{A}\right)_{x=L}}{\left(P-P_{A}\right)_{x=0}}\right]\) (11.30)

 

Species A is the water vapor. Species B is the air.

 

At x = 0 (water surface), \(P_{A}=P_{s a t} \text { at } 20 C =2.339\) kPa (from steam tables)

 

At x = L (pail top), \(P_{A}=0 kPa \text { (dry air) }\)

 

L = average height of air above water surface \(=14 \text { inch } x (0.0254 m / inch )=0.3556 m\).

 

\(P=1 atm =101.325 kPa . M_{A}=\) molecular weight of water = 18 kg/kmol.

 

From Table 11.3, \(D_{A B}\) for a water-air mixture at 300 K and 1 atm is \(0.26 \times 10^{-4} m ^{2} / s\). Using Eq. (11.15), \(D_{A B}\) at 293.15 K and 1 atm is \(0.26 \times 10^{-4}\left(\frac{293.15}{300}\right)^{1.75}=0.25 \times 10^{-4} m ^{2} / s\).

 

\(\left(D_{A B}\right)_{2}=\left(D_{A B}\right)_{1}\left(\frac{P_{1}}{P_{2}}\right)\left(\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}\right)^{1.75}\) (11.15)

 

From Eq. (11.30), the mass flow of water vapor out of the pail is

 

\(\dot{m}_{A}=D_{A B} A\left(\frac{M_{A}}{\bar{R} T}\right)\left(\frac{P}{L}\right) \ln \left[\frac{\left(P-P_{A}\right)_{x=L}}{\left(P-P_{A}\right)_{x=0}}\right]\)

 

\(=\left(0.25 \times 10^{-4}\right)(0.0730)\left(\frac{18}{(8.31446)(293.15)}\right)\left(\frac{101.325}{0.3556}\right) \ln \left(\frac{101.325-0}{101.325-2.339}\right)\)

 

\(=8.97 \times 10^{-8} kg / s\)

 

The time to evaporate the 1.85 kg of water in the pail is therefore

 

\(\Delta t=\frac{1.85 kg }{8.97 \times 10^{-8} kg / s }=\left(2.06 \times 10^{7} s \right)\left(\frac{1 h }{3600 s }\right)\left(\frac{1 day }{24 h }\right)=238 \text { days }\)

 

It will take about 8 months to evaporate the 1 inch of water in the pail.
