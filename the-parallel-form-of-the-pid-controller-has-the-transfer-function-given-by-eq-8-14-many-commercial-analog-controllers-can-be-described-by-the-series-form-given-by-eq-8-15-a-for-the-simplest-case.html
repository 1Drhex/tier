Guest Question.

The parallel form of the PID controller has the transfer function given by Eq. 8-14. Many commercial analog controllers can be described by the series form given by Eq. 8-15.

\(\frac{P^{\prime}(s)}{E(s)}=K_{c}\left[1+\frac{1}{\tau_{I} s}+\tau_{D} s\right]\) (8-14)

\(\frac{P^{\prime}(s)}{E(s)}=K_{c}\left(\frac{\tau_{I} s+1}{\tau_{I} s}\right)\left(\frac{\tau_{D} s+1}{\alpha \tau_{D} s+1}\right)\) (8-15)

(a) For the simplest case, \(\alpha \rightarrow 0\), find the relations between the settings for the parallel form \(\left(K_{c}^{\dagger}, \tau_{l}^{\dagger}, \tau_{D}^{\dagger}\right)\) and the settings for the series form \(\left(K_{c}, \tau_{I}, \tau_{D}\right)\)

(b) Does the series form make each controller setting \(\left(K_{c}, \tau_{I}\right.\), or \(\tau_{D}\) ) larger or smaller than would be expected for the parallel form?

(c) What are the magnitudes of these interaction effects for \(K_{c}=4, \tau_{I}=10 min , \tau_{D}=2 min \)?

(d) What can you say about the effect of nonzero \(\alpha\) on these relations? (Discuss only first-order effects.)

Admin
Solution

a) From Eq. \(8-14\), the parallel form of the PID controller is:

\(\frac{P^{\prime}(s)}{E(s)}=K_{c}\left[1+\frac{1}{\tau_{I} s}+\tau_{D} s\right]\) (8-14)

\(G_{i}(s)=K_{c}^{\prime}\left[1+\frac{1}{\tau_{I}^{\prime} s}+\tau_{D}^{\prime} s\right]\)

From Eq. 8-15, for \(\alpha \rightarrow 0\), the series form of the PID controller is:

\(\frac{P^{\prime}(s)}{E(s)}=K_{c}\left(\frac{\tau_{I} s+1}{\tau_{I} s}\right)\left(\frac{\tau_{D} s+1}{\alpha \tau_{D} s+1}\right)\) (8-15)

\(\begin{aligned}G_{a}(s) &=K_{c}\left[1+\frac{1}{\tau_{I} s}\right]\left[\tau_{D} s+1\right] \\&=K_{c}\left[1+\frac{\tau_{D}}{\tau_{I}}+\frac{1}{\tau_{I} s}+\tau_{D} s\right] \\&=K_{c}\left(1+\frac{\tau_{D}}{\tau_{I}}\right)\left[1+\frac{1}{\left(1+\frac{\tau_{D}}{\tau_{I}}\right) \tau_{I} s}+\frac{\tau_{D} s}{\left(1+\frac{\tau_{D}}{\tau_{I}}\right)}\right]\end{aligned}\)

Comparing \(G_{a}(s)\) with \(G_{i}(s)\)

\(\begin{aligned}&K_{c}^{\prime}=K_{c}\left(1+\frac{\tau_{D}}{\tau_{I}}\right) \\&\tau_{I}^{\prime}=\tau_{I}\left(1+\frac{\tau_{D}}{\tau_{I}}\right)\end{aligned}\)

\(\tau_{D}^{\prime}=\frac{\tau_{D}}{1+\frac{\tau_{D}}{\tau_{I}}}\)

b) Since \(\left(1+\frac{\tau_{D}}{\tau_{I}}\right) \geq 1\) for all \(\tau_{D}, \tau_{I}\), therefore

\(K_{c} \leq K_{c}^{\prime} \quad, \quad \tau_{I} \leq \tau_{I}^{\prime} \text { and } \tau_{D} \geq \tau_{D}^{\prime}\)

c) For \(K_{c}=4, \tau_{I}=10 min , \tau_{D}=2 min\)

\(K_{c}^{\prime}=4.8, \quad \tau_{I}^{\prime}=12 min \quad, \quad \tau_{D}^{\prime}=1.67 min\)

d) Considering only first-order effects, a non-zero value \(\alpha\) will dampen all responses, making them slower.
