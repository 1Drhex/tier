Guest Question.

Use the method of sections to find the forces in members BC, BG, HG, and DG for the truss shown in Fig. 6.20(a).

Admin
Solution

The slope of the diagonal members of the truss is calculated in Fig. 6.20(c). The free-body diagram of the entire truss is shown in Fig. 6.20(b). For equilibrium of the entire truss,

\(ΣM_{A} = E_{y}(8) – 24(4) – 8(3) = 0\)

 

\(E_{y} = 15.0 \ kN \uparrow\)

 

\(ΣM_{E} = -A_{y}(8) – 8(3) + 24(4) = 0\)

 

\(A_{y} = 9.0 \ kN \uparrow\)

 

\(ΣF_{x} = -A_{x} + 8 = 0\)

 

\(A_{x} = 8.0 \ kN \longleftarrow\)

Check:

\(ΣF_{y} = A_{y} + E_{y} – 24 = 9 + 15 – 24 =0\) OK

The free-body diagram for part of the truss to the left of section 1-1 is shown in Fig. 6.20(d). The x and y components of the force \(F_{BG}\) are shown on the diagram. For equilibrium of the part of the truss to the left of section 1-1,

\(ΣM_{B} = F_{HG}(3) – 8(3) – 9(2) = 0\)

 

\(F_{HG} = 14.0 \ kN\) (tension)

 

\(ΣM_{G} = -F_{BC}(3) – 9(4) = 0\)

 

\(F_{BC} = -12.0 \ kN\) (compression)

 

\(ΣF_{y} = 9.0 – 0.832F_{BG} = 0\)

 

\(F_{BG} = 10.82 \ kN\) (tension)

Check:

\(ΣF_{x} = F_{HG} + 0.555F_{BG} + F_{BC} – 8\)

= 14.0 + 0.555(10.82) + (-12) – 8 =0 OK

The free-body diagram for part of the truss to the right of section 2-2 is shown in Fig. 6.20(e). The x and y components of the force \(F_{DG}\) are shown on the diagram. For equilibrium of the part of the truss to the right of section 2-2,

\(ΣF_{y} = 15 – 0.832F_{DG} = 0\)

 

\(F_{DG} = 18.03 \ kN\) (tension)
