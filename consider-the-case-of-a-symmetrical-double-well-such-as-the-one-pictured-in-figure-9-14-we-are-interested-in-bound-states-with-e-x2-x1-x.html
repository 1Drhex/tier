Guest Question.

Consider the case of a symmetrical double well, such as the one pictured in Figure 9.14. We are interested in bound states with E <V(x).

(a) Write down the WKB wave functions in regions (i) \(x>x_{2} \), \( x_{1}<x<x_{2}, \text { and (iii) } 0<x<x_{1} \) . Impose the appropriate connection formulas at x1 and x2 (this has already been done, in Equation 9.47, for \( x_{2} ; \text { you will have to work out } x_{1} \) for yourself), to show that

\( \psi(x) \approx \begin{cases}\frac{2 D}{\sqrt{p(x)}} \sin \left[\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x}^{x_{2}} p\left(x^{\prime}\right) d x^{\prime}+\frac{\pi}{4}\right], & x<x_{2} \\ \frac{D}{\sqrt{\mid p(x)}} \exp \left[-\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x_{2}}^{x}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime}\right], & x>x_{2}\end{cases} \) (9,47).

\( \psi(x) \approx\left\{\begin{array}{l} \frac{D}{\sqrt{|p(x)|}} \exp \left[-\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x_{2}}^{x}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime}\right], \\ \frac{2 D}{\sqrt{p(x)}} \sin \left[\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x}^{x_{2}} p\left(x^{\prime}\right) d x^{\prime}+\frac{\pi}{4}\right], \\ \frac{D}{\sqrt{|p(x)|}}\left[2 \cos \theta e^{\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x}^{x_{1}}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime}}+\sin \theta e^{-\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x}^{x_{1}}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime}}\right] \end{array}\right. \),

where

\( \theta \equiv \frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x_{1}}^{x_{2}} p(x) d x \) (9.59).

(b) Because V(x) is symmetric, we need only consider even (+) and odd (-) wave functions. In the former case \( \psi^{\prime}(0)=0 \), and in the latter case \( \psi(0)=0 \).

Show that this leads to the following quantization condition:

\( \tan \theta=\pm 2 e^{\phi} \), (9.60).

where

\( \phi \equiv \frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{-x_{1}}^{x_{1}}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime} \) (9.61).

Equation 9.60 determines the (approximate) allowed energies (note that E comes into \( x_{1} \text { and } x_{2} \text {, so } \theta \text { and } \phi \) are both functions of .

(c) We are particularly interested in a high and/or broad central barrier, in which case ϕ is large, and \( e^{\phi} \) is huge. Equation 9.60 then tells us that θ must be very close to a half-integer multiple of π. With this in mind, write \( \theta=(n+1 / 2) \pi+\epsilon, \text { where }|\epsilon| \ll 1 \), and show that the quantization condition becomes

\( \theta \approx\left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right) \pi \mp \frac{1}{2} e^{-\phi} \). (9.62).

(d) Suppose each well is a parabola:

\( V(x)= \begin{cases}\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2}(x+a)^{2}, & x<0 \\ \frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2}(x-a)^{2}, & x>0\end{cases} \) (9.63).

Sketch this potential, find θ (Equation 9.59), and show that

\( E_{n}^{\pm} \approx\left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right) \hbar \omega \mp \frac{\hbar \omega}{2 \pi} e^{-\phi} \) (9.64).

Comment: If the central barrier were impenetrable ( Φ →∞), we would simply have two detached harmonic oscillators, and the energies, \( E_{n}=(n+1 / 2) \hbar \omega \), would be doubly degenerate, since the particle could be in the left well or in the right one. When the barrier becomes finite (putting the two wells into “communication”), the degeneracy is lifted. The even states \( \left(\psi_{n}^{+}\right) \) have slightly lower energy, and the odd ones \( \left(\psi_{n}^{-}\right) \) have slightly higher energy.

(e) Suppose the particle starts out in the right well—or, more precisely, in a state of the form

\( \Psi(x, 0)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\psi_{n}^{+}+\psi_{n}^{-}\right) \),

which, assuming the phases are picked in the “natural” way, will be concentrated in the right well. Show that it oscillates back and forth between the wells, with a period

\( \tau=\frac{2 \pi^{2}}{\omega} e^{\phi} \) (9.65).

(f) Calculate ϕ, for the specific potential in part (d), and show that for

\( V(0) \gg E, \phi \sim m \omega a^{2} / \hbar \).

Solution

(a) (i) \( \psi_{ WKB }(x)=\frac{D}{\sqrt{|p(x)|}} e^{-\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x_{2}}^{x}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime}} \) \( \left(x>x_{2}\right) \);

\( \text { (ii) } \psi_{ WKB }(x)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{p(x)}}\left[B e^{\frac{i}{\hbar} \int_{x}^{x_{2}} p\left(x^{\prime}\right) d x^{\prime}}+C e^{-\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x}^{x_{2}} p\left(x^{\prime}\right) d x^{\prime}}\right]\left(x_{1}<x<x_{2}\right) \);

\( \text { (iii) } \psi_{ WKB }(x)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{|p(x)|}}\left[F e^{\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x}^{x_{1}}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime}}+G e^{-\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x}^{x_{1}}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime}}\right] \quad\left(0<x<x_{1}\right) \).

Equation 9.47 ⇒ \( \text { (ii) } \psi_{ WKB }=\frac{2 D}{\sqrt{p(x)}} \sin \left[\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x}^{x_{2}} p\left(x^{\prime}\right) d x^{\prime}+\frac{\pi}{4}\right] \) \( \left(x_{1}<x<x_{2}\right) \).

\( \psi(x) \approx \begin{cases}\frac{2 D}{\sqrt{p(x)}} \sin \left[\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x}^{x_{2}} p\left(x^{\prime}\right) d x^{\prime}+\frac{\pi}{4}\right], & x<x_{2} \\ \frac{D}{\sqrt{\mid p(x)}} \exp \left[-\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x_{2}}^{x}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime}\right], & x>x_{2}\end{cases} \) (9,47).

To effect the join at \( x_1\) , first rewrite (ii):

\( \text { (ii) } \psi_{ WKB }=\frac{2 D}{\sqrt{p(x)}} \sin \left[\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x_{1}}^{x_{2}} p\left(x^{\prime}\right) d x^{\prime}-\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x_{1}}^{x} p\left(x^{\prime}\right) d x^{\prime}+\frac{\pi}{4}\right]=-\frac{2 D}{\sqrt{p(x)}} \sin \left[\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x_{1}}^{x} p\left(x^{\prime}\right) d x^{\prime}-\theta-\frac{\pi}{4}\right] \).

where θ is defined in Eq. 9.59. Now shift the origin to \( x_1\) :

\( \psi_{ WKB }=\left\{\begin{array}{cc} \frac{1}{\sqrt{|p(x)|}}\left[F e^{\frac{1}{\hbar}} \int_{x}^{0}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime}+G e^{-\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x}^{0}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime}}\right](x<0) \\ -\frac{2 D}{\sqrt{p(x)}} \sin \left[\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{0}^{x} p\left(x^{\prime}\right) d x^{\prime}-\theta-\frac{\pi}{4}\right] & (x>0) \end{array}\right\} \).

\( \theta \equiv \frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x_{1}}^{x_{2}} p(x) d x \) (9.59).

Following : \( \psi_{p}(x)=a A i(-\alpha x)+b B i(-\alpha x), \text { with } \alpha \equiv\left(\frac{2 m\left|V^{\prime}(0)\right|}{\hbar^{2}}\right)^{1 / 3} ; p(x)=\hbar \alpha^{3 / 2} \sqrt{x}\).

\( \underline{\text { Overlap region } 1(x<0):} \int_{x}^{0}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime}=\frac{2}{3} \hbar(-\alpha x)^{3 / 2} \).

\( \left.\begin{array}{rl} \psi_{ WKB } & \approx \frac{1}{\hbar^{1 / 2} \alpha^{3 / 4}(-x)^{1 / 4}}\left[F e^{\frac{2}{3}(-\alpha x)^{3 / 2}}+G e^{-\frac{2}{3}(-\alpha x)^{3 / 2}}\right] \\ \psi_{p} & \approx \frac{a}{2 \sqrt{\pi}(-\alpha x)^{1 / 4}} e^{-\frac{2}{3}(-\alpha x)^{3 / 2}}+\frac{b}{\sqrt{\pi}(-\alpha x)^{1 / 4}} e^{\frac{2}{3}(-\alpha x)^{3 / 2}} \end{array}\right\} \Longrightarrow a=2 G \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{\hbar \alpha}} ; \quad b=F \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{\hbar \alpha}} \).

\( \underline{\text { Overlap region } 2(x>0):} \int_{0}^{x} p\left(x^{\prime}\right) d x^{\prime}=\frac{2}{3} \hbar(\alpha x)^{3 / 2} \).

\( \Longrightarrow \psi_{ WKB } \approx-\frac{2 D}{\hbar^{1 / 2} \alpha^{3 / 4} x^{1 / 4}} \sin \left[\frac{2}{3}(\alpha x)^{3 / 2}-\theta-\frac{\pi}{4}\right] \).

\( \psi_{p} \approx \frac{a}{\sqrt{\pi}(\alpha x)^{1 / 4}} \sin \left[\frac{2}{3}(\alpha x)^{3 / 2}+\frac{\pi}{4}\right]+\frac{b}{\sqrt{\pi}(\alpha x)^{1 / 4}} \cos \left[\frac{2}{3}(\alpha x)^{3 / 2}+\frac{\pi}{4}\right] \).

Equating the two expressions: \( \frac{-2 D}{\hbar^{1 / 2} \alpha^{3 / 4}} \frac{1}{2 i}\left[e^{i \frac{2}{3}(\alpha x)^{3 / 2}} e^{-i \theta} e^{-i \pi / 4}-e^{-i \frac{2}{3}(\alpha x)^{3 / 2}} e^{i \theta} e^{i \pi / 4}\right] \).

\( =\frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi} \alpha^{1 / 4}}\left\{\frac{a}{2 i}\left[e^{i \frac{2}{3}(\alpha x)^{3 / 2}} e^{i \pi / 4}-e^{-i \frac{2}{3}(\alpha x)^{3 / 2}} e^{-i \pi / 4}\right]+\frac{b}{2}\left[e^{i \frac{2}{3}(\alpha x)^{3 / 2}} e^{i \pi / 4}+e^{-i \frac{2}{3}(\alpha x)^{3 / 2}} e^{-i \pi / 4}\right]\right\} \),

\( \Longrightarrow\left\{\begin{array}{lll} -2 D \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{\alpha \hbar}} e^{-i \theta} e^{-i \pi / 4}=(a+i b) e^{i \pi / 4}, \quad \text { or } \quad(a+i b)=2 D \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{\alpha \hbar}} i e^{-i \theta} \\ 2 D \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{\alpha \hbar}} e^{i \theta} e^{i \pi / 4}=(-a+i b) e^{-i \pi / 4}, \quad \text { or } \quad(a-i b)=-2 D \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{\alpha \hbar}} i e^{i \theta}\end{array}\right\}\).

\( \Longrightarrow\left\{\begin{array}{l} 2 a=2 D \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{\alpha \hbar}} i\left(e^{-i \theta}-e^{i \theta}\right) \Rightarrow a=2 D \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{\alpha \hbar}} \sin \theta \\ 2 i b=2 D \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{\alpha \hbar}} i\left(e^{-i \theta}+e^{i \theta}\right) \Rightarrow b=2 D \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{\alpha \hbar}} \cos \theta\end{array}\right\} \).

Combining these with the results from overlap region 1 ⇒

\( 2 G \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{\alpha \hbar}}=2 D \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{\alpha \hbar}} \sin \theta, \quad \text { or } \quad G=D \sin \theta ; \quad F \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{\alpha \hbar}}=2 D \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{\alpha \hbar}} \cos \theta, \quad \text { or } \quad F=2 D \cos \theta \).

Putting these into (iii) : \( \psi_{ WKB }(x)=\frac{D}{\sqrt{|p(x)|}}\left[2 \cos \theta e^{\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x}^{x_{1}}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime}}+\sin \theta e^{-\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x}^{x_{1}}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime}}\right] \) \( \left(0<x<x_{1}\right) \).

(b)

\(\operatorname{Odd}(-) \text { case: (iii) } \Longrightarrow \psi(0)=0 \Rightarrow 2 \cos \theta e^{\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{0}^{x_{1}}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime}}+\sin \theta e^{-\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{0}^{x_{1}}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| dx^{\prime}}=0\).

\( \frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{0}^{x_{1}}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime}=\frac{1}{2} \phi, \text { with } \phi \text { defined by Eq. 8.60. So } \sin \theta e^{-\phi / 2}=-2 \cos \theta e^{\phi / 2}, \text { or } \tan \theta=-2 e^{\phi} \).

\( A_{\pm}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\left(1 \pm I^{2}\right)}} \) (8.60).

\( \operatorname{Even}(+) \text { case: (iii) } \Longrightarrow \psi^{\prime}(0)=0 \Rightarrow-\left.\frac{1}{2} \frac{D}{(|p(x)|)^{3 / 2}} \frac{d|p(x)|}{d x}\right|_{0}\left[2 \cos \theta e^{\phi / 2}+\sin \theta e^{-\phi / 2}\right] \) \( +\frac{D}{\sqrt{|p(x)|}}\left[2 \cos \theta e^{\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{0}^{x_{1}}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime}}\left(-\frac{1}{\hbar}|p(0)|\right)+\sin \theta e^{-\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{0}^{x_{1}}\left|p\left(x^{\prime}\right)\right| d x^{\prime}}\left(\frac{1}{\hbar}|p(0)|\right)\right]=0 \)

\( \text { Now } \quad \frac{d|p(x)|}{d x}=\frac{d}{d x} \sqrt{2 m[V(x)-E]}=\sqrt{2 m} \frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{\sqrt{V-E}} \frac{d V}{d x}, \text { and }\left.\frac{d V}{d x}\right|_{0}=0, \text { so }\left.\frac{d|p(x)|}{d x}\right|_{0}=0 \).

\( 2 \cos \theta e^{\phi / 2}=\sin \theta e^{-\phi / 2} \text {, or } \tan \theta=2 e^{\phi} \text {. Combining the two results: } \tan \theta=\pm 2 e^{\phi} \).QED

(c)

\( \tan \theta=\tan \left[\left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right) \pi+\epsilon\right]=\frac{\sin \left[\left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right) \pi+\epsilon\right]}{\cos \left[\left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right) \pi+\epsilon\right]}=\frac{(-1)^{n} \cos \epsilon}{(-1)^{n+1} \sin \epsilon}=-\frac{\cos \epsilon}{\sin \epsilon} \approx-\frac{1}{\epsilon} \).

\( \text { So }-\frac{1}{\epsilon} \approx \pm 2 e^{\phi}, \text { or } \epsilon \approx \mp \frac{1}{2} e^{-\phi}, \text { or } \theta-\left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right) \pi \approx \mp \frac{1}{2} e^{-\phi}, \quad \text { so } \theta \approx\left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right) \pi \mp \frac{1}{2} e^{-\phi} \). QED

[Note: Since θ (Eq. 9.59) is positive, n must be a non-negative integer: n = 0, 1, 2….. This is like harmonic oscillator (conventional) numbering, since it starts with n = 0.]

\( \theta \equiv \frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x_{1}}^{x_{2}} p(x) d x \) (9.59).

(d)

\( \theta=\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x_{1}}^{x_{2}} \sqrt{2 m\left[E-\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2}(x-a)^{2}\right]} d x . \quad \text { Let } z=x-a \) (shifts the origin to a).

\( =\frac{2}{\hbar} \int_{0}^{z_{2}} \sqrt{2 m\left[E-\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} z^{2}\right]} d z, \quad \text { where } \quad E=\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} z_{2}^{2} \).

\( =\frac{2}{\hbar} m \omega \int_{0}^{z_{2}} \sqrt{z_{2}^{2}-z^{2}} d z=\left.\frac{m \omega}{\hbar}\left[z \sqrt{z_{2}^{2}-z^{2}}+z_{2}^{2} \sin ^{-1}\left(z / z_{2}\right)\right]\right|_{0} ^{z_{2}}=\frac{m \omega}{\hbar} z_{2}^{2} \sin ^{-1}(1)=\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{m \omega}{\hbar} z_{2}^{2} \).

\( =\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{m \omega}{\hbar} \frac{2 E}{m \omega^{2}}=\frac{\pi E}{\hbar \omega} \).

Putting this into Eq. 9.62 yields \( \frac{\pi E}{\hbar \omega} \approx\left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right) \pi \mp \frac{1}{2} e^{-\phi}, \quad \text { or } E_{n}^{\pm} \approx\left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right) \hbar \omega \mp \frac{\hbar \omega}{2 \pi} e^{-\phi} \).

\( \theta \approx\left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right) \pi \mp \frac{1}{2} e^{-\phi} \). (9.62).

(e)

\( \Psi(x, t)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\psi_{n}^{+} e^{-i E_{n}^{+} t / \hbar}+\psi_{n}^{-} e^{-i E_{n}^{-} t / \hbar}\right) \Longrightarrow \)

\( |\Psi(x, t)|^{2}=\frac{1}{2}\left[\left|\psi_{n}^{+}\right|^{2}+\left|\psi_{n}^{-}\right|^{2}+\psi_{n}^{+} \psi_{n}^{-}\left(e^{i\left(E_{n}^{-}-E_{n}^{+}\right) t / \hbar}+e^{-i\left(E_{n}^{-}-E_{n}^{+}\right) t / \hbar}\right)\right] \).

(Note that the wave functions (i), (ii), (iii) are real ). But \( \frac{E_{n}^{-}-E_{n}^{+}}{\hbar} \approx \frac{1}{\hbar} 2 \frac{\hbar \omega}{2 \pi} e^{-\phi}=\frac{\omega}{\pi} e^{-\phi}, \text { so } \).

\( |\Psi(x, t)|^{2}=\frac{1}{2}\left[\psi_{n}^{+}(x)^{2}+\psi_{n}^{-}(x)^{2}\right]+\psi_{n}^{+}(x) \psi_{n}^{-}(x) \cos \left(\frac{\omega}{\pi} e^{-\phi} t\right) \).

It oscillates back and forth, with period \( \tau=\frac{2 \pi}{(\omega / \pi) e^{-\phi}}=\frac{2 \pi^{2}}{\omega} e^{\phi} \). QED

(f)

\( \phi=2 \frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{0}^{x_{1}} \sqrt{2 m\left[\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2}(x-a)^{2}-E\right]} d x=\frac{2}{\hbar} \sqrt{2 m E} \int_{0}^{x_{1}} \sqrt{\frac{m \omega^{2}}{2 E}(x-a)^{2}-1} d x \).

\( \text { Let } z \equiv \sqrt{\frac{m}{2 E}} \omega(a-x), \quad \text { so } d x=-\sqrt{\frac{2 E}{m}} \frac{1}{\omega} d z . \quad \text { Limits: }\left\{\begin{array}{l}x=0 \Rightarrow z=\sqrt{\frac{m}{2 E}} \omega a \equiv z_{0} \\ x=x_{1} \Longrightarrow \text { radicand }=0 \Longrightarrow z=1\end{array}\right\} \).

\( \phi=\frac{2}{\hbar} \sqrt{2 m E} \sqrt{\frac{2 E}{m}} \frac{1}{\omega} \int_{1}^{z_{0}} \sqrt{z^{2}-1} d z=\frac{4 E}{\hbar \omega} \int_{1}^{z_{0}} \sqrt{z^{2}-1} d z=\left.\frac{4 E}{\hbar \omega} \frac{1}{2}\left[z \sqrt{z^{2}-1}-\ln \left(z+\sqrt{z^{2}-1}\right)\right]\right|_{1} ^{z_{0}} \).

= \( \frac{2 E}{\hbar \omega}\left[z_{0} \sqrt{z_{0}^{2}-1}-\ln \left(z_{0}+\sqrt{z_{0}^{2}-1}\right)\right] \),

where \( z_{0}=a \omega \sqrt{\frac{m}{2 E}} \).

\( V(0)=\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} a^{2}, \quad \text { so } \quad V(0) \gg E \Rightarrow \frac{m}{2} \omega^{2} a^{2} \gg E \Rightarrow a \omega \sqrt{\frac{m}{2 E}} \gg 1, \quad \text { or } z_{0} \gg 1 \).

In that case

\( \phi \approx \frac{2 E}{\hbar \omega}\left[z_{0}^{2}-\ln \left(2 z_{0}\right)\right] \approx \frac{2 E}{\hbar \omega} z_{0}^{2}=\frac{2 E}{\hbar \omega} a^{2} \omega^{2} \frac{m}{2 E}=\frac{m \omega a^{2}}{\hbar} \).

This, together with Eq. 9.65, gives us the period of oscillation in a double well.

\( \theta \equiv \frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{x_{1}}^{x_{2}} p(x) d x \) (9.59).
