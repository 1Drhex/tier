Guest Question.

A single-phase, 50-kVA, 2400/240-V, 60-Hz distribution transformer has the following parameters:
Resistance of the 2400-V winding: \(R _{1}\) = 0.75 Ω
Resistance of the 240-V winding: \(R _{2}\) = 0.0075 Ω
Leakage reactance of the 2400-V winding: \(X _{1}\) = 1.0 Ω
Leakage reactance of the 240-V winding: \(X _{2}\) = 0.01 Ω
Exciting admittance on the 240-V side = 0.003 – j0.02 S
(a) Draw the equivalent circuit referred to the high-voltage side of the transformer.
(b) Draw the equivalent circuit referred to the low-voltage side of the transformer. Show the numerical values of impedances on the equivalent circuits.

Admin
Solution

(a)

\(\begin{aligned}&a=2400 / 240=10 \\&R_{2}^{\prime}=a^{2} R_{2}=\left(\frac{2400}{240}\right)^{2} 0.0075=0.75 \Omega \\&X_{2}^{\prime}=a^{2} X_{2}=(10)^{2} 0.01=1.0 \Omega\end{aligned}\)

Referred to the HV-side, the exciting branch conductance and susceptance are given by

\(\begin{aligned}&\left(1 / a^{2}\right) 0.003=(1 / 100) 0.003=0.03 \times 10^{-3} S \\\text { and } &\left(1 / a^{2}\right) 0.02=(1 / 100) 0.02=0.2 \times 10^{-3} S\end{aligned}\)
