Guest Question.

Determine the member end moments and reactions for the continuous beam shown in Fig. \( 15.7 (\mathrm{a})\) by the slope-deflection method.

Admin
Solution

This beam was previously analyzed in by the method of consistent deformations.

From Fig. 15.7(a), we can see that all three joints of the beam are free to rotate. Thus the beam can be considered to have three degrees of freedom, \(\theta_{A}, \theta_{B}\), and \(\theta_{D}\), and it can be analyzed by using the usual slope-deflection equations (Eq. (15.9)) for members rigidly connected at both ends. However, this approach is quite time consuming, since it requires solving three simultaneous equations to determine the three unknown joint rotations.

\( M_{n f}=\frac{2 E I}{L}\left(2 \theta_{n}+\theta_{f}-3 \psi\right)+\mathrm{FEM}_{n f} \) (15.9)

Since the end supports \(A\) and \(D\) of the beam are simple supports at which no external moment is applied, the moments at the end \(A\) of member \(A B\) and at the end \(D\) of member \(B D\) must be zero. (This can easily be verified by considering moment equilibrium of the free bodies of joints \(A\) and \(D\) shown in Fig. 15.7(b).) Thus the end \(A\) of member \(A B\) and the end \(D\) of member \(B D\) can be considered to be hinged ends, and the modified slope-deflection equations (Eqs. (15.15)) can be used for these members. Furthermore, since the modified slope-deflection equations do not contain the rotations of the hinged ends, by using these equations the rotations \(\theta_{A}\) and \(\theta_{D}\) of the simple supports can be eliminated from the analysis, which will then involve only one unknown joint rotation, \(\theta_{B}\). It should be noted that once \(\theta_{B}\) has been evaluated, the values of the rotations \(\theta_{A}\) and \(\theta_{D}\), if desired, can be computed by using Eq. (15.16). In the following, we use this simplified approach to analyze the continuous beam.

\( \begin{array}{c} M_{r h}=\frac{3 E I}{L}\left(\theta_{r}-\psi\right)+\left(\mathrm{FEM}_{r h}-\frac{\mathrm{FEM}_{h r}}{2}\right) \\\end{array} \) (15.15a)

\( \begin{array}{c} M_{h r}=0 \\\end{array} \) (15.15b)

\( \begin{array}{c} \theta_{h}=-\frac{\theta_{r}}{2}+\frac{3}{2} \psi-\frac{L}{4 E I}\left(\mathrm{FEM}_{h r}\right) \end{array} \) (15.16)

Degrees of Freedom. \(\theta_{B}\)

Fixed-End Moments.

\( \begin{array}{l} \mathrm{FEM}_{A B}=\frac{15(10)^{2}}{12}=125 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}\curvearrowleft \quad \text { or } \quad+125 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ \mathrm{FEM}_{B A}=125 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}\curvearrowright \quad \text { or } \quad-125 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ \mathrm{FEM}_{B D}=\frac{60(10)}{8}+\frac{15(10)^{2}}{12}=200 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \curvearrowleft \quad \text { or } \quad+200 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ \mathrm{FEM}_{D B}=200 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \curvearrowright \quad \text { or } \quad-200 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \end{array} \)

Slope-Deflection Equations. Since both members of the beam have one end hinged, we use Eqs. (15.15) to obtain the slopedeflection equations for both members. Thus

\( \begin{array}{c} M_{r h}=\frac{3 E I}{L}\left(\theta_{r}-\psi\right)+\left(\mathrm{FEM}_{r h}-\frac{\mathrm{FEM}_{h r}}{2}\right) \\\end{array} \) (15.15a)

\( \begin{array}{c} M_{h r}=0 \\\end{array} \) (15.15b)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{A B}=0 \\ M_{B A}=\frac{3 E I}{10}\left(\theta_{B}\right)+\left(-125-\frac{125}{2}\right)=0.3 E I \theta_{B}-187.5 \\\end{array} \) (1)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{B D}=\frac{3 E(2 I)}{10}\left(\theta_{B}\right)+\left(200+\frac{200}{2}\right)=0.6 E I \theta_{B}+300 \\ M_{D B}=0 \end{array} \) (2)

Equilibrium Equation. By considering the moment equilibrium of the free body of joint \(B\) (Fig. 15.7(b)), we obtain the equilibrium equation

\( M_{B A}+M_{B D}=0 \) (3)

Joint Rotation. To determine the unknown joint rotation \(\theta_{B}\), we substitute the slope-deflection equations (Eqs. (1) and (2)) into the equilibrium equation (Eq. (3)) to obtain

\( \left(0.3 E I \theta_{B}-187.5\right)+\left(0.6 E I \theta_{B}+300\right)=0 \)

or

\( 0.9 E I \theta_{B}=-112.5 \)

from which

\( E I \theta_{B}=-125 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{2} \)

Member End Moments. The member end moments can now be computed by substituting the numerical value of \(E I \theta_{B}\) into the slope-deflection equations (Eqs. (1) and (2)). Thus

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{B A}=0.3(-125)-187.5=-225 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \quad \text { or } \quad 225 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \curvearrowright \\ M_{B D}=0.6(-125)+300=225 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}\curvearrowleft \end{array} \)

Member End Shears and Support Reactions. See Fig. 15.7(c) and (d).

Equilibrium Check. See Fig. 15.7(d).

\(\begin{array}{rr} +\uparrow \sum F_{y}=0 \quad 52.5-15(20)+225-60+82.5=0 \quad & \\ +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{D}=0 & \\ -52.5(20)+15(20)(10)-225(10)+60(5)=0 & \end{array} \)
