Inductive reactance: three-phase line with bundled conductors

Each of the 1,590,000 cmil conductors in is replaced by two 795,000 cmil ACSR 26/2 conductors, as shown in Figure 4.15. Bundle spacing is 0.40 m. Flat horizontal spacing is retained, with 10 m between adjacent bundle centers. Calculate the inductive reactance of the line and compare it with that of .

Admin
Solution

From Table A.4, the GMR of a 795,000 cmil 26/2 ACSR conductor is

\(D_{s} =0.0375ft\times \frac{1m}{3.28ft}=0.0114m \)

 

From (4.6.19), the two-conductor bundle GMR is

 

\(D_{SL} =\sqrt[4]{\left(D_{S} \times d\right)^{2} } =\sqrt{D_{S}d }\)

 

\(D_{SL} =\sqrt{\left(0.0114\right)\left(0.40\right) }=0.0676m \)

 

Since \( D_{eq}= 12.6\) m is the same as in ,

 

\(L_{a}=2\times 10^{-7} \ln \left(\frac{12.6}{0.0676}\right) \left(1000\right) \left(200\right) =0.209\) H

 

\(X_{a}=2\pi fL_{1}=\left(2\pi \right)\left(60\right) \left(0.209\right)=78.8\Omega \)

 

The reactance of the bundled line, 78.8 Î©, is 22% less than that of , even though the two conductor bundle has the same amount of conductor material (that is, the same cmil per phase). One advantage of reduced series line reactance is smaller line-voltage drops. Also, the loadability of medium and long EHV lines is increased (see Chapter 5).
