Guest Question.

GEAR SPECIFICATION
Continue the case study by specifying appropriate gears, including pitch diameter,diametral pitch, face width, and material. Achieve safety factors of at least 1.2 for wear and bending.

Admin
Solution

Estimate the minimum diametral pitch for overall gearbox height = 22 in.

From Eq. (18–3) and Fig. 18–1,

\(P = (N_{3} + N_{2}/2 + N_{5}/2 + 2)/(Y − clearances − wall thicknesses)\) (18–3)

\(P_{min} =\frac{\left(N_{3} +\frac{N_{2}}{2} +\frac{N_{5}}{2} + 2\right)}{(Y − clearances − wall thickness)}\)

Allow 1.5 in for clearances and wall thicknesses:

\(P_{min} =\frac{\left(72 +\frac{16}{2} +\frac{72}{2}+ 2\right)}{(22 − 1.5)} = 5.76\) teeth/in

Start with P = 6 teeth/in

\(d_{2} = d_{4} = N_{2}/P = 16/6 = 2.67\) in
\(d_{3} = d_{5} = 72/6 = 12.0\) in

Shaft speeds were previously determined to be

\(ω_{2} = 1750 rev/min ω_{3} = ω_{4} = 388.9 rev/min ω_{5} = 86.4 rev/min\)

Get pitch-line velocities and transmitted loads for later use.

\(V_{23} =\frac{πd_{2}ω_{2}}{12} =\frac{π(2.67)(1750)}{12} = 1223\) ft/ min Eq. (13–34), p. 687

\(V = πdn/12 \) (13–34)

\(V_{45} =\frac{πd_{5}ω_{5}}{12} = 271.5\) ft/ min

\(W^{t}_{23} = 33000\frac{H}{V_{23}} = 33000 \left(\frac{20}{1223}\right)= 540.0\) lbf Eq. (13–35), p. 687

\(W_{t} = 33 000 \frac{H}{V}\) (13–35)
\(W^{t}_{45}= 33000 \frac{H}{V_{45}} = 2431\) lbf

Start with gear 4, since it is the smallest gear, transmitting the largest load.
It will likely be critical. Start with wear by contact stress, since it is often the limiting factor.

Gear 4 Wear

\(I =\frac{cos 20° sin 20°}{2(1)} \left(\frac{4.5}{4.5 + 1}\right)= 0.1315\) Eq. (14–23), p. 735

\(I =\begin{cases} \frac{cos \phi_{t} sin \phi_{t}}{2m_{N}}{m_{G}}{m_{G} + 1} & external gears \\ \frac{cos \phi_{t} sin \phi_{t}}{2m_{N}}{m_{G}}{m_{G} – 1}& internal gears \end{cases}\) (14.23)
For \(K_{v}, assume Q_{v} = 7. B = 0.731, A = 65.1\) Eq. (14–29), p. 736

\((V_{t} )_{max} =\begin{cases}[A + (Q_{v} − 3)]^{2} & ft/min\\\frac{ [A + (Q_{v} − 3)]^{2}}{200}& m/s\end{cases}\) (14–29)
\(K_{v} =\left(\frac{65.1 + \sqrt{271.5}}{65.1}\right)^{0.731}= 1.18\) Eq. (14–27), p. 736

\(K_{v} =\begin{cases} \left(\frac{A + \sqrt{V}}{A}\right)^{B} & V in ft/min\\ \left(\frac{A + \sqrt{200V}}{A}\right)^{B}&V in m/s\end{cases}\) (14-27)

Face width F is typically from 3 to 5 times circular pitch. Try

\(F = 4\left(\frac{π}{P}\right)= 4 \left(\frac{π}{6}\right)= 2.09\) in .

Since gear specifications are readily available on the Internet, we might as well check for commonly available face widths. On www.globalspec.com, entering P = 6 teeth/in and d = 2.67 in, stock spur gears from several sources have face widths of 1.5 in or 2.0 in. These are also available for the meshing gear 5 with d = 12 in.

Choose F = 2.0 in.

For \(K_{m}, C_{p f} = 0.0624\) Eq. (14–32), p. 740

\(C_{p f} =\begin{cases} \frac{F}{10d} − 0.025 & F ≤ 1 in \\ \frac{F}{10d} − 0.0375 + 0.0125F & 1 < F ≤ 17 in\\ \frac{F}{10d} − 0.1109 + 0.0207F − 0.000 228F^{2}&17 < F ≤ 40 in \end{cases} \) (14-32)

\(C_{mc}\) = 1 uncrowned teeth Eq.(14–31), p. 740

\(C_{mc} =\begin{cases} 1&for uncrowned teeth\\ 0.8&for crowned teeth\end {cases}\) (14-31)
\(C_{pm}\) = 1 straddle-mounted Eq. (14–33), p. 740

\(C_{p m} =\begin{cases} 1& for straddle-mounted pinion with S_{1}/S < 0.175 \\ 1.1 & for straddle-mounted pinion with S_{1}/S ≥ 0.175 \end{cases}\) (14.33)
\(C_{ma}\) = 0.15 commercial enclosed unit Eq. (14–34), p. 740

\(C_{e}\) = 1 Eq. (14–35), p. 740

\(C_{e} =\begin{cases} 0.8&for gearing adjusted at assembly, or compatibility is improved by lapping, or both\\ 1&for all other conditions\end {cases}\) (14-35)
\(K_{m}\) = 1.21 Eq. (14–30), p. 739

\(K_{m} = C_{mf} = 1 + C_{mc}(C_{p f} C_{pm} + C_{ma}C_{e}) \) (14–30)
\(C_{p}\) = 2300 Table 14–8, p. 737

\(K_{o} = K_{s} = C_{f}\) = 1

\(σ_{c} = 2300 \sqrt{ \frac{2431(1.18)(1.21)}{2.67(2)(0.1315)}} = 161 700\) psi Eq. (14–16), p. 726

\(σ_{c} =\begin{cases} C_{p} \sqrt{ W^{t} K_{o}K_{v}K_{s} \frac{K_{m}}{d_{P F}} \frac{C_{f}}{I}} & (U.S. customary units) \\ C_{p} \sqrt{ W^{t} K_{o}K_{v}K_{s} \frac{K_{H}}{d_{w1}b} \frac{Z_{R}}{Z_{I}}}& (SI units) \end{cases} \) (14-16)

Get factors for \(σ_{c.all}\). For life factor \(Z_{N}\) , get number of cycles for specified life of 12 000 h.

\(L_{4} = (12 000 h) \left(60 \frac{min}{h}\right) \left( 389 \frac{rev}{min}\right)= 2.8 × 10^{8} rev\)

\(Z_{N} = 0.9\) Fig. 14–15, p. 743
\(K_{R} = K_{T} = C_{H} = 1\)

For a design factor of 1.2,

\(σ_{c.all} = S_{c} Z_{N} /S_{H} = σ_{c}\) Eq. (14–18), p. 730

\(σ_{c,all} =\begin{cases} \frac{S_{c}}{S_{H}} \frac{Z_{N}C_{H}}{K_{T} K_{R}} & (U.S. customary units)\\ \frac{S_{c}}{S_{H}}\frac{Z_{N} Z_{W}}{Y_{θ}Y_{Z}}&(SI units)\end{cases}\) (14–18)

\(S_{c} =\frac{S_{H}σ_{c}}{Z_{N}} =\frac{1.2(161 700)}{0.9} = 215 600 psi\)

From Table 14–6, p. 731, this strength is achievable with Grade 2 carburized and hardened with \(S_{c}\) = 225 000 psi. To find the achieved factor of safety, \(n_{c} = σ_{c,all}/σ_{c}\) with \(S_{H}\) = 1. The factor of safety for wear of gear 4 is

\(n_{c} =\frac{σ_{c,all}}{σ_{c}} =\frac{S_{c} Z_{N}}{σ_{c}} =\frac{225 000(0.9)}{161 700} = 1.25\)
