Guest Question.

For the plate bending problems (CPT and SDT) given in Fig. P12.2, give the specified primary and secondary degrees of freedom and their values for the meshes shown. The dashed lines in the figures indicate simply supported boundary conditions Use E, \(\nu \), h, a, and b in formulating the data. You are required to give values of the loads for at least a couple of representative loads.

Admin
Solution

For CPT, use \(\frac{\partial w}{\partial x}\) in place of \(\phi_{x} \text { and } \frac{\partial w}{\partial y}\) in place of \(\phi_{y}\).

Use symmetry about x = 0 line. All primary degrees of freedom are zero along the y = 0 line (fixed edge); w = 0 and \(\frac{\partial w}{\partial x} \text { or } \phi_{x}=0\) along the symmetry line (i.e., x = 0 line). Nodal forces at the y = 10 in. line for FSDT are given by \(\frac{q_{0} h}{2}\) at the outside nodes and \(q_{0}h\) at the inner nodes, h is the element length parallel to the x-axis). Thus, we have \(F_{7}=F_{9}=750 lb \text { and } F_{8}=1,500 lb\) .
