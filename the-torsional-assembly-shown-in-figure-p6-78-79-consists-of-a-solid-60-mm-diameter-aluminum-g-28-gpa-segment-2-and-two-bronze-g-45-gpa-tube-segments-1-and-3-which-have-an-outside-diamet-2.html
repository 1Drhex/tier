Guest Question.

The torsional assembly shown in Figure P6.78/79 consists of a solid 60-mm-diameter aluminum [G = 28 GPa] segment (2) and two bronze [G = 45 GPa] tube segments (1) and (3), which have an outside diameter of 75 mm and a wall thickness of 5 mm. If concentrated torques of \(T_{B}\) = 9 kN-m and \(T_{C}\) = 9 kN-m are applied in the directions shown, determine:
(a) the maximum shear stress magnitude in bronze tube segments (1) and (3).
(b) the maximum shear stress magnitude in aluminum segment (2).
(c) the rotation angle of joint C.

Admin
Solution

Section Properties: The polar moments of inertia for the aluminum and bronze segments are:

\(\begin{aligned}&J_{1}=J_{3}=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[(75 mm )^{4}-(65 mm )^{4}\right]=1,353,830 mm ^{4} \\&J_{2}=\frac{\pi}{32}(60 mm )^{4}=1,272,345 mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Equilibrium: Consider a free-body diagram cut around joint B, where the external torque \(T_{B}\) is applied:

\(\Sigma M_{x}=-T_{1}+T_{2}+T_{B}=0\) (a)

and also consider a FBD cut around joint C, where the external torque \(T_{C}\) is applied:

\(\Sigma M_{x}=-T_{2}+T_{3}-T_{C}=0\) (b)

Geometry-of-Deformation Relationship: Since the two ends of the torsion structure are securely attached to fixed supports at A and D, the sum of the angles of twist in the three shafts must equal zero:

\(\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}+\phi_{3}=0\) (c)

Torque-Twist Relationships:

\(\phi_{1}=\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}} \quad \phi_{2}=\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}} \quad \phi_{3}=\frac{T_{3} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}\) (d)

Compatibility Equation: Substitute the torque-twist relationships [Eqs. (d)] into the Geometry-ofdeformation relationship [Eq. (c)] to obtain the compatibility equation:

\(\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}+\frac{T_{3} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}=0\) (e)

Solve the Equations: The approach used here will be to reduce the variables in Eq. (e) by replacing \(T_{1}\) and \(T_{3}\) with equivalent expressions involving \(T_{2}\). From Eq. (a):

\(T_{1}=T_{2}+T_{B}\) (f)

and from Eq. (b):

\(T_{3}=T_{2}+T_{C}\) (g)

Substitute Eqs. (f) and (g) into Eq. (e) and simplify to derive an expression for \(T_{2}\):

\(\begin{array}{r}\frac{\left(T_{2}+T_{B}\right) L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}+\frac{\left(T_{2}+T_{C}\right) L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}=0 \\T_{2}\left[\frac{L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}+\frac{L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}\right]=-\frac{T_{B} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}-\frac{T_{C} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}} \\T_{2}=-\frac{\frac{T_{B} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{T_{C} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}}{\frac{L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}+\frac{L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}} (h)\end{array}\)

Since \(G_{1}=G_{3} \text { and } J_{1}=J_{3} \text {, }\), Eq. (h) can be simplified further and \(T_{2}\) can be computed as:

\(\begin{aligned}T_{2} &=-\frac{1}{J_{1} G_{1}}\left[\frac{\left(T_{B} L_{1}+T_{C} L_{3}\right)}{\frac{L_{1}+L_{3}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}}\right] \\&=-\frac{1}{\left(1,353,830 mm ^{4}\right)\left(45,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}\left[\frac{(9,000,000 N – mm )(325 mm )+(9,000,000 N – mm )(325 mm )}{\frac{325 mm +325 mm }{\left(1,353,830 mm ^{4}\right)(45,000)}+\frac{400 mm }{\left(1,272,345 mm ^{4}\right)(28,000)}}\right] \\&=-4,385,216 N – mm\end{aligned}\)

Backsubstitute this result into Eqs. (f) and (g) to obtain \(T_{1} \text { and } T_{3} \text { : }\)

\(\begin{aligned}&T_{1}=-4,385,216 N – mm +9,000,000 N – mm =4,614,784 N – mm \\&T_{3}=-4,385,216 N – mm +9,000,000 N – mm =4,614,784 N – mm\end{aligned}\)

Maximum Shear Stress: The maximum shear stress magnitudes in the three segments are:

\(\begin{aligned}&\tau_{1}=\frac{T_{1} c_{1}}{J_{1}}=\frac{(4,614,784 N – mm )(75 mm / 2)}{1,353,830 mm ^{4}}=127.8 MPa \\&\tau_{2}=\frac{T_{2} c_{2}}{J_{2}}=\frac{(4,385,216 N – mm )(60 mm / 2)}{1,272,345 mm ^{4}}=103.4 MPa \\&\tau_{3}=\frac{T_{3} c_{3}}{J_{3}}=\frac{(4,614,784 N – mm )(75 mm / 2)}{1,353,830 mm ^{4}}=127.8 MPa\end{aligned}\)

(a) Maximum Shear Stress Magnitude in Bronze Segments:

\(\tau_{1}=127.8 MPa\)

(b) Maximum Shear Stress Magnitude in Aluminum Segment:

\(\tau_{2}=103.4 MPa\)

(c) Rotation Angle of Joint C Relative to Support A: The angle of twist in member (1) can be defined by the difference in rotation angles at the two ends:

\(\phi_{1}=\phi_{B}-\phi_{A} \quad \therefore \phi_{B}=\phi_{A}+\phi_{1}\) (i)

Similarly, the angle of twist in member (2) can be defined by:

\(\phi_{2}=\phi_{C}-\phi_{B} \quad \therefore \phi_{C}=\phi_{B}+\phi_{2}\) (j)

To derive an expression for \(\phi_{C}\), substitute Eq. (i) into Eq. (j), and note that joint A is restrained from rotating; therefore, \(\phi_{A}=0\).

\(\phi_{C}=\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}\)

The angle of twist in member (1) is:

\(\phi_{1}=\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}=\frac{(4,614,784 N – mm )(325 mm )}{\left(1,353,830 mm ^{4}\right)\left(45,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=0.024618 rad\)

The angle of twist in member (2) is:

\(\phi_{2}=\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}=\frac{(-4,385,216 N – mm )(400 mm )}{\left(1,272,345 mm ^{4}\right)\left(28,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=-0.049237 rad\)

The rotation angle of flange C is thus:

\(\phi_{C}=\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}=0.024618 rad +(-0.049237 rad )=-0.024618 rad =-0.0246 rad\)
