Guest Question.

A two-stage reciprocating compressor consists of two piston-cylinder arrangements of different size. The connecting rod from each piston is bolted to a common crankshaft onto which a flywheel is attached. An external electric motor turns the flywheel, which in turn causes the pistons to reciprocate and provide compressed air. Each cylinder has fins for cooling. The flywheel has ribs that are inclined at an angle so that when the flywheel rotates, the ribs act as fan blades that force air past the finned cylinder walls (see Figure 2.42).

Figure 2.43 is a schematic of the cross section of the larger cylinder. There are nine fins all taken to be rectangular in cross section. Each fin is 3 mm thick and 2.5 cm long. The outside diameter of the cylinder, fin tip to tip, is 21.9 cm. The distance between adjacent fins is 1.27 cm.

(In the real cylinder, the fins are cast with a slight taper, because this aids in the casting process. Also, the fins get shorter near the bottom of the cylinder; piston movement is nearer the top, and that is where most of the heat is generated.)

The cylinder is made of cast iron. During operation the root wall temperature reaches 300°C. Ambient air temperature is 25°C. With air moving past the fins at several meters per second, \(\bar{h_{c}}=20 W/(m^{2} ⋅ K)\). (a) Determine the amount of heat transferred from the cylinder to the air. (b) Repeat the calculations for a plain cylinder wall without fins. (c) Determine the fin effectiveness.

Admin
Solution

The fins are not identical. Piston movement is near the top of the cylinder so, near the bottom, where there is less heat to dissipate, the fins are not as long as those near the top. Moreover, in the real cylinder, extra material is cast as part of the cylinder wall for bolt holes, which are needed to attach the cylinder head.

 

Assumptions

 

1. One-dimensional steady conduction heat transfer exists within the fin.

 

2. Material properties are constant.

 

3. All fins are identical, having the same dimensions, efficiency, and surface area.

 

4. Heat is transferred away by convection; radiation is neglected.

 

5. The presence of the material into which bolt holes are drilled and threaded is neglected.

 

6. The fin root temperature is constant over the cylinder height.

 

a. The heat transferred by the cylinder will equal that transferred from nine fins \((q_{f})\) plus that transferred by the wall sections between adjacent fins \((q_{w})\); or

 

\(q=q_{f}+q_{w}\)

 

The fin heat-transfer rate is

 

\(q_{f}=N\eta _{e}q_{\max }\)

 

where N is the number of fins, \(\eta _{e}\) the fin efficiency to be found from Figure 2.41, and \(q_{max}=\bar{h_{c}}A_{s}(T_{w}-T_{\infty })\). It is assumed that all fins are identical, having the same dimensions, efficiency, and surface area. It is also assumed that the fin root temperature is constant. The surface area of each fin is \(A_{s}=2\pi (R^{2}_{2c}-R^{2}_{1})\). The parameters of the problem are

 

\(2\delta =3\, mm\)

 

or

 

\(\delta = 0.001 5\, m\)

 

\(L_{c}=L+\delta =0.025+0.0015=0.0265\)

 

\(R_{2c}=R_{2}+\delta =0.219/2+0.0015=0.111\, m\)

 

\(R_{1}=R_{2}-L=0.219/2-0.025=0.0845\, m\)

 

\(T_{w}=300^{\circ}C\)

 

\(T_{\infty }=25^{\circ}C\)

 

\(\bar{h_{c}}=25\, W/(m^{2}\cdot K)\)

 

From Appendix Table B.2 for cast iron, k = 52 W/(m · K). We now calculate the areas of importance.

 

Profile and surface area of one fin

 

\(A_{p}=2\delta L_{c}=2(0.0015)(0.0265)=7.95\times 10^{-5}m^{2}\)

 

\(A_{s}=2\pi (R^{2}_{2c}-R^{2}_{1})=3.26\times 10^{-2}m^{2}\)

 

To use Figure 2.38 for finding efficiency, we first determine

 

\(R_{2c}/R_{1}=1.3\)

 

and

 

\(L^{3/2}_{c}(\bar{h_{c}}/kA_{p})^{1/2}=0.34\)

 

With these parameters, Figure 2.38 shows

 

\(\eta _{e}=0.90\)

 

So the heat transferred by the nine fins is

 

\(q_{f}=N\eta _{e}\bar{h_{c}}A_{s}(T_{w}-T_{\infty })=9(0.90)(25)(3.26\times 10^{-2})(300-25)\)

 

\(q_{f}=1813\, W\)

 

The heat transferred by the exposed cylinder surface (between the fins) is

 

\(q_{w}=h_{c}a_{sw}(T_{w}-T_{\infty })\)

 

The exposed surface area we will take to consist of nine sections or rings of radius \(0.084 5 m (R_{1})\) and height 12.7 mm. We find

 

\(A_{sw}=2\pi R_{1}(S_{p})(9)=2\pi (0.0845)(0.0127)(9)\)

 

\(=6.07\times 10^{-2}m^{2}\)

 

where \(S_{p}\) is fin spacing. The heat transferred is

 

\(q_{w}=25(6.07\times 10^{-2})(300-25)\)

 

\(q_{w}=516\, W\)

 

Th e total heat transferred from the cylinder is \( q_{f} + q_{w}\) or

 

q = 2230 W

 

b. In the case of no fins, the surface area is the total cylinder height \(\times 2\pi R_{1}\) The height is

 

\(H=9(0.0127+0.003)=0.1413\, m\)

 

The surface area without fins is

 

\(A_{so}=2\pi (0.0845)(0.1413)=7.502\times 10^{-2}m^{2}\)

 

The heat transferred without fins is

 

\(q_{o}=\bar{h_{c}}A_{so}(T_{w}-T_{\infty })=25(7.502\times 10^{-2})(300-25)\)

 

\(q_{o}=516\, W\)

 

c. Fin effectiveness is defined in Equation 2.66

 

\(\eta _{f}=\frac{Heat \, flux\, with \,fins}{Heat \,flux \,without \,fins}\)

 

\(\eta _{f}=\frac{Heat \,flux \,from \,wall \,after \,adding \,fin}{Heat \,flux \,from \,wall \,before \,adding \,fin}\) (2.66)

 

\(\eta _{f}=\frac{q}{q_{o}}=\frac{2 230}{516}\)

 

\(\eta _{f}=4.32\)
