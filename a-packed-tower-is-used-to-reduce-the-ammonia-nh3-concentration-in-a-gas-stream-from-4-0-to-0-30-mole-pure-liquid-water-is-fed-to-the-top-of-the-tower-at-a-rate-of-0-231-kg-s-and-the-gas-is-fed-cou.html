Guest Question.

A packed tower is used to reduce the ammonia \( \left( NH _{3}\right) \) concentration in a gas stream from 4.0 to 0.30 mole%. Pure liquid water is fed to the top of the tower at a rate of 0.231 kg/s, and the gas is fed countercurrently to the bottom of the tower at a volumetric rate of \( 0.20 m ^{3} / s \). The tower is packed with 1.0-inch Raschig rings, and is operated at 293 K and 1.0 atm. The bulk properties of the liquid can be taken as the properties of liquid water. At 298 K, for liquid water, the density is \( 998.2 kg / m ^{3} \), and the viscosity is \( 993 \times 10^{-6} kg / m \cdot s \). Calculate the diameter of the packed tower if the gas pressure drop is limited to \( 200 N / m ^{2} \) per meter of packing.

Admin
Solution

The Flooding correlation will be used to estimate the tower diameter. To use the Flooding correlation, the mass flow rates of
the gas and liquid in countercurrent flow must be determined. For gas absorption, the highest mass flow rates of both gas and liquid are located at the bottom of the tower. Therefore, \( A G_{1}^{\prime} \) and \( A L_{1}^{\prime} \) must be determined. The inlet molar flow rate of gas is determined from the volumetric flow rate using the ideal gas law:

 

\( A G_{1} = \dot{V}_{1} \frac{P}{R T} = \left(0.20 \frac{ m ^{3}}{ s }\right) \frac{(1.0 atm )}{\left(0.08206 \frac{ m ^{3} \cdot atm }{ kgmole \cdot K }\right)(293 K )} \)

 

\( = 8.32 \times 10^{-3} kgmole / s \)

 

The average molecular weight of the inlet gas is determined by

 

\( M_{w, G_{1}} = y_{A_{1}} M_{A} + \left(1 – y_{A_{1}}\right) M_{B} = (0.040)(17)\)

 

\( + (1 – 0.040)(29) = 28.5 kg / kgmole \)

 

where component B refers to the carrier gas (air). The mass flow rate of entering gas is

 

\( A G_{1}^{\prime} = A G_{1} \cdot M_{W, G_{1}} = \)

 

\( \left(8.32 \times 10^{-3} kgmole / s \right)(28.5 kg / kgmole )= 0.237 kg / s \)

 

The inlet molar flow rate of the pure water solvent \( \left(x_{A_{2}} = 0\right) \) is

 

\( A L_{2} = A L_{2}^{\prime} / M_{w, L_{2}} \)

 

\( = (0.231 kg / s ) /(18 kg / kgmole ) = 1.28 \times 10^{-3} kgmole / s \)

 

The outlet gas molar flow rate is calculated by

 

\( A G_{2} = \frac{A G_{S}}{1-y_{A_{2}}} = \frac{A G_{1}\left(1 – y_{A_{1}}\right)}{1 – y_{A_{2}}} = \frac{\left(8.32 \times 10^{-3}\right)(1 – 0.040)}{(1-0.003)} \)

 

\( = 8.01 \times 10^{-3} kgmole / s \)

 

From an overall balance on the terminal streams, the outlet liquid molar flow rate is

 

\( A L_{1} = A L_{2} + \left(A G_{1} – A G_{2}\right) = 1.28 \times 10^{-3} + \)

 

\( \left(8.32 \times 10^{-3} – 8.01 \times 10^{-3}\right) = 1.31 \times 10^{-2} kgmole / s \)

 

A balance of solute A around the terminal streams is given by

 

\( y_{A_{1}} A G_{1} + x_{A_{2}} A L_{2} = y_{A_{2}} A G_{2} + x_{A_{1}} A L_{1} \)

 

Or, since \( x_{A_{2}} = 0 \),

 

\( x_{A_{1}} = \frac{y_{A_{1}} A G_{1} – y_{A_{2}} A G_{2}}{A L_{1}}\)

 

\( = \frac{(0.040)\left(8.32 \times 10^{-3}\right) – (0.0030)\left(8.01 \times 10^{-3}\right)}{1.31 \times 10^{-2}} = 0.024 \)

 

The average molecular weight of the outlet liquid stream is

 

\( M_{w, L_{1}} = x_{A_{1}} M_{A}+\left(1 – x_{A_{1}}\right) M_{B} = \)

 

\( (0.024)(17) + (1 – 0.024)(18) = 18 kg / kgmole \)

 

where component B refers to the solvent (water). Finally, the outlet mass flow rate of liquid is

 

\( A L_{1}^{\prime} = A L_{1} \cdot M_{w, L_{1}} = \left(1.31 \times 10^{-2 } kgmole / s \right)(18 kg / kgmole ) \)

 

\( = 0.237 kg / s \)

 

With the mass flow rates known, the x-axis on the Flooding correlation will now be determined. First, the ratio of the liquid to gas mass flow rate at the bottom of the tower is

 

\( \frac{L^{\prime}}{G} = \frac{A L^{\prime}}{A G^{\prime}} = \frac{0.237 kg / s }{0.237 kg / s } = 1.00 \)

 

Note that this ratio can be evaluated without knowing the diameter or cross-sectional area of the empty tower. Next, the density of the gas stream entering the tower is

 

\( \rho_{G} = \frac{P}{R T} M_{W, G_{1}} = \frac{(1.0 atm )}{\left(0.08206 \frac{ m ^{3} \cdot atm }{ kgmole \cdot K }\right)(293 K )}(28.5 kg / kgmole ) \)

 

\( = 1.19 kg / m ^{3} \)

 

Therefore, the x-axis on the Flooding correlation (Figure) is

 

\( \frac{L^{\prime}}{G}\left(\frac{\rho_{G}}{\rho_{L} – \rho_{G}}\right)^{1 / 2} = 1.00\left(\frac{1.19}{998.2 – 1.19}\right)^{1 / 2} = 0.034 \)

 

which is a dimensionless quantity. At a pressure drop of \( 200 N / m ^{2} \) per meter of packing depth, the y-axis value of the Flooding correlation is 0.049 if the x-axis value is 0.034.

Consequently,

\( 0.049 = \frac{\left(G^{\prime}\right)^{2} c_{f}\left(\mu_{L}\right)^{0.1} J}{\rho_{G}\left(\rho_{L} – \rho_{G}\right) g_{c}} \)

 

This expression for the y-axis is rearranged to determine the required superficial molar velocity of gas, \( G^{\prime} \):

 

\( G^{\prime} = \sqrt{\frac{0.049 \rho_{G}\left(\rho_{L} – \rho_{G}\right) g_{c}}{c_{f}\left(\mu_{L}\right)^{0.1} J}} \)

 

From Table, \( c_{f} = 155 \) for 1.0 inch Raschig rings. Therefore, the required superficial mass velocity of the gas is

 

\( G^{\prime} = \sqrt{\frac{0.049\left(1.19 \frac{ kg }{ m ^{3}}\right)\left(998.2 – 1.19 \frac{ kg }{ m ^{3}}\right)(1.00)}{(155)\left(993 \times 10^{-6} kg / m \cdot s \right)^{0.1}(1.0)}} = 0.865 kg / m ^{2} s \)

 

The cross-sectional area of the tower is backed out from \( G^{\prime} \) by

 

\( A = \frac{A G^{\prime}}{G^{\prime}} = \frac{0.237 kg / s }{0.865 kg / m ^{2} \cdot s } = 0.274 m ^{2} \)

 

and the so the tower diameter is

 

\( D = \sqrt{\frac{4 A}{\pi}} = \sqrt{\frac{4\left(0.274 m ^{2}\right)}{\pi}} = 0.59 m \)
