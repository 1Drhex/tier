Production of Ammonia at 300 Bar

Consider the production of ammonia from the catalytic reaction of a stoichiometric feed of nitrogen and hydrogen as in . Consider again the reaction temperature of 500째C. Now the reactor pressure is increased to 300 bar (see for discussion). What is the maximum possible conversion?

(a) Take the gas to be ideal.

(b) Use the van der Waals equation and the Lewis fugacity rule to account for gas-phase nonideality.

Admin
Solution

(a) Considering the gas to be ideal, Equation (E9.7) is still valid. At a pressure of 300 bar:

 

\(K=\frac{\left(\frac{2 \xi}{4-2 \xi}\right)^{2}}{\left(\frac{1-\xi}{4-2 \xi}\right)\left(\frac{3-3 \xi}{4-2 \xi}\right)^{3}} P^{-2}=\frac{(2 \xi)^{2}(4-2 \xi)^{2}}{(1-\xi)(3-3 \xi)^{3}} P^{-2}\) (E9.7)

 

\(K_{T}=\frac{\left(\frac{2 \xi}{4-2 \xi}\right)^{2}}{\left(\frac{1-\xi}{4-2 \xi}\right)\left(\frac{3-3 \xi}{4-2 \xi}\right)^{3}} P^{-2}=\frac{(2 \xi)^{2}(4-2 \xi)^{2}}{(1-\xi)(3-3 \xi)^{3}} P^{-2}\)

 

where, \(K_{T}=1.51 \times 10^{-5} \quad \text { and } \quad P=300 \text { bar }\)

 

Solving for the extent of reaction gives:

 

\(\xi=0.37\)

 

The conversion has increased dramatically at the higher pressure.

(b) At 300 bar, the gas is not ideal. To account for nonideal behavior, we can use Equation (9.26). Recall that this equation uses the Lewis fugacity rule to approximate the fugacity coefficient:

 

\(K=\prod\left(y_{i}\right)^{v_{i}} \prod\left(\varphi_{i}\right)^{v_{i}} P^{v}\) (9.26)

 

\(K=\prod\left(y_{i}\right)^{v_{i}} \prod\left(\varphi_{i}\right)^{v_{i}} P^{v}=\frac{y_{ NH _{3}}^{2} \varphi_{ NH _{3}}^{2}}{y_{ N _{2}} \varphi_{ N _{2}} y_{ H _{2}}^{3} \varphi_{ H _{2}}^{3}} P^{-2}\)

 

Replacing the mole fraction with expressions for \(\xi\) determined in , we obtain:

 

\(K=\frac{(2 \xi)^{2} \varphi_{ NH _{3}}^{2}(4-2 \xi)^{2}}{(1-\xi) \varphi_{ N _{2}}(3-3 \xi)^{3} \varphi_{ H _{2}}^{3}} P^{-2}\) (E9.9)

 

A summary of the solution is shown in Table E9.9. The solution algorithm is as follows:

1. Look up \(P_{c}, T_{c}\) (from Appendix A.1)

2. Calculate van der Waals constants, a and b, from corresponding states:

 

\(a=\frac{27}{64} \frac{\left(R T_{c}\right)^{2}}{P_{c}} \quad b=\frac{\left(R T_{c}\right)}{8 P_{c}}\)

 

3. Calculate \(v_{i}\) from van der Waals equation of state:

 

\(P=\frac{R T}{v_{i}-b}-\frac{a}{v_{1}^{2}}\)

 

In practice, we would use a more modern and accurate equation of state; however, the van der Waals equation is used in this example to be consistent with the development

4. Calculate \(\varphi_{i}\) from he result obtained in :

 

\(\ln \left[\frac{f_{i}^{v}}{P}\right]=\ln \left[\varphi_{i}^{v}\right]=-\ln \left[\frac{\left(v_{i}-b\right) P}{R T}\right]+\frac{b}{\left(v_{i}-b\right)}-\frac{2 a}{R T v_{i}}\)

 

Now, plugging values into Equation (E9.9) and solving gives:

 

\(\xi=0.33\)

 

A correction of about 10% results from accounting for nonideal behavior; however, the conversion is still significant in comparison to .
