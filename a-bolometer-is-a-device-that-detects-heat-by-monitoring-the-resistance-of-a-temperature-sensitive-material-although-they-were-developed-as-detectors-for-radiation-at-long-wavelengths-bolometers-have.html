Guest Question.

CONTEXT A bolometer is a device that detects heat by monitoring the resistance of a temperature-sensitive material. Although they were developed as detectors for radiation at long wavelengths, bolometers have also been used in the laboratory to intercept molecules. When a molecule under study strikes the bolometer element, it transfers heat to the detector. When a vibrationally excited molecule strikes the element, it transfers even more heat and increases the detector signal.his efect has been used as an energy detector for molecules carried in beams of helium or other noble gases. In this problem, we estimate roughly what the momentum would be for a helium atom traveling in a beam when it strikes a bolometer element, using the root mean square (rms) momentum along the X axis as a representative value.

PROBLEM Calculate the root mean square momentum along the X axis \(\left\langle P^{2}_{X} \right\rangle ^{1/2 }\) in kg m \(s^{-1}\) of a helium atom in the gas-phase when the temperature is 312 K. (We donâ€™t want to calculate \(\left\langle P_{X} \right\rangle\) because that should average to zero, motion in the +X and +X directions being equally likely.)

Solution

Use the classical average value theorem. We are looking for the average of \(P^{2}_{X} = (mv_{X} )^{2}\) , a function that depends on \(v_{X} \). he translational energy along the X axis \(mv^{2}_{X}/2\) is the contribution to the energy that depends on that coordinate, the energy we put into the exponent is \(mv^{2}_{X}/2\) :

\(\left\langle P^{2}_{X} \right\rangle = \frac{\int_{0}^{\infty }{(mv_{X} )^{2}e^{-mv^{2}_{X}/(2K_{B}T )}dv_{X} } }{\int_{0}^{\infty }{e^{-mv^{2}_{X}/(2K_{B}T )}dv_{X} }} =\frac{\frac{m^{2}\sqrt{\pi } }{4(m/(2K_{B}T )^{3/2} )} }{\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{\frac{\pi }{(m/(2K_{B}T )} } }\)

 

\(= mK_{B}T\)

The equipartition principle also predicts this result. Using \(K_{X}\) for the kinetic energy for motion along the X axis,

\(P^{2}_{X}= 2mK_{X} = 2m(K_{B}T/2 )= mK_{B}T\).

Substituting in the mass of one helium atom \(m= 6.67\cdot 10^{-27} kg \) and

\(K_{B}T = (1.381\cdot 10^{-23}JK^{-1} )(312 K)= 4.31\cdot 10^{-21} J\), we get

\(\left\langle P^{2}_{X} \right\rangle ^{1/2 }= \sqrt{(6.67\cdot 10^{-27} kg )(4.31\cdot 10^{-21} J )}= 5.36\cdot 10^{-24} kg m s^{-1}\)
