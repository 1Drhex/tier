Guest Question.

Japanese researchers very carefully followed the oxidation of sodium sulfide \(\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{~S}(\mathrm{~A})\) to sodium thiosulfate \(\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{~S}_{2} \mathrm{O}_{3}(\mathrm{R})\) in a batch reactor. Intermediates were measured and the results found were sketched in Fig. E8.3.

(a) Think up a simple network of reactions, all of first-order, to represent this oxidation.

(b) Evaluate the rate constants of this network.

This problem draws on a number of techniques.

(a) First, Look for Clues in the Graph. We first note that the initial slopes of the \(\mathrm{R}, \mathrm{S},\) and \(\mathrm{T}\) curves are all nonzero, suggesting that these compounds are formed directly from \(\mathrm{A}or ,\)

(b) Evaluate the Rate Constants. Noting that A disappears by first-order kinetics,we have

\(\ln \frac{C_{\mathrm{A} 0}}{C_{\mathrm{A}}}=\ln \frac{185}{100}=\left(k_{1}+k_{2}+k_{3}\right) t=k_{123} t=k_{123}(30)\)

from which

\(k_{123}=\underline{0.0205 \mathrm{~min}^{-1}}\)

From the initial rate for formation of \(\mathrm{R}\)

\(\frac{d C_{\mathrm{R}}}{d t}=k_{2} C_{\mathrm{A} 0}\)

or

\(k_{2}=\frac{d C_{\mathrm{R}} / d t}{C_{\mathrm{A} 0}}=\frac{2.0}{185}=\underline{\underline{0.0108 \mathrm{~min}^{-1}}}\)

Similarly for \(\mathrm{S}\)

\(k_{1}=\frac{d C_{\mathrm{S}} / d t}{C_{\mathrm{A} 0}}=\frac{1.3}{185}=\underline{\underline{0.0070 \mathrm{~min}^{-1}}}\)

So by difference

\(k_{3}=k_{123}-k_{1}-k_{2}=0.0205-0.0108-0.0070=0.0027 \mathrm{~min}^{-1}\)

Next, let us look at the maxima of the \(\mathrm{S}\) and T curves. For \(\mathrm{S},\) by an extension of Eq. \(49,\) we can write

\(\frac{C_{\mathrm{Smax}}}{C_{\mathrm{A} 0}}=\frac{k_{1}}{k_{123}}\left(\frac{k_{123}}{k_{4}}\right)^{k_{4} /\left(k_{4}-k_{123}\right)}\)

or

\(\frac{32}{185}=\frac{0.0070}{0.0205}\left(\frac{0.0205}{k_{4}}\right)^{k_{4} /\left(k_{4}-0.0205\right)}\)

Solving for \(k_{4}\) by trial and error gives

\(\underline{k_{4}}=0.0099 \mathrm{~min}^{-1}\)

Similarly for \(\mathrm{T}\)

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{10}{185} &=\frac{0.0027}{0.0205}\left(\frac{0.0205}{k_{5}}\right)^{k_{5} /\left(k_{5}-0.0205\right)} \\\underline{k_{5}} &=0.0163 \mathrm{~min}^{-1}\end{aligned}\)
