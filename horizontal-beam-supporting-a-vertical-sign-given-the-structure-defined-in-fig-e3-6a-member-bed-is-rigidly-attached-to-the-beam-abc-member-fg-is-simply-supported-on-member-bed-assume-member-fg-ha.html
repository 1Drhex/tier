Guest Question.

Horizontal Beam Supporting a Vertical Sign

Given: The structure defined in Fig. E3.6a. Member BED is rigidly attached to the beam, ABC. Member FG is simply supported on member BED. Assume member FG has some self-weight, W and is acted upon by a uniform horizontal wind load p. This structure is an idealization of a highway sign supported on a beam.

Determine: The reactions.

Admin
Solution

We work with two free body diagrams, one for member FG and the other for the remaining part of the structure.

 

Consider first member FG. Enforcing equilibrium leads to:

 

\(V_{F} = W\)

 

\(H_{F}= H_{G} = \frac{Ph}{4}\)

 

Next, we apply these forces to the structure composed of member ABC and member BED. The free body diagram is shown below.

 

Summing moments about A leads to \(R_{C}\)

 

\(\sum M_{A} = 0\)

 

\(W\frac{L}{2} = \frac{Ph}{4}(2h)+ \frac{ph}{4}(2.5h)+R_{C}L\)

 

\(\therefore R_{C} = \frac{W}{2} – ph\left ( 1.125\frac{h}{L} \right )\)

 

The horizontal and vertical reactions at A are

 

\(R_{Ax} = \frac{ph}{2}\)

 

\(R_{Ay}= \frac{W}{2} + ph \left ( 1.125\frac{h}{L} \right )\)

 

Note that the vertical reaction at C may become negative if ph is large with respect to W and h is of the order of L.
