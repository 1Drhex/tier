Guest Question.

A SOURCE IN A COMPLETE CIRCUIT

We add a 4-Ω resistor to the battery in Conceptual , forming a complete circuit (Fig. 25.17). What are the voltmeter and ammeter readings \(V_{ab}\) and I now?

Admin
Solution

IDENTIFY and SET UP:

Our target variables are the current I through the circuit \(a a^{\prime} b^{\prime} b\) and the potential difference \(V_{ab}\). We first find I from Eq. (25.16). To find \(V_{ab}\), we can use either Eq. (25.11) or Eq. (25.15).

\(V=I R\) (25.11)

\(V_{a b}=\mathcal{E}-\operatorname{Ir}\) (25.15)

\(\mathcal{E}-I r=I R \quad \text { or } \quad \mathrm{I}=\frac{\mathcal{E}}{R+r}\) (25.16)

EXECUTE:

The ideal ammeter has zero resistance, so the total resistance external to the source is R = 4 Ω. From Eq. (25.16), the current through the circuit \(a a^{\prime} b^{\prime} b\) is then

\(I=\frac{\mathcal{E}}{R+r}=\frac{12 \mathrm{~V}}{4 \Omega+2 \Omega}=2 \mathrm{~A}\)

Our idealized conducting wires and the idealized ammeter have zero resistance, so there is no potential difference between points \(a \text { and } a^{\prime}\) or between points \(b \text { and } b^{\prime}\); that is, \(V_{a b}=V_{a^{\prime} b^{\prime}}\).
We find \(V_{ab}\) by considering a and b as the terminals of the resistor: From Ohm’s law, Eq. (25.11), we then have

\(V_{a^{\prime} b^{\prime}}=I R=(2 \mathrm{~A})(4 \Omega)=8 \mathrm{~V}\)

Alternatively, we can consider \(a\) and b as the terminals of the source. Then, from Eq. (25.15),

\(V_{a b}=\mathcal{E}-I r=12 \mathrm{~V}-(2 \mathrm{~A})(2 \Omega)=8 \mathrm{~V}\)

Either way, we see that the voltmeter reading is 8 V.

 

EVALUATE: With this current flowing through the source, the terminal voltage \(V_{ab}\) is less than the emf \(\mathcal{E}\). The smaller the internal resistance r, the less the difference between \(V_{ab}\) and \(\mathcal{E}\).
