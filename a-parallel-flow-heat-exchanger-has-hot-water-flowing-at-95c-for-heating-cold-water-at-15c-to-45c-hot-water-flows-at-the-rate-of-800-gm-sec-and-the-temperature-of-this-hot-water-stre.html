Guest Question.

A parallel flow heat exchanger has hot water flowing at 95°C for heating cold water at 15°C to 45°C. Hot water flows at the rate of 800 gm/sec and the temperature of this hot water stream should not be less than 50°C at exit. Estimate the second law efficiency and rate of exergy destruction considering dead state temperature of 25°C.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{Let hot stream and cold stream be shown to enter at section 1–1 and leave at 2 – 2}\\\text{Given;}\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad T_{H_{1}} = 95°C, T_{H_{2}} = 50°C, m_{H} = 800 gm/s\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad T_{C_{1}} = 15°C, T_{C_{2}} = 45°C m_{H} = 0.8 kg/s\\\text{For parallel flow heat exchanger as shown in figure;}\\m_{H} × C_{p,H} . (T_{H_{1}} – T_{H_{2}}) = m_{c} × C_{p,c} . (T_{C_{2}} – T_{C_{1}})\\⇒ \qquad \qquad 0.8 × (95 – 50) = m_{c} (45 – 15)\\⇒\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad m_{c} = 1.2 kg/s\\ \qquad \text{Second law efficiency} = \frac{\text{(Rate exergy increase in cold stream)}}{\text{(Rate of exergy input to heat exchanger through hot stream)}}\\\text{The exergy entering (input) through hot water stream,}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad A_{H_{1}} = m_{H}\left\{ (h_{H_{1}} – h_{0}) – T_{0} (s_{H_{1}} – s_{0} )\right\} \\\text{Using steam tables}\\\begin{aligned}\text{At 25°C} \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad h_{0}&= 104.89 kJ/kg, s_{0} = 0.3674 kJ/kg.K \text{ saturated liquid yields.}\\\text{At 95°C}\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad h_{H_{1}}&= h_{\text{f at 95°C}} = 397.96 kJ/kg\\\text{(for hot stream)} \qquad \qquad \qquad &\\s_{H_{1}}&= s_{\text{f at 95°C}} = 1.2500 kJ/kg.K\\\text{At 50°C}\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad h_{H_{2}}&= h_{\text{f at 50°C}} = 209.33 kJ/kg.K\\\text{(for hot stream)} \qquad \qquad \qquad &\\s_{H_{2}}&= s_{\text{f at 50°C}} = 0.7038 kJ/kg.K\\\text{At 45°C}\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad h_{c_{2}}&= h_{\text{f at 45°C}} = 188.45 kJ/kg.K\\\text{(for cold stream)}\qquad\qquad\qquad &\\s_{c_{2}}&= s_{\text{f at 45°C}} = 0.6387 kJ/kg.K\\\text{At 15°C}\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad h_{c_{1}}&= h_{\text{f at 15°C}} = 62.99 kJ/kg.K\\\text{(for cold stream)}\qquad\qquad\qquad &\\s_{c_{1}}&= s_{\text{f at 15°C }}= 0.2245 kJ/kg.K\\\text{Rate of exergy input through }& \text{hot water stream,} \\A_{H_{1}}&= 0.8 × \left\{(397.96 – 104.89) – 298(1.25 – 0.3674)\right\} \\A_{H_{1}}&= 24.04 kJ/s\\\text{Rate of exergy increase in col}&\text{d stream;}\\ΔA_{c}&= m_{c}\left\{ (h_{c_{2}} – h_{c_{1}}) – T_{0} (s_{c_{2}} – s_{c_{1}})\right\} \\ΔA_{c}&= 1.2 \left\{(188.45 – 62.99) – 298(0.6387 – 0.2245)\right\} \\Δ_{A_{c}}&= 2.43 kJ/s\\\text{Second law efficiency}&= \frac{ΔA_{c}}{A_{H_{1}}} = \frac{2.43}{24.04} = 0.1011\\&\text{or } \textbf{10.11 }\pmb{\%}\\\text{Rate of exergy loss in hot str}&\text{eam,}\\ΔA_{H}&= m_{H} \left\{(h_{H_{1}} – h_{H_{2}}) – T_{0} (s_{H_{1}} – s_{H_{2}})\right\} \\ΔA_{H}&= 0.8 \left\{(397.96 – 209.33) – 298(1.25 – 0.7038)\right\} \\ΔA_{H}&= 20.69 kJ/s\\\text{Hence exergy destruction} &\\&= ΔA_{H} – ΔA_{C}\\&= 20.69 – 2.43\\&= \textbf{18.26 kJ/s}\end{aligned}\)
