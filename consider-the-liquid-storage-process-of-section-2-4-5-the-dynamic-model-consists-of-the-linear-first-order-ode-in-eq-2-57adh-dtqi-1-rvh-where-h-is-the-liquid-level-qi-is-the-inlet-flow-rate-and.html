Guest Question.

Consider the liquid storage process of Section 2.4.5. The dynamic model consists of the linear first-order ODE in Equation: \(A\frac{dh}{dt} =q_{i} -\frac{1}{R_{v}}h \) where h is the liquid level, \( q_{i} \) is the inlet flow rate, and A and \( R_{v} \) are constants.
(a) Derive a transfer function model between h and \( q_{i} \) (in deviation variables).
(b) Use the transfer function model to determine the response h(t) to a step change in \( q_{i} \) of magnitude M at t = 0.

Admin
Solution

(a) For this example, there are two deviation variables, \(h^{\prime } \ and \ q^{\prime}_{i}\):
\(h^{\prime }\triangleq h-\bar{h} \quad and \quad q^{\prime }_{i}\triangleq q_{i}-\bar{q}_{i}\) (1)
Deviation variables can be introduced by subtracting the steady-state version of a dynamic model from the model itself. The steady-state version of Equation
\(A\frac{dh}{dt} =q_{i} -\frac{1}{R_{v}}h \) (2)
is
\(0=\bar{q}_{i}-\frac{1}{R_{v}}\bar{h}\) (3)
Subtracting Equation (3) from Equation (2) gives
\(A\frac{dh}{dt}=(q_{i}-\bar{q}_{i})-\frac{1}{R_{v}}(h-\bar{h}) \) (4)
Because \( \bar{h} \) is a constant, it follows from Equation (1) that
\(\frac{dh^{\prime }}{dt}=\frac{d(h-\bar{h})}{dt}=\frac{dh}{dt} \) (5)
Substituting Equation (5) into the left side of Equation (4), and then substituting Equation (1) gives the dynamic model in deviation variable form:
\( A\frac{dh^{\prime }}{dt}=q^{\prime }_{i}-\frac{1}{R_{v}}h^{\prime }\) (6)
Next, we assume that the liquid storage system is initially at the nominal steady state. Thus, \(h(0) =\bar{h}\), and from Equation (1) , it follows that \(h^{\prime}(0)=h(0)-\bar{h}=h(0)-h(0)=0\). Taking the Laplace transform of both sides of Equation (6) gives
\( A[sH^{\prime}(s)- \cancel{h^{\prime }(0)}]=Q^{\prime}_{i}(s)-\frac{1}{R_{v}} H^{\prime}(s) \) (7)
Rearranging gives the desired transfer function, G(s):
\(G(s)=\frac{H^{\prime}(s)}{Q^{\prime}_{i}(s)}=\frac{R_{v}}{AR_{v}s+1} \) (8)
(b) In order to derive the step response, first rearrange Equation (8):
\(H^{\prime}(s)=(\frac{R_{v}}{AR_{v}s+1} ){Q^{\prime}_{i}(s)}\) (9)
For a step change of magnitude M at t = 0,\(u_{i}(t)=M \ and \ Q_{i}(s)=M/s\). Substituting in Equation (9) gives
\(H^{\prime}(s)=(\frac{R_{v}}{AR_{v}s+1} )\frac{M}{s} \) (10)
From the table of Laplace transformss (Table 3.1), taking the inverse Laplace transform of Equation (10) gives the liquid level response:
\(h^{\prime}(t)=R_{v}M(1-e^{-t/AR_{v}})\) (11)
Finally, substituting Equation (1) and rearranging gives the desired result:
\(h(t)=\bar{h}+h^{\prime}(t)= \bar{h}+R_{v}M(1-e^{-t/AR_{v}})\) (12)
