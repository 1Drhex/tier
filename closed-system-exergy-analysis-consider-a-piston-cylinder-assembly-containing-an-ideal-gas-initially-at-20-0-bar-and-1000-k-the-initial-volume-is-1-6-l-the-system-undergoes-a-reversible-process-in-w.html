Closed System Exergy Analysis

Consider a piston-cylinder assembly containing an ideal gas, initially at 20.0 bar and 1000 K. The initial volume is 1.6 L. The system undergoes a reversible process in which it is expands to 1 bar. Take the environment to be at 1 bar and 20째C. Take \(c_{ p }=(7 / 2) R\). The pressure volume relationship during this process is given by:

\(P v^{1.5}=\text { const }\)

(a) Calculate the work obtained during this process.

(b) Calculate the useful work that could be obtained by subtracting the energy needed to push back the ambient pressure.

(c) Calculate the exergy and the ideal work.

(d) Calculate the lost work.

Admin
Solution

(a) A schematic of the process is shown in Figure E3.17, where we defi ne the initial state as state 1 and the fi nal state as state 2.

Because this is a reversible process, we can calculate work as follows:

 

\(w=-\int P_{E} d v=-\int P d v\)

 

For the limits on the integral, we need to know the molar volume of the initial and final state. For state 1, we apply the ideal gas law:

 

\(v_{1}=\frac{R T_{1}}{P_{1}}=\frac{8.314 \times 1000\left[\frac{ J }{ mol }\right]}{2 \times 10^{6}\left[\frac{ J }{ m ^{3}}\right]}=0.0042\left[\frac{ m ^{3}}{ mol }\right]\)

 

We can calculate \(v_{2}\)from the equation in the problem statement:

 

\(P v^{1.5}=\text { const }=P_{1} v_{1}^{1.5}=P_{2} v_{2}^{1.5}\)

 

Solving for \(v_{2}\) gives:

 

\(v_{2}=\left(\frac{P_{1} v_{1}^{1.5}}{P_{2}}\right)^{1 / 1.5}=\left(20 \times 0.0042^{1.5}\right)^{1 / 1.5}=0.031\left[\frac{ m ^{3}}{ kg }\right]\)

 

Now we can solve for work:

 

\(w=-\int_{0.0042}^{0.031} P d \hat{v}=-\int_{0.0042}^{0.031} \frac{P_{1} v_{1}^{1.5}}{v^{1.5}} d v=2 P_{1} v_{1}^{1.5}\left[\frac{1}{v_{2}^{0.5}}-\frac{1}{v_{1}^{0.5}}\right]_{0.0042}^{0.031}=-10.6\left[\frac{ kJ }{ mol }\right]\)

 

The sign of work is negative because we are transferring energy from the system to the surroundings to perform work.

 

(b) To calculate the useful work, we need to fi nd the molar volume, \(v_{0}\), at the dead state:

 

\(v_{0}=\frac{R T_{0}}{P_{0}}=\frac{8.314 \times 293\left[\frac{ J }{ mol }\right]}{1 \times 10^{5}\left[\frac{ J }{ m ^{3}}\right]}=0.024\left[\frac{ m ^{3}}{ mol }\right]\)

 

As in Equation (3.38), the work needed to push back the envirounment is given by:

 

\(\Delta u=q+w=-q_{H}+\left[w_{i d, P_{v}}-P_{o}\left(v_{0}-v_{1}\right)\right]\) (3.38)

 

\(P_{o}\left(\nu_{1}-\nu_{0}\right)=2.0\left[\frac{ kJ }{ mol }\right]\)

 

So the amount of useful work is:

 

\(w_{u}=w+P_{o}\left(v_{1}-v_{0}\right)=-10.6+2.0=-8.6\left[\frac{ kJ }{ mol }\right]\)

 

(c) To calculate exergy of state 1, we use Equation (3.39):

 

\(b_{1} \equiv\left(u_{1}-u_{0}\right)+P_{o}\left(v_{1}-v_{0}\right)-T_{0}\left(s_{1}-s_{0}\right)\) (3.39)

 

\(b_{1}=\left(u_{1}-u_{0}\right)+P_{o}\left(v_{1}-v_{0}\right)-T_{0}\left(s_{1}-s_{0}\right)\)

 

\(=c_{v}\left(T_{1}-T_{0}\right)+P_{o}\left(v_{1}-v_{0}\right)-T_{0}\left[c_{v} \ln \left(\frac{T_{1}}{T_{0}}\right)+R \ln \left(\frac{v_{1}}{v_{0}}\right)\right]\)

 

\(b_{1}=\frac{5}{2} R(1000-293)+1 \times 10^{5}(0.0042-0.024)-293 R\left[2.5 \ln \left(\frac{1000}{293}\right)+\ln \left(\frac{0.0042}{0.024}\right)\right]\)

 

\(=9.5\left[\frac{ kJ }{ mol }\right]\)

 

Since the ideal work is the negative value of exergy, we get:

 

\(w_{i d}=-9.5\left[\frac{ kJ }{ mol }\right]\)

 

The magnitude of the lost work is about 10% of the useful work; results like this allow us to prioritize our engineering design and improvement efforts. In addition, we see that the value for exergy, which indicates the maximum useful work, calculated in part (c), is actually lower than the work calculated in part (a). Can you explain why?
