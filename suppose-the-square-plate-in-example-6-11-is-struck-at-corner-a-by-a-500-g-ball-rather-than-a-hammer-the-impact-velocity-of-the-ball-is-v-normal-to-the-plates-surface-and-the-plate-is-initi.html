Guest Question.

Suppose the square plate in is struck at corner \(A\) by a 500 -g ball, rather than a hammer. The impact velocity of the ball is \(v\) normal to the plate’s surface, and the plate is initially at rest. The coefficient of restitution is \(\varepsilon=0.6\). In the impulsive force had an average value of \(5000 \mathrm{~N}\) over a 4-ms duration. Determine the initial speed \(v\) of the ball bearing that will generate an impact equal to the hammer blow. For this initial speed, determine the angular velocity of the plate and the velocity of the ball at the instant following the impact, and the average reaction at the support.

Admin
Solution

This example introduces techniques for solving collision problems featuring spatial motion. It also is intended to shed some light on the quantitative magnitude of collision effects. The free-body diagram of the plate is unchanged from , but we also need to consider the ball. Contact occurs along the \(x y\) plane, so the impact force \(\bar{F}\) acts parallel to the \(z\) axis.

Much of the solution resembles the one followed previously, except that the linear impulse–momentum equation for the ball and the equation for the coefficient of restitution need to be considered.

Let point \(C\) designate the corner where the ball strikes the plate. We designate the plate as body 1 and the ball as body \(2 .\) The initial state is characterized by \(\left(\bar{\omega}_{1}\right)_{0}=\) \(\overline{0},\left(\bar{v}_{B}\right)_{0}=v \bar{k}\), whereas the rebound is described by arbitrary an arbitrary \(\bar{\omega}_{2}\), so that

\(\begin{array}{c}\left(\bar{\omega}_{1}\right)_{f}=\omega_{x} \bar{i}+\omega_{y} \bar{j}+\omega_{z} \bar{k}, \\\\\left(\bar{v}_{G}\right)_{f}=\left(\bar{\omega}_{1}\right)_{f} \times \bar{r}_{G / A}=0.06 \omega_{z} \bar{i}+0.18 \omega_{z} \bar{j}+\left(-0.06 \omega_{x}-0.18 \omega_{y}\right) \bar{k}.\end{array} (1) \)

Because the plate executes a pure rotation about the pivot, whereas Eq. (6.4.52) refers to point \(G\), we use Eq. (6.4.51) to describe the coefficient of restitution’s role. Toward that end we form the velocity of the contact point on the plate:

\(\begin{array}{l} {\left[\left(\bar{v}_{B}\right)_{f} \cdot \bar{i}-\left(\bar{v}_{A}\right)_{f} \cdot \bar{i}\right]+\left(\bar{\omega}_{2}\right)_{f} \cdot\left(\bar{r}_{C / B} \times \bar{i}\right)-\left(\bar{\omega}_{1}\right)_{f} \cdot\left(\bar{r}_{C / A} \times \bar{i}\right)} \\\\ \quad=\varepsilon\left\{\left[\left(\bar{v}_{A}\right)_{0} \cdot \bar{i}-\left(\bar{v}_{B}\right)_{0} \cdot \bar{i}\right]+\left(\bar{\omega}_{1}\right)_{f} \cdot\left(\bar{r}_{C / A} \times \bar{i}\right)-\left(\bar{\omega}_{2}\right)_{f} \cdot\left(\bar{r}_{C / B} \times \bar{i}\right)\right\}. \end{array} (6.4.52)\)

 

\(\left[\left(\bar{v}_{C 2}\right)_{f} \cdot \bar{i}-\left(\bar{v}_{C 1}\right)_{f} \cdot \bar{i}\right]=\varepsilon\left[\left(\bar{v}_{C 1}\right)_{0} \cdot \bar{i}-\left(\bar{v}_{C 2}\right)_{0} \cdot i\right]. (6.4.51)\)

 

\(\left(\bar{v}_{C}\right)_{f}=\left(\bar{\omega}_{1}\right)_{f} \times \bar{r}_{C / A}=-0.12 \omega_{z} \bar{i}+0.36 \omega_{z} \bar{j}+\left(0.12 \omega_{x}-0.36 \omega_{y}\right) \bar{k}. (2) \)

The impulse–momentum equations for the plate are like those previously written in :

\(\left(\bar{H}_{A}\right)_{2} \cdot \bar{i}=\left(0.144 \omega_{x}+0.108 \omega_{y}\right)=2.4,\)

\(\left(\bar{H}_{A}\right)_{2} \cdot \bar{j}=\left(0.432 \omega_{y}+0.108 \omega_{x}\right)=-7.2,\) (3)

\(\left(\bar{H}_{A}\right)_{2} \cdot \bar{k}=0.576 \omega_{z}=0.\)

In view of \(\left(\bar{v}_{G}\right)_{f}\) in Eqs. (1), the linear impulse-momentum principle for the plate gives

\(\begin{array}{l}10\left[0.06 \omega_{z} \bar{i}+0.18 \omega_{z} \bar{j}+\left(-0.06 \omega_{x}-0.18 \omega_{y}\right) \bar{k}\right] \\\\\quad=\left[A_{x} \bar{i}+A_{y} \bar{j}+\left(A_{z}+5000\right) \bar{k}\right](0.004).\end{array} (4) \)

The linear impulse-momentum equation for the ball is

\(0.5\left(\bar{v}_{2}\right)_{f}=0.5(v \bar{k})-(5000 \bar{k}) 0.004. (5)\)

The last of the basic equations is the one featuring the coefficient of restitution. Here \(\bar{k}\) is the normal to the contact plane, so we have

\(\left(\bar{v}_{2}\right)_{f} \cdot \bar{k}-\left(\bar{v}_{C}\right)_{f} \cdot \bar{k}=\varepsilon[0-v]\)

Substitution of Eq. (2) gives

\(\left(\bar{v}_{2}\right)_{f} \cdot \bar{k}-\left(0.12 \omega_{x}-0.36 \omega_{y}\right)=-0.6 v. (6)\)

We find the final angular velocity components and normal velocity of the ball by simultaneously solving Eqs. (3), which yield

\(\omega_{x}=35.90, \quad \omega_{y}=-25.64~ \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s} \text { . }\)

These values are substituted into Eq. (6), which is solved simultaneously with the \(\bar{k}\) component of Eq. (5) to find

\(\left(\bar{v}_{2}\right)_{f} \cdot \bar{k}=-16.54, \quad v=23.46 \mathrm{~m} /\mathrm{s}.\)

According to Eq. (5), there is no change in the velocity components parallel to the plate because the contact force does not act in those directions, so the rebound velocity of the ball is

\(\left(\bar{v}_{2}\right)_{f}=-16.54 \bar{k} \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}.\)

Because \(F\) here is stated to match the impulsive force in , the average reaction forces, which are obtained from Eq. (4), are the same as those of the previous results:

\(A_{x}=A_{y}=0, \quad A_{z}=1153 \mathrm{~N}.\)

When one considers that the impact was generated by a \(0.5\text{-}kg\) ball moving at an initial speed of almost \(24 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\), these average reaction forces are not exceptionally large. However, these are average values that vary inversely to the time duration, corresponding to a fixed impulse.
