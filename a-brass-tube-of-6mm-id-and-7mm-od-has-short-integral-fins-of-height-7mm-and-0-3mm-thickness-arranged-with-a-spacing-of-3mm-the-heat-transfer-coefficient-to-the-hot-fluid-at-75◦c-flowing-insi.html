Guest Question.

A brass tube of 6mm ID and 7mm OD has short integral fins of height 7mm and 0.3mm thickness arranged with a spacing of 3mm. The heat transfer coefficient to the hot fluid at 75°C flowing inside the tube is \( 270W/m^{2}°C\) . The fins and the external surface of the tube are exposed to air at 20°C with a heat transfer coefficient of \( 45W/m^{2}°C\) . Determine the heat transfer from a meter length of tube.

Admin
Solution

Step 1 The nomenclature for this problem is based on Fig. 4.20. The given data is written down.

Tube inner radius: \(r_{i}= 0.003 m\)
Tube outer radius:\( r_{o} = 0.0035 m\)
Fin length: \( L = 0.007 m\)
Fin tip radius:\(r_{2} = r_{o} + L\)
\(= 0.0035 + 0.007 = 0.0105 m\)
Fin semi-thickness: \( t = 0.00015 m\)
Fin spacing: \( S = 0.003 m\)
Thermal conductivity of
tube and fin material:\( k = 111 W/m°C\)
Tube side heat transfer coefficient: \(h_{1} = 270 W/m^{2} °C\)
Fin side heat transfer coefficient: \(h_{2} = 45 W/m^{2} °C\)
Tube side fluid temperature: \(T_{1} = 75 °C\)
Fin side fluid temperature: \(T_{2} = 20 °C\)

 

Step 2 Non-dimensional fin parameter is calculated as

\(\mu =\sqrt{\frac{h_{2} }{kt} } \cdot r_{2} =\sqrt{\frac{45}{111\times 0.00015} }\cdot 0.0105=0.546\)

 

Radius ratio that characterizes the fin is

\(\alpha =\frac{r_{2}}{r_{0}} =\frac{0.0105}{0.0035}=3\)

 

Step 3 Fin efficiency is read off in Fig. 4.13 as η = 0.92. The various areas needed are calculated per meter length of tube basis. Since the fin spacing is S = 3mm or 0.003 m, the center to center distance between fins \(\acute{S}\) is

\(\acute{S} = S + 2t = 0.003 + 2 × 0.00015 = 0.0033 m\)

The number of fins per m length of tube is determined as

\(n=\frac{1}{\acute{S}} =\frac{1}{0.0033} \approx 303\)

 

The above is obtained by rounding the number to an integer. The parent area per m of tube length is obtained as

\(A_{p}= 2πr_{o} = 2 × π × 0.0035 = 0.02199m^{2}\)

 

Fin area per m of tube length is

\(A_{f} = 2π(r^{2}_{2} − r^{2}_{o} )n = 2 × π × (0.0105^{2} − 0.0035^{2}) × 303 = 0.1866m^{2}\)

 

The total exposed area on the outside is

\(A_{t} = A_{f} + 2πr_{o}(n − 1)S\)

 

\(= 0.1866 + 2 × π×0.0035 × (303 − 1) × 0.003 = 0.2065m^{2}\)

 

Step 4 The overall surface efficiency may now be calculated, using Eq. 4.70 as

\(\eta _{0} =\frac{Q_{t} }{Q_{max}}=\left[1-\frac{A_{f} }{A_{t}}(1-\eta ) \right]\) (4.70)

 

\(\eta _{0} =\left[1-\frac{0.1866 }{0.2065}(1-0.92 ) \right] =0.928\)

 

The three resistances that correspond to those shown in Fig. 4.20 are

\(R_{1} =\frac{1}{2\pi r_{i}h_{1}} =\frac{1}{2\times \pi \times 0.003\times 270} =0.1965m°C/W\)

 

\(R_{2} =\frac{1}{2\pi k}\ln \left(\frac{r_{0} }{r_{2}} \right) =\frac{1}{2 \pi \times 111}\ln \left(\frac{0.0035}{0.003} \right) =0.1965m2.2103\times 10^{-4}m°C/W\)

 

\(R_{3} =\frac{1}{h_{2}A_{t}\eta _{0} } =\frac{1}{45\times 0.2065\times 0.928}=0.1160m°C/W\)

 

The overall thermal resistance is then given by

\(R_{tot} = 0.1965 + 2.2103 × 10^{-4} + 0.1160 = 0.3127m°C/W\)

 

Step 5 Tube heat loss per m of tube length is then given by

\(Q=\frac{T_{1}-T_{2}}{R_{tot}} =\frac{75-20}{0.3127}=175.9W/m\)
