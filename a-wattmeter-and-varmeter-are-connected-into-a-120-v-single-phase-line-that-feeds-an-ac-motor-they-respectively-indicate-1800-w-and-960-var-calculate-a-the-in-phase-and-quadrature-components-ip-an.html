Guest Question.

A wattmeter and varmeter are connected into a 120 V single-phase line that feeds an ac motor. They respectively indicate 1800 W and 960 var.

Calculate

a. The in-phase and quadrature components \(I_{ p }\) and \(I_{ q }\)

b. The line current I
c. The apparent power supplied by the source
d. The phase angle between the line voltage and line current

Admin
Solution

Referring to Fig. 11, where the load is now a motor, we have

 

a. \(\begin{aligned}&I_{ p }=P / E=1800 / 120=15 A \\&I_{ q }=Q / E=960 / 120=8 A\end{aligned}\)

 

b. From the phasor diagram we have

 

\(\begin{aligned}I &=\sqrt{I_{ p }^{2}+I_{ q }^{2}}=\sqrt{15^{2}+8^{2}} \\&=17 A\end{aligned}\)

 

c. The apparent power is

 

\(S=E I=120 \times 17=2040 VA\)

 

d. The phase angle Î¸ between E and I is

 

\(\begin{aligned}\theta &=\arctan Q / P=\arctan 960 / 1800 \\&=28.1^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)
