Guest Question.

The only known sources of helium are the atmosphere (mole fraction approximately \(5 \times 10^{-6}\)) and natural gas. A large unit is being constructed to separate 100 \(m ^{3} / s\) of natural gas, assumed to be 0.001 He mole fraction and 0.999 \(CH _{4}\). The gas enters the unit at 150 kPa, 10°C. Pure helium exits at 100 kPa, 20°C, and pure methane exits at 150 kPa, 30°C. Any heat transfer is with the surroundings at 20°C. Is an electrical power input of 3000 kW sufficient to drive this unit?

Admin
Solution
\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ n }_{1}=& P _{1} \dot{ V }_{1} / RT _{1}=150 kPa \times 100 m ^{3} / s /(8.3145 \times 283.2) kJ / kmol =6.37 kmol / s \\& \Rightarrow \dot{ n }_{2}=0.001 ; \quad \dot{ n }_{1}=0.00637 ; \quad \dot{ n }_{3}=6.3636 kmol / s\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\overline{ C }_{ P He }=4.003 kg / kmol \times 5.193 kJ / kg – K =20.7876 kJ / kmol K, \\&\overline{ C }_{ P CH _{4}}=16.043 kg / kmol \times 2.254 kJ / kg – K =36.1609 kJ / kmol K\end{aligned}\)

 

Energy Eq.:

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ Q }_{ CV } &=\dot{ n }_{2} \overline{ h }_{2}+\dot{ n }_{3} \overline{ h }_{3}-\dot{ n }_{1} \overline{ h }_{1}+\dot{ W }_{ CV }=\dot{ n }_{2} \overline{ C }_{ P 0 He }\left( T _{2}- T _{1}\right)+\dot{ n }_{3} \overline{ C }_{ P 0 CH _{4}}\left( T _{3}- T _{1}\right)+\dot{ W }_{ CV } \\&=0.00637 \times 20.7876(20-10)+6.3636 \times 36.1609(30-10)+(-3000) \\&=+1600 kW\end{aligned}\)

 

Entropy Eq.:

\(\begin{aligned}&\begin{aligned}\dot{ S }_{ gen }=& \dot{ n} _{2} \overline{ s }_{2}+\dot{ n }_{3} \overline{ s }_{3}-\dot{ n }_{1} \overline{ s }_{1}-\dot{ Q }_{ CV } / T _{0} \\=& 0.00637\left[20.7876 \ln \frac{293.2}{283.2}-8.3145 \ln \frac{100}{0.001 \times 150}\right] \\& \quad+6.3636\left[36.1609 \ln \frac{303.2}{283.2}-8.3145 \ln \frac{140}{0.999 \times 150}\right]-1600 / 293.2\end{aligned}\\&=+ 1 3 . 5 ~ k W / K > 0 \quad \text { Since positive, this is possible }\end{aligned}\)
