Guest Question.

Let \(H(z)\) be the z-transform of a real-valued discretetime signal \(h(n)\). If \(p(z) = H(z) \,H (\frac{1}{z})\) has a zero at \(z = \frac{1}{z} + \frac{1}{2} \,j\) and \(p(z)\) has a total of four zeros, which one of the following plots represents all the zeros correctly?

Admin
Solution
\(P(z)=H(z)\cdot H(\frac{1}{z})\)

 

Zero at \(z = \frac{1}{z} + \frac{1}{2} \,j\)

 

if \(z\) is zero, \(\bar{z}\) will also be zero

 

\(\bar{z}=\overline{(\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{2}\, j)}=\frac{1}{2}\,-\,\frac{1}{2}\, j\)

 

also \(\frac{1}{z}\) & \(\frac{1}{z}\) will also be zero

 

\(\frac{1}{z}=\frac{1}{\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{2}\, j}\times \frac{\frac{1}{2}\,-\,\frac{1}{2}\, j}{\frac{1}{2}\,-\,\frac{1}{2}\, j}=1\,-\,j\)

 

\(\frac{1}{z}=1+j\)

 

Hence, the correct option is (D).
