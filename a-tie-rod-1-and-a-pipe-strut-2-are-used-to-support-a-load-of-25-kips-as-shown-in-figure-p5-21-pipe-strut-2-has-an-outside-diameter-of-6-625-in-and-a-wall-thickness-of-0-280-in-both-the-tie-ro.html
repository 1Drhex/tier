Guest Question.

A tie rod (1) and a pipe strut (2) are used to support a load of 25 kips as shown in Figure P5.21. Pipe strut (2) has an outside diameter of 6.625 in. and a wall thickness of 0.280 in. Both the tie rod and the pipe strut are made of structural steel with a modulus of elasticity of E = 29,000 ksi and a yield strength of \(\sigma_{Y}\) = 36 ksi. For the tie rod, the minimum factor of safety with respect to yield is 1.5 and the maximum allowable axial elongation is 0.30 in. Assume that a = 21 ft, b = 9 ft, and c = 27 ft.
(a) Determine the minimum diameter required to satisfy both constraints for tie rod (1).
(b) Draw a deformation diagram showing the final position of joint B.

Admin
Solution

The angles of inclination for members (1) and (2) are:

\(\begin{array}{ll}\tan \theta_{1}=\frac{21 ft }{27 ft } & \therefore \theta_{1}=37.875^{\circ} \\\tan \theta_{2}=\frac{9 ft }{27 ft } \quad & \therefore \theta_{2}=18.435^{\circ}\end{array}\)

The member lengths are:

\(\begin{aligned}&L_{1}=\sqrt{(21 ft )^{2}+(27 ft )^{2}}=34.205 ft =410.463 in. \\&L_{2}=\sqrt{(9 ft )^{2}+(27 ft )^{2}}=28.460 ft =341.526 in.\end{aligned}\)

 

(a) Minimum diameter for tie rod (1): Consider a FBD of joint B and write two equilibrium equations:

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma F_{x}=-F_{1} \cos 37.875^{\circ}-F_{2} \cos 18.435^{\circ}=0 \\&\Sigma F_{y}=F_{1} \sin 37.875^{\circ}-F_{2} \sin 18.435^{\circ}-25 \text { kips }=0\end{aligned}\)

Solve these two equations simultaneously to obtain \(F_{1}=28.5044 kips\) and \(F_{2}=-23.7171 \text { kips. }\)

The allowable normal stress for tie rod (1) is

\(\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\sigma_{Y}}{F S}=\frac{36 ksi }{1.5}=24 ksi\)

The minimum cross-sectional area required to satisfy the normal stress requirement is

\(A \geq \frac{F_{1}}{\sigma_{\text {allow }}}=\frac{28.5044 kips }{24 ksi }=1.1877 in .^{2}\)

The maximum allowable axial deformation for the tie rod is 0.30 in. The minimum cross-sectional area required to satisfy the deformation requirement is

\(A \geq \frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{\delta_{1} E_{1}}=\frac{(28.5044 kips )(410.463 in .)}{(0.30 in .)(29,000 ksi )}=1.3448 in .^{2}\)

To satisfy both the stress and deformation requirements, the tie rod must have a minimum crosssectional area of \(A=1.3448 in .^{2}\) . The corresponding rod diameter is

\(\frac{\pi}{4} d_{ rod }^{2} \geq 1.3448 in .^{2} \quad d_{ rod } \geq 1.309 in.\)

 

(b) The pipe has a cross-sectional area of \(A_{2}=5.5814 in .^{2}\)

\(\delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}=\frac{(-23.7171 kips )(341.526 in .)}{\left(5.5814 in .{ }^{2}\right)(29,000 ksi )}=-0.0500 in.\)

Rod (1) elongates 0.30 in. and pipe (2) contracts 0.0500 in. Therefore, the deformation diagram showing the final position of joint B is shown.
