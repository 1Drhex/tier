Guest Question.

Determine the resultant of the two forces S = 1.5 kip and T = 2.0 kip shown in Fig. 7.4.

Admin
Solution

Force S = 1.5 kip acts from A to B. The coordinates of B and A are B(0, 14, 0) ft and A(4, 6, 2) ft. Subtracting the coordinates of A from the coordinates of B, we have

\(d_{x} = x_{B} – x_{A}\) = 0 – 4 = -4 ft

 

\(d_{y} = y_{B} – y_{A}\) = 14 – 6 = 8 ft

 

\(d_{z} = z_{B} – z_{A}\) = 0 – 2 = -2 ft

and

\(d=\sqrt{d_{x}^{2}+d_{y}^{2}+d_{z}^{2}}=\sqrt{(-4)^{2} + (8)^{2} + (-2)^{2}}=\sqrt{84}ft\)

Therefore, the components of S are

\(S_{x}=\frac{Sd_{x}}{d}=\frac{1.5(-4)}{\sqrt{84}}=-0.655\ kip\)

 

\(S_{y}=\frac{Sd_{y}}{d}=\frac{1.5(8)}{\sqrt{84}}=1.309\ kip\)

 

\(S_{z}=\frac{Sd_{z}}{d}=\frac{1.5(-2)}{\sqrt{84}}=-0.327\ kip\)

Force T = 2.0 kip acts from A to C. The coordinates of C and A are C(10, 18, 6) ft and A(4, 6, 2) ft. Subtracting the coordinates of A from the coordinates of C, we have

\(d_{x} = x_{C} – x_{A}\) = 10 – 4 = 6 ft

 

\(d_{y} = y_{C} – y_{A}\) = 18 – 6 = 12 ft

 

\(d_{z} = z_{C} – z_{A}\) = 6 – 2 = 4 ft

and

\(d=\sqrt{d_{x}^{2}+d_{y}^{2}+d_{z}^{2}}=\sqrt{(6)^{2} + (12)^{2} + (4)^{2}}=14\ ft\)

Therefore, the components of T are

\(T_{x}=\frac{Td_{x}}{d}=\frac{2.0(6)}{14}=0.857\ kip\)

 

\(T_{y}=\frac{Td_{y}}{d}=\frac{2.0(12)}{14}=1.741\ kip\)

 

\(T_{z}=\frac{Td_{z}}{d}=\frac{2.0(4)}{14}=0.571\ kip\)

Summing forces in the x, y, and z directions,

\(R_{x} = ΣF_{x} = S_{x} + T_{x}\) = -0.655 + 0.857 = 0.202 kip

 

\(R_{y} = ΣF_{y} = S_{y} + T_{y}\) = 1.309 + 1.714 = 3.023 kip

 

\(R_{z} = ΣF_{z} = S_{z} + T_{z}\) = -0.327 + 0.571 = 0.244 kip

The magnitude and direction of the resultant are given by

\(d=\sqrt{R_{x}^{2}+R_{y}^{2}+R_{z}^{2}}\)

 

\(=\sqrt{(0.202)^{2} + (3.023)^{2} + (0.244)^{2}}=3.04\ kip\)

and

\(\cos \theta_{x}=\frac{R_{x}}{R}=\frac{0.202}{3.04}=0.0665\) \(θ_{x} = 86.2^{\circ}\)

 

\(\cos \theta_{y}=\frac{R_{y}}{R}=\frac{3.023}{3.04}=0.9944\) \(θ_{y} = 6.06^{\circ}\)

 

\(\cos \theta_{z}=\frac{R_{z}}{R}=\frac{0.244}{3.04}=0.0803\) \(θ_{z} = 85.4^{\circ}\)

Check:

\(\cos^{2}\theta_{x}+\cos^{2}\theta_{y}+\cos^{2}\theta_{z}=(0.0665)^{2}+(0.9944)^{2}+(0.0803)^{2}=1000\) OK
