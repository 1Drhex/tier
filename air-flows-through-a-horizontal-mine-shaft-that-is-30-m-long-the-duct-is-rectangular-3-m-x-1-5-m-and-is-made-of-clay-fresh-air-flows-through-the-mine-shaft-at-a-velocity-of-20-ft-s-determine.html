Guest Question.

Air flows through a horizontal mine shaft that is 30 m long. The duct is rectangular (3 m × 1.5 m) and is made of clay. Fresh air flows through the mine shaft at a velocity of 20 ft/s. Determine the pressure drop

Admin
Solution

Air is a compressible fluid, but compressibility effects are negligible at velocities less than several hundred feet per second. So at the given velocity, we can model this problem as if the air were incompressible.
From the various property tables, we get
air \(ρ = 1.17 kg/m^3\) \(µ = 18 \times 10^{-6} N·s/m^2\) [App. Table C.1]
clay \(ε =\frac{ 0.03 + 0.3}{2} cm = 0.165 cm = 0.001 65\) m (avg value) [Table 3.1]
For the rectangular duct,
\(A = hw = 3(1.5) = 4.5 m^2\)
\(D_h = \frac{2h w}{h + w} = \frac{2(3)(1.5)}{3 + 1.5}\) = 2 m
The flow velocity is given as
V = 20 ft/s = 6.1 m/s
The continuity equation applied over the length of duct is
\(Q = A_1V_1 = A_2V_2\)
With \(A_1 = A_2\), we conclude that \(V_1 = V_2\). The Bernoulli equation applied to this system is
\(\frac{p_1g_c}{ρg} +\frac{V^2_1}{2g} + z_1 = \frac{p_2g_c}{ρg} +\frac{V^2_2}{2g} + z_2 + \frac{f L}{D_h}\frac{V^2}{2g}\)
With \(V_1 = V_2\) and \(z_1 = z_2\), the preceding equation simplifies to
\(\frac{(p_1 – p_2)g_c}{ρg} = \frac{f L}{D_h}\frac{V^2}{2g}\) (i)

All parameters are known except the friction factor f, which we now determine. The Reynolds number is

\(Re = \frac{ρVD_h}{µg_c}=\frac{ 1.17(6.1)(2)}{18 \times 10^{-6}}\)

or

\(\left. \begin{matrix}Re= 7.93\times 10^5\\ \\Also\ \frac{ε}{D_h} = \frac{0.001 65}{2} = 0.000 83 \end{matrix} \right\}\) f = 0.019 (Figure 3.3)

Substituting into the right-hand side of Equation i gives
\(\frac{(p_1 – p_2)g_c}{ρg} = \frac{0.019(30)}{2 }\frac{(6.1)^2}{2(9.81)} = 0.545 m\) (ii)
The pressure drop then is
\(p_1 – p_2 = 1.17(9.81)(0.545)\)
\(p_1 – p_2 = 6.2 N/m^2 = 6.2\) Pa

The problem asks for the pressure drop; however, it is customary when dealing with air flows to express the pressure loss in terms of a head of water. Thus,

\(∆h_{H_2O} = \frac{(p_1 – p_2)g_c}{ρH_2Og} = \frac{6.2}{1 000(9.81)}\)
\(∆h_{H_2O} =0.000 64\) m of \(H_2O = 0.064\) cm of \(H_2O\)
