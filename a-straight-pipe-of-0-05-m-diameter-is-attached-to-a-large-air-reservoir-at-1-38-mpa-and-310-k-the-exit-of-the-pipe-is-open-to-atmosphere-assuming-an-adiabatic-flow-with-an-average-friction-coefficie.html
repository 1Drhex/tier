Guest Question.

A straight pipe of 0.05 m diameter is attached to a large air reservoir at 1.38 MPa and 310 K. The exit of the pipe is open to atmosphere. Assuming an adiabatic flow with an average friction coefficient of 0.005, calculate the pipe length necessary to obtain a mass flow rate of \(2.25 kg s ^{-1}\).

Admin
Solution

Let the subscripts 1 and 2 refer to conditions at entry and exit of the pipe, respectively. Given:

\(\dot{m}=2.25 kg s ^{-1}\)

 

By Eq. (8.2), the mass velocity is

\(\frac{\dot{m}}{A}=\rho V=G\) (8.2)

\(G=\frac{\dot{m}}{A}=\frac{2.25}{(\pi / 4)(0.05)^{2}}\)

 

\(=0.1146 \times 10^{4} kg \left( m ^{2} s \right)^{-1}\)

 

Also,

\(G=p_{1} M_{1}\left(\frac{\gamma}{R T_{1}}\right)^{1 / 2}=p_{2} M_{2}\left(\frac{\gamma}{R T_{2}}\right)^{1 / 2}=\text { constant }\)

 

Now the relation

\(p_{1} M_{1}\left(\frac{\gamma}{R T_{1}}\right)^{1 / 2}=G\)

 

can be rewritten as

\(\frac{p_{1}}{p_{0}}\left(\frac{T_{0}}{T_{1}}\right)^{1 / 2} M_{1}=\left(\frac{R}{\gamma}\right)^{1 / 2} G \frac{T_{0}^{1 / 2}}{p_{0}}=\frac{\sqrt{\gamma R T_{0}}}{\gamma} \frac{G}{p_{0}}\)

 

\(=\frac{352.9}{1.4} \times \frac{0.1146 \times 10^{4}}{13.8 \times 10^{5}}\)

 

\(= 0.209\) (i)

 

The left-hand side of Eq. (i) has three parameters and out of them the pressure ratio and temperature ratio are uniquely related to Mach number. By trial and error, we can solve this equation as follows:

Let \(M_{1}=0.21\). Then

\(LHS =\frac{0.21 \times 0.9697}{\sqrt{0.9913}}=0.2045\)

 

LHS < RHS

Let \(M_{1}=0.22\) Then

\(LHS =\frac{0.22 \times 0.9668}{\sqrt{0.9904}}=0.2136\)

 

LHS > RHS

Hence, \(M_{1}\) lies between 0.21 and 0.22. For \(M_{1}=0.213\), LHS is nearly equal to RHS. Therefore, \(M_{1}=0.213\) can be taken as the correct solution. For this value of \(M_{1}\),

\(\frac{p_{1}}{p_{0}}=0.969, \quad \frac{p_{1}}{p^{*}}=5.12\)

 

Thus,

\(p_{1}=1.337 MPa\)

 

\(p^{*}=261.1 kPa\)

 

For \(M_{1}=0.213\), from Eq. (8.20),

\(4 \bar{f} \frac{L_{\max }}{D}=\frac{1-M^{2}}{\gamma M^{2}}+\frac{\gamma+1}{2 \gamma} \ln \left[\frac{(\gamma+1) M^{2}}{2\left(1+\frac{\gamma-1}{2} M^{2}\right)}\right]\) (8.20)

\(4 f \frac{L_{\max }}{D}=12.11\)

 

Therefore,

\(L_{\max }=\frac{12.11 \times 0.05}{4 \times 0.005}\)

 

\(= 30.275 m\)
