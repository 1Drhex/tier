Guest Question.

Designing a depletion MOSFET source follower Design a source follower as shown in Fig. 7.42(a) to yield \( R_{ i } \geq 500 k \Omega \text { and } i_{ D }=10 mA \). The MOS parameters are \( V_{ p }=-4 V , I_{ DSS }=20 mA , \text { and } V_{ M }=-200 V \). Assume \( V_{ DD }=20 V \).

Admin
Solution

The design of a common-drain (CD) amplifier is very simple; it requires determining the values of \( R_{ SR } \). We know that

 

\( K_{ n }=\frac{I_{ DSS }}{V_{ p }^{2}}=\frac{20 mA }{(-4)^{2}}=1.25 mA / V ^{2} \)

 

Step 1. For the depletion MOSFET, we can use the self-biasing circuit arrangement as shown in Fig. 7.24(b) where \( R_{1}=\infty \) . Calculate the gate resistance \( R_{2} \):

 

\( R_{2}=R_{ i }=500 k \Omega \)

 

Step 2. For known values of \( i_{ D }, I_{ DSS }, \text { and } V_{ p } \), calculate \( v_{ GS } \) from Eq. (7.21), \( i_{ D }=K_{ n }\left(v_{ GS }-V_{ p }\right)^{2} \):

 

\(\begin{aligned}i_{ D } &=K_{ n }\left[2\left(v_{ GS }-V_{ p }\right)\left(v_{ GS }-V_{ p }\right)-\left(v_{ GS }-V_{ p }\right)^{2}\right] \\&=K_{ n }\left(v_{ GS }-V_{ p }\right)^{2}\end{aligned} \) (7.21)

 

\( 10 mA =1.25 mA / V ^{2} \times\left(v_{ GS }+4\right)^{2} \)

 

which gives \( v_{ GS }=-1.172 V \text { or }-6.828 V .\) The acceptable value is \( v_{ GS }=-1.172 V .\)

Step 3. For the known value of \( v_{ GS }, \text { calculate } R_{ SR } \):

Using KVL through the loop formed by the gate, \( R_{ G } \text { and } R_{ SR } \) and in Fig. 7.24(b), we get

\( 0=v_{ GS }+R_{ SR } i_{ D } \)

Which gives

\( R_{ SR }=-\frac{v_{ GS }}{i_{ D }}=-\left(\frac{-1.172 V }{10 mA }\right)=117.2 \Omega \)

 

Step 4. Find the small-signal parameters of the transistor. From Eq. (7.30),

\( g_{ m }=\frac{\delta i_{ D }}{\delta v_{ GS }}=2 K_{ n }\left(v_{ GS }-V_{ p }\right) \quad \text { for depletion MOSFETs } \) (7.30)

 

\( g_{ m }=2 K_{ n }\left(v_{ GS }-V_{ p }\right)=2 \times 1.25 m \times(-1.172+4)=7.07 mA / V \)

 

From Eq. (7.26),

\( \frac{1}{r_{ o }}=\frac{d i_{ D }}{d v_{ DS }}=\frac{I_{ D }}{\left|V_{ M }\right|}=\lambda I_{ D } \quad \text { for all MOSFETs } \) (7.26)

 

\( r_{ o 1}=\frac{\left|V_{ M }\right|}{i_{ D }}=\frac{200 V }{10 mA }=20 k \Omega \)

 

Thus,

\( \mu_{ g }=g_{ m } r_{ o1 }=7.07 \times 20=141.1 V / V \)

Step 5. Find the values of \( C_{1} \text { and } C_{2} \text {. Let us choose } C_{1}=C_{2}=10 \mu F \text {. } \)

 

Step 6. Calculate the output resistance \( R_{ o } \) and the open-circuit voltage gain \( A_{ vo } \).

 

\(\begin{aligned}&R_{ o }=\frac{1}{g_{ m }}\left\|r_{ o1 }\right\| R_{ SR }=\frac{1}{7.07 m }\|20 k \| 117.2=63.95 \Omega \\&A_{ vo }=\frac{g_{ m }\left(R_{ SR } \| r_{ o1 }\right)}{1+g_{ m }\left(R_{ SR } \| r_{ o1 }\right)}=\frac{7.07 m \times(117.2 \| 20 k )}{1+7.07 m \times(117.2 \| 20 k )}=0.451\end{aligned}\)
