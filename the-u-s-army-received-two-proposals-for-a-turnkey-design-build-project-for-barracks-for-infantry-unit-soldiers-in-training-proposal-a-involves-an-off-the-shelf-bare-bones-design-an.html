Guest Question.

The U.S. Army received two proposals for a turnkey design-build project for barracks for infantry unit soldiers in training. Proposal \(A\) involves an off-the-shelf “bare-bones” design and standard grade construction of walls, windows, doors, and other features. With this option, heating and cooling costs will be greater, maintenance costs will be higher, and replacement will be sooner than for proposal \(B\). The initial cost for \(A\) will be $\(750,000\). Heating and cooling costs will average $\(72,000\) per year, with maintenance costs averaging $\(24,000\) per year. Minor remodeling will be required in years \(5, 10\), and \(15\) at a cost of $\(150,000\) each time in order to render the units usable for \(20\) years. They will have no salvage value.
