Guest Question.

Crude oil is pumped from the test separator at A to the stock tank using a galvanized iron pipe that has a diameter of 4 in. If the total pipe length is 180 ft, and the volumetric flow at A is 400 gal/min, determine the required horsepower supplied by the pump. The pressure at A is 4 psi, and the stock tank is opened to the atmosphere. The frictional head loss in the pipe is 0.25 in./ft, and the head loss for each of the four bends is \( K (V^{2}/2g), \) where K = 0.09 and V is the velocity of the flow in the pipe. Take \(g_o = 55 lb/ft^{3}. \) Note that \(1 ft^{3} = 7.48 gal. \)

Admin
Solution

The discharge is

 

\(Q =(400\frac{gal}{min}) (\frac{1ft^{3}}{7.48 gal})(\frac{1min}{60 s}) = 0.8913 ft^{3}/s \)

 

Thus,

\(Q = V_{A}A_{A}; 0.8913 ft^{3}/s = V_{A}[\pi (\frac{2}{12}ft)^{2}] \)

 

\(V_{A} = 10.21 ft/s \)

Energy Equation.

Take the water from A to B on the control volume. Then

\(\frac{P_{A}}{y_{0}} +\frac{V^{2}_{A}}{2g} + z_{A} + h_{pump} =\frac{P_{B}}{y_{0}} +\frac{V^{2}_{B}}{2g} + z_{B} + h_{turb} + h_{L} \)

 

Since point B is exposed to atmosphere, \( P_{B} = p_{atm} = 0.\) Also,

 

\(P_{A}=(4\frac{lb}{in^{2}})(\frac{12in}{1ft})^{2} = 576 lb/ft^{2} \)

Since the stock tank is a large reservoir\(V_{B} = 0.\) The frictional head loss is

 

\((h_{L})_{f} = [\frac{(0.25/12) ft}{ft}](180ft) = 3.75 ft \)

 

There are four bends between point A and B. Thus, the head losses due to the bends is

 

\((h_{L})_{M} = 4(k\frac{V^{2}}{2g}) = 4{(0.09)[\frac{(10.21ft/s)^{2}}{2(32.2ft/s^{2})}]} = 0.5831 ft \)

 

With reference to the datum set through point A,\( z_{A} = 0 and z_{B} = 30 ft .\) Substituting these results into the energy equation,

 

\(\frac{576 lb/ft^{2}}{55.0 lb/ft^{3}} + \frac{(10.21 ft/s)^{2}}{2(32.2 ft/s^{2})} + 0 + h_{pump} = 0 + 0 + 30 ft + 0 + (3.75 ft + 0.5831 ft) \)

 

\(h_{pump} = 22.24 ft \)

The required output power can be determined from

\(W_{s} = Qy_{co}h_{pump} = (0.8913 ft^{3}/s)(55.0 lb/ft^{3})(22.24 ft) \) \(= (1090.23 ft \cdot lb/s)(\frac{1 hb}{550 ft \cdot lb/s})\)

= 1.98 hp
