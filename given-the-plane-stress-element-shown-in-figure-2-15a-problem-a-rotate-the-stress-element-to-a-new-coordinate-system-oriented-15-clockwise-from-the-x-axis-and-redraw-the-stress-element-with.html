Guest Question.

Given: The plane stress element shown in Figure 2.15a.

PROBLEM

a. Rotate the stress element to a new coordinate system oriented 15° clockwise from the x-axis, and redraw the stress element with all stress components properly oriented.

b. Determine the principal stresses and principal stress coordinate system, and redraw the stress element with the principal stress components properly oriented.

Admin
Solution

a. The following components of stress are implied by the stress element shown (note that the shear stress is algebraically negative, in accordance with the sign convention discussed in Section 2.5):

\(σ_{xx}\) =70 MPa

\(σ_{yy}\)= 15 MPa

\(τ_{xy}\) = – 50MPa

The stress element is to be rotated clockwise. That is, the +x′-axis is rotated away from the +y-axis. Applying the right-hand rule it is clear that this is a negative rotation:

θ = – 15°

Equation 2.20

\(\left \{ \begin{matrix}\sigma _{x’x’} \\ \sigma _{y’y’} \\ \tau _{x’y’} \end{matrix} \right \}=\left [ \begin{matrix}\cos^2(\theta ) & \sin^2(\theta ) & 2\cos(\theta )\sin(\theta ) \\ \sin^2(\theta ) & \cos^2(\theta ) & -2\cos(\theta )\sin(\theta ) \\ – \cos(\theta )\sin(\theta ) & \cos(\theta )\sin(\theta ) &\cos^2(\theta ) – \sin^2(\theta ) \end{matrix} \right ]\left \{ \begin{matrix}\sigma _{xx} \\ \sigma _{yy} \\ \tau _{xy} \end{matrix} \right \}\) (2.20)

becomes:

\(\left \{ \begin{matrix}\sigma _{x’x’} \\ \sigma _{y’y’} \\ \tau _{x’y’} \end{matrix} \right \}=\left [ \begin{matrix}\cos^2(- 15° ) & \sin^2(- 15° ) & 2\cos(- 15° )\sin(- 15°) \\ \sin^2(- 15°) & \cos^2(- 15° ) & -2\cos(- 15° )\sin(- 15° ) \\ – \cos(- 15° )\sin(- 15° ) & \cos(- 15° )\sin(- 15°) &\cos^2(- 15° ) – \sin^2(- 15° ) \end{matrix} \right ]\times \left \{ \begin{matrix}70 \\ 15 \\ – 50 \end{matrix} \right \}\)

 

\(\left \{ \begin{matrix}\sigma _{x’x’} \\ \sigma _{y’y’} \\ \tau _{x’y’} \end{matrix} \right \}=\left [ \begin{matrix}0.9330 & 0.0670 & −0.5000 \\ 0 0670 & 0.9330 & 0.5000 \\ 0.2500 & -0.2500 &0.8660 \end{matrix} \right ] \left \{ \begin{matrix}70 \\ 15 \\ – 50 \end{matrix} \right \}=\left \{ \begin{matrix}91.3\\ -6.32\\ – 29.6 \end{matrix} \right \}\)MPa

The rotated stress element is shown in Figure 2.15b.

b. The principal stresses are found through application of Equation 2.26:

\(\sigma _{p_1},\sigma _{p_2}=\frac{\sigma _{xx}+\sigma _{yy}}{2}\pm \sqrt{\left\lgroup\frac{\sigma _{xx}-\sigma _{yy}}{2} \right\rgroup^2 + \tau _{xy}^2 }\) (2.26)

 

\(\sigma _{p_1},\sigma _{p_2}=\frac{70+15}{2}\pm \sqrt{\left\lgroup\frac{70-15}{2} \right\rgroup^2 + (-50)^2}= 42.5 \pm 57.1\) MPa

\(\sigma _{p_1}\) = 99.6 MPa

\(\sigma _{p_2}\)= -14.6MPa

The orientation of the principal stress coordinate system is given by Equation 2.27:

\(\theta _p = \frac{1}{2} \arctan\left\lgroup\frac{2\tau _{xy}}{\sigma _{xx} – \sigma _{yy}} \right\rgroup\) (2.27)

 

\(\theta _p = \frac{1}{2} \arctan\left\lgroup\frac{2(-50)}{70 – 15} \right\rgroup = -31°\)

Since \(θ_p\) is negative, the +p1-axis is oriented 31° clockwise from the x-axis. The stress element is shown in the principal stress coordinate system in Figure 2.15c.
