Guest Question.

Determine the forces in all members of the truss in Figure 9.13.AE is constant for all bars.

Admin
Solution

The truss in Figure 9.13a is internally indeterminate to the first degree. The unknown forces—bars and reactions—total nine, but only 2n = 8 equations are available for their solution. From a physical point of view, an extra diagonal member that is not required for stability has been added to transmit lateral load into support A. Application of the 40-kip horizontal force atDproduces forces in all bars of the truss. We will select the axial force \( F_{AC}\) in bar AC as the redundant and represent it by the symbol X. We now imagine that bar AC is cut by passing
an imaginary section 1-1 through the bar. On each side of the cut, the redundant X is applied to the ends of the bar as an external load (Figure 9.13b). A detail at the cut is shown in Figure 9.13c. To show the action of the internal forces on each side of the cut, the bars have been offset. The zero dimension between the longitudinal axis of the bars indicates that the bars are actually collinear. To show that no gap exists between the ends of the bars, we have noted on the sketch that the relative displacement between the ends of the bars \( \Delta _{Rel}\) equals zero.

 

\( \Delta _{Rel}=0\) (9.9)

 

The requirement that no gap exists between the ends of the bars in the actual
structure forms the basis of the compatibility equation. As in previous examples, we next divide the analysis into two parts. In Figure 9.13d the released structure is analyzed for the applied load of 40 kips. As the stressed bars of the released structure deform, a gap \( \Delta _{0}\) opens between the ends of the bars at section 1-1. The Q-system required to compute \( \Delta _{0} \) is shown in Figure 9.13e. In Figure 9.13f, the released structure is analyzed for the action of the redundant. The relative displacement \( \delta _{00}\) of the ends of the bar produced by the unit value of the redundant equals the sum of the displacements \( \delta _{1} \) and \( \delta _{2}\) . To compute \( \delta _{00}\) , we again use the force system shown in Figure 9.13e as the Q-system. In this case the Q-system and the P-system are identical. Expressing the geometric condition given by Equation 9.9 in terms of the displacements produced by the applied loads and the redundant, we can write

 

\( \Delta _{0}+X\delta _{00}=0\) (9.10)

 

Substituting numerical values of \( \Delta _{0} \) and \( \delta _{00}\) into Equation 9.10 and solving for X give

 

\( -0.346 + 0.0138 X =0\)

 

\( X=25.07 \) kips

 

The computations for \( \Delta _{0} \) and \( \delta _{00} \) using virtual work are given below.

\( \Delta _{0}\) :

Use the P-system in Figure 9.13d and Q-system in Figure 9.13e:

 

\( W_{Q}=\sum \frac{F_{Q}F_{P}L}{AE}\)

 

\( 1kip\left ( \Delta _{0} \right )=\frac{1\left ( -50 \right )\left ( 20 \times 12 \right )}{AE}+\frac{-0.8\left ( 40 \right )\left ( 16\times 12 \right )}{AE}+\frac{-0.6\left ( 30 \right )\left ( 12\times 12 \right )}{AE}\)

 

\( \Delta _{0}=-\frac{20736}{AE}=-\frac{20736}{2\left ( 30000 \right )}=-0.346 \) in.

 

\( \delta _{00} \) :

P-system in Figure 9.13f and Q-system in Figure 9.13e (note: P- and Q-systems are the same; therefore, \( F_{Q}=F_{P}\) ):

 

\( W_{Q}=\sum \frac{F^{2}_{Q}L}{AE}\)

 

\( 1 kip\left ( \delta _{1} \right )+1kip\left ( \delta _{2} \right )=\frac{\left ( -0.6 \right )^{2}\left ( 12\times 12 \right )}{AE}\left ( 2 \right )+\frac{\left ( -0.8 \right )^{2}\left ( 16\times 12 \right )}{AE}\left ( 2 \right )+\frac{1^{2}\left ( 20\times 12 \right )}{AE}\)

 

Since \( \delta _{1}+\delta _{2}=\delta _{00}\) ,

 

\( \delta _{00}=\frac{829.44}{AE}=\frac{829.44}{2\left ( 30000 \right )}=0.0138 \) in.

 

Bar forces are established by superposition of the forces in Figure 9.13d and f. For example, the forces in bars DC, AB, and DB are

 

\( F_{DC}=0+\left ( -0.8 \right )\left ( 25.07 \right )=-20.06 \) kips

 

\( F_{AB}=40+\left ( -0.8 \right )\left ( 25.07 \right )=19.95 \) kips

 

\( F_{DB}=-50+1 \left ( 25.07 \right )=-24.93 \) kips

 

Final results are summarized in Figure 9.13g.
