Guest Question.

Pannetier and Souchay (6) indicated that when an equimolar gaseous mixture of butadiene and acrolein is allowed to react for 40 min at 330C and 1 atm, 78.7% of the initial butadiene and 64.9% of the acrolein have been consumed.For the conditions cited, what are the numerical values of the two rate constants?

Admin
Solution

The forms of equations (A) and (B) differ from the competitive parallel reactions considered in Section 5.2.1.2. Thus, it will be necessary to derive appropriate equations for use in the course of our analysis.

 

From the indicated data and basic stoichiometric principles, one finds that complete reaction leads to:

 

\(C_{A}=C_{A0}(1-0.787)=C_{A0}-2\xi _{1}^{\ast }-\xi _{2}^{\ast }\) (C)

 

and

 

\(C_{B}=C_{B0}(1-0.649)=C_{B0}-2\xi _{2}^{\ast }=C_{A0}-\xi _{2}^{\ast }\)

 

where we have noted that \(C_{A0}=C_{B0}\). Hence,

 

\(\xi _{2}^{\ast }=0.649C_{A0}\)

 

and

 

\(\xi _{1}^{\ast }=\frac{0.787-0.649}{2}C_{A0}=0.069C_{A0}\)

 

In the present case, elimination of time as an independent variable between equations (A) and (B) gives

 

\(\frac{d\xi _{1}^{\ast }}{d\xi _{2}^{\ast }}=\frac{k_{1}C^{2}_{A}}{k_{2}C_{A}C_{B}}=\frac{k_{1}C_{A}}{k_{2}C_{B}}\)

 

or, using equations (C) and (D), we have

 

\(\frac{d\xi _{1}^{\ast }}{d\xi _{2}^{\ast }}=\frac{k_{1}}{k_{2}}\left ( \frac{C_{A0}-2\xi _{1}^{\ast }-\xi _{2}^{\ast }}{C_{A0}-\xi _{2}^{\ast }} \right )=\frac{k_{1}}{k_{2}}\left ( 1-\frac{2\xi _{1}^{\ast }}{C_{A0}-\xi _{2}^{\ast }} \right )\)

 

The product distribution thus changes as the reaction proceeds.

 

Rearrangement and recognition that \(d(C_{A0}-\xi _{2}^{\ast })=-d\xi _{2}^{\ast }\) gives

 

\(\frac{d\xi _{1}^{\ast }}{d(C_{A0}-\xi _{2}^{\ast })}-\frac{2k_{1}}{k_{2}}\left ( \frac{\xi _{1}^{\ast }}{C_{A0}-\xi _{2}^{\ast }} \right )=-\frac{k_{1}}{k_{2}}\)

 

If we let \(x=C_{A0}-\xi _{2}^{\ast }\) and \(r=k_{1}/k_{2}\),

 

\(\frac{d\xi _{1}^{\ast }}{dx}-2r\frac{\xi _{1}^{\ast }}{x}=-r\)

 

This first-order linear differential equation may be solved using an integrating factor approach to obtain

 

\(\xi _{1}^{\ast }=\frac{r}{2r-1}\left [ x-C_{A0}\left ( \frac{x}{C_{A0}} \right )^{2r} \right ]\)

 

which can be rewritten as

 

\(\frac{\xi _{1}^{\ast }}{C_{A0}}=\frac{r}{2r-1}\left [ 1-\frac{\xi _{2}^{\ast }}{C_{A0}}-\left ( 1-\frac{\xi _{2}^{\ast }}{C_{A0}} \right )^{2r} \right ]\) (E)

 

This transcendental equation must be solved for r using the numerical values indicated and recognizing that 0 < r < 1 for the product distribution cited:

 

\(0.069=\frac{r}{2r-1}[1-0.649-(1-0.649)^{2r}]\)

 

The result is \(r=k_{1}/k_{2}=0.123\).

 

To evaluate each of the rate constants individually, it is necessary to obtain another relation between \(k_{1}\) and \(k_{2}\).

 

This evaluation will involve integration of one of the rate expressions. From equations (B) to (D),

 

\(\frac{d\xi _{2}^{\ast }}{dt}=k_{2}(C_{A0}-2\xi _{1}^{\ast }-\xi _{2}^{\ast })(C_{A0}-\xi _{2}^{\ast })\)

 

Rearrangement and integration give

 

\(k_{2}C_{A0}t=\int_{0}^{0.649}\frac{d(\xi _{2}^{\ast }/C_{A0})}{[1-2(\xi _{1}^{\ast }/C_{A0})-(\xi _{2}^{\ast }/C_{A0})][1-(\xi _{2}^{\ast }/C_{A0})]}\) (F)

 

Equation (E) relates \(\xi _{1}^{\ast }\) and \(\xi _{2}^{\ast }\),so \(\xi _{1}^{\ast }\) may be evaluated at various values of \(\xi _{2}^{\ast }\) using the value of r determined above:

 

\(\frac{\xi _{1}^{\ast }}{C_{A0}}=\left ( \frac{0.123}{2(0.123)-1} \right )\left ( 1-\frac{\xi _{2}^{\ast }}{C_{A0}} \right )\left [ 1-\left ( 1-\frac{\xi _{2}^{\ast }}{C_{A0}} \right )^{2(0.123)-1} \right ]\)

 

\(=0.163\left ( 1-\frac{\xi _{2}^{\ast }}{C_{A0}} \right )\left [ \left ( 1-\frac{\xi _{2}^{\ast }}{C_{A0}} \right )^{-0.754}-1 \right ]\)

 

Using the values of \(\xi _{1}^{\ast }/C_{A0}\) determined from this relation, one can integrate equation (F) numerically. The result is \(k_{2}C_{A0}t=2.37\).

 

The initial reactant concentration may be determined from the ideal gas law:

 

\(C_{A0}=\frac{y_{A}P}{RT}=\frac{0.5(1)}{0.082(603)}=1.01\times 10^{-2}\)mol/L

 

Thus,

 

\(k_{2}=\frac{2.37}{(1.01\times 10^{-2})40}=5.86\) L/(mol× min)

 

Consequently,

 

\(k_{1}=\left ( \frac{k_{1}}{k_{2}} \right )k_{2}=0.123(5.86)=.72\) L/(mol× min)

 

These values of the reaction rate constants differ from those cited by Pannetier and Souchay (6) because these authors treated the two reactions erroneously, as if they were of the simple parallel type instead of as if there were a competition between the acrolein and butadiene molecules for other butadiene molecules.
