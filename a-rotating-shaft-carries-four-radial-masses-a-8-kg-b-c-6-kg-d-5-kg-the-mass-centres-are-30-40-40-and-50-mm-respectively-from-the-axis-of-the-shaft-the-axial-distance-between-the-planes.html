Guest Question.

A rotating shaft carries four radial masses A = 8 kg, B, C = 6 kg, D = 5 kg. The mass centres are 30, 40, 40 and 50 mm, respectively, from the axis of the shaft. The axial distance between the planes of rotation of A and B is 400 mm and between B and C is 500 mm. The masses A and C are at right angles to each other. Find for a complete balance, (a) the angle of the masses B and D from mass A, (b) the axial distance between the planes of rotation of C and D, and (c) the magnitude of mass B.

From Table 12.19, we have

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}=0.24+0.25 \cos \theta_{4} \).

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}=0.24+0.25 \sin \theta_{4} \).

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}=-0.096 \).

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}=0.12 \).

\( \left[\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}\right)^{2}+\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5}=M_{2} r_{2} l_{2} \).

\( \left[(-0.096)^{2}+(0.12)^{2}\right]^{0.5}=0.25 l_{4} \).

\( l_{4}=\frac{0.15367}{0.25}=0.6147 m \text { or } 614.7 mm \).

\( d=614.7-500=114.7 mm \).

\( \tan \theta_{4}=\frac{-\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}}{-\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}} \).

\( =\frac{-(0.12)}{-(-0.096)}=-1.25 \).

\( \theta_{4}=-51.34^{\circ} \text { i.e. } 308.66^{\circ} \).

Since the numerator is negative and denominator is positive, therefore \( \theta_{4} \) lies in the fourth quadrant.

\( M_{2} r_{2}=\left[\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}+M_{M} r_{M} \cos \theta_{M}\right)^{2}+\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}+M_{M} r_{M} \sin \theta_{M}\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5} \).

\( 0.04 M_{2}=\left[\left(0.25 \cos 308.66^{\circ}+0.24\right)^{2}+\left(0.25 \sin 308.66^{\circ}+0.24\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5} \).

\( =\left[(0.3962)^{2}+(0.04478)^{2}\right]^{0.5} \).

\( =0.39869 \).

\( M_{2}=9.967 kg \).

\( \tan \theta_{2}=\frac{-\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}+M_{M} r_{M} \sin \theta_{M}\right)}{-\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}+M_{M} r_{M} \cos \theta_{M}\right)} \).

\( =\frac{-0.04478}{-0.3962}=0.11302 \).

\( \theta_{2}=6.45^{\circ} \text { i.e. } 186.45^{\circ} \).

Since the numerator and denominator are both negative, therefore, \( \theta_{2} \) lies in the third quadrant.
