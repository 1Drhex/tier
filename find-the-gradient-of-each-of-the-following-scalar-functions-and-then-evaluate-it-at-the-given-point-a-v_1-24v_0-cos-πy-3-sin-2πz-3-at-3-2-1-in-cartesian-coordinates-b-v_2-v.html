Guest Question.

Find the gradient of each of the following scalar functions and then evaluate it at the given point.
(a)\( V_1 = 24V_0 cos (πy/3) sin (2πz/3) at (3, 2, 1)\) in Cartesian coordinates,
(b) \(V_2 = V_0e^{−2r} sin 3\phi at (1, π/2, 3) \) in cylindrical coordinates,
(c) \( V_3 = V_0 (a/R) cos 2θ at (2a, 0, π)\) in spherical coordinates.

Admin
Solution

(a) Using Eq. (3.72) for ∇,

\( \nabla T = grad T = \hat{x}\frac{\delta T}{\delta x}+\hat{y}\frac{\delta T}{\delta y}+\hat{z\frac{\delta T}{\delta z} } \)

 

\( \nabla V_1 = \hat{x}\frac{\delta V_1}{\delta x}+\hat{y}\frac{\delta V_1}{\delta y}+\hat{z}\frac{\delta V_1}{\delta z} \)

 

\(= -\hat{y}8\pi V_0 \sin \frac{\pi y}{3} \sin \frac{2\pi z}{3} +\hat{z}16πV_0 \cos \frac{\pi y}{3}\cos \frac{2\pi z}{3} \)

 

\( = 8πV_0 \left[-\hat{y}\sin \frac{\pi y}{3} \sin \frac{2\pi z}{3} +\hat{z}2πV_0 \cos \frac{\pi y}{3}\cos \frac{2\pi z}{3}\right] \)

 

At (3, 2, 1),

\(\nabla V_1= 8πV_0 \left[-\hat{y}\sin^2 \frac{2\pi }{3}+\hat{z}2 \cos^2 \frac{2\pi }{3}\right] = πV_0\left[-\hat{y}6+\hat{z}4\right]. \)

 

(b) The function \(V_2 \) is expressed in terms of cylindrical
variables. Hence, we need to use Eq. (3.82) for \(\nabla \):

 

\(\nabla V_2=(\hat{r}\frac{\delta}{\delta r}+\hat{\phi }\frac{1}{r}\frac{\delta}{\delta \phi } +\hat{z}\frac{\delta}{\delta z} ) V_0e^{-2r} \sin 3\phi \)

 

\(=-\hat{r}2 V_0e^{-2r} \sin 3\phi + \hat{\phi }(3V_0e^{-2r}\cos 3\phi )/r \)

 

\(=[-\hat{r}2\sin 3\phi + \hat{\phi } \frac{3\cos 3\phi}{r} ]V_0e^{-2r} \)

 

\( At (1, π/2, 3), r = 1 and \phi = π/2. \)

 

\( \nabla V_2 = [-\hat{r}2\sin \frac{3\pi }{2}+ \hat{\phi }3\cos \frac{3\pi }{2} ]V_0e^{-2}=-\hat{r}2V_0e^{-2}= \hat{r} 0.27V_0. \)

 

(c) As \(V_3 \) is expressed in spherical coordinates, we apply Eq. (3.83) to \(V_3 \):

\(\nabla = \hat{R}\frac{\delta}{\delta R}+ \hat{\theta }\frac{1}{R}\frac{\delta}{\delta \theta }+\hat{\phi }\frac{1}{R \sin \theta }\frac{\delta}{\delta \phi } \)

 

\(\nabla V_3 = (\hat{R}\frac{\delta}{\delta R}+ \hat{\theta }\frac{1}{R}\frac{\delta}{\delta \theta }+\hat{\phi }\frac{1}{R \sin \theta }\frac{\delta}{\delta \phi } ) V_0 (\frac{a}{R} )\cos 2\theta \)

 

\(=-\hat{R}\frac{V_0 a}{R^2}\cos 2\theta – \hat{\theta }\frac{2 V_0a}{R^2} \sin 2\theta \)

 

\(=-[\hat{R}\cos 2\theta + \hat{\theta }2 \sin 2\theta ]\frac{ V_0a}{R^2} \)

 

\( At (2a, 0, π), R = 2a and θ = 0, \) \( \nabla V_3= – \hat{R}\frac{ V_0}{4a}. \)
