Guest Question.

A regenerative Rankine cycle has steam entering turbine at 200 bar, 650ºC and leaving at 0.05 bar. Considering feed water heaters to be of open type determine thermal efficiency for the following conditions;
(a) there is no feed water heater
(b) there is only one feed water heater working at 8 bar
(c) there are two feed water heaters working at 40 bar and 4 bar respectively.
Also give layout and T-s representation for each of the case described above.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{Case (a) When there is no feed water heater}\\\text{Thermal efficiency of cycle} = \frac{(h_{2} – h_{3}) – (h_{1} – h_{4})}{(h_{2} – h_{1})}\\\text{From steam tables,}\\\begin{aligned}h_{2}&= h_{\text{at 200 bar, 650ºC}} = 3675.3 kJ/kg\\s_{2}&= s_{\text{at 200 bar, 650ºC}} = 6.6582 kJ/kg · K\\h_{4}&= h_{\text{ƒ at 0.05 bar}} = 137.82 kJ/kg\\v_{4}&= v_{\text{ƒ at 0.05 bar}} = 0.001005 m^{3}/kg.\\h_{\text{ƒ at 0.05 bar}}&= 137.82 kJ/kg, h_{\text{ƒg at 0.05 bar}} = 2423.7 kJ/kg\\s_{\text{ƒ at 0.05 bar}}&= 0.4764 kJ/kg · K, s_{\text{ƒg at 0.05 bar}} = 7.9187 kJ/kg · K\\\text{For process 2 – 3, }s_{2}&= s_{3} .\text{ Let dryness fraction at 3 be }x_{3}.\\s_{3}&= 6.6582 = s_{\text{ƒ at 0.05 bar}} + x_{3} · s_{\text{ƒg at 0.05 bar}}\\6.6582&= 0.4764 + x_{3} · 7.9187\\x_{3}&= 0.781\\h_{3}&= h_{\text{ƒ at 0.05 bar}} + x_{3} · h_{\text{fg at 0.05 bar}}\\&= 2030.73 kJ/kg\\\text{For pumping process 4-1,}\qquad \qquad \qquad &\\h_{1} – h_{4}&= v_{4} · Δp\\h_{1} – 137.82&= 0.001005 × (200 – 0.05) × 10^{2}\\h_{1}&= 157.92 kJ/kg\\\text{Thermal efficiency of cycle}&= \frac{(3675.3 – 2030.73) – (157.92 – 137.82)}{(3675.3 – 157.92)}\\&= \text{0.4618 or 46.18 }\%\end{aligned}\\\text{Case (b) When there is only one feed water heater working at 8 bar Here, let mass of steam bled for feed heating be m kg}\\\text{For process 2-6, }s_{2} = s_{6} = 6.6582 kJ/kg · K\\\text{Let dryness fraction at state 6 be }x_{6}\\\begin{aligned}s_{6}&= s_{\text{f at 8 bar}} + x_{6} · s_{\text{fg at 8 bar}}\\\text{From steam tables, }h_{\text{ƒ at 8 bar}}&= 721.11 kJ/kg\\v_{\text{ƒ at 8 bar}}&= 0.001115 m^{3}/kg, h_{\text{ƒg at 8 bar}} = 2048 kJ/kg\\s_{\text{ƒ at 8 bar}}&= 2.0462 kJ/kg · K, s_{\text{ƒg at bar}} = 4.6166 kJ/kg · K\\\text{Substituting entropy values, }\qquad \qquad x_{6}&= 0.999\end{aligned}\\h_{6} = h_{\text{ƒ at 8 bar}} + x_{6} · h_{\text{ƒg at 8 bar}} = 721.11 + (0.999 × 2048) = 2767.06 kJ/kg\\\text{Assuming the state of fluid leaving open feed water heater to be saturated liquid at 8 bar.}\\\begin{aligned}h_{7}&= h_{\text{ƒ at 8 bar}}= 721.11 kJ/kg.\\\text{For process 4-5, }h_{5} = h_{4} + v_{4} × (8 – 0.05) × 10^{2}&= 137.82 + (0.001005 × 7.95 × 10^{2}) = 138.62 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Applying energy balance at open feed water heater,}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad m × h_{6} + (1 – m) × h_{5}&= 1 × h_{7}\\(m × 2767.06) + ((1 – m) × 138.62)&= 721.11\\m&= 0.2216 kg\\\text{For process 7-1, }h_{1}&= h_{7} + v_{7} (200 – 8) × 10^{2};\text{ here }h_{7} = h_{\text{ƒ at 8 bar}}, v_{7} = v_{\text{f at 8 bar}}\\h_{1}&= h_{7} +v_{\text{f at 8 bar}} (200 – 8) × 10^{2} = 721.11 + (0.001115 × 192 × 10^{2})\\h_{1}&= 742.518 kJ/kg\\\text{Thermal efficiency of cycle}&=\frac{(h_{2} – h_{6}) + (1 – m) . (h_{6} – h_{3}) – \left\{(1 – m)(h_{5} – h_{4}) + (h_{1} – h_{7})\right\} } {(h_{2} – h_{1})}\end{aligned}\\\qquad \qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad \qquad\qquad =\frac{(3675.3 – 2767.06) + (1 – 0.2216) × (2767.06 – 2030.73) – \left\{(1 – 0.2216) × (138.62 – 137.82) + (742.518 – 721.11)\right\} }{(3675.3 – 742.518)} \\\qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad\text{Thermal efficiency of cycle}= \text{0.4976 or 49.76 }\%\\\text{Case (c) When there are two feed water heaters working at 40 bar and 4 bar}\\\text{Here, let us assume the mass of steam at 40 bar, 4 bar to be }m_{1}\text{ kg, and }m_{2}\text{ kg respectively.}\\\text{For process 2–10–9–3, }s_{2} = s_{10} = s_{9} = s_{3} = 6.6582 kJ/kg ·K\\\text{At state 10. }s_{10} > s_{\text{g at 40 bar}} (6.0701 kJ/kg · K)\text{ so state 10 lies in superheated region at 40 bar pressure.}\\\text{From steam table by interpolation, }T_{10} = 370.36ºC \text{ so, }h_{10} = 3141.81 kJ/kg\\\begin{aligned}\text{Let dryness fraction at state 9 be }x_{9}&\text{ so,}\\s_{9}&= 6.6582 = s_{\text{f at 4 bar}} + x_{9} · s_{\text{fg at 4 bar}}\\6.6582&= 1.7766 + x_{9} × 5.1193\\x_{9} &= 0.9536\\h_{9}&= h_{\text{f at 4 bar}} + x_{9} × h_{\text{fg at 4 bar}} = 604.74 + 0.9536 × 2133.8\\h_{9}&= 2639.53 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Assuming the state of fluid leaving open feed water heater to be saturated liquid at respective pressures i.e.}\\\begin{aligned}h_{11}&= h_{\text{f at 4 bar}} = 604.74 kJ/kg, v_{11} = 0.001084 m^{3}/kg = v_{\text{f at 4 bar}}\\h_{13}&= h_{\text{f at 40 bar}} = 1087.31 kJ/kg, v_{13} = 0.001252 m^{3}/kg = v_{\text{f at 40 bar}}\\\text{For process 4–8, i.e. in CEP.} \qquad \qquad &\\h_{8}&= h_{4} + v_{4} × (4 – 0.05) × 10^{2}\\&= 137.82 + (0.001005 × 3.95 × 10^{2})\\h_{8}&= 138.22 kJ/kg\\\text{For process 11-12, i.e. in }FP_{2},\qquad \qquad & \\h_{12}&= h_{11} + v_{11}(40 – 4) × 10^{2}\\&= 604.74 + (0.001084 × 36 × 10^{2})\\h_{12}&= 608.64 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{For process 13-1}^{\prime}\text{ i.e. in }FP_{1}, h^{\prime}_{1}= h_{13} + v_{13}(200 – 40) × 10^{2}\\\begin{aligned}&= 1087.31 + (0.001252 × 160 × 10^{2})\\h^{\prime}_{1}&= 1107.34 kJ/kg\\(m_{1} × 3141.81) + (1 – m_{1}) × 608.64&= 1087.31\\m_{1}&= 0.189 kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Applying energy balance an open feed water heater 1 (OFWH1)}\\\begin{aligned}(m_{1} × h_{10}) + (1 – m_{1}) × h_{12}&= 1 × h_{13}\\(m_{1} × 3141.81) + (1 – m_{1}) × 608.64&= 1087.31\\m_{1}&= 0.189 kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Applying energy balance an open feed water heater 2 (OFWH2)}\\\begin{aligned}m_{2} × h_{9} + (1 – m_{1} – m_{2}) h_{8}&= (1 – m_{1}) × h_{11}\\(m_{2} × 2639.53) + (1 – 0.189 – m_{2}) × 138.22&= (1 – 0.189) × 604.74, m_{2} = 0.151 kg\\\end{aligned}\\\text{Thermal efficiency of cycle,}\\η = \frac{\left\{(h_{2} – h_{10}) + (1 – m_{1})(h_{10} – h_{9}) + (1 – m_{1} – m_{2})(h_{9} – h_{3})\right\} – \left\{W_{CEP} + W_{FP_{1}} + W_{FP_{2}}\right\} }{(h_{2} – h_{1}^{\prime})}\\\begin{aligned}W_{CEP}&= (1 – m_{1} – m_{2}) (h_{8} – h_{4}) = 0.264 kJ/kg\text{ steam from boiler}\\W_{FP_{1}}&= (h_{1}^{\prime} – h_{13}) = 20.03 kJ/kg\text{ of steam from boiler}\\W_{FP_{2}}&= (1 – m_{1}) (h_{12} – h_{11}) = 3.16 kJ/kg\text{ of steam from boiler}\\W_{CEP} + W_{FP_{1}} + W_{FP_{2}}&= 23.454 kJ/kg\text{ of steam from boiler}\end{aligned}\\η = \frac{\left\{(3675.3 – 3141.81) + (1 – 0.189)(3141.81 – 2639.53) + (1 – 0.189 – 0.151)(2639.53 – 2030.73)\right\} – \left\{23.454\right\}}{(3675.3 – 1107.34)}\\\begin{aligned}&= \text{0.5137 or 51.37 }\%\\\text{Cycle thermal efficiency }η_{a}&= \textbf{46.18 }\pmb{\%}\\η_{b}&= \textbf{49.76 }\pmb{\%}\\η_{c}&= \textbf{51.37 }\pmb{\%}\end{aligned}\\\text{Hence it is obvious that efficiency increases with increase in number of feed heaters.}\)
