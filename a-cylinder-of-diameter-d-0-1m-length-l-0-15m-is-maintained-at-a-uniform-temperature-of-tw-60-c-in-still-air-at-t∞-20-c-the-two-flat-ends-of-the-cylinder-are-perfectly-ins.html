Guest Question.

A cylinder of diameter D = 0.1m, length L = 0.15m is maintained at a uniform temperature of \(T_{w}=60^{\circ} C\) in still air at \(T_{\infty}=20^{\circ} C\). The two flat ends of the cylinder are perfectly insulated. Compare the heat loss from the cylinder if its axis is (a) horizontal and (b) vertical.

Admin
Solution

We base the thermo-physical properties of air at \(T_{m}=\frac{T_{w}+T_{\infty}}{2}=\frac{60+20}{2}=40^{\circ} C =273 + 40 = 313 K\).

Kinematic viscosity: \(v_{m}=17.07 \times 10^{-6} m ^{2} / s\)

Thermal conductivity: \(k_{m}=0.0274 W / m ^{\circ} C\)

Prandtl number: \(P r_{m}=0.699\)

Isobaric expansion coefficient: \(\beta_{m}=\frac{1}{313}=0.0032 K ^{-1}\)

Case (a) Cylinder axis vertical

The characteristic dimension in this case is \(L_{c h}=L=0.15 m\). We have for the cylinder \(\frac{D}{L}=\frac{0.1}{0.15}=0.667\) . The Rayleigh number is

\(R a_{L}=\frac{9.81 \times 0.0032(60-20) 0.15^{3}}{\left(17.07 \times 10^{-6}\right)^{2}} 0.699=1.017 \times 10^{7}\)

The Grashof number is given by \(G r_{L}=\frac{R a_{L}}{P r_{m}}=\frac{1.017 \times 10^{7}}{0.699}=1.454 \times 10^{7}\) . We then have

\(\frac{35}{G r_{L}^{\frac{1}{4}}}=\frac{35}{\left(1.454 \times 10^{7}\right)^{\frac{1}{4}}}=0.567<0.667\)

Hence, the correlation for a vertical plate may be made use of. The flow is laminar and we make use of Eq. 16.51 to get

\(\overline{N u}_{L}=0.55 R a_{L}^{\frac{1}{4}}\) (16.51)

\(\overline{N u}_{L}=0.55 \times\left(1.017 \times 10^{7}\right)^{\frac{1}{4}}=31.06\)

The average heat transfer coefficient is given by

\(\bar{h}=\frac{31.06 \times 0.0274}{0.15}=5.67 W / m ^{2 \circ} C\)

The heat loss from the cylinder is then given by

\(Q_{\text {vert }}=\bar{h} \pi D L\left(T_{w}-T_{\infty}\right)=5.67 \times \pi \times 0.1 \times 0.15 \times 40=10.7 W\)

Case (b) Cylinder axis horizontal

The characteristic dimension in this case is \(L_{c h}=D=0.1m\). The Rayleigh number is

\(R a_{D}=\frac{9.81 \times 0.0032(60-20) 0.1^{3}}{\left(17.07 \times 10^{-6}\right)^{2}} 0.699=3.013 \times 10^{6}\)

The flow is laminar and we make use of Eq. 16.59 to get

\(\overline{N u}_{D}=0.53 R a_{D}^{\frac{1}{4}}, 10^{4}<R a_{D}<10^{9}\) (16.59)

\(\overline{N u}_{L}=0.53 \times\left(3.013 \times 10^{6}\right)^{\frac{1}{4}}=22.08\)

The average heat transfer coefficient is given by

\(\bar{h}=\frac{22.08 \times 0.0274}{0.1}=6.05 W / m ^{2{ }^{\circ} C }\)

The heat loss from the cylinder is then given by

\(Q_{h o r}=\bar{h} \pi D L\left(T_{w}-T_{\infty}\right)=6.05 \times \pi \times 0.1 \times 0.15 \times 40=11.4 W\)

The cylinder with its axis horizontal loses marginally more heat than when the cylinder axis is vertical.
