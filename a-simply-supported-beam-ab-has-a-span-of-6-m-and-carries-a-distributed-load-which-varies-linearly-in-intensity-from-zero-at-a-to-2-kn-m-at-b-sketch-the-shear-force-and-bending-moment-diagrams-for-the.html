Guest Question.

A simply supported beam AB has a span of 6 m and carries a distributed load which varies linearly in intensity from zero at A to 2 kN/m at B. Sketch the shear force and bending moment diagrams for the beam and insert the principal values.

Admin
Solution

The beam and its loading are shown in Fig. S.3.15(a)
Taking moments about B

\( R_{ A } \times 6-\frac{1}{2} \times 6 \times 2 \times 2=0 \)

from which

\( R_{ A }=2 kN \)

Shear force
The shear force at any section a distance x from A is given by

\( S_{ AB }=-2+\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) x w \)

where w=(l/3)x from similar triangles. Then

\( S_{ AB }=-2+\frac{x^{2}}{6} \) (i)

When \( x=0, S_{ AB }=-2 kN \text { and when } x=6 m , S_{ AB }=4 kN \). Examination of Eq. (i) shows that \( S_{ AB }=0 \text { when } x=3.46 m \text { and that }\left( d S_{ AB } / d x\right)=0 \text { when } x=0 \). The distribution is shown in Fig. S.3.15(b).

Bending moment
The bending moment is given by

\( M_{ AB }=2 x-\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) x w\left(\frac{x}{3}\right)=2 x-\frac{x^{3}}{18} \) (ii)

From Eq. (ii) \( M_{ AB }=0 \text { when } x=0 \text { and } x=6 m \text {. Also, from Eq. (i), } M_{ AB } \) is a maximum when x=3.46 m. Then, from Eq. (ii)

\( M_{ AB }(\max )=4.62 kNm .\)

and the distribution is as shown in Fig. S.3.15(c).
