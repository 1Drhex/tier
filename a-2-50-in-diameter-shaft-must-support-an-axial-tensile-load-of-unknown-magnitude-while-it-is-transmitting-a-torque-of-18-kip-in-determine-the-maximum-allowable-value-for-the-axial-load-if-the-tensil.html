Guest Question.

A 2.50-in.-diameter shaft must support an axial tensile load of unknown magnitude while it is transmitting a torque of 18 kip-in. Determine the maximum allowable value for the axial load if the tensile principal stress on the outside surface of the shaft must not exceed 10,000 psi.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(A=\frac{\pi}{4}(2.5 \text { in. })^{2}=4.908739 in .^{2} \quad J=\frac{\pi}{32}(2.5 in .)^{4}=3.834952 in .{ }^{4}\)

Shear stress magnitude:
The torque in the shaft is:

\(\tau=\frac{T c}{J}=\frac{(18 kip – in .)(2.5 in . / 2)}{3.834952 in .^{4}}=5,867.088 psi\)

(sense of shear stress cannot be established definitively from the information given)

Principal stress calculations:
The tensile principal stress must not exceed 10,000 psi; therefore:

\(\begin{gathered}\sigma_{p 1}=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2}+\sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}}{2}\right)^{2}+\tau_{x y}^{2}} \\10,000 psi \geq \frac{\sigma_{x}+0}{2}+\sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{x}-0}{2}\right)^{2}+(5,867.088 psi )^{2}}\end{gathered}\)

Rearrange and then square both sides of the equation to obtain:

\(\left(10,000 psi -\frac{\sigma_{x}}{2}\right)^{2} \geq\left(\frac{\sigma_{x}}{2}\right)^{2}+(5,867.088 psi )^{2}\)

and further reduce this equation to compute \(\sigma_{x}\):

\(\begin{aligned}&(10,000 psi )^{2}-(10,000 psi ) \sigma_{x}+\frac{\sigma_{x}^{2}}{4} \geq \frac{\sigma_{x}^{2}}{4}+(5,867.088 psi )^{2} \\&(10,000 psi )^{2}-(5,867.088 psi )^{2} \geq(10,000 psi ) \sigma_{x} \\&\therefore \sigma_{x} \leq \frac{65,577,277.560 psi ^{2}}{10,000 psi }=6,557.728 psi\end{aligned}\)

The axial force applied to the shaft must be limited to:

\(P \leq \sigma_{x} A=(6,557.728 psi )\left(4.908739 in .^{2}\right)=32,190 lb =32.2 kips\)
