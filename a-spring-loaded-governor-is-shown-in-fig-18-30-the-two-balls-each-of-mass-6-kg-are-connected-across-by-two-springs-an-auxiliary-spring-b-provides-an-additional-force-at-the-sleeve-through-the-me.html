Guest Question.

A spring loaded governor is shown in Fig. 18.30. The two balls, each of mass 6 kg, are connected across by two springs . An auxiliary spring B provides an additional force at the sleeve through the medium of a lever which pivots about a fixed centre at its left hand end. In the mean position, the radius of the governor balls is 120 mm and the speed is 600 r.p.m. The tension in each spring is then 1 kN. Find the tension in the spring B for this position.
When the sleeve moves up 15 mm, the speed is to be 630 r.p.m. Find the necessary stiffness of the spring B, if the stiffness of each spring A is 10 kN/m. Neglect the moment produced by the mass of the balls.

 

Admin
Solution

Given : m = 6 kg ; \(r=r_{1}=120 mm =0.12 m ; N=N_{1}=600 r.p.m . \text { or }\) \(\omega_{1}=2 \pi \times 600 / 60=62.84 rad / s\)

Tension in spring B

Let \(S_{ B 1}\) = Spring force or tension in spring B, and

M.g = Total load at the sleeve.

We know that centrifugal force at the minimum speed,

\(F_{ Cl }=m\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2} r_{1}=6(62.84)^{2} 0.12=2843 N\)

Since the tension in each spring A is 1 kN and there are two springs, therefore

Total spring force in spring A,

\(S_{ Al }=2 \times 1=2 kN =2000 N\)

Taking moments about the pivot P (neglecting the moment produced by the mass of balls) in order to find the force Mg on the sleeve, in the mean position as shown in Fig. (a),

\(F_{ Cl } \times 90=S_{ Al } \times 90+\frac{M \cdot g}{2} \times 90 \quad \text { or } \quad F_{ Cl }=S_{ Al }+\frac{M \cdot g}{2}\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(M g=2 F_{ Cl }-2 S_{ Al }=2 \times 2843-2 \times 2000=1686 N\)

Now taking moments about point Q,

\(S_{ B 1} \times 160=M . g .(80+160)=1686 \times 240=404640\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(S_{ B 1}=404640 / 160=2529 N\)

Stiffness of the spring B

Given : h = 15 mm = 0.015 m ; \(N_{2}=630 \text { r.p.m. } \quad \text { or } \quad \omega_{2}=2 \pi \times 630 / 60=66 rad / s\);\(S_{ A }=10 kN / m =10 \times 10^{3} N / m\)

Let \(S_{ B }\) = Stiffness of spring B.

The maximum position is shown in Fig. (b).

First of all, let us find the maximum radius of rotation \(\left(r_{2}\right)\) when the sleeve moves up by 0.015 m. We know that

\(h=\left(r_{2}-r_{1}\right) \frac{y}{x} \text { or } r_{2}=r_{1}+h \times \frac{x}{y}=0.12+0.015=0.135 m\) \(\ldots(\because x=y=90 mm =0.09 m )\)

\(\therefore \) Centrifugal force at the maximum speed,

\(F_{ C 2}=m\left(\omega_{2}\right)^{2} r_{2}=6(66)^{2} 0.135=3528 N\)

We know that extension of the spring A,

\(=2\left(r_{2}-r_{1}\right) \times \text { No. of springs }=2(0.135-0.12) 2=0.06 m\)

\(\therefore \) Total spring force in spring A,

\(S_{ A 2}=S_{ A 1}+\text { Extension of springs } \times \text { Stiffness of springs }\left(s_{ A }\right)\)

 

\(=2000+0.06 \times 10 \times 10^{3}=2600 N\)

Now taking moments about P, neglecting the obliquity of arms,

\(F_{ C 2} \times 90=S_{ A 2} \times 90+\frac{M \cdot g}{2} \times 90 \quad \text { or } \quad F_{ C 2}=S_{ A 2}+\frac{M \cdot g}{2}\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(M g=2 F_{ C 2}-2 S_{ A 2}=2 \times 3528-2 \times 2600=1856 N\)

Again taking moments about point Q (neglecting the moment produced by the mass of balls) in order to find the spring force \(\left(S_{ B 2}\right)\) when sleeve rises as shown in Fig.(b),

\(S_{ B 2} \times 160=M g(80+160)=1856 \times 240=445440\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(S_{ B 2}=445440 / 160=2784 N\)

When the sleeve rises 0.015 m, the extension in spring B

\(=0.015\left(\frac{160}{80+160}\right)=0.01 m\)

\(\therefore \) Stiffness of spring B,

\(s_{ B }=\frac{S_{ B 2}-S_{ B 1}}{\text { Extension of spring } B}=\frac{2784-2529}{0.01}=25500 N / m\)

= 25.5 N/mm
