Guest Question.

A rectangular channel 3 m wide is conveying water at a depth \(y_{1} = 1.55 m\), and velocity \(V_{1} = 1.83 m/s\). The flow enters a transition region as shown in Fig. E10.4, in which the bottom elevation is raised by h = 0.20 m. Determine the depth and velocity in the transition, and the value of h for choking to occur.

Admin
Solution

Use Eq. \(10.4 .4\) to find the specific discharge to be

\(q=\frac{Q}{b}=V y (10.4.4)\)

\(\begin{aligned}q &=V_{1} y_{1} \\&=1.83 \times 1.55=2.84 \mathrm{~m}^{2} / \mathrm{s}\end{aligned}\)

The Froude number at location 1 is

\(\begin{aligned}\mathrm{Fr} &=\frac{V_{1}}{\sqrt{g y_{1}}} \\&=\frac{1.83}{\sqrt{9.81 \times 1.55}}=0.47\end{aligned}\)

which is less than unity. Hence, the flow at location 1 is subcritical. The specific energy at location 1 is found, using Eq. 10.4.3, to be

\(E=y+\frac{V^{2}}{2 g} (10.4.3)\)

\(\begin{aligned}E_{1} &=y_{1}+\frac{V_{1}^{2}}{2 g} \\&=1.55+\frac{1.83^{2}}{2 \times 9.81}=1.72 \mathrm{~m}\end{aligned}\)

The specific energy at location 2 is found, using Eq. \(10.4 .16\), to be

\(E_{1} =E_{2} + h (10.4.16)\)

\(\begin{aligned}E_{2} &=E_{1}-h \\&=1.72-0.20=1.52 \mathrm{~m}\end{aligned}\)

If \(E_{2}>E_{c}\), it is possible to find the depth \(y_{2}\). Therefore, \(E_{c}\) is calculated first. From Eqs. \(10.4 .10\) and \(10.4 .11\) the critical conditions are

\(y=y_{c}=\left(\frac{q^{2}}{g}\right)^{1 / 3} (10.4.10)\)

\(E_{c}=\frac{3}{2} y_{c} (10.4.11)\)

\(\begin{array}{l}y_{c}=\left(\frac{q^{2}}{g}\right)^{1 / 3}=\left(\frac{2.84^{2}}{9.81}\right)^{1 / 3}=0.94 \mathrm{~m} \\\\E_{c}=\frac{3 y_{c}}{2}=3 \times \frac{0.94}{2}=1.41 \mathrm{~m}\end{array}\)

Hence, since \(E_{2}>E_{c}\), we can proceed with calculating \(y_{2}\). The depth \(y_{2}\) can be evaluated by substituting known values into Eq. 10.4.5:

\(E=y+\frac{q^{2}}{2 g y^{2}} (10.4.5)\)

\(1.52=y_{2}+\frac{2.84^{2}}{2 \times 9.81 \times y_{2}^{2}}\)

The solution is

\(\begin{aligned}y_{2} &=1.26 \mathrm{~m} \\\therefore V_{2} &=\frac{q}{y_{2}} \\&=\frac{2.84}{1.26}=2.25 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\end{aligned}\)

The flow at location 2 is subcritical since there is no way in which the flow can become supercritical in the transition with the given geometry.

\(\quad\quad\)The value of \(h\) for critical flow to appear at location 2 is determined by setting \(E_{2}=E_{c}\) in Eq. 10.4.16:

\(h=E_{1}-E_{c}=1.72-1.40=0.31 \mathrm{~m}\)
