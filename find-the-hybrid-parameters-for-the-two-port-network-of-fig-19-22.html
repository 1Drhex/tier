Guest Question.

Find the hybrid parameters for the two-port network of Fig. 19.22.

Admin
Solution

To find \( h_{11} \) and \( h_{21} \), we short-circuit the output port and connect a current \( I_1 \) source to the input port as shown in Fig. 19.23(a). From Fig. 19.23(a),

\( V_1 = I_1(2+3\parallel 6) = 4I_1 \)

Hence,

\( h_{11} = \frac{V_1}{I_1} = 4Î©\)

Also, from Fig. 19.23(a) we obtain, by current division,

\( -I_2=\frac{6}{6+3} I_1 = \frac{2}{3} I_1 \)

Hence,

\( h_{21} = \frac{I_2}{I_1} = -\frac{2}{3} \)

To obtain \( h_{12} \) and \( h_{22} \), we open-circuit the input port and connect a voltage source \( V_2 \) to the output port as in Fig. 19.23(b). By voltage division,

\( V_1 = \frac{6}{6+3} V_2 = \frac{2}{3} V_2 \)

Hence,

\( h_{12} = \frac{V_1}{V_2} = \frac{2}{3} \)

Also,

\( V_2 = (3 + 6)I_2 = 9I_2 \)

\( h_{22} = \frac{I_2}{V_2} = \frac{1}{9} \) S.
