Guest Question.

Consider the Diels–Alder reaction between 1,4-butadiene (B) and methyl acrylate (M) to form an adduct (C):

\(B+M\rightarrow C\)

If the operating conditions used in Illustration 8.3 are again employed, determine the volume of a single continuous flow stirred-tank reactor which will give 40% conversion of butadiene when the liquid flow rate is 0.500 \(m^{3}\)/Ks.

Admin
Solution

For the conditions cited the reaction rate expression is of the form

 

\(-r_{B}=kC_{B0}(1-f_{B})C_{AlCl_{3}\cdot 0}\) (A)

 

with

 

\(k=1.15\times 10^{-3}\, m^{3}/(mol\cdot Ks)\)

 

\(C_{B0}=96.5 mol/m_{3}\)

 

\(C_{AlCl_{3}\cdot 0}=6.63 mol/m_{3}\)

 

Equation (8.3.6) may be used as the basic design relation.

 

\(\tau =\frac{C_{B0}\int_{0}^{f_{B}}df_{B}}{(-r_{BF})}=\frac{C_{B0}f_{B}}{(-r_{BF})}\) (B)

 

Combination of equations (A) and (B) gives

 

\(\tau =\frac{C_{B0}f_{B}}{kC_{B0}(1-f_{B})C_{AlCl_{3}\cdot 0}}=\frac{f_{B}}{k(1-f_{B})C_{AlCl_{3}\cdot 0}}\)

 

Substitution of numerical values gives

 

\(\tau =\frac{0.40}{(1.15\times 10^{-3})(1-0.4)(6.63)}=87.4 ks = 24.3\) h

 

From the definition of the space time and the inlet volumetric flow rate,

 

\(V_{R}=\tau \nu _{0}=87.4(0.500)=43.7\, m^{3}\)

 

This volume is appreciably larger than the volume of the plug flow reactor determined in Illustration 8.3 for the same reaction conditions and fraction conversion. However, the cost of such a reactor would be considerably less than the cost of a tubular reactor of the size determined in Illustration 8.3.
