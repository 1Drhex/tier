Guest Question.

Two gear wheels mesh externally and are to give a velocity ratio of 3 to 1.The teeth are of involute form ; module = 6 mm, addendum = one module, pressure angle = \(20^{\circ}\). The pinion rotates at 90 r.p.m. Determine : 1. The number of teeth on the pinion to avoid interference on it and the corresponding number of teeth on the wheel, 2. The length of path and arc of contact, 3.The number of pairs of teeth in contact, and 4. The maximum velocity of sliding.

Admin
Solution

Given : G = T / t = 3 ; m = 6 mm ; \(A_{ P }=A_{ W }=1 \text { module }=6 mm ; \phi=20^{\circ}\) ; \(N_{1}=90 \text { r.p.m. or } \omega_{1}=2 \pi \times 90 / 60=9.43 rad / s\)

 

1. Number of teeth on the pinion to avoid interference on it and the corresponding number of teeth on the wheel

We know that number of teeth on the pinion to avoid interference,

\(t=\frac{2 A_{ p }}{\sqrt{1+G(G+2) \sin ^{2} \phi}-1}=\frac{2 \times 6}{\sqrt{1+3(3+2) \sin ^{2} 20^{\circ}}-1}\)

 

= 18.2 say 19

and corresponding number of teeth on the wheel,

T = G.t = 3 × 19 = 57

 

2. Length of path and arc of contact

We know that pitch circle radius of pinion,

r = m.t / 2 = 6 × 19/2 = 57 mm

\(\therefore \) Radius of addendum circle of pinion,

\(r_{ A }=r+\text { Addendum on pinion }\left(A_{ p }\right)=57+6=63 mm\)

and pitch circle radius of wheel,

R = m.T / 2 = 6 × 57 / 2 = 171 mm

\(\therefore \) Radius of addendum circle of wheel,

\(R_{ A }=R+\text { Addendum on wheel }\left(A_{ W }\right)=171+6=177 mm\)

We know that the path of approach (i.e. path of contact when engagement occurs),

\(K P=\sqrt{\left(R_{ A }\right)^{2}-R^{2} \cos ^{2} \phi}-R \sin \phi\) …(Refer Fig. 12.11)

 

\(=\sqrt{(177)^{2}-(171)^{2} \cos ^{2} 20^{\circ}}-171 \sin 20^{\circ}=74.2-58.5=15.7 mm\)

and the path of recess (i.e. path of contact when disengagement occurs),

\(P L=\sqrt{\left(r_{ A }\right)^{2}-r^{2} \cos ^{2} \phi}-r \sin \phi\)

 

\(=\sqrt{(63)^{2}-(57)^{2} \cos ^{2} 20^{\circ}}-57 \sin 20^{\circ}=33.17-19.5=13.67 mm\)

\(\therefore \) Length of path of contact,

\(K L=K P+P L=15.7+13.67=29.37 mm\)

We know that length of arc of contact

\(=\frac{\text { Length of path of contact }}{\cos \phi}=\frac{29.37}{\cos 20^{\circ}}=31.25 mm\)

 

3. Number of pairs of teeth in contact

We know that circular pitch,

\(p_{c}=\pi \times m=\pi \times 6=18.852 mm\)

\(\therefore \) Number of pairs of teeth in contact

\(=\frac{\text { Length of arc of contact }}{p_{c}}=\frac{31.25}{18.852}=1.66 \text { say } 2\)

 

4. Maximum velocity of sliding

Let \(\omega_{2}\) = Angular speed of wheel in rad/s.

We know that \(\frac{\omega_{1}}{\omega_{2}}=\frac{T}{t} \text { or } \omega_{2}=\omega_{1} \times \frac{t}{T}=9.43 \times \frac{19}{57}=3.14 rad / s\)

\(\therefore \) Maximum velocity of sliding,

\(v_{ S }=\left(\omega_{1}+\omega_{2}\right) K P\) \(\ldots(\because K P>P L)\)

 

\(=(9.43+3.14) 15.7=197.35 mm / s\)
