Guest Question.

In a spring-controlled governor, the curve of controlling force is a straight line. When balls are 400 mm apart, the controlling force is 1200 N, and when 200 mm apart, 450 N. At what speed will the governor run when the balls are 250 mm apart. What initial tension on the spring would be required for isochronism and what would then be the speed? The weight of each ball is 100 N.

Admin
Solution

Given: \( F_{c 1}=1200 N , r_{1}=400 mm \).

\( F_{c 2}=450 N , r_{2}=200 mm \).

w=100 N.

For the stability of the spring controlled governor, we have

\( F_{c}=a r-b \).

\( 1200=0.2 a-b \).

\( 450=0.1 a-b \).

Solving for a and b, we get

a=7500, b=300.

\( F_{c}=7500 r-300 \).

\( \text { For } r=125 mm , F_{c}=7500 \times 0.125-300=638.5 N \).

\( F_{c}=(w / g)(2 \pi N / 60)^{2} r \).

\( 638.5=(100 / 9.81)(2 \pi \times N / 60)^{2} \times 0.125 \).

N=213.6 rpm.

For an isochronous governor, b=0. Therefore, the initial tension required is 300 N.

\( F_{c}=a r \).

\( (w / g) \omega^{2} r=a r \).

or \( \omega^{2}=a g / w =7500 \times 9.81 / 100 \).

= 735.75.

\( \omega=28.125 rad / s \).

N=259 rpm.
