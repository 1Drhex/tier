Guest Question.

Retaining Wall with Footing

Given: The walls defined in Figs. E8.3a, E8.3b, and E8.3c. These schemes are modified versions of the wall analyzed in . We have extended the footing to further stabilize the wall.

Determine: The factor of safety against sliding; the factor of safety against overturning; the base pressure distribution. Use the Rankine theory for soil pressure computations. Neglect the passive pressure acting on the toe.

Admin
Solution

Case “A”: We work with the free body diagram shown in Fig. E8.3d. The vertical surface is taken to pass through the heel.

 

From :

 

\(W_{1}1.5\, kip\; \; \; W_{2}=2.25\, kip\; \; \; P_{a}=2.88\, kip\; \; \; M_{B_{overturning}}=11.52\) kip ft

 

The weight of the footing is

 

\(W_{3}=(0.150)(7)(2)(1\, ft)=2.1\) kip

 

The weight of soil is \(W_{4}=(0.120)(10)(3)(1\, ft)=3.6\) kip

 

Then

 

\(N=\sum W_{i}=1.5+2.25+2.1+3.6=9.45\) kip

 

\(W_{4}+W_{3}+W_{2}+W_{1}\)

 

\(F_{max}=\mu N=0.577(9.45)=5.45\) kip

 

\(F.S._{sliding}=\frac{F_{max}}{P_{a}}=\frac{5.45}{2.88}=1.89\)

 

We sum moments about the toe:

 

\(M_{B_{resisting}}=W_{1}(3.5)+W_{2}(2)+W_{3}(3.5)+W_{4}(5.5)\)

 

\(=1.5(3.5)+2.25(2)+2.1(3.5)+3.6(5.5)=36.69\) kip ft

 

\(M_{B_{overturning}}=11.52\) kip ft

 

Using these moments, the factor of safety is

 

\(F.S._{overturning}=\frac{M_{B_{resisting}}}{M_{B_{overturning}}}=\frac{36.9}{11.52}=3.2\)

 

Next, we determine the line of action of the resultant

 

\(M_{net}=M_{B_{overturning}}-M_{B_{resisting}}=25.38\) kip ft

 

\(\bar{x}=\frac{M_{net}}{N}=\frac{25.38}{9.45}=2.68\) ft

 

\(e=\frac{L}{2}-\bar{x}=3.5-2.68=0.82\, ft< \frac{L}{6}=1.167\) ft

 

Lastly, we compute the pressure loading acting on the base.

 

\(q=\frac{N}{L}\left ( 1\pm \frac{6e}{L} \right )=\frac{9.45}{7}\left ( 1\pm \frac{6(0.82)}{7} \right )\Rightarrow q_{1}=2.3\, kip/ft^{2},q_{2}=0.4\, kip/ft^{2}\)

 

Case “B”: For this case, we work with the free body diagram shown in Fig. E8.3e. The dimensions are defined in Fig. E8.3b. \(W_{3}=(0.150)(6)(2)(1\, ft)=1.8 kip. W_{5}=(0.120)(2)(2)(1\, ft)=0.48\) kip

 

The calculations proceed as follows:

 

\(N=W_{1}+W_{2}+W_{3}+W_{5}=1.5+2.25+1.8+0.48=6.03\) kip

 

\(F_{max}=\mu N=0.577(6.03)=3.48\) kip

 

\(F.S._{sliding}=\frac{F_{max}}{P_{a}}=\frac{3.48}{2.88}=1.2\)

 

We sum moments about the toe:

 

\(M_{B_{resisting}}=W_{1}(5.5)+W_{2}(4)+W_{3}(3)+W_{5}(1)\)

 

\(=1.5(5.5)+2.25(4)+1.8(3)+0.48(1)=23.13\) kip ft

 

\(M_{B_{overturning}}=11.52\) kip ft

 

\(F.S._{overturning}=\frac{M_{B_{resisting}}}{M_{B_{overturning}}}=\frac{23.13}{11.52}=2.0\)

 

\(M_{net}=M_{B_{overturning}}-M_{B_{resisting}}=11.61\) kip ft

 

\(\bar{x}=\frac{M_{net}}{N}=\frac{11.61}{6.03}=1.925\) ft

 

\(e=\frac{L}{2}-\bar{x}=3-1.925=1.07\, ft> \frac{L}{6}=1.0\, ft\; \; \; \therefore \bar{x}=\frac{a}{2}\; \; a=5.77\) ft

 

\(q_{1}=\frac{2N}{a}=\frac{2(6.03)}{5.77}=2.1\, kip/ft^{2}\)

 

Note that the line of action of the normal force is within the base but the pressure is negative at the heel.

 

Case “C”: We work with the free body diagram shown in Fig. E8.3f. The dimensions are defined in Fig. E8.3c. The revised value of \(W_{3} is W_{3}=(0.15)(9)(2)(1\, ft)=2.7\) kip

 

Then

 

\(N=W_{1}+W_{2}+W_{3}+W_{4}+W_{5}=1.5+2.25+2.7+3.6+0.48=10.53\) kip

 

\(F_{max}=\mu N=0.577(10.53)=6.1\) kip

 

\(F.S._{sliding}=\frac{F_{max}}{P_{a}}=\frac{6.1}{2.88}=2.12\)

 

We sum moments about the toe:

 

\(M_{B_{balancing}}=W_{1}(5.5)+W_{2}(4)+W_{3}(4.5)+W_{4}(7.5)+W_{5}(1)\)

 

\(=1.5(5.5)+2.25(4)+2.7(4.5)+3.6(7.5)+0.48(1)=56.88\) kip ft

 

\(F.S._{overturning}=\frac{M_{B_{resisting}}}{M_{B_{overturning}}}=\frac{56.88}{11.52}=4.94\)

 

\(M_{net}=M_{B_{overturning}}-M_{B_{resisting}}=45.36\) kip ft

 

\(\bar{x}=\frac{M_{net}}{N}=\frac{45.36}{10.53}=4.3\) ft

 

\(e=\frac{L}{2}-\bar{x}=4.5-4.3=0.2\) ft

 

\(\left | e \right |< L/6=1.5\) ft

 

\(\therefore q=\frac{N}{L}\left ( 1\pm \frac{6e}{L} \right )=\frac{10.53}{9}\left ( 1\pm \frac{6(0.2)}{9} \right )\Rightarrow q_{1}=1.3\, kip/ft^{2},q_{2}=1.0\, kip/ft^{2}\)

 

We point out that case C has the lowest peak pressure. The analysis results are summarized in the table below.
