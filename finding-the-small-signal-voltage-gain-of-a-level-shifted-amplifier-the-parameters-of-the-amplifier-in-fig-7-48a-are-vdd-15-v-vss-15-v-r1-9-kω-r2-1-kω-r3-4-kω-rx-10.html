Guest Question.

Finding the small-signal voltage gain of a level-shifted amplifier The parameters of the amplifier in Fig. 7.48(a) are \( V_{ DD }=15 V ,-V_{ SS }=-15 V , R_{1}=9 k \Omega, R_{2}=1 k \Omega, R_{3}=4 k \Omega, R_{ x }=10 k \Omega \), and \( R_{ y }=6.5 k \Omega \) . The circuit is biased at a DC gate-source voltage of \( V_{ G }=1 V \). The MOS parameters are , \( V_{ t }=1 V \), \( K_{ n }=3.25 mA / V ^{2}, K_{ p }=6.5 mA / V ^{2} \text { for } W = L , \text { and }\left|V_{ M }\right|=1 / \lambda=100 V \).

(a) Find the small-signal voltage \( A_{ vo }\) and the maximum possible gain.

(b) Use SPICE to plot the small-signal output voltage for a sinusoidal input signal of 1 mV at 1 kHz.

Admin
Solution

From Eq. (8.92),

 

\( A_{ vo }=\frac{v_{ o }}{v_{ b }}=\frac{-g_{ m } r_{\pi} R_{ C }}{r_{\pi}+\left(1+g_{ m } r_{\pi}\right) R_{ E 1}}=\frac{-\beta_{ f } R_{ C }}{r_{\pi}+\left(1+\beta_{ f }\right) R_{ E 1}} \)

(8.92)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&V_{ G }+V_{ SS }-V_{ GS 1}-V_{ GS 1}-\left(R_{1}+R_{2}\right) K_{ n }\left(V_{ GS 1}-V_{ t }\right)^{2}=0 \text { or } \\&1+12-V_{ GS 1}-V_{ GS 1}-(1 k +9 k ) \times 3.25 \times 10^{-3} \times\left(V_{ GS 1}-1\right)^{2}=0\end{aligned}\)

 

which gives

 

\(V_{ GS }=1.552 V\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&I_{ D 1}=K_{ n }\left(V_{ GS 1}-V_{ t }\right)^{2}=3.25 m \times(1.552-1)^{2}=990 \mu A \\&V_{ GS 4}=\sqrt{2 I_{ D 1} / K_{ n }}+V_{ t }=\sqrt{2 \times 990 \mu / 3.25 m }+1=1.784 V\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&g_{ m 2}=g_{ m 3}=g_{ m 5}=g_{ m 6}=g_{ m 7}=2 K_{ n }\left(V_{ GS 1}-V_{ t }\right)=2 \times 3.25 m \times(1.552-1)=3.606 mA / V \\&g_{ m 4}=2 K_{ n }\left(V_{ GS 4}-V_{ t }\right)=2 \times 3.25 m \times(1.784-1)=5.099 mA / V\end{aligned}\)

 

\(r_{ o 7}=\frac{V_{ M }}{I_{ D 7}}=\frac{100}{990 \mu}=100 k \Omega\)

 

Substituting the values in Eq. (7.103), we get

 

\( A_{ Vx }=\frac{v_{ x }}{v_{ g }}=\frac{\left(R_{3}+1 / g_{ m 4}\right) R_{ x } g_{ m 1}}{\left(R_{3}+1 / g_{ m 4}+1 / g_{ m 5}\right)\left[1+\left(R_{2}+1 / g_{ m 2}\right) g_{ m 1}\right]} \)

(7.103)

 

\(\begin{aligned}A_{ vx } &=\frac{\left(R_{3}+1 / g_{ m 4}\right) R_{ x } g_{ m 1}}{\left(R_{3}+1 / g_{ m 4}+1 / g_{ m 5}\right)\left[1+\left(R_{2}+1 / g_{ m 2}\right) g_{ m 1}\right]} \\&=\frac{(10 k +1 / 5.099 m ) \times 10 k \times 3.606 m }{(10 k +1 / 5.099 m +1 / 3.606 m )[1+(1 k +1 / 3.606 m ) \times 3.606 m ]}=6.262 V / V\end{aligned}\)

 

Substituting the values in Eq. (7.104), we get

 

\(A_{ vo }=\frac{g_{ m 7}\left(r_{ o 7} \| r_{ o 8}\right)}{1+g_{ m 7}\left(r_{ o 7} \| r_{ o 8}\right)} A_{ vx }=\frac{\left(R_{3}+1 / g_{ m 4}\right) R_{ x } g_{ m 1}}{\left(R_{3}+1 / g_{ m 4}+1 / g_{ m 5}\right)\left[1+\left(R_{2}+1 / g_{ m 2}\right) g_{ m 1}\right]}\)\(\times \frac{g_{ m 7}\left(r_{ o 7} \| r_{ o 8}\right)}{1+g_{ m 7}\left(r_{ o 7} \| r_{ o 8}\right)}\)

(7.104)

 

\( A_{ vo }=\frac{g_{ m 7}\left(r_{ o 7} \| r_{ o 8}\right)}{1+g_{ m 7}\left(r_{ o 7} \| r_{ o 8}\right)} A_{ vx }=\frac{3.606 m \times(100 k \| 100 k )}{1+3.606 m \times(100 k \| 100 k )} \times 6.262=6.227 V / V \)

 

Substituting the values for \( R_{2}=0 \) in Eq. (7.104) gives \( A_{ vo (\max )}=17.454 V / V .\)
