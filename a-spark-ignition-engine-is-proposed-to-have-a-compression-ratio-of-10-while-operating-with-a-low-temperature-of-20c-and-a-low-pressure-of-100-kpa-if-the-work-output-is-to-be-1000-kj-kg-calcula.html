Guest Question.

A spark-ignition engine is proposed to have a compression ratio of 10 while operating with a low temperature of 20°C and a low pressure of 100 kPa. If the work output is to be 1000 kJ/kg, calculate the maximum possible thermal efficiency and compare with that of a Carnot cycle. Also calculate the MEP.

Admin
Solution

The Otto cycle provides the model for this engine. The maximum possible thermal efficiency for the engine would be

\( \eta=1-\frac{1}{r^{k-1}}=1-\frac{1}{(10)^{0.4}}=0.602 \quad \text { or } \quad 60.2 \% \).

Since process 1 → 2 is isentropic, we find that

\( T_{2}=T_{1}\left(\frac{v_{1}}{v_{2}}\right)^{k-1}=(293)(10)^{0.4}=736 K \).

The net work for the cycle is given by

\( w_{\text {net }}=w_{1-2}+\cancel {w}_{2-3}^{0}+w_{3-4}+\cancel {w}_{4-1}^{0}=c_{v}\left(T_{1}-T_{2}\right)+c_{v}\left(T_{3}-T_{4}\right) \quad \text { or } \quad 1000=(0.717)\left(293-136+T_{3}-T_{4}\right) \).

But, for the isentropic process 3 → 4,

\( T_{3}=T_{4}\left(\frac{v_{4}}{v_{3}}\right)^{k-1}=\left(T_{4}\right)(10)^{0.4}=2.512 T_{4} \).

\( \text { Solving the last two equations simultaneously, we find } T_{3}=3054 K \text { and } T_{4}=1216 K \text {, so that } \)

\( \eta_{\text {carnot }}=1-\frac{T_{L}}{T_{H}}=1-\frac{293}{3054}=0.904 \quad \text { or } \quad 90.4 \% \).

The Otto cycle efficiency is less than that of a Carnot cycle operating between the limiting temperatures because the heat transfer processes in the Otto cycle are not reversible.
The MEP is found by using the equation

\( w_{\text {net }}=( MEP )\left(v_{1}-v_{2}\right) \).

We have

\( v_{1} \frac{R T_{1}}{P_{1}}=\frac{(0.287)(293)}{100}=0.841 m ^{3} / kg \quad \text { and } \quad v_{2}=\frac{v_{1}}{10} \).

Thus

\( MEP =\frac{w_{\text {net }}}{v_{1}-v_{2}}=\frac{1000}{(0.9)(0.841)}=1321 kPa \).
