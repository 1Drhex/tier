Guest Question.

For the double long shoe external drum brake illustrated in Figure 10.25 determine the limiting force on the lever such that the maximum pressure on the brake lining does not exceed 1.4 MPa and determine the torque capacity of the brake. The face width of the shoes is 30 mm and the coefficient of friction between the shoes and the drum can be taken as 0.28.

Admin
Solution

First it is necessary to calculate values for \( \theta _{1} \) and \( \theta _{2} \) as these are not indicated directly on the diagram.

\(\theta _{1}=20°-\tan ^{-1} \frac{20}{120}=10.54° \)

 

\(\theta _{2}=20°+130°-\tan ^{-1} \frac{20}{120}=140.5°\)

The maximum value of \( \sin \theta \) would be \( \sin 90=1 \)
The distance between the pivot and the drum centre is
\(b = \sqrt{0.02^{2}+0.12^{2} } =0.217 \)m

The normal moment is given by

\( M_{n} =\frac{wrbp_{\max} }{(\sin \theta )_{\max}} \times \frac{1}{2}(\theta _{2}-\theta _{1})-\frac{1}{4}(\sin 2\theta _{2} -\sin 2\theta _{1} ) \)

 

\( = \frac{0.03 \times 0.1\times 0.1217 \times 1.4\times 10^{6} }{\sin 90}\times \frac{1}{2}\downarrow 140.5 -10.54\rightarrow \times \frac{2\pi }{360}-\frac{1}{4}(\sin 281 -\sin 21.08) =751.1 \) Nm

\( M_{f}=\frac{\mu wrp_{\max} }{(\sin \theta )_{\max}} r(\cos \theta _{1}- \cos \theta _{2})+\frac{b}{4}(\cos 2\theta _{2}-\cos 2\theta _{1}) \)

\( =\frac{0.28\times 0.03\times 0.1\times 1.4\times 10^{6} }{\sin 90} \times 0.1(\cos 10.54-\cos 140.5)+\frac{0.1217}{4}(\cos 281-\cos 21.08)=179.8 \) Nm

The orthogonal distance between the actuation force and the pivot is a = 0.12 +0.115 +0.05 = 0.285 m.

The actuation load on the left-hand shoe is given by

\( F_{a left shoe}=\frac{M_{n}-M_{f} }{a} =\frac{751.1-179.8}{0.285}=2004 \) N

The torque contribution from the left-hand shoe is given by

\( T_{left shoe}=\mu wr^{2}\frac{p_{\max} }{(\sin \theta )_{\max}} (\cos \theta _{1}-\cos \theta _{2} ) \)

 

\( =0.28\times 0.03\times 0.1^{2}\times 1.4 \times 10^{6}(\cos 10.54-\cos 140.5) =206.4\)Nm .

The actuation force on the right-hand shoe can be determined by considering each member of the lever mechanism as a free body (see Figure 10.26).

\( F-A_{V}+B_{V}=0 \)

 

\( A_{H}=B_{H} \)

 

\( B_{H}=C_{H} \)

 

\( A_{H}=C_{H} \)

\(0.2 F=0.05 B_{H} , F= B_{H} /4\) ;

\( B_{H} =2004 N; F=2004/4=501 N\).

So the limiting lever force is F=501 N.

\( C_{V}=0 \) , \( B_{V}=0 \) .

The actuating force for the right-hand lever is the resultant of F and \( B_{H} \) . The resultant angle is given by \( \tan^{-1}(0.05/0.2)=14.04ْ \)
\( F_{a right shoe}=\frac{2004}{\cos 14.04} \)=2065 N

The orthogonal distance between the actuation force vector and the pivot (Figure 10.27) is given by

\( a= (0.235-0.01969 \tan 14.04)\times \cos 14.04=0.2232 \) m

The normal and frictional moments for the right-hand shoe can be determined using Eqs 10.47 and 10.48.

\( M^{′}_{n}=\frac{M_{n}p^{′}_{\max} }{p_{\max} } =\frac{751.1 p^{′}_{\max} }{1.4\times 10^{6} } \)

 

\(M^{′}_{f}=\frac{M_{f}p^{′}_{\max} }{p_{\max} } =\frac{179.8 p^{′}_{\max} }{1.4\times 10^{6} } \)

For the right-hand shoe, the maximum pressure can be determined from

\(F_{a right shoe}=\frac{M^{′}_{n}+M^{′}_{f}}{a}=2065 \)

 

\(=\frac{751.1 p^{′}_{\max}-179.8 p^{′}_{\max}}{1.4\times 10^{6}\times 0.2232} \)

 

\(p^{′}_{\max}=1.130\times 10^{6} N/m^{2} \)

The torque contribution from the right-hand shoe is

\( T_{right shoe}=\mu wr^{2}\frac{p^{′}_{\max}}{(\sin \theta )_{\max}}(\cos \theta _{1}-\cos \theta _{2} ) =0.28\times 0.03\times 0.1^{2}\times 1.13\times 10^{6}\times (\cos 10.54 -\cos 140.5 ) \) =166.6 Nm.

The total torque is given by

\(T_{total}=T_{left shoe} +T_{right shoe} \)

=206.4+166.6=373 Nm.
