Guest Question.

In Figure P17.92/93, use Castigliano’s second theorem to find the horizontal displacement of joint D for the truss. The assumption is that each member has a cross-sectional area of A = 2.25 \(\text { in. }{ }^{2}\) and an elastic modulus of E = 29,000 ksi and that the loads acting on the truss are P = 13 kips and Q = 25 kips.

Admin
Solution

Truss Reaction Forces

\(A_{x}=Q \leftarrow \quad A_{y}=Q+4.3333 \text { kips } \downarrow \quad C_{y}=Q+30.3333 \text { kips } \uparrow\)

 

\(\begin{array}{cc|cccc}\hline \text { Member } & F & \frac{\partial F}{\partial Q} & F \text { (for } Q=25 \text { kips }) & L & \left(\frac{\partial F}{\partial Q}\right) F L \\& \text { (kips) } & & \text { (kips) } & \text { (in.) } & \text { (kip-in.) } \\\hline A B & 1.2019 Q+5.2080 & 1.2019 & 35.2543 & 259.5997 & 10,999.7953 \\A C & 0.3333 Q-2.8889 & 0.3333 & 5.4444 & 216.0000 & 391.9576 \\B C & -1.0541 Q-18.2709 & -1.0541 & -44.6233 & 227.6840 & 10,709.6677 \\B D & Q+8.6667 & 1.0000 & 33.6667 & 216.0000 & 7,272.0072 \\C D & -15.6241 & 0.0000 & -15.6241 & 259.5997 & 0.0000 \\\hline & &&& \sum\left(\frac{\partial F}{\partial Q}\right) F L= & 29,373.4278 \\\hline\end{array}\)

 

Apply Equation (17.39)

\(\Delta=\frac{1}{A E} \sum\left(\frac{\partial F}{\partial Q}\right) F L\)

to calculate the deflection from the tabulated results:

\(\Delta_{D}=\frac{(29,373.4278 \text { kip-in. })}{\left(2.25 in .^{2}\right)(29,000 ksi )}=0.450 \text { in. } \rightarrow\)

Since the external load was applied in the +x direction at D, the positive value of the result confirms that joint D does displace to the right.
