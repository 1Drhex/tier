Guest Question.

A new process for treatment of a special material is to be evaluated. The material, a sphere of radius \(r_{o} = 5 mm\), is initially in equilibrium at \(400^{\circ}C\) in a furnace. It is suddenly removed from the furnace and subjected to a two-step cooling process.

Step 1 Cooling in air at \(20^{\circ}C\) for a period of time \(t_{a}\) until the center temperature reaches a critical value, \(T_{a}(0,t_{a})=335^{\circ}C\). For this situation, the convection heat transfer coefficient is \(h_{a}=10W/m^{2}\cdot K\).

After the sphere has reached this critical temperature, the second step is initiated.

Step 2 Cooling in a well-stirred water bath at \(20^{\circ}C\), with a convection heat transfer coefficient of \(h_{w}=6000W/m^{2}\cdot K\).

The thermophysical properties of the material are \(\rho =3000 kg/m^{3}, k=20/m\cdot K, c=1000J/kg\cdot K\) and \(\alpha =6.66\times 10^{-6}m^{2}/s\).

1. Calculate the time \(t_{a}\) required for step 1 of the cooling process to be completed.

2. Calculate the time \(t_{w}\) required during step 2 of the process for the center of the sphere to cool from \(335^{\circ}C\) (the condition at the completion of step 1) to \(50^{\circ}C\).

Admin
Solution
Known: Temperature requirements for cooling a sphere.

Find:
1. Time \(t_{a}\) required to accomplish desired cooling in air.
2. Time \(t_{w}\) required to complete cooling in water bath.

Assumptions:
1. One-dimensional conduction in r.
2. Constant properties.

Analysis:
1. To determine whether the lumped capacitance method can be used, the Biot number is calculated. From Equation 5.10, with \(L_{c}=r_{o}/3\),

 

\(Bi=\frac{hL_{c}}{k}< 0.1\) (5.10)

 

\(Bi=\frac{h_{a}r_{o}}{3k}=\frac{10W/m^{2}\cdot K\times 0.005m}{3\times 20W/m\cdot K}=8.33\times 10^{-4}\)

 

Accordingly, the lumped capacitance method may be used, and the temperature is nearly uniform throughout the sphere. From Equation 5.5 it follows that

\(\frac{\rho Vc}{hA_{s}}\ln \frac{\theta i}{\theta }=t\) (5.5)

 

\(t_{a}=\frac{\rho Vc}{h_aA_{s}}\ln \frac{\theta_i}{\theta_a }=\frac{\rho r_{o}c}{3h_{a}}\ln \frac{T_{i}-T_{\infty }}{T_{a}-T_{\infty }}\)

 

where \(V=(4/3)\pi r_{o}^{3}\) and \(A_{s}=4\pi r_{o}^{2}\). Hence

 

\(t_{a}=\frac{3000kg/m^{3}\times 0.005m\times 1000J/kg\cdot K}{3\times 10W/m^{2}\cdot K}\ln \frac{400-20}{335-20}=94 s\)

 

2. To determine whether the lumped capacitance method may also be used for the second step of the cooling process, the Biot number is again calculated. In this case

 

\(Bi=\frac{h_{w}r_{o}}{3k}=\frac{6000W/m^{2}\cdot K\times 0.005m}{3\times 20W/m\cdot K}=0.50\)

 

and the lumped capacitance method is not appropriate. However, to an excellent approximation, the temperature of the sphere is uniform at \(t=t_{a}\), and, as will become evident, the one-term approximation may be used for the calculations. The time \(t_{w}\) at which the center temperature reaches \(50^{\circ}C\),that is,\(T(0, t_{w})=50^{\circ}C\), can be obtained by rearranging Equation 5.53c

 

\(\theta _{o}^{\ast }=C_{1}\exp (-\zeta _{1}^{2}Fo)\) (5.53c)

 

\(Fo=-\frac{1}{\zeta _{1}^{2}}\ln \left [ \frac{\theta _{o}^{\ast }}{C_{1}} \right ]= -\frac{1}{\zeta _{1}^{2}} \ln \left [ \frac{1}{C_{1}}\times \frac{T(0,t_{w})-T_{\infty }}{T_{i}-T_{\infty }} \right ]\)

 

where \(t_{w}=F_o r_{o}^{2}/\alpha \). With the Biot number now defined as

 

\(Bi=\frac{h_{w}r_{o}}{k}=\frac{6000W/m^{2}\cdot K\times 0.005m}{20W/m\cdot K}=1.50\)

 

Table 5.1 yields \(C_{1}=1.376\) and \(\zeta _{1}=1.800 rad\). It follows that

\(Fo=-\frac{1}{(1.800 rad )^{2}}\ln \left [ \frac{1}{1.376} \times \frac{(50-20)^{\circ}C}{(335-20)^{\circ}C}\right ]=0.82\)

 

and

Note that, with Fo = 0.82, use of the one-term approximation is justified.

 

Comments:
1. If the temperature distribution in the sphere at the conclusion of step 1 were not uniform, the calculations of step 2 could not be performed using either the one-term approximation or the exact series solution, since both assume a uniform initial temperature.

2. The surface temperature of the sphere at the conclusion of step 2 may be obtained from Equation 5.53b. With \(\theta _{o}^{\ast }=0.095\) and \(r^{\ast }=1\),

 

\(\theta ^{\ast }=\theta _{o}^{\ast }\frac{1}{\zeta _{1}r^{\ast }}\sin (\zeta _{1}r^{\ast })\) (5.53b)

 

\(\theta ^{\ast }(r_{o})=\frac{T(r_{o})-T_{\infty }}{T_{i}-T_{\infty }}=\frac{0.095}{1.800 rad}\sin (1.800 rad)=0.0514\)

 

and

 

\(T(r_{o})=20^{\circ}C+0.0514(335-20)^{\circ}C=36^{\circ}C\)

For \((t-t_{a})> 0.75 s\), satisfactory convergence is provided by the one-term approximation.
