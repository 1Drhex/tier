Guest Question.

Design a straight-bevel gear mesh for shaft centerlines that intersect perpendicularly, to deliver 6.85 hp at 900 rev/min with a gear ratio of 3:1, temperature of 300°F, normal pressure angle of 20°, using a design factor of 2. The load is uniform-uniform.
Use a pinion of 20 teeth. The material is to be AGMA grade 1 and the teeth are to be crowned. The reliability goal is 0.995 with a pinion life of \(10^{9}\) revolutions.

Admin
Solution

First we list the a priori decisions and their immediate consequences.
Function: 6.85 hp at 900 rev/min, gear ratio \(m_{G}=3,300^{\circ} F\) environment, neither gear straddle-mounted, \(K_{m b}=1.25\) [Eq. (15–11)], R = 0.995 at \(10^{9}\) revolutions of the pinion,

 

Eq. (15–14): \(C_{L}=\left\{\begin{array}{ll}2 & 10^{3} \leq N_{L}<10^{4} \\3.4822N_{L}^{-0.0602} &10^{4} \leq N_{L} \leq 10^{10}\end{array}\right.\)

 

\(\left(C_{L}\right)_{G}=3.4822\left(10^{9} / 3\right)^{-0.0602}=1.068\)

 

\(\left(C_{L}\right)_{P}=3.4822\left(10^{9}\right)^{-0.0602}=1\)

 

Eq. (15–15): \(K_{L}=\left\{\begin{array}{lll}2.7 & 10^{2} \leq N_{L}<10^{3} & \\6.1514 N_{L}^{-0.1192} & 10^{3} \leq N_{L}<3\left(10^{6}\right) & \\1.683 N_{L}^{-0.0323} &3\left(10^{6}\right) \leq N_{L} \leq 10^{10} & \text { critical } \\1.3558 N_{L}^{-0.0178} &3\left(10^{6}\right) \leq N_{L}\leq 10^{10} & \text { general }\end{array}\right.\)

 

\(\left(K_{L}\right)_{G}=1.683\left(10^{9} / 3\right)^{-0.0323}=0.8929\)

 

\(\left(K_{L}\right)_{P}=1.683\left(10^{9}\right)^{-0.0323}=0.8618\)

 

Eq. (15–18): \(K_{T}=\left\{\begin{array}{ll}1 & 32^{\circ} F \leq t \leq 250^{\circ} F \\(460+t) / 710 & t>250^{\circ} F\end{array}\right.\)

\(K_{T}=C_{T}=(460+300) / 710=1.070\)

 

Design factor: \(n_{d}=2, S_{F}=2, S_{H}=\sqrt{2}=1.414\).


Tooth system: crowned, straight-bevel gears, normal pressure angle 20°,

 

Eq. (15–13): \(K_{x}=Y_{\beta}=1\)

 

\(K_{x}=1\)

 

Eq. (15–12): \(C_{x c}=Z_{x c}=\left\{\begin{array}{ll}1.5 & \text { properly crowned teeth } \\2.0 & \text { or larger uncrowned teeth }\end{array}\right.\)

 

\(C_{x c}=1.5\)

 

With \(N_{P}=20 \text { teeth, } N_{G}=(3) 20=60\) teeth and from Fig. 15–14,

 

\(\gamma=\tan ^{-1}\left(N_{P} / N_{G}\right)=\tan ^{-1}(20 / 60)=18.43^{\circ} \quad \Gamma=\tan ^{-1}(60 / 20)=71.57^{\circ}\)

 

From Figs. 15–6 and 15–7, \(I=0.0825, J_{P}=0.248 \text { , and } J_{G}=0.202 \text { . Note that }\)\(J_{P}>J_{G}\)

 

Decision 1: Trial diametral pitch, \(P_{d}=8\) teeth/in.

 

Eq. (15–10): \(K_{S}=\left\{\begin{array}{ll}0.4867+0.2132 / P_{d} & 0.5 \leq P_{d} \leq 16 \text { teeth } / in \\0.5 & P_{d}>16 \text { teeth/in }\end{array}\right.\)

 

\(K_{s}=0.4867+0.2132 / 8=0.5134\)

 

\(d_{P}=N_{P} / P_{d}=20 / 8=2.5 \text { in }\)

 

\(d_{G}=2.5(3)=7.5 \text { in }\)

 

\(v_{t}=\pi d_{P} n_{P} / 12=\pi(2.5) 900 / 12=589.0 ft / min\)

 

\(W^{t}=33000 hp / v_{t}=33000(6.85) / 589.0=383.8 lbf\)

 

Eq. (15–25): \(A_{0}=d_{P} /(2 \sin \gamma)=2.5 /\left(2 \sin 18.43^{\circ}\right)=3.954 \text { in }\)

 

\(A_{0}=\frac{d_{P}}{2 \sin \gamma}=\frac{d_{G}}{2 \sin \Gamma}\)

 

Eq. (15–24): \(F=\min \left(0.3 A_{0}, 10 / P_{d}\right)\)

 

\(F=\min \left(0.3 A_{0}, 10 / P_{d}\right)=\min [0.3(3.954), 10 / 8]=\min (1.186,1.25)=1.186 \text { in }\)

 

Decision 2: Let F = 1.25 in. Then,

 

Eq. (15–9): \(C_{s}=0.125(1.25)+0.4375=0.5937\)

 

Eq. (15–11): \(K_{m}=1.25+0.0036(1.25)^{2}=1.256\)

 

Decision 3: Let the transmission accuracy number be 6. Then, from Eq. (15–6),

 

\(\begin{array}{l}A=50+56(1-B)\\B=0.25\left(12-Q_{v}\right)^{2 / 3}\end{array}\) (15–6)

 

\(B=0.25(12-6)^{2 / 3}=0.8255\)

 

\(A=50+56(1-0.8255)=59.77\)

 

Eq. (15–5): \(\begin{array}{ll}K_{v} &=\left(\frac{A+\sqrt{v_{t}}}{A}\right)^{B} & \text { (U.S. customary units) } \\K_{v} &=\left(\frac{A+\sqrt{200 v_{e t}}}{A}\right)^{B} & \text { (SIunits) }\end{array}\)

 

\(K_{v}=\left(\frac{59.77+\sqrt{589.0}}{59.77}\right)^{0.8255}=1.325\)

 

Decision 4: Pinion and gear material and treatment. Carburize and case-harden grade ASTM 1320 to
Core 21 HRC (\(H_{B}\)is 229 Brinell)
Case 55-64 HRC (\(H_{B}\) is 515 Brinell)
From Table 15–4, \(s_{a c}=200000 \text { psi }\) and from Table 15–6, \(s_{a t}=30000 \text { psi }\).
Gear bending: From Eq. (15–3), the bending stress is

 

\(\begin{aligned}s_{t} &=\frac{W^{t}}{F} P_{d} K_{o} K_{v} \frac{K_{s} K_{m}}{K_{x} J} & & \text { (U.S. customary units) } \\\sigma_{F} &=\frac{1000 W^{t}}{b} \frac{K_{A} K_{v}}{m_{e t}} \frac{Y_{x} K_{H \beta}}{Y_{\beta} Y_{J}} & & \text { (SI units) }\end{aligned}\) (15–3)

 

\(\left(s_{t}\right)_{G}=\frac{W^{t}}{F} P_{d} K_{o} K_{v} \frac{K_{s} K_{m}}{K_{x} J_{G}}=\frac{383.8}{1.25} 8(1) 1.325 \frac{0.5134(1.256)}{(1) 0.202}\)

 

\(=10390 psi\)

 

The bending strength, from Eq. (15–4), is given by

 

\(\begin{aligned}s_{t} &=\frac{W^{t}}{F} P_{d} K_{o} K_{v} \frac{K_{s} K_{m}}{K_{x} J} & & \text { (U.S. customary units) } \\\sigma_{F}&=\frac{1000 W^{t}}{b} \frac{K_{A} K_{v}}{m_{e t}} \frac{Y_{x} K_{H \beta}}{Y_{\beta} Y_{J}} & & \text { (SI units) }\end{aligned}\) (15–4)

 

\(\left(s_{w t}\right)_{G}=\left(\frac{s_{a t} K_{L}}{S_{F} K_{T} K_{R}}\right)_{G}=\frac{30000(0.8929)}{2(1.070) 1.075}=11640 psi\)

The strength exceeds the stress by a factor of 11640y10390 5 1.12, giving an actual
factor of safety of \((S_{F})_{G}=2(1.12)=2.24\)

Pinion bending: The bending stress can be found from

\((S_{t})_{p}=(S_{t})_{_{G}}\frac{J_{G}}{J_{p}}=10.390\frac{0.202}{0.248}=8463 psi\)

The bending strength, again from Eq. (15–4), is given by

\((S_{wt})_{p}=(\frac{s_{atK_{L}}}{S_{F}K_{T}K_{R}})_{p}=\frac{30000(0.8618)}{2(1.070)1.075}=11240 psi\)

The strength exceeds the stress by a factor of 11 \(240 /8463=1.33\), giving an actual factor of safety of \(\left(S_{F}\right)_{P}=2(1.33)=2.66\).

 

Gear wear: The load-induced contact stress for the pinion and gear, from Eq. (15–1), is

 

\(s_{c}=C_{p}\left(\frac{W^{t}}{F d_{P} I} K_{o} K_{v} K_{m} C_{s} C_{x c}\right)^{1 / 2}\)

 

\(=2290\left[\frac{383.8}{1.25(2.5) 0.0825}(1) 1.325(1.256) 0.5937(1.5)\right]^{1 / 2}\)

 

\(=107560 psi\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}s_{c}&=\sigma_{c}=C_{p}\left(\frac{W^{t}}{F d_{P} I} K_{o} K_{v} K_{m} C_{s} C_{x c}\right)^{1 / 2} & & \text { (U.S. customary units) } \\\sigma_{H}&=Z_{E}\left(\frac{1000 W^{t}}{b d Z_{1}} K_{A} K_{v} K_{H \beta} Z_{x} Z_{x c}\right)^{1 /2} & & \text { (SI units) }\end{aligned}\) (15–1)

 

From Eq. (15–2) the contact strength of the gear is

 

\(\begin{aligned}s_{w c}&=\left(\sigma_{c}\right)_{\text {all }}=\frac{s_{a c} C_{L} C_{H}}{S_{H} K_{T} C_{R}} & & \text { (U.S. customary units) } \\\sigma_{H P}&=\frac{\sigma_{H \lim } Z_{N T} Z_{W}}{S_{H} K_{\theta} Z_{Z}} & & \text { (SI units) }\end{aligned}\) (15–2)

 

\(\left(s_{w c}\right)_{G}=\left(\frac{s_{a c} C_{L} C_{H}}{S_{H} K_{T} C_{R}}\right)_{G}=\frac{200000(1.068)(1)}{\sqrt{2}(1.070) 1.037}=136120 psi\)

 

The strength exceeds the stress by a factor of 136 \(120 / 107560=1.266,\) giving an
actual factor of safety of \(\left(S_{H}\right)_{G}^{2}=1.266^{2}(2)=3.21\).
Pinion wear: From Eq. (15–2) the contact strength of the pinion is

 

\(\left(s_{w c}\right)_{P}=\left(\frac{s_{a c} C_{L} C_{H}}{S_{H} K_{T} C_{R}}\right)_{P}=\frac{200000(1)(1)}{\sqrt{2}(1.070) 1.037}=127450 psi\)

 

The strength exceeds the stress by a factor of 127 \(450 / 107560=1.185\), giving an
actual factor of safety of \(\left(S_{H}\right)_{P}^{2}=1.185^{2}(2)=2.81 .\)

The actual factors of safety are 2.24, 2.66, 3.21, and 2.81. Making a direct comparison of the factors, we note that the primary threat is from gear bending. We also note that the other three factors of safety are considerably higher than the target design factor. If optimization is desired, our goal would be to make changes in the design decisions that drive the factors closer to 2.
