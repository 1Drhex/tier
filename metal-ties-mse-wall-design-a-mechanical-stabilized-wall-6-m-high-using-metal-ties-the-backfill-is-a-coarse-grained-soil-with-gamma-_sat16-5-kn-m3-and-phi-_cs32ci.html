Guest Question.

Metal Ties MSE Wall
Design a mechanical stabilized wall 6 m high using metal ties. The backfill is a coarse-grained soil with \(\gamma _{sat}=16.5\, kN/m^{3}\: \: and\: \: {\Phi _{cs}}'=32^{\circ}\). Galvanized steel ties are available with a yield strength \(f_{y}=2.5*10^{5}\: kPa\) and a rate of corrosion of 0.025 mm/year. A factor of safety of 3 is desired for a design life of 50 years. The soil–tie interface friction value is \(21^{\circ}\). The foundation soil is coarse-grained with parameters \(\gamma _{sat}=18\, kN/m^{3},\, {\Phi _{p}}'=35^{\circ},\: and\: {\Phi _{b}}'=20^{\circ}\) The surcharge is 15 kPa.

Strategy: You have to guess the spacing of the ties. You can obtain standard widths and properties of ties from manufacturers’ catalogs. Follow the procedure for the coherent gravity method.

Admin
Solution

Step 1: Assume spacing and width of ties.

Assume \(S_{z}=0.5\, m,\: \: S_{y}=1\, m,\: \: and\: \: w=75\, mm\)

 

Step 2: Calculate required thickness of reinforcement.

\(K_{o}=1-sin\, {\Phi _{cs}}'=1-sin\, 32^{\circ}=0.47\)

 

\(K_{aR}=tan^{2}(45-{\Phi _{cs}}'/2)=tan^{2}(45-32/2)=0.31\)

 

\(t_{r}=\frac{K_{aR}({\gamma }'H_{o}+q_{s})S_{z}S_{y}(FS)_{tr}}{wf_{y}}=\frac{0.31(16.5*6+15)*0.5*1*3}{0.075*2.5*10^{5}}\)

 

\(=283*10^{-5}m=2.8\, mm\)

 

\(t_{corrosion}\) = annual corrosion rate * design life = 0.025 * 50 = 1.25 mm

 

\(t_{design}\) = calculated thickness + corrosion thickness = 2.8 + 1.25 = 4.05 mm

 

You will select a tie thickness from standard sizes closest to 4.05 mm. Use t = 5 mm.

 

Step 3: Determine the length of reinforcement required at the base.

From Equation (15.16): \(K_{aC}=\frac{cos^{2}({\Phi }'-\, \eta )}{cos^{2}\eta \, cos(\eta +\delta )\begin{bmatrix}1+\begin{Bmatrix}\frac{sin({\Phi }'+\delta )sin(\eta -\beta )}{cos(\eta +\delta )\, cos(\eta -\beta )}\end{Bmatrix}^{1/2}\end{bmatrix}^{2}}\)

 

\(K_{aC}=0.28\: \: \: ({\Phi _{cs}}'=32,\, \delta =20^{\circ})\)

 

\((K_{aC})_{x}=K_{aC}\, cos\, \delta =0.28\, cos\, 20=0.26\)

 

\(L_{b}=\frac{(K_{aC})_{x}(0.5H_{o}+q_{s}/\gamma )(FS)_{T}}{tan\, {\Phi _{b}}'}=\frac{0.26(0.5*6+15/16.5)1.5}{tan\, 20^{\circ}}=4.2\, m\)

 

For internal stability, the effective length at the wall base is

\(L_{e}=\frac{K_{aR}S_{z}S_{y}(FS)_{t}}{2w\, tan\, \Phi _{i}}=\frac{0.31*0.5*1*1.3}{2*0.075*tan\, 21^{\circ}}=3.5\, m< 4.2\, m\)

 

Step 4: Determine the total length of reinforcement.

Use a table, as shown below \((FS)_{t}=1.3\).

 

Equation (15.78): \(K=K_{aR}\frac{z}{6}+K_{o}(1-\frac{z}{6})\: \: for\: \: z\leq 6\)

 

Equation (15.71): \(L_{e}=\frac{K_{aR}({\sigma _{z}}'+q_{s})S_{z}S_{y}(FS)_{t}}{2w({\sigma _{z}}'+q_{s})tan\, \Phi _{i}}=\frac{K_{aR}S_{z}S_{y}(FS)_{t}}{2w\, tan\, \Phi _{i}}\)

 

Equation (15.80): \(L_{R}=0.2H_{o}+(0.1H_{o}-\frac{z}{6})\: \: for\: \: z\leq 0.6\, H_{o}\)

Step 5: Check for external stability.
Translation is satisfied because L used at the base is greater than \(L_{b}\)
Check bearing capacity.

\((\sigma _{z})_{max}=\gamma H_{o}=16.5*6=99\, kPa\)

 

\(H=0.5\gamma K_{(aC)_{x}}H^{2}_{o}+K_{(aC)_{x}}H_{o}q_{s}=0.5*16.5*0.26*6^{2}+0.26*6*15=100.6\, kN\)

 

\(V_{n}=\gamma H_{o}L_{b}=16.5*6*4.2=415.8\, kN;\)

 

\(\frac{H}{V_{n}}=\frac{100.6}{415.8}=0.24;\)

 

\(\omega =tan^{-1}\frac{H}{V_{n}}\, tan^{-1}(0.24)=13.5^{\circ}\)

 

\(n=(2+\frac{{B}'}{{L}'})\, /\, (1+\frac{{B}'}{{L}'})=2;\: \: \: i_{\gamma }=(1-\frac{H}{V_{n}})=(1-0.24)^{2+1}=0.44\)

 

\(N_{\gamma }=0.1054\, exp\, (9.6\, {\Phi _{p}}')=0.1054\, exp\, (9.6*35*\frac{\pi }{180})=37.2\)

 

\(q_{u}=0.5\gamma BN_{\gamma }i_{\gamma }=0.5*18*4.2*37.2*0.44=618.7\, kPa\)

 

\((FS)_{B}=\frac{q_{u}}{(\sigma _{z})_{max}}=\frac{618.7}{99}=6.2> 3\); okay
