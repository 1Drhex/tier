Guest Question.

Find the dynamic model of a two-link Cartesian arm in the case when the second joint axis forms an angle of π/4 with the first joint axis; compare the result with the model of the manipulator in Fig. 7.3.

Admin
Solution

Consider the Cartesian arm in Fig. S7.1, for which the vector of generalized coordinates is \(q = \left[\begin{matrix} d_{1} & d_{2} \end{matrix} \right]^{T} \)

let \( m_{\ell_{1}}\) \( m_{\ell_{2}}\) be the masses of the two links and \( m_{m_{1}}\), \( m_{m_{2}}\) the masses of the rotors of the two joint motors. Let also \( I_{m_{1}}\) , \( I_{m_{2}}\) be the moments of inertia about the axes of the two rotors. It is assumed that \( p_{m_{i}}\) = \( P_{i – 1}\) and \( z_{m_{i}}\) = \( z_{i – 1}\) for i =1,2, i.e., the motors are located on the joint axes with centres of mass located at the origins of the respective frames. With the choice of the frames in Fig. S7.1, it is \( z_{1}\) = \( \left[\begin{matrix} 1/\sqrt{2} & 0 & 1/\sqrt{2}\end{matrix} \right]^{T}\) Hence, computation of the Jacobians in (7.16), (7.18) yields

\(J^{\left(\ell_{i} \right) }_{P}\) = \(\left[\jmath ^{\left(\ell_{i} \right) }_{P1} . . . \jmath ^{\left(\ell_{i} \right) }_{Pi} 0 . . . 0\right] \) (7.16)

\(\jmath ^{\left(\ell_{i} \right) }_{Pj} \) = \( \begin{cases}z_{j – 1}& for a prismatic joint \\ z_{j – 1}\times \left(p_{\ell_{i} } – p_{j – 1} \right)& for a revolute joint \end{cases}\) (7.18)

\( J^{\left(\ell_{1} \right) }_{p}\) = \( \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

\( J^{\left(\ell_{2} \right) }_{p}\) = \( \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & 1/\sqrt{2} \\ 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 1/\sqrt{2} \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Obviously it is \( J^{\left(\ell_{1} \right) }_{O}\) = \( J^{\left(\ell_{2} \right) }_{O}\) = 0

Computation of the Jacobians in (7.25), (7.26), (7.28), (7.29) respectively
yields

\(J^{\left(m_{i} \right) }_{P}\) = \(\left[\jmath ^{\left(m_{i} \right) }_{P1} . . . \jmath ^{\left(m_{i} \right) }_{P,i – 1} 0 . . . 0\right]\) (7.25)

\(\jmath ^{\left(m_{i} \right) }_{Pj} \) = \( \begin{cases} z_{j – 1} & for a prismatic joint \\ z_{j – 1}\times \left(p_{m_{i} } – p_{j – 1} \right) & for a revolute joint \end{cases} \) (7.26)

\(J^{\left(m_{i} \right) }_{O}\) = \(\left[\jmath ^{\left(m_{i} \right) }_{O1} . . . \jmath ^{\left(m_{i} \right) }_{O,i – 1} \jmath ^{\left(m_{i} \right) }_{O,i} 0 . . . 0 \right]\) (7.28)

\(\jmath ^{\left(m_{i} \right) }_{Oj} \) = \( \begin{cases} \jmath ^{\left(\ell_{i} \right) }_{Oj} & j = 1, . . . . , i – 1 \\ k_{r_{i} } z_{m_{i} } & j = i \end{cases} \) (7.29)

\( J^{\left(m_{1} \right) }_{P}\) = \( \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \) \( J^{\left(m_{1} \right) }_{P}\) = \( \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \) \( J^{\left(m_{1} \right) }_{O}\) = \( \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \\ k_{r_{1} } & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \) \( J^{\left(m_{2} \right) }_{O}\) = \( \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & k_{r_{2} }/\sqrt{2} \\ 0 & 0 \\ 0 & k_{r_{2} }/\sqrt{2} \end{matrix} \right ] \)

where \(k_{r_{i} } \) is the gear reduction ratio of motor i. From (7.32), the inertia matrix is

B(q) = \( \sum\limits_{i = 1}^{n}{\left(m_{\ell_{i} } J^{\left(\ell_{i}\right) T }_{P} J^{\left(\ell_{i}\right) }_{P} + J^{\left(\ell_{i}\right) T }_{O} R_{i}I^{i}_{\ell_{i}} R^{T}_{i} J^{\left(\ell_{i}\right) T }_{O} + m_{m_{i} } J^ {\left(m_{i}\right) T }_{P} J^{\left(m_{i}\right) }_{P} J^{\left(m_{i}\right) T }_{O} R_{m_{i} } I^{m_{i}}_{m_{i}} R^{T}_{m_{i}} J^{\left(m_{i}\right) }_{O} \right) }\) (7.32)

B = \(\left[\begin{matrix} m_{\ell_{1} } + m_{m_{2} } + k^{2}_{r_{1}} I_{m_{1} } + m_{\ell_{2} } & m_{\ell_{2} }/\sqrt{2} \\ m_{\ell_{2} } /\sqrt{2} & m_{\ell_{2} } + k^{2}_{r_{2}} I_{m_{2} } \end{matrix} \right] \)

Notice that B is constant, which implies that C = O. As for the gravitational
terms, with \( g_{0}\) = \(\left[ 0 0 -g\right]^{T}\) , (7.39) gives:

\(\frac{\partial \mathcal{U} }{\partial q_{i}} = -\sum\limits_{j=1}^{n}{\left(m_{\ell _j}g_{0}^{T}\frac{\partial p_{\ell _j}}{\partial q_i}+m_{m_j} g_{0}^{T}\frac{\partial p_{m _j}}{\partial q_i}\right) } = -\sum\limits_{j=1}^{n}{\left(m_{\ell _j}g_{0}^{T}\jmath _ {P_i}^{\left(\ell _j\right) }(q)+m_{m _j}g_{0}^{T}\jmath _ {P_i}^{\left(m _j\right) }(q) \right) } = g_i(q)\) (7.39)

\( g_{1}\) = (\( m_{\ell_{1} } + m_{m_{2} } + m_{\ell_{2} }\) ) g \( g_{2}\) = \(m_{\ell_{2} }{g}/{\sqrt{2} }\)

In the absence of friction and tip contact forces, the resulting equations of motion are

\( \left( m_{\ell_{1} } + m_{m_{2} } + k^{2}_{r_{1}} I_{m_{1} } + m_{\ell_{2} } \right) \ddot{d_{1}} + \frac{m_{\ell_{2} }}{\sqrt{2} } \ddot{d_{2}} + \left( m_{\ell_{1} } + m_{m_{2} } + m_{\ell_{2} } \right) g \) = \(\tau_{1}\)

\( \frac{m_{\ell_{2} }}{\sqrt{2} } \ddot{d_{1}} + \left( m_{\ell_{2} } + k^{2}_{r_{2}} I_{m_{2} } \right) \ddot{d_{2}} + \frac{m_{\ell_{2} }}{\sqrt{2} } g \) = \(\tau_{2}\)

where \(\tau_{1}\) and \(\tau_{2}\) denote the forces applied to the two joints. It is worth pointing out that, differently from the arm in Fig. 7.3, the dynamic model is coupled (nonnull off-diagonal terms in the inertia matrix) and gravity acts also on the second joint.
