Guest Question.

The reaction A + B → products has the following physical and chemical properties:

\(\Delta\underline{H}_{rxn}\)=-21111 cal/g-mol of A,

\(E_{A} \)= 18 500 cal/g-mol,

\(k_{i} = 6.2 × 104 m^{3}\)/g-mol min,

\(\rho = 1000 kg/m^{3}\),

\(\hat{C_{p}} = 1 cal/g ^{◦}C\).

Initial conditions are\( C_{Ai} = C_{Bi} \)\(= 8020 g-mol/m^{3}\), \(T_{i} = 93.3^{ ◦}C\). Estimate the temperature and concentration variation with time.

Admin
Solution

According to Eq. (2.5.7)

\(T-T_{i}=-\frac{(\Delta\underline{ H}_{rxn} )}{\rho \hat{C_{p}} }(C_{A}-C_{Ai})\) (2.5.7)

the temperature will rise monotonically from \(T_{i}\) to some limiting value as \(C_{A}\) varies from \(C_{Ai}\) to zero. It is convenient to calculate the limiting temperature directly as a check on the subsequent calculations. Setting \(C_{A}\) = 0 in Eq. (2.5.7), we obtain

\(t\rightarrow \infty \): \(T=T_{i}+\frac{(-\Delta\underline{ H}_{rxn} )C_{Ai}}{\rho \hat{C_{p}} }\)

\(=93.3+\frac{(2111 cal/g-mol)(8020 g-mol/m^{3})}{(10^{3}kg/m^{3})(1cal/g^{\circ }C)(10^{3}g/kg)}\)

\(=262.6^{\circ }C\)

The activation energy is given in cgs units, for which the gas constant is R = 1.99 cal/g-mol K. For consistency, the temperature must be expressed in degrees Kelvin, using Eq. (2.5.7) as follows:

\(T-T_{i}=-\frac{(\Delta\underline{ H}_{rxn} )}{\rho \hat{C_{p}} }(C_{A}-C_{Ai})\) (2.5.7)

= 93.3 + 169.3 − 0.021\(C_{A}\)+ 273 K

= (535.6 − 0.021\(C_{A}\)) K.

Equation (2.5.9) can then be written as

\(\int\limits_{C_{Ai}}^{C_{A}}{\frac{dC}{C^{2}\exp (-\frac{E_{A}}{R[T_{i}+\frac{-\Delta\underline{ H}_{rxn}}{\rho \hat{C_{p}} }(C_{Ai}-C) ]} } } =-K_{0}t,\) (2.5.9)

\(t=-\frac{1}{k_{0}} \int\limits_{C_{Ai}}^{C_{A}}\frac{dC}{C^{2}\exp (-E_{A}/RT)}\)

\(=-\frac{1}{6.2\times 10^{4}m^{3}/g-mol min}\int\limits_{8017.6}^{C_{A}}{\frac{dC}{C^{2}\exp [-18500/1.99(535.6-0.021C)]} }\)

\(=-1.6\times10^{-5}\int\limits_{8017.6}^{C_{A}}{\frac{dC}{C^{2}\exp [-9296.5/(535.6-0.021C)]} }min\)

The integral cannot be readily evaluated in terms of known functions, but for each value of \(C_{A}\) the integration can be carried out numerically. Table 2.7 contains the computed concentration–time relations and the corresponding temperatures. As
seen, for this exothermic reaction (-Hrxn < 0) the reactor temperature rises signifi-cantly. A similar analysis can be performed to determine Q, the heat necessary to be removed, if isothermal operation were desired, or necessitated because of product degradation, safety, or unwanted side reactions.
