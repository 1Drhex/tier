Guest Question.

The beam shown in Figure 5.28 must support two concentrated live loads of 20 kips each at the quarter points. The maximum live load deflection must not exceed L/240. Lateral support is provided at the ends of the beam. Use A992 steel and select a W shape.

Admin
Solution

If the weight of the beam is neglected, the central half of the beam is subjected to a uniform moment, and

\(M_{A }= M_{B} = M_{C} = M_{max}\), \(∴ Cb = 1.0\)

Even if the weight is included, it will be negligible compared to the concentrated loads, and \(C_{b}\) can still be taken as 1.0, permitting the charts to be used without modification.

Temporarily ignoring the beam weight, the factored-load moment is

\(M_{u} = 6(1.6 × 20) = 192\) ft-kips

From the charts, with \(L_{b }= 24\) ft, try W12 × 53:

 

\(\phi_{b}M_{n }= 209\) ft-kips \(> 192\) ft-kips (OK)

Now, we account for the beam weight:

\(M_{u}=192+\frac{1}{8}(1.2×0.053)(24)^{2}=197\) ft-kips \(<209\) ft-kips (OK)

The shear is

\(V_{u}=1.6(20)+\frac{1.2(0.053)(24)}{2}=32.8\) ft-kips

From the \(Z_{x}\) table (or the uniform load table),

\(\phi_{v}V_{n }= 125\) kips \(> 32.8\) kips (OK)

The maximum permissible live load deflection is

\(\frac{L}{240}=\frac{24(12)}{240}=1.20\) in.

From Table 3-23, “Shears, Moments, and Deflections,” in Part 3 of the Manual, the maximum deflection (at midspan) for two equal and symmetrically placed loads is

\(Δ=\frac{P_{a}}{24EI}(3L^{2}-4a^{2})\)

where
P = magnitude of concentrated load
a = distance from support to load
L = span length

\(Δ=\frac{20(6×12)}{24EI}[3(24×12)^{2}-4(6×12)^{2}]=\frac{13.69×10^{6}}{EI}\)

 

\(=\frac{13.69×10^{6}}{29,000(425)}=1.11\) in. \(<1.20\) in. (OK)

Use a W12 × 53.

The required flexural strength (not including the beam weight) is

\(M_{a} = 6(20) = 120\) ft-kips

From the charts, with \(L_{b} = 24\) ft, try W12 × 53.

\(\frac{M_{n}}{Ω_{b}}=139\) ft-kips \(> 120\) ft-kips (OK)

Account for the beam weight:

\(M_{a}=6(20)+\frac{1}{8}(0.053)(24)^{2}= 124\) ft-kips \(<139\) ft-kips (OK)
The required shear strength is

\(V_{a}=20+\frac{0.053(24)}{2}=20.6\) kips

From the \(Z_{x}\) table (or the uniform load table),

\(\frac{V_{n}}{Ω_{v}}=83.2\) kips \(> 20.6\) kips (OK)

Since deflections are computed with service loads, the deflection check is the same for both LRFD and ASD. From the LRFD solution,
\(Δ = 1.11\) in. \(< 1.20\) in. (OK)

Use a W12 × 53.
