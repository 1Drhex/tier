Guest Question.

A free particle of mass m is confined to a ring of circumference L such that ψ (x+L) = ψ(x) . The unperturbed Hamiltonian is

\( H^{0}=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \frac{d^{2}}{d x^{2}} \),

to which we add a perturbation

\( H^{\prime}=V_{0} \cos \left(2 \pi \frac{x}{L}\right) \).

(a) Show that the unperturbed states may be written

\( \psi_{n}^{0}(x)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{L}} e^{i 2 \pi n x / L} \).

\( \text { for } n=0, \pm 1, \pm 2 \) and that, apart from n = 0, all of these states are two-fold degenerate.

(b) Find a general expression for the matrix elements of the perturbation:

\( H_{m n}^{\prime}=\left\langle\psi_{m}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle \).

(c) Consider the degenerate pair of states with \( n=\pm 1 \). Construct the matrix W and calculate the first-order energy corrections,\( E^1\) . Note that the degeneracy does not lift at first order. Therefore, diagonalizing does not tell us what the “good” states are.

(d) Construct the matrix W² () for the states n = ±1, and show that the degeneracy lifts at second order. What are the good linear combinations of the states with n = ±1?

(e) What are the energies, accurate to second order, for these states?.

Admin
Solution

(a)

\( -\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \frac{d^{2} \psi}{d x^{2}}=E \psi \Rightarrow \frac{d^{2} \psi}{d x^{2}}=-k^{2} \psi \Rightarrow \psi=A e^{i k x} ; \psi(x+L)=\psi(x) \Rightarrow e^{i k L}=1 \).

\( \Rightarrow k L=2 n \pi(n=0, \pm 1, \pm 2, \ldots) ; \psi_{n}^{0}(x)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{L}} e^{i 2 \pi n x / L} \).

(b)

\( H_{m n}^{\prime}=\frac{V_{0}}{L} \int_{0}^{L} e^{-i 2 \pi m x / L} \cos \left(2 \pi \frac{x}{L}\right) e^{i 2 \pi n x / L} d x=\frac{V_{0}}{2 L} \int_{0}^{L} e^{i 2 \pi(n-m) x / L}\left(e^{i 2 \pi x / L}+e^{-i 2 \pi x / L}\right) d x \).

\( =\frac{V_{0}}{2 L} \int_{0}^{L}\left[e^{i 2 \pi(n-m+1) x / L}+e^{i 2 \pi x(n-m-1) / L}\right] d x \).

But, for any nonzero integer N,

\( \int_{0}^{L} e^{i 2 \pi N x / L} d x=\left.\frac{e^{i 2 \pi N x / L}}{i 2 \pi N / L}\right|_{0} ^{L}=\frac{e^{i 2 \pi N}-1}{i 2 \pi N / L}=\frac{1-1}{i 2 \pi N / L}=0 \).

while for N = 0 the integral is (obviously) L, so

\( H_{m n}^{\prime}=\frac{V_{0}}{2}\left(\delta_{m, n+1}+\delta_{m, n-1}\right) \).

(c)

\( [W]_{1,1}=H_{11}^{\prime}=0,[W]_{1,-1}=H_{1-1}^{\prime}=0,[W]_{-1,1}=H_{-11}^{\prime}=0,[W]_{-1,-1}=H_{-1-1}^{\prime}=0 \),

so

\( W =\left(\begin{array}{ll} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{array}\right) \), and (Equation 7.33) there are no first-order corrections to the energies.

\( E_{\pm}^{1}=\frac{1}{2}\left[W_{a a}+W_{b b} \pm \sqrt{\left(W_{a a}-W_{b b}\right)^{2}+4\left|W_{a b}\right|^{2}}\right] \) (7.33).

(d)

\( \left[W^{2}\right]_{1,1}=\sum_{n \neq 1,-1} \frac{H_{1 n}^{\prime} H_{n 1}^{\prime}}{E_{1}^{0}-E_{n}^{0}}=\frac{\left|H_{10}^{\prime}\right|^{2}}{E_{1}^{0}-E_{0}^{0}}+\frac{\left|H_{21}^{\prime}\right|^{2}}{E_{1}^{0}-E_{2}^{0}}=\left(\frac{V_{0}}{2}\right)^{2}\left(\frac{1}{E_{1}^{0}-0}+\frac{1}{E_{1}^{0}-4 E_{1}^{0}}\right)=\frac{V_{0}^{2}}{6 E_{1}^{0}} \),

\( \left[W^{2}\right]_{1,-1}=\sum_{n \neq 1,-1} \frac{H_{1 n}^{\prime} H_{n-1}^{\prime}}{E_{1}^{0}-E_{n}^{0}}=\frac{H_{10}^{\prime} H_{0-1}^{\prime}}{E_{1}^{0}-E_{0}^{0}}=\left(\frac{V_{0}}{2}\right)^{2}\left(\frac{1}{E_{1}^{0}-0}\right)=\frac{V_{0}^{2}}{4 E_{1}^{0}} \),

\( \left[W^{2}\right]_{-1,1}=\sum_{n \neq 1,-1} \frac{H_{-1 n}^{\prime} H_{n 1}^{\prime}}{E_{1}^{0}-E_{n}^{0}}=\frac{H_{-10}^{\prime} H_{01}^{\prime}}{E_{1}^{0}-E_{0}^{0}}=\left(\frac{V_{0}}{2}\right)^{2}\left(\frac{1}{E_{1}^{0}-0}\right)=\frac{V_{0}^{2}}{4 E_{1}^{0}} \),

\( \left[W^{2}\right]_{-1,-1}=\sum_{n \neq 1,-1} \frac{H_{-1 n}^{\prime} H_{n-1}^{\prime}}{E_{1}^{0}-E_{n}^{0}}=\frac{\left|H_{-10}^{\prime}\right|^{2}}{E_{1}^{0}-E_{0}^{0}}+\frac{\left|H_{-2-1}^{\prime}\right|^{2}}{E_{1}^{0}-E_{2}^{0}}=\left(\frac{V_{0}}{2}\right)^{2}\left(\frac{1}{E_{1}^{0}-0}+\frac{1}{E_{1}^{0}-4 E_{1}^{0}}\right)=\frac{V_{0}^{2}}{6 E_{1}^{0}} \).

so

\( W ^{2}=\frac{V_{0}^{2}}{12 E_{1}^{0}}\left(\begin{array}{ll} 2 & 3 \\ 3 & 2 \end{array}\right) \).

\( \text { The eigenvalues of }\left(\begin{array}{ll} 2 & 3 \\ 3 & 2 \end{array}\right) \text { are given by } \)

\( \left|\begin{array}{cc} (2-\lambda) & 3 \\ 3 & (2-\lambda) \end{array}\right|=(2-\lambda)^{2}-9 \Rightarrow \lambda_{\pm}=2 \pm 3 ; \lambda_{+}=5, \lambda_{-}=-1 \).

so the degeneracy lifts at second order. The eigenvectors are given by

\( \left(\begin{array}{ll} 2 & 3 \\ 3 & 2 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} x \\ y<br /> \end{array}\right)=\lambda\left(\begin{array}{l} x \\ y \end{array}\right) \Rightarrow 2 x+3 y=\lambda x \Rightarrow 2 x+3 y=\lambda x \).

\( \lambda_{+}=5 \Rightarrow 2 x+3 y=5 x \Rightarrow y=x \Rightarrow v_{+}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) \).

\( \lambda_{-}=-1 \Rightarrow 2 x+3 y=-x \Rightarrow y=-x \Rightarrow v_{-}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right) \).

so the good states are

\( \psi_{+}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\psi_{1}+\psi_{-1}\right)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \frac{1}{\sqrt{L}}\left(e^{i 2 \pi x / L}+e^{-i 2 \pi x / L}\right)= \sqrt{\frac{2}{L}} \cos \left(\frac{2 \pi x}{L}\right) \).

\( \psi_{-}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\psi_{1}-\psi_{-1}\right)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \frac{1}{\sqrt{L}}\left(e^{i 2 \pi x / L}-e^{-i 2 \pi x / L}\right)=i \sqrt{\frac{2}{L}} \sin \left(\frac{2 \pi x}{L}\right) \).

(e) The energy, to second order, is

\( E=E^{0}+E^{1}+E^{2}=E_{1}^{0}+0+\left(\frac{V_{0}^{2}}{12 E_{1}^{0}}\right)(2 \pm 3), \text { where } E_{1}^{0}=\frac{\hbar^{2} k^{2}}{2 m}=\frac{2 \pi^{2} \hbar^{2}}{m L^{2}} \).

so the perturbed energies are

\( E_{+}=\frac{2 \pi^{2} \hbar^{2}}{m L^{2}}+5 \frac{V_{0}^{2} m L^{2}}{24 \pi^{2} \hbar^{2}}, \quad E_{-}=\frac{2 \pi^{2} \hbar^{2}}{m L^{2}}-\frac{V_{0}^{2} m L^{2}}{24 \pi^{2} \hbar^{2}} \).
