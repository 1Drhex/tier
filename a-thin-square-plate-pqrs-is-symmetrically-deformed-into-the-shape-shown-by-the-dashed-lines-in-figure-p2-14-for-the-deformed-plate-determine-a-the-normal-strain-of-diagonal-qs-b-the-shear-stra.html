Guest Question.

A thin square plate PQRS is symmetrically deformed into the shape shown by the dashed lines in Figure P2.14. For the deformed plate, determine:
(a) the normal strain of diagonal QS.
(b) the shear strain \(\gamma_{x y}\) at corner P.

Admin
Solution

(a) Normal strain of diagonal QS. The deformation of diagonal QS is

\(\delta_{Q S}=\frac{249.7 mm -250 mm }{250 mm }=-0.3 mm\)

Thus, the normal strain of diagonal QS is

\(\varepsilon_{Q S}=\frac{-0.3 mm }{250 mm }=-0.001200 mm / mm =-1,200 \mu \varepsilon\)

(b) Shear strain at corner P. Before deformation, side PS makes an angle of π/4 radians with the x axis. After deformation, the angle that side PS makes with the x axis can be found from

\(\begin{aligned}\tan \alpha &=\frac{(249.7 mm / 2)}{(251.2 mm / 2)}=0.994029 rad \\& \therefore \alpha=0.782404 rad\end{aligned}\)

Before deformation, the interior angle at P (i.e., angle SPQ) was π/2 radians. After deformation, the interior angle at P is 2(0.782404 radians) = 1.564807. Refer to Figure 2.5a and 2.5b in the text. If we denote the interior angle at P after deformation as

\(\frac{\pi}{2}-\gamma_{P}\)

then the shear strain at corner P is:

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{\pi}{2}-\gamma_{P} &=1.564807 rad \\& \therefore \gamma_{P}=\frac{\pi}{2}-1.564807 rad =0.005989 rad=5,989 \mu rad\end{aligned}\)
