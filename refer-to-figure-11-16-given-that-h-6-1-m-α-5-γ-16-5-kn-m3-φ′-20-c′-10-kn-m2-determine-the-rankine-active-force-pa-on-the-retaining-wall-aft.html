Guest Question.

Refer to Figure 11.16. Given that H = 6.1 m, \(\pmb{\alpha} = \textbf{5}^{\circ},\, \pmb{\gamma} = \textbf{16.5 kN/}\textbf{m}^{\textbf{3}},\, \pmb{\phi}^{\prime} = \textbf{20}^{\circ},\, \textbf{c}^{\prime} = \textbf{10 kN/}\textbf{m}^{\textbf{2}},\) determine the Rankine active force \(\textbf{P}_{\textbf{a}}\) on the retaining wall after the tensile crack occurs.

Admin
Solution

From Eq. (11.53), the depth of tensile crack is

\(z_{o}=\frac{2 c^{\prime}}{\gamma} \sqrt{\frac{1+\sin \phi^{\prime}}{1-\sin \phi^{\prime}}}=\frac{(2)(10)}{16.5} \sqrt{\frac{1+\sin 20}{1-\sin 20}}=1.73 m\)

So

\(\bullet At\,z = 0, \, \sigma^{\prime}_{a} = 0\\\bullet At\,z = 6.1\,m,\,\sigma^{\prime}_{a} = \gamma z K^{″}_{a}\cos \alpha\\\frac{c^{\prime}}{\gamma z} = \frac{10}{(16.5)(6.1)}\approx 0.1\)

From Table 11.4, for \(\alpha = 5^{\circ}\,and\, c^{\prime}/\gamma z = 0.1 ,\) the magnitude of \(K^{″}_{a} = 0.357.\,So\\\sigma^{\prime}_{a} = (16.5)(6.1)(0.357)(\cos 5^{\circ}) = 35.8\,kN/m^{2}\\Hence,\\P_{a} = \frac{1}{2}(H – z_{o})(35.8) = \frac{1}{2}(6.1 – 1.73)(35.8) = \textbf{78.2 kN/m}\)
