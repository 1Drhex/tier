Net Present Value

Watertown Paper Corporation is considering adding another machine for the manufacture of corrugated cardboard. The machine would cost $900,000. It would have an estimated life of 6 years and no salvage value. The company estimates that annual cash inflows would increase by $400,000 and that annual cash outflows would increase by $190,000. Management has a required rate of return of 9%. Calculate the net present value on this project and discuss whether it should be accepted.

Admin
Solution
Estimated annual cash inflows $400,000
Estimated annual cash outflows 190,000
Net annual cash flow $210,000

 

Cash Flow 9% Discount Factor Present Value
Present value of net annual cash flows $210,000 4.48592\(^{\text{a}}\) $942,043
Less: Capital investment 900,000
Net present value $42,043
\(^{\text{a}}\)Table 4, Appendix A, 9%, 6 years

 

Since the net present value is greater than zero, Watertown should accept the project.

 

 

ACTION PLAN
• Recall that estimated annual cash inflows − Estimated annual cash outflows = Net annual cash flow.
• Use the NPV technique to calculate the difference between net cash flows and the initial investment.
• Accept the project if the net present value is positive.
