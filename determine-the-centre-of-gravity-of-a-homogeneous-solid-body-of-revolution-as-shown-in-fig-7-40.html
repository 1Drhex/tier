Guest Question.

Determine the centre of gravity of a homogeneous solid body of revolution as shown in Fig. 7.40.

Admin
Solution

Refer to Fig. 7.41.

The solid body consists of the following three parts :
(i) Hemisphere …(1)
(ii) Cylinder …(2)
(iii) Cone …(3)
The solid body is obtained by removing the mass corresponding to the cone (3) from the composite body consisting of hemisphere and the cylinder.

 

Since, the body is symmetrical about X-axis, therefore we shall find out \(\bar{y}\) only with the following table.

 

Vx (\(mm^{4}\)) Centroidal distance ‘x’ from MM (mm) Volume (\(mm^{3}\)) V Components
\(2604156\pi\) (+) \(50-\frac{3r}{8}=50-\frac{3\times 50}{8}=31.25\) \(\frac{2}{3}\pi r^{3}=\frac{2}{3}\pi \times 50^{3}=8.3333\pi\) (+) Hemispherical (1)
\(25000000\pi\) (+) \(50+\frac{100}{2}=100\) \(\pi r^{2}h=\pi \times 50^{2}\times 100=250000\pi\) (+) Cylinder (2)
\(10416625\pi\) (–) \(50+100-\frac{100}{4}=125\) \(\frac{1}{3}\pi r^{2}h=\frac{1}{3}\times \pi \times 50^{2}\times 100=83333\pi\) (-) Cone (3)
\(\sum Vx = 17187531\pi\) _ \(\sum V = 250000\pi \) Total

 

 

\(\bar{x}=\frac{\sum Vx}{\sum V}=\frac{17187531\pi }{250000\pi }=68.7\) mm.

 

and \(\bar{y}=\frac{100}{2}=50\) mm.
