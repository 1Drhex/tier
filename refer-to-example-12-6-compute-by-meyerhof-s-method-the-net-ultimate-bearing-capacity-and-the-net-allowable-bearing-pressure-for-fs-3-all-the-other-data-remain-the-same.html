Guest Question.

Refer to . Compute by Meyerhof ‘s method the net ultimate bearing capacity and the net allowable bearing pressure for \(F_{s}=3\). All the other data remain the same.

Admin
Solution

From we have \(B=10 ft , L=20 ft , D_{f}=6 ft , \text { and } \gamma=114 lb / ft ^{3}\). From Eq. (12.27) for c = 0 and i = 1 , we have

 

\(q_{u}=c N_{c} s_{c} d_{c} i_{c}+q_{o}^{\prime} N_{q} s_{q} d_{q} i_{q}+\frac{1}{2} \gamma B N_{\gamma} s_{\gamma} d_{\gamma} i_{r}\) (12.27)

 

\(q_{n u}=q_{u}-\gamma D_{f}=\gamma D_{f}\left(N_{q}-1\right) s_{q} d_{q}+\frac{1}{2} \gamma B N_{\gamma} s_{\gamma} d_{\gamma}\)

 

From Table 12.3

 

\(s_{q}=1+0.1 N_{\phi} \frac{B}{L}=1+0.1 \tan ^{2} 45^{\circ}+\frac{35}{2} \quad \frac{10}{20}=1.185\)

 

\(s_{\gamma}=s_{q}=1.185\)

 

\(d_{q}=1+0.1 \sqrt{N_{\phi}} \frac{D_{f}}{B}=1+0.1 \tan 45^{\circ}+\frac{35}{2} \quad \frac{6}{10}=1.115\)

 

\(d_{\gamma}=d_{q}=1.115\)

 

From Table 12.2 for \(\phi=35^{\circ}, \text { we have } N_{q}=33.55, N_{\gamma}=37.75\). By substituting the known values, we have

 

\(q_{n u}=114 \times 6(33.55-1) \times 1.185 \times 1.115+\frac{1}{2} \times 114 \times 10 \times 37.75 \times 1.185 \times 1.115\)

 

\(=29,417+28,431=57,848 lb / ft ^{2}\)

 

\(q_{n a}=\frac{57,848}{3}=19,283 lb / ft ^{2}\)

 

By Terzaghi’s method \(q_{n a}=16,462 lb / ft ^{2}\).

Meyerhof’s method gives a higher value for \(q_{n a}\) by about 17%.
