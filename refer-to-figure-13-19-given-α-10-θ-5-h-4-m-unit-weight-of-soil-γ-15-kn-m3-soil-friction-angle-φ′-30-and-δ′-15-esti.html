Guest Question.

Refer to Figure 13.19. Given: α = 10°; θ= 5°; H = 4 m; unit weight of soil, \(\gamma= 15 kN/m^{3}\); soil friction angle,\(\phi ^{\prime }\)= 30°; and \(\delta ^{\prime }\)= 15°. Estimate the active force, \(P_{a}\), per unit length of the wall. Also, state the direction and location of the resultant force, \(P_{a}\).

Admin
Solution

From Eq. (13.53),

\(p_{a} =\frac{1}{2} \gamma H^{2} K_{a}\)

For \(\phi ^{\prime }=30°; \delta ^{\prime }=15°\)— that is, \(\frac{ \delta ^{\prime }}{\phi ^{\prime }} =\frac{15}{30} =\frac{1}{2} ;\alpha =10° \) ;and θ=5°, the magnitude of \(5°, the magnitude\) is 0.3872 (Table 13.6). So,

\(P_{a}=\frac{1}{2} (15)(4)^{2} (0.3872)=46.46 kN/m\)

The resultant will act at a vertical distance equal to H/3 = 4/3 =1.33 m above the bottom of the wall and will be inclined at an angle of 15° (\(=\delta ^{\prime }\)) to the back face of the wall.
