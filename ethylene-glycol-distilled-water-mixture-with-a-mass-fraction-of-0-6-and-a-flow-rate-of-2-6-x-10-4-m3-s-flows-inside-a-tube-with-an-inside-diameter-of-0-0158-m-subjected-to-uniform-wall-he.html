Guest Question.

Ethylene glycol-distilled water mixture with a mass fraction of 0.6 and a flow rate of \( 2.6 \times 10^{-4} m ^{3} / s\) flows inside a tube with an inside diameter of 0.0158 m subjected to uniform wall heat flux. For this flow, determine the Nusselt number at the location x / D=90 if the inlet configuration of the tube is: (a) re-entrant, (b) square-edged, and (c) bell-mouth. At this location, the local Grashof number is Gr =51,770 . The properties of ethylene glycoldistilled water mixture at the location of interest are \( \operatorname{Pr}=29.2, \nu=3.12 \times 10^{-6} m ^{2} / s\) and \( \mu_{d} / \mu_{s}=1.77 . \)

 

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION A liquid mixture flowing in a tube is subjected to uniform wall heat flux. The Nusselt number at a specified location is to be determined for three different tube inlet configurations.

Assumptions Steady operating conditions exist.

Properties The properties of the ethylene glycol-distilled water mixture are given to be \( \operatorname{Pr}=29.2, \nu=3.12 \times 10^{-6} m ^{2} / s _{1}\) and \( \mu_{b} / \mu_{s}=1.77\)

Analysis For a tube with a known diameter and volume flow rate, the type of flow regime is determined before making any decision regarding which Nusselt number correlation to use. The Reynolds number at the specified location is

\( Re =\frac{\left(\dot{V} / A_{c}\right) D}{v}=\frac{\left[\left(2.6 \times 10^{-4} m ^{3} / s \right)\left(1.961 \times 10^{-4} m ^{2}\right)\right](0.0158 m )}{3.12 \times 10^{-6} m ^{2} / s }=6714\)

since

\( A_{c}=\pi D^{2} / 4=\pi(0.0158 m )^{2} / 4=1.961 \times 10^{-4} m ^{2}\)

Therefore, the flow regime is in the transition region for all three inlet configurations (thus use the information given in Table (8-9) with x / D=90, and therefore Eq.(8-87)

\(Nu _{\text {tans }}= Nu _{\text {lam }}+\left\{\exp [(a- Re ) / b]+ Nu _{\text {turb }}^{c}\right\}^{c} \)

should be used with the constants\( a_{t} b, c\) found in Table 8-8. However, \( Nu _{\text {lam }}\) and \( Nu _{\text {turb }}\) are the inputs to Eq. (8-87), and they need to be evaluated first from Eqs. (8-88)

\(Nu _{ lam }=1.24\left[\left(\frac{ Re \operatorname{Pr} D}{x}\right)+0.025( Gr \operatorname{Pr})^{0.75}\right]^{1 / 3}\left(\frac{\mu_{b}}{\mu_{s}}\right)^{0.14} \)

and (8-89)

\(Nu _{ turb }=0.023 Re ^{0.8} Pr ^{0.385}\left(\frac{x}{D}\right)^{-0.0054}\left(\frac{\mu_{b}}{\mu_{s}}\right)^{0.14} \)

respectively. It should be mentioned that the correlations for \( Nu _{\text {lam }}\) and \( Nu _{\text {turb }}\) have no inlet dependency. From Eq.(8-88) :

\(Nu _{\text {lam }} =1.24\left[\left(\frac{ RePr D}{x}\right)+0.025( Gr \operatorname{Pr})^{0.75}\right]^{1 / 3}\left(\frac{\mu_{b}}{\mu_{s}}\right)^{0.14} =1.24\left[\left(\frac{(6714)(29.2)}{90}\right)+0.025[(51,770)(29.2)]^{0.75}\right]^{1 / 3}(1.77)^{0.14}=19.9\)

From Eq. 8–89:

\(Nu _{ turb } =0.023 Re ^{0.8} P r ^{0.385}\left(\frac{x}{D}\right)^{-0.0054}\left(\frac{\mu_{b}}{\mu_{s}}\right)^{0.14} =0.023(6714)^{0.8}(29.2)^{0.385}(90)^{-0.0054}(1.77)^{0.14}=102.7 \)

Then the transition Nusselt number can be determined from Eq.(8-87),

\( Nu _{\text {trans }}= Nu _{\text {lam }}+\left\{\exp [(a- Re ) / b]+ Nu _{\text {turb }}^{c}\right\}^{c}\)

Case 1 : For re-entrant inlet:

\( Nu _{\text {trans }}=19.9+\left\{\exp [(1766-6714) / 276]+102.7^{-0.955}\right\}^{-0.955}=88.2\)

 

Case 2: For square-edged inlet:

\( Nu _{\text {trans }}=19.9+\left\{\exp [(2617-6714) / 207]+102.7^{-0.950}\right\}^{-0.950}=85.3\)

 

Case 3: For bell-mouth inlet:

\( Nu _{\text {trans }}=19.9+\left\{\exp [(6628-6714) / 237]+102.7^{-0.980}\right\}^{-0.980}=21.3\)

 

Discussion It is worth mentioning that, for the re-entrant and square-edged inlets, the flow behaves normally. For the bell-mouth inlet, the Nusselt number is low in comparison to the other two inlets. This is because of the unusual behavior of the bell-mouth inlet noted earlier see Fig. (8-39) ; i.e., the boundary layer along the tube wall is at first laminar and then changes through a transition region to the turbulent condition.
