Guest Question.

Show that \({ }^{55} Fe\) may undergo electron capture, but not \(\beta^{+}\) decay.

Strategy The two possible reactions are

\({ }_{26}^{55} Fe \rightarrow{ }_{25}^{55} Mn +\beta^{+}+\nu \quad \beta^{+} \text {decay }\) \({ }_{26}^{55} Fe +e^{-} \rightarrow{ }_{25}^{55} Mn +\nu \quad \text { Electron capture }\)

We determine the disintegration energies Q for each reaction, Equations (12.41) and (12.44). The reactions are possible if Q is positive.

\(Q=\left[M\left({ }_{Z}^{A} X\right)-M\left({ }_{Z-1}^{\quad A} D\right)-2 m_{e}\right] c^{2} \quad \beta^{+} \text {decay }\) (12.41)

\(Q=\left[M\left({ }_{Z}^{A} X\right)-M\left({ }_{Z-1}^{\quad A} D\right)\right] c^{2} \quad \text { Electron capture }\) (12.44)

Admin
Solution

We first find the masses from Appendix 8 needed to determine the disintegration energy Q of Equations (12.41) and (12.44):

 

\(\begin{aligned}M\left({ }_{26}^{55} Fe \right) &=54.938298 u , M\left({ }_{25}^{55} Mn \right)=54.938050 u , \text { and } \\m_{e} &=0.000549 u .\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\beta ^{+}\) decay:

 

\(\begin{aligned}Q=&[54.938298 u -54.938050 u -2(0.000549 u )] c^{2} \\& \times\left(\frac{931.5 MeV }{c^{2} \cdot u }\right) \\=&-0.79 MeV \quad \beta^{+} \text {decay not allowed }\end{aligned}\)

 

Electron capture:

 

\(\begin{aligned}Q &=[54.938298 u -54.938050 u ] c^{2}\left(\frac{931.5 MeV }{c^{2} \cdot u }\right) \\&=0.23 MeV \quad \text { Electron capture is allowed }\end{aligned}\)

 

Our determination agrees with the experiment result that only electron capture is allowed, with \(t_{1 / 2}=2.7\) years.
