Guest Question.

A boiler is capable of generating 8 kg steam per kg of fuel at 400°C. Feed water is supplied at pressure of 30 bar, 40°C and leaves economiser at 150°C for entering to evaporator. Steam leaves evaporator with dryness fraction of 0.98 and enters the superheater. Fuel used has calorific value of 29000 kJ/kg. Considering no pressure loss inside the boiler determine boiler efficiency and fraction of heat given to steam in each section of boiler.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{From steam table; }h_{\text{f at 30 bar}} = 1008.42 kJ/kg, h_{\text{g at 30 bar}} = 2804.2 kJ/kg\\\begin{aligned}\text{Enthalpy of final steam generated}&= h_{\text{at 30 bar, 400°C}}\\&= 3230.9 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Heat supplied per kg of fuel = 1 × 29000 kJ/kg = 29000 kJ/kg}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Heat to be added for per kg steam generation}&= h_{\text{at 30 bar, 400°C}} – h_{\text{water at 40°C}}\\&= 3230.9 – 4.18(40)\\&= 3063.7 kJ/kg\\\text{Boiler efficiency} = \frac{8 × 3063.7}{29000}&= 0.8452 \approx 84.52\%\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Heat added in economiser per kg of steam generated}&= 4.18 × (150 – 40)\\&= 459.8 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\%\text{Fraction of heat in economiser}&= \frac{459.8}{3063.7} × 100\\&= 15.01\%\end{aligned}\\\text{Heat added in evaporator per kg of steam generated}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad&= (h_{\text{f at 30 bar}} + 0.98 × h_{\text{fg at 30 bar}}) – (4.18 × 150)\\&= 1008.42 + (0.98 × 1795.78) – 627\\&= 2141.28 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\%\text{Fraction of heat in evaporator } = \frac{2141.28}{3063.28} × 100 = 69.89\%\\\text{Heat added in super heater per kg of steam generated, by difference}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad&= (3063.7 – 459.8 – 2141.28)\\&= 462.62 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\%\text{Fraction of heat in superheater }= \frac{462.62}{3063.7} × 100 = 15.1\%\\\text{Boiler efficiency = 84.52}\%\\\%\text{fraction of heat in economiser, evaporator and superheater}\\ = 15.01\%, 69.89\% and 15.1\%\)
