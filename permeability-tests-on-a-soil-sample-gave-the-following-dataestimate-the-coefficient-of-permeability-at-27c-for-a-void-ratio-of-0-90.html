Guest Question.

Permeability tests on a soil sample gave the following data:

\(\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|}\hline \text { Test No. } & \text { Void ratio } & \text { Temperature }{ }^{\circ} C & \begin{array}{c}\text { Permeability in } 10^{-3} \\mm / s\end{array} \\\hline 1 & 0.63 & 20 & 0.36 \\2 & 1.08 & 36 & 1.80 \\\hline\end{array}\)
Admin
Solution
\(\left.\begin{array}{l}\text { Viscosity at } 20^{\circ} C =1.009 \times 10^{-9} N . sec / mm ^{2} \\\text { Viscosity at } 36^{\circ} C =0.706 \times 10^{-9} N . sec / mm ^{2} \\\text { Viscosity at } 27^{\circ} C =0.855 \times 10^{-9} N . sec / mm ^{2}\end{array}\right\} \text { Unit weight } \gamma=9.81 \times 10^{-6} N / mm ^{3}\)

According to Poisuelle’s equation adapted to flow through soil,

\(k=D_{s}{ }^{2} \cdot \frac{\gamma}{\mu} \cdot \frac{e^{3}}{(1+e)} \cdot C\)

From test 1,

\(\frac{0.36 \times 10^{-3}}{D_{s}{ }^{2} \cdot C}=\frac{9.81 \times 10^{-6}}{1009 \times 10^{-9}} \cdot \frac{(0.63)^{3}}{(163)}=1491.46\)

∴ \(D_{s}^{2} \cdot C=2.414 \times 10^{-7}\)

From test 2,

\(\frac{1.80 \times 10^{-3}}{D_{s}{ }^{2} \cdot C}=\frac{9.81 \times 10^{-6}}{0.706 \times 10^{-9}} \cdot \frac{(1.08)^{3}}{2.08}=8415.35\)

∴ \(D_{s}{ }^{2} \cdot C=2.139 \times 10^{-7}\)

Average value of \(D_{s}{ }^{2} \cdot C=2.2765 \times 10^{-7}\)

∴ At 27°C and a void ratio of 0.90,

\(k=2.2765 \times 10^{-7} \times \frac{9.81 \times 10^{-6}}{0.855 \times 10^{-9}} \cdot \frac{(0.90)^{3}}{19}=1.002 \times 10^{-3} mm / s\).
