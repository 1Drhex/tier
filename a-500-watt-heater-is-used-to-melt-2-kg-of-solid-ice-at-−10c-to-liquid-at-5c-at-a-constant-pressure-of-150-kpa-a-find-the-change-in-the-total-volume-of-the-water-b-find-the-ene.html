Guest Question.

A 500 Watt heater is used to melt 2 kg of solid ice at −10°C to liquid at +5°C at a
constant pressure of 150 kPa.
a) Find the change in the total volume of the water.
b) Find the energy the heater must provide to the water.
c) Find the time the process will take assuming uniform T in the water.

Admin
Solution

Take CV as the 2 kg of water. \(m _{2}= m _{1}= m\) ;

Energy Eq.3.5 \(m \left( u _{2}- u _{1}\right)={ }_{1} Q _{2}-{ }_{1} W _{2}\)

State 1: Compressed solid, take saturated solid at same temperature.

\(v = v _{ i }(-10)=0.0010891 m ^{3} / kg , h = h _{ i }=-354.09 kJ / kg\)

State 2: Compressed liquid, take saturated liquid at same temperature

\(v = v _{ f }=0.001, h = h _{ f }=20.98 kJ / kg\)

Change in volume:

\(V _{2}- V _{1}= m \left( v _{2}- v _{1}\right)=2(0.001-0.0010891)=- 0 . 0 0 0 1 7 8 m ^{3}\)

Work is done while piston moves at constant pressure, so we get

\({ }_{1} W _{2}=\int P d V =\text { area }= P \left( V _{2}- V _{1}\right)=-150 \times 0.000178=-0.027 kJ =-27 J\)

Heat transfer is found from energy equation

\({ }_{1} Q _{2}= m \left( u _{2}- u _{1}\right)+{ }_{1} W _{2}= m \left( h _{2}- h _{1}\right)=2 \times[20.98-(-354.09)]= 7 5 0 k J\)

The elapsed time is found from the heat transfer and the rate of heat transfer

\(t ={ }_{1} Q _{2} / \dot{ Q }=(750 kJ / 500 W ) \times 1000 J / kJ =1500 s = 2 5 min\)
