Guest Question.

A cylindrical soil sample prepared for laboratory testing has a diameter of 2.8 in., a height of 5.6 in., and weighs 2.4 lb. If the degree of saturation is 93% and the specific gravity of soil solids is 2.71, determine:

a. void ratio
b. water content
c. water content under fully saturated condition

Admin
Solution

Part a
Given:W = 2.4 lb; S = 93%;\(G_{s} = 2.71\)

Specimen volume, \(V = \frac{\pi }{4} (2.8)^2(5.6)(\frac{1}{12} )^3=0.02 ft^3\)

\(W = W_{w}+W_{s} \) (a)

We know

\(\gamma _{w}=\frac{W_{w} }{V_{w} } ;G_{s}=\frac{W_{s} }{V_{s}\gamma _{w} } ;S= \frac{V_{w} }{V_{v} };and e=\frac{V_{v} }{V_{s} } \)

Therefore, Eq. (a) becomes

\(W=\gamma _{w}V_{w} +G_{s}V_{s}\gamma _{w} =\gamma _{w}SV_{v} +G_{s}V_{s}\gamma _{w}= \gamma _{w}SeV_{v}+G_{s}V_{s}\gamma _{w}\)

or

\(W=(Se +G_{s} )\gamma _{w}V_{s} \) (b)

Since

\(e+1=\frac{V_{v}+V_{s} }{ V_{s}}=\frac{V}{V_{s}}\) (c)

Substituting \(V_{s}\) in Eq. (b), we get

\(w=\frac{\gamma _{w} V}{1+e}(se+G_{s} )\) (d)

Substituting the known values in Eq. (d),

\(2.4=\frac{(62.4)(0.02)}{1+e} = (0.93e+2.71)\)

Therefore,

e = 0.792

Part b
Using Eq. (c),

\(0.792 + 1=\frac{0.02}{V_{s} }\)

Therefore,\(V_{s} = 0.0111 ft^3\)

and

\(W_{s} =G_{s}V_{s}\gamma _{w}=(2.71)(0.0111)(62.4)=1.88 lb\)

 

\(W_{w} =W-W_{s}=2.4 – 1.88 = 0.52 lb \)

Water content is given by

\(w=\frac{W_{w} }{W_{s} } =\frac{0.52}{1.88}= \) 0.276 or 27.6%

Part c

\(V_{v} = V-V_{s}=0.02 – 0.0111 = 0.0089 ft^3 \)

For saturated conditions, \(V_{w} = V_{v}= 0.0089 ft^3 \)

Therefore,

\(W_{w}(saturated) = \gamma _{w} V_{w}= (62.4)(0.0089)=0.55 lb \)

 

\(w_{sat} = \frac{W_{w} }{W_{s} }=\frac{0.55}{1.88}=\)0.292 or 29.2%
