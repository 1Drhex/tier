Guest Question.

In Figure P17.85, calculate the minimum moment of inertia I required for the beam if the maximum beam deflection must not exceed 0.5 in. Assume that E = 29,000 ksi and employ the virtualwork method.

Admin
Solution

Virtual Moment m: The maximum beam deflection will occur at B. To determine the deflection of the beam at B, first remove the real loads from the beam and then apply a virtual unit load downward at B. The beam with this loading is shown.

 

 

Cut a free-body diagram through the beam at section a–a between A and B.
Derive a moment equation m from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{array}{rl}\Sigma M_{a-a} & =-(1 kip ) x-m=0 \\& \therefore m=-(1 kip ) x & 0 \leq x<15 ft\end{array}\)

 

 

Real Moment M: Remove the virtual load and reapply the real loads. Draw a free-body diagram that cuts through the beam at section a–a between A and B and derive a moment equation M from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{a-a} &=-\left(\frac{1.5 kips / ft }{2}\right) x^{2}-(75 kip – ft )-M=0 \\& \therefore M=-\left(\frac{1.5 kips / ft }{2}\right) x^{2}-(75 kip – ft ) \quad 0 \leq x<15 ft\end{aligned}\)

 

Virtual-Work Equation for Beam Deflection: From Equation (17.35), an expression for the beam deflection at B can now be developed.

\(\begin{aligned}(1 kip ) \cdot \Delta_{B} &=\int_{0}^{L} m\left(\frac{M}{E I}\right) d x \\&=\frac{1}{E I} \int_{0}^{15}(-1 x)\left[-\left(\frac{1.5}{2}\right) x^{2}-75\right] d x=\frac{0.75}{E I} \int_{0}^{15} x^{3} d x+\frac{75}{E I} \int_{0}^{15} x d x \\&=\frac{0.75}{4 E I}\left[x^{4}\right]_{0}^{15}+\frac{75}{2 E I}\left[x^{2}\right]_{0}^{15}=\frac{9,492.1875}{E I}+\frac{8,437.5}{E I} \\&=\frac{\left(17,929.6875 kip ^{2}- ft ^{3}\right)(12 in . / ft )^{3}}{E I}=\frac{30,982,500 kip ^{2}- in .^{3}}{E I}\end{aligned}\)

The maximum beam deflection must be limited to 0.5 in.

\(\Delta_{B}=\frac{30,982,500 kip ^{2}- in .^{3}}{E I} \leq 0.5 in.\)

Solving for the minimum moment of inertia gives

\(I \geq \frac{30,982,500 \text { kip-in. }^{3}}{(29,000 ksi )(0.5 in .)}=2,136.7241 in .{ }^{4}=2,140 in .^{4}\)
