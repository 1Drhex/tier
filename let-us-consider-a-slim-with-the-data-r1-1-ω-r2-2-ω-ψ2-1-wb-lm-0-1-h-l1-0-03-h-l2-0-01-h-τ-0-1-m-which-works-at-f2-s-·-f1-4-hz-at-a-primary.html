Guest Question.

Let us consider a SLIM with the data:\(R_{1}=1 \Omega , R^{'} _{2}=2\Omega ,Ψ^{'} _{2}=1 w b, L_{m}=0.1 H, L_{1/}=0.03 H, L^{'} _{2/}=0.01H,τ=0, 1 m \),which works at \(f_{2}=S\cdot f_{1}=4 Hz,\) at a primary frequency of 40 Hz.
Calculate:

1. The speed
2. IM, IT, and thrust
3.VM, \(V_{T}, V_{1}\) (RMS per phase), \(I_{1}\)(RMS per phase), the efficiency and power factor \((\eta _{1}, \cos φ_{1} )\)

 

Admin
Solution

1. The synchronous speed \(U_{s}=2\cdot τ\cdot f_{1}=2\cdot 0.1\cdot 40=8 m/s,\) while the LIM speed \(U=2\cdot τ\cdot (f_{1}-f_{2})=2\cdot 0.1\cdot (40-4)=7.2 m/s\).

2. From (4.51), \(I_{M}= \frac{Ψ^{'} _{2}}{L_{m} }=\frac{1}{0.1}=10A, I_{T}=i_{M}\cdot \frac{L^{'} _{2}}{R^{'} _{2}}\cdot s\cdot \omega _{1}\)

\(\bar{i} _{s}=\frac{Ψ^{'} _{2}}{L_{m} }+j\cdot s\cdot \omega _{1}\frac{\bar{Ψ}^{'} _{2}}{L_{m}}\cdot \frac{L^{'} _{2}}{R^{'} _{2}}=i_{M}+j\cdot i_{T}\) (4.51)

\(=10\cdot \frac{0.11}{2}\cdot 2\cdot \pi \cdot 4=13.81A(L^{'} _{2}=L_{m}+L^{'} _{2/}=0.1+0.01=0.11H )\)

The short circuit inductance \(L_{sc}(4.54)\) is \(L_{sc}=L_{1}-L^{2}_{m}/L^{'} _{2}=0.13-0.1^{2}/0.11=0.0391H\).

\(L_{sc}=L_{1}-L^{2}_{m}/L^{'} _{2}\) (4.54)

So the thrust (4.53) is

\(F_{x}=\frac{-3}{2}\cdot \frac{\pi }{τ}\cdot \text{Imag}(\overline{\Psi} _1\ast \overline{i}_1 )=\frac{-3}{2}\cdot \frac{\pi }{τ}\cdot (L_{1}-L_{sc} )\cdot I_{M}\cdot I_{T}\) (4.53)

\(F_{x}=\frac{-3}{2}\cdot \frac{\pi }{τ}\cdot (L_{1}-L_{sc} )\cdot I_{M}\cdot I_{T}=-\frac{\pi }{0.1}\cdot \frac{3}{2}\cdot (0.13-0.0391)\cdot 10\cdot 13.816=-591.5N\)

3. \(V_{M}\) and \(V_{T}\) from (4.56) are

\(V_{M}=R_{1}\cdot I_{M}-\omega _{1}\cdot L_{sc}\cdot I_{T}\) (4.56)

\(V_{T}=R_{1}\cdot I_{T}-\omega _{1}\cdot L_{1}\cdot I_{M}\)

\(V_{M}=1.10-2\cdot \pi \cdot 10\cdot 0.0394\cdot 13.816=-126V\)

\(V_{T}=1\cdot 13.816+2\cdot \pi \cdot 40\cdot 0.13\cdot 10=340.37V\)

\(V_{1}=\sqrt{\frac{V^{2}_{M}+V^{2}_{T} }{2} }=256V\) (RM S perphase)

\(I_{1}=\sqrt{\frac{I^{2}_{M}+I^{2}_{T} }{2} }=12.06A\) (RM S perphase)

The\(\eta _{1}\cos φ_{1}\)(with zero core losses and mechanical losses) is

\(\eta _{1}\cos φ_{1}=\frac{F_{x}\cdot U }{3\cdot V_{1}\cdot I_{1}}=\frac{591.5\times 7.2}{3\times 256\times 12.06}=0.4598 \)

And efficiency \(\eta _{1}\):

\(\eta _{1}=\frac{F_{x}\cdot U}{F_{x}\cdot U+3\cdot R^{'}_{2}\cdot I^{'2}_{2}+3\cdot R^{'}_{1}\cdot I^{'2}_{1}} \)

\(I^{'}_{2}\sqrt{2}=\frac{-L_{m}\cdot I_{T}}{L^{'}_{2}}(\bar{\Psi }^{'}_{2T}=0 )\) and thus \(I^{'}_{2}=\frac{0.1}{0.11}\cdot \frac{13.816}{\sqrt{2}}=8.907A\) (RM S/phase).

So

\(\eta _{1}=\frac{591.5\cdot 7.2}{591.5\cdot 7.2+3\cdot 2\cdot 8.907^{2}+3\cdot 1\cdot 13.816^{2}}=0.8\)

Finally, the power factor \(\cos φ_{1}\) =0.4598/0.8=0.573.

Note: The results show acceptable efficiency but rather low power factor; only with a cage secondary and rather small airgap (1–1.5 mm), power factor values above 0.7 can be hoped for.
