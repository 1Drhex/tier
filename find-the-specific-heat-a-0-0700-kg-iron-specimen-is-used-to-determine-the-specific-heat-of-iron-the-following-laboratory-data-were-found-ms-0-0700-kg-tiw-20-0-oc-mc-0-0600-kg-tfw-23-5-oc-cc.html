Guest Question.

Find the specific heat. A 0.0700-kg iron specimen is used to determine the specific heat of iron. The following laboratory data were found:

\( m_s = 0.0700 kg t_{iw} = 20.0 ºC\)

 

\( m_c = 0.0600 kg t_{fw} = 23.5 ºC\)

 

\( c_c = 900 J/kg ºC m_w = 0.150 kg\)

 

 

\( t_s = 100 ºC\)

Find the specific heat of the specimen.

Admin
Solution

The specific heat of the iron specimen, found from equation 14.13, is

\(c_s=\frac{m_wc_w(t_{fw} − t_{iw}) + m_cc_c(t_{fw} − t_{iw})}{m_s(100 − t_{fw})}\)

 

\(=\frac{(0.150 kg)(4186 J/kg ºC)(23.5 ºC − 20.0 ºC)+(0.0600 kg)(900 J/kg ºC)(23.5 ºC − 20 ºC)}{(0.0700 kg)(100 ºC − 23.5 ºC)}\)

= 446 J/kg ºC

which is in good agreement with the accepted value of the specific heat of iron of 452 J/kg ºC
