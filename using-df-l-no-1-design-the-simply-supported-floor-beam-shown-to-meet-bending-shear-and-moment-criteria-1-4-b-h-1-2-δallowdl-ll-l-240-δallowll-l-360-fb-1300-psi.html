Guest Question.

Using DF-L No. 1, design the simply supported floor beam shown to meet bending, shear, and moment criteria.

1/4 < b/h < 1/2

\(Δ_{ allow_{( DL + LL )} } \) = L/240; \(Δ_{ allow_{( LL )} } \) = L/360

\(F_{ b }\)= 1,300 psi; \(F_{v }\) = 85 psi; E = 1.6 × \(10^{6}\) psi

Admin
Solution

Bending

\(M_{\max }=\frac{\omega L^{2}}{8}=\frac{200 \# / ft.\left(20^{\prime}\right)^{2}}{8}=10,000 \#- ft.\)

 

\(S_{\text {required }}=\frac{M_{\max }}{F_{ b }}=\frac{10 k – ft . \times 12 . \text { in. } / ft .}{1.3 \times 10^{3} k / in .^{2}}=92.3 in .{ }^{3}\)

 

Shear

\(V_{\max }=\frac{ωL}{2}=\frac{200 \# / ft \cdot\left(20^{\prime}\right)}{2}=2,000 \#\)

 

\(A_{\text {required }}=\frac{1.5 V_{\max }}{F_{ v }}=\frac{1.5 \times(2,000 \#)}{85 \# / in .^{2}}\)=35.3 in.²

 

Deflection (Allowable)

\(Δ_{\text {allow }_{( DL + LL )}}=\frac{L}{240}=\frac{20^{\prime} \times 12 in . / ft .}{240}= \)1″

or

\(Δ_{\text {allow }_{(L L)}}=\frac{L}{360}=\frac{20^{\prime} \times 12 \text { in. } / ft .}{360}\)=0.67″

Note that the calculated \(S_{\text {required }}\) and \( A_{\text {required }} \) values do not account for the beam’s own weight.

Try 6 × 12 S4S.

(A = 63.25 in.²; \(S_{x}\) = 121.23 in.³ ; \(I_{x}=697.07 in.^{4} \))

Economical (efficient) beams usually have width-to-depth (b/h) ratios of \(\frac{1}{4}<b / h<\frac{1}{2}\).

Check the effect of the beam’s weight as it affects the bending and shear stress condition.

Bending

\(S_{\text {add. }}=\frac{M_{\text {add }}}{F_{ b }}\)

where \(M_{\text {add }}\) = additional bending moment due to the beam’s weight.

\(M_{\text {add. }}=\frac{ω_{\text {beam }} L^{2}}{8}\)

Conversion for wood density of 35 pcf (Douglas fir and Southern Pine) to pounds per lineal foot (plf) of beam is

\(ω_{\text {beam }}\)=0.252 × cross-sectional area of beam

\(ω_{\text {beam }}\)=0.252 × 63.25 = 16 plf

\(\therefore M_{\text {add. }}=\frac{16 \# / ft \cdot\left(20^{\prime}\right)^{2}}{8}=800 \# \text { -ft. }\)

 

\(S_{\text {add. }}=\frac{M_{\text {add. }}}{F_{ b }}=\frac{800 \# ft . \times 12 \text { in. } / ft. ^{2}}{1,300 psi }=7.4 in .^{3}\)

 

\(\therefore S_{\text {total }}\)=92.3 in.³ + \( S_{\text {add. }}\) = 92.3 in.³ + 7.4 in.³

\(S_{\text {total }}=99.7 \text { in. }^{3}<121.2 \text { in. }^{3} \therefore \text { OK }\)

Shear

\(V_{\text {add. }}\)=additional shear developed due to the beam’s weight

\(\therefore V_{\text {add. }}=\frac{ω_{\text {beam }} L}{2}=\frac{16 \# / ft .\left(20^{\prime}\right)}{2}=160 \#\)

 

\(A_{ add .}=\frac{1.5 V _{ add. }}{F_{ v }}=\frac{1.5 \times 160 \#}{85 psi }=2.8 in .{ }^{2}\)

 

\(\therefore A_{\text {total }}=35.3 in .^{2}+A_{\text {add. }}=35.3 \text { in. }^{2}+2.8 \text { in. }^{2} \)

 

\(A_{\text {total }}=38.1 in .^{2}<63.25 in .^{2} \therefore OK\)

Deflection (Actual)

\(\Delta_{\text {actual }}=\frac{5 \omega_{L L} L^{4}}{384 E I}=\frac{5(100 \# / ft .)\left(20^{\prime}\right)^{4}\left(1,728 in \cdot^{3} / ft .^{3}\right)}{384\left(1.6 \times 10^{6} psi \right)\left(697.1 in .^{4}\right)}=0.32^{\prime \prime}\)

 

\(\Delta_{\text {actual }_{( LL )}}=0.32^{\prime \prime}<\Delta_{\text {allow }_{( LL )}}=0.67^{\prime \prime}\)

 

\(\Delta_{\text {actual }}=\frac{5 \omega_{\text {total }} L^{4}}{384 E I}=\frac{5(216 \# / ft .)\left(20^{\prime}\right)^{4}\left(1,728 in .^{3} / ft. ^{3}\right)}{384\left(1.6 \times 10^{6} psi \right)\left(697.1 in .^{4}\right)}=0.7^{\prime \prime}\)

Note:\(ω_{\text {total }}=216 \) #/ft. includes the beam weight.

\(\Delta_{\text {actual }_{( DL + LL )}}=0.7^{\prime \prime}<\Delta_{\text {actual }_{( DL + LL )}}=1^{\prime \prime}\)

 

\(\therefore \text { OK } \quad \text { Use: } 6 \times 12 \text { S4S. }\)
