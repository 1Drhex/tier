Guest Question.

Total Heat Rate and Effectiveness Calculation. If the tube described in had a length of one meter and fin pitch (S) of 10 mm, what would be the total surface area for heat transfer, the exposed tube base total heat transfer rate, the overall efficiency of the surface, and the overall surface effectiveness?

Admin
Solution

Calculate the number of fins in the tube length:

\(N_f=\frac{L}{S}=\frac{1}{0.01}=100\ fins;\ \ \ S=10\ mm=0.01\ m\)

Calculate the unfinned base area:

\(L_{be}\) = unfinned (exposed) base length = \(L – N_fz_{f,t}\)

= 1 – (100)(0.001) = 0.9 m

\(A_{be}\) = unfinned base area = 2π\(r_oL_{be}\)

= 2π(0.0125)(0.9) = 0.0707 \(m^2\)

The total transfer surface area, \(A_t\) , may now be calculated:

\(A_t = A_{be} + N_fA_f\)

= 0.0707 + (100)(3.94×\(10^{-3}\) )= 0.465 \(m^2\)

Equation (8.22) is used to obtain the total heat rate without fins:

\(Q_{t,w/o,f}=h(2\pi r_oX)\theta _b\) (8.22)

= (130)(2π)(0.0125)(1)(145) = 1480 W

Calculate the heat flow rate from the exposed tube base:

\(Q_{be} = hA_{be}θ_b\)

= (130)(0.0707)(145) = 1332.7 W = 4548 Btu/hr

Calculate the heat flow rate from all the fins:

\(Q_{f,total} = N_fQ_f\)

= (100)(64) = 6400 W

The total heat flow rate may now be calculated:

\(Q_t=Q_{be}+Q_{f,total}\)

= 1332.7 + 6400 = 7732.7 W

Calculate the maximum heat transfer rate from Equation (8.24):

\(Q_{t,max}=hA_t\theta _b\) (8.24)

= (130)(0.465)(145) = 8765.3 W

By definition, the overall fin efficiency is given in Equation (8.25):

\(\eta _{o,f}=\frac{Q_t}{Q_{t,max}}\) (8.25)

\(=\frac{7732.7}{8765.3}\) = 0.882 = 88.2%

The corresponding overall effectiveness is therefore given by Equation (8.27):

\(\varepsilon _{o,f}=\frac{Q_t}{Q_{t,w/o,f}}\) (8.27)

Substitution,

\(=\frac{7732.7}{1480}=5.22\)
