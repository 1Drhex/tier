Guest Question.

Determine the member end moments and reactions for the frame shown in Fig. \( 15.13 (\mathrm{a})\) by the slope-deflection method.

Admin
Solution

Degrees of Freedom. The joints \( C, D\) , and \( E\) of the frame are free to rotate. However, we will eliminate the rotation of the simple support at end \( E\) by using the modified slope-deflection equations for member \( D E\) . Thus the analysis will involve only two unknown joint rotations, \( \theta_{C}\) and \( \theta_{D}\) .

Fixed-End Moments. By using the fixed-end moment expressions given inside the back cover of the book, we obtain

\( \begin{array}{l} \mathrm{FEM}_{A C}=\frac{40(20)}{8}=100 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowleft \quad \text { or } \quad+100 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \\ \mathrm{FEM}_{C A}=100 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowright \quad \text { or } \quad-100 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \\ \mathrm{FEM}_{B D}=\mathrm{FEM}_{D B}=0 \\ \mathrm{FEM}_{C D}=\mathrm{FEM}_{D E}=\frac{2(30)^{2}}{12}=150 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowleft \quad \text { or } \quad+150 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \\ \mathrm{FEM}_{D C}=\mathrm{FEM}_{E D}=150 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowright \quad \text { or } \quad-150 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \end{array} \)

Slope-Deflection Equations. As indicated in Fig. 15.13(a), the moments of inertia of the columns and the girders of the frame are \( 800 \mathrm{in} .^{4}\) and 1,600 in. \( ^{4}\) , respectively. Using \( I=I_{\text {column }}=800 \mathrm{in} .^{4}\) as the reference moment of inertia, we express \( I_{\text {girder }}\) in terms of \( I\) as

\( I_{\text {girder }}=1,600=2(800)=2 I \)

Next, we write the slope-deflection equations by applying Eq. (15.9) to members \( A C, B D\) , and \( C D\) , and Eqs. (15.15) to member \( D E\) . Thus

\( M_{n f}=\frac{2 E I}{L}\left(2 \theta_{n}+\theta_{f}-3 \psi\right)+\mathrm{FEM}_{n f} \) (15.9)

\( \begin{array}{c} M_{r h}=\frac{3 E I}{L}\left(\theta_{r}-\psi\right)+\left(\mathrm{FEM}_{r h}-\frac{\mathrm{FEM}_{h r}}{2}\right) \\\end{array} \) (15.15a)

\( \begin{array}{c} M_{h r}=0 \\\end{array} \) (15.15b)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{A C}=\frac{2 E I}{20}\left(\theta_{C}\right)+100=0.1 E I \theta_{C}+100 \\\end{array} \) (1)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{C A}=\frac{2 E I}{20}\left(2 \theta_{C}\right)-100=0.2 E I \theta_{C}-100 \\\end{array} \) (2)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{B D}=\frac{2 E I}{20}\left(\theta_{D}\right)=0.1 E I \theta_{D} \\\end{array} \) (3)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{D B}=\frac{2 E I}{20}\left(2 \theta_{D}\right)=0.2 E I \theta_{D} \\\end{array} \) (4)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{C D}=\frac{2 E(2 I)}{30}\left(2 \theta_{C}+\theta_{D}\right)+150=0.267 E I \theta_{C}+0.133 E I \theta_{D}+150 \\\end{array} \) (5)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{D C}=\frac{2 E(2 I)}{30}\left(2 \theta_{D}+\theta_{C}\right)-150=0.133 E I \theta_{C}+0.267 E I \theta_{D}-150 \\\end{array} \) (6)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{D E}=\frac{3 E(2 I)}{30}\left(\theta_{D}\right)+\left(150+\frac{150}{2}\right)=0.2 E I \theta_{D}+225 \\\end{array} \) (7)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{E D}=0 \end{array} \)

Equilibrium Equations. By applying the moment equilibrium equation, \( \sum M=0\) , to the free bodies of joints \( C\) and \( D\) (Fig. 15.13(b)), we obtain the equilibrium equations

\( \begin{array}{r} M_{C A}+M_{C D}=0 \\\end{array} \) (8)

\( \begin{array}{r} M_{D B}+M_{D C}+M_{D E}=0 \end{array} \) (9)

Joint Rotations. Substitution of the slope-deflection equations into the equilibrium equations yields

\( \begin{array}{l} 0.467 E I \theta_{C}+0.133 E I \theta_{D}=-50 \\\end{array} \) (10)

\( \begin{array}{l} 0.133 E I \theta_{C}+0.667 E I \theta_{D}=-75 \end{array} \) (11)

By solving Eqs. (10) and (11) simultaneously, we determine the values of \( E I \theta_{C}\) and \( E I \theta_{D}\) to be

\( \begin{array}{l} E I \theta_{C}=-79.545 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}^{2} \\ E I \theta_{D}=-96.591 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}^{2} \end{array} \)

Member End Moments. The member end moments can now be computed by substituting the numerical values of \( E I \theta_{C}\) and \( E I \theta_{D}\) into the slope-deflection equations (Eqs. (1) through (7)).

\( \begin{array}{lll}M_{A C}=92 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowleft \\ M_{C A}=-115.9 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \quad\text { or } \quad 115.9 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \curvearrowright \\ M_{B D}=-9.7 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \quad \text { or } \quad 9.7 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowright \\ M_{D B}=-19.3 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \quad \text { or } \quad 19.3 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowright \\M_{C D}=115.9 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowleft \\ M_{D C}=-186.4 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \quad \text { or } \quad 186.4 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowright \\M_{D E}=205.7 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowleft &\end{array}\)

To check that the solution of the simultaneous equations (Eqs. (10) and (11)) has been carried out correctly, we substitute the numerical values of member end moments back into the equilibrium equations (Eqs. (8) and (9)) to obtain

\( \begin{array}{cl}M_{C A}+M_{C D}=-115.9+115.9=0 &\\ M_{D B}+M_{D C}+M_{D E}=-19.3-186.4+205.7=0 & \end{array}\)

Member End Shears. The member end shears, obtained by considering the equilibrium of each member, are shown in Fig. 15.13(c).

Member Axial Forces. With end shears known, member axial forces can now be evaluated by considering the equilibrium of joints \( C\) and \( D\) in order. The axial forces thus obtained are shown in Fig. 15.13(c).

Support Reactions. See Fig. 15.13(d).

Equilibrium Check. The equilibrium equations check.
