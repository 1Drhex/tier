Guest Question.

For the beam and loading shown, use discontinuity functions to compute the deflection of the beam at D. Assume a constant value of EI = 1,750 \(\text { kip-ft }^{2}\) for the beam.

Admin
Solution

Support reactions: A FBD of the beam is shown to the right.

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{A}=&-(5 kips )(4 ft )-(3 kips )(13 ft )+C_{y}(10 ft )=0 \\& \therefore C_{y}=5.90 kips \\\Sigma F_{y}=& A_{y}+C_{y}-5 kips -3 kips =0 \\& \therefore A_{y}=2.10 kips\end{aligned}\)

Load function w(x):

\(w(x)=2.10 kips \langle x-0 ft \rangle^{-1}-5 kips \langle x-4 ft \rangle^{-1}+5.90 kips \langle x-10 ft \rangle^{-1}-3 kips \langle x-13 ft \rangle^{-1}\)

Shear-force function V(x) and bending-moment function M(x):

\(\begin{aligned}&V(x)=2.10 kips \langle x-0 ft \rangle^{0}-5 kips \langle x-4 ft \rangle^{0}+5.90 kips \langle x-10 ft \rangle^{0}-3 kips \langle x-13 ft \rangle^{0} \\&M(x)=2.10 kips \langle x-0 ft \rangle^{1}-5 kips \langle x-4 ft \rangle^{1}+5.90 kips \langle x-10 ft \rangle^{1}-3 \operatorname{kips}\langle x-13 ft \rangle^{1}\end{aligned}\)

Equations for beam slope and beam deflection:
From Eq. (10.1), we can write:

\(E I \frac{d^{2} v}{d x^{2}}=M(x)=2.10 kips \langle x-0 ft \rangle^{1}-5 kips \langle x-4 ft \rangle^{1}+5.90 kips \langle x-10 ft \rangle^{1}-3 kips \langle x-13 ft \rangle^{1}\)

Integrate the moment function to obtain an expression for the beam slope:

\(\begin{aligned}E I \frac{d v}{d x} &=\frac{2.10 kips }{2}\langle x-0 ft \rangle^{2}-\frac{5 kips }{2}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{2} \\&+\frac{5.90 kips }{2}\langle x-10 ft \rangle^{2}-\frac{3 kips }{2}\langle x-13 ft \rangle^{2}+C_{1} (a)\end{aligned}\)

Integrate again to obtain the beam deflection function:

\(\begin{aligned}E I v &=\frac{2.10 \text { kips }}{6}\langle x-0 ft \rangle^{3}-\frac{5 kips }{6}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{3} \\&+\frac{5.90 kips }{6}\langle x-10 ft \rangle^{3}-\frac{3 kips }{6}\langle x-13 ft \rangle^{3}+C_{1} x+C_{2} (b)\end{aligned}\)

Evaluate constants using boundary conditions: Boundary conditions are specific values of deflection v or slope dv/dx that are known at particular locations along the beam span. For this beam, the deflection v is known at the pin support (x = 0 ft) and at the roller support (x = 10 ft). Substitute the boundary condition v = 0 at x = 0 ft into Eq. (b) to obtain:

\(C_{2}=0\)

Next, substitute the boundary condition v = 0 at x = 10 ft into Eq. (b) to obtain:

\(\begin{aligned}E I v &=\frac{2.10 kips }{6}(10 ft )^{3}-\frac{5 kips }{6}(6 ft )^{3}+C_{1}(10 ft )=0 \\& \therefore C_{1}=-17 kip – ft ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

The beam slope and elastic curve equations are now complete:

\(\begin{aligned}E I \frac{d v}{d x} &=\frac{2.10 kips }{2}\langle x-0 ft \rangle^{2}-\frac{5 \text { kips }}{2}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{2} \\&+\frac{5.90 kips }{2}\langle x-10 ft \rangle^{2}-\frac{3 \text { kips }}{2}\langle x-13 ft \rangle^{2}-17 kip – ft ^{2} (c)\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}E I v &=\frac{2.10 kips }{6}\langle x-0 ft \rangle^{3}-\frac{5 kips }{6}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{3} \\&+\frac{5.90 kips }{6}\langle x-10 ft \rangle^{3}-\frac{3 \text { kips }}{6}\langle x-13 ft \rangle^{3}-\left(17 kip – ft ^{2}\right) x (d)\end{aligned}\)

 

Beam deflection at D: At the tip of the overhang where x = 13 ft, the beam deflection is:

\(\begin{aligned}E I v_{D} &=\frac{2.10 kips }{6}(13 ft )^{3}-\frac{5 kips }{6}(9 ft )^{3}+\frac{5.90 kips }{6}(3 ft )^{3}-\left(17 kip – ft ^{2}\right)(13 ft ) \\&=-33.000 kip – ft ^{3} \\& \therefore v_{D}=-\frac{33.000 kip – ft ^{3}}{1,750 kip – ft ^{2}}=-0.018857 ft =0.226 \text { in. } \downarrow\end{aligned}\)
