Guest Question.

Butane is burned with 200% theoretical air, and the products of combustion, an equilibrium mixture containing only \(CO _{2}, H _{2} O , O _{2}, N _{2}, NO\), and \(NO _{2}\), exit from the combustion chamber at 1400 K, 2 MPa. Determine the equilibrium composition at this state.

Admin
Solution

Combustion:

\(C _{4} H _{10}+13 O _{2}+48.9 N _{2} \rightarrow 4 CO _{2}+5 H _{2} O +6.5 O _{2}+48.9 N _{2}\)

Dissociation:

1) \(N _{2}+ O _{2} \Leftrightarrow 2 NO\) 2) \(N _{2}+2 O _{2} \Leftrightarrow 2 NO _{2}\)

change -a -a +2a change -b -2b +2b

At equilibrium:

\(\begin{array}{lll}n _{ H 2 O }=5 & n _{ N 2}=48.9- a – b & n _{ NO }=2 a \\n _{ CO 2}=4 & n _{ O 2}=6.5- a -2 b & n _{ NO 2}=2 b \\& & n _{ TOT }=64.4- b\end{array}\)

 

At 1400 K, from A.11: \(K _{1}=3.761 \times 10^{-6}, K _{2}=9.026 \times 10^{-10}\)

\(K _{1}=\frac{(2 a )^{2}}{(48.9- a – b )(6.5- a -2 b )} ; \quad K _{2}=\frac{(2 b )^{2}(64.4- b )}{(6.5- a -2 b )^{2}(48.9- a – b )}\left(\frac{ P }{ P ^{0}}\right)^{-1}\)

As \(K _{1}\) and \(K _{2}\) are both very small, with \(K _{2}<< K _{1}\), the unknowns a & b will both be very small, with b << a. From the equilibrium eq.s, for a first trial

\(a \sim \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{ K _{1} \times 48.9 \times 6.5} \sim 0.0173 ; b \sim \frac{1}{2} \times 6.5 \sqrt{ K _{2} \times \frac{2}{0.1} \times \frac{48.9}{64.4}} \sim 0.00038\)

Then by trial & error,

\(\frac{a^{2}}{(48.9-a-b)(6.5-a-2 b)}=\frac{3.761 \times 10^{-6}}{4}=0.94025 \times 10^{-6}\)

 

\(\frac{b^{2}(64.4-b)}{(6.5-a-2 b)^{2}(48.9-a-b)}=\frac{9.026 \times 10^{-10} \times\left(\frac{2}{0.1}\right)}{4}=45.13 \times 10^{-10}\)

 

Solving, a = 0.017 27, b = 0.000 379

\(\begin{array}{llll}n _{ CO 2}=4, & n _{ H 2 O }=5, & n _{ N 2}=48.882, & n _{ O 2}=6.482, \\y _{ CO 2}=0.06211, & y _{ H 2 O }=0.07764, & y _{ N 2}=0.75904, & y _{ O 2}=0.10065\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{array}{ll}n _{ NO }=0.03454, & n _{ NO 2}=0.00076 \\y _{ NO }=0.00055, & y _{ NO 2}=0.00001\end{array}\)
