Guest Question.

The following data refer to a four-coupled wheel locomotive with two inside cylinders, as shown in Fig.12.23.
Reciprocating mass per cylinder = 300 kg
Revolving mass per cylinder = 250 kg
Diameter of the driving wheel = 1.9 m
Revolving parts for each coupling rod crank = 120 kg
Engine crank radius = 0.3 m
Coupling rod crank radius = 0.25 m.

Distance of centre of balances mass in the planes of the driving wheels from the axle centre = 0.75 m
Angle between the engine cranks = 90°.
Angle between the coupling rod crank with adjacent engine crank = 180°
The balance mass required for the reciprocating parts is equally divided between each pair of coupled wheels. Determine
(a) the magnitude and position of the balance mass required to balance 2/3rd of reciprocating and the complete revolving parts.
(b) The hammer blow and
(c) The maximum variation of tractive force when the locomotive speed is 75 km/h.

Admin
Solution

Leading wheels

\( \text { Balance mass }=250+0.5 \times 2 / 3 \times 300=350 kg \).

\( \text { Take } 2 \text { as reference plane with } \theta_{3}=0^{\circ} \).

\( \text { From Table 12.14, we have } \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}=75 \).

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}=75 \).

\( \sum M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}=58.5 \).

\( \sum M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}=61.5 \).

\( \left[\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}\right)^{2}+\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5}=M_{5} r_{5} l_{5} \).

\( \left[(58.5)^{2}+(61.5)^{2}\right]^{0.5}=1.2 M_{5} \).

\( M_{5}=\frac{84.88}{1.2}=70.73 kg \).

\( \tan \theta_{5}=\frac{-\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}}{-\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}}=\frac{-61.5}{-58.5}=1.0513 \).

\( \theta_{5}=180^{\circ}+46.43^{\circ}=226.43^{\circ} ccw \).

From Table 12.15, we have

\( \sum M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}=0 \).

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}=0 \).

\( \Sigma M r l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}=21 \).

\( \sum M r l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}=-21 \).

\( \left[\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}\right)^{2}+\left(\Sigma M_{i} r l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5}=M_{5} r_{5} l_{5} \).

\( \left[(21)^{2}+(-21)^{2}\right]^{0.5}=1.2 M_{5} \).

\( M_{5}=\frac{29.698}{1.2}=24.75 kg \).

\( \tan \theta_{5}=\frac{-\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}}{-\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}}=\frac{-(-21)}{-(21)}=-1 \).

\( \theta_{5}=180^{\circ}-45^{\circ}=135 ccw \).

\( \text { Since the numerator is positive and denominator is negative, therefore } \theta_{5} \text { lies in the second quadrant. } \)

By symmetry,

\( M_{2}=24.75 kg \).

\( \tan \theta_{2}=\frac{-(21)}{-(-21)}=-1 \).

\( \theta_{2}=360^{\circ}-45^{\circ}=315^{\circ} \).

\( \text { Since the numerator is negative and denominator is positive, therefore } \theta_{2} \text { lies in the fourth quadrant. } \).

(b) \( v=75 \times \frac{1000}{3600}=20.83 m / s \).

\( \omega=v / r=\frac{20.83}{0.95}=21.93 rad / s \).

\( \text { Neglecting } M \text {, and } M_{6} \text {, we have }\)

\( 1.2 M_{5}=\left[15^{2}+33^{2}\right]^{0.5}=36.249 \).

\( M_{5}=30.2 kg \).

Hammer blow \( =M_{5} b \omega^{2}=30.2 \times 0.75 \times(21.93)^{2}=10893 N \).

\( \text { (c) Maximum variation in tractive effort }=\pm \sqrt{2}(1-c) R \omega^{2} r \).

\( =\pm \sqrt{2}(1-2 / 3) \times 300 \times(21.93)^{2} \times 0.3=\pm 20404 N \).
