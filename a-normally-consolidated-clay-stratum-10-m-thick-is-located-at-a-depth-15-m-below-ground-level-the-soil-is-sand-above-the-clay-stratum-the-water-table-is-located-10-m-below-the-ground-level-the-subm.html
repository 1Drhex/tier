Guest Question.

A normally consolidated clay stratum 10 m thick is located at a depth 15 m below ground level. The soil is sand above the clay stratum. The water table is located 10 m below the ground level. The submerged unit weight of the sand \(\left(\gamma _{sub} \right)\) is \(12 kN/m^{3}\) and the unit weight is \(18 kN/m^{3}\) above the water table. The increase in pressure at the center of the clay stratum is \(150 kN/m^{2}\) due to the weight of a building. Estimate the expected ultimate settlement of the structure due to primary consolidation.

Properties of the clay layer:

Natural moisture content: 45%

Liquid limit: 52%

Specific gravity: 2.77

Admin
Solution

The ground profile is shown in Figure 6.7.

Settlement for normally consolidated soil:

\(S_{c}= \frac{c_{c} }{1+e_{0} } H \log \left\lgroup\frac{\sigma ^{\mid }_{1} }{\sigma ^{\mid }_{1} } \right\rgroup\)

\(c_{c} =0.009 \left(w_{L}-10 \right)\) from Eq. 6.5

\(c_{c} =0.009 \left(52-10 \right)\)

 

\(c_{c}=0.38\)

Clay

\(e=wG=0.45\times 2.77=1.25\) \(\gamma _{sat} =\left\lgroup \frac{G+e}{1+e} \right\rgroup \gamma _{W} \)

\(\gamma _{sat} =\left\lgroup \frac{2.77+1.25}{1+1.25} \right\rgroup 9.81\)

 

\(\gamma _{sat}=17.52 kN/m^{3} \)

Atz=20 m from GL:

Initial condition:

\(\sigma _{0} ^{\prime } =\left(10\times 18\right) +\left(12\times 5\right) +\left(\left(17.52-9.81\right)\times 5 \right)\)

 

\(\sigma _{0} ^{\prime }=278.55 kPa\)

Considering the pressure from the weight of the building:\

\(\sigma _{0} ^{\prime } =\left(10\times 18\right) +\left(12\times 5\right) +\left(\left(17.52-9.81\right)\times 5 \right) +150\)

 

\(\sigma _{0} ^{\prime }=240+188.55\)

 

\(\sigma _{0} ^{\prime }=428.55 kPa\)

 

\(S_{c} =\frac{0.38}{1+1.25}\times 10\times \log \left\lgroup \frac{428.55}{278.55} \right\rgroup \)

 

\(S_{c} = 0.32 m\)
