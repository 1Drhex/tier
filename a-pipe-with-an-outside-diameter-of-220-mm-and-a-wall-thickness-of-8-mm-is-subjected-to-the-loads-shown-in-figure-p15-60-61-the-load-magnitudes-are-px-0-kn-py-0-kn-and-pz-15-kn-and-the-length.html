Guest Question.

A pipe with an outside diameter of 220 mm and a wall thickness of 8 mm is subjected to the loads shown in Figure P15.60/61. The load magnitudes are \(P_{x}\) = 0 kN, \(P_{y}\) = 0 kN and \(P_{z}\) = 15 kN, and the length dimensions are a = 1.9 m and b = 1.3 m. The pipe contains an internal pressure of 2,000 kPa. Determine the normal and shear stresses on the outer surface of the pipe
(a) at point H.
(b) at point K.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&d=220 mm -2(8 mm )=204 mm \\&A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[(220 mm )^{2}-(204 mm )^{2}\right]=5,328.141 mm ^{2} \\&J=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[(220 mm )^{4}-(204 mm )^{4}\right]=59.9522 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4} \\&I_{y}=I_{z}=\frac{\pi}{64}\left[(220 mm )^{4}-(204 mm )^{4}\right]=29.9761 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Equivalent forces at H and K: Equivalent moments at H and K:

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{x}=0 kN =0 N \\&F_{y}=0 kN =0 N \\&F_{z}=15 kN =15,000 N\end{aligned}\) \(\begin{aligned}M_{x} &=(15 kN )(1.3 m )=19.5 kN – m =19.5 \times 10^{6} N – mm \\M_{y} &=-(15 kN )(1.9 m )=-28.5 kN – m =-28.5 \times 10^{6} N – mm \\M_{z} &=0 N – mm\end{aligned}\)

Each of the non-zero forces and moments will be evaluated to determine whether stresses are created at the point of interest.

 

(a) Point H
Force \(F _{z}\) creates a transverse shear stress in the zx plane at H. The magnitude of this shear stress is:

\(\tau_{z x}=\frac{(15,000 N )\left(179,861.333 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(29.9761 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\right)[(220 mm )-(204 mm )]}=5.625 MPa\)

Moment \(M _{x}\), which is a torque, creates a torsion shear stress in the zx plane at H. The magnitude of this shear stress is:

\(\tau_{z x}=\frac{M_{x} c}{J}=\frac{\left(19.5 \times 10^{6} N – mm \right)(220 mm / 2)}{59.9522 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}}=35.778 MPa\)

(Sign to be determined by inspection)

Moment \(M _{y}\) does not create bending stress at H because H is located on the neutral axis for bending about the y axis.

Stresses due to internal pressure:
The 2,000-kPa internal fluid pressure creates tension normal stresses in the 8-mm-thick wall of the pipe.
The longitudinal stress in the pipe wall is:

\(\sigma_{\text {long }}=\frac{p d}{4 t}=\frac{(2.0 MPa )(204 mm )}{4(8 mm )}=12.750 MPa ( T )\)

and the circumferential stress is:

\(\sigma_{\text {hoop }}=\frac{p d}{2 t}=\frac{(2.0 MPa )(204 mm )}{2(8 mm )}=25.500 MPa ( T )\)

Summary of stresses:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{z} &=25.500 MPa =25.5 MPa \\\sigma_{x} &=12.750 MPa =12.75 MPa \\\tau_{z x} &=5.625 MPa +35.778 MPa \\&=41.404 MPa =41.4 MPa\end{aligned}\)

 

 

(b) Point K
Force \(F _{z}\) does not cause either a normal stress or a shear stress at K.

Moment \(M _{ x }\), which is a torque, creates a torsion shear stress in the yx plane at K. The magnitude of this shear stress is:

\(\tau_{y x}=\frac{M_{x} c}{J}=\frac{\left(19.5 \times 10^{6} N – mm \right)(220 mm / 2)}{59.9522 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}}=35.778 MPa\)

(Sign to be determined by inspection)

Moment \(M _{ y }\) creates bending stress at K. The magnitude of this stress is:

\(\sigma_{x}=\frac{M_{y} z}{I_{y}}=\frac{\left(28.5 \times 10^{6} N – mm \right)(220 mm / 2)}{29.9761 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}}=104.583 MPa\)

Stresses due to internal pressure:
The 2,000-kPa internal fluid pressure creates tension normal stresses in the 8-mm-thick wall of the pipe.
The longitudinal stress in the pipe wall is:

\(\sigma_{\text {long }}=\frac{p d}{4 t}=\frac{(2.0 MPa )(204 mm )}{4(8 mm )}=12.750 MPa ( T )\)

and the circumferential stress is:

\(\sigma_{ hoop }=\frac{p d}{2 t}=\frac{(2.0 MPa )(204 mm )}{2(8 mm )}=25.500 MPa ( T )\)

Summary of stresses:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{y} &=25.500 MPa =25.5 MPa \\\sigma_{x} &=12.750 MPa -104.583 MPa \\&=-91.833 MPa =-91.8 MPa \\\tau_{y x} &=35.778 MPa =35.8 MPa\end{aligned}\)
