Guest Question.

A single-phase half-controlled rectifier has the following specifications:

 

\(\widetilde{V}_{i}=220 V 50 Hz , R =1 \Omega, L =100 mH , \text { and } \alpha=45^{\circ} .\)

 

Perform the following:
(a) calculate the average output voltage and current;
(b) calculate the amplitude of the dominant harmonic component of the output current;
(c) calculate the voltage and current ratings of the semiconductor devices; and
(d) verify the theoretical results with simulation results.

Admin
Solution

Solution

 

a) \(\begin{aligned}&\overline{ V }_{ o }=\frac{ \tilde{V} _{ i } \sqrt{2}}{\pi}(\cos \alpha+1)=\frac{220 \sqrt{2}}{\pi}\left(\cos 45^{\circ}+1\right)=169.15 V \\&\overline{ I }_{ o }=\frac{\overline{ V }_{ o }}{ R }=\frac{169.15}{1}=169.15 A\end{aligned}\)

 

b) As it was discussed in Section 5.1.1.3 the order of the output voltage harmonic components are n =0, 2, 4, 6,. Moreover, the dominant harmonic component of the output voltage is the second and according to Fig. 5.5 under worst operating condition (when a=\(90^{\circ}\)) has an amplitude of:

 

\(\widehat{ v }_{ o , 2}=0.85 \sqrt{2} \widetilde{ V }_{ i }=0.85 \sqrt{2} \times 220=264.46 V\)

 

Therefore, the amplitude of the output current second harmonic component is:

 

\(\widehat{ I }_{ o , 2}=\frac{\widehat{ V }_{ o , 2}}{\left|Z_{ o , 2}\right|}=\frac{\widehat{ V }_{ o , 2}}{\sqrt{ R ^{2}+(2 \omega L )^{2}}}=\frac{264.46}{\sqrt{1^{2}+(2 \pi \times 50 \times 0.1)^{2}}}=8.42 A\)

 

c) The voltage and current ratings of the thyristors and diodes are the same and under worst operating conditions are given by:

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\overline{ I }_{\text {device }}=\frac{169.15}{2}=84.57 A \\&\widetilde{ I }_{\text {device }}=\frac{\overline{ I }_{ o }}{\sqrt{2}}=\frac{169.15}{\sqrt{2}}=119.6 A \\&\widehat{ I }_{\text {device }}=\overline{ I }_{ o }+\widehat{ I }_{ o , 2}=169.15+8.42=177.57 A \\&\widehat{ V }_{\text {device }}=\sqrt{2} \widetilde{ V }_{ i }=\sqrt{2} \times 220=311.13 V\end{aligned}\)

 

c) Using the PSIM program the results of Fig. 5.24 are obtained. As can be seen the simulation results are in close agreement with the theoretical ones.
