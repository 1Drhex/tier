Guest Question.

Pure \(CO_{2}\) at 1 bar = 100 kPa pressure is confined between two parallel, black plates, spaced 1 m apart, which are kept isothermal at \(T_{1}\) = 1000 K and \(T_{2}\) = 2000 K, respectively. Assuming conduction and convection to be negligible, estimate the radiative heat flux between the two plates.

we find that carbon dioxide has three important bands in the infrared: At 3660 \(cm^{-1}\) (2.7 μm), 2410 \(cm^{-1}\) (4.3 μm), and 667 \(cm^{-1}\) (15 μm). Since the walls are at 1000 and 2000K, respectively, the most important wavelength regime will be between approximately 1 μm and 4 μm (Wien’s displacement law, Chapter 1). At first glance it would appear that the 2.7 μm band is the most important.
However, the 4.3 μm band is approximately 20 times stronger and, therefore, should be modeled most accurately. Before we can employ the box model we must find suitable box parameters for these bands.
We shall do this by comparing the band absorptances of the box model with those of the (more accurate) exponential wide band model [32], by applying equations (19.34) \(\alpha _{n}\simeq \kappa _{n}\triangle \eta _{n}\) and (19.35). Since we should like to use a single \(\overline{\kappa }\) for all bands we cannot use all of these conditions, and shall apply equation (19.35) \(X=X_{m} : A_{n}(X_{m})=\Delta \eta _{n}(1-e^{-\kappa _{n}X_{m}})\) only for the (most important) 4.3 μm band. Thus,

\(\overline{\kappa }\Delta \eta _{i}=\alpha _{i} ,\) for all three bands,

\(\Delta \eta _{4.3}= A_{4.3}(X_{m}),\) for the 4.3 μm band.

In the last relation we have assumed \(\overline{\kappa }X_{m}\gg 1\) , which needs to be verified.
To simplify the analysis further we shall calculate the box model parameters at a single temperature, say \(T^{4}_{av}= (T^{4}_{1}+ T^{4}_{2})/2 or T_{av} \simeq 1700 K\) . With a gas constant of R = 0.18892kJ/kg K for \(CO_{2}\) [44] and the data in Table 10.3, and Fig. 10-19 we get

\(\rho _{CO_{2}}= p/RT= 100 KPa/(0.18892\times 1700 kJ/jg)=311.4 g/m^{3},\)

2.7 \mu m band :

\(\alpha \rho _{CO_{2}} = \alpha _{0}(\alpha /\alpha _{0})\rho =4.0\times 1.61\times 311.4=2005 cm^{-1}/m ,\)

\(w= 23.5\times \sqrt{17}=96.9 cm^{-1}\)

4.3 \mu m band :

\(\alpha \rho _{CO_{2}} = 110.0\times 1\times 311.4= 34,254 cm^{-1}/m,\)

\(w= 11.2 \times \sqrt{17}= 46.2 cm^{-1},\)

\(\gamma =\gamma _{0}(\gamma /\gamma _{0}) = 0.247\times 24.8=6.12,\)

\(P_{e}= 1.3^{0.8} = 1.234 ,\beta =6.12\times 1.234 = 7.55,\)

15 \mu m band :

\(\alpha \rho _{CO_{2}} = 19.0\times 1\times 311.4\times =5917 cm^{-1}/m,\)

\(w= 12.7\times \sqrt{17} = 52.4 cm^{-1}\).

With an average mean beam length of \(L_{m}=1.76\times 1 m=1.76 m\) , we obtain for the important 4.3 μm band from Table 10.3

\(\Delta \eta _{4.3}=A_{4.3}=w_{4.3}\left[\ln \frac{\alpha _{4.3} \rho _{CO_{2}} L_{m}}{w_{4.3}} +1 \right]\)

\(=46.2 cm^{-1}\left[\ln \frac{34,254\times 1.76}{46.2} +1 \right] = 377.6 cm^{-1},\)

\(\overline{\kappa } =(\alpha \rho _{CO_{2}}/\Delta \eta )_{4.3}=34.254/377.6 = 90.7 m^{-1}=0.907 cm^{-1}\).

Noting that \(\overline{\kappa }\) is a linear absorption coefficient, and multiplying with \(L_{m}\), we find \(\overline{\kappa } L_{m}=0.907\times 176 =160 \gg 1 \), so neglecting the exponential in equation (19.35) \(X=X_{m} : A_{n}(X_{m})=\Delta \eta _{n}(1-e^{-\kappa _{n}X_{m}})\) was indeed justified.
The widths of the other two bands follow from the same relationship as

\(\Delta \eta _{2.7}=\alpha _{2.7}\rho _{CO_{2}}\sqrt{\kappa }=2005/90.7 =22.1 cm^{-1}\),

\(\Delta \eta _{15}=\alpha _{15}\rho _{CO_{2}}\sqrt{\kappa }=5917/90.7 =65.2 cm^{-1}\).

We are now in a position to calculate the nondimensional heat flux between the plates from equation (19.47) \(\Psi =\frac{q}{J_{1}-J_{2}}=\frac{q_{w}+q_{B}}{J_{1}-J_{2}} =1- \frac{J_{B1}-J_{B2}}{J_{1}-J_{2}}(1-\Psi _{B})\) with \(\overline{\tau }= \overline{\kappa }L=90.7\). \(ψ_{B} (\overline{\tau })\) obeys the same equation as \(ψ_{b} (\tau )\) in Chapter 13 and, thus, may be evaluated from Table 13.1. Since the bands are essentially opaque we find \(ψ_{B}=0.015 \gg 1\), and

\(\Psi =1-0.985\frac{E_{B1}-E_{B2}}{E_{b1}-E_{b2}} \simeq 1-\frac{0.985}{E_{b1}-E_{b2}}\sum\limits_{n=1}^{3}{}(E_{b\eta_{n},1 }-E_{b\eta _{n},2})\Delta \eta _{n} ,\)

where radiosity is replaced by emissive power for the black-walled enclosure, and we assume that the bands are narrow (to justify evaluation of \(E_{B}\) as value at band center × band width). To look up values for spectral emissive power in Appendix C, we write with \(\eta _{2.7}=3660 cm^{-1},\eta _{4.3}\simeq 10^{4}/4.3=2326 cm^{-1}\) (since the 4.3 μm band is a band with head, it is better to evaluate \(E_{bη}\) near the center of the band), and \(η_{15}=667 cm^{-1}\),

\(\Psi =1-\frac{0.985}{\sigma (T^{4}_{1}-T^{4}_{2})}\sum\limits_{n=1}^{3}{}\left[\frac{E_{b\eta }(\eta _{n}/T_{1})}{T^{3}_{1}} T^{3}_{1}- \frac{E_{b\eta }(\eta _{n}/T_{2})}{T^{3}_{2}} T^{3}_{2} \right] \Delta \eta _{n}\)

\(=1-\frac{0.985}{5.670\times 10^{-8}(1000^{4}-2000^{4})}\times \left[\left(0.9523 \times 10^{-8+9}-1.7747 \times 2^{3}\times 10\right)\times 22.1 + \left(1.7157\times 10-1.3589\times 2^{3}\times 10 \right)\times 377.6 +\left(0.6885\times 10-0.2251\times 2^{3}\times 10\right)\times 65.2 \right]\)

=0.956 .

Thus, the heat flux is reduced only by 4.7%, as compared with the no-gas case, to

\(q= 0.956\times 5.670\times 10^{-8}(2000^{4}-1000^{4})=813,000 W/m^{2}=81.3 W cm^{2}\)

Note that, since all bands are essentially opaque, the (somewhat arbitrary) choice for band widths is of extreme importance in this model.
