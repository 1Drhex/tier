Guest Question.

In a horizontal plane, a fluid jet hits with the velocity \(v_{0 }\) a wall under the angle \(\alpha \). The jet depth in vertical direction is constant: \(h = const\) .

a) Determine the velocities \(v_{1 }\) and \(v_{2 }\) of the two off-flowing jets.

b) Calculate the width \(b_{1}\) and \(b_{2}\)of the off-flowing jets.

c) Determine the normal force exerted on the wall.

Admin
Solution

a) The velocities \(v_{1}\) and \(v_{2}\) can be determined from Bernoulli’s theorem applied to streamlines between the points ⓪ and ① and between the points ⓪ and ②:

\(\frac{1}{2}\varrho v^{2}_{0}+p_{0}=\frac{1}{2}\varrho v^{2}_{1}+p_{0} \rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{v_{1}=v_{0} } } \) ,

\(\frac{1}{2}\varrho v^{2}_{0}+p_{0}=\frac{1}{2}\varrho v^{2}_{2}+p_{0} \rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{v_{2}=v_{0} } } \) .

b) From the continuity equation follows with \(v_{1}\) and \(v_{2}\) the relation between the jet-widths:

\(v_{0}b_{0}h =v_{1}b_{1}h+v_{2}b_{2}h \rightsquigarrow b_{0}= b_{1}+b_{2} \) .

The balance of momentum in wall direction

\(\longrightarrow :\varrho Q_{2} v_{2}-\varrho Q_{1}v_{1} – \varrho Q_{0}v_{0}\cos \alpha =0 \)

yields with \(Q_{0}=b_{0}hv_{0} \) , \( Q_{1}=b_{1}hv_{1}\) , \(Q_{2}=b_{2}hv_{2} \)

 

\(b_{0}\cos \alpha =b_{2}-b_{1} \) .

This provides the jet-widths

\(\underline{\underline{b_{1}=\frac{1}{2}b_{0}\left(1-\cos \alpha \right) } } \) , \(\underline{\underline{b_{2}=\frac{1}{2}b_{0}\left(1+\cos \alpha \right) } } \) .

c) The normal force exerted on the wall is directly obtained from the balance of momentum perpendicular to the wall:

\(\uparrow :\varrho Q_{0} v_{0}\sin \alpha =N \rightsquigarrow \underline{ \underline{N=\varrho v^{2}_{0}b_{0}h\sin \alpha } } \) .
