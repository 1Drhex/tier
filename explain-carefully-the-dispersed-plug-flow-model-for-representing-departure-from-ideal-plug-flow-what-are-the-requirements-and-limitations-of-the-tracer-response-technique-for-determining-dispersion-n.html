Explain carefully the dispersed plug-flow model for representing departure from ideal plug flow. What are the requirements and limitations of the tracer response technique for determining Dispersion Number from measurements of tracer concentration at only one location in the system?Discuss the advantages of using two locations for tracer concentration measurements.

Time (s) 110 120 130 140 150 160 170 180 190 200 210 220

Calculate (a) the mean residence time of tracer in the vessel and (b) the dispersion number. If the reaction vessel is 0.8 m in diameter and 12 m long, calculate also the volume flowrate through the vessel and the dispersion coefficient.

Solution

For equally-spaced sampling intervals, the following approximate approach may be used.

Mean residence time, \(\bar{t}=\Sigma C_{i}t_{i}/\Sigma C_{i}\)

Variance, \(\sigma ^{2}=(\Sigma C_{i}t_{i}^{2}/\Sigma C_{i})-\bar{t}^{2}\)

If, for convenience, all times are divided by 100, then the following data are obtained.

Inlet sampling point

\(\begin{matrix}t_{i}\\ C_{i}\\ C_{i}t_{i}\\ C_{i}t_{i}^{2}\end{matrix} \begin{vmatrix}0.3 &0.4 &0.5 &0.6 &0.7 &0.8 &0.9 &1.0 &1.1 \\ — &0.1 &3.1 &10.0 &16.5 &12.8 &3.7 &0.6 &0.2 \\ — &0.04 &1.55 &6.0 &11.55 &10.24 &3.33 &0.6 &0.22 \\ — &0.016 &0.775 &3.6 &8.085 &8.192 &2.997 &0.6 &0.242 \end{vmatrix}\begin{matrix}\\ \Sigma =47.0\\ \Sigma =33.53\\ \Sigma =24.507\end{matrix}\)

\(\bar{t_{1}}\)= (33.53/47.0) = 0.7134 or 71.34 s

\(\sigma _{1}^{2}\)= (24.507/47.0) – \(0.7134^{2}\)= 0.01248 or 124.8 \(s^{2}\)

Outlet sampling point

\(\begin{matrix}t_{i}\\ C_{i}\\ C_{i}t_{i}\\ C_{i}t_{i}^{2}\end{matrix}\begin{vmatrix}1.2 &1.3 &1.4 &1.5 &1.6 &1.7 &1.8 &1.9 &2.0 &2.1 \\ 0.1 &0.9 &3.6 &7.5 &9.9 &8.6 &5.3 &2.5 &0.8 &0.2 \\ 0.12 &1.17 &5.04 &11.25 &15.84 &14.62 &9.54 &4.75 &1.6 &0.42 \\ 0.144 &1.521 &7.056 &16.875 &25.344 &24.854 &17.172 &9.025 &3.2 &0.882 \end{vmatrix}\begin{matrix}\\ \Sigma =39.4\\ \Sigma= 64.35\\ \Sigma= 106.073\end{matrix}\)

\(\bar{t}_{2}\)= (64.35/39.4) = 1.633 or 163.3 s

\(\sigma _{2}^{2}\)=(106.073/39.4) – \(1.633^{2}\) = 0.02471 or 247.1 \(s^{2}\)

For two sample points: \((\Delta \sigma ^{2})/(\Delta \bar{t})^{2}\)=2(D/uL) (equation 2.21)

Thus the Dispersion Number, (D/uL) = (247.1 – 124.8)/[2\((163.3-71.34)^{2}\) ]

= 0.0072

The mean residence time, \((\bar{t}_{2}-\bar{t}_{1})\)= (163.3 – 71.34)

= 92 s

The volume of the vessel, V =\(12(\pi 0.8^{2}/4)\)= 6.03 \(m^{3}\)

and hence: volume flowrate, \(\upsilon \)= (6.03/92) = 0.0656 \(m^{3}\)/s

The velocity in the pipe, u = (L/t)

= (12/92) = 0.13 m/s

and hence: Dispersion coefficient, D = (0.0072 × 0.13 × 12)

= 0.0113 \(m^{2}\)/s
