Guest Question.

Draw a circle in the xy plane. At a few representative points draw the vector v tangent to the circle, pointing in the clockwise direction. By comparing adjacent vectors, determine the sign of \(∂\upsilon _{x} /∂y and ∂\upsilon _{y} /∂x\). According to Eq. 1.41, then, what is the direction of ∇ ×v? Explain how this example illustrates the geometrical interpretation of the curl.

\(∇\times v=\left | \begin{matrix} \hat{x} & \hat{y} & \hat{z} \\\partial /\partial x & \partial/\partial y & \partial /\partial z\\ \upsilon _{x} & \upsilon _{y} & \upsilon _{z} \end{matrix} \right |=\hat{x} \left(\frac{\partial \upsilon _{z} }{\partial y} -\frac{\partial \upsilon _{y} }{\partial z} \right) +\hat{y} \left(\frac{\partial \upsilon _{x} }{\partial z}-\frac{\partial \upsilon _{z} }{\partial x} \right) +\hat{z}\left(\frac{\partial \upsilon _{y} }{\partial x} -\frac{\partial \upsilon _{x} }{\partial y} \right) \) . (1.41)

Admin
Solution

As we go from point A to point B (9 o’clock to 10 o’clock), x increases, y increases, \(\upsilon _{x} \) increases, and \(\upsilon _{y} \) decreases, so \(\partial \upsilon _{x} /\partial y\gt 0 \) , while \(\partial \upsilon _{y} /\partial y\lt 0\) . On the circle, \(\upsilon _{z}=0 \) , and there is no dependence on z, so Eq. 1.41 says

\(∇\times v=\hat{z}\left(\frac{\partial \upsilon _{y} }{\partial x}-\frac{\partial \upsilon _{x} }{\partial y} \right) \)

points in the negative z direction (into the page), as the right hand rule would suggest. (Pick any other nearby points on the circle and you will come to the same conclusion.) [I’m sorry, but I cannot remember who suggested this cute illustration.]
