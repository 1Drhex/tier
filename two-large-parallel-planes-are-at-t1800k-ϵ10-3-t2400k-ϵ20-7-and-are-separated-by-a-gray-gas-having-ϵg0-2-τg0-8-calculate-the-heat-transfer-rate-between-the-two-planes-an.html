Guest Question.

Two large parallel planes are at \(T_{1 } =800 K, \epsilon _{1} =0.3, T_{2} =400 K, \epsilon _{2} =0.7\) and are separated by a gray gas having \(\epsilon _{g} =0.2, τ_{g} =0.8.\). Calculate the heat-transfer rate between the two planes and the temperature of the gas using a radiation network. Compare with the heat transfer without presence of the gas.

Admin
Solution

The network shown in Figure 8-39 applies to this problem. All the shape factors are unity for large planes and the various resistors can be computed on a unit-area basis as:

\(\frac{1−\epsilon _{1} }{\epsilon _{1} }=\frac{0.7}{0.3}=2.333\quad \frac{1}{F_{12} (1−\epsilon _{g} )} =\frac{1}{1-0.2}=1.25 \)

 

\(\frac{1−\epsilon _{2} }{\epsilon _{2} }=\frac{0.3}{0.7}=0.4286\quad \frac{1}{F_{1g} \epsilon _{g} }=\frac{1}{F_{2g} \epsilon _{g} } =\frac{1}{0.2}=5.0 \)

 

\(Eb_{1} =σT^{ 4} _{1} =23,220 \> W/m^{2} \enspace Eb_{2} =σT^{ 4} _{2} =1451 \> W/m^{2} \)

The equivalent resistance of the center “triangle” is

\(R=\frac{1}{1/1.25+1/(5.0+5.0)}=1.1111 \)

The total heat transfer is then

\(\frac{q}{A}=\frac{E_{b1} − E_{b2} }{\sum{R} } =\frac{23,200−1451}{2.333+1.111+0.4286}=5616\; W/m^{2} \)

If there were no gas present the heat transfer would be given by Equation (8-42):

\(\frac{q}{A} =\frac{σ(T ^{4} _{1} −T ^{4} _{2} )}{1/\epsilon _{1} +1/\epsilon_{ 2} −1} \hspace{30 pt} [8-42] \)

 

\(\frac{q}{A} =\frac{23,200−1451}{1/0.3+1/0.7−1}=5781\; W/m^{2} \)

The radiosities may be computed from

\(\frac{q}{A} =(E_{b1} −J_{1}) (\frac{\epsilon _{1} }{1-\epsilon _{1} } )=(J_{2} −E_{b2} )(\frac{\epsilon _{2} }{1-\epsilon _{2} } )= 5616\; W/m^{2} \)

which gives \(J_{1} =10,096\, W/m^{2}\; and\> J_{2} =3858\, W/m^{2} .\). For the network \(E_{bg} \) is just the mean of these values

\(E_{bg}=\frac{1}{2}(10,096+3858)=6977=σT ^{4} _{g} \)

so that the temperature of the gas is

\(T_{g} =592.3\> \; K \)
