Guest Question.

Calculate the pH of an aqueous 0.0010 M NaOH solution.

Admin
Solution

Because NaOH is a strong base, the hydroxide ion concentration is equal to the NaOH concentration. The hydronium ion concentration is calculated by using the ion-product of water:

\(K_{W}=\left[H_{3}O ^{+} \right] \times 0.0010 =1.0\times 10^{-14}\)

 

\(\left[H_{3}O ^{+} \right]=1.0\times 10^{-11}M\)

The pH is most easily calculated by using the relation

\(\left[H_{3}O ^{+} \right]=10^{-PH}\)

This can be rewritten as: \(PH=-log \left[H_{3}O ^{+} \right] \).By consulting Table 9.3, we can see that the pH = 11.00. The pH can also be calculated directly with a hand calculator fitted with a logarithm function key:

Step 1. To enter the molar concentration of hydronium ion, enter 1, press EE, enter 11, press the change sign key, and press the log key. The result is -11.

Step 2. Press the change sign key,

.The answer is now+ 11, so the pH = 11.00.
