Guest Question.

Gaseous propane and stoichiometric air are mixed and fed to a burner both at ambient \(P _{ o }\) and \(T _{ o }\). After combustion the products cool down and eventually reach ambient \(T _{ o }\). How much heat transfer was given out for 1 kg of propane?

Admin
Solution

The reaction equation for stoichiometric mixture is:

\(C _{3} H _{8}+ v _{ O 2}\left( O _{2}+3.76 N _{2}\right) \rightarrow a H _{2} O + b CO _{2}+ c N _{2}\)

C balance: 3 = b ;

H balance: 8 = 2a => a = 4

O balance: \(2 v_{ O 2}= a +2 b =4+2 \times 3\)

\(\Rightarrow v_{ O 2}=5\)

\(N _{2}\) balance: \(3.76 v _{ O 2}= c =18.8\)

A/F = 5 × 4.76 × 28.97 / 44.094 = 15.637

The reaction is

\(C _{3} H _{8}+5\left( O _{2}+3.76 N _{2}\right) \rightarrow 4 H _{2} O +3 CO _{2}+18.8 N _{2}\)

First we solve the problem assuming all the water is in the vapor phase and then we can solve with the proper split of water into liquid and vapor masses.

Energy Eq.:

\(\begin{aligned}& H _{ R }= Q _{ out }+ H _{ P }=>\quad H _{ R }^{ o }= Q _{ out }+ H _{ P }^{ o } \\& Q _{ out }= H _{ R }^{ o }- H _{ P }^{ o }=- H _{ RP }^{\circ}= HV =46352 kJ / kg \text { fuel }\end{aligned}\)

At \(T _{ o }\) we need to check for condensation of water partial P limited to \(P _{ g }\).

\(y_{v \max }=\frac{P_{g}}{P}=\frac{3.169}{100}=\frac{n_{v \max }}{n_{v \max }+3+18.8}\)

⇒ \(n _{ v \max }=0.7135 \text { and then } \quad n _{ liq }=4- n _{ v \max }=3.2865\)

So now we know the split of the 4 moles of water into vapor and liquid. The change in the heating value becomes \(\text { ( } \left. HV _{\text {liq water }}=50343 kJ / kg \text { fuel }\right)\)

\(Q _{\text {out }}= HV =\frac{0.7135}{4} 46352+\frac{3.2865}{4} 50343= 4 9 6 3 1 ~ k J / kg \text { fuel }\)
