Guest Question.

Adiabatic Flame Temperature Calculations

Propane is placed in an adiabatic, constant pressure combustion chamber at 25°C and allowed to react as follows. What is the final temperature in each case? Assume complete combustion.

(a) It is mixed with a stoichiometric amount of oxygen and reacts to form \(H _{2} O \text { and } CO _{2}\).

(b) It is mixed with a stoichiometric amount of air and reacts to form \(H _{2} O \text { and } CO _{2}\).

(c) It is mixed with a stoichiometric amount of air, and the carbon distribution in the product stream contains 90% \(CO _{2} \text { and } 10 \% CO\).

Admin
Solution

(a) For part (a), a balanced equation of the chemical reaction is written as follows:

 

\(C _{3} H _{8}+5 O _{2} \rightarrow 3 CO _{2}+4 H _{2} O\) (E15.1)

 

A schematic of this process, assuming Reaction E15.1 goes to completion, is shown in Figure E2.15A. An energy balance on the closed system at constant pressure gives [see Equation (2.28)]:

 

\(\Delta h=q \quad \text { closed system, const } P\) (2.28)

 

\(\Delta H=\sum\left(n_{i} h_{i}\right)_{2}-\sum\left(n_{i} h_{i}\right)_{1}=Q=0\) (E15.2)

 

We now need a path to calculate \(\Delta H . A\) convenient choice is illustrated by the solid lines in Figure E2.15B. That fi gure also shows the overall energy balance constraint of Equation (E15.2) as a dashed line. Because enthalpy of reaction data are available at 298 K (Appendix A.3), we choose a hypothetical path where we fi rst completely combust propane at 298 K, then heat the products to a temperature \(T_{2}\), which makes the enthalpy change between states 1 and 2 zero. Using this hypothetical path, Equation (E15.2) becomes:

 

\(\Delta H_{r x n, 298}+\int_{298}^{T_{2}} \sum\left(n_{i}\right)_{2}\left(c_{p}\right)_{i} d T=0\)

 

The enthalpy of reaction can be found based on the reaction stoichiometry given by Reaction E15.1:

 

\(\Delta h_{r x n, 298}=\sum v_{i}\left(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\right)_{i}=v_{ CO _{2}}\left(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\right)_{ CO _{2}}+v_{ H _{2} O }\left(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\right)_{ H _{2} O }+v_{ C _{3} H _{3}}\left(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\right)_{ C _{3} H _{ s }}+v_{ O _{2}}\left(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\right)_{ O _{2}}\)

 

Using values from Appendix A.3, we get:

 

\(\Delta h_{r x n, 298}=3(-393.51)+4(-241.82)-1(-103.85)-0=-2.044 \times 10^{6}\left[\frac{ J }{ mol }\right]\)

 

and using a basis of 1 mol \(C _{3} H _{8}\), the extensive enthalpy of reaction is:

 

\(\Delta H_{r x n, 298}=n_{ C _{3} H _{8}} \Delta h_{r x n, 298}=-2.044 \times 10^{6}[ J ]\)

 

The enthalpy of reaction must be counteracted by an equal but opposite increase in the sensible heat. This effect will allow us to calculate \(T_{2}\). The sensible heat can be found through a summation of heat capacities, as follows:

 

\(\int_{298}^{T_{2}} \sum\left(n_{i}\right)_{2}\left(c_{p}\right)_{i} d T=\int_{298}^{T_{2}} n_{ CO _{2}}\left(c_{p}\right)_{ CO _{2}} d T+\int_{298}^{T_{2}} n_{ H _{2} O }\left(c_{p}\right)_{ H _{2} O } d T\) (E15.3)

 

Values for heat capacity parameters can be found in Appendix A.3. They are summarized in Table E15.1:

 

 

Table E2.15 Heat Capacity Parameters for Species in State 2
Species A B D
\(CO_{2}\) 5.457 \(1.045\times 10^{3}\) \(-1.157\times 10^{5}\)
\(H_{2}O\) 3.47 \(1.45\times 10^{_3}\) \(1.21\times 10^{4}\)
\(H_{2}O\) 3.28 \(5.93\times 10^{-4}\) \(4.00\times 10^{3}\)
\(H_{2}O\) 3.376 \(5.57\times 10^{-4}\) \(-3.10\times 10^{3}\)

Integrating each term on the right of Equation E15.3 gives:

 

\(\int_{298}^{T_{2}} n_{ CO _{2}}\left(c_{p}\right)_{ CO _{2}} d T=(3) R\left[A_{ CO _{2}}\left(T_{2}-298\right)+\frac{B_{ CO _{2}}}{2}\left(T_{2}^{2}-(298)^{2}\right)\right.\)

 

\(\left.-D_{ CO _{2}}\left(\frac{1}{T_{2}}-\frac{1}{298}\right)\right]\) (E15.4A)

 

\(\int_{298}^{T_{2}} n_{ H _{2} O }\left(c_{p}\right)_{ H _{2} O } d T=(4) R\left[A_{ H _{2} O }\left(T_{2}-298\right)+\frac{B_{ H _{2} O }}{2}\left(T_{2}^{2}-(298)^{2}\right)\right.\)

 

\(\left.-D_{ H _{2} O }\left(\frac{1}{T_{2}}-\frac{1}{298}\right)\right]\) (E15.4B)

 

The only unknown, \(T_{2}\), can be solved for implicitly by the value at which the sum of the two preceding equations equal \(-\Delta H_{r x n, 298}, 2.044 \times 10^{6}[J] . T_{2}\) is found to be:

 

\(T_{2}=4,910[ K ]\)

 

This value is known as the adiabatic flame temperature, and in this case is quite large. The adiabatic fl ame temperature indicates the maximum temperature a reactor can reach for a given fuel. If there is heat transfer out of the system, the temperature will be lower.

If you used stoichiometric amount of air, do you think the adiabatic fl ame temperature would be higher, lower, or the same? How about excess air? How about if some CO formed as well? We will see the results in the cases that follow.

 

(b) If we use air instead of pure oxygen, the reaction described in part (a) remains the same; thus, the enthalpy of reaction remains \(-2.044 \times 10^{6}[ J ]\). However, we must now account
for the inert \(N _{2}\) which does not participate in the reaction, in the sensible heat term. This species will provide more “thermal mass,” and consequently, we expect the adiabatic flame temperature to be lower. We have 5 moles of oxygen, so we calculate:

 

\(n_{ N _{2}}=\left(\frac{0.79}{0.21}\right) 5=18.8[ mol ]\)

 

The value of \(n_{N_{2}}\) is large compared to the two reaction products so we expect \(T_{2}\) will reduce signifi cantly. To calculate the sensible heat, we must add a term for \(N _{2}\) as follows:

 

\(\int_{298}^{T_{2}} \sum\left(n_{i}\right)_{2}\left(c_{p}\right)_{i} d T=\int_{298}^{T_{2}} n_{ CO _{2}}\left(c_{p}\right)_{ CO _{2}} d T+\int_{298}^{T_{2}} n_{ H _{2} O }\left(c_{p}\right)_{ H _{2} O } d T+\int_{298}^{T_{2}} n_{ N _{2}}\left(c_{p}\right)_{ N _{2}} d T\)

 

Using the following equation in addition to Equations (E15.4A ) and (E15.4B):

 

\(\int_{298}^{T_{2}} n_{ N _{2}}\left(c_{p}\right)_{ N _{2}} d T=(18.8) R\left[A_{ N _{2}}\left(T_{2}-298\right)+\frac{B_{ N _{2}}}{2}\left(T_{2}^{2}-(298)^{2}\right)\right.\)

 

\(\left.-D_{ N _{2}}\left(\frac{1}{T_{2}}-\frac{1}{298}\right)\right]\) (E15.4C)

 

Again, values for heat capacity parameters for \(N_{2}\) can be found in Appendix A.3 and are reported in Table E15.1. The only unknown, \(T_{2}\), can similiarly be solved for implicitly by the value at which the sum of the three preceding equations equal \(-\Delta H_{r x n, 298}, 2.044 \times 10^{6}[ J ] . T_{2}\) is found to be:

 

\(T_{2}=2,370[ K ]\)

 

Note \(T_{2}\) has dropped signifi cantly from the value of 4,910 [K] found in part (a).

 

(c) We must now account for CO production as well. In addition to Reaction E15.1, we need to consider the following reaction:

 

\(C _{3} H _{8}+\frac{7}{2} O _{2} \rightarrow 3 CO +4 H _{2} O\) (E15.5)

 

with,

 

\(\Delta h_{r x n, 298}^{I I}=v_{ CO }\left(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\right)_{ CO }+v_{ H _{2} O }\left(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\right)_{ H _{2} O }-v_{ C _{3} H _{8}}\left(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\right)_{ C _{3} H _{8}}-v_{ O _{2}}\left(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\right)_{ O _{2}}\)

 

\(=-1.195 \times 10^{6}\left[\frac{ J }{ mol }\right]\)

 

Because the carbon distribution in the product stream contains 90% \(CO _{2}\) and 10% CO, for a 1 mol basis of \(C _{3} H _{8}\), we multiple the fi rst reaction (E15.1) by \(n_{ C _{3} H _{8}}^{I}=0.9\) and the second reaction (E15.5) by \(n_{ C _{3} H _{8}}^{I I}=0.1\). Thus, the total enthalpy of reaction is:

 

\(\Delta H_{r x n, 298}=n_{ C _{3} H _{8}}^{I} \Delta h_{r x n, 298}^{I}+n_{ C _{3} H _{8}}^{I I} \Delta h_{r x n, 298}^{I I}=-1.96 \times 10^{6}[ J ]\)

 

and the following species concentrations are obtained: \(n_{ CO _{2}}=2.7[ mol ], n_{ CO }=0.3[ mol ]\), \(n_{ H _{2} O }=4[ mol ], \text { and } n_{ N _{2}}=\left(\frac{0.79}{0.21}\right)\left(0.9 \times 5+0.1 \times \frac{7}{2}\right)=18.2[ mol ]\). To calculate the sensible heat, we must add a term for CO to the expression we used in part (b):

 

\(\int_{298}^{T_{2}} \sum\left(n_{i}\right)_{2}\left(c_{p}\right)_{i} d T=\int_{298}^{T_{2}} n_{ CO _{2}}\left(c_{p}\right)_{ CO _{2}} d T+\int_{298}^{T_{2}} n_{ CO }\left(c_{p}\right)_{ CO } d T+\int_{298}^{T_{2}} n_{ H _{2} O }\left(c_{p}\right)_{ H _{2} O } d T+\int_{298}^{T_{2}} n_{ N _{2}}\left(c_{p}\right)_{ N _{2}} d T\)

 

\(T_{2}\) is found to be:

 

\(T_{2}=2,350[ K ]\)

 

Note \(T_{2}\) is similar to the value of 2,370 [K] found in part (b).
