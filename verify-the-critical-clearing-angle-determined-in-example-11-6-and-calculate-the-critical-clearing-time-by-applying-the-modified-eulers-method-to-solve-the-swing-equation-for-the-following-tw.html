Guest Question.

Euler’s method: computer solution to swing equation and critical clearing time

Verify the critical clearing angle determined in , and calculate the critical clearing time by applying the modified Euler’s method to solve the swing equation for the following two cases:

Case 1 The fault is cleared at δ = 1.95 radians 5112° (which is less than \(\delta _{cr}\))

Case 2 The fault is cleared at δ = 2.09 radians 5 120° (which is greater than \(\delta _{cr}\))

For calculations, use a step size Δt = 0.01 s, and solve the swing equation from t = 0 to t = T = 0.85 s.

Admin
Solution

Equations (11.4.7) through (11.4.14) are solved by a computer program written in BASIC. From , the initial conditions at t = 0 are

\(\frac{d\delta _{t} }{dt} =\omega _{t} -\omega _{syn}\) (11.4.7)

\( \frac{d\omega _{t} }{dt}=\frac{p_{ap.u.t} \omega _{syn} }{2H\omega _{p.u.t} }\) (11.4.8)

\(\overline{\delta }=\delta _{t} +\left(\frac{d\delta _{t} }{dt} \right) \Delta t\) (11.4.9)

\( \overline{\omega } =\omega _{t}+\left(\frac{d\omega _{t} }{dt} \right) \Delta t \) (11.4.10)

\(\frac{d\overline{\delta } }{dt} =\overline{\omega }-\omega _{syn} \) (11.4.11)

\(\frac{d\overline{\omega } }{dt} =\frac{\overline{p}_{ap.u.}\omega _{syn} }{2H\overline{\omega }_{p.u.} } \) (11.4.12)

\( \delta _{t+\Delta t}=\delta _{t} +\frac{\left(\frac{d\delta _{t} }{dt}+\frac{d\overline{\delta } }{dt} \right) }{2}\Delta t \) (11.4.13)

\( \omega _{t+\Delta t}=\omega _{t} +\frac{\left(\frac{d\omega _{t} }{dt}+\frac{d\overline{\omega } }{dt} \right) }{2}\Delta t \) (11.4.14)

\(\delta _{0}=0.4179\) rad

\(\omega _{0}=\omega _{syn} =2\pi 60\) rad/s

Also, the H constant is 3.0 p.u.-s, and the faulted accelerating power is

\( p_{ap.u.} =1.0-0.9152\sin \delta\)

The postfault accelerating power is

\(p_{ap.u.} =1.0-2.1353\sin \delta \) per unit

The computer program and results at 0.02 s printout intervals are listed in Table 11.1. As shown, these results agree with , since the system is stable for Case 1 and unstable for Case 2. Also from Table 11.1, the critical clearing time is between 0.34 and 0.36 s.
