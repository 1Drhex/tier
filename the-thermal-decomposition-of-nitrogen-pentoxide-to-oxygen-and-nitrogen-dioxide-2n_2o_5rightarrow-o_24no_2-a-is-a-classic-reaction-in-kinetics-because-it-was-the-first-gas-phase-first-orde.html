Guest Question.

The thermal decomposition of nitrogen pentoxide to oxygen and nitrogen dioxide
\(2N_{2}O_{5}\rightarrow O_{2}+4NO_{2}\) (A)
is a classic reaction in kinetics because it was the first gas phase first-order reaction to be reported (4).
Analysis of reports by a number of investigators indicates that the following mechanism appears to give the best agreement with the available experimental facts.
\( N_{2}O_{5}\overset{k_{1}}{\underset{k_{2}}{\leftrightarrow }}NO_{2}+NO_{_{3}}\) (B)
\( NO_{3}+NO_{2}\overset{k_{3}}{\rightarrow}NO+O_{2}+NO_{2}(slow)\) (C)
\( NO+NO_{3}\overset{k_{4}}{\rightarrow}2NO_{2}\) (D)
In reaction (C) the \( NO_{2}\) itself does not react but plays the role of a collision partner that may effect the decomposition of \( NO_{3}\) . The \( NO_{2}\) and \( NO_{3}\) species may react via the two paths indicated by the rate constants \( k_{2}\) and \( k_{3}\) .The first of these reactions is believed to have a very small activation energy; the second reaction is endothermic and consequently will have an appreciable activation energy. On the basis of this reasoning, Ogg (5) postulated that \( k_{3}\) is much less than \( k_{2}\) and that reaction (C) is the rate-controlling step in the decomposition. Reaction (D), which we have included, differs from the final step postulated by Ogg. Derive an expression for the overall reaction rate based on the mechanism above.

Admin
Solution

The rate of the overall reaction is given by either of the following expressions:
\(r=\frac{-d\left [ N_{2}O_{5} \right ]}{2dt}=\frac{d\left [ O_{2} \right ]}{dt}\) (E)
The second is more useful for our purposes, because oxygen is produced in only one step of the mechanism and that step is the rate-controlling step. Thus,
\(r=r_{RLS}=k_{3}\left [ NO_{3} \right ]\left [ NO_{2} \right ]\) (F)
Equation (F) contains the concentration of an intermediate \(\left ( NO_{3} \right )\) that does not appear in the overall stoichiometric equation. This term may be eliminated by means of the Bodenstein steady-state approximation:
\(\frac{d\left [ NO_{3} \right ]}{dt}=k_{1}\left [ N_{2}O_{5} \right ]-k_{2}\left [ NO_{2} \right ]\left [ NO_{3} \right ]-k_{3}\left [ NO_{3} \right ]\left [ NO_{2} \right ]-k_{4}\left [ NO \right ]\left [ NO_{3} \right ]\approx 0\) (G)
Thus,
\(\left [ NO_{3} \right ]_{ss}=\frac{k_{1}\left [ N_{2}O_{5} \right ]}{k_{2}\left [ NO_{2} \right ]+k_{3}\left [ NO_{2} \right ]+k_{4}\left [ NO \right ]}\) (H)
The subscript SS refers to a steady-state value.
Equation (H) also involves the concentration of a reaction intermediate [NO]. If we make the steady-state approximation for this species,
\(\frac{d\left [ NO \right ]}{dt}=k_{3}\left [ NO_{2} \right ]\left [ NO_{3} \right ]-k_{4}\left [ NO \right ]\left [ NO_{3} \right ]\approx 0\) (I)
or
\(\left [ NO_{3} \right ]_{ss}=\frac{k_{3}}{k_{4}}\left [ NO_{2} \right ]\) (J)
Combination of equations (H) and (J) gives
\(\left [ NO_{3} \right ]_{ss}=\frac{k_{1}\left [N_{2}O_{5} \right ]}{k_{2}\left [ NO_{2} \right ]+2k_{3}\left [ NO_{2} \right ]}\) (K)
This relation may now be substituted into equation (F) to obtain an expression for the overall rate of reaction that contains only the concentrations of species that appear in the stoichiometric equation:
\(r=\frac{k_{1}k_{3}\left [ N_{2}O_{5} \right ]}{k_{2}+2k_{3}}\) (L)
This relation is consistent with the first-order rate expression that is observed experimentally.
Instead of using the steady-state approximation in the manipulation of the individual rate expressions, one may arrive at the same result by assuming that a pseudo equilibrium condition is established with respect to reaction (B) and that reaction (C) continues to be the rate-limiting step. From the pseudo-equilibrium condition,
\(\left [ NO_{3} \right ]_{PE}=\frac{k_{1}}{k2}\frac{N_{2}O_{5}}{NO_{2}}\) (M)
where the subscript PE is used to indicate the pseudo equilibrium concentration.
As above,
\(r=r_{RLS}=k3\left [ NO_{3} \right ]\left [ NO_{2} \right ]\) (N)
Combination of equations (M) and (N) gives
\(r=\frac{k_{1}k_{3}}{k_{2}}\left [ N_{2}O_{5} \right ]\) (O)
This expression is the same as that obtained by the steadystate approach if one makes the assumption that \(k_{2}\gg k_{3}\).
