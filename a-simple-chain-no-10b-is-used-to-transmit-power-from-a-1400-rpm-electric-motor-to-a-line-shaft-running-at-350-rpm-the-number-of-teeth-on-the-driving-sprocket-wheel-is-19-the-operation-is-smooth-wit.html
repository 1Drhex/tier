Guest Question.

A simple chain No. 10B is used to transmit power from a 1400 rpm electric motor to a line shaft running at 350 rpm. The number of teeth on the driving sprocket wheel is 19. The operation is smooth without any shocks. Calculate
(i) the rated power for which the chain drive can be recommended;
(ii) the tension in the chain for this rated power; and
(iii) the factor of safety for the chain based on the breaking load.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given Chain 10B } \quad z_{1}=19 \quad n_{1}=1400 rpm \).

\( n_{2}=350 rpm \).

\( =\frac{11.67(1)(1.11)}{1} \) (i).

From Eq. (14.4)

\( v=\frac{z p n}{60 \times 10^{3}} \) (14.4).

\( v=\frac{z_{1} p n_{1}}{60 \times 10^{3}}=\frac{19(15.875)(1400)}{60 \times 10^{3}}=7.04 m / s \).

The chain tension is given by

\( P_{1}=\frac{1000 kW }{v}=\frac{1000(12.95)}{7.04}=1839.49 N \) (ii).

Step III Factor of safety
From Table 14.1, the breaking load for the chain 10B is 22 200 N. Thus,

\( \left(f_{s}\right)=\frac{22200}{1839.49}=12.07 \) (iii).
