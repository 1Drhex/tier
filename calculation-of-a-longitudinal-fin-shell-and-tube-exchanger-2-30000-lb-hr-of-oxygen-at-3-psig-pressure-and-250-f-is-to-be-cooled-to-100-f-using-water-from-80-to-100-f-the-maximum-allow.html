Guest Question.

Calculation of a Longitudinal Fin Shell and tube Exchanger [2]. 30,000 lb/hr of oxygen at 3 psig pressure and 250 °F is to be cooled to 100 °F using water from 80 to 100 °F. The maximum allowable pressure drop for the gas is 2.0 psi and for the water 10.0 psi. Fouling factors of not less than 0.0060 should be provided. Available for the service is a 19¼ in. ID 1–2 exchanger equipped with 70 16-ft tubes each with 20 fins ½ in. high of 20 BWG (0.035 in.) steel. The tubes are 1 in. OD, 12 BWG and are laid out on 2 in. triangular pitch for four passes. Will the exchanger fulfill the service? What is the final dirt factor?

In this derivation, heat transfer from the edge/tip of the fin is neglected.

Admin
Solution

Shell-Side

ID = 19¼ in

Baffle space = ring
supports

 

Passes = 1

 Tube-Side

OD, BWG, pitch = 1 in, 12 BWG, 2-in triangular pitch

(1) Heat Balance:

Hot Oxygen (at 17.7psia), \(T_{avg}=\frac{250+100}{2}=175°F\)

C ≈ 0.225Btu/(lb · °F) at 175°F

Q = (30,000)(0.225)(250 – 200) = 1,010,000 Btu/hr

Cold Water, \(t_{avg}=\frac{80+100}{2}=90°F\)

C ≈ 1 Btu/(lb · °F) at 90°F

Q = (50,500)(1)(100 – 80) = 1,010,000 Btu/hr

(2) Δ\(T^*\):

\(ΔT=LMTD=\frac{\Delta T_a-\Delta T_b}{\ln(\Delta T_a/\Delta T_b)}=\frac{150-20}{\ln (150/20)}=64.5°F\)

\(R=\frac{T_1-T_2}{t_2-t_1}=\frac{150}{20}=7.5\)

\(P=\frac{t_2-t_1}{T_1-t_1}=\frac{20}{250-80}=0.1176\)

\(F_T=\left\lgroup\frac{\sqrt{R^2+1}}{R-1}\right\rgroup\frac{\ln \left[(1-S)/(1-RP)\right] }{\ln \left[(2-P(R+1-\sqrt{R^2+1}))/(2-P(R+1+\sqrt{R^2+1}))\right]}\)

\(=\left\lgroup\frac{\sqrt{7.5^2+1}}{7.5-1}\right\rgroup\times\frac{\ln \left[(1-0.1176)/(1-(7.5)(0.1176))\right] }{\ln \left[(2-0.1176(7.5+1-\sqrt{7.5^2+1}))/(2-0.1176(7.5+1+\sqrt{7.5^2+1}))\right]}\)

= 0.825

\(\Delta T^*=LMTD\times F_T=(64.5°F)(0.825)=53.2°F\)

(3) \(T_{avg} and t_{avg}\): obtained in step (1).

\(=\frac{(70)(0.479)}{144(4)}=0.0582\ ft^2\)

(5′) Mass Velocity, \(G_s\)

\(G_s=\frac{\dot{M}}{a_s}=\frac{30,000}{1.47}\)

= 20,400lb/(hr · \(ft^2\))

 (5) Mass Velocity, \(G_t\)

\(G_t=\frac{\dot{m}}{a_t}=\frac{50,500}{0.0582}\)

= 868,000lb/(hr · \(ft^2\))
(6′) Obtain μ at \(T_{avg}\)

μ = 0.0225 cP at 175°F

= 0.0225 cP\(\left\lgroup\frac{2.42lb/(ft\ \cdot\ hr )}{1\ cP}\right\rgroup\)

= 0.0545lb/(ft · hr)

 (6) Obtain μ at \(t_{avg}\)

μ = 0.80 cP at 90°F

= 0.80 cP\(\left\lgroup\frac{2.42lb/(ft\ \cdot\ hr )}{1\ cP}\right\rgroup\)

= 1.94lb/(ft · hr)
(7′) Reynolds Number, \(Re_s\)

\(Re_s=\frac{D_eG_s}{\mu}\)

 

\(=\frac{(0.0436)(20,400)}{0.0545}\)

= 16,300

 (7) Reynolds Number, \(Re_t\)

\(Re_t=\frac{DG_t}{\mu}\)

 

\(=\frac{(0.0652)(868,000)}{1.94}\)

= 29,200
(8′) Obtain k at \(T_{avg}\)

k = 0.0175Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F/ft at 175°F

 (8) Obtain k at \(t_{avg}\)

k = 0.36 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F/ft at 90°F

(9’) Solve for \(h_o\) of gaseous stream flowing through shell:

\(\frac{h_oD_e}{k}=0.023\left\lgroup\frac{D_eG_s}{\mu}\right\rgroup^{0.8}\left\lgroup\frac{C\mu}{k}\right\rgroup^{0.4}\) ⇐ Dittus-Boelter equation (heating fluid)

\(h_o=0.023\left\lgroup\frac{D_eG_s}{\mu}\right\rgroup^{0.8}\left\lgroup\frac{C\mu}{k}\right\rgroup^{0.4}\left\lgroup\frac{k}{D_e}\right\rgroup\)

 

\(=0.023\left[\frac{(0.0436)(20,400)}{0.0545}\right]^{0.8}\left[\frac{(0.225)(0.0545)}{0.0175}\right]^{0.4}\left\lgroup\frac{0.0175}{0.0436}\right\rgroup\)

= 18.8 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F

\(\phi_s\) ≈ 1 ⇐ For gases

(9) Solve for \(h_i\) of non-viscous water stream flowing through inner tubes:

\(\frac{h_iD}{k}=0.023\left\lgroup\frac{DG_t}{\mu}\right\rgroup^{0.8}\left\lgroup\frac{C\mu}{k}\right\rgroup^{0.3}\) ⇐ Dittus-Boelter equation (cooling fluid)

\(h_i=0.023\left\lgroup\frac{DG_t}{\mu}\right\rgroup^{0.8}\left\lgroup\frac{C\mu}{k}\right\rgroup^{0.3}\left\lgroup\frac{k}{D}\right\rgroup\)

 

\(=0.023\left[\frac{(0.0652)(868,000)}{1.94}\right]^{0.8}\left[\frac{(1.0)(1.94)}{0.36}\right]^{0.3}\left\lgroup\frac{0.36}{0.0652} \right\rgroup\)

= 786 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F

\(\phi_t\) ≈ 1 ⇐ Assume non-viscous fluid

(10) Surface area calculation (per tube):

Total fin surface, \(A_{f,t}=\frac{2N_fz_{f,c}L}{L}=2N_f(z_f+0.5z_{f,t})\)

= (2)(20)[0.5 + (0.5)(0.035)] · \(\left\lgroup\frac{1\ ft}{12\ in}\right\rgroup\)

= 1.725 \(ft^2\) /lin. ft

Bare exposed pipe surface, \(A_{be}=\frac{d_2\pi L-N_fz_{f,t}L}{L}=d_2\pi -N_fz_{f,t}\)

= [(1)π – (20)(0.035)] · \(\frac{1\ ft}{12\ in}\)

= 0.203 \(ft^2\)/lin. ft

Total outside surface, \(A_t=A_{f,t}+A_{be}=1.725+0.203=1.928\ ft^2/lin.ft\)

Total inside surface, \(A_i\) = 0.205 \(ft^2\)/lin.ft ⇐ [Table AT.8]

(11) Fin efficiency, \(η_f\) and \(η_{o,f}\) (for a rectangular fin):

Refer to Table 8.3.

Table 8.3 Efficiency of straight rectangular fins, triangular fins, and parabolic profile fins.
For rectangular fins:
\(Y=(4.5128)X^3-(10.079)X^2-(31.413)X+101.47\) (a)
For triangular and parabolic profile fins:
\(Y=(3.1453)X^3-(7.5664)X^2-(25.536)X+101.18\) (b)
Note:
Y = \(η_f\),%
X = \(z^{³⁄²}_{f,c}\left\lgroup\frac{h}{kA_p}\right\rgroup^{½}=\sqrt{\frac{z^3_{f,c}h}{kA_p}}\)

\(z_{f,c}=z_f+\frac{z_{f,t}}{2}=0.5+\frac{0.035}{2}=0.5175\ in=0.0431\ ft\)

\(A_p=(z_{f,c})(z_{f,t})=(0.5175)(0.0175)=0.00906\ in^2=6.29\times 10^{-5}ft^2\)

\(k_w\) = 26Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F/ft

Generate the X-coordinate.

\(X=\sqrt{\frac{z^3_{f,c}h_o}{k_wA_p}}=\sqrt{\frac{(0.0431)^3(18.8)}{(26)(6.29\times 10^{-5})}}=0.959\)

Calculate \(η_f\) and \(η_{o,f}\).

\(\eta _f\) = (4.5128) \(X^3\) – (10.079) \(X^2 – (31.413) X\) + 101.47

= (4.5128)\((0.959)^3\) – (10.079)\((0.959)^2\) – (31.413)(0.959) + 101.47

= 66.1% = 0.661

\(\eta_{o,f}=1-\left\lgroup\frac{N_fA_f}{A_t}\right\rgroup(1-\eta_f)=1-\left\lgroup\frac{A_{f,t}}{A_t}\right\rgroup(1-\eta _f)\)

\(=1-\frac{\overset{2}{1.725}}{1.928}(0.661-1)\)

= 1.30

(12) Clean overall coefficient, \(U_C\) :

\(\frac{1}{U_iA_i}=\frac{1}{h_iA_i}+\frac{1}{\eta_{o,f}h_oA} ⇐ Assume\frac{z_{f,t}}{k_wA_{LM}}\) is negligible

\(U_C=\left[\left\lgroup\frac{1}{h_iA_i}+\frac{1}{\eta_{o,f}h_oA_t}\right\rgroup A_i\right]^{-1}\)

\(=\left[\left\lgroup\frac{1}{(786)(0.205)}+\frac{1}{(1.30)(18.8)(1.928)}\right\rgroup(0.205)\right]^{-1}\)

= 177.8 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F

(13) Design overall coefficient, \(U_{D,i}\):

\(a^{''}=0.2048\ ft^2\ per\ lin.ft\) ⇐ [Table AT.7]

(Note that instead of obtaining the exterior surface area, use the interior surface area where the fins are not present.)

\(A_{t(i)}=a^{''}N_tL=(0.205)(70)(16)=230ft^2\)

\(U_{D,i}=\frac{Q}{A_{t(i)}\Delta T_{LM}}=\frac{1,010,000}{(230)(53.2)}=82.5Btu/hr\cdot ft^2\cdot °F\)

(14) Dirt factor \(R_d\):

\(R_d=\frac{U_C-U_D}{U_CU_D}=\frac{177.8-82.5}{(177.8)(82.5)}=0.00650\ hr\cdot ft^2\cdot F/Btu\)
