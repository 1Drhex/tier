Guest Question.

The rigid structure ABD in Figure P4.6 is supported at B by a 35-mm-diameter tie rod (1) and at A by a 30-mm-diameter pin used in a single shear connection. The tie rod is connected at B and C by 24- mm-diameter pins used in double shear connections. Tie rod (1) has a yield strength of 250 MPa, and each of the pins has an ultimate shear strength of 330 MPa. A concentrated load of P = 50 kN acts as shown at D. Determine:
(a) the normal stress in rod (1).
(b) the shearing stress in the pins at A and B.
(c) the factor of safety with respect to the yield strength for tie rod (1).
(d) the factor of safety with respect to the ultimate strength for the pins at A and B.

Admin
Solution

Member (1) is a two-force member that is oriented at θ with respect to the horizontal axis:

\(\begin{aligned}\tan \theta &=\frac{8 m }{5.3 m }=1.509434 \\& \therefore \theta=56.4755^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

From a FBD of rigid structure ABD, the following equilibrium equations can be written:

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma F_{x}=P \cos 60^{\circ}-F_{1} \cos 56.4755^{\circ}+A_{x}=0 (a) \\&\Sigma F_{y}=-P \sin 60^{\circ}-F_{1} \sin 56.4755^{\circ}+A_{y}=0 (b)\\&\Sigma M_{A}=\left(F_{1} \cos 56.4755^{\circ}\right)(8 m )-\left(P \cos 60^{\circ}\right)(3.4 m )-\left(P \sin 60^{\circ}\right)(7.5 m )=0 (c) \end{aligned}\)

From Eq. (c):

\(F_{1}=P \frac{(3.4 m ) \cos 60^{\circ}+(7.5 m ) \sin 60^{\circ}}{(8 m ) \cos 56.4755^{\circ}}=(50 kN ) \frac{(3.4 m ) \cos 60^{\circ}+(7.5 m ) \sin 60^{\circ}}{(8 m ) \cos 56.4755^{\circ}}=92.7405 kN\)

Substitute \(F_{1}\) into Eq. (a) to obtain \(A_{x}:\)

\(A_{x}=F_{1} \cos 56.4755^{\circ}-P \cos 60^{\circ}=(92.7405 kN ) \cos 56.4755^{\circ}-(50 kN ) \cos 60^{\circ}=26.2199 kN\)

and substitute \(F_{1}\) into Eq. (b) to obtain \(A_{y}\):

\(A_{y}=F_{1} \sin 56.4755^{\circ}+P \sin 60^{\circ}=(92.7405 kN ) \sin 56.4755^{\circ}+(50 kN ) \sin 60^{\circ}=120.6144 kN\)

The resultant pin force at A is found from \(A_{x} \text { and } A_{y} \text { : }\)

\(|A|=\sqrt{A_{x}^{2}+A_{y}^{2}}=\sqrt{(26.2199 kN )^{2}+(120.6144 kN )^{2}}=123.4314 kN\)

 

(a) The cross-sectional area of the 35-mm-diameter tie rod is:

\(A_{1}=\frac{\pi}{4}(35 mm )^{2}=962.112750 mm ^{2}\)

and thus, the normal stress in rod (1) is:

\(\sigma_{1}=\frac{F_{1}}{A_{1}}=\frac{92,740.5 N }{962.112750 mm ^{2}}=96.3925 MPa =96.4 MPa\)

 

(b) The 30-mm-diameter single shear pin at A has a shear area of

\(A_{V, A}=\frac{\pi}{4}(30 mm )^{2}=706.858347 mm ^{2}\)

Consequently, the shear stress in pin A is

\(\tau_{A}=\frac{123,431.4 N }{706.858347 mm ^{2}}=174.6197 MPa =174.6 MPa\)

The 24-mm-diameter double shear pins at B and C have a shear area of

\(A_{V, B}=(2 \text { surfaces }) \frac{\pi}{4}(24 mm )^{2}=904.778685 mm ^{2}\)

The shear stress in pins B and C is

\(\tau_{B}=\frac{92,740.5 N }{904.778685 mm ^{2}}=102.5008 MPa =102.5 MPa\)

 

(c) The factor of safety for tie rod (1) is

\(FS _{1}=\frac{\sigma_{Y}}{\sigma_{1}}=\frac{250 MPa }{96.3925 MPa }=2.59\)

 

(d) The factor of safety with respect to the ultimate strength for the pin at A is

\(FS _{A}=\frac{\tau_{U}}{\tau_{A}}=\frac{330 MPa }{174.6197 MPa }=1.890\)

and the factor of safety for pin B is

\(FS _{B}=\frac{\tau_{U}}{\tau_{B}}=\frac{330 MPa }{102.5008 MPa }=3.22\)
