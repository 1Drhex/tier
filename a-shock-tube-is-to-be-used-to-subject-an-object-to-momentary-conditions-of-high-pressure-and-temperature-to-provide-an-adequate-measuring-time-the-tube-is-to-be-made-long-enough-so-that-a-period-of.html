Guest Question.

A shock tube is to be used to subject an object to momentary conditions of high pressure and temperature. To provide an adequate measuring time, the tube is to be made long enough so that a period of 100 ms is provided between the time of passage over the body of the initial shock and the time of passage of the shock reflected from the closed end of the tube. The initial pressure ratio across the diaphragm is 400 to 1, with the object located 3 m from the diaphragm. The initial temperature of the air (γ = 1.4, R = 287 J/kg·K) in the shock tube is 35°C. Determine a suitable length for the low-pressure end of the tube.

Admin
Solution

Incident Shock:

 

To begin we can calculate \(a_{1}=\sqrt{(1.4)(287)(308)}=351.7874 m / s . \text { For } p _{4} / p _{1}=400\) we find \(p _{2} / p _{1}\) using the iterative procedure described in Example 5.6. From that calculation the shock pressure ratio is determined to be \(p _{2} / p _{1}=9.2853\). Now from Eq,(4.12)

 

\(\frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}=\frac{2 \gamma M_{1}^{2}}{\gamma+1}-\frac{\gamma-1}{\gamma+1}\) (4.12)

 

\(M _{1}=\sqrt{\left(\frac{\gamma+1}{2 \gamma}\right)\left(\frac{ p _{2}}{ p _{1}}+\frac{\gamma-1}{\gamma+1}\right)}=\sqrt{\frac{6}{7}\left(9.2853+\frac{1}{6}\right)}=2.8463\)

 

\(S _{ I }= M _{1} a _{1}=(2.8463)(351.7874)=1001.3087 m / s\)

 

Now at \(M _{1}\) we can also find the density ratio and temperature ratio across the shock. The first ratio will give the velocity behind the shock, \(V _{2}\), and the second will produce \(a _{2}\).

 

\(\frac{\rho_{2}}{\rho_{1}}=3.7102, \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}=2.5026\)

 

Hence,

 

\(\frac{\rho_{2}}{\rho_{1}}=\frac{ S _{ I }}{ S _{ I }- V _{2}}=\frac{1001.3087}{1001.3087- V _{2}}=3.7102\)

 

From which we find, \(V _{2}=731.4287\) m/s. Also,

 

\(T _{2}=\left(\frac{ T _{2}}{ T _{1}}\right) T _{1}=2.5026(308)=770.8008 K\).

 

Thus,

 

\(a_{2}=\sqrt{(1.4)(287)(770.8008)}=556.5139 m / s\)

 

Reflected Shock:

 

To compute the reflected shock speed use Eq.(5.10). However the speed S that appears in the expression must be replaced with \(S _{ R }+ V _{2} \text { and } V \text { is replaced with } V _{2}\) to agree with the current problem. Accordingly we may write:

 

\(S=\left(\frac{\gamma+1}{4}\right) V+\sqrt{\left[\left(\frac{\gamma+1}{4}\right) V\right]^{2}+a_{1}^{2}}\) (5.10)

 

\(S _{ R }+ V _{2}=\left(\frac{\gamma+1}{4}\right) V _{2}+\sqrt{\left[\left(\frac{\gamma+1}{4}\right) V _{2}\right]^{2}+ a _{2}^{2}}\)

 

Thus,

 

\(S _{ R }=\left(\frac{\gamma-3}{4}\right) V _{2}+\sqrt{\left[\left(\frac{\gamma+1}{4}\right) V _{2}\right]^{2}+ a _{2}^{2}}\)

 

\(=-(0.4) 731.4287+\sqrt{[(0.6) 731.4287]^{2}+556.5139^{2}}\)

 

\(=416.1622 m / s\)

 

Time to test \(=\frac{ L -3}{1001.3087}+\frac{ L -3}{416.1622}=100 \times 10^{-3} s\)

 

\(L =3+29.3979=32.3979 m\)
