Guest Question.

A simple pendulum (length l, mass \(m_{2} \)) pivots on a block (mass \(m_{1} \)) as shown in Fig. 4.7a. The block is conntected to a wall by a spring (stiffness k) and can glide without friction on the support.

Find the equations of motion.

Admin
Solution

The system is conservative. Its position is uniquely determined by the displacement x of the block (measured from the equilibrium position of the unstretched spring) and the angle Ï† (Fig. 4.7b). Hence, the system has two degrees of freedom. We choose

\(q_{1}=x, q_{2} =\varphi \)

as generalized coordinates. The kinetic energy is given by

\(T=\frac{1}{2}m_{1}\upsilon ^{2}_{1}+\frac{1}{2}m_{2}\upsilon ^{2}_{2} \) .

The velocity of mass \(m_{1} \) is \(\upsilon _{1}=\dot{x} \) whereas the velocity of mass \(m_{2} \) is determined by the translation of mass \(m_{1} \) and the superimposed rotation of the pendulum. According to Fig. 4.7b we obtain \(\upsilon ^{2}_{2}= \left(\dot{x}+l\dot{\varphi }\cos \varphi \right)^{2} +\left(l\dot{\varphi }\sin \varphi \right)^{2}\) . Thus the kinetic energy can be written as

\(T=\frac{1}{2} m_{1}\dot{x}^{2}+\frac{1}{2}m_{2} \left[\left(\dot{x}+l\dot{\varphi } \cos \varphi \right)^{2} +\left(l\dot{\varphi }\sin \varphi \right)^{2} \right]\) . (a)

If the zero-level of the potential of the weight is chosen at the level of the block, the total potential energy of the system is

\(V=\frac{1}{2} c x^{2} -m_{2}gl\cos \varphi\) . (b)

With (a) and (b) the Lagrangian is given as

\(L=T-V=\frac{1}{2} \left(m_{1}+m_{2} \right) \dot{x}^{2}+m_{2} l\dot{x}\dot{\varphi }\cos \varphi +\frac{1}{2} m _{2}l^{2} \dot{\varphi }^{2}-\frac{1}{2}kx^{2} +m_{2}gl\cos \varphi\) .

To set up the Lagrange equations (4.36)

\(\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{q}_{j} } \right)-\frac{\partial L}{\partial q_{j} } =0 , j=1,…,f\) (4.36)

\(\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{x} } \right)-\frac{\partial L}{\partial x} =0, \frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\varphi } } \right)-\frac{\partial L}{\partial \varphi } =0 \) (c)

the following derivatives must be calculated:

\(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{x} } =\left(m_{1} +m_{2} \right) \dot{x} +m_{2} l\dot{\varphi } \cos \varphi \),

\(\frac{d}{dt} \left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{x} } \right)=\left(m_{1}+m_{2} \right)\ddot{x}+m_{2}l\ddot{\varphi }\cos \varphi -m_{2}l\dot{\varphi }^{2}\sin \varphi \),

\(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\varphi } } =m_{2}l\dot{x} \cos \varphi +m_{2}l^{2}\dot{\varphi } \),

\(\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\varphi } } \right) =m_{2}l\ddot{x}\cos \varphi -m_{2}l\dot{x}\dot{\varphi}\sin \varphi +m_{2}l^{2}\ddot{\varphi }\),

\(\frac{\partial L}{\partial x}=-kx,\frac{\partial L}{\partial \varphi } =-m_{2}l\dot{x}\dot{\varphi } \sin \varphi -m_{2} gl\sin \varphi\) .

Insertion into (c) yields the equations of motion

\(\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right)\ddot{x}+m_{2}l\ddot{\varphi }\cos \varphi -m_{2} l\dot{\varphi }^{2}\sin \varphi +k x=0\),

\(\ddot{x}\cos \varphi +l\ddot{\varphi }+g\sin \varphi =0\) .
