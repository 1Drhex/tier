Guest Question.

A square loop, side a, resistance R, lies a distance s from an infinite straight wire that carries current I (Fig. 7.29). Now someone cuts the wire, so I drops to zero. In what direction does the induced current in the square loop flow, and what total charge passes a given point in the loop during the time this current flows? If you don’t like the scissors model, turn the current down gradually:

\(I(t)= \begin{cases}(1-\alpha t) I, & \text { for } 0 \leq t \leq 1 / \alpha , \\ 0, & \text { for } t>1 / \alpha .\end{cases}\)
Admin
Solution

\(\Phi=\int B \cdot d a ; B =\frac{\mu_{0} I}{2 \pi s} \hat{\phi} ; \Phi=\frac{\mu_{0} I a}{2 \pi} \int_{s}^{s+a} \frac{d s^{\prime}}{s^{\prime}}=\frac{\mu_{0} I a}{2 \pi} \ln \frac{s+a}{s}\) ;

\(ε =I_{ loop } R=\frac{d Q}{d t} R=-\frac{d \Phi}{d t}=-\frac{\mu_{0} a}{2 \pi} \ln (1+a / s) \frac{d I}{d t}\) .

\(d Q=-\frac{\mu_{0} a}{2 \pi R} \ln (1+a / s) d I \Rightarrow Q=\frac{I \mu_{0} a}{2 \pi R} \ln (1+a / s)\) .

The field of the wire, at the square loop, is out of the page, and decreasing, so the field of the induced current must point out of page, within the loop, and hence the induced current flows counterclockwise .
