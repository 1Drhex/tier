Guest Question.

It is proposed to manufacture oxamide by reacting cyanogen with water using a strong

solution of hydrogen chloride which acts as a catalyst according to the reaction:

\((CN)_{2}+2H_{2}O\rightarrow (CONH_{2})_{2}\)

The reaction is pseudo first-order with respect to dissolved cyanogen, the rate constant

at the operating temperature of 300 K being 0.19 × \(10^{-3} s^{-1}\). An agitated tank will be used containing 15 \(m^{3}\) of liquid with a continuous flow of a cyanogen–air mixture

at 300 kN/\(m^{2}\) (3 bar) total pressure, composition 0.20 mole fraction cyanogen, and a continuous feed and outflow of the hydrogen chloride solution; the gas feed flowrate

will be 0.01 \(m^{3}\) /s total and the liquid flowrate 0.0018 \(m^{3}\) /s. At the chosen conditions of

agitation the following estimates have been made:

Liquid-phase mass transfer coefficient \(k_{L}=1.9\times 10^{-5}\) m/s

Gas–liquid interfacial area per unit

volume of dispersion a = 47 \(m^{2}\)/\(m^{3}\)

Gas volume fraction in dispersion \(\varepsilon _{g}\) = 0.031

Diffusivity of cyanogen in solution D = 0.6 × \(10^{-9} m^{2}\)/s

Henry law coefficient \(P_{A}/C_{A}\) = H = 1.3 bar \(m^{3}\)/kmol

where \(P_{A}\) is partial pressure and \(C_{A}\) is

concentration in liquid at equilibrium

Gas constant R = 8314 J/kmol K

Assuming ideal mixing for both gas and liquid phases, calculate:

(a) the concentration of oxamide in the liquid outflow (the inflow contains no dissolved

oxamide);

(b) the concentration of dissolved but unreacted cyanogen in the liquid outflow, and

(c) the fraction of cyanogen removed from the gas stream. What further treatment

would you suggest for the liquid leaving the tank before separation of the oxamide?

Calculate the value of \(\beta \) for this system and suggest another type of reactor that might

be considered for this process.

 

Admin
Solution

(a) A material balance on cyanogen transferred from gas to liquid gives:

\(\begin{matrix}\upsilon _{G_{0}}y_{0}P/RT- &\upsilon _{G}y_{0}P/RT- &V_{d}k_{L}a(C_{i}-C_{L}) &=0 \\ (in) &(out) &(transferred) & \end{matrix}\)

or: \((P/RT)(\upsilon _{G_{0}}y_{0}-\upsilon _{G}y)-V_{d}k_{L}a[(yP/H)-C_{L}]=0\) (i)

(b) A material balance on the cyanogen in the liquid gives:

\(\begin{matrix}0- &\upsilon _{L}C_{L} + &V_{d}k_{L}a(C_{i}-C_{L})- & kVC_{L} &=0 \\ (in) &(out) &(transferred) &(reaction) & \end{matrix}\)

or: \(V_{d}k_{L}a[(yP/H)-C_{L}]-(\upsilon _{L}+kV)C_{L}=0\) (ii)

It may be noted that \(V_{d}\) is the volume of dispersion and:

\(V=(1-\varepsilon _{G})V_{d}\)

or: \(V_{d}=V/(1-\varepsilon _{G})=V/0.969\)

(c) A material balance for the oxamide gives:

\(\begin{matrix}0- &\upsilon _{L}C_{M}+ &kVC_{L} &=0 \\ (in) &(out) & (reaction) & \end{matrix}\) (iii)

(d) A material balance for the air, which is required because the volume flow out, \(\upsilon _{G}\) , is less than the flowrate in, \(\upsilon _{G_{0}}\) , gives:

\(\upsilon _{G_{0}}(1-y_{0})=\upsilon _{G}(1-y)\) (iv)

or: \(\upsilon _{G}=\upsilon _{G_{0}}(1-y_{0})/(1-y)\)

= 0.01(1 – 0.20)/(1 – y) = 0.008/(1 – y).

From equation (i):

[3 × \(10^{5}\) /(8314 × 300)]{(0.01 × 0.2) – [0.008y/(1 – y)]}
-[(15 × 1.9 × \(10^{-5}\) × 47)/0.969][(3y/1.3) – \(C_{L}\) ] = 0

or: 0.12[0.002 – 0.008y/(1 – y)] – 0.0138(2.31y – \(C_{L}\) ) = 0 (v)

From equation (ii):

0.0138(2.31y – \(C_{L}\) ) – [0.0018 + (0.19 × \(10^{-3}\) × 15)]\(C_{L}\) = 0

or: \(C_{L}\) = (0.0319y/0.0185) = 1.72y (vi)

Substituting from equation (vi) into equation (v):

0.002 – [(0.008y)/(1 – y)] – (0.0138/0.12)(2.31y – 1.72y) = 0

or: 0.002 – 0.078y + 0.068\(y^{2}\) = 0

Solving the quadratic equation and noting that y < 1, then:

y = 0.0265

and: \(C_{L}\) = (1.72 × 0.0265) = 0.046 kmol/\(m^{3}\)

From equation (iv):

cyanogen absorbed from the gas stream, \(\upsilon _{G}\) = 0.01(1 – 0.20)/(1 – 0.0265)

= 0.0082 \(m^{3}\)/s.

The fraction absorbed = \((\upsilon _{G_{0}}y_{0}-\upsilon _{G}y)/\upsilon _{G_{0}}y_{0}=1-(\upsilon _{G}y/\upsilon _{G_{0}}y_{0})\)

= 1 – (0.0082 × 0.0265)/(0.01 × 0.20)

= 0.89

From equation (iii):

oxamide in the outflow \(C_{M}=(kV/\upsilon _{L})C_{L}\)

= (0.19 × \(10^{-3}\) × 15) × 0.046/0.0018 = 0.073 kmol/\(m^{3}\)

\(\beta (kD)^{0.5}/k_{L}\)=\((0.19\times 10^{-3}\times 0.6\times 10-9)^{0.5}\)/(1.9 × \(10^{-5}\)) = 0.018

and hence a bubble column is the preferred design.
