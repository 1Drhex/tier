Guest Question.

The two buffers at one end of a truck each require a force of 0.7 MN/m of compression and engage with similar buffers on a truck which it overtakes on a straight horizontal track. The truck has a mass of 10 tonnes and its initial speed is 1.8 m/s, while the second truck has mass of 15 tonnes with initial speed 0.6 m/s, in the same direction.
Find : 1. the common velocity when moving together during impact, 2. the kinetic energy lost to the system, 3. the compression of each buffer to store the kinetic energy lost, and 4. the velocity of each truck on separation if only half of the energy offered in the springs is returned.

 

Admin
Solution

Given : s = 0.7 MN/m = 0.7 × \(10^{6}\) N/m ; m = 10 t = 10 × \(10^{3}\) kg ; \(v_{1}\) = 1.8 m/s; \(m_{2}\) = 15 t = 15 × \(10^{3}\) kg ; \(v_{2}\) = 0.6 m/s

1. Common velocity when moving together during impact

Let v = Common velocity.

We know that momentum before impact = Momentum after impact

i.e. \(m_{1} \cdot v_{1}+m_{2} \cdot v_{2}=\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right) v\)

\(10 \times 10^{3} \times 1.8+15 \times 10^{3} \times 0.6=\left(10 \times 10^{3}+15+10^{3}\right) v\) \(27 \times 10^{3}=25 \times 10^{3} v \quad \text { or } \quad v=27 \times 10^{3} / 25 \times 10^{3}\) \(=1.08 m / s\)

2. Kinetic energy lost to the system

Since the kinetic energy lost to the system is the kinetic energy before impact minus the kinetic energy after impact, therefore

Kinetic energy lost to the system

\(=\left(\frac{1}{2} m_{1} v_{1}^{2}+\frac{1}{2} m_{2} v_{2}^{2}\right)-\frac{1}{2}\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right) v^{2}\) \(=\left[\frac{1}{2} \times 10 \times 10^{3}(1.8)^{2}+\frac{1}{2} \times 15 \times 10^{3}(0.6)^{2}\right]\) \(-\frac{1}{2}\left(10 \times 10^{3}+15 \times 10^{3}\right)(1.08)^{2}\) \(=4.35 \times 10^{3} N – m =4.35 kN – m\)

3. Compression of each buffer spring to store kinetic energy lost

Let x = Compression of each buffer spring in metre, and

s = Force required by each buffer spring or stiffness of each spring

= 0.7 MN/m = 0.7 × \(10^{6}\) N/m … (Given)

Since the strain energy stored in the springs (four in number) is equal to kinetic energy lost in impact, therefore

\(4 \times \frac{1}{2} s . x^{2}=4.35 \times 10^{3}\) \(4 \times \frac{1}{2} \times 0.7 \times 10^{6} x^{2}=4.35 \times 10^{3}\)

or \(1.4 \times 10^{6} x^{2}=4.35 \times 10^{3}\)

\(\therefore\) \(x^{2}=4.35 \times 10^{3} / 1.4 \times 10^{6}=3.11 \times 10^{-3}\)

or x = 0.056 m = 56 mm

4. Velocity of each truck on separation

Let \(v_{3}\) = Velocity of separation for 10 tonnes truck, and

\(v_{5}\) = Velocity of separation for 15 tonnes truck.

The final kinetic energy after separation is equal to the kinetic energy at the instant of common velocity plus strain energy stored in the springs. Since it is given that only half of the energy stored in the springs is returned, therefore

Final kinetic energy after separation

= Kinetic energy at common velocity + \(\frac{1}{2}\) Energy stored in springs

or \(\frac{1}{2} m_{1}\left(v_{3}\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{2} m_{2}\left(v_{4}\right)^{2}=\frac{1}{2}\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right) v^{2}+\frac{1}{2}\left(4 \times \frac{1}{2} s \cdot x^{2}\right)\)

\(\frac{1}{2} \times 10 \times 10^{3}\left(v_{3}\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{2} \times 15 \times 10^{3}\left(v_{4}\right)^{2}=\frac{1}{2}\left(10 \times 10^{3}+15 \times 10^{3}\right)(1.08)^{2}+\frac{1}{2}\left(4.35 \times 10^{3}\right)\) \(\ldots\left(\because 4 \times \frac{1}{2} s \cdot x^{2}=4.35 \times 10^{3}\right)\)

\(10\left(v_{3}\right)^{2}+15\left(v_{4}\right)^{2}=33.51\) …(i)

We know that initial momentum and final momentum must be equal, i.e.

\(m_{1} \cdot v_{3}+m_{2} \cdot v_{4}=\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right) v\) \(10 \times 10^{3} \times v_{3}+15 \times 10^{3} \times v_{4}=\left(10 \times 10^{3}+15 \times 10^{3}\right) 1.08\)

\(10 v_{3}+15 v_{4}=27\) …(ii)

From equations (i) and (ii), \(v_{3}=0.6 m / s , \text { and } v_{4}=1.4 m / s\)
