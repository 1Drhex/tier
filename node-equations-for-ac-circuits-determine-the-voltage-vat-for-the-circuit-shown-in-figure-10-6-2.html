Guest Question.

Node Equations for AC Circuits. Determine the voltage \(v_a(t)\) for the circuit shown in Figure 10.6-2.

Admin
Solution

First, we represent the circuit in the frequency domain as shown in Figure 10.6-3. Next, we express the currents \(I_1, I_2, and I_3\) in terms of the node voltages. Figure 10.6-4 illustrates this process by focusing attention on each of the currents in turn. In Figure 10.6-4a, we consider the impedance corresponding to the 8-Î© resistor connected in series with the 36-mH inductor. (Notice that the current \(I_1\) is directed from the node having the node voltage \(20 \angle 0^\circ V\) toward the node having the node voltage \(V_a(\omega)\).) Comparing Figure 10.6-4a to Figure 10.6-1c, we write

\(I_1 = \frac{20 \angle 0^\circ – V_a(\omega)}{8 – j9}\)

In Figure 10.6-4b, we consider the impedance corresponding to the capacitor. The node voltage at the reference node is 0 V, so we write

\(I_2 = \frac{V_a(\omega) – 0}{-j16} = \frac{V_a(\omega)}{-j16}\)

In Figure 10.6-4b, we consider the impedance corresponding to the 80-mH inductor. We have

\(I_3 = \frac{V_a(\omega) – 4V_a(\omega)}{j20} = -\frac{3V_a(\omega)}{j20}\)

Applying KCL at the top node of the capacitor gives

\(I_1 + 1.2 \angle 45^\circ = I_2 + I_3\)

Substituting for \(I_1, I_2, and I_3\) gives

\(\frac{20 \angle 0^\circ – V_a(\omega)}{8 – j9} + 1.2 \angle 45^\circ = \frac{V_a(\omega)}{-j16} + \left(-\frac{3V_a(\omega)}{j20}\right)\)

Collecting the terms involving \(V_a(\omega)\) gives

\(\frac{20 \angle 0^\circ}{8 – j9} + 1.2 \angle 45^\circ = \left(\frac{1}{8 – j9} + \frac{1}{-j16} – \frac{3}{j20}\right)V_a(\omega)\)

Solving for \(V_a(\omega)\), perhaps using MATLAB (see Figure 10.6-5), gives

\(V_a(\omega) = 12.43 \angle -81.2^\circ V\)

The corresponding sinusoid is

\(v_a(t) = 12.43 \cos (250t – 81.2^\circ) V\)
