FLOW RATE IN A RECTANGULAR CHANNEL

An 8-ft-wide rectangular channel with a bed slope of 0.0004 ft/ft has a depth of flow of 2 ft. Assuming steady uniform flow, determine the discharge in the channel. The Manning roughness coefficient is \(n = 0.015\).

Admin
Solution

Given: Geometry of rectangular channel and flow depth.
Find: Flow rate Q.
Solution: Use the appropriate form of Manning’s equation. For a problem in English Engineering units, this is Eq. 11.48b.

\(Q=\frac{1.49}{n} A R_{h}^{2 / 3} S_{b}^{1 / 2}\) (11.48b)
Governing equations:

\(Q=\frac{1.49}{n} A R_{h}^{2 / 3} S_{b}^{1 / 2} \quad\quad\quad R_{h}=\frac{b y}{b+2 y}\) (Table 11.1)

 

Using this equation with the given data

\(\begin{aligned}Q &=\frac{1.49}{n} A R_{h}^{2 / 3} S_{b}^{1 / 2} \\\quad\\&=\frac{1.49}{0.015} \times(8 \mathrm{ft} \times 2 \mathrm{ft}) \times\left(\frac{8 \mathrm{ft} \times 2 \mathrm{ft}}{8 \mathrm{ft}+2 \times 2 \mathrm{ft}}\right)^{2 / 3} \times\left(0.004 \frac{\mathrm{ft}}{\mathrm{ft}}\right)^{1 / 2} \\\quad\\Q &=38.5 \mathrm{ft}^{3} / \mathrm{s}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad Q}\end{aligned}\)

 

This Example demonstrates use of Manning’s equation to solve for flow rate, \(Q\). Note that because this is an “engineering” equation, the units do not cancel.
