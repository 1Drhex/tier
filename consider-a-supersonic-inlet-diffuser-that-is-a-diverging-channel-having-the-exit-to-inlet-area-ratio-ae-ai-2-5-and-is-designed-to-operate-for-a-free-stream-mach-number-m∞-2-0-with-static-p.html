Guest Question.

Consider a supersonic inlet/diffuser that is a diverging channel having the exit to inlet area ratio \(A_{e}/A_{i}= 2.5\) and is designed to operate for a free stream Mach number \(M_{∞} = 2.0\) with static pressure and temperature equal to 10 kPa and 216 K, respectively. Consider the scenarios where a normal shock wave stands (1) at the inlet and (2) at the exit. What is the loss in stagnation pressure and what is the back pressure in each scenario?

Admin
Solution

Scenario 1 (Normal shock at inlet) –

\(M_{1} = M_{∞} = 2.0\), therefore \(A_{i}/A^{*}_{ 1} = 1.6875 p_{1}/p_{t1} = p_{∞}/p_{t∞} = 0.1278\) and \(p_{t2}/p_{t1} = A^{*}_{ 1}/A^{*}_{ 2} = 0.7209\) (a 28% loss in stagnation pressure).

Thus \(M_{2} = 0.5774\), \(p_{2}/p_{t2} ≈ 0.79\).

To find \(p_{b}\), the \(M_{e}\) is needed, which in turn requires

\(A_{e}/A^{*}_{e} = A_{e}/A^{*}_{2} = ( A_{e}/A_{i}) (A_{i}/A^{*}_{1}) (A^{*}_{1}/A^{*}_{2}) = 2.5 \times 1.6875 \times 0.7209 ≈ 3.04\).

Therefore,

\(M_{e} ≈ 0.20\) and \(p_{e}/p_{t2} ≈ 0.9725\) and

\(p_{b} = p_{e} = (p_{e}/p_{t2}) (p_{t2}/p_{t1})( p_{t1}/p_{t∞}) (p_{t∞}/p_{∞}) p_{∞} = 0.9725 \times 0.7209 \times 1 \times (1/0.1278) \times 10 kPa = 54.85 kPa\).

\(p_{t1} – p_{t2} = (1 – 0.7209) (p_{t1}/p_{1}) p_{1} = 0.279 \times (1/0.1278) \times 10 kPa = 21.8 kPa\).

Scenario 2 (Normal Shock at Exit) \(–p_{∞}/p_{t∞} = 0.1278\)

\(A_{1}/A^{*}_{1} = (A_{e}/A_{i}) (A_{i}/A^{*}_{i})( A^{*}_{i} /A^{*}_{1})= 2.5 \times 1.6875 \times 1 = 4.218\)

\(M_{1} ≈ 3.0\) , \(p_{1}/p_{t1} = 0.0272\), \(M_{2} ≈ 0.475\), \(p_{t2}/p_{t1} ≈ 0.328\) and \(p_{2}/p_{t2} ≈ 0.856\)

\(p_{b} = p_{2} = (p_{2}/p_{t2}) (p_{t2}/p_{t1}) (p_{t1}/p_{1}) p_{1} = 0.858 \times 0.328 \times (1/0.1278) \times 10 kPa ≈ 22.0 kPa\)

 

\(p_{t1} – p_{t2} = (1 – 0.288) (p_{t1}/p_{t∞}) (p_{t∞}/p_{∞}) p_{∞} = 0.712 \times 1 \times (1/0.1278) \times 10 kPa = 55.7 kPa\)

Comments –

This is a problem where the imaginary extension of the channel would resemble that shown in Fig. 5.6, where scenario 1 corresponds to a normal shock standing at location i and scenario 2, a normal standing at location e. In fact one can also imagine that the imaginary extension in the upstream direction is connected to a reservoir where \(p_{r} = p_{t∞} = (p_{t∞}/p_{∞}) p_{∞} = 10 kPa/0.1278 = 72.24 kPa\). Then this would become a nozzle problem, where \(p_{b} = 22.0 kPa\) causes a normal shock to stand at the nozzle exit, while a higher \(p_{b}\), equal to 55.4 kPa, would cause the normal shock wave to move upstream, to a location where the cross-sectional area is 1/2.5 = 40% of \(A_{e}\).
