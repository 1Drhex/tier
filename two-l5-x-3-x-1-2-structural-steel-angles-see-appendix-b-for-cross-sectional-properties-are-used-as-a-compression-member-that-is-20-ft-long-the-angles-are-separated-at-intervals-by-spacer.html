Guest Question.

Two L5 × 3 × 1/2 structural steel angles (see Appendix B for cross-sectional properties) are used as a compression member that is 20-ft long. The angles are separated at intervals by spacer blocks and connected by bolts (as shown in Figure P16.8), which ensure that the double-angle shape acts as a unified structural member. Assume pinned connections at each end of the column and use E = 29,000 ksi for the steel. Determine the Euler buckling load for the double-angle column if the spacer block thickness is (a) 0.25 in. or (b) 0.75 in.

Admin
Solution

The following section properties for a standard steel L5 × 3 × 1/2 shape are given in Appendix B:

\(A=3.75 \text { in. }^{2}, I_{x}=9.43 \text { in. }^{4}, I_{y}=2.55 in .^{4}, x=0.746 \text { in. }\)

(a) 0.25-in. spacer block thickness:
Consider buckling about horizontal cross-sectional axis:

\(\begin{aligned}&I=2\left(9.43 in .^{4}\right)=18.86 in.^{4} \\&P_{c r}=\frac{\pi^{2} E I}{L^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(29,000 ksi )\left(18.86 in .^{4}\right)}{[(20 ft )(12 in. / ft )]^{2}}=93.717 kips (a)\end{aligned}\)

Consider buckling about vertical cross-sectional axis:

\(\begin{aligned}&I=2\left[2.55 in.^{4}+(0.25 in. / 2+0.746 in .)^{2}\left(3.75 in .^{2}\right)\right]=10.790 in. ^{4} \\&P_{c r}=\frac{\pi^{2} E I}{L^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(29,000 ksi )\left(10.790 in. ^{4}\right)}{[(20 ft )(12 in . / ft )]^{2}}=53.615 kips\end{aligned}\)

Euler buckling load for 0.25-in. spacer blocks:

\(P_{c r}=53.6 kips\)

 

(b) 0.75-in. spacer block thickness:
Consider buckling about horizontal cross-sectional axis:

\(P_{c r}=93.717 \text { kips }\) calculated previously in Eq. (a)

Consider buckling about vertical cross-sectional axis:

\(\begin{aligned}&I=2\left[2.55 in .^{4}+(0.75 in . / 2+0.746 in .)^{2}\left(3.75 in .^{2}\right)\right]=14.525 in .^{4} \\&P_{c r}=\frac{\pi^{2} E I}{L^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(29,000 ksi )\left(14.525 in .^{4}\right)}{[(20 ft )(12 in . / ft )]^{2}}=72.175 kips\end{aligned}\)

Euler buckling load for 0.75-in. spacer blocks:

\(P_{c r}=72.2 kips\)
