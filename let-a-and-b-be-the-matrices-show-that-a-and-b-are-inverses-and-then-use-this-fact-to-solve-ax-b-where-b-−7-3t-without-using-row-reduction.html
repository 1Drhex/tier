Guest Question.

Let A and B be the matrices

\(A=\begin{bmatrix} 4 & 5 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}, B=\begin{bmatrix} 2/3 & -5/3 \\ -1/3 & 4/3 \end{bmatrix}\)

Show that A and B are inverses, and then use this fact to solve Ax = b, where \(b = [−7 3]^{T}\), without using row reduction.

Admin
Solution

The reader should verify that the following matrix products indeed hold:

\(AB=\begin{bmatrix} 4 & 5 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2/3 & -5/3 \\ -1/3 & 4/3 \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\)

and

\(BA=\begin{bmatrix} 2/3 & -5/3 \\ -1/3 & 4/3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 5 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} =\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\)

This shows that indeed \(B=A^{−1}\). Note, equivalently, that \(A=B^{−1}\). Now, we can easily solve the equation Ax = b where b is the given vector:

\(x=A^{−1}b=\begin{bmatrix} 2/3 & -5/3 \\ -1/3 & 4/3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} -7 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix} -29/3 \\ 19/3 \end{bmatrix}\)
