Guest Question.

Uniform flow occasionally occurs in a 5-m-diameter circular concrete conduit (Fig 10.4c), but the depth of flow can vary. The Manning coefficient is n = 0.013, and the channel slope is \( S_{0} = 0.0005\). (a) Calculate the discharge for y = 3 m, (b) Plot the discharge-depth curve.

Admin
Solution

(a) First, use Eq. \(10.3 .10\) to find the angle \(\alpha\) :

\(\alpha=\cos ^{-1}\left(1-2 \frac{y}{d}\right) (10.3.10)\)

\(\begin{aligned}\alpha &=\cos ^{-1}(1-2 y / d) \\&=\cos ^{-1}(1-2 \times 3 / 5)=101.54^{\circ} \text { or } 101.54 \times \pi / 180=1.772 \mathrm{rad}\end{aligned}\)

Using Eqs. 10.3.7 and \(10.3 .8\), the area and wetted perimeter are

\(A=\frac{d^{2}}{4}(\alpha-\sin \alpha \cos \alpha) (10.3.7)\)

\(P=\alpha d (10.3.8)\)

\(A=\frac{d^{2}}{4}(\alpha-\sin \alpha \cos \alpha)=\frac{5^{2}}{4}\left(1.772-\sin 101.54^{\circ} \cos 101.54^{\circ}\right)=12.3 \mathrm{~m}^{2}\)

\(p=\alpha d=1.772 \times 5=8.86 \mathrm{~m}\)

The hydraulic radius is then

\(R=\frac{A}{P}=\frac{12.3}{8.86}=1.388 \mathrm{~m}\)

Finally, the discharge, when \(y=3 \mathrm{~m}\), is

\(Q=\frac{1}{n} A R^{2 / 3} S^{1 / 2}=\frac{1}{0.013} \times 12.3 \times 1.388^{2 / 3} \times 0.0005^{0.5}=26.3 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{s}\)

(b) Mathcad is employed to generate the curve. Note that the solution is generalized, so that any diameter, Manning coefficient, or channel slope can be entered into the algorithm. Equations \(10.3 .7\) and \(10.3 .8\) are used to define the area and wetted perimeter, respectively. Either SI or English units can be employed by properly defining the parameter \(c_{1}\). In this problem a value of \(1.0\) is used. A MaTLAB solution is shown in Appendix \(\mathrm{E}\), Fig E.1.

\(\quad\quad\)Input diameter, Manning coefficient, and channel slope:

\(\begin{array}{llll}\mathrm{d}:=5 & \mathrm{n}:=0.013 & \mathrm{~S}_{0}:=0.0005 & \mathrm{c}_{1}:=1.0\end{array}\)

\(\quad\quad\)Define geometric functions:

\(\begin{aligned}\alpha(\mathrm{y}) &:=\operatorname{acos}\left(1-2 \cdot \frac{\mathrm{y}}{\mathrm{d}}\right) \\\mathrm{A}(\mathrm{y}) &:=\frac{\mathrm{d}^{2}}{4} \cdot(\alpha(\mathrm{y})-\sin (\alpha(\mathrm{y})) \cdot \cos (\alpha(\mathrm{y}))) \\\mathrm{P}(\mathrm{y}) &:=\alpha(\mathrm{y}) \cdot \mathrm{d} \\\mathrm{R}(\mathrm{y}) &:=\frac{\mathrm{A}(\mathrm{y})}{\mathrm{P}(\mathrm{y})}\end{aligned}\)

\(\quad\quad\)Define discharge function, (i.e., Manning’s equation):

\(\mathrm{Q}(\mathrm{y}):=\frac{\mathrm{c}_{1}}{\mathrm{n}} \cdot \mathrm{A}(\mathrm{y}) \cdot \mathrm{R}(\mathrm{y})^{\frac{2}{3}} \cdot \sqrt{\mathrm{S}_{0}}\)

\(\quad\quad\)Plot depth versus discharge:

\(\mathrm{y}:=0,0.01 . . \mathrm{d}\)
