Guest Question.

The elementary reaction

\(A + R \rightarrow 2 R\)

with a rate constant k = 0.02 L·\(mol ^{-1} \cdot s ^{-1}\) is to be carried out isothermally in flow reactors. We wish to process 120 L/min of a feed containing 5 mol/L of A to the highest conversion possible in the reactor system consisting of one 100 L PFR and one 100 L CSTR, connected as you wish and any feed arrangement.

a. Sketch your recommended design and feed arrangement;

b. Determine the final conversion.

Admin
Solution

To begin with the solution, let us first examine the reaction stoichiometry and kinetics. The reaction system can be considered as constant density—isothermal, no molar change. Stoichiometry leads to

\(C_{ A }+C_{ R }=C_{ A 0}+C_{ R0 }=C_{ A 0}\) (E5.5.1)

The reaction rate is given by

\(r_{ A }=v_{ A } r=-k C_{ A } C_{ R }=-k C_{ A }\left(C_{ A 0}-C_{ A }\right)\) (E5.5.2)

a. Eq. (E5.5.2) \(r_{ A }=v_{ A } r=-k C_{ A } C_{ R }=-k C_{ A }\left(C_{ A 0}-C_{ A }\right)\) shows that when \(C_{ R }\) = 0, the reaction rate is zero. That is, A PFR with a fresh feed would not convert any reactant to the product. The CSTR is thus placed in the front, followed by the PFR. Fig. E5.5.1 shows a sketch of the reactor system.

b. Mole balance of A around the first reactor, CSTR, as shown in Fig. E5.5.1 leads to

\(Q C_{ A 0}-Q_{C A 1}+r_{ A 1} V_{1}=0 \) (E5.5.3)

Substituting Eq. (E5.5.2) \(r_{ A }=v_{ A } r=-k C_{ A } C_{ R }=-k C_{ A }\left(C_{ A 0}-C_{ A }\right)\) into (E5.5.3) \(Q C_{ A 0}-Q_{C A 1}+r_{ A 1} V_{1}=0 \) , we obtain

\(Q C_{ A 0}-Q C_{ A 1}-k C_{ A 1}\left(C_{ A 0}-C_{ A 1}\right) V_{1}=0\) (E5.5.4)

which is a quadratic equation on \(C_{ A 1} \) that can be solved to give

\(C_{ A 1}=\frac{Q+k C_{ A 0} V_{1} \pm \sqrt{\left(Q+k C_{ A 0} V_{1}\right)^{2}-4 Q k C_{ A 0} V_{1}}}{2 k V_{1}}=\frac{Q+k C_{ A 0} V_{1} \pm\left(Q-k C_{ A 0} V_{1}\right)}{2 k V_{1}}\) (E5.5.5)

There are two solutions: \(C_{ A 1} \) = \(C_{ A 0} \) and

\(C_{A 1}=\frac{Q}{k V_{1}}\) (E5.5.6)

Clearly, this second solution is the one desired. The first solution is the trivial solution where no product was present prior to the reactor start-up. Substitute the numbers into Eq. (E5.5.6) \(C_{A 1}=\frac{Q}{k V_{1}}\) , we obtain

\(C_{A 1}=\frac{Q}{k V_{1}}=\frac{120 / 60}{0.02 \times 100} mol / L =1 mol / L\)

Mole balance of A in a differential volume along the axis of the PFR (V, V + dV) leads to

\(– d \left(Q C_{ A }\right)+r_{ A } d V=0 \) (E5.5.7)

Since Q is constant, substituting Eq. (E5.5.2) \(r_{ A }=v_{ A } r=-k C_{ A } C_{ R }=-k C_{ A }\left(C_{ A 0}-C_{ A }\right)\) into (E5.5.7) \(– d \left(Q C_{ A }\right)+r_{ A } d V=0 \) and rearranging, we obtain

\(k d V=\frac{Q d C_{A}}{C_{A}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A}\right)}=- \left\lgroup\frac{1}{C_{A}}-\frac{1}{C_{A 0}-C_{A}}\right\rgroup \frac{Q d C_{A}}{C_{A 0}}\) (E5.5.8)

Integrating Eq. (E5.5.8) \(k d V=\frac{Q d C_{A}}{C_{A}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A}\right)}=- \left\lgroup\frac{1}{C_{A}}-\frac{1}{C_{A 0}-C_{A}}\right\rgroup \frac{Q d C_{A}}{C_{A 0}}\) around the PFR (or the second reactor in Fig. E5.5.1),

\(\int_{0}^{v_{2}} k d V=\int_{C_{A 1}}^{C_{A e}}-\left\lgroup \frac{1}{C_{A}}-\frac{1}{C_{A 0}-C_{A}} \right\rgroup \frac{Q d C_{A}}{C_{A 0}}\) (E5.5.9)

we obtain

\(k V_{2}=\frac{Q}{C_{A 0}} \ln \frac{C_{A 1}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A e}\right)}{C_{A e}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A 1}\right)}\) (E5.5.10)

Eq. (E5.5.10) \(k V_{2}=\frac{Q}{C_{A 0}} \ln \frac{C_{A 1}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A e}\right)}{C_{A e}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A 1}\right)}\) can be rearranged to give

\(C_{A e}=\frac{C_{A 0}}{1+\frac{C_{A 0}-C_{A 1}}{C_{A 1}} \exp\left\lgroup \frac{k V_{2} C_{A 0}}{Q}\right\rgroup }\) (E5.5.11)

Substituting in the numbers, we have

\(C_{A e}=\frac{C_{A 0}}{1+\frac{C_{A 0}-C_{A 1}}{C_{A 1}} \exp \left\lgroup \frac{k V_{2} C_{A 0}}{Q}\right\rgroup }\)

 

\(=\frac{5}{1+\frac{5-1}{1} \exp \left\lgroup \frac{0.02 \times 100 \times 5}{120 / 60}\right\rgroup } mol / L =0.008408 mol / L \)

The final conversion is thus

\(f_{A e}=\frac{Q C_{A 0}-Q C_{A e}}{Q C_{A 0}}=\frac{C_{A 0}-C_{A e}}{C_{A 0}}=\frac{5-0.008408}{5}=0.9983\)

The conversion is at 99.83%.

Fig. E5.5.2 illustrates the solution of this example on the \(\left(-r_{A}\right)^{-1}\) versus CA plot. This figure helps us understand the volume of the CSTR is proportional to the rectangular area with the top line corresponding to the reactor exit condition. Lower \(\left(-r_{ Ae }\right)^{-1}\) or higher reaction rate at the reactor outlet reduces the reactor volume requirement. The volume of the PFR, on the other hand, is proportional to the area under the rate curve.
