Guest Question.

The (time-independent) momentum space wave function in three dimensions is defined by the natural generalization of Equation 3.54:

\( \Phi(p, t)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi \hbar}} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-i p x / \hbar} \Psi(x, t) d x \) (3.54).

\( \phi( p ) \equiv \frac{1}{(2 \pi \hbar)^{3 / 2}} \int e^{-i( p \cdot r ) / \hbar} \psi( r ) d^{3} r \). (4.223).

(a) Find the momentum space wave function for the ground state of hydrogen (Equation 4.80). Hint: Use spherical coordinates, setting the polar axis along the direction of p. Do the θ integral first. Answer:

\( \psi_{100}(r, \theta, \phi)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi a^{3}}} e^{-r / a} \) (4.80).

\( \phi( p )=\frac{1}{\pi}\left(\frac{2 a}{\hbar}\right)^{3 / 2} \frac{1}{\left[1+(a p / \hbar)^{2}\right]^{2}} \). (4.224).

(b) Check that Φ(p) is normalized.

(c) Use Φ(p) to calculate \( \left\langle p^{2}\right\rangle \), in the ground state of hydrogen.

(d) What is the expectation value of the kinetic energy in this state? Express your answer as a multiple of \( E_1 \), and check that it is consistent with the virial theorem (Equation 4.218).

\( \langle T\rangle=-E_{n} ; \quad\langle V\rangle=2 E_{n} \) (4.218).

Admin
Solution

(a)

\( \psi=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi a^{3}}} e^{-r / a} \Rightarrow \phi( p )=\frac{1}{(2 \pi \hbar)^{3 / 2}} \frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi a^{3}}} \int e^{-i p \cdot r / \hbar} e^{-r / a} r^{2} \sin \theta d r d \theta d \phi \).

With axes as suggested, \( p \cdot r =p r \cos \theta \). Doing the (trivial) Φ integral:

\( \phi( p )=\frac{2 \pi}{(2 \pi a \hbar)^{3 / 2}} \frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi}} \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{2} e^{-r / a}\left[\int_{0}^{\pi} e^{-i p r \cos \theta / \hbar} \sin \theta d \theta\right] d r \).

\( \int_{0}^{\pi} e^{-i p r \cos \theta / \hbar} \sin \theta d \theta=\left.\frac{\hbar}{i p r} e^{-i p r \cos \theta / \hbar}\right|_{0} ^{\pi}=\frac{\hbar}{i p r}\left(e^{i p r / \hbar}-e^{-i p r / \hbar}\right)=\frac{2 \hbar}{p r} \sin \left(\frac{p r}{\hbar}\right) \).

\( \phi( p )=\frac{1}{\pi \sqrt{2}} \frac{1}{(a \hbar)^{3 / 2}} \frac{2 \hbar}{p} \int_{0}^{\infty} r e^{-r / a} \sin \left(\frac{p r}{\hbar}\right) d r \).

\( \int_{0}^{\infty} r e^{-r / a} \sin \left(\frac{p r}{\hbar}\right) d r=\frac{1}{2 i}\left[\int_{0}^{\infty} r e^{-r / a} e^{i p r / \hbar} d r-\int_{0}^{\infty} r e^{-r / a} e^{-i p r / \hbar} d r\right] \).

\( =\frac{1}{2 i}\left[\frac{1}{(1 / a-i p / \hbar)^{2}}-\frac{1}{(1 / a+i p / \hbar)^{2}}\right]=\frac{1}{2 i} \frac{(2 i p / a \hbar) 2}{\left[(1 / a)^{2}+(p / \hbar)^{2}\right]^{2}} \).

\( =\frac{(2 p / \hbar) a^{3}}{\left[1+(a p / \hbar)^{2}\right]^{2}} \).

\( \phi( p )=\sqrt{\frac{2}{\hbar}} \frac{1}{a^{3 / 2}} \frac{1}{\pi p} \frac{2 p a^{3}}{\hbar} \frac{1}{\left[1+(a p / \hbar)^{2}\right]^{2}}=\frac{1}{\pi}\left(\frac{2 a}{\hbar}\right)^{3 / 2} \frac{1}{\left[1+(a p / \hbar)^{2}\right]^{2}} \).

(b)

\( \int|\phi|^{2} d^{3} p =4 \pi \int_{0}^{\infty} p^{2}|\phi|^{2} d p=4 \pi \frac{1}{\pi^{2}}\left(\frac{2 a}{\hbar}\right)^{3} \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{p^{2}}{\left[1+(a p / \hbar)^{2}\right]^{4}} d p \).

From math tables: \( \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{x^{2}}{\left(m+x^{2}\right)^{4}} d x=\frac{\pi}{32} m^{-5 / 2} \), so

\( \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{p^{2}}{\left[1+(a p / \hbar)^{2}\right]^{4}} d p=\left(\frac{\hbar}{a}\right)^{8} \frac{\pi}{32}\left(\frac{\hbar}{a}\right)^{-5}=\frac{\pi}{32}\left(\frac{\hbar}{a}\right)^{3} ; \int|\phi|^{2} d^{3} p =\frac{32}{\pi}\left(\frac{a}{\hbar}\right)^{3} \frac{\pi}{32}\left(\frac{\hbar}{a}\right)^{3}=1 \).

(c)

\( \left\langle p^{2}\right\rangle=\int p^{2}|\phi|^{2} d^{3} p =\frac{1}{\pi^{2}}\left(\frac{2 a}{\hbar}\right)^{3} 4 \pi \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{p^{4}}{\left[1+(a p / \hbar)^{2}\right]^{4}} d p . \) From math tables:

\( \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{x^{4}}{\left[m+x^{2}\right]^{4}} d x=\left(\frac{\pi}{32}\right) m^{-3 / 2} \text {. So }\left\langle p^{2}\right\rangle=\frac{4}{\pi}\left(\frac{2 a}{\hbar}\right)^{3}\left(\frac{\hbar}{a}\right)^{8} \frac{\pi}{32}\left(\frac{\hbar}{a}\right)^{-3}=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{a^{2}} .\)

(d)

\( \langle T\rangle=\frac{1}{2 m}\left\langle p^{2}\right\rangle=\frac{1}{2 m} \frac{\hbar^{2}}{a^{2}}=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \frac{m^{2}}{\hbar^{4}}\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)^{2}=\frac{m}{2 \hbar^{2}}\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)^{2}=-E_{1} \).

which is consistent with Eq. 4.218.

\( \langle T\rangle=-E_{n} ; \quad\langle V\rangle=2 E_{n} \) (4.218).
