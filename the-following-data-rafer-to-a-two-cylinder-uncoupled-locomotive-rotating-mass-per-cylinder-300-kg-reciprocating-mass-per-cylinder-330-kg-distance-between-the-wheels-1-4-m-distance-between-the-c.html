Guest Question.

The following data rafer to a two cylinder uncoupled locomotive:
Rotating mass per cylinder = 300 kg
Reciprocating mass per cylinder = 330 kg
Distance between the wheels = 1.4 m
Distance between the cylinder centres = 0.6 m
Diameter of treads of the driving wheels = 1.8 m
Crank radius = 0.3 m

Radius of centre of the balance mass = 0.6 m
Speed of the locomotive = 45 km/h
Angle between the cylinder cranks = 90°
Dead load of each wheel = 40 kN
Determine
(a) the balancing mass required in the planes of driving wheels if the complete revolving and 2/3rd of the reciprocating masses are to be balanced;
(b) swaying couple;
(c) variation in tractive effort;
(d) maximum and minimum pressure on rails; and
(e) maximum speed of locomotive without lifting the wheels from the rails.

Admin
Solution

(a) Mass to be balanced = 300 = 2 × 300/ 3 = 520 kg.

From Table 12.10, we have \( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}=156 \).

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}=156 \).

\( \sum M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}=62.4 \).

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}=156 \).

\( \left[\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}\right)^{2}+\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5}=M_{4} r_{4} l_{4} \).

\( \left[(62.4)^{2}+(156)^{2}\right]^{0.5}=0.84 M_{4} \).

\( M_{4}=\frac{168.02}{0.84}=200 kg \).

\( \tan \theta_{4}=\frac{-\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}}{-\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}}=\frac{-(156)}{-(62.4)}=2.5 \).

\( \theta_{4}=180+68.2^{\circ}=248.2^{\circ} ccw \).

\( \text { Since the numerator and denominator are both negative, therefore } \theta_{4} \text { lies in the third quadrant. } \)

\( M_{1} r_{1}=\left[\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}+M_{4} r_{4} \cos \theta_{4}\right)^{2}+\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}+M_{4} r_{4} \sin \theta_{4}\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5} \).

\( 0.6 M_{1}=\left[\left(156+0.6 \times 200 \times \cos 248.2^{\circ}\right)^{2}+\left(156+0.6 \times 200 \times \sin 248.2^{\circ}\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5} \).

\( =\left[(111.43)^{2}+(44.58)^{2}\right]^{0.5}=120.02 \).

\( M_{1}=200 kg \).

\( \tan \theta_{1}=\frac{-\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}+M_{4} r_{4} \sin \theta_{4}\right)}{-\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}+M_{4} r_{4} \cos \theta_{4}\right)}=\frac{-44.58}{-111.43}=0.4 \).

\( \theta_{1}=180^{\circ}+21.8^{\circ}=201.8^{\circ} \).

\( \text { Since the numerator and denominator are both negative, therefore } \theta_{1} \text { lies in the third quadrant. } \)

(b) \( v=45 \times \frac{1000}{3600}=12.5 m / s \).

\( \omega=\frac{v}{r}=\frac{12.5}{0.9}=13.889 rad / s \).

Swaying couple \( =\left[\frac{1-c}{\sqrt{2}}\right] \times R r \omega^{2} l \).

\( =\left[\frac{1-2 / 3}{\sqrt{2}}\right] \times 300 \times 0.3 \times(13.889)^{2} \times 0.6 \).

\( =2455.3 Nm \).

(c) Variation in tractive effort \( =\pm \sqrt{2}(1-c) R \omega^{2} r \).

\( =\pm \sqrt{2}\left(1-\frac{2}{3}\right) \times 300 \times(13.889)^{2} \times 0.3 \).

\( =\pm 8184.2 N \).

(d) Balance mass for the reciprocating parts only, \( R_{1}=200 \times \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{330}{520}=84.6 kg \).

\( \text { Hammer blow }=R_{1} b \omega^{2}=84.6 \times 0.6 \times(13.889)^{2}=9791.8 N \).

Dead weight = 40 kN.

\( \text { Maximum pressure on rails }=40,000+9791.8=49791.8 N \).

\( \text { Minimum pressure on rails }=40,000-9791.8=30208.2 N \).

\( \text { (e) Let } \omega_{1} \text { be the speed, then } \).

\( 84.6 \times 0.6 \times \omega_{1}^{2}=40,000 \).

\( \omega_{1}=28.07 rad / s \).

\( v=28.07 \times 0.9 \times \frac{3600}{1000}=90.95 km / h \).
