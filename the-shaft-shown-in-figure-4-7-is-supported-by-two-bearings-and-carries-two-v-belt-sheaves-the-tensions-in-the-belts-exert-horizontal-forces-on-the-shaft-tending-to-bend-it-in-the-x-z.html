Guest Question.

The shaft shown in Figure 4–7 is supported by two bearings and carries two V-belt sheaves. The tensions in the belts exert horizontal forces on the shaft, tending to bend it in the x–z plane. Sheave \(B\) exerts a clockwise torque on the shaft when viewed toward the origin of the coordinate system along the x-axis.
Sheave \(C\) exerts an equal but opposite torque on the shaft. For the loading condition shown, determine the principal stresses and the maximum shear stress on element \(K\) on the front surface of the shaft (on the positive z-side) just to the right of sheave \(B\). Use the procedure for constructing the 2D Mohr’s circle in this section.

Admin
Solution

Objective: Determine the principal stresses and the maximum shear stresses on element \(K\).

Given: Shaft and loading pattern shown in Figure 4–7.

Analysis: Use the Procedure for Constructing a 2D Mohr’s Circle. Some intermediate results will be taken from the
solution to and from Figures 4–7 to 4–9.

Results: Steps 1 and 2. The stress analysis for the given loading was completed in .
Figure 4–15 is identical to Figure 4–9 and represents the results of Step 2 of the 2D Mohr’s circle procedure, the original stress element.

Steps 3-6. Figure 4-16 shows the results. The first point plotted was
\(\sigma_{x}=8030 \mathrm{psi}, \tau_{x y}=2870 \mathrm{psi}\)
The second point was plotted at
\(\sigma_{y}=0 \mathrm{psi}, \tau_{y x}=-2870 \mathrm{psi}\)
Then a line was drawn between them, crossing the \(\sigma\)-axis at \(O\). The value of the stress at \(O\) is
\(\sigma_{\text {avg }}=\left(\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}\right) / 2=(8030 \mathrm{psi}+0 \mathrm{psi}) / 2=4015 \mathrm{psi}\)
Extend the line through the point \(\left(\sigma_{x}, \tau_{x y}\right)\) and label it, x-axis.
Step 7. We compute the values for a, \(b\), and \(R\) from
\(\begin{aligned}&a=\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{\text {avg }}\right)=(8030 \mathrm{psi}-4018 \mathrm{psi})=4015 \mathrm{psi} \\&b=\tau_{x y}=2870\mathrm{psi} \\&R=\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}}=\sqrt{(4015 \mathrm{psi})^{2}+(2870 \mathrm{psi})^{2}}=4935 \mathrm{psi}\end{aligned}\)
Step 8. Figure 4-17(a) shows the completed 2D Mohr’s circle. The circle has its center at \(O\) and the radius \(R\). Note that the circle passes through the two points originally plotted. It must do so because the circle represents all possible states of stress on the element \(K\).
Step 9. The maximum principal stress is at the right side of the circle.
\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{1}=\sigma_{\text {avg }}+R \\&\sigma_{1}=4015+4935=8950 \mathrm{psi}\end{aligned}\)

Step 10. The minimum principal stress is at the left side of the circle.
\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{2}=\sigma_{\text {avg }}-R \\&\sigma_{2}=4015-4935=-920 \mathrm{psi}\end{aligned}\)
Step 11. At the top of the circle,
\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma=\sigma_{\text {avg }}=4015 \mathrm{psi} \\&\tau=\tau_{\max }=R=4935 \mathrm{psi}\end{aligned}\)
The value of the normal stress on the element that carries the maximum shear stress is the same as the coordinate of \(O\), the center of the circle.
Step 12. Compute the angles \(\alpha, 2 \phi_{\sigma}\), and then \(\phi_{\sigma}\). Use the circle as a guide.
\(\begin{aligned}\alpha=2 \phi_{\sigma} &=\arctan (b / a)=\arctan (2870 \mathrm{psi}) /(4015 \mathrm{psi})=35.6^{\circ} \\\phi_{\sigma}&=35.6^{\circ}/ 2=17.8^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)
Note that \(\phi_{\sigma}\) must be measured clockwise from the original x-axis to the direction of the line of action of \(\sigma_{1}\) for this set of data. The principal stress element will be rotated in the same direction as part of step 13 .

Step 13. See Figure 4-18(a) where the original stress element has been reproduced. Now draw the principal stress element to the right (Part (b) of the figure). Rotate the element \(17.8^{\circ} \mathrm{clockwise}\) from the x-axis as shown. Then draw the vectors for the maximum principal stress, \(\sigma_{1}=8950 \mathrm{psi}\) (tensile), on that face and the one opposite. Complete the element by drawing vectors for \(\sigma_{2}=-920\) psi (compressive) on the other two faces. Label the element as shown in the figure.
Step 14. Compute the angle \(2 \phi_{\tau}\) and then \(\phi_{\tau}\). From the circle we see that
\(\begin{aligned}2 \phi_{\tau} &=90^{\circ}-\alpha=90^{\circ}-35.6^{\circ}=54.4^{\circ} \\\phi_{\tau} &=54.4^{\circ} / 2=27.2^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)
Note that the stress element on which the maximum shear stress acts must be rotated counterclockwise from the orientation of the original element for this set of data.

Step 15. In Figure 4-18(c), we draw the maximum shear stress element to the right of the principal stress element. It is rotated counterclockwise \(27.2^{\circ}\) from the x-axis.
a. On that face we show the positive maximum shearing stress, \(\tau_{\max }=4935\) psi.
b. Then we complete \(\tau_{\max }\) by drawing an equal shearing stress vector on the opposite face acting in the opposite direction so that the two vectors tend to rotate the element clockwise, indicating a positive shearing stress.
c. Now we can draw the two vectors that make up \(-\tau_{\max }\) on the other two faces. The set of four vectors now put the element in rotational equilibrium.
d. The element is completed by drawing four equal vectors, \(\sigma_{\text {avg }}\), on all four faces of the element.

Step 16. Summary of results:
– Maximum principal stress \(=\sigma_{1}=8950\) psi-Tension
– Minimum principal stress \(=\sigma_{2}=-920\) psi-Compression
– Maximum shear stress \(=\tau_{\max }=4935 \mathrm{psi}\)
– Angle of rotation of principal stress element \(=\varphi_{\sigma}=17.8^{\circ}\) clockwise below the x-axis
– Angle of rotation of the maximum shear stress element \(=\sigma_{\tau}=27.2^{\circ}\) counterclockwise above the x-axis
