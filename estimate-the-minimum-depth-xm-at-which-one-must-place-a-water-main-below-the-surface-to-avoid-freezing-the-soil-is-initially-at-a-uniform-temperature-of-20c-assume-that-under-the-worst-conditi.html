Guest Question.

Estimate the minimum depth \(x_{m} \)at which one must place a water main below the surface to avoid freezing. The soil is initially at a uniform temperature of 20°C. Assume that under the worst conditions anticipated it is subjected to a surface temperature of -15°C for a period of 60 days. Use the following properties for soil (300 K):

\(ρ = 2050 kg/m^3\) \( k = 0.52 W/m K\) \( c = 1840 J/kg K \)

\(\alpha = \frac{K}{\rho c}= 0.138 × 10^{-6} m^2/s\)

A sketch of the system is shown in Fig. 2.41.

Admin
Solution

To simplify the problem assume that
1. Conduction is one-dimensional.
2. The soil is a semi-infinite medium.
3. The soil has uniform and constant properties.

The prescribed conditions correspond to those of Case 1 of Fig. 2.39, and the transient temperature response of the soil is governed by Eq. (2.112). At the time t = 60 days after the change in surface temperature, the temperature distribution in the soil is

\(\frac{T(x,t) – T_i}{T_\infty – T_i} = erfc (\frac{x}{2\sqrt{\alpha t} } ) – \exp (\frac{\overline{h} x}{k} + \frac{\overline{h}^2 \alpha t}{k^2} ) erfc (\frac{x}{2\sqrt{\alpha t} } +\frac{\overline{h} \sqrt{\alpha t} }{k} )\) (2.112)

\(\frac{T(X_{m},t )-T_S}{T_i – T_S} =erf \left(\frac{x_m}{2\sqrt{\alpha t} } \right)\)

 

or

\(\frac{0-(-15°C)}{20°C-(-15°C)} =0.43=erf\left(\frac{x_m}{2\sqrt{\alpha t} } \right)\)

 

From Table 43 we find by interpolation that when \( x_{ m}/2\sqrt{\alpha t} =0.40,erf(0.4)=0.43 \) to satisfy the above relation. Thus

\( x_{ m}=(0.4)\sqrt{\alpha t} =0.8[(0.138 \times 10^{-6} m^2/s)(60 days)(24 h/day)(3600s/h)]^{1/2} = 0.68 m\)

 

To use Fig. 2.40, first calculate \([T(x, t) – T_s]/(T_{\infty }- T_{S })=(0-20)/(-15-20)=0.57\) , then enter the curve for\( \bar{h}\sqrt{\alpha t }/K =\infty\) and obtain \(x/2\sqrt{\alpha t }=0.4\) ,the same
result as above.
