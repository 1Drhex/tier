Guest Question.

Links (1) and (2), which are made from a polymer material [E = 16 GPa], support the rigid beam in Figure P17.44. Link (1) has a crosssectional area of 300 \(mm ^{2}\) and a length of 1.00 m. Link (2) has a cross-sectional area of 650 \(mm ^{2}\) and a length of 1.25 m. A concentrated load of P = 40 kN is applied to the rigid beam at C. For this structure, a = 750 mm, and b = 425 mm. Compute the vertical displacement of the rigid beam at point C.

Admin
Solution

From a FBD of the rigid beam, write two equilibrium equations:

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{A} &=(750 mm ) F_{2}-(1,175 mm )(40 kN )=0 \\& \therefore F_{2}=62.6667 kN \\\Sigma M_{B} &=(750 mm ) F_{1}-(425 mm )(40 kN )=0 \\& \therefore F_{1}=22.6667 kN\end{aligned}\)

The strain energy in link (1) is

\(U_{1}=\frac{F_{1}^{2} L_{1}}{2 A_{1} E_{1}}=\frac{(22,666.7 N )^{2}(1.0 m )}{2\left(300 mm ^{2}\right)\left(16,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=53.5185 N – m\)

The strain energy in link (2) is

\(U_{2}=\frac{F_{2}^{2} L_{2}}{2 A_{2} E_{2}}=\frac{(62,666.7 N )^{2}(1.25 m )}{2\left(650 mm ^{2}\right)\left(16,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=236.0043 N – m\)

The total strain energy of the assembly is therefore

\(\begin{aligned}U &=U_{1}+U_{2} \\&=53.5185 N – m +236.0043 N – m \\&=289.5228 N – m\end{aligned}\)

External work of the 40-kN load:

\(W=\frac{1}{2}(40,000 N ) \Delta_{C}\)

From conservation of energy

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{1}{2}(40,000 N ) \Delta_{C} &=(289.5228 N – m )(1,000 mm / m ) \\& \therefore \Delta_{C}=14.48 mm \downarrow\end{aligned}\)
