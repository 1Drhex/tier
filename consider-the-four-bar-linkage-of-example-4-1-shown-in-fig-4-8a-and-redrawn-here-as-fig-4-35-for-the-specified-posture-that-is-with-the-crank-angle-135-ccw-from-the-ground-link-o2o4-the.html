Guest Question.

Consider the four-bar linkage of , shown in Fig. 4.8a and redrawn here as Fig. 4.35. For the specified posture (that is, with the crank angle 135° ccw from the ground link, O2O4), the angular velocity and angular acceleration of the coupler link, AB, are ω3 = 5 rad/s ccw and α3 = 20 rad/s2 cw, respectively. For the instantaneous motion of the coupler link, show: (a) the velocity pole, I, centrode tangent, T, and centrode normal, N; (b) the inflection circle and Bresse circle; and (c) the instantaneous center of acceleration of the coupler link, AB. Then, determine: (d) the radius of curvature of the trajectory of coupler point C where RCB = 6 in, (e) the magnitude and direction of the velocity of coupler point C, ( f) the magnitude and direction of the angular velocity of the crank, (g) the magnitude and direction of the pole velocity, (h) the magnitude and direction of the acceleration of the velocity pole I, and (i) the magnitude and direction of the acceleration of C.

Solution

(a) The velocity pole, I, is coincident with instant center I13, as shown in Fig. 4.36. The instant center I24 (labeled point Q) and the collineation axis, IQ, are also shown in Fig. 4.36. From Bobillier’s theorem, the angle from the collineation axis to the first ray (say, link 2) is obtained from a scaled drawing as

α = 29.08° cw. (1)

This is equal to the angle from the second ray (say, link 4) to the centrode tangent, T. Therefore, we draw the centrode tangent, T, and the centrode normal, N, which is 90° counterclockwise from T, as shown in Fig. 4.36.

(b) Now, by measuring ray RAI = 14.14 in, we can use Eq. (4.43) to locate the inflection point JA,

\(\rho=R_{A A^{\prime}}=\frac{R_{A I}^{2}}{R_{A J_{A}}}\) (4.43)

\(R_{A J_{A}}=\frac{R_{A I}^{2}}{R_{A A^{\prime}}}=\frac{(14.14 in )^{2}}{6.00 in }=33.31 in.\)

 

Similarly, measuring ray RBI = 18.20 in, we locate the inflection point JB,

\(R_{B J_{B}}=\frac{R_{B I}^{2}}{R_{B B^{\prime}}}=\frac{(18.20 in )^{2}}{12.00 in }=27.60 in.\)

Erecting perpendiculars from these two rays, we locate the inflection pole, J, and draw the inflection circle, as shown in Fig. 4.36. The diameter of the inflection circle is

RJI = 19.26 in. (2)

The diameter of the Bresse circle, from Eq. (4.48), is

\(b=R_{J I} \frac{\omega^{2}}{\alpha}\) (4.48)

 

\(b=R_{J I} \frac{\omega_{3}^{2}}{\alpha_{3}}=(19.26 \text { in }) \frac{(5 rad / s )^{2}}{-20 rad / s ^{2}}=-24.08 in\) (3)

where the negative sign indicates that the Bresse circle lies on the positive side of the centrode tangent.

(c) The instantaneous center of acceleration, Γ , of the coupler link is as shown in Fig. 4.36.

(d) The radius of curvature of the path of coupler point C (where RCB = 6 in) is

\(\rho_{C}=R_{C C^{\prime}}=\frac{R_{C I}^{2}}{R_{C J_{C}}}=\frac{(14.67 in )^{2}}{28.62 in }=7.52 in.\) (4)

The center of curvature of the path traced by point C—that is, C′—is shown in Fig. 4.36.

(e) The velocity of coupler point C is

\(V_{C}=\omega_{3} R_{C I}=(5 rad / s )(14.65 in )=73.25 in / s.\) (5)

The orientation of the velocity vector is as shown in Fig. 4.36.

(f) The angular velocity of the crank can be written as

\(\omega_{2}=\frac{R_{I_{23} I_{13}}}{R_{I_{23} I_{12}}} \omega_{3}=\frac{14.14 in }{6.00 \text { in }}(5 rad / s )=11.78 rad / s ccw.\) (6)

(g) The pole velocity, from Eq. (4.47), is

ν = ωRJI. (4.47)

\(v=\omega_{3} R_{J I}=(5 rad / s )(19.26 in )=96.3 in / s.\) (7)

The direction of the pole velocity is as shown in Fig. 4.36.

(h) The acceleration of the velocity pole, I, from Eq. (4.46), is

AI = ων, (4.46)

\(A_{I}=\omega_{3} v=(5 rad / s )(96.3 in / s )=481.5 in / s ^{2}.\) (8)

The acceleration, AI , is directed from I toward J, as shown in Fig. 4.36.

(i) The acceleration of coupler point C is found by rearranging Eq. (4.45); that is,

\(R_{\Gamma A}=\frac{A_{A}}{\sqrt{\omega^{4}+\alpha^{2}}}=\frac{A_{A} \cos \gamma}{\omega^{2}}.\) (4.45)

 

\(A_{C}=R_{\Gamma C} \sqrt{\omega_{3}^{4}+\alpha_{3}^{2}}=(29.64 in ) \sqrt{(5 rad / s )^{4}+\left(20 rad / s ^{2}\right)^{2}}=948.94 in / s ^{2}\) (9)

The direction of the acceleration of point C, from Eq. (4.44), is

\(\gamma=\tan ^{-1} \frac{\alpha}{\omega^{2}}\) (4.44)

 

\(\gamma=\tan ^{-1} \frac{\alpha_{3}}{\omega_{3}^{2}}=\tan ^{-1} \frac{-20 rad / s ^{2}}{(5 rad / s )^{2}}=-38.66^{\circ},\) (10)

which is a clockwise angle from AC to line CΓ , as shown in Fig. 4.36.
