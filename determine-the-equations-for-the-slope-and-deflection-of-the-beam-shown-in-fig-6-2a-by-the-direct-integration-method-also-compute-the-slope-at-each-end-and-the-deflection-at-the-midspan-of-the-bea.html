Guest Question.

Determine the equations for the slope and deflection of the beam shown in Fig. \( 6.2 (\mathrm{a})\) by the direct integration method. Also, compute the slope at each end and the deflection at the midspan of the beam. EI is constant.

Admin
Solution

Reactions. See Fig. 6.2(b).

\( \begin{aligned} +\rightarrow \sum F_{x} &=0 \quad A_{x}=0 \\ +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{B} &=0 \\ -A_{y}(L)+w(L)\left(\frac{L}{2}\right) &=0 \quad A_{y}=\frac{w L}{2} \uparrow \\ +\uparrow \sum F_{y} &=0 \\ \left(\frac{w L}{2}\right)-(w L)+B_{y} &=0 \quad B_{y}=\frac{w L}{2} \uparrow \end{aligned} \)

Equation for Bending Moment. To determine the equation for bending moment for the beam, we pass a section at a distance \(x\) from support \(A\), as shown in Fig. 6.2(b). Considering the free body to the left of this section, we obtain

\( M=\frac{w L}{2}(x)-(w x)\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)=\frac{w}{2}\left(L x-x^{2}\right) \)

Equation for \( \boldsymbol{ M / E I } \). The flexural rigidity, \(E I\), of the beam is constant, so the equation for \(M / E I\) can be written as

\( \frac{d^{2} y}{d x^{2}}=\frac{M}{E I}=\frac{w}{2 E I}\left(L x-x^{2}\right) \)

Equations for Slope and Deflection. The equation for the slope of the elastic curve of the beam can be obtained by integrating the equation for \(M / E I\) as

\( \theta=\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{w}{2 E I}\left(\frac{L x^{2}}{2}-\frac{x^{3}}{3}\right)+C_{1} \)

Integrating once more, we obtain the equation for deflection as

\( y=\frac{w}{2 E I}\left(\frac{L x^{3}}{6}-\frac{x^{4}}{12}\right)+C_{1} x+C_{2} \)

The constants of integration, \(C_{1}\) and \(C_{2}\), are evaluated by applying the following boundary conditions:

\( \begin{array}{lll} \text { At end } A, & x=0, & y=0 \\ \text { At end } B, & x=L, & y=0 \end{array} \)

By applying the first boundary condition—that is, by setting \(x=0\) and \(y=0\) in the equation for \(y\)—we obtain \(C_{2}=0 .\) Next, by using the second boundary condition—that is, by setting \(x=L\) and \(y=0\) in the equation for \(y\)—we obtain

\( 0=\frac{w}{2 E I}\left(\frac{L^{4}}{6}-\frac{L^{4}}{12}\right)+C_{1} L \)

from which

\( C_{1}=-\frac{w L^{3}}{24 E I} \)

Thus, the equations for slope and deflection of the beam are

\( \begin{array}{l} \theta=\frac{w}{2 E I}\left(\frac{L x^{2}}{2}-\frac{x^{3}}{3}-\frac{L^{3}}{12}\right) \\ \end{array} \) (1)

\( \begin{array}{l} y=\frac{w x}{12 E I}\left(L x^{2}-\frac{x^{3}}{2}-\frac{L^{3}}{2}\right) \end{array} \) (2)

Slopes at Ends \(\boldsymbol{A} \) and \(\boldsymbol{B} \). By substituting \(x=0\) and \(L\), respectively, into Eq. (1), we obtain

\( \begin{aligned} \theta_{A} &=-\frac{w L^{3}}{24 E I} \quad \text { or } \quad \theta_{A}=\frac{w L^{3}}{24 E I} \\ \end{aligned} \)

 

\(\begin{aligned} \theta_{B} &=\frac{w L^{3}}{24 E I} \quad \text { or } \quad \theta_{B}=\frac{w L^{3}}{24 E I} \end{aligned} \)

Deflection at Midspan. By substituting \(x=L / 2\) into Eq. (2), we obtain

\( y_{C}=-\frac{5 w L^{4}}{384 E I} \quad \text { or } \quad y_{C}=\frac{5 w L^{4}}{384 E I} \downarrow \)
