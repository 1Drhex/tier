Guest Question.

Air enters a gas turbine at 1600 K and exits at 100 kPa, 830 K. The turbine efficiency is estimated to be 85%. What is the turbine inlet pressure?
Control volume: Turbine.
Inlet state: \(T_{i}\)known.
Exit state: \(P_{e}, T_{e}\) known; state fixed.
Process: Steady state.
Model: Air tables, Table A.7.

The efficiency, which is 85%, is given by Eq. 7.27,

\(\eta_{\text {turbine }}=\frac{w}{w_{s}}=\frac{h_{i}-h_{e}}{h_{i}-h_{e s}} (7.27)\)

\(\eta_{\text {turbine }}=\frac{w}{w_{s}}\)

The energy equation for the real, irreversible process is

\(h_{i}=h_{e}+w\)

For the ideal, isentropic process from \(P_{i}, T_{i} \text { to } P_{e}\), the energy equation is

\(h_{i}=h_{e s}+w_{s}\)

and the second law is, from Eq. 6.19,

\(s_{2}-s_{1}=\left(s_{T 2}^{0}-s_{T 1}^{0}\right)-R \ln \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} (6.19)\)

\(s_{e s}-s_{i}=0=s_{T e s}^{0}-s_{T i}^{0}-R \ln \frac{P_{e}}{P_{i}}\)

(Note that this equation is only for the ideal isentropic process and not for the real process, for which\(s_{e}-s_{i}>0\).)

 

 

Admin
Solution

From the air tables, Table A.7, at 1600 K, we get

\(h_{i}=1757.3 kJ / kg , \quad s_{T i}^{0}=8.6905 kJ / kg K\)

From the air tables at 830 K (the actual turbine exit temperature),

\(h_{e}=855.3 kJ / kg\)

Therefore, from the energy equation for the real process,

\(w=1757.3-855.3=902.0 kJ / kg\)

Using the definition of turbine efficiency,

\(w_{s}=902.0 / 0.85=1061.2 kJ / kg\)

From the energy equation for the isentropic process,

\(h_{e s}=1757.3-1061.2=696.1 kJ / kg\)

so that, from the air tables,

\(T_{e s}=683.7 K , \quad s_{T e s}^{0}=7.7148 kJ / kg K\)

and the turbine inlet pressure is determined from

\(0=7.7148-8.6905-0.287 \ln \frac{100}{P_{i}}\)

or

\(P_{i}=2995 kPa\)
