Guest Question.

A bracket is subjected to two forces \(P_{1}\) and \(P_{2}\) as shown in figure Ex. 9. If the resultant of the two forces has a magnitude of 525 N and direction as shown in the figure, determine magnitudes of \(P_{1}\) and \(P_{2}\).

Admin
Solution

Here first consider angle of \(P_{1} ,P_{2}\) and resultant force are respectively \(\theta_{1}, \theta_{2}\), and \(\theta_{3}\) from x-axis.

Now \(\theta_{1}=\tan ^{-1}=\frac{1}{4}=14.03^{\circ} \theta_{2}=\tan ^{-1}=\frac{2}{1}=63.43^{\circ} \theta_{3}=\tan ^{-1}=\frac{2}{3}=33.70^{\circ}\)

Now taking a 525 N force as a resultant, construct a parallelogram taking \(P_{1}\) and \(P_{2}\) as two adjacent sides of parallelogram as shown in figure Ex.9 (a).

Now in triangle OAC, apply sine rule

\( \frac{525}{\sin 49.4^{\circ}}=\frac{P_{1}}{\sin 47.73^{\circ}}=\frac{P_{2}}{\sin 82.87 ^{\circ}}\)

 

\(\therefore P_{2}=\frac{525 \sin 82.87 ^{\circ}}{sin 49.4^{\circ}}=686.11 N\)

 

\( \therefore P_{1}=\frac{525 \sin 47.73 ^{\circ}}{sin 49.4^{\circ}}=511.66 N\)
