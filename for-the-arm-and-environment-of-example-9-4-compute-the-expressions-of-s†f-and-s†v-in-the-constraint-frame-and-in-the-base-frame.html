Guest Question.

For the arm and environment of Example 9.4 compute the expressions of \(S^{\dagger }_{f} \) and \(S^{\dagger }_{\upsilon }\) in the constraint frame and in the base frame.

Admin
Solution

The computation of \(S^{ c\dagger }_{f} \) using the compliance matrix

\( C^{c}\) = \(\left[\begin{matrix} c_{1,1} & c_{1,2} \\ c_{1,2} & c_{2,2} \end{matrix} \right]\)

as a weighting matrix gives

\(S^{ c\dagger }_{f} \) = \(\left(S^{cT}_{f} C^{c} S^{c}_{f} \right)^{-1} S^{cT}_{f} C^{c} \) = \(\left[c^{-1}_{2,2} c_{1,2} 1 \right] \)

On the other hand, computing \(S^{ \dagger }_{f} \) in the base frame gives

\(S^{ \dagger }_{f} \) = \(\left(S^{T}_{f} C S_{f} \right)^{-1} S^{T}_{f} C \) = \(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} } \left[\frac{c_{1,2} – c_{2,2}}{c_{2,2}} \frac{c_{1,2} + c_{2,2}}{c_{2,2}} \right] \)

where C = \(R_{c} C^{c} R^{T}_{c}\) Notice that \(S^{ \dagger }_{f} \) = \(S^{ c\dagger }_{f} R^{T}_{c} \) and the same transformation
rule holds for \(S^{\dagger }_{\upsilon }\) Also, if \( f^{ c}_{e} \) = \(\left[f^{c}_{x} f^{c}_{y}\right]^{T} \) ,then

\(\lambda \) = \(S^{c\dagger }_{f} f^{c}_{e}\) = \(S^{\dagger }_{f} f_{e}\) = \(\frac{c_{1,2}}{c_{2,2}} f^{c}_{x} + f^{c}_{y}\)

independently from the frame to which \( S^{ \dagger }_{f} \) and \(f_{e}\) are referred. On the other hand, if the contact force \( f^{c}_{e}\) belongs to subspace R \( \left(S^{c}_{f}\right)\), i.e., \( f^{c}_{e}\) = \(\left[0 f^{c}_{y}\right]^{T} \) then it is \(\lambda\) = \( f^{c}_{y}\) independently from the particular weighting matrix.
Analogously, the computation of \(S^{c\dagger }_{\upsilon }\) using the stiffness matrix

\( K^{c} \) = \(\left[\begin{matrix}k_{1,1} & k_{1,2} \\ k_{1,2} & k_{2,2} \end{matrix} \right] \)

as a weighting matrix gives

\(S^{ c\dagger }_{\upsilon} \) = \(\left(S^{cT}_{\upsilon} K^{c} S^{c}_{\upsilon} \right)^{-1} S^{cT}_{\upsilon} K^{c} = \left[1 k^{-1}_{1,1} k_{1,2} \right] \)

in the constraint frame, and

\(S^{ \dagger }_{\upsilon} \) = \(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} } \left[\frac{k_{1,1} – k_{1,2}}{k_{1,1}} \frac{k_{1,1} + k_{1,2}}{k_{1,1}} \right] \)

in the base frame.
