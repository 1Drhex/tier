Guest Question.

Determine the maximum deflection for the beam shown in Fig. \( 6.7 (\mathrm{a})\) by the moment-area method.

Admin
Solution

\( \boldsymbol{ M / E I } \) Diagram. The \(M / E I\) diagram is shown in Fig. \(6.7(\mathrm{~b})\)

Elastic Curve. The elastic curve for the beam is shown in Fig. \(6.7\) (c).

Slope at \(\boldsymbol{A} .\) The slope of the elastic curve is not known at any point on the beam, so we will use the tangent at support \(A\) as the reference tangent and determine its slope, \(\theta_{A}\), from the conditions that the deflections at the support points \(A\) and \(C\) are zero. From Fig. 6.7(c), we can see that

\( \theta_{A}=\frac{\Delta_{C A}}{15} \)

To evaluate the tangential deviation \(\Delta_{C A}\), we apply the second moment-area theorem:

\( \begin{aligned} \Delta_{C A} &=\text { moment of the area of the } M / E I \text { diagram between } A \text { and } C \text { about } C \\ \Delta_{C A} &=\frac{1}{E I}\left[\frac{1}{2}(400)(10)\left(\frac{10}{3}+5\right)+\frac{1}{2}(400)(5)\left(\frac{10}{3}\right)\right] \\ &=\frac{20,000 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3}}{E I} \end{aligned} \)

Therefore, the slope at \(A\) is

\( \theta_{A}=\frac{20,000 / E I}{15}=\frac{1,333.33 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{2}}{E I} \)

Location of the Maximum Deflection. If the maximum deflection occurs at point \(D\), located at a distance \(x_{m}\) from the left support \(A\) (see Fig. \(6.7\) (c)), then the slope at \(D\) must be zero; therefore,

\( \theta_{D A}=\theta_{A}=\frac{1,333.33 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{2}}{E I} \)

which indicates that in order for the slope at \(D\) to be zero (i.e., the maximum deflection occurs at \(D\) ), the area of the \(M / E I\) diagram between \(A\) and \(D\) must be equal to \(1,333.33 / E I\). We use this condition to determine the location of point \(D\) :

\( \theta_{D A}=\text { area of the } \frac{M}{E I} \text { diagram between } A \text { and } D=\frac{1,333.33}{E I} \)

or

\( \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{40 x_{m}}{E I}\right) x_{m}=\frac{1,333.33}{E I} \)

from which

\( x_{m}=8.16 \mathrm{~m} \)

Maximum Deflection. From Fig. 6.7(c), we can see that

\( \Delta_{\max }=\Delta_{A D} \)

where

\( \begin{aligned} \Delta_{A D} &=\text { moment of the area of the } M / E I \text { diagram between } A \text { and } D \text { about } A \\ &=\frac{1}{2} \frac{(40)(8.16)}{E I}(8.16)\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)(8.16) \\ &=\frac{7,244.51 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3}}{E I} \end{aligned} \)

Therefore,

\( \Delta_{\max }=\frac{7,244.51 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3}}{E I} \)

Substituting \(E=200 \mathrm{GPa}=200\left(10^{6}\right) \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{2}\) and \(I=700\left(10^{6}\right) \mathrm{mm}^{4}=700\left(10^{-6}\right) \mathrm{m}^{4}\), we obtain

\( \begin{array}{l} \Delta_{\max }=\frac{7,244.51}{200\left(10^{6}\right)(700)\left(10^{-6}\right)}=0.0517 \mathrm{~m} \\ \Delta_{\max }=51.7 \mathrm{~mm} \downarrow \end{array} \)
