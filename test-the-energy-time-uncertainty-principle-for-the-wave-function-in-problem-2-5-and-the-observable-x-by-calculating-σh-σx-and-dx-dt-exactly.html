Guest Question.

Test the energy-time uncertainty principle for the wave function in and the observable x, by calculating \( \sigma_{H}, \sigma_{x}, \text { and } d\langle x\rangle / d t \) exactly.

Admin
Solution

\( \Psi(x, t)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\psi_{1} e^{-i E_{1} t / \hbar}+\psi_{2} e^{-E_{2} t / \hbar}\right) . \quad H^{2} \Psi=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left[\left(H^{2} \psi_{1}\right) e^{-E_{1} t / \hbar}+\left(H^{2} \psi_{2}\right) e^{-i E_{2} t / \hbar}\right] \).

\( H \psi_{1}=E_{1} \psi_{1} \Rightarrow H^{2} \psi_{1}=E_{1} H \psi_{1}=E_{1}^{2} \psi_{1}, \text { and } H^{2} \psi_{2}=E_{2}^{2} \psi_{2} \), so

\( \left\langle H^{2}\right\rangle=\frac{1}{2}\left\langle\left(\psi_{1} e^{-i E_{1} t / \hbar}+\psi_{2} e^{-i E_{2} t / \hbar}\right) \mid\left(E_{1}^{2} \psi_{1} e^{-i E_{1} t / \hbar}+E_{2}^{2} \psi_{2} e^{-i E_{2} t / \hbar}\right)\right\rangle \).

\( =\frac{1}{2}\left(\left\langle\psi_{1} \mid \psi_{1}\right\rangle e^{i E_{1} t / \hbar} E_{1}^{2} e^{-i E_{1} t / \hbar}+\left\langle\psi_{1} \mid \psi_{2}\right\rangle e^{i E_{1} t / \hbar} E_{2}^{2} e^{-i E_{2} t / \hbar}\right. \)

\( \left.+\left\langle\psi_{2} \mid \psi_{1}\right\rangle e^{i E_{2} t / \hbar} E_{1}^{2} e^{-i E_{1} t / \hbar}+\left\langle\psi_{2} \mid \psi_{2}\right\rangle e^{i E_{2} t / \hbar} E_{2}^{2} e^{-i E_{2} t / \hbar}\right)=\frac{1}{2}\left(E_{1}^{2}+E_{2}^{2}\right) \).

Similarly, \( \langle H\rangle=\frac{1}{2}\left(E_{1}+E_{2}\right) \) (e)):

\( \sigma_{H}^{2}=\left\langle H^{2}\right\rangle-\langle H\rangle^{2}=\frac{1}{2}\left(E_{1}^{2}+E_{2}^{2}\right)-\frac{1}{4}\left(E_{1}+E_{2}\right)^{2}=\frac{1}{4}\left(2 E_{1}^{2}+2 E_{2}^{2}-E_{2}^{2}-E_{1}^{2}-2 E_{1} E_{2}\right) \).

\( =\frac{1}{4}\left(E_{1}^{2}-2 E_{1} E_{2}+E_{2}^{2}\right)=\frac{1}{4}\left(E_{2}-E_{1}\right)^{2} \). \( \sigma_{H}=\frac{1}{2}\left(E_{2}-E_{1}\right) \).

\( \left\langle x^{2}\right\rangle=\frac{1}{2}\left[\left\langle\psi_{1}\left|x^{2}\right| \psi_{1}\right\rangle+\left\langle\psi_{2}\left|x^{2}\right| \psi_{2}\right\rangle+\left\langle\psi_{1}\left|x^{2}\right| \psi_{2}\right\rangle e^{i\left(E_{1}-E_{2}\right) t / \hbar}+\left\langle\psi_{2}\left|x^{2}\right| \psi_{1}\right\rangle e^{i\left(E_{2}-E_{1}\right) t / \hbar}\right] \).

\( \left\langle\psi_{n}\left|x^{2}\right| \psi_{m}\right\rangle=\frac{2}{a} \int_{0}^{a} x^{2} \sin \left(\frac{n \pi}{a} x\right) \sin \left(\frac{m \pi}{a} x\right) d x=\frac{1}{a} \int_{0}^{a} x^{2}\left[\cos \left(\frac{n-m}{a} \pi x\right)-\cos \left(\frac{n+m}{a} \pi x\right)\right] d x \).

Now \( \int_{0}^{a} x^{2} \cos \left(\frac{k}{a} \pi x\right) d x=\left.\left\{\frac{2 a^{2} x}{k^{2} \pi^{2}} \cos \left(\frac{k}{a} \pi x\right)+\left(\frac{a}{k \pi}\right)^{3}\left[\left(\frac{k \pi x}{a}\right)^{2}-2\right] \sin \left(\frac{k}{a} \pi x\right)\right\}\right|_{0} ^{a} \).

\( =\frac{2 a^{3}}{k^{2} \pi^{2}} \cos (k \pi)=\frac{2 a^{3}}{k^{2} \pi^{2}}(-1)^{k} \) . (for k = nonzero integer).

\( \therefore\left\langle\psi_{n}\left|x^{2}\right| \psi_{m}\right\rangle=\frac{2 a^{2}}{\pi^{2}}\left[\frac{(-1)^{n-m}}{(n-m)^{2}}-\frac{(-1)^{n+m}}{(n+m)^{2}}\right]=\frac{2 a^{2}}{\pi^{2}}(-1)^{n+m} \frac{4 n m}{\left(n^{2}-m^{2}\right)^{2}} \).

So \( \left\langle\psi_{1}\left|x^{2}\right| \psi_{2}\right\rangle=\left\langle\psi_{2}\left|x^{2}\right| \psi_{1}\right\rangle=-\frac{16 a^{2}}{9 \pi^{2}} \) . Meanwhile, from , \( \left\langle\psi_{n}\left|x^{2}\right| \psi_{n}\right\rangle=a^{2}\left[\frac{1}{3}-\frac{1}{2(n \pi)^{2}}\right] \).

Thus \( \left\langle x^{2}\right\rangle=\frac{1}{2}\left\{a^{2}\left[\frac{1}{3}-\frac{1}{2 \pi^{2}}\right]+a^{2}\left[\frac{1}{3}-\frac{1}{8 \pi^{2}}\right]-\frac{16 a^{2}}{9 \pi^{2}}[\underbrace{e^{i\left(E_{2}-E_{1}\right) t / \hbar}+e^{-i\left(E_{2}-E_{1}\right) t / \hbar}}_{2 \cos \left(\frac{E_{2}-E_{1}}{h} t\right)}]\right\} \).

\( \frac{E_{2}-E_{1}}{\hbar}=\frac{(4-1) \pi^{2} \hbar^{2}}{2 m a^{2} \hbar}=\frac{3 \pi^{2} \hbar}{2 m a^{2}}=3 \omega \). [in the notation of (b)]:

\( \left\langle x^{2}\right\rangle=\frac{a^{2}}{2}\left[\frac{2}{3}-\frac{5}{8 \pi^{2}}-\frac{32}{9 \pi^{2}} \cos (3 \omega t)\right] \).(c), \( \langle x\rangle=\frac{a}{2}\left[1-\frac{32}{9 \pi^{2}} \cos (3 \omega t)\right] \).

So \( \sigma_{x}^{2}=\left\langle x^{2}\right\rangle-\langle x\rangle^{2}=\frac{a^{2}}{4}\left[\frac{4}{3}-\frac{5}{4 \pi^{2}}-\frac{64}{9 \pi^{2}} \cos (3 \omega t)-1+\frac{64}{9 \pi^{2}} \cos (3 \omega t)-\left(\frac{32}{9 \pi^{2}}\right)^{2} \cos ^{2}(3 \omega t)\right] \).

\( \sigma_{x}^{2}=\frac{a^{2}}{4}\left[\frac{1}{3}-\frac{5}{4 \pi^{2}}-\left(\frac{32}{9 \pi^{2}}\right)^{2} \cos ^{2}(3 \omega t)\right] \). And, from (d): \( \frac{d\langle x\rangle}{d t}=\frac{8 \hbar}{3 m a} \sin (3 \omega t) \).

Meanwhile, the energy-time uncertainty principle (Eq. 3.74) says

\( \sigma_{H} \sigma_{Q} \geq \frac{\hbar}{2}\left|\frac{d\langle Q\rangle}{d t}\right| \) (3.74).

\( \sigma_{H}^{2} \sigma_{x}^{2} \geq \frac{\hbar^{2}}{4}\left(\frac{d\langle x\rangle}{d t}\right)^{2} \) . Here

\( \sigma_{H}^{2} \sigma_{x}^{2}=\frac{1}{4}(3 \hbar \omega)^{2} \frac{a^{2}}{4}\left[\frac{1}{3}-\frac{5}{4 \pi^{2}}-\left(\frac{32}{9 \pi^{2}}\right)^{2} \cos ^{2}(3 \omega t)\right]=(\hbar \omega a)^{2}\left(\frac{3}{4}\right)^{2}\left[\frac{1}{3}-\frac{5}{4 \pi^{2}}-\left(\frac{32}{9 \pi^{2}}\right)^{2} \cos ^{2}(3 \omega t)\right] \).

\( \frac{\hbar^{2}}{4}\left(\frac{d\langle x\rangle}{d t}\right)^{2}=\left(\frac{\hbar}{2} \cdot \frac{8 \hbar}{3 m a}\right)^{2} \sin ^{2}(3 \omega t)=\left(\frac{8}{3 \pi^{2}}\right)^{2}(\hbar \omega a)^{2} \sin ^{2}(3 \omega t), \text { since } \frac{\hbar}{m a}=\frac{2 a \omega}{\pi^{2}} \).

So the uncertainty principle holds if

\( \left(\frac{3}{4}\right)^{2}\left[\frac{1}{3}-\frac{5}{4 \pi^{2}}-\left(\frac{32}{9 \pi^{2}}\right)^{2} \cos ^{2}(3 \omega t)\right] \geq\left(\frac{8}{3 \pi^{2}}\right)^{2} \sin ^{2}(3 \omega t) \).

which is to say, if

\( \frac{1}{3}-\frac{5}{4 \pi^{2}} \geq\left(\frac{32}{9 \pi^{2}}\right)^{2} \cos ^{2}(3 \omega t)+\left(\frac{4}{3} \frac{8}{3 \pi^{2}}\right)^{2} \sin ^{2}(3 \omega t)=\left(\frac{32}{9 \pi^{2}}\right)^{2} \).

Evaluating both sides: \( \frac{1}{3}-\frac{5}{4 \pi^{2}}=0.20668 ; \quad\left(\frac{32}{9 \pi^{2}}\right)^{2}=0.12978 \) So it holds. (Whew!)
