Guest Question.

An exothermic reaction, \(A \rightarrow 2 B\), takes place adiabatically in a stirred-tank system. This liquid phase reaction occurs at constant volume in a 100 -gal reactor. The reaction can be considered to be first order and irreversible with the rate constant given by

\(k=2.4 \times 10^{15} e^{-20,000 / T}\left(\min ^{-1}\right)\)

where \(T\) is in \({ }^{\circ} R\). Using the information below, derive a transfer function relating the exit temperature \(T\) to the inlet concentration \(c_{A i}\). State any assumptions that you make. Simplify the transfer function by making a first-order approximation and show that the approximation is valid by comparing the step responses of both the original and the approximate models.

Available Information

(i) Nominal steady-state conditions are:

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{T}=150{ }^{\circ} F , \quad \hat{c}_{A i}=0.8 lb mole / ft ^{3} \\&q=20 gal / min =\text { flow rate in and out of the reactor }\end{aligned}\)

(ii) Physical property data for the mixture at the nominal steady state: \(C_{p}=0.8 Btu / lb ^{\circ} F\),

\(\rho=52 lb / ft ^{3}, \quad-\Delta H_{R}=500 kJ / lb mole\)

Admin
Solution

Assumptions:

1) Perfectly mixed reactor
2) Constant fluid properties and heat of reaction

a) Component balance for A,

\(V \frac{d c_{A}}{d t}=q\left(c_{A i}-c_{A}\right)-V k(T) c_{A}\) (1)

Energy balance for the tank,

\(\rho V C \frac{d T}{d t}=\rho q C\left(T_{i}-T\right)+\left(-\Delta H_{R}\right) V k(T) c_{A}\) (2)

Since a transfer function with respect to \(c_{A i}\) is desired, assume the other inputs, namely \(q\) and \(T_{i}\), are constant. Linearize (1) and (2) and note that

\(\frac{d c_{A}}{d t}=\frac{d c_{A}^{\prime}}{d t}, \quad \frac{d T}{d t}=\frac{d T^{\prime}}{d t}\),

\(V \frac{d c_{A}^{\prime}}{d t}=q c_{A i}^{\prime}-(q+V k(\bar{T})) c_{A}^{\prime}-V \bar{c}_{A} k(\bar{T}) \frac{20000}{\bar{T}^{2}} T^{\prime}\) (3)

\(\rho V C \frac{d T^{\prime}}{d t}=-\left(\rho q C+\Delta H_{R} V \bar{c}_{A} k(\bar{T}) \frac{20000}{\bar{T}^{2}}\right) T^{\prime}-\Delta H_{R} V k(\bar{T}) c_{A}^{\prime}\) (4)

Taking Laplace transforms and rearranging

\([V s+q+V k(\bar{T})] C_{A}^{\prime}(s)=q C_{A i}^{\prime}(s)-V \bar{c}_{A} k(\bar{T}) \frac{20000}{\bar{T}^{2}} T^{\prime}(s)\) (5)

\(\left[\rho V C s+\rho q C-\left(-\Delta H_{R}\right) V \bar{c}_{A} k(\bar{T}) \frac{20000}{\bar{T}^{2}}\right] T^{\prime}(s)=\left(-\Delta H_{R}\right) V k(\bar{T}) C_{A}^{\prime}(s)\) (6)

Substituting \(C_{A}^{\prime}(s)\) from Eq. 5 into Eq. 6 and rearranging,

\(\frac{T^{\prime}(s)}{C_{A}^{\prime}(s)}=\frac{\left(-\Delta H_{R}\right) V k(\bar{T}) q}{[V s+q+V k(\bar{T})]\left[\rho V C s+\rho q C-\left(-\Delta H_{R}\right) V \bar{c}_{A} k(\bar{T}) \frac{20000}{\bar{T}^{2}}\right]+\left(-\Delta H_{R}\right) V^{2} \bar{c}_{A} k^{2}(\bar{T}) \frac{20000}{\bar{T}^{2}}}\) (7)

\(\bar{c}_{A}\) is obtained from Eq. 1 at steady state,

\(\bar{c}_{A}=\frac{q \bar{c}_{A i}}{q+V k(\bar{T})}=0.01159 lb mol / cu . ft.\)

Substituting the numerical values of \(\bar{T}, \rho, C,-\Delta H_{R}, q, V, \bar{c}_{A}\) into Eq. 7 and simplifying,

\(\frac{T^{\prime}(s)}{C_{A i}^{\prime}(s)}=\frac{12.69}{(0.082 s+1)(5 s+1)}\)

For step response, \(C_{A i}^{\prime}(s)=1 / s\)

\(\begin{aligned}&T^{\prime}(s)=\frac{12.69}{(0.082 s+1)(5 s+1) s} \\&T^{\prime}(t)=12.69\left[1+\frac{1}{(50-0.082)}\left(0.082 e^{-t / 0.082}-5 e^{-t / 5}\right)\right]\end{aligned}\)

A first-order approximation of the transfer function is

\(\frac{T^{\prime}(s)}{C_{A_{i}}^{\prime}(s)}=\frac{12.69}{5 s+1}\)

For step response, \(T^{\prime}(s)=\frac{12.69}{s(5 s+1)} \text { or } T^{\prime}(t)=12.69\left[1-e^{-t / 5}\right]\)

The two step responses are very close to each other hence the approximation is valid. The ODE calculation indicates a slightly different gain due to linearization.
