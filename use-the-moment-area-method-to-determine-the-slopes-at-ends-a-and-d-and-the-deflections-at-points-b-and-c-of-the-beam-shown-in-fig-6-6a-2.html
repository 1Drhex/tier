Guest Question.

Use the moment-area method to determine the slopes at ends \(A\) and \(D\) and the deflections at points \(B\) and \(C\) of the beam shown in \(Fig. 6.6(a)\).

Admin
Solution

\(M/EI\) Diagram Because \(EI\) is constant along the length of the beam, the shape of the \(M/EI\) diagram is the same as that of the bending moment diagram. The \(M/EI\) diagram is shown in Fig. 6.6(b).

Elastic Curve The elastic curve for the beam is shown in Fig. 6.6(c).

Slope at \(A\) The slope of the elastic curve is not known at any point on the beam, so we will use the tangent at support \(A\) as the reference tangent and determine its slope, \(\theta_A\), from the conditions that the deflections at the support points \(A\) and \(D\) are zero. From Fig. 6.6(c), we can see that

\(\theta_A=\frac{\Delta_{DA}}{L}\)

in which \(\theta_A\)is assumed to be so small that \(\tan \theta_A \approx \theta_A\). To evaluate the tangential deviation \(\Delta_{DA}\), we apply the second moment-area theorem:

\(\Delta_{DA}=\)moment of the area of the \(M/EI\) diagram between \(A\) and \(D\) about \(D\)

 

\(\Delta_{DA}=\frac{1}{EI}[\frac{1}{2}(800)(20)(\frac{20}{3}+20)+\frac{1}{2}(200)(10)(\frac{20}{3}+10)\)

 

\(+600(10)(15)+\frac{1}{2}(600)(10)(\frac{20}{3})]\)

 

\(=\frac{340,000 k-ft^3}{EI}\)

Therefore, the slope at \(A\) is

\(\theta_A=\frac{\Delta_{DA}}{L}=\frac{340,000/EI}{40}=\frac{8,500 k-ft^2}{EI}\)

Substituting the numerical values of \(E\) and \(I\) , we obtain

\(\theta_A=\frac{8,500(12)^2}{(1,800)(46,000)}= 0.015 rad\)

 

\(\theta_A= 0.015 rad\)

Ans.

Slope at \(D\) From Fig. 6.6(c), we can see that

\(\theta_D=\theta_{DA} – \theta_A\)

in which, according to the first moment-area theorem,

\(\theta_{DA}=\)area of the \(M/EI\) diagram between \(A\) and \(D\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{EI}[\frac{1}{2}(800)(20) +\frac{1}{2}(200)(10) + 600(10) +\frac{1}{2}(600)(10)]\)

 

\(=\frac{18,000 k-ft^2}{EI}\)

Therefore,

\(\theta_{D}=\frac{18,000}{EI}-\frac{8,500}{EI}=\frac{9,500 k-ft^2}{EI}\)

 

\(\theta_{D}=\frac{9,500 (12)^2}{(1,800)(46,000)}= 0.017 rad\)

 

\(\theta_{D}= 0.017 rad\)

Ans.

Deflection at \(B\) Considering the portion \(AB\) of the elastic curve in Fig. 6.6(c), and realizing that \(\theta_{A}\) is so small that \(\tan \theta_A \approx \theta_A\), we write

\(\theta_A = \frac{\Delta_B + \Delta_{BA}}{20}\)

from which

\(\Delta_B = 20\theta_A – \Delta_{BA}\)

where

\(\Delta_{BA}=\) moment of the area of the \(M/EI\) diagram between \(A\) and \(B\) about \(B\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{EI}[\frac{1}{2}(800)(20)(\frac{20}{3})]\)

 

\(\frac{53,333.33 k-ft^3}{EI}\)

Therefore,

\( \Delta_B=20(\frac{8,500}{EI})-\frac{53,333.33}{EI}=\frac{116,666.67 k-ft^3}{EI}\)

 

\( \Delta_B=\frac{116,666.67(12)^3}{(1,800)(46,000)}=2.43 in.\)

 

\( \Delta_B=2.43 in. \downarrow \) Ans.

Deflection at \(C\) Finally, considering the portion \(CD\) of the elastic curve in Fig. 6.6(c) and assuming \(\theta _D\) to be small (so that \(\tan \theta_D \approx \theta_D\)), we write

\(\theta_D= \frac{\Delta_C +\Delta_{CD}}{10}\)

or

\(\Delta_C =10\theta_D – \Delta_{CD}\)

where

\(\Delta_{CD}=\frac{1}{EI}[\frac{1}{2}(600)(10)(\frac{10}{3})]=\frac{10,000 k-ft^3}{EI}\)

Therefore,

\(\Delta_C= 10(\frac{9,500}{EI})-\frac{10,000}{EI}=\frac{85,000 k-ft^3}{EI}\)

 

\(\Delta_C=\frac{85,000(12)^3}{(1,800)(46,000)}=1.77 in.\)

 

\(\Delta_C=1.77 in. \downarrow \) Ans.
