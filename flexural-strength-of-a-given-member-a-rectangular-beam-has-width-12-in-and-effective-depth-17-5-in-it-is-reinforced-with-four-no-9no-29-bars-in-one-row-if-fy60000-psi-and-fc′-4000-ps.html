Guest Question.

Flexural strength of a given member. A rectangular beam has width 12 in. and effective depth 17.5 in. It is reinforced with four No. 9 (No. 29) bars in one row. If \(f_{y}\) = 60,000 psi and \(\acute{f}_{c}\) = 4000 psi, what is the nominal flexural strength, and what is the maximum moment that can be utilized in design, according to the ACI Code?

of Appendix A, the area of four No. 9 (No. 29) bars is 4.00 in². Assuming that the beam is underreinforced and using Eq.(4.28),

 

\(a=\frac{A_{s}f_{y}}{0.85\acute{f} _{c}b}=\frac{4.00\times 60}{0.85\times 4\times 12}=5.88 \) in.

 

The depth of the neutral axis is c = a/β1 = 5.88/0.85 = 6.92, giving

 

\(\frac{c}{d_{t} }=\frac{6.92}{17.5}=0.395 \)

 

which is between 0.429 and 0.375, the values corresponding, respectively, to \(\epsilon _{t}\)= 0.004 and \(\epsilon _{t}\)= 0.005, as shown in Fig. 4.9 . Thus, the beam is, as assumed, underreinforced, and from Eq. (4.27)

 

\( M_{n} =A_{s}f_{y}\left( d-\frac{a}{2}\right)=4.00\times 60\left(17.5- \frac{5.88}{2} \right) =3490\) in-kips

 

The fact that the beam is unreinforced could also have been established by calculating ρ = 4.00/(12 × 17.5) = 0.190, which just exceeds \(\rho_{0.005}\) , which is calculated using Eq. (4.26 c ).

 

\(\rho_{0.005}=0.85\beta _{1}\frac{\acute{f} _{c}}{f_{y}}\frac{\epsilon _{u} }{\epsilon _{u}+ 0.005}=0.85\times 0.85\frac{4}{60}\frac{0.003}{0.003+ 0.005}=0.0181 \)

 

Because the net tensile strain \(\epsilon _{t}\) is between 0.004 and 0.005, ϕ must be calculated: \(\epsilon _{t}= \epsilon _{u}\) ( d − c )/c = 0.003 × (17.5 − 6.92)/6.92 = 0.00458. Using linear interpolation from Fig. 4.8 , ϕ = 0.87, and the design strength is taken as

 

\(\phi M_{n}\) = 0.87 × 3490 = 3040 in-kips

 

The ACI Code limits on the reinforcement ratio

 

\(\rho_{0.004}=0.85\beta _{1}\frac{\acute{f} _{c}}{f_{y}}\frac{\epsilon _{u} }{\epsilon _{u}+ 0.004}=0.85\times 0.85\frac{4}{60}\frac{0.003}{0.003+ 0.004}=0.0206 \)

 

\( \rho _{\min } =\frac{3\sqrt{\acute{f} _{c}} }{f_{y}} \geq \frac{200}{f_{y}} =\frac{3\sqrt{4000} }{60,000}\geq \frac{200}{60,000}=0.0033\)

 

are satisfied for this beam.
