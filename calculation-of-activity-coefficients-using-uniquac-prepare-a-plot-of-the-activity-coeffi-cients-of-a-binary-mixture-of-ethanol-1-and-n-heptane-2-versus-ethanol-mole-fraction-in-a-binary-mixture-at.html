Calculation of Activity Coefficients Using UNIQUAC

Prepare a plot of the activity coefficients of a binary mixture of ethanol (1) and n-heptane (2) versus ethanol mole fraction in a binary mixture at 50째C using the UNIQUAC activity coefficient model. The UNIQUAC parameters are reported as follows:

ethanol n-heptane
r 2.11 5.17 and \(a_{12}=-105.23\)
q 1.97 4.40 \(a_{21}=-1380.3\)
q’ 0.92 4.40

Compare the results to the following set of measured data:

\(\begin{array}{llllllllllll}x_{1} & 0.0514 & 0.118 & 0.3022 & 0.4383 & 0.5862 & 0.6646 & 0.7327 & 0.772 & 0.823 & 0.8788 & 0.9274 & 0.9769 \\\gamma_{1} & 9.837 & 5.978 & 2.650 & 1.876 & 1.427 & 1.283 & 1.184 & 1.136 & 1.091 & 1.143 & 1.148 & 1.045 \\\gamma_{2} & 0.985 & 1.069 & 1.320 & 1.629 & 2.158 & 2.550 & 3.161 & 3.561 & 4.249 & 4.184 & 4.540 & 6.719\end{array}\)

 

Admin
Solution

We need to calculate \(\gamma_{1} \text { and } \gamma_{2}\) over the range of mole fractions. We illustrate the calculation procedure using the value of \(x_{1}=0.3022\). From the expressions in Table 7.4, we have:

 

ethanol n-heptane
\(\Phi_{i}^{*}=\frac{x_{i} r_{i}}{x_{1} r_{1}+x_{2} r_{2}}\) 0.150 0.850
\(\theta_{i}=\frac{x_{1} q_{i}}{x_{1} q_{1}+x_{2} q_{2}}\) 0.162 0.838
\(\theta_{i}^{\prime}=\frac{x_{i} q_{i}^{\prime}}{x_{1} q_{1}^{\prime}+x_{2} q_{2}^{\prime}}\) 0.083 0.917
\(l_{i}\) -0.41 -0.32

 

For the combinatorial part of the activity coefficients, we get:

 

\(\ln \gamma_{1, \text { combinatorial }}=\ln \frac{\Phi_{1}^{*}}{x_{1}}+\frac{z}{2} q_{1} \ln \frac{\theta_{1}}{\Phi_{1}^{*}}+\Phi_{2}^{*}\left(l_{1}-l_{2} \frac{r_{1}}{r_{2}}\right)=-0.17\)

 

and,

 

\(\ln \gamma_{2, \text { combinatorial }}=\ln \frac{\Phi_{2}^{*}}{x_{2}}+\frac{z}{2} q_{2} \ln \frac{\theta_{2}}{\Phi_{2}^{*}}+\Phi_{1}^{*}\left(l_{2}-l_{1} \frac{r_{2}}{r_{1}}\right)=-0.0184\)

 

For the residual part of the activity coefficients, we calculate the energy parameters:

 

\(\tau_{12}=\exp \left(-\frac{a_{12}}{T}\right)=1.39 \text { and } \tau_{21}=\exp \left(-\frac{a_{21}}{T}\right)=0.014\)

 

With these values, we get:

 

\(\ln \gamma_{1, \text { residual }}=-q_{1}^{\prime} \ln \left(\theta_{1}^{\prime}+\theta_{2}^{\prime} \tau_{21}\right)+\theta_{2}^{\prime} q_{1}^{\prime}\left(\frac{\tau_{21}}{\theta_{1}^{\prime}+\theta_{2}^{\prime} \tau_{21}}-\frac{\tau_{12}}{\theta_{1}^{\prime} \tau_{12}+\theta_{2}^{\prime}}\right)=1.148\)

 

and,

 

\(\ln \gamma_{2, \text { residual }}=-q_{2}^{\prime} \ln \left(\theta_{1}^{\prime} \tau_{12}+\theta_{2}^{\prime}\right)+\theta_{1}^{\prime} q_{2}^{\prime}\left(\frac{\tau_{12}}{\theta_{1}^{\prime} \tau_{12}+\theta_{2}^{\prime}}-\frac{\tau_{21}}{\theta_{1}^{\prime}+\theta_{2}^{\prime} \tau_{21}}\right)=0.299\)

 

Summing the combinatorial and residual parts and taking the exponential gives:

 

\(\gamma_{1}=2.67 \text { and } \gamma_{2}=1.32\).

 

These values are close to the experimentally measured values of \(\gamma_{1}^{\exp }=2.65 \text { and } \gamma_{2}^{\exp }=1.32\).

 

In this manner, we can calculate the activity coefficients for this system for different mole fractions of ethanol. A plot of the resulting data is shown in Figure E7.12. The UNIQUAC activity coefficient model represents the data well. Some discrepancy shows at high ethanol mole fractions where hydrogen bonding contributes significantly to the system behavior.
