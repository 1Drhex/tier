Guest Question.

Determine the response of the system of Figure 6.5 when using \( x_{1}\) and \( x_{2}\) as generalized coordinates when \(\dot{2}(0)=2\frac{m}{s} \) and all other initial conditions are zero.

Admin
Solution

The differential equations of motion for the system are

\( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 2 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\left[\begin{matrix} \ddot{x}_{1} \\ \ddot{x}_{2} \end{matrix} \right] +\begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} \dot{x}_{1} \\ \dot{x}_{2} \end{matrix} \right]+\begin{bmatrix} 6 & -2 \\ -2 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \end{matrix} \right]=\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{matrix} \end{matrix}\) (a)

Assume a solution of

\(\left[\begin{matrix} x_{1}(t) \\ x_{2}(t) \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 \\ \chi \end{matrix} \right]e^{\lambda t} \) (b)

The values of \(\lambda\) which lead to a non-trivial solution of Equation (b) are the roots of

\( \begin{vmatrix} \lambda ^{2}+2\lambda +6& -\lambda -2 \\ -\lambda -2 & 2\lambda^{2}+3\lambda +8 \end{vmatrix} =0\) (c)

Evaluation of the determinant leads to

\( (\lambda ^{2}+2\lambda+6)(2\lambda ^{2}+3\lambda +8)-(\lambda +2)^{2}=0\) (d)

The roots of the fourth-order equation are \( \lambda =-0.5122\pm 1.7436i,-1.2378\pm 2.2648i\). The system vibrates at frequencies \(\omega _{1}=1.7436 \) and \(\omega _{2}=2.2468 \). The complex modal fraction is determined from

\(\begin{bmatrix} \lambda ^{2}+2\lambda +6 & -\lambda -2 \\ -\lambda -2 & 2\lambda ^{2}+\lambda +8 \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} 1 \\ \chi \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right] \) (e)

The two equations represented by Equation (e) for the values of \(\lambda\) obtained previously are dependent. Thus, only the first equation is used, as

\((\lambda ^{2}+2\lambda +6)-(\lambda +2)\chi =0 \) (f)

or

\(\chi \frac{\lambda ^{2}+2\lambda +6}{\lambda +2} \) (g)

For \(\lambda =-0.5122-1.7436i \), the evaluation leads to

\( \chi =\frac{(-0.5122-1.7436i)^{2}+2(-0.5122-1.7436i)+6}{2-0.5122-1.7436i} \)

\(=(1.817+0.248i) \) (h)

For \(\lambda =-0.5122+1.7436i\), the evaluation leads to \(\chi =(1.817-0.248i)\). For \(-1.2378\pm 2.2648i\) the evaluation of Equation (g) leads to \(\chi =(-0.435\mp 0.115i)\).

Using Equation (6.21), the response can be written as

\(\begin{bmatrix} x_{1}(t) \\ x_{2}(t) \end{bmatrix}=e^{-0.5122t}\left(C_{1} \begin{bmatrix} &1 &\\ 1.817 & -&0.248i \end{bmatrix}e^{il.7436t}+ C_{2}\begin{bmatrix} &1& \\ 1.817 & -&0.248i& \end{bmatrix}e^{il.7436t}\right) \)

\(+e^{-1.2378t}\left(C_{3} \begin{bmatrix} &1 &\\-0.435 & – &0.115i \end{bmatrix}e^{i2.2468}+ C_{4}\begin{bmatrix} &1& \\ -0.435 & +& 0.115& \end{bmatrix}e^{i2.2468}\right) \) (i)

\(_{X}(t)=C_{1}X_{1}e^{\lambda _{1}t} +C_{2}X_{2}e^{\lambda _{2}t}+C_{3}X_{3}e^{\lambda _{3}t}+C_{4}X_{4}e^{\lambda _{4}t}\) (6.21)

or

\(\left[\begin{matrix} x_{1}(t) \\ x_{2}(t) \end{matrix} \right]=e^{-0.5122t }\left\{A_{1}\left(\left[\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 1.817 \end{matrix} \right]\cos1.744t- \left[\begin{matrix}0 \\ 0.248 \end{matrix} \right]\sin1.744 t\right) \\ \\+A_{2}\left(\left[\begin{matrix}1 \\ 1.817 \end{matrix} \right]\sin1.744t +\left[\begin{matrix}0 \\ 0.248 \end{matrix} \right] \cos1.744t \right) \right\} \)

\(+e^{-1.2378t}\left\{A_{3}\left(\left[\begin{matrix} 1 \\ -0.435 \end{matrix} \right]\cos2.247t -\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ -0.115 \end{matrix} \right]\sin2.247t \right)\\+A_{4}\left(\left[\begin{matrix} 1 \\ -0.435 \end{matrix} \right]\sin2.247t+\left[\begin{matrix}0 \\-0.115 \end{matrix} \right]\cos2.247t \right) \right\} \) (j)

Applying the initial conditions leads to

\(\left[\begin{matrix} x_{1}(0) \\ x_{2}(0) \\ \dot{x}_{2}(0)\\ \dot{x}_{2}(0) \end{matrix} \right]= \left[\begin{matrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\2 \end{matrix} \right]= \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0&1&0 \\ 1.817&0.248 &-0.435 &-0.115 \\-0.5122 & 1.744 &-1.238 &2.247 \\-1.390 &3.295 &0.871 &-0.258\end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix}A_{1} \\ A_{2} \\ A_{3} \\A_{4} \end{matrix} \right] \) (k)

Solution of Equation (k) leads to \(A_{1}=4.49,A_{2}=-1.95, A_{3}=-2.12\) and \(A_{4}=3.29\).

Substitution of these results into Equation (j) leads to

\(\left[\begin{matrix} x_{1}(t) \\ x_{2} (t) \end{matrix} \right]=e^{-0.512t }\left(\left[\begin{matrix} 4.49 \\ 7.68 \end{matrix}\right]\cos1.74t+\left[\begin{matrix} -1.95 \\ -4.66 \end{matrix} \right]\sin 1.74t \right) \)

\(+e^{-1.237t}\left(\left[\begin{matrix} -2.13 \\0.54\end{matrix} \right] \cos2.25t+\left[\begin{matrix}3.29 \\ -1.67 \end{matrix} \right]\sin 2.25t \right) \) (l)
