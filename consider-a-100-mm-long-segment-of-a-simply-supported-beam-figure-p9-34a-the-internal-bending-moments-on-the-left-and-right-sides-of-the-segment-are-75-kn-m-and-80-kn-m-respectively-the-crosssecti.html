Guest Question.

Consider a 100-mm-long segment of a simply supported beam (Figure P9.34a). The internal bending moments on the left and right sides of the segment are 75 kN-m and 80 kN-m, respectively. The crosssectional dimensions of the flanged shape are shown in Figure P9.34b. Determine the maximum horizontal shear stress in the beam at this location.

Admin
Solution

Centroid location in y direction:

\(\begin{array}{lccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} \text { (from bottom) } \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 9,000 & 300 & 2,700,000 \\\text { web } & 8,400 & 165 & 1,386,000 \\\text { bottom flange } & 15,000 & 30 & 450,000 \\\hline & 32,400 mm ^{2} & & 4,536,000 mm ^{3}\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{4,536,000 mm ^{3}}{32,400 mm ^{2}} &=140 mm \text{(from bottom of shape to centroid)} \\&=190 mm \text{(from top of shape to centroid)}\end{aligned}\)

 

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

\(\begin{aligned}&\begin{array}{lcccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 2,700,000 & 160 & 230,400,000 & 233,100,000 \\\text { web } & 30,870,000 & 25 & 5,250,000 & 36,120,000 \\\text { bottom flange } & 4,500,000 & -110 & 181,500,000 & 186,000,000 \\\hline\end{array}\\&\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( mm ^{4}\right)=455,220,000\end{aligned}\)

 

Shear force in beam:

\(V=\frac{\Delta M}{\Delta x}=\frac{80 kN – m -75 kN – m }{100 mm }=\frac{5 kN – m }{0.1 m }=50 kN\)

Maximum horizontal shear stress:
At neutral axis:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q_{\max }=(250 mm )(60 mm )(110 mm )+(40 mm )(80 mm )(40 mm )=1,778,000 mm ^{3} \\&\tau_{\max }=\frac{(50,000 N )\left(1,778,000 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(455,220,000 mm ^{4}\right)(40 mm )}=4.88 MPa\end{aligned}\)
