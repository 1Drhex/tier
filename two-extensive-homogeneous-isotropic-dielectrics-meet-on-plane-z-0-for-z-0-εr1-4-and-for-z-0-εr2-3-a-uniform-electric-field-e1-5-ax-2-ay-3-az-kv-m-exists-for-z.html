Guest Question.

Two extensive homogeneous isotropic dielectrics meet on plane \(z = 0\). For \(z\gt 0, \varepsilon _{r1}=4\) and for \(z\lt 0\), \(\varepsilon _{r2}=3\). A uniform electric field \(E_{1}=5a_{x}-2a_{y}+3a_{z}kV/m\) exists for \(z\geq 0\). Find

(a) \(E_{2}\) for \(z\leq 0\)

(b) The angles \(E_{1}\) and \(E_{2}\) make with the interface

(c) The energy densities (in \(J/m^{3}\)) in both dielectrics

(d) The energy within a cube of side 2m centered at \((3, 4, -5)\)

Admin
Solution

Let the problem be as illustrated in Figure 5.15.
(a) Since \(a_{z}\) is normal to the boundary plane, we obtain the normal components as

\(E_{1n}=E_{1}\cdot a_{n}=E_{1}\cdot a_{z}=3\)

\(E_{1n}=3a_{z}\)

\(E_{2n}=\left(E_{2}\cdot a_{z}\right)a_{z}\)

Also

\(E=E_{n}+E_{t}\)

Hence

\(E_{1t}=E_{1}-E_{1n}=5a_{x}-2a_{y}\)

Thus

\(E_{2t}=E_{1t}=5a_{x}-2a_{y}\)

Similarly

\(D_{2n}=D_{1n}\rightarrow \varepsilon_{r2}E_{2n}=\varepsilon_{r1}E_{1n}\)

or

\(E_{2n}=\frac{\varepsilon_{r1}}{\varepsilon_{r2}}E_{1n}=\frac{4}{3}\left(3a_{z}\right)=4a_{z}\)

Thus

\(E_{2}=E_{2t}+E_{2n}\)

\(=5a_{x}-2a_{y}+4a_{z}kV/m\)

(b) Let \(\alpha_{1}\) and \(\alpha_{2}\) be the angles \(E_{1}\) and \(E_{2}\) they make with the interface while \(\theta_{1}\) and \(\theta_{2}\) are the angles they make with the normal to the interface as shown in Figure 5.15; that is

\(\alpha_{1}=90-\theta_{1}\)

\(\alpha_{2}=90-\theta_{2}\)

Since \(E_{1n}=3\) and

\(E_{1t}=\sqrt{25+4}=\sqrt{29}\)

\(\tan\theta_{1}=\frac{E_{1t}}{E_{1n}}=\frac{\sqrt{29}}{3}=1.795\rightarrow \theta_{1}=60.9^{\circ}\)

Hence

\(\alpha _{1}=29.1^{\circ}\)

Alternatively

\(E_{1}\cdot a_{n}=\left|E_{1}\right|\cdot 1\cdot\cos\theta_{1}\)

or

\(\cos\theta_{1}=\frac{3}{\sqrt{38}}=0.4867\rightarrow \theta_{1}=60.9^{\circ}\)

Similarly

\(E_{2n}=4, E_{2t}=E_{1t}=\sqrt{29}\)

\(\tan\theta_{2}=\frac{E_{2t}}{E_{2n}}=\frac{\sqrt{29}}{4}=1.346\rightarrow \theta_{2}=53.4^{\circ}\)

Hence

\(\alpha _{2}=36.6^{\circ}\)

Note that \(\frac{\tan\theta_{1}}{\tan\theta_{2}}=\frac{\varepsilon _{r1}}{\varepsilon _{r2}}\) is satisfied.

(c) The energy densities are given by

\(w_{E1}=\frac{1}{2}\varepsilon _{1}\left|E_{1}\right|^{2}=\frac{1}{2}\times 4\times \frac{10^{-9}}{36\pi}\times\left(25+4+9\right)\times 10^{6}=672\mu J/m^{3} \)

\(w_{E2}=\frac{1}{2}\varepsilon _{2}\left|E_{2}\right|^{2}=\frac{1}{2}\times 3\times \frac{10^{-9}}{36\pi}\times\left(25+4+16\right)\times 10^{6}=597\mu J/m^{3}\)

(d) At the center \((3, 4, -5)\) of the cube of side \(2 m, z=-5\lt 0\); that is, the cube is in region 2 with \(2\leq x\leq 4, 3\leq y\leq 5, -6\leq z\leq -4\).

Hence

\(W_{E}=\int w_{E2}dv=\int_{x=2}^{4}\int_{y=3}^{5}\int_{z=-6}^{-4}w_{E2}dzdydz=w_{E2}\left(2\right)\left(2\right)\left(2\right)=597\times 8 \mu J=4.776mJ\)
