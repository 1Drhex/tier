Guest Question.

An air receiver consisting of a cylinder closed by hemispherical ends is shown in Fig. 22.3. It has a storage capacity of 0.25 mÂ³ and an operating internal pressure of 5 MPa. It is made of plain carbon steel 10C4 \( \left(S_{u t}=340 N / mm ^{2}\right) \) and the factor of safety is 4. Neglecting the effect of welded joints, determine the dimensions of the receiver.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } V=0.25 m ^{3} \quad P_{i}=5 MPa \quad L=2 D_{i} \).

\( S_{u t}=340 N / mm ^{2} \quad(f s)=4 \).

Step I Diameter and length of cylindrical portion

\( V=\frac{\pi}{4} D_{i}^{2} L+\frac{\pi}{6} D_{i}^{3} \quad \text { and } \quad L=2 D_{i} \).

\( V=\frac{\pi}{4} D_{i}^{2}\left(2 D_{i}\right)+\frac{\pi}{6} D_{i}^{3}=\pi D_{i}^{3}\left[\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{6}\right]=\frac{2}{3} \pi D_{i}^{3} \).

Therefore,

\( D_{i}=\sqrt[3]{\frac{3 V}{2 \pi}}=\sqrt[3]{\frac{3(0.25)}{2 \pi}}=0.492 m \text { or } 500 mm \) (i).

\( L=2 D_{i}=2(500)=1000 mm \) (ii)
Step II Thickness of cylindrical portion

\( \sigma_{t}=\frac{S_{u t}}{\left(f_{s}\right)}=\frac{340}{4}=85 N / mm ^{2} \).

The thickness of the cylinder wall is given by

\( t=\frac{P_{i} D_{i}}{2 \sigma_{t}}=\frac{(5)(500)}{2(85)}=14.7 mm \text { or } 15 mm \). (iii)
Step III Thickness of hemispherical ends
The thickness of the hemispherical ends is given by,

\( t=\frac{P_{i} D_{i}}{4 \sigma_{t}}=\frac{(5)(500)}{4(85)}=7.35 mm \text { or } 8 mm \) (iv).
