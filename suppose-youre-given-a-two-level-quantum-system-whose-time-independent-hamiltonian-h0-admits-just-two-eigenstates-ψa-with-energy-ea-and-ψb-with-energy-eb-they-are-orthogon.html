Guest Question.

Suppose you’re given a two-level quantum system whose (time-independent) Hamiltonian \( H^{0} \) admits just two eigenstates, \( \psi_{a} \)(with energy \( \left.\left.E_{a}\right), \text { and } \psi_{b} \text { (with energy } E_{b}\right) \). They are orthogonal, normalized, and nondegenerate (assume \( E_a \) is the smaller of the two energies). Now we turn on a perturbation H′, with the following matrix elements:

\( \left\langle\psi_{a}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{a}\right\rangle=\left\langle\psi_{b}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{b}\right\rangle=0 ; \quad\left\langle\psi_{a}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{b}\right\rangle=\left|\psi_{b}\right| H^{\prime}\left|\psi_{a}\right\rangle=h \) (8.74).

where h is some specified constant.

(a) Find the exact eigenvalues of the perturbed Hamiltonian.

(b) Estimate the energies of the perturbed system using second-order perturbation theory.

(c) Estimate the ground state energy of the perturbed system using the variational principle, with a trial function of the form

\( \psi=(\cos \phi) \psi_{a}+(\sin \phi) \psi_{b} \) (8.75).

where ϕ is an adjustable parameter. Note: Writing the linear combination in this way is just a neat way to guarantee that ψ is normalized.

(d) Compare your answers to (a), (b), and (c). Why is the variational principle so accurate, in this case?

Admin
Solution

(a)

\( H =\left(\begin{array}{cc} E_{a} & h \\ h & E_{b} \end{array}\right) ; \operatorname{det}( H -\lambda)=\left(E_{a}-\lambda\right)\left(E_{b}-\lambda\right)-h^{2}=0 \Longrightarrow \lambda^{2}-\lambda\left(E_{a}+E_{b}\right)+E_{a} E_{b}-h^{2}=0 \).

\( \lambda=\frac{1}{2}\left(E_{a}+E_{b} \pm \sqrt{E_{a}^{2}+2 E_{a} E_{b}+E_{b}^{2}-4 E_{a} E_{b}+4 h^{2}}\right) \Rightarrow E_{\pm}=\frac{1}{2}\left[E_{a}+E_{b} \pm \sqrt{\left(E_{a}-E_{b}\right)^{2}+4 h^{2}}\right] \).

(b) Zeroth order: \( E_{a}^{0}=E_{a}, E_{b}^{0}=E_{b} . \text { First order: } E_{a}^{1}=\left\langle\psi_{a}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{a}\right\rangle=0, E_{b}^{1}=\left\langle\psi_{b}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{b}\right\rangle=0 . \text { Second order: } \)

\( E_{a}^{2}=\frac{\left|\left\langle\psi_{b}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{a}\right\rangle\right|^{2}}{E_{a}-E_{b}}=-\frac{h^{2}}{E_{b}-E_{a}} ; \quad E_{b}^{2}=\frac{\left|\left\langle\psi_{a}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{b}\right\rangle\right|^{2}}{E_{b}-E_{a}}=\frac{h^{2}}{E_{b}-E_{a}} \).

\( E_{-} \approx E_{a}-\frac{h^{2}}{\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right)} ; \quad E_{+} \approx E_{b}+\frac{h^{2}}{\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right)} . \)

(c)

\( \langle H\rangle=\left\langle\cos \phi \psi_{a}+\sin \phi \psi_{b}\left|\left(H^{0}+H^{\prime}\right)\right| \cos \phi \psi_{a}+\sin \phi \psi_{b}\right\rangle \).

\( =\cos ^{2} \phi\left\langle\psi_{a}\left|H^{0}\right| \psi_{a}\right\rangle+\sin ^{2} \phi\left\langle\psi_{b}\left|H^{0}\right| \psi_{b}\right\rangle+\sin \phi \cos \phi\left\langle\psi_{b}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{a}\right\rangle+\sin \phi \cos \phi\left\langle\psi_{a}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{b}\right\rangle \).

\( =E_{a} \cos ^{2} \phi+E_{b} \sin ^{2} \phi+2 h \sin \phi \cos \phi \).

\( \frac{\partial\langle H\rangle}{\partial \phi}=-E_{a} 2 \cos \phi \sin \phi+E_{b} 2 \sin \phi \cos \phi+2 h\left(\cos ^{2} \phi-\sin ^{2} \phi\right)=\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right) \sin 2 \phi+2 h \cos 2 \phi=0 \).

\( \tan 2 \phi=-\frac{2 h}{E_{b}-E_{a}}=-\epsilon \text { where } \quad \epsilon \equiv \frac{2 h}{E_{b}-E_{a}} . \frac{\sin 2 \phi}{\sqrt{1-\sin ^{2} 2 \phi}}=-\epsilon ; \quad \sin ^{2} 2 \phi=\epsilon^{2}\left(1-\sin ^{2} 2 \phi\right) \)

or \( \sin ^{2} 2 \phi\left(1+\epsilon^{2}\right)=\epsilon^{2} ; \quad \sin 2 \phi=\frac{\pm \epsilon}{\sqrt{1+\epsilon^{2}}} ; \quad \cos ^{2} 2 \phi=1-\sin ^{2} 2 \phi=1-\frac{\epsilon^{2}}{1+\epsilon^{2}}=\frac{1}{1+\epsilon^{2}} \);

\( \left.\cos 2 \phi=\frac{\mp 1}{\sqrt{1+\epsilon^{2}}} \quad \text { (sign dictated by } \tan 2 \phi=\frac{\sin 2 \phi}{\cos 2 \phi}=-\epsilon\right) \text {. } \)

\( \cos ^{2} \phi=\frac{1}{2}(1+\cos 2 \phi)=\frac{1}{2}\left(1 \mp \frac{1}{\sqrt{1+\epsilon^{2}}}\right) ; \quad \sin ^{2} \phi=\frac{1}{2}(1-\cos 2 \phi)=\frac{1}{2}\left(1 \pm \frac{1}{\sqrt{1+\epsilon^{2}}}\right) \).

\( \langle H\rangle_{\min }=\frac{1}{2} E_{a}\left(1 \mp \frac{1}{\sqrt{1+\epsilon^{2}}}\right)+\frac{1}{2} E_{b}\left(1 \pm \frac{1}{\sqrt{1+\epsilon^{2}}}\right) \pm h \frac{\epsilon}{\sqrt{1+\epsilon^{2}}}=\frac{1}{2}\left[E_{a}+E_{b} \pm \frac{\left(E_{b}-E_{a}+2 h \epsilon\right)}{\sqrt{1+\epsilon^{2}}}\right] \).

But \( \frac{\left(E_{b}-E_{a}+2 h \epsilon\right)}{\sqrt{1+\epsilon^{2}}}=\frac{\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right)+2 h \frac{2 h}{\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right)}}{\sqrt{1+\frac{4 h^{2}}{\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right)^{2}}}}=\frac{\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right)^{2}+4 h^{2}}{\sqrt{\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right)^{2}+4 h^{2}}}=\sqrt{\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right)^{2}+4 h^{2}} \), So

\( \langle H\rangle_{\min }=\frac{1}{2}\left[E_{a}+E_{b} \pm \sqrt{\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right)^{2}+4 h^{2}}\right] \) we want the minus sign (+ is maximum)

\( =\frac{1}{2}\left[E_{a}+E_{b}-\sqrt{\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right)^{2}+4 h^{2}}\right] \).

(d) If h is small, the exact result (a) can be expanded: \( E_{\pm}=\frac{1}{2}\left[\left(E_{a}+E_{b}\right) \pm\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right) \sqrt{1+\frac{4 h^{2}}{\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right)^{2}}} .\right. \)

\( \Longrightarrow E_{\pm} \approx \frac{1}{2}\left\{E_{a}+E_{b} \pm\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right)\left[1+\frac{2 h^{2}}{\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right)^{2}}\right]\right\}=\frac{1}{2}\left[E_{a}+E_{b} \pm\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right) \pm \frac{2 h^{2}}{\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right)}\right] \).

\( \text { so } \quad E_{+} \approx E_{b}+\frac{h^{2}}{\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right)}, \quad E_{-} \approx E_{a}-\frac{h^{2}}{\left(E_{b}-E_{a}\right)} \),

confirming the perturbation theory results in (b). The variational principle (c) gets the ground state \((E_-)\) exactly right-not too surprising since the trial wave function Eq. 8.75 is almost the most general state (there could be a relative phase factor \( e^{i \theta} \).
