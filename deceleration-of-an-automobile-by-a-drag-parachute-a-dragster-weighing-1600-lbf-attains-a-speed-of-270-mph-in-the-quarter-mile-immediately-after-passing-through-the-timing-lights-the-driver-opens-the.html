DECELERATION OF AN AUTOMOBILE BY A DRAG PARACHUTE

A dragster weighing 1600 lbf attains a speed of 270 mph in the quarter mile. Immediately after passing through the timing lights, the driver opens the drag chute, of area \(A = 25\)ft\(^2\). Air and rolling resistance of the car may be neglected. Find the time required for the machine to decelerate to 100 mph in standard air.

Admin
Solution

Given: Dragster weighing 1600 lbf, moving with initial speed \(V_0 270\) mph, is slowed by the drag force on a chute of area

\(A = 25\)ft\(^2\). Neglect air and rolling resistance of the car. Assume standard air.
Find: Time required for the machine to decelerate to 100 mph.
Solution:
Taking the car as a system and writing Newton’s second law in the direction of motion gives

\(V_0 = 270\) mph
\(V_f = 100\) mph
\(ρ = 0.00238\) slug=ft\(^3\)

 

Since \(C_{D}=\frac{F_{D}}{\frac{1}{2} \rho V^{2} A}\), then \(F_{D}=\frac{1}{2} C_{D} \rho V^{2} A\).
Substituting into Newton’s second law gives

\(-\frac{1}{2} C_{D} \rho V^{2} A=m \frac{d V}{d t}\)

Separating variables and integrating, we obtain

\(\begin{gathered}-\frac{1}{2} C_{D} \rho \frac{A}{m} \int_{0}^{t} d t=\int_{V_{0}}^{V_{f}} \frac{d V}{V^{2}} \\\left.-\frac{1}{2} C_{D} \rho \frac{A}{m} t=-\frac{1}{V}\right]_{V_{0}}^{V_{f}}=-\frac{1}{V_{f}}+\frac{1}{V_{0}}=-\frac{\left(V_{0}-V_{f}\right)}{V_{f} V_{0}}\end{gathered}\)

Finally,

\(t=\frac{\left(V_{0}-V_{f}\right)}{V_{f} V_{0}} \frac{2 m}{C_{D} \rho A}=\frac{\left(V_{0}-V_{f}\right)}{V_{f} V_{0}} \frac{2 W}{C_{D} \rho A g}\)

 

Model the drag chute as a hemisphere (with open end facing flow). From Table 9.3, \(C_{D}=1.42\left(\right.\) assuming \(\left.R e>10^{3}\right)\). Then, substituting numerical values,

\(\begin{aligned}t=&(270-100) \mathrm{mph} \times 2 \times 1600 \mathrm{lbf} \times \frac{1}{100 \mathrm{mph}} \times \frac{\mathrm{hr}}{270 \mathrm{mi}} \times \frac{1}{1.2} \times \frac{\mathrm{ft}^{3}}{0.00238 \mathrm{slug}} \\& \times \frac{1}{25 \mathrm{ft}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{s}^{2}}{32.2 \mathrm{ft}} \times \frac{\mathrm{slug} \cdot \mathrm{ft}}{\mathrm{lbf} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{mi}}{5280 \mathrm{ft}} \times 3600 \frac{\mathrm{s}}{\mathrm{hr}} \end{aligned}\)

 

\(t= 5.05 \mathrm{~s} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad t}\)

Check the assumption on Re:

\(\begin{aligned}\operatorname{Re} &=\frac{D V}{\nu}=\left[\frac{4 A}{\pi}\right]^{1 / 2} \frac{V}{\nu} \\&=\left[\frac{4}{\pi} \times 25 \mathrm{ft}^{2}\right]^{1 / 2} \times 100 \frac{\mathrm{mi}}{\mathrm{hr}} \times \frac{\mathrm{hr}}{3600 \mathrm{~s}} \times 5280 \frac{\mathrm{ft}}{\mathrm{mi}} \times \frac{\mathrm{s}}{1.57 \times 10^{-4} \mathrm{ft}^{2}} \\\operatorname{Re} &=5.27 \times 10^{6}\end{aligned}\)

Hence the assumption is valid.
