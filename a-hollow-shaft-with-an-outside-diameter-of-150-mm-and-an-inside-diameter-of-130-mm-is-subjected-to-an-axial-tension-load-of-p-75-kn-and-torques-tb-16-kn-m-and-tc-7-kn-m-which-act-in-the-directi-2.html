Guest Question.

A hollow shaft with an outside diameter of 150 mm and an inside diameter of 130 mm is subjected to an axial tension load of P = 75 kN and torques \(T_{B}\) = 16 kN-m and \(T_{C}\) = 7 kN-m, which act in the directions shown in Figure P15.9/10.
(a) Determine the principal stresses and the maximum shear stress at point H on the surface of the shaft.
(b) Show these stresses on an appropriate sketch.

Admin
Solution

Equilibrium:

\(\Sigma M_{x}=-T_{1}-16 kN – m +7 kN – m =0 \quad \therefore T_{1}=-9 kN – m\)

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[(150 mm )^{2}-(130 mm )^{2}\right]=4,398.230 mm ^{2} \\&J=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[(150 mm )^{4}-(130 mm )^{4}\right]=21,661,281 mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Normal and shear stress magnitudes:

\(\begin{aligned}&\left.\sigma=\frac{P}{A}=\frac{(75 kN )(1,000 N / kN )}{4,398.230 mm ^{2}}=17.052 MPa ( T ) \quad \text { (same } \sigma \text { for both points } H \text { and } K\right) \\&\tau_{H}=\frac{T_{1} c}{J}=\frac{(9 kN – m )(150 mm / 2)(1,000 N / kN )(1,000 mm / m )}{21,661,281 mm ^{4}}=31.162 MPa\end{aligned}\)

(sense of shear stress to be determined by inspection)

The stress element for point H is shown below.

 

Principal stress calculations for point H:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq. (12.12):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}}{2}\right)^{2}+\tau_{x y}^{2}} \\&=\frac{(17.052)+(0)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(17.052)-(0)}{2}\right)^{2}+(31.162)^{2}} \\&=8.526 \pm 32.307\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1} &=40.8 MPa \text { and } \quad \sigma_{p 2}=-23.8 MPa \\\tau_{\max } &=32.3 MPa \text { (maximum in-plane shear stress) } \\\sigma_{\text {avg }} &=8.53 MPa ( T ) \quad \text { (normal stress on planes of maximum in-plane shear stress) }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan 2 \theta_{p} &=\frac{\tau_{x y}}{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) / 2}=\frac{31.162}{[(17.052)-(0)] / 2}=\frac{31.162}{8.526}=3.6548 \\&\left.\therefore \theta_{p}=37.35^{\circ} \quad \text { (counterclockwise from the } x \text { axis to the direction of } \sigma_{p 1}\right)\end{aligned}\)
