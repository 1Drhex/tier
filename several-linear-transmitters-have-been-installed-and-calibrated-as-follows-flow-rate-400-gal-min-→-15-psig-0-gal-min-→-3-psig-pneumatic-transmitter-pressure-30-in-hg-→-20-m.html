Guest Question.

Several linear transmitters have been installed and calibrated as follows:

\(\left.\begin{array}{l}\text{Flow rate:} & 400 gal / min \rightarrow 15 psig \\& 0 gal / min \quad \rightarrow 3 psig\end{array}\right\} \quad \begin{aligned}&\text { pneumatic } \\&\text { transmitter }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\left.\begin{array}{ll}\text{Pressure:} & 30 in Hg & \rightarrow 20 mA \\&10 in Hg & \rightarrow 4 mA\end{array}\right\} \begin{aligned}&\text { current } \\&\text { transmitter }\end{aligned}\)

 

\( \left.\begin{array}{lll}\text { Level: } & 10 m & \rightarrow 5 VDC \\&0.5 m & \rightarrow 1 VDC\end{array}\right\} \begin{aligned}&\text { voltage } \\&\text { transmitter }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\left.\begin{array}{ll}\text { Concentration: } & 20 g / L & \rightarrow 10 VDC \\& 3 g / L & \rightarrow 1 VDC\end{array}\right\} \begin{aligned}&\text { voltage } \\&\text { transmitter }\end{aligned}\)

(a) Develop an expression for the output of each transmitter as a function of its input. Be sure to include appropriate units.

(b) What is the gain of each transmitter? Zero? Span?

Admin
Solution

a) Flow rate transmitter:

\(\begin{aligned}q_{m}( psig ) &=\left(\frac{15 psig -3 psig }{400 gpm -0 gpm }\right)(q gpm -0 gpm )+3 psig \\&=\left(0.03 \frac{ psig }{ gpm }\right) q( gpm )+3 psig\end{aligned}\)

Pressure transmitter:

\(\begin{aligned}P_{m}( mA ) &=\left(\frac{20 mA -4 mA }{30 in \cdot Hg -10 in \cdot Hg }\right)(p \text { in.Hg }-10 in \cdot Hg )+4 mA \\&=\left(0.8 \frac{ mA }{ in \cdot Hg }\right) p( in \cdot Hg )-4 mA\end{aligned}\)

Level transmitter:

\(\begin{aligned}h_{m}( VDC ) &=\left(\frac{5 VDC -1 VDC }{10 m -0.5 m }\right)(h( m )-0.5 m )+1 VDC \\&=\left(0.421 \frac{ VDC }{ m }\right) h( m )+0.789 VDC\end{aligned}\)

Concentration transmitter:

\(\begin{aligned}C_{m}( VDC ) &=\left(\frac{10 VDC -1 VDC }{20 g / L -3 g / L }\right)(C( g / L )-3 g / L )+1 VDC \\&=\left(0.529 \frac{ VDC }{ g / L }\right) C( g / L )-0.59 VDC\end{aligned}\)

b) The gains, zeros and spans are:

Flow Pressure Level Concentration
Gain 0.03 psig/gpm 0.8 mA/in.Hg 0.421 VDC/m 0.529 VDC/g/L
Zero 0 gpm 10 in.Hg 0.5 m 3 g/L
Span 400 gpm 20 in.Hg 9.5 m 17 g/L
