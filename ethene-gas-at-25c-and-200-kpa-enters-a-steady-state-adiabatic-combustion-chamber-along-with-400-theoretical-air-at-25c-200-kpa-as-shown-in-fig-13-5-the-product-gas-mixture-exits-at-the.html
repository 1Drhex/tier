Guest Question.

Ethene gas at 25°C and 200 kPa enters a steady-state adiabatic combustion chamber along with 400% theoretical air at 25°C, 200 kPa, as shown in Fig. 13.5. The product gas mixture exits at the adiabatic flame temperature and 200 kPa. Calculate the irreversibility per kmol of ethene for this process.

Control volume: Combustion chamber
Inlet states: P, T known for each component gas stream
Exit state: P, T known
Model: All ideal gases, Tables A.9 and A.10
Sketch: Fig. 13.5

The combustion equation is

\(C _{2} H _{4}(g)+12 O _{2}+12(3.76) N _{2} \rightarrow 2 CO _{2}+2 H _{2} O ( g )+9 O _{2}+45.1 N _{2}\)

The adiabatic flame temperature is determined first.
Energy equation:

\(H_{R}=H_{P}\)

\(\sum_{R} n_{i}\left(\bar{h}_{f}^{0}\right)_{i}=\sum_{P} n_{e}\left(\bar{h}_{f}^{0}+\Delta \bar{h}\right)_{e}\)

Admin
Solution

\(52467=2\left(-393522+\Delta \bar{h} CO _{2}\right)+2\left(-241826+\Delta \bar{h}_{ H _{2} O ( g )}\right)+9 \Delta \bar{h}_{ O _{2}}+45.1 \Delta \bar{h}_{ N _{2}}\)

By a trial-and-error solution we find the adiabatic flame temperature to be 1016 K.
Wenow proceed to find the change in entropy during this adiabatic combustion process.

\(S_{R}=S_{C_{2} H_{4}}+S_{\text {air }}\)

From Eq. 13.17.

\(=H V+\Delta H_{R}-\Delta H_{P} (13.17)\)

\(S_{ C _{2} H _{4}}=1\left(219.330-8.3145 \ln \frac{200}{100}\right)=213.567 kJ / K\)

From Eqs. 13.21 and 13.22,

\(\bar{s}_{\operatorname{mix}}^{*}=\sum_{i} y_{i} \bar{S}_{i}^{*} (13.21)\)

\(\bar{S}_{i}^{*}=\bar{s}_{T_{i}}^{0}-\bar{R} \ln \frac{P}{P^{0}}-\bar{R} \ln y_{i}=\bar{s}_{T i}^{0}-\bar{R} \ln \frac{y_{i} P}{P^{0}} (13.22)\)

\(S_{\text {air }}=12\left(205.147-8.3145 \ln \frac{0.21 \times 200}{100}\right)\)

\(+45.1\left(191.610-8.3145 \ln \frac{0.79 \times 200}{100}\right)\)

\(=12(212.360)+45.1(187.807)=11018.416 kJ / k\)

\(S_{R}=213.567+11018.416=11231.983 kJ / K\)

For a multicomponent product gas mixture, it is convenient to set up a table, as follows:

\(\bar{S}_{i}\) \(\overline{ s }_{T i}^{0}\) \(\bar{R} \ln\frac{y_{i} P}{P^{0}}\) \(y _{i}\) \(n _{i}\) Comp
292.448 270.194 -22.254 0.0344 2 \(CO _{2}\)
255.609 233.355 -22.254 0.0344 2 \(H _{2} O\)
253.878 244.135 -9.743 0.1549 9 \(O _{2}\)
225.033 228.691 3.658 0.7763 45.1 \(N _{2}\)

Then, with values from this table for \(n_{i}\)and \(\bar{S}_{i}\) for each component i

\(S_{P}=\sum n_{i} \bar{S}_{i}=13530.004 kJ / K\)

Since this is an adiabatic process, the irreversibility is, from Eq. 13.25,

\(I=W^{ rev }-W=\sum_{P} n_{e} T_{0} \bar{s}_{e}-\sum_{R} n_{i} T_{0} \bar{s}_{i}-Q_{ c . v .} (13.25)\)

\(I=T_{0}\left(S_{P}-S_{R}\right)=298.15(13530.004-11231.983)=685155 kJ / kmol C _{2} H _{4}\)

\(=\frac{685155}{28.054}=24423 kJ / kg\)
