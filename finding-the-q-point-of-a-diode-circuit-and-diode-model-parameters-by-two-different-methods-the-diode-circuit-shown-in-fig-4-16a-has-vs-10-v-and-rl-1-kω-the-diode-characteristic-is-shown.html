Guest Question.

Finding the Q-point of a diode circuit and diode model parameters by two different methods The diode circuit shown in Fig. 4.16(a) has \( V_{ S }=10 V \text { and } R_{ L }=1 k \Omega \) . The diode characteristic is shown in Fig. 4.16(b). Determine the diode voltage \( v_{ D } \), the diode current \( i_{ D } \), and the load voltage \( v_{ O } \) by using (a) the piecewise linear DC model and (b) the constant-drop DC model.

Admin
Solution
\( V_{ S }=10 V \text { and } R_{ L }=1 k \Omega . \text { Thus, } i_{ D (\max )}=\frac{V_{ S }}{R_{ L }}=\frac{10 V }{1 k \Omega}=10 mA \)

 

(a) If we follow the steps described in Sec. 4.6.2, the tangent or best-fit line gives \( V_{ TD }=0.6 V \text { and } v_{ X }=0.8 V \text { at } i_{ X }=i_{ D (\max )}=10 mA \). From Eq. (4.20), the resistance \( R_{ D } \) of the current-dependent part is

 

\( R_{ D }=\left.\frac{\Delta v_{ D }}{\Delta i_{ D }}\right|_{\text {at estimated } Q \text {-point }}=\frac{v_{ X }-V_{ TD }}{i_{ X }} \) (4.20)

 

\( R_{ D }=\frac{v_{ X }-V_{ TD }}{i_{ X }}=\frac{(0.8-0.6) V }{(10 mA )}=20 \Omega \)

 

From Fig. 4.15(b), the diode current is

 

\( i_{ D }=\frac{V_{ S }-V_{ TD }}{R_{ L }+R_{ D }}=\frac{(10-0.6) V }{1 k \Omega+20 \Omega}=9.22 mA \)

 

From Fig. 4.15(b), the diode voltage is

 

\( v_{ D }=V_{ TD }+R_{ D } i_{ D }=0.6+20 \times 9.22 \times 10^{-3}=0.784 V \)

 

Thus, the load voltage becomes

 

\( v_{ O }=V_{ S }-V_{ D }=10-0.784=9.216 V \)

 

NOTE: The diode mode parameters are \( V_{ TD }=0.6 V \text { and } R_{ D }=20 \Omega \).

 

(b) Using Eq. (4.19) for the constant-drop DC model of Fig. 4.14(b), we get the diode current

 

\( i_{ D }=\frac{V_{ S }-V_{ TD }}{R_{ L }} \) (4.19)

 

\( i_{ D }=\frac{V_{ S }-V_{ TD }}{R_{ L }}=\frac{(10-0.6) V }{1 k \Omega}=9.4 mA \)

 

The load voltage is

 

\( v_{ O }=V_{ S }-V_{ TD }=10-0.6=9.4 V \)

 

for an error of (9.4 – 9.216) ⁄ 9.4 = 1.96% compared to the piecewise linear model.

NOTES:

1. The diode mode parameters are \( V_{ TD }=0.6 V \text { and } R_{ D }=0 \Omega \) .
2. If the supply voltage \( V_{ S } \) is much greater than the diode voltage drop \( v_{ D } \), the constant-drop DC model will give acceptable results. If the diode voltage \( v_{ D } \) is comparable to the supply voltage \( V_{ S } \), the piecewise linear DC model, which gives better results, is generally acceptable in most applications.
