Guest Question.

A gear drive proposal consists of a 20° pressure angle spur pinion with 18 teeth driving a 50 tooth gear. The module of the gears is 2.5 and the face width is 30 mm. The pinion runs at 1425 rpm. The gear set is to transmit 3 kW and the load and drive can be considered smooth. The gear material proposed is grade 1 steel and the Brinell hardness of the pinion and gear is 240 and 200, respectively. The gears are to be manufactured to number 6 AGMA quality standard and the teeth are uncrowned. The gears are straddle mounted within an enclosed unit. Assume a pinion life of \(10^8\) cycles and a reliability of 90 per cent. Calculate the AGMA bending and contact stresses and the corresponding factors of safety for both the pinion and the gear.

Admin
Solution

The pinion diameter, \( d_{P}=18×2.5=45 \)mm.

The gear diameter, \( d_{G}=50×2.5=125 \)mm.

The speed ratio, Eq. 7.24, is given by :

\( m_{G}= \frac{N_{G}}{N_{P}} \) (7.24)

\( m_{G}= \frac{N_{G}}{N_{P}} = \frac{50}{18}=2.778 \)

Assume the overload factor, \( K_{O}=1. \)

\( V=\omega r=1425\times \frac{2\pi }{60}\times\frac{ 0.045}{2}=3.358 \) m/s

\( W_{t}=\frac{Power}{V}=\frac{3000}{3.358}=893.5 \) N

From Eqs 7.15 and Eqs 7.16:

\( B=\frac{12-Q^{2/3}_{V} }{4} \) (7.15)

\( B=\frac{12-Q^{2/3}_{V} }{4} =\frac{12-6^{2/3 }}{4} =0.8255\)

A = 50 + 56(1 – B) (7.16)

=50+56(1-0.8255)=59.77

The dynamic factor, Eq. 7.14, is given by:

\( K^{′}_{V}=\frac{\downarrow A+\sqrt{200V}^{B}}{\rightarrow A} \) (7.14)

\( K^{′}_{V}=\frac{\downarrow 59.77+\sqrt{200×3.358}^{0.8255}}{\rightarrow 59.77} \) =1.346

Assume the size factor \( K_{s}= 1. \) The teeth are uncrowned, so \( C_{mc}= 1. \) F =30 mm, so from Eq. 7.19:

\( C_{pf}=\frac{F}{10d}-0.0375+0.4921F \) (7.19)

\( =\frac{0.03}{10\times 0.045}-0.0375+0.4921\times 0.03 =0.04392 \)

 

\( C_{pm}=1 \)

 

\( C_{e}=1 \)

For commercially enclosed units, from Eq.7.21:

\( C_{ma}=A+BF+CF_{2} \) (7.21)

\( C_{ma}=0.127+0.622F-0.01442F^{2} \)

 

\( =0.127+0.622×0.03-0.01442×0.03^{2} =0.1456\)

The load distribution factor \( K_{H} \) , Eq. 7.17, is given by:

\( K_{H}=1+C_{mc}(C_{pf} C_{pm}+C_{ma}C_{e}) \) (7.17)

\( K_{H}=1+C_{mc}(C_{pf} C_{pm}+C_{ma}C_{e}) = 1+1(0.04392×1+0.1456×1=1.190 \)

The repeatedly applied bending strength stress cycle factor for the pinion, \( Y_{Np} \) from Figure 7.3, taking the worst case line for \( 10^{8} \) cycles, is given by

\( Y_{Np}=1.6831(1×10^{8})^{-0.0323} =0.9284 \)

The gear does comparatively fewer cycles and the repeatedly applied bending strength stress cycle factor for the gear, \( Y_{NG} \) , is given by

\( Y_{NG}=1.6831 \frac{\downarrow 1\times 10^{8}}{\rightarrow m_{G}} ^{-0.0323} \)

\( =1.6831 \frac{\downarrow 1\times 10^{8}}{\rightarrow 2.778} ^{-0.0323} \) =0.9284

The pitting resistance stress cycle factor for the pinion, \( Z_{NP} \) from Figure 7.4, taking the worst case line for \( 10^{8} \) cycles, is given by

\( Z_{NP}=2.466N^{-0.056} \)

 

\( =2.466(1×10^{8})^{-0.056} =0.879 \)

The gear does comparatively fewer cycles and the pitting resistance stress cycle factor for the gear, \( Z_{NG} \) , is given by

\( Z_{NG}=2.466 \frac{\downarrow 1\times 10^{8}}{\rightarrow m_{G}} ^{-0.056} \)

 

\( = 2.466 \frac{\downarrow 1\times 10^{8}}{\rightarrow 2.778} ^{-0.056} \) =0.9308

From Table 7.4,

the reliability factor \( Y_{Z}=0.85 \) for a reliability of 90 per cent.

Assume the temperature factor \( Y_{θ}=1 \)

Assume the surface condition factor \( Z_{R}=1 \)

For a steel pinion in mesh with a steel gear the elastic coefficient,

\( Z_{E}=191(MPa)^{0.5} \) from Table 7.1.

For the gearThe allowable bending stress for through hardened grade 1 steel, in the case of the pinion from Eq. 7.25 is given by
\( σ_{FP}=0.533 H_{B}+88.3 \) (7.25)

=0.533×240+88.3=216MPa

\( σ_{FG}=0.533 ×200+88.3=195 \)MPa

The allowable contact stress for through hardened steel gears, in the case of the pinion, Eq. 7.30, is given by

\( σ_{HP}=2.22H_{B} +200 \) (7.30)

=2.22×240+200=733 MPa

For the gear

\( σ_{HG}=2.22×200 +200 \)=644 MPa

From Eq. 7.34

\( A^{′} =8.98\times 10^{-3} \frac{\downarrow H_{BP}}{\rightarrow H_{BG} }-8.29\times 10^{-3}\) (7.34)

\( =8.98\times 10^{-3} \frac{\downarrow 240}{\rightarrow 200 }-8.29\times 10^{-3} \)

= 2.486 × \( 10^{-3} \)

The hardness ratio factor,\( Z_{W} \) , Eq. 7.33, is given by

\( Z_{W}=C_H=1+A^{′}(m_{G}-1 ) \) (7.33)

\( Z_{W}=1+A^{′}(m_{G}-1 ) \)

=1+2.486 × \( 10^{-3}(2.778-1) \)

=1.004

From Figure 7.2, the bending strength geometry factors \( Y_{JP}=0.316 \) and \( Y_{JG}=0.396 \).

The geometry factor \( Z_{I} \) , Eq. 7.22, is given by

\( Z_{I}=I=\frac{\cos\phi_{t} \sin \phi_{ t} }{2m_{N} } \frac{m_{G} }{m_{G}+1 } \) (7.22)

\( Z_{I}=\frac{\cos\phi_{t} \sin \phi_{ t} }{2m_{N} } \frac{m_{G} }{m_{G}+1 } \)

 

\(= \frac{\cos20 \sin 20 }{2} \frac{2.778 }{2.778+1 }=0.1182 \)

The AGMA bending stress, Eq. 7.6, is given by

\( \sigma =W_{t}K_{O}K^{′}_{V}K_{s}\frac{1}{Fm}\frac{K_{H}K_{B} }{Y_{J} }\)

Substitution for the factors and geometrical variables gives, for the pinion,

\( \sigma _{P}=893.5\times 1\times 1.346\times 1 \times \frac{1}{0.03\times 0.0025}\times \frac{1.190\times 1}{0.316}=60.39\times 10^{6} \) Pa

For the gear

\( \sigma _{G}=893.5\times 1\times 1.346\times 1 \times \frac{1}{0.03\times 0.0025}\times \frac{1.190\times 1}{0.396}=48.18\times 10^{6} \) Pa

The allowable bending stress,Eq.7.10,is given by

\( \sigma _{all}=\frac{\sigma _{FP}Y_{N} }{S_{F}Y_{θ}Y_{Z}} \)

Setting \( S_{F} \) = 1, and determining the allowable bending stress for the pinion gives

\( \sigma _{all}=\frac{216\times 0.9284}{1\times 1\times 0.85}=236 \) MPa

The factor of safety for the pinion,\( S_{FP} \) , is given by

\( S_{FP}=\frac{236}{60.4} =3.91 \)

Setting \( S_{F} \) = 1, and determining the allowable bending stress for the gear gives

\( \sigma _{all}=\frac{195\times 0.9583}{1\times 1\times 0.85}=220 \) MPa

The factor of safety for the gear, \( S_{FG} \) , is given by

\( S_{FG}=\frac{220}{48.2} =4.56\)

The values for\( S_{FP} \) and \( S_{FG} \) are greater than 1, indicating that the design seems acceptable at this stage. The contact stress, Eq. 7.8, is given by

\( \sigma _{c}=Z_{e}\overset{\downarrow }{\underset{\rightarrow } { W_{t} }}K_{O}K^{'}_{V}K_{s}\frac{K_{H}}{F_{d} }\frac{Z_{R} }{Z_{1} } ^{0.5} \)

For the pinion

\( \sigma _{c}=191\sqrt{893.5\times 1\times 1.346\times 1\times \frac{1.190}{0.045\times 0.03}\times \frac{1}{0.1182} } \) KPa

=191×2994 KPa=572 MPa

For the gear

\( \sigma _{c}=191\sqrt{893.5\times 1\times 1.346\times 1\times \frac{1.190}{0.125\times 0.03}\times \frac{1}{0.1182} } \) KPa

=191×1796 KPa =342 MPa

The allowable contact stress, Eq. 7.12, is given by

\( \sigma _{c,call} =\frac{\sigma _{HP} }{S_{H}} \frac{Z_{N}Z_{W} }{Y_{\theta } Y_{Z} } \)

For the pinion, omitting \( Z_W \) and setting \( S_{H}= 1 \) gives

\( \sigma _{c,call} =\frac{\sigma _{HP} }{S_{H}} \frac{Z_{N}Z_{W} }{Y_{\theta } Y_{Z} } =\frac{732.8}{1}\times \frac{0.879}{1\times 0.85} =758\)MPa

\( S_{HP}=\frac{758}{572} =1.33 \)

For the gear

\( \sigma _{c,call} =\frac{644}{1} \times \frac{0.9308\times 1.004}{1\times 0.85}=708 \) MPa

\( S_{HG}=\frac{708}{342} =2.06 \)

The values for \( S_{HP} \) and \( S_{HG} \) are greater than 1 indicating that the design seems acceptable at this stage.
