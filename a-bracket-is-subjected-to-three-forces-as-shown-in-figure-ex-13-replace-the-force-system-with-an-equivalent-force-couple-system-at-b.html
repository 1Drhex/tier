Guest Question.

A bracket is subjected to three forces as shown in figure Ex.13. Replace the force system with an equivalent force – couple system at B.

Admin
Solution

Here first resolve the forces along x and y-axes, and then determine algebraic sum of horizontal components and vertical components,
\(\xrightarrow{+} \sum H= 400 \cos 30^{\circ} + 300 \sin 45^{\circ} = 558.54 N \)
\(+\uparrow \sum V= 200 – 400 \sin 30^{\circ} – 300 \cos 45^{\circ} = – 212.13 N \)
(here ‘–’ sign indicate that \(\sum V \) is acting toward negative y direction)
Now we know that resultant R is calculated as,

\( R=\sqrt{(\sum H)^{2}+(\sum V)^{2}} \)

 

\( \therefore R=\sqrt{(558.54)^{2}+(212.13)^{2}} \)

\( \therefore \) R = 597.47 N

Let \(\theta\) be the angle of resultant R with x-axis, then

\(\tan\theta =\frac{\sum V}{\sum H} \)

 

\(\therefore \tan\theta =\frac{212.13}{558.54} \therefore \theta=20.8 ^{\circ}\)

Now determine sum of moments of all forces about B, (treating anticlockwise as positive)

\( \sum M_{B} = (200 × 6) – (400 \cos 30^{\circ} × 2)\)
\(– (400 \sin 30^{\circ} × 6) – (300 \sin 45^{\circ} ×\left\lgroup 2 – \frac{3}{6}× 2\right\rgroup – (300 \cos 45 ^{\circ}×3) \)

= –1541.34 N.m = 1541.34 N.m clockwise

Resultant force and couple moment at point A of given system is shown in figure Ex.13 (a).
