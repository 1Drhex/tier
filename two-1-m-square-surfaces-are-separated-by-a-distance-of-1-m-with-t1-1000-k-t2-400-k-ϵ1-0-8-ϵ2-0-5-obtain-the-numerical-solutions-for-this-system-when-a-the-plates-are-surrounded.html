Guest Question.

Two 1-m-square surfaces are separated by a distance of 1 m with \( T_{1} = 1000 K, T_{2} = 400 K, ϵ_{1} = 0.8, ϵ_{2} = 0.5\). Obtain the numerical solutions for this system when (a) the plates are surrounded by a large room at 300 K and (b) the surfaces are connected by a re-radiating wall perfectly insulated on its outer surface. Part (a) of this example is identical in principle to the problem that is solved by the network method in

Admin
Solution

Consulting Figure, we obtain

\( F_{12} = 0.2 F_{21} = 0.2 F_{11} = 0 = F_{22} \)
\(F_{13} = 0.8 F_{23} = 0.8\)
\(A_{1} = A_{2} = 1 m^{2}\)
(surface 3 is the surroundings or insulated surface). For part (a)

\(E_{b_{1}} = \sigma T^{ 4}_{ 1} = 56.69 kW/m^{2} [17,970 Btu/h · ft^{2}]\)
\(E_{b_{2}} = \sigma T^{ 4}_{ 2} = 1.451 kW/m^{2} \)
\(E_{b_{3}} = \sigma T^{ 4}_{ 3} = 0.459 kW/m^{2} \)

Because \(A_{3} →∞, F_{31}\) and \(F_{32}\) must approach zero since \(A_{1}F_{13} = A_{3}F_{31}\) and \(A_{2}F_{23} = A_{3}F_{32}\). The nodal equations are written in the form of Equation \(J_{i}=\frac{1}{1-F_{ii}}\sum\limits_{j\neq i}{F_{ij}J_{j}} for \frac{q_{i}}{A_{i}} =0\):

surface 1: \( J_{1} − (1 − ϵ_{1})(F_{11}J_{1} + F_{12}J_{2} + F_{13}J_{3}) = ϵ_{1}E_{b_{1}}\)
surface 2: \(J_{2} − (1 − ϵ_{2})(F_{21}J_{1} + F_{22}J_{2} + F_{23}J_{3}) =ϵ_{ 2}E_{b_{2}} \) [a]
surface 3: \( J_{3} − (1 − ϵ_{3})(F_{31}J_{1} + F_{32}J_{2} + F_{33}J_{3}) = ϵ_{3}E_{b_{3}}\)

Because \(F_{31}\) and \(F_{32}\) approach zero, \(F_{33}\) must be 1.0. Inserting the numerical values for the various terms, we have

\(J_{1} − (1 − 0.8)[(0)J_{1} + (0.2)J_{2} + (0.8)J_{3}] = (0.8)(56.69)\)
\(J_{2} − (1 − 0.5)[(0.2)J_{1} + (0)J_{2} + (0.8)J_{3}] = (0.5)(1.451) \) [b]
\(J_{3} − (1 − 3)[(0)J_{1} + (0)J_{2} + (1.0)J_{3}] = ϵ_{3}(0.459)\)

The third equation yields \(J_{3} = 0.459 kW/m^{2}\) · K. Because the room is so large it acts like a hohlraum, or blackbody. But it does not have zero heat transfer.
Finally, the equations are written in compact form as

\(J_{1} − 0.04J_{2} − 0.16J_{3} = 45.352\)
\(−0.1J_{1} + J_{2} − 0.4J_{3} = 0.7255\) [c]
\(J_{3} = 0.459\)

Of course, there only remain two unknowns, \(J_{1}\) and \(J_{2}\), in this set.
For part (b), \(A_{3}\) for the enclosing wall is \(4.0 m^{2}\), and we set \(J_{3} = E_{b_{3}}\) because surface 3 is
insulated. From reciprocity we have

\(A_{1}F_{13} = A_{3}F_{31} F_{31}=\frac{(1.0)(0.8)}{4.0} =0.2\)

 

\(A_{2}F_{23} = A_{3}F_{32} F_{32}=\frac{(1.0)(0.8)}{4.0} =0.2\)

Then from \(F_{31} + F_{32} + F_{33} = 1.0\) we have \(F_{33} = 0.6\).
The set of equations in (a) still applies, so we insert the numerical values to obtain (with \(J_{3} = E_{b_{3}}\) )

\(J_{1} − (1 − 0.8)[(0)J_{1} + (0.2)J_{2} + (0.8)J3] = (0.8)(56.69)\)
\(J_{2} − (1 − 0.5)[(0.2)J_{1} + (0)J_{2} + (0.8)J_{3}] = (0.5)(1.451) \) [d]
\(J_{3} − (1 − ϵ_{3})[(0.2)J_{1} + (0.2)J_{2} + (0.6)J_{3}] = ϵ_{3}J_{3}\)

Notice that the third equation of set (d) can be written as

\(J_{3}(1 − ϵ_{3}) − (1 − ϵ_{3})[(0.2)J_{1} + (0.2)J_{2} + (0.6)J_{3}] = 0\)

so that the \(1 − ϵ_{3}\) term drops out, and we obtain our final set of equations as

\(J_{1} − 0.04J_{2} − 0.16J_{3} = 45.352\)
\(−0.1J_{1} + J_{2} − 0.4J_{3} = 0.7255 \) [e]
\(−0.2J_{1} − 0.2J_{2} + 0.4J_{3} = 0\)

To obtain the heat transfers the set of equations is first solved for the radiosities. For set (c),
\(J_{1} = 45.644 kW/m^{2}\)
\(J_{2} = 5.474 kW/m^{2}\)
\(J_{3} = 0.459 kW/m^{2}\)

The heat transfers are obtained from Equation \(\frac{q_{i}}{A_{i}} =\frac{\epsilon _{i}}{1-\epsilon _{i}} (E_{b_{i}}-J_{i})\):

\( q_{1} =\frac{A_{1}\epsilon _{1}}{1-\epsilon _{1}} (E_{b_{1}}-J_{1})=\frac{(1.0)(0.8)}{1 − 0.8} (56.69 − 45.644) = 44.184 kW [150,760 Btu/h] \)

 

\( q_{2} =\frac{A_{2}\epsilon _{2}}{1-\epsilon _{2}} (E_{b_{2}}-J_{2})=\frac{(1.0)(0.5)}{1 − 0.5}(1.451 − 5.474) = −4.023 kW [−13,730 Btu/h] \)

The net heat absorbed by the room is the algebraic sum of \( q_{1}\) and \( q_{2}\) or

\(q_{3, absorbed} = 44.184 − 4.023 = 40.161 kW [137,030 Btu/h] \)

For part (b) the solutions to set (e) are
\(J_{1} = 51.956 kW/m^{2} J_{2} = 20.390 kW/m^{2} J_{3} = 36.173 kW/m^{2}\)

The heat transfers are

\(q_{1} =\frac{A_{1}\epsilon _{1}}{1-\epsilon _{1}} (E_{b_{1}}-J_{1})=\frac{(1.0)(0.8)}{1 − 0.8}(56.69 − 51.965) = 18.936 kW \)

 

\( q_{2} =\frac{A_{2}\epsilon _{2}}{1-\epsilon _{2}} (E_{b_{2}}-J_{2})=\frac{(1.0)(0.5)}{1 − 0.5}(1.451 − 20.390) = −18.936 kW \)

Of course, these heat transfers should be equal in magnitude with opposite sign because the insulated wall exchanges no heat. The temperature of the insulated wall is obtained from

\(J_{3} = E_{b_{3}} = \sigma T^{ 4}_{ 3} = 36.173 kW/m^{2}\)
and
\(T_{3} = 894 K [621^{\circ }C, 1150^{\circ }F]\)
