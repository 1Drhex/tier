Guest Question.

in the formulation of a Lyapunov function, requiring\( \hat{V}(x) \) to be positive-definite, may be relaxed to \( \hat{V}(x)\) being positive-semidefinite provided \( \hat{V}(x)\) is not identically zero along any trajectory. A third-order system, in the absence of an input, is modelled by the state equation
\( \hat{x}\) =Ax
where x=\(\begin{bmatrix}x_{1} & x_{2} & x_{3}\end{bmatrix}^{T}\) and
A=\(\begin{bmatrix}0 & 1 & 0\\ 0 & -2 & \\1 -k & 0 & -1\end{bmatrix}\)

with k being a constant scalar. It is required to use the Lyapunov approach todetermine the constraints on k to ensure asymptotic stability.

(a) In choose Q to be the positivesemidefinite matrix
Q=\(\begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 0 &0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1\end{bmatrix}\)

so that
\(\hat{V}(x)=-x^{T}Qx=-x_{3}^{2}\)
Verify that \(\hat{V}(x)\) is identically zero only at the origin (equilibrium point) and is therefore not identically zero along any trajectory
(b) Using this matrix Q solve the matrix equation
\(A^{T}\)P+PA=-Q
to determine the matrix P.
(c) Using Sylvesterâ€™s criterion show that the system is asymptotically stable for 0< k< 6.

 

Admin
Solution

(a)\(x_{1}^{\cdot }=x_{2}\) (i)
\(x_{2}^{\cdot }=-2x_{2}+x_{3}\) (ii)
\(x_{3}^{\cdot }=-kx_{1}-x_{3}\) (iii)
If V(x)\(^{\cdot }\) is identically zero then \(x_{3}\) is identically zero \(\Rightarrow x_{1}\) is identically zero from (iii)
\(\Rightarrow x_{2}\) is identically zero from (i)
Hence \(v(x)^{\cdot }\) is identically zero only at the origin.
(b) \(A^{T}P + PA = -Q \Rightarrow \)
\(\begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 & -k\\ 1 & -2 & 0\\ 0 & 1 & -1\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}p_{11} & p_{12} & p_{13}\\ p_{12}& p_{22} & p_{23}\\ p_{13} & p_{23} & p_{33}\end{bmatrix}+\begin{bmatrix}p_{11} & p_{12} & p_{13}\\ p_{12}& p_{22} & p_{23}\\ p_{13} & p_{23} & p_{33}\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}0 & 1 & 0\\ 0 & -2 &1 \\ -k & 0 & -1\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 &0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 0 &-1 \end{bmatrix}\)

Equating elements and solving for the elements of P gives the matrix
P = \(\begin{bmatrix}\frac{k^{2}+12k}{12-2k} & \frac{6k}{12-2k} &0 \\ \frac{6k}{12-2k} & \frac{3k}{12-2k} & \frac{k}{12-2k}\\ 0 &\frac{k}{13-2k} & \frac{6}{12-2k}\end{bmatrix}\)

(c) Principal minors of Pare:
\(\Delta _{1}=\frac{k^{2}+12k}{12-2k}\)> 0 if k > 0 and (12-2k)> 0\(\Rightarrow 0< k< 6\)
\(\Delta _{2}=\left [ \frac{k^{2}+12k}{12-2k} \right ]\left [ \frac{3k}{12-2k} \right ]-\frac{36k^{2}}{12-2k}=\frac{3k^{3}}{(12-2k)^{2}} > 0 if k > 0\)
\(\Delta _{3} = \frac{(k^{2}+12k)(8k-k^{2})}{(12-2k)^{3}}-\frac{216k^{2}}{(12-2k)^{3}}> 0 if (6k^{3}-k^{4})> 0 \Rightarrow 0< k< 6\)
Thus system asymptotically stable for \(0 < k< 6\) .
