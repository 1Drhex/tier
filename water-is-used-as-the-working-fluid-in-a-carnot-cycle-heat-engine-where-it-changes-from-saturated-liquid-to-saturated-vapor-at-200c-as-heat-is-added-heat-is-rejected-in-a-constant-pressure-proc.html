Guest Question.

Water is used as the working fluid in a Carnot cycle heat engine, where it changes from saturated liquid to saturated vapor at 200°C as heat is added. Heat is rejected in a constant pressure process (also constant T) at 20 kPa. The heat engine powers a Carnot cycle refrigerator that operates between –15°C and +20°C. Find the heat added to the water per kg water. How much heat should be added to the water in the heat engine so the refrigerator can remove 1 kJ from the cold space?

Admin
Solution

Carnot cycle heat engine:

Constant T ⇒ constant P from 1 to 2, Table B.2.1

\(\begin{aligned}q _{ H } &=\int Tds = T \left( s _{2}- s _{1}\right)= T s _{ fg }= h _{ fg } \\&=473.15(4.1014)= 1 9 4 0 k J / kg\end{aligned}\)

States 3 & 4 are two-phase, Table B.2.1

\(\Rightarrow T _{ L }= T _{3}= T _{4}= T _{ sat }( P )=60.06^{\circ} C\)

Carnot cycle refrigerator (\(T _{ L }\) and \(T _{ H }\) are different from above):

\(\beta_{ ref }=\frac{ Q _{ L }}{ W }=\frac{ T _{ L }}{ T _{ H }- T _{ L }}=\frac{273-15}{20-(-15)}=\frac{258}{35}=7.37\)

 

\(W =\frac{ Q _{ L }}{\beta}=\frac{1}{7.37}=0.136 kJ\)

 

The needed work comes from the heat engine

 

\(W =\eta_{ HE } Q _{ H H 2 O } ; \quad \eta_{ HE }=1-\frac{ T _{ L }}{ T _{ H }}=1-\frac{333}{473}=0.296\)

 

\(Q _{ H H 2 O }=\frac{ W }{\eta_{ HE }}=\frac{0.136}{0.296}= 0 . 4 6 k J\)
