Guest Question.

A condenser in a power plant receives 10 lbm/s steam at 130 F, quality 90% and rejects the heat to cooling water with an average temperature of 62 F. Find the power given to the cooling water in this constant pressure process and the total rate of enropy generation when condenser exit is saturated liquid.

Admin
Solution

C.V. Condenser. Steady state with no shaft work term.

Energy Eq.4.12: \(\dot{ m } h _{ i }+\dot{ Q }=\dot{ m }h_{ e }\)

Entropy Eq.7.8: \(\dot{ m } s _{ i }+\dot{ Q } / T +\dot{ S }_{ gen }=\dot{ m } s _{ e }\)

Properties are from Table F.7.1

\(h _{ i }=98.0+0.9 \times 1019.8=1015.8 Btu / lbm , h _{ e }=98.0 Btu / lbm\)

 

\(s _{ i }=0.1817+0.9 \times 1.7292=1.7380 Btu / lbm R , \quad s _{ e }=0.1817 Btu / lbm R\)

 

\(\dot{Q}_{\text {out }}=-\dot{Q}=\dot{m}\left(h_{i}-h_{e}\right)=10(1015.8-98.0)= 9 1 7 8 B t u / s\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ S }_{\text {gen }}=& \dot{ m }\left( s _{ e }- s _{ i }\right)+\dot{ Q }_{\text {out }} / T \\&=10(0.1817-1.738)+9178 /(459.7+62) \\&=-15.563+17.592= 2 . 0 3 B t u / s – R\end{aligned}\)
