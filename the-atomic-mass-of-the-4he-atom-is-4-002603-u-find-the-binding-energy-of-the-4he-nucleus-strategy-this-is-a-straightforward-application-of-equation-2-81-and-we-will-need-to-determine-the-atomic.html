Guest Question.

The atomic mass of the \({ }^{4} He\) atom is 4.002603 u. Find the binding energy of the \({ }^{4} He\) nucleus.

Strategy This is a straightforward application of Equation (2.81), and we will need to determine the atomic masses.

\(E_{B}=\sum_{i} m_{i} c^{2}-M_{\text {bound system }} c^{2}\) (2.81)

Admin
Solution

Equation (2.81) gives

 

\(E_{B}\left({ }^{4} He \right)=2 m_{p} c^{2}+2 m_{n} c^{2}-M_{^{4}He} c^{2}\)

 

Later we will learn to deal with atomic masses in cases like this, but for now we will subtract the two electron masses from the atomic mass of \({ }^{4} He\) to obtain the mass of the \({ }^{4} He\) nucleus. The mass of the electron is given on the inside of the front cover, along with the masses of the proton and neutron.

 

\(\begin{aligned}M_{{ }^{4} He }(\text { nucleus }) &=4.002603 u -2(0.000549 u ) \\&=4.001505 u\end{aligned}\)

 

We determine the binding energy of the \({ }^{4} He\) nucleus to be

 

\(\begin{gathered}E_{B}\left({ }^{4} He \right)=[2(1.007276 u )+2(1.008665 u )-4.001505 u ] c^{2} \\=0.0304 c^{2} u\end{gathered}\)

 

\(E_{B}\left({ }^{4} He \right)=\left(0.0304 c^{2} u \right) \frac{931.5 MeV }{c^{2} u }=28.3 MeV\)

 

The binding energy of the \({ }^{4} He\) nucleus is large, almost 1% of its rest energy.
