Guest Question.

The symmetrical cable of Fig. 6.45 has a span of L = 30 m, has a sag of h = 2 m, and carries a uniform external load of w = 3500 N/m.

a. Find \(T_{min}\) and \(T_{max}\) for this cable.

b. Determine the required cable length, \(L_{C}\).

c. Repeat (a) and (b) if h = 4 m.

Admin
Solution

a. Because the cable system is symmetrical, Eqs. (6.4) \(T_{0}=T_{min}=\frac{wL^{2}}{8h}\) and (6.5) \(T_{max}=\frac{wL}{2}\sqrt{1+\left(\frac{L}{4h} \right)^{2} }\) apply.

\(T_{min}=\frac{wL^{2}}{8h}=\frac{(3500 \ N/m)(30 \ m^{2})}{8(2 \ m)}\)

 

\(T_{min}=196.9 \ kN\)

 

\(T_{max}=\frac{wL}{2}\sqrt{1+\left(\frac{L}{4h} \right)^{2} }\)

 

\(T_{max}=\frac{(3500 \ N/m)(30\ m)}{2}\sqrt{1+\left(\frac{(30\ m)}{4(2\ m)} \right)^{2} }\)

 

\(T_{max} = 203.8 \ kN\)

b. Eq. (6.6) \(L_{C}=L\left[1+\frac{8}{3}\left(\frac{h}{L} \right)^{2}-\frac{32}{5}\left(\frac{h}{L} \right)^{4}+\ ….. \right]\) can be used to compute \(L_{C}\), but first we must check the restriction for a maximum cable slope of \(45^{\circ}\) or less.

Letting \(x = x_{max} = 15 \ m\) in Eq. (6.1) \(\tan \theta =\frac{wx}{T_{0}}\) :

\(\tan \theta =\frac{wx}{T_{0}}=\frac{(3500\ N/m)(15\ m)}{196,900\ N}=0.2666\)

 

\(\theta =14.9^{\circ}\lt 45^{\circ}\) OK

Then for the sag ratio (h/L) = (2 m/30 m) = 0.066667:

\(L_{C}=(30\ m)\left[1+\frac{8}{3}(0.066667)^{2}-\frac{32}{5}(0.066667)^{4} \right]\)

 

\(L_{C} = 30.4 \ m\)

An alternate solution involves use of Eq. (6.7) \(L_{A}=a\left[1+\frac{2}{3}\left(\frac{h_{A}}{a} \right)^{2}-\frac{2}{5}\left(\frac{h_{A}}{a} \right)^{4}+\ ….. \right]\) to compute half the cable length, \(L_{A}\), with a = 15 m and \(h_{A}\) = 2 m. Then (\(h_{A}\) /a) = (2 m/15 m ) = (0.1333), and

\(L_{A}=(15\ m)\left[1+\frac{2}{3}(0.1333)^{2}-\frac{2}{5}(0.1333)^{4} \right]\)

 

\(L_{A} = 15.18 \ m\)

Then: \(L_{C} = 2L_{A} = 2(15.18 \ m) = 30.4 \ m\).

Note that Eq. (6.7) is valid here because its use restriction is satisfied, i.e.:

\((h_{A}/a) = (2 \ m/15 \ m) = 0.1333 \lt 0.5\)

c. Following the procedures of (a) and (b) above with h = 4 m and (h/L) = (4 m/30 m) = 0.1333:

\(T_{min} = 98.4 \ kN\)

 

\(T_{max} = 111.6 \ kN\)

 

\(L_{C} = 31.4 \ m\)

Notice the effect that a doubling of the sag ratio has on cable tensions and length. Do the results agree with what you would expect on an intuitive basis?
