Guest Question.

A thermometer with first-order time constant \(=0.1 min\) and gain \(=1.0\) is placed in a temperature bath \(\left(25^{\circ} C \right)\). After the thermometer comes to equilibrium with the bath, the temperature of the bath is increased linearly at a rate of \(1^{\circ} / min\).

(a) What is the difference between the measured temperature \(T_{m}\) and the bath temperature \(T\) at \(t=0.1 min\) and \(t=1.0 min\) after the change in temperature?

(b) What is the maximum deviation between \(T_{m}(t)\) and \(T(t)\) ? When does it occur?

(c) Plot both \(T(t)\) and \(T_{m}(t)\) to 3 mins. For large values of \(t\), determine the time lag between \(T_{ m }\) and \(T\).

Admin
Solution

(a)

At time \(0, T_{m}(0)\) and \(T(0)\) are the same which is \(t_{o}\). Then \(T\) (bath temperature) follows a ramp:

\(T(t)=t+T_{0}\) (1)

Define deviation variables: \(T_{m}{ }^{\prime}=T_{m}-T_{s s}=T_{m}-T_{0} ; T^{\prime}=T-T_{s s}=T-T_{0}\), substitute these into Eq. 1 :

\(T^{\prime}(t)=t\) and LT: \(T^{\prime}(s)=\frac{1}{s^{2}}\) (2)

As known, thermometer can be modeled by a first order system with time constant \(0.1\) and gain 1 :

\(\frac{T_{m}{ }^{\prime}(s)}{T^{\prime}(s)}=\frac{G}{\tau s+1}=\frac{1}{0.1 s+1}\) (3) Eq. 5-19 in the book

\(Y(s)=\frac{K a \tau^{2}}{\tau s+1}-\frac{K a \tau}{s}+\frac{K a}{s^{2}}\) (5-19)

Combine Eqs. 2 and 3 :

\(T_{m}{ }^{\prime}(s)=\frac{1}{0.1 s+1} T^{\prime}(s)=\frac{1}{0.1 s+1} \cdot \frac{1}{s^{2}}\) (4)

Apply PFE to Eq. 4:

\(\begin{aligned}&T_{m}{ }^{\prime}(s)=\frac{1}{0.1 s+1} T^{\prime}(s)=\frac{1}{0.1 s+1} \cdot \frac{1}{s^{2}}=\frac{a_{1}}{0.1 s+1}+\frac{a_{2}}{s}+\frac{a_{3}}{s^{2}} \\&a_{1}=\left.\frac{1}{s^{2}}\right|_{s=-10}=0.01 \\&a_{3}=\left.\frac{1}{0.1 s+1}\right|_{s=0}=1 \\&\text { set } s=10, \frac{1}{0.1 s+1} \cdot \frac{1}{s^{2}}=0.005 ; \frac{a_{1}}{0.1 s+1}+\frac{a_{2}}{s}+\frac{a_{3}}{s^{2}}=0.015+0.1 a_{2} \\&a_{2}=-0.1\end{aligned}\)

As a result:

\(T_{m}^{\prime}(s)=\frac{0.01}{0.1 s+1}-\frac{0.1}{s}+\frac{1}{s^{2}}\) (5)

Use inverse LT to time domain:

\(\begin{aligned}&T_{m}{ }^{\prime}(t)=0.01 \frac{1}{0.1} \exp \left(-\frac{t}{0.1}\right)-0.1+t \\&T_{m}{ }^{\prime}(t)=0.1(\exp (-10 t)-1)+t\end{aligned}\) Eq. 5-21 in the book

\(y(t) \approx K a(t-\tau)\) (5-21)

At t = 0.1 min and t = 1.0 min after the change in T(t), the difference would be:

\(\begin{aligned}&T_{m}(t)=T_{m}(0)+0.1(\exp (-10 t)-1)+t \\&T(t)=T(0)+t \\&\Delta T_{m}=T_{m}(t)-T(t)=0.1(\exp (-10 t)-1)\end{aligned}\) (6)

\(\begin{aligned}&\Delta T_{m}(0.1)=-0.0632 \\&\Delta T_{m}(0.1)=-0.1\end{aligned}\)

(b)

By looking at Figure \(5.5\), the maximum difference occurs when \(t \rightarrow \infty\) and the corresponding difference is: \(\underset{t\rightarrow \infty }{\lim } \{0.1(\exp (-10 t)-1)\}=-0.1\)

(c)

For large time, exp(-10t) approaches zero and:

\(T_{m}(t)=T_{m}(0)+0.1(\exp (-10 t)-1)+t=T_{m}(0)+(t-0.1)\)

which indicates there is a 0.1 min time delay between measurement and true value after a long time.
