Guest Question.

It is required to design a helical compression spring subjected to a maximum force of 7.5 kN. The mean coil diameter should be 150 mm from space consideration. The spring rate is 75 N/mm. The spring is made of oil-hardened and tempered steel wire with ultimate tensile strength of 1250 N/mm². The permissible shear stress for the spring wire is 30% of the ultimate tensile strength (G = 81 370 N/mm²). Calculate
(i) wire diameter; and
(ii) number of active coils.

Admin
Solution

Given P = 7.5 kN D = 150 mm k = 75 N/mm.

\( S_{u t}=1250 N / mm ^{2} \quad G=81370 N / mm ^{2} \quad \tau=0.3 S _{ ut } \).

Step I Wire diameter
The permissible shear stress is given by,

\( \tau=0.3 S_{u t}=0.3(1250)=375 N / mm ^{2} \).

\(C=\frac{D}{d}=\frac{150}{d} \quad \text { or } \quad d=\frac{150}{C} \) (a).

From Eq. (10.13),

\( \tau=K\left(\frac{8 P C}{\pi d^{2}}\right) \) (10.13).

\( \tau=K\left(\frac{8 P C}{\pi d^{2}}\right) \).

Substituting Eq. (a) in the above expression,

\( \tau=K\left\{\frac{8 P C^{3}}{\pi(150)^{2}}\right\} \).

\( \text { or, } \quad K C^{3}=\frac{\pi(150)^{2} \tau}{8 P}=\frac{\pi(150)^{2}(375)}{8(7500)} \).

\( K C^{3}=441.79 \) (b).

\( \text { where, } \quad K=\frac{4 C-1}{4 C-4}+\frac{0.615}{C} \).

Equation (b) is to be solved by the trial and error method. The values are tabulated in the following way:

\(K C^{3}\) K C
163.88 1.311 5
270.65 1.253 6
416.06 1.213 7
606.21 1.184 8
504.98 1.197 7.5
433.07 1.210 7.1
450.14 1.206 7.2
467.99 1.203 7.3

It is observed from the above table that the spring index should be between 7.1 and 7.2 to satisfy Eq. (b).
C = 7.2

\( d=\frac{150}{C}=\frac{150}{7.2}=20.83 \text { or } 21 mm \). (i).

Step II Number of active coils
From Eq. (10.9),

\( k=\frac{G d^{4}}{8 D^{3} N} \) (10.9).

\( k=\frac{G d^{4}}{8 D^{3} N} \quad 75=\frac{(81370)(21)^{4}}{8(150)^{3} N} \).

\( \therefore \quad N=7.81 \text { or } 8 \text { coils } \) (ii).
