Guest Question.

Find the flows and fluxes of exergy in the condenser of . Use those to determine the second law efficiency, \(T _{o}\) = 15°C.

Admin
Solution

For this case we select \(T _{o}\) = 15°C = 288 K, the ocean water temperature.
The states properties from Tables B.1.1 and B.1.3

1: 45°C, x = 0: \(h _{1}=188.42 kJ / kg\),

3: 3.0 MPa, 600°C: \(s _{3}=7.5084 kJ / kg K\)

C.V. Turbine : \(w _{ T }= h _{3}- h _{4} ; s _{4}= s _{3}\)

\(\begin{aligned}s _{4}= s _{3} &=7.5084=0.6386+ x _{4}(7.5261) \quad \Rightarrow \quad x _{4}=0.9128 \\&=> h _{4}=188.42+0.9128(2394.77)=2374.4 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

C.V. Condenser : \(q _{ L }= h _{4}- h _{1}=2374.4-188.42=2186 kJ / kg\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{ Q }_{ L }=\dot{ m } q _{ L }=25 \times 2186=54.65 MW =\dot{ m }_{\text {ocean }} C _{ p } \Delta T \\&\dot{ m }_{\text {ocean }}=\dot{ Q }_{ L } / C _{ p } \Delta T =54650 /(4.18 \times 3)=4358 kg / s\end{aligned}\)

The net drop in exergy of the water is

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{\Phi}_{\text {water }} &=\dot{ m }_{\text {water }}\left[ h _{4}- h _{1}- T _{ o }\left( s _{4}- s _{1}\right)\right] \\&=25[2374.4-188.4-285(7.5084-0.6386)] \\&=54650-48947= 5 7 0 3 kW\end{aligned}\)

The net gain in exergy of the ocean water is

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{\Phi}_{\text {ocean }} &=\dot{ m }_{\text {ocean }}\left[ h _{6}- h _{5}- T _{o}\left( s _{6}- s _{5}\right)\right] \\&=\dot{ m }_{\text {ocean }}\left[ C _{ p }\left( T _{6}- T _{5}\right)- T _{o} C _{ p } \ln \left(\frac{ T _{6}}{ T _{5}}\right)\right] \\&=4358\left[4.18(15-12)-285 \times 4.18 \ln \frac{273+15}{273+12}\right] \\&=54650-54364= 2 8 6 k W\end{aligned}\)

The second law efficiency is

\(\eta_{ II }=\dot{\Phi}_{ ocean } / \dot{\Phi}_{ water }=\frac{286}{5703}= 0 . 0 5\)

In reality all the exergy in the ocean water is destroyed as the 15°C water mixes with the ocean water at 12°C after it flows back out into the ocean and the efficiency does not have any significance. Notice the small rate of exergy relative to the large rates of energy being transferred.
