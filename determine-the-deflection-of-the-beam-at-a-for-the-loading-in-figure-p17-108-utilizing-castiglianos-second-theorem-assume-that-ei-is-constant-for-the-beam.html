Guest Question.

Determine the deflection of the beam at A for the loading in Figure P17.108 utilizing Castigliano’s second theorem. Assume that EI is constant for the beam.

Admin
Solution

To determine the deflection of the cantilever beam at A, a dummy load Q will be applied downward at A.

 

 

 

Draw a free-body diagram that cuts through the beam at section a–a and derive a moment equation M from this free-body diagram.

\(\Sigma M_{a-a}=Q x+M=0 \quad \therefore M=-Q x \quad 0 \leq x<a\)

Differentiate this expression to obtain ∂M/∂Q.

\(\frac{\partial M}{\partial Q}=-x\)

Substitute Q = 0 into the bending moment equation to obtain

\(M=0 \quad 0 \leq x<a\)

 

 

Draw a second free-body diagram that cuts through the beam at section b–b and derive a moment equation M from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{b-b} &=Q x+P(x-a)+M=0 & \\& \therefore M=-Q x-P(x-a) & a \leq x<L\end{aligned}\)

Differentiate this expression to obtain ∂M/∂Q.

\(\frac{\partial M}{\partial Q}=-x\)

Substitute Q = 0 into the bending moment equation to obtain

\(M=-P(x-a) \quad a \leq x<L\)

Castigliano’s second theorem applied to beam deflections can be expressed by Equation (17.40).

\(\Delta=\int_{0}^{L}\left(\frac{\partial M}{\partial Q}\right) \frac{M}{E I} d x\)

Thus:

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta_{A} &=\int_{0}^{a}(-x)(0) d x+\int_{a}^{L}(-x)\left[-\frac{P}{E I}(x-a)\right] d x=\frac{P}{E I} \int_{a}^{L}\left(x^{2}-x a\right) d x \\&=\frac{P}{E I} \int_{a}^{L} x^{2} d x-\frac{P a}{E I} \int_{a}^{L} x d x \\&=\frac{P}{E I} \frac{1}{3}\left[x^{3}\right]_{a}^{L}-\frac{P a}{E I} \frac{1}{2}\left[x^{2}\right]_{a}^{L} \\&=\frac{P}{3 E I}\left(L^{3}-a^{3}\right)-\frac{P a}{2 E I}\left(L^{2}-a^{2}\right) \\&=\frac{2 P L^{3}}{6 E I}-\frac{2 P a^{3}}{6 E I}-\frac{3 P a L^{2}}{6 E I}+\frac{3 P a^{3}}{6 E I} \\& \therefore \Delta_{A}=\frac{P}{6 E I}\left(2 L^{3}-3 a L^{2}+a^{3}\right) \downarrow\end{aligned}\)

 

This expression can be further simplified by substituting a + b for L. After substantial manipulation, this expression can be rewritten as

\(\Delta_{A}=\frac{P b^{2}}{6 E I}(3 L-b) \downarrow\)
