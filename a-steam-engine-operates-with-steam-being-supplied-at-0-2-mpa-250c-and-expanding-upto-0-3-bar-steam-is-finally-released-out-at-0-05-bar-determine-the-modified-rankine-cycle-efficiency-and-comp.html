Guest Question.

A steam engine operates with steam being supplied at 0.2 MPa, 250°C and expanding upto 0.3 bar. Steam is finally released out at 0.05 bar. Determine the modified Rankine cycle efficiency and compare it with the efficiency of Carnot cycle operating between given limits of pressure. Neglect pump work.

Admin
Solution

Here states 1, 2, and 3 refer to cut-off point, state at the end of expansion and after release from engine.\(\\\text{At inlet to engine, from steam tables,}\\\begin{aligned}h_{1}&= 2971 kJ/kg\\s_{1}&= 7.7086 kJ/kg · K\\\text{Let dryness fr}&\text{action at 2 be x}_{2}.\\s_{1}&=s_{2}; \\s_{2}&= 7.7086 = s_{\text{f at 0.3 bar}} + x_{2} · s_{\text{fg at 0.3 bar}}\\⇒ \qquad \quad x_{2}&= 0.99\\v_{2}&= v_{\text{f at 0.3 bar}} + x_{2} · v_{\text{fg at 0.3 bar}} = 5.1767 m^{3}/kg\\h_{2}&= 2601.97 kJ/kg\\\text{Work output}&\text{ from engine cycle per kg of steam }= (h_{1} – h_{2}) + v_{2}(p_{2} – p_{3})\\&= (2971 – 2601.97) + 5.1767 (0.3 – 0.05) × 10^{2}\\&= 511.95 kJ/kg\\\text{Heat input p}&\text{er kg of steam }= h_{1} – h_{\text{f at 0.05 bar}}\\&= 2971 – 137.82 = 2833.18 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Efficiency of modified Rankine cycle}&= \frac{511.95}{2833.18} = 0.1807\\&= 18.07 \%\end{aligned}\\\text{In order to find out Carnot cycle efficiency let us find out saturation temperature corresponding to}\\\text{0.2 MPa and 0.05 bar.}\\\begin{aligned}T_{max}&= T_{\text{sat at 0.2 MPa}} = \text{120.23°C or 393.23 K}\\T_{min}&= T_{\text{sat at 0.05 bar}} = \text{32.88°C or 305.88 K}\\\text{Carnot cycle efficiency}&= 1 – \frac{T_{min}}{T_{max}} = \text{0.2221 or 22.21}\%\end{aligned}\\\qquad \qquad \text{Modified Rankine cycle efficiency = 18.07}\%,\\\qquad \qquad \text{Carnot efficiency = 22.21}\%\)
