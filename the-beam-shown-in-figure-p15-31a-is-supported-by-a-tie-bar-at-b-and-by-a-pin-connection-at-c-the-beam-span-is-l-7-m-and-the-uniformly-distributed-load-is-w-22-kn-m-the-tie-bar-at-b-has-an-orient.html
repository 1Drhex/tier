Guest Question.

The beam shown in Figure P15.31a is supported by a tie bar at B and by a pin connection at C. The beam span is L = 7 m and the uniformly distributed load is w = 22 kN/m. The tie bar at B has an orientation of θ = 25°. The cross-sectional dimensions of the beam shown in Figure P15.31b are \(b_{f}\) = 130 mm, \(t_{f}\) = 12 mm, d = 360 mm, \(t_{w}\) = 6 mm, and \(y_{H}\) = 50 mm. Determine the principal stresses and the maximum shear stress acting at point H. Show these stresses on an appropriate sketch.

Admin
Solution

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

\(\begin{array}{lcccccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Width } b \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Height } h \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 130 & 12 & 18,720.0 & 174.0 & 47,230,560.0 & 47,249,280.0 \\\text { web } & 6 & 336 & 18,966,528.0 & 0.0 & 0.0 & 18,966,528.0 \\\text { bottom flange } & 130 & 12 & 18,720.0 & -174.0 & 47,230,560.0 & 47,249,280.0 \\\hline {\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( mm ^{4}\right)=} & 113,465,088.0\end{array}\)

 

Shear-force and bending-moment diagram:

Shear force at H:
V = –46.20 kN

Bending moment at H:
M = 85.24 kN-m

Axial force at H:

\(P=\frac{A_{y}}{\sin \left(25^{\circ}\right)}=\frac{77.00 kN }{\sin \left(25^{\circ}\right)}=182.198 kN\)

Axial stress at H:

\(\sigma_{x}=\frac{182,198 N }{5,136 mm ^{2}}=34.475 MPa\)

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Bending stress at point H: (y = −50 mm)

\(\sigma_{x}=-\frac{M y}{I_{z}}=-\frac{(86.240 kN – m )(-50 mm )(1,000 N / kN )(1,000 mm / m )}{113,465,088.0 mm ^{4}}=38.003 MPa\)

Transverse and horizontal shear stress at point H:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q=(130 mm )(12 mm )(174 mm )+(6 mm )(118 mm )(109 mm )=348,612 mm ^{3} \\&\tau=\frac{V Q}{I t}=\frac{(46,200 N )\left(348,612 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(113,465,088.0 mm ^{4}\right)(6 mm )}=23.658 MPa \quad \text { (Sense to be determined by inspection) }\end{aligned}\)

Stress element at H:

 

Principal stress calculations:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq. (12.12):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}}{2}\right)^{2}+\tau_{x y}^{2}} \\&=\frac{(73.477)+(0)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(73.477)-(0)}{2}\right)^{2}+(-23.658)^{2}} \\&=36.739 \pm 43.697\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{p 1}=80.4 MPa \text{and} \sigma_{p 2}=-6.96 MPa \\&\tau_{\max }=43.7 MPa \text{(maximum in-plane shear str )} \\&\sigma_{\text {avg }}=36.7 MPa ( T ) \text{(normal stress on planes of maximum in-plane shear str s es )}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan 2 \theta_{p} &=\frac{\tau_{x y}}{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) / 2}=\frac{-23.658}{[(73.477)-(0)] / 2}=\frac{-23.658}{36.739}=-0.6439 \\&\left.\therefore \theta_{p}=-16.39^{\circ} \quad \text { (clockwise from the } x \text { axis to the direction of } \sigma_{p 1}\right)\end{aligned}\)
