Guest Question.

Finding the frequency response of an op-amp integrator The op-amp integrator in Fig. 3.21(a) has \( R_{1}=1 k \Omega, R_{ F }=200 k \Omega, C_{ F }=0.1 \mu F , C_{ i }=1.5 pF , R_{ i }=2 M \Omega, R_{ o }=75 \Omega \), and open-loop voltage gain \( A_{0}=2 \times 10^{5} \).

(a) Calculate the low-frequency (or DC) voltage gain \( A_{ PB }=v_{ o } / v_{ S } \).

(b) Use the zero-value method to calculate the high 3-dB frequency \( f_{ H } \).

(c) Use PSpice/SPICE to plot the frequency response.

Admin
Solution

(a) Assuming all capacitors are open-circuited, the low-frequency pass-band voltage gain can be found as follows:

\( A_{ PB } \approx-\frac{R_{ F }}{R_{1}}=-\frac{200 k \Omega}{1 k \Omega}=-200 \)

 

(b) The high-frequency equivalent circuits of the op-amp integrator are shown in Fig. 3.23. If CF is opencircuited and the voltage-controlled voltage source is converted to a voltage-controlled current source, we get the circuit in Fig. 3.23(a). The transconductance \( g_{ m } \) is given by

\( g_{ m }=\frac{A_{ o }}{R_{ o }} \) (3.64)

 

\( =\frac{2 \times 10^{5}}{75}=2.67 kA / V \)

 

Applying a test voltage \( v_{ X } \) and KVL, we get

\( v_{ x }=R_{ F } i_{ x }+\left(R_{ o } \| R_{ L }\right)\left(i_{ x }-g_{ m } v_{ x }\right) \)

 

which gives the resistance \( R_{ X } \) as

 

\( R_{ x }=\frac{v_{ x }}{i_{ x }}=\frac{R_{ F }+ R _{ o } \| R _{ L }}{1+g_{ m }\left( R _{ o } \| R _{ L }\right)} \) (3.65)

 

\( =\frac{200 k \Omega+75 \Omega \| 10 k \Omega}{1+2.67 \times 10^{3} A / V \times(75 \Omega \| 10 k \Omega)}=1 \Omega \)

 

Thevenin’s equivalent resistance presented to \( C_{ i } \) is

\( R_{ Ci }=R_{1}\left\|R_{ i }\right\| R_{ x }=1 k \Omega\|2 M \Omega\| 1 \Omega \approx 1 \Omega \)

 

and \( f_{ Ci }=\frac{\omega_{ i }}{2 \pi}=\frac{1}{\left[2 \pi\left(C_{ i } R_{ Ci }\right)\right]}=\frac{1}{[2 \pi \times(1.5 pF \times 1)]}=106.1 \times 10^{9} Hz \)

 

If we assume \( C_{ i } \) is open-circuited, the equivalent circuit is shown in Fig. 3.23(b). Applying a test voltage \( v_{y} \) and using Eq. (2.116), we can find the equivalent resistance \( R_{ y }=v_{ y } / i_{ y } \). That is,

\(R_{eq}=\frac{v_x}{i_x} =R_L+R_i(1+g_mR_L)\) (2.116)

\( R_{ y }=\frac{v_{ y }}{i_{ y }}=R_{ o } \| R_{ L }+\left(R_{1} \| R_{ i }\right)\left[1+g_{ m }\left(R_{ o } \| R_{ L }\right)\right] \) (3.66)

 

\( =75 \Omega \| 10 k \Omega+(1 k \Omega \| 2 M \Omega)\left[1+2.667 \times 10^{3} A / V \times(75 \Omega \| 10 k \Omega)\right]=198.4 M \Omega \)

 

Thevenin’s equivalent resistance presented to \( C_{ F } \) is

\( R_{ CF }=R_{ F }\left\|R_{ y }=200 k \Omega\right\| 198.4 M \Omega=199.8 k \Omega \)

 

Thus, using Eq. (2.112), high 3-dB frequency \( f_{ H } \) is

 

\( f_{ H }=\frac{1}{2 \pi \sum\limits_{j=1}^{n} \tau_{ Cj }}=\frac{1}{2 \pi \sum\limits_{ j =1}^{n} C_{ j } R_{ Cj }} \) (2.112)

 

\( f_{ H }=\frac{1}{2 \pi\left(R_{ Ci } C_{ i }+R_{ CF } C_{ F }\right)}=\frac{1}{2 \pi(1 \times 1.5 pF +199.8 k \Omega \times 0.1 \mu F )}=7.97 Hz \)

 

which is dominated by \( C_{ F } \) as expected and can be approximated by

 

\( f_{ H }=\frac{\omega_{ F }}{2 \pi}=\frac{1}{\left[2 \pi\left(C_{ F } R_{ CF }\right)\right]}=\frac{1}{[2 \pi \times(0.1 \mu F \times 199.8 k \Omega)]}=7.97 Hz \)

 

There really was no need to find the value of \( R_{ y } \), which is usually very large for an op-amp circuit, and \( R_{ CF } \approx R_{ F } \).

(c) Node numbers are assigned to the AC equivalent circuit of Fig. 3.21(a) for PSpice simulation. The PSpice plot of the frequency response is shown in Fig. 3.24, which gives the midfrequency gain as \( \left|A_{\text {low }}\right|=198.16 \). The high 3-dB frequency is approximately \( f_{ H }=7.96 Hz \). The expected values are \( f_{ H }=7.97 Hz \text { and } A_{ PB }=-200 \).
