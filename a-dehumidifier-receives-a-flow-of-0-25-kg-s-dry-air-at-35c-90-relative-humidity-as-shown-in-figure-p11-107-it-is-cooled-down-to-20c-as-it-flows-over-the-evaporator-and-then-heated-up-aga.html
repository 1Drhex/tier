Guest Question.

A dehumidifier receives a flow of 0.25 kg/s dry air at 35°C, 90% relative humidity as shown in figure P11.107. It is cooled down to 20°C as it flows over the evaporator and then heated up again as it flows over the condenser. The standard refrigeration cycle uses R-410A with an evaporator temperature of –5°C and a condensation pressure of 3000 kPa. Find the amount of liquid water removed and the heat transfer in the cooling process. How much compressor work is needed? What is the final air exit temperature and relative humidity?

Admin
Solution

This set-up has a standard refrigeration cycle with R-410A. This cycle and the air flow interacts through the two heat transfer processes. The cooling of the air is provided by the refrigeration cycle and thus requires an amount of work that depends on the cycle COP.

Refrigeration cycle:

State 1: \(x =1 \quad h _{1}=277.53 kJ / kg , s _{1}=1.0466 kJ / kg K\)

State 2: \(s _{2}= s _{1}, \quad h _{2}=318.97 kJ / kg , T _{2}=72.9^{\circ} C\)

State 3: \(x _{3}=0.0, h _{3}= h _{ f }=141.78 kJ / kg , \quad\left( T _{3}=49.07^{\circ} C \right)\)

State 4: \( h _{4}= h _{3} \text { and } P _{4}= P _{1}\)

Now for the cycle we get

\(\begin{aligned}& w _{ C }= h _{2}- h _{1}=318.97-277.53=41.44 kJ / kg \\& q _{ H }= h _{2}- h _{3}=318.97-141.78=177.19 kJ / kg \\& q _{ L }= h _{1}- h _{4}=277.53-141.78=135.75 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

For the air processes let us use the psychrometric chart.

Air inlet: \(w _{ in }=0.019, \quad \tilde{ h }_{ in }=96 kJ / kg \text { dry air, } T _{ dew }=24^{ o } C >15^{\circ} C\)

Air 15: \(\phi=100 \%, w _{20}=0.0148, \quad \tilde{ h }_{20}=77.5, \quad h _{ f }=83.94\) (B.1.1)

Now do the continuity (for water) and energy equations for the cooling process

\(\dot{ m }_{ liq }=\dot{ m }_{ air }\left( w _{ in }- w _{20}\right)=0.25(0.019-0.0148)= 0 . 0 0 1 0 5 \textrm{kg } / s\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ Q }_{ cool } &=\dot{ m }_{ air }\left(\tilde{ h }_{ in }-\tilde{ h }_{20}\right)-\dot{ m }_{ liq } h _{ f }=0.25(96-77.5)-0.00105 \times 83.94 \\&= 4 . 5 3 7 k W\end{aligned}\)

 

Now the heater from the R-410A cycle has

\(\dot{ Q }_{\text {heat }}=\dot{ Q }_{\text {cool }}\left( q _{ H } / q _{ L }\right)=4.537(177.19 / 135.75)=5.922 kW\)

so the compressor work is the balance of the two

\(\dot{ W }_{ C }=\dot{ Q }_{\text {heat }}-\dot{ Q }_{ cool }=5.922-4.537= 1 . 3 8 5 \mathrm { k } W\)

Energy eq. for the air flow being heated

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{ Q }_{\text {heat }}=\dot{ m }_{ air }\left(\tilde{ h }_{ ex }-\tilde{ h }_{20}\right) \Rightarrow \tilde{ h }_{ ex }=\tilde{ h }_{20}+\dot{ Q }_{\text {heat }} / \dot{ m }_{ air } \\&\tilde{ h }_{ ex }=77.5+5.922 / 0.25=101.2 kJ / kg \text { dry air } \quad \text { and } \quad w _{ ex }= w _{20}\end{aligned}\)

Locate state in the psychrometric chart

\(T =43.5^{\circ} C \text { and } \quad \phi=27 \%\)
