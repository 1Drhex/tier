Cash Flow for Mara Corporation This problem will give you some practice working with financial statements and figuring cash flow. Based on the following information for Mara Corporation, prepare an income statement for 1999 and balance sheets for 1998 and 1999. Next, following our U.S. Corporation examples in the chapter, calculate cash flow from assets, cash flow to creditors, and cash flow to stockholders for Mara for 1999. Use a 35 percent tax rate throughout. You can check your answers against ours, found in the following section.

1999 1998
$4,507 $4,203 Sales
2,633 2,422 Cost of goods sold
952 785 Depreciation
196 180 Interest
250 225 Dividends
2,429 2,205 Current assets
7,650 7,344 Net fixed assets
1,255 1,003 Current liabilities
2,085 3,106 Long-term debt
Admin
Solution

In preparing the balance sheets, remember that shareholders’ equity is the residual. With this in mind, Mara’s balance sheets are as follows:

MARA CORPORATION
Balance sheets as of December 31, 1998 and 1999

1999 1998 1999 1998
$ 1,255 $1,003 Current liabilities $ 2,429 $2,205 Current assets
2,085 3,106 Long-term debt \(\underline{7,650}\) \(\underline{7,344}\) Net fixed assets
\(\underline{6,739}\) \(\underline{5,440}\) Equity
\(\underline{\underline{ \$10,079}}\) \(\underline{\underline{\$9,549}}\) Total liabilities and shareholders’ equity \(\underline{\underline{ \$10,079}}\) \(\underline{\underline{\$9,549}}\) Total assets

The income statement is straightforward:

MARA CORPORATION
1999 Income Statement

$4,507 Sales
2,633 Cost of goods sold
\(\underline{952}\) Depreciation
$ 922 Earnings before interest and taxes
\(\underline{196}\) Interest paid
$ 726 Taxable income
\(\underline{254}\) Taxes (35%)
\(\underline{\underline{\$ 472}}\) Net income
$250 Dividends
222 Addition to retained earnings

Notice that we’ve used an average 35 percent tax rate. Also notice that the addition to retained earnings is just net income less cash dividends.

We can now pick up the figures we need to get operating cash flow:

MARA CORPORATION
1999 Operating Cash Flow

Earnings before interest and taxes $ 922
+ Depreciation 952
-Taxes \(\underline{\$ 254}\)
Operating cash flow \(\underline{\underline{\$1,620}}\)

Next, we get the net capital spending for the year by looking at the change in fixed assets, remembering to account for depreciation:

$7,650 Ending net fixed assets
7,344 -Beginning net fixed assets
\(\underline{952}\) +Depreciation
\(\underline{\underline{\$1,258}}\) Net capital spending

After calculating beginning and ending NWC, we take the difference to get the change in NWC:

$1,174 Ending NWC
\(\underline{1,202}\) -Beginning NWC
\(\underline{\underline{-\$ 28}}\) Change in NWC

We now combine operating cash flow, net capital spending, and the change in net working capital to get the total cash flow from assets:

MARA CORPORATION
1999 Cash Flow from Assets

$1,620 Operating cash flow
1,258 -Net capital spending
\(\underline{-28}\) -Change in NWC
\(\underline{\underline{\$ 390}}\) Cash flow from assets

To get cash flow to creditors, notice that long-term borrowing decreased by $1,021 during the year and that interest paid was $196, so:

MARA CORPORATION
1999 Cash Flow to Creditors

$ 196 Interest paid
\(\underline{-1,021}\) -Net new borrowing
\(\underline{\underline{\$ 1,217}}\) Cash flow to creditors

Finally, dividends paid were $250. To get net new equity raised, we have to do some extra calculating. Total equity was up by $6,739-5,440=$1,299.

Of this increase, $222 was from additions to retained earnings, so $1,077 in new equity was raised during the year. Cash flow to stockholders was thus:

MARA CORPORATION
1999 Cash Flow to Stockholders

$ 250 Dividends paid
\(\underline{1,077}\) -Net new equity raised
–\(\underline{\underline{\$ 827}}\) Cash flow to stockholders

As a check, notice that cash flow from assets ($390) does equal cash flow to creditors plus cash flow to stockholders ($1,217-827=$390).
