Guest Question.

Field vane shear tests (FVST) were conducted in a layer of organic clay (not peat). The rectangular vane dimensions were 63.5 mm width × 127 mm height. At the 2 m depth, the torque T required to cause failure was 51 N.m. The liquid limit LL and plastic limit PL of the clay were 50 and 20, respectively. The effective unit weight \( {\gamma }' \) of the clay is \(18 kN/ m^{3} \). Estimate the design undrained vane shear strength \( s_{u} \), the preconsolidation pressure \( {\sigma}'_{c} \) and the ratio OCR of the clay.

Admin
Solution
\( T = 51N m = 51 \times 10^{-3} kN m; \)

 

\( D= 63.5 mm = 63 5 \times 10^{-3} m \)

 

\( H = 127 mm = 127 \times 10^{-3} m;H / D= 2 \)

 

\( S_{u,v} = \frac{6T}{7\pi D^{3}} = \frac{6 \times 51 \times 10^{-3}}{7 \times \pi \left ( 63.5 \times 10 – 3 \right)^{3}} = 54.32 kN/m^{2} \)

 

\( \lambda = 1.7 – 0.54 log [PI \left ( \% \right )] = 1.7 – 0.54 log \left ( 50 – 20 \right) = 0.902 \)

 

As the soil is organic (not peat), an additional correction factor of 0.85 is recommended. Design

 

\( s_{u,v} = 0.85 \times \lambda \times s_{u,v} = 0.85 \times 0.902 \times 54.32 = 41.65 kN/m^{2} \)

 

\( {\sigma }'_{c} = 7.04 [c_{u,v}\left ( field \right )]^{0.83} = 7.04 \left ( 54.32 \right )^{0.83} = 193.9 kN/m^{2} \)

 

\( OCR = \beta \frac{c_{u,v}\left ( field \right )}{{\sigma }_{O}'} \)

 

\( \beta = 22\left ( PI \right )^{-0.48} = 22 \left ( 50 – 20 \right )^{- 0.48} = 4.3 \)

 

\( OCR = \beta \frac{c_{u,v}\left ( ield \right )}{{\sigma }_{O}'} = 4.3 \times \frac{54.32}{2 \times 18} = 6.5 > \left ( \frac{193.9}{2 \times 18} = 5.4 !? \right ) \)

 

\( \beta = \frac{1}{0.08 + 0.0055\left ( PI \right )} = \frac{1}{0.08 + 0.0055 \left ( 50 – 20 \right )} = 4.08 \)

 

\( OCR = \beta \frac{c_{u,v}\left ( ield \right )}{{\sigma }_{O}'} = 4.08 \times \frac{54.32}{2 \times 18} = 6.2 > \left ( \frac{193.9}{2 \times 18} = 5.4 !? \right ) \)
