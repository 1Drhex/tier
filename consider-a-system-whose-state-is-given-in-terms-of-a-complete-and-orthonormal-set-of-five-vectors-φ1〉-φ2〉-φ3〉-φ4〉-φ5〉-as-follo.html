Guest Question.

Consider a system whose state is given in terms of a complete and orthonormal set of five
vectors \(|\phi _1〉,|\phi _2〉,|\phi _3〉,|\phi _4〉,|\phi _5〉 \) as follows:

\(|\psi 〉=\frac{1}{\sqrt{19} } |\phi _1〉+\frac{2}{\sqrt{19} } |\phi _2〉+\sqrt{\frac{2}{19} }|\phi _3〉 +\sqrt{\frac{3}{19} }|\phi _4〉+\sqrt{\frac{5}{19} }|\phi _5〉, \)

where \(|\phi _n〉\) are eigenstates to the system’s Hamiltonian, \(\hat{H} |\phi _n〉=n\varepsilon _0| \phi _n〉 \) with n = 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and where \(\varepsilon _0 \) has the dimensions of energy.

(a) If the energy is measured on a large number of identical systems that are all initially in the same state | ψ〉 , what values would one obtain and with what probabilities?

(b) Find the average energy of one such system.

Admin
Solution

First, note that | ψ〉 is not normalized:

\(〈\psi |\psi 〉=\sum\limits_{n=1}^{5}{a^{2}_{n} }〈\phi _n|\phi _n 〉=\sum\limits_{n=1}^{5}{a^{2}_{n} }=\frac{1}{19}+\frac{4}{19}+\frac{2}{19}+\frac{3}{19}+\frac{5}{19}=\frac{15}{19}, \) (3.36)

since \(〈\phi _j |\phi _k 〉=\delta _{jk} \) with j, k = 1, 2, 3, 4, 5.

(a) Since \(E_n=〈\phi _n |\hat{H} |\phi _n 〉=n\varepsilon _0(n=1,2,3,4,5), \) the various measurements of the energy of the system yield the values \(E_1=\varepsilon _0,E_2=2\varepsilon _0,E_3=3\varepsilon _0,E_4=4\varepsilon _0,E_5=5\varepsilon _0\) with the following probabilities:

\(P_1(E_1)=\frac{|〈\phi _1 |\psi 〉|^2}{〈\psi |\psi 〉}=\left|\frac{1}{\sqrt{19} }〈\phi _1 |\phi _1 〉 \right|^2\times \frac{19}{15}=\frac{1}{15}, \) (3.37)

\(P_2(E_2)=\frac{|〈\phi _2 |\psi 〉|^2}{〈\psi |\psi 〉}=\left|\frac{2}{\sqrt{19} }〈\phi _2 |\phi _2 〉 \right|^2\times \frac{19}{15}=\frac{4}{15}, \) (3.38)

\(P_3(E_3)=\frac{|〈\phi _3 |\psi 〉|^2}{〈\psi |\psi 〉}=\left| \sqrt{\frac{2}{19} } 〈\phi _3 |\phi _3 〉 \right|^2\times \frac{19}{15}=\frac{2}{15}, \) (3.39)

\(P_4(E_4)=\frac{|〈\phi _4 |\psi 〉|^2}{〈\psi |\psi 〉}=\left| \sqrt{\frac{3}{19} } 〈\phi _4 |\phi _4 〉 \right|^2\times \frac{19}{15}=\frac{3}{15}, \) (3.40)

and

\(P_5(E_5)=\frac{|〈\phi _5 |\psi 〉|^2}{〈\psi |\psi 〉}=\left| \sqrt{\frac{5}{19} } 〈\phi _5 |\phi _5 〉 \right|^2\times \frac{19}{15}=\frac{5}{15}, \) (3.41)

(b) The average energy of a system is given by

\(E=\sum\limits_{j=1}^{5}{P_jE_j}=\frac{1}{15}\varepsilon _0+\frac{8}{15} \varepsilon _0+\frac{6}{15}\varepsilon _0+\frac{12}{15}\varepsilon _0+\frac{25}{15}\varepsilon _0=\frac{52}{15}\varepsilon _0. \) (3.42)

This energy can also be obtained from the expectation value of the Hamiltonian:

\(E=\frac{〈\psi |\hat{H} |\psi 〉}{〈\psi |\psi 〉}=\frac{19}{15}\sum\limits_{n=1}^{5}{a^2_n} 〈\phi_n |\hat{H} |\phi _n 〉 =\frac{19}{15}\left(\frac{1}{19}+\frac{8}{19}+\frac{6}{19}+\frac{12}{19}+\frac{25}{19} \right) \varepsilon _0 \)

\(=\frac{52}{15} \varepsilon _0 , \) (3.43)

where the values of the coefficients \(a^2_n \) are listed in (3.36).
