Guest Question.

Express the vector \(B=\frac{10}{r}a_{r}+r\cos\theta a_{\theta}+a_{\phi}\) in Cartesian and cylindrical coordinates. Find B(-3, 4, 0) and B(5, π/2, -2).

Admin
Solution

Using eq. (2.28):

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} A_{x} \\ A_{y} \\ A_{z} \end{matrix} \right ]=\left [ \begin{matrix} \sin\theta\cos\phi & \cos\theta\cos\phi & -\sin\phi \\ \sin\theta\sin\phi & \cos\theta\sin\phi & \cos\phi \\ \cos\theta & -\sin\theta & 0 \end{matrix} \right ]\left [ \begin{matrix} A_{r} \\ A_{\theta} \\ A_{\phi} \end{matrix} \right ]\)

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} B_{x} \\ B_{y} \\ B_{z} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} \sin\theta\cos\phi & \cos\theta\cos\phi & -\sin\theta \\ \sin\theta\sin\phi & \cos\theta\sin\phi & \cos\phi \\ \cos\theta & -\sin\theta & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} \frac{10}{r} \\ r\cos\theta \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

or

\(B_{x}=\frac{10}{r} \sin\theta\cos\phi+r\cos^{2}\theta\cos\phi-\sin\phi\)

\(B_{y}=\frac{10}{r} \sin\theta\sin\phi+r\cos^{2}\theta\sin\phi-\cos\phi\)

\(B_{z}=\frac{10}{r} \cos\theta-r\cos\theta\sin\theta\)

But \(r=\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}, \theta=\tan^{-1}\frac{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}}}{z}\) and \(\phi=\tan^{-1}=\frac{y}{x}\)

Hence

\(\sin\theta=\frac{\rho}{r}=\frac{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}} }{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}}, \cos\theta=\frac{z}{r}=\frac{z}{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}} } \)

\(\sin\phi=\frac{y}{\rho}=\frac{y}{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}}}, \cos\phi=\frac{x}{\rho}=\frac{x}{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}}}\)

Substituting all these gives

\(B_{x}=\frac{10\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}} }{\left(x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}\right) }\cdot \frac{x}{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}} }+\frac{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}} }{\left(x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}\right) }\cdot \frac{z^{2}x}{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}} }- \frac{y}{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}} }\)

\(=\frac{10x}{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}} +\frac{xz^{2}}{\sqrt{\left(x^{2}+y^{2}\right) \left(x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}\right)}} -\frac{y}{\sqrt{\left(x^{2}+y^{2}\right) } }\)

\(B_{y}=\frac{10\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}} }{\left(x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}\right) } \cdot \frac{y}{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}} }+\frac{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}} }{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2} }\cdot \frac{z^{2}y}{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}} }+\frac{x}{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}} }\)

\(=\frac{10y}{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}+ \frac{yz^{2}}{\sqrt{\left(x^{2}+y^{2}\right)\left(x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}\right)}}+\frac{x}{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}} }\)

\(B_{z}=\frac{10z}{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}-\frac{z\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}} }{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}} }\)

\(B=B_{x}a_{x}+B_{y}a_{y}+B_{z}a_{z}\)

where \(B_{x}\), \(B_{y}\), and \(B_{z}\) are as just given.

At (-3, 4, 0), x = -3, y = 4, and z = 0, so

\(B_{x}=-\frac{30}{25} +0-\frac{4}{5} =-2\)

\(B_{y}=\frac{40}{25} +0-\frac{3}{5} =1\)

\(B_{z}=0-0 =0\)

Thus

\(B=-2a_{x}+a_{y}\)

For spherical to cylindrical vector transformation (see Problem 2.16):

(a) Show that point transformation between cylindrical and spherical coordinates is obtained using

\(r=\sqrt{\rho^{2}+z^{2}}, \theta=\tan^{-1}\frac{\rho}{z}, \phi=\phi\)

or

\(\rho=r\sin\theta, z=rcos\theta, \phi=\phi\)

(b) Show that vector transformation between cylindrical and spherical coordinates is obtained using

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} A_{x} \\ A_{\theta} \\ A_{\phi} \end{matrix} \right ]=\left [ \begin{matrix} \sin\theta & 0 & \cos\theta \\ \cos\theta & 0 & -sin\theta \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} A_{\rho} \\ A_{\phi} \\ A_{z} \end{matrix} \right ]\)

or

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} A_{\rho} \\ A_{\phi} \\ A_{z} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} \sin\theta & \cos\theta & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ \cos\theta & -\sin\theta & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} A_{r} \\ A_{\theta} \\ A_{\phi} \end{matrix} \right ]\)

(Hint: Make use of Figures 2.5 and 2.6.)

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} B_{\rho} \\ B_{\phi} \\ B_{z} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} \sin\theta & \cos\theta & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ \cos\theta & -\sin\theta & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} \frac{10}{r} \\ r\cos\theta \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

or

\(B_{\rho}=\frac{10}{r} \sin\theta+r\cos^{2}\theta\)

\(B_{\phi}=1\)

\(B_{z}=\frac{10}{r} \cos\theta-r\sin\theta\cos\theta\)

But \(r=\sqrt{\rho^{2}+z^{2}}\) and \(\theta=\tan^{-1}\frac{\rho}{z}\)

Thus

\(\sin\theta=\frac{\rho}{\sqrt{\rho^{2}+z^{2}}}, \cos\theta=\frac{z}{\sqrt{\rho^{2}+z^{2}} }\)

\(B_{\rho}=\frac{10\rho}{\rho^{2}+z^{2}}+\sqrt{\rho^{2}+z^{2}}\cdot \frac{z^{2}}{\rho^{2}+z^{2}}\)

\(B_{z}=\frac{10z}{\rho^{2}+z^{2}}-\sqrt{\rho^{2}+z^{2}}\cdot \frac{\rho z}{\rho^{2}+z^{2}}\)

Hence

\(B=\left(\frac{10\rho}{\rho^{2}+z^{2}}+\frac{z^{2}}{\sqrt{\rho^{2}+z^{2}}}\right)a_{\rho}+a_{\phi}+\left(\frac{10z}{\rho^{2}+z^{2}}-\frac{\rho z}{\sqrt{\rho^{2}+z^{2}} } \right) a_{z}\)

At \(\left(5,\pi/2,-2\right), \rho=5, \phi=\pi/2\), and z = -2, so

\(B=\left(\frac{50}{29}+\frac{4}{\sqrt{29} } \right) a_{\rho}+a_{\phi}+\left(\frac{-20}{29}+\frac{10}{\sqrt{29} } \right)a_{z}\)

\(=2.467a_{\rho}+a_{\phi}+1.167a_{z}\)

Note that at (-3, 4, 0),

\(\left|B\left(x,y,z\right) \right| =\left|B\left(\rho,\phi,z\right) \right| =\left|B\left(r,\theta,\phi\right) \right| =2.907\)

This may be used to check the correctness of the result whenever possible.
