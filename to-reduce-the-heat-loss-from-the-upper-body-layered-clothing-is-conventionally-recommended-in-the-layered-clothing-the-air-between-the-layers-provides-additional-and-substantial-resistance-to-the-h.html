Guest Question.

To reduce the heat loss from the upper body, layered clothing is conventionally recommended. In the layered clothing, the air between the layers provides additional and substantial resistance to the heat flow. Consider a single, planar layer of fabric of thickness \(L_{1} = 4.5\) mm and conductivity of\( k_{f} = 0.1\) W/m-K. This is shown in Figure (a) . Then consider three fabric layers each having a thickness \(L_{2}\) such that the sum of the three thicknesses is equal to\( L_{1}\), i.e.,\( 3L_{2} = L_{1}\). For the three-layer clothing allow for an air gap of thickness \(L_{a} = 1\) mm between the adjacent fabric layers, with \(k_{a} = 0.025\) W/m-K, as shown in Figure (b). The temperatures at either side of the single and multilayer (i.e., fabric and air layers) composites are prescribed as \(T_{1} \) and \(T_{2}\), with \(T1 = 28^{◦}C\) and \(T_{2} = 15^{◦}C\).

(a) Draw the thermal circuit diagram.
(b) Determine the heat loss per unit area \(q_{k}(W/m^{2})\) for the single and multilayer composites.
(c) Write the expression for the composite resistance in terms of the porosity defined by \( \epsilon =\frac{V_{f}}{V_{s}+V_{f}} prosity \epsilon \).

Admin
Solution

(a) Figure (c) shows the thermal circuit model for the two conditions.
(b) The conduction heat flow per unit area through the single and multilayer composites is one dimensional and given by \( Q_{k,1-2}=\frac{T_{1}-T_{2}}{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{R_{k,i}} }\equiv \frac{T_{1}-T_{2}}{R_{k\sum }}, R_{k,\sum }\equiv \sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{R_{k,i}}, G_{k\sum }\equiv \frac{1}{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{R_{k,i}} } \) , i.e.,

\( Q_{k,1-2}=A_{k}q_{k}=\frac{T_{1}-T_{2}}{R_{k\sum }}\)

For the single-layer fabric we have the only resistance as

\( R_{k,\sum }=R_{k,1-2}=\frac{L_{1}}{A_{k}k_{f}} \)

For the multilayer composite we have a series arrangement of five resistances, i.e., \( Q_{k,1-2}=\frac{T_{1}-T_{2}}{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{R_{k,i}} }\equiv \frac{T_{1}-T_{2}}{R_{k\sum }}, R_{k,\sum }\equiv \sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{R_{k,i}}, G_{k\sum }\equiv \frac{1}{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{R_{k,i}} } \) , as shown in Figure (b), and these are

\(R_{k,\sum }=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{5}{R_{k,i}}=R_{k,1-1^{\prime }}+ R_{k,1^{\prime}-1^{\prime\prime }}+ R_{k,1^{\prime \prime }-2^{\prime \prime }}+R_{k,2^{\prime \prime }-2^{\prime }}+R_{k,2^{\prime }-2}\) \(=\frac{L_{2}}{A_{k}k_{f}}+\frac{L_{a}}{A_{k}k_{a}} +\frac{L_{2}}{A_{k}k_{f}}+\frac{L_{a}}{A_{k}k_{a}}+\frac{L_{2}}{A_{k}k_{f}}=\frac{3L_{2}}{A_{k}k_{f}}+\frac{2L_{a}}{A_{k}k_{a}}\)

So by substituting for these resistances, for the single-layer fabric we have

\( Q_{k,1-2}=A_{k}q_{k}=\frac{T_{1}-T_{2}}{\frac{L_{1}}{A_{k}k_{f}} } \)

or

\( q_{k}=\frac{T_{1}-T_{2}}{\frac{L_{1}}{k_{f}} }=\frac{(28-15)(^{\circ }C)}{\frac{0.0045(m)}{0.1(W/m-K)} }=\frac{13(^{\circ }C)}{0.045[^{\circ }C/(W/m^{2})]}=288.9 W/m^{2} \) for single-layer fabric.

For the multilayer composite, we have

\(q_{k}=\frac{T_{1}-T_{2}}{\frac{3L_{2}}{k_{f}}+\frac{2L_{a}}{k_{a}} }=\frac{(28-15)(^{\circ }C)}{\frac{3\times 0.0015(m)}{0.1(W/m-^{\circ }C)}+\frac{2\times 0.001(m)}{0.025(W/m-^{\circ }C)} }=\frac{13(^{\circ }C)}{0.045[^{\circ }C/(W/m^{2})]+0.080[^{\circ }C/(W/m^{2})]} =104.0 W/m^{2} \) for multilayer composite.

(c) The porosity is given by \( \epsilon =\frac{V_{f}}{V_{s}+V_{f}} prosity \epsilon \) and using \(V_{a}\) and \(V_{f} \) for air and fiber volumes, we have

\( \epsilon =\frac{V_{a}}{V_{f}+V_{a}}=\frac{A_{k}2L_{a}}{A_{k}3L_{2}+A_{k}2L_{a}}=\frac{2L_{a}}{3L_{2}+2L_{a}} \)

Then we write for \( R_{k,\sum }\)

\(R_{k,\sum }=\frac{1}{A_{k}}\left(\frac{3L_{1}}{k_{f}}+\frac{2L_{a}}{k_{a}} \right) =\frac{3L_{f}+2L_{a}}{A_{k}}\left(\frac{1-\epsilon }{k_{f}}+\frac{\epsilon }{k_{a}} \right) \)

where we have used

\( =\frac{3L_{2}}{3L_{2}+2L_{a}}=1-\frac{2L_{a}}{3L_{f}+2L_{a}}=1-\epsilon \)
