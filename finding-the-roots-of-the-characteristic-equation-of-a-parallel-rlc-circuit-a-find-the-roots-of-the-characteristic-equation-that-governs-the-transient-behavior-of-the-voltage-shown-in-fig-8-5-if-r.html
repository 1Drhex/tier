Guest Question.

Finding the Roots of the Characteristic Equation of a Parallel RLC Circuit

a) Find the roots of the characteristic equation that governs the transient behavior of the voltage shown in Fig. 8.5 if R = 200 Ω, L = 50 mH, and C = 0.2 μF.

b) Will the response be overdamped, underdamped, or critically damped?

c) Repeat (a) and (b) for R = 312.5 Ω .

d) What value of R causes the response to be critically damped?

Admin
Solution

a) For the given values of R, L, and C,

\(α = \frac {1}{2RC} =\frac {10^{6}}{(400)(0.2)} = 1.25 × 10^{4} rad/s\),

\(ω_{0}^{2} = \frac {1}{LC} = \frac{(10^{3})(10^{6})}{(50)(0.2)} = 10^{8} rad^{2}/s^{2}\).

From Eqs. 8.14 \(s_{1} = -α + \sqrt { α ^{2} – ω_{0}^{2}} \) , and 8.15 \(s_{2} = -α – \sqrt{ α ^{2} – ω_{0}^{2}} \) ,

\(s_{1} = -1.25 × 10^{4} + \sqrt {1.5625 × 10^{8} – 10^{8}}\)

= -12,500 + 7500 = -5000 rad/s,

\(s_{2} = -1.25 × 10^{4} – \sqrt {1.5625 × 10^{8} – 10^{8}}\)

b) The voltage response is overdamped because

\(ω_{0}^{2} < α^{2}\)

c) For R = 312.5 Ω,

\( α = \frac{10^{6}}{(625)(0.2)} = 8000 rad/s\),

\(α^{2} = 64 × 10^{6} = 0.64 × 10^{8} rad^{2}/s^{2}\).

As \(ω_{0}^{2}\) remains at \(10^{8} rad^{2}/s^{2}\),

\(s_{1}\) = -8000 + j6000 rad/s,

\(s_{2}\) = -8000 – j6000 rad/s.

(In electrical engineering, the imaginary number \( \sqrt {-1}\) is represented by the letter j, because the letter\( i \)represents current.)

In this case, the voltage response is underdamped since \(ω_{0}^{2} > α^{2}\)

d) For critical damping, \(α^{2} = ω_{0}^{2}\), so

\( \left( \frac {1}{ 2RC}\right)^{2} = \frac {1}{LC} = 10^{8}\),

Or

\(\frac {1}{ 2RC} = 10^{4}\)

and

\(R =\frac {10^{6}}{(2 × 10^{4})(0.2)}= 250 Ω\).
