Guest Question.

Calculate the approximate Reynolds numbers and state if the flow is laminar or turbulent for the following :
(i) A 10 m long yatch sailing at 13 km/h in sea water, \(ρ = 1000 kg/m^3\) and \(µ = 1.3 × 10^{–3} kg/ms.\)
(ii) A compressor disc of radius 0.3 m rotating at 15000 r.p.m. in air at 5 bar and 400°C and \(µ = \frac{1.46 × 10^{-6} T^{3/2}}{(110 + T)}kg/ms.\)
(iii) 0.05 kg/s of \(CO_2\) gas at 400 K flowing in a 20 mm dia. pipe and \(µ = \frac{1.56 × 10^{-6} T^{3/2}}{(233 + T)}kg/ms.\)

Admin
Solution

(i) Given : Yatch sails on sea water
L = 10 m,
\(u_∞ = 13 km/h =\frac{13 × 10^3}{60 × 60} = 3.61 m/s\)
\(ρ = 1000 kg/m^3,\)
\(µ = 1.3 × 10^{–3} kg/ms.\)
To find :
(a) Reynolds number,
(b) Type of flow.
Analysis : (a) The Reynolds number can be
calculated as
\(Re = \frac{ρu_∞ L}{µ} =\frac{1000 × 3.61 × 10}{1.3 × 10^3} \)

\(= 2.78 × 10^7.\)
(b) The \(Re > 5 × 10^5,\) thus flow is turbulent.
(ii) Given : A compressor disc with
\(r_o = 0.3 m or D = 0.6 m\)
N = 15000 rpm,
p = 5 bar, T = 400°C
and \(µ = \frac{1.46 × 10^{-6} T^{3/2}}{(110 + T)}\)

To find :
(a) Reynolds number,
(b) Type of flow.
Assumption : For air R = 0.287 kJ/kg.K.
Analysis : The equivalent linear velocity of compressor disc
\(u_∞ = \frac{ π DN}{60} = \frac{\pi ×0.6 × 1500}{60}= 471.23 m/s\)

The density of air at 5 bar and 400°C (= 673 K)
\(ρ = \frac{p}{RT} =\frac{5 × 100 kPa}{(0.287 kJ / kg . K ) ×(673 K)} \)

\(= 2.588 kg/m^3\)
The viscosity at 400°C
\(µ = \frac{1.46 × 10^{-6} (673)^{3/2}}{(110 + 673)}\)
\(= 3.3 × 10^{–5} kg/ms\)
(a) The Reynolds number
\(Re = \frac{ρu_∞ D}{µ} =\frac{2.588 × 471.23 × 0.6}{3.3 × 10^{-5}} \)

\(= 22.17 × 10^6.\)
(b) The \(Re > 5 × 10^5,\) thus flow is turbulent.
(iii) Given : \(CO_2 \) gas
m = 0.05 kg/s, T = 400 K,
\(D_i = 20 mm = 20 × 10^{–3} m,\)
and \(µ = \frac{1.56 × 10^{-6} T^{3/2}}{(233 + T)} kg/ms.\)
To find :
(a) Reynolds number,
(b) Type of flow.
Analysis : At 400 K, the density of \(CO_2\)
\(ρ = 1.3257 kg/m^3\)
(a) The Reynolds number for pipe flow can also be calculated as
\(Re = \frac{4\dot{m}}{\pi D_i µ} \)
where \(µ = \frac{1.56 × 10^{-6} T^{3/2}}{(233 + T)} \)

Then \(µ = \frac{1.56 × 10^{-6} (400)^{3/2}}{(233 + 400)} \)

\(= 1.97 × 10^{–5} kg/ms.\)
Then \(Re = \frac{4 × 0.05}{\pi ×20 × 10^{-3} × 1.97 ×10^{-5} } \)

\(= 1.61 × 10^5. \)
(b) Re ≥ 2300 for tube flow, thus the flow is turbulent
