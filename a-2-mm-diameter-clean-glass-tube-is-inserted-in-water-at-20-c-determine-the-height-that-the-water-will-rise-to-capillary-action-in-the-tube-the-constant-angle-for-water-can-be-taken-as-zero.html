Guest Question.

A \(2 mm\) diameter clean glass tube is inserted in water at \(20 ^{\circ }C\). Determine the height that the water will rise to capillary action in the tube. The constant angle for water can be taken as zero.

Admin
Solution

A free-body diagram of water is shown in Fig. 1.5. It shows that the upward surface tension force is equal and opposite to the weight of water.

Weight of the water = \( W = mg =\rho v.g=\rho gv=\gamma \frac{\pi }{4}D^{2}h\)

Surface tension force \(F=\sigma \times length\)

Surface tension length = \(\sigma \pi D\)
