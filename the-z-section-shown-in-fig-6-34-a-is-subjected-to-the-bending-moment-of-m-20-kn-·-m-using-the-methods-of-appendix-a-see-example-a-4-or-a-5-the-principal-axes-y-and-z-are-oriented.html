Guest Question.

The Z -section shown in Fig. 6–34 a is subjected to the bending moment of M = 20 kN · m. Using the methods of Appendix A (see or ), the principal axes y and z are oriented as shown, such that they represent the minimum and maximum principal moments of inertia, \(I_y = 0.960(10^{-3}) m^4\) and \(I_z = 7.54(10^{-3}) m^4\) , respectively. Determine the normal stress at point P and the orientation of the neutral axis.

Admin
Solution

For use of \(\tan \alpha =\frac{I_z}{I_y}\tan \theta\) , it is important that the z axis represent the principal axis for the maximum moment of inertia. (Note that most of the area is located furthest from this axis.)

Internal Moment Components. From Fig. 6–34 a ,

\(M_y=20kN·m \sin 57.1°=16.79kN·m \)

 

\(M_z=20kN·m \cos 57.1°=10.86kN·m \)

Bending Stress. The y and z coordinates of point P must be determined first. Note that the y′, z′ coordinates of P are (-0.2 m, 0.35 m). Using the colored triangles from the construction shown in Fig. 6–34 b , we have

\(y_P=-0.35 \sin 32.9°-0.2 \cos 32.9°=-0.3580m\)

 

\(z_P=0.35 \cos 32.9° -0.2 \sin 32.9°=0.1852m\)

Applying Eq. 6–17,

\(\sigma =-\frac{M_zy}{I_z}+\frac{M_yz}{I_y} \) ( 6–17)

 

\(\sigma_P =-\frac{M_zy_P}{I_z}+\frac{M_yz_P}{I_y} \)

 

\(=-\frac{(10.86(10^3)N\cdot m)(-0.3580m)}{7.54(10^{-3})m^4}+\frac{(16.79(10^3)N\cdot m)(0.1852m)}{0.960(10^{-3})m^4} \)

 

\(=3.76MPa\)

Orientation of Neutral Axis. The angle \(θ = 57.1°\) is shown in Fig. 6–34 a . Thus,

\(\tan \alpha =\left[\frac{7.54(10^{-3})m^4}{0.960(10^{-3})m^4} \right] \tan 57.1^\circ \)

 

\(\alpha =85.3°\)

The neutral axis is oriented as shown in Fig. 6–34 b
