Guest Question.

Let \(M_{22}\) be the vector space of all 2 by 2 matrices. Show that the following matrices do not form a basis for \(M_{22}\) :

\(\mathbf{A}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}1 & 0 \\0 & 3\end{array}\right), \mathbf{B}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}0 & 1 \\2 & 0\end{array}\right) \text { and } \mathrm{C}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}2 & 1 \\1 & 5\end{array}\right)\)
Admin
Solution

What is the dimension of \(M_{22}\) ?

Recall from Table 3.1 in section 3.4.2 that \(\operatorname{dim}\left(M_{22}\right)=2 \times 2=4\). In the above list, we have three matrices so they cannot form a basis for \(M_{22}\)

Why not?

Because \(\operatorname{dim}\left(M_{22}\right)=4\), and by Definition (3.17) we need exactly four matrices for a basis.

Definition (3.17): The number of vectors in a basis of a non-zero vector space V is the

dimension of the space.

Table 3.1 Some vector spaces and their dimensions

Vector space Dimension
\(R^n\) n
\(P_{n}\) n+1
\(M_{mn}\) mn
