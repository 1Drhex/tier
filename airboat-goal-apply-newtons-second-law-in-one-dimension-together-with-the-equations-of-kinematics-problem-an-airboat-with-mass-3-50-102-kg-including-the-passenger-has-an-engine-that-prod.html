Guest Question.

Airboat

GOAL Apply Newton’s second law in one dimension, together with the equations of kinematics.

PROBLEM An airboat with mass \(3.50 \times 10^{2}\) kg, including the passenger, has an engine that produces a net horizontal force of \(7.70 \times 10^{2} N\), after accounting for forces of resistance (see Fig. 4.6). (a) Find the acceleration of the airboat. (b) Starting from rest, how long does it take the airboat to reach a speed of 12.0 m/s? (c) After reaching that speed, the pilot turns off the engine and drifts to a stop over a distance of 50.0 m. Find the resistance force, assuming it’s constant.

STRATEGY In part (a), apply Newton’s second law to find the acceleration, and in part (b) use that acceleration in the one-dimensional kinematics equation for the velocity. When the engine is turned off in part (c), only the resistance forces act on the boat in the x-direction, so the net acceleration can be found from \(v^{2}-v_{0}^{2}=2 a \Delta x\). Then Newton’s second law gives the resistance force.

Admin
Solution

(a) Find the acceleration of the airboat.

 

Apply Newton’s second law and solve for the acceleration: \(\begin{aligned}m a=F_{\text {net }} \rightarrow \quad a=\frac{F_{\text {net }}}{m} &=\frac{7.70 \times 10^{2} N }{3.50 \times 10^{2} kg } \\&=2.20 m / s ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Find the time necessary to reach a speed of 12.0 m/s.

 

Apply the kinematics velocity equation: \(v=a t+v_{0}=\left(2.20 m / s ^{2}\right) t=12.0 m / s \rightarrow t=5.45 s\)

 

c) Find the resistance force after the engine is turned off.

 

Using kinematics, find the net acceleration due to resistance forces: \(\begin{aligned}&v^{2}-v_{0}^{2}=2 a \Delta x \\&0-(12.0 m / s )^{2}=2 a(50.0 m ) \rightarrow a=-1.44 m / s ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

Substitute the acceleration into Newton’s second law, finding the resistance force: \(F_{\text {resist }}=m a=\left(3.50 \times 10^{2} kg \right)\left(-1.44 m / s ^{2}\right)=-504 N\)

 

REMARKS The propeller exerts a force on the air, pushing it backwards behind the boat. At the same time, the air exerts a force on the propellers and consequently on the airboat. Forces always come in pairs of this kind, which are formalized in the next section as Newton’s third law of motion. The negative answer for the acceleration in part (c) means that the airboat is slowing down.
