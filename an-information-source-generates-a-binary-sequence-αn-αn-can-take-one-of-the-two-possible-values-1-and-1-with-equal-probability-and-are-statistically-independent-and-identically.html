Guest Question.

An information source generates a binary sequence \(\left\{\alpha_{n}\right\} . \alpha_{n}\) can take one of the two possible values –1 and +1 with equal probability and are statistically independent and identically distributed. This sequence is precoded to obtain another sequence \(\left\{\beta_{n}\right\}, \text { as } \beta_{n}=\alpha_{n}+k \alpha_{n-3}\). The sequence \(\left\{\beta_{n}\right\}\) is used to modulate a pulse g(t) to generate the base band signal

\(X(t)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} \beta_{n} g(t-n T)\)

where

\(g(t)=\left\{\begin{array}{lc}1, & 0 \leq t \leq T \\0, & \text { otherwise }\end{array}\right.\)

If there is a null at \(f=\frac{1}{3 T}\) in the power spectral density of X(t), then k is _____.

Admin
Solution
\(\begin{aligned}S_{x}(f) &=\frac{|G(f)|^{2}}{T}\left[\sum_{m=-\infty}^{\infty} R_{b}(\tau) e^{j 2 \pi f \tau T}\right] \\R_{b}(\tau) &=E\left[\beta_{n} \beta_{n-\tau}\right] \\&=E\left[\left(\alpha_{n}+k \alpha_{n-3}\right)\left(\alpha_{n-\tau}+k_{\alpha n-\tau-3}\right)\right] \\&=\left(1+k^{2}\right) R(\tau)+K R(\tau+3)+K R(\tau-3) \\& R_{b}(\tau)=\left[\begin{array}{cc}1+k^{2} & \tau=0 \\k & \tau=\pm 3 \\0 & \text { otherwise }\end{array}\right]\end{aligned}\)

PSD, \(S_{b}(f)=1+ k ^{2}+2 K \cos (2 \pi f 3 T )\)

Null occurs at \(f=\frac{1}{3 T}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\Rightarrow f=\frac{1}{3 T}, S_{b}=1+K^{2}+2 K \cos 2 \pi\left(\frac{1}{3 T}\right) \times 3 T \\&\text { i.e., } \quad 1+k^{2}+2 k=0\end{aligned}\)

⇒ k = –1
Hence, the correct Answer is (–1)
