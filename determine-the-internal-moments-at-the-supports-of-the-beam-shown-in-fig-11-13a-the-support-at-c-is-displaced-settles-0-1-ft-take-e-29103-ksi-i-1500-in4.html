Guest Question.

Determine the internal moments at the supports of the beam shown in Fig. \(11-13 (\mathrm{a})\). The support at \(C\) is displaced (settles) \(0.1 \mathrm{ft}\). Take \(E=29\left(10^{3}\right) \mathrm{ksi}, I=1500 \mathrm{in}^{4}\).

Admin
Solution

Slope-Deflection Equations. Three spans must be considered in this problem. Equation 11-8 applies since the end supports \(A\) and \(D\) are fixed. Also, only span \(A B\) has FEMs.

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=2 E k\left(2 \theta_{N}+\theta_{F}-3 \psi\right)+(\text { FEM })_{N}\\ \text For Internal Span or End Span with Far End Fixed \end{array} \) (11-8)

\( \begin{array}{l} (\mathrm{FEM})_{A B}=-\frac{w L^{2}}{12}=-\frac{1}{12}(1.5)(24)^{2}=-72.0 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ (\mathrm{FEM})_{B A}=\frac{w L^{2}}{12}=\frac{1}{12}(1.5)(24)^{2}=72.0 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \end{array} \)

As shown in Fig. 11-13b, the displacement (or settlement) of the support \(C\) causes \(\psi_{B C}\) to be positive, since the cord for span \(B C\) rotates clockwise, and \(\psi_{C D}\) to be negative, since the cord for span \(C D\) rotates counterclockwise. Hence,

\( \psi_{B C}=\frac{0.1 \mathrm{ft}}{20 \mathrm{ft}}=0.005 \mathrm{rad} \quad \psi_{C D}=-\frac{0.1 \mathrm{ft}}{15 \mathrm{ft}}=-0.00667 \mathrm{rad} \)

Also, expressing the units for the stiffness in feet, we have

\( \begin{array}{l} k_{A B}=\frac{1500}{24(12)^{4}}=0.003014 \mathrm{ft}^{3} \quad k_{B C}=\frac{1500}{20(12)^{4}}=0.003617 \mathrm{ft}^{3} \\ k_{C D}=\frac{1500}{15(12)^{4}}=0.004823 \mathrm{ft}^{3} \end{array} \)

Noting that \(\theta_{A}=\theta_{D}=0\) since \(A\) and \(D\) are fixed supports, and applying the slope-deflection Eq. 11-8 twice to each span, we have

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=2 E k\left(2 \theta_{N}+\theta_{F}-3 \psi\right)+(\text { FEM })_{N}\\ \text For Internal Span or End Span with Far End Fixed \end{array} \) (11-8)

For span \(A B\) :

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{A B}=2\left[29\left(10^{3}\right)(12)^{2}\right](0.003014)\left[2(0)+\theta_{B}-3(0)\right]-72 \\ M_{A B}=25173.6 \theta_{B}-72 \\ \end{array}\) (1)

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{B A}=2\left[29\left(10^{3}\right)(12)^{2}\right](0.003014)\left[2 \theta_{B}+0-3(0)\right]+72 \\ M_{B A}=50347.2 \theta_{B}+72 \end{array}\) (2)

For \(\operatorname{span} B C\) :

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{B C}=2\left[29\left(10^{3}\right)(12)^{2}\right](0.003617)\left[2 \theta_{B}+\theta_{C}-3(0.005)\right]+0 \\ M_{B C}=60416.7 \theta_{B}+30208.3 \theta_{C}-453.1 \\ \end{array}\) (3)

\(\begin{array}{l}M_{C B}=2\left[29\left(10^{3}\right)(12)^{2}\right](0.003617)\left[2 \theta_{C}+\theta_{B}-3(0.005)\right]+0 \\ M_{C B}=60416.7 \theta_{C}+30208.3 \theta_{B}-453.1 \end{array}\) (4)

For span \(C D:\)

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{C D}=2\left[29\left(10^{3}\right)(12)^{2}\right](0.004823)\left[2 \theta_{C}+0-3(-0.00667)\right]+0 \\ M_{C D}=80555.6 \theta_{C}+0+805.6 \\ \end{array}\) (5)

\(\begin{array}{l}M_{D C}=2\left[29\left(10^{3}\right)(12)^{2}\right](0.004823)\left[2(0)+\theta_{C}-3(-0.00667)\right]+0 \\ M_{D C}=40277.8 \theta_{C}+805.6 \end{array}\) (6)

Equilibrium Equations. These six equations contain eight unknowns. Writing the moment equilibrium equations for the supports at \(B\) and \(C\), Fig. 10-13c, we have

\(\begin{array}{ll}\curvearrowleft +\sum M_{B}=0 ; & M_{B A}+M_{B C}=0 \\ \end{array} \) (7)

\(\begin{array}{ll}\curvearrowleft +\sum M_{C}=0 ; & M_{C B}+M_{C D}=0 \end{array} \) (8)

In order to solve, substitute Eqs. (2) and (3) into Eq. (7), and Eqs. (4) and (5) into Eq. (8). This yields

\( \begin{array}{r} \theta_{C}+3.667 \theta_{B}=0.01262 \\ -\theta_{C}-0.214 \theta_{B}=0.00250 \end{array} \)

Thus,

\( \theta_{B}=0.00438 \mathrm{rad} \quad \theta_{C}=-0.00344 \mathrm{rad} \)

The negative value for \(\theta_{C}\) indicates counterclockwise rotation of the tangent at \(C\), Fig. 11-13a.Substituting these values into Eqs. (1)-(6) yields

\( \begin{aligned} M_{A B} &=38.2 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ M_{B A} &=292 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ M_{B C} &=-292 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ M_{C B} &=-529 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ M_{C D} &=529 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ M_{D C} &=667 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \end{aligned} \)
