Guest Question.

A hollow cylinder with inner radius 30 mm and outer radius 50 mm is heated at the inner surface at a rate of 105 \(W/m^2\) and dissipated heat by convection from outer surface into a fluid at 80°C with heat transfer coefficient of 400 \(W/m^2.K\). There is no energy generation and thermal conductivity of the material is constant at 15 W/m.K. Calculate the temperatures of inside and outside surfaces of the cylinder.

Admin
Solution

Given : A hollow cylinder is heated at its inner
surface
\(r_1 = 30 mm, r_2 = 50 mm\)
\(q = 105 W/m^2, T_∞ = 80°C\)
\(h = 400 W/m^2.K, k = 15 W/m.K.\)

To find : Temperatures of inner and outer surfaces of the cylinder.
Analysis : The individual thermal resistance
\(R_1 = \frac{ln(r_2/r_1 )}{2πL k}= \frac{ln ( 50/30 )}{ 2πL × 15}=\frac{0.0340}{ 2πL}\)
\(R_2 = \frac{1}{2π r_2L h}= \frac{1}{ (2πL) × 0.05 × 400}=\frac{0.05}{ 2πL}\)
These resistances are in series, total thermal resistance
\(ΣR_{th} =R_1 + R_2 =\frac{0.0340}{ 2πL} +\frac{0.05}{ 2πL}=\frac{0.084}{ 2πL}\)
The heat flow rate through the network
\(Q = q (2πr_1L) =\frac{∆T}{ΣR_{th}}=\frac{(T_1 – T_∞) × 2πL}{0.084}\)

Equating 2nd and 4th terms
\(10^5 × (2πL) × 0.03 = \frac{(T_1 – 80)× 2πL}{0.084}\)

or \(T_1 – 80 = 252°C or T_1 = 252 + 80\)
= 332°C.
The temperature of inner surface is 332°C.
Further, heat convection rate
\(Q = q (2πr_1L) = h(2π r_2L) (T_2 – T_∞)\)
or \(10^5 × 0.03 = 400 × 0.05 × (T_2 – 80)\)
or \(T_2 = \frac{3000}{20} + 80 = 230°C. \)
The temperature of outer surface is 230°C.
