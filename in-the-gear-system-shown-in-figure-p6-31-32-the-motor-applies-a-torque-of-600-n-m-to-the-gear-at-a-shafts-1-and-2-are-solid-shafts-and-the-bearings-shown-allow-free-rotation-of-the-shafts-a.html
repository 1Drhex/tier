Guest Question.

In the gear system shown in Figure P6.31/32, the motor applies a torque of 600 N-m to the gear at A. Shafts (1) and (2) are solid shafts, and the bearings shown allow free rotation of the shafts.
(a) Determine the torque \(T_{E}\) provided by the gear system at gear E.
(b) If the allowable shear stress in each shaft must be limited to 70 MPa, determine the minimum permissible diameter for each shaft.

Admin
Solution

(a) Torque relationship: The torque on gear B is:

\(T_{B}=T_{A} \frac{72}{24}=3 T_{A}=3(600 N – m )=1,800 N – m\)

Shaft (1) has the same torque as gear B; therefore, \(T_{1}\) = 1,800 N-m and \(T_{C}\) = 1,800 N-m. The torque on gear D is found from:

\(T_{D}=T_{C} \frac{60}{30}=2 T_{C}=2(1,800 N – m )=3,600 N – m\)

Shaft (2) has the same torque as gear D; therefore, \(T_{2}\) = 3,600 N-m and

\(T_{E}=3,600 N – m\)

To summarize:

\(\begin{array}{|ll|}\hline T_{1}=1,800 N – m & T_{2}=3,600 N – m \\\hline\end{array}\)

(b) Determine minimum diameters: The elastic torsion formula can be rearranged and rewritten in terms of the unknown diameter d:

\(\tau=\frac{T c}{J} \quad \therefore \frac{J}{c}=\frac{\frac{\pi}{32} d^{4}}{d / 2}=\frac{\pi}{16} d^{3}=\frac{T}{\tau}\)

Using this expression, solve for the minimum acceptable diameter of each shaft:

\(\begin{aligned}d_{1}^{3} & \geq \frac{16}{\pi} \frac{T_{1}}{\tau_{\text {allow }}}=\frac{16(1,800 N – m )(1,000 mm / m )}{\pi\left(70 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=130,961.782 mm ^{3} \\& \therefore d_{1} \geq 50.783 mm =50.8 mm\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}d_{2}^{3} & \geq \frac{16}{\pi} \frac{T_{2}}{\tau_{\text {allow }}}=\frac{16(3,600 N – m )(1,000 mm / m )}{\pi\left(70 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=261,923.563 mm ^{3} \\& \therefore d_{2} \geq 63.982 mm =64.0 mm\end{aligned}\)
