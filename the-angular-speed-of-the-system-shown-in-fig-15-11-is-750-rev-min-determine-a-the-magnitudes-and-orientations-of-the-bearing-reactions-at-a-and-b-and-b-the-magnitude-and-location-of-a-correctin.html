Guest Question.

The angular speed of the system shown in Fig. 15.11 is 750 rev/min. Determine (a) the magnitudes and orientations of the bearing reactions at A and B, and (b) the magnitude and location of a correcting (or balancing) mass to be added at a radius of 0.25 m.

Admin
Solution

(a) The angular velocity of this system is

ω = (750 rev/min) (2π rad/rev)/(60 s/min) = 78.54 rad/s.

The inertia forces caused by the rotating masses are

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{1}=m_{1} r_{1} \omega^{2}=(12 kg )(0.2 m )(78.54 rad / s )^{2}=14.80 kN \\&F_{2}=m_{2} r_{2} \omega^{2}=(3 kg )(0.3 m )(78.54 rad / s )^{2}=5.55 kN\end{aligned}\)

 

and

\(F_{3}=m_{3} r_{3} \omega^{2}=(10 kg )(0.15 m )(78.54 rad / s )^{2}=9.25 kN\)

These forces can be written in vector form as

\(\begin{aligned}& F _{1}=14.80 kN \angle 0^{\circ}=14.80 \hat{ i } kN \\& F _{2}=5.55 kN \angle 135^{\circ}=-3.93 \hat{ i }+3.93 \hat{ j } kN \\& F _{3}=9.25 kN \angle-150^{\circ}=-8.01 \hat{ i }-4.63 \hat{ j } kN\end{aligned}\)

To find the bearing reaction at B we take moments about bearing A. This equation is written as

\(\begin{aligned}\sum M _{A}=&(-0.3 \hat{ k } m ) \times[(14.80 \hat{ i } kN )+(-3.93 \hat{ i }+3.93 \hat{ j } kN )\\&+(-8.01 \hat{ i }-4.63 \hat{ j } kN )]+(-0.5 \hat{ k } m ) \times F _{B}= 0\end{aligned}\)

Taking the cross products and rearranging gives

\((0.5 \hat{ k } m ) \times F _{B}=-0.21 \hat{ i }-0.86 \hat{ j } kN \cdot m\)

Solving this equation for FB gives

\(F _{B}=-1.72 \hat{ i }+0.42 \hat{ j } kN =1.77 kN \angle 166.28^{\circ}\)

Summing forces gives

\(F _{A}+ F _{1}+ F _{2}+ F _{3}+ F _{B}= 0\)

Therefore, the reaction at A is

\(\begin{aligned}F _{A}=&-(14.80 \hat{ i } kN )-(-3.93 \hat{ i }+3.93 \hat{ j } kN )-(-8.01 \hat{ i }-4.63 \hat{ j } kN ) \\&-(-1.72 \hat{ i }+0.42 \hat{ j } kN ) \end{aligned}\)

or

\(F _{A}=-1.14 \hat{ i }+0.28 \hat{ j } kN =1.17 \angle 166.20^{\circ} kN\)

(b) Let FC be the correcting force. Then, for zero bearing reactions

\(\sum F = F _{1}+ F _{2}+ F _{3}+ F _{C}= 0\)

Thus,

\(\begin{aligned}F _{C} &=-(14.80 \hat{ i } kN )-(-3.93 \hat{ i }+3.93 \hat{ j } kN )-(-8.00 \hat{ i }-4.63 \hat{ j } kN ) \\&=-2.86 \hat{ i }+0.70 \hat{ j } kN =2.94 kN \angle 166.25^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

Therefore, the correcting mass should be added at 166.25°.

The correcting mass at a radius of 0.25 m is

\(m_{C}=\frac{F_{C}}{r_{C} \omega^{2}}=\frac{2.94(10)^{3} N }{0.25 m (78.54 rad / s )^{2}}=1.91 kg\)
