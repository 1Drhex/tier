Guest Question.

Air (\(\gamma\) = 1.4‚ molecular weight = 28.8 kg/kmol) enters a 5 cm by 5 cm square duct at 300 K, 100 kPa and a velocity of 905 m/s. If the duct length is 2 m, find the flow properties at the exit. Take ƒ =0.02.

Admin
Solution

From the give data, we have \(M_{1}=u_{1} / \sqrt{\gamma RT_{1} } =2.6.\) From the gas tables, for \(M_{1}=2.6,\) we can get \(ƒL^{\ast }_{1} / D=0.4526\). Since the cross-section of the duct is non-circular, we use the hydraulic diameter, \(D_{h} =4 A/ P=5\) cm to get \(L^{\ast }_{1}=1.1315 \) m. The given duct length is greater than this length and so there will be a normal shock standing somewhere in the duct.

Since no information is given about the exit static pressure, we will assume
the exit state to be the sonic state. The location of the normal shock has to
be determined iteratively. Let the condition immediately upstream of the
shock be denoted with subscript x, and condition immediately downstream
with subscript y, and let the location of the shock from the inlet be denoted
by \(L_{s} \). With an assumed value for \(L_{s} \) during each iteration, the calculations proceed along each row from left to right as shown in the table. After 3 iterations, we can say that the normal shock is located at \(L_{s}\approx 0.25m \) from the inlet.

 

\(L-L_{s}\) (m) \(L^{\ast }_{y}\) (from Fanno table) (m) \(M_{y}\) (from Normal shock table) \(M_{x}\) (from Fanno table) \(L^{\ast }_{x}=L^{\ast }_{1} -L_{s}\)(m) \(L_{s}\) (m) Iteration No
1 0.166 0.8071 \(\approx\)1.26 0.1315 1 1
1.5 1.155 0.6078 \(\approx\)1.84 0.6315 0.5 2
1.75 1.77 0.5537 2.1686 0.8815 0.25 3

 

Flow properties just before the shock (from Fanno table) are

\(T_{x} =\left(\frac{T_{x} }{T^{\ast }_{1} } \right) \left(\frac{T^{\ast }_{1}}{T_{1} } \right) T_{1} =\frac{0.6235}{0.5102} \ast 300=366.62K \)

 

\(P_{x} =\left(\frac{P_{x} }{P^{\ast }_{1} } \right) \left(\frac{P^{\ast }_{1}}{P_{1} } \right) P_{1} =\frac{0.3673}{0.2747} \ast 100=133.71 kPa\)

and

\(P_{0,x}=\left(\frac{P_{0,x} }{P^{\ast }_{0,1} } \right) \left(\frac{P^{\ast }_{0,1}}{P_{0,1}} \right) \left(\frac{P_{0,1}}{P_{1} } \right) P_{1}\)

 

\(=\frac{1.919}{2.896} \ast 19.95\ast = 1322 kPa .\)

Flow properties just after the shock (from normal shock table) are

 

\(T_{y} =\frac{T_{y}}{T_{x}} T_{x}=1.813\ast 366.62= 664.68K\)

 

\(P_{y} =\frac{P_{y}}{P_{x}} P_{x}=5.226\ast 133.71= 699 kPa\)

 

\(P_{0,y} =\frac{P_{0,y}}{P_{0,x}} P_{0,x}=0.6511\ast 1322= 860.75 kPa\)

Flow properties at the exit (from Fanno table) are

 

\(T_{2} =T^{\ast }_{2}=\frac{T^{\ast }_{2}}{T_{y}} T_{y}=\frac{664.68}{1.1305} =588K,\)

 

\(P_{2} =P^{\ast }_{2}=\frac{P^{\ast }_{2}}{P_{y}} P_{y}=\frac{699}{1.9202} =364 kPa ,\)

 

\(P_{0,2} =P^{\ast }_{0,2}=\frac{P^{\ast }_{0,2}}{P_{0,y}} P_{y}=\frac{860.75}{1.249} =689 kPa ,\)

 

\(T_{0,2} =T_{0,1} =\frac{T_{0,1} }{T_{1}} T_{1}=2.352\ast 300= 705.6K.\)

Note the 65 percent loss of stagnation pressure at the duct exit.
