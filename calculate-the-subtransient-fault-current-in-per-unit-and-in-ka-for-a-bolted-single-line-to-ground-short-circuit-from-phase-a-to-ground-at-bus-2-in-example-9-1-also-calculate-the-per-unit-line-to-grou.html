Single line-to-ground short-circuit calculations using sequence networks

Calculate the subtransient fault current in per-unit and in kA for a bolted single line-to-ground short circuit from phase a to ground at bus 2 in . Also calculate the per-unit line-to-ground voltages at faulted bus 2.

Admin
Solution

The zero-, positive-, and negative-sequence networks in Figure 9.5 are connected in series at the fault terminals, as shown in Figure 9.8.

Since the short circuit is bolted, \(Z_{F} =0\). From (9.2.7), the sequence currents are:

\(I_{0} =I_{1}=I_{2}=\frac{V_{F} }{Z_{0}+Z_{1}+Z_{2}+3Z_{F} } \) (9.2.7)

\(I_{0} =I_{1}=I_{2}=\frac{1.05\angle 0°}{j\left(0.25+0.13893+0.14562\right) } =\frac{1.05}{j0.53455}=-j1.96427 \) per unit

From (9.2.8), the subtransient fault current is

\(I_{a}=I_{0}+I_{1}+I_{2}=3I_{1}=\frac{3V_{F} }{Z_{0} +Z_{1}+Z_{2}\left(3Z_{F} \right) } \) (9.2.8)

\( I^{\prime \prime }_{a} =3\left(-j1.96427\right) =-j5.8928\) per unit

The base current at bus 2 is \(100/\left(13.8\sqrt{3} \right)=4.1837\) kA. Therefore,

\(I^{\prime \prime }_{a} =\left(-j5.8928\right) \left(4.1837\right) =24.65\angle -90°\) kA

From (9.1.1), the sequence components of the voltages at the fault are

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} V_{0} \\ V_{1} \\ V_{2} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ V_{F} \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ] -\left [ \begin{matrix} Z_{0} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & Z_{1} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & Z_{2} \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} I_{0} \\ I_{1} \\ I_{2} \end{matrix} \right ] \) (9.1.1)

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} V_{0} \\ V_{1} \\ V_{2} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1.05\angle 0° \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ]-\left [ \begin{matrix} j0.25 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & j0.13893 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & j0.14562 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} -j1.96427 \\ -j1.96427 \\ -j1.96427 \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} -0.49107 \\ 0.77710 \\ -0.28604 \end{matrix} \right ] \) per unit

Transforming to the phase domain, the line-to-ground voltages at faulted bus 2 are

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} V_{ag} \\ V_{bg} \\ V_{cg} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & a^{2} & a \\ 1 & a & a^{2} \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} -0.49107 \\ 0.77710 \\ -0.28604 \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1.179\angle 231.3°\\ 1.179\angle 128.7° \end{matrix} \right ] \) per unit

Note that \(V_{ag} =0\), as specified by the fault conditions. Also \(I^{\prime \prime }_{b} =I^{\prime \prime }_{c}=0\).

Open PowerWorld Simulator case Example 9_3 to see this example. This case includes the positive-, negative-, and zero-sequence values for the system devices. To see these values, right-click on an object on the oneline (e.g., a generator), open the object’s Information dialog, and select the Fault tab. The process for simulating an unsymmetrical fault is almost identical to that for a balanced fault. That is, from the oneline, first right-click on the bus symbol corresponding to the fault location. This displays the local menu. Select “Fault..” to display the Fault Analysis dialog, and in the list on the left side of the dialog, set the Fault Defini-tions to Single Fault. Verify that the correct bus is selected, and then set the Fault Type field to Single Line-to-Ground. Finally, click on Calculate to determine the fault currents and voltages. The results are shown in the tables at the bottom of the dialog.Notice that with an unsymmetrical fault the phase magnitudes are no longer identical and the phase angles no longer differ by 1208. The fault values can be shown on the oneline by selecting Options in the left list, and changing the Oneline Display field value.
