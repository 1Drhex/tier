Guest Question.

Consider the circuit shown in Fig. 3.6a with a dc source in the load side. Assume ideal diodes, zero initial inductor current and \(\ V_{dc} <V_{s} \) . Find the expressions for \(\ i_{D1} , i_{D2} ,i_{o} and v_{o} for 0<t<2T \), where T is the period of \(\ v_{s} \) given by T=2π/ω. Assume \(\ v_{s} = V_{s} \sin ωt with V_{s} =100 V, ω= 377 rad/s., T=16.67 ms. \)

Admin
Solution

At t=0 the switch closes and \(\ v_{s} \) is switched into the circuit. However, when \(\ v_{s} \leq V_{dc} , D_{1} \) remains OFF and so does \(\ D_{2} \).

At \(\ t=t_{1}, v_{s} =V_{dc} \) , which force \(\ D_{1} \) to turn ON and \(\ D_{2} \) to turn OFF, resulting in the equivalent circuit of Mode 1 for \(\ t>t_{1} \) as shown in Fig. 3.6b. \(\ t_{1} \) can be determined by the following equation:

\(\ t_{1} = \frac{1}{\omega } \sin^{-1} \left(\frac{V_{dc}}{V_{s}} \right) = 0.67 ms \)

The differential equation for Mode 1 is given by:

\(\ \frac{di_{L}}{dt} + \frac{R}{L}i_{L} = \frac{v_{s} – V_{dc} }{L} \)

The transient response of\(\ i_{L} \left(t\right) \) is:

\(\ i_{L , tran} \left(t\right)= I_{1} e^{-\left(t – t_{1} \right)/ τ } \)

where τ=L/R= 3.33 ms, and the steady-state response is given by:

\(\ i_{L , ss} \left(t\right) = \frac{V_{s}}{ \mid Z\mid } \sin \left(\omega t +\theta \right) -\frac{V_{dc}}{R} = 20.75 \sin \left(377 t – 51.5^{\circ } \right) -8.33 A \)

 

\(\ where \mid Z\mid = \sqrt{ R^{2} + \left(\omega L\right) ^{2} } = 4.82 \Omega and \theta =\tan^{-1} \left(\omega L/ R \right) = 51.5^{\circ } . \)

The overall response is given by:

\(\ i_{L} \left(t\right) = I_{1} e^{-\left(t – t_{1} \right)/ τ } + \frac{V_{s}}{ \mid Z\mid } \sin \left(\omega t -\theta \right) +\frac{V_{dc}}{R} \) (3.19)

The constant I1 is obtained by setting \(\ i_{L}=0 at t= t_{1} \) in Eq. (3.19) which yields:

\(\ I_{1} = – \frac{V_{s}}{ \mid Z\mid } \sin \left(\omega t -\theta \right) -\frac{V_{dc}}{R} = 20.82 A \)

The general solution for \(\ i_{L}\left(t\right) \) is given by:

\(\ i_{L} \left(t\right) = 24.75 e^{-\left(t – t_{1} \right)/ \tau } +20.75 \sin\left(377t – 51.5^{\circ } \right) – 8.33 0 \leq t \lt T/2 \) (3.20)

where

\(\ i_{L} \left(T/2 \right) = 10.03 A \) (3.21)

At t=T/2 the source voltage becomes negative, forcing \(\ D_{2} \) to turn ON and \(\ D_{1} \) toturn OFF. The equivalent circuit for Mode 2 is shown in Fig. 3.6c. The inductor current at t=T/2 becomes the initial inductor current for the next cycle at t=T/2. This mode remains until the inductor current become zero again and the transient response of \(\ i_{L} \left(t \right) \) at Mode 2 is given by:

\(\ i_{L , tran} \left(t\right) = I_{2} e^{-\left(t – T/ 2 \right)/ τ } \)

and the steady-state response is given by:

\(\ i_{L , ss} \left(t\right) = -\frac{V_{dc}}{R} = -8.33 A \)

resulting in the following expression for \(\ i_{L} \left(t \right) \) :

\(\ i_{L } \left(t\right) = I_{2} e^{-\left(t – T/ 2 \right)/ τ } -8.33 A \)

The constant \(\ I_{2} \) is obtained by setting \(\ i_{L}=10.03 at t=T/2 \) which yields:

\(\ I_{2} = 10.03 + 8.33 = 18.36 A \)

and the overall response for Mode 2 can be given by:

\(\ i_{L } \left(t\right) = 18.36 e^{-\left(t – T/ 2 \right)/ τ } -8.33 A T/ 2 \leq t \lt t_{2} \)

where \(\ t_{2} \) is the time when \(\ i_{L } \left(t\right) \) decreases to zero. It can be found setting \(\ i_{L }=0 at t= t_{2} \) which yields:

\(\ t_{2} = -τ In \frac{8.33}{18.36} +T/ 2 = 10.97 ms \)

After that \(\ i_{L } \left(t\right) \) will remain zero until \(\ t=T + t_{1}\) . The sketch for \(\ i_{L } \left(t\right) \) for 0<t<2T is shown in Fig. 3.6d.
