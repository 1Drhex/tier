Risk Analysis for the SMP Investment
To illustrate the calculation of the expected value and the variance of present worth, recall the SMP investment: $500,000 initial investment; $92,500 annual savings; $50,000 salvage value; 10-year planning horizon;and 10 percent MARR. Suppose the annual savings and the salvage value are random variables, distributed as shown in Table 11.2.

Solution

Computing the expected value for the annual cash fl ow gives $92,500. Similarly, the expected value for the salvage value is $50,000. The variance for annual savings is
V(A) = E\((A)^{2}\) – [E\((A^{2}\)) ]
= \((92,500)^{2}\) – 8,637,649,998
= 81,400,001.7
and the variance for salvage value is
V(SA) = E(\(SV^{2}\)) – \([E(SV)]^{2}\)
= \((50,000)^{2}\) – 2,530,000,000
= 30,000,000
Table 11.3 provides the results of applying the expected values and variances for the annual savings and salvage value to obtain the statistical parameters for the SMP investment’s present worth. As noted, the expected present worth is $87,649.62, and the variance of present worth is 334,461,261.82. Notice, the expected cash flow in the tenth year equals the sum of the expected annual savings and the expected salvage value; likewise, the variance of the cash fl ow in the tenth year equals the sum of the annual savings variance and the salvage value variance.
