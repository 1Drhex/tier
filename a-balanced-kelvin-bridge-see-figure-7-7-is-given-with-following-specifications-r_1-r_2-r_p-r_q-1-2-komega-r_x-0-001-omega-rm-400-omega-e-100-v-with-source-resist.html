Guest Question.

A balanced Kelvin bridge (see Figure 7.7) is given with following specifications:

\(R_{1} = R_{2} = R_{p} = R_{q} = 1.2 k\Omega\) , \(R_{x} = 0.001 \Omega \) , \( R_{m} = 400 \Omega \) , E = 100 V with source resistance.

\(R_{b} = 10 \Omega \) .

Determine:

(a) resistance \(R_{3}\)

(b) current fl owing through \(R_{3}\)

Admin
Solution

The unknown resistance \(R_{x}\) can be found using the following relation:

\(R_{x}=\frac{R_{2}R_{3}}{R_{1}}\)

Substituting the given values, we obtain:

\(0.001=\frac{1.2\times 10^{3}R_{3}}{1.2\times 10^{3}}\)

On solving, this gives:

\(R_{3} = 0.001 \Omega \)

(b) For calculating the current through \(R_{3}\), we must know the value of battery current. Therefore, let us first determine \(I_{b}\) as:

\(I_{b}=\frac{E}{R_{b}+R_{3}+R_{x}}\) \(I_{b}=\frac{100}{10+0.001+0.001}=9.99 A\)

Now, current I can be found as:

\(I=\frac{I_{b}(R_{1}+R_{2})}{(R_{1}+R_{2}+(R_{3}+R_{x})}\)

Substituting the required values, we get:

\(I=\frac{9.9 (1.2 \times 10^{3}+1.2\times 10^{3})}{(1.2\times 10^{3}+1.2\times 10^{3})+(0.001+0.001)}=9.9 A\)
