Guest Question.

A four-wheel trolley car of total mass 2500 kg running on rails of 1.6 m gauge, negotiates a curve of 40 m radius at 60 km/h. The track is banked at 10째. The wheels have an external diameter of 0.8 m and each pair with axle has a mass of 250 kg. The radius of gyration for each pair is 0.4 m. The height of centre of gravity of the car above the wheel base is 0.9 m. Determine the pressure on each rail, allowing for centrifugal and gyroscopic couple actions.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Here } M=2500 kg , a=1.6 m , R=40 m , v=\frac{60 \times 1000}{3600}=16.67 m / s , \theta=10^{\circ}, r_{w}=0.4 m \),

\( M_{w}=250 kg , K_{w}=0.4 m , h=0.9 m \).

\( \text { Let } R_{A} \text { and } R_{B} \text { be } \) the reactions at A and B, respectively. The various forces acting on the trolley car are shown in Fig.13.14.

Resolving the forces perpendicular to the track, we have

\( R_{A}+R_{B}=M g \cos \theta+\frac{M v^{2} \sin \theta}{R} \).

\( =2500 \times 9.81 \times \cos 10^{\circ}+\frac{2500 \times(16.67)^{2} \times \sin 10^{\circ}}{40} \).

\( =24152.4+3015.93 \).

\( =27168.33 N \).

Now taking moments about B, we have

\( R_{A} \times a=\left(M g \cos \theta+\frac{M v^{2} \sin \theta}{R}\right) \cdot\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)+\left(M g \cos \theta-\frac{M v^{2} \cos \theta}{R}\right) \cdot h \).

\( R_{A}=\left[2500 \times 9.81 \times \cos 10^{\circ}+\frac{2500 \times(16.67)^{2} \times \sin 10^{\circ}}{40}\right] \times 0.5 \)

\( +\left[2500 \times 9.81 \times \sin 10^{\circ}-\frac{2500 \times(16.67)^{2} \times \cos 10^{\circ}}{40}\right] \cdot(0.9/1.6) \).

\( =13584.17-7225.58=6358.59 N \).

\( R_{B}=20809.74 N \).

\( \omega_{w}=\frac{v}{r_{w}}=\frac{16.67}{0.4}=41.675 rad / s \).

\( \omega_{p}=\frac{v}{R}=\frac{16.67}{40}=0.41675 rad / s \).

\( C=I \cdot \omega \cos \theta \cdot \omega_{p} \).

\( =M_{w} K_{w}^{2} \cdot \omega_{w} \cos \theta \cdot \omega_{p} \).

\( =250 \times(0.4)^{2} \times 41.675 \times \cos 10^{\circ} \times 0.41675 \).

\( =684.17 Nm \).

The force at each pair of wheels on each rail due to the gyroscopic couple,

\( P=\frac{C}{a} \).

\( =\frac{684.17}{1.6}=427.6 N \).

Due to this force the car would tend to overturn about the outer wheels. Total pressure on the inner rail,

\( P_{i}=R_{A}-P \).

\( =6358.59-427.6 \).

= 5930.99 N.

Pressure on the outer rail,

\( P_{o}=R_{B}+P \).

= 20809.74+427.6.

= 21237.34 N.
