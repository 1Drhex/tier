Guest Question.

A 1.05 GHz generator circuit with series impedance \(Z_g = 10 Ω \) and voltage source given by \(υ_g(t) = 10 sin(ωt + 30^°) (V) \) is connected to a load \(Z_L = (100 + j50) Ω \) through a 50 Ω, 67 cm long lossless transmission line. The phase velocity of the line is 0.7c, where c is the velocity of light in a vacuum. Find υ(z, t) and i(z, t) on the line.

Admin
Solution

From the relationship \(u_p = λf ,\) we find the
wavelength

\(λ =\frac{u_p}{f} = \frac{0.7 × 3 × 10^8}{1.05 × 10^9} = 0.2 m,\)

and

\(βl =\frac{2π}{λ}l = \frac{2π}{0.2}× 0.67 = 6.7π = 0.7π = 126^°,\)

where we have subtracted multiples of 2π. The voltage
reflection coefficient at the load is

\(\Gamma =\frac{Z_L − Z_0}{Z_L + Z_0} = \frac{(100 + j50) − 50}{(100 + j50) + 50} = 0.45e^{j26.6^{°}},\)

With reference to Fig. 2-18, the input impedance of the line,
given by Eq. (2.76), is

\(Z_{in} =Z_0\left[\frac{1+\Gamma _{l} }{1-\Gamma _{l}} \right] \) \( =Z_0\left[\frac{1+\Gamma e^{-j2\beta l} }{1-\Gamma e^{-j2\beta l}} \right] \) \(=50\left(\frac{1+0.45e^{j26.6^{°} } e^{-j252^{\circ } }}{1-0.45e^{j26.6^{\circ } } e^{-j252^{\circ } }} \right) =(21.9 + j17.4) \Omega . \)

Rewriting the expression for the generator voltage with the
cosine reference, we have

\(υ_g(t) = 10 sin(ωt + 30^°) = 10 cos(90^° − ωt − 30^°) = 10 cos(ωt − 60^°) = \Re e[10e^{−j60^{°}}e^{jωt}] = \Re e[\tilde{V} _ge^{jωt}] (V).\)

Hence, the phasor voltage \(\tilde{V} _g\) is given by

\(\tilde{V} _g = 10 e^{−j60^{°}} = 10∠−60^° (V).\)

Application of Eq. (2.82) gives

\(V^{+ }_{0} =\left(\frac{\tilde{V} _{g}Z_{in} }{Z_g+Z_{in}} \right)\left(\frac{1}{e^{j\beta l}+\Gamma e^{-j\beta l} } \right) \) \(=\left[\frac{10e^{−j60^{°}}(21.9+j17.4)}{10+21.9+j17.4} \right] (e^{j126^{\circ } }+0.45e^{j26.6^{°}}e^{-j126^{\circ } } )^{-1} =10.2e^{j159^{°}} (V) \)

Using Eq. (2.63a)

with z = –d, the phasor voltage on the line is

\(\tilde{V} (d) = V^{+ }_{0} (e^{−jβz} + \Gamma e^{jβz}) \)

\(= 10.2e^{j159^{°}}(e^{jβd} + 0.45e^{j26.6^{°}}e^{−jβd}),\)

and the corresponding instantaneous voltage υ(d, t) is

\(υ(d, t) = \Re e[\tilde{V} (d) e^{jωt}] = 10.2 cos(ωt + βd + 159^°)+ 4.55 cos(ωt − βd + 185.6^°) (V). \)

Similarly, Eq. (2.63b) \(\tilde{I} (z) =\frac{V^{+ }_{0}}{Z_0} (e^{−jβz} -\Gamma e^{jβz})\)

leads to

\(\tilde{I}(d) = 0.20e^{j159^{°}}(e^{jβd} − 0.45e^{j26.6^{°}}e^{−jβd}) \) \(i(d, t) = 0.20 cos(ωt + βd + 159^°) + 0.091 cos(ωt − βd + 185.6^°) (A). \)
