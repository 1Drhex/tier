Guest Question.

Peak Heat Flux in Nucleate Boiling

Water in a tank is to be boiled at sea level by a 1-cm-diameter nickel plated steel heating element equipped with electrical resistance wires inside, as shown in Figure 10–16. Determine the maximum heat flux that can be attained in the nucleate boiling regime and the surface temperature of the heater surface in that case.

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION Water is boiled at 1 atm pressure on a nickel plated steel surface. The maximum (critical) heat flux and the surface temperature are to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Steady operating conditions exist. 2 Heat losses from the boiler are negligible.

Properties The properties of water at the saturation temperature of \( 100^{\circ}C \) are \( \sigma = 0.0589 N/m \) (Table 10–1) and, from Table A-9,

\( \rho_{l} = 957.9 kg/m^{3} h_{fg} = 2257.0 \times 10^{3} J/kg \)

 

\( \rho_{v} = 0.6 kg/m^{3} \mu_{l} = 0.282 \times 10^{-3} kg \cdot m/s \)

 

\( Pr_{l} = 1.75 C_{pl} = 4217 J/kg \cdot ^{\circ}C \)

 

Also, \( C_{sf} = 0.0060 \) and n = 1.0 for the boiling of water on a nickel plated surface (Table 10–3). Note that we expressed the properties in units specified under Eqs. \( \dot{q}_{nucleate} = \mu _{l} h_{fg} \left [ \frac{g \left ( \rho_{l} – \rho_{v}\right )}{\sigma } \right ]^{1/2} \left [ \frac{C_{pl} \left ( T_{s} – T_{sat} \right )}{C_{sf}h_{fg} Pr_{l}^{n}} \right ]^{3} \) and \( \dot{q}_{max} = C_{cr} h_{fg} \left [ \sigma g \rho_{v}^{2} \left ( \rho _{l} – \rho _{v} \right )\right ]^{1/4} \) in connection with their definitions in order to avoid unit manipulations.

Analysis The heating element in this case can be considered to be a short cylinder whose characteristic dimension is its radius. That is, L = r = 0.005 m. The dimensionless parameter \( L^{\ast } \) and the constant \( C_{cr} \) are determined from Table 10–4 to be

\( L^{\ast } = L \left ( \frac{g \left ( \rho _{l} – \rho _{v} \right )}{\sigma } \right )^{1/2} \)

 

\( = \left ( 0.005 \right ) \left ( \frac{\left ( 9.81 \right ) \left ( 957.8 – 0.6) \right )}{0.0589 } \right )^{1/2} = 2.00 > 1.2 \)

 

which corresponds to \( C_{cr} = 0.12.\)
Then the maximum or critical heat flux is determined from Eq. \( \dot{q}_{max} = C_{cr} h_{fg} \left [ \sigma g \rho_{v}^{2} \left ( \rho _{l} – \rho _{v} \right )\right ]^{1/4} \) to be

 

\( \dot{q}_{max} = C_{cr} h_{fg} \left [ \sigma g \rho_{v}^{2} \left ( \rho _{l} – \rho _{v} \right )\right ]^{1/4} \)

 

\( = 0.12 \left ( 2257 \times 10^{3} \right )\left [ 0.0589 \times 9.8 \times \left ( 0.6 \right )^{2} \left ( 957.9 – 0.6 \right ) \right ]^{1/4} \)

 

\( = 1.02 \times 10^{6} W/m^{2} \)

 

The Rohsenow relation, which gives the nucleate boiling heat flux for a specified surface temperature, can also be used to determine the surface temperature when the heat flux is given. Substituting the maximum heat flux into Eq. \( \dot{q}_{nucleate} = \mu _{l} h_{fg} \left [ \frac{g \left ( \rho_{l} – \rho_{v}\right )}{\sigma } \right ]^{1/2} \left [ \frac{C_{pl} \left ( T_{s} – T_{sat} \right )}{C_{sf}h_{fg} Pr_{l}^{n}} \right ]^{3} \) together with other properties gives

\( \dot{q}_{nucleate} = \mu _{l} h_{fg} \left [ \frac{g \left ( \rho_{l} – \rho_{v}\right )}{\sigma } \right ]^{1/2} \left [ \frac{C_{pl} \left ( T_{s} – T_{sat} \right )}{C_{sf} h_{fg} Pr_{l}^{n}} \right ]^{3} \)

 

\( 1,017,200 = \left ( 0.282 \times 10^{-3} \right )\left ( 2257 \times 10^{3} \right ) \left [ \frac{9.81 \times \left ( 957.9 – 0.6 \right )}{0.0589} \right ]^{1/2}\)

 

\( \left [ \frac{4217 \left ( T_{s} – 100 \right )}{0.0130 \left ( 2257 \times 10^{3} \right )1.75} \right ]^{3} T_{s} = 119^{\circ}C \)

 

Discussion Note that heat fluxes on the order of \( 1 MW/m^{2} \) can be obtained in nucleate boiling with a temperature difference of less than \( 20^{\circ}C \).
