Guest Question.

An ideal gas undergoes four reversible processes known as the Brayton engine cycle (Fig. 7.19):
1 → 2 adiabatic compression,
2 → 3 isobaric expansion,
3 → 4 adiabatic decompression,
4 → 1 isobaric contraction.
The pressures \(p_1\) and \(p_2\) and the volumes \(V_1\) and \(V_3\) are given.
1. Determine \(W_{34}\), the work performed during the adiabatic decompression 3 → 4.
2. Determine \(Q_{23}\), the heat provided during the isobaric expansion 2 → 3.
3. Determine \(ΔS_{41}\), the entropy variation during the isobaric contraction 4 → 1.
4. Draw the (T, S) diagram of the cycle.

Admin
Solution

1. According to expression (7.14) for the work performed during an adiabatic process,

\(W_{if}=\Delta U_{if}=c N R\int_{T_i}^{T_f}{dT} =C N R (T_f -T_i)\).

\(W_{34}=\Delta U_{34}=c N R\int_{T_3}^{T_4}{dT} =C N R (T_4 -T_3)\).

Taking into account the ideal gas equation of state (5.47),

p V = NR T

\(p_3V_3 = NR T_3\) and \(p_4V_4 = NR T_4\).

the properties of isobaric processes,

\(p_3 = p_2\) and \(p_4 = p_1\)

and the property (5.90) of adiabatic processes,

\(p V^γ = const \).

\(p_3 V^γ_3 = p_4 V^γ_4\). ⇒ \(V_4 = \Bigl(\frac{p_3}{p_4} \Bigr)^{1/\gamma }\) \(V_3 = \Bigl(\frac{p_2}{p_1} \Bigr)^{1/\gamma } V_3\).

the work \(W_{34}\)can be recast as,

\(W_{34} = c (p_4 V_4 − p_3 V_3) = c (p_1 V_4 − p_2 V_3) = c V_3\biggl(p_1 \Bigl(\frac{p_2}{p_1} \Bigr)^{1/\gamma }-p_2\biggr)\).

2. According to expression (7.22), the heat provided during an isobaric process is,

\(Q_{if}=\Delta H_{if} = \int_{H_i}^{H_f}{d H} =(c+1)N R \int_{T_i}^{T_f}{d T} =(c+1)N R (T_f – T_i)\).

\(Q_{23}=\Delta H_{23} = (c+1)N R \int_{T_2}^{T_3}{d T} =(c+1)N R (T_3 – T_2)\).

Taking into account the ideal gas equation of state (5.47),

p V = NR T

\(p_2 V_2 = N R T_2 and p_3 V_3 = N R T_3\).

the property of an isobaric process,

\(p_3 = p_2\).

and the property (5.90) of adiabatic processes,

\(p V^γ = const \).

\(p_1 V^γ_1= p_2 V^γ_2\). ⇒ \(V_2 = \Bigl(\frac{p_1}{p_2} \Bigr)^{1/\gamma } V_1\).

the heat \(Q_{23}\) can be recast as,

\(Q_{23} = (c + 1) (p_3 V_3 − p_2 V_2) = (c + 1) p_2 (V_3 − V_2) = (c+1)p_2\biggl(V_3 – \Bigl(\frac{p_1}{p_2} \Bigr)^{1/\gamma } V_1\biggr)\).

3. According to definition (7.23) of the entropy variation during an isobaric process, the ideal gas equation of state (5.47) and the property of adiabatic processes stated above, we have,

\(\Delta S_{if} = \int_{S_i}^{S_f}{dS} =\int_{i}^{f}{\frac{dH}{T} } =(c+1)N R\int_{T_i}^{T_f}{\frac{dT}{T} } =(c+1) N R \ln \Bigl(\frac{T_f}{T_i} \Bigr)\).

p V = NR T

\(\Delta S_{41} = \int_{4}^{1}{\frac{dH}{T} } =(c+1)N R\int_{T_4}^{T_1}{\frac{dT}{T} } =(c+1) N R \ln \Bigl(\frac{T_1}{T_4} \Bigr) = (c+1)N R \ln \Bigl(\frac{V_1}{V_4} \Bigr) =(c+1)N R \ln \biggl(\Bigl(\frac{p_1}{p_2} \Bigr)^{1/\gamma }\frac{V_1}{V_3} \biggr)\).

4. According to equation (7.12), the adiabatic processes are vertical lines on a (T, S) diagram. Solving equation (7.23) for the temperature shows that isobaric processes are exponentials on a (T, S) diagram (Fig. 7.20).

\(\Delta S_{if} = \int_{S_i}^{S_f}{dS} =0\).

\(\Delta S_{if} = \int_{S_i}^{S_f}{dS} =\int_{i}^{f}{\frac{dH}{T} } =(c+1)N R\int_{T_i}^{T_f}{\frac{dT}{T} } =(c+1) N R \ln \Bigl(\frac{T_f}{T_i} \Bigr)\).
