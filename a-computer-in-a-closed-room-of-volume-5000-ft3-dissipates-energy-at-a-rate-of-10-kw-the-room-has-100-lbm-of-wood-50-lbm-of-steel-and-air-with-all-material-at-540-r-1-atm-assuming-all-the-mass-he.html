Guest Question.

A computer in a closed room of volume 5000 \(ft ^{3}\) dissipates energy at a rate of 10 kW. The room has 100 lbm of wood, 50 lbm of steel and air, with all material at 540 R, 1 atm. Assuming all the mass heats up uniformly how long time will it take to increase the temperature 20 F?

Admin
Solution

C.V. Air, wood and steel. \(m _{2}= m _{1} ; \text { no work }\)

Energy Eq.3.5: \(U _{2}- U _{1}={ }_{1} Q _{2}=\dot{ Q } \Delta t\)

The total volume is nearly all air, but we can find volume of the solids.

\(V _{\text {wood }}= m / \rho =100 / 44.9=2.23 ft ^{3} ; V _{\text {steel }}=50 / 488=0.102 ft ^{3}\)

 

\(V _{ air }=5000-2.23-0.102=4997.7 ft ^{3}\)

 

\(m _{\text {air }}= PV / RT =14.7 \times 4997.7 \times 144 /(53.34 \times 540)=367.3 lbm\)

 

We do not have a u table for steel or wood so use specific heat.

 

\(\Delta U =\left[ m _{\text {air }} C _{ v }+ m _{\text {wood }} C _{ v }+ m _{\text {steel }} C _{ v }\right] \Delta T\)

 

\(=(367.3 \times 0.171+100 \times 0.3+50 \times 0.11) Btu / F \times 20 F\)

 

\(=1256.2+600+110=1966 Btu =\dot{ Q } \times \Delta t =(10 / 1.055) \times \Delta t\)

 

\(\Rightarrow \quad \Delta t =[1966 / 10] 1.055=207 sec =3.5 \text { minutes }\)
