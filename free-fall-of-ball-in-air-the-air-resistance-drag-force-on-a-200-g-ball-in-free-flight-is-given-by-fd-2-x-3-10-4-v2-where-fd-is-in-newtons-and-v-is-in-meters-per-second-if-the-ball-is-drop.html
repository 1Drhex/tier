FREE FALL OF BALL IN AIR

The air resistance (drag force) on a 200 g ball in free flight is given by \(F_D = 2 × 3 10^{-4} V^2\), where \(F_D\) is in newtons and \(V\) is in meters per second. If the ball is dropped from rest 500 m above the ground, determine the speed at which it hits the ground. What percentage of the terminal speed is the result? (The terminal speed is the steady speed a falling body eventually attains.)

Admin
Solution

Given: Ball, \(m=0.2 \mathrm{~kg}\), released from rest at \(y_{0}=500 \mathrm{~m}\).
Air resistance, \(F_{D}=k V^{2}\), where \(k=2 \times 10^{-4} \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2} / \mathrm{m}^{2}\).
Units: \(F_{D}(\mathrm{~N}), V(\mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s})\).
Find: (a) Speed at which the ball hits the ground.
(b) Ratio of speed to terminal speed.
Solution:
Governing equation: \(\Sigma \vec{F}=m \vec{a}\)
Assumption: Neglect buoyancy force.
The motion of the ball is governed by the equation
\(\Sigma F_{y}=m a_{y}=m \frac{d V}{d t}\)

Since \(V=V(y)\), we write \(\Sigma F_{y}=m \frac{d V}{d y} \frac{d y}{d t}=m V \frac{d V}{d y}\) Then,
\(\Sigma F_{y}=F_{D}-m g=k V^{2}-m g=m V \frac{d V}{d y}\)
Separating variables and integrating,
\(\begin{gathered}\int_{y_{0}}^{y} d y=\int_{0}^{V} \frac{m V d V}{k V^{2}-m g} \\y-y_{0}=\left[\frac{m}{2 k} \ln \left(k V^{2}-m g\right)\right]_{0}^{V}=\frac{m}{2 k} \ln \frac{k V^{2}-m g}{-m g}\end{gathered}\)
Taking antilogarithms, we obtain
\(k V^{2}-m g=-m g e^{\left[(2 k / m)\left(y-y_{0}\right)\right]}\)
Solving for \(V\) gives
\(V=\left\{\frac{m g}{k}\left(1-e^{\left[(2 k / m)\left(y-y_{0}\right)\right]}\right)\right\}^{1 / 2}\)
Substituting numerical values with \(y=0\) yields

\(\begin{aligned}&V=\left\{0.2 \mathrm{~kg} \times 9.81 \frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{m}^{2}}{2\times 10^{-4} \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}{\mathrm{~kg} \cdot\mathrm{m}}\left(1-e^{\left[2 \times 2 \times 10^{-4} / 0.2(-500)\right]}\right)\right\} \\&V=78.7 \mathrm{~m} /\mathrm{s} \xleftarrow[]{V} \end{aligned}\)

At terminal speed, \(a_{y}=0\) and \(\Sigma F_{y}=0=k V_{t}^{2}-m g\).
\(\text { Then, } \begin{aligned}V_{t} &=\left[\frac{m g}{k}\right]^{1 / 2}=\left[0.2 \mathrm{~kg} \times 9.81\frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{m}^{2}}{2 \times 10^{-4} \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}{\mathrm{~kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}}\right]^{1 / 2} \\&=99.0\mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\end{aligned}\)
The ratio of actual speed to terminal speed is
\(\frac{V}{V_{t}}=\frac{78.7}{99.0}=0.795, \text { or } 79.5 \% \xleftarrow[]{\frac{V}{V_{t}}}\)
