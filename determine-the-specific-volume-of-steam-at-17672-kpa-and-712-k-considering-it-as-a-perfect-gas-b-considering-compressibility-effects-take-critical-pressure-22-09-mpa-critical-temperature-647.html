Guest Question.

Determine the specific volume of steam at 17672 kPa and 712 K considering it as (a) perfect gas, (b) considering compressibility effects. Take critical pressure = 22.09 MPa, critical temperature =647.3 K, \(\textbf{R}_{\textbf{steam}}\) = 0.4615 kJ/kg·K.

Admin
Solution

(a) Considering steam as perfect gas,

\(\begin{aligned} \text{Sp. volume}&= \frac{R_{steam}·T}{p}\\&= \frac{0.4615 × 712}{17672}\\\text{Specific volume}&= \textbf{0.0186 }\textbf{m}^{\textbf{3}}\textbf{/kg}\\\end{aligned}\)

(b) Considering compressibility effects, the specific volume can be given by product of compressibility factor ‘Z’ and “specific volume when perfect gas”.

\(\begin{aligned}\text{Reduced pressure}&= \frac{p}{\text{Critical pressure}}\\&= \frac{17672}{22.09 × 10^{3}}\\\text{Reduced pressure}&= 0.8\\\text{Reduced temperature}&= \frac{T}{\text{Critical temperature}}\\&= \frac{712}{647.3}\\\text{Reduced temperature}&= 1.1\end{aligned}\)

From generalized compressibility chart compressibility factor ‘Z’ can be seen for reduced pressure and reduced temperatures of 0.8 and 1.1. We get,

\(\begin{aligned}Z&= 0.785\\\text{Actual specific volume}&= 0.785 × 0.0186\\&= \textbf{0.0146 }\textbf{m}^{\textbf{3}}\textbf{/kg.}\end{aligned}\)
