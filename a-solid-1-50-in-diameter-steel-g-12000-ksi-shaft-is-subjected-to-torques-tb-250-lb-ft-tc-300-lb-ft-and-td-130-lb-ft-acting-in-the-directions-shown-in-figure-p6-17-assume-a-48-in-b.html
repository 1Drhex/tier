Guest Question.

A solid 1.50-in.-diameter steel [G = 12,000 ksi] shaft is subjected to torques \(T_{B}\) = 250 lb-ft, \(T_{C}\) = 300 lb-ft, and \(T_{D}\) = 130 lb-ft, acting in the directions shown in Figure P6.17. Assume a = 48 in., b = 72 in., and c = 36 in.
(a) Prepare a diagram that shows the internal torque and the maximum shear stress in segments (1), (2), and (3) of the shaft. Use the sign convention presented in Section 6.6.
(b) Determine the rotation angle of pulley C with respect to the support at A.
(c) Determine the rotation angle of pulley D with respect to the support at A.

Admin
Solution

Section properties: The polar moment of inertia for the solid 1.50-in.-diameter steel shaft segments will be needed for this calculation.

\(J_1=\frac{\pi}{32}d_1^4=\frac{\pi}{32}(1.50in.)^4=0.497010in.^4=J_2=J_3\)

(a) Equilibrium

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{x}=&-250 lb – ft +300 lb – ft -130 lb – ft -T_{1}=0 \\& \therefore T_{1}=-80 lb – ft\end{aligned}\)

\(\begin{gathered}\Sigma M_{x}=300 lb – ft -130 lb – ft -T_{2}=0 \\\therefore T_{2}=170 lb – ft\end{gathered}\)

\(\begin{gathered}\Sigma M_{x}=-130 lb – ft -T_{3}=0 \\\therefore T_{3}=-130 lb – ft\end{gathered}\)

Shear stress:

\(\begin{aligned}&\tau_{1}=\frac{T_{1} c_{1}}{J_{1}}=\frac{(-80 lb – ft )(1.50 in . / 2)(12 in. / ft )}{0.497010 in .^{4}}=-1,448.7 psi =-1,449 psi \\&\tau_{2}=\frac{T_{2} c_{2}}{J_{2}}=\frac{(170 lb – ft )(1.50 in. / 2)(12 in. / ft )}{0.497010 in .^{4}}=3,078.4 psi =3,080 psi \\&\tau_{3}=\frac{T_{3} c_{3}}{J_{3}}=\frac{(-130 lb – ft )(1.50 in . / 2)(12 in. / ft )}{0.497010 in. ^{4}}=-2,354.1 psi =-2,350 psi\end{aligned}\)

The maximum shear stress in the entire shaft is \(\tau_{\max }=3,080 psi\).

 

(b) Rotation angle of C with respect to A:
The angles of twist in the three shaft segments are:

\(\begin{aligned}&\phi_{1}=\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}=\frac{(-80 lb – ft )(48 in .)(12 in. / ft )}{\left(0.497010 in .{ }^{4}\right)(12,000,000 psi )}=-0.007726 rad \\&\phi_{2}=\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}=\frac{(170 lb – ft )(72 in. )(12 in . / ft )}{\left(0.497010 in .^{4}\right)(12,000,000 psi )}=0.024627 rad \\&\phi_{3}=\frac{T_{3} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}=\frac{(-130 lb – ft )(36 in .)(12 in. / ft )}{\left(0.497010 in .^{4}\right)(12,000,000 psi )}=-0.009416 rad\end{aligned}\)

The rotation angle of C with respect to A is found from the sum of the angles of twist in segments (1) and (2):

\(\phi_{C}=\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}=-0.007726 rad +0.024627 rad =0.016901 rad =0.01690 rad\)

 

(c) Rotation angle of D with respect to A:
The rotation angle of D with respect to A is found from the sum of the angles of twist in segments (1),(2), and (3):

\(\begin{aligned}\phi_{D} &=\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}+\phi_{3} \\&=-0.007726 rad +0.024627 rad +(-0.009416 rad ) \\&=0.007485 rad =0.00749 rad\end{aligned}\)
