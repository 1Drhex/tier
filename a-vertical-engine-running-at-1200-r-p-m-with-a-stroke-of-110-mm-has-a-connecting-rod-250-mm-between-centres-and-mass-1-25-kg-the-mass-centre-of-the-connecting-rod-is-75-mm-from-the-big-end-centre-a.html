Guest Question.

A vertical engine running at 1200 r.p.m. with a stroke of 110 mm, has a connecting rod 250 mm between centres and mass 1.25 kg. The mass centre of the connecting rod is 75 mm from the big end centre and when suspended as a pendulum from the gudgeon pin axis makes 21 complete oscillations in 20 seconds.
1. Calculate the radius of gyration of the connecting rod about an axis through its mass centre.
2. When the crank is at\(40^{\circ}\) from the top dead centre and the piston is moving downwards, find analytically, the acceleration of the piston and the angular acceleration of the connecting rod. Hence find the inertia torque exerted on the crankshaft. To make the two-mass system to be dynamically equivalent to the connecting rod, necessary correction torque has to be applied and since the engine is vertical, gravity effects are to be considered.

Admin
Solution

Given : N = 1200 r.p.m. or \(\omega=2 \pi \times 1200 / 60=125.7 rad / s\) ; L = 110 mm or r = L/2 = 55 mm = 0.055 m ; l = PC = 250 mm = 0.25 m ; \(m_{ C }=1.25 kg ; C G=75 mm =0.075 m ; \theta=40^{\circ}\)

The configuration diagram of the engine is shown in Fig. 15.26.

 

1. Radius of gyration of the connecting rod about an axis through its mass centre

Let \(k_{ G }\) = Radius of gyration of the connecting rod about an axis through its mass centre,

\(l_{1}\) = Distance of the centre of gravity from the point of suspension = PG

= 250 â€“ 75 = 175 mm = 0.175 m

Since the connecting rod makes 21 complete oscillations in 20 seconds, therefore frequency of oscillation,

\(n=\frac{21}{20}=1.05 H _{ Z }\)

We know that for a compound pendulum, frequency of oscillation,

\(n=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \sqrt{\frac{g \cdot l_{1}}{\left(k_{ G }\right)^{2}+\left(l_{1}\right)^{2}}} \text { or } n^{2}=\frac{1}{4 \pi^{2}} \times \frac{g \cdot l_{1}}{\left(k_{ G }\right)^{2}+\left(l_{1}\right)^{2}}\) …(Squaring both sides)

 

and \(\left(k_{ G }\right)^{2}=\frac{g l_{1}}{4 \pi^{2} n^{2}}-\left(l_{1}\right)^{2}=\frac{9.81 \times 0.175}{4 \pi^{2} \times(1.05)^{2}}-(0.175)^{2}=0.0088 m ^{2}\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(k_{ G }=0.094 m =94 mm\)

 

2. Acceleration of the piston

We know that acceleration of the piston,

\(a_{ P }=\omega^{2} \cdot r\left(\cos \theta+\frac{\cos 2 \theta}{n}\right)=(125.7)^{2} 0.055\left(\cos 40^{\circ}+\frac{\cos 80^{\circ}}{0.25 / 0.055}\right)\) \(\ldots(\because n=l / r)\)

\(=698.7 m / s ^{2}\)

 

Angular acceleration of the connecting rod

We know that mass of the connecting rod at P,

\(\alpha_{ PC }=\frac{-\omega^{2} \sin \theta}{n}=\frac{-(125.7)^{2} \sin 40^{\circ}}{0.25 / 0.055}=-2234.4 rad / s ^{2}\)

 

Inertia torque exerted on the crankshaft

We know that mass of the connecting rod at P,

\(m_{1}=\frac{l-l_{1}}{l} \times m_{ C }=\frac{0.25-0.175}{0.25} \times 1.25=0.375 kg\)

\(\therefore \) Vertical inertia force,

\(F_{ I }=m_{1} \cdot a_{ P }=0.375 \times 698.7=262 N\)

and corresponding torque due to \(F_{ I }\)

\(T_{ I }=-F_{ I } \times O M=-262 \times 0.0425=-11.135 N – m\)

 

= 11.135 N-m (anticlockwise) …(By measurement, OM = 0.0425 m)

We know that the equivalent length of a simple pendulum when swung about an axis passing through P,

\(L=\frac{\left(k_{ G }\right)^{2}+\left(l_{1}\right)^{2}}{l_{1}}=\frac{(0.094)^{2}+(0.175)^{2}}{0.175}=0.225 m\)

 

\(\therefore \) Correction couple,

 

\(T^{\prime}=-m_{ C } l_{1}(l-L) \alpha_{ PC }=-1.25 \times 0.175(0.25-0.225) 2234.4=-12.22 N – m\)

Corresponding torque on the crankshaft,

\(T_{ C }=\frac{T^{\prime} \cos \theta}{n}=\frac{-12.22 \times \cos 40^{\circ}}{0.25 / 0.055}=-2.06 N – m =2.06 N – m \text { (anticlockwise) }\)

Torque due to the mass at P,

\(T_{ P }=m_{1} \times g \times O M=0.375 \times 9.81 \times 0.0425=0.156 N – m \text { (clockwise) }\)

Equivalent mass of the connecting rod at C,

\(m_{2}=m_{ C } \times \frac{l_{1}}{l}=1.25 \times \frac{0.175}{0.25}=0.875 kg\)

Torque due to mass at C,

\(T_{ W }=m_{2} \times g \times N C=0.875 \times 9.81 \times 0.035=0.3 N – m \text { (clockwise) }\) …(By measurement, NC = 0.035 m)

\(\therefore \) Inertia torque exerted on the crankshaft

\(=T_{ I }+T_{ C }-T_{ P }-T_{ W }\)

 

\(=11.135+2.06-0.156-0.3=12.739 N – m \text { (anticlockwise) }\)
