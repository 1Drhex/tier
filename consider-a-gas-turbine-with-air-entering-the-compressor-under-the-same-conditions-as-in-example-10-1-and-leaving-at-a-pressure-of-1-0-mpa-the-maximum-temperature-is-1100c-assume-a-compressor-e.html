Guest Question.

Consider a gas turbine with air entering the compressor under the same conditions as in and leaving at a pressure of 1.0 MPa. The maximum temperature is 1100째C.
Assume a compressor efficiency of 80%, a turbine efficiency of 85%, and a pressure drop between the compressor and turbine of 15 kPa. Determine the compressor work, turbine work, and cycle efficiency.
As in the previous example, for each control volume the model is ideal gas with constant specific heat, at 300 K, and each process is steady state with no kinetic or potential energy changes. In this example the diagram is Fig. 10.3.
We consider the compressor, the turbine, and the high-temperature heat exchanger in turn.
Control volume: Compressor.
Inlet state: \(P_{1}, T_{1}\) known; state fixed.
Exit state: \(P_{2}\) known.

Admin
Solution

Analysis :

Energy Eq. real process: \(w_{c}=h_{2}-h_{1}\)

Entropy Eq. ideal process: \(s_{2 s}=s_{1} \Rightarrow \frac{T_{2_{s}}}{T_{1}}=\left(\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}\)

In addition,

\(\eta_{c}=\frac{h_{2_{s}}-h_{1}}{h_{2}-h_{1}}=\frac{T_{2_{s}}-T_{1}}{T_{2}-T_{1}}\)

Solution :

Solving for \(T_{2_{s}}\) , we get

\(\left(\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}=\frac{T_{2_{s}}}{T_{1}}=10^{0.286}=1.932, \quad T_{2 s}=556.8 K\)

The efficiency is

\(\eta_{c}=\frac{h_{2 s}-h_{1}}{h_{2}-h_{1}}=\frac{T_{2 s}-T_{1}}{T_{2}-T_{1}}=\frac{556.8-288.2}{T_{2}-T_{1}}=0.80\)

Therefore,

\(T_{2}-T_{1}=\frac{556.8-288.2}{0.80}=335.8, \quad T_{2}=624.0 K\)

\(w_{c}=h_{2}-h_{1}=C_{p}\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right)\)

\(=1.004(624.0-288.2)=337.0 kJ / kg\)

Control volume: Turbine.
Inlet state: \(P_{3}\left(P_{2}-\text { drop }\right)\) known, \(T_{3}\)known; state fixed.
Exit state: \(P_{4}\) known.

Analysis :

Energy Eq. real process: \(w_{c}=h_{3}-h_{4}\)

Entropy Eq. ideal process: \(s_{4_{s}}=s_{3} \Rightarrow \frac{T_{3}}{T_{4_{s}}}=\left(\frac{P_{3}}{P_{4}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}\)

In addition,

\(\eta_{t}=\frac{h_{3}-h_{4}}{h_{3}-h_{4}}=\frac{T_{3}-T_{4}}{T_{3}-T_{4_{s}}}\)

Solution :

Substituting numerical values, we obtain

\(P_{3}=P_{2}-\text { pressure drop }=1.0-0.015=0.985 MPa\)

\(\left(\frac{P_{3}}{P_{4}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}=\frac{T_{3}}{T_{4_{s}}}=9.85^{0.286}=1.9236, \quad T_{4_{s}}=713.9 K\)

\(\eta_{t}=\frac{h_{3}-h_{4}}{h_{3}-h_{4_{s}}}=\frac{T_{3}-T_{4}}{T_{3}-T_{4_{3}}}=0.85\)

\(T_{3}-T_{4}=0.85(1373.2-713.9)=560.4 K\)

\(T_{4}=812.8 K\)

\(w_{t}=h_{3}-h_{4}=C_{p}\left(T_{3}-T_{4}\right)\)

\(=1.004(1373.2-812.8)=562.4 kJ / kg\)

\(w_{\text {net }}=w_{t}-w_{c}=562.4-337.0=225.4 kJ / kg\)

Finally, for the heat exchanger:

Control volume: High-temperature heat exchanger.
Inlet state: State 2 fixed (as given).
Exit state: State 3 fixed (as given).

Analysis :

Energy Eq.: \(q_{H}=h_{3}-h_{2}\)

Solution :

Substituting, we have

\(q_{H}=h_{3}-h_{2}=C_{p}\left(T_{3}-T_{2}\right)\)

\(=1.004(1373.2-624.0)=751.8 kJ / kg\)

so that

\(\eta_{ th }=\frac{w_{ net }}{q_{H}}=\frac{225.4}{751.8}=30.0 \%\)
