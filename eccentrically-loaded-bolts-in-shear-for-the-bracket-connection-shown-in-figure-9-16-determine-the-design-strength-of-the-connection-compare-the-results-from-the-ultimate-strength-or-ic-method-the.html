Eccentrically Loaded Bolts in Shear

Solution

Ultimate Strength, or IC, Method:

A location for the IC must first be assumed. By trial and error, a value of \(r_{0}= 1.90\) in. has been determined. A free- body diagram of the bolt group is shown in Figure 9-17.

From the free-body diagram, the distance of each bolt from the IC can be determined:

For the IC method, the bolt or bolts most remote from the IC are assumed to be stressed and deformed to failure; the deformation in the remaining bolts varies linearly. The bolts at locations 2 and 8 are the most remote (\(r_{2}=r_{8}=5.64\) in. ); therefore, the deformation of each is assumed to be Œî = 0.34 in. The deformation of the remaining bolts is determined as follows:

\(\Delta _{1}=\Delta _{7}=\frac{4.52 in.}{5.64 in.}(0.34 in.) = 0.272\) in.

\(\Delta _{3}=\Delta _{5}=\frac{1.55 in.}{5.64 in.}(0.34 in.) = 0.094\) in.

\(\Delta _{4}=\Delta _{6}=\frac{3.72 in.}{5.64 in.}(0.34 in.) = 0.224\) in.

\(\Delta _{2}=\Delta _{8}=0.34\) in.

\(R=R_{ult}(1-e^{-10\Delta } )^{0.55}\), (9-10)

\(R_{2} =R_{8}= (74)(1-e^{-(10)(0.34)} )^{0.55}=72.6\) kips

Since the equilibrium equations have been satisfied, the assumed location for the IC is correct.

For the IC method, the maximum shear and deformation values of \(R_{ult}=74\) k and Œî = 0.34 in. are used as baseline values. For fasteners of other sizes and grades, linear interpolation is conservatively used. The nominal strength of the connection is then

\(P\left(\frac{R_{n} }{R_{ult} } \right)=256 kips\left[\frac{(60 ksi)(0.442 in^{2} )}{74 kips} \right]= 92.0\) kips.

The design strength of the connection is

\(P_{u}=\phi R_{n}= (0.75)(92.0) = 69\) kips.

Elastic Method:

For this method, the critical fastener force is determined from the bolt most remote from the centroid of the bolt group. For this connection, bolts at locations 1, 2, 7, and 8 are the most remote. The capacity of one of these bolts will be used to determine the strength of the connection. The shear is distributed equally to all of the bolts in the group. Since there is no horizontal component for the applied load, equation (9-16) is satisfied. From equation (9-17),

\(r_{px}=\frac{P_{x} }{n},\) (9-16)

\(r_{py}=\frac{P_{y} }{n}\) (9-17)

\(r_{py}=\frac{P_{y} }{n}=\frac{P}{8}\)

The shear in the bolts due to the eccentricity is determined from equations (9-18) and (9-19):

\(r_{mx}=\frac{MC_{y} }{I_{p} },\) (9-18)

\(r_{my}=\frac{MC_{x} }{I_{p} },\) (9-19)

e = 6.5 in.

\(I_{p}=\sum{(c_{x} ^{2}+c_{y} ^{2} )}=4[(1.5)^{2}+(4.5)^{2} ]+4[(1.5)^{2}+(1.5)^{2} ]=108 in^{4}\).

\(r_{mx}=\frac{MC_{y} }{I_{p} }=\frac{Pec_{y} }{I_{p} }=\frac{P(6.5)(4.5)}{108}= 0.271P\)

\(r_{my}=\frac{MC_{x} }{I_{p} }=\frac{Pec_{x} }{I_{p} }=\frac{P(6.5)(1.5)}{108}= 0.0902P\).

Equation (9-20) will be used to determine the capacity of the bolt most remote from the centroid. Using \(r=\phi r_{n}\),

\(r=\sqrt{(r_{px}+r_{mx} )^{2}+(r_{py}+r_{my})^{2} }\) (9-20)

\((0.75)(60)(0.442)=\sqrt{(0 + 0.271P)^{2}+\left(\frac{P}{8}+0.0902P \right) ^{2} }\).

Solving for P,

\(P= P_{u}= 57.5\) kips.

The elastic method is more conservative than the IC method, so it is expected that the connection capacity is lower (57.5 kips < 69 kips).
