Guest Question.

The driving crank AB of the quick-return mechanism, as shown in Fig. 8.30, revolves at a uniform speed of 200 r.p.m. Find the velocity and acceleration of the tool-box R, in the position shown, when the crank makes an angle of \(60^{\circ}\) with the vertical line of centres PA. What is the acceleration of sliding of the block at B along the slotted lever PQ ?

Admin
Solution

Given : \(N_{ BA }=200 \text { r.p.m. or } \omega_{ BA }=2 \pi \times 200 / 60=20.95 rad / s\) ; A B = 75 mm = 0.075 m

We know that velocity of B with respect to A,

\(v_{ BA }=\omega_{ BA } \times A B=20.95 \times 0.075=1.57 m / s\) …(Perpendicular to A B)

 

Velocity of the tool-box R

First of all draw the space diagram, to some suitable scale, as shown in Fig. (a). Now the velocity diagram, as shown in Fig. (b), is drawn as discussed below:

1. Since A and P are fixed points, therefore these points are marked as one point in the velocity diagram. Now draw vector ab in a direction perpendicular to A B, to some suitable scale, to represent the velocity of B with respect to A or simply velocity of B (i.e. \(v_{ BA } \text { or } v_{ B }\)), such that

\(\text { vector } a b=v_{ BA }=v_{ B }=1.57 m / s\)

2. From point p, draw vector pb’ perpendicular to PB’ to represent the velocity of coincident point B’ with respect to P (i.e. \(v_{ B ^{\prime} P } \text { or } v_{ B }\)) and from point b, draw vector bb’ parallel to the path of motion of B’ (which is along PQ) to represent the velocity of coincident point B’ with respect to the slider B i.e. \(v_{ B ^{\prime} B }\). The vectors pb’ and bb’ intersect at b’.

Note. The vector b’b will represent the velocity of the slider B with respect to the coincident point B’ i.e.\(v_{ BB ^{\prime}}\).

3. Since the point Q lies on PB’ produced, therefore divide vector pb’ at q in the same ratio as Q divides PB’. In other words,

\(p b^{\prime} / p q=P B^{\prime} / P Q\)

The vector pq represents the velocity of Q with respect to P i.e. \(v_{ QP }\).

4. Now from point q, draw vector qr perpendicular to QR to represent the velocity of R with respect to Q i.e. \(v_{ RQ }\), and from point a draw vector ar parallel to the path of motion of the tool-box R (which is along the horizontal), to represent the velocity of R i.e. \(v_{ R }\).The vectors qr and ar intersect at r.

By measurement, we find that velocity of the tool-box R,

\(v_{ R }=\text { vector } a r=1.6 m / s\)

We also find that velocity of B’ with respect to B,

\(v_{ B ^{\prime} B }=\text { vector } b b^{\prime}=1.06\)

Velocity of B’ with respect to P,

\(v_{ B ^{\prime} P }=\operatorname{vector} p b^{\prime}=1.13 m / s\)

Velocity of R with respect to Q,

\(v_{ RQ }=\text { vector } q r=0.4 m / s\)

Velocity of Q with respect to P,

\(v_{ QP }=\text { vector } p q=1.7 m / s\)

\(\therefore \) Angular velocity of the link PQ,

\(\omega_{ PQ }=\frac{v_{ QP }}{Q P}=\frac{1.7}{0.375}=4.53 rad / s\) …\((\because P Q=0.375 m )\)

 

Acceleration of the tool box R

We know that the radial component of the acceleration of B with respect to A,

\(a_{ BA }^{r}=\omega_{ BA }^{2} \times A B=(20.95)^{2} \times 0.075=32.9 m / s ^{2}\)

Coriolis component of the acceleration of the slider B with respect to coincident point B′.

\(a_{ BB ^{\prime}}^{c}=2 \omega \cdot v=2 \omega_{ QP } \times v_{ BB ^{\prime}}=2 \times 4.53 \times 1.06=9.6 m / s ^{2}\) \(\ldots\left(\because \omega=\omega_{ QP }, \text { and } v=v_{ BB }^{\prime}\right)\)

Radial component of the acceleration of R with respect to Q ,

\(a_{ RQ }^{r}=\frac{v_{ RQ }^{2}}{Q R}=\frac{(0.4)^{2}}{0.5}=0.32 m / s ^{2}\)

Radial component of the acceleration of B’ with respect to P,

\(a_{ B ^{\prime} P }^{r}=\frac{v_{ B ^{\prime} P }}{P B^{\prime}}=\frac{(1.13)^{2}}{0.248}=5.15 m / s ^{2}\) …(By measurement, PB’ = 248 mm = 0.248 m)

Now the acceleration diagram, as shown in Fig.(d), is drawn as discussed below:

1. Since A and P are fixed points, therefore these points are marked as one point in the acceleration diagram. Draw vector a’b’ parallel to A B, to some suitable scale, to represent the radial component of the acceleration of B with respect to A i.e. \(a_{ BA }^{r}, \text { or } a_{ B }\) such that

\(\text { vector } a^{\prime} b^{\prime}=a_{ BA }^{r}=a_{ B }=32.9 m / s ^{2}\)

2. The acceleration of the slider B with respect to the coincident point B’ has the following two components:

(i) Coriolis component of the acceleration of B with respect to B’ i.e. \(a_{ BB ^{\prime}}^{c}\) and

(ii) Radial component of the acceleration of B with respect to B’ i.e. \(a_{ BB ^{\prime}}^{r}\)

These two components are mutually perpendicular. Therefore from point b’, draw vector b’x perpendicular to BP [i.e. in a direction as shown in Fig.(c)] to represent \(a_{ BB ^{\prime}}^{c}=9.6 m / s ^{2}\) . The direction of \(a_{ BB ^{\prime}}^{c}\) is obtained by rotating \(v_{ BB ^{\prime}}\) (represented by vector b’b in the velocity diagram) through \(90^{\circ}\) in the same sense as that of link PQ which rotates in the clockwise direction. Now from point x, draw vector xb” perpendicular to vector b’x (or parallel to B’P) to represent \(a_{ BB ^{\prime}}^{r}\) whose magnitude is yet unknown.

3. The acceleration of the coincident point B’ with respect to P has also the following two components:

(i) Radial component of the acceleration of B’ with respect to P i.e. \(a_{ B ^{\prime} P }^{r}\) and

(ii) Tangential component of the acceleration of B’ with respect to P i.e. \(a_{ B ^{\prime} P }^{t}\)

These two components are mutually perpendicular. Therefore from point p’ draw vector p’y parallel to B’P to represent \(a_{ B ^{\prime} p }^{r}=5.15 m / s ^{2}\), and from point y draw vector yb” perpendicular to vector p’y to represent \(a_{ B ^{\prime} P }^{t}\). The vectors xb” and yb” intersect at b”, join p’b”. The vector p’b” represents the acceleration of B’ with respect to P i.e. \(a_{ B ^{\prime} P }\) and the vector b”b’ represents the acceleration of B with respect to B’ i.e. \(a_{ BB ^{\prime}}\) .

4. Since the point Q lies on PB’ produced, therefore divide vector p’b” at q’ in the same ratio as Q divides PB in the space diagram. In other words,

\(p^{\prime} b^{\prime \prime} / p^{\prime} q^{\prime}=P B^{\prime} / P Q\)

5. The acceleration of the tool-box R with respect to Q has the following two components:

(i) Radial component of the acceleration of R with respect to Q i.e. \(a_{ RQ }^{r}\) and

(ii) Tangential component of the acceleration of R with respect to Q i.e. \(a_{ RQ }^{t}\).

These two components are mutually perpendicular. Therefore from point q’, draw vector a’z parallel to QR to represent \(a_{ RQ }^{r}=0.32 m / s ^{2}\). Since the magnitude of this component is very small, therefore the points q’ and z coincide as shown in Fig. (d). Now from point z (same as q’), draw vector zr’ perpendicular to vector q’z (or QR) to represent \(a_{ RQ }^{t}\) whose magnitude is yet unknown.

6. From point a’ draw vector a’r’ parallel to the path of motion of the tool-box R (i.e. along the horizontal) which intersects the vector zr’ at r’. The vector a’r’ represents the acceleration of the tool-box R i.e. \(a_{ R }\).

By measurement, we find that

\(a_{ R }=\text { vector } a^{\prime} r^{\prime}=22 m / s ^{2}\)

 

Acceleration of sliding of the block B along the slotted lever PQ

By measurement, we find that the acceleration of sliding of the block B along the slotted lever PQ

\(=a_{ BB ^{\prime}}=\text { vector } b^{\prime \prime} x=18 m / s ^{2}\)
