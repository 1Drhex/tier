Guest Question.

Consider steady state conduction in a composite slab with heat generation in the middle portion of the slab. The problem description is shown in Fig. 2.24. Given \(T_2=270^\circ C\) , \(T_3=220^\circ C\), determine the thermal conductivity of the middle portion of the slab and the volumetric heat generation in the middle portion of the slab, for steady state one dimensional conduction with constant properties of all the materials.

Admin
Solution

Under steady sate

\(Q=\frac{T_2-30}{\frac{1}{hA}+\frac{L_a}{K_aA} } \)

 

\(q_{_{left}}=\frac{270-30}{\frac{1}{1000}+\frac{0.03}{16} } \)

 

\(q_{_{left}}=83478W/m^2\)

Similarly from the right side, we have

\(q_{_{right}}=\frac{220-30}{\frac{1}{1000}+\frac{0.02}{50} } \)

 

\(q_{_{right}}=135714W/m^2\)

 

\(q_{_{total}}=83478+135714=219192W/m^2\)

In order to obtain the volumetric heat generation rate, \(q_v\) , we use energy balance.

Total heat transferred = Total heat generated

\(q_{_{total}}A=q_vAL\)

 

\(q_v=\frac{219192}{0.03}=7306.4kW/m^3\)

Now, for the thermal conductivity calculation, we consider only the middle wall. The governing equation for the middle wall is

\(\frac{d^2T}{dx^2}+\frac{q_v}{k_b}=0 \)

 

\(\frac{dT}{dx}=-\frac{q_v}{k_b}x+c_1\)

 

\(T=-\frac{q_v}{k_b}\frac{x^2}{2}+c_1x+c_2 \)

At the left of the middle wall: x=0 ; \(T=T_2\) ; \(q_{_{left}}=-k\frac{dT}{dx} \)

At the right of middle wall : x=L=0.03m; \(T=T_3\) ; \(q_{_{right}}=-k\frac{dT}{dx} \)

At x=0; \(T=T_2\)

\(c_2=T_2=270^\circ C\)

At x=L=0.03m; \(T=T_3\)

\(220=\frac{-7.306\times 10^6\times 0.03^2}{k_b}+c_1\times 0.03+270\) (2.158)

At the left of middle wall : x=0; \(T=T_2\) ; \(q_{_{left}}=-k\frac{dT}{dx} \)

\(\therefore k\frac{dT}{dx} \mid _{_{ _{_{_{x=0}}}}}=83478=c_1k_b\)

 

\(k_b=\frac{83478}{c_1} \)

Substituting for \(k_b\) in Eq. (2.158), we have

\(c_1=1026.7^\circ C/m\)

 

\(k_b=81.3W/mK\)
