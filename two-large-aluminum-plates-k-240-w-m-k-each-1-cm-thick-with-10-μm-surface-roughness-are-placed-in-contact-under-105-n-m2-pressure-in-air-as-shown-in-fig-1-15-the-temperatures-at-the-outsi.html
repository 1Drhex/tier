Guest Question.

Two large aluminum plates (k = 240 W/m K) each 1-cm thick, with 10-μm surface roughness are placed in contact under \(10^{5} \space N/m^{2}\) pressure in air as shown in Fig. 1.15. The temperatures at the outside surfaces are 395°C and 405°C.Calculate (a) the heat flux and (b) the temperature drop due to the contact resistance.

Admin
Solution

(a) The rate of heat flow per unit area, \(q^{\prime \prime}\) , through the sandwich wall is :

\(q=\frac{T_{s 1}-T_{s 3}}{R_{1}+R_{2}+R_{3}}=\frac{\Delta T}{(L / k)_{1}+R_{i}+(L / k)_{2}}\)

From Table 1.6 :

Resistance,\(R_{i}\left(m^{2}K/W\times 10^{4}\right)\)
Interface Material Contact Pressure \( 100\space KN/m^{2}\) Contact Pressure \( 10,000\space KN/m^{2}\)
Stainless steel 6–25 0.7–4.0
Copper 1–10 0.1–0.5
Magnesium 1.5–3.5 0.2–0.4
Aluminum 1.5–5.0 0.2–0.4

the contact resistance \(R_{i} \space is \space 2.75 \times 10^{-4} {~m}^{2} {~K}\) While each of the other two resistances is equal to

\((L / k)=(0.01 {~m}) /(240 {~W} / {m} {K})=4.17 \times 10^{-5} {~m}^{2} {~K} /{W}\)

Hence, the heat flux is :

\(q^{\prime \prime} =\frac{(405-395)^{\circ} {C}}{\left(4.17 \times 10^{-5}+2.75 \times 10^{-4}+4.17 \times 10^{-5}\right) {m}^{2} {~K} / {W}}\\=2.79 \times 10^{4} {~W} / {m}^{2} {~K}\)

(b) The temperature drop in each section of this one-dimensional system is proportional to the resistance. The fraction of the contact resistance is :

\(R_{i} / \sum\limits_{n=1}^{3}R_{n}{=2.75 / 3.584=0.767}\)

Hence 7.67°C of the total temperature drop of 10°C is the result of the contact resistance.
