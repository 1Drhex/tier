Guest Question.

A steel insulated tank of 6 \(\textbf{m}^{\textbf{3}}\) volume is equally divided into two chambers using a partition. The two portions of tank contain \(\textbf{N}_{\textbf{2}}\) gas at 800 kPa and 480 K and \(\textbf{CO}_{\textbf{2}}\) gas at 400 kPa and 390 K. Determine the equilibrium temperature and pressure of mixture after removing the partition. Use \(\pmb{\gamma}\) = 1.4 for \(\textbf{N}_{\textbf{2}}, \pmb{\gamma}\)= 1.3 for \(\textbf{CO}_{\textbf{2}}\).

Admin
Solution

Since tank is insulated so adiabatic mixing can be considered. Let us consider \(N_{2}\) and \(CO_{2}\) to behave as perfect gas.
\(\begin{aligned}\text{No. of moles of N}_{2}& \\n_{N_{2}}&= \frac{p_{N_{2}}. V_{N_{2}}}{\overline{R} . T_{N_{2}}} = \frac{800 \times 10^{3} \times 3}{8314 \times 480} = 0.601\\\text{No of moles of CO}_{2}\\ n_{CO_{2}}&= \frac{p_{CO_{2}}. V_{CO_{2}}}{\overline{R} . T_{CO_{2}}} = \frac{400 \times 10^{3} \times 3}{8314 \times 390} = 0.370\\\text{Total no. of moles of mixture,} \\n&= n_{N_{2}} + n_{CO_{2}}\\&= 0.601 + 0.370\\&= 0.971\\\text{Specific heat for N}_{2}\text{ at constant volume,}\\C_{v, N_{2}}&= \frac{R_{N_{2}}}{(\gamma N_{2} – 1)} = \frac{(8314 / 28)}{(1.4 −1)}\\C_{v, N_{2}}&= 742.32 J/kg . K\\\text{Specific heat for CO}_{2}\text{ at constant volume,}\\C_{v, CO_{2}}&= \frac{R_{CO_{2}}}{(\gamma CO_{2} – 1)} = \frac{(8314 / 44)}{(1.3 −1)}\\C_{v, CO_{2}}&= 629.85 J/kg . K\end{aligned}\\\)
\(\text{Mass of N}_{2} = n_{N_{2}} × Mol. wt. of N_{2} = 0.601 × 28 = 16.828 kg\\\text{Mass of CO}_{2} = n_{CO_{2}} × Mol. wt. of CO_{2} = 0.370 × 44 = 16.28 kg.\)
Let us consider the equilibrium temperature of mixture after adiabatic mixing at T. Applying energy conservation principle :
\(m_{N_{2}} .C_{v, N_{2}} . (T −T_{N_{2}} ) + m_{CO_{2}} .C_{v, CO_{2}} . (T − T_{CO_{2}} ) = 0\\\left\{16.828 × 742.32 (T – 480)\right\} + \left\{16.28 × 629.85 (T – 390)\right\} = 0\\22745.7 . T = 9995088.881\\\text{Equilibrium temperature, }\textbf{T = 439.4 K}\\\text{Equilibrium pressure of mixture, T}_{mixture} = 439.4 K, V_{mixture} = 6 m^{3}\\p_{mixture} = \frac{n . \overline{R} . T_{mixture}}{V_{mixture}} = \frac{0.971 × 8314 × 439.4}{6}\\\text{Equilibrium pressure = }\textbf{591.205 kPa}\)
