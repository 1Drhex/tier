Guest Question.

Ethanol is classified by the National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) as a flammable fluid because of its low flash point of \( 17^{\circ} C\). This means that at \( 17^{\circ} C\) or higher, ethanol can vaporize and become a mixture in air that would ignite when an ignition source is present. Thus, in an environment that ignition sources are present, keeping ethanol at a temperature below its flash point can help to prevent fire hazard. Consider a process where ethanol is cooled by water in a 1 -shell-pass heat exchanger that can accommodate a maximum of 14 -tube-passes (Fig. 11-32). The tubes are made of copper and thin-walled with an inner diameter of 1.5 cm. The length of each tube pass that can be fitted inside the heat exchanger is 3 m, and the overall heat transfer coefficient is \( 700 W / m ^{2} \cdot K\). Ethanol \( \left( c _{ p }=2630 J / kg \cdot K \right) \) enters the heat exchanger at\( 55^{\circ} C\) and flows through the shell at a rate of 0.28 kg / s. Water enters the heat exchanger at \( 2^{\circ} C\) and flows through the tubes at a rate of \( 1.3 kg / s\). To prevent fire hazard, the ethanol is to be cooled to \( 15^{\circ} C\), which is below its flash point. Determine the number of tube passes that is necessary inside the shell-andtube heat exchanger to cool the ethanol to the prescribed temperature. Discuss whether or not this heat exchanger is suitable for this application. Evaluate any required property of water at \( 5^{\circ} C . \) Is this a good assumption?

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION In this example, the concepts of Prevention through Design (PtD) are applied in conjunction with the concept of effectiveness-NTU method for heat exchanger analysis.

Assumptions 1 Steady operating conditions exist. 2 The heat exchanger is well insulated so that heat loss to the surroundings is negligible. 3 Changes in the kinetic and potential energies of fluid streams are negligible. 4 Fluid properties are constant. 5 Thermal resistance of tube wall is negligible.

Properties The specific heat of ethanol is given to be \( c_{p h}=2630 J / kg \cdot K \) The specific heat of water at \( 5^{\circ} C\) is \( c_{p c}=4205 J / kg \cdot K \) Table A -9

Analysis In the effectiveness-NTU method, we first determine the heat capacity rates of the hot and cold fluids and identify the smaller one:

\( C_{h}=\dot{m}_{h} c_{p h}=(0.28 kg / s )(2630 J / kg \cdot K )=736.4 W / K \)

 

\( C_{c}=\dot{m}_{c} c_{p c}=(1.3 kg / s )(4205 J / kg \cdot K )=5466.5 W / K\)

 

Therefore,

\( C_{\min }=C_{h}=736.4 W / K\)

and

\( c=C_{\min } / C_{\max }=736.4 / 5466.5=0.1347\)

Then the maximum heat transfer rate is determined from Eq. 11 – 32

\(\dot{Q}_{\max }=C_{\min }\left(T_{h, \text { in }}-T_{c, \text { in }}\right)\)

to be

\( \dot{Q}_{\max }=C_{\min }\left(T_{h, in }-T_{c, in }\right)=(736.4 W / K )(55-2) K =39,029 W\)

The actual rate of heat transfer is

\( \dot{Q}=C_{h}\left(T_{h, \text { in }}-T_{h \text { ,out }}\right)=(736.4 W / K )(55-15) K =29,456 W\)

Thus, the effectiveness of the heat exchanger is

\( \varepsilon=\frac{\dot{Q}}{\dot{Q}_{\max }}=\frac{29,456}{39,029}=0.7547\)

For this one-shell pass heat exchanger, knowing the effectiveness \( \varepsilon\), the NTU of this heat exchanger can be determined from the appropriate relation from Table 11-5:

\( NTU _{1} =-\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+c^{2}}} \ln \left(\frac{2 / \varepsilon_{1}-1-c-\sqrt{1+c^{2}}}{2 / \varepsilon_{1}-1-c+\sqrt{1+c^{2}}}\right) \)

 

\( =-\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+0.1347^{2}}} \ln \left(\frac{2 / 0.7547-1-0.1347-\sqrt{1+0.1347^{2}}}{2 / 0.7547-1-0.1347+\sqrt{1+0.1347^{2}}}\right) \)

 

\( =1.592\)

 

Note that for this one-shell pass heat exchanger \( \varepsilon_{1}=\varepsilon\) and \( NTU _{1}= NTU\)

Then the number of tube passes can be determined using

\( NTU =\frac{U A_{s}}{C_{\min }}=\frac{U(\pi D L) n}{C_{\min }}=1.592\)

Thus,

\( n=\frac{C_{\min } NTU }{U \pi D L}=\frac{(736.4 W / K )(1.592)}{\left(700 W / m ^{2} \cdot K \right) \pi(0.015 m )(3 m )}=11.85 \rightarrow 12 \text { -tube-passes }\)

Discussion To reduce the ethanol temperature from \( 55^{\circ} C\) to \( 15^{\circ} C\), the 1 -shell pass heat exchanger requires at least 12-tube-passes of 1.5- cm -diameter and 3-m-long tubes. At \( 15^{\circ} C\), the temperature is below the flash point of ethanol, and the risk of fire hazard is alleviated.The heat exchanger can accommodate up to 14 -tube-passes, therefore it is suitable for this application. Note that the outlet temperature of water can be determined from

\( \dot{Q}=C_{c}\left(T_{c, \text { out }}-T_{c, \text { in }}\right) \rightarrow T_{c, \text { out }}=\frac{\dot{Q}}{C_{c}}+T_{c, \text { in }}=\frac{29,456 W }{5466.5 W /{ }^{\circ} C }+2^{\circ} C =7.4^{\circ} C\)

Then the bulk mean temperature for the water is \( T_{c, b}=\left(T_{c, \text { out }}+T_{c, \text { in }}\right) / 2=4.7^{\circ} C\). Thus, \( 5^{\circ} C\) is an appropriate temperature for evaluating the specific heat of water.
