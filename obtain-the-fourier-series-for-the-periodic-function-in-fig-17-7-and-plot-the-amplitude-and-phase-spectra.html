Guest Question.

Obtain the Fourier series for the periodic function in Fig. 17.7 and plot the amplitude and phase spectra.

Admin
Solution

The function is described as

\( f(t)=\begin{cases} t, & 0<t<1 \\ 0, & 1<t<2\end{cases} \)

Since \( T = 2, ω_0 = 2π/T = π \) Then

\( a _0 = \frac{1}{T} \int_{0}^{T}{f(t)} dt = \frac{1}{2}\biggl[\int_{0}^{1}{t}dt + \int_{1}^{2}{0} dt \biggr] = \frac{1}{2} \frac{t^2}{2} \mid ^1_0 = \frac{1}{4} \) (17.2.1)

To evaluate \( a_n \) and \( b_n \) we need the integrals in Eq. (17.15):

\( \int{\cos a t} dt = \frac{1}{a } \sin a t \) (17.15a)

\( \int{\sin a t} dt = -\frac{1}{a } \cos a t \) (17.15b)

\( \int{t \cos a t} =\frac{1}{a ^2} \cos a t + \frac{1}{ a } t\sin a t \) (17.15c)

\( \int{t \sin a t} =\frac{1}{a ^2} \sin a t – \frac{1}{a } t\cos a t \) (17.15d)

\( a _n =\frac{2}{T} \int_{0}^{T}{f(t) \cos n\omega _0t} dt = \frac{2}{2} \biggl[\int_{0}^{1}{t\cos n\pi t} dt + \int_{1}^{2}{0\cos n\pi t}dt \biggr] = \biggl[\frac{1}{n^2\pi ^2} \cos n\pi t + \frac{t}{n\pi } \sin n\pi t\biggr]\mid ^1_0 = \frac{1}{n^2\pi ^2}(\cos n\pi -1) +0 = \frac{(-1)^n-1}{n^2\pi ^2} \) (17.2.2)

since \( \cos n π = (1)^n \); and

\( b_n = \frac{2}{T} \int_{0}^{T}{f(t)\sin n\omega _0 t} dt = \frac{2}{2} \biggl[\int_{0}^{1}{t\sin n\pi t} dt+\int_{1}^{2}{0\sin n\pi t} dt \biggr] = \biggl[\frac{1}{n^2\pi ^2} \sin n\pi t – \frac{t}{n\pi }\cos n\pi t \biggr]\mid ^1_0 = 0- \frac{\cos n\pi }{n\pi } =\frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n\pi } \) (17.2.3)

Substituting the Fourier coefficients just found into Eq. (17.3) yields

\( f(t) = \underbrace{a _0}_{dc} + \underbrace{\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty }{(a _n \cos n\omega _0t+b_n\sin n \omega _0 t)} }_{ac} \) (17.3)

\( f(t) = \frac{1}{4} + \sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty }{\biggl[\frac{[(-1)^n-1]}{(n\pi )^2} \cos n\pi t+\frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n\pi }\sin n\pi t \biggr] } \)

To obtain the amplitude and phase spectra, we notice that, for even

harmonics, \( α_n =0, b_n = -1/nπ \) , so that

\( A_n\angle \phi _n = \alpha _n – jb_n = 0+j\frac{1}{n\pi } \) (17.2.4)

Hence,

\( A_n = \left|b_n\right| = \frac{1}{n\pi } \) n = 2, 4, . . .

\( \phi _n = 90° \) n = 2, 4, . . . (17.2.5)

For odd harmonics, \( a_n = -2/(n^2 π^2),b_n = 1/(nπ) \) so that

\( A_n\angle \phi _n = a _n-jb_n = -\frac{2}{n^2 \pi ^2} -j\frac{1}{n\pi } \) (17.2.6)

That is,

\( A_n = \sqrt{a ^2_n + b^2_n} = \sqrt{\frac{4}{n^4\pi ^4}+\frac{1}{n^2\pi ^2} } = \frac{1}{n^2 \pi ^2} \sqrt{4+n^2\pi ^2} \) , n = 1, 3, . . . (17.2.7)

From Eq. (17.2.6), we observe that Φ lies in the third quadrant, so that

\( \phi _n = 180^\circ +\tan ^{-1}\frac{n\pi }{2} \) n = 1, 3, . . . (17.2.8)

From Eqs. (17.2.5), (17.2.7), and (17.2.8), we plot \( A_n \) and \( \phi _n \) for different values of \( nω_0 = nπ \) to obtain the amplitude spectrum and phase spectrum as shown in Fig. 17.8.
