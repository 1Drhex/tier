Guest Question.

A smooth sphere (weight W, radius R) rests on three points A, B and C. These three points form an equilateral triangle in a horizontal plane. The height of the triangle is \(3a=\frac{3}{4} \sqrt{3} R\) (see Fig. 2.30). The action line of the applied force F passes through the center of the sphere.

Determine the contact forces at A, B and C. Find the force F required to lift the sphere off at C.

Admin
Solution

The contact forces A, B and C are perpendicular to the smooth surface of the sphere. Thus, these forces and W and F are a spatial system of concurrent forces. They satisfy the equilibrium condition

A + B + C +W + F = 0 .

We now use the coordinate system shown in Fig. 2.30 with its origin at the center of the sphere. In order to obtain the force vectors we first write down their direction vectors with the aid of the coordinates of the points of contact. To this end we introduce the length b which follows from the geometry:

\((2b)^{2} =b^{2} +(3a)^{2} \rightarrow b=\sqrt{3} a\) .

Then, for example, the unit vector in the direction from A to the origin of the coordinate system is obtained as

\(e_{A} =\frac{1}{a\sqrt{1+3+4/3} } \left(\begin{matrix} -a \\ \sqrt{3}a \\ 2a/\sqrt{3} \end{matrix} \right)=\frac{1}{4} \left(\begin{matrix} -\sqrt{3} \\ 3 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right)\).

Hence, the force vector A (oriented towards the interior of the sphere) is given by

A \(=\frac{A}{4} \left(\begin{matrix} -\sqrt{3} \\ 3 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right)\) .

Similarly, the other force vectors are found to be

\(B=\frac{B}{4} \left(\begin{matrix} -\sqrt{3} \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right) , C=\frac{C}{4} \left(\begin{matrix} 2\sqrt{3} \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right) , F=\left(\begin{matrix} F \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right) , W=\left(\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ -W \end{matrix} \right)\) .

Introduction into the vector equilibrium condition leads to the three equations

\(-\sqrt{3} A -\sqrt{3} B +2\sqrt{3} C=-4F ,\)

\(3A-3B=0 \) ,

\(2A+2B+2C=4W\)

from which we can calculate the contact forces:

\(\underline{\underline{A=B=\frac{2}{3} (W+\frac{1}{\sqrt{3} }F )} } , \underline{\underline{C=\frac{2}{3} (W-\frac{2}{\sqrt{3} }F )} }\).

When the sphere lifts off at point C, the corresponding contact force vanishes: C = 0. This yields the force which is necessary for the lift-off:

\(W-\frac{2}{\sqrt{3} }F=0\rightarrow \underline{\underline{F=\frac{\sqrt{3} }{2}W } }\).

Note that this force may also be determined from the moment equilibrium condition about the axis passing through the points A and B:

\(\sum{M^{(\overline{AB} )} } =0 : aW-\frac{R}{2} F=0\) .

Thus, we obtain again

\(\underline{\underline{F} } =\frac{2a}{R} W=\underline{\underline{\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2 }W} }\) .
