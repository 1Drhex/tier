Guest Question.

(a) Show that \(Tr(\hat{A} \hat{B} )=Tr(\hat{B} \hat{A}).\)

(b) Show that the trace of a commutator is always zero.

(c) Illustrate the results shown in (a) and (b) on the following matrices:

\(A=\left(\begin{matrix} 8-2i & 4i & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 1-i \\ -8 & i & 6i \end{matrix} \right), B=\left(\begin{matrix} -i & 2 & 1-i \\ 6 & 1+i & 3i \\ 1 & 5+7i & 0 \end{matrix} \right).\)

 

Admin
Solution

(a) Using the definition of the trace,

\(Tr(\hat{A} \hat{B} )=\sum\limits_{n}{〈\phi _n | \hat{A}\hat{B} | \phi _n〉 },\) (2.207)

and inserting the unit operator between Â and \(\hat{B}\) we have

\(Tr(\hat{A} \hat{B} )=\sum\limits_{n}{〈\phi _n | \hat{A}}\left(\sum\limits_{m}{| \phi_m 〉〈\phi_m |} \right) \hat{B} | \phi _n 〉=\sum\limits_{nm}{〈\phi _n | \hat{A} | \phi _m〉 〈\phi _m | \hat{B}| \phi _n〉 }\)

\(=\sum\limits_{nm}{A_{nm}}B_{mn}.\) (2.208)

On the other hand, since \(Tr(\hat{A} \hat{B} )=\sum\limits_{n}{〈\phi _n | \hat{A}\hat{B} | \phi _n〉 },\) we have

\(Tr(\hat{B} \hat{A} )=\sum\limits_{m}{〈\phi _m | \hat{B}}\left(\sum\limits_{n}{| \phi_n 〉〈\phi_n |} \right) \hat{A} | \phi _m 〉=\sum\limits_{m}{〈\phi _m | \hat{B} | \phi _n〉 〈\phi _n | \hat{A} | \phi _m〉 }\)

\(=\sum\limits_{nm}{B_{mn}}A_{nm}.\) (2.209)

Comparing (2.208) and (2.209), we see that \(Tr(\hat{A} \hat{B} )=Tr(\hat{B} \hat{A} ).\)

(b) Since \(Tr(\hat{A} \hat{B} )=Tr(\hat{B} \hat{A} ).\) we can infer at once that the trace of any commutator is always zero:

\(Tr([\hat{A}, \hat{B}] )=Tr(\hat{A} \hat{B} )-Tr(\hat{B} \hat{A} )=0.\) (2.210)

(c) Let us verify that the traces of the products AB and BA are equal. Since

\(AB=\left(\begin{matrix} -2+16i & 12 & -6-10i \\ 1-2i & 14+2i & 1-i \\ 20i & -59+31i & -11+8i \end{matrix} \right) , BA=\left(\begin{matrix} -8 & 5+i & 8+4i \\ 49-35i & -3+24i & -16 \\ 13+5i & 4i & 12+2i \end{matrix} \right) ,\) (2.211)

we have

\(Tr(AB)=Tr\left(\begin{matrix} -2+16i & 12 & -6-10i \\ 1-2i & 14+2i & 1-i \\ 20i & -59+31i & -11+8i \end{matrix} \right)=1+26i,\) (2.212)

\(Tr(BA)=Tr\left(\begin{matrix} -8 & 5+i & 8+4i \\ 49-35i & -3+24i & -16 \\ 13+5i & 4i & 12+2i \end{matrix} \right)=1+26i=Tr(AB).\) (2.213)

This leads to Tr(AB) – Tr(BA) = (1 + 26i) – (1 + 26i) = 0 or Tr([A, B]) = 0.
