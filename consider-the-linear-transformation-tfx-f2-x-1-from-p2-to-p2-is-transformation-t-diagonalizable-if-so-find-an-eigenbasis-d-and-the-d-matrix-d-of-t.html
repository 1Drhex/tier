Guest Question.

Consider the linear transformation \(T(f(x))=f(2 x-1)\) from \(P_{2}\) to \(P_{2}\) . Is transformation \(T\) diagonalizable? If so, find an eigenbasis \(\mathfrak{D}\) and the \(\mathfrak{D}\)-matrix \(D\) of \(T\) .

Admin
Solution

Here it would be hard to find eigenvalues and eigenfunctions “by inspection;” we need a systematic approach. The idea is to find the matrix \(A\) of \(T\) with respect to some convenient basis \(\mathfrak{A}\) . Then we can use Theorem 7.4.4 to determine whether \(A\) is diagonalizable, and, if so, to find an eigenbasis for \(A\) . Finally we can transform this basis back into \(P_{2}\) to find an eigenbasis \(\mathfrak{D}\) for \(T\) .

We will use a commutative diagram to find the matrix \(A\) of \(T\) with respect to the standard basis \(\mathfrak{A} =\left(1, x, x^{2}\right)\) :

\(a+b x+c x^{2}\quad \xrightarrow[\mathfrak{}]{\qquad T \qquad } \quad T\left (a+b x+c x^{2}\right) =a+b(2 x-1)+c(2 x-1)^{2} =a-b+c+(2 b-4 c) x+4 c x^{2}\)

 

\(\downarrow L_\mathfrak{A} \hspace{120 pt} \downarrow L_\mathfrak{A}\)

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{l} a \\ b \\ c \end{array}\right] \quad \xrightarrow[A=\begin{bmatrix} 1&-1&1\\0&2&-4\\0&0&4 \end{bmatrix}]{} \qquad \left[\begin{array}{c} a-b+c \\ 2 b-4 c \\ 4 c \end{array}\right] \)

The upper triangular matrix \(A\) has the three distinct eigenvalues, 1, 2, and 4, so that \(A\) is diagonalizable, by Theorem 7.4.3b.

Theorem 7.4.3b: If an n × n matrix \(A\) has n distinct eigenvalues, then \(A\) is diagonalizable.

\(A\) straightforward computation produces the eigenbasis:

\(\left[\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right], \quad\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right], \quad\left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\)

for \(A\) . Transforming these vectors back into \(P_{2}\) , we find the eigenbasis \(\mathfrak{D}\) for \(T\) consisting of:

\(1, \quad x-1, \quad x^{2}-2 x+1=(x-1)^{2}\)

To check our work, we can verify that these are indeed eigenfunctions of \(T\) :

\(T(1) =1\) ,

\(T(x-1) =(2 x-1)-1=2 x-2=2(x-1)\) ,

\(T\left((x-1)^{2}\right) =((2 x-1)-1)^{2}=(2 x-2)^{2}=4(x-1)^{2}\)

 

The \(\mathfrak{D}\)-matrix of \(T\) is:

\( D= \begin{bmatrix}1&0&0\\0&2&0\\0&0&4 \end{bmatrix} \)

Consider Figure 2, where: \( S= \begin {bmatrix} 1&-1&1 \\ 0&1 &-2 \\0&0&1 \end{bmatrix} \) is the change of basis matrix from \(\mathfrak{D}\) to \(\mathfrak{A}\) .
