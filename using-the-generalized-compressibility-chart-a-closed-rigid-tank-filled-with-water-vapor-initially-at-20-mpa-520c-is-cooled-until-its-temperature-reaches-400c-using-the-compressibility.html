Using the Generalized Compressibility Chart

A closed, rigid tank filled with water vapor, initially at 20 MPa, 520°C, is cooled until its temperature reaches 400°C. Using the compressibility chart, determine

a. the specific volume of the water vapor in \(m ^{3} / kg\) at the initial state.

b. the pressure in MPa at the final state.

Compare the results of parts (a) and (b) with the values obtained from the superheated vapor table, .

Admin
Solution

Known Water vapor is cooled at constant volume from 20 MPa, 520°C to 400°C.

Find Use the compressibility chart and the superheated vapor table to determine the specific volume and final pressure and compare the results.

Schematic and Given Data:

Engineering Model

1. The water vapor is a closed system.

2. The initial and final states are at equilibrium.

3. The volume is constant.

Analysis

a. From , \(T_{ c }=647.3 K \text { and } p_{c}=22.09\) MPa for water. Thus,

 

1 \(T_{ R 1}=\frac{793}{647.3}=1.23, \quad p_{ R 1}=\frac{20}{22.09}=0.91\)

 

With these values for the reduced temperature and reduced pressure, the value of Z obtained from Fig. A-1 is approximately 0.83.

 

Since Z = pυ/RT, the specific volume at state 1 can be determined as follows:

 

\(v_{1}=Z_{1} \frac{R T_{1}}{p_{1}}=0.83 \frac{\bar{R} T_{1}}{M p_{1}}\)

 

2 \(=0.83\left(\frac{8314 \frac{ N \cdot m }{ kmol \cdot k }}{18.02 \frac{ kg }{ kmol }}\right)\left(\frac{793 K }{20 \times 10^{6} \frac{ N }{ m ^{2}}}\right)=0.0152 m ^{3} / kg\)

 

The molecular weight of water is from .

Turning to , the specific volume at the initial state is 0.01551 \(m ^{3} / kg\). This is in good agreement with the compressibility chart value, as expected.

b. Since both mass and volume remain constant, the water vapor cools at constant specific volume and, thus, at constant \(v_{ R }^{\prime}\). Using the value for specific volume determined in part (a), the constant \(v_{ R }^{\prime}\) value is

 

\(v_{ R }^{\prime}=\frac{v p_{ c }}{R T_{ c }}=\frac{\left(0.0152 \frac{ m ^{3}}{ kg }\right)\left(22.09 \times 10^{6} \frac{ N }{ m ^{2}}\right)}{\left(\frac{8314}{18.02} \frac{ N \cdot m }{ kg \cdot K }\right)(647.3 K )}=1.12\)

 

At state 2

 

\(T_{ R 2}=\frac{673}{647.3}=1.04\)

 

Locating the point on the compressibility chart where \(v_{ R }^{\prime}=1.12\) and \(T_{ R }=1.04\), the corresponding value for \(p_{ R }\) is about 0.69. Accordingly

 

\(p_{2}=p_{c}\left(p_{ R 2}\right)=(22.09 MPa )(0.69)=15.24 MPa\)

 

Interpolating in the superheated vapor tables gives \(p_{2}=15.16\) MPa. As before, the compressibility chart value is in good agreement with the table value.

 

1 Absolute temperature and absolute pressure must be used in evaluating the compressibility factor Z, the reduced temperature \(T_{ R }\), and reduced pressure \(P_{ R }\).

2 Since Z is unitless, values for p, υ, R, and T must be used in consistent units.
