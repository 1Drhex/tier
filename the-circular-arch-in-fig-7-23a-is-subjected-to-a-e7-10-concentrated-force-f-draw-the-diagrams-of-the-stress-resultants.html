Guest Question.

The circular arch in Fig. 7.23a is subjected to a E7.10 concentrated force F.

Draw the diagrams of the stress resultants.

Admin
Solution

The reactions at the supports follow from the equilibrium conditions for the the entire arch (Fig. 7.23b):

\(B = \frac{1}{4}F , A_{V}=-B=-\frac{1}{4}F ,A_{H}=F.\)

We introduce the dashed line and cut the arch at an arbitrary position φ in region I (0 < φ < 30°). The free-body diagram of the corresponding part of the arch is shown in Fig. 7.23c. The equilibrium conditions lead to the stress resultants:

\(\nearrow : N + A_{V}\cos \varphi -A_{H} \sin \varphi =0\) \(\rightarrow \underline{\underline{N} } =(\sin \varphi +\frac{1}{4} \cos \varphi )F ,\)

 

\(\searrow : V – A_{V}\sin \varphi -A_{H} \cos \varphi =0\) \(\rightarrow \underline{\underline{V} } =( \cos \varphi-\frac{1}{4} \sin \varphi )F ,\)

 

\(\overset{\curvearrowleft }{C} : M – r\sin \varphi A_{H} -r(1-\cos \varphi)A_{V} =0\) \(\rightarrow \underline{\underline{M} } =( \sin\varphi+\frac{1}{4} \cos \varphi-\frac{1}{4} )rF.\)

Since the arch is sectioned at an arbitrary position φ, these equations describe the variations of the stress resultants in region I. Similarly, introducing the angle ψ = π −φ, the stress resultants in region II (30° < φ < 180°) are obtained from the equilibrium conditions for the free-body diagram in Fig. 7.23d:

\(\nwarrow : N + B\cos \psi =0\) \(\rightarrow \underline{\underline{N} } =-\frac{1}{4}F\cos \psi =\frac{1}{4}F\cos\varphi ,\)

 

\(\nearrow : V + B\sin \psi =0\) \(\rightarrow \underline{\underline{V} } =-\frac{1}{4}F\sin \psi =-\frac{1}{4}F\sin \varphi,\)

 

\(\overset{\curvearrowleft }{C} : -M +r(1-\cos\psi )B =0\) \(\rightarrow \underline{\underline{M} } =\frac{1}{4}( 1-\cos \psi)rF=\frac{1}{4}( 1+\cos\varphi )rF.\)

The stress resultants are drawn perpendicularly to the axis of the arch in Fig. 7.23e. The jumps ΔN = F/2 in the normal force and ΔV =\(\sqrt{3} \)F/2 in the shear force at φ= 30° are equal to the components of F tangential and orthogonal to the arch, respectively.
