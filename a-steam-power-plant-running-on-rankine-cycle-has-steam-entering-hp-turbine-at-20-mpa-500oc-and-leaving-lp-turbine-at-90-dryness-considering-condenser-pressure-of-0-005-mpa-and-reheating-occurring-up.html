Guest Question.

A steam power plant running on Rankine cycle has steam entering HP turbine at 20 MPa, 500ºC and leaving LP turbine at 90% dryness. Considering condenser pressure of 0.005 MPa and reheating occurring upto the temperature of 500ºC determine,
(a) the pressure at wich steam leaves HP turbine
(b) the thermal efficiency

Admin
Solution

Let us assume that the condensate leaves condenser as saturated liquid and the expansion in turbine and pumping processes are isentropic.
From steam tables,\(\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad h_{2}&= h_{\text{at 20 MPa, 500ºC}} = 3238.2 kJ/kg\\s_{2}&= 6.1401 kJ/kg · K\\h_{5}&= h_{\text{at 0.005 MPa, 0.90 dry}}\\h_{5}&= h_{\text{ƒ at 0.005 MPa,}} + 0.9 × h_{\text{ƒg at 0.005 MPa}}\\&= 137.82 + (0.9 × 2423.7)\\h_{5}&= 2319.15 kJ/kg\\s_{5}&= s_{\text{ƒ at 0.005 MPa,}} + 0.9 × s_{\text{ƒg at 0.005 MPa}}\\&= 0.4764 + (0.9 × 7.9187)\\s_{5}&= 7.6032 kJ/kg · K\\h_{6}&= h_{\text{ƒ at 0.005 MPa}} = 137.82 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\)It is given that temperature at state 4 is 500ºC and due to isentropic process \(s_{4} = s_{5} = 7.6032 kJ/kg ·K.\)The state 4 can be conveniently located on Mollier chart by the intersection of 500ºC constant temperature line and entropy value of 7.6032 kJ/kg · K and the pressure and enthalpy obtained. But these shall be approximate.
The state 4 can also be located by interpolation using steam table. The entropy value of 7.6032 kJ kg · K lies between the superheated steam states given under, p = 1.20 MPa,\(s_{\text{at 1.20 MPa and 500ºC}} = 7.6759 kJ/kg · K\\p = 1.40 MPa, s_{\text{at 1.40 MPa and 500ºC}} = 7.6027 kJ/kg · K\\\begin{aligned}\text{By interpolation state 4 lies at pressure}& \\&= 1.20 + \frac{(1.40 – 1.20)}{(7.6027 – 7.6759)}(7.6032 – 7.6759)\\&= 1.399 MPa ≈ 1.40 MPa\end{aligned}\\\text{Thus, steam leaves HP turbine at 1.4 MPa}\\\quad \qquad \qquad \qquad \text{Enthalpy at state 4, }h_{4} = 3474.1 kJ/kg\\\text{For process 2-3, }s_{2} = s_{3} = 6.1401 kJ/kg · K.\text{ The state 3 thus lies in wet region as }s_{3} < s_{\text{g at 1.40 MPa}}.\\\text{Let dryness fraction at state 3 be }x_{3}.\\\begin{aligned}s_{3}&= s_{\text{ƒ at 1.4 MPa}} + x_{3} · s_{\text{fg at 1.4 MPa}}\\6.1401&= 2.2842 + x_{3} · 4.1850\\x_{3}&= 0.9214\\h_{3}&= h_{\text{ƒ at 1.4 MPa}} + x_{3} · h_{\text{ƒg at 1.4 MPa}}\\&= 830.3 + (0.9214 × 1959.7) = 2635.97 kJ/kg\\\text{Enthalpy at 1, }h_{1}&= h_{6} + v_{6}(p_{1} – p_{6})\\&= h_{\text{ƒ at 0.005 MPa}} + v_{\text{ƒ at 0.005 MPa}} (20 – 0.005) × 10^{3}\\&= 137.82 + (0.001005 × 19.995 × 10^{3})\\h_{1}&= 157.91 kJ/kg\\\text{Net work per kg of steam}&= (h_{2} – h_{3}) + (h_{4} – h_{5}) – (h_{1} – h_{6})\\&= 1737.09 kJ/kg\\\text{Heat added per kg of steam}&= (h_{2} – h_{1}) = 3080.29 kJ/kg\\\text{Thermal efficiency}&= \frac{\text{Net work}}{\text{Heat added}} = \frac{1737.09}{3080.29} = \text{0.5639 or 56.39 }\%\\\text{Pressure of steam leaving HP turbine}&= \textbf{1.40 MPa}\\\text{Thermal efficiency}&= \textbf{56.39 }\pmb{\%}\end{aligned}\)
