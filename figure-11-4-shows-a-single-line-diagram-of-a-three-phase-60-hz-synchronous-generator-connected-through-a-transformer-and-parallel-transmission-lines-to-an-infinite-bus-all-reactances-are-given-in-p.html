Generator internal voltage and real power output versus power angle

Figure 11.4 shows a single-line diagram of a three-phase, 60-Hz synchronous generator, connected through a transformer and parallel transmission lines to an infinite bus. All reactances are given in per-unit on a common system base. If the infinite bus receives 1.0 per unit real power at 0.95 p.f. lagging, determine (a) the internal voltage of the generator and (b) the equation for the electrical power delivered by the generator versus its power angle δ.

Admin
Solution

a. The equivalent circuit is shown in Figure 11.5, from which the equivalent reactance between the machine internal voltage and infinite bus is

\(X_{eq} =X^{\prime }_{d} +X_{TR }+X_{12}\parallel \left(X_{13}+X_{23}\right) =0.30+0.10+0.20\parallel\left(0.10+0.20\right) =0.520\) per unit

The current into the infinite bus is

\(I=\frac{P}{V_{bus}\left(P.f.\right) } \angle -\cos ^{-1} \left(P.f.\right) =\frac{\left(1.0\right) }{\left(1.0\right)\left(0.95\right) }\angle -\cos ^{-1} 0.95=1.05263\angle -18.195°\) per unit

and the machine internal voltage is

\(E^{\prime }= E^{\prime }\angle \delta =V_{bus}+jX_{eq} I=1.0\angle 0°+\left(j0.520\right)\left(1.05263\angle -18.195°\right)=1.0\angle 0°+0.54737\angle 71.805°=1.1709+j0.5200=1.2812\angle 23.946°\) per unit

b. From (11.2.1),

\(P_{e} =\frac{E^{\prime } V_{bus} }{X_{eq} } \sin \delta\) (11.2.1)

\(P_{e} =\frac{\left(1.2812\right)\left(1.0\right) }{0.520} \sin \delta =2.4628\sin \delta \) per unit
