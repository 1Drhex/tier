Guest Question.

Determine \(G_{p}(s)\) for temperature \( T^{\prime}\) and input \(Q^{\prime}\) for using Equations
\(G_{p}(s)\triangleq C[sI-A]^{-1}B \) (1)
and
\(G_{d}(s)\triangleq C[sI-A]^{-1}E \) (2)
and the MATLAB command ss2tf.

Admin
Solution

For part (a) of , \(Y(s) = X_{1}(s)\), and there is one manipulated variable and no disturbance variable. Consequently, Equation
\(Y(s)=G_{p}(s)U(s)+G_{d}(s)D(s)\)
reduces to
\(Y(s)=C(sI-A)^{-1}BU(s)\)
where \(G_{p}(s)\) is now a scalar transfer function.
To find the multivariable transfer function for \(T^{\prime}(s)/Q^{\prime}(s)\), we use the following matrices from the state-space model Equation
\(\begin{bmatrix} T^{\prime} \\ T^{\prime}_{e} \end{bmatrix} =\begin{bmatrix} -0.2 & 0.1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} T^{\prime} \\ T^{\prime}_{e} \end{bmatrix}+\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0.5 \end{bmatrix}Q^{\prime}\) :

\(A=\begin{bmatrix} -0.2 & 0.1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \quad B=\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0.05 \end{bmatrix} \quad C=\begin{bmatrix} 1 &0 \end{bmatrix}\)

Using the MATLAB command ss2tf yields
\(G_{p}(s)=\frac{0.005}{S^{2}+1.2s+0.1} \)
which is the same result as in Equation
\( T^{\prime}(t)=20[1-1.089e^{-0.09t}+0.0884e^{-1.11t}]\) .
The reader can also derive \(G_{d}(s) \) relating \(T^{\prime}(s) \space and \space T^{\prime}_{i}(s)\) using the matrix-based
approach in Equation (2).
It is also possible to convert a general transfer function matrix in the form of Equation (1) to a state-space model, and vice versa, using a single command in MATLAB. Using such software is recommended when the state matrix is larger than 2 × 2.
