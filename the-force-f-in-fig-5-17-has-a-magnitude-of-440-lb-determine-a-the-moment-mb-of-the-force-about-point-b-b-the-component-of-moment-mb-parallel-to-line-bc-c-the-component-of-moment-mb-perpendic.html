Guest Question.

The force F in Fig. 5-17 has a magnitude of 440 lb. Determine
(a) The moment \(M_B \) of the force about point B.
(b) The component of moment \(M_B \) parallel to line BC.
(c) The component of moment \(M_B \) perpendicular to line BC.
(d) The unit vector associated with the component of moment \(M_B \) perpendicular to line BC.

Admin
Solution

(a) The force F and the position vector r from point B to point A can be written in Cartesian vector form as

\(F=440[(6/11)i+(7/11)j-(6/11)k]=(240i+280j-240k)lb\)

 

\(r_{A/B}=(3i+13j)ft\)

The moment MB is given by

\(M_O=r\times F=\left|\begin{matrix} i & j &k \\ r_x & r_y & r_z \\ F_x & F_y & F_z \end{matrix} \right| =(r_yF_z-r_zF_y)i+(r_zF_x-r_xF_z)j+(r_xF_y-r_yF_x)k=M_{Ox}i+M_{Oy}j+M_{Oz}k\) as

\(M_B=r_{A/B} \times F=\left|\begin{matrix} i & j &k \\ r_x & r_y & r_z \\ F_x & F_y & F_z \end{matrix} \right| = \left|\begin{matrix} i & j &k \\ 3 & 13 & 0 \\ 240 & 280 & -240 \end{matrix} \right|\)

 

\(=(-3120i+720j-2280k)lb.ft\)

(b) The unit vector \(e_{BC}\) associated with line BC is

\(e_{BC}=-0.60i+0.80j\)

The component of moment \(M_B\) parallel to line BC is given by

\(M_{OB}=M_{||}=(M_O.e_n)e_n=[(r \times F).e_n]e_n=M_{OB}e_n\) as

\(M_{BC}=M_{||}=(M_B.e_{BC})e_{BC}=M_{BC}e_{BC}\)

 

\(M_{BC}=M_B.e_{BC}\)

 

\(=(-3120i+720j-2280k).(-0.60i+0.80j)\)

 

\(=(-3120)(-0.60)+(720)(0.80)=2448lb.ft\)

 

\(M_{BC}=M_{BC}e_{BC}\)

 

\(=2448(-0.60i+0.80j)\)

 

\(=(1469i+1958j)lb.ft\)

Alternatively, the moment MBC can be determined in a single operation by using

\(M_{OB}=M_O.e_n=(r\times F).e_n=\left|\begin{matrix} e_{nx} & e_{ny} & e_{nz} \\ r_x & r_y & r_z \\ F_x & F_y & F_z \end{matrix} \right| \) .

A different point on the line can also be used. For example, consider point C and the position vector \(r_{D/C}\)

\(r_{D/C}=(2j+6k)ft\)

From \(M_{OB}=M_O.e_n=(r\times F).e_n=\left|\begin{matrix} e_{nx} & e_{ny} & e_{nz} \\ r_x & r_y & r_z \\ F_x & F_y & F_z \end{matrix} \right| \):

\(M_{BC}=\left|\begin{matrix} e_{nx} & e_{ny} & e_{nz} \\ r_x & r_y & r_z \\ F_x & F_y & F_z \end{matrix} \right| = \left|\begin{matrix} -0.60 & 0.80 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 6 \\ 240 & 280 & -240 \end{matrix} \right|\)

 

\(=(-0.60)(-2160)-(0.80)(-1440)=2448lb.ft\)

(c) The moment \(M_⊥\) is obtained as the difference between \(M_B\) and \(M_{||}\) since \(M_{||}\) and \(M_⊥\) are the two rectangular components of \(M_B\). Thus,

\(M_⊥=M_B-M_{||}=M_B-M_{BC}\)

 

\(=(-3120i+720j-2280k)-(-1469i+1958j)\)

 

\(=(-1651i-1238j-2280k)lb.ft\)

(d) The magnitude of moment \(M_⊥\) is

\(|M_⊥|=\sqrt{(-1651)^2+(-1238)^2+(-2280)^2}=3075lb.ft\)

Therefore

\(e_⊥=(-1651/3075)i+(-1238/3075)j+(-2280/3075)k\)

 

\(=-0.537i-0.403j-0.741k\)

As a check:

\(e_{||}.e_⊥=(-0.60i+0.80j).(-0.5369i-0.4026j-0.7415k)\)

 

\(=0.00006≅0\)

which verifies, except for round-off in the expression for \(e_⊥\), that the moment components \(M_{||}\) and \( M_⊥\) are perpendicular.
