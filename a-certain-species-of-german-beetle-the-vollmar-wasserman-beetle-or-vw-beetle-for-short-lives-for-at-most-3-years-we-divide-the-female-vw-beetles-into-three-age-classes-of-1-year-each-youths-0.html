Guest Question.

A certain species of German beetle, the Vollmar-Wasserman beetle (or VW beetle, for short), lives for at most 3 years. We divide the female VW beetles into three age classes of 1 year each: youths (0-1 year), juveniles (1-2 years), and adults (2-3 years). The youths do not lay eggs; each juvenile produces an average of four female beetles; and each adult produces an average of three females.

 

The survival rate for youths is \(50 \%\) (that is, the probability of a youth’s surviving to become a juvenile is \(0.5\) ), and the survival rate for juveniles is \(25 \% .\) Suppose we begin with a population of 100 female VW beetles: 40 youths, 40 juveniles, and 20 adults. Predict the beetle population for each of the next 5 years.

Admin
Solution

After 1 year, the number of youths will be the number produced during that year:

\( 40 \times 4+20 \times 3=220 \)

The number of juveniles will simply be the number of youths that have survived:

\( 40 \times 0.5=20 \)

Likewise, the number of adults will be the number of juveniles that have survived:

\( 40 \times 0.25=10 \)

We can combine these into a single matrix equation

\(\left[\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 4 & 3 \\ 0.5 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0.25 & 0 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} 40 \\ 40 \\ 20 \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c} 220 \\ 20 \\ 10 \end{array}\right] \)

or \(L \mathbf{x}_{0}=\mathbf{x}_{1}\), where \(\mathbf{x}_{0}=\left[\begin{array}{l}40 \\ 40 \\ 20\end{array}\right]\) is the initial population distribution vector and \(\mathbf{x}_{1}=\left[\begin{array}{r}220 \\ 20 \\ 10\end{array}\right]\) is the distribution after 1 year. We see that the structure of the equation is exactly the same as for Markov chains: \(\mathbf{x}_{k+1}=L \mathbf{x}_{k}\) for \(k=0,1,2, \ldots\) (although the interpretation is quite different). It follows that we can iteratively compute successive population distribution vectors. (It also follows that \(\mathbf{x}_{k}=L^{k} \mathbf{x}_{0}\) for \(k=0,1,2, \ldots\), as for Markov chains, but we will not use this fact here.)

We compute

\( \begin{aligned} &\mathbf{x}_{2}=L \mathbf{x}_{1}=\left[\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 4 & 3 \\ 0.5 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0.25 & 0 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{r} 220 \\ 20 \\ 10 \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{r} 110 \\ 110 \\ 5 \end{array}\right] \\ &\mathbf{x}_{3}=L \mathbf{x}_{2}=\left[\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 4 & 3 \\ 0.5 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0.25 & 0 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{r} 110 \\ 110 \\ 5 \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c} 455 \\ 55 \\ 27.5 \end{array}\right] \end{aligned}\) \( \begin{aligned} &\mathbf{x}_{4}=L \mathbf{x}_{3}=\left[\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 4 & 3 \\ 0.5 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0.25 & 0 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c} 455 \\ 55 \\ 27.5 \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c} 302.5 \\ 227.5 \\ 13.75 \end{array}\right] \\ &\mathbf{x}_{5}=L \mathbf{x}_{4}=\left[\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 4 & 3 \\ 0.5 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0.25 & 0 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c} 302.5 \\ 227.5 \\ 13.75 \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c} 951.2 \\ 151.2 \\ 56.88 \end{array}\right] \end{aligned} \)

Therefore, the model predicts that after 5 years there will be approximately 951 young female VW beetles, 151 juveniles, and 57 adults. (Note: You could argue that we should have rounded to the nearest integer at each step-for example, 28 adults after step 3 -which would have affected the subsequent iterations. We elected not to do this, since the calculations are only approximations anyway and it is much easier to use a calculator or CAS if you do not round as you go.)
