Guest Question.

Test with Respect to an Identical PDF

Let two hypotheses for the scalar RV x:

\(H_{0}:f(x)=f_{0}(x),\)

\(H_{1}:f(x)=f_{1}(x),\)

where \(f_{0}\) and \(f_{1}\) represent two PDFs, as illustrated in Figure 10.25.

Objective: Derive the MP-LRT.

Admin
Solution

The LRT is expressed as:

\(\Lambda (x)=\frac{f_{1}(x)}{f_{0}(x)}^{\begin{matrix} H_{1} \\ > \end{matrix} }_{\begin{matrix} < \\ H_{0} \end{matrix} } \eta ,\)

or, alternatively:

\(f_{1}(x)^{\begin{matrix} H_{1} \\ > \end{matrix} }_{\begin{matrix} < \\ H_{0} \end{matrix} } \eta f_{0}(x).\)

In Figure 10.26, it is obvious that for a conditional n, the decision area \(H_{1}\) is:

\(\chi _{1}=\begin{cases} \left\{\eta /4<x<1-\eta /4\right\}, & 0\leq \eta \leq 2 \\ \varnothing & otherwise \end{cases} \)

In order to choose the appropriate threshold η, we must consider the constraint \(P_{F} = a.\)

At first, we try to set a value to η without randomization (q = 0). Therefore, we consider η ∈ [0,2]:

\(a=P(x\in \chi _{1}|H_{0})=\int\limits_{\eta /4}^{1-\eta /4}{f_{0}(x)dx}\)
\(=1-\eta /2.\)

Thus, by solving with respect to η, it yields:

\( \eta= 2(1− a),\)

and, therefore, no randomization is required.
The MP-LRT power (probability of detection) is expressed as:

\(P_{D}=P(x\in \chi _{1}|H_{1})=\int\limits_{\eta /4}^{1-\eta /4}{f_{1}(x)dx}\)
\(=2\int\limits_{\eta /4}^{1/2}{f_{1}(x)dx}= 2\int\limits_{\eta /4}^{1/2}{4x dx}\)
\(=1-\eta ^{2}/4\)

By substituting η = 2(1 – a) in the above relation, the ROC curve is derived:

\(\beta =1-(1-a)^{2}\)
