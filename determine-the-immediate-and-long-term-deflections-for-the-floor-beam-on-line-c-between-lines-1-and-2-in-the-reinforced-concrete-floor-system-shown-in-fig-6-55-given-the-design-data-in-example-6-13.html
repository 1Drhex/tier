Guest Question.

Determine the immediate and long-term deflections for the floor beam on line C between lines 1 and 2 in the reinforced concrete floor system shown in Fig. 6.55, given the design data in . Assume that the beam is not supporting or attached to nonstructural elements likely to be damaged by large deflections. Also assume that 30% of the live load is sustained and that the beam is reinforced with five No 9 bars at the midspan and five No. 10 bars at the interior support.

Admin
Solution

Step 1: Determine the service loads and bending moments.

\(w_{D} =[(\frac{8}{12} × 150)+ 30]× 15 +(\frac {18 × 16}{144 × 150}= 2,250\) plf
\(w_{L} = 100 × 15 = 1,500\) plf

The approximate moment coefficients given in ACI 8.3.3 are used to determine the service bending moments (see Fig. 3.17). The bending moments at the external support are not required for the calculation of the effective moment of inertia \(I_{e}\) for a member that is continuous on one end [see Eqs. (6.69) and (6.72)].

At the midspan,

\(M^{+}_{D} =\frac{\omega_{D} \ell^{2}_{n}}{14} =\frac{2.25\times 33.5^{2}}{14} =180.4\) ft kips

\(M^{+}_{L} =\frac{\omega_{L} \ell^{2}_{n}}{14} =\frac{1.50\times 33.5^{2}}{14} =120.2\) ft kips

Sustained bending moment \( M^{+}_{sus}= M^{+}_{D} +0.3M^{+}_ {L}=180.4+ (0.3 \times 120.22 )=216.5\)

At the exterior face of the first interior support,

\(M^{-}_{D} =\frac{\omega_{D} \ell^{2}_{n}}{10} =\frac{2.25\times 33.5^{2}}{10} =252.5\) ft kips

\(M^{-}_{L} =\frac{\omega_{L} \ell^{2}_{n}}{10} =\frac{1.50\times 33.5^{2}}{10} =168.3\) ft kips

Sustained bending moment \(M^{-}_{sus}= M^{-}_{D} +0.3M^{-}_ {L}=252.5+(0.3 \times 168.3 )=303.0\)

Step 2: Determine the material properties of the concrete and the steel.

For normal-weight concrete, \(f_{r} = 7.5 λ\sqrt{ f '_{c}} = 7.5 × 1.0\sqrt{4,000} = 474\) psi

From ACI 8.5.1,\( E_{c} = 57,000\sqrt{ f '_{c}}= 57,000 \sqrt{4,000} = 3,605,000\) psi
From ACI 8.5.2, \(E_{s} = 29,000,000\) psi
The modular ratio \(n = E_{s}/E_{c} = 8.0\)

Step 3: Determine the gross and cracked moments of inertia. Determine the effective width of the flange be from Fig. 5.23:

\(b_{e}=\begin{cases}span length/4 = 35 × 12/4 = 105.0 in (governs)\\b_{w} + 16hf = 18 + (16 × 8.0) = 146.0 in\\beam spacing = 15 × 12 = 180 in\end{cases}\)

Positive moment section: The gross section at the positive moment section at the midspan is depicted in Fig. 6.63.
The gross section properties are (see Fig. 6.61)

\(y_{t}=\frac{(b_{e}-b_{w})(h-0.5h_{f}) h_{f}+ 0.5 b_{w}h^{2}}{(b_{e}-b_{w})h_{f}b_{w}h} =\frac {(105-118)\times[24-(0.5\times 8)]\times 8+(0.5 \times 18 \times 24^{2})}{[(105-18)\times 8]+(18 \times 24)} =16.9\) in

\(I_{g}=\frac{1}{12}(b_{e}-b_{w})h^{3}_{f}+ (b_{e}-b_{w})h_{f}(h-0.5h_{f}-y_{t})^{2}+\frac{1}{12}b_{w}h^{3}+b_{w}h(y_{t}0.5h)^{2}\).

\(=\frac{1}{12}(105-18)(8)^{3}+(105-18)(8)(24-4-16.9)^{2}+\frac{1}{12}(18)(24)^{3}+(18)(24)(16.9-12)^{2}\).

\(=41,509 in^{4}\).

Assuming a rectangular compression area, the cracked section properties are the following (see Figs. 6.61 and 6.64):

\(a_{1}=\frac{b_{e}}{nA_{s}}=\frac{105}{8 \times (5\times 1.0)}=2.6\)/in

\(k d=\frac{\sqrt{2 d a_{1}+1}-1}{a_{1}}=\frac{\sqrt{(2 \times 21.5 \times 2.6)+1}-1}{2.6}=3.7 \text { in }<h_{f}=8.0 \text { in }\).

Therefore, the assumption of a rectangular compression area is correct.

\(I_{cr}=\frac{b_{e}(kd)^{3}}{3} +nA_{s}(d-kd)^{2}\)

=\(\frac{105\times 3.7^{3}}{3} +8 \times 5(21.5-3.7)^{2}=14,447 in^{4}\)

Negative moment section: The negative moment section is the 18 × 24 in web. Thus,

\(I_{g}=\frac{1}{12}(b_{e}-b_{w})h^{3}=\frac{1}{12}\times 18 \times24^{3}=20,736 in^{4}\)

\(a_{1}=\frac{b_{w}}{nA_{s}}=\frac{18}{8 \times (5\times 1.27)}=0.35\)/in

\(kd=\frac{\sqrt{2da_{1}+1}-1 }{a_{1}} =\frac{\sqrt{(2\times 21.5 \times 0.35)+1}-1 }{0.35} =8.6\) in

\(I_{cr}=\frac{b_{w}}{kd}^{3}+nA_{s}(d-kd)^ {2}=\frac{18\times 8.6^{3}}{3}+8 \times 6.35 \times(21.5-8.6)^{2} =12,270 in^{4}\)

Step 4: Determine the effective moments of inertia.
Positive moment section: The cracking moment is determined by Eq. (6.65):

\(M_{cr}=\frac{f_{r}I_{g}}{y_{t}}=\frac{474 \times 41,509}{16.9 \times 12,000} =97.0\)ft kips

The effective moment of inertia \(I_{e} \) is determined by Eq. (6.69) for dead, sustained, and dead plus live load cases:

\(\frac{M_{cr}}{M^{+}_{D} }=\frac{97.0}{180.4} =0.54\)

\((I_{e})^{+}_{D}= \left(\frac{M_{cr}}{M^{+}_{D} }\right) ^{3}I_{g}+\left[1-\left(\ frac{ M_{cr}}{M^{ +}_ {D} }\right) ^{3}\right] I_{cr}\)

=\([(0.54)^{3}\times 41,509]+[1-(0.54)^{3}]\times 14.447=18,708 in^{4}< I_{g}\)

\(\frac{M_{cr}}{M^{+}_{sus} }=\frac{97.0}{216.5}= 0.45\)

\((I_{e})^{+}_{sus}=\left(\frac{M_{cr}}{M^{+}_{sus} }\right)^{3}I_{g}+\left[1-\left(\frac{M_{cr}}{M^{+}_ {sus} }\right)^{3}\right] I_{cr}\)

=\([(0.45)^{3}\times 41,509]+[1-(0.45)^{3}]\times 14,447=16,91 in^{4}< I_{g}\)

\(\frac{M_{cr}}{M^{+}_{D+L} }=\frac{97.0}{300.6}=0.32\)

\((I_{e})^{+}_{D+L}=\left(\frac{M_{cr}}{M^{+} _{ D+L} }\right) ^{3}I_{g}+\left[1-\left(\frac{M_{cr}}{M^{+}_{D+L} }\right) ^{3}\right] I_{cr}\)

\([(0.32)^{3}\times41,509]+[1-(0.32)^{3}] \times 14,447 =15,334 in^{4} < I_{g}\)

Negative moment section:

\(M_{cr}=\frac{f_{r}I_{g}}{y_{t}}=\frac {474\times 20,736}{12\times 12,000}=68.3\) ft kips

\(\frac{M_{cr}}{M^{-}_{D}}=\frac{68.3}{303.0}=0.23 \)

\((I_{e})^{-}_{sus}=\left(\frac{M_{cr}}{M^{-}_{sus} }\right) ^{3}I_{g}+\left[1-\left(\frac {M_{cr}}{M^{-}_{sus} }\right) ^{3}\right] I_{cr}\)

=\([(0.23)^{3}\times20,736]+[1-(0.23)^{3}] \times 12,270 =12,373 in^{4} < I_{g}\)

\(\frac{M_{cr}}{M^{-}_{D+L}}=\frac{68.3}{420.8}=0.16\)

\((I_{e})^{-}_{D+L}=\left(\frac{M_{cr}}{M^{-}_{D+L} }\right) ^{3}I_{g}+\left[1-\left(\frac{M_{cr}}{M^{-}_{D+L} }\right) ^{3}\right] I_{cr}\)

=\([(0.16)^{3}\times 20,736]+[1-(0.16)^{3}] \times 12,270 =12,305 in^{4}<I_{g}\)

Average effective moments of inertia: The average effective moments of inertia are obtained from
Eq. (6.72) for one end continuous:

\((I_{e})_{D}=0.85(I_{e})^{+}_{D}+ 0.15(I_{ e } )^{-}_{D}=( 0.85\times18,708)+(0.15\times 12,437 ) = 17,767 in^{4}\)
\((I_{e})_{sus}=0.85(I_{e})^{+}_{sus}+0.15(I_ {e})^{-}_{sus}=(8.85\times16,913)+(0.15\times 12,373) = 16,232 in^{4}\)
\((I_{e})_{D+L}=0.85(I_{e})^{+}_{D+L}+ 0.15(I _{e})^{-}_{D+L}=(.85\times 15,334)+(0.15\times 12,305)=14,880 in^{4}\)

Step 5: Determine the immediate deflections and check the maximum permissible deflection for live load. The immediate deflections i are determined using Eq. (6.73) and Table 6.7:

\(\Delta i=\frac{5KM_{a}\ell^{2}}{48E_{c} I_{e}}\)

For continuous members,

\(K = 1.2 − 0.2(M_{o}/M_{a} )\)

where \(M_{o} = w\ell^{2}/8\)
\(M_{a} = w\ell^{2}/14\)

Thus, K = 1.2 − 0.2(14/8) = 0.85.

\((\Delta i)_{D}=\frac{5KM^{+}_{D}\ell^{2}}{48E_{c}(I_{e})_{D}}=\frac{5 \times 0.85\times 180.4 \times 12,000\times(33.5\times12)^{2} }{48\times 3,605,000 \times 17,767 }=0.48\) in

\((\Delta i)_{sus}=\frac{5KM^{+}_{sus}\ell^{2}}{48E_{c}(I_{e})_{sus}}=\frac{5 \times 0.85\times 216.5 \times12,000\times(33.5\times12)^{2} }{48 \times 3,605,000 \times16,232 }=06.4\) in

\((\Delta i)_{D+L}=\frac{5KM^{+}_{D+L}\ell^{2}}{48E _{c}(I_{e})_{D+L}}=\frac{5 \times 0.85\times 300.6 \times12,000\times(33.5\times12)^{2} }{48\times 3,605,000 \times 14,880 }=0.96\) in.

\(\left(\Delta_{i}\right)_{L}=\left(\Delta_{i}\right)_{D+L}-\left(\Delta_{i}\right)_{D}=0.96-0.48=0.48 \text { in }\).

For a floor member that is not supporting or attached to nonstructural elements likely to be damaged by large deflections, the maximum permissible immediate live load deflection from ACI
Table 9.5(b) is \(\ell/360 = 33.5 × 12/360 = 1.1\) in > 0.48 in.

Step 6: Determine the long-term deflections and check the maximum permissible deflection.
Determine the factor \(λ_{\Delta}\) by Eq. (6.74) and Table 6.8, assuming a 5-year duration of loading:

\(\Delta _{cs}=\lambda_{\Delta }(\Delta _{i})_{sus}= 2.0\times0.64=1.3\) in

\(\lambda _{\Delta }=\frac{\xi }{1+50\acute{\rho } } =\frac{2.0}{1+0}=2.0\)

The long-term deflection due to creep and shrinkage cs is determined by Eq. (6.75), using the immediate deflection for the sustained loads:

The sum of the long-term deflection due to the sustained loads and the immediate deflection due to any additional live load is 1.3 + 0.48 = 1.8 in.
For a floor member that is not supporting or attached to nonstructural elements likely to be damaged by large deflections, the maximum permissible deflection from ACI Table 9.5(b) is \(\ell/240 =33.5 × 12/240 = 1.7 \)in < 1.8 in.

Comments
Generally, the most efficient way to decrease deflections is to increase the depth of a member.
However, this may not always be possible because of architectural or other considerations.
In this case, because the long-term deflection due to the sustained loads plus the immediate deflection due to any additional live load are slightly larger than the permissible value, increasing the width of the beam and the compressive strength of the concrete are two viable options for decreasing deflections. Also, using compressive reinforcement would decrease the factor \(λ_{\delta}\) and the long-term deflection \(\delta _{cs}\).
