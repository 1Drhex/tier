Guest Question.

A pair of 20째 pressure angle gears in mesh has the following data:
Speed of pinion = 400 rpm
Number of teeth on pinion = 24
Number of teeth on gear = 28
Module = 10 mm
Determine the addendum of the gears if the path of approach and recess is half the maximum value.
Determine also the arc of contact and the maximum velocity of sliding between the mating surfaces.

Admin
Solution

Given: \( z_{1}=24, z_{2}=28, m=10 mm , h_{a}=?, a=20^{\circ}, n_{1}=400 rpm \).

\( r_{1}=\frac{m z_{1}}{2}=10 \times \frac{24}{2}=120 mm , r_{2}=10 \times \frac{28}{2}=140 mm \).

\( r_{b 1}=r_{1} \cos a=120 \cos 20^{\circ}=112.76 mm , r_{b 2}=140 \cos 20^{\circ}=131.56 mm \).

\( r_{a 1}=r_{1}+h_{a 1}=120+h_{a 1}, r_{a 2}=140+h_{a 2} \).

Let pinion be the driver,
Length of approach, \( L_{a}=0.5\left(L_{a}\right)_{\max } \).

\( \left(r_{a 2}^{2}-r_{b 2}^{2}\right)^{0.5}-r_{2} \sin \alpha=0.5 r_{1} \sin \alpha \).

\( \left[r_{a 2}^{2}-(131.56)^{2}\right]^{0.5}-140 \sin 20^{\circ}=0.5 \times 120 \times \sin 20^{\circ} \).

\( r_{a 2}^{2}-(131.56)^{2}=(200)^{2} \sin ^{2} 20^{\circ} \).

\( r_{a 2}^{2}=21987, r_{a 2}=148.3 mm \).

\( h_{a 2}=148.3-140=8.3 mm \).

Length of recess, \( L_{r}=0.5 \times\left(L_{r}\right)_{\max } \).

\( \left(r_{a 1}^{2}-r_{b 1}^{2}\right)^{0.5}-r_{1} \sin \alpha=0.5 r_{2} \sin \alpha \).

\( \left[r_{a 1}^{2}-(112.76)^{2}\right]^{0.5}-120 \sin 20^{\circ}=0.5 \times 140 \times \sin 20^{\circ} \).

\( r_{a 1}^{2}-(112.76)^{2}=(190)^{2} \sin ^{2} 20^{\circ} \).

\( r_{a 1}^{2}=16938, r_{a 1}=130.14 mm \).

\( h_{a 1}=130.14-120=10.14 mm \).

Arc of contact \( =\frac{L_{p}}{\cos \alpha}=0.5\left(r_{1}+r_{2}\right) \tan \alpha \).

\( =0.5(120+140) \tan 20^{\circ}=47.3 mm \).

\( L_{r}=\left(r_{a 1}^{2}-r_{b 1}^{2}\right)^{0.5}-r_{1} \sin a \).

Path of recess, \( =\left[(130.14)^{2}-(112.76)^{2}\right]^{0.5}-120 \sin 20^{\circ} \).

\( =23.93 mm \).

\( \omega_{1}=2 \pi \times \frac{400}{60}=41.88 rad / s \).

\(\omega_{2}=41.88 \times \frac{24}{28}=35.9 rad / s \).

\( \text { Maximum velocity of sliding }=\left(\omega_{1}+\omega_{2}\right) \times L_{r} \).

\( =(41.88+35.9) \times \frac{23.91}{1000}=1.86 m / s \).
