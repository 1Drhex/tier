Guest Question.

A heat exchanger like that shown in Figure is used to heat an oil in the tubes \((c = 1.9 kJ/kg · ^{\circ}C) \)from \(15^{\circ}C\) to \( 85^{\circ}C\). Blowing across the outside of the tubes is steam that enters at \( 130^{\circ}C\) and leaves at \(110^{\circ}C\) with a mass flow of 5.2 kg/sec. The overall heat-transfer coefficient is \(275 W/m^{2} ·^{\circ} C\) and c for steam is \(1.86 kJ/kg ·^ {\circ}C\). Calculate the surface area of the heat exchanger.

Admin
Solution

The total heat transfer may be obtained from an energy balance on the steam

\(q =\dot{m}_{s}c_{s}\Delta T_{s} = (5.2)(1.86)(130 − 110) = 193 kW\)

We can solve for the area from Equation \(q = UAF\Delta T_{m}\). The value of \(\Delta T_{m}\) is calculated as if the exchanger were counterflow double pipe (i.e., as shown in Figure ). Thus,

\( \Delta T_{m} = \frac{(130 − 85) − (110 − 15)}{ln\left(\frac{130 − 85}{110 − 15} \right) } = 66.9^{\circ }C \)

Now, from Figure,\( t_{1}\) and \( t_{2}\) will represent the unmixed fluid (the oil) and \( T_{1}\) and \( T_{2}\) will represent the mixed fluid (the steam) so that

\( T_{1} = 130 T_{2} = 110 t_{1} = 15 t_{2} = 85^{\circ}C \)

and we calculate

\( R = \frac{130 − 110}{85 − 15} = 0.286 P = \frac{85 − 15}{130 − 15} = 0.609\)

Consulting Figure we find

F = 0.97

so the area is calculated from

\( A = \frac{q}{UF \Delta T_{m}} =\frac{ 193,000}{(275)(0.97)(66.9)} = 10.82 m^{2} \)
