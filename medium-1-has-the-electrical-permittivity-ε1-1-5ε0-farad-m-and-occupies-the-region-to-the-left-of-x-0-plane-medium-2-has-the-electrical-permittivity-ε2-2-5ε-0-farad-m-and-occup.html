Guest Question.

Medium 1 has the electrical permittivity \(\varepsilon 1=1.5 \varepsilon_{0}\) farad/m and occupies the region to the left of x = 0 plane Medium 2 has the electrical permittivity \(\varepsilon_{2}=2.5 \varepsilon_{0}\) farad/m and occupies the region to the right of x = 0 plane. If E1 in medium 1 is \(E_{1}=\left(2 u_{x}-3 u_{y}+1 u_{z}\right)\) volt/m, then \(E_{2}\) in medium 2 is

(a) \(\left(2.0 u_{x}-7.5 u_{y}+2.5 u_{z}\right) volt / m\)

(b) \(\left(2-0 u_{x}-2.0 u_{y}+0.6 u_{z}\right) volt / m\)

(c) \(\left(1.2 u_{x}-3.0 u_{y}+1.0 u_{z}\right) volt / m\)

(d) \(\left(1.2 u_{x}-2.0 u_{y}+06 u_{z}\right) volt / m\)

Admin
Solution
\(\begin{aligned}&E_{t_{1}}=E_{t_{2}} \\&E_{t_{1}}=-3 u y+u z=E_{t_{2}} \\&\epsilon_{1} E_{n_{1}}=\epsilon_{2} E_{n_{2}} \\&1.5 \epsilon_{0} .2 u x=2.5 \epsilon_{0} E_{n_{2}} \\&E_{n_{2}}=1.2 u_{x} \\&\text { So, } E_{2}=1.2 u x-3 u y+u z\end{aligned}\)

Hence, the correct option is (c)
