Guest Question.

The bevel pinion shown in Fig. 11.19 rotates at 600 rev/min in the direction indicated and transmits 5 hp to the gear. The mounting distances are shown, together with the locations of the bearings on each shaft. Bearings A and C are capable of taking both radial and axial loads, whereas bearings B and D are designed to receive only radial loads. The teeth of the gears have a 20Â° pressure angle. Find the components of the forces that the bearings exert on the shafts in the x, y, and z directions.

Admin
Solution

The pitch angles for the pinion and gear are

 

\(\gamma=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{1.5 in }{4.5 in }\right)=18.4^{\circ}\)

 

\(\Gamma=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{4.5 in }{1.5 in }\right)=71.6^{\circ}\)

 

The radii to the midpoints of the teeth are shown on the drawing and are R2 = 1.293 in and R3 = 3.875 in for the pinion and gear, respectively.
Let us first determine the forces acting on the pinion. Using Eq. (13.13b), we find the transmitted force to be

\(F_{32}^{t}=\frac{P}{R_{2} \omega_{2}}=\frac{(5 hp )(33000 ft \cdot lb / min / hp )(12 in / ft )}{(1.293 in )(600 rev / min )(2 \pi rad / rev )}=406 lb\)

This force acts in the negative z direction, that is, into the plane of Fig. 11.19. The radial and axial components of F32 are obtained from Eqs. (11.20) and (11.21),

\(F^{r}=F^{t} \tan \phi \cos \gamma\) (11.20)

 

\(F^{a}=F^{t} \tan \phi \sin \gamma\) (11.21)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{32}^{r}=406 lb \tan 20^{\circ} \cos 18.4^{\circ}=140 lb \\&F_{32}^{a}=406 lb \tan 20^{\circ} \sin 18.4^{\circ}=46.6 lb\end{aligned}\)

 

where \( F _{32}^{r} \) acts in the positive y direction and \(F _{32}^{a}\) in the positive x direction. These three forces are components of the total force,F32 . Thus,

\(F _{32}=46.6 \hat{ i }+140 \hat{ j }-406 \hat{ k } lb\)

 

The torque applied to the pinion shaft must be

\(T _{12}=- R _{2} \times F _{32}^{t}=-(-1.293 \hat{ j } in ) \times(-406 \hat{ k } lb )=-525 \hat{ i } in \cdot lb\)

 

A free-body diagram of the pinion and shaft is shown schematically in Fig. 11.20a. The dimensions, torque T12, and force F32 are known, and the problem is to determine the bearing reactions FA and FB. To determine FB, we sum moments about A:

\(\sum M _{A}= T _{12}+ R _{B A} \times F _{B}+ R _{P A} \times F _{32}= 0\) (1)

where the two position-difference vectors are

\(R _{B A}=3.0 \hat{ i } \text { in } \quad \text { and } \quad R _{P A}=-2.625 \hat{ i }-1.293 \hat{ j } \text { in }\)

Therefore, the second and third terms of Eq. (1), respectively, are

\(\begin{aligned} R _{B A} \times F_{B} &=(3.0 in ) \hat{ i } \times\left(F_{B}^{y} \hat{ j }+F_{B}^{z} \hat{ k }\right) \\ &=-(3.0 \text { in }) F_{B}^{z} \hat{ j }+(3.0 \text { in }) F_{B}^{y} \hat{ k } \end{aligned} \) (2)

 

\( \begin{aligned} R _{P A} \times F _{32} &=(-2.625 \hat{ i }-1.293 \hat{ j } in ) \times(46.6 \hat{ i }+140 \hat{ j }-406 \hat{ k } lb ) \\ &=525 \hat{ i }-1066 \hat{ j }-307 \hat{ k } \text { in } \cdot lb . \end{aligned} \) (3)

 

Substituting the value of T12 and Eqs. (2) and (3) into Eq. (1) and solving, the bearing reaction at B is

\(F _{B}=102 \hat{ j }-355 \hat{ k } lb\)

The magnitude of the bearing reaction at B is 370 lb.

Next, to determine the bearing reaction at A, we write

\(\sum F = F _{32}+ F _{A}+ F _{B}= 0\)

Then, solving this equation, the bearing reaction at A is

\(F _{A}=-46.6 \hat{ i }-242 \hat{ j }+761 \hat{ k } lb\)

The magnitude of the bearing reaction at A is 798 lb. The results are shown in Fig. 11.20b.
A similar procedure is used for the gear shaft. The results are displayed in Fig. 11.20c.
