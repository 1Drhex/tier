Guest Question.

Find the current in R of Figure 19-1 using the superposition theorem. Assume the internal source impedances are zero.

Admin
Solution

Step 1: Replace \(V_{s2}\) with its internal impedance (zero in this case), and find the current in R due to \(V_{s1}\), as indicated in Figure 19-2.

\(X_{C1}= \frac{1}{2\pi fC_{1}}= \frac{1}{2\pi (10 \ kHz)(0.01 \ \mu F)}= 1.59 \ k\Omega\)

 

\(X_{C2}= \frac{1}{2\pi fC_{2}}= \frac{1}{2\pi (10 \ kHz)(0.022 \ \mu F)}= 723 \ \Omega\)

Looking from \(V_{s1}\),the impedance is

\(Z= X_{C1}+ \frac{RX_{C2}}{R+ X_{C2}}= 1.59\angle -90°k\Omega + \frac{(1.0\angle 0°k\Omega)(723\angle -90°\Omega)}{1.0 \ k\Omega – j 723 \ \Omega }\)

 

\(\ \ \ =1.59\angle -90°k\Omega + 588\angle -54.1°\Omega\)

 

\(\ \ \ =-j1.59 \ k\Omega + 345 \ \Omega – j476 \ \Omega = 345 \ \Omega – j2.07 \ k\Omega \)

Converting to polar form yields

\(Z=2.10\angle -80.5°k\Omega \)

The total current from \(V_{s1}\) is

\(I_{s1}= \frac{V_{s1}}{Z}= \frac{10\angle 0°V} {2.10\angle -80.5°k\Omega}= 4.76\angle 80.5°mA \)

Use the current-divider formula. The current through R due to \(V_{s1}\) is

\(I_{R1}= \left(\frac{X_{C2}\angle -90°}{R- jX_{C2}} \right)I_{s1}= \left(\frac{723\angle -90°}{1.0 \ k\Omega – j723 \ \Omega } \right)4.76\angle 80.5°mA\)

 

\(\ \ \ \ \ \ =(0.588 \angle -54.9°\Omega )(4.76\angle 80.5°mA)= 2.80 \angle 25.6°mA\)

Step 2: Find the current in R due to source \(V_{s2}\) by replacing \(V_{s1}\) with its internal impedance (zero), as shown in Figure 19-3.

Looking from \(V_{s2}\), the impedance is

\(Z= X_{C2}+ \frac{RX_{C1}}{R+ X_{C1}}= 723\angle -90°\Omega + \frac{(1.0\angle 0°k\Omega)(1.59\angle -90°k\Omega)}{1.0 \ k\Omega – j 1.59 \ k\Omega }\)

 

\(\ \ \ =723\angle -90°\Omega + 847\angle -32.2°\Omega\)

 

\(\ \ \ =-j723 \ \Omega + 717 \ \Omega – j451 \ \Omega = 717 \ \Omega – j1174 \ \Omega \)

Converting to polar form yields

\(Z=1376\angle -58.6°\Omega \)

The total current from \(V_{s2}\) is

\(I_{s2}= \frac{V_{s2}}{Z}= \frac{8\angle 0°V} {1376\angle -58.6°\Omega}= 5.81\angle 58.6°mA \)

Use the current-divider formula. The current through R due to \(V_{s2}\) is

\(I_{R2}= \left(\frac{X_{C1}\angle -90°}{R- jX_{C1}} \right)I_{s2}\)

 

\( \ \ \ \ \ \ =\left(\frac{1.59\angle -90°}{1.0 \ k\Omega – j1.59 \ k\Omega } \right)5.81\angle 58.6°mA= 4.91 \angle 26.4°mA\)

Step 3: Convert the two individual resistor currents to rectangular form and add to get the total current through R.

\(I_{R1}= 2.80\angle 25.6°mA= 2.53 \ mA + j1.21 \ mA\)

 

\(I_{R2}= 4.91\angle 26.4°mA= 4.40 \ mA + j2.18 \ mA\)

 

\(I_{R}=I_{R1}+ I_{R2}= 6.93 \ mA + j3.39 \ mA= 7.71\angle 26.1°mA\)
