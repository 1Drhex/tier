Guest Question.

A certain college graduate, Sallie Evans, has $24,000 in student-loan debt at the end of her college career. The interest rate on this debt is 0.75% per month. If monthly payments on this loan are $432.61, how many months will it take for Sallie to repay the entire loan? (4.6)

Admin
Solution

Here we are given P = $24,000, A = $432.61 per month, and i = 0.75% per month. The equivalence relationship is:
$24, 000 = $432.61 (P/A, 0.75% per month, N) We can determine N by inspecting Table C-3. From Table C-3 when N = 72 months, we see the following:
$432.61(55.4768) = $24, 000.
Thus, the loan will be completely repaid in 72 months.
