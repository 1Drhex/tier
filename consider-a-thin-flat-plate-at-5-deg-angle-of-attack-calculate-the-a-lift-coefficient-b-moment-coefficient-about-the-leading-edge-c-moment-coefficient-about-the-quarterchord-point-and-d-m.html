Guest Question.

Consider a thin flat plate at 5 deg. angle of attack. Calculate the: (a) lift coefficient, (b) moment coefficient about the leading edge, (c) moment coefficient about the quarterchord point, and (d) moment coefficient about the trailing edge.

Admin
Solution

although couched in terms of a thin symmetric airfoil, apply in particular to a flat plate with zero thickness.
(a) From Equation (4.33),

\(\pi \alpha =\frac{c_{l}}{2} (4.33)\)

where α is in radians

\(\alpha =\frac{5}{57.3}=0.08873\) rad

\(c_{l}=2\pi (0.0873)=0.5485\)

(b) From Equation (4.39),

\(c_{m,le}=-\frac{c_{l}}{4} (4.39)\)

\(c_{m,le}=-\frac{c_{l}}{4}=-\frac{0.5485}{4}=-0.137\)

(c) From Equation (4.41),

\(c_{m,c/4}=0 (4.41)\)

(d) Figure 4.27 is a sketch of the force and moment system on the plate. We place the lift at the quarter-chord point, along with the moment about the quarter- hord point.

This represents the force and moment system on the plate. Recall that the force and moment system can be represented by the lift acting through any point on the plate, and giving the moment about that point. Here, for convenience, we
place the lift at the quarter-chord point.

The lift acts perpendicular to \(V_{∞}\). [Part of the statement of the Kutta-Joukowski theorem given by Equation (3.140) is that the direction of the force associated with the circulation Γ is perpendicular to \(V_{∞}.\)] From Figure 4.27, the moment arm from \({L}'\) to the trailing edge is the length a, where

Equation (3.140): \(\acute{L} =\rho _{\infty }V_{\infty }\Gamma\)

\(a=(\frac{3}{4}c)\cos \alpha =)(\frac{3}{4}c)\cos 5^{\circ}\)

One of the assumptions of thin airfoil theory is that the angle of attack is small, and hence we can assume that cos α ≈ 1. Therefore, the moment arm from the point of action of the lift to the trailing edge is reasonably given by \(\frac{3}{4}c\). (Note that, in the previous Figure 4.26, the assumption of small α is already implicit because the moment arm is drawn parallel to the plate.)

Examining Figure 4.27, the moment about the trailing edge is

\({M}'_{te}=(\frac{3}{4}c){L}'+{M}'_{c/4}\)

\(c_{m,te}=\frac{{M}'_{te}}{q_{\infty }c^{2}}=(\frac{3}{4}c)\frac{{L}'}{q_{\infty }c^{2}}+\frac{{M}'_{c/4}}{q_{\infty }c^{2}}\)

\(c_{m,te}=\frac{3}{4}c_{l}+c_{m,c/4}\)

Since \( c_{m,c/4}=0\) we have

\(c_{m,te}=\frac{3}{4}c_{l}\)

\(c_{m,te}=\frac{3}{4}(0.5485)=0.411\)
