Guest Question.

PROTON SPIN FLIPS

Protons are placed in a 2.30-T magnetic field that points in the positive z-direction. (a) What is the energy difference between states with the z-component of proton spin angular momentum parallel and antiparallel to the field? (b) A proton can make a transition from one of these states to the other by emitting or absorbing a photon with the appropriate energy. Find the frequency and wavelength of such a photon.

Admin
Solution

IDENTIFY and SET UP:

The proton is a spin-\(\frac{1}{2}\) particle with a magnetic moment \(\vec μ\) in the same direction as its spin \(\vec S\), so its energy depends on the orientation of its spin relative to an applied magnetic field \(\vec B\). If the z-component of \(\vec S\) is aligned with \(\vec S\), then \(μ_z\) is equal to the positive value given in Eq. (43.8). If the z-component of \(\vec S\) is opposite \(\vec S\), then \(μ_z\) is the negative of this value. The interaction energy in either case is \(U = -μ_zB\); the difference between these energies is our target variable in part (a). We find the photon frequency and wavelength by using \(E = hf = hc/λ\).

\(\left|\mu_{s z}\right|_{\text {proton }}=2.7928 \mu_{\mathrm{n}}\) (43.8)

EXECUTE:

(a) When the z-components of \(\vec S\) and \(μ_z\) are parallel to \(\vec B\), the interaction energy is

\(\begin{aligned}U &=-\left|\mu_{z}\right| B=-(2.7928)\left(3.152 \times 10^{-8} \mathrm{eV}/ \mathrm{T}\right)(2.30 \mathrm{~T}) \\&=-2.025 \times 10^{-7} \mathrm{eV}\end{aligned}\)

When the z-components of \(\vec S\) and \(μ_z\) are antiparallel to the field, the energy is +2.025 × \(10^{-7}\) eV. Hence the energy difference between the states is

\(\Delta E=2\left(2.025 \times 10^{-7} \mathrm{eV}\right)=4.05 \times 10^{-7} \mathrm{eV}\)

(b) The corresponding photon frequency and wavelength are

\(\begin{gathered}f=\frac{\Delta E}{h}=\frac{4.05 \times 10^{-7} \mathrm{eV}}{4.136 \times10^{-15}\mathrm{eV} \cdot \mathrm{s}}=9.79 \times 10^{7} \mathrm{~Hz}=97.9 \mathrm{MHz} \\\lambda=\frac{c}{f}=\frac{3.00 \times 10^{8} \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}}{9.79 \times 10^{7}\mathrm{~s}^{-1}}=3.06 \mathrm{~m}\end{gathered}\)

 

EVALUATE: This frequency is in the middle of the FM radio band.
When a hydrogen specimen is placed in a 2.30-T magnetic field and irradiated with radio waves of this frequency, proton spin flips can be detected by the absorption of energy from the radiation.
