Guest Question.

The periodic waveform depicted in Fig. 2.2b can be represented by Eq. (2.1) with x(t) given by

\( \tilde{x}(t)=\sum\limits_{r=-\infty }^{\infty }{x(t+r\tau _{0} )} \) (2.1)

 

\( x(t)=\left|\sin \frac{1}{2}\omega _{0}t \right|\) for \( -\frac{1}{2}\tau _{0}\lt t\leq \frac{1}{2}\tau _{0} \)

and \( \tau _{0}=2\pi /\omega _{0} \). ( a) Obtain its Fourier-series representation in terms of Eq. (2.3). (b) Obtain and plot the amplitude and phase spectrums of \( \tilde{x}(t) \) . (c) Express the Fourier series obtained in part (a) as a linear combination of sines.

\( \tilde{x}(t)=\sum\limits_{k=-\infty }^{\infty }{X_{k} }e^{jk\omega _{0}t } \) for \( -\tau _{0}/2\leq t\leq \tau _{0}/2 \) (2.3)

Admin
Solution

(a) As x(t) is an even function of t, Eqs. (2.14a) and (2.14b) give

\( X_{0}=\frac{1}{\tau _{0} }\int_{-\tau _{0}/2 }^{\tau _{0}/2}{x}(t)dt=\frac{2}{\tau _{0} }\int_{0 }^{\tau _{0}/2}{x}(t)dt \) (2.14a)

\( X_{k}=\frac{1}{\tau _{0} }\int_{-\tau _{0}/2 }^{\tau _{0}/2}{x}(t)\cos k\omega _{0}t dt-j\frac{1}{\tau _{0} }\int_{-\tau _{0}/2 }^{\tau _{0}/2}{x}(t)sin k\omega _{0}t dt \)

 

\( =\frac{2}{\tau _{0} }\int_{0 }^{\tau _{0}/2}{x}(t)\cos k\omega _{0}tdt \) for k=1,2,.. (2.14b)

\( X_{-k}=\frac{1}{\tau_{0}}\int_{-\tau_{0}/2}^{\tau_{0}/2}{x(t)}\cos k\omega_{0}tdt+j\frac{1}{\tau _{0} } \int_{-\tau_{0}/2}^{\tau _{0}/2}x(t)\sin k\omega _{0}tdt\)

 

\( X_{k}=\frac{2}{\tau _{0} } \int_{0}^{\tau _{0}/2}{x(t)}\cos k\omega _{0}tdt=\frac{2}{\tau _{0} } \int_{0}^{\tau _{0}/2}\sin \frac{1}{2} \omega _{0}t\cos k\omega _{0}tdt \)

 

\( =\frac{2}{\tau _{0} } \int_{0}^{\tau _{0}/2}\cos k\omega _{0}t\sin \frac{1}{2}\omega _{0}t dt \)

 

From trigonometry

\( \cos \theta \sin \psi =\frac{1}{2}[\sin (\theta +\psi )-\sin (\theta -\psi )] \)

and hence we obtain

\( X_{k}=\frac{1}{\tau _{0} }\int_{0}^{\tau _{0}/2}[\sin (k+\frac{1}{2} )\omega _{0}t-\sin (k-\frac{1}{2} )\omega _{0}t] dt \)

 

\( =\frac{1}{\tau_{0} }[\frac{-\cos (k+\frac{1}{2} )\omega _{0}t }{(k+\frac{1}{2} )\omega _{0} }-\frac{-\cos (k-\frac{1}{2} )\omega _{0}t }{(k-\frac{1}{2} )\omega _{0} }]^{\tau _{0}/2 }_{0 } \)

On evaluating the limits, straightforward manipulation gives

\( X_{k}=\frac{2}{\pi (1-4k^{2} )} \)

Thus for any value of k including zero, we have

\( \left|X_{k}\right| =\left|\frac{2}{\pi (1-4k^{2} )}\right| \)

and

\(\arg X_{k}=\begin{cases} 0 & ifk=0 \\ -\pi &otherwise \end{cases} \)

(b) From Eqs. (2.12a)–(2.12d), we have

\( A_{0}=\left|a_{0} \right|=2\left|X_{0} \right| \) (2.12a)

\( \phi _{k}=\tan ^{-1}(-\frac{b_{k} }{a_{k} } )=\arg X_{k} \) (2.12d)

\( A_{k}=2\left|X_{k} \right|=\left|\frac{4}{\pi (1-4k^{2} )} \right| \) for k ≥0

\( \phi _{k}=\arg X_{k}=\begin{cases} 0 & if k=0 \\ -\pi & for k > 0 \end{cases} \)

The amplitude and phase spectrums of the waveform are illustrated in Fig. 2.4 for the case where \( \omega _{0}=1 \) rad/s.

(c) Now Eq. (2.13b) yields

\( \tilde{x}(t)=\frac{1}{2}A_{0}\sin(\phi _{0}+\frac{1}{2}\pi )+\sum\limits_{k=1}^{\infty }{A_{k}\sin (k\omega _{0}t+\phi _{k}+\frac{1}{2}\pi ) } \) (2.13b)

\( \tilde{x}=\frac{2}{\pi }+\sum\limits_{k=1}^{\infty }{\left|\frac{4}{\pi(1-4k^{2} )} \right|\sin (k\omega _{0}t-\pi +\frac{1}{2}\pi ) }\)

 

\( =\frac{2}{\pi }+\frac{4}{3\pi } \sin (\omega _{0}t-\frac{1}{2} \pi )+ \frac{4}{15\pi } \sin (2 \omega _{0}t-\frac{1}{2} \pi ) \)

 

\( =\frac{4}{35\pi } \sin (3 \omega _{0}t-\frac{1}{2} \pi )\frac{4}{63\pi } \sin (4 \omega _{0}t-\frac{1}{2} \pi )+… \)
