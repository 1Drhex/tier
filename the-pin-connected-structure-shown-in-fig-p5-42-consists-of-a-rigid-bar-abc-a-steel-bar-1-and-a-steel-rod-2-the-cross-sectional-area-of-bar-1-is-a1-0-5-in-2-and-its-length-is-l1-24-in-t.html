Guest Question.

The pin-connected structure shown in Fig. P5.42 consists of a rigid bar ABC, a steel bar (1), and a steel rod (2). The cross-sectional area of bar (1) is \(A_{1}=0.5 in .^{2}\) and its length is \(L_{1}\) = 24 in. The diameter of rod (2) is \(d_{2}\) = 0.375 in. and its length is \(L_{2}\) = 70 in. Assume E = 30,000 ksi for both axial members. Using a = 18 in., b = 32 in., c = 20 in., and P = 7 kips, determine:
(a) the normal stresses in bar (1) and rod (2).
(b) the deflection of pin C from its original position.

Admin
Solution

Equilibrium
Consider a FBD of the rigid bar. Assume tension forces in members (1) and (2). A moment equation about pin B gives the best information for this situation:

\(\Sigma M_{B}=-F_{1} a+F_{2} b-P c=0\) (a)

Geometry-of-deformation relationship
Draw a deformation diagram of the rigid bar. The deflections of the rigid bar are related by similar triangles:

\(\frac{v_{A}}{a}=\frac{v_{C}}{b}\) (b)

There are no gaps, clearances, or other misfits at pins A and C; therefore, Eq. (b) can be rewritten in terms of the member deformations as:

\(\frac{-\delta_{1}}{a}=\frac{\delta_{2}}{b}\)

Note: To understand the negative sign associated with \(\delta_{1}\), see Section 5.5 for a discussion of statically indeterminate rigid bar configurations with opposing members.

Force-Temperature-Deformation Relationships

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}} \quad \delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}\) (d)

Compatibility Equation

\(-\frac{1}{a} \frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}=\frac{1}{b} \frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}\) (e)

Solve the Equations
Solve Eq. (e) for \(F_{1}\) :

\(F_{1}=-\frac{a}{b} \frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}} \frac{A_{1} E_{1}}{L_{1}}=-F_{2} \frac{a}{b} \frac{L_{2}}{L_{1}} \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \frac{E_{1}}{E_{2}}=-F_{2} \frac{a}{b} \frac{L_{2}}{L_{1}} \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\)

Substitute this expression into equilibrium equation (a) and solve for \(F_{2}\):

\(\begin{aligned}-\left[-F_{2} \frac{a}{b} \frac{L_{2}}{L_{1}} \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\right] a+F_{2} b &=P c \\F_{2}\left[\frac{a^{2}}{b} \frac{L_{2}}{L_{1}} \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}+b\right] &=P c \\F_{2} &=\frac{P c}{\frac{a^{2}}{b} \frac{L_{2}}{L_{1}} \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}+b}\end{aligned}\)

The cross-sectional area of bar (1) is given as \(A_{1}=0.5 in .^{2}\) . The area of rod (2) is

\(A_{2}=\frac{\pi}{4}(0.375 in .)^{2}=0.110447 in .^{2}\)

Substitute these areas and the other given values to determine \(F_{2}\):

\(F_{2}=\frac{(7 kips )(20 in .)}{\frac{(18 in .)^{2}}{32 in .}\left(\frac{70 in. }{24 in. }\right)\left(\frac{0.5 in.^{2}}{0.110447 in .^{2}}\right)+32 in. }=0.844951 kips\)

Use this value to calculate \(F_{1}\):

\(F_{1}=-(0.844951 kip )\left(\frac{18 in .}{32 in. }\right)\left(\frac{70 in .}{24 in .}\right)\left(\frac{0.5 in. { }^{2}}{0.110447 in .^{2}}\right)=-6.275643 kips\)

 

(a) Normal stresses in bar (1) and rod (2): The normal stresses in each axial member can now be calculated:

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{1}=\frac{F_{1}}{A_{1}}=\frac{-6.275643 kips }{0.5 in .^{2}}=12.55 ksi ( C ) \\&\sigma_{2}=\frac{F_{2}}{A_{2}}=\frac{0.844951 kips }{0.110447 in .^{2}}=7.65 ksi ( T )\end{aligned}\)

(b) Deflection of the rigid bar at C: Calculate the deformation of member (2):

\(\delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}=\frac{(0.844951 kips )(70 in .)}{\left(0.110447 in. ^{2}\right)(30,000 ksi )}=0.017851 in.\)

Since there are no gaps at pin C, the rigid bar deflection at C is equal to the deformation of member (2); therefore:

\(v_{C}=\delta_{2}=0.01785 in . \downarrow\)
