Guest Question.

Estimate the actual endurance limit of cast steel having an ultimate strength of 120 ksi when used in a bar subjected to a reversed, repeated, bending load. The bar will be machined to a rectangular cross section, 1.50 in wide × 2.00 in high.

Admin
Solution

Objective: Compute the estimated actual endurance limit of the bar material.

Given: Cast steel, machined: \(s_{u}=120 \mathrm{ksi}\).
Size of section: \(b=1.50\) in., \(h=2.00\) in rectangular
Type of stress: Repeated, reversed bending.
Analysis: Use the Procedure for Estimating Actual Endurance Limit, \(s_{n}^{\prime}\).
Step 1: The ultimate tensile strength is given to be \(s_{u}=120 \mathrm{ksi}\).
Step 2: Surfaces are machined.
Step 3: From Figure 5-11, \(s_{n}=44\) ksi.
Step 4: Material factor for cast steel: \(C_{m}=0.80\).
Step 5: Type-of-stress factor for bending: \(C_{\text {st }}=1.00\).
Step 6: Specify a desired reliability of 0.99. Then \(C_{R}=0.81\) (Design decision).
Step 7: Size factor for rectangular section: First use Equation (5-25) (\(D_{e}=0.808 \sqrt{h b}\)) to determine the equivalent diameter,
\(D_{e}=0.808 \sqrt{h b}=0.808 \sqrt{(2.00 \mathrm{in})(1.50 \mathrm{in})}=1.40 \mathrm{in}\)
Then from Figure 5-12, \(C_{s}=0.85\).
Step 8: Use Equation (5-21) (\(s_{n}^{\prime}=s_{n}\left(C_{m}\right)\left(C_{s t}\right)\left(C_{R}\right)\left(C_{s}\right)\)), to compute the estimated actual endurance limit.
\(s_{n}^{\prime}=s_{n}\left(C_{m}\right)\left(C_{s t}\right)\left(C_{R}\right)\left(C_{s}\right)=44 \mathrm{ksi}(0.80)(1.00)(0.81)(0.85)=24.2 \mathrm{ksi}\)
