Guest Question.

A beam is loaded and supported as shown in Fig. P11.15.
(a) Use the double-integration method to determine the reactions at supports A and C.
(b) Draw the shear-force and bendingmoment diagrams for the beam.
(c) Determine the deflection in the middle of the span.

Admin
Solution

Beam FBD:

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma F_{y}=A_{y}+C_{y}-P=0 \\&\Sigma M_{A}=M_{C}+C_{y} L-P\left(\frac{L}{2}\right)=0\end{aligned}\)

 

 

Moment equation:

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{a-a} &=M(x)-A_{y} x=0 \\& \therefore M(x)=A_{y} x \quad\left(0 \leq x \leq \frac{L}{2}\right)\end{aligned}\)

 

 

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{b-b} &=M(x)-A_{y} x+P\left(x-\frac{L}{2}\right)=0 \\& \therefore M(x)=A_{y} x-P x+\frac{P L}{2} \quad\left(\frac{L}{2} \leq x \leq L\right)\end{aligned}\)

 

Integration:
For beam segment AB: For beam segment BC:

\(\begin{array}{ll}E I \frac{d^{2} v}{d x^{2}}=M(x)=A_{y} x & E I \frac{d^{2} v}{d x^{2}}=M(x)=A_{y} x-P x+\frac{P L}{2} \\E I \frac{d v}{d x}=\frac{A_{y} x^{2}}{2}+C_{1} & E I \frac{d v}{d x}=\frac{A_{y} x^{2}}{2}-\frac{P x^{2}}{2}+\frac{P L x}{2}+C_{3} \\E I v=\frac{A_{y} x^{3}}{6}+C_{1} x+C_{2} & E I v=\frac{A_{y} x^{3}}{6}-\frac{P x^{3}}{6}+\frac{P L x^{2}}{4}+C_{3} x+C_{4}\end{array}\)

 

Boundary conditions and evaluate constants:

\(\text { at } x=0, v=0 \quad \frac{A_{y}(0)^{3}}{6}+C_{1}(0)+C_{2}=0 \quad \therefore C_{2}=0\)

 

\(\text { at } x=L, \frac{d v}{d x}=0 \quad \frac{A_{y}(L)^{2}}{2}-\frac{P(L)^{2}}{2}+\frac{P L(L)}{2}+C_{3}=0 \quad \therefore C_{3}=-\frac{A_{y} L^{2}}{2}\)

 

\(\text { at } x=L, v=0 \quad \frac{A_{y}(L)^{3}}{6}-\frac{P(L)^{3}}{6}+\frac{P L(L)^{2}}{4}-\frac{A_{y} L^{2}}{2}(L)+C_{4}=0 \quad \therefore C_{4}=\frac{A_{y} L^{3}}{3}-\frac{P L^{3}}{12}\)

 

Slope continuity condition at x = L/2:

\(\begin{aligned}&\text { at } x=\frac{L}{2},\left.\frac{d v}{d x}\right|_{A B}=\left.\frac{d v}{d x}\right|_{B C} \\&\frac{A_{y}(L / 2)^{2}}{2}+C_{1}=\frac{A_{y}(L / 2)^{2}}{2}-\frac{P(L / 2)^{2}}{2}+\frac{P L(L / 2)}{2}-\frac{A_{y} L^{2}}{2} \\&\therefore C_{1}=\frac{P L^{2}}{8}-\frac{A_{y} L^{2}}{2}\end{aligned}\)

Deflection continuity condition at x = L/2:

\(\begin{gathered}\text { at } x=\frac{L}{2},\left.v_{B}\right|_{A B}=\left.v_{B}\right|_{B C} \\\frac{A_{y} x^{3}}{6}+\frac{P L^{2} x}{8}-\frac{A_{y} L^{2} x}{2}=\frac{A_{y} x^{3}}{6}-\frac{P x^{3}}{6}+\frac{P L x^{2}}{4}-\frac{A_{y} L^{2} x}{2}+\frac{A_{y} L^{3}}{3}-\frac{P L^{3}}{12}\end{gathered}\)

eliminate terms and rearrange:

\(\frac{A_{y} L^{3}}{3}=\frac{P x^{3}}{6}-\frac{P L x^{2}}{4}+\frac{P L^{2} x}{8}+\frac{P L^{3}}{12}\)

Substitute x = L/2 to obtain:

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{A_{y} L^{3}}{3}=& \frac{P(L / 2)^{3}}{6}-\frac{P L(L / 2)^{2}}{4}+\frac{P L^{2}(L / 2)}{8}+\frac{P L^{3}}{12}=\frac{5 P L^{3}}{48} \\& \therefore A_{y}=\frac{5 P}{16}\end{aligned}\)

(a) Beam reaction forces:

\(A_{y}=\frac{5 P}{16} \text { } \quad C_{y}=\frac{11 P}{16}\)

(a) Beam reaction moment:

\(M_{C}=P\left(\frac{L}{2}\right)-C_{y} L=\frac{P L}{2}-\frac{11 P L}{16}=-\frac{3 P L}{16} \quad M_{C}=-\frac{3 P L}{16}=\frac{3 P L}{16}( cw )\)

Elastic curve equation for beam segment AB:

\(\begin{aligned}E I v &=\frac{A_{y} x^{3}}{6}+\frac{P L^{2} x}{8}-\frac{A_{y} L^{2} x}{2}=\frac{5 P x^{3}}{96}+\frac{P L^{2} x}{8}-\frac{5 P L^{2} x}{32}=\frac{5 P x^{3}}{96}-\frac{3 P L^{2} x}{96} \\& \therefore v=\frac{P x}{96 F I}\left[5 x^{2}-3 L^{2}\right]\end{aligned}\)

(c) Midspan deflection:

\(v_{B}=\frac{P(L / 2)}{96 E I}\left[5\left(\frac{L}{2}\right)^{2}-3 L^{2}\right]=-\frac{7 P L^{3}}{768 E I}\)
