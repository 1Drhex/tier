Guest Question.

Finding the performance parameters of a single-phase half-wave rectifier The single-phase halfwave rectifier of Fig. 5.2(a) is supplied from a 120-V, 60-Hz source through the step-down transformer of Fig. 5.2(b) with turns ratio n = 10:1. The load resistance \( R_{ L } \text { is } 5 \Omega \). Determine (a) the average output voltage \( V_{ o ( av )} \), (b) the average load current \( I_{ o ( av )} \), (c) the rms load voltage \( V_{ o ( rms )} \), (d) the rms load current \( I_{ o ( rms )} \), (e) the ripple factor RF of the output voltage, (f) the rms ripple voltage \( V_{ r ( rms )} \), (g) the average diode current \( I_{ D ( av )} \), (h) the rms diode current \( I_{ D ( rms )} \), (i) the peak inverse voltage PIV of the diode, ( j) the average output power \( P_{ o ( ac )} \), (k) the DC output power \( P_{ o ( dc )} \), (l) the frequency \( f_{ r } \) of the output ripple voltage, and (m) the input power factor PF.

Admin
Solution

The primary transformer voltage is \( V_{ p }=120 V \). From Eq. (5.15), the secondary transformer voltage is \( V_{ s }= V_{ p } / n=120 / 10=12 V \). The peak input voltage of the rectifier is

 

\( \frac{V_{ p }}{V_{ s }}=\frac{N_{ p }}{N_{ s }}=n \) (5.15)

 

\( V_{ m }=\sqrt{2} V_{ s }=\sqrt{2} \times 12=16.97 V \)

 

(a) From Eq. (5.1),

 

\( V_{ o ( av )}=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} v_{ O } d(\omega t)=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} V_{ m } \sin \omega t d(\omega t)=\frac{V_{ m }}{\pi}=0.318 V_{ m } \) (5.1)

 

\( V_{ o ( av )}=0.318 V_{ m }=0.318 \times 16.97=5.4 V \)

 

(b) From Eq. (5.2),

 

\( I_{ o ( av )}=\frac{V_{ o ( av )}}{R_{ L }}=\frac{V_{ m }}{\pi R_{ L }}=\frac{0.318 V_{ m }}{R_{ L }} \) (5.2)

 

\( I_{ o ( av )}=\frac{V_{ o ( av )}}{R_{ L }}=\frac{5.4}{5}=1.08 A \)

 

(c) From Eq. (5.3),

 

\( V_{ o ( rms )}=\left[\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} v_{ O }^{2} d(\omega t)\right]^{1 / 2}=\left[\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} V_{ m }^{2} \sin ^{2} \omega t d(\omega t)\right]^{1 / 2} \) (5.3)

 

\( V_{ o ( rms )}=0.5 V_{ m }=0.5 \times 16.97=8.49 V \)

 

(d) From Eq. (5.4),

 

\( I_{ o ( rms )}=\frac{V_{ o ( rms )}}{R_{ L }}=\frac{0.5 V_{ m }}{R_{ L }} \) (5.4)

 

\( I_{ o ( rms )}=\frac{V_{ o ( rms )}}{R_{ L }}=\frac{8.49}{5}=1.7 A \)

 

(e) From Eq. (5.8), RF = 1.21, or 121%.

 

\( RF =\frac{V_{ r ( rms )}}{V_{ o ( av )}}=\frac{1.21 V_{ o ( av )}}{V_{ o ( av )}}=1.21 \text { or } 121 \% \) (5.8)

 

(f ) From Eq. (5.8),

 

\( V_{ r ( rms )}= RF \times V_{ o ( av )}=1.21 \times 5.4=6.53 V \)

 

(g) The average diode current \( I_{ D }( av ) \) will be the same as that of the load. That is, \( I_{ D ( av )}=I_{ O ( av )}=1.08 A \).

(h) The rms diode current \( I_{ D ( rms )} \) will be the same as that of the load. That is, \( I_{ D ( rms )}=I_{ o ( rms )}=1.7 A \).

(i) \( PIV =V_{ m }=16.97 V \).

(j) From Eq. (5.9),

 

\( P_{ o ( ac )}=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{0}^{2 \pi} i_{ O }^{2} R_{ L } d(\omega t)=I_{ o ( rms )}^{2} R_{ L }=V_{ o ( rms )} I_{ o ( rms )}=\left(\frac{V_{ m }}{2}\right)^{2} \frac{1}{R_{ L }} \) (5.9)

 

\( P_{ o ( ac )}=I_{ o ( rms )}^{2} R_{ L }=(1.7)^{2} \times 5=14.45 W \)

 

(k) From Eq. (5.11),

 

\( P_{ o ( dc )}=V_{ o ( av )} I_{ o ( av )}=\frac{V_{ m }^{2}}{\pi^{2} R_{ L }} \) (5.11)

 

\( P_{ o ( dc )}=V_{ o ( av )} I_{ o ( av )}=5.4 \times 1.08=5.83 W \)

 

(l) Notice from Fig. 5.1(d) that the frequency of the output ripple voltage is the same as the input frequency, \( f_{ r }=f=60 Hz \).

 

(m) \( V_{ s }=12 V , I_{ s }=I_{ o ( ms )}=1.7 A , \text { and } P_{\text {in }}=P_{ o(ac )}=14.45 W \) From Eq. (5.14),

 

\( PF =\frac{P_{ o ( ac )}}{V_{ s } I_{ s }}=\frac{\left(V_{ m } / 2\right)^{2} / R_{ L }}{\left(V_{ m } / \sqrt{2}\right)\left(V_{ m } / 2 R_{ L }\right)}=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}=0.707 \) (5.14)

 

\( PF =\frac{P_{ o ( ac )}}{V_{ s } I_{ s }}=\frac{14.45}{12 \times 1.7}=0.7071 \)
