Guest Question.

The scheme below shows a 2 × 2 m square footing with a tilted base, rests on a \( c - \varnothing \) soil of the given properties. The ground surface is level and horizontal. The foundation depth equals 0.4 m. The design requires a minimum SF = 2.5 against bearing capacity failure and 1.5 against sliding under the given loading conditions.
(a) Check whether the footing is safe against sliding. Assume: \( \delta = \varnothing; c_{a} = c; \) resisting passive earth pressure is neglected.
(b) Considering the bearing capacity failure only, check the adequacy of the footing dimensions. Use both Hansen and Vesic methods.

Admin
Solution

(a) Safety against sliding of the footing.

Maximum resisting force \( = H_{max} = P \tan \delta + c_{a} A_{f} \)

\( H_{max} = 650 \times \tan 25 + 25 \times 2 \times 2 = 403 kN \)

The sliding force \( H_{B} = 200 kN \)

Safety factor \( = SF = \frac{H_{max}}{H_{B}} = \frac{403}{200} = 2.02 > 1.5 \) ( required )

Therefore, the footing is safe against sliding.

(b) Adequacy of the footing dimensions.

(i) Hansen method:

Table 4.4: \( \varnothing = 25^{\circ}; N_{c} = 20.7; N_{q} = 10.7; N_{y} = 6.8; \frac{N_{q}}{N_{c}} = 0.514 \)

 

\( 2 \tan \varnothing \left ( 1 – \sin \varnothing \right )^{2} = 0.311; \frac{D}{B} = \frac{0.4}{2} = 0.2 = k; \frac{{B}'}{{L}'} = \frac{B}{L} = \frac{2}{2} = 1\)

 

Table 4.7: \( \beta = 0^{\circ}; \) hence, all g factors are one.

 

Equation : \( gross q_{ult} = c N_{c} s_{c, B} d_{c, B} i_{c, B} b_{c, B} + \overline{q} N_{q} s_{q, B} d_{q, B} i_{q, B} b_{q, B} + 0.5 {y}' {B}' N_{y} s_{y, B} d_{y, B} i_{y, B} b_{y, B}\)

Refer to Tables 4.6 and 4.7 and Section 4.7:

\( \eta = 15^{\circ}= 0.262 \) radians

 

\( b_{c,B} = 1 – \frac{\eta^{\circ}}{147^{\circ}} = 1 – \frac{15}{147^{\circ}} = 0.9 \)

 

\( b_{q,B} = exp \left ( – 2 \eta \tan \varnothing \right ) = exp \left [ – 2 \left ( 0.262 \right )\left ( \tan 25 \right ) \right ] = 0.783\)

 

\( b_{y,B} = exp \left ( – 2.7 \eta \tan \varnothing \right ) = exp \left [ – 2.7 \left ( 0.262 \right )\left ( \tan 25 \right ) \right ] = 0.719\)

 

\( b_{c,B} = 1 + 0.4 k = 1 + 0.4 \times 0.2 = 1.08 \)

 

\( b_{q,B} = 1 + 2 \tan \varnothing \left ( 1 – \sin \varnothing \right )^{2}k = 1 + 0.311 \times 0.2 = 1.062 \)

 

\( b_{y,B} = 1 \)

 

\( V = P = 650 kN. H_{B} = 200 kN. \) Assume

 

\( \alpha_{1} = 3 \) and \( \alpha_{2} = 4 \)

\( V + A_{f}c_{a}\cot \varnothing = 650 + 2 \times 2 \times 25 \times \cot 25 = 864.5 \)

 

\( i_{q,B} = \left [ 1 – \frac{0.5 H_{B}}{V + A_{f}c_{a}\cot \varnothing } \right ]^{\alpha_{1}} = \left [ 1 – \frac{0.5 \times 200}{864.5} \right ]^{3} = 0.692 \)

 

\( i_{y,B} = \left [ 1 – \frac{\left [ \left ( 0.7 \right) – \eta^{\circ} /450^{\circ} \right ] H_{B}}{V + A_{f}c_{a}\cot \varnothing } \right ]^{\alpha_{2}} = \left [ 1 – \frac{0.678 \times 200}{864.5} \right ]^{4} = 0.506 \)

 

\( i_{c,B} = i_{q} – \frac{1 – i_{q}}{N_{q} – 1} = 0.692 – \frac{1 – 0.692}{10.7 – 1} = 0.66 \)

 

\( s_{c,B} = 1 + \frac{N_{q}}{N_{c}} \times \frac{Bi_{c,B}}{L} = 1 + 0.514 \times \frac{2 \times 0.66}{2} = 1.34 \)

 

\( s_{q,B} = 1 + \sin \varnothing \left ( Bi_{q,B}/L \right ) = 1 + \left ( \sin 25 \right )\left ( 2 \times 0.692 /2 \right ) = 1.29 \)

 

\( s_{y,B} = 1 – 0.4 \left ( Bi_{y,B}/Li_{y,L} \right ). \) The value of \( i_{y,L} = 1, \) since \( H_{L} = 0 \); hence,

 

\( s_{y,B} = 1 – 0.4 \left ( \frac{2 \times 0.506}{2 \times 1} \right ) = 0.798 > 0.6 \)

 

\( gross q_{ult} = 25 \times 20.7 \times 1.34 \times 1.08 \times 0.66 \times 0.9 \)

 

\( + 0.4 \times 18 \times 10.7 \times 1.29 \times 1.062 \times 0.692 \times 0.783 \)

 

\( + 0.5 \times 18 \times 2 \times 6.8 \times 0.798 \times 1 \times 0.506 \times 0.719 \)

 

\( = 444.9 + 57.2 + 35.5 = 537.6 kPa \)

 

gross contact pressure, \( gross q = \frac{P}{A} = \frac{650}{2 \times 2} = 162.5 kPa \)

 

Safety factor \( SF = \frac{gross q_{ult}}{gross q} = \frac{537.6}{162.5} = 3.3 > 2.5 \) (required); hence,

The footing dimensions are adequate according to the Hansen method.

(ii) Vesic method:

Table 4.4: \( \varnothing = 25^{\circ}; N_{c} = 20.7; N_{q} = 10.7; N_{y} = 10.9; \frac{ N_{q}}{ N_{c}} = 0.514 \)

 

\( 2 \tan \varnothing \left ( 1 – \sin \varnothing \right )^{2} = 0.311; \frac{B}{L} = \frac{2}{2} = 1. \)

Refer to Tables 4.6 and 4.8:

\( \eta = 15^{\circ} = 0.262 radians; \beta = 0^{\circ} \)

 

\( b_{c} = 1 – \frac{2\beta }{5.14 \tan \varnothing } = 1 \) ( since ground slope \(\beta = 0^{\circ} \))

 

\( b_{q} = b_{y} = \left ( 1 – \eta \tan \varnothing \right )^{2} = \left [ 1 – \left ( 0.262 \right )\left ( \tan 25 \right ) \right ]^{2} = 0.771\)

 

\( d_{c} = 1.080; d_{q} = 1.062; d_{y} = 1 \) (The same as Hansen depth factors)

 

\( s_{c} = 1 + \frac{N_{q}}{N_{c}} \times \frac{B}{L} = 1 + 0.514 \times \frac{2}{2} = 1.514 \)

 

\( s_{q} = 1 + \left ( \tan \varnothing \right ) \left ( \frac{B}{L} \right ) = 1 + \left ( \tan 25 \right ) \left ( \frac{2}{2} \right ) = 1.466 \)

 

\( s_{y} = 1 – 0.4 \left ( \frac{B}{L} \right ) = 1 – 0.4 \times \frac{2}{2} = 0.6 \)

 

\( m = \frac{2 + \frac{L}{B}}{1 + \frac{L}{B}} = \frac{2 + 1}{1 + 1} = 1.5\)

 

\( V = P = 650 kN. H = 200 kN.\)

 

\( V + A_{f}c_{a}\cot \varnothing = 650 + 2 \times 2 \times 25 \times \cot 25 = 864.5 \)

 

\( i_{q} = \left [ 1 – \frac{H}{ V + A_{f}c_{a}\cot \varnothing} \right ]^{m} = \left ( 1 – \frac{200}{864.5} \right )^{1.5} = 0.674\)

 

\( i_{y} = \left [ 1 – \frac{H}{ V + A_{f}c_{a}\cot \varnothing} \right ]^{m + 1} = \left ( 1 – \frac{200}{864.5} \right )^{2.5} = 0.518\)

 

\( i_{c} = i_{q} – \frac{1 – i_{q}}{N_{q} – 1} = i_{c,B} = 0.674 – \frac{1 – 0.674}{10.7 – 1} = 0.64 \)

 

\( g_{c} = i_{q} – \frac{1 – i_{q}}{5.14 \tan \varnothing } = 0.674 – \frac{1 – 0.674}{5.14 \tan 25} = 0.538 \)

 

\( g_{q} = g_{y} = \left ( 1 – \tan \beta \right )^{2} = 1\)

 

\( gross q_{ult} = c N_{c} s_{c} d_{c} i_{c} b_{c} g_{c} + q N_{q} s_{q} d_{q} i_{q} b_{q} g_{q} + 0.5 {y}' {B}' N_{y} s_{y} d_{y} i_{y} b_{y} g_{y}\)

 

\( gross q_{ult} = 25 \times 20.7 \times 1.514 \times 1.08 \times 0.64 \times 1 \times 0.538 \)

 

\( + 0.4 \times 18 \times 10.7 \times 1.466 \times 1.062 \times 0.674 \times 0.771 \times 1 \)

 

\( + 0.5 \times 18 \times 2 \times 10.9 \times 0.6 \times 1 \times 0.518 \times 0.771 \times 1 \)

 

\( = 291.4 + 62.3 + 47.0 = 400.7 kPa \)

 

gross contact pressure, \( gross q = \frac{P}{A} = \frac{650}{2 \times 2} = 162.5 kPa \)

 

Safety factor \( = SF = \frac{gross q_{ult} }{gross q } = \frac{400.7}{162.5} = 2.5 = 2.5\) (required); hence:

The footing dimensions are probably adequate according to the Vesic method.

The large difference between the two results is mainly due to the \( g_{c}\) factor of the predominating cohesion term; it is considered as unity in the Hansen equation, whereas in the Vesic equation it is about half (0.538) of this value, which reduces the cohesion term by about 50%.
