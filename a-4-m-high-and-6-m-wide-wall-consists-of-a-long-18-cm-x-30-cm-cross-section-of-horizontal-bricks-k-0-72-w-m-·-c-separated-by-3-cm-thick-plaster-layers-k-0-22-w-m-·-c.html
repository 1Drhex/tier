Guest Question.

A 4-m-high and 6-m-wide wall consists of a long \(18- cm \times 30- cm\) cross section of horizontal bricks (\(k = 0.72 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\)) separated by 3-cm-thick plaster layers (\( k = 0.22 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\)). There are also 2-cm-thick plaster layers on each side of the wall, and a 2-cm-thick rigid foam (\( k = 0.026 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\)) on the inner side of the wall. The indoor and the outdoor temperatures are 22°C and – 4°C, and the convection heat transfer coefficients on the inner and the outer sides are \(h_{1}=10 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\) and \(h_{2}=20 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\), respectively. Assuming one-dimensional heat transfer and disregarding radiation, determine the rate of heat transfer through the wall.

Admin
Solution

A wall consists of horizontal bricks separated by plaster layers. There are also plaster layers on each side of the wall, and a rigid foam on the inner side of the wall. The rate of heat transfer through the wall is to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Heat transfer is steady since there is no indication of change with time. 2 Heat transfer through the wall is one-dimensional. 3 Thermal conductivities are constant. 4 Heat transfer by radiation is disregarded.

Properties The thermal conductivities are given to be \(k=0.72 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\) for bricks, \(k = 0.22 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\) for plaster layers, and \( k=0.026 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\) for the rigid foam.

Analysis We consider 1 m deep and 0.33 m high portion of wall which is representative of the entire wall. The thermal resistance network and individual resistances are

\( R_{i}=R_{\text {conv }, 1}=\frac{1}{h_{1} A}=\frac{1}{\left(10 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(0.33 \times 1 m ^{2}\right)}=0.303{ }^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\( R_{1}=R_{\text {foam }}=\frac{L}{k A}=\frac{0.02 m }{\left(0.026 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(0.33 \times 1 m ^{2}\right)}=2.33{ }^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\(R_{2}=R_{6}=\underset{\text { side }}{R_{\text {plaster }}}=\frac{L}{k A}=\frac{0.02 m }{\left(0.22 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(0.30 \times 1 m ^{2}\right)}=0.303{ }^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\( R_{3}=R_{5}=R_{\text {plaster center}}=\frac{L}{h_{o} A}=\frac{0.18 m }{\left(0.22 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\right)\left(0.015 \times 1 m ^{2}\right)}=54.55^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\( R_{4}=R_{\text {brick }}=\frac{L}{k A}=\frac{0.18 m }{\left(0.72 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(0.30 \times 1 m ^{2}\right)}=0.833{ }^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\( R_{ o }=R_{\text {conv }, 2}=\frac{1}{h_{2} A}=\frac{1}{\left(20 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(0.33 \times 1 m ^{2}\right)}=0.152{ }^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\( \frac{1}{R_{\text {mid }}}=\frac{1}{R_{3}}+\frac{1}{R_{4}}+\frac{1}{R_{5}}=\frac{1}{54.55}+\frac{1}{0.833}+\frac{1}{54.55} \longrightarrow R_{\text {mid }}=0.81^{\circ} C / W \)

 

\( R_{\text {total }}=R_{i}+R_{1}+2 R_{2}+R_{\text {mid }}+R_{o}\)

 

\( = 0.303 + 2.33 + 2(0.303) + 0.81 + 0.152 = 4.201^{\circ} C / W \)

 

The steady rate of heat transfer through the wall per \(0.33 m ^{2}\) is

\( \dot{Q}=\frac{T_{\infty 1}-T_{\infty 2}}{R_{\text {total }}}=\frac{\left[(22-(-4)]^{\circ} C \right.}{4.201^{\circ} C / W }=6.19 W\)

 

Then steady rate of heat transfer through the entire wall becomes

\( \dot{Q}_{\text {total }}=(6.19 W ) \frac{(4 \times 6) m ^{2}}{0.33 m ^{2}} = 4 5 0 W\)
