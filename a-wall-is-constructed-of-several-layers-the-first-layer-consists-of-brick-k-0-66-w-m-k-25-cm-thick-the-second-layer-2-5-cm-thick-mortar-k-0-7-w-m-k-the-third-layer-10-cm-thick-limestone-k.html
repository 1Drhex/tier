Guest Question.

A wall is constructed of several layers. The first layer consists of brick (k = 0.66 W/m.K), 25 cm thick, the second layer 2.5 cm thick mortar (k = 0.7 W/m.K), the third layer 10 cm thick limestone (k = 0.66 W/m.K) and outer layer of 1.25 cm thick plaster (k = 0.7 W/m.K). The heat transfer coefficients on interior and exterior of the wall fluid layers are 5.8 \(W/m^2.\)K and 11.6 \(W/m^2.K,\) respectively. Find :
(i) Overall heat transfer coefficient,
(ii) Overall thermal resistance per \(m^2\),
(iii) Rate of heat transfer per \(m^2,\) if the interior of the room is at 26°C while outer air is at – 7°C,
(iv) Temperature at the junction between mortar and limestone.

Admin
Solution

Given : A multilayer composite exposed to
different atmospheres on both boundary surfaces.
\(L_1 = 25 cm = 0.25 m, k_1 = 0.66 W/m.K,\)
\(L_2 = 2.5 cm = 0.025 m, k_2 = 0.7 W/m.K,\)
\(L_3 = 10 cm = 0.1 m, k_3 = 0.66 W/m.K,\)
\(L_4 = 1.25 cm = 0.0125 m, k_4 = 0.7 W/m.K,\)
\(h_1 = 5.8 W/m^2.K, h_2 = 11.6 W/m^2.K,\)
\(T_{∞_{1}} = 26°C, T_{∞_{2}} = – 7°C.\)
To find :
(i) Overall heat Transfer coefficient,
(ii) Total thermal resistance, \(Σ_{Rth},\)
(iii) The heat flow rate through composite, Q,
(iv) \(T_3,\) temperature at the interface of mortar and limestone.
Assumptions :
(i) Steady state heat conduction rate through 1 \(m^2\) area.
(ii) Heat transfer in one direction only.
(iii) Constant properties.
(iv) No thermal contact resistance at interfaces.

Analysis : The schematic and equivalent thermal resistances for composite wall are shown in Fig. 3.10 :
where, \(R_1 = \frac{1}{h_1 A}=\frac{1}{5.8 \times 1} = 0.1724 K/W\)
\(R_2 = \frac{L_1}{k_1 A}=\frac{0.25}{0.66 \times 1} = 0.378 K/W\)
\(R_3 = \frac{L_2}{k_2 A}=\frac{0.025}{0.7 \times 1}= 0.0357 K/W\)
\(R_4 = \frac{L_3}{k_3 A}=\frac{0.1}{0.66 \times 1} = 0.1515 K/W\)
\(R_5 = \frac{L_4}{k_4 A}=\frac{0.0125}{0.7 \times 1}= 0.0178 K/W\)
\(R_6 =\frac{1}{h_2 A}=\frac{1}{11.6 \times 1} = 0.0862 K/W\)
(i) Overall heat transfer coefficient,
\(U =\frac{1}{A \sum R_{th}}=\frac{1}{A(R_1+ R_2+ R_3 + R_4 + R_5 + R_6)}\)
\(U= \frac{1}{1 \times( 0.1724 +0.378+ 0.0357+0.1515+ 0.0178 +0.0862)}\)
\(=\frac{1}{0.8424}\)
or\( U = 1.187 W/m^2.K.\)
(ii) The overall thermal resistances
\(Σ_{Rth} = \frac{1}{UA}=\frac{1}{1.187 \times 1}\)

= 0.8424 K/W.
(iii) The heat flow rate through the composite per \(m^2 ;\)
\(Q =\frac{T_{∞_{1}} – T_{∞_{2}}}{Σ_{Rth}}= \frac{26 -(-7)}{0.8424}\)
\(= 39.17 W/m^2. \)
(iv) The temperature at the interface of mortar and limestone can be calculated as :
\(Q =\frac{T_{∞_{1}} – T_{3}}{R_1+ R_2+ R_3}\)

or \(T_3 = T_{∞_{1}} – 39.17 × (0.1724 + 0.378 + 0.0357) = 3°C.\)
