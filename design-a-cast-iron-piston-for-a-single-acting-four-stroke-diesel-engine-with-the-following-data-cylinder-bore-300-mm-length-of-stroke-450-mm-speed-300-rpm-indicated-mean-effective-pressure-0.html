Guest Question.

Design a cast iron piston for a single acting four-stroke diesel engine with the following data: Cylinder bore = 300 mm Length of stroke = 450 mm
Speed = 300 rpm
Indicated mean effective pressure = 0.85 MPa
Maximum gas pressure = 5 MPa
Fuel consumption = 0.30 kg per BP per h
Higher calorific value of fuel = 44 000 kJ/kg
Assume suitable data if required and state the assumptions you make.

Admin
Solution

Given D = 300 mm l = 450 mm = 0.45 m

\( N=300 rpm \quad p_{m}=0.85 MPa =0.85 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( p_{\max .}=5 MPa =5 N / mm ^{2} \quad m=0.3 kg \text { per BP per } h \).

HCV = 44 000 kJ/kg.

Step I Piston head or crown
Assumption No. 1 The permissible tensile stress for the cast iron piston is 40 N/mm².
From Eq. 25.16, the thickness of the piston head by strength consideration is given by,

\( t_{h}=D \sqrt{\frac{3}{16} \frac{p_{\max }}{\sigma_{b}}}=300 \sqrt{\frac{3}{16} \frac{(5)}{(40)}} \).

= 45.93 mm (a).

For four-stroke engine,

\( n=\frac{N}{2}=\frac{300}{2}=150 \text { strokes } / min \).

\( IP =\frac{p_{m} l A n}{60}=\frac{(0.85)(0.45)}{(60)}\left(\frac{\pi(300)^{2}}{4}\right)(150) \).

= 67 593.33 W or 67.59 kW
Assumption No. 2 The mechanical efficiency (η) is 80% or 0.8.

\( BP =\eta I P=0.8(67.59)=54.07 kW \).

m = 0.3 kg per BP per h

\( =\left(\frac{0.3}{60 \times 60}\right) kg \text { per BP per second } \).

\( =\left(83.33 \times 10^{-6}\right) kg \text { per BP per second } \).

Assumption No. 3 The ratio of heat absorbed by the piston to the total heat developed in the cylinder is 0.05 or 5% (C = 0.05).
From Eq. 25.19,

\( H=[C \times HCV \times m \times BP ] \times 10^{3} \) (25.19).

\( H=[C \times HCV \times m \times BP ] \times 10^{3} \).

\( =\left[0.05 \times 44000 \times\left(83.33 \times 10^{-6}\right) \times 54.07\right] \times 10^{3} \).

= 9912.44 W
Assumption No. 4 The thermal conductivity factor (k) for cast iron is 46.6 W/m/°C.
Assumption No. 5 The temperature difference \( \left(T_{c}-T_{e}\right) \) between the centre and the edge of the piston head is 220°C.
From Eq. 25.18, the thickness of the piston head by thermal consideration is given by,

\( t_{h}=\left[\frac{H}{12.56 k\left(T_{c}-T_{e}\right)}\right] \times 10^{3} \). (25.18).

\( t_{h}=\left[\frac{H}{12.56 k\left(T_{c}-T_{e}\right)}\right] \times 10^{3} \).

\( =\left[\frac{9912.44}{12.56(46.6)(220)}\right] \times 10^{3} \)

\( t_{h}=76.98 mm \). (b)
From (a) and (b), thermal consideration is the criterion for piston thickness

\( t_{h}=76.98 \text { or } 77 mm \).

Step II Radial ribs

\( \text { Since } t_{h}=77 mm \quad t_{h}>6 mm \)

Therefore, ribs are required.
Assumption No. 6 The number of radial ribs is 4.

From Eq. 25.22,

\( t_{R}=\left(\frac{t_{h}}{3}\right) \text { to }\left(\frac{t_{h}}{2}\right) \) (25.22).

\( t_{R}=\left(\frac{t_{h}}{3}\right) \text { to }\left(\frac{t_{h}}{2}\right)=\left(\frac{77}{3}\right) \text { to }\left(\frac{77}{2}\right) \) .

= 25.67 to 38.5

\( t_{R}=30 mm \).

Step III Requirement of cup

\( \left(\frac{l}{D}\right)=\left(\frac{450}{300}\right)=1.5 \).

Therefore, a cup is required.
Radius of cup = 0.7 D = 0.7(300) = 210 mm.

Step IV Piston rings
Assumption No. 7 The allowable radial pressure \( \left(p_{w}\right) \) on the cylinder wall is 0.035 MPa.
Assumption No. 8 The piston rings are made of cast iron and the permissible tensile stress is 90 N/mm².
Assumption No. 9 The number of compression rings is 3 and there is one oil ring.
From Eq. 25.25,

\( b=D \sqrt{\frac{3 p_{w}}{\sigma_{t}}} \) (25.25).

\( b=D \sqrt{\frac{3 p_{w}}{\sigma_{t}}}=300 \sqrt{\frac{3(0.035)}{90}} 10.25 \text { or } 10.5 mm \).

From Eq. 25.26,

h = (0.7 b) to b = (0.7 × 10.5) to 10.5
= 7.35 to 10.5 mm
h = 8 mm
z = 3 + 1= 4
Also,

\( h_{\min .}=\left(\frac{D}{10 z}\right)=\left(\frac{300}{10(4)}\right)=7.5 mm \quad h>h_{\min .} \)

The gap between free ends of the piston ring before assembly is given by,

\( G_{1}=3.5 b \text { to } 4 b \).

= 3.5(10.5) to 4(10.5) = 36.75 to 42 mm.

\( G_{1}=40 mm \).

The gap between free ends of the piston ring after assembly is given by,

\( G_{2}=0.002 D \text { to } 0.004 D \).

0.002(300) to 0.004(300) = 0.6 to 1.2 mm.

\( G_{2}=0.8 mm \).

From Eq. 25.28, the width of the top land is given by,

\( h_{1}=\left(t_{h}\right) \text { to }\left(1.2 t_{h}\right) \). (25.28).

\( h_{1}=\left(t_{h}\right) \text { to }\left(1.2 t_{h}\right)=(77) \text { to }(1.2 \times 77) \).

= 77 to 92.4 mm.

\( h_{1}=85 mm \)

From Eq. 25.29, the width of ring grooves is given by,

\( h_{2}=0.75 h \text { to } h=0.75(8) \text { to } 8=6 \text { to } 8 \)

= 7 mm.

Step V Piston barrel
From Eq. 25.30, the thickness of barrel at the top end is given by

\( t_{3}=(0.03 D+b+4.9) \). (25.30)

\( t_{3}=(0.03 D+b+4.9)=0.03(300)+10.5+4.9 \).

= 24.4 mm or 25 mm
From Eq. 25.31, the thickness of the barrel at the open end is given by,

\( t_{4}=\left(0.25 t_{3}\right) \text { to }\left(0.35 t_{3}\right) \) (25.31).

\( t_{4}=\left(0.25 t_{3}\right) \text { to }\left(0.35 t_{3}\right)=(0.25 \times 25) \text { to }(0.35 \times 25) \).

= 6.25 to 8.75 mm.

\( t_{4}=7 mm \).

Step VI Piston skirt
Assumption No. 10 The allowable bearing pressure \( \left(p_{b}\right) \) for the skirt portion of the piston is 0.45 MPa.
Assumption No. 11 The ratio of side thrust on liner to maximum gas load on piston is 0.1
(μ = 0.1).
From Eq. 25.32, the length of the skirt is given by,

\( \mu\left(\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}\right) p_{\max .}=p_{b} D l_{s} \) (25.32).

\( \mu\left(\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}\right) p_{\max .}=p_{b} D l_{s} \text { or } 0.1\left(\frac{\pi(300)^{2}}{4}\right)(5)=(0.45)(300) l_{s} \) .

\( l_{s}=261.8 \text { or } 262 mm \).

Step VII Piston length
Refer to Fig. 25.10.

\( \text { Length of ring section }=4 h+3 h_{2}=4(8)+3(7) \).

= 53 mm [Refer Fig. 25.4].

\( \text { Length of piston }=h_{1}+\text { length of ring section }+l_{ s } \).

= 85 + 53 + 262 = 400 mm
L = 400 mm
According to empirical relationship,
L = D to 1.5D = 300 to 450 mm.

\( \therefore \quad D<L>1.5 D \).

Step VIII Piston pin
Assumption No. 12 The bearing pressure \( \left(p_{b}\right)_{1} \) at the bush of the small end of connecting rod is 30 MPa.
Assumption No. 13 The length of the piston pin in the bush of the small end of the connecting rod is (0.45 D).
Assumption No. 14 The piston pin is made of case hardened alloy steel and the permissible tensile stress is 140 N/mm².

\( P=\text { Force on piston }=\left(\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}\right) p_{\max } \).

\( =\left(\frac{\pi(300)^{2}}{4}\right)(5)=353429.17 N \).

From Eq. 25.37,

\( \left(\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}\right) p_{\max .}=\left(p_{b}\right)_{1} \times d_{o} \times l_{1} \quad\left[l_{1}=0.45 D\right] \) (25.37).

\( \left(\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}\right) p_{\max .}=\left(p_{b}\right)_{1} \times d_{o} \times l_{1} \quad\left[l_{1}=0.45 D\right] \).

\( 353429.17=30 d_{o}(0.45 \times 300) \).

\( d_{o}=87.27 \text { or } 90 mm \).

In order to make the shaft hollow, we will increase the diameter to 120 mm.

\( d_{o}=120 mm \).

From Eq. 25.39,

\( M_{b}=\left(\frac{P D}{8}\right) \) (25.39).

\( M_{b}=\left(\frac{P D}{8}\right)=\frac{(353429.17)(300)}{8} \) .

\( =13253.59 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

\( I=\frac{\pi\left(d_{o}^{4}-d_{i}^{4}\right)}{64}=\frac{\pi\left[(120)^{4}-d_{i}^{4}\right]}{64}= mm ^{4} \).

\( y=\left(\frac{d_{o}}{2}\right)=\left(\frac{120}{2}\right)=60 mm \).

\( \sigma_{b}=\frac{M_{b} y}{I} \quad 140=\frac{\left(13253.59 \times 10^{3}\right)(60)}{\frac{\pi}{64}\left[(120)^{4}-d_{i}^{4}\right]} \).

\( (120)^{4}-d_{i}^{4}=115.71 \times 10^{6} \).

\( d_{i}=97.84 \text { or } 95 mm \).

The mean diameter of the piston boss is given by,
mean diameter of the piston boss \( =1.4 d_{o} \).

= 1.4(120) = 168 mm.
