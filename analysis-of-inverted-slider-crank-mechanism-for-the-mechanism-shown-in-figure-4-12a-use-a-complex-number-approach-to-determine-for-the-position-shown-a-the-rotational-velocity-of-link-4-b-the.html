Guest Question.

ANALYSIS OF INVERTED SLIDER CRANK MECHANISM

For the mechanism shown in Figure 4.12(a), use a complex number approach to determine, for the position shown,
(a) the rotational velocity of link 4
(b) the Coriolis acceleration of point B\(_3\) with respect to point B\(_2\)

r\(_{O_2O_4}\) = 3.0 cm; r\(_{O_4D}\) = r\(_4\) = 8.0 cm; θ\(_2\)=60°; \(\dot θ_2\)=30 rad/sec CW

Admin
Solution

The complex vector representation is shown in Figure 4.12(b). The loop closure equation is

\(R_3+R_2-R_5-R_4-R_1=0\) (4.3-35)

However, since

\(R_3=R_5\) (4.3-36)

Equation (4.3-35) reduces to

\(R_2-R_4-R_1=0\) (4.3-37)

The real component of Equation (4.3-37) is

\(r_2\cos\theta_2-r_4\cos\theta_4-r_1=0\) (4.3-38)

The imaginary component of Equation (4.3-37) is

\(r_2\sin\theta_2-r_4\sin\theta_4=0\)

The independent variable is θ\(_2\) , and the dependent variables are θ\(_4\) , and r\(_2\).
Solve for dependent variable θ\(_4\) and eliminate r\(_2\) by taking

Equation (4.3-38)\(\times \sin\theta_2- \)Equation (4.3-39 ) \(\times \cos\theta_2\)

which gives

\(r_2\cos\theta_2\sin\theta_2-r_4\cos\theta_4\sin\theta_2-r_1\sin \theta_2 -r_2\sin\theta_2\cos \theta_2+r_4\sin\theta_4 \cos\theta_2=0\) (4.3-40)

Simplifying and rearranging Equation (4.3-40) and also solving for θ\(_4,\) we obtain

\(\theta_4=\theta_2+\sin^{-1}\left\lgroup \frac{r_1\sin\theta_2}{r_4}\right\rgroup= 60°+\sin^{-1}\left\lgroup \frac{3.0\sin60°}{8.0}\right\rgroup=78.9°\) (4.3-41)

From Equation (4.3-39) and also solving for r\(_2\), we have

\(r_2=r_4\frac{sin \theta _4}{\sin \theta_2}=8.0 cm \frac{\sin78.9°}{\sin 60°}=9.07\) cm (4.3-42)

The time derivative of Equation (4.3-38) is

\(-r_2\dot\theta_2 \sin\theta_2+\dot r_2 \cos\theta_2+ r_4\dot \theta_4 \sin\theta_4=0\) (4.3-43)

The time derivative of Equation (4.3-39) is

\(r_2\dot\theta_2 \cos\theta_2+\dot r_2 \sin\theta_2+r_4 \dot \theta_4 \cos\theta_4=0\) (4.3-44)

To solve for the rotational speed of link 4, we take

Equation (4.3-43)\(\times \sin\theta_2-\) Equation (4.3-44 ) \(\times \cos\theta_2\)

which gives after simplification

\(\dot{\theta_4}=\frac{r_2\dot{\theta_2}}{ r_4 \cos(\theta_2-\theta_4)}=\left[\frac{9.07(-30)}{8.0\cos(60° -78.9°)} \right] rad/sec =-35.9\) rad/sec (3.4-45)

To solve for the time derivative of the second dependent variable, we take

Equation (4.3-43)\(\times \cos\theta_2- \)Equation (4.3-44 ) \(\times \sin\theta_2\)

which gives after simplification

\(\dot r_2=r_4\dot\theta_4\sin(\theta_2-\theta_4)=[8.0(-35.9)\sin (60°-78.9°)]cm/sec =-39.0\) cm/sec (4.3-46)
