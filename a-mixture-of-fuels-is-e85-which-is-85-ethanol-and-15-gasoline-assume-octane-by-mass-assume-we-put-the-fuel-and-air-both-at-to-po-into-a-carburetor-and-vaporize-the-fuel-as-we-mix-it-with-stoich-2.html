Guest Question.

A mixture of fuels is E85, which is 85% ethanol and 15% gasoline (assume octane) by mass. Assume we put the fuel and air, both at \(T _{ o }, P _{ o }\), into a carburetor and vaporize the fuel as we mix it with stoichiometric air before it flows to an engine. Assume the engine has an efficiency as work divided by the lower heating value of 30%, and we want it to deliver 55 hp. We use heat from the exhaust flow (900 R) for the carburetor. Find the lower heating value of this fuel (Btu/lbm), the rate of fuel consumption, the heating rate needed in the carburetor, and the rate of entropy generation in the carburetor.

Admin
Solution

The heating value of the liquid fuel blend (values from Tbl. 13.3 converted)

\(\begin{aligned}LHV &=0.85 LHV _{\text {ethanol }}+0.15 LHV _{\text {octane }} \\&=(0.85 \times 26811+0.15 \times 44425) 0.42992 Btu / lbm =12662 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\dot{ W }=0.3 \dot{ m }_{ fuel } LHV\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ m }_{\text {fual }} &=\dot{ W } /(0.3 \times LHV )=55 hp \times 2544 Btu / h – hp /(0.3 \times 12662 Btu / bm ) \\&=36.835 lbm / h =0.0102 lbm / s\end{aligned}\)

 

The difference in heating value based on liquid versus vapor fuel is

 

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta LHV &=[0.85(27731-26811)+0.15(44788-44425)] 0.42992 \\&=(0.85 \times 920+0.15 \times 363) 0.42992=359.6 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\dot{ Q }=\dot{ m }_{\text {fuel }} \Delta LHV =0.0102 lbm / s \times 359.6 Btu / lbm = 3 . 6 8 Btu / s\)

 

Entropy generation is from vaporizing the fuel by transfer of heat from 900 R exhaust gas to the intake system at To and then mixing it with air. The mole fractions are

Ethanol: M = 46.069; Octane: M = 114.232

\(\begin{aligned}\text { DIV }=&[0.85 / 46.069]+[0.15 / 114.232]=0.01845+0.001313=0.019764 \\& y_{\text {ethanol }}=0.01845 / 0.019764=0.9336 \\& y_{\text {octane }}=1-y_{\text {ethanol }}=0.06644\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{gathered}0.93356 C _{2} H _{5} OH +0.06644 C _{8} H _{18}+3.63118\left( O _{2}+3.76 N _{2}\right) \Rightarrow \\2.39864 CO _{2}+3.39864 H _{2} O +13.65324 N _{2}\end{gathered}\)

 

\(\begin{array}{ll}C \text { balance: } & 0.93356 \times 2+0.06644 \times 8=2.39864=v_{ CO 2} \\H \text { balance: } & 0.93356 \times 6+0.06644 \times 18=6.79728=2 v _{ H 2 O }\end{array}\)

 

So the A/F ratio on a mole and mass basis are

\(\begin{aligned}& A / F – mole =3.63118 \times 4.76 / 1=17.2844 \\& A / F – mass =17.2844 \times 28.97 / 50.5996=9.896\end{aligned}\)

 

The fuel was mixed before the carburetor so afterwards we have a 1: 17.2844 mix.

\(\begin{aligned}& s _{ fg \text { ethanol } }=(67.434-38.321) / 46.069=0.63194 Btu / lbm – R \\& s _{ fg \text { octane }}=(111.399-86.122) / 114.232=0.22128 Btu / lbm – R \\& s _{ fg \text { fuel mix }} =0.85 \times 0.63194+0.15 \times 0.22128=0.57034 Btu / lbm – R \\& M _{\text {fuel }}=0.9336 \times 46.069+0.06644 \times 114.232=50.5996\end{aligned}\)

 

Entropy Eq.:

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ S }_{ gen }=& \text { out }- in =\dot{ m }_{\text {fuel }}\left[ s _{ fg }- R \ln \left( y _{\text {fual }}\right)\right]+\dot{ m }_{\text {air }}\left[- R \ln \left( y _{\text {air }}\right)\right]-\dot{ Q } / T _{\text {exhaust }} \\=& 0.0102 \times\left[0.57034-\frac{1.98589}{50.5996} \ln \left(\frac{1}{18.2844}\right)\right] Btu / s – R \\&-0.0102 \times 9.896 \times 0.06855 \ln \left(\frac{17.2844}{18.2844}\right) Btu / s – R -\frac{3.68}{900} Btu / s – R \\=& 0.00698+0.000389-0.004089= 0 . 0 0 3 2 8 Btu / s – R\end{aligned}\)
