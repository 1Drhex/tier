Guest Question.

The slender bar of and Figure 1.16 is pinned at A and held in the horizontal position by a cable. The cable is cut at t = 0.

(a) What is the bar’s angular velocity after it has rotated through 10°?

(b) What are the reactions the pin support after it has rotated through 10°?

Admin
Solution

(a) Let position 1 refer to the bar immediately after the cable is cut. Let position 2 refer to the bar after it has rotated through 10°. All external forces are conservative; thus, conservation of energy applies between positions 1 and 2 as

\(T_{1}+V_{1}=T_{2}+V_{2}\) (a)

Take the datum for potential energy calculations for the gravity force to be position 1, then\(V_{1}=0,\) and \(V_{2}= -\frac{mgL}{3} \sin 10°\). The kinetic energy in position 1 is zero, and

\(T_{1}=\frac{1}{2}m\overline{v} ^{2}_{2}+\frac{1}{2} \left(\frac{1}{12} mL^{2}\right)\omega ^{2}_{2} \) (b)

Kinematics (the relative velocity equation) is used to relate the velocity of the mass center to the angular velocity of the bar so that Substituting into Equation (a), we have \(\overline{v}=\frac{L}{3}\omega\)

\(0=\frac{1}{2} m\left(\frac{L}{3}\omega \right) ^{2} +\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{1}{12} mL^{2}\right)\omega ^{2}_{2} -\frac{mgL}{3} \sin 10° \) (c)

which is solved to yield

\(\omega =\sqrt{\frac{24g}{7L} \sin 10°} =0.818\sqrt{\frac{g}{L} } \) (d)

(b) Summing moments about the pin support on the free-body diagrams after the body has rotated through 10° are illustrated in Figure 1.23. Taking moments about the pin support yields \(\alpha =\frac{12g}{7L},\) which is the same as the initial value. This is to be expected, as the external forces are constant, which implies uniformly accelerated motion. Summing forces using the free-body diagrams according to \(\left(\sum{F} \right)_{ext}=\left(\sum{F} \right)_{eff}\) give

\(R_{x}i+\left(R_{y}-mg\right)j =m\frac{L}{3}\left(\frac{12g}{7L}\right) \left(-\sin 10°i-\cos10°j \right)\)

\(+m\frac{1}{3}\left(\frac{24g}{7L}\sin10° \right) \left(-\cos10°i+\sin10°j \right)\) (e)

By equating coefficients of the unit vectors, the reactions are determined as

\(R_{x}= -\frac{4mg}{7} \sin 10°\left(1+2\cos 10°\right) =- 0.295mg \) (f)

\(R_{y}=mg \left(1-\frac{4}{7} \cos 10°+\frac{8}{7} \sin ^{2}10° \right) =0.472 mg\) (g)
