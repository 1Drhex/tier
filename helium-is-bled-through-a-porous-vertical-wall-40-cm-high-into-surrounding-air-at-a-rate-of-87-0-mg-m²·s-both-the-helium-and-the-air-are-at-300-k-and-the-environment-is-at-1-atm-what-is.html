Guest Question.

Helium is bled through a porous vertical wall, 40 cm high, into surrounding air at a rate of 87.0 mg/m²·s. Both the helium and the air are at 300 K, and the environment is at 1 atm. What is the average concentration of helium at the wall, \(\overline{m_{He,s}}\) ?

Admin
Solution

This is a uniform flux natural convection problem. Here \(\overline{g_{m,He}}\) and \(\overline{\Delta \rho }\) depend on \(\overline{m_{He,s}} \), so the calculation is not as straightforward as it was for thermally driven natural convection.

To begin, let us assume that the concentration of helium at the wall will be small enough that the mass transfer rate is low. Since \(m_{He,e} = 0 , if m_{He,s} \ll 1, then \overline{m_{He,s}-m_{He,e}} \ll 1\)
as well. Both conditions for the analogy to heat transfer will be met.
The mass flux of helium at the wall, \(n_{He,s}\), is known, and because low rates prevail,

 

\(n_{He,s}\approx j_{He,s}=\overline{g_{m,He}} (\overline{m_{He,s}-m_{He,e}})\)

 

Hence,

 

\(\overline{Nu}_{m,L}=\frac{\overline{g_{m,He}}L }{\rho D_{He,air}}=\frac{n_{He,s}L}{\rho D_{He,air}(\overline{m_{He,s}-m_{He,e}} )}\)

 

The appropriate Nusselt number is obtained from the mass transfer analog of eqn. (8.44b):

 

\(\overline{Nu}_{L}=\frac{6}{5}(\frac{Ra^\ast _{L}Pr}{4+9\sqrt{Pr}+10 Pr } )^{1/5}\)

 

\(\overline{Nu}_{m,L}=\frac{6}{5}(\frac{Ra^\ast _{L}Sc}{4+9\sqrt{Sc}+10 Sc } )^{1/5}\)

 

with

 

\(Ra^\ast _{L}=Ra_{L}\overline{Nu}_{m,L}=\frac{g\overline{\Delta \rho }n_{He,s}L^4 }{\mu \rho D^4_{He,air}(\overline{m_{He,s}-m_{He,e}} )}\)

 

The Rayleigh number cannot easily be evaluated without assuming a value of the mass fraction of helium at the wall. As a first guess, we pick \(\overline{m_{He,s}} = 0.010\). Then the film composition is

 

\(m_{He,f} = (0.010+0)/2 = 0.005\)

 

From eqn. (11.8)

 

\(M=\sum\limits_{i}{x_{i}M_{i}} or \frac{1}{M}= \sum\limits_{i}{\frac{m_{i}}{M_{i}} }\)

 

and the ideal gas law, we obtain estimates for the film density (at the film composition) and the wall density

 

\(ρ_{f} = 1.141 kg/m³ and \overline{ρ_{s}} = 1.107 kg/m³\)

 

From eqn. (11.42)

 

\(D_{AB}=\frac{(1.8583\times 10^{-7})T^{3/2}}{p\sigma ^2_{AB}\Omega _{D}} \sqrt{\frac{1}{M_{A}}+\frac{1}{M_{B}} }\)

 

the diffusion coefficient is

 

\(D_{He,air} = 7.119 × 10_{-5} m²/s.\)

 

At this low concentration of helium, we expect the film viscosity to be close to that of pure air. From Appendix A, for air at 300 K

 

\(μ_{f} ≅ μ_{air} = 1.857× 10^{-5} kg/m·s.\)

 

The corresponding Schmidt number is \(Sc = (μ_{f}/ρ_{f}) / D_{He,air} = 0.2286.\)

Furthermore,

 

\(ρ_{e} = ρ_{air} = 1.177 kg/m³\)

 

From these values,

 

\(Ra^\ast _{L}=\frac{9.806(1.177-1.107)(87.0\times 10^{-6})(0.40)^4}{(1.857\times 10^{-5})(1.141)(7.119\times 10^{-5})^2(0.010)}=1.424\times 10^9\)

 

We may now evaluate the mass transfer Nusselt number

 

\(\overline{Nu}_{m,L}=\frac{6[(1.424\times 10^9)(0.2286)]^{1/5}}{5[4+9\sqrt{0.2286}+10(0.2286) ]^{1/5}} =37.73\)

 

From this we calculate

 

\((\overline{m_{He,s}-m_{He,e}})=\frac{n_{He,s}L}{\rho D_{He,air}\overline{Nu}_{m,L} }\)

 

\(=\frac{(87.0\times 10^{-6})(0.40)}{(1.141)(7.119\times 10^{-5})(37.73)}=0.01136\)

 

We have already noted that \((\overline{m_{He,s}- m_{He,e}}) = \overline{m_{He,s}}\), so we have obtained an average wall concentration 14% higher than our initial guess of 0.010.
Using \(\overline{m_{He,s}}= 0.01136\) as our second guess, we repeat the preceding calculations with revised values of the densities to obtain

 

\(\overline{m_{He,s}} = 0.01142\)

 

Since this result is within 0.5% of our second guess, a third iteration is unnecesary.
