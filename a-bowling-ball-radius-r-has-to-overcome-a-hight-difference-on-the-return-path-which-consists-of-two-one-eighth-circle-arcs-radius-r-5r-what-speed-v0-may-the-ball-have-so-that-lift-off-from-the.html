Guest Question.

A bowling ball (radius \(r\) ) has to overcome a hight difference on the return path which consists of two one-eighth circle arcs (radius \(R = 5r\) ).

What speed \(v_{0} \) may the ball have, so that lift-off from the path in the upper sector \(A\) is prevented?

Admin
Solution

We first determine the velocity in sector \(A\) from the the energy conservation law

\(T+V=T_{0}+V_{0}\) .

With \(\Theta _{c}=2mr^{2}/5\) , \(v=r\omega \) and \(h=2R\left(1-\cos 45°\right) =5r\left(2-\sqrt{2} \right) \) follow (zero level of potential at depth of \(D\) )

\(T_{0}=\frac{1}{2}mv^{2}_{0}+\frac{1}{2}\Theta _{c}\omega ^{2}_{0}=\frac{7}{10}mv^{2}_{0} \) , \(T=\frac{7}{10}mv^{2}\) ,

\(V_{0}=mg\left(R-h+r\right)=\left(5\sqrt{2} -4\right)mgr\) , \(V=mg6r\sin \varphi \) .

Solving for \(v\) leads to

\(v^{2 }=v^{2}_{0}-\frac{60\sin \varphi +40-50\sqrt{2} }{7}gr \) .

In sector \(A\) the centripetal acceleration of the center of mass \(C\) is \(a_{n}=v^{2}/6r\) . Thus, from the equation of motion

\(\searrow :ma_{n}=-N+mg\sin \varphi\) ,

we obtain

\(N\left(\varphi \right)=-\frac{mv^{2}_{0} }{6r}+\frac{mg}{42}\left(102\sin \varphi -50\sqrt{2}+40 \right) \) .

The minimum force \(N^{*} \) in sector \(A\) appears at \(\varphi =45°\) . Therefore, so that lift-off at this point is prevented, the following condition must be fulfilled:

\(N^{*}=N\left(45\right)\geq 0 \rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{v_{0}\leq \sqrt{gr\frac{\sqrt{2}+40 }{7} } =2.43\sqrt{gr} } }\) .
