Guest Question.

Assume that the diode in the figure has \(V_{\text {on }}=0.7 V\), but is otherwise ideal The magnitude of the current \(i_{2}\) (in mA) is equal to ____.

Admin
Solution

Let the diode is in ON state as shown below in figure

\(\text { Let, } i_{2}=i_{1}+i_{D}=\)

Applying KVL

\(\begin{aligned}&i_{2}=\frac{2-0.7}{6}=0.216 mA \\&i_{1}=\frac{0.7}{2} mA =0.35 mA \\&i_{2}<i_{1}\end{aligned}\)

The Diode is forward biased and it required positive current to flow through it for its conduction.
So, diode current \(i_{D}=-0.134\) mA

\(i_{D}\)is negative so, Diode is in OFF position

\(\therefore \quad i_{2}=\frac{2}{2+6} mA =0.25 mA\)

Here, we obtained negative \(i_{D}\) so, the diode will remain off.
Hence, the correct Answer is (0.25 mA)
