Müller’s Method

Use Müller’s method with guesses of \( x_0, x_1 \), and \(x_2 = 4.5, 5.5, \), and 5, respectively, to determine a root of the equation

\(f(x) = x^3 − 13x − 12 \)

Note that the roots of this equation are −3, −1, and 4.

Admin
Solution

First, we evaluate the function at the guesses:

\( \begin{matrix}f(4.5) = 20.625&& f(5.5) = 82.875&&f(5) = 48 \end{matrix} \)

which can be used to calculate

\(\begin{matrix}h_{0}=5.5 − 4.5 = 1&& h_1 = 5 − 5.5 = −0.5\\ \\ \delta _{0}=\frac{82.875 − 20.625}{5.5 − 4.5} = 62.25 && \delta _{1}=\frac{48 − 82.875}{5 − 5.5}= 69.75 \end{matrix} \)

These values in turn can be substituted into Eqs. (7.24) through (7.26) to compute

\( \begin{matrix} a=\frac{69.75 − 62.25}{−0.5 + 1}= 15&& b = 15(−0.5) + 69.75 = 62.25&& c = 48\end{matrix} \)

\( a=\frac{\delta _{1}=\delta _{0}}{h_{1}+h_{0}} \) (7.24)

\(c=f(x_{2}) \) (7.26)

The square root of the discriminant can be evaluated as

\(\sqrt{62.25^2 − 4(15)48} = 31.54461 \)

Then, because \(\left|62.25 + 31.54451\right|\gt \left|62.25 − 31.54451\right| \), a positive sign is employed in
the denominator of Eq. (7.27b), and the new root estimate can be determined as

\( x_{3}=5+\frac{−2(48)}{62.25 + 31.54451 }= 3.976487 \)

\(x_{3}=x_{2}+\frac{−2c}{b\pm \sqrt{b^2 − 4ac} } \) (7.27b)

for which the approximate error is

\(\varepsilon _{a}=\left|\frac{−1.023513}{3.976487} \right| \)100% = 25.74%

Because the error is large, new guesses are assigned; \(x_0\) is replaced by \(x_1\), \(x_1\) is replaced by \(x_2\), and \(x_2\) is replaced by \(x_3\). Therefore, for the new iteration,

\( \begin{matrix}x_0 = 5.5 &&x_1 = 5 && x_2 = 3.976487 \end{matrix} \)

and the calculation is repeated. The results, tabulated below, show that the method converges rapidly on the root, \(x_r = 4\):

i \( x_{r}\) \(\varepsilon _{a} \)(%)
0 5
1 3.976487 25.74
2 4.00105 0.6139
3 4 0.0262
4 4 0.0000119
