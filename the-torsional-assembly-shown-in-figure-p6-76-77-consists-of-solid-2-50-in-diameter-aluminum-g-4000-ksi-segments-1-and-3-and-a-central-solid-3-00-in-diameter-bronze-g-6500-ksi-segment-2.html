Guest Question.

The torsional assembly shown in Figure P6.76/77 consists of solid 2.50-in.-diameter aluminum [G = 4,000 ksi] segments (1) and (3) and a central solid 3.00-in.-diameter bronze [G = 6,500 ksi] segment (2). Concentrated torques of \(T_{B}=T_{0} \text { and } T_{C}=2 T_{0}\) are applied to the assembly at B and C, respectively. If \(T_{0}\) = 20 kip-in., determine:
(a) the maximum shear stress magnitude in aluminum segments (1) and (3).
(b) the maximum shear stress magnitude in bronze segment (2).
(c) the rotation angle of joint C.

Admin
Solution

Section Properties: The polar moments of inertia for the aluminum and bronze segments are:

\(\begin{aligned}&J_{1}=J_{3}=\frac{\pi}{32}(2.50 \text { in. })^{4}=3.834952 in. ^{4} \\&J_{2}=\frac{\pi}{32}(3.00 in .)^{4}=7.952156 in .^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Equilibrium: Consider a free-body diagram cut around joint B, where the external torque \(T_{B}\) is applied:

\(\Sigma M_{x}=-T_{1}+T_{2}+T_{0}=0\) (a)

and also consider a FBD cut around joint C, where the external torque \(T_{C}\) is applied:

\(\Sigma M_{x}=-T_{2}+T_{3}+2 T_{0}=0\) (b)

Geometry-of-Deformation Relationship: Since the two ends of the torsion structure are securely attached to fixed supports at A and D, the sum of the angles of twist in the three shafts must equal zero:

\(\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}+\phi_{3}=0\) (c)

Torque-Twist Relationships:

\(\phi_{1}=\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}} \quad \phi_{2}=\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}} \quad \phi_{3}=\frac{T_{3} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}\) (d)

Compatibility Equation: Substitute the torque-twist relationships [Eqs. (d)] into the Geometry-ofdeformation relationship [Eq. (c)] to obtain the compatibility equation:

\(\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}+\frac{T_{3} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}=0\) (e)

Solve the Equations: The approach used here will be to reduce the variables in Eq. (e) by replacing \(T_{1}\) and \(T_{3}\) with equivalent expressions involving \(T_{2}\). From Eq. (a):

\(T_{1}=T_{2}+T_{0}\) (f)

and from Eq. (b):

\(T_{3}=T_{2}-2 T_{0}\) (g)

Substitute Eqs. (f) and (g) into Eq. (e) and simplify to derive an expression for \(T_{2}\):

\(\begin{array}{r}\frac{\left(T_{2}+T_{0}\right) L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}+\frac{\left(T_{2}-2 T_{0}\right) L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}=0 \\T_{2}\left[\frac{L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}+\frac{L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}\right]=\frac{2 T_{0} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}-\frac{T_{0} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}} \\T_{2}=\frac{\frac{2 T_{0} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}-\frac{T_{0} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}}{\frac{L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}+\frac{L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}} (h)\end{array}\)

Since \(G_{1}=G_{3} \text { and } J_{1}=J_{3}\), Eq. (h) can be further simplified and \(T_{2}\) can be computed as:

\(\begin{aligned}T_{2} &=\frac{T_{0}}{J_{1} G_{1}}\left[\frac{\left(2 L_{3}-L_{1}\right)}{\frac{L_{1}+L_{3}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}}\right] \\&=\frac{20 \text { kip-in. }}{\left(3.834952 in. ^{4}\right)(4,000 ksi )}\left[\frac{2(12 in .)-12 in .}{\frac{12 in.+12 in .}{\left(3.834952 in. ^{4}\right)(4,000 ksi )}+\frac{24 in .}{\left(7.952156 in .^{4}\right)(6,500 ksi )}}\right] \\&=7.711461 kip \text {-in. }\end{aligned}\)

Backsubstitute this result into Eqs. (f) and (g) to obtain \(T_{1} \text { and } T_{3}\):

\(\begin{aligned}&T_{1}=7.711461 \text { kip-in. }+20 \text { kip-in. }=27.711461 \text { kip-in. } \\&T_{3}=7.711461 \text { kip-in. }-2(20 \text { kip-in. })=-32.288539 \text { kip-in. }\end{aligned}\)

Maximum Shear Stress: The maximum shear stress magnitudes in the three segments are:

\(\begin{aligned}&\tau_{1}=\frac{T_{1} c_{1}}{J_{1}}=\frac{(27.711461 kip – in .)(2.500 in . / 2)}{3.834952 in .^{4}}=9.03 ksi \\&\tau_{2}=\frac{T_{2} c_{2}}{J_{2}}=\frac{(7.711461 kip – in .)(3.000 in . / 2)}{7.952156 in .^{4}}=1.455 ksi \\&\tau_{3}=\frac{T_{3} c_{3}}{J_{3}}=\frac{(32.288539 kip – in .)(2.500 in . / 2)}{3.834952 in .^{4}}=10.52 ksi\end{aligned}\)

(a) Maximum Shear Stress Magnitude in Aluminum Segments:

\(\tau_{3}=10.52 ksi\)

(b) Maximum Shear Stress Magnitude in Bronze Segment:

\(\tau_{2}=1.455 ksi\)

(c) Rotation Angle of Flange C Relative to Support A: The angle of twist in member (1) can be defined by the difference in rotation angles at the two ends:

\(\phi_{1}=\phi_{B}-\phi_{A} \quad \therefore \phi_{B}=\phi_{A}+\phi_{1}\) (i)

Similarly, the angle of twist in member (2) can be defined by:

\(\phi_{2}=\phi_{C}-\phi_{B} \quad \therefore \phi_{C}=\phi_{B}+\phi_{2}\) (j)

To derive an expression for \(\phi_{C}\), substitute Eq. (i) into Eq. (j), and note that joint A is restrained from rotating; therefore, \(\phi_{A}=0\).

\(\phi_{C}=\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}\)

The angle of twist in member (1) is:

\(\phi_{1}=\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}=\frac{(27.711461 kip – in .)(12 in .)}{\left(3.834952 in. ^{4}\right)(4,000 ksi )}=0.021678 rad\)

The angle of twist in member (2) is:

\(\phi_{2}=\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}=\frac{(7.711461 \text { kip-in. })(24 in .)}{\left(7.952156 in .^{4}\right)(6,500 ksi )}=0.003581 rad\)

The rotation angle of flange C is thus:

\(\phi_{C}=\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}=0.021678 rad +0.003581 rad =0.025259 rad =0.0253 rad\)
