Guest Question.

Chloroform M=119.38 kg / kmol is classified as a hazardous material because prolonged exposure to it is harmful to health. Inhalation of large amount of chloroform may cause liver and kidney damage. The permissible exposure limit (PEL) of chloroform, established by the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (O S H A), is \( 240 mg / m ^{3}\). A lab technician, working in a lab space of 30 \( m ^{3}\), has poured liquid chloroform into 10 test tubes with inner diameter of 25 mm. Each test tube holds equal amount of liquid chloroform at a level of 1 cm from the top (Fig.14-38 ). The chloroform vapor pressure is 21 kPa, and evaporation of chloroform to the surrounding air is occurring at \( 25^{\circ} C\) and 1 atm. If the diffusion coefficient of chloroform in air is \( D_{A B}=0.104 cm ^{2} / s\), determine whether or not it is safe for the technician to remain in the lab for an hour during the evaporation of chloroform from the test tubes.

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION In this example, the concepts of Prevention through Design (PtD) are applied in conjunction with the concepts of diffusion in a moving medium.

Assumptions 1 Steady operating conditions exist. 2 Chloroform vapor and atmospheric air are ideal gases. 3 The amount of air dissolved in liquid chloroform is negligible. 4 Temperatures of air and chloroform remain constant at \( 25^{\circ} C\).

Properties The molar mass of chloroform is given as M=119.38 kg / kmol. The diffusion coefficient of chloroform in air is given as \( D_{A B}=0.104 cm ^{2} / s\).

Analysis The vapor pressure of chloroform at \( 25^{\circ} C\) is given as \( P_{A 0}=21 kPa _{\text {, }}\) and the mole fraction of chloroform vapor (species A) at the interface is determined from

\( y_{A, 0}=\frac{P_{A, 0}}{P}=\frac{21 kPa }{101.325 kPa }=0.20725\)

The total molar density throughout the tube remains constant because of the constant temperature and pressure conditions and is determined to be

\( C=\frac{P}{R_{u} T}=\frac{101.325 kPa }{\left(8.314 kPa \cdot m ^{3} / kmol \cdot K \right)(273+25) K }=0.0409 kmol / m ^{3}\)

Then, the diffusion rate per unit interface area for one test tube is

\( \frac{\dot{N}_{A}}{A}=\frac{C D_{A B}}{L} \ln \left(\frac{1-y_{A, L}}{1-y_{A, 0}}\right) \)

 

\( =\frac{\left(0.0409 kmol / m ^{3}\right)\left(0.104 cm ^{2} / s \right)\left(1 \times 10^{-4} m ^{2} / cm ^{2}\right)}{0.01 m } \ln \left(\frac{1-0}{1-0.20725}\right) \)

 

\( =9.8789 \times 10^{-6} kmol / s \cdot m ^{2}\)

 

The evaporation rate of chloroform from one test tube is

\( \dot{m} =M D^{2} \frac{\pi}{4} \frac{\dot{N}_{A}}{A} \)

 

\( =(119.38 kg / kmol )(0.025 m )^{2} \frac{\pi}{4}\left(9.8789 \times 10^{-6} kmol / s \cdot m ^{2}\right) \)

 

\( =5.789 \times 10^{-7} kg / s \)

 

\( =34.734 mg / min \)

 

The total rate of chloroform being evaporated from ten test tubes to the surrounding air is

\( \dot{m}_{\text {total }}=10(34.734 mg / min )=347.34 mg / min \)

For a lab space of \( 30 m ^{3}\), the time for the chloroform to reach the PEL (permissible exposure limit) from the evaporation is Time \( = PEL \times \frac{\text { Lab space }}{\dot{m}_{\text {total }}}=\left(240 mg / m ^{3}\right)\left(\frac{30 m ^{3}}{347.34 mg / min }\right)=20.7 min <60 min\)

Discussion It takes 20 minutes and 44 seconds for the evaporated chloroform to reach the PEL established by the OSHA. Therefore, it would not be safe for the technician to remain in the lab for an hour. Substances that can release toxic vapors should be handled under a fume hood.
