Guest Question.

Given: A state of stress referenced to an x−y−z coordinate is known to be:

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} \sigma _{xx} & \sigma_{xy} & \sigma _{xz} \\ \sigma_{yx} & \sigma _{yy} & \sigma_{yz} \\ \sigma_{zx} & \sigma _{zy} & \sigma _{zz} \end{matrix} \right ]= \left [ \begin{matrix} 50 & - 10 & 15 \\ - 10 & 25 & 30 \\ 15 & 30 & - 5 \end{matrix} \right ]\)(Ksi)

PROBLEM

Find (a) the principal stresses and (b) the direction cosines that define the principal stress coordinate system.

Admin
Solution

This is the same stress tensor considered in As a part of that problem the first, second, and third stress invariants were found to be

Θ = 70 Ksi

\(\Phi =- 350(Ksi)^2\)

 

\(\Psi =- 65375(Ksi)^3\)

a. Determining the Principal Stresses: In accordance with Equation 2.17,

\(\sigma _{pm}^3 -\Theta \sigma _{pm}^2 + \Phi \sigma _{pm}- \Psi=0\) (2.17)

the three principal stresses are the roots of the following cubic equation:

\(\sigma ^3 -70 \sigma^2 – 350 \sigma + 65375=0\)

The three roots of this equation represent the three principal stresses, and are given by

\(\sigma_{p1}= 54.21 Ksi, \sigma_{p2}= 43.51 Kis and \sigma_{p3}= -27.72 Ksi\)

b. Determining the Direction Cosines: The first two of Equations 2.16 and 2.8 are used to form three independent equations in three unknowns. We have

\((\sigma _{pn}-\sigma _{xx})c_{pnx}-\tau _{xy}c_{pny}-\tau _{xz}c_{pnz}=0\) (2.16a)

\(-\tau _{xy}c_{pnx}+(\sigma _{pn}-\sigma _{yy})c_{pny}-\tau _{yz}c_{pnz}=0\) (2.16b)

\(-\tau _{xz}c_{pnx}-\tau _{yz}c_{pny}+(\sigma _{pn}-\sigma _{zz})c_{pnz}=0\) (2.16c)

\((c_{x^\prime x})^2+(c_{x^\prime y })^2+(c_{x^\prime z})^2=1\) (2.8)

\((\sigma _{pn}- \sigma _{xx})c_{pnx} – \tau _{xy}c_{pny} – \tau _{xz}c_{pnz}= 0\)

 

\(– \tau _{xy}c_{pny}+(\sigma _{pn}- \sigma _{yy})c_{pny} -\tau _{yz}c_{pnz}= 0\)

 

\((c_{pnx})^2 + (c_{pny})^2 + (c_{pnz})^2 = 1\)

Direction cosines for \(σ_{p1}\): The three independent equations become:

\((54.21 – 50)c_{plx} + 10c_{ply} – 15c_{plz} =0\)

 

\(10c_{plx} + (54.21 – 25)c_{ply} -30c_{plz} = 0 \)

 

\((c_{plx})^2+(c_{ply})^2+(c_{plz})^2 =1\)

Solving simultaneously, we obtain:

\(c_{plx}\) = -0.9726

\(c_{ply}\)= 0.1666

\(c_{plz}\)= -0.1620

Direction cosines for σp2: The three independent equations become:

\((43.51 – 50)c_{p2x} +10c_{p2y} – 15c_{p2z}= 0\)

 

\(10c_{p2x} +(43.51 – 25)c_{p2y} – 30c_{p2z}=0\)

 

\((c_{p2x})^2 +(c_{p2y})^2 +(c_{p2z})^2 =1\)

 

Solving simultaneously, we obtain

\(c_{p2x}= -0.05466\)

 

\(c_{p2y}= -0.8416\)

 

\(c_{p2z}= -0.5738\)

 

Direction cosines for \(σ_{p3}\) : The three independent equations become:

\((-27.72 -50)c_{p3x} + 10c_{p3y} – 15c_{p3z} =0\)

 

\(10c_{p3x} +(-27.72 -25)c_p3y – 30c_{p3z} =0\)

 

\((c_{p3x})^2 +(c_{p3y})^2 +(c_{p3z})^2 =1\)

 

Solving simultaneously, we obtain:

\(c_{p3x} = -0.8276\)

 

\(c_{p3y} = 0.2259\)

 

\(c_{p3z} = 0.5138\)
