Guest Question.

As shown in Fig. 8–17a, two plates are clamped by washer-faced \(\frac{1}{2}\) in-20 UNF \(\times 1 \frac{1}{2}\) in
SAE grade 5 bolts each with a standard \(\frac{1}{2}\) N steel plain washer.
(a) Determine the member spring rate \(k_{m}\) if the top plate is steel and the bottom plate is gray cast iron.
(b) Using the method of conical frusta, determine the member spring rate \(k_{m}\) if both plates are steel.
(c) Using Eq. (8–23), determine the member spring rate \(k_{m}\) if both plates are steel. Compare the results with part (b).

(d) Determine the bolt spring rate\(k_{b} \text { . }\)
\(\frac{k_{m}}{E d}=A \exp (B d / l)\) (8–23)

Admin
Solution

From Table A–32, the thickness of a standard 1/2 N plain washer is 0.095 in.
(a) As shown in Fig. 8–17b, the frusta extend halfway into the joint the distance

 

\(\frac{1}{2}(0.5+0.75+0.095)=0.6725 \text { in }\)

 

The distance between the joint line and the dotted frusta line is 0.6725 – 0.5 – 0.095 = 0.0775 in. Thus, the top frusta consist of the steel washer, steel plate, and 0.0775 in of the cast iron. Since the washer and top plate are both steel with \(E=30\left(10^{6}\right) \text { psi, }\) they can be considered a single frustum of 0.595 in thick. The outer diameter of the frustum of the steel member at the joint interface is \(0.75+2(0.595) \tan 30^{\circ}=1.437 \text { in }\) The outer diameter at the midpoint of the entire joint is \(0.75+2(0.6725) \tan 30^{\circ}=\) 1.527 in. Using Eq. (8–20), the spring rate of the steel is

 

\(k=\frac{0.5774 \pi E d}{\ln \frac{(1.155 t+D-d)(D+d)}{(1.155 t+D+d)(D-d)}}\) (8–20)

 

\(k_{1}=\frac{0.5774 \pi(30)\left(10^{6}\right) 0.5}{\ln \left\{\frac{[1.155(0.595)+0.75-0.5](0.75+0.5)}{[1.155(0.595)+0.75+0.5](0.75-0.5)}\right\}}=30.80\left(10^{6}\right) lbf / in\)

 

From Tables 8–8 or A–5, for gray cast iron, E = 14.5 Mpsi. Thus for the upper castiron frustum

 

\(k_{2}=\frac{0.5774 \pi(14.5)\left(10^{6}\right) 0.5}{\ln \left\{\frac{[1.155(0.0775)+1.437-0.5](1.437+0.5)}{[1.155(0.0775)+1.437+0.5](1.437-0.5)}\right\}}=285.5\left(10^{6}\right) lbf / in\)

 

For the lower cast-iron frustum

 

\(k_{3}=\frac{0.5774 \pi(14.5)\left(10^{6}\right) 0.5}{\ln \left\{\frac{[1.155(0.6725)+0.75-0.5](0.75+0.5)}{[1.155(0.6725)+0.75+0.5](0.75-0.5)}\right\}}=14.15\left(10^{6}\right) lbf / in\)

 

The three frusta are in series, so from Eq. (8–18)

 

\(\frac{1}{k_{m}}=\frac{1}{k_{1}}+\frac{1}{k_{2}}+\frac{1}{k_{3}}+\cdots+\frac{1}{k_{i}}\) (8–18)

 

\(\frac{1}{k_{m}}=\frac{1}{30.80\left(10^{6}\right)}+\frac{1}{285.5\left(10^{6}\right)}+\frac{1}{14.15\left(10^{6}\right)}\)

 

This results in \(k_{m}=9.378\left(10^{6}\right) lbf / in .\)
(b) If the entire joint is steel, Eq. (8–22) with \(l=2(0.6725)=1.345\) in gives

 

\(k_{m}=\frac{0.5774 \pi E d}{2 \ln \left(5 \frac{0.5774 l+0.5 d}{0.5774 l+2.5 d}\right)}\) (8–22)

 

\(k_{m}=\frac{0.5774 \pi(30.0)\left(10^{6}\right) 0.5}{2 \ln \left\{5\left[\frac{0.5774(1.345)+0.5(0.5)}{0.5774(1.345)+2.5(0.5)}\right]\right\}}=14.64\left(10^{6}\right) lbf / in .\)

 

(c) From Table 8–8, A = 0.787 15, B = 0.628 73. Equation (8–23) gives

 

\(\frac{k_{m}}{E d}=A \exp (B d / l)\) (8–23)

 

\(k_{m}=30\left(10^{6}\right)(0.5)(0.78715) \exp [0.62873(0.5) / 1.345]=14.92\left(10^{6}\right) lbf / in\)

 

For this case, the difference between the results for Eqs. (8–22) and (8–23) is less than 2 percent.

 

\(k_{m}=\frac{0.5774 \pi E d}{2 \ln \left(5 \frac{0.5774 l+0.5 d}{0.5774 l+2.5 d}\right)}\) (8–22)

 

\(\frac{k_{m}}{E d}=A \exp (B d / l)\) (8–23)

(d) Following the procedure of Table 8–7, the threaded length of a 0.5-in bolt is \(L_{T}=2(0.5)+0.25=1.25 \text { in. }\) The length of the unthreaded portion is \(l_{d}=1.5-\)\(1.25=0.25 \text { in. }\). The length of the unthreaded portion in grip is \(l_{t}=1.345-0.25=\)\(1.095 \text { in. }\). The major diameter area is \(A_{d}=(\pi / 4)\left(0.5^{2}\right)=0.1963 in ^{2}\) From Table 8–2, the tensile-stress area is \(A_{t}=0.1599 in ^{2}\) From Eq. (8–17)

 

\(k_{b}=\frac{A_{d} A_{t} E}{A_{d} l_{t}+A_{t} l_{d}}\) (8–17)

 

\(k_{b}=\frac{0.1963(0.1599) 30\left(10^{6}\right)}{0.1963(1.095)+0.1599(0.25)}=3.69\left(10^{6}\right) lbf / in\)
