Guest Question.

Find the capacity of the raft shown in Figure 30.1. The following information is given.
• The raft dimensions are \(100\) ft. \(\times 100\) ft.
• Average SPT (N) value of the soil is 15.
• Total density of soil is 115 pcf.

Admin
Solution

Step 1: Write down the equation for the allowable bearing capacity of the raft allowing a settle-ment of 2 in. \([q_{(allowable) Raft}].\)

\(q_{(allowable) Raft}=0.22\times N\times C_{w}+\gamma D_{f}\)

Step 2: Find the correction factor for groundwater \((C_{w})\)

\(C_{w}=0.5+0.5\frac{D_{w}}{(D_{f}+B)}\)

\(D_{w},\) depth to groundwater measured from the ground surface (ft.) = 16 ft.; \(D_{f},\) depth to bottom of the footing measured from the ground surface = 11 ft.; B, width of the raft = 100 ft.

\(C_{w}=0.5+0.5\frac{16}{11+100}=0.572\)

Step 3: Use the equation in Step 1 to find \(q_{(allowable) Raft}.\)

\(q_{(allowable) Raft}= 0.22\times N\times C_{w}+\gamma D_{f}\)

 

\(N = 15\)
\(C_{w} = 0.572\)
\(\gamma = 115\) pcf = 0.0575 tcf (\(\gamma\) needs to be converted to tcf since the equation is unit sensitive)
\(D_{f} = 11\) ft.

\(q_{(allowable) Raft}= 0.22\times N\times C_{w}+\gamma D_{f}\)

 

\(q_{(allowable) Raft}=0.22\times 15\times 0.572+0.0575\times 11=2.52\)tsf

Step 4:

Total load that can be carried by the raft \(= 2.52 (100 \times 100\) ft.) tons
\(= 25,200\) tons
