Guest Question.

Restrained Pile at the Head for Offshore Structure (Matlock and Reese, 1961)

Data

The data for the problem are taken from Matlock and Reese (1961). The pile is restrained at the head by the structure on the top of the pile. The pile considered is below the sea bed. The undrained shear strength c and submerged unit weights are obtained by working back from the known values of \(n_{h}\) and T. The other details are

Pile diameter, d = 33 in, pipe pile

\(\begin{aligned}E I &=42.35 \times 10^{10} lb - in ^{2} \\c &=500 lb / ft ^{2} \\\gamma &=40 lb / ft ^{3} \\P_{t} &=150,000 lbs\end{aligned}\)

(a) \(\frac{M_{t}}{P_{t} T}=\frac{-T}{12.25+1.078 T}\), (b) \(T=\frac{E I}{n_{h}}^{\frac{1}{5}}\), (c) \(P_{e}=P_{t} 1-0.67 \frac{e}{T}\)

Required

(a) deflection at the pile head

(b) moment distribution diagram

Admin
Solution

Substituting the known values in Eq (16.30) and simplifying,

 

\(n_{h}=\frac{125 c^{1.5} \sqrt{E I \gamma d} /(1+e / d)^{1.5}}{P_{e}^{1.5}}\) (16.30)

 

\(n_{h}=\frac{458 \times 10^{6}}{P_{e}^{1.5}(1+e / d)^{1.5}}\) (d)

 

Calculations

1. Assume \(e=0, P_{e}=P_{t}=150,000 lb\)

 

From Eqs (d) and (b) \(n_{h}=7.9 lb / in ^{2}, \quad T=140 \text { in }\)

 

From Eq. (a) \(\frac{M_{t}}{P_{t} T}=\frac{-140}{12.25+1.078 \times 140}=-0.858\)

 

or \(M_{t}=-0.858 P_{t} T=P_{t} e\)

 

Therefore \(e=0.858 \times 140=120 \text { in }\)

 

2. \(P_{e}=P_{t} 1-0.67 \frac{e}{T}=1.5 \times 10^{5} 1-0.67 \times \frac{120}{140}=63.857 lb\)

 

\(1+\frac{e}{d}^{1.5}=1+\frac{120}{33}^{1.5}=10\)

 

Now from Eq. (d), \(n_{h}=2.84 lb / in ^{3}\) from Eq. (b) T= 171.64 in After substitution in Eq. (a)

 

\(\frac{M_{t}}{P_{t} T}=-0.875 \text {, and } e=0.875 \times 171.64=150.2 \text { in }\)

 

\(P_{e}=1-0.67 \times \frac{150.2}{171.64} \times 1.5 \times 10^{5}=62,205 lbs\)

 

3. Continuing this process for a few more steps there will be convergence of values of \(n_{h}\), \(P_{e}\). The final values obtained are

 

\(n_{h}=2.1 lb / in ^{3}, T=182.4 in , \text { and } P_{e}=62,246 lb\)

 

\(M_{t}=-P_{t} e=-150,000 \times 150.2=-22.53 \times 10^{6} lb – in ^{2}\)

 

\(y_{g}=\frac{2.43 P_{e} T^{3}}{E I}=\frac{2.43 \times 62,246 \times(182.4)^{3}}{42.35 \times 10^{10}}=2.17 in\)

 

Moment distribution along the pile may now be calculated by making use of Eq. (16.11) and Table 16.2. Please note that \(M_{t}\) has a negative sign. The moment distribution curve is given in Fig. 16.19. There is a very close agreement between the computed values by direct method and the Reese and Matlock method. The deflection and the negative bending moment as obtained by Reese and Matlock are

 

\(M=\left[P_{t} T\right] A_{m}+\left[M_{t}\right] B_{m}\) (16.11)

 

\(y_{m}=2.307 \text { in and } M_{t}=-24.75 \times 10^{6} lb – in ^{2}\)
