ANALYSIS OF A TWO-STAGE HARMONIC DRIVE

Figure 6.47 shows the cross section of a two-stage harmonic drive. The numbers of teeth are

\(N_{f_1}\)=104; \(N_{c_1}\)=106; \(N_{f_2}\)=108; \(N_{c_2}\)=110

Determine the speed ratio of
(a) circular spline 1 with respect to the wave generator if circular spline 2 is held fixed by brake D
(b) circular spline 2 with respect to the wave generator if circular spline 1 is held fixed by brake C

Admin
Solution

The drive is equivalent to combining two harmonic drives. The wave generators of both stages share the same rotational speed, and the flex splines of both stages are rigidly connected. We employ Table 6.14 to generate the following equations:
• Connection of wave generators:

\(x_1 + y_1= x_2 + y_2\) (6.7-6)

• Connection of flex splines:

\(x_1-\frac{2}{N_{f_1}}y_1=x_2-\frac{2}{N_{f_2}}y_2\) (6.7-7)

where subscripts 1 and 2 indicate the stage number. Equations (6.7-6) and (6.7-7) are valid for parts (a) and (b) of this problem. We now proceed to calculate the speed ratios when either circular spline 1 or circular spline 2 is held fixed.
(a) If circular spline 2 is fixed, then from Table 6.14 we have

\(ω_{c_2}= x_2= 0\) (6.7-8)

Solving Equations (6.7-6)–(6.7-8) and (6.7-1) \([N_c=N_f+2]\) for x\(_1\) and y\(_1\) in terms of y\(_2\), we obtain

\(x_1=\frac{2(N_{f_2}-N_{f_1})}{N_{c_1}N_{f_2}}y_2;\) \(y_1=\frac{N_{c_1}N_{f_2}}{N_{c_1}N_{f_2}}y_2\) (6.7-9)

The speed ratio may be expressed as

\(e_{c_1/w}=\frac{ω_{c_1}}{ω_w}=\frac{x_1}{x_1+y_1}\) (6.7-10)

Substituting Equations (6.7-9) in Equation (6.7-10) and simplifying, we obtain

\(e_{c_1/w}=\frac{2(N_{f_2}-N_{f_1})}{N_{c_1}N_{f_2}}=\frac{2(108-104)}{106\times 108}=\frac 1{1431}\) (6.7-11)

(b) If circular spline 1 is fixed, then from Table 6.14 we have

\(ω_{c_1}=x_{1} = 0\) (6.7-12)

Solving Equations (6.7-6), (6.7-7), (6.7-12), and (6.7-1) for x\(_2\) and y\(_1\) in terms of y\(_2\), we obtain

\(x_2=\frac{2(N_{f_1}-N_{f_2})}{N_{c_1}N_{f_2}}y_2;\) \(y_1=\frac{N_{f_1}N_{c_2}}{N_{c_1}N_{f_2}}y_2\) (6.7-13)

The speed ratio may be expressed as

\(e_{c_2/w}=\frac{ω_{c_2}}{ω_w}=\frac{x_z}{x_1+y_1}\) (6.7-14)

Substituting Equations (6.7-12) and (6.7-13) in Equation (6.7-14) and simplifying, we get

\(e_{c_2/w}=\frac{2(N_{f_1}-N_{f_2})}{N_{f_1}N_{ c_2 }}=\frac {2( 104-108)}{104\times 110}=-\frac 1{1431}\) (6.7-15)
