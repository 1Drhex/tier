Guest Question.

Bolts are used to hold the cover plate on a pressure vessel, which is subjected to an internal pressure varying from zero to 2 MPa. The area over which the pressure acts may be taken to correspond to a 400 mm diameter circle. The bolts are preloaded to the extent of 1.3 times the maximum force exerted by the fluid on the cover plate. The combined stiffness of the parts, held
together by the bolt (including the copper gasket), is four times the stiffness of the bolt. The following data is given for the bolts:
Ultimate tensile strength = 900 N/mm²
Yield strength = 700 N/mm²
Endurance limit in bending = 300 N/mm²
Fatigue stress concentration factor = 2.2
Factor of safety = 1.5
Number of bolts = 8
Determine the size of the bolts assuming fine threads.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } S_{u t}=900 N / mm ^{2} \quad S_{y t}=700 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( S_{e}^{\prime}=300 N / mm ^{2} \quad(f s)=1.5 \quad K_{f}=2.2 \quad k_{c}^{\prime}=4 k_{b}^{\prime} \).

For vessel, D = 400 mm p = 0 to 2 MPa
Step I Endurance limit stress for bolt

\( S_{e}^{\prime}=300 N / mm ^{2} \).

The surface finish factor is incorporated in the fatigue stress concentration factor. The size factor is 1 for the axial load.

\( K_{d}=\frac{1}{K_{f}}=\frac{1}{2.2} \).

\( S_{e}=K_{b} K_{d} S_{e}^{\prime}=(1.0)(1 / 2.2)(300) \).

= 136.36 N/mm²
Step II Construction of Goodman diagram
Maximum force of fluid on cover = P

\( P=\frac{\pi}{4} D^{2} p=\frac{\pi}{4}(400)^{2}(2)=251327.41 N \).

\( P_{i}=1.3 P=1.3(251327.41)=326725.64 N\).

\( \left(\frac{k_{b}^{\prime}}{k_{b}^{\prime}+k_{c}^{\prime}}\right)=\left(\frac{k_{b}^{\prime}}{k_{b}^{\prime}+4 k_{b}^{\prime}}\right)=\frac{1}{5}=0.2 \).

From Eq. (7.23),

\( \Delta P=P\left(\frac{k_{b}^{\prime}}{k_{b}^{\prime}+k_{c}^{\prime}}\right) \) (7.23).

\( \Delta P=P\left(\frac{k_{b}^{\prime}}{k_{b}^{\prime}+k_{c}^{\prime}}\right)=0.2 P \).

From Eq. (7.24), the resultant load on the bolt,
i.e., \( \left(P_{b}\right) \) is given by,

\( P_{b}=P_{i}+\Delta p=P_{i}+0.2 P \).

Therefore, the maximum and minimum forces in the bolt are given by,

\( P_{\max }=P_{i}+(0.20) P \).

= 326 725.64 + (0.20)(251 327.41)
= 376 991.12 N.

\( P_{\min }=P_{i}+(0.20)(0)=326725.64 N \).

\( P_{m}=\frac{1}{2}\left(P_{\max }+P_{\min }\right) \).

\( =\frac{1}{2}(376991.12+326725.64) \).

= 351 858.38 N

\( P_{a}=\frac{1}{2}\left(P_{\max }-P_{\min }\right) \).

\( =\frac{1}{2}(376991.12-326725.64) \).

= 25 132.74 N.

The Goodman diagram for this example is shown in Fig. 7.42.
Step III Permissible stress amplitude
The co-ordinates of point \( C\left(S_{m}, S_{a}\right) \) are obtained by solving the following two equations simultaneously,

\( \frac{S_{m}}{900}+\frac{S_{a}}{136.36}=1 \) (a).

\( S_{m}=\frac{P_{i}}{A}+S_{a}=\frac{326725.64}{A}+S_{a} \) (b).

where A is the tensile stress area of the bolt.

The solution is obtained by Eq. (7.30).
From Eq. (7.30),

\( S_{a}=\frac{S_{u t}-\left(P_{i} / A\right)}{1+\left(S_{u t} / S_{e}\right)}\) (7.30).

\( S_{a}=\frac{S_{u t}-\left(P_{i} / A\right)}{1+\left(S_{u t} / S_{e}\right)}=\frac{900-(326725.64 / A)}{1+(900 / 136.36)} \).

\( =\frac{900-(326725.64 / A)}{7.6} \).

Since \( \sigma_{a}=\frac{S_{a}}{(f s)} \quad \therefore \frac{P_{a}}{A}=\frac{S_{a}}{(f s)} \).

or \( \frac{25132.74}{A}=\frac{900-(326725.64 / A)}{7.6(1.5)} \).

\( \frac{25132.74}{A}=78.95-\frac{28660.14}{A} \).

or \( \frac{53792.88}{A}=78.95 \).

A = 681.35 mm²
There are 8 bolts. Therefore, the area of each bolt is given by,

\( A=\frac{681.35}{8}=85.17 mm ^{2} \).
