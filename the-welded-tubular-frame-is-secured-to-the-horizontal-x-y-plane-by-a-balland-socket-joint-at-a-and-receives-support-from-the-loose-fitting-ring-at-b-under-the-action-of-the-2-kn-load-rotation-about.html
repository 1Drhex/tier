Guest Question.

The welded tubular frame is secured to the horizontal x-y plane by a balland- socket joint at A and receives support from the loose-fitting ring at B. Under the action of the 2-kN load, rotation about a line from A to B is prevented by the cable CD, and the frame is stable in the position shown. Neglect the weight of the frame compared with the applied load and determine the tension T in the cable, the reaction at the ring, and the reaction components at A.

Admin
Solution

The system is clearly three-dimensional with no lines or planes of symmetry, and therefore the problem must be analyzed as a general space system of forces. The free-body diagram is drawn, where the ring reaction is shown in terms of its two components. All unknowns except T may be eliminated by a moment sum about the line AB. The direction of AB is specified by the unit vector

\(n=\frac{1}{\sqrt{6^{2}+4.5^{2} } } (4.5j+6k)=\frac{1}{5}(3j+4k)\)The moment of T about AB

is the component in the direction of AB of the vector moment about the point A and equals\(r_{1}\times T\cdot n\) Similarly the moment of the applied load F about AB is \(r_{2}\times F\cdot n\) With \(\overline{CD} =\sqrt{46.2}\) m, the vector expressions for T, F, \(r_{1}\) , and \(r_{2}\) are

\(T=\frac{T}{\sqrt{46.2}}(2i+2.5j-6k)\) \( F=2j\) kN

 

\( r_{1} =-i+2.5j\) m \( r_{2} =2.5i+6k\) m

The moment equation now becomes

\([\sum{M_{AB} }=0 ]\) \((-1+2.5j)\times \frac{T}{\sqrt{46.2}}(2i+2.5j-6k)\cdot\frac{1}{5} (3j+4k)\)

 

\(+(2.5i+6k)\times (2j)\cdot \frac{1}{5}(3j+4k)=0\)

Completion of the vector operations gives

\(-\frac{48T}{\sqrt{46.2}}+20=0\) \(T=2.83\) kN

and the components of T become

\(T_{x} =0.833\) kN \(T_{y} =1.042\) kN \(T_{z} =-2.50\) kN

We may find the remaining unknowns by moment and force summations as follows:

\([\sum{M_{z} }=0 ]\) \(2(2.5)-4.5B_{x}-1.042(3)=0\) \(B_{x}=0.417\) kN

\([\sum{M_{x} }=0 ]\) \(4.5B_{z}-2(6)-1.042(6)=0\) \(B_{z}=4.06\) kN

\([\sum{F_{x} }=0 ]\) \(A_{x}+0.417+0.833=0\) \(A_{x}=-1.250\) kN

\([\sum{F_{y} }=0 ]\) \(A_{y}+2+1.042=0\) \(A_{y}=-3.04\) kN

\([\sum{F_{z} }=0 ]\) \(A_{z}+4.06-2.50=0\) \(A_{z}=-1.556\) kN

Helpful Hints
1) The advantage of using vector notation in this problem is the freedom to take moments directly about any axis. In this problem this freedom permits the choice of an axis that eliminates five of the unknowns.

2) Recall that the vector r in the expression \(r\times F\) for the moment of a force is a vector from the moment center to any point on the line of action of the force. Instead of r1, an equally simple choice would be the vector \(\overrightarrow{AC}\) .

3) The negative signs associated with the A-components indicate that they are in the opposite direction to those shown on the free-body diagram.
