Guest Question.

Determine the moments at each joint of the frame shown in Fig. \(11-19 (\mathrm{a})\). \(EI\) is constant.

Admin
Solution

Slope-Deflection Equations. Since the ends \(A\) and \(D\) are fixed, Eq. 11-8 applies for all three spans of the frame. Sidesway occurs here since both the applied loading and the geometry of the frame are nonsymmetric. Here the load is applied directly to joint \(B\) and therefore no FEMs act at the joints. As shown in Fig. 11-19a, both joints \(B\) and \(C\) are assumed to be displaced an equal amount \(\Delta\). Consequently, \(\psi_{A B}=\Delta / 12\) and \(\psi_{D C}=\Delta / 18\). Both terms are positive since the cords of members \(A B\) and \(C D\) “rotate” clockwise. Relating \(\psi_{A B}\) to \(\psi_{D C}\), we have \(\psi_{A B}=(18 / 12) \psi_{D C}\). Applying Eq. 11-8 to the frame, we have

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=2 E k\left(2 \theta_{N}+\theta_{F}-3 \psi\right)+(\text { FEM })_{N}\\ \text For Internal Span or End Span with Far End Fixed \end{array} \) (11-8)

\( \begin{array}{l}M_{A B}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{12}\right)\left[2(0)+\theta_{B}-3\left(\frac{18}{12} \psi_{D C}\right)\right]+0=E I\left(0.1667 \theta_{B}-0.75 \psi_{D C}\right) \\ \end{array} \) (1)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{B A}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{12}\right)\left[2 \theta_{B}+0-3\left(\frac{18}{12} \psi_{D C}\right)\right]+0=E I\left(0.333 \theta_{B}-0.75 \psi_{D C}\right) \\ \end{array} \) (2)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{B C}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{15}\right)\left[2 \theta_{B}+\theta_{C}-3(0)\right]+0=E I\left(0.267 \theta_{B}+0.133 \theta_{C}\right) \\ \end{array} \) (3)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{C B}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{15}\right)\left[2 \theta_{C}+\theta_{B}-3(0)\right]+0=E I\left(0.267 \theta_{C}+0.133 \theta_{B}\right) \\ \end{array} \) (4)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{C D}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{18}\right)\left[2 \theta_{C}+0-3 \psi_{D C}\right]+0=E I\left(0.222 \theta_{C}-0.333 \psi_{D C}\right) \\ \end{array} \) (5)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{D C}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{18}\right)\left[2(0)+\theta_{C}-3 \psi_{D C}\right]+0=E I\left(0.111 \theta_{C}-0.333 \psi_{D C}\right) \end{array} \) (6)

Equations of Equilibrium. The six equations contain nine unknowns. Two moment equilibrium equations for joints \(B\) and \(C\), Fig. 11-19b, can be written, namely,

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{B A}+M_{B C}=0 \\ \end{array} \) (7)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{C B}+M_{C D}=0 \end{array} \) (8)

Since a horizontal displacement \(\Delta\) occurs, we will consider summing forces on the entire frame in the \(x\) direction. This yields

\(:^{+} \Sigma F_{x}=0 ; \quad 40-V_{A}-V_{D}=0\)

The horizontal reactions or column shears \(V_{A}\) and \(V_{D}\) can be related to the internal moments by considering the free-body diagram of each column separately, Fig. 11-19c. We have

\( \begin{array}{ll} \Sigma M_{B}=0 ; & V_{A}=-\frac{M_{A B}+M_{B A}}{12} \\ \Sigma M_{C}=0 ; & V_{D}=-\frac{M_{D C}+M_{C D}}{18} \end{array} \)

Thus,

\( 40+\frac{M_{A B}+M_{B A}}{12}+\frac{M_{D C}+M_{C D}}{18}=0 \) (9)

In order to solve, substitute Eqs. (2) and (3) into Eq. (7), Eqs. (4) and (5) into Eq. (8), and Eqs. (1), (2), (5), (6) into Eq. (9). This yields

\( \begin{aligned} 0.6 \theta_{B}+0.133 \theta_{C}-0.75 \psi_{D C} &=0 \\ 0.133 \theta_{B}+0.489 \theta_{C}-0.333 \psi_{D C} &=0 \\ 0.5 \theta_{B}+0.222 \theta_{C}-1.944 \psi_{D C} &=-\frac{480}{E I} \end{aligned} \)

Solving simultaneously, we have

\( E I \theta_{B}=438.81 \quad E I \theta_{C}=136.18 \quad E I \psi_{D C}=375.26 \)

Finally, using these results and solving Eqs. (1)-(6) yields

\(\begin{array}{lrl}M_{A B} & =-208 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ M_{B A} & =-135 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ M_{B C} & =135 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft}\\ M_{C B} & =94.8 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ M_{C D} & =-94.8 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ M_{D C} & =-110 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \end{array}\)
