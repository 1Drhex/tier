Guest Question.

It is required to design a two-stage spur gear reduction unit with 20° full-depth involute teeth. The input shaft rotates at 1440 rpm and receives 10 kW power through a flexible coupling. The speed of the output shaft should be approximately 180 rpm. The gears are made of plain carbon steel 45C8 \( \left(S_{u t}=700 N / mm ^{2}\right) \) and heat-treated to a surface hardness of 340 BHN. The gears are to be machined to the requirement, of Grade 6.
The service factor can be taken as 1.5.
(i) Assuming that the dynamic load to be proportional to the pitch-line velocity, estimate the required value of the module. The factor of safety is 1.5.
(ii) Select the first preference value of the module and determine the correct value of factor of safety for bending, using Buckingham’s equation.
(iii) Determine the factor of safety against pitting.
(iv) Give a list of gear dimensions.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } k W=10 \quad n=1440 rpm \quad C_{s}=1.5 \).

\( S_{u t}=700 N / mm ^{2} \quad(f s)=1.5 \quad BHN =340 \).

Grade of machining = 6
speed of output shaft = 180 rpm
Step I Estimation of module based on beam strength
The total transmission ratio i′ is given by,

\( i^{\prime}=\frac{\text { angular velocity of the first driving gear }}{\text { angular velocity of the last driven gear }} \).

\( =\frac{1440}{180}=8 \).

As discussed in Section 17.15, the speed reduction at each stage (i) is given by,

\( i=\sqrt{i^{\prime}}=\sqrt{8}=2.8284 \)

For a 20° pressure angle, the minimum number of teeth to avoid interference is 18.

\( \therefore \quad z_{p}=18 \text { and } z_{g}=i z_{p}=2.8284 \text { (18) } \).

= 50.91 or 51
The layout of gears is shown in Fig. 17.44. For ease of manufacturing, the pinions 1 and 3 are made identical, while gears 2 and 4 are also made identical.

\( z_{1}=z_{3}=18 \text { and } z_{2}=z_{4}=51 \).

The speeds of the shafts are as follows,

\( n_{A}=1440 rpm \).

\( n_{B}=n_{A} \times\left(\frac{z_{1}}{z_{2}}\right)=1440 \times\left(\frac{18}{51}\right)=508.235 rpm \).

\( n_{C}=n_{B} \times\left(\frac{z_{3}}{z_{4}}\right)=508.235 \times\left(\frac{18}{51}\right)=179.38 rpm \).

The speed of the output shaft is 179.38 or approximately 180 rpm. The velocity factor is unknown at this stage. Assuming the pitch line velocity as 5 m/s.

\( C_{v}=\frac{3}{3+v}=\frac{3}{3+5}=\frac{3}{8} \).

The pair at the second stage, i.e., pinion 3 and gear 4, transmits more torque than the pair consisting of gears 1 and 2. Therefore, pinion 3 and gear 4 are to be designed. From Table 17.3, the Lewis form factor for 18 teeth is 0.308.

From Eq. (17.30),

\( m=\left[\frac{60 \times 10^{6}}{\pi}\left\{\frac{( kW ) C_{s}(f s)}{z_{3} n_{3} C_{v}\left(\frac{b}{m}\right)\left(\frac{S_{u t}}{3}\right) Y}\right\}\right]^{1 / 3} \) (17.30)

\( =\left[\frac{60 \times 10^{6}}{\pi}\left\{\frac{(10)(1.5)(1.5)}{(18)(508.235)\left(\frac{3}{8}\right)(10)\left(\frac{700}{3}\right)(0.308)}\right\}\right]^{1 / 3} \).

= 5.59 mm
Step II Gear dimensions
The fi rst preference value of the module is 6 mm.
m = 6 mm.

\( d_{3}^{\prime}=m z_{3}=6(18)=108 mm \).

\( d_{4}^{\prime}=m z_{4}=6(51)=306 mm\).

b = 10 m = 10(6) = 60 mm
Step III Factor of safety using Buckingham’s equation
Beam strength

\( S_{b}=m b \sigma_{b} Y=6(60)\left(\frac{700}{3}\right)(0.308)=25872 N \).

Tangential force due to rated torque

\( M_{t}=\frac{60 \times 10^{6}( kW )}{2 \pi n_{B}}=\frac{60 \times 10^{6}(10)}{2 \pi(508.235)} \).

\( =187891.36 N – mm \).

\( P_{t}=\frac{2 M_{t}}{d_{3}^{\prime}}=\frac{2(187891.36)}{108}=3479.47 N \).

Dynamic load
For Grade 6,

\( e=8+0.63 \phi \).

For pinion,

\( \phi=m+0.25 \sqrt{d_{3}^{\prime}}=6+0.25 \sqrt{108} \).

\( e_{p}=8+0.63 \phi=13.4168 \mu m \).

For gear,

\( \phi=m+0.25 \sqrt{d_{4}^{\prime}}=6+0.25 \sqrt{306} \).

\( e_{g}=8+0.63 \phi=14.5351 \mu m \).

\( \therefore \quad e=e_{p}+e_{g}=13.4168+14.5351 \).

\( =27.9519 \mu m \text { or }\left(27.9519 \times 10^{-3}\right) mm \).

From Table 17.7, the value of deformation factor C is 11 400 N/mm².

\( v=\frac{\pi d_{3}^{\prime} n_{3}}{60 \times 10^{3}}=\frac{\pi(108)(508.235)}{60 \times 10^{3}}=2.874 m / s \),

\( \text { Also, } \quad b=60 mm \quad P_{t}=3479.47 N \).

From Eq. (17.26),

\( P_{d}=\frac{21 v\left( Ceb +P_{t}\right)}{21 v+\sqrt{\left( Ceb +P_{t}\right)}} \) (17.26),

\( P_{d}=\frac{21 v\left( Ceb +P_{t}\right)}{21 v+\sqrt{\left( Ceb +P_{t}\right)}} \).

\( =\frac{21(2.874)\left[11400\left(27.9519 \times 10^{-3}\right)(60)+3479.47\right]}{21(2.874)+\sqrt{\left[11400\left(27.9519 \times 10^{-3}\right)(60)+3479.47\right]}} \).

= 6473.80 N
Effective load

\( P_{ eff }=\left(C_{s} P_{t}+P_{d}\right)=1.5(3479.47)+6473.80 \).

= 11 693 N
Factor of safety

\( \therefore \quad(f s)=\frac{S_{b}}{P_{\text {eff }}}=\frac{25872}{11693}=2.21 \).

The design is satisfactory and the module should be 6 mm.

Step IV Factor of safety against pitting

\( S_{w}=b Q d_{p}^{\prime} K=60(1.4783)(108)(0.16)\left(\frac{340}{100}\right)^{2} \).

= 17 718 03 N.

\( \therefore \quad(f s)=\frac{S_{w}}{P_{ eff }}=\frac{17718.03}{11693}=1.52 \).

The design is satisfactory and the factor of safety
is adequate against pitting failure.
