Guest Question.

A body (mass \(m\)) is driven along a rough horizontal path (kinetic friction coefficient \(\mu\) ) by a periodically acting force, such that\(v\left(t_{0} \right)=v_{0}\) and \(v_{1}=v\left(t_{1} \right)=v_{0}/2\) . During the driving phase \(\Delta t\) , the force profile \(F\left(t\right) \) is triangular.

a) Determine the period \(T\) and the required peak force \(F_{0}\) for a given \(\Delta t\).

b) Calculate the work \(U\) done by \(F\) during a period \(T\) .

Admin
Solution

a) We consider one period and start counting time at the end of the driving phase. First we apply the impulse law over the full period \(T\) . With \(R = \mu N = \mu mg = const\) and the given \(F\left(t\right) \) profile, the linear impulses of the friction force and the driving force are given by

\(\widehat{R}= \mu mgT , \widehat{F}=\int_{\Delta t }^{}{F\left(t\right)dt }=F_{0}\Delta t/2 \) ,

Thus, with \(v\left(0\right)=v\left(t_{0} \right)=v_{0}\) , the impulse law leads to

\(\longrightarrow :mv_{0}-mv_{0}=0=-\mu mgT+\frac{1}{2}F_{0}\Delta t \) .

In the same way, we obtain with \(v\left(t_{0} \right)=v_{0}\) and \(v_{1}=v\left(t_{1} \right)=v_{0}/2\) from the impulse law applied over the driving phase \(\Delta t\)

 

\(\longrightarrow :mv_{0}-mv_{0}/2=-\mu mg \Delta t+\frac{1}{2}F_{0}\Delta t \) .

From these two equations for the two unknowns \(T\) and \(F_{0}\) , it follow

\(\underline{\underline{T=\Delta t+\frac{v_{0} }{2\mu g} } } \) , \(\underline{\underline{F_{0}=2\mu mg \left[1+\frac{v_{0} }{2\mu mg\Delta t} \right] } } \) .

b) The work \(U\) done by \(F\left(t\right) \) during time \(T\) is determined by using the work-energy theorem, i.e. from the difference of kinetic energies at \(t_{0} \) and \(t_{1}\) :

\(\underline{\underline{U} }=T \left( t_{0} \right) -T \left( t_{1} \right)=\frac{1}{2}mv^{2}_{0}-\frac{1}{2}m\left(v_{0}/2 \right)^{2}= \underline{\underline{\frac{3}{8}mv^{2}_{0} } } \) .

Remark: The work of F and of \(R\) during \(T\) are equal but have opposite signs. This easily allows calculating the covered distance: \( l=3v^{2}_{0}/\left(8\mu g\right) \) .
