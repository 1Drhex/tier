Guest Question.

A mixture of 60% helium and 40% nitrogen by mole enters a turbine at 150 \(lbf / in .^{2}\), 1500 R at a rate of 4 lbm/s. The adiabatic turbine has an exit pressure of 15 \(lbf / in .^{2}\) and an isentropic efficiency of 85%. Find the turbine work.

 

Admin
Solution

Assume ideal gas mixture and take CV as turbine.

Energy Eq. ideal turbine: \(w _{ T s }= h _{ i }- h _{ es }\),

Entropy Eq. ideal turbine: \(s _{ es }= s _{ i } \Rightarrow T _{ es }= T _{ i }\left( P _{ e } / P _{ i }\right)^{( k -1) / k }\)

Properties from Eq.11.23, 11.15 and 6.23

\(\overline{ C }_{ P \text {mix }}=0.6 \times 1.25 \times 4.003+0.4 \times 0.248 \times 28.013=5.7811 Btu / lbmol R\)

 

\(( k -1) / k =\overline{ R } / \overline{ C }_{ P \operatorname{mix}}=1545 /(5.7811 \times 778)=0.3435\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}& M _{ mix }=0.6 \times 4.003+0.4 \times 28.013=13.607, \\& C _{ P }=\overline{ C }_{ P } / M _{ mix }=0.4249 Btu / lbm R \\& T _{ es }=1500 R (15 / 150)^{0.3435}=680 R \\& w _{ Ts }= C _{ P }\left( T _{ i }- T _{ es }\right)=348.4 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\)

 

Then do the actual turbine

\(w _{ T \text { ac }}=\eta w _{ Ts }=296.1 Btu / lbm ; \quad \dot{ W }=\dot{ m } w _{ Ts }= 1 1 8 4 Btu / s\)

Eq.11.15:
\(\begin{aligned}R_{\operatorname{mix}} &=\frac{1}{m}\left(n_{A} \bar{R}+n_{B} \bar{R}\right) \\&=\frac{1}{m}\left(m_{A} R_{A}+m_{B} R_{B}\right) \\&=c_{A} R_{A}+c_{B} R_{B}\end{aligned}\)

Eq.11.23 : \(C_{p 0 mix }=c_{A} C_{p 0 A}+c_{B} C_{p 0 B}\)

Eq.6.23 : \(\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}=\left(\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}\)
