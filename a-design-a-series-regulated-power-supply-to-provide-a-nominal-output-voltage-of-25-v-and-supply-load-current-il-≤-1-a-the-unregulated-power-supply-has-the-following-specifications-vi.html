Guest Question.

(a) Design a series-regulated power supply to provide a nominal output voltage of 25 V and supply load current IL ≤ 1 A. The unregulated power supply has the following specifications: Vi = 50 ± 5 V and ro = 10 Ω. (b) Find the stability factor SV. (c) Find the output resistance Ro. (d) Compute the change in output voltage ΔVo due to input-voltage changes of ± 5 V and load current IL variation from zero to 1 A.

Admin
Solution

(a) Select a silicon reference diode with VR ≈ Vo/2. Two 1N755 diodes in series provide VR = 7.5 + 7.5 = 15 V and Rz = 12 Ω at Iz = 20 mA. Refer to Figs 18.4 and 18.5. Choose IC2 ≈ IE2 = 10 mA. The Texas Instruments 2N930 silicon transistor can provide the collector current of 10 mA. For this transistor the manufacturer specifies IC (max) = 30 mA and VCE (max) = 45 V. At IC2 = 10 mA, the following parameters were measured:

hFE2 = 220 hfe2 = 200 hie2 = 800 Ω

Choose ID = 10 mA, so that D1, D2 operate at Iz = 10 + 10 = 20 mA. Then

\(R_{D}=\frac{V_{o}-V_{R}}{I_{D}}=\frac{25-15}{10}=1 \) K

The ratio R1/R2 may be found from Eq. (18.12) , \(V_{o}=(V_{R}+V_{BE2})\left\lgroup 1+ \frac{R_{1}}{R_{2}} \right\rgroup \) , Each resistor is determined as follows:

\(I_{B2}=\frac{I_{C2}}{h_{FE2}}=\frac{10 \mu A}{220}=45 \mu A \)

Since we require I1>> IB2 , we select I1 = 10 mA; then, since VBE = 0.6 V,

V2 = VBE2 + VR = 15.6 V

\(R_{1}=\frac{V_{o}-V_{2}}{I_{1}}=\frac{25-15.6}{10\times 10^{-3}}=940 \Omega \)

\(R_{2}\approx \frac{V_{2}}{I_{1}}=\frac{15.6}{10\times 10^{-3}}=1,560 \Omega \)

If we select the Texas Instruments 2N1722 silicon power transistor for Q1, we measure at IC1 = 1 A the following parameters:

hFE2 = 125 hfe1 = 100 hic1 = 20 Ω

\(I_{B1} =\frac{I_{L}+I_{1}+I_{D}}{h_{FE1}}=\frac{1,000+10+10}{125}\approx 8 \) mA

The current I through resistor R3 is I = IB1 + IC2 = 8 + 10 = 18 mA. The value for R3 corresponding to Vi = 45 and to IL = 1 A is given by

\(R_{3}=\frac{(V_{i}-(V_{BE1}+V_{o}))}{I}=\frac{50-25.6}{18\times 10^{-3}}=1,360 \Omega \)

The complete circuit is shown in Fig. 18.5.

(b) From Eq. (18.16) , \(S_{V}=\frac{(R_{1}||R_{2})+h_{ie2}+(1+h_{fe2})R_{z}}{h_{fe2}R_{3}} \) ,we find

\(S_{V}=\frac{2.50}{1.56}\times \frac{586+800+(201)(12)}{(200)(1,360)}=0.022 \)

(c) The output resistance is found from Eqs (18.15) , \(i_{c2}\approx h_{fe2}\frac{R_{2}}{R_{1}+R_{2}}\frac{v_{o}}{(R_{1}||R_{2})+h_{ie2}+(1+h_{fe2})R_{z}} \equiv G_{m}v_{o} \) , and (18.17) , \(R_{o}\approx \frac{r_{o}+\frac{R_{3}+h_{ie1}}{1+h_{fe1}} }{1+G_{m}(R_{3}+r_{o})} \) , Since

\(G_{m}=\frac{(200)(1.56)}{2.50} \times \frac{1}{586+800+(201)(12)}=0.033 \)

\(R_{o}=\frac{10+(1,360+20)/101}{1+(0.033)(1,360+10)}=0.51 \Omega \)

(d) The net change in output voltage, assuming constant temperature, is obtained using Eq. (18.5) , \(\Delta V_{o}=\frac{\partial V_{o}}{\partial V_{i}}\Delta V_{i} +\frac{\partial V_{o}}{\partial I_{L}}\Delta I_{L}+\frac{\partial V_{o}}{\partial T}\Delta T \) , :

\(\Delta V_{o}=S_{V}\Delta V_{t}+R_{o}\Delta I_{L}=0.022\times 10+0.51\times 1=0.22+0.51=0.73 \) V .

The circuit designed in this example was built in the laboratory, and excellent agreement between measured and calculated values was obtained.

Very often it is necessary to design a power supply with much smaller value for SV . From Eq. (18.16) we see that SV can be improved if R3 is increased. Since R3 ≈ (Vi − Vo)/I, we can increase R3 by decreasing I. The current I can be decreased by using a Darlington pair (Fig. 10.15) for Q1. For ever greater improvement in SV, R3 is replaced by a constant-current source (so that R3 → ∞ ), as shown in Fig. 18.6 (see also Sec. 10.12). For this circuit, which incorporates a Darlington pair, values of SV = 0.00014 and Ro = 0.1 Ω have been obtained.2 The constant-current source in Fig. 18.6 is often called a transistor preregulator. Other types of preregulators (Prob.18.7 ) are possible.2 The 0.01 μF capacitor in Fig. 18.6 is added to prevent high-frequency oscillation.
