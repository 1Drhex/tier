Guest Question.

A solid 0.75-in.-diameter shaft is subjected to the torques shown in Figure P6.8. The bearings shown allow the shaft to turn freely. (a) Plot a torque diagram showing the internal torque in segments (1), (2), and (3) of the shaft. Use the sign convention presented in Section 6-6. (b) Determine the maximum shear stress magnitude in the shaft.

Admin
Solution

Equilibrium:

\(\begin{array}{ll}\Sigma M_{x}=T_{1}+10 lb – ft =0 & \therefore T_{1}=-10 lb – ft \\\Sigma M_{x}=T_{2}+10 lb – ft -50 lb – ft =0 & \therefore T_{2}=40 lb – ft \\\Sigma M_{x}=-T_{3}-30 lb – ft =0 & \therefore T_{3}=-30 lb – ft\end{array}\)

Section properties:

\(J_{1}=J_{2}=J_{3}=\frac{\pi}{32}(0.75 in .)^{4}=0.031063 in .^{4}\)

Shear stress magnitudes:

\(\begin{aligned}&\tau_{1}=\frac{T_{1} c_{1}}{J_{1}}=\frac{(10 lb – ft )(0.75 in . / 2)(12 in. / ft )}{0.031063 in .^{4}}=1,448.7 psi \\&\tau_{2}=\frac{T_{2} c_{2}}{J_{2}}=\frac{(40 lb – ft )(0.75 in . / 2)(12 in. / ft )}{0.031063 in .^{4}}=5,794.7 psi \\&\tau_{3}=\frac{T_{3} c_{3}}{J_{3}}=\frac{(30 lb – ft )(0.75 in . / 2)(12 in. / ft )}{0.031063 in .^{4}}=4,346.0 psi\end{aligned}\)

The maximum shear stress in the shaft occurs in segment (2):

\(\tau_{\max }=5,790 psi\)
