Guest Question.

A load of 6,100 N acts on the machine part shown in Figure P12.10a. The machine part has a uniform thickness of 15 mm (i.e., 15-mm thickness in the z direction). Determine the normal and shear stresses acting at points H and K, which are shown in detail in Figure P12.10b. For each point, show these stresses on a stress element.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(A=(15 mm )(40 mm )=600 mm ^{2} \quad I_{z}=\frac{(15 mm )(40 mm )^{3}}{12}=80,000 mm ^{4}\)

 

Equilibrium:

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma F_{x}=& A_{x}-F_{B C}+(6,100 N ) \cos 40^{\circ}=0 \\\Sigma F_{y}=& A_{y}+(6,100 N ) \sin 40^{\circ}=0 \\ \Sigma M_{A}=&(6,100 N )(185 mm ) \sin 40^{\circ} \\&-F_{B C}(90 mm )=0\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{B C}=8,059.842 N \\&A_{x}=3,386.971 N \quad A_{y}=-3,921.004 N\end{aligned}\)

 

Internal forces at H:

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma F_{x} &=-F_{H}+(6,100 N ) \cos 40^{\circ}=0 & \therefore F_{H}=4,672.871 N \\\Sigma F_{y} &=V_{H}+(6,100 N ) \sin 40^{\circ}=0 & \therefore V_{H}=-3,921.004 N \\\Sigma M_{H} &=-M_{H}+(6,100 N )(135 mm ) \sin 40^{\circ}=0 \\& \therefore M_{H}=529,335.597 N – mm\end{aligned}\)

 

Axial stress at H:

\(\sigma_{\text {axial }}=\frac{4,672.871 N }{600 mm ^{2}}=7.788 MPa ( T )\)

Shear stress at H:

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{H} &=(15 mm )(10 mm )(20 mm -5 mm )=2,250 mm ^{3} \\\tau_{H} &=\frac{(3,921.004 N )\left(2,250 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(80,000 mm ^{4}\right)(15 mm )}=7.352 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Bending stress at H:

\(\sigma_{\text {bend }}=\frac{(529,335.597 N – mm )(10 mm )}{80,000 mm ^{4}}=66.167 MPa ( C ) \quad \text { (by inspection) }\)

 

Summary of stresses at H:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=7.788 MPa -66.167 MPa =-58.4 MPa \\\sigma_{y} &=0 MPa \\\tau_{x y} &=7.35 MPa\end{aligned}\)

 

 

Internal forces at K:

\(\begin{array}{ll}\Sigma F_{x}=-F_{B C}+V_{K}=0 & \therefore V_{K}=8,059.842 N \\\Sigma F_{y}=F_{K}=0 \\\Sigma M_{K}=M_{K}-F_{B C}(40 mm )=0 & \therefore M_{K}=322,393.697 N – mm\end{array}\)

 

 

Axial stress at K:

\(\sigma_{\text {axial }}=0 MPa\)

Shear stress at K:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q_{K}=(15 mm )(15 mm )(20 mm -7.5 mm )=2,812.5 mm ^{3} \\&\tau_{K}=\frac{(8,059.842 N )\left(2,812.5 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(80,000 mm ^{4}\right)(15 mm )}=18.890 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Bending stress at K:

\(\sigma_{ bend }=\frac{(322,393.697 N – mm )(5 mm )}{80,000 mm ^{4}}=20.150 MPa ( T ) \quad \text { (by inspection) }\)

 

Summary of stresses at K:

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{x}=0 MPa \\&\sigma_{y}=0 MPa +20.150 MPa =20.2 MPa \\&\tau_{x y}=18.89 MPa\end{aligned}\)
