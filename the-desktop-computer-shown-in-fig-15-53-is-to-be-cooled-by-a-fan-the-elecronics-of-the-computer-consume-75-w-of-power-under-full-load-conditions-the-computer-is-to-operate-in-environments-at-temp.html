Guest Question.

The desktop computer shown in Fig. (15-53) is to be cooled by a fan. The elecronics of the computer consume 75 W of power under full-load conditions. The computer is to operate in environments at temperatures up to \( 40^{\circ} C\) and at elevations up to 2000 m, where the atmospheric pressure is 79.50 kPa. The exit temperature of air is not to exceed \( 70^{\circ} C\) to meet reliability requirements. Also, the average velocity of air is not to exceed 75 m / min at the exit of the computer case, where the fan is installed, to keep the noise level down. Determine the flow rate of the fan that needs to be installed and the diameter of the casing of the fan.

 

 

 

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION A desktop computer is to be cooled by a fan safely in hot environments and high elevations. The airflow rate of the fan and the diameter of the casing are to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Steady operation under worst conditions is considered. 2 Air is an ideal gas. Properties The specific heat of air at the average temperature of \( (40+70) / 2=55^{\circ} C\) is \( 1007 J / kg \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\)

Analysis We need to determine the flow rate of air for the worst-case scenario. Therefore, we assume the inlet temperature of air to be \( 40^{\circ} C\) and the atmos. pheric pressure to be 79.50 kPa and disregard any heat transfer from the outer surfaces of the computer case. Note that any direct heat loss from the computer case will provide a safety margin in the design. Noting that all the heat dissipated by the electronic components is absorbed by air, the required mass flow rate of air to absorb heat at a rate of 75 W can be determined from

\( \dot{Q}=\dot{m}_{p}\left(T_{\text {out }}-T_{\text {in }}\right) \)

Solving for \( \dot{m}\) and substituting the given values, we obtain

\(\dot{m}=\frac{\dot{Q}}{c_{p}\left(T_{\text {out }}-T_{\text {in }}\right)}=\frac{75 J / s }{\left(1007 J / kg \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)(70-40)^{\circ} C }\)

 

\( =0.00248 kg / s =0.148 kg / min\)

 

In the worst case, the exhaust fan will handle air at\( 70^{\circ} C\). Then the density of air entering the fan and the volume flow rate become

\( \rho=\frac{P}{R T}=\frac{79.50 kPa }{\left(0.287 kPa \cdot m ^{3} / kg \cdot K \right)(70+273) K }=0.8076 kg / m ^{3} \)

 

\( \dot{V}=\frac{\dot{m}}{\rho}=\frac{0.148 kg / min }{0.8076 kg / m ^{2}}=0.183 m ^{3} / min\)

 

Therefore, the fan must be able to provide a flow rate of \( 0.183 m ^{3} / min\) or 6.5 cfm (cubic feet per minute). Note that if the fan were installed at the inlet instead of the exit, then we would need to determine the flow rate using the density of air at the inlet temperature of\( 40^{\circ} C\), and we would need to add the power consumed by the motor of the fan to the heat load of 75 W. The result may be a slightly smaller or larger fan, depending on which effect dominates. For an average velocity of 75 m / min, the diameter of the duct in which the fan is installed can be determined from

\( \dot{V}=A_{c} V=\frac{1}{4} \pi D^{2} V\)

Solving for D and substituting the known values, we obtain

\( D=\sqrt{\frac{4 \dot{V}}{\pi V}}=\sqrt{\frac{4 \times 0.183 m ^{3} / min }{\pi(7.5 m / min )}}=0.176 m =17.6 cm\)

Therefore, a fan with a casing diameter of 17.6 cm and a flow rate of \( 0.183 m ^{3} / min\) will meet the design requirements.
