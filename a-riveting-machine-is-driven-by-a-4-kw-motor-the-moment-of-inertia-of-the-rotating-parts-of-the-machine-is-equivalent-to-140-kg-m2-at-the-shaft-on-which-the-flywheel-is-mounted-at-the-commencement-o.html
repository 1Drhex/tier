Guest Question.

A riveting machine is driven by a 4 kW motor. The moment of inertia of the rotating parts of the machine is equivalent to 140 kg-m2 at the shaft on which the flywheel is mounted.
At the commencement of operation, the flywheel is making 240 r.p.m. If closing a rivet occupies 1 second and consumes 10 kN-m of energy, find the reduction of speed of the flywheel. What is the maximum rate at which the rivets can be closed ?

Admin
Solution

Given : P = 4 kW = 4000 W ; I = 140 kg-\(m^{2}\) ; \(N_{1}=240 \text { r.p.m. or } \omega_{1}=2 \pi \times \)240/60 = 25.14 rad/s

Reduction of speed of the flywheel

Let \(\omega_{2}\) = Angular speed of the flywheel immediately after closing a rivet.

Since the power of motor is 4000 W, therefore energy supplied by motor in 1 second,

\(E_{1}=4000 N – m\) \(\ldots(\because 1 W =1 N – m / s )\)

We know that energy consumed in closing a rivet in 1 second,

\(E_{2}=10 kN – m =10000 N – m\)

\(\therefore\) Loss of kinetic energy of the flywheel during the operation,

\(E=E_{2}-E_{1}=10000-4000=6000 N – m\)

We know that kinetic energy of the flywheel at the commencement of operation

\(=\frac{1}{2} I\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2}=\frac{1}{2} \times 140(25.14)^{2}=44240 N – m\)

\(\therefore\) Kinetic energy of the flywheel at the end of operation

= 44 240 â€“ 6000 = 38 240 N-m … (i)

We also know that kinetic energy of the flywheel at the end of operation

\(=\frac{1}{2} I\left(\omega_{2}\right)^{2}=\frac{1}{2} \times 140\left(\omega_{2}\right)^{2}=70\left(\omega_{2}\right)^{2}\) …(ii)

Equating equations (i) and (ii),

\(70\left(\omega_{2}\right)^{2}=38240 \text { or } \quad\left(\omega_{2}\right)^{2}=38240 / 70=546.3 \text { and } \omega=23.4 rad / s\)

\(\therefore\) Reduction of speed

\(=\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}=25.14-23.4=1.74 rad / s\)

\(=1.74 \times 60 / 2 \pi=16.6 r.p.m\) \(\ldots(\because \omega=2 \pi N / 60)\)

Maximum rate at which the rivets can be closed

Maximum rate at which the rivets can be closed per minute

\(=\frac{\text { Energy supplied by motor per min }}{\text { Energy consumed to close a rivet }}=\frac{4000 \times 60}{10000}=24\)
