Guest Question.

For the single-phase full-bridge fully controlled thyristor rectifier of Fig. 5.1(a), the following specifications are given:

\(v _{ i }=230 \sqrt{2} \sin (120 \pi t ), L =40 mH , R =4 \Omega, E =-80 V , \alpha=30^{\circ}\)

and continuous output current. Calculate the following:
a) The average output current.
b) The rms output current.
c) The power supplied by the load dc source E.
d) The input power factor.

 

Admin
Solution

Solution

a) Since the output current is continuous, the average output voltage is:

 

\(\overline{ V }_{ o }=\frac{2 \sqrt{2} \widetilde{ V }_{ i }}{\pi} \cos \alpha=\frac{2 \sqrt{2} \times 230}{\pi} \cos 30^{\circ}=179 V\)

Also

\(\overline{ I }_{o} R + E -\overline{ V }_{ o }=0 \quad \text { or } \quad \overline{ I }_{ o }=\frac{\overline{ V }_{ o }- E }{ R }=\frac{179+80}{4}=64.8 A\)

 

b) The rms output current is given by \(\widetilde{ I }_{ o } \approx \sqrt{\overline{ I }_{ o }^{2}+\widetilde{ I }_{ o , 2}^{2}+\widetilde{ I }_{ o , 4}^{2}+\widetilde{ I }_{ o , 6}^{2}}\) According to Fig. 5.5, the amplitudes of the second, fourth, and sixth harmonic components \(\left(\text { for } \alpha=30^{\circ}\right)\) are respectively given by:

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\frac{\widehat{ V }_{ o , 2}}{\sqrt{2} \widetilde{ V }_{ i }}=0.55 \quad \text { or } \quad \widehat{ V }_{ o , 2}=0.55 \sqrt{2} \times 230=179 V \\&\frac{\widehat{ V }_{ o , 4}}{\sqrt{2} \widetilde{ V }_{ i }}=0.18 \quad \text { or } \quad \widehat{ V }_{ o , 4}=0.18 \sqrt{2} \times 230=59 V\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\frac{\widehat{ V }_{ o , 6}}{\sqrt{2} \widetilde{ V }_{ i }}=0.1 \quad \text { or } \quad \widehat{ V }_{ o , 6}=0.1 \sqrt{2} \times 230=33 V\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\widetilde{ I }_{o,2} &=\frac{\widehat{ V }_{ o , 2}}{\sqrt{2}\left| z _{ o , 2}\right|}=\frac{179}{\sqrt{2} \times \sqrt{ R ^{2}+(2 \omega L )^{2}}}=\frac{179}{\sqrt{2} \times \sqrt{4^{2}+(2 \times 120 \times \pi \times 0.04)^{2}}} \\&=4.2 A \\\widetilde{ I }_{ o , 4} &=\frac{\widehat{ V }_{ o , 4}}{\sqrt{2}\left| z _{ o , 4}\right|}=\frac{59}{\sqrt{2 \times} \sqrt{4^{2}+(4 \times 120 \times \pi \times 0.04)^{2}}}<<\widetilde{ I }_{o,2} \\\widetilde{ I }_{ o , 6} &=\frac{\widehat{ V }_{ o , 6}}{\sqrt{2}\left| z _{ o , 6}\right|}<<\widetilde{ I }_{ o, 2}\end{aligned}\)

 

Therefore, the rms output current is:

 

\(\widetilde{ I }_{ o } \approx \sqrt{(64.8)^{2}+(4.2)^{2}}=64.8 A\)

 

c) The power supplied by the dc source E to the rectifier input is:

 

\(P _{ E }=\overline{ I }_{ o } E =64.8 \times 80=5180 W\)

 

d) The rms output current is the same as the rms input current. Assuming that the rectifier is ideal, the rectifier power factor is:

 

\(\lambda=\frac{P_{i}}{S_{i}}=\frac{P_{o}}{S_{i}}=\frac{P_{R}-P_{E}}{\widetilde{V}_{i} \widetilde{I}_{i}}=\frac{\widetilde{I}_{o}^{2} R-5180}{\widetilde{V}_{i} \widetilde{I}_{o}}=\frac{4 \times 64.8^{2}-5180}{230 \times 64.8}=0.78\)
