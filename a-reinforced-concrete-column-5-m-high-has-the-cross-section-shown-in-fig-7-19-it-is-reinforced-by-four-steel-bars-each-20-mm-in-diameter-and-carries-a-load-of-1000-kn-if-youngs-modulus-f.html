Guest Question.

A reinforced concrete column, 5 m high, has the cross section shown in Fig. 7.19. It is reinforced by four steel bars each 20 mm in diameter and carries a load of 1000 kN. If Young’s modulus for steel is 200000 N/mm² and that for concrete is 15 000 N/mm² , calculate the stress in the steel and in the concrete and also the shortening of the column.

Admin
Solution

The total cross-sectional area, \(A_{S}\), of the steel reinforcement is

\( A_{S}=4 \times \frac{\pi}{4} \times 20^{2}=1257 mm ^{2}\)

The cross-sectional area, \(A_{C}\), of the concrete is reduced due to the presence of the steel and is given by

\( A_{C}=400^{2}-1257=158743 mm ^{2} \)

Equations (7.38) then give

\( \sigma_{ S }=\frac{E_{ S }}{A_{ C } E_{ C }+A_{ S } E_{ S }} P \sigma_{ C }=\frac{E_{ C }}{A_{ C } E_{ C }+A_{ S } E_{ S }} P \) (7.38)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{ S } &=\frac{200000 \times 1000 \times 10^{3}}{158743 \times 15000+1257 \times 200000}=76.0 N / mm ^{2} \\\sigma_{ C } &=\frac{15000 \times 1000 \times 10^{3}}{158743 \times 15000+1257 \times 200000}=5.7 N / mm ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

The deflection, δ, of the column is obtained using either side of Eq. (7.40). Thus

\(\frac{\sigma_{ C } L}{E_{ C }}=\frac{\sigma_{ S } L}{E_{ S }}\) (7.40)

 

\( \delta=\frac{\sigma_{ C } L}{E_{ C }}=\frac{5.7 \times 5 \times 10^{3}}{15000}=1.9 mm \)
