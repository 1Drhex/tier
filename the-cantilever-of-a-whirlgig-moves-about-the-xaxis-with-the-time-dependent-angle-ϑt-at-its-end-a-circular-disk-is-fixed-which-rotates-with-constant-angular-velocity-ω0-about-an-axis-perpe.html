Guest Question.

The cantilever of a whirlgig moves about the xaxis with the time-dependent angle \(ϑ\left(t\right)\) . At its end a circular disk is fixed which rotates with constant angular velocity \(\omega _{0}\) about an axis perpendicular to the cantilever.

Determine for point \(P\) of the disk the absolute velocity and absolute acceleration by using

a) the cantilever-fixed systems \(\xi , \eta , \zeta\) and

b) the disk-fixed systems \(\xi^{*} , \eta^{*} , \zeta^{*} \) .

Admin
Solution

The absolute velocity and acceleration are determined from the general relations

\(v=\dot{r}_{0}+\omega \times r_{0P}+v_{r} \) ,

\(a=\ddot{r}_{0}+\dot{\omega } \times r_{0P}+\omega \times \left(\omega \times r_{0P} \right)+2\omega \times v_{r}+a_{r}\) .

a) For the coordinate system \(\xi , \eta , \zeta\) with the unit vectors \(e_{\xi} , e_{\eta } , e_{\zeta }\) we first have

\(r_{0}=l e_{\eta }\) , \(\omega =\dot{ϑ} e_{\xi}\) , \(r_{0P}=r\cos \varphi e_{\xi }+r\sin \varphi e_{\eta } \) .

From these relations, by introducing \(\left(\dot{e}_{i}=\omega \times e_{i}\right) \)

 

\( \dot{e}_{\xi }=0 \) , \(\dot{e}_{\eta }=\dot{ϑ} e_{\zeta }\) , \( \dot{e}_{\zeta }=-\dot{ϑ} e_{\eta }\) , \(\dot{\varphi }=\omega_{0} \) ,

we obtain

\(\dot{r}_{0}=l\dot{ϑ}e_{\zeta } \) , \(\ddot{r}_{0}=l\ddot{ϑ}e_{\zeta }-l\dot{ϑ}^{2} e_{\eta } \) , \(\dot{\omega}= \ddot{ϑ}e_{\xi} \) .

With reference to this system, the movement of \(P\) is a circular motion with constant angular velocity \(\omega_{0}\) , i.e. we have

\(v_{r}=r\omega_{0}\left(-\sin \varphi e_{\xi}+\cos \varphi e_{\eta } \right)\) , \(a_{r}=-r\omega^{2} _{0}\left(\cos \varphi e_{\xi}+\sin \varphi e_{\eta } \right) \) .

Introducing these relations with

\(\omega \times r_{0P}=r \dot{ϑ}\sin \varphi e_{\zeta } \) , \(\dot{\omega} \times r_{0P}=r \ddot{ϑ}\sin \varphi e_{\zeta }\) ,

\(\omega \times \left(\omega \times r_{0P}\right) =-r \dot{ϑ}^{2} \sin \varphi e_{\eta }\) , \(\omega\times v_{r}=r \omega_{0} \dot{ϑ}\cos \varphi e_{\zeta} \)

finally leads to the results

\(\underline{\underline{v=-r\omega_{0}\sin\varphi e_{\xi}+r\omega_{0}\cos \varphi e_{\eta}+\left(l+r\sin \varphi \right)\dot{ϑ}e_{\zeta} } }\) ,

\(\underline{\underline{a=-r\omega^{2} _{0}\cos \varphi e_{\xi}-\left[\left(l+r\sin \varphi\right)\dot{ϑ}^{2}+r\omega^{2}_{0}\sin \varphi \right]e_{\eta} } }\)

 

\(\underline{\underline{+\left[\left(l+r\sin\varphi\right)\ddot{ϑ}+2r\omega_{0}\dot{ϑ}\cos\varphi \right]e_{\zeta} } }\) .

b) For the coordinate system \(\xi^{*} , \eta^{*} , \zeta^{*} \) from the representations

\(r_{0}=l\left(\sin \varphi e^{*}_{\xi}+\cos \varphi e^{*}_{\eta} \right) \) , \(r_{0P}= r e^{*}_{\xi}\) ,

\(\omega =\dot{ϑ}\left(\cos \varphi e^{*}_{\xi}-\sin \varphi e^{*}_{\eta} \right) +\omega _{0}e^{*}_{\zeta} \) ,

with

\(\dot{e}^{*}_{\xi}=\omega _{0}e^{*}_{\eta} +\dot{ϑ}\sin\varphi e^{*}_{\zeta} \) , \(\dot{e}^{*}_{\eta}=-\omega _{0}e^{*}_{\xi} +\dot{ϑ}\cos\varphi e^{*}_{\zeta}\) ,

\(\dot{e}^{*}_{\zeta}=-\dot{ϑ} \left(\sin\varphi e^{*}_{\xi}+\cos \varphi e^{*}_{\eta}\right) \) , \(\dot{\varphi } =\omega_{0} \)

follow the relations

\(\dot{r}_{0}=l \dot{ϑ}e^{*}_{\zeta}\) , \(\ddot{r}_{0}=-l \dot{ϑ}^{2}\left(\sin\varphi e^{*}_{\xi}+\cos \varphi e^{*}_{\eta} \right) +l\ddot{ϑ} e^{*}_{\zeta} \) ,

\(\dot{\omega }=\left(\ddot{ϑ}\cos \varphi -\dot{ϑ}\omega _{0} \sin \varphi \right)e^{*}_{\xi}-\left(\ddot{ϑ}\sin \varphi +\dot{ϑ}\omega _{0} \cos \varphi\right)e^{*}_{\eta} \) .

Since P with reference to the system \(\xi^{*} , \eta^{*} , \zeta^{*} \) is at rest, we now have \(v_{r}=a_{r}=0 \) . With

\(\omega \times r_{0P}=r\omega _{0} e^{*}_{\eta}+r \dot{ϑ}\sin \varphi e^{*}_{\zeta}\) ,

\(\dot{\omega } \times r_{0P}=r\left(\ddot{ϑ}\sin \varphi +\dot{ϑ}\omega_{0}\cos \varphi \right) e^{*}_{\zeta} \) ,

\(\omega \times \left( \omega \times r_{0P}\right)=-r\left(\dot{\omega }^{2}_{0}+ \dot{ϑ}^{2}\sin ^{2}\varphi \right)e^{*}_{\xi}-r\dot{ϑ}^{2}\sin \varphi \cos \varphi e^{*}_{\eta} +r\omega_{0}\dot{ϑ}\cos \varphi e^{*}_{\zeta} \)

we then obtain

\(\underline{\underline{v=r\omega_{0}e^{*}_{\eta} + \dot{ϑ}\left(l+r\sin \varphi \right)e^{*}_{\zeta } } } \) ,

\(\underline{\underline{a=\left[r\omega^{2}_{0}- \dot{ϑ}^{2} \left(l+r\sin \varphi \right)\sin \varphi\right] e^{*}_{\xi} -\dot{ϑ}^{2} \left(l+r\sin \varphi \right)\cos \varphi e^{*}_{\eta } } } \)

 

\(\underline{\underline{+\left[\ddot{ϑ}\left(l+r\sin \varphi \right)+2r\omega _{0}\dot{ϑ}\cos \varphi \right] e^{*}_{\zeta}}} \) .

Remark: The representations in a) and b) can be transformed into each other using \(e_{\xi }= e^{*}_{\xi }\cos \varphi -e^{*}_{\eta }\sin \varphi \) , \(e_{\eta}= e^{*}_{\xi }\sin \varphi +e^{*}_{\eta }\cos \varphi \) .
