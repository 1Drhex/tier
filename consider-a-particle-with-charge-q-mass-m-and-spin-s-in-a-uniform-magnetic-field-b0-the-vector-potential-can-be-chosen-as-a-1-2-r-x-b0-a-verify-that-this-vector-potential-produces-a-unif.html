Guest Question.

Consider a particle with charge q, mass m, and spin s, in a uniform magnetic field \( B_0\). The vector potential can be chosen as

\( A =-\frac{1}{2} r \times B _{0} \).

(a) Verify that this vector potential produces a uniform magnetic field \( B_0\).

(b) Show that the Hamiltonian can be written

\( H=\frac{p^{2}}{2 m}+q \varphi- B _{0} \cdot\left(\gamma_{o} L +\gamma S \right)+\frac{q^{2}}{8 m}\left[r^{2} B_{0}^{2}-\left( r \cdot B _{0}\right)^{2}\right] \) (4.230).

where \( \gamma_{o}=q / 2 m \) is the gyromagnetic ratio for orbital motion.
Note: The term linear in \( B_0\) makes it energetically favorable for the magnetic
moments (orbital and spin) to align with the magnetic field; this is the origin of paramagnetism in materials. The term quadratic in \( B_0\) leads to the opposite effect: diamagnetism.

Admin
Solution

(a)

\( B =\nabla \times A =-\frac{1}{2} \nabla \times\left( r \times B _{0}\right) \).

\( =-\frac{1}{2}\left[\left( B _{0} \cdot \nabla\right) r -( r \cdot \nabla) B _{0}+ r \left(\nabla \cdot B _{0}\right)- B _{0}(\nabla \cdot r )\right]=-\frac{1}{2}\left[ B _{0}+0+0-3 B _{0}\right]= B _{0} \).

(b) The Hamiltonian (Equations 4.188 and 4.158) is

\( H=\frac{1}{2 m}( p -q A )^{2}+q \varphi \) (4.188).

\( H =-\gamma B \cdot S \) (4.158).

\( H=\frac{1}{2 m}( p -q A )^{2}+q \varphi-\gamma S \cdot B _{0}=\frac{p^{2}}{2 m}-\frac{q}{2 m}( p \cdot A + A \cdot p )+\frac{q^{2}}{2 m} A^{2}+q \varphi-\gamma S \cdot B _{0} \).

Now

\( A^{2}=\frac{1}{4}\left( r \times B _{0}\right) \cdot\left( r \times B _{0}\right)=\frac{1}{4}\left[ r \cdot\left( B _{0} \times\left( r \times B _{0}\right)\right)\right]=\frac{1}{4} r \cdot\left[ r B_{0}^{2}- B _{0}\left( r \cdot B _{0}\right)\right]=\frac{1}{4}\left[r^{2} B_{0}^{2}-\left( r \cdot B _{0}\right)^{2}\right] \).

To evaluate the middle term we use a test function f(r):

\( p \cdot( A f)+ A \cdot p f=-i \hbar[(\nabla \cdot A ) f+2 A \cdot \nabla f]=\frac{1}{2} i \hbar\left[\nabla \cdot\left( r \times B _{0}\right) f+2\left( r \times B _{0}\right) \cdot \nabla f\right] \).

\( =\frac{1}{2} i \hbar\left[ B _{0} \cdot \cancel{(\nabla \times r )} f- r \cdot\left(\cancel{\nabla \times B _{0}}\right) f-2 B _{0} \cdot( r \times \nabla f)\right]= B _{0} \cdot L f \).

Combining these expressions gives the desired result.
