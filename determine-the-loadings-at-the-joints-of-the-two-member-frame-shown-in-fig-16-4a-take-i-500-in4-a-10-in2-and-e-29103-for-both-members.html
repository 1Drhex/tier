Guest Question.

Determine the loadings at the joints of the two-member frame shown in Fig. \(16-4 (\mathrm{a})\). Take \(I=500 \mathrm{in}^{4}, A=10 \mathrm{in}^{2}\), and \(E=29\left(10^{3}\right) \mathrm{ksi}\) for both members.

Admin
Solution

Notation. By inspection, the frame has two elements and three nodes, which are identified as shown in Fig. 16-4b. The origin of the global coordinate system is located at ①. The code numbers at the nodes are specified with the unconstrained degrees of freedom numbered first. From the constraints at ① and ③, and the applied loading, we have

\( \mathbf{D}_{k}=\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] \begin{array}{ll} 6 \\7 \\ 8 \\ 9 \end{array} \quad \mathbf{Q}_{k}=\left[\begin{array}{l} 5 \\ 0 \\0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] \begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 2 \\3 \\4 \\ 5 \end{array} \)

Structure Stiffness Matrix. The following terms are common to both element stiffness matrices:

\( \begin{aligned} \frac{A E}{L} &=\frac{10\left[29\left(10^{3}\right)\right]}{20(12)}=1208.3 \mathrm{k} / \text { in. } . \\ \frac{12 E I}{L^{3}} &=\frac{12\left[29\left(10^{3}\right)(500)\right]}{[20(12)]^{3}}=12.6 \mathrm{k} / \text { in. } . \\ \frac{6 E I}{L^{2}} &=\frac{6\left[29\left(10^{3}\right)(500)\right]}{[20(12)]^{2}}=1510.4 \mathrm{k} \\ \frac{4 E I}{L} &=\frac{4\left[29\left(10^{3}\right)(500)\right]}{20(12)}=241.7\left(10^{3}\right) \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{in} . \\ \frac{2 E I}{L} &=\frac{2\left[29\left(10^{3}\right)(500)\right]}{20(12)}=120.83\left(10^{3}\right) \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{in.} . \end{aligned} \)
