Porting CMOS TIAs across technology nodes Consider the CMOS TIAs shown in Figure 8.18 used in a wireline \(43 \mathrm{~Gb} / \mathrm{s}\) receiver. The load resistor in the output buffer is \(60 \Omega\). This circuit was first designed in a \(90 \mathrm{~nm}\) CMOS technology with the same technology parameters as those in . It was then ported into a \(65 \mathrm{~nm}\) CMOS process without any modifications. Computer simulations of the TIA noise figure at 36GHz for a signal source impedance \(Z_{0}=50 \Omega\) are illustrated in Figure 8.19 . The \(65 \mathrm{~nm}\)CMOS technology parameters are listed below:
\(L=45 \mathrm{~nm},|V T|=0.3 \mathrm{~V} ;\) Idsn at minimum noise \(=0.15 \mathrm{~mA} / \mu \mathrm{m} ; g_{m n}^{\prime}=2 g_{m p}^{\prime}=1.3 \mathrm{mS} / \mu \mathrm{m}\) \(g_{d s n}^{\prime}=g_{d s p}^{\prime}=0.18 \mathrm{mS} / \mu \mathrm{m}\); the peak \(f_{T}\) of \(170 \mathrm{GHz}\) occurs at \(0.3 \mathrm{~mA} / \mu \mathrm{m}\) for \(0.3 \mathrm{~V}<V_{D S}<1 \mathrm{~V}\).
For both \(\mathrm{n}\)-channel and p-channel devices: \(C_{g s}^{\prime}=0.8 \mathrm{fF} / \mu \mathrm{m} ; C_{g d}^{\prime}=0.4 \mathrm{fF} / \mu \mathrm{m} ; C_{s b}^{\prime}=\) \(C_{d b}^{\prime}=0.7 \mathrm{fF} / \mu \mathrm{m}\)
\(R_{s}^{\prime}(\mathrm{n}-\mathrm{MOS})=R_{d}^{\prime}=160 \Omega \times \mu \mathrm{m} ; R_{s}^{\prime}(\mathrm{p}-\mathrm{MOS})=R_{d}^{\prime}=320 \Omega \times \mu \mathrm{m}\)
\(R_{\text {shg }}(\mathrm{n} / \mathrm{p}-\mathrm{MOS})=15 \Omega / \mathrm{sq} ;\) poly contact resistance \(R_{C O N}=40 \Omega\) .
Total \(R_{g} / 0.7 \mu \mathrm{m}\) finger contacted on one side is \(158 \Omega\). Noise parameters: \(P=1, C=0.4\), \(R=0.25\)

(a) Calculate the technology parameters of the CMOS inverter in both processes.
(b) Determine the open loop voltage gain, the input impedance and the input and output capacitance of the TIA stage in the 65nm CMOS process.
(c) Calculate the optimal n-MOSFET gate width in both technologies for a TIA bandwidth of 36GHz assuming 0.7μm gate fingers in 65nm CMOS and 1μm gate fingers in 90nm CMOS.

Solution

(a) Given that \(g_{m n}^{\prime} / g_{m p}^{\prime}=2\), the technology noise parameters for the CMOS inverter in the \(65 \mathrm{~nm}\) CMOS process become

\(R\left(C M O S_{i n v}\right)=\frac{P}{2 g_{m}^{\prime}}+\frac{R_{s}^{\prime}}{2}+\frac{R_{g}^{\prime}\left(W_{f}=0.7 \mu \mathrm{m}\right)}{2}=543.5 \Omega \cdot \mu \mathrm{m}\)

 

\(G\left(C M O S_{i n v}\right)=\frac{P \frac{R_{s}^{\prime}}{2} \cdot 9\left(C_{g s}^{\prime}+C_{g d}^{\prime}\right)^{2}}{P+\left(R_{s}^{\prime}+R_{g}^{\prime}\left(W_{f}=1\right)\right) g_{m}^{\prime}}=7.33 \cdot 10^{-28} \frac{\mathrm{F}^{2}}{\mu \mathrm{m}^{2}}\) ; \(G_{C} \approx 0 \)

 

\(B\left(C M O S_{i n v}\right)=\frac{P \cdot 3 \cdot\left(C_{g s}^{\prime}+C_{g d}^{\prime}\right)}{P+\left(R_{s}^{\prime}+R_{g}^{\prime}\left(W_{f}=1\right)\right) g_{m}^{\prime}}=2.544 \cdot 10^{-15} \frac{\mathrm{F}}{\mu \mathrm{m}}\)

and \(\sqrt{\frac{1}{\frac{G_{C M O S i n v}}{R_{C M O S i n v}}+G_{C, C M O S i n v}^{2}+B_{C M O S i n v}^{2}}}=3.57 \cdot 10^{14} \mu \mathrm{m}\)

Similarly, in the 90nm process \(\sqrt{\frac{1}{\frac{G_{C M O S i n v}}{R_{C M O S i n v}}+G_{C, C M O S i n v}^{2}+B_{C M O S i n v}^{2}}}=2.99 \cdot 10^{14} \mu \mathrm{m}\)

(b) First, we determine \(g_{\text {meff }}^{\prime}=1.05 \mathrm{mS} / \mu \mathrm{m}\) and \(g_{\text {oeff }}^{\prime}=0.143 \mathrm{mS} / \mu \mathrm{m}\) for the \(65 \mathrm{~nm}\) n-MOSFET. The open loop gain and input impedance become

\(A= -\frac{g_{m 1}+g_{m 2}}{g_{o 1}+g_{o 2}+\frac{1}{R_{F}}}=\frac{-42 \mathrm{mS}}{8.58 \mathrm{mS}+5 \mathrm{~ms}}=-3.09\)

 

\(Z_{\mathrm{IN}}=\frac{R_{F}}{1-A}=\frac{200}{4.09}=49 \Omega\)

The voltage gain of the output buffer is \( A_{V 2}=\frac{-g_{\text {meff/3 }}}{g_{\text {oeff } 3}+\frac{1}{25}}=\frac{-42 \mathrm{mS}}{5.72 \mathrm{mS}+36.66 \mathrm{mS}}=-0.99\) while the
node capacitances are given by

\(C_{I N}=C_{g s 1}+C_{g s 2}+(1-A)\left(\mathrm{C}_{\mathrm{gd} 1}+\mathrm{C}_{\mathrm{gd} 2}\right)=16 \mathrm{fF}+32 \mathrm{fF}+4.09 \times 24 \mathrm{fF}=146.16 \mathrm{fF}\)

 

\(C_{o u t}=C_{d b 1}+C_{g d 1}+C_{g d 2}+C_{d b 2}+C_{g s 3}+2 C_{g d 3}=12+8+24+16+32+32=124 \mathrm{fF}\)

Ignoring \(L_{F}\) , \(f_{1}\) and \(f_{2} \) are given by

\(f_{1}=\frac{1}{2 \pi C_{I N} R_{F}}=5.43 \mathrm{GHz} \) and

\(f_{2}=\frac{g_{\text {out }}}{2 \pi C_{\text {out }}}=\frac{g_{o 1}+g_{o 2}+\frac{1}{R_{F}}}{2 \pi C_{\text {out }}}=17.44 \mathrm{GHz}\)

For the 65nm TIA

\(W_{O P T}=\frac{1}{\omega} \sqrt{\left(\frac{1}{Z_{0}}+\frac{1}{R_{F}} \frac{1}{1+\omega_{f}^{2}}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{1}{R_{F}} \frac{\omega_{f}}{1+\omega_{f}^{2}}\right)^{2}} \sqrt{\frac{1}{\frac{G_{F E T}}{R_{F E T}}+\underline{G}_{C, F E T}^{2}+B_{F E T}^{2}}}\) \(=\frac{0.02342 \cdot 3.57 \cdot 10^{14}}{2.26 \cdot 10^{11}}=37 \mu \mathrm{m}\)

Both values calculated using analytical expressions are very close to the simulated values in Figure 8.19. Both are larger than the values used in the schematic. Note that the optimal size increases in more advanced nodes due to the fact that the gate-source and drain-bulk capacitances have decreased between the \(90 \mathrm{~nm}\) and \(65 \mathrm{~nm}\) nodes. Constant-field scaling no longer applies between the \(90 \mathrm{~nm}\) and \(65 \mathrm{~nm}\) nodes. Nevertheless, because \(g_{m}^{\prime}\) increases and \(C_{g s}^{\prime}\) and \(C_{d b}^{\prime}\) decrease, the noise and bandwidth of the TIA improve from the \(90 \mathrm{~nm}\) to the \(65 \mathrm{~nm}\) nodes.
Note also that the transistor size and the node capacitances have decreased between the \(\mathrm{n}\) MOS – only and the CMOS inverter TIA. The measured data for \(90 \mathrm{~nm}\) implementations of the n-MOS and CMOS TIA stages are summarized in Table 8.3 .
