Guest Question.

Find \( E (X_{1}^{2})\) for the stationary response of the oscillator shown here when the base acceleration \( \{\ddot Y(t)\}\) is mean-zero white noise with auto spectral density \(S_{0} = 0.1 (m/s^2)/(rad/s) \).

Admin
Solution

The system is described by Eq. 8.13

\( m\ddot{\vec{X}} (t)+c\dot{\vec{X}}(t)+k \vec{X}(t)=\vec{F} (t) \)

with \(m, k \), and \( c \) as given in , \( F_{1}(t)=-m_{11}\ddot{Y}(t)\), and \( F_{2}(t)=-m_{22}\ddot{Y}(t) \). One approach is to write

\( X_{1}(t)=-\int_{0}^{\infty }{[m_{11}h_{11}(\tau)+m_{22}h_{22}(\tau )]\ddot{Y}(t-\tau ) d\tau } \)

Using \( E[\ddot{Y}(u)\ddot{Y}(v)] = 2\pi S_{0} \delta(u-v) \) and substituting tho \( h_{11}(s) \) and \( h_{12}(s)\) impulse response functions found in then gives

\( E(X_{1}^{2})=2\pi S_{0}\int_{0}^{\infty }{[m_{11}h_{11}(s) +m_{22}h_{12}(s)]^{2}ds} \) \( =2\pi S_{0}\left(2.488\times 10^{-3}\int_{0}^{\infty }{e^{-0.2929s}\sin ^{2}(17.11s)ds +3.536\times 10^{-4}\int_{0}^{\infty }e^{-1.0s}\sin (17.11S)\sin (41.31S)ds + 1.257\times 10^{-5}\int_{0}^{\infty }{e^{-1.707s}\sin ^{2}(41.31s)ds} } \right) \)

Performing the integration then reduces this to

\( E(X_{1}^{2})=2\pi S_{0}(4.246\times 10^{-3}+2.498\times 10^{-7}+3.680\times 10^{-6})=2.671\times 10^{-3} m^{2} \)

Note that the first term in this form is due to the first mode of the system, the second term is cross-modal, and the final term is due to the second mode. Clearly the cross-modal term and the second mode term are both much smaller than the first mode term, as discussed in conjunction with Eq. 8.39

\(K_{X_{j}(t)X_{l}(s)}\approx \sum\limits_{r_{1}=1}^{n}\sum\limits_{r_{2}=1}^{n}\sum\limits_{r_{3}=1}^{n} {\theta _{jr_{1}}\theta _{r_{2}r_{1}} [K_{FF}(,\nu )]_{r_{2}r_{3}}\theta _{r_{3}r_{1}}\theta _{lr_{1}}q_{r_{1}r_{1}} } \)

The insignificance of the second mode is exaggerated in this example by the fact that the excitation has \( F_{1}(t)\) in phase with \( F_{2}(t)\), and this distribution of forces is not effective in exciting the second mode, which has \( X_{1}(t) 180^{ \circ}\) out of phase with \( X_{2}(t)\).

Alternatively, one can investigate the mean-squared modal responses, then use these in finding \(E(X_{1}^{2})\). For the excitation given, the modal equations of Eq. 8.21

\(\ddot{\vec{Z}}(t) +\beta \dot{\vec{Z}}(t)+\lambda \vec{Z}(t)=\hat{m}^{-1}\theta ^{T}\vec{F} (t)\)

can be written as

\( \hat{m} \ddot{\vec{Z}} (t) +\hat{m} \beta \dot{\vec{Z}} (t) + \hat{m} \lambda \vec{Z} (t)=-\theta ^{T}\begin{pmatrix} m_{11} \\ m_{22} \end{pmatrix} \ddot{Y}(t) =-\begin{pmatrix} 1707 \\ 292.9 \end{pmatrix} \ddot{Y} (t)\)

and this gives the modal responses as

\( E(Z_{1}^{2})=\frac{\pi S_{0}}{2\hat{m}_{11}^{2}\zeta _{1}\omega _{1}^{3}} (1707)^{2}=(2\pi S_{0})(4.246\times 10^{-3}) \)

and

\( E(Z_{2}^{2})=\frac{\pi S_{0}}{2\hat{m}_{22}^{2}\zeta _{2}\omega _{2}^{3}} (292.9)^{2}=(2\pi S_{0})(3.680\times 10^{-6})\)

The cross-modal response from Eq. 8.37

\(q_{r_{1}r_{4}}=\frac{2(\zeta _{r_{1}} \omega _{r_{1}} + \zeta _{r_{4}} \omega _{r_{4}} )} {\hat{m}_{r_{1}r_{1}} \hat{m}_{r_{4}r_{4}} [(\omega _{r_{1}}^{2} – \omega _{r_{4}}^{2})^{2} + 4\omega _{r_{1}} \omega _{r_{4}} (\zeta _{r_{1}} \omega _{r_{1}} + \zeta _{r_{4}} \omega _{r_{4}}) (\zeta _{r_{1}} \omega _{r_{4}} + \zeta _{r_{4}} \omega _{r_{4}} ) ] } \)

is

\( E(Z_{1}Z_{2})=\frac{4\pi S_{0}(\zeta _{1}\omega _{1}+\zeta _{2}\omega _{2})(1707)(292.9)}{\hat{m}_{11}\hat{m}_{22}[(\omega _{1}^{2}-\omega _{2}^{2})^{2}+4\omega _{1}\omega _{2}(\zeta _{1}\omega _{1}+\zeta _{2}\omega _{2})(\zeta _{1}\omega _{2}+\zeta _{2}\omega _{1})]} =(2\pi S_{0})(1.249\times 10^{-7})\)

Using the fact that \( X_{1}(t)=\theta _{11}Z_{1}(t)+\theta _{12}Z_{2}(t) \) then gives

\( E(X_{1}^{2})=\theta _{11}^{2}E(Z_{1}^{2})+2\theta _{11}\theta _{12}E (Z_{1} Z_{2})+\theta _{12}^{2}E(Z_{2})^{2} \) \( =(2\pi S_{0})(4.246\times 10^{-3}+2.498\times 10^{-7}+3.680\times 10^{-6}) \) \( =2.671\times 10^{-3} m^{2} \)

Note that exactly the same individual terms are summed in the two approaches to solving the problem. The difference is simply in the order of performance of the operations.
