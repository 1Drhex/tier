Guest Question.

For the circuit of Figure 20–58, determine the value of the load resistor, \(R_{L}\), such that maximum power will be delivered to the load.

Admin
Solution

Notice that the load impedance consists of a resistor in series with a capacitance of 0.010 μF. Since the capacitive reactance is determined by the frequency, it is quite likely that the maximum power for this circuit may only be a relative maximum, rather than the absolute maximum. For the absolute maximum power to be delivered to the load, the load impedance would need to be

\(Z_{L}=3k\Omega \angle -53.13^{\circ}=1.80k\Omega -j2.40k\Omega \)

The reactance of the capacitor at a frequency of 10 kHz is determined to be

\(X_{C}=\frac{1}{2\pi (10kHz)(0.010\mu F)} =1.592k\Omega \)

Because the capacitive reactance is not equal to the inductive reactance of the Norton impedance, the circuit will not deliver the absolute maximum power to the load. However, relative maximum power will be delivered to the load when

\(R_{L}=\sqrt{R_{Th}^{2}+(X-X_{Th})^{2}}\)

 

\(=\sqrt{(1.800k\Omega )^{2}+(1.592k\Omega -2.4k\Omega )^{2}} \)

 

\(=1.973 k\Omega\)

Figure 20–59 shows the circuit with all impedance values.

The load current will be

\(I_{L} =\frac{Z_{N}}{Z_{N}+Z_{L}} I_{N}\)

 

\(=\frac{1.80k\Omega +j2.40k\Omega }{(1.80k\Omega +j2.40k\Omega )+(1.973k\Omega -j1.592k\Omega )} (5mA\angle 0^{\circ})\)

 

\(=\frac{3k\Omega \angle 53.13^{\circ}}{3.773k\Omega +j0.808k\Omega } (5mA\angle 0^{\circ})\)

 

\(=\frac{15.0V\angle 53.13^{\circ}}{3.859k\Omega \angle 12.09^{\circ}} =3.887mA\angle 41.04^{\circ}\)

We now determine the power delivered to the load for the given conditions as

\(P_{L}=I^{2}_{L}R_{L}\)

 

\(=(3.887mA)^{2}(1.973k\Omega )=29.82mW\)

If we had applied Equation 20–6, we would have found the absolute maximum power to be

\(P_{max}=\frac{I_{N} ^{2}Z_{N} ^{2}}{4R_{N}}\) (20-6)

\(P_{max}=\frac{(5mA)^{2}(3.0k\Omega )^{2}}{4(1.8k\Omega )} =31.25mW\)
