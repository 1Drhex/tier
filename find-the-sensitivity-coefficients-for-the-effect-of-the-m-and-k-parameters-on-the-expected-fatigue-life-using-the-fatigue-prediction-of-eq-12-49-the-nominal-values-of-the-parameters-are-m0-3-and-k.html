Guest Question.

Find the sensitivity coefficients for the effect of the \( \breve{m} \) and \( K \) parameters on the expected fatigue life using the fatigue prediction of Eq. 12.49

\(E(T)=\frac{2\pi K(GR)}{2^{3\vec{m}/2}(\lambda _{2/\vec{m}})^{\vec{m}/2}\Gamma (1+\vec{m}/2)}\)

The nominal values of the parameters are \( \breve{m} _{0}=3 \) and \( K_{0}=4 \) , and the autospectral density of the \( \left\{X(t)\right\} \) stress is one for which it has been shown in that the single-moment method gives \( E(T) \) values significantly greater than those from the Rayleigh approximation. In particular, it has a limiting bimodal form of

\( S_{XX}(\omega )=B[\delta (\omega +\omega _{0})+\delta (\omega -\omega _{0})]+(B/10)[\delta (\omega +15\omega _{0})+\delta (\omega -15\omega _{0})] \)
Admin
Solution

We let \( A \) and \( \breve{M} \) be random variables denoting uncertainty about the values of \( K \) and \( \breve{m} \) , respectively, and define dimensionless parameters as \( \Re _{1}=A/K_{0} \) and \( \Re _{2}=\check{M} /\check{m}_{0} \) . Also we define the normalized response quantity \( Q \) as the ratio of E(T) for parameters . \( A \) and \( M \) to the value for parameters \( K_{0} \) and \( \check{m}_{0} \) .

Note that the simplified autospectral density for \( \left\{X(t)\right\} \) reduces the need for some numerical integration, because it gives \( \lambda _{2/\check{m} }=2B(\omega _{0})^{2/\check{m} }[1+15^{2/\check{m} }/10] \) .

Carrying through the formulas now gives \( \beta _{1}=-0.8, \beta _{11}=1.28,\beta _{2}=-5.402, \) and \( \beta _{22}=26.58 \) .

Thus, in this problem the fatigue life is quite sensitive to the value of \( \check{m} \) but only moderately sensitive to \( K \) . The accompanying sketch shows the dependence of \( Q \) on \( \check{m} \) for a relatively wide range of the parameter. The shape of the plot is quite similar to that in for the dependence of the first-passage probability on the amount of damping in the system. As in that problem, none of the simple perturbation approximations match the actual value very well for such a wide range of the parameter.
