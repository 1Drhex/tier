Guest Question.

The 2 by 2 matrix AB =(2 by 3)(3 by 2) has a “Cauchy-Binet formula” for det AB:

det AB = sum of (2 by 2 determinants in A) (2 by 2 determinants in B)

(a) Guess which 2 by 2 determinants to use from A and B.

(b) Test your formula when the rows of A are 1,2,3 and 1,4,7 with B = \({A}^{T}\).

Admin
Solution

The Cauchy-Binet formula gives the determinant of a square matrix AB (and \(A{A}^{T}\) in particular) when the factors A, B are rectangular.

For (2 by 3) times (3 by 2) there are 3 products of 2 by 2 determinants from A and B (printed in boldface):

\(\left[ \begin{matrix} a & b & c \\ d & e & f \end{matrix} \right] \left[ \begin{matrix} g & j \\ h & k \\ i & l \end{matrix} \right] \quad \left[ \begin{matrix} a & b & c \\ d & e & f \end{matrix} \right] \left[ \begin{matrix} g & j \\ h & k \\ i & l \end{matrix} \right] \quad \left[ \begin{matrix} a & b & c \\ d & e & f \end{matrix} \right] \left[ \begin{matrix} g & j \\ h & k \\ i & l \end{matrix} \right] \)

Check \(A = \left[ \begin{matrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 1 & 4 & 7 \end{matrix} \right]\) ,

\(B =\left[ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 \\ 2 & 4 \\ 3 & 7 \end{matrix} \right]\) ,

\(AB = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 30 \\ 30 & 36 \end{bmatrix}\)

Cauchy-Binet:

(4 – 2)(4 – 2) + (7 – 3)(7 – 3) + (14 – 12)(14 – 12) = 24

(14)(66) – (30)(30) = 24
