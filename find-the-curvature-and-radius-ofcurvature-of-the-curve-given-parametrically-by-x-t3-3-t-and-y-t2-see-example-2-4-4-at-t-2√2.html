Guest Question.

Find the curvature and radius of curvature of the curve given parametrically by x = (t³/3) – t and y = t² (see ) at t = 2√2.

Admin
Solution

We have \(d x / d t=t^{2}-1, d^{2} x / d t^{2}=2 t, d y / d t=2 t\), and \(d^{2} y / d t^{2}=2\),

so

\(\kappa(t)=\frac{\left|2\left(t^{2}-1\right)-2 t(2 t)\right|}{\left[\left(t^{2}-1\right)^{2}+(2 t)^{2}\right]^{3 / 2}}=\frac{2\left(t^{2}+1\right)}{\left(t^{4}+2 t^{2}+1\right)^{3 / 2}}=\frac{2\left(t^{2}+1\right)}{\left(t^{2}+1\right)^{3}}=\frac{2}{\left(t^{2}+1\right)^{2}}\).

Then \(\kappa(2 \sqrt{2})=2 / 81\), and the radius of curvature \(\rho(2 \sqrt{2})=1 / \kappa(2 \sqrt{2})=81 / 2\). A portion of this curve near \(t=2 \sqrt{2}\) is sketched in Figure 1. For reference purposes the unit tangent vector \(T\) at \(t=2 \sqrt{2}\) is included.
