Guest Question.

A 1 MHz sinusoidal carrier is amplitude modulated by a symmetrical square wave of period 100 μ sec. Which of the following frequencies will NOT be present in the modulated signal?

(a) 990 kHz

(b) 1010 kHz

(c) 1020 kHz

(d) 1030 kHz

Admin
Solution

1 MHz ⇒ 1000 kHz = \(f_{c}\) (carrier frequency)
100 μs ⇒10 kHz = \(f_{ m }\) (message signal frequency)

\(\text { Frequencies Present }=1000 \pm 10\left(f_{ c } \pm f_{ m }\right)\)

= 970,1030.

Half wave symmetric wave . So, only odd harmonics are present.

Hence, the correct option is (c).
