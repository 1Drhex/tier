Guest Question.

(a) Determine the transfer function for a SDOF system of natural frequency 10 rad/s and a damping ratio of 1.5 due to a force input. The mass of the system is 2 kg.

(b) Find the response of the system due to a force \(F(t) = 10e^{-3t}\)

Admin
Solution

(a) The differential equation governing the motion of the system is

\( \ddot{x}+30\dot{x}+100x =\frac{1}{2}F(t)\) (a)

Taking the Laplace transform of Equation (a) and setting both initial conditions to zero leads to

\( (s^{2}+30s+100)X(s)=\frac{1}{2}F(s) \) (b)

Rearranging Equation (b) leads to

\( G(s)=\frac{X(s)}{F(s)}=\frac{1}{2(s^{2}+30s+100)}\) (c)

(b) The Laplace transform of \( F(t)= 10e^{-3t}\) is \( F(s)=\frac{10}{s+3}\). From Equation (5.49),

\( X(s)=F(s)G(s)=\frac{10}{2(s^{2}+30s+100)(s+3)}\) (d)

\(\mathcal{L}^{-1}\left\{\frac{\left(s+2 \zeta \omega_{n}\right) x(0)+\dot{x}(0)}{s^{2}+2 \zeta \omega_{n} s+\omega_{n}^{2}}\right\}\).

\(=x(0) e^{-\zeta \omega_{n} t} \cos \left(\omega_{n} \sqrt{1-\zeta^{2}} t\right)\).

\(+\left[\dot{x}(0)+\zeta \omega_{n} x(0)\right] e^{-\zeta \omega_{n} t} \sin \left(\omega_{n} \sqrt{1-\zeta^{2}} t\right) \) (5.49)

 

The system is overdamped, so the denominator of its transfer function is factorable with real factors as

\( X(s)=\frac{5}{(s+3.82)(s+26.18)(s+30)}\) (e)

Performing a partial fraction decomposition on the right-hand side of Equation (e), we have

\( X(s)=\frac{-0.244}{s+3.82}+\frac{9.69\times 10^{-3}}{s+26.18}+ \frac{0.234}{s+3}\) (f)

Inverting Equation (f) leads to

\( x(t)= 0.234e^{-3t}+ 9.69 \times 10^{-3}e^{-26.18t}-0.244e^{-3.82t}\) (g)
