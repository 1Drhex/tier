Energy Balance for Multiple Incomplete Reactions

Consider an isobaric chemical reactor where the following two simultaneous chemical reactions occur:

\(CO +\frac{1}{2} O _{2} \rightarrow CO _{2}\) (1)

\(C +\frac{1}{2} O _{2} \rightarrow CO\) (2)

Initially, the reactor contains 4 mol of CO, 4 mol of \(O _{2}\), and 2 mol of C. At the end of the reaction process, the reactor contains 2 mol of CO and 2 mol of \(O _{2}\). If the initial temperature is 25°C and the final temperature is 225°C, determine the amount of heat transferred during the process.

Admin
Solution

An energy balance gives:

 

\(Q=\Delta H=\Delta H_{r x n}+\int_{298}^{498} \sum\left(n_{i}\right)_{2}\left(c_{p}\right)_{i} d T\)

 

where the extensive enthalpy change of the system has been decomposed into a component for the reaction and a component for the sensible heat in a way similar to that shown in Figure E2.15B.

 

To determine the enthalpy of reaction, we need thermochemical data for each of the two reactions listed above. For Reaction 1 we have:

 

\(\Delta h_{r x n, 298}^{1}=\sum v_{i}\left(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\right)_{i}=\left(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\right)_{ CO _{2}}-\left(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\right)_{ CO }-\frac{1}{2}\left(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\right)_{ O _{2}}\)

 

\(\Delta h_{r x n, 298}^{1}=(-393.51)-1(-110.53)-0=-2.83 \times 10^{5}\left[\frac{ J }{ mol }\right]\) (E2.16A)

 

Similarly, for Reaction 2:

 

\(\Delta h_{r x n, 298}^{2}=\sum v_{i}\left(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\right)_{i}=\left(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\right)_{ CO }-\left(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\right)_{ C }-\frac{1}{2}\left(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\right)_{ O _{2}}\)

 

\(\Delta h_{r x n, 298}^{1}=1(-110.53)-0-0=-1.11 \times 10^{5}\left[\frac{ J }{ mol }\right]\) (E2.16B)

 

The number of moles in the denominator of Equations (E2.16A) and (E2.16B) refer to the moles of CO or C, respectively, which have reacted. We can defi ne this amount as the extent of reaction,\(\xi\). We will learn more about the extent of reaction when we cover Chemical Reaction Equilibrium

Thus, the number of moles of any species i can be related to the extent of k reactions through the stoichiometry of the reactions and their extent:

 

\(n_{i}=n_{i}^{0}+\sum_{k} \nu_{i} \xi_{k}\) (E2.16C)

 

where \(n_{i}^{0}\) is the initial number of moles of i and \(n_{i}\) is the final number of moles after reaction. Using Equation (E2.16C), the number of moles of CO can be written:

 

\(n_{ CO }= n _{ CO }^{\circ}-\xi_{1}+\xi_{2}\)

 

Substituting in values we get:

 

\(2 mol =4 mol -\xi_{1}+\xi_{2}\)

 

Similarly for \(O _{2}\), we have:

 

\(n_{ O _{2}}=n_{ O _{2}}^{0}-\frac{1}{2} \xi_{1}-\frac{1}{2} \xi_{2}\)

 

and,

 

\(2 mol =4 mol -\frac{1}{2} \xi_{1}-\frac{1}{2} \xi_{2}\)

 

Solving the simultaneous equations gives:

 

\(\xi_{1}=3 mol\)

 

\(\xi_{2}=1 mol\)

 

To calculate the extensive enthalpy of reaction we can multiply the molar enthalpy of reaction for each reaction by its extent:

 

\(\Delta H_{r x n}=\xi_{1} \Delta h_{r x n, 1}+\xi_{2} \Delta h_{r x n, 2}=-9.59 \times 10^{5}[ J ]\) (E2.16D)

 

Finally, to calculate the sensible heat, we need to determine the final species concentrations. Using Equation (E2.16C), we have:

 

\(n_{ CO , 2}=4-\xi_{1}+\xi_{2}=2 mol\)

 

\(n_{ O _{2}, 2}=4-\frac{1}{2} \xi_{1}-\frac{1}{2} \xi_{2}=2 mol\)

 

\(n_{ CO _{2}, 2}=0+\xi_{1}=3 mol\)

 

\(n_{ C , 2}=2-\xi_{2}=1 mol\)

 

so,

 

\(\int_{298}^{498} \sum\left(n_{i}\right)_{2}\left(c_{p}\right)_{i} d T=52,000[ J ]\) (E2.16E)

 

where the data from Appendix A.3 were used, similarly to . Calculating the amount of energy transferred by heat:

 

\(Q=\Delta H=\Delta H_{r x n}+\int_{298}^{498} \sum\left(n_{i}\right)_{2}\left(c_{p}\right)_{i} d T=-9.1 \times 10^{5}[ J ]\)

 

where the values given by Equations (E2.16D) and (E.2.16E) were used. The negative sign indicates that heat must be removed from the reactor to the surroundings.
