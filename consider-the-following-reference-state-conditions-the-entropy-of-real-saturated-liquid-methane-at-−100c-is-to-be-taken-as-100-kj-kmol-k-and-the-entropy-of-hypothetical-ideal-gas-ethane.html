Guest Question.

Consider the following reference state conditions: the entropy of real saturated liquid methane at −100°C is to be taken as 100 kJ/kmol K, and the entropy of hypothetical ideal gas ethane at −100°C is to be taken as 200 kJ/kmol K. Calculate the entropy per kmol of a real gas mixture of 50% methane, 50% ethane (mole basis) at 20°C, 4 MPa, in terms of the specified reference state values, and assuming Kay’s rule for the real mixture behavior.

Admin
Solution
\(\begin{aligned}& CH _{4}: T _{0}=-100{ }^{\circ} C , \quad \overline{ s }_{ LIQ 0}=100 kJ / kmol K \\& C _{2} H _{6}: T _{0}=-100^{\circ} C , \quad P _{0}=1 MPa , \quad \overline{ s }_{0}^{*}=200 kJ / kmol K\end{aligned}\)

Also for \(CH _{4}: T _{ C }=190.4 K , P _{ C }=4.60 MPa\)

For a 50% mixture Kay’s rule Eq.12.84:

\(\begin{aligned}& T _{ cmix }=0.5 \times 190.4+0.5 \times 305.4=247.9 K \\& P _{ cmix }=0.5 \times 4.60+0.5 \times 4.88=4.74 MPa\end{aligned}\)

IG MIX at \(T _{0}\left(=-100^{\circ} C \right), P _{0}(=1 MPa )\):

\(CH _{4}: T _{ r 0}=0.91, \quad P _{ G }=0.57 \times 4.60=2.622 MPa\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\overline{ s }_{0 CH 4}^{*} &=\overline{ s }_{ LIQ 0 P _{ G }}+\left(\overline{ s }^{*}-\overline{ s }_{ LIQ }\right)_{ at P _{ G }}-\overline{ R } \ln \left( P _{0} / P _{ G }\right) \\&=100+4.01 \times 8.3145-8.3145 \ln (1 / 2.622)=141.36\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\overline{ s }_{0 MIX }^{*}=0.5 \times 141.36+0.5 \times 200-8.3145(0.5 \ln 0.5+0.5 \ln 0.5)=176.44 \\&\overline{ C }_{ P 0 MIX }=0.5 \times 16.04 \times 2.254+0.5 \times 30.07 \times 1.766=44.629 \\&\overline{ s }_{ TP MIX }^{*}=176.44+44.629 \ln \frac{293.2}{173.2}-8.3145 \ln \frac{4}{1}=188.41 kJ / kmol K\end{aligned}\)

 

For the mixture at T, P: \(T _{ r }=1.183, \quad P _{ r }=0.844\)

Entropy departure \(\overline{ s }_{ TP MIX }^{*}-\overline{ s }_{ TP MIX }=0.4363 \times 8.3145=3.63 kJ / kmol K\)

Therefore, \(\overline{ s }_{ TP MIX }=188.41-3.63= 1 8 4 . 7 8 kJ / kmol K\)

An alternative is to form the ideal gas mixture at T, P instead of at \(T _{0}, P _{0}:\)

\(\begin{gathered}\overline{ s }_{ TP CH _{4}}^{*} =\overline{ S }_{ LIQ 0}+\left(\overline{ s }^{*}-\overline{ s }_{ LIQ }\right)+\overline{ C }_{ P 0 CH 4} \ln \frac{ T }{ T _{0}}-\overline{ R } \ln \frac{ P }{ P _{ G }} \\P _{ G }, T _{0} \text { at } P _{ G }, T _{0}\end{gathered}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&=100+33.34+16.04 \times 2.254 \ln \frac{293.2}{173.2}-8.3145 \ln \frac{4}{2.6} \\&=100+33.34+19.03-3.53=148.84 kJ / kmol K\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\overline{ s }_{ TP C 2 H 6}^{*} &=200+30.07 \times 1.766 \ln \frac{293.2}{173.2}-8.3145 \ln \frac{4}{1} \\&=200+27.96-11.53=216.43 kJ / kmol K\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\overline{ s }_{ TP MIX }^{*}=& 0.5 \times 148.84+0.5 \times 216.43 \\&-8.3145(0.5 \ln 0.5+0.5 \ln 0.5)=188.41 kJ / kmol K\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\overline{ s }_{ TP MIX }=188.41-3.63= 1 8 4 . 7 8 kJ / kmol K\)
