Guest Question.

The beam shown in Fig. P11.46 consists of a W610 × 140 structural steel wide-flange shape [E = 200 GPa; I = 1,120 × \(10^{6} mm ^{4}\)]. For the loading shown, determine:
(a) the reactions at A, B, and D.
(b) the magnitude of the maximum bending stress in the beam.

Admin
Solution

(a) Reactions at A, B, and D. Choose the reaction force at B as the redundant; therefore, the released beam is simply supported between A and D.

 

Consider downward deflection of simply supported beam at B due to uniformly distributed load.
[Appendix C, SS beam with uniformly distributed load over a portion of the span.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{B}=-\frac{w x}{24 E I}\left(L^{3}-2 L x^{2}+x^{3}\right)\) (elastic curve)

Values:

\(w=60 kN / m , L=7.5 m , x=1.5 m\)

 

Calculation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{B} &=-\frac{w x}{24 E I}\left(L^{3}-2 L x^{2}+x^{3}\right) \\&=-\frac{(60 kN / m )(1.5 m )}{24 E I}\left[(7.5 m )^{3}-2(7.5 m )(1.5 m )^{2}+(1.5 m )^{3}\right]=-\frac{1,468.125 kN – m ^{3}}{E I}\end{aligned}\)

 

Consider downward deflection of simply supported beam at B due to concentrated load.
[Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated load not at midspan.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{B}=-\frac{P b x}{6 L E I}\left(L^{2}-b^{2}-x^{2}\right)\) (elastic curve)

Values:

\(P=125 kN , L=7.5 m , b=2.5 m , x=1.5 m\)

 

Calculation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{B} &=-\frac{P b x}{6 L E I}\left(L^{2}-b^{2}-x^{2}\right) \\&=-\frac{(125 kN )(2.5 m )(1.5 m )}{6(7.5 m ) EI }\left[(7.5 m )^{2}-(2.5 m )^{2}-(1.5 m )^{2}\right]=-\frac{497.396 kN – m ^{3}}{E I}\end{aligned}\)

 

Consider upward deflection of simply supported beam at B due to concentrated load \(B_{y}\).
[Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated load not at midspan.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{B}=-\frac{P a b}{6 L E I}\left(L^{2}-a^{2}-b^{2}\right)\)

Values:

\(P=-B_{y}, L=7.5 m , a=1.5 m , b=6 m\)

 

Calculation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{B} &=-\frac{P a b}{6 L E I}\left(L^{2}-a^{2}-b^{2}\right) \\&=-\frac{\left(-B_{y}\right)(1.5 m )(6 m )}{6(7.5 m ) E I}\left[(7.5 m )^{2}-(1.5 m )^{2}-(6 m )^{2}\right]=\frac{\left(3.6 m ^{3}\right) B_{y}}{E I}\end{aligned}\)

Compatibility equation for deflection at B:

\(\begin{gathered}-\frac{1,468.125 kN – m ^{3}}{E I}-\frac{497.396 kN – m ^{3}}{E I}+\frac{\left(3.6 m ^{3}\right) B_{y}}{E I}=0 \\\therefore B_{y}=\frac{1,965.521 kN – m ^{3}}{3.6 m ^{3}}=545.978 kN =546 kN \uparrow\end{gathered}\)

 

Equilibrium equations for entire beam:

 

 

\(\begin{gathered}\Sigma M_{A}=B_{y}(1.5 m )+D_{y}(7.5 m )-(60 kN / m )(7.5 m )(3.75 m )-(125 kN )(5 m )=0 \\\therefore D_{y}=\frac{(60 kN / m )(7.5 m )(3.75 m )+(125 kN )(5 m )-(545.978 kN )(1.5 m )}{7.5 m } \\=199.138 kN =199.1 kN \uparrow\end{gathered}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma F_{y}=A_{y}+B_{y} &+D_{y}-(60 kN / m )(7.5 m )-125 kN =0 \\\therefore A_{y} &=(60 kN / m )(7.5 m )+125 kN -545.978 kN -199.138 kN \\&=-170.116 kN =170.1 kN \downarrow\end{aligned}\)

 

Shear-force and bending-moment diagrams

(b) Magnitude of maximum bending stress:

Section properties (from Appendix B):

\(\begin{aligned}&I=1,120 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4} \quad d=617 mm \\&S=3,640 \times 10^{3} mm ^{3}\end{aligned}\)

Maximum bending moment magnitude

\(M_{\max }=322.67 kN – m\)

Bending stresses at maximum moment

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\frac{(322.67 kN – m )(617 mm / 2)(1,000)^{2}}{1,120 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}} \\&=88.9 MPa\end{aligned}\)

or using the tabulated section modulus value:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\frac{(322.67 kN – m )(1,000)^{2}}{3,640 \times 10^{3} mm ^{3}} \\&=88.6 MPa\end{aligned}\)
