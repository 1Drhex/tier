Guest Question.

The light cable supports a mass of 12 kg per meter of horizontal length and is suspended between the two points on the same level 300 m apart. If the sag is 60 m, find the tension at midlength, the maximum tension, and the total length of the cable.

Admin
Solution

With a uniform horizontal distribution of load, the solution of part (b) of Art. 5/8 applies, and we have a parabolic shape for the cable. For \(h = 60 m\), \(L = 300 m\), and \(w = 12(9.81)(10^{-3})\) kN/m the relation following Eq. 5/14 \(y=\frac{wx^{2} }{2T_{0} }\) with \( l_{A} = L/2 \) gives for the midlength tension

 

\([T_{0}=\frac{wL^{2} }{8h} ] T_{0} =\frac{0.1177(300)^{2} }{8(60)} =22.1 kN\)

 

The maximum tension occurs at the supports and is given by Eq. 5/15b. Thus,

 

\([T_{max}=\frac{wL}{2}\sqrt{1+(\frac{L}{4h} )^{2} } ]\)

 

\(T_{max}=\frac{12(9.81)(10)^{-3}(300) }{2} \sqrt{1+(\frac{300}{4(60)} )^{2} } =28.3\) kN

 

The sag-to-span ratio is \(60/300 = 1/5 < 1/4\). Therefore, the series expression developed in Eq. 5/16a \(S=L[1+\frac{8}{3}(\frac{h}{L} )^{2}-\frac{32}{5} \frac{h}{L} )^{4} +…. ]\) is convergent, and we may write for the total length

 

\(S=300[1+\frac{8}{3}(\frac{1}{5} )^{2}-\frac{32}{5}(\frac{1}{5} )^{4} +…. ]\)

 

\(=300[1+0.1067-0.01024…..]\)

\(=329 m\)

 

Helpful Hint
1) Suggestion: Check the value of \(T_{max}\) directly from the free-body diagram of the right-hand half of the cable, from which a force polygon may be drawn.
