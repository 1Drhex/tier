Guest Question.

A hollow shaft is subjected to an axial load P and a torque T, acting in the directions shown in Figure P15.17. The shaft is made of bronze [E = 105 GPa; ν = 0.34], and it has an outside diameter of 55 mm and an inside diameter of 45 mm. A strain gage is mounted at an angle of θ= 40° with respect to the longitudinal axis of the shaft, as shown in Figure P15.17.
(a) If P = 13,000 N and T = 260 N-m, what is the strain reading that would be expected from the gage?
(b) If the strain gage gives a reading of –195 με when the axial load has a magnitude of P = 6,200 N, what is the magnitude of the torque T applied to the shaft?

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[(55 mm )^{2}-(45 mm )^{2}\right]=785.398 mm ^{2} \quad J=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[(55 mm )^{4}-(45 mm )^{4}\right]=495,782.591 mm ^{4}\)

 

(a) Normal and shear stress magnitudes:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma &=\frac{P}{A}=\frac{-13,000 N }{785.398 mm ^{2}}=16.552 MPa ( C ) \\\tau &=\frac{T c}{J}=\frac{(260 N – m )(55 mm / 2)(1,000 mm / m )}{495,782.591 mm ^{4}}=14.422 MPa\end{aligned}\)

(sense of shear stress to be determined by inspection)
A stress element showing the normal and shear stresses is shown below.

 

Generalized Hooke’s Law:
Since the stresses are known, the normal strains in the x and y directions can be computed from Eqs. (13.21):

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{x} &=\frac{1}{E}\left(\sigma_{x}-v \sigma_{y}\right)=\frac{1}{105,000 MPa }[-16.552 MPa -(0.34)(0)] \\&=-157.639 \times 10^{-6} mm / mm =-157.639 \mu \varepsilon \\\varepsilon_{y} &=\frac{1}{E}\left(\sigma_{y}-v \sigma_{x}\right)=\frac{1}{105,000 MPa }[0-(0.34)(-16.552 MPa )] \\&=53.597 \times 10^{-6} mm / mm =53.597 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)

From Eq. (13.18), determine the shear modulus G:

\(G=\frac{E}{2(1+v)}=\frac{105,000 MPa }{2(1+0.34)}=39,179.104 MPa\)

and compute the shear stress \(\tau_{x y}\) from Eq. (13.20):

\(\gamma_{x y}=\frac{\tau_{x y}}{G}=\frac{-14.422 MPa }{39,179.104 MPa }=-368.095 \times 10^{-6} rad\)

Strain transformation equation:
Determine the strain that should be measured by the strain gage oriented at θ = +40°:

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{n} &=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2} \theta+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2} \theta+\gamma_{x y} \sin \theta \cos \theta \\&=(-157.639 \mu \varepsilon) \cos ^{2}\left(40^{\circ}\right)+(53.597 \mu \varepsilon) \sin ^{2}\left(40^{\circ}\right)+(-368.095 \mu \operatorname{rad}) \sin \left(40^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(40^{\circ}\right) \\&=-251.613 \mu \varepsilon=-252 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Normal stress magnitude:
For an applied load of P = 6,200 N:

\(\sigma=\frac{P}{A}=\frac{-6,200 N }{785.398 mm ^{2}}=-7.894 MPa\)

Generalized Hooke’s Law:
The normal stresses are known; therefore, the normal strains in the x and y directions can be computed from Eqs. (13.21):

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{x} &=\frac{1}{E}\left(\sigma_{x}-v \sigma_{y}\right)=\frac{1}{105,000 MPa }[-7.894 MPa -(0.34)(0)] \\&=-75.182 \times 10^{-6} mm / mm =-75.182 \mu \varepsilon \\\varepsilon_{y} &=\frac{1}{E}\left(\sigma_{y}-v \sigma_{x}\right)=\frac{1}{105,000 MPa }[0-(0.34)(-7.894 MPa )] \\&=25.562 \times 10^{-6} mm / mm =25.562 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)

Strain transformation equation:
The strain gage measures a normal strain of \(\varepsilon_{n}=-195\) at an orientation of θ = +40°. From the normal strain transformation equation, determine the shear strain:

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{n} &=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2} \theta+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2} \theta+\gamma_{x y} \sin \theta \cos \theta \\-195 \mu \varepsilon &=(-75.182 \mu \varepsilon) \cos ^{2}\left(40^{\circ}\right)+(25.562 \mu \varepsilon) \sin ^{2}\left(40^{\circ}\right)+\gamma_{x y} \sin \left(40^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(40^{\circ}\right)\end{aligned}\)

therefore:

\(\gamma_{x y}=-\frac{(-75.182 \mu \varepsilon) \cos ^{2}\left(40^{\circ}\right)+(25.562 \mu \varepsilon) \sin ^{2}\left(40^{\circ}\right)+195 \mu \varepsilon}{\sin \left(40^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(40^{\circ}\right)}=-327.867 \mu rad\)

Shear stress:
The shear stress \(\tau_{x y} \) can be computed from Eq. (13.20):

\(\tau_{x y}=G \gamma_{x y}=(39,179.104 MPa )\left(-327.867 \times 10^{-6} rad \right)=-12.846 MPa\)

Torque:
The torque required to produce this shear stress magnitude is found from:

\(T=\frac{\tau J}{c}=\frac{\left(12.846 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(495,782.591 mm ^{4}\right)}{55 mm / 2}=231,585 N – mm =232 N – m\)
