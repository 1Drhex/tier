Guest Question.

Design a low-noise 60GHz differential Colpitts VCO in the 45nm CMOS technology from . Calculate its frequency tuning range assuming a tank Q of 5 and a varactor capacitance ratio of 2:1.

Admin
Solution

We start by setting \( V_{O S C}\) to \(0.6 \mathrm{~V}\), the maximum value allowed for safe operation, such that \( V_{D D}<=1.2 \mathrm{~V}: \)
1. We select \( L=44 \mathrm{pH} \) as a reasonably small value. We note that an uncertainty of \( +/-2 \mathrm{pH} \) in interconnect inductance translates into \( \mathrm{a}+/-4.5 \% \) error in tank inductance.
2. A \( C_{e q} \) value of \( 160 \mathrm{fF} \) is calculated from \( f_{O S C}=60 \mathrm{GHz} \) and \( L=44 \mathrm{pH} \) . If we assume a tank Q of 5 , an \( R_{p} \) of \( 83 \Omega \) is calculated at \( 60 \mathrm{GHz} \) .
3. Since \( R_{P} \) and \( V_{O S C} \) are known, a minimum value for \( G_{m}=48.2 \mathrm{mS} \) is estimated from (10.41). An initial guess on \( I_{B I A S}>7.23 \mathrm{~mA} \) is made from (10.42), assuming \( C_{1}=C_{2}=2 C_{e q} \) .
4. To minimize phase noise, the MOSFETs are biased at \( J_{O P T}=0.15 \mathrm{~mA} / \mu \mathrm{m} \) at \( V_{D S}=V_{D D} / 2=0.6 \mathrm{~V} \) . This sets the minimum MOSFET gate width to \( 48.2 \mu \mathrm{m} \) . We select \( W=72 \mu \mathrm{m} \) , about 1.5 times larger, which results in a total bias current of \( 2 \times 72 \mu \mathrm{m} \times 0.15 \mathrm{~mA} / \mu \mathrm{m}=21.6 \mathrm{~mA} \) .

5. Knowing W and \(C_{1}=C_{2}=2\left(C_{e q}-C_{g d}\right)=2(160 \mathrm{fF}-27.36 \mathrm{fF})=265.28 \mathrm{fF}\) we calculate \(C_{1}^{\prime}=265.28 \mathrm{fF}-(0.65 \mathrm{fF}) \times 72 \mu \mathrm{m}=218.48 \mathrm{fF}\) and \(C_{2}^{\prime}=265.28 \mathrm{fF}-(0.3 \mathrm{fF}) \times 72 \mu \mathrm{m}=243.68 \mathrm{fF}\).
We can now calculate the high-frequency limit of the tuning range knowing that the minimum value of the varactor capacitance \(C_{2}^{\prime}\) is \(243.68 / 2 \mathrm{fF}=121.89 \mathrm{fF}\). This makes \(C_{2}=27.36 \mathrm{fF}+121.89 \mathrm{fF}=149.25 \mathrm{fF}\) and
\(C_{e q}=27.36 \mathrm{fF}+\frac{(265.38 \mathrm{fF})(149.25 \mathrm{fF})}{265.38 \mathrm{fF}+149.25 \mathrm{fF}}=122.87 \mathrm{fF} \)

which leads to \( f_{O S C 2}=68.48 \mathrm{GHz}\). The resulting VCO tuning range extends from \(60 \mathrm{GHz}\) to 68.48GHz.

In reality the tuning range will be smaller and will shift to a lower frequency because we have ignored the parasitic capacitance associated with the inductor and the interconnect.

*equation(10.41) \(\frac{G_{m}}{\omega_{O S C}^{2} C_{1} C_{2}}=\frac{R_{P}}{Q^{2}}=>G_{m} R_{P}=\frac{\left(C_{1}+C_{2}\right)^{2}}{C_{1} C_{2}} \geq 4 \)

*equation(10.42) \( G_{m} \approx \frac{2 I_{B I A S}}{V_{1}}=\frac{2 I_{B I A S}\left(1+\frac{C_{1}}{C_{2}}\right)}{V_{O S C}}=2 \frac{I_{B I A S}}{V_{O S C}} C_{1} L \omega_{O S C}^{2}\)
