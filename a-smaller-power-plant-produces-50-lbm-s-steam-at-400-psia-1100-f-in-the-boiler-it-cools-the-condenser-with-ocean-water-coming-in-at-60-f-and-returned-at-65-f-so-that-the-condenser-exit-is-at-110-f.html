Guest Question.

A smaller power plant produces 50 lbm/s steam at 400 psia, 1100 F, in the boiler. It cools the condenser with ocean water coming in at 60 F and returned at 65 F so that the condenser exit is at 110 F. Find the net power output and the required mass flow rate of the ocean water

Admin
Solution

The states properties from Tables F.7.1 and F.7.2

1: \(110 F , x =0: h _{1}=78.01 Btu / lbm , v _{1}=0.01617 ft ^{3} / lbm , P _{ sat }=1.28 psia\)

3: \(400 psia , 1100 F : \quad h _{3}=1577.44 Btu / lbm , \quad s _{3}=1.7989 Btu / lbm R\)

C.V. Pump Reversible and adiabatic.
Energy: \(w _{ p }= h _{2}- h _{1}\) ; Entropy: \(s _{2}= s _{1}\)

since incompressible it is easier to find work (positive in) as

\(w _{ p }=\int v dP = v _{1}\left( P _{2}- P _{1}\right)=0.01617(400-1.3) \frac{144}{778}=1.19 Btu / lbm\)

C.V. Turbine : \(w _{ T }= h _{3}- h _{4} ; s _{4}= s _{3}\)

\(\begin{aligned}s _{4}= s _{3} &=1.7989=0.1473+ x _{4}(1.8101) \quad \Rightarrow \quad x _{4}=0.9124 \\& \Rightarrow h _{4}=78.01+0.9124(1031.28)=1018.95 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{gathered}w _{ T }=1577.44-1018.95=558.5 Btu / lbm \\\dot{ W }_{ NET }=\dot{ m }\left( w _{ T }- w _{ p }\right)=50(558.5-1.19)= 2 7 8 6 6 Btu / s\end{gathered}\)

 

C.V. Condenser : \(q _{ L }= h _{4}- h _{1}=1018.95-78.01=940.94 Btu / lbm\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{ Q }_{ L }=\dot{ m } q _{ L }=50 \times 940.94=47047 Btu / s =\dot{ m }_{\text {ocean }} C _{ p } \Delta T \\&\dot{ m }_{\text {ocean }}=\dot{ Q }_{ L } / C _{ p } \Delta T =47047 /(1.0 \times 5)= 9 4 0 9 lbm / s\end{aligned}\)
