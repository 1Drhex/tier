Guest Question.

Given: chip length = 95 mm; uncut chip length = 245 mm; rake angle = \(20^°\); depth of cut = 0.5 mm Horizontal and vertical components of cutting force are 2300 N and 250 N, respectively Considering orthogonal cutting, find out (a) shear plane angle, (b) chip thickness, (c) friction angle and (d) resultant cutting force.

Admin
Solution

Given: \(l_c\) = 95 mm; l = 245 mm; \(\alpha = 20^°\); t = 0.5 mm; \(F_H (= F_t)\) = 2300 N and \(F_v (= F_f)/\) = 250 N
Shear plane angle (Φ)
\( r = \frac{chip length l_c}{uncut chip length (l))} = \frac{95}{245}= 0.38\)
\( (\phi )=\tan ^{-1} \left[\frac{r \cos \alpha }{1 – r \sin \alpha} \right] = \tan ^{-1} \left[\frac{0.38 \cos 20^{°} }{1 – 0.38 \sin 20^{°} } \right] = \mathbf {22.29^{°}(Ans.)} \)
Chip thickness \(\mathbf {(t_c)} \)
\(r = \frac{depth of cut (t)}{chip thickniess (t_c)} \)
or \( t_c = \frac{t}{r} = \frac{0.5}{0.38} \)= 1.31 mm (Ans.)

Friction angle (β)
μ = tan μ = \(\frac{F_f + F_t\tan \alpha }{F_t – F_f \tan \alpha }\)
\(= \frac{250+2300\tan 20}{2300-250\tan 20} = \frac{1084.9}{2209.1} =0.491\)
or β = \(\tan ^{-1} (0.491) \mathbf {= 26.15^°}\)
Resultant cutting force (R)
\( = \sqrt{Ft^{2} + Fa^{2} } \)
\( = \sqrt{(2300)^{2} + (250)^{2} } \)
= 2313.54 N (Ans.)
