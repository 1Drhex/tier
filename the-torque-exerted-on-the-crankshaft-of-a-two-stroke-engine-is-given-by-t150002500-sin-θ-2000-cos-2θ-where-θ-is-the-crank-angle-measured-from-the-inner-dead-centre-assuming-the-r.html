Guest Question.

The torque exerted on the crankshaft of a two stroke engine is given by

\( T=15000+2500 \sin \theta-2000 \cos 2 \theta \) .

where θ is the crank angle measured from the inner dead centre. Assuming the resulting torque to be constant, determine (a) the power of the engine when the speed is 180 rpm, (b) the moment of inertia of the flywheel if the speed variation is not to exceed ±0.5% of the mean speed, and (c) the angular acceleration of the flywheel when the crank has turned through 30° from inner dead centre.

Admin
Solution

(a) For a two-stroke engine, cycle is repeated after every crank rotation of 180°.

\( T_{m}=\left(\frac{1}{\pi}\right) \int_{0}^{\pi} T d \theta=\left(\frac{1}{\pi}\right) \int_{0}^{\pi}(15000+2500 \sin 2 \theta-2000 \cos 2 \theta) d \theta \).

\( =\frac{1}{\pi}|15000 \theta-1250 \cos 2 \theta-1000 \sin 2 \theta|_{0}^{\pi}=15000 Nm \).

\( \text { Power }=\frac{2 \pi \times 180 \times 15000}{60 \times 10^{3}}=283.74 kW \).

\( \text { (b) Change in torque, } \Delta T=T-T_{m}=(2500 \sin 2 \theta-2000 \cos 2 \theta) \),

\( \text { For } \Delta T=0, \tan 2 \theta=0.8,2 \theta=38.66^{\circ} \text { and } 218.66^{\circ} \).

\( \text { The } T-\theta \text { diagram is shown in Fig. } 10.15 .\)

\( \text { Fluctuation of energy, } E_{f}=\int_{38.66^{\circ}}^{218.66^{\circ}}(2500 \sin 2 \theta-2000 \cos 2 \theta) d \theta \).

\( =|-1250 \cos 2 \theta-1000 \sin 2 \theta|_{38.66^{\circ}}^{218.66^{\circ}} \).

\( =-1250 \cos 218.66^{\circ}-1000 \sin 218.66^{\circ}+1250 \cos 38.66^{\circ}+1000 \sin 38.66^{\circ} \).

\( =3201.6 Nm \).

\( I=\frac{900 E_{f}}{\pi^{2} N_{m}^{2} C_{s}} \).

(c) \( =\frac{900 \times 3201.6}{\pi^{2} \times 180^{2} \times 0.005}=1802 kg m ^{2} \).

\( T_{\text {excess }}=2500 \sin 2 \theta-2000 \cos 2 \theta \).

\( \text { At } \theta=30^{\circ}, T_{\text {excess }}=1165 Nm \).

\( T_{\text {excess }}=I \cdot a \).

Angular acceleration, \( a=\frac{1165}{1802}=0.6465 rad / s ^{2} \).
