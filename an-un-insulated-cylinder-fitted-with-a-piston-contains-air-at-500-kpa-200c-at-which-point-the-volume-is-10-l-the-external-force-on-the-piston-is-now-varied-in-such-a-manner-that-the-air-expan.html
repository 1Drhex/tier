Guest Question.

An un-insulated cylinder fitted with a piston contains air at 500 kPa, 200°C, at which point the volume is 10 L. The external force on the piston is now varied in such a manner that the air expands to 150 kPa, 25 L volume. It is claimed that in this process the air produces 70% of the work that would have resulted from a reversible, adiabatic expansion from the same initial pressure and temperature to the same final pressure. Room temperature is 20°C.
a) What is the amount of work claimed?
b) Is this claim possible?

Admin
Solution

C.V.: Air; \(R =0.287 kJ / kg – K , C _{ p }=1.004 kJ / kg K , C _{ v }=0.717 kJ / kg K\)

State 1: \(T _{1}=200^{\circ} C , P _{1}=500 kPa , V _{1}=10 L =0.01 m ^{3}\);

\(m _{1}= V _{1} / v _{1}= P _{1} V _{1} / RT _{1}=0.0368 kg\)

State 2: \(P _{2}=150 kPa , V _{2}=25 L =0.025 m ^{3}\)

\(\eta_{ S }=\) 70% ; Actual Work is 70% of Isentropic Work

a) Assume Reversible and Adiabatic Process; \(s_{1}=s_{2 s}\)

\(T _{2 s }= T _{1}\left(\frac{ P _{2}}{ P _{1}}\right)^{\frac{ k -1}{ k }}=473.15(150 / 500)=335.4 K\)

Energy Eq.: \({ }_{1} Q _{2 s }= m \left( u _{2 s }- u _{1}\right)+{ }_{1} W _{2 s } ; \quad{ }_{1} Q _{2 s }=0\)

Assume constant specific heat

\({ }_{1} W _{2 s }= mC _{ v }\left( T _{1}- T _{2 s }\right)=3.63 kJ\)

 

\({ }_{1} W _{2 ac} =0.7 \times{ }_{1} W _{2 s }= 2 . 5 4 k J\)

 

b) Use Ideal Gas Law; \(T _{2 ac }= T _{1} P _{2} V _{2} / P _{1} V _{1}=354.9 K\)

Energy Eq.: \({ }_{1} Q _{2 ac} = m C _{ v }\left( T _{2 ac }- T _{1}\right)+{ }_{1} W _{2 ac }=-0.58 kJ\)

\(2^{\text {nd }} \text { Law: }_{1} S_{2 \text { gen }}=m\left(s_{2}-s_{1}\right)-\frac{Q_{c v}}{T_{o}} ; \quad Q_{C V}={ }_{1} Q_{2 ac} , T_{o}=20^{\circ} C\)

 

\(s _{2}- s _{1}= Cp \ln \frac{ T _{2}}{ T _{1}}- R \ln \frac{ P _{2}}{ P _{1}}=0.0569 kJ / kg – K\)

 

\({ }_{1} S _{2 \text { gen }}=0.00406 kJ / K \geq 0\); Process is Possible
