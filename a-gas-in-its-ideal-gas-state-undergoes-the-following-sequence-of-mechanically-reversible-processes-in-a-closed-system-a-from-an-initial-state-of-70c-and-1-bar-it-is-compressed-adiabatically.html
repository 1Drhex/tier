Guest Question.

A gas in its ideal-gas state undergoes the following sequence of mechanically reversible processes in a closed system:
(a) From an initial state of \(70^{\circ}C\) and 1 bar, it is compressed adiabatically to \(150^{\circ}C.\)
(b) It is then cooled from 150 to \(70^{\circ}C\) at constant pressure.
(c) Finally, it expands isothermally to its original state.
Calculate \(W, Q, \Delta U^{ig},\) and \(\Delta H^{ig}\) for each of the three processes and for the entire cycle.
Take \(C_{V}^{ig} = 12.471\) and \(C_{P}^{ig} = 20.785 J\cdot mol ^{−1}\cdot K ^{−1} .\)

Admin
Solution

Take as a basis 1 mol of gas.
(a) For adiabatic compression, \(Q = 0,\) and

\( \Delta U^{ig}=W=C^{ig}_{V}\Delta T=(12.471)(150-70)=998 J\)

 

\( \Delta H^{ig}=C^{ig}_{P}\Delta T=(20.785)(150-70)=1663 J\)

Pressure \(P_{2}\) is found from Eq. (3.23b):

\( TP^{(1-\gamma )/\gamma }=const\) (3.23b)

 

\( P_{2}=P_{1}\left(\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}} \right)^{\gamma /(\gamma -1)}=(1)\left(\frac{150+273.15}{70+273.15} \right)^{2.5}=1.689 bar\)

(b) For this constant-pressure process,

\( Q=\Delta H^{ig}=C^{ig}_{P}\Delta T=(20.785)(70-150)=-1663 J\)

 

\( \Delta U=C^{ig}_{V}\Delta T=(12.471)(70-150)=-998 J\)

 

\( W=\Delta U^{ig}-Q=-998-(-1663)=665 J\)

(c) For this isothermal process, \(\Delta U^{ig}\) and \(\Delta H^{ig}\) are zero; Eq. (3.20) yields:

\( Q=-W=RT\ln \frac{V^{ig}_{2}}{V^{ig}_{1}}=RT\ln \frac{P_{1}}{P_{2}}\) (const T) (3.20)

 

\( Q=-W=RT\ln \frac{P_{3}}{P_{1}}=RT\ln \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}=(8.314)(343.15)\ln \frac{1.689}{1}=1495 J\)

For the entire cycle,

\( Q=0-1663+1495=-168 J\)

 

\( W=998+665-1495=168 J\)

 

\( \Delta U^{ig}=998-998+0=0\)

 

\( \Delta H^{ig}=1663-1663+0=0\)

The property changes \(\Delta U^{ig}\) and \(\Delta H^{ig}\) both are zero for the entire cycle because the initial and final states are identical. Note also that \(Q = −W\) for the cycle. This follows from the first law with \(\Delta U^{ig} = 0.\)
