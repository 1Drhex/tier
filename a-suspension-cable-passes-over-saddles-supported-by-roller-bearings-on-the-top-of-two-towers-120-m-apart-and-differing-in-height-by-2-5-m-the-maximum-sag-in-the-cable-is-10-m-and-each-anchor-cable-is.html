Guest Question.

A suspension cable passes over saddles supported by roller bearings on the top of two towers 120 m apart and differing in height by 2.5 m. The maximum sag in the cable is 10 m and each anchor cable is inclined at 55° to the horizontal. If the cable carries a uniform horizontally distributed load of 25 kN/m and is to be made of steel having an allowable tensile stress of 240 N/mm² , determine its minimum diameter. Calculate also the vertical load on the tallest tower.

Admin
Solution

The suspension cable is shown in Fig. S.5.7. Since the towers are of different height the lowest point in the cable will not be at mid-span. The position of the lowest point may be found by either using Eq. (5.17) directly or by working from first principles. We shall adopt the latter approach.

\(L_{2}=\frac{L}{\left(\sqrt{\frac{D-h}{D}}+1\right)}\) (5.17)

Taking moments about B for CB

\( H \times 10=\frac{25 L_{2}^{2}}{2} \)

i.e.

\( H=\frac{5 L_{2}^{2}}{4} \) (i)

Now taking moments about A for CA

\( H \times 7.5=\frac{25 L_{1}^{2}}{2} \)

i.e.

\( H=\frac{5 L_{1}^{2}}{3}\) (ii)

Equating Eqs (i) and (ii) gives

\( L_{1}=0.866 L_{2} \)

Then, since \( L_{1}+L_{2}=120 m \),

\( L_{2}=64.31 m \)

From Eq. (i)

\( H=5169.7 kN \)

H is equal to the horizontal component of the maximum tension in the suspension cable which must occur at B since the span CB is greater than the span CA. The vertical component of the maximum tension is equal to 25×64.31=1607.8 kN. Then

\( T_{\max }=\sqrt{5169.7^{2}+1607.8^{2}}=5413.9 kN \)

We must now investigate the tension in the anchor cable since this will be different to the tension in the suspension cable. There is no resultant horizontal force on the top of a tower since the saddles are on rollers. Therefore

\( T_{ AC } \cos 55^{\circ}=H=5169.7 kN \)

i.e.

\( T_{ AC }=9013.1 kN \)

The maximum tension in the cable is therefore 9013.1 kN and the maximum stress is

\(\sigma_{\max }=\frac{9013.1 \times 10^{3}}{\left(\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}\right)}=240\)

Then

\( D=218.7 mm \)

The vertical load on the tallest tower \( =1607.8+9013.1 \sin 55^{\circ}=8990.9 kN \).
