Guest Question.

Design of Integral Control

PROBLEM: Consider the plant of Eqs. (12.116):

\(\dot{x} =\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 \\ -3 & -5 \end{matrix} \right] x +\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right] u\)

 

\(y=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right] x\)

a. Design a controller without integral control to yield a 10% overshoot and a settling time of 0.5 second. Evaluate the steady-state error for a unit step input.

b. Repeat the design of Part a using integral control. Evaluate the steady-state error for a unit step input.

Admin
Solution

a. Using the requirements for settling time and percent overshoot, we find that the desired characteristic polynomial is

\(s^{2} +16s+183.1\)

Since the plant is represented in phase-variable form, the characteristic polynomial for the controlled plant with state-variable feedback is

\(s^{2}+(5+k_{2})s+(3+k_{1})\)

Equating the coefficients of Eqs. (12.117)

\(s^{2} +16s+183.1\)

and (12.118), \(s^{2}+(5+k_{2})s+(3+k_{1})\)

we have

\(K=\left[\begin{matrix} k_{1} & k_{2} \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix} 180.1 & 11 \end{matrix} \right]\)

From Eqs. (12.3),

\(\dot{x}=Ax+B(-Kx+r)=(A-BK)x+Br\)

 

\(y=Cx\)

the controlled plant with state-variable feedback represented in phasevariable form is

\(\dot{x}= (A-BK)x+Br=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 \\ -183.1 & -16 \end{matrix} \right] x+\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right] r\)

 

\(y=Cx=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right]x\)

Using Eq. (7.96),

\(e(\infty )=1-y_{ss}=1-CV=1+CA^{-1}B\)

we find that the steady-state error for a step input is

\(e(\infty )=1+C(A-BK)^{-1} B\)

 

\(=1+\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 \\ -183.1 & -16 \end{matrix} \right] ^{-1} \left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right]\)

=0.995

b. We now use Eqs. (12.115)

\(\left[\begin{matrix} \dot{x} \\ \dot{x_{N} } \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix} (A-BK) & BK_{e} \\ -C & 0 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} x \\ x_{N} \end{matrix} \right] +\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right] r\)

 

\(y=\left[\begin{matrix} C & 0 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} x \\ x_{N} \end{matrix} \right] \)

to represent the integral-controlled plant as follows:

\(\left[\begin{matrix} \dot{x_{1} } \\ \dot{x_{2} } \\ \dot{x_{N} } \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix} \left(\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 \\ -3 & -5 \end{matrix} \right]-\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} k_{1} & k_{2} \end{matrix} \right] \right) & \left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right]K_{e} \\ -\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right] & 0 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{N} \end{matrix} \right] +\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right] r\)

 

\(=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -(3+k_{1} ) & -(5+k_{2}) & K_{e} \\ -1 & 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{N} \end{matrix} \right] +\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right] r\)

 

\(y=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0\end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{N} \end{matrix} \right]\)

Using Eq. (3.73)

\(T(s)=\frac{Y(s)}{U(s)} =C(sI-A)^{-1} B+D\)

and the plant of Eqs. (12.116),

\(\dot{x} =\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 \\ -3 & -5 \end{matrix} \right] x +\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right] u\)

 

\(y=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right] x\)

we find that the transfer function of the plant is \(G(s) =1/ (s^{2}+5s+3 )\). The desired characteristic polynomial for the closedloop integral-controlled system is shown in Eq. (12.117).

\(s^{2} +16s+183.1\)

Since the plant has no zeros, we assume no zeros for the closed-loop system and augment Eq. (12.117) with a third pole, (s+100), which has a real part greater than five times that of the desired dominant secondorder poles. The desired third-order closed-loop system characteristic polynomial is

\((s+100)(s^{2}+16s+183.1 )=s^{3} +116s^{2} +1783.1s+18,310 \)

The characteristic polynomial for the system of Eqs. (12.112)

\(\dot{x} =Ax+Bu\)

 

\(\dot{x_{N} } =-Cx+r\)

 

\(y=Cx\)

is

\(s^{3}+(s+k_{2} )s^{2}+(3+k_{1} )s+K_{e}\)

Matching coefficients from Eqs. (12.123)

\((s+100)(s^{2}+16s+183.1 )=s^{3} +116s^{2} +1783.1s+18,310 \)

and (12.124),

\(s^{3}+(s+k_{2} )s^{2}+(3+k_{1} )s+K_{e}\)

we obtain

\(k_{1}=1780.1\) \(k_{2}=111\) \(k_{e}=18,310\)

Substituting these values into Eqs. (12.122)

\(\left[\begin{matrix} \dot{x_{1} } \\ \dot{x_{2} } \\ \dot{x_{N} } \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix} \left(\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 \\ -3 & -5 \end{matrix} \right]-\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} k_{1} & k_{2} \end{matrix} \right] \right) & \left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right]K_{e} \\ -\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right] & 0 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{N} \end{matrix} \right] +\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right] r\)

 

\(=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -(3+k_{1} ) & -(5+k_{2}) & K_{e} \\ -1 & 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{N} \end{matrix} \right] +\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right] r\)

 

\(y=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0\end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{N} \end{matrix} \right]\)

yields this closed-loop integral-controlled system:

\(\left[\begin{matrix} \dot{x_{1} } \\ \dot{x_{2} } \\ \dot{x_{N} } \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -1783.1 & -116 & 18,310 \\ -1 & 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{N} \end{matrix} \right] +\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right]r\)

 

\(y=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0\end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{N} \end{matrix} \right]\)

In order to check our assumption for the zero, we now apply Eq. (3.73) to Eqs. (12.126)

\(\left[\begin{matrix} \dot{x_{1} } \\ \dot{x_{2} } \\ \dot{x_{N} } \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -1783.1 & -116 & 18,310 \\ -1 & 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{N} \end{matrix} \right] +\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right]r\)

 

\(y=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0\end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{N} \end{matrix} \right]\)

and find the closed-loop transfer function to be

\(T(s)=\frac{18,310}{s^{3}+116s^{2}+1783.1s+18,310 }\)

Since the transfer function matches our design, we have the desired transient response. Now let us find the steady-state error for a unit step input. Applying Eq. (7.96)to Eqs. (12.126), we obtain

\(e(\infty )=1+\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0\end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -1783.1 & -116 & 18,310 \\ -1 & 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right] ^{-1} \left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end {matrix} \right] =0\)

Thus, the system behaves like a Type 1 system.
