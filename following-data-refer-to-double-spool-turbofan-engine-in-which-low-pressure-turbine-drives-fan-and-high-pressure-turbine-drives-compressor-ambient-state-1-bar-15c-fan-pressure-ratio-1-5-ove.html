Guest Question.

Following data refer to double spool turbofan engine in which low pressure turbine drives fan and high pressure turbine drives compressor.
Ambient state : 1 bar, 15°C
Fan pressure ratio : 1.5
Overall pressure ratio : 28
By pass ratio, \(\left\lgroup\frac{m^{′}_{ p}}{m^{′}_{ c}}\right\rgroup\) : 5.5
Mass flow rate of air : 200 kg/s.
Stagnation temperature at turbine inlet : 1300°C
Mechanical efficiency \(\pmb{η}_{mech.}\) = 96%
Polytropic efficiency of fan and compressor = \(\pmb{η}_{poly, fan} =\pmb{η}_{poly, compr.}\) = 90%
Polytropic efficiency of turbine, \(\pmb{η}_{poly, turb}\) = 95%
Pressure loss in combustion chamber = 1.5 bar
Nozzle efficiency, \(\pmb{η}_{nozzle}\) = 95%
\(\begin{aligned}\text{Lower calorific value of fuel} &: 43100 kJ/kg\\C_{p_{air}} &= 1.005 kJ/kg·K\\C_{p_{gas}}&= 1.14 kJ/kg·K\\γ_{gas} &= 1.33\end{aligned}\\\)Determine air-fuel ratio, specific fuel consumption and specific thrust.

Admin
Solution

Using the polytropic efficiency for compression and expansion the index of compression and expansion can be obtained\(\\\begin{aligned}\text{For compression: }\left\lgroup\frac{n_{e} – 1}{n_{e}} \right\rgroup &= \frac{1}{η_{poly , compr}}\left\lgroup\frac{γ – 1}{γ} \right\rgroup \\&=\frac{1}{0.87}\left\lgroup\frac{1.4 – 1}{1.4} \right\rgroup = 0.328\\\text{For expansion: }\left\lgroup\frac{n_{T} – 1}{n_{T}} \right\rgroup &= η_{poly , turb} \left\lgroup\frac{γ_{gas} – 1}{γ_{gas}} \right\rgroup = 0.90 \left\lgroup\frac{0.33}{1.33} \right\rgroup \\&= 0.223\\\text{In fan, }\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad \frac{T_{02^{′}}}{T_{01}}&= \left\lgroup\frac{p_{02}}{p_{01}} \right\rgroup^{\left\lgroup\frac{n_{c} – 1}{n_{c}} \right\rgroup } ; Take, p_{01} = p_{a} and T_{01} = T_{a}\\T_{02^{′}}&= 288 \left\lgroup\frac{1.5}{1} \right\rgroup^{0.328} \\T_{02^{′}}&= 328.96 K\\\text{In compressor}\quad\qquad\qquad \frac{T_{03^{′}}}{T_{02^{′}}}&= \left\lgroup\frac{p_{03}}{p_{02}} \right\rgroup^{\left\lgroup\frac{n_{T} – 1}{n_{T}} \right\rgroup }\\T_{03^{′}}&= 328.96\left\lgroup\frac{28}{1.5} \right\rgroup^{0.223} = 631.82 K\end{aligned}\\\)For flow of bypass atmospheric air through nozzle the nozzle may be checked for whether it is in choked state or not.\(\\\begin{aligned}\text{Critical pressure ratio for by pass air nozzle, }\frac{p_{c}}{p_{02}}&= \left\lgroup\frac{2}{γ + 1} \right\rgroup^{γ/(γ – 1)} \\\frac{p_{c}}{p_{02}} = \left\lgroup\frac{2}{1.4 + 1} \right\rgroup^{\frac{1.4}{(1.4 – 1)}} &⇒ \frac{p_{c}}{p_{02}} = 0.528\\⇒ \quad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad \frac{p_{02}}{p_{c}}&= 1.89\\\text{Actual pressure ratio for this nozzle, }\frac{p_{02}}{p_{a}} = \frac{1.5}{1}&= 1.5\end{aligned}\\\)Since critical pressure ratio (1.89) is more than actual expansion pressure ratio (1.5) so it means that by pass air nozzle is not choked and \(p_{8}\) may be taken equal to \(p_{a}.\\\text{Using nozzle efficiency}\\\begin{aligned}T_{02^{′}} − T_{8^{′}}&= η_{nozzle} T_{02^{′}} \left[1 – \left\lgroup\frac{p_{a}}{p_{02}} \right\rgroup^{\frac{(γ – 1)}{γ} } \right] \\&= 0.95 × 328.96 \left[1 – \left\lgroup\frac{1}{15} \right\rgroup^{1/3.5} \right] \\T_{02^{′}} − T_{8^{′}}&= 34.22 K\\\text{Velocity at exit of nozzle,}\qquad &\\C_{8}&= \sqrt{\left[2 × 1.005 × 10^{3} × 34.22\right] } = 26.2 m/s\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Mass flow rate of bypass air, }M^{′}_{ b}&= \frac{m^{′}_{ a} B}{(B + 1)} = \frac{200 × 5.5}{(6.5)} = 169.23 kg/s\end{aligned}\\\text{Mass flow rate of hot gases, }m_{c^{′}} = 200 – 169.23 = 30.77 kg/s\\\text{Thrust available due to by pass air,}\\\begin{aligned}T_{b}&= m^{′}_{ b} × C_{8}\\&= 169.23 × 262.26\\T_{b}&= 44.382 kN\\\text{Applying energy balance on c}&\text{ombustion chamber we get,}\\\left\lgroup1 + \frac{m^{′}_{ f}}{m^{′}_{ c}} \right\rgroup C_{p_{gas}} T_{04}&= C_{p_{air}} ⋅ T_{03^{′}} + \frac{m^{′}_{ f}}{m^{′}_{ c}} × LCV\\\left\lgroup1 + \frac{m^{′}_{ f}}{m^{′}_{ c}} \right\rgroup × 1.14 × 1573&= (1.005 × 631.82) + \left\lgroup\frac{m^{′}_{ f}}{m^{′}_{ c}} \right\rgroup × 43100\\\frac{m^{′}_{ f}}{m^{′}_{ c}}&= 0.028\end{aligned}\\\)For HP turbine driving the HP compressor the work output from HP turbine may be equated to work required in HP compressor.\(\\\begin{aligned}\qquad\qquad\qquad C_{p_{air}}(T_{03^{′}} – T_{02^{′}})&= η_{mech}\left\lgroup 1 + \frac{m^{′}_{ f}}{m^{′}_{ c}} \right\rgroup × C_{p_{gas}}( T_{04} – T_{05^{′}})\\1.005 (631.82 – 328.96)&= 0.96 (1 + 0.028) × 1.14 × (1573 – T_{05^{′}})\\T_{05^{′}}&= 1302.45 K\end{aligned}\\\text{LP turbine is used for driving fan so, output of LP turbine goes for driving fan.}\\\begin{aligned}C_{p_{gas}} ⋅ η_{mech} (T_{05^{′}} – T_{06^{′}}) &= (1 + B) C_{p_{air}}(T_{02^{′}} – T_{01})\\⇒ 1.14 × 0.96 (1302.45 – T_{06^{′}})&= (1 + 55) × 1.005 (328.96 – 288)\\T_{06^{′}}&= 1057.96 K\end{aligned}\\\text{Pressure at inlet to HP turbine, }p_{04} = p_{03} – Δp_{loss} = 28 – 1.5 = 26.5 bar\\\text{Pressure at exit of LP turbine, }(p_{06})\text{ may be estimated as under,}\\\begin{aligned}p_{05}&= p_{04} \left\lgroup\frac{T_{05^{′}}}{T_{04}} \right\rgroup^{\left\lgroup\frac{n_{T}}{n_{T} – 1} \right\rgroup } = 26.5 \left\lgroup\frac{1302.45}{1573} \right\rgroup^{1/ 0.223} = 11.37 bar\\\text{and }\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad p_{06}&= p_{05} \left\lgroup\frac{T_{06^{′}}}{T_{05^{′}}} \right\rgroup^{\left\lgroup\frac{n_{T}}{n_{T} – 1} \right\rgroup } = 11.37 \left\lgroup\frac{1057.96}{1302.45} \right\rgroup^{1/ 0.223} = 4.476 bar\\\text{Critical expansion pressure}&\text{ ratio of nozzle handling hot gases,} \\&= \left\lgroup\frac{γ_{gas} + 1}{2} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{γ_{gas}}{(γ_{gas} – 1)} } = \left\lgroup\frac{2.33}{2} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{1.33}{0.33} }\\\text{Critical pressure ratio}&= 1.85\end{aligned}\\\)Since critical pressure ratio is less than expansion ratio in nozzle for hot gas so nozzle will be in choked state.\(\\\begin{aligned}\frac{p_{06}}{p_{a}}& > \text{Critical pressure ratio}\\\text{so, }\qquad \quad\qquad\qquad\qquad \frac{p_{06}}{p_{7}}&= 1.85\\\text{For exit nozzle ⇒ }\qquad\quad p_{7}&= 2.42 bar\\(T_{06^{′}} − T_{7^{′}} )&= T_{06^{′}} \left[1 – \left\lgroup\frac{p_{7}}{p_{06}} \right\rgroup^{\left\lgroup\frac{γ_{gas} – 1}{γ_{gas}} \right\rgroup} \right] \\&= 0.95 × 1057.96 \left[1 – \left\lgroup\frac{1}{1.85} \right\rgroup^{\left\lgroup\frac{0.33}{1.33} \right\rgroup} \right] \\(T_{06^{′}} − T_{7^{′}} )&= 142.28 K\\\text{Velocity of hot gases at exit }&\text{of nozzle,}\\C_{7}&= \sqrt{2 C_{p_{gas}} × (T_{06^{′}} − T_{7^{′}} )}\\C_{7}&= \sqrt{2 × 1.14 × 1000 × 142.28}\\C_{7}&= 569.55 m/s\\\text{Thrust due to hot gases}&= m_{c^{′}} × C_{7}\\&= 30.77 × 569.55 = 17.525 kN\\\text{Total thrust}&= \text{thrust due to bypass air + thrust due to hot gases}\\&= 44.382 + 17.525 = 61.91 kN\\\text{Specific thrust}&= \frac{61.91}{200} = 0.309 kN/kg/s\\\text{Specific fuel consumption}&= \frac{\left\lgroup\frac{m^{′}_{ f}}{m^{′}_{ c}} \right\rgroup × m^{′}_{ c} × 3600}{\text{Total thrust}}\\&= \frac{0.028 × 30.77 × 3600}{61.90 × 10^{3}} = 0.05 kg/h.N\end{aligned}\)
