Guest Question.

A car (weight \(W = mg\)) passes a bend (static friction coefficient \(\mu _{0} \) ), whose curvature \(1/\rho \) increases proportional to the covered distance s, i.e. \(s=A^{2} /\rho \) (clothoid). At time \(t_{0}=0\) , the car is at \(s_{0}=0\) and has an initial speed \(v_{0}\). At which speed \(v\) , where and when the car ‘skids off the bend’ if

a) it moves with constant speed,

b) it brakes with constant deceleration \(a_{0}\) ?

Given.: \(A = 35 m\) , \(\mu _{0} = 0.6\) , \(a _{0}=g/4\) , \(v_{0} = 72 km/h\) .

Admin
Solution

a) For \(a _{t}=0\) , Newton’s law yields with \(a_{n}=v^{2}_{0}/\rho \) the friction force

\(\swarrow :H=H_{n}= ma_{n}=m \frac{v^{2}_{0}}{\rho} \) .

The car leaves its path when the limit friction force is attained:

\(H=\mu_{0}mg \rightsquigarrow m\frac{v^{2}_{0} }{\rho _{1} } =\mu_{0}mg \rightsquigarrow \rho _{1}=\frac{v^{2}_{0} }{\mu_{0}g } \)

This leads with \(s=A^{2} /\rho = v_{0}t \) to (\(v_{1}=v_{0} \))

\(\underline{\underline{s_{1}=\frac{\mu_{0} gA^{2} }{\rho_{1} } =18 m } } \) , \(\underline{\underline{t_{1}=\frac{s_{1} }{v_{0} }=0.9 s } } \) .

b) When the motion is decelerated, an additional force acts in tangential direction. With \(a_{T}=-a_{0} \) follows

\(\searrow : ma_{T}=-m a_{0}=-H_{T}\) ,

\( \swarrow : m a_{0}=m\frac{v^{2} }{\rho } =H_{n} \) .

Static limit friction is attained for

\( H=\sqrt{H^{2}_{n}+H^{2}_{t} } =\mu_{0}mg \rightsquigarrow \sqrt{\left(\frac{mv^{2} }{\rho_{2} } \right)^{2}+\left(ma_{0} \right)^{2} } =\mu_{0} mg \)

 

\(\rightsquigarrow \frac{v^{2} }{\rho_{2} }=\sqrt{\mu^{2}_{0} g^{2} – a^{2}_{0} } \) .

Thus, with \(v=\sqrt{v^{2}_{0}-2a_{0}s } =v_{0}-a_{0}t \) (constant deceleration) and \(s=A^{2} /\rho\) , we obtain

\(\underline{\underline{s_{2} } }=\frac{v^{2}_{0} }{4a_{0} } \left(\pm \right)\sqrt{\left(\frac{v^{2}_{0} }{4a_{0} } \right)^{2}-\frac{A^{2} }{2a_{0} }\sqrt{\mu^{2}_{0} g^{2}-a^{2}_{0} } }= \underline{\underline{22.7 m} }\) ,

\(\underline{\underline{v_{2}} } = \sqrt{v^{2}_{0}-2a_{0}s_{2} } =\underline{\underline{61.2 km/h} } \) , \(\underline{\underline{t_{2}} }=\frac{v_{0}-v_{2} }{a_{0} }=\underline{\underline{1.22 s} } \) .
