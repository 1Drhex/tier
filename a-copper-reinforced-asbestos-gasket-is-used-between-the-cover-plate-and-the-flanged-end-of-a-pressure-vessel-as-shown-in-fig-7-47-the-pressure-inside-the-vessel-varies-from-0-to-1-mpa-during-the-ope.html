Guest Question.

A copper reinforced asbestos gasket is used between the cover plate and the flanged end of a pressure vessel as shown in Fig. 7.47. The pressure inside the vessel varies from 0 to 1 MPa during the operation. The gasket requires a seating pressure of 5 MPa to make the joint leakproof. The total number of bolts used is 8. The ratio of stiffness of the parts held together by the bolts to the stiffness of the bolts is 4. The effective sealing area may be taken up to the mean radius of the gasket. The factor of safety is to be 2 for the fluctuating load. The following data is given for the bolts:

Bolt material = 40Ni3
Ultimate tensile strength = 780 MPa
Yield point strength = 580 MPa
Endurance limit in bending = 260 MPa
Fatigue stress concentration factor = 3.0
Determine the size of the bolts.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } S_{u t}=780 N / mm ^{2} \quad S_{y t}=580 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( S_{e}^{\prime}=260 N / mm ^{2} \quad(f s)=2 \quad K_{f}=3 \quad k_{c}^{\prime}=4 k_{b}^{\prime} \).

For vessel, p = 0 to 1 MPa seating pressure of gasket = 5 MPa.

Step I Endurance limit stress for bolt

\( S_{e}^{\prime}=260 MPa \quad \text { or } \quad 260 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( K_{d}=\frac{1}{K_{f}}=\frac{1}{3} \).

\( S_{e}=K_{d} S_{e}^{\prime}=(1 / 3)(260)=86.67 N / mm ^{2} \).

Step II Construction of Goodman diagram
The seating pressure is 5 MPa (or 5 N/mm²).
Therefore, the initial preload on eight bolts is given by,
Preload = seating pressure × gasket area

\( =(5)\left[\frac{\pi}{4}\left(400^{2}-300^{2}\right)\right]=274889.36 N \).

\( P_{i}=\text { Preload per bolt }=\left(\frac{1}{8}\right)(274889.36) \).

= 34 361.17 N
The effective sealing area is up to the mean radius of the gasket. Therefore, the total external load is given by,

\( \text { External load }=\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right)(300+50)^{2}(1)=96211.28 N \).

\( \text { External load per bolt }=\left(\frac{1}{8}\right)(96211.28) \).

\( =12026.41 N \).

\( \left(\frac{k_{b}^{\prime}}{k_{b}^{\prime}+k_{c}^{\prime}}\right)=\left(\frac{k_{b}^{\prime}}{k_{b}^{\prime}+4 k_{b}^{\prime}}\right)=\frac{1}{5}=0.20 \).

From Eq. (7.23),

\( \Delta P=P\left(\frac{k_{b}^{\prime}}{k_{b}^{\prime}+k_{c}^{\prime}}\right) \) (7.23).

\( \Delta P=P\left(\frac{k_{b}^{\prime}}{k_{b}^{\prime}+k_{c}^{\prime}}\right)=0.20 P \).

From Eq. (7.24), the resultant load on the bolt,
i.e., \( \left(P_{b}\right) \) is given by,

\( P_{b}=P_{i}+\Delta P \) (7.24).

\( P_{b}=P_{i}+\Delta P=P_{i}+0.20 P \).

\( P_{\max }=P_{i}+(0.20) P \).

= 34 361.17 + (0.20)(12 026.41)
= 36 766.45 N.

\( P_{\min }=P_{i}+(0.20)(P) \).

= 34 361.17 + (0.20)(0) = 34 361.17 N.

\( P_{m}=\frac{1}{2}\left(P_{\max }+P_{\min }\right) \).

\( =\frac{1}{2}(36766.45+34361.17) \).

= 35 563.81 N.

\( P_{a}=\frac{1}{2}\left(P_{\max }-P_{\min }\right) \).

\( =\frac{1}{2}(36766.45-34361.17) \).

= 1202.64 N
The Goodman line for this example is shown in Fig. 7.48.

Step III Permissible stress amplitude
The co-ordinates of the point C \( \left(S_{m}, S_{a}\right) \) are obtained by solving the following two equations simultaneously:

\( \frac{S_{m}}{780}+\frac{S_{a}}{86.67}=1 \) (a).

\( S_{m}=\frac{P_{i}}{A}+S_{a}=\frac{34361.17}{A}+S_{a} \) (b).

where A is tensile stress area of the bolt.
The solution is obtained by Eq. (7.30).
From Eq. (7.30),

\( S_{a}=\frac{S_{u t}-\left(P_{i} / A\right)}{1+\left(S_{u t} / S_{e}\right)} \) (7.30).

\( S_{a}=\frac{S_{u t}-\left(P_{i} / A\right)}{1+\left(S_{u t} / S_{e}\right)}=\frac{780-(34361.17 / A)}{1+(780 / 86.67)} \).

\( =\frac{780-(34361.17 / A)}{10} \).

Step IV Size of bolt

\( \text { Since } \quad \sigma_{a}=\frac{S_{a}}{(f s)} \quad \therefore \frac{P_{a}}{A}=\frac{S_{a}}{(f s)} \).

\( \text { or } \frac{1202.64}{A}=\frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{780-(34361.17 / A)}{10}\right] \).

\( \text { or } \quad \frac{2920.7}{A}=39 \).

A = 74.89 mm²
From Table 7.2, bolts with fi ne threads M12 × 1.5 (A = 88.1 mm²) are suitable for this application.
