Guest Question.

Determine the shear flow distribution in the walls of the thin-walled closed section beam shown in Fig. 16.15; the wall thickness, t, is constant throughout.

Admin
Solution

Since the x axis is an axis of symmetry, \(I_{x y}=0\), and since \(S_{x}=0 \text {, }\) Eq. (16.15) reduces to

 

\(q_{s}=-\left(\frac{S_{x} I_{x x}-S_{y} I_{x y}}{I_{x x} I_{y y}-I_{x y}^{2}}\right) \int_{0}^{s} t x d s-\left(\frac{S_{y} I_{y y}-S_{x} I_{x y}}{I_{x x} I_{y y}-I_{x y}^{2}}\right) \int_{0}^{s} t y d s+q_{s, 0}\) (16.15)

 

\(q_{s}=-\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right) \int_{0}^{s} t y d s+q_{s, 0}\) (i)

 

where

 

\(I_{x x}=\left(\pi t r^{3} / 2\right)+2 \times 2 r t \times r^{2}+\left[t(2 r)^{3} / 12\right]=6.24 t r^{3}\)

 

(see Section 15.4.5) We now “cut” the beam section at 1. Any point may be chosen for the “cut” but the amount of computation will be reduced if a point is chosen which coincides with the axis of symmetry. Then

 

\(q_{ b , 12}=-\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right) \int_{0}^{\theta} \operatorname{tr} \sin \theta r d \theta\)

 

which gives

 

\(q_{ b , 12}=0.16\left(S_{y} / r\right)[\cos \theta]_{0}^{\theta}\)

 

so that

 

\(q_{ b , 12}=0.16\left(S_{y} / r\right)(\cos \theta-1)\) (ii)

 

When \(\theta=\pi / 2, q_{ b , 2}=-0.16\left(S_{y} / r\right)\). The \(q_{ b }\) shear flow in the wall 23 is then

 

\(q_{ b , 23}=-\left(S_{y} / I_{x x}\right) \int_{0}^{s_{1}} t r d s_{1}-0.16\left(S_{y} / r\right)\)

 

i.e.,

 

\(q_{ b , 23}=-0.16\left(S_{y} / r^{2}\right)\left(s_{1}+r\right)\) (iii)

 

and when \(s_{1}=2 r, q_{ b , 3}=-0.48\left(S_{y} / r\right)\). Then, in the wall 34

 

\(q_{ b , 34}=-0.16\left(S_{y} / t r^{3}\right) \int_{0}^{S_{2}} t\left(r-s_{2}\right) d s_{2}-0.48\left(S_{y} / r\right)\)

 

i.e.,

 

\(q_{ b , 34}=-0.16\left(S_{y} / r^{3}\right)\left(r s_{2}-0.5 s_{2}^{2}+3 r^{2}\right)\) (iv)

 

The remaining distribution follows from symmetry. Now taking moments about O and using Eq. (16.18)

 

\(0=\oint p q_{ b } d s+2 A q_{s, 0}\) (16.18)

 

\(0=2\left[\int_{0}^{\pi / 2} q_{ b , 12} r^{2} d \theta+\int_{0}^{2 r} q_{ b , 23} r d s_{1}+\int_{0}^{r} q_{ b , 34} 2 r d s_{2}\right]+2\left[4 r^{2}+\left(\pi r^{2} / 2\right)\right] q_{s, 0}\) (v)

 

Substituting in Eq. (v) for \(q_{ b , 12}\) etc from Eqs. (ii), (iii) and (iv) gives

 

\(q_{s, 0}=0.32 S_{y} / r\)

 

Adding \(q_{s, 0}\) to the \(q_{ b }\) distributions of Eqs. (ii), (iii) and (iv) gives

 

\(q_{12}=0.16\left(S_{y} / r^{3}\right)\left(r^{2} \cos \theta+r^{2}\right)\)

 

\(q_{23}=0.16\left(S_{y} / r^{3}\right)\left(r^{2}-r s_{1}\right)\)

 

\(q_{34}=0.16\left(S_{y} / r^{3}\right)\left(0.5 s_{2}^{2}-r s_{2}-r^{2}\right)\)

 

Note that \(q_{23}\) changes sign at \(s_{1}=r\). The shear flow distribution in the lower half of the section follows from symmetry and the complete distribution is shown in Fig. 16.16.
