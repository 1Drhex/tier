Guest Question.

A rigid body is subjected to three forces: \(F_{1}=F(-2,3,1)^{T} ,F_{2}=F(7,1,-4)^{T}, F_{3}=F(3,-1,-3)^{T}\).Their points of application are given by the position vectors \(r_{1} =a(4,3,2)^{T} , r_{2} =a(3,2,4)^{T}, r_{3} =a(3,5,0)^{T}\) .

Determine the resultant force R and the resultant moment \(M^{(A)}_{R}\) with respect to point A given by \(r_{A} =a(3,2,1)^{T}\) .

Admin
Solution

The components of the resultant force vector are obtained as the sum of the given force components:

\(R_{x} =\sum{F_{ix} } =(-2+7+3)F=8F\) ,

\(R_{y} =\sum{F_{iy} } =(3+1-1)F=3F\) ,

\(R_{z} =\sum{F_{iz} } =(1-4-3)F=-6F\) .

Hence, the resultant can be written as the row vector

\(\underline{\underline{R} } =(R_{x},R_{y},R_{z})^{T} =\underline{\underline{F(8,3,-6)^{T} } }\).

To determine the resultant moment about point A, we first write down the vectors \(r_{i} -r_{A}(i=1,2,3)\) from point A to the points of application of the forces \(F_{i}\).For example,

\(r_{1} -r_{A} =a(1,1,1)^{T}\) .

The moment of force \(F_{1}\) about point A is obtained from

\(M^{(A)}_{1} =(r_{1} -r_{A} )\times F_{1}\rightarrow M^{(A)}_{1}=aF(-2,-3,5)^{T}\) .

Similarly, we find

\(M^{(A)}_{2}=aF(-3,21,0)^{T} M^{(A)}_{3}=aF(-10,-3,-9)^{T}\) .

The moment vector \(M^{(A)}_{R}\) is the sum of the vectors \(M^{(A)}_{i}\):

\(M^{(A)}_{R}=\sum{M^{(A)}_{i}} \rightarrow \underline{\underline{M^{(A)}_{R}=aF(-15,15,-4)^{T}} }\).
