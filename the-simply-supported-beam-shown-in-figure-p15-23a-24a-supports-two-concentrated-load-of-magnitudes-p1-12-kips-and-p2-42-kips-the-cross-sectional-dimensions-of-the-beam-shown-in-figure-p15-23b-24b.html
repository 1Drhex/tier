Guest Question.

The simply supported beam shown in Figure P15.23a/24a supports two concentrated load of magnitudes \(P_{1}\) = 12 kips and \(P_{2}\) = 42 kips. The cross-sectional dimensions of the beam shown in Figure P15.23b/24b are \(b_{f}\) = 10.00 in., \(t_{f}\) = 0.65 in., d = 16.00 in., \(t_{w}\) = 0.40 in., and \(y_{H}\) = 6.00 in. Using a = 9 ft and \(x_{H}\) = 3 ft, determine the principal stresses and the maximum shear stress acting at point H. Show these stresses on an appropriate sketch.

Admin
Solution

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

\(\begin{array}{lcccccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Width } b \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Height } h \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( in .^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\( in .)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( in .^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( in .^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 10 & 0.65 & 0.229 & 7.675 & 382.887 & 383.115 \\\text { web } & 0.40 & 14.70 & 105.884 & 0.000 & 0.000 & 105.884 \\\text { bottom flange } & 10 & 0.65 & 0.229 & -7.675 & 382.887 & 383.115 \\\hline {\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left(\text { in. }^{4}\right)=} & 872.115\end{array}\)

 

Shear-force and bending-moment diagram:

Shear force at H:
V = 22.00 kips
Bending moment at H:
M = 66.00 kip-ft

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Bending stress at point H: (y = −1.50 in.)

\(\sigma_{x}=-\frac{M y}{I_{z}}=-\frac{(66.00 kip – ft )(-6.0 in. )(12 in . / ft )}{872.115 in .^{4}}=5.449 ksi\)

Transverse and horizontal shear stress at point H:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q=(10 in .)(0.65 in .)(7.675 in .)+(0.40 in .)(1.350 in .)(6.675 in .)=53.492 in .^{3} \\&\tau=\frac{V Q}{I t}=\frac{(22 kips )\left(53.492 in .^{3}\right)}{\left(872.115 in. ^{4}\right)(0.40 in .)}=3.373 ksi \quad \text { (Sense to be determined by inspection) }\end{aligned}\)

Stress element at H:

 

Principal stress calculations:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq. (12.12):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}}{2}\right)^{2}+\tau_{x y}^{2}} \\&=\frac{(5.449)+(0)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(5.449)-(0)}{2}\right)^{2}+(-3.373)^{2}} \\&=2.724 \pm 4.336\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1} &=7.06 ksi \text { and } \quad \sigma_{p 2}=-1.612 ksi \\\tau_{\max } &=4.34 ksi \text { (maximum in-plane shear stress) } \\\sigma_{\text {avg }} &=2.72 ksi ( T ) \quad \text { (normal stress on planes of maximum in-plane shear stress) }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan 2 \theta_{p} &=\frac{\tau_{x y}}{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) / 2}=\frac{-3.373}{[(5.449)-(0)] / 2}=\frac{-3.373}{2.724}=-1.2382 \\&\left.\therefore \theta_{p}=-25.54^{\circ} \quad \text { (clockwise from the } x \text { axis to the direction of } \sigma_{p 1}\right)\end{aligned}\)
