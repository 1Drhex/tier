Guest Question.

A W360 × 72 structural steel [E = 200 GPa] wide-flange shape is loaded and supported as shown in Fig. P11.65. The beam is supported at B by a timber [E = 12 GPa] post having a cross-sectional area of 20,000 \(mm ^{2}\) . After a uniformly distributed load of 50 kN/m is applied to the beam, determine:
(a) the reactions at supports A, B, and C.
(b) the maximum bending stress in the beam.
(c) the deflection of the beam at B.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{array}{ll} W 360 \times 72: \quad & I_{\text {beam }}=201 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4} \quad d=351 mm \quad E_{\text {beam }}=200,000 MPa \\& S_{\text {beam }}=1,150 \times 10^{3} mm ^{3}\end{array}\)

Post (1): \(A_{1}=20,000 mm ^{2} \quad E_{1}=12,000 MPa\)

 

(a) Reactions at supports A, B, and C.
The reaction force from post (1) will be taken as the redundant, leaving a simply supported beam as the released beam. To be consistent with earlier sign conventions (e.g., Chapter 5), we will assume that the force in the axial member is tension (even though intuitively we recognize that the post must be in compression).

 

 

Downward deflection of W360 × 72 beam at B due to 50 kN/m uniformly distributed load.
[Appendix C, SS beam with uniformly distributed load over a portion of the span.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{B}=-\frac{w a^{3}}{24 L E I}\left(4 L^{2}-7 a L+3 a^{2}\right)\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&P=50 kN / m , L=13 m , a=6 m ,\\&E I=40,200 kN – m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Calculation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{B} &=-\frac{w a^{3}}{24 L E I}\left(4 L^{2}-7 a L+3 a^{2}\right) \\&=-\frac{(50 kN / m )(6 m )^{3}}{24(13 m )\left(40,200 kN – m ^{2}\right)}\left[4(13 m )^{2}-7(6 m )(13 m )+3(6 m )^{2}\right]=-204.937 \times 10^{-3} m\end{aligned}\)

 

Since we are assuming that a tension force exists in post (1), the tension force from the post will
cause a downward deflection of W360 × 72 beam at B.
[Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated load not at midspan.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{B}=-\frac{P a b}{6 L E I}\left(L^{2}-a^{2}-b^{2}\right)\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&P=F_{1}, L=13 m , a=6 m , b=7 m, \\&E I=40,200 kN – m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Calculation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{B} &=-\frac{P a b}{6 L E I}\left(L^{2}-a^{2}-b^{2}\right) \\&=-\frac{\left(F_{1}\right)(6 m )(7 m )}{6(13 m )\left(40,200 kN – m ^{2}\right)}\left[(13 m )^{2}-(6 m )^{2}-(7 m )^{2}\right]=-\left(1.12514 \times 10^{-3} m / kN \right) F_{1}\end{aligned}\)

Deformation of wood post (1) due to force \(F _{1}\).

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}=\frac{(5 m )}{\left(20,000 mm ^{2}\right)\left(12,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)(1 kN / 1,000 N )} F_{1}=\left(20.8333 \times 10^{-6} m / kN \right) F_{1}\)

The deflection of the W360 × 72 beam at B will not equal zero in this instance because the post deforms. If we are consistent and assume that there is tension in the post, then the steel beam must deflect upward at B. Therefore,

\(v_{B}=\delta_{1}=\left(20.8333 \times 10^{-6} m / kN \right) F_{1}\)

 

Compatibility equation at B:
The sum of the downward deflection caused by the 50 kN/m uniformly distributed load and the downward deflection caused by the force in post (1) must equal the deformation of the wood post.

\(\begin{gathered}-204.937 \times 10^{-3} m -\left(1.12514 \times 10^{-3} m / kN \right) F_{1}=\left(20.8333 \times 10^{-6} m / kN \right) F_{1} \\\therefore F_{1}=\frac{204.937 \times 10^{-3} m }{-1.14598 \times 10^{-3} m / kN }=-178.832 kN =178.8 kN ( C )=B_{y}\end{gathered}\)

 

Equilibrium equations for entire beam:

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{A}=&-F_{1}(6 m )+C_{y}(13 m )-(50 kN / m )(6 m )(3 m )=0 \\& \therefore C_{y}=\frac{(50 kN / m )(6 m )(3 m )+(-178.832 kN )(6 m )}{13 m }=-13.3071 kN =13.31 kN \downarrow\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\Sigma F_{y}=A_{y}-F_{1}+C_{y}-(50 kN / m )(6 m )=\) 0

\(\begin{aligned}\therefore A_{y} &=(50 kN / m )(6 m )+(-178.832 kN )-(-13.3071 kN ) \\&=134.475 kN =134.5 kN \uparrow\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Determine maximum bending stress in the W360 × 72 beam:

Maximum bending moment magnitude
\(M_{\max }=180.836 kN – m\)

Bending stress at maximum moment

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\frac{(180.836 kN – m )(351 mm / 2)(1,000)^{2}}{201 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}} \\&=157.9 MPa\end{aligned}\)

or using the tabulated value for the section modulus of the W360 × 72 beam:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\frac{(180.836 kN – m )(1,000)^{2}}{1,150 \times 10^{3} mm ^{3}} \\&=157.2 MPa\end{aligned}\)

 

(c) Beam deflection at B.
The beam deflection at B is equal to the deformation of the wood post:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{B}=\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}} &=\frac{(-178.832 kN )(5 m )(1,000 N / kN )(1,000 mm / m )}{\left(20,000 mm ^{2}\right)\left(12,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)} \\&=-3.7257 mm =3.73 mm \downarrow\end{aligned}\)
