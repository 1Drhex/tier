Guest Question.

A three-phase balanced Y-connected load with series impedances of (6 + j24) Ω per phase and mutual impedance between any two phases of j3 Ω is supplied by a three-phase unbalanced source with line-to-neutral voltages of \(V_{a n}=200 \angle 25^{\circ}, V_{b n}=100 \angle-155^{\circ}, V_{c n}=80 \angle 100^{\circ} V .\) The load and source neutrals are both solidly grounded. Determine: (a) the load sequence impedance matrix, (b) the symmetrical components of the line-to-neutral voltages, (c) the symmetrical components of the load currents, and (d) the load currents.

Admin
Solution

(a) \(\bar{Z}_{s}=A^{-1} \bar{Z}_{p} A ;A=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a^{2} & a \\1 & a & a^{2}\end{array}\right] ; A^{-1}=\frac{1}{3}\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a & a^{2} \\1 & a^{2} & a\end{array}\right]\)

The load sequence impedance matrix comes out as

\(\bar{Z}_{s}=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}6+j 30 & 0 & 0 \\0 & 6+j 21 & 0 \\0 & 0 & 6+j 21\end{array}\right] \Omega\)

see the result of

 

(b) \(\bar{V}_{p}=\left[\begin{array}{l}200 \angle 25^{\circ} \\100 \angle-155^{\circ} \\80 \angle 100^{\circ}\end{array}\right] ; \bar{V}_{s}=A^{-1} \bar{V}_{p} ; A^{-1}=\frac{1}{3}\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a & a^{2} \\1 & a^{2} & a\end{array}\right]\)

Symmetrical components of the line-to-neutral voltages are given by:

\(\bar{V}_{0}=47.7739 \angle 57.6268^{\circ} ; \bar{V}_{1}=112.7841 \angle-0.0331^{\circ} ; \bar{V}_{2}=61.6231 \angle 45.8825^{\circ} V\)

 

(c) \(\bar{V}_{s}=\bar{Z}_{s} \bar{I}_{s} ; \bar{I}_{s}=\bar{Z}_{s}^{-1} \bar{V}_{s}\) , which results in

\(\bar{I}_{0}=1.562 \angle-21.06^{\circ} ; \bar{I}_{1}=5.164 \angle-74.0877^{\circ} ; \bar{I}_{2}=2.822 \angle-28.1722^{\circ} A\)

 

(d) \(\bar{I}_{p}=A \bar{I}_{s} ; A=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a^{2} & a \\1 & a & a^{2}\end{array}\right]\)

The result is:

\(\bar{I}_{a}=8.706 \angle-51.99^{\circ} ; \bar{I}_{b}=5.062 \angle 136^{\circ} ; \bar{I}_{c}=3.129 \angle 32.04^{\circ} A\)
