Guest Question.

A W8 × 48 structural steel [E = 29,000 ksi] section (see Appendix B for cross-sectional properties) is used as a column with an actual length of L = 27 ft. The column is supported only at its ends and it may buckle in any direction. The column is fixed at its base and pinned at its upper end. Determine the maximum load P that may be supported by the column if a factor of safety of 2.5 with respect to buckling is specified.

Admin
Solution

The following section properties for a standard steel W8 × 48 shape are given in Appendix B:

\(A=14.1 \text { in. }^{2}, I_{x}=184 \text { in. }^{4}, I_{y}=60.9 \text { in. }^{4} \)

Maximum allowable column load:

\(\begin{aligned}&P_{c r}=\frac{\pi^{2} E I}{(K L)^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(29,000 ksi )\left(60.9 in. ^{4}\right)}{[(0.7)(27 ft )(12 in . / ft )]^{2}}=338.867 \text { kips } \\&P_{\text {allow }}=\frac{338.867 kips }{2.5}=135.547 kips =135.5 kips\end{aligned}\)
