Guest Question.

A bolted assembly is subjected to an external force, which varies from 0 to 10 kN. The combined stiffness of the parts, held together by the bolt, is three times the stiffness of the bolt. The bolt is initially so tightened that at 50% overload condition, the parts held together by the bolt are just about to separate. The bolt is made of plain carbon steel 5OC4 \( \left(S_{u t}=660 N / mm ^{2}\right. \) and \( \left.S_{y t}=460 N / mm ^{2}\right) \). The fatigue stress concentration factor is 2.2 and the expected reliability is 90%. The factor of safety is 2. Determine the size of the bolt with fi ne threads.

Admin
Solution

Given P = 0 to 10 kN (fs) = 2

\( \text { For bolts, } \quad S_{u t}=660 N / mm ^{2} \quad S_{y t}=460 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( K_{f}=2.2 \quad R=90 \% \quad k_{c}^{\prime}=3 k_{b}^{\prime} \).

Step I Endurance limit stress for bolt

\( S_{e}^{\prime}=0.5 S_{u t}=0.5(660)=330 N / mm ^{2} \).

The surface finish factor is incorporated in the fatigue stress concentration factor. The size factor is 1 for axial load.
For 90% reliability,

\( K_{c}=0.897 \).

\( K_{d}=\frac{1}{K_{f}}=\frac{1}{2.2}=0.4545 \).

\( S_{e}=K_{b} K_{c} K_{d} S_{e}^{\prime}=(1.0)(0.897)(0.4545)(330) \).

= 134.54 N/mm².

Step II Construction of Goodman diagram
At 50% overload condition, the external force will be (1.5 × 10) or 15 kN. From Eq. (7.25),

\( \left(P_{b}\right)_{\max .}=P_{i}\left(\frac{k_{b}^{\prime}+k_{c}^{\prime}}{k_{c}^{\prime}}\right) \) (7.25).

\( \left(P_{b}\right)_{\max .}=P_{i}\left(\frac{k_{b}^{\prime}+k_{c}^{\prime}}{k_{c}^{\prime}}\right) \).

\( \text { or }\left(15 \times 10^{3}\right)=P_{i}\left(\frac{k_{b}^{\prime}+3 k_{b}^{\prime}}{3 k_{b}^{\prime}}\right) \).

\( P_{i}=11250 N \).

Similarly,

\( \left(\frac{k_{b}^{\prime}}{k_{b}^{\prime}+k_{c}^{\prime}}\right)=\left(\frac{k_{b}^{\prime}}{k_{b}^{\prime}+3 k_{b}^{\prime}}\right)=\frac{1}{4}=0.25 \).

The maximum and minimum forces in the bolt are given by

\( P_{\max }=P_{i}+(0.25) P \).

= 11 250 + (0.25)(10 000) = 13 750 N.

\( P_{\min }=P_{i}+(0.25)(0) \).

= 11 250 + (0.25)(0) = 11 250 N.

\( P_{m}=1 / 2\left(P_{\max }+P_{\min }\right) \).

\( =1 / 2(13750+11250)=12250 N \).

\( P_{a}=1 / 2\left(P_{\max }-P_{\min }\right) \).

\( =1 / 2(13750-11250)=1250 N \).

The Goodman diagram for this example is shown in Fig. 7.40.

Step III Permissible stress amplitude
The co-ordinates of the point C \( \left(S_{m}, S_{a}\right) \) are obtained by solving the following two equations simultaneously:

\( \frac{S_{m}}{660}+\frac{S_{a}}{134.54}=1 \) (a).

\( S_{m}=\frac{P_{i}}{A}+S_{a}=\frac{11250}{A}+S_{a} \) (b).

where A is the tensile stress area of the bolt.
The solution is obtained by Eq. (7.30).
From Eq. (7.30),

\( S_{a}=\frac{S_{u t}-\left(P_{i} / A\right)}{1+\left(S_{u t} / S_{e}\right)} \) (7.30).

\( S_{a}=\frac{S_{u t}-\left(P_{i} / A\right)}{1+\left(S_{u t} / S_{e}\right)} \).

\( =\frac{660-(11250 / A)}{1+(660 / 134.54)}=\frac{660-(11250 / A)}{5.9} \).

Step IV Size of bolt
Since \( \sigma_{a}=\frac{S_{a}}{(f s)} \quad \therefore \frac{P_{a}}{A}=\frac{S_{a}}{(f s)} \).

\( \text { or } \quad \frac{1250}{A}=\frac{660-(11250 / A)}{5.9(2)} \).

\( \frac{1250}{A}=55.93-\frac{953.39}{A} \text { or } \frac{2203.39}{A}=55.93 \).

A = 39.4 mm²
From Table 7.2, a bolt with fi ne threads M8 × 1 (A = 39.2 mm²2) is suitable for this application.
