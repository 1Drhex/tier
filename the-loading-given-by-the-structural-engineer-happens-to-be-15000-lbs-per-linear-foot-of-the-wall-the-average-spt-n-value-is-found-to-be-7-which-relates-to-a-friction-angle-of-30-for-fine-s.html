Guest Question.

The loading given by the structural engineer happens to be 15,000 lbs per linear foot of the wall. The average SPT (N) value is found to be 7, which relates to a friction angle of \(30^{\circ}\) for fine sands. The groundwater is 2 ft. below the ground surface. The bottom of the footing is placed 3 ft. below the ground surface to provide protection against frost (Figure 8.24). Compute the bearing capacity of a 4 ft. wide strip footing.

Admin
Solution

Step 1: In this case, the groundwater is above the bottom of the footing.
Hence \(\gamma_{e} = \gamma^{\prime }.\)

Step 2: Terzaghi’s bearing capacity equation:
Ultimate bearing capacity \((q_{ult}) = cN_{c}s_{c} + q^{\prime }N_{q} + 0.5 \gamma^{\prime }BN_{\gamma}s_{\gamma}\)
c = cohesion (zero for sands)
\(\varphi = 30^{\circ}\) for \(N = 7,\) for fine sands. (Table 2.2)

Soil type SPT (\(N_{70}\) value) Consistency Friction angle \((\varphi ^{\prime })\) Relative density \((D_{r})\)

3–6 Loose 28–30 0.15–0.35
7–15 Medium 30–33 0.35–0.65
16–30 Dense 33–38 0.65–0.85
\(\lt 30\) Very dense \(\lt 38\) \(\lt 0.85\)
Medium sand 2–3 Very loose 27–30 0–0.15
4–7 Loose 30–32 0.15–0.35
8–20 Medium 32–36 0.35–0.65
21–40 Dense 36–42 0.65–0.85
\(\lt 40\) Very dense \(\lt 42 \) \(\lt 0.85\)

5–9 Loose 30–33 0.15–0.35

\(N_{c}, N_{q},\) and \(N_{\gamma} =\) Terzaghi’s bearing capacity factors. For \(\varphi = 30^{\circ},\)
Obtain \(N_{c} = 37.2, N_{q} = 22.5,\) and \(N_{\gamma} = 19.7.\) \(\gamma^{\prime } =\) buoyant soil density

\(\gamma^{\prime }\) is taken to be equal to \(\gamma^{\prime } = \gamma − \gamma_{w}\)

(\(\gamma_{w} =\) density of water).

Step 3:

• The first term \((cN_{c}S_{c} )\) in the Terzaghi’s bearing capacity equation does not change due to the groundwater elevation. (For sands, “c” (cohesion) is zero).
• The second term \(( q^{\prime }N_{q})\) will change depending on the elevation of the groundwater. \(q^{\prime }\) is de-fined as the effective stress at the bottom of the footing.

\(q^{\prime } = (110) \times 2 + (110 – 62.4) \times 1 = 267.6\) psf
\(q^{\prime }N_{q} = 267.6 \times 22.5 = 6021\) psf

The term \((110 \times 2)\) gives the effective stress due to soil above the groundwater level. The total density \((\gamma)\) is used to compute the effective stress above the groundwater level. The next term \([(110 − 62.4) \times 1]\) gives the effective stress due to soil below the groundwater table. Buoyant density \((\gamma^{\prime })\) is used to compute the effective stress below the groundwater level.

Step 4: \(\gamma_{e}\) is given by

\(\gamma _{e}=\frac{(2H-d_{w})}{H^{2}}\times d_{w}\times \gamma _{total}+\gamma ^{\prime }\times \frac{(H-d_{w})^{2}}{H^{2}}\)

When groundwater is above the bottom of the footing, \(\gamma _{e} = \gamma^{\prime }\)

\(\gamma _{e} =(110 – 62.4) 47.6\) psf

Apply the Terzaghi’s bearing capacity equation.
Ultimate bearing capacity \((q_{ult}) = cN_{c}S_{c} + q^{\prime }N_{q} + 0.5 \gamma ^{\prime }BN_{\gamma}S_{\gamma}\)

Ultimate bearing capacity \((q_{ult})=0 + 6,021 + 0.5 \times 47.6 \times 4 \times 19.7 \times 1\)

\( = 7896\) psf

Allowable bearing capacity = ultimate bearing capacity/factor of safety

\( =7896/3.0 = 2632\) psf
