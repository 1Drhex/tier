Guest Question.

A steel ball weighing 2 Ib is dropped from a height of 3000 ft with no velocity. As it falls, the ball encounters air resistance numerically equal to v/8 (in pounds), where v denotes the velocity of the ball (in feet per second). Find (a) the limiting velocity for the ball and (b) the time required for the ball to hit the ground.

Admin
Solution

Locate the coordinate system as in Fig. 7-5 with the ground now situated at x = 3000. Here w = 2 Ib and k= 1/8. Assuming gravity g is 32 \(ft / sec ^{32}\), we have from the formula w = mg that 2 = m(32) or that the mass of the ball is m= 1/16 slug. Equation (7.4) becomes

\(\frac{d v}{d t}+2 v=32\)

which has as its solution

\(v(t)=c e^{-2 t}+16\) (1)

At t= 0, we are given that v = 0. Substituting these values into (1), we obtain

\(0=c e^{-2(0)}+16=c+16\)

from which we conclude that c = -16 and (1) becomes

\(v(t)=-16 e^{-2 t}+16\) (2)

(a) From (1) or (2), we see that as \(t \rightarrow \infty, v \rightarrow 16\) so the limiting velocity is \(16 ft / sec ^{2} .\)

(b) To find the time it takes for the ball to hit the ground (x = 3000), we need an expression for the position of the ball at any time t. Since v = dx/dt, (2) can be rewritten as

\(\frac{d x}{d t}=-16 e^{-2 t}+16\)

Integrating both sides of this last equation directly with respect to t, we have

\(x(t)=8 e^{-2 t}+16 t+c_{1}\) (3)

where \(c_{1}\) denotes a constant of integration. At t = 0, x = 0. Substituting these values into (3), we obtain

\(0=8 e^{-2(0)}+16(0)+c_{1}=8+c_{1}\)

from which we conclude that \(c_{1}\) = -8 and (3) becomes

\(x(t)=8 e^{-2 t}+16 t-8\) (4)

The ball hits the ground when x(t) = 3000. Substituting this value into (4), we have

\(3000=8 e^{-2 t}+16 t-8\)

or \(376=e^{-2 t}+2 t\) (5)

Although (5) cannot be solved explicitly for t, we can approximate the solution by trial and error, substituting different values of t into (5) until we locate a solution to the degree of accuracy we need. Alternatively, we note that for any large value of t, the negative exponential term will be negligible. A good approximation is obtained by setting 2t = 376 or t = 188 sec. For this value of t, the exponential is essentially zero.
