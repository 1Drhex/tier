Guest Question.

A shaft has three eccentrics of mass 1 kg each. The central plane of the eccentrics is 50 mm apart. The distances of the centers from the axis of rotation are 20, 30 and 20 mm and their angular positions are 120° apart. Find the amount of out-of-balance force and couple at 600 rpm. If the shaft is balanced by adding two masses at a radius of 70 mm and at a distance of 100 mm from the central plane of the middle eccentric, find the amount of the masses and their angular positions.

From Table 12.5, we have

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}=-0.005 \).

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}=0.00866 \).

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}=-0.005 \).

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}=0 \).

\( \left[\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}\right)^{2}+\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5}=M M r_{M} l_{M} \).

\( \left[(-0.002)^{2}+(0)^{2}\right]^{0.5}=0.014 M_{M} \).

\( M_{M}=\frac{0.002}{0.014}=0.1428 kg \).

\( \tan \theta_{M}=\frac{-\sum M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}}{-\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}}=\frac{-(0)}{-(-0.002)}=0 \).

\( \theta_{M}=0^{\circ} \text { or } 360^{\circ} \).

\( \text { Since the numerator is negative and denominator is positive, therefore } \theta_{M} \text { lies in the fourth quadrant. } \)

\( M_{L} r_{L}=\left[\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}+M_{M} r_{M} \cos \theta_{M}\right)^{2}+\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}+M_{M} r_{M} \sin \theta_{M}\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5} \).

\( 0.07 M_{L}=\left[\left(-0.005+0.07 \times 0.1428 \times \cos 0^{\circ}\right)^{2}+\left(0.00866+0.07 \times 0.1428 \times \sin 0^{\circ}\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5} \).

\( =\left[(0.005)^{2}+(0.00866)^{2}\right]^{0.5}=0.01 \).

\( M_{L}=0.1428 kg \).

\( \tan \theta_{L}=\frac{-\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}+M_{M} r_{M} \sin \theta_{M}\right)}{-\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}+M_{M} r_{M} \cos \theta_{M}\right)} \).

\( =\frac{-0.00866}{-0.005}=1.732, \theta_{L}=240^{\circ} \).

\( \text { Since the numerator and denominator are both negative, therefore } \theta_{L} \text { lies in the third quadrant. } \)

Graphical Method

Out-of-balance force: The out-of-balance force is obtained by drawing the force polygon, as shown in Fig.12.8(c), drawn from the data in column 4 of the Table 12.5.
The resultant oc represents the out-of-balance force.

\( \text { Out-of-balance force }=\text { vector } o c \times \omega^{2}=0.01 \times\left(2 \pi \times \frac{600}{60}\right)^{2} \).

\( =39.48 N \).

Out-of-balance couple: The out-of-balance couple is obtained by drawing the couple polygon from the data in column 9, as shown in Fig.12.8(d).

\( \text { Out-of-balance couple }=o^{\prime} c^{\prime} \times \omega^{2}=0.002\left(2 \pi \times \frac{600}{60}\right)^{2} \).

\( =7.9 Nm \).
