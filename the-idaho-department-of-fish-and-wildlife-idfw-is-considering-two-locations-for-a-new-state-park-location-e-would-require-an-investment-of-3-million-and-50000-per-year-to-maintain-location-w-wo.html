Guest Question.

The Idaho Department of Fish and Wildlife \((IDFW)\) is considering two locations for a new state park. Location \(E\) would require an investment of $\(3\) million and $\(50,000\) per year to maintain. Location \(W\) would cost $\(7\) million to construct, but the \(IDFW\) would receive an additional $\(25,000\) per year in park fees. The operating cost of location \(W\) will be $\(65,000\) per year. The revenue to park concessionaires will be $\(500,000\) per year at location \(E\) and $\(700,000\) at location \(W\). The disbenefits associated with each location are $\(30,000\) per year for location \(E\) and $\(40,000\) per year for location \(W\). Assume the park will be maintained indefinitely. Use an interest rate of \(12\)% per year to determine which location, if either, should be selected on the basis of \(( a )\) the \(B/C\) method and \(( b )\) the modified \(B/C\) method.

Admin
Solution

\((a)\) Revenue alternatives; compare location \(E\) to \(DN\)

Location \(E\)

 

\(AW\) of \(C = 3,000,000(0.12) + 50,000\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad =\) $\(410,000\)

 

Revenue \(= B =\) $\(500,000\) per year

 

Disbenefits \(= D =\) $\(30,000\) per year

 

Location \(W\)

 

\(AW\) of \(C = 7,000,000 (0.12) + 65,000 – 25,000\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad =\) $\(880,000\)

 

Revenue \(= B =\) $\(700,000\) per year

 

Disbenefits \(= D =\) $\(40,000\) per year

 

\(B/C\) ratio for location \(E:\)

 

\((B – D)/C = (500,000 – 30,000)/410,000\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad = 1.15\)

 

Location \(E\) is economically justified. \(W\) is now incrementally compared to \(E.\)

\(W\) vs. \(E: \Delta C = 880,000 – 410,000\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad \quad =\) $\(470,000\)

 

\(\Delta B = 700,000 – 500,000\)

 

\(\quad \quad =\) $\(200,000\)

 

\(\Delta D = 40,000 – 30,000\)

 

\(\quad \quad =\) $\(10,000\)

 

\(\Delta (B-D)/C = (200,000 – 10,000)/470,000\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad \quad = 0.40\)

 

Since \(\Delta (B – D)/C < 1, W\) is not justified; select location \(E.\)

 

\((b)\) Location \(E\)

 

\(B = 500,000 – 30,000 – 50,000 =\) $\(420,000\)

 

\(C = 3,000,000 (0.12) =\) $\(360,000\)

 

Modified \(B/C = 420,000/360,000 = 1.17\)

 

Location \(E\) is justified. Incrementally compare \(W\) to \(E\).

 

\(W\) vs. \(E: \Delta B =\) $\(200,000\)

 

\(\Delta D = \)$\(10,000\)

 

\(\Delta\) initial cost \(= (7 million – 3 million)(0.12)\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad \quad\quad =\) $\(480,000\)

 

\(\Delta\) operating costs \(= (65,000 – 25,000) – 50,000\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad =\) $\(-10,000\)

 

Note that operating cost is now an incremental advantage for \(W\).

 

Modified \(\Delta B/C = \frac { 200000-10000-\left( -10000 \right) }{ 480000 } =0.42\)

\(W\) is not justified; select location \(E.\)
