Guest Question.

The horizontal boom OC, which is supported by a ball-and-socket joint and two cables, carries the vertical force P = 8000 N. Calculate TAD and TCE, the tensions in the cables, and the components of the force exerted on the boom by the joint at O. Use the given FBD (the weight of the boom is negligible).

Admin
Solution

Method of Analysis

As shown in the FBD, the force system acting on the boom is the special case in which all forces intersect an axis, namely, the y-axis. Therefore, there are five independent equilibrium equations. Because there are also five unknowns in the FBD (\(T_{AD}\), \(T_{CE}\), \(O_{x}\) , \(O_{y}\) , and \(O_{z}\) ), the problem is statically determinate.

Consider the moment equations about the x- and z-axes (\(ΣM_{y}\) = 0 is trivially satisfied):

\(ΣM_{x}\) = 0—contains the unknowns \(T_{AD}\) and \(T_{CE}\)

 

\(ΣM_{z}\) = 0—contains the unknowns \(T_{AD}\) and \(T_{CE}\)

These two equations can be solved simultaneously for \(T_{AD}\) and \(T_{CE}\). After these tensions have been found, the reactions at O can be determined using the force equations of equilibrium.

Mathematical Details

A convenient method of writing \(ΣM_{x}\) = 0 and \(ΣM_{z}\) = 0 is to sum the moments about point O using vector representation (recall that \(M_{O} = M_{x}i + M_{y}j + M_{z}k\)). Referring to the FBD, we have

\(ΣM_{o}\) = \((r_{OA} × T_{AD}) + (r_{OC} × T_{CE}) + (r_{OB} × P)\) = 0

where

\(r_{OA}\) = 3j m \(r_{OC}\) = 6j m \(r_{OB}\) = 5j m

 

\(T_{AD}=T_{AD}\lambda _{AD}=T_{AD}\frac{\overrightarrow{AD} }{\left|\overrightarrow{AD} \right| }=T_{AD}\left(\frac{2.5i − 3j + 3k}{4.924} \right)\)

= \(T_{AD}\)(0.5077i − 0.6093j + 0.6093k)

 

\(T_{CE}=T_{CE}\lambda _{CE}=T_{CE}\frac{\overrightarrow{CE} }{\left|\overrightarrow{CE} \right| }=T_{CE}\left(\frac{−3.5i − 6j + 3k}{7.566} \right)\)

= \(T_{CE}\)(−0.4626i − 0.7930j + 0.3965k)

P = −8000k N

Using the determinant form for the cross products, we have

\(\Sigma M_{O}=T_{AD}\left|\begin{matrix} i & j & k \\ 0 & 3 & 0 \\ 0.5077 & −0.6093 & 0.6093 \end{matrix} \right|\)

+\(T_{CE}\left|\begin{matrix} i & j & k \\ 0 & 6 & 0 \\ −0.4626 & −0.7930 & 0.3965 \end{matrix} \right|\)

+\(\left|\begin{matrix} i & j & k \\ 0 & 5 & 0 \\0 & 0 & −8000 \end{matrix} \right|=0\)

Expanding the determinants and equating the x- and z-components (the ycomponents are identically zero, as expected), we get

\(\Sigma M_{x}=0\) 1.828\(T_{AD}\) + 2.379\(T_{CE}\) − 40 000 = 0

\(\Sigma M_{z}=0\) −1.523\(T_{AD}\) + 2.776\(T_{CE}\) = 0

from which we find

\(T_{AD}\) = 12 770 N \(T_{CE}\) = 7010 N

After the tensions have been computed, the reactions at O can be calculated by using the force equation ΣF = 0:

\(ΣF_{x}\) = 0 \(O_{x} + 0.5077T_{AD} − 0.4626T_{CE}\) = 0

 

\(ΣF_{y}\) = 0 \(O_{y} − 0.6093T_{AD} − 0.7930T_{CE}\) = 0

 

\(ΣF_{z}\)=0 \(O_{z} + 0.6093T_{AD} + 0.3965T_{CE}\) − 8000 = 0

Substituting the previously found values for \(T_{AD}\) and \(T_{CE}\), we obtain

\(O_{x}\) = −3240 N \(O_{y}\) = 13 340 N \(O_{z}\) = −2560 N

The negative values for \(O_{x}\) and \(O_{z}\) indicate that the directions of these components are opposite to the directions shown in the FBD.
