Calculation of Composition in VLLE

A binary mixture exhibits vapor–liquid–liquid equilibrium at 300 K. The excess Gibbs energy is described by the two-suffix Margules equation with A = 6235 [J/mol]. Determine the composition of the three phases and the total pressure. The saturation pressures are given by \(P_{a}^{\text {sat }}=100[ kPa ] \text { and } P_{b}^{\text {sat }}=50[ kPa ]\).

Admin
Solution

We begin by solving for the composition of the two liquid phases at 300 K. We can use the approach of either or . Using the former, we equate the fugacities of a and b in the liquid phases. In analogy to Equations (E8.19C) and (E8.19D), equating the liquidphase fugacities and applying the two-suffix Margules equation gives:

 

\(x_{a}^{\alpha} \exp \left[\frac{A}{R T}\left(1-x_{a}^{\alpha}\right)^{2}\right]=x_{a}^{\beta} \exp \left[\frac{A}{R T}\left(1-x_{a}^{\beta}\right)^{2}\right]\) (E8.22A)

 

and,

 

\(\left(1-x_{a}^{\alpha}\right) \exp \left[\frac{A}{R T}\left(x_{a}^{\alpha}\right)^{2}\right]=\left(1-x_{a}^{\beta}\right) \exp \left[\frac{A}{R T}\left(x_{a}^{\beta}\right)^{2}\right]\) (E8.22B)

 

Since we know the parameter A, Equations (E8.22A) and (E8.22B) represent two equations with two unknowns, \(x_{a}^{\alpha} \text { and } x_{a}^{\beta}\). Again, it helps to have an idea where the solution to these nonlinear equations lies, so that we do not obtain the trivial answer \(x_{a}^{\alpha}=x_{a}^{\beta}\). Figure E8.22 plots the quantities \(x_{a} \gamma_{a} \text { and }\left(1-x_{a}\right) \gamma_{b} \text { vs. } x_{a}\). The solution occurs at the two compositions of \(x_{a}\) where Equations (E8.22A) and (E8.22B) are simultaneously satisfied. In this case, both solutions \(x_{a} \gamma_{a} \text { and }\left(1-x_{a}\right) \gamma_{b}\) occurs at the same value, as is indicated in the figure.

In Figure E8.22, we can see that the plot \(x_{a} \gamma_{a}\) is a mirror image of \(\left(1-x_{a}\right) \gamma_{b}\). This result is not surprising, since the activity coefficient model is symmetric. We can use this feature to formulate an alternative solution to Equations (E8.21A) and (E8.21B). Since the activity coefficients are symmetric, inspection of Figure E8.22 shows that we must have \(x_{a}^{\alpha}=1-x_{a}^{\beta}\). Thus Equation (E8.22A) becomes:

 

\(x_{a}^{\alpha} \exp \left[\frac{A}{R T}\left(1-x_{a}^{\alpha}\right)^{2}\right]=\left(1-x_{a}^{\alpha}\right) \exp \left[\frac{A}{R T}\left(x_{a}^{\alpha}\right)^{2}\right]\) (E8.22C)

 

Rewriting Equation (E8.22C) gives:

 

\(\ln \left(\frac{x_{a}^{\alpha}}{1-x_{a}^{\alpha}}\right)=\frac{A}{R T}\left(2 x_{a}^{\alpha}-1\right)\) (E8.22D)

 

Equations (E8.22C) and (E8.22D) are valid only for the symmetric two-suffix Margules equation. Solving either Equation (E8.22D) or Equations (E8.22A) and (E8.22B):

 

\(x_{a}^{\alpha}=0.855 \quad \text { and } \quad x_{a}^{\beta}=0.145\)

 

To find the pressure, we add Equations (8.35) and (8.36):

 

\(y_{a} P=x_{a}^{\alpha} \exp \left[\frac{A}{R T}\left(x_{b}^{\alpha}\right)^{2}\right] P_{a}^{\text {sat }}=x_{a}^{\beta} \exp \left[\frac{A}{R T}\left(x_{b}^{\beta}\right)^{2}\right] P_{a}^{\text {sat }}\) (8.35)

 

\(y_{b} P=x_{b}^{\alpha} \exp \left[\frac{A}{R T}\left(x_{a}^{\alpha}\right)^{2}\right] P_{b}^{\text {sat }}=x_{b}^{\beta} \exp \left[\frac{A}{R T}\left(x_{a}^{\beta}\right)^{2}\right] P_{b}^{\text {sat }}\) (8.36)

 

\(P=x_{a}^{\alpha} \exp \left[\frac{A}{R T}\left(x_{b}^{\alpha}\right)^{2}\right]_{P_{a}^{\text {sat }}}+x_{b}^{\alpha} \exp \left[\frac{A}{R T}\left(x_{a}^{\alpha}\right)^{2}\right]_{P_{b}^{\text {sat }}}=135[ kpa ]\)

 

Finally, solving Equation (8.35), we get:

 

\(y_{a}=\frac{x_{a}^{\alpha} \exp \left[\frac{A}{R T}\left(x_{b}^{\alpha}\right)^{2}\right] P_{a}^{\text {sat }}}{P}=0.667\)
