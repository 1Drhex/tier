Guest Question.

Compute the compressive strength of a WT12 \(\times \)81 of A992 steel. The effective length with respect to the x-axis is 25 feet 6 inches, the effective length with respect to the y-axis is 20 feet, and the effective length with respect to the z-axis is 20 feet.

Admin
Solution

First, compute the flexural buckling strength for the x-axis (the axis of no symmetry):
\(\frac{L_{cx}}{r_{x}}=\frac{25.5 \times12 }{3.50}=87.43\)

 

\(F_{e}=\frac{\pi ^{2}E}{(L_{c}/r)^{2}}=\frac{\pi ^{2}(29,000)}{(87.43)^{2}}=37.44ksi\)

 

4.71\(\sqrt{\frac{E}{F_{y}}}=4.71\sqrt{\frac{29,000}{50}}=113\)

 

Since \(\frac{L_{cx}}{r_{x}} < 4.71\sqrt{\frac{E}{F_{y}}}\), AISC Equation E3-2 applies:

 

\(F_{cr}= 0.658^{(F_{y}/F_{e})}F_{y}= 0.658^{(50/37.44)}(50) = 28.59 ksi\)
The nominal flexural buckling strength is

\(P_{n} = F_{cr} A_{g} = 28.59(23.9) = 683.3 kips\)
Compute the flexural-torsional buckling strength about the y-axis (the axis of symmetry):

\(\frac{L_{cy}}{r_{y}}=\frac{20 \times 12}{3.05}=78.69\)

 

\(F_{ey}=\frac{\pi ^{2}E}{(L_{cy}/r_{y})^{2}}=\frac{\pi ^{2}(29,000)}{(78.69)^{2}}=37.46.22ksi\)
From Equation 4.14,

 

\(F_{ez}=\frac{GJ}{A_{g}\bar{r}_{0}^{2}}\)
Because the shear center of a tee is located at the intersection of the centerlines of the flange and the stem,
\(x_{0} = 0\)
\(y_{0} = \bar{y} – \frac{t_{f}}{2} = 2.70 – \frac{1.22}{2} = 2.090 in.\)

 

\(\bar{r}_{0}^{2}=x_{0}^{2}+y_{0}^{2}+\frac{I_{x}+I_{y}}{A_{g}}=0+(2.090)^{2}+\frac{293+221}{23.9}=25.87 in.^{2}\)

 

\(H = 1 – \frac{x_{0}^{2}+y_{0}^{2}}{{r}_{0}^{2}}=1 – \frac{0+(2.090)^{2}}{25.87}=0.8312\)

 

\(F_{ez}=\frac{GJ}{A_{g}\bar{r}_{o}^{2}}=\frac{11,200(9.22)}{23.9(25.87)}=167.0 ksi\)

 

\(F_{ey}+F_{ez}= 31.79 + 167.0 = 198.8 ksi\)

 

\(F_{e}=\left ( \frac{F_{ey}+F_{ez}}{2H} \right )\left [ 1 – \sqrt{1 – \frac{4F_{ey}F_{ez}2H}{(F_{ey}+F_{ez})^{2}}} \right ]\)

 

\(= \frac{198.8}{2(0.8312)} \left [ 1 – \sqrt{1 – \frac{ 4(31.79)(167.0)(0.8312)}{(198.8)^{2}}} \right ]=30.63ksi\)

 

\(\frac{F_{y}}{F_{e}}=\frac{50}{30.63}=1.632\)

Since 1.632 < 2.25, use AISC Equation E3-2:

 

\(F_{cr}= 0.658^{(F_{y}/F_{e})}F_{y}= 0.658^{1.632}(50) =25.25 ksi\)

 

\(P_{n} = F_{cr}A_{g}\) = 25.25(23.9) = 603.5 kips
The flexural–torsional buckling strength controls, and the nominal strength is 603.5 kips.
For LRFD, the design strength is \(\phi _{c}P_{n} \) = 0.90(603.5) = 543 kips.
For ASD, the allowable stress is \(F_{a}= 0.6F_{cr}\) = 0.6(25.25) = 15.15 ksi, and the allowable strength is \(F_{a} A_{g}\) = 15.15(23.9) = 362 kips.
