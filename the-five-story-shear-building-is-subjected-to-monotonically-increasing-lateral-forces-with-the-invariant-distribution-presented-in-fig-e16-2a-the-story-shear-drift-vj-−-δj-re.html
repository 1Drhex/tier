Guest Question.

The five-story shear building is subjected to monotonically increasing lateral forces with the invariant distribution presented in Fig. E16.2a. The story shear–drift (\(V_{j} − \delta _{j}\)) relationship is identical for all stories; it is bilinear with initial stiffness k = 100 kips/in., post yield stiffness ratio \(\alpha = 0.05\), and yield shear \(V_{jy} = 125 kips\) (Fig. E16.2b). Conduct nonlinear static analysis of the building for the pref shown and force factors:

\(\lambda ^{T} = \langle \begin{matrix} 0 & 1.0 & 1.1 & 1.2& 1.3& 1.4& 1.5 & 1.6\end{matrix} \rangle\)

Note that the base shear associated with pref is \(V_{b} \)= 125 kips, the yield value of base shear.

Admin
Solution

The procedure is implemented as follows:
1.0 State determination for \(u_{0} = 0\) Gravity load effects would not produce any lateral displacements; thus \(u_{0} = 0, (fS)_{0} = 0\) and

\(\mathbf{k_{T}}_{0} = k \begin{bmatrix} 2 &-1&&&& \\-1&2&-1&& & \\ &-1&2&-1&&\\&&-1&2&-1&\\ &&&-1&2&-1\\ &&&&-1&1\end{bmatrix}\)

To demonstrate calculations of steps 2.0 and 3.0 , they are implemented for the force step from i = 1 to i = 2.

2.0 Calculations for i = 1

2.1 Initialize j = 1

\(\mathbf{u}_{i+1}^{(1)} = \mathbf{u}_{i} = \langle \begin{matrix} 1.250 & 2.417 & 3.417 & 4.167 & 4.583 \end{matrix} \rangle ^{T}\) ; see Table E16.2. State determination at i = 1(λ = 1.0) leads to

\(\mathbf{f _{S}}_{i+1}^{(1)} = \mathbf{f _{S}}_{i} = \langle \begin{matrix} 8.333 & 16.67 & 25.00 & 33.33 & 41.67 \end{matrix} \rangle ^{T}\)

 

\(\mathbf{k_{T}}_{i+1}^{(1)} = \mathbf{k_{T}}_{i} = k \begin{bmatrix} 1.05 &-1&&&& \\-1&2&-1&& & \\ &-1&2&-1&&\\&&-1&2&-1&\\ &&&-1&2&-1\\&&&&-1&1\end{bmatrix}\)

2.2 \( p_{i}+1 = λ_{i}+1 p_{ref}\), where pref is shown in Fig. E16.2a.

\(\mathbf{P}_{i+1} = \langle \begin{matrix} 9.167 & 18.33 & 27.50 & 36.67 & 45.83 \end{matrix} \rangle ^{T} .\)

3.0 First iteration, j = 1

3.1 \(\mathbf{R}_{i+1}^{(1)} = \mathbf{P}_{i+1} – \mathbf{f _{S}}_{i+1}^{(1)} = \langle \begin{matrix} 0.833 & 1.667 & 2.500 & 3.333 & 4.167\end{matrix} \rangle ^{T} .\)

3.2 Check convergence:
Because \(|| \mathbf{R}_{i+1}|| = 6.180\) exceeds \(\epsilon R = 10^{−3}\), chosen for this example, implement steps 3.3 to 3.6.

3.3 Solve \(\mathbf{k_{T}}_{i+1}^{(1)} \Delta \mathbf{u}^{(1)} = \mathbf{R}_{i+1}^{(1)} => \mathbf{u}^{(1)} = \langle \begin{matrix} 2.500 & 2.617 & 2.717 & 2.792 &2.833 \end{matrix} \rangle ^{T}\)

3.4. \(\mathbf{u}_{i+1}^{(2)} =\mathbf{u}_{i+1}^{(1)}+\Delta \mathbf{u}^{1} = \left \langle \begin{matrix} 3.750& 5.033 &6.133 &6.958 &7.417 \end{matrix} \right \rangle^{T}\)

3.5 State determination.

First determine story drifts:

\(\mathbf{\delta }_{i+1}^{(2)}= \langle \begin{matrix} 3.750 & 1.283 & 1.100 & 0.825 & 0.458 \end{matrix} \rangle ^{T}\)

Knowing story drift, the story stiffness and story shear force can be obtained from the story shear–story drift relationship (Fig. E16.2b); e.g., the first story drift, δ1 = 3.750 in. is larger than the yield drift, δ1y = 1.25 in.; therefore, the story stiffness is 0.05k and the shear force is 125 + 0.05 k (3.75 − 1.25) = 137.5 kips. Knowing the stiffness of each story, the stiffness matrix can be obtained using the procedure .
\(\mathbf{k_{T}}_{i+1}^{(2)} = k \begin{bmatrix} 0.10 &-0.05 &&&& \\-0.05&1.05 &-1&& & \\ &-1&2&-1&&\\&&-1&2&-1&\\ &&&-1&2&-1\\ &&&&-1&1\end{bmatrix}\)

The resisting force \(f_{S1}\) at the first floor is the difference between story shears in the first and second stories; \(f_{Sj}\) at other stories are determined similarly to obtain

\(\mathbf{f _{S}}_{i+1}^{(2)} = \begin{bmatrix} 137.50 – 125.17 \\ 125.17-110.00 \\ 110.00-82.50 \\ 82.50 – 45.83 \\ 45.83 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 12.33 \\ 15.17 \\ 27.50 \\ 36.67 \\ 45.83 \end{bmatrix}\)

3.0 Second iteration, j = 2 3.1

\(\mathbf{R}_{i+1}^{(2)}= \mathbf{P}_{i+1} – \mathbf{f _{S}}_{i+1}^{(2)} = \langle \begin{matrix} – 3.167 & 3.167 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{matrix} \rangle ^{T} .\)

3.2 Check convergence:
Because \(\left \| R(2) _{i+1}\right \| = 4.478\) exceeds \(\varepsilon R,\) implement steps 3.3 to 3.6.

3.3 Solve \(\mathbf{k_{T}}_{i+1}^{(1)} \Delta \mathbf{u}^{(2)} = \mathbf{R}_{i+1}^{(2)} => \mathbf{u_{i}}^{(2)} = \langle \begin{matrix} 0 & 0.633 & 0.633 & 0.633 &0.633 \end{matrix} \rangle ^{T}\)

3.4 \(\mathbf{u_{i}}^{(3)} = \mathbf{u_{i+1}}^{(2)} \Delta \mathbf{u^{(2)}} = \langle \begin{matrix} 3.750 & 5.667 & 6.767 & 7.592 & 8.050 \end{matrix} \rangle ^{T}.\)

3.5 State determination:

\(\mathbf{\delta }_{i+1}^{(3)}= \langle \begin{matrix} 3.750 & 1.917 & 1.100 & 0.825 & 0.458 \end{matrix} \rangle ^{T}\)

 

\(\mathbf{f _{S}}_{i+1}^{(3)} = \begin{bmatrix} 9.167 \\ 18.33 \\ 27.50 \\ 36.67 \\ 45.83 \end{bmatrix}\)

 

\(\mathbf{k_{T}}_{i+1}^{(3)} = \mathbf{k_{T}}_{i} = k \begin{bmatrix} 0.10 &-0.05&&&& \\-0.05&1.05&-1&& & \\ &-1&2&-1&&\\&&-1&2&-1&\\ &&&-1&2&-1\\&&&&-1&1\end{bmatrix}\)

3.0 Third iteration, j = 3

3.1 \(R(3)_{ i+1} = p_{i}+1 − (f_{S}) (3) _{i+1} \simeq 0.\)

3.2 Check convergence: Because \(\left \| R(3)_{ i+1}\right \| = 0\) is less than εR, iteration is terminated and the final value is denoted as ui+1; see row 3 of Table E16.2.

4.0 Repetition for the next time step: Steps 2.0 and 3.0 are implemented for i = 0, 1, 2, 3,… to obtain the floor displacements \(u_{1}, u_{2}, u_{3}, u_{4}\), and \(u_{5}\) presented in Table E16.2 and plotted in Fig. E16.2c. The base shear is plotted as a function of the roof displacement \(u_{5}\) in Fig. E16.2d. Story shear–story drift relationships are presented in Figs. E16.2e–i for the five stories of the building.

1.1 State determination for initial \(\mathbf{u}=\mathbf{u}_{0}: \left ( \mathbf{f_{S}} \right )_{0}.\)

1.4 \(\mathbf{u}_{-1} =\mathbf{u}_{0}-\Delta t\mathbf{\dot{u}}_{0}+ \frac{\left ( \Delta t \right )^{2}}{2}\mathbf{\ddot{u}}_{0}\)
1.5 \(\mathbf{\hat{k}}=\frac{1}{\left ( \Delta t \right )^{2}}\mathbf{m}+\frac{1}{2\Delta t}\mathbf{c}\)
1.6 \(\mathbf{a}=\frac{1}{\left ( \Delta t \right )^{2}}\mathbf{m}-\frac{1}{2\Delta t}\mathbf{c} and \mathbf{b} = -\frac{2}{\left ( \Delta t \right )^{2}}\mathbf{m}\)

2.2 solve \(\mathbf{\hat{k}u}_{i+1} =\mathbf{\hat{p}}_{i}\Rightarrow \mathbf{u}_{i+1}\)
