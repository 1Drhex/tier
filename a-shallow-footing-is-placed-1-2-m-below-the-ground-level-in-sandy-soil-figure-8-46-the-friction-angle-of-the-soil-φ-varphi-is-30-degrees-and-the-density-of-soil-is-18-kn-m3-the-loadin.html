Guest Question.

A shallow footing is placed 1.2 m below the ground level in sandy soil (Figure 8.46). The friction angle of the soil \(\varphi^{\prime }\) is \(30^{\circ }\) degrees and the density of soil is \(18 kN/m^{3}.\) The loading is vertical and the footing is not inclined. Length of the footing is 4 m and the width of the footing is 3 m. Assum-ing a factor of safety of 3.0, find the allowable load on the footing.

Admin
Solution

Step 1: Write down the bearing capacity equation for drained condition. For sandy soils, drained equation is used.

\(R/A^{\prime }=c^{\prime }N_{c}b_{c}s_{c}i_{c}+q^{\prime }N_{q}b_{q}s_{q}i_{q}+½\gamma ^{\prime }B^{\prime }N_{\gamma }b_{\gamma }s_{\gamma }i_{\gamma }\)

We can ignore the “b” factors and “i” factors. Hence, the equation can be simplified to the
following equation:

\(R/A^{\prime }=c^{\prime }N_{c}s_{c}+q^{\prime }N_{q}s_{q}+½\gamma ^{\prime }B^{\prime }N_{\gamma }s_{\gamma }\)

\(A^{\prime } = A =\) area of the footing when the load is applied at the center of gravity of the footing vertically.

Step 2: Find the parameters.
For sandy soils \(c^{\prime } = 0.\)

\(q^{\prime }=\gamma D=18\times 1.1=19.8 kN/m^{2}.\)

 

\(N_{q}=e^{\pi \tan \varphi ^{\prime }}\tan ^{2}(45+\varphi ^{\prime }/2)=e^{\pi \tan 30}\tan ^{2}(45+30/2)=6.13\times3.0=18.40\)

 

\(N_{\gamma }=2\times (N_{q}-1)\tan \varphi ^{\prime }=2\times (18.40-1)\times 0.577=20.1\)

Shape factors:

\(s_{q}=1+\sin \varphi ^{\prime }=1.5\)

 

\(s_{c}=(s_{q}N_{q}-1)/(N_{q}-1)=26.6/17.4=1.53\)

 

\(s_{\gamma }=1-0.3(B/L)=1-0.3\times 3/4=0.775\)

Step 3: Apply the bearing capacity equation.

\(R/A^{\prime }=c^{\prime }N_{c}s_{c}+q^{\prime }N_{q}s_{q}+½\gamma ^{\prime }B^{\prime }N_{\gamma }s_{\gamma }\)

 

\(R/A^{\prime }=0+19.8\times 18.40\times 1.5+½\times 18\times 3\times 20.1\times 0.775=546.5+420.6=967.1 kN/m^{2}.\)

 

\(R =\) area \(\times 967.1 = 3 \times 4 \times 967.1 = 11,605.2\)

Allowable load on the footing \(= 11605/FOS = 11605/3.0 = 3868.4 kN\)
