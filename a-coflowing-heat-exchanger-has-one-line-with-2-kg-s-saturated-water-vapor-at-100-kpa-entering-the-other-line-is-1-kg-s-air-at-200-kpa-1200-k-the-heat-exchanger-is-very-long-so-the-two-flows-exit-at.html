Guest Question.

A coflowing heat exchanger has one line with 2 kg/s saturated water vapor at 100 kPa entering. The other line is 1 kg/s air at 200 kPa, 1200 K. The heat exchanger is very long so the two flows exit at the same temperature. Find the exit temperature by trial and error. Calculate the rate of entropy generation.

Admin
Solution

C.V. Heat exchanger, steady 2 flows in and two flows out.
No W, no external Q

Flows: \(\dot{ m }_{1}=\dot{ m }_{2}=\dot{ m }_{ H _{2} O } ; \quad \dot{ m }_{3}=\dot{ m }_{4}=\dot{ m }_{ air }\)

Energy: \(\dot{ m }_{ H _{2} O }\left( h _{2}- h _{1}\right)=\dot{ m }_{ air }\left( h _{3}- h _{4}\right)\)

State 1: Table B.1.2 \(h _{1}=2675.5 kJ / kg\) State 2: 100 kPa, \(T _{2}\)

State 3: Table A.7 \(h _{3}=1277.8 kJ / kg\), State 4: 200 kPa, \(T _{2}\)

Only one unknown \(T _{2}\) and one equation the energy equation:

\(2\left( h _{2}-2675.5\right)=1\left(1277.8- h _{4}\right) \quad \Rightarrow \quad 1 kg / s \times\left(2 h _{2}+ h _{4}\right)=6628.8 kW\)

At 500 K: \(h _{2}=2902.0, h _{4}=503.36 \Rightarrow LHS =6307 \) too small

At 700 K: \(h _{2}=3334.8, h _{4}=713.56 \Rightarrow LHS =7383\) too large

Linear interpolation \(T _{2}=560 K , h _{2}=3048.3, h _{4}=565.47 \Rightarrow LHS =6662\)

Final states are with \(T _{2}=554.4 K =281^{\circ} C\)

H2O: Table B.1.3, \(h _{2}=3036.8 kJ / kg , s _{2}=8.1473, s _{1}=7.3593 kJ / kg K\)

AIR: Table A.7, \(h _{4}=559.65 kJ / kg , s _{ T 4}=7.4936, s _{ T 3}=8.3460 kJ / kg K\)

The entropy balance equation, Eq.7.7, is solved for the generation term:

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ S }_{\text {gen }}=& \dot{ m }_{ H _{2} O }\left( s _{2}- s _{1}\right)+\dot{ m }_{\text {air }}\left( s _{4}- s _{3}\right) \\&=2(8.1473-7.3593)+1(7.4936-8.3460)= 0 . 7 2 4 k W / K\end{aligned}\)

No pressure correction is needed as the air pressure for 4 and 3 is the same.
