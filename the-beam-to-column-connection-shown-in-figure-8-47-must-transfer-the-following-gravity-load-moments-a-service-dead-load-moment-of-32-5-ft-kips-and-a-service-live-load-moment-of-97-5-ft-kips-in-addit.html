Guest Question.

The beam-to-column connection shown in Figure 8.47 must transfer the following gravity load moments: a service dead-load moment of 32.5 ft-kips and a service live load moment of 97.5 ft-kips. In addition, there is a service axial dead load of 30 kips and a service axial live load of 90 kips. A992 steel is used for the beam and column, and A36 is used for the plate material. E70XX electrodes are used. Investigate column stiffener and web panel-zone reinforcement requirements. The connection is not near the end of the column. Assume that V = 0.

 

Admin
Solution

The factored-load moment is
\(M_u = 1.2M_D + 1.6(M_L) = 1.2(32.5) + 1.6(97.5) = 195.0 ft-kips\)
The flange force is

 

\(P_{bf} =\frac{M_u}{d_b – t_b}=\frac{195(12)}{17.9 – 0.525} = 134.7 kips\)
To check local flange bending, use AISC Equation J10-1:
\(\phi R_n = \phi(6.25F_{yf} t_{f}^{2} ) = 0.90[6.25(50)(0.560)^{2}] = 88.20 \)kips < 134.7 kips (N.G.)

\(\therefore\) Stiffeners are required to prevent local flange bending.

To check for local web yielding, use AISC Equation J10-2:
\(\phi R_n = \phi[F_{yw} t_w(5k + l_b)]\)

 

\(= 1.0{50(0.360)[5(0.954) + 0.525]} = 95.31 kips < 134.7 kips (N.G.)\)
\(\therefore \)Stiffeners are required to prevent local web yielding.
To check web crippling strength, use AISC Equation J10-4:
\(\phi R_n = \phi0.80t_{w}^{2}\left [ 1 + 3 \left ( \frac{l_b}{d} \right )\left ( \frac{t_{w}}{t_{f}} \right )^{1.5} \right ]\sqrt{\frac{EF_{yw} t_{f}}{t_{w}}}Q_{f}\)

 

\(= 0.75(0.80)(0.360)^{2}\left [1 + 3 \left ( \frac{0.525}{8.25} \right )\left ( \frac{0.360}{0.560} \right )^{1.5} \right ]\sqrt{\frac{29,000(50)(0.560)}{0.360}}(1.0)\)
\(= 128.3 kips < 134.7 kips (N.G.)\)
\(\therefore \)Stiffeners are required to prevent web crippling. The local flange bending strength of 88.20 kips is the smallest of the three limit states. From Equation 8.7, the required stiffener area is
\(A_{st} =\frac {P_{bf} – \phi R_{n min}}{\phi_{st}F_{yst}}= \frac {134.7 – 88.20}{0.90(36)} = 1.44 in.^{2}\)

 

The stiffener dimensions will be selected on the basis of the criteria given in AISC Section J10.8, and the area of the resulting cross section will then be checked.
The minimum width is
\(b \geq \frac{b_b}{3} – \frac{t_w}{2}\)

 

\(= \frac{6.02}{3} – \frac{0.360}{2} = 1.83 in.\)
If the stiffeners are not permitted to extend beyond the edges of the column flange,
the maximum width is
\(b \leq \frac{8.07 – 0.360}{2} = 3.86 in.\)
The minimum thickness is
\(\frac{t_b}{2}= \frac{0.525}{2}= 0.263 in.\)
Try a plate 5⁄16 \(\times 3:\)
\(A_{st} = 3\left ( \frac{5}{16} \right ) \times\) 2 stiffeners \(= 1.88 in.^{2} > 1.44 in.^{2} (OK)\)
Check for\( t_{st} \geq b/16:\)
\(\frac{b}{16} = \frac{3}{16} = 0.188 in. < \frac{5}{16} in. (OK)\)

This connection is one-sided, so full-depth stiffeners are not required. Try a depth of
\(\frac{d}{2} =\frac{ 8.25}{2} = 4.125 in.\) Try 4 1⁄2 inches.
Try two plates 5⁄16\( \times 3 \times \)0′- 4 1⁄2″. (Clip the inside corners to avoid the fillets at the column flange-to-web intersection. Clip at a \( 45^{\circ} \)angle for 5⁄8 inch.)
For the stiffener to column web welds,
Minimum size = \(\frac{3}{16} in.\) (AISC Table J2.4, based on plate thickness)
The size required for strength, in sixteenths of an inch, is
\(D = \frac{force resisted by stiffener}{1.392L}\)
Since flange local bending controls in this example, the force to be resisted by the stiffener is
134.7 – 88.20 = 46.50 kips
The length available for welding the stiffener to the web is
\(L = (length – clip) \times 2 sides \times\) 2 stiffeners
\(=\left ( 4.5 -\frac{5}{8} \right )(2)(2) = 15.5 in.\) (See Figure 8.48.)
The required weld size is therefore
\(D = \frac{46.5}{1.392(15.5)}= 2.16 sixteenths < \frac{3}{16} in.\) minimum
Try w = 3⁄16 inch: Check the base metal shear strength. From Equation 7.35, the shear yield strength of the web is
\(\phi R_n = 0.6F_yt \times 2\) sides of stiffener = 0.6(50)(0.360)(2) = 21.6 kips/in.

(The load is shared by two thicknesses of web, one on each side of the stiffener pair.)
From Equation 7.36, the shear rupture strength is
\(\phi R_n = 0.45F_ut \times 2 = 0.45(65)(0.360)(2) = 21.06 kips/in.\)
For the stiffener shear yield strength,
\(\phi R_n = 0.6F_yt_{st} \times 2 = 0.6(36)\left ( \frac{5}{16} \right )(2) = 13.5 kips/in.\)
The shear rupture strength is
\(\phi R_n = 0.45Fut_{st}\times 2 =0.45(58)\left ( \frac{5}{16} \right )(2) = 16.31 kips/in.\)
The base metal shear strength is therefore 13.5 kips/in. The required strength of the weld is
\(1.392D \times 2 \times 2 \)= 1.392(2.16)(2)(2)
= 12.03 kips/in. < 13.5 kips/in. (OK)
Use two plates 5⁄16\( \times 3\) \(\times\) 0′ – 4 1⁄2″. Clip the inside corners to avoid the fillets at the column flange-to-web intersection. Clip at a 45′ angle for 5⁄8 inch. Use a 3⁄16-inch fillet weld for the stiffener-to-column web welds.
Stiffener-to-column flange welds: Since the beam tension flange force is an issue (for both column flange bending and web yielding), the stiffener must be welded to the column flange. Use a weld sufficient to develop the yield strength of the stiffener.
Let
\(1.392 DL(1.5) = \phi _{st}F_{yst}A_{st}\)
where
L = (b – clip) \(\times \) 2 sides \(\times \) 2 stiffeners
and the factor of 1.5 is used because the load is perpendicular to the axis of the weld. Then,
\(D = \frac{\phi _{st}F_{yst}A_{st}}{1.392L(1.5)}= \frac{0.90(36)(1.88)}{1.392[ (3 – 5/8) \times 2 \times 2](1.5)}= 3.07\) sixteenths
Minimum size = \(\frac{3}{16} in. \)(AISC Table J2.4, based on plate thickness)
Use a 1⁄4-inch fillet weld for the stiffener-to-column flange connection. (Because the applied moment is caused by gravity loads and is not reversible, the stiffeners opposite the beam compression flange can be fitted to bear on the column flange and need not be welded, but this option is not exercised here.)

Check the column web for shear. From Equation 8.9,
\(F =\frac{ (M_1 – M_2)}{d_m} – V = \frac{M_u}{d_b – t_b} – V_u = \frac{195(12)}{17.9 – 0.525 }-0= 134.7 kips\)
Determine which panel zone shear strength equation to use.
\(P_y = F_yA_g = 50(11.7) = 585 kips\)

 

\(0.4P_y = 0.4(585) = 234 kips\)

 

\(P_r = 1.2P_D + 1.6P_L = 1.2(30) + 1.6(90) = 180 kips\)

 

\(\alpha P_r = 1.0(180) = 180 kips < 234 kips \therefore \)Use AISC Equation J10-9.

 

\(R_n = 0.60F_yd_c t_w = 0.60(50)(14.2)(0.485) = 89.1 kips\)
The design strength is

 

\(\phi R_n = 0.90(89.1) = 80.19 kips < 134.7 (N.G.)\)
Alternative 1: Use a Web Doubler Plate Use AISC Equation J10-9 to find the required doubler plate thickness. Multiplying
both sides by \(\phi\) and solving for\( t_w\) gives
\(t_w =\frac{\phi R_n }{\phi(0.60F_yd_c)}\)
Substituting the plate thickness td for tw and using the yield stress of the doubler plate, we get
\(t_d = \frac{\phi R_n }{\phi(0.60F_yd_c)}= \frac{134.7 – 80.19}{0.90(0.60)(36)(8.25)}= 0.340 in.\)
where 134.7 – 80.19 is the extra strength, in kips, to be furnished by the doubler plate. The design of the welds connecting the doubler plate to the column will depend on the exact configuration of the plate, including whether the plate extends beyond the transverse stiffeners. For this and other details, refer to AISC Design Guide 13, Stiffening of Wide-Flange Columns at Moment Connections: Wind and Seismic Applications (Carter, 1999).
Use a 3⁄8-inch-thick doubler plate.
Alternative 2: Use a Diagonal Stiffener
With this alternative, use full-depth horizontal stiffeners along with the diagonal stiffener as shown in Figure 8.49.
The shear force to be resisted by the web reinforcement is 134.7 – 80.19 = 54.51 kips. If this force is taken as the horizontal component of an axial compressive force P in the stiffener,
\(P cos \theta = 54.51 kips\)

where
\(\theta = tan^{-1}\left (\frac{d_b}{d_c} \right )= tan^{-1}\left (\frac{17.9}{8.25} \right )=65.26^{\circ} \)

\(P =\frac{ 54.51}{cos(65.26^{\circ})}= 130.3 kips\)
Since the stiffener is connected along its length, we will treat it as a compression
member whose effective length,\( L_c, \)is zero. From AISC J4.4, for compression elements with\( L_c/r < 25,\) the nominal strength is
\(P_n = F_yA_g\)
and for LRFD,\( \phi 5 0.90\)
\(\phi P_n = 0.90(36)A_g = 32.4A_g\)
Equating this strength to the required strength, we obtain the required area of stiffener:
\(32.4A_g = 130.3\)
\(A_g = 4.02 in.^2\)

Try two stiffeners, 3⁄4 \(\times \)3, one on each side of the web:
\(A_{st} = 2(3)\left ( \frac{3}{4} \right ) = 4.50 in.^2 > 4.02 in.^2 required (OK)\)
Check for \(t_{st} \geq b/16:\)
\(\frac{b}{16}= \frac{3}{16}= 0.188 in. < \frac{3}{4} in. (OK)\)

Design the welds. The approximate length of each diagonal stiffener is
\(L_{st} = \frac{d_c}{cos \theta } = \frac{8.25}{cos(65.26^{\circ})}= 19.7 in.\)
If welds are used on both sides of the stiffeners, the available length for welding is
\(L = 19.7(4) = 78.8 in.\)
The weld size, in sixteenths of an inch, required for strength is
\(D = \frac{P}{1.392L} =\frac{ 130.3}{1.392(78.8)}= 1.2 \)sixteenths
Based on the web thickness, use a minimum size of 3⁄16 inch (AISC Table J2.4).
Because of the small size required for strength, investigate the possibility of using intermittent welds. From AISC J2.2b,
Minimum length\( = 4w = 4\left ( \frac{3}{4} \right )=\) 0.75 in., but not less than 1.5 in. (1.5 in. controls)
For a group of four welds, the capacity is
\(4(1.392DL) = 4(1.392)(3)(1.5) = 25.06 kips\)
Required weld capacity per inch =\( \frac{P}{L_{st}}= \frac{130.3}{19.7} = 6.614 kips/in\).
Check the base metal shear strength of the column web. From Equation 7.35,
the shear yield strength per unit length (considering a web thickness on each side of the stiffener pair) is
\(\phi R_n = 0.6F_yt_w \times 2 = 0.6(50)(0.360)(2) = 21.6 kips/in.\)
From Equation 7.36, the shear rupture strength is
\(\phi R_n = 0.45F_ut_w \times 2 = 0.45(65)(0.360)(2) = 21.1 kips/in.\)
For the stiffener, the shear yield strength is
\(\phi R_n =0.6F_yt_{st} \times 2 stiffeners = 0.6(36)(3/4)(2) = 32.4 kips/in.\)
and the shear rupture strength is
\(\phi R_n = 0.45F_ut_{st} \times 2 = 0.45(58)(3/4) \times 2 = 39.15 kips/in.\)
The controlling base metal shear strength is 21.1 kips/in., but this is greater than the weld strength of 6.614 kips/in.
Required spacing of welds\( =\frac {strength of weld group (kips)}{required strength per inch (kips/in.)} = \frac{25.06}{6.614}= 3.79 in.\)

Use two stiffeners, 3⁄4 \(\times\) 3, one on each side of the web, and 3⁄16-inch \(\times 1\) 1⁄2-inch intermittent fillet welds spaced at 3 1⁄2 inches on center, on each side of each diagonal stiffener.
The service load moment is
\(M_a = M_D + M_L = 32.5 + 97.5 = 130 ft-kips\)
The flange force is
\(P_{bf} =\frac{ M_a}{d_b – t_b}= \frac{130(12)}{17.9 – 0.525} = 89.78 kips\)
Check local flange bending with AISC Equation J10-1:
\(\frac{R_n}{\Omega} =\frac{ 6.25F_{yf} t_{f}^{2} }{\Omega}= \frac{6.25(50)(0.560)^2}{1.67} = 58.68 kips < 89.78 kips (N.G.)\)
Check local web yielding with AISC Equation J10-2:
\(\frac{R_n}{\Omega} =\frac{F_{yw} t_w(5k + l_b)}{\Omega}\)

 

\(=\frac{ 50(0.360)[5(0.954) + 0.525]}{1.50} = 63.54 kips < 89.78 kips (N.G.)\)
Check web crippling with AISC Equation J10-4:

 

\(\frac{R_n}{\Omega} =\frac{0.80t\left [ 1 + 3 \left ( \frac{l_b}{d} \right )\left ( \frac{t_{w}}{t_{f}} \right )^{1.5} \right ]\sqrt{\frac{EF_{yw} t_{f}}{t_{w}}}Q_{f}}{\Omega}\)

 

\(= \frac{0.80(0.360)^2 \left [1 + 3 \left ( \frac{0.525}{8.25} \right )\left ( \frac{0.360}{0.560} \right )^{1.5} \right ]\sqrt{\frac{29,000(50)0.560}{0.360}}(1.0)}{2.00} =85.52 kips > 89.78 kips (N.G.)\)

 

Stiffeners are required. The smallest strength is 58.68 kips, for the limit state of local flange bending. From Equation 8.8, the required stiffener area is
\(A_{st} = \frac{P_{bf} – (R_n/\Omega)_{min}}{F_{yst}/\Omega} = \frac{89.78 – 58.68}{36/1.67} = 1.44 in.^2\)
The minimum width is

 

\(b \geq \frac{b_b}{3} – \frac{t_w}{2}\)

 

\(= \frac{6.02}{3} – \frac{0.360}{2} = 1.83 in.\)

Use a maximum width of

 

\(b \leq \frac{8.07 – 0.360}{2} = 3.86 in.\)
The minimum thickness is

 

\(\frac{t_b}{2}= \frac{0.525}{2} = 0.263 in.\)
Try a plate 5⁄16 \(\times 3:\)

 

\(A_{st} = 3\left ( \frac{5}{16} \right ) \times 2\) stiffeners\( \times 1.88 in.^2 > 1.44 in.^2 (OK)\)
Check for \(t_{st} \geq b/16:\)
\(\frac{b}{16}= \frac{3}{16}= 0.188 in. < \frac{5}{16} in. (OK)\)

This connection is one-sided, so full-depth stiffeners are not required. Use a depth of
\(\frac{d}{2} = \frac{8.25}{2} = 4.125 in\). Try 4 1⁄2 inch
Try two plates 5⁄16 \(\times 3 \times\) 0′ – 4 1⁄2″:(Clip the inside corners to avoid the fillets at the column flange-to-web intersection. Clip at a 45° angle for 5⁄8 inch.)

Stiffener-to-column web welds: The size required for strength, in sixteenths of an inch, is
\(D = \frac{force resisted by stiffener}{0.9279L}\)
The force to be resisted by the stiffener = 89.78 – 58.68 = 31.10 kips. The length available for welding the stiffener to the web is
L = (length – clip) \(\times\) 2 sides \(\times\) 2 stiffeners
\(= \left (4.5 – \frac{5}{8}\right )(2)(2) = 15.5 in.\) (See Figure 8.48.)

\(D = \frac{31.10}{0.9279(15.5)}= \) 2.16 sixteenths

Try w = 3⁄16 inch: From Equation 7.37, the base metal shear yield strength of the web is
\(\frac{R_n}{\Omega} =\frac{ 0.4F_yt} \times 2 sides of stiffener = 0.4(50)(0.360)(2) = 14.4 kips/in.\)
