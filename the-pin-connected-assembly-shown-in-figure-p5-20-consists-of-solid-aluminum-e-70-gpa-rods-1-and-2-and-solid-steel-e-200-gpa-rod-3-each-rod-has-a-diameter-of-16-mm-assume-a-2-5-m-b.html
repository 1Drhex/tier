Guest Question.

The pin-connected assembly shown in Figure P5.20 consists of solid aluminum [E = 70 GPa] rods (1) and (2) and solid steel [E = 200 GPa] rod (3). Each rod has a diameter of 16 mm. Assume a = 2.5 m, b = 1.6 m, and c = 0.8 m. If the normal stress in any rod may not exceed 150 MPa, determine:
(a) the maximum load P that may be applied at A.
(b) the magnitude of the resulting deflection at A.

Admin
Solution

The lengths of rods (1) and (2) are:

\(L_{1}=\sqrt{(1.6 m )^{2}+(0.8 m )^{2}}=1.78885 m =1,788.8544 mm =L_{2}\)

and the cross-sectional area of a 16-mm-diameter rod is

\(A=\frac{\pi}{4}(16 mm )^{2}=201.062 mm ^{2}\)

Determine the inclination angles for rods (1) and (2):

\(\tan \theta_{1}=\frac{0.8 m }{1.6 m } \quad \therefore \theta_{1}=26.5651^{\circ}=\theta_{2}\)

 

(a) Maximum load P: From a FBD of joint A, the force in rod (3) is

\(F_{3}=P\)

Next, consider a FBD of joint B and write two equilibrium equations:

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma F_{x}=-F_{3}+F_{1} \cos 26.5651^{\circ}+F_{2} \cos 26.5651^{\circ}=0 \\&\Sigma F_{y}=F_{1} \sin 26.5651^{\circ}-F_{2} \sin 26.5651^{\circ}=0\end{aligned}\)

From the second equation, \(F_{1}=F_{2}\) . The first equation gives \(F_{1}=0.559017 F_{3}=0.559017 P\) .

The allowable normal stress for each rod is 150 MPa; therefore, the allowable force in any rod is

\(F_{\text {allow }}=\sigma_{\text {allow }} A=(150 MPa )\left(201.062 mm ^{2}\right)=30,159.3 N\)

Setting this allowable force equal to \(F_{3}\), the force in rods (1) and (2) is

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{3}=F_{\text {allow }}=30,159.3 N \\&F_{1}=F_{2}=0.559017 F_{3}=(0.559017)(30,159.3 N )=16,859.6 N \leq F_{\text {allow }} OK\end{aligned}\)

The maximum force P that can be applied to the assembly is thus:

\(P=30.2 kN\)

 

(b) Deflection at A: The elongation of steel rod (3) for a load of P = 30,159.3 N is

\(\delta_{3}=\frac{F_{3} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}=\frac{(30,159.3 N )(2,500 mm )}{\left(201.062 mm ^{2}\right)\left(200,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=1.8750 mm\)

The elongation of aluminum rod (1) [and rod (2)] is

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}=\frac{(16,859.6 N )(1,788.8544 mm )}{\left(201.062 mm ^{2}\right)\left(70,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=2.1429 mm\)

Thus, the length of rod (1) [and rod (2)] after deformation is

\(L_{1}^{\prime}=1,788.8544 mm +2.1429 mm =1,790.9972 mm\)

The distance c does not change with the deformation of rod (1), but the distance b will increase as the length of rod increases. The new dimension b’ after the deformation of rod (1) is

\(b^{\prime}=\sqrt{(1,790.9972 mm )^{2}-(800 mm )^{2}}=1,602.3954 mm\)

This means that joint B moves to the left:

\(u_{B}=1,602.3954 mm -1,600 mm =2.3954 mm \leftarrow\)

The deflection of joint A is the sum of this deflection and the elongation of rod (3):

\(u_{A}=2.3954 mm +1.8750 mm =4.27 mm \leftarrow\)

 

Note: The deflection of joint B in the horizontal direction can be approximated from

\(u_{B}=\frac{\delta_{1}}{\cos 26.5651^{\circ}}=\frac{2.1429 mm }{\cos 26.5651^{\circ}}=2.3958 mm\)
