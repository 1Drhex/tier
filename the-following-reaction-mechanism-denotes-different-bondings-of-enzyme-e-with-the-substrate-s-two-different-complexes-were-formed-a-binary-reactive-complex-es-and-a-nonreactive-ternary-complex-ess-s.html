Guest Question.

The following reaction mechanism denotes different bondings of enzyme E with the substrate S. Two different complexes were formed: a binary reactive complex ES and a nonreactive ternary complex ESS.
\(S+E\overset{k_{2}/k_{1}}{\leftrightarrow }ES\)
\(ES+S\overset{k_{3}}{\rightarrow}K_{2}\)
\(ES\overset{k_{4}/k_{5}}{\leftrightarrow }E+P\)

(a) Describe the reaction rate of the product \(P(r_{p})\) based on this mechanism?
(b) Show how inhibition occurs in the product rate equation P when compared to the reaction rate based on the Michaelis-Menten mechanism for a single site.
(c) Compare the maximum rates for both models and for what S value it occurs \((V_{max} =k_{r}C_{[E0]})\). Plot \(r_{p}\) as function of [S].
\(S_{1}+E\overset{k_{2}/k_{1}}{\leftrightarrow }ES_{1}\)
\(S_{2}+E\overset{k_{2}/k_{1}}{\leftrightarrow }ES_{2}\)
\(ES\overset{k_{3}}{\rightarrow}E+P_{1}\)
\(ES\overset{k_{4}}{\rightarrow }E+P_{2}\)

Admin
Solution

(a) Rate equations for ES and ESS
\(r_{ES}=k_{1}[E][S]-k'_{1}[ES][S]+k'_{2}[ESS]-k_{t}[ES]\) (1)
\(r_{ESS}=k_{2}[ES][S]+k'_{2}[ESS]\) (2)

Assuming steady state for [ES] and [ESS], we have:
\(dr_{ES}/dt =0\)
\(dr_{ES}/dt =0\)

The total enzyme concentration is:
\([E_{0}]=[E] + [ES] + [ESS]\) (3)

From Equation (1):
\([ES^{*}]=\frac{k'_{1}[ES]+k_{2}[ES][S]-k'_{2}[ESS]+k_{t}[S]}{k_{1}[S]}\)

From Equation (2):
\(k'_{2}[ESS]=k_{2}[ES][S]\)

From Equation (3):
\([ESS]=\frac{k_{2}}{k'_{2}}[ES][S]\)

Substitution of [ESS] results:
\([ES]=\frac{[E_{0}]}{\frac{k'_{1}+k_{t}}{k_{1}[S]}+1+\frac{[S]}{K_{2}}}\)

where: \(k_{m}=\frac{k'_{1}+k_{t}}{k_{1}}\)
since \(k_{p}=k_{r}[ES]\) we have:
\(k_{p}=\frac{k_{t}[E_{0}[S]]}{\frac{k_{m}}{[S]}+[S]\frac{[S]^{2}}{K_{2}}}\) (4)

where: \(k_{r}[E_{0}]=V_{max}\)
\(k_{p}=\frac{V_{max}[S]}{k_{m}+[S]+\frac{[S]^{2}}{K_{2}}}\) (5)

(b) The nonreactive complex formation promotes inhibition because the amount of enzymes decreases with increasing product formation. Comparing Equation (11.30) with the Michaelis-Menten equation, it shows that the term \([S]^{2}/K_{2}\) is responsible for the decreasing rate formation of the product. The inhibition depends on \(k_{m}\), [S], and \(K_{2}\). \((K_{2}\) decreases with more [ESS] formation).
(c) Since:

Eq (11.30): \(R_{3}=\frac{k_{1}[M]}{2k_{4}[R_{1}]} \)

\(r_{p}=\frac{V_{max}[S]}{k_{m}+[S]+\frac{[S]^{2}}{K_{2}}}\) (6)

The rate is maxima when:\(\frac{dr_{p}}{d[S]}=0\)
Thus:
\(r_{pmax}=\frac{k_{t}[E_{0}]\sqrt{k_{m}K_{2}}}{k_{m}+\sqrt{k_{m}K_{2}}+\frac{k_{m}K_{2}}{K_{2}}}\) (7)

when \(V_{max} =k_{r} [E_{0}]\) (item C)
Therefore:
\(r_{pmax}=\frac{V_{max}\sqrt{k_{m}K_{2}}}{k_{m}+\sqrt{k_{m}K_{2}}+\frac{k_{m}K_{2}}{K_{2}}}\)

Vmax is smaller when compared to Michaelis-Menten mechanism. For the Michaelis-Menten it is: \(V_{máx}k_{r}[E_{0}]\)
