Guest Question.

Consider the torsion of a hollow square cross section member. The stress function \(\Psi \) is required to satisfy the Poisson equation (8.5.60) and the following boundary conditions:
\(\Psi =0\) on the outer boundary; \(\Psi =2r^{2}\) on the inner boundary
where r is the ratio of the outside dimension to the inside dimension, r = 6a/2a.
Formulate the problem for finite element analysis using the mesh shown in Fig. P8.46.

 

Eq. (8.5.60) : \(-\bigtriangledown ^{2}\Psi =2\) in \(\Omega \)
\(\Psi = 0\) on \(\Gamma \)

 

Admin
Solution

 

Figure P8.46(a)

The ratio of outside to inside dimensions is 3. Hence, \(\Psi =18\). The value of \(\Psi \) at nodes 3, 6, 8 and 9 is zero, and at nodes 1 and 4 it is 18. Thus the unknown values are at nodes 2, 5, and 7. We have

\(K_{22}U_{2}+K_{25}U_{5}=-K_{21}U_{1}-K_{24}U_{4}\)

 

\(K_{52}U_{2}+K_{55}U_{5}+K_{57}U_{7}=-K_{51}U_{1}-K_{54}U_{4}\)

 

\(K_{75}U_{5}+K_{77}U_{7}=-K_{74}U_{4}\)

where

\(K_{21}=K_{21}^{R}\) , \(K_{22}=K_{11}^{R}+K_{22}^{R}\) , \(K_{24}=K_{24}^{R}\) , \(K_{25}=K_{23}^{R}+K_{14}^{R}\)

\(K_{51}=K_{31}^{R}\) , \(K_{54}=K_{34}^{R}+K_{21}^{T}\) , \(K_{55}=K_{11}^{R}+K_{33}^{R}+K_{44}^{R}+K_{22}^{T}\) , \(K_{57}=K_{14}^{R}+K_{23}^{T}\)

and the coefficient matrices associated with rectangular (R) and triangular (T) elements are

\([K^{T}]=\frac{1}{2}\begin{bmatrix}1 &-1 &0 \\ -1 &2 &-1 \\ 0 &-1 &1 \end{bmatrix}\) , \([K^{R}]=\frac{1}{6}\begin{bmatrix}4 &-1 &-2 &-1 \\ -1 &4 &-1 &-2 \\ -2 &-1 &4 &-1 \\ -1 &-2 &-1 &4 \end{bmatrix}\)

are the element matrices associated with the triangular element (right-angle is numbered as node 2) and rectangular element. The source coefficients for these elements are (a = b):

\(f_{i}^{T}=\frac{2ab}{6}\) , \(f_{i}^{R}=\frac{2ab}{4}\)

 

 

Figure P8.46(b)

 

For the case of the mesh of triangles, all element matrices are the same and equal to \([K^{T}]\) of Problem 8.46. The finite element equations associated with nodes 2, 5 and 7 are

\(K_{22} U_{2}+K_{25} U_{5}=-K_{21} U_{1}\)

 

\(K_{52} U_{2}+K_{55} U_{5}+K_{57} U_{7}=-K_{51} U_{1}-K_{54} U_{4}\)

 

\(K_{75} U_{5}+K_{77} U_{7}=-K_{74} U_{4}\)

 

with

\(K_{21}=K_{21}^{T}, K_{22}=K_{22}^{T}+K_{11}^{T}+K_{33}^{T}, K_{25}=K_{23}^{T}+K_{32}^{T}\)

 

\(K_{51}=K_{13}^{T}+K_{31}^{T}, K_{54}=K_{12}^{T}+K_{21}^{T}\)

 

\(K_{55}=2\left(K_{11}^{T}+K_{33}^{T}+K_{22}^{T}\right), K_{57}=K_{23}^{T}+K_{32}^{T}\)

The condensed equations are given by

\(\left[\begin{array}{rrr}2.0 & -1.0 & 0.0 \\-1.0 & 4.0 & -1.0 \\0.0 & -1.0 & 0.5\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}U_{2} \\U_{5} \\U_{7}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{r}1.0 \\0.0 \\18.0\end{array}\right\}+\left\{\begin{array}{l}1.0 \\2.0 \\1.0\end{array}\right\}\)

The solution of these equations is: \(U_{2}=9.25, U_{5}=8.5, U_{7}=4.75\).
