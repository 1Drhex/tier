Guest Question.

For the gear pair described in , calculate the forces on the pinion and the gear if they are transmitting 2.50 hp with a pinion speed of 600 rpm. The geometry factors computed in apply. The data are summarized here.

Summary of Pertinent Results from and Given Data
Number of teeth in the pinion: \(N_P = 16\)
Number of teeth in the gear: \(N_G = 48\)
Diametral pitch: \(P_d = 8\)
Pitch diameter of pinion: \(d = 2.000\) in
Pressure angle: \(\phi = 20°\)
Pinion pitch cone angle: \(γ = 18.43°\)
Gear pitch cone angle: \(Γ = 71.57°\)
Face width: \(F = 1.00\) in
Rotational speed of pinion: \(n_P = 600\) rpm
Power transmitted: \(P = 2.50\) hp

Admin
Solution

Forces on the pinion are described by the following equations:
\(W_t = T/r_m\)
But
\(T_p = 63 000(P)/n_P = [63 000(2.50)]/600 = 263\) lb . in
\(r_m = d/2 – (F/2)\) sin γ
\(r_m = (2.000/2) – (1.00/2)\) sin (18.43°) = 0.84 in

Then
\(W_t = T_P/r_m = 263\) lb . in/0.84 in = 313 lb
\(W_r = W_t\) tan \(\phi\) cos γ = 313 lb tan(20°) cos(18.43°) = 108 lb
\(W_x = W_t\) tan \(\phi\) sin γ = 313 lb tan(20°) sin(18.43°) = 36 lb
To determine the forces on the gear, first let’s calculate the rotational speed of the gear:
\(n_G = n_P(N_P /N_G) = 600\) rpm(16/48) = 200 rpm

Then
\(T_G = 63 000(2.50)/200 = 788\) lb . in
\(R_m = D/2 – (F/2)\) sin Γ
\(R_m = 6.000/2 – (1.00/2)\) sin\((71.57°) = 2.53\) in
\(W_t = T_G/R_m = (788\) lb . in\()/(2.53 in) = 313\) lb
\(W_r = W_t\) tan \(\phi\) cos \(Γ = 313\) lb tan(20°) cos(71.57°) = 36 lb
\(W_x = W_t\) tan \(\phi\) sin \(Γ = 313\) lb tan(20°) sin(71.57°) = 108 lb
