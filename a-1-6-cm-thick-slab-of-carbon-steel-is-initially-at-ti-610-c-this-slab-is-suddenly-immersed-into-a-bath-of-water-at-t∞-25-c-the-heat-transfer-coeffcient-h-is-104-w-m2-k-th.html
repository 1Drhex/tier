Guest Question.

A 1.6 cm thick slab of carbon steel is initially at \(T_i=610^\circ C\). This slab is suddenly immersed into a bath of water at \(T_\infty =25^\circ C\). The heat transfer coeffcient h is \(10^4W/m^2K\) . The properties of a carbon steel are k = 40 W/m K, and \(\alpha =1\times 10^5cm^2/s\) .
1. Determine the time t at which the temperature in the mid-plane of the slab drops to \(T_c=100^\circ C\). Also determine the average temperature of the slab corresponding to this time.
2. Calculate the corresponding temperature in a plane situated 0.2 cm from one of the ends.

Admin
Solution

The problem is solved in both the methods, i.e., through the analytical method and by using the Heisler’s charts.

\(Bi=\frac{hL_c}{k} \)

\(L_c=t/2\) for a plate \(=\frac{1.6\times 10^{-2}}{2} =0.008m\)

 

\(Bi=\frac{10^4\times 0.008}{40} =2\gg 0.1\)

Since \(Bi\gg 0.1\) the lumped capacitance method is not applicable.

Solution through the analytical method:

From the analytical solution, the frst term approximation for Eq. (3.92)

\(\frac{\theta }{\theta _i} =\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty }{\frac{4\sin (\xi _{_{n}})}{2\xi _{_{n}}+\sin (2\xi _{_{n}})} e^{-\xi^ {2}_{_{n}}Fo} \cos (\lambda _nx)} \) (3.92)

is given as

\(\frac{\phi }{\phi _i} =\frac{4\sin (\xi _{_{n}})}{2\xi _{_{n}}+\sin (2\xi _{_{n}})} e^{\xi^ {2}_{_{n}}Fo} \cos (\lambda _nx)\)

a. For mid-plane x = 0, so the frst term approximation for Eq. (3.92) becomes

\(\frac{\phi_{_{x=0}} }{\phi _i} =\frac{4\sin (\xi _{_{n}})}{2\xi _{_{n}}+\sin (2\xi _{_{n}})} e^{-\xi^ {2}_{_{n}}Fo} \)

From Eq. (3.88)

\(\xi \tan \xi =Bi\) (Where \(\xi =\lambda L\)) (3.88)

 

\(\xi \tan \xi =Bi\)

 

\(\xi \tan \xi =2\)

 

\(\tan \xi =\frac{2}{\xi } \)

 

\(\xi =\tan ^{-1}\left\lgroup\frac{2}{\xi } \right\rgroup \)

Solving for ξ using the successive substitution method(i.e., iteratively),

\( \xi =1.076\)

 

\(\frac{\phi _{_{t,x=0}}}{\phi _{i}}=\frac{T_{_{(t,x=0)}}-T_\infty }{T_i-T_\infty } =\frac{100-25}{610-25}=0.1282 \)

 

\(\Rightarrow 0.1282=\frac{4\times \sin \left\lgroup1.076\times \frac{180}{\pi } \right\rgroup }{2\times 1.076+\sin \left\lgroup2\times 1.076\times \frac{180}{\pi } \right\rgroup } \times e^{-\xi ^2Fo}\)

 

\(\Rightarrow 0.1282=1.178\times e^{-\xi ^2Fo}\)

 

\(\Rightarrow e^{-\xi ^2Fo}=\frac{0.1282}{1.178}=0.1086 \)

 

\(\Rightarrow -\xi ^2Fo=-2.22 \)

 

\(\Rightarrow Fo=1.917\)

 

\(\Rightarrow \frac{\alpha t}{L^{^2}_{{c}}} =1.917\)

 

\(\Rightarrow t=\frac{1.917\times (0.008)^2}{0.1\times 10^{-4}} \)

 

\(\therefore t=12.27s\)

From eq. 3.103 we know that

\(\frac{Q}{Q_{_{max}}}=1-\frac{1}{L}A_1e^{-\xi ^2Fo} \left[\frac{\sin \left\lgroup\xi \frac{x}{L} \right\rgroup }{\frac{\xi }{L} } \right]^L_0 \)

 

\(\therefore \frac{Q}{Q_{_{max}}}=1-A_1e^{-\xi ^2Fo} \frac{\sin (\xi )}{\xi } \)

 

\(\frac{T_{_{avg}}-T_\infty }{T_i-T_\infty } =1- \frac{Q}{Q_{_{max}}}=Ae^{-\xi ^2Fo} \frac{\sin (\xi )}{\xi }\)

 

\(\frac{T_{_{avg}}-T_\infty }{T_i-T_\infty } =0.1272\times \frac{\sin \left\lgroup1.076\times \frac{180}{pi} \right\rgroup }{1.076} \)

 

\(T_{_{avg}}=T_\infty +0.1041\times (T_i-T_\infty )\)

 

\(=25+0.1041\times (610-25)=85.59^\circ C\)

 

\(\therefore T_{_{avg}}=85.89^\circ C\)

b. Temperature in a plane situated 0.2 cm from the cooled surface of the plate (i.e.,temperature in a plane at x = 0.006 m).

\(\frac{\phi _{t,x}} {\phi _i} =\frac{4\sin (\xi _{_n})}{2\xi _{_{n}}+\sin (2\xi _{_{n}})} \cos \left\lgroup\xi \times \frac{x}{L} \right\rgroup e^{-\xi ^{^{2}}_{_{n}}Fo}\)

 

\(F_{_0}=1.917\), \(\xi =1.076\), \(x=0.006\) , \(t=12.27s\frac{x}{L}=\frac{0.006}{0.008}=0.75\)

 

\(\frac{\phi _{_{x,t}}}{\phi _{_t}} =\frac{4\sin \left\lgroup1.076\times \frac{180}{pi} \right\rgroup }{2\times 1.076+\sin \left\lgroup2\times 1.076\times \frac{180}{pi} \right\rgroup } \)

 

\(\times \cos \left\lgroup1.076\times \frac{180}{pi}\times 0.75 \right\rgroup e^{-1.076^2\times 1.917}\)

 

\(=1.178\times 0.691\times 0.108=0.0879\)

 

\(\frac{\phi _{_{x,t}}}{\phi _{_{i}}} =\frac{T(x,t)-T_\infty }{T_i-T_\infty } =0.0879\)

 

\(\therefore T(x,t)=76.42^\circ C\)

Solution using Heisler charts:

\(\phi =\frac{T-T_\infty }{T_i-T_\infty } \)

 

\(\phi =\frac{100-25}{610-25}=0.1282 \)

 

\(\frac{1}{Bi}=\frac{k}{hL_c} =0.5\)

From the Heisler charts the centerline temperature chart for the case of a plane wall (or plate) is given by

\(\frac{\alpha t}{L^2} =2\)

 

\(\therefore t=\frac{2\times 0.008^2}{0.1\times 10^{-4}} \)

 

\(t=12.8s\)

 

\(Bi^2\times Fo=\frac{h^2\alpha t}{k^2} \)

 

\(=\frac{(10^4)^2\times 10^{-4}\times 12.8}{40^2} =8\)

 

\(\therefore \phi _{_{avg}}=\frac{T_{_{avg}}-T_\infty }{T_i-T_\infty } =1-\frac{Q}{Q_{_{max}}} \)

From Grober charts:

\(\frac{Q}{Q_{_{max}}} =0.875\)

 

\(Q_{_{avg}}=1-0.875=0.125\)

 

\(\therefore T_{_{avg}}=0.125\times (610-25)+25\)

 

\(=98.125^\circ C\)

The temperature at 0.2 cm from one of the cooled surfaces of the plate is determined as

\(\frac{x}{L}=\frac{0.008-0.002}{0.002} \)

 

\(\therefore \phi =\frac{T_{_{(0.006.t)}}-T_\infty }{T_o-T_\infty } =0.65\)

 

\(\therefore T_{_{(0.006.t)}}=0.65\times (T_o-T_\infty )+T_\infty \)

 

\(=0.65\times (100-25)+25\)

 

\(=73.75^\circ C\)
