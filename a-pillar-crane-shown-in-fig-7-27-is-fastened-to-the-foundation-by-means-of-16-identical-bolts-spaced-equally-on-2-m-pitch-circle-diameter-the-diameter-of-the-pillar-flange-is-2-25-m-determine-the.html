Guest Question.

A pillar crane, shown in Fig. 7.27, is fastened to the foundation by means of 16 identical bolts spaced equally on 2 m pitch circle diameter. The diameter of the pillar flange is 2.25 m. Determine the size of the bolts if a load of 50 kN acts at a radius of 7.5 m from the axis of the crane.
The maximum permissible tensile stress in the bolt is limited to 75 N/mm².

Admin
Solution

Given 2a = 2250 mm 2b = 2000 mm P = 50 kN

\( r=7500 mm \quad\left(\sigma_{t}\right)_{\max }=75 N / mm ^{2} \).

Step I Absolute maximum force on bolt
As shown in Fig. 7.27, the tendency of the pillar crane is to tilt about the point C under the action of the eccentric load P. Therefore, the moment arm l for load P about the point C is (r–a). Or

l = r – a = 7500 – 1125 = 6375 mm
The pillar crane can rotate about its axis and the location of P will change with respect to the bolts.
Therefore, the bolts are to be designed for absolute maximum value of force.
From Eq. (7.16),

\( P_{\max }=\frac{2 P l(a+b)}{n\left(2 a^{2}+b^{2}\right)} \) (7.16).

\( P_{\max .}=\frac{2 P l(a+b)}{n\left(2 a^{2}+b^{2}\right)} \).

\( =\frac{2\left(50 \times 10^{3}\right)(6375)(1125+1000)}{16\left(2 \times 1125^{2}+1000^{2}\right)} \).

= 23 976.77 N
Step II Size of cap screw
The bolts are subjected to tensile stress. Therefore,

\( A\left(\sigma_{t}\right)_{\max }=P_{\max } \quad A(75)=23976.77 \).

∴ A = 319.69 mm²
From Table 7.1, the standard size of the bolts is M24 (A = 353 mm²).
