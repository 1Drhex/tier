Guest Question.

Two surge tanks are placed in series so that the exit flow from the top tank flows into the lower tank, as shown in Fig. 4.4. If an exit flow rate is proportional to liquid level (or head) in that tank, derive the transfer function that relates changes in the exit flow rate \(q_{2}\) of the lower tank to changes in the inlet flow rate to the top tank \(q_{i}\). Show how this overall transfer function, \(Q^{\prime}_{2}(s)/Q^{\prime}_{i}(s)\), is related to the individual transfer functions, \( H^{\prime}_{1}(s)/Q^{\prime}_{i}(s), \space Q^{\prime}_{1}(s)/H^{\prime}_{1}(s), \space H^{\prime}_{2}(s) /Q^{\prime}_{1}(s), \ and \space Q^{\prime}_{2}(s)/ H^{\prime}_{2}(s)\). \( H^{\prime}_{1}(s) \ and \ H^{\prime}_{2}(s)\) denote the Laplace transforms of the deviations in Tank 1 and Tank 2 levels, respectively. Assume that the two tanks have cross-sectional areas, \(A_{1} \ and \ A_{2}\), and valve resistances, R1 and R2, respectively.

Admin
Solution

Equations
\(q=\frac{1}{R_{v}}h \)
and
\(A\frac{dh}{dt}=q_{i}-\frac{1}{R_{v}}h \)
are valid for each tank; for Tank 1,
\(A_{1}\frac{dh_{1}}{dt}=q_{i}-q_{1}\) (1)
\(q_{1}=\frac{1}{R_{1}}h_{1} \) (2)
Substituting Equation (2) into Equation (1) eliminates \(q_{1}\):
\(A_{1}\frac{dh_{1}}{dt}=q_{i}-\frac{1}{R_{1}}h_{1} \) (3)
Putting Equation (3) and Equation (2) into deviation variable form gives
\(A_{1}\frac{dh^{\prime}_{1}}{dt}=q^{\prime}_{i}-\frac{1}{R_{1}}h^{\prime}_{1} \) (4)
\( q^{\prime}_{1}=\frac{1}{R_{1}} h^{\prime}_{1}\) (5)
Transfer function \(H^{\prime}_{1}(s)/Q^{\prime}_{1i}(s)\) is obtained by Laplace transforming Equation (4) and rearranging to obtain
\(\frac{H^{\prime}_{1}(s)}{Q^{\prime}_{1}(s)}=\frac{R_{1}}{A_{1}R_{1}s+1}=\frac{K_{1}}{\tau _{1}s+1} \) (6)
where \(K_{1}\triangleq R_{1} \space and \space \tau_{1}\triangleq A_{1}R_{1} \). Similarly, the transfer function relating \(Q^{\prime}_{1}(s) \space to \space H^{\prime}_{1}(s)\) is obtained by Laplace transforming Equation (5).
\(\frac{Q^{\prime}_{1}(s)}{H ^{\prime}_{1}(s)}=\frac{1}{R_{1}}=\frac{1}{K_{1}} \) (7)
The same procedure leads to the corresponding transfer functions for Tank 2,
\( \frac{H^{\prime}_{2}(s)}{Q ^{\prime}_{1}(s)}=\frac{R_{2}}{A_{2}R_{2}s+1}=\frac{K_{2}}{\tau _{2}s+1} \) (8)

\(\frac{Q^{\prime}_{2}(s)}{H ^{\prime}_{2}(s)}=\frac{1}{R_{2}}=\frac{1}{K_{2}} \) (9)
where \(K_{2}\triangleq R_{2} \space and \space \tau_{2}\triangleq A_{2}R_{2} \). The overall transfer function relating the outflow from Tank 2 to the inflow to Tank 1 can be derived by forming the product of Equation (6) through Equation (9).
\(\frac{Q^{\prime}_{2}(s)}{Q^{\prime}_{i}(s)} =\frac{Q^{\prime}_{2}(s)}{H^{\prime}_{2}(s)} \frac{H^{\prime}_{2}(s)}{Q^{\prime}_{1}(s)} \frac{Q^{\prime}_{1}(s)}{H^{\prime}_{1}(s)} \frac{H^{\prime}_{1}(s)}{Q^{\prime}_{i}(s)} \) (10)
or
\(\frac{Q^{\prime}_{2}(s)}{Q^{\prime}_{i}(s)} =\frac{1}{K_{2}}\frac{K_{2}}{\tau _{2}s+1}\frac{1}{K_{1}}\frac{K_{1}}{\tau _{1}s+1} \) (11)
which can be simplified to yield
\(\frac{Q^{\prime}_{2}(s)}{Q^{\prime}_{i}(s)} =\frac{1}{(\tau _{1}s+1)(\tau _{2}s+1)}\) (12)
which is a second-order transfer function (does the unity gain make sense on physical grounds?). Figure 4.5 is a block diagram showing the information flow for this system.
