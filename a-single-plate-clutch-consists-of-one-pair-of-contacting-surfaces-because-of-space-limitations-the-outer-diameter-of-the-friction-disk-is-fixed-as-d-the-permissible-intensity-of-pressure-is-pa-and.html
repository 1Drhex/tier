Guest Question.

A single plate clutch consists of one pair of contacting surfaces. Because of space limitations, the outer diameter of the friction disk is fixed as D. The permissible intensity of pressure is \(p_{a}\) and the coefficient of friction, μ. Assuming uniform wear theory, plot the variation of the torque transmitting capacity against the ratio of diameters (d/D). Show that the torque transmitting capacity of the clutch is maximum, when (d/D) is equal to 0.577.

Admin
Solution

Step I Variation of torque capacity against the ratio (d/D)

\( \frac{d}{D}=x \) (a).

From Eq. (11.7),

\( M_{t}=\frac{\pi \mu p_{a} d}{8}\left(D^{2}-d^{2}\right) \) (11.7).

\( M_{t}=\frac{\pi \mu P_{a} D}{8}\left(D^{2}-d^{2}\right) \) (b).

Substituting Eq. (a) in Eq. (b), we have

\( M_{t}=\frac{\pi \mu P_{a} D^{3}}{8}\left[x\left(1-x^{2}\right)\right] \) (c).

For maximum torque capacity,

\( \frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left(M_{t}\right)=0 \quad \text { or } \quad \frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left[x\left(1-x^{2}\right)\right]=0 \).

\( \therefore 1-3 x^{2}=0 \).

\( \therefore x=\sqrt{\frac{1}{3}}=0.577 \) (d).

Equation (c) is rearranged in the following form:

\( \frac{8 M_{t}}{\pi \mu p_{a} D^{3}}=x\left(1-x^{2}\right) \).

The left hand side is called the torque characteristic. The variation of the torque characteristic against x is shown in Fig. 11.6.

Step II Optimum ratio
From (d), the condition for maximum torque capacity is given by

\( \frac{d}{D}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{3}}=0.577 \).

\( \frac{D}{d}=\sqrt{3}=1.732 \).

Therefore, it is a good practice to use (D/d) ratio between 1.5 and 2.
