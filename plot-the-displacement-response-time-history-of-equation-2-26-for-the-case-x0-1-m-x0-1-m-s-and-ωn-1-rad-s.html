Guest Question.

Plot the displacement response time history of Equation 2.26 for the case\(x\left(t\right)=x\left(0\right)\cos\omega _{n} t+\frac{\dot{x} \left(0\right)}{\omega _{n}} \sin \omega _{n}t\)for the case \(x\left(0\right)=1 m,\dot{x} \left(0\right)=1 m/s, and \omega _{n} =1 rad/s\)

Admin
Solution

From \(\omega _{n} \) the cyclic natural frequency is \(f_{n} =\omega _{n}/2\pi =0.159 Hz \) Hz and thus the period is \(T=1/f_{n}= 2\pi s or 6.28 s \). The response is of the form

\(x\left(t\right) =C_{1} \cos t+C_{2}\sin t \)

Applying initial conditions, we find

\(x\left(t\right) =1 \cos t+1\sin t m\).

If we wish to express the response in terms of cosine with an amplitude and phase such that

\(x\left(t\right)= A \cos \left(t-\phi \right)\),

we can use the cosine sum identity given by

\(A \cos \left(t-\phi \right) =A \cos \phi \cos t+ A\sin\phi \sin t\).

Comparing the two expressions for the response, we find
that

\(A \cos \phi =1 and \sin\phi =1\),

or

\(A=\sqrt{2} \) and \(\tan \phi =1 \).

We need to take care that the phase angle is in the correct quadrant. The quadrant where cos ∅ is positive and sin ∅ is positive is the first quadrant, and thus the correct phase angle is

\(\phi =\pi /4 rad\).

The response is given by

\(x\left(t\right)=\sqrt{2} \cos \left(t-\pi /4\right)m\).

It is possible to express the response in terms of a sine function with an amplitude and phase. Using the sine sum formula, the response is given by

\(x\left(t\right)=1\cos t+ \sin t=A \sin \left(t-\phi \right) =A\cos \phi \sin t- A \sin\phi \cos t\).

Then,

\(A\cos \phi=1\) and \(A \sin\phi= -1\)

We find

\(A=\sqrt{2}\) and \(\phi =-\pi /4 rad\).

The response is given by

\(x\left(t\right)=\sqrt{2} \sin\left(t+\pi /4\right) m\).

The response is graphed in Figure 2.17.

\(m\frac{dv}{dt} =-v_{rel} \frac{dm}{dt}\)

The initial conditions are satisfied as indicated by the displacement and slope at t = 0. There is no decay in the solution since there is no damping.
