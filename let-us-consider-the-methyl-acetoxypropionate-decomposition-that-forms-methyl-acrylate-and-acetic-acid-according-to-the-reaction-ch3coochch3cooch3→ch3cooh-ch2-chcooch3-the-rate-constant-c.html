Guest Question.

Let us consider the methyl acetoxypropionate decomposition that forms methyl acrylate and acetic acid according to the reaction:
\(CH_{3}COOCH(CH_{3})COOCH_{3}→CH_{3}COOH + CH_{2} =CHCOOCH_{3}\)
The rate constant can be calculated by:
\(k=7.8 × 10^{9} · exp ( − 38, 200/(RT)) s^{−1}\)
This gas-phase reaction was carried out in a reactor with high gas mixing, aiming to obtain a perfect mixture. One introduces 250 kg/h of reactant with 20% inert, at 600◦C and 4 atm. Calculate the reactor volume, the space time and compare it with the mean residence time. The final conversion should be 65%.

Admin
Solution

 

Reaction: A→R + S
According to the reaction stoichiometry 1:2 reactant: products, the gas will expand. Since the unit of rate constant is per second, we have a first-order reaction (irreversible). Therefore, the rate will be:
\((−r_{A})=7 × 8 × 10^{9} exp (−38, 200/(RT))C_{A}\) (14.32)

where:
\(C_{A}\) =reactant concentration \([CH_{3}COOCH(CH_{3})COOCH_{3}] mol/L\)
But the reaction volume varies, then:
\(C_{A}=\frac{C_{A0}(1-X_{A})}{(1+ \varepsilon _{A}X_{A})}\)

By CSTR equation:
\(\tau =C_{A0}\frac{X_{A}}{7\times 8\times 10^{9}exp(_38, 200/(RT))\frac{C_{A0}(1-X_{A})}{(1+ \varepsilon _{A}X_{A})}}\) (14.33)

Calculating \(\varepsilon _{A}\):

A → R + S inert total
0.8 0 0 0.2 1
0 0.8 0.8 0.2 1.8

Then:
\(\varepsilon _{A}\) =0.80

Substituting into Equation 14.33, with \(X_{A} =0.65\), we obtain:
\(\tau =1.43 s\)

The mean residence time can be calculated by :
\(\bar{t}=\frac{V_{reactor}}{\nu _{output}}=\frac{\tau }{(1+\varepsilon _{A}X_{A})}=0.94 s\)

The space time is different from the mean residence time due to the volume variation of gas during reaction.
