Guest Question.

The following unbalanced line-to-ground voltages are applied to the balanced-Y load shown in Figure 3.3: \(V_{a g}=100 \angle 0^{\circ}, V_{b g}=75 \angle 180^{\circ}\), and \(V_{c g}=50 \angle 90^{\circ}\) volts. The Y load has \(Z_{ Y }=3+j 4 \Omega\) per phase with neutral impedance \(Z_{n}=j 1 \Omega \text {. }\) (a) Calculate the line currents \(I_{a}, I_{b}, \text { and } I_{c}\) without using symmetrical components, (b) Calculate the line currents \(I_{a}, I_{b}, \text { and } I_{c}\) using symmetrical components. Which method is easier?

Admin
Solution

writing KVL equations [see Eqs (8.2.1) – (8.2.3)]:

\(\begin{gathered}\bar{V}_{a g}=\bar{Z}_{y} \bar{I}_{a}+\bar{Z}_{n}\left(\bar{I}_{a}+\bar{I}_{b}+\bar{I}_{c}\right) \\\bar{V}_{b g}=\bar{Z}_{y} \bar{I}_{b}+\bar{Z}_{n}\left(\bar{I}_{a}+\bar{I}_{b}+\bar{I}_{c}\right) \\\bar{V}_{c g}=\bar{Z}_{y} \bar{I}_{c}+\bar{Z}_{n}\left(\bar{I}_{a}+\bar{I}_{b}+\bar{I}_{c}\right)\end{gathered}\)

In matrix format [see Eq (8.2.4)]

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc}\left(\bar{Z}_{y}+\bar{Z}_{n}\right) & \bar{Z}_{n} & \bar{Z}_{n} \\\bar{Z}_{n} & \left(\bar{Z}_{y}+\bar{Z}_{n}\right) & \bar{Z}_{n} \\\bar{Z}_{n} & \bar{Z}_{n} &\left(\bar{Z}_{y}+\bar{Z}_{n}\right)\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{a} \\\bar{I}_{b} \\\bar{I}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}\bar{V}_{a g} \\\bar{V}_{b g} \\\bar{V}_{c g}\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{c|c|c}(3+j 5) & j_{1} & j_{1} \\\hline j_{1} & (3+j 5) & j_{1} \\\hline j_{1} & j_{1} & (3+j 5)\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{a} \\\bar{I}_{b} \\\bar{I}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}100 \angle 0^{\circ} \\75 \angle 180^{\circ} \\50 \angle 90^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{a} \\\bar{I}_{b} \\\bar{I}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c|c|c}(3+j 5) & j_{1} & j_{1} \\\hline j_{1} & (3+j 5) & j_{1} \\\hline j_{1} & j_{1} & (3+j 5)\end{array}\right]^{-1}\left[\begin{array}{c}100 \angle 0^{\circ} \\75 \angle 180^{\circ} \\50 \angle 90^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\)

 

Performing the indicated matrix inverse (a computer solution is suggested):

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{a} \\\bar{I}_{b} \\\bar{I}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}0.1763 \angle-56.50^{\circ} & 0.02618 \angle 150.2^{\circ} & 0.02618 \angle 150.2^{\circ} \\0.02618 \angle 150.2^{\circ} & 0.1763 \angle-56.50^{\circ} & 0.02618 \angle 150.2^{\circ} \\0.02618 \angle 150.2^{\circ} & 0.02618 \angle 150.2^{\circ} & 0.1763 \angle-56.50^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}100 \angle 0^{\circ} \\75 \angle 180^{\circ} \\50 \angle 90^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\)

 

Finally, performing the indicated matrix multiplication:

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{a} \\\bar{I}_{b} \\\bar{I}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}17.63 \angle-56.50^{\circ}+1.964 \angle 330.2^{\circ}+1.309 \angle 240.2^{\circ} \\2.618 \angle 150.2^{\circ}+13.22 \angle 123.5^{\circ}+1.309 \angle 240.2^{\circ} \\2.618 \angle 150.2^{\circ}+1.964 \angle 330.2^{\circ}+8.815 \angle 33.5^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{a} \\\bar{I}_{b} \\\bar{I}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}10.78-j 16.81 \\-10.22+j 11.19 \\6.783+j 5.191\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}19.97 \angle-57.32^{\circ} \\15.15 \angle 132.4^{\circ} \\8.541 \angle 37.43^{\circ}\end{array}\right] A\)

 

(b) Step (1): Calculate the sequence components of the applied voltage:

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{V}_{g 0} \\\bar{V}_{g 1} \\\bar{V}_{g 2}\end{array}\right]=\frac{1}{3}\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a & a^{2} \\1 & a^{2} & a\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}100 \angle 0^{\circ} \\75 \angle 180^{\circ} \\50 \angle 90^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{3}\left[\begin{array}{c}100 \angle 0^{\circ}+75 \angle 180^{\circ}+50 \angle 90^{\circ} \\100 \angle 0^{\circ}+75 \angle 300^{\circ}+50 \angle 330^{\circ} \\100 \angle 0^{\circ}+75 \angle 60^{\circ}+50 \angle 210^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(=\left[\begin{array}{c}8.333+j 16.667 \\60.27-j 29.98 \\31.40+j 13.32\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}18.63 \angle 63.43^{\circ} \\67.32 \angle-26.45 \\34.11 \angle 22.99^{\circ}\end{array}\right] V\)

 

Step (2): Draw sequence networks:

 

Step (3): Solve sequence networks

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{I}_{0}=\frac{\bar{V}_{g_{0}}}{\bar{Z}_{0}}=\frac{\bar{V}_{g_{0}}}{\bar{Z}_{y}+3 \bar{Z}_{n}}=\frac{18.63 \angle 63.43^{\circ}}{3+j 7}=\frac{18.63 \angle 63.43^{\circ}}{7.616 \angle 66.80^{\circ}} \\&\bar{I}_{0}=2.446 \angle-3.37^{\circ} A \\&\bar{I}_{1}=\frac{\bar{V}_{g_{1}}}{\bar{Z}_{1}}=\frac{67.32 \angle-26.45^{\circ}}{3+j 4}=\frac{67.32 \angle-26.45^{\circ}}{5 \angle 53.13^{\circ}}=13.46 \angle-79.58 A \\&\bar{I}_{2}=\frac{\bar{V}_{g 2}}{\bar{Z}_{2}}=\frac{34.11 \angle 22.99^{\circ}}{5 \angle 53.13^{\circ}}=6.822 \angle-30.14^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

 

Step (4): Calculate the line currents (phase components):

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{a} \\\bar{I}_{b} \\\bar{I}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a^{2} & a \\1 & a & a^{2}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}2.446 \angle-3.37^{\circ} \\13.46 \angle-79.58^{\circ} \\6.822 \angle-30.14^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(=\left[\begin{array}{l}2.446 \angle-3.37^{\circ}+13.46 \angle-79.58^{\circ}+6.822 \angle-30.14^{\circ} \\2.446 \angle-3.37^{\circ}+13.46 \angle 160.42^{\circ}+6.822 \angle 89.86^{\circ} \\2.446 \angle-3.37^{\circ}+13.46 \angle 40.42^{\circ}+6.822 \angle 209.86^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(=\left[\begin{array}{c}10.78-j 16.81 \\-10.22+j 11.19 \\6.773+j 5.187\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}19.97 \angle-57.32^{\circ} \\15.15 \angle 132.4^{\circ} \\8.531 \angle 37.45^{\circ}\end{array}\right] A\)
