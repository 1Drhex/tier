Guest Question.

The load L is being hoisted by the pulley and cable arrangement shown. Each cable is wrapped securely around its respective pulley so it does not slip. The two pulleys to which L is attached are fastened together to form a single rigid body. Calculate the velocity and acceleration of the load L and the corresponding angular velocity ω and angular acceleration α of the double pulley under the following conditions:

Case (a) Pulley 1: \(\omega_{1}=\dot{\omega}_{1}=0\) (pulley at rest)

Pulley 2: \(\omega_{2}=2 rad / sec , \alpha_{2}=\dot{\omega}_{2}=-3 rad / sec ^{2}\)

Case (b) Pulley 1: \(\omega_{1}=1 rad / sec , \alpha_{1}=\dot{\omega}_{1}=4 rad / sec ^{2}\)

Pulley 2: \(\omega_{2}=2 rad / sec , \alpha_{2}=\dot{\omega}_{2}=-2 rad / sec ^{2}\)

Admin
Solution

The tangential displacement, velocity, and acceleration of a point on the rim of pulley 1 or 2 equal the corresponding vertical motions of point A or B since the cables are assumed to be inextensible.

Case (a). With A momentarily at rest, line AB rotates to AB′ through the angle dθ during time dt. From the diagram we see that the displacements and their time derivatives give

 

1 \(d s_{B}=\overline{A B} d \theta \quad v_{B}=\overline{A B} \omega \quad\left(a_{B}\right)_{t}=\overline{A B} \alpha\)

 

\(d s_{O}=\overline{A O} d \theta \quad v_{O}=\overline{A O} \omega \quad a_{O}=\overline{A O} \alpha\)

 

With \(v_{D}=r_{2} \omega_{2}=4(2)=8 \text { in./sec and } a_{D}=r_{2} \alpha_{2}=4(-3)=-12 \text { in./sec }^{2}\), we have for the angular motion of the double pulley

 

2 \(\omega=v_{B} / \overline{A B}=v_{D} / \overline{A B}=8 / 12=2 / 3 rad / sec ( CCW )\) Ans.

 

\(\alpha=\left(a_{B}\right)_{t} / \overline{A B}=a_{D} / \overline{A B}=-12 / 12=-1 rad / sec ^{2}( CW )\) Ans.

 

The corresponding motion of O and the load L is

 

3 \(v_{O}=\overline{A O} \omega=4(2 / 3)=8 / 3 in . / sec\) Ans.

 

\(a_{O}=\overline{A O} \alpha=4(-1)=-4 in . / sec ^{2}\) Ans.

 

Case (b). With point C, and hence point A, in motion, line AB moves to A′B′ during time dt. From the diagram for this case, we see that the displacements and their time derivatives give

 

\(\begin{aligned}d s_{B}-d s_{A} &=\overline{A B} d \theta & v_{B}-v_{A} &=\overline{A B} \omega &\left(a_{B}\right)_{t}-\left(a_{A}\right)_{t} &=\overline{A B} \alpha \\d s_{O}-d s_{A} &=\overline{A O} d \theta & v_{O}-v_{A} &=\overline{A O} \omega & a_{O}-\left(a_{A}\right)_{t} &=\overline{A O} \alpha\end{aligned}\)

 

With \(\begin{array}{ll}v_{C}=r_{1} \omega_{1}=4(1)=4 \text { in./sec } & v_{D}=r_{2} \omega_{2}=4(2)=8 \text { in./sec } \\a_{C}=r_{1} \alpha_{1}=4(4)=16 \text { in./sec }^{2} & a_{D}=r_{2} \alpha_{2}=4(-2)=-8 \text { in./sec }^{2}\end{array}\)

 

we have for the angular motion of the double pulley

 

4 \(\omega=\frac{v_{B}-v_{A}}{\overline{A B}}=\frac{v_{D}-v_{C}}{\overline{A B}}=\frac{8-4}{12}=1 / 3\) rad/sec (CCW) Ans.

 

\(\alpha=\frac{\left(a_{B}\right)_{t}-\left(a_{A}\right)_{t}}{\overline{A B}}=\frac{a_{D}-a_{C}}{\overline{A B}}=\frac{-8-16}{12}=-2 rad / sec ^{2}( CW )\) Ans.

 

The corresponding motion of O and the load L is

 

\(v_{O}=v_{A}+\overline{A O} \omega=v_{C}+\overline{A O} \omega=4+4(1 / 3)=16 / 3 in . / sec\) Ans.

 

\(a_{O}=\left(a_{A}\right)_{t}+\overline{A O} \alpha=a_{C}+\overline{A O} \alpha=16+4(-2)=8 \text { in./sec }^{2}\) Ans.

 

Helpful Hints

1 Recognize that the inner pulley is a wheel rolling along the fixed line of the left-hand cable. Thus, the expressions of hold.

2 Since B moves along a curved path, in addition to its tangential component of acceleration \(\left(a_{B}\right)_{t}\), it will also have a normal component of acceleration toward O which does not affect the angular acceleration of the pulley.

3 The diagrams show these quantities and the simplicity of their linear relationships. The visual picture of the motion of O and B as AB rotates through the angle dθ should clarify the analysis.

4 Again, as in case (a), the differential rotation of line AB as seen from the figure establishes the relation between the angular velocity of the pulley and the linear velocities of points A, O, and B. The negative sign for \(\left(a_{B}\right)_{t}=a_{D}\) produces the acceleration diagram shown but does not destroy the linearity of the relationships.
