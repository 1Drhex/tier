Guest Question.

Calculate the frequencies of free vibration and find the corresponding mode shapes for pinned-pinned beam. The beam has length l , mass per unit length m, modulus of elasticity E, and moment of inertia of cross-sectional area I .

Admin
Solution

The shape of vibration may be presented in form (14.19).

\(X(x)= A\cosh k x +B\sinh k x +C\cos k x +D\sin k x,\) (14.19)

For pinned-pinned beam displacement and bending moment at x = 0 and at x = l equal zero. Expression for bending moment is

\(X^″(x) = k^2 (A \cosh kx + B \sinh kx – C \cos kx – D \sin kx) .\)

 

Conditions X(0) = 0 and \(X^″(0) =0\) leads to the equations

A +C = 0

A -C =0

Thus A = C = 0.

Conditions X(l) = 0 and \(X^″(l) =0\) leads to the equations

B sinh kl + D sin kl = 0

B sinh kl – D sin kl = 0

Thus B = 0 and D sin kl = 0. Non-trivial solution occurs, if sin kl = 0. This is frequency equation. Solution of this equation is kl = π, 2π,. . . Thus, the frequencies of vibration are

\(\omega =k^2\sqrt{a} \frac{EI}{m}, \omega _1 =\frac{3.1416^2}{l^2}\sqrt{\frac{EI}{m} }, \omega _2 =\frac{6.2832^2}{l^2}\sqrt{\frac{EI}{m} }\)

 

The mode shape of vibration is

\(X_i (x) = D \sin k_ix =D\sin\frac{i\pi }{l}x, i=1,2,3,. . .\)
