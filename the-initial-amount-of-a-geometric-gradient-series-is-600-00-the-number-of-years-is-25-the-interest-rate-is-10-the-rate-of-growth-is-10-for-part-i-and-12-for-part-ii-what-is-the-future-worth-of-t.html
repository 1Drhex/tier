Guest Question.

The initial amount of a geometric gradient series is $600.00, the number of years is 25, the interest rate is 10%, the rate of growth is 10% for Part I, and 12% for Part II. What is the future worth of the gradient? Figure 5.18 is the cash flow diagram for for the growth rate of 10% and Figure 5.19 is the cash flow diagram for the growth rate of 12%.

Admin
Solution

Part I

r=i

Use Equation 5.10

\(P_0 =\frac{C}{1+i}\left[\frac{(1+w)^n-1}{w} \right]\) \(P/C\Rightarrow \infty \) (5.10)

to calculate the present worth of the geometric gradient at time 0 and then calculate the future worth at year 25:

\(P_0=\frac{C\times n}{(1+r)}=\frac{C\times n}{(1+i)}\)

 

\(=\frac{\$600.00 \times 25}{(1+0.10)}\)

 

\(=\frac{\$15,000.00}{(1.10)}\)

 

\(=\$13,636.36\)

 

\(F_{25}=P(F/P, i ,n)\)

 

\(=\$13,636.36(F/P ,10,25)\)

 

\(=\$13,636.36(10.834)\)

 

\(=\$147,736.32\)

Part II

r>i

Use Equation 5.9

\(G =P_0(i^2)\) (5.9)

to calculate the present worth of the geometric gradient series at time 0 and then calculate the future worth at 25 years:

\(w =\frac{1 +r}{1 +i}-1\)

 

\(w =\frac{1 +0.12}{1 +0.10}-1\)

 

\(w =\frac{1.12}{1.10}-1\)

 

\(=0.018182\)

 

\(P_0 =\frac{C}{1 + i}\left[\frac{(1+w)^n-1}{w} \right]\)

 

\(P_0 =\frac{\$600.00 }{(1 + 0.10)}\left[\frac{(1+0.018182)^{25}-1}{0.018182} \right]\)

 

\(=\$545.45\left\lgroup\frac{0.569045}{0.018182} \right\rgroup\)

 

\(=\$545.45(31.297)\)

 

\(=\$17,070.95\)

 

\(F_{25}=P(F/P, i , n)\)

 

\(=\$17,070.95(F/P ,10,25)\)

 

\(=\$17,071.04(10.834)\)

 

\(=\$184,946.66\)
