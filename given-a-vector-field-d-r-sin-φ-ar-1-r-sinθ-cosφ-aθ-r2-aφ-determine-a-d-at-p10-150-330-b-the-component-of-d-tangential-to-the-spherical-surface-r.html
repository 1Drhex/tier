Guest Question.

Given a vector field \(D=r\sin\phi a_{r}-\frac{1}{r}\sin\theta\cos\phi a_{\theta}+r^{2}a_{\phi}\) determine

(a) D at P(10, 150°, 330°)

(b) The component of D tangential to the spherical surface r = 10 at P

(c) A unit vector at P perpendicular to D and tangential to the cone θ = 150°

Admin
Solution

(a) At P, r = 10, θ = 150°, and φ = 330°. Hence

\(D=10\sin330^{\circ}a_{r}-\frac{1}{10}\sin 150^{\circ}\cos 330^{\circ}a_{\theta}+100a_{\phi}=\left(-5,-0.043,100\right)\)

(b) Any vector D can always be resolved into two orthogonal components:

\(D=D_{t}+D_{n}\)

where \(D_{t}\) is tangential to a given surface and \(D_{n}\) is normal to it. In our case, since \(a_{r}\) is normal to the surface r = 10

\(D_{n}=r\sin\phi a_{r}=-5a_{r}\)

Hence

\(D_{t}=D-D_{n}=-0.043a_{\theta}+100a_{\phi}\)

(c) A vector at P perpendicular to D and tangential to the cone θ = 150° is the same as the vector perpendicular to both D and \(a_{\theta }\). Hence

\(D\times a_{\theta}=\left | \begin{matrix} a_{r} & a_{\theta} & a_{\phi} \\ -5 & -0.043 & 100 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right | =-100a_{r}-5a_{\phi}\)

A unit vector along this is

\(a=\frac{-100a_{r}-5a_{\phi}}{\sqrt{100^{2}+5^{2}} }=-0.9988a_{r}-0.0499a_{\phi}\)
