Guest Question.

Figure 4.21 shows a toggle clamp used for securing a workpiece during a machining operation. Analytically determine the angle that the handle must be displaced in order to lift the clamp arm 30° clockwise. 1.Draw a Kinematic Diagram 2.Analyze the Geometry in the Original Configuration 3.Analyze the Geometry in the Displaced Configuration 4.Calculate the Resulting Displacement.

Admin
Solution

1: The kinematic diagram for the clamp is given in Figure 4.22a. The end of the handle was labeled as point of interest X. The clamp nose was identified as point of interest Y.

2: This mechanism is the common four-bar linkage. In order to more closely analyze the geometry, Figure 4.22b focuses on the kinematic chain ABCD. A diagonal is created by connecting B and D , forming two triangles.

Prior to observing the mechanism in a displaced configuration, all properties of the original configuration must be determined. Notice that the lower triangle, ABD, is a right triangle. The length of BD can be found using the Pythagorean theorem introduced in equation \(a^{2}+b^{2}=c^{2}\).

\(BD=\sqrt{(AB)^{2}+(AD)^{2}}=\sqrt{(12)^{2}+(25)^{2}}=27.73\ mm\)

The internal angles, ∠ABD and ∠BDA, can be determined from the following basic trigonometric functions:

\(\angle ABD=sin^{-1}(\frac{25\ mm}{27.73\ mm} )=64.4^{\circ }\)

\(\angle BDA=cos^{-1}(\frac{25\ mm}{27.73\ mm} )=25.6^{\circ }\)

Focusing on the top triangle, the internal angle ∠BCD can be found from the law of cosines, introduced in equation \(c^{2}=a^{2}+b^{2}-2ab\ cos\angle C\):

\(\angle BCD=cos^{-1}(\frac{BC^{2}+CD^{2}-BD^{2}}{2(BC)(CD)} )\)

 

\(=cos^{-1}(\frac{(20\ mm)^{2}+(15\ mm)^{2}-(27.73\ mm)^{2}}{2(20\ mm)\ (15\ mm)} )=103.9^{\circ }\)

The internal angle ∠CBD can be determined from the law of sines:

\(\angle CBD=sin^{-1}\left[(\frac{CD}{BD})sin\ \angle BCD )\right]\)

 

\(=sin^{-1}\left[(\frac{15\ mm}{27.73\ mm} )sin\ 103.9^{\circ }\right] =31.7^{\circ }\)

The interior angle at ∠BDC can be found because the sum of all interior angles in any triangle must total 180°. Thus

∠BDC = 180° – (103.9° + 31.7°) = 44.4°

The total mechanism angles at joint B (between links 2 and 3) and at joint D (between links 1 and 4) can be
determined.

At joint B:

∠ABC = ∠ABD + ∠CBD = 64.4° + 31.7° = 96.1°

At joint D:

∠CDA = ∠BDC + ∠BDA = 44.4° + 25.6° = 70.0°

3: The displaced configuration is shown in Figure 4.22c with the clamp nose, link 2, rotated clockwise 30°. Notice that this leaves the interior angle at joint A, ∠DAB’, as 60°. Also, the lower triangle is no longer a right triangle. The length of diagonal B’D can be found by using the lower triangle, ΔABD, and the law of cosines:

\(B'D=\sqrt{(12\ mm)^{2}+(25\ mm)^{2}-2(12\ mm)(25\ mm)\ cos\ 60^{\circ }} =21.66\ mm\)

The internal angle ∠AB’D can also be determined from the law of cosines:

\(\angle AB'D=cos^{-1}\frac{(AB)^{2}+(B'D)^{2}-(AD)^{2}}{2(AB')(B'D)}\)

 

\(=cos^{-1}\left[\frac{(12)^{2}+(21.66)^{2}-(25)^{2}}{2(12)(21.66)} \right] =91.3^{\circ }\)

The total of the interior angles of any triangle must be 180°. Therefore, angle ∠B’DA can be readily determined:

∠B’DA = 180° – (∠DAB’ + ∠AB’D)

=180° – (60° + 91.3°) = 28.7°

Focusing on the top triangle, the internal angle ∠ B’C’D can be found from the law of cosines:

\(\angle B'C'D=cos^{-1}\left[\frac{(B'c')^{2}+(C'D)^{2}-(B'D)^{2}}{2(B'C')(C'D)} \right]\)

 

\(=cos^{-1}\left[\frac{(20\ mm)^{2}+(15\ mm)^{2}-(21.66\ mm)^{2}}{2(20\ mm)(15\ mm)} \right]=74.9^{\circ }\)

The internal angle ∠C’B’D can be determined from the law of sines:

\(\angle C'B'D=sin^{-1}\left[(\frac{C'D}{B'D})sin\angle B'C'D \right]\)

 

\(=sin^{-1}\left[(\frac{15\ mm}{21.66\ mm})sin\ 74.9^{\circ } \right] =42.0^{\circ }\)

The final interior angle, ∠B’DC’, of the upper triangle can be found by the following:

∠B’DC’ = 180° – (∠C’B’D + ∠B’C’D) = 180° – (42.0° + 74.9°) = 63.1°

The total mechanism angles at joint B (between links 2 and 3) and at joint D (between links 1 and 4) can be
determined by the following:

At joint B’:

∠AB’C’ = ∠AB’D + ∠C’B’D = 91.3° + 42.0° = 133.3°

At joint D:

∠C’DA = ∠B’DC’ + ∠B’DA = 63.1° + 28.7° = 91.8°

4: The angular displacement of the handle, link 3, can be determined by focusing on joint B, as shown in Figure 4.22d. For the original configuration, the angle of link 3 above the horizontal is expressed as

∠ABC – 90° = 96.1° – 90.0° = 6.1°

For the displaced configuration, the angle of link 3 above the horizontal is expressed as

∠AB’C’ – 120° = 133.3° – 120.0° = 13.3°

Finally, the angular displacement of link 3 is determined by

\(\Delta \theta _{3}=13.3^{\circ }-6.1^{\circ }=7.2^{\circ }\), counterclockwise
