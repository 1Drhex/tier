Guest Question.

Use the AISC equations to determine the allowable axial load \(P_{\text {allow }}\) that may be supported by a W8 × 48 wide-flange column for the following effective lengths: (a) KL = 13 ft and (b) KL = 26 ft. Assume E = 29,000 ksi and \(\sigma_{Y}\) = 50 ksi.

Admin
Solution

The following section properties for a standard steel W8 × 48 shape are given in Appendix B:

\(A=14.1 \text { in. }^{2}, r_{x}=3.61 \text { in., } r_{y}=2.08 \text { in. }\)

Limiting slenderness ratio

\(4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{Y}}}=4.71 \sqrt{\frac{29,000 ksi }{50 ksi }}=113.432\)

(a) Slenderness ratios for KL = 13 ft:

\(\begin{aligned}&K L / r_{x}=(13 ft )(12 in . / ft ) /(3.61 in .)=43.213 \\&K L / r_{y}=(13 ft )(12 in . / ft ) /(2.08 in .)=75.000\end{aligned}\)

Elastic critical buckling stress

\(\sigma_{e}=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{\left(\frac{K L}{r}\right)^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(29,000 ksi )}{(75.000)^{2}}=50.883 ksi\)

Critical and allowable buckling stresses

Since \(\frac{K L}{r} \leq 4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{y}}}\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\left.\sigma_{c r}=\left[0.658^{\left(\frac{\sigma_{y}}{\sigma_{e}}\right)}\right] \sigma_{Y}=\left[0.658^{\left(\frac{50 ksi }{50.883 ksi }\right)}\right)\right](50 ksi )=33.140 ksi \\&\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\sigma_{c r}}{1.67}=\frac{33.140 ksi }{1.67}=19.844 ksi\end{aligned}\)

Allowable axial load:

\(P_{\text {allow }}=\sigma_{\text {allow }} A=(19.844 ksi )\left(14.1 in .^{2}\right)=280 kips\)

 

(b) Slenderness ratios for KL = 26 ft:

\(\begin{aligned}&K L / r_{x}=(26 ft )(12 in . / ft ) /(3.61 \text { in. })=86.427 \\&K L / r_{y}=(26 ft )(12 in . / ft ) /(2.08 \text { in. })=150.000\end{aligned}\)

Elastic critical buckling stress

\(\sigma_{e}=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{\left(\frac{K L}{r}\right)^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(29,000 ksi )}{(150.000)^{2}}=12.721 ksi\)

Critical and allowable buckling stresses

Since \(\frac{K L}{r}>4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{y}}}\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{c r}=0.877 \sigma_{e}=(0.877)(12.721 ksi )=11.156 ksi \\&\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\sigma_{c r}}{1.67}=\frac{11.156 ksi }{1.67}=6.680 ksi\end{aligned}\)

Allowable axial load:

\(P_{\text {allow }}=\sigma_{\text {allow }} A=(6.680 ksi )\left(14.1 in .^{2}\right)=94.2 kips\)
