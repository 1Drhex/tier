Guest Question.

At a point in a structural member subjected to plane stress there are normal and shearing stresses on horizontal and vertical planes through the point, as shown in Fig. 10-11a.
(a) Determine the principal stresses and the maximum shearing stress at the point.
(b) Locate the planes on which these stresses act and show the stresses on a complete sketch.

Admin
Solution

(a) On the basis of the axes shown in Fig. 10-11a and the established sign conventions, \(σ_x\) is positive, whereas \(σ_y\) and \(τ_{xy}\) are negative. For use in \(tan2\theta _p=\frac{2\tau _{xy}}{\sigma _x-\sigma _y} \) and \(\sigma _{p1,p2}=\frac{\sigma _x+\sigma _y}{2}\pm \sqrt{(\frac{\sigma _x-\sigma _y}{2} )^2+\tau _{xy}^2} \), the given values are

\( σ_x=+10,000psi \quad\quad σ_y=-8000psi \quad\quad τ_{xy}=-4000psi\)

When these values are substituted in \(\sigma _{p1,p2}=\frac{\sigma _x+\sigma _y}{2}\pm \sqrt{(\frac{\sigma _x-\sigma _y}{2} )^2+\tau _{xy}^2} \), the principal stresses are found to be

\(\sigma _{p1,p2}=\frac{\sigma _x+\sigma _y}{2}\pm \sqrt{(\frac{\sigma _x-\sigma _y}{2} )^2+\tau _{xy}^2} \)

 

\(=\frac{10,000+(-8000)}{2}\pm \sqrt{(\frac{10,000-(-8000)}{2} )^2+(-4000)^2} \)

 

\(=1000\pm 9849\)

 

\(\sigma _{p1}=1000+9849=10,849psi\cong 10.850psi \quad T\)

 

\(\sigma _{p2}=1000-9849=-8849psi\cong 8850psi \quad C\)

 

\(\sigma _{p3}=\sigma _z=0\)

Since \(σ_{p1}\) and \(σ_{p2}\) are of opposite sign, the maximum shearing stress is given by \(\tau _{max}=\frac{\sigma _{max}-\sigma _{min}}{2}\) as

\(\tau _{max}=\frac{\sigma _{max}-\sigma _{min}}{2} \)

 

\(=\frac{10,849-(-8849)}{2}=9849psi\cong 9850psi \)

The sum of the normal stresses on any two orthogonal planes is a constant for plane stress:

\(\sigma _x+\sigma _y=10,000+(-8000)=2000psi\)

 

\(=\sigma _{p1}+\sigma _{p2}=10,850+(-8850)\)

Also, the normal stress on the planes of maximum shear stress is equal to the average of the normal stresses

\(\sigma _n=\frac{\sigma _x+\sigma _y}{2} =\frac{\sigma _{p1}+\sigma _{p2}}{2} =1000psi\)

(b) When the given data are substituted in \(tan2\theta _p=\frac{2\tau _{xy}}{\sigma _x-\sigma _y}\), the results are

\(tan2\theta _p=\frac{2\tau _{xy}}{\sigma _x-\sigma _y}=\frac{2(-4000)}{10,000-(-8000)}=-0.4444 \)

from which

\(2\theta _p=-23.96^\circ ,+156.04^\circ ,\cdot \cdot \cdot \)

and \(\theta _p=-11.98^\circ ,+78.02^\circ ,\cdot \cdot \cdot \cong 11.98^\circ \swarrow \quad and \quad 78.0^\circ \nwarrow \)

when \(\theta =-11.98^\circ \) , \(\sigma _n=\sigma _xcos^2\theta +\sigma _ysin^2\theta +2\tau _{xy}sin\theta cos\theta \) gives

\(\sigma _n=\sigma _xcos^2\theta +\sigma _ysin^2\theta +2\tau _{xy}sin\theta cos\theta \)

 

\(=10,000cos^2(-11.98^\circ )+(-8000)sin^2(-11.98^\circ )+2(-4000)sin(-11.98^\circ )cos(-11.98^\circ )\)

 

\(=\sigma _{p1}=10.849psi\cong 10.850psi \quad T\)

The result is shown in Fig. 10-11b. When θ =+78.02°, \(\sigma _n=\sigma _xcos^2\theta +\sigma _ysin^2\theta +2\tau _{xy}sin\theta cos\theta \) gives

\(\sigma _n=\sigma _xcos^2\theta +\sigma _ysin^2\theta +2\tau _{xy}sin\theta cos\theta \)

 

\(10,000cos^2(+78.02°)+(-8000)sin^2(+78.02°)+2(-4000)sin(+78.02°)cos(+78.02°)\)

 

\(=σ_{p2}=-8849psi≅8850psi \quad C\)

The result is shown in Fig. 10-11c.
The maximum in-plane shear stress occurs on a surface oriented at 45° to the principal directions. When θ=-11.98° + 45° =+33.02°, \(\sigma _n=\sigma _xcos^2\theta +\sigma _ysin^2\theta +2\tau _{xy}sin\theta cos\theta \) and \(\tau _{nt}=-(\sigma _x-\sigma _y)sin\theta cos\theta +\tau _{xy}[cos^\theta -sin^2\theta ]\) give

\(\sigma _n=\sigma _xcos^2\theta +\sigma _ysin^2\theta +2\tau _{xy}sin\theta cos\theta \)

 

\(10,000cos^2(+33.02°)+(-8000)sin^2(+33.02°)+2(-4000)sin(+33.02°)cos(+33.02°)\)

 

\(=+999.6psi≅1000psi \quad T\)

 

and \(\tau _{nt}=-(\sigma _x-\sigma _y)sin\theta cos\theta +\tau _{xy}[cos^2 \theta -sin^2\theta ]\)

 

\(=-[10,000-(-8000)]sin(+33.02°)cos(+33.02°)+(-4000)[cos^2(+33.02°)-sin^2(+33.02°)]\)

 

\(=-9849psi ≅-9850psi\)

The result is shown in Fig. 10-11d.
All three of these results can be combined in the triangular stress block shown in Fig. 10-11e.
