Guest Question.

It is found experimentally that the power stroke expansion in an internal combustion engine can be approximated with a polytropic process with a value of the polytropic exponent n somewhat larger than the specific heat ratio k. Repeat but assume the expansion process is reversible and polytropic (instead of the isentropic expansion in the Otto cycle) with n equal to 1.50.

Admin
Solution

First find states 2 and 3. Based on the inlet state we get

\(\begin{aligned}& v _{4}= v _{1}= RT _{1} / P _{1}=53.34 \times 510 / 13 \times 144=14.532 ft ^{3} / lbm \\& v _{3}= v _{2}= v _{1} / 7=2.076 ft ^{3} / lbm\end{aligned}\)

After compression we have constant s leads to Eq.6.24 and Eq.6.25

\(\begin{aligned}& P _{2}= P _{1}\left( v _{1} / v _{2}\right)^{ k }=13(7)^{1.4}=198.2 lbf / in ^{2} \\& T _{2}= T _{1}\left( v _{1} / v _{2}\right)^{ k -1}=510(7)^{0.4}=1110.7 R\end{aligned}\)

Constant volume combustion

\(\begin{aligned}& T _{3}= T _{2}+ q _{ H } / C _{ V 0}=1110.7+800 / 0.171=5789 R \\& P _{3}= P _{2} T _{3} / T _{2}=198.2 \times 5789 / 1110.7=1033 lbf / in ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Process 3 to 4: \(Pv ^{1.5}=\text { constant. }\)

\(\begin{aligned}& P _{4}= P _{3}\left( v _{3} / v _{4}\right)^{1.5}=1033(1 / 7)^{1.5}=55.78 lbf / in ^{2} \\& T _{4}= T _{3}\left( v _{3} / v _{4}\right)^{0.5}=5789(1 / 7)^{0.5}=2188 R\end{aligned}\)

For the mean effective pressure we need the net work and therefore the induvidual process work terms

\(\begin{aligned}{}_{1}w _{2} &=\int P d v = R \left( T _{2}- T _{1}\right) /(1-1.4) \\&=-53.34(1110.7-510) /(0.4 \times 778)=-102.96 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}{ }_{3} w _{4} &=\int P d v = R \left( T _{4}- T _{3}\right) /(1-1.5) \\&=-53.34(2188-5789) /(0.5 \times 778)=493.8 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}& w _{ NET }=493.8-102.96=390.84 Btu / lbm \\&\eta_{ CYCLE }= w _{ NET } / q _{ H }=390.84 / 700= 0 . 4 8 8\end{aligned}\)

 

\(P_{\text {meff }}=w_{ NET } /\left( v _{1}- v _{2}\right)=390.84 \times 778 /(14.532-2.076)= 1 6 9 . 5 lbf / in ^{2}\)

Notice a smaller \(w _{ NET }, \eta_{ CYCLE }, P _{ meff }\) compared to ideal cycle.
