Guest Question.

Air enters the compressor of a gas turbine at 100 kPa and 25°C. For a pressure ratio of 5 and a maximum temperature of 850°C, determine the back work ratio and the thermal efficiency using the Brayton cycle.

Admin
Solution

To find the back work ratio we observe that

\( \frac{w_{\text {comp }}}{w_{\text {turb }}}=\frac{C_{p}\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right)}{C_{p}\left(T_{3}-T_{4}\right)}=\frac{T_{2}-T_{1}}{T_{3}-T_{4}} \).

\( \text { The temperatures are } T_{1}=298 K , T_{3}=1123 K \text {, and } \)

\( T_{2}=T_{1}\left(\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}=(298)(5)^{0.2857}=472.0 K \quad T_{4}=T_{3}\left(\frac{P_{4}}{P_{5}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}=(1123)\left(\frac{1}{5}\right)^{0.2857}=709.1 K \).

The back work ratio is then

\( \frac{w_{\text {comp }}}{w_{\text {tuph }}}=\frac{472.0-298}{1123-709}=0.420 \quad \text { or } \quad 42.0 \% \).

\( \text { The thermal efficiency is } \eta=1-r^{(1-k) / k}=1-(5)^{-0.2857}=0.369 \text { or } 36.9 \% \text {. } \)
