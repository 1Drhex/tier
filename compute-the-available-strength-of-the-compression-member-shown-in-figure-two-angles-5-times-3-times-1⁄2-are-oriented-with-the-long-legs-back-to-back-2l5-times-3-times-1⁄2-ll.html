Guest Question.

Compute the available strength of the compression member shown in Figure. Two angles, 5 \(\times\) 3 \(\times\) 1⁄2, are oriented with the long legs back-to-back (2L5\( \times \)3 \(\times\) 1⁄2 LLBB) and separated by 3⁄8 inch. The effective length, \(L_{c}\), is 16 feet, and there are three fully tightened intermediate connectors. A36 steel is used.

Admin
Solution

Compute the flexural buckling strength for the x-axis:

\(\frac{L_{cx}}{r_{x}}=\frac{16(12) }{1.58}=121.5\)

 

\(F_{e}=\frac{\pi ^{2}E}{(L_{c}/r)^{2}}=\frac{\pi ^{2}(29,000)}{(121.5)^{2}}=19.39ksi\)

 

\(4.71\sqrt{\frac{E}{F_{y}}}=4.71\sqrt{\frac{29,000}{36}}=134\)

Since \(\frac{L_{c}}{r} < 4.71\sqrt{\frac{E}{F_{y}}}\),use AISC Equation E3-2:

\(F_{cr}= 0.658^{(F_{y}/F_{e})}F_{y}= 0.658^{(36/19.39)}(36) =16.55ksi\)
The nominal strength is
\(P_{n} = F_{cr}A_{g} = 16.55(7.50) = 124.1 kips\)
To determine the flexural-torsional buckling strength for the y-axis, use the modified slenderness ratio, which is based on the spacing of the connectors. The unmodified slenderness ratio is
\(\left ( \frac{L_{c}}{r} \right )_{0}=\frac{L_{c}}{r_{y}}=\frac{16(12)}{1.24}=154.8\)
The spacing of the connectors is
\(a = \frac{16(12)}{4 spaces}= 48 in\)
Then, from Equation 4.15,
\(\frac{a}{r_{i}}=\frac{a}{r_{z}}=\frac{48}{0.642}=74.77 < 0.75(154.8) =116.1 (OK)\)
Compute the modified slenderness ratio, \((L_{c}/r)_{m}:\)
\(\frac{a}{r_{i}}=\frac{48}{0.642}=74.77 > 40 \therefore \)Use AISC Equation E6-2b.

\(\frac{K_{i}a}{r_{i}}=\frac{0.5(48)}{0.642}=37.38 \)

\(\left ( \frac{L_{c}}{r} \right )_{m}=\sqrt{\left ( \frac{L_{c}}{r} \right )_{o}^{2}+\left ( \frac{K_{i}a}{r_{i}} \right )^{2}}=\sqrt{(154.8)^{2} + (37.38)^{2}}=159.2\)
This value should be used in place of \(L_{c}/r_{y}\) for the computation of \(F_{ey}\):

\(F_{ey}=\frac{\pi ^{2}E}{(L_{c}/r)^{2}}=\frac{\pi ^{2}(29,000)}{(159.2)^{2}}=11.29ksi\)
From Equation 4.14,
\(F_{ez}=\frac{GJ}{A_{g}\bar{r}_{0}^{2}}=\frac{ 11,200(2 \times 0.322)}{7.50(2.51)^{2}}=152.6 ksi\)

 

\(F_{ey} + F_{ez} = 11.29 + 152.6 = 163.9 ksi\)

 

\(F_{e}=\left ( \frac{F_{ey}+F_{ez}}{2H} \right )\left [ 1 – \sqrt{1 – \frac{4F_{ey}F_{ez}2H}{(F_{ey}+F_{ez})^{2}}}\right ]\)

 

\(= \frac{163.9}{2(0.8312)} \left [ 1 – \sqrt{1 – \frac{ 4(11.29)(152.6)(0.646)}{(163.9)^{2}}} \right ]=10.99 ksi\)

 

\(\frac{F_{y}}{F_{e}}=\frac{36}{10.99}=3.276\)

Since 3.276 > 2.25, use AISC Equation E3-3:
\(F_{cr} = 0.877F_{e} = 0.877(10.99) = 9.638 ksi\)
The nominal strength is
\(P_{n} = F_{cr}A_{g} = 9.638(7.50) = 72.29 kips\)
Therefore the flexural-torsional buckling strength controls.
The design strength is
\(\phi_{c}P_{n} = 0.90(72.29) = 65.1 kips\)
From Equation 4.7, the allowable stress is
\(F_{a} = 0.6F_{cr} = 0.6(9.638) = 5.783 ksi\)
The allowable strength is
\(F_{a}A_{g} = 5.783(7.50) = 43.47 kips\)
Design compressive strength = 65.1 kips. Allowable compressive strength = 43.4 kips.
