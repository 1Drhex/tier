Guest Question.

A taper cone, nominal included angle 25° rests in a symmetrical vee of included angle 90° To what angle must the vee be inclined for the upper edge of the taper cone to be parallel with the surface plate upon which the vee-block rests?
If dial indicator readings taken over the taper cone show a difference of 0.03 mm over a length of 50 mm, lower at the large end of the taper, what error of included angle is indicated?

Admin
Solution

By equation 16.3:

\(sin α=\frac {sin θ}{sin Φ}\)

From the given conditions 2θ = 25° 2Φ = 90° and α is required.

\(sin α=\frac {sin 12.5°}{sin 45°}\)

=0.306 09

α = 17 °49.5′

Angle of inclination required = 17°49v5′ + 12 °30′ =30° 19.5′

Angular error of (α +θ) indicated =\(\frac {0.03}{50} radians\)
= 0.0006 radians or 2.06′

a is however a function of θ. By differentiation of equation 16.3, α being regarded as a constant,

\(cos α .Δα=\frac {cos θ}{sin Φ}.Δθ\)(very nearly)

\(Δα=\frac {cos 12.5°}{cos 17°50′×sin 45 °}\)

\(=\frac {0.9763}{0.952×0.7071}.Δθ \)

=1.45Δθ

But (Δα + Δθ)= 2.06′
