Guest Question.

For the emitter-stabilized direct-coupled amplifier of \(Fig. 61.21\) , find

\((i) A_{v.1}\) , \((ii) A_{v.2} , \)(iii) A_v and \((iv) r_i \).Neglect \(V_{BE}\) and use \(r_e = 50 mV/I_E\) .

Admin
Solution

As seen, emitter resistors \(R_3\) and \(R_5\) have been used to improve temperature stability. The voltage divider \(R_1 – R_2\) together with \(R_3\) determines the emitter current of \(Q_1\). The resistor \(R_4\) has dual function \((i)\) it acts as load resistor for \(Q_1\) and \((ii)\) it establishes base bias of \(Q_2\) .

\((i)\) Since unbypassed resistor \(R_3\) is present

\(A_{v.1}=\frac{r_{0.1} }{r_{e.1}+R_{3} }\)

As seen from the ac equivalent diagram of \(Fig. 61.22\)

 

\(r_{0.1} = R_4 \left|\right| \beta_2. (r_{e.2} + R_5)\)

Now \(r_{e.2}=\frac{50}{I_{E.2} } \)

For finding \(I_{E.2}\) , let us first find \(I_{E.1}\)

Drop across \(R_{2}=20\times \frac{20}{180+20}=2V \)

Same is the drop across \(R_3\) since \(V_{BE}\) has been neglected.

\(\therefore\) \(I_{E.1} = 2V/1 K = 2 mA\)

Now, \(I_{E.2}=\frac{V_{CC}-I_{E.1}R_{4} }{R_{5} }=\frac{20-2\times 8}{2}=2mA \)

 

\(\therefore\) \(r_{e.2} = 50/2 = 25 \Omega\) and \(\beta_2 (r_{e.2} + R_5) \cong 200 K\)

 

\(r_{0.1}=8K\left|\right| 200K=7.7K_{i} r_{e.1}=\frac{50}{2}=25\Omega \)

 

\(\therefore\) \(A_{v.1} =\frac{7,700}{\left(25+1000\right) }=7.5 \)

 

\((ii)\) \(A_{v.2} =\frac{r_{0.2} }{\left(r_{e.2}+R_{5} \right) }\) ; \(r_{0.2}=R_6=6000 \Omega\) \(\therefore\) \(A_{V.2} =\frac{6000}{\left(25+2000 \right) }\cong 3\)

 

\((iii)\) \(A_v =7.5 \times 3 = 22.5\)

 

\((iv)\) As seen from \(Fig. 61.22\)

 

\(r_i = R_1 \left|\right| R_2 \left|\right| \beta_1 (r_{e.1} + R_3) = 180 K \left|\right| 20 K \left|\right| 100 K\) —neglecting \(r_{e.1}\)

\(= 15.25 K\)
