Guest Question.

Black–Scholes OPM: Part II Calculate the Black–Scholes price for a nine-month option given the following:

\(S_{0}\) = $80
E = $70
σ = .30 per year
\(R_{f}\) = 10% per year
t = 9 months

Admin
Solution

We fi rst calculate \(d_{1}\) and \(d_{2}\):

\(d_{1}=\left[In(S_{0}/E )+(R_{f}+1/2\times \sigma ^{2} )\times t\right]/\left[\sigma \times \sqrt{t} \right]\)

\(=\left[In(80/70 )+(.10+1/2\times .30 ^{2} )\times (.75)\right]/\left[.30 \times \sqrt{.75} \right]\)

= .9325

\(d_{2}=d_{1}-\sigma \times \sqrt{t}\)

\(=.9325-.30\times \sqrt{.75}\)

= .6727

From Table 25A.1, \(N(d_{1} )\) appears to be roughly .825, and \(N(d_{2} )\) is about .75. Plugging these in, we determine that the option’s value is $17.12. Notice again that we used an annual t value of 9/12 = .75 in this case.
