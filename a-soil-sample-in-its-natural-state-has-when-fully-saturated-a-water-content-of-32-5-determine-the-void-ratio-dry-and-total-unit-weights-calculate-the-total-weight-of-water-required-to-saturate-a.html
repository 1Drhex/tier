Guest Question.

A soil sample in its natural state has, when fully saturated, a water content of 32.5%. Determine the void ratio, dry and total unit weights. Calculate the total weight of water required to saturate a soil mass of volume 10 \(m ^{3} . \text { Assume } G_{s}=2.69\).

Admin
Solution

Void ratio (Eq. 3.14a)

 

\(S=\frac{w G_{s}}{e} \text { or } e=\frac{w G_{s}}{S}\) (3.14a)

 

\(e=\frac{w G_{s}}{S}=\frac{32.5 \times 2.69}{(1) \times 100}=0.874\)

 

Total unit weight (Eq. 3.15a)

 

\(\gamma_{t}=\frac{G_{s} \gamma_{w}(1+w)}{1+e}\) (3.15a)

 

\(\gamma_{t}=\frac{G_{s} \gamma_{w}(1+w)}{1+e}=\frac{2.69(9.81)(1+0.325)}{1+0.874}=18.7 kN / m ^{3}\)

 

Dry unit weight (Eq. 3.18a)

 

\(\gamma_{d}=\frac{\gamma_{w} G_{s}}{1+e}\) (3.18a)

 

\(\gamma_{d}=\frac{\gamma_{w} G_{s}}{1+e}=\frac{2.69 \times 9.81}{1+0.874}=14.08 kN / m ^{3}\)

 

FromEq. (3.6a), \(W=\gamma_{t} V=18.66 \times 10=186.6 kN\)

 

\(\gamma_{t}=\frac{W}{V}\) (3.6a)

 

From Eq. (3.7a), \(W_{s}=\gamma_{d} V=14.08 \times 10=140.8 kN\)

 

\(\gamma_{d}=\frac{W_{s}}{V}\) (3.7a)

 

Weight of water \(=W-W_{s}=186.6-140.8=45.8 kN\)
