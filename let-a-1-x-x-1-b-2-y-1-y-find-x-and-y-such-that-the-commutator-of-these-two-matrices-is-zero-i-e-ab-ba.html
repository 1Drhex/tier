Guest Question.

let

\(A = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & -x \\ x & 1 \end{matrix} \right ] \) , \(B = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 & y \\ 1 & -y \end{matrix} \right ] \)

 

Find \( x\) and \(y\) such that the commutator of these two matrices is zero, i.e., \(AB = BA\).

Admin
Solution

We compute \(AB\):

\( AB = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & -x \\ x & 1 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 & y \\ 1 & -y \end{matrix} \right ] = \left [ \begin{matrix} (1) (2) + (-x) (1) & (1) (y) + (-x) (-y) \\ (x) (2) + (1) (1) & (x) (y) + (1) (-y) \end{matrix} \right ] \)

 

\(= \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 – x & y + xy \\ 2x + 1 & xy – y \end{matrix} \right ] \)

 

For \(BA\) we find

 

\(BA = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 & y \\ 1 & -y \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & -x \\ x & 1 \end{matrix} \right ] = \left [ \begin{matrix} (2) (1) + (y) (x) & (2) (-x) + (y) (1) \\ (1) (1) + (-y) (x) & (1) (-x) + (-y) (1) \end{matrix} \right ] \)

 

\(= \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 + xy & -2x + y \\ 1 – xy & -x – y \end{matrix} \right ] \)

 

For these to be equal we must have

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 2 – x & y + xy \\ 2x + 1 & xy – y \end{matrix} \right ] = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 + xy & -2x + y \\ 1 – xy & -x – y \end{matrix} \right ]\)

 

Each term that belongs to the matrix on the left-hand side must be equal to the corresponding term in the matrix on the right side. This means that

\(2 − x = 2 + xy\)

\(y + xy = −2x + y\)

\(2x + 1 = 1 − xy\)

\(xy − y = −x − y\)

We examine the first equation \(2 − x = 2 + xy\) . Subtracting 2 from both sides we have

\(−x = xy\)

Now divide both sides by \(x\) . This gives

\(y = −1\)

Inserting this value into the second equation

\(y + xy = −2x + y\)

we find

\(−1 − x = −2x − 1\)

Now add 1 to both sides and divide by −1, which gives

\(x = 2x\)

This equation implies that \(x = 0\). You can check that these values satisfy the other two equations. Therefore, if we take \( x = 0\) and \(y = −1\), then the matrices are

\(A = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ] \) , \(B = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 & -1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

The matrix \(A\) is a special matrix called the identity matrix.
