Guest Question.

The steel pipe shown in Figure P8.67 has an outside diameter of 195 mm, a wall thickness of 10 mm, an elastic modulus of E = 200 GPa, and a coefficient of thermal expansion of α = 11.7 × \(10^{-6}\) /°C. Using a = 300 mm, b = 900 mm, and θ = 70°, calculate the normal strains at H and K after a load of P = 40 kN has been applied and the temperature of the pipe has been increased by 25°C.

Admin
Solution

Section properties

\(\begin{aligned}&d=D-2 t=195 mm -2(10 mm )=175 mm \\&A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[D^{2}-d^{2}\right]=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[(195 mm )^{2}-(175 mm )^{2}\right]=5,811.95 mm ^{2} \\&I=\frac{\pi}{64}\left[D^{4}-d^{4}\right]=\frac{\pi}{64}\left[(195 mm )^{4}-(175 mm )^{4}\right]=24,936,883 mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Internal forces and moments

\(\begin{aligned}&F=(40,000 N ) \sin 70^{\circ}=37,587.7 N \\&M=(40,000 N ) \sin 70^{\circ}(300 mm )+(40,000 N ) \cos 70^{\circ}(900 mm )=23,589,036.6 N – mm\end{aligned}\)

Stresses

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{\text {axial }}=\frac{F}{A}=\frac{37,587.7 N }{5,811.95 mm ^{2}}=6.4673 MPa ( C ) \\&\sigma_{\text {bending }}=\frac{M c}{I}=\frac{(23,589,036.6 N – mm )(195 mm / 2)}{24,936,883 mm ^{4}}=\pm 92.2301 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Normal stresses at H and K

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{H} &=-6.4673 MPa -92.2301 MPa =-98.6974 MPa \\\sigma_{K} &=-6.4673 MPa +92.2301 MPa =85.7628 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Normal strains at H and K due to load P

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{H} &=\frac{-98.6974 MPa }{200,000 MPa }=-493.5 \times 10^{-6} mm / mm \\\varepsilon_{K} &=\frac{85.7628 MPa }{200,000 MPa }=428.8 \times 10^{-6} mm / mm\end{aligned}\)

Thermal strain

\(\varepsilon_{T}=\alpha \Delta T=\left(11.7 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(25^{\circ} C \right)=292.5 \times 10^{-6} mm / mm\)

Total normal strains at H and K due to load P and temperature change

\(\begin{aligned}&\varepsilon_{H}=-493.5 \mu \varepsilon+292.5 \mu \varepsilon=-201 \mu \varepsilon \\&\varepsilon_{K}=428.8 \mu \varepsilon+292.5 \mu \varepsilon=721 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)
