Guest Question.

Suppose that a non-ideal gas has an expansion (called a virial expansion) in terms of pressure of the form

\(\frac{v\left(T , p\right) }{RT} = \frac{1}{p} \left[1 + \tilde{B}p + \tilde{C}p^{2} + . . . \right] = \frac{1}{p} + \tilde{B} + \tilde{C}p + \cdot \cdot \cdot ,\) (5.70)

where \(\tilde{B}\left(T\right) and \tilde{C}\left(T\right)\) are virial coefficients that depend on the temperature. Calculate the fugacity of this gas. For a simple model based on a potential consisting of a hard repulsive core of diameter \(\sigma _{1} , \) an attractive potential well of constant depth \(\varepsilon\) in the annular region between a sphere of diameter \(\sigma _{2} , \) and the repulsive core, and zero potential beyond, the first virial coefficient is given by

\(\tilde{B}\left(T\right) = \frac{2\pi }{3} \left[\frac{\sigma ^{3}_{1} - \left(e^{\varepsilon /k_{B}T } - 1 \right) \left(\sigma ^{3}_{2} - \sigma ^{3}_{1} \right) }{k_{B}T} \right] .\) (5.71)

If this is the only important virial coefficient, discuss briefly the effect of temperature on the fugacity.

Admin
Solution

Equation (5.69) becomes

\(ln(f/p)=\int_{0}^{p}{\left[\frac{v(T,p^\prime )}{RT}-\frac{1}{p^\prime } \right] } dp^\prime .\) (5.69)

 

\(ln[f(T/p)/p]=\int_{0}^{p}{\left[\widetilde{B}+\widetilde{C}p^\prime +… \right] } dp^\prime=\widetilde{B}(T)p+(\widetilde{C}(T)/2 )p^2 + … \) (5.72)

Thus,

\(f(T,p)=p exp[\widetilde{B}(T)p+(\widetilde{C}(T)/2 )p^2+… ].\) (5.73)

The first virial coefficient given by Eq. (5.71) becomes \(\widetilde{B}(T) = −(2π/3)(σ^3_2 − σ^3_1 )(e^{ε/k_BT} /k_BT)\) at low temperatures and \(\widetilde{B}(T) = (2π/3)(σ^3_1 /k_BT)\) at high temperatures. It therefore changes sign from negative to positive as the temperature increases. If this is the only important virial coefficient, f < p and varies strongly with temperature for low temperatures and f > p and varies weakly with temperature for high temperatures.
