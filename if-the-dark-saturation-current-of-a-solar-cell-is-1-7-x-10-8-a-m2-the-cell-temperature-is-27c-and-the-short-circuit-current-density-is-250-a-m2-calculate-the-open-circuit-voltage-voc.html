Guest Question.

If the dark saturation current of a solar cell is 1.7 × \(10^-8 \)A/\(m^2\), the cell temperature is 27°C, and the short-circuit current density is 250 A/\(m^2\), calculate the open circuit voltage, \(V_{oc}\); voltage at maximum power, \(V{max}\); current density at maximum power, \(I_{max}\); maximum power, \(P_{max}\); and maximum efficiency, \(η_{max}\).

What cell area is required to get an output of 20 W when the available solar
radiation is 820 W/\(m^2\)?

Admin
Solution

First the value of \( e/kT_{C}\) is evaluated, which is used in many relations:

\(\frac{e}{kT_{C}} =\frac{1.602\times 10^{-19}}{1.381\times 10^{-23}\times 300} =38.67 V^{-1}\)

Using Eq. (9.9),

\(V_{oc}=\frac{kT_{C}}{e} 1n\left\lgroup\frac{I_{Sc}}{I_{o}}+1 \right\rgroup =\frac{1}{38.67} 1n\left\lgroup\frac{250}{1.7\times 10^{-8}}+1 \right\rgroup =0.605 V\)

Voltage at maximum power can be found from Eq. (9.14) by trial and error:

\(exp\left\lgroup\frac{eV_{max}}{kT_{C}} \right\rgroup \left\lgroup1+\frac{eV_{max}}{kT_{C}} \right\rgroup =1+\frac{I_{sc}}{I_{o}}\)

or

\(exp(38.67V_{max})(1+38.67V_{max})=1+\frac{250}{1.7\times 10^{-8}}\)

which gives\( V_{max}\) = 0.47 V.

The current density at maximum power point can be estimated from Eq. (9.16):

\(I_{max}=\frac{eV_{max}}{kT_{C}+eV_{max}} (I_{sc}+I_{o})\)

\(=\frac{1.602\times 10^{-19}x0.47}{1.381\times 10^{-23} \times 300+1.602×10^{-19}\times 0.47}(250+1.7\times 10^{-8})\)

\(=237 A/m^2\)

Maximum power, \(P_{max}\), is obtained from Eq. (9.5):

\(P_{max} = I_{max}V_{max} = 237 × 0.47 = 111.4 W/m^2\)

Maximum efficiency, \(\eta _{max}\), is obtained from Eq. (9.18):

\(\eta _{max}=\frac{P_{max}}{P_{in}} =\frac{111.4}{820} \)= 13.58%

Finally, the cell area required to get an output of 20 W is

\(A =\frac{P_{req}}{P_{max}} =\frac{20}{111.4} =0.18 m^2\)
