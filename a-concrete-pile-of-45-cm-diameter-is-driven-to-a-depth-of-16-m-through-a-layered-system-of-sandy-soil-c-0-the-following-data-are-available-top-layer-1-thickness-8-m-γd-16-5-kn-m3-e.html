Guest Question.

A concrete pile of 45 cm diameter is driven to a depth of 16 m through a layered system of sandy soil (c = 0). The following data are available.

Top layer 1: Thickness \(=8 m , \gamma_{d}=16.5 kN / m ^{3}, e=0.60 \text { and } \phi=30^{\circ}\).

Layer 2: Thickness \(=6 m , \gamma_{d}=15.5 kN / m ^{3}, e=0.65 \text { and } \phi=35^{\circ}\).

Layer 3: Extends to a great depth, \(\gamma_{d}=16.00 kN / m ^{3}, e=0.65 \text { and } \phi=38^{\circ}\).

Assume that the value of δ in all the layers of sand is equal to 0.75\(\phi\). The value of \(\bar{K}_{s}\) for each
layer as equal to half of the passive earth pressure coefficient. The water table is at ground level. Calculate the values of \(Q_{u} \text { and } Q_{a} \text { with } F_{s}=2.5\) by the conventional method for \(Q_{f}\) and Berezantsev’s method for \(Q_{b}\).

Admin
Solution

The soil is submerged throughout the soil profile. The specific gravity \(G_{s}\) is required for calculating \(\gamma_{\text {sat }}\).

 

(a) Using the equation \(\gamma_{d}=\frac{\gamma_{w} G_{s}}{1+e}\), calculate \(G_{s}\) for each layer since \(\gamma_{d}, \gamma_{w}\) and e are known.

 

(b) Using the equation \(\gamma_{\text {sat }}=\frac{\gamma_{w}\left(G_{s}+e\right)}{1+e}, \text { calculate } \gamma_{\text {sat }}\) for each layer and then \(\gamma_{b}=\gamma_{\text {sat }}-\gamma_{w}\) for each layer.

 

(c) For a layered system of soil, the ultimate load can be determined by making use of Eq. (15.9). Now

 

\(Q_{u}=q_{o}^{\prime} N_{q} A_{b}+A_{s} \bar{q}_{o}^{\prime} \bar{K}_{s} \tan \delta\) (15.9)

 

\(Q_{u}=Q_{b}+Q_{f}=q_{0}^{\prime} N_{q} A_{b}+P \underset{0}{L} \bar{q}_{0}^{\prime} \bar{K}_{s} \tan \delta \Delta L\)

 

(d) \(q_{0}^{\prime}\) at the tip of the pile is

 

\(q_{0}^{\prime}=\gamma_{b 1} \Delta L_{1}+\gamma_{b 2} \Delta L_{2}+\gamma_{b 3} \Delta L_{3}\)

 

(e) \(\vec{q}_{0}^{\prime}\) at the middle of each layer is

 

\(\bar{q}_{01}^{\prime}=\frac{1}{2} \Delta L_{1} \gamma_{b 1}\)

 

\(\bar{q}_{02}^{\prime}=\Delta L_{1} \gamma_{b 1}+\frac{1}{2} \Delta L_{2} \gamma_{b 2}\)

 

\(\bar{q}_{03}^{\prime}=\Delta L_{1} \gamma_{b l}+\Delta L_{2} \gamma_{b 2}+\frac{1}{2} \Delta L_{3} \gamma_{b 3}\)

 

(f) \(N_{q}=95 \text { for } \phi=38^{\circ} \text { and } \frac{L}{d}=\frac{15}{0.45}=33.33\) from Fig. 15.9

 

(g) \(A_{b}=0.159 m ^{2}, P=1.413 m\).

 

(h) \(\bar{K}_{s}=\frac{1}{2} \tan ^{2}\left(45^{\circ}+\frac{\phi}{2}\right)=\frac{1}{2} K_{p} \cdot \bar{K}_{s}\) for each layer can be calculated.

 

(i) \(\delta=0.75 \phi\). The values of tan δ can be calculated for each layer.

The computed values for all the layers are given below in a tabular form.

 

Layer no. \(G_{s}\) \(\gamma_{b}\) \(\bar{q}_{0}^{\prime}\) \(\bar{K}_{s}\) \(\tan \delta\) \(\Delta L\)
\(kN / m ^{3}\) \(kN / m ^{2}\) m
1 2.69 10.36 41.44 1.5 0.414 8
2 2.61 9.57 111.59 1.845 0.493 6
3 2.69 10.05 150.35 2.1 0.543 2
From middle of layer 3 to tip of pile = 10.05
At the tip of pile \(q_{0}^{\prime}=160.40 kN / m ^{2}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{u}=&(160.4 \times 95 \times 0.159+1.413(41.44 \times 1.5 \times 0.414 \times 8\\&+111.59 \times 1.845 \times 0.493 \times 6+150.35 \times 2.10 \times 0.543 \times 2) \\=& 2423+1636 \approx 4059 kN\end{aligned}\)

 

\(Q_{a}=\frac{Q_{u}}{2.5}=\frac{4059}{2.5}=1624 kN\)
