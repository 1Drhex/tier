Guest Question.

Beams, girders, and columns support a reinforced 4-in.-thick concrete floor as shown in the plan view of Fig. 14.34. The beams, girders, and floor continue on all sides, and the concrete floor provides full lateral support for the members. Reinforced concrete weighs \(150 lb/ft^{3}\). Design beam \(B_{1}\) and girder \(G_{1}\) if the floor supports a live load of \(65 lb/ft^{2}\) and the dead load of its own weight. Assume that all members are simply supported and use steel that has an allowable bending stress of 24 ksi and an allowable shear stress of 14.5 ksi. (Girders are major beams that support cross beams.)

Admin
Solution

Design of Beam \(B_{1}\): The weight of the 4-in.-thick concrete floor per square foot is (4/12)(150) = 50 \(lb/ft^{2}\). The beam supports a strip of floor of width \(L_{3} = 8 \ ft\) as shown in the figure; therefore, the dead load (weight) of the concrete floor per linear foot of the beam

\(w_{1} = 50\ lb/ft^{2} (8 ft) = 400\ lb/ft of beam\)

and the live load per linear foot of the beam

\(w_{2} = 65\ lb/ft^{2} (8 ft) = 520\ lb/ft\) of beam

Therefore, the total load per foot of the beam, not including the weight of the beam,

\(w = w_{1} + w_{2} = 400 + 520 = 920\ lb/ft\) of beam

The beam is simply supported and has a span of 25 ft and a uniform load w = 920 lb/ft = 0.920 kip/ft. From Eq. (c) of

\(V_{\max }=\frac{w L}{2} \quad and \quad M_{\max }=\frac{w L^{2}}{8}\quad(c)\)

 

\(M_{max}=\frac{wL^{2}}{8}=\frac{0.92(25)^{2}}{8}=71.88\ kip-ft=862.5\ kip-in\)

and from Eq. (14.13), the required section modulus

 

\(\sigma_{a} \geq \frac{M_{\max }}{S} \text { or } S_{\mathrm{req}} \geq \frac{M_{\max }}{\sigma_{a}}\)

 

\(S_{req}\geq \frac{M_{max}}{\sigma _{a}}=\frac{862.5}{24}=35.9\ in.^{3}\)

We select a W12 × 30 with \(S = 38.6\ in.^{3}\) from Table A.3 of the Appendix. It is the lightest beam in any depth group that has a section modulus greater than \(35.9 in.^{3}\) The uniform load including the weight of the beam w = 0.92 + 0.03 = 0.95 kip/ft; therefore,

\(M_{max}=\frac{wL^{2}}{8}=\frac{0.95(25)^{2}}{8}=74.2\ kip-ft=890.6\ kip-in.\)

and the required section modulus

\(S_{req}\geq \frac{M_{max}}{\sigma _{a}}=\frac{890.6}{24}=37.1\ in.^{3}\lt S_{x}=38.6\ in.^{3}\) OK

Check of shear stress: From Eq. (c) of ,

\(R=V_{max}=\frac{wL}{2}=\frac{0.95(25)}{2}=11.88\ kip\)

\(\tau _{max}=\frac{V_{max}}{dt}=\frac{11.88}{12.34(0.260)}=3.70\ ksi\lt \tau _{a}=14.5\ ksi\) OK

Use W12 × 30 for beam \(B_{1}\).

Design of Girder \(G_{1}\): Each girder supports the end reactions (\(R_{B} = 11.88\ kip\)) from four beams—two end reactions at each third point of the girder—and its own weight. The shear-force and bending-moment diagrams for a beam loaded at its third points are shown in Fig. 14.13 of . From that example, \(P = 2R_{B} = 2(11.88) = 23.76\ kip\) and L = 24 ft. The maximum shear force \(V_{max} = P = 23.76\ kip\), and the maximum moment \(M_{max} = PL/3 = 23.76(24)/3 = 190.1\ kip-ft = 2280\ kip-in.\) The required section modulus from Eq. (14.13)

\(S_{req}\geq \frac{M_{max}}{\sigma _{a}}=\frac{2280}{24}=95\ in.^{3}\)

We select a W18 × 55 from Table A.3 of the Appendix with a section modulus S = 98.3 \(in.^{3}\) It is the lightest beam in any depth group with a section modulus greater than 95 \(in.^{3}\) The beam weighs 55 lb/ft = 0.055 kip/ft; therefore, the maximum moment including the weight of the beam

\(M_{max}=190.1+\frac{0.055(24)^{2}}{8}=194.1\ kip-ft=2329\ kip-in.\)

Accordingly, the required section modulus

\(S_{req}\geq \frac{M_{max}}{\sigma _{a}}=\frac{2329}{24}=97.0\ in.^{3}\lt S_{x}=98.3\ in.^{3}\) OK

Check of shear stress: The maximum shear force including the weight of the beam

\(V_{max}=23.76+\frac{0.055(24)}{2}=24.4\ kip\)

Therefore, the maximum shear stress

\(\tau _{max}=\frac{V_{max}}{dt}=\frac{24.4}{18.11(0.390)}=3.46\ ksi\lt\tau _{a}=14.5\ ksi\) OK

Use W18 × 55 for girder \(G_{1}\).
