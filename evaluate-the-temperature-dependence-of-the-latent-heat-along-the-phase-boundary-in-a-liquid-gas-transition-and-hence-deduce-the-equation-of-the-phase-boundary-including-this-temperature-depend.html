Guest Question.

Evaluate the temperature dependence of the latent heat along the phase boundary in a liquid–gas transition and hence deduce the equation of the phase boundary including this temperature dependence.

Admin
Solution

Along the phase boundary, we can write that the gradient in the tem-perature is (see Fig. 28.5)

\( \frac{d}{dT}=\left(\frac{\partial}{\partial T} \right) _{p}+\frac{dp}{dT}\left(\frac{\partial}{\partial p} \right)_{T}.\) (28.24)

Hence, applying this to the quantity \(\Delta S=S_{v}-S_{L}=L/T\) where the subscripts v and L refer to vapour and liquid respectively, we have that

\( \frac{d}{dT}\left(\frac{L}{T} \right) = \left(\frac{\partial (\Delta S)}{\partial T} \right)_{p}+\frac{dp}{dT}\left(\frac{\partial (\Delta S)}{\partial p} \right)_{T}\)
\( =\frac{C_{pv}-C_{pL}}{T}+\left[\left(\frac{\partial S_{v}}{\partial p} \right)_{T}-\left(\frac{\partial S_{L}}{\partial p} \right)_{T} \right]\frac{dp}{dT},\) (28.25)

so that

\( \frac{d}{dT}\left(\frac{L}{T} \right)=\frac{C_{pv}-C_{pL}}{T}-\left[\frac{\partial}{\partial T}(V_{v}-V_{L}) \right]\frac{dp}{dT}.\) (28.26)

Using \(V_{v}\gg V_{L}\) and \(pV_{v} = RT,\) we have that

\( \frac{d}{dT}\left(\frac{L}{T} \right)=\frac{C_{pv}-C_{pL}}{T}-\frac{R}{p}\times \frac{Lp}{RT^{2}},\) (28.27)

and expanding

\( \frac{d}{dT}\left(\frac{L}{T} \right)=\frac{1}{T}\frac{dL}{dT}-\frac{L}{T^{2}},\) (28.28)

yields

\( dL=(C_{pv}-C_{pL})dT,\) (28.29)

so that

\( L=L_{0}+(C_{pv}-C_{pL})T.\) (28.30)

Thus the latent heat contains a linear temperature dependence and this is shown by the solid line in Fig. 28.4. The slope is negative because \( C_{pL} \gt C_{pv}.\) Substituting this value of L into eqn 28.19 yields the equa-tion of the phase boundary:

\( p=p_{0}\exp \left(-\frac{L_{0}}{RT}+\frac{(C_{pv}-C_{pL})\ln T}{R} \right).\) (28.31)

Fig. 28.4 The temperature depen-dence of the latent heat of water. The solid line is according to eqn 28.30. Fig. 28.5 The phase boundary.
