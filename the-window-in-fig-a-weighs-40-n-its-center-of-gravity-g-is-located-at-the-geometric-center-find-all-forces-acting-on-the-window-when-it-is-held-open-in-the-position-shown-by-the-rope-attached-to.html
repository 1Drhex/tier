Guest Question.

The window in Fig. (a) weighs 40 N; its center of gravity G is located at the geometric center. Find all forces acting on the window when it is held open in the position shown by the rope attached to C. Assume that the hinge at A can provide an axial thrust whereas the hinge at B cannot.

Admin
Solution

We begin by drawing the FBD of the window—see Fig. (b). In addition to its 40-lb weight and the tension \(T_{CD}\) in the rope, the window is acted on by the hinge reactions at A and B. Note that an axial thrust (force component in the x-direction) is shown only for the hinge at A.

The FBD contains six unknowns: \(A_{x} , A_{y} , A_{z} , B_{y} , B_{z}\) , and \(T_{CD}\). Because the force system is the general case, there are also six independent equilibrium equations, which means that the problem is statically determinate.

Point A plays an important role in the analysis of this problem, because three of the unknowns (\(A_{x} , A_{y}\) , and \(A_{z}\) ) pass through A. Referring to the FBD, we examine the following moment equations:

\(Σ(M_{A})_{x}\) = 0— contains the unknown \(T_{CD}\)

 

\(Σ(M_{A})_{y}\) = 0— contains the unknowns \(T_{CD}\) and \(B_{z}\) (\(B_{y}\) is parallel to this axis)

 

\(Σ(M_{A})_{z}\) = 0— contains the unknowns \(T_{CD}\) and \(B_{y}\) (\(B_{z}\) is parallel to this axis)

These three scalar equations can be solved for the unknowns \(T_{CD}\), \(B_{y}\) , and \(B_{z}\) . Once these values are known, the three force components at A (\(A_{x}\) , \(A_{y}\) , and \(A_{z}\) ) can be found from the force equation ΣF = 0.

Mathematical Details

We will use the vector representation to find the moment equations about the axes passing through A; that is, \(\Sigma M_{A}=(\Sigma M_{A})_{x}i+(\Sigma M_{A})_{y}j+(\Sigma M_{A})_{z}k=0\)

\(ΣM_{A} = 0\) \((r_{AD} × T_{CD}) + (r_{AB} × B) + (r_{AG} ×W) = 0\)

Writing the forces and position vectors in rectangular form, we have

\(T_{CD}=T_{CD}\lambda _{CD}=T_{CD}\frac{\overrightarrow{CD} }{\left|\overrightarrow{CD} \right| }=T_{CD}\left(\frac{1.9i − 2.2j + 3.8k}{4.784} \right)\)

 

\(B = B_{y}j + B_{z}k\) W = −40k N

 

\(r_{AD}\) = −1.5i + 2k m \(r_{AB}\) = −3i m

 

\(r_{AG}\) = −1.5i + 1.1j − 0.9k m

Then, the determinant form of the equation \(ΣM_{A}\) = 0 is

 

\(\frac{T_{CD}}{4.784}\left|\begin{matrix} i & j & k \\ -1.5 & 0 & 2 \\ 1.9 & -2.2 & 3.8 \end{matrix} \right|+\left|\begin{matrix} i & j & k \\ -3 & 0 & 0 \\0 & B_{y} & B_{z} \end{matrix} \right|+\left|\begin{matrix} i & j & k \\ -1.5 & 1.1 & -0.9 \\ 0 & 0 & -40 \end{matrix} \right|=0\)

 

Expanding the above determinants and equating like components, we get

(x-component) 0.9197\(T_{CD}\) −44.0=0

 

(y-component) 1.9858\(T_{CD}\) +3\(B_{z}\) − 60.0=0

 

(z-component) 0.6898\(T_{CD}\) − 3\(B_{y}\) =0

Solving these equations gives

\(T_{CD}\) = 47.84 N \(B_{y}\) = 11.00 N \(B_{z}\) = −11.67 N

Omitting the details, the remaining three unknowns are found from the force equation ΣF = 0 to be

\(A_{x}\) = −19.00 N \(A_{y}\) = 11.00 N \(A_{z}\) = 13.67 N
