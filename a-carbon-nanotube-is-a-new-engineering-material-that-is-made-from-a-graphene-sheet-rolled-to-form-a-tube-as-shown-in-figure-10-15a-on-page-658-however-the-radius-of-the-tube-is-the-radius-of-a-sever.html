Guest Question.

A carbon nanotube is a new engineering material that is made from a graphene sheet rolled to form a tube as shown in Figure 10.15a on page 658. However, the radius of the tube is the radius of a several carbon atoms. The radius of 1 carbon atom is 0.34 nm. If the nanotube is long enough, the continuum assumption may be used and the tube can be modeled as a beam. Determine the five lowest natural frequencies and their corresponding mode shapes of a carbon nanotube with a mean radius of 2 nm and length of 100 nm. The elastic modulus of a carbon nanotube is 1 GPa, and its mass density is \(2.3 g/cm^3\) . Assume the tube is fixed at one end and free at the other.

The nondimensional natural frequencies are the square roots of the solutions of this equation. If \(\omega_{k}\) is a nondimensional natural frequency, the corresponding dimensional natural frequency is

\(\tilde{\omega}_{k}=\omega _{k}\sqrt{\frac{EI}{\rho AL^{4}} } \) (b)

where

\(\sqrt{\frac{EI}{\rho AL^{4}} }=\sqrt{\frac{\left(1\times 10^{12}\frac{N}{m^{2}}\right) \frac{\pi}{2}[(2\times10^{-9}m)^{4}-(1.66\times10^{-9}m)^{4}]}{\left(2.3\frac{g}{cm^{3}} \right) \left(\frac{1kg}{1000g} \right) \left(\frac{100cm}{m} \right)^{3} \pi[(2\times 10^{-9}m)^{2}](100\times10^{-9}m)^{4}} }\)

\(=2.71\times10^{9}\frac{1}{s} \) (c)

The corresponding mode shapes are also given in Table 10.4. For a fixed-free beam they are

\( X_{k}(x)=C_{k}[\cosh\lambda ^{1/4}x-\cos\lambda ^{1/4}x-\alpha _{k}(\sinh\lambda ^{1/4}x-\sin\lambda ^{1/4}x)]\) (i)

where

\(\alpha _{k}=\frac{\cos\lambda ^{1/4}+\cosh\lambda ^{1/4}}{\sin\lambda ^{1/4}+\sinh} \) (j)

The first five nondimensional mode shapes are

\(X_{1}(x)=C_{1}[\cosh1.87x-\cos1.87x-0.73(\sinh1.87x-\sin1.87x)] \) (k)

\( X_{2}(x)=C_{2}[\cosh4.69x-\cos4.69x-1.02(\sinh4.69-\sin4.69x)]\) (l)

\( X_{3}(x)=C_{3}[\cosh7.86x-\cos7.86x-0.999(\sinh7.86-\sin7.87x)]\) (m)

\(X_{4}(x)=C_{4}[\cosh11.0x-\cos11.0x-sinh11.0+\sin11.0x] \) (n)

\(X_{5}(x)=C_{5}[\cosh14.14x-\cos14.14x-\sinh14.14+\sin14.14x] \) (o)

The normalization of the mode shapes is with respect to the kinetic energy scalar product which yields

\( C_i=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\int_{0}^{1}{[\cosh\lambda _{i}x+\cos\lambda _{i}x-\alpha _{i}(\sinh\lambda _{i}x-\sin\lambda _{i}x)]^{2}dx} } } \) (p)

Evaluation of the first five constants yields

\(\begin{matrix} C_{1}=1.003,&C_{2}=1,&C_{3}=1,&C_{4}=1,&C_{5}=1 \end{matrix} \) (q)
