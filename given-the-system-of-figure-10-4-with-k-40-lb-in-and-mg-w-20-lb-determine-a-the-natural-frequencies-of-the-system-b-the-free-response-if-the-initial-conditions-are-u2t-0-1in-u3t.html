Guest Question.

Given the system of Figure 10.4 with k = 40 lb/in. and mg= W = 20 lb, determine

(a) The natural frequencies of the system.

(b) The free response, if the initial conditions are

\(\ U_{2}(t=0) = 1 in. \) \(\ U_{3}(t=0) = 0.5 in. \) \(\ \dot{U} _{2}(t=0) = \dot{U} _{3}(t=0) = 0 \)

These initial conditions are speciﬁed in reference to the equilibrium position of the system, so the computed displacement functions do not include the effect of gravity.

Admin
Solution

Per Equation 10.41, the natural circular frequencies are

\(\ \omega _{1} = \sqrt{\frac{K}{m} } \)

(10.41)

\(\ \omega _{2} = \sqrt{6\frac{K}{m} } \)

 

\(\ \omega _{1} = \sqrt{\frac{K}{m} } = \sqrt{\frac{40}{20/ g} } = \sqrt{\frac{40(386.4)}{20} } = 27.8rad/sec \)

\(\ \omega _{2} = \sqrt{\frac{6K}{m} } = \sqrt{\frac{6(40)}{20/ g} } = \sqrt{\frac{6(40)(386.4)}{20}} = 68.1 rad/ sec \)

The free-vibration response is given by Equation 10.35 as

\(\ U_{2}(t) = A_{2}sin(\omega t+\phi ) \)

(10.35)

\(\ U_{3}(t) = A_{3}sin(\omega t+\phi ) \)

 

\(\ U_{2}(t) = A^{(1)}_{2}sin(27.8t+\phi _{1} ) + A^{(2)}_{2}sin(68.1t+\phi _{2} ) \)

\(\ U_{3}(t) = A^{(1)}_{2}sin(27.8t+\phi _{1} ) – 0.5 A^{(2)}_{2}sin(68.1t+\phi _{2} ) \)

The amplitudes and phase angles are determined by applying the initial conditions, which are

\(\ U_{2}(0) = 1 = A^{(1)}_{2}sin\phi _{1} + A^{(2)}_{2}sin\phi _{2} \)

\(\ U_{3}(0) = 0.5 = 2A^{(1)}_{2}sin\phi _{1} – 0.5A^{(2)}_{2}sin\phi _{2} \)

\(\ \dot{U} _{2} (0) = 0 = 27.8A^{(1)}_{2}cos\phi _{1} + 68.1A^{(2)}_{2}cos\phi _{2} \)

\(\ \dot{U} _{3} (0) = 0 = 2(27.8)A^{(1)}_{2}cos\phi _{1} – 0.5(68.1)A^{(2)}_{2}cos\phi _{2} \)

The initial conditions produce a system of four algebraic equations in the four unknown \(\ A^{(1)}_{2},A^{(2)}_{2},\phi _{1},\phi _{2} \) . Solution of the equations is not trivial, owing to the presence of the trigonometric functions. Letting \(\ P=A^{(1)}_{2}sin\phi _{1} \) and \(\ Q=A^{(2)}_{2}sin\phi _{2} \) , the displacement initial condition equations become

P + Q = 1

2P −0.5Q = 0.5

which are readily solved to obtain

\(\ P=A^{(1)}_{2}sin\phi _{1}=0.4 \)

\(\ Q=A^{(2)}_{2}sin\phi _{2}=0.6 \)

Similarly, setting \(\ R=A^{(1)}_{2}cos\phi _{1} \) and \(\ S=A^{(2)}_{2}sin\phi _{2} \) , the initial velocity equations are

27.8R +68.1S = 0

2(27.8)R −0.5(68.1)S = 0

representing a homogeneous system in the variables R and S. Nontrivial solutions exist only if the determinant of the coefﬁcient matrix is zero. In this case, the determinant is not zero, as may easily be veriﬁed by direct computation. There are no nontrivial solutions; hence, R = S = 0. Based on physical argument, the amplitudes cannot be zero, so we must conclude that \(\ cos \phi _{1} = cos \phi _{2} = 0 ⇒ \phi _{1} = \phi _{2} \) = π/2. It follows that the sine function of the phase angles have unity value; hence, \(\ A^{(1)}_{2} = 0.4 \) and \(\ A^{(2)}_{2}= 0.6 \) . Substituting the amplitudes into the general solution form while noting that sin(ωt +π/2) = cos ωt, the free-vibration response of each mass is

\(\ U_{2}(t) \) = 0.4cos 27.8t +0.6cos 68.1t

\(\ U_{3}(t) \) = 0.8cos 27.8t −0.3cos 68.1t

The displacement response of each mass is seen to be a combination of motions corresponding to the natural circular frequencies of the system. Such a phenomenon is characteristic of vibrating structural systems. All the natural modes of vibration participate in the general motion of a structure.
