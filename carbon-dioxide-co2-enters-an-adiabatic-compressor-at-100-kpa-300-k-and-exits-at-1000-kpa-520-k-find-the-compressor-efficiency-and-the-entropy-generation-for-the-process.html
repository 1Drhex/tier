Guest Question.

Carbon dioxide, \(CO _{2}\), enters an adiabatic compressor at 100 kPa, 300 K, and exits at 1000 kPa, 520 K. Find the compressor efficiency and the entropy generation for the process.

Admin
Solution

C.V. Ideal compressor. We will assume constant heat capacity.
Energy Eq.4.13: \(w _{ c }= h _{1}- h _{2}\),

Entropy Eq.7.9: \(s _{2}= s _{1}: T _{2 s }= T _{1}\left(\frac{ P _{2}}{ P _{1}}\right)^{\frac{ k -1}{ k }}=300\left(\frac{1000}{100}\right)^{0.2242}=502.7 K\)

\(w _{ cs }= C _{ p }\left( T _{1}- T _{2 s }\right)=0.842(300-502.7)=-170.67 kJ / kg\)

C.V. Actual compressor

\(\begin{array}{l}w_{\text {cac }}=C_{p}\left(T_{1}-T_{2 a c}\right)=0.842(300-520)=-185.2 kJ / kg \\\eta_{c}=w_{c s} / w_{c a c}=-170.67 /(-185.2)= 0 . 9 2\end{array}\)

 

Use Eq.6.16 for the change in entropy

\(\begin{array}{l}s _{ gen }= s _{2 ac }- s _{1}= C _{ p } \ln \left( T _{2 ac } / T _{1}\right)- R \ln \left( P _{2} / P _{1}\right) \\\quad=0.842 \ln (520 / 300)-0.1889 \ln (1000 / 100)= 0 . 0 2 8 k J / kg K\end{array}\)

 

Constant heat capacity is not the best approximation. It would be more accurate to use Table A.8. Entropy change in Eq.6.19 and Table A.8:

\(s _{ T 2}^{ o }= s _{ T 1}^{ o }+ R \ln \left( P _{2} / P _{1}\right)=4.8631+0.1889 \ln (1000 / 100)=5.29806\)

Interpolate in A.8 \(\Rightarrow T _{2 s }=481 K , \quad h _{2 s }=382.807 kJ / kg =>\)

\(– w _{ cs }=382.807-214.38=168.43 kJ / kg ; – w _{ cac }=422.12-214.38=207.74 kJ / kg\)

 

\(\eta_{ c }= w _{ cs } / w _{ cac }=-168.43 /(-207.74)= 0 . 8 1\)

 

\(s _{ gen }= s _{2 ac }- s _{1}=5.3767-4.8631-0.1889 \ln (10)=0.0786 kJ / kgK\)
