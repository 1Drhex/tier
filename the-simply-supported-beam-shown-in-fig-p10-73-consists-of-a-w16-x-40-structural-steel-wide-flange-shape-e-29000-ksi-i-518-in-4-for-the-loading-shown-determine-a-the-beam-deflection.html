Guest Question.

The simply supported beam shown in Fig. P10.73 consists of a W16 × 40 structural steel wide-flange shape [E = 29,000 ksi; I = 518 \(\text { in. }^{4}\)]. For the loading shown, determine:
(a) the beam deflection at point C.
(b) the beam deflection at point F.

Admin
Solution

(a) Beam deflection at point C
Consider 40-kip concentrated load at B.
[Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated load not at midspan.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{C}=-\frac{P b x}{6 L E I}\left(L^{2}-b^{2}-x^{2}\right)\) (elastic curve)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&P=40 \text { kips, } L=18 ft , b=4 ft , x=10 ft ,\\&E I=1.5022 \times 10^{7} kip – in .^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{C} &=-\frac{P b x}{6 L E I}\left(L^{2}-b^{2}-x^{2}\right) \\&=-\frac{(40 kips )(4 ft )(10 ft )(12 in . / ft )^{3}}{6(18 ft )\left(1.5022 \times 10^{7} kip – in .{ }^{2}\right)}\left[(18 ft )^{2}-(4 ft )^{2}-(10 ft )^{2}\right]=-0.354467 in .\end{aligned}\)

 

Consider 30-kip concentrated load at D.
[Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated load not at midspan.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{C}=-\frac{P b x}{6 L E I}\left(L^{2}-b^{2}-x^{2}\right) \text { (elastic curve) }\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&P=30 kips , L=18 ft , b=6 ft , x=8 ft, \\&E I=1.5022 \times 10^{7} kip – in. ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{C} &=-\frac{P b x}{6 L E I}\left(L^{2}-b^{2}-x^{2}\right) \\&=-\frac{(30 kips )(6 ft )(8 ft )(12 in. / ft )^{3}}{6(18 ft )\left(1.5022 \times 10^{7} kip – in .{ }^{2}\right)}\left[(18 ft )^{2}-(6 ft )^{2}-(8 ft )^{2}\right]=-0.343560 in .\end{aligned}\)

 

Consider 20-kip concentrated load at F. [Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated moment.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{C}=-\frac{M x}{6 L E I}\left(2 L^{2}-3 L x+x^{2}\right)\) (elastic curve)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&M=-(20 kips )(6 ft )=-120 kip – ft , L=18 ft, \\&x=10 ft , E I=1.5022 \times 10^{7} kip – in .{ }^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{C} &=-\frac{M x}{6 L E I}\left(2 L^{2}-3 L x+x^{2}\right) \\&=-\frac{(-120 kip – ft )(10 ft )(12 in . / ft )^{3}}{6(18 ft )\left(1.5022 \times 10^{7} kip – in .{ }^{2}\right)}\left[2(18 ft )^{2}-3(18 ft )(10 ft )+(10 ft )^{2}\right]=0.265850 in .\end{aligned}\)

Beam deflection at C

\(v_{C}=-0.354467 \text { in. }-0.343560 \text { in. }+0.265850 in .=-0.432177 in .=0.432 in . \downarrow\)

 

(b) Beam deflection at point F
Consider 40-kip concentrated load at B.
[Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated load not at midspan.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(\theta_{E}=\frac{P a\left(L^{2}-a^{2}\right)}{6 L E I} \text { (slope magnitude) }\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&P=40 kips , L=18 ft , a=4 ft ,\\&E I=1.5022 \times 10^{7} kip – in .^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}&\theta_{E}=\frac{P a\left(L^{2}-a^{2}\right)}{6 L E I}=\frac{(40 kips )(4 ft )(12 in . / ft )^{2}}{6(18 ft )\left(1.5022 \times 10^{7} kip – in .{ }^{2}\right)}\left[(18 ft )^{2}-(4 ft )^{2}\right]=0.0043740 rad \\&v_{F}=(6 ft )(12 in . / ft )(0.0043740 rad )=0.314930 in .\end{aligned}\)

 

Consider 30-kip concentrated load at D.
[Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated load not at midspan.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(\theta_{E}=\frac{P a\left(L^{2}-a^{2}\right)}{6 L E I} \text { (slope magnitude) }\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&P=30 \text { kips, } L=18 ft , x=8 ft , a=12 ft, \\&E I=1.5022 \times 10^{7} \text { kip-in. }^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}&\theta_{E}=\frac{P a\left(L^{2}-a^{2}\right)}{6 L E I}=\frac{(30 kips )(12 ft )(12 in . / ft )^{2}}{6(18 ft )\left(1.5022 \times 10^{7} kip – in ^{2}\right)}\left[(18 ft )^{2}-(12 ft )^{2}\right]=0.0057516 rad \\&v_{F}=(6 ft )(12 in . / ft )(0.0057516 rad )=0.414113 in.\end{aligned}\)

 

Consider deflection at F resulting from rotation at E caused by 20-kip load on overhang EF.
[Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated moment.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(\theta_{E}=\frac{M L}{3 E I} \text { (slope magnitude) }\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&M=(20 kips )(6 ft )=120 kip – ft , L=18 ft, \\&E I=1.5022 \times 10^{7} kip – in .{ }^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}&\theta_{E}=\frac{M L}{3 E I}=\frac{(120 kip – ft )(18 ft )(12 in . / ft )^{2}}{3\left(1.5022 \times 10^{7} kip – in .{ }^{2}\right)}=0.0069019 rad \\&v_{F}=-(6 ft )(12 in . / ft )(0.0069019 rad )=-0.496935 in .\end{aligned}\)

 

Determine cantilever deflection due to concentrated load on overhang EF.
[Appendix C, Cantilever beam with concentrated load.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{F}=-\frac{P L^{3}}{3 E I} \text { (assuming fixed support at } E \text { ) }\)

Values:

\(P=20 \text { kips, } L=6 ft , E I=1.5022 \times 10^{7} \text { kip-in. }{ }^{2}\)

Computation: \(v_{F}=-\frac{P L^{3}}{3 E I}=-\frac{(20 \text { kips })(6 ft )^{3}(12 in . / ft )^{3}}{3\left(1.5022 \times 10^{7} kip – in .^{2}\right)}=-0.165645 in .\)

Beam deflection at F

\(\begin{aligned}v_{F} &=0.314930 in .+0.414113 in .-0.496935 in .-0.165645 in . \\&=0.066463 in .=0.0665 in . \uparrow\end{aligned}\)
