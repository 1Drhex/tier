Guest Question.

Determine the force in each member of the space truss shown in Fig. \( 3-38 (\mathrm{a})\). The truss is supported by a ball-and-socket joint at \(A\), a slotted roller joint at \(B\), and a cable at \(C\).

Admin
Solution

The truss is statically determinate since \(b+r=3 j\) or \(9+6=3(5)\), Fig. 3-38b.

Support Reactions. We can obtain the support reactions from the free-body diagram of the entire truss, Fig. 3-38b, as follows:

\( \begin{array}{rrrl} \Sigma M_{y} & =0 ; & -600(4)+B_{x}(8) & =0 & B_{x} & =300 \mathrm{lb} \\ \Sigma M_{z} & =0 ; & C_{y} & =0 & \\ \Sigma M_{x} & =0 ; & B_{y}(8)-600(8) & =0 & B_{y} & =600 \mathrm{lb} \\ \Sigma F_{x} & =0 ; & 300-A_{x} & =0 & A_{x} & =300 \mathrm{lb} \\ \Sigma F_{y} & =0 ; & A_{y}-600 & =0 & A_{y} & =600 \mathrm{lb} \\ \Sigma F_{z} & =0 ; & A_{z}-600 & =0 & A_{z} & =600 \mathrm{lb} \end{array} \)

Joint B. We can begin the method of joints at \(B\) since there are three unknown member forces at this joint, Fig. 3-38c. The components of \(\mathbf{F}_{B E}\) can be determined by proportion to the length of member \(B E\), as indicated by Eqs. 3-5. We have

\( F_{x}=F\left(\frac{x}{l}\right) \quad F_{y}=F\left(\frac{y}{l}\right) \quad F_{z}=F\left(\frac{z}{l}\right) \) (3-5)

\( \begin{array}{llc} \Sigma F_{y}=0 ; & -600+F_{B E}\left(\frac{8}{12}\right)=0 & F_{B E}=900 \mathrm{lb}(\mathrm{T}) \\ \Sigma F_{x}=0 ; & 300-F_{B C}-900\left(\frac{4}{12}\right)=0 & F_{B C}=0 \\ \Sigma F_{z}=0 ; & F_{B A}-900\left(\frac{8}{12}\right)=0 & F_{B A}=600 \mathrm{lb}(\mathrm{C}) \end{array} \)

Joint A. Using the result for \(F_{B A}=600 \mathrm{lb}(\mathrm{C})\), the free-body diagram of joint \(A\) is shown in Fig. 3-38d. We have

\(\begin{array}{cc}\Sigma F_{z}=0 ; & 600-600+F_{A C} \sin 45^{\circ}=0 \\ & F_{A C}=0 \\\Sigma F_{y}=0 ; & -F_{A E}\left(\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}\right)+600=0 \\ & F_{A E}=670.8 \mathrm{lb}(\mathrm{C}) \\ \Sigma F_{x}=0 ;& -300+F_{A D}+670.8\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\right)=0 \\ & F_{A D}=0 & \end{array}\)

Joint D. By inspection the members at joint \(D\), Fig. 3-38a, support zero force, since the arrangement of the members is similar to either of the two cases discussed in reference to Figs. 3-36 and 3-37. Also, from Fig. 3-38e,

\( \begin{array}{ll} \Sigma F_{x}=0 ; & F_{D E}=0 \\ \Sigma F_{z}=0 ; & F_{D C}=0 \end{array} \)

Joint C. By observation of the free-body diagram, Fig. 3-38f,

\( F_{C E}=0 \)
