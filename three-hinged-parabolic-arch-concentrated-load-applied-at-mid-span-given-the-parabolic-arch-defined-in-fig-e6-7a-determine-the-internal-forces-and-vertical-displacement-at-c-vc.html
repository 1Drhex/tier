Three-Hinged Parabolic Arch—Concentrated Load Applied at Mid-Span

Given: The parabolic arch defined in Fig. E6.7a

Determine: The internal forces and vertical displacement at C \((v_{c})\).

Admin
Solution

Enforcing equilibrium leads to the following expressions for the internal forces (Fig. E6.7b):

 

Segment AC \(0\leq x< L/2\)

 

\(F_{x}=-\frac{PL}{4h}\)

 

\(F_{y}=-\frac{P}{2}\)

 

\(M=\frac{P}{2}x-\frac{PL}{4h}y\)

 

Segment CB \(L/2< x\leq L\)

 

\(F_{x}=-\frac{PL}{4h}\)

 

\(F_{y}=-\frac{P}{2}\)

 

\(M=-\frac{P}{2}x-\frac{PL}{4h}y+\frac{PL}{2}\)

 

The corresponding transformed internal forces are

 

Segment AC \(0\leq x< L/2\)

 

\(F=-\frac{P}{2}\sin \theta -\frac{PL}{4h}\cos \theta\)

 

\(V=-\frac{P}{2}\cos \theta +\frac{PL}{4h}\sin \theta\)

 

\(M=\frac{P}{2}x-PL\left [ \frac{x}{L}-\left ( \frac{x}{L} \right )^{2} \right ]=PL\left ( -\frac{x}{2L}+\frac{x^{2}}{L^{2}} \right )\)

 

Segment CB \(L/2< x\leq L\)

 

\(F=\frac{P}{2}\sin \theta -\frac{PL}{4h}\cos \theta\)

 

\(V=\frac{P}{2}\cos \theta +\frac{PL}{4h}\sin \theta\)

 

\(M=PL\left ( -\frac{3x}{2L}+\frac{x^{2}}{L^{2}}+\frac{1}{2} \right )\)

The maximum moment occurs at the location where \(dM/dx = 0\). Note that \(M_{max}=+PL/4\) for a straight member.

 

\(\frac{dM}{dx}=0\Rightarrow \left.\begin{matrix}x\end{matrix}\right|_{M_{max}}=\frac{L}{4}\Rightarrow M_{max}=-\frac{PL}{16}\)

We consider only bending deformation. The displacement at C is given by

 

\(v_{c}=2\int_{0}^{L/2}\frac{P}{2}\left ( x-\frac{L}{2h}y \right )\frac{1}{2}\left ( x-\frac{L}{2h}y \right )\frac{dx}{EI\cos \theta }\)

 

When I is a function of x, we use either symbolic or numerical integration. However, when I is taken as \(I_{0}/\cos \theta\) , the integral simplifies and one can obtain an analytical solution. The analytical solution corresponding to this assumption is

 

\(v_{c}=\frac{PL^{3}}{EI_{0}}\left ( \frac{1}{30} \right )\)
