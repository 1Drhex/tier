Guest Question.

A 741 op amp has an open-loop voltage gain of 2 × \(10^5\), input resistance of 2 MΩ, and output resistance of 50 Ω. The op amp is used in the circuit of Fig. 5.6(a). Find the closed-loop gain \(v_o/v_s\). Determine current i when \(v_s\) = 2 V.

Admin
Solution

Using the op amp model in Fig. 5.4, we obtain the equivalent circuit of Fig. 5.6(a) as shown in Fig. 5.6(b). We now solve the circuit in Fig. 5.6(b) by using nodal analysis. At node 1, KCL gives

\(\frac{v_s-v_1}{10\times 10^3}=\frac{v_1}{2000\times 10^3}+\frac{v_1-v_o}{20\times 10^3}\)

Multiplying through by 2000 × \(10^3\), we obtain

\(200v_s=301v_1-100v_o\)

or

\(2v_s\simeq3v_1-v_o\) ⇒ \(v_1=\frac{2v_s+v_o}{3}\) (5.1.1)

At node O,

\(\frac{v_1-v_o}{20\times 10^3}=\frac{v_o-Av_d}{50}\)

But \(v_d=-v_1\) and A = 200,000. Then

\(v_1-v_o=400(v_o+200,000v_1)\) (5.1.2)

Substituting \(v_1\) from Eq. (5.1.1) into Eq. (5.1.2) gives

\(0\simeq26,667,067v_o+53,333,333v_s\) ⇒ \(\frac{v_o}{v_s}\) = -1.9999699

This is closed-loop gain, because the 20-kΩ feedback resistor closes the loop between the output and input terminals. When \(v_s\) = 2 V, \(v_o\) = -3.9999398 V. From Eq. (5.1.1), we obtain \(v_1\) = 20.066667 μV. Thus,

i = \(\frac{v_1-v_o}{20×10^3}\) = 0.19999 mA

It is evident that working with a nonideal op amp is tedious, as we are dealing with very large numbers.
