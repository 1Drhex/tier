Guest Question.

A 40-lb light is supported at the midpoint of a 10-ft length of wire that is made of 0.2% C hardened steel, as shown in Fig. 4-45a. For reasons of safety, a factor of safety of 3 based on the yield strength of the wire is specified. Spools of wire are available with diameters of 10, 20, 30, 40, and 50 mil (1 mil = 0.001 in.). What spool size would you select for suspending the light?

Admin
Solution

The forces transmitted by cables AB and AC are obtained from the free-body diagram of joint A of the cable system shown in Fig. 4-45b. The wire angle is

\(\theta =cos^{-1}\frac{4}{5}=36.87^\circ \)

From the horizontal component of the equilibrium equation

\(+\rightarrow \sum{F_x}=0: \quad\quad\quad T_{AC}cos\theta -T_{AB}cos\theta =0\) (a)

we get that the two tension forces must be equal, \(T_{AC}= T_{AB}\). Then, from the vertical component of the equilibrium equation

\(+\uparrow \sum{F_y}=0:\quad\quad\quad T_{AC}sin36.87^\circ +T_{AB}sin36.87^\circ-40=0\) (b)

Substituting \(T_{AC} = T_{AB}\) into Eq. (b) gives

\(T_{AC}=T_{AB}=33.33lb\)

For 0.2% C hardened steel (see Table A-17), \(σ_y = 62 ksi\). Therefore, proceeding as in the previous examples

\(d^2\geq \frac{4F(FS)}{\pi \sigma _y} \)

 

\(d^2\geq \frac{4(33.33)(3)}{\pi (62)(10^3)} \)

 

\(d≥0.0453 in\)

The required spool size is 50 (use 50-mil wire).
