Guest Question.

Determine the rank and nullity of the linear transformation (mapping) \(T: P_{2} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) :

\(T(\mathbf{p})=\int_{0}^{1} p(x) d x\)

where \(P_{2}\) is the vector space of polynomials of degree 2 or less.

Admin
Solution

What do you notice about the given linear transform?

It’s the same transform as in of the last section. We have already established that \(\operatorname{range}(T)=\mathbb{R}\) in

What is the rank of \(T ?\)

By the above definition (5.11) we have

\(\operatorname{rank}(T)=\operatorname{dim}(\operatorname{range}(T))=\operatorname{dim}(\mathbb{R})\)

\(\operatorname{dim}(\mathbb{R})=1\) because of the general rule \(\operatorname{dim}\left(\mathbb{R}^{m}\right)=m\). Thus \(\operatorname{rank}(T)=1\)

We also need to find the nullity of \(T\) which is the dimension of the kernel. How?

By using the above formula (5.12):

\(\operatorname{dim}(\operatorname{range}(T))+\operatorname{dim}(\operatorname{ker}(T))=n\)

What is the value of \(n\), the dimension of \(P_{2}\) equal to?

From chapter 3 , Table \(3.1\) states that \(\operatorname{dim}\left(P_{k}\right)=k+1\) so \(\operatorname{dim}\left(P_{2}\right)=3\).

Substituting \(n=\operatorname{dim}\left(P_{2}\right)=3\) and \(\operatorname{dim}(\operatorname{range}(T))=1\) into

\(\operatorname{dim}(\operatorname{range}(T))+\operatorname{dim}(\operatorname{ker}(T))=n\)

gives

\(1+\operatorname{dim}(\operatorname{ker}(T))=3 \quad \text { which yields } \quad \operatorname{dim}(\operatorname{ker}(T))=2\)

Hence the nullity of \(T\) is 2 and the rank of \(T\) is 1 .

Since \(\operatorname{rank}(T)=1\) but dimension of the start vector space \(P_{2}\) is 3 , some of the information has been lost by the linear transform \(T\).

(5.12) Dimension or rank-nullity theorem is

\(\operatorname{rank}(T)+\operatorname{nullity}(T)=n\)

Definition (5.11). Let \(T: V \rightarrow W\) be a linear transform (map) and range \((T)\) be the range. Then the dimension of range \((T)\) is called the rank of \(T\) denoted rank(T) (Fig. 5.21).
