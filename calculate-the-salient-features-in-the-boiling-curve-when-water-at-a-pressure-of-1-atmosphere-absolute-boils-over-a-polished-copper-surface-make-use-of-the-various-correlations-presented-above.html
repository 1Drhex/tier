Guest Question.

Calculate the salient features in the boiling curve when water at a pressure of 1 atmosphere absolute boils over a polished copper surface. Make use of the various correlations presented above.

Admin
Solution

Step 1 Properties of saturated water and saturated steam at \(T_{s}=100{ }^{\circ} C\) are

Saturated water at \(T_{s}=100{ }^{\circ} C\)

Density: \(\rho_{f}=958.4 kg / m ^{3}\)

Thermal conductivity: \(k_{f}=0.680 W / m ^{\circ} C\)

Dynamic viscosity: \(\mu_{f}=2.79 \times 10^{-4} kg / m s\)

Specific heat: \(C_{p f}=4216 J / kg ^{\circ} C\)

Prandtl number: \(\operatorname{Pr}_{f}=1.73\)

Surface tension: \(\sigma=0.059 N / m\)

Saturated steam at \(T_{s}=100{ }^{\circ} C\)

Density: \(\rho_{g}=0.5981 kg / m ^{3}\)

Thermal conductivity: \(k_{g}=0.0248 W / m ^{\circ} C\)

Latent heat of vaporization: \(h_{f g}=2.257 \times 10^{6} J / kg\)

Specific heat: \(C_{p g}=2020 J / kg ^{\circ} C\)

Dynamic viscosity: \(\mu_{g}=1.2 \times 10^{-5} kg / m s\)

Step 2 The critical heat flux is calculated using Eq. 17.83 as

\(q_{c r}=0.18 \rho_{g} h_{f g}\left[\frac{\sigma g\left(\rho_{f}-\rho_{g}\right)}{\rho_{g}^{2}}\right]^{\frac{1}{4}} \approx 0.18 \rho_{g} h_{f g}\left[\frac{\sigma g \rho_{f}}{\rho_{g}^{2}}\right]^{\frac{1}{4}}\) (17.83)

\(q_{c r}=0.18 \times 0.5981 \times 2.257 \times 10^{6}\left[\frac{0.059 \times 9.81(958.4-0.5981)}{0.5981^{2}}\right]^{\frac{1}{4}}= 1.524 Ã— 10^6 W/m^2\)

Step 3 Using Rohsenow correlation (17.88) we calculate the wall superheat parameter under DNB as

\(\Delta T=\frac{C h_{f g}}{C_{p f}}\left[\left(\frac{q_{n b}}{\mu_{f} h_{f g}}\right)\left(\frac{\sigma}{g\left(\rho_{f}-\rho_{g}\right)}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}}\right]^{0.33} \operatorname{Pr}_{f}^{m+1}\) (17.88)

\(\Delta T_{D N B}=\frac{0.0182 \times 2.257 \times 10^{6}}{4216}\left(\frac{1.524 \times 10^{6}}{2.79 \times 10^{-4} \times 2.257 \times 10^{6}}\right)^{0.33} \times\) \(\left(\frac{0.059}{9.81(958.4-0.5981)}\right)^{0.165} \times 1.73=17.66{ }^{\circ} C\)

where the constant C has been chosen as 0.0182 for water boiling over polished copper.

Step 4 Assuming that nucleate boiling initiates when \(\Delta T=6.5 K\), the corresponding heat flux is given by

\(q_{I B}=q_{c r}\left(\frac{\Delta T_{I B}}{\Delta T_{D N B}}\right)^{\frac{1}{0.33}}=1.524 \times 10^{6}\left(\frac{6.5}{\Delta 17.66}\right)^{\frac{1}{0.33}}=73722 W / m ^{2}\)

Step 5 The minimum heat flux under film boiling condition is calculated based on Eq. 17.91

\(q_{M F B}=\frac{q_{c r}}{2 \sqrt{\frac{\rho_{f}+\rho_{g}}{\rho_{g}}}}\) (17.91)

\(q_{M F B}=\frac{1.524 \times 10^{6}}{2 \sqrt{\frac{958.4+0.5981}{0.5981}}}=19029.8 W / m ^{2}\)

We shall assume that the heat transfer coefficient in film boiling is given by Eq. 17.92. The heat flux is then given by \(q_{M F B}=h_{f} \Delta T_{M F B}\). We may then solve for \(\Delta T_{M F B}\) as

\(\Delta T_{M F B}=\frac{\left(\frac{q_{M F B}}{0.425}\right)^{\frac{4}{3}}}{\left(\frac{k_{g}^{3} \rho_{g}\left(\rho_{f}-\rho_{g}\right) g h_{e}}{\mu_{g} \frac{L}{2 \pi}}\right)^{\frac{1}{3}}}\)

To begin with let us assume that \(h_{e}=h_{e}^{0} \approx h_{f g}=2.257 \times 10^{6} J / kg\). The characteristic length \(L_{c}\) is given by

\(L_{c}=2 \pi \sqrt{\frac{0.059}{9.81(958.4-0.5981)}}=0.1157 m\)

Then we get

\(\Delta T_{M F B}^{0}=\frac{\left(\frac{19029.8}{0.425}\right)^{\frac{4}{3}}}{\left(\frac{0.0248^{3} \times 0.5981(958.4-0.5981) \times 9.81 \times 2.257 \times 10^{6}}{1.2 \times 10^{-5} \times \frac{0.1157}{2 \pi}}\right)^{\frac{1}{3}}}=166.3\)

We shall now correct the result by correcting \(h_{e}\) as

\(h_{e}^{1}=h_{f g}+0.4 C_{p g} \Delta T=2.257 \times 10^{6}+0.4 \times 2020 \times 166.3=2.3913 \times 10^{6}\)

The corrected value of superheat is then obtained as

\(\Delta T_{M F B}^{1}=\frac{\Delta T_{M F B}^{0}}{\left(\frac{h_{e}^{1}}{h_{e}^{0}}\right)^{0.33}}=\frac{\Delta 166.3}{\left(\frac{2.391 \times 10^{6}}{2.257 \times 10^{6}}\right)^{0.33}}=163.1{ }^{\circ} C\)

Step 6 We may also calculate the film boiling heat flux with another superheat value of \(\Delta T=500^{\circ} C\). Then we have

\(h_{e}^{1}=2.257 \times 10^{6}+0.4 \times 2020 \times 500=2.661 \times 10^{6}\)

The corresponding heat flux is then obtained using Eq. 17.92 as

\(h_{f}=0.425\left[\frac{k_{g}^{3} \rho_{g}\left(\rho_{f}-\rho_{g}\right) g h_{e}}{\mu_{g} \Delta T\left(\frac{L_{c}}{2 \pi}\right)}\right]^{\frac{1}{4}}\) (17.92)

\(q_{f}=0.425 \times 500^{\frac{3}{4}}\left[\frac{0.0248^{3} \cdot 0.5981(958.4-0.5981) \cdot 9.81 \cdot 2.661 \times 10^{6}}{1.2 \times 10^{-5}\left(\frac{0.1157}{2 \pi}\right)}\right]^{\frac{1}{4}}= 45296.3 W/m^2\)

The calculations made in this example are used to make a plot of the boiling curve as shown in Fig. 17.19. We join the points by straight lines on the log-log plot as shown in the figure. Experimental boiling curves indicate fair agreement with the values calculated here.
