Guest Question.

A Hartnell governor having a central sleeve spring and two right-angled bell crank levers moves between 290 r.p.m. and 310 r.p.m. for a sleeve lift of 15 mm. The sleeve arms and the ball arms are 80 mm and 120 mm respectively. The levers are pivoted at 120 mm from the governor axis and mass of each ball is 2.5 kg. The ball arms are parallel to the governor axis at the lowest equilibrium speed. Determine : 1. loads on the spring at the lowest and the highest equilibrium speeds, and 2. stiffness of the spring.

Admin
Solution

Given : \( N_{1}=290 \text { r.p.m. or } \omega_{1}=2 \pi \times 290 / 60=30.4 rad / s ; N_{2}=310 \text { r.p.m. or } \) \( \omega_{2}=2 \pi \times 310 / 60=32.5 rad / s ; h=15 mm =0.015 m ; y=80 mm =0.08 m ; x=120 mm = \) 0.12 m ; r = 120 mm = 0.12 m ; m = 2.5 kg

 

1. Loads on the spring at the lowest and highest equilibrium speeds

Let S = Spring load at lowest equilibrium speed, and

\(S_{2}\) = Spring load at highest equilibrium speed.

Since the ball arms are parallel to governor axis at the lowest equilibrium speed (i.e. at \(N_{1}\) = 290 r.p.m.), as shown in Fig. (a), therefore

\(r=r_{1}=120 mm =0.12 m\)

We know that centrifugal force at the minimum speed,

\(F_{ Cl }=m\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2} r_{1}=2.5(30.4)^{2} 0.12=277 N\)

Now let us find the radius of rotation at the highest equilibrium speed, i.e. at \(N_{2}\) = 310 r.p.m.

The position of ball arm and sleeve arm at the highest equilibrium speed is shown in Fig. (b).

Let \(r_{2}\) = Radius of rotation at \(N_{2}\) = 310 r.p.m.

We know that \(h=\left(r_{2}-r_{1}\right) \frac{y}{x}\)

or \(r_{2}=r_{1}+h\left(\frac{x}{y}\right)=0.12+0.015\left(\frac{0.12}{0.08}\right)=0.1425 m\)

\(\therefore \) Centrifugal force at the maximum speed,

\(F_{ C 2}=m\left(\omega_{2}\right)^{2} r_{2}=2.5 \times(32.5)^{2} \times 0.1425=376 N\)

Neglecting the obliquity effect of arms and the moment due to the weight of the balls, we have for lowest position,

\(M \cdot g+S_{1}=2 F_{ Cl } \times \frac{x}{y}=2 \times 277 \times \frac{0.12}{0.08}=831 N\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(S_{2}=831 N\) \((\because M=0)\)

and for highest position,

\(M \cdot g+S_{2}=2 F_{ C 2} \times \frac{x}{y}=2 \times 376 \times \frac{0.12}{0.08}=1128 N\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(S_{1}=1128 N\) \((\because M=0)\)

 

2. Stiffness of the spring

We know that stiffness of the spring,

\(s=\frac{S_{2}-S_{1}}{h}=\frac{1128-831}{15}=19.8 N / mm\)
