Guest Question.

For the frame shown in Figure 6.27, calculate the support reactions caused by the applied loads and draw the axial force, shear force and bending moment diagrams. Assume EI is constant throughout.

Admin
Solution

The analysis of the frame is carried out assuming that no sidesway can occur because of the restraining action provided by the roller support.

Member AB: As the member is not subjected to external loads, the fixed-end moments are zero. With \( θ_A = 0 \), the slope-deflection equations for member AB are (Equations 6.17):

\( M_A = \frac{EI}{L} \biggl(4 \theta _A + 2 \theta _B – \frac{6\Delta }{L} \biggr) + M_{FE.A} \) \( M_B = \frac{EI}{L} \biggl(2 \theta _A + 4 \theta _B – \frac{6\Delta }{L} \biggr) + M_{FE.B} \) (6.17a,b).

\( (M_A)_{AB} = \frac{EI}{L} \biggl(4 \theta _A + 2 \theta _B – \frac{6\Delta }{L} \biggr) = \frac{EI}{5} (2 \theta _B) = EI (0.4 \theta _B) \) (1).

\( (M_B)_{AB} = \frac{EI}{L} \biggl(2 \theta _A + 4 \theta _B – \frac{6\Delta }{L} \biggr) = \frac{EI}{5} (4 \theta _B) = EI (0.8 \theta _B) \) (2).

Member BC: From Appendix B, the fixed-end moments are:

\( M_{FE.B} = – \frac{-100 × 3 × 6^2}{9^2} – \frac{-60 × 6 × 3^2}{9^2} = + 173.3 \) kNm.

\( M_{FE.C} = – \frac{-100 × 3^2× 6}{9^2} + \frac{-60 × 6^2 × 3}{9^2} = – 146.6 \) kNm.

and the slope-deflection equations are:

\( (M_B)_{BC} = \frac{EI}{L} \biggl(4 \theta _B + 2 \theta _C – \frac{6\Delta }{L} \biggr) + M_{FE.B} = \frac{EI}{9} (4 \theta _B + 2 \theta _C) + 173.3 \)

\( = EI (0.444 \theta _B + 0.222 \theta _C) + 173.3 \) (3).

\( (M_C)_{BC} = \frac{EI}{L} \biggl(2 \theta _B + 4 \theta _C – \frac{6\Delta }{L} \biggr) + M_{FE.C} = \frac{EI}{9} (2 \theta _B + 4 \theta _C) – 146.6 \)

\( = EI (0.222 \theta _B + 0.444 \theta _C) – 146.6 \) (4).

Member CD: The slope-deflection equation for this member pinned at D is:

\( (M_C)_{CD} = \frac{EI}{L} \biggl(3 \theta _C – \frac{3\Delta }{L} \biggr) + M_{FE^*.C} = \frac{EI}{5} ( 3 \theta _C) = 0.6 EI \theta _C \) (5).

Equilibrium at corner C: For moment equilibrium at corner C:

\( (M_C)_{BC} + (M_C)_{CD} = 0 \) and therefore:

\( EI(0.222\theta _B + 0.444\theta _C ) – 146.6 +EI(0.6\theta _C ) = 0 \).

\( EI(0.222\theta _B + 1.044\theta _C ) – 146.6 = 0 \). (6).

Equilibrium at corner B: For moment equilibrium at corner B:

\( (M_B)_{AB} + (M_B)_{BC} = 0 \) and therefore:

\( EI(0.8\theta _B) +EI(0.444\theta _B + 0.222\theta _C ) +173.3 =0 \). (7).

Solving Equations 6 and 7 simultaneously, we get:

\(\theta _B = – \frac{170.8}{EI} \) and \(\theta _C = – \frac{176.8}{EI} \).

which substituted into Equations 1 through 5 gives:

\( (M_A)_{AB} = EI(0.4 θ_B) = −68.3 \) kNm.

\( (M_B)_{AB} = EI(0.8 θ_B) = −136.7 \) kNm.

\( (M_B )_{BC} = EI(0.444θ_B + 0.222 θ_C ) +173.3 = +136.7 \) kNm.

\( (M_C )_{BC} = EI(0.222 θ_B + 0.444 θ_C) −146.6 = −106.1 \) kNm.

\( (M_C)_{CD} = 0.6EI θ_C = +106.1 \) kNm.

Equilibrium: The moment reaction at A is a negative (clockwise) couple \( (M_A)_{AB} \) of magnitude 68.3 kNm, as shown on the free-body diagram in Figure 6.28a, together with the other five force reactions, \( H_A, V_A, H_C, H_D \) and \( V_D \). Free-body diagrams of AB, BCD and CD are shown in Figure 6.28b, c and d, respectively, and are used to calculate the unknown reactions.

Summing the moments about C in Figure 6.28d:

\( (H_D × 5) − 106.1 = 0 \) ∴ \( H_D = +21.21 \) kN (i.e. ←).

Summing the moments about B in Figure 6.28b:

\( 68.3 + 136.6 − (H_A × 5) = 0 \) ∴ \( H_A = 41.00 \) kN (i.e. →).

Summing the horizontal forces on Figure 6.28a:

\( 41.00 − 21.21 − H_C = 0 \) ∴ \( H_C = 19.79 \) kN (i.e. ←).

Summing the moments about B in Figure 6.28c:

\( 100 × 3 + 60 × 6 + (21.21 × 5) − 136.7 − (V_D × 9) = 0 \) ∴ \( V_D = +69.93 \) kN (i.e. ↑).

Summing the vertical forces on Figure 6.28a:

\( V_A + 69.93 − 100 − 60 = 0 \) ∴ \( V_A = 90.07 \) kN (i.e. ↑).

The above calculations can now be verified by checking moment equilibrium about the support at D in Figure 6.28a:

68.3 + (90.07 × 9) – (100 × 6 ) – (60 × 3) – (19.79 × 5) = 68.3 + 810.6 – 600 – 180 – 98.9 = 0 ∴ OK.

The axial force, shear force and bending moment diagrams are readily determined from statics and are shown in Figure 6.29.
