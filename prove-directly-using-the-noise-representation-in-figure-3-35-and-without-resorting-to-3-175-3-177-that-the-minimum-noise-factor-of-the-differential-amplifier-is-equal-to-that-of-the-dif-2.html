Guest Question.

Prove directly using the noise representation in Figure 3.35, and without resorting to (3.175)– (3.177), that the minimum noise factor of the differential amplifier is equal to that of the differential-mode half circuit and that the optimum noise impedance is twice as large.

Admin
Solution

Assuming that the noise sources in the two half circuits are not statistically correlated, we can derive the expressions for the noise parameters of the differential pair in Figure 3.35 [12] from the definition of the noise factor in the noise impedance formalism (3.120)

\(F=1+\frac{\overline{v_{u 1}^{2}}+\overline{v_{u 2}^{2}}}{\overline{v_{n s 1}^{2}}+\overline{v_{n s 2}^{2}}}+\frac{\left|Z_{c o r 1}^{\prime}+Z_{s 1}\right|^{2} \overline{i_{n 1}^{2}}+\left|Z_{c o r 2}^{\prime}+Z_{s 2}\right|^{2} \overline{i_{n 2}^{2}}}{\overline{v_{n s 1}^{2}}+\overline{v_{n s 2}^{2}}}\)

 

\(F=1+\frac{R_{u 1}^{\prime}+R_{u 2}^{\prime}}{R_{s 1}+R_{s 2}}+\frac{\left|Z_{c o r 1}^{\prime}+Z_{s 1}\right|^{2} G_{n 1}^{\prime}+\left|Z_{c o r 2}^{\prime}+Z_{s 2}\right|^{2} G_{n 2}^{\prime}}{R_{s 1}+R_{s 2}}\)

Since the two differential half circuits are identical, the following relations hold

\(Z_{s 1}=Z_{s 2}=\frac{Z_{s}}{2} ; R_{u 1}^{\prime}=R_{u 2}^{\prime} ; G_{n 1}^{\prime}=G_{n 2}^{\prime} ; Z_{c o r 1}^{\prime}=Z_{c o r 2}^{\prime}\)

As a consequence, the expressions of the noise parameters become

\(F=1+\frac{2 R_{u 1}^{\prime}}{R_{s}}+\frac{\left|2 Z_{\text {cor } 1}^{\prime}+Z_{s}\right|^{2} \frac{G_{n 1}^{\prime}}{2}}{R_{s}} ; R_{u}^{\prime}=2 R_{u 1}^{\prime} ; G_{n}^{\prime}=\frac{G_{n 1}^{\prime}}{2} ; Z_{\text {cor }}^{\prime}=2 Z_{\text {cor }}^{\prime} \)

 

\(X_{\text {soptdif }}=2 X_{\text {sopt }} ; R_{\text {soptdif }}=\sqrt{4 R_{\text {cor1 }}^{2}+4 \frac{R_{u 1}^{\prime}}{G_{n 1}^{\prime}}}=2 R_{\text {sopt }} ; F_{\text {MINdif }}=1+\left|2 R_{\text {sopt }}+2 R_{\text {cor1 }}^{\prime}\right| \frac{G_{n 1}^{\prime}}{2}=F_{\text {MIN }}\)

 

*formalism (3.120) : \(G_{n}^{\prime} \stackrel{\text { def }}{=} \frac{\overline{i_{n}{ }^{2}}}{4 k T \Delta f}\)

*equation 3.175: \(G_{n}^{\prime \text { def }}=\frac{\overline{i_{n}^{2}}}{4 k T \Delta f}=\frac{\left|Z_{21 f}\right|^{2} G_{n f}^{\prime}+\left|Z_{21 a}\right|^{2} G_{n a}^{\prime}}{\left|Z_{21}\right|^{2}}=\left|A_{f}\right|^{2} G_{n f}^{\prime}+\left|A_{a}\right|^{2} G_{n a}^{\prime}\)

*equation 3.176: \(Z_{\text {cor }}^{\prime} \stackrel{\text { def }}{=} \frac{\overline{v_{n} \times i_{n}^{*}}}{\overline{i_{n}^{2}}}=\frac{\left(Z_{\text {corf }}^{\prime}+B_{f}\right) A_{f}^{*} \overline{{i}_{n f}^{2}} +\left(Z_{\text {cora }}^{\prime}+B_f\right) A_{a}^{*} \overline{{i}_{n a}^{2}}}{\left|A_{f}\right|^{2} \overline{{i}_{n f}^{2}} +\left|A_{a}\right|^{2} \overline{{i}_{n a}^{2}}}\)

\(=\frac{\left(Z_{\text {corf }}^{\prime}+B_{f}\right) A_{f}^{*} G_{n f}^{\prime}+\left(Z_{\text {cora }}^{\prime}+B_{a}\right) A_{a}^{*} G_{n a}^{\prime} }{G_{n}^{\prime}}\)

*equation 3.177 : \(R_{u}^{\prime} \stackrel{\text { def }}{=} \frac{\overline{v_{u}^{2}}}{4 k T \Delta f}=\frac{\overline{\left(v_{n}-Z_{c o r}^{\prime} i_{n}\right)^{2}}}{4 k T \Delta f} \)

\(=\frac{\overline{\left(v_{n u f}+v_{n u a}+\left(Z_{c o r f}^{\prime}+B_{f}-Z_{c o r}^{\prime} A_{f}\right) i_{n f}+\left(Z_{c o r a}^{\prime}+B_{a}-Z_{c o r}^{\prime} A_{a}\right) i_{n a}\right)^{2}} }{4 k T \Delta f}\)

 

\(=R_{u f}^{\prime}+R_{u a}^{\prime}+\left|Z_{c o r f}^{\prime}+B_{f}-Z_{c o r}^{\prime} A_{f}\right|^{2} G_{n f}^{\prime}+\left|Z_{c o r a}^{\prime}+B_{a}-Z_{c o r}^{\prime} A_{a}\right|^{2} G_{n a}^{\prime}\)
