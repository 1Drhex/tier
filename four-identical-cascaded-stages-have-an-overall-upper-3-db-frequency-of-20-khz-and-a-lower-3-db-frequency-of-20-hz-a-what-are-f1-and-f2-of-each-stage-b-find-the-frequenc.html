Guest Question.

Four identical cascaded stages have an overall upper 3 dB frequency of 20 kHz and a lower 3 dB frequency of 20 Hz.

(a) What are f1 and f2 of each stage?

(b) Find the frequency range over which the voltage gain is down by less than 2 dB from its midband value .

Admin
Solution

(a) Using the overall upper 3 dB frequency \(f^{(4)}_{2} \) = 20 kHz in Eq. (14.26) , \(\frac{f^{(n)}_{2}}{f_{2}}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2^{1/n}-1} } \) ,with n = 4, the upper 3 dB frequency f2 of each stage is obtained as

\(f_{2}=\frac{f^{(4)}_{2}}{\sqrt{2^{\frac{1}{4} }-1} }=\frac{20kHz}{0.435}\approx 46 kHz \)

Similarly, using the overall lower 3 dB frequency \(f^{(4)}_{1} \) = 20 Hz for n = 4 in Eq.(14.27) , \(\frac{f^{(n)}_{2}}{f_{2}}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2^{1/n}-1} } \) ,with n = 4, the lower 3 dB frequency f1 of each stage is obtained as

\(f_{1}=f^{(4)}_{1}\sqrt{2^{\frac{1}{4} }-1}=20Hz \times 0.435\approx 8.7 Hz \)

(b) Now, let AL and AU denote the voltage gains at lower and upper frequencies fL and fU respectively at which the gains are lowered by 2 dB from its midband gain, say A0. Thus, from Eqs (14.4) , \(|A_{1}| =\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+(f_{1}/f)^2} }\theta _{1} =-\arctan \frac{f_{1}}{f} \) , we can write

\(20 \log_{10}\left| \frac{A_{L}}{A_{0}} \right| =20\log_{10}\left[\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+(f^{(4)}_{1}/f_{L})^2} } \right] =-2 \)

Or , \(-10\log _{10}\left[1+(f^{(4)}_{1}/f_{L})^2\right] =-2 \)

Or , \(\log _{10}\left[1+(f^{(4)}_{1}/f_{L})^2\right] =0.2 \)

Or , \(1+(f^{(4)}_{1}/f_{L})^2=10^{0.2}=1.585 \)

Or , \(\frac{f^{(4)}_{1}}{f_{L}}=\sqrt{0.585}=0.765 \)

which gives the value of fL for \(f^{(4)}_{2} =20 Hz \) as

\(f_{L}=\frac{20Hz}{0.765} =26.14 Hz \)

Similarly, from Eq. (14.5) , \(|A_{2}| =\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+(f/f_{2})^2} }\theta _{2} =\arctan \frac{f}{f_{2}} \) , we get

\(20\log _{10}\left|\frac{A_{U}}{A_{0}} \right|=20\log _{10}\left[\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+(f_{U}/f^{(4)}_{2})^{2}} } \right]=-2 \)

Or , \(\frac{f_{U}}{f^{(4)}_{2}} =0.765 \)

which gives the value of fU for \(f^{(4)}_{2} =20 KHz \) as

\(f_{U}=0.765\times 20 KHz = 15.3 kHz \)

Thus, the frequency range over which the gain is decreased by less than 0.5 dB with respect to the midband gain is from 26.14 Hz to 15.3 kHz.
