Guest Question.

Investigate how sensitivity can play a role in control system design by determining the sensitivity of the transfer function for gain \(K\) variations in the two system block diagrams in Figure 10.24.

Admin
Solution

Solution: The transfer functions of the block diagrams are

 

\(\begin{aligned}&T_{1}(s)=\frac{Y_{1}(s)}{R_{1}(s)}=\frac{K}{s^{2}+3 s+7} \\&T_{2}(s)=\frac{Y_{2}(s)}{R_{2}(s)}=\frac{K}{(s+1)(s+2)+K} .\end{aligned}\)

 

For gain value \(K=5, T_{1}(s)=T_{2}(s)\)

 

Prior to determining the sensitivity of each transfer function, we first write a general transfer function that includes the above as special cases:

 

\(T(s)=\frac{C_{1}(s)+p C_{2}(s)}{C_{3}(s)+p C_{4}(s)}\)

 

where \(G_{i}(s)\) are polynomials in \(s\), and \(p\) is any parameter of interest, as before. Equation 10.16, applied to the general transfer function, results in

 

\(S_{p}^{T}=\lim _{\Delta p \rightarrow 0} \frac{\Delta T}{\Delta p} \frac{p}{T}=\frac{\partial T}{\partial p} \frac{p}{T}\) (10.16)

 

\(S_{p}^{T}=\frac{p\left(C_{2} C_{3}-C_{1} C_{4}\right)}{\left(C_{3}+p C_{4}\right)\left(C_{1}+p C_{2}\right)}\)

 

For the example for \(T_{1}(s)\), we have \(p\)= \(K\),

\(C_{1}\) = \(C_{4}\)=0, \(C_{2}\)=1,and \(C_{3}=s^{2}+4 s+7\). Then, \(S_{K}^{T_{1}}=1\), a constant regardless of the value of gain \(K\). For \(T_{2}(s)\), \(p\)= \(K\), \(C_{1}\)=0, \(C_{2}\) = \(C_{4}\)=1, and \(C_{3}=s^{2}+3 s+2\). The sensitivity function for \(T_{2}(s)\) is then

 

\(\begin{aligned}S_{K}^{T_{2}} &=\frac{K\left(s^{2}+3 s+2\right)}{\left(s^{2}+3 s+2+K\right) K} \\&=\frac{\left(s^{2}+3 s+2\right)}{\left(s^{2}+3 s+2+K\right)}\end{aligned}\) (10.19)

 

Here, for the closed-loop system, the sensitivity is a function of gain \(K\) and the complex variable \(s\). Thus, to reduce sensitivity to parameter variation, a designer would select an appropriate gain or maintain the frequencies of the input within an appropriate range. Since the sensitivity is a function of complex variable \(s\), the frequency range of the application is important.
