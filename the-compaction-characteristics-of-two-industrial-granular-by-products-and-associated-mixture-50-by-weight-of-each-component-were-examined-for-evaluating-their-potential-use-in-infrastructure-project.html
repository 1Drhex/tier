Guest Question.

The compaction characteristics of two industrial granular by-products and associated mixture (50% by weight of each component) were examined for evaluating their potential use in infrastructure projects. The compaction data is given in Table 2.8.

Plot the compaction data using the conventional\(\left(\gamma _{d}-w \right) \) and alternative \(\left(e-e_{w}\right) \) representation. The specific gravity of the granular waste materials A and B is given in Table 2.9.

Solution

To plot the compaction data in the \(\left(\gamma _{d}-w \right) \), the dry density corresponding to the different water content and bulk density pairs can be determined with the relationship:

\(\rho _{d} =\frac{\rho _{b} }{\left(1+w\right) }\)

and \(\gamma _{d} =\rho _{d} g\) as shown in Table 2.10.

For plotting the different zero air void (ZAV) lines, the following relationship can be used (Table 2.11):

\(\rho _{d} =\frac{G_{s}\rho _{w} }{1+e} \Leftrightarrow \rho _{d}=\frac{G_{s}\rho _{w}}{1+\frac{wG_{s} }{S_{r} } }\)

 

\(\therefore \rho _{d\left(ZAV,S_{r}=1 \right) } =\frac{G_{s}\rho _{w} }{1+wG_{s} }\)

Then the results can be plotted in terms of \(\left(\gamma _{d}-w \right)\) as shown in Figure 2.10.
