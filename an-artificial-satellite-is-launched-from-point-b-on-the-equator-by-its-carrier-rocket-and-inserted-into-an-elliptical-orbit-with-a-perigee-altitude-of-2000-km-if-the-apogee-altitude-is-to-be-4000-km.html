Guest Question.

An artificial satellite is launched from point B on the equator by its carrier rocket and inserted into an elliptical orbit with a perigee altitude of 2000 km. If the apogee altitude is to be 4000 km, compute (a) the necessary perigee velocity \(v_{P}\) and the corresponding apogee velocity \(v_{A}\), (b) the velocity at point C where the altitude of the satellite is 2500 km, and (c) the period Ï„ for a complete orbit.

Admin
Solution

(a) The perigee and apogee velocities for specified altitudes are given by Eqs. 3 /48, where

 

\(\begin{aligned}&v_{P}=R \sqrt{\frac{g}{a}} \sqrt{\frac{1+e}{1-e}}=R \sqrt{\frac{g}{a}} \sqrt{\frac{r_{\max }}{r_{\min }}} \\&v_{A}=R \sqrt{\frac{g}{a}} \sqrt{\frac{1-e}{1+e}}=R \sqrt{\frac{g}{a}} \sqrt{\frac{r_{\min }}{r_{\max }}}\end{aligned}\) (3/48)

 

1 \(r_{\max }=6371+4000=10371 km\)

 

\(r_{\min }=6371+2000=8371 km\)

 

\(a=\left(r_{\min }+r_{\max }\right) / 2=9371 km\)

 

Thus,

 

\(v_{P}=R \sqrt{\frac{g}{a}} \sqrt{\frac{r_{\max }}{r_{\min }}}=6371\left(10^{3}\right) \sqrt{\frac{9.825}{9371\left(10^{3}\right)}} \sqrt{\frac{10371}{8371}}\)

 

\(=7261 m / s \quad \text { or } \quad 26140 km / h\) Ans.

 

\(v_{A}=R \sqrt{\frac{g}{a}} \sqrt{\frac{r_{\min }}{r_{\max }}}=6371\left(10^{3}\right) \sqrt{\frac{9.825}{9371\left(10^{3}\right)}} \sqrt{\frac{8371}{10371}}\)

 

\(=5861 m / s \quad \text { or } \quad 21099 km / h\) Ans.

 

(b) For an altitude of 2500 km the radial distance from the center of the earth is r = 6371 + 2500 = 8871 km. From Eq. 3 /47 the velocity at point C becomes

 

\(v^{2}=2 g R^{2}\left(\frac{1}{r}-\frac{1}{2 a}\right)\) (3/47)

 

2 \(v_{C}^{2}=2 g R^{2}\left(\frac{1}{r}-\frac{1}{2 a}\right)=2(9.825)\left[(6371)\left(10^{3}\right)\right]^{2}\left(\frac{1}{8871}-\frac{1}{18742}\right) \frac{1}{10^{3}}\)

 

\(=47.353\left(10^{6}\right)( m / s )^{2}\)

 

\(v_{C}=6881 m / s \quad \text { or } \quad 24773 km / h\) Ans.

 

(c) The period of the orbit is given by Eq. 3 /44, which becomes

 

\(\tau=2 \pi \frac{a^{3 / 2}}{R \sqrt{g}}\) (3/44)

 

3 \(\tau=2 \pi \frac{a^{3 / 2}}{R \sqrt{g}}=2 \pi \frac{\left[(9371)\left(10^{3}\right)\right]^{3 / 2}}{(6371)\left(10^{3}\right) \sqrt{9.825}}=9026 s\)

 

or \(\tau=2.507 h\) Ans.

 

Helpful Hints

1 The mean radius of 12 742/2 = 6371 km from Table D/2 in Appendix D is used. Also the absolute acceleration due to gravity \(g=9.825 m / s ^{2}\) from Art. 1/5 will be used.

2 We must be careful with units. It is often safer to work in base units, meters in this case, and convert later.

3 We should observe here that the time interval between successive overhead transits of the satellite as recorded by an observer on the equator is longer than the period calculated here since the observer will have moved in space due to the counterclockwise rotation of the earth, as seen looking down on the north pole.
