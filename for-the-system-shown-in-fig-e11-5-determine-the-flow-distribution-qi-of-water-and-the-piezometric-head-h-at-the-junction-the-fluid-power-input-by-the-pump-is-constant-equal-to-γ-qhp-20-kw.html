Guest Question.

For the system shown in Fig. E11.5, determine the flow distribution \(Q_{i}\) of water and the piezometric head H at the junction. The fluid power input by the pump is constant, equal to \(γ QH_P = 20 kW\). Assume constant friction factors.

Pipe 1 2 3
L (m) 50 100 300
D (m) 0.15 0.1 0.1
f 0.02 0.015 0.025
ΣK 2 1 1
Admin
Solution

The equivalent lengths and resistance coefficients are computed from Eqs. 11.3.6 and 11.3.8 to be

 

 

\(\left(L_{e}\right)_{i}=\frac{D_{i}}{f_{i}} \Sigma K (11.3.6)\)

 

\(\bar{R}_{i}=\frac{8 f_{i}\left[L_{i}+\left(L_{e}\right)_{i}\right]}{g \pi^{2} D_{i}^{5}} (11.3.8)\)

 

\(\begin{array}{ll}\left(L_{e}\right)_{1}=\frac{0.15}{0.02} \times 2=15 \mathrm{~m} & \bar{R}_{1}=\frac{8 \times 0.02 \times 65}{9.81 \times \pi^{2} \times(0.15)^{5}}=1.42 \times 10^{3} \mathrm{~s}^{2} / \mathrm{m}^{5} \\\\ \left(L_{e}\right)_{2}=\frac{0.10}{0.015} \times 1=6.7 \mathrm{~m} & \bar{R}_{2}=\frac{8 \times 0.015 \times 106.7}{9.81 \times \pi^{2} \times(0.10)^{5}}=1.32 \times 10^{4} \mathrm{~s}^{2} / \mathrm{m}^{5} \\\\ \left(L_{e}\right)_{3}=\frac{0.10}{0.025} \times 1=4 \mathrm{~m} & \bar{R}_{3}=\frac{8 \times 0.025 \times 304}{9.81 \times \pi^{2} \times(0.10)^{5}}=6.28 \times 10^{4} \mathrm{~s}^{2} / \mathrm{m}^{5}\end{array}\)

Assume flow directions as shown. The energy equation for pipe 1 from the reservoir to the junction \(\mathrm{B}\) is

 

\(z_{1}+H_{P}=H+\bar{R}_{1} Q_{1}^{2}\)

 

in which \(H\) is the piezometric head at B. Substituting in known parameters and solving for \(H\) results in

 

\(\begin{aligned}H &=10+\frac{20 \times 10^{3}}{9800 Q_{1}}-1.42 \times 10^{3} Q_{1}^{2} \\&=10+\frac{2.04}{Q_{1}}-1420 Q_{1}^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

An iterative solution is shown in the accompanying table. For each iteration, a value of \(Q_{1}\) is estimated. Then \(H\) is calculated and \(Q_{2}\) and \(Q_{3}\) are evaluated from the relations

 

\(\begin{array}{l}Q_{2}=\left(\frac{H-z_{2}}{\bar{R}_{2}}\right)^{1 / 2}=\left(\frac{H-30}{1.32 \times 10^{4}}\right)^{1 / 2} \\Q_{3}=\left(\frac{H-z_{3}}{\bar{R}_{3}}\right)^{1 / 2}=\left(\frac{H-15}{6.28 \times 10^{4}}\right)^{1 / 2}\end{array}\)

 

In the last column of the table, a continuity balance is employed to check the accuracy of the estimate of \(Q_{1}\). The third estimate of \(Q_{1}\) is based on a linear interpolation by setting \(\Sigma Q=0\) and using values of \(Q_{1}\) and \(\Delta Q\) from the first two iterations.

Iteration 1 2 3
\(Q_{1}\) 0.05 0.055 0.054
H 47.25 42.8 43.64
\(Q_{2}\) 0.0362 0.0311 0.0322
\(Q_{3}\) 0.0227 0.0210 0.0214
\(\Delta Q = Q_{1} – Q_{2} – Q_{3}\) – 0.0089 + 0.0029 + 0.0004
The approximate solution is \(H=43.6 \mathrm{~m}, Q_{1}=54 \mathrm{~L} / \mathrm{s}, Q_{2}=32 \mathrm{~L} / \mathrm{s}\), and \(Q_{3}=21 \mathrm{~L} / \mathrm{s}\). Ifgreater precision is desired, a solution should be employed similar to that shown in .
