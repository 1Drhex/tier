Guest Question.

Two large aluminium plates (k = 240 W/m.K), each 1 cm thick with 10 µm surface roughness are placed in contact under pressure of 1 bar in air (k = 0.026 W/m.K). The temperature at inside and outside surfaces are 400°C and 150°C. Calculate (a) the heat flux, and (b) temperature drop due to contact resistance.

Admin
Solution

Given : Two large aluminium plates in contact.
\(k = 240 W/m.K, L = 1 cm = 0.01 m\)
\(L_a = 10 µm = 10 × 10^{–6} m, T_1 = 400°C\)
\(T_2 = 150°C, k_a = 0.026 W/m.K.\)
\(T_o\) find :
(a) Heat flux
(b) Temperature drop \((T_3 – T_4)\) due to contact resistance.
Analysis : (a) The individual thermal resistance per 1 \(m^2\) area
\(R_1 =R_3 = \frac{L}{k} = \frac{0.01}{240} = 4.167 × 10^{–5} m^2.K/W\)
From Table 3.1, contact resistance Rcontact is
\(2.75 × 10^{–4} m^2.K/W\)
i.e., \(R_2 =R_{contact} = 2.75 × 10^{–4} m^2.K/W\)
The heat flux
\(q = \frac{∆T}{ΣR_{th}}=\frac{T_1 – T_2}{R_1 +R_2 +R_3}\)

or \(q = \frac{400 – 150}{4.167 \times 10^{-5}+ 2.75 × 10^{-4} +4.167 \times 10^{-5}}\)

\(= 2.79 × 10^4 W/m^2.\)

(b) The temperature drop across contact resistance :
Heat flux across contact resistance is given as :
\(q = \frac{T_{c_1} – T_{c_2}}{R_{contact}}\)

or \((T_{c_1} – T_{c_2}) = q R_{contact} = 2.79 × 10^4 × 2.75 × 10^{–4}\)

= 7.67°C.
