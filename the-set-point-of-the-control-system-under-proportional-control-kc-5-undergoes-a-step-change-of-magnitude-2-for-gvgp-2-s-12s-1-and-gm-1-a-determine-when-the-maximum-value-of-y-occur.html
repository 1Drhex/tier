Guest Question.

The set-point of the control system under proportional control \(\left(K_{c}=5\right)\) undergoes a step change of magnitude 2 . For

\(G_{v} G_{p}=\frac{2}{(s+1)(2 s+1)}\) and \(G_{m}=1\)

(a) Determine when the maximum value of \(y\) occurs.

(b) Determine the offset.

(c) Determine the period of oscillation.

(d) Sketch \(y(t)\) as a function of time, showing key characteristics as determined in (a), (b), and (c).

Admin
Solution

First we use Eq. 11-26 to get the closed loop transfer function

\(\frac{Y}{Y_{s p}}=\frac{K_{m} G_{c} G_{v} G_{p}}{1+G_{c} G_{v} G_{p} G_{m}}\) (11-26)

\(\frac{Y}{Y_{s p}}=\frac{\frac{10}{(s+1)(2 s+1)}}{1+\frac{10}{(s+1)(2 s+1)}}=\frac{10}{(s+1)(2 s+1)+10}=\frac{10}{2 s^{2}+3 s+11}\)

Or in standard form

\(\frac{Y}{Y_{s p}}=\frac{10 / 11}{\frac{2}{11} s^{2}+\frac{3}{11} s+1} \quad \zeta=\frac{3 \sqrt{22}}{44} \quad \tau=\frac{\sqrt{22}}{11}\)

The time at which the maximum occurs is given by Eq. \(5-52\)

\(\text { Decay ratio: } \quad DR =( OS )^{2}=\exp \left(-2 \pi \zeta / \sqrt{1-\zeta^{2}}\right)\) (5-52)

\(t_{p}=\pi \tau / \sqrt{1-\zeta^{2}} \quad t_{p}=1.41\)

(b)

The response is given by

\(Y(s)=\frac{20}{s\left(2 s^{2}+3 s+11\right)}\)

The Final Value Theorem gives the steady state value as

\(y(\infty)=\frac{20}{11}\)

Subtracting the steady state value from the set point change gives offset as

\(\text { offset }=\frac{2}{11}\)

(c)

The period of oscillation is given by Eq. \(5-55\)

P=3060-1000=2060 s (5-55)

\(P=\frac{2 \pi \tau}{\sqrt{1-\zeta^{2}}} \quad P=2.83\)

(d)

Hint: You do not need to obtain the analytical response \(y(t)\) to answer the above questions. Use the standard second order model expressed in terms of \(\zeta\) and \(\tau\) (see Chapter 5 ).
