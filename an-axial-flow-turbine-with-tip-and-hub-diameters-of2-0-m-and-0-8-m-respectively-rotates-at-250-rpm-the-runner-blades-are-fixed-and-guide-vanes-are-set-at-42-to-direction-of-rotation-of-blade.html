Guest Question.

An axial flow turbine with tip and hub diameters of 2.0 m and 0.8 m, respectively, rotates at 250 rpm. The runner blades are fixed and guide vanes are set at \(42^{\circ}\) to direction of rotation of blade at mean diameter. Also, the blade in let angle is \(148^{\circ}\) from the direction of velocity of blade. Calculate,

– axial velocity
– flow rate
– exit blade angle
– Euler’s power

Admin
Solution

Given \(D_{t} = 2.0 m; D_{h} = 0.8 m; N = 250 rpm\)

\( \alpha_{1} = 42^{\circ} ; \beta_{1} = 148^{\circ}\)

 

\( D = \frac{2 + 0.8}{2} = 1.4 m\)

 

\( u = \frac{\pi D N}{60} =\frac {\pi \times 1.4 \times 250}{60} = 18.3 {m}/{s}\)

From inlet triangle of velocities diagram of Fig. 7.11, we have

\( V_{f} = \tan \left(\pi – \beta_{1}\right) \left(u – v_{1w}\right) = \tan \alpha_{1} V_{1w} \)

 

\(\therefore \tan \left(\pi – 148\right) \left(18.3 – V_{1w}\right) = \tan 42 V_{1w}\)

 

\(\therefore V_{1w} = 7.5 {m}/{s}\)

 

\(\tan \alpha_{1} = \frac{V_{f}}{V_{1w}} ,\)

 

\( V_{f} = 7.5 \times \tan 42 = 6.75 {m}/{s}\)

 

\( Q = \frac{\pi}{4} \left(D^{2}_{t} – D^{2}_{h}\right) V_{f} = \frac{\pi}{4} \left(2^{2} – 0.8^{2}\right) 6.75 = 17.8 {m^{3}}/{s}\)

Euler’s head \( E =\frac{u V_{1w}}{g} = \frac{18.3 \times 7.5}{9.8} = 14 m\)

Euler’s power \( \gamma Q E = \frac{9800 \times 17.8 \times 14}{1000} = 2.4 kW\)

\( \tan \beta_{2} = \frac {V_{f}}{u} = \frac {6.75}{18.3} = 0.368 \)

 

\( \beta_{2} = 20^{\circ}\)
