Guest Question.

A convergent-divergent nozzle has a throat diameter of 0.05 m and an exit diameter of 0.1 m. The inlet stagnation state is 500 kPa, 500 K. Find the back pressure that will lead to the maximum possible flow rate and the mass flow rate for three different gases as: air; hydrogen or carbon dioxide.

Admin
Solution

There is a maximum possible flow when M = 1 at the throat,

\(T ^{*}=\frac{2}{ k +1} T _{ o } ; \quad P ^{*}= P _{ o }\left(\frac{2}{ k +1}\right)^{\frac{ k }{ k -1} ;} \quad \rho^{*}=\rho_{ o }\left(\frac{2}{ k +1}\right)^{\frac{1}{ k -1}}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{ m }=\rho^{*} A ^{*} V =\rho^{*} A ^{*} c = P ^{*} A ^{*} \sqrt{ k / RT ^{*}} \\& A ^{*}=\pi D ^{2} / 4=0.001963 m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{array}{lcccccc}& k & T ^{*} & P ^{*} & c & \rho^{*} & \dot{ m } \\\text { a) } & 1.400 & 416.7 & 264.1 & 448.2 & 2.209 & 1.944 \\\text { b) } & 1.409 & 415.1 & 263.4 & 1704.5 & 0.154 & 0.515 \\\text { c) } & 1.289 & 436.9 & 273.9 & 348.9 & 3.318 & 2.273\end{array}\)

 

\(A_{E} / A^{*}=\left(D_{E} / D^{*}\right)^{2}=4\) . There are 2 possible solutions corresponding to points c and d in Fig. 15.13 and Fig. 15.17. For these we have

 

\(\begin{array}{ccccc}& \text { Subsonic solution } & \text { Supersonic solution } \\& M _{ E } P _{ E } / P _{ o } & M _{ E } P _{ E } / P _{ o } \\\text { a) } & 0.1466 0.985 & 2.940 0.0298 \\\text { b) } & 0.1464 0.985 & 2.956 0.0293 \\\text { c) } & 0.1483 0.986 & 2.757 0.0367\end{array}\)

 

\(P _{ B }= P _{ E } \cong 0.985 \times 500=492.5 kPa\) all cases point c

\(\begin{array}{ll}\text { a) } & P _{ B }= P _{ E }=0.0298 \times 500=14.9 kPa , \text { point } d \\\text { b) } & P _{ B }= P _{ E }=0.0293 \times 500=14.65 kPa , \text { point } d \\\text { c) } & P _{ B }= P _{ E }=0.0367 \times 500=18.35 kPa , \text { point } d\end{array}\)
