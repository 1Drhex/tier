Guest Question.

The simply supported beam shown in Fig. 12–11 a supports the triangular distributed loading. Determine its maximum deflection. EI is constant.

Admin
Solution

I

Elastic Curve. Due to symmetry, only one x coordinate is needed for the solution, in this case 0 ≤ x≤ L/2. The beam deflects as shown in Fig. 12–11 a . The maximum deflection occurs at the center since the slope is zero at this point.

Moment Function. A free-body diagram of the segment on the left is shown in Fig. 12–11 b . The equation for the distributed loading is

\(w=\frac{2w_0}{L}x \) (1)

Hence,

\(\searrow +\sum{M_{NA}}=0; \quad\quad M+\frac{w_0x^2}{L}\left(\frac{x}{3} \right)-\frac{w_0L}{4}(x)=0 \)

 

\(M=-\frac{w_0x^3}{3L}+\frac{w_0L}{4}x \)

Slope and Elastic Curve. Using \(EI\frac{d^2v}{dx^2}=M(x) \) and integrating twice, we have

\(EI\frac{d^2v}{dx^2}=M=-\frac{w_0}{3L}x^3+\frac{w_0L}{4}x \) (2)

 

\(EI\frac{dv}{dx}=-\frac{w_0}{12L}x^4+\frac{w_0L}{8}x^2+C_1\)

 

\(EIv=-\frac{w_0}{60L}x^5+\frac{w_0L}{24}x^3+C_1x+C_2\)

The constants of integration are obtained by applying the boundary condition \(v = 0\) at \(x = 0\) and the symmetry condition that \(dv/dx = 0\) at \(x = L/2\). This leads to

\(C_1=-\frac{5w_0L^3}{192} \quad\quad C_2=0 \)

Hence,

\(EI\frac{dv}{dx}=-\frac{w_0}{12L}x^4+\frac{w_0L}{8}x^2-\frac{5w_0L^3}{192} \)

 

\(EIv=-\frac{w_0}{60L}x^5+\frac{w_0L}{24}x^3-\frac{5w_0L^3}{192}x \)

Determining the maximum deflection at \(x = L/2\), we have

\(v_{max}=-\frac{w_0L^4}{120EI} \)

II

Since the distributed loading acts downward, it is negative according to our sign convention. Using Eq. 1 and applying \(EI\frac{d^4v}{dx^4}=w(x) \) , we have

\(EI\frac{d^4v}{dx^4}=-\frac{2w_0}{L}x \)

 

\(EI\frac{d^3v}{dx^3}=V=-\frac{w_0}{L}x^2+C_1^\prime \)

Since \(V = +w_0L/4\) at \(x = 0\), then \(C_1 ^\prime = w_0 L/4\). Integrating again yields

\(EI\frac{d^3v}{dx^3}=V=-\frac{w_0}{L}x^2+\frac{w_0L}{4} \)

 

\(EI\frac{d^2v}{dx^2}=M=-\frac{w_0}{3L}x^3+\frac{w_0L}{4}x+C_2^\prime \)

Here \(M = 0\) at \(x = 0\), so \(C_2 ^\prime = 0\). This yields Eq. 2 . The solution now proceeds as before.
