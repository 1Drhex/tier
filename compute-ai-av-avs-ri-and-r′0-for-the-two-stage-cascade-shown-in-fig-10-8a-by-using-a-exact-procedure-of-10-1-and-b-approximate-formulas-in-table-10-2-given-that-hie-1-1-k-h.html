Solution

(a) The ac equivalent form of the given circuit is shown in Fig. 10.8b. As discussed in , it is advantageous to start the calculation from the last stage as follows:

Second Stage: From Table 9.4,

\(A_{I}=-\frac{h_{f}}{1+h_{o}Z_{L}} \)
\(Z_{i}=h_{i}+h_{r}A_{I}Z_{L}=h_{i}-\frac{h_{f}h_{r}}{h_{o}+Y_{L}} \)
\(A_{V}=\frac{A_{I}Z_{L}}{Z_{i}} \)
\(Y_{o}=h_{o}-\frac{h_{f}h_{r}}{h_{i}+R_{s}}=\frac{1}{Z_{o}} \)
\(A_{Vs}=\frac{A_{V}Z_{i}}{Z_{i}+R_{s}}=\frac{A_{I}Z_{L}}{Z_{s}+R_{s}}=\frac{A_{Is}Z_{L}}{R_{s}} \)
\(A_{Is}=\frac{A_{I}R_{s}}{Z_{i}+R_{s}} \)

with RL = RL2 = 4K, the current gain and input impedance Ri2 of the second stage are obtained as

\(A_{I2}=-\frac{I_{0}}{I_{b2}}=\frac{-h_{fe}}{1+h_{oe}R_{L2}}=\frac{-50}{1+\frac{4K}{40K} }=-45.5 \)

\(R_{ie}=h_{ie}+h_{re}A_{I2}=1.1K+(2.5\times 10^{-4})\times (-45.5)\times (4K) =1055\) Ω

Note the low input impedance of the CE stage. The voltage gain of the second is obtained as

\(A_{V2}=A_{I2}\frac{R_{L2}}{R_{i2}}=(-45.5)\times \frac{4K}{1.055K}=-172 \)

First Stage: From the ac equivalent circuit of Fig. 10.8b, we observe that 15 K resistor of the collector circuit of Q1 is in parallel with voltage divider resistors of 36 K and 4 K of the second stage transistor. Since, the input resistance Ri2 of the second stage appears in parallel with the above resistors, the total load resistance of the first stage is given by

RL1 = 15 K || 36 K || 4 K || 1.055 K = 0.776 K

Hence , we obtain

\(A_{I1}=-\frac{I_{c1}}{I_{b1}}=\frac{-50}{1+\frac{0.776K}{40K} }=-49.1 \)

The input impedance of the first stage is given by

\(R_{i1}=1.1K+(2.5\times 10^{-4})\times (-49.1)\times (0.776K)=1090.5 \) Ω

The voltage gain of the first stage is

\(A_{V1}=(-49.1)\times \frac{776\Omega }{1090.5\Omega }=-34.9 \)

Note that the total source resistance appearing in series with base of Q1 is obtained from Fig. 10.8b by considering Vs = 0 which can be obtained as

Rs1 = 1K||200 K||10 K = 905 Ω

Thus, from Eq. (9.39) , \(C_{b}\geq \frac{1.59}{f_{smin}(R_{s}+R_{in}(total))}=C_{bmin} \) , or Table 9.4, the output admittance of the second stage is obtained as

\(Y_{01}=h_{oe}-\frac{h_{fe}h_{re}}{h_{ie}+R_{s1}} \)

\(=\frac{1}{40K}-\frac{50\times (2.5\times 10^{-4})}{1.1K+0.905K} \)

\(=\frac{0.25}{K}-\frac{0.0062}{K} \)

\(=\frac{0.0188}{K} \)

Thus , \(R_{01}=\frac{1}{Y_{01}} =\frac{K}{0.0188}=53.3 \) K

Note that the effective source resistance Rs2 appearing in series with the base of Q2 is the output resistance of the first stage by taking into consideration of 15 K || 36 K || 4 K. Thus,

Rs2 = 53.3 K || 15K || 36 K || 4 K = 2.75 K

The output admittance of the second stage is obtained as

\(Y_{01}=h_{oe}-\frac{h_{fe}h_{re}}{h_{ie}+R_{s1}} \)

\(=\frac{1}{40K}-\frac{50\times (2.5\times 10^{-4})}{1.1K+2.75K} \)

\(=\frac{0.218}{K} \)

Thus, the output impedance (resistance) of the second stage given by

\(R_{02}=\frac{1}{Y_{02}} =\frac{K}{0.0218}\approx 46 \) K

For the Entire Amplifier Circuit: The overall voltage gain is given by

AV = AV2 AV2 = (−34.9) × (−172) = 6002

The overall output resistance is obtained as

R′0 = R02||4 K = 46 K||4 K = 3.68 K

The input impedance Ri is given as

Ri = Ri1 ||10 K||20 K = 1090.5 Ω || 10 K || 20 K = 980 Ω

Thus, the overall voltage gain by taking source resistance Rs into consideration is obtained as

\(A_{Vs}=A_{V}\frac{R_{i}}{R_{i}+R_{s}}=6002\times \frac{980\Omega }{980\Omega +1K}\approx 2791 \)

The overall current gain is obtained as

\(A_{I}=-\frac{I_{0}}{I_{b1}} \)

\(=\frac{V_{0}/R_{L2}}{V_{i}/R_{i1}} \)

\(=\frac{A_{V}V_{i}/R_{L2}}{V_{i}/R_{i1}}=\frac{A_{V}R_{i1}}{R_{L2}}=\frac{6002\times 1090.5 \Omega }{4K}\approx 1636 \)

The overall current gain by taking Rs into consideration can be obtained by replacing AV by AVs and Ri1 by Ri + Rs in the above calculations for overall current gain which is obtained as

\(A_{Is}=-\frac{I_{0}}{I_{s} }=\frac{A_{Vs}V_{s}/R_{L2}}{V_{s}/(R_{i}+R_{s})}=\frac{A_{Vs}(R_{i}+R_{s})}{R_{L2}}=-\frac{2971\times (980\Omega +1K)}{4K} \approx 1471 \)

(b) Second Stage: From Table10.2 ,

CE CE with \(R_{e} \) CC CB
\(A_{I} \) \(-h_{fe} \) \(-h_{fe} \) \(1+h_{fe} \) \(-h_{fb}=\frac{h_{fe}}{1+h_{fe}} \)
\(R_{i} \) \(h_{ie} \) \(h_{ie}+(1+h_{fe})R_{e} \) \(h_{ie}+(1+h_{fe})R_{L} \) \(h_{ib}=\frac{h_{ie}}{1+h_{fe}} \)
\(A_{V}\) \(-\frac{h_{fe}R_{L}}{h_{ie}} \) \(-\frac{h_{fe}R_{L}}{R_{i}} \) \(1-\frac{h_{ie}}{R_{i}} \) \(h_{fe}\frac{R_{L}}{h_{ie}} \)
\(R_{o}\) \(\infty \) \(\infty \) \(\infty \)
\(R^{′}_{o}\) \(R_{L}\) \(R_{L}\) \(R_{o}||R_{L}\) \(R_{L}\)

we get

AI2 = –hfe = – 50 ;

Ri2 = hie = 1.1 K ;

\(A_{V2}=\frac{-h_{fe}R_{L2}}{h_{ie}}=\frac{-50\times 4K}{1.1K}=-182 \)

R02 = ∞ ;

RL1 = 15 K || 36 K || 4 K || Ri2 = 15 K || 36 K || 4 K || 1.1 K = 0.8 K

First Stage: AI1 = –hfe = −50;

Ri1 = hie = 1.1 K ;

\(A_{V1}=\frac{-h_{fe}R_{L1}}{h_{ie}}=\frac{-50\times 0.8K}{1.1K}=-36.3 \) ;

R01 = ∞ ;

Overall calculations: Following the similar methodology as used in Part-(a), the overall amplifier parameters are obtained as follows:

AV = AV1 AV2 = (−36.3) × (−182) = 6600;

Ri = Ri1 || 10 K || 20 K = 1.1 K || 10 K || 20 K = 986 Ω;

R′0 = RL2 = 4 K;

\(A_{Vs}=A_{V}\frac{R_{i}}{R_{i}+R_{s}}=6600\times \frac{986\Omega }{986\Omega +1K}\approx 3276 \) ;

The overall current gain is obtained as

\(A_{I}=\frac{A_{V}R_{i1}}{R_{L2}}=\frac{6600\times 1.1K}{4K}=1815 \) ;

\(A_{Is}=\frac{A_{Vs}(R_{i}+R_{s})}{R_{L2}}=-\frac{3276\times (986\Omega +1K)}{4K} \approx 1627 \)
