Guest Question.

Methane combustion on \(La_{2}O_{3} \)-based catalysts has been studied by Toops et al. [46]. With a 4% Sr-promoted \(La_{2}O_{3} \) catalyst \(\left(2.5 \ m^{2} g^{-1} \right)\) operating between 773 and 973 K, 0.5 – 5 Torr \(CH_{4}\) and 3- 23 Torr \(O_{2}\) (760 Torr = 1 atm) under differential reactor conditions, an apparent activation energy of 29 kcal \(mole^{-1}\) for \(CO_{2}\) formation was observed. Near 900 K, selectivity to \(CO_{2}\), rather than CO, was about 75% or higher, and the reaction orders from a power rate law are given in Table 1. Propose a L-H type model for \(CO_{2}\) formation with a sequence of elementary steps that results in a derived rate expression consistent with these results. It can be assumed that only the adsorbed reactants and products need be included in the site balance, and dissociative \(O_{2}\) adsorption occurs. Under low-conversion conditions, the surface concentrations of the products can be ignored, so what is the form of the rate equation? Fitting this latter equation to the data produced the optimized rate parameters listed in Table 2, where k0 is the lumped apparent rate constant. Evaluate them to determine if they are consistent and state why.

Table 1. Reaction orders for \(CH_{4}\) combustion on
Sr-\(La_{2}O_{3} \) (from ref. 46)

Solution

The rate can be defined as \(r_{m}= \frac{1}{m} \frac{dN_{N_{2} } }{dt} = \frac{1}{m}\left\lgroup – \frac{d N_{CH_{4} } }{dt} \right\rgroup \) for the reaction

\(CH_{4} + 2 O_{2}\longrightarrow CO_{2}+ 2 H_{2}O\) With dissociative \(0_2\) adsorption:

(1) \(O_{2}+ 2 * \xrightleftharpoons[]{ K_{O_{2} } } 2 O *\)

 

(2) \(CH_{4}+ * \xrightleftharpoons[]{ K_{CH_{4} } } CH_{4}*\)

 

(3) \(CH_{4}* + O* \xrightarrow[]{K_{1} } CH_{3}* + OH * \)

 

(4) \(CH_{3}* + O * \xrightleftharpoons[]{K_{2} } CH_2O* + H *\)

 

(5) \(CH_{2}O* + O* \xrightleftharpoons[]{K_{3} } HCO * + OH *\)

 

(6) \(HCO* + O* \xrightleftharpoons[]{K_{4} } CO * + OH *\)

 

(7) \(H* + OH* \xrightleftharpoons[]{K_{5} } H_{2}O * + *\)

 

(8) \(CO * + O * \xrightleftharpoons[]{K_{6} } CO_{2}*+ * \)

 

(9) \(CO_{2}* \xrightleftharpoons[]{1/K_{CO_{2}}} CO_{2}+ * \)

 

(10) \(2 OH * \xrightleftharpoons[]{K_{7} } H_{2}O * + O*\)

 

(11) \(2 \left[H_{2}O * \xrightleftharpoons[]{1/K_{H_{2} O} } H_{2}O + * \right] \)

 

(12) \(CO * \xrightleftharpoons[]{1/K_{CO} } CO + * \)

——————————————————–

\(CH_{4}+ 2 O_{2}\Longrightarrow CO_{2}+ 2 H_{2}O \)

Step 12 allows for CO desorption as a product. The rate based on this sequence is given by step 3 (the RDS):

\(r_{m} = K_{1}\left[ CH_{4} *\right] \left[O*\right] \)

 

From step 1 , \(K_{O_{2}}= \left[O*\right]^{2} / P_{O_{2} } \left[*\right]^{2} \) and \(\left[O*\right]= K^{1/2}_{O_{2} } P^{1/2}_{O_{2} } \left[*\right] \)

 

From step 2 , \(K_{CH_{4}}= \left[CH_{4}*\right] / P_{CH_{4}} \left[*\right] \) and \(\left[CH_{4}* \right] = K_{CH_{4}} P_{CH_{4}} \left[*\right] \)

 

Thus \(r_{m}= k_{1} k_{CH_{4}} K^{1/2}_{O_{2} } P_{CH_{4}} P^{1/2}_{O_{2} } \left[*\right]^{2} \)

The site balance with only adsorbed reactants and products is:

\(L= \left[CH_{4} *\right] + \left[O*\right] + \left[CO_{2}*\right]+ \left[H_{2}O*\right]+ \left[CO*\right] + \left[*\right]\)

Steps 9, 11, and 12 give, respectfully:

\(\left[CO_2* \right] = K_{CO_{2}} P_{CO_{2}} \left[* \right] , \left[H_{2}O* \right]= K_{H_{2}O} P_{H_{2}O} \left[* \right] , and \left[CO* \right] = K_{CO} P_{CO} \left[* \right]\)

 

thus \(\left[* \right] = L / \left(1+ K_{CH_{4}} P_{CH_{4}} + K^{1/2}_{O_{2}}P^{1/2}_{O_{2}}+ K_{CO_{2}} P_{CO_{2}}+ K_{H_{2}O} P_{H_{2}O} + K_{CO} P_{CO} \right)\)

and

 

\(r_{m}= L K^{\prime }_{1} k_{CH_{4}} K^{1/2}_{O_{2} } P_{CH_{4}} P^{1/2}_{O_{2} } / \left(1+ K_{CH_{4}} P_{CH_{4}}+ K^{1/2}_{O_{2} } P^{1/2}_{O_{2} }+ K_{CO_{2}} P_{CO_{2}} + K_{H_{2}O} P_{H_{2}O}+ K_{CO} P_{CO} \right) ^{2}\)

From Arrhenius plots of \(K_{CH_{4}}\) & \(K_{O_{2}}\) ,

For \(CH_{4} := \Delta H^{o}_{ad}= -20 \ kcal \ mole^{-1} , \Delta S^{o}_{ad}= -13 e.u\)

For \(O_{2}: \Delta H^{o}_{ad}= -30 \ kcal \ mole^{-1} , \Delta S^{o}_{ad}= -25 e.u \left(1 \ e.u = 1 \ cal \ mole^{-1} \ K^{-1} \right) \)
