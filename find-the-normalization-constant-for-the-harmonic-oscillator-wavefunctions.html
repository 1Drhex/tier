Guest Question.

Find the normalization constant for the harmonic oscillator wavefunctions.

Admin
Solution

Collect your thoughts A wavefunction is normalized (to 1) by evaluating the integral of \(|ψ|^2\) over all space and then finding the normalization factor from eqn 7B.3(\(N=1/\left(\int{\Psi^*\Psi d\tau} \right)^{1/2} \)). The normalized wavefunction is then equal to \(Nψ\). In this one-dimensional problem, the volume element is \(dx\) and the integration is from \(−∞\) to \(+∞\). The wavefunctions are expressed in terms of the dimensionless variable \(y = x/α\), so begin by expressing the integral in terms of \(y\) by using \(dx = αdy\). The integrals required are given in Table 7E.1.

Table 7E.1 The Hermite polynomials

\(v\) \(H_v(y)\)
\(0\) \(1\)
\(1\) \(2y\)
\(2\) \(4y^2-2\)
\(3\) \(8y^3-12y\)
\(4\) \(16y^4-48y^2+12\)
\(5\) \(32y^5-160y^3+120y\)
\(6\) \(64y^6-480y^4+720y^2-120\)

The unnormalized wavefunction is

\(\Psi_v(x)=H_v(y)e^{-y^2/2}\)

It follows from the integrals given in Table 7E.1 that

\(\int_{-\infty}^{+\infty}{\Psi_v^*\Psi_v dx}=\alpha\int_{-\infty}^{+\infty}{\Psi_v^*\Psi_v dy}=\alpha\int_{-\infty}^{+\infty}{H_v^2(y)e^{-y^2} dy} =\alpha\pi^{1/2}2^vv!\)

where \(v! = v(v − 1)(v − 2) … 1\) and \(0! ≡ 1\). Therefore,

\(N_v=\left\lgroup\frac{1}{\alpha\pi^{1/2}2^vv!}\right\rgroup^{1/2}\) Normalization constant (7E.10)

Note that \(N_v\) is different for each value of \(v\).
