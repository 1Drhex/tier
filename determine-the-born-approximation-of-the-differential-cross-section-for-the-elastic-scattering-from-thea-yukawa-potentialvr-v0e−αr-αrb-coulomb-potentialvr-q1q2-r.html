Guest Question.

Determine the Born approximation of the differential cross-section for the elastic scattering from the:

(a) Yukawa potential:

\(V(r)=V_{0} \frac{e^{-\alpha r}}{\alpha r}\)

(b) Coulomb potential:

\(V(r)=\frac{q_{1} q_{2}}{r}\)

 

Admin
Solution

The scattering amplitude in the Born approximation is given by (A.84).

(a) For the Yukawa potential, we get

\(\begin{aligned}f_{B}(q) &=-\frac{2 m V_{0}}{\hbar^{2} q} \int_{0}^{\infty} d r \sin (q r) \frac{e^{-\alpha r}}{\alpha r} r=\\&=-\frac{2 m V_{0}}{\hbar^{2} q \alpha} \Im\left(\int_{0}^{\infty} d r e^{-\alpha r+i q r}\right)=\\&=-\frac{2 m V_{0}}{\hbar^{2} q \alpha} \Im\left(\frac{1}{\alpha-\imath q}\right)=\\&=-\frac{2 m V_{0}}{\hbar^{2} \alpha} \frac{1}{\alpha^{2}+q^{2}}\end{aligned}\)

The differential cross-section is

\(\frac{d \sigma_{B}}{d \Omega}=\left(\frac{2 m V_{0}}{\hbar^{2} \alpha}\right)^{2}\left(\frac{1}{\alpha^{2}+4 k^{2} \sin ^{2} \frac{\theta}{2}}\right)^{2}\)

where \(\theta\) is the angle between the incident and the scattering direction.

(b) We can obtain the differential cross-section for the Coulomb potential from the previous formulas through the limit

\(\alpha \rightarrow 0, \quad V_{0} \rightarrow 0, \quad \frac{V_{0}}{\alpha} \rightarrow q_{1} q_{2}\)

The result is

\(\frac{d \sigma_{B}}{d \Omega}=\frac{q_{1}^{2} q_{2}^{2}}{16 E^{2} \sin ^{4} \frac{\theta}{2}}\)

where \(E=\hbar^{2} k^{2} / 2 m\) is the energy of the particle incident on the center of force. This result coincides with the classic Rutherford one and with the exact quantum result (notice that the cross-section does not depend on \(\hbar\) ).
