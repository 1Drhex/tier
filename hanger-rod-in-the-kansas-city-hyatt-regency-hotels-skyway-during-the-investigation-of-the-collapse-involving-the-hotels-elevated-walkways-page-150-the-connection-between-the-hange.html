Guest Question.

Hanger Rod in the Kansas City Hyatt Regency Hotel’s Skyway

During the investigation of the collapse involving the hotel’s elevated walkways (page 150), the connection between the hanger rod and the upper walkway was found to have broken at a load of approximately 20,500 lb. In the dimensions of ksi, determine the stress in the 1.25-in.-diameter hanger rod when the tension was 20,500 lb.

Approach
The hanger rod carries a tension force in the manner shown in Figure 5.3
with\( F = 2.05 × 10^4 \) lb and d = 1.25 in. We are to calculate the stress by applying Equation (5.2).

\(\sigma =\frac{F}{A} \) (5.2)
Admin
Solution

By using Equation (5.1),

\(A=\pi \frac{d^2}{4}\) (5.1)

the cross-sectional area of the hanger rod is

\(A=\frac{\pi (1.25in.)^2}{4} \longleftarrow \left[A=\frac{\pi d^2}{4} \right] \)

 

\(=1.227in^2 \)

The tensile stress is

\(\sigma =\frac{2.05\times 10^4lb}{1.227 in^2} \longleftarrow \left[\sigma =\frac{F}{A} \right]\)

 

\(= 1.670 \times 10^4\frac{lb}{in^2} \)

 

\(= 1.670 \times 10^4 psi\)

where we have substituted the definition of the derived unit psi (pound per square inch) for stress from Table 2.5. It is conventional to apply the abbreviation “ksi” to represent the factor of 1000 psi in a more compact form:

\(\sigma= (1.670 \times 10^4 psi)\left(10^{-3 }\frac{ksi}{psi}\right) \)

 

\(=16.70 (\cancel{psi})(\frac{ksi}{\cancel{psi}} )\)
= 16.70 ksi

Discussion
As we’ll see in the following section, this level of stress is not particularly high when compared to the strength of steel materials. Although the tension may not have been sufficient to break the hanger rod, it was great enough to destroy the connection between the rod and the pedestrian walkway. As was the case in the skyways’ collapse, it is not an uncommon situation in mechanical engineering for the connections between components to be weaker than the components themselves.

\(\sigma=16.70 ksi\)
