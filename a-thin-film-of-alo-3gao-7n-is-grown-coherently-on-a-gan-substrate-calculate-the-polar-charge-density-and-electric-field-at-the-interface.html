Guest Question.

A thin film of \(Al_{0.3} Ga_{0.7} N \) is grown coherently on a GaN substrate. Calculate the polar charge density and electric field at the interface.

Admin
Solution

The lattice constant of \(Al_{0.30}Ga_{0.7} N \) is given by Vegard’s law

\(a_{all} = 0.3 a_{AlN} +0.7 a _{GaN} = 3.111 \mathring{A} \)

The strain tensor components are

\(\epsilon_{xx} = 0.006 = \epsilon_{yy} \)

Using the elastic constant values from Table 6.4

MATERIAL \(C_{11} \left(N / m^2 \right)\) \(C_{12} \left(N/m^2 \right)\) \(C_{44} \left(N/ m^2 \right)\)
Si \(1.66 \times 10^{11}\) \(0.64 \times 10^{11}\) \(0.8 \times 10^{11}\)
Ge \(1.29 \times 10^{11}\) \(0.48 \times 10^{11}\) \(0.67 \times 10^{11}\)
GaAs \(1.2 \times 10^{11}\) \(0.54 \times 10^{11}\) \(0.59 \times 10^{11}\)
C \(10.76 \times 10^{11}\) \(1.25 \times 10^{11}\) \(5.76 \times 10^{11}\)

 

MATERIAL \(C_{13} \left(N/m^2 \right) \) \(C_{33} \left(N/m^2 \right) \)
GaN \(10.9 \times 10^{11}\) \(35.5 \times 10^{11}\)
AlN \(12.0 \times 10^{11}\) \(39.5 \times 10^{11}\)

Table 6.4: Elastic constant for some fee- and hep-based semiconductors. (For Si, Ge, and GaAs see H. J. McSkimin and P. Andreatch, J. Appl. Phys., 35, 2161 (1964) and D. I. Bolef and M. Meres, J. Appl Phys., 31, 1010 (1960). For nitrides see J. H. Edgar, Properties of III-V Nitrides, INSPEC, London (1994) and R. B. Schwarz, K. Khachaturyan, and E. R. Weber, Appl. Phys. Lett., 74, 1122 (1997).)

\(\epsilon_{zz} = -0.6 \times 0.006 = 0.0036 \)

The piezoelectric effect induced polar charge then becomes

\(P_{pz} = 0.0097 C/m^2 \)

This corresponds to a density of \(6.06 \times 10^{12} cm^{-2} \) electronic charges.

In addition to the piezoelectric charge the spontaneous polarization charge is

\(P_{sp} = 0.3 \left(0.089 \right) + 0.7 \left(0.029 \right) – 0.029 = 0.018 C/m^2 \)

which corresponds to a density of \(1.125 \times 10^{13} cm^{-2} \) charges. The total charge (fixed) arising at the interface is the sum of the two charges
