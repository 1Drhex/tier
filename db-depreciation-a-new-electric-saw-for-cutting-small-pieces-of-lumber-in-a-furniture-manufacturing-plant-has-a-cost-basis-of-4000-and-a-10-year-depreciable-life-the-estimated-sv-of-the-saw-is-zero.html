Guest Question.

DB Depreciation
A new electric saw for cutting small pieces of lumber in a furniture manufacturing plant has a cost basis of $4,000 and a 10-year depreciable life. The estimated SV of the saw is zero at the end of 10 years. Use the DB method to calculate the annual depreciation amounts when
(a) R=2/N(200% DB method)
(b) R=1.5/N(150% DB method).
Tabulate the annual depreciation amount and BV for each year.

Admin
Solution

Annual depreciation, cumulative depreciation, and BV are determined by using
Equations (7-6), (7-7), and (7-8),

\(d_{K}\) = \( B\left(1-R\right)^{K-1} R \) (7-6)

\(d^{\ast }_{k} \) = \(B\left[1-\left(1-R\right)^{k} \right] \) (7-7)

\(BV_{K} = B\left(1-R\right)^{K} \) (7-8)

respectively. Sample calculations for year six are as follows:

(a) R=2/10=0.2,

\(d_{6} \) = $4,000\(\left(1-0.2\right)^{5}\) (0.2) = $262.14

\(d^{\ast }_{6} \) = $4,000[ 1- \(\left(1-0.2\right)^{6}\)] = $2,951.42,

\(BV_{6}\) = $4,000 \(\left(1-0.2\right)^{6}\) = $1,048.58.

(b) R =1.5/10 = 0.15,
\(d_{6} \) = $4,000\(\left(1-0.15\right)^{5} \)(0.15) = $266.22,

\(d^{\ast }_{6} \) = $4,000[1- \(\left(1-0.15\right)^{5}\)] = $2,491.40

\(BV_{6}\) = $4,000 \(\left(1-0.15\right)^{5}\) = $1,508.60.
