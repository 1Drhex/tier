Guest Question.

Figure 5.13 indicates a set of design reference flows for the evening peak hour at a proposed urban-based priority intersection. All flows are in passenger car units per hour.

Estimate the ratio of flow to capacity for each of the opposed movements and, for the one with the highest ratio, estimate the average queue length and delay per vehicle during the peak hour.

Geometric parameters:

The width of the major carriageway (W) is 9.5m.
The lane widths for traffic exiting the minor road turning both left and right (\(w_{ba}, w_{bc}\)) are 2.5m.
The lane width on the major road for traffic waiting to turn right onto the minor road (\(w_{cb}\)), is 2.2m.

Visibility to the right and left for traffic exiting the minor road turning right (\(Vr_{ba}, Vl_{ba}\)) is 30m and 50m respectively.
Visibility to the right for traffic exiting the minor road turning left (\(Vr_{bc}\)) is 30m.
Visibility to the right for traffic exiting the major road, turning right onto the minor road (\(Vr_{cb}\)) is 50m.

Admin
Solution

When using Equations 5.1 to 5.3 for estimating the ratios of flow to capacity for the various opposed turning movements, the design reference flows shown in Fig. 5.13 should be multiplied by 1.125 in order to allow for shortterm variations in the traffic flow. (These short-term variations are allowed or in PICADY.)

\(\mu _{ba} = D \left(627 + 14 W_{cr} – Y \left[0.364 q_{ac} + 0.114 q_{ab}+ 0.229 q_{ca} + 0.520q_{cb} \right] \right)\)
\(\mu _{bc} = E \left(745 – Y \left[0.364q_{ac} + 0.114 q_{ab}\right] \right)\)
\(\mu _{cb} = F \left(745 -0.364 \left[q_{ac} + q_{ab}\right] \right)\)

Factored flows:

\(q_{ab} = 169 (150 \times1.125)\)
\(q_{ac} = 619 (550 \times1.125)\)
\(q_{ca} = 844 (750 \times1.125)\)
\(q_{cb} = 84 (75 \times1.125)\)
\(q_{ba} = 197 (175 \times1.125)\)
\(q_{bc} = 141 (125 \times1.125)\)

Parameters D, E and F:

\(D = \left[1 + 0.094\left(2.5-3.65\right)\right]\left[1+0.0009\left(30-120\right) \right]\left[1+0.0006+\left(50-150\right)\right]= 0.77\)

 

\(E = \left[1 + 0.094 \left(2.5 – 3.65\right) \right] \left[1+0.0009\left(30-120\right) \right] = 0.82 \)

 

\(F = \left[1 + 0.094 \left(2.2 – 36.5\right) \right] \left[1+0.0009\left(50-120\right) \right] = 0.81 \)

 

\(Y= \left[1 – \left(0.345 \times 9.5\right) \right] = 0.67 \)

Capacities:

\(\mu _{ba} = 0.77\left(627 – 0.67\left[0.364 (619) + 0.114 (169)+ 0.229 (844) + 0.520(84) \right] \right) = 234 pcu/hr\)

\(\mu _{bc} = 0.82 \left(745 – 0.67\left[0.364(619) + 0.114 (169)\right] \right) = 477 pcu/hr\)

\(\mu _{cb} = \left[\left(745 -(0.364 \times 0.67) (619 + 169) \right)\right] = 448 pcu/hr\)

Ratios of flow to capacity (RFC):
For the most critical movement (traffic exiting onto the major road, turning
right) the ratio is just below the maximum allowed in urban areas of 0.85.
The maximum reduces to 0.75 in rural areas.

Table 5.1 RFCs for opposed flow movements

RFC Capacity (pcu/hr) Flow (pcu/hr) Stream
0.84 234 197 \(q_{ba}\)
0.30 477 141 \(q_{bc}\)
0.19 448 84 \(q_{cb}\)

Queue length at stream B-A:

\(L = 0.5 \times \left(\left(A^{2} + B \right) ^{{1}/{2}} – A \right) \)

Assuming random arrivals (therefore C = 1)

\(A = (1 – ρ)μt + 1 – L_{0}\)

 

\(B = 4(L_{0} + ρμt)\)

 

\(ρ =0.84\)

 

\(μ=234 pcu/hr\)

 

\(t = 1 hour\)

 

\(L_{0} = 0 \)(no cars waiting at start of time period)

Therefore:

\(A =[ (1 – 0.84)\times 234 \times 1] + 1 = 38.44\)

 

\(B = 4 \times 0.84 \times 234 \times 1 =786.24\)

 

\(L = 0.5 \times \left(\left(38.44^{2} + 786.24 \right) ^{{1}/{2}} – 32.45 \right) = 5 cars \)

Total delay during peak hour:

\(D_{t}= 0.5 \times \left(\left(F^{2} +G\right) ^{{1}/{2}} – F\right)\)

 

\( F= [0.5 \times (1 – 0.84)\times234 \times 1 ]+ 1 = 11.72\)

 

\(G = 2 \times 0.84 \times 234 \times 1 = 393.12 \)

 

\(D_{t}= 0.5 \times \left(\left(19.72^{2} + 393.12 \right) ^{{1}/{2}} – 17.72\right) = 4.1 hours\)

Delay per arriving vehicle (excluding delay at stop-line):

\(D_{v}= 0.5 \times \left(\left(P^{2} + Q \right) ^{{1}/{2}} -P \right)+{1}/{μ} \)

\(P = \left[0.5 \times 0.16\right] + \frac{1}{234}\)

= 0 .08 + 0.00427

= 0.0843

\(Q = \frac{2}{234} \times 0.84\)

= 0.0072

\(D_{v}=\left\{0.5\times\left[\left(0.0843^{2} + 0.0072\right)^{{1}/{2}}-0.0843\right]\right\}+{1}/{234}\)

= 0.0219 hours

=79 seconds per vehicle

Thus, the average delay per vehicle is 1.3 minutes, with a queue length of five vehicles.
