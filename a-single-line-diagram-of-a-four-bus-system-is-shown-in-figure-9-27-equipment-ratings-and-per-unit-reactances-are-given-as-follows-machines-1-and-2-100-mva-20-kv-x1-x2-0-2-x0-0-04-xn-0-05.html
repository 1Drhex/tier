Guest Question.

A single-line diagram of a four-bus system is shown in Figure 9.27. Equipment ratings and per-unit reactances are given as follows.

Machines 1 and 2: 100 MVA 20 kV \(X _{1}= X _{2}=0.2\) \(X _{0}=0.04 \quad X _{n}=0.05\)

Transformers \(T _{1} \text { and } T _{2}\): 100 MVA 20Δ/345Y kV \(X _{1}= X _{2}= X _{0}=0.08\)

On a base of 100 MVA and 345 kV in the zone of the transmission line, the series reactances of the transmission line are \(X _{1}= X _{2}=0.15\) and \(X _{0}=0.5\) per unit. (a) Draw each of the sequence networks and determine the bus impedance matrix for each of them. (b) Assume the system to be operating at nominal system voltage without prefault currents when a bolted line-to-line fault occurs at bus 3. Compute the fault current, the line-to-line voltages at the faulted bus, and the line-to-line voltages at the terminals of machine 2. (c) Assume the system to be operating at nominal system voltage without prefault currents, when a bolted double line-toground fault occurs at the terminals of machine 2. Compute the fault current and the line-to-line voltages at the faulted bus.

 

 

 

Admin
Solution

(a)

Negative-sequence network is same as above without sources.

 

 

\(\bar{Z}_{B U S 1}=\bar{Z}_{B U S 2}= \begin{matrix}(1)\\ (2)\\ (3)\\ (4)\end{matrix}\overset{\begin{matrix} (1) & (2) & (3) & (4) \end{matrix}}{\left[\begin{array}{llll}j 0.1437 & j 0.1211 & j 0.0789 & j 0.0563 \\j 0.1211 & j 0.1696 & j 0.1104 & j 0.0789 \\j 0.0789 & j 0.1104 & j 0.1696 & j 0.1211 \\j 0.0563 & j 0.0789 & j 0.1211 & j 0.1437\end{array}\right]}\)

 

\(\bar{Z}_{B U S 0}= \begin{matrix}(1)\\ (2)\\ (3)\\ (4)\end{matrix}\overset{\begin{matrix} (1) & (2) & (3) & (4) \end{matrix}}{\left[\begin{array}{cccc}j 0.19 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\0 & j 0.08 & j 0.08 & 0 \\0 & j 0.08 & j 0.58 & 0 \\0 & 0 & 0 & j 0.19\end{array}\right]}\)

 

 

(b) For the line-to-line fault, Thévenin equivalent circuit:

Upper case A is used because fault is in the HV-transmission line circuit.

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{I}_{f A 1}=-\bar{I}_{f A 2}=\frac{1 \angle 0^{\circ}}{j 0.1696+j 0.1696}=-j 2.9481 \\&\bar{I}_{f A}=\bar{I}_{f A 1}+\bar{I}_{f A 2}=0 \\&\bar{I}_{f B}=a^{2} \bar{I}_{f A 1}+a \bar{I}_{f A 2}=-5.1061+j 0=855 \angle 180^{\circ} A \\&\bar{I}_{f C}=-\bar{I}_{f B}=5.1061+j 0=855 \angle 0^{\circ} A\end{aligned}\)

 

∵ Base current in HV transmission line is \(\frac{100,000}{\sqrt{3} \times 345}=167.35 A\)

Symmetrical components of phase-A voltage to ground at bus (3) are

\(\bar{V}_{3 A 0}=0 ; \bar{V}_{3 A 1}=\bar{V}_{3 A 2}=1-(j 0.1696)(-j 2.9481)=0.5+j 0\)

Line-to-Ground voltages at fault bus (3) are

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{V}_{3 A}=\bar{V}_{3 A 0}+\bar{V}_{3 A 1}+\bar{V}_{3 A 2}=0+0.5+0.5=1 \angle 0^{\circ} \\&\bar{V}_{3 B}=\bar{V}_{3 A 0}+a^{2} \bar{V}_{3 A 1}+a \bar{V}_{3 A 2}=0.5 \angle 180^{\circ} \\&\bar{V}_{3 C}=\bar{V}_{3 B}=0.5 \angle 180^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

Line-to-line voltages at fault bus (3) are

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{V}_{3, A B}=\bar{V}_{3 A}-\bar{V}_{3 B}=1.5 \angle 0^{\circ}=1.5 \times \frac{345}{\sqrt{3}}=299 \angle 0^{\circ} kV \\&\bar{V}_{3, B C}=\bar{V}_{3 B}-\bar{V}_{3 C}=0 \\&\bar{V}_{3, C A}=\bar{V}_{3 C}-\bar{V}_{3 A}=1.5 \angle 180^{\circ}=299 \angle 180^{\circ} kV\end{aligned}\)

Avoiding, for the moment, phase shifts due to ∆ −Y transformer connected to machine 2, sequence voltages of phase A at bus (4) using the bus-impedance matrix are calculated as

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{V}_{4 A 0}=-\bar{Z}_{430} \bar{I}_{f A 0}=0 \\&\bar{V}_{4 A 1}=\bar{V}_{f}-\bar{Z}_{431} \bar{I}_{f A 1}=1-(j 0.1211)(-j 2.9481)=0.643 \\&\bar{V}_{4 A 2}=-\bar{Z}_{432} \bar{I}_{f A 2}=-(j 0.1211)(j 2.9481)=0.357\end{aligned}\)

Accounting for phase shifts

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{V}_{4 a 1}=\bar{V}_{4 A 1} \angle-30^{\circ}=0.643 \angle-30^{\circ}=0.5569-j 0.3215 \\&\bar{V}_{4 a 2}=\bar{V}_{4 A 2} \angle 30^{\circ}=0.357 \angle 30^{\circ}=0.3092+j 0.1785 \\&\bar{V}_{4 a}=\bar{V}_{4 a 0}+\bar{V}_{4 a 1}+\bar{V}_{4 a 2}=0.8661-j 0.143=0.8778 \angle-9.4^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

Phase-b voltages at terminals of machine 2 are

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{V}_{4 b 0}=\bar{V}_{4 a 0}=0 \\&\bar{V}_{4 b 1}=a^{2} \bar{V}_{4 a 1}=0.643 \angle 240^{\circ}-30^{\circ}=-0.3569-j 0.3215 \\&\bar{V}_{4 b 2}=a \bar{V}_{4 a 2}=0.357 \angle 120^{\circ}+30^{\circ}=-0.3092+j 0.1785 \\&\bar{V}_{4 b}=\bar{V}_{4 b 0}+\bar{V}_{4 b 1}+\bar{V}_{4 b 2}=-0.8661-j 0.143=0.8778 \angle-170.6^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

For phase C of machine 2,

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{V}_{4 c 0}=\bar{V}_{4 a 0}=0 \\&\bar{V}_{4 c 1}=a \bar{V}_{4 a 1}=0.643 \angle 90^{\circ} ; \bar{V}_{4 c 2}=a^{2} \bar{V}_{4 a 2}=0.357 \angle-90^{\circ} \\&\bar{V}_{4 c}=\bar{V}_{4 c 0}+\bar{V}_{4 c 1}+\bar{V}_{4 c 2}=j 0.286\end{aligned}\)

Line-to-line voltages at terminals of machine 2 are given by

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{V}_{4, a b}=\bar{V}_{4 a}-\bar{V}_{4 b}=1.7322+j 0=1.7322 \times \frac{20}{\sqrt{3}}=20 \angle 0^{\circ} kV \\&\bar{V}_{4, b c}=\bar{V}_{4 b}-\bar{V}_{4 c}=-0.8661-j 0.429=0.9665 \angle-153.65^{\circ}=11.2 kV \angle-153.65^{\circ} \\&\bar{V}_{4, c a}=\bar{V}_{4 c}-\bar{V}_{4 a}=-0.8661+j 0.429=0.9665 \angle 153.65^{\circ}=11.2 kV \angle 153.65^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

 

(c) For the double line-to-line fault, connection of Thévenin equivalents of sequence networks is shown below:

\(\bar{I}_{f a 1}=\frac{1 \angle 0^{\circ}}{j 0.1437+\frac{j 0.1437(j 0.19)}{j(0.1437+0.19)}}=-j 4.4342\)

Sequence voltages at the fault are

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{V}_{4 a 1}=\bar{V}_{4 a 2}=\bar{V}_{4 a 0}=1-(-j 4.4342)(j 0.1437)=0.3628 \\&\bar{I}_{f a 2}=j 4.4342 \frac{j 0.19}{j(0.1437+0.19)}=j 2.5247 \\&\bar{I}_{f a 0}=j 4.4342 \frac{j 0.1437}{j(0.1437+0.19)}=j 1.9095\end{aligned}\)

Currents out of the system at the fault point are

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{I}_{f a}=\bar{I}_{f a 0}+\bar{I}_{f a 1}+\bar{I}_{f a 2}=0 \\&\bar{I}_{f b}=\bar{I}_{f a 0}+a^{2} \bar{I}_{f a 1}+a \bar{I}_{f a 2}=-6.0266+j 2.8642=6.6726 \angle 154.6^{\circ} \\&\bar{I}_{f c}=\bar{I}_{f a 0}+a \bar{I}_{f a 1}+a^{2} \bar{I}_{f a 2}=6.0266+j 2.8642=6.6726 \angle 25.4^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

Current \(I_{f}\) into the ground is

\(\bar{I}_{f}=\bar{I}_{f b}+\bar{I}_{f c}=3 \bar{I}_{f a 0}=j 5.7285\)

a-b-c voltages at the fault bus are

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{V}_{4 a}=\bar{V}_{4 a 0}+\bar{V}_{4 a 1}+\bar{V}_{4 a 2}=3 \bar{V}_{4 a 1}=3(0.3628)=1.0884 \\&\bar{V}_{4 b}=\bar{V}_{4 c}=0 \\&\bar{V}_{4, a b}=\bar{V}_{4 a}-\bar{V}_{4 b}=1.0884 ; \bar{V}_{4, b c}=\bar{V}_{4 b}-\bar{V}_{4 c}=0 ;\\&\bar{V}_{4, c a}=\bar{V}_{4 c}-\bar{V}_{4 a}=-1.0884\end{aligned}\)

Base current \(=\frac{100 \times 10^{3}}{\sqrt{3} \times 20}=2887 A\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\therefore \bar{I}_{f a}=0 ; \bar{I}_{f b}=19.262 \angle 154.6^{\circ} kA ; \bar{I}_{f c}=19.262 \angle 25.4^{\circ} kA \\&\bar{I}_{f}=16.538 \angle 90^{\circ} kA\end{aligned}\)

Base line-to-neutral voltage in machine 2 is \(20 / \sqrt{3} kV\)

\(\therefore \bar{V}_{4, a b}=12.568 \angle 0^{\circ} kV ; \bar{V}_{4, b c}=0 ; \bar{V}_{4, c a}=12.568 \angle 180^{\circ} kV\)
