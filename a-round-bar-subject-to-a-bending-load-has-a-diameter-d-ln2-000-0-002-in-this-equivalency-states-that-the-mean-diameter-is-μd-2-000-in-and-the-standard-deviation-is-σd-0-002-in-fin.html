Guest Question.

A round bar subject to a bending load has a diameter d = LN(2.000, 0.002) in. This equivalency states that the mean diameter is \(μ_{d}\) = 2.000 in and the standard deviation is \(\hat{σ}_{d}\) = 0.002 in. Find the mean and the standard deviation of the second moment of area.

Admin
Solution

The second moment of area is given by the equation

\(I =\frac{πd^{4}}{64}\)

The coefficient of variation of the diameter is

\(C_{d} = \frac{\hat{σ}_{d}}{μ_{d}} =\frac{0.002}{2 }= 0.001\)

Using Table 20–6, we find

\(μ_{I} = (π/64)μ^{4}_{d}(1 + 6C^{2}_{d})= (π/64)(2.000)^{4}[1 + 6(0.001)^{2}] = 0.785 in^{4}\)

\( \hat{σ}_{I} = 4μ^{4}_{d}C_{d} [1 + (9/4)C^{2}_{d}]= 4(2.000)^{4}(0.001)[1 + (9/4)(0.001)^{2}] = 0.064 in^{4}\)

These results can be expressed in the form

\(I = LN(0.785, 0.064) = 0.785LN(1, 0.0815) in^{4}\)
