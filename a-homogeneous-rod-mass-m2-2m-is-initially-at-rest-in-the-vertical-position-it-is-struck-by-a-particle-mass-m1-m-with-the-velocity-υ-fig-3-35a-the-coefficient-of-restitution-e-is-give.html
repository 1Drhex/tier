Guest Question.

A homogeneous rod (mass \(m_{2} \) = 2m) is initially at rest in the vertical position. It is struck by a particle (mass \(m_{1} \) = m) with the velocity υ (Fig. 3.35a). The coefficient of restitution e is given. Calculate the velocity of the particle and the angular velocity of the rod immediately after the impact.

Admin
Solution

The particle performs a direct collision with the rod; the linear impulse acts along the line of impact (Fig. 3.35b). The principle of linear impulse and momentum for the particle is given by

\(\rightarrow :m_{1} \left(\overline{\upsilon }-\upsilon \right) =-\hat{F}\)

and the principle of angular impulse and momentum for the rod (ω = 0) reads

\(\overset{\curvearrowleft }{A} :\Theta _{A}\overline{\omega }=\frac{2}{3}l\hat{F} \) .

The velocity of the rod at the point of impact just after the collision is \(\frac{2}{3} l\overline{\omega } \). Thus, Equation (3.38) becomes

\(e=-\frac{\overline{\upsilon }^{P}_{1x}-\overline{\upsilon }^{P}_{2x} }{\upsilon ^{P}_{1x}-\upsilon ^{P}_{2x} }\) (3.38)

\(e=-\frac{\overline{\upsilon }-\frac{2}{3}l\overline{\omega } }{\upsilon }\) .

Now we have three equations for the three unknowns \(\hat{F} ,\overline{\upsilon } \) and \(\overline{\omega } \). Introduction of the given values of the masses and of \(\Theta _{A} =m_{2}l^{2} /3 \) yields the velocities

\(\overline{\upsilon }=\frac{\upsilon }{5}\left(2-3e\right), \overline{\omega }=\frac{3}{5} \frac{\upsilon }{l} \left(1+e\right)\) .

In the case of an ideal plastic impact (e = 0) we obtain \(\overline{\upsilon }=\frac{2}{5}\upsilon \) and \(\overline{\omega }=\frac{3}{5}\upsilon/l \) . An ideal elastic impact (e = 1) results in \(\overline{\upsilon }=-\upsilon /5 \) and \(\overline{\omega }=\frac{6}{5} \upsilon /l \) . There is no loss of kinetic energy in the case of an ideal elastic impact: \(\bar{T} =\frac{1}{2}m\bar{\upsilon }^{2}+\frac{1}{2}\Theta _{A}\bar{\omega }^{2} =\frac{1}{2} m\upsilon ^{2}=T \) .
