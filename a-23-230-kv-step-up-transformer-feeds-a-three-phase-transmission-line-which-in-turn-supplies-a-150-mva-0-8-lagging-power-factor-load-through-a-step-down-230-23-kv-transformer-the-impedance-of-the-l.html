Guest Question.

A 23/230-kV step-up transformer feeds a three-phase transmission line, which in turn supplies a 150-MVA, 0.8 lagging power factor load through a step-down 230/23-kV transformer. The impedance of the line and transformers at 230 kV is 18 + j60 立. Determine the tap setting for each transformer to maintain the voltage at the load at 23 kV.

Admin
Solution

A radial line with tap-changing transformers at both ends is shown below:

\(\bar{V}_{1}^{\prime} \text { and }\bar{V}_{2}^{\prime}\) are the supply phase voltage and the load phase voltage, respectively, referred to the high-voltage side. \( \bar{V}_{S} \text { and } \bar{V}_{R} \) are the phase voltages at both ends of the line. \(t_{S} \text { and } t_{R} \) are the tap settings in per unit. The impedance \(\bar{Z}\) includes the line impedance plus the referred impedances of the sending end and the receiving end transformers to the highvoltage side. After drawing the voltage phasor diagram for the KVL \(\bar{V}_{S}=\bar{V}_{R}+(R+j X) \bar{I}\) , neglecting the phase shift between \(\bar{V}_{S} \text { and } \bar{V}_{R}\) as an approximation, and noting that \(\bar{V}_{S}=t_{S} \bar{V}_{1}^{\prime} \text { and } \bar{V}_{R}=t_{R} \bar{V}_{2}^{\prime}\) , it can be shown that

\(t_{S}=\sqrt{\frac{\left|\bar{V}_{2}^{\prime}\right| /\left|\bar{V}_{1}^{\prime}\right|}{1-\frac{R P_{\phi}+X Q_{\phi}}{\left|\bar{V}_{1}^{\prime}\right|\left|\bar{V}_{2}^{\prime}\right|}}}\)

where \(P_{\phi} \text { and } Q_{\phi}\) are the load real and reactive powers per phase and it is assumed that \(t_{S} t_{R}=1\).

In our problem, \(P_{\phi}=\frac{1}{3}(150 \times 0.8)=40 MW\)

and

\(\begin{aligned}&Q_{\phi}=\frac{1}{3}(150 \times 0.6)=30 MVAR \\&\left|\bar{V}_{1}^{\prime}\right|=\left|\bar{V}_{2}^{\prime}\right|=\frac{230}{\sqrt{3}} kV\end{aligned}\)

\(t_{S}\) is calculated as \(k_{S}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{1-\frac{(18)(40)+(60)(30)}{(230 / \sqrt{5})^{2}}}}=1.08 pu\)

and \(t_{R}=\frac{1}{1.08}=0.926 pu\)
