Guest Question.

Formic acid has a pKa of 3.75. What is the pH of an aqueous solution of concentration\( 5.0 \times 10^{-3} mol dm^{-3}?\)

Admin
Solution

Formic acid is a weak acid and is not fully dissociated in aqueous solution:

\(HCO_{2}H(aq)+H_{2}O(l)\leftrightharpoons H_{3}O^{+}HCO_{2}^{+}(aq)\)

Therefore, the equilibrium concentration of \(H_{3}O^{+} \)ions is not the same as the initial concentration of \(HCO_{2}H\). To find\([ H_{3}O^{+}]\), we need the equilibrium constant:

\(K_{a}=\frac{[H_{3}O^{+}][HCO_{2}]^{-}}{[HCO_{2}H]}=10^{-pk_{a}}\).

\(\frac{[H_{3}O^{+}][HCO_{2}]^{-}}{[HCO_{2}H]}=10^{-3.75}=1.\times 10^{-4}\)

From the stoichiometry of this equation, \([H_{3}O^{+}]=[HCO_{2}]^{-} therefore:\).

\(\frac{[H_{3}O^{+}]^{2}}{[HCO_{2}H]}=1.8\times 10^{-4}\).

\([H_{3}O^{+}]^{2}=1.8\times 10^{-4}\times [HCO_{2}H]\).

\([H_{3}O]^{+}=\sqrt{1.\times 10^{-4}\times [HCO_{2}H]}\).

\([H_{3}O]^{+}=\sqrt{1.\times 10^{-4}\times 5.0 \times 10^{-3} } mol dm^{-3}\)

If you compare this concentration to the initial concentration of\( HCO_{3}H of 5.0 \times 10^{-3} mol dm^{-3}\), it shows that 19% of the acid has dissociated, and this is a significant amount:.

Degree of dissociation =\(\frac{(9.5\times 10^{-4}moldm^{-3})}{(5.0\times 10^{-3}moldm^{-3})}\times 100= 19%\)

Therefore, we need to solve this problem more rigorously:

\(HCO_{2}H(aq)+H_{2}O(l)\rightleftharpoons H_{3}O^{+}(aq)+HCO_{2} ^{-}(aq)\).

\(K_{a}=1.8\times 10^{-4}=\frac{[H^{3}O^{+}[HCO_{2}^{-}]]}{[HCO_{2}^{-}H]}=\frac{x^{2}}{(5.0\times 10^{-3})-x}\).

\(x^{2}=(1.8\times 10^{-4})(5.0\times 10^{-3})-(1.8\times 10^{-4})x\).

\(x=\frac{-(1.8\times 10^{-4})\pm \sqrt{(1.8\times 10^{-4})^{2}+4(9.0\times 10^{-7})}}{2}\)

= \(8\times 10^{-4}\)

Clearly, only the positive value is possible and therefore:

\(x=[H_{3}O]^{+}=8.6\times 10^{-4}moldm^{-3}\)

Now we can find the pH of the solution:

\(pH=-log[H_{3}O^{+}]=-log (8.6\times 10^{-4})=3.07\)
