Guest Question.

Consider the rigid body shown in Figure 2.14, which is subjected to two forces (\(F_{1}\) , \(F_{2}\) ) whose
components are as follows:
– For \(F_{1}\) applied at A(20,8,0): \(F_{1x}\) = 40 kN, \(F_{1y}\) = 30 kN, \(F_{1z}\) = 20 kN
– For \(F_{2}\) applied at B(0,0,12): \(F_{2x}\) = 15 kN, \(F_{2y}\) = 10 kN, \(F_{2z}\) = 30 kN
Calculate the resultant force and moment applied at the centre of gravity G(10,4,6).

Admin
Solution

The components of the resultant force are calculated by summing the components of all forces
in the same direction:

\(R_{x}=\sum{F_{x} }= 55 KN\) ; \(R_{y}=\sum{F_{y} }= 40 KN\) ;\(R_{z}=\sum{F_{z} }= 50 KN\)

and the resultant force is:

\(R= \sqrt{55^{2}+ 40^{2}+50^{2} }= 84.41 KN \)

The inclination of the line of action of R is defined by the direction cosines, evaluated from R and
its components using Equations 2.4:

\(F_{x}= F\cos \theta _{x}=lF\) where \(l= \cos \theta _{x}\) (2.4 a)

 

\(F_{y}= F\cos \theta _{y}=mF\) where \(m= \cos \theta _{y}\) (2.4 b)

 

\(F_{z}= F\cos \theta _{z}=nF\) where \(n= \cos \theta _{z}\) (2.4 c)

 

\(I=\frac{R_{x} }{R}= 0.652\); \(m=\frac{R_{y} }{R}= 0.474\); \(n=\frac{R_{z} }{R}= 0.592\)

The moment calculated about each coordinate axis through G(10,4,6) is determined using
Equations 2.10 for each of the applied forces:

\(M_{x(B)}=F_{z}(y_{A}-y_{B} )-F_{y}(z_{A}-z_{B} )\) (2.10 a)

 

\(M_{y(B)}=F_{x}(z_{A}-z_{B} )-F_{z}(x_{A}-x_{B} )\) (2.10 b)

 

\(M_{z(B)}=F_{y}(x_{A}-x_{B} )-F_{x}(y_{A}-y_{B} )\) (2.10 c)

 

\(M_{xR(G)}= F_{z1}(y_{A}-y_{G} )-F_{y1}(z_{A}-z_{G} )+ F_{z2}(y_{B}-y_{G} ) – F_{y2}(z_{B}-z_{G} )\)

 

\(= 20(8-4)-30(0-6)+30(0-4)-10(12-6)=80 kNm\)

 

\(M_{yR(G)}= 40(0-6)-20(20-10)+15(12-6)-30(0-10)=-50kNm\)

 

\(M_{zR(G)}= 30(20-10)-40(8-4)+10(0-10)-15(0-4)=100kNm\)

The resultant moment \(M_{R}\) at point G is therefore:

\(M_{R(G)}= \sqrt{80^{2}+(-50)^{2}+100^{2} }= 137.5 kNm\)
