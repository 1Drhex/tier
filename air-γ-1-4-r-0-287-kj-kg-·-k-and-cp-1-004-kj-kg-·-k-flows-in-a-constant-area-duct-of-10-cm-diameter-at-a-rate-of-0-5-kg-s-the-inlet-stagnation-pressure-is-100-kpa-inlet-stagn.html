Guest Question.

Air \(\left(\gamma=1.4, \quad R=0.287 kJ / kg \cdot K \text { and } c_{ p }=1.004 kJ / kg \cdot K \right)\) flows in a constant-area duct of 10 cm diameter at a rate of 0.5 kg/s. The inlet stagnation pressure is 100 kPa; inlet stagnation temperature is 35°C. Find the following:

(a) Two possible values of inlet Mach number.

(b) For each inlet Mach number of part (a), determine the heat addition rate in kilowatts necessary to choke the duct.

Admin
Solution
\(\dot{ m }=\rho_{1} AV _{1}=\left(\frac{ p _{1}}{ RT _{1}}\right) AM _{1} \sqrt{\gamma RT _{1}}=\left[\frac{\left(\frac{ p _{1}}{ p _{ o1 }}\right) p _{ o 1}}{ R \left(\frac{ T _{1}}{ T _{ o 1}}\right) T _{ o1 }}\right]\left(\frac{\pi}{4} D ^{2}\right) M _{1} \sqrt{\gamma R \left(\frac{ T _{1}}{ T _{ o1 }}\right) T _{ o 1}}\)

 

\(\dot{ m }=0.5 kg / s =\left[\frac{\left(\frac{ p _{1}}{ p _{ o 1}}\right) 100}{0.287\left(\frac{ T _{1}}{ T _{ o1 }}\right) 308}\right]\left(\frac{\pi}{4} 0.1^{2}\right) M _{1} \sqrt{(1.4)(287)\left(\frac{ T _{1}}{ T _{ o1 }}\right)(308)}\)

 

\(=3.1256 \frac{\left(\frac{p_{1}}{p_{ o1 }}\right) M _{1}}{\sqrt{\frac{ T _{1}}{ T _{ o1 }}}}=3.1256\frac{ M _{1} \sqrt{\left[1+\left(\frac{\gamma-1}{2}\right) M _{1}^{2}\right]}}{\left[1+\left(\frac{\gamma-1}{2}\right) M _{1}^{2}\right]^{\frac{\gamma}{\gamma-1}}}\)

 

This is a nonlinear algebraic equation that can be solved by the Newton-Raphson method. The following is a table that presents the iterations to determine the two Mach numbers by this approach. The function that is to be solved to determine the Mach numbers and its derivative are

 

\(f ( M )=\frac{\gamma-1}{2} M ^{4}+ M ^{2}- c ^{2}\left(1+\frac{\gamma-1}{2} M ^{2}\right)^{\frac{2 \gamma}{\gamma-1}}=0\)

 

\(\frac{d f}{d M}(M)=2(\gamma-1) M^{3}+2 M-2 \gamma c^{2} M\left(1+\frac{\gamma-1}{2} M^{2}\right)^{\frac{\gamma+1}{\gamma-1}}\)

 

where c = 0.5/(3.1256) = 0.1600.

(a) So \(M _{1}=0.1625 \text { and } 2.8345\) are the two possible initial Mach numbers for the given conditions.

(b) At \(M _{1}=0.1625\), from the Rayleigh relation we find that \(T _{ o 1} / T _{ o }^{*}=0.1185\). Hence, \(T _{ o }{ }^{*}=308 / 0.1185=2599.1561 K\).

The heat transfer rate for choked flow is

 

\(\dot{ q }=\dot{ m } c _{ p }\left( T _{ o }^{*}- T _{ o1}\right)=(0.5)(1.004)(2599.1561-308)=1150.1604 kW\)

 

At \(M _{1}=2.8345\), from the Rayleigh relation we find that \(T _{ o 1} / T _{ o }^{*}=0.6702 \text {. Hence, } T _{ o }^{*}=308/0.6702 = 459. 5643K\).

The heat transfer rate for choked flow is

 

\(\dot{ q }=\dot{\operatorname{mc}}_{ p }\left( T _{ o }^{*}- T _{ o1 }\right)=(0.5)(1.004)(459.5643-308)=76.0853 kW\)
