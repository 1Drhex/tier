Guest Question.

A sphere (weight \(W_{1}\) ) and a wedge (weight \(W_{2}\) ) are jammed between two vertical walls with rough surfaces (Fig. 9.11). The coefficient of static friction between the sphere and the left wall and between the wedge and the right wall, respectively, is \(\mu _{0}\) . The inclined surface O of the wedge is smooth.

Determine the required value of \(\mu _{0}\) in order to keep the system in equilibrium.

Admin
Solution

First we draw the free-body diagrams of the isolated

sphere and of the complete system. The force \(H_{r}\) of static friction is directed such that it opposes the direction of motion that would occur in the absence of friction. The force \(H_{l}\) is zero as can be seen from the equilibrium conditions at the sphere:

\(\overset{\curvearrowright }{C} : H_{l} =0\) ,

\(\uparrow : N \sin \alpha – W_{1} =0\) ,

\(\rightarrow : N_{l} -N \cos \alpha =0 \rightarrow N_{l}= W_{1}\cot \alpha\) .

The normal force \(N_{r}\) and the friction force \(H_{r}\) follow from equilibrium of the complete system:

\(\rightarrow : N_{l} -N_{r} =0 \rightarrow N_{r}=N_{l} =W_{1}\cot \alpha\) ,

\(\uparrow : H_{r} – W_{1} – W_{2} =0 \rightarrow H_{r} = W_{1} + W_{2}\) .

They have to satisfy the condition of static friction (the corresponding condition at the left hand side is automatically satisfied since \(H_{l} =0\) ):

\(H_{r}\leq \mu _{0} N_{r} \rightarrow \underline{\underline{\mu _{0} \geq (1+ W_{2}/W_{1} )\tan \alpha} }\) .
