Guest Question.

Determine the natural frequency \(\omega_{n}\) of vertical vibration of the 3-kg collar to which are attached the two uniform 1.2-kg links, which may be treated as slender bars. The stiffness of the spring, which is attached to both the collar and the foundation, is k = 1.5 kN/ m, and the bars are both horizontal in the equilibrium position. A small roller on end B of each link permits end A to move with the collar. Frictional retardation is negligible.

Admin
Solution

In the equilibrium position, the compression P in the spring equals the weight of the 3-kg collar, plus half the weight of each link or \(P=3(9.81)+2\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)(1.2)(9.81)=41.2\)N. The corresponding static deflection of the spring is \(\delta_{ st }=P / k=41.2 / 1.5\left(10^{3}\right)=27.5\left(10^{-3}\right)\) m. With the displacement variable y measured downward from the equilibrium position, which becomes the position of zero potential energy, the potential energy for each member in the displaced position is

 

\(\begin{aligned}\text { (Spring) } V_{e} &=\frac{1}{2} k\left(y+\delta_{ st }\right)^{2}-\frac{1}{2} k \delta_{ st }^{2}=\frac{1}{2} k y^{2}+k \delta_{ st } y \\&=\frac{1}{2}(1.5)\left(10^{3}\right) y^{2}+1.5\left(10^{3}\right)(27.5)\left(10^{-3}\right) y \\&=750 y^{2}+41.2 y J\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\text { (Collar) } \quad V_{g}=-m_{c} g y=-3(9.81) y=-29.4 y J\)

 

1 \(\text { (Each link) } \quad V_{g}=-m_{l} g \frac{y}{2}=-1.2(9.81) \frac{y}{2}=-5.89 y J\)

 

The total potential energy of the system then becomes

 

2 \(V=750 y^{2}+41.2 y-29.4 y-2(5.89) y=750 y^{2} J\)

 

The maximum kinetic energy occurs at the equilibrium position, where the velocity y˙ of the collar has its maximum value. In that position, in which links 3 AB are horizontal, end B is the instantaneous center of zero velocity for each link, and each link rotates with an angular velocity \(\dot{y} / 0.3\). Thus, the kinetic energy of each part is

 

\(\text { (Collar) } \quad T=\frac{1}{2} m_{c} \dot{y}^{2}=\frac{3}{2} \dot{y}^{2} J\)

 

\(\text { (Each link) } \quad T=\frac{1}{2} I_{B} \omega^{2}=\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{1}{3} m_{l} l^{2}\right)(\dot{y} / l)^{2}=\frac{1}{6} m_{l} \dot{y}^{2}\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{6}(1.2) \dot{y}^{2}=0.2 \dot{y}^{2}\)

 

Thus, the kinetic energy of the collar and both links is

 

\(T=\frac{3}{2} \dot{y}^{2}+2\left(0.2 \dot{y}^{2}\right)=1.9 \dot{y}^{2}\)

 

With the harmonic motion expressed by \(y=y_{\max } \sin \omega_{n} t, \text { we have } \dot{y}_{\max }=y_{\max } \omega_{n}\), 4 so that the energy balance \(T_{\max }=V_{\max } \text { with } \dot{y}=\dot{y}_{\max }\) becomes

 

5 \(1.9\left(y_{\max } \omega_{n}\right)^{2}=750 y_{\max }{ }^{2} \quad \text { or } \quad \omega_{n}=\sqrt{750 / 1.9}=19.87 Hz\)

 

Helpful Hints

1 Note that the mass center of each link moves down only half as far as the collar.

2 We note again that measurement of the motion variable y from the equilibrium position results in the total potential energy being simply \(V=\frac{1}{2} k y^{2}\).

3 Our knowledge of rigid-body kinematics is essential at this point.

4 To appreciate the advantage of the work-energy method for this and similar problems of interconnected systems, you are encouraged to explore the steps required for solution by the force and moment equations of motion of the separate parts.

5 If the oscillations were large, we would find that the angular velocity of each link in its general position would equal \(\dot{y} / \sqrt{0.09-y^{2}}\), which would cause a nonlinear response no longer described by \(y=y_{\max } \sin \omega t\).
