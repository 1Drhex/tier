Guest Question.

In an economic system of three industries, suppose that the external demands are, respectively, 10, 25, and 20. Suppose that \(a_{11}=0.2, a_{12}=0.5, a_{13}=0.15, a_{21}=0.4\), \(a_{22}=0.1, a_{23}=0.3, a_{31}=0.25, a_{32}=0.5\), and \(a_{33}=0.15\). Find the output in each industry such that supply exactly equals demand.

Admin
Solution

Here \(n=3,1-a_{11}=0.8,1-a_{22}=0.9\), and \(1-a_{33}=0.85\). Then system (9) is

\(\begin{array}{r}0.8 x_{1}-0.5 x_{2}-0.15 x_{3}=10 \\-0.4 x_{1}+0.9 x_{2}-0.3 x_{3}=25 \\-0.25 x_{1}-0.5 x_{2}+0.85 x_{3}=20\end{array}\)

Solving this system by using a calculator, we obtain successively (using five-decimalplace accuracy and Gauss-Jordan elimination)

\(\left(\begin{array}{ccc|c}0.8 & -0.5 & -0.15 & 10 \\-0.4 & 0.9 & -0.3 & 25 \\-0.25 & -0.5 & 0.85 & 20\end{array}\right) \stackrel{M_{1}\left(\frac{1}{0.8}\right)}{\longrightarrow}\left(\begin{array}{ccc|c}1 & -0.625 & -0.1875 & 12.5 \\-0.4 & 0.9 & -0.3 & 25 \\-0.25 & -0.5 & 0.85 & 20\end{array}\right)\) \(\begin{array}{r}A_{1,2}(0.4) \\A_{1,3}(0.25) \\{\longrightarrow}\end{array}\left(\begin{array}{ccc|l}1 & -0.625 & -0.1875 & 12.5 \\0 & 0.65 & -0.375 & 30 \\0 & -0.65625 & 0.80313 & 23.125\end{array}\right)\) \(\stackrel{M_{2}\left(\frac{1}{0.65}\right)}{\longrightarrow}\left(\begin{array}{ccc|c}1 & -0.625 & -0.1875 & 12.5 \\0 & 1 & -0.57692 & 46.15385 \\0 & -0.65625 & 0.80313 & 23.125\end{array}\right)\) \(\begin{aligned}&\begin{array}{r}A_{2,1}(0.625) \\A_{2,3}(0.65625) \\{\longrightarrow}\end{array}\left(\begin{array}{ccc|c}1 & 0 & -0.54808 & 41.34616 \\0 & 1 & -0.57692 & 46.15385 \\0 & 0 & 0.42453 & 53.41346\end{array}\right) \\&\stackrel{M_{3}(1 / 0.42453)}{\longrightarrow}\left(\begin{array}{ccc|c}1 & 0 & -0.54808 & 41.34616 \\0 & 1 & -0.57692 & 46.15385 \\0 & 0 & 1 & 125.81787\end{array}\right) \\&\begin{array}{r}A_{3,1}(0.54808) \\A_{3,2}(0.57692) \\{\longrightarrow}\end{array}\left(\begin{array}{ccc|c}1 & 0 & 0 & 110.30442 \\0 & 1 & 0 & 118.74070 \\0 & 0 & 1 & 125.81787\end{array}\right)\end{aligned}\)

We conclude that the outputs needed for supply to equal demand are, approximately ,\(x1= 110, x2= 119\), and \(x3= 126\).
