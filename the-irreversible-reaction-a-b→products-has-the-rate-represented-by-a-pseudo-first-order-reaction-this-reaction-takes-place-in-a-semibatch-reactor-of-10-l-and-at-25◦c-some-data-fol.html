Guest Question.

The irreversible reaction A + B→products, has the rate represented by a pseudo first-order reaction. This reaction takes place in a semibatch reactor of 10 L and at 25◦C, some data follow:
\(t =0 → C_{A0} =3 × 10^{−4} mol/L\)
→ \(C_{Ai} =5 × 10^{−5} mol/L\)
→ \(\nu _{0} =2L/min\)
→ \((−r_{A})=0.38 mol/(L min)\)
Calculate the time required to reach the stabilized conversion.

Admin
Solution

By Equation 15.14 we calculate:
\(\tau _{0}=\frac{V}{\nu _{0}}=5 min\)

With \(k^{∗} =0.38\), we calculate:
\(\tau _{0}k^{*}=1.9\)

Substituting into Equation 15.14 :

Eq (15.14): \(\frac{C_{A}}{C_{A0}}=\frac{1}{(t+\tau _{0})k^{*}}-((\frac{1}{\tau _{0}k^{*}})-\frac{C_{Ai}}{C_{A0}} )\cdot \frac{\tau _{0}}{(t+\tau _{0})}\cdot e^{-k^{*}t} \)

\(\frac{C_{A}}{C_{A0}}=\frac{1}{0.38(t+5)}-(\frac{1}{1.9}-\frac{5\times 10^{-5}}{3\times 10^{-4}}). \frac{5}{(t+5)}e^{0.38t}\)

By the definition of conversion:
\(X_{A}=1-\frac{C_{A}}{C_{A0}}\)

We have:
\(X_{A}=1-\frac{2.63}{(t+5)}+\frac{1.8}{(t+5)}e^{-0.38t}\)

t (min) 0 0.5 1 1.5 3 5 10 20 25
\(X_{A}\) 0.474 0.792 0.766 0.751 0.743 0.764 0.827 0.895 0.912
