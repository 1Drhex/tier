Guest Question.

Consider any observable A associated with the state of the system in quantum mechanics. Show that there is an uncertainty relation of the form

\( \Delta E\left(\frac{\Delta A}{|d\left\langle A\right\rangle/d_{1}| } \right)\geq \frac{\hbar }{2}\)

provided the operator \(\hat{A}\) does not depend explicitly on time. The quantity \(\Delta A/|d\left\langle A\right\rangle/dt|\) is a time that we may call \(\Delta t\). What is the physical significance of \(\Delta t\)?

Admin
Solution

Recall that \(\left[\hat{A},\hat{B} \right]=i\hat{C}\) implies that \(\Delta A\Delta B\geq |\left\langle C\right\rangle|/2\). Start with the commutator \(\left[\hat{A},\hat{H} \right]\),then

\( \Delta A\Delta E\geq\frac{1}{2}|\left\langle \psi |\left[\hat{A},\hat{H} \right]|\psi \right\rangle|\)

But since

\( \frac{d\left\langle A\right\rangle }{dt}=\frac{i}{\hslash }\left\langle \psi |\left[\hat{H},\hat{A} \right]|\psi \right\rangle\)

then

\( \Delta A\Delta E\geq \frac{\hslash }{2}\left|\frac{d\left\langle A\right\rangle }{dt} \right|\)

or

\( \Delta E\left(\frac{\Delta A}{|d\left\langle A\right\rangle /dt|} \right)\geq \frac{\hslash }{2}\)

If we define

\( \Delta t=\frac{\Delta A }{|d\left\langle A\right\rangle/dt| }\)

then

\( \Delta A\Delta t\geq \frac{\hslash }{2}\)

The time \(\Delta t\) is the time necessary for the expectation value to change by an amount on the order of the uncertainty. Thus it is the time you need to wait to be sure that the results of measuring A have really changed. For example, forposition,if \(\Delta x=1\) cm and \(d\left\langle x\right\rangle/dt=1\) mm /s,then \(\Delta x /|d\left\langle x\right\rangle/dt|=10s\) .which is the time necessary for \(\left\langle x\right\rangle\) to shift by an amount \(\Delta x\).
