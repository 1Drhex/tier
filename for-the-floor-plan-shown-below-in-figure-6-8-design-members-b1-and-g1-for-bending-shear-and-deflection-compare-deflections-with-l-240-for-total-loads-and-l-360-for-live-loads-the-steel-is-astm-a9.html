Guest Question.

For the floor plan shown below in Figure 6-8, design members B1 and G1 for bending, shear, and deflection. Compare deflections with L/240 for total loads and L/360 for live loads. The steel is ASTM A992, grade 50; assume that \(C_{b} = 1.0\) for bending. The dead load (including the beam weight) is assumed to be 85 psf and the live load is 150 psf. Assume that the floor deck provides full lateral stability to the top flange of B1. Ignore live load reduction. Use the design tables in the AISCM where appropriate.

Admin
Solution

Since the dead load is more than half of the live load, the total load deflection of L/240 will control.

Summary of Loads (see Figure 6-9):

\(p_{s}=85+150=235 \mathrm{psf}=0.235 \mathrm{ksf}\) (total service load)

\(p_{u}=(1.2)(85)+(1.6)(150)=342 \mathrm{psf}=0.342 \mathrm{ksf}\) (total factored load)

B1 G1

\( \begin{array}{ll}\text { Tributary width }=6^{\prime}-8^{\prime \prime} & \text { Tributary area }=\left(6^{\prime}-8^{\prime \prime}\right)\left(30^{\prime}\right)=200 \mathrm{ft} .^{2} \\ w_{s}=(6.67)(0.235)=1.57 \mathrm{kips} / \mathrm{ft.} & P_{s}=(200)(0.235)=47 \mathrm{kips} \\ w_{u}=(6.67)(0.342)=2.28 \mathrm{kips} / \mathrm{ft.} & P_{u}=(200)(0.342)=68.4 \mathrm{kips} \\ V_{u}=\frac{w_{u} L}{2}=\frac{(2.28)(30)}{2}=34.2 \mathrm{kips} & V_{u}=P_{u}=68.4 \mathrm{kips} \\ M_{u}=\frac{w_{u} L^{2}}{8}=\frac{(2.28)(30)^{2}}{8}=257 \mathrm{ft} .\mathrm{-kips} & M_{u}=\frac{P_{u} L}{3}=\frac{(68.4)(20)}{3}=456 \mathrm{ft} .\mathrm{-kips}\end{array}\)

The loading diagrams for B-1 and G-1 are shown in Figure 6-9.

  1. Design of Beam B1

    We will use the beam charts and the required moment of inertia method to select a beam size.
    \(M_{u} = 257 ft.-kips\) and \(L_{b} = 0\)From AISCM, Table 3-10, a W16 × 40 is selected as the most economical size for bending, with \(\phi_{b}M_{n} = 274\) ft.-kips However, note that a W18 × 40 has the same beam weight and, therefore, virtually the same cost, but provides more strength (\(\phi_{b}M_{n} = 294\) ft.-kips) and more stiffness (\(I = 612 in.^4\) versus \(I = 518 in.^4\)). Therefore, a W18 × 40 is initially selected.

    From Table 6-5 (total load controls deflection),

    L/360 L/240 Deflection Loading
    \(I_{reqd} =\frac{ML}{5.37} \) \(I_{reqd} =\frac{ML}{8.056} \) \(\Delta =\frac{ML^2}{(161.1)I} \) Variable moment\(^{2}\)
    \(I_{reqd} =\frac{PL^2}{26.85} \) \(I_{reqd} =\frac{PL^2}{40.28} \) \(\Delta =\frac{PL^3}{806I} \) Concentrated load at midspan
    \(I_{reqd} =\frac{wL^3}{42.96} \) \(I_{reqd} =\frac{wL^3}{64.44} \) \(\Delta =\frac{wL^4}{1289I} \) Uniformly loaded
    \(I_{\text {required }}=\frac{w L^{3}}{64.44}=\frac{(1.57)(30)^{3}}{64.44}=658 \mathrm{in} .^{4}\)

    The required moment of inertia is greater than the moment of inerita of the W18 × 40, which is \(612 in.^4\); therefore, a new size needs to be selected.

    \(\mathrm{W} 16 \times 50, I=659 \mathrm{in}^{4} \\ \mathrm{~W} 18 \times 46, I=712 \mathrm{in}^{4} \\ \bold{\boldsymbol{~W} 21 \times \mathbf{4 4}, \boldsymbol{I}=\mathbf{8 4 3} \boldsymbol{in}^{4} \leftarrow \text{Select}} \\ \mathrm{W} 24 \times 55, I=1350 \mathrm{in.}^{4}\)

    The W21 × 44 is the lightest, so this beam is selected. Alternatively, from AISCM, Table 3-3, a W21 × 44
    is found to be the lightest section with a moment of inertia greater than \(658 in.^4\) Checking the moment capacity with AISCM, Table 3-10, we find that \(\phi_{b} M_{n}=358\)ft.-kips > \(M_{u}=257\) ft.-kips.
    Checking shear, note that a W21 × 44 does not have a slender web; therefore, the design shear strength is determined from equation (6-19), with \(C_{v} = 1.0\) and \(\phi_{v}=1.0\):

    \(\phi_{v} V_{n}=\phi_{v} 0.6 F_{y} A_{w} C_{v}\) (6-19)
    \(=(1.0)(0.6)(50)(0.35)(20.7)(1.0)=217 \text { kips }>V_{u}=34.2 \text { kips OK }\)
    Alternatively, the shear strength can be found from AISCM, Table 3-6 (\(\phi V_{n}=217\) kips, same as above).
    A W21 × 44 is selected for member B1.

  2. Design of Girder G1 

    We will use the beam charts and the required moment of inertia method to select a beam size.\(M_{u}=456 \mathrm{ft}\).-kips and \(L_{b}=6.67 \mathrm{ft}\)

    As an alternate approximate check, use equation (6-24) to determine the deflection:
    \(\Delta =\frac{ML^2}{(161.1)I} \) (6-24)

    \(\begin{aligned}&M_{s}=\frac{P_{s} L}{3}=\frac{(47)(20)}{3}=313.3 \mathrm{ft} . \text { -kips } \\&\Delta=\frac{M L^{2}}{(161.1) I}=\frac{(313.3)(20)^{2}}{(161.1)(1350)}=0.589 \mathrm{in} .\end{aligned}\)

    Recall that equation (6-24) was developed for the uniform load case, but the results for the concentrated loads at 1⁄3 points were close to the actual value of Δ = 0.589 in. (less than a 3% difference). Therefore, equations (6-25) and (6-26) could have reasonably been used for a quick size selection based on stiffness.
    \(I_{reqd} =\frac{ML}{8.056} \) (required moment of inertia for L=240) (6.25)
    \(I_{reqd} =\frac{ML}{5.37} \)(required moment of inertia for L=360) (6.26)

    Check Shear:

    Note that from Section 6-5, the design shear strength is determined from equation (6-19), with \(C_v = 1.0\) and \(\phi_{v} = 0.9\)

    \(\phi_{v} V_{n}=\phi_{v} 0.6 F_{y} A_{w} C_{v}=(0.9)(0.6)(50)(0.395)(23.6)(1.0)=251 \text { kips }>V_{u}=68.4 \text { kips OK }\)
    Alternatively, the shear strength can be found from AISCM, Table 3-6 (\(\phi V_n = 251\) kips, same as above).
    A W24 × 55 is selected for member G1.
