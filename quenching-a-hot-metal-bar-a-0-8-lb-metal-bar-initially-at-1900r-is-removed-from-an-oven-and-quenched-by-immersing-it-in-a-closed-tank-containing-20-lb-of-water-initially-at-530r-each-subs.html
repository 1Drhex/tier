Guest Question.

Quenching a Hot Metal Bar

A 0.8-lb metal bar initially at 1900°R is removed from an oven and quenched by immersing it in a closed tank containing 20 lb of water initially at 530°R. Each substance can be modeled as incompressible. An appropriate constant specific heat value for the water is \(c_{ w }=1.0 Btu / lb \cdot{ }^{\circ} R\), and an appropriate value for the metal is \(c_{ m }=0.1 Btu / lb \cdot{ }^{\circ} R\). Heat transfer from the tank contents can be neglected. Determine (a) the final equilibrium temperature of the metal bar and the water, in °R, and (b) the amount of entropy produced, in Btu/°R.

Admin
Solution

Known A hot metal bar is quenched by immersing it in a closed tank containing water.

Find Determine the final equilibrium temperature of the metal bar and the water and the amount of entropy produced.

Schematic and Given Data:

Engineering Model

1. The metal bar and the water within the tank form a system, as shown on the accompanying sketch.

2. There is no energy transfer by heat or work: The system is isolated.

3. There is no change in kinetic or potential energy.

4. The water and metal bar are each modeled as incompressible with known specific heats.

Analysis

a. The final equilibrium temperature can be evaluated from an energy balance for the isolated system

 

\(\Delta KE ^{\nearrow0}+\Delta PE ^{\nearrow0}+\Delta U=Q^{\nearrow0}-W^{\nearrow0}\)

 

where the indicated terms vanish by assumptions 2 and 3. Since internal energy is an extensive property, its value for the isolated system is the sum of the values for the water and metal, respectively. Thus, the energy balance becomes

 

\(\left.\Delta U]_{\text {water }}+\Delta U\right]_{\text {metal }}=0\)

 

Using Eq. 3.20a to evaluate the internal energy changes of the water and metal in terms of the constant specific heats,

 

\(u_{2}-u_{1}=c\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right)\) (3.20a)

 

\(m_{ w } c_{ W }\left(T_{ f }-T_{ wi }\right)+m_{ m } c_{ m }\left(T_{ f }-T_{ mi }\right)=0\)

 

where \(T_{ f }\) is the final equilibrium temperature, and \(T_{ wi } \text { and } T_{ mi }\) are the initial temperatures of the water and metal, respectively. Solving for \(T_{ f }\) and inserting values

 

\(T_{ f }=\frac{m_{ w }\left(c_{ W } / c_{ m }\right) T_{ wi }+m_{ m } T_{ mi }}{m_{ w }\left(c_{ w } / c_{ m }\right)+m_{ m }}\)

 

\(=\frac{(20 lb )(10)\left(530^{\circ} R \right)+(0.8 lb )\left(1900^{\circ} R \right)}{(20 lb )(10)+(0.8 lb )}\)

 

\(=535^{\circ} R\)

 

b. The amount of entropy production can be evaluated from an entropy balance. Since no heat transfer occurs between the isolated system and its surroundings, there is no accompanying

 

entropy transfer, and an entropy balance for the isolated system reduces to

 

\(\Delta S=\int_{1}^{2}\left(\frac{\delta Q}{T}\right)_{ b }^{\nearrow0}+\sigma\)

 

Entropy is an extensive property, so its value for the isolated system is the sum of its values for the water and the metal, respectively, and the entropy balance becomes

 

\(\left.\Delta S]_{\text {water }}+\Delta S\right]_{\text {metal }}=\sigma\)

 

Evaluating the entropy changes using Eq. 6.13 for incompressible substances, the foregoing equation can be written as

 

\(s_{2}-s_{1}=c \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}\) (incompressible, constant c) (6.13)

 

\(\sigma=m_{ w } c_{ w } \ln \frac{T_{ f }}{T_{ wi }}+m_{ m } c_{ m } \ln \frac{T_{ f }}{T_{ mi }}\)

 

Inserting values

 

\(\sigma=(20 lb )\left(1.0 \frac{ Btu }{ lb \cdot{ }^{\circ} R }\right) \ln \frac{535}{530}+(0.8 lb )\left(0.1 \frac{ Btu }{ lb \cdot{ }^{\circ} R }\right) \ln \frac{535}{1900}\)

 

1 2 \(=\left(0.1878 \frac{ Btu }{{ }^{\circ} R }\right)+\left(-0.1014 \frac{ Btu }{{ }^{\circ} R }\right)=0.0864 \frac{ Btu }{{ }^{\circ} R }\)

 

1 The metal bar experiences a decrease in entropy. The entropy of the water increases. In accord with the increase of entropy principle, the entropy of the isolated system increases.

2 The value of σ is sensitive to roundoff in the value of \(T_{ f }\).

Quick Quiz

If the mass of the metal bar were 0.45 lb, determine the final equilibrium temperature, in °R, and the amount of entropy produced, in Btu/°R, keeping all other given data the same. Ans. 533°R, 0.0557 Btu/°R.
