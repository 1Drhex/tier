Guest Question.

Consider a double compound pendulum attached to a pin at point O as depicted in Figure 5.12. Using the variables defined in the figure, use Newton’s second law to derive the two governing equations of motion.

Admin
Solution

The links are subjected to two torques, \(T_{1}\) and \(T_{2}\), as shown. Angular displacement \(θ_{2}\) is measured from the position of link one. We use kinematics to determine the accelerations of the center of mass of each link. Then we apply Newton’s second law of motion to obtain the equations of motion for each link. The accelerations derived above are substituted into these equations and then simplified algebraically.
Kinematics: The position vector to the center of mass of link one is

\(r _{C_{1} / O}=r_{1} \cos \theta_{1} i +r_{1} \sin \theta_{1} j\)

The second derivative of this expression is the acceleration of the center of mass,

\(\begin{aligned}a _{C_{1} / o}=&-r_{1} \dot{\theta}_{1}^{2} \cos \theta_{1} i -r_{1} \ddot{\theta}_{1} \sin \theta_{1} i \\&-r_{1} \dot{\theta}_{1}^{2} \operatorname{ain} \theta_{1} j +r_{1} \ddot{\theta}_{1} \sin \theta_{1} j\end{aligned}\)

The position vector to the center of mass of link two is

\(\begin{aligned}r _{C_{2} / o} &=\left[r_{2} \cos \left(\theta_{1}+\theta_{2}\right)+l_{1} \cos \theta_{1}\right] i \\&+\left[r_{2} \sin \left(\theta_{1}+\theta_{2}\right)+l_{1} \sin \theta_{1}\right] j\end{aligned}\)

and the acceleration is given by

\(\begin{aligned}a _{C_{2} / o} & \\=&\left[-r_{2}\left(\dot{\theta}_{1}+\dot{\theta}_{2}\right)^{2} \cos \left(\theta_{1}+\theta_{2}\right)-r_{2}\left(\ddot{\theta}_{1}+\ddot{\theta}_{2}\right) \operatorname{ain}\left(\theta_{1}+\theta_{2}\right)\right.\\&\left.-l_{1} \dot{\theta}_{1}^{2} \cos \theta_{1}-l_{1} \ddot{\theta}_{1} \sin \theta_{1}\right] i \\&+\left[r_{2}\left(\dot{\theta}_{1}+\dot{\theta}_{2}\right)^{2} \sin \left(\theta_{1}+\theta_{2}\right)+r_{2}\left(\ddot{\theta}_{1}+\ddot{\theta}_{2}\right) \cos \left(\theta_{1}+\theta_{2}\right)\right.\\&\left.-l_{1} \dot{\theta}_{1}^{2} \sin \theta_{1}+l_{1} \ddot{\theta}_{1} \cos \theta_{1}\right] j \\\equiv & a _{C_{2 n}} i + a _{C_{2}} j\end{aligned}\)

Kinetics: We apply Newton’s second law of motion to each free-body diagram in the\(x\), \(y\), and θ directions. For link one, the sum of forces and sum of moments are

\(\begin{aligned}\sum F_{z} &=a_{z}+E_{x} \\\sum F_{y} &=a_{y}+E_{y}+m_{1} g \\\sum M_{O} &=E_{y}\left(l_{1} \cos \theta_{1}\right)-E_{x}\left(l_{1} \sin \theta_{1}\right) \\&+T_{1}-T_{2}-m_{1} g r_{1} \cos \theta_{1},\end{aligned}\)

where \(E_{1}\) and \(E_{2}\) are the reaction forces between the two links at \(E .\) For link two, the sum of forces and sum of moments are

\(\begin{aligned}\sum F_{x} &=-E_{x} \\\sum F_{y} &=-E_{y}-m_{2} g \\\sum M_{C_{2}} &=E_{y}\left(r_{2} \cos \left(\theta_{1}+\theta_{2}\right)\right)-E_{x}\left(r_{2} \sin \left(\theta_{1}+\theta_{2}\right)\right)\end{aligned}\)

Substituting these forces and moments into Newton’s second law for link one we find

\(\begin{aligned}&\sum F_{x}=m_{1} a_{C_{2} x} \\&\sum F_{y}=m_{1} a_{C_{1} y} \\&\sum M_{O}=I_{O} \alpha_{1}\end{aligned}\)

where \(I_{O}=I_{1}+m_{1} r_{1}^{2}\) and \(\alpha_{1}=\ddot{\theta}_{1}\). Similarly, for link
two, we find

\(\begin{aligned}\sum F_{x} &=m_{2} a_{C_{1} w} \\\sum F_{y} &=m_{2} a_{C_{1} y} \\\sum M_{O} &=I_{2} \alpha_{2}\end{aligned}\)

where \(\alpha_{2}=\ddot{\theta}_{1}+\ddot{\theta}_{2}\)

since \(θ_{2}\) is measured from \(θ_{1}\). We substitute the forces and moments, respectively, and then substitute the expressions for the accelerations from the kinematics. After considerable algebra, we arrive at the two equations of motion,

\(\begin{gathered}{\left[I_{2}+m_{2} r_{2}^{2}+m_{2} r_{2} l_{1} \cos \theta_{2}\right] \ddot{\theta}_{1}+\left[I_{2}+m_{2} r_{2}^{2}\right] \ddot{\theta}_{2}} \\+\left[m_{2} r_{2} l_{1} \sin \theta_{2}\right] \dot{\theta}_{1}^{2}+m_{2} r_{2} g \cos \left(\theta_{1}+\theta_{2}\right)=T_{2}\end{gathered}\)

and

\(\begin{gathered}{\left[I_{1}+m_{1} r_{1}^{2}+m_{2}\left(l_{1}^{2}+r_{2}^{2}+2 l_{1} r_{2} \cos \theta_{2}\right)+I_{2}\right] \ddot{\theta}_{1}} \\+\left[I_{2}+m_{2} r_{2}\left(r_{2}+l_{1} \cos \theta_{2}\right)\right] \ddot{\theta}_{2}-\left[2 m_{2} r_{2} l_{1} \sin \theta_{2}\right] \dot{\theta}_{1} \dot{\theta}_{2}\end{gathered}\) \(\begin{gathered}-\left[m_{2} r_{2} l_{1} \operatorname{sin} \theta_{2}\right] \dot{\theta}_{2}^{2}+g\left[m_{1} r_{1} \cos \theta_{1}\right. \\\left.+m_{2}\left(l_{1} \cos \theta_{1}+r_{2} \cos \left(\theta_{1}+\theta_{2}\right)\right)\right]=T_{1}+T_{2}\end{gathered}\)

These two equations of motion are highly coupled and nonlinear.
