Guest Question.

Effect of axial forces on \(V_c\). A beam with dimensions b = 12 in., d = 24 in., and h = 27 in., with \(f_c^{\prime}\) = 4000 psi, carries a single concentrated factored load of 100 kips at midspan. Find the maximum shear strength of the concrete \(V_c\) at the first critical section for shear at a distance d from the support (a) if no axial forces are present, (b) if axial compression of 60 kips acts, and (c) if axial tension of 60 kips acts. In each case, compute \(V_c\) by both the more complex and simplified expressions of the ACI Code. Neglect the self-weight of the beam. At the section considered, tensile reinforcement consists of three No. 10 (No. 32) bars with a total area of 3.81 \(in^2\).

Admin
Solution

At the critical section, \(V_u\) = 50 kips and \(M_u = 50 \times 2 = 100\) ft-kips, while \(ρ = 3.81/(12 \times 24) = 0.013\).
(a) If \(N_u\) = 0, Eq. (5.12a) predicts

\(V_c=\left(1.9\lambda \sqrt{f_c^{\prime}}+2500\frac{\rho _w V_u d}{M_u} \right)b_w d \)

 

\( =\left(1.9\times1.0\sqrt{4000}+2500\frac{0.013\times50\times24}{100\times12} \right)12\times\frac{24}{1000}=44.0 kips \)

not to exceed the values calculated using Eq. (5.12b) and (5.12c), which are, respectively,

\(V_c=\left(1.9\lambda \sqrt{f_c^{\prime}}+2500\rho _w \right)b_w d=\left(1.9\times1.0\sqrt{4000}+2500\times0.013 \right)12\times\frac{24}{1000}=44.0 kips \)

and

\(V_c=3.5\lambda \sqrt{f_c^{\prime}}b_w d=3.5\times1.0\sqrt{4000}\times12\times\frac{24}{1000}=63.8 kips \)

If the simplified Eq. (5.12d) is used,

\(V_c=2\lambda \sqrt{f_c^{\prime}}b_w d=2\times1.0\sqrt{4000}\times12\times\frac{24}{1000}=36.4 kips\)

which is about 17 percent below the more exact value of Eq. (5.12a).

(b) With a compression of 60 kips introduced, the modified moment is found from Eq. (5.16) to be

\(M_m=M_u-N_u\frac{4h-d}{8}=100-60\frac{4\times27-24}{8\times12}=47.5 ft-kips \)

After introduction of that value into Eq. (5.12a) in place of \(M_u\), the concrete shear strength is

\(V_c=\left(1.9\times1.0\sqrt{4000}+2500\frac{0.013\times50\times2}{47.5} \right)12\times\frac{24}{1000}=54.3 kips\)

and, according to Eq. (5.17), should not exceed

\(V_c=3.5\lambda \sqrt{f_c^{\prime}}b_w d\sqrt{1+\frac{N_u}{500A_g} }=63.8\sqrt{1+\frac{60,000}{500\times12\times27} }=74.6 kips \)

If the simplified Eq. (5.18) is used,

\(V_c=2\left(1+\frac{N_u}{2000A_g} \right) \lambda \sqrt{f_c^{\prime}}b_w d=2\left(1+\frac{60,000}{2000\times12\times27} \right)\times1.0\sqrt{4000}\times12\times\frac{24}{1000}=39.8 kips \)

Comparing the results of the more exact calculation for (a) and (b), one sees that the introduction of an axial compressive stress of \(60,000/(12 \times 27) = 185 psi\) increases the concrete shear \(V_c\) by about 25 percent.
(c) With an axial tension of 60 kips acting, the reduced \(V_c\) is found from Eq. (5.19) to be

\(V_c=2\left(1+\frac{N_u}{500A_g} \right) \lambda \sqrt{f_c^{\prime}}b_w d=2\left(1+\frac{-60,000}{500\times12\times27} \right)\times1.0\sqrt{4000}\times12\times\frac{24}{1000}=22.9 kips \)

a reduction of almost 50 percent from the value for \(N_u = 0\). The alternative of using Eq. (5.19) for this case, according to the ACI Commentary, would be to set \(V_c = 0\). In all cases above, the strength reduction factor ϕ = 0.75 would be applied to \(V_c\) to obtain the design strength.
