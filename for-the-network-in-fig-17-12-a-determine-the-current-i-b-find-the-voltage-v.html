Guest Question.

For the network in Fig. 17.12:
a. Determine the current I.
b. Find the voltage V.

Admin
Solution

a. The rules for parallel current sources are the same for dc and ac networks. That is, the equivalent current source is their sum or difference (as phasors). Therefore,

\(I_T=6 mA ∠20° – 4 mA ∠0° (in \; the\; direction\; of\; I_1)\\[0.5cm] = 5.638 mA + j 2.052 mA – 4 mA\\[0.5cm] = 1.638 mA + j 2.052 mA\\[0.5cm] = 2.626 mA ∠51.402°\)

Redrawing the network using block impedances results in the network in Fig. 17.13, where

\(Z_1 = 2 kΩ ∠0° 6.8 kΩ ∠0° = 1.545 kΩ ∠0°\\[0.5cm] and\qquad Z_2 = 10 kΩ – j 20 kΩ = 22.361 kΩ ∠-63.435°\)

Note that I and V are still defined in Fig. 17.13.

Using the current divider rule gives

\(I=\frac{Z_1I_T}{Z_1+Z_2}=\frac{(1.545 kΩ ∠0°)(2.626 mA ∠51.402°)}{1.545 kΩ + 10 kΩ – j 20 kΩ}\\[0.5cm] =\frac{4.057 A ∠51.402°}{11.545 \times10^3+j 20 \times10^3}=\frac{4.057 A ∠51.402°}{23.093 \times10^3∠-60.004°}=0.18mA\angle 111.41° \)

 

b. \(V = IZ_2\\[0.5cm] = (0.176 mA ∠111.406°)(22.36 kΩ ∠-63.435°)\\[0.5cm] =3.94V\angle 47.97°\)
