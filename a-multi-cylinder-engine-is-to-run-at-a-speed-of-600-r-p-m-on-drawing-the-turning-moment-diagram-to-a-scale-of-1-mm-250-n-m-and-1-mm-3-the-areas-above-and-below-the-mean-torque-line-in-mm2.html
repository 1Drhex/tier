Guest Question.

A multi-cylinder engine is to run at a speed of 600 r.p.m. On drawing the turning moment diagram to a scale of 1 mm = 250 N-m and 1 mm = \(3^{\circ}\), the areas above and below the mean torque line in \(m m^{2}\) are : + 160, – 172, + 168, – 191, + 197, – 162
The speed is to be kept within \(\pm 1 \%\) of the mean speed of the engine. Calculate the necessary moment of inertia of the flywheel. Determine the suitable dimensions of a rectangular flywheel rim if the breadth is twice its thickness. The density of the cast iron is \( 7250 kg / m ^{3}\) and its hoop stress is 6 MPa. Assume that the rim contributes 92% of the flywheel effect.

Admin
Solution

Given : N = 600 r.p.m. or \(\omega=2 \pi \times 600 / 60=62.84 rad / s ; \rho=7250 kg / m ^{3}\); \(\sigma=6 MPa =6 \times 10^{6} N / m ^{2}\)

Since the fluctuation of speed is \(\pm 1 \%\) of mean speed, therefore, total fluctuation of speed,

\(\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}=2 \% \omega=0.02 \omega\)

and coefficient of fluctuation of speed,

\(C_{ S }=\frac{\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}}{\omega}=0.02\)

 

Moment of inertia of the flywheel

Let I = Moment of inertia of the flywheel in \(kg – m ^{2}\).

The turning moment diagram is shown in Fig. 16.19. The turning moment scale is 1 mm = 250 N-m and crank angle scale is 1 mm = \(3^{\circ}=\pi / 60 rad\), therefore,

\(1 mm ^{2}\) of turning moment diagram

\(=250 \times \pi / 60=13.1 N – m\)

Let the total energy at A = E. Therefore from Fig. 16.19, we find that

Energy at B = E + 160

Energy at C = E + 160 – 172 = E – 12

Energy at D = E – 12 + 168 = E + 156

Energy at E = E + 156 – 191 = E – 35 . . . (Minimum energy)

Energy at F = E – 35 + 197 = E + 162 . . . (Maximum energy)

Energy at G = E + 162 – 162 = E = Energy at A

We know that maximum fluctuation of energy,

\(\Delta E=\text { Maximum energy – Minimum energy }\)

 

\(=(E+162)-(E-35)=197 mm ^{2}\)

 

\(=197 \times 13.1=2581 N – m\)

 

We also know that maximum fluctuation of energy \((\Delta E)\),

\(2581=I . \omega^{2} \cdot C_{ S }=I \times(62.84)^{2} \times 0.02=79 I\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(I=2581 / 79=32.7 kg – m ^{2}\)

 

Dimensions of the flywheel rim

Let t = Thickness of the flywheel rim in metres,

b = Breadth of the flywheel rim in metres = 2 t …(Given)

D = Mean diameter of the flywheel in metres, and

v = Peripheral velocity of the flywheel in m/s.

We know that hoop stress \((\sigma )\),

\(6 \times 10^{6}=\rho \cdot v^{2}=7250 v^{2} \quad \text { or } \quad v^{2}=6 \times 10^{6} / 7250=827.6\)

 

\(\therefore \quad v=28.8 m / s\)

 

We know that \(v=\pi D N / 60, \quad \text { or } \quad D=v \times 60 / \pi N=28.8 \times 60 / \pi \times 600=0.92 m\)

Now, let us find the mass (m) of the flywheel rim. Since the rim contributes 92% of the flywheel effect, therefore maximum fluctuation of energy of rim,

\(\Delta E_{r i m}=0.92 \times \Delta E=0.92 \times 2581=2375 N – m\)

We know that maximum fluctuation of energy of rim \(\left(\Delta E_{r i m}\right)\),

\(2375=m \cdot v^{2} \cdot C_{ S }=m \times(28.8)^{2} \times 0.02=16.6 m\)

 

\(\therefore \) m = 2375/16.6 = 143 kg

 

Also \(m=\text { Volume } \times \text { density }=\pi D . A . \rho=\pi D . \text { b.t. } \rho\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(143=\pi \times 0.92 \times 2 t \times t \times 7250=41914 t^{2}\)

 

\(t^{2}=143 / 41914=0.0034 m ^{2}\)

 

or t = 0.0584 m = 58.4 mm

 

and b = 2 t = 116.8 mm
