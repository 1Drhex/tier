Guest Question.

A single-stage gas turbine operates at its design condition with an axial absolute flow at entry and exit from the stage. The absolute flow angle at nozzle exit is 70°. At stage entry, the total pressure and temperature are 311 kPa and 850°C, respectively. The exhaust static pressure is 100 kPa, the total-to-static efficiency is 0.87, and the mean blade speed is 500 m/s. Assuming constant axial velocity through the stage, determine
1. the specific work done;
2. the Mach number leaving the nozzle;
3. the axial velocity;
4. the total-to-total efficiency;
5. the stage reaction.
Take \(C_{p}=1.148 kJ/(kg °C) \) and \(\gamma =1.33 \) for the gas.

Admin
Solution

1. From Eq. (4.19a), total-to-static efficiency is

 

\( \eta _{ts} =\frac{h_{01}-h_{03}}{h_{01}-h_{3ss}} =\frac {h_{01}-h_{03}}{h_{01}-h_{03}+\left(h_{03}-h_{3}\right) +h_{3}-h_{3ss}} \cong \left[1+\frac{0.5c^{2}_{3} +T_{3} \left(s_{3}-s_{3ss} \right)}{h_{01}-h_{03}} \right] ^{-1} \) (4.19a)

\( \Rightarrow \eta _{ts} \cong \left[1+\frac{\zeta _{N} c^{2}_{2} T_{3} /T_{2} +w^{2}_{3}\zeta _{R} +c^{2}_{3} }{2\left(h_{01}-h_{03}\right) } \right] ^{-1}\)

 

\( \eta _{ts} =\frac{h_{01}-h_{03}}{h_{01}-h_{3ss}} =\frac{\Delta W}{h_{01}\left[1-\left(p_{3}/p_{01} \right) ^{\left(\gamma -1\right)/\gamma } \right]} \)

 

Thus, the specific work is

\(\Delta W = \eta _{ts} C_{p}T_{01} \left[1-\left(p_{3}/p_{01} \right) ^{\left(\gamma -1\right)/\gamma } \right] \)

\(=0.87\times 1148\times 1123\times \left[1-\left(1/3.11\right) ^{0.248}\right]=276 kJ/kg\)

 

2. At nozzle exit, the Mach number is

\(M_{2} =c_{2} /\left(\gamma RT_{2} \right) ^{1/2} \)

and it is necessary to solve the velocity diagram to find c2 and, hence, to determine \(T_{2} \) . As

\( c_{\theta 3} =0 , \Delta W=Uc_{\theta 2} \)

 

\( c_{\theta 2} =\frac{ \Delta W}{U} =\frac{276\times 10^{3} }{500} =552 m/s \)

 

\( c_{2} =\frac{c_{\theta 2} }{\sin \alpha _{2} } = 588 m/s \)

Referring to Figure 4.1, across the nozzle \( h_{01}=h_{02}=h_{2}+\left(1/2\right) c^{2}_{2} \) , thus,

\( T_{2}=T_{01}-\frac{1}{2} c^{2}_{2} /C_{p}= 973 K\)

Hence,\(M_{2} =0.97 \) with \(\gamma R=\left(\gamma -1\right) C_{p} \)

 

3. The axial velocity, \(c_{x} = c_{2}\cos \alpha _{2}=200 m/s \)

 

4. \( \eta _{u} =\frac{\Delta W}{\left(h_{01}+h_{3ss}-\left(1/2\right)c^{2}_{3} \right) } \)

 

After some rearrangement,

\( \frac{1}{ \eta _{u}} =\frac{1}{\eta _{ts} } -\frac{c^{2}_{3}}{2\Delta W} =\frac{1}{0.87} -\frac{200^{2} }{2\times 276\times 10^{3} } =1.0775 \)

Therefore, \( \eta _{tt}=0.93 \)

 

5. Using Eq. (4.13a), the reaction is

\( R=1-\frac{\phi }{2}\left(\tan \alpha _{2} -\tan \alpha _{1}\right) \) (4.13a)

 

\(\therefore R=1-\frac{\left(200/500\right)}{2}\tan 70°=0.451 \)
