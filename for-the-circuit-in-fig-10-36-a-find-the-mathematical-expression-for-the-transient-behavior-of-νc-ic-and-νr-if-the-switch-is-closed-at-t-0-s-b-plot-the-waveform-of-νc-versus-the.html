Guest Question.

For the circuit in Fig. 10.36:
a. Find the mathematical expression for the transient behavior of \(ν_C, i_C, and ν_R\) if the switch is closed at t = 0 s.

b. Plot the waveform of \(ν_C\) versus the time constant of the network.

c. Plot the waveform of \(ν_C\) versus time.

d. Plot the waveforms of \(i_C and ν_R\) versus the time constant of the network.

e. What is the value of \(ν_C\) at t = 20 ms?

f. On a practical basis, how much time must pass before we can assume that the charging phase has passed?

g. When the charging phase has passed, how much charge is sitting on the plates?

h. If the capacitor has a leakage resistance of 10,000 MΩ, what is the initial leakage current? Once the capacitor is separated from the circuit, how long will it take to totally discharge, assuming a linear (unchanging) discharge rate?

Admin
Solution

a. The time constant of the network is

\(\tau = RC = (8 kΩ)(4 μF) = 32 ms\)

resulting in the following mathematical equations:

\(\nu _C=E(1-e^{-{t}/{\tau }})=40V(1-e^{{-t}/{32ms}})\\[0.5cm] i_C=\frac{E}{R}e^{-{t}/{\tau }}=\frac{40V}{8 kΩ} e^{-{t}/{32ms }}=5mAe^{-{t}/{32ms }}\\[0.5cm] \nu_R=Ee^{-{t}/{\tau }}=40Ve^{-{t}/{32ms}}\)

 

b. The resulting plot appears in Fig. 10.37.

c. The horizontal scale will now be against time rather than time constants, as shown in Fig. 10.38. The plot points in Fig. 10.38 were taken from Fig. 10.37.

d. Both plots appear in Fig. 10.39.

e. Substituting the time t = 20 ms results in the following for the exponential part of the equation:

\(e^{{t}/{\tau }}=e^{-20ms/32ms}=e^{-0.625}=0.535\) (using a calculator)

so that \(\nu _C=40V(1-e^{\tau /32ms})=40V(1-0.535)=(40 V)(0.465) = 18.6 V\) (as verified by Fig. 10.38)

f. Assuming a full charge in five time constants results in

\(5\tau = 5(32 ms) = 160 ms = 0.16 s\)

g. Using Eq. (10.6) gives

Q = CV (coulombs, C) (10.6)

Q = CV = (4 μF)(40 V) = 160 μC

h. Using Ohm’s law gives

\(I_{leakage}=\frac{40V}{10,000 MΩ}=4 nA \)

Finally, the basic equation I = Q/t results in

\(t=\frac{Q}{I}=\frac{160\mu C}{4nA}=(40,000\cancel{s})(\frac{1\cancel{min}}{60\cancel{s}} )(\frac{1h}{60\cancel{min}} )=11.11h \)
