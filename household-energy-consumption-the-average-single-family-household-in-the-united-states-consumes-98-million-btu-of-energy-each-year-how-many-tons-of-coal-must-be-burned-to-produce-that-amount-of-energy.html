Solution

The mass of coal is

\(m=\frac{98\times 10^6Btu}{12.9\times 10^3Btu/lbm} \longleftarrow \left[Q = mH\right] \)

\(=7.597 \times 10^3(\cancel{Btu})\left(\frac{lbm}{\cancel{Btu}} \right) \)

\(= 7.597 \times 10^3 lbm\)

Since a 1-lbm object also weighs 1 lb, the weight is \(w=7.597\times 10^3lb\) By using the definition of the derived unit ton in the USCS,

\(w=(7.597\times 10^3lb)\left(\frac{1}{2000}\frac{ton}{lb} \right) \)

\(=3.798 (\cancel{lb})\left(\frac{ton}{\cancel{lb}} \right) \)

\(= 3.798 tons\)

Discussion
Compared to a sedan automobile that weighs 2500 lb, this amount of coal has the equivalent weight of three vehicles. Since coal is a nonrenewable source of energy, mechanical engineers must continue to develop other renewable sources of energy to meet the needs of households around the world.
