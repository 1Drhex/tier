Guest Question.

A peg A that can rotate without friction and a fixed peg B are attached to a curved member (weight W) as depicted in Fig. 9.16. The rope supports a load (weight \(W_{K} =W/5\)) .

Determine the number of coils of the rope around peg B that are needed to prevent slipping. Calculate the angle β in the equilibrium position.

Admin
Solution

We draw the free-body diagram of the complete system. The force equilibrium condition in the horizontal direction shows that the force S in the rope above peg A has to be vertical (\(S_{H}=0\)) . The force equilibrium condition in the vertical direction and the moment equilibrium condition about B yield

\(\uparrow : S_{A} – W_{K} -W =0 \rightarrow S_{A} =6W/5\) ,

\(\overset{\curvearrowleft }{B} : a S_{A} -bW =0 \rightarrow 6a=5b\) .

We obtain the angle β if we insert the geometrical relations

\(a=l\sin \beta , b= \frac{l}{2} \sin \beta + \frac{l}{4} \cos \beta\)

into the moment equilibrium condition:

\(\tan \beta =5/14 \rightarrow \underline{\underline{\beta =19.7^{\circ } } }\) .

Now we apply Euler’s formula for belt friction

\(S_{2} \leq S_{1 } e^{\mu _{0}\alpha }\)

where \( S_{1 } = W_{K}= W/5\) and \(S_{2}= S_{A} =6W/5\) . Solving yields the angle of wrap:

\(\alpha \geq \frac{\ln 6}{\mu _{0}} \rightarrow \alpha \geq 17.9 =2.85(2\pi )\).

Since \(360^{\circ } -\beta \triangleq 0.95(2\pi )\gt 0.85 (2\pi )\), three coils of the rope around peg B are needed to prevent slipping.
