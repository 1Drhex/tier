Guest Question.

A centrally loaded column footing (pad) of length L = 2.5 m, width B = 1.5 m and depth (thickness) d = 0.5 m is required to carry a vertical imposed permanent action \( V_{Gk} = 800 kN \) and a vertical imposed variable action \( V_{Qk} = 450 kN \). The footing base is horizontal (i.e. \( \alpha = 0 \) ) and located at a depth of 0.5 m below ground surface (i.e. D = 0.5 m). The weight density (unit weight) of the reinforced concrete is \( γ_{c, k} = 25 kN/m^{3} \) (see Table 6.12). The footing is founded on dry sand which has the following characteristic parameters:

• Angle of shearing resistance \( \varnothing _{k} = 35^{\circ} \)
• Effective cohesion \( {c}'_{k} = 0.0 kPa \)
• weight density \( y_{k} = 18 kK/m^{3} \)

It is required to perform verification of strength (GEO ultimate limit state), that is verification of bearing resistance, using all the three Design Approaches. Note: In order to concentrate on the EN 1997 rather than the geotechnical related issues a relatively simple problem has been selected which excludes the effects of groundwater.

Material Bulk density ( \( kN/m^{3}\) )

Wet Normal weight reinforced concrete 26.0

Solution

(A) Design Approach 1
DA1 – Combination 1: A1 “ + ”M1 “ + ”R1
– Combination 2: A2 + M2 + R1
Actions and effects:

Characteristic self-weight of footing \( W_{Gk} = y_{c, k} \times L \times B \times d \)

 

\( = 25 \times 2.5 \times 1.5 \times 0.5 = 46.9 kN \)

 

Area of base \( A_{b} = L \times B = 2.5 \times 1.5 = 3.75 m^{2} \)

 

Partial factors from Table 6.6 for \( \binom{Combination 1}{Combination 2} : \)

 

\( y_{G} = \binom{1.35}{1}; y_{Q} = \binom{1.5}{1.3} \)

 

Design vertical action: \( V_{d} = y_{G} \left ( W_{Gk} + V_{Gk} \right ) + y_{Q} V_{Qk} \)

 

\( = \binom{1.35 \left ( 46.9 + 800 \right ) + 1.5 \times 450}{1 \left ( 46.9 + 800 \right ) + 1.3 \times 450} = \binom{1818.3}{1431.9} kN\)

 

Design bearing pressure [Equation (6.8)]:

\( q_{Ed} = \frac{V_{d}}{A_{b}} = \binom{\frac{1818.3}{3.75}}{\frac{1431.9}{3.75}} = \binom{484.9}{381.8} kPa \)

 

Materials properties and resistance:

Partial factors from Table 6.7 for \(\binom{Combination 1}{Combination 2} \):

\( y_{\varnothing} = \binom{1}{1.25}; y_{c} = \binom{1}{1.25} \)

 

\( \varnothing_{d} = \tan^{-1} \left ( \frac{\tan \varnothing_{k}}{y_{\varnothing}} \right ) = \left [ \binom{\tan^{-1} \left ( \frac{\tan 35}{1} \right )}{\tan^{-1} \left ( \frac{\tan 35}{1.25} \right )} \right ] \)

 

Design angle of shearing resistance

\( = \binom{35}{29.3}^{\circ} \)

 

Design effective cohesion \( {c}'_{d} = \frac{{c}'_{k}}{y_{c}} = \binom{0}{0} kPa \)

 

Bearing capacity factors from Annex D of EN 1997-1:

For effect of overburden, \( N_{q} = e^{\pi \tan \varnothing_{d}} \tan^{2} \left ( 45^{\circ} + \frac{\varnothing_{d}}{2}\right ) = \binom{33.3}{17.0} \)

 

For effect of cohesion, \( N_{c} = \left ( N_{q} – 1 \right ) \cot \varnothing_{d} = \binom{46.1}{28.4} \)

 

For effect of self-weight, \( N_{y} = 2 \left ( N_{q} – 1 \right ) \tan \varnothing_{d} = \binom{45.2}{17.8} \)

 

Shape factors:

For effect of overburden, \( s_{q} = 1 + \frac{B}{L} \sin \varnothing_{d} = \binom{1.34}{1.29} \)

 

For effect of cohesion, \( s_{c} = \frac{\left ( s_{q} N_{q} – 1 \right )}{N_{q} – 1} = \binom{1.35}{1.31} \)

 

For effect of self-weight, \( s_{y} = 1 – 0.3 \frac{B}{L} = \binom{0.82}{0.82} \)

 

Depth factors:

The suggested method in Annex D does not include depth factors which are present in other formulations of the extended bearing capacity formula (see Section 4.5 of Chapter 4). There has been concern in using these depth factors as their influence can be significant and the reliance on the additional capacity provided by its inclusion is not conservative.
Load-inclination factors, caused by a horizontal load H:
Since H = 0, each of the load-inclination factors \( i_{c}, i_{q} \) and \( i_{y} \) is 1.0.
Base-inclination factors:
Since the foundation base is horizontal, that is \( \alpha = 0 \), each of the base-inclination factors \( b_{c}, b_{q} \) and \( b_{y} \) is 1.0.
Ground-inclination factors:
The suggested method in Annex D omits the ground-inclination factors which are present in other formulations of the extended bearing capacity formula. However, neglecting these factors is unsafe.
Effective overburden pressure \( {q}' \left ( or {\sigma}'_{vk,b} \right ): \)

\( {q}' = y_{k} \times D = 18 \times 0.5 = 9 kN/m^{2} \)

 

Gross ultimate bearing capacity ( \( Gross q_{ult} \) ) or gross bearing resistance \( \left ( R / {A}'\right ) \) :

Annex D of EN 1997-1 suggests that bearing resistance may be calculated from

 

\( R / {A}' = \underbrace{{c}' N_{c} b_{c} s_{c} i_{c}}_{Cohesion} + \underbrace{{q}' N_{q} b_{q} s_{q} i_{q}}_{Overburden} + \underbrace{0.5 {y}' {B}' N_{y} b_{y} s_{y} i_{y}}_{Self-weight} \)

 

• From cohesion: \( \left ( Gross q_{ult} \right )_{1} = \binom{0}{0} \binom{46.1}{28.4} \binom{1.35}{1.31} = \binom{0}{0} kPa \)

 

• From overburden: \( \left ( Gross q_{ult} \right )_{2} = \binom{9}{9} \binom{33.3}{17.0} \binom{1.34}{1.29} = \binom{402}{197} kPa \)

 

• From self-weight: \( \left ( Gross q_{ult} \right )_{3} = \binom{0.5}{0.5} \binom{18}{18} \binom{1.5}{1.5} \binom{45.2}{17.8} \binom{0.82}{0.82} = \binom{500}{197} kPa \)

 

\( Gross bearing resistance R/ {A}' = Gross q_{ult} = \sum_{i = 1}^{3} \left ( Gross q_{ult} \right )_{i} \)

 

\( = \binom{902}{394} kPa \)

 

From Set R1 in Table A.5 of Annex A to EN 1997-1: the partial factor on bearing resistance is

 

\( y_{R;v} = \binom{1}{1} \)

 

Design bearing resistance: \( q_{Rd} = \frac{R/{A}'}{y_{R;v}} = \frac{\binom{902}{394}}{\binom{1}{1}} = \binom{902}{394} kPa \)

 

Verification of bearing resistance

Utilization factor \( A_{GEO} = \frac{q_{Ed}}{q_{Rd}} = \binom{484.9/902}{381.8/394} = \binom{ 54 \%}{97 \%} \)

 

Since design is unacceptable if utilisation factor is greater than 100%, combination 2 of the Design Approach 1(i.e. DA1-2) is critical with a utilisation factor of 97%, implying that the requirements of the Eurocode are only just met.

 

(B) Design Approach 2 \( \underline{A1 “+” M1 “+” R2} \)
Actions and effects:

Characteristic self-weight of footing \( W_{Gk} = y_{c, k} \times L \times B \times d \)

 

\( = 25 \times 2.5 \times 1.5 \times 0.5 = 46.9 kN \)

 

Area of base \( A_{b} = L \times B = 2.5 \times 1.5 = 3.75 m^{2} \)

 

Partial factors from Set A1 in Table A.3 of Annex A to EN 1997-1:

\( y_{G} = 1.35; y_{Q} = 1.5 \)

 

Design vertical action: \( V_{d} = y_{G} \left ( W_{Gk} + V_{Gk} \right ) + y_{Q} V_{Qk} \)

 

\( = 1.35 \left ( 46.9 + 800 \right ) + 1.5 \times 450 = 1818.3 kN \)

 

Design bearing pressure [Equation]: \( q_{Ed} = \frac{V_{d}}{A_{b}} = \frac{1818.3}{3.75} = 484.9 kPa \)

 

Materials properties and resistance:
Partial factors from Set M1 in Table A.4 of Annex A to EN 1997-1:

\( y_{\varnothing} = 1; y_{c} = 1 \)

 

Design angle of shearing resistance \( \varnothing_{d} = \tan^{-1} \left ( \frac{\tan \varnothing_{k}}{y_{\varnothing }} \right ) = \tan^{-1} \left ( \frac{\tan 35}{1} \right ) = 35^{\circ} \)

 

Design effective cohesion \( {c}'_{d} = \frac{{c}'_{k}}{y_{c}} = \frac{0}{1} = 0 kPa \)

 

Bearing capacity factors from Annex D of EN 1997-1:

For effect of overburden, \( N_{q} = e^{\pi \tan \varnothing_{d}} \tan^{2} \left ( 45^{\circ} + \frac{\varnothing_{d}}{2} \right ) = 33.3 \)

 

For effect of cohesion, \( N_{c} = \left ( N_{q} – 1 \right ) \cot \varnothing_{d} = 46.1 \)

 

For effect of self-weight, \( N_{y} = 2 \left ( N_{q} – 1 \right ) \tan \varnothing_{d} = 45.2 \)

 

Shape factors:

For effect of overburden, \( s_{q} = 1 + \frac{B}{L} \sin \varnothing_{d} = 1.34 \)

 

For effect of cohesion, \( s_{c} = \frac{\left ( s_{q}N_{q} – 1 \right )}{\left ( N_{q} – 1 \right )} = 1.35 \)

 

For effect of self-weight, \( s_{y} = 1 – 0.3 \frac{B}{L} = 0.82 \)

Depth factors:[see the comments given in (A) Design Approach 1]
Load-inclination factors, caused by a horizontal load H:
Since H = 0, each of the load-inclination factors \( i_{c}, i_{q} \) and \( i_{y} \) is 1.0.
Base-inclination factors:
Since the foundation base is horizontal, that is \( \alpha = 0 \), each of the base-inclination factors \( b_{c}, b_{q} \) and \( b_{y} \) is 1.0.
Ground-inclination factors: [see the comments given in (A) Design Approach 1]
Effective overburden pressure \( {q}' \left ( or {\sigma}'_{vk,b} \right ) \):

 

\( {q}' = y_{k} \times D = 18 \times 0.5 = 9 kN/m^{2} \)

 

Gross ultimate bearing capacity \( \left ( Gross q_{ult} \right ) \) or gross bearing resistance \( \left ( R / {A}' \right ) \):

Annex D of EN 1997-1 suggests that bearing resistance may be calculated from

 

\( R / {A}' = \underbrace{{c}' N_{c} b_{c} s_{c} i_{c} }_{Cohesion} + \underbrace{{q}' N_{q} b_{q} s_{q} i_{q} }_{Overburden} + \underbrace{ 0.5 {y}' {B}' N_{y} b_{y} s_{y} i_{y} }_{Self-weight} \)

 

• From cohesion: \( \left ( Gross q_{ult} \right )_{1} = \left ( 0 \right )\left ( 46.1 \right )\left ( 1.35 \right ) = 0 kPa \)

 

• From overburden: \( \left ( Gross q_{ult} \right )_{2} = \left ( 9 \right )\left ( 33.3 \right )\left ( 1.34 \right ) = 402 kPa \)

 

• From self-weight: \( \left ( Gross q_{ult} \right )_{3} = \left ( 0.5 \right )\left ( 18 \right )\left ( 1.5 \right )\left ( 45.2 \right )\left ( 0.82 \right ) = 500 kPa \)

 

\( Gross bearing resistance R / {A}' = Gross q_{ult} = \sum_{i = 1}^{3} \left ( Gross q_{ult} \right )_{i} \)

 

\( = 902 kPa \)

 

From Set R2 in Table A.5 of Annex A to EN 1997-1: the partial factor on bearing resistance is

\( y_{R;v} = 1.4 \)

As it may be noticed, for Design Approach 2 the uncertainty in the calculation is covered through partial factors on the actions and on overall factor on the calculated resistance.

 

Design bearing resistance: \( q_{Rd} = \frac{R / {A}'}{y_{R;v}} = \frac{902}{1.4} = 644.3 kPa \)

 

Verification of bearing resistance

 

\( Utilization factor A_{GEO} = \frac{q_{Ed}}{q_{Rd}} = \frac{484.9}{644.3} = 75 \% \)

 

Design is unacceptable if utilisation factor is greater than 100 %.
The calculated utilisation factor (75 %) would indicate that, for the design situation of this problem, according to DA2 the footing is potentially over-designed.

 

(C) Design Approach 3 A1 “ + ”M2 “ + ”R3
Actions and effects:

Characteristic self-weight of footing \( W_{Gk} = y_{c, k} \times L \times B \times d \)

 

\( = 25 \times 2.5 \times 1.5 \times 0.5 = 46.9 kN \)

 

Area of base \( A_{b} = L \times B = 2.5 \times 1.5 = 3.75 m^{2} \)

 

Partial factors on structural actions from Set A1 in Table A.3 of Annex A to EN 1997-1:

\( y_{G} = 1.35; y_{Q} = 1.5 \)

 

Design vertical action:

\( V_{d} = y_{G} \left ( W_{Gk} + V_{Gk} \right ) + y_{Q} V_{Qk} \)

 

\( = 1.35 \left ( 46.9 + 800 \right ) + 1.5 \times 450 = 1818.3 kN \)

 

Design bearing pressure [ Equation ]: \( q_{Ed} = \frac{V_{d}}{A_{b}} = \frac{1818.3}{3.75} = 484.9 kPa \)

 

Materials properties and resistance:

Partial factors from Set M2 in Table A.4 of Annex A to EN 1997-1:

\( y_{\varnothing} = 1.25; y_{c} = 1.25 \)

 

Design angle of shearing resistance \( \varnothing_{d} = \tan^{-1} \left ( \frac{\tan \varnothing_{k}}{y_{\varnothing }} \right ) = \tan^{-1} \left ( \frac{\tan 35}{1.25} \right ) = 29.3^{\circ} \)

 

Design effective cohesion \( {c}'_{d} = \frac{{c}'_{k}}{y_{c}} = \frac{0}{1.25} = 0 kPa \)

 

Bearing capacity factors from Annex D of EN 1997-1:

For effect of overburden, \( N_{q} = e^{\pi \tan \varnothing_{d}} \tan^{2} \left ( 45^{\circ} + \frac{\varnothing_{d}}{2} \right ) = 17 \)

 

For effect of cohesion, \( N_{c} = \left ( N_{q} – 1 \right ) \cot \varnothing_{d} = 28.4 \)

 

For effect of self-weight, \( N_{y} = 2 \left ( N_{q} – 1 \right ) \tan \varnothing_{d} = 17.8 \)

 

Shape factors:

For effect of overburden, \( s_{q} = 1 + \frac{B}{L} \sin \varnothing_{d} = 1.29 \)

 

For effect of cohesion, \( s_{c} = \frac{\left ( s_{q}N_{q} – 1 \right )}{\left ( N_{q} – 1 \right )} = 1.31 \)

 

For effect of self-weight, \( s_{y} = 1 – 0.3 \frac{B}{L} = 0.82 \)

 

Depth factors: [see the comments given in (A) Design Approach 1]
Load-inclination factors, caused by a horizontal load H:
Since H = 0, each of the load-inclination factors \( i_{c}, i_{q} \) and \( i_{y} \) is 1.0.
Base-inclination factors:
Since the foundation base is horizontal, that is \( \alpha = 0 \), each of the base-inclination factors \( b_{c}, b_{q} \) and \( b_{y} \) is 1.0.
Ground-inclination factors: [see the comments given in (A) Design Approach 1]
Effective overburden pressure \( {q}' \left ( or {\sigma}'_{vk,b} \right ) \):

 

\( {q}' = y_{k} \times D = 18 \times 0.5 = 9 kN/m^{2} \)

 

Gross ultimate bearing capacity \( \left ( Gross q_{ult} \right ) \) or gross bearing resistance \( \left ( R / {A}' \right ) \):

Annex D of EN 1997-1 suggests that bearing resistance may be calculated from

 

\( R / {A}' = \underbrace{{c}' N_{c} b_{c} s_{c} i_{c} }_{Cohesion} + \underbrace{{q}' N_{q} b_{q} s_{q} i_{q} }_{Overburden} + \underbrace{ 0.5 {y}' {B}' N_{y} b_{y} s_{y} i_{y} }_{Self-weight} \)

 

• From cohesion: \( \left ( Gross q_{ult} \right )_{1} = \left ( 0 \right )\left ( 28.4 \right )\left ( 1.31 \right ) = 0 kPa \)

 

• From overburden: \( \left ( Gross q_{ult} \right )_{2} = \left ( 9 \right )\left ( 17 \right )\left ( 1.29 \right ) = 197 kPa \)

 

• From self-weight: \( \left ( Gross q_{ult} \right )_{3} = \left ( 0.5 \right )\left ( 18 \right )\left ( 1.5 \right )\left ( 17.8 \right )\left ( 0.82 \right ) = 197 kPa \)

 

\( Gross bearing resistance R/{A}' = Gross q_{ult} = \sum_{i = 1}^{3} \left ( Gross q_{ult} \right )_{i} \)

 

\( = 394 kPa \)

 

From Set R3 in Table A.5 of Annex A to EN 1997-1: the partial factor on bearing resistance is

\( y_{R;v} = 1.0 \)

As it may be noticed, Design Approach 3 applies partial factors to both actions and material properties at the same time.

Design bearing resistance: \( q_{Rd} = \frac{R / {A}'}{y_{R;v}} = \frac{394}{1.0} = 394 kPa \)

 

Verification of bearing resistance

\( Utilisation factor A_{GEO} = \frac{q_{Ed}}{q_{Rd}} = \frac{484.9}{394} = 123 \% \)

 

The design is unacceptable since the utilisation factor is greater than 100%. Thus the DA3 calculation suggests the design is unsafe and re-design would be required.
Finally, one may comment on the three DAs calculations as follows:
The three design approaches gives different evaluation of the suitability of the proposed foundation for the given design situation and loading. Of the three approaches, DA1 suggests the footing is only just satisfactory whilst DA3 suggests redesign would be required and DA2 may indicate that the footing is overdesigned! It may not be so easy to decide on which approach is the most appropriate or which one to be neglected or considered. However, it would appear that DA3 is unnecessary conservative as providing significant partial factors on both actions and material properties, and therefore, the footing redesign would not be necessary. At the end, considering both safety and economy requirements, one may decide on the DA1-2 calculations; knowing that this approach generally governs the size of the foundations.
