Guest Question.

The stresses shown in Figure P12.39 act at a point on the free surface of a machine component. Determine the normal stresses \(\sigma_{x} \text { and } \sigma_{y}\) and the shear stress \(\tau_{x y}\) at the point.

Admin
Solution

Redefine the axes, calling the rotated axes x and y. The angle from the rotated element to the unrotated element is now a positive value (since it is counterclockwise). Thus, the given stress values can be expressed as:

\(\sigma_{x}=59 MPa , \sigma_{y}=-48 MPa , \tau_{x y}=-82 MPa , \theta=30^{\circ}\)

The normal stress transformation equation [Eq. (12.3)] gives \(\sigma_{n}\), which is actually the normal stress in the horizontal direction (i.e., the original x direction) on the unrotated element:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{n} &=\sigma_{x} \cos ^{2} \theta+\sigma_{y} \sin ^{2} \theta+2 \tau_{x y} \sin \theta \cos \theta \\&=(59 MPa ) \cos ^{2}\left(30^{\circ}\right)+(-48 MPa ) \sin ^{2}\left(30^{\circ}\right)+2(-82 MPa ) \sin \left(30^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(30^{\circ}\right) \\&=-38.764 MPa =38.8 MPa ( C )\end{aligned}\)

To find \(\sigma_{t}\), which is actually the normal stress in the vertical direction (i.e., the original y direction) on the unrotated element, add 90° to the value of θ used in Eq. (12.3):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{t} &=\sigma_{x} \cos ^{2} \theta+\sigma_{y} \sin ^{2} \theta+2 \tau_{x y} \sin \theta \cos \theta \\&=(59 MPa ) \cos ^{2}\left(30^{\circ}+90^{\circ}\right)+(-48 MPa ) \sin ^{2}\left(30^{\circ}+90^{\circ}\right)+2(-82 MPa ) \sin \left(30^{\circ}+90^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(30^{\circ}+90^{\circ}\right) \\&=(59 MPa ) \cos ^{2}\left(120^{\circ}\right)+(-48 MPa ) \sin ^{2}\left(120^{\circ}\right)+2(-82 MPa ) \sin \left(120^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(120^{\circ}\right) \\&=49.764 MPa =49.8 MPa ( T )\end{aligned}\)

The shear stress transformation equation [Eq. (12.4)] gives \(\tau_{n t}\), which is actually the shear stress on the horizontal and vertical faces of the unrotated element:

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{n t} &=-\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) \sin \theta \cos \theta+\tau_{x y}\left(\cos ^{2} \theta-\sin ^{2} \theta\right) \\&=-[(59 MPa )-(-48 MPa )] \sin \left(30^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(30^{\circ}\right)+(-82 MPa )\left[\cos ^{2}\left(30^{\circ}\right)-\sin ^{2}\left(30^{\circ}\right)\right] \\&=-87.332 MPa =-87.3 MPa\end{aligned}\)
