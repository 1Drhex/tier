Guest Question.

An 8-in.-diameter centrifugal pump operating at 1200 rpm is geometrically similar to the 12-in.-diameter pump having the performance characteristics of Figs. 11.12a and 11.12b while operating at 1000 rpm. The working fluid is water at 60 째F.

FIND For peak efficiency, predict the discharge, actual head rise, and shaft horsepower for this smaller pump.

Admin
Solution

As is indicated by \(\eta =\phi _{3}(\frac{Q}{\omega D^{3}} )\), for a given efficiency the flow coefficient has the same value for a given family of pumps. From Fig. 11.12b we see that at peak efficiency \(C_{Q}=0.0625\).

Thus, for the 8-in. pump

\(Q=C_{Q}\omega D^{3}=(0.0625)({1200}/{60}{rev}/{s})(2\pi {rad}/{rev})({8}/{12}ft)^{3}\)

\(Q=2.33{ft^{3}}/{s}\)

or in terms of gpm

\(Q=(2.33{ft^{3}}/{s})(7.48{gal}/{ft^{3}})(60 {s}/{min})=1046gpm\)

The actual head rise and the shaft horsepower can be determined in a similar manner since at peak efficiency \(C_{H}\)= 0.19 and \(C_{\mathscr{P}}\)= 0.014, so that with \(\omega =1200{rev}/{min}({1min}/{60s})\times (2\pi {rad}/{rev})=126{rad}/{s}\)

\(h_{p}=\frac{C_{H}\omega ^{2}D^{2}}{g}=\frac{(0.19)(126{rad}/{s})^{2}({8}/{12}ft)^{2}}{32.2{ft}/{s^{2}}}=41.6ft \)

Also,

\(\dot{W}_{shaft} =C_{\mathscr{P}}\rho \omega ^{3}D^{5}=(0.014)(1.94{slugs}/{ft^{3}})(126{rad}/{s})^{3}({8}/{12}ft)^{5}=7150{ft\cdot lb}/{s}\)

\(\dot{W}_{shaft} =\frac{7150{ft\cdot lb}/{s}}{550{{ft\cdot lb}/{s}}/{hp}} =13.0hp\)

COMMENT The last result gives the shaft horsepower, which is the power supplied to the pump shaft. The power actually gained by the fluid is equal to \(\gamma Qh_{p}\), which in this example is

\(\mathscr{P}_{f}=\gamma Qh_{p}=(62.4{lb}/{ft^{3}})(2.33{ft^{3}}/{s})(41.6ft)=6050{ft\cdot lb}/{s}\)

Thus, the efficiency, \(\eta \), is

\(\eta =\frac{\mathscr{P}_{f}}{\dot{W}_{shaft}}=\frac{6050}{7150}=85 \)%

which checks with the efficiency curve of Fig. 11.12b.
