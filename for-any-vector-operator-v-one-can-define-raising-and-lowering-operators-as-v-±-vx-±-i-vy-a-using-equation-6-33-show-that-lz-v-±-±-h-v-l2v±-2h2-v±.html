Guest Question.

For any vector operator \( \hat{ V } \) one can define raising and lowering operators as

\( \hat{V}_{\pm}=\hat{V}_{x} \pm i \hat{V}_{y} \) .

(a) Using Equation 6.33, show that

\( \left[\hat{L}_{i}, \hat{V}_{j}\right]=i \hbar \epsilon_{i j k} \hat{V}_{k} \) (6.33).

\( \left[\hat{L}_{z}, \hat{V}_{\pm}\right]=\pm \hbar \hat{V}_{+} \) .

\( \left[\hat{L}^{2}, \hat{V}_{\pm}\right]=2 \hbar^{2} \hat{V}_{\pm} \pm 2 \hbar \hat{V}_{\pm} \hat{L}_{z} \mp 2 \hbar \hat{V}_{z} \hat{L}_{\pm} \).

(b) Show that, if ψ is an eigenstate of \( \hat{L}^{2} \text { and } \hat{L}_{z} \) with eigenvalues \( \ell(\ell+1) \hbar^{2} \text { and } \ell \hbar \) respectively, then either \( \hat{V}_{+} \psi \text { is zero or } \hat{V}_{+} \psi \) is also an eigenstate of \( \hat{L}^{2} \text { and } \hat{L}_{z} \text { with eigenvalues }(\ell+1)(\ell+2) \hbar^{2} \) and \( (\ell+1) \hbar \) respectively. This means that, acting on a state with maximal \( m_{\ell}=\ell, \) the operator \( \hat{V}_{+} \) either “raises” both the \( \ell \) and m values by 1 or destroys the state.

Admin
Solution

(a) First,

\( \left[L_{z}, V_{\pm}\right]=\left[L_{z}, V_{x}\right] \pm i\left[L_{z}, V_{y}\right]=i \hbar V_{y} \pm i\left(-i \hbar V_{x}\right) \).

\( =\hbar\left(\pm V_{x}+i V_{y}\right)=\pm \hbar\left(V_{x} \pm i V_{y}\right)=\pm \hbar V_{\pm} \).

If you’re comfortable working with index notation, the next part could be quicker, but I’ll do it the long way. Here we go:

\( \left[L^{2}, V_{\pm}\right]=\left[L_{x}^{2}+L_{y}^{2}+L_{z}^{2}, V_{x} \pm i V_{y}\right] \).

\( =\left[L_{x}^{2}, V_{x}\right] \pm i\left[L_{x}^{2}, V_{y}\right]+\left[L_{y}^{2}, V_{x}\right] \pm i\left[L_{y}^{2}, V_{y}\right]+\left[L_{z}^{2}, V_{\pm}\right] \).

\( =0 \pm i L_{x}\left[L_{x}, V_{y}\right] \pm i\left[L_{x}, V_{y}\right] L_{x}+L_{y}\left[L_{y}, V_{x}\right]+\left[L_{y}, V_{x}\right] L_{y} \)

\( +0+L_{z}\left[L_{z}, V_{\pm}\right]+\left[L_{z}, V_{\pm}\right] L_{z} \).

\( =\pm i L_{x} i \hbar V_{z} \pm i\left(i \hbar V_{z}\right) L_{x}+L_{y}\left(-i \hbar V_{z}\right)+\left(-i \hbar V_{z}\right) L_{y} \)

\( +L_{z}\left(\pm \hbar V_{\pm}\right) \pm \hbar V_{\pm} L_{z} \).

where we’ve used the result proved above. Finally we need to get all L’s to the right of all V ‘s:

\( \left[L^{2}, V_{\pm}\right]=\mp \hbar V_{z} L_{x} \mp \hbar\left[L_{x}, V_{z}\right] \mp \hbar V_{z} L_{x}-i \hbar V_{z} L_{y}-i \hbar\left[L_{y}, V_{z}\right] \)

\( -i \hbar V_{z} L_{y} \pm \hbar V_{\pm} L_{z} \pm \hbar\left[L_{z}, V_{\pm}\right] \pm \hbar V_{\pm} L_{z} \).

\( =\hbar V_{z}\left(\mp 2 L_{x}-2 i L_{y}\right) \mp \hbar\left(-i \hbar V_{y}\right)-i \hbar i \hbar V_{x} \)

\( \pm 2 \hbar V_{\pm} L_{z} \pm \hbar\left(\pm \hbar V_{\pm}\right) \).

\( =\mp 2 \hbar V_{z}\left(L_{x} \pm i L_{y}\right)+\hbar^{2}\left(V_{x} \pm i V_{y}\right) \pm 2 \hbar V_{\pm} L_{z}+\hbar^{2} V_{\pm} \).

\( =2 \hbar^{2} V_{\pm} \pm 2 \hbar V_{\pm} L_{z} \mp 2 \hbar V_{z} L_{\pm} \).

This is the desired result.

(b) Acting on a state \( \psi_{n \ell \ell} \) with each of the commutator relations form part a we get

\( \left[L_{z}, V_{+}\right] \psi_{n \ell \ell}=\hbar V_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell} \).

\( L_{z} V_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}-V_{+} \underbrace{L_{z} \psi_{n \ell \ell}}_{\ell \hbar \psi_{n \ell \ell}}=\hbar V_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell} \).

\( L_{z}\left(V_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}\right)=(\ell+1) \hbar\left(V_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}\right) \).

and

\( \left[L^{2}, V_{+}\right] \psi_{n \ell \ell}=\left(2 \hbar^{2} V_{+}+2 \hbar V_{+} L_{z}-2 \hbar V_{z} L_{+}\right) \psi_{n \ell \ell} \).

\( L^{2} V_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}-V_{+} \underbrace{L^{2} \psi_{n \ell \ell}}_{\ell(\ell+1) \hbar^{2} \psi_{n \ell \ell}}=2 \hbar^{2} V_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}+2 \hbar V_{+} \underbrace{L_{z} \psi_{n \ell \ell}}_{\ell \hbar \psi_{n \ell \ell}}-2 \hbar V_{z} \underbrace{L_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}}_{0} \).

\( L^{2}\left(V_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}\right)=[\ell(\ell+1)+2+2 \ell] \hbar^{2}\left(V_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}\right) \).

\( =(\ell+1)(\ell+2) \hbar^{2}\left(V_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}\right) \).

From these results, the state \( V_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell} \text { has an } m \text { value of } \ell+1 \text { and an } \ell \text { value of } \ell+1 \).
