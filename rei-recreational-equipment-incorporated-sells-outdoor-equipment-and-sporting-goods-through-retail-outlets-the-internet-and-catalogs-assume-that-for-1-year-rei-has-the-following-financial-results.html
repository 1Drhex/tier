Guest Question.

REI (Recreational Equipment Incorporated) sells outdoor equipment and sporting goods through retail outlets, the Internet, and catalogs. Assume that for 1 year REI has the following financial results in the state of Kentucky, which has a flat tax rate of 6% on corporate taxable income:

Total revenue $19.9 million
Operating expenses $8.6 million
Depreciation and other allowed deductions $1.8 million

(a) Determine the state taxes and federal taxes due using Table 17–1 rates.
(b) Find the average federal tax rate paid for the year.
(c) Determine a single-value tax rate useful in economic evaluations using the average federal
tax rate determined in part (b).
(d) Estimate federal and state taxes using the single-value rate, and compare their total with the
total in part (a).

Admin
Solution

(a) Calculate TI by Equation [17.2] and use Table 17–1 rates for federal taxes due.
Kentucky state TI = GI − OE − D = 19.9 million − 8.6 million − 1.8 million
= $9.5 million
Kentucky state taxes = 0.06(TI) = 0.06(9,500,000) = $570,000
Federal TI = GI − OE − D − state taxes = 9,500,000 − 570,000
= $8,930,000
Federal taxes = 113,900 + 0.34(8,930,000 − 335,000) = $3,036,200
Total federal and state taxes = 3,036,200 + 570,000 = $3,606,200 ….[17.8]

(b) From Equation [17.5]

\(\text { Average tax rate }=\frac{\text { total taxes paid }}{\text { taxable income }}=\frac{\text { taxes }}{\text { TI }}\),

the average tax rate paid is approximately 32% of TI.

Average federal tax rate = 3,036,200∕9,500,000 = 0.3196

(c) By Equation [17.6]\(T_{e}\) = state rate + (1 − state rate)(federal rate),\( T_{e}\) is slightly over 36% per year for combined state and federal taxes.

\(T_{e} = 0.06 + (1 − 0.06)(0.3196) = 0.3604 (36.04\%)\)

(d) Use the effective tax rate and TI = $9.5 million from part (a) in Equation [17.7] Taxes = (Te)(TI) to approximate total taxes.

Taxes = 0.3604(9,500,000) = $3,423,800

Compared to Equation [17.8]

Total federal and state taxes = 3,036,200 + 570,000 = $3,606,200,

this approximation is $182,400 low, a 5.06% underestimate.
