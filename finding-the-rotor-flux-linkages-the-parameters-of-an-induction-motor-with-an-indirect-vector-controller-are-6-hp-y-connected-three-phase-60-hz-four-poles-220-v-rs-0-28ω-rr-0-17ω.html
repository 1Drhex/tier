Guest Question.

Finding the Rotor Flux Linkages

The parameters of an induction motor with an indirect vector controller are 6 hp, Y-connected, three phase, 60 Hz, four poles, \(220 V, R_s = 0.28 \Omega , R_r = 0.17 \Omega , Lm = 61 mH, L_r = 56 mH,L_s = 53 mH, J = 0.01667 kg-m^2\), rated speed = 1800 rpm. Find (a) the rated rotor flux linkages and the corresponding stator currents \(i_{ds}\) and \(i_{qs}\), (b) the total stator current \( I_s\), (c) the torque angle \(θ_T\), and (d) the slip gain \(K_{sl}\).

Admin
Solution
\(HP = 6 hp, P_o = 745.7 × 6 = 4474 W, V_L = 220 V, f = 60 Hz, p = 4, R_s = 0.28Ω, R_r = 0.17Ω, L_m = 61 mH, L_r = 56 mH, L_s = 53 mH, J = 0.01667 kg·m^2, N = 1800 rpm. \omega =2\pi =2\pi \times 60=376.991 rad/s,\omega _m=(2\pi N)/60=188.496 rad/s, \omega _r=(p/2)\times \omega _m=(4/2)\times 157.08 =376.991 rad/s, \omega _{c(max)}=\omega _r=314.159 rad/s, V_{qs}=(\sqrt{2}V_L )/\sqrt{3}=(\sqrt{2}\times 220 )/\sqrt{3}=179.629 rmV , V_{ds}=0\)

a. For \( \omega_{sl} = 0\) , Eq. (15.122)

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} \upsilon _{qs} \\ \upsilon _{ds} \\ \upsilon _{qr} \\ \upsilon _{dr} \end{matrix} \right ]=\left [ \begin{matrix} R_s + DL_s & \omega L_s & DL_m & \omega L_m \\ -\omega L_s &R_s + DL_s & -\omega L_m &DL_m \\ DL_m & (\omega -\omega _r)L_m & R_r + DL_r & (\omega -\omega _r)L_r \\ -(\omega -\omega _r)L_m & DL_m& -(\omega -\omega _r)L_r & R_r + DL_r \end{matrix} \right ]\left [ \begin{matrix} i_{qs} \\ i_{ds} \\ i_{qr} \\ i_{dr} \end{matrix} \right ] \) (15.122)

gives

\(\left ( \begin{matrix} i_{qs} \\ i_{ds} \\ i_{qr} \\ i_{dr} \end{matrix} \right )=\left ( \begin{matrix} R_s &\omega L_s &0 &\omega L_m \\ -\omega L_s & R_s & -\omega L_m & 0 \\ 0 & \omega _{sl}L_m & R_r & \omega _{sl}L_r \\ -\omega _{sl}L_m &0 & -\omega _{sl}L_r & R_r \end{matrix} \right )^{-1}\left ( \begin{matrix} \upsilon _{qs} \\ \upsilon _{ds} \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right )=\left ( \begin{matrix} 0.126 \\ 8.988 \\ 0 \\0 \end{matrix} \right ) \)

Thus, \(i_{qs} = 0.126 A, i_{ds} = 8.988 A, i_{qr} = 0\), and \(i_{dr }= 0\)

From Eqs. (15.123) to (15.125), we get the stator flux linkages as

\(\Psi_{qs}=L_si_{qs} + L_mi_{qr} = 53 \times 10^{-3} \times 0.126 + 61 \times 10^{-3} \times 0 = 6.678 mWb- turn \)

 

\(\Psi_{ds}=L_si_{ds} + L_mi_{dr} = 53 \times 10^{-3} \times 8.988 + 61 \times 10^{-3} \times 0 = 0.476 Wb–turn\)

 

\({\Psi }_{s}=\sqrt{\Psi ^2_{qs}+\Psi ^2_{ds}}=\sqrt{(6.678 \times 10^{-2})^2+0.476^2}=0.476 Wb–turn \)

From Eqs. (15.126) to (15.128), we get the rotor flux linkages as

\(\Psi_{qr}=L_ri_{qr} + L_mi_{qs} = 56 \times 10^{-3} \times 0 + 61 \times 10^{-3} \times 0.126 = 6.678 mWb-turn \)

 

\(\Psi_{dr}=L_ri_{dr }+ L_mi_{ds} = 56 \times 10^{-3} \times 0 + 61 \times 10^{-3} \times 8.988 = 0.548 Wb-turn\)

 

\({\Psi }_r=\sqrt{\Psi ^2_{qr}+\Psi ^2_{dr}}=\sqrt{(7.686 \times 10^{-2})^2 + 0.548^2}= 0.548 Wb-turn\)

From Eqs. (15.129) to (15.131), we get the magnetizing flux linkages as

\(\Psi _{mq}=L_m(i_{qs} + i_{qr}) = 61 × 10^{-3} × (0.216 + 0)= 7.686 mWb\)

 

\(\Psi _{md}=L_m + (i_{ds} + i_{dr}) = 61 × 10^{-3} × (8.988 + 0) = 0.548 Wb\)

 

\({\Psi } _{m}=\sqrt{\Psi ^2_{mq}+\Psi ^2_{md}}=\sqrt{(7.686 \times 10^{-2})^2 + 0.548^2}=0.548 Wb\)

b. The flux-producing stator (or field) current to produce the mmf \(\Psi _{m}\) is

\(I_f = I_s\sqrt{i^2_{ds}+i^2_{qs}}=\sqrt{8.988^2 + 0.216^2}=8.989 A\)

And Eq. (15.132) gives the corresponding torque as

\(T_d=\frac{3p}{4}(\Psi _{ds}i_{qs}-\Psi _{ds}i_{ds})=\frac{3\times 4}{4}(0.474\times 0.216-6.678 \times 10^{-3}\times 8.988)\)

= 0 (as expected)

Equation (15.149) also gives the approximate value of the corresponding torque as

\(T_d=\frac{3P}{4}\times \frac{L^2_m}{L_r}i_{ds}i_{qs}=\frac{3\times 4}{4}\times \frac{(61\times 10^{-3})}{56 \times 10^{-3}}\times 8.988\times 0.216=0.226 N.m\)

c. The torque needed to produce the output power \(P_o\) is

\(T_e = P_o/ω_m = 4744/ 188.496 = 23.736 N.m.\)

Equation (15.147)

\(T_d=\frac{3P}{2} \frac{L_m}{L_r}\left(\frac{\Psi_{dr}i_{qs}-\Psi _{qr}i_{ds}}{2} \right)=\frac{3p}{2\times 2}\frac{L_m}{L_r} \hat{\Psi }_ri_{qs}\) (15.147)

gives the torque constant \(K_e = (3p/4) (L_m/L_r) = (3 × 4/4) × (61/56) = 3.268\) and the rotor current needed to produce the torque \(T_e\) is \(I_r = i_{qs} =T_e/(K_e\Psi _r) = 23.736/(3.268 × 0.548) = 13.247 A\).

Therefore, the total stator current is

\(I_s=\sqrt{I^2_f+I^2_r} =\sqrt{8.989^2 + 13.247^2}=16.01 A\)

And the torque angle \(θ_T = \tan^{-1}(I_s/I_f) = \tan^{-1}(16.01/8.989) = 60.69°\)

d. From Eq. (15.151),

\(K_{sl}=\frac{\omega ^*_{sl}}{i^{*}_{qs}}=\frac{L_m}{\hat{\Psi }_r }\times \frac{R_r}{L_r} =\frac{R_r}{L_r}\times \frac{1}{i^\ast _{ds}}\) (15.151)

 

\(\omega _{sl}=\frac{R_r}{L_r}\times \frac{I_s}{I_f}=\frac{0.17}{56 \times 10^{-3}}\times \frac{16.01}{8.989}=5.06 rad/s\)

 

\(K_{sl}=\frac{L_mR_r}{\Psi _rL_r}=\frac{61 \times 10^{-3}}{0.548}\times \frac{0.17}{56 \times 10^{-3}}=0.338 \)
