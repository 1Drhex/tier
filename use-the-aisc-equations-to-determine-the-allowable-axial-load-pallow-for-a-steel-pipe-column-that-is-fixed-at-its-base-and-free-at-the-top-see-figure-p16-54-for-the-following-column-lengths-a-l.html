Guest Question.

Use the AISC equations to determine the allowable axial load \(P_{\text {allow }}\) for a steel pipe column that is fixed at its base and free at the top (see Figure P16.54) for the following column lengths: (a) L = 3 m and (b) L = 4 m. The outside diameter of the pipe is 168 mm and the wall thickness is 11 mm. Assume E = 200 GPa and \(\sigma_{Y}\) = 250 MPa.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&d=168 mm -2(11 mm )=146 mm \\&A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[(168 mm )^{2}-(146 mm )^{2}\right]=5,425.531 mm ^{2} \\&I=\frac{\pi}{64}\left[(168 mm )^{4}-(146 mm )^{4}\right]=16,798,798.85 mm ^{4} \\&r=\sqrt{\frac{16,798,798.85 mm ^{4}}{5,425.531 mm ^{2}}}=55.644 mm\end{aligned}\)

Limiting slenderness ratio

\(4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{Y}}}=4.71 \sqrt{\frac{200,000 MPa }{250 MPa }}=133.219\)

(a) Slenderness ratios for L = 3 m:

\(K L / r=\frac{(2.0)(3,000 mm )}{55.644 mm }=107.828\)

Elastic critical buckling stress

\(\sigma_{e}=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{\left(\frac{K L}{r}\right)^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(200,000 MPa )}{(107.828)^{2}}=169.771 MPa\)

Critical and allowable buckling stresses

Since \(\frac{K L}{r} \leq 4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{y}}}\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{c r}=\left[0.658^{\left(\frac{\sigma_{Y}}{\sigma_{e}}\right)}\right] \sigma_{Y}=\left[0.658^{\left(\frac{250 MPa }{169.771 MPa }\right)}\right](250 MPa )=134.978 MPa \\&\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\sigma_{c r}}{1.67}=\frac{134.978 MPa }{1.67}=80.825 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Allowable axial load:

\(P_{\text {allow }}=\sigma_{\text {allow }} A=\left(80.825 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(5,425.531 mm ^{2}\right)=438,521 N =439 kN\)

(b) Slenderness ratios for L = 4 m:

\(K L / r=\frac{(2.0)(4,000 mm )}{55.644 mm }=143.771\)

Elastic critical buckling stress

\(\sigma_{e}=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{\left(\frac{K L}{r}\right)^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(200,000 MPa )}{(143.771)^{2}}=95.496 MPa\)

Critical and allowable buckling stresses

Since \(\frac{K L}{r}>4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{y}}}\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{c r}=0.877 \sigma_{e}=(0.877)(95.496 MPa )=83.750 MPa \\&\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\sigma_{c r}}{1.67}=\frac{83.750 MPa }{1.67}=50.150 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Allowable axial load:

\(P_{\text {allow }}=\sigma_{\text {allow }} A=\left(50.150 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(5,425.531 mm ^{2}\right)=272,089 N =272 kN\)
