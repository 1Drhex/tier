Guest Question.

The simply supported beam shown in Fig. P10.64 consists of a W21 × 44 structural steel wide-flange shape [E = 29,000 ksi; I = 843 \(\text { in. }^{4}\)]. For a loading of w = 8 kips/ft, determine:
(a) the beam deflection at point C.
(b) the beam deflection at point E.

Admin
Solution

(a) Beam deflection at point C
Consider deflection at C from moment caused by linear load on overhang.
[Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated moment.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{C}=-\frac{M x}{6 L E I}\left(2 L^{2}-3 L x+x^{2}\right) \text { (elastic curve) }\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&M=1 / 2(8 kips / ft )(12 ft )(4 ft )=192 kip – ft , \\&L=18 ft , x=9 ft , E I=2.4447 \times 10^{7} \text { kip-in. }^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{C} &=-\frac{M x}{6 L E I}\left(2 L^{2}-3 L x+x^{2}\right) \\&=-\frac{(192 kip – ft )(9 ft )(12 in . / ft )^{3}}{6(18 ft )\left(2.4447 \times 10^{7} kip – in .{ }^{2}\right)}\left[2(18 ft )^{2}-3(18 ft )(9 ft )+(9 ft )^{2}\right]=-0.274816 in .\end{aligned}\)

 

Consider uniformly distributed loads between supports.
[Appendix C, SS beam with uniformly distributed load.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{C}=-\frac{5 w L^{4}}{384 E I}\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&w=-8 kips / ft , L=18 ft, \\&E I=2.4447 \times 10^{7} kip – in. ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(v_{C}=-\frac{5 w L^{4}}{384 E I}=-\frac{5(-8 kips / ft )(18 ft )^{4}(12 in . / ft )^{2}}{384\left(2.4447 \times 10^{7} kip – in .^{2}\right)}=0.772924 in .\)

 

Consider deflection at C resulting from moment caused by uniform load on overhang DE.
[Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated moment.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{C}=-\frac{M x}{6 L E I}\left(2 L^{2}-3 L x+x^{2}\right) \text { (elastic curve) }\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&M=(8 kips / ft )(6 ft )(3 ft )=144 kip – ft ,\\&L=18 ft , x=9 ft , E I=2.4447 \times 10^{7} kip – in .^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{C} &=-\frac{M x}{6 L E I}\left(2 L^{2}-3 L x+x^{2}\right) \\&=-\frac{(144 kip – ft )(9 ft )(12 in . / ft )^{3}}{6(18 ft )\left(2.4447 \times 10^{7} kip – in .{ }^{2}\right)}\left[2(18 ft )^{2}-3(18 ft )(9 ft )+(9 ft )^{2}\right]=-0.206113 in .\end{aligned}\)

Beam deflection at C

\(v_{C}=-0.274816 \text { in. }+0.772924 \text { in. }-0.206113 \text { in. }=0.291995 in .=0.292 \text { in. } \uparrow\)

 

(b) Beam deflection at point E
Consider deflection at E resulting from rotation at D caused by linear load on overhang.
[Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated moment.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(\theta_{D}=\frac{M L}{6 E I} \text { (slope magnitude) }\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&M=1 / 2(8 kips / ft )(12 ft )(4 ft )=192 kip – ft, \\&L=18 ft , E I=2.4447 \times 10^{7} \text { kip-in. }^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}&\theta_{D}=\frac{M L}{6 E I}=\frac{(192 kip – ft )(18 ft )(12 in . / ft )^{2}}{6\left(2.4447 \times 10^{7} kip – in .^{2}\right)}=0.0033928 rad \\&v_{E}=(6 ft )(12 in . / ft )(0.0033928 rad )=0.244282 in .\end{aligned}\)

 

Consider uniformly distributed loads between supports.
[Appendix C, SS beam with uniformly distributed load.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(\theta_{D}=\frac{w L^{3}}{24 E I} \text { (slope magnitude) }\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&w=8 kips / ft , L=18 ft ,\\&E I=2.4447 \times 10^{7} kip – in .^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}&\theta_{D}=\frac{w L^{3}}{24 E I}=\frac{(8 kips / ft )(18 ft )^{3}(12 in . / ft )^{2}}{24\left(2.4447 \times 10^{7} kip – in .{ }^{2}\right)}=0.0114507 rad \\&v_{E}=-(6 ft )(12 in . / ft )(0.0114507 rad )=-0.824448 in .\end{aligned}\)

 

Consider deflection at E resulting from rotation at D caused by uniform load on overhang DE.
[Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated moment.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(\theta_{D}=\frac{M L}{3 E I} \text { (slope magnitude) }\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&M=(8 kips / ft )(6 ft )(3 ft )=144 kip – ft, \\&L=18 ft , E I=2.4447 \times 10^{7} kip – in .^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}\theta_{D} &=\frac{M L}{3 E I}=\frac{(144 kip – ft )(18 ft )(12 in . / ft )^{2}}{3\left(2.4447 \times 10^{7} kip – in. ^{2}\right)}=0.0050892 rad \\v_{E} &=(6 ft )(12 in . / ft )(0.0050892 rad )=0.366422 in .\end{aligned}\)

 

Determine cantilever deflection due to uniformly distributed load on overhang DE.
[Appendix C, Cantilever beam with uniformly distributed load.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{E}=-\frac{w L^{4}}{8 E I} \text { (assuming fixed support at } D \text { ) }\)

Values:

\(w=-8 kips / ft , L=6 ft , E I=2.4447 \times 10^{7} kip – in .^{2}\)

Computation:

\(v_{E}=-\frac{w L^{4}}{8 E I}=-\frac{(-8 kips / ft )(6 ft )^{4}(12 in . / ft )^{3}}{8\left(2.4447 \times 10^{7} kip – in .{ }^{2}\right)}=0.091605 in .\)

Beam deflection at E

\(\begin{aligned}v_{E} &=0.244282 \text { in. }-0.824448 \text { in. }+0.366422 \text { in. }+0.091605 \text { in. } \\&=-0.122139 in .=0.1221 in . \downarrow\end{aligned}\)
