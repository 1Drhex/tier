Guest Question.

The motion of a particle is given by \(a=t^{3}-3 t^{2}+5\), where a is the acceleration in m/\(s^{2}\) and t is the time in seconds. The velocity of the particle at t = 1 second is 6.25 m/s, and the displacement is 8.30 metres. Calculate the displacement and the velocity at t = 2 seconds.

Admin
Solution

Given : \(a=t^{3}-3 t^{2}+5\)

We know that the acceleration, \(a=d v / d t\). Therefore the above equation may be written as

\(\frac{d v}{d t}=t^{3}-3 t^{2}+5 \quad \text { or } \quad d v=\left(t^{3}-3 t^{2}+5\right) d t\)

Integrating both sides

\(v=\frac{t^{4}}{4}-\frac{3 t^{3}}{3}+5 t+C_{1}=\frac{t^{4}}{4}-t^{3}+5 t+C_{1}\) (i)

where \(C_{1}\) is the first constant of integration. We know that when t = 1 s, \(\nu \) = 6.25 m/s. Therefore substituting these values of t and v in equation (i),

\(6.25=0.25-1+5+C_{1}=4.25+C_{1} \quad \text { or } \quad C_{1}=2\)

Now substituting the value of \(C_{1}\) in equation (i),

\(v=\frac{t_{4}}{4}-t^{3}+5 t+2\) (ii)

Velocity at t = 2 seconds

Substituting the value of t = 2 s in the above equation,

\(v=\frac{2^{4}}{4}-2^{3}+5 \times 2+2=8 m / s\)

Displacement at t = 2 seconds

We know that the velocity, \(v=d s / d t\), therefore equation (ii) may be written as

\(\frac{d s}{d t}=\frac{t^{4}}{4}-t^{3}+5 t+2 \quad \text { or } \quad d s=\left(\frac{t^{4}}{4}-t^{3}+5 t+2\right) d t\)

Integrating both sides,

\(s=\frac{t^{5}}{20}-\frac{t^{4}}{4}+\frac{5 t^{2}}{2}+2 t+C_{2}\) (iii)

where \(C_{2}\) is the second constant of integration. We know that when t = 1 s, s = 8.30 m. Therefore substituting these values of t and s in equation (iii),

\(8.30=\frac{1}{20}-\frac{1}{4}+\frac{5}{2}+2+C_{2}=4.3+C_{2} \quad \text { or } \quad C_{2}=4\)

Substituting the value of \(C_{2}\) in equation (iii),

\(s=\frac{t^{5}}{20}-\frac{t^{4}}{4}+\frac{5 t^{2}}{2}+2 t+4\)

Substituting the value of t = 2 s, in this equation,

\(s=\frac{2^{5}}{20}-\frac{2^{4}}{4}+\frac{5 \times 2^{2}}{2}+2 \times 2+4=15.6 m\)
