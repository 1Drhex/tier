Guest Question.

Let \(\mathbf{A}=\left(\begin{array}{lll}1 & 2 & 5 \\ 4 & 6 & 9\end{array}\right), \mathbf{B}=\left(\begin{array}{lll}2 & 5 & 6 \\ 1 & 7 & 2 \\ 9 & 6 & 1\end{array}\right)\) and \(\mathrm{C}=\left(\begin{array}{lll}5 & 4 & 9 \\ 7 & 4 & 0 \\ 6 & 9 & 8\end{array}\right)\)

Compute the following:

(a) \(\mathrm{B}+\mathrm{C}\) (b) \(\mathrm{A}+\mathrm{B}\) (c) \(3 \mathrm{~B}+2 \mathrm{C}\) (d) \(\mathrm{AB}\) (e) \(\mathrm{BA}\)

What do you notice about your results to parts \((d)\) and (e)?

Admin
Solution

(a) Adding the corresponding entries by hand gives

\(\mathrm{B}+\mathrm{C}=\left(\begin{array}{lll}2 & 5 & 6 \\1 & 7 & 2 \\9 & 6 & 1\end{array}\right)+\left(\begin{array}{lll}5 & 4 & 9 \\7 & 4 & 0 \\6 & 9 & 8\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}2+5 & 5+4 & 6+9 \\1+7 & 7+4 & 2+0 \\9+6 & 6+9 & 1+8\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}7 & 9 & 15 \\8 & 11 & 2 \\15 & 15 & 9\end{array}\right)\) To use MATLAB, enter the matrices \(\mathrm{B}\) and \(\mathrm{C}\) after the command prompt \(>>\) Separate each matrix by a comma. Then enter \(\mathrm{B}+\mathrm{C}\) and the output should give the same result as above.

(b) \(\mathrm{A}+\mathrm{B}\) is impossible because matrices are of different size.

What sizes are matrix \(A\) and matrix \(B ?\)

\(\mathrm{A}\) is a \(2 \times 3\) matrix and \(\mathrm{B}\) is a \(3 \times 3\) matrix, so we cannot add (or subtract) these matrices. If we try this in MATLAB, we receive a message saying ‘Matrix dimensions must agree’.

(c) We have

\(\begin{aligned}3 \mathrm{~B}+2 \mathrm{C} &=3\left(\begin{array}{lll}2 & 5 & 6 \\1 & 7 & 2 \\9 & 6 & 1\end{array}\right)+2\left(\begin{array}{lll}5 & 4 & 9 \\7 & 4 & 0 \\6 & 9 & 8\end{array}\right) \\&=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}6 & 15 & 18 \\3 & 21 & 6 \\27 & 18 & 3\end{array}\right)+\left(\begin{array}{rrr}10 & 8 & 18 \\14 & 8 & 0 \\12 & 18 & 16\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{lll}16 & 23 & 36 \\17 & 29 & 6 \\39 & 36 & 19\end{array}\right)\left[\begin{array}{l}\text { Adding the } \\\text { corresponding } \\\text { entries }\end{array}\right]\end{aligned}\)

In MATLAB, multiplication is carried out by using the command \(^{*}\). Once the matrices \(\mathrm{B}\) and C have been entered, you do not need to enter them again. Enter \(3^{*} \mathrm{~B}+2^{*} \mathrm{C}\) to check the above evaluation.

(d) Using row by column multiplication we have

\(\begin{aligned}\mathrm{AB} &=\left(\begin{array}{lll}1 & 2 & 5 \\4 & 6 & 9\end{array}\right) \times\left(\begin{array}{lll}2 & 5 & 6 \\1 & 7 & 2 \\9 & 6 & 1\end{array}\right) \\&=\left(\begin{array}{ll}(1 \times 2)+(2 \times 1)+(5 \times 9)(1 \times 5)+(2 \times 7)+(5 \times 6) & (1 \times 6)+(2 \times 2)+(5 \times 1) \\(4 \times 2)+(6 \times 1)+(9 \times 9)(4 \times 5)+(6 \times 7)+(9 \times 6) & (4 \times 6)+(6 \times 2)+(9 \times 1)\end{array}\right) \\&=\left(\begin{array}{lll}2+2+45 & 5+14+30 & 6+4+5 \\8+6+81 & 20+42+54 & 24+12+9\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}49 & 49 & 15 \\95 & 116 & 45\end{array}\right)\end{aligned}\)

(e) BA is impossible to calculate because the number of columns in the left hand matrix, B, is notequal to the number of rows in the right hand matrix, A.
How many columns does the matrix B have?
Matrix B has three columns but matrix A has two rows. Since the number of columns of matrix B, three,does not match the number of rows of matrix A, two, we cannot evaluate BA.Notice that the matrix multiplication AB does not equal the matrix multiplication BA. In fact we havejust demonstrated that BA cannot be evaluated while AB is computed, as shown in part (d) above.It is important to note that matrix multiplication is not the same as multiplying two real numbers. Inmatrix multiplication the order of the multiplication does matter.
