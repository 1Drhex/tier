Guest Question.

A bar of length \(l\) moves in plane. In the displayed position, the velocity \(v_{A} \) of point \(A\) and the angular velocity \(\omega \) are known.

a) Determine the magnitude and direction of the velocity of point \(B\) .

b) Where on the bar is point \(C\) located, whose instantaneous velocity has the \(x\)-direction?

c) Identify the location of the instantaneous center of rotation \(Π\).

Given: \(v_{A} = 3m/s\) , \(\alpha = 60°\) , \(\omega = 6/s\) , \(l = 1/2m\) , \(a = b = 1/4 m\) .

Admin
Solution

a) From the relation \(v_{B}=v_{A}+\omega \times r_{AB} \) and

\(v_{A}=-v_{A}\cos \alpha e_{x}-v_{A}\sin \alpha e_{y}\) , \(\omega =\omega e_{z}\) , \(r_{AB}=le_{x} \) ,

\(v_{B}=-v_{A}\cos \alpha e_{x} +\left(\omega l-v_{A}\sin \alpha \right)e_{y} \rightsquigarrow \begin{cases} \begin{matrix} v_{Bx}=-1.5m/s , \\ v_{By}=+0.402m/s \end{matrix} \end{cases} \)

we obtain

\(\underline{\underline{v_{B} } }=\sqrt{v^{2}_{Bx}+v^{2}_{Bx} }=\underline{\underline{1.55 m/s} } \) , \(\cos \beta =\frac{v_{Bx} }{v_{B} } \rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{\beta =165.4°} } \) .

b) The condition \(v_{Cy} = 0\) leads with

\(v_{C}=v_{A}+\omega \times r_{AC}\)

 

\(=-v_{A}\cos \alpha e_{x}+\left(\omega _{c}-v_{A}\sin \alpha \right)e_{y} \)

to

\(\underline{\underline{c=\frac{v_{A}\sin \alpha }{\omega }=0.433 m } } \) .

c) From the condition for the instantaneous center of rotation \(Π\)

 

\(v_{Π}=r_{A}+\omega \times r_{AΠ} =0 \rightsquigarrow \omega \times v_{A}=-\omega \times \left(\omega\times r_{AΠ} \right)=\omega ^{2} r_{AΠ} \)

follows with \(r_{Π}=r_{A}+r_{AΠ} \) the location of \(Π\) :

\(r_{Π}=r_{A}+\frac{1}{\omega ^{2} }\omega \times v_{A}=\left(a+\frac{v_{A} }{\omega }\sin \alpha \right)e_{x}+\left(b-\frac{v_{A} }{\omega }\cos \alpha \right) e_{y} \) ,

\(\rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{ x_{Π}=a+\frac{v_{A} }{\omega }\sin \alpha =0.683 m } } \) , \( \underline{\underline{ y_{Π}=b-\frac{v_{A} }{\omega }\cos \alpha =0 } } \) .
