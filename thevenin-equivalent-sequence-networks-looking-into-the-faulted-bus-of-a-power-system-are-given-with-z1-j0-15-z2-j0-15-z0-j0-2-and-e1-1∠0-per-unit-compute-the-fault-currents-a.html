Guest Question.

Thévenin equivalent sequence networks looking into the faulted bus of a power system are given with \(Z_{1}=j 0.15, Z_{2}=j 0.15, Z_{0}=j 0.2\), and \(E_{1}=1 \angle 0^{\circ}\) per unit. Compute the fault currents and voltages for the following faults occurring at the faulted bus:
(a) Balanced three-phase fault
(b) Single line-to-ground fault
(c) Line-line fault
(d) Double line-to-ground fault
Which is the worst fault from the viewpoint of the fault current?

Admin
Solution

(a) Following of the text:

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\begin{gathered}\bar{I}_{1}=\frac{1 \angle 0^{\circ}}{j 0.15}=-j 6.67 ; \bar{I}_{2}=0 ; \bar{I}_{0}=0 \\\bar{V}_{0}=\bar{V}_{1}=\bar{V}_{2}=0 \\\bar{I}_{a}=6.67 \angle-90^{\circ} ; \bar{I}_{b}=6.67 \angle 150^{\circ} ; \bar{I}_{c}=6.67 \angle 30^{\circ} \leftarrow\end{gathered}\\&\left.\bar{V}_{a}=\bar{V}_{b}=\bar{V}_{c}=0 \text { (Bolted 3-phase fault }\right) \leftarrow\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Following of the text

\(\begin{gathered}\bar{I}_{0}=\bar{I}_{1}=\bar{I}_{2}=\frac{1 \angle 0^{\circ}}{j(0.15+0.15+0.2)} \\=-j 2 \\\bar{I}_{a}=3(-j 2)=-j 6 ; \bar{I}_{b}=\bar{I}_{c}=0 \quad \leftarrow\end{gathered}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{V}_{0} \\\bar{V}_{1} \\\bar{V}_{2}\end{array}\right] &=\left[\begin{array}{c}0 \\1 \angle 0^{\circ} \\0\end{array}\right]-\left[\begin{array}{ccc}j 0.2 & 0 & 0 \\0 & j 0.15 & 0 \\0 & 0 & j 0.15\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}-j 2 \\-j 2 \\-j 2\end{array}\right] \\&=\left[\begin{array}{c}-0.4 \\0.7 \\-0.3\end{array}\right]\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{V}_{a} \\\bar{V}_{b} \\\bar{V}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a^{2} & a \\1 & a & a^{2}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}-0.4 \\0.7 \\-0.3\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}0 \\1.11 \angle 233^{\circ} \\1.11 \angle 125^{\circ}\end{array}\right] \leftarrow\)

 

(c) Following of the text:

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{I}_{1} &=-\bar{I}_{2}=\frac{1 \angle 0^{\circ}}{j(0.15+0.15)} \\&=-j 3.333 \\\bar{I}_{0} &=0\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\left.\begin{array}{l}\bar{I}_{b}=(-j \sqrt{3})(-j 3.333)=-5.773 \\\bar{I}_{c}=5.773 ; \bar{I}_{a}=0\end{array}\right\} \leftarrow\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{V}_{1}=\bar{V}_{2}=\bar{I}_{1}(j 0.15)=0.5 ; \bar{V}_{0}=0\\&\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{V}_{a} \\\bar{V}_{b} \\\bar{V}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a^{2} & a \\1 & a & a^{2}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}0 \\0.5 \\0.5\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}1.0 \\-0.5 \\-0.5\end{array}\right] \leftarrow\end{aligned}\)

 

(d) Following of the text:

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{I}_{1} &=\frac{1 \angle 0^{\circ}}{j\left[0.15+\frac{0.15 \times 0.2}{0.15+0.2}\right]} \\&=-j 4.242\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{I}_{2}=(j 4.242)\left(\frac{0.2}{0.2+0.15}\right) \\&\quad=j 2.424 \\&\bar{I}_{0}=(j 4.242)\left(\frac{0.15}{0.2+0.15}\right)=j 1.818\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{a} \\\bar{I}_{b} \\\bar{I}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a^{2} & a \\1 & a & a^{2}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}j 1.818 \\-j 4.242 \\j 2.424\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}0 \\5.5 \angle 118.2^{\circ} \\5.5 \angle 61.8^{\circ}\end{array}\right] \leftarrow\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{V}_{0}=\bar{V}_{1}=\bar{V}_{2}=-j 1.818(j 0.2)=0.364\\&\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{V}_{a} \\\bar{V}_{b} \\\bar{V}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a^{2} & a \\1 & a & a^{2}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}0.364 \\0.364 \\0.364\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}1.092 \\0 \\0\end{array}\right] \leftarrow\end{aligned}\)

Worst fault: 3-phase fault with a fault current of 6.67 pu ←
