Guest Question.

Find (a) the network function \( H ( s )= V _{2} / V _{1} \) in the circuit shown in Fig. 12-13, (b) H(jω) for \( L C=2 / \omega_{0}^{2} \text { and } L / C=R^{2} \) , and (c) the magnitude and phase angle of \( H (j \omega) \text { in }(b) \text { for } \omega_{0}=1 rad / s \text {. } \).

Admin
Solution

a) Assume \( V _{2} \) is known. Use generalized impedances Ls and 1/Cs and solve for \( V _{1} \).

From \( I _{R}= V _{2} / R \),

\( V _{A}=(R+L s ) I _{R}=\frac{R+L s }{R} V _{2} \) (3)

 

\( I _{C}=C s V _{A}=\frac{\operatorname{Cs}(R+L s )}{R} V _{2} \quad \text { and } \quad I _{1}= I _{R}+ I _{C}=\frac{ V _{2}}{R}+\frac{C s (R+L s )}{R} V _{2}=\frac{1+C s (R+L s )}{R} V _{2} \)

 

Then, \( V _{1}= V _{A}+R I _{1}=\frac{R+L s }{R} V _{2}+[1+C s (R+L s )] V _{2} \)

 

and \( H ( s )=\frac{ V _{2}}{ V _{1}}=\frac{1}{2+(L / R+C R) s +L C s ^{2}} \) (4a)

 

(b) From \( L C=2 / \omega_{0}^{2} \text { and } L / C=R^{2} \text { we get } L=\sqrt{2} R / \omega_{0} \text { and } C=\sqrt{2} / R \omega_{0} \text {. } \). Substituting L and C into (4a) gives

\( H ( s )=\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{1}{1+\sqrt{2}\left( s / \omega_{0}\right)+\left( s / \omega_{0}\right)^{2}}\right) \quad \text { or } \quad H (j \omega)=\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{1}{1+j \sqrt{2}\left(\omega / \omega_{0}\right)-\left(\omega / \omega_{0}\right)^{2}}\right) \) (4b)

 

\( | H |^{2}=\frac{1}{4}\left(\frac{1}{1+\left(\omega / \omega_{0}\right)^{4}}\right) \quad \text { and } \quad \theta=-\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\sqrt{2} \omega_{0} \omega}{\omega_{0}^{2}-\omega^{2}}\right) \)

 

Note that H(jω) is independent of R. The network passes low-frequency sinusoids and rejects, or attenuates, the high-frequency sinusoids. It is a low-pass filter with a half-power frequency of \( \omega=\omega_{0} \) and, in this case, the magnitude of the frequency response is \( \left| H \left(j \omega_{0}\right)\right|=| H (0)| / \sqrt{2}=\sqrt{2} / 4 \) and its phase angle is \( \angle {H}\left(j \omega_{0}\right)=-\pi / 2 \).

(c) For \( \omega_{0}=1 \),

\( H ( s )=\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{1}{1+\sqrt{2} s + s ^{2}}\right) \quad \text { or } \quad H (j \omega)=\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{1}{1+j \sqrt{2} \omega-\omega^{2}}\right) \) (4c)

 

\( | H |^{2}=\frac{1}{4} \frac{1}{1+\omega^{4}} \quad \text { and } \quad \theta=-\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\sqrt{2} \omega}{1-\omega^{2}}\right) \)

The RC network of Fig 12-4(b) was defined as a first-order low-pass filter with half-power frequency at \( \omega_{0}=1 / R_{1} C_{2} \). The circuit of Fig. 12-13 is called a second-order Butterworth filter. It has a sharper cutoff.
