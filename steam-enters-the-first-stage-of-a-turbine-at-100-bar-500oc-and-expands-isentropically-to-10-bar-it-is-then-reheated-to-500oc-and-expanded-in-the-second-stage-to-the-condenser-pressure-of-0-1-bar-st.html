Guest Question.

Steam enters the first stage of a turbine at 100 bar, 500ºC and expands isentropically to 10 bar. It is then reheated to 500ºC and expanded in the second stage to the condenser pressure of 0.1 bar. Steam is bled from the first stage at 20 bar and fed to a closed feed water heater. Feed water leaves the closed heater at 100 bar, 200ºC (enthalpy = 856.8 kJ/kg), while the condensate is supplied to the open heater into which steam is bled at 4 bar pressure. Saturated liquid at 4 bar exits from the open heater and enters the closed heater. The net output of the turbine is 50 MW. Assuming the turbine and pump processes to be isentropic, determine the mass of steam bled at each feed water heater per kg of steam entering the first stage, the mass of steam entering the first stage per second, and the thermal efficiency. [U.P.S.C. 1995]

Admin
Solution
\(\text{From steam table, at inlet to first stage of turbine,}\\h_{1} = h_{\text{at 100 bar, 500ºC}} = 3373.7 kJ/kg\\s_{1} = s_{\text{at 100 bar, 500ºC}} = 6.5966 kJ/kg · K\\\text{Due to isentropic expansion, }s_{1} = s_{6} = s_{2} and s_{3} = s_{8} = s_{4}\\\text{State at 6 i.e. bleed state from HP turbine, Temperature by interpolation from steam table = 261.6ºC}\\\begin{aligned}\text{At inlet to second stage of turbine, }h_{6}&= 2930.572 kJ/kg\\h_{3}= h_{\text{at 10 bar, 500ºC}} &= 3478.5 kJ/kg\\s_{3}= s_{\text{at 10 bar, 500ºC}} &= 7.7622 kJ/kg · K\end{aligned}\\\text{At exit from first stage of turbine i.e. at 10 bar and entropy of 6.5966 kJ/kg . K Temperature by interpolation}\\\text{steam table at 10 bar and entropy of 6.5966 kJ/kg . K}\\\qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad \quad\begin{aligned}T_{2}&= 181.8ºC\\h_{2}&= 2782.8 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{State at 8, i.e. bleed state from second stage of expansion, i.e. at 4 bar and entropy of 7.7622 kJ/kg . K Temperature by}\\\text{ interpolation from steam table,}\\\qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad \begin{aligned}T_{8}&= 358.98ºC \simeq 359ºC\\h_{8}&= 3188.7 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{State at 4 i.e. at condenser pressure of 0.1 bar and entropy of 7.7622 kJ/kg. K the state lies in wet region.}\\\text{So let the dryness fraction be }x_{4},\\\begin{aligned}s_{4}&= s_{\text{ƒ at 0.1 bar}} + x_{4} · s_{\text{ƒg at 0.1 bar}}\\7.7622&= 0.6493 + x_{4} · 7.5009\\x_{4}&= 0.95\\h_{4}&= h_{\text{ƒ at 0.1 bar}} + x_{4} · h_{\text{ƒg at 0.1 bar}}\\&= 191.83 + (0.95 × 2392.8)\\h_{4}&= 2464.99 kJ/kg\\\text{Given,}\qquad \qquad\quad h_{11}&= 856.8 kJ/kg, h_{9} = h_{\text{ƒ at 4 bar}}\\h_{9}&= 604.74 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Considering pump work, the net output can be given as,}\\\begin{aligned}W_{net}&= W_{HPT} + W_{LPT} – (W_{CEP} + W_{FP})\\\text{where}\qquad \qquad W_{HPT}&= \left\{(h_{1} – h_{6}) + (1 – m_{6}) (h_{6} – h_{2})\right\} \text{ per kg of steam from boiler.}\\W_{LPT}&= \left\{(1 – m_{6}) + (h_{3} – h_{8}) (1 – m_{6} – m_{8}) (h_{8} – h_{4})\right\} \text{ per kg of steam from boiler.}\end{aligned}\\\text{For closed feed water heater, energy balance yields;}\\\qquad \quad m_{6} · h_{6} + h_{10} = m_{6} · condensate extraction pump, }h_{5} – h_{4^{\prime}} = v_{4^{\prime}} · Δp\\\begin{aligned}h_{5} – h_{\text{ƒ at 0.1 bar}}&= v_{\text{ƒ at 0.1 bar}} · (4 – 0.1) × 10^{2}\\h_{5} – 191.83&= (0.001010) × (3.9 × 10^{2})\\h_{5}&= 192.224 kJ/kg\\\text{For feed pump, }h_{10} – h_{9}&= v_{9} · Δp\\h_{10} – 604.74&= v_{\text{ƒ at 4 bar}} × (100 – 4) × 10^{2}\\h_{10} – 604.74&= 0.001084 × 96 × 10^{2}\\h_{10} &= 615.15 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Substituting in energy balance upon closed feed water heater,}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad m_{6} × 2701.2 + 615.15&= m_{6} × 908.79 + 856.8\\m_{6}&= \text{0.135 kg/s}\\\text{Thermal efficiency }= \textbf{54.06 }\pmb{\%}\)
