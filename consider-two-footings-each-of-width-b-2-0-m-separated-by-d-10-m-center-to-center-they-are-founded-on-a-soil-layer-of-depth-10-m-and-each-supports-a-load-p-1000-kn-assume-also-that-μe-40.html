Guest Question.

Consider two footings each of width B = 2.0 m separated by D = 10 m center to center. They are founded on a soil layer of depth 10 m and each supports a load P = 1000 kN. Assume also that \(\mu_{E}=40\) MPa, \(\sigma_{E}=40 MPa , \theta_{\ln E}=1.0\) m, and Poisson’s ratio is 0.25. If the footings support a floor or beam not attached to elements likely to be damaged by large deflection, then differential settlement is limited to D/360 = 2.8 cm.What is the probability that \(|\Delta|>2.8\) cm?

Admin
Solution

See the previous single-footing example for some of the earlier details; note, however, that the correlation length has changed in this example.

1. A deterministic finite-element analysis of this problem gives \(\delta_{ det }=0.03578\) under each footing (this number is very slightly different than that found in the single-footing case due to interactions between the two footings). For \(\sigma_{\ln E}^{2}=0.69315\), the log-settlement statistics under either footing are \(\mu_{\ln \delta}=\ln \left(\delta_{\text {det }}\right)+\frac{1}{2} \sigma_{\ln E}^{2}=-2.9838 \quad \text { and } \quad \sigma_{\ln \delta}=\sqrt{\gamma(B, H) \sigma_{\ln E}^{2}}=\sqrt{(0.055776)(0.69315)}=0.19662\).

2. To calculate \(C_{\ln \delta}\), a short program written to implement the approach presented in Appendix C gives \(C_{\ln \delta}=3.1356 \times 10^{-7}\).

3. In terms of actual settlement under each footing, the mean, standard deviation, and correlation coefficient are \(\mu_{\delta}=\exp \left\{\mu_{\ln \delta}+\frac{1}{2} \sigma_{\ln \delta}^{2}\right\}=0.051587, \sigma_{\delta}=\mu_{\delta} \sqrt{e^{\sigma_{\ln \delta}^{2}}-1}=0.010242, \text { and } \rho_{\delta}=e^{C_{\ln \delta}}-1 / e^{\sigma_{\ln \delta}^{2}}-1=7.9547 \times 10^{-6}\), respectively. A 5000-realization
simulation run for this problem gave estimates of settlement mean and standard deviation of 0.0530 and 0.0081, respectively, and an estimated correlation coefficient of −0.014 (where the negative correlation coefficient estimate is clearly due to a bias in the classical estimator; see Section 5.4.1.1 for a discussion of this issue).

4. The differential settlement \(\Delta \text { has parameters } \mu_{\Delta}=0 \quad \text { and } \quad \sigma_{\Delta}^{2}=2\left(1-7.9547 \times 10^{-6}\right)(0.010242)^{2}= 0.0002098\) and the mean absolute differential settlement in this case is predicted to be \(\mu_{|\Delta|}=\sqrt{2(0.0002098) / \pi}=0.011\). The simulation run estimated the mean absolute differential settlement to be 0.009.

5. The desired probability is predicted to be \(P[|\Delta|>0.028]=2 \Phi(-0.028 / \sqrt{0.0002098})=2 \Phi(-1.933)= 0.0532\). The empirical estimate of this probability from the simulation run is 0.0204.
