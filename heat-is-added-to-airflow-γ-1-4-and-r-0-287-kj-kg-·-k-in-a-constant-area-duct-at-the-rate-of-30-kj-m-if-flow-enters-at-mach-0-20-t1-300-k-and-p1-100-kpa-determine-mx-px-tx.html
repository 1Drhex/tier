Guest Question.

Heat is added to airflow (γ = 1.4 and R = 0.287 kJ/kg · K) in a constant-area duct at the rate of 30 kJ/m. If flow enters at Mach 0.20, \(T _{1}=300 K , \text { and } p _{1}=100\) kPa, determine M(x), p(x), T(x), and \(p _{ o }( x )\).

Admin
Solution

From isentropic relations at \(M _{1}=0.2, T _{1} / T _{01}=0.9921\). Accordingly,

 

\(T _{ o 1}=\frac{ T _{ o1 }}{ T _{1}} T _{1}=\left(\frac{1}{0.9921}\right) 300=302.3889 K\)

 

From the Rayleigh relations at \(M _{1}=0.2, T _{ o 1} / T _{ o }^{*}=0.1736\). Hence,

 

\(T _{ o }^{*}=\frac{ T _{ o }^{*}}{ T _{ o 1}} T _{ o 1}=\left(\frac{1}{0.1736}\right) 302.3889=1741.8714 K\)

 

The maximum length that the pipe may have without affecting the mass flow rate is obtained when \(T _{ oe }= T ^{*}_{o}\). Therefore from Eq.(10.10),

 

\(q=c_{p}\left(T_{o 2}-T_{o 1}\right)\) (10.10)

 

\(q _{\max }= q ^{\prime} L _{\max }= c _{ p }\left( T _{ o }^{*}- T _{ o 1}\right)\)

 

Solving for the maximum length

 

\(\begin{aligned}L _{\max } &=\frac{ c _{ p }\left( T _{ o }^{*}- T _{ o 1}\right)}{ q ^{\prime}}=\frac{\gamma R }{\gamma-1} \frac{ T _{ o }^{*}- T _{ o1 }}{ q ^{\prime}}=\frac{(1.4)(287)}{0.4}\left(\frac{1741.8714-302.3889}{30,000}\right) \\&=48.1987 m\end{aligned}\)

 

The distribution of \(T _{ o }( x )\) can also be determined from Eq.(10.10)

 

\(T _{ o }( x )= T _{ o 1}+\frac{ x }{ L _{\max }}\left( T _{ o }^{*}- T _{ o 1}\right)=302.3889+29.8656 x\)

 

Hence,

 

\(\frac{ T _{ o }( x )}{ T _{ o }^{*}}=\frac{302.3889+29.8656 x }{1741.8714}=0.1736+0.017146 x\)

 

Now for a given x, Eq.(10.14) may be used to determine M(x), i.e., letting \(t = T _{ o } / T _{ o }^{ *}\), \(b =1-\gamma( t -1) \text { and } a =1+\gamma^{2} \left(t -1\right)\) we have

 

\(\frac{T_{o}}{T_{o}^{*}}=\frac{(1+\gamma)^{2}}{\left(1+\gamma M^{2}\right)^{2}} \frac{M^{2}}{1} \frac{\left(1+\frac{\gamma-1}{2} M^{2}\right)}{\left(1+\frac{\gamma-1}{2}\right)}=\frac{(1+\gamma) M^{2}\left[2+(\gamma-1) M^{2}\right]}{\left(1+\gamma M^{2}\right)^{2}}\) (10.14)

 

\(M ^{2}=\frac{ b -\sqrt{ b ^{2}- at }}{ a }\)

 

Note the − sign in front of the radical is used to obtain a subsonic Mach number. With this Mach number it is an easy matter to obtain the static pressure and temperature distributions from Eqs.(10.8) and (10.9), respectively. The stagnation pressure distribution can be obtained from

 

\(\frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}=\frac{\left(1+\gamma M_{1}^{2}\right)}{\left(1+\gamma M_{2}^{2}\right)}\) (10.8)

 

\(\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}=\frac{M_{2}^{2}}{M_{1}^{2}} \frac{\left(1+\gamma M_{1}^{2}\right)^{2}}{\left(1+\gamma M_{2}^{2}\right)^{2}}\) (10.9)

 

\(p _{ o }( x )=\frac{ p _{ o }( x )}{ p ( x )} p ( x )= p ( x )\left(1+\frac{\gamma-1}{2} M ^{2}\right)^{\frac{\gamma}{\gamma-1}}\)
