DOWNSTREAM DEPTH IN HYDRAULIC JUMP

Water flows in a trapezoidal channel at a rate of 300 cfs. The channel has a bottom width of 10 ft and side slopes of 1 vertical to 1 horizontal. If a hydraulic jump is forced to occur where the upstream depth is 1.0 ft, what will be the downstream depth and velocity? What are the values of \(Fr _{1} \text { and } Fr _{2}\)?

Problem Definition

Situation: Known upstream conditions, hydraulic jump is forced to occur (details not described).

Find:

1. Downstream depth and velocity.

2. Values of \(Fr _{1} \text { and } Fr _{2}\).

Sketch:

Properties: Water (50Â°F), Table A.5: \(\gamma=62.4 lbf / ft ^{3}, \text { and } \rho=1.94 \text { slugs } / ft ^{3}\).

Plan

1. Find cross section, velocity, and hydraulic depth in the upstream section.

2. Find pressure in the upstream section to use for left-hand side of Eq. (15.37).

\(\bar{p}_{1} A_{1}+\rho Q V_{1}=\bar{p}_{2} A_{2}+\rho Q V_{2}\) (15.37)

3. Use channel geometry information to solve for \(y_{2}\) in right-hand side of Eq. (15.37).

4. Use Eq. (15.2) to solve for the Froude number at both sections.

\(Fr =\frac{V}{\sqrt{g L}}\) (15.2)

Admin
Solution

1. By inspection, for the upstream section, the cross-sectional flow area is 11 \(ft ^{2}\).

Therefore, the mean velocity is \(V_{1}=Q / A_{1}=27.3\) ft/s.

The hydraulic depth is \(D_{1}=A_{1} / T_{1}=11 ft ^{2} / 12 ft =0.9167\) ft.

2. The location of the centroid \((\bar{y})\) of the area \(A_{1}\) can be obtained by taking moments of the sub-areas about the water surface (see example sketch).

 

\(A_{1} \bar{y}_{1}=A_{1 A} \times 0.333 ft +A_{1 B} \times 0.500 ft +A_{1 C} \times 0.333 ft\)

 

\(\left(11 ft ^{2}\right) \bar{y}_{1}=(0.333 ft )\left(0.500 ft ^{2} \times 2\right)+(0.50 ft )\left(10.00 ft ^{2}\right)\)

 

\(\bar{y}=0.485 ft\)

 

Pressure \(p_{1}=62.4 lbf / ft ^{3} \times 0.485 ft =30.26 lbf / ft ^{2}\).

 

Therefore,

\(30.26 \times 11+1.94 \times 300 \times 27.3=\bar{p}_{2} A_{2}+\rho Q V_{2}\)

 

3. Using right-hand side of Eq. (15.37), solve for \(y_{2}\).

 

\(\bar{p}_{2} A_{2}+\rho Q V_{2}=16,221\) lbf

 

\(\gamma \bar{y}_{2} A_{2}+\frac{\rho Q^{2}}{A_{2}}=16,221\)

 

\(\bar{y}_{2}=\frac{\sum A_{i} y_{i}}{A_{2}}=\frac{B y_{2}^{2} / 2+y_{2}^{3} / 3}{A_{2}}\)

 

Using \(B=10 ft , Q=300 ft ^{2} / s\), and material properties assumed earlier,

 

\(y_{2}=5.75\) ft

 

4. Froude numbers at both sections are

 

\(Fr _{1}=\frac{V_{1}}{\sqrt{g D_{1}}}=\frac{27.3 ft / s }{\sqrt{32.2 ft / s ^{2} \times 0.9167 ft }}=5.02\)

 

\(V_{2}=\frac{Q}{A_{2}}=\frac{300}{57.5+5.75^{2}}=3.31\) ft/s

 

\(D_{2}=\frac{A_{2}}{T_{2}}=\frac{90.56}{21.5}=4.21\) ft

 

\(Fr _{2}=\frac{V}{\sqrt{g D}}=\frac{3.31}{\sqrt{32.2 \times 4.21}}=0.284\)
