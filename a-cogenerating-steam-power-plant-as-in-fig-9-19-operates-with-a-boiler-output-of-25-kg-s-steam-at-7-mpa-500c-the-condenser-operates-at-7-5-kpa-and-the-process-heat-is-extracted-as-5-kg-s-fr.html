Assume all components are ideal and find the temperature after pump 1, the total turbine output and the total process heat transfer.

Solution

Pump 1: Inlet state is saturated liquid: \(h _{1}=168.79 kJ / kg , v _{1}=0.001008 m ^{3} / kg\)

\(\begin{aligned}& w _{ P 1}=\int v dP = v _{1}\left( P _{2}- P _{1}\right)=0.001008(100-7.5)=0.093 kJ / kg \\& w _{ P1 }= h _{2}- h _{1}=> h _{2}= h _{1}+ w _{ P1 }=168.88 kJ / kg , T _{2}= 4 0 . 3 ^{\circ} C\end{aligned}\)

Turbine: \(h _{5}=3410.3 kJ / kg , \quad s _{5}=6.7974 kJ / kg K\)

\(\begin{array}{ll}P _{6}, s _{6}= s _{5} & => x _{6}=0.9952, h _{6}=2738.6 kJ / kg \\P _{7}, s _{7}= s _{5} & => x _{7}=0.8106, h _{7}=2119.0 kJ / kg\end{array}\)

From the continuity equation we have the full flow from 5 to 6 and the remainder after the extraction flow is taken out flows from 6 to 7.

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ W }_{ T }=& \dot{ m }_{5}\left( h _{5}- h _{6}\right)+0.80 \dot{ m }_{5}\left( h _{6}- h _{7}\right)=25(3410.3-2738.6) \\&+20(2738.6-2119)=16792.5+12392= 2 9 . 1 8 5 M W\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\dot{ Q }_{\text {proc }}=\dot{ m }_{6}\left( h _{6}- h _{8}\right)=5(2738.6-417.46)= 1 1 . 6 0 6 MW\)
