Guest Question.

Rigid beam ABC is supported by a pinned connection at A and by a 180 mm by 180 mm square timber post that is pin-connected at B and D, as shown in Figure P16.12. The length dimensions of the beam are \(x_{1}\) = 3.6 m and \(x_{1}\) = 2.8 m. The timber post has a length of L = 4 m and an elastic modulus of E = 12 GPa. If a factor of safety of 2.0 with respect to buckling is specified, determine the magnitude of the maximum distributed load w that may be supported by the beam.

Admin
Solution

Euler buckling load:

\(\begin{aligned}&I=\frac{(180 mm )^{4}}{12}=87.48 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4} \\&P_{c r}=\frac{\pi^{2} E I}{L^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}\left(12,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(87.48 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\right)}{(4,000 mm )^{2}}=647,544.745 N\end{aligned}\)

Allowable column load:

\(P_{\text {allow }}=\frac{P_{c r}}{F S}=\frac{647,544.745 N }{2.0}=323,772.373 N =323.772 kN ( C )\)

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Equilibrium of rigid beam ABC:

\(\begin{gathered}\Sigma M_{A}=-w(6.4 m )(3.2 m )-(-323.772 kN )(3.6 m )=0 \\\therefore w=\frac{1,165.57920 kN – m }{20.480 m ^{2}}=56.9 kN / m\end{gathered}\)
