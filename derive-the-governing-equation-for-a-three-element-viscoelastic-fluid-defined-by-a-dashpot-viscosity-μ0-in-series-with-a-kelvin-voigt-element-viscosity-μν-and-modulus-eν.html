Guest Question.

Derive the governing equation for a three-element viscoelastic fluid defined by a dashpot (viscosity \(\mu _{0}\)) in series with a Kelvin–Voigt element (viscosity \(\mu _{\nu},\) and modulus \(E_{\nu}\)).

Admin
Solution

Let the total stress and strain of the three-element model (draw it, including free body diagrams) be \(\sigma\) and \(\varepsilon ,\) respectively. By equilibrium, the stress in the single dashpot \(\sigma_{0}\) must balance the stress in the Kelvin–Voigt element \(\sigma_{\nu},\) and both must equal \(\sigma.\) Note, therefore, that

\( \sigma _{0}=\mu _{0}\dot{\varepsilon }_{0}, \sigma _{\nu }=E_{\nu }\varepsilon _{\nu }+\mu _{\nu }\dot{\varepsilon }_{\nu }.\)

Moreover, the total strain \(\varepsilon =\varepsilon _{0}+\varepsilon _{\nu },\) where the strains in the spring and dashpot of the Kelvin–Voigt element are the same. Hence, with \(\sigma =\sigma _{\nu }\) and \(\varepsilon _{\nu }=\varepsilon -\varepsilon _{0},\) we have

\( \sigma =E_{\nu }(\varepsilon -\varepsilon _{0})+\mu _{\nu }(\dot{\varepsilon }-\dot{\varepsilon }_{0} ),\)
whereby
\( \dot{\sigma }=E_{\nu }(\dot{\varepsilon } -\dot{\varepsilon } _{0})+\mu _{\nu }(\ddot{\varepsilon } -\ddot{\varepsilon } _{0} ).\)

Now, note that \(\dot{\varepsilon }_{0}=\sigma _{0}/\mu _{0}\) and thus \(\ddot{\varepsilon }_{0}=\dot{\sigma }_{0}/\mu _{0}\equiv \dot{\sigma }/\mu _{0}.\) Hence, we have

\( \dot{\sigma }=E_{\nu }\left(\dot{\varepsilon }-\frac{\sigma _{0}}{\mu _{0}} \right)+\mu _{\nu }\left(\ddot{\varepsilon }-\frac{\dot{\sigma }_{0} }{\mu _{0}} \right)\)
or
\( \dot{\sigma }\left(1+\frac{\mu _{0}}{\mu _{0}} \right)+\sigma \left(\frac{E_{\nu }}{\mu _{0}} \right)=E_{\nu }\dot{\varepsilon }+\mu _{\nu }\ddot{\varepsilon }\)

or
\( \sigma +\dot{\sigma }\left(\frac{\mu _{0}+\mu _{\nu }}{E_{\nu }} \right)=\mu _{0}\dot{\varepsilon }+\frac{\mu _{\nu }\mu _{0}}{E_{\nu }}\ddot{\varepsilon }.\)

Compare this to the three-element (standard) viscoelastic solid.
