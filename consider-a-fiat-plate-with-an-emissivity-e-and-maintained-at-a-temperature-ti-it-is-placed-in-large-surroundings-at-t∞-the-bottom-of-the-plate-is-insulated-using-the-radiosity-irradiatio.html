Guest Question.

Consider a fiat plate with an emissivity \((\varepsilon)\), and maintained at a temperature \(T_{i}\). It is placed in large surroundings at \(T_{\infty}\). The bottom of the plate is insulated. Using the radiosity-irradiation method, determine the net radiation heat transfer from the plate.

Admin
Solution

First, we enclose the plate with an imaginary hemispherical basket at \(T_{\infty}\) for which \(\varepsilon=1\) and call this as surface 2. Now, we have an enclosure in hand.

View factor matrix for the two surface enclosures = \(\left[\begin{array}{cc}F_{11} & F_{12} \\F_{21} & F_{22}\end{array}\right]\)

\(J_{1}=\varepsilon_{1} \sigma T_{1}^{4}+\left(1-\varepsilon_{1}\right)\left(F_{11} J_{1}+F_{12} J_{2}\right)\) (4.207)

\(J_{2}=\varepsilon \sigma T_{\infty}^{4}+\left(1-\varepsilon_{2}\right)\left(F_{21} J_{1}+F_{22} J_{2}\right)\) (4.208)

\(J_{2}=1 \times \sigma T_{\infty}^{4}+0 \times\left(F_{21} J_{1}+F_{22} J_{2}\right)\) (4.209)

\(J_{2}=\sigma T_{\infty}^{4}\) (4.210)

Now do we understand why we don’t have to determine \(F_{21} \text { and } F_{22} ?\)

Whenever we have surroundings like this where \(\varepsilon=1\), there is no need to waste time calculating those view factors.

\(J_{1}=\varepsilon_{1} \sigma T_{1}^{4}+\left(1-\varepsilon_{1}\right)\left(0+1 \times J_{2}\right)\) (4.211)

\(J_{1}=\varepsilon_{1} \sigma T_{1}^{4}+\left(1-\varepsilon_{1}\right) J_{2}\) (4.212)

Substituting for \(J_{2}\),

\(J_{1}=\varepsilon_{1} \sigma T_{1}^{4}+\left(1-\varepsilon_{1}\right) \sigma T_{\infty}^{4}\) (4.213)

\(G_{1}=F_{11} J_{1}+F_{12} J_{2}=\sigma T_{\infty}^{4}\) (4.214)

\(q_{1}=J_{1}-G_{1}\) (4.215)

\(q_{1}=\varepsilon_{1} \sigma T_{1}^{4}+σ T_{\infty}^{4}-\varepsilon_{1} \sigma T_{\infty}^{4}-\sigma T _{\infty}^{4}\) (4.216)

\(q_{1}=\varepsilon_{1} \sigma\left(T_{1}^{4}-T_{\infty}^{4}\right)\) (4.217)

This is one of the most used and abused formula in heat transfer. Now, having gone through this course, we must be aware of the limiting conditions under which formula is valid.

1. Single plate at a uniform temperature
2. Plate has uniform radiosity
3. Plate is characterized by one hemispherical spectral emissivity
4. Surroundings are at constant temperature
5. There is no irradiation from any other object
6. There is no other object in the vicinity

Looking at the formula, it must now be clear under which situations this formula can be used and in which ones, its use is incorrect.
