Guest Question.

Determine the shear flow distribution in the walls of the thin-walled closed section beam shown in Fig. 10.22, the wall thickness, t, is constant throughout.

Admin
Solution

Since the z axis is an axis of symmetry, \( I_{z y}=0, \text { and since } S_{z}=0 \), Eq. (10.24) reduces to

\(q_{s}=\frac{S_{y} I_{z y}-S_{z} I_{z}}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t z d s+\frac{S_{z} I_{z y}-S_{y} I_{y}}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s+q_{s, 0}\) (10.24)

 

\( q_{s}=-\left(S_{y} / I_{z}\right) \int_{0}^{s} t y d s+q_{s, 0} \)

where

\( I_{z}=\left(\pi t r^{3} / 2\right)+2 \times 2 r t \times r^{2}+\left[t(2 r)^{3} / 12\right]=6.24 t r^{3} \)

We now “cut” the beam section at 1. Any point may be chosen for the “cut” but the amount of computation will be reduced if a point is chosen which coincides with the axis of symmetry. The

\( q_{ b , 12}=-\left(S_{y} / I_{z}\right) \int_{0}^{\theta} \operatorname{tr} \sin \theta r d \theta \)

which gives

\( q_{ b , 12}=0.16\left(S_{y} / r\right)[\cos \theta]_{0}^{\theta} \)

so that

\( q_{ b , 12}=0.16\left(S_{y} / r\right)(\cos \theta-1) \) (i)

When \( \theta=\pi / 2, q_{ b , 2}=-0.16\left(S_{y} / r\right) \). The shear flow in the wall 23 is then

\( q_{ b , 23}=-\left(S_{y} / I_{z}\right) \int_{0}^{S_{1}} \operatorname{tr} d s_{1}-0.16\left(S_{y} / r\right) \)

so that

\(q_{ b , 23}=-0.16\left(S_{y} / r^{2}\right)\left(s_{1}+r\right)\) (ii)

and when \( s_{1}=2 r, q_{ b , 3}=-0.48\left(S_{y} / r\right) \). Then, in the wall 34

\( q_{ b , 34}=-0.16\left(S_{y} / t r^{3}\right) \int_{0}^{S_{2}} t\left(r-s_{2}\right) d s_{2}-0.48\left(S_{y} / r\right) \)

so that

\(q_{ b , 34}=-0.16\left(S_{y} / r\right)\left(r s_{2}-0.5 s_{2}^{2}+3 r^{2}\right)\) (iii)

The remaining distribution follows from symmetry. Now taking moments about O and using Eq. (10.26)

\( 0=\oint p q_{ b } d s+2 A q_{s, 0} \) (10.26)

 

\(0=2\left[\int_{0}^{\pi / 2} q_{ b , 12} r^{2} d \theta+\int_{0}^{2 r} q_{ b , 23} r d s_{1}+\int_{0}^{r} q_{ b , 34} 2 r d s_{2}\right]+2\left[4 r^{2}+\left(\pi r^{2} / 2\right)\right] q_{s, 0}\) (iv)

Substituting in Eq. (iv) for \(q_{ b , 12}\) etc from Eqs (i), (ii) and (iii) gives

\(q_{s, 0}=0.32 S_{y} / r\)

Adding \( q_{s, 0} \text { to the } q_{b} \) distributions of Eqs (i), (ii) and (iii) gives

\(\begin{aligned}&q_{12}=0.16\left(S_{y} / r^{3}\right)\left(r^{2} \cos \theta+r^{2}\right) \\&q_{23}=0.16\left(S_{y} / r^{3}\right)\left(r^{2}-r_{1}\right) \\&q_{34}=0.16\left(S_{y} / r^{3}\right)\left(0.5 s_{2}^{2}-r_{2}-r^{2}\right)\end{aligned}\)

Note that \( q_{23} \text { changes sign at } s_{1}=r \). The shear flow distribution in the lower half of the section follows from symmetry and the complete distribution is shown in Fig. 10.23.
