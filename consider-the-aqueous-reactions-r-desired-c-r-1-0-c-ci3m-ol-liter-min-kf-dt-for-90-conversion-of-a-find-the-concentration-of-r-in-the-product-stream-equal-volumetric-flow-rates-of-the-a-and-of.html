Guest Question.

Consider the aqueous reactions

For \(90 \%\) conversion of \(\mathrm{A}\) find the concentration of \(\mathrm{R}\) in the product stream. Equal volumetric flow rates of the A and of B streams are fed to the reactor, and each stream has a concentration of \(20 \mathrm{~mol} /\) liter of reactant. The flow in the reactor follows.

(a) Plug flow

(b) Mixed flow

(c) The best of the four plug-mixed contacting schemes of Example 7.1 .

 

Admin
Solution

As a warning, be careful to get the concentrations right when you mix streams. We show this in the three sketches for this problem. Also, the instantaneous fractional yield of the desired compound is

\(\varphi\left(\frac{\mathrm{R}}{\mathrm{A}}\right)=\frac{d C_{\mathrm{R}}}{d C_{\mathrm{R}}+d C_{\mathrm{S}}}=\frac{k_{1} C_{\mathrm{A}}^{1.5} C_{\mathrm{B}}^{0.3}}{k_{1} C_{\mathrm{A}}^{1.5} C_{\mathrm{B}}^{0.3}+k_{2} C_{\mathrm{A}}^{0.5} C_{\mathrm{B}}^{1.8}}=\frac{C_{\mathrm{A}}}{C_{\mathrm{A}}+C_{\mathrm{B}}^{1.5}}\)

Now let us proceed.

(a) Plug Flow Referring to Fig. E7.2a, noting that the starting concentration of each reactant in the combined feed is \(C_{\mathrm{A} 0}=C_{\mathrm{B} 0}=10 \mathrm{~mol} /\) liter and that \(C_{\mathrm{A}}=C_{\mathrm{B}}\) everywhere, we find from Eq. 9 that

\(\Phi_{p}=\frac{-1}{C_{\mathrm{A} 0}-C_{\mathrm{Af}}} \int \varphi d C_{\mathrm{A}}=\frac{-1}{10-1} \int_{10}^{1} \frac{C_{\mathrm{A}} d C_{\mathrm{A}}}{C_{\mathrm{A}}+C_{\mathrm{A}}^{1.5}}=\frac{1}{9} \int_{1}^{10} \frac{d C_{\mathrm{A}}}{1+C_{\mathrm{A}}^{0.5}}\)

Let \(C_{\mathrm{A}}^{0.5}=x,\) then \(C_{\mathrm{A}}=x^{2}\) and \(d C_{\mathrm{A}}=2 x d x .\) Replacing \(C_{\mathrm{A}}\) by \(x\) in the above expression gives

\(\begin{aligned}\Phi_{p} &=\frac{1}{9} \int_{1}^{\sqrt{10}} \frac{2 x d x}{1+x}=\frac{2}{9}\left[\int_{1}^{\sqrt{10}} d x-\int_{1}^{\sqrt{10}} \frac{d x}{1+x}\right] \\&=0.32 \\\therefore C_{\mathrm{Rf}} &=9(0.32)=\underline{\underline{2.86}} \\C_{\mathrm{Sf}} &=9(1-0.32)=\underline{6.14}\end{aligned}\)

(b) Mixed Flow Referring to Fig. E7.2b, we have from Eq. \(10,\) for \(C_{\mathrm{A}}=C_{\mathrm{B}}\)

\(\Phi_{m}\left(\frac{\mathrm{R}}{\mathrm{A}}\right)=\varphi_{\mathrm{at} \mathrm{exit}}=\frac{1}{1+C_{\mathrm{A}}^{0.5}}=0.5\)

Therefore Eq. 13 gives

\(\begin{array}{l}C_{\mathrm{Rf}}=9(0.5)=\underline{\underline{4.5 \mathrm{~mol} / \text { liter }}} \\C_{\mathrm{Sf}}=9(1-0.5)=\underline{4.5 \mathrm{~mol} / \text { liter }}\end{array}\)

(c) Plug Flow A – Mixed Flow \(B\) Assuming that \(\mathrm{B}\) is introduced into the reactor in such a way that \(C_{\mathrm{B}}=1 \mathrm{~mol} /\) liter throughout, we find concentrations as shown in Fig. E7.2c. Then accounting

for the changing \(C_{\mathrm{A}}\) in the reactor, we find

\(\begin{aligned}\Phi\left(\frac{\mathrm{R}}{\mathrm{A}}\right) &=\frac{-1}{C_{\mathrm{A} 0}-C_{\mathrm{Af}}} \int_{C_{\mathrm{A} 0}}^{C_{\mathrm{A} f}} \varphi d C_{\mathrm{A}}=\frac{-1}{19-1} \int_{19}^{1} \frac{C_{\mathrm{A}} d C_{\mathrm{A}}}{C_{\mathrm{A}}+(1)^{1.5}} \\&=\frac{1}{18}\left[\int_{1}^{19} d C_{\mathrm{A}}-\int_{1}^{19} \frac{d C_{\mathrm{A}}}{C_{\mathrm{A}}+1}\right]=\frac{1}{18}\left[(19-1)-\ln \frac{20}{2}\right]=0.87\end{aligned}\)

Therefore

\(\begin{array}{l}C_{\mathrm{Rf}}=9(0.87)=\underline{\underline{7.85 \mathrm{~mol} / \text { liter }}} \\C_{\mathrm{Sf}}=9(1-0.87)=\underline{\underline{1.15 \mathrm{~mol} /} \text { liter }}\end{array}\)

To summarize

For plug flow: \(\Phi\left(\frac{\mathrm{R}}{\mathrm{A}}\right)=0.32\) and \(C_{\mathrm{Rf}}=2.86 \mathrm{~mol} /\) liter

For mixed flow: \(\quad \Phi\left(\frac{\mathrm{R}}{\mathrm{A}}\right)=0.50 \quad\) and \(\quad C_{\mathrm{Rf}}=4.5 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{liter}\)

For the optimum: \(\quad \Phi\left(\frac{\mathrm{R}}{\mathrm{A}}\right)=0.87 \quad\) and \(\quad C_{\mathrm{Rf}}=7.85 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{liter}\)
