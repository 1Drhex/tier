Guest Question.

The gross power absorbed by the forward and backward field of a 230V, 4-pole, 50 Hz, single-phase induction motor is 180 W and 30 W respectively at a motor speed of 1425 rpm. Find the shaft torque if the no-load friction losses are 50W.

Admin
Solution

Here, \(V = 230 V; P = 4; f = 50 Hz; N = 1425 rpm \)

\(P_{f} = 180 W; P_{b} = 30 W; Mechanical loss = 50 W \)

 

Synchronous speed, \(N_{S} = \frac{120 f}{P} = \frac{120 \times 50 }{4} = 1500 rpm \)

Slip, \(S = \frac{N_{S} – N}{N_{S}} = \frac{1500 – 1425}{1500} = 0.05 \)

Power input to the rotor, \(P_{i\left(r\right) } = P_{f} – P_{b} = 180 – 30 = 150 W \)

Mechanical power developed, \(P_{m} = P_{i\left(r\right) } \left(1–S\right) = 150 \left(1– 0.05\right) = 142.5 W\)

Power at the shaft, \(P_{sh} = P_{m} – Mechanical loss = 142.5 – 50 = 92.5 W\)

Shaft torque, \(T_{sh} = T_{sh} = \frac{P_{sh}}{\omega } = \frac{P_{sh}}{2 \pi N / 60} = \frac{92.5 \times 60}{2 \pi \times 1425} = \mathbf{0.62 Nm} \)
