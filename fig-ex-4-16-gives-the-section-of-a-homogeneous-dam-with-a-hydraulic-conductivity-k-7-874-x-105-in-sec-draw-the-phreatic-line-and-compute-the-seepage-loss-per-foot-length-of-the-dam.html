Guest Question.

Fig. Ex. 4.16 gives the section of a homogeneous dam with a hydraulic conductivity \( k=7.874 \times 10^{-5} \) in/sec. Draw the phreatic line and compute the seepage loss per foot length of the dam.

Admin
Solution

The depth h of water on upstream side = 32.81 ft.

The projected length of slope A ‘B on the water surface = 32.81 ft.

The point A on the water level is a point on the basic parabola. Therefore

AA’ = 0.3×32.81=9.84 ft.

F is the focus of the parabola. The distance of the directrix from the focus F is

\(y_{0}=\sqrt{d^{2}+h^{2}}-d\)

where d = 68.9 ft, h = 32.81 ft. Therefore

\(y_{0}=\sqrt{(68.9)^{2}+(32.81)^{2}}-68.9=7.413 \mathrm{ft}\)

The distance of the vertex of the parabola from F is

\(F V=a_{0}=\frac{y_{0}}{2}=\frac{7.413}{2}=3.706 \mathrm{ft}\)

The (x, y) coordinates of the basic parabola may be obtained from Eq. (4.58) as

 

\(y=\sqrt{2 x y_{0}+y_{0}^{2}}\) (4.58)

 

\(x=\frac{y^{2}-y_{0}^{2}}{2 y_{0}}=\frac{y^{2}-(7.413)^{2}}{2 \times 7.413}=\frac{y^{2}-55}{14.83}\)

or \(y=\sqrt{14.83 x+55}\)

Given below are values of y for various values of x

 

x (ft) 0 15 30 45 68.9
y(ft) 7.416 16.65 22.36 26.88 32.81

 

The parabola has been constructed with the above coordinates as shown in Fig. Ex. 4.16.

From Fig. Ex. 4.16 \(\Delta a+a=24.6 \mathrm{ft}\)

From Fig. 4.23, for a slope angle \(\beta=45^{\circ}\)

\(\frac{\Delta a}{a+\Delta a}=0.35\)

or \(\Delta a=0.35(a+\Delta a)=0.35 \times 24.6=8.61 \mathrm{ft}\)

From Eq. (4.60)

 

\(q=k \frac{d y}{d x} y_{0}=k y_{0}\) (4.60)

 

\(q=k y_{0}\)

where \(k=7.874 \times 10^{-5} \mathrm{in} / \mathrm{sec} \text { or } 6.56 \times 10^{-6} \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{sec} \text { and } y_{0}=7.413 \mathrm{ft}\)

 

\(q=6.56 \times 10^{-6} \times 7.413=48.63 \times 10^{-6} \mathrm{ft}^{3} / \mathrm{sec}\) per ft length of dam.
