Guest Question.

A load of 2,700 N acts on the machine part shown in Figure P12.11a. The machine part has a uniform thickness of 12 mm (i.e., 12-mm thickness in the z direction). Determine the normal and shear stresses acting at points H and K, which are shown in detail in Figure P12.11b. For each point, show these stresses on a stress element.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&A=(12 mm )(40 mm )=480 mm ^{2} \\&I_{z}=\frac{(12 mm )(40 mm )^{3}}{12}=64,000 mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

 

Equilibrium:

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma F_{x} &=B_{x}-(2,700 N ) \cos 30^{\circ}=0 \\\Sigma F_{y} &=A_{y}+B_{y}+(2,700 N ) \sin 30^{\circ}=0 \\\Sigma M_{B} &=(2,700 N )(125 mm ) \cos 30^{\circ}-A_{y}(185 mm )=0\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&A_{y}=1,579.911 N \\&B_{x}=2,338.269 N \quad B_{y}=-2,929.911 N\end{aligned}\)

 

 

Internal forces at H:

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma F_{x} &=F_{H}=0 \\\Sigma F_{y} &=A_{y}-V_{H}=0 \quad \therefore V_{H}=1,579.911 N \\\Sigma M_{H} &=M_{H}-A_{y}(135 mm )=0 \\& \therefore M_{H}=213,288.013 N – mm\end{aligned}\)

Axial stress at H:

\(\sigma_{\text {axial }}=0 MPa\)

Shear stress at H:

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{H} &=(12 mm )(12 mm )(20 mm -6 mm )=2,016 mm ^{3} \\\tau_{H} &=\frac{(1,579.911 N )\left(2,016 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(64,000 mm ^{4}\right)(12 mm )}=4.147 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Bending stress at H:

\(\sigma_{\text {bend }}=\frac{(213,288.013 N – mm )(8 mm )}{64,000 mm ^{4}}=26.661 MPa ( T ) \quad \text { (by inspection) }\)

 

Summary of stresses at H:

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{x}=0 MPa +26.661 MPa =26.7 MPa \\&\sigma_{y}=0 MPa \\&\tau_{x y}=-4.15 MPa\end{aligned}\)

 

 

Internal forces at K:

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma F_{x}=B_{x}-V_{K}=0 \quad \therefore V_{K}=2,338.269 N \\&\Sigma F_{y}=A_{y}+B_{y}+F_{K}=0 \quad \therefore F_{K}=1,350 N \\&\Sigma M_{K}=M_{K}-A_{y}(185 mm )+B_{x}(50 mm )=0 \\&\therefore M_{K}=175,370.144 N – mm\end{aligned}\)

 

Axial stress at K:

\(\sigma_{\text {axial }}=\frac{1,350 N }{480 mm ^{2}}=2.813 MPa ( T )\)

Shear stress at K:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q_{K}=(12 mm )(15 mm )(20 mm -7.5 mm )=2,250 mm ^{3} \\&\tau_{K}=\frac{(2,338.269 N )\left(2,250 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(64,000 mm ^{4}\right)(12 mm )}=6.850 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Bending stress at K:

\(\sigma_{\text {bend }}=\frac{(175,370.144 N – mm )(5 mm )}{64,000 mm ^{4}}=13.701 MPa ( C ) \quad \text { (by inspection) }\)

 

Summary of stresses at K:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=0 MPa \\\sigma_{y} &=2.813 MPa -13.701 MPa =-10.89 MPa \\\tau_{x y} &=-6.85 MPa\end{aligned}\)
