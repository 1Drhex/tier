Guest Question.

A chimney stack of mass \(m\) and length \(l\) is blown up at the base and falls over.

Assuming a hinged support at the base and a constant mass distribution, determine the stress resultants during the motion. At which point \(x _{B}\) and at which angle \(\varphi _{B}\) the maximum transmissible moment \(M_{B}=mgh/100 \) is exceeded?
Calculate the normal force at this point.

Admin
Solution

We first determine the angular acceleration \(\ddot{\varphi } \) and angular velocity \(\dot{\varphi } \) of the chimney. With \(\Theta _{A}=m h^{2}/3 \) , the principle of angular momentum leads to

\(\overset{\curvearrowright }{A}:\Theta _{A}\ddot{\varphi } =mg\frac{h}{2}\sin \varphi \rightsquigarrow \ddot{\varphi }=\frac{3}{2}\frac{g}{h}\sin \varphi \) .

To calculate \(\dot{\varphi } \) it is practical to use the energy conservation law:

\(-mg\frac{h}{2}\left(1-\cos \varphi \right)+\frac{1}{2}\Theta _{A} \dot{\varphi }^{2}=0 \rightsquigarrow \dot{\varphi }^{2}=3\frac{g}{h}\left(1-\cos \varphi \right) \) .

Now we cut the system, introduce the stress resultants and formulate the equations of motion for the cut part:

\(\nwarrow :\bar{m} \ddot{y}_{\bar{C} }=V-\bar{m}g\sin \varphi \) ,

\(\nearrow :\bar{m} \ddot{x}_{\bar{C} }=-N-\bar{m}g\cos \varphi \) ,

\(\overset{\curvearrowright }{\bar{C} }:\Theta _{\bar{C} }\ddot{\varphi }=M+\frac{1}{2}\left(h-x\right)V \) .

The unknown accelerations \(\ddot{x}_{\bar{C} } , \ddot{y}_{\bar{C} }\) therein can be calculated from the known quantities \(\ddot{\varphi } \) and \(\dot{\varphi }^{2} \) :

\(\ddot{x}_{\bar{C} }=-x_{\bar{C}} \dot{\varphi }^{2}=-\frac{h+x}{2}3\frac{g}{h}\left(1-\cos \varphi \right)=-\frac{3}{2}g \left(1+\frac{x}{h} \right)\left(1-\cos \varphi \right) \) ,

\(\ddot{y}_{\bar{C} }=-x_{\bar{C}} \ddot{\varphi }=-\frac{h+x}{2}\frac{3}{2} \frac{g}{h}\sin \varphi =-\frac{3}{4}g \left(1+\frac{x}{h} \right)\sin \varphi \) .

Thus, with

\(\bar{m}=\frac{h-x}{h}x=\left(1-\frac{x}{h} \right)m \) , \(\Theta _{\bar{C}} =\frac{\bar{m} \left(h-x\right)^{2} }{12}x=\frac{mh^{2} }{12}\left(1-\frac{x}{h} \right)^{3} \) ,

we obtain the stress resultants

\(\underline{\underline{V\left(x\right) } } =\bar{m} \left(\ddot{y}_{\bar{C} }+g\sin \varphi \right)=mg\left(1-\frac{x}{h} \right)\left[-\frac{3}{4}\left(1+\frac{x}{h} \right)\sin \varphi +\sin \varphi \right]\)

 

\(\underline{\underline{=\frac{mg}{4}\sin \varphi \left(1-\frac{x}{h} \right) \left(1-3\frac{x}{h} \right) } } \) ,

\(\underline{\underline{N\left(x\right) } }=-\bar{m}\left(\ddot{x} _{\bar{C} }+g\cos \varphi \right) \)

 

\(=\underline{\underline{-mg\left(1-\frac{x}{h} \right)\left[-\frac{3}{2}\left(1+\frac{x}{h} \right)\left(1-\cos\varphi \right)+\cos \varphi \right] } } \) ,

\(\underline{\underline{M\left(x\right) } }=\Theta _{\bar{C} } \ddot{\varphi } -\frac{h}{2}\left(1-\frac{x}{h} \right)\left[\bar{m}\ddot{y} _{\bar{C} }+\bar{m}g\sin \varphi \right] \)

 

\(=\frac{mh^{2} }{12}\left(1-\frac{x}{h} \right)^{3}\frac{3}{2}\frac{g}{h}\sin \varphi -\frac{h}{2}mg\left(1-\frac{x}{h} \right)^{2} \left[-\frac{3}{4}\left(1+\frac{x}{h} \right)+1 \right]\sin \varphi \)

 

\(=\underline{\underline{\frac{mgh}{4}\sin \varphi \left(1-\frac{x}{h} \right)^{2}\frac{x}{h} } }\) .

The bending moment has its maximum, where the shear force is zero.
From \(V = 0\) follows

\(\left(1-\frac{x}{h} \right)\left(1-3\frac{x}{h} \right)=0 \rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{x_{B}=\frac{h}{3} } } \)

(The 2nd solution \(x = h\) is not of interest because the bending moment is zero at this point). This leads to the maximum bending moment

\(M_{max} =M\left(x_{B} \right)=\frac{1}{27}mgh\sin \varphi \) .

From the condition \(M_{max}= M_{B}\) the angle \(\varphi _{B} \) follows:

\(\frac{mgh}{27}\sin \varphi _{B}=\frac{mgh}{100} \rightsquigarrow \sin \varphi _{B} =\frac{27}{100}\rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{\varphi _{B}=15.66° } }\) .

Finally, the normal force at \(x _{B}\) and at the angle \(\varphi _{B}\) is obtained as

\(\underline{\underline{N\left(\frac{h}{3},\varphi _{B} \right) } }=-\frac{2mg}{3}\left[-\frac{3}{2}\frac{4}{3}\left(1-\cos \varphi _{B} \right)+\cos \varphi _{B} \right] \)

 

\(=-\frac{2}{3}mg\left(-2+3\cos \varphi _{B} \right) =\underline{\underline{-0.59mg} } \) .
