Guest Question.

Air enters the turbocharger compressor (see Fig. P8.51), of an automotive engine at 100 kPa, 30°C, and exits at 200 kPa. The air is cooled by 50°C in an intercooler before entering the engine. The isentropic efficiency of the compressor is 75%. Determine the temperature of the air entering the engine and the irreversibility of the compression-cooling process.

Admin
Solution

a) Compressor. First ideal which is reversible adiabatic, constant s:

\(T _{2 S }= T _{1}\left(\frac{ P _{2}}{ P _{1}}\right)^{\frac{ k -1}{ k }}=303.15\left(\frac{200}{100}\right)^{0.2857}=369.5 K\)

 

\(w _{ S }= C _{ P 0}\left( T _{1}- T _{2 S }\right)=1.004(303.15-369.5)=-66.565 kJ / kg\)

 

Now the actual compressor

\(\begin{array}{c}w = w _{ S } / \eta_{ S }=-66.565 / 0.75=-88.75 kJ / kg = C _{ P }\left( T _{1}- T _{2}\right) \\\Rightarrow T _{2}=391.55 K\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}T _{3}(\text { to engine }) &= T _{2}-\Delta T _{ INTERCOOLER }=391.55-50 \\&=341.55 K = 6 8 . 4 ^{\circ} C\end{aligned}\)

 

b) Irreversibility from Eq.8.15 with rev. work from Eq.8.14, \(\left( q =0 \text { at } T _{ H }\right)\)

\(s _{3}- s _{1}=1.004 \ln \left(\frac{341.55}{303.15}\right)-0.287 \ln \left(\frac{200}{100}\right)=-0.0792 \frac{ kJ }{ kg K }\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}i &= T \left( s _{3}- s _{1}\right)-\left( h _{3}- h _{1}\right)- w \quad\left[ w ^{ rev }=\left( h _{1}- Ts _{1}\right)-\left( h _{3}- Ts _{3}\right)\right] \\&= T \left( s _{3}- s _{1}\right)- C _{ P }\left( T _{3}- T _{1}\right)- C _{ P }\left( T _{1}- T _{2}\right) \\&=303.15(-0.0792)-1.004(-50)=+26.2 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)
