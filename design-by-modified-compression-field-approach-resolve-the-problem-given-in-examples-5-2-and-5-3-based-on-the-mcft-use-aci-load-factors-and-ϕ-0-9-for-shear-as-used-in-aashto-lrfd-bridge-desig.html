Guest Question.

Design by modified compression field approach. Resolve the problem given in and based on the MCFT. Use ACI load factors and ϕ = 0.9 for shear, as used in AASHTO LRFD Bridge Design Specifications (Ref. 5.12). Assume an aggregate size \(a_g of \frac{3}{4}\) in.

The critical section for shear is located a distance \(d_v\) = 19.8 in. = 1.65 ft from the support where \(V_u = 94 − 9.4 \times 1.65 = 78.5 kips\). Calculating 0.125\(f_c^{\prime} b_v d_v = 0.125 \times 4000 \times 16 \times 19.8 = 158,400 lb\) leads to maximum stirrup spacing equal to the smallest of 0.8\(d_v = 0.8 \times 19.8\) = 15.8 in., 24 in., and [for No. 3 (No. 10) stirrups]

\(s_{max}=\frac{A_v f_{yt}}{\sqrt{f_c^{\prime}}b_v }=\frac{0.22\times60,000}{\sqrt{4000}\times16 }=13.0 in. \)

Using Eq. (5.28),

\(\epsilon _s=\frac{ \left|M_u\right| /d_v-0.5N_u+ \left|V_u\right| }{E_s A_s} \leq 0.006\)

the strain in the longitudinal tension steel is approximated as

\(\epsilon _s=\frac{\left|M_u\right| /19.8+\left|V_u\right| }{29,000\times7.62} \)

with \(M_u and V_u\) in in-kips and kips, respectively.

These values are used to calculate θ using Eq. (5.30) and β using Eqs. (5.29) and (5.32) for sections with and without minimum stirrups, respectively.

\(\theta =29+3500\epsilon _s\) (5.30)

\(\beta =\frac{4.8}{1+750\epsilon _s} \) (5.29)

\(\beta =\frac{4.8}{1+750\epsilon _s} \frac{51}{39+s_{xe}} \) (5.32)

Where the section meets the minimum stirrup criterion, the values of β are used to calculate the values of \(V_c\), which are then used, along with the values of θ, to calculate \(V_s\) and the required stirrup spacing s (see Table 5.2).

For transverse reinforcement less than the minimum, the values of β are based on \(\epsilon_{s}\) and \(s_x\). The latter may be taken as the lesser of \(d_v\) or the spacing of longitudinal crack control reinforcement. In this case, \(d_v\) = 19.8 in. controls since crack control reinforcement is not used. The equivalent crack spacing parameter \(s_{xe} = s_x because a_g\) = 0.75 in. These values of β are used to determine the point where \(ϕ V_c /2 ≥ V_u\), the point at which stirrups may be terminated (Table 5.2). The values of \(V_u, ϕ V_c\) with at least minimum stirrups, and \(ϕ V_c /2\) for less than minimum stirrups are plotted in Fig. 5.22a. The following stirrup spacings can be used for this case:

1space at 6 in. = 6 in.

For this example, \(V_s\) is selected based on \(V_u\) at each point, not the minimum \(V_u\) on a crack with angle θ. This simplifies the design procedure and results in a somewhat more conservative design. Even so, only 7 No. 3 (No. 10) stirrups are needed, or 9 stirrups if the stirrups are continued at the maximum spacing through the middle region of the beam. These values compare favorably with the minimum number of stirrups per half-span, 11 and 14, previously calculated () using the two methods required by the ACI Code. The resulting stirrup pattern is shown in Fig. 5.22b.

The MCFT recognizes that shear increases the force in the flexural steel, although, as explained earlier, the maximum tensile force in the steel is not affected. Equation (5.31) should be used to calculate the tensile force T along the span, which will then govern the locations where tensile steel may be terminated.

\(A_s f_y\geq T=\frac{\left|M_u\right| }{\phi _f}-\frac{0.5N_u}{\phi _c}+\left(\frac{\left|V_u\right| }{\phi _v}-0.5V_s \right)\cot \theta \)

This will be discussed further in Chapter 6.
