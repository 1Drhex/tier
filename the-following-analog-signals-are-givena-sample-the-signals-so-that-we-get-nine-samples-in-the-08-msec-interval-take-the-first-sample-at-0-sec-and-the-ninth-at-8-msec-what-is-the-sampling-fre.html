Guest Question.

The following analog signals are given:

\(x_{1}(t)= \begin{cases} \cos \left\lgroup\frac{\pi }{4m sec}t \right\rgroup,t \in [0,8]m sec \\ 0,t\notin [0,8]m sec\end{cases}\) and \(x_{2}(t)= \begin{cases} -1,t \in [0,8]m sec \\ 0,t\notin [0,8]m sec\end{cases} \)

a. Sample the signals so that we get nine samples in the [0,8] msec interval. (Take the first sample at 0 sec, and the ninth at 8 msec.) What is the sampling frequency, \(f_{s'}\), and what are the exact values of the respective samples?

b. Assuming 3-bit and 4-bit rounding quantization, respectively, for level coding by arithmetic with two’s complement, what are the respective values of the samples of both signals after the quantization?

Admin
Solution

The analog signal \(x_{1}(t)\) is written: \(x_{1}(t)=\cos \left\lgroup\frac{\pi }{4msec}t \right\rgroup \)\(=\cos \left\lgroup\frac{2\pi }{8msec}t \right\rgroup\) for t ∈[0,8msec]. So the period of the signal is T = 8m sec.

In order to have 9 samples in the interval [0,8msec], sampling frequency should be \(T_{s}=\frac {1}{8}T=\frac {1}{8}.8msec.\) Thus \(f_{s}=\frac {1}{T_{s}}=\frac {1}{1msec}=1KHZ.\)

The exact values of the samples of both signals are:

\(x_{1}(0)=\cos (\frac{\pi }{4}.0 )=1,x_{1}(1)=\cos (\frac{\pi }{4}.1 )=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2},x_{1}(2)=\cos (\frac{\pi }{4}.2 )=0\)

\(x_{1}(3)=\cos (\frac{\pi }{4}.3 )=-\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2},x_{1}(4)=\cos (\frac{\pi }{4}.4 )=-1,x_{1}(5)=\cos (\frac{\pi }{4}.5 )=-\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\)

\(x_{1}(6)=\cos (\frac{\pi }{4}.6 )=0,x_{1}(7)=\cos (\frac{\pi }{4}.7 )=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\kappa \alpha \iota x_{1}(8)=\cos (\frac{\pi }{4}.8 )=1\)

For the second signal, we have:

\(x_{2} (0) = x_{2} (1) = x_{2} (2) = x_{2} (3) = x_{2} (4) = x_{2} (5) = x_{2} (6) = x_{2} (7) = x_{2} (8) = −1.\)

b. For quantization of 3 bits level ⇒ \(2^{3} = 8\) levels So, the two signals after the output of the 3-bit quantizer will have the following values:

\(\hat{x_{1}} (0)=0.75, \hat{x_{1}} (1)=0.75, \hat{x_{1}} (2)=0,\hat{x_{1}} (3)=-0.75, \hat{x_{1}} (4)=-1,\hat{x_{1}} (5)=-0.75, \hat{x_{1}} (6)=0,\hat{x_{1}} (7)=0.75\kappa \alpha \iota \hat{x_{1}} (8)=0.75.\)

whereas

\(\hat{x_{2}} (0)=\hat{x_{2}} (1)=\hat{x_{2}} (2)=\hat{x_{2}} (3)=\hat{x_{2}} (4)=\hat{x_{2}} (5)=\hat{x_{2}} (6)=\hat{x_{2}} (7)=\hat{x_{2}} (8)=-1.\)

For quantization of 4 bits, there are ⇒ \(2^{4} = 16\) levels, and the quantization step is

\(q=\frac{2|x_{max}| }{2^{4}}=\frac{2.1}{2^{4}}=2^{-3}=0.125\)

Therefore, the two signals at the output of the 4-bit quantizer will have the following values:

\(\hat{x}'_{1}(0)=0.875, \hat{x}'_{1}(1)=0.75,\hat{x}'_{1}(2)=0, \hat{x}'_{1}(3) =-0.75,\hat{x}'_{1}(4)=-1, \hat{x}'_{1}(5) =-0.75,\hat{x}'_{1}(6) =0,\hat{x}'_{1}(7)=0.75 \quad and\quad \hat{x}'_{1}(8)=0.875,\)

whereas

\(\hat{x}'_{2}(0)=\hat{x}'_{2}(1)=\hat{x}'_{2}(2)=\hat{x}'_{2}(3)=\hat{x}'_{2}(4)=\hat{x}'_{2}(5)=\hat{x}'_{2}(6)=\hat{x}'_{2}(7) =\hat{x}'_{2}(8) =-1.\)
