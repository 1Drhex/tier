Statically Determinate Truss

Given: The truss shown in Fig. E12.9a.

Determine: The joint displacements, reactions, and member forces using the Displacement Method.

\(A_{(1)}=A_{(2)}=A_{(3)}=A=2 \text { in. }^{2}, \alpha=6.5 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} F\), and E = 29,000 ksi.

(a) Due to the loading shown

(b) Due to temperature decrease of 40 \({ }^{\circ} F\) for all members.

Admin
Solution

We start with the geometric data. The topological and geometric information is listed below.

0.36 0.64 0.48 0.6 0.8 53.13 (1)

We determine the individual member matrices using (12.33).

\( k _{AA }= k _{BB }= k =\left(\frac{A E} {L}\right)\left[\begin{array}{cc} \cos ^{2} \alpha & \sin \alpha \cos \alpha \\ \sin \alpha \cos \alpha& \sin ^{2} \alpha \end{array}\right] \) (12.33)

 

\( k _{AB }= k _{ BA }=- k =-\left(\frac{A E} {L}\right)\left[\begin{array}{cc} \cos ^{2} \alpha & \sin \alpha \cos \alpha \\ \sin \alpha \cos \alpha & \sin ^{2} \alpha \end{array}\right] \) (12.33)

 

\( k _{(m) AA }= k _{(m) BB }= k _{(m)}=\left(\frac{A E} {L}\right)_{(m)}\left[\begin{array}{cc} \cos ^{2} \alpha_{(m)} & \sin \alpha_{(m)} \cos \alpha_{(m)} \\ \sin \alpha_{(m)} \cos \alpha_{(m)} & \sin ^{2} \alpha_{(m)} \end{array}\right] \)

 

\( k _{(m) AB }= k _{(m) BA }=- k _{(m)}=-\left(\frac{A E} {L}\right)_{(m)}\left[\begin{array}{cc} \cos ^{2} \alpha_{(m)} & \sin \alpha_{(m)} \cos \alpha_{(m)} \\ \sin \alpha_{(m)} \cos \alpha_{(m)} & \sin ^{2} \alpha_{(m)} \end{array}\right] \)

The system stiffness matrix and displacement vector have the following form:

\( K =\left[\begin{array}{ccc} k _{(1) AA } & k _{(1) AB } & 0 \\ k _{(1) BA } & k _{(1) BB } & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]+\left[\begin{array}{ccc} k _{(2) AA } & 0 & k _{(2) AB } \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \\ k _{(2) BA } & 0 & k _{(2) BB } \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{ccc} 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & k _{(3) AA } & k _{(3) AB } \\ 0 & k _{(3) BA } & k _{(3) BB } \end{array}\right] \)

 

\( =\left[\begin{array}{ccc} \left( k _{(1) AA }+ k _{(2) AA }\right) & k _{(1) AB } & k _{(2) AB } \\ k _{(1) BA } & \left( k _{(1) BB }+ k _{(3) AA }\right) & k _{(3) AB } \\ k _{(2) BA } & k _{(3) BA } & \left( k _{(2)} BB + k _{(3) BB }\right) \end{array}\right] \)

 

\( U =\left\{\begin{array}{l} U _{ 1 } \\ U _{ 2 } \\ U _{ 3 } \end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{l} u_{1} \\ v_{1} \\ u_{2} \\ v_{2} \\ u_{3} \\ v_{3} \end{array}\right\} \)

By substituting for individual matrices using (12.33), the system stiffness matrix takes the following form:

\( K =\left(\begin{array}{cccccc} 259.62 & 116 & -87 & -116 & -172.62 & 0 \\ 116 & 154.66 & -116 & -154.66 & 0 & 0 \\ -87 & -116 & 193.79 & 9.21 & -106.79 & 106.79 \\ -116 & -154.66 & 9.21 & 261.457 & 106.79 & -106.79 \\ -172.62 & 0 & -106.9 & 106.79 & 279.409 & -106.79 \\ 0 & 0 & 106.9 & -106.79 & -106.79 & 106.79 \end{array}\right) \)

We note that node 1 is fully fixed but node 3 is partially fixed. We rearrange the rows and columns of K, and the rows of U. This step leads to the resulting partitioned vectors and matrices listed below.

\( U ^{\prime}=\left\{\begin{array}{l} u_{2} \\ v_{2} \\ u_{3} \end{array}\right\} \quad U ^{\prime \prime}=\left\{\begin{array}{l} u_{1} \\ v_{1} \\ v_{3} \end{array}\right\} \)

 

\( K _{11}^{\prime}=\left(\begin{array}{ccc} 193.79 & 9.21 & -106.79 \\ 9.21 & 261.46 & 106.79 \\ -106.79 & 106.79 & 279.41 \end{array}\right) \)

 

\( K _{12}^{\prime}=\left(\begin{array}{ccc} -87 & -116 & 106.79 \\ -116 & -154.66 & -106.79 \\ -172.62 & 0 & -106.79 \end{array}\right) \)

 

\( K _{21}^{\prime}=\left(\begin{array}{ccc} -87 & -116 & -172.62 \\ -116 & -154.66 & 0 \\ 106.79 & -106.79 & -106.79 \end{array}\right) \)

 

\( K _{22}^{\prime}=\left(\begin{array}{ccc} 259.62 & 116 & 0 \\ 116 & 154.66 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 106.79 \end{array}\right) \)

We need the inverse of \(K'_{11}\) to solve for the displacement due to a given loading. Its form is

\( \left( K _{11}^{\prime}\right)^{-1}=\left(\begin{array}{ccc} 0.007 & -0.002 & 0.003 \\ -0.002 & 0.005 & -0.002 \\ 0.003 & -0.002 & 0.006 \end{array}\right) \)

Part (a): Loading shown in Fig. E12.9a
The system load vectors corresponding to this loading are

\( P _{ E }^{\prime}=\left\{\begin{array}{l} -6 \\ -10 \\ 0 \end{array}\right\} \quad P _{ I }^{\prime}= P _{ I }^{\prime \prime}=0 \)

We determine the unknown displacements using

\( U ^{\prime}=\left(K _{11}^{\prime}\right)^{-1}\left(P _{E}^{\prime}\right) \Rightarrow\left\{\begin{array}{l} u_{2} \\ v_{2} \\ u_{3} \end{array}\right\}=\left( K _{11}^{\prime}\right)^{-1}\left( P _{E }^{\prime}\right)=\left\{\begin{array}{c} -0.026 \\ -0.039 \\ 0.005 \end{array}\right\} \)

Given the displacements, one determines the reactions with

\( P _{E}^{\prime \prime}= K _{21}^{\prime} U ^{\prime} \Rightarrow\left\{\begin{array}{l} R_{1 x } \\ R_{1 y} \\ R_{3 y} \end{array}\right\}= K _{21}^{\prime} U ^{\prime}=\left\{\begin{array}{r} 6 \\ 9.14 \\ 0.86 \end{array}\right\} \)

Finally, we determine member forces:

\( F_{\ell_{(m)} B }=\left(\frac{A E} {L}\right)_{(m)}\left[\cos \alpha_{(m)} \sin \alpha_{(m)}\right]\left( U _{n^{+}}- U _{n^{-}}\right)+F_{\ell_{(m)^{ B }}}^{ F } \)

 

\( F_{\ell_{(1)} B }=241.7\left[\begin{array}{ll} 0.6 & 0.8 \end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l} -0.026 \\ -0.039 \end{array}\right\}+0=-11.43 kip \)

 

\( F_{\ell_{(2)} B }=172.6\left[\begin{array}{ll} 1 & 0 \end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l} 0.005 \\ 0 \end{array}\right\}+0=+0.86 kip \)

 

\( F_{\ell_{(3)} B }=213.6\left[\begin{array}{ll} -0.707 & 0.707 \end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l} (-0.026-0.005) \\ -0.039 \end{array}\right\}+0=-1.21 kip \)

Part (b): ΔT = –40° for all members

We determine the fixed end actions caused by the temperature decrease (Figs. E12.9c and E12.9d).

\( F_{l(m)}^{ F }=E A \alpha \Delta T=(29,000)(2)\left(6.5 \times 10^{-6}\right)(40)=15.08 kip \)

The system load vectors corresponding to these loads are

\( P _{ E }^{\prime}=0 \quad P _{I }^{\prime}=\left\{\begin{array}{l} -1.61 \\ 22.73 \\ 25.74 \end{array}\right\} \quad P _{ I }^{\prime \prime}=\left\{\begin{array}{l} -24.13 \\ -12.06 \\ -10.66 \end{array}\right\} \)

We determine the unknown displacements using

\(U' = (K_{11}^{'})^{-1} (-P_{I}^{'}) ⇒\)

 

\( \left\{\begin{array}{l} u_{2} \\ v_{2} \\ u_{3} \end{array}\right\}=\left(K _{11}^{\prime}\right)^{-1}\left(- P _{I }^{\prime}\right)=\left\{\begin{array}{rr} -0.037 \text { in. } \\ -0.05 \text { in. } \\ -0.087 \text { in. } \end{array}\right\} \Rightarrow\left\{\begin{array}{l} u_{2}=0.037 \text { in. } \leftarrow \\ v_{2}=0.05 \text { in. } \downarrow \\ u_{3}=0.087 \text { in. } \leftarrow \end{array}\right. \)

Given the displacements, one determines the reactions. For a determinate truss, the reactions will be zero. Also the member forces will be zero.

\( P^{′′} _{ E }= K^{′} _{21} U^{′}+ P^{′′} _{ I } ⇒ \begin{Bmatrix} R_{1x} \\ R_{1y} \\ R_{3y} \end{Bmatrix} = K^{′} _{21} U^{′}+ P^{′′} _{ I } =\begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{Bmatrix} \)

 

\( F_{\ell_{(m)} B }=\left(\frac{A E} {L}\right)_{(m)}\left[\cos \alpha_{(m)} \sin \alpha_{(m)}\right]\left( U _{n^{+}}- U _{n^{-}}\right)+F_{\ell_{(m)} B }^{ F } \)

 

\(F_{\ell_{(1)} B } = F_{\ell_{(2)} B } = F_{\ell_{(3)} B } =0 \)
