Guest Question.

Determine the voltage across the inductor for the network in Fig. 18.23.

Admin
Solution

Steps 1 and 2 are as indicated in Fig. 18.24.

Step 3: Note Fig. 18.25 for the application of Kirchhoff’s current law to node \(V_1\):

\(\sum{I_i}=\sum{I_o}\\[0.5cm] 0 = I_1 + I_2 + I_3\\[0.5cm] \frac{V_1-E}{Z_1}+\frac{V_1}{Z_2}+\frac{V_1-V_2}{Z_3}=0 \)

Rearranging terms gives

\(V_1\left[\frac{1}{Z_1}+\frac{1}{Z_2}+\frac{1}{Z_3} \right]-V_2\left[\frac{1}{Z_3} \right]=\frac{E_1}{Z_1} \) (18.1)

Note Fig. 18.26 for the application of Kirchhoff’s current law to node \(V_2\).

\(0 = I_3 + I_4 + I\\[0.5cm] \frac{V_2-V_1}{Z_3}+\frac{V_2}{Z_4}+I=0 \)

Rearranging terms gives

\(V_2\left[\frac{1}{Z_3}+\frac{1}{Z_4} \right] -V_1\left[\frac{1}{Z_3} \right] =-I\) (18.2)

Grouping equations 18.1 and 18.2 gives

\(V_1\left[\frac{1}{Z_1}+\frac{1}{Z_2}+\frac{1}{Z_3} \right]-V_2\left[\frac{1}{Z_3} \right]=\frac{E}{Z_1}\\[0.5cm]\underline{ V_1\left[\frac{1}{Z_3} \right]\hspace{30 pt} \qquad -V_2\left[\frac{1}{Z_3} +\frac{1}{Z_4}\right] =I}\\[0.5cm] \frac{1}{Z_1}+\frac{1}{Z_2}+\frac{1}{Z_3}=\frac{1}{0.5 kΩ}+\frac{1}{j 10 kΩ}+\frac{1}{2 kΩ}=2.5 mS ∠-2.29°\\[0.5cm] \frac{1}{Z_3}+\frac{1}{Z_4}=\frac{1}{2 kΩ}+\frac{1}{-j 5 kΩ}=0.539 mS ∠21.80°\\[0.5cm] \)

and

\( V_1[2.5 mS ∠-2.29°] – V_2[0.5 mS ∠0°] = 24 mA ∠0°\\[0.5cm] \underline{V_1[0.5 mS ∠0°]- V_2[0.539 mS ∠21.80°] = 4 mA ∠0°}\)

with

\(V_1=\frac{\left|\begin{matrix} 24 mA ∠0° & -0.5 mS ∠0° \\ 4 mA ∠0° & -0.539 mS ∠21.80° \end{matrix} \right| }{\left|\begin{matrix} 2.5 mS ∠-2.29° & -0.5 mS ∠0° \\ 0.5 mS ∠0° & -0.539 mS ∠21.80° \end{matrix} \right| } \\[0.5cm] =\frac{(24 mA ∠0°)(-0.539 mS ∠21.80°) + (0.5 mS ∠0°)(4 mA ∠0°)}{(2.5 mS ∠-2.29°)(-0.539 mS ∠21.80°) + (0.5 mS ∠0°)(0.5 mS ∠0°)}\\[0.5cm] =\frac{-12.94 \times10^{-6}V∠21.80° +2\times10^{-6}V∠0°}{-1.348 \times10^{-6}∠19.51° + 0.25 \times10^{-6}∠0°} \\[0.5cm] =\frac{-(12.01 + j 4.81) \times10^{-6}V+2\times10^{-6}V}{-(1.271 + j 0.45) \times10^{-6}+0.25\times10^{-6}}\\[0.5cm] =\frac{-10.01 V – j 4.81 V}{-1.021 – j 0.45}=\frac{11.106 V ∠-154.33°}{1.116 ∠-156.21°}\\[0.5cm] V_1=9.95V\angle 1.88° \)
