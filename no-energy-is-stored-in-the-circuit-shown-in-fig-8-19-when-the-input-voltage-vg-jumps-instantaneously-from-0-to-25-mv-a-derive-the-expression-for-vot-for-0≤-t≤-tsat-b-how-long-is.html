Guest Question.

No energy is stored in the circuit shown in Fig. 8.19 when the input voltage \(v_{g}\) jumps instantaneously from 0 to 25 mV.

a) Derive the expression for\(v_{o}\left(t\right)\) for \( 0≤ t≤ t_{sat}\).

b) How long is it before the circuit saturates?

Admin
Solution

a) Figure 8.19 indicates that the amplifier scaling factors are

\(\frac{1}{R_{1}C_{1}}= \frac{1000}{\left(250\right)\left(0.1\right)}=40 \),

\(\frac{1}{R_{2}C_{2}}= \frac{1000}{\left(500\right)\left(1\right)}=2 \).

Now, because \(v_{g}=25 mV\) for t> 0 Eq. 8.75 \(\frac{d^{2}v_{o}}{dt^{2}}=\frac{1}{R_{1}C_{1}}\frac{1}{R_{2}C_{2}}v_{g}\).becomes

\(\frac{d^{2}v_{o}}{dt^{2}}=\left(40\right)\left(2\right)\left(25\times 10^{-3}\right)=2\).

To solve for\(v_{o}\) , we let

\(g\left(t\right)=\frac{dv_{o}}{dt}\),

then,

\(\frac{dg\left(t\right)}{dt}=2\), and \(dg\left(t\right)=2dt\).

Hence

\(\int _{g\left(0\right)}^{g\left(t\right)}{dy}=2\int _{0}^{t}{dx}\),

from which

\(g\left(t\right)-g\left(0\right)=2t\).
However,

\(g\left(0\right)=\frac{dv_{o}\left(0\right)}{dt}\),

because the energy stored in the circuit initially is zero, and the op amps are ideal. (See Problem 8.57.) Then,

\(\frac{dv_{o}}{dt}=2t\), and \(v_{o}=t^{2}+v_{o}\left(0\right)\).

But \(v_{o}\left(0\right)=0\),so the experssion for \(v_{o}\) becomes

\(v_{o}=t^{2}\), \( 0≤ t≤ t_{sat}\).

b) The second integrating amplifier saturates when \(v_{o}\) reaches 9 V or t=3s. But it is possible that the first integrating amplifier saturates before t=3s.To explore this possibility, use Eq. 8.71 \(\frac{dv_{o1}}{dt}=-\frac{1}{R_{1}C_{1}}v_{g}\). to find :

\(\frac{dv_{o1}}{dt}=-40\left(25\right)\times 10^{-3}=-1\).

Solving for \(v_{o1}\)yields

\(v_{o1}=-t\).

Thus, at t=3s, \(v_{o1}=-3 V\), because the power supply voltage on the first integrating amplifier is ±5 V, the circuit reaches saturation when the second amplifier saturates. When one of the op amps saturates, we no longer can use the linear model to predict the behavior of the circuit.
