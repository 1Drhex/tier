Guest Question.

Verify, in accordance with EC3, the splice connection in Figure E5.2.1 (dimensions in millimetres), which connects the end of two members of the chord of a trussed beam and transfers a design axial tension load \(N_{Ed}\) of 2250 kN (506 kips).The flanges of the beam are composed by 340 × 16mm (13.4 × 0.63 in.) plates and a plate 260 × 12mm (10.2 × 0.472 in.) forms the beam web. Single cover plates 340 × 16mm (13.4 × 0.63 in.) are bolted to the beam flange in normal holes (\(d_{0}\)= 26mm= 1.02 in.).
Material:
S235−EN10025−2 \(f_{y}\)= 235 MPa(34 ksi) \(f_{u}\)= 355 MPa (51.5 ksi)

Admin
Solution

Area of the chord:
\(A_{b} =2b_{f}.t_{f} + b_{w}.t_{w} = 2 × 340 × 16 + 260 × 12 = 14000 mm^{2}(21.7 in.^{2})\)
Area of the cover plates:
\(A_{g} = 2 × 340 × 16 = 10880 mm^{2}(15.88 in.^{2})\)
Bolt diameter: d =24mm (0.945 in.)
Standard hole: \(d_{0}\) =26 mm(1.02 in.)
Verification for plastic collapse. Calculate the tensile yield strength of the cover plates (being their area lower than the one of the H-shaped profile):
\(N_{pl,Rd}=\frac{A_{f},f_{y}}{\gamma _{M_{0}}}=\frac{10880\times 235}{1.00}.10^{-3}=2556.8kN(575 kips)\)
Check:\(N_{Ed} = 2250kN≤N_{pl,Rd}\)= 2556.8 kN(506 < 575 kips) OK
Verification for brittle collapse. Due to the presence of staggered holes (Figure E5.2.2), the total area to deduce to the gross resisting area has to be considered the minimum between (Eq. (5.6)):

\(t\cdot n\cdot d_{0}-\sum \frac{s^{2}\cdot t}{4\cdot p}\) …(5.6)

\(2 ×(26 × 16) = 832 mm^{2}(1.29 in.^{2})\)
and \(t.n.d_{0}-\sum \frac{s^{2}.t}{4.p}=\left [ 4×(26×16) −\left ( 2×\frac{80^{2}×16}{4×80} \right ) \right ]=1024 mm^{2}(1.587in.^{2})\)
\(A_{net} = 10880−2 × 1024 = 8832 mm^{2}(13.69 in.^{2})\)
It should be noted that coefficient 2 is due to the presence of two cover plates.
\(N_{u,Rd}=0.9\frac{A_{net}.f_{u}}{\gamma _{M2}}=0.9\times \frac{8832 × 355}{1 25}.10^{-3}=2257.5KN (507.5kips)\)
Check:\(N_{Ed} = 2250 kN≤N_{u,Rd}\) = 2257.5 kN (506 < 507.5 kips) OK
