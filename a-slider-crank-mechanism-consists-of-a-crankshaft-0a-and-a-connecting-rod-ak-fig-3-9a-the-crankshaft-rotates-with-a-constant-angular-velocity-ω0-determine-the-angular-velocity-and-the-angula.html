Guest Question.

A slider crank mechanism consists of a crankshaft \(\overline{0A} \) and a connecting rod \(\overline{AK} \) (Fig. 3.9a). The crankshaft rotates with a constant angular velocity \(\omega _{0}\).

Determine the angular velocity and the angular acceleration of the connecting rod as well as the velocity and the acceleration of the piston K in an arbitrary position.

Admin
Solution

We choose an x, y-coordinate system and the angles α and \(\varphi \) as shown in Fig. 3.9b. The piston K can move only in the horizontal direction. Therefore, its vertical displacement is zero:

\(y_{K}=r\sin \alpha -l\sin \varphi =0\) . (a)

This yields the relation

\(\sin \varphi =\frac{r}{l}\sin \alpha \longrightarrow \cos \varphi =\sqrt{1-\frac{r^{2} }{l^{2} }\sin ^{2} \alpha }\) (b)

between the angles α and \(\varphi \). If we differentiate (a), we obtain the angular velocity \(\dot{\varphi } \) and the angular acceleration \(\ddot{\varphi } \) of the connecting rod \(\left(\dot{\alpha } =\omega _{0}=const \right) \):

\(\dot{y}_{K}=r\omega _{0}\cos \alpha -l\dot{\varphi }\cos \varphi =\)0

\(\rightarrow \dot{\varphi } =\omega _{0}\frac{r}{l}\frac{\cos \alpha }{\cos \varphi }\),

\(\ddot{y}_{K}=-r\omega ^{2}_{0} \sin \alpha +l\dot{\varphi }^{2} \sin \varphi -l\ddot{\varphi }\cos \varphi =\)0

\(\rightarrow \ddot{\varphi } =-\omega ^{2}_{0}\frac{r}{l} \frac{\sin \alpha }{\cos \varphi }+\dot{\varphi }^{2}\frac{\sin \varphi }{\cos \varphi }=\omega ^{2}_{0}\frac{r}{l}\left[-\frac{\sin \alpha }{\cos \varphi }+\frac{r}{l} \frac{\cos ^{2}\alpha \sin \varphi }{\cos ^{3}\varphi } \right] \) .

The velocity \(\dot{x}_{K} \) and the acceleration \(\ddot{x}_{K} \) of the piston follow from the position \(x_{K} \) and (b):

\(x_{K} =r\cos \alpha +l\cos \varphi\),

\(\dot{x}_{K}=-r\omega _{0}\sin \alpha -l\dot{\varphi }\sin \varphi =-r\omega _{0} \sin \alpha \left[1+\frac{r}{l}\frac{\cos \alpha }{\cos \varphi } \right]\) ,

\(\ddot{x}_{K}=-r\omega ^{2}_{0} \cos \alpha -l\dot{\varphi }^{2}\cos \varphi -l\ddot{\varphi }\sin \varphi =-r\omega ^{2}_{0}\left[\cos \alpha -\frac{r}{l} \left(\frac{\sin ^{2} \alpha }{\cos \varphi } -\frac{\cos ^{2} \alpha }{\cos ^{3} \varphi } \right) \right]\).

The angle \(\varphi \) can be replaced by the angle α with the aid of (b). If the velocity and the acceleration have to be determined as functions of the time t (instead of functions of the angle α), α has to be replaced by \(\alpha =\omega _{0}t\), where the initial condition α = 0 for t = 0 has been assumed.
