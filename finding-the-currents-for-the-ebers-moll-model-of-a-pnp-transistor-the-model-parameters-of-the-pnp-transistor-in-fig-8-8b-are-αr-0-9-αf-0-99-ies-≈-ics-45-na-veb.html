Guest Question.

Finding the currents for the Ebers–Moll model of a pnp transistor The model parameters of the pnp transistor in Fig. 8.8(b) are \( \alpha_{ R }=0.9, \alpha_{ F }=0.99, I_{ ES } \approx I_{ CS }=45 nA , V_{ EB }=0.4 V , \text { and } V_{ CB }=0.3 V \). Determine (a) the currents for the transport version of the Ebers–Moll model in Fig. D.2(a) and (b) \( \beta_{ F }=\beta_{\text {forced }} \). Assume thermal voltage \( V_{ T }=25.8 mV \).

Admin
Solution

For \( V_{ EB }=0.4 V \text { and } V_{ CB }=0.3 V \), the collector–base and emitter–base junctions are forward biased. Therefore, the transistor is operated in the saturation region.
(a) For a pnp transistor, all the polarities of voltages and currents are reversed; thus, Eqs. (D.3) and (D.4) become

\(I_{ E }=-I_{ F }+\alpha_{ R } I_{ R }\) (D.3)

 

\(I_C =\alpha_{ F } I_{ ES }\left(\exp \frac{V_{ BE }}{V_{ T }}-1\right)-I_{ CS }\left(\exp \frac{V_{ BC }}{V_{ T }}-1\right)\) (D.4)

 

\(I_{ E }=I_{ ES }\left(\exp \frac{V_{ EB }}{V_{ T }}-1\right)-\alpha_{ R } I_{ CS }\left(\exp \frac{V_{ CB }}{V_{ T }}-1\right)\) (D.12)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&=45 \times 10^{-9}\left(\exp \frac{0.4}{25.8 m }-1\right)-0.9 \times 45 \times 10^{-9}\left(\exp \frac{0.3}{25.8 m }-1\right) \\&=243.48 mA -4.54 mA =238.94 mA\end{aligned}\)

 

and \( I_{ C }=-\alpha_{ F } I_{ ES }\left(\exp \frac{V_{ EB }}{V_{ T }}-1\right)+I_{ CS }\left(\exp \frac{V_{ CB }}{V_{ T }}-1\right) \) (D.13)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&=-0.99 \times 45 \times 10^{-9}\left(\exp \frac{0.4}{25.8 m }-1\right)+45 \times 10^{-9}\left(\exp \frac{0.3}{25.8 m }-1\right) \\&=-241.04 mA +5.05 mA =-235.99 mA \\\text { Thus, } \quad I_{ B } &=-\left(I_{ E }+I_{ C }\right)=-(238.94-235.99)=-2.95 mA\end{aligned}\)

(b) Since the transistor is operated in the saturation region, the value of the forward current gain becomes less than that of the active region. The forward current gain is known as the forced current gain \( \beta_{\text {forced }} \):

 

\(\beta_{\text {forced }}=\frac{I_{ C }}{I_{ B }}=\frac{235.99 mA }{2.95 mA }=80\)
