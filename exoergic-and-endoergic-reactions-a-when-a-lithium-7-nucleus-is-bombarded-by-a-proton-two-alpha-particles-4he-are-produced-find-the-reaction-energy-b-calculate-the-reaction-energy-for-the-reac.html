Guest Question.

EXOERGIC AND ENDOERGIC REACTIONS

(a) When a lithium-7 nucleus is bombarded by a proton, two alpha particles (\({ }^{4} \mathrm{He}\)) are produced. Find the reaction energy. (b) Calculate the reaction energy for the reaction \({ }_{2}^{4} \mathrm{He}+{ }_{7}^{14} \mathrm{~N} \longrightarrow{ }_{8}^{17} \mathrm{O}+{ }_{1}^{1} \mathrm{H}\).

Admin
Solution

IDENTIFY and SET UP:

The reaction energy \(Q\) for any nuclear reaction equals \(c^2\) times the difference between the total initial mass and the total final mass, as in Eq. (43.23). Table 43.2 gives the required masses.

\(Q=\left(M_{A}+M_{B}-M_{C}-M_{D}\right) c^{2}\) (43.23)

EXECUTE:

(a) The reaction is \({ }_{1}^{1} \mathrm{H}+{ }_{3}^{7} \mathrm{Li} \rightarrow{ }_{2}^{4} \mathrm{He}+{ }_{2}^{4} \mathrm{He}\). The initial and final masses and their respective sums are

\(\begin{array}{llll}A:{ }_{1}^{1} \mathrm{H} & 1.007825 \mathrm{u} & C:{ }_{2}^{4}\mathrm{He} & 4.002603 \mathrm{u} \\B:{ }_{3}^{7} \mathrm{Li} & \underline{7.016005\mathrm{u}} & D:{ }_{2}^{4} \mathrm{He} & \underline{4.002603\mathrm{u}} \\\end{array}\)

. 8.023830 u 8.005206 u

The mass decreases by 0.018624 u. From Eq. (43.23), the reaction energy is

\(Q=(0.018624 \mathrm{u})(931.5 \mathrm{MeV} / \mathrm{u})=+17.35 \mathrm{MeV}\)

(b) The initial and final masses are

\(\begin{array}{lrlr}A:{ }_{2}^{4} \mathrm{He} & 4.002603 \mathrm{u} & C:{ }_{8}^{17}\mathrm{O} & 16.999132 \mathrm{u} \\B:{ }_{7}^{14} \mathrm{~N} & 14.003074 \mathrm{u} & D:{ }_{1}^{1} \mathrm{H} & 1.007825 \mathrm{u} \\\hline\end{array}\)

. 18.005677 u 18.006957 u

The mass increases by 0.001280 u, and the corresponding reaction energy is

\(Q=(-0.001280 \mathrm{u})(931.5 \mathrm{MeV} / \mathrm{u})=-1.192 \mathrm{MeV}\)

 

EVALUATE: The reaction in part (a) is exoergic: The final total kinetic energy of the two separating alpha particles is 17.35 MeV greater than the initial total kinetic energy of the proton and the lithium nucleus. The reaction in part (b) is endoergic: In the center-of-mass system—that is, in a head-on collision with zero total momentum—the minimum total initial kinetic energy required for this reaction to occur is 1.192 MeV.
