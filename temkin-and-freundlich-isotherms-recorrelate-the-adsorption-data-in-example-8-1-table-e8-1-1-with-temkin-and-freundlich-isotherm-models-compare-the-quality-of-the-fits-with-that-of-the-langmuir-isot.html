Guest Question.

Temkin and Freundlich Isotherms

Recorrelate the adsorption data in , Table E8.1.1, with Temkin and Freundlich isotherm models. Compare the quality of the fits with that of the Langmuir isotherm fit.

Admin
Solution

We first look at the power-law model (Freundlich isotherm). Substituting Eq. (8.38) \(\theta_{A}=\frac{n_{A S}}{n_{A S \infty}}=\frac{C_{S A}}{C_{S A \infty}} \), into Eq. (8.53) \(\theta_{A}=c C_{A}^{m}\). , we obtain

\(\theta_{A}=c^{\prime} C_{A}^{m}\). (8.55)

Therefore we can fit the experimental data with Eq. (8.55) \(\theta_{A}=c^{\prime} C_{A}^{m}\). to check if the model can reasonably describe the experimental data.
Since the power-law model is not valid at high coverages, we perform the least square fits on a spreadsheet program either with the highest coverage point removed or kept. The solutions are illustrated in Tables E8.2.1 and E8.2.2.

One can observe from Table 8.2.1 that the Freundlich model fit is not as good as the Langmuir fit as shown in . The deviation of the model with the experimental data is higher at the high coverage region. Fig. E8.2.1 shows the visual quality of the fit. Visual inspection of the data shown in Fig. E8.2.1 reinforces our assessment based on Table 8.2.1.

One can observe from Table E8.2.2 and Fig. E8.2.1 that the Freundlich model fit with the highest coverage data removed has improved quality of fit. Within the region of fit, the Freundlich isotherm fit and the Langmuir isotherm fit are of similar quality, that is both within the apparent experimental data error.

We next examine the Temkin isotherm fit to the same data. Substituting Eq. (8.38) \(\theta_{A}=\frac{n_{A S}}{n_{A S \infty}}=\frac{C_{S A}}{C_{S A \infty}} \), into Eq. (8.54) \(\theta_{A} \approx \frac{\ln \left(K_{A} C_{A} e^{f_{T}}\right)}{f_{T}}\), we obtain

\(C_{S A}=f_{\text {Tm }} \ln \left(K_{A T} C_{A}\right)\), (8.56)

where \(f_{T m}=\left(R T / E_{m a x}\right) C_{S A \infty} \text { and } K_{A T}=K_{A} \exp \left(E_{\max } / R T\right)=K_{A}^{0} \exp \left[\left(-\Delta H_{\text {ad }}+E_{\text {max }}\right) / R T\right. \text { . }\) Since the Temkin isotherm is only valid in the intermediate coverage region, we remove the zero coverage data from consideration. Table E8.2.3 shows the least square fit using the Temkin isotherm to the experimental data. Visual representation of the fit as compared with Langmuir isotherm fit is shown in Fig. E8.2.2.

One can observe from Table E8.2.3 and Fig. E8.2.2 that the Temkin model fit is consistent with the experimental data. Within the region of fit, the Temkin isotherm fit and the Langmuir isotherm fit are of similar quality, both within the apparent experimental data error.
