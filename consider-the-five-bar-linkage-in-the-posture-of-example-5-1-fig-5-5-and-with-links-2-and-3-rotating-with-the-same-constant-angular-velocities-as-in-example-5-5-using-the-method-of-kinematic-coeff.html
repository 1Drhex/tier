Guest Question.

Consider the five-bar linkage in the posture of (Fig. 5.5), and with links 2 and 3 rotating with the same constant angular velocities as in . Using the method of kinematic coefficients, determine the angular velocities of coupler links 4 and 5, and the velocity of coupler point P.

Admin
Solution

We begin the analysis by writing the loop-closure equation as we did in , and separating the real and imaginary parts as in Eqs. (3) and (4) of that example. As we demonstrated there, those equations can be solved for the position variables, θ4 and θ5. Next, we take the partial derivatives of Eqs. (3) and (4) of with respect to independent variable θ2; that is,

\(r_{2} \cos \theta_{2}+r_{4} \cos \theta_{4} -r_{5} \cos \theta_{5}-r_{3} \cos \theta_{3} – r_1=0,\) (3)

\(r_{2} \sin \theta_{2}+r_{4} \sin \theta_{4} -r_{5} \sin \theta_{5}-r_{3} \sin \theta_{3}=0.\) (4)

\(-r_{2} \sin \theta_{2}-r_{4} \sin \theta_{4} \theta_{42}^{\prime}+r_{5} \sin \theta_{5} \theta_{52}^{\prime}=0,\) (1a)

\(r_{2} \cos \theta_{2}+r_{4} \cos \theta_{4} \theta_{42}^{\prime}-r_{5} \cos \theta_{5} \theta_{52}^{\prime}=0.\) (1b)

Rearranging these two equations and expressing them in matrix form gives

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc}-r_{4} \sin \theta_{4} & r_{5} \sin \theta_{5} \\r_{4} \cos \theta_{4} & -r_{5} \cos\theta_{5}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}\theta_{42}^{\prime}\\\theta_{52}^{\prime}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}r_{2} \sin \theta_{2} \\-r_{2}\cos \theta_{2}\end{array}\right].\) (2)

 

Now we can solve this matrix equation for the first-order kinematic coefficients \(\theta_{42}^{\prime} \text { and } \theta_{52}^{\prime} \text {. }\) Using the data obtained so far, the results are \(\theta_{42}^{\prime}=-0.237 \text { and } \theta_{52}^{\prime}=0.456,\)which are both dimensionless (rad/rad).

Similarly, taking the partial derivative of Eqs. (3) and (4) of with respect to independent variable θ3, and rearranging the resulting equations into matrix form, gives

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc}-r_{4} \sin \theta_{4} & r_{5} \sin \theta_{5} \\r_{4} \cos \theta_{4} & -r_{5} \cos \theta_{5}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}\theta_{43}^{\prime} \\\theta_{53}^{\prime}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}-r_{3} \sin \theta_{3} \\r_{3} \cos \theta_{3}\end{array}\right]\)

(3)

 

We can solve this matrix equation for the first-order kinematic coefficients \( \theta_{43}^{\prime} \text { and } \theta_{53}^{\prime} \text {. }\)

The results are \(\theta_{43}^{\prime}=0.705 rad / rad \text { and } \theta_{53}^{\prime}=-0.109 rad / rad.\)

As indicated in Sec. 4.12, it is not a coincidence that the (2×2) coefficient matrices in Eqs. (2) and (3) are identical. This is always true for all sets of derivative equations determined by differentiating loop-closure equations. This coefficient matrix is called the Jacobian of the system. In this example, the determinant of the Jacobian is

\(\Delta=r_{4} r_{5} \sin \left(\theta_{4}-\theta_{5}\right).\) (4)

At the current posture, this determinant has a value of Δ = −32.72 in2 and does not cause numeric difficulty. However, we note that this determinant becomes zero when θ5 = θ4 or when \(\theta_{5}=\theta_{4} \pm 180^{\circ}.\) This occurs when the coupler links are either fully extended or folded on top of each other—that is, when the five-bar linkage is in the posture of a quadrilateral with the two coupler links aligned. The kinematic coefficients become indeterminate in such a posture.

The angular velocities of links 4 and 5 can be determined from Eq. (5.4). At the posture shown in Fig. 5.1, the values are

\(\omega_{i}=d \theta_{i} / d t=\theta_{i j}^{\prime} \omega_{j}+\theta_{i k}^{\prime} \omega_{k}\) (5.4)

\(\omega_{4}=\theta_{42}^{\prime} \omega_{2}+\theta_{43}^{\prime} \omega_{3}=-0.237(10.0 rad / s )+0.705(-5.0 rad / s )=-5.90 rad / s ( cw ),\)

 

\(\omega_{5}=\theta_{52}^{\prime} \omega_{2}+\theta_{53}^{\prime} \omega_{3}=0.456(10.0 rad / s )-0.109(-5.0 rad / s )=5.10 rad / s ( ccw ).\)

 

These confirm the results found in both and .
