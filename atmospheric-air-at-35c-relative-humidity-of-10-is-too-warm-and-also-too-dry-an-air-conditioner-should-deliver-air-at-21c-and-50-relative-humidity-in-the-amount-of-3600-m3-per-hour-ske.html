Guest Question.

Atmospheric air at 35°C, relative humidity of 10%, is too warm and also too dry. An air conditioner should deliver air at 21°C and 50% relative humidity in the amount of 3600 \(m ^{3}\) per hour. Sketch a setup to accomplish this, find any amount of liquid (at 20°C) that is needed or discarded and any heat transfer.

Admin
Solution

CV air conditioner. First we must check if water should be added or subtracted. We can know this from the absolute humidity ratio.

Properties from the tables and formulas

State 1:

\(\begin{aligned}& P _{ g 35}=5.628 kPa ; P _{ v 1}=0.10 \times 5.628 kPa =0.5628 kPa \\& w _{1}=0.622 \times 0.5628 /(101.325-0.5628)=0.003474\end{aligned}\)

State 2:

\(\begin{aligned}& P _{ g 21}=2.505 kPa ; P _{ v 2}=0.5 \times 2.505 kPa =1.253 kPa \\& w _{2}=0.622 \times 1.253 /(101.325-1.253)=0.007785\end{aligned}\)

As w goes up we must add liquid water. Now we get

Continuity Eq.: \(\dot{ m }_{ air }\left(1+ w _{1}\right)+\dot{ m }_{ liq }=\dot{ m }_{ air }\left(1+ w _{2}\right)\)

Energy Eq.: \(\dot{ m }_{ air } \tilde{ h }_{1}+\dot{ m }_{ liq } h _{ f }+\dot{ Q }_{ CV }=\dot{ m }_{ air } \tilde{ h }_{2}\)

For the liquid flow we need the air mass flowrate out, \(3600 m ^{3} / h =1 m ^{3} / s\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{ m }_{\text {air }}= P _{ a 2} \dot{ V } / RT =(101.325-1.253) 1 / 0.287 \times 294.15=1.185 kg / s \\&\dot{ m }_{ liq }=\dot{ m }_{ air }\left( w _{2}- w _{1}\right)=0.00511 kg / s = 1 8 . 4 kg / h\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\dot{ Q }_{ CV }=\dot{ m }_{\text {air }}\left[ C _{ p a }\left( T _{2}- T _{1}\right)+ w _{2} h _{ v 2}- w _{1} h _{ v 1}\right]-\dot{ m }_{ liq } h _{ f }\)

= 1.185 [ 1.004 (21 – 35) + 0.007785 × 2539.9 – 0.003474 × 2565.3]
– 0.00511 × 83.96 = – 4.21 kW

If from psychrometric chart.

Inlet: \(w _{1}=0.0030, \tilde{ h }_{1}=63.0, h _{ f , 20}=83.96 kJ / kg\)

Exit: \(w _{2}=0.0076, \quad \tilde{ h }_{2}=60.2 kJ / kg \text { dry air, } P _{ v 2}, \dot{ m }_{\text {air }} \text { same as above }\)

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ Q }_{ CV }=& \dot{ m }_{ air }\left(\tilde{ h }_{2}-\tilde{ h }_{1}\right)-\dot{ m }_{ liq } h _{ f }=1.185(60.2-63)-0.00511 \times 83.96 \\&=-3.74 kW\end{aligned}\)
