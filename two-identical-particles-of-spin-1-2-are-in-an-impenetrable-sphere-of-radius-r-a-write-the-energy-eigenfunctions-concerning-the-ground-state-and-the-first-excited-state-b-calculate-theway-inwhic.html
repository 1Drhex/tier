Guest Question.

Two identical particles of spin \(\frac{1}{2}\) are in an impenetrable sphere of radius \(R .\)

(a) Write the energy eigenfunctions concerning the ground state and the first excited state.

(b) Calculate the way in which the degeneracy is modified if the following interaction occurs:

\(V=\alpha \mathbf{S}_{1} \cdot \mathbf{S}_{2}\)

where \(\mathbf{S}_{1}\) and \(\mathbf{S}_{2}\) are the two particles’ spins.

 

Admin
Solution

In the case of a single spin \(\frac{1}{2}\) particle in an impenetrable sphere

\(\psi_{n, \ell, m, m_{s}}(r, \theta, \varphi)=\psi_{n, \ell, m}(r, \theta, \varphi) \chi_{m_{s}}\)

where \(\chi_{m_{s}}\) are the \(S_{z}\) eigenstates corresponding to eigenvalues \(m_{s} \hbar=\pm \frac{\hbar}{2}\) and

\(\psi_{n, \ell, m}(r, \theta, \varphi)=R_{n, \ell}(r) Y_{\ell}^{m}(\theta, \varphi)\)

where

\(R_{n, \ell}(r)=N j_{\ell}\left(k_{n, \ell} r\right) \quad \text { and } \quad k_{n, \ell}=\frac{z_{n, \ell}}{R}\)

Here, \(j_{\ell}\) is the \(\ell\) th spherical Bessel function, while \(z_{n, \ell}\) is the \(n\) th zero of \(j_{\ell} . k_{n, \ell}\) is related to the energy eigenvalue \(E_{n, \ell}\) through the relation

\(E_{n, \ell}=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} k_{n, \ell}^{2}\)

The ground state is not degenerate and is obtained for \(\ell=0\) and \(n=1\)

\(k_{1,0}=\frac{\pi}{R}, \quad E_{1,0}=\frac{\hbar^{2} \pi^{2}}{2 m R^{2}}\)

The first excited state is obtained for \(\ell=1\) and \(n=1\) and is three times degenerate \((m=0,\pm 1)\)

Now we come to the case of two spin \(\frac{1}{2}\) particles.

(a) The two fermions’ wave function must be completely antisymmetric for particle exchange. In the case of the ground state, there is only one possibility, corresponding to the singlet state for the total spin, \(\chi_{S^{2}, S_{z}}=\chi_{0,0} .\) So, we have

\(\psi_{1, s=0}\left(\mathbf{r}_{1}, \mathbf{r}_{2}\right)=\psi_{1,0,0}\left(\mathbf{r}_{1}\right), \psi_{1,0,0}\left(\mathbf{r}_{2}\right) \chi_{0,0}\)

The first excited level of the system must have one particle in the ground state and the other in the first excited state. This allows both the singlet state

\(\psi_{2, m, s=0}\left(\mathbf{r}_{1}, \mathbf{r}_{2}\right)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left[\psi_{1,0,0}\left(\mathbf{r}_{1}\right), \psi_{1,1, m}\left(\mathbf{r}_{2}\right)+\psi_{1,1, m}\left(\mathbf{r}_{1}\right), \psi_{1,0,0}\left(\mathbf{r}_{2}\right)\right] \chi_{0,0}\)

which has 3 possible determinations in correspondence of the 3 values of \(m=\) \(0,\pm 1,\) and the triplet state

\(\psi_{2, m, s=1}\left(\mathbf{r}_{1}, \mathbf{r}_{2}\right)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left[\psi_{1,0,0}\left(\mathbf{r}_{1}\right), \psi_{1,1, m}\left(\mathbf{r}_{2}\right)-\psi_{1,1, m}\left(\mathbf{r}_{1}\right), \psi_{1,0,0}\left(\mathbf{r}_{2}\right)\right] \chi_{1, m_{s}}\)

which has 9 possible determinations at the 3 values of \(m=0,\pm 1\) and the 3 values of \(m_{s}=0,\pm 1\)

(b) The interaction \(V=\alpha \mathbf{S}_{1} \cdot \mathbf{S}_{2}\) can be rewritten in the form

\(V=\frac{\alpha}{2}\left(S^{2}-S_{1}^{2}-S_{2}^{2}\right)=\frac{\alpha}{2}\left(S^{2}-\frac{3}{2} \hbar^{2}\right)\)

In the singlet states, \(V=-\frac{3}{4} \alpha \hbar^{2},\) while, in the triplet states, \(V=\frac{1}{4} \alpha \hbar^{2} .\) So, there is a different contribution to the energy eigenvalue, depending on the total spin state. With regard to degeneracy, it is still absent in the case of the ground state with a change in the energy value. Degeneracy is only present in the case of the first excited state, which now gives rise to two distinct levels:

\(\begin{array}{l}E_{1, s=0}=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m R^{2}}\left(z_{1,1}^{2}+\pi^{2}\right)-\frac{3}{4} \alpha \hbar^{2} \quad 3 \text { times degenerate } \\E_{1, s=1}=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m R^{2}}\left(z_{1,1}^{2}+\pi^{2}\right)+\frac{1}{4} \alpha \hbar^{2} \quad 9 \text { times degenerate }\end{array}\)

where \(z_{1,1}=4.49341\)
