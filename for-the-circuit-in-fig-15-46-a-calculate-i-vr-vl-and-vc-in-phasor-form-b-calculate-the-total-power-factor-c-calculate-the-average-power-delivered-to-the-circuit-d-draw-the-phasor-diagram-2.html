Guest Question.

For the series R-L circuit in Fig. 15.64.

a. Determine the frequency at which \(X_L = R\).

b. Develop a mental image of the change in total impedance with frequency without doing any calculations.

c. Find the total impedance at f = 100 Hz and 40 kHz, and compare your answer with the assumptions of part (b).

d. Plot the curve of \(V_L\) versus frequency.

e. Find the phase angle of the total impedance at f = 40 kHz. Can the circuit be considered inductive at this frequency? Why?

Admin
Solution

a. \( X_L = 2\pi f_1L = R\\[0.5cm] and\qquad f_1=\frac{R}{2\pi L}=\frac{2 kΩ}{2\pi (40 mH)}=7957.7 Hz \)

 

b. At low frequencies, \(R > X_L\) and the impedance will be very close to that of the resistor, or 2 kΩ. As the frequency increases, \(X_L\) increases to a point where it is the predominant factor. The result is that the curve starts almost horizontal at 2 kΩ and then increases linearly to very high levels.

 

c. \(Z_T = R + jX_L = Z_T ∠\theta_T =\sqrt{R^2+X^2_L}∠\tan^{-1}\frac{X_L}{R}\\[0.5cm] At\enspace f = 100 Hz: \\[0.5cm] X_L = 2\pi fL = 2\pi(100 Hz)(40 mH) = 25.13 Ω\\[0.5cm] and\qquad Z_T =\sqrt{R^2+X^2_L}=\sqrt{(2 kΩ)^ 2 + (25.13 Ω)^ 2} =2000.16 Ω ≅ R\\[0.5cm] At\enspace f = 40 kHz:\\[0.5cm] X_L = 2\pi fL = 2\pi (40 kHz)(40 mH) ≅ 10.05 kΩ\\[0.5cm] and\qquad Z_T=\sqrt{R^2+X^2_L}=\sqrt{(2 kΩ)^ 2 + (10.05 kΩ) ^2} =10.25 kΩ ≅ X_L\)

Both calculations support the conclusions of part (b).

d. Applying the voltage divider rule gives

\(V_L=\frac{Z_LE}{Z_T} \)

From part (c), we know that at 100 Hz, \(Z_T ≅ R, so that V_R ≅ 20 V and V_L ≅ 0 V\). Part (c) revealed that at 40 kHz, \(Z_T ≅ X_L, so that V_L ≅ 20 V and V_R ≅ 0 V\). The result is two plot points for the curve in Fig. 15.65.

\(At\enspace 1 kHz\qquad X_L = 2\pi fL ≅ 0.25 kΩ\\[0.5cm] and\qquad V_L=\frac{(0.25 kΩ ∠90°)(20 V ∠0°)}{2 kΩ + j 0.25 kΩ}=2.48 V ∠82.87°\\[0.5cm] At\enspace 5 kHz \qquad X_L = 2\pi fL ≅ 1.26 kΩ\\[0.5cm] and\qquad V_L=\frac{(1.26 kΩ∠90°)(20 V∠0°)}{2 kΩ + j 1.26 kΩ}=10.68 V ∠57.79° \\[0.5cm] At 10 kHz\enspace \qquad X_L = 2\pi fL ≅ 2.5 kΩ\\[0.5cm] and\qquad V_L=\frac{(2.5 kΩ ∠90°)(20 V ∠0°)}{2.5 kΩ + j 2.5 kΩ}=15.63 V ∠38.66° \)

The complete plot appears in Fig. 15.65.

e. \(\theta_T=\tan^{-1}\frac{X_L}{R}=\tan^{-1}\frac{10.05 kΩ}{2 kΩ}=78.75° \)

The angle \(\theta_T\) is closing in on the 90° of a purely inductive network. Therefore, the network can be considered quite inductive at a frequency of 40 kHz.
