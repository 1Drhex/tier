POWER PRODUCED BY A TURBINE

At the maximum rate of power generation, a small hydroelectric power plant takes a discharge of 14.1 \(m ^{3} / s\) through an elevation drop of 61 m. The head loss through the intakes, penstock, and outlet works is 1.5 m. The combined efficiency of the turbine and electrical generator is 87%.

What is the rate of power generation?

Problem Definition

Situation: A small hydroelectric plant is producing electrical power.

Find: Electrical power generation (in kW).

Properties: Water (10Â°C), Table A.5: \(\gamma=9810 N / m ^{3}\).

Plan

1. Write the energy equation (7.29) between section 1 and section 2.

\(\left(\frac{p_{1}}{\gamma}+\alpha_{1} \frac{\bar{V}_{1}^{2}}{2 g}+z_{1}\right)+h_{p}=\left(\frac{p_{2}}{\gamma}+\alpha_{2} \frac{\bar{V}_{2}^{2}}{2 g}+z_{2}\right)+h_{t}+h_{L}\) (7.29)

2. Analyze each term in the energy equation.

3. Solve for the head of the turbine \(h_{t}\).

4. Find the input power to the turbine using the power equation (7.30a).

\(\dot{W}_{p}=\gamma Q h_{p}=\dot{m} g h_{p}\) (7.30a)

5. Find the output power from generator by using the efficiency equation (7.32).

\(\eta \equiv \frac{\text { power output from a machine or system }}{\text { power input to a machine or system }}=\frac{P_{\text {output }}}{P_{\text {input }}}\) (7.32)

Solution

1. Energy equation (general form)

 

\(\frac{p_{1}}{\gamma}+\alpha_{1} \frac{\bar{V}_{1}^{2}}{2 g}+z_{1}+h_{p}=\frac{p_{2}}{\gamma}+\alpha_{2} \frac{\bar{V}_{2}^{2}}{2 g}+z_{2}+h_{t}+h_{L}\)

 

2. Term-by-term analysis

â¢ Velocity heads are negligible because \(V_{1} \approx 0\) and \(V_{2} \approx 0\).

â¢ Pressure heads are zero because \(p_{1}=p_{2}=0\) gage.

â¢ \(h_{p}=0\) because there is no pump in the system.

â¢ Elevation head terms are given.

 

3. Combine steps 1 and 2:

 

\(h_{t}=\left(z_{1}-z_{2}\right)-h_{L}\)

 

\(=(61 m )-(1.5 m )=59.5 m\)

 

Interpretation: Head supplied to the turbine (59.5 m) is equal to the net elevation change of the dam (61 m) minus the head loss (1.5 m).

 

4. Power equation

 

\(P_{\text {input to turbine }}=\gamma Q h_{t}=\left(9810 N / m ^{3}\right)\left(14.1 m ^{3} / s \right)(59.5 m )\)

 

= 8.23 MW

 

5. Efficiency equation

 

\(P_{\text {output from generator }}=\eta P_{\text {input to turbine }}=0.87(8.23 MW )\)

 

= 7.16 MW
