Guest Question.

Determine the nodal voltages for the network in Fig. 8.55.

Admin
Solution

Steps 1 and 2: As indicated in Fig. 8.56:

Step 3: Included in Fig. 8.56 for the node \(V_1\). Applying Kirchhoff’s current law gives

\( 4 A = I_1 + I_3\)

 

and \(4A=\frac{V_1}{R_1}+\frac{V_1-V_2}{R_3}=\frac{V_1}{2\Omega} +\frac{V_1-V_2}{12\Omega} \)

 

Expanding and rearranging gives

\(V_1(\frac{1}{2\Omega}+\frac{1}{12\Omega} )-V_2(\frac{1}{12\Omega} )=4A\)

 

For node \(V_2\), the currents are defined as in Fig. 8.57.

Applying Kirchhoff’s current law gives

\(0 = I_3 + I_2 + 2 A\)

 

and \(\frac{V_2-V_1}{R_3}+\frac{V_2}{R_2}+2A=0\longrightarrow \frac{V_2-V_1}{12\Omega}+\frac{V_2}{6\Omega} +2A=0 \)

 

Expanding and rearranging gives

\(V_2(\frac{1}{12\Omega}+\frac{1}{6\Omega} )-V_1(\frac{1}{12\Omega} )=-2A\)

 

resulting in the following two equations and two unknowns:

\(\left. \begin{matrix} V_1(\frac{1}{2\Omega}+\frac{1}{12\Omega})-V_2(\frac{1}{12\Omega}) =+4A \\ V_2(\frac{1}{12\Omega}+\frac{1}{6\Omega} )-V_1(\frac{1}{12\Omega} )=-2A \end{matrix} \right\} \hspace{30 pt} (8.1)\)

 

producing

\(\left. \begin{matrix} \frac{7}{12}V_1-\frac{1}{12}V_2=+4 \\ \underline{-\frac{1}{12}V_1+\frac{3}{12}V_2=-2 } \end{matrix} \right\} \hspace{30 pt} \begin{matrix} 7V_1-V_2=48 \\ -1V_1+3V_2=-24 \end{matrix} \)

 

and \(V_1=\frac{\left|\begin{matrix} 48 & -1 \\ -24 & 3 \end{matrix} \right| }{\left|\begin{matrix} 7 & -1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{matrix} \right| } =\frac{120}{20}=+6V\\[0.5cm] V_2=\frac{\left|\begin{matrix} 7 & 48 \\ -1 & -24 \end{matrix} \right| }{20} =\frac{-120}{20}=-6V \)

 

Since \(V_1 is greater than V_2\), the current through \(R_3\) passes from \(V_1 to V_2\). Its value is

\(I_{R_3}=\frac{V_1-V_2}{R_3}=\frac{6V-(-6V)}{12\Omega}=\frac{12V}{12\Omega} =1A \)

 

The fact that \(V_1\) is positive results in a current \(I_{R_1} from V_1\) to ground equal to

\(I_{R_1}=\frac{V_{R_1}}{R_1}=\frac{V_1}{R_1}= \frac{6V}{2\Omega}=3A \)

 

Finally, since \(V_2\) is negative, the current \(I_{R_2}\) flows from ground to \(V_2\) and is equal to

\(I_{R_2}=\frac{V_{R_2}}{R_2}=\frac{V_2}{R_2}= \frac{-6V}{6\Omega}=-1A \)
