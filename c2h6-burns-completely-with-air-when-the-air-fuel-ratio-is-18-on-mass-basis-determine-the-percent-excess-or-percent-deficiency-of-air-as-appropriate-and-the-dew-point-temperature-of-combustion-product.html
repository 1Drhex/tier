Guest Question.

\(C_{2}H_{6}\) burns completely with air when the air-fuel ratio is 18 on mass basis. Determine the percent excess or percent deficiency of air as appropriate and the dew point temperature of combustion products when cooled at 1 atm.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{Given air-fuel ratio on mass basis is first transformed into the air-fuel ratio on molar basis.}\\\begin{aligned}\text{A/F ratio on molar basis}&= \frac{\text{No.of moles of air}}{\text{No.of moles of fuel}} = \frac{\left\lgroup\frac{\text{Mass of air}}{\text{Mol.wt.of air}} \right\rgroup }{\left\lgroup\frac{\text{Mass of fuel}}{\text{Mol.wt .of fuel}} \right\rgroup } \\&= \frac{\text{Mass of air}}{\text{Mass of fuel}} × \frac{\text{Mol.wt .of fuel}}{\text{No.of moles of air}}\\&= \text{(A/F ratio on mass basis) } × \frac{\text{Mol.wt.of }C_{2}H_{6}}{\text{Mol.wt.of air}}\\&= 18 × \frac{30}{29}\\\text{A/F ratio on molar basis}&= 18.62\end{aligned}\\\text{Chemical reaction for complete combustion may be given as,}\\C_{2}H_{6} + n(O_{2} + 3.76 N_{2}) → aCO_{2} + bN_{2} + dO_{2} + eH_{2}O\\\text{Also, for complete combustion there will be }\left\lgroup\frac{18.62}{4.76} \right\rgroup \text{ moles of air for each mole of fuel,}\\So, n = 3.912\\\text{Equating coefficient}\\\begin{aligned}C: 2&= a \qquad \qquad \qquad \text{Solving we get,}\qquad \qquad \\H: 6&= 2e\\O: 2n&= 2a + 2d + e\\N: (3.76 × 2n)&= 2b\end{aligned}\begin{aligned}a&= 2\\b&= 14.71\\d&= 0.412\\e&= 3\end{aligned}\\\text{Complete combustion reaction may be given as,}\\\qquad \quad C_{2}H_{6} + 3.912 (O_{2} + 3.76 N_{2}) → 2CO_{2} + 14.71 N_{2} + 0.412 O_{2} + 3H_{2}O\\\text{or}\quad C_{2}H_{6} + 3.5(O_{2} + 3.76 N_{2}) → 2CO_{2} + 13.16 N_{2} + 3H_{2}O \\\text{Hence air-fuel ratio (theoretical) on mol. basis}\\\qquad \qquad (A/F) = \frac{3.5 × 4.76}{1} = 16.66\\\text{Since theoretical air-fuel ratio is less than actual so it means excess air is supplied.}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Percentage of excess air}&= \frac{\text{(A/F)}_{actual} – \text{(A/F)}_{theoretical}}{\text{(A/F)}_{theoretical}} × 100\\&= \left\lgroup\frac{18.62 – 16.66}{16.66} × 100\right\rgroup \\&= 11.76\%\\\text{Percentage of excess air}&= 11.76\%\\\text{Total amount of mixture}&= (a + b + d + e)\\&= (2 + 14.71 + 0.412 + 3)\\&= \text{20.122 kg/mol. of fuel}\\\text{Partial pressure of water vapour P}&= \left\lgroup\frac{e}{a + b + d + e} \right\rgroup × 1.013\\&= 0.151 bar\end{aligned}\\\text{From steam table saturation temperature corresponding to 0.151 bar is seen to be 54ºC.}\\\qquad \text{Dew point temperature }= 54ºC\)
