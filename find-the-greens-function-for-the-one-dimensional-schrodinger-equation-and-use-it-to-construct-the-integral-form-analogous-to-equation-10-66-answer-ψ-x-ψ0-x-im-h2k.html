Guest Question.

Find the Green’s function for the one-dimensional Schrödinger equation, and use it to construct the integral form (analogous to Equation 10.66). Answer:

\( \psi(x)=\psi_{0}(x)-\frac{i m}{\hbar^{2} k} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{i k \mid x-x_{0} l} V\left(x_{0}\right) \psi\left(x_{0}\right) d x_{0} \). (10.102).

\( \left(\nabla^{2}+k^{2}\right) G_{0}( r )=0 \) (10.66).

Admin
Solution

\( \left(\frac{d^{2}}{d x^{2}}+k^{2}\right) G(x)=\delta(x) \quad \text { (analog to Eq. 10.52). } G(x)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi}} \int e^{i s x} g(s) d s \quad \text { (analog to Eq. 10.54). } \).

\( \left(\nabla^{2}+k^{2}\right) G( r )=\delta^{3}( r ) \) (10.52).

\( G( r )=\frac{1}{(2 \pi)^{3 / 2}} \int e^{i s \cdot r } g( s ) d^{3} s \) (10.54).

\( \left(\frac{d^{2}}{d x^{2}}+k^{2}\right) G=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi}} \int\left(-s^{2}+k^{2}\right) g(s) e^{i s x} d s=\delta(x)=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int e^{i s x} d s \Longrightarrow g(s)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi}\left(k^{2}-s^{2}\right)} \).

\( G(x)=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{e^{i s x}}{k^{2}-s^{2}} d s \). Skirt the poles as in Fig. 10.10. For x > 0; close above:

\( G(x)=-\frac{1}{2 \pi} \oint\left(\frac{e^{i s x}}{s+k}\right) \frac{1}{s-k} d s=-\left.\frac{1}{2 \pi} 2 \pi i\left(\frac{e^{i s x}}{s+k}\right)\right|_{s=k}=-i \frac{e^{i k x}}{2 k} \). For x < 0; close below:

\( G(x)=+\frac{1}{2 \pi} \oint\left(\frac{e^{i s x}}{s-k}\right) \frac{1}{s+k} d s=\left.\frac{1}{2 \pi} 2 \pi i\left(\frac{e^{i s x}}{s-k}\right)\right|_{s=-k}=-i \frac{e^{-i k x}}{2 k} \).

In either case, then, \( G(x)=-\frac{i}{2 k} e^{i k|x|} \). (Analog to Eq. 10.65.)

\( G( r )=\frac{i}{8 \pi^{2} r}\left[\left(i \pi e^{i k r}\right)-\left(-i \pi e^{i k r}\right)\right]=-\frac{e^{i k r}}{4 \pi r} \) (10.65).

\( \psi(x)=\int G\left(x-x_{0}\right) \frac{2 m}{\hbar^{2}} V\left(x_{0}\right) \psi\left(x_{0}\right) d x_{0}=-\frac{i}{2 k} \frac{2 m}{\hbar^{2}} \int e^{i k\left|x-x_{0}\right|} V\left(x_{0}\right) \psi\left(x_{0}\right) d x_{0} \),

plus any solution \( \psi_{0}(x) \) to the homogeneous Schrödinger equation:

\( \left(\frac{d^{2}}{d x^{2}}+k^{2}\right) \psi_{0}(x)=0 \). So:

\( \psi(x)=\psi_{0}(x)-\frac{i m}{\hbar^{2} k} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{i k\left|x-x_{0}\right|} V\left(x_{0}\right) \psi\left(x_{0}\right) d x_{0} \).
