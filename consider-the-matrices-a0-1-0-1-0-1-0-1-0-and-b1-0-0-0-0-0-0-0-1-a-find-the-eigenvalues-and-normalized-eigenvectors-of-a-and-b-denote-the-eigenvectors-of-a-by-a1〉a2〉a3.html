Guest Question.

Consider the matrices \(A=\left(\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right)\) and \(B=\left(\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -1 \end{matrix} \right)\).

(a) Find the eigenvalues and normalized eigenvectors of A and B. Denote the eigenvectors of A by \(\mid a_{1}〉,\mid a_{2}〉,\mid a_{3}〉\) and those of B by \(\mid b_{1}〉,\mid b_{2}〉,\mid b_{3}〉\). Are there any degenerate eigenvalues?

(b) Show that each of the sets \(\mid a_{1}〉,\mid a_{2}〉,\mid a_{3}〉\) and \(\mid b_{1}〉,\mid b_{2}〉,\mid b_{3}〉\) forms an orthonormal and complete basis, i.e., show that \(〈a_{j}\mid a_{k}〉=\delta _{jk}\) and \(\Sigma ^{3}_{j=1}\mid a_{j}〉〈a_{j}\mid=I\), where I is the 3 × 3 unit matrix; then show that the same holds for \(\mid b_{1}〉,\mid b_{2}〉,\mid b_{3}〉\).

(c) Find the matrix U of the transformation from the basis \(\left\{\mid a〉\right\}\) to \(\left\{\mid b〉\right\}\). Show that \(U^{-1}=U^{\dagger }\). Verify that \(U^{\dagger } U=I\). Calculate how the matrix A transforms under U, i.e., calculate \(A^{′} =UAU^{\dagger }\).

Admin
Solution

(a) It is easy to verify that the eigenvalues of A are \(a_{1}=0,a_{2}= \sqrt{2},a_{3}=-\sqrt{2}\) and their corresponding normalized eigenvectors are

\(\mid a_{1}〉=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} }\left(\begin{matrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right) ,\mid a_{2}〉=\frac{1}{2} \left(\begin{matrix} 1 \\ \sqrt{2} \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right), \mid a_{3}〉=\frac{1}{2} \left (\begin {matrix} 1 \\ -\sqrt{2} \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right)\). (2.446)

The eigenvalues of B are \(b_{1}=1,b_{2}=0,b_{3}=-1\) and their corresponding normalized eigenvectors are

\(\mid b_{1}〉=\left(\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right), \mid b_{2}〉=\left(\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right), \mid b_{3}〉=\left(\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right)\). (2.447)

None of the eigenvalues of A and B are degenerate.
(b) The set \(\mid a_{1}〉,\mid a_{2}〉,\mid a_{3}〉\) is indeed complete because the sum of \(\mid a_{1}〉〈a_{1}\mid, \mid a_{2}〉〈a_{2}\mid,\) and \(\mid a_{3}〉〈a_{3}\mid \) as given by

\(\mid a_{1}〉〈a_{1}\mid=\frac{1}{2}\left(\begin{matrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right)\left(\begin{matrix} -1 & 0 & 1\end{matrix} \right) =\frac{1}{2}\left(\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right) \), (2.448)

 

\(\mid a_{2}〉〈a_{2}\mid=\frac{1}{4}\left(\begin{matrix} 1 \\ \sqrt{2} \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right)\left(\begin{matrix} 1 & \sqrt{2} & 1\end{matrix} \right) =\frac{1}{4}\left(\begin{matrix} 1 & \sqrt{2} & 1 \\ \sqrt{2} & 2 & \sqrt{2} \\ 1 & \sqrt{2} & 1 \end{matrix} \right)\), (2.449)

 

\(\mid a_{3}〉〈a_{3}\mid=\frac{1}{4}\left(\begin{matrix} 1 \\ -\sqrt{2} \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right)\left(\begin{matrix} 1 & -\sqrt{2} & 1\end{matrix} \right) =\frac{1}{4}\left(\begin{matrix} 1 & -\sqrt{2} & 1 \\ -\sqrt{2} & 2 & -\sqrt{2} \\ 1 & -\sqrt{2} & 1 \end{matrix} \right)\). (2.450)

is equal to unity:

\(\sum^{3}_{j=1}\mid a_{j}〉〈a_{j}\mid=\frac{1}{2}\left(\begin {matrix} 1 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right) +\frac{1}{4}\left(\begin{matrix} 1 & \sqrt{2} & 1 \\ \sqrt{2} & 2 & \sqrt{2} \\ 1 & \sqrt{2} & 1 \end{matrix} \right)\)

 

\(+\frac{1}{4}\left(\begin{matrix} 1 & -\sqrt{2} & 1 \\ -\sqrt{2} & 2 & -\sqrt{2} \\ 1 & -\sqrt{2} & 1 \end{matrix} \right)\)

 

\(=\left(\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right) \). (2.451)

The states \(\mid a_{1}〉,\mid a_{2}〉,\mid a_{3}〉\) are orthonormal, since \(〈a_{1}\mid a_{2}〉=〈a_{1}\mid a_{3}〉=〈a_{3}\mid a_{2}〉=0\) and \(〈a_{1}\mid a_{1}〉=〈a_{2} \mid a_{2}〉=〈a_{3}\mid a_{3}〉=1\). Following the same procedure, we can ascertain that

\(\mid b_{1}〉〈b_{1}\mid+\mid b_{2}〉〈b_{2}\mid+\mid b_{3}〉〈b_{3}\mid=\left(\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right)\). (2.452)

We can verify that the states \(\mid b_{1}〉,\mid b_{2}〉,\mid b_{3}〉\) are orthonormal, since \(〈b_{1}\mid b_{2}〉=〈b_{1}\mid b_{3}〉=〈b_{3}\mid b_{2}〉=0\) and \(〈b_{1} \mid b_{1}〉=〈b_{2}\mid b_{2}〉=〈b_{3}\mid b_{3}〉=1\).

(c) The elements of the matrix U, corresponding to the transformation from the basis \(\left\{\mid a〉\right\}\) to \(\left\{\mid b〉\right\}\), are given by \(U_{jk}=〈b_{j}\mid b_{k}〉\) where j , k = 1, 2, 3:

\(U=\left(\begin{matrix} 〈b_{1}\mid a_{1}〉 & 〈b_{1}\mid 1_{2}〉 & 〈b_{1}\mid a_{3}〉 \\ 〈b_{2}\mid a_{1}〉 & 〈b_{2}\mid a_{2}〉 & 〈b_{2}\mid a_{3}〉 \\ 〈b_{3}\mid a_{1}〉 & 〈b_{3}\mid b_{2}〉 & 〈b_{3}\mid b_{3}〉 \end{matrix} \right) \), (2.453)

where the elements \(〈b_{j}\mid b_{k}〉\) can be calculated from (2.446) and (2.447):

\(U_{11}=〈b_{1}\mid a_{1}〉=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left (\begin {matrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right)\left(\begin{matrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right)=-\frac{\sqrt{2} }{2} \), (2.454)

 

\(U_{12}=〈b_{1}\mid 1_{2}〉=\frac{1}{2}\left(\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right)\left(\begin{matrix} 1 \\ \sqrt{2} \\ 1 \end {matrix} \right)=\frac{1}{2} \), (2.455)

 

\(U_{13}=〈b_{1}\mid a_{3}〉=\frac{1}{2}\left(\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right)\left(\begin{matrix} 1 \\ -\sqrt{2} \\ 1 \end {matrix} \right)=\frac{1}{2} \), (2.456)

 

\(U_{21}=〈b_{2}\mid a_{1}〉=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin {matrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right)\left(\begin{matrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right)=0 \), (2.457)

 

\(U_{22}=〈b_{2}\mid a_{2}〉=\frac{1}{2}\left(\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right)\left(\begin{matrix} 1 \\ \sqrt{2} \\ 1 \end {matrix} \right)=\frac{\sqrt{2} }{2} \), (2.458)

 

\(U_{23}=〈b_{2}\mid a_{3}〉=\frac{1}{2}\left(\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right)\left(\begin{matrix} 1 \\ -\sqrt{2} \\ 1 \end {matrix} \right)=-\frac{\sqrt{2} }{2} \), (2.459)

 

\(U_{31}=〈b_{3}\mid a_{1}〉=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin {matrix} 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right)\left(\begin{matrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right)=\frac{\sqrt{2} }{2} \), (2.460)

 

\(U_{32}=〈b_{3}\mid b_{2}〉=\frac{1}{2}\left(\begin{matrix} 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right)\left(\begin{matrix} 1 \\ \sqrt{2} \\ 1 \end {matrix} \right)=\frac{1}{2} \), (2.461)

 

\(U_{33}=〈b_{3}\mid b_{3}〉=\frac{1}{2}\left(\begin{matrix} 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right)\left(\begin{matrix} 1 \\ -\sqrt{2} \\ 1 \end {matrix} \right)=\frac{1}{2} \). (2.462)

Collecting these elements, we obtain

\(U=\frac{1}{2}\left(\begin{matrix} -\sqrt{2} & 1 & 1 \\ 0 & \sqrt{2} & -\sqrt{2} \\ \sqrt{2} & 1 & 1 \end{matrix} \right)\). (2.463)

Calculating the inverse of U as we did in (2.200), we see that it is equal to its Hermitian adjoint:

\(U^{-1}=\frac{1}{2}\left(\begin{matrix} -\sqrt{2} & 0 & \sqrt{2} \\ 1 & \sqrt{2} & 1 \\ 1 & -\sqrt{2} & 1 \end{matrix} \right)=U^ {\dagger }\). (2.464)

This implies that the matrix U is unitary. The matrix A transforms as follows:

\(A^{′}=UAU^{\dagger }=\frac{1}{4}\left(\begin{matrix} -\sqrt {2} & 1 & 1 \\ 0 & \sqrt{2} & -\sqrt{2} \\ \sqrt{2} & 1 & 1 \end{matrix} \right)\left(\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end {matrix} \right)\left(\begin{matrix} -\sqrt{2} & 0 & \sqrt{2} \\ 1 & \sqrt{2} & 1 \\ 1 & -\sqrt{2} & 1 \end{matrix} \right)\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{2}\left(\begin{matrix} 1-\sqrt{2} & -1 & 1 \\ -1 & -2 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 & 1+\sqrt{2} \end{matrix} \right)\). (2.465)
