Guest Question.

In the photoelectric effect, light can ionize an atom if its energy \( (\hbar \omega) \) exceeds the binding energy of the electron. Consider the photoelectric effect for the ground state of hydrogen, where the electron is kicked out with momentum \( \hbar k \) . The initial state of the electron is \( \psi_{0}(r) \)Equation 4.80) and its final state is

\( \psi_{100}(r, \theta, \phi)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi a^{3}}} e^{-r / a} \) (4.80).

\( \psi_{f}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{l^{3}}} e^{i k \cdot r } \),

as in Example 11.2.

(a) For light polarized along the z direction, use Fermi’s Golden Rule to compute the rate at which electrons are ejected into the solid angle dΩ in the dipole approximation.

\( \left[\text { Answer }: R_{i \rightarrow d \Omega}=256 \frac{\epsilon_{0} E_{0}^{2} a^{3}}{\hbar} \frac{(k a)^{3}}{\left[1+(k a)^{2}\right]^{6}} \cos ^{2} \theta d \Omega .\right] \).

Hint: To evaluate the matrix element, use the following trick. Write

\( z e^{i k \cdot r }=-i \frac{d}{d k_{z}} e^{i k \cdot r } \),

pull \( d / d k_{z} \) outside the integral, and what remains is straightforward to compute.

(b) The photoelectric cross section is defined as

\( \sigma(k)=\frac{R_{i \rightarrow \text { all }} \hbar \omega}{\frac{1}{2} \epsilon_{0} E_{0}^{2} c} \),

where the quantity in the numerator is the rate at which energy is absorbed \( \left(\hbar \omega=\frac{\hbar^{2} k^{2}}{2 m}-E_{1} \text { per photoelectron }\right) \) and the quantity in the denominator is the intensity of the incident light. Integrate your result from (a) over all angles to obtain \( R_{i \rightarrow \text { all }} \), and compute the photoelectric
cross section.

(c) Obtain a numerical value for the photoelectric cross section for ultraviolet light of wavelength (n.b. this is the wavelength of the incident light, not the scattered electron). Express your answer in mega-barns \( \left( Mb =10^{-22} m ^{2}\right) \).

Admin
Solution

(a) \( R=\frac{2 \pi}{\hbar}\left|\frac{V_{i f}}{2}\right|^{2} \rho\left(E_{f}\right) \). From Eqs. 11.31 and 11.39,

\( V_{a b} \equiv\left\langle\psi_{a}|V| \psi_{b}\right\rangle \) (11.31).

\( H^{\prime}=-q E_{0} z \cos (\omega t) \) (11.39).

\( V_{i f}=\left\langle\psi_{i}|V| \psi_{f}\right\rangle, \text { where } V=e E_{0} z, \text { so } \),

\( V_{i f}=e E_{0}\left\langle\psi_{0}|z| \psi_{f}\right\rangle=e E_{0} \frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi a^{3}}} \frac{1}{\sqrt{l^{3}}} \int e^{-r / a} z e^{i k \cdot r } d^{3} r =\frac{e E_{0}}{\sqrt{\pi a^{3} l^{3}}}\left(-i \frac{\partial}{\partial k_{z}}\right) \int e^{-r / a} e^{i k \cdot r } d^{3} r \).

To evaluate the integral, set the z axis along k, so

\( \int e^{-r / a} e^{i k \cdot r } d^{4} r =\int e^{-r / a} e^{i k r \cos \theta} r^{2} \sin \theta d r d \theta d \phi=2 \pi \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{2} e^{-r / a}\left\{\int_{0}^{\pi} e^{i k r \cos \theta} \sin \theta d \theta\right\} d r \).

\( =2 \pi \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{2} e^{-r / a}\left\{-\left.\frac{e^{i k r \cos \theta}}{i k r}\right|_{0} ^{\pi}\right\} d r=\frac{2 \pi i}{k} \int_{0}^{\infty} r e^{-r / a}\left(e^{-i k r}-e^{i k r}\right) d r \).

\( =\frac{2 \pi i}{k}\left\{\int_{0}^{\infty} r e^{-r(1 / a+i k)} d r-\int_{0}^{\infty} r e^{-r(1 / a-i k)} d r\right\}= \)

\( =\frac{2 \pi i}{k}\left[\frac{1}{(1 / a+i k)^{2}}-\frac{1}{(1 / a-i k)^{2}}\right]=\left(\frac{2 \pi a^{2} i}{k}\right) \frac{(1-i k a)^{2}-(1+i k a)^{2}}{\left[1+(k a)^{2}\right]^{2}} \).

\( =\left(\frac{2 \pi a^{2} i}{k}\right) \frac{(-4 i k a}{\left[1+(k a)^{2}\right]^{2}}=\frac{8 \pi a^{3}}{\left[1+(k a)^{2}\right]^{2}} \).

\( V_{i f}=-\frac{i e E_{0}}{\sqrt{\pi a^{3} l^{3}}} \frac{\partial}{\partial k_{z}}\left(\frac{8 \pi a^{3}}{\left[1+k^{2} a^{2}\right]^{2}}\right)=-8 i e E_{0} \sqrt{\frac{\pi a^{3}}{l^{3}}} \frac{-4 a^{2} k_{z}}{\left[1+k^{2} a^{2}\right]^{3}}=\frac{32 i e E_{0} a^{2} k \cos \theta}{\left[1+k^{2} a^{2}\right]^{3}} \sqrt{\frac{\pi a^{3}}{l^{3}}} \).

Finally, quoting Eq. 11.85,

\( \rho(E)=\left(\frac{l}{2 \pi}\right)^{3} \frac{\sqrt{2 m^{3} E}}{\hbar^{3}} d \Omega \) (11.85).

\( \rho\left(E_{f}\right)=\left(\frac{l}{2 \pi}\right)^{3} \frac{\sqrt{2 m^{3} E_{f}}}{\hbar^{3}} d \Omega, \text { where } E_{f}=\frac{\hbar^{2} k^{2}}{2 m}, \quad \text { so } \rho\left(E_{f}\right)=\left(\frac{l}{2 \pi}\right)^{3} \frac{m k}{\hbar^{2}} d \Omega \).

Putting it all together,

\( R=\frac{\pi}{2 \hbar}\left(\frac{32 e E_{0} a^{2} k \cos \theta}{\left[1+k^{2} a^{2}\right]^{3}} \sqrt{\frac{\pi a^{3}}{l^{3}}}\right)^{2}\left(\frac{l}{2 \pi}\right)^{3} \frac{m k}{\hbar^{2}} d \Omega, \quad \text { or, using } \quad m e^{2}=\frac{4 \pi \epsilon_{0} \hbar^{2}}{a} \).

\( =\frac{256}{\hbar} \epsilon_{0} E_{0}^{2} \frac{k^{3} a^{6}}{\left[1+k^{2} a^{2}\right]^{6}} \cos ^{2} \theta d \Omega \).

(b) Integrating over all angles, using \( d \Omega=\sin \theta d \theta d \phi \),

\( R_{i \rightarrow all }=\frac{256}{\hbar} \epsilon_{0} E_{0}^{2} \frac{k^{3} a^{6}}{\left[1+k^{2} a^{2}\right]^{6}} \int \cos ^{2} \theta \sin \theta d \theta d \phi=\frac{256}{\hbar} \epsilon_{0} E_{0}^{2} \frac{k^{3} a^{6}}{\left[1+k^{2} a^{2}\right]^{6}}\left(\frac{4 \pi}{3}\right) \).

and noting that

\( \hbar \omega=\frac{\hbar^{2} k^{2}}{2 m}+\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m a^{2}}=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m a^{2}}\left(1+k^{2} a^{2}\right) \).

the cross-section is

\( \sigma(k)=\frac{1}{\frac{1}{2} \epsilon_{0} E_{0}^{2} c} \frac{256}{\hbar} \epsilon_{0} E_{0}^{2} \frac{k^{3} a^{6}}{\left[1+k^{2} a^{2}\right]^{6}}\left(\frac{4 \pi}{3}\right) \frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m a^{2}}\left(1+k^{2} a^{2}\right)=\frac{1024 \pi \hbar}{3 m c} \frac{k^{3} a^{4}}{\left[1+k^{2} a^{2}\right]^{5}} \).

(c)

\( \lambda=\frac{2 \pi c}{\omega} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \hbar \omega=\hbar \frac{2 \pi c}{\lambda}=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m a^{2}}\left(1+k^{2} a^{2}\right) \quad \Rightarrow \quad\left(1+k^{2} a^{2}\right)=\frac{2 m a^{2}}{\hbar^{2}} \frac{\hbar 2 \pi c}{\lambda}=\frac{4 \pi m a^{2} c}{\hbar \lambda} \).

But \( m a^{2}=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2\left|E_{1}\right|} \), so

\( \left(1+k^{2} a^{2}\right)=\frac{4 \pi c}{\hbar \lambda} \frac{\hbar^{2}}{2\left|E_{1}\right|}=\frac{2 \pi \hbar c}{\lambda\left|E_{1}\right|}=\frac{2 \pi\left(1.055 \times 10^{-34}\right)\left(3 \times 10^{8}\right)}{\left(220 \times 10^{-10}\right)(13.6)\left(1.6 \times 10^{-19}\right)}=4.15 \).

\( (k a)^{2}=3.15 \Rightarrow k a=1.77 \).

\( \sigma=\frac{1024 \pi\left(1.055 \times 10^{-34}\right)(1.77)^{3}\left(5.29 \times 10^{-11}\right)}{3\left(9.11 \times 10^{-31}\right)\left(3 \times 10^{8}\right)(4.15)^{5}}=0.986 \times 10^{-22} m ^{2}=1 Mb . \)
