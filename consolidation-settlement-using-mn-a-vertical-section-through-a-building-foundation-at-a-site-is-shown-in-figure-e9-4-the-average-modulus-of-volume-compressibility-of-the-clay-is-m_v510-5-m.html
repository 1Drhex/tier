Consolidation Settlement Using mn
A vertical section through a building foundation at a site is shown in Figure E9.4. The average modulus of volume compressibility of the clay is \(m_{v}=5*10^{-5}\: m^{2}/kN\). Determine the primary consolidation settlement.

Strategy: To find the primary consolidation settlement, you need to know the vertical stress increase in the clay layer from the building load. Since the clay layer is finite, we will have to use the vertical stress influence values in Appendix C. If we assume a rough base, we can use the influence values specified by Milovic and Tournier (1971) or, if we assume a smooth base, we can use the values specified by Sovinc (1961). The clay layer is 10 m thick, so it is best to subdivide the clay layer into sublayers \(\leq 2\) m thick.

Solution

Step 1: Find the vertical stress increase at the center of the clay layer below the foundation. \(B=10\, m,\: \: L=20\, M,\: \: \frac{l}{B}=2,\: \: q_{s}=200\, kPa\)

Step 2: Calculate the primary consolidation settlement.
Use Equation (9.20).

Equation (9.20): \(\rho _{pc}=H_{o}m_{\nu }\Delta \sigma _{z}\)

\(\rho _{pc}=\sum_{i=1}^{n}(H_{o}m_{\nu }\Delta \sigma _{z})_{i}=2*5*10^{-5}*(198.4 + 190.2 + 175.2 + 156.2 + 137.2)=0.086\, m=86\, mm\)
