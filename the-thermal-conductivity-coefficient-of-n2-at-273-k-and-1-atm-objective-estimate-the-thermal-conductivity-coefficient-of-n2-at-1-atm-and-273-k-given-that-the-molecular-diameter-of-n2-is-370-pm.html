The Thermal Conductivity Coefficient of \(N_2\), at 273 K and 1 atm
Objective Estimate the thermal conductivity coefficient of \(N_{2}\), at 1 atm and 273 K, given that the molecular diameter of \(N_{2}\) is 370 pm.

Method Use equation 4.17 \([k=\frac{<v>C_{v}}{3\sqrt{2}\pi d^{2}N_{A} } ]\) recalling that \(C_{v} \thickapprox 5R/2\).

Admin
Solution

First calculate \(<v>= (8kT/\pi m)^{1/2}\):

\(=\left\{\frac{8(1.38\times 10^{-23} J K^{-1})(273 K)(6.02 × 10^{23} amu/g)(1000 g/kg)}{[3.1415 (28 amu)]} \right\}^{1/2}\) .

=454 m/s (4.18)

Then evaluate \(C_{v} = 5R/2 = 5(8.314 J mol^{ -1}K^{-1}) /2 = 20.8 J mol^{ -1}K^{-1}\).

Finally,

\(\kappa =\frac{(454 m/s)(20.8 j/mol)}{3\sqrt{2} \pi(6.02 \times 10^{23}molec/mol)(370\times 10^{-12 }m)^{2} }=8.59\times 10^{-3} j m^{-1}s^{-1}K^{-1}\) (4.19)

Comment

Note that the information that the pressure is 1 atm is irrelevant to the solution. Since we used approximations in arriving at equation 4.17 and in evaluating the heat capacity, the answer is not exactly
equal to the measured value listed in Table 4.2.
