Guest Question.

Charge density

\(\sigma(\phi)=a \sin 5 \phi\)

(where a is a constant) is glued over the surface of an infinite cylinder of radius R (Fig. 3.25). Find the potential inside and outside the cylinder. [Use your result from .]

Admin
Solution

Inside: \(V(s, \phi)=a_{0}+\sum\limits_{k=1}^{\infty }{} s^{k}\left(a_{k} \cos k \phi+b_{k} \sin k \phi\right)\) . (In this region ln s and \( s^{-k}\) are no good—they blow up at s = 0.)

Outside: \(V(s, \phi)=\bar{a}_{0}+\sum\limits_{k=1}^{\infty } \frac{1}{s^{k}}\left(c_{k} \cos k \phi+d_{k} \sin k \phi\right)\) . \(\text { (Here } \left.\ln s \text { and } s^{k} \text { are no good at } s \rightarrow \infty\right)\) .

\(\sigma=-\left.\epsilon_{0}\left(\frac{\partial V_{\text {out }}}{\partial s}-\frac{\partial V_{\text {in }}}{\partial s}\right)\right|_{s=R}\) (Eq. 2.36).

\(\frac{\partial V_{\text {above }}}{\partial n}-\frac{\partial V_{\text {below }}}{\partial n}=-\frac{1}{\epsilon_{0}} \sigma\) (2.36)

Thus

\(a \sin 5 \phi=-\epsilon_{0} \sum\limits_{k=1}^{\infty }\left\{-\frac{k}{R^{k+1}}\left(c_{k} \cos k \phi+d_{k} \sin k \phi\right)-k R^{k-1}\left(a_{k} \cos k \phi+b_{k} \sin k \phi\right)\right\}\) .

Evidently \(a_{k}=c_{k}=0 ; b_{k}=d_{k}=0 \text { except } k=5 ; a=5 \epsilon_{0}\left(\frac{1}{R^{6}} d_{5}+R^{4} b_{5}\right)\) . Also, V is continuous at s = R:

\(a_{0}+R^{5} b_{5} \sin 5 \phi=\bar{a}_{0}+\frac{1}{R^{5}} d_{5} \sin 5 \phi \text {. So } a_{0}=\bar{a}_{0} \text { (might as well choose both zero) } ; R^{5} b_{5}=R^{-5} d_{5}, \text { or } d_{5}=R^{10} b_{5}\) .

Combining these results: \(a=5 \epsilon_{0}\left(R^{4} b_{5}+R^{4} b_{5}\right)=10 \epsilon_{0} R^{4} b_{5} ; b_{5}=\frac{a}{10 \epsilon_{0} R^{4}} ; d_{5}=\frac{a R^{6}}{10 \epsilon_{0}}\) . Therefore

\(V(s, \phi)=\frac{a \sin 5 \phi}{10 \epsilon_{0}}\left\{\begin{array}{l}s^{5} / R^{4}, \text { for } s<R, \\R^{6} / s^{5}, \text { for } s>R\end{array}\right\}\) .
