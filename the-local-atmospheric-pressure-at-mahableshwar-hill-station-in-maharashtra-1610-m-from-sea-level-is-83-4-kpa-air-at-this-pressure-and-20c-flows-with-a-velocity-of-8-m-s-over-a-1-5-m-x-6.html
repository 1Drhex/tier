Guest Question.

The local atmospheric pressure at Mahableshwar hill station in Maharashtra (1610 m from sea level) is 83.4 kPa. Air at this pressure and 20°C flows with a velocity of 8 m/s over a 1.5 m × 6 m flat plate whose temperature is 134°C. Determine the rate of heat transfer from the plate, if the air flows parallel to (a) 6 m long side, and (b) the 1.5 m side.

 

Admin
Solution

To find : Rate of heat transfer from the plate, if
(a) L = 6 m, and (b) L = 1.5 m.
Analysis : The film temperature
\(T_f = \frac{T_s + T_∞}{2}= \frac{20 + 134}{2} = 77°C = 350 K\)
The density of air
\(ρ = \frac{p}{RT}=\frac{83.4kPa}{(0.287 kJ/kg.K) (20 + 273)(K) }= 0.991 kg/m^3\)
The density is only the function of pressure and other properties are independent of pressure. Thus from Table A-4 ;
\(k_f = 0.030 W/m.K, µ = 2.075 × 10^{–5} kg/ms\)
Pr = 0.697
(a) When air flows parallel to 6 m side, the Reynolds number
\(Re_L =\frac{ρ u_∞ L}{µ}=\frac{0.991 ×8 ×6}{2.075 × 10^{-5}} = 2292434\)
which is greater than 5 × 105, thus there would be a combined laminar and turbulent flow. The average Nusselt number
\(Nu = (0.037 Re_L^{0.8} – 871) Pr^{1/3}\)
\(= [0.037 × (2292434)^{0.8} – 871] × (0.697)^{1/3} = 3248\)
Then \(h = \frac{Nu k_f}{L} = \frac{3248 × 0.030 }{6} = 16.25 W/m^2.K\)
The heat transfer rate from the plate
\(Q = h (wL) (T_s – T_∞)\)
\(= 16.25 × (1.5 × 6) × (134 – 20)\)
= 16,672 W.
(b) When air flows is parallel to 1.5 m side of plate
\(Re_L = \frac{0.991 ×8 ×1.5}{2.075 × 10^{-5}} = 5.73 × 10^5\)
which is again slightly greagter than \(5 × 10^5,\) thus using
\(Nu = [0.037 (Re_L)^{0.8} – 871] Pr^{1/3} = 553.5\)
and \(h = \frac{553.5 × 0.030}{1.5}\)
\( = 11.07 W/m^2.K\)

The heat transfer rate
Q = 11.07 × (1.5 × 6) × (134 – 20)
= 11,358 W.
