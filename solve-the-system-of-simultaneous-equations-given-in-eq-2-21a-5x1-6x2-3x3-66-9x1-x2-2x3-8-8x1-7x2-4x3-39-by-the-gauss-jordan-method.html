Guest Question.

Solve the system of simultaneous equations given in Eq. 2.21(a) \(5x_{1} + 6x_{2} -3x_{3} =66 , 9x_{1} - x_{2} + 2x_{3} =8 , 8x_{1} - 7x_{2} + 4x_{3} = -39\) by the Gauss–Jordan method.

Admin
Solution

The given system of equations can be written in matrix form as
Ax = P

\(\left[\begin{matrix} 5 & 6 & -3 \\ 9 & -1 & 2 \\ 8 & -7 & 4 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{3} \end{matrix} \right] = \left[\begin{matrix}66 \\ 8 \\-39 \end{matrix} \right]\) (1)

from which we form the augmented matrix

\( G=[A \overset{:}{:} P]=\left[\begin{matrix} 5 &6 & -3\overset{:}{:} & 66 \\9 & -1 & 2 \overset{:}{:} & 8 \\ 8 & -7 & 4 \overset{:}{:}&-39 \end{matrix} \right]\) (2)

We begin Gauss–Jordan elimination by dividing row 1 of the augmented matrix by \(G_{11} = 5\) to obtain

\( G=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 &1.2 & -0.6\overset{:}{:} & 13.2 \\9 & -1 & 2 \overset{:}{:} & 8 \\ 8 & -7 & 4 \overset{:}{:}&-39 \end{matrix} \right]\) (3)

Next, we multiply row 1 by \(G_{21}\) = 9 and subtract it from row 2; then multiply row 1 by \(G_{31}\) = 8 and subtract it from row 3. This yields

\( G=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 &1.2 & -0.6\overset{:}{:} & 13.2 \\0 & -11.8 & 7.4\overset{:}{:} &-110.8 \\ 0 & -16.6 & 8.8 \overset{:}{:}&-144.6 \end{matrix} \right]\) (4)

We now divide row 2 by \(G_{22}\)= −11.8 to obtain

\( G=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 &1.2 & -0.6\overset{:}{:} & 13.2 \\0 & 1& -0.6271\overset{:}{:} &9.39 \\ 0 & -16.6 & 8.8 \overset{:}{:}&-144.6 \end{matrix} \right]\) (5)

Next, we multiply row 2 by \(G_{12}=1.2\) and subtract it from row 1, and then multiply row 2 by \(G_{32}\)= −16.6 and subtract it from row 3. Thus,

\( G=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 &0 & 0.1525\overset{:}{:} &1.932 \\0 & 1& -0.6271\overset{:}{:} &9.39 \\ 0 & 0& -1.61\overset{:}{:}&11.27 \end{matrix} \right]\) (6)

By dividing row 3 by \(G_{33}\)= −1.61, we obtain

\( G=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 &0 & 0.1525\overset{:}{:} &1.932 \\0 & 1& -0.6271\overset{:}{:} &9.39 \\ 0 & 0&1\overset{:}{:}&-7 \end{matrix} \right]\) (7)

Finally, we multiply row 3 by \(G_{13}\) = 0.1525 and subtract it from row 1; then multiply row 3 by \(G_{23}\)= −0.6271 and subtract it from row 2 to obtain

\( G=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 &0 & 0\overset{:}{:} &3 \\0 & 1& 0\overset{:}{:} &5 \\ 0 & 0&1\overset{:}{:}&-7 \end{matrix} \right]\) (8)

Thus, the solution of the given system of equations is

\(x=\left[\begin{matrix} 3 \\ 5 \\ -7 \end{matrix} \right]\)

To check our solution, we substitute the numerical value of x back into Eq. (1). This yields

\(\left[\begin{matrix}5 & 6 &-3 \\ 9 & -1 & 2 \\ 8 & -7 & 4\end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} 3 \\ 5 \\ -7 \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix} 15+30+21=66 \\27-5-14=8\\ 24-35-28=-39 \end{matrix} \right]\)
