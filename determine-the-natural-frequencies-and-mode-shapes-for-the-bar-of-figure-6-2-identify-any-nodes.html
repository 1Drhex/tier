Guest Question.

Determine the natural frequencies and mode shapes for the bar of Figure 6.2. Identify any nodes

Admin
Solution

The differential equation of the system is derived in . The natural frequencies do not depend on the choice of generalized coordinates, but the mode shape vectors are specific to the choice of generalized coordinates. The nodes are not dependent on the choice of generalized coordinates. Using x and as generalized coordinates, the natural frequencies are determined through application of Equation (6.7).

\( \begin{vmatrix} -\omega ^{2}m+2k & -k\frac{L}{4} \\ -k\frac{L}{4} & -\omega ^{2}\frac{1}{12}mL^{2}+k\frac{5L^{2}}{16} \end{vmatrix} =0\) (a)

\( \chi _{1}=\frac{\begin{vmatrix}-\omega ^{2}m_{1,1}& +& k_{1,1}&0\\ -\omega ^{2}m_{2,1}& +& k_{2,1}&0 \end{vmatrix} }{\det (-\omega ^{2}M +k)}\) (6.7)

Evaluation of the determinant leads to

\((-\omega ^{2}m+2k)\left(-\omega ^{2}\frac{1}{12}mL^{2}+k\frac{5L^{2}}{16} – \right)\biggl(-k\frac{L}{4} \biggr)\biggl(-k\frac{L}{4}\biggr) =0 \) (b)

Expansion of the above gives

\(\frac{1}{12}m^{2}L^{2}\omega ^{4} -\frac{46}{96}mkL^{2}\omega ^{2}+\frac{9}{16}k^{2}L^{2} =0\) (c)

Multiplying Equation (c) by \(12/m(m^{2}L^{2})\) and defining \(\phi =k/m \) and \(\lambda =\omega ^{2} \) leads to

\(\lambda ^{2}-\frac{23}{4}\phi \lambda +\frac{27}{4}\phi =0 \) (d)

Using the quadratic formula to solve Equation (d) gives

\(\lambda =\left(\frac{\frac{23}{4}\pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{23}{4} \right)^{2}-4\left(\frac{27}{4} \right) } }{2} \right)\phi =1.64\phi ,4.11\phi (e) \) (e)

Recalling that \(\omega =\sqrt{\lambda } \) and \(\phi =k/m \) yields

\(\omega _{1}=1.28\sqrt{\frac{k}{m} } \) \(\omega _{2}=2.02\sqrt{\frac{k}{m} } \) (f)

The mode shapes are calculated using Equation (6.9). For \(\omega _{1}=1.28\sqrt{\phi } \), this yields

\(\chi _{1}=\frac{\left(1.64\frac{k}{m} \right)m -2k}{-k\frac{L}{4} }=\frac{1.42}{L} \) (g)

\(\begin{bmatrix} -\omega^{2}m _{1,1} &+&k_{1,1}&-\omega^{2} m_{1,2} &+&k_{1,2} \\ -\omega^{2}m _{2,1} &+&k_{2,1}&-\omega^{2} m_{2,2} &+&k_{2,2} \end{bmatrix}\left[\begin{matrix} \chi _{1} \\ \chi _{2} \end{matrix} \right] = \left[\begin{matrix}0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ]\) (6.9)

For \(\omega _{2}=2.07\sqrt{\phi } \), Equation (6.9) gives

\(\chi _{2}=\frac{\left(4.11\frac{k}{m} \right)m-2k }{-k\frac{L}{4} }=\frac{8.42}{L} \) (h)

The mode shape vectors are

\( X_{1}=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 \\ \frac{1.42}{L} \end{matrix} \right] \) \(X_{2}=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 \\ \frac{-8.42}{L} \end{matrix} \right] \) (i)

The mode shapes are illustrated in Figure 6.4. The first mode has no nodes on the bar, but it represents rigid-body motion about an axis through point O, which is not on the bar. Point O is a distance 0.19L from the end of the bar. The second mode has one node and represents a rigid-body motion about an axis through point P, which is a distance of 0.118 to the right of the mass center.
