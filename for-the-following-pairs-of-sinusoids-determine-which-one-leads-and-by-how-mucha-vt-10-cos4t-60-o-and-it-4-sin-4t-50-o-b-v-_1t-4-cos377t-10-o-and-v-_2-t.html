Guest Question.

For the following pairs of sinusoids, determine which one leads and by how much

(a) v(t) = 10 cos(4t – \(60 ^{o}\) ) and i(t) = 4 sin (4t + \(50 ^{o}\) )

(b) \(v _{1}\)(t) = 4 cos(377t + \(10 ^{o}\) ) and \(v _{2}\) (t) = -20 cos 377

(c) x(t) = 13 cos 2t + 5 sin 2t and y(t) = 15 cos(2t –\(11.8 ^{o}\) )

Admin
Solution

(a) \( \text { v }(\text { t })=10 \cos \left(4 t-60^{\circ}\right)\)
\(\mathrm{i}(\mathrm{t})=4 \sin \left(4 \mathrm{t}+50^{\circ}\right)=4 \cos \left(4 \mathrm{t}+50^{\circ}-90^{\circ}\right)=4 \cos \left(4 \mathrm{t}-40^{\circ}\right)\)
Thus, i(t) leads v(t) by \(20^{\circ}\)

(b) \(\quad \mathrm{v}_{1}(\mathrm{t})=4 \cos \left(377 \mathrm{t}+10^{\circ}\right)\)
\(\mathrm{v}_{2}(\mathrm{t})=-20 \cos (377 \mathrm{t})=20 \cos \left(377 \mathrm{t}+180^{\circ}\right)\)
Thus, \({v}_{2}({t})\) leads \({v}_{1}(f{t})\) by \({1} 70^{\circ}\)

(c) x(t)=\(13 \cos (2 t)+5 \sin (2 t)=13 \cos (2 t)+5 \cos \left(2 t-90^{\circ}\right)\)
\( X=13 \angle 0^{\circ}+5 \angle-90^{\circ}=13-\mathrm{j} 5=13.928 \angle-21.04^{\circ}\)
x(t)=\(13.928 \cos \left(2 t-21.04^{\circ}\right)\)
y(t)=\(15 \cos \left(2 t-11.8^{\circ}\right)\)
phase difference \(=-11.8^{\circ}+21.04^{\circ}=9.24^{\circ}\)
Thus, y(t) leads x(t) by \(9.24^{\circ}\)
