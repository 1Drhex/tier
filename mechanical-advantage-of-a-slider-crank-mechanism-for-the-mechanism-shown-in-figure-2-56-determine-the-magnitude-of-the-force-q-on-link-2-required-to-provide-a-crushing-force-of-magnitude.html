MECHANICAL ADVANTAGE OF A SLIDER CRANK MECHANISM

For the mechanism shown in Figure 2.56, determine the magnitude of the force \(\overline{ Q}\) on link 2 required to provide a crushing force of magnitude 20 N from link 4 for the position shown.

c = 5.0 cm; d = 15.0 cm

Admin
Solution

The instantaneous centers are shown in Figure 2.57. This mechanism is in the group of rotating-translating. From Figure 2.57 we have

\(r_{in}=c=5.0\)cm; \(r_{p_{1,2}p_{2,4}}=4.5\) cm

Using Equation (2.15-12), the mechanical advantage of the mechanism is

\(M.A=\frac{F_{out}}{F_{in}}=\frac{r_{in}}{r_{p_{1,in} p_{in,out} }}=\frac{c}{r_{p_{1,2}p_{2,4}}}=\frac{5.0}{4.5}=1.1\)

and therefore

\(F_{in}=\frac{F_{out}}{M.A}=\frac{2.0N}{1.1}=18 N\)

which is the magnitude of the force to be applied perpendicular to link 2 at B (see Figure 2.57). Comparing Figures 2.56 and 2.57, the magnitude of the required force \(\overline{ Q}\) is determined by recognizing that an equivalent moment about the base pivot O\(_2\) is obtained if

\(Qd =F_{ in}c\)

and therefore

\(Q =\frac cd F_{in} = \frac{5.0}{15.0}18N= 6.0\) N

An alternative method of force analysis of mechanisms is presented in Chapter 8.
