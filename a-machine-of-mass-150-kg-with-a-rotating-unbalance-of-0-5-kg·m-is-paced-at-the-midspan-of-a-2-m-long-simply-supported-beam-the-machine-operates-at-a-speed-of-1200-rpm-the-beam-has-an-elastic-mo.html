Guest Question.

A machine of mass 150 kg with a rotating unbalance of \(0.5 kg\cdot m\) is paced at the midspan of a 2-m-long simply supported beam. The machine operates at a speed of 1200 rpm. The beam has an elastic modulus of \(210 \times 10^9 N/m^2\) and a cross-sectional moment of inertia of \(2.1×10^6 m^4\).
(a) What is the steady-state amplitude of the primary system without an absorber?
(b) Design the dynamic vibration absorber of minimum mass such that, when attached to the midspan of the beam, the vibrations of the beam will cease and the steady-state amplitude of the absorber will be less than 20 mm.
(c) What are the system’s natural frequencies when the absorber is in place?
(d) What is the effective operating range such that the midspan deflection does not exceed 5 mm when the absorber is in place?

Admin
Solution

Modeling the vibrations of the machine on the beam using a SDOF system model and ignoring the mass of the beam, the stiffness and natural frequency of the primary system are calculated as

\( k_{1}=\frac{48EI}{L^{3}}=\frac{48(210\times10^{9}N/m^{2})(2.1\times10^{-6}m^{4})}{(2m)^{3}} =2.65\times10^{6}=2.65\times 10^{6} N/m\) (a)

and

\(\omega _{11}=\sqrt{\frac{k_{1}}{m_{1}} } =\sqrt{\frac{2.65\times10^{6}N/m}{150kg} }=132.9 rad/s \) (b)

The operating speed is

\(\omega =(1200rpm)\left(2\pi\frac{rad}{rev} \right) \left(\frac{1 \min }{60 s} \right)=125.7 rad/s \) (c)

(a) Since the excitation speed is near the natural frequency of the primary system, it will have large amplitude vibrations without an absorber. The frequency ratio is

\( r=\frac{\omega }{\omega_{11}}=\frac{125.7 rad/s}{132.9 rad/s}=0.945 \) (d)

Steady-state amplitude of the machine is

\( X_{1}=\frac{m_{0}e}{m}\Lambda (0.945,0)=\left(\frac{0.5kg\cdot m}{150kg} \right) \frac{(0.945)^{2}}{1-(0.945)^{2}}=0.285m \) (e)

(b) Steady-state vibrations of the primary system are eliminated when the absorber is tuned
to the excitation frequency using

\(\omega _{22}=\sqrt{\frac{k_{2}}{m_{2}} }=125.7 rad/s \) (f)

Since the ratio of the absorber stiffness to absorber mass is fixed, the absorber with the minimum mass is also the absorber with the minimum stiffness. The amplitude of the absorber is to be limited to 20 mm, which from Equation (6.94) leads to

\(X_{2}=\frac{F_{0}}{k_{2}}\Rightarrow k_{2}\geq \frac{F_{0}}{X_{2}}=\frac{(0.5kg\cdot m)(125.7 rad/s)^{2}}{0.002m} =3.95\times10^{5} N/m \) (g)

\( X_{2}=\frac{F_{0}}{k_{2}} \) (6.94)

The minimum absorber stiffness is \(3.95 \times 10^{5} N/m,\) leading to an absorber mass of

\(m_{2}=\frac{k_{2}}{\omega ^{2}_{22}}=\frac{3.95\times10^{5}N/m}{(125.7 rad/s)^{2}}=25 kg \) (h)

(c) The natural frequencies of the two degree-of-freedom system are calculated from Equation (6.88) using \(\mu =\frac{25kg}{150kg}=0.167 \) as

\( \omega _{1}=105.7 rad/s \) (i)

\( \omega =\frac{\omega _{1,1}}{\sqrt{2} }=\sqrt{1+\left(\frac{\omega _{2,2}}{\omega _{1,1}}\right)^{2}(1+\mu )\pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{\omega _{2,2}}{\omega 1,1} \right)^{4} }(1+\mu )^{2}+2\left(\frac{\omega _{2,2}}{\omega 1,1}\right)^{2} (\mu -1)+1 } \) (6.88)

(d) The effective operating range is obtained by setting \( F_{0}=0.5 \omega ^{2}\) and using Equation (6.90). The denominator is negative between the two natural frequencies, and the numerator is positive for \(r_{2}\lt 1 \). Take away the absolute value symbol and set \(X_{1}=0.005m \) in this
case. Rearrange the equation to

\(\omega ^{4}-7.63\times10^{4}\omega ^{2}+8.28\times 10^{8}=0 \) (j)

which (when solved for ) leads to a lower bound on the operating range of 114.8 rad/s. For \(r_{2}\gt 1 \), set \(X_{1}=0.005 m \), leading to

\( \omega^{4}-2.79\times/10^{4}\omega^{2}+1.67\times 10^{8}=0\) (k)

and a upper bound on the operating range of 138.5 rad/s. Thus, the effective operating range is

\( 114.8 rad/s\lt \omega \lt 138.5 rad/s \) (l)
