LEAKAGE FLOW PAST A PISTON

A hydraulic system operates at a gage pressure of 20 MPa and 55°C. The hydraulic fluid is SAE 10W oil. A control valve consists of a piston 25 mm in diameter, fitted to a cylinder with a mean radial clearance of 0.005 mm. Determine the leakage flow rate if the gage pressure on the low-pressure side of the piston is 1.0 MPa. (The piston is 15 mm long.)

Admin
Solution

Given: Flow of hydraulic oil between piston and cylinder, as shown. Fluid is SAE 10W oil at 55°C.
Find: Leakage flow rate, \(Q\).
Solution:
The gap width is very small, so the flow may be modeled as flow between parallel plates. Equation 8.6c may be applied.
Governing equation:

\(\frac{Q}{l}=\frac{a^3 Δp}{12μL}\) (8.6c)

 

Assumptions:

(1) Laminar flow.
(2) Steady flow.
(3) Incompressible flow.
(4) Fully developed flow.
(Note \(L / a=15 / 0.005=3000 !\) )
The plate width, \(l\), is approximated as \(l=\pi D\). Thus

\(Q=\frac{\pi D a^{3} \Delta p}{12 \mu L}\)

For SAE \(10 \mathrm{~W}\) oil at \(55^{\circ} \mathrm{C}, \mu=0.018 \mathrm{~kg} /(\mathrm{m} \cdot \mathrm{s})\), from Fig. A.2, Appendix A. Thus

\(Q =\frac{\pi}{12} \times 25 \mathrm{~mm} \times(0.005)^{3} \mathrm{~mm}^{3} \times(20-1) 10^{6} \frac{\mathrm{N}}{\mathrm{m}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{m} \cdot \mathrm{s}}{0.018 \mathrm{~kg}} \times \frac{1}{15 \mathrm{~mm}} \times \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}\)

 

\(Q =57.6 \mathrm{~mm}^{3} / \mathrm{s} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad Q}\)

 

To ensure that flow is laminar, we also should check the Reynolds number.

\(\bar{V}=\frac{Q}{A}=\frac{Q}{\pi D a}=57.6 \frac{\mathrm{mm}^{3}}{\mathrm{~s}} \times \frac{1}{\pi} \times \frac{1}{25 \mathrm{~mm}} \times \frac{1}{0.005 \mathrm{~mm}} \times \frac{\mathrm{m}}{10^{3} \mathrm{~mm}}=0.147 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\)

and

\(\operatorname{Re}=\frac{\rho \bar{V} a}{\mu}=\frac{\mathrm{SG} \rho_{\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}} \bar{V} a}{\mu}\)

For SAE \(10 \mathrm{~W}\) oil, SG \(=0.92\), from Table A.2, Appendix A. Thus

 

\(R e=0.92 \times 1000 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}} \times 0.147 \frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}} \times 0.005 \mathrm{~mm} \times \frac{\mathrm{m} \cdot \mathrm{s}}{0.018 \mathrm{~kg}} \times \frac{\mathrm{m}}{10^{3} \mathrm{~mm}}=0.0375\)

Thus flow is surely laminar, since \(\operatorname{Re} \ll 1400\).
