Guest Question.

Let us consider an elementary single-phase L-SRM with the data: airgap g = 0.3 m, U-shape core leg width a = 10 mm, the primary and secondary pole length \(b_{p}=b_{s}=10mm\). Calculate
1. Thrust and maximum normal force for \(\mu _{iron}=\infty\), Ni = 500 A turns
2. The normal airgap flux density \(B_{g1}\)
3. Copper losses for \(j_{co}=3 A/mm^{2}\) and coil equal width and height \((W_{c}=h_{c})\)

Admin
Solution

1. We may use directly Equation 11.9 for \(x=b_{p}\):

\(F_{ni}\approx -\frac{\mu _0W_1^2i^2}{2} \cdot \frac{a\cdot x}{2g^2} \lt 0\) (11.9)

\(F_{x}=\frac{1.1256\times 10^{-6}\times 500^{2} }{2}\times \frac{10^{-2} }{2\times 0.3\times 10^{-3} }=2.616N\) (11.11)

\(F_{nm ax}=-\frac{1.1256\times 10^{-6}\times 500^{2}\times 10^{-2}\times 10^{-2} }{4\times 0.3^{2}\times 10^{-6} }=87.22N\) (11.12)

With a total active area of primary\( A=2a\cdot b_{p}=2 cm^{2}\), it means a specific thrust \( f_{x}=1.308 N/cm^{2}\) and a specific maximum normal thrust \( f_{nmax}=-43.61N/cm^{2} \).
2. The normal airgap flux density \( B_{n}\) is

\(B_{n}=\frac{\mu _{0}W1 }{2g}=\frac{1.256\times 10^{-6}\times 500 }{2\times 0.3\times 10^{-3} }=1.0466T\) (11.13)

3. There are two semicoils. The cross section of each of them, \(A_{Co}\), is

\(A_{Co}=\frac{\left\lgroup \frac{WI}{2} \right\rgroup }{j_{co}K_{fill} }=\frac{\frac{500}{2} }{3\times 0.50}=166.66mm^{2} \) (11.14)

But \(A_{Co}=h_{c}\cdot w_{c}=h^{2}_{c}=166.66mm^{2},h_{c}= w_{c}=12.91mm. \)

The turn average length \(I_{c} \) is

\(I_{c}\approx 2a+2b_{p}+4W_{c}=2\times 10^{-2}+2\times 10^{-2}+4\times 1.291\times 10^{-2}=9.164 \times 10^{-2} m\) (11.15)

So the copper losses are

\(P_{Co}=\frac{2\delta_{Co}I_{c}Wj_{Co}i^{2} }{i}=2\delta_{Co}I_{c}Wj_{Co}i=2.21\times 10^{-8} \times 9.164 \times 10^{-2} \times 250\times 3\times 10^{6} =2.886 W \) (11.16)

Note: Though the numerical example is elementary, the force densities \(f_{x},f_{n},\) and the thrust/losses (N/W) are typical for well-designed practical L-SRMs.
