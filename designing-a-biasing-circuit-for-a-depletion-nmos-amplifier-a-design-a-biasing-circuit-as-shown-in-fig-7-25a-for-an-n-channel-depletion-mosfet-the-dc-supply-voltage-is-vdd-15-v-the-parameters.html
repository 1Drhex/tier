Guest Question.

Designing a biasing circuit for a depletion NMOS amplifier

(a) Design a biasing circuit as shown in Fig. 7.25(a) for an n-channel depletion MOSFET. The DC supply voltage is \( V_{ DD }=15 V \). The parameters are \( I_{ DSS }=12.65 mA \text { and } V_{ p }=-3.5 V \). Assume operation in the saturation region.

(b) Calculate the small-signal parameters \( g_{ m } \text { and } r_{ o } \) of the transistor.

(c) Use PSpice/SPICE to verify your design.

Admin
Solution

(a) To accommodate the maximum AC swing and the variations in depletion MOS parameters, the following conditions as listed in Table 7.2 are recommended for biasing for the Q-point \( \left(I_{ D }, V_{ DS }\right) \):

 

\( i_{ D }=\frac{I_{ DSS }}{2} \) (7.49)

 

\( v_{ DS }=\frac{V_{ DD }}{3} \) (7.50)

 

That is,

 

\( i_{ D }=\frac{I_{ DSS }}{2}=\frac{12.65 mA }{2}=6.3 mA \quad \text { and } \quad v_{ DS }=\frac{V_{ DD }}{3}=\frac{15}{3}=5 V \)

 

Substituting \( K_{ n }=I_{ DSS } / V_{ p }^{2} \) in Eq. (7.21),

 

\(\begin{aligned}i_{ D } &=K_{ n }\left[2\left(v_{ GS }-V_{ p }\right)\left(v_{ GS }-V_{ p }\right)-\left(v_{ GS }-V_{ p }\right)^{2}\right] \\&=K_{ n }\left(v_{ GS }-V_{ p }\right)^{2}\end{aligned}\) (7.21)

 

\( 6.3 mA =12.65 mA \times\left(1+\frac{v_{ GS }}{3.5}\right)^{2} \text { or }\left(1+\frac{v_{ GS }}{3.5}\right)^{2}=\pm 0.706 \)

 

which gives \( v_{ GS }=-1.03 V \text { or }-5.97 V \). Since \( v_{ GS }>V_{ p }(=-3.5 V ), \), the operational value of \( v_{ GS } \text { is }-1.03 V \).

 

Since \( v_{ GS } \) of a depletion MOSFET is negative (-1.03 V), we do not need the biasing resistance \( R_{1}(=\infty) \) because \( R_{ SR } \) will cause a voltage drop of \( v_{ GS }=-R_{ SR } i_{ D } \). This arrangement is known as self-biasing of the depletion MOSFET as shown in Fig. 7.24(b).

We find that

\( R_{ SR }=\frac{-v_{ GS }}{i_{ D }}=\frac{1.03 V }{6.3 mA }=163.5 \Omega \)

 

and its power rating is

\( P_{ RSR }=\left(6.3 \times 10^{-3} A \right)^{2} \times 163.5 \Omega=6.49 mW \)

 

Since \( R_{ D } i_{ D }=V_{ DD }-R_{ SR } i_{ D }-v_{ DS }=15-1.03-5=8.97 V \)

 

\( R_{ D }=\frac{8.97 V }{6.3 mA }=1424 \Omega \)

 

and its power rating is

 

\( P_{ RD }=\left(6.3 \times 10^{-3} A \right)^{2} \times 1424 \Omega=56.52 mW \)

 

Since one side of \( R_{2} \) is connected to the ground and the gate–source junction is like a reverse-biased diode, the DC current flowing through \( R_{2} \) is very small, tending to zero. \( R_{2} \) provides continuity of the circuit for the gate–source biasing voltage. In selecting the value of \( R_{2} \), it is important to keep two things in mind: (1) \( R_{2} \) should match the reverse-bias resistance of the gate–source junction, and (2) \( R_{2} \) will carry current when an AC signal is applied to the gate terminal. A value of \( R_{2} \) between 50 kΩ and 500 kΩ is generally suitable. Let \( R_{2} = \) 500 kΩ.

(b) \( K_{ n }=I_{ DSS } / V_{ p }^{2}=12.65 mA /(3.5 V )^{2}=1.033 mA / V ^{2} .\) From Eq. (7.30),

 

\( g_{ m }=\frac{\delta i_{ D }}{\delta v_{ GS }}=2 K_{ n }\left(v_{ GS }-V_{ p }\right) \quad \text { for depletion MOSFETs } \) (7.30)

 

\( g_{ m }=2 K_{ n }\left(v_{ GS }-V_{ p }\right)=2 \times 1.033 m \times(-1.03+3.5)=5.10 mA / V \)

 

From Eq. (7.26),

 

\( \frac{1}{r_{ o }}=\frac{d i_{ D }}{d v_{ DS }}=\frac{I_{ D }}{\left|V_{ M }\right|}=\lambda I_{ D } \text { for all MOSFETs } \) (7.26)

 

\( r_{ o }=\frac{1}{\lambda i_{ D }}=\frac{1}{5.929 m \times 6.3 mA }=26.77 k \Omega \)

 

(c) The biasing circuit for PSpice simulation is shown in Fig. 7.27. For PSpice simulation, we use \( K_{ p }=2 \times K_{ n }=2 \times 1.033=2.066 mA / V ^{2} \text { for } W=L=1 \mu m \) and \( V_{ to }=V_{ p }=-3.5 V .\) The details of the DC bias calculations by the PSpice simulation are given here:

 

\(\begin{array}{ll}ID =6.46 E -03(6.3 mA ) & VGS =-1.06 E +00(-1.03 V ) \quad \text { VDS }=4.74 E +00(5 V ) \\GM =5.29 E -03(5.10 mA / V ) & GDS =6.17 E -05\left(1 / r_{ o }=1 / 15.81 k =63.25 \mu A / V \right)\end{array}\)

 

NOTE: Notice from the output file of EX7-4.SCH that PSpice uses VTO = -3 V (instead of -3.5 V), LAMBDA = 2.250000E-03 \( \text { (instead of } \left.5.929 E -3 V ^{-1}\right) \), and BETA = 1.304000E-03 \( \text { (instead of } \left.1.033 mA / V ^{2}\right) \). For this reason, the results from PSpice and hand calculations differ significantly. If we recalculated the values of \( R_{ D } \text { and } R_{ SR } \) with the PSpice parameters or changed the MOS parameters in the model statement, the results would be very close. If you run the simulation with EX7-4.CIR, the results will be closer to the hand calculations: \( I_{ D }=6.14 mA \text { and } V_{ GS }=1.11 V \).
