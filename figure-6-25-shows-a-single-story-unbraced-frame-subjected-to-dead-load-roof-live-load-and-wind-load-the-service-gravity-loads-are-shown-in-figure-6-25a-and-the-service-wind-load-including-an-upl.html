Guest Question.

Figure 6.25 shows a single-story, unbraced frame subjected to dead load, roof live load, and wind load. The service gravity loads are shown in Figure 6.25a, and the service wind load (including an uplift, or suction, on the roof) is shown in Figure 6.25b. Use A992 steel and select a W12 shape for the columns (vertical members). Design for a drift index of 1⁄400 based on service wind load. Bending is about the strong axis, and each column is laterally braced at the top and bottom. Use LRFD.

Admin
Solution

Figure 6.26 shows the results of first-order analyses for dead load, roof live load, wind acting on the roof, and lateral wind load. The dead load results include the effects of an added notional load. Member stiffnesses were not reduced, and the effective length method is applicable. All vertical loads are symmetrically placed and contribute only to the \(M_{nt}\) moments. The lateral load produces an \(M_{\ell t}\) moment.

 

Load combinations involving dead load, D, roof live load, \(L_{r}\), and wind, W, are
as follows.

Combination 2: \(1.2 D + 0.5 L_{r}\)

\(P_{nt} = 1.2(14) + 0.5(26) = 29.8\) kips
\(M_{nt} = 1.2(50) + 0.5(94) = 107\) ft-kips
\(M_{\ell t} = 0\)

Combination 3: \(1.2 D + 1.6 L_{r} + 0.5 W\)

\(P_{nt} = 1.2(14) + 1.6(26) = 58.4\) kips, \(P_{\ell t }= 0.5(–9 + 1) = − 4.0\) kips (use 0.)
\(M_{nt} = 1.2(50) + 1.6(94) = 210.4\) ft-kips,
\(M_{\ell t} = 0.5(–32 + 20) = – 6.0\) ft-kips (use 0.)

Combination 4: \(1.2D + 1.0W + 0.5Lr\)

\(P_{nt} = 1.2(14) + 0.5(26) = 29.8 kips, P_{\ell t} = 1.0(–9 + 1) = − 8.0\) kips (use 0.)
\(M_{nt }= 1.2(50) + 0.5(94) = 64.7\) ft-kips
\(M_{\ell t} = 1.0(–32 + 20) = –12.0\) ft-kips (use 0.)

Load combination 3 will obviously govern. It produces the largest axial load and the largest total moment. (Combination 4 could not control unless \(B_{2}\), the amplification factor for \(M_{\ell t}\), were unrealistically large.)

For purposes of selecting a trial shape, assume that \(B_{1} = 1.0\). The value of \(B_{2}\) can be computed using the design drift index and a value of \(P_{e story}\) from AISC Equation A-8-7:

\(P_{e story}=R_{M}\frac{HL}{Δ_{H}}=R_{M}\frac{H}{Δ_{H}/L}=0.85(\frac{2.7}{1/400})=918.0\) kips

(The unfactored horizontal load, H = 2.7 kips, is used because the drift index is based on the maximum drift caused by service loads.)

\(B_{2}=\frac{1}{1-\frac{αP_{ story}}{P_{e story}}}≈\frac{1}{1-\frac{1.0(58.4)}{918.0}}=1.068\)

 

The amplified axial load is

\(P_{r} = P_{u} = P_{nt} + B_{2}P_{\ell t} = 58.4 + 0 = 58.4\) kips

and the amplified bending moment is

\(M_{u }= B_{1}M_{nt} + B_{2}M_{\ell t} = 1.0(210.4) + 0 = 210.4\) ft-kips

Without knowing the frame member sizes, the alignment chart for the effective length factor cannot be used. Table C-A-7.1 in the Commentary to the Specification reveals that case (f) corresponds most closely to the end conditions for the sidesway case of this example and that \(K_{x} = 2.0\).

For the braced condition, \(K_{x} = 1.0\) will be used, and because the member is braced in the out-of-plane direction, \(K_{y} = 1.0\) will be used. A shape can be selected by using the method given in Part 6 of the Manual. Since the bending moment appears to be dominant in this member, use Equation 6.10.

Try a W12 × 53. From Table 6-1, with \(KL = L_{b} = 15\) ft, \(p = 2.09 × 10^{−3}\), and \(b_{x} = 3.45 × 10^{−3}\).

Check to see which axis controls the axial compressive strength. From Table 6-1, \(r_{x}/r_{y} = 2.11\) and

\(\frac{K_{x}L}{r_{x}/r_{y}}=\frac{2.0(15)}{2.11}=14.2\) ft \( <K_{y}L=15 ft \) ∴ KL=15 ft as assumed.

Verify that Equation 6.10 is the correct one:

\(\frac{P_{u}}{Φ_{c}P_{n}}=p P_{u}=(2.09\times 10^{-3})(54.97)=0.1149<0.2\)

∴ Equation 6.10 controls as assumed.

For a bending moment that varies linearly from zero at one end to a maximum at the other, the value of \( C_{b}\) is 1.67 (see Figure 5.15g). Modify \(b_{x}\) to account for \( C_{b}.\)

\( \frac{b_{x} }{C_{b} } =\frac{3.45\times 10^{-3} }{1.67} =2.07\times 10^{-3}\)

For \( L_{b} = 0, b_{x} = 3.04 × 10^{–3}.\)

Since \( 3.04 × 10^{–3} > 2.07 × 10^{–3}\) , use \( b_{x} = 3.04 × 10^{–3}.\)

\( P_{e1}=\frac{^{2}EI_{x} }{(K_{x}L )^{2} } =\frac{^{2}(29,000)(425) }{(1.0\times 15 \times 12)^{2} }=3754\) kips

\( C_{m}=0.6-0.4\left(\frac{M_{1} }{M_{2} } \right) =0.6-0.4\left(\frac{0}{M_{2}} \right) =0.6\)

From AISC Equation A-8-3,

\( B_{1} =\frac{C_{m} }{1-(P_{r}/P_{e1} )} =\frac{C_{m} }{1-[(P_{nt}+ P_{\ell t})/P_{e1}]}\)

\( =\frac{0.6}{1-1.0[(58.4+0)]/3754} =0.610\lt 1.0 \) \( \therefore\) Use \( B_{1} =1.0.\)

Since \(B_{1}\) is the value originally assumed and \( B_{2}\) will not change, the previously computed value of \( M_{u} = 205.7\) ft-kips is unchanged.

Check Equation 6.10 with \( p = 2.09 × 10^{–3}\) and \( b_{x} = 3.04 × 10^{–3}.\)

\( 0.5 p P_{u}+(\frac{9}{8} )(b_{x}M_{ux} + b_{y}M_{uy} )=0.5(2.09 × 10^{-3})(54.97)\)

\( +(\frac{9}{8} )[(3.04×10^{-3})(205.7)+0]=0.761<1.0 \) (OK)

This result is significantly smaller than 1.0, so try a shape two sizes smaller.

 

Try a W12 × 45. From Table 6-1, \( p = 3.16 × 10^{–3}, b_{x} = 4.58 × 10^{–3}\) , and \( r_{x}/r_{y} = 2.64.\)

\(\frac{K_{x}L}{r_{x}/r_{y}}=\frac{2.0(15)}{2.64}=11.36\) ft \(<K_{y}L=15\) ft ∴ KL=15 ft as assumed.

 

\(\frac{P_{u}}{Φ_{c}P_{n}}=p P_{u}=(3.16\times 10^{-3})(54.97)=0.1737<0.2\)

∴ Equation 6.10 controls as assumed.

Modify \( b_{x}\) to account for \(C_{b}\).

\( \frac{b_{x} }{C_{b} } =\frac{4.58\times 10^{-3} }{1.67} =2.74\times 10^{-3}\)

This is less than \(b_{x}\) for \( L_{b} = 0\), which is \(3.69 × 10^{−3}\), so use \( b_{x} = 3.69 × 10^{−3}.\)
For the braced condition,

\( P_{e1}=\frac{^{2}EI_{x} }{(K_{x}L )^{2} } =\frac{^{2}(29,000)(348) }{(1.0\times 15\times 12)^{2} }=3074\) kips

From AISC Equation A-8-3,

\( B_{1} =\frac{C_{m} }{1-(P_{r}/P_{e1} )} =\frac{C_{m} }{1-[(P_{nt}+ P_{\ell t})/P_{e1}]}\)

\( =\frac{0.6}{1-1.0[(58.4+0)]/3074} =0.612\lt 1.0\) \( \therefore\) Use \( B_{1} =1.0.\)

From Equation 6.10,

\( 0.5 pP_{u}+(\frac{9}{8} )(b_{x}M_{ux} + b_{y}M_{uy} )=0.5(3.16× 10^{-3})(54.97)\)

\( +(\frac{9}{8} )[(3.69×10^{-3})(205.7)+0]=0.941<1.0\) (OK)

Use a W12 × 45.
