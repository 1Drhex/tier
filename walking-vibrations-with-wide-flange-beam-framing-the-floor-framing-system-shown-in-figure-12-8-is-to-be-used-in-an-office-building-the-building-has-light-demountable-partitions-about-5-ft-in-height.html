Walking Vibrations with Wide-flange Beam Framing

The floor framing system shown in Figure 12-8 is to be used in an office building. The building has light, demountable partitions about 5 ft. in height. Check if the floor is adequate for walking vibrations. Assume normal weight concrete with a density of 145 pcf and a 28-day compressive strength of 4000 psi. The floor loads are as follows:

Floor dead load = 55 psf (includes weight of slab, deck, finishes, mechanical and electrical fixtures, and partitions) Sustained live load = 11 psf (see Table 12-1)

Table 12-1 Recommended sustained live load values

Occupancy Sustained Live Load
Office floors 11 psf
Residential floors 6 psf
Footbridges, gymnasiums, shopping center floors 0 psf
Admin
Solution

Beam Section Properties and Deflection
From Part 1 of the AISCM for a W16 × 26:

d = 15.7 in.

A = 7.68 \(in.^{2} \)

I = 301 \(in.^{4} \)

Effective slab width, \(b_{eff}\) ≤ 0.4 × Beam span = (0.4)(24 ft.) = 9.6 ft.

≤Beam tributary width = 6 ft. = 72 in. → Governs

\(t_{c}\)= Concrete slab thickness above deck ribs = 2 in.

\(h_{r}\) = Depth of metal deck ribs = 2 in.

Note: Only concrete above the deck ribs is used to compute the \(I_{comp}\) for the beam

\(E_{s}\) = 29,000 ksi

\(E_{c} =33w^{1.5} _{c}\sqrt{f′_{c} } \)

 

\( =(33)(145)^{1.5}\sqrt{4000 } \) =3644 ksi

\(n= \frac{E_{s} }{1.35E_{c} }\)

 

\( = \frac{29,000}{(1.35)(3644) }= 5.9\)

\(b_{e}\)= Transformed effective width of the concrete slab

\( =\frac{b_{eff} }{n}\)

 

\( =\frac{72 in. }{5.9} = 12.2\) in.

The centroid (see Figure 12-9) is found by summing the moments of areas about the top of the slab. From statics,

\(\overline{y} =\frac{\sum{Ay} }{\sum{A} }\)

 

\(= \frac{\left[(2 in.)(12.2 in.)\left(\frac{2 in.}{2} \right) \right]+\left[(7.68 in.^{2} )\left(\frac{15.7 in.}{2}+4 in. \right) \right] }{[(2 in.)(12.2 in.)] +7.68 in.^{2} } = 3.6\) in.

The composite moment of inertia is

\(I_{comp}=\sum{I} + Ad^{2}\)

 

\(=(12.2 in.)\left(\frac{(2)^{3} }{12} \right)+(12.2 in.)(2 in.)\left(3.6 in. – \frac{2 in.}{2} \right) ^{2}\)

 

\(+ 301 in.^{4}+(7.68 in.^{2} )\left(\frac{15.7 in.}{2}+4 in. – 3.6 in. \right) ^{2}\)

 

= 996 \( in.^{4}\)

w = (55 psf + 11 psf)(6 ft.) = 396 lb./ft.

= 33 lb./in.

\(\Delta _{j}= \frac{5wL^{4} _{j} }{384EI_{j} }\)

 

\(= \frac{(5)(33)(24 ft. \times 12)^{4} }{(384)(29 \times 10^{6} )(996)} = 0.102\) in.

Girder Section Properties and Deflection

From Part 1 of the AISCM for a W24 × 76:

d = 23.9 in.

A = 22.4 \(in.^{2}\)

I = 2100 \(in.^{4}\)

Effective slab width, \(b_{eff}\)≤0.4 × Girder span = 0.4 × 18 ft. = 7.2 ft. = 86.4 in. → Governs

≤ Girder tributary width = 24 ft.

\(t_{c}\) = Concrete slab thickness above deck ribs = 2 in.

\(h_{r}\) =Depth of metal deck ribs = 2 in.

\(d_{e}=t_{c}+\frac{1}{2}h_{r}\)

 

=\( (2 in.)+\frac{1}{2} (2 in.)= 3\) in.

Note: Concrete within and above the deck ribs is used to compute the \(I_{comp}\) for the girder.

\(b_{e}\)= Transformed effective width of the slab

\( = \frac{b_{eff} }{n} \)

 

\( = \frac{86.4 in.}{5.9}=14.6\) in.

The centroid (see Figure 12-10) is found by taking a summation of the moments of the areas about the top of the slab. From statics,

\(\overline{y} =\frac{\sum{Ay} }{\sum{A} }\)

 

\( = \frac{\left[(3 in.)(14.6 in.)\left(\frac{3 in.}{2} \right) \right]+\left[(22.4 in.^{2} )\left(\frac{23.9 in.}{2}+ 4 in. \right) \right] }{[(3 in.)(14.6 in.)]+ 22.4 in.^{2}} = 6.4\) in.

The composite moment of inertia is

\(I_{comp}=\sum{I} + Ad^{2}\)

 

\( =(14.6 in.)\left(\frac{(3)^{3} }{12} \right) + (14.6 in.)(3 in.)\left(6.4 in. – \frac{3 in.}{2} \right) ^{2} \)

 

\(+ 2100 in.^{4}+(22.4 in.^{2} ) \left(\frac{23.9 in.}{2} + 4 in. – 6.4 in.\right) ^{2}\)

=5230 \(in.^{2}\)

w = (55 psf + 11 psf)(24 ft.) = 1584 plf

= 132 lb./in.

\(\Delta _{g}=\frac{5wL^{4} _{g} }{384EI_{g} }\)

 

\( = \frac{(5)(132)(18 ft. \times 12)^{4} }{(384)(29 \times 10^{6} )(5230)} = 0.025\) in.

Beam effective weight,\(W_{j}\)

\(W_{j}=\gamma w_{t}B_{j}L_{j}\)

The beams are likely to be connected to girders at both ends with simple shear connectors (e.g., clip angles and shear plates) and the adjacent beam span is greater than 70% of the span of the beam being considered (both spans are equal). Therefore, γ = 1.5.

\(D_{s}=\frac{d^{3} _{e} }{n}\)

 

\( =\frac{(3 in.)^{3} }{5.9}=4.6 in.^{4}/ft.\)

 

\(D_{j}= \frac{I_{j} }{S}\)

 

\(= \frac{996 }{6 ft.} =166 in.^{4}/ft.\)

 

\(C_{j}\) = 2.0 (interior beam is being considered)

\(B_{j}= C_{j}\left(\frac{D_{s} }{D_{j} } \right) ^{0.25} L_{j}\leq 2⁄3\) floor width

\( = 2.0\left(\frac{4.6 }{166 } \right) ^{0.25} (24 ft.)\leq (2⁄3) (90 ft.)\)

=19.6 ft. < 60 ft.

\(B_{j}\)= 19.6 ft.

\(W_{j}\) = (1.5)(55 psf + 11 psf)(19.6 ft.)(24 ft.) = 46,570 lb.

Girder effective weight, \(W_{g}\):

\(W_{g}= w_{t}B_{g}L_{g}\)

Since the span of the girder is less than the width of the beam panel(\(L_{g}\) = 18 ft.< \(B_{j}\) 19.6 ft.), the girder deflection has to be modified using equation (12-25):

\(\Delta _{g} ^{′}=\frac{L_{g} }{B_{j} }\Delta _{g}\geq 0.5\Delta _{g}\) (12-25)

\( =\left(\frac{18 ft.}{19.6 ft.} \right)(0.025 in.)\geq(0.5)(0.025 in.)\)

 

=0.023 in. > 0.013in.

\(\Delta _{g} ^{′}\) = 0.023 in.

\(D_{g}=\frac{I_{g} }{L_{j} }\)

 

\( =\frac{5230 }{24 ft. } = 218 in.^{4}/ft.\)

 

\(C_{g}\) = 1.8 (girders supporting rolled steel beams)

\(B_{g}=C_{g}\left( \frac{D_{j} }{D_{g} } \right)^{0.25}L_{g}\leq 2⁄3 floor length\)

 

\( =1.8\left( \frac{166 }{218 } \right)^{0.25}18 ft.\leq (2⁄3) (72 ft.)\)

= 30.3 ft. < 48 ft.

\(B_{g}\) = 30.3 ft.

\(W_{g}\) = (55 psf + 11 psf)(30.3 ft.)(18 ft.) = 36,000 lb.

Effective weight of the floor system, W:

From equation (12-13),

\(W = \left(\frac{\Delta _{j} }{\Delta _{j}+\Delta _{g}} \right) W_{b}+ \left(\frac{\Delta _{g} }{\Delta _{j}+\Delta _{g}} \right)W_{g}\) (12-13)

 

\(W = \left(\frac{0.102 in. }{0.102 in.+0.023 in.} \right)(46,570 lb.) + \left(\frac{0.023 in. }{0.102 in. + 0.023 in.} \right)(36,000 lb.)\)

 

= 44,625 lb.

The natural frequency of the floor system is determined from equation (12-4):

\(f_{n}= 0.18\sqrt{\frac{g}{\Delta _{j}+\Delta _{g} } }\) (12-4)

\( = 0.18\sqrt{\frac{386}{ 0.102 in. + 0.023 in.} } = 10.0 Hz(\gt 3 Hz, initially OK.)\)

 

From Table 12-2,

Table 12-2 Recommended vibration values for variables in equation (12-12)

Occupancy Constant Force,\( P_{o}\) Damping Ratio,β Acceleration Limit,\(\frac{a_{o} }{g}\times\) 100%
Offices, residences, churches 65 lb. \(0.02 – \times 0.05^{1}\) 0.5%
Shopping malls 65 lb. 0.02 1.5%
Footbridges—Indoor 92 lb. 0.01 1.5%
Footbridges—Outdoor 92 lb. 0.01 5.0%

\(^{1}\)β = 0.02 for floors with few nonstructural components (e.g., paperless or electronic office)

= 0.03 for floors with nonstructural components and furnishings and small demountable partitions = 0.05 for fixed full-height partitions between floors

 

\(P_{o}\)= 65 lb., and

β = 0.03 (light, demountable partitions, less than 5 ft. in height).

The acceleration of the floor system due to walking vibrations is calculated using equation (12-12):

\(\frac{a_{p} }{g}=\frac{P_{o}e^{(- 0.35f_{n} )} }{\beta W}\leq \frac{a_{0} }{g}\) (12-12)

 

\( =\frac{65e^{(- 0.35)(10.0)} }{(0.03)(44,625)}=0.00146\), or 0.146%.

From Table 12-2, the recommended acceleration limit is \(a_{0}/g\) = 0.5%, so this floor is adequate for walking vibrations. Since the natural frequency of the floor is greater than 9 Hz, the floor stiffness is required to be greater than 5.7 kips/in. (see equation (12-26)). This condition will now be checked.

\(K_s = \frac{1}{\Delta _p} \geq 5700 \) lb/in., (12-26)

The number of effective floor beams is determined from equation (12-31), but we must first determine whether the aforementioned limitations are met (see eqs. (12-32) through (12-34)):

\(N_{eff}= \text{Number of effective beams or joists} \\ \quad = 0.49 + 34.2 \frac{d_{e}}{S} + (9.0 × 10^{-9})\frac{L_j^4}{I_t}-0.00059\left(\frac{L_j}{S} \right)\geq 1.0\) (12-31)

\(0.018\leq \frac{d_{e} }{s}\leq 0.208\) (12-32)

 

\(0.018\leq \frac{3 in. }{72 in.}=0.042\leq 0.208 OK\)

 

\(4.5\times 10^{6}\leq \frac{L^{4} _{j} }{I_{j} }\leq 257 \times 10^{6}\)

 

\(4.5\times 10^{6}\leq \frac{(24\times 12)^{4} }{996}=6.9 \times 10^{6} \leq 257 \times 10^{6} OK\)

 

\(2\leq \frac{L_{j} }{s}\leq 30\) (12-34)

 

\(2\leq \frac{24 ft. }{6 ft.}=4\leq 30 OK\)

If the above parameters calculated from equations (12-32) through (12-34) were not satisfied, the variables in these equations would have to be changed until the conditions were met.

The number of effective beams is

\(N_{eff} =0.49 +34.2 \frac{d_{e} }{S}+ (9.0 \times 10^{- 9} )\frac{L^{4} _{j} }{I_{j} }- 0.00059\left(\frac{L_{j} }{S} \right) ^{2} \geq 1.0\)

 

\( =0.49 +34.2\left( \frac{3 in. }{72 in. }\right) + (9.0 \times 10^{- 9} )\frac{(24 \times 12)^{4} }{966 }- 0.00059\left(\frac{24 }{6} \right) ^{2} \geq 1.0\)

= 1.97

The individual joist or beam deflection under a unit concentrated load is

\(\Delta _{oj}=\frac{L^{3} }{48EI_{j} }\) (a factor of 1/48 is conservatively assumed)

\( =\frac{(24\times 12)^{3} }{48(29\times 10^{6} )(996) }= 17.2 \times 10^{-6}\) in./lb. (deflection under a unit load of 1 lb.)

Beam panel deflection under a unit concentrated load:

\(\Delta _{jp}=\frac{\Delta _{oj}}{N _{eff}}\)

 

\(=\frac{0.0000172}{1.97}=8.73\times 10^{-6}\) in.lb.

The girder panel deflection under a unit concentrated load is

\(\Delta _{gp}=\frac{L^{3} _{g}}{48EI_{g} }\) (a factor of 1/48 is conservatively assumed)

\( =\frac{(18\times12 )^{3}}{48(29\times 10^{6} )(5230) }=1.38\times 10^{-6}\) in./lb. (deflection under a unit load of 1 lb.)

Total floor deflection under a unit concentrated load:

\(\Delta _{p}=\Delta _{jp}+\frac{\Delta _{gp}}{2}\)

 

\( =(8.73\times 10^{- 6} )+\frac{1.38\times 10^{- 6} }{2}=9.42\times 10^{- 6}\) in.lb.

The floor stiffness is then

\(K_{s}=\frac{1}{\Delta _{p}}\geq 5700\) lb./in.

 

\( =\frac{1}{9.42\times 10^{- 6} }=106,000 lb./in.\geq 5700\) lb./in. Floor stiffness is OK
