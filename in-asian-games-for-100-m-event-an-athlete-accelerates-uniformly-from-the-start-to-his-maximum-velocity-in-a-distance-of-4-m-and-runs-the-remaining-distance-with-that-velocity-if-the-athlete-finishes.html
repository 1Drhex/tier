Guest Question.

In Asian games, for 100 m event an athlete accelerates uniformly from the start to his maximum velocity in a distance of 4 m and runs the remaining distance with that velocity. If the athlete finishes the race in 10.4 seconds, determine (i) his initial acceleration and (ii) his maximum velocity.

Admin
Solution

Method I : By using equation of motion

(i) Consider the uniform acceleration motion (uam) from A to B [Refer to Fig. 11.5(a)].

Initial velocity u = 0 ; Final velocity \( ν = ν_{max} \) ; Displacement s = 4 m ;

Acceleration a = ? ; Time t = ?

ν= u + at.

\( ν_{max} = 0 + at = at \) ….. (I)

\( s = ut + \frac{1}{2} at^2 \) ⇒ \( 4= 0 + \frac{1}{2} at^2 \) ⇒ 8 = (at)t.

\( 8 = (ν_{max})t \) ∴ \( ν_{max} = \frac{8}{t} \) ….. (II)

(ii) Consider the uniform velocity motion (uνm) from B to C.

Velocity = \( ν_{max}\) ; Time = (10.4 – t).

Displacement = Velocity × Time.

\( 96 =ν_{max} (10.4 – t) \).

Substituting \( ν_{max} = \frac{8}{t} \) from Eq. (II), we get \( 96 =\frac{8}{t} (10.4 – t) \).

∴ 96t = 8 × 10.4 – 8t ⇒ 104t = 8 × 10.4 ∴ t = 0.8 sec.

(iii) From Eq. (II)

\( ν_{max} = \frac{8}{t} =\frac{8}{0.8}\) ∴ \( ν_{max} = 10 \) m/s Ans.

(iv) From equation (I), \( ν_{max} = at \).

\( a = \frac{ ν_{max}}{t} = \frac{10}{0.8} \) ∴ \( a = 12 m/s^2\) Ans.

Method II : By using ν-t diagram

Consider the v-t diagram shown in Fig. 11.5(b).

(i) Let t be the time taken by athlete to attain maximum velocity \( ( ν_{max}) \) in a distance of 4 m.

∴ Time taken for remaining distance 96 m will be (10.4 – t).

Area under ν-t diagram is displacement, so we have

\( 4 = \frac{1}{2} × t × ν_{max} \) and \( 96 = ν_{max} (10.4 – t) \)

∴ \( ν_{max} = \frac{8}{t} \) and \( ν_{max} = \frac{96}{10.4 – t} \)

Equating \( ν_{max} \), we have

∴ \( \frac{8}{t} = \frac{96}{10.4 – t} \) ∴ t = 0.8 sec.

(ii) \( ν_{max} = \frac{8}{t} = \frac{8}{0.8} = 10\) m/s. Ans.

(iii) Acceleration = Slope of ν-t diagram

\( a = \frac{ν_{max}}{t} = \frac{10}{0.8}\) ∴ \( a = 12.5 m/s^2\) Ans.
