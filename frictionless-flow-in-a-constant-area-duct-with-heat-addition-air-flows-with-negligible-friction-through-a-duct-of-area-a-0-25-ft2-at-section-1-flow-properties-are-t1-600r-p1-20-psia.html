FRICTIONLESS FLOW IN A CONSTANT-AREA DUCT WITH HEAT ADDITION

Air flows with negligible friction through a duct of area \(A = 0.25\) ft\(^2\). At section (1), flow properties are \(T_1 = 600\)°R, \(p_1 = 20\) psia, and \(V_1 = 360\)ft/s. At section (2), \(p_2 = 10\) psia. The flow is heated between sections (1) and (2). Determine the properties at section (2), the energy added, and the entropy change. Finally, plot the process on a \(Ts\) diagram.

Admin
Solution

Given: Frictionless flow of air in duct shown:


\(T_1 = 600\)°R
\(p_1 = 20\) psia \(p_2 = 10\) psia
\(V_1 = 360\) ft/s \(A_1 = A_2 = A = 0.25\) ft\(^2\)
Find:

(a) Properties at section (2).
(b) \(δQ/dm\).
(c) \(s_2 – s_1\).
(d) \(Ts\) diagram.
Solution:
The momentum equation (Eq. 13.43b) is

 

\(p_{1} A-p_{2} A=\dot{m} V_{2}-\dot{m} V_{1}\) (13.43b)

or

\(p_{1}-p_{2}=\frac{\dot{m}}{A}\left(V_{2}-V_{1}\right)=\rho_{1} V_{1}\left(V_{2}-V_{1}\right)\)

Solving for \(V_{2}\) gives

\(V_{2}=\frac{p_{1}-p_{2}}{\rho_{1} V_{1}}+V_{1}\)

For an ideal gas, Eq. 13.43e,

\(\begin{aligned}&\rho_{1}=\frac{p_{1}}{R T_{1}}=20 \frac{\mathrm{lbf}}{\mathrm{in} .^{2}} \times 144 \frac{\mathrm{in} \cdot{ }^{2}}{\mathrm{ft}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{lbm} \cdot \mathrm{R}}{53.3 \mathrm{ft} \cdot \mathrm{lbf}} \times \frac{1}{600^{\circ} \mathrm{R}}=0.0901 \mathrm{lbm} / \mathrm{ft}^{3} \\\quad\\&V_{2}=(20-10) \frac{\mathrm{lbf}}{\mathrm{in} .^{2}} \times 144 \frac{\mathrm{in} .^{2}}{\mathrm{ft}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{ft}^{3}}{0.0901 \mathrm{lbm}} \times \frac{\mathrm{s}}{360 \mathrm{ft}} \times 32.2 \frac{\mathrm{lbm}}{\mathrm{slug}} \times \frac{\mathrm{slug} \cdot \mathrm{ft}}{\mathrm{lbf} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}+360 \frac{\mathrm{ft}}{\mathrm{s}} \\\quad\\&V_{2}=1790 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad V_2}\end{aligned}\)

 

From continuity, Eq. 13.43a, \(G=\rho_{1} V_{1}=\rho_{2} V_{2}\), so

\(\rho_{2}=\rho_{1} \frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}}=0.0901 \frac{\mathrm{lbm}}{\mathrm{ft}^{3}}\left(\frac{360}{1790}\right)=0.0181 \mathrm{lbm} / \mathrm{ft}^{3}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad \rho_2}\)

Solving for \(T_{2}\), we obtain

 

\(T_{2}=\frac{p_{2}}{\rho_{2} R}=10 \frac{\mathrm{lbf}}{\mathrm{in} .^{2}} \times 144 \frac{\mathrm{in} .^{2}}{\mathrm{ft}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{ft}^{3}}{0.0181 \mathrm{lbm}} \times \frac{\mathrm{lbm} \cdot \mathrm{R}}{53.3 \mathrm{ft} \cdot \mathrm{lbf}}=1490^{\circ} \mathrm{R}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad T_{2}}\)

 

The local isentropic stagnation temperature is given by Eq. 12.21 b,

\(\begin{aligned}T_{0_{2}} &=T_{2}\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{2}^{2}\right) \\\quad\\c_{2} &=\sqrt{k R T_{2}}=1890 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s} ; \quad M_{2}=\frac{V_{2}}{c_{2}}=\frac{1790}{1890}=0.947 \\\quad\\T_{0_{2}} &=1490^{\circ} \mathrm{R}\left[1+0.2(0.947)^{2}\right]=1760^{\circ} \mathrm{R}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad T_{0_{2}}}\end{aligned}\)

 

and

\(p_{0_{2}}=p_{2}\left(\frac{T_{0_{2}}}{T_{2}}\right)^{k /(k-1)}=10 \mathrm{psia}\left(\frac{1760}{1490}\right)^{3.5}=17.9 \mathrm{psia}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad p_{0_{2}}}\)

The heat addition is obtained from the energy equation (Eq. 13.43c),

\(\frac{\delta Q}{d m}=\left(h_{2}+\frac{V_{2}^{2}}{2}\right)-\left(h_{1}+\frac{V_{1}^{2}}{2}\right)=h_{0_{2}}-h_{0_{1}}\) (13/43c)

or

\(\frac{\delta Q}{d m}=h_{0_{2}}-h_{0_{1}}=c_{p}\left(T_{0_{2}}-T_{0_{1}}\right)\)

 

We already obtained \(T_{0_{2}}\). For \(T_{0_{1}}\) we have

\(\begin{aligned}&T_{0_{1}}=T_{1}\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{1}^{2}\right) \\\quad\\&c_{1}=\sqrt{k R T_{1}}=1200 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s} ; \quad M_{1}=\frac{V_{1}}{c_{1}}=\frac{360}{1200}=0.3 \\\quad\\&T_{0_{1}}=600^{\circ} \mathrm{R}\left[1+0.2(0.3)^{2}\right]=611^{\circ} \mathrm{R}\end{aligned}\)

so

 

\(\frac{\delta Q}{d m}=0.240 \frac{\mathrm{Btu}}{\mathrm{lbm}^{\circ} \mathrm{R}}(1760-611)^{\circ} \mathrm{R}=276 \mathrm{Btu} / \mathrm{lbm}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad δQ/dm}\)

For the change in entropy (Eq 13.43g),

\(\Delta s=s_{2}-s_{1}=c_{p} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-R \ln \frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}=c_{p} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-\left(c_{p}-c_{v}\right) \ln \frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}\) (13.43g)

Then

\(\begin{aligned}&s_{2}-s_{1}=0.240 \frac{\mathrm{Btu}}{\mathrm{lbm} \cdot{ }^{\circ} \mathrm{R}} \times \ln \left(\frac{1490}{600}\right)-(0.240-0.71) \frac{\mathrm{Btu}}{\mathrm{lbm} \cdot{ }^{\circ} \mathrm{R}} \times \ln \left(\frac{10}{20}\right) \\\quad\\&s_{2}-s_{1}=0.266 \mathrm{Btu} /(\mathrm{lbm} \cdot \mathrm{R})\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad s_{2}-s_{1}}\end{aligned}\)

 

The process follows a Rayleigh line:

To complete our analysis, let us examine the change in \(p_{0}\) by comparing \(p_{0_{2}}\) with \(p_{0_{1}}\).

 

\(\begin{aligned} p_{0_{1}} &=p_{1}\left(\frac{T_{0_{1}}}{T_{1}}\right)^{k /(k-1)}=20.0 \mathrm{psia}\left(\frac{611}{600}\right)^{3.5} \\\quad\\&=21.3 \mathrm{psia} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad p_{0_{1}}} \end{aligned}\)

 

Comparing, we see that \(p_{0_{2}}\) is less than \(p_{0_{1}}\).

 

This problem illustrates the use of the basic equations, Eqs. 13.43, for analyzing frictionless flow of an ideal gas in a duct with heat exchange.

\(\begin{gathered}\rho_1V_1=\rho_2V_2=\rho VA\equiv G=\frac{\dot m}{A}=\text{constant}\\\quad\\p_{1} A-p_{2} A=\dot{m} V_{2}-\dot{m} V_{1} \\\quad\\\frac{\delta Q}{d m}=\left(h_{2}+\frac{V_{2}^{2}}{2}\right)-\left(h_{1}+\frac{V_{1}^{2}}{2}\right)=h_{0_{2}}-h_{0_{1}} \\\quad\\\dot{m}\left(s_{2}-s_{1}\right) \geq \int_{\mathrm{CS}} \frac{1}{T}\left(\frac{\dot{Q}}{A}\right) d A \\\quad\\p=\rho R T \\\quad\\\Delta h=h_{2}-h_{1}=c_{p} \Delta T=c_{p}\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right) \\\quad\\\Delta s=s_{2}-s_{1}=c_{p} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-R \ln \frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}\end{gathered}\) (13.43 a, b, c, d, e, f, g)
