Guest Question.

A plate girder is one of two supporting a railway bridge deck, which has a simply supported span of 12 m. In a worst-case scenario, a stationary railway engine produces two concentrated loads of 1500 kN, 4 m apart on the bridge deck with each load being 4 m from the end of the bridge. If the cross section of a girder is that shown in Fig. P.10.18 where the flange plates are welded to the girder web, calculate the shear force/mm on a weld and recommend a suitable weld size (see Section 22.2) assuming an allowable weld stress of 100 N/mm² . For the purposes of calculation, regard the cross section of the girder as thin walled and take the density of steel to be 7750 kg/mm³.

Admin
Solution

The weight/unit length of girder is given by, referring to Fig. P.10.18,
\( \text { Weight/unit length }=(2 \times 30 \times 400+1400 \times 10) \times 7750 \times 1 \times 9.81 \times 10^{-3}=2.9 kN / m \text {. } \)
Therefore, one girder is subjected to the loading system shown in Fig. S.10.18(a).

The total load on a girder \( =2 \times 750+2.9 \times 12=1534.8 kN.\)

The support reactions are then both = 767.4 kN, which will be the maximum shear force on a girder.
Since the girder section is doubly symmetrical and only a single vertical shear force is applied, Eq. (10.22) reduces to

\( q_{s}=\frac{\left(S_{y} I_{z y}-S_{z} I_{z}\right)}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t z d s+\frac{\left(S_{z} I_{z y}-S_{y} I_{y}\right)}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s \) (10.22)

 

\( q_{s}=-\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s \) (i)

Referring to Fig. P.10.18

\(I_{z}=2 \times 400 \times 30 \times 700^{2}+10 \times 1400^{3} / 12=1.4 \times 10^{10} mm ^{4}\)

Substituting for \( S_{y}(=767.4 kN ) \text { and } I_{z} \) in Eq. (i) gives

\( q_{s}=-5.5 \times 10^{-5} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s \)

Now referring to Fig. S.10.18(b)

in the top flange

\( q_{12}=-5.5-10^{-5} \int_{0}^{s_{1}} 30 \times 700 d s_{1} \)

which gives

\( q_{12}=-1.16 s_{1} \)

Then, when \( s_{1}=200 mm q_{2}=-232 N/mm \). There will be an identical contribution from the shear flow in the flange 23 so that the total shear flow at the top of the web will be 464 N/mm. This is resisted by the two fillet welds so that the shear force/mm of weld is 232 N. The required weld throat thickness, \( t_{ W } \), is given by Eq. (22.10)
i.e.

\(F=\sigma t\) (22.10)

\( t_{ W }=\frac{232}{100}=2.32 mm \)

The leg length of the weld (see Fig. 22.12(c)) is then

\( \text { Leg length }=\sqrt{2} \times 2.32=3.3 mm \)

Say, a leg length of 4 mm.
