Guest Question.

The 9 m long 254 × 146 UB 37 braced beam of S275 steel shown in Figure 6.31a and b has a central concentrated design load of 70 kN (which includes an allowance for self-weight) and a design end moment of 70 kNm. Lateral deflections v and twist rotations Φ are effectively prevented at both ends and by a brace at mid-span. Check the adequacy of the braced beam.

Admin
Solution

Design bending moment.

 

\(R_{3} = ((70 × 4.5) − 70)/9 = 27.22 kN, M_{Ed,2} = 27.22 × 4.5 = 122.5 kNm.\)

 

Section resistance.

 

\(t_{f} = 10.9 mm, f_{y} = 275 N/mm^2\)

 

\(ε = √(235/275) = 0.924\)

 

\(c_{f} /(t_{f} ε) = (146.4/2 − 6.3/2 − 7.6)/(10.9 × 0.924) = 6.20 < 9\) and the flange is Class 1.

 

\(c_{w}/(t_{w}ε) = (256 − 2 × 10.9 − 2 × 7.6)/(6.3 × 0.924) = 37.6 < 72\) and the web is Class 1.

 

\(M_{c,Rd} = 275 × 483 × 10^3 Nmm = 132.8 kNm > 122.5 kNm = M_{Ed}\)

 

and the section resistance is adequate.

 

Elastic buckling moment.

The beam is fully restrained at mid-span, and so consists of two equal length segments 12 and 23. By inspection, the check will be controlled by segment 23 which has the lower moment gradient, and therefore the lower value of \(α_{m}\). Using Figure 6.7, \(α_{m} = 1.75\).

Using equation 6.3, \(M_{zx} =\sqrt{}\left\{\left(\frac{π^2EI_{z}}{L^2} \right)\left(GI_{t} +\frac{π^2EI_{w}}{L^2} \right) \right\} \)

 

\(M_{zx} =\sqrt{\left\{\frac{π^2 × 210 000 × 571 × 10^4}{4500^2} \times \left(81 000 × 15.3 × 10^4 +\frac{π^2 × 210 000 × 0.0857 × 10^{12}}{4500^2} \right)\right\} }Nmm = 111.2 kNm\)

 

Using equation 6.4,

\(M_{cr} = α_{m}M_{zx}\)

 

\(M_{cr} = 1.75 × 111.2 = 194.6 kNm\)

 

(Using the computer program PRFELB [18] on the segment 23 alone leads to \(M_{cr} = 204.5 kNm.\) Using PRFELB on the complete beam leads to \(M_{cr} = 237.9 kNm\), which indicates the increase caused by the restraint offered by segment 12 to segment 23. The increased elastic buckling moment may be approximated by using the improved method given in Section 6.8.2, as demonstrated in .)

 

Member resistance.

Using equation 6.25, \(\bar{\lambda }_{LT}=\sqrt{(W_{y}f_{y}/M_{cr})}\)

 

\(\bar{\lambda } _{LT} = √(132.8/194.6) = 0.826\)

 

\(h/b = 256/146.4 = 1.75 < 2\)

 

Using the EC3 simple general method with \(β = 1.0,\bar{\lambda }_{LT,0} = 0.2\) (Clause 6.3.2.2) and \(α_{LT} = 0.21\) (Tables 6.4 and 6.3), and equation 6.27,

\(Φ_{LT} = 0.5\left\{1 + α_{LT} (\bar{\lambda }_{LT} − \bar{\lambda }_{LT,0}) + β\bar{\lambda }^2_{LT}\right\},\)

 

\(Φ_{LT} = 0.5{\left\{1 + 0.21(0.826 − 0.2) + 1.0 × 0.826^2\right\} } = 0.907\)

 

Using equation 6.26,

\(\frac{M_{b,Rd}γ_{M1}}{W_{y}f_{y}}=\frac{1}{Φ_{LT} +\sqrt{Φ^2_{LT} − β\bar{\lambda }^2_{LT}} }\)

 

\(M_{b,Rd} = 132.8/(0.907 + √(0.907^2 − 0.826^2)) kNm = 103.7 kNm < 122.5 kNm = M_{Ed}\)

 

and the beam appears to be inadequate.

Using the EC3 less conservative method with \(β = 0.75, \bar{\lambda }_{LT,0} = 0.4\) (Clause 6.3.2.3) and \(α_{LT} = 0.34\) (Tables 6.5 and 6.3), and equation 6.27,

\(Φ_{LT} = 0.5\left\{1 + α_{LT} (\bar{\lambda }_{LT} − \bar{\lambda }_{LT,0}) + β\bar{\lambda }^2_{LT}\right\},\)

 

\(Φ_{LT} = 0.5{\left\{1 + 0.34(0.826 − 0.4) + 0.75 × 0.826^2\right\} } = 0.828\)

 

Using equation 6.26,

\(\frac{M_{b,Rd}γ_{M1}}{W_{y}f_{y}}=\frac{1}{Φ_{LT} +\sqrt{Φ^2_{LT} − β\bar{\lambda }^2_{LT}} }\)

 

\(M_{b,Rd} = 132.8/(0.828 + √(0.828^2 − 0.75 × 0.826^2)) kNm = 106.6 kNm.\)

 

The design moment resistance is further increased by using equations 6.29, 6.30 and 6.28 to find

 

\(6.29, f = 1 − 0.5(1 − k_{c})\left\{1 − 2(\bar{\lambda }_{LT} − 0.8)^2\right\}\)

 

\(6.30, k_{c} = 1/√C_{1} = 1/√α_{m}\)

 

\(6.28, M_{b,Rd,mod} = M_{b,Rd} /f ≤ M_{b,Rd}\)

 

\(f = 1 − 0.5 × (1 − 1/ √1.75)(1 − 2 × (0.826 − 0.8)^2) = 0.878,\) and

\(M_{b,Rd,mod} = 106.6/0.878 = 121.4 kNm < 122.5 kNm = M_{Ed}\)

 

and the design moment resistance is just inadequate.
