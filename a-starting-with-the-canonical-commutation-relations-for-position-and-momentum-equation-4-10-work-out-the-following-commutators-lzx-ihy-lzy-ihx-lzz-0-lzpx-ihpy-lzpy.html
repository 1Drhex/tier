Guest Question.

(a) Starting with the canonical commutation relations for position and momentum (Equation 4.10), work out the following commutators:

\( \left[r_{i}, p_{j}\right]=-\left[p_{i}, r_{j}\right]=i \hbar \delta_{i j}, \quad\left[r_{i}, r_{j}\right]=\left[p_{i}, p_{j}\right]=0 \) (4.10).

\( \left[L_{z}, x\right]=i \hbar y, \quad\left[L_{z}, y\right]=-i \hbar x, \quad\left[L_{z}, z\right]=0 \).

\( \left[L_{z}, p_{x}\right]=i \hbar p_{y}, \quad\left[L_{z}, p_{y}\right]=-i \hbar p_{x}, \quad\left[L_{z}, p_{z}\right]=0 \) (4.122).

(b) Use these results to obtain \( \left[L_{z}, L_{x}\right]=i \hbar L_{y} \) directly from Equation 4.96.

\( L_{x}=y p_{z}-z p_{y}, \quad L_{y}=z p_{x}-x p_{z}, \quad L_{z}=x p_{y}-y p_{x} \) (4.96).

(c) Find the commutators \( \left[L_{z}, r^{2}\right] \text { and }\left[L_{z}, p^{2}\right] \) (where, of course, \( r^{2}=x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2} \text { and } p^{2}=p_{x}^{2}+p_{y}^{2}+p_{z}^{2} \)).

(d) Show that the Hamiltonian \( H=\left(p^{2} / 2 m\right)+V \) commutes with all
three components of L, provided that V depends only on r. (Thus \( H, L^{2}\) and \( L_{z} \) are mutually compatible observables.)

Admin
Solution

(a)

\( \left[L_{z}, x\right]=\left[x p_{y}-y p_{x}, x\right]=\left[x p_{y}, x\right]-\left[y p_{x}, x\right]=0-y\left[p_{x}, x\right]=i \hbar y \).

\( \left[L_{z}, y\right]=\left[x p_{y}-y p_{x}, y\right]=\left[x p_{y}, y\right]-\left[y p_{x}, y\right]=x\left[p_{y}, y\right]-0=-i \hbar x \).

\( \left[L_{z}, z\right]=\left[x p_{y}-y p_{x}, z\right]=\left[x p_{y}, z\right]-\left[y p_{x}, z\right]=0-0=0 \).

\( \left[L_{z}, p_{x}\right]=\left[x p_{y}-y p_{x}, p_{x}\right]=\left[x p_{y}, p_{x}\right]-\left[y p_{x}, p_{x}\right]=p_{y}\left[x, p_{x}\right]-0=i \hbar p_{y} \).

\( \left[L_{z}, p_{y}\right]=\left[x p_{y}-y p_{x}, p_{y}\right]=\left[x p_{y}, p_{y}\right]-\left[y p_{x}, p_{y}\right]=0-p_{x}\left[y, p_{y}\right]=-i \hbar p_{x} \).

\( \left[L_{z}, p_{z}\right]=\left[x p_{y}-y p_{x}, p_{z}\right]=\left[x p_{y}, p_{z}\right]-\left[y p_{x}, p_{z}\right]=0-0=0 \).

(b)

\( \left[L_{z}, L_{x}\right]=\left[L_{z}, y p_{z}-z p_{y}\right]=\left[L_{z}, y p_{z}\right]-\left[L_{z}, z p_{y}\right]=\left[L_{z}, y\right] p_{z}-\left[L_{z}, p_{y}\right] z \).

\( =-i \hbar x p_{z}+i \hbar p_{x} z=i \hbar\left(z p_{x}-x p_{z}\right)=i \hbar L_{y} \).

(So, by cyclic permutation of the indices, \( \left[L_{x}, L_{y}\right]=i \hbar L_{z} \)).

(c)

\( \left[L_{z}, r^{2}\right]=\left[L_{z}, x^{2}\right]+\left[L_{z}, y^{2}\right]+\left[L_{z}, z^{2}\right]=\left[L_{z}, x\right] x+x\left[L_{z}, x\right]+\left[L_{z}, y\right] y+y\left[L_{z}, y\right]+0 \).

\( =i \hbar y x+x i \hbar y+(-i \hbar x) y+y(-i \hbar x)=0 \).

\( \left[L_{z}, p^{2}\right]=\left[L_{z}, p_{x}^{2}\right]+\left[L_{z}, p_{y}^{2}\right]+\left[L_{z}, p_{z}^{2}\right]=\left[L_{z}, p_{x}\right] p_{x}+p_{x}\left[L_{z}, p_{x}\right]+\left[L_{z}, p_{y}\right] p_{y}+p_{y}\left[L_{z}, p_{y}\right]+0 \).

\( =i \hbar p_{y} p_{x}+p_{x} i \hbar p_{y}+\left(-i \hbar p_{x}\right) p_{y}+p_{y}\left(-i \hbar p_{x}\right)=0 \).

(d) It follows from (c) that all three components of L commute with \( r^{2} \text { and } p^{2} . L_{z} \) also commutes with r, as we can show using a test function f(r):

\( \left[L_{z}, r\right] f=-i \hbar\left[x \frac{\partial}{\partial y}-y \frac{\partial}{\partial x}, r\right] f=-i \hbar\left(x \frac{\partial(r f)}{\partial y}-y \frac{\partial(r f)}{\partial x}-r x \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}+r y \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\right)=-i \hbar\left(x \frac{\partial r}{\partial y}-y \frac{\partial r}{\partial x}\right) f \).

But

\( \frac{\partial r}{\partial x}=\frac{\partial}{\partial x} \sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}=\frac{x}{r}, \quad \text { so } \quad\left(x \frac{\partial r}{\partial y}-y \frac{\partial r}{\partial x}\right)=0 \).

and hence \( \left[L_{z}, r\right]=0 \) (and the same goes for the other two components). So L commutes with H = p²/2m + V(r). QED
