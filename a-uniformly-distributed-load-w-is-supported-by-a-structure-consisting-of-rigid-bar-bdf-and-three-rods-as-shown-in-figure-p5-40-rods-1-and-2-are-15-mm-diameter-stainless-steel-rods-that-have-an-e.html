Guest Question.

A uniformly-distributed load w is supported by a structure consisting of rigid bar BDF and three rods, as shown in Figure P5.40. Rods (1) and (2) are 15-mm-diameter stainless steel rods that have an elastic modulus of E = 193 GPa and a yield strength of \(\sigma_{Y}\) = 250 MPa. Rod (3) is a 20-mm-diameter bronze rod that has an elastic modulus of E = 105 GPa and a yield strength of \(\sigma_{Y}\) = 330 MPa. Use a = 1.5 m and L = 3 m. If a minimum factor of safety of 2.5 is specified for the normal stress in each rod, calculate the maximum distributed load magnitude w that may be supported.

Admin
Solution

Equilibrium: Consider a FBD of the rigid bar. Sum forces in the vertical direction to obtain:

\(\Sigma F_{y}=F_{1}+F_{2}+F_{3}-w(2 a)=0\) (a)

Sum moments about joint F to obtain:

\(\Sigma M_{F}=w(2 a)(a)-F_{2}(2 a)-F_{1}(3 a)=0\) (b)

Geometry of Deformations: When the distributed load w is applied to the rigid bar, the bar will deflect downward but it will not remain horizontal. Rods (1), (2), and (3) are not equally spaced; however, we can use the principle of similar triangles to find that

\(\frac{v_{B}-v_{F}}{3 a}=\frac{v_{D}-v_{F}}{2 a} \quad \therefore 2 v_{B}+v_{F}=3 v_{D}\)

The rods are attached to the rigid bar with perfect connections (i.e., no gaps or clearances), therefore:

\(\delta_{1}=v_{B} \quad \delta_{2}=v_{D} \quad \delta_{3}=v_{F}\)

The geometry-of-deformation equation can be written in terms of the rod deformations as:

\(2 \delta_{1}+\delta_{3}=3 \delta_{2}\) (c)

Force-Deformation Relationships:

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}} \quad\delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}} \quad\delta_{3}=\frac{F_{3} L_{3}}{A_{3} E_{3}}\) (d)

Compatibility Equation
Substitute the force-deformation relationships (d) into the geometry-of-deformation equation (c) to derive the compatibility equation:

\(2 \frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\frac{F_{3} L_{3}}{A_{3} E_{3}}=3 \frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}\) (e)

Section Properties
For stainless steel rods (1) and (2),

\(\begin{aligned}&A_{1}=A_{2}=\frac{\pi}{4}(15 mm )^{2}=176.7146 mm ^{2} \\&\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{250 MPa }{2.5}=100 MPa \\&F_{\text {allow, } 1}=F_{\text {allow }, 2}=\left(100 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(176.7146 mm ^{2}\right)=17,671.5 N\end{aligned}\)

and for bronze rod (3),

\(\begin{aligned}&A_{3}=\frac{\pi}{4}(20 mm )^{2}=314.1593 mm ^{2} \\&\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{330 MPa }{2.5}=132 MPa \\&F_{\text {allow } 3}=\left(132 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(314.1593 mm ^{2}\right)=41,469.0 N\end{aligned}\)

Solve the Equations: Begin with the two equilibrium equations

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{1}+F_{2}+F_{3}=w(2 a) \\&F_{1}(3 a)+F_{2}(2 a)=w(2 a)(a) \quad \text { which can be simplified to } \quad 3 F_{1}+2 F_{2}=w(2 a)\end{aligned}\)

Combine these two equations to obtain:

\(2 F_{1}+F_{2}=F_{3}\)

Substitute this relationship into Eq. (e) to obtain a relationship between \(F_{1} \text { and } F_{2}\):

\(\begin{aligned}2 & \frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\frac{\left(2 F_{1}+F_{2}\right) L_{3}}{A_{3} E_{3}}=3 \frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}} \\& 2 F_{1}\left[\frac{L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\frac{L_{3}}{A_{3} E_{3}}\right]=F_{2}\left[\frac{3 L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}-\frac{L_{3}}{A_{3} E_{3}}\right]\end{aligned}\)

or, since all rods have the same length:

\(2 F_{1}\left[\frac{1}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\frac{1}{A_{3} E_{3}}\right]=F_{2}\left[\frac{3}{A_{2} E_{2}}-\frac{1}{A_{3} E_{3}}\right]\)

Solve for \(F_{1} \text { : }\)

\(F_{1}=\frac{F_{2}}{2}\left[\frac{\frac{3}{A_{2} E_{2}}-\frac{1}{A_{3} E_{3}}}{\frac{1}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\frac{1}{A_{3} E_{3}}}\right]=\frac{F_{2}}{2}\left[\frac{3-\frac{A_{1} E_{1}}{A_{3} E_{3}}}{1+\frac{A_{1} E_{1}}{A_{3} E_{3}}}\right]\)

Enter values for area, elastic modulus, and the allowable force for rod (2). Also, note that \(A_{1} E_{1}=A_{2} E_{2}\) .

Solve for \(F_{1} \text { : }\)

\(F_{1}=\frac{F_{2}}{2}\left[\frac{3-\left(\frac{176.7146 mm ^{2}}{314.1593 mm ^{2}}\right)\left(\frac{193 GPa }{105 GPa }\right)}{1+\left(\frac{176.7146 mm ^{2}}{314.1593 mm ^{2}}\right)\left(\frac{193 GPa }{105 GPa }\right)}\right]=\frac{F_{2}}{2}\left[\frac{3-1.033929}{1+1.033929}\right]=0.483319 F_{2}\)

 

\(=0.483319(17,671.5 N )=8,541.0 N \leq F_{\text {allow }, 1}\) OK

Now, the forces F1 and F2 for the stainless steel rods are tentatively known. Next, check the force in rod (3) given the forces that have just been calculated.

\(F_{3}=2 F_{1}+F_{2}=2(8,541.0 N )+17,671.5 N =34,753.4 N \leq F_{\text {allow, } 3}\) OK

The forces in all three rods have now been determined, with the strength of rod (2) controlling the capacity of the structure.

Finally, the magnitude of the allowable distributed load w can be calculated from Eq. (a):

\(\begin{aligned}w &=\frac{F_{1}+F_{2}+F_{3}}{2 a} \\&=\frac{8,541.0 N +17,671.5 N +34,753.4 N }{2(1.5 m )} \\&=20,322.0 N / m =20.3 kN / m\end{aligned}\)
