Guest Question.

A 15 hp, 240 V, 1780 r/min dc series motor has a full-load rated current of 54 A. Its operating characteristics are given by the per-unit curves of Fig. 11.

Calculate

a. The current and speed when the load torque is 24 N·m
b. The efficiency under these conditions

Admin
Solution

a. We first establish the base power, base speed, and base current of the motor. They correspond to the full-load ratings as follows:

 

\(a\begin{aligned}P_{ B } &=15 hp =15 \times 746=11190 W \\n_{ B } &=1780 r / min \\I_{ B } &=54 A\end{aligned}\)

 

The base torque is, therefore,

 

\(\begin{aligned}T_{ B } &=\frac{9.55 P_{ B }}{n_{ B }}=9.55 \times 11190 / 1780 \\&=60 N \cdot m\end{aligned}\)

 

A load torque of 24 N·m corresponds to a perunit torque of

 

\(T( pu )=24 / 60=0.4\)

 

Referring to Fig. 11, a torque of 0.4 pu is attained at a speed of 1.4 pu. Thus, the speed is

 

\(\begin{aligned}n &=n( pu ) \times n_{ B }=1.4 \times 1780 \\&=2492 r / min\end{aligned}\)

 

From the T vs I curve, a torque of 0.4 pu requires a current of 0.6 pu. Consequently, the load current is

 

\(I=I( pu ) \times I_{ B }=0.6 \times 54=32.4 A\)

 

b. To calculate the efficiency, we have to know \(P_{ o }\) and \(P_{ i }\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}P_{ i } &=E I=240 \times 32.4=7776 W \\P_{ o } &=n T / 9.55=2492 \times 24 / 9.55 \\&=6263 W \\\eta &=P_{ o } / P_{ i }=6263 / 7776=0.805 \text { or } 80.5 \%\end{aligned}\)
