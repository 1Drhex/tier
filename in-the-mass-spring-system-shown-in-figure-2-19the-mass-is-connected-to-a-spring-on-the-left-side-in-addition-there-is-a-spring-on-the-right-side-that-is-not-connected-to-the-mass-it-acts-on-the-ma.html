Guest Question.

In the mass-spring system shown in Figure 2.19,\(T+V= constant\) the mass is connected to a spring on the left side. In addition, there is a spring on the right side that is not connected to the mass. It acts on the mass if the mass displaces a distance greater than the gap d. Determine the equation or equations of motion that can account for the possibility of the mass coming in contact with the right spring. Also determine the period for a cycle. The parameter values are m = 20 kg, \(k_{1}=2.5 N/mm\), \(k_{2}=6.0 N/mm\), The mass is moved 80 mm to the left from static equilibrium and released from rest. At equilibrium the mass is d = 30 mm from the right spring.

Admin
Solution

The behavior is analyzed in two segments. In the first segment, the mass moves only under the action of the left spring force. Defining\(x_{1}\) as the position of the mass, with positive direction to the left, the equation of
motion is

\(m\ddot{x_{1} }+ k_{1} x_{1}=0\).

From Equation 2.25, the displacement response is

\(x\left(t\right)=C_{1} \sin \sqrt{\frac{k}{m} } t+C_{2}\cos \sqrt{\frac{k}{m} } t\)

\(x_{1}\left(t\right)=A \sin \omega _{n} t+B\cos \omega _{n} t\),

where \(\omega _{n} =\sqrt{k_{1}/m}=\sqrt{2.5\cdot 1000 /20}=11.18 rad/s\).

Since the initial conditions are \(x_{1}\left(0\right)=80 mm\) and \(\dot{x_{1} } \left(0\right)=0\) ,

\(x_{1}\left(t\right)=0.08 \cos 11.18t m\).

The next step is to calculate the time it takes to arrive at the right spring, traveling to position \(x_{1}\left(t_{1}\right) -0.03m\),

\(-0.03=0.08\cos 11.18 t_{1}\)

giving

\(t_{1}=0.175 s\).

When the mass touches the right spring, we reset the time to zero and define the deflection of both springs in terms of \(x_{2}\)with positive direction to the right.
The equation of motion now includes the forces of both springs, and is given by

\(m\ddot{x_{2} }+k_{1} \left(x_{2}+0.3\right) +k_{2}x_{2}=0\),

or

\(20\ddot{x_{2} }+2500 \left(x_{2}+0.3\right) +6000 x_{2}=0\)

or

\(\ddot{x_{2} }+425 x_{2}=-3.75\).

The solution is

\( x_{2}\left(t\right)=C\sin 20.62t +D\cos 20.62t-0.00882 m\).

To find C and D we satisfy the initial conditions,\( x_{2}\left(0\right)=0\) and \( \dot{x_{2} } \left(0\right)=- \dot{x_{1} }\left(0.175\right) \), where

\( \dot{x_{1} }\left(t\right) =-0.08\left(11.18\right)\sin 11.18t\).

Therefore, \(\dot{x_{2} } \left(0\right)=0.829 m/s\). Thus, D = 0.00882 and C = 0.0402, yielding

\( x_{2} \left(t\right)=0.0402 \sin 20.62t +0.00882 \cos 20.62t-0.00882 m\).

Finally, we need to calculate the time at which the mass stops moving,\(t_{2}\), which, when added to \(t_{1}\), yields the half period of oscillation. The value of \(t_{2}\) is found by setting \( \dot{x_{2} } \left(t\right)=0\) ,

\( \dot{x_{2} } \left(t_{2}\right)=0.829 \cos 20.62 t_{2}-0.181 \sin 20.62 t_{2}=0\),

or

\( \tan 20.62 t_{2}=\frac{0.829}{0.181} \)

 

\( t_{2}=\frac{1.356}{20.62}=0.0658s\)

Therefore, the period of oscillation is

\( T=2\left(t_{1}+t_{2}\right) =2\left(0.175+0.0658\right) =0.482 s\),

and the cycles continue without slowing down since there is no damping in the system.
