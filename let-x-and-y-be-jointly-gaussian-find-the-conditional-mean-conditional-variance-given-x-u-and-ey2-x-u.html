Guest Question.

Let \( X \) and \( Y \) be jointly Gaussian. Find the conditional mean, conditional variance, given \( X = u \) , \( and E(Y^{2} \mid X = u)\).

Admin
Solution

From the definition of the conditional probability density function and the forms given in and we obtain

\( p_{Y}(v \mid X = u) = \frac{1}{(2\pi)^{1/2} \sigma _{Y}(1-\rho ^{2})^{1/2}} \exp (-\frac{1}{2(1-\rho ^{2})} [(\frac{u-\mu _{X}}{\sigma _{X}} )^{2} – 2\rho (\frac{u-\mu _{X}}{\sigma _{X}} ) (\frac{v-\mu _{Y}}{\sigma _{Y}} ) + (\frac{v-\mu _{Y}}{\sigma _{Y}} )^{2}]+\frac{1}{2} (\frac{u-\mu _{X}}{\sigma _{X}} )^{2} )\)

in which \( \rho \equiv \rho _{XY} \) . Defining new quantities as

\( \tilde{\sigma }_{Y} =\sigma _{Y}(1-\rho ^{2})^{1/2}, \tilde{\mu }_{Y} =\mu _{Y}+\rho \frac{\sigma _{Y}}{\sigma _{X}} (u-\mu _{X})\)

allows this to be rewritten as

\( p_{Y}(v \mid X =u)=\frac{1}{(2\pi )^{1/2}\tilde{\sigma }_{Y} } \exp (-\frac{1}{2} (\frac{v-\tilde{\mu }_{Y} }{\tilde{\sigma }_{Y} } )^{2} )\)

Note that this expression shows that a jointly Gaussian distribution implies that the components are conditionally Gaussian. Comparing this with the standard scalar Gaussian form also shows that the conditional mean and variance are

\( E(Y \mid X=u)=\tilde{\mu }_{Y}=\mu _{Y} +\rho \frac{\sigma _{Y}}{\sigma _{X}} (u-\mu _{X}), Var (Y \mid X =u)=\tilde{\sigma }_{Y}^{2} = \sigma _{Y}^{2}(1-\rho ^{2})\)

We can now say that

\( E(Y^{2}\mid X=u)=[E(Y^{2}\mid X=u)]^{2} + Var (Y \mid X=u)\)

or

\( E(Y^{2}\mid X=u)= (\mu _{Y}+\rho \frac{\sigma _{Y}}{\sigma _{Y}} (u-\mu _{X}) )^{2} + \sigma _{Y}^{2}(1-\rho ^{2}) \)
