Guest Question.

A forced jet of water at \( 20^{\circ} C \) hits a moving vane, which is moving at a velocity of 4 m/sec (abs), at a velocity of 7 m/sec (abs), and a flowrate of \( 0.075 m^{3} /sec\), as illustrated in Figure EP 5.7. The vane angle α is \(45^{\circ}\) , and assume that the jet and the vane lie in a horizontal plane (x, y) and that flow is ideal. (a) Determine the reaction force acting on the moving vane due to the thrust force of the jet (anchoring force required to keep the moving vane from accelerating).

Admin
Solution

(a) In order to determine the reaction force acting on the moving vane due to the thrust force of the jet, the integral form of the momentum equation is applied in both the x and y directions for the control volume illustrated in Figure EP 5.7. The flow enters at control face 1 and leaves at control face 2. The viscous forces are ignored because of the assumption of ideal flow, there is no pressure force component because the jet flow is open to the atmosphere \( (p_{1} = p_{2} = 0 N/m^{2})\), and there is no gravitational force component along the x- and y-axes because the pipe is horizontal \((z_{1} = z_{2} = 0)\). Furthermore, the velocity of the jet flow at control faces 1 and 2 relative to the control volume, \(v_{1}\) and \(v_{2}\) , respectively, are computed by applying Equation 5.29 \(\overset{\rightarrow }{v_{i}} = \overset{\rightarrow }{v_{i}}(abs) – \overset{\rightarrow }{v_{cv}}(abs) \).

\( \sum{F_{x}} = R_{x}= \rho Q(v_{2x}- v_{1x}) \)
\( \sum{F_{y}} = R_{y}= \rho Q(v_{2y}- v_{1y}) \)

\( V_{1abs}: = 7 \frac{m}{sec} \) \( V_{2abs}: = 7 \frac{m}{sec} \) \( Q: = 0.075 \frac{m^{3}}{sec} \) \( \alpha : = 45 deg \)

\( V_{ vaneabs}: = 4 \frac{m}{sec} \) \( V_{cvabs}: = V_{ vaneabs} = 4 \frac{m}{sec} \)

\( V_{1}: = V_{1abs} – V_{cvabs}= 3 \frac{m}{sec} \) \( V_{2}: = V_{2abs} – V_{cvabs}= 3 \frac{m}{sec} \)

\( \rho : = 998 \frac{kg}{m^{3}} \) \( V_{1x}: = V_{1} = 3 \frac{m}{s} \) \( V_{1y}: = 0 \frac{m}{sec} \)

\( V_{2x}: = V_{2} \cos (\alpha ) = 2.121 \frac{m}{s} \) \( V_{2y}: = V_{2} \sin (\alpha ) = 2.121 \frac{m}{s} \)

Guess value: \( R_{x}: = 1000 N \) \( R_{y}: = 1000 N \)

Given

\( – R_{x} = \rho .Q (V_{2x} – V_{1x} ) \)
\( R_{y} = \rho .Q (V_{2y} – V_{1y} ) \)

 

\( \left ( \begin{matrix} R_{x} \\ R_{y} \end{matrix} \right ) : = Find (R_{x}, R_{y}) = \begin{matrix} 65.769 \\ 158.781 \end{matrix} N \)

\( R: = \sqrt{R_{x}^{2} + R_{y}^{2}} = 171.863 N \) \( \theta : = atan \left(\frac{\left|R_{y}\right| }{\left|R_{x}\right| } \right) = 67.5 deg \)

Thus, the reaction force acting on the moving vane, \(R_{x} \) acts to the left, and the reaction force acting on the moving vane, \(R_{y} \) acts upwards, both as assumed and illustrated in Figure EP 5.7.
