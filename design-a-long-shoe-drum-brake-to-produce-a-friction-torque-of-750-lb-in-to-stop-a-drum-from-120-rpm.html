Guest Question.

Design a long shoe drum brake to produce a friction torque of 750 lb . in to stop a drum from 120 rpm.

Admin
Solution

Step 1. Select a brake friction material, and specify the desired maximum pressure and the design value for the coefficient of friction. Table 22–2 lists some general properties for friction materials. Actual test values or specific manufacturer’s data should be used where possible. The design value for \(p_{max}\) should be far less than the permissible pressure listed in Table 22–2 in order to improve wear life.
For this problem, let’s choose a woven fabric material and design for approximately 75 psi maximum pressure. Note, as shown in Figure 22–19, that the maximum pressure occurs at the section 90° from the pivot. If the shoe does not extend at least 90°, the equations used here are not valid. (See Reference 4.) Also, let’s use \(f = 0.25\) for the design.

Step 2. Propose trial values for the geometry of the brake drum and the brake pad. Several design decisions must be made here. The general arrangement shown in Figure 22–19 can be used as a guide.
But your specific application and creativity may lead you to modify the arrangement.
Trial values are \(r = 4.0\) in; \(C = 8.0\) in; \(L = 15\) in; \(θ_1 = 30°\); and \(θ_2 = 150°\).
Step 3. Solve for the required width of the shoe from Equation (22–18):

\(w=\frac{T_{f}}{r^{2} f p_{\max }\left(\cos \theta_{1}-\cos \theta_{2}\right)}\)
For this problem,
\(w=\frac{750 \mathrm{lb} \cdot \mathrm{in}}{(4.0 \mathrm{in})^{2}(0.25)\left(75 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{in}^{2}\right)\left(\cos 30^{\circ}-\cos 150^{\circ}\right)}=1.443 \text { in }\)
For convenience, let \(w=1.50\) in. Because the maximum pressure is inversely proportional to the width, the actual maximum pressure will be
\(p_{\max }=75 \mathrm{psi}(1.44 / 1.50)=72.17 \mathrm{psi}\)
Step 4. Compute \(M_{N}\) from Equation (22-20). The value of \(\theta_{2}-\theta_{1}\) must be in radians, with \(\pi\) radians \(=180^{\circ}\).

\(M_N=0.25P_{max}wrC[2(\theta_2-\theta_1)-sine 2\theta_2+sin2\theta_1]\) (22.20)

Then
\(\theta_{2}-\theta_{1}=120^{\circ}\left(\pi \mathrm{rad} / 180^{\circ}\right)=2.0944 \mathrm{rad}\)
The moment of the normal force on the shoe is
\(\begin{aligned}M_{N}=& 0.25\left(72.17 \mathrm{Ib} / \mathrm{in}^{2}\right)(1.50\mathrm{in})(4.0 \mathrm{in})(8.0 \mathrm{in}) \\&\left[2(2.09)-\sin\left(300^{\circ}\right)+\sin \left(60^{\circ}\right)\right] \\M_{N}=& 5128\mathrm{lb} \cdot \mathrm{in}\end{aligned}\)

Step 5. Compute the moment of the friction force on the shoe, \(M_{f}\), from Equation (22-21):

\(M_f=fP_{max}wr[r(cos\theta_1-cos\theta_2)+0.25C(cos2\theta_2-cos2\theta_1)]\) (22.21)

\(\begin{aligned}M_{f}=& 0.25\left(72.17 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{in}^{2}\right)(1.50\mathrm{in})(4.0 \mathrm{in}) \\&\left[(4.0 \mathrm{in})\left(\cos 30^{\circ}-\cos150^{\circ}\right)\right.\\&\left.+0.25(8.0 \mathrm{in})\left(\cos 300^{\circ}-\cos60^{\circ}\right)\right] \\M_{f}=& 749.8 \mathrm{lb} \cdot \mathrm{in}\end{aligned}\)

Step 6. Compute the required actuation force, \(W\), from Equation (22-19):
\(W=\left(M_{N}-M_{f}\right) / L=(5128-749.8) /(15)=291.8 \mathrm{lb}\)
Note the minus sign for \(M_{f}\) because the drum surface is moving away from the pivot.
Step 7. Compute the frictional power from Equation (22-22):
\(P_{f}=T_{f} n /(63000)=(749.8)(120) /(63000)=1.428 \mathrm{hp}\)
Step 8. Compute the projected area of the brake shoe from Equation (22-23).
\(\begin{aligned}&A=L_{s} w=2 w r \sin \left[\left(\theta_{2}-\theta_{1}\right) /2\right]\\&A=2(1.50 \mathrm{in})(4.0 \mathrm{in}) \sin (120 \%)=10.392 \mathrm{in}^{2}\end{aligned}\)
Step 9. Compute the wear ratio, \(WR\):
\(W R=P_{f} / A=1.428 \mathrm{hp} / 10.392 \mathrm{in}^{2}=0.137 \mathrm{hp} /\mathrm{in}^{2}\)
Step 10. Evaluate the suitability of the results. In this problem, we would need more information about the application to evaluate the results. However, the wear rating seems reasonable for average service (see Section 22-11), and the geometry seems acceptable.
