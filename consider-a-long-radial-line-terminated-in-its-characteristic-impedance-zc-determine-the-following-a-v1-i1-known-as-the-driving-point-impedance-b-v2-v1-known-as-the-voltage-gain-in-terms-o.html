Guest Question.

Consider a long radial line terminated in its characteristic impedance \(Z_c\). Determine the following:
(a) \(V_{1} / I_{1}\), known as the driving point impedance.
(b) \(\left|V_{2}\right| / V_{1} \mid,\), known as the voltage gain, in terms of \(\alpha \ell\).
(c) \(\left|I_{2}\right|/| / I_{1} \mid\), known as the current gain, in terms of \(\alpha \ell\).
(d) The complex power gain,\(-S_{21} / S_{12}\), in terms of \(\alpha \ell\).
(e) The real power efficiency, \(\left(- P _{21} / P _{12}\right)=\eta\), terms of \(\alpha \ell\).
Note: 1 refers to sending end and 2 refers to receiving end. \(\left(S_{21}\right)\) is the complex power received at 2; \(S_{12}\) is sent from 1.

Admin
Solution

(a) \(\bar{V}_{2}=\bar{Z}_{C} \bar{I}_{2}\) since the line is terminated in \(\bar{Z}_{C}\).

Then \(\bar{V}_{1}=\bar{V}_{2}(\cosh \bar{\gamma} l+\sinh \bar{\gamma} l)=\bar{V}_{2} e^{\bar{\gamma} l}=\bar{V}_{2} e^{\alpha l} e^{j \beta l}\) (1)

\(\bar{I}_{1}=\bar{I}_{2}(\cosh \bar{\gamma} l+\sinh \bar{\gamma} l)=\bar{I}_{2} e^{\bar{\gamma} l}=\bar{I}_{2} e^{\alpha l} e^{j \beta l}\) (2)

\(\therefore \frac{\bar{V}_{1}}{\bar{I}_{1}}=\frac{\bar{V}_{2}}{\bar{I}_{2}}=\bar{Z}_{C} \leftarrow(\text { Note }: \bar{\gamma}=\alpha+j \beta)\)

 

(b) \(V_{1}=\left|\bar{V}_{1}\right|=V_{2} e^{\alpha l} \text { or } \frac{V_{2}}{V_{1}}=e^{-\alpha l}\) From (1) ←

 

(c) \(\frac{I_{2}}{I_{1}}=e^{-\alpha l}\) From (2) ←

 

(d)

\(\begin{aligned}-\bar{S}_{21}=\bar{V}_{2} \bar{I}_{2}^{*}=& \bar{V}_{1} e^{-\alpha l} e^{-j \beta l} \bar{I}_{1}^{*} e^{-\alpha l} e^{j \beta l} \\&=\bar{S}_{12} e^{-2 \alpha l}\end{aligned}\)

Thus \(-\frac{\bar{S}_{21}}{\bar{S}_{12}}=e^{-2 \alpha l} \leftarrow\)

which is \(\left(I_{2}^{2} / I_{1}^{2}\right)\).

 

(e) Noting that α is real,

\(\eta=\frac{-P_{21}}{P_{12}}=e^{-2 \alpha l} \leftarrow\)
