Guest Question.

A steam engine has steam being supplied at 10 bar, 100°C into a cylinder of 30 cm diameter and stroke to bore ratio of 2. Steam is expanded upto 0.75 bar and then released into condenser at 0.25 bar. Determine (i) the modified Rankine efficiency and (ii) the modified stroke length considering that the same steam is expanded from inlet state to the condenser pressure. Neglect pump work.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{States 1, 2 and 3 refer to point of cut off, end of expansion and release.}\\\text{At 10 bar, 100°C}\\\qquad \qquad h_{1} = 2676.2 kJ/kg\\\text{From steam table,}\\\qquad \qquad s_{1} = 7.3614 kJ/kg · K\\\begin{aligned}\text{At state 2, s}_{1}&= s_{2}\\s_{2}&= 7.3614 = s_{\text{f at 0.75 bar}} + x_{2} · s_{\text{fg at 0.75 bar}}\\⇒\qquad \quad x_{2}&= 0.9848\\v_{2}&= v_{\text{f at 0.75 bar}} + x_{2} · v_{\text{fg at 0.75 bar}}\\v_{2}&= 2.1833 m^{3}/kg\\h_{2}&= h_{\text{f at 0.75 bar}} + x_{2} · h_{\text{fg at 0.75 bar}}\\h_{2}&= 2628.35 kJ/kg\\h_{4}&= h_{\text{f at 0.25 bar}} = 271.93 kJ/kg\\\text{Work outpu}&\text{t from steam engine per kg of steam,}\\w_{net}&= (h_{1} – h_{2}) + v_{2}(0.75 – 0.25) × 10^{2}\\&=157.015 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Heat added, }q_{add} = (h_{1} – h_{4}) = 2676.2 – 271.93 = 2404.27 kJ/kg\\\text{Efficiency of modified Rankine cycle η }= \frac{w_{net}}{q_{add}} = \text{0.0653 or }\textbf{6.53}\pmb{\%}\\\text{Let us now consider the expansion to occur up to condenser pressure i.e. state 6}\\\begin{aligned}s_{1}&= s_{2} = s_{6} = 7.3614 kJ/kg · K\\s_{6}&= 7.3614 = s_{\text{f at 0.25 bar}} + x_{6} · s_{\text{fg at 0.25 bar}}\\⇒ x_{6}&= 0.9323\\h_{6}&= h_{\text{f at 0.25 bar}} + x_{6} · h_{\text{fg at 0.25 bar}} = 2459.38 kJ/kg\\v_{6}&= v_{\text{f at 0.25 bar}} + x_{6} · v_{\text{fg at 0.25 bar}} = 5.784 m^{3}/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Given volume of cylinder, V }= \frac{π}{4} · d^{2} · L = \frac{π}{4} × (0.3)^{2} × 0.6 = 0.0424\\\text{In a stroke the mass of steam at 2, m }= \frac{V}{v_{2}} = 0.0194 kg\\\text{Let the modified stroke length be L}^{\prime} m.\\\text{Volume requirement at 6, V}^{\prime} = m · v_{6} = 0.0194 × 5.784\\V^{\prime} = 0.1122 m^{3}\\\begin{aligned}\text{or}\qquad V^{\prime}&= \frac{π}{4} d^{2} L^{\prime} = 0.1122\\⇒ \qquad L^{\prime}&= \text{1.5873 m or 158.73 cm}\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \quad\text{Modified Rankine cycle efficiency}&= 6.53\%\\\text{New stroke length}&= 158.73 cm\end{aligned}\)
