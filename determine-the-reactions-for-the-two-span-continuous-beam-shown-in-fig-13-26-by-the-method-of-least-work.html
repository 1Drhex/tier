Guest Question.

Determine the reactions for the two-span continuous beam shown in Fig. \(13.26\) by the method of least work.

Admin
Solution

The beam is supported by four reactions, \(A_{x}, A_{y}, B_{y}\), and \(D_{y}\). Since there are only three equilibrium equations, the degree of indeterminacy of the beam is equal to 1 . Let us select the reaction \(B_{y}\) to be the redundant. The magnitude of the redundant will be determined by minimizing the strain energy of the beam with respect to \(B_{y}\).

The strain energy of a beam subjected only to bending is expressed as

\( U=\int_{0}^{L} \frac{M^{2}}{2 E I} d x \) (1)

According to the principle of least work,

\( \frac{\partial U}{\partial B_{y}}=\int_{0}^{L}\left(\frac{\partial M}{\partial B_{y}}\right) \frac{M}{E I} d x=0 \) (2)

Before we can obtain the equations for bending moments, \(M\), we must express the reactions at the supports \(A\) and \(D\) of the beam in terms of the redundant \(B_{y}\). Applying the three equilibrium equations, we write

\( +\rightarrow \sum F_{x}=0 \quad A_{x}=0 \)

\( \begin{aligned} +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{D}=0 & \\ -A_{y}(20)+30(10)(15)-B_{y}(10)+80(5) &=0 \\ A_{y} &=245-0.5 B_{y} \\ +\uparrow \sum F_{y} &=0 \\\end{aligned} \) (3)

\( \begin{aligned} \left(245-0.5 B_{y}\right)-30(10)+B_{y}-80+D_{y} &=0 \\ D_{y} &=135-0.5 B_{y} \end{aligned} \) (4)

To determine the equations for bending moments, \(M\), the beam is divided into three segments, \(A B, B C\), and \(C D\). The \(x\) coordinates used for determining the equations are shown in Fig. 13.26, and the bending moment equations, in terms of \(B_{y}\), are tabulated in Table 13.7. Next, the derivatives of the bending moments with respect to \(B_{y}\) are evaluated. These derivatives are listed in the last column of Table \(13.7\).

Table 13.7
x coordinate
Segment Origin Limits (m) M \( \partial M/\partial B_{y} \)
AB A 0-10 (245-0.5 \( B_{y} \))x-15 \( x^{2} \) -0.5x
DC D 0-5 (135-0.5 \( B_{y} \))x -0.5x
CB D 5-10 (135-0.5 \( B_{y} \))x-80(x-5) -0.5x

By substituting the expressions for \(M\) and \(\partial M / \partial B_{y}\) into Eq. (2), we write

\( \begin{aligned} \frac{1}{E I} &\left[\int_{0}^{10}(-0.5 x)\left(245 x-0.5 B_{y} x-15 x^{2}\right) d x\right.\\ &+\int_{0}^{5}(-0.5 x)\left(135 x-0.5 B_{y} x\right) d x \\ &\left.+\int_{5}^{10}(-0.5 x)\left(55 x-0.5 B_{y} x+400\right) d x\right]=0 \end{aligned} \)

By integrating, we obtain

\( -40,416.667+166.667 B_{y}=0 \)

from which

\( B_{y}=242.5 \mathrm{kN} \uparrow \)

By substituting the value of \(B_{y}\) into Eqs. (3) and (4), respectively, we determine the vertical reactions at supports \(A\) and \(D\).

\( \begin{array}{l} A_{y}=123.75 \mathrm{kN} \uparrow \\ D_{y}=13.75 \mathrm{kN} \uparrow \end{array} \)
