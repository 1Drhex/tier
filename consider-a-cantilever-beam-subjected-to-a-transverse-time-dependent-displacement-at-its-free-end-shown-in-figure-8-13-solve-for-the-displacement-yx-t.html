Guest Question.

Consider a cantilever beam subjected to a transverse time-dependent displacement at its free end, shown in Figure 8.13. Solve for the displacement \(y\)(\(x\), \(t\)).

Admin
Solution

Solution: For this case, we have the following boundary conditions,

 

\(y(0, t)=f_{0}(t)\) prescribed displacement at free end

\(\frac{\partial^{2} y(0, t)}{\partial x^{2}}=0\) zero moment resistance at free end

\(\frac{\partial y(L, t)}{\partial x}=0\) zero slope at fixed end

\(y(L, t)=0\) zero displacement at fixed end.

To simplify the algebra, we assume zero initial conditions,

 

\(\begin{aligned}y(x, 0) &=0 \\\frac{\partial y(x, 0)}{\partial t} &=0\end{aligned}\)

 

We proceed with the general transformation, as developed above,

 

\(y(x, t)=z(x, t)+\sum_{i=0}^{3} f_{i}(t) g_{i}(x)\)

 

(except that in this case, only \(f_{0}(t) \neq 0\)),

 

\(z(0, t)=f_{0}(t)-\sum_{i=0}^{3} f_{i}(t) g_{i}(0)\) (8.79)

 

\(z(L, t)=-\sum_{i=0}^{3} f_{i}(t) g_{i}(L)\) (8.80)

 

\(\frac{\partial z(L, t)}{\partial x}=-\sum_{i=0}^{3} f_{i}(t) \frac{d g_{i}(L)}{d x}\) (8.81)

 

\(\frac{\partial^{2} z(0, t)}{\partial x^{2}}=-\sum_{i=0}^{3} f_{i}(t) \frac{d^{2} g_{i}(0)}{d x^{2}}\) (8.82)

 

Given that the boundary conditions are known to the second order, we assume

 

\(g_{i}(x)=\alpha_{i}+\beta_{i} x+\gamma_{i} x^{2}+\delta_{i} x^{3} .\)

 

This equation can be simplified significantly by recognizing that the only remaining \(g_{i}(x)\) will be for \(i\) = 0. In Equations 8.79 to 8.82, we set all boundary conditions on \(z\)(\(x\), \(t\)) to zero. Therefore,

 

\(f_{0}(t)-\sum_{i=0}^{3} f_{i}(t) g_{i}(0)=0\) (8.83)

 

\(-\sum_{i=0}^{3} f_{i}(t) g_{i}(L)=0\) (8.84)

 

\(-\sum_{i=0}^{3} f_{i}(t) \frac{d g_{i}(L)}{d x}=0\) (8.85)

 

\(-\sum_{i=0}^{3} f_{i}(t) \frac{d^{2} g_{i}(0)}{d x^{2}}=0\) (8.86)

 

From Equation 8.83, we have

 

\(f_{0}(t)=f_{0}(t) g_{0}(0)\)

 

where \(g_{0}(0)=\alpha_{0}\) and, therefore, \(\alpha_{0}\) = 1. From Equation 8.86, for \(x\) = 0,

 

\(0=-f_{0}(t)\left(2 \gamma_{0}\right)\)

 

or \(\gamma_{0}=0\) From Equation 8.85, for \(x=L\),

 

\(0=-f_{0}(t)\left(\beta_{0}+3 \delta_{0} L^{2}\right)\)

 

and from Equation 8.84, for \(x=L\)

 

\(0=-f_{0}(t)\left(1+\beta_{0} L+\delta_{0} L^{3}\right)\)

 

from which we find \(\beta_{0}=-3 / 2 L\) and \(\delta_{0}=1 / 2 L^{3}\), giving

 

\(g_{0}=1-\frac{3}{2 L} x+\frac{1}{2 L^{3}} x^{3}\)

 

Now we use the general solution, Equation 8.76, for \(X_{n}(x)\) to evaluate constants \(C_{\text {in }}\) by satisfying the boundary conditions. We note that all boundary conditions for \(z\)(\(x\), \(t\)) equal zero,

 

\(\begin{aligned}z(0, t) &=0 \\z^{\prime}(L, t) &=0 \\z^{\prime \prime}(0, t) &=0 \\z(L, t) &=0\end{aligned}\)

 

Therefore, \(X_{n}(0)=0, X_{n}(L)=0, d X_{n}(L) / d x=0\), and \(d^{2} X_{n}(0) / d x^{2}=0\). Applying this to Equation 8.76 leads
to the following equations,

 

\(C_{1 n}=0, \quad C_{3 n}=0\)

 

\(0=C_{2 n} \sin m_{n}+C_{4 n} \sinh m_{n}\) (8.87)

 

\(0=C_{2 n} \cos m_{n}+C_{4 n} \cosh m_{n}\) (8.88)

 

The latter two equations allow us to find the characteristic equation, \(\tan m_{n}=-\tanh m_{n}\), leading to the values of \(m_{n}\). They can be evaluated recursively or via plotting. With the values of \(m_{n}\) determined, the modes are given by

 

\(X_{n}(x)=C_{2 n} \sin \frac{m_{n} x}{L}+C_{4 n} \sinh \frac{m_{n} x}{L}\)

 

Using either Equation 8.87 or 8.88 we can relate the two remaining coefficients. From Equation 8.87, we have

 

\(C_{4 n}=-C_{2 n} \frac{\sin m_{n}}{\sinh m_{n}}\)

 

and then

 

\(\begin{aligned}X_{n}(x)=& \frac{C_{2 n}}{\sinh m_{n}}\left(\sinh m_{n} \sin \frac{m_{n} x}{L}\right.\\&\left.-\sin m_{n} \sinh \frac{m_{n} x}{L}\right) .\end{aligned}\) (8.89)

 

For compactness, we can define\(C_{2 n}^{\prime} \equiv C_{2 n} / \sinh m_{n}\). Next, we evaluate all the coefficients and parameters that are part of the solution. Since the external load acts at the end of the beam, there is no spatial variation, and therefore \(Q_{n}\) = 0 and \(q(x)\) = 0 in Equations 8.71 and 8.70, respectively.

 

From Equation 8.72,

 

\(G_{0 n}=\frac{\int_{0}^{L} g_{0}(x) X_{n}(x) d x}{\int_{0}^{L} X_{n}^{2}(x) d x}\)

 

where \(g_{0}(x)\) and \(X_{n}(x)\) have both been evaluated. Substituting, integrating, and simplifying leads to

 

\(G_{0 n}=\frac{2}{m_{n} C_{2 n}^{\prime}\left(\sinh m_{n}-\sin m_{n}\right)}\)

 

Since all the other \(g_{i}(x)\) = 0, we have \(G_{2 n}\) = \(G_{3 n}\) = \(G_{4 n}\) = 0. Similarly, from Equation 8.73, \(H_{\text {in }}\) = 0. Equations 8.77 and 8.78, with \(y_{o}(x)\) = 0 and \(v_{o}(x)\) = 0, are used to evaluate \(A_{n}\) and \(B_{n}\),

 

\(\begin{aligned}&A_{n}=\frac{\int_{0}^{L}\left(-\sum_{i=0}^{3} f_{i}(0) g_{i}(x) X_{n}(x)\right) d x}{\int_{0}^{L} X_{n}^{2}(x) d x}=-f_{0}(0) G_{0 n} \\&B_{n}=\frac{\int_{0}^{L}\left(-\sum_{i=0}^{3} \dot{f}_{i}(0) g_{i}(x) X_{n}(x)\right) d x}{\omega_{n} \int_{0}^{L} X_{n}^{2}(x) d x}=-\frac{\dot{f}_{0}(0) G_{0 n}}{\omega_{n}} .\end{aligned}\)

 

The complete solution is then

 

\(\begin{aligned}y(x, t)=& z(x, t)+\sum_{i=0}^{3} f_{i}(t) g_{i}(x) \\=& \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} X_{n}(x)\left[-f_{0}(0) G_{0 n} \cos \omega_{n} t\right.\\&-\frac{\dot{f}_{0}(0) G_{0 n}}{\omega_{n}} \sin \omega_{n} t \\&\left.+\frac{1}{\omega_{n}} \int_{0}^{t} P(\tau) \sin \omega_{n}(t-\tau) d \tau\right] \\&+f_{0}(t)\left(1-\frac{3 x}{2 L}+\frac{x^{3}}{2 L^{3}}\right),\end{aligned}\)

 

where

 

\(\begin{aligned}P(t) &=\frac{p(t) Q_{n}}{m}-\sum_{i=0}^{3}\left[\frac{E I}{m} f_{i}(t) H_{i n}+\ddot{f}_{i} G_{i n}\right] \\&=-\ddot{f}_{0}(t) G_{0}\end{aligned}\)

 

For zero initial conditions, we have \(f_{0}(0)\) = 0 and\(\dot{f}_{0}(0)\) = 0, leading to

 

\(\begin{aligned}y(x, t)=& f_{0}(t)\left(1-\frac{3 x}{2 L}+\frac{x^{3}}{2 L^{3}}\right) \\-& \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left[\frac{2 X_{n}(x)}{m_{n} C_{2 n}^{\prime}\left(\sinh m_{n}-\sin m_{n}\right)}\right.\\&\left.\times \frac{1}{\omega_{n}} \int_{0}^{t} \ddot{f}_{0}(\tau) \sin \omega_{n}(t-\tau) d \tau\right],\end{aligned}\)

 

where the factor \(C_{2 n}^{\prime}\) in the denominator cancels with that factor within \(X_{n}(x)\) in the numerator, as per Equation 8.89. The convolution integral can be integrated by parts twice so that

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\frac{1}{\omega_{n}} \int_{0}^{t} \ddot{f}_{0}(\tau) \sin \omega_{n}(t-\tau) d \tau \\&\quad=f_{0}(t)-\omega_{n} \int_{0}^{t} f_{0}(\tau) \sin \omega_{n}(t-\tau) d \tau .\end{aligned}\)

 

The problem is solved except for the substitution of a particular function for \(f_{0}(t)\).
