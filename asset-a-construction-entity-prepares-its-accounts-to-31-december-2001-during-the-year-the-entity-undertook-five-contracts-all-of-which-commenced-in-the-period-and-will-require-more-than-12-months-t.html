Guest Question.

 

Basset, a construction entity, prepares its accounts to 31 December 2001. During the year the entity undertook five contracts all of which commenced in the period and will require more than 12 months to complete.
The position of each contract at 31 December 2001 is as follows:

Contract 1001 1002 1003 1004 1005
$000 $000 $000 $000 $000
Contract value 1,800 390 260 4,800 2,000
Certified work to date 1,010 240 200 1,500 1,700
Progress payments received 1,300 200 200 1,900 1,400
Costs to be transferred to cost of sales 1,010 240 200 1,200 1,300
Costs incurred to date 1,310 240 200 1,450 1,400

In addition to the amounts transferred to cost of sales, foreseeable losses are anticipated on two contracts: contract 1001 a loss of $50,000 and contract 1002 a loss of $80,000. Basset recognise turnover based on the value of work certified at the balance sheet date.
Requirement
Prepare a summary statement showing profits and losses on all five contracts and the related balance sheet totals for each.

Inventories
Inventories are calculated by comparing the costs incurred to date with amounts transferred to cost of sales. Any balance remaining is included in ‘gross amounts due from customers for contract work’.
