Guest Question.

The torsional assembly of Figure P6.75a consists of a solid 75-mm-diameter bronze [G = 45 GPa] segment (1) securely connected at flange B to solid 75-mm-diameter stainless steel [G = 86 GPa] segments (2) and (3). The flange at B is secured by four 14-mm-diameter bolts, which are each located on a 120-mm-diameter bolt circle (Figure P6.75b). The allowable shear stress of the bolts is 90 MPa, and friction effects in the flange can be neglected for this analysis. Determine:
(a) the allowable torque \(T_{C}\) that can be applied to the assembly at C without exceeding the capacity of the bolted flange connection.
(b) the maximum shear stress magnitude in bronze segment (1).
(c) the maximum shear stress magnitude in stainless steel segments (2) and (3).

Admin
Solution

Section Properties: For bronze segment (1) and stainless steel segments (2) and (3), the polar moments of inertia are identical:

\(J_{1}=J_{2}=J_{3}=\frac{\pi}{32}(75 mm )^{4}=3,106,311 mm ^{4}\)

Equilibrium: Consider a free-body diagram cut around flange B:

\(\Sigma M_{x}=-T_{1}+T_{2}=0 \quad \therefore T_{1}=T_{2}\) (a)

and also consider a FBD cut around joint C, where the external torque \(T_{C}\) is applied:

\(\Sigma M_{x}=-T_{2}+T_{3}+T_{C}=0\) (b)

Capacity of Flange B: The flange at B is secured by four 14-mm-diameter bolts, which are each located on a 120-mm-diameter bolt circle. The allowable shear stress of the bolts is 90 MPa, and each bolt acts in single shear. The cross-sectional area of a single bolt is:

\(A_{ bolt }=\frac{\pi}{4}(14 mm )^{2}=153.9380 mm ^{2}\)

The shear force that can be provided by each bolt is:

\(V_{\text {bolt }}=\left(90 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(153.9380 mm ^{2}\right)=13,854.4236 N\)

The total torque that can resisted by the bolts is thus:

\(T_{B, \max }=(4 \text { bolts })(13,954.4236 N / bolt )(120 mm / 2)=3,325,062 N – mm\)

The internal torques in segments (1) and (2) cannot exceed this magnitude; therefore, based on Eq. (a):

\(T_{1}=T_{2}=3,325,062 N – mm\) (c)

From Eq. (b), we observe that the external torque applied to the shaft is related to internal torques \(T_{2}\) and \(T_{3}\). We must determine the relationship between these two internal torques by developing a compatibility equation, which is based on the geometry of deformations for this configuration.

Geometry-of-Deformation Relationship: Since the two ends of the torsion structure are securely attached to fixed supports at A and D, the sum of the angles of twist in the three segments must equal zero:

\(\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}+\phi_{3}=0\) (d)

Torque-Twist Relationships:

\(\phi_{1}=\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}} \quad \phi_{2}=\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}} \quad \phi_{3}=\frac{T_{3} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}\) (e)

Compatibility Equation: Substitute the torque-twist relationships [Eqs. (e)] into the Geometry-ofdeformation relationship [Eq. (d)] to obtain the compatibility equation:

\(\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}+\frac{T_{3} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}=0\) (f)

Solve the Equations: Solve Eq. (f) for \(T_{3}\), noting that \(J_{1}=J_{2}=J_{3}, G_{2}=G_{3} \text {, and } T_{1}=T_{2}\):

\(T_{3}=-\left[\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}\right] \frac{G_{3} J_{3}}{L_{3}}=-T_{1}\left[\frac{L_{1}}{L_{3}} \frac{G_{3}}{G_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{L_{3}}\right]\) (g)

Substitute the values determined for \(T_{1} \text { and } T_{2}\) in Eq. (c) into Eq. (g) to compute \(T_{3}\):

\(\begin{aligned}T_{3} &=-(3,325,062 N – mm )\left[\left(\frac{400 mm }{400 mm }\right)\left(\frac{86 GPa }{45 GPa }\right)+\frac{750 mm }{400 mm }\right] \\&=-(3,325,062 N – mm )(3.786111) \\&=-12,589,054 N – mm\end{aligned}\)

(a) Allowable Torque \(T _{C}\): From equilibrium equation (b),

\(\begin{aligned}T_{C} &=T_{2}-T_{3} \\&=3,325,062 N – mm -(-12,589,054 N – mm ) \\&=15,914,116 N – mm =15.91 kN – m\end{aligned}\)

(b) Shear Stress Magnitude in Bronze Segment (1):

\(\tau_{1}=\frac{T_{1} c_{1}}{J_{1}}=\frac{(3,325,062 N – mm )(75 mm / 2)}{3,106,311 mm ^{4}}=40.1 MPa\)

(c) Shear Stress Magnitude in Stainless Steel Segments (2) and (3):

\(\tau_{3}=\frac{T_{3} c_{3}}{J_{3}}=\frac{(12,589,054 N – mm )(75 mm / 2)}{3,106,311 mm ^{4}}=152.0 MPa\)
