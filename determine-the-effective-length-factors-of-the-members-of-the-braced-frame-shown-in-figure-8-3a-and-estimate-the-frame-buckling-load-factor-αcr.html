Guest Question.

Determine the effective length factors of the members of the braced frame shown in Figure 8.3a, and estimate the frame buckling load factor \(α_{cr}\).

Admin
Solution

For the vertical member 13, using equation 8.5, \(k = \frac{I/L}{ I/L + \sum{} (β_{e}α_{r}I_{r}/L_{r})}\)

 

\(k1 = 1.0\) (theoretical value for a frictionless hinge).

Using the form of equation 3.45 \(k_{1} = \frac{(I/L)_{12}}{0.5\sum\limits_{1}{}(I/L)(1 − N/N_{cr,L}) + (I/L)_{12} } \)

and Figure 3.19,

\(k_{3} =\frac{2 × 2210 × 10^4/10 000}{0.5 × 4 × 8503 × 10^4/12 000 + 2.0 × 2210 × 10^4/10 000}= 0.238\)

 

\(k_{cr13} = 0.74\) (using Figure 3.21a).

 

\( (k_{cr13} = 0.73\) if a base stiffness ratio of 0.1 is used so that

 

\(k1 = (2 × 2210 × 10^4/10 000)/(2 × 1.1 × 2210 × 10^4/10 000) = 0.909) \)

 

\(N_{cr13} = π^2 × 210 000 × 2210 × 10^4/(0.74 × 10 000)^2N = 836.5 kN\)

 

\(α_{cr13} = 836.5/71.6 = 11.7\)

 

For the horizontal member 35, using equation 8.5, \(k = \frac{I/L}{ I/L + \sum{} (β_{e}α_{r}I_{r}/L_{r})}\)

\(k_{3} =\frac{ 2 × 8503 × 10^4/12 000}{ 0.5 × 3 × 2210 × 10^4/10 000 + 2 × 8503 × 10^4/12 000} = 0.810. \)

 

\(k_{5} = 0\) (theoretical value for a fixed end).

 

\(k_{cr35} = 0.66\) (using Figure 3.21a).

 

\( (k_{cr35} = 0.77\) if a base stiffness ratio of 1.0 is used so that \(k_{5} = 0.5) \)

 

\(N_{cr35} =\frac{ π^2 × 210 000 × 8503 × 10^4}{ (0.66 × 12 000)^2} N = 2809.6 kN\)

 

\(α_{cr35} = 2809.6/25.3 = 111 > 11.7 = α_{cr13}\)

 

∴ \(α_{cr} = 11.7\) (compare with the computer analysis value of \(α_{cr} = 11.32\) given in Figure 8.3d).

A slightly more accurate estimate might be obtained by using equation 8.5, \(k = \frac{I/L}{ I/L + \sum{} (β_{e}α_{r}I_{r}/L_{r})}\)

with \(α_{r}\) obtained from Figure 3.19 as

 

\(α_{r} = 1 −\frac{ 25.3 × 10^3}{ (2 × π^2 × 210 000 × 8503 × 10^4/12 000^2)} = 0.990\)

 

so that

\(k_{3} =\frac{ 2 × 2210 × 10^4/10000}{ 0.5 × 4 × 0.990 × 8503 × 10^4/12 000 + 2 × 2210 × 10^4/10 000} = 0.240\)

but \(k_{cr13}\) is virtually unchanged, and so therefore is \(α_{cr}.\)
