Guest Question.

For a lead-lag unit,

\(\frac{Y(s)}{X(s)}=\frac{K\left(\tau_{a} s+1\right)}{\tau_{1} s+1}\)

show that for a step input of magnitude \(M\) :

(a) The value of \(y\) at \(t=0^{+}\)is given by \(y\left(0^{+}\right)=K M \tau_{a} / \tau_{1}\).

(b) Overshoot occurs only for \(\tau_{a}>\tau_{1}\).

(c) Inverse response occurs only for \(\tau_{a}<0\).

Admin
Solution
\(\frac{Y(s)}{X(s)}=\frac{K\left(\tau_{a} s+1\right)}{\left(\tau_{1} s+1\right)} \quad, \quad X(s)=\frac{M}{s}\)

From Eq. 6-13

\(\frac{Y(S)}{U(S)} = \frac{K_1}{\tau _1s+1} + \frac{K_2}{\tau _2s+1} \\[0.5cm] =\frac{K_1(\tau _2s+1)+K_2(\tau _1s+1)}{(\tau _1s+1)(\tau _2s+1)} \) (6-13)

\(y(t)=K M\left[1-\left(1-\frac{\tau_{a}}{\tau_{1}}\right) e ^{-t / \tau_{1}}\right]=K M\left[1+\frac{\tau_{a}-\tau_{1}}{\tau_{1}} e ^{-t / \tau_{1}}\right]\)

a)

\(y\left(0^{+}\right)=K M\left[1+\frac{\tau_{a}-\tau_{1}}{\tau_{1}}\right]=\frac{\tau_{a}}{\tau_{1}} K M\)

b)

\(\quad\) Overshoot \(\rightarrow y(t)>K M\)

\(K M\left[1+\frac{\tau_{a}-\tau_{1}}{\tau_{1}} e^{-t / \tau_{1}}\right]>K M\)

or when, \(\tau_{a}-\tau_{1}>0 \quad\), that is, \(\tau_{a}>\tau_{1}\)

\(\dot{y}=-K M \frac{\left(\tau_{a}-\tau_{1}\right)}{\tau_{1}^{2}} e ^{-t / \tau_{1}}<0 \quad \text { for } \quad K M>0\)

c)

Inverse response \(\rightarrow y(t)<0\)

\(\begin{aligned}&K M\left[1+\frac{\tau_{a}-\tau_{1}}{\tau_{1}} e ^{-t / \tau_{1}}\right]<0 \\&\frac{\tau_{a}-\tau_{1}}{\tau_{1}}<- e ^{+t / \tau_{1}} \quad \text { or } \quad \frac{\tau_{a}}{\tau_{1}}<1- e ^{+t / \tau_{1}}<0 \quad \text { at } t=0\end{aligned}\)

Therefore, \(\tau_{ a }<0\).
