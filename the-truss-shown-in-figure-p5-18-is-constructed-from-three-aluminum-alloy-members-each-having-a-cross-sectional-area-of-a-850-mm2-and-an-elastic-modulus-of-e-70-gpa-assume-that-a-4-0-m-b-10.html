Guest Question.

The truss shown in Figure P5.18 is constructed from three aluminum alloy members, each having a cross-sectional area of A = 850 \(mm ^{2}\) and an elastic modulus of E = 70 GPa. Assume that a = 4.0 m, b = 10.5 m, and c = 6.0 m. Calculate the horizontal displacement of roller B when the truss supports a load of P = 12 kN.

Admin
Solution

To determine the horizontal displacement of roller B, we need only determine the deformation in member AB. This requires calculation of the internal force in member AB.

Overall equilibrium:
Begin the solution by determining the external reaction forces acting on the truss at support B. The free-body diagram (FBD) of the entire truss is shown. The following equilibrium equation can be written to directly obtain the reaction force at B.

\(\begin{gathered}\Sigma M_{A}=B_{y}(4.0 m +10.5 m )-(12 kN )(4.0 m )=0 \\\therefore B_{y}=3.3103 kN\end{gathered}\)

Method of joints:
The geometry of the truss will be used to determine the magnitude of the inclination angles of member BC. Use the definition of the tangent function to determine \(\theta_{B C}:\)

\(\tan \theta_{B C}=\frac{6.0 m }{10.5 m }=0.571429 \quad \therefore \theta_{B C}=29.7449^{\circ}\)

 

Joint B:
Consider only those forces acting directly on joint B. In this instance, two axial members, AB and BC, are connected at joint B. Additionally, the reaction force \(B_{y}\) acts at joint B. Tension forces will be assumed in each truss member.

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma F_{x}=-F_{A B}-F_{B C} \cos 29.7449^{\circ}=0 (a) \\&\Sigma F_{y}=B_{y}+F_{B C} \sin 29.7449^{\circ}=0 (b)\end{aligned}\)

Solve Eq. (b) for \(F_{B C}:\)

\(F_{B C}=\frac{-B_{y}}{\sin 29.7449^{\circ}}=\frac{-3.310 kN }{\sin 29.7449^{\circ}}=-6.6722 kN\)

and then compute \(F_{A B}\) using Eq. (a):

\(\begin{aligned}F_{A B} &=-F_{B C} \cos 29.7449^{\circ} \\&=-(-6.672 kN ) \cos 29.7449^{\circ}=5.7931 kN\end{aligned}\)

Axial deformation of member AB: The axial deformation of member AB can be calculated from

\(\delta_{A B}=\frac{F_{A B} L_{A B}}{A_{A B} E_{A B}}=\frac{(5,793.1 N )(14,500 mm )}{\left(850 mm ^{2}\right)\left(70,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=1.412 mm\)

As member AB elongates, the roller support at B moves to the right; therefore

\(\Delta x_{B}=1.412 mm \rightarrow\)
