Guest Question.

From the dependence of the chemical reaction rate (i.e., combustion rate) on the temperature, given by chemical kinetic models, e.g., \( \dot{n}_{r,F}=-a_{r}\rho _{F}^{a_{F}}\rho_{O}^{a_{O}}e^{-\Delta E_{a}/R_{g}T} \) and \( \dot{n}_{r,F}=-a_{r}e^{-\Delta E_{a}/R_{g}T} \), we note that the reaction rate \(\dot{n}_{r,F} \) increases with increase in temperature T . Using \( \dot{n}_{r,F}=-a_{r}e^{-\Delta E_{a}/R_{g}T} \), determine \(\dot{n}_{r,F} =\dot{n}_{r,F}(T) \), and the volumetric energy conversion (volumetric heat generation) rate \(\dot{s}_{r,c}=\dot{n}_{r,F} \Delta h_{r,F} \), for the methane-air reaction at (a) T = 300 K, (b) 800 K, and (c) 2,000 K.

Use the constants given in .

Admin
Solution

The exponential dependence of \(\dot{n}_{r,F} \) on T suggests a strong temperature dependence.
From \( \dot{n}_{r,F}=-a_{r}e^{-\Delta E_{a}/R_{g}T} \)we have

\(\dot{n}_{r,F}(Kg/m^{3}-s)=-a_{r}(Kg/m^{3}-s)exp\left(-\frac{\Delta E_{a}}{R_{g}T} \right)\)

The constants are given in and are \(a_{r}=1.3\times 10^{8}Kg/m^{3}\) and \( \Delta E_{a}=2.10\times 10^{8}J/Kmole \). Then using the numerical values, we have

(a) \( \dot{n}_{r,F}(T=300 K)=-1.3\times 10^{8}(Kg/m^{3}-s)exp\left[-\frac{2.1\times 10^{8}(J/Kmole)}{8.314\times 10^{3}(J/Kmole)\times 300(K)} \right] \)

\( =-3.535\times 10^{-29}Kg/m^{3}-s \) volumetric methane depletion rate

\(\dot{s}_{r,c}(T=300 K)=\dot{n}_{r,F}(T=300K)\Delta h_{r,A}=-3.535\times 10^{-29}(Kg/m^{3}-s)\times \left[-5.55\times 10^{7}(J/Kg)\right] \)

\(=1.962\times 10^{-21}J/m^{3}-s=1.962\times 10^{-21}W/m^{3} \) volumetric heat release rate.

(b) \( \dot{n}_{r,F}(T=800 K)=-1.3\times 10^{8}(Kg/m^{3}-s)exp\left[-\frac{2.1\times 10^{8}(J/Kmole)}{8.314\times 10^{3}(J/Kmole)\times 800(K)} \right] \)

\( =-1.940\times 10^{-14}Kg/m^{3}-s\)

 

\(\dot{s}_{r,c}(T=800 K)=(-1.940\times 10^{-14})\times (-5.55\times 10^{7})=1.077\times 10^{-6}W/m^{3}\)

(c) \( \dot{n}_{r,F}(T=2,000 K)=-1.3\times 10^{8}(Kg/m^{3}-s)exp\left[-\frac{2.1\times 10^{8}(J/Kmole)}{8.314\times 10^{3}(J/Kmole)\times 2,000(K)} \right] \)

\( =-4.257\times 10^{2}Kg/m^{3}-s \)

 

\(\dot{s}_{r,c}(T=2,000 K)=(-4.257\times 10^{2})\times (-5.55\times 10^{7})=2.363\times 10^{10}W/m^{3} \)
