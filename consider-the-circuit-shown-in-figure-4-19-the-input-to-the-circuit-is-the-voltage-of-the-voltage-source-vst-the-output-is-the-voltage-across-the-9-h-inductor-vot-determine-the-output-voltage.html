Guest Question.

Consider the circuit shown in Figure 4.19. The input to the circuit is the voltage of the voltage source \(V_{\mathrm{s}}(t)\). The output is the voltage across the \(9 \mathrm{H}\) inductor, \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{o}}(\mathrm{t})\). Determine the output voltage, \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{o}}(\mathrm{t})\). Write MATLAB program to verify the answers.

Admin
Solution

Given \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{s}}(\mathrm{t})=75.5 \cos \left(4 \mathrm{t}+26^{\circ}\right) \mathrm{V}\), so \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{s}}=75.5 \angle 26^{\circ} \mathrm{V}\),

where frequency \(\omega=4 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{sec}\).

The impedance offered by inductor \(9 \mathrm{H}\) is:

\( \begin{array}{c} Z_{\mathrm{L}}=J \omega \\ L=J(4 \times 9)=J 36 \Omega \end{array} \)

The total impedance referred to the primary side using turns ratio is given as:

\( Z=8+30 \times\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^{2}+(J 36) \times\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^{2} \)

\( \begin{array}{c} Z=8+67.5+J 81 \\ Z=75.5+J 81 \end{array} \)

The current from the source is:

\( I_{s}=\frac{V_{s}}{Z}=\frac{75.5 \angle 26^{\circ}}{75.5+\mathrm{J} 81}=0.682 \angle-21.01^{\circ} \mathrm{A} \)

Now current in the secondary is:

\( I_{o}=I_{s} \times \frac{3}{2}=0.682 \angle-21.01^{\circ}=1.023 \angle-21.01^{\circ} \mathrm{A} \)

Now output voltage is:

\( \begin{array}{c} V_{o}=I_{o} Z_{L}=1.023 \angle-21.01^{\circ} \times J 36=36.82 \angle 68.99 \mathrm{~V} \\ \mathrm{~V}_{\mathrm{o}}(\mathrm{t})=36.82 \cos \left(4 \mathrm{t}+68.99^{\circ}\right) \mathrm{V} \end{array} \)
