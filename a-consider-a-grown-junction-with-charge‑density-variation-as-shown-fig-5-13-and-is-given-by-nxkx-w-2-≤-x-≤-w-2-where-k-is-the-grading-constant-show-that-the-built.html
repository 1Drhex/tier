Guest Question.

(a) Consider a grown junction with charge‑density variation as shown Fig. 5.13 and is given by

\(N(x)=Kx ; -\frac{W}{2}\leq x\leq \frac{W}{2} \)

where K is the grading constant. Show that the built‑in potential across the junction \(V_{o} \) can be obtained by solving the following equation:

\(V_{o}-\frac{2\overline{k}T }{e}1n\left[\frac{K}{2n_{i}}\left\lgroup\frac{12\epsilon }{eK}V_{o} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{1}{3} } \right] =0 \)

(b) Derive an expression for the transition capacitance of the grown p‑n junction of part (a) with an applied bias voltage \(V_{a} \).

Admin
Solution

(a) The one‑dimensional Poisson’s equation in the space‑charge region can be written as

\(\frac{d^2V}{dx^2} =-\frac{eN(x)}{\epsilon }=-\frac{eKx}{\epsilon }; -\frac{W}{2}\leq x\leq \frac{W}{2} \)

Integrating twice with respect to x, the potential function in the space‑charge region can be given as

\(V(x)=\frac{eK}{6\epsilon }x^3 +C_{1}x +C_{2} \)

where \(C_{1} \) and \(C_{2} \) are arbitrary constants.

Using the boundary conditions: \(\varepsilon (x)=-\frac{dV}{dx}=0 \) at \( x = \frac{W}{2} \) and \( V(x)=0 \) at \( x =- \frac{W}{2} \) we can write

\( V(x)=-\left\lgroup\frac{eK}{6\epsilon } \right\rgroup x^3 +\left\lgroup\frac{eKW^2}{8\epsilon } \right\rgroup x +\frac{eKW^3}{24\epsilon } \)

Since the built‑in potential \( V_{o} \) is the difference between the potential of the n‑ and p‑sides of the junctions we can write

\( V_{o}=V\left\lgroup\frac{W}{2} \right\rgroup -0=\frac{eKW^3}{12\epsilon } \)

or

\( W=\left\{ \left\lgroup\frac{12\epsilon }{eK}\right\rgroup V_{o}\right\}^{\frac{1}{3} } \)

The doping concentrations at the edges of the depletion regions in the p‑ and n‑sides of the junction can be obtained as

\( N_{A}=\left|K\left\lgroup-\frac{W}{2} \right\rgroup \right| =\frac{KW}{2} =N_{D} \)

Using Eq. (5.8), \( E_{o}=kT 1n\frac{N_{D}N_{A}}{n^2_{i}} \) , the built‑in potential approximately can be given by

\( V_{o}=\frac{E_{o}}{e}=\frac{\overline{k}T }{e} 1n \left\lgroup\frac{N_{A}N_{D}}{n^2_{i}} \right\rgroup \)

\( =\frac{2\overline{k}T }{e} 1n\left\lgroup\frac{KW}{2n_{i}} \right\rgroup \)

Substituting \( W=\left\{ \left\lgroup\frac{12\epsilon }{eK}\right\rgroup V_{o}\right\}^{\frac{1}{3} } \) in the above equation, we can get the desired expression

\( V_{o}-\frac{2kT}{e}1n \left[\frac{K}{2n_{i}} \left\{ \left\lgroup\frac{12\epsilon }{eK}\right\rgroup V_{o}\right\}^{\frac{1}{3} } \right] =0 \)

Note that the above equation can be solved by numerical method to obtain the value of \( V_{o} \) provided that the other parameters are known.

(b) Under biasing condition, the total depletion width \( W_{a} \) can be obtained by simply replacing as \( V_{o} \) by \( V_{B} = V_{o}-V_{a} \)

in the expression of W. Thus we get

\( W_{a}=\left\{ \left\lgroup\frac{12\epsilon }{eK}\right\rgroup (V_{o}-V_{a})\right\}^{\frac{1}{3} } \)

The total charge in the depletion region in either side of the junction is given by

\(Q=A\int_{0}^{\frac{W_{a}}{2} }{eK{x}dx}= \frac{AeKW^2_{a}}{8} =\frac{AeK}{8} \left\{ \left\lgroup\frac{12\epsilon }{eK}\right\rgroup (V_{o}-V_{a})\right\}^{\frac{2}{3} } \)

Thus the transition capacitance is obtained as

\(C_{T}=\left|\frac{dQ}{dV_{a}} \right| =A\left[\frac{eK\epsilon ^2}{12(V_{o}-V_{a})} \right]^{\frac{1}{3} } =\frac{\epsilon A}{W_{a}} \)
