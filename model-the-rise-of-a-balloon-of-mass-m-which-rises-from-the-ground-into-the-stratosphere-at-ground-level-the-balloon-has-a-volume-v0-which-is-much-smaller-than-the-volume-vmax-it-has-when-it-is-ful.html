Guest Question.

Model the rise of a balloon of mass M, which rises from the ground into the stratosphere. At ground level, the balloon has a volume \(V_0\), which is much smaller than the volume \(V_{max}\) it has when it is fully inflated. Nonetheless, the volume \(V_0\) is sufficient to lift the payload. The ballom is filled with helium, which is considered an ideal gas. Use Archimedes’ principle given in and the model of Earth’s atmosphere established in exercise .
a) Find the maximum height \(z _{max}\) reached by the balloon.
b) Show that Archimedes’ force \(F_A\) exerted on the balloon is uniform as long as the balloon is not fully inflated.

Admin
Solution

a) The mass density profile established in exercise 10.5 is written as,

\( m(z _{max}) = m_0 \Bigl(\frac{T (z_{ max})}{T_0}\Bigr)^c = m_0 \Bigl(1-\frac{g}{c^*_p T_0} z_{max}\Bigr)^c \).

which implies that the maximum height \( z _{max}\) is given by,

\( z _{max} = \frac{c^*_p T_0}{g} \Bigl(1-\frac{m(z_{ max})}{m_0} \Bigr)^{1/c}\).

At the maximum height \( z_{ max}\), Archimedes’ force, given in exercise 10.4.5, is equal and opposite to the weight. Thus, these forces have equal norms,

\( m(z_{ max}) V_{max} g = Mg \).

which implies that,

\( m(z_{ max}) =\frac{M}{V_{max}}\).

Thus, the maximum height \( z_{ max} \) is recast as,

\( z _{max} = \frac{c^*_p T_0}{g} \Bigl(1-\frac{M}{m_0 V_{max} } \Bigr)^{1/c}\).

b) As the balloon rises, i.e. \( V < V_{max}\), the pressure inside the balloon is equal to the atmospheric pressure and the balloon is always at thermal equilibrium with the atmospheric air because it rises so slowly. At height z, Archimedes’ force is given by,

\( F_A (z) = m (z) V (z) g \hat{z} \).

According to exercise 10.5,

m V = const thus \( m(z) V (z) = m_0 V_0 \).

Hence, Archimedes’ force is constant,

\( F_A (z) = m_0 V_0 g\hat{z} = const \).
