Guest Question.

A four-cylinder engine has two outer cranks at 120° to each other and their reciprocating masses are each 400 kg. The distance between the planes of rotation of adjacent cranks are 0.4, 0.7, 0.7 and 0.5 m. Find the reciprocating mass and the relative angular position for each of the inner cranks, if the engine is to be in complete primary balance. Also find the maximum secondary force, if the length of each crank is 0.4 m, the length of each connecting rod 1.8 m and the engine speed 480 rpm.

From Table 12.23, we have

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}=80 \).

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}=138.564 \).

\( \sum M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}=-160 \).

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}=166.277 \).

\( \left[\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}\right)^{2}+\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5}=M_{3} r_{3} l_{3} \).

\( \left[(-160)^{2}+(166.277)^{2}\right]^{0.5}=0.28 M_{3} \).

\( M_{3}=\frac{230.755}{0.28}=824.12 kg \).

\( \tan \theta_{3}=\frac{-\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}}{-\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}}=\frac{-(166.277)}{-(-160)}=-1.03923 \).

\( \theta_{3}=46.1^{\circ} \text { i.e. } 314.9^{\circ} \).

\( \text { Since the numerator is negative and denominator is positive, therefore } \theta_{3} \text { lies in the fourth quadrant. } \)

\( M_{2} r_{2}=\left[\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}+M_{3} r_{3} \cos \theta_{3}\right)^{2}+\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}+M_{3} r_{3} \sin \theta_{3}\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5} \).

\( 0.4 M_{2}=\left[\left(0.4 \times 824.12 \cos 314.9^{\circ}+80\right)^{2}+\left(0.4 \times 824.12 \sin 314.9^{\circ}+138.564\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5} \).

\( =\left[(308.58)^{2}+(-98.964)^{2}\right]^{0.5} \).

\( =324.06 \).

\( M_{2}=810.15 kg \).

\( \tan \theta_{2}=\frac{-\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}+M_{3} r_{3} \sin \theta_{3}\right)}{-\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}+M_{3} r_{3} \cos \theta_{3}\right)} \).

\( =\frac{-(-98.964)}{-308.58}=-0.32070 \).

\( \theta_{2}=-17.78^{\circ} \text { i.e. } 162.22^{\circ} \).

Since the numerator is positive and the denominator is negative, therefore, \( \theta_{2} \) lies in the second quadrant.

From Table 12.24, we have

\( \sum M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}=4.4 \).

\( \sum M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}=-7.621 \).

\( \sum M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}=-8.8 \).

\( \sum M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}=-9.145 \).

\( \left[\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}\right)^{2}+\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5}=M_{3} r_{3} l_{3} \).

\( \left[(-8.8)^{2}+(9.145)^{2}\right]^{0.5}=0.0154 M_{3} \).

\( M_{3}=\frac{12.691}{0.0154}=824.12 kg \).

\( \tan \theta_{3}=\frac{-\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}}{-\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}}=\frac{-(-9.145)}{-(-8.8)}=1.0392 \).

\( \theta_{3}=46.1^{\circ} \).

Since the numerator and denominator are both positive, therefore \( \theta_{3} \) lies in the first quadrant,

\( M_{2} r_{2}=\left[\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}+M_{3} r_{3} \cos \theta_{3}\right)^{2}+\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}+M_{3} r_{3} \sin \theta_{3}\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5} \).

\( 0.022 M_{2}=\left[\left(0.022 \times 824.12 \cos 46.1^{\circ}+4.4\right)^{2}+\left(0.022 \times 824.12 \sin 46.1^{\circ}-7.621\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5} \).

\( =\left[(16.972)^{2}+(5.443)^{2}\right]^{0.5}=17.823 \).

\( M_{2}=810.15 kg \).

\( \tan \theta_{2}=\frac{-\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}+M_{3} r_{3} \sin \theta_{3}\right)}{-\left(\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}+M_{3} r_{3} \cos \theta_{3}\right)} \).

\( \frac{-5.443}{-16.972}=0.3207 \).

\( \theta_{2}=17.78^{\circ} \text { i.e. } 197.78^{\circ} \).

\( \text { Since the numerator and denominator are both negative, therefore, } \theta_{2} \text { lies in the third quadrant } \).

\( \text { Speed of secondary crank }=2 \pi \times \frac{480}{60}=50.27 rad / s \).

\( \text { Maximum unbalanced secondary force }=\frac{760 \times(50.27)^{2}}{4.5}=426795 N \).
