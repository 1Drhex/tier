Gauss elimination: triangularizing a matrix

Use Gauss elimination to triangularize

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 2 & 3 & -1 \\ -4 & 6 & 8 \\ 10 & 12 & 14 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{3} \end{matrix} \right ]=\left [ \begin{matrix} 5 \\ 7 \\ 9 \end{matrix} \right ] \)
Admin
Solution

There are (N – 1) = 2 Gauss elimination steps. During Step 1, subtract \(A_{21} /A_{11} =-4/2=-2\) times Equation 1 from Equation 2, and subtract \(A_{31} /A_{11} =10/2=5\) times Equation 1 from Equation 3, to give

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} A_{11} & A_{12} & … & A_{1N} \\ A_{21} & A_{22} & … & A_{2N} \\ \vdots & & \vdots & \\ A_{N1} & A_{N2} & … & A_{NN} \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ \vdots \\ x_{N} \end{matrix} \right ]= \left [ \begin{matrix} y_{1} \\ y_{2} \\ \vdots \\ y_{N} \end{matrix} \right ] \) (6.1.1)

or

Ax = y (6.1.2)

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} A_{11} & A_{12…} & & A_{1N} \\ 0 & A_{22…} & & A_{2N} \\ a_{31} & & & \\ 0 & 0… & A_{N-1,N-1} & A_{N-1,N} \\ 0 & 0…0 & & A_{NN} \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2}\\ \vdots\\ x_{N-1} \\ x_{N} \end{matrix} \right ]=\left [ \begin{matrix} y_{1} \\ y_{2} \\ \vdots \\ y_{N-1} \\ y_{N} \end{matrix} \right ] \) (6.1.3)

 

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 2 & 3 & -1 \\ 0 & 6-(-2)(3) & 8-(-2)(-1) \\ 0 & 12-(5)(3) & 14-(5)(-1) \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{3} \end{matrix} \right ]=\left [ \begin{matrix} 5 \\ 7-(-2)(5) \\ 9-(5)(5) \end{matrix} \right ] \)

or

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 2 & 3 & -1 \\ 0 & 12 & 6 \\ 0 & -3 & 19 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{3} \end{matrix} \right ]= \left [ \begin{matrix} 5 \\ 17 \\ -16 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

which is\(A^{\left(1\right) }X=y^{\left(1\right) } \). During Step 2, subtract \(A^{\left(1\right) }_{32} /A^{\left(1\right) }_{22} =-3/12=-0.25\) times Equation 2 from Equation 3, to give

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 2 & 3 & -1 \\ 0 & 12 & 6 \\ 0 & 0 & 19-(-.25)(6) \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{3} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 5 \\ 17 \\ -16-(-.25)(17) \end{matrix} \right ] \)

or

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 2 & 3 & -1 \\ 0 & 12 & 6 \\ 0 & 0 & 20.5 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{3} \end{matrix} \right ]=\left [ \begin{matrix} 5 \\ 17 \\ -11.75 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

which is triangularized. The solution x now can be easily obtained via back substitution.
