Guest Question.

Assume that a certain spherical mirror has a focal length of+ 10.0 cm. Locate and describe the image for object dis- tances of (a) 25.0 cm, (b) 10.0 cm, and (c) 5.00 cm.

Admin
Solution

Because the focal length is positive, we know that this is a concave mirror (see Table 36.1 ) (a) This situation is analogous to that in Figure 36.15 \( \mathrm{a}\); hence, we expect the \( \mathrm{im}\) age to be real and closer to the mirror than the object. Acconding to the figure, it should also be inverted and reduced in size. We find the image distance by using the Equation 36.6 form of the mimor equation:

\( \frac{1}{t}+\frac{1}{q} =\frac{1}{f} \) \( \frac{1}{25.0 \mathrm{~cm}}+\frac{1}{q} =\frac{1}{10.0 \mathrm{~cm}}\) \( q =16.7 \mathrm{~cm}\)

The magnification is given by Equation 36.2

\( M=-\frac{q}{t}=-\frac{16.7 \mathrm{~cm}}{25.0 \mathrm{~cm}}=-0.668\)

The fact that the absolute value of M is less than unity tells u that the image is smaller than the object, and the negative \( \operatorname{sig}\) n for M tells us that the image is inverted. Because q is poeitive, the image is located on the front side of the mirror and is real. Thus, we see that our predictions were correct.

 

(b) When the object distance is \( 10.0 \mathrm{~cm},\) the object is located at the focal point. Now we find that

\( \frac{1}{10.0 \mathrm{~cm}}+\frac{1}{q}=\frac{1}{10.0 \mathrm{~cm}}\) \( q=\infty\)

which means that rays originating from an object positioned at the focal point of a mirror are reflected so that the image is fomed at an infinite distance from the mirror; that is, the rays travel parallel to one another after reflection. This is the situation in a flashlight, where the bulb filament is placed at the focal point of a reflector, producing a parallel beam of light.

 

(c) When the object is at \( t=5.00 \mathrm{~cm},\) it lies between the focal point and the mirror surface, as shown in Figure 36.15 \( \mathrm{~b}\). Thus, we expect a magnified, virtual, upright image. In this case, the mirror equation gives

\( \frac{1}{5.00 \mathrm{~cm}}+\frac{1}{q} =\frac{1}{10.0 \mathrm{~cm}} \) \( q =-10.0 \mathrm{~cm}\)

The image is wirtual because it is located behind the mirror as expected. The magnification is

\( M=-\frac{q}{t}=-\left(\frac{-10.0 \mathrm{~cm}}{5.00 \mathrm{~cm}}\right)=2.00\)

The image is twice as large as the object, and the positive sign for \( \mathrm{Mindicates}\) that the image is upright (see Fig. 36.15 \( \mathrm{~b}\) )

 

TABLE 36.1 Sign Conventions for Mirrors
p is positive if object is in front of mirror (real object).
p is negative if object is in back of mirror (virtual object).
q is positive if image is in front of mirror (real image).
q is negative if image is in back of mirror (virtual image).
Both f and R are positive if center of curvature is in front of mirror (concave mirror).
Both f and R are negative if center of curvature is in back of mirror (convex mirror).
If M is positive, image is upright.
If M is negative, image is inverted.
