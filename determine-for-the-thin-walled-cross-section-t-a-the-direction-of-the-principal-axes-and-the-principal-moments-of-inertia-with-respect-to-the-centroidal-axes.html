Guest Question.

Determine for the thin-walled cross section \((t\ll a)\) the direction of the principal axes and the principal moments of inertia with respect to the centroidal axes.

Admin
Solution

Initially, the center of gravity is determined:

\(\bar{y}_{C}=\frac{\frac{3}{2}a 5at }{2\cdot 5at}=\frac{3}{4}a , \bar{z}_{C}=\frac{2a 5at+\frac{5}{2}a 5at }{2\cdot 5at}=\frac{9}{4}a.\)

The moment of inertia for the oblique partial area with respect to its centro-idal axes can be derived by introducing the coordinate s, whereby the following holds:

\(dA=t ds \) and \( s^{2}=\hat{y}^{2}+\hat{z}^{2} .\)

With the slope m of the corresponding part of the cross section, \(\hat{z}\) can be ex-pressed by \(\hat{z} = m \hat{y},\) such that \(\hat{y}\) and \(\hat{z}\) can be expressed by s:

\(\hat{y}^{2}=\frac{1} {1+m^{2}}s^{2} , \hat{z}^{2}=\frac{m^{2}}{1+m^{2}}s^{2} .\)

The moments of inertia result in

\(I_{\hat{y} }=\int{\hat{z}^{2} }dA=\int_{-2.5a}^{2.5a}{\frac{m^{2}}{1+m^{2}} }s^{2}t ds=\frac{m^{2}}{1+m^{2}}\frac{125}{12}a^{3}t ,\)

 

\(I_{\hat{z} }=\int{\hat{y}^{2} }dA=\int_{-2.5a}^{2.5a}{\frac{1}{1+m^{2}} }s^{2}t ds=\frac{1}{1+m^{2}}\frac{125}{12}a^{3}t ,\)

 

\(I_{\hat{y}\hat{z} }=-\int{\hat {y} }\hat{z} dA=\int_{-2.5a}^{2.5a}{\frac{m}{1+m^{2}} }s^{2}t ds=-\frac{m}{1+m^{2}}\frac{125}{12}a^{3}t .\)

For the given cross section, the slope is \(m = \frac{4}{3} ,\) such that

\(I_{\hat{y} }=\frac{20}{3}a^{3}t , I_{\hat{z} }=\frac{15}{4}a^{3}t , I_{\hat{y}\hat{z} }=-5a^{3}t . \)

Another way to derive this result is to apply the transformation equa-tions. For the given geometry, \(I_{\hat{y} }\) can be determined with the moments of inertia in the rotated configuration: \(I_{\eta }=(5a)^{3} t/12, I_{\zeta }=I_{\eta \zeta }=0\) and the corresponding angle \(\varphi = −arctan \frac{3}{4}= -36.87^{\circ } .\) Thus,

\(I_{\hat{y} }=\frac{I_{\eta }+I_{\zeta }}{2}+\frac{I_{\eta -I_{\zeta }}}{2}\cos 2\varphi +I_{\eta \zeta }\sin 2\varphi\)

 

\( =\frac{1}{2}\left[1+\cos (-73.74^{\circ })\right]\frac{(5a)^{3}t}{12}=\frac{20}{3}a^{3}t .\)

For the calculation of the moments of inertia for the complete cross section with respect to the centroidal axes, the parallel-axis theorem needs to be applied for each part of the cross section, such that

\(I_{y}=\frac{20}{3}a^{3} t+5at(\frac{9}{4}a-2a )^{2}+\frac{(5a)^{3}t}{12}+5at(\frac{5}{2}a-\frac{9}{4}a )^{2}=\frac{425}{24}a^{3} t ,\)

 

\(I_{z}=\frac{15}{4}a^{3} t+5at(\frac{3}{2}a+\frac{3}{4}a )^{2}+0+5at(\frac{3}{4}a )^{2}=\frac{225}{24}a^{3}t ,\)

 

\(I_{yz}=-5 a^{3} t-5at(\frac{3}{2}a-\frac{3}{4}a )(2a-\frac{9}{4}a )-5at(-\frac{3}{4}a )(\frac{5}{2}a-\frac{9}{4}a )\)

 

\( =-\frac{25}{8}a^{3}t .\)

With these results, the directions of the principal axes are

\( \tan 2\varphi ^{*}=\frac{2 I_{yz}}{I_{y}-I_{z}}=\frac{-\frac{25}{4}a^{3} t }{\frac{425-225}{24}a^{3}t }=-\frac{3} {4} \rightsquigarrow \begin{matrix} \underline{\underline{\varphi ^{*}_{1}=-18.43^{\circ } } }, \\ \underline{\underline{\varphi ^{*}_{2}= 71.57^{\circ } } }. \end{matrix}\)

One can derive the result also by consi-dering the symmetry of the cross section with respect to the axis 2–2. The slope of the axis 2–2 is

\(m_{2−2} = 3 ,\)

which leads to \(\varphi ^{*}_{2}=71.57^{\omicron }. \)

The principal moments of inertia are

\(I_{1,2}=\left[\frac{425+225}{48}\pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{425-255}{48} \right)^{2}+\left(\frac{25}{8} \right)^{2} } \right]a^{3}t\)

 

\( =\left[\frac{325}{24}\pm \frac{125}{24} \right]a^{3} t\)

 

\( \rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{I_{1}=\frac{75}{4}a^{3} t } }, \underline{\underline{I_{2}=\frac{25}{3}a^{3} t } }.\)
