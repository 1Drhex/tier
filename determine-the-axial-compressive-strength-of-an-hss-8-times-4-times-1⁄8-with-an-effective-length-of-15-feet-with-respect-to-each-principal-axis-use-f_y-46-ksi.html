Guest Question.

Determine the axial compressive strength of an HSS 8 \(\times\) 4 \(\times\) 1⁄8 with an effective length of 15 feet with respect to each principal axis. Use \(F_{y} \)= 46 ksi.

Admin
Solution

Compute the overall, or flexural, buckling strength.
\(L_{c} = 15 ft\)
The maximum slenderness ratio is
\(\frac{L_{c}}{r_{y}}=\frac{15 \times 12}{1.71}= 105.3 < 200\) (OK)
\(4.71\sqrt{\frac{E}{F_{y}}}=4.71\sqrt{\frac{29,000}{46}}=118\)
Since 105.3 < 118, use AISC Equation E3-2. \(F_{cr} = 0.658^{(F_{y}/F_{e})}F_{y}.\)
\(F_{e} = \frac{\pi ^{2}E}{(L_{c}/r)^{2}}=\frac{\pi ^{2}(29,000)}{(105.3)^{2}}=25.81 ksi\)
\(F_{cr}= 0.658^{(F_{y}/F_{e})}F_{y}= 0.658^{(46/25.81)}(46) = 21.82 ksi\)
The nominal strength is
\(P_{n} = F_{cr}A_{g} = 21.82(2.70) = 58.91 kips\)
\(\textbf{Check width-to-thickness ratios:}\) From the dimensions and properties table in the Manual, the width-to-thickness ratio for the larger overall dimension is
\(\lambda =\frac{h}{t}= 66.0\)
The ratio for the smaller dimension is
\(\lambda =\frac{b}{t}=31.5\)
From AISC Table B4.1a, Case 6 (and Figure 4.9 in this book), the upper limit for nonslender elements is
\(\lambda_{r} =1.40\sqrt{\frac{E}{F_{y}}}=1.40\sqrt{\frac{29,000}{46}}=35.15\)
Since h/t > \(1.40\sqrt{E/F_{y}} (i.e.\lambda >\lambda_{r}),\) the larger dimension element is slender and the local buckling strength must be computed. (Although the limiting width-tothickness ratio is labeled b/t in the table, that is a generic notation, and it applies to h/t as well.)

\(\textbf{Determine be for the 8-inch side:}\)
\(\lambda_{r}\sqrt{\frac{F_{y}}{F_{cr}}}=35.15\sqrt{\frac{46}{21.82}}=51.04\)
\(Since \lambda =66.0 > 51.04,\)
\(b_{e}=b\left ( 1 – c_{1}\sqrt{\frac{F_{el}}{F_{cr}}} \right )\sqrt{\frac{F_{el}}{F_{cr}}}\) (AISC Equation E7-3)
From AISC Table E7.1, Case b,
\(c_{1}\) = 0.20
\(c_{2}\) = 1.38
The elastic buckling stress is
\(F_{el}=\left ( c_{2}\frac{\lambda_{r}}{\lambda} \right )^{2}F_{y}\)
\(=\left [ 1.38\left ( \frac{35.15}{66.0} \right ) \right ]^{2}(46)=24.85ksi\) (AISC Equation E7-5)
From AISC B4.1(b) and the discussion in Part 1 of the Manual, the unreduced
length of the 8-inch side between the corner radii can be taken as
b = 8 – 3t = 8 – 3(0.116) = 7.652 in.
and the effective width is
\(b_{e}=b\left ( 1 – c_{1}\sqrt{\frac{F_{el}}{F_{cr}}} \right )\sqrt{\frac{F_{el}}{F_{cr}}}\)
\(=7.652\left ( 1 – 0.20\sqrt{\frac{24.85}{21.82}} \right )\sqrt{\frac{24.85}{21.82}}= 6.423 in.\)
The effective cross-sectional area is therefore
\(A_{e} = A_{g} – \sum(b – b_{e})t\)
= 2.70 – 2(7.652 – 6.423)0.116 = 2.415 \(in.^{2}\)
From AISC Equation E7-1,
\(P_{n} = F_{cr}A_{e}\)= 21.82(2.415) = 52.70 kips
Since this is less than the flexural buckling strength of 58.91 kips, local buckling controls.
Design strength =\( \phi_{c}P_{n}\) = 0.90(52.70) = 47.4 kips
Design strength = 47.4 kips
Allowable strength\( =\frac{P_{n}}{\Omega }=\frac{52.70}{1.67}=31.6 kips\)
(Allowable stress \(= 0.6F_{cr}\) = 0.6(21.82) = 13.1 ksi)
Allowable strength = 31.6 kips
