Radiation Resistance and Efficiency of a Hertzian Dipole

A 4 cm long center-fed dipole is used as an antenna at 75 MHz. The antenna wire is made of copper and has a radius a = 0.4 mm. From Eqs. (7.92a)

\(R_s\frac{1}{σδ_s} = \sqrt{\frac{πf μ}{σ} }( \Omega ) (7.92a)\)

and (7.94),

\(R^\prime _1=\frac{R}{l} = \frac{R_s}{2πa} ( \Omega/m). (7.94)\)

the loss resistance of a circular wire of length l is given by

\(R_{loss} =\frac{l}{2πa} \sqrt{\frac{πf μ_c }{\sigma _c} } (9.35) \)


where \(μ_c and σ_c\) are the magnetic permeability and conductivity of the wire, respectively. Calculate the radiation resistance and the radiation efficiency of the dipole antenna.

Admin
Solution

At 75 MHz,

\(\lambda =\frac{c}{f}=\frac{3 × 10^8}{7.5 × 10^7} = 4 m. \)

The length to wavelength ratio is \(l/λ = 4 cm/4 m = 10^{−2}\)


Hence, this is a short dipole. From Eq. (9.24),

\(D = \frac{4πR^2S_{max}}{P_{rad}} =\frac{S_{max}}{S_{av}} (9.24)\)

 

\(P_{rad}=\frac{4πR^2}{D} S_{max}. (9.36) \)

For the Hertzian dipole, \(S_{max}\) is given by Eq. (9.14),

\(S_{max} = S_0 =\frac{η_0k^2I_0^2l^2}{32π^2R^2} =\frac{15πI^2_0}{R^2}(\frac{l}{\lambda } )^2 (W/m^2), (9.14) \)

and from we established that D = 1.5. Hence,

\(P_{rad}=\frac{4πR^2}{1.5}\times \frac{15πI^2_0}{R^2}(\frac{l}{\lambda } )^2= 40π^2I^2_0(\frac{l}{\lambda } )^2 (9.37) \)

Equating this result to Eq. (9.33a)

\(P_{rad}=\frac{1}{2} I^2_0 R_{rad} (9.33a)\)


and then solving for the radiation resistance \(R_{rad}\)
 leads to

\(R_{rad}=80π^2(l/λ)^2 ( \Omega ) (short dipole). (9.38)\)

For \( l/λ = 10^{−2}, R_{rad} = 0.08 \Omega .\)
 Next, we determine the loss resistance \(R_{loss}\). For copper, Appendix B gives \(μ_c ≈ μ_0 = 4π × 10^{−7} H/m and σ_c = 5.8 × 10^7 S/m. \) Hence,

\(R_{loss}=\frac{l}{2πa}\sqrt{\frac{πf μ_c}{σ_c} } \) \(=\frac{4 × 10^{−2}}{2π × 4 × 10^{−4}} (\frac{π × 75 ×10^6× 4π 10^{−7}}{5.8 ×10^7} )^{1/2} = 0.036 \Omega . \)

Therefore, the radiation efficiency is

\(\xi =\frac{R_{rad}}{R_{rad}+R_{loss}} =\frac{0.08}{0.08 + 0.036}= 0.69 \)

Thus, the dipole is 69% efficient.
