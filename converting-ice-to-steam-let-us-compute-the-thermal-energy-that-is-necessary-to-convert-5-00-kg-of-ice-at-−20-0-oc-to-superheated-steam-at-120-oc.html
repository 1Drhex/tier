Guest Question.

Converting ice to steam. Let us compute the thermal energy that is necessary to convert 5.00 kg of ice at −20.0 ºC to superheated steam at 120 ºC.

Admin
Solution

The necessary thermal energy is given by

\( Q = Q_i + Q_f + Q_w + Q_v + Q_s\) (14.14)

where
\(Q_i\) is the energy needed to heat the ice up to 0 ºC
\(Q_f\) is the energy needed to melt the ice
\(Q_w\) is the energy needed to heat the water to 100 °C
\(Q_v\) is the energy needed to boil the water
\(Q_s\) is the energy needed to heat the steam to 120 ºC

The necessary thermal energy to warm up the ice from −20.0 ºC to 0 ºC is found from

\( Q_i = m_ic_i[0º − (−20.0ºC)]\)

The latent heat of fusion is the amount of heat needed per kilogram to melt the ice. The total amount of heat needed to melt all the ice is the heat of fusion times the number of kilograms of ice present. Hence, the thermal energy needed to melt the ice is

\( Q_f = m_iL_f\) (14.15)

The thermal energy needed to warm the water from 0 ºC to 100 ºC is

\( Q_w = m_wc_w(100 ºC − 0 ºC)\)

The latent heat of vaporization is the amount of heat needed per kilogram to boil the water. The total amount of heat needed to boil all the water is the heat of vaporization times the number of kilograms of water present. Hence, the thermal energy needed to convert the liquid water at 100 ºC to steam at 100 ºC is

\( Q_v = m_wL_v\) (14.16)

and

\( Q_s = m_sc_s(120 ºC − 100 ºC)\)

is the thermal energy needed to convert the steam at 100 ºC to superheated steam at 120 ºC. Substituting all these equations into equation 14.14 gives

\(Q = m_ic_i[0 ºC − (−20 ºC)] + m_iL_f + m_wc_w(100 ºC − 0 ºC) + m_wL_v + m_sc_s(120 ºC − 100 ºC)\) (14.17)

Using the values of the specific heat from table 14.1

we get

\(Q =(5.00 kg)\left\lgroup2093 \frac{J}{kg ºC} \right\rgroup(20.0 ºC)+(5.00 kg)\left\lgroup3.34\times 10^5\frac{J}{kg} \right\rgroup +(5.00 kg)\left\lgroup 4186\frac{J}{kg ºC}\right\rgroup(100.0 ºC)+(5.00 kg)\left\lgroup2.26\times 10^6\frac{J}{kg} \right\rgroup+(5.00 kg)\left\lgroup2013 \frac{J}{kg ºC}\right\rgroup(20.0 ºC)\)

 

\(= 0.209 × 10^6 J + 1.67 × 10^6 J + 2.09 × 10^6 J + 11.3 × 10^6 J + 0.201 × 10^6 J\)

 

\(= 15.5 × 10^6 J\)

Therefore, we need \(15.5 × 10^6 J\) of thermal energy to convert 5.00 kg of ice at −20.0 ºC to superheated steam at 120 ºC. Note the relative size of each term’s contribution to the total thermal energy.
