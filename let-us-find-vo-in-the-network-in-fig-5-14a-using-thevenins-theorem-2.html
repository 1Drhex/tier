Guest Question.

Let us find \(V_{o}\) in the network in Fig. 5.14a using Thévenin’s theorem.

Admin
Solution

\(V_{oc}\) is determined from the network in Fig. 5.14b. Note that

\(I_{1} = \frac{V^{\prime}_{x}}{2k}\)
\(I_{2}\) = 2 mA

and

\(V^{\prime}_{x} = 4k \Big(\frac{V^{\prime}_{x}}{2k} – 2 × 10^{-3}\Big) \)

Solving these equations yields \(I_{1}\) = 4 mA and, hence,

\(V_{oc} = 2k I_{1}\) + 3 = 11 V

\(I_{sc}\) is derived from the circuit in Fig. 5.14c. Note that if we collapse the short circuit, the network is reduced to that in Fig. 5.14d. Although we have temporarily lost sight of \(I_{sc}\)′, we can easily find the branch currents and they, in turn, will yield \(I_{sc}\)′. KCL at the node at the bottom left of the network is

\(\frac{V^{\prime \prime}_{x}}{4k} = \frac{V^{\prime \prime}_{x}}{2000 } – 2 × 10^{-3}\)

or

\(V^{\prime \prime}_{x}\) = 8 V

Then since

\(I_{3} =\frac{3}{2k} = \frac{3}{2}\) mA

as shown in Fig. 5.14c,

\(I_{sc} =\frac{V^{\prime \prime}_{x}}{2000} + I_{3}\)

= \(\frac{11}{2}\) mA

Then

\(R_{Th} = \frac{V_{oc}}{I_{sc}}\) = 2 kΩ

Connecting the Thévenin equivalent circuit to the remainder of the original network produces the circuit in Fig. 5.14e. Simple voltage division yields

\(V_{o} = 11 \huge(\frac{6k}{2k + 6k} \huge) \)

= \(\frac{33}{4}\) V
