Guest Question.

A cylinder fitted with a piston has an initial volume of \(0.5 \mathrm{~m}^{3}\). It contains \(2.0 \mathrm{~kg}\) of air at \(400 \mathrm{kPa}\) absolute. Heat is transferred to the air while the pressure remains constant until the temperature is \(300^{\circ} \mathrm{C} .\) Calculate the heat transfer and the work done. Assume constant specific heats.

Admin
Solution

Using the first law, Eq. 1.7.9, and the definition of enthalpy, we see that

\(\begin{aligned} W_{1-2} &=\int_{l_{1}}^{l_{2}} F d l \\ &=\int_{l_{1}}^{l_{2}} p A d l=\int_{V_{1}}^{V_{2}} p d V \end{aligned}\) (1.7.9)

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{1-2} &=p_{2} V_{2}-p_{1} V_{1}+m \tilde{u}_{2}-m \tilde{u}_{1} \\&=m \tilde{u}_{2}+p_{2} V_{2}-\left(m \tilde{u}_{1}+p_{1} V_{1}\right) \\&=H_{2}-H_{1}=m\left(h_{2}-h_{1}\right)=m c_{p}\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right)\end{aligned}\)

where Eq. 1.7.12 is used assuming \(c_{p}\) to be constant. The initial temperature is

\(\Delta h=\int c_{p} d T (1.7.12)\)

\(T_{1}=\frac{p_{1} V_{1}}{m R}=\frac{400 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{2} \times 0.5 \mathrm{~m}^{3}}{2.0 \mathrm{~kg} \times 0.287 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}}=348.4 \mathrm{~K}\)

(Use \(\mathrm{kJ}=\mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}\) to check the units.) Thus the heat transfer is \(\left(c_{p}\right.\) is found in Table B.4)

\(Q_{1-2}=2.0 \times 1.0[(300+273)-348.4]=449 \mathrm{~kJ}\)

The final volume is found using the ideal-gas law:

\( V_{2}=\frac{m R T_{2}}{p_{2}}=\frac{2 \mathrm{~kg} \times(0.287 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}) \times 573 \mathrm{~K}}{400 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{2}}=0.822 \mathrm{~m}^{3}\)

The work done for the constant-pressure process is, using Eq. \(1.7 .9\) with \(p=\) const,

\(\begin{aligned}W_{1-2} &=p\left(V_{2}-V_{1}\right) \\&=400 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{2}(0.822-0.5) \mathrm{m}^{3}=129 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \text { or } 129 \mathrm{~kJ}\end{aligned}\)
