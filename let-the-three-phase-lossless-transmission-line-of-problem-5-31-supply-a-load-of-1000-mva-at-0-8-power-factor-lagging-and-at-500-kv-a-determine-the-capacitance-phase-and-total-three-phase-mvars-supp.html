Guest Question.

Let the three-phase lossless transmission line of supply a load of 1000 MVA at 0.8 power factor lagging and at 500 kV. (a) Determine the capacitance/phase and total three-phase Mvars supplied by a three-phase, Δ-connected shunt-capacitor bank at the receiving end to maintain the receiving-end voltage at 500 kV when the sending end of the line is energized at 500 kV. (b) If series capacitive compensation of 40% is installed at the midpoint of the line, without the shunt capacitor bank at the receiving end, compute the sending-end voltage and percent voltage regulation.

Admin
Solution

(a) From the solution of ,

\(\bar{Z}_{C}=290.43 \Omega ; \beta l=21.641^{\circ}\)

For a lossless line, the equivalent line reactance is given by

\(X^{\prime}=\bar{Z}_{C} \sin \beta l=(290.43) \sin 21.641^{\circ}=107.11 \Omega\)

The receiving end power \(\bar{S}_{R(3 \phi)}=1000 \angle \cos ^{-1} 0.8=800+j 600 MVA\)

Since \(P_{3 \phi}=\frac{V_{S(L-L)} V_{R(L-L)}}{X^{\prime}} \sin \delta\), the power angel \(\delta\) is obtained from

\(\begin{aligned}&800=(500 \times 500 / 107.11) \sin \delta \\&\text { or } \quad \delta=20.044^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

The receiving end reactive power is given by (approximately)

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{R(3 \phi)} &=\frac{V_{S(L-L)} V_{R(L-L)}}{X^{\prime}} \cos \delta-\frac{V_{R(L-L)}^{2}}{X^{\prime}} \cos \beta l \\&=\frac{500 \times 500}{107.11} \cos \left(20.044^{\circ}\right)-\frac{(500)^{2}}{107.11} \cos \left(21.641^{\circ}\right) \\&=23.15 MVAR\end{aligned}\)

Then the required capacitor MVAR is \(\bar{S}_{C}=j 23.15-j 600=-j 576.85\)

The capacitive reactance is given by (see Eq. 2.3.5 in text)

\(X_{C}=\frac{-j V_{L}^{2}}{\bar{S}_{C}}=\frac{-j 500^{2}}{-j 567.85}=433.38 \Omega\)

or \(C=\frac{10^{6}}{2 \pi(60) 433.38}=6.1 \mu F\)

 

(b) For 40% compensation, the series capacitor reactance per phase is

\(X_{\text {ser }}=0.4 X^{\prime}=0.4(107.1)=42.84 \Omega\)

The new equivalent π -circuit parameters are given by

\(\begin{gathered}\bar{Z}^{\prime}=j\left(X^{\prime}-X_{\text {ser }}\right)=j 64.26 \Omega ; \bar{Y}^{\prime}=j \frac{2}{\bar{Z}_{C}} \tan \left(\frac{\beta l}{2}\right)=j 0.001316 S \\\bar{B}_{\text {new }}=j 64.26 \Omega ; \bar{A}_{\text {new }}=1+\frac{\bar{Z}^{\prime} \bar{Y}^{\prime}}{2}=0.9577\end{gathered}\)

The receiving end voltage per phase \(\bar{V}_{R}=\frac{500}{\sqrt{3}} \angle 0^{\circ} kV =288.675 \angle 0^{\circ} kV\)

The receiving end current is \(\bar{I}_{R}=\bar{S}_{R(3 \phi)}^{*} / 3 \bar{V}_{R}^{*}\)

Thus \(\bar{I}_{R}=\frac{1000 \angle-36.87^{\circ}}{3 \times 288.675 \angle 0^{\circ}}=1.1547 \angle-36.87^{\circ} kA\)

The sending end voltage is then given by

\(\bar{V}_{S}=\bar{A} \bar{V}_{R}+\bar{B} \bar{I}_{R}=326.4 \angle 10.47^{\circ} kV ; \quad V_{S(L-L)}=\sqrt{3} 326.4=565.4 kV\)

Percent voltage regulation \(=\frac{(565.4 / 0.958)-500}{500} \times 100=18 \%\)
