Guest Question.

Calculating the first-order correction to the energy
A small step in the potential energy is introduced into the one-dimensional squarewell problem (Fig. 6.5). Calculate the first-order correction to the energy of a particle confined to the well and evaluate it for a = L/10, so the blip in the potential occupies the central 10 per cent of the well, and for (a) n = 1, (b) n = 2.

Method We need to evaluate eqn 6.24 by using

\(H^{(1)} = \begin{cases} \varepsilon & if \frac{1}{2}(L-a)\leq x\leq\frac{1}{2}(L+a) \\ 0 & \text{if x is outside this region} \end{cases}\)

 

\(E^{(1)}_{0} =\left\langle0\left|H^{(1)} \right|0 \right\rangle =H^{(1)}_{00} \) (6.24)

The wavefunctions are given in eqn 2.31. We should anticipate that the effect of the perturbation will be much smaller for n = 2 than for n = 1 because in the former the perturbation is applied in the vicinity of a node.

\(\Psi _{n} (x)=\left(\frac{2}{L} \right)^{1/2} \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right)\) (2.31)

\(E_{n} =\frac{n^{2}h^{2} }{8mL^{2} }\) \( n=1,2,...\)

Admin
Solution

The integral required is

\(E^{(1)}_{n} =\frac{2\varepsilon }{L}\int_{\frac{1}{2}(L-a) }^{\frac{1}{2}(L+a)}{\sin ^{2}\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) dx }= \varepsilon \left\{\frac{a}{L} -\frac{(-1)^{n} }{n\pi }\sin \left(\frac{n\pi a}{L} \right) \right\} \)

We have used the integral

\(\int{\sin ^{2}kxdx } =\frac{1}{2}x-\frac{1}{4k}\sin 2kx+\)constant

and the relations

\(\cos (n\pi ) =(-1)^{n}\) and \(\sin (x\pm y)=\sin x\cos y\pm \sin y\cos x\)

With \(a=L/10,(a)\) for \(n=1,E^{(1)} =0.1984\varepsilon ; (b) \) for \( n=2,E^{(1)}=0.0065\varepsilon\).

Comment The relative sizes of the two first-order corrections to the energy are consistent with the perturbation being close to an antinode for n = 1 and a node for n = 2. When n is very large, \(E^{(1)} \approx (a/L)\varepsilon \), independent of n. At such high quantum numbers, the probability of finding the particle in the region a is a/L regardless of n. Note that if \(\varepsilon \gt 0\), then the energy of the states is increased from the unperturbed values.
