Guest Question.

For the system shown in Fig. E11.2, find the required power to pump 100 L/s of liquid (S = 0.85, \(\nu = 10^-5 m^2/s\)). The pump is operating at an efficiency η = 0.75. Pertinent data are given in the figure.

Line 1: L = 10 m, D = 0.20 m, e = 0.05 mm, \(K_{1} = 0.5\),\( K_{v} = 2\)

Line 2: L = 500 m, D = 0.25 m, e = 0.05 mm, \(K_{e} = 0.25\), \(K_{2} = 1\)

Admin
Solution

This is a category 1 problem. The energy relation, Eq. 11.3.3, for the system is

\(\left(\frac{p}{\gamma}+z\right)_{\mathrm{A}}-\left(\frac{p}{\gamma}+z\right)_{\mathrm{B}}=\left[\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N} \left(R_{i}+\frac{\Sigma K}{2 g A_{i}^{2}}\right)\right] Q^{2} (11.3.3)\)

\(\left(\frac{p}{\gamma}+z\right)_{\mathrm{A}}+H_{P}=\left(\frac{p}{\gamma}+z\right)_{\mathrm{B}}+\left[R_{1}+\frac{K_{1}+K_{v}}{2 g A_{1}^{2}}+R_{2}+\frac{2 K_{e}+K_{2}}{2 g A_{2}^{2}}\right] Q^{2}\)

The resistance coefficients, \(R_{1}\) and \(R_{2}\), are calculated with Eq. \(11.2 .4\) after first evaluating \(\mathrm{Re}\) and \(e / D\) :

\(R=1.07\left(\frac{L}{g D^{5}}\right)\left\{\ln \left[0.27\left(\frac{e}{D}\right)+5.74\left(\frac{1}{\mathrm{Re}}\right)^{0.9}\right]\right\}^{-2} (11.2.4)\)

\(\begin{aligned}\operatorname{Re}_{1}&=\frac{4 Q}{\pi D_{1} \nu}=\frac{4 \times 0.10}{\pi \times 0.20 \times 10^{-5}}=6.37 \times 10^{4} \quad\left(\frac{e}{D}\right)_{1}=\frac{0.05}{200}=0.00025 \\\\\operatorname{Re}_{2}&=\frac{4 Q}{\pi D_{2} \nu}=\frac{4 \times 0.10}{\pi \times 0.25 \times 10^{-5}}=5.09 \times 10^{4} \quad\left(\frac{e}{D}\right)_{2}=\frac{0.05}{250}=0.0002 \\\\R_{1}&=1.07\left(\frac{10}{9.81 \times 0.20^{5}}\right) \\\\ &\times\left\{\ln \left[0.27 \times 0.00025+5.74 \times\left(6.37 \times 10^{4}\right)^{-0.9}\right]\right\}^{-2}=53.4 \\\\R_{2}&=1.07\left(\frac{500}{9.81 \times 0.25^{5}}\right) \\\\ & \times\left\{\ln \left[0.27 \times 0.0002+5.74 \times\left(5.09 \times 10^{4}\right)^{-0.9}\right]\right\}^{-2}=904\end{aligned}\)

The minor loss coefficient terms are calculated to be

\(\begin{array}{l}\frac{K_{1}+K_{v}}{2 g A_{1}^{2}}=\frac{0.5+2}{2 \times 9.81 \times\left[\pi / 4 \times 0.20^{2}\right]^{2}}=129.1 \\\\\frac{2 K_{e}+K_{2}}{2 g A_{2}^{2}}=\frac{2 \times 0.25+1}{2 \times 9.81 \times\left[\pi / 4 \times 0.25^{2}\right]^{2}}=31.7\end{array}\)

Substitute these values into the energy equation and obtain

\(0+10+H_{P}=\frac{200 \times 10^{3}}{0.85 \times 9800}+20+(53.4+129.1+904+31.7) 0.1^{2}\)

This relation reduces to

\(10+H_{P}=24+20+11.2\)

Solving for the head across the pump gives \(H_{P}=45.2 \mathrm{~m}\). The required input power is

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{W}_{P} &=\frac{\gamma Q H_{P}}{\eta} \\&=\frac{(9800 \times 0.85) \times 0.10 \times 45.2}{0.75} \\&=5.0 \times 10^{4} \mathrm{~W} \quad \text { or } \quad 50 \mathrm{~kW}\end{aligned}\)
