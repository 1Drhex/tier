Guest Question.

Declining Balance, (\(B_{N} \lt S\))

Compute the double-declining-balance (DDB) depreciation schedule for the data used in , this time with the asset having a salvage value of $2,000. Then

Cost basis of the asset, I = $10,000
Useful life, N = 5 years
Salvage value, S = $2,000

\(\alpha = \left(\frac{1}{5} \right) \left(2\right) = 40\%\)

Admin
Solution

Given: I = $10,000, S = $2,000, N = 5 years, and \(\alpha\) = 40%.
Find: \(D_{n}\) and \(B_{n}\) for n = 1 to 5.

The given data result in the accompanying table.

End of Year \(D_{n}\) \(B_{n}\)
1 0.4($10,000) = $4,000 $10,000 – $4,000 = $6,000
2 0.4(6,000) = 2,400 6,000 – 2,400 = 3,600
3 0.4(3,600) = 1,440 3,600 – 1,440 = 2,160
4 0.4(2,160) = 864 \(\gt\) 160 2,160 – 160 = 2,000
5 0 2,000 – 0 = 2,000
Total = $8,000

Note that \(B_{4}\) would be less than S = $2,000 if the full deduction ($864) had been taken. Therefore, we adjust \(D_{4}\) to $160, making \(B_{4}\) = $2,000. \(D_{5}\) is zero, and \(B_{5}\) remains at $2,000. Year 4 is equivalent to \(n"\) in Figure 9.3(b).
