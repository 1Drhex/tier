CONTROL VOLUME ANALYSIS OF IDEALIZED FLOW THROUGH A PROPELLER

Consider the one-dimensional model for the idealized flow through a propeller shown in Fig. 10.41. The propeller advances into still air at steady speed \(V_1\). Obtain expressions for the pressure immediately upstream and the pressure immediately downstream from the actuator disk. Write the thrust on the propeller as the product of this pressure difference times the disk area. Equate this expression for thrust to one obtained by applying the linear momentum equation to the control volume. Show that half the velocity increase occurs ahead of and half behind the propeller disk.

Admin
Solution

Given: Propeller advancing into still air at speed \(V_1\), as shown in Fig. 10.41.

Find:

(a) Expressions for the pressures immediately upstream and immediately downstream from the actuator disk.
(b) Expression for the air speed at the actuator disk. Then show that half the velocity increase occurs ahead of the actuator disk and half occurs behind the actuator disk.
Solution:
Apply the Bernoulli equation and the \(x\) component of linear momentum using the CV shown.
Governing equations:

\(\begin{gathered}\frac{p}{\rho}+\frac{V^{2}}{2}+\cancel{g z}^{\approx 0(5)}=\text { constant } \\\quad\\F_{S_{x}}+\cancel{F_{B_{x}}}^{=0(5)}=\cancel{\frac{\partial}{\partial t}}^{=0(1)} \int_{\mathrm{CV}} u_{x y z} \rho d \forall+\int_{\mathrm{CS}} u_{x y z} \rho \vec{V} \cdot d \vec{A}\end{gathered}\)

Assumptions:

(1) Steady flow relative to the CV.
(2) Incompressible flow.
(3) Flow along a streamline.
(4) Frictionless flow.
(5) Horizontal flow: neglect changes in \(z ; F_{B_{x}}=0\).
(6) Uniform flow at each section.
(7) \(p_{\mathrm{atm}}\) surrounds the CV.
Applying the Bernoulli equation from section (1) to section (2) gives

 

\(\frac{p_{\mathrm{atm}}}{\rho}+\frac{V_{1}^{2}}{2}=\frac{p_{2}}{\rho}+\frac{V_{2}^{2}}{2} ; \quad p_{2(\mathrm{gage})}=\frac{1}{2} \rho\left(V_{1}^{2}-V_{2}^{2}\right)\)

Applying Bernoulli from section (3) to section (4) gives

\(\frac{p_{3}}{\rho}+\frac{V_{3}^{2}}{2}=\frac{p_{\mathrm{atm}}}{\rho}+\frac{V_{4}^{2}}{2} ; \quad p_{3(\mathrm{gage})}=\frac{1}{2} \rho\left(V_{4}^{2}-V_{3}^{2}\right)\)

The thrust on the propeller is given by

\(F_{T}=\left(p_{3}-p_{2}\right) A=\frac{1}{2} \rho A\left(V_{4}^{2}-V_{1}^{2}\right) \quad\left(V_{3}=V_{2}=V\right)\)

From the momentum equation, using relative velocities,

\(\begin{aligned}&R_{x}=F_{T}=u_{1}(-\dot{m})+u_{4}(+\dot{m})=\rho V A\left(V_{4}-V_{1}\right) \quad\left\{u_{1}=V_{1}, u_{4}=V_{4}\right\} \\\quad\\&F_{T}=\rho V A\left(V_{4}-V_{1}\right)\end{aligned}\)

 

Equating these two expressions for \(F_{T}\),

\(\begin{gathered}F_{T}=\frac{1}{2} \rho A\left(V_{4}^{2}-V_{1}^{2}\right)=\rho V A\left(V_{4}-V_{1}\right) \\\quad\\\text { or } \frac{1}{2}\left(V_{4}+V_{1}\right)\left(V_{4}-V_{1}\right)=V\left(V_{4}-V_{1}\right)\end{gathered}\)

Thus, \(\quad V=\frac{1}{2}\left(V_{1}+V_{4}\right)\), so

\(\begin{aligned}&\Delta V_{12}=V-V_{1}=\frac{1}{2}\left(V_{1}+V_{4}\right)-V_{1}=\frac{1}{2}\left(V_{4}-V_{1}\right)=\frac{\Delta V}{2} \\\quad\\&\Delta V_{34}=V_{4}-V=V_{4}-\frac{1}{2}\left(V_{1}+V_{4}\right)=\frac{1}{2}\left(V_{4}-V_{1}\right)=\frac{\Delta V}{2}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad \text{Velocity Increase }}\end{aligned}\)

 

The purpose of this problem is to apply the continuity, momentum, and Bernoulli equations to an idealized flow model of a propeller, and to verify the Rankine theory of 1885 that half the velocity change occurs on either side of the propeller disk.
