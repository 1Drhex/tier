Guest Question.

Boeing Aircraft collected the following cost data on the first 8 units of its new business jet.

Unit Number Cost ($ millions)
1 $100
2 83
3 73
4 62
5 60
6 57
7 53
8 51
Admin
Solution

a. First, estimate the learning curve rate by calculating the average learning rate with each doubling of production.

Units 1 to 2 = 83/100 = 83%

Units 2 to 4 = 62/83 = 74.7%

Units 4 to 8 = 51/62 = 82.26%

Average = (83 + 74.7 + 82.26)/3 = 80%

b. The average cost of the first 1,000 units can be estimated using Exhibit 6.5. The cumulative improvement factor for the 1,000th unit at 80% learning is 158.7. The cost to produce the first 1,000 units is

$100M Ã— 158.7 = $15,870M

The average cost for each of the first 1,000 units is

$15,870M/1,000 = $15.9M

c. To estimate the cost to produce the 1,000th unit, use Exhibit 6.4.
The unit improvement factor for the 1,000th unit at 80% is .1082.
The cost to produce the 1,000th unit is

$100M Ã— .1082 = $10.82M
