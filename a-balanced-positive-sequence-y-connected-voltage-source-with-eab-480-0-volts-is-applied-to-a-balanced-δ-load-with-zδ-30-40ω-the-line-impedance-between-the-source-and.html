Balanced-Δ and-Y loads

A balanced, positive-sequence, Y-connected voltage source with \(E_{ab}=480\angle 0° \) volts is applied to a balanced-Δ load with \(Z_{\Delta } =30\angle 40°\Omega \).The line impedance between the source and load is \(Z_{L } =1\angle 85°\Omega \) for each phase. Calculate the line currents, the Δ-load currents, and the voltages at the load terminals.

Admin
Solution

The solution is most easily obtained as follows. First, convert the Δ load to an equivalent Y. Then connect the source and Y-load neutrals with a zero-ohm neutral wire. The connection of the neutral wire has no effect on the circuit, since the neutral current \(I_{n} = 0\) in a balanced system. The resulting circuit is shown in Figure 2.17. The line currents are

\(I_{A} =\frac{E_{an} }{Z_{L} +Z_{Y} }=\frac{\frac{480}{\sqrt{3} }\angle -30° }{1\angle 85°+\frac{30}{3} \angle 40° }=\frac{277.1\angle -30° }{\left(0.0872+j0.9962\right)+\left(7.660+j6.428\right) } =\frac{277.1\angle -30°}{\left(7.748+j7.424\right) } =\frac{277.1 \angle -30° }{10.73\angle 43.78°}=25.83\angle -73.78° \) A (2.5.18)

\(I_{B} =25.83\angle 166.22°\) A

\(I_{C} =25.83\angle 46.22°\) A

The Δ-load currents are, from (2.5.14),

\(I_{a} =\sqrt{3}I_{AB} \angle -30°\)

 

\(I_{b} =\sqrt{3}I_{BC} \angle -30° \)

 

\(I_{C} =\sqrt{3}I_{CA}\angle -30° \) (2.5.14)

\(I_{AB}=\frac{I_{a} }{\sqrt{3} }\angle +30° =\frac{25.83}{\sqrt{3} }\angle -73.78°+30°=14.91\angle -43.78° \) A

\(I_{BC} =14.91\angle -163.78°\)A (2.5.19)

\(I_{CA}=14.91\angle +76.22°\) A

The voltages at the load terminals are

\(E_{AB}=Z_{\Delta }I_{AB} =\left(30\angle 40° \right)\left(14.91\angle -43.78° \right) =447.3\angle -3.78°\)

 

\(E_{BC} =447.3\angle -123.78° \) (2.5.20)

 

\(E_{CA}=447.3\angle 116.22° \) volts
