Guest Question.

A one-story frame structure is to be built to house a chemical laboratory. The experiments performed in the laboratory involve highly volatile chemicals and the possibility of explosion is great. It is estimated that the worst explosion will generate a force of \(5 \times 10^{6}N\) lasting 0.5 s. The structure is to be designed such that the maximum displacement due to such an explosion is 10 mm. The equivalent mass of the structure is 500,000 kg. Draw the shock spectrum for the structure subject to a rectangular pulse and determine the minimum allowable stiffness for the structure.

Admin
Solution

The laboratory frame structure of Figure 5.14 is modeled as an undamped SDOF system with x(t) representing the displacement at the rigid girder. The excitation is modeled as a rectangular pulse of magnitude \(F_{0} = 5 \times 10^{6} N\) and duration \(t_{0} = 0.5 s\). The response of an undamped SDOF system to a rectangular pulse with zero initial conditions is calculated using superposition and Table 5.1 as

TABLE 5.1
Response of an undamped SDOF to common forms of
excitation
Delayed impulse
Excitation: \(F(t)=A\delta (t-t_{0})\)
Response: \(m_{eq}\omega ^{2}_{n}x(t)/ A=\omega _{n}\sin \omega _{n}(t-t_{0})u(t-t_{0})\)
Delayed step function
Excitation: \(F(t ) = Au(t – t_{0})\)
Response: \( m_{eq}\omega^{2}_{n}x(t)/A=[1-\cos \omega_{n} (t-t_{0})] u (t-t_{0})\)
Delayed ramp function
Excitation: \(F(t)=(At+B)u(t-t_{0})\)
Response: \(m_{eq}\omega^{2}_{n}x(t)/A=[t+B/A – (t_{0}+B/A)\cos \omega_{n} (t-t_{0})- \frac{1}{\omega _{n}}\sin \omega _{n}(t – t_{0})]u(t – t_{0})\)
Delayed exponential function
Excitation: \(F(t) = Ae ^{-\alpha (t – t_{0})}u(t – t_{0})\)
Response: \(m_{eq} \omega^{2}_{n}x(t)/A=[e^{-\alpha(t -t_{0})}+\alpha/\omega_{n}\sin \omega_{n}(t-t_{0})\\ -\cos\omega_{n}(t-t_{0})]/(1+\alpha^{2}/\omega^{2}_{n}) u (t-t_{0})\)
Delayed sine function:
Excitation:\(F(t) = A \sin[\omega (t – t_{0})]u(t – t_{0})\)
Response: \(\begin{matrix} \frac{m_{eq}\omega ^{2}_{n}xt}{A} =\frac{1}{2}\left\{\left(\frac{1}{\omega /\omega _{n}-1} \right)[\sin\omega (t-t_{0})-\sin\omega _{n}(t-t_{0})]\right. \\ \left. -\left(\frac{1}{\omega /\omega _{n}+1} \right)[\sin \omega (t-t_{0})+\sin\omega _{n}(t-t_{0})] \right\} u(t-t_{0}) \end{matrix} \)
This table provides the response of an undamped SDOF system to common forms of excitation. Man forms of excitation can be written as combinations of the excitations whose system responses are provided in the table. Superposition can be used to determine the response due to these excitations. In other cases, excitations can be approximated by combinations of excitations in this table. Then this table and superposition is used to approximate the response of an undamped SDOF system. The table provides the mathematical form of the excitation and response as well as graphical representations. In all cases, values of \(\omega_{n}= 10 rad/s\) and \(r_{0} = 0.5 s\) were used to generate the graphs. The values of specific parameters used for specific excitations are given.

\(x(t)=\frac{F_{0}}{k}\left\{1-\cos\omega _{n}t-u(t-t_{0})\left[1-\cos\omega _{n}(t-t_{0})\right] \right\} \) (a)

For \(t\lt t_{0}\), the nondimensional force ratio is

\(\frac{kx}{F_{0}}= 1-\cos\omega _{n}t\) (b)

The preceding function increases until \(t=\pi/\omega _{0}\) when it reaches a maximum value of 2. If \(t_{0}\lt \pi/\omega _{n}\), the maximum nondimensional force ratio in this interval is

\(\frac{kx_{max}}{F_{0}}= 1-\cos\omega _{n}t_{0}\) (c)

However, since the response is continuous, the maximum response for \(t\gt \pi/\omega _{0}\) must be at least this large. For \(t\gt t_{0}\), the nondimensional force ratio is

\(\frac{kx}{F_{0}}=\cos\omega _{n}(t-t_{0})-\cos\omega _{n}t\) (d)

Trigonometric identities are used on the above equation to obtain

\(\frac{kx}{F_{0}}=2\sin \frac{\omega_{n}t_{0}}{3}\sin (\omega _{n}t-\alpha )\) (e)

where

\(\tan \alpha =\frac{\cos \omega _{n}t_{0}-1}{\sin \omega _{n}t_{0}} \) (f)

Thus,

\(\begin{matrix} \frac{kx_{\max}}{F_{0}}=2\sin \frac{\omega _{n}t_{0}}{2} &&&t_{0}\lt \frac{\pi}{\omega _{n}} \end{matrix}\) (g)

In summary,

\(\frac{kx_{\max}}{F_{0}} = \begin{cases}2\sin\frac{\omega _{n}t_{0}}{2} & t_{0}\lt \frac{\pi }{\omega _{n}}\left(\frac{t_{0}}{\tau }\leq \frac{1}{2} \right) \\ 2 & t_{0 }\gt \frac{\pi }{\omega _{n}} \left(\frac{t_{0}}{\tau }\gt \frac{1}{2} \right) \end{cases} \) (h)

The shock spectrum is plotted in Figure 5.15

Returning to the specific problem,\(t_{0}=0.5 s,F_{0}=5\times10^{6}N,x_{\max}=0.01m \) and m 500,000 kg. The natural frequency is \(\omega_{n}=\sqrt{{k}/{m}}\), and the problem is to determine appropriate values of k. The natural period is \(T=2\pi/\omega _{n}\). First assume \(t_{0}/T\lt 0.5\), which
is equivalent to

\(\frac{\omega _{n}t_{0}}{2\pi}\lt 0.5\) (i)

or

\(\omega _{n}\lt 2\pi rad/s\) (j)

The equation to solve for k is

\(\frac{k(0.01m)}{5\times10^{6}}=2\sin\left(\sqrt{\frac{k}{500,0000 kg} } \frac{0.5 s}{2} \right)\) (k)

Equation (k) becomes

\(1\times 10^{-8}k=\sin\left(3.54\times 10^{-4}\sqrt{k} \right) \) (l)

Equation (l) is a transcendental equation to solve for k with the smallest positive solution being \(k=5.33\times 10^{7}N/m\). The natural frequency with this value of k is

\(\omega _{n}=\sqrt{\frac{5.33\times 10^{7}N/m}{500,000 kg} }=10.32 rad/s\gt 2\pi rad/s \) (m)

Thus, there is no solution for \(\frac{\omega _{n}t_{0}}{2\pi}\lt 0.5\). Hence, \(\omega _{n}\gt 2\pi rad/s\) and \(\frac{kx_{\max}}{F_{0}} =2 \), which leads to

\(\frac{k(0.01m)}{5\times 10^{6}N} =2 \) (n)

which is solved yielding

\(k=1\times 10^{9}N/m\) (o)

If \(k\gt 1\gt 10^{9}N/m\), the maximum displacement will be less than 0.01 m
