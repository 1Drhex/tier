Guest Question.

Calculate the source currents in without using symmetrical components. Compare your solution method with that of . Which method is easier?

Admin
Solution

Converting the ∆ load to an equivalent Y, and then writing two loop equations:

\(\left[\begin{array}{c|c}2\left(\bar{Z}_{L}+\bar{Z}_{Y}\right) & -\left(\bar{Z}_{L}+\bar{Z}_{Y}\right) \\\hline-\left(\bar{Z}_{L}+\bar{Z}_{Y}\right) & 2\left(\bar{Z}_{L}+\bar{Z}_{Y}\right)\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{c} \\-\bar{I}_{b}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{V}_{c g}-\bar{V}_{a g} \\\bar{V}_{a g}-\bar{V}_{b g}\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{c|c}21.46 \angle 43.78^{\circ} & -10.73 \angle 43.78^{\circ} \\\hline-10.73 \angle 43.78^{\circ} & 21.46 \angle 43.78^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\) \(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{c} \\-\bar{I}_{b}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}295 \angle 115^{\circ}-277 \angle 0^{\circ} \\277 \angle 0^{\circ}-260 \angle-120^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{c} \\-\bar{I}_{b}\end{array}\right]=\) \(\left[\begin{array}{c|c}21.46 \angle 43.78^{\circ} & -10.73 \angle 43.78^{\circ} \\\hline-10.73 \angle 43.78^{\circ} & 21.46 \angle 43.78^{\circ}\end{array}\right]^{-1}\) \(\left[\begin{array}{l}482.5 \angle 146.35^{\circ} \\465.1 \angle 28.96^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{c} \\-\bar{I}_{b}\end{array}\right]=\) \(\left[\begin{array}{c|c}0.06213 \angle-43.78^{\circ} & 0.03107 \angle-43.78^{\circ} \\\hline 0.03107 \angle-43.78^{\circ} & 0.06213 \angle-43.78^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\) \(\left[\begin{array}{c}482.5 \angle 146.35^{\circ} \\465.1 \angle 28.96^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{c} \\-\bar{I}_{b}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}29.98 \angle 102.57^{\circ}+14.45 \angle -14.82^{\circ} \\14.99 \angle 102.57^{\circ}+28.90 \angle -14.82^{\circ}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}7.445+j 25.57 \\24.68+j 7.239\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{c} \\-\bar{I}_{b}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}26.62 \angle 73.77^{\circ} \\25.71 \angle 16.34^{\circ}\end{array}\right] A\)

 

Also, \(\bar{I}_{a}=-\bar{I}_{b}-\bar{I}_{c}\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{I}_{a}=(24.68+j 7.239)-(7.445+j 25.57) \\&\bar{I}_{a}=17.23-j 18.33=25.15 \angle-46.76^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{a} \\\bar{I}_{b} \\\bar{I}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}25.15 \angle-46.76^{\circ} \\25.71 \angle 196.34^{\circ} \\26.62 \angle 73.77^{\circ}\end{array}\right] A\)

 

which agrees with . The symmetrical components method is easier because it
avoids the need to invert a matrix.
