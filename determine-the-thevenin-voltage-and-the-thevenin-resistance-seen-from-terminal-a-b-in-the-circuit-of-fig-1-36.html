Guest Question.

Determine the Thevenin voltage and the Thevenin resistance seen from terminal a-b in the circuit of Fig. 1.36.

Admin
Solution

2.36. Since the circuit does not include any dependent source, the Thevenin resistance of the circuit can be simply calculated by using the series-parallel rule, while all the independent sources are turned off. As is illustrated in Fig. 2.36.2, theindependent voltage sources are replaced by the short-circuit branches, and the independent current sources are replaced by the open circuit branches.

Moreover, to determine the Thevenin voltage of the circuit, the open circuit voltage of the terminal is calculated by using nodal analysis, as can be seen in Fig. 2.36.3.

To calculate the Thevenin resistance (see Fig. 2.36.2):

\(R_{eq}=5||(1+2 ||10)=5 ||(+\frac{2×10}{2+10})=5||(1+\frac{5}{3})=5||(\frac{8}{3})=\frac{5×\frac{8}{3}}{5+\frac{8}{3}}=\frac{\frac{40}{3}}{\frac{23}{3}}=\frac{40}{23}Ω\) \(R_{Th}=R_{eq}=\frac{40}{23}Ω\)

To calculate the Thevenin voltage (see Fig. 2.36.3):

Finding a relation between the node voltages by defining the voltage of the 30 V independent voltage source based on the node voltages:

\(V_{oc}-e_{1}=30\Rightarrow e_{1}=V_{oc}-30 \)…..(1)

KCL in the supernode:

\(-1+\frac{V_{oc}-e_{2}}{10}+\frac{e_{1}-e_{2}}{2}+\frac{V_{oc}-50}{5}=0\Rightarrow \frac{1}{2}e_{1}-\frac{6}{10}e_{2}+\frac{3}{10}V_{oc}-11=0\)

\(Using (1)\Rightarrow \frac{1}{2}(V_{oc}-30) -\frac{6}{10}e_{2}+\frac{3}{10}V_{oc}-11=0\Rightarrow -\frac{6}{10}e_{2}+\frac{8}{10}V_{oc}-26=0.\)…..(2)

KCL in node 2:

\(\frac{e_{2}-e_{1}}{2}+\frac{e_{2}}{1}-7+\frac{e_{2}-V_{oc}}{10}=0\Rightarrow -\frac{1}{2}e_{1}+\frac{16}{10}e_{2}-\frac{1}{10}V_{oc}-7=0\)

\(Using(1)\Rightarrow -\frac{1}{2}(V_{oc}-30)+\frac{16}{10}e_{2}-\frac{1}{10}V_{oc}-7=0 \Rightarrow \frac{16}{10}e_{2} -\frac{6}{10}V_{oc}+8=0\)…..(3)

Solving (2) and (3):

\(-\frac{6}{10}(\frac{10}{16}(\frac{6}{10}V_{oc}-8))+\frac{8}{10}V_{oc}-26=0\Rightarrow \frac{23}{40}V_{oc}-23=0\Rightarrow V_{oc}=40 \) \(\Rightarrow V_{Th}=40V\)
