Guest Question.

A precast concrete pile of size 18 x 18 in is driven into stiff clay. The unconfmed compressive strength of the clay is 4.2 \(\text { kips } / f t^{2}\). Determine the length of pile required to carry a safe working load of 90 kips with \(F_{s}=2.5\).

Admin
Solution

The equation for \(Q_{u}\) is

 

\(Q_{u}=N_{c} c_{u} A_{b}+\alpha \bar{c}_{u} A_{s}\)

 

we have

 

\(Q_{u}=2.5 \times 90=225 kips\),

 

\(N_{c}=9, c_{u}=2.1 kips / ft ^{2}\)

 

\(\alpha=0.48 \text { from Fig. } 15.15, \bar{c}_{u}=c_{u}=2.1 kips / ft ^{2}, A_{b}=2.25 ft ^{2}\)

 

Assume the length of pile = L ft

 

Now, \(A_{s}=4 \times 1.5 L=6 L\)

 

Substituting the known values, we have

 

\(225=9 \times 2.1 \times 2.25+0.48 \times 2.1 \times 6 L\)

 

or \(225=42.525+6.05 L\)

 

Simplifying, we have

 

\(L=\frac{225-42.525}{6.05}=30.2 ft\)
