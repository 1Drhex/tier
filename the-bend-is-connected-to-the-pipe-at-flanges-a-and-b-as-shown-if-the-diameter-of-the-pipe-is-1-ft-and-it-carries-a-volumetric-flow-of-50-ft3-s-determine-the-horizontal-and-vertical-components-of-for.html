Guest Question.

The bend is connected to the pipe at flanges A and B as shown. If the diameter of the pipe is 1 ft and it carries a volumetric flow of \(50 ft^3/s \), determine the horizontal and vertical components of force and the moment exerted at the fixed base D of the support. The total weight of the bend and the water within it is 500 lb, with a mass center at point G. The pressure of the water at A is 15 psi. Assume that no force is transferred to the flanges at A and B.

Admin
Solution

From the discharge,

\(Q = V_A; 50 ft^3/s = V[\pi (0.5 ft)^2] \)

 

\(V_A = V_B = V = 63.66 ft/s \)

The flow is steady and water can be considered as an ideal fluid (incompressible and inviscid), such that \(\gamma _w = 62.4 Ib/ft^2.\) Average velocities will be used.

Bernoulli Equation

\(p_B = 1983.5 Ib/ft^2 \)

 

\(F_A = p_AA_A = 15 Ib/in^2(\pi )(6 in.)^2 = 1696.46 Ib \)

 

\(F_B = p_BA_B = 1983.5 Ib/ft^2(\pi )(0.5 ft)^2 = 1557.84 Ib \)

 

\(\frac{P_A}{\rho } + \frac{V^2_A}{2} + gz_A = \frac{P_B}{\rho } + \frac{V^2_B}{2} + gz_B \)

 

\(\frac{15(144) Ib/ft^2}{(\frac{62.4 Ib/ft^2}{32.2 ft/s^2})} + \frac{V^2}{2} + 0 = \frac{p_B}{(\frac{62.4 Ib/ft^2}{32.2 ft/s^2})} + \frac{V^2}{2}(32.2 ft/s^3)(4 ft \sin 45^{\circ}) \)

Applying the linear momentum equation.

\(\sum F = \frac{\partial }{\partial t}\int_{cv} V\rho_w dV + \int_{cs} V \rho_w V \cdot dA \)

Writing the scalar component of this equation along x and y axes by referring to the FBD of the control volume, Fig. a

\(\overset{+}{\rightarrow} \sum F_x = 0 + V_A\rho _w( – V_AA_A) + (V_B cos 45°)\rho _w(V_BA_B) \)

 

\(1696.46 Ib – [(1557.84 Ib) cos 45°] – D_x = (63.66 ft/s)(\frac{62.4 Ib/ft^3}{32.2 ft/s^2})(-50 ft^3/s)+ [ (63.66 ft/s) cos 45°] (\frac{62.4 Ib/ft^3}{32.2 ft/s^2})(50 ft^3/s) \)

 

\(D_x = 2401.6 Ib = 2.40 kip \)

 

\(+ ↑ \sum Fy = 0 + (V_B sin 45°)\rho w(V_BA_B) \)

 

\(D_y – 500 – [(1557.84) sin 45°] = [ (63.66 ft/s) sin45°](\frac{62.4 Ib/ft^3}{32.2 ft/s^2})(50 ft^3/s) \)

 

\(D_y = 5963.3 lb = 5.96 kip \)

Applying the Angular Momentum equation

\(\sum M = \frac{\partial }{\partial t} \int_{cv}(r × V ) \rho_w dV + \int_{cs}(r × V) \rho_w V \cdot dA \)

Writing the scalar component of this equation about D by referring to the FBD

\(+ \sum M_D = 0 + (-r_AV_A)\rho w(-V_AA_A) + (-r_BV_B cos 45°)\rho_w(V_BA_B) \)

 

\(M_D + [(1559.84 Ib) cos 45°](4 ft) – (1696.46 lb)(4 ft) – (500 Ib)[(1.5 ft) cos 45°] \)

 

\(= -(4 ft)(63.66 ft/s)(\frac{62.4 Ib/ft^3}{32.2 ft/s^2})( -50 ft^3/s) + (-4 ft) [ (63.66 ft/s)cos45°](\frac{62.4 Ib/ft^3}{32.2 ft/s^2})(50 ft^3/s) \)

 

\(M_D = 10136.8 lb ft = 10.1 kip \cdot ft \)
