Guest Question.

A thermopane window consists of two 5 mm thick glass (k = 0.78 W/m.K) sheets separated by 10 mm stagnant air gap (k = 0.025 W/m.K). The convection heat transfer coefficient for inner and outside air are 10 \(W/m^2.K \)and 50\(W/m^2.K, \)respectively. (a) Determine the rate of heat loss per \(m^2 \)of the glass surface for a temperature difference of 60°C between the inside and outside air. (b) Compare the result with the heat loss, if the window had only a single sheet of glass of thickness 5 mm instead of thermopane. (c) Compare the result with the heat flow, if window has no stagnant air (i.e., a sheet of glass, 10 mm thick).

Admin
Solution

Given : A thermopane glass window :
\(L_1 =L_3 = 5 mm = 0.005 m,\)
\(L_2 = 10 mm = 0.01 m,\)
\(k_1 = k_3 = 0.78 W/m.K,\)
\(k_2 = 0.025 W/m.K\)
\(h_1 = 10 W/m^2.K,\)
\(h_2 = 50 W/m^2.K\)
\(∆T = T_{∞_{1} }– T_{∞_{2} }= 60°C.\)
To find :
(a) Heat flow rate through the glass per \(m^2.\)
(b) Heat flow rate when window has only a glass sheet of 5 mm thick.
(c) Heat flow rate when window has only a glass sheet of 10 mm thick.

Assumptions :
(i) One dimensional steady state heat flow.
(ii) Constant properties.
Analysis : The various specific thermal
resistances (for 1 \(m^2)\) are shown and calculated as :
\(R_1 = \frac{1}{h_1 A}=\frac{1}{10 \times 1} = 0.10 K/W\)
\(R_2 = R_4 = \frac{L_1}{k_1 A}=\frac{0.005}{0.78 \times 1} = 0.00641 K/W \)

\(R_3 = \frac{L_2}{k_2 A}=\frac{0.01}{0.025 \times 1} = 0.40 K/W\)
\(R_5 = \frac{1}{h_1 A}=\frac{1}{50 \times 1} = 0.020 K/W\)
(a) The heat flow rate through the thermopane window is given by
\(Q =\frac{∆T}{Σ R_{th}} =\frac{T_{∞_{1}} – T_{∞_{2}}}{R_1 +R_2 +R_3 +R_4 +R_5}\)
\(= \frac{60}{0.1 + 2 \times 0.00641 + 0.4 +0.020}\)
\(=\frac{60}{0.53282} = 112.60 W/m^2.\)

(b) If the window has a single sheet of glass of 5 mm thick, the total thermal resistance
\(ΣR_{th} =R_1 + R_2 + R_5\)
\(= 0.1 + 0.00641 + 0.02 = 0.12641 K/W\)
\(Q =\frac{60}{0.12641 } = 474.65 W/m^2.\)

The heat loss is about four times that of previous case.
(c) If the window has a glass sheet of 10 mm thick only, then total resistance
\(Σ_{Rth} =R_1 + 2 (R_2) + R_5\)
= 0.1 + 2 × 0.00641 + 0.02
= 0.13282 K/W
The heat loss per\( m^2 ;\)
\(Q = \frac{60}{0 =.13282}= 451.74 W/m^2.\)
Heat loss does not decrease appreciably by increasing the glass thickness.
