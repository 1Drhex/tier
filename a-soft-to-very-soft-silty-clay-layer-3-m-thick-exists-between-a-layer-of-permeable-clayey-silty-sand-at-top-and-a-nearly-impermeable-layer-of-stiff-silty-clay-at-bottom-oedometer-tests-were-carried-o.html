Guest Question.

A soft to very soft silty clay layer 3 m thick exists between a layer of permeable clayey silty sand at top and a nearly impermeable layer of stiff silty clay at bottom. Oedometer tests were carried out on a sample of the soft clay 19 mm thick. From the plot of \(\Delta H \) versus log time, the value of time \(t_{100} \) (the time at 100% primary consolidation) was 100 min. From the graph of e versus log \({\sigma}' \) the compression index \(C_{c} \) was 0.32 and the void ratio \(e_{p} \) at the end of the primary consolidation was 0.72. Estimate the secondary compression of the silty clay layer that would occur from time of completion of the primary consolidation to 30 years of the load application in the field.

Admin
Solution

Equation : \( S_{s} = {C}'_{\alpha} H log \left ( t_{2}/t_{1} \right ), \) where \( {C}' \alpha = \frac{C_{\alpha }}{\left ( 1 + e_{P} \right )}. \)

Equation : estimate \( \frac{C_{\alpha }}{C_{c}} = 0.04; \) hence \( C_{a} = 0.04 \times 0.32 = 0.013 \)

\( {C}'_{\alpha } = \frac{0.013}{1 + 0.72} = 75.6 \times 10^{-4} \)

 

For the same clay soil:

\( T_{v} = \frac{c_{v} \times t_{field}}{d_{field}^{2}} =\) \( \frac{c_{v} \times t_{lab}}{d_{lab}^{2}}; \) hence \( t_{field} = \frac{d_{field}^{2} \times t_{lab} }{d_{lab}^{2}} = \frac{3000^{2} \times 100}{\left ( \frac{19}{2} \right )^{2}} \)

 

\( t_{field} = 99.723 \times 10^{5} min = \frac{99.723 \times 10^{5}}{365 \times 24 \times 60} = 19 years \)

 

\( t_{1} = t_{field} = 19 years; t_{2} = 30 years \)

 

\( S_{s} = 75.6 \times 10^{-4} \times 3000 \times log \left ( 30 / 19 \right ) = 4.5 mm \)

However, it is very likely that the secondary compression will be larger than this small value, as some will occur during primary consolidation. Theoretically, at the end of the 19 years there is no excess pore pressure anywhere in the 3-m clay layer; however during this time period dissipation occurs from the top down, with secondary compression beginning before 19 years have elapsed in the upper regions.
