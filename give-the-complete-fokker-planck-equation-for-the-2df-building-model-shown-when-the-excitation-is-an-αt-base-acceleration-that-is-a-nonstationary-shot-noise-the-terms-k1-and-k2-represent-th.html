Guest Question.

Give the complete Fokker-Planck equation for the 2DF building model shown when the excitation is an \( \left\{\alpha (t)\right\} \) base acceleration that is a nonstationary shot noise. The terms \( K_{1}\) and \( k_{2} \) represent the total stiffness of all columns in the stories, and \( X_{1}(t) \) and \( X_{2}(t) \) are motions relative to the base of the structure.

Admin
Solution

The equation of motion is Eq. 9.5

\( A\dot{\vec{Y}} (t)+B\vec{Y}(t)=\vec{Q} (t)\)

with \( n_{Y}=4\) , and

\( \vec{Y} (t)=\left(\begin{matrix} X_{1}(t) \\ X_{2}(t) \\ \dot{X}_{1}(t) \\ \dot{X}_{2}(t) \end{matrix} \right) \) , \( A=\left(\begin{matrix}-k_{1}-k_{2} & k_{2} & 0 & 0 \\ k_{2} & -k_{2} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{c_{1}+c_{2}}{m_{1}} & \frac{-c_{2}}{0} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & m_{2} \end{matrix} \right) \)

\( B=\left(\begin{matrix}0 & 0 & k_{1}+k_{2} & -k_{2} \\0 & 0 & -k_{2} & k_{2} \\ k_{1}+k_{2} & -k_{2} & c_{1}+c_{2} & -c_{2} \\ -k_{2} & k_{2} & -c_{2} & c_{2} \end{matrix} \right) \) \( \vec{Q}(t)=\left(\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ -m_{1}a(t) \\ -m_{2}a(t) \end{matrix} \right) \)

The first-order coefficients in the Fokker-Planck equation are found from Eq.9.84

\( \vec{C}^{(1)}(\vec{u},t)=A^{-1}\vec{\mu }_{Q}(t)-A^{-1}B\vec{u} \)

which can be written as

\( C^{(1)}_{j}=[A^{-1}\vec{\mu }_{Q}(t) ]_{j}-[A^{-1}B\vec{u} ]_{j}=\sum\limits_{l=1}^{4}{A^{-1}_{lj}}\mu _{Q_{j}}(t)-\sum\limits_{l=1}^{4}{[A^{-1}B]_{lj}u_{l}} \)

Giving \( C^{(1)}_{l}=-u_{3} \) , \( C^{(1)}_{2}=-u_{4} \) .

\( C^{(1)}_{3}=-\mu _{a}(t)+\frac{k_{1}+k_{2}}{m_{1}}u_{1}-\frac{k_{2}}{m_{1}}u_{2}+\frac{c_{1}+c_{2}}{m_{1}}u_{3}-\frac{c_{2}}{m_{1}}u_{4} \) \( C^{(1)}_{4}=-\mu _{a}(t)+\frac{k_{2}}{m_{2}}u_{1}-\frac{k_{2}}{m_{2}}u_{2}+\frac{c_{2}}{m_{2}}u_{3}-\frac{c_{2}}{m_{2}}u_{4} \)

which can be further simplified by noting that \( \mu _{a}(t)=\mu _{F}\dot{\mu }_{Z}(t) \) for the nonstationary shot noise, in which \( F \) denotes a typical pulse of the shot noise, and \( \dot{\mu }_{Z}(t) \) is the expected arrival rate for the pulses (see ). Because the excitation is a non-Gaussian delta-correlated process, the nonzero higherorder coefficients are found from Eq. 9.87

\(C^{(j_{1},…,j_{n_{Y}})}(\vec{u},t)=(2\pi )^{j-1}S_{j_{1},…,j_{n_{Y}}}(t)\)

with \( S_{j_{1}j_{2}j_{3}j_{4}}(t) \) being the \( (j_{3}+j_{4})(t) \) -order autospectral density of \( \left\{-a(t)\right\} \) if \( j_{1}=j_{2}=0 \) and being zero otherwise. In we found that the \( J\)th-order autospectral density of the shot noise is \( E(F^{J})\dot{\mu }_{Z}(t)/(2\pi )^{J-1} \) .Thus, Eq. 9.87 gives

\( C^{(j_{1},j_{2},j_{3},j_{4})}=E[(-F)^{j_{3}+j_{4}}]\dot{\mu }_{Z}(t) \) for \( j_{1}=j_{2}=0 \)

\( =0 \) otherwise

Using the simplified notation of \( p(\vec{u}) \) for \( p_{X_{1}(t)X_{2}(t)\dot{X}_{1}(t)\dot{X}_{2}(t)}(u_{1},u_{2},u_{3},u_{4}) \) ,the Fokker-Planck relationship from Eq. 9.79

\(\frac{\delta}{\delta t} p_{\vec{Y}(t)}(\vec{u})= \sum\limits_{j_{1}=0}^{\infty } \sum\limits_{j_{n_{Y}}=0}^{\infty } {\frac{(-1)^{j_{1}+…+j_{n_{Y}}}}{j_{1}!…j_{n_{Y}}!} } \frac{\delta^{j_{1}+…+j_{n_{Y}}}}{\delta u_{1}^{j_{1}}…\delta u_{n_{Y}}^{j_{n_{Y}}}} \times [C^{j_{1},…,j_{n_{Y}}} (\vec{u},t)p_{\vec{Y}(t)}(\vec{u} )]\)

is

\( \frac{\partial}{\partial t}p(\vec{u} )=-\frac{\partial}{\partial u_{1}}[-u_{3}p(\vec{u} )]-\frac{\partial}{\partial u_{2}}[-u_{4}p(\vec{u} )]- \) \( \frac{\partial}{\partial u_{3}}\left(\left[-\mu _{F}\dot{\mu }_{Z}(t)+\frac{k_{1}+k_{2}}{m_{1}}u_{1}-\frac{k_{2}}{m_{1}}u_{2}+\frac{c_{1}+c_{2}}{m_{1}}u_{3}-\frac{c_{2}}{m_{1}}u_{4}\right]p(\vec{u} ) \right) – \) \( \frac{\partial}{\partial u_{3}}\left(\left[-\mu _{F}\dot{\mu }_{Z}(t)+\frac{k_{2}}{m_{2}}u_{1}-\frac{k_{2}}{m_{2}}u_{2}+\frac{c_{2}}{m_{2}}u_{3}-\frac{c_{2}}{m_{2}}u_{4}\right]p(\vec{u} ) \right) + \) \( E(F^{2})\dot{\mu }_{Z}(t)\left(\frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial ^{2}}{\partial u^{2}_{3}}p(\vec{u} )+\frac{\partial ^{2}}{\partial u_{3}\partial u_{4}}p(\vec{u} )+\frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial ^{2}}{\partial u^{2}_{4}}p(\vec{u} )\right) + \) \( E(F^{3})\dot{\mu }_{Z}(t)\left(\frac{1}{6}\frac{\partial ^{3}}{\partial u^{3}_{3}}p(\vec{u} )+\frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial ^{3}}{\partial u^{2}_{3}\partial u_{4}}p(\vec{u} )+\frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial ^{3}}{\partial u_{3}\partial u^{2}_{4}}p(\vec{u} )+\frac{1}{6}\frac{\partial ^{3}}{\partial u^{3}_{4}}p(\vec{u} )\right) +… \)

Or

\( \frac{\partial}{\partial t}p(\vec{u} )=u_{3}\frac{\partial}{\partial u_{1}}p(\vec{u} )+u_{4}\frac{\partial}{\partial u_{2}}p(\vec{u} )-\frac{c_{1}+c_{2}}{m_{1}}p(\vec{u} )+\frac{c_{2}}{m_{2}}p(\vec{u} )- \) \( \left(-\mu _{F}\dot{\mu }_{Z}(t)+\frac{k_{1}+k_{2}}{m_{1}}u_{1}-\frac{k_{2}}{m_{1}}u_{2}+\frac {c{1}+c_{2}}{m_{1}}u_{3}-\frac{c_{2}}{m_{1}}u_{4}\right) \frac{\partial}{\partial u_{3}}p(\vec{u} )- \) \( \left(-\mu _{F}\dot{\mu }_{Z}(t)+\frac{k_{2}}{m_{2}}u_{1}-\frac{k_{2}}{m_{2}}u_{2}+\frac {c_{2}}{m_{2}}u_{3}-\frac{c_{2}}{m_{2}}u_{4}\right) \frac{\partial}{\partial u_{4}}p(\vec{u} )+ \) \( \dot{\mu }_{Z}(t)\sum\limits_{J=2}^{\infty }{E(F^{J})} \sum\limits_{l=0}^{J}{\frac{J!}{(J-l)!l!}} \frac{\partial ^{2}}{\partial u^{l}_{3}\partial u^{J-l}_{4}}p(\vec{u} ) \)
