Guest Question.

A body A is projected vertically upwards from the top of a tower with a velocity of 40 m/s, the tower being 180 m high. After t seconds, another body B is allowed to fall from the same point. Both the bodies reach the ground simultaneously. Calculate t and the velocities of A and B on reaching the ground.

Admin
Solution

(i) Motion of Body A

\( u = u_A = 40 \) m/s (↑) ;

h = -180 m ; g = -9.81 m/s².

\( t = t_A \) .

\( h = ut + \frac{1}{2} g t^2 \) .

\( -180 = 40 t_A +\frac{1}{2} (-9.81) × (t_A)^2 \) .

\( 4.905 t^2_A – 40 t_A – 180 = 0 \).

\( t_A = \frac{-(-40) \pm \sqrt{(-40)^2 – 4(4.905)(-180)} }{2\times 4.905} \) .

\( t_A = 11.38 \) sec.

Motion of Body B

\( u = u_B= 0 \) (↓).

h = 180 m ; g = 9.81 m/s².

\( t = t_B \).

\( h = ut + \frac{1}{2} g t^2 \) .

\( 180 = 0 + \frac{1}{2} (9.81) ×(t_B )^2 \) .

\( t_B = 6.06 \) sec.

(ii) ν = u + gt.

\( ν_A = u_A + gt_A \).

\( ν_A = 40 + (-9.81) × (11.38) \).

\( ν_A = -71.64\) m/s.

\( ν_A = 71.64\) m/s (↓).

ν = u + gt.

\( ν_B = u_B + gt_B \).

\( ν_B = 0 + (9.81) × (6.06) \).

\( ν_B = 59.45\) m/s (↓).

(iii) \( t = t_A – t_B = 11.38 – 6.06 \).

t = 5.32 sec Ans.
