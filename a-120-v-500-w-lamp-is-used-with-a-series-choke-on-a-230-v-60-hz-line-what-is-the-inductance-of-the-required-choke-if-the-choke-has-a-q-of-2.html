Guest Question.

A 120 V, 500 W lamp is used with a series choke on a 230 V, 60 Hz line, what is the inductance of the required choke if the choke has a Q of 2?

Admin
Solution

Here we have a 500 W lamp rated at 120 volt whereas the supply voltage is 230 V. Naturally we have to connect some circuit element R, L or C so that the bulb gets 120 V . The resistance of the lamp is given by

 

\(\frac{V^{2}}{R}=500=\frac{120^{2}}{R}\)

 

or \(R=\frac{120^{2}}{500}=28.8\Omega\)

 

Here it is suggested to connect a choke which has both resistance as well inductance. It is also given that Q of the coil is 2

 

i.e. \(\frac{\omega L}{R}=2\) where R is the resistance of the choke itself.

 

Now \(\omega =2\pi \times 60=376.8\) rad/sec

 

\(376.8L = 2R\)

 

or \(R = 188.4 L\)

 

Now current in the circuit is

 

\(120 \times I = 500\)

 

or \(I=\frac{500}{120}=4.167\) A

 

Hence impedance of the circuit is

 

\(\frac{230}{4.167}=55.2\Omega\)

 

Also that impedance is

 

\((28.8+188.4L)^{2}+(367.8L)^{2}=55.2^{2}\)

 

The solution gives L = 87 mH.
