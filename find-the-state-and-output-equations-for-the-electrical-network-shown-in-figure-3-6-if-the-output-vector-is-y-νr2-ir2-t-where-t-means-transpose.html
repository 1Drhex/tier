Guest Question.

Find the state and output equations for the electrical network shown in Figure 3.6 if the output vector is \( \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix}\nu _{R_{2}} & i_{R_{2}}\end{bmatrix} ^{T}\) , where T means transpose.

Admin
Solution

Immediately notice that this network has a voltage-dependent current source.

Step 1 Label all of the branch currents on the network, as shown in Figure 3.6.

Step 2 Select the state variables by listing the voltage-current relationships for all of the energy-storage elements:

\( L \frac{d i_{L}}{dt} = \nu _{L}\) (3:30a)

\( C \frac{d\nu _{C}}{dt} = i_{C}\) (3:30b)

From Eqs. (3.30) select the state variables to be the differentiated variables. Thus, the state variables, \( x_{1}\) and \( x_{2}\) , are

\( x_{1} =i_{L}; x_{2} = \nu _{C}\) (3:31)

Step 3 Remembering that the form of the state equation is

\( \mathbf{\dot {x} = Ax + Bu}\) (3:32)

we see that the remaining task is to transform the right-hand side of Eq. (3.30) into linear combinations of the state variables and input source current. Using Kirchhoff’s voltage and current laws, we find \( \nu _{L} \) and \( i_{C}\) in terms of the state variables and the input current source.
Around the mesh containing L and C,

\( \nu _{L} = \nu _{C} + \nu _{R_{2}} = \nu _{C} + i _{R_{2}} R_{2}\) (3:33)

But at Node 2, \( i_{R_{2}} = i_{C} + 4\nu _{L}.\) Substituting this relationship for \( i_{R_{2}}\) into Eq. (3.33) yields

\( \nu _{L} = \nu _{C} +( i_{C} + 4 \nu _{L} ) R_{2}\) (3:34)

Solving for \( \nu _{L}\) , we get

\( \nu _{L} = \frac {1}{1- 4 R_{2}}( \nu _{C}+ i_{C} R_{2})\) (3:35)

Notice that since \( \nu _{C}\) is a state variable, we only need to find\( i _{C}\) in terms of the state variables. We will then have obtained \( \nu _{L}\) in terms of the state variables.

Thus, at Node 1 we can write the sum of the currents as

\( i_{C} = i(t) – i_{R_{1}} – i_{L}\)

 

\( = i(t) – \frac{\nu _{R_{1}}}{R_{1}} – i_{L}\)

\( = i(t) – \frac{\nu _{L}}{ R_{1}} – i_{L} \) (3:36)

where \( \nu _{R_{1}} = \nu _{L} \) . Equations (3.35) and (3.36) are two equations relating \( \nu _{L}\) and\( i _{C}\) in terms of the state variables \( i _{L}\) and \( \nu _{C}\) . Rewriting Eqs. (3.35) and (3.36), we obtain two simultaneous equations yielding \( \nu _{L} \) and \( i_{C}\) as linear combinations of the state variables \( i_{L}\) and \( \nu _{C}:\)

\( (1- 4R_{2})\nu _{L} – R_{2}i_{C} = \nu _{C}\) (3:37a)

\( -\frac{1}{R_{1}} \nu _{L} – i_{C} = i_{L} – i(t)\) (3:37b)

Solving Eq. (3.37) simultaneously for \( \nu _{L}\) and \( i _{C}\) yields

\( \nu _{L} = \frac{1}{\Delta }[R_{2}i_{L}- \nu _{C} – R_{2}i(t) ]\) (3:38)

and

\( i_{C} = \frac{1}{\Delta } [(1- 4 R_{2}) i_{L} + \frac{1}{R_{1}} \nu _{C} – (1- 4 R_{2}) i(t) ]\) (3:39)

where

\( \Delta = – [(1- 4 R_{2}) + \frac{R_{2}}{R_{1}}]\) (3:40)

Substituting Eqs. (3.38) and (3.39) into (3.30), simplifying, and writing the result in vector-matrix form renders the following state equation:

\( \begin{bmatrix} \dot {i_{L}} \\ \dot {\nu _{C}} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} R_{2} / (L \Delta )& – 1/ (L \Delta )\\ (1-4R_{2}) / (C\Delta )& 1/( R_{1} C\Delta) \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} i_{L} \\ \nu _{C} \end{bmatrix}\)

\( + \begin{bmatrix} – R_{2} / (L \Delta )\\ – (1-4R_{2}) / (C\Delta ) \end{bmatrix}\) (3:41)

Step 4 Derive the output equation. Since the specified output variables are \( \nu _{R_{2}}\) and \( i _{R_{2}}\) , we note that around the mesh containing C, L, and \( R_{2}\) ,

\( \nu _{R_{2}} = – \nu _{C} + \nu _{L}\) (3:42a)

\( i _{R_{2}} = i_{C} + 4\nu _{L} \) (3:42b)

Substituting Eqs. (3.38) and (3.39) into Eq. (3.42), \( \nu _{R_{2}}\) and \( i _{R_{2}}\) are obtained as linear combinations of the state variables, \( i _{L}\) and \( \nu _{C}\) . In vector-matrix form, the output equation is

\( \begin{bmatrix} \nu _{R_{2}} \\ i_{R_{2}} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} R_{2} / \Delta & – ( 1+1 / \Delta )\\ 1 / \Delta & (1- 4 R_{1}) / (\Delta R_{1} )\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} i_{L} \\ \nu _{C} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -R_{2} / \Delta \\ -1/ \Delta \end{bmatrix} i\left ( t \right )\)
(3:43)
