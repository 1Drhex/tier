Guest Question.

The catalytically active form of an enzyme can depend on its state of ionization; consequently, the pH (pH = – log \([H^{+} ]\) of the reacting medium can have a significant effect on the rate of reaction. Assuming that the active form of an enzyme is that after the loss of a single proton, determine the influence of the proton concentration on the maximum reaction rate. The equations governing the two inactive forms, E and \(E^{-2} \), due to the protona-tion and deprotonation of the active species, \(E^{-} \), are, respectively:

(1)\(E \xrightleftharpoons[]{K_{1} } E^{-}+ H^{+}\)

 

(1)\(E^{-} \xrightleftharpoons[]{K_{2} } E^{-2}+ H^{+}\)

Such ionization reactions are very rapid compared with most reaction rates in solution, thus quasi-equilibrium can be assumed.

Admin
Solution

\(\left[E\right] \xrightleftharpoons[]{K_{1} } \left[E\right] + \left[H^{+} \right] \) \(K_{1}= \frac{\left[H^{-} \right] \left[H^{+} \right] }{ E} \)

\(\left[E \right] \xrightleftharpoons[]{K_{2} } \left[E^{-2} \right] + \left[H^{+} \right]\) \(K_{2}= \frac{\left[E^{-2} \right] \left[H^{+} \right] }{\left[E^{-} \right]} \)

 

\(L_{e} = \left[E \right] + \left[E^{-} \right] + \left[E^{-2} \right]\)

 

active fraction = \(y^{-} = \frac{\left\lfloor E^{-}\right\rfloor }{L_{e}} = \frac{\left\lfloor E^{-}\right\rfloor }{\left(\left[E \right] + \left[E^{-} \right]+\left[E^{-2} \right]\right) } \)

 

\(y^{-} = \frac{\left[E^{-} \right]}{ \left\lgroup \frac{\left[H^{+} \right] \left[H^{-} \right]}{ k_{1} } + \left[E^{-} \right] + k_{2} \left[E^{-} \right] / \left[H^{+} \right] \right\rgroup } = \frac{1}{\left(1+\left[H^{+} \right]/K_{1}+K_{2} / \left[H^{+} \right]\right) } \) while

 

\(y = \frac{\left[E \right]}{L_{e} } = \frac{1}{\left(1+\left[H^{+} \right]/K_{1}+K_{2} / \left[H^{+} \right]^{2}\right) } \) and

 

\(\mathrm{y}^{-2}=\frac{\left[\mathrm{E}^{-2}\right]}{\mathrm{L}_{\mathrm{e}}}=\frac{1}{\left(1+\left[\mathrm{H}^{+}\right] / \mathrm{K}_{2}+\left[\mathrm{H}^{+}\right]^{2} / \mathrm{K}_{1} \mathrm{~K}_{2}\right)} \)

 

Thus the maximum rate is proportional to the active fraction and

\(r_{max}= L_{e} K_{2} y^{-} = \frac{L_{e} K_{2}}{ \left(1+\left[H^{+} \right]/ K_{1}+ K_{2}/K_{2} \right) } \)
