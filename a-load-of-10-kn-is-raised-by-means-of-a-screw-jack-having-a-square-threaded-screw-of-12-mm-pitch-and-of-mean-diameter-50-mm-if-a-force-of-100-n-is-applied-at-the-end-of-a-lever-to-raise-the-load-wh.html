Guest Question.

A load of 10 kN is raised by means of a screw jack, having a square threaded screw of 12 mm pitch and of mean diameter 50 mm. If a force of 100 N is applied at the end of a lever to raise the load, what should be the length of the lever used? Take coefficient of friction = 0.15. What is the mechanical advantage obtained? State whether the screw is self locking.

Admin
Solution

Given : W = 10 kN = 10 × \(10^{3}\) N ; p = 12 mm ; d = 50 mm ; \(P_{1}=100 N\) ; \(\mu=\tan \phi=0.15\)

 

Length of the lever

Let l = Length of the lever.

We know that \(\tan \alpha =\frac{p}{\pi d}=\frac{12}{\pi \times 50}=0.0764 \)

\(\therefore \) Effort required at the circumference of the screw to raise the load,

\(P=W\tan (\alpha +\phi )=W\left [ \frac{\tan \alpha +\tan \phi }{1-\tan \alpha \cdot \tan \phi } \right ]\)

 

\(=10\times 10^{3}\left [ \frac{0.0764+0.15}{1-0.0764\times 0.15} \right ]=2290N\)

and torque required to overcome friction,

T = P × d/2 = 2290 × 50/2 = 57 250 N-mm …(i)

We know that torque applied at the end of the lever,

T = \(P_{1}\) × l = 100 × l N-mm …(ii)

Equating equations (i) and (ii)

l = 57 250/100 = 572.5 mm

 

Mechanical advantage

We know that mechanical advantage,

\(M\cdot A\cdot =\frac{W}{P_{1}}=\frac{10\times 10^{3}}{100}=100\)

 

Self locking of the screw

We know that efficiency of the screw jack,

\(\eta =\frac{\tan \alpha }{\tan (\alpha +\phi )}=\frac{\tan \alpha (1-\tan \alpha \cdot \tan \phi )}{\tan \alpha +\tan \phi }\)

 

\(=\frac{0.0764(1-0.0764\times 0.15)}{0.0764+0.15}=\frac{0.0755}{0.2264}\) = 0.3334 or 33.35%

Since the efficiency of the screw jack is less than 50%, therefore the screw is a self locking screw.
