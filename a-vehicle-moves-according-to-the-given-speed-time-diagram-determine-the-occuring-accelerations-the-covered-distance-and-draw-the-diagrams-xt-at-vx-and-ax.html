Guest Question.

A vehicle moves according to the given speed-time diagram. Determine the occuring accelerations, the covered distance and draw the diagrams x(t), a(t), v(x) and a(x).

Admin
Solution

It is advantageous to devide the motion into 3 time sections:

1. Section \(0\leq t_{1} \leq 100 s\) (starting with constant acceleration \(a_{1}\)) : From

\(v_{1} = a_{1}t_{1}\) follows

\(\underline{\underline{a_{1} } } = \frac{v_{1}\left(100\right) }{100} = \frac{20}{100} = \underline{\underline{\frac{1}{5}m/ s^{2} } } \) , \(x_{1} = \frac{1}{2}a_{1}t^{2}_{1}\) ,

 

\(\underline{\underline{s_{1} } } = x_{1}\left(100\right) = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{5}\left(100\right) ^{2} = \underline{\underline{1000 m} } \) , \(v_{1}\left(x_{1} \right) = \sqrt{2a_{1}x_{1} }\) .

2. Section \(0\leq t_{2} \leq 200 s\) (uniform motion): From \(v_{2} = 20 m/ s=const\) results

\(\underline{\underline{a_{2} =0 } }\) , \(x_{2}=v_{2}t_{2}\) , \(\underline{\underline{s_{2} } }=x_{2}\left(200\right)=20\cdot 200=\underline{\underline{4000 m} }\) .

3. Section \(0\leq t_{3}\leq 60 s\) (braking with constant deceleration \(a_{3}\)): With

\(v_{3}=20 m/ s+ a_{3}t_{3}\) we obtain

\(\underline{\underline{a_{3} } }= -\frac{20}{60}=\underline{\underline{-\frac{1}{3}m/ s^{2} } }\) , \(x_{3}=20t_{3} +\frac{1}{2}a_{3}a^{2}_{3}\) ,

 

\(\underline{\underline{s_{3} } }=x_{3}\left(60\right) =20 \cdot 60 -\frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{3}\left(60\right)^{2} = \underline{\underline{600 m} }\) , \(v_{3} = \sqrt{400+2a_{3}x_{3} }\) .

In total, the vehicle covers the distance

\(\underline{\underline{s} }= s_{1} + s_{2} + s_{3} = 1000 + 4000+600 =\underline{\underline{5600 m} }\) .
