Guest Question.

In Figure P4.12, rigid beam ABD is supported at A by a 20-mm-diameter pin in a double shear connection and at B by a solid 38-mm-diameter rod. Rod (1) is supported at B and C by 16-mm-diameter pins in double shear connections. The yield strength of rod (1) is 340 MPa. The ultimate shear strength of each pin is 620 MPa. Assume a = 1.8 m, b = 0.9 m, c = 1.2 m, and d = 1.4 m. Determine the allowable distributed load w that may be applied to the rigid beam if an overall factor of safety of 2.5 is required.

Admin
Solution

Rod (1) is a two-force member that is oriented at θ with respect to the horizontal axis:

\(\tan \theta=\frac{1.4 m }{0.9 m }=1.5556 \quad \therefore \theta=57.2648^{\circ}\)

From a FBD of beam ABD, the following equilibrium equations can be written:

\(\Sigma F_{x}=A_{x}-F_{1} \cos 57.2648^{\circ}=0\) (a)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma F_{y}=A_{y} &-F_{1} \sin 57.2648^{\circ} \\&-(1.8 m +0.9 m +1.2 m ) w=0 (b)\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{array}{r}\Sigma M_{A}=-\left(F_{1} \sin 57.2648^{\circ}\right)(2.7 m ) \\\quad-(3.9 m ) w\left(\frac{3.9 m }{2}\right)=0 (c)\end{array}\)

 

From Eq. (c):

\(F_{1}=-\frac{0.5(3.9 m )^{2}}{(2.7 m )\left(\sin 57.2648^{\circ}\right)} w=-(3.34848 m ) w\)

Substitute \(F_{1}\) into Eq. (a) to obtain \(A_{x}:\)

\(A_{x}=F_{1} \cos 35.3112^{\circ}=(-3.3485 m )\left(\cos 57.2648^{\circ}\right) w=(-1.81071 m ) w\)

and substitute \(F_{1}\) into Eq. (b) to obtain \(A_{y}\):

\(\begin{aligned}A_{y} &=F_{1} \sin 57.2648^{\circ}+(3.9 m ) w \\&=\left[(-3.3485 m ) \sin 57.2648^{\circ}+3.9 m \right] w \\&=(1.08333 m ) w\end{aligned}\)

The resultant pin force at A is found from \(A_{x} \text { and } A_{y} \text { : }\)

\(|A|=\sqrt{A_{x}^{2}+A_{y}^{2}}=\sqrt{[(-1.81071 m ) w]^{2}+[(1.08333 m ) w]^{2}}=w \sqrt{\left(4.45230 m ^{2}\right)}=(2.11005 m ) w\)

 

Allowable w based on rod normal stress: The allowable normal stress for the rod is

\(\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\sigma_{Y}}{ FS }=\frac{340 MPa }{2.5}=136 MPa\)

The cross-sectional area of 38-mm-diameter rod (1) is

\(A_{1}=\frac{\pi}{4}(38 mm )^{2}=1,134.115 mm ^{2}\)

The allowable force in rod (1) is

\(F_{\text {allow, } 1}=\sigma_{\text {allow }} A_{1}=\left(136 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(1,134.115 mm ^{2}\right)=154,239.6 N\)

and thus, the corresponding allowable distributed load w is

\(w=\left|\frac{154,239.6 N }{-3.34848 m }\right|=46,062.6 N / m =46.063 kN / m\) (d)

 

Allowable w based on shear stress in pins B and C: The allowable shear stress for each pin is

\(\tau_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\tau_{U}}{ FS }=\frac{620 MPa }{2.5}=248 MPa\)

The cross-sectional area of a 16-mm-diameter pin is

\(A_{\text {pin } B}=\frac{\pi}{4}(16 mm )^{2}=201.062 mm ^{2}\)

Since the pins are in double shear connections, the allowable force that can be applied to each of these pins is:

\(F_{\text {allow, } 1}=\tau_{\text {allow }}\left(2 \times A_{\text {pin } B}\right)=(248 MPa )\left(2 \times 201.062 mm ^{2}\right)=99,726.8 N\)

The corresponding allowable distributed load w is

\(w=\left|\frac{99,726.8 N }{-3.34848 m }\right|=29,782.7 N / m =29.783 kN / m\) (e)

 

Allowable w based on shear stress in pin A: The cross-sectional area of a 20-mm-diameter pin is

\(A_{\text {pin } A}=\frac{\pi}{4}(20 mm )^{2}=314.159 mm ^{2}\)

Pin A is in a double shear connection; accordingly, the allowable reaction force at A is:

\(|A|_{\text {allow }}=\tau_{\text {allow }}\left(2 \times A_{\text {pin } A}\right)=(248 MPa )\left(2 \times 314.159 mm ^{2}\right)=155,822.9 N\)

The corresponding allowable distributed load w is

\(w=\left|\frac{155,822.9 N }{2.11005 m }\right|=73,847.9 N / m =73.848 kN / m\) (f)

 

Allowable w: Compare the results in (d), (e), and (f) to conclude that the allowable distributed load w is

\(w_{\text {allow }}=29.8 kN / m\)
