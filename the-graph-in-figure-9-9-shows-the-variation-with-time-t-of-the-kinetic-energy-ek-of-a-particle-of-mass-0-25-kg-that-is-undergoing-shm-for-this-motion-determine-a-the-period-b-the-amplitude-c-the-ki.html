Guest Question.

The graph in Figure 9.9 shows the variation with time t of the kinetic energy \(E_K\) of a particle of mass 0.25 kg that is undergoing SHM.
For this motion, determine:
a the period
b the amplitude
c the kinetic energy when the displacement is 0.080 m.

Exam tip
It is important to understand how to find the period from this graph.

Admin
Solution

a At t = 0 the kinetic energy is zero, meaning that the particle is at one extreme of the oscillation. It is zero again at about 0.17 s when it is at the other end. To find the period the particle has to return to its original position and that happens at 0.35 s, so T = 0.35 s.

b The angular frequency is found from \(\omega =\frac{2\pi}{T} \):

\(\omega =\frac{2\pi}{0.35}=17.95rads^{-1} \)

The maximum kinetic energy is given by \(E_{Kmax}=\frac{1}{2}m\omega ^2x_0^2 \) and so:

\(0.80=\frac{1}{2}× 0.25 × 17.95^2 × x_0^2 \)

Rearranging:

\(x_0^2=\sqrt{\frac{2 × 0.80}{0.25 × 17.95^2} } \)

 

\(x_0 ≈ 0.14 m\)

c We know the displacement so use a formula that gives kinetic energy in terms of displacement, i.e.

\(E_K=\frac{1}{2}m\omega ^2(x_0^2-X^2) \).This gives:

\(E_K=\frac{1}{2}× 0.25 × 17.95^2(0.142 − 0.0802)\)

 

\(E_K = 0.53 J\).

Exam tip
More significant figures are being used here. This is all right – this is an intermediate calculation.
