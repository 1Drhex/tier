Guest Question.

The crank and connecting rod lengths of an engine are 125 mm and 500 mm respectively. The mass of the connecting rod is 60 kg and its centre of gravity is 275 mm from the crosshead pin centre, the radius of gyration about centre of gravity being 150 mm.
If the engine speed is 600 r.p.m. for a crank position of \(45^{\circ}\) from the inner dead centre, determine, using Klien’s or any other construction 1. the acceleration of the piston; 2. the magnitude, position and direction of inertia force due to the mass of the connecting rod.

Admin
Solution

Given : r = OC = 125 mm ; l = PC = 500 mm; \(m_{ C }=60 kg ; P G=275 mm\) ; \(m_{ C }=60 kg ; P G=275 mm ; k_{ G }=150 mm ; N=600 \text { r.p.m. or } \omega=2 \pi \times 600 / 60=62.84 rad / s ; \theta=45^{\circ}\)

 

1. Acceleration of the piston

Let \(a_{ P }\) = Acceleration of the piston.

First of all, draw the configuration diagram OCP, as shown in Fig. 15.24, to some suitable scale, such that

\(O C=r=125 mm ; P C=l=500 mm ; \text { and } \theta=45^{\circ}\).

Now, draw the Klien’s acceleration diagram OCQN, as shown in Fig. 15.24, in the same manner as already discussed. By measurement,

NO = 90 mm = 0.09 m

\(\therefore \) Acceleration of the piston,

\(a_{ P }=\omega^{2} \times N O=(62.84)^{2} \times 0.09=355.4 m / s\)

 

2. The magnitude, position and direction of inertia force due to the mass of the connecting rod

The magnitude, postition and direction of the inertia force may be obtained as follows:

(i) Replace the connecting rod by dynamical equivalent system of two masses, assuming that one of the masses is placed at P and the other mass at D. The position of the point D is obtained.

(ii) Locate the points G and D on NC which is the acceleration image of the connecting rod. Let these points are g and d on NC. Join gO and dO. By measurement,

gO = 103 mm = 0.103 m

\(\therefore \) Acceleration of \(a_{ G }=\omega^{2} \times g O\) , \(a_{ G }=\omega^{2} \times g O\).

(iii) From point D, draw DE parallel to dO. Now E is the point through which the inertia force of the connecting rod passes. The magnitude of the inertia force of the connecting rod is given by

\(F_{ C }=m_{ C } \times \omega^{2} \times g O=60 \times(62.84)^{2} \times 0.103=24400 N =24.4 kN\)

(iv) From point E, draw a line parallel to gO, which shows the position of the inertia force of the connecting rod and acts in the opposite direction of gO.
