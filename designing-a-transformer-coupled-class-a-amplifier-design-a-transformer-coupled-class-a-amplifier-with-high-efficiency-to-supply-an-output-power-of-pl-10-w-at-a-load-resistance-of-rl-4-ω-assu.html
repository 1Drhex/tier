Guest Question.

Designing a transformer-coupled class A amplifier Design a transformer-coupled class A amplifier with high efficiency to supply an output power of \( P_{ L }=10 W \) at a load resistance of \( R_{ L }=4 \Omega \). Assume a DC supply voltage of 12 V and BJTs of \( \beta_{ F }=h_{ fe }=100 \text { and } V_{ CE (\text { sat })}=0.7 V \text {. Note that } h_{ fe } \) is the hybrid parameter and both \( \beta_{ f }\left(\approx \beta_{ F }\right) \text { and } h_{ fe } \) represent the current gain of a BJT.

Admin
Solution

The design steps are as follows:

Step 1. Determine the maximum collector-to-emitter voltage of the transistors:

\( V_{ CE (\max )} \geq 2 V_{ CC }=2 \times 12=24 V \)

Step 2. From Eq. (11.40), calculate the collector power dissipation, which must be at least twice the AC power:

\(F_m=\frac{V^2_{CC}/R^{\prime }_L}{V^2_{CC}/2R^{\prime }_L} =2\) (11.40)

\( P_{ C } \geq F_{ m } P_{ L }=2 P_{ L }=2 \times 10=20 W \)

Step 3. Calculate the value of the quiescent collector current \( I_{ C }\):

\( I_{ C }=\frac{P_{ C }}{V_{ CC }}=\frac{20}{12}=1.67 A \)

Step 4. Calculate the slope of the load line to find the AC load resistance \( R_{ L }^{\prime} \):

\( R_{ L }^{\prime}=\frac{V_{ CC }}{I_{ C }}=\frac{12}{1.67}=7.19 \Omega \)

Step 5. From Eq. (11.34), calculate the required turns ratio of the transformer:

\(R_{ L }^{\prime}=\left(\frac{V_{ pL }}{I_{ pL }}\right)=\left(\frac{n_{ p }}{n_{ s }}\right)^{2}\left(\frac{V_{ sL }}{I_{ sL }}\right)=\left(\frac{n_{ p }}{n_{ s }}\right)^{2} R_{ L }\) (11.34)

\( \frac{n_{ p }}{n_{ s }}=\left(\frac{R_{ L }^{\prime}}{R_{ L }}\right)^{1 / 2}=\left(\frac{7.19}{4}\right)^{1 / 2}=1.34 \)

Step 6. Calculate the peak collector current:

\( I_{ C (\max )}=2 I_{ C }=2 \times 1.67=3.34 A \)

Step 7. Calculate the quiescent base current \( I_{ B } \):

\( I_{ B }=\frac{I_{ C }}{h_{ fe }}=\frac{1.67}{100}=16.7 mA \)

Step 8. Calculate the base resistance \( R_{ B } \):

\( R_{ B }=\frac{V_{ CC }-V_{ CE ( sat )}}{I_{ B }}=\frac{12 V -0.7 V }{16.7 mA }=677 \Omega \)
