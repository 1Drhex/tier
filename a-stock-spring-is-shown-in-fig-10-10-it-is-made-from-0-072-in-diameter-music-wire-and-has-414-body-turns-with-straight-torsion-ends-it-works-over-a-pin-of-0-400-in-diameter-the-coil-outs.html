Guest Question.

A stock spring is shown in Fig. 10–10. It is made from 0.072-in-diameter music wire and has \( 4\frac{1}{4}\) body turns with straight torsion ends. It works over a pin of 0.400 in diameter.The coil outside diameter is \(\frac{19}{32}\) in.
(a) Find the maximum operating torque and corresponding rotation for static loading.
(b) Estimate the inside coil diameter and pin diametral clearance when the spring is subjected to the torque in part (a).

(c) Estimate the fatigue factor of safety \(n_f\) if the applied moment varies between \(M_{min} = 1 \ to \ M_{max} = 5 \ lbf . in\).

Admin

we find that \(A = 201 \ kpsi · in^m \ and \ m = 0.145\).
Therefore,
\(S_{ut} = \frac{A}{d^m}=\frac{201}{\left(0.072\right)^{0.145}}= 294.4 \ kpsi \)
Using Eq. (10–57)

\(S_y =\begin{cases} 0.78 \ S_{ut} & Music \ wire \ and \ cold-drawn \ carbon \ steels \\ 100 \ kpsi & OQ\&\ T \ carbon \ and \ low-alloy \ steels \\ 0.61 \ S_{ut} & Austenitic \ stainless \ steel \ and \ nonferrous \ alloys \end{cases} \)
gives
\(S_y = 0.78 S_{ut} = 0.78\left(294.4\right) = 229.6 \ kpsi\)

The mean coil diameter is \( D = {19}/{32} − 0.072 = 0.5218\) in. The spring index
\(C = {D}/{d} = {0.5218}/{0.072} = 7.247\).The bending stress-correction factor \(K_i\) from Eq. (10–43),
\( K_i=\frac{4C^2-C-1}{4C\left(C-1\right) } \ \ K_0=\frac{4C^2+C-1}{4C\left(c+1\right) } \)
is
\( K_i = \frac{4\left(7.247\right)^2 − 7.247 − 1}{4\left(7.247\right)\left(7.247 − 1\right)}= 1.115\)
Now rearrange Eq. (10–44),

\(\sigma =K_i \frac{32Fr}{\pi d^3}\)
substitute \(S_y \ for \ σ \), and solve for the maximum torque Fr to obtain
\(M_{max} = \left(Fr\right)_{max} = \frac{\pi d^3 S_y}{32K_i}= \frac{\pi \left(0.072\right)^3 229 600}{32\left(1.115\right)}= 7.546 \ lbf · in\)
Note that no factor of safety has been used. Next, from Eq. (10–54)

\(\theta' _c=\frac{10.8MDN_b}{d^4 E} \)

the number of turns of the coil body \(\theta' _c\) is
\(\theta' _c= \frac{10.8 M D N_b}{d^4 E}= \frac{10.8 \left(7.546\right)0.5218 \left(4.25\right)}{0.072^4\left(28.5\right)10^6} = 0.236 \ turn \\ \left(\theta'_c \right)_{deg} = 0.236\left(360◦\right) = 85.0◦\)

The active number of turns Na, from Eq. (10–48),
\(N_a=N_b+\frac{l_1+l_2}{3 \pi D}\)
is
\(N_a = N_b + \frac{l_1 + l_2}{3\pi D}= 4.25 +\frac{ 1 + 1}{3 \pi \left(0.5218\right)}= 4.657 \) turns
The spring rate of the complete spring, from Eq. (10–51),

\( k'=\frac{d^4 E}{10.8 D N_a}\)
is
\(k'=\frac{0.072^4 \left(28.5\right)10^6 }{10.8 \left(0.5218\right) 4.657} =29.18 \ lbf · in/turn\)

The number of turns of the complete spring \(\theta'\) is

\(\theta'=\frac{M}{K'}=\frac{7.546}{29.18}= 0.259 \ turn \\ \left(\theta'_s\right) _{deg}=0.259\left(360^\circ \right) =93.24^\circ \)

(b) With no load, the mean coil diameter of the spring is 0.5218 in. From Eq. (10–53),

\(D'=\frac{N_b D}{N_b+\theta _c'} \)

 

\(D'=\frac{N_b D}{N_b+\theta _c'} =\frac{4.25 \left(0.5218\right) }{4.25+0.236} =0.494\) in

The diametral clearance between the inside of the spring coil and the pin at load is
\( \Delta =D'-d-D_P=0.494-0.072-0.400=0.022 \) in
(c) Fatigue:
\(M_a={\left(M_{max}-M_{min}\right)} /{2}={\left(5-1\right) }/{2}=2 \ lbf.in \\ M_m={\left(M_{max}+M_{min}\right)} /{2}={\left(5+1\right) }/{2}=3 \ lbf.in \\ r=\frac{M_a}{M_m} =\frac{2}{3} \\\sigma _a=K_i\frac{32 M_a}{\pi d^3} =1.115\frac{32\left(2\right) }{\pi0.072^3} =60 857 \ psi \\ \sigma_m=\frac{M_m}{M_a} \sigma _a=\frac{3}{2} \left(60 857\right) =91 286 \ psi\)

\(S_r = 0.50S_{ut} = 0.50\left(294.4\right) = 147.2 \ kpsi\).Then
\(S_e = \frac{{147.2}/{2}}{1 −\left(\frac{{147.2}/{2}}{297.4} \right)^2}=78.51 \ kpsi\)
The amplitude component of the strength Sa, from Eq. (10–59),
\(S_a=\frac{r^2S^2_{ut}}{2S_e} \left[-1+\sqrt{1+\left(\frac{2S_e}{rS_{ut}} \right)^2 } \right] \)
is
\(S_a =\frac{\left({2}/{3}\right)^2 294.4^2 }{2\left(78.51\right) } \left[-1+\sqrt{1+\left(\frac{2}{{2}/{3}}\frac{788.51}{294.4} \right)^2 } \right] =68.85 \ kpsi\)
The fatigue factor of safety is
\(n_f = \frac{S_a}{\sigma_a}=\frac{68.85}{60.86}= 1.13\)
