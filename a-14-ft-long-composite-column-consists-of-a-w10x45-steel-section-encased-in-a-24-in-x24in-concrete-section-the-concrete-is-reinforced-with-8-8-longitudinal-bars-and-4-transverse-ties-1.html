Guest Question.

A 14 ft long composite column consists of a W10×45 steel section encased in a 24 in.×24in. concrete section. The concrete is reinforced with 8-#8 longitudinal bars and #4 transverse ties @ 12 in. o.c., as illustrated below. Determine if the member has sufficient available strength to support an axial dead load of 260 kips and an axial live load of 780 kips in compression, as well as a dead load moment of 80 k-ft and a live load moment of 240 k-ft. The load is applied directly to the concrete encasement.

The available strength of the composite section subjected to combined axial and flexural loads is determined by constructing an interaction curve. The curve is generated by calculating the available strength of the section at a series of points on the interaction curve and reducing the strength for slenderness effects and multiplying by the resistance factor for LRFD or dividing by the safety factor for ASD. The defining equations of the interaction curve were given earlier in Figure I-1a, and will be used to construct the curve illustrated in Commentary Figure C4-3, and repeated here.
