Guest Question.

For the rectifier shown below calculate the average and rms output voltage when \(\widetilde{V}_{i}=\) 200 V; 50 Hz, α= 120°, E=-120 V, and R = 1 Ω.

\(\begin{aligned} \overline{ V }_{ o } &=\frac{1}{ T } \int_{0}^{ T } v _{ o }( t ) d t =\frac{1}{\pi}\left[\int_{\alpha}^{\beta} \sqrt{2} \widetilde{ V }_{ i } \sin (\omega t ) d (\omega t )+\int_{\beta}^{\alpha+\pi} Ed (\omega t )\right] \\&=\frac{1}{\pi}\left[-\left.\sqrt{2} \widetilde{ V }_{ i } \cos \omega t \right|_{\alpha} ^{\beta}+ E \times\left.\omega t \right|_{\beta} ^{\alpha+\pi}\right] \\ &=\frac{1}{\pi}\left[-\sqrt{2} \widetilde{ V }_{ i } \cos \beta+\sqrt{2} \widetilde{ V }_{ i } \cos \alpha+ E \times(\alpha+\pi)- E \times \beta\right] \\ &=\frac{1}{\pi}\left[\sqrt{2} \widetilde{ V }_{ i }(\cos \alpha-\cos \beta)+ E \times(\alpha+\pi-\beta)\right] \end{aligned}\)

where \(\alpha=120^{\circ} \text { or } 2.09 rads\)

 

 

Admin
Solution

Solution

The waveforms of the given rectifier are shown in Fig. 5.33. From the output voltage
waveform the average output voltage is obtained: Moreover, according to Fig. 5.33, the turn-off angle β of thyristors \(Q_{1}\) and \(Q_{4}\) is located exactly at the intersection point of input voltage and load dc voltage E and is given by the following equation:

 

\(\beta=\sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{ E }{\sqrt{2} \widetilde{ V }_{ i }}\right)+\pi=\sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{120}{\sqrt{2} \times 200}\right)+\pi=205^{\circ} \text { or } 3.57 rads \)

Substituting the values of α, β, and π into the average output voltage equation
yields:

 

\(\begin{aligned} \overline{ V }_{ o } &=\frac{1}{\pi}\left[240 \sqrt{2}\left(\cos 120^{\circ}-\cos 205^{\circ}\right)-120(2.09+\pi-3.57)\right] \\ &=\frac{1}{\pi}[137.9-199.2]=-19.52 V \end{aligned}\)

 

The rms output voltage is evaluated as follows:

 

\(\begin{aligned} &\vec{V}_{o}=\left[\frac{1}{T} \int_{0}^{T} v_{o}^{2}(t) d t\right]^{1 / 2}=\left[\frac{1}{\pi}\left[\int_{\alpha}^{\beta}\left(\sqrt{2} \bar{V}_{i} \sin \omega t\right)^{2} d(\omega t)+\int_{\beta}^{\pi+\alpha} E^{2} d(\omega t)\right]\right]^{1 / 2}\\ &=\left[\frac{1}{\pi}\left[\int_{\alpha}^{\beta} 2 \widetilde{V}_{i}^{2}\left(\frac{1-\cos 2 \omega t}{2}\right) d (\omega t)+\int_{\beta}^{\pi+\alpha} E ^{2} d (\omega t)\right]\right]^{1 / 2}\\ &=\left[\frac{1}{\pi}\left[\int_{\alpha}^{\beta} \vec{V}_{i}^{2} d(\omega t)-\int_{\alpha}^{\beta} \tilde{V}_{i}^{2}(-\cos 2 \omega t) d(\omega t)+\int_{\beta}^{\pi+\alpha} E^{2} d(\omega t)\right]\right]^{1 / 2}\\ &=\left[\frac{1}{\pi}\left[\vec{V}_{i}^{2}(\beta-\alpha)+\frac{\vec{V}_{i}^{2}}{2}(\sin 2 \beta-\sin 2 \alpha)+\bar{E}^{2} \times((\pi+\alpha)-\beta)\right]\right]^{1 / 2} \end{aligned}\)

 

Substituting the parameters with their numerical values, the rms output voltage is
found to be 140.7 V. The values of α, β, and π are in rads.
