Guest Question.

Let the mean-zero, Gaussian stress process \( \left\{X(t)\right\} \) have an autospectral density function of \( S_{XX}(\omega )=S_{0}U(\omega _{0}-\left|\omega \right| ) \) .For a specimen with \( m = 4 \) , compare the fatigue lives predicted by the Rayleigh approximation, the peak-approximation method, the single-moment method, the formula of Ortiz and Chen, and the formula of Wirsching and Light.

Admin
Solution

For this spectral density, we find that the spectral moments are given by

\( \lambda _{j}=2S_{0}(\omega _{0})^{j+1}/(j+1) \)

From Eqs. 11.50 \(E(\Delta D)=K^{-1}(2)^{3m/2}\lambda _{0}^{m/2}\Gamma \left(1+\frac{m}{2} \right) \) and 11.51 \( E(T)= \frac{2\pi \sigma _{X}}{E(\Delta D)\sigma _{X}} =\frac{2\pi }{E(\Delta D)}\left(\frac{\lambda _{0}}{\lambda _{2}} \right) ^{1/2}\) we obtain the Rayleigh approximation as

\( E(T_{Ray})=2\pi \left(\frac{\lambda _{0}}{\lambda _{2}} \right)^{1/2} \frac{K}{2^{6}\lambda ^{2}_{0}(2)} =\frac{\pi K}{64\lambda ^{3/2}_{0}\lambda ^{1/2}_{2}} =\frac{3^{1/2}\pi K}{256S^{2}_{0}\omega ^{3}_{0}} \approx \frac{K}{47.05S^{2}_{0}\omega ^{3}_{0}} \)

The irregularity factor is \( IF=\lambda _{2}/(\lambda _{0}\lambda _{4})^{1/2}=5^{1/2}/3 \) , and Eqs. 11.53 \( E[P^{m}(t) \mid P(t)\geq 0]\approx (2)^{m/2}\sigma _{X}^{m}\left(1+\frac{IF(1-IF)}{4} \right)^{(-1+m/2)} \frac{\Gamma \left(\frac{m+1+IF}{2} \right) }{\Gamma \left(\frac{1+IF}{2} \right) } \) and 11.54 \( E(T)=\frac{2\pi K}{2^{m}E[P^{m}(t)\mid P(t)\geq 0]} \frac{2(IF)}{(1+IF)} \frac{\sigma _{X}}{\sigma _{\dot{X} }} = \frac{4\pi K(IF)}{2^{m}(1+IF)E[P^{m}(t) \mid P(t)\geq 0]} \left(\frac{\lambda _{0}}{\lambda _{2}} \right) ^{1/2}\) then give the result of the peak-approximation method as

\( E(T_{peak})\approx K/(47.15S^{2}_{0}\omega ^{3}_{0}) \)

Similarly, Eqs. 11.58 \(CF=a+(1-a)\left(1-[1-(IF)^{2}]^{1/2}\right) ^{b}\) and 11.59 \(a=0.926-0.033m\), \(b=1.587m-2.323\) give \( a\approx 0.794,b\approx 4.025 \) , and \( RR=(CF)^{-1}\approx 1.256 \) To evaluate the single-moment approximation, we first find \( \lambda _{2/m}=\lambda _{1/2}=4S_{0}(\omega _{0})^{3/2}/3 \) ; Eq. 11.55 \( E(T)=\frac{2\pi K}{(2)^{3m/2}\Gamma \left(1+\frac{m}{2} \right)(\lambda _{2/m})^{m/2} } \) then gives

\( E(T_{sm})\approx K/(36.22S^{2}_{0}\omega ^{3}_{0}) \)

Finally, we also evaluate \( \lambda _{2+2/m}=\lambda _{5/2}=4S_{0}(\omega _{0})^{7/2}/7 \) , and Eq. 11.56 \( E(T)=\frac{2\pi K (\lambda _{2+2/m})^{m/2}}{(2)^{3m/2}\Gamma \left(1+\frac{m}{2} \right)(\lambda _{2/m})^{m/2} \lambda _{2}^{(m-1)}\lambda _{4}^{1/2}} \) gives the Ortiz and Chen approximation as

\( E(T_{oc})\approx K/(38.18S^{2}_{0}\omega ^{3}_{0}) \)

Comparing the results, we see that the peak-approximation method predicts a fatigue life that is 0.2% smaller than the Rayleigh approximation. The single-moment method, the approximation of Ortiz and Chen, and the Wirsching and Light correction factor all predict a fatigue life that is greater than the Rayleigh prediction, with the increases being 30%, 23%, and 26%, respectively. The spectral density used here is of a type considered by Wirsching and Light in developing their empirical correction factor from rainflow simulation data, so their formula should be expected to be quite accurate for this problem. Thus, it is safe to conclude that the Rayleigh and peak-approximation approximations significantly underpredict the failure time for this problem, as compared with the rainflow method.
