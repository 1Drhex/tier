Guest Question.

Figure P17.113/114 shows a compound steel [E = 200 GPa] rod with a diameter of 20 mm in segments AB and DE and a diameter of 35 mm in segments BC and CD. For the given loading, calculate the deflection of the compound rod at C using Castigliano’s second theorem.

Admin
Solution

To determine the deflection of the simply-supported beam, first apply a dummy concentrated load P downward at C. The beam free-body diagram for this loading is shown.

 

 

 

Draw a free-body diagram that cuts through the compound rod at section a–a and derive a moment equation M from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{a-a} &=-\left(7,800 N +\frac{P}{2}\right) x_{1}+M=0 \\& \therefore M=\left(7,800 N +\frac{P}{2}\right) x_{1} \quad 0 \leq x_{1}<100 mm\end{aligned}\)

Differentiate this expression to obtain ∂M/∂P.

\(\frac{\partial M}{\partial P}=0.5 x_{1}\)

Substitute P = 0 into the bending-moment equation to obtain

\(M=(7,800 N ) x_{1}\)

 

Cut a second free-body diagram through the compound rod at section b–b, and derive a second moment equation M.

\(\begin{array}{rl}\Sigma M_{b-b} & =-\left(7,800 N +\frac{P}{2}\right) x_{2}+\frac{(120 N / mm )}{2}\left(x_{2}-100 mm \right)^{2}+M=0 \\& \therefore M=-\frac{(120 N / mm )}{2}\left(x_{2}-100 mm \right)^{2}+\left(7,800 N +\frac{P}{2}\right) x_{2} & 100 mm \leq x_{2}<230 mm\end{array}\)

Differentiate this expression to obtain ∂M/∂P.

\(\frac{\partial M}{\partial P}=0.5 x_{2}\)

Substitute P = 0 into the bending-moment equation to obtain

\(M=-\frac{(120 N / mm )}{2}\left(x_{2}-100 mm \right)^{2}+(7,800 N ) x_{2}\)

 

Cut a third free-body diagram through the compound rod at section c–c, and derive a third moment equation M.

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma M_{c-c}=\left(7,800 N +\frac{P}{2}\right) x_{3}-M=0 \\&\therefore M=\left(7,800 N +\frac{P}{2}\right) x_{3} \quad 0 mm \leq x_{3}<100 mm\end{aligned}\)

Differentiate this expression to obtain ∂M/∂P.

\(\frac{\partial M}{\partial P}=0.5 x_{3}\)

Substitute P = 0 into the bending-moment equation to obtain

\(M=(7,800 N ) x_{3}\)

Moments of Inertia: The moment of inertia for 20-mm-diameter segments AB and DE is:

\(I_{A B}=I_{D E}=\frac{\pi}{64}(20 mm )^{4}=7,853.98 mm ^{4}\)

and the moment of inertia for 35-mm-diameter segments BC and CD is:

\(I_{B C}=I_{C D}=\frac{\pi}{64}(35 mm )^{4}=73,661.76 mm ^{4}\)

Castigliano’s second theorem applied to beam deflections can be expressed by Equation (17.40).

\(\Delta=\int_{0}^{L}\left(\frac{\partial M}{\partial P}\right) \frac{M}{E I} d x\)

Since the loading is symmetrical on the beam, the integrals will be written for the interval [0,165] and then doubled. The beam deflection at C can now be determined.

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta_{C} &=2 \int_{0}^{100}\left(0.5 x_{1}\right)\left(\frac{7,800}{E I_{A B}} x_{1}\right) d x_{1}+2 \int_{100}^{165}\left(0.5 x_{2}\right)\left(-\frac{60}{E I_{B C}}\left(x_{2}-100\right)^{2}+\frac{7,800}{E I_{B C}} x_{2}\right) d x_{2} \\&=\frac{7,800}{E I_{A B}} \int_{0}^{100} x_{1}^{2} d x_{1}-\frac{1}{E I_{B C}} \int_{100}^{165} x_{2}\left(60\left(x_{2}-100\right)^{2}-7,800 x_{2}\right) d x_{2} \\&=\frac{7,800}{E I_{A B}} \int_{0}^{100} x_{1}^{2} d x_{1}-\frac{1}{E I_{B C}} \int_{100}^{165} x_{2}\left(60\left(x_{2}-100\right)^{2}-7,800 x_{2}\right) d x_{2} \\&=\frac{7,800}{E I_{A B}} \int_{0}^{100} x_{1}^{2} d x_{1}-\frac{60}{E I_{B C}} \int_{100}^{165} x_{2}\left(\left(x_{2}-100\right)^{2}\right) d x_{2}+\frac{7,800}{E I_{B C}} \int_{100}^{165} x_{2}^{2} d x_{2} \\&=\frac{7,800}{E I_{A B}} \frac{(100)^{3}}{3}-\frac{60}{E I_{B C}}\left[\frac{(65)^{4}}{4}+\frac{(100)(65)^{3}}{3}\right]+\frac{7,800}{E I_{B C}} \frac{(165)^{3}-(100)^{3}}{3} \\&=\frac{2,600 \times 10^{6}}{E I_{A B}}-\frac{817.009 \times 10^{6}}{E I_{B C}}+\frac{9,079.525 \times 10^{6}}{E I_{B C}} \\&=\frac{2,600 \times 10^{6}}{(200,000)(7,853.98)}+\frac{8,262.516 \times 10^{6}}{(200,000)(73,661.76)} \\&=1.655212+0.560842=2.216053 \\& \therefore \Delta_{C}=2.22 mm \downarrow\end{aligned}\)
