Guest Question.

A FUSION REACTION

Two deuterons fuse to form a triton (a nucleus of tritium, or \({ }^{3} \mathrm{H}\)) and a proton. How much energy is liberated?

Admin
Solution

IDENTIFY and SET UP:

This is a nuclear reaction of the type discussed in Section 43.6. We use Eq. (43.23) to find the energy released.

\(Q=\left(M_{A}+M_{B}-M_{C}-M_{D}\right) c^{2}\) (43.23)
EXECUTE:

Adding one electron to each nucleus makes each a neutral atom; we find their masses in Table 43.2.

Substituting into Eq. (43.23), we find

\(\begin{aligned}Q=&[2(2.014102 \mathrm{u})-3.016049 \mathrm{u}-1.007825 \mathrm{u}] \\&\times(931.5 \mathrm{MeV} / \mathrm{u})=4.03 \mathrm{MeV}\end{aligned}\)

 

EVALUATE: Thus 4.03 MeV is released in the reaction; the triton and proton together have 4.03 MeV more kinetic energy than the two deuterons had together.
