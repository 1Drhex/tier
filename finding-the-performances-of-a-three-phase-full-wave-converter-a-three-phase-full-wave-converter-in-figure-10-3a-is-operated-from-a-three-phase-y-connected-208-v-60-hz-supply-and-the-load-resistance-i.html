Finding the Performances of a Three-Phase Full-Wave Converter

A three-phase full-wave converter in Figure 10.3a is operated from a three-phase Y-connected \(208-V, 60-Hz\) supply and the load resistance is \(R = 10 Ω\). If it is required to obtain an average output voltage of 50% of the maximum possible output voltage, calculate (a) the delay angle α, (b) the rms and average output currents, (c) the average and rms thyristor currents, (d) the rectification efficiency, (e) the TUF, and (f) the input PF.

Admin
Solution

The phase voltage \(V_s = 208/\sqrt{3} = 120.1 V, V_m = \sqrt{2} V_s = 169.83, V_n = 0.5, and R = 10 Ω\). The maximum output voltage \(V_{dm} = 3\sqrt{3} V_m/π = 3\sqrt{3}× 169.83/π = 280.9 V\). The average output voltage \(V_{dc} = 0.5 × 280.9 = 140.45 V\).

a. From Eq. (10.16),

\(V_n=\frac{V_{dc}}{V_{dm}}=\cos \alpha\) (10.16)

\(0.5 = \cos α\), and the delay angle \(α = 60°\).

b. The average output current \(I_{dc} = V_{dc}/R = 140.45/10 = 14.05 A\). From Eq. (10.17),

\( V_{rms} =\sqrt{3}V_m\left(\frac{1}{2}+\frac{3\sqrt{3} }{4\pi }\cos 2\alpha \right)^{1/2}\) (10.17)

\(V_m=\sqrt{3}\times 169.83 \left[\frac{1}{2}+\frac{3\sqrt{3} }{4\pi }\cos(2\times 60°) \right]^{1/2}=159.29 V\)

and the rms current \(I_{rms} = 159.29/10 = 15.93 A\).

c. The average current of a thyristor \(I_A = I_{dc}/3 = 14.05/3 = 4.68 A\), and the rms current of a thyristor \(I_R = I_{rms}\sqrt{2/6} = 15.93\sqrt{2/6} = 9.2 A\).

d. From Eq. (3.3) the rectification efficiency is

\(\eta =\frac{P_{dc}}{P_{ac}} \) (3.3)

\(\eta =\frac{V_{dcI_{dc}}}{V_{rms}I_{rms}}=\frac{140.45 \times 14.05}{159.29 \times 15.93}=0.778\) or \(77.8\%\)

e. The rms input line current \(I_s = I_{rms}\sqrt{4/6} = 13\) A and the input VAR rating \(V_I = 3V_s I_s = 3 × 120.1 × 13 = 4683.9 VA\). From Eq. (3.8), \(TUF = V_{dc} I_{dc}/VI = 140.45 × 14.05/4683.9 = 0.421\).

\(TUF =\frac{P_{dc}}{V_sI_s} \) (3.8)

f. The output power \(P_o = I_{rms}^2 R = 15.932 × 10 = 2537.6 W\). The \(PF = P_o/VI = 2537.6/ 4683.9 = 0.542\) (lagging).

Note: The PF is less than that of three-phase semiconverters, but higher than that of three-phase half-wave converters.
