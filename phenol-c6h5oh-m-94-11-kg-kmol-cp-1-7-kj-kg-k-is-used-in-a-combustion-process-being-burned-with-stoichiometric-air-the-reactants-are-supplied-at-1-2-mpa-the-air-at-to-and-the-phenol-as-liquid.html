Guest Question.

Phenol \(C _{6} H _{5} OH\) (M = 94.11 kg/kmol, \(\text { Cp }\) = 1.7 kJ/kg-K) is used in a combustion process being burned with stoichiometric air. The reactants are supplied at 1.2 MPa, the air at \(T _{ o }\) and the phenol as liquid at 100째C. Enough heat transfer \(Q _{1}\), takes place after the combustion so the products reach 1500 K. After the heat transfer the products are sent through a turbine where they expand to 120 kPa.
a) Find the heat transfer \(Q _{1}\) [kJ/kmol fuel].
b) Find the enthalpy of formation of the fuel as vapor.
c) Find the specific turbine work output W [kJ/kmol fuel].

Admin
Solution

First do the combustion equation for Phenol \(C _{6} H _{5} OH\)

\(C _{6} H _{5} OH + v _{ O _{2}}\left( O _{2}+3.76 N _{2}\right) \rightarrow 6 CO _{2}+3 H _{2} O +26.32 N _{2}\)

O balance: \(1+2 v_{ O _{2}}=6 \times 2+3=15 \Rightarrow v_{ O _{2}}=7\)

\(N _{2}\) balance: \(v_{ N _{2}}=3.76 v _{ O _{2}}=3.76 \times 7=26.32\)

Energy Eq.: \(0= Q _{1}+ H _{ R 1}- H _{ P 2}= Q _{1}- H _{ RP }^{\circ}+\Delta H _{ R 1}-\Delta H _{ P 2}\)

Table 13.3:

\(\begin{aligned}&H _{ RP }^{\circ}=94.11(-31117)=-2928421 kJ / kmol , \text { (fuel as liquid) }\\&\Delta H _{ R 1}= M Cp \left( T _{1}- T _{0}\right)=94.11 \times 1.7 \times(100-25)=11999 kJ / kmol\end{aligned}\)

Table A.9:

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta H _{ P 2} &=6 \Delta \bar{ h }_{ CO 2}+3 \Delta \bar{ h }_{ H 2 O }+26.32 \Delta \bar{ h }_{ N 2} \\&=6 \times 61705+3 \times 481549+26.32 \times 38405=1525497 kJ / kmol\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}Q _{1} &= H _{ RP }^{\circ}-\Delta H _{ R 1}+\Delta H _{ P 2}=-2928421-11999+1525497 \\&=-1414923 kJ / kmol\end{aligned}\)

 

\(H _{ RP }^{\circ}=94.11(-31774)=-2990251=6 \overline{ h }_{ f CO _{2}}^{\circ}+3 \overline{ h }_{ f H _{2} O }^{\circ}-\overline{ h }_{ f \text { fuel }}^{\circ} \)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\overline{ h }_{ f \text { fuel }}^{\circ} &=3 \overline{ h }_{ fH _{2} O }^{\circ}+6 \overline{ h }_{ f CO _{2}}^{\circ}- H _{ RP }^{\circ} \\&=3(-241826)+6(-393522)-(-2990251)=-96 3 5 9 k J / k m o l\end{aligned}\)

 

For \(W _{ T }\) we need exit T and as product is mainly \(N _{2}\) use air approximation (same k):

Constant \(C _{ P }\), k: \(T _{3}= T _{2}\left( P _{3} / P _{2}\right)^{\frac{ k -1}{ k }}=1500(120 / 1200)^{0.2857}=776.95 K\)

Variable \(C _{ P }\), k: \(P _{ r 3}= P _{ r 2}\left( P _{3} / P _{2}\right)=483.16 / 10=48.316 \quad \Rightarrow \quad T _{3}= 8 5 0 ~ K\)

See Table A.7.2 for this or use the standard entropy for this calculation

\(\begin{aligned}&\Delta H _{ P 3}=6 \times 25418+3 \times 19969.5+26.32 \times 16634.5=650237 kJ / kmol \\& W =\Delta H _{ P 2}-\Delta H _{ P 3}=1525497-650237=875260 kJ / kmol\end{aligned}\)
