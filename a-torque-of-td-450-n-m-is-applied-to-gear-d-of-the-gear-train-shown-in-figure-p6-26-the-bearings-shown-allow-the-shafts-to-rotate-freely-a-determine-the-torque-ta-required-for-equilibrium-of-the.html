Guest Question.

A torque of \(T_{D}\) = 450 N-m is applied to gear D of the gear train shown in Figure P6.26. The bearings shown allow the shafts to rotate freely.
(a) Determine the torque \(T_{A}\) required for equilibrium of the system.
(b) Assume shafts (1) and (2) are solid 30-mmdiameter steel shafts. Determine the magnitude of the maximum shear stresses acting in each shaft.
(c) Assume shafts (1) and (2) are solid steel shafts, which have an allowable shear stress of 60 MPa. Determine the minimum diameter required for each shaft.

Admin
Solution

(a) Torque \(T _{A}\) required for equilibrium:

\(T_{A}=T_{D} \frac{90 mm }{150 mm }=(450 N – m ) \frac{90 mm }{150 mm }=270 N – m\)

(b) Shear stress magnitudes if shafts are solid 30-mm-diameter:

\(\begin{aligned}&J=\frac{\pi}{32} d^{4}=\frac{\pi}{32}(30 mm )^{4}=79,521.564 mm ^{4} \\&\tau_{1}=\frac{T_{1} c_{1}}{J_{1}}=\frac{(270 N – m )(30 mm / 2)(1,000 mm / m )}{79,521.564 mm ^{4}}=50.930 MPa =50.9 MPa \\&\tau_{2}=\frac{T_{2} c_{2}}{I}=\frac{(450 N – m )(30 mm / 2)(1,000 mm / m )}{79521564 mm ^{4}}=84.883 MPa =84.9 MPa\end{aligned}\)

(c) Determine minimum diameters: The elastic torsion formula can be rearranged and rewritten in terms of the unknown diameter d:

\(\tau=\frac{T c}{J} \quad \therefore \frac{J}{c}=\frac{\frac{\pi}{32} d^{4}}{d / 2}=\frac{\pi}{16} d^{3}=\frac{T}{\tau}\)

Using this expression, solve for the minimum acceptable diameter of each shaft:

\(\begin{aligned}&d_{1}^{3} \geq \frac{16}{\pi} \frac{T_{1}}{\tau_{\text {allow }}}=\frac{16(270 N – m )(1,000 mm / m )}{\pi\left(60 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=22,918.312 mm ^{3} \\&\therefore d_{1} \geq 28.405 mm =28.4 mm\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&d_{2}^{3} \geq \frac{16}{\pi} \frac{T_{2}}{\tau_{\text {allow }}}=\frac{16(450 N – m )(1,000 mm / m )}{\pi\left(60 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=38,197.186 mm ^{3} \\&\therefore d_{2} \geq 33.678 mm =33.7 mm\end{aligned}\)
