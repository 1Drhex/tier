Guest Question.

Determine the required negative reinforcement at the exterior face of the first interior support for the beam along column line B of and (see Fig. 3.3). Assume \(f '_{c} = 4,000\) psi, a 3/4-in maximum aggregate, and Grade 60 reinforcement.

Admin
Solution

The height and the width of the beams are 20.5 and 22.0 in, respectively, which were determined in . For a negative bending moment, the reinforcement is located within the top portion of the beam. The flowchart shown in Fig. 6.4 is utilized to determine \(A_{s}\).

Step 1: Assume tension-controlled section. Sections of flexural members should be tension-controlled whenever possible. Thus, assume that the strength reduction factor φ = 0.9.

Step 2: Determine the nominal strength coefficient of resistance \(R_{n}\). For a rectangular section, \(R_{n}\) is determined by Eq. (6.5), which is a function of the factored bending moment Mu. The negative factored bending moment at the exterior face of the first interior support in the end span is equal to 198.1 ft kips (see Table 3.3). Also assume that d = h − 2.5 = 20.5 − 2.5 = 18.0 in

\(R_{n} = \frac{M_{u}}{φb_{w}d^{2}} = \frac{198.1 × 12,000}{0.9 × 22.0 × 18.02} = 370.6\) psi

Step 3: Determine the required reinforcement ratio ρ. The reinforcement ratio ρ is determined by Eq. (6.7):

\(\rho = \frac{0.85 f '_{c}}{f_{y}}[1 −\sqrt{1 −\frac{ 2 R _ {n} }{0.85 f '_{c}}}]= \frac{0.85 × 4}{60} [1 −\sqrt{1 −\frac{ 2 × 370.6}{0.85 × 4,000}}]= 0.0066\)

Step 4: Determine the required area of tension reinforcement \(A_{s}\).

\(A_{s} = ρbd = 0.0066 × 22.0 × 18.0 = 2.61 in_{2}\)

Step 5: Determine the minimum required area of reinforcement \( A_{s,min}\).The minimum amount
of reinforcement is determined by ACI 10.5.1. Because the compressive strength of the concrete is less than 4,400 psi, the minimum amount is determined by the lower limit given in that section:

\(A_{s,min} = \frac{200b_{w}d}{f_{y}}= {200 × 22.0 × 18.0}{60,000} = 1.32 in^{2} < 2.61 in^{2}\)

Use \( A_{s} = 2.61 in^{2}\).
Step 6: Determine the depth of the equivalent rectangular stress block a.

\(a = \frac{A_{s} f_{y}}{0.85 f '_{c} b} =\frac{ 2.61 × 60,000}{0.85 × 4,000 × 22.0} = 2.1\) in

Step 7: Determine \(β_{1}\). According to ACI 10.2.73, \(β_{1} = 0.85\) for \(f '_{c} = 4,000\) psi (see Section 5.2).

Step 8: Determine the neutral axis depth c.

\(c = \frac{a}{β_{1}}= \frac{2.1}{0.85}= 2.5 in\)

Step 9: Determine \(ε_{t}\).

\(\varepsilon_{\mathrm{t}}=0.0030\left(\frac{d_{\mathrm{f}}}{c}-1\right)=0.0030\left(\frac{18.0}{2.5}-1\right)=0.0186>0.0040\)

Because \(ε_{t} > 0.0040\), the maximum reinforcement requirement of ACI 10.3.5 is satisfied. Also, the section is tension-controlled because \(ε_{t} > 0.0050\) (ACI 10.3.4), and the initial assumption that the section is tension-controlled is correct.

Step 10: Choose the size and spacing of the reinforcing bars. The required area of reinforcement is \(2.61 in^{2}\). Try six No. 6 bars (provided \(A_{s} = 2.64 in^{2})\).

Bar spacing is governed by the crack control requirements of ACI 10.6.4 and the spacing limits of ACI 7.6 and 3.3.2. From Table 6.2, three No. 6 bars are required in a single layer for crack control for a 22-in-wide beam. From Table 6.3, 10 No. 6 bars can fit within a 22-in-wide beam without violating the spacing limits of ACI 7.6 and 3.3.2. Providing six No. 6 bars automatically satisfies the requirements of ACI 10.6.4, 7.6, and 3.3.2.

As an exercise, assume that a No. 3 stirrup (\(d_{s} = 0.375\) in) is provided in the beam, and determine the minimum and maximum numbers of No. 6 bars that can be provided in the section.

The maximum bar spacing is determined by Eq. (6.23) where \(c_{c} = 1.5 + 0.375 = 1.875\) in:

\(s = 15(\frac{40,000}{fs})− 2.5cc = 15(\frac{40,000}{2 ×60,000/3}) − (2.5 × 1.875) = 10.3 in (governs) ≤ 12 (\frac {40,000}{f_{s}}) = 12(\frac{ 40,000}{2 × 60,000/3})= 12.0 \) in

The minimum number of bars \(n_{min}\) required in a single layer to control cracking is determined by Eq. (6.24):

\(\begin{aligned} n_{\min } &=\frac{b_{w}-2\left(c_{c}+0.5 d_{b}\right)}{s}+1 \\ &=\frac{22-2[1.875+(0.5 \times 0.75)]}{10.3}+1=2.7 \end{aligned}\)

Thus, the minimum number of No. 6 bars required in the section is three. From Fig. 6.10,

Minimum clear space \(=\begin{cases} 1.0 in \\d_{b} = 0.75 in \\ 1.33 (maximum aggregate size) = 1.33 × 0.75 = 1.0 in\end {cases}\)

Therefore, the minimum clear space is 1.0 in.
The maximum number of bars \(n_{max}\) that can fit in a single layer on the basis of the spacing limits of ACI 7.6 and 3.3.2 is determined by Eq. (6.25):

\(n_{max} =\frac{ b_{w} − 2(c_{s} + d_{s} + r)}{(clear space) + d_{b}}+ 1 =\frac{ 22 − 2[1.5 + 0.375 + (2 × 0.375)]}{1.0 + 0.75 }+ 1 = 10.6\)

Thus, the maximum number of No. 6 bars permitted in the section is 10.
