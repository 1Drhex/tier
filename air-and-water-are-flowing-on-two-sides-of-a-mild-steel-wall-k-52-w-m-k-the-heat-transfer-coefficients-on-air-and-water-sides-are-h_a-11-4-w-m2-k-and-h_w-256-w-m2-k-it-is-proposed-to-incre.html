Guest Question.

Air and water are flowing on two sides of a mild steel wall (k = 52 W/m.K). The heat transfer coefficients on air and water sides are, \(h_a = 11.4 W/m^2.K \)and \(h_w = 256 W/m^2.K.\) It is proposed to increase the heat transfer rate by adding rectangular mild steel fins of the following features :
Fin thickness = 0.13 cm, Fin height = 2.5 cm,
Fin spacing = 1.3 cm.
What percentage increase in the heat transfer rate can be realised by adding fins on
(i) Air side only.
(ii) Water side only.
(iii) Fins on both sides.

 

Admin
Solution

Given : The mild steel wall with proposed finite
long rectangular fins
\(k = 52 W/m.K, h_a = 11.4 W/m^2.K,\)
\(h_w = 256 W/m^2.K, t = 0.13 cm,\)
L = 2.5 cm, S = 1.3 cm.
To find : The percentage increase in the heat transfer rate, when
(i) Fins are added on air side.
(ii) Fins are added on water side.
(iii) Fins are added on both sides.
Assumptions :
1. Steady state conditions.
2. Negligible thermal resistance offered by wall thickness.
3. No radiation from fins.
4. No internal heat generation.
5. Wall size 1 m × 1 m or \(A_w = 1 m^2.\)
Analysis : The heat transfer rate through mild steel wall, without fins
\(Q_{no fin} = \frac{∆T}{\frac{1}{h_a A _w} + \frac{1}{h_w A _w}}\)

\(= \frac{∆T}{\frac{1}{11.4 × 1} + \frac{1}{256 × 1}}= 10.91 ∆T\)
For fins and finned surface :
\(A_c= w × t\)
\(= 1 × 0.13 × 10^{–2}\)
\(= 1.3 × 10^{–3} m^2\)
P = 2w = 2 m,
\(L_c = L + (t/2)\)
= 2.5 + (0.13/2) = 2.565 cm.
The number of fins/m,
\(N_{fin} = \frac{1 m}{S} = \frac{100 cm}{1.3 cm} = 77 fins/m\)
Fins surface area,
\(A_{fin} =N_{fin}PL_c\)
\(= 77 × 2 × 2.565 × 10^{–2}\)
\(= 3.95 m^2/m^2\) of wall
Unfinned (bare) area,
\(A_{unfin} =1 m^2 – N_{fin} A_c\)
\(= 1 – 77 × 1.3 × 10^{–3}\)
\(= 0.9 m^2/m^2\) of wall area
(i) Heat transfer rate with fins on air side.
\(m_a = \sqrt{\frac{hP}{kA_c}}= \sqrt{\frac{11.4 × 2}{52 × 0.13 × 10^{-2}}}\)
\(= 18.37 m^{–1}\)
\(η_{fin, a} = \frac{tanh (m_a L_c ) }{m_a L_c }\)

\(= \frac{tanh (18.37 × 2.565 × 10^{-2} )}{18.37 × 2.565 × 10^{-2}} = 0.932\)
Since two resistances act parallel on air side,hence equivalent resistance,
\(\frac{1}{R_{a finned }}= \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2}\)

\(= h_a A_{unfin} + h_a η_{fin, a} A_{fin}\)
or \(R_{a finned} = \frac{1}{h_a ( A_{unfin} + η_{fin, a} A_{fin})} \)
Then
\(Q_{fin, a} = \frac{∆T}{R_{water} + R_{a finned}}\)
\(= \frac{∆T}{\frac{1}{h_w A_w} +\frac{1}{h_a(A_{unfin} + η_{fin,a}A_{fin})}} \)

\(= \frac{∆T}{\frac{1}{256 ×1} +\frac{1}{11.4×(0.9+ 0.932 × 3.95)}}\)
= 43.38 ∆T
The percentage increase in heat transfer rate with fins on air side
\(= \frac{Q_{fin,a} – Q_{no fin} }{Q_{no fin}} × 100\)
\(= \frac{43.38 – 10.91}{10.91} × 100\)

= 298%.
(ii) The heat transfer with fins on water side (two parallel resistances on water side)
\(m_w = \sqrt{\frac{h_wP}{kA_c}} – \sqrt{\frac{256 × 2}{52 × 0.13 × 10^{-3}}}\)

\(= 87.03 m^{–1}\)
\(η_{fin, w} = \frac{tanh (m_w L_c ) }{m_w L_c }\)
\(= \frac{tanh (87.03 × 2.565 × 10^{-3} )}{87.03 × 2.565 × 10^{-2}}\)

= 0.438
\(Q_{fin, w} = \frac{∆T}{R_{air} + R_{w finned}}\)
\(= \frac{∆T}{\frac{1}{h_a A_w} +\frac{1}{h_w(A_{unfin} + η_{fin,w}A_{fin})} }\)

= 11.21 ∆T
The percentage increase in heat transfer
\(= \frac{Q_{fin,w} – Q_{w} }{Q_{w}} × 100\)
\(= \frac{11.21 – 10.91}{10.91} × 100\)

= 2.75%.
(iii) Heat transfer with fins on both sides
\(Q_{fin, both}= \frac {∆T}{\frac {1}{h_a(A_{unfin} + η_{fin,a}A_{fin})}}+ \frac{1}{h_w(A_{unfin} + η_{fin,w}A_{fin})}\)

\(Q_{fin, both}=\frac{∆T}{\frac{1}{11.4 ×(0.9 + 0.932 × 3.95)}}+ \frac{1}{256 × (0.9 + 0.438 × 3.95)}\)

= 48.46 ∆T
The percentage increase in heat transfer
\(= \frac{48.46 – 10.91}{10.91} ×100 = 345%.\)

Comment :
(i) The addition of fins on air side is very effective due to its low value of heat transfer coefficient.
(ii) The addition of fins on water sides is not effective thus cannot be justified.
(iii) The addition of fins on both sides has only marginal effect due to addition of material resistance on both sides. It is not practicable.
