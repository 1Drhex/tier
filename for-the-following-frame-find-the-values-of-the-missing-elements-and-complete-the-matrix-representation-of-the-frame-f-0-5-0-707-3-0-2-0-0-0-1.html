Guest Question.

For the following frame, find the values of the missing elements and complete the matrix representation of the frame:

\(F= \left[\begin{matrix} ? & 0 & ? & 5 \\ 0.707 & ? & ?&3\\ ?& ? & 0& 2\\ 0& 0& 0& 1\end{matrix} \right ]\)
Admin
Solution

Obviously, 5,3,2 represent the position of the origin of the frame and do not affect the constraint equations. Notice that only three values for directional vectors are given. This is all that is needed. Using Eq. (2.10), we get:

These constraints translate into the following six constraint equations:

1) n . o = 0 the dot product of n and o vectors must be zero
2) n . a=0
3) a . o=0
4) \(\left|n\right| = 1\) the magnitude of the lenght of the vector must be 1 (2.10)
5) \(\left|o\right| =1\)
6) \(\left|a\right| =1\)

\(n_{x} o_{x} +n_{y} o_{y} +n_{z} o_{z} =0 or n_{x}(0) + 0.707(o_{y}) +n_{z}(o_{z})=0\)

 

\(n_{x} a_{x} +n_{y} a_{y} +n_{z} a_{z} =0 or n_{x}(a_{x}) + 0.707(a_{y}) +n_{z}(0)=0\)

 

\(a_{x} o_{x} +a_{y} o_{y} +a_{z} o_{z} =0 or a_{x}(0) + a_{y}(o_{y}) +0(o_{z})=0\)

 

\(n^{2}_{x} +n^{2}_{y}+n^{2}_{z}=1 or n^{2}_{x}+0.707^{2} +n^{2}_{z}=1\)

 

\(o^{2}_{x} +o^{2}_{y}+o^{2}_{z}=1 or 0^{2} +o^{2}_{y} +o^{2}_{z}=1\)

 

\(a^{2}_{x} +a^{2}_{y}+a^{2}_{z}=1 or a^{2}_{x} +a^{2}_{y} +0^{2}=1\)

 

Simplifying these equations yields:

\(0.707 o_{y} + n_{z}o_{z} = 0\)

 

\(n_{x}a_{x} + 0.707 a_{y} = 0\)

 

\(a_{y}o_{y} = 0\)

 

\(n^{2}_{x} +n^{2}_{z} = 0.5\)

 

\(o^{2}_{y} + o^{2}_{z} = 1\)

 

\(a^{2}_{x} + a^{2}_{y} = 1\)

 

Solving these six equations yields \(n_{x} = ± 0.707,n_{z} = 0, o_{y} = 0, o_{z} = 1, a_{x} = ± 0.707, and a_{y}= −0.707\). Notice that both \(n_{x}\) and \(a_{x}\) must have the same sign. The reason for multiple solutions is that with the given parameters, it is possible to have two sets of mutually perpendicular vectors in opposite directions. The final matrix is:

\(F_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 0.707 & 0 &0.707 & 5 \\ 0.707 & 0 & -0.707 & 3 \\0 & 1 & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1\end{matrix} \right ] or F_{2} = \left [ \begin{matrix} -0.707 & 0 &-0.707 & 5 \\ 0.707 & 0 & -0.707 & 3 \\0 & 1 & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1\end{matrix} \right ]\)

 

As you can see, both matrices satisfy all the requirements set by the constraint equations, but only \(F_{1}\) follows the right-hand rule. It is important to realize that the values representing the three direction vectors are not arbitrary, but are bound by these equations. Therefore, you may not randomly use any desired values in the matrix. The problem may also be solved using n × o = a, or:

\( \left [ \begin{matrix} i & j &k \\ n_{x} & n_{y} & n_{z} \\ o_{x} & o_{y} & o_{z} \end{matrix} \right ]=a_{x} i+a_{y} j+a_{z} k\)

or

\(i(n_{y}o_{z}-n_{z}o_{y})-j(n_{x}o_{z}- n_{z}o_{x})+k(n_{x}o_{y}- n_{y}o_{x})=a_{x}i+a_{y}j+a_{z}k\) (2.12)

Substituting the values into this equation yields:

\(i(0.707o_{z}-n_{z}o_{y})-j(n_{x}o_{z})+k(n_{x}o_{y})=a_{x}i+a_{y}j+0k\)

Solving the three simultaneous equations results in:
\(0 707o_{z} −n_{z}o_{y} = a_{x}\)

 

\(−n_{x}o_{z} = a_{y}\)

 

\(n_{x}o_{y} =0\)

which replace the three equations for the dot products. Together with the three unit-vector lengthconstraint
equations, there are six equations. Note that using the cross-product constraint equation results only in \(F_{1}\) .
