Guest Question.

Find the inverse of the matrix given in which is

\(\mathrm{A}=\left(\begin{array}{rrr}1 & -1 & 5 \\3 & 9 & 7 \\-2 & 1 & 0\end{array}\right)\)
Admin
Solution

We need to find \(\mathrm{A}^{-1}\) which is given by the above Proposition: \(\mathrm{A}^{-1}=\frac{1}{\operatorname{det}(\mathrm{A})} \operatorname{adj}(\mathbf{A})\) What is \(a d j(\mathrm{~A})\) equal to?

Remember, \(a d j(\mathrm{~A})\) is the cofactor matrix transposed, and was found in above:

\(a d j(\mathbf{A})=\mathbf{C}^{T}=\left(\begin{array}{ccc}-7 & 5 & -52 \\-14 & 10 & 8 \\21 & 1 & 12\end{array}\right)\)

We only need to find \(\operatorname{det}(\mathrm{A})\). Expanding along the bottom row of the given matrix \(\mathrm{A}\), because it contains a 0 :

Substituting these into formula (6.13) gives

\( \mathrm{A}^{-1}=\frac{1}{\operatorname{det}(\mathbf{A})} a d j(\mathrm{~A})=\frac{1}{112}\left(\begin{array}{ccc} -7 & 5 & -52 \\ -14 & 10 & 8 \\ 21 & 1 & 12 \end{array}\right) \)

Theorem (6.13). If \(\operatorname{det}(\mathbf{A}) \neq 0\) (not zero) then

\(\mathrm{A}^{-1}=\frac{1}{\operatorname{det}(\mathbf{A})} \operatorname{adj}(\mathrm{A})\)
