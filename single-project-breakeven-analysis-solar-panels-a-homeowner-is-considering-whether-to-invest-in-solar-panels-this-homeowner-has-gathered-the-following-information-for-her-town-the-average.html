Single Project Breakeven Analysis: Solar Panels

A homeowner is considering whether to invest in solar panels. This homeowner has gathered the follow in information for her town :

• The average cost for residential energy is $0.124/kWh.

• The government subsidizes 70% of whatever is invested by the household.

• Each square foot of panel produces 19.13 kWh every year.

• The cost of installation per square foot of solar panel is $93.91.

The house has 350 square feet of roof space. What is the breakeven point in years for the installation of solar panels? Assume the homeowner’s interest rate is 6% per year.

Admin
Solution

The breakeven point is where the annual savings from the solar panels offsets the initial investment. The initial investment is calculated as follows.

350 square feet of roof space × $93.91/square foot = $32,869

With a 70% government subsidy, the purchase price is (0.3)($32,869) = $9,861.

To find the breakeven point in years (X), we set the purchase price equal to the potential energy savings.

$9,861 = (350 square feet)(19.13 kWh/sq ft-year) ($0.124/kWh)(P/A, 6%, X)

$9,861 = ($830.24)(P/A, 6%, X)

11.87 = (P/A, 6%, X)

From Table C-9 (i = 6%), we see that 21 ≤ X ≤ 22 years. It would take approximately 22 years of energy savings to offset the installation cost of the solar panels. A graph of this breakeven situation is shown i n Figure 11-1.
