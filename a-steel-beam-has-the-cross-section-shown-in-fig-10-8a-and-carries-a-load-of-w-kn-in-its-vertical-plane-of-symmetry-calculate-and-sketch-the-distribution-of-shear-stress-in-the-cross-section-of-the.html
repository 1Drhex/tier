Guest Question.

A steel beam has the cross section shown in Fig. 10.8(a) and carries a load of W kN in its vertical plane of symmetry. Calculate and sketch the distribution of shear stress in the cross section of the beam and hence determine the maximum allowable value of W if the shear stress in the beam is limited to 100 N/mmÂ².

Admin
Solution

The second moment of area of the beam section about the z axis is given by (see Section 9.6)

\(I_{z}=\frac{120 \times 240^{3}}{12}-\frac{105 \times 200^{3}}{12}=6.8 \times 10^{7} mm ^{4}\)

Then, from Eq. (10.8), the shear stress distribution in the flange is

\(\tau_{ f }=-\frac{S_{y}}{2 I_{z}}\left(\frac{D^{2}}{4}-y_{ f }^{2}\right)\) (10.8)

 

\(\tau_{ f }=-\frac{W \times 10^{3}}{2 \times 6.8 \times 10^{7}}\left(\frac{240^{2}}{4}-y_{ f }^{2}\right)\)

so that

\(\tau_{ f }=-7.4 \times 10^{-6} W \left(120^{2}-y_{ f }^{2}\right) N / mm ^{2}\) (i)

Then, when \( y_{ f }=120 mm , \tau_{ f }=0 \) and when \( y_{ f }=100 mm , \tau_{ f }=-0.033 W N / mm ^{2} \). The shear stress distribution in the web is obtained directly from Eq. (10.11) and is

\(\tau_{ w }=-\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}}\left[\frac{B}{8 t_{ w }}\left(D^{2}-d^{2}\right)+\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{d^{2}}{4}-y_{ w }^{2}\right)\right] \) (10.11)

 

\(\tau_{ w }=-\frac{W \times 10^{3}}{6.8 \times 10^{7}}\left[\frac{120}{8 \times 15}\left(240^{2}-200^{2}\right)+\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{200^{2}}{4}-y_{ w }^{2}\right)\right]\)

which gives

\(\tau_{ w }=-9.8 \times 10^{-7} W\left(339000-7.5 y_{ w }^{2}\right)\) (ii)

From Eq. (ii), when \( y_{ w }=100 mm , \tau_{ w }=-0.259 W N / mm ^{2} \text { and when } y_{ w }=0, \tau_{ w }=-0.332 W N / mm ^{2}=\tau_{\max } \). The complete distribution is shown in Fig. 10.8(b).
For a limiting value of shear stress of 100 N/mmÂ²,

100 = 0.332W

so that

W = 301 kN.

In this example we have applied Eqs (10.8) and (10.11) directly. However, rather than attempt to commit these lengthy formulae to memory it is generally better to work from first principles.
