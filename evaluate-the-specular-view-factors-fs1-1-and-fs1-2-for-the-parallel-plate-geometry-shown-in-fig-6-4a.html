Guest Question.

Evaluate the specular view factors \(F^{s}_{1-1}\) and \(F^{s}_{1-2}\) for the parallel plate geometry shown in Fig. 6-4a.

Admin
Solution

We note that, because of the one-dimensionality of the problem, \(F^{s}_{d1-1}\) must be the same for any \(dA_{1}\) on surface \(A_{1}\). Since \(F^{s}_{1-2}\) is nothing but a surface average of \(F^{s}_{d1-1}\), we conclude that \(F^{s}_{d1-1} = F^{s}_{1-2}\) sufficient to consider energy leaving from an infinitesimal area (rather than all of \(A_{1}\)). Examining Fig. 6- Ab we see that every beam (assumed to have unity strength) leaving \(dA_{1}\), regardless of its direction, must travel to surface \(A_{2}\) (a beam of strength “1” is intercepted). After reflection at \(A_{2}\) a beam of strength \(\rho ^{s}_{2}\) returns to \(A_{1}\) specularly, where it is reflected again and a beam of strength \(\rho ^{s}_{2}\rho ^{s}_{1}\) returns to \(A_{2}\) specularly.

After one more reflection a beam of strength (P2Pj)p5 returns to Au and so on. Thus, the specular view factor may be evaluated as

\(F_{d1-2}^{s}=F^{s}_{1-2}=1+\rho ^{s}_{1}\rho ^{s}_{2}+(\rho ^{s}_{1}\rho ^{s}_{2})^{2}+(\rho ^{s}_{1}\rho ^{s}_{2})^{3}+….\) (6.7)

Since\(\rho ^{s}_{1}\rho ^{s}_{2} < 1\) the sum in this equation is readily evaluated by the methods given in Wylie [3], and

\(F_{1-2}^{s}=\frac{1}{1-\rho ^{s}_{1}\rho ^{s}_{2}} =F^{s}_{2-1}\) (6.8)

The last part of this relation is found by switching subscripts or by invoking reciprocity (and \(A_{1}\) = \(A_{2}\)).
We notice that specular view factors are not limited to values between zero and one, but are often greater than unity because much of the radiative energy leaving a surface is accounted for more than once.
All energy from \(A_{1}\) is intercepted by \(A_{2}\) after direct travel, but only the fraction (\(1-\rho ^{s}_{2}\)) is removed (by absorption and/or diffuse reflection) from the specular reflection path. The fraction \(\rho ^{s}_{2}\) travels on specularly and is, therefore, counted a second time, etc. Thus, it is (\(1-\rho ^{s}_{2}) F^{s}_{1-2}\)that must have a value between zero and one, and the summation relation, equation (4.17) \(\sum\limits_{j=1}^{N}{F_{di-j}}=\sum\limits_{j=1}^{N}{F_{i-j}}=1\) , must be replaced by

\(\sum\limits_{j=1}^{N}{(1-\rho ^{s}_{j})} F^{s}_{i-j}=1\) (6.9)

Equation (6.9), fonned here through intuition, will be developed rigorously in the next section.
\(F^{s}_{1-1}\) may be found similarly as

\(F^{s}_{1-1}=\rho ^{s}_{2}+(\rho ^{s}_{1}\rho ^{s}_{2})\rho ^{s}_{2}+(\rho ^{s}_{1}\rho ^{s}_{2})^{2}\rho ^{s}_{2}+…=\frac{\rho ^{s}_{2}}{1-\rho ^{s}_{1}\rho ^{s}_{2}}\)

We note in passing that

\((1-\rho ^{s}_{1})F^{s}_{1-1}+(1-\rho ^{s}_{2})F^{s}_{1-2}=\frac{(1-\rho ^{s}_{1})\rho ^{S}_{2}+1-\rho ^{s}_{2}}{1-\rho ^{s}_{1}\rho ^{s}_{2}} =1,\)

as postulated by equation (6.9).
