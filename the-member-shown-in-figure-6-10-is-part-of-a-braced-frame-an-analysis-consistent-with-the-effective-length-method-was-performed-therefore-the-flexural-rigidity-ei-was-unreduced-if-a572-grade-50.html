Guest Question.

The member shown in Figure 6.10 is part of a braced frame. An analysis consistent with the effective length method was performed; therefore, the flexural rigidity, EI, was unreduced. If A572 Grade 50 steel is used, is this member adequate?
\(K_{x} = K_{y} = 1.0\).

Admin
Solution

The factored loads, computed from load combination 2, are shown in Figure 6.11. Determine which interaction formula to apply. The required compressive strength is

\(P_{r} = P_{u} = P_{nt} + B_{2}P_{\ell t} = 420 + 0 = 420 \) kips \( (B_{2} = 0 \) for a braced frame)

From the column load tables, for KL = 1.0 × 14 = 14 feet, the axial compressive strength of a W12 × 65 is

\(\phi _{c} P_{n} =685 \) kips

\(\frac{P_{u} }{\phi _{c} P_{n}} =\frac{420}{685} =0.6131\gt 0.2\) \(\therefore \) Equation 6.3 (AISC Equation H1-1a).

In the plane of bending,

\(P_{e1} =\frac{\pi ^{2}EI }{(K_{1}L )^{2} } =\frac{\pi ^{2}EI_{x} }{(K_{x}L )^{2} } =\frac{\pi ^{2}(29,000)(533)}{(1.0\times 14\times 12)^{2} } =5405 \) kips

\(C_{m}=0.6-0.4\left(\frac{M_{1} }{M_{2} } \right) =0.6-0.4\left(-\frac{70.8}{82.4} \right) =0.9437 \)

\(B_{1}=\frac{C_{m}}{1-(\alpha P_{r}/P_{e1} )} =\frac{C_{m}}{1-(1.00 P_{u}/P_{e1} )} =\frac{0.9437}{1-(420/5405)} =1.023 \)

From the Beam Design Charts in Part 3 of the Manual with \(C_{b} = 1.0 \) and \(L_{b} = 14 \) feet, the moment strength is

\(\phi _{b}M_{n} = 345 \) ft-kips

For the actual value of \(C_{b} \), refer to the moment diagram of Figure 6.11:

\(C_{b} =\frac{12.5M_{max} }{2.5M_{max}+3M_{A}+4M_{B}+3M_{C}} \)

\(=\frac{12.5(82.4) }{2.5(82.4)+3(73.7)+4(76.6)+3(79.5)} =1.06 \)

\(∴ \) \(\phi _{b}M_{n} = C_{b}(345) = 1.060(345) = 366 \) ft-kips

But \(\phi _{b}M_{p} = 356 \) ft-kips (from the charts) < 366 ft-kips \(∴ \) Use \(\phi _{b}M_{n} = 356 \) ft-kips.

(Since a W12 × 65 is noncompact for \(F_{y} = 50 \) ksi, 356 ft-kips is the design strength based on FLB rather than full yielding of the cross section.) The factored load moments are

\(M_{nt} = 82.4 \) ft-kips \( M_{\ell t} = 0 \)

From AISC Equation A-8-1, the required moment strength is

\(M_{r} = M_{u} = B_{1}M_{nt} + B_{2}M_{\ell t} = 1.023(82.4) + 0 = 84.30\) ft-kips \(= M_{ux}\)

From Equation 6.3 (AISC Equation H1-1a),

\(\frac{P_{u} }{\phi _{c}P_{n}} +\frac{8}{9}\left(\frac{M_{ux} }{\phi _{b}M_{nx}}+\frac{M_{uy} }{\phi _{b}M_{ny}} \right) =0.6131+\frac{8}{9}\left(\frac{84.30}{356}+0 \right) =0.824\lt 1.0 \) (OK)

The member is satisfactory.

The service loads, computed from load combination 2, are shown in Figure 6.12. Determine which interaction formula to apply. The required compressive strength is

\(P_{r} = P_{a} = P_{nt} + B_{2}P_{\ell t} = 280 + 0 = 280 \) kips \( (B_{2} = 0 \) for a braced frame)

From the column load tables, for KL = 1.0 × 14 = 14 feet, the axial compressive strength of a W12 × 65 is

\(\frac{P_{n} }{Ω _{c}}=456 \) kips

\(\frac{P_{a} }{P_{n}/Ω _{c} } =\frac{280}{456} =0.6140\gt 0.2 \) \( \therefore \) Equation 6.5 (AISC Equation H1-1a).

In the plane of bending,

\(P_{e1} =\frac{\pi ^{2}EI }{(K_{1}L )^{2} } =\frac{\pi ^{2}EI_{x} }{(K_{x}L )^{2} } =\frac{\pi ^{2}(29,000)(533)}{(1.0\times 14\times 12)^{2} } =5405 \) kips

\(C_{m}=0.6-0.4\left(\frac{M_{1} }{M_{2} } \right) =0.6-0.4\left(-\frac{47}{55} \right) =0.9418 \)

\(B_{1}=\frac{C_{m}}{1-(\alpha P_{r}/P_{e1} )} =\frac{C_{m}}{1-(1.60 P_{a}/P_{e1} )} =\frac{0.9418}{1-(1.60 \times280/5405)} =1.027 \)

Next, From the Beam Design Charts with \(C_{b} = 1.0 \) and \(L_{b} = 14 \) feet, the moment strength is

\(\frac{M_{n}}{Ω _{b}} = 230 \) ft-kips

For the actual value of \( C_{b}\) , refer to the moment diagram of Figure 6.12:

\( C_{b} =\frac{12.5M_{max} }{2.5M_{max}+3M_{A}+4M_{B}+3M_{C}}\)

\( =\frac{12.5(55) }{2.5(55)+3(49)+4(51)+3(53)} =1.062\)

\( ∴\) \( \frac{M_{n}}{Ω _{b}} = C_{b}(230) = 1.062(230) = 244.3 \) ft-kips

But \( \frac{M_{p}}{Ω _{b}} = 237 \) ft-kips (from the charts) < 244.3 ft-kips , so use \( \frac{M_{n}}{Ω _{b}} = 237 \) ft-kips.

(Since a WI2 × 65 is noncompact for \( F_{y} = 50\) ksi, 237 ft-kips is the strength based on FLB rather than full yielding of the cross section.) The unamplified moments are

\( M_{nt} = 55\) ft-kips \( M_{\ell t} = 0\)

From AISC Equation A-8-1, the required moment strength is

\( M_{r} = M_{a} = B_{1}M_{nt} + B_{2}M_{\ell t} = 1.027(55) + 0 = 56.49\) ft-kips \(= M_{ax}\)

From Equation 6.5 (AISC Equation H1-1a),

\( \frac{P_{a} }{P_{n}/\Omega _{c}} +\frac{8}{9}\left(\frac{M_{ax} }{M_{nx}/\Omega _{b}}+\frac{M_{ay} }{M_{ny}/\Omega _{b}} \right) =\frac{280}{456} +\frac{8}{9}\left(\frac{56.49}{237}+0 \right) =0.826\lt 1.0\) (OK)

The member is satisfactory.
