Guest Question.

In a wind tunnel experiment, an aluminum plate 2.0 m in length is electrically heated at a power density of 1 kW/m² and is cooled on one surface by air flowing at 10 m/s. The air in the wind tunnel has a temperature of 290 K and is at 1 atm pressure, and the Reynolds number at the end of turbulent transition regime is observed to be 400,000. Estimate the average temperature of the plate .

Admin
Solution

For this low heat flux, we expect the plate temperature to be near the air temperature, so we evaluate properties at 300 K: \(ν = 1.578 × 10^{-5}\) m²/s , k = 0.02623 W/m·K, and Pr = 0.713. At 10 m/s, the plate Reynolds number is \(Re_{L} = (10)(2)/(1.578×10^{5}) = 1.267× 10^{6}\). From eqn. (6.118),

 

\(\bar{Nu_{L} }=0.037 Pr^{0.43}\left\{Re^{0.8}_{L}-[Re^{0.8}_{trans} -17.95Pr^{-0.097}(Re_{trans} )^{1/2} ] \right\}\)

 

we get

 

\(\bar{Nu_{L} }=0.037 (0.713)^{0.43}\left\{(1.267\times \times 10^{6} )^{0.8}-[(400,000)^{0.8} -17.95(0.713)^{-0.097}(400,00)^{1/2} ] \right\} = 1,845\)

so

 

\(\bar{h}=\frac{1845 k}{L}=\frac{1845(0.02623)}{2.0}=24.19 W/m^2 K\)

It follows that the average plate temperature is

 

\(\bar{T_{w} }=290 K+\frac{10^3 W/m^2}{24.19 W/m^2 K}=331 K.\)

The film temperature is (331+290)/2 = 311 K; if we recalculate using properties at 311 K, the h changes by less than 4%, and Tw by1.3 K. To take better account of the transition regime, we can use Churchill’s equation, (6.120 c).

 

\(\bar{Nu_{L} }=0.45+(0.677 \phi ^{1/2} ) \left\{1+\frac{(\phi /12,500)^{3/5}}{[1+(\phi _{um}/\phi )^{7/2}]^{2/5}} \right\}^{1/2}\)

 

First, we evaluate \(\phi\) :

 

\(\phi =\frac{(1.267\times 10^6)(0.713)^{2/3}}{[1+(0.0468/0.713)^{2/3}]^{1/2}}=9.38\times 10^5\)

We then estimate

\(\phi _{um}=1.875 · \phi (Re_{L}=400,000)=\frac{(1.857)(400,000)(0.713)^{2/3}}{[1+(0.0468/0.713)^{2/3}]^{1/2}}=5.55\times 10^5\)

Finally,

\(\bar{Nu_{L} }=0.45+(0.6774 )(9.38\times 10^5)^{1/2} \times \left\{1+\frac{(9.38\times 10^5 /12,500)^{3/5}}{[1+(5.55\times 10^5/9.38\times 10^5 )^{7/2}]^{2/5}} \right\}^{1/2} =2,418\)

which leads to

\(\bar{h}=\frac{2418k}{L}=\frac{2418(0.02623)}{2.0}=31.71 W/m^2 K\)

and

\(\bar{T_{w} }=290 K +\frac{10^3 W/m^2}{31.71 W/m^3 K}=322 K.\)

 

Thus, in this case, the average heat transfer coefficient is 33% higher when the transition regime is included.
