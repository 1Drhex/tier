HYDROSTATIC FORCE ON A CURVED SURFACE

Sketch:

Surface AB is a circular arc with a radius of 2 m and a width of 1 m into the paper. The distance EB is 4 m. The fluid above surface AB is water, and atmospheric pressure prevails on the free surface of the water and on the bottom side of surface AB. Find the magnitude and line of action of the hydrostatic force acting on surface AB.

Problem Definition

Situation: A body of water is contained by a curved surface.

Find:

1. Hydrostatic force (in newtons) on the curved surface AB.

2. Line of action of the hydrostatic force.

Properties: Water (10°C), Table A.5: \(\gamma=9810 N / m ^{3}\).

Plan

Apply equilibrium concepts to the body of fluid ABC.

1. Find the horizontal component of F by applying Eq. (3.29).

\(F_{x}=F_{A C}\) (3.29)

2. Find the vertical component of F by applying Eq. (3.30).

\(F_{y}=W+F_{C B}\) (3.30)

3. Find the line of action of F by finding the lines of action of components and then using a graphical solution.

Admin
Solution

1. Equilibrium in the horizontal direction

 

\(F_{x}=F_{H}=\bar{p} A=(5 m )\left(9810 N / m ^{3}\right)\left(2 \times 1 m ^{2}\right)\)

 

=98.1 KN

 

2. Equilibrium in the horizontal direction

 

• Vertical force on side CB

 

\(F_{V}=\bar{p}_{0} A=9.81 kN / m ^{3} \times 4 m \times 2 m \times 1 m =78.5\) KN

 

• Weight of the water in volume ABC

 

\(W=\gamma V_{A B C}=(\gamma)\left(\frac{1}{4} \pi r^{2}\right)(w)\)

 

\(=\left(9.81 kN / m ^{3}\right) \times\left(0.25 \times \pi \times 4 m ^{2}\right)(1 m )=30.8\) KN

 

• Summing forces

 

\(F_{y}=W+F_{V}=109.3\) KN

 

3. Line of action (horizontal force)

 

\(y_{ cp }=\bar{y}+\frac{\bar{I}}{\bar{y} A}=(5 m )+\left(\frac{1 \times 2^{3} / 12}{5 \times 2 \times 1} m \right)\)

 

\(y_{ cp }=5.067\) m

 

4. The line of action \(\left(x_{c p}\right)\) for the vertical force is found by summing moments about point C:

 

\(x_{c p} F_{y}=F_{V} \times 1 m +W \times \bar{x}_{W}\)

 

The horizontal distance from point C to the centroid of the area ABC is found using Fig. A.1: \(\bar{x}_{W}=4 r / 3 \pi=0.849\) m. Thus,

 

\(x_{c p}=\frac{78.5 kN \times 1 m +30.8 kN \times 0.849 m }{109.3 kN }=0.957\) m
