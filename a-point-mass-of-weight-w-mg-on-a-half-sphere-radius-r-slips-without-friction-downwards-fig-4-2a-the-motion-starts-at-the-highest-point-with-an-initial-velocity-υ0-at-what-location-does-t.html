Guest Question.

A point mass of weight W = mg on a half-sphere (radius r) slips without friction downwards (Fig. 4.2a). The motion starts at the highest point with an initial velocity \(\upsilon _{0} \). At what location does the point mass lift-off from the sphere?

Admin
Solution

Until lift-off the point mass moves along a circular path; its location can be described by the coordinate φ (Fig. 4.2b). The point mass is subjected to its weight W (active force) and to the normal force N (reaction force). With the tangential acceleration \(a_{t}=r\ddot{\varphi } \) (in the positive φ-direction) and the normal acceleration \(a_{n}=r\dot{\varphi }^{2} \) (directed towards 0) the inertial forces of magnitude \(ma_{t} \) (opposite to \(a_{t} \)) and \(ma_{n} \) (opposite to \(a_{n} \)) can be drawn in the free-body diagram (Fig. 4.2c). Force equilibrium according to (4.4) in the normal and tangential directions yields the equations of motion

\(F+F_{I} =0\) (4.4)

\(\nearrow :N-mg\cos \varphi +mr\dot{\varphi }^{2}=0\) , (a)

\(\searrow :mg\sin \varphi -mr\ddot{\varphi }=0\) . (b)

We multiply (b) with \(\dot{\varphi } \) and integrate to obtain

\(\dot{\varphi } \ddot{\varphi } =\frac{g}{r}\sin \varphi \dot{\varphi }\rightarrow \frac{1}{2} \dot{\varphi }^{2}=-\frac{g}{r}\cos \varphi +C\) .

Using \(\upsilon =r\dot{\varphi } \) , the constant of integration C can be determined from the initial condition \(\upsilon \left(\varphi =0\right)=\upsilon _{0} \):

\(\frac{1}{2} \frac{\upsilon ^{2}_{0} }{r^{2} } =-\frac{g}{r}+C\rightarrow C=\frac{1}{2}\frac{\upsilon ^{2}_{0} }{r_{2} }+\frac{g}{r} \) .

Thus we have

\(\dot{\varphi }^{2}=-\frac{2g}{r}\cos \varphi +\frac{\upsilon ^{2}_{0} }{r^{2} }+\frac{2g}{r}=\frac{2g}{r}\left(1-\cos \varphi \right)+ \frac{\upsilon ^{2}_{0} }{r^{2} }\) .

Insertion into (a) yields the normal force N as a function of the angle φ:

\(N=mg\cos \varphi -mr\dot{\varphi }^{2} =mg\left(3\cos \varphi -2\right) -m\frac{\upsilon ^{2}_{0} }{r}\) .

The location of lift-off is characterized by the condition of a vanishing normal force:

N = 0 \(\rightarrow mg\left(3\cos \varphi -2\right)-m\frac{\upsilon ^{2}_{0} }{r}=0\) .

Thus, the lift-off angle φ* is given by

\(\cos \varphi ^{*}=\frac{2}{3}+\frac{\upsilon ^{2}_{0} }{3gr}\) .

Since cosφ ≤ 1 it follows that \(\upsilon _{0} \leq \sqrt{gr} \). For \(\upsilon _{0} \geq \sqrt{gr} \) the point mass immediately loses contact with the sphere.
