Guest Question.

A separately excited DC motor drives a rated load torque of \(40 \mathrm{Nm}\) at \(1000 \mathrm{rpm}\) as shown in Figure 9.25. The field circuit resistance is \(200 \Omega\) and armature circuit resistance is \(0.05 \Omega\). The field winding, connected to single-phase, \(120 \mathrm{~V}\) source, is fed through 1-phase full converter with zero degrees firing angle. The armature circuit is also fed through another full converter from the same converter single-phase, \(120 \mathrm{~V}\) source.

With magnetic saturation neglected, the motor constant is \(0.8 \mathrm{~V}\)-sec/A-rad. For ripple free armature and field currents. Determine:

  1. Rated armature current
  2. Firing angle delay of armature converter at rated load
  3. Speed regulation at full load
  4. Input power factor (PF) of the armature converter and the drive at rated load.
Admin
Solution
  1. For field converter, angle delay \(\alpha=0^{\circ}\).

The field voltage,

\( \begin{array}{c} V_{f}=\frac{2 V_{m}}{\pi}=\frac{2 \sqrt{2} \times 120}{\pi}=108.03 \mathrm{~V} \\ I_{f}=\frac{V_{f}}{r_{f}}=\frac{108.03}{200}=0.54 \mathrm{~A} . \end{array} \)

With magnetic saturation neglected, \(\phi_{1}=K_{1} I_{f}\)

\( E_{a}=K_{a} \phi \omega_{m}=K_{a}\cdot K_{1}\cdot I_{f} \omega_{m}=K \cdot I_{f}\cdot \omega_{m} \)

and the torque \(T=K_{a} \phi \mathrm{I}_{a}=K_{a} \cdot K_{1} \cdot I_{f}\cdot I_{a}=K \cdot I_{f}\cdot I_{a}\).

The rated armature current, \(\mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{a}}\) :

\( \begin{array}{c} I_{a}=\frac{T}{K \cdot I_{f}} \\ I_{a}=\frac{40}{0.8 \times 0.54}=92.59 \mathrm{~A} \end{array} \)

2 .

\( \begin{array}{c} V_{0}=V_{t}=\frac{2 V_{m}}{\pi}(\cos \alpha)=E_{a}+I_{a} r_{a} \\ V_{0}=V_{t}=\frac{2 \sqrt{2} \times 120}{\pi}(\cos \alpha)=0.8 \times 0.54 \times \frac{2 \pi \times 1000}{60}+92.59 \times 0.05 \\ \alpha=62.51^{\circ} . \end{array} \)

  1. At the same firing angle of \(\alpha=62.51^{\circ}\) motor EMF at no-load,

\( E_{a}=K_{a} \phi \omega_{m}=K_{a}\cdot K_{1} \cdot I_{f}\cdot \omega_{m}=K \cdot I_{f}\cdot \omega_{m}=V_{t}=49.87 \mathrm{~V} . \)

The speed at no-load:

\( \omega_{m o}=\frac{E_{a}}{K \cdot I_{f}}=\frac{49.87}{0.8 \times 0.54}=115.43 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{sec} . \)

Or \(1102.37 \mathrm{rpm}\).

Speed regulation at full load:

\( \begin{array}{c} N_{R} \%=\frac{\text { No loadspeed }-\text { Full load speed }}{\text { Full loadspeed }} \\ N_{R} \%=\frac{1102.37-1000}{1000}=10.23 \% . \end{array} \)

  1. The input power factor of the armature converter

\( \begin{array}{c} p f=\frac{V_{t} \cdot I_{a}}{V_{s} \cdot I_{\text {ar }}} \\ p f=\frac{49.87 \times 92.59}{120 \times 92.59}=0.415 \quad \text { lagging } \end{array} \)

RMS value of current in armature converter,

\( I_{a r}=92.59 \mathrm{~A} \text {. } \)

RMS value of current in field circuit,

\( 1_{f \mathrm{~T}}=I_{\mathrm{f}}=0.54 \mathrm{~A} . \)

Total current taken from the source,

\( \begin{array}{c} I_{s r}=\sqrt{I_{a r}^{2}+I_{f r}^{2}} \\ I_{s r}=\sqrt{92.59^{2}+0.54^{2}}=92.591 \mathrm{~A} . \end{array} \)

Input \(S=V_{\mathrm{s}} \cdot \mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{sr}}=120 \times 92.591=11.11 \mathrm{kVA} .\)

With no loss in the converters, total power input to motor and field:

\( =V_{t}. I_{a}+V_{f}. I_{f}=49.87 \times 59.03+108.03 \times 0.54=3 \mathrm{KW} \text {. } \)

\( \text { Input power factor }=\frac{P}{S}=\frac{3}{11.11}=0.27 \text { lagging. } \)
