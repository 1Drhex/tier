Guest Question.

Following data refer to the velocity compounded impulse turbine with two rows of moving blades:
Steam supply: 60 bar, 500°C
Speed of rotation: 3600 rpm
Mean radius of blades: 60 cm
Steam supply: 6 kg/s
Blade speed to steam velocity ratio: 0.2
Nozzle efficiency: 90%
Nozzle angle: 20°
Exit angles: 25°, 25° and 40° for first moving, fixed and second moving blades respectively
Blade velocity coefficient: 0.85 for all blades
Disc friction and Windage loss: 20 hp.
Determine,
(a) the stage pressure
(b) the blading efficiency
(c) the power output
(d) the state of steam leaving stage.

Admin
Solution
\(\begin{aligned}\text{Blade speed, }\quad\qquad\qquad\qquad U&= rω = \frac{60 × 10^{-2} × 2 × π × 3600}{60} = 226.2 m/s\\ρ&= 0.2 = \frac{U}{C_{1}} so C_{1} = 1131 m/s\\\text{Actual heat drop in stage, } Δh^{\prime}_{ 01}&= \frac{C_{1}^{2}}{2} = \frac{(1131)^{2}}{2} = 639.58 kJ/kg\\\text{Theoretical heat drop }\quad Δh_{01}&= \left\lgroup\frac{Δh^{\prime}_{ 01}}{0.9} \right\rgroup = 710.64 kJ/kg\\\text{Enthalpy at state 1 , } \quad\qquad h_{1}&= h_{0} – Δh_{01}\\\text{Enthalpy at inlet to nozzle, }h_{0}&= h_{\text{at 60 bar, 500°C}}\\h_{0}&= 3422.2 kJ/kg\\\text{Entropy }\qquad\qquad\qquad \qquad s_{0}&= 6.8803 kJ/kg·K\\\end{aligned}\\\text{hence, }h_{1} = 2711.56 kJ/kg, s_{1} = s_{0} = 6.8803 kJ/kg·K\\\text{From mollies diagram, }P_{1} = 3.8 bar\text{ corresponding to }h_{1}\text{ vertically below 60 bar and 500°C state.}\\\qquad \text{Stage pressure = 3.8 bar}\\\text{Velocity diagram is drawn using scale of 1 cm = 100 m/s and following,}\\\begin{aligned}U = 226.2 m/s, C_{1} &= 1131 m/s, α_{1} = 20°, β_{2} = 25°, α_{3} = 25°, β_{4} = 40°\\\frac{V_{2}}{V_{1}}& = \frac{V_{4}}{V_{3}} = 0.85\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{From diagram,}\qquad V_{1}&= 935 m/s, V_{2} = 794.75 m/s, C_{2} = 610 m/s\\\frac{C_{3}}{C_{2}}&= 0.85, ⇒ C_{3} = 0.85 × C_{2} = 518.5 m/s\\V_{3}&= 335 m/s ⇒ V_{4} = 284.75 m/s\end{aligned}\\\quad\text{Velocity diagram is drawn in following steps:}\\\text{(i) Draw AB corresponding to U (= 226.2 m/s)}\\\text{(ii) Draw AD at angle 20° corresponding to C}_{1}\text{ (= 1131 m/s)}\\\text{(iii) Join D with B and measure BD (= 9.35 cm) or V}_{1}\text{ = 935 m/s, Estimate }V_{2} = 0.85 × V_{1}\\\text{(iv) Draw BE corresponding to V}_{2}\text{ = (794.75 m/s) at angle }β_{2}\text{ (= 25°)}\\\text{(v) Join E with A and measure AE (= 6.1 cm) or C}_{2}\text{ = 610 m/s. For fixed blade C}_{3} = 0.85 × C_{2}, \text{ Estimate }C_{3}\\\text{(vi) Draw AG at angle }α_{3}\text{ (= 25°) corresponding to C}_{3}\text{ (= 518.5 m/s)}\\\text{(vii) Join G with B and measure BG (= 3.35 cm) or V}_{3}\text{ = 335 m/s. Estimate }V_{4} = 0.85 × V_{3}\text{ for IInd moving blade.}\\\text{(viii) Draw BF at angle 40° corresponding to V}_{4}\text{ (= 284.75 m/s)}\\\text{(ix) Join F with A and measure AF (= 2.8 cm) or C}_{4}\text{ = 280 m/s.}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Length KH (= 15.6 cm) or }ΔC_{\text{w Ist moving}}&= 1560 m/s\\\text{Length JI (= 5.45 cm) or }ΔC_{\text{w II moving}}&= 545 m/s\\\text{Total Work done = }(U · ΔC_{\text{w Ist moving}})&+ (U · ΔC_{\text{w II moving}})\\\text{ per kg of steam}\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad &\\&= 226.2 (1560 + 545)\\&= 476.15 kJ/kg\\\text{Blading efficiency}&=\frac{476.15}{639.58} = 0.7444 or 74.44\%\\\text{Net power output}&=\left\lgroup\frac{476.15 × 6}{0.7457} \right\rgroup – 20\\&= 3811.16 hp\\\text{Energy loss at exit per kg}&= \left\lgroup\frac{C_{4}^{2}}{2} \right\rgroup= \frac{(280)^{2}}{2} = 39.2 kJ/kg \\\text{Heat equivalent of net work}&= \frac{3811.16 × 0.7457}{6} = 473.66 kJ/kg\\\text{Hence Δh}&= 473.66 + 39.2 = 512.86 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Corresponding to enthalpy drop of 512.86 kJ/kg from }h_{0}\text{ the final condition of steam can be seen from mollier diagram.}\\\text{The state is at 225° (superheated steam at 3.8 bar (approximately)}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \text{Blading efficiency}&= 74.44\%\\\text{Net output}&= 3811.16 hp\\\text{State of steam leaving stage}&= 225°C\end{aligned}\)
