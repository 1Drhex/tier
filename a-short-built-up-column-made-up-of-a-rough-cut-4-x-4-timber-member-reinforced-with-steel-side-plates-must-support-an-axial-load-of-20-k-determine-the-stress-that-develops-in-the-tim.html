Guest Question.

A short, built-up column made up of a rough-cut \(4^{\prime \prime }\times 4^{\prime \prime }\) timber member, reinforced with steel side plates, must support an axial load of 20 k. Determine the stress that develops in the timber and in the steel plates if

\(E_{timber} =1.76\times 10^3 \space ksi \space ; \space E_{steel} =29\times 10^3 \space ksi\)

The rigid plates at the top and bottom allow the timber and steel side plates to deform uniformly under the action of \(P\).

Admin
Solution

From the condition of equilibrium:

\(f_{t} =\) Stress developed in the timber

 

\( f_{s}= \) Stress developed in the steel

 

\(\left[\sum{F_{y}=0 }\right] – 20 \space k + f_{s} A_{s} +f_{t} A_{t} =0\)

 

where

 

\(A_{s}=2\times \frac{1}{4}^{\prime \prime }\times 4^{\prime \prime }=2 \space in.^2 \)

 

\( A_{t}= 4^{\prime \prime }\times 4^{\prime \prime }=16 \space in.^2 \)

 

\( 2 \space in.^2 (f_{s} )+16 \space in.^2 (f_{t})=20 \space k\)

 

Obviously, the equation of equilibrium alone is insufficient to solve for the two unknowns. Therefore, another equation, involving a deformation relationship, must be written.

 

\(\delta =\delta _{t} = \delta _{s} \)

 

Because both the timber and the steel members are of equal length , \(L_{t} = L_{s} \space ; \) then ,

 

\(\varepsilon =\frac{\delta _{t}}{L_{t}} =\frac{\delta _{s} }{ L_{s} } \space ; \space \space \varepsilon_{t} = \varepsilon_{s} \)

 

but \(E=\frac{f}{ \varepsilon} \) or \(\varepsilon=\frac{f}{E}\)

 

\(\therefore \frac{f_{t} }{E_{t} } =\frac{f_{s} }{E_{s} } \space ; \space \space f_{t}=f_{s}\frac{E_{t}}{E_{s}} \)

 

\(f_{t}=\frac{1.76\times 10^3 \space ksi}{29 \times 10^3 \space ksi} (f_{s})=0.061 \space (f_{s})\)

 

Substituting into the equilibrium equation,

 

\(2 \space in.^2(f_{s})+16 \space in.^2 (0.061 \space f_{s})=20 \space k\)

 

\(2f_{s}+0.976f_{s}=20 \space ksi \space ; \space 2.976 \space f_{s}=20 \space ksi\)

 

\(f_{s}=6.72 \space ksi\)

 

\(f_{t}=0.061 (6.72 \space ksi )=0.41 \space ksi\)
