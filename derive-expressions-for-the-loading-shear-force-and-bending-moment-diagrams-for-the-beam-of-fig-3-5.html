\(q = R_{1} 〈x〉^{−1} − F_{1} 〈x − a_{1}〉^{−1} − F_{2} 〈x − a_{2}〉^{−1} + R_{2} 〈x − l〉^{−1}\) (1)

Next, we use Eq. (3–5) to get the shear force.

\(\int_{V_{A}}^{V_{B}}{dV} =\int_{x_{A}}^{x_{B}}{qdx}=V_{B}-V_{A}\) (3.5)

\(V=\int{q dx}=R_{1} 〈x〉^{0} − F_{1} 〈x − a_{1}〉^{0} − F_{2} 〈x − a_{2}〉^{0} + R_{2} 〈x − l〉^{0}\) (2)

Note that \(V = 0 at x = 0^{−}\).

A second integration, in accordance with Eq. (3–6), yields

\(\int_{M_{A}}^{M_{B}}{dM} =\int_{x_{A}}^{x_{B}}{V dx}=M_{B}-M_{A}\) (3.6)

\(M=\int{V dx}=R_{1} 〈x〉^{1} − F_{1} 〈x − a_{1}〉^{1} − F_{2} 〈x − a_{2}〉^{1} + R_{2} 〈x − l〉^{1}\) (3)

The reactions \(R_{1}\) and \(R_{2}\) can be found by taking a summation of moments and forces as usual, or they can be found by noting that the shear force and bending moment must be zero everywhere except in the region 0 ≤ x ≤ l . This means that Eq. (2) should give V = 0 at x slightly larger than l. Thus

\(R_{1} − F_{1} − F_{2} + R_{2} = 0\) (4)

Since the bending moment should also be zero in the same region, we have, from Eq. (3),

\(R_{1}l − F_{1}(l − a_{1}) − F_{2}(l − a_{2}) = 0\) (5)

Equations (4) and (5) can now be solved for the reactions \(R_{1}\) and \(R_{2}\).
