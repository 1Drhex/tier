Guest Question.

The simply supported beam shown in Figure P8.48a/49a carries a uniformly distributed load w on overhang BC. The beam is constructed of a Southern pine [E = 12 GPa] timber that is reinforced on its upper surface by a steel [E = 200 GPa] plate (Figure P8.48b/49b). The beam spans are L = 4 m and a = 1.25 m. The wood beam has dimensions of bw = 150 mm and \(d_{w}\) = 280 mm. The steel plate dimensions are \(b_{s}\) = 230 mm and \(t_{s}\) = 6 mm. Assume that the allowable bending stresses of the wood and the steel are 9 MPa and 165 MPa, respectively. Determine the largest acceptable magnitude for distributed load w. (You may neglect the weight of the beam in your calculations.)

Admin
Solution

Let the wood be denoted as material (1) and the steel as material (2). The modular ratio is:

 

\(n=\frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}=\frac{200 GPa }{12 GPa }=16.6667\)

 

Transform the steel plate into an equivalent amount of wood by multiplying its width by the modular ratio: \(b_{s, \text { trans }}\) = 16.6667(230 mm) = 3,833.333 mm. Thus, for calculation purposes, the 230 mm Ã— 6 mm steel plate is replaced by a wood board that is 3,833.333-mm wide and 6-mm thick.

Centroid location of the transformed section in the vertical direction

 

\(\begin{array}{lccccc} {\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Width } b \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Height } h \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c} y_{i}(\text { from bottom }) \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { wood beam } & 150 & 280 & 42,000 & 140 & 5,880,000 \\\text { transformed steel plate } & 3,833.333 & 6 & 23,000 & 283 & 6,509,000 \\\hline & & & 65,000 & & 12,389,000\end{array}\)

 

\(\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{12,389,000 mm ^{3}}{65,000 mm ^{2}}=190.600 mm\) (measured upward from bottom of cross section)

 

Moment of inertia about the horizontal centroidal axis

\(\begin{array}{ccccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { wood beam } & 274,400,000 & -50.6 & 107,535,120 & 381,935,120 \\\text { transformed steel plate } & 69,000 & 92.4 & 196,368,480 & 196,437,480 \\\hline {\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }=} & && & 578,372,600 mm ^{4}\end{array}\)

 

Determine maximum w
If the allowable bending stress in the wood is 9 MPa, then the maximum bending moment that may be supported by the beam is:

 

\(\sigma_{1}=\frac{M y}{I} \quad \therefore M_{\max } \leq \frac{\sigma_{1} I}{y}=\frac{\left(9 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(578,372,600 mm ^{4}\right)}{190.6 mm }=27.3104 \times 10^{6} N – mm\)

If the allowable bending stress in the steel is 165 MPa, then the maximum bending moment that may be supported by the beam is:

 

\(\sigma_{2}=n \frac{M y}{I} \quad \therefore M_{\max } \leq \frac{\sigma_{2} I}{n y}=\frac{\left(165 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(578,372,600 mm ^{4}\right)}{(16.6667)(280 mm +6 mm -190.6 mm )}=60.0198 \times 10^{6} N – mm\)

 

Note: The negative signs were omitted in the previous two equations because only the moment magnitude is of interest here.

From these two results, the maximum moment that the beam can support is 27.3104Ã—\(10^{6}\) N-mm. A formula for the maximum bending moment can be easily derived using the process described in Chapter 7. For this simply supported beam with a uniformly distributed load on the overhang, the maximum bending moment occurs at support B and it has a value given by:

 

\(M_{\max }=\frac{w a^{2}}{2}\)

 

Using this formula, the maximum distributed load magnitude w that can be supported is found from the following calculation:

 

\(w=\frac{2 M_{\max }}{a^{2}}=\frac{2\left(27.3104 \times 10^{6} N – mm \right)(1 m / 1000 mm )}{(1.25 m )^{2}}=34,957.3 N / m =35.0 kN / m\)
