Guest Question.

By appropriate modification of the hydrogen formula, determine the hyperfine splitting in the ground state of (a) muonic hydrogen (in which a muon—same charge and g-factor as the electron, but 207 times the mass— substitutes for the electron), (b) positronium (in which a positron—same mass and g-factor as the electron, but opposite charge—substitutes for the proton), and (c) muonium (in which an anti-muon—same mass and g-factor as a muon, but opposite charge—substitutes for the proton). Hint: Don’t forget to use the reduced mass () in calculating the “Bohr radius” of these exotic “atoms,” but use the actual masses in the gyromagnetic ratios. Incidentally, the answer you get for positronium \( \left(4.82 \times 10^{-4} eV \right) \) is quite far from the experimental value \( \left(8.41\times 10^{-4} eV \right) \); the large discrepancy is due to pair annihilation \( \left(e^{+}+e^{-} \rightarrow \gamma+\gamma\right) \), which contributes an extra \( (3 / 4) \Delta E \), and does not occur (of course) in ordinary hydrogen, muonic hydrogen, or muonium.

Admin
Solution

From Eq. 7.93 we see that \( \Delta E \propto\left(\frac{g}{m_{p} m_{e} a^{3}}\right) \); we want reduced mass in \( a \text {, but not in } m_{p} m_{e} \) (which come from Eq. 7.89); the notation in Eq. 7.97 obscures this point.

\( E_{ hf }^{1}=\frac{\mu_{0} g_{p} e^{2}}{3 \pi m_{p} m_{e} a^{3}}\left( S _{p} \cdot S _{e}\right) \) (7.93).

\( \mu _{p}=\frac{g_{p} e}{2 m_{p}} S _{p}, \quad \mu _{e}=-\frac{e}{m_{e}} S _{e} \) (7.89).

\( \Delta E=\frac{4 g_{p} \hbar^{4}}{3 m_{p} m_{e}^{2} c^{2} a^{4}}=5.88 \times 10^{-6} eV \) (7.97).

\( \text { (a) } g \text { and } m_{p} \text { are unchanged; } m_{e} \rightarrow m_{\mu}=207 m_{e}, \text { and } a \rightarrow a_{\mu} \text {. From Eq. 4.72, } a \propto 1 / m \text {, so } \)

\( \frac{a}{a_{\mu}}=\frac{m_{\mu}(\text { reduced })}{m_{e}}=\frac{m_{\mu} m_{p}}{m_{\mu}+m_{p}} \cdot \frac{1}{m_{e}}=\frac{207}{1+207\left(m_{e} / m_{p}\right)}=\frac{207}{1+207 \frac{\left(9.11 \times 10^{-31}\right)}{\left.1.67 \times 10^{-27}\right)}}=\frac{207}{1.11}=186 \).

\( a \equiv \frac{4 \pi \epsilon_{0} \hbar^{2}}{m_{e} e^{2}}=0.529 \times 10^{-10} m \) (4.72).

\( \Delta E=\left(5.88 \times 10^{-6} eV \right)(1 / 207)(186)^{3}=0.183 eV \).

(b) g : 5.59 → 2; \( m_{p} \rightarrow m_{e} ; \quad \frac{a}{a_{p}}=\frac{m_{p}(\text { reduced })}{m_{e}}=\frac{m_{e}^{2}}{m_{e}+m_{e}} \cdot \frac{1}{m_{e}}=\frac{1}{2} \).

\( \Delta E=\left(5.88 \times 10^{-6} eV \right)\left(\frac{2}{5.59}\right)\left(\frac{1.67 \times 10^{-27}}{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}\right)\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{3}=4.82 \times 10^{-4} eV \).

\( \text { (c) } g: 5.59 \rightarrow 2 ; \quad m_{p} \rightarrow m_{\mu} ; \quad \frac{a}{a_{m}}=\frac{m_{m}(\text { reduced })}{m_{e}}=\frac{m_{e} m_{\mu}}{m_{e}+m_{\mu}} \cdot \frac{1}{m_{e}}=\frac{207}{208} \text {. } \)

\( \Delta E=\left(5.88 \times 10^{-6}\right)\left(\frac{2}{5.59}\right)\left(\frac{1.67 \times 10^{-27}}{(207)\left(9.11 \times 10^{-31}\right)}\right)\left(\frac{207}{208}\right)^{3}=1.84 \times 10^{-5} eV \).
