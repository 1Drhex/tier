Guest Question.

A CPC has an aperture area of \(4m^2\) and a concentration ratio of 1.7. Estimate the collector efficiency given the following:
Total radiation = 850 W/\(m^2\) .
Diffuse to total radiation ratio = 0.12.
Receiver absorptivity = 0.87.
Receiver emissivity = 0.12.
Mirror reflectivity = 0.90.
Glass cover transmissivity = 0.90.
Collector heat loss coefficient = 2.5 W/\(m^2\)-K.
Circulating fluid = water.
Entering fluid temperature = 80°C.
Fluid flow rate = 0.015 kg/s.
Ambient temperature = 15°C.
Collector efficiency factor = 0.92.

Admin
Solution

The diffuse radiation correction factor,\( \gamma\) , is estimated from Eq. (3.92):

\(\gamma =1-\left\lgroup1-\frac{1}{C} \right\rgroup \frac{G_{D}}{G_{t}} =1-\left\lgroup1-\frac{1}{1.7} \right\rgroup 0.12=0.95\)

From Figure 3.34 for C = 1.7, the average number of reflections for a full CPC is n = 0.6. Therefore, from Eq. (3.94),

\(\tau _{CPC}=\rho ^n = 0.90^{0.6}=0.94\)

The absorber radiation is given by Eq. (3.91):

\(S=G_{t}\tau _{cover}\tau _{CPC}\alpha _{r}\gamma =850\times 0.90\times 0.94\times 0.87\times 0.95=594.3 W/m^2\)

The heat removal factor is estimated from Eq. (3.58):

\(F_{R}=\frac{\dot{m}c_{p} }{A_{c}U_{L}} \left[1-exp\left\lgroup-\frac{U_{L}F^{′}A_{c}}{\dot{m}c_{p}} \right\rgroup \right] =\frac{0.015\times 4180}{4\times 2.5} \left[1-exp\left\lgroup-\frac{2.5\times 0.92\times 4}{0.015\times 4180} \right\rgroup \right] =0.86\)

The receiver area is obtained from Eq. (3.77), C=\frac{A_{a}}{A_{r}} :

\(A_{r}=A_{a}/C=4/1.7=2.35 m^2\)

The useful energy gain can be estimated from Eq. (3.86):

\(Q_{u}=F_{R}[SA_{a}-A_{r}U_L(T_{i}-T_{a})]=0.86[594.3\times 4-2.35\times 2.5(80-15)]=1716 W\)

The collector efficiency is given by Eq. (3.85):

\(\eta =\frac{Q_{u}}{A_{a}G_{t}} =\frac{1716}{4\times 850} =0.504\) or 50.4%
