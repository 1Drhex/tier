Guest Question.

A device that is intended to gauge the length of parts is shown in Figure 12.12. The concept utilizes a differential screw, such that the rotation of knob A slides nut D until it is firmly pressed against part E. Nut D also has a pointer that can be used to determine the length of part E. The objective is to configure the system such that one rotation of knob A causes a 0.1-mm traverse of nut D. Select threads B and C to accomplish this requirement.

Admin
Solution

Use the following differential screw kinematics equation:

\(ΔR_{nut} = (L_B – L_C)Δθ_{screw}\)

\(0.1\ mm = (L_B – L_C) (1\ rev)\)

\((L_B – L_C ) = 0.1\ mm/rev\)

Several arrangements are possible. The standard threads listed in Table 12.2 are utilized. These have a single thread, so the lead and pitch are identical. Then two threads need to be selected that have a difference in pitch of 0.1 mm. Although a few options are feasible, arbitrarily select coarse pitch threads.

For thread B: M5 × 0.8

For thread C: M4 × 0.7
