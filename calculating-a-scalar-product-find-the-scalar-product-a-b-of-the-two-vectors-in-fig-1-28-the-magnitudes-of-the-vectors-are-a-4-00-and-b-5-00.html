CALCULATING A SCALAR PRODUCT

Find the scalar product \(\overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{A}} \cdot \overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{B}}\) of the two vectors in Fig. 1.28. The magnitudes of the vectors are A = 4.00 and B = 5.00.

Admin
Solution

IDENTIFY and SET UP: We can calculate the scalar product in two ways: using the magnitudes of the vectors and the angle between them (Eq. 1.16 : \(\overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{A}} \cdot \overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{B}}=A B \cos \phi=|\overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{A}}||\overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{B}}| \cos \phi\)), and using the components of the vectors (Eq. 1.19: \(\overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{A}} \cdot \overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{B}}=A_{x} B_{x}+A_{y} B_{y}+A_{z} B_{z}\)).
We’ll do it both ways, and the results will check each other.

EXECUTE: The angle between the two vectors \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) is Φ = 130.0° – 53.0° = 77.0°, so Eq. (1.16) gives us

\(\overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{A}} \cdot \overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{B}}=A B \cos \phi=(4.00)(5.00) \cos 77.0^{\circ}=4.50\).

To use Eq. (1.19), we must first find the components of the vectors.
The angles of \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) are given with respect to the +x-axis and are measured in the sense from the +x-axis to the +y-axis, so we can use:

Eqs. (1.5): \(\frac{A_{x}}{A}=\cos \theta \quad \text { and } \quad \frac{A_{y}}{A}=\sin \theta\) , \(A_{x}=A \cos \theta \quad \text { and } \quad A_{y}=A \sin \theta\)

 

\(A_{x}\)= (4.00) cos 53.0° = 2.407

 

\(A_{y}\)= (4.00) sin 53.0° = 3.195

 

\(B_{x}\)= (5.00) cos 130.0° = -3.214

 

\(B_{y}\)= (5.00) sin 130.0° = 3.830

 

As in , we keep an extra significant figure in the components and round at the end. Equation (1.19) now gives us

\(\overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{A}} \cdot \overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{B}}=A_{x} B_{x}+A_{y} B_{y}+A_{z} B_{z}\)

= (2.4072)(-3.214) + (3.195)(3.830) + (0)(0) = 4.50

EVALUATE: Both methods give the same result, as they should.
