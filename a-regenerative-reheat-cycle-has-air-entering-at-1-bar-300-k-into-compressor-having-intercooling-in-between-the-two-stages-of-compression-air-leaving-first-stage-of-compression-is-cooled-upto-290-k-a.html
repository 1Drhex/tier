Guest Question.

A regenerative-reheat cycle has air entering at 1 bar, 300 K into compressor having intercooling in between the two stages of compression. Air leaving first stage of compression is cooled upto 290 K at 4 bar pressure in intercooler and subsequently compressed upto 8 bar. Compressed air leaving second stage compressor is passed through a regenerator having effectiveness of 0.80. Subsequent combustion chamber yields 1300 K at inlet to turbine having expansion upto 4 bar and then reheated upto 1300 K before being expanded upto 1 bar. Exhaust from turbine is passed through regenerator before discharged out of cycle. For the fuel having heating value of 42000 kJ/kg determine fuel-air ratio in each combustion chamber, total turbine work and thermal efficiency. Consider compression and expansion to be isentropic and air as working fluid throughout the cycle.

Admin
Solution
\(T_{1} = 300 K, P_{1} = 1 bar, P_{2} = P_{3} = 4 bar, T_{3} = 290 K, T_{6} = 1300 K,\\P_{6} = P_{4} = 8 bar, T_{8} = 1300 K, P_{8} = 4 bar\\\text{For process 1-2,}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}} = \left\lgroup\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} \right\rgroup^{\frac{\gamma – 1}{\gamma } } ⇒ T_{2} = 445.79 K\\\text{For process 3-4,}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \frac{T_{4}}{T_{3}} = \left\lgroup\frac{P_{4}}{P_{3}} \right\rgroup^{\frac{\gamma – 1}{\gamma } } ⇒ T_{4} = 353.51 K\\\text{For process 6-7,}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \frac{T_{7}}{T_{6}} = \left\lgroup\frac{P_{7}}{P_{6}} \right\rgroup^{\frac{\gamma – 1}{\gamma } } = \left\lgroup\frac{4}{8} \right\rgroup^{\frac{1.4 – 1}{1.4 } } ⇒ T_{7} = 1066.43 K\\\text{For process 8-9,}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \frac{T_{9}}{T_{8}} = \left\lgroup\frac{P_{9}}{P_{8}} \right\rgroup^{\frac{\gamma – 1}{\gamma } } ⇒ T_{9} = 1300 × \left\lgroup\frac{1}{4} \right\rgroup^{\frac{1.4 – 1}{1.4 } } = 874.83 K\\ \begin{aligned}\text{In regenerator, effectiveness }&= 0.80 = \frac{T_{5} – T_{4}}{T_{9} – T_{4}} = \frac{T_{5} – 353.51}{(874.83 – 353.51)}\\⇒ \qquad \quad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad T_{5}&= 770.56 K\\\text{Compressor work per kg air, }W_{c}&= c_{p} (T_{2} – T_{1}) + c_{p} (T_{4} – T_{3})\\W_{c}&= 209.97 kJ/kg\\\text{Turbine work per kg air, }W_{T}&= c_{p} (T_{6} – T_{7}) + c_{p} (T_{8} – T_{9})\\W_{T}&= 660.85 kJ/kg\\\text{Heat added per kg air, }q_{add}&= c_{p} (T_{6} – T_{5}) + c_{p} (T_{8} – T_{7})\\q_{add}&= 765.45 kJ/kg\\\text{Total fuel required per kg of air}&= \frac{765.45}{42000} = 0.0182\\\text{Net work }W_{net}&= W_{T} – W_{C} = 450.88 kJ/kg\\\text{Cycle thermal efficiency, }η&= \frac{W_{net}}{q_{add}} = \frac{450.88}{765.45} = 0.5890 or 58.9\%\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Fuel required per kg air in combustion chamber 1,}&= \frac{c_{p}(T_{6} – T_{5})}{42000} = 0.0126\\\text{Fuel required per kg air in combustion chamber 2,}&= \frac{c_{p}(T_{8} – T_{7})}{42000} = 0.0056\\\text{Fuel-air ratio in two combustion chambers}&= \textbf{0.0126, 0.0056}\\\text{Total turbine work}&= \textbf{660.85 kJ/kg}\\\text{Cycle thermal efficiency}&= \textbf{58.9}\pmb{\%}\end{aligned}\\\)
