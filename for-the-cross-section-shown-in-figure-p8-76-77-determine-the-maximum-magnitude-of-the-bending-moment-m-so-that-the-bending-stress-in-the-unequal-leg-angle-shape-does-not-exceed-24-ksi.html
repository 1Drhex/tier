Guest Question.

For the cross section shown in Figure P8.76/77, determine the maximum magnitude of the bending moment M so that the bending stress in the unequal-leg angle shape does not exceed 24 ksi.

Admin
Solution

Section properties
Centroid location in y direction:

\(\begin{array}{lccccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}\text { Width } b \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Height } h \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left(\text { in. }^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} \text { (from bottom) } \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} A_{i} \\\left( in .^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { upright leg } & 0.375 & 4.000 & 1.5000 & 2.00 & 3.00 \\\text { bottom leg } & 2.625 & 0.375 & 0.9844 & 0.1875 & 0.18457 \\\hline & & & 2.4844 & & 3.18457\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{3.18457 in. ^{3}}{2.4844 in .^{2}} &=1.2818 in . \text{(from bottom of shape to centroid)} \\&=2.7182 in . \text{(from top of shape to centroid) } \end{aligned}\)

 

Centroid location in z direction:

\(\begin{array}{cccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left(\text { in. }^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}z_{i} \text { (from right edge) } \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}z_{i} A_{i} \\\left(\text { in. }^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { upright leg } & 1.5000 & 0.1875 & 0.2813 \\\text { bottom leg } & 0.9844 & 1.6875 & 1.6612 \\\hline & 2.4844 & & 1.94243\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{z}=\frac{\Sigma z_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{1.94243 in .^{3}}{2.4844 in .{ }^{2}} &=0.7818 in . \text{(from right edge of shape to centroid)}\\&=2.2182 in . \text{(from left edge of shape to centroid)}\end{aligned}\)

 

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

\(\begin{array}{ccccc}\text { Shape } & I_{C} & d=y_{i}-\bar{y} & d^{2} A & I_{C}+d^{2} A \\& \left( in ^{4}\right) & (\text { in. }) & \left( in .{ }^{4}\right) & \left( in .{ }^{4}\right) \\\hline \text { upright leg } & 2.000 & 0.7182 & 0.77372 & 2.7737 \\\text { bottom leg } & 0.011536 & -1.0943 & 1.17881 & 1.1903 \\\hline \text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)= &&&& 3.9640\end{array}\)

 

Moment of inertia about the y axis:

\(\begin{array}{lcccc}\text { Shape } & I_{C} & d=z_{i}-\bar{z} & d^{2} A & I_{C}+d^{2} A \\& \left(\text { in. }^{4}\right) & \text { (in.) } & \left(\text { in. }^{2}\right) & (\text { in. }^{ 4}) \\\hline \text { upright leg } & 0.017578 & -0.5943 & 0.52979 & 0.5474 \\\text { bottom leg } & 0.565247 & 0.9057 & 0.80750 & 1.3727 \\\hline & \text { Moment of inertia about the } y \text { axis }\left(\text { in } .^{4}\right)= &&& 1.9201\end{array}\)

Product of inertia about the centroidal axes:

\(\begin{array}{lcccccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}I_{y^{\prime} z^{\prime}} \\( in .^{4})\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{c} \\( in .)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}z_{c} \\( in .)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left( in. ^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{c} z_{c} A_{i} \\( in .^{4})\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{y z} \\\left( in .{}^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { upright leg } & 0 & 0.7182 & -0.5943 & 1.5000 & -0.6402 & -0.6402 \\\text { bottom leg } & 0 & -1.0943 & 0.9057 & 0.9844 & -0.9757 & -0.9757 \\\hline & & & & \text { Product of inertia }\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)= && -1.6159\end{array}\)

Orientation of neutral axis
Since the angle shape has no axis of symmetry, it is helpful to determine the orientation of the neutral axis from Eq. (8.23) before beginning the stress calculations:

\(\begin{aligned}\tan \beta &=\frac{M_{y} I_{z}+M_{z} I_{y z}}{M_{z} I_{y}+M_{y} I_{y z}}=\frac{(20 kip – in .)\left(-1.6159 in.^{4}\right)}{(20 kip – in .)\left(1.9201 in .{ }^{4}\right)}=-0.8416 \\& \therefore \beta=-40.08^{\circ} &\text { (i.e., } \left.40.08^{\circ} CCW \text { from }+z \text { axis }\right)\end{aligned}\)

Allowable moments based on maximum tension and compression bending stresses
Sketch the orientation of the neutral axis. By inspection, the points on the angle cross section that are farthest from the neutral axis are point H and the corner of the angle. To compute the normal stress at H, use (y, z) coordinates of y = 2.7182 in. and z = −0.4068 in.:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\left[\frac{-I_{y} y+I_{y z} z}{I_{y} I_{z}-I_{y z}^{2}}\right] M_{z}=\left[\frac{-\left(1.9201 in .{ }^{4}\right)(2.7182 in .)+\left(-1.6159 in .{ }^{4}\right)(-0.4068 in .)}{\left(1.9201 in .{ }^{4}\right)\left(3.9640 in. ^{4}\right)-\left(-1.6159 in. ^{4}\right)^{2}}\right] M_{z} \\&=\left[\frac{-4.5619 in .^{5}}{5.0001 in .{ }^{8}}\right] M_{z}=\left(-0.9124 in. ^{-3}\right) M_{z}\end{aligned}\)

Therefore, based on an allowable bending stress of 24 ksi at H, the maximum magnitude of \(M_{z}\) is:

\(\begin{aligned}&\left(0.9124 in. ^{-3}\right) M_{z} \leq 24 ksi \\&\therefore M_{z} \leq 26.3054 \text { kip-in. } (a)\end{aligned}\)

To compute the normal stress at the corner of the angle, use (y, z) coordinates of y = −1.2818 in. and z = −0.7818 in.

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\left[\frac{-I_{y} y+I_{y z} z}{I_{y} I_{z}-I_{y z}^{2}}\right] M_{z}=\left[\frac{-\left(1.9201 in. ^{4}\right)(-1.2818 in .)+\left(-1.6159 in .^{4}\right)(-0.7818 in .)}{\left(1.9201 in. ^{4}\right)\left(3.9640 in. ^{4}\right)-\left(-1.6159 in .^{4}\right)^{2}}\right] M_{z} \\&=\left[\frac{3.7245 in .^{5}}{5.0001 in. ^{8}}\right] M_{z}=\left(0.7449 in. ^{-3}\right) M_{z}\end{aligned}\)

Therefore, based on the bending stress at the corner of the angle, the maximum magnitude of \(M_{z}\) is:

\(\begin{aligned}&\left(0.7449 in. ^{-3}\right) M_{z} \leq 24 ksi \\&\therefore M_{z} \leq 32.2197 kip – in . (b)\end{aligned}\)

Maximum bending moment \(M_{z}\)
Compare the results in Eqs. (a) and (b) to find that the maximum bending moment that can be applied to the angle shape is:

\(M_{z} \leq 26.3 \text { kip-in. }\)
