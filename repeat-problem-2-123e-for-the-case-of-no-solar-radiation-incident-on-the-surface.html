Guest Question.

Repeat for the case of no solar radiation incident on the surface.

Admin
Solution

A large plane wall is subjected to a specified temperature on the left (inner) surface and heat loss by radiation to space on the right (outer) surface. The temperature of the right surface of the wall and the rate of heat transfer are to be determined when steady operating conditions are reached.

Assumptions 1 Steady operating conditions are reached. 2 Heat transfer is one-dimensional since the wall is large relative to its thickness, and the thermal conditions on both sides of the wall are uniform. 3 Thermal properties are constant. 4 There is no heat generation in the wall.

Properties The properties of the plate are given to be \( k = 1.2 Btu / h \cdot ft \cdot{ }^{\circ} F\) and \( \varepsilon=0.80\).

Analysis In steady operation, heat conduction through the wall must be equal to net heat transfer from the outer surface. Therefore, taking the outer surface temperature of the plate to be \( T_{2} \) (absolute, in R),

\( k A_{s} \frac{T_{1}-T_{2}}{L}=\varepsilon \sigma A_{s} T_{2}^{4} \)

 

Canceling the area A and substituting the known quantities,

\( \left(1.2 Btu / h \cdot ft \cdot{ }^{\circ} F \right) \frac{(520 R )-T_{2}}{0.5 ft }=0.8\left(0.1714 \times 10^{-8} Btu / h \cdot ft ^{2} \cdot R ^{4}\right) T_{2}^{4}\)

 

Solving for \( T_{2} \) gives the outer surface temperature to be \( T_{2}= 4 8 7 . 7 R\)

Then the rate of heat transfer through the wall becomes

\( \dot{q}=k \frac{T_{1}-T_{2}}{L}=\left(1.2 Btu / h \cdot ft \cdot{ }^{\circ} F \right) \frac{(520-487.7) R }{0.5 ft }=77.5 Btu / h \cdot ft ^{2}\) (per unit area)

 

Discussion The positive sign indicates that the direction of heat transfer is from the inside to the outside. Therefore, the structure is losing heat as expected.
