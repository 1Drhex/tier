Per-unit circuit: three-zone single-phase network

Three zones of a single-phase circuit are identified in Figure 3.10(a). The zones are connected by transformers \(T_{1}\) and \(T_{2} \), whose ratings are also shown. Using base values of 30 kVA and 240 volts in zone 1, draw the per-unit circuit and determine the per-unit impedances and the per-unit source voltage. Then calculate the load current both in per-unit and in amperes. Transformer winding resistances and shunt admittance branches are neglected.

Admin
Solution

First the base values in each zone are determined.\(S_{base}=30KVA \) is the same for the entire network. Also, \(V_{base1}=240\) volts, as specified for zone 1. When moving across a transformer, the voltage base is changed in proportion to the transformer voltage ratings. Thus,

\(V_{base2}=\left(\frac{480}{240} \right)\left(240\right)=480\) volts

and

\(V_{base3}=\left(\frac{115}{460} \right)\left(480\right)=120\) volts

The base impedances in zones 2 and 3 are

\(Z_{base2}=\frac{V^{2}_{base2} }{S_{base} }=\frac{480^{{2}} }{30,000}=7.86\) Ω

and

\(Z_{base3}=\frac{V^{2}_{base3} }{S_{base} } =\frac{120^{2} }{30,000} =0.48\) Ω

and the base current in zone 3 is

\(I_{base3}=\frac{S_{base} }{V_{base3} } =\frac{30,000}{120}=250\) A

Next, the per-unit circuit impedances are calculated using the system base values. Since \(S_{base}=30KVA \) is the same as the kVA rating of transformer \(T_{1}\) and \(V_{base1}=240\) volts is the same as the voltage rating of the zone 1 side of transformer \(T_{1}\), the per-unit leakage reactance of \(T_{1}\) is the same as its nameplate value \(X_{T1P.U.}=0.1\) per unit. However, the per-unit leakage reactance of transformer \(T_{2}\) must be converted from its nameplate rating to the system base. Using (3.3.11) and \(V_{base2}=480\) volts,

\(Z_{p.u.new}=Z_{p.u.old} \left(\frac{V_{baseold} }{V_{basenew} } \right) ^{2}\left(\frac{S_{basenew} }{S_{baseold}} \right) \) (3.3.11)

\(X_{T2P.u.} =\left(0.10\right)\left(\frac{460}{480} \right)^{2}\left(\frac{30,000}{20,000} \right) =0.1378\) per unit

Alternatively, using \(V_{base3} =120\) volts,

\(X_{T2P.u.} =\left(0.10\right)\left(\frac{115}{120} \right)^{2}\left(\frac{30,000}{20,000} \right) =0.1378 \) per unit

which gives the same result. The line, which is located in zone 2, has a per-unit reactance

\(X_{lineP.u.}=\frac{X_{line} }{Z_{base2 } }=\frac{2}{7.68} =0.2604 \) per unit

and the load, which is located in zone 3, has a per-unit impedance

\(Z_{loadP.u.}=\frac{Z_{load} }{Z_{base3 } }=\frac{0.9+j0.2}{0.48} =1.875+j0.4167 \) per unit

The per-unit circuit is shown in Figure 3.10(b), where the base values for each zone, per-unit impedances, and the per-unit source voltage are shown. The per-unit load current is then easily calculated from Figure 3.10(b) as follows:

\(I_{loadp.u.}=I_{sp.u.}=\frac{V_{sp.u.} }{j\left(X_{T1P.u.} +X_{linep.u.}+X_{T2P.u.} \right)+Z_{loadp.u.} }=\frac{0.9167\angle {0°} }{j\left(0.10+0.2604+0.1378\right)+\left(1.875+j0.4167\right) } =\frac{0.9167\angle {0°} }{1.875+j0.9149}=\frac{0.9167\angle {0°} }{2.086\angle {26.01°} }=0.4395\angle {-26.01°} \) per unit

The actual load current is

\(I_{load}=\left(I_{loadp.u.} \right)I_{base3}=\left(0.4395\angle {-26.01°} \right)\left(250\right)=109.9\angle {-26.01°} \) A

Note that the per-unit equivalent circuit of Figure 3.10(b) is relatively easy to analyze, since ideal transformer windings have been eliminated by proper selection of base values.
