Guest Question.

The copper cube (k =400 W/m C, ρ=8930 kg/\(m^{3}\), c=390 J/kg C) shown below is 2 cm by 2 cm by 2 cm. It is initially at 200 C in an oil bath. The cube is taken out of the bath and placed on an insulating board to cool by convection to the surrounding air. Assume the surrounding air is at 20 C and the convective coefficient is 10 W/\(m^{2}\) C.

(a) What is the temperature of the center of the cube after 45 min?
(b) How much heat (J) flows to the surrounding air during the 45 min?
(c) If the cooling continues, how long will it take for the cube to reach the air temperature

Admin
Solution

We will first determine the Biot number to determine if the lumped analysis is appropriate.

\(Bi_{lumped} = \frac{h(\frac{V}{A} )}{k} = \frac{(10)\frac{(0.02)^{3} }{(5)(0.02)^{2} } }{400} = 0.0001\)

(Please note that for the surface area, we have only included five of the six faces of the cube. One face is on the insulating board. This face does not convect to the air and we assume that there is no conduction to the board.)
The Biot number is much less that 0.1, so we can use the lumped analysis

(a) Using Eq. (4.8), we have

\(T(t)= T_{\infty } +(T_{i}-T_{\infty } )e^{-(\frac{hA}{ρcV} )t} \)

For this problem, \(T_{\infty }\) = 20 C.

\(T_{i }\) = 200 C.

h =10 W/ \(m^{2 }\) C.

A =5 \((0.02)^{2 }\)=0.002 \(m^{2 }\)

V =\((0.02)^{3 }\) =0.000008 \((0.02)^{3 }\)

ρ=8930 kg/ \(m^{3 }\)

c =390 J/kg C.

t =45 min ×60 s/min =2700 s

\((\frac{hA}{\rho cV}t )= (\frac{10(0.02)}{8930(390)(0.000008)}(2700) )= 1.938\)

So, from Eq. (4.8), we have

T(45 min)=20 + (200 -20)\(e^{-1.938}\)= 45.9 C

This is the temperature of the cube after 45 min. As we have a lumped system, all points in the cube will be at that temperature, including the center.

(b) Eq. (4.13) gives the heat transferred during time t.

Eq. (4.13):

\(Q= \rho cV (T_{i}-T_{\infty } )[1-e^{-(\frac{hA}{\rho cV} )t} ]\)

So, from Eq. (4.13), we have

\(Q=(8930)(390)(0.000008)(200-20)[1-e^{-1.938} ]= 4293 J\)

Therefore, 4293 J of heat will be transferred from the cube to the surrounding air during the 45 min process

(c) Eq. (4.8) gives the temperature of the cube after any amount of time. If we use this equation to see how long it will take to have the cube reach \(T_{\infty }\), the result is that it will take an infinite amount of time. How can this possibly be true?
