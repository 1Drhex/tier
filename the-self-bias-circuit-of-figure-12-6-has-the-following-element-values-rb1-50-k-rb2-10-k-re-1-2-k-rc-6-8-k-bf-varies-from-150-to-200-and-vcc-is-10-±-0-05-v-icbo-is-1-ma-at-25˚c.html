Guest Question.

The self-bias circuit of Figure 12.6 has the following element values: \(R_{B 1}= 50\)\( \mathrm{~K}, R_{B 2}=10 \mathrm{~K}, R_{E}=1.2 \mathrm{~K}, R_{C}=6.8 \mathrm{~K}, \beta_{F}\) varies from 150 to 200 , and \(V_{C C}\) is \(10 \pm 0.05\) V. \(I_{C B O}\) is \(1 \mu \mathrm{A}\) at \(25^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\). Calculate the collector current at \(25^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\), and plot the change in collector current for temperatures between 25 and \(100^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\). Assume \(V_{B E}\) and \(\beta_{F}\) at \(25^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\) are \(0.7 \mathrm{~V}\) and 150, respectively.

Admin
Solution

Equation (12.25), Equation (12.26), and Equation (12.30) can be used to calculate the collector current. At each temperature, the stability factors are calculated using Equation (12.37), Equation (12.39), Equation (12.48), and Equation (12.49). The changes in \(V_{BE}\) and \(I_{CBO}\) with temperature are obtained using Equation (12.32) and Equation (12.33), respectively. The change in IC for each temperature is calculated using Equation (12.36).

\(R_{B}=R_{B 1} \| R_{B 2}\) (12.25)

 

\(V_{B B}=I_{B} R_{B}+V_{B E}+I_{E} R_{E}\) (12.26)

 

\(V_{CE}=V_{CC}-I_{C}R_{C}-I_{E}R_{E}\) (12.30)

\(\Delta V_{B E} \cong-2\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right) \mathrm{mV}\) (12.32)

 

\( ΔI_{CBO}=I_{CBO2}-I_{CBO1}=I \\ =I_{C B O}\left[2^{\left(\left(T_{2}-25\right) / 10\right)}-1\right]\) (12.33)

\(\Delta I_{C}=S_{V} \Delta V_{B E}+S_{\beta} \Delta \beta_{F}+S_{I} \Delta I_{C B O}+S_{V C C} \Delta V_{C C}\) (12.36)

\(S_{V}=\frac{d I_{C}}{d V_{B E}}=-\frac{1}{R_{B} / \beta_{F}+R_{E}\left(\beta_{F}+1 / \beta_{F}\right)}\) (12.37)

\(S_{V C C}=\frac{d I_{C}}{d V_{C C}}=\frac{1}{R_{C}+R_{E} / \alpha_{F}}\) (12.39)

 

\(S_{I}=\frac{\partial I_{C}}{\partial I_{C B O}}=\frac{R_{B B}+R_{E}}{\left(R_{B B}+R_{E}\right) / \beta_{F}+R_{E}}\) (12.48)

\(S_{\beta}=\frac{\partial I_{\mathrm{C}}}{\partial \beta}=\frac{\left(R_{B}+R_{E}\right)\left[V_{B B}-V_{B E}+\left(R_{B}+R_{E}\right) I_{C B O}\right]}{\left(R_{B}+R_{E}+\beta R_{E}\right)^{2}}\) (12.49)
