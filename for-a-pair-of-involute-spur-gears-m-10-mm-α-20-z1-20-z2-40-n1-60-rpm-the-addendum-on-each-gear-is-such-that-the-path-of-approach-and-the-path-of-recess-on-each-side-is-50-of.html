Guest Question.

For a pair of involute spur gears, \( m=10 mm , \alpha=20^{\circ}, z_{1}=20, z_{2}=40, n_{1}=60 \) rpm. The addendum on each gear is such that the path of approach and the path of recess on each side is 50% of the maximum possible length. Determine the addendum for the pinion and the gear and the length of arc of contact.

Admin
Solution

\( r_{1}=m z_{1} / 2=10 \times 20 / 2=100 mm , r_{2}=m z_{2} / 2=10 \times 40 / 2=200 mm \).

Let the pinion be the driver.
Maximum possible length of approach \( =r_{1} \sin \alpha=100 \sin 20^{\circ}=34.2 mm \).

Actual length of approach \( =\left[r_{a 2}^{2}-r_{b 2}^{2}\right]^{0.5}-r_{2} \sin \alpha \).

\( =\left[r_{a 2}^{2}-\left(200 \cos 20^{\circ}\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5}-200 \sin 20^{\circ} \).

\( =\left[r_{a 2}^{2}-35321\right]^{0.5}-68.4=0.5 \times 34.2=17.1 \).

or \( r_{a 2}^{2}-35321=(85.5)^{2} \).

\( r_{a 2}=206.5 mm \).

\( h_{a 2}=206.5-200=6.05 mm \).

Maximum possible length of recess \( =r_{2} \sin \alpha \).

\( =200 \sin 20^{\circ}=68.4 mm \).

\( \text { Actual length of recess }=\left[r_{a l}^{2}-r_{b 1}^{2}\right]^{0.5}-r_{1} \sin \alpha \).

\( =\left[r_{a l}^{2}-\left(100 \cos 20^{\circ}\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5}-100 \sin 20^{\circ} \).

\( =\left[r_{a 1}^{2}-8830\right]^{0.5}-34.2=0.5 \times 68.4=34.2 \).

or \( r_{a 1}^{2}-8830=(68.4)^{2} \).

\( r_{a l }=116.2 mm \).

\( h_{a 1}=116.2-100=16.2 mm \).

\( \text { Arc of contact }=\frac{\text { Path of contact }}{\cos \alpha}=0.5\left(r_{1}+r_{2}\right) \frac{\sin \alpha}{\cos \alpha} \).

\( =0.5(100+200) \tan 20^{\circ} \).

\( =54.6 mm \).
