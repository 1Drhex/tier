Guest Question.

Design of headed stud reinforcement. Repeat , using headed stud reinforcement. The No. 5 (No. 16) bars will be used as negative flexural reinforcement. The yield strength is \(f_{yt}\) = 51,000 psi for studs.

Admin
Solution

The minimum height of the shear stud assembly equals the thickness of the slab less the cover over the rail, the cover over the reinforcement, and one-half the reinforcement bar diameter. Thus, from Fig 13.25b the minimum height is
\(7.5 − 0.75 − 0.75 − 0.5 \times 0.625\) = 5.68 in.

A stud height of 6 in. will be used, which is consistent with the effective depth and exceeds the minimum requirement. As in , the design shear strength of the concrete alone at the critical section from the face of the column based on an effective depth of 6 in., by the controlling Eq. (13.11a), is

\(V_c=4\lambda \sqrt{f_c^\prime}b_od\)
\(ϕ V_c = 0.75 \times 4 \times 1 \sqrt{4000} \times 72 \times 6\) = 82.0 kips

which is less than \(V_u\) = 120 kips, indicating that shear reinforcement is required. The maximum design strength allowed by the ACI Code when headed stud reinforcement is used is
\(ϕ V_n = 0.75 \times 8 \sqrt{4000} \times 72 \times 6\) = 163.9 kips

which is satisfactorily above the actual \(V_u\). The maximum concrete strength allowed by ACI in conjunction with headed shear studs is
\(ϕ V_c = 0.75 \times 3 \times 1 \sqrt{4000} \times 72 \times 6\) = 61.5 kips

The maximum spacing between the stud rails must be less than 2d, so two lines of studs are needed for a 12 in. square column; a center-to-center spacing of 11 in. will be used. The shear stress in the slab at the first critical section is approximately \(4.4 \sqrt{f_c^\prime}\), which is below \(ϕ 6 \sqrt{f_c^\prime} = 4.5 \sqrt{f_c^\prime}\), giving a maximum stud spacing of 0.75d. A spacing of 4.5 in., equal to the maximum, is selected. The area of the studs is found from Eq. (5.14a):

\(s=\frac{\phi A_v f_{yt} d}{V_u-\phi V_c}\)

 

\(A_v=\frac{(V_u-\phi V_c)s}{\phi f_{yt}d}=\frac{(120-61.5)\times4.5}{0.75\times51\times6}=1.15 in^2 \)

A peripheral line of studs contains eight studs, requiring a cross-sectional area of 0.14 \(in^2\) per stud, so 0.500 in. diameter studs with a cross-sectional area of 0.20 \(in^2\) per stud are selected.

The required perimeter of the second critical section, at which the concrete alone can carry the shear, is based on a maximum shear stress of \(ϕ 2 λ \sqrt{f_c^\prime}\). Thus,
\(ϕ V_c = 0.75 \times 2 \times 1 \sqrt{4000} \times b_o \times 6\) = 120,000 lb

from which the minimum perimeter \(b_o\) = 211 in. The first stud is placed at 2 in. \((\leqq d/2)\)a from the column face. Six studs at a spacing of 4.5 in. provide a minimum perimeter of 228 in.
