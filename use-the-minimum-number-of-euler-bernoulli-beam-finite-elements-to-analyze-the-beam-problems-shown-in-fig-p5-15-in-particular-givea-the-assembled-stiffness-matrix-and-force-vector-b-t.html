Guest Question.

Use the minimum number of Euler—Bernoulli beam finite elements to analyze the beam problems shown in Fig. P5.15. In particular, give:

(a) the assembled stiffness matrix and force vector;

(b) the specified global displacements and forces, and the equilibrium conditions;

(c) the condensed matrix equations for the primary unknowns (i.e., generalized forces) separately.

Admin
Solution

(a) For this problem, we have \([K^{1}]=[K^{2}]\) and \(\left \{ f^{2} \right \}=\left \{ 0 \right \}\). The assembled set of equations are

\(\frac{2EI}{L^{3}}\begin{bmatrix}6 &-3L &-6 &-3L &0 &0 \\ -3L &3L^{2} &3L &L^{2} &0 &0 \\ -6 &3L &6+6 &3L-3L &-6 &-3L \\ -3L &L^{2} &3L-3L &2L^{2}+2L^{2} &3L &L^{2} \\ 0 & 0 &-6 &3L &6 &3L \\ 0&0 &-3L &L^{2} &3L &2L^{2} \end{bmatrix}\begin{Bmatrix}U_{1}\\ U_{2}\\ U_{3}\\ U_{4}\\ U_{5}\\ U_{6}\end{Bmatrix}\)

 

= \(\begin{Bmatrix}Q_{1}^{1}\\ Q_{2}^{1}\\ Q_{3}^{1}+Q_{1}^{2}\\ Q_{4}^{1}+Q_{2}^{2}\\ Q_{3}^{2}\\ Q_{4}^{2}\end{Bmatrix}+\frac{q_{0}L}{12}\begin{Bmatrix}6\\ -L\\ 6+0\\ L+0\\ 0\\ 0\end{Bmatrix}\) (1)

 

where L = 5 m, EI = 2 × \(10^{6}\) N-\(m^{2}\) and \(q_{0}\) = 1, 000 N/m.

(b) The specified generalized displacements and forces are

\(U_{1}=U_{2}=U_{3}=0\); \(Q_{4}^{1}+Q_{2}^{2}=-M_{0}\) , \(Q_{3}^{2}=F_{0}-kU_{5}\) , \(Q_{4}^{2}=0\) (2)

(c) The condensed equations for the generalized displacements are,

\(\frac{2EI}{L^{3}}\begin{bmatrix}4L^{2} &3L &L^{2} \\ 3L &6 &3L \\ L^{2} &3L &2L^{2} \end{bmatrix}\begin{Bmatrix}U_{4}\\ U_{5}\\ U_{6}\end{Bmatrix}=-\frac{q_{0}L}{12}\begin{Bmatrix}L\\ 0\\ 0\end{Bmatrix}+\begin{Bmatrix}-M_{0}\\ F_{0}-kU_{5}\\ 0\end{Bmatrix}\) (3)

or

\(\frac{2EI}{L^{3}}\begin{bmatrix}4L^{2} &3L &L^{2} \\ 3L &6+\mu &3L \\ L^{2} &3L &2L^{2} \end{bmatrix}\begin{Bmatrix}U_{4}\\ U_{5}\\ U_{6}\end{Bmatrix}=\frac{q_{0}L}{12}\begin{Bmatrix}L\\ 0\\ 0\end{Bmatrix}+\begin{Bmatrix}-M_{0}\\ F_{0}\\ 0\end{Bmatrix}\) (4)

where \(\mu =kL^{3}/2EI\). Using the given values of the parameters

L = 5, \(q_{0}\) = 1, 000, \(M_{0}\) = -1,250, \(F_{0}\) = 2,500, EI = 2 × \(10^{6}\), k = \(10^{-4}\)EI

we obtain the solution,

\(U_{4}\) = -0.7237 × \(10^{-4}\), \(U_{5}\) = 0.0879 × \(10^{-2}\)m, \(U_{6}\) = -0.2275 × \(10^{-3}\) (5)

The condensed equations for the generalized forces are given by

\(\begin{Bmatrix}Q_{1}^{1}\\ Q_{2}^{1}\\ Q_{3}^{1}+Q_{1}^{2}\end{Bmatrix}=\frac{2EI}{L^{3}}\begin{bmatrix}-3L &0 &0 \\ L^{2} &0 &0 \\ 0 &-6 &-3L \end{bmatrix}\begin{Bmatrix}U_{4}\\ U_{5}\\ U_{6}\end{Bmatrix}-\frac{q_{0}L}{12}\begin{Bmatrix}6\\ -L\\ 6\end{Bmatrix}\) (6)

The bending moment at a point, for example at x = 7.5 m or \(\bar{x}\) = 2.5m, can be computed from (note that \(U_{3}\) = 0)

\(M^{c}=EI\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} w}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}|_{x=7.5}=EI\sum_{i=1}^{4}u_{i}^{2}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} \phi _{i}^{2}}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}|_{x=7.5}\)

= \(EI\left ( U_{3}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} \phi _{1}^{2}}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}+U_{4}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} \phi _{2}^{2}}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}+U_{5}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} \phi _{3}^{2}}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}}+U_{6}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} \phi _{4}^{2}}{\mathrm{d} x^{2}} \right )|_{x=7.5}\)

= \(EI\left ( U_{3}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} \phi _{1}^{2}}{\mathrm{d} \bar{x}^{2}}+U_{4}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} \phi _{2}^{2}}{\mathrm{d} \bar{x}^{2}}+U_{5}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} \phi _{3}^{2}}{\mathrm{d} \bar{x}^{2}}+U_{6}\frac{\mathrm{d^{2}} \phi _{4}^{2}}{\mathrm{d} \bar{x}^{2}} \right )|_{\bar{x}=2.5}\)

= 6,206 N-m (7)
