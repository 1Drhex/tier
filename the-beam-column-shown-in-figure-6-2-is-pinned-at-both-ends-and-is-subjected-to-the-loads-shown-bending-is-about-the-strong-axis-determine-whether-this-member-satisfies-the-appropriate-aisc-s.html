Guest Question.

The beam–column shown in Figure 6.2 is pinned at both ends and is subjected to the loads shown. Bending is about the strong axis. Determine whether this member satisfies the appropriate AISC Specification interaction equation.

Admin
Solution

From the column load tables, the axial compressive design strength of a W10 × 49 with \(F_{y} = 50\) ksi and an effective length of \(K_{y}L = 1.0 × 17 = 17\) feet is

\(\phi_{c}P_{n} = 405\) kips

Since bending is about the strong axis, the design moment, \(\phi_{b}M_{n}\), for \(C_{b} = 1.0\) can be obtained from the beam design charts in Part 3 of the Manual.

For an unbraced length \(L_{b} = 17\) ft,

\(\phi_{b}M_{n} = 197\) ft-kips

For the end conditions and loading of this problem, \(C_{b} = 1.32\) (see Figure 5.15c).

For \(C_{b }= 1.32\), the design strength is

\(\phi_{b}M_{n} = C_{b} × 197 = 1.32(197) = 260\) ft-kips

This moment is larger than \(\phi_{b}M_{p} = 226.5\) ft-kips (also obtained from the beam design charts), so the design moment must be limited to \(\phi_{b}M_{p}\). Therefore,

\(\phi_{b}M_{n} = 226.5\) ft-kips

Factored loads:

\(P_{u} = 1.2P_{D} + 1.6P_{L} = 1.2(35) + 1.6(99) = 200.4\) kips

\(Q_{u} = 1.2Q_{D} + 1.6Q_{L} = 1.2(5) + 1.6(12) = 25.2\) kips

The maximum bending moment occurs at midheight, so

\(M_{u}=\frac{25.2(17)}{4}=107.1\) ft-kips

Determine which interaction equation controls:

\(\frac{P_{u}}{\phi_{c}P_{n}}=\frac{200.4}{405}=0.4948>0.2\) \(\therefore\) Use Equation 6.3 (AISC Eq. H1-1a).

 

\(\frac{P_{u}}{\phi_{c}P_{n}}+\frac{8}{9}(\frac{M_{ux}}{\phi_{b}M_{nx}}+\frac{M_{uy}}{\phi_{b}M_{ny}})=\frac{200.4}{405}+\frac{8}{9}(\frac{107.1}{226.5}+0)=0.915<1.0\) (OK)

This member satisfies the AISC Specification.

From the column load tables, the allowable compressive strength of a W10 × 49 with \(F_{y }= 50\) ksi and \(K_{y}L = 1.0 × 17 = 17\) feet is

\(\frac{P_{n}}{Ω_{c}}=270\) kips

From the design charts in Part 3 of the Manual, for \(L_{b} = 17\) ft and \(C_{b} = 1.0\),

\(\frac{M_{n}}{Ω_{b}}=131\) ft-kips

From Figure 5.15c, \(C_{b} = 1.32\). For \(C_{b} = 1.32\),

\(\frac{M_{n}}{Ω_{b}}=C_{b}×131=1.32(131)=172.9\) ft-kips

This is larger than \(M_{p}/Ω_{b} = 151\) ft-kips, so the allowable moment must be limited to \(M_{p}/Ω_{b}\). Therefore,

\(\frac{M_{n}}{Ω_{b}}=151\) ft-kips

The total axial compressive load is

\(P_{a} = P_{D} + P_{L} = 35 + 99 = 134\) kips

The total transverse load is

\(Q_{a} = Q_{D} + Q_{L} = 5 + 12 = 17\) kips

The maximum bending moment is at midheight

\(M_{a}=\frac{17(17)}{4}=72.25\) ft-kips

Determine which interaction equation controls:

\(\frac{P_{a}}{P_{n}/Ω_{c}}=\frac{134}{270}=0.4963>0.2\) \(\therefore\) Use Equation 6.5 (AISC Equation H1-1a).

 

\(\frac{P_{a}}{P_{n}/Ω_{c}}+\frac{8}{9}(\frac{M_{ax}}{M_{nx}/Ω_{b}}+\frac{M_{ay}}{M_{ny}/Ω_{b}})=\frac{134}{270}+\frac{8}{9}(\frac{72.25}{151}+0)=0.922<1.0\) (OK)

This member satisfies the AISC Specification.
