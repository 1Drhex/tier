An insulated piston/cylinder contains 0.1 \(m^3\) air at 250 kPa, 300 K and it maintains constant pressure. More air flows in through a valve from a line at 300 kPa, 400 K so the volume increases 60%. Use constant specific heats to solve for the final temperature and the total entropy generation.

Admin
Solution

C.V. Piston/cylinder volume, no heat transfer.

Continuity Eq.6.15: \(m _{2}- m _{1}= m _{ i }\)

Energy Eq.6.16: \(m _{2} u _{2}- m _{1} u _{1}= m _{ i } h _{ i }-{ }_{1} W _{2}\)

Entropy Eq.7.12: \(m _{2} s _{2}- m _{1} s _{1}= m _{ i } s _{ i }+\int dQ / T +{ }_{1} S _{2 gen} \)

Process Eq.: \({ }_{1} Q _{2}=0, V _{2}=1.6 V _{1} ; P = C\),

\({ }_{1} W _{2}= P \left( V _{2}- V _{1}\right)= P _{1}(1.6-1) V _{1}=0.6 P _{1} V _{1}=0.6 m _{1} RT _{1}\)

\(m _{1}= P _{1} V _{1} / RT _{1}=\frac{250 \times 0.1}{0.287 \times 300}=0.2904 kg\);

Write \(h _{ i }= u _{ i }+ RT _{ i }\) then the energy equation contains only uâ€™s so we can substitute the u differences with \(C _{ v 0} \Delta T\) and get

\(\begin{array}{l}m _{2} C _{ v 0} T _{2}- m _{1} C _{ v 0} T _{1}= m _{ i } C _{ v 0} T _{ i }+ m _{ i } RT _{ i }-0.6 m _{1} RT _{1} \\m _{2} T _{2}= m _{1} T _{1}+ m _{ i } T _{ i }\left[1+\left( R / C _{ v 0}\right)\right]-0.6 m _{1} T _{1} R / C _{ v 0}\end{array}\)

 

In this equation \(m _{2}, T _{2} \text { and } m _{ i }\) are unknows however we also have

\(m _{2} T _{2}= P _{2} V _{2} / R =1.6 P _{1} V _{1} / R =1.6 m _{1} T _{1}\)

\(\begin{array}{l}m _{ i } T _{ i }\left[1+\left( R / C _{ v 0}\right)\right]=1.6 m _{1} T _{1}- m _{1} T _{1}+0.6 m _{1} T _{1} R / C _{ v 0} \\m _{ i } T _{ i }\left[1+\left( R / C _{ v 0}\right)\right]=0.6\left[1+ R / C _{ v 0}\right] m _{1} T _{1} \\m _{ i }=0.6 m _{1}\left( T _{1} / T _{ i }\right)=0.6 \times 0.2904(300 / 400)=0.13068 kg \\m _{2}= m _{ i }+ m _{1}=0.42108 kg ; T _{2}=1.6 \left( m _{1} / m _{2}\right) T _{1}= 3 3 1 K\end{array}\)
