Guest Question.

Design of beam bearing detail. A precast beam must be designed to resist a support reaction, at factored loads, of \(V_u\) = 100 kips applied to a \(3 \times 3\) steel angle, as shown in Fig. 5.25. In lieu of a calculated value, a horizontal force \(N_u\), owing to restrained volume change, will be assumed to be 20 percent of the vertical reaction, or 20 kips. Determine the required auxiliary reinforcement, using steel of yield strength \(f_y\) = 60,000 psi. Concrete strength \(f_c^{\prime}\) = 5000 psi.

Admin
Solution

A potential crack will be assumed at 20°, initiating at a point 4 in. from the end of the beam, as shown in Fig. 5.25a. The total required steel \(A_{vf}\) is the sum of that required to resist the resultant of \(V_u\) and \(N_u\) acting parallel to the cracks = \(V_u \cos 20° + N_u \sin 20°\). Equation (5.35) is modified accordingly:

\(A_{vf}=\frac{V_u}{\phi \mu f_y} \)

 

\(A_{vf}=\frac{V_u \cos 20^\circ +N_u\sin 20^\circ }{\phi \mu f_y} \)

 

\( =\frac{100\times0.940+20\times0.340}{0.75\times1.4\times60}=\frac{101 kips}{63 ksi} \)

 

\( = 1.60 in^2\)

The net compression normal to the potential crack would be no less than \(V_u \sin 20° − N_u \cos 20°\) = 15.4 kips. This could be counted upon to reduce the required shear-friction steel, according to the ACI Code, but it will be discounted conservatively here. Four No. 6 (No. 19) bars will be used, providing an area of 1.76 \(in^2\). They will be welded to the 3 × 3 angle and will extend into the beam a sufficient distance to develop the yield strength of the bars. According to the ACI Code, the development length for a No. 6 (No. 19) bar is 26 in., 32 in. without the \(ψ_s\) factor (see Chapter 6). Considering the uncertainty of the exact crack location, the bars will be extended 32 in. into the beam as shown in Fig. 5.25a. The bars will be placed at an angle of 15° with the bottom face of the member. For the crack oriented at an angle of 20°, as assumed, the area of the crack is

\(A_c=16\left(\frac{4}{\sin 20^\circ } \right) =187 in^2\)

Thus, according to the ACI Code, the maximum nominal shear strength of the surface is not to exceed \(V_n = 0.2f_c^{\prime} A_c\) = 187 kips, \(V_n = (480 + 0.08 f_c^{\prime})A_c\) = 165 kips, or \(V_n = 1600 A_c\) = 299 kips. The maximum design strength to be used is \(ϕ V_n = 0.75 \times 165 = 124 kips\). As calculated earlier, the applied shear on the interface at factored loads is

\(V_u = 100 \cos 20° + 20 \sin 20°\) = 101 kips

and so the design is judged satisfactory to this point.
A second possible crack must be considered, as shown in Fig. 5.25b, resulting from the tendency of the entire anchorage weldment to pull horizontally out of the beam.
The required steel area \(A_{sh}\) and the concrete shear stress will be calculated based on the development of the full yield tension in the bars \(A_{vf}\). (Note that the factor ϕ need not be used here because it has already been introduced in computing \(A_{vf}\).)

\(A_{sh}=\frac{A_{vf}f_y \cos 15^\circ }{\mu f_y} \)

 

\( =\frac{1.76\times0.966}{1.4} \)

 

\( =1.21 in^2\)

Four No. 4 (No. 13) hoops will be used, providing an area of 1.60 \(in^2\).
The maximum shear force that can be transferred, according to the ACI Code limits, will be based conservatively on a horizontal plane 32 in. long. No strength reduction factor need be included in the calculation of this maximum value because it was already introduced in determining the steel area \(A_{vf}\) by which the shear force is applied. Accordingly,

\(V_n ≤ (480 + 0.08 f_c^{\prime}) \times 16 \times 32 = 451 kips \)

The maximum shear force that could be applied in the given instance is the value used to calculate \(A_{sh}\),
\(V_u = 1.76 \times 60 \cos 15° = 102 kips \)

which is well below the specified maximum.
The first hoop will be placed 2 in. from the end of the member, with the others spaced at 8 in., as shown in Fig. 5.25c. Also shown in Fig. 5.25d are four No. 5 (No. 16) bars that will provide anchorage for the hoop steel.
