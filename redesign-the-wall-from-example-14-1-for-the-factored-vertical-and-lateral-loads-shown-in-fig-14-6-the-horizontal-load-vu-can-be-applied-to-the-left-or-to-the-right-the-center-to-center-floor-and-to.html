Guest Question.

Redesign the wall from for the factored vertical and lateral loads shown in Fig. 14.6. The horizontal load \(V_u\) can be applied to the left or to the right. The center-to-center floor and total wall height \(h_w = ℓ_c = 12\) ft-6 in., \(f_c^\prime\) = 4000 psi, and \(f_y\) = 60,000 psi.

Admin
Solution

Assume a wall thickness h = 12 in. giving a wall dead load of \(w_d = h/12 \times 150 pcf \times h_w = 12/12 \times 150 \times 12.5 = 1875 plf\). The critical loads occur at the bottom corner of the wall. Thus, the loads on the wall are

\(P_u = (1.2 \times w_d + w_u ) ℓ_w = (1.2 \times 1875 + 15,000) \times 12/1000 = 207\) kips
\(M_u = 96 kip \times 12.5 ft\) = 1200 kip-ft
\(V_u\) = 96 kips

Based on the very similar axial load and the use of a wall thickness twice that used in , the out-of-plane effects are not of concern.

For the in-plane effects, the axial load 207 kips is less than \(0.10 f_c^\prime A_g = 0.10 \times 4000 \times 12 \times 12 \times 12/1000\) = 691 kips, allowing the wall to be treated as a cantilever beam for the purposes of calculating flexural reinforcement. In accordance with ACI Code 11.5.4, the effective depth d may be taken as 0.8\(ℓ_w\) = 9.6 ft = 115 in.

Using the design aids in Appendix A,

\(R=\frac{M_u}{\phi f_c^\prime bh^2}=\frac{1200\times12,000}{0.85\times4000\times12\times115.4^2}=0.334 \)

giving ρ = 0.006 from Table A.5b and a required area of reinforcement \(A_s = 0.006 bd = 0.006 \times 12 \times 115.2 = 8.29 in^2\). This can be satisfied using nine No. 9 (No. 29) bars placed at each end of the wall.

The longitudinal bars require ties with a minimum spacing equal to the lesser of 48 tie diameters, 12 longitudinal bar diameters, or the wall thickness. In this instance, 12 in. is the maximum tie spacing using No. 3 (No. 10) ties.

The factored shear on the wall \(V_u\) = 96 kips. The shear capacity of the concrete alone is \(ϕ V_c = ϕ 2 \sqrt{f_c^\prime} bd = 0.75 \times 2 \times \sqrt{4000} \times 12 \times 115/1000\) = 131 kips. The factored shear is, thus, greater than \(ϕ V_c /2\) and less than \(ϕ V_c\), meaning that, in accordance with ACI Code 11.6.2, \(ρ_t \geq 0.0025\) and Eq. (14.2) must be used to calculate \(ρ_ℓ\). As pointed out in Section 14.2c, when using Eq. (14.2) with \(ρ_t\) = 0.0025, \(ρ_ℓ\) = 0.0025. Thus, the minimum required wall reinforcement in both the horizontal and the vertical directions is \(0.0025 \times h \times 12\) in./ft = \(0.0025 \times 12 \times 12\) in./ft = 0.36 \(in^2/ft\). The wall is greater than 10 in. thick; consequently, reinforcement must be placed in two layers. The maximum reinforcement spacing is 18 in. or 3 h, with 18 in. being the controlling value. Using No. 5 (No. 16) bars placed at 18 in. in both directions near each face gives 0.41 \(in^2/ft\), exceeding the minimum requirements. Final detailing of the wall is shown in Fig. 14.7.

\(ρ_ℓ \geq 0.0025 + 0.5(2.5 − h_w/ℓ_w )(ρ_t − 0.0025)\) (14.2)

Lastly, the footing must be designed to develop the shear wall reinforcement and prevent the wall from sliding or overturning.
