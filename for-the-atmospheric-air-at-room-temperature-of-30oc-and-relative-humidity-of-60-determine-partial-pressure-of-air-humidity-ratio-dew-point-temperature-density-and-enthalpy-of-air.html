Guest Question.

For the atmospheric air at room temperature of 30ºC and relative humidity of 60% determine partial pressure of air, humidity ratio, dew point temperature, density and enthalpy of air.

Admin
Solution

\(\text{At 30ºC from steam table, saturation pressure, }p_{v, sat} = 0.0425 bar\\\begin{aligned}\text{Partial pressure of vapour}&= \text{Relative humidity} × p_{v, sat}\\&= 0.6 × 0.0425 = 0.0255 bar\\\text{Partial pressure of air}&= \text{Total pressure of mixture – Partial pressure of vapour}\\&= 1.013 – 0.0255\\&= 0.9875 bar\\\text{Partial pressure of air}&= \textbf{0.9875 bar}\\\text{Humidity ratio, }ω&= 0.622 \frac{p_{v}}{p – p_{v}}\\&= 0.622 \left\lgroup\frac{0.0255}{1.013 – 0.0255} \right\rgroup \\ω&= \text{0.01606 kg/kg of dry air.}\\\text{Humidity ratio}&= \textbf{0.01606 kg/kg of dry air}\end{aligned}\\\)Dew point temperature may be seen from the steam table. The saturation temperature corresponding to the partial pressure of vapour is 0.0255 bar. Dew point temperature can be approximated as 21.4ºC by interpolation.\(\\\begin{aligned} \text{Dew point temperatrure}&= \textbf{21.4ºC}\\\text{Density of mixture}&= \text{Density of air }(ρ_{a}) + \text{Density of vapour }(ρ_{v})\\&= ρ_{a} + ρ_{v}\\&= ρ_{a} + ω · ρ_{a}\\&= ρ_{a} (1 + ω)\\&= \frac{P_{a}}{RT_{a}} (1 + 0.01606)\\&= \frac{1.013 × 10^{2} × 1.01606}{0.287 × 303}\\&= 1.1835 kg/m^{3}\\\text{Density}&= \textbf{1.1835 kg/m}^{3}\\\text{Enthalpy of mixture, }h&= C_{p} · T + ω (h_{\text{g at 30ºC}} + 1.860 (30 – 21.4))\\&= (1.005 × 30) + (0.01606 × (2540.1 + 1.86 × 8.6))\\&= \text{71.2 kJ/kg of dry air}\\\text{Enthalpy of mixture}&= \textbf{71.2 kJ/kg }\text{of dry air}\end{aligned}\)
