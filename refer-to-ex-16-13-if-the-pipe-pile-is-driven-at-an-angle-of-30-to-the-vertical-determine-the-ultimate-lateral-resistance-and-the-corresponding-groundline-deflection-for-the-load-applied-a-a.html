Guest Question.

Refer to . If the pipe pile is driven at an angle of 30° to the vertical, determine the ultimate lateral resistance and the corresponding groundline deflection for the load applied (a) against batter, and (b) in the direction of batter.

In both the cases the load is applied normal to the pile axis.

All the other data given in remain the same.

Admin
Solution

From , the expression for \(n_{h}\) for vertical pile is

 

\(n_{h}=n_{h}^{o}=\frac{204 \times 10^{4}}{P_{i}} kN / m ^{3}\) (a)

+ 30° Batter pile

 

From Fig. 16.20 \(\frac{n_{h}^{+b}}{n_{h}^{o}}=0.1 \text { and } n=2\) (b)

 

FromEq.(16.14b) \(T=T_{b}=\frac{E I}{n_{h}^{b}}=\frac{1}{n+4}=\frac{E I}{n_{h}^{b}}^{\frac{1}{2+4}}=\frac{E I}{n_{h}^{b}}^{\frac{1}{6}}\) (c)

 

\(T=\left[\frac{E I}{n_{h}}\right]^{\frac{1}{n+4}}\) (6.14b)

 

Determination of \(P_{u}\)

Stepl

Assume \(P_{e}=P_{t}=500 kN\).

Following the Step 1 in , and using Eq. (a) above

 

\(n_{h}^{o}=4,083 kN / m ^{3}, \text { hence } n_{h}^{+b}=4083 \times 0.1 \approx 408 kN / m ^{3}\)

 

\(n_{h}^{o}=4,083 kN / m ^{3}, \text { hence } n_{h}^{+b}=4083 \times 0.1 \approx 408 kN / m ^{3}\)

 

Form Eq (c), \(T_{b}=\frac{43.5 \times 10^{4}}{408}^{\frac{1}{6}}=3.2 m\)

 

As before, \(M_{\max }=0.77 P_{t} T_{b}=0.77 \times 500 \times 3.2=1232 kN – m <M_{y}\)

 

Step 2

Try \(P_{t}=1,000 kN\)

Proceeding in the same way as given in Step 1 we have \(T_{b}=3.59 m , M_{ max }=2764\) kN-m which is more than \(M_{y} . \text { The actual } P_{u}\) is

 

\(P_{u}=500+(1000-500) \times \frac{(2349-1232)}{(2764-1232)}=865 kN\)

 

Step 3

As before the corresponding \(T_{b} \text { for } P_{u}=865 kN \text { is } 3.5\) m.

Step 4

The groundline deflection is

 

\(y_{g}^{+b}=\frac{2.43 \times 865 \times(3.5)^{3}}{43.5 \times 10^{4}}=0.2072 m =20.72 cm\)

 

-30° Batter pile

 

From Fig. 16.20, \(\frac{n_{h}^{-b}}{n_{h}^{o}}=2.2 \text { and } n=1.0\)

 

and \(T_{b}=\frac{E I}{n_{h}^{-b}}^{\frac{1}{n+4}}=\frac{E I}{n_{h}^{-b}}^{\frac{1}{1+4}}=\frac{E I}{n_{h}^{-b}}^{\frac{1}{5}}\)

 

Determination of \(P_{u}\)

Step 1

Try \(P_{t}=1000\)

From Eq. (a) \(n_{h}^{o}=2040 kN / m ^{3} \text { and from Eq. (d) } n_{h}^{-b}=2.2 \times 2040=4488 kN / m ^{3}\)

Now from Eq. (e) \(T_{b}=2.5 m\)

As before \(M_{\max }=0.77 \times 1000 \times 2.5=1925 kN – m\)

which is less than \(M_{y}=2349 kN – m\)

Step 2

Try \(P_{t}=1,500 kN\)

Proceeding as in Step 1, \(T_{b}=2.71 m , \text { and } M_{\max }=0.77 \times 1500 \times 2.71=3130\) kN-m which is greater than \(M_{y}\).

Step 3

The actual value of \(P_{u}\) is therefore

 

\(P_{u}=1000+(1500-1000) \times \frac{(2349-1925)}{(2764-1925)}=1253 kN\)

 

Step 4

Groundline deflection

 

\(T_{b}=2.58 m \text { for } P_{u}=1253 kN\)

 

Now \(y_{g}^{-b}=\frac{2.43 \times 1176 \times(2.58)^{3}}{43.5 \times 10^{4}}=0.1202 m =12.0 cm\)

 

The above calculations indicate that the negative batter piles are more resistant to lateral loads than vertical or positive batter piles. Besides, the groundline deflections of the negative batter piles are less than the vertical and corresponding positive batter piles.
