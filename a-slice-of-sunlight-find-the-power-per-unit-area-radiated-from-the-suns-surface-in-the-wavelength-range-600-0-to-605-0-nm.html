Guest Question.

A SLICE OF SUNLIGHT

Find the power per unit area radiated from the sun’s surface in the wavelength range 600.0 to 605.0 nm.

Admin
Solution

IDENTIFY and SET UP:

This question concerns the power emitted by a blackbody over a narrow range of wavelengths, and so involves the spectral emittance \(I(λ)\) given by the Planck radiation law, Eq. (39.24). This requires that we find the area under the \(I(λ)\) curve between 600.0 and 605.0 nm. We’ll approximate this area as the product of the height of the curve at the median wavelength λ = 602.5 nm and the width of the interval, ∆λ = 5.0 nm. From , \(T\) = 5800 K.

\(I(\lambda)=\frac{2 \pi h c^{2}}{\lambda^{5}\left(e^{h c / \lambda k T}-1\right)}\) (39.24)

EXECUTE:

To obtain the height of the \(I(λ)\) curve at λ = 602.5 nm = 6.025 × \(10^{-7}\) m, we first evaluate the quantity \(h c / \lambda k T\) in Eq. (39.24) and then substitute the result into Eq. (39.24):

\(\frac{h c}{\lambda k T}=\frac{\left(6.626 \times 10^{-34} \mathrm{~J} \cdot \mathrm{s}\right)\left(2.998 \times 10^{8} \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\right)}{\left(6.025 \times 10^{-7} \mathrm{~m}\right)\left(1.381 \times 10^{-23} \mathrm{~J} / \mathrm{K}\right)(5800 \mathrm{~K})}=4.116\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}I(\lambda) &=\frac{2 \pi\left(6.626 \times 10^{-34} \mathrm{~J} \cdot\mathrm{s}\right)\left(2.998 \times 10^{8} \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\right)^{2}}{\left(6.025 \times 10^{-7} \mathrm{~m}\right)^{5}\left(e^{4.116}-1\right)} \\&=7.81\times10^{13} \mathrm{~W} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\end{aligned}\)

The intensity in the 5.0-nm range from 600.0 to 605.0 nm is then approximately

\(\begin{aligned}I(\lambda) \Delta \lambda &=\left(7.81 \times 10^{13} \mathrm{~W} /\mathrm{m}^{3}\right)\left(5.0 \times 10^{-9} \mathrm{~m}\right) \\&=3.9 \times 10^{5}\mathrm{~W} / \mathrm{m}^{2}=0.39 \mathrm{MW} / \mathrm{m}^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

EVALUATE: In part (b) of , we found the power radiated per unit area by the sun at all wavelengths to be \(I = 64.2 MW/m^2\); here we have found that the power radiated per unit area in the wavelength range from 600 to 605 nm is \(I(\lambda) \Delta \lambda=\) \(0.39 MW/m^2\), about 0.6% of the total.
