Ideal, single-phase two-winding transformer

A single-phase two-winding transformer is rated 20 kVA, 480/120 V, and 60 Hz. A source connected to the 480-V winding supplies an impedance load connected to the 120-V winding. The load absorbs 15 kVA at 0.8 p.f. lagging when the load voltage is 118 V. Assume that the transformer is ideal and calculate the following:
a. The voltage across the 480-V winding.
b. The load impedance.
c. The load impedance referred to the 480-V winding.
d. The real and reactive power supplied to the 480-V winding.

Admin
Solution

a. The circuit is shown in Figure 3.3, where winding 1 denotes the 480-V winding and winding 2 denotes the 120-V winding. Selecting the load voltage \(E_{2} \) as the reference,

\(E_{2} =118\angle {0°} V\)

The turns ratio is, from (3.1.13),

\(\frac{E_{1} }{E_{2} }=\frac{N_{1} }{N_{2} } \) (3.1.13)

\(a_{t}\frac{N_{1} }{N_{2} } =\frac{E_{1rated} }{E_{2rated} } =\frac{480}{120} =4 \)

and the voltage across winding 1 is

\(E_{1}=a_{1}E_{2}=4\left(118\angle {0°} \right) =472\angle {0°}V \)

b. The complex power \(S_{2} \) absorbed by the load is

\(S_{2} =E_{2}I^{\ast }_{2}=118I^{\ast }_{2}=15,000\angle {\cos ^{-1} } \left(0.8\right) =15,000\angle {36.87°}VA \)

Solving, the load current \(I_{2} \) is

\(I_{2} =127.12\angle {-36.87°}A \)

The load impedance \(Z_{2} \) is

\(Z_{2} =\frac{E_{2} }{I_{2} } =\frac{118\angle {0°} }{127.12\angle {-36.87°} } =0.9283\angle {36.87°}\Omega \)

c. From (3.1.21), the load impedance referred to the 480-V winding is

\(Z^{\prime }_{2}=\frac{E_{1} }{I_{1} } =\frac{a_{t}E_{2} }{I_{2}/a_{t} }=a^{2}_{t}Z_{2} =\left(\frac{N_{1} }{N_{2} } \right)^{2}Z_{2}\) (3.1.21)

\(Z^{\prime }_{2} =a^{2}_{t} Z_{2}=\left(4\right)^{2}\left(0.9283\angle {36.87°} \right)=14.85\angle {36.87°}\Omega \)

d. From (3.1.19)

\( S_{1} =E_{1}I^{\ast }_{1}=\left(a_{t}E_{2} \right) \left(\frac{I_{2} }{a_{t} } \right)^{\ast } =E_{2} I^{\ast }_{2}=S_{2} \) (3.1.19)

\(S_{1} =S_{2}=15,000\angle {36.87°}=12,000+j9000 \)

Thus, the real and reactive powers supplied to the 480-V winding are

\(P_{1}=ReS_{1} =12,000W=12K\) W

\(Q_{1}=ImS_{1} =9000var=9Kvar \)
