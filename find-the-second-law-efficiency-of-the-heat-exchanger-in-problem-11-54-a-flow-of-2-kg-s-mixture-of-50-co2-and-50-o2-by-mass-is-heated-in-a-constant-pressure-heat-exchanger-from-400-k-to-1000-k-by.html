Guest Question.

Find the second law efficiency of the heat exchanger in . A flow of 2 kg/s mixture of 50% \(CO _{2}\) and 50% \(O _{2}\) by mass is heated in a constant pressure heat exchanger from 400 K to 1000 K by a radiation source at 1400 K. Find the rate of heat transfer and the entropy generation in the process.

Admin
Solution

The second law efficiency where the wanted term is the flow increase of exergy and the source is the radiation.

\(\dot{\Phi}_{\text {flow }}=\dot{ m }\left(\psi_{ ex }-\psi_{ in }\right) ; \quad \dot{\Phi}_{\text {source }}=\dot{ Q }_{\text {in }}\left(1-\frac{ T _{ o }}{ T _{\text {source }}}\right)\)

Heat exchanger Energy Eq.4.12: \(\dot{ Q }_{ in }=\dot{ m }\left( h _{ e }- h _{ i }\right)\)

Values from Table A.8 due to the high T.

\(\dot{Q}_{\text {in }}=2\left[\frac{1}{2} \times(971.67-303.76)+\frac{1}{2} \times(980.95-366.03)\right]=1282.8 kW\)

 

\(\dot{\Phi}_{\text {source }}=\dot{Q}_{\text {in }}\left(1-\frac{ T _{ o }}{ T _{\text {source }}}\right)=1282.8\left(1-\frac{298.15}{1400}\right)=1009.6 kW\)

 

Entropy Eq.7.8: \(\dot{ m }_{ e } s _{ e }=\dot{ m }_{ i } s _{ i }+\dot{ Q } / T _{ s }+\dot{ S }_{ gen }\)

As P = C, the pressure correction in Eq.6.28 drops out to give generation as

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ S }_{ gen } &=\dot{ m }\left( s _{ e }- s _{ i }\right)-\dot{ Q } / T _{ s } \\&=2[0.5 \times(6.119-5.1196)+0.5 \times(7.6121-6.6838)]-1282.8 / 1400 \\&=1.01 kW / K\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{\Phi}_{\text {flow }} &=\dot{\Phi}_{\text {source }}-\dot{\Phi}_{\text {destruction }}=\dot{\Phi}_{\text {source }}- T \dot{ S }_{ gen } \\&=1009.6-298.15 \times 1.01=708.5\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\eta=\frac{\dot{\Phi}_{\text {flow }}}{\dot{\Phi}_{\text {source }}}=\frac{708.5}{1009.6}=0.70\)

Remark: We could also explicitly have found the flow exergy increase.
