Guest Question.

Air at 1 atm and a temperature of 290 K enters a 1.27-cm-ID tube at a velocity of 24 m/s. The wall temperature is maintained at 372 K by condensing steam. Evaluate the convective heat-transfer coefficient for this situation if the tube is 1.52 m long.

Admin
Solution

As in the previous example, it will be necessary to evaluate the exiting air temperature by

\( ln \frac{T_{L} – T_{s}}{T_{o} – T_{s}} + 4 \frac{L}{D} \frac{h}{\rho \upsilon c_{p}} = 0 \)

 

To determine the type of flow, we first evaluate the Reynolds number at the tube entrance.

\( Re = \frac{D \upsilon }{\nu } = \frac{\left ( 0.0127 m \right )\left ( 24 m/s \right )}{1.478 \times 10^{-5} Pa \cdot s} = 20.600 \)

 

Flow is clearly turbulent and Re is sufficiently large that equation ( \( Nu_{D} = 0.023 Re_{D}^{0.8} Pr^{n} \) ), (\( St = 0.023 Re_{D}^{-0.2} Pr^{-2/3} \)), or (\( St = 0.023 Re_{D}^{-0.2} Pr^{-2/3} \left ( \frac{\mu_{b}}{\mu_{w}} \right )^{0.14} \)) may be used.

 

Equation (\( St = 0.023 Re_{D}^{-0.2} Pr^{-2/3} \)) will be used. An exit temperature of 360 K will be assumed; the corresponding mean bulk temperature is 325 K and \( T_{f} = 349. \) We now have

 

\( St = \frac{h}{\rho \upsilon c_{p}} = 0.023 Re^{-0.2} Pr^{-2/3} \)

 

\( = 0.023 \left [ \frac{\left ( 0.0127 \right )\left ( 24 \right )}{2.05 \times 10^{-5}} \right ]^{-0.2} \left ( 0.697 \right )^{-2/3} = 0.00428\)

 

Substituting into equation ( \( \frac{dT}{dx} + \frac{h}{\rho \upsilon_{x} c_{p}} \frac{4}{D} \left ( T – T_{s} \right ) = 0 \) ), we have

 

\( \frac{T_{L} – T_{s}}{T_{o} – T_{s}} = exp \left [ – 4 \left ( \frac{1.52 m }{0.0127 m} \right ) \left ( 0.00428 \right ) \right ] = 0.129 \)

 

and the calculated value of \( T_{L} \) is

\( T_{L} = 372 – \left ( 0.129 \right )\left ( 372 – 209 \right ) = 361 K \)

 

This value agrees closely with the initially assumed value for \( T_{L} \), so there is no need to perform a second calculation. The heattransfer coefficient is now evaluated as

\( h = \rho \upsilon c_{p} St \)

 

\( = \left ( 1.012 kg/m^{3} \right )\left ( 24 m/s \right )\left ( 1009 J/kg \cdot K \right )\left ( 0.00428 \right ) = 105 W/m^{2} \cdot K \)
