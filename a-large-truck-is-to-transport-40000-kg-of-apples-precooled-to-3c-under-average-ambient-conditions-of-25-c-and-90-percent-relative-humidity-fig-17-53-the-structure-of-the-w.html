Guest Question.

A large truck is to transport 40,000 kg of apples precooled to \( 3^{\circ} C\) under average ambient conditions of \( 25^{\circ} C\) and 90 percent relative humidity (Fig. 17-53). The structure of the walls of the truck is such that the rate of heat transmission is U A=70 W per \( { }^{\circ} C\) temperature difference between the ambient and the apples. From past experience, ambient air is estimated to enter the cargo space of the truck through the cracks at a rate of 6 L / s, and the average heat of respiration of the apples at \( 3^{\circ} C\) is 0.015 w / kg for this particular load. Determine the refrigeration load of this truck and the amount of ice needed to meet the entire refrigeration need of the truck for a 20 -h-long trip.

 

 

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION A large truck is to transport 40,000 kg of apples. The refrigeration load of the truck and the amount of ice needed to meet this during a 20 h long trip is to be determined. Assumptions 1 Infiltrating air exits the truck saturated at \( 3^{\circ} C .2\) The moisture in the air is condensed out at the exit temperature of \( 3^{\circ} C\).

Properties The humidity ratio of air is 0.0180 kg water vapor/kg dry air at \( 25^{\circ} C\) and 90 percent relative humidity, and 0.0047 at \( 3^{\circ} C\) and 100 percent relative humidity. The latent heat of vaporization of water at \( 3^{\circ} C\) is 2494 kJ / kg (Table A-9). The density of air at the ambient temperature of \( 25^{\circ} C\) and 1 atm is \( 1.184 kg / m ^{3}\), and its specific heat is \( c_{\rho}=1.007 kJ / kg \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\) (Table A -15 ). The latent heat of ice is 333.7 kJ / kg.

Analysis The total refrigeration load of the truck is due to the heat gain by transmission, infiltration, and respiration. Noting that UA is given to be \( 70 W { }^{\circ} C\), the rate of heat gain by transmission is determined to be

\( Q_{\text {transmission }}=U A \Delta T=\left(70 W /{ }^{\circ} C \right)(25-3)^{\circ} C =1540 W\)

The rate of heat generation by the apples as a result of respiration is

\( \dot{Q}_{\text {respiration }}=m h_{\text {respiration }}=(40,000 kg )(0.015 W / kg )=600 W\)

Ambient air enters the cargo space at a rate of 6 L / s, which corresponds to a mass flow rate of

\( \dot{m}_{\text {air }}=\rho \dot{V}_{\text {air }}=\left(1.184 kg / m ^{3}\right)\left(0.006 m ^{3} / s \right)=0.00710 kg / s\)

Noting that an equal amount of air at\( 3^{\circ} C\) must leave the truck, the sensible heat gain due to infiltration is

\( \dot{Q}_{\text {infiltration,sensible }}=\left(\dot{m} c_{p} \Delta T\right)_{\text {air }}=(0.00710 kg / s )\left(1.007 kJ / kg \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)(25-3)^{\circ} C\) \( = 0.157 kJ / s =157 W\)

Ambient air enters with a humidity ratio of 0.0180 kg \(H _{2}\) O / kg air and leaves at of \( 0.0047 kg H _{2} O / kg\) air. The difference condenses in the truck and is drained out as a liquid, releasing \( h_{ fg }=2494 kJ / kg\) of latent heat. Then the latent heat gain of the truck becomes

\( Q_{\text {infiltration.latent }} =\dot{m}_{\text {water }} h_{f g}\) \( =(0.0180-0.0047)(0.00710 kg / s )(2494 kJ / kg )=236 W\)

Then the refrigeration load, or the total rate of heat gain by the truck, becomes

\( Q_{\text {total }} =\dot{Q}_{\text {transmission }}+\dot{Q}_{\text {respiration }}+\dot{Q}_{\text {infiltration }}\) \( =1540+600+(157+236)=2533 W\)

The total amount of heat gain during the 20 -h-long trip is

\( Q_{\text {total }}=\dot{Q}_{\text {total }} \Delta t=(2533 J / s )(20 \times 3600 s )=182,376,000 J =182,376 kJ\)

Then the amount of ice needed to meet this refrigeration load is determined from

\( m_{\text {ice }}=\frac{Q_{\text {total }}}{h_{\text {latent }, i \text { ce }}}=\frac{182,376 kJ }{333.7 kJ / kg }=547 kg\)

Discussion Note that about half a ton of ice (1.5 percent of the mass of the load) is sufficient in this case to maintain the apples at \( 3^{\circ} C\).
