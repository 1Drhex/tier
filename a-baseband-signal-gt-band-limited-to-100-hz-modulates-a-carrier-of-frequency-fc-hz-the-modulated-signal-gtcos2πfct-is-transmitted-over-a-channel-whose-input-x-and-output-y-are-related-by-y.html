Guest Question.

A baseband signal g(t) band limited to 100 Hz modulates a carrier of frequency \(f_{c}\) Hz. The modulated signal \(g(t) \cos 2 \pi f_{c} t\) is transmitted over a channel whose input x and output y are related by \(y=2 x+x^{2}\). The spectrum of g(t) is shown in figure. Sketch the spectrum of the transmitted signal and the spectrum of the received signal.

Admin
Solution
\(\begin{aligned}&x(t)=g(t) \cdot \cos \left(2 \pi f_{ c } t\right) \\&x(t)=\frac{1}{2}\left[G\left(f-f_{c}\right)+G\left(f+f_{c}\right)\right]\end{aligned}\)

Received signal

\(\begin{aligned}&y(t)=2 x+x^{2}=y_{1}(t)+y_{2}(t) \\&y_{1}(t)=2 x(t) \leftrightarrow 2 x(f)=y_{1}(f) \\&y_{2}(f)=x^{2}(t)=g^{2}(t) \cdot \cos ^{2} 2 f c t \\&2 \pi f c t=\frac{g^{2}(t)}{2}+\frac{g^{2}(t)}{2} \cos 4 \pi f_{c} t \\&\quad=y_{3}(t)+y_{4}(t) \\&y_{3}(t)=\frac{g^{2}(t)}{2}=\frac{1}{2}[G(g) * G(f)] \\&x_{4}(t)=g^{2}(t) \cos \left(2 \pi 2 f_{c} t\right)=y_{3}(t) \cdot \cos \left(2 \pi 2 f_{c} t\right) \\&y_{4}(t)=\frac{1}{2}\left[y_{3}\left(f-2 f_{c}\right)+y_{3}\left(f+2 f_{c}\right)\right. \\&y_{2}(f)=y_{3}(f)+y_{4}(f) \\&y_{2}(f)=y_{1}(f)+y_{2}(f) .\end{aligned}\)
