CRITICAL DEPTH FOR TRIANGULAR SECTION

A steep-sided triangular section channel (\(α = 60°\)) has a flow rate of 300 m[/latex]^3[/latex]/s. Find the critical depth for this flow rate. Verify that the Froude number is unity.

Admin
Solution

Given: Flow in a triangular section channel.


Find: Critical depth; verify that \(F r=1\).
Solution:
Use the critical flow equation, Eq. 11.21.
Governing equations:

\(Q^{2}=\frac{g A_{c}^{3}}{b_{s_{c}}}\quad\quad (11.21) \quad \quad F r=\frac{V}{\sqrt{g y_{h}}}\)

The given data is:

\(Q=300 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{s} \quad \alpha=60^{\circ}\)

 

From Table 11.1 we have the following:

\(A=y^{2} \cot \alpha\)

and from basic geometry

\(\tan \alpha=\frac{y}{b_{s} / 2} \quad \text { so } \quad b_{s}=2 y \cot \alpha\)

Using these in Eq. 11.21

\(Q^{2}=\frac{g A_{c}^{3}}{b_{s_{c}}}=\frac{g\left[y_{c}^{2} \cot \alpha\right]^{3}}{2 y_{c} \cot \alpha}=\frac{1}{2} g y_{c}^{5} \cot ^{2} \alpha\)

 

Hence

\(y_{c}=\left[\frac{2 Q^{2} \tan ^{2} \alpha}{g}\right]^{1 / 5}\)

Using the given data

\(y_{c}=\left[2 \times 300^{2}\left(\frac{\mathrm{m}^{3}}{\mathrm{~s}}\right)^{2} \times \tan ^{2}\left(\frac{60 \times \pi}{180}\right) \times \frac{\mathrm{s}^{2}}{9.81 \mathrm{~m}}\right]^{1 / 5}=\left[5.51 \times 10^{4} \mathrm{~m}^{5}\right]^{1 / 5}\)

Finally

\(y_{c}=8.88 \mathrm{~m}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad y_c}\)

 

To verify that \(F r=1\), we need \(V\) and \(y_{h}\).
From continuity

\(V_{c}=\frac{Q}{A_{c}}=\frac{Q}{y_{c}^{2} \cot \alpha}=300 \frac{\mathrm{m}^{3}}{\mathrm{~s}} \times \frac{1}{8.88^{2} \mathrm{~m}^{2}} \times \frac{1}{\cot \left(\frac{60 \times \pi}{180}\right)}=6.60 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\)

 

and from the definition of hydraulic depth

\(y_{h_{c}}=\frac{A_{c}}{b_{s}}=\frac{y_{c}^{2} \cot \alpha}{2 y_{c} \cot \alpha}=\frac{y_{c}}{2}=4.44 \mathrm{~m}\)

Hence

\(F r_{c}=\frac{V_{c}}{\sqrt{g y_{h_{c}}}}=\frac{6.60 \frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}}}{\sqrt{9.81 \frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}^{2}} \times 4.44 \mathrm{~m}}}=1\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad F r_{c}}\)

 

We have verified that at critical depth the Froude number is unity.

 

As with the rectangular channel, the triangular section channel analysis leads to an explicit equation for \(y_c\) from Eq. 11.21. Other more complicated channel cross sections often lead to an implicit equation that needs to be solved numerically
