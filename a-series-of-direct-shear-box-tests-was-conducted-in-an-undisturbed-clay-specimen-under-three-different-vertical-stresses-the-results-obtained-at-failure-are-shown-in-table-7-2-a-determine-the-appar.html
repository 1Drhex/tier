Guest Question.

A series of direct shear box tests was conducted in an undisturbed clay specimen under three different vertical stresses. The results obtained at failure are shown in Table 7.2.

Table 7.2 Test results.

400 300 200 \(\sigma _{V} (kPa)\)
162 130 95 \(T _{S} (kPa)\)
Admin
Solution

a. The problem may be solved either analytically or graphically.

Both methods are illustrated.

Graphic method (Fig. 7.7):

Measure the angle and cohesion intercept.

Cohesion intercept: 28 kPa

Angle of friction 18.5 degrees

Analytically,

Determine the slope of the failure line.

\(m=\frac{\left(162-95\right) }{\left(400-200\right) }=0.335\)

 

\(\tan ^{-1} \left(0.335\right)=18.5^{\circ }\)

Whereas the cohesion intercept can be found using equation of a line \(\left(e.g.y=mx+b\right) \):

\(\tau =c+m\times \sigma\)

 

\(95 = c + 0.335 \times 200\)

 

\(c=28 kPa\)

b. For a vertical stress of 250 kPa, then

\(\tau =28+ 0 335 \times 250\)

 

\(\tau =111.75 kPa \)

c. From the relationship of the Mohr circle geometry, we have

\(\sigma _{1} =\sigma _{3} \left\lgroup\frac{1+\sin \phi }{1-\sin \phi } \right\rgroup +2c\frac{\cos \phi }{1-\sin \phi }\)

If \(\sigma _{3} =150kPa,\) \( \phi =18.5 °\) and c=28 then

\(\sigma _{1} =150 \left\lgroup\frac{1+\sin 18.5}{1-\sin 18.5 } \right\rgroup +2\times 28\frac{\cos 18.5 }{1-\sin 18.5 }=367.2kPa\)
