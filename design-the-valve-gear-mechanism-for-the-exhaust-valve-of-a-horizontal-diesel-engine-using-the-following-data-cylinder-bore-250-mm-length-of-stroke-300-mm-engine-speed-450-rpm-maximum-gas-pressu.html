Guest Question.

Design the valve gear mechanism for the exhaust valve of a horizontal diesel engine using the following data:
Cylinder bore = 250 mm
Length of stroke = 300 mm
Engine speed = 450 rpm

Maximum gas pressure = 3.5 MPa
Effective length of each arm = 150 mm
Angle between two arms = 165°
Seat angle of valve = 45°
Mass of valve = 0.5 kg
Back pressure when the exhaust valve opens = 0.4 MPa
Maximum suction pressure = 0.02 MPa (below atmosphere)
The valve opens 33° before outer dead centre and closes 1° after inner dead centre. The valve is to open and close with constant acceleration and deceleration for each half of the lift. Assume suitable data and state the assumptions you make.

Admin
Solution

Given D = 250 mm l = 300 mm N = 450 rpm

\( p_{\max .}=3.5 MPa =3.5 N / mm ^{2} \quad a=150 mm \).

\( \theta=165^{\circ} \quad m=0.5 kg \quad \alpha=45^{\circ} \).

\( p_{c}=0.4 MPa =0.4 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( p_{s}=0.02 MPa =0.02 N / mm ^{2} \).

Part 1: Design of valve
Step I Diameter of valve port

\( a=\text { area of piston }=\left(\frac{\pi(250)^{2}}{4}\right) mm ^{2} \).

v = mean velocity of piston

\( =2 l\left(\frac{N}{60}\right)=2\left(\frac{300}{1000}\right)\left(\frac{450}{60}\right) m / s \).

Assume [from Table 25.2],

Table 25.2 Allowable mean velocities of the gas \( \left(v_{p}\right) \)

Mean velocity of gas (m/s) Type of engine
Exhaust valve Inlet valve
40–50 33–40 Low-speed
engine
50–60 35–45 Medium speed
engine
90–100 80–90 High-speed
engine

\( v_{p}=50 m / s \).

\( a_{p}=\left(\frac{\pi d_{p}^{2}}{4}\right) mm ^{2} \).

Therefore,

\( a \times v=a_{p} \times v_{p} \).

\( \left(\frac{\pi(250)^{2}}{4}\right) \times 2\left(\frac{300}{1000}\right)\left(\frac{450}{60}\right)=\left(\frac{\pi d_{p}^{2}}{4}\right) \times(50) \).

\( d_{p}^{2}=5625 mm ^{2} \).

\( d_{p}=75 mm \) (i).

Step II Diameter of valve head
For a seat angle of 45°, the projected width of the valve seat is given by following empirical relationship:

\( w=(0.05 \text { to } 0.07) d_{p} \).

or \( w=0.06 d_{p}=0.06(75)=4.5 mm \).

\( \text { The diameter of the valve head }\left(d_{v}\right) \text { is given by, } \)

\( d_{v}=\left(d_{p}+2 w\right)=75+2 \times 4.5=84 mm \) (ii).

Step III Thickness of the valve head
For steel valve,

\( k=0.42 \quad \text { Assume } \sigma_{b}=70 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( t=k d_{p} \sqrt{\frac{p_{\max }}{\sigma_{b}}}=(0.42)(75) \sqrt{\frac{3.5}{70}}=7.04 \text { or } 8 mm \). (iii)

Step IV Diameter of valve stem

\( d_{s}=\left[\frac{d_{p}}{8}+6.35\right] \text { to }\left[\frac{d_{p}}{8}+11\right] \).

\( =\left[\frac{75}{8}+6.35\right] \text { to }\left[\frac{75}{8}+11\right] \).

= 15.73 to 20.38 mm

\( d_{s}=18 mm \). (iv)

Step V Maximum lift of valve

\( h_{\max .}=\frac{d_{p}}{4 \cos \alpha}=\frac{75}{4 \cos (45)^{\circ}}=26.52 \text { or } 27 mm \). (v)

Part 2: Forces acting on rocker arm

\( \text { The gas load } P_{g} \text { is given by, } \)

\( P_{g}=\left(\frac{\pi d_{v}^{2}}{4}\right) p_{c}=\left(\frac{\pi(84)^{2}}{4}\right)(0.4) \).

= 2216.71 N (a)

\( \text { The initial spring force } P_{i} \text { is given by. } \)

\( P_{i}=\left(\frac{\pi d_{v}^{2}}{4}\right) p_{s}=\left(\frac{\pi(84)^{2}}{4}\right)(0.02) \).

= 110.84 N (b)
The acceleration of the valve is calculated by using the following steps:
(i) Speed of camshaft
For four-stroke engine

\( \text { Speed of camshaft }=\frac{1}{2}(\text { speed of crankshaft }) \)

\( =\frac{N}{2}=\frac{450}{2}=225 rpm \).

(ii) Angle turned by camshaft per second
Since, 1 revolution = 360°.

Angle turned by camshaft per second = (Number of revolutions/s)(360°).

\( =\left(\frac{225}{60}\right)(360)=1350^{\circ} \text { per } s \).

In other words, 1350° of cam rotation takes
1 second.
(iii) Total crankshaft angle when the valve is open = 33 + 180 + 1 = 214°
(iv) Total angle of cam action For four-stroke engine,

\( \text { angle of camshaft }=\frac{1}{2} \text { (angle of crankshaft) } \).

\( =\frac{1}{2}(214)=107^{\circ} \).

As shown in Fig. 25.41, during half of this rotation, the valve will open and in the remaining half, the valve will close. Therefore, cam rotation for valve opening is (107/2) or 53.5°. Out of this, half the angle is occupied by constant acceleration and half the angle by constant deceleration. Therefore, cam rotation during the constant acceleration period is (53.5/2) or 26.75°. The valve lift is 27 mm.
The valve will be raised by (27/2) or 13.5 mm during constant acceleration and 13.5 mm during the constant deceleration period.

Cam angle during constant acceleration 26.75°.

Valve lift during constant acceleration (s)

\( =13.5 mm =13.5 \times 10^{-3} m \).

(v) Time taken by valve during constant acceleration period
Since, 1350º of cam rotation = 1 second
Time taken by valve during constant acceleration period (t)

\( =\frac{\text { Angle of cam action }}{\text { Angle turned by camshaft per second }} \).

\( =\frac{26.75}{1350}=0.0198 s \).

\( s=u t+\frac{1}{2} \alpha t^{2} \).

\( s=13.5 \times 10^{-3} m \quad u=0 \quad t=0.0198 s \).

Substituting,

\( 13.5 \times 10^{-3}=0(0.0198)+\frac{1}{2} \alpha(0.0198)^{2} \).

\( \alpha=68.87 m / s ^{2} \).

\( \text { The inertia force } P_{a} \text { is given by, } \)

\( P_{a}=m \alpha=0.5(68.87)=34.44 N \). (c).

Adding (a), (b) and (c), the total force \( \left(P_{e}\right) \) on the rocker arm of the exhaust valve is given by,

\( P_{e}=P_{g}+P_{a}+P_{j}=2216.71+34.44+110.84 \).

= 2361.99 or 2362 N
Assumptions
(i) The permissible bending stress is 70 N/mm².
(ii) The permissible shear stress is 42 N/mm².
(iii) The permissible compressive stress is 50 N/mm².
(iv) The permissible bearing pressure is 5 MPa.
(v) The (l/d) ratio for fulcrum and roller pins is 1.25.
Part 3: Design of fulcrum pin
The reaction at the fulcrum pin is given by,

\( R_{f}=\sqrt{\left(P_{e}\right)^{2}+\left(P_{c}\right)^{2}-2 P_{e} P_{c} \cos \theta} \).

When arms are equal,

\( a=b \quad P_{e}=P_{c}=2362 N \text { and } \theta=165^{\circ} \).

\( R_{f}=\sqrt{(2362)^{2}+(2362)^{2}-2(2362)(2362) \cos (165)} \).

\( =2362 \sqrt{1+1-2 \cos (165)}=4683.59 N \).

Suppose,

\( d_{1}=\text { diameter of fulcrum pin }( mm ) \).

\( l_{1}=\text { length of fulcrum pin }( mm ) \).

Considering the bearing at the fulcrum pin,

\( p_{b}=\frac{R_{f}}{d_{1} l_{1}} \quad\left(p_{b}=5 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(l_{1} / d_{1}=1.25\right) \).

Substituting,

\( 5=\frac{4683.59}{d_{1}\left(1.25 d_{1}\right)} \quad d_{1}^{2}=749.37 \).

\( d_{1}=27.37 \text { or } 30 mm \).

\( l_{1}=1.25 d_{1}=1.25(30)=37.5 \text { or } 40 mm \).

The pin should be checked for double shear stress. The shear stress is given by,

\( \tau=\frac{R_{f}}{2\left(\frac{\pi d_{1}^{2}}{4}\right)}=\frac{4683.59}{2\left(\frac{\pi(30)^{2}}{4}\right)}=3.31 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \tau<42 N / mm ^{2} \).

The outside diameter of the boss of the rocker arm at the fulcrum pin is assumed as twice the diameter of the fulcrum pin.

\( \text { Outside diameter of boss at fulcrum pin }=D_{1}= \) \( 2 d_{1}=2(30)=60 mm \).

A phosphor bronze bush of 3 mm thickness is press fitted in the pinhole of the rocker arm.
Inside diameter of hole at the boss \( =d_{1}+3 \times 2= \) \( 30+3 \times 2=36 mm \).
The cross-section of the rocker arm passing through the fulcrum pin is shown in Fig. 25.42. Let us check this cross-section for bending stress.

\( M_{b}=P_{e} \times a=2362 \times 150=354.3 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

\( I=\frac{(40) \times(60)^{3}}{12}-\frac{(40) \times(36)^{3}}{12} \).

\( =\frac{1}{12}(40)\left[(60)^{3}-(36)^{3}\right]=564.48 \times 10^{3} mm ^{4} \).

\( y=\frac{60}{2}=30 mm \).

\( \sigma_{b}=\frac{M_{b} y}{I}=\frac{\left(354.3 \times 10^{3}\right)(30)}{\left(564.48 \times 10^{3}\right)}=18.83 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \sigma_{b}<70 N / mm ^{2} \).

Part 4: Design of forked end
The forked end carries a roller that revolves on the pin. The force acting on the roller pin is \( P_{c} \).

\( P_{c}=P_{e}=2362 N \).

Suppose,
\( d_{2}= \) diameter of roller pin (mm)
\( l_{2}= \) length of roller pin (mm)
Considering the bearing at the roller pin,

\( p_{b}=\frac{P_{c}}{d_{2} l_{2}} \quad\left(p_{b}=5 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(l_{2} / d_{2}=1.25\right) \).

Substituting,

\( 5=\frac{2362}{d_{2}\left(1.25 d_{2}\right)} \quad d_{2}^{2}=377.92 \).

\( d_{2}=19.44 \text { or } 20 mm \).

\( l_{2}=1.25 d_{2}=1.25(20)=25 mm \).

The shear stress is given by,

\( \tau=\frac{P_{c}}{2\left(\frac{\pi d_{2}^{2}}{4}\right)}=\frac{2362}{2\left(\frac{\pi(20)^{2}}{4}\right)}=3.76 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \tau<42 N / mm ^{2}\).

The roller pin is fixed in the eye. The dimensions of the eye are as follows:

\( \text { Thickness of each eye }=l_{2} / 2=25 / 2=12.5 mm \).

\( \text { Outer diameter of eye }=D_{2}=2 d_{2}=2(20) \) = 40 mm.

The diameter of the roller should be more than the outer diameter of the eye by at least 5 mm.
Diameter of roller = 40 + 5 = 45 or 50 mm When the roller pin is tight in the two eyes of the fork, failure occurs due to shear. On the other hand, when the pin is loose, it is subjected to bending moment. It acts as a beam as shown in Fig. 25.38. It is assumed that the load acting on the pin is uniformly distributed in the portion that is in contact with the roller. It is uniformly varying in two parts of the fork.
The bending moment is maximum at the central plane of the pin. From Eq. 25.89,

\( M_{b}=\left(\frac{5}{24}\right) P_{c} l_{2}=\left(\frac{5}{24}\right)(2362)(25) \). (25.89).

\( M_{b}=\left(\frac{5}{24}\right) P_{c} l_{2}=\left(\frac{5}{24}\right)(2362)(25) \).

= 12 302.08 N-mm

\( Z=\frac{\pi d_{2}^{3}}{32}=\frac{\pi(20)^{3}}{32}=785.4 mm ^{3} \).

\( \sigma_{b}=\frac{M_{b}}{Z}=\frac{12302.08}{785.4}=15.66 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \sigma_{b}<70 N / mm ^{2} \).

Part 5: Design of cross-section of rocker arm
As shown in Fig. 25.39(a), the arm is subjected to bending moment under the action of the force \( P_{e} \).
We will consider the cross-section of the arm near the boss of the fulcrum pin. The outside diameter of the boss of the rocker arm at the fulcrum pin is twice the diameter of the fulcrum pin \( \left(2 d_{1}\right) \). Therefore,

\( M_{b}=P_{e}\left[a-d_{1}\right]=2362[150-30] \).

\( =283.44 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

The proportions of cross-section for the rocker arm are shown in Fig. 25.43.

From Eq. 25.91,

\( I=\left(37 t^{4}\right) \quad \text { and } \quad y=(3 t) \) (25.91)

\( I=\left(37 t^{4}\right) \quad \text { and } \quad y=(3 t) \) ,

\( \sigma_{b}=\frac{M_{b} y}{I} \quad \text { or } \quad 70=\frac{283.44 \times 10^{3}(3 t)}{\left(37 t^{4}\right)} \).

\( t^{3}=328.31 \).

t = 6.9 or 7 mm
The dimensions of cross-section as shown in
Fig. 25.43 are as follows:

Width of flange = 2.5t = 2.5 (7) = 17.5 mm
Depth of section = 6t = 6 (7) = 42 mm
Part 6: Design of tappet
A tappet is a stud, which is subjected to compressive
force \( \left(P_{e}\right) \). Suppose,
\( d_{c}= \) core diameter of stud (mm).

\( \sigma_{c}=\frac{P_{e}}{\left(\frac{\pi d_{c}^{2}}{4}\right)} \quad \text { or } \quad 50=\frac{2362}{\left(\frac{\pi d_{c}^{2}}{4}\right)}\).

\( d_{c}^{2}=60.15 \).

\( d_{c}=7.76 mm \).

The nominal diameter (d) of the stud is given by,

\( d=\frac{d_{c}}{0.8}=\frac{7.76}{0.8}=9.7 \text { or } 10 mm \).

The diameter of the circular end of the rocker arm \( \left(D_{3}\right) \text { and its depth }\left(t_{3}\right) \) are calculated by the following relationships:

\( D_{3}=2 d=2(10)=20 mm \).

\( t_{3}=2 d=2(10)=20 mm \).

Part 7: Design of valve spring
Assumptions
(i) The spring index is 8.
(ii) The stiffness of spring is 10 N/mm.
(iii) Permissible torsional shear stress for the spring wire is 300 N/mm²
(iv) Modulus of rigidity for the spring wire is
\( 84 \times 10^{3} N / mm ^{2} \)
(v) Total gap between consecutive coils, when the spring is compressed by maximum force, is 15% of maximum compression.

Step I Maximum force on spring
The initial spring force \( P_{i} \) is calculated as,

\( P_{i}=110.84 N \).

\( \text { The maximum force on spring }\left(P_{\max }\right) \text { is given by} \),

\( P_{\max }=P_{i}+k \delta=110.84+10(27) \).

\( =380.84 N \quad(\delta=h=27 mm ) \).

Step II Wire diameter
The Wahl factor is given by,

\( K=\frac{4 C-1}{4 C-4}+\frac{0.615}{C}=\frac{4(8)-1}{4(8)-4}+\frac{0.615}{8}=1.184 \).

\( \tau=K\left(\frac{8 P_{\max .} C}{\pi d^{2}}\right) \text { or } 300=1.184\left(\frac{8(380.84)(8)}{\pi d^{2}}\right) \).

\( d^{2}=30.62 \).

d = 5.53 or 6 mm

Step III Mean coil diameter
D = Cd = 8 (6) = 48 mm

Step IV Number of active turns

\( N=\frac{G d^{4}}{8 D^{3} k}=\frac{\left(84 \times 10^{3}\right)(6)^{4}}{8(48)^{3}(10)}=12.3 \text { or } 13 \).

Step V Total number of turns
For square and ground ends

\( N_{t}=N+2=13+2=15 \).

Step VI Maximum compression of spring

\( \delta_{\max .}=\frac{8 P_{\max .} D^{3} N}{G d^{4}}=\frac{8(380.84)(48)^{3}(13)}{\left(84 \times 10^{3}\right)(6)^{4}} \).

= 40.24 mm.

Step VII Solid length of spring

\( \text { Solid length }=N_{t} d=15(6)=90 mm \).

Step VIII Free length of spring

\( \text { Free length }=\text { solid length }+\delta_{\max }+0.15 \delta_{\max } \text {. } \)

= 90 + 40.24 + 0.15 (40.24)
= 136.28 mm.

Step IX Pitch of coils

\( \text { Pitch of coils }=\frac{\text { free length }}{\left(N_{t}-1\right)}=\frac{136.28}{(15-1)} \).

= 9.73 mm.

Part 8: Design of cam
The diameter of camshaft (D′) is obtained by the following empirical relationship:

\( D^{\prime}=0.16 D+12.5 \).

where,
D = cylinder bore (mm)
D′ = 0.16D + 12.5 = 0.16 (250) + 12.5
= 52.5 or 55 mm
The base circle diameter of the cam is at least 5 mm more than the camshaft diameter.
Base circle diameter of cam = 55 + 5 = 60 mm
The diameter of the roller is calculated in the design of the forked arm.

Roller diameter = 50 mm
Roller width = \( l_{2} \) = 25 mm
Cam width = roller width = 25 mm
Lift of valve = 27 mm.

Displacement diagram
The displacement diagram is shown in Fig. 25.44.
The step by step approach to construct this diagram is as follows:

(i) Draw a horizontal line OY. It represents the angular rotation of the cam when the valve opens, i.e., 53.5°. It is divided into two equal parts—OX and XY. The line OX indicates the constant acceleration phase (26.75°) and line XY represents the constant deceleration phase (26.75°).
(ii) Divide lines OX and XY each into four equal parts. The total of eight parts are shown by numbers 1, 2, 3, …, 8. Each part represents (53.5º/8) or 6.6875º of cam rotation.
(iii) Draw a vertical line YH equal to the valve lift, i.e., 27 mm.
(iv) Draw ordinates through points 1, 2, 3, ……, 8.
(v) Draw a vertical line XZ equal to the valve lift (i.e., 27 mm). Divide the line XZ into eight equal parts. They are shown by points a, b, c, …, h. Therefore, each part represents (27/8) or 3.375 mm of the valve lift.
(vi) Join lines Oa, Ob, Oc, Od that intersects the ordinates through 1, 2, 3 and 4 at points A, B, C, and D respectively.
(vii) Also, join lines Hd, He, Hf, and Hg that intersect the ordinates through 4, 5, 6 and 7
at points D, E, F, and G respectively.

(viii) Draw a smooth curve passing through points O, A, B, C and D. It represents the parabolic curve for the constant acceleration phase.
(ix) Similarly, draw a smooth curve passing through points D, E, F, G, and H. It represents the parabolic curve for the constant deceleration phase.
Cam profile
The profile of the cam is shown in Fig. 25.45. The step by step approach to construct this profile is as follows:
(i) Draw a base circle with \( O_{1} \) as centre and radius equal to 30 mm.
(ii) Draw a prime circle with \( O_{1} \) as centre and radius equal to (radius of base circle + radius of roller), i.e., (30 + 25) or 55 mm.
(iii) Draw angle \( \angle O O_{1} H \) equal to 53.5º. It represents the angular rotation of cam when
the valve opens.
(iv) Divide the angle \( \angle O O_{1} H \) (53.5º) into eight equal parts. Therefore, each part represents (53.5º/8) or 6.6875º of cam rotation. The eight parts are shown by angles \( \angle O O_{1} 1, \), \( \angle 1 O_{1} 2, \angle 2 O_{1} 3 \text {, etc. } \)

(v) Join points 1, 2, 3, etc., with the centre \( O_{1} \) and extend the radial lines beyond the prime circle as shown in Fig. 25.45.
(vi) Mark the distances 1A, 2B, 3C,… etc., from the displacement diagram shown in Fig. 25.44, on the extension of radial lines beyond the prime circle.
(vii) Draw a smooth curve passing through points O, A, B, C, D, E, F, G, and H. This curve is called the ‘pitch curve’.
(viii) Draw circles with radii equal to the radius of the roller (25 mm) and centres at points O, A, B, C, D, E, F, G, and H.
(ix) The profile of the cam is a curve drawn tangential to these circles at the bottom as shown in Fig. 25.45. This curve indicates the cam profile when the valve is open. Only half the profile is drawn, but it is symmetrical about the line \( O_{1} H \).
