IRR for a Mixed Investment by Trial and Error

Consider project D in . The project has the following cash flow:

n \(A_n\)
0 -$1,000
1 3,900
2 -5,030
3 2,145
Admin
Solution

Given: Cash flow as stated for mixed investment and \(MARR = 6\%\).

Find: \(IRR\).

For \(MARR = 6\%\) , we must compute \(i\) by trial and error. Suppose we guess \(i = 8\%\):

\(PB(8\%, 6\%)_0 = -\$ 1,000\),

\(PB(8\%, 6\%)_1 = -\$1,000(1 + 0.08) + \$3,900 = \$2,820\).

\(PB(8\%, 6\%)_2 = +\$2,820(1 + 0.06) – \$5,030 = -\$2,040.80\),

\(PB(8\%, 6\%)_3 = -\$2,040.80(1 + 0.08) + \$2,145 = -\$59.06\).

The net investment is negative at the end of the project, indicating that our trial \(i = 8\%\) is in error. After several trials, we conclude that, for \(MARR = 6\%\), the \(IRR\) is approximately \(6.13\%\). To verify the results, we write

\(PB(6.13\%, 6\%)_0 = -\$1,000\),

\(PB(6.13\%, 6\%)_1 = -\$1,000.00(1 + 0.0613) + \$3,900 = \$2,838.66\).

\(PB(6.13\%, 6\%)_2 = +\$2,838.66(1 + 0.0600) – \$5,030 = -\$2,021.02\),

\(PB(6.13\%, 6\%)_3 = -\$2,021.02(1 + 0.0613) + \$2,145 = 0\).

The positive balance at the end of year 1 indicates the need to borrow from the project during year 2. However, note that the net investment becomes zero at the end of the project life, confirming that \(6.13\%\) is the \(IRR\) for the cash flow. Since \(IRR \gt MARR\) , the investment is acceptable.

COMMENTS: On the basis of the \(NPW\) criterion, the investment would be acceptable if the \(MARR\) was between zero and \(10\%\) or between \(30\%\) and \(50\%\). The rejection region is \(10\% \lt i \lt 30\%\) and \(i \gt 50\%\). This can be verified in \(Figure 7.1(b)\). Note that the project also would be marginally accepted under the \(NPW\) analysis at \(MARR = i = 6\%\):

\(PW(6\%) = -\$1,000 + 3,900(P/F, 6\%, 1)\)

 

\(= -\$ 5,030(P/F, 6\%, 2) + 2,145(P/F, 6\%, 3)\)

 

\(= \$3.55 \gt 0\).
