Guest Question.

On the free surface of a steel [E = 207 GPa; ν = 0.29] component, a strain rosette located at point A in Figure P13.61 was used to obtain the following normal strain data: \(\varepsilon_{a}=133 \mu \varepsilon, \varepsilon_{b}=-92 \mu \varepsilon, \text { and } \varepsilon_{c}=-319 \mu \varepsilon \text {. }\) If θ = 50°, determine the stresses \(\sigma_{n}, \sigma_{t} \text {, and } \tau_{n t}\) that act at point A.

Admin
Solution

Write three normal strain transformation equations [Eq. (13.3)], one for each strain gage, using the measured normal strain. In each equation, the angle θ associated with each strain gage will be referenced from the positive x axis.

\(\begin{aligned}&133 \mu \varepsilon=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2}\left(0^{\circ}\right)+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2}\left(0^{\circ}\right)+\gamma_{x y} \sin \left(0^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(0^{\circ}\right) (a)\\&-92 \mu \varepsilon=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2}\left(120^{\circ}\right)+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2}\left(120^{\circ}\right)+\gamma_{x y} \sin \left(120^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(120^{\circ}\right) (b) \\&-319 \mu \varepsilon=\varepsilon_{x} \cos ^{2}\left(60^{\circ}\right)+\varepsilon_{y} \sin ^{2}\left(60^{\circ}\right)+\gamma_{x y} \sin \left(60^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(60^{\circ}\right) (c)\end{aligned}\)

From Eq. (a):

\(\varepsilon_{x}=133 \mu \varepsilon\)

Using this result, solve Eqs. (b) and (c) simultaneously to obtain:

\(\varepsilon_{y}=-318.333 \mu \varepsilon \quad \text { and } \quad \gamma_{x y}=-262.117 \mu rad\)

From Eqs. (13.23), compute \(\sigma_{x}\):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\frac{E}{1-v^{2}}\left(\varepsilon_{x}+v \varepsilon_{y}\right)=\frac{207,000 MPa }{1-(0.29)^{2}}\left[\left(133 \times 10^{-6}\right)+(0.29)\left(-318.333 \times 10^{-6}\right)\right] \\&=9.195 MPa\end{aligned}\)

and \(\sigma_{y}\):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{y} &=\frac{E}{1-v^{2}}\left(\varepsilon_{y}+v \varepsilon_{x}\right)=\frac{207,000 MPa }{1-(0.29)^{2}}\left[\left(-318.333 \times 10^{-6}\right)+(0.29)\left(133 \times 10^{-6}\right)\right] \\&=-63.229 MPa\end{aligned}\)

From Eq. (13.18), determine the shear modulus G:

\(G=\frac{E}{2(1+v)}=\frac{207,000 MPa }{2(1+0.29)}=80,232.558 MPa\)

and compute the shear stress \(\tau_{x y}\) from Eq. (13.20):

\(\tau_{x y}=G \gamma_{x y}=(80,232.558 MPa )\left(-262.117 \times 10^{-6} rad \right)=-21.030 MPa\)

To summarize, normal and shear stresses in the x-y plane are:

\(\sigma_{x}=9.195 MPa , \sigma_{y}=-63.229 MPa , \tau_{x y}=-262.117 MPa\)

A normal stress transformation equation [Eq. (12-3)] can now be written to determine the normal stress at an orientation of θ = 50°:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{n} &=\sigma_{x} \cos ^{2} \theta+\sigma_{y} \sin ^{2} \theta+2 \tau_{x y} \sin \theta \cos \theta \\&=(9.195 MPa ) \cos ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)+(-63.229 MPa ) \sin ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)+2(-21.030 MPa ) \sin \left(50^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(50^{\circ}\right) \\&=-54.016 MPa =54.0 MPa ( C )\end{aligned}\)

For \(\sigma_{t}\), use θ = 140°:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{t} &=\sigma_{x} \cos ^{2} \theta+\sigma_{y} \sin ^{2} \theta+2 \tau_{x y} \sin \theta \cos \theta \\&=(9.195 MPa ) \cos ^{2}\left(140^{\circ}\right)+(-63.229 MPa ) \sin ^{2}\left(140^{\circ}\right)+2(-21.030 MPa ) \sin \left(140^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(140^{\circ}\right) \\&=-0.01800 MPa =0.01800 MPa ( C )\end{aligned}\)

A shear stress transformation equation [Eq. (12-4)] can now be written to determine the shear stress at an orientation of θ = 50°:

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{n t} &=-\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) \sin \theta \cos \theta+2 \tau_{x y}\left(\cos ^{2} \theta-\sin ^{2} \theta\right) \\&=[(9.195 MPa )-(-63.229 MPa )] \sin \left(50^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(50^{\circ}\right)+2(-21.030 MPa )\left[\cos ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)-\sin ^{2}\left(50^{\circ}\right)\right] \\&=-32.010 MPa =-32.0 MPa\end{aligned}\)
