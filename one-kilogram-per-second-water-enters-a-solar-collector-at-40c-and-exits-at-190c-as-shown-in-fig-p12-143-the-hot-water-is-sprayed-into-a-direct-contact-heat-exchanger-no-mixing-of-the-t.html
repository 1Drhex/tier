Guest Question.

One kilogram per second water enters a solar collector at 40°C and exits at 190°C, as shown in Fig. P12.143. The hot water is sprayed into a direct-contact heat exchanger (no mixing of the two fluids) used to boil the liquid butane. Pure saturated-vapor butane exits at the top at 80°C and is fed to the turbine. If the butane condenser temperature is 30°C and the turbine and pump isentropic efficiencies are each 80%, determine the net power output of the cycle.

Admin
Solution

\(H _{2} O\) cycle: solar energy input raises 1 kg/s of liquid \(H _{2} O\) from 40°C to 190°C.
Therefore, corresponding heat input to the butane in the heat exchanger is

\(\dot{ Q }_{ H }=\dot{ m }\left( h _{ F 190 C }- h _{ F 40 C }\right)_{ H 2 O }=1(807.62-167.57)=640.05 kW\)

\(C _{4} H _{10}\) cycle

\(\begin{aligned}& T _{1}=80{ }^{\circ} C , x _{1}=1.0 ; T _{3}=30^{\circ} C , x _{3}=0.0 \\&\eta_{ ST }=\eta_{ SP }=0.80 \\& T _{ r1 }=\frac{353.2}{425.2}=0.831\end{aligned}\)

From D.1, D.2 and D.3:

\(\begin{aligned}P_{1} &=0.325 \times 3800=1235 kPa \\\left( h _{1}^{*}- h _{1}\right) &=0.14304 \times 425.2 \times 0.56=34.1 \\\left( s _{1}^{*}- s _{1}\right) &=0.14304 \times 0.475=0.0680\end{aligned}\)

 

\(T _{ r 3}=\frac{303.2}{425.2}=0.713\)

From D.1, D.2 and D.3: \(P _{3}=0.113 \times 3800=429 kPa\)

sat. liq.: \(\left( h ^{*}- h _{ F }\right)= RT _{ C } \times 4.81=292.5 ; \quad\left( s ^{*}- s _{ F }\right)= R \times 6.64=0.950\)

sat. vap.: \(\left( h ^{*}- h _{ G }\right)= RT _{ C } \times 0.235=14.3 ; \quad\left( s ^{*}- s _{ G }\right)= R \times 0.22=0.031\)

Because of the combination of properties of \(C _{4} H _{10}\) (particularly the large \(C _{ P 0} / R\) ) , \(s _{1}\) is larger than \(s _{ G } \text { at } T _{3}\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\left( s _{1}^{*}-s _{ G 3}^{*} \right)=1.7164 \ln \frac{353.2}{303.2}-0.14304 \ln \frac{1235}{429}=0.1107 \\&\left( s _{1}- s _{ G 3}\right)=-0.0680+0.1107+0.031=+0.0737 kJ / kg K\end{aligned}\)

so that \(T _{2 S }\) will be \(> T _{3}\) , as shown in the T-s diagram. A number of other heavy hydrocarbons also exhibit this behavior.

Assume \(T _{2 S }=315 K , T _{ r 2 S }=0.741\)

From D.2 and D.3:

\(\left( h _{2 S }^{*}- h _{2 S }\right)= RT _{ C } \times 0.21=12.8 \quad \text { and } \quad\left( s _{2 S }^{*}- s _{2 S }\right)= R \times 0.19=0.027\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\left( s _{1}^{*}- s _{2 S }^{*}\right)=1.7164 \ln \frac{353.2}{315}-0.14304 \ln \frac{1235}{429}=+0.0453 \\&\left( s _{1}- s _{2 S }\right)=-0.0680+0.0453+0.027 \approx 0 \\&\Rightarrow T _{2 S }=315 K\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\left( h _{1}^{*}- h _{2 S }^{*}\right)=1.7164(353.2-315)=65.6 \\& w _{ ST }= h _{1}- h _{2 S }=-34.1+65.6+12.8=44.3 kJ / kg \\& w _{ T }=\eta_{ S } \times w _{ ST }=0.80 \times 44.3=35.4 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

 

At state 3,

\(\begin{aligned}& v _{3}=\frac{0.019 \times 0.14304 \times 303.2}{429}=0.00192 m ^{3} / kg \\&- w _{ SP } \approx v _{3}\left( P _{4}- P _{3}\right)=0.00192(1235-429)=1.55 kJ / kg \\&- w _{ P }=\frac{- w _{ SP }}{\eta_{ SP }}=\frac{1.55}{0.8}=1.94 kJ / kg \\& w _{ NET }= w _{ T }+ w _{ P }=35.4-1.94=33.46 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

 

For the heat exchanger,

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{ Q }_{ H }=640.05=\dot{ m }_{ C 4 H 10}\left( h _{1}- h _{4}\right) \\&\text { But } \quad h _{1}- h _{4}= h _{1}- h _{3}+ w _{ P } \\& h _{1}- h _{3}=\left( h _{1}- h _{1}^{*}\right)+\left( h _{1}^{*}- h _{3}^{*}\right)+\left( h _{3}^{*}- h _{3}\right) \\&\quad=-34.1+1.716(80-30)+292.5=344.2 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

 

Therefore

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{ m }_{ C 4 H 10}=\frac{640.05}{344.2-1.94}=1.87 kg / s \\&\dot{ W }_{ NET }=\dot{ m }_{ C 4 H 10} w _{ NET }=1.87 \times 33.46= 6 2 . 5 7 kW\end{aligned}\)
