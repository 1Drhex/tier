Guest Question.

During the trial of a single-cylinder double acting steam engine following is observed. Duration of trial = 15 min, Bore = 25 cm, Stroke = 30 cm, Brake diameter = 1.5 m, Net brake load = 300 N, Speed = 240 rpm, Steam pressure = 10 bar, Dryness fraction = 0.9, Cover end mean effective pressure = 0.9 bar, Crank end mean effective pressure = 0.9 bar, Steam utilized = 15 kg. Condensate temperature = 45°C. Amount of water circulating = 450 kg/hr. Rise in cooling water temperature = from 20°C to 30°C. Determine, (i) steam used per ihp. hr, (ii) mechanical efficiency, (iii) brake thermal efficiency, (iv) heat balance sheet on one hour basis.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{From given data;}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Average value of mean effective pressure}&= \frac{1}{2}(\text{mep}_{\text{cover end}} + \text{mep}_{\text{ crank end}})\\\text{mep}&= 0.9 bar\\\text{Steam consumption per hour}&= \frac{15}{15} × 60 = 60 kg/hr\\\text{Indicated horse power}&= \frac{\text{mep} × L × A × N × 2}{0.7457 × 60}, \left\{\text{as 1 HP = 0.7457 kW}\right\} \\&= \frac{0.9 × 10^{2} × 0.30 × \frac{π}{4}(0.25)^{2} × 240 × 2}{60 × 0.7457}\\&= \text{14.22 hp or 10.604 kJ/s or 38174.4 kJ/hr}\\\text{Therefore, steam used per ihp. hr}&= \frac{60}{14.22} = \text{4.22 kg/ihp. hr}\end{aligned}\\\text{Steam used per ihp. hr = 4.22 kg/ihp. hr}\\\text{Brake power can be obtained using the dynamometer data given i.e. brake load, brake drum diameter etc.}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Brake horse power}&= \frac{2 π N · T}{60 × 0.7457 × 10^{3}} = \frac{2 × π × 240 × 300 × 0.75}{60 × 0.7457 × 10^{3}}\\\text{Brake horse power}&= \text{7.58 hp or 5.65 kJ/s or 20340 kJ/hr}\\\text{Mechanical efficiency}&= \frac{\text{Brake horse power}}{\text{Indicated horse power}} = \text{0.5331 or 53.31}\%\\\text{Heat available with steam/hr}&=60 \left[h_{\text{at 10 bar, 0.9 dry}} – h_{\text{at 45°C}}\right] \\&=60 \left[(762.81 + 0.9 × 2015.3) – (4.18 × 45)\right] \\&= 143308.8 kJ/hr\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Brake thermal efficiency}&= \frac{\text{Heat equivalent of bhp}}{\text{Heat available with steam / sec}}\\&= \frac{7.58 × 0.7457 × 3600}{143308.8}\\&= \text{0.14199 or 14.19}\%\\\text{Heat carried away by cir}&\text{culating water in condenser} \\&= 450 × 4.18 × [30 – 20]\\&= 18810 kJ/hr\end{aligned}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \text{Heat balance sheet on one hour basis}\)
