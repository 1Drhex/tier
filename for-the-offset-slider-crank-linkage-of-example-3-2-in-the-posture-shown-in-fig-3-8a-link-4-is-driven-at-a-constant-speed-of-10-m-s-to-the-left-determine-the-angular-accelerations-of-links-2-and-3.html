Guest Question.

For the offset slider-crank linkage of in the posture shown in Fig. 3.8a, link 4 is driven at a constant speed of 10 m/s to the left. Determine the angular accelerations of links 2 and 3, and the acceleration of coupler point D.

Admin
Solution

The angular accelerations of links 2 and 3 are obtained by differentiating Eqs. (3) in ; with respect to the input displacement, r4; that is,

\(-r_{2} \sin \theta_{2} \theta_{2}^{\prime}-r_{3} \sin \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}=1,\)

 

\(r_{2} \cos \theta_{2} \theta_{2}^{\prime}+r_{3} \cos \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}=0,\) (3)

 

\(-r_{2} \cos \theta_{2} \theta_{2}^{\prime 2}-r_{2} \sin \theta_{2} \theta_{2}^{\prime \prime}-r_{3} \cos \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime 2}-r_{3} \sin \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime \prime}=0,\)

 

\(-r_{2} \sin \theta_{2} \theta_{2}^{\prime 2}+r_{2} \cos \theta_{2} \theta_{2}^{\prime \prime}-r_{3} \sin \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime 2}+r_{3} \cos \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime \prime}=0,\) (1)

where the second-order kinematic coefficients of links 2 and 3 are defined as

\(\theta_{2}^{\prime \prime}=\frac{d^{2} \theta_{2}}{d r_{4}^{2}} \text { and } \theta_{3}^{\prime \prime}=\frac{d^{2} \theta_{3}}{d r_{4}^{2}}.\)

We recall the first-order kinematic coefficients from Eq. (8) in ; they are

 

\(\theta_{2}^{\prime}=-19.856 rad / m \quad \text { and } \quad \theta_{3}^{\prime}=+5.447 rad / m,\) (8)

 

\(\theta_{2}^{\prime}=-19.856 rad / m \quad \text { and } \quad \theta_{3}^{\prime}=5.447 rad / m \text {. }\) (2)

Substituting Eqs. (2) and the known data into Eqs. (1), the second-order kinematic coefficients of links 2 and 3 are

\(\theta_{2}^{\prime \prime}=-246.25 rad / m ^{2} \quad \text { and } \quad \theta_{3}^{\prime \prime}=162.73 rad / m ^{2}.\) (3)

The angular accelerations of links 2 and 3 can be written from Table 4.2 as

\(\alpha_{2}=\theta_{2}^{\prime \prime} \dot{r}_{4}^{2}+\theta_{2}^{\prime} \ddot{r}_{4} \quad \text { and } \quad \alpha_{3}=\theta_{3}^{\prime \prime} \dot{r}_{4}^{2}+\theta_{3}^{\prime} \ddot{r}_{4}\) (4)

Substituting Eqs. (2) and (3), and the constant input speed \(\dot{r}_{4}=-10 m / s\) into Eq. (4), the angular accelerations of links 2 and 3 are

\(\alpha_{2}=-24625 rad / s ^{2}( cw ) \text { and } \alpha_{3}=16273 rad / s ^{2} ccw.\)

To obtain the acceleration of point D, we differentiate Eqs. (12) in with respect to the input displacement, r4. The second-order kinematic coefficients for point D are

\(x_{D}^{\prime}=-r_{2} \sin \theta_{2} \theta_{2}^{\prime}-r_{D B} \sin \left(\theta_{3}-\beta\right) \theta_{3}^{\prime}\) (12a)

 

\(y_{D}^{\prime}=r_{2} \cos \theta_{2} \theta_{2}^{\prime}+r_{D B} \cos \left(\theta_{3}-\beta\right) \theta_{3}^{\prime}\) (12b)

 

\(x_{D}^{\prime \prime}=-r_{2} \cos \theta_{2} \theta_{2}^{\prime 2}-r_{2} \sin \theta_{2} \theta_{2}^{\prime \prime}-r_{D B} \cos \left(\theta_{3}-\beta\right) \theta_{3}^{\prime 2}-r_{D B} \sin \left(\theta_{3}-\beta\right) \theta_{3}^{\prime \prime},\)

 

\(y_{D}^{\prime \prime}=-r_{2} \sin \theta_{2} \theta_{2}^{\prime 2}+r_{2} \cos \theta_{2} \theta_{2}^{\prime \prime}-r_{D B} \sin \left(\theta_{3}-\beta\right) \theta_{3}^{\prime 2}+r_{D B} \cos \left(\theta_{3}-\beta\right) \theta_{3}^{\prime \prime}.\) (5)

Substituting Eqs. (2) and (3) and the known data into Eqs. (5), we find

\(x_{D}^{\prime \prime}=6.52 m / m ^{2} \quad \text { and } \quad y_{D}^{\prime \prime}=-12.31 m / m ^{2} .\) (6)

Differentiating the velocity of point D, Eq. (14) in , with respect to time, the acceleration of point D can be written as

\(V_{D}=\left(x_{D}^{\prime} \hat{i}+y_{D}^{\prime} \hat{j}\right) \dot{r}_{4}\)​ (14)

\(A _{D}=\left(x_{D}^{\prime \prime} \dot{r}_{4}^{2}+x_{D}^{\prime} \ddot{r}_{4}\right) \hat{ i }+\left(y_{D}^{\prime \prime} \dot{r}_{4}^{2}+y_{D}^{\prime} \ddot{r}_{4}\right) \hat{ j }.\) (7)

Next, we recall the first-order kinematic coefficients for point D from Eq. (13) in as

\(x_{D}^{\prime}=1.123 m / m \quad \text { and } \quad y_{D}^{\prime}=-0.407 m / m .\) (13)

 

\(x_{D}^{\prime}=1.123 m / m \quad \text { and } \quad y_{D}^{\prime}=-0.407 m / m \text {. }\) (8)

Substituting Eqs. (6) and (8) and the constant input speed into Eq. (7), we get

\(A _{D}=652 \hat{ i }-1231 \hat{ j } m / s ^{2}.\)

 

Therefore, the magnitude of the acceleration of point D is AD = 1 393 m/s2.
