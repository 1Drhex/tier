Guest Question.

A local stoichiometric combustion of butene \(C _{4} H _{8}\) results in only half the C atoms burns to \(CO _{2}\) and the other half generates CO. This means the products should contain a mixture of \(H _{2} O , CO , CO _{2}, O _{2}\) and \(N _{2}\) which after heat transfer are at 1000 K.
a) Write the combustion equation assuming no hydrogen in the products
b) Use the water gas reaction to estimate the amount of hydrogen \(H _{2}\) present.

Admin
Solution

Select the amount of air for stoichiometric combustion
Combustion:

\(C _{4} H _{8}+6\left( O _{2}+3.76 N _{2}\right) \Rightarrow 2 CO _{2}+2 CO +4 H _{2} O +1 O _{2}+22.56 N _{2}\)

Since we have 2 CO we did not use \(1 O _{2}\) which is then left in the products.

Water-gas reaction: \(H _{2}+ CO _{2} \Leftrightarrow H _{2} O + CO\)

\(\begin{array}{lcccccl}& CO _{2} & CO & H _{2} O & H _{2} & O _{2} & N _{2} \\\text { Start } & 2 & 2 & 4 & 0 & 1 & 22.56 & \\\text { Change } & – x & x & x & – x & 0 & 0 & \\\text { Final } & 2- x & 2+ x & 4+ x & – x & 1 & 22.56 &\text { tot }=31.56\end{array}\)

 

Notice the total number of moles is constant. See for the equilibrium K

\(\begin{gathered}\text { At } 1000 K : \quad \ln K =0.5\left(\ln K _{1}-\ln K _{2}\right)=0.5[-47.052-(-46.321)]=-0.3655 \\K =\exp (-0.3655)=\left[ y _{ CO } y _{ H 2 O } / y _{ CO 2} y _{ H 2}\right] 1=\frac{2+ x }{2- x } \frac{4+ x }{- x }\end{gathered}\)

 

Reduce and solve: \((2-x)(-x) \exp (-0.3655)=(2+x)(4+x)\)

\(\Rightarrow-\left(2 x-x^{2}\right) \exp (-0.3655)=8+6 x+x^{2}\)

\(-2<x<0\) for all pos. concentrations \(\Rightarrow \quad x=-1.136\)

\(n _{ H 2}= 1 . 1 3 6 ~ m o l / \text { mol fuel or } 3.6 \%\)
