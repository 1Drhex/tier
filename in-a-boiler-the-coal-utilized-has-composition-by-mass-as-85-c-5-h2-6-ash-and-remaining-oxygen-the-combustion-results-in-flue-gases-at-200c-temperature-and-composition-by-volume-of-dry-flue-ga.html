Guest Question.

In a boiler the coal utilized has composition by mass as 85% C, 5% \(\textbf{H}_{2}\), 6% ash and remaining oxygen. The combustion results in flue gases at 200°C temperature and composition by volume of dry flue gas as 11% \(\textbf{CO}_{2}\), 1% CO, 8% \(\textbf{O}_{2}\) and 80% \(\textbf{N}_{2}\). The temperature of air is 20°C and the pressure of flue gas is 1.5 bar. Consider the specific heat of dry flue gas as 1.0032 kJ/kg, specific heat of superheated steam 2.05 kJ/kg.K, air to have 23% \(\textbf{O}_{2}\) by mass and calorific value of 1 kg CO getting burnt to \(\textbf{CO}_{2}\) as 10,000 kJ/kg.
Determine,
(a) total air supplied per kg of coal,
(b) heat carried away by moist flue gas per kg of coal, and
(c) the partial pressure of steam in hot flue gas.

Admin
Solution

Here for getting the mass of air supplied per kg of coal the combustion analysis may be carried out as under.

(b) = \(\frac{a}{Σ a}\)

Total mass of carbon = 0.0478
CO = 0.01 28 0.01 × 28 = 0.28 0.0093
O2 = 0.08 32 0.08 × 32 = 2.56 0.0085
N2 = 0.80 28 0.80 × 28 = 22.4 0.7457

\(\text{Mass of dry flue gas per kg of coal}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad &= \frac{\text{Mass of carbon per kg of coal (given as 0.85)}}{\text{Mass of carbon per kg of dry flue gas}}\\&= \frac{0.85}{0.0478} = \text{17.78 kg/kg of coal}\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{H}_{2}\text{O generated during combustion}&= \text{0.05 × 9 = 0.45 kg/kg of coal }\\\text{Mass of air supplied per kg of coal}&= 17.78 – (1 – ash – H_{2}O)\\&= 17.78 – (1 – 0.06 – 0.45)\\&= \text{17.29 kg/kg of coal}\\\text{Total air supplied per kg of coal}&= 17.29 kg\end{aligned}\\\text{For the pressure of flue gas being 1.5 bar the partial pressure of steam can be given as}\\\begin{aligned}\frac{\text{No.of H}_{2}\text{O mol in flue gas}}{\text{Total no.of mol in flue gas}} &× \text{Total pressure}\\& = \frac{\left\lgroup\frac{0.45}{18} \right\rgroup }{\left\lgroup\frac{17.78}{30.04}+ \frac{0.45}{18} \right\rgroup } × 1.05 = 0.04 bar\end{aligned}\\\text{The enthalpy of this steam from steam table,}\\\begin{aligned}h_{\text{g at 0.04bar}}&= 2554.4 kJ/kg, T_{sat} = 28.96°C\\\text{Therefore, heat in vapour}&=0.45 \left\{(2554.4 – 4.18 × 20) + 2.05 × (200 – 28.96)\right\} \\&= \text{1269.64 kJ/kg of coal}\end{aligned}\\\text{Heat in dry flue gas = 1.0032 × (17.78 × (200 – 20)) = 3210.64 kJ/kg of coal}\\\text{Heat in CO }= \frac{0.28}{30.04}\text{× 17.78 × 10000 = 1657.26 kJ/kg of coal}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Total heat carried by most flue gas}&= 1269.64 + 3210.64 + 1657.26\\&= \text{6137.54 kJ/kg of coal}\end{aligned}\\\text{Partial pressure of steam = 0.04 bar}\\\text{Heat carried by moist flue gas per kg of coal = 6137.54 kJ}\)
