Guest Question.

Determine the maximum compressive load that a HSS6 × 4 × 1/4 structural steel column (see Appendix B for cross-sectional properties) can support if it is 24 ft long and a factor of safety of 1.92 is specified. Use E = 29,000 ksi for the steel.

Admin
Solution

The following section properties for a standard steel HSS6 × 4 × 1/4 shape are given in Appendix B:

\(A=4.30 in .^{2}, I_{x}=20.9 in .^{4}, r_{x}=2.20 \text { in., } I_{y}=11.1 in .^{4}, r_{y}=1.61 in.\)

Euler buckling load:

\(P_{c r}=\frac{\pi^{2} E I}{L^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(29,000 ksi )\left(11.1 in .^{4}\right)}{[(24 ft )(12 in . / ft )]^{2}}=38.303 kips =38.3 kips\)

Allowable column load:

\(P_{\text {allow }}=\frac{P_{c r}}{ FS }=\frac{38.303 kips }{1.92}=19.95 kips\)
