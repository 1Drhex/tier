Guest Question.

A composite beam is made of wood and reinforced with a steel strap located on its bottom side. It has the cross-sectional area shown in Fig. 6–38 a . If the beam is subjected to a bending moment of \(M = 2 kN · m \), determine the normal stress at points B and C. Take \(E_w = 12 GPa \) and \(E_{st} = 200 GPa \).

Admin
Solution

Section Properties. Although the choice is arbitrary, here we will transform the section into one made entirely of steel. Since steel has a greater stiffness than wood \((E_{st}>E_w)\), the width of the wood is reduced to an equivalent width for steel. For this to be the case, \(n = E_w/E_{st}\), so that

\(b_{st}=nb_w=\frac{12GPa}{200GPa}(150mm)=9mm\)

The transformed section is shown in Fig. 6–38 b.
The location of the centroid (neutral axis), calculated from a reference axis located at the bottom of the section, is

\(\bar{y}=\frac{\sum{\bar{y} A} }{\sum{A} } =\frac{[0.01m](0.02m)(0.150m)+[0.095m](0.009m)(0.150m)}{0.02m(0.150m)+0.009m(0.150m) } =0.03638m\)

The moment of inertia about the neutral axis is therefore

\(I_{NA}=\left[\frac{1}{12}(0.150m)(0.02m)^3+(0.150m)(0.02m)(0.03638m-0.01m)^2 \right] \)

 

\(+\left[\frac{1}{12}(0.009m)(0.150m)^3+(0.009m)(0.150m)(0.095m-0.03638m)^2 \right] \)

 

\(=9.358(10^{-6})m^4\)

Normal Stress. Applying the flexure formula, the normal stress at B′ and C is

\(\sigma _{B^\prime }=\frac{2(10^3)N\cdot m(0.170m-0.03638m)}{9.358(10^{-6})m^4}=28.6MPa \)

 

\(\sigma _{C}=\frac{2(10^3)N\cdot m(0.03638m)}{9.358(10^{-6})m^4}=7.78MPa \)

The normal-stress distribution on the transformed (all steel) section is shown in Fig. 6–38 c .
The normal stress in the wood at B in Fig. 6–38 a , is determined from Eq. 6–21 ; that is,

\(\sigma _{B}=n\sigma _{B^\prime }=\frac{12GPa}{200GPa}(28.56MPa)=1.71MPa \)

Using these concepts, show that the normal stress in the steel and the wood at the point where they are in contact is \(σ_{st} = 3.50 MPa\) and \(σ_w = 0.210 MPa\), respectively. The normal-stress distribution in the actual beam is shown in Fig. 6–38 d .
