Guest Question.

The pin-connected structure shown in Figure P5.41 consists of two cold-rolled steel [E = 30,000 ksi] bars (1) and a bronze [E = 15,000 ksi] bar (2) that are connected at pin D. All three bars have cross-sectional areas of 0.375 \(\text { in. }^{2}\) . A load of P = 11 kips is applied to the structure at pin D. Using a = 3 ft and b = 5 ft, calculate:
(a) the normal stresses in bars (1) and (2).
(b) the downward displacement of pin D.

Admin
Solution

Equilibrium
Consider a FBD of joint D. Assume tension forces in bars (1) and (2). (Also note that the forces in the two steel bars must be the same because of symmetry.)

\(\begin{aligned}\tan \theta &=\frac{b}{a}=\frac{5 ft }{3 ft }=1.66667 \quad \theta=59.0362^{\circ} \\\Sigma F_{x} &=-F_{1} \cos \theta+F_{1} \cos \theta=0 \\\Sigma F_{y} &=F_{1} \sin \theta+F_{2}+F_{1} \sin \theta-P=0 \\& \therefore 2 F_{1} \sin \theta+F_{2}=P (a) \end{aligned}\)

Geometry-of-deformation relationship
Draw a deformation diagram. We imagine temporarily removing the pin at D and allowing the members to elongate independently. Then, after deformation, bars (1) are rotated so that they intersect the lower end of bar (2), and the pin at D is reinserted. If we assume that the deformations will be small, then the angle after deformation is approximately the same as the angle before deformation. From the deformation diagram geometry, we can assert that:

\(\frac{\delta_{1}}{\sin 59.0362^{\circ}}=\delta_{2}\) (b)

Force-Deformation Relationships
The relationship between the internal force and the deformation of an axial member can be stated for bars (1) and (2) as:

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}} \quad \delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}\) (c)

Compatibility Equation
Substitute the force-deformation relationships (c) into the geometry-of-deformation relationship (b) to derive the compatibility equation:

\(\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{\left(\sin 59.0362^{\circ}\right) A_{1} E_{1}}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}\) (d)

Solve the Equations
From the problem statement, \(A_{1}=A_{2}\) . The bar lengths are:

\(L_{1}=\sqrt{(3 ft )^{2}+(5 ft )^{2}}=5.8310 ft =69.9714 in . \quad L_{2}=5 ft =60 in.\)

Use these facts and solve Eq. (d) for \(F_{2}\):

\(F_{2}=F_{1} \frac{1}{\left(\sin 59.0362^{\circ}\right)}\left(\frac{L_{1}}{L_{2}}\right)\left(\frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}\right)=F_{1} \frac{1}{\left(\sin 59.0362^{\circ}\right)}\left(\frac{69.9714 in. }{60 in .}\right)\left(\frac{15,000 ksi }{30,000 ksi }\right)=0.6800 F_{1}\)

Substitute this result in Eq. (a) and solve for \(F_{1}\):

\(\begin{aligned}2 F_{1} \sin 59.0362^{\circ}+0.6800 F_{1} &=11 kips \\& \therefore F_{1}=\frac{11 kips }{2.3950}=4.5929 kips\end{aligned}\)

The force in bar (2) is therefore:

\(F_{2}=0.6800(4.5929 kips )=3.1232 kips\)

 

(a) Normal stresses in bars (1) and (2). The normal stresses in bars (1) and (2) can now be calculated:

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{1}=\frac{F_{1}}{A_{1}}=\frac{4.5929 kips }{0.375 in .^{2}}=12.25 ksi \\&\sigma_{2}=\frac{F_{2}}{A_{2}}=\frac{3.1232 kips }{0.375 in .^{2}}=8.33 ksi\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Downward displacement of pin D. The downward displacement of pin D is simply equal to the deformation of bar (2).

\(\begin{aligned}\delta_{2} &=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}=\frac{(3.1232 kips )(60 in .)}{\left(0.375 in .^{2}\right)(15,000 ksi )}=0.0333 in . \\& \therefore v_{D}=0.0333 in. \downarrow\end{aligned}\)
