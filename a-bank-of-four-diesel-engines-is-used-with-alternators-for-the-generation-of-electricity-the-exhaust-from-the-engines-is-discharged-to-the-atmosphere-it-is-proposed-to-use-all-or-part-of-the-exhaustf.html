Guest Question.

A bank of four diesel engines is used with alternators for the generation of electricity. The exhaust
from the engines is discharged to the atmosphere. It is proposed to use all or part of the exhaust
for heating air, which can then be used for space heating to reduce costs. From measurements of
velocity, it is determined that the mass flow rate of exhaust available from the engines is 90 kg/hr.
The exhaust-gas temperature is 600 K. The air is available at 20°C and is not of much use unless it
can be heated to at least 80°C at a mass flow rate of 100 kg/hr. There are a number of 4 × 3 doublepipe
exchangers that are 2 m long and made of type K copper tubing with compression fittings.
Determine (a) how many of them are required, (b) the overall coefficient of (all) the exchanger(s),
and (c) the pressure drop for each stream. Assume a counterfl ow arrangement.

 

Admin
Solution

The air is heated by the diesel exhaust with air inlet and outlet temperatures known. The diesel exhaust properties are not known.
Assumptions
1. Air properties are constant and evaluated at the average of the inlet and outlet temperatures.
2. Diesel exhaust has the same properties as carbon dioxide gas.
3. Diesel exhaust properties are constant. For lack of better information (i.e., outlet temperature of the exhaust gas), evaluate the exhaust properties at the average of the inlet temperatures:
\((600 + [20 + 273])/2 = 480 ≅ 500 K.\)
4. The system is at steady state.

From Appendix Table D.l, for air at 323 K,

 

\(\rho =1.088kg/m^{3} \) \( k_{f} =0.02814W/\left(m.K\right)\)

 

 

\(c_{p} =1007J/\left(kg.K\right) \) \( \alpha =0.26\times 10^{-4} m^{2} /s\)

 

 

\(\nu =18.2\times 10^{-6} m^{2} /s\) Pr=0.703

 

Also,\(\dot{m}_{b} =100kg/hr, t_{1} =20^{\circ } C=293K\),and \(t_{2} =80^{\circ }C=353K.\)

 

From Appendix Table D.2, for carbon dioxide gas at 500 K,

 

\(\rho =1.0732kg/m^{3}\) \( k_{f} =0.03352W/\left(m.K\right)\)

 

 

\(c_{p} =1013J/\left(kg.K\right) \) \(\alpha =0.3084\times 10^{-4} m^{2} /s\)

 

 

\(\nu =21.67\times 10^{-6} m^{2} /s\) Pr=0.702

 

Also,\(\dot{m}_{h} =90kg/hr and T_{1} =600K.\)

 

From Appendix Table F.2, for seamless copper tubing,

 

4 standard type K \( ID_{a} =9.8cm\)

 

3 standard type K \(ID_{p} =7.384cm\)

 

\( OD_{p} =7.938\)

 

Composing the calculations in a systematic order, we write

I. Flow Areas

 

\(A_{p} =\pi \left(0.07384 \right)^{2} /4=4.28\times 10^{-3} m^{2} \)

 

\(A_{a} =\pi \left(0.098^{2}-0.07984 \right)^{2} /4=2.59\times 10^{-3}m^{2} \)

 

Because\(A_{p}> A_{a}\),direct the air through the pipe (inner tube).

 

II. Heat Balance

 

\(Air q=\frac{100}{3600} \left(1007\right) \left(80-20\right) =1.68\times 10^{3} W\)

 

\(CO_{2} T_{2}=T_{1}-q/\dot{m}_{h} c_{ph} =600-\frac{1.68\times 10^{3}\left(3600\right) }{90\left(1013\right) } =534 K\)

 

 

III. LMTD

 

\(LMTD=\frac{\left(600-353\right)-\left(534-293\right) }{\ln \left(247/241\right) }=244 K\)

 

IV. Annulus Equivalent Diameters

 

\(D_{h}= 0.098-0.07938=0.01862m\)

 

\(D_{e}=\left(0.098^{2} -0.07938^{2} \right)/0.07938=0.0416 m\)

 

V. Reynolds Numbers

 

\(Air Re_{p}=\frac{100}{3600}\frac{1}{1.088\left(0.00428\right) } \frac{0.07384}{18.2\times 10^{-6} }=2.42\times 10^{4}\)

 

\(CO_{2} Re_{a}=\frac{90}{3600}\frac{1}{1.0732\left(0.00259\right) } \frac{0.0416}{21.67\times 10^{-6} }=1.73\times 10^{4}\)

VI. Nusselt Numbers

Air \(Nu_{p}=0.023(2.42\times 10^{4})^{{4}/{5}}(0.703)^{0.4}=64.2\)

 

\(CO_{2}NU_{2}=0.023(1.73\times 10^{4})^{{4}/{5}}(0.702)^{0.3}=50.8\)

VII. Film Coefficients

 

\(Air h_{i} =\frac{64.2\left(0.02814\right) }{0.07384}=24.5\)

 

\(h_{i}=24.5\frac{0.07384}{0.07938} =22.8 W/\left(m^{2}.K \right)\)

 

\(CO_{2} h_{o}=\frac{50.8\left(0.03352\right) }{0.0416}=40.9 W/\left(m^{2}.K \right)\)

 

VIII. Fouling Factors

\(Air R_{di}=0.0004 \left(m^{2}.K \right)/W \)

 

\(CO_{2} R_{do}=\left(0.002\right) \left(m^{2}.K \right)/W \)

 

IX. Exchanger Coefficients

\(\frac{1}{U_{o} }=\frac{1}{22.8} +\frac{1}{40.9} \) \( U_{o} =14.6 W/(m^{2}\cdot K)\)

 

\(\frac{1}{U_{o} } +\frac{1}{14.6} +0.0004+0.002 \) \(U_{o} =14.1 \left(m^{2}.K \right)/W \)

 

X. Area Required

 

\(q=UA_{o}\) LMTD \(A_{o}=\frac{1.68\times 10^{3} }{\left(14.1\right) \left(244\right) } =0.487 m^{2}\)

One exchanger has a surface area of \(A_{o} =\pi D_{o} L.So,\)

\(L=\frac{0.49}{\pi \left(0.07938\right) } =1.95 m\)

 

Each available exchanger is 2 m in length. Therefore, only one exchanger is required.

XI. Friction Factors

 

\(\left. { \begin{matrix} Air \ Re_p=2.42 × 10^4 \\ smooth – wall \ tubing \end{matrix}} \right\}f_{p}=0.0245\) (Figure 6.14)

 

\(CO_{2} Re_{a}=\frac{90}{3600}\frac{1}{1.0732\left(0.00259\right) } \frac{0.01862}{21.67\times 10^{-6} } \)

 

\(\left.{ \begin{matrix}=7.73\times 10^{3} \\ smooth – wall \ tubing\end{matrix} } \right\}f_{a} =0.033\) (Figure 6.14)

 

 

XII. Velocities\(\left(=\dot{m} /\rho A\right) \)

 

Air \(V_{p} =\frac{100/3600}{1.088\left(0.00428\right) } =5.97 m/s\)

 

\(CO_{2} V_{a}=\frac{90/3600}{1.073\left(0.00259\right) } =9.0 m/s\)

 

XIII. Pressure drops \(\left(L=2 m actual length\right) \)

\(Air \Delta P_{p} =\frac{0.0245\left(2\right) }{0.07384} \frac{1.088\left(5.97\right) ^{2} }{2} =12.87 Pa\)

 

\(CO_{2} \Delta P_{a} =\frac{1.073\left(9\right) ^{2} }{2}\left[\frac{0.033\left(2\right) }{0.01862}+1 \right] =197 Pa\)

XIV. Summary of requested information
a. One exchanger required

b. \( U_{o} =14.6,U=14.1 W/\left(m^{2} .K\right)\)

c. Air pressure drop = 12.87 Pa
Diesel exhaust pressure drop = 197 Pa

 

Because the length of each exchanger is longer than that required by the conditions of the problem, more heat than specified will be exchanged. This means that the outlet temperature of the air will be greater than 80°C, and the outlet temperature of the diesel exhaust will be lower than 583 K. The actual outlet temperatures can be calculated with Equations 9.14 and 9.16.

 

Double-pipe heat exchangers can be set up in a multitude of ways. The fluids can travel through a bank of exchangers all in series. Alternatively, the annulus fluid can travel through a bank arranged in parallel, while the tube fluid can travel through the same bank in series; or vice versa. Such modifi cations are used for increased heat recovery or to reduce the pressure drop of one of the streams.*
