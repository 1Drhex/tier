Guest Question.

A displacement diagram of the piston operating in a compressor was plotted in . This diagram was plotted relative to the crankshaft rotation. Use this data to plot the piston displacement relative to time when the crankshaft is driven by an electric motor at 1750 rpm. 1.Calculate the Time for 30° of Crank Rotation 2.Add Time Column to Displacement Table 3.Use Displacement and Time Data to Plot a Displacement Curve.

Admin
Solution

1: The main task of this problem is to convert the increment of crank angle in Figure 4.41 to time. The x-axis increment is 30° and the crankshaft rotates at 1750 rpm. To keep units consistent, the x-axis increment is converted to revolutions.

Δθ = 30° \((\frac{1\ rev}{360^\circ } )\) = 0.08333 rev

The time increment for the crank to rotate 0.08333 rev (30°) can be computed from equation \(\Delta t=\frac{\Delta \theta }{\omega }\).

\(\Delta t=\frac{\Delta \theta }{\omega }=\frac{(0.08333\ rev)}{(1750\ rev/min)}\)

= 0.0000476 min

= (0.0000476 min) \((\frac{60\ s}{1\ min} )\) = 0.00286 s

2: The results of position analysis are reproduced with the time increment inserted into a spreadsheet. This is shown as Figure 6.40, which shows time tabulated in thousandths of a second. If not familiar with a spreadsheet, refer to Chapter 8.

3: Using a spreadsheet, these values are plotted in Figure 6.41 to form a displacement diagram relative to time.

These displacement diagrams relative to time can be used to generate a velocity curve because

Velocity = \(\frac{d(displacement)}{d(time)}\)

Differential calculus declares that the velocity at a particular instant is the slope of the displacement diagram at that instant. The task is to estimate the slope of the displacement diagram at several points.
