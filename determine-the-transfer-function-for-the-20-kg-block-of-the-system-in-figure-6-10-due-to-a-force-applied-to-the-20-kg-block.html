Guest Question.

Determine the transfer function for the 20 kg block of the system in Figure 6.10 due to a force applied to the 20 kg block.

Admin
Solution

The differential equations governing the system are

\(20\ddot{x}_{1}+2000\dot{x}_{1}-2000\dot{x}_{2}+60,000x_{1}-20,0000x_{2} = 0\) (a)
\(40\ddot{x}_{2}+2000\dot{x}_{1}-12000\dot{x}_{2}+20,000x_{1}-20,000x_{2}=F(t)\) (b)

Taking the Laplace transform of both equations and using the properties of the transform of derivatives and linearity yields

\( (20s^{2}+2000s+60.000)X_{1}(s)-(2000s+20,000)X_{2}(s)=0\) (c)

\(-(2000s + 20,000)X_{1}(s)+(40s^{2}+2000s+20,000)X_{2}(s)=F(s) \) (d)

Rewriting Equations (c) and (d) in matrix form

\(\begin{bmatrix} 20s^{2}+2000s +60,000 & -2000S – 20,000 \\ -2000S-20,000 & 40S^{2}+2000S+20.000 \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix}X_{1}(s)\\ X_{2}(s) \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ F(s) \end{matrix} \right] \) (e)

Cramerâ€™s rule is used to solve for \(X_{2}(s)\), leading to

\(X_{1}(s)=\frac{\begin{vmatrix} 20s^{2}+2000s+60,000 & 0 \\ -2000s+20,000 & F(s)\end{vmatrix} }{\begin{vmatrix} 20s^{2}+2000s +60,000 & -2000s-20,000 \\ -2000s-20,000 & 40s^{2}+2000s+20,000 \end{vmatrix}} \) (f)

Evaluation of the determinants leads to

\(X_{2}(s)=\frac{(20s^{2}+2000s+60.000)F(s)}{800s^{4}+1.2\times10^5s^3+2.8\times 10^{6}s^2+8\times10^{7}s+8\times10^{8}} \) (g)

The appropriate transfer function is

\(G_{22}(s)=\frac{20s^{2}+2000s+60,000}{800s^{4}+1.2\times10^{5}s^{3}+2.8\times10^{6}s^{2}+8\times 10^{7}s+8\times 10^{8}} \) (h)
