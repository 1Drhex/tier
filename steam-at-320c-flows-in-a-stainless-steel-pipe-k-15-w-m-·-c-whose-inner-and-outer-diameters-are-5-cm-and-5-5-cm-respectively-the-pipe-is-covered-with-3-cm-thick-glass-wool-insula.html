Guest Question.

Steam at 320°C flows in a stainless steel pipe (\(k = 15 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \)) whose inner and outer diameters are 5 cm and 5.5 cm, respectively. The pipe is covered with 3-cm-thick glass wool insulation (\(k = 0.038 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\)). Heat is lost to the surroundings at 5°C by natural convection and radiation, with a combined natural convection and radiation heat transfer coefficient of \(15 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\). Taking the heat transfer coefficient inside the pipe to be \(80 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\), determine the rate of heat loss from the steam per unit length of the pipe. Also determine the temperature drops across the pipe shell and the insulation.

Admin
Solution

A steam pipe covered with 3-cm thick glass wool insulation is subjected to convection on its surfaces. The rate of heat transfer per unit length and the temperature drops across the pipe and the insulation are to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Heat transfer is steady since there is no indication of any change with time. 2 Heat transfer is one-dimensional since there is thermal symmetry about the center line and no variation in the axial direction. 3 Thermal conductivities are constant. 4 The thermal contact resistance at the interface is negligible.

Properties The thermal conductivities are given to be \(k=15 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\) for steel and \( k = 0.038 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\) for glass wool insulation

Analysis The inner and the outer surface areas of the insulated
pipe per unit length are

\( A_{i}=\pi D_{i} L=\pi(0.05 m )(1 m )=0.157 m ^{2}\)

 

\( A_{o}=\pi D_{o} L=\pi(0.055+0.06 m )(1 m )=0.361 m ^{2}\)

 

The individual thermal resistances are

\( R_{i}=\frac{1}{h_{i} A_{i}}=\frac{1}{\left(80 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(0.157 m ^{2}\right)}=0.08{ }^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\( R_{1}=R_{\text {pipe }}=\frac{\ln \left(r_{2} / r_{1}\right)}{2 \pi k_{1} L}=\frac{\ln (2.75 / 2.5)}{2 \pi\left(15 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)(1 m )}=0.00101^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\( R_{2}=R_{\text {insulation }}=\frac{\ln \left(r_{3} / r_{2}\right)}{2 \pi k_{2} L}=\frac{\ln (5.75 / 2.75)}{2 \pi\left(0.038 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)(1 m )}=3.089{ }^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\( R_{o}=\frac{1}{h_{o} A_{o}}=\frac{1}{\left(15 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(0.361 m ^{2}\right)}=0.1847{ }^{\circ} C / W \)

 

\( R_{\text {total }}=R_{i}+R_{1}+R_{2}+R_{o}=0.08+0.00101+3.089+0.1847=3.355^{\circ} C / W \)

 

Then the steady rate of heat loss from the steam per m. pipe length becomes

\( \dot{Q}=\frac{T_{\infty 1}-T_{\infty 2}}{R_{\text {total }}}=\frac{(320-5)^{\circ} C }{3.355^{\circ} C / W }=93.9 W\)

 

The temperature drops across the pipe and the insulation are

\( \Delta T_{\text {pipe }}=\dot{Q} R_{\text {pipe }}=(93.9 W )\left(0.00101^{\circ} C / W \right)= 0 . 0 9 5 ^{\circ} C\)

 

\( \Delta T_{\text {insulation }}=\dot{Q} R_{\text {insulation }}=(93.9 W )\left(3.089^{\circ} C / W \right)= 2 9 0 ^{\circ} C\)
