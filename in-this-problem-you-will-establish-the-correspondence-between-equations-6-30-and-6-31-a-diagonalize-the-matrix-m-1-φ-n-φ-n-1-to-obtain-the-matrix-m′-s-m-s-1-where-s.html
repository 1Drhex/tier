Guest Question.

In this problem you will establish the correspondence between Equations 6.30 and 6.31.

\( \left(\begin{array}{c} \hat{x}^{\prime} \\ \hat{y}^{\prime} \\ \hat{z}^{\prime} \end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & -\delta & 0 \\ \delta & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} \hat{x} \\ \hat{y} \\ \hat{z} \end{array}\right) \) (6.30).

\( \left(\begin{array}{l} \hat{x}^{\prime} \\ \hat{y}^{\prime} \\ \hat{z}^{\prime} \end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{ccc} \cos \varphi & -\sin \varphi & 0 \\ \sin \varphi & \cos \varphi & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} \hat{x} \\ \hat{y} \\ \hat{z} \end{array}\right) ? \) (6.31).

(a) Diagonalize the matrix

\( M=\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & -\varphi / N \\ \varphi / N & 1 \end{array}\right) \),

to obtain the matrix \( M ^{\prime}= SMS ^{-1} \) ,

where \( S ^{-1} \) is the unitary matrix whose columns are the (normalized) eigenvectors of M.

(b) Use the binomial expansion to show that \( \lim _{N \rightarrow \infty}\left( M ^{\prime}\right)^{N} \) is a diagonal matrix with entries \( e^{-i \varphi} \text { and } e^{i \varphi} \) on the diagonal.

(c) Transform back to the original basis to show that

\( \lim _{N \rightarrow \infty} M ^{N}= S ^{-1}\left[\lim _{N \rightarrow \infty}\left( M ^{\prime}\right)^{N}\right] S \).

agrees with the matrix in Equation 6.31.

\( \left(\begin{array}{l} \hat{x}^{\prime} \\ \hat{y}^{\prime} \\ \hat{z}^{\prime} \end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{ccc} \cos \varphi & -\sin \varphi & 0 \\ \sin \varphi & \cos \varphi & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} \hat{x} \\ \hat{y} \\ \hat{z} \end{array}\right) ? \) (6.31).

Solution

(a) We first compute the eigenvalues of M.

\( 0=|M-\lambda 1|=\left|\begin{array}{cc} 1-\lambda & -\varphi / N \\ \varphi / N & 1-\lambda \end{array}\right|=\left(1-\lambda^{2}\right)+\left(\frac{\varphi}{N}\right)^{2} \).

so that \( \lambda=1 \pm i \varphi / N \). The corresponding eigenvectors are

\( M v _{\pm}=(1 \pm i \varphi / N) v _{\pm} \) \( \left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & -\varphi / N \\ \varphi / N & 1 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} a \\ b \end{array}\right)=(1 \pm i \varphi / N)\left(\begin{array}{l} a \\ b \end{array}\right) \)

\( \biggl(\begin{matrix}a – \varphi /Nb \\ & \end{matrix}\biggr) = \biggl(\begin{matrix}a \pm i \varphi / N a \\ & \end{matrix}\biggr) \).

\( \text { so that } b=\mp i a \text {. The matrix } S ^{-1} \text { is then } \).

\( S =\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & 1 \\ -i & i \end{array}\right) \).

and the matrix M′ is

\( M ^{\prime}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & i \\ 1 & -i \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & -\varphi / N \\ \varphi / N & 1 \end{array}\right) \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & 1 \\ -i & i \end{array}\right) \).

\( =\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1+i \varphi / N & 0 \\ 0 & 1-i \varphi / N \end{array}\right) \).

(b) Now

\( \left(1 \pm i \frac{\varphi}{N}\right)^{N}=\sum_{k=0}^{N}\left(\begin{array}{l} N \\ k \end{array}\right)\left(\pm i \frac{\varphi}{N}\right)^{k}=\sum_{k=0}^{N} \frac{N !}{(N-k) ! N^{k}} \frac{(\pm i \varphi)^{k}}{k !} \).

The coefficient

\( \frac{N !}{(N-k) ! N^{k}}=\prod_{i=0}^{k-1}\left(1-\frac{i}{N}\right) \).

becomes 1 in the limit that N → ∞ and

\( \lim _{N \rightarrow \infty}\left(1 \pm i \frac{\varphi}{N}\right)^{N}=\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{(\pm i \varphi)^{k}}{k !}=e^{\pm i \varphi} \).

Therefore

\( \lim _{N \rightarrow \infty}\left( M ^{\prime}\right)^{N}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} e^{i \varphi} & 0 \\ 0 & e^{-i \varphi} \end{array}\right) \).

(c) Finally we transform back to the original basis to get

\( \lim _{N \rightarrow \infty} M ^{N}= S ^{-1} \lim _{N \rightarrow \infty}\left( M ^{\prime}\right)^{N} S \).

\( =\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & 1 \\ -i & i \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{cc} e^{i \varphi} & 0 \\ 0 & e^{-i \varphi} \end{array}\right) \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & i \\ 1 & -i \end{array}\right) \).

\( =\left(\begin{array}{cc} \cos \varphi & -\sin \varphi \\ \sin \varphi & \cos \varphi \end{array}\right) \).
