Guest Question.

In Figure P4.11, rigid bar ABC is supported at A by a single shear pin connection and at B by a strut, which consists of two 2 in. wide by 0.25 in. thick steel bars. The pins at A, B, and D each have a diameter of 0.5 in. The yield strength of the steel bars in strut (1) is 36 ksi, and the ultimate shear strength of each pin is 72 ksi. Determine the allowable load P that may be applied to the rigid bar at C if an overall factor of safety of 3.0 is required. Use \(L_{1}\) = 36 in. and \(L_{2}\) = 24 in.

Admin
Solution

From a FBD of the rigid beam ABC, the following equilibrium equations can be written:

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma F_{x}=A_{x}=0 (a) \\&\Sigma F_{y}=A_{y}-F_{1}-P=0 (b)\\&\Sigma M_{A}=-L_{1} F_{1}-\left(L_{1}+L_{2}\right) P=0 (c)\end{aligned}\)

From Eq. (c), express \(F_{1}\) in terms of the unknown load P:

\(F_{1}=-\frac{L_{1}+L_{2}}{L_{1}} P=-\frac{36 in. +24 in .}{36 in .} P=-1.666667 P\) (d)

Substitute this result into Eq. (b) to express \(A_{y}\) in terms of P:

\(A_{y}=P+F_{1}=P+(-1.666667 P)=-0.666667 P\)

The resultant reaction force at pin A can now be expressed as a function of P:

\(|A|=\sqrt{A_{x}^{2}+A_{y}^{2}}=\sqrt{(0)^{2}+(-0.666667 P)^{2}}=0.666667 P\) (e)

Strut (1):
The allowable normal stress for strut (1) is

\(\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\sigma_{Y}}{ FS }=\frac{36 ksi }{3.0}=12.0 ksi\)

Therefore, the allowable axial force for strut (1) is

\(F_{\text {allow }, 1}=\sigma_{\text {allow }} A_{1}=(12.0 ksi )(2 \times 2 in . \times 0.25 in .)=12.0 \text { kips }\)

From Eq. (d), the maximum load that may be applied to the rigid bar based on the strut normal stress limitation is

\(P_{\max } \leq \frac{F_{\text {allow, } 1}}{1.666667}=\frac{12.0 kips }{1.666667}=7.20 kips\) (f)

Pins:
The allowable shear stress for the pins at A, B, and D is

\(\tau_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\tau_{U}}{ FS }=\frac{72 ksi }{3.0}=24.0 ksi\)

The cross-sectional area of a 0.5-in.-diameter pin is

\(A_{ pin }=\frac{\pi}{4}(0.5 in .)^{2}=0.196350 in .^{2}\)

 

Pin A:
The pin at A is a single shear connection; therefore, \(A_{V, A}=A_{\text {pin }}\) . The allowable reaction force at A is

\(|A|_{\text {allow }}=\tau_{\text {allow }} A_{V, A}=(24.0 ksi )\left(0.196350 in. ^{2}\right)=4.7124 \text { kips }\)

From Eq. (e), the maximum load that may be applied to the rigid bar based on the limitations of pin A is

\(P_{\max } \leq \frac{|A|_{\text {allow }}}{0.666667}=\frac{4.7124 kips }{0.666667}=7.0686 kips\) (g)

Pin B and D:
The pins at B and D are in double shear connections; therefore, \(A_{V, B}=2 A_{\text {pin }}\) . The allowable force in strut (1) based on the capacity of the pins at B and D is

\(F_{\text {allow }, 1}=\tau_{\text {allow }} A_{V, B}=(24.0 ksi )(2)\left(0.196350 in.^{2}\right)=9.4248 \text { kips }\)

From Eq. (d), the maximum load that may be applied to the rigid bar based on the limitations of the pins at B and D is

\(P_{\max } \leq \frac{F_{\text {allow }, 1}}{1.666667}=\frac{9.4248 kips }{1.666667}=5.6549 kips\) (h)

Maximum load P:
Comparing the results in Eqs. (f), (g), and (h), the maximum load P that may be applied to the rigid bar is

\(P_{\max }=5.6549 kips =5.65 kips\)
