Guest Question.

Prove (3.11)

\(RS\left(\omega \right)R^{T} \) = \(S\left(R\omega \right)\) (3.11)

Admin
Solution

Let

\(R = \left[x y z\right]^{T}\)

 

where the unit vector triplet (x,y,z) forms a right-handed frame. Then, the product \(RS\left(\omega \right)R^{T}\) can be written as

\(RS\left(\omega \right)R^{T}\) = \(\left[\begin{matrix} x^{T} \\ y^{T} \\ z^{T} \end{matrix} \right] S\left(\omega \right) \left[x y z\right]\)

= \(\left[\begin{matrix} x^{T} S\left(\omega \right)x & x^{T} S\left(\omega \right)y & x^{T} S\left(\omega \right)z\\ y^{T} S\left(\omega \right)x & y^{T} S\left(\omega \right)y & y^{T} S\left(\omega \right)z \\ z^{T} S\left(\omega \right)x & z^{T} S\left(\omega \right)y & z^{T} S\left(\omega \right)z \end{matrix} \right]\)

= \(\left[\begin{matrix} x^{T} \left(\omega × x\right) & x^{T} \left(\omega × y\right) & x^{T} \left(\omega × z\right) \\ y^{T} \left(\omega × x \right) & y^{T} \left(\omega × y \right) & y^{T} \left(\omega × z \right) \\ z^{T} \left(\omega × x\right) & z^{T} \left(\omega × y\right) & z^{T} \left(\omega × z\right) \end{matrix} \right]\)

In view of the properties of scalar triple products, this matrix is skew symmetric and then

\(RS\left(\omega \right)R^{T}\) = \(S\left(\omega^{\prime } \right) \) = \(\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & -\omega^{\prime }_{z} & \omega^{\prime }_{y}\\ \omega^{\prime }_{z} & 0 & -\omega^{\prime }_{x}\\ -\omega^{\prime }_{y} & \omega^{\prime }_{x} &0 \end{matrix} \right] \) (S3.1)

where the elements of the previous matrix can be written as

\(\omega^{\prime }_{x} \) = \( z^{T} \left(\omega × y\right)\) = \(\omega^{T} \left(y × z\right) \) = \(\omega^{T} x \)

\(\omega^{\prime }_{y} = x^{T} \left(\omega × z\right)\) = \( \omega^{T} \left(z × x\right)\) = \(\omega^{T} y\)

\(\omega^{\prime }_{z} \) = \( y^{T} \left(\omega × x\right) \) = \(\omega^{T} \left(x × y\right)\) = \(\omega^{T} z\)

Hence, it is

\( \omega ^{\prime }= \left[\begin{matrix} \omega^{\prime }_{z} & \omega^{\prime }_{z} & \omega^{\prime }_{z}\end{matrix} \right]^{T} \\ \quad = \left[\omega^{T } \left[x y z\right]\right] ^{T} \\ \quad = R\omega\)

 

Substituting \(\omega ^{\prime }\) in (S3.1) leads to conclude

\(RS\left(\omega \right)R^{T} \) = \(S\left(R\omega \right)\)
