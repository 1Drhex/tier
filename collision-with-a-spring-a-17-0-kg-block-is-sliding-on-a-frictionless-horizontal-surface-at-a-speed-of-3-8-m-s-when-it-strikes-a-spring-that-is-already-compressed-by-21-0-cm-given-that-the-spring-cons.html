Collision with a Spring

A 17.0 kg block is sliding on a frictionless horizontal surface at a speed of 3.8 m/s when it strikes a spring that is already compressed by 21.0 cm. Given that the spring constant is 710 N/m, find the distance by which the spring is further compressed during the collision.

Admin
Solution

Since the force exerted by the spring on the block is not constant, the deceleration of the box is also not constant. Therefore, the kinematics equations for constant acceleration developed in Chapter 4 are not applicable in this situation. However, we can apply the workâ€“energy theorem because the work done by the spring on the block depends only on the initial and final lengths.

The only force doing work on the block is the force exerted by the spring; therefore by work-energy theorem,

 

\(W_{\text {net }}=W_{\text {by-spring }}=\Delta K\)

 

\(W_{\text {by-spring }}=\frac{1}{2} k x_{ i }^{2}-\frac{1}{2} k x_{ f }^{2}\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{2} m v_{ f }^{2}-\frac{1}{2} m v_{ i }^{2}\) (1)

 

Since the final speed of the block is zero \(\left(v_{ f }=0\right)\) at maximum spring compression, we get

 

\(\frac{1}{2} k x_{ f }^{2}=\frac{1}{2} m v_{ i }^{2}+\frac{1}{2} k x_{ i }^{2}\) (2)

 

and

 

\(x_{ f }^{2}=\frac{1}{k}\left(m v_{ i }^{2}+k x_{ i }^{2}\right)\)

 

\(x_{ f }^{2}=\frac{1}{710 N / m }\left[(17.0 kg )(3.8 m / s )^{2}+(710 N / m )(0.210 m )^{2}\right]\)

 

\(x_{ f }=0.62 m\)

 

The distance by which the spring further compresses is the difference between the final and initial compressions:

 

\(\Delta x=x_{ f }-x_{ i }=0.62 m -0.210 m =0.41 m\)

 

Making sense of the result

 

If the spring had not been compressed initially, the result would have been

 

\(\frac{1}{2} k x_{f}^{2}=\frac{1}{2} m v_{i}^{2} \text { and } \Delta x=0.59 m\)

 

Since the more compressed a spring is, the harder it becomes to compress it further, an already compressed spring will therefore compress less than 0.59 m.
