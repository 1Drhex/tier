Guest Question.

Determine the steam consumption rate and the heat available for process heating from the pass out turbine installation having steam entering HP turbine stage at 20 bar, 350°C and the steam pressure before first stage nozzles is 17.5 bar. Steam is available for process heating at 5 bar pressure, the pressure before LP turbine’s first stage nozzles is 4 bar and the exhaust pressure is 0.075 bar. Condensed steam temperature after process heating is 90°C. Electric power developed is 8 mW, alternator efficiency is 97.5%, turbine’s mechanical losses are 50 kW, HP & LP turbine efficiency are 80% each and condenser discharge is 8 kg/s.

Admin
Solution

All the salient states are tentatively shown on h-s diagram. Steam table can be used to get various enthalpy values at salient states.
Also mollier diagram can be used for quick but approximate analysis.
\(\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad h_{1}&= h_{\text{20bar, 350°C}} = 3137 kJ/kg.\\s_{1}&= 6.9563 kJ/kg.K\\h_{1}^{\prime}&= h_{1} = 3137 kJ/kg.\end{aligned}\\\text{Let us assume total steam consumption rate be }m_{1}\text{ kg/s and }m_{h}\text{ be the mass flowrate of steam for process heating and }\\m_{3}\text{ kg/s be steam flow rate to LP stage for expansion.}\\\text{Let us find out remaining enthalpy and other properties through mollier diagram.}\\\begin{aligned}\text{entropy} \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad s_{1}^{\prime}&= s_{2} = 7 kJ/kg.K.\\h_{2}&= 2830 kJ/kg.\\\text{Considering HP stage efficiency,}\quad \qquad &\\0.8&= \frac{h_{1^{\prime}} – h_{2^{\prime}}}{h_{1^{\prime}} – h_{2}}\\0.8&= \frac{(3137 – h_{2^{\prime}})}{(3137 – 2830)} ⇒ h_{2^{\prime}} = 2891.4 kJ/kg\\h_{3}&= h_{2^{\prime}} = 2891.4 kJ/kg\\\text{From mollier diagram, }\qquad\qquad \qquad s_{3}&= s_{4} = 7.1 kJ/kg.K\\h_{4}&= 2210 kJ/kg\\\text{Considering LP stage efficiency,} \qquad &\\0.8&= \frac{(h_{3} – h_{4^{\prime}})}{(h_{3} – h_{4})}\\0.8&= \frac{(2891.4 – h_{4^{\prime}})}{(2891.4 – 2210)} ⇒ h_{4^{\prime}}= 2346.28 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Consider discharge of 8 kg/s to be the steam flow rate through LP turbine, i.e., }m_{3} = 8 kg/s.\\\text{The power made available to alternator can be estimated by considering electric power developed.}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Power available to alternator}&= \frac{8}{0.975} = 8.2051 MW\\\text{Total power produced}&= 8.2051 × 10^{3} + 50 = 8255.1 kW\\\text{Power produced by LP turbine}&= m_{3} × (h_{3} – h_{4^{\prime}}) = 5451.2 kW.\\\text{Power produced by HP turbine}&= 8255.1 – 5451.2\\&= 2803.9 kW\\\text{Mass flow rate through HP turbine ⇒ }m_{1}&= \frac{2803.9}{(h_{1^{\prime}} – h_{2^{\prime}})}\\m_{1}&= \frac{2803.9}{(3137 − 2891.4)}\\\text{Total steam consumption rate. } \quad\qquad m_{1}&= 11.42 kg/s\\\text{Heat available for process heating}&= m_{h} × (h_{2} – h_{\text{f at 90°C}})\\&=(m_{1} – m_{3}) (2830 – 376.92) \\&= (11.42 – 8) (2830 – 376.92)\\&=8389.53 kW\end{aligned}\)
