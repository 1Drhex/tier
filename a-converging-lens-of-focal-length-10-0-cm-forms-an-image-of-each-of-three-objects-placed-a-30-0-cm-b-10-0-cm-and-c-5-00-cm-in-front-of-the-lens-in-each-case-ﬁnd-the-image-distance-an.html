Guest Question.

A converging lens of focal length 10.0 cm forms an image of each of three objects placed (a) 30.0 cm, (b) 10.0 cm, and (c) 5.00 cm in front of the lens. In each case, ﬁnd the image distance and describe the image.

Admin
Solution

Solution (a) The thin-lens equation can be used again:

\( \frac{1}{t}+\frac{1}{q} =\frac{1}{f} \) \( \frac{1}{30.0 \mathrm{~cm}}+\frac{1}{q} =\frac{1}{10.0 \mathrm{~cm}} \) \( q =15.0 \mathrm{~cm}\)

The poeitive sign indicates that the image is in back of the lens and real. The magnification is

\( M=-\frac{q}{t}=-\frac{15.0 \mathrm{~cm}}{30.0 \mathrm{~cm}}=-0.500\)

The image is reduced in size by one half, and the negativesign for M means that the image is inverted. The situation is like that pictured in Figure 36.27\( \mathrm{a}\).

(b) No calculation is necessary for this case because we know that when the object is placed at the focal point, the image is fomed at infinity. We can readily werify this by substituting \( b=10.0 \mathrm{~cm}\) into the thin-lens equation.

(c) We now move inside the focal point, to an object distance of \( 5.00 \mathrm{~cm}:\)

\( \frac{1}{5.00 \mathrm{~cm}}+\frac{1}{q} =\frac{1}{10.0 \mathrm{~cm}}\) \( q =-10.0 \mathrm{~cm} \) \( M =-\frac{q}{t}=-\left(\frac{-10.0 \mathrm{~cm}}{5.00 \mathrm{~cm}}\right)=2.00\)

The negative image distance indicates that the image is in front of the lens and virtual. The image is enlarged, and the poeititre sign for M tells us that the image is upright, as shown in Figure \( 36.27 \mathrm{~b}\).
