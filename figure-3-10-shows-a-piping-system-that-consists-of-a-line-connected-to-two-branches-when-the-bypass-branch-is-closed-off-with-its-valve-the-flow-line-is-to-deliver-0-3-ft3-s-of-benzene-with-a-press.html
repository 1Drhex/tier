Guest Question.

Figure 3.10 shows a piping system that consists of a line connected to two branches. When the bypass branch is closed off with its valve, the flow line is to deliver \(0.3 ft^3/s\) of benzene with a pressure drop of \(p_1 – p_2\) of 8.5 psi. Select a suitable size for the pipe if it is made of uncoated cast iron and has regular threaded fittings. The length of pipe required is 700 ft. Due to cost considerations, it is desirable to use schedule 40 pipe.

Admin
Solution

The control volume we select includes all the fluid in the pipe from the gage at section 1 to the gage at section 2, excluding the bypass. The
method of solution is
benzene \(ρ = 0.876(62.4) lbm/ft^3\) \(µ = 1.26 \times 10^{-5} lbf·s/ft^2\) [App. Table B.1]
uncoated cast iron ε = 0.00085 ft [Table 3.1]
Continuity equation:
\(Q = A_1V_1 = A_2V_2 A_1 = A_2\), therefore \(V_1 = V_2\).
Modified Bernoulli equation 3.21:

\(\frac{p_1g_c}{ρg} +\frac{V^2_1}{2g} + z_1 = \frac{p_2g_c}{ρg} + \frac{V^2_2}{2g} + z_2 + \frac{f L}{D_h}\frac{V^2}{2g} + Σ K\frac{V^2}{2g}\) (3.21)

\(\frac{(p_1 – p_2)g_c}{ρg} = \frac{8.5(144)(32.2)}{0.876(62.4)(32.2)} = 22.4\) ft of benzene
\(V_1 =V_2\) \(z_1 = 8\) ft \(z_2 = 10\) ft L = 700 ft
\(ΣK = K _{\begin{matrix}gate \\ valve\end{matrix}} + 5K_{90° elbow} + K_{T-joint}\)
ΣK = 0.15 + 5(1.4) + 1.9 = 9.05

Flow velocity V = Q/A:

\(V = \frac{4Q}{πD^2} =\frac{ 4(0.3)}{πD^2} =\frac{ 0.382}{D^2}\)

Equation of motion:

\(\frac{p_1g_c}{ρg} – \frac{p_2g_c}{ρg} + z_1 – z_2 = \left\lgroup \frac{f L}{D_h}+ Σ K\right\rgroup \frac{V^2}{2g}\)

Substituting,

\(22.4 + 8 – 10 = \left\lgroup \frac{ 700f}{D} + 9.05 \right\rgroup \frac{(0.382)^2}{2D^4(32.2)}\)

or

\(9003 = \frac{700f}{D_5} +\frac{9.05}{D^4}\)

Rearranging and simplifying, we obtain

f = 12.86D\(^5\) – 0.01293D (i)

Reynolds number \(Re = ρVD/µg_c\):

\(Re =\frac{ 0.876(62.4)(0.382/D^2)D}{1.26 times 10^{-5}}\) (32.2)
\(Re = \frac{5.15 \times 10^4}{D}\) (ii)

Relative roughness:

\(\frac{ε}{D} =\frac{ 0.00085}{D}\) (iii)

The solution method involves a trial-and-error procedure, which begins by assuming a diameter:
1st trial: D = 1/4 = 0.25 ft; f = 0.00933 (Eq. i)
then

\(\left. \begin{matrix}Re= 5.15 \times 10^ 4/D = 2.06\times 10^5\\ \\ \frac{ε}{D} = \frac{0.00085}{0.25} = 0.0034 \end{matrix} \right\}\) f = 0.028 (Figure 3.3)

2nd trial: D = 1/3 = 0.333 ft; f = 0.0484
then

\(\left. \begin{matrix}Re = 5.15 \times 10^4/D = 1.54 \times 10^5\\ \\ \frac{ε}{D} = \frac{0.00085}{0.333} = 0.00255\end{matrix} \right\}\) f = 0.026 (Figure 3.3)

The diameter we seek falls between 0.25 ft and 0.333 ft. Continuing,
3rd trial: D = 0.3 ft; f = 0.027
then

\(\left. \begin{matrix}Re = 5.15 \times 10^4/D = 1.72 \times 10^5\\ \\ \frac{ε}{D} = \frac{0.00085}{0.3} = 0.0028\end{matrix} \right\}\) f = 0.027 (Figure 3.3)

which is close enough. Thus D = 0.3 ft. Referring to Appendix Table D.1, we read:
31/2-nom sch 40 D = 0.2957 ft
4-nom sch 40 D = 0.3355 ft
The question now arises as to which of these pipes to select. The smaller size will deliver the required flow rate but at a pressure drop that will exceed that which was specified. So we use 4-nominal schedule 40 pipe. We must now go back and calculate the actual pressure drop. What was given in the problem statement (∆p = 8.5 psi and \(Q = 0.3 ft^3/s\)) will be satisfied only with a diameter of 0.3 ft. The diameter we are using, however, is 0.3355 ft, which is larger than necessary.
At \(Q = 0.3 ft^3/s\), we calculate the actual pressure drop with the equation of motion:
\(\frac{p_1g_c}{ρ_g }– \frac{p_2g_c}{ρ_g} + z_1 – z+2 = \frac{f L}{D_h}+ Σ K\frac{V^2}{2g}\)
For \(D = 0.3355 ft, A = πD^2/4 = 0.08840 ft^2\). The average velocity then is \(V =\frac{ Q}{A} =\frac{ 0.3}{0.08840} = 3.39 ft/s\)

and

\(Re = 1.53 \times 10^5\) \( ε/D = 0.00253\) \(f = 0.027\)

Substituting,

\(\frac{(p_1 – p_2)32.2}{0.876(62.4)(32.2)} + 8 – 10 = \left\lgroup \frac{ 0.027(700)}{0.3355} + 9.05\right\rgroup \frac{(3.39)^2}{2(32.2)}\)

Solving, we get

\(p_1 – p_2 = 5.1\) psi

This pressure drop is less than the 8.5 psi that was specified. Had we used 3 1/2-nominal schedule 40, the pressure drop would have been 8.9 psi.
