Guest Question.

The flatcar moves with a constant speed \(v_{0}\) and carries a winch which produces a constant tension P in the cable attached to the small carriage. The carriage has a mass m and rolls freely on the horizontal surface starting from rest relative to the flatcar at x = 0, at which instant \(X=x_{0}=b\). Apply the workenergy equation to the carriage, first, as an observer moving with the frame of reference of the car and, second, as an observer on the ground. Show the compatibility of the two expressions.

Admin
Solution

To the observer on the flatcar, the work done by P is

 

1 \(U_{ rel }=\int_{0}^{x} P d x=P x\) for constant P

 

The change in kinetic energy relative to the car is

 

\(\Delta T_{ rel }=\frac{1}{2} m\left(\dot{x}^{2}-0\right)\)

 

The work-energy equation for the moving observer becomes

 

\(\left[U_{\text {rel }}=\Delta T_{\text {rel }}\right] \quad P x=\frac{1}{2} m \dot{x}^{2}\)

 

To the observer on the ground, the work done by P is

 

\(U=\int_{b}^{X} P d X=P(X-b)\)

 

The change in kinetic energy for the ground measurement is

 

2 \(\Delta T=\frac{1}{2} m\left(\dot{X}^{2}-v_{0}^{2}\right)\)

 

The work-energy equation for the fixed observer gives

 

\(P(X-b)=\frac{1}{2} m\left(\dot{X}^{2}-v_{0}^{2}\right)\)

 

To reconcile this equation with that for the moving observer, we can make the following substitutions:

 

\(X=x_{0}+x, \quad \dot{X}=v_{0}+\dot{x}, \quad \ddot{X}=\ddot{x}\)

 

Thus,

 

\(P(X-b)=P x+P\left(x_{0}-b\right)=P x+m \ddot{x}\left(x_{0}-b\right)\)

 

3 \(=P x+m \ddot{x} v_{0} t=P x+m v_{0} \dot{x}\)

 

and

 

\(\dot{X}^{2}-v_{0}^{2}=\left(v_{0}^{2}+\dot{x}^{2}+2 v_{0} \dot{x}-v_{0}^{2}\right)=\dot{x}^{2}+2 v_{0} \dot{x}\)

 

The work-energy equation for the fixed observer now becomes

 

\(P x+m v_{0} \dot{x}=\frac{1}{2} m \dot{x}^{2}+m v_{0} \dot{x}\)

 

which is merely \(P x=\frac{1}{2} m \dot{x}^{2}\), as concluded by the moving observer. We see, therefore, that the difference between the two work-energy expressions is

 

\(U-U_{ rel }=T-T_{ rel }=m v_{0} \dot{x}\)

 

Helpful Hints

1 The only coordinate which the moving observer can measure is x.

2 To the ground observer, the initial velocity of the carriage is \(v_{0}\) so its initial kinetic energy is \(\frac{1}{2} m v_{0}^{2}\).

3 The symbol t stands for the time of motion from x = 0 to x = x. The displacement \(x_{0}-b\) of the carriage is its velocity \(v_{0}\) times the time t or \(x_{0}-b=v_{0} t\). Also, since the constant
acceleration times the time equals the velocity change, \(\ddot{x} t=\dot{x}\).
