Guest Question.

Determine the centroidal coordinates of the volume shown in Fig. (a) that is generated by rotating the area under the curve \(z^{2}\)=18y about the y-axis. The coordinates are measured in mm.

Admin
Solution

By symmetry, \(\bar{x} =\bar{z} =0\). Integration must be used to find \(\bar{y}\). There are two convenient single-integration techniques for volumes of revolution; the method of thin disks and the method of thin shells.

Method I: Thin Disks

In this method, a differential element of the generating area is the vertical strip shown in Fig. (b). When the generating area is rotated about the y-axis to form
the volume of revolution, the differential area element generates the thin disk of
thickness dy and radius z that is shown in Fig. (c). Thus, the properties of the volume may be determined by integrating the properties of the disk.

The volume of the disk is

\(dV = πz^{2} dy = 18πy dy\)

Integrating to determine the volume V, we obtain

\(V=18\pi \int_{0}^{72}{y dy}=18\pi \left[\frac{y^{2}}{2} \right]_{0}^{72}= 46656π mm^{3}\)

Because the distance from the disk to the xz-plane is y, the corresponding first moment is \(dQ_{xz} = y dV\), which on substituting the expression for dV becomes

\(dQ_{xz} = y(18πy dy) = 18πy^{2} dy\)

Integration yields for the first moment of the volume

\(Q_{xz}=18\pi \int_{0}^{72}{y^{2} dy}=18\pi \left[\frac{y^{3}}{3} \right]_{0}^{72}= 2239488π mm^{4}\)

Therefore, the centroidal coordinates of the volume are

\(\bar{y}=\frac{Q_{xz}}{V}=\frac{2239488π}{46656π}=48 mm\) \(\bar{x}=\bar{z}=0\)

Method II: Thin Shells

In this method, a differential element of the generating area is the horizontal strip shown in Fig. (d). Rotation about the y-axis generates a shell of infinitesimal thickness dz, as shown in Fig. (e).

The volume of the thin shell (circumference × thickness × length) is

\(dV=2\pi z dz(72-y)=2\pi z\left(72-\frac{z^{2}}{18} \right)dz=\frac{\pi }{9}(1296z-z^{3})dz\)

Noting that the range of z is from 0 to 36 mm, the volume is given by

\(V=\frac{\pi }{9} \int_{0}^{36}{(1296z-z^{3})dz}= \frac{\pi }{9}\left[648z^{2}-\frac{z^{4}}{4} \right]_{0}^{36}=46656\pi mm^{3}\)

Referring to Fig. (d), we see that the y-coordinate of the centroid of the thin shell is \(\bar{y}_{el} = y + (72 − y)/2 = 36 + (y/2) = 36 + (z^{2}/36)\), and its first moment with respect to the xz-plane becomes

\(dQ_{xz}=\bar{y}_{el}dV=\left(36+\frac{z^{2}}{36} \right)\frac{\pi }{9}(1296z-z^{3})dz=\frac{\pi }{9}\left(46656z-\frac{z^{5}}{36} \right)dz\)

Therefore, we obtain

\(Q_{xz}=\frac{\pi }{9} \int_{0}^{36}{(46656z-\frac{z^{5}}{36} )dz}= \frac{\pi }{9}\left[23328z^{2}-\frac{z^{6}}{216} \right]_{0}^{36}=2239488π mm^{4}\)

and the centroidal coordinates of the volume become

\(\bar{y}=\frac{Q_{xz}}{V}=\frac{2239488π}{46656π}=48 mm\) \(\bar{x}=\bar{z}=0\)

Of course, these answers are identical to those obtained by the method of thin disks.
