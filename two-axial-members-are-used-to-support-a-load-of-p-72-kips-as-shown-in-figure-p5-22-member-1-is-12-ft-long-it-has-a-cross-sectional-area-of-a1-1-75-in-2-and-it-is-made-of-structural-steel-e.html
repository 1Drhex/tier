Guest Question.

Two axial members are used to support a load of P = 72 kips as shown in Figure P5.22. Member (1) is 12-ft long, it has a cross-sectional area of \(A_{1}=1.75 \text { in. }^{2}\) , and it is made of structural steel [E = 29,000 ksi]. Member (2) is 16-ft long, it has a crosssectional area of \(A_{2}=4.50 in. ^{2}\) , and it is made of an aluminum alloy [E = 10,000 ksi].
(a) Compute the normal stress in each axial member.
(b) Compute the deformation of each axial member.
(c) Draw a deformation diagram showing the final position of joint B.
(d) Compute the horizontal and vertical displacements of joint B.

Admin
Solution

(a) Consider a FBD of joint B and write two equilibrium equations:

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma F_{x}=-F_{1}+F_{2} \cos 55^{\circ}=0 \\&\Sigma F_{y}=F_{2} \sin 55^{\circ}-72 kips =0\end{aligned}\)

Solve these two equations simultaneously to obtain \(F_{1}\) = 50.4149 kips and \(F_{2}\) = 87.8958 kips.

The normal stress in member (1) is:

\(\sigma_{1}=\frac{F_{1}}{A_{1}}=\frac{50.4149 kips }{1.75 in .^{2}}=28.8 ksi\)

and the normal stress in member (2) is:

\(\sigma_{2}=\frac{F_{2}}{A_{2}}=\frac{87.8958 kips }{4.50 in .^{2}}=19.53 ksi\)

 

(b) The member deformations are

\(\begin{aligned}&\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}=\frac{(50.4149 kips )(12 ft )(12 in. / ft )}{\left(1.75 in .^{2}\right)(29,000 ksi )}=0.1430 in . \\&\delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}=\frac{(87.8958 kips )(16 ft )(12 in . / ft )}{\left(4.50 in .^{2}\right)(10,000 ksi )}=0.375 in .\end{aligned}\)

 

(c) Member (1) elongates 0.1430 in. and member (2) elongates 0.375 in. Therefore, the deformation diagram showing the final position of joint B is shown.

 

(d) The horizontal displacement of B is

\(\Delta x=0.1430 in .\)

The vertical displacement is found from

\(\begin{aligned}\sin 55^{\circ} &=\frac{0.375 in .+0.1430 \cos 55^{\circ}}{\Delta y} \\& \therefore \Delta y=\frac{0.375 in .+0.1430 \cos 55^{\circ}}{\sin 55^{\circ}}=\frac{0.375 in.+0.0820 in .}{\sin 55^{\circ}}=0.558 in . \downarrow\end{aligned}\)
