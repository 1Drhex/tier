Engine’s Fuel Consumption

A gasoline-powered engine generates an average power output of 50 kW. Neglecting any inefficiency that may be present, calculate the volume of fuel consumed each hour. Express your result in the dimensions of liters and gallons.

Solution

In terms of the definition for the derived unit kW, the engine produces

\(W=\left(50\frac{kJ}{s} \right)(3600s) \longleftarrow \left[P_{avg}=\frac{W}{\Delta t} \right] \)

\(=1.8 \times 10^5\left(\frac{kJ}{\cancel{s}} \right) (\cancel{s})\)

\(=1.8 \times 10^5kJ\)

in 1 h. This energy output is equivalent to 180 MJ. The mass of gasoline that must be burned to release that amount of heat is

\(m=\frac{180MJ}{45MJ/kg} \longleftarrow \left[Q=mH\right] \)

\(=4(\cancel{MJ})\left(\frac{kg}{\cancel{MJ}} \right) \)

\(= 4 kg \)

We next determine the volume of the fuel:

\(V=\frac{4kg}{680 kg/m^3} \longleftarrow \left[m=\rho V\right] \)

\(=5.882\times 10^{-3}(\cancel{kg})\left(\frac{m^3}{\cancel{kg}} \right) \)

\(=5.882\times 10^{-3} m^3 \)

By using the definition of the derived unit liter from Table 3.2,

Although a change in temperature of 1 Kelvin equals a change of 1 degree Celsius, numerical values are converted using the formula.

\(V = ( 5.882 \times 10^{-3} m^3 )\left(1000\frac{L}{m^3} \right) \)

\(=5.882(\cancel{m^3})\left(\frac{L}{\cancel{m^3}} \right) \)

\(=5.882 L\)

In the USCS, the quantity of fuel is

\(V = (5.882 L)\left(0.2642\frac{gal}{L} \right) \)

\(=1.554(\cancel{L})\left(\frac{gal}{\cancel{L}} \right) \)

\(=1.554 gal\)

Discussion
By disregarding the engine’s inefficiency when converting the heat of
combustion into mechanical energy, we recognize that our calculation will underestimate the actual rate of fuel consumption.
