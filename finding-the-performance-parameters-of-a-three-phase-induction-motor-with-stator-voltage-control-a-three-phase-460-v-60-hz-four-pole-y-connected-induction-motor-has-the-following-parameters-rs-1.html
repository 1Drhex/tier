Finding the Performance Parameters of a Three-Phase Induction Motor with Stator Voltage Control

A three-phase, 460-V, 60-Hz, four-pole Y-connected induction motor has the following parameters: \(R_s=1.01 \Omega ,R_r^′=0.69 \Omega ,X_s=1.3 \Omega ,X_r^′=1.94 \Omega \), and \(X_m = 43.5 Ω\). The no-load loss, \(P_{no load}\), is negligible. The load torque, which is proportional to the speed squared, is 41 N · m at 1740 rpm. If the motor speed is 1550 rpm, determine (a) the load torque \(T_L\), (b) the rotor current \(I_r\), (c) the stator supply voltage \(V_a\), (d) the motor input current \(I_i\), (e) the motor input power \(P_i\), (f) the slip for maximum current \(s_a\), (g) the maximum rotor current \(I_{r(max)}\), (h) the speed at maximum rotor current \(ω_a\), and (i) the torque at the maximum current \(T_a\).

Admin
Solution

\(p=4, f =60 Hz, V_s=460/\sqrt{3}=265.58 V, R_s=1.01 \Omega ,R_r^′=0.69 \Omega ,X_s=1.3 \Omega ,X_r^′=1.94 \Omega , X_m=43.5 \Omega ,\omega =2\pi \times 60=377 rad/s\), and \(\omega _s=377\times 2/4=188.5 rad/s\). Because torque is proportional to speed squared,

\(T_L = K_mω_m^2\) (15.40)

At \(\omega _m=1740 \pi /30=182.2 rad/s,T_L=41 N\cdot m\) and Eq. (15.40) yields \(K_m = 41/182.2^2 = 1.235 × 10^{-3}\) and \(\omega _m=1550\pi /30=162.3 rad/s\). From Eq. (15.4), \(s = (188.5 – 162.3)/ 188.500 = 0.139\).

\(s=\frac{\omega _s-\omega _m}{\omega _s} \) (15.4)

a. From Eq. (15.40), \(T_L = 1.235 × 10^{-3} × 162.3^2 = 32.5 N · m\).

b. From Eqs. (15.10) and (15.12),

\(P_d=P_g-P_{ru}=3(I_r^′)^2\frac{R_r^′}{s}(1-s)\) (15.10)

 

\(T_d=\frac{P_d}{\omega _m}\) (15.12)

 

\(P_d=P_g-P_{ru}=3(I_r^′)^2\frac{R_r^′}{s}(1-s)=T_L\omega _m+P_{no load}\) (15.41)

For negligible no-load loss,

\(I_r=\left[\frac{sT_L\omega _m}{3R_r^′(1-s)} \right]^{1/2}\) (15.42)

 

\(I_r=\left[\frac{0.139\times 32.5\times 162.3}{3\times 0.69(1-0.139)} \right]^{1/2}=20.28 A\)

c. The stator supply voltage

\(V_a=I_r^′\left[\left(R_s+\frac{R_r^′}{s} \right)^2+(X_s+X_r^′) \right]^{1/2}\) (15.43)

 

\(=20.28\times \left[\left(1.01+\frac{0.69}{0.139} \right)^2+(1.3+1.94)^2 \right]^{1/2}=137.82\)

d. From Eq. (15.15),

\(Z_i=\frac{-X_m(X_s+X_r^′)+jX_m(R_s+R_r^′/s)}{R_s+R_r^′/s+j(X_m+X_s+X_r^′)}\) (15.15)

 

\(Z_i=\frac{-43.5\times (1.3+1.94)+j43.5\times (1.01+0.69/0.139)}{1.01+0.69/0.139+j(43.5+1.3+1.94)}=6.27\angle 35.82°\)

 

\(I_i=\frac{V_a}{Z_i}=\frac{137.82}{6.27}\angle {-144.26°}=22\angle {-35.82°}A\)

e. \(PF_m = \cos( -35.82° ) = 0.812\) (lagging). From Eq. (15.13),

\(P_i = 3 V_s I_s \cos θ_m\) (15.13)

\(Pi = 3 × 137.82 × 22.0 × 0.812 = 7386 W\)

f. Substituting \(\omega _m=\omega _s(1-s)\) and \(T_L=K_m\omega _m^2\) in Eq. (15.42) yields

\(I_r^′=\left[\frac{sT_L\omega _m}{3R_r^′(1-s)} \right]^{1/2}=(1-s)\omega _s\left(\frac{sK_m\omega _s}{3R_r^′} \right)^{1/2}\) (15.44)

The slip at which \(I_r^′\) becomes maximum can be obtained by setting \(dI_r/ds = 0\), and this yields

\(s_a=\frac{1}{3}\) (15.45)

g. Substituting \(s_a=\frac{1}{3}\) Eq. (15.44) gives the maximum rotor current

\(I_{r(max)}^′=\omega _s\left(\frac{4K_m\omega _s}{81R_r^′} \right)^{1/2}\) (15.46)

 

\(=188.5\times \left(\frac{4\times 1.235\times 10^{-3}\times 188.5}{81\times 0.69} \right)^{1/2}=24.3 A\)

h. The speed at the maximum current

\(\omega _a=\omega _s(1-s_a)=(1/2)\omega _s=0.6667\omega _s\) (15.47)

 

\(= 188.5 × 2/3 = 125.27 rad/s\) or 1200 rpm

i. From Eqs. (15.9), (15.12a), and (15.44),

\(T_a=9I_{r(max)}^2\frac{R_r}{\omega _s}\) (15.48)

 

\(=9\times 24.3^2\times \frac{0.69}{188.5}=19.45 N\cdot m\)
