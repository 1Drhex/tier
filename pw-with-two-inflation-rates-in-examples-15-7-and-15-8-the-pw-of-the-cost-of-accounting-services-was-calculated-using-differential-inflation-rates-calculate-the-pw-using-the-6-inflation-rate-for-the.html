PW with Two Inflation Rates

In and , the PW of the cost of accounting services was calculated using differential inflation rates. Calculate the PW using the 6% inflation rate for the services \(\left(f_{\mathrm{a}}\right)\) and the 4% inflation rate for the economy ( f ).

Admin
Solution

First, the year-1 dollars of \(CF_{1}\) are chosen as the constant-value unit. The next step is to apply Equation 15.1 (\(F_{T+t}=F_{T}(1+f)^{t}\) to calculate the nominal-dollar cash flows shown in column B of Exhibit 15.6.
These are calculated at the 6% inflation rate \(\left(f_{\mathrm{a}}\right)\) for accounting services. Then, Equation 15.4 and f, the economyâ€™s inflation rate of 4%, are used to calculate the real, or constant-value, cash flows shown in column C. Column B can be calculated recursively; that is:

\(\text { Nominal } \mathrm{CF}_{t}=\text { Nominal } \mathrm{CF}_{t-1}\left(1+f_{a}\right)\).

However, column C cannot be computed recursively; it must use Equation 15.4 (\(\text { Real } \mathrm{CF}_{t}=\text { Nominal } \mathrm{CF}_{t} /(1+f)^{t-1}\)).
Those column C values are simply multiplied by the (P/F,8%,t) values in column D. The result is shown and then summed in column E. If an EAW were needed, then the PW would be multiplied by (A/P,8%,5). As in the previous examples, columns D and E could be replaced by NPV(A5,C9:C13) function.
This approach is identical mathematically to that used in . As before, the constant-value dollar cash flows and the PW are measured in year-1 dollars. A market interest rate computed using Equation 15.2 ((1 + Market rate)= (1 + i) (1 + f ) ) can be used to compute the PW from the nominal cash flows.
The NPV function computes the PW in year-0 dollars. Thus, the formula in B14 includes (1 + A2) or (1 + f) to convert to year-1 dollars.
