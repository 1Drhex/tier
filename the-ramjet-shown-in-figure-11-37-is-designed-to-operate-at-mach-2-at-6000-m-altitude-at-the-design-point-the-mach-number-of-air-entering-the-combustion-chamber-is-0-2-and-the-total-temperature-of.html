Guest Question.

The ramjet, shown in Figure 11.37, is designed to operate at Mach 2 at 6000 m altitude. At the design point, the Mach number of air entering the combustion chamber is 0.2 and the total temperature of the combustion gas entering the exhaust nozzle is 1600K. The calorific value of the fuel is \(20000 kJ kg ^{-1}\) and the combustion efficiency is 1.0. At the design operation the exhaust nozzle is choked. Neglecting the weight of the fuel, assuming the flow inside the ramjet to be frictionless and the specific heats ratio for both the air and the combustion gas to be 1.4, determine (a) the cross-sectional area of the combustion chamber \(A_{2}\), (b) the area ratio of the exhaust nozzle \(A_{3} / A_{4}\), (c) the mass flow rate of air, (d) the ratio of total pressure at the nozzle entry to freestream static pressure \(\left(p_{03} / p_{\infty}\right)\), (e) the jet velocity \(u_{e}\), (f) the fuel flow rate and the specific thrust, (g) the overall efficiency, (h) the TSFC, and (i) the propulsive and thermal efficiencies.

Admin
Solution

(a) At 6000m altitude, from the gas tables in [3],

\(p_{\infty}=47.2 kPa , \quad T_{\infty}=249.15 K , \quad \rho=0.660 kg m ^{-3}\)

 

For \(M_{\infty}=2\), from the isentropic table (),

\(\frac{p_{\infty}}{p_{0 \infty}}=0.1278, \frac{T_{\infty}}{T_{0 \infty}}=0.5556, \frac{A_{\infty}}{A^{*}}=1.688\)

 

For \(M_{\infty}=2\), from the normal shock table (),

\(M_{1}=0.58, \frac{p_{1}}{p_{\infty}}=4.5\)

 

For \(M_{1}=0.58\), from the isentropic table,

\(\frac{A_{1}}{A^{*}}=1.213, \frac{p_{1}}{p_{01}}=0.7962\)

 

For \(M_{2}=0.2\), from the isentropic table,

\(\frac{A_{2}}{A^{*}}=2.964, \quad \frac{T_{2}}{T_{02}}=0.9921\)

 

Therefore,

\(\frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}}=\frac{A_{2} / A^{*}}{A_{1} / A^{*}}\)

 

\(=\frac{2.964}{1.213} \\[0.5cm] = 2.444\)

 

\(A_{2}=2.444 \times A_{1} \\[0.5cm] = 2.444 × 0.2 \\[0.5cm] =0.489 m ^{2}\)

 

(b) Flow through the ram duct is friction free; therefore,

\(T_{02}=T_{0 \infty}=\frac{T_{\infty}}{0.5556} \\[0.5cm] =\frac{249.15}{0.5556} \\[0.5cm] = 448.43 K\)

 

The flow from 2 to 3 is a Rayleigh flow. Therefore,

\(\frac{T_{03}}{T_{0}^{*}}=\frac{T_{03}}{T_{02}} \times \frac{T_{02}}{T_{0}^{*}}\)

 

From the Rayleigh flow table (), for \(M_{2}=0.2, \frac{T_{02}}{T_{0}^{*}}=0.1735\).

\(\frac{T_{03}}{T_{0}^{*}}=\frac{1600}{448.43} \times 0.1735 \\[0.5cm] = 0.619\)

 

For \(\frac{T_{03}}{T_{0}^{*}}=0.619\), from the Rayleigh flow table, \(M_{3} \approx 0.45\).

From the isentropic table, for \(M_{3}=0.45, \frac{A_{3}}{A^{*}}=1.45\), and for \(M_{4}=1, \frac{A_{4}}{A^{*}}=1\). Hence,

 

\(\frac{A_{3}}{A_{4}}=\frac{A_{3}}{A^{*}} \times \frac{A^{*}}{A_{4}} \\[0.5cm] = 1.45 × 1 \\[0.5cm] = 1.45\)

 

(c) The mass flowrate is

\(\dot{m}=\rho_{\infty} A_{\infty} V_{\infty}\)

 

The velocity \(V_{\infty}\) is

\(V_{\infty}=M_{\infty} a_{\infty}\)

 

\(=M_{\infty} \times \sqrt{\gamma R T_{\infty}}\)

 

\(=2 \times \sqrt{1.4 \times 287 \times 249.15}\)

 

\(=632.8 m s ^{-1}\)

 

Therefore,

\(\dot{m}=0.660 \times 0.2 \times 632.8\)

 

\(=83.53 kg s ^{-1}\)

 

(d) The pressure ratio \(\frac{p_{03}}{p_{\infty}}\) can be expressed as

\(\frac{p_{03}}{p_{\infty}}=\frac{p_{03}}{p_{02}} \times \frac{p_{02}}{p_{0 \infty}} \times \frac{p_{0 \infty}}{p_{\infty}}\)

 

For \(M_{\infty}=2\), from the isentropic table, \(\frac{p_{0 \infty}}{p_{\infty}}=\frac{1}{0.1278}\), and from the normal shock table, \(\frac{p_{01}}{p_{0 \infty}}= 0.7209.\)

There is no pressure loss from 1 to 2; therefore,

\(\frac{p_{02}}{p_{0 \infty}}=\frac{p_{01}}{p_{0 \infty}}\)

 

For the Rayleigh flow table, for \(M_{3}=0.45, \frac{p_{03}}{p_{0}^{*}}=1.135, \text { and for } M_{3}=0.2, \frac{p_{02}}{p_{0}^{*}}=1.235 .\) Thus,

\(\frac{p_{03}}{p_{02}}=\frac{p_{03} / p_{0}^{*}}{p_{02} / p_{0}^{*}}\)

 

\(=\frac{1.135}{1.235} \\[0.5cm] = 0.919\)

 

Hence,

\(\frac{p_{03}}{p_{\infty}}=0.919 \times 0.7209 \times \frac{1}{0.1278} \\[0.5cm] = 5.18\)

 

(e) By Eq. (11.3), the exit velocity is

\(u_{e}=\sqrt{\frac{T_{04}}{T_{0 \infty}}} u\) (11.3)

\(u_{e}=\sqrt{\frac{T_{04}}{T_{0 \infty}}} × u\)

 

where u is the freestream velocity,

\(u=M_{\infty} a_{\infty}\)

 

\(=2 \times \sqrt{1.4 \times 287 \times 249.15}\)

 

\(=632.8 m s ^{-1}\)

 

Therefore,

\(u_{e}=\sqrt{\frac{1600}{448.43}} \times 632.8\)

 

\(1195.30 m s ^{-1}\)

 

(f) By Eq. (11.5)

\(f=\frac{\left(T_{04} / T_{0 \infty}\right)-1}{\left[Q_{R} /\left(c_{p} T_{0 \infty}\right)\right]-T_{04} / T_{0 \infty}}\) (11.5)

\(f=\frac{\left(T_{03} / T_{0 \infty}\right)-1}{Q_{R} /\left(c_{p} T_{0 \infty}\right)-T_{03} / T_{0 \infty}}\)

 

\(=\frac{(1600 / 448.43)-1}{20000 /(1.0045 \times 448.43)-1600 / 448.43}\)

 

\(=\frac{2.568}{44.4-3.568} \\[0.5cm] = 0.063\)

 

Therefore, the fuel flow rate is

\(\dot{m}_{f}=f \times \dot{m} \\[0.5cm] = 0.063 × 83.53 \\[0.5cm] =5.26 kg s ^{-1}\)

 

The specific thrust is

\(\frac{ Th }{\dot{m}}=(1+f) u_{e}-u\)

 

\(=(1+0.063) \times 1195.30-632.8\)

 

\(=1.063 \times 1195.30-632.8\)

 

\(=637.8 N s kg ^{-1}\)

 

\(=0.6378 kN s kg ^{-1}\)

 

(g) The overall efficiency is

\(\eta_{o}=\frac{ Th \times u}{\dot{m}_{f} \times Q_{R}}\)

 

\(=\frac{(T h / \dot{m}) \times u}{\left(\dot{m}_{f} / \dot{m}\right) \times Q_{R}}\)

 

\(=\frac{637.8 \times 632.8}{0.063 \times\left(20000 \times 10^{3}\right)} \\[0.5cm] = 0.32\)

 

(h) The TSFC is

\(TFSC =\frac{f}{ Th / \dot{ m }}\)

 

\(=\frac{0.063}{0.6378}\)

 

\(=0.0988 kg ( s kN )^{-1} \\[0.5cm] = 3.6 × 0.0988 \\[0.5cm] =0.3557 kg ( hr N )^{-1}\)

 

(i) The propulsive efficiency is

\(\eta_{p}=\frac{T h \times u}{\dot{m}\left[(1+f)\left(u_{e}^{2} / 2\right)-u^{2} / 2\right]}\)

 

\(=\frac{(T h / \dot{m}) \times u}{\left[(1+f)\left(u_{e}^{2} / 2\right)-u^{2} / 2\right]}\)

 

\(=\frac{637.8 \times 632.8}{\left[(1+0.063) \times\left(1195.3^{2} / 2\right)-632.8^{2} / 2\right]}\)

 

\(=\frac{403599.84}{559158.50} \\[0.5cm] = 0.72\)

 

The thermal efficiency is

\(\eta_{ th }=\frac{\dot{m}\left[(1+f)\left(u_{e}^{2} / 2\right)-u^{2} / 2\right]}{\dot{m}_{f} Q_{R}}\)

 

\(=\frac{\left[(1+f)\left(u_{e}^{2} / 2\right)-u^{2} / 2\right]}{\left(\dot{m}_{f} / \dot{m}\right) Q_{R}}\)

 

\(=\frac{(1+0.063) \times\left(1195.3^{2} / 2\right)-632.8^{2} / 2}{0.063 \times\left(20000 \times 10^{3}\right)} \\[0.5cm] = 0.444\)
