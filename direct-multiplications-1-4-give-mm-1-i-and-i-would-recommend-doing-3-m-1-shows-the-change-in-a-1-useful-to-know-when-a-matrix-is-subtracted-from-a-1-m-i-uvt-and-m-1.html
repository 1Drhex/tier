Guest Question.

Direct multiplications 1-4 give \(M{M}^{-1} = I\), and I would recommend doing #3. \({M}^{-1}\) shows the change in \({A}^{-1}\) (useful to know) when a matrix is subtracted from A:
1 \(M = I -u{v}^{T}\) and \({M}^{-1} = I + u{v}^{T} /(1- {v}^{T}u)\) (rank 1 change in I)
2 \(M = A-u{v}^{T}\) and \({M}^{-1} = {A}^{-1} + {A}^{-1}u{v}^{T}{A}^{-1} /(1- {v}^{T}{A}^{-1}u) \)
3 \(M = I -UV\) and \({M}^{-1} = {I}_{n} + U({I}_{m} - VU)^{-1} V\)
4 \(M=A-U{W}^{-1}V\) and \({M}^{-1} = {A}^{-1} + {A}^{-1}U(W - V{A}^{-1}U)^{-1}V{A}^{-1}\)
The Woodbury-Morrison fonnula 4 is the “matrix inversion lemma” in engineering.
The Kalman filter for solving block tridiagonal systems uses fonnula 4 at each step.
The four matrices \({M}^{-1}\) are in diagonal blocks when inverting these block matrices (\({v}^{T}\) is 1 by n, u is n by 1, V is m by n, U is n by m).
\(\begin{bmatrix} I & u \\ { v }^{ T } & 1 \end{bmatrix}\quad \begin{bmatrix} A & u \\ { v }^{ T } & 1 \end{bmatrix}\quad \begin{bmatrix} { I }_{ n } & U \\ V & { I }_{ m } \end{bmatrix}\quad \begin{bmatrix} A & U \\ V & W \end{bmatrix}\)

Admin
Solution

\(M{M}^{-1}=( { I }_{ n }-UV)( { I }_{ n }+U( { I }_{ m }-VU)^{-1}V)\) (this is testing formula 3)

=\({ I }_{ n }-UV+U( { I }_{ m }-VU)^{-1}V-UVU( { I }_{ m }-VU)^{-1}V\) (keep simplifying)

=\({ I }_{ n }-UV+U( { I }_{ m }-VU)( { I }_{ m }-VU)^{-1}V= { I }_{ n } \)(formulas 1, 2, 4 are similar)
