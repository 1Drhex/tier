Solution

This problem involves three different mechanisms that contribute to the total strain induced in the laminate: the applied stresses, the temperature change, and the change in moisture content. The total strains induced by all three mechanisms can be calculated using Equation 5.29:

\(\left \{ \begin{matrix} \varepsilon _{xx} \\\varepsilon _{yy} \\ \gamma _{xy} \end{matrix} \right \}=\left [ \begin{matrix} \overline{S}_{11} & \overline{S}_{12} & \overline{S}_{16} \\ \overline{S}_{12} & \overline{S}_{22} & \overline{S}_{26} \\ \overline{S}_{16}& \overline{S}_{26} & \overline{S}_{66}\end{matrix} \right ]\left \{ \begin{matrix} \sigma _{xx} \\ \sigma _{yy}\\ \tau _{xy} \end{matrix} \right \}+\Delta T\left \{ \begin{matrix} \alpha _{xx} \\\alpha _{yy} \\ \alpha _{xy} \end{matrix} \right \}+\Delta M\left \{ \begin{matrix} \beta _{xx} \\ \beta _{yy} \\ \beta _{yy} \end{matrix} \right \} \) (5.29)

Numerical values for the stresses and transformed reduced compliance matrix are given in The linear thermal and moisture expansion coefficients for graphite–epoxy, referenced to the x−y coordinate system, are calculated using Equations 5.25 and 5.28, respectively. The thermal expansion coefficients are

\(\alpha _{xx}=\alpha _{11}\cos^2(\theta )+\alpha _{22}\sin^2(\theta ) \\[0.5cm] \alpha _{yy}=\alpha _{11}\sin^2(\theta )+\alpha _{22}\cos^2(\theta ) \\[0.5cm] \alpha _{xy}= 2\cos(\theta )\sin(\theta )(\alpha _{11}-\alpha _{22})\) (5.25)

\(\beta _{xx}=\beta _{11}\cos^2(\theta )+\beta _{22}\sin^2(\theta ) \\[0.5cm] \beta _{yy}=\beta _{11}\sin^2(\theta )+\beta _{22}\cos^2(\theta ) \\[0.5cm] \beta _{xy}= 2\cos(\theta )\sin(\theta )(\beta _{11}-\beta _{22})\) (5.28)

\(\alpha _{xx}=\alpha _{11}\cos^2(\theta )+\alpha _{22}\sin^2(\theta )=(-0.9\times 10^{-6})\cos^2(30°)+(27\times 10^{-6})\sin^2(30°)\)

 

\(\alpha _{xx}=6.1μm/m/°C\)

 

\(\alpha _{yy}=\alpha _{11}\sin^2(\theta )+\alpha _{22}\cos^2(\theta )=(-0.9\times 10^{-6})\sin^2(30°)+(27\times 10^{-6})\cos^2(30°)\)

 

\(\alpha _{yy}=20.0μm/m/°C\)

 

\(\alpha _{xy}= 2\cos(\theta )\sin(\theta )(\alpha _{11}-\alpha _{22})=2\cos(30°)\sin(30°)(-0.9\times 10^{-6}-27\times 10^{-6})\)

 

\(\alpha _{xy}=- 24.2 μrad/°C\)

 

The moisture expansion coefficients are

 

\(\beta _{xx}=\beta _{11}\cos^2(\theta )+\beta _{22}\sin^2(\theta )=(150\times 10^{-6})\cos^2(30°)+(4800\times 10^{-6})\sin^2(30°)\)

 

\(\beta _{xx}=1313 μm/m/\%M\)

 

\(\beta _{yy}=\beta _{11}\sin^2(\theta )+\beta _{22}\cos^2(\theta )=(150\times 10^{-6})\sin^2(30°)+(4800\times 10^{-6})\cos^2(30°)\)

 

\(\beta _{yy}=3638 μm/m/\%M\)

 

\(\beta _{xy}= 2\cos(\theta )\sin(\theta )(\beta _{11}-\beta _{22})=2\cos(30°)\sin(30°)(150\times 10^{-6}-4800\times 10^{-6})\)

 

\(\beta _{xy}= -4027 μrad/\%M\)

 

Hence, Equation 5.29 becomes

\(\left \{ \begin{matrix} \varepsilon _{xx} \\\varepsilon _{yy} \\ \gamma _{xy} \end{matrix} \right \}=\left [ \begin{matrix} 23.32\times 10^{-12} &4.327\times 10^{-12} & -33.72\times 10^{-12} \\ 4.327\times 10^{-12} & 70.38\times 10^{-12} & -47.79\times 10^{-12} \\ -33.72\times 10^{-12}& -47.79\times 10^{-12} & 101.3\times 10^{-12}\end{matrix} \right ]\left \{ \begin{matrix} 200\times 10^6 \\ -30\times 10^6\\ -50\times 10^6 \end{matrix} \right \}+(-155)\left \{ \begin{matrix} 6.1\times 10^{-6} \\20.0\times 10^{-6} \\ -24.2\times 10^{-6} \end{matrix} \right \}+(0.5)\left \{ \begin{matrix} 1313\times 10^{-6} \\ 3638\times 10^{-6} \\ -4027\times 10^{-6} \end{matrix} \right \}\)

 

Completing the matrix multiplication indicated, we obtain:

\(\left \{ \begin{matrix} \varepsilon _{xx} \\\varepsilon _{yy} \\ \gamma _{xy} \end{matrix} \right \}=\left \{ \begin{matrix} 6220μm/m \\ 1144μm/m\\ -10375μrad \end{matrix} \right \}+\left \{ \begin{matrix} -946μm/m \\ -3100μm/m \\ 3751μrad \end{matrix} \right \}+\left \{ \begin{matrix} 657μm/m \\ 1819μm/m \\ -2014μrad \end{matrix} \right \}=\left \{ \begin{matrix} 5931μm/m \\ -137μm/m \\ -8638μrad \end{matrix} \right \}\)

 

An implicit assumption in this problem is that the composite is free to expand or contract, as dictated by changes in temperature and/or moisture content. Consequently neither ΔT nor ΔM affect the state of stress, but rather affect only the state of strain.
