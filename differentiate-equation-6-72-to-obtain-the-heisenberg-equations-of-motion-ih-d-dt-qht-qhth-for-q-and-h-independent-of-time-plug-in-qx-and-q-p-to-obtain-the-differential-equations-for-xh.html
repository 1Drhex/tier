Guest Question.

Differentiate Equation 6.72 to obtain the Heisenberg equations of motion

\( \hat{Q}_{H}(t)=\hat{U}^{\dagger}(t) \hat{Q} \hat{U}(t) \) (6.72).

\( i \hbar \frac{d}{d t} \hat{Q}_{H}(t)=\left[\hat{Q}_{H}(t), \hat{H}\right] \) (6.78).

(for \( \hat{Q} \text { and } \hat{H} \) independent of time). Plug in \( \hat{Q}=\hat{x} \text { and } \hat{Q}=\hat{p} \) to obtain the differential equations for \( \hat{x}_{H} \text { and } \hat{p}_{H} \) in the Heisenberg picture for a single particle of mass m moving in a potential V(x).

Admin
Solution

We start with

\( i \hbar \frac{d}{d t} \hat{Q}_{H}(t)=i \hbar \frac{d}{d t}\left[\hat{U}^{\dagger}(t) \hat{Q} \hat{U}(t)\right] \).

\( =i \hbar \frac{d U^{\dagger}}{d t} \hat{Q} \hat{U}(t)+\hat{U}^{\dagger}(t) \hat{Q} i \hbar \frac{d \hat{U}}{d t} \).

Since \( \hat{H} \) is time independent,

\( \frac{d U}{d t}=\frac{d}{d t} e^{-i \hat{H} t / \hbar}=-\frac{i}{\hbar} \hat{H} e^{-i \hat{H} t / \hbar}=-\frac{i}{\hbar} \hat{H} \hat{U}(t) \).

and taking the adjoint of this expression

\( \frac{d U^{\dagger}}{d t}=\frac{i}{\hbar} \hat{U}^{\dagger}(t) \hat{H} \).

\( \hat{H} \text { clearly commutes with } \hat{U} \text { or } \hat{U}^{\dagger} \text { and therefore } \)

\( i \hbar \frac{d}{d t} \hat{Q}_{H}(t)=-\hat{U}^{\dagger}(t) \hat{H} \hat{Q} \hat{U}(t)+\hat{U}^{\dagger}(t) \hat{Q} \hat{H} \hat{U}(t) \).

\( =\hat{U}^{\dagger}(t) \hat{Q} \hat{U}(t) \hat{H}-\hat{H} \hat{U}^{\dagger}(t) \hat{Q} \hat{U}(t) \).

\( =\hat{Q}_{H}(t) \hat{H}-\hat{H} \hat{Q}_{H}(t) \).

which is the desired result.

For the operator \( \hat{x} \)

\( \frac{d}{d t} \hat{x}_{H}=\frac{1}{i \hbar}\left[\hat{x}_{H}, \hat{H}\right]=\frac{1}{i \hbar}\left[\hat{U}^{\dagger} \hat{x} \hat{U}, \hat{H}\right] \).

\( =\frac{1}{i \hbar}(\hat{U}^{\dagger} \hat{x} \underbrace{[\hat{U}, \hat{H}]}_{0}+\hat{U}^{\dagger}[\hat{x}, \hat{H}] \hat{U}+\underbrace{\left[U^{\dagger}, \hat{H}\right]}_{0} \hat{x} \hat{U}) \).

\( =\frac{1}{i \hbar} \hat{U}^{\dagger}\left[\hat{x}, \frac{\hat{p}^{2}}{2 m}+V\right] \hat{U} \).

\( =\frac{1}{i \hbar} \hat{U}^{\dagger}(\frac{1}{2 m}\left[\hat{x}, \hat{p}^{2}\right]+\underbrace{[\hat{x}, V]}_{0}) \hat{U} \).

\( =\frac{1}{i \hbar} \hat{U}^{\dagger} \frac{1}{2 m}(\hat{p} \underbrace{[\hat{x}, \hat{p}]}_{i \hbar}+[\hat{x}, \hat{p}] \hat{p}) \hat{U} \).

\( =\frac{1}{m} \hat{U}^{\dagger} \hat{p} \hat{U}=\frac{1}{m} \hat{p}_{H}(t) \).

And for the operator \( \hat{p} \) we reproduce the first two lines of the above calculation to arrive at

\( \frac{d}{d t} \hat{p}_{H}=\frac{1}{i \hbar} \hat{U}^{\dagger}\left[\hat{p}, \frac{\hat{p}^{2}}{2 m}+V\right] \hat{U} \).

\( =\frac{1}{i \hbar} \hat{U}^{\dagger}(\underbrace{\left[\hat{p}, \frac{\hat{p}^{2}}{2 m}\right]}_{0}+[\hat{p}, V]) \hat{U} \).

\( =\frac{1}{i \hbar} \hat{U}^{\dagger}\left(-i \hbar \frac{d V}{d x}\right) \hat{U}=\left(-\frac{d V}{d x}\right)_{H} \).

These have an identical structure to the classical equations of motion for the dynamic variables x and p:

\( m \frac{d \hat{x}_{H}}{d t}=\hat{p}_{H}(t) \quad \frac{d \hat{p}_{H}}{d t}=\left(-\frac{d V}{d x}\right)_{H} \).
