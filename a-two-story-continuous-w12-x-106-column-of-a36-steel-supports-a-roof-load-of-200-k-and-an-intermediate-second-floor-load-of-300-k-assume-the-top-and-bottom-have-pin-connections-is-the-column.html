Guest Question.

cA two-story, continuous W12 × 106 column of A36 steel supports a roof load of 200 k and an intermediate (second floor) load of 300 k. Assume the top and bottom have pin connections. Is the column section shown adequate?

Note: Assume the second-floor load to be applied at the top of the column—this will result in a somewhat conservative answer. The concept of intermediate loads is much more complicated and will not be discussed further in this text.

Admin
Solution

Weak Axis:

W 12 × 106; A = 31.2in.2; rx = 5.47″; ry = 3.11″

KL = 14′

\(\frac{KL}{r_{y}} = \frac{14^{'}\times 12 in./ft.}{3.11^{"}} = 54\)

 

From the Table 10.1; Fa = 17.99ksi

Pa = 17.99 k/in.2 × 31.2in.2 = 561k > 500k ∴ OK

 

Strong Axis:

Assumes that the 2nd floor beam loads are applied at the top of the column. This is a bit conservative.

 

\(\frac{KL}{r_{x}} = \frac{(1)(26^{'}\times 12 in./ft.)}{5.47^{"}} = 57\)

 

From the Table 10.1; Fa = 17.71ksi
Pa = 17.71 k/in.2 × 31.2in.2 = 553k > 500k ∴ OK
