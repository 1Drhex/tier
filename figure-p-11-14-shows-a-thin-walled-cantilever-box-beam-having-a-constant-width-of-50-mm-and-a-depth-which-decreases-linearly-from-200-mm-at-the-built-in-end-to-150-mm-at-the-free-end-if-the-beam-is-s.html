Guest Question.

Figure P.11.14 shows a thin-walled cantilever box-beam having a constant width of 50 mm and a depth which decreases linearly from 200 mm at the built-in end to 150 mm at the free end. If the beam is subjected to a torque of 1 kNm at its free end, plot the angle of twist of the beam at 500 mm intervals along its length and determine the maximum shear stress in the beam section. Take G = 25 000 N/mm².

Admin
Solution

The torque is constant along the length of the beam and is 1 kNm. Also the thickness is constant round the beam section so that the shear stress will be a maximum where the area enclosed by the mid-line of the section wall is a minimum, i.e. at the free end. Then

\( \tau_{\max }=\frac{1000 \times 10^{3}}{2 \times 50 \times 150 \times 2}=33.3 N / mm ^{2} \)

The rate of twist is given by Eq. (11.25) in which \(\oint d s / t \) varies along the length of the beam as does the area enclosed by the mid-line of the section wall. Then

\(\frac{ d θ}{dx} = \frac{ T}{4A^2G} \oint \frac{ d s}{t} \) (11.25)

\( \oint \frac{ d s}{t}=\left[\frac{2 \times 50+2\left(150+\frac{50 x}{2500}\right)}{2}\right]=200+0.02 x \)

where x is measured from the free end of the beam.
Also

\( A=50\left(150+\frac{50 x}{2500}\right)=7500+x \)

Then

\( \frac{ d \theta}{ d x}=\left(\frac{1 \times 10^{6}}{4 \times 25000}\right) \frac{(200+0.02 x)}{(7500+x)^{2}} \)

or

\( \frac{ d \theta}{ d x}=10\left[\frac{(20000+2 x)}{100(7500+x)^{2}}\right] \)

i.e.

\( \frac{ d \theta}{ d x}=0.2\left[\frac{2500} {(7500+x)^{2}}+\frac{1}{(7500+x)}\right] \)

Then

\( \theta=0.2\left[\frac{-2500}{(7500+x)}+\log _{ e }(7500+x)+ B \right] \)

When \( x=2500 mm , \theta=0 \text { so that } B =-8.96 \) and

\( \theta=0.2\left[\frac{-2500}{(7500+x)}+\log _{ e }(7500+x)-8.96\right] rad \)

When \( x=0, \theta=4.2^{\circ} \) etc.
