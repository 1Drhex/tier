Guest Question.

Determine the force in each member of the Fink truss shown in Fig. \(4.26(\mathrm{a})\).

Admin
Solution

The Fink truss shown in Fig. 4.26(a) is a compound truss formed by connecting two simple trusses, \(A C L\) and \(D F L\), by a common joint \(L\) and a member \(C D\).

Static Determinacy. The truss contains 27 members and 15 joints and is supported by 3 reactions. Because \(m+r=2 j\) and the reactions and the members of the truss are properly arranged, it is statically determinate.

Reactions. The reactions at supports \(A\) and \(F\) of the truss, as computed by applying the three equations of equilibrium to the free-body diagram of the entire truss (Fig. 4.26(b)), are

\( A_{x}=0 \quad A_{y}=42 \mathrm{k} \uparrow \quad F_{y}=42 \mathrm{k} \uparrow \)

Joint \(\boldsymbol{A}\). The method of joints can now be started at joint \(A\), which has only two unknown forces, \(F_{A B}\) and \(F_{A l}\), acting on it. By inspection of the forces acting at this joint (see Fig. \(4.26(b))\), we obtain the following:

\( \begin{array}{c} F_{A I}=93.91 \mathrm{k}(\mathrm{C}) \\F_{A B}=84 \mathrm{k}(\mathrm{T}) \end{array} \)

Joint \(\boldsymbol{I}\). The free-body diagram of joint \(I\) is shown in Fig. \(4.26(\mathrm{c})\). Member \(B I\) is perpendicular to members \(A I\) and \(I J\), which are collinear, so the computation of member forces can be simplified by using an \(\bar{x}\) axis in the direction of the collinear members, as shown in Fig. 4.26(c).

\( \begin{array}{rl} +\nwarrow \sum F_{\bar{y}}=0 & -\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}(12)-F_{B I}=0 \\ F_{B I} & =-10.73 \mathrm{k} \\ F_{B I} & =10.73 \mathrm{k}(\mathrm{C}) \\ +\nearrow \sum F_{\bar{x}}=0 & 93.91-\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}(12)+F_{I J}=0 \\ F_{I J} & =-88.54 \mathrm{k} \\ F_{I J} & =88.54 \mathrm{k}(\mathrm{C}) \end{array} \)

Joint \(\boldsymbol{B}\). Considering the equilibrium of joint \(B\), we obtain (see Fig. \(4.26\) (b)) the following:

\( \begin{array}{ll} +\uparrow \sum F_{y}=0 & -\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}(10.73)+\frac{4}{5} F_{B J}=0 \\ & F_{B J}=12 \mathrm{k}(\mathrm{T}) \\ +\rightarrow \sum F_{x}=0 & -84+\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}(10.73)+\frac{3}{5}(12)+F_{B C}=0 \\ & F_{B C}=72 \mathrm{k}(\mathrm{T}) \end{array} \)

Section \(\boldsymbol{ a a }\). Since at each of the next two joints, \(C\) and \(J\), there are three unknowns \(\left(F_{C D}, F_{C G}\right.\), and \(F_{C J}\) at joint \(C\) and \(F_{C J}, F_{G J}\) and \(F_{J K}\) at joint \(J\) ), we calculate \(F_{C D}\) by using section \(a a\), as shown in Fig. \(4.26(\) a). (If we moved to joint \(F\) and started computing member forces from that end of the truss, we would encounter similar difficulties at joints \(D\) and \(N\).)
The free-body diagram of the portion of the truss on the left side of section \(a a\) is shown in Fig. \(4.26(\mathrm{~d})\). We determine \(F_{C D}\) by summing moments about point \(L\), the point of intersection of the lines of action of \(F_{G L}\) and \(F_{K L}\).

\( \begin{array}{c} +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{L}=0 \quad-42(32)+12(24)+12(16)+12(8)+F_{C D}(16)=0 \\ F_{C D}=48 \mathrm{k}(\mathrm{T}) \end{array} \)

Joint \(\boldsymbol{C}\). With \(F_{C D}\) now known, there are only two unknowns, \(F_{C G}\) and \(F_{C J}\), at joint \(C .\) These forces can be determined by applying the two equations of equilibrium to the free body of joint \(C\), as shown in Fig. \(4.26(\mathrm{e})\).

\( \begin{array}{rr} +\uparrow \sum F_{y}=0 & \frac{2}{\sqrt{5}} F_{C J}+\frac{4}{5} F_{C G}=0 \\ +\rightarrow \sum F_{x}=0 & -72+48-\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} F_{C J}+\frac{3}{5} F_{C G}=0 \end{array} \)

Solving these equations simultaneously, we obtain

\( \begin{array}{c} F_{C J}=-21.47 \mathrm{k} \quad \text { and } \quad F_{C G}=24 \mathrm{k} \\ F_{C J}=21.47 \mathrm{k}(\mathrm{C}) \\ F_{C G}=24 \mathrm{k}(\mathrm{T}) \end{array} \)

Joints \(\boldsymbol{J}, \boldsymbol{K}\), and \(\boldsymbol{G} \). Similarly, by successively considering the equilibrium of joints \(J, K\), and \(G\), in that order, we determine the following:

\( \begin{array}{l} F_{J K}=83.18 \mathrm{k}(\mathrm{C}) \\ F_{G J}=12 \mathrm{k}(\mathrm{T}) \\ F_{K L}=77.81 \mathrm{k}(\mathrm{C}) \\ F_{G K}=10.73 \mathrm{k}(\mathrm{C}) \\ F_{G L}=36 \mathrm{k}(\mathrm{T}) \end{array} \)

Symmetry. Since the geometry of the truss and the applied loading are symmetrical about the center line of the truss (shown in Fig. 4.26(b)), its member forces will also be symmetrical with respect to the line of symmetry. It is, therefore, sufficient to determine member forces in only one-half of the truss. The member forces determined here for the left half of the truss are shown in Fig. \(4.26(b)\). The forces in the right half can be obtained from the consideration of symmetry; for example, the force in member \(M N\) is equal to that in member \(J K\), and so forth. The reader is urged to verify this by computing a few member forces in the right half of the truss.
