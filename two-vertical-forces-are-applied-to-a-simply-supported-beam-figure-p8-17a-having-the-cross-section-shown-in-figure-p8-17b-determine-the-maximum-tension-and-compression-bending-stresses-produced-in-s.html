Guest Question.

Two vertical forces are applied to a simply supported beam (Figure P8.17a) having the cross section shown in Figure P8.17b. Determine the maximum tension and compression bending stresses produced in segment BC of the beam.

Admin
Solution

Centroid location in y direction:

 

\(\begin{array}{lccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} \text { (from bottom) } \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 3,000.0 & 167.5 & 502,500.0 \\\text { stem } & 1,440.0 & 80.0 & 115,200.0 \\\hline & 4,440 mm ^{2} & & 617,700 mm ^{3}\end{array}\)

 

\(\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{617,700 mm ^{3}}{4,440 mm ^{2}}=139.1216 mm\)

 

(measured upward from bottom edge of stem)

 

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

\(\begin{array}{lcccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 56,250.00 & 28.38 & 2,415,997.08 & 2,472,247.08 \\\text { stem } & 3,072,000.00 & -59.12 & 5,033,327.25 & 8,105,327.25 \\\hline & \text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( mm ^{4}\right)= & 10,577,574.32\end{array}\)

 

Shear-force and bending-moment diagrams:

The maximum moment occurs between B and C. The moment magnitude is 12 kN-m.

 

Maximum tension bending stress:
For a positive bending moment, the maximum tension bending stress will occur at the bottom surface of this cross section. From the flexure formula, the bending stress at the bottom of the tee stem is:

 

\(\sigma_{x}=-\frac{M y}{I_{z}}=-\frac{(12 kN – m )(-139.1216 mm )(1,000 N / kN )(1,000 mm / m )}{10.5776 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}}=157.8 MPa ( T )\)

 

Maximum compression bending stress:
The maximum compression bending stress will occur at the top of the flange:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=-\frac{M y}{I_{z}} \\&=-\frac{(12 kN – m )(175 mm -139.1216 mm )(1,000 N / kN )(1,000 mm / m )}{10.5776 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}} \\&=-40.7 MPa =40.7 MPa ( C )\end{aligned}\)
