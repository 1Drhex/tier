Guest Question.

Consider two infinitely long parallel plates that are separated by a distance L, as shown in Fig. 8.26. The plates are very deep in the direction perpendicular to the plane of the paper. The space between the two plates is evacuated. The two plates are maintained at temperatures of 810 K and 490 K, respectively. The two plates have emissivities of 0.95 and 0.45 respectively. Determine the net radiation heat transfer between the two plates.

Admin
Solution

\(F_{11}=0\) and \(F_{12}=1\)

\(F_{21}=1\) and \(F_{22}=0\)

View factor matrix = \(\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \)

We can solve this problem using the radiosity-irradiation method.

\(J_i=\varepsilon _i\sigma T_i^4+(1-\varepsilon _i)G_i\)

 

\(G_i=\sum\limits_{j=1}^{N}{F_{ji}J_j} \)

 

\(q_i=J_i-G_i\)

Now, we write the radiosity and irradiation expressions for the two surfaces 1 and 2.

\(G_1= F_{21}J_2+F_{11}J_1\Longrightarrow G_1=J_2\) \(F_{21}→1, F_{11}→0\)

 

\(G_2= F_{12}J_1+F_{22}J_2\Longrightarrow G_2=J_1\) \(F_{12}→1, F_{22}→0\)

 

\(J_1=\varepsilon _1\sigma T_1^4+(1-\varepsilon _1)J_2\) (8.145)

 

\(J_2=\varepsilon _2\sigma T_2^4+(1-\varepsilon _2)J_1\) (8.146)

Substituting \(J_2\) from Eq. (8.146) in Eq. (8.145), we have

\(J_1=\varepsilon _1\sigma T_1^4+(1-\varepsilon _1)\left[\varepsilon _2\sigma T_2^4 +(1-\varepsilon _2)J_1\right] \)

 

\(J_1\left[1-(1-\varepsilon _1)(1-\varepsilon _2)\right] =\varepsilon _1\sigma T_1^4+(1-\varepsilon _1)\varepsilon _2\sigma T_2^4\)

 

\(J_1 =\frac{ \varepsilon _1\sigma T_1^4 + \varepsilon _2\sigma T_2^4 -\varepsilon _1 \varepsilon _2\sigma T_2^4}{1-(1-\varepsilon _1)(1-\varepsilon _2)} \)

 

\(q_1=J_1-G_1\Rightarrow q_1=J_1-J_2\)

 

\(q_1=\frac{\varepsilon _1\varepsilon _2\left[\sigma T_1^4-\sigma T_2^4\right] }{1-(1-\varepsilon _1)(1-\varepsilon _2)} \)

 

\(q_1=\frac{\sigma \left[T_1^4-T_2^4\right] }{\frac{1}{\varepsilon _1}+\frac{1}{\varepsilon _2}-1 } \)

 

\(q_1=\frac{5.67\times 10^{-8}\left[810^4-490^4\right] }{\frac{1}{0.95}+\frac{1}{0.45}-1 } \)

 

\(\therefore q_1=9292.4 W/m^2\)
