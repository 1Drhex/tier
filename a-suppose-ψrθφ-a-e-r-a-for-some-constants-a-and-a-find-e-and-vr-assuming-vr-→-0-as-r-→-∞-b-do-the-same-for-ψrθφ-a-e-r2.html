Guest Question.

(a) Suppose \( \psi(r, \theta, \phi)=A e^{-r / a} \), for some constants A and a. Find E and V(r) , assuming V(r) → 0 as r → ∞. (b) Do the same for \( \psi(r, \theta, \phi)=A e^{-r^{2} / a^{2}} \) , assuming V(0) = 0 .

Admin
Solution

(a) Using Equations 4.8 and 4.13,

\( -\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \nabla^{2} \psi+V \psi=E \psi \) (4.8).

\( \nabla^{2}=\frac{1}{r^{2}} \frac{\partial}{\partial r}\left(r^{2} \frac{\partial}{\partial r}\right)+\frac{1}{r^{2} \sin \theta} \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta}\left(\sin \theta \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta}\right)+\frac{1}{r^{2} \sin ^{2} \theta}\left(\frac{\partial^{2}}{\partial \phi^{2}}\right) \) (4.13).

\( -\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m}\left\{\frac{1}{r^{2}} \frac{d}{d r}\left(r^{2} \frac{d \psi}{d r}\right)\right\}+V \psi=E \psi, \quad \frac{1}{r^{2}} \frac{d}{d r}\left(r^{2} \frac{d \psi}{d r}\right)=\frac{1}{r^{2}}\left[r^{2} \frac{d^{2} \psi}{d r^{2}}+2 r \frac{d \psi}{d r}\right]=\frac{d^{2} \psi}{d r^{2}}+\frac{2}{r} \frac{d \psi}{d r}=\frac{1}{a^{2}} \psi-\frac{2}{r a} \psi \).

\( -\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m}\left(\frac{1}{a^{2}} \psi-\frac{2}{r a} \psi\right)+V \psi=E \psi \quad \Rightarrow \quad-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m a^{2}}+\frac{\hbar^{2}}{m a r}+V(r)=E \).

At r → ∞ we get \( E=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m a^{2}} \), so \( V(r)=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{m a r} \).

(b) This time

\( \frac{d \psi}{d r}=-\frac{2 r}{a^{2}} \psi, \quad \frac{d^{2} \psi}{d r^{2}}=-\frac{2}{a^{2}} \psi+\left(\frac{2 r}{a^{2}}\right)^{2} \psi \).

so

\( \frac{1}{r^{2}} \frac{d}{d r}\left(r^{2} \frac{d \psi}{d r}\right)=-\frac{2}{a^{2}} \psi+\frac{4 r^{2}}{a^{4}} \psi+\frac{2}{r}\left(-\frac{2 r}{a^{2}} \psi\right)=-\frac{6}{a^{2}} \psi+\frac{4 r^{2}}{a^{4}} \psi \).

\( -\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m}\left(-\frac{6}{a^{2}}+\frac{4 r^{2}}{a^{4}}\right)+V(r)=E \).

At r = 0 we get \( E=\frac{3 \hbar^{2}}{m a^{2}} \) so \( V(r)=\frac{2 \hbar^{2}}{m a^{4}} r^{2} \).
