Guest Question.

Consider the geometry given in Figure 16.2. The two plates are separated by a distance of 0.01 in (the same as in ). The temperature of the two plates is equal, at a value of 288 K (standard sea level temperature). The air pressure is constant throughout the flow and equal to 1 atm. The upper plate is moving at Mach 3. The shear stress at the lower wall is \(72 N / m ^{2}\). (This is about \(1.5 lb/ft^{2}\)—a much larger value than that associated with the low-speed case treated in .) Calculate the heat transfer to either plate. (Since the shear stress is constant throughout the flow, and the plates are at equal temperature, the heat transfer to the upper and lower plates is the same.)

Admin
Solution

The velocity of the upper plate is

\(u_{e}=M_{e} a_{e}=M_{e} \sqrt{\gamma R T_{e}}=3 \sqrt{(1.4)(288)(287)}=1020 m / s\)

The air density at both plates is (noting that 1 atm \(= 1.01 × 10^{5}N/m^{2})\)

\(\rho_{e}=\frac{p_{e}}{R T_{e}}=\frac{1.01 \times 10^{5}}{(287)(288)}=1.22 kg / m ^{3}\)

Hence, the skin-friction coefficient is

\(c_{f}=\frac{\tau_{w}}{\frac{1}{2} \rho_{e} u_{e}^{2}}=\frac{72}{(0.5)(1.22)(1020)^{2}}=1.13 \times 10^{-4}\)

From Reynolds analogy, Equation (16.92), we have

\(\frac{k}{\mu}=\frac{c_{p}}{\operatorname{Pr}} (16.92)\)

\(C_{H}=\frac{c_{f}}{2 \operatorname{Pr}}=\frac{1.13 \times 10^{-4}}{2(0.71)}=8 \times 10^{-5}\)

The adiabatic wall enthalpy, from Equation (16.82), is

\(h_{a w}=h_{e}+\operatorname{Pr} \frac{u_{e}^{2}}{2} (16.82)\)

\(h_{a w}=h_{e}+\operatorname{Pr} \frac{u_{e}^{2}}{2}=c_{p} T_{e}+\operatorname{Pr} \frac{u_{e}^{2}}{2}\)

For air, \(c_{p}= 1004.5 J/kg · K\). Thus,

\(h_{a w}=(1004.5)(288)+(0.71) \frac{(1020)^{2}}{2}=6.59 \times 10^{5} J / kg\)

[Note: This gives \(T_{a w}=h_{a w} / c_{p}=\left(6.59 \times 10^{5}\right) / 1004.5=656 K .\) In the adiabatic case, the wall would be quite warm.] Hence, from the definition of the Stanton number [Equation (16.55)], and noting that \(h_{w}=c_{p} T_{w}=(1004.6)(288)=2.89 \times 10^{5} J / kg\),

\(C_{H}=\frac{\dot{q}_{w}}{\rho_{e} u_{e}\left(h_{a w}-h_{w}\right)} (16.55)\)

\(\dot{q}_{w}=\rho_{e} u_{e}\left(h_{a w}-h_{w}\right) C_{H}=(1.22)(1020)\left[(6.59-2.89) \times 10^{5}\right]\left(8 \times 10^{-5}\right)\)

\(=3.68 \times 10^{4} W / m ^{2}\)
