Guest Question.

A pipeline consists of two pipe segments in series (Fig. P11.20).The specific gravity of the fluid is 0.81. If pump A has a constant power input of 1 MW, find the discharge, the pressure head in pumps A and B, and the required power for pump B. The minimum allowable pressure on the suction side of pump B is 150 kPa, and both pumps have an efficiency of 0.76.

Pipe L(m) D(mm) ΣK f
1 5000 750 2 0.023
2 7500 750 10 0.023
Admin
Solution

Write energy eqn. from junction at A to suction side of pump at B:

\(H_{P_A}=\left\lgroup R_1+\frac{\Sigma K}{2gA_1^2} \right\rgroup Q^2+\frac{p_B}{\gamma }+z_B \) ,

where \(H_{P_A}\) = pump head and \(p_B\) = pressure. The only unknown in the relation is Q, since

\(H_{P_A}=\frac{\dot{W}_{P_A}\eta }{\gamma Q} =\frac{1\times 10^6\times 0.76}{0.81\times 9810\times Q} =\frac{95.6}{Q} \) .

Evaluate the constants:
\(R_1=\frac{8f_1L_1}{g\pi ^2D_1^5} =\frac{8×0.23×5000}{9.81×\pi ^2×0.75^5}=40.0 , frac{\Sigma K}{2gA_1^2}=\frac{2}{2×9.81× (\pi / 4)^2×0.75^4} =0.52 \),

\(\frac{p_B}{\gamma }+z_B=\frac{150×10^3}{0.81×9810} +27=45.9 m \).

Substituting into the energy eqn., \(\frac{95.6}{Q}= 40.5 Q^2+ 45.9\).

Solve using Newton’s method: \(Q =\underline{1.053 m^3 /s} , and H_{P_A} =95.6/1.053= \underline{90.8 m}\) .

The second reach is now analyzed. Write energy eqn. from suction side of pump at B to the downstream reservoir:

\(\frac{p_B}{\gamma }+z_B +H_{P_A}=\left\lgroup R_2+\frac{\Sigma K}{2gA_2^2} \right\rgroup Q^2+50\).

Evaluate the constants:

\(R_2=\frac{8f_2L_2}{g\pi ^2D_2^5} =\frac{8×0.23×7500}{9.81×\pi ^2×0.75^5}=60.1 , frac{\Sigma K}{2gA_1^2}=\frac{10}{2×9.81× (\pi / 4)^2×0.75^4} =2.61 \),

∴ \(45.9 + H_{P_A} = (60.1 + 2.6) × 1.053^2 + 50, or H_{P_A} = \underline{73.6 m} \).

∴ \(\dot{W}_{P_B} =\frac{\gamma QH_{P_A}}{\eta } =\frac{0.81\times 9810\times 1.053\times 73.6}{0.76} =8.10\times 10^5 W \), or

\(\dot{W}_{P_B}=\underline{810 KW}\) .
