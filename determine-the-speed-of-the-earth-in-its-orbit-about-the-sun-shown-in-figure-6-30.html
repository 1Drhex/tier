Guest Question.

Determine the speed of the earth in its orbit about the sun, shown in figure 6.30.

Admin
Solution

The mass of the sun is \(m_{sun} = 1.99 × 10^{30 }kg\). The mean orbital radius of the earth around the sun, found in table 6.2, is \(r_{es} = 1.50 × 10^{11}\) m.

The speed of the earth around the sun \(v_{es}\), found from equation 6.49, is

\(v=\sqrt{\frac{Gm_e}{r} }\) (6.49)

\(v_{es}=\sqrt{\frac{Gm_s}{r_{es}} }\)

 

\(= \sqrt{\left\lgroup6.67\times 10^{-11}\frac{N m^2}{kg^2} \right\rgroup\frac{\left(1.99 \times 10^{30} kg\right) }{\left(1.50\times 10^{11} m\right) } }\)

 

\(=2.97 × 10^4 m/s = 29.7 km/s = 66,600 mi/hr\)

That is, as you sit and read this book, you are speeding through space at 66,600 mph. This is a little over 18 miles each second. The mean orbital speed of any of the planets or satellites can be determined in the same way.
