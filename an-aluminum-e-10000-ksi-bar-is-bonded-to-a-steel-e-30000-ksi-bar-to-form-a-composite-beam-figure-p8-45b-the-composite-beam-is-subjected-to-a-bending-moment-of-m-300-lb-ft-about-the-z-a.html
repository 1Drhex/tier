Guest Question.

An aluminum [E = 10,000 ksi] bar is bonded to a steel [E = 30,000 ksi] bar to form a composite beam (Figure P8.45b). The composite beam is subjected to a bending moment of M = +300 lb-ft about the z axis (Figure P8.45a). Determine:
(a) the maximum bending stresses in the aluminum and steel bars.
(b) the stress in the two materials at the joint where they are bonded together.

Admin
Solution

Denote the aluminum as material (1) and denote the steel as material (2). The modular ratio is:

\(n=\frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}=\frac{30,000 ksi }{10,000 ksi }=3\)

Transform the steel bar (2) into an equivalent amount of aluminum (1) by multiplying its width by the modular ratio: \(b_{2, \text { trans }}\) = 3(2.00 in.) = 6.00 in. Thus, for calculation purposes, the 2.00 in. Ã— 0.75 in. steel bar is replaced by an aluminum bar that is 6.00-in. wide and 0.75-in. thick.

Centroid location of the transformed section in the vertical direction

\(\begin{array}{lccccc}& & & & \\\text { Shape } & \text { Width } b & \text { Height } h & \text { Area } A_{i} & \text { (from bottom) } & y_{i} A_{i} \\& \text { (in.) } & \text { (in.) } & \left(\text { in. }^{2}\right) & \text { (in.) } & \left(\text { in. }^{3}\right) \\\hline \text { aluminum bar (1) } & 2.00 & 0.50 & 1.00 & 0.25 & 0.2500 \\\text { transformed steel bar (2) } & 6.00 & 0.75 & 4.50 & 0.875 & 3.9375 \\\hline & & & 5.50 & & 4.1875\end{array}\)

 

\(\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{4.1875 in. ^{3}}{5.50 in .^{2}}=0.7614 in.\) (measured upward from bottom edge of section)

Moment of inertia about the horizontal centroidal axis

\(\begin{array}{ccccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( in .^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { aluminum bar (1) } & 0.02083 & -0.5114 & 0.2615 & 0.2823 \\\text { transformed steel bar (2) } & 0.2109 & 0.1136 & 0.05811 & 0.2690 \\\hline {\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }=} & & && 0.5514 \text { in. }^{4}\end{array}\)

 

(a) Maximum bending stress in aluminum bar (1)
From the flexure formula, the maximum bending stress in aluminum bar (1) is:

\(\sigma_{1}=-\frac{M y}{I}=-\frac{(300 lb – f )(-0.7614 in .)(12 in. / ft )}{0.5514 in .{ }^{4}}=4,970 psi ( T )\)

(a) Maximum bending stress in steel bar (2)
The bending stress in the transformed material must be multiplied by the modular ratio n. Therefore, the maximum bending stress in steel bar (2) is:

\(\sigma_{2}=-\frac{M y}{I}=-(3) \frac{(300 lb – ft )(1.250 in .-0.7614 in .)(12 in. / ft )}{0.5514 in. ^{4}}=9,570 psi ( C )\)

(b) Bending stress in aluminum bar (1) at interface

\(\sigma_{1}=-\frac{M y}{I}=-\frac{(300 lb – ft )(0.50 in .-0.7614 in .)(12 in . / ft )}{0.5514 in. ^{4}}=1,706 psi ( T )\)

(b) Bending stress in steel bar (2) at interface

\(\sigma_{2}=-\frac{M y}{I}=-(3) \frac{(300 lb – ft )(0.50 in .-0.7614 in .)(12 in. / ft )}{0.5514 in .^{4}}=5,120 psi ( T )\)
