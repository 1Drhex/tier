Guest Question.

Derive the Q of (a) the series RLC circuit, (b) the parallel RLC circuit.

Admin
Solution

(a) In the time domain, the instantaneous stored energy in the circuit is given by

\( W_{s}=\frac{1}{2} L i^{2}+\frac{q^{2}}{2 C} \)

For a maximum,

\( \frac{d W_{s}}{d t}=L i \frac{d i}{d t}+\frac{q}{C} \frac{d q}{d t}=i\left(L \frac{d i}{d t}+\frac{q}{C}\right)=i\left(v_{L}+v_{C}\right)=0 \)

 

Thus, the maximum stored energy is \( W_{s} \text { at } i=0 \text { or } W_{s} \text { at } v_{L}+v_{C}=0, \) whichever is the larger. Now the capacitor voltage, and therefore the charge, lags the current by 90Â°; hence, i = 0 implies \( q=\pm Q_{\max } \)and

\( \left.W_{s}\right|_{i=0}=\frac{Q_{\max }^{2}}{2 C}=\frac{1}{2} C V_{C \max }^{2}=\frac{1}{2} C\left(\frac{I_{\max }}{\omega C}\right)^{2}=\frac{I_{\max }^{2}}{2 C \omega^{2}} \)

On the other hand, \( v_{L}+v_{C}=0 \text { implies } v_{L}=v_{C}=0 \text { and } i=\pm I_{\max } \) (see the phasor diagram, Fig. 12-37), so that

\( \left.W_{s}\right|_{v_{L}+v_{C}=0}=\frac{1}{2} L I_{\max }^{2} \)

It follows that

\( W_{s \max }= \begin{cases} I _{\max }^{2} / 2 C \omega^{2} & \left(\omega \leq \omega_{0}\right) \\ L I_{\max }^{2} / 2 & \left(\omega \geq \omega_{0}\right)\end{cases} \)

 

The energy dissipated per cycle (in the resistor) is \( W_{d}=I_{\max }^{2} R \pi / \omega \). Consequently,

\( Q=2 \pi \frac{W_{s \max }}{W_{d}}= \begin{cases}1 / \omega C R & \left(\omega \leq \omega_{0}\right) \\ \omega L / R & \left(\omega \geq \omega_{0}\right)\end{cases} \)

(b) For the parallel combination with applied voltage v(t),

\( W_{s}=\frac{1}{2} L I_{L}^{2}+\frac{1}{2 C} q_{C}^{2} \)

 

and \( \frac{d W_{s}}{d t}=L i_{L} \frac{d i_{L}}{d t}+\frac{q_{C}}{C} i_{C}=v\left(i_{L}+i_{C}\right)=0 \)

 

If \( v=0 \text {, then } q_{C}=0 \) and

\( i_{L}=\pm I_{L \max }=\pm \frac{V_{\max }}{\omega L} \)

 

giving \( W_{s \mid v=0}=\frac{V_{\max }^{2}}{2 L \omega^{2}} \)

 

If \( i_{L}+i_{C}=0 \), then (see Fig. 12-38) \( i_{L}=i_{C}=0 \text { and } q_{C}=\pm C V_{\max } \), giving

\( \left.W_{s}\right|_{i_{L}+i_{C}=0}=\frac{1}{2} C V_{\max }^{2} \)

 

Therefore \( W_{s \max }= \begin{cases}V_{\max }^{2} / 2 L \omega^{2} & \left(\omega \leq \omega_{a}\right) \\ C V_{\max }^{2} / 2 & \left(\omega \geq \omega_{a}\right)\end{cases} \)

 

The energy dissipated per cycle in \( R \text { is } W_{d}=V_{\max }^{2} \pi / R \omega \). Consequently,

 

\( Q=2 \pi \frac{W_{s \max }}{W_{d}}=\left\{\begin{array}{cc} R / L \omega & \left(\omega \leq \omega_{a}\right) \\ \omega C R & \left(\omega \geq \omega_{a}\right) \end{array}\right\} \)
