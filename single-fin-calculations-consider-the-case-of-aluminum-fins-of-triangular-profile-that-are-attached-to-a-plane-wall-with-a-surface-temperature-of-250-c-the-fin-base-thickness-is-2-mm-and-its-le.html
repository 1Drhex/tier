Guest Question.

Single Fin Calculations. Consider the case of aluminum fins of triangular profile that are attached to a plane wall with a surface temperature of 250 °C. The fin base thickness is 2 mm and its length is 6 mm. The system is in ambient air at a temperature of 20 °C and the surface convection coefficient is 40W/\(m^2\) · K. Consider a 1 m width of a single fin. Determine:

1. the heat transfer rate without the fin,

2. the maximum heat transfer rate from the fin, and

3. the fin efficiency, thermal resistance, and effectiveness.

Properties of the aluminum may be evaluated at the average temperature, \(\overline{T}=(T_b+T_\infty)\)/2 = (250 + 20)/2 = 135°C = 408 K, where k ≈ 240 W/m · K.

Admin
Solution

Determine the base area of the fin:

\(A_b = z_{f,t}w = (0.002)(1) = 0.002 m^2\)

Calculate the excess temperature at the base of the fin:

\(\theta _b=T_b-T_\infty\) = 250 – 20 = 230°C = 230 K

Calculate the heat transfer rate without a fin, \(Q_{w/o, f}\), employing
Equation (8.19).

\(Q_{f,max}=hA_{f}\theta _{b}\)

\(=(130)(3.96\times 10^{-3})(145)=74.35W\) (8.19)

\(Q_{w/o,f}=hA_b\theta _b\)

Substitution,

\(Q_{w/o,f}\) = (40)(0.002)(230) = 18.4 W

Also, calculate the maximum heat transfer rate, \(Q_{f ,max}\) :

\(Q_{f ,max}=hA_fθ_b\)

= (40)(0.012)(230) = 110 W

where,

\(A_f\) = 0.0118 ≈ 0.012 \(m^2\)

Check the Biot number criterion to determine if the use of the fin efficiency
figure is valid,

\(Bi_f=\frac{h(z_{f,t}/2)}{k}\)

\(=\frac{(40)(0.002/2)}{240}=1.67\times 10^{-4}\lt 0.25\)

Since \(Bi_f\) < 0.25, the use of the figure is permitted:

\(z_{f,c}\) = corrected length = \(z_f\) = 0.006 m

\(A_p = profile\ area = L(z_{f,t}/2) = (0.006)(0.002/2) = 6×10^{-6}m^2\)

\(A_f\) = fin surface area = 2L\(\sqrt{z^2_f-(z_{f,t/2})^2}\approx 0.012m^2\)

Determine the abscissa for the fin efficiency figure (see Figure 8.8):

\(Abscissa=\frac{\sqrt{z^3_{f,c}h}}{kA_p}\)

\(=\sqrt{\frac{(0.006)^3(40)}{(240)(6\times 10^{-6})}}\approx 0.0775\)

Obtain the fin efficiency from Figure 8.8 for triangular profile straight fins,

\(η_f\) = 0.99

Calculate the fin heat transfer rate, Qf , employing Equation (8.7),

\(Q_f = η_fQ_{f,max}\) = (0.99)(110) = 108.9 W

The fin thermal resistance, \(R_{t , f}\), is therefore,

\(R_{t,f}=\frac{\theta_b}{Q_f}\)

\(=\frac{230}{108.9}=2.1 °C/W\)
