Replacement Analysis under the Finite Planning Horizon (PW Approach)

Consider again the defender and the challenger in . Suppose that the firm has a contract to perform a given service, using the current defender or the challenger for the next eight years. After the contract work, neither the defender nor the challenger will be retained. What is the best replacement strategy?

Admin
Solution

Given: AEC values for both the defender and the challenger over eight years, planning horizon = 8 years, and i = 15%.
Find: Most economical replacement scenario.

Recall again the annual equivalent costs for the defender and challenger under the assumed holding periods (a boxed number denotes the minimum AEC value at \(N_{D }*\) = 2 and \(N_{C }*\) = 4, respectively):

Annual Equivalent Cost ($)
Challenger Defender n
7,700 5,380 1
6,184 5,203 2
5,756 5,469 3
5,625 5,844 4
5,631 6,258 5
5,721 6,682 6

Many replacement scenario options would fulfill an eight-year planning horizon, as shown in Figure 14.11. Of these options, six appear to be the most likely by inspection. These options are listed, and the present equivalent cost for each option is calculated, as follows.

  • Option 1: \(\left(j_{0},0 \right) , \left(j, 4\right) , \left(j, 4\right)\)

\(PW\left(15\%\right) _{1}\) = $5,625(P/A, 15%, 8)
= $25,241

  • Option 2: \(\left(j_{0},1 \right) , \left(j, 4\right) , \left(j, 3\right)\)

\(PW\left(15\%\right) _{2}\) = $5,380(P/F, 15%, 1)
+ $5,625(P/A, 15%, 4)(P/F, 15%, 1)
+ $5,756(P/A, 15%, 3)(P/F, 15%, 5)
= $25,177

  • Option 3: \(\left(j_{0},2 \right) , \left(j, 4\right) , \left(j, 2\right)\)

\(PW\left(15\%\right) _{3}\) = $5,203(P/A, 15%, 2)
+ $5,625(P/A, 15%, 4)(P/F, 15%, 2)
+ $6,184(P/A, 15%, 2)(P/F, 15%, 6)
= $24,948

  • Option 4: \(\left(j_{0},3 \right) , \left(j, 5\right)\)

\(PW\left(15\%\right) _{4}\) = $5,469(P/A, 15%, 3)
+$5,631(P/A, 15%, 5)(P/F, 15%, 3)
= 24,898 \(\leftarrow\) minimum cost

  • Option 5: \(\left(j_{0},3 \right) , \left(j, 4\right) , \left(j, 1\right)\)

\(PW\left(15\%\right) _{5}\) = $5,469(P/A, 15%, 3)
+$5,625(P/A, 15%, 4)(P/F, 15%, 3)
+$7,700(P/F, 15%, 8)
= $25,563

  • Option 6: \(\left(j_{0},4 \right) , \left(j, 4\right)\)

\(PW\left(15\%\right) _{6}\) = $5,844(P/A, 15%, 4)
+$5,625(P/A, 15%, 4)(P/F, 15%, 4)
= $25,866

An examination of the present equivalent cost of a planning horizon of eight years indicates that the least-cost solution among six decision options considered appears to be option 4: Retain the defender for three years, purchase the challenger, and keep it for five years.

Comments: In this example, we examined only six decision options that were likely to lead to the best solution, but it is important to note that several other possibilities have not been looked at. To explain, consider Figure 14.12, which shows a graphical representation of various replacement strategies under a finite planning horizon. For example, the replacement strategy \(\left[\left(j_{0},2 \right) , \left(j, 3\right) , \left(j, 3\right) \right]\) (shown as a solid line in the figure) is certainly feasible, but we did not include it in the previous computation. Naturally, as we extend the planning horizon, the number of possible decision options can easily multiply. To make sure that we indeed find the optimal solution for such a problem, an optimization technique such as dynamic programming can be \(used.^{3}\)
