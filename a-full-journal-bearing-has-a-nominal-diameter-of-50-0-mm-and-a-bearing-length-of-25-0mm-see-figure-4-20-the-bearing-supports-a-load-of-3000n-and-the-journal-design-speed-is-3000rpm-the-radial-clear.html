Guest Question.

A full journal bearing has a nominal diameter of 50.0 mm and a bearing length of 25.0mm (see Figure 4.20).The bearing supports a load of 3000N, and the journal design speed is 3000rpm.The radial clearance has been specified as 0.04mm.An SAE 10 oil has been chosen and the lubricant supply temperature is 50°C. Find the temperature rise of the lubricant, the lubricant flow rate, the minimum film thickness, the torque required to overcome friction and the heat generated in the bearing.

Admin
Solution

The primary data are D = 50.0 mm, L =25 mm, W = 3000 N, N =3000 rpm,c = 0.04 mm, SAE 10, \( T_{1} =50°C. \) Guess a value for the lubricant temperature rise ΔT across the bearing to be say ΔT = 20°C.

\( T_{av} = T_{1} + \frac{\Delta T} {2}= 50+\frac{20} {2}= 60^{\circ } C \)

From Figure 4.7 for SAE 10 at 60°C, μ=0.014 Pa s. \( N_{s} =3000/60=50rps \)

L/D = 25/50 = 0.5.

\( P=\frac{W}{LD}=\frac{3000}{0.025\times 0.05} =2.4\times 10^{6} N/m^{2} \)

 

\( S=\frac{\downarrow r}{\rightarrow c}^{2} \frac{\mu N_{s} }{P} \)

 

\( = \frac{\downarrow 25\times 10^{-3} }{\rightarrow 0.04\times 10^{-3} } \frac{0.014\times 50}{2.4\times 10^{6} } =0.1139 \)

From Figure 4.12 with S = 0.1139 and L/D=0.5,(r/c)f=3.8.

From Figure 4.13 with S = 0.1139 and L/D=0.5, \( (rcN_{s}L)=5.34 \)

From Figure 4.14 with S = 0.1139 and L/D = 0.5, \( Q_{s}/Q =0.852. \)

The value of the temperature rise of the lubricant can now be calculated using Eq. 4.9:

\( \Delta T=\frac{8.3\times 10^{-6}P }{1-0.5\times (Q_{S}/Q )} \times \frac{(r/c)f}{Q/(rcN_{s}L )} \)

 

\( =\frac{8.3\times 10^{-6}\times 2.4\times 10^{6} }{1-(0.5\times 0.852)} \times \frac{3.8}{5.34} =24.70^{\circ }C \)

As the value calculated for the lubricant temperature rise is significantly different to the estimated value, it is necessary to repeat the above calculation, but using the new improved estimate for determining the average lubricant temperature.

Using ΔT=24.70ْ C to calculate \( T_{av} \) gives:
\( T_{av} =50+\frac{24.70}{2} =62.35ْ°C \)

Repeating the procedure using the new value for \( T_{av} \) gives:

\( \mu =0.0136 Pa s \)

S=0.1107.

From Figure 4.12 with S =0.1107 and L/D=0.5,(r/c)f=3.7.

From Figure 4.13 with S = 0.1107 andL/D =0.5, Q/\( (rcN_{s}L)=5.35 \)

From Figure 4.14 with S = 0.1107 andL/D = 0.5, \(Q_s/Q =0.856\).

\( \Delta T=\frac{8.3\times 10^{-6}\times 2.4\times 10^{6} }{1-0.5\times (0.856 )} \times \frac{3.7}{5.34} \times \frac{3.7}{5.35} =24.08°C \)

 

\( T_{av}=50+\frac{24.08}{2}=62.04°C \)

This value for \( T_{av} \) is close to the previouscalculated value suggesting that the solution has converged. For \( T_{av} \) =62.04°C, 0.0136 Pa s and S 0.1107. The other parameters can now be found. μ= 0.0136 Pa s and S =0.1107.

The other parameters can now be found

Q= rc\( N_{s} \times 5.35 \\ = 25 \times 0.04 \times 50 \times 25 \times 5.35 \\ = 6688 m m^{3}/s \).

From Figure 4.11, \( h_{0} /c=0.22,h_{0}=0.0088 mm. \)

f= 3.7 ×(c/r) = 3.7×(0.04/25)=0.00592

The torque is given by

Torque \(= fWr \\ = 0.00592 \times 3000 \times 0.025 \\=0.444\) Nm

The power dissipated in the bearing is given by

Power = 2π × Torque × \( N_{s} \) = 139.5 W.
