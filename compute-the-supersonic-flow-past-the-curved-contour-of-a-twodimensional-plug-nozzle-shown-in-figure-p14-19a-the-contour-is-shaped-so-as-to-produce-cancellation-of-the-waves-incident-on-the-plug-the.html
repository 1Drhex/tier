Guest Question.

Compute the supersonic flow past the curved contour of a twodimensional plug nozzle shown in Figure P14.19a. The contour is shaped so as to produce cancellation of the waves incident on the plug. The nozzle is to provide a flow of air (γ = 1.4) at Mach 1.9502856. The Mach number at the throat of the nozzle is sonic. Use the region-to-region method for a 5 wave expansion as indicated in Figure 14.19b. Determine the Mach number distribution and the inclinations of the characteristics.

Admin
Solution

In going from region 1 to region 6 we would have to cross 5 characteristics of Type I for which \(\Delta v=\Delta \alpha \text { or } v_{6}-v_{1}=\alpha_{6}-\alpha_{1} . \text { Since } M_{1}=1 \text { and } M_{6}=1.9502856, v_{1}=0 \text { and } v_{6}=25.0000\), respectively. And since \(\alpha_{6}=0, \text { we see that } \alpha_{1}=-25.0000^{\circ}\). Because we are considering the expansion to take place across 5 waves, the flow angle increases by 5° in passing from region-to-region. The following table is readily established:
