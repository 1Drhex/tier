Guest Question.

For the system of , compute the fault current and voltages at the fault for the following faults at bus 3: (a) a bolted single line-toground fault, (b) a bolted line-to-line fault, (c) a bolted double line-toground fault. Also, for the single line-to-ground fault at bus 3, determine the currents and voltages at the terminals of generators Gl and G2.

Admin
Solution

(a) For a single line-to-ground fault at bus 3, the interconnection of the sequence networks is shown below.
(See solution of )

\(\bar{I}_{0}=\bar{I}_{1}=\bar{I}_{2}=\frac{1 \angle 0^{\circ}}{j(0.199+0.175+0.175)}=-j 1.82\)

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{a} \\\bar{I}_{b} \\\bar{I}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a^{2} & a \\1 & a & a^{2}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}-j 1.82 \\-j 1.82 \\-j 1.82\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}-j 5.46 \\0 \\0\end{array}\right]\)

 

Sequence voltages are given by

\(\begin{gathered}\bar{V}_{0}=-j 0.199(-j 1.82)=-0.362 ; \bar{V}_{1}=1-j 0.175(-j 1.82)=0.681 ; \\\bar{V}_{2}=-j 0.175(-j 1.82)=-0.319\end{gathered}\)

The phase voltages are calculated as

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{V}_{a} \\\bar{V}_{b} \\\bar{V}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a^{2} & a \\1 & a & a^{2}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}-0.362 \\0.681 \\-0.319\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}0 \\1.022 \angle 238^{\circ} \\1.022 \angle 122^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\)

 

(b) For a line-to-line fault at bus 3, the sequence networks are interconnected as shown below:

\(\bar{I}_{1}=-\bar{I}_{2}=\frac{1 \angle 0^{\circ}}{j 0.175+j 0.175}=-j 2.86\)

Phase currents are then

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{a} \\\bar{I}_{b} \\\bar{I}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a^{2} & a \\1 & a & a^{2}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}0 \\-j 2.86 \\j 2.86\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}0 \\-4.95 \\4.95\end{array}\right]\)

The sequence voltages are

\(\bar{V}_{0}=0 ; \bar{V}_{1}=\bar{V}_{2}=\bar{I}_{1}(j 0.175)=0.5\)

Phase voltages are calculated as

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{V}_{a} \\\bar{V}_{b} \\\bar{V}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a^{2} & a \\1 & a & a^{2}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}0 \\0.5 \\0.5\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}1.0 \\-0.5 \\-0.5\end{array}\right]\)

 

(c) For a double line-to-ground fault at bus 3, the sequence network interconnection is shown below:

Sequence currents are calculated as

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{I}_{1}=\frac{1 \angle 0^{\circ}}{j 0.175+[j 0.175(j 0.199) /(j 0.175+j 0.199)]}=-j 3.73 \\&\bar{I}_{2}=\frac{0.199}{0.175+0.199}(j 3.73)=j 1.99 \\&\bar{I}_{0}=\frac{0.175}{0.175+0.199}(j 3.73)=j 1.75\end{aligned}\)

Phase currents are given by

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{a} \\\bar{I}_{b} \\\bar{I}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a^{2} & a \\1 & a & a^{2}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}j 1.75 \\-j 3.73 \\j 1.99\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}0 \\5.6 \angle 152.1^{\circ} \\5.6 \angle 27.9^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\)

The neutral fault current is \(\bar{I}_{b}+\bar{I}_{c}=3 \bar{I}_{0}=j 5.25\)

Sequence voltages are obtained as

\(\bar{V}_{0}=\bar{V}_{1}=\bar{V}_{2}=-(j 1.75)(j 0.199)=0.348\)

Phase voltages are then

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{V}_{a} \\\bar{V}_{b} \\\bar{V}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a^{2} & a \\1 & a & a^{2}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}0.348 \\0.348 \\0.348\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}1.044 \\0 \\0\end{array}\right]\)

Generator G1 (Bus 4):

From the circuit above, \(\bar{I}_{1}=\bar{I}_{2}=\frac{1}{2} \bar{I}_{f}=-j 0.91\)

Generator G2 (Bus 5):
From the interconnected sequence networks and solution of Prob. 9.31,

\(\bar{I}_{f}=-j 1.182 ; \quad \bar{I}_{1}=\bar{I}_{2}=\frac{1}{2} \bar{I}_{f}=-j 0.91 ; \quad \bar{I}_{0}=0\)

Recall that Y – ∆ transformer connections produce 30° phase shifts in sequence quantities. The HV quantities are to be shifted 30° ahead of the corresponding LV quantities for positive sequence, and vice versa for negative sequence. One may however neglect phase shifts. Since bus 5 is the LV side, considering phase shifts,

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{I}_{1}=0.91 \angle-90^{\circ}-30^{\circ}=0.91 \angle-120^{\circ}; \\&\bar{I}_{2}=0.91 \angle-90^{\circ}+30^{\circ}=0.91 \angle-60^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

Phase currents are then given by

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I}_{a} \\\bar{I}_{b} \\\bar{I}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a^{2} & a \\1 & a & a^{2}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}0 \\0.91 \angle-120^{\circ} \\0.91 \angle-60^{\circ}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}1.58 \angle-90^{\circ} \\1.58 \angle+90^{\circ} \\0\end{array}\right]\)

Positive and negative sequence voltages are the same as on the G1 side:

\(\bar{V}_{1}=0.818 ; \bar{V}_{2}=-0.182 ; \bar{V}_{0}=0 ;\)

With phase shift

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{V}_{1}=0.818 \angle-30^{\circ} \\&\bar{V}_{2}=0.182 \angle 210^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

Phase voltages are calculated as

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{V}_{a} \\\bar{V}_{b} \\\bar{V}_{c}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a^{2} & a \\1 & a & a^{2}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}0 \\0.818 \angle-30^{\circ} \\0.182 \angle 210^{\circ}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}0.744 \angle-42.2^{\circ} \\0.744 \angle 222.2^{\circ} \\1.00 \angle 90^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\)
