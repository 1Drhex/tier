Guest Question.

In the circuit shown in Fig. 7.8, the initial currents in inductors \(L_{1}\) and \(L_{2}\) have been established by sources not shown. The switch is opened at t=0.

a) Find \(i_{1}\) ,\(i_{2}\), and \(i_{3}\) for t≥0.

b) Calculate the initial energy stored in the parallel inductors.

c) Determine how much energy is stored in the inductors as t→∞.

d) Show that the total energy delivered to the resistive network equals the difference between the results obtained in (b) and (c).

Admin
Solution

a) The key to finding currents \(i_{1}\) ,\(i_{2}\),and \(i_{3}\) lies in knowing the voltage \(v\left(t \right)\) . We can easily find \(v\left(t \right)\) if we reduce the circuit shown in Fig. 7.8 to the equivalent form shown in Fig. 7.9. The parallel inductors simplify to an equivalent inductance of 4 H, carrying an initial current of 12 A. The resistive network reduces to a single resistance of 8Ω. Hence the initial value of \(i\left(t \right)\) is 12 A and the time constant is \(\frac{4}{8}\) , or 0.5 s. Therefore

 

\(i\left(t \right)=12e^{-2t} A , t \geq 0\).

 

Now \(v\left(t \right)\) is simply the product 8i so

 

\(v\left(t \right)=96e^{-2t} V , t \geq 0^{+}\).

 

The circuit shows that \(v\left(t \right)=0\) at \( t =0^{-}\), so the expression for \(v\left(t \right)\). is valid for \( t \geq 0^{+}\). After obtaining \(v\left(t \right)\) we can calculate \(i_{1}\) ,\(i_{2}\), and \(i_{3}\) :

 

\(i_{1}=\frac{1}{5}\int_{0}^{t}{96e^{-2x} dx-8} \)

 

\(= 1.6 -9.6 e^{-2t} A, t \geq 0,\)

 

\(i_{2}=\frac{1}{20}\int_{0}^{t}{96e^{-2x }dx-4} \)

 

\(=-1.6-2.4 e^{-2t} A, t \geq 0,\)

 

\(i_{3}=\frac{v\left(t \right)}{10}\frac{15}{25}=5.76e^{-2 t} A, t \geq 0^{+} \).

 

Note that the expressions for the inductor currents \(i_{1}\)
and \(i_{2}\) are valid for \( t \geq 0\). whereas the expression for the resistor current \(i_{3}\) is valid for \( t \geq 0^{+}\).

 

b) The initial energy stored in the inductors is

 

\(w=\frac{1}{2}\left(5\right)\left(64\right)+\frac{1}{2}\left(20\right)\left(16\right)=320 J \).

 

c) As t→∞,\(i_{1}→1.6 A\) and \(i_{2}→-1.6 A\). Therefore, a long time after the switch has been opened, the energy stored in the two inductors is

 

\(w=\frac{1}{2}\left(5\right)\left(1.6\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{2}\left(20\right)\left(-1.6\right)^{2}=32 J \).

 

d) We obtain the total energy delivered to the resistive network by integrating the expression for the instantaneous power from zero to infinity:

 

\(w=\int_{0}^{\infty}{p dt}=\int_{0}^{\infty}{1152e^{-4 t}dt}\)

 

\(=1152\frac{e^{-4 t}}{-4}2^{\infty}_{0}=288 J\).

 

This result is the difference between the initially stored energy (320 J) and the energy trapped in the parallel inductors (32 J). The equivalent inductor for the parallel inductors (which predicts the terminal behavior of the parallel combination) has an initial energy of 288 J; that is, the energy stored in the equivalent inductor represents the amount of energy that will be delivered to the resistive network at the terminals of the original inductors.
