Guest Question.

Compute the 3 × 3 per-unit zero-, positive-, and negative-sequence bus impedance matrices for the power system given in (a). Use a base of 1000 MVA and 500 kV in the zone of line 1–2.

Admin
Solution

Zero sequence bus impedance matrix:

Step (1): Add \(\bar{Z}_{b}=j 0.10\) from the reference to bus 1 (type 1) \(\bar{Z}_{b u s-0}=j 0.10\) per unit

Step (2): Add \(\bar{Z}_{6}=j 0.60\) from bus 1 to bus 2 (type 2)

\(\bar{Z}_{b u s-0}=j\left[\begin{array}{ll}0.10 & 0.10 \\0.10 & 0.70\end{array}\right] \text { per unit }\)

Step (3): Add \(\bar{Z}_{b}=j 0.10\) from the reference to bus 2 (type 3)

\(\underline{\bar{Z}}_{\text {bus }-0}=j\left[\begin{array}{ll}0.10 & 0.10 \\0.10 & 0.70\end{array}\right]-\frac{j}{0.80}\left[\begin{array}{l}0.10 \\0.70\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{ll}0.10 & 0.70\end{array}\right]=j\left[\begin{array}{ll}0.0875 & 0.0125 \\0.0125 & 0.0875\end{array}\right]\)

Step (4): Add \(\bar{Z}_{b}=j 0.40\) from bus 2 to bus 3 (type 2)

\(\underline{\bar{Z}}_{b u s-0}=j\left[\begin{array}{lll}0.0875 & 0.0125 & 0.0125 \\0.0125 & 0.0875 & 0.0875 \\0.0125 & 0.0875 & 0.4875\end{array}\right] \text { per unit }\)

Step (5): Add \(\bar{Z}_{b}=j 0.40\) from bus 1 to bus 3 (type 4)

\(\underline{\bar{Z}}_{b u s-0}=j\left[\begin{array}{lll}0.0875 & 0.0125 & 0.0125 \\0.0125 & 0.0875 & 0.0875 \\0.0125 & 0.0875 & 0.4875\end{array}\right]-\frac{ j }{0.95}\left[\begin{array}{c}0.075 \\-0.075 \\-0.475\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{lll}0.075 & -0.075 & -0.475\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(=j\left[\begin{array}{lll}0.08158 & 0.01842 & 0.050 \\0.01842 & 0.08158 & 0.050 \\0.050 & 0.050 & 0.250\end{array}\right] \text { per unit }\)

 

Positive sequence Bus Impedance Matrix:

Step (1): Add \(\bar{Z}_{b}=j 0.25\) from the reference to bus 1 (type 1)

\(\bar{Z}_{b u s-1}=j 0.25 \text { per unit }\)

Step (2): Add \(\bar{Z}_{b}=j 0.20\) from bus 1 to bus 2 (type 2)

\(\underline{\bar{Z}}_{b u s-1}=j\left[\begin{array}{ll}0.25 & 0.25 \\0.25 & 0.45\end{array}\right] \text { per unit }\)

Step (3): Add \(\bar{Z}_{b}=j 0.35\) from the reference to bus 2 (types)

\(\bar{Z}_{b u s-1}=j\left[\begin{array}{ll}0.25 & 0.25 \\0.25 & 0.45\end{array}\right]-\frac{j}{0.8}\left[\begin{array}{l}0.25 \\0.45\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{ll}0.25 & 0.45\end{array}\right]=j\left[\begin{array}{ll}0.1719 & 0.1094 \\0.1094 & 0.1969\end{array}\right]\)

Step (4): Add \(\bar{Z}_{b}=j 0.16\) from bus 2 to bus 3 (type 2)

\(\underline{\bar{Z}}_{b u s-1}=j\left[\begin{array}{lll}0.1719 & 0.1094 & 0.1094 \\0.1094 & 0.1969 & 0.1969 \\0.1094 & 0.1969 & 0.3569\end{array}\right] \text { per unit }\)

Step (5): Add \(\bar{Z}_{b}=j 0.16\) from bus 1 to bus 3 (type 4)

\(\bar{Z}_{b u s-1}=j\left[\begin{array}{lll}0.1719 & 0.1094 & 0.1094 \\0.1094 & 0.1969 & 0.1969 \\0.1094 & 0.1969 & 0.3569\end{array}\right] \frac{-j}{0.47}\left[\begin{array}{c}0.0625 \\-0.0875 \\-0.2475\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{lll}0.0625 & -0.0875 & -0.2475]\end{array}\right.\)

 

\(=j\left[\begin{array}{lll}0.1636 & 0.1210 & 0.1423 \\0.1210 & 0.1806 & 0.1508 \\0.1423 & 0.1508 & 0.2266\end{array}\right] \text { per unit }\)

 

Step (6): Add \(\bar{Z}_{b}=j(0.5786 \| 0.4853)=j 0.2639\) from the reference to bus 3 (type 3)

\(\bar{Z}_{\text {bus – } 1}=j\left[\begin{array}{ccc}0.1636 & 0.1210 & 0.1423 \\0.1210 & 0.1806 & 0.1508 \\0.1423 & 0.1508 & 0.2266\end{array}\right]-\frac{j}{0.4905}\left[\begin{array}{l}0.1423 \\0.1508 \\0.2266\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{lll}0.1423 & 0.1508 & 0.2266\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(=j\left[\begin{array}{lll}0.1223 & 0.0773 & 0.0766 \\0.0773 & 0.1342 & 0.0811 \\0.0766 & 0.0811 & 0.1219\end{array}\right] \text { per unit }\)

 

Negative Sequence Bus Impedance Matrix: Steps (1)–(5) are the same as for \(\bar{Z}_{b u s-1}\) .

Step (6): Add \(\bar{Z}_{b}=j(0.5786 \| .5981)=j 0.2941\) from the reference to bus 3 (types 3).

\(\bar{Z}_{b u s-1}=j\left[\begin{array}{ccc}0.1636 & 0.1210 & 0.1423 \\0.1210 & 0.1806 & 0.1508 \\0.1423 & 0.1508 & 0.2266\end{array}\right]-\frac{j}{0.5207}\left[\begin{array}{l}0.1423 \\0.1508 \\0.2266\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{lll}0.1423 & 0.1508 & 0.2266]\end{array}\right.\)

 

\(=j\left[\begin{array}{lll}0.1247 & 0.0798 & 0.0804 \\0.0798 & 0.1369 & 0.0852 \\0.0804 & 0.0852 & 0.1280\end{array}\right]\)
