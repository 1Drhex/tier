Guest Question.

For the beam and loading shown, use discontinuity functions to compute (a) the slope of the beam at B and (b) the deflection of the beam at C. Assume a constant value of EI = 34×\(10^{6} lb - ft ^{2}\) for the beam.

Admin
Solution

Support reactions: A FBD of the beam is shown to the right.

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{B}=&-\frac{1}{2}(7,000 lb / ft )(9 ft )(3 ft )+D_{y}(14 ft )=0 \\& \therefore D_{y}=6,750 lbs \\\Sigma F_{y}=B &_{y}+D_{y}-\frac{1}{2}(7,000 lb / ft )(9 ft )=0 \\& \therefore B_{y}=24,750 lbs\end{aligned}\)

Load function w(x):

\(\begin{aligned}w(x)=& 24,750 lbs \langle x-4 ft \rangle^{-1}-7,000 lb / ft \langle x-4 ft \rangle^{0}+\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{9 ft }\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{1} \\&-\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{9 ft }\langle x-13 ft \rangle^{1}+6,750 lbs \langle x-18 ft \rangle^{-1}\end{aligned}\)

Shear-force function V(x) and bending-moment function M(x):

\(\begin{aligned}V(x)=& 24,750 lbs \langle x-4 ft \rangle^{0}-7,000 lb / ft \langle x-4 ft \rangle^{1}+\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{2(9 ft )}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{2} \\&-\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{2(9 ft )}\langle x-13 ft \rangle^{2}+6,750 lbs \langle x-18 ft \rangle^{0} \\M(x)=& 24,750 lbs \langle x-4 ft \rangle^{1}-\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{2}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{2}+\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{6(9 ft )}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{3} \\&-\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{6(9 ft )}\langle x-13 ft \rangle^{3}+6,750 lbs \langle x-18 ft \rangle^{1}\end{aligned}\)

Equations for beam slope and beam deflection:
From Eq. (10.1), we can write:

\(\begin{gathered}E I \frac{d^{2} v}{d x^{2}}=M(x)=24,750 lbs \langle x-4 ft \rangle^{1}-\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{2}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{2}+\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{6(9 ft )}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{3} \\-\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{6(9 ft )}\langle x-13 ft \rangle^{3}+6,750 \operatorname{lbs}\langle x-18 ft \rangle^{1}\end{gathered}\)

Integrate the moment function to obtain an expression for the beam slope:

\(\begin{aligned}E I \frac{d v}{d x}=& \frac{24,750 lbs }{2}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{2}-\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{6}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{3}+\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{24(9 ft )}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{4} \\&-\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{24(9 ft )}\langle x-13 ft \rangle^{4}+\frac{6,750 lbs }{2}\langle x-18 ft \rangle^{2}+C_{1} (a)\end{aligned}\)

Integrate again to obtain the beam deflection function:

\(\begin{aligned}E I v=& \frac{24,750 lbs }{6}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{3}-\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{24}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{4}+\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{120(9 ft )}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{5} \\&-\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{120(9 ft )}\langle x-13 ft \rangle^{5}+\frac{6,750 lbs }{6}\langle x-18 ft \rangle^{3}+C_{1} x+C_{2} (b)\end{aligned}\)

Evaluate constants using boundary conditions: Boundary conditions are specific values of deflection v or slope dv/dx that are known at particular locations along the beam span. For this beam, the deflection v is known at the pin support (x = 4 ft) and at the roller support (x = 18 ft). Substitute the boundary condition v = 0 at x = 4 ft into Eq. (b) to obtain:

\(C_{1}(4 ft )+C_{2}=0\) (c)

Next, substitute the boundary condition v = 0 at x = 18 ft into Eq. (b) to obtain:

\(\begin{aligned}&0=\frac{24,750 lbs }{6}(14 ft )^{3}-\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{24}(14 ft )^{4}+\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{120(9 ft )}(14 ft )^{5}-\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{120(9 ft )}(5 ft )^{5} \\&\quad+C_{1}(18 ft )+C_{2} \\&\therefore C_{1}(18 ft )+C_{2}=3,579,975 lb – ft ^{3} (d)\end{aligned}\)

Solve Eqs. (c) and (d) simultaneously for the two constants of integration \(C_{1} \text { and } C_{2} :\)

\(C_{1}=-255,712.5 lb – ft ^{2} \quad \text { and } \quad C_{2}=1,022,850 lb – ft ^{3}\)

The beam slope and elastic curve equations are now complete:

\(\begin{aligned}E I \frac{d v}{d x}=& \frac{24,750 lbs }{2}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{2}-\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{6}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{3}+\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{24(9 ft )}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{4} \\&-\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{24(9 ft )}\langle x-13 ft \rangle^{4}+\frac{6,750 lbs }{2}\langle x-18 ft \rangle^{2}-255,712.5 lb – ft ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}E I v=& \frac{24,750 lbs }{6}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{3}-\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{24}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{4}+\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{120(9 ft )}\langle x-4 ft \rangle^{5} \\&-\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{120(9 ft )}\langle x-13 ft \rangle^{5}+\frac{6,750 lbs }{6}\langle x-18 ft \rangle^{3}-\left(255,712.5 lb – ft ^{2}\right) x+1,022,850 lb – ft ^{3}\end{aligned}\)

 

(a) Beam slope at B: The beam slope at B is:

\(\begin{aligned}\left.E I \frac{d v}{d x}\right|_{B} &=-255,712.5 lb – ft ^{2} \\&\left.\therefore \frac{d v}{d x}\right|_{B}=-\frac{255,712.5 lb – ft ^{2}}{34 \times 10^{6} lb – ft ^{2}}=-0.0075210 rad =-0.00752 rad\end{aligned}\)

(b) Beam deflection at C: At C where x = 13 ft, the beam deflection is:

\(\begin{gathered}E I v_{C}=\frac{24,750 lbs }{6}(9 ft )^{3}-\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{24}(9 ft )^{4}+\frac{7,000 lb / ft }{120(9 ft )}(9 ft )^{5} \\-\left(255,712.5 lb – ft ^{2}\right)(13 ft )+1,022,850 lb – ft ^{3} \\\therefore v_{C}=-\frac{825,187.5 kip – ft ^{3}}{34 \times 10^{6} lb – ft ^{2}}=-0.0242702 ft =0.291 in . \downarrow\end{gathered}\)
