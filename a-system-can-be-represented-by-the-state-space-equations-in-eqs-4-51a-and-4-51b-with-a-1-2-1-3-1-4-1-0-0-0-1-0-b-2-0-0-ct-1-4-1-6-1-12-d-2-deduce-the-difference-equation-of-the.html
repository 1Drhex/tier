Guest Question.

A system can be represented by the state-space equations in Eqs. (4.51a) and (4.51b) with

\(q (n T+T)= A q (n T)+ b x(n T)\) (4.51a)

\(y(n T)= c ^{T} q (n T)+d x(n T)\) (4.51b)

\(A =\left[\begin{array}{ccc}-\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{1}{3} & -\frac{1}{4} \\1 & 0 & 0 \\0 & 1 & 0\end{array}\right] \quad b =\left[\begin{array}{l}2 \\0 \\0\end{array}\right] \quad c ^{T}=\left[-\frac{1}{4} \frac{1}{6} \frac{1}{12}\right] \quad d=2\)

Deduce the difference equation of the system.

Admin
Solution

From Eqs. (4.51a) and (4.51b), the state-space equations can be written out as

 

\(q_{1}(n T+T)=-\frac{1}{2} q_{1}(n T)-\frac{1}{3} q_{2}(n T)-\frac{1}{4} q_{3}(n T)+2 x(n T)\) (4.54a)

 

\(q_{2}(n T+T)=q_{1}(n T)\) (4.54b)

 

\(q_{3}(n T+T)=q_{2}(n T)\) (4.54c)

 

and

 

\(y(n T)=-\frac{1}{4} q_{1}(n T)+\frac{1}{6} q_{2}(n T)+\frac{1}{12} q_{3}(n T)+2 x(n T)\) (4.55)

 

From Eqs. (4.54b) and (4.54c), we can write

 

\(E q_{2}(n T)=q_{1}(n T) \quad \text { or } \quad q_{2}(n T)= E ^{-1} q_{1}(n T)\) (4.56a)

 

\(E q_{3}(n T)=q_{2}(n T) \quad \text { or } \quad q_{3}(n T)= E ^{-1} q_{2}(n T)\)

 

\(= E ^{-2} q_{1}(n T)\) (4.56b)

 

Now if we eliminate \(q_{2}(n T) \text { and } q_{3}(n T)\) in Eq. (4.54a), we get

 

\(E q_{1}(n T)=-\frac{1}{2} q_{1}(n T)-\frac{1}{3} E ^{-1} q_{1}(n T)-\frac{1}{4} E ^{-2} q_{1}(n T)+2 x(n T)\)

 

and hence

 

\(\left[ E +\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{3} E ^{-1}+\frac{1}{4} E ^{-2}\right] q_{1}(n T)=2 x(n T)\)

 

or

 

\(q_{1}(n T)=\frac{2}{ E +\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{3} E ^{-1}+\frac{1}{4} E -2} x(n T)\) (4.57)

 

Similarly, if we eliminate \(q_{2}(n T) \text { and } q_{3}(n T)\) in Eq. (4.55), we get

 

\(y(n T)=-\frac{1}{4} q_{1}(n T)+\frac{1}{6} E ^{-1} q_{1}(n T)+\frac{1}{12} E ^{-2} q_{1}(n T)+2 x(n T)\)

 

\(=\left[-\frac{1}{4}+\frac{1}{6} E ^{-1}+\frac{1}{12} E ^{-2}\right] q_{1}(n T)+2 x(n T)\) (4.58)

 

If we now eliminate \(q_{1}(n T)\) in Eq. (4.58) using Eq. (4.57), we have

 

\(y(n T)=\frac{2\left[-\frac{1}{4}+\frac{1}{6} E ^{-1}+\frac{1}{12} E ^{-2}\right]}{ E +\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{3} E ^{-1}+\frac{1}{4} E ^{-2}} x(n T)+2 x(n T)\)

 

\(=\frac{\left[2+\frac{1}{2} E ^{-1}+ E ^{-2}+\frac{2}{3} E ^{-3}\right]}{1+\frac{1}{2} E ^{-1}+\frac{1}{3} E ^{-2}+\frac{1}{4} E ^{-3}} x(n T)\)

 

Alternatively, we can write

 

\(\left[1+\frac{1}{2} E ^{-1}+\frac{1}{3} E ^{-2}+\frac{1}{4} E ^{-3}\right] y(n T)=\left[2+\frac{1}{2} E ^{-1}+ E ^{-2}+\frac{2}{3} E ^{-3}\right] x(n T)\)

 

or

 

\(y(n T)=2 x(n T)+\frac{1}{2} x(n T-T)+x(n T-2 T)+\frac{2}{3} x(n T-3 T)\)

 

\(-\frac{1}{2} y(n T-T)-\frac{1}{3} y(n T-2 T)-\frac{1}{4} y(n T-3 T)\)
