Guest Question.

The oscillator circuit shown in \(Fig. 64.5\) uses a \(UJT\) with \( R_{BB } = 10 K\) , \(\eta = 0.6\) , \(V_{B } = 0.7 V\) , \(V = 50 V\) , \(R_{1} = 90 K\) , \(R = 100 K\) and \(C = 0.05 \mu F\) . When \(UJT\) is in conduction, \(R_{B1} = 10 \Omega \) and \( V_{ V } = V_{B}\) . Find \((i)\) ramp rise time, \( t_{s} \) \((ii)\) approximate discharge time, \(t_{d}\) and \((iii)\) frequency of oscillation.

Admin
Solution
\(V_{BB }= V.R_{BB }/(R_{BB }+ R_{1 })=50 \times 10/100 = 5 V\)

 

\( V_{P } = \eta V_{BB } + V_{B} = 0.6 \times 5 + 0.7 = 3.7 V\)

 

\(T = CR = 0.05 \times 10^{-6} × 100 × 10^{3} = 5 ms\)

 

\((i)\) The capacitor charges from \(V_{c} = V_{B} = 0.7 V\) to \(V_{P}\) towards \(V\) in the time \(t_{s}\) given by

\(t_{s}=T\log _{e}\left(V-V_{V} \right)/\left(V-V_{P} \right) =5\log _{e}\left(50-0.7\right)/\left(50-3.7\right)=0.315 ms \) .

\((ii)\) The discharge time, \( t_{d} =C R_{B1} = 0.05 \times 10 = 0.5 \mu s\) . The time taken by \(C\) to discharge from \(V_{P}\) to \(V_{v} (= 0.7 V) \) towards \(0\) volt is

\(T_{d} \cong t_{d}\log _{e}V_{P}/V_{V}=0.5\log_{e} 3.7/0.7=1.66\mu s \)

 

\((iii)\) \(f = 1/(t_{s} + t_{d}) = 1/(0.315 + 0.0016) = 3.158 kHz\) .
