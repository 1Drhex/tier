Guest Question.

Two flows of air both at 200 kPa; one has 2 kg/s at 400 K and the other has 1 kg/s at 290 K. The two flows are mixed together in an insulated box to produce a single exit flow at 200 kPa. Find the exit temperature and the total rate of entropy generation.

Admin
Solution

Continuity Eq.4.9: \(\dot{ m }_{1}+\dot{ m }_{2}=\dot{ m }_{3}=2+1=3 kg / s\)

Energy Eq.4.10: \(\dot{ m }_{1} h _{1}+\dot{ m }_{2} h _{2}=\dot{ m }_{3} h _{3}\)

Entropy Eq.7.7: \(\dot{ m }_{1} s _{1}+\dot{ m }_{2} s _{2}+\dot{ S }_{ gen }=\dot{ m }_{3} s _{3}\)

Using constant specific heats from A.5 and Eq.6.16 for s change.

Divide the energy equation with \(\dot{ m }_{3} C _{ Po }\)

\(T _{3}=\left(\dot{ m }_{1} / \dot{ m }_{3}\right) T _{1}+\left(\dot{ m }_{2} / \dot{ m }_{3}\right) T _{2}=\frac{2}{3} \times 400+\frac{1}{3} \times 290= 3 6 3 . 3 3 K\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ S }_{ gen } &=\dot{ m }_{1}\left( s _{3}- s _{1}\right)+\dot{ m }_{2}\left( s _{3}- s _{2}\right) \\&=\left[1 \times 1.004 \ln \left(\frac{363.33}{400}\right)+2 \times 1.004 \ln \left(\frac{363.33}{290}\right)\right] kg / s \times kJ / kg – K \\&= 0 . 3 5 6 kW / K\end{aligned}\)

 

Using A.7.1 and Eq.6.19 for change in s.

\(h _{3}=\left(\dot{ m }_{1} / \dot{ m }_{3}\right) h _{1}+\left(\dot{ m }_{2} / \dot{ m }_{3}\right) h _{2}=\frac{2}{3} \times 401.3+\frac{1}{3} \times 290.43=364.34 kJ / kg\)

 

From A.7.1: \(T _{3}= 3 6 4 . 4 4 K \quad s _{ T _{3}}=7.06216 kJ / kg K\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ S }_{\text {gen }}=&[2(7.06216-7.15926)+1(7.06216-6.83521)] kg / s \times kJ / kgK \\&= 0 . 0 3 2 7 5 k W / K\end{aligned}\)

 

The pressure correction part of the entropy terms cancel out as all three states have the same pressure.
