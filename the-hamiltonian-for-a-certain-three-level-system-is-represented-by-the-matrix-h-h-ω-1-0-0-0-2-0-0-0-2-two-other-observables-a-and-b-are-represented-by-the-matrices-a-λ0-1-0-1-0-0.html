Guest Question.

The Hamiltonian for a certain three-level system is represented by the matrix

\( H=\hbar \omega\left(\begin{array}{lll} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 \end{array}\right) \) .

Two other observables, A and B, are represented by the matrices

\( A =\lambda\left(\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 \end{array}\right), \quad B =\mu\left(\begin{array}{lll} 2 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right) \),

where ω, , and μ are positive real numbers.

(a) Find the eigenvalues and (normalized) eigenvectors of H,A, and B.

(b) Suppose the system starts out in the generic state

\( | S (0)\rangle=\left(\begin{array}{l} c_{1} \\ c_{2} \\ c_{3} \end{array}\right) \),

with \( \left|c_{1}\right|^{2}+\left|c_{2}\right|^{2}+\left|c_{3}\right|^{2}=1 \). Find the expectation values (at t=0) of H, A, and B.

(c) What is |S(t)〉? If you measured the energy of this state (at time t), what values might you get, and what is the probability of each? Answer the same questions for observables A and for B.

Admin
Solution

(a) H:

\( E_{1}=\hbar \omega, E_{2}=E_{3}=2 \hbar \omega ; \quad\left|h_{1}\right\rangle=\left(\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right),\left|h_{2}\right\rangle=\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right),\left|h_{3}\right\rangle=\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) \).

A:

\( \left|\begin{array}{ccc} -a & \lambda & 0 \\ \lambda & -a & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & (2 \lambda-a) \end{array}\right|=a^{2}(2 \lambda-a)-(2 \lambda-a) \lambda^{2}=0 \Rightarrow a_{1}=2 \lambda, a_{2}=\lambda, a_{3}=-\lambda \).

\( \lambda\left(\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} \alpha \\ \beta \\ \gamma \end{array}\right)=a\left(\begin{array}{l} \alpha \\ \beta \\ \gamma \end{array}\right) \Rightarrow\left\{\begin{array}{r} \lambda \beta=a \alpha \\ \lambda \alpha=a \beta \\ 2 \lambda \gamma=a \gamma \end{array}\right. \).

(1)

\( \left.\begin{array}{rl} \lambda \beta & =2 \lambda \alpha \Rightarrow \beta=2 \alpha \\ \lambda \alpha & =2 \lambda \beta \\ 2 \lambda \gamma & =2 \lambda \gamma \end{array}\right\} \quad \alpha=\beta=0 \); \( \left|a_{1}\right\rangle=\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) \).

(2)

\( \left.\begin{array}{r} \lambda \beta=\lambda \alpha \Rightarrow \beta=\alpha \\ \lambda \alpha=\lambda \beta \Rightarrow \alpha=\beta \\ 2 \lambda \gamma=\lambda \gamma ; \Rightarrow \gamma=0 \end{array}\right\} \) \( \left|a_{2}\right\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right) \).

(3)

\( \left.\begin{array}{r} \lambda \beta=-\lambda \alpha \Rightarrow \beta=-\alpha \\ \lambda \alpha=-\lambda \beta \Rightarrow \alpha=-\beta \\ 2 \lambda \gamma=-\lambda \gamma ; \Rightarrow \gamma=0 \end{array}\right\} \) \( \left|a_{3}\right\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right) \).

B:

\( \left|\begin{array}{ccc} (2 \mu-b) & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -b & \mu \\ 0 & \mu & -b \end{array}\right|=b^{2}(2 \mu-b)-(2 \mu-b) \mu^{2}=0 \Rightarrow b_{1}=2 \mu, b_{2}=\mu, b_{3}=-\mu \).

\( \mu\left(\begin{array}{lll} 2 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} \alpha \\ \beta \\ \gamma \end{array}\right)=b\left(\begin{array}{l} \alpha \\ \beta \\ \gamma \end{array}\right) \Rightarrow\left\{\begin{array}{c} 2 \mu \alpha=b \alpha \\ \mu \gamma=b \beta \\ \mu \beta=b \gamma \end{array}\right. \).

(1)

\( \left.\begin{array}{rl} 2 \mu \alpha & =2 \mu \alpha \\ \mu \gamma & =2 \mu \beta \Rightarrow \gamma=2 \beta \\ \mu \beta & =2 \mu \gamma \Rightarrow \beta=2 \gamma \end{array}\right\} \quad \beta=\gamma=0 \); \( \left|b_{1}\right\rangle=\left(\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right) \).

(2)

\( \left.\begin{array}{c} 2 \mu \alpha=\mu \alpha \Rightarrow \alpha=0 \\ \mu \gamma=\mu \beta \Rightarrow \gamma=\beta \\ \mu \beta=\mu \gamma ; \Rightarrow \beta=\gamma \end{array}\right\} \) \( \left|b_{2}\right\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right) \).

(3)

\( \left.\begin{array}{c} 2 \mu \alpha=-\mu \alpha \Rightarrow \alpha=0 \\ \mu \gamma=-\mu \beta \Rightarrow \gamma=-\beta \\ \mu \beta=-\mu \gamma ; \Rightarrow \beta=-\gamma \end{array}\right\} \) \( \left|b_{3}\right\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right) \).

(b)

\( \langle H\rangle=\langle S (0)|H| S (0)\rangle=\hbar \omega\left(\begin{array}{ccc} c_{1}^{*} & c_{2}^{*} c_{3}^{*} \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{lll} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} c_{1} \\ c_{2} \\ c_{3} \end{array}\right)=\hbar \omega\left(\left|c_{1}\right|^{2}+2\left|c_{2}\right|^{2}+2\left|c_{3}\right|^{2}\right) \).

\( \langle A\rangle=\langle S (0)|A| S (0)\rangle=\lambda\left(\begin{array}{lll} c_{1}^{*} & c_{2}^{*} & c_{3}^{*} \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} c_{1} \\ c_{2} \\ c_{3} \end{array}\right) =\lambda\left(c_{1}^{*} c_{2}+c_{2}^{*} c_{1}+2\left|c_{3}\right|^{2}\right) \).

\( \langle B\rangle=\langle S (0)|B| S (0)\rangle=\mu\left(\begin{array}{lll} c_{1}^{*} & c_{2}^{*} & c_{3}^{*} \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{lll} 2 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} c_{1} \\ c_{2} \\ c_{3} \end{array}\right)= \mu\left(2\left|c_{1}\right|^{2}+c_{2}^{*} c_{3}+c_{3}^{*} c_{2}\right) \).

(c)

\( | S (0)\rangle=c_{1}\left|h_{1}\right\rangle+c_{2}\left|h_{2}\right\rangle+c_{3}\left|h_{3}\right\rangle \quad \Rightarrow \)

\( | S (t)\rangle=c_{1} e^{-i E_{1} t / \hbar}\left|h_{1}\right\rangle+c_{2} e^{-i E_{2} t / \hbar}\left|h_{2}\right\rangle+c_{3} e^{-i E_{3} t / \hbar}\left|h_{3}\right\rangle=c_{1} e^{-i \omega t}\left|h_{1}\right\rangle+c_{2} e^{-2 i \omega t}\left|h_{2}\right\rangle+c_{3} e^{-2 i \omega t}\left|h_{3}\right\rangle \).

\( =e^{-2 i \omega t}\left[c_{1} e^{i \omega t}\left(\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right)+c_{2}\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right)+c_{3}\left(\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right)\right]= e^{-2 i \omega t}\left(\begin{array}{c} c_{1} e^{i \omega t} \\ c_{2} \\ c_{3} \end{array}\right) \).

H: \( h_{1}=\hbar \omega, \text { probability }\left|c_{1}\right|^{2} ; \quad h_{2}=h_{3}=2 \hbar \omega, \text { probability }\left(\left|c_{2}\right|^{2}+\left|c_{3}\right|^{2}\right) \).

A: \( a_{1}=2 \lambda \), \( \left\langle a_{1} \mid S (t)\right\rangle=e^{-2 i \omega t}\left(\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{c} c_{1} e^{i \omega t} \\ c_{2} \\ c_{3} \end{array}\right)=e^{-2 i \omega t} c_{3} \Rightarrow \text { probability }\left|c_{3}\right|^{2} \).

\( a_{2}=\lambda \), \( \left\langle a_{2} \mid S (t)\right\rangle=e^{-2 i \omega t} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{lll} 1 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{c} c_{1} e^{i \omega t} \\ c_{2} \\ c_{3} \end{array}\right)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} e^{-2 i \omega t}\left(c_{1} e^{i \omega t}+c_{2}\right) \Rightarrow \)

\( \text { probability }=\frac{1}{2}\left(c_{1}^{*} e^{-i \omega t}+c_{2}^{*}\right)\left(c_{1} e^{i \omega t}+c_{2}\right)= \frac{1}{2}\left(\left|c_{1}\right|^{2}+\left|c_{2}\right|^{2}+c_{1}^{*} c_{2} e^{-i \omega t}+c_{2}^{*} c_{1} e^{i \omega t}\right) \).

\( a_{3}=-\lambda \), \( \left\langle a_{3} \mid S (t)\right\rangle=e^{-2 i \omega t} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{ll} 1 & -1 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{c} c_{1} e^{i \omega t} \\ c_{2} \\ c_{3} \end{array}\right)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} e^{-2 i \omega t}\left(c_{1} e^{i \omega t}-c_{2}\right) \Rightarrow \)

\( \text { probability }=\frac{1}{2}\left(c_{1}^{*} e^{-i \omega t}-c_{2}^{*}\right)\left(c_{1} e^{i \omega t}-c_{2}\right)=\frac{1}{2}\left(\left|c_{1}\right|^{2}+\left|c_{2}\right|^{2}-c_{1}^{*} c_{2} e^{-i \omega t}-c_{2}^{*} c_{1} e^{i \omega t}\right) \).

Note that the sum of the probabilities is 1.

B: \( b_{1}=2 \mu \), \( \left\langle b_{1} \mid S (t)\right\rangle=e^{-2 i \omega t}\left(\begin{array}{lll} 1 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{c} c_{1} e^{i \omega t} \\ c_{2} \\ c_{3} \end{array}\right)=e^{-i \omega t} c_{1} \Rightarrow \text { probability }\left|c_{1}\right|^{2} \).

\( b_{2}=\mu \), \( \left\langle b_{2} \mid S (t)\right\rangle=e^{-2 i \omega t} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 1 & 1 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{c} c_{1} e^{i \omega t} \\ c_{2} \\ c_{3} \end{array}\right)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} e^{-2 i \omega t}\left(c_{2}+c_{3}\right) \Rightarrow \)

\( \text { probability }=\frac{1}{2}\left(c_{2}^{*}+c_{3}^{*}\right)\left(c_{2}+c_{3}\right)= \frac{1}{2}\left(\left|c_{2}\right|^{2}+\left|c_{3}\right|^{2}+c_{2}^{*} c_{3}+c_{3}^{*} c_{2}\right) \).

\( b_{3}=-\mu \), \( \left\langle b_{3} \mid S (t)\right\rangle=e^{-2 i \omega t} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{lll} 0 & 1 & -1 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{c} c_{1} e^{i \omega t} \\ c_{2} \\ c_{3} \end{array}\right)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} e^{-2 i \omega t}\left(c_{2}-c_{3}\right) \Rightarrow \)

\( \text { probability }=\frac{1}{2}\left(c_{2}^{*}-c_{3}^{*}\right)\left(c_{2}-c_{3}\right)= \frac{1}{2}\left(\left|c_{2}\right|^{2}+\left|c_{3}\right|^{2}-c_{2}^{*} c_{3}-c_{3}^{*} c_{2}\right) \).

Again, the sum of the probabilities is 1.
