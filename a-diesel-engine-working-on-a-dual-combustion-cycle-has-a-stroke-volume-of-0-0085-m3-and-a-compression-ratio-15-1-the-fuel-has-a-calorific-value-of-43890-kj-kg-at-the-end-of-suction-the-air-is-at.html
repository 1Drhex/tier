Guest Question.

A Diesel engine working on a dual combustion cycle has a stroke volume of\(0.0085 m ^{3}\) and a compression ratio 15 : 1. The fuel has a calorific value of 43890 kJ/kg. At the end of suction, the air is at 1 bar and 100°C. The maximum pressure in the cycle is 65 bar and air fuel ratio is 21 : 1. Find for ideal cycle the thermal efficiency. Assume \(c_{p}=1.0 \text { and } c_{v}=0.71\).

Admin
Solution

Refer to Fig. 4.24.

 

Initial temperature, \(T_{1}=100+273=373 K\)

 

Initial pressure, \(p_{1}=1 \text { bar }\)

 

Maximum pressure in the cycle, \(p_{3}=p_{4}=65 \text { bar }\)

 

Stroke volume, \(V_{s}=0.0085 m ^{3}\)

 

Air-fuel ratio = 21 : 1

Compression ratio, r = 15 : 1

Calorific value of fuel, C = 43890 kJ/kg

 

\(c_{p}=1.0, c_{v}=0.71\)

 

Thermal efficiency :

 

\(V_{s}=V_{1}-V_{2}=0.0085\)

 

\(\text { and as } \quad r=\frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}}=15, \text { then } V_{1}=15 V_{2}\)

 

\(\therefore \quad 15 V_{2}-V_{2}=0.0085\)

 

or \(14 V_{2}=0.0085\)

 

or \(V_{2}=V_{3}=V_{c}=\frac{0.0085}{14}=0.0006 m ^{3}\)

 

or \(V_{1}=15 V_{2}=15 \times 0.0006=0.009 m ^{3}\)

 

— For adiabatic compression process 1-2,

 

\(p_{1} V_{1}^{\gamma}=p_{2} V_{2}^{\gamma}\)

 

or \(p_{2}=p_{1} \cdot\left(\frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}}\right)^{\gamma}=1 \times(15)^{1.41} \quad\left[\gamma=\frac{c_{p}}{c_{v}}=\frac{1.0}{0.71}=1.41\right]\)

 

\(=45.5 \text { bar }\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\text { Also, }\\&\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}=\left(\frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}}\right)^{\gamma-1}=(r)^{\gamma-1}=(15)^{1.41-1}=3.04\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\therefore \quad T_{2}=T_{1} \times 3.04=373 \times 3.04=1134 K \text { or } 861^{\circ} C\)

 

— For constant volume process 2-3,

 

\(\frac{p_{2}}{T_{2}}=\frac{p_{3}}{T_{3}}\)

 

or \(T_{3}=T_{2} \times \frac{p_{3}}{p_{2}}=1134 \times \frac{65}{45.5}=1620 K \text { or } 1347^{\circ} C\)

 

According to characteristic equation of gas,

 

\(p_{1} V_{1}=m R T_{1}\)

 

\(\therefore \quad m=\frac{p_{1} V_{1}}{R T_{1}}=\frac{1 \times 10^{5} \times 0.009}{287 \times 373}=0.0084 kg \text { (air) }\)

 

Heat added during constant volume process 2-3,

 

\(=m \times c_{v}\left(T_{3}-T_{2}\right)\)

 

= 0.0084 × 0.71 (1620 – 1134)

= 2.898 kJ

Amount of fuel added during the constant volume process 2-3,

 

\(=\frac{2.898}{43890}=0.000066 kg\)

 

Also as air-fuel ratio is 21 : 1.

\(\therefore\) Total amount of fuel added =\(\frac{0.0084}{21}=0.0004 kg\)

 

Quantity of fuel added during the process 3-4,

= 0.0004 – 0.000066 = 0.000334 kg

\(\therefore\) Heat added during the constant pressure operation 3-4

= 0.000334 × 43890 = 14.66 kJ

 

\(\text { But }(0.0084+0.0004) c_{p}\left(T_{4}-T_{3}\right)=14.66\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\text { or }\\&0.0088 \times 1.0\left(T_{4}-1620\right)=14.66\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\therefore \quad T_{4}=\frac{14.66}{0.0088}+1620=3286 K \text { or } 3013^{\circ} C\)

 

Again for operation 3-4,

 

\(\frac{V_{3}}{T_{3}}=\frac{V_{4}}{T_{4}}\)

 

\(V_{4}=\frac{V_{3} T_{4}}{T_{3}}=\frac{0.0006 \times 3286}{1620}=0.001217 m ^{3}\)

 

For adiabatic expansion operation 4-5,

 

\(\frac{T_{4}}{T_{5}}=\left(\frac{V_{5}}{V_{4}}\right)^{\gamma-1}=\left(\frac{0.009}{0.001217}\right)^{1.41-1}=2.27\)

 

\(T_{5}=\frac{T_{4}}{2.27}=\frac{3286}{2.27}=1447.5 K \text { or } 1174.5^{\circ} C\)

 

Heat rejected during constant volume process 5-1,

 

\(=m c_{v}\left(T_{5}-T_{1}\right)\)

 

= (0.00854 + 0.0004) × 0.71 (1447.5 – 373) = 6.713 kJ

Work done = heat supplied – heat rejected

= (2.898 + 14.66) – 6.713 = 10.845 kJ

 

\(\therefore\) Thermal efficiency,

 

\(\eta_{\text {th }}=\frac{\text { work done }}{\text { heat supplied }}=\frac{10.845}{(2.898+14.66)}\)

 

= 0.6176 or 61.76%.
