Guest Question.

A chemostat is a continuous stirred tank bioreactor that can carry out fermentation of a plant cell culture. Its dynamic behavior can be described by the following model:

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{X} &=\mu(S) X-D X \\\dot{S} &=-\mu(S) X / Y_{X / S}+D\left(S_{f}-S\right)\end{aligned}\)

\(X\) and \(S\) are the cell and substrate concentrations, respectively, and \(S_{f}\) is the substrate feed concentration. The dilution rate \(D\) is defined as the feed flow rate divided by the bioreactor volume. \(D\) is the input, while the cell concentration \(X\) and substrate concentration \(S\) are the output variables. Typically, the rate of reaction is referred to as the specific growth rate \(\mu\) and is modeled by a Monod equation,

\(\mu(S)=\frac{\mu_{m} S}{K_{s}+S}\)

Assume \(\mu_{m}=0.20 h ^{-1}, K_{s}=1.0 g / L\), and \(Y_{X / S}=0.5 g / g .\) Use a steady-state operating point of \(\bar{D}=0.1 h ^{-1}, \bar{X}=4.5 g / L\), \(\bar{S}=1.0 g / L\), and \(\bar{S}_{f}=10 g / L\)

Using linearization, derive a transfer function relating the deviation variables for the cell concentration \((X-\bar{X})\) to the dilution ration \((D-\bar{D})\)

Admin
Solution

Assumptions:

\(\begin{aligned}\quad\quad&\text{1. Constant physical properties}\\&\text{2. Perfect mixing}\end{aligned}\)

Dynamic model: Balances on cell mass and substrate concentration

\(\frac{d X}{d t}=\mu(S) X-D X=f_{1}(S, X, D)\) (1)

\(\frac{d S}{d t}=-\mu(S) X / Y_{X / S}+D\left(S_{f}-S\right)=f_{2}\left(S, X, D, S_{f}\right)\) (2)

where:

\(\mu(S) X\) is defined as \(\frac{\mu_{m} S}{K_{s}+S} X\)

D is defined as \(\frac{F}{V}\)

Linearization of (1) about the nominal steady state gives a linearized model of the form:

\(\left.\frac{d X^{\prime}}{d t} \approx \frac{\partial f_{1}}{\partial S}\right|_{s s} S^{\prime}+\left.\frac{\partial f_{1}}{\partial X}\right|_{s s} X^{\prime}+\left.\frac{\partial f_{1}}{\partial D}\right|_{s s} D^{\prime}\)

\(\frac{d X^{\prime}}{d t} \approx\left(\frac{\mu_{m}\left(K_{s}+\bar{S}\right)-\mu_{m} \bar{S}}{\left(K_{s}+\bar{S}\right)^{2}} \bar{X}\right) S^{\prime}+\left(\frac{\mu_{m} \bar{S}}{K_{s}+\bar{S}}-\bar{D}\right) X^{\prime}-\bar{X} D^{\prime}\) (3)

Linearization of (2) about the nominal steady state:

\(\left.\frac{d S^{\prime}}{d t} \approx \frac{\partial f_{2}}{\partial S}\right|_{s s} S^{\prime}+\left.\frac{\partial f_{2}}{\partial X}\right|_{s s} X^{\prime}+\left.\frac{\partial f_{2}}{\partial D}\right|_{s s} D^{\prime}+\left.\frac{\partial f_{2}}{\partial S_{f}}\right|_{s s} S_{f}^{\prime}\)

\(\frac{d S^{\prime}}{d t} \approx\left(-\frac{1}{Y_{X / S}} \frac{\mu_{m}\left(K_{s}+\bar{S}\right)-\mu_{m} \bar{S}}{\left(K_{s}+\bar{S}\right)^{2}} \bar{X}-\bar{D}\right) S^{\prime}+\left(-\frac{1}{Y_{X / S}} \frac{\mu_{m} \bar{S}}{K_{s}+\bar{S}}\right) X^{\prime}+\left(\bar{S}_{f}-\bar{S}\right) D^{\prime}+\bar{D} S_{f}^{\prime}\) (4)

Substituting the numerical values gives:

\(\frac{dX^\prime }{dt} = 0.113S^\prime -2.25D^\prime \\[0.5cm] \frac{dS^\prime }{dt} = -0.326S^\prime -0.2X^\prime -9D^\prime +0.1^\prime _f\)

Taking Laplace transforms, assuming steady state initially:

\(sX^\prime (s)=0.113S^\prime -2.25D^\prime (s) \\[0.5cm] sS^\prime (s)=-0.326S^\prime -0.2X^\prime (s) -9D^\prime (s) +0.1S_f^\prime (s) \)

In order to derive the transfer function between X and D, assume that \(S_f\) is constant at its nominal steady-state value, \(S_f(t)=\overline{S}_f \); thus \(S_f^\prime =0 \). Rearranging gives,

\(X^\prime (s)=\frac{0.113}{s}S^\prime (s) -\frac{2.25}{s} D^\prime (s) \) (5)

\(S^\prime (s)=\left\lgroup\frac{-0.2}{s+0.326}\right\rgroup X^\prime (s) -\left\lgroup\frac{9}{s+0.126}\right\rgroup D^\prime (s) \) (6)

Substitute (6) into (5) and rearrange gives,

\(\frac{X^\prime (s)}{D^\prime (s)} =\frac{-(2.25s+1.7)}{s^2+0.326s+0.0226} \) (7)

Rearrange (7) to a standard form:

\(\frac{X^\prime (s)}{D^\prime (s)} =\frac{K(\tau _as+1)}{\tau ^2s^2+2\tau \zeta s+1} \)

where:

\(K=-77.4 \text{gh/L } \\[0.5cm] \tau _a=0.778 \text{h} \\[0.5cm] \tau =6.65\text{h} \\[0.5cm] \zeta =1.08\)

Note that the step response will be overdamped because \(\zeta \gt 1\).
