Guest Question.

Consider a point at a depth of 5 m in a sandy soil. (Fines \(= 40\%\)). Total density of soil is \(1800 kg/m^{3}.\) The groundwater is at a depth of 2 m. \((\gamma w = 1000 kg/m^{3}).\) Corrected \((N_{1})_{60}\) value is 15. (All the correction parameters \(C_{N}, C_{E}, C_{B}, C_{R}\) are applied, except for the fines content). Peak horizontal acceleration at the ground surface \((a_{\max})\) was found to be 0.15 g for an earthquake of magnitude 8.5. Check to see whether the soil at a depth of 5 m would liquefy under this earthquake load.

Admin
Solution

Step 1: Find the CSR.

\( CSR=0.65(a_{\max }/g)\times (\sigma /\sigma ^{\prime })\times r_{d}\) (1)

\( \sigma =5\times 1800=9000 kg/m^{2}; \sigma ^{\prime }=2\times 1800+3(1800-1000)=6000 kg/m^{2}\)

Since the depth of concern is 5 m (which is less than 9.15 m) use Equation (1.1)

\(r_{d}=1.0-0.00765 Z\) for \(Z \lt 9.15 m; r_{d}=1.0-0.00765\times 5=0.962\)

Hence \(CSR=0.65\times(0.15)\times \frac{9000}{6000}\times 0.962=0.1407\)

Step 2: Provide the correction factor for fines content
For soils with \( 40\%\) fines \(\rightarrow a = 5\) and \(b = 1.2\) (Equations (5.3) and (5.6)).
\((N_{1})_{60 c} = a + b (N_{1})_{60}\)
\((N_{1})_{60 c} = 5 + 1.2 \times 15 = 23\)

\(a = 5.0\) for \(FC \gt 35\%\) (5.3)

 

\(b = 1.2\) for \(FC \gt 35\%\) (5.6)

Step 3: Find the soil resistance to liquefaction

\(CRR_{7.5}=\frac{1}{[34-(N_{1})_{60}]}+\frac{(N_{1})_{60}}{135}+\frac{50}{[10.(N_{1})_{60}+45]^{2}}-\frac{2}{200}.\) (2)

\((N_{1})_{60 c}\) value is found to be 23. (see Step 2) Hence \(CRR_{7.5} = 0.25\) (from Equation 2)
Factor of Safety \((FS)= (CRR_{7.5} /CSR) \times MSF\) (4)

MSF = Magnitude scaling factor.
Obtain MSF from Table 5.1.

MSF = 0.72 for an earthquake of 8.5 magnitude. (Table 5.3)

CSR = 0.1407 (see Step 1)
Factor of safety \((FS) = (0.25/0.1407) \times 0.72 = 1.27\)
The soil would not liquefy.
