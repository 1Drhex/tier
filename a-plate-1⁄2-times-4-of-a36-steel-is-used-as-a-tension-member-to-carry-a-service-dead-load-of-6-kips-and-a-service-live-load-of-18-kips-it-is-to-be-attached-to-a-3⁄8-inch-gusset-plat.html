Guest Question.

A plate 1⁄2\( \times \) 4 of A36 steel is used as a tension member to carry a service dead load of 6 kips and a service live load of 18 kips. It is to be attached to a 3⁄8-inch gusset plate, as shown in Figure 7.43. Design a welded connection.

Admin
Solution

The base metal in this connection is A36 steel, so E70XX electrodes will be used. No restrictions on connection length have been stipulated, so weld length will not be limited, and the smallest permissible size will be used:
Minimum size = \(\frac{3}{16} in. \)(AISC Table J2.4)
Try a 3⁄16-in. fillet weld, using E70XX electrodes. The design strength per inch is
1.392D = 1.392(3) = 4.176 kips/in.
The shear yield strength of the base metal is
\(0.6F_{y}t = 0.6(36) \left ( \frac{3}{8} \right )= 8.1 kips/in.\)
and the shear rupture strength of the base metal is
\(0.45F_{u}t = 0.45(58)\left ( \frac{3}{8} \right )= 9.788 kips/in.\)
The weld strength of 4.176 kips/in. governs. The factored load is
\(P_{u} = 1.2D + 1.6L = 1.2(6) + 1.6(18) = 36 kips\)
and
Required length = \(\frac{36}{4.176} = 8.62 in\).
Minimum length =\( 4w = 4\left ( \frac{3}{16} \right )= 0.75 in. < 8.62 in. (OK)\)
Use two 4.5-in.-long side welds for a total length of 2 \(\times\) 4.5 = 9 in.
Use a 3/16-inch fillet weld with E70XX electrodes, with a total length of 9 inches, as shown in Figure 7.44.

Try a 3⁄16-in. fillet weld, using E70XX electrodes. The allowable strength per inch is
0.9279D = 0.9279(3) = 2.784 kips/in.
The allowable shear yield strength of the base metal is
\(0.4F_{y}t = 0.4(36)\left ( \frac{3}{8} \right ) = 5.4 kips/in.\)
and the allowable shear rupture strength of the base metal is
\(0.3F_{u}t = 0.3(58)\left ( \frac{3}{8} \right ) = 6.525 kips/in.\)
The weld strength of 2.784 kips/in. governs. The load to be resisted is
\(P_a = D + L = 6 + 18 = 24 kips\)
and
Required length = \(\frac{24}{2.784} = 8.62 in.\)
Minimum length = \(4w = 4 \left ( \frac{3}{16} \right )= 0.75 in. < 8.62 in. (OK)\)
Use two 4.5-in.-long side welds for a total length of 2 \(\times\) 4.5 = 9 in. For this type of connection, the length of the side welds must be at least equal to the transverse distance between them, or 4 inches in this case. The provided length of 4.5 inches will therefore be adequate.
Use a 3⁄16-inch fillet weld with E70XX electrodes, with a total length of 9 inches, as shown in Figure 7.44.
