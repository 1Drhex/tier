Guest Question.

A closed insulated container has frictionless and smooth moving insulated partition as shown in Fig. 3.40 such that it equally divides total 1 \(\textbf{m}^{\textbf{3}}\) of volume, when both the gases are at initial pressure of 0.5 MPa and ambient temperature of 27°C. Subsequently the nitrogen is heated using electrical heating element such that volume of \(\textbf{N}_{\textbf{2}}\) becomes 3/4 of total volume of container. Determine, (i) final pressure of hydrogen, (ii) Work done by partition, (iii) Work done by \(\textbf{N}_{\textbf{2}}\) and \(\textbf{H}_{\textbf{2}}\) (iv) Heat added to \(\textbf{N}_{\textbf{2}}\) by electric heater.
Take \(C_{p, N_{2}} = 1.039 kJ/kg. K, C_{p, H_{2}} = 14.307 kJ/kg . K, R_{N_{2}} = 0.2968 kJ/kg . K, R_{H_{2}} = 4.1240 kJ/kg . K\)

Admin
Solution

With the heating of \(N_{2}\) it will get expanded while \(H_{2}\) gets compressed simultaneously. Compression of \(H_{2}\) in insulated chamber may be considered of adiabatic type.
Adiabatic Index of compression for \(H_{2}\) can be obtained as,
\(\begin{aligned}C_{p, H_{2}}&= R_{H_{2}} \left\lgroup\frac{γ _{H_{2}}}{γ_{ H_{2}} − 1} \right\rgroup \\14.307&= 4.124 \left\lgroup\frac{γ_{ H_{2}}}{γ _{H_{2}} − 1} \right\rgroup\\γ_{ H_{2}}&= 1.405\\\text{Adiabatic Index of expansion for N}_{2}, &C_{p, N_{2}} = R_{N_{2}} \left\lgroup\frac{γ_{ N_{2}}}{γ _{N_{2}} − 1} \right\rgroup\\1.039&= 0.2968 \left\lgroup\frac{γ _{N_{2}}}{γ_{N_{2}} − 1}\right\rgroup\\γ_{N_{2}}&=1.399\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned} \text{(i) For hydrogen, } p_{1}V_{1}^{γ} &= p_{2}V_{2}^{γ}\\\text{Here } γ = γ_{ H_{2}}= 1.405, V_{1}& = 0.5 m^{3}\\p_{1} = 0.5 × 10^{6} Pa, V_{2}& = 0.25 m^{3}\\\text{Final pressure of H}_{2}&= 0.5 × 10^{6}\left\lgroup\frac{0.5}{0.25} \right\rgroup^{1.405} \\&= \textbf{1.324 MPa}\end{aligned}\\ \text{(ii) Since partition remains in equilibrium throughout hence no work is done by partition.}\\ \text{ It is a case similar to free expansion.}\\ \textbf{Partition work = 0.}\\ \text{(iii) Work done upon H}_{2},\\ W_{H_{2}} = \frac{P_{1}V_{1} − p_{2} V_{2}}{(γ _{H_{2}} − 1)}\\\text{Here } p_{1} = 0.5 × 10^{6} Pa, p_{2} = 1.324 × 10^{6} Pa, V_{1}= 0.5 m^{3}, V_{2} = 0.25 m^{3}.\\\begin{aligned}\text{Work done by hydrogen, }W_{H_{2}} &= \frac{(−)0.081 × 10^{6}}{0.405}\\&= \textbf{(–) 2 ×}\textbf{10}^{\textbf{5}}\textbf{ J}\end{aligned}\\\text{Work done by N}_{2} = \text{Work done upon H}_{2}\\\text{Work done by nitrogen = }\textbf{+ 2 × }\textbf{10}^{\textbf{5}}\textbf{ J}\\\text{(iv) Heat added to N}_{2}\text{ can be obtained using first law of thermodynamics as}\\ Q_{N_{2}} = ΔU_{N_{2}} + W_{N_{2}} \Longrightarrow Q_{N_{2}} = mc_{v}(T_{2} – T_{1}) + W_{N_{2}}\\\text{Final temperature of N}_{2}\text{ can be obtained considering it as perfect gas.}\\\text{Therefore,} T_{2} = \frac{p_{2}V_{2}T_{1}}{p_{1}V_{1}}\\p_{2} =\text{ Final pressure of N}_{2}\text{ which will be equal to that of H}_{2}\text{ as the partition is free and frictionless.}\\\begin{aligned}p_{2}&= 1.324 × 10^{6} Pa.\\T_{2} = \text{Final temperature of N}_{2}&= \frac{1.324 × 10^{6} × 0.75 × 300}{0.5 × 10^{6} × 0.5}\\&= 1191.6 K\\\text{mass of N}_{2}, m&= \frac{p_{1}V_{1}}{RT_{1}} = \frac{0.5 × 10^{6} × 0.5}{0.2968 × 10^{3} × 300} = 2.8 kg.\\\text{Specific heat at constant volume, C}_{v}&= C_{p} – R \Longrightarrow C_{v, N_{2}} = 0.7422 kJ/kg . K.\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Heat added to N}_{2}, Q_{N_{2}} &= \left\{2.8 × (1191.6 – 300) × 0.7422 × 10^{3}\right\} + 2 × 10^{5}\\&= \textbf{2052.9 kJ}\end{aligned}\)
