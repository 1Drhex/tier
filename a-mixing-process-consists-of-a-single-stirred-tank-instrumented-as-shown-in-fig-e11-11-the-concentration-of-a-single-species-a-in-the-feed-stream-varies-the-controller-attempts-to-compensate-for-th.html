Guest Question.

A mixing process consists of a single stirred-tank instrumented as shown in Fig. E11.11. The concentration of a single species A in the feed stream varies. The controller attempts to compensate for this by varying the flow rate of pure A through the control valve. The transmitter dynamics are negligible.

(a) Draw a block diagram for the controlled process.

(b) Derive a transfer function for each block in the block diagram.

Process

(i) The volume is constant \(\left(5 m ^{3}\right)\).

(ii) The feed flow rate is constant \(\left(\bar{q}_{F}=7 m ^{3} / min \right)\).

(iii) The flow rate of the A stream varies but is small compared to \(\bar{q}_{F}\left(\bar{q}_{A}=0.5 m ^{3} / min \right)\).

(iv) \(\bar{c}_{F}=50 kg / m ^{3}\) and \(\bar{c}_{A}=800 kg / m ^{3}\).

(v) All densities are constant and equal.

Transfer Line

(i) The transfer line is \(20 m\) long and has \(0.5 m\) inside diameter.

(ii) Pump volume can be neglected.

Composition Transmitter Data

\(c\left( kg / m ^{3}\right)\) \( c_{m}( m A )\)

Solution

a)

b) Transfer Line:

Volume of transfer line \(=\pi / 4(0.5 m )^{2}(20 m )=3.93 m ^{3}\)

Nominal flow rate in the line \(=\bar{q}_{A}+\bar{q}_{F}=7.5 m ^{3} / min\)

Time delay in the line \(=\frac{3.93 m ^{3}}{7.5 m ^{3} / min }=0.52 min\)

\(G_{T L}(s)=e^{-0.52 s}\)

Composition Transmitter:

\(G_{m}(s)=K_{m}=\frac{(20-4) ma }{(200-0) kg / m ^{3}}=0.08 \frac{ ma }{ kg / m ^{3}}\)

Controller

From the ideal controller in Eq. 8-14

\(\frac{P^{\prime}(s)}{E(s)}=K_{c}\left[1+\frac{1}{\tau_{I} s}+\tau_{D} s\right]\) (8-14)

\(P^{\prime}(s)=K_{c}\left(1+\frac{1}{\tau_{I} s}\right) E(s)+K_{c} \tau_{D} s\left[\widetilde{C}_{s p}^{\prime}(s)-C_{m}^{\prime}(s)\right]\)

In the above equation, set \(\widetilde{C}_{s p}^{\prime}(s)=0\) in order to get the derivative on the process output only. Then,

\(G_{P I}(s)=K_{c}\left(1+\frac{1}{\tau_{I} s}\right)\)

 

\(G_{D}(s)=-K_{c} \tau_{D} s\)

with \(K_{c}>0\) as the controller should be reverse-acting, since \(P(t)\) should increase when \(C_{m}(t)\) decreases.

I/P transducer

\(K_{I P}=\frac{(15-3) psig }{(20-4) ma }=0.75 \frac{ psig }{ ma }\)

Control valve

\(G_{v}(s)=\frac{K_{v}}{\tau_{v} s+1}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&5 \tau_{v}=1 \quad, \quad \tau_{v}=0.2 min \\&K_{v}=\left.\frac{d q_{A}}{d p_{v}}\right|_{p_{v}=\bar{p}_{v}}=0.03(1 / 12)(\ln 20)(20)^{\frac{\bar{p}_{v}-3}{12}}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&q_{A}=0.5=0.17+0.03(20)^{\frac{\bar{p}_{v}-3}{12}} \\&0.03(20)^{\frac{\bar{p}_{v}-3}{12}}=0.5-0.17=0.33 \\&K_{v}=(1 / 12)(\ln 20)(0.33)=0.082 \frac{ m ^{3} / min }{ psig } \\&G_{v}(s)=\frac{0.082}{0.2 s+1}\end{aligned}\)

Process

Assume \(c_{A}\) is constant for pure A. Material balance for A:

\(V \frac{d c}{d t}=q_{A} \bar{c}_{A}+\bar{q}_{F} c_{F}-\left(q_{A}+\bar{q}_{F}\right) c\) (1)

Linearizing and writing in deviation variable form

\(V \frac{d c^{\prime}}{d t}=\bar{c}_{A} q_{A}^{\prime}+\bar{q}_{F} c_{F}^{\prime}-\left(\bar{q}_{A}+\bar{q}_{F}\right) c^{\prime}-\bar{c} q_{A}^{\prime}\)

Taking Laplace transform

\(\left[V s+\left(\bar{q}_{A}+\bar{q}_{F}\right)\right] C^{\prime}(s)=\left(\bar{c}_{A}-\bar{c}\right) Q_{A}^{\prime}(s)+\bar{q}_{F} C_{F}^{\prime}(s)\) (2)

From Eq. 1 at steady state, \(d c / d t=0\)

\(\bar{c}=\left(\bar{q}_{A} \bar{c}_{A}+\bar{q}_{F} \bar{c}_{F}\right) /\left(\bar{q}_{A}+\bar{q}_{F}\right)=100 kg / m ^{3}\)

Substituting numerical values in Eq. 2 ,

\(\begin{aligned}&{[5 s+7.5] C^{\prime}(s)=700 Q_{A}^{\prime}(s)+7 C_{F}^{\prime}(s)} \\&{[0.67 s+1] C^{\prime}(s)=93.3 Q_{A}^{\prime}(s)+0.93 C_{F}^{\prime}(s)}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&G_{p}(s)=\frac{93.3}{0.67 s+1} \\&G_{d}(s)=\frac{0.93}{0.67 s+1}\end{aligned}\)
