Guest Question.

For the mechanism in the posture shown in Fig. 3.19, gear 3 is rolling without slip on input gear 2. Determine: (a) the first-order kinematic coefficients of links 3, 4, and 5; and (b) the angular velocities of links 3, 4, and 5 if the input gear is rotating with a constant angular velocity of 10 rad/s ccw.

Admin
Solution

The loop-closure equation for the mechanism is

\(\overset{\sqrt{}\sqrt{}}{r_{4}} + \overset{\sqrt{}C}{r_{3}} – \overset{\sqrt{}?}{r_{5}} – \overset{\sqrt{}\sqrt{}}{r_{1}} = 0,\) (1)

where the C refers to the rolling contact constraint that is defined later by Eq. (5a). The two scalar component equations are

r4 cos θ4 +r3 cos θ3 −r5 cos θ5 −r1 cos θ1 = 0, (2a)

r4 sin θ4 +r3 sin θ3 −r5 sin θ5 −r1 sin θ1 = 0. (2b)

Since the input is the angular displacement of gear 2, differentiating Eqs. (2) with respect to θ2 gives

\(-r_{4} \sin \theta_{4} \theta_{4}^{\prime}-r_{3} \sin \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}+r_{5} \sin \theta_{5} \theta_{5}^{\prime}=0\) (3a)

 

\(r_{4} \cos \theta_{4} \theta_{4}^{\prime}+r_{3} \cos \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}-r_{5} \cos \theta_{5} \theta_{5}^{\prime}=0\) (3b)

where

 

\(\theta_{3}^{\prime}=\frac{d \theta_{3}}{d \theta_{2}}, \quad \theta_{4}^{\prime}=\frac{d \theta_{4}}{d \theta_{2}}, \quad \text { and } \quad \theta_{5}^{\prime}=\frac{d \theta_{5}}{d \theta_{2}}\) (4)

are the first-order kinematic coefficients of links 3, 4, and 5. Note that the variables θ2 , θ3 , and θ4 are not independent, since gear 3 is constrained to roll on gear 2. The rolling contact condition can be written to ensure that the arc lengths passed during a small movement are equal on the two surfaces. This rolling contact (no-slip) condition can be written as shown in Sec. 2.18:

ρ2 (Δθ2 − Δθ4) = −ρ3 (Δθ3 − Δθ4), (5a)

where Δθ2 , Δθ3 , and Δθ4 are the angular displacements of links 2, 3, and 4 from the posture shown in Fig. 3.19. The negative sign for the right-hand term arises from the reversal in the direction of the angular displacement differences.

Equation (5a) can be divided by a change in the input angular displacement, Δθ2 , and the limit taken for small increments. This yields the equivalent constraint equation written in terms of first-order kinematic coefficients; that is,

\(\rho_{2}\left(\theta_{2}^{\prime}-\theta_{4}^{\prime}\right)=-\rho_{3}\left(\theta_{3}^{\prime}-\theta_{4}^{\prime}\right)\) (5b)

Since the input is the angular displacement of gear 2, then, by definition, \(\theta_{2}^{\prime}=1\), and by substituting ρ2 = 2ρ3 , Eq. (5b) can be written as

\(\theta_{3}^{\prime}=3 \theta_{4}^{\prime}-2\) (6)

Substituting Eq. (6) into Eqs. (3) and writing the resulting equations in matrix form gives

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc}-r_{4} \sin \theta_{4}-3 r_{3} \sin \theta_{3} &r_{5} \sin \theta_{5} \\r_{4} \cos \theta_{4}+3 r_{3} \cos \theta_{3} & -r_{5} \cos \theta_{5}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}\theta_{4}^{\prime} \\\theta_{5}^{\prime}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}-2 r_{3} \sin \theta_{3} \\2 r_{3} \cos \theta_{3}\end{array}\right]\) (7)

 

The determinant of the (2×2) coefficient matrix in Eqs. (7) can be written as

\(\Delta=r_{5}\left[r_{4} \sin \left(\theta_{4}-\theta_{5}\right)+3 r_{3} \sin \left(\theta_{3}-\theta_{5}\right)\right] \text {. }\) (8)

From Eqs. (7), using Cramer’s rule, the symbolic equations for the first-order kinematic coefficients of links 4 and 5 are

 

\(\theta_{4}^{\prime}=\frac{2 r_{3} r_{5} \sin \left(\theta_{3}-\theta_{5}\right)}{\Delta}\) (9a)

 

\(\theta_{5}^{\prime}=\frac{2 r_{3} r_{4} \sin \left(\theta_{3}-\theta_{4}\right)}{\Delta}\) (9b)

 

For the posture shown in Fig. 3.19, we have θ3 = 90°, θ4 = 90°, and θ5 = 0°; therefore, Eq. (8) can be written as

Δ = r5 (r4 +3r3). (10)

Also, Eqs. (9) reduce to

\(\theta_{4}^{\prime}=\frac{2 r_{3}}{r_{4}+3 r_{3}}=\frac{2}{9} rad / rad \quad \text { and } \quad \theta_{5}^{\prime}=0 .\) (11)

It is interesting to note that link 5 is not rotating in this posture; that is, the angular velocity ω5 =0 at this instant. Substituting Eq. (11) into Eq. (6), the first- rder kinematic coefficient of gear 3 is

\(\theta_{3}^{\prime}=-\frac{4}{3} rad / rad\) (12)

The angular velocity of link j can now be written as

\(\omega_{j}=\theta_{j}^{\prime} \omega_{2}\) (13)

Therefore, substituting Eqs. (11) and (12) into Eq. (13), and setting ω2 = 10 rad/s ccw, the angular velocities of gear 3 and links 4 and 5 are

ω3 = −13.33 rad/s, ω4 = +2.22 rad/s, and ω5 = 0, (14)

where the negative sign indicates clockwise and the positive sign indicates counterclockwise rotation.
