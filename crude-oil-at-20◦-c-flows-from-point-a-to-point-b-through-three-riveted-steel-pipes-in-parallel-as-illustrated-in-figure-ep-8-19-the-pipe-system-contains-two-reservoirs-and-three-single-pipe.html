Guest Question.

Crude oil at \( 20^{\circ} C \) flows from point A to point B through three riveted steel pipes in parallel, as illustrated in Figure EP 8.19. The pipe system contains two reservoirs and three single pipes. The three pipe entrances from reservoir A are square-edged, and the three pipe exits to reservoir B are square-edged. The first pipe is 0.75 m in diameter and 1000 m long. The second pipe is 0.7 m in diameter and 1000 m long, and the third pipe is 0.65 m in diameter and 1000m long. The difference in elevation between points A and B is 10 m. Assume the Darcy–Weisbach friction factor is chosen to model the flow resistance. (a) Determine the total flowrate and the flowrate in each of the three pipes. (b) Determine the flow regime. (c) Determine the total head loss due to pipe friction and pipe components between points A and B.

Admin
Solution

(a) Assuming the datum is at point B, the velocity in each pipe is computed by applying the energy equation between points A and B as follows:

\( \frac{P_{A}}{\gamma } + Z_{A} + \frac{v^{2}_{A}}{2g} – \sum{h_{ f,maj }} – \sum{h_{ f,min }} = \frac{P_{B}}{\gamma } + Z_{B} + \frac{v^{2}_{B}}{2g} \)

The discharge is computed from the continuity equation. The major head loss due to pipe friction for each pipe is computed from Equation 8.34 \( h_{f} = \frac{\tau _{w}L}{\gamma R_{h}} = \frac{\Delta p}{\gamma } = S_{f}L = \frac{v^{2}L}{C^{2}R_{h}} = \frac{v^{2}L}{\left(\frac{8g}{f} \right) \left(\frac{D}{4} \right) } = f \frac{L}{D} \frac{v^{2}}{2g} \) (Darcy–Weisbach head loss equation) as a Type 1 problem. The absolute pipe roughness for riveted steel is given in Table 8.3, and the Colebrook equation for the friction factor is assumed. The minor head loss coefficients and minor head losses are determined for the following pipe components: the three square-edged entrances from reservoir A (see Figure 8.10b) and the three square-edged pipe exits to reservoir B (see Figure 8.13b). The density, ρ and the viscosity, μ for crude oil at\( 20^{\circ} C \) are given in Table A.4 in Appendix A. The governing head loss equation, \( h_{ftotalAB} = h_{ftotalpipe1} = h_{ftotalpipe2} = h_{ftotalpipe3} \) establishes the relative velocities and thus the relative flowrates in each of the three pipes in parallel, solved as three independent Type 2 problems, where \( Q = Q_{1} + Q_{2} + Q_{3} \). Thus, in total, 23 equations are solved for
23 unknowns as follows:

\( D_{1}: = 0.75 m \) \( D_{2}: = 0.7 m \) \( D_{3}: = 0.65 m \) \( \varepsilon : = 0.9 mm \)

\( L_{1}: = 1000 m \) \( L_{2}: = 1000 m \) \( L_{3}: = 1000 m \)

\( A_{1}: = \frac{\pi .D^{2}_{1}}{4} = 0.442 m^{2} \) \( A_{2}: = \frac{\pi .D^{2}_{2}}{4} = 0.385 m^{2} \)

\( A_{3}: = \frac{\pi .D^{2}_{3}}{4} = 0.332 m^{2} \) \( \frac{\varepsilon }{D_{1}} = 1.2 \times 10^{-3} \) \( \frac{\varepsilon }{D_{2}} = 1.286 \times 10^{-3} \)

\( \frac{\varepsilon }{D_{3}} = 1.385 \times 10^{-3} \) \( \rho : = 856 \frac{kg}{m^{3}} \) \( \mu : = 7.2 \times 10^{-3} N \frac{sec}{m^{2}} \)

\( g: = 9.81 \frac{m}{sec^{2}} \) \( \gamma : = \rho .g = 8.397 \times 10^{3} \frac{kg}{m^{2}.S^{2}} \) \( P_{A}: = 0 \frac{N}{m^{2}} \)

\( P_{B}: = 0 \frac{N}{m^{2}} \) \(Z_{A}: = 0 m \) \(Z_{B}: = 0 m \)

\( V_{A}: = 0 \frac{m}{sec} \) \( V_{B}: = 0 \frac{m}{sec} \) \( k_{ent }: = 0.5 \) \( k_{exit }: = 1 \)

Guess values: \( f_{1}: = 0.01 \) \( f_{2}: = 0.01 \) \( f_{3}: = 0.01 \)

\( R_{1}: = 400.000 \) \( R_{2}: = 400.000 \) \( R_{3}: = 400.000 \)

\( V_{1}: = 1 \frac{m}{sec} \) \( V_{2}: = 1 \frac{m}{sec} \) \( V_{3}: = 1 \frac{m}{sec} \)

\( h_{f1}: = 10 m \) \( h_{f2}: = 10 m \) \( h_{f3}: = 10 m \)

\( h_{fmin1}: = 1 m \) \( h_{fmin2}: = 1 m \) \( h_{fmin3}: = 1 m \)

\( h_{ftotal}: = 20 m \) \( h_{ftotal1}: = 20 m \) \( h_{ftotal2}: = 20 m \)

\( h_{ftotal3}: = 60 m \) \( Q: = 3 \frac{m^{3}}{sec} \)

\( Q_{1}: = 1 \frac{m^{3}}{sec} \) \( Q_{2}: = 1 \frac{m^{3}}{sec} \) \( Q_{3}: = 1 \frac{m^{3}}{sec} \)

Given

\( \frac{P_{A}}{\gamma } + Z_{A} + \frac{V^{2}_{A}}{2g} – h_{ftotal} = \frac{P_{B}}{\gamma } + Z_{B} + \frac{V^{2}_{B}}{2g} \)

\( \frac{1}{\sqrt{f_{1}} } = – 2. log \left(\frac{\varepsilon /D_{1}}{3.7} + \frac{2.51}{R_{1}.\sqrt{f_{1}} } \right) \) \( R_{1} = \frac{\rho .V_{1}.D_{1} }{\mu } \) \( h_{f1} = f_{1} . \frac{L_{1}}{D_{1}} \frac{V^{2}_{1}}{2g} \)

\( \frac{1}{\sqrt{f_{2}} } = – 2. log \left(\frac{\varepsilon /D_{2}}{3.7} + \frac{2.51}{R_{2}.\sqrt{f_{2}} } \right) \) \( R_{2} = \frac{\rho .V_{2}.D_{2} }{\mu } \) \( h_{f2} = f_{2} . \frac{L_{2}}{D_{2}} \frac{V^{2}_{2}}{2g} \)

\( \frac{1}{\sqrt{f_{3}} } = – 2. log \left(\frac{\varepsilon /D_{3}}{3.7} + \frac{2.51}{R_{3}.\sqrt{f_{3}} } \right) \) \( R_{3} = \frac{\rho .V_{3}.D_{3} }{\mu } \) \( h_{f3} = f_{3} . \frac{L_{3}}{D_{3}} \frac{V^{2}_{3}} {2g} \)

\( h_{fmin1}= k_{ent} \left( \frac{V^{2}_{1}} {2g} \right) + k_{exit} \left( \frac{V^{2}_{1}} {2g} \right) \) \( h_{fmin2} = k_{ent} \left( \frac{V^{2}_{2}} {2g} \right) + k_{exit} \left( \frac{V^{2}_{2}} {2g} \right) \)
\( h_{fmin3} = k_{ent} \left( \frac{V^{2}_{3}} {2g} \right) + k_{exit} \left( \frac{V^{2}_{3}} {2g} \right) \)

\( h_{ftotal1}= h_{f1} + h_{fmin1} \) \( h_{ftotal2} = h_{f2} + h_{fmin2} \) \( h_{ftotal3} = h_{f3} + h_{fmin3} \)

\( h_{ftotal}=h_{ftotal1}\) \( h_{ftotal} = h_{ftotal2} \) \( h_{ftotal} = h_{ftotal3} \)

\( V_{1} = \frac{Q_{1}}{A_{1}} \) \( V_{2} = \frac{Q_{2}}{A_{2}} \) \( V_{3} = \frac{Q_{3}}{A_{3}} \) \( Q = Q_{1} + Q_{2} + Q_{3} \)

\(\left ( \begin{matrix} f_{1} \\ f_{2} \\ f_{3} \\ R_{1} \\ R_{2} \\ R_{3} \\ V_{1} \\ V_{2} \\ V_{3} \\ h_{f1} \\ h_{f2} \\ h_{f3} \\ h_{fmin1} \\ h_{fmin2} \\ h_{fmin3} \\ h_{ftotal} \\h_{ftotal1} \\ h_{ftotal2} \\ h_{ftotal3} \\ Q \\ Q_{1} \\ Q_{2} \\ Q_{3} \end{matrix} \right ) : = Find (f_{1},f_{2},f_{3}, R_{1}, R_{2}, R_{3} ,V_{1},V_{2},V_{3},,h_{f1},h_{f2},h_{f3}, , h_{fmin1}, h_{fmin2}, h_{fmin3}, h_{ftotal} ,h_{ftotal1},h_{ftotal2} ,h_{ftotal3}, Q ,Q_{1},Q_{2},Q_{3} )\)

\( f_{1} = 0.022 \) \( f_{2} = 0.022\) \( f_{3} = 0.023 \)

\(R_{1} = 2.267 \times 10^{5} \) \(R_{2} = 2.028 \times 10^{5} \) \(R_{3} = 1.8 \times 10^{5} \)

\(V_{1} = 2.542 \frac{m}{s} \) \(V_{2} = 2.437 \frac{m}{s} \) \(V_{3} = 2.329 \frac{m}{s} \)

\( h_{f1} = 9.506 m \) \( h_{f2} = 9.546 m \) \( h_{f3} = 8.585 m \)

\( h_{fmin1} = 0.494 m \) \( h_{fmin2} = 0.454 m \) \( h_{fmin3} = 0.415 m \)

\(h_{ftotal} = 10 m \) \(h_{ftotal1} = 10 m \) \(h_{ftotal2} = 10 m \)

\(h_{ftotal3} = 10 m \) \( Q = 2.834 \frac{m^{3}}{s} \)

\( Q_{1} = 1.123 \frac{m^{3}}{sec} \) \( Q_{2} = 0.938 \frac{m^{3}}{sec} \) \( Q_{3} = 0.773 \frac{m^{3}}{sec} \)

where the continuity governing equation is satisfied (checks) as follows:

\( Q_{1} + Q_{2} + Q_{3} = 2.834 \frac{m^{3}}{s} \)

(b) Thus, because the Reynolds number, R is greater than 4000, the flow is turbulent.
(c) Thus, the total head loss due to pipe friction and pipe components between points A and B is given as follows:

\( h_{ftotal3} = 10 m \)

where the potential energy, \( Z_{A} = 10 m \) is converted into the total head loss, \( h_{ftotal3} = 10 m \).
