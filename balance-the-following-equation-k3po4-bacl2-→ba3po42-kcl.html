Guest Question.

Balance the following equation:

\(K_{3} PO_{4} +BaCl_{2} \longrightarrow Ba_{3}\left(PO_{4} \right)_{2} +KCl\)
Admin
Solution

Focus on the cation of greatest charge,\(Ba^{2+}\):

Ba(X)→\(Ba_{3}\) (Y)

The barium atoms can be balanced, provisionally, by placing a 3 before the Ba on the left-hand side.

3Ba(X)→\(Ba_{3}\) (Y)

The consequence of inserting this balancing coefficient is that there are now six chlorine atoms on the left-hand side. Inserting a coefficient of 6 in front of the Cl-containing compound on the right-hand side increases the number of potassium atoms in the product to six. To balance those 6 K, we must now place a coefficient of 2 in front of the reactant\( K_{3} PO_{4}\) . The final balanced equation is

\(2K_{3} PO_{4}+3BaCl_{2}→Ba_{3}\left(PO_{4} \right)_{2} +6KCl\)
