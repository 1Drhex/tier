Guest Question.

A 50 kg block is attached to mass M using string which is passing over a smooth pulley as shown in Figure Ex. 8. Determine the range of mass M that will keep the block in equilibrium. Assume \(\mu_{s} = 0.30\).

Admin
Solution

Here it is clear that slipping of the block down or up the inclined plane will impend when the mass M reaches to its minimum value or maximum value respectively. At minimum value of mass M, the block will impend down the plane and at maximum value of mass M, the block will impend up the plane. Also as pulley is smooth, the tension in string (M × 9.81) will remain same throughout.

Now first consider impending motion of the block down the plane and then
apply conditions of equilibrium i.e. \( \sum H = 0\) along the inclined plane, and \( \sum V = 0\) perpendicular to the inclined plane. Free body diagram of the block is shown in figure Ex 8(a).

\( \sum H = 0 : F + M × 9.81 – 50 × 9.81 × \sin 30° = 0\)
Now for impending motion from (Eq. 5.1), we know \( F_{max} = \mu_{s} N\) .
\( \therefore \mu_{s} N + M × 9.81 – 50 × 9.81 × \sin 30° = 0\)
\( M × 9.81 = 50 × 9.81 × \sin 30° – 0.30 × N\) …(Eq.1)
Now, \( \sum V = 0 : N – 50 × 9.81 × \sin 30° = 0\)
\(\therefore N = 245.25\)
Substituting value of N in to Eq. 1,
\( M × 9.81 = 50 × 9.81 × \sin 30° – 0.30 × 245.25\)
\( \therefore M = M_{min} = 17.5 kg\)

Now consider impending motion of the block up the plane and then apply conditions of equilibrium i.e. \( \sum H = 0\) along the inclined plane, and \( \sum V = 0\) perpendicular to the inclined plane. Free body diagram of the block is shown in figure Ex 8(b).

\(\sum H = 0: – F + M × 9.81 – 50 × 9.81 × \sin 30° = 0 \)
Now for impending motion from (Eq. 5.1), we know \(F_{max} = \mu_{s} N \).
\(\therefore – \mu_{s} N + M × 9.81 – 50 × 9.81 × \sin 30° = 0 \)
\(M × 9.81 = 50 × 9.81 × \sin 30° + 0.30 × N \) …(Eq.2)
Now, \( \sum V = 0 : N – 50 × 9.81 × \sin 30° = 0 \)
\( \therefore N = 245.25 \)
Substituting value of N in to Eq. 2,
\(M × 9.81 = 50 × 9.81 × \sin 30° + 0.30 × 245.25 \)
\( \therefore M = M_{max} = 32.5 kg \)
\( \therefore \) Range of mass M is from 17.5 kg to 32.5 kg that can keep the block in equilibrium.
