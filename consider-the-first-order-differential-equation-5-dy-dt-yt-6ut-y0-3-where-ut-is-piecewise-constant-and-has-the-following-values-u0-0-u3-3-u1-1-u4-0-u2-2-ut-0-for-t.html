Guest Question.

Consider the first-order differential equation

\(5 \frac{d y}{d t}+y(t)=6 u(t) \quad y(0)=3\)

where \(u(t)\) is piecewise constant and has the following values:

\(\begin{array}{ll}u(0)=0 & u(3)=3 \\u(1)=1 & u(4)=0 \\u(2)=2 & u(t)=0 \quad \text { for } t>4\end{array}\)

Derive a difference equation for this ordinary equation using \(\Delta t=1\) and

(a) Exact discretization

(b) Finite difference approximation

Compare the integrated results for \(0 \leq t \leq 10\). Examine whether \(\Delta t=0.1\) improves the accuracy of finite difference model

Admin
Solution

a) Replacing \(\tau\) by 5, and \(K\) by 6 in Eq. 7-25

\(y(k)=e^{-\Delta t / \tau} y(k-1)+K\left(1-e^{-\Delta t / \tau}\right) u(k-1)\) (7-25)

\(y(k)=e^{-\Delta t / 5} y(k-1)+\left[1-e^{-\Delta t / 5}\right] 6 u(k-1)\)

b) Replacing \(\tau\) by 5, and \(K\) by 6 in Eq. \(7-22\)

\(\frac{\tau(y(k)-y(k-1))}{\Delta t}+y(k-1)=K u(k-1)\) (7-22)

\(y(k)=\left(1-\frac{\Delta t}{5}\right) y(k-1)+\frac{\Delta t}{5} 6 u(k-1)\)

In the integrated results tabulated below for \(\Delta t=0.1\), the values are shown only at integer values of \(t\), for comparison.
