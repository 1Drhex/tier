Guest Question.

Determine if the anchor group for the attachment shown in Fig. 21.15 is adequate to carry the 5 kip factored sustained load using the characteristic bond stresses from Table 21.7. The A36 steel anchors have 10 threads per inch, are \(\frac{3}{4}\) in. in diameter, and are embedded 8 in. into an exterior concrete wall with \(f_c^\prime\) = 4000 psi. The anchor group is well away from any edges, supplementary reinforcement is present, and the anchors are considered Category 2—medium sensitivity and reliability.

The shear on the anchor group is equal to the applied load and is 5 kips. To calculate the design shear capacity,

\(f_{uta} = 1.9 f_{ya} = 1.9 \times 36\) = 68.4 ksi

The net area, \(A_{se,V}, = \frac{\pi}{4}\left(d_a-\frac{0.9743}{10 threads/in.} \right)= 0.334 in^2\), then

\(ϕ V_{sa} = ϕ n A_{se,V} f_{uta} = 0.60 \times 4 \times 0.475 \times 68.4\) = 54.9 kips

The tensile breakout capacity of the anchor is as follows, noting that the ACI Code requires that \(τ_{uncr}\) to be multiplied by 0.4 for the sustained loads:

\(c_{Na}=10d_a\sqrt{\frac{0.4\tau_{uncr}}{1100}}=10\times0.75\sqrt{\frac{0.4\times200}{1100}}=3.65 in. \)

The basic projected area is \(A_{Nao} = (2 c_{Na} )^2 = (2 \times 3.65)^2 = 53.2 in^2\), and the projected area of the two top anchors is \(A_{Na} = 2 c_{Na} (2 c_{Na} + s ) = 2 \times 3.65 \times (2 \times 3.65 + 6) = 96.9 in^2.\)

The basic bond strength of one adhesive anchor with a factor of 0.4 applied to \(τ_{cr}\) for sustained loads is

\(N_{ba} = 0.4 τ_{cr} \pi d_a h_{ef} = 0.4 \times 200 \times \pi \times 0.75 \times 8\) = 1.51 kips

The two top anchors then provide a capacity of

\(N_a=\frac{A_{Na}}{A_{Nao}}ψ_{ec,Na} ψ_{ed,Na} ψ_{pc,Na} N_{ba} = \frac{96.9}{53.2}\times 1 \times 1 \times 1 \times 1.51 = 2.75 kips\)

The design load on the anchors is the applied load \(P_u\) times the eccentricity divided by the distance between the anchors, and thus, \(N_u = 5 \times 3 / 8\) = 1.875 kips, which is more than 20 percent of the nominal capacity, so the combined loading must be checked, giving

\(\frac{N_u}{\phi N_a}+\frac{V_u}{\phi V_{sa}}=\frac{1.875}{0.65\times2.75}+\frac{5}{0.65\times54.9}=1.19 \lt 1.2 \)

The interaction meets the code requirements, and thus, the attachment can carry the applied load. Comparing the anchor group in , the capacity of the attachment with adhesive anchors is approximately one-seventh the capacity of the attachment with cast-in anchors. This comparison clearly shows that cast-in anchors are structurally more efficient than adhesive anchors when the latter are designed based on the minimum characteristic bond stresses. In addition, a comparison of the characteristic bond stress values in the ACI Code with those obtained for commercial adhesive anchors derived through the ACI 355.4 qualification process (see, for example, values shown in Problem 21.8 at the end of this chapter) demonstrates that the Code values are highly conservative.
