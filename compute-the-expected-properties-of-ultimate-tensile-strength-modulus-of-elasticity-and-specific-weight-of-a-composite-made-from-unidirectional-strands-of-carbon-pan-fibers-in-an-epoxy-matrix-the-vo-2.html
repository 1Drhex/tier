Guest Question.

Compute the expected properties of ultimate tensile strength, modulus of elasticity, and specific weight of a composite made from unidirectional strands of carbon-PAN fibers in an epoxy matrix. The volume fraction of fibers is 30%. Use data from Table 2–19.

TABLE 2-19 Example Properties of Matrix and Reinforcement Materials
ksi Mpa \(10^6\) psi Gpa lb/\(in^3\) kN/\(\)m^3]
Matrix materials:
Polyester 10 69 0.4 2.76 0.047 12.7
Epoxy 18 124 0.56 3.86 0.047 12.7
Aluminum 45 310 10 69 0.1 27.1
Titanium 170 1170 16.5 114 0.16 43.4
Reinforcement materials:
S-glass 600 4140 12.5 86.2 0.09 24.4
Carbon-PAN 470 3240 33.5 231 0.064 17.4
Carbon-PAN (high-strength) 820 5650 40 276 0.065 17.7
Carbon (high-modulus) 325 2200 100 690 0.078 21.2
Aramid 500 3450 19 131 0.052 14.1
Admin
Solution

Objective: Compute the expected values of \(s_{u c}, E_{c} \), and \(\gamma_{c} \) for the composite.
Given: \(\quad \) Matrix-epoxy: \(s_{\text {um }}=18 \mathrm{ksi} ; E_{m}=0.56 \times 10^{6} \mathrm{psi} ; \gamma_{m}=0.047 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{in}^{3} \).
Fiber-carbon-PAN: \(s_{u f}=470 \mathrm{ksi} ; E_{f}=33.5 \times 10^{6} \mathrm{psi} ; \gamma_{f}=0.064 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{in}^{3} \).
Volume fraction of fiber: \(V_{f}=0.30 \), and \(V_{m}=1.0-0.30=0.70 \).

Analysis and Results: The ultimate tensile strength, \(s_{uc}\), is computed from Equation (2–10):

\(s_{u c}=s_{u f} V_{f}+\sigma_{m}^{\prime} V_{m}\)

To find \(\sigma_{m}^{\prime}\), we first find the strain at which the fibers would fail at \(s_{\text {uf }}\). Assume that the fibers are linearly elastic to failure. Then
\(\epsilon_{f}=s_{u f} / E_{f}=\left(470 \times 10^{3} \mathrm{psi}\right) /\left(33.5 \times 10^{6} \mathrm{psi}\right)=0.014\)
At this same strain, the stress in the matrix is
\(\sigma_{m}^{\prime}=E_{m} \epsilon=\left(0.56 \times 10^{6} \mathrm{psi}\right)(0.014)=7840 \mathrm{psi}\)
Then, in Equation (2-10),
\(s_{u c}=(470000 \mathrm{psi})(0.30)+(7840 \mathrm{psi})(0.70)=146500 \mathrm{psi}\)
The modulus of elasticity computed from Equation (2-12):
\(\begin{aligned}&E_{c}=E_{f} V_{f}+E_{m} V_{m}=\left(33.5 \times 10^{6}\right)(0.30)+\left(0.56\times 10^{6}\right)(0.70) \\&E_{c}=10.4 \times 10^{6} \mathrm{psi}\end{aligned}\)

The specific weight is computed from Equation (2-14):
\(\gamma_{c}=\gamma_{f} V_{f}+\gamma_{m} V_{m}=(0.064)(0.30)+(0.047)(0.70)=0.052 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{in}^{3}\)
Summary of Results:

\(\begin{gathered}s_{u c}=146500 \mathrm{psi} \\E_{c}=10.4 \times 10^{6} \mathrm{psi} \\\gamma_{c}=0.052 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{in}^{3}\end{gathered}\)
Comment: Note that the resulting properties for the composite are intermediate between those for the fibers and the matrix.
