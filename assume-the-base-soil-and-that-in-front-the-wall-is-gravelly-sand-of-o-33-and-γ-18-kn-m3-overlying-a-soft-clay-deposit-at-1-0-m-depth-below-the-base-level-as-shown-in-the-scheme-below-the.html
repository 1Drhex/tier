Guest Question.

Assume the base soil and that in front the wall is gravelly sand of \( \varnothing = 33^{\circ} \) and \( γ = 18 kN/m^{3} \), overlying a soft-clay deposit at 1.0 m depth below the base level, as shown in the scheme below. The ground water table is also located at the same depth. The soft clay has an average \( c = 20 kPa, \varnothing = 0^{\circ} \) and \( γ = 17.5 kN/m^{3}\). Determine the safety factor (SF) against a deep-seated shear failure (or rotational instability) for a trial cylindrical slip surface through the heel shown in Scheme. Assume radius of the cylindrical slip surface is 4.33 m, and its centre O located at 2.87 m above the foundation level. Use the conventional method of slices (the Fellenius or Swedish solution).

Admin
Solution

Normally, the safety factor (SF) against a deep-seated shear failure (or rotational instability) should be at least two. For this analysis, several trial failure surfaces (circles) should be drawn, the safety factor computed, and the minimum value taken. It may be useful to mention that the trial failure circles passing through the heel apply for ground or backfill slope angles of \( \beta = 0\) to perhaps \( \beta = 10^{\circ}\); for larger values of \( \beta \), irregular surfaces or surcharges, the assumed failure circles may not pass through the heel, and other failure locations should be investigated. In this case, we try to compute the safety factor for the circle indicated in the given scheme of the Problem as an example. Due to the repetitive nature of the calculations and the need to select an adequate number of trial surfaces, the method of slices is particularly suitable for solution by computer, especially, where more complex slope geometry and different soil strata are required to be introduced.

Step 1. Draw the wall-soil system to a convenient scale, as shown below.

Step 2. Divide the circular segment amnf and the material above by vertical planes into a series of slices, as shown in the scheme below. The base b of each slice is assumed to be a straight line. For any slice the inclination of the base to the horizontal is \( \alpha \) and the height, measured on the centre line, is h. Weight W (including surcharge) of each slice is resolved into its normal component \( N = W \cos \alpha \) and tangential component \( T = W \sin \alpha \), which are determined graphically, as shown. Alternatively, the value of \( \alpha \) can be measured or calculated.

Note: Since the centre of rotation O is centered with respect to the circular arc mn (located in the soft clay), it is not necessary to divide this arc and the material above into slices and find N and T; their resultant effects will be zero (neglecting the difference in weights of a portion of the concrete footing and displaced soil). However, some
correction was applied to include the weight of a portion of the concrete stem in slice 5, as shown in the scheme.

Step 3. Determine values of N and T per metre length of the retaining wall for each slice, as indicated in Table 5.10.

Step 4. Determine the driving and resisting moments about centre of rotation at point O, then compute the safety factor SF.

(a) Driving moment \( M_{d} \).
This moment consists of moment of the total active thrust \( P_{a} \) plus moment of \( \sum T \), computed as

\( M_{d} = \overline{y} P_{a} + R \sum T \)

 

The thrust \( P_{a} \) is located at 1.21 m above the foundation level, whereas, point O is located at 2.87 m above the same level. Therefore,

\( \overline{y} = 2.87 – 1.21 = 1.66 m \)

 

\( M_{d} = 1.66 \times 50.7 + 4.33 \times 42.8 = 269.5 kN.m/m \)

 

(b) Resisting moment \( M_{r} \).
This moment equals to moment of the total shear strength \( T_{f} \) along the circular arc \( a m n f \), computed as

\( M_{r} = R \sum T_{f} \)

 

\( T_{f} = cohesion force + friction force = \left ( arc length \times c \right ) + \left ( N \tan \varnothing^{\circ} \right ) \)

 

Gravelly sand:

The base of slice 5 is located in soft clay. Therefore, N for this slice should not be considered.

\( \sum T_{f} = 0 + \left ( \sum N – N of slice 5 \right ) \tan \varnothing^{\circ} = \left ( 137.8 – 9 \right ) \tan 33 \)

 

\( = 83.6 kN/m \)

 

Soft clay:

\( \sum T_{f} = \left ( length of arc mn \times c \right ) + 0 = \frac{53}{360} \left ( 2 \pi R \right ) \left ( c \right ) \)

 

\( = \frac{53}{360} \times 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 4.33 \times 20 = 80.1 kN/m \)

 

\( M_{r} = 4.33 \left ( 83.6 + 80.1 \right ) = 708.8 kN.m/m \)

 

Safety factor is

\( SF = \frac{M_{r}}{M_{d}} = \frac{708.8}{269.5} = 2.63 > 2 \) ( OK.)
