Guest Question.

Draw the shear and moment diagrams for the beam shown in Fig. \(11-11 (\mathrm{a})\). \(EI\) is constant.

Admin
Solution

Slope-Deflection Equations. Two spans must be considered in this problem. Equation 11–8 applies to span \(AB\). We can use Eq. 11–10 for span \(BC\) since the end \(C\) is on a roller. Using the formulas for the FEMs tabulated on the inside back cover, we have

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=2 E k\left(2 \theta_{N}+\theta_{F}-3 \psi\right)+(\text { FEM })_{N}\\ \text For Internal Span or End Span with Far End Fixed \end{array} \) (11-8)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=3 E k\left(\theta_{N}-\psi\right)+(\mathrm{FEM})_{N}\\ Only for End Span with Far End Pinned or Roller Supported \end{array} \) (11-10)

\( \begin{array}{l} (\mathrm{FEM})_{A B}=-\frac{w L^{2}}{12}=-\frac{1}{12}(2)(24)^{2}=-96 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ (\mathrm{FEM})_{B A}=\frac{w L^{2}}{12}=\frac{1}{12}(2)(24)^{2}=96 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ (\mathrm{FEM})_{B C}=-\frac{3 P L}{16}=-\frac{3(12)(8)}{16}=-18 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \end{array} \)

Note that \((\mathrm{FEM})_{A B}\) and \((\mathrm{FEM})_{B C}\) are negative since they act counterclockwise on the beam at \(A\) and \(B\), respectively. Also, since the supports do not settle, \(\psi_{A B}=\psi_{B C}=0\). Applying Eq. 11-8 for span \(A B\) and realizing that \(\theta_{A}=0\), we have

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=2 E k\left(2 \theta_{N}+\theta_{F}-3 \psi\right)+(\text { FEM })_{N}\\ \text For Internal Span or End Span with Far End Fixed \end{array} \) (11-8)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{L}\right)\left(2 \theta_{N}+\theta_{F}-3 \psi\right)+(\mathrm{FEM})_{N} \\ M_{A B}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{24}\right)\left[2(0)+\theta_{B}-3(0)\right]-96 \\ \end{array}\)

\(\begin{array}{l}M_{A B}=0.08333 E I \theta_{B}-96 \\ \end{array}\) (1)

\(\begin{array}{l}M_{B A}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{24}\right)\left[2 \theta_{B}+0-3(0)\right]+96 \\ \end{array}\)

\(\begin{array}{l}M_{B A}=0.1667 E I \theta_{B}+96 \end{array}\) (2)

Applying Eq. 11-10 with \(B\) as the near end and \(C\) as the far end, we have

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=3 E k\left(\theta_{N}-\psi\right)+(\mathrm{FEM})_{N}\\ Only for End Span with Far End Pinned or Roller Supported \end{array} \) (11-10)

\(\begin{array}{c} M_{N}=3 E\left(\frac{I}{L}\right)\left(\theta_{N}-\psi\right)+(\mathrm{FEM})_{N} \\ M_{B C}=3 E\left(\frac{I}{8}\right)\left(\theta_{B}-0\right)-18 \\ \end{array}\)

\(\begin{array}{c}M_{B C}=0.375 E I \theta_{B}-18 \end{array}\) (3)

Remember that Eq. 11-10 is not applied from \(C\) (near end) to \(B\) (far end).

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=3 E k\left(\theta_{N}-\psi\right)+(\mathrm{FEM})_{N}\\ Only for End Span with Far End Pinned or Roller Supported \end{array} \) (11-10)

Equilibrium Equations. The above three equations contain four unknowns. The necessary fourth equation comes from the conditions of equilibrium at the support \(B\). The free-body diagram is shown in Fig. 11-11b. We have

\( \curvearrowleft +\sum M_{B}=0 ; \quad M_{B A}+M_{B C}=0 \) (4)

To solve, substitute Eqs. (2) and (3) into Eq. (4), which yields

\( \theta_{B}=-\frac{144.0}{E I} \)

Since \(\theta_{B}\) is negative (counterclockwise) the elastic curve for the beam has been correctly drawn in Fig. 11-11a. Substituting \(\theta_{B}\) into Eqs. (1)-(3), we get

\( \begin{aligned} M_{A B} &=-108.0 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ M_{B A} &=72.0 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ M_{B C} &=-72.0 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \end{aligned} \)

Using these data for the moments, the shear reactions at the ends of the beam spans have been determined in Fig. 11-11c. The shear and moment diagrams are plotted in Fig. 11-11d.
