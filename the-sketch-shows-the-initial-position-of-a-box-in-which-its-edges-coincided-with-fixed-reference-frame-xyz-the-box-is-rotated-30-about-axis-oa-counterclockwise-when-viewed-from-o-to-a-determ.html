Guest Question.

The sketch shows the initial position of a box, in which its edges coincided with fixed reference frame XYZ. The box is rotated 30° about axis OA, counterclockwise when viewed from O to A. Determine the displacement of point B in terms of components relative to xyz, and relative to XYZ.

Admin
Solution

This example consolidates many of the developments for rotation transformations. To follow corner B as it moves, we attach the xyz coordinate system to the box, such that the axes coincide with XYZ prior to rotation. Thus the initial transformation is \([R]_{o} = [U]\) , and the initial coordinates of point B with respect to xyz are

\(\left\{\begin{array}{l}\left(x_{B}\right)_{0} \\\left(y_{B}\right)_{0}\\\left(z_{B}\right)_{0}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{c}1.5\\2\\1\end{array}\right\}\)

We need to establish \([R]_{f}\) , which transforms XYZ to xyz components in the final position. The rotation takes place about an axis that does not coincide with either set of axes, so we define an \(X^{\prime} Y^{\prime} Z^{\prime}\) coordinate system whose \(Z^{\prime}\) axis coincides with the diagonal OA. This system may be obtained by a simple rotation about the negative X axis by \(\theta_{X}=\tan ^{-1}(2 / 1)\). The corresponding transformation from \(\bar{I} \bar{J} \bar{K}\) to \(\bar{I}^{\prime} \bar{J}^{\prime} \bar{K}^{\prime}\) components is

\(\left[R^{\prime}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \cos \theta_{X} & -\sin \theta_{X} \\0 & \sin \theta_{X} & \cos \theta_{X} \end{array}\right]\)

The specified rotation about the \(Z^{\prime}\) axis is φ = −30º, so

\(\left[R_{\phi}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}\cos 30^{\circ} & -\sin 30^{\circ} & 0 \\\sin 30^{\circ} & \cos 30^{\circ} & 0 \\0 & 0 & 1\end{array}\right]\)

From Eq. (3.1.34) we find that

\(\left\{\begin{array}{l}A_{x} \\A_{y} \\A_{z}\end{array}\right\}=[R]\left\{\begin{array}{l}A_{X}\\A_{Y} \\A_{Z}\end{array}\right\}, \quad[R]=\left[R^{\prime}\right]^{\mathrm{T}}\left[R_{\phi}\right]\left[R^{\prime}\right]\) (3.1.34)

 

\([R]_{\mathrm{f}}=\left[R^{\prime}\right]^{\mathrm{T}}\left[R_{\phi}\right]\left[R^{\prime}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}0.8660 & -0.2236 & 0.4472 \\0.2236 & 0.9732 & 0.0536 \\-0.4472 & 0.0536 & 0.8928 \end{array}\right]\).

The origin \(O^{\prime}\) was placed at a fixed point, so there is no translational displacement, \(\Delta \bar{r}_{O^{\prime}}=\overline{0}\). Point B is a part of the box, so it does not move relative to xyz. Thus its coordinates relative to xyz are constant and its relative displacement is \(\left(\Delta \bar{r}_{B}\right)_{x y z}=\overline{0}\). We may directly employ either Eq. (3.2.7) or Eq. (3.2.8). Setting \([R]_{o} = [U]\) in the latter shows that the displacement of point B in terms of xyz components is

\(\begin{aligned}\left\{\begin{array}{c}\Delta \bar{r}_{P} \cdot \bar{I} \\\Delta \bar{r}_{P} \cdot \bar{J} \\\Delta \bar{r}_{P} \cdot \bar{K}\end{array}\right\}=&\left\{\begin{array}{l}\Delta \bar{r}_{O^{\prime}} \cdot \bar{I} \\\Delta \bar{r}_{O^{\prime}} \cdot \bar{J} \\\Delta\bar{r}_{O^{\prime}} \cdot \bar{K}\end{array}\right\}+[R]_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{T}}\left\{\begin{array}{l}\left(\Delta \bar{r}_{P}\right)_{x y z} \cdot \bar{i} \\\left(\Delta \bar{r}_{P}\right)_{x y z} \cdot \bar{j} \\\left(\Delta \bar{r}_{p}\right)_{x y z} \cdot \bar{k}\end{array}\right\}\\\\&+\left[[R]_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{T}}-[R]_{\mathrm{o}}^{\mathrm{T}}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}\left(x_{P}\right)_{\mathrm{o}} \\\left(y_{P}\right)_{\mathrm{o}}\\\left(z_{P}\right)_{\mathrm{o}}\end{array}\right\}\end{aligned}\) (3.2.7)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\left\{\begin{array}{c}\Delta \bar{r}_{P} \cdot \bar{i} \\\Delta \bar{r}_{P} \cdot \bar{j} \\\Delta \bar{r}_{P} \cdot \bar{k}\end{array}\right\}=&\left\{\begin{array}{l}\Delta \bar{r}_{O^{\prime}} \cdot \bar{i} \\\Delta \bar{r}_{O^{\prime}} \cdot \bar{j}\\\Delta \bar{r}_{O^{\prime}} \cdot \bar{k}\end{array}\right\}+[R]_{\mathrm{f}}[R]_{\mathrm{o}}^{\mathrm{T}}\left\{\begin{array}{l}\left(\Delta \bar{r}_{P}\right)_{x y z} \cdot \bar{i} \\\left(\Delta \bar{r}_{P}\right)_{x y z} \cdot \bar{j} \\\left(\Delta \bar{r}_{p}\right)_{x y z} \cdot \bar{k}\end{array}\right\} \\\\ &+\left[[U]-[R]_{\mathrm{f}}[R]_{\mathrm{o}}^{\mathrm{T}}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{c}\left(x_{P}\right)_{\mathrm{f}} \\\left(y_{P}\right)_{\mathrm{f}} \\\left(z_{P}\right)_{\mathrm{f}}\end{array}\right\} .\end{aligned}\) (3.2.8)

 

\(\left\{\begin{array}{c}\Delta \bar{r}_{B} \cdot \bar{i} \\\Delta \bar{r}_{B} \cdot \bar{j} \\\Delta \bar{r}_{B}\cdot\bar{k}\end{array}\right\}=\left[[U]-[R]_{\mathrm{f}}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{c}\left(x_{B}\right)_{\mathrm{f}}\\\left(y_{B}\right)_{\mathrm{f}}\\\left(z_{B}\right)_{\mathrm{f}}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{c}0.2010 \\-0.3354 \\0.6708\end{array}\right\} \mathrm{m}\).

We could use Eq. (3.2.7) to find the XYZ displacement components, but here it is simpler to use the rotation transformation \([R]_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{T}}\) f to transform to XYZ components:

\(\left\{\begin{array}{c}\Delta \bar{r}_{B} \cdot \bar{I} \\\Delta \bar{r}_{B} \cdot \bar{J} \\\Delta \bar{r}_{B} \cdot \bar{K}\end{array}\right\}=[R]_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{T}}\left\{\begin{array}{c}0.2010 \\-0.3354 \\0.6708 \end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{c} 0.5490 \\ -0.2455 \\ 0.4911\end{array}\right\} \mathrm{m}\).
