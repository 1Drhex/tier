Heat Calculation Using Mean Heat Capacity for Air

You need to preheat a stream of air flowing steadily at 10 mol/min from 600 K to 900 K. Determine the heat rate required using the mean heat capacity data from .

Admin
Solution

This process occurs at steady-state with one stream in and one stream out; hence Equation (2.19) can be written as follows:

 

\(0=\dot{n}_{1}\left(h+M W \frac{\vec{V}^{2}}{2}+M Wg z\right)_{1}-\dot{n}_{2}\left(h+M W \frac{\vec{V}^{2}}{ 2 }+MWgz\right)_{2}+\dot{Q}+\dot{ Q}_{s}\)

 

where we have set the bulk kinetic and potential energies and shaft work to zero. A mole balance yields:

 

\(\dot{n}_{1}=\dot{n}_{2}=\dot{n}_{ air }\)

 

so the fi rst law balance simplifi es to:

 

\(\dot{Q}=\dot{n}_{\text {air }}\left(h_{900}-h_{600}\right)=\dot{n}_{\text {air }}\left[\left(h_{900}-h_{298}\right)-\left(h_{600}-h_{298}\right)\right]\) (E2.9A)

 

where \(h_{900}\) is the enthalpy of air at 900 K, \(h_{600}\) is the enthalpy at 600 K, and \(h_{298}\) is the enthalpy at 298 K. Equation (E2.9A) was rewritten to use the defi nition for mean heat capacity given by Equation (2.33):

 

\(\bar{c}_{P}=\frac{\int_{298}^{T} c_{P} d T}{T-298}\) (2.33)

 

\(\left(h_{900}-h_{298}\right)=\bar{c}_{P, 900}(900-298)\) (E2.9B)

 

\(\left(h_{600}-h_{298}\right)=\bar{c}_{P, 600}(600-298)\) (E2.9C)

 

Substituting Equations (E2.9B) and (E2.9C) into (E2.9A) and using the values from Table E2.8, we get:

\(\dot{Q}=\dot{n} \Delta h=10[30.71(900-298)-29.97(600-298)]=94.363[ J / min ]\)
