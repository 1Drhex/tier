Guest Question.

Determine the joint displacements, member end forces, and support reactions for the continuous beam shown in Fig. 7.9(a), due to the combined effect of the uniformly distributed load shown and the settlements of 45 mm and 15 mm, respectively, of supports 3 and 4. Use the matrix stiffness method.

Admin
Solution

Analytical Model: See Fig. 7.9(b). The structure has two degrees of freedom and six restrained coordinates. Note that member 3 is modeled as being hinged at its right end (i.e., MT = 2), because the moment at that end of the member must be 0. This approach enables us to eliminate the rotational degrees of freedom of joint 4 from the analysis, by modeling it as a hinged joint with its rotation restrained by an imaginary clamp.

Structure Stiffness Matrix and Fixed-Joint Forces Due to Member Loads:

Members 1 and 2 (MT = 0) By substituting \(E = 70(10^{6}) kN/m^{2}, I = 102(10^{−6}) m^{4},\) and L = 8 m into Eq. (5.53) \(k=\frac{EI}{L^{3}}=\left[\begin{matrix}12& 6L& −12& 6L\\ 6L& 4L^{2}& −6L& 2L^{2} \\−12 &−6L& 12& −6L\\6L& 2L^{2}& −6L& 4L^{2} \end{matrix} \right]\) , we evaluate the member stiffness matrices k as

(1)

Using the equations given inside the front cover, we evaluate the fixed-end shears and moments due to the 15 kN/m uniformly distributed load as

\(FS_{b} = FS_{e} = 60 kN FM_{b} = −FM_{e} = 80 kN · m\) (2)

Thus, using Eq. (5.99) \(Q_{f}=\left[\begin{matrix} Q_{f1} \\ Q_{f2} \\ Q_{f3}\\Q_{f4} \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix} FS_{b} \\ FM_{b} \\ FS_{e}\\FM_{e} \end{matrix} \right]\) , we obtain the member fixed-end force vectors:

(3)

Next, using the code numbers for member 1 (3, 4, 5, 1) and member 2 (5, 1, 6, 2), we store the pertinent elements of \(k_{1}\) and \(k_{2}\) into the structure stiffness matrix S, as shown in Fig. 7.9(c). Similarly, the pertinent elements of \(Q_{f1}\) and \(Q_{f2}\) are stored in the structure fixed-joint force vector Pf, as shown in Fig. 7.9(d).

Member 3 (MT = 2) Because MT = 2 for this member, we use Eqs. (7.18) \(k=\frac{EI}{L^{3}}=\left[\begin{matrix}3& 3L& −3& 0\\ 3L& 3L^{2}& −3L& 0 \\−3 &−3L& 3& 0\\0& 0& 0& 0 \end{matrix} \right]\) and (7.19) \(Q_{f}=\left[\begin{matrix} FS_{b}-\frac{3}{2L}FM_{e} \\ FM_{b}-\frac{1}{2}FM_{e} \\ FS_{e}+\frac{3}{2L}FM_{e}\\0 \end{matrix} \right]\) to determine its stiffness matrix k and fixed-end force vector \(Q_{f}\), respectively.
Thus, by applying Eq. (7.18), we obtain

(4)

Next, by substituting the values of the fixed-end shears and moments from Eq. (2) into Eq. (7.19), we obtain the fixed-end force vector for the released member 3 as

\(Q_{f3}=\left[\begin{matrix}60-\frac{3(-80)}{2(8)} \\ 80-\frac{1}{2} (-80) \\60+\frac{3(-80)}{2(8)}\\0 \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix} 75 \\ 120\\45\\0\end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 6 \\ 2 \\7\\8 \end{matrix}\) (5)

The relevant elements of \(k_{3}\) and \(Q_{f3}\) are stored in S and \(P_{f}\) , respectively, using the member code numbers 6, 2, 7, 8. The completed structure stiffness matrix S, and the \(P_{f}\) vector containing the structure fixed-joint forces due to member loads, are shown in Figs. 7.9(c) and (d), respectively.

Structure Fixed-Joint Forces Due to Support Displacements: From the analytical model given in Fig. 7.9(b), we observe that the given support displacements can be expressed as

\(d_{s6} = −0.045 m d_{s7} = −0.015 m\)

As members 2 and 3 are attached to the supports that undergo displacements, we compute, for these members, the fixed-end forces due to support displacements, and add them to the previously formed \(P_{f}\) vector due to member loads.

Member 2 Using the member code numbers 5, 1, 6, 2, we form its fixed-end displacement vector due to support displacements, as

\(u_{fs2}=\left[\begin{matrix} u_{fs1} \\u_{fs2} \\ u_{fs3}\\u_{fs4} \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 5 \\1\\6\\2 \end{matrix} =\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\d_{s6}\\0 \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix}0 \\ 0 \\ -0.045\\0 \end{matrix} \right] m\)

Next, using the member stiffness matrix from Eq. (1) and the member stiffness relationship \(Q_{fs} = ku_{fs}\), we evaluate the fixed-end force vector due to support displacements, as

\(Q_{fs2}=k_{2}u_{fs2}=\left[\begin{matrix} 167.34 & 669.38& −167.34& 669.38 \\ 669.38& 3,570& −669.38& 1,785\\ −167.34 &−669.38& 167.34& −669.38 \\669.38 & 1,785& −669.38& 3,570 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix}0 \\ 0 \\ -0.045\\0 \end{matrix} \right]\)

\(=\left[\begin{matrix}\underset{…………..}{7.53} \\ \underset{…………..}{30.122} \\\underset{…………..}{-7.53}\\{30.122} \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 5 \\1 \\ 6\\2 \end{matrix}\)

The relevant elements of \(Q_{fs2}\) are now added into the previously formed \(P_{f}\), using the member code numbers, as indicated in Fig. 7.9(e).

Member 3 Based on the member code numbers 6, 2, 7, 8, its fixed-end displacement vector, due to support displacements, is written as

\(u_{fs3}=\left[\begin{matrix} u_{fs1} \\u_{fs2} \\ u_{fs3}\\u_{fs4} \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 6 \\2\\7\\8 \end{matrix} =\left[\begin{matrix} d_{s6} \\ 0 \\d_{s7}\\0 \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix} -0.045 \\ 0 \\ -0.015\\0 \end{matrix} \right] m\)

Using \(k_{3}\) from Eq. (4), we calculate

\(Q_{fs3}=k_{3}u_{fs3}=\left[\begin{matrix} 41.836 &334.69& −41.836 &0\\334.69& 2,677.5& −334.69& 0\\ −41.836 &−334.69& 41.836& 0 \\0 & 0& 0& 0 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} -0.045 \\ 0 \\ -0.015\\0 \end{matrix} \right]\)

\(=\left[\begin{matrix}\underset{…………..}{-1.2551} \\ \underset{…………..}{-10.041} \\{1.2551}\\{0} \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 6 \\2 \\ 7\\8 \end{matrix}\)

The pertinent elements of \(Q_{fs3}\) are stored in \(P_{f}\) using the member code numbers. The completed structure fixed-joint force vector \(P_{f}\), due to member loads and support displacements, is given in Fig. 7.9(e).

Joint Load Vector: Since no external loads are applied to the joints of the beam, its joint load vector is 0; that is

P = 0

Joint Displacements: By substituting the numerical values of P, \(P_{f}\), and S into Eq. (5.109), we write the stiffness relations for the entire beam as

\(P − P_{f} = Sd\)

\(\left[\begin{matrix}0 \\0 \end{matrix} \right]- \left[\begin{matrix}30.122 \\60.081 \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix}-30.122 \\-60.081 \end{matrix} \right]=\begin{bmatrix} 7,140 & 1,785 \\ 1,785& 6,247.5 \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} d_{1} \\d_{2} \end{matrix} \right]\)

By solving these equations, we determine the joint displacements to be

\(d=\left[\begin{matrix}−1.9541\\−9.0585 \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \times 10^{-3}rad\)

Member End Displacements and End Forces:

Member 1 Using the member code numbers 3, 4, 5, 1, we write its end displacement
vector as

\(u_{1}=\left[\begin{matrix} u_{1} \\u_{2} \\u_{3}\\u_{4} \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 3\\4\\5\\1 \end{matrix} =\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\0\\0\\d_{1} \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\0 \\0\\-1.9541 \end{matrix} \right]\times 10^{-3}\) (6)

The member end forces can now be calculated using the member stiffness relationship \(Q = ku + Q_{f}\) (Eq. (5.4)). Thus, using \(k_{1}\) from Eq. (1), \(Q_{f1}\) from Eq. (3), and \(u_{1}\) from Eq. (6), we calculate

\(Q_{1}=k_{1}u_{1}+Q_{f1}=\left[\begin{matrix}58.692 kN \\76.512 kN\cdot m\\\underset{…………}{61.308 kN}\\−86.976 kN \cdot m \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix}3 \\ 4\\5\\1 \end{matrix}\)

The end forces for member 1 are depicted in Fig. 7.9(f). To generate the support reaction vector R, we store the pertinent elements of \(Q_{1}\) in R, using the member code numbers, as shown in Fig. 7.9(g).
Member 2

\(u_{2}=\left[\begin{matrix} u_{1} \\u_{2} \\u_{3}\\u_{4} \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 5\\1\\6\\2 \end{matrix} =\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\d_{1}\\d_{s6}\\d_{2} \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix}0\\−1.9541\\−45\\−9.0585 \end{matrix} \right]\times 10^{-3}\) (7)

Note that the support displacement \(d_{s6}\) is included in the foregoing end displacement vector for member 2. Using \(k_{2}\) from Eq. (1), \(Q_{f2}\) from Eq. (3), and \(u_{2}\) from Eq. (7), we determine

\(Q_{2}=k_{2}u_{2}+Q_{f2}=\left[\begin{matrix}\underset{…………}{60.159 kN} \\\underset{………….}{86.976 kN \cdot m} \\\underset{…………}{59.841 kN}\\{−85.705 kN \cdot m } \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix}5 \\ 1\\6\\2 \end{matrix}\)

Member 3

\(u_{3}=\left[\begin{matrix} u_{1} \\u_{2} \\u_{3}\\u_{4} \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 6\\2\\7\\8 \end{matrix} =\left[\begin{matrix}d_{s6} \\d_{2}\\d_{s7}\\0 \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix}−45\\−9.0585\\−15\\0 \end{matrix} \right]\times 10^{-3}\) (8)

The rotation, \(u_{4}\), of the released end of this member, if desired, can be evaluated using Eq. (7.20) \(u_{4}=\frac{3}{2L}(-u_{1}+u_{3})-\frac{1}{2}u_{2}-\frac{L}{4EI}FM_{e}\) . Finally, using \(k_{3}\) from Eq. (4), \(Q_{f3}\) from Eq. (5), and \(u_{3}\) from Eq. (8), we calculate the member end forces as

\(Q_{3}=k_{3}u_{3}+Q_{f3}=\left[\begin{matrix}\underset{…………}{70.713 kN} \\\underset{………….}{85.705 kN \cdot m} \\\underset{…………}{49.287 kN}\\{0} \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix}6 \\ 2\\7\\8\end{matrix}\)

The member end forces are shown in Fig. 7.9(f ).

Support Reactions: The completed reaction vector R is shown in Fig. 7.9(g), and the support reactions are depicted on a line diagram of the beam in Fig. 7.9(h).
