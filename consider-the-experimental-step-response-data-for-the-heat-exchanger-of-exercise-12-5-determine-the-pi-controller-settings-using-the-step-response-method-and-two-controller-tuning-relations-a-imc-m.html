Guest Question.

Consider the experimental step response data for the heat exchanger of . Determine the PI controller settings using the step response method and two controller tuning relations:

(a) IMC method with \(\tau_{c}=\tau / 3\)

(b) Z-N settings in Table \(12.7\).

Which controller provides the more conservative controller settings?

Solution

From the solution to , the process reaction curve method yields,

\(K=1.65, \theta=1.7 min , \tau=5.5 min\)

a) IMC method:

From Table 12.1, Controller \(G\) with \(\tau_{ c }=\tau / 3\) :

\(\begin{aligned}&K_{c}=\frac{1}{K} \frac{\tau}{\tau_{c}+\theta}=\frac{1}{1.65} \frac{5.5}{(5.5 / 3)+1.7}=0.94 \\&\tau_{I}=\tau=5.5 min\end{aligned}\)

b) Ziegler-Nichols settings:

\(G(s)=\frac{1.65 e^{-1.7 s}}{5.5 s+1}\)

First, determine the stability limits; the characteristic equation is:

\(1+G_{c} G=0\)

Substitute the Padé approximation,

\(e^{-s} \approx \frac{1-0.85 s}{1+0.85 s}\)

into the characteristic equation:

\(0=1+G_{c} G=1+\frac{1.65 K_{c}(1-0.85 s)}{4.675 s^{2}+6.35 s+1}\)

Rearrange,

\(4.675 s^{2}+\left(6.35-1.403 K_{c}\right) s+1+1.65 K_{c}=0\)

Substitute \(s=j \omega_{u}\) at \(K_{c}=K_{c u}\) :

\(-4.675 \omega_{u}^{2}+j\left(6.35-1.403 K_{c u}\right) \omega_{u}+1+1.65 K_{c u}=0+ j 0\)

Equate real and imaginary coefficients,

\(\left(6.35-1.403 K_{c u}\right) \omega_{u}=0\)

\(1+1.65 K_{c u}-4.675 \omega_{u}^{2}=0\)

Ignoring \(\omega_{u}=0\), the approximate values are:

\(K_{c u}=4.53\) and \(\omega_{u}=1.346 rad / min\)

\(P_{u}=\frac{2 \pi}{\omega_{u}}=4.67 min\)

The Z-N PI settings from Table \(12.7\) are:

\(K_{c}=2.04 \text { and } \tau_{I}=3.89 min \quad \text { (approximate) }\)

Note that the values of \(K_{c u}\) and \(\omega_{u}\) are approximate due to the Padé approximation. By using Simulink, more accurate values can be obtained by trial and error. For this case, no Padé approximation is needed and:

\(K_{c u}=3.76 \quad P_{u}=5.9 min\)

The Z-N PI settings from Table \(12.7\) are:

\(K_{c}=1.69 \quad \tau_{I}=4.92 min \quad \text { (more accurate) }\)

Compared to the Z-N settings, the IMC method setting gives a smaller \(K_{c}\) and a larger \(\tau_{I}\), and therefore provides more conservative controller settings.
