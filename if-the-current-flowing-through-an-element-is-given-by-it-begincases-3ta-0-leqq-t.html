Guest Question.

If the current flowing through an element is given by

\(i(t) =\begin{cases} 3tA, & 0 & \leqq t<6\quad s \\ 18A, & 6 & \leqq t<10\quad s \\ -12A, & 10 & \leqq t<15\quad s \\ 0, & & t\geqq 15\quad s \end{cases}\)

Plot the charge stored in the element over 0 < t < 20 s.

Admin
Solution

For 0 < t < 6s, assuming q(0) = 0,

\(q(t) =\int_{0}^{t} {i}dt +q(0)=\int_{0}^{t} {3t}dt +0=1.5 {t}^{2}\)

At t=6, q(6) = \(1.5(6)^{2} = 54\)

For 6 < t < 10s,

\(q(t) =\int_{6}^{t} {i}dt +q(6)=\int_{6}^{t} {18}dt +54=18t-54\)

At t=10, q(10) = 180 – 54 = 126

For 10<t<15s,

\(q(t) =\int_{10}^{t} {i}dt +q(10)=\int_{10}^{t} {-12}dt +126=-12t+246\)

At t=15, q(15) = \(-12 \times 15 + 246 = 66\)

For 15<t<20s,

\(q(t) =\int_{15}^{t} {0}dt +q(15)=66\)

Thus,

\(q(t)=\begin{cases} 1.5{t}^{2}\quad C, & 0<t<6s \\ 18t-54\quad C, & 6<t<10s \\ -12t+246\quad C, & 10<t<15s \\ 66\quad C, & 15<t<20s \end{cases}\)
