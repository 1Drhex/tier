Guest Question.

Finding the performance of a noninverting amplifier with series-shunt feedback The oninverting amplifier shown in Fig. 10.7(b) has \(R_{ L }=10 k \Omega \text { and } R_{ s }=5 k \Omega\). The feedback resistors are \( R_{1}=10 k \Omega \text { and } R_{ F }=90 k \Omega \text {. } \) The op-amp parameters are \( R_{ i }=2 M \Omega \text { and } R_{ o }=75 \Omega \), and the open-loop voltage gain is \( \mu_{ g }=2 \times 10^{5} \).

(a) Determine the input resistance seen by the source \( R_{ if }=v_{ s } / i_{ s } \), the output resistance \( R_{ of } \), and the closed-loop voltage gain \( A_{ f }=v_{ o } / v_{ s } \).
(b) Use PSpice/SPICE to verify your results.

Admin
Solution

\(R_{ L }=10 k \Omega, R_{ s }=5 k \Omega, R_{1}=10 k \Omega, R_{ F }=90 k \Omega, R_{ i }=2 M \Omega, R_{ o }=75 \Omega, \text { and } \mu_{ g }=2 \times 10^{5}.\) Replacing the op-amp by its equivalent circuit gives the amplifier shown in Fig. 10.16(a).

(a) The steps in analyzing the feedback network are as follows:

Step 1. \( R_{1} \text { and } R_{ F } \), which constitute the feedback network as shown in Fig. 10.16(a), produce a feedback voltage \( v_{ f } \)proportional to the output voltage \( v_{ o } \).

Step 2. The amplifier uses series-shunt feedback. Thus, A must be in V/V; \(A=\mu_{ g }=2 \times 10^{5}\).

Step 3. The effect of the feedback network at the input side is taken into account by short-circuiting the shunt feedback at the output side. Similarly, the effect at the output side is taken into account by severing the series feedback at the input side. We ground \(R_{ F }\)on side 2 and sever the feedback circuit on side 1 from \( R_{ i } \). This modification is shown in Fig. 10.16(b). Then

\(\begin{aligned}&R_{ x }=R_{1}\left\|R_{ F }=10 k \right\| 90 k =9 k \Omega \\&R_{ y }=R_{1}+R_{ F }=10 k +90 k =100 k \Omega\end{aligned}\)

 

Step 4. If we represent the amplifier of Fig. 10.16(b) by an equivalent voltage amplifier, the input resistance is

\( R_{ ie }=R_{ s }+R_{ i }+\left(R_{1} \| R_{ F }\right)=R_{ s }+R_{ i }+R_{ x }=5 k +2 M +9 k =2014 k \Omega \)

and the output resistance is

\(R_{ oe }=R_{ o }\left\|\left(R_{1}+R_{ F }\right)\right\| R_{ L }=75\|(10 k +90 k )\| 10 k =74.4 \Omega\)

From Eq. (10.52), the modified open-loop gain \( A_{ e } \) is

\(A_{ e }=\frac{v_{ o }}{v_{ c 1}}=\frac{R_{ y } \| R_{ L }}{\left(R_{ y } \| R_{ L }\right)+R_{ o }} \times \frac{R_{ i }}{R_{ i }+R_{ x }+R_{ s }} A\) (10.52)

 

\(\begin{aligned}A_{ e } &=\frac{\left(R_{1}+R_{ F }\right) \| R_{ L }}{\left(R_{1}+R_{ F }\right) \| R_{ L }+R_{ o }} \times \frac{R_{ i }}{R_{ s }+R_{ i }+\left(R_{1} \| R_{ F }\right)} A \\&=\frac{[(10 k +90 k ) \| 10 k ] \times 2000 k \times 2 \times 10^{5}}{[(10 k +90 k ) \| 10 k +75](5 k +2000 k +9 k )}=1.9698 \times 10^{5}\end{aligned}\)

 

Step 5. From Fig. 10.16(c), the feedback factor β is given by

\(\beta=\left.\frac{v_{ f }}{v_{ o }}\right|_{i_{ f }=0}=\frac{R_{1}}{R_{1}+R_{ F }}=\frac{10 k }{10 k +90 k }=0.1 V / V\)

Step 6. The input resistance (seen by the source) with feedback is

\(R_{ if }=\frac{v_{ s }}{i_{ s }}=R_{ ie }\left(1+\beta A_{ e }\right)=2014 k \times\left(1+0.1 \times 1.9698 \times 10^{5}\right)=39.67 G \Omega\)

Step 7. The output resistance with feedback is

\(R_{ of }=\frac{R_{ oe }}{1+\beta A_{ e }}=\frac{74.4}{1+0.1 \times 1.9698 \times 10^{5}}=3.778 m \Omega\)

Step 8. The closed-loop voltage gain \( A_{ f } \) is

\( A_{ f }=\frac{v_{ o }}{v_{ s }}=\frac{A_{ e }}{1+\beta A_{ e }}=\frac{1.9698 \times 10^{5}}{1+0.1 \times 1.9698 \times 10^{5}}=9.999 V / V \)

 

which is very close to the closed-loop gain we would get if we were to use Eq. (3.18):

\(A_{ f }=\frac{v_{ O }}{v_{ S }}=1+\frac{R_{ F }}{R_{1}}\) (3.18)

 

\(A_{ f }=1+\frac{R_{ F }}{R_{1}}=1+\frac{90 k }{10 k }=10\)

(b) The series-shunt feedback circuit for PSpice simulation is shown in Fig. 10.17. The results of PSpice simulation are shown below, with hand calculations to the right:

\(\begin{array}{ll}V(5) / V S=9.999 E +00=9.999 & A_{ f }=9.99 \\\text { INPUT RESISTANCE AT VS=3.967E+10=39.67 G}\Omega & R_{ if }=39.67 G \Omega \\\text { OUTPUT RESISTANCE AT V(5)=3.776E-02=37.7 } m \Omega & R_{ of }=37.8 m \Omega\end{array}\)

The PSpice results are very close to the hand-calculated values.

NOTE: The main objective of PSpice/SPICE simulation in this chapter is to verify the techniques for analyzing feedback amplifiers. To verify hand-calculated results, we will use the simple DC op-amp model shown in Fig. 3.7 and the simple π model for BJTs. If we were to run simulations using the model provided in PSpice/SPICE, the results would differ slightly.
