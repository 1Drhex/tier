Guest Question.

Determine the nominal flexural strength \(M_{n}\) of the beam shown in Fig. 5.19 with the following modification: use four No. 7 compression bars located at \(\acute{d} = 2.4\) in instead of two No. 4 compression bars located at \(\acute{d} = 2.3\) in.

Admin
Solution

The flowchart shown in Fig. 5.18 is utilized to determine \(M_{n}\).
Step 1: Determine \(A_{s,min}\).

\(A_{s,min} = 0.82 in^{2} < A_{s} = 8 × 0.60 = 4.80 in^{2}\)(see Step 1 in )

Step 2: Determine \(β_{1}\).

\(β_{1} = 0.85 \) for \(\acute{f}_{c} = 4,000 psi\) (see Step 2 in ).

Step 3: Determine the depth of equivalent stress block a. Assuming that the compression steel yields, the depth of the equivalent stress block a is determined by Eq. (5.19c):

\(a=\frac{\left(A_{s}-A_{s}^{\prime}\right) f_{y}}{0.85 f_{c}^{\prime} b}\)

 

\(a =\frac{(A_{s} − \acute{A}_{s})f_{y}}{0.85 \acute{f}_{c} b} = \frac{(4.80 − 2.40) × 60}{0.85 × 4 × 12} = 3.5 in\)

Step 4: Determine neutral axis depth c.

\(c = \frac{a}{β_{1}}= \frac{3.5}{0.85} = 4.1 \)in

Step 5: Determine\( \acute{d}_{y}\). Because Grade 60 reinforcement is specified in , use Eq.(5.18)

\(d_{y}^{\prime}=0.31 c\).

to check the assumption that the compression reinforcement yields:

\(\acute{d}_{y} = 0.31c = 0.31 × 4.1 = 1.3 in \)

Thus, the compression reinforcement layer located 2.4 in from the extreme compression fiber does not yield because \(\acute{d} > \acute{d}_{y}\).
Step 6: Determine the revised neutral axis depth c. Equation (5.26c)

\(c=\frac{-b_{1} \pm \sqrt{b_{1}^{2}+348 a_{1} A_{s}^{\prime} d^{\prime}}}{2 a_{1}}\).

is used to determine the revised neutral axis depth c:

\(c = \frac{−b1 ±\sqrt{b^{2}_{1} + 348a_{1} \acute{A}_{s}\acute{d}}}{2a_{1}a_{1}}\).

\(a_{1}=0.85 f_{c}^{\prime} b \beta_{1}=0.85 \times 4 \times 12 \times 0.85=34.7\).

\(b_{1} = 87\acute{A}_{s} − A_{s} f_{y} = (87 × 2.40) − (4.80 ×60) = −79.2 \)
\(c = \frac{79.2 +\sqrt{(−79.2)^{2} + (348 × 34.7 × 2.40 ×2.4)}}{2 × 34.7} = 5.1 \) in

Step 7: Determine the stress in the compression reinforcement\( \acute{f }_{s}\). Equation (5.24)

\(f_{s}^{\prime}=0.0030 E_{s}\left(1-\frac{d^{\prime}}{c}\right)\).

is used to determine \(\acute{f }_{s}\):

\(\acute{f }_{s} = 0.003E_{s}(1 − \frac{\acute{d }}{c}) = 0.0030 × 29,000(1 − \frac{2.4}{5.1}) = 46.1\) ksi

Step 8: Determine \(ε_{t}\). The strain εt is determined by similar triangles:

\(\frac{c}{0.0030} =\frac{ d_{t} − c}{ε_{t}}or ε_{t} = 0.0030 (\frac{d_{t}}{c} − 1)\)

Therefore,

\(ε_{t} = 0.0030(\frac{21.6}{5.1 }− 1)= 0.0097 > 0.0040\)

Because \( ε_{t} > 0.0040\), the maximum reinforcement requirement of ACI 10.3.5 is satisfied. Also, the section is tension-controlled because \(ε_{t} > 0.0050 \) (ACI 10.3.4).
Verify that the tension reinforcement located 19.7 in from the extreme compression fiber yields:

\(\frac{ε_{s}}{19.7 − 5.1} = \frac{0.0030}{5.1}\) or \(ε_{s} = 0.0086 > ε_{y} = 0.00207\)

Step 9: Determine the revised depth of the equivalent stress block a.

\(a = β_{1}c = 0.85 × 5.1 = 4.3\) in

Step 10: Determine the nominal flexural strength \(M_{n}\). Equation (5.27)

\(M_{n}=0.85 f_{c}^{\prime} a b\left(d-\frac{a}{2}\right)+A_{s}^{\prime} f_{s}^{\prime}\left(d-d^{\prime}\right)\).

is used to determine \(M_{n}\):

\(M_{n} = 0.85 \acute{f }_{c} ab (d −\frac{ a}{2})+ \acute{A}_{s} \acute{f}_{s}(d − \acute{d})\)
\(=[0.85 × 4,000 × 4.3 × 12 ×(20.6 −\frac{ 4.3}{2})+ 2.40 × 46,100 × (20.6 − 2.4)]/12,000= 269.7 + 167.8 = 437.5\) ft kips

Comments
Comparing the results from and 5.5, it is evident that the depth of the neutral axis c determined in step 6 of this example is less than that determined in step 4 of . A.

reduction in the neutral axis depth occurs whenever the compression steel does not yield (\(\acute{ε}_{s} < ε_{y}\)).
It is also evident that increasing \(\acute{A}_{s}\) increases \(ε_{t}\) (approximately an 80% increase in these examples; see Fig. 5.20). In general, this can lead to larger values of the strength reduction factor φ.
The sections in these examples are tension-controlled, so φ = 0.90 in both cases; however, increasing \(\acute{A}_{s}\) in certain situations can transform a section in the transition zone to one that is tension-controlled.
Also, an increase in \(\acute{A}_{s}\) increases \(M_{n}\). As expected, the increase is not linear: Approximately a 5% increase in \(M_{n}\) is realized when \(\acute{A}_{s}\) is increased by a factor of 6.
