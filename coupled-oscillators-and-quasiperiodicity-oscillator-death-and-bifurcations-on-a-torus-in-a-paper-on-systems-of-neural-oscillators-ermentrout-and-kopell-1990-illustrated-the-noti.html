Coupled Oscillators and Quasiperiodicity

(“Oscillator death” and bifurcations on a torus) In a paper on systems of neural oscillators, Ermentrout and Kopell (1990) illustrated the notion of “oscillator death” with the following model:

\(\dot{\theta}_{1}=\omega_{1}+\sin \theta_{1} \cos \theta_{2}, \quad \dot{\theta}_{2}=\omega_{2}+\sin \theta_{2} \cos \theta_{1},\)

where \(\omega_{1}, \omega_{2} \geq 0\).

a) Sketch all the qualitatively different phase portraits that arise as \(\omega_{1}, \omega_{2}\) vary.

b) Find the curves in \(\omega_{1}, \omega_{2}\) parameter space along which bifurcations occur, and classify the various bifurcations.

c) Plot the stability diagram in \(\omega_{1}, \omega_{2}\) parameter space.

Admin
Solution

\(\dot{\theta}_{1}=\omega_{1}+\sin \left(\theta_{1}\right) \cos \left(\theta_{2}\right) \quad \dot{\theta}_{2}=\omega_{2}+\sin \left(\theta_{2}\right) \cos \left(\theta_{1}\right) \quad \omega_{1}, \omega_{2} \geq 0\)

a)

Let \(\phi_{1}=\theta_{1}-\theta_{2}\), which corresponds to the phase difference of the two oscillators.

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{\phi}_{1} &=\dot{\theta}_{1}-\dot{\theta}_{2}=\omega_{1}+\sin \left(\theta_{1}\right) \cos \left(\theta_{2}\right)-\omega_{2}-\sin \left(\theta_{2}\right) \cos \left(\theta_{1}\right) \\&=\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}+\sin \left(\theta_{1}-\theta_{2}\right)=\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}+\sin \left(\phi_{1}\right)\end{aligned}\)

The fixed points correspond to phase-locked solutions of the original system; i.e., periodic trajectories on the torus.

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{\phi}_{1}=\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}+\sin \left(\phi_{1}\right)=0 \\&\phi_{1}=2 \pi-\arcsin \left(\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}\right) \quad \pi+\arcsin \left(\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}\right) \quad\left|\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}\right| \leq 1\end{aligned}\)

A graphical approach for \(\omega_{1}-\omega_{2} \geq 0\) (and similarly for \(\omega_{1}-\omega_{2} \leq 0\) ) shows

Next, we’ll look at \(\phi_{2}=\theta_{1}+\theta_{2}\), the fixed points of which correspond to periodic trajectories on the torus running in opposite directions, in contrast to the fixed points of \(\phi_{1} \cdot\left(\dot{\theta}_{1}+\dot{\theta}_{2}=0 \Rightarrow \dot{\theta}_{1}=-\dot{\theta}_{2}\right)\)

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{\phi}_{2} &=\dot{\theta}_{1}+\dot{\theta}_{2}=\omega_{1}+\sin \left(\theta_{1}\right) \cos \left(\theta_{2}\right)+\omega_{2}+\sin \left(\theta_{2}\right) \cos \left(\theta_{1}\right) \\&=\omega_{1}+\omega_{2}+\sin \left(\theta_{1}+\theta_{2}\right)=\omega_{1}+\omega_{2}+\sin \left(\phi_{2}\right)\end{aligned}\)

The fixed points are

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{\phi}_{2}=\omega_{1}+\omega_{2}+\sin \left(\phi_{2}\right)=0 \\&\phi_{2}=2 \pi-\arcsin \left(\omega_{1}+\omega_{2}\right) \quad \pi+\arcsin \left(\omega_{1}+\omega_{2}\right) \quad \omega_{1}+\omega_{2} \leq 1\end{aligned}\)

A graphical approach for \(\omega_{1}-\omega_{2} \geq 0\) is the same as previously, except this time there is no \(\omega_{1}+\omega_{2} \leq 0\) case to consider because \(\omega_{1}, \omega_{2} \geq 0\).

Now we have to go through all the possible cases for \(\omega_{1}\) and \(\omega_{2}\) and the existence of fixed points in the \(\left(\phi_{1}, \phi_{2}\right)\) system and see what happens in the \(\left(\theta_{1}, \theta_{2}\right)\) system. (Actually we only show graphs from about half the regions due to symmetry.)

Fixed points

\(\left(\theta_{1}, \theta_{2}\right)=\left(\frac{\pi}{12}, \frac{13 \pi}{12}\right),\left(\frac{5 \pi}{12}, \frac{17 \pi}{12}\right),\left(\frac{7 \pi}{12}, \frac{7 \pi}{12}\right),\left(\frac{11 \pi}{12}, \frac{11 \pi}{12}\right),\left(\frac{13 \pi}{12}, \frac{\pi}{12}\right),\left(\frac{17 \pi}{12}, \frac{5 \pi}{12}\right),\left(\frac{19 \pi}{12}, \frac{19 \pi}{12}\right)\)

Stable and unstable limit cycles are present.

Fixed points

\(\left(\theta_{1}, \theta_{2}\right)=\left(\frac{\pi}{4}, \frac{5 \pi}{4}\right),\left(\frac{3 \pi}{4}, \frac{3 \pi}{4}\right),\left(\frac{5 \pi}{4}, \frac{\pi}{4}\right),\left(\frac{7 \pi}{4}, \frac{7 \pi}{4}\right)\)

In this phase portrait, the fixed points are about to disappear in an infinite-period bifurcation if we view the phase portrait as on a torus. In their places will be limit cycles.

Fixed points

\(\begin{aligned}&\left(\theta_{1}, \theta_{2}\right)=\left(\frac{0 \pi}{2}, \frac{0 \pi}{2}\right),\left(\frac{0 \pi}{2}, \frac{2 \pi}{2}\right),\left(\frac{0 \pi}{2}, \frac{4 \pi}{2}\right),\left(\frac{1 \pi}{2}, \frac{1 \pi}{2}\right),\left(\frac{1 \pi}{2}, \frac{3 \pi}{2}\right),\left(\frac{2 \pi}{2}, \frac{0 \pi}{2}\right), \\&\left(\frac{2 \pi}{2}, \frac{2 \pi}{2}\right),\left(\frac{2 \pi}{2}, \frac{4 \pi}{2}\right),\left(\frac{3 \pi}{2}, \frac{1 \pi}{2}\right),\left(\frac{3 \pi}{2}, \frac{3 \pi}{2}\right),\left(\frac{4 \pi}{2}, \frac{0 \pi}{2}\right),\left(\frac{4 \pi}{2}, \frac{2 \pi}{2}\right),\left(\frac{4 \pi}{2}, \frac{4 \pi}{2}\right)\end{aligned}\)

Fixed points

\(\left(\theta_{1}, \theta_{2}\right)=\left(\frac{0 \pi}{2}, \frac{3 \pi}{2}\right),\left(\frac{2 \pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}\right),\left(\frac{4 \pi}{2}, \frac{3 \pi}{2}\right)\)

b)

We have saddle-node bifurcation of cycles occurring along the \(\left|\omega_{1}+\omega_{2}\right|=1\) lines and infinite-period bifurcations occurring along the \(\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}=1\) line.

c)

The oscillator death region is the lower-left triangle. Once we cross the infinite-period bifurcation \(\omega_{1}+\omega_{2}=1\) line, the limit cycles are broken by fixed points and the oscillations stop.
