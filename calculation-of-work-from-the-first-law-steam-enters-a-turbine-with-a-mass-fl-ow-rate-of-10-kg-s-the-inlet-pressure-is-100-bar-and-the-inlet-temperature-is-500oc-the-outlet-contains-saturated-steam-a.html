Calculation of Work from the First Law

Steam enters a turbine with a mass fl ow rate of 10 kg/s. The inlet pressure is 100 bar and the inlet temperature is 500ºC. The outlet contains saturated steam at 1 bar. At steady-state, calculate the power (in kW) generated by the turbine.

Admin
Solution

The steady-state energy balance is given by Equation (2.20):

 

\(0=\sum_{\text {in }} \dot{m}_{\text {in }} \hat{h}_{\text {in }}-\sum_{\text {out }} \dot{m}_{\text {out }} \hat{h}_{\text {out }}+\dot{Q}+\dot{W}_{s}\) (2.20)

 

\(0=\sum_{\text {in }} \dot{m}_{ in } \hat{h}_{ in }-\sum_{\text {out }} \dot{m}_{ out } \hat{h}_{ out }+\dot{Q}+\dot{W}_{s}\) (E2.5A)

 

For a turbine, there is one stream in and one stream out. Moreover, heat dissipation is negligible, since \(\dot{Q}<<\dot{W}_{s}\). If we label the inlet stream “1” and the outlet stream “2,” Equation (E2.5A) becomes:

 

\(0=\dot{m}_{1} \hat{h}_{1}-\dot{m}_{2} \hat{h}_{2}+\dot{W}_{s}\) (E2.5B)

 

At steady-state, the mass balance can be written as:

 

\(\dot{m}_{1}=\dot{m}_{2}=\dot{m}\) (E2.5C)

 

Plugging in Equation (E2.5C) into (E2.5B) gives:

 

\(\dot{W}_{s}=\dot{m}\left(\hat{h}_{2}-\hat{h}_{1}\right)\) (E2.5D)

 

We can look up values for specifi c enthalpy from the steam tables. For state 2, we use saturated steam at 1 bar (= 100 kPa) :

 

\(\hat{h}_{2}=2675.5[ kJ / kg ]\)

 

while state 1 is superheated steam at 500ºC and 100 bar (= 10 Mpa) :

 

\(\hat{h}_{1}=3373.6[ k J / kg ]\)

 

Plugging these numerical values into Equation (E2.5D) gives:

 

\(\dot{W}_{s}=10[ kg / s ](2675.5-3373.6)[ kJ / kg ]=-6981[ kW ]\)

 

Thus, this turbine generates approximately 7 MW of power. Note the negative sign, which indicates that we are getting useful work from the system. This is the equivalent power to that delivered by approximately 70 automobiles running simultaneously.
