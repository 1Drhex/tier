Guest Question.

Determine the member end moments, the support reactions, and the horizontal deflection of joint \(F\) of the two-story frame shown in \(Fig. 16.19(a)\) by the slopedeflection method.

Admin
Solution

Degrees of Freedom From Fig. 16.19(a), we can see that the joints \(C,D,E\), and \(F\) of the frame are free to rotate, and translate in the horizontal direction. As shown in Fig. 16.19(b), the horizontal displacement of the first-story joints \(C\) and \(D\) is designated as \(\Delta_1\), whereas the horizontal displacement of the secondstory joints \(E\) and \(F\) is expressed as \(\Delta_1 + \Delta_2\), with \(\Delta_2\) representing the displacement of the second-story joints relative to the first-story joints. Thus, the frame has six degrees of freedomâ€”that is, \(\theta_C, \theta_D, \theta_E, \theta_F ,\Delta_1\), and \(\Delta_2\).

Fixed-End Moments The nonzero fixed-end moments are

\(FEM_{CD} = FEM_{EF} = 200 k-ft\)

 

\(FEM_{DC} = FEM_{FE} = -200 k-ft\)

Chord Rotations See Fig. 16.19(b).

\(\psi_{AC} = \psi_{BD} = -\frac{\Delta_1}{20}\)

 

\(\psi_{CE} = \psi_{DF} = -\frac{\Delta_2}{20}\)

 

\(\psi_{CD} = \psi_{EF} = 0\)

Slope-Deflection Equations Using \(I_{column} = I\) and \(I_{girder} = 2I\) , we write

\(M_{AC} = 0.1EI\theta_C + 0.015EI\Delta_1\) (1)

\(M_{CA} = 0.2EI\theta_C + 0.015EI\Delta_1\) (2)

\(M_{BD} = 0.1EI\theta_D + 0.015EI\Delta_1\) (3)

\(M_{DB} = 0.2EI\theta_D + 0.015EI\Delta_1\) (4)

\(M_{CE} = 0.2EI\theta_C + 0.1EI\theta_E + 0.015EI\Delta_2\) (5)

\(M_{EC} = 0.2EI\theta_E + 0.1EI\theta_C + 0.015EI\Delta_2\) (6)

\(M_{DF} = 0.2EI\theta_D + 0.1EI\theta_F+ 0.015EI\Delta_2\) (7)

\(M_{FD} = 0.2EI\theta_F + 0.1EI\theta_D + 0.015EI\Delta_2\) (8)

\(M_{CD} = 0.2EI\theta_C + 0.1EI\theta_D + 200\) (9)

\(M_{DC} = 0.2EI\theta_D + 0.1EI\theta_C – 200\) (10)

\(M_{EF} = 0.2EI\theta_E + 0.1EI\theta_F + 200\) (11)

\(M_{FE} = 0.2EI\theta_F + 0.1EI\theta_E – 200\) (12)

Equilibrium Equations By considering the moment equilibrium of joints \(C, D, E\), and \(F\), we obtain

\(M_{CA} +M_{CD} +M_{CE} = 0\) (13)

\(M_{DB} +M_{DC} +M_{DF} = 0\) (14)

\(M_{EC} +M_{EF} = 0\) (15)

\(M_{FD} +M_{FE} = 0\) (16)

To establish the remaining two equilibrium equations, we successively pass a horizontal section just above the lower ends of the columns of each story of the frame and apply the equation of horizontal equilibrium \((\sum{F_X}=0)\) to the free body of the portion of the frame above the section. The free-body diagrams thus obtained are shown in Fig. 16.19(c) and (d). By applying the equilibrium equation \(\sum{F_X}=0\) to the top story of the frame (Fig. 16.19(c)), we obtain

\(S_{CE} + S_{DF} = 10\)

Similarly, by applying \(\sum{F_X}=0\) to the entire frame (Fig. 16.19(d)), we write

\(S_{AC} + S_{BD} = 30\)

By expressing column end shears in terms of column end moments as

\(S_{AC} =\frac{M_{AC} +M_{CA}}{20}\) \(S_{BD} =\frac{M_{BD} +M_{DB}}{20}\)

 

\(S_{CE} =\frac{M_{CE} +M_{EC}}{20}\) \(S_{DF} =\frac{M_{DF} +M_{FD}}{20}\)

and by substituting these expressions into the force equilibrium equations, we obtain

\(M_{CE} +M_{EC} +M_{DF} +M_{FD} = 200\) (17)

\(M_{AC} +M_{CA} +M_{BD} +M_{DB} = 600\) (18)

Joint Displacements Substitution of the slope-deflection equations (Eqs. (1) through (12)) into the equilibrium equations (Eqs. (13) through (18)) yields

\(0.6EI\theta_C + 0.1EI\theta_D + 0.1EI\theta_E + 0.015EI\Delta_1 + 0.015EI\Delta_2 = -200\) (19)

\(0.1EI\theta_C + 0.6EI\theta_D + 0.1EI\theta_F + 0.015EI\Delta_1 + 0.015EI\Delta_2 = 200\) (20)

\(0.1EI\theta_C + 0.4EI\theta_E + 0.1EI\theta_F + 0.015EI\Delta_2 = -200\) (21)

\(0.1EI\theta_D + 0.4EI\theta_E + 0.1EI\theta_F + 0.015EI\Delta_2 = 200\) (22)

\(0.3EI\theta_C + 0.3EI\theta_D + 0.3EI\theta_E + 0.3EI\theta_F + 0.06EI\Delta_2 = 200\) (23)

\(0.1EI\theta_C + 0.1EI\theta_D + 0.02EI\Delta_1 = 200\) (24)

By solving Eqs. (19) through (24) by the Gauss-Jordan elimination method (Appendix \(B\)), we determine

\(EI\theta_C = -812.988 k-ft^2\)

 

\(EI\theta_D = -241.556 k-ft^2\)

 

\(EI\theta_E = -789.612 k-ft^2\)

 

\(EI\theta_F = 353.248 k-ft^2\)

 

\(EI\Delta_1 = 15,272.728 k-ft^3\) or \(\Delta_1 = 0.0758 ft = 0.91 in.\longrightarrow\)

 

\(EI\Delta_2 = 10,787.878 k-ft^3\) or \(\Delta_2 = 0.0536 ft = 0.643 in.\longrightarrow\)

Thus, the horizontal deflection of joint \(F\) of the frame is as follows:

\(\Delta_F = \Delta_1 + \Delta_2 = 0.91 + 0.643 = 1.553 in. \longrightarrow\)

Member End Moments By substituting the numerical values of the joint
displacements into the slope-deflection equations (Eqs. (1) through (12)), we obtain

\(M_{AC} = 147.8 k-ft \circlearrowleft\) Ans.

\(M_{CA} = 66.5 k-ft \circlearrowleft\) Ans.

\(M_{BD} = 204.9 k-ft \circlearrowleft\) Ans.

\(M_{DB} = 180.8 k-ft \circlearrowleft\) Ans.

\(M_{CE} = -79.7 k-ft\) or \(79.7 k-ft \circlearrowright\) Ans.

\(M_{EC} = -77.4 k-ft\) or \(77.4 k-ft \circlearrowright\) Ans.

\(M_{DF} = 148.8 k-ft \circlearrowleft\) Ans.

\(M_{FD} = 208.3 k-ft \circlearrowleft\) Ans.

\(M_{CD} = 13.2 k-ft \circlearrowleft\) Ans.

\(M_{DC} = -329.6 k-ft\) or \(329.6 k-ft \circlearrowright\) Ans.

\(M_{EF} = 77.4 k-ft \circlearrowleft\) Ans.

\(M_{DC} = -208.3 k-ft\) or \(208.3 k-ft \circlearrowright\) Ans.

Back substitution of the numerical values of member end moments into the equilibrium equations yields

\(M_{CA} +M_{CD} +M_{CE} = 66.5 + 13.2 – 79.7 = 0\) Checks

\(M_{DB} +M_{DC} +M_{DF} = 180.8 – 329.6 + 148.8 = 0\) Checks

\(M_{EC} +M_{EF} = -77.4 + 77.4 = 0\) Checks

\(M_{FD} +M_{FE} = 208.3 – 208.3 = 0\) Checks

\(M_{CE} +M_{EC} +M_{DF} +M_{FD} = -79.7 – 77.4 + 148.8 + 208.3 = 200\) Checks

\(M_{AC} +M_{CA} +M_{BD} +M_{DB} = 147.8 + 66.5 + 204.9 + 180.8 = 600\) Checks

Member End Shears and Axial Forces See Fig. 16.19(e).

Support Reactions See Fig. 16.19(f ). Ans.
