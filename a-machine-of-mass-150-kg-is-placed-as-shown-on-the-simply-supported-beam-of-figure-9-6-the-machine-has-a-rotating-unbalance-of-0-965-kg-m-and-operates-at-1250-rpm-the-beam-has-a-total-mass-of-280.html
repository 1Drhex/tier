Guest Question.

A machine of mass 150 kg is placed as shown on the simply supported beam of Figure 9.6. The machine has a rotating unbalance of \(0.965 kg \cdot m\) and operates at 1250 rpm. The beam has a total mass of 280 kg, a cross-sectional moment of inertia of \(1.2 \times 10^-4 m^4\), a length of 3 m, and an elastic modulus of \(210 \times 10^9 N/m^2\). Model the beam with three degrees of freedom and use modal analysis to predict the steady-state amplitude of displacement for the point where the machine is attached.

Admin
Solution

The beam is modeled as three particles with a mass of 70 kg, as shown in Figure 9.6(b). The mass matrix for this model is

\( \pmb{M}=\begin{bmatrix} 70 & 0 & 0\\0 & 70 & 0\\0 & 0 & 220 \end{bmatrix}kg \) (a)

Flexibility influence coefficients are used to determine the flexibility matrix as

\(\pmb{A}=10^{-9}\begin{bmatrix}12.53 & 15.33 & 9.75\\15.33 & 22.29 & 15.33 \\ 9.75 & 15.33 & 12.53\end{bmatrix}m/N \) (b)

The governing differential equations are

\( \pmb{AM\ddot{X}}+\pmb{X}=\pmb{AF}\) (c)

where

\(\pmb{F}(t)=\begin{bmatrix} 0\\0\\16,500\sin130.9t \end{bmatrix}N \) (d)

The natural frequencies and normalized mode shapes are determined as the reciprocals of the square roots of the eigenvalues of \(\pmb{AM} \). They are

\(\omega _{1}=455.8 rad/s \) \(\omega _{2}=1.735\times 10^{3}rad/s \) \(\omega _{3}=4.474\times 10^{3} rad/s \) (e)

The normalized eigenvectors comprise the modal matrix \(\pmb{P}\), which is

\( \pmb{P}=\begin{bmatrix} 0.0453 & -0.0851 & -0.0707\\0.0666 & -0.4000 & 0.0908\\0.0498 & 0.0416 & -0.0182 \end{bmatrix} \) (f)

The vector \(\pmb{G}(t)\) is calculated as

\(\pmb{G}(t)=\pmb{P}^{T}\pmb{F}=\begin{bmatrix} 0.0453 & 0.0666 & 0.0498\\-0.0851 & -0.4000 & 0.0416\\-0.0707 & 0.0908 & -0.0182 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\0\\16,500\sin130.9t \end{bmatrix} \)

\(=\begin{bmatrix} 821.8\\687.0\\300.3 \end{bmatrix}\sin130.9t N(kg)^{-1/2} \) (g)

The differential equations for the principal coordinates are written by using Equation (9.24)

\(\ddot{p}_{1}+(455.8)^{2}p_{1}=821.8 \sin130.9t\) (h)

\(\ddot{p}_{2}+(1736.5)^{2}p_{2}=687.0\sin130.9t \) (i)

\( \ddot{p}_{3}+(4474)^{2}p_{3}=-300.3\sin130.9t\) (j)

\( \ddot{p}_{j}+\omega ^{2}_{j}p_{j}=g_{j}(t)\) (9.24)

The steady-state solution of

\(\ddot{p}_{i}+\omega ^{2}_{i}p_{i}=F_{i}\sin\omega t \) (k)

is

\(p_{i}(t)=\frac{F_{i}}{\omega ^{2}_{i}-\omega ^{2}_{i}}\sin\omega t \) (l)

The steady-state solution for the principal coordinates is

\( \begin{bmatrix}p_{1}\\p_{2}\\p_{3} \end{bmatrix}=10^{-5}\begin{bmatrix} 432.0\\22.93\\-1.501 \end{bmatrix}\sin130.9t(kg)^{1/2} \) (m)

Equation (9.20) is used to determine \(x_3(t)\) as

\( x_{3}(t)=0.0498p_{1}(t)+0.0416p_{2}(t)-0.0182p_{3}(t)=2.25\times10^{-4}\sin130.9tm\) (n)

\( \pmb{X=Pp}\) (9.20)

Thus, the maximum steady-state displacement of the point on the beam where the machine is placed is 0.225 mm.
