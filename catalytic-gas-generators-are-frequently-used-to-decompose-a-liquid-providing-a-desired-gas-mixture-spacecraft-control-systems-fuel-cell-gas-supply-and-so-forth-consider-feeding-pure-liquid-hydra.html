Guest Question.

Catalytic gas generators are frequently used to decompose a liquid, providing a desired gas mixture (spacecraft control systems, fuel cell gas supply, and so forth). Consider feeding pure liquid hydrazine, \(N _{2} H _{4}\), to a gas generator, from which exits a gas mixture of \(N _{2}, H _{2}, \text { and } NH _{3}\) in chemical equilibrium at 100°C, 350 kPa. Calculate the mole fractions of the species in the equilibrium mixture.

Admin
Solution

Initially, \(2 N _{2} H _{4} \rightarrow 1 N _{2}+1 H _{2}+2 NH _{3}\)

 

\(\begin{array}{lcccc}\text { Reaction: } & N _{2}&+ & 3 H _{2} & \Leftrightarrow 2 NH _{3} & \\\text { initial } & 1& & 1 & 2 & \\\text { change } & – x& & -3 x & +2 x & \\\text { equil. } & (1- x ) && (1-3 x ) & (2+2 x ) & n _{\text {TOTAL }}=(4-2 x )\end{array}\)

 

\(K =\frac{ y _{ NH 3}^{2}}{ y _{ N 2} y _{ H 2}^{3}}\left(\frac{ P }{ P ^{0}}\right)^{-2}=\frac{(2+2 x )^{2}(4-2 x )^{2}}{(1- x )(1-3 x )^{3}}\left(\frac{350}{100}\right)^{-2}\)

 

At 100 °C = 373.2 K, for \(NH _{3}\) use A.5 \(\overline{ C }_{ P 0}=17.03 \times 2.130=36.276\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\overline{ h }_{ NH 3}^{0}=-45720+36.276(373.2-298.2)=-42999 kJ / kmol \\&\overline{ S }_{ NH 3}^{0}=192.572+36.276 \ln \frac{373.2}{298.2}=200.71 kJ / kmol K\end{aligned}\)

Using A.9,

\(\begin{aligned}&\Delta H _{100 C }^{0}=2(-42999)-1(0+2188)-3(0+2179)=-94723 kJ \\&\Delta S _{100 C }^{0}=2(200.711)-1(198.155)-3(137.196)=-208.321 kJ / K \\&\Delta G _{100 C }^{0}=\Delta H ^{0}- T \Delta S ^{0}=-94723-373.2(-208.321)=-16978 kJ\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\ln K =-\frac{\Delta G ^{0}}{\overline{ R } T }=\frac{+16978}{8.3145 \times 373.2}=5.4716 \quad \Rightarrow \quad K =237.84\)

Therefore,

\(\left[\frac{(1+x)(2-x)}{(1-3 x)}\right]^{2} \frac{1}{(1-x)(1-3 x)}=\frac{237.84 \times 3.5^{2}}{16}=182.096\)

By trial and error, x = 0.226

\(\left.\begin{array}{l}n _{ N 2}=0.774 n _{ NH 3}=2.452 \\n _{ H 2}=0.322 n _{ TOT }=3.518\end{array}\right\}\left\{\begin{array}{l}y _{ N 2}=0.2181 \\y _{ H 2}=0.0908 \\y _{ NH 3}=0.6911\end{array}\right.\)
