Guest Question.

If |p〉 and |q〉 are photon states with momentum p and q, respectively, and |R〉 and|L〉 are right-circularly and left-circularly polarized photon states, respectively, then which of the following states are possible states of two photons?

(a) \(\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} }|p〉_{1|}q〉_{2}+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} }|q〉_{1}|p〉_{2} \right)\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} }|R〉_{1}|L〉_{2}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} }|L〉_{1}|R〉_{2} \right) \)

(b) \(\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} }|p〉_{1|}q〉_{2}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} }|q〉_{1}|p〉_{2} \right)|R〉_{1}|R〉_{2}\)

(c) \(\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} }|p〉_{1|}q〉_{2}+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} }|q〉_{1}|p〉_{2} \right)\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} }|R〉_{1}|L〉_{2}+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} }|L〉_{1}|R〉_{2} \right)\)

(d) \( |p〉_{1}|q〉_{2}|R〉_{1}|L〉_{2}\)

(e) \( \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} }|p〉_{1|}q〉_{2}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} }|q〉_{1}|p〉_{2} \right)\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} }|R〉_{1}|L〉_{2}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} }|L〉_{1}|R〉_{2} \right)\)

Admin
Solution

Since photons are spin~ I particles, the two-photon state must be symmetric under exchange. Only states (c) and (e) have the property that \(\hat{P} _{12}|\psi〉=|\psi 〉\). In (c) the two-photon momentum and the two-photon polarization states me both symmetric under exchange, while in (e) they are both antisymmetric under exchange. In either case these states are symmetric under exchange when all the attributes of the photons are swapped, as is required for a state consisting of identical bosons. In contrast, the states (a) and (b) cu·e overall antisymmetric under exchange and the state (d) does not have any definite exchange symmetry. Thus the states (a), (b), and (d) are not possible two-photon states.
