Guest Question.

Consider proportional-only control of the stirred-tank heater control system in Fig. E11.13. The temperature transmitter has a span of \(50^{\circ} F\) and a zero of \(55^{\circ} F\). The nominal design conditions are \(\bar{T}=80^{\circ} F\) and \(\bar{T}_{i}=65^{\circ} F\). The controller has a gain of 5, while the gains for the control valve and current-to-pressure transducer are \(K_{v}=1.2\) (dimensionless) and \(K_{I P}=0.75 psi / mA\), respectively. The time constant for the tank is \(\tau=5\) min. The control valve and transmitter dynamics are negligible. After the set point is changed from 80 to \(85^{\circ} F\), the tank temperature eventually reaches a new steady-state value of \(84.14^{\circ} F\), which was measured with a highly accurate thermometer.

(a) What is the offset?

(b) What is the process gain \(K_{2}\) ?

(c) What is the pressure signal \(p_{t}\) to the control valve at the final steady state?

Admin
Solution
\(G_{c}(s)=K_{c}=5 ma / ma\)

Assume \(\tau_{m}=0, \tau_{v}=0\), and \(K_{l}=1\), in Fig 11.7.

a) Offset \(=T_{s p}^{\prime}(\infty)-T^{\prime}(\infty)=5^{\circ} F-4.14^{\circ} F=0.86^{\circ} F\)

b) \(\quad \frac{T^{\prime}(s)}{T_{s p}^{\prime}(s)}=\frac{K_{m} K_{c} K_{I P} K_{v}\left(\frac{K_{2}}{\tau s+1}\right)}{1+K_{m} K_{c} K_{I P} K_{v}\left(\frac{K_{2}}{\tau s+1}\right)}\)

Using the standard current range of 4-20 ma,

\(\begin{aligned}&K_{m}=\frac{20 ma -4 ma }{50^{\circ} F }=0.32 ma /{ }^{\circ} F \\&K_{v}=1.2, K_{I P}=0.75 psi / ma , \tau=5 min , T_{s p}^{\prime}(s)=\frac{5}{s} \\&T^{\prime}(s)=\frac{7.20 K_{2}}{s\left(5 s+1+1.440 K_{2}\right)} \\&T^{\prime}(\infty)=\lim _{s \rightarrow 0} s T^{\prime}(s)=\frac{7.20 K_{2}}{\left(1+1.440 K_{2}\right)} \\&T^{\prime}(\infty)=4.14^{\circ} F \quad K_{2}=3.34^{\circ} F / psi\end{aligned}\)

c) From Fig. 11.7, since \(T_{i}^{\prime}=0\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\quad P_{t}^{\prime}(\infty) K_{v} K_{2}=T^{\prime}(\infty), \quad P_{t}^{\prime}(\infty)=1.03 psi \\&\text { and } \quad P_{t}^{\prime} K_{v} K_{2}+\bar{T}_{i} K_{1}=\bar{T}, \bar{P}_{t}=3.74 psi \\&P_{t}(\infty)=\bar{P}_{t}-P_{t}^{\prime}(\infty)=4.77 psi\end{aligned}\)
