Guest Question.

A two-stage cycle replaces the refrigeration cycle of . Determine the rate of refrigeration and the coefficient of performance and compare with those of . Use \( \dot{m}_{L}=0.6 kg / s \).

Admin
Solution

Refer to Fig. 10.5 for the various state designations. Using \( T_{1}=-20^{\circ} C \text {, we find } P_{L}=133 kPa \text {. Also, } P_{H}= 1000 \) kPa. Then, (10.3) results in

\( P_{i}=\left(P_{H} P_{L}\right)^{1 / 2} \) (10.3).

\( P_{i}=\left(P_{L} P_{H}\right)^{1 / 2}=[(133)(1000)]^{1 / 2}=365 kPa \).

From Appendix D we find

\( h_{1}=235.3 kJ / kg \quad s_{1}=s_{2}=0.9332 kJ / kg \cdot K \quad h_{7}=h_{8}=105.3 kJ / kg \).

\( h_{3}=h_{4}=\left(\frac{365-360}{400-360}\right)(62.0-57.8)+57.8=58.3 kJ / kg \).

\( s_{5}=s_{6}=\left(\frac{365-360}{400-360}\right)(0.9160-0.9145)+0.9145=0.9147 kJ / kg \cdot K \).

\( h_{5}=\left(\frac{365-320}{400-320}\right)(252.3-250.6)+250.6=250.8 kJ / kg \).

\( \text { At } P_{i}=365 kPa \text { we interpolate and obtain } \).

T = 10°C s = 0.9289 kJ/kg⋅K h = 254.4 kJ/kg.
T = 20°C s = 0.9617 kJ/kg⋅K h = 263.8 kJ/kg.

This gives

\( h_{2}=\left(\frac{0.9332-0.9289}{0.9617-0.9289}\right)(263.8-254.4)+254.4=255.6 kJ / kg \).

\( \text { Also, interpolating at } P_{6}=1.0 MPa \text {, we find } \)

\( h_{6}=\left(\frac{0.9147-0.9066}{0.9428-0.9066}\right)(280.2-268.7)+268.7=271.3 kJ / kg \).

\( \text { From the above, } \dot{Q}_{E}=\dot{m}_{L}\left(h_{1}-h_{4}\right)=(0.6)(235.3-58.3)=106.2 kW \). This compares with a value of 78.0 kW from the simple refrigeration cycle of . That represents a 36 percent increase in the rate of refrigeration. The mass flux in the high-pressure stage is found from (10.5) to be

\( \dot{m}_{H}=\dot{m}_{L} \frac{h_{2}-h_{3}}{h_{5}-h_{8}}=(0.6)\left(\frac{255.6-58.3}{250.8-105.3}\right)=0.814 kg / s \).

The power input to the compressors is

\( \dot{W}_{\text {in }}=\dot{m}_{L}\left(h_{2}-h_{1}\right)+\dot{m}_{H}\left(h_{6}-h_{5}\right)=(0.6)(255.6-235.3)+(0.814)(271.3-250.8)=28.9 kW \).

The coefficient of performance is now calculated to be

\( COP _{R}=\frac{\dot{Q}_{E}}{\dot{W}_{\text {in }}}=\frac{106.2}{28.9}=3.68 \).

This compares with a value of 3.10 from the refrigeration cycle of , a 19 percent increase. The advantages of using two stages is obvious when considering the increased refrigeration and performance; the equipment is much more expensive, however, and must be justified economically.
