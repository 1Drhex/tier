Guest Question.

A wooden beam is fabricated from one 2 × 10 and two 2 × 4 pieces of dimension lumber to form the I-beam cross section shown in Figure P9.41. The I-beam will be used as a simply supported beam to carry a concentrated load P at the center of a 20-ft span. The wood has an allowable bending stress of 1,200 psi and an allowable shear stress of 90 psi. The flanges of the beam are fastened to the web with nails that can safely transmit a force of 120 lb in direct shear.
(a) If the nails are uniformly spaced at an interval of s = 4.5 in. along the span, what is the maximum concentrated load P that can be supported by the beam? Demonstrate that the maximum bending and shear stresses produced by P are acceptable.
(b) Determine the magnitude of load P that produces the allowable bending stress in the span (i.e., \(\sigma_{b}\) = 1,200 psi). What nail spacing s is required to support this load magnitude? Demonstrate that the maximum horizontal shear stresses produced by P are acceptable.

Solution

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

\(\begin{array}{lcccccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Width } b \\(\text { in. })\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Height } h \\(\text { in. })\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\( in .)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 4 & 2 & 2.667 & 6.000 & 288.000 & 290.667 \\\text { web } & 2 & 10 & 166.667 & 0.000 & 0.000 & 166.667 \\\text { bottom flange } & 4 & 2 & 2.667 & -6.000 & 288.000 & 290.667 \\\hline {\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left(\text { in } .{ }^{4}\right)=} & 748.000\end{array}\)

 

(a) Maximum concentrated load P:

\(Q=(4 \text { in. })(2 in .)(6 in .)=48 in .{ }^{3}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&q s \leq n_{f} V_{f} \\&\quad \therefore q \leq \frac{n_{f} V_{f}}{s}=\frac{(1 \text { nail })(120 lb / \text { nail })}{4.5 in .}=26.667 lb / in.\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}q &=\frac{V Q}{I} \\& \therefore V=\frac{q I}{Q}=\frac{(26.667 lb / in. )\left(748 in .^{4}\right)}{48 in .^{3}}=415.556 lb\end{aligned}\)

 

\(V=\frac{P}{2} \quad \therefore P_{\max }=831 lb\)

 

Check maximum bending and shear stresses:

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{\max }=\frac{M c}{I_{z}}=\frac{(P L / 4) c}{I_{z}}=\frac{[(831.1 lb )(240 \text { in. }) / 4](7 \text { in. })}{748 in. ^{4}}=466.7 psi \leq 1,200 psi OK \\&Q_{\max }=(2 in .)(5 in .)(2.5 in .)+(4 in. )(2 in .)(6 in .)=73 in.{ }^{3} \\&\tau_{\max }=\frac{V Q}{I_{z} t}=\frac{(P / 2) Q_{\max }}{I_{z} t}=\frac{(831.1 lb / 2)\left(73 in .^{3}\right)}{\left(748 in. ^{4}\right)(2 in .)}=20.3 psi \leq 90 psi OK\end{aligned}\)

(b) Magnitude of load P that produces the allowable bending stress in the span:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\frac{M c}{I} \leq 1,200 psi \\& \therefore M \leq \frac{(1,200 psi )\left(748 in .^{4}\right)}{7 in .}=128,228.566 lb – in .\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}M_{\max } &=\frac{P L}{4} \\& \therefore P_{\max }=\frac{4 M_{\max }}{L}=\frac{4(128,228.566 lb – in .)}{(20 ft )(12 in. / ft )}=2,137.143 lb =2,140 lb\end{aligned}\)

 

Required nail spacing s:

\(\begin{aligned}V_{\max } &=\frac{P_{\max }}{2}=\frac{2,137.143 lb }{2}=1,068.571 lb \\q &=\frac{V Q}{I}=\frac{(1,068.571 lb )\left(48 in .^{3}\right)}{748 in .^{4}}=68.571 lb / in . \\q s & \leq n_{f} V_{f} \\& \therefore s \leq \frac{n_{f} V_{f}}{q}=\frac{(1 \text { nail })(120 lb )}{68.571 lb / in .}=1.750 in.\end{aligned}\)

Check maximum shear stresses:

\(\tau_{\max }=\frac{V Q}{I_{z} t}=\frac{(P / 2) Q_{\max }}{I_{z} t}=\frac{(2,137.143 lb / 2)\left(73 in .^{3}\right)}{\left(748 in. ^{4}\right)(2 in .)}=52.143 psi \leq 90 psi\) OK
