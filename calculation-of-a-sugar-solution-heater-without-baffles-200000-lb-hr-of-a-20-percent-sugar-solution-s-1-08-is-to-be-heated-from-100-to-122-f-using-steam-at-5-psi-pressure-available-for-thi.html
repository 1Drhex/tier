Guest Question.

Calculation of a Sugar-solution Heater without Baffles. 200,000 lb/hr of a 20 percent sugar solution (s = 1.08) is to be heated from 100 to 122 °F using steam at 5 psi pressure. Available for this service is a 12 in ID 1–2 exchanger without baffles having 76 ¾ in OD, 16 BWG tubes 16’0” long laid out on a 1-in square pitch. The bundle is arranged for two passes. Can the exchanger provide a 0.003 dirt factor without exceeding a 10.0 psi solution pressure drop?

Admin
Solution

Shell-Side

ID = 12 in

Baffle space = half circles

 

Passes = 1

 Tube-Side

Number and length = 76 tubes, 16’0”

OD, BWG, pitch = ¾ in, 18 BWG, 1-in square pitch

Passes = 2

Heat Balance:

Cold Sugar Solution, \(t_{avg}=\frac{100+122}{2}\) = 111°F

\(C_{sugar}\) = 0.30 Btu/(lb · °F) at 111 °F

\(C_{H_2O}\) ≈ 1.0 Btu/(lb · °F) at 111 °F

Therefore, \(C = x_{sugar} ·c_{sugar} + x_{H_2O} ·c_{H_2O}\); x = mass fraction

= (0.2)(0.30) + (0.8)(1.0)

= 0.86 Btu/(lb · °F)

Q = (200,000)(0.86)(122 – 100) = 3,790,000 Btu/hr

Hot Steam, \(H_L\) = 960.1 Btu/lb at T = 228°F

Q = \(\dot{M}H_L\) = (3,950)(960.1) = 3,790,000 Btu/hr

(2) Δ\(T^*\):

\(LMTD=\frac{\Delta t_2-\Delta t_1}{\ln (\Delta t_2/\Delta t_1)}=\frac{106-128}{\ln (106/128)}=116.7°F\)

\(R=\frac{T_1-T_2}{t_2-t_1}=\frac{0}{22}=0\)

\(P=\frac{t_2-t_1}{T_1-t_1}=\frac{22}{228-100}=0.172\)

\(F_T=\left\lgroup\frac{\sqrt{R^2+1} }{R-1} \right\rgroup\frac{\ln \left[(1-P)/(1-RP)\right] }{\ln \left[(2-P(R+1-\sqrt{R^2+1}))/(2-P(R+1+\sqrt{R^2+1} ))\right] }\)

\(=\left\lgroup\frac{\sqrt{0+1} }{0-1} \right\rgroup\frac{\ln \left[(1-0.172)/(1-(0)(0.172))\right] }{\ln \left[(1-0.172(0+1-\sqrt{0+1} ))/(2-0.172(0+1+\sqrt{0+1}) )\right] }\)

= 1

Whenever R = 0, \(F_T\) must be 1. Therefore, ΔT = LMTD at R = 0.

Δ\(T^*\) = 116.7°F

(3) \(T_{avg} and t_{avg}: t_{avg}\) was obtained in step (1) and \(T_{avg}\) is simply the given T.

 

Hot Fluid (steam) – Tube-Side (t)

(4) Flow Area, \(a_t\) :

\(a^{'}_t\) = 0.302 \(in^2\) ⇐ [Table AT.8]

\(a_t=\frac{N_ta^{'}_t}{144n}\)

\(=\frac{(76)(0.302)}{144(2)}=0.0797ft^2\)

 Cold Fluid (Sugar Solution) – Shell-Side (s)

(4′) Flow Area, \(a_s\):

\(a_s\) = (area of shell) – (area of tubes)

\(=\left\lgroup\frac{1}{144}\right\rgroup\left\lgroup\frac{D^2_s\pi}{4}-\frac{N_td^2_o\pi}{4}\right\rgroup\)

 

\(=\left\lgroup\frac{1}{144}\right\rgroup\left\lgroup\frac{(12)^2\pi}{4}-\frac{(76)(0.75)^2\pi}{4}\right\rgroup\)

=0.55\(ft^2\)

 

(5) Mass Velocity, \(G_t\) :

\(G_t=\frac{W}{a_t}=\frac{3,950}{0.0797}\)

\(=49,500lb/(hr\cdot ft^2)\)

 (5′) Mass Velocity, \(G_s\) :

\(G_s=\frac{w}{a_s}=\frac{200,000}{0.55} \)

\(=364,000lb/(hr\cdot ft^2)\)

 

(6) Obtain D from Table At.8:

D = 0.62in ⇐ [Table AT.8]

\(=\frac{0.62in}{12in/ft}\) = 0.0517ft

 

 

 

Obtain μ at T:

μ = 0.0128 cP at 228°F

\(=0.0128\ cP\left\lgroup\frac{2.42lb/(ft\cdot hr)}{1\ cP} \right\rgroup\)

= 0.031lb/(ft · hr)

 (6′) Compute \(D_e\):

\(D_e=\frac{4a_s}{wetted\ perimeter}\)

 

\(=\frac{4a_s}{N_td_oπ}=\frac{4(0.55)}{(76)(0.75/12)π}\)

= 0.148ft

 

 

 

 

 

Obtain μ at \(t_{mean}:\)

μ = 2\(μ_{water}\) ⇐ for nonviscous, aqueous salt solution

\(μ_{water}\) = 0.65cP at 111°F

μ = 2(0.65) = 1.30cP

\(=1.30\ cP\left\lgroup\frac{2.42lb/(ft\cdot hr)}{1\ cP}\right\rgroup\)

= 3.14lb/(ft·hr)

 

(7) Reynolds Number, R\(e_t\) :

\(Re_t=\frac{DG_t}{\mu}\)

 

\(=\frac{(0.0517)(49,500)}{0.031}=82,500\)

(\(Re_t\) is for pressure drop only)

 (7′) Reynolds Number, \(Re_s\) :

\(Re_s=\frac{D_eG_s}{\mu}\)

 

\(=\frac{(0.148)(364,000)}{3.14}=17,100\)

 

(8) Skip this step since k and C are not required for a phase changing stream. (8′) Obtain c and k at \(t_{avg}\):

c = 0.86Btu/lb · °F at 111°F ⇐ [Step 1]

k = 0.9\(k_{water}\) ⇐ for low concentration salt solution

\(k_{water}\) = 0.37Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F/ft at 111°F

k = 0.9(0.37) = 0.333 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F/ft

 

(9) \(h_i\) is approximately equal to 1500 Btu/hr∙\(ft^2\)∙°F for steam condensation.

 

\(\phi _t=\left\lgroup\frac{\mu }{\mu _w} \right\rgroup^{0.14}\approx 1.0\)

 Solve for \(h_o\) and \(\phi_s\) at \(t_w\):

\(t_w\) = 212°F ⇐from iterative calculation

\(μ_w\) = (0.26 × 2)cP at 212°F

= 0.52cP

\(=0.52cP\left\lgroup\frac{2.42lb/(ft · hr)}{1cP}\right\rgroup\)

= 1.26lb/(ft · hr)

\(\phi _s=\left\lgroup\frac{\mu}{\mu_w}\right\rgroup^{0.14}=\left\lgroup\frac{3.14}{1.26}\right\rgroup^{0.14}=1.14\)

 

\(\frac{h_oD_e}{k}=0.023\left\lgroup\frac{D_eG_s}{\mu}\right\rgroup^{0.8}\left\lgroup\frac{C\mu}{k}\right\rgroup^{0.3}\)

\(h_o\) = 237 Btu/hr · \(ft^2\) · °F at 212°F

(10) Clean overall coefficient, \(U_C\) :

\(U_C=\frac{h_ih_o}{h_i+h_o}=\frac{(1500)(237)}{1500+237}=205Btu/hr\cdot ft^2\cdot °F\)

(11) Design overall coefficient, \(U_D\) :

\(a^{"}\) = 0.1963 \(ft^2\) per line.ft ⇐ [Table AT.8]

A = \(a^{''}N_t\)L = (0.1963)(76)(16) = 238 \(ft^2\)

\(U_D=\frac{Q}{A\Delta T^*}=\frac{3,790,000}{(238)(116.7)}=136Btu/hr\cdot ft^2\cdot °F\)

(15) Dirt factor \(R_d\):

\(R_d=\frac{U_C-U_D}{U_CU_D}=\frac{205-136}{(205)(136)}=0.00247hr\cdot ft^2\cdot °F/Btu\)

 

Summary
Variable Value Units
\(h_o\) = 237 \(\frac{Btu}{hr·ft^2·°F/ft}\)
\(h_i\) = 1500
\(U_C\) = 205 \(\frac{Btu}{hr·ft^2·°F}\)
\(U_D\) = 136
\(R_d\) (calculated) = 0.00247 \(\frac{hr·ft^2·°F}{Btu}\)
\(R_d\) (required) = 0.00200

Pressure Drop

(1) For \(Re_t\) and T in (6) above:

f = 0.000155\(\frac{ft^2}{in^2}\)at \(Re_t\) = 82,500

\(\dot{V}\) = 20.0\(ft^3\)/lb (specific volume)

\(s=1.0\left\lgroup\frac{\dot{V}}{\rho}\right\rgroup=1.0\left\lgroup\frac{20.0}{62.5}\right\rgroup\)

= 0.00080 ⇐convert liq. to gas

 (1′) Compute \(D^{'}_e and Re^{'}_s\) to find f:

\(D^{'}_e=\frac{4a_s}{frictional\ wetted\ perimeter}\)

 

\(=\frac{4a_s}{N_td_o\pi+D_s\pi}=\frac{4a_s}{(N_td_o+D_s)\pi}\)

 

\(=\frac{4(0.55)}{\left[(76)(0.75/12)+(12/12)\right]\pi}\)

= 0.122ft ⇐[eq.\(r_H=\frac{1}{4}(D_{o,i}-D_{i,o})\)]

 

\(Re^{'}_s=\frac{D^{'}_eG_s}{\mu}=\frac{(0.122)(364,000)}{3.14}\)

= 14,100

f = 0.00025\(\frac{ft^2}{in^2}\)at \(Re^{'}_s\) = 14,100

s = 1.08 ⇐give

 

(2) \(\Delta P_t=\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{fG^2_tLn}{(5.22\times 10^{10})D_es\phi _t}\)

\(=\frac{(0.000155)(49,500)^2(16)(2)}{(5.22\times 10^{10})(0.0517)(0.0008)(1.0)}\)

= 2.8psi

 (2′) \(\Delta P_s=\frac{fG^2_sLn}{(5.22\times 10^{10})D_es\phi _s}\)

\(=\frac{(0.00025)(364,000)^2(16)(1)}{(5.22\times 10^{10})(0.122)(1.08)(1.14)}\)

= 0.07psi

(Note: The ½ term above arises because of the half circle pitch)
This is a relatively high pressure drop for steam with a gravity condensate return.
However, the exchanger is satisfactory.
