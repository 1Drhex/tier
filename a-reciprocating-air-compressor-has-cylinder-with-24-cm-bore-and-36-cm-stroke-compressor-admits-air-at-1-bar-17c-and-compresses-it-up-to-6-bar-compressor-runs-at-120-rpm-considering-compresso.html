Guest Question.

A reciprocating air compressor has cylinder with 24 cm bore and 36 cm stroke. Compressor admits air at 1 bar, 17°C and compresses it up to 6 bar. Compressor runs at 120 rpm. Considering compressor to be single acting and single stage determine mean effective pressure and the horse power required to run compressor when it compresses following the isothermal process and polytropic process with index of 1.3. Also find isothermal efficiency when compression is of polytropic and adiabatic type.

Admin
Solution
\(\begin{aligned}\text{Compression ratio }= \frac{P_{2}}{P_{a}} = 6&= r\\\text{From cylinder dimensions the stro}&\text{ke volume}\\&= \frac{\pi}{4} × (0.24)^{2}× (0.36)\\&= 0.01628 m^{3}\\\text{Volume of air compressed per min}&\text{ute }= 0.01628 × 120\\&= 1.954 m^{3}/min\\\text{Let us neglect clearance volume. }&\\\text{Work done in isothermal process }&\\W_{iso}&= P_{1} V_{1} \ln r\\\text{Mean effective pressure in isother}&\text{mal process}\\mep_{iso}&= \frac{p_{1} V_{1} \ln r}{V_{1}} = P_{1} \ln r\\&=1 × 10^{2} × \ln 6 = 179.18 kPa\\\text{Work done in polytropic process }&\text{with index n = 1.3, i.e. }PV^{1.3} = C\\W_{poly}&= \frac{n}{n – 1} P_{1} V_{1} \left[\left\lgroup\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} \right\rgroup^{\frac{n – 1}{n} } – 1\right] \\\text{Mean effective pressure in polytr}&\text{opic process,}\\mep_{poly}&= \frac{\left\lgroup\frac{n}{n – 1} \right\rgroup P_{1} V_{1}}{V_{1}}\left[\left\lgroup\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} \right\rgroup^{\frac{n – 1}{n} } – 1\right]\\mep_{poly}&= \left\lgroup\frac{n}{n – 1} \right\rgroup P_{1} \left[\left\lgroup\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} \right\rgroup^{\frac{n – 1}{n} } – 1\right]\\&= \left\lgroup\frac{1.3}{1.3 – 1} \right\rgroup × 1 × 10^{2} \left[(6)^{\frac{1.3 – 1}{1.3} } – 1\right] \\mep_{poly}&= 221.89 kPa\end{aligned}\\\text{Work done in adiabatic process, }W_{adibatic} = \frac{γ}{γ – 1} P_{1} V_{1} \left[\left\lgroup\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} \right\rgroup^{\frac{γ – 1}{γ} } – 1\right] \\\text{Mean effective pressure in adiabatic process, }mep_{adiabatic} = \frac{W_{adiabatic}}{V_{1}}\\\begin{aligned}mep_{adiabatic}&= \frac{γ}{γ – 1} P_{1} \left[\left\lgroup\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} \right\rgroup^{\frac{γ – 1}{γ} } – 1\right] \\&= \left\lgroup\frac{1.4}{1.4 – 1} \right\rgroup × 1 × 10^{2} \left[(6)^{\frac{1.4 – 1}{1.4} } – 1\right] \\mep_{adiabatic}&= 233.98 kPa\\\text{Horse power required for iso}&\text{thermal process,}\\HP_{iso}&= \frac{mep_{iso} × \text{Volume per minute}}{0.7457 × 60}\\\text{(As 1 hp = 0.7457 kW)}\quad &\\&= \frac{179.18 × 1.954}{0.7457 × 60}\\HP_{iso}&= 7.825 hp\\\text{Horse power required for po}&\text{lytropic process,}\\HP_{poly}&= \frac{mep_{poly} × \text{Volume per minute}}{0.7457 × 60}\\&= \frac{221.89 × 1.954}{0.7457 × 60}\\HP_{poly}&= 9.69 hp\\\text{Horse power required for ad}&\text{iabatic process,}\\HP_{adiabatic}&= \frac{mep_{adiabatic} × \text{Volume per minute}}{0.7457 × 60}\\&= \frac{233.98 × 1.954}{0.7457 × 60}\\&= 10.22 hp\\\text{Isothermal efficiency}&= \frac{\text{Isothermal process power required}}{\text{Actual power required}}\\\text{Isothermal efficiency}_{poly}&= \frac{HP_{iso}}{HP_{poly}} = \frac{7.825}{9.69} = 0.8075 or 80.75\%\\\text{Isothermal efficiency}_{adiabatic}& = \frac{HP_{adiabatic}}{HP_{poly}} = \frac{7.825}{10.22} = 0.7657 or 76.57\%\end{aligned}\\\text{mep: 179.18 kPa for isothermal, 221.89 kPa for polytropic process}\\\text{HP required: 7.825 HP for isothermal, 9.69 HP for polytropic}\\\text{Isothermal efficiency: 80.75}\%\text{ for polytropic process, 76.57}\%\text{ for adiabatic process}\)
