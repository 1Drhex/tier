Guest Question.

The solid 30-mm-diameter steel [E = 200 GPa] shaft shown in Fig. P10.68 supports two belt pulleys. Assume that the bearing at A can be idealized as a pin support and that the bearing at E can be idealized as a roller support. For the loading shown, determine the shaft deflection at pulley D.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(I=\frac{\pi}{64}(30 mm )^{4}=39,760.78 mm ^{4}\)

 

Shaft deflection at pulley D
Consider pulley B load. [Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated load not at midspan.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{D}=-\frac{P b x}{6 L E I}\left(L^{2}-b^{2}-x^{2}\right)\) (elastic curve)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&P=750 N , L=1,000 mm , x=200 mm \\&b=300 mm , E I=7.95216 \times 10^{9} N – mm ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{D} &=-\frac{P b x}{6 L E I}\left(L^{2}-b^{2}-x^{2}\right) \\&=-\frac{(750 N )(300 mm )(200 mm )}{6(1,000 mm )\left(7.95216 \times 10^{9} N – mm ^{2}\right)}\left[(1,000 mm )^{2}-(300 mm )^{2}-(200 mm )^{2}\right] \\&=-0.82053 mm\end{aligned}\)

 

Consider pulley D load. [Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated load not at midspan.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{D}=-\frac{P a b}{6 L E I}\left(L^{2}-a^{2}-b^{2}\right)\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&P=500 N , L=1,000 mm , a=800 mm ,\\&b=200 mm , E I=7.95216 \times 10^{9} N – mm ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{D} &=-\frac{P a b}{6 L E I}\left(L^{2}-a^{2}-b^{2}\right) \\&=-\frac{(500 N )(800 mm )(200 mm )}{6(1,000 mm )\left(7.95216 \times 10^{9} N – mm ^{2}\right)}\left[(1,000 mm )^{2}-(800 mm )^{2}-(200 mm )^{2}\right] \\&=-0.53654 mm\end{aligned}\)

Shaft deflection at D

\(v_{D}=-0.82053 mm -0.53654 mm =-1.35707 mm =1.357 mm \downarrow\)
