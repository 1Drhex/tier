Finding the Values of L’s and C’s for a Class E Rectifier

The class E rectifier of Figure 7.23a supplies a load power of \(P_L = 400 mW\) at \(V_o = 4 V\). The peak supply voltage is \(V_m = 10 V\). The supply frequency is f = 250 kHz. The peak-to-peak ripple on the dc output voltage is \(ΔV_o = 40 mV\). (a) Determine the values of L, C, and \(C_f\); and (b) the rms and dc currents of L and C. (c) Use PSpice to plot the output voltage \(v_o\) and the inductor current \(i_L\).

Admin
Solution

\(V_m = 10 V, V_o = 4 V, ΔV_o = 40 mV\) and \(f = 250 kHz\).

a. Choose a suitable value of C. Let \(C = 10 nF\). Let the resonant frequency be \(f_o = f = 250 kHz. 250 kHz = f_o =1/[2\pi \sqrt{L\times 10 nF} ]\), which gives \(L = 40.5 µH\). \(P_L = V_o^ 2 /R\) or \(400 mW = 4^2/R\), which gives \(R = 40 Ω. I_o = V_o/R = 4/40 = 100 mA\). The value of capacitance \(C_f\) is given by

\(C_f=\frac{I_0}{2f \Delta V_0}=\frac{100 mA}{2 \times 250 kHz \times 40 mV}=5 \mu F\)

b. \(I_m = V_m/R = 10/40 = 250 mA\). The rms inductor current \(I_L\) is

\(I_L(rms)=\sqrt{100^2+\frac{250^2}{2} }=203.1 mA\)

 

\(I_{L(dc)} = 100 mA\)

The rms current of the capacitor C is

\(I_{c(rms)}=\frac{250}{\sqrt{2} }=176.78 mA\)

 

\(I_{C(dc)} = 0\)
