Guest Question.

Design a connecting rod for a high-speed IC engine using the following data:
Cylinder bore = 125 mm
Length of connecting rod = 300 mm
Maximum gas pressure = 3.5 MPa
Length of stroke = 125 mm
Mass of reciprocating parts = 1.6 kg
Engine speed = 2200 rpm
Assume suitable data and state the assumptions you make.

Admin
Solution

Given D = 125 mm

\( p_{\max .}=3.5 MPa =3.5 N / mm ^{2} \quad L=300 mm =0.3 m \).

\( l=125 mm =0.125 m \quad N=2200 rpm m_{r}=1.6 kg \).

Step I Cross-section of connecting rod

\( P_{c}=\left(\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}\right) p_{\max .}=\left(\frac{\pi(125)^{2}}{4}\right)(3.5)=42951.46 N \).

Assumption 1 The factor of safety against buckling failure is 5.

\( P_{c r}=P_{c}(f s)=42951.46(5)=214757.3 N \).

Substituting,

\( A=11 t^{2} \quad k_{x x}=1.78 t \quad a=\frac{1}{7500} \).

\( \sigma_{c}=330 N / mm ^{2} \).

in Eq. (25.48),

\( P_{c r}=\frac{\sigma_{c} A}{1+a\left(\frac{L}{k_{x x}}\right)^{2}} \) (25.48).

\( P_{c r}=\frac{\sigma_{c} A}{1+a\left(\frac{L}{k_{x x}}\right)^{2}} \)

\( \text { or } \quad 214757.3=\frac{(330)\left(11 t^{2}\right)}{1+\frac{1}{7500}\left(\frac{300}{1.78 t}\right)^{2}} \).

\( \frac{214757.3}{(330)(11)}=\frac{t^{2}}{1+\frac{3.79}{t^{2}}} \quad \text { or } \quad 59.16=\frac{t^{4}}{t^{2}+3.79} \).

\( t^{4}-59.16 t^{2}-224.22=0 \).

The above expression is a quadratic equation in \( \left(t^{2}\right) \).

\( t^{2}=\frac{59.16 \pm \sqrt{(59.16)^{2}+4(224.22)}}{2} \).

\( =\frac{59.16 \pm 66.31}{2} \).

\( t^{2}=62.74 \)

t = 7.92 or 8 mm
Dimensions of cross-section (Fig. 25.14)
B = 4t = 4(8) = 32 mm
H = 5t = 5(8) = 40 mm
Thickness of web = t = 8 mm
Thickness of flanges = t = 8 mm
The width (B = 32 mm) is kept constant throughout the length of the connecting rod.
Variation of height
at the middle section , H = 5t = 40 mm
at the small end, \( H_{1} \) = 0.85 H = 0.85 (40) = 34 mm
at the big end, \( H_{2} \) = 1.2 H = 1.2 (40) = 48 mm
dimensions (B/H) of section at big end = 32 mm × 48 mm.

dimensions (B/H) of section at middle = 32 mm × 40 mm
dimensions (B/H) of section at small end = 32 mm × 34 mm
Step II Small and big end bearings
Assumption 2 The (l/d) ratio for piston pin bearing is 1.8.
Assumption 3 The (l/d) ratio for crank pin bearing is 1.1.
Assumption 4 The allowable bearing pressure for piston pin bearing is 14 MPa.
Assumption 5 The allowable bearing pressure for crank pin bearing is 8.5 MPa.
Piston-pin bearing

From Eq. 25.51,

\( P_{c}=d_{p} l_{p}\left(p_{b}\right)_{p} \) (25.51).

\( P_{c}=d_{p} l_{p}\left(p_{b}\right)_{p} \quad \text { or } \quad 42951.46=d_{p}\left(1.8 d_{p}\right)(14) \).

\( d_{p}^{2}=1704.42 \).

\( d_{p}=41.28 \text { or } 42 mm \).

\( l_{p}=1.8(42)=75.6 \text { or } 76 mm \).

Crank pin bearing
From Eq. 25.53,

\( P_{c}=d_{c} l_{c}\left(p_{b}\right)_{c} \) (25.53).

\( P_{c}=d_{c} l_{c}\left(p_{b}\right)_{c} \quad \text { or } \quad 42951.46=d_{c}\left(1.1 d_{c}\right)(8.5) \) .

\( d_{c}^{2}=4593.74 \).

\( d_{c}=67.78 \text { or } 68 mm \).

\( l_{c}=1.1(68)=74.8 \text { or } 75 mm \).

Step III Nominal diameter of bolts for the cap
Assumption 6 The permissible tensile stress for the bolt material is 100 N/mm².

\( r=\left(\frac{l}{2}\right)=\left(\frac{125}{2}\right)=62.5 mm =0.0625 m \).

\( n_{1}=\left(\frac{L}{r}\right)=\frac{300}{62.5}=4.8 \).

\( \omega=\left(\frac{2 \pi N}{60}\right)=\left(\frac{2 \pi(2200)}{60}\right)=230.38 rad / s \).

From Eq. 25.59,

\( \left(P_{i}\right)_{\max .}=m_{r} \omega^{2} r\left[1+\frac{1}{n_{1}}\right] \) (25.59).

\( \left(P_{i}\right)_{\max .}=m_{r} \omega^{2} r\left[1+\frac{1}{n_{1}}\right] \).

\( =(1.6)(230.38)^{2}(0.0625)\left[1+\frac{1}{4.8}\right] \).

= 6413 22 N.

From Eq. 25.60,

\( \left(P_{i}\right)_{\max .}=2\left(\frac{\pi d_{c}^{2}}{4}\right) \sigma_{t} \). (25.60).

\( \left(P_{i}\right)_{\max .}=2\left(\frac{\pi d_{c}^{2}}{4}\right) \sigma_{t} \text { or } \)

\( 6413.22=2\left(\frac{\pi d_{c}^{2}}{4}\right)(100) \).

\( d_{c}^{2}=40.83 \).

\( d_{c}=6.39 mm \).

\( d=\left(\frac{d_{c}}{0.8}\right)=\left(\frac{6.39}{0.8}\right)=7.99 \text { or } 8 mm \).

Step IV Thickness of cap
Assumption 7 The permissible tensile stress for cap material is 100 N/mm².

\( b_{c}=l_{c}=75 mm \).

From Eq. 25.63,
l = diameter of crank pin + 2 (thickness of bush) + nominal diameter of bolt (d) + clearance (3 mm) (25.63).

l = diameter of crank pin + 2 (thickness of bush) + nominal diameter of bolt (d) + clearance (3 mm)
= 68 + 2(3) + 8 + 3
= 85 mm.

\( M_{b}=\frac{\left(P_{i}\right)_{\max .} l}{6}=\frac{(6413.22)(85)}{6}=90853.95 N – mm \).

\( I=\left[\frac{\left(b_{c}\right)\left(t_{c}\right)^{3}}{12}\right]=\left[\frac{(75)\left(t_{c}\right)^{3}}{12}\right]=6.25 t_{c}^{3} \).

\( y=\left(\frac{t_{c}}{2}\right) \).

Substituting the above expressions,

\( \sigma_{b}=\frac{M_{b} y}{I} \quad \text { or } \quad 100=\frac{(90853.95)}{\left(6.25 t_{c}^{3}\right)}\left(\frac{t_{c}}{2}\right) \).

\( t_{c}^{2}=72.68 \).

\( t_{c}=8.53 mm \text { or } 10 mm \).

Step V Whipping stress
Assumption 8 The mass density of the connecting rod material is 7800 kg/m³.

\( t=8 mm =\left(8 \times 10^{-3}\right) m \).

From Eq. 25.65,

\( m_{l}=\left(11 t^{2}\right) \rho \). (25.65).

\( m_{l}=\left(11 t^{2}\right) \rho=11\left(8 \times 10^{-3}\right)^{2}(7800)=5.49 kg / m \).

From Eq. 25.67,

\( \left(M_{b}\right)_{\max .}=m_{l} \omega^{2} r \frac{L^{2}}{9 \sqrt{3}} \) (25.67).

\( \left(M_{b}\right)_{\max .}=m_{l} \omega^{2} r \frac{L^{2}}{9 \sqrt{3}} \) .

\( =(5.49)(230.38)^{2}(0.0625) \frac{(0.3)^{2}}{9 \sqrt{3}} \).

= 105.14 N-m.

\( \left(M_{b}\right)_{\max .}=\left(105.14 \times 10^{3}\right) N – mm \).

\( I_{x x}=\left(\frac{419}{12}\right) t^{4} \quad \text { and } \quad y=\left(\frac{5 t}{2}\right) \).

\( \sigma_{b}=\frac{M_{b} y}{I}=\frac{\left(105.14 \times 10^{3}\right)\left(\frac{5 \times 8}{2}\right)}{\left(\frac{419}{12}\right)(8)^{4}}=14.7 N / mm ^{2} \).
