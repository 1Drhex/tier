Guest Question.

A pipe with an outside diameter of 220 mm and a wall thickness of 5 mm is subjected to the load shown in Figure P15.62. The internal pressure in the pipe is 2,000 kPa. Determine the normal and shear stresses on the top surface of the pipe at point H.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&d=220 mm -2(5 mm )=210 mm \\&A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[(220 mm )^{2}-(210 mm )^{2}\right]=3,377.212 mm ^{2} \\&J=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[(220 mm )^{4}-(210 mm )^{4}\right]=39.0490 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4} \\&I_{x}=I_{y}=\frac{\pi}{64}\left[(220 mm )^{4}-(210 mm )^{4}\right]=19.5245 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4} \\&Q=\frac{1}{12}\left[(220 mm )^{3}-(210 mm )^{3}\right]=115,583.333 mm ^{3}\end{aligned}\)

Vector expression for the 25-kN load:
The 25-kN load can be expressed in vector form as:

\(F =-(25 kN ) \sin 40^{\circ} j +(25 kN ) \cos 40^{\circ} k\)

The equivalent forces acting at H and K are thus

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{x}=0 N \\&F_{y}=-16.0697 kN =-16,069.690 N \\&F_{z}=19.1511 kN =19,151.111 N\end{aligned}\)

The position vector from the section of interest to a point on the line of action of F is:

\(r =(700 mm ) i +(1,500 mm ) k\)

The equivalent moments are found from the cross-product r Ã— F.

\(\begin{aligned}r \times F &=\left|\begin{array}{ccc} i & j & k \\0.700 & 0 & 1.500 \\0 & -16,069.690 & 19,151.111\end{array}\right| \\&=24,104.535 N – m i -13,405.778 N – m j -11,248.783 N – m k\end{aligned}\)

Equivalent moments:

\(\begin{aligned}&M_{x}=24.1045 \times 10^{6} N – mm \\&M_{y}=-13.4058 \times 10^{6} N – mm \\&M_{z}=-11.2488 \times 10^{6} N – mm\end{aligned}\)

Each of the non-zero forces and moments will be evaluated to determine whether stresses are created at the point of interest.

 

(a) Consider point H.
Force \(F _{y}\) does not cause either a normal stress or a shear stress at H.

Force \(F _{z}\) creates an axial stress at H. The magnitude of this normal stress is:

\(\sigma_{z}=\frac{19,151.111 N }{3,377.212 mm ^{2}}=5.671 MPa\)

Moment \(M _{x}\) creates bending stress at H. The magnitude of this stress is:

\(\sigma_{z}=\frac{M_{x} y}{I_{x}}=\frac{\left(24.1045 \times 10^{6} N – mm \right)(220 mm / 2)}{19.5245 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}}=135.804 MPa\)

Moment \(M _{y}\) does not create bending stress at H because H is located on the neutral axis for bending about the y axis.

Moment \(M _{z}\), which is a torque, creates a torsion shear stress in the xz plane at H. The magnitude of this shear stress is:

\(\tau_{x z}=\frac{M_{z} c}{J}=\frac{\left(11.2488 \times 10^{6} N – mm \right)(220 mm / 2)}{39.0490 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}}=31.688 MPa\)

Stresses due to internal pressure:
The 2,000-kPa internal fluid pressure creates tension normal stresses in the 5-mm-thick wall of the pipe.
The longitudinal stress (which acts in the z direction) in the pipe wall is:

\(\sigma_{\text {long }}=\frac{p d}{4 t}=\frac{(2,000 kPa )(210 mm )}{4(5 mm )}=21,000 kPa =21 MPa ( T )\)

and the circumferential stress is:

\(\sigma_{\text {hoop }}=\frac{p d}{2 t}=\frac{(2,000 kPa )(210 mm )}{2(5 mm )}=42,000 kPa =42 MPa ( T )\)

The hoop stress acts in the x direction at H.

Summary of stresses at H:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=42 MPa ( T ) \\\sigma_{z} &=5.671 MPa +135.804 MPa +21 MPa \\&=162.474 MPa =162.5 MPa ( T ) \\\tau_{x z} &=31.688 MPa =31.7 MPa\end{aligned}\)
