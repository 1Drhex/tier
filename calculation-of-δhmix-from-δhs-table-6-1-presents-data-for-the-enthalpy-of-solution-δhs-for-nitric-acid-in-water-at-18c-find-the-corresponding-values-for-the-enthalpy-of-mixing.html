Guest Question.

Calculation of \(\Delta h_{ mix }\) from \(\Delta \widetilde{h}_{s}\)

Table 6.1 presents data for the enthalpy of solution, \(\Delta \widetilde{h}_{s}\), for nitric acid in water at 18°C. Find the corresponding values for the enthalpy of mixing vs. mole fraction of solute.

Admin
Solution

Let the solute, \(HNO _{3}\), be species 1 and the solvent, \(H _{2} O\), be species 2. The enthalpy of solution can be written by dividing the enthalpy of mixing by the mole fraction solute:

 

\(\Delta \widetilde{h}_{s}=\frac{\Delta h_{ mix }}{x_{1}}\) (E6.10A)

 

The mole fraction of solute is defi ned as the number of moles of solute, 1, divided by the total number of moles in the system, 1 + n:

 

\(x_{1}=\frac{1}{1+n}\) (E6.10B)

 

Substituting Equation (E6.10B) in (E6.10A) and rearranging gives:

 

\(\Delta h_{ mix }=\frac{\Delta \widetilde{h}_{s}}{(1+n)}\) (E6.10C)

 

Table E6.10 presents values for \(\Delta h_{ mix }\) as calculated from Equation (E6.10C) vs. mole fraction as calculated from Equation (E6.10B) for the data presented in Table 6.1. The order of table entries has been reversed so that the values for mole fraction appear in ascending order.
