Guest Question.

A packed tower is used for absorbing sulphur dioxide from air by means of a caustic soda solution containing 20 kg/\(m^{3}\) NaOH. At an air flow of 2 kg/\(m^{2}\)s, corresponding to a Reynolds number of 5160, the friction factor R/\(\rho u^{2}\) is found to be 0.020.
Calculate the mass transfer coefficient in kg \(SO_{2}\)/s \(m^{2}\) (kN/\(m^{2}\)) under these conditionsif the tower is at atmospheric pressure. At the temperature of absorption, the diffusion coefficient \(SO_{2}\) is 1.16 Ã— \(10^{-5}\) \(m^{2}\)/s, the viscosity of the gas is 0.018 mN s/\(m^{2}\) and the density of the gas stream is 1.154 kg/\(m^{3}\).

Admin
Solution

For wetted-wall columns, the data are correlated by:

\(\mathbf{\left ( \frac{h_{d}}{u} \right )\left ( \frac{P_{Bm}}{P} \right )\left ( \frac{\mu }{\rho D} \right )^{0.56}}=B'Re^{-0.17}=j_{d}\) (equation 12.25)

\(j_{d}\simeq R/\rho u^{2}\)

In this problem: G’= 2.0 kg/\(m^{2}\)s, Re = 5160 and \(R/\rho u^{2}\) = 0.020

D = 0.116\(\times 10^{-4} m^{2}/s\) , \(\mu \times 10^{-5} Ns/m^{2}\) , and \(\rho =1.154 kg/m^{3}\)

Substituting these values gives:

\(\left ( \frac{\mu }{\rho D} \right )^{0.56}=\left ( \frac{1.8\times 10^{-5}}{1.154\times 0.116\times 10^{-4}} \right )^{0.56}=1.18\)

Thus: \(\left (\frac{h_{d}}{u} \right )\left ( \frac{P_{Bm}}{P} \right )=(0.020/1.18)=0.0169\)

\(G'=\rho u=2.0 kg/m^{2}s\)

and: and: u = (2.0/1.154) = 1.73 m/s

Thus: \(h_{d}(P_{Bm}/P)\)=(0.0169 Ã— 1.73) = 0.0293

d may be obtained from d = \(Re\mu /\rho \mu \) = 0.046 m (46 mm), which is the same order of size of wetted-wall column as that which was originally used in the research work.

\(k_{G}=\left ( \frac{h_{d}}{RT} \right )\left ( \frac{P_{Bm}}{P} \right )\)

R = 8314 \(m^{3}\)(N/\(m^{2}\))/K kmol and T will be taken as 298 K, and hence:

\(k_{G}\)=[0.0293/(8314 Ã— 298)] = 1.18 \(\times 10^{-8} kmol/m^{2}s(N/m^{2})\)

= \(7.56\times 10^{-4} \) kg \(SO_{2}/m^{2}s(kN/m^{2})\)
