Guest Question.

A 250-kg pump operates at speeds between 1000 and 2400 rpm and has a rotating unbalance of \(2.5 kg \cdot m\). The pump is placed at a location in an industrial plant where it has been determined that the maximum repeated force that should be applied to the floor is \(F_{max}\). Specify the stiffness of an isolator of damping ratio 0.1 that can be used to reduce the repeating component of the transmitted force to an acceptable level. Solve for (a) \(F_{max} = 15,000 N\); (b) \(F_{max} =10,000 N.\)

Admin
Solution

If the pump is placed directly on the floor, the repeating component of the transmitted force is 27,400 N at 1000 rpm and 157,800 N at 2400 rpm. Thus isolation is necessary. (a) From Figure 4.22, for \( \zeta = 0.1\) the minimum value of R occurs for r = 2.94. If \( \omega _{n}\) is chosen such that r = 2.94 is at the center of the operating range, then

\( \omega _{n}= \frac{1700 rpm}{2.94}=578.2 rpm= 60.55 rad/s\) (a)

At the lower end of the operating range, the frequency ratio is 1.73 and the transmitted force is

\(F_{T}= m_{0}e\omega ^{2}_{n}R(1.73, 0.1)\)

 

\(=2.5 kg\cdot m (60.55 rad/s)^{2}(1.73)^{2}\sqrt{\frac{1+ (0.346)^{2}}{\left[1- (1.73)^{2}\right]^{2}+(0.346)^{2} } }\)

\(= 14,350 N\) (b)

At the upper end of the operating range, the frequency ratio is 4.15 and the transmitted force is

\(F_{T}= m_{0}e\omega ^{2}_{n}R(4.15, 0.1) \)

 

\(=(2.5 kg\cdot m) (60.55 rad/s)^{2}(4.15)^{2}\sqrt{\frac{1+ (0.830)^{2}}{\left[1- (4.15)^{2}\right]^{2}+(0.830)^{2} } }\)

\(= 12,630 N\) (c )

Thus, choosing an isolator such that r = 2.94 corresponds to 1200 rpm will reduce the transmitted force to less than 15,000 N at all speeds between 1000 and 2400 rpm. The stiffness of such an isolator is

\( k= m\omega ^{2}_{n}= (250 kg)(60.55 rad/s)^{2}= 9.17 \times 10^{5} N/m\) (d)

(b) The above analysis works for \( F_{\max} = 15,000 N\) but does not work for \( F_{\max} = 10,000 N\), as the transmitted force at both ends of the operating range is larger than 10,000 N when the center of the operating range corresponds to the minimum value of R. Setting \( F_{\max} = 10,000 N\) for \( \omega = 1000 rpm\) leads to

\( T(r, 0.1)= \frac{F_{T_{\max}}}{m_{0}e\omega ^{2}}=\frac{10,000 N}{(2.5 kg\cdot m)(104.7 rad/s)^{2}}=0.365 \) (e)

which leads to r =2.012. Then

\( \omega _{n}=\frac{104.7 rad/s}{2.102} = 52.02 rad/s\) (f)

Then for \(\omega= 2400 rpm,r=4.83\) and

\( F_{T}= m_{0}e\omega ^{2}_{n}R (4.83, 0.1)=9810 N\) (g)

Thus, the transmitted force is less than 10,000 at all speeds within the operating range and

\( k=m\omega ^{2}_{n}= (250 kg)(52.02 rad/s)^{2}= 6.77 \times 10^{5 }N/m\) (h)
