Guest Question.

Compare estimates the diffusion coefficient of an ethanol-water mixture at \( 10^{\circ} C \) (283 K), under conditions that (1) ethanol is the solute and water is the solvent and (2) water is the solute and ethanol is the solvent. Ethanol has the molecular formula \( C_{2}H_{5}OH \) and a molecular weight of 46 g/gmole, and water (\( H_{2}O \)) has a molecular weight of 18 g/gmole. At \( 10^{\circ} C \), the liquid viscosity of water is \( 1.306 \times 10^{-3} Pa \cdot s \left ( 1.306 cP \right ) \), and the liquid viscosity of ethanol is \( 1.394 \times 10^{-3} Pa \cdot s \left ( 1.394 cP \right ) \).

Admin
Solution

The Wilke–Chang correlation will be used for estimation of the diffusion coefficients. This correlation will require the molar volume of each species at its normal boiling point. From Table 24.4, the molecular volume of water is \( 18.9 cm^{3}/gmole \). From Table 24.5, using the group contribution method, the molar volume of ethanol (EtOH) is estimated as

\( V_{EtOH} = 2 \cdot V_{C} + 1 \cdot V_{O} + 6 \cdot V_{H} = 2 \left ( 14.8 \right ) + 1 \left ( 7.4 \right ) + 6 \left ( 3.7 \right ) = 59.2 cm^{2}/gmole \)

 

For the ethanol-water system, let A = ethanol and B = water. First, if ethanol is the solute, \( V_{A} = 59.2 cm^{3}/gmole \), and if water is the solvent, \( \Phi_{B} = 2.6 \) and \( \mu_{B}= 1.394 cP. \) Consequently, by equation ( \( \frac{D_{AB} \mu_{B}}{T} = \frac{7.4 \times 10^{-8} \left ( \Phi_{B} M_{B} \right )^{1/2}}{V_{A}^{0.6}} \) )

 

\( D_{AB} = \frac{T}{\mu_{B}} \frac{7.4 \times 10^{-8} \left ( \Phi_{B} M_{B} \right )^{1/2}}{V_{A}^{0.6}} = \frac{\left ( 283 \right )}{\left (1.394 \right )} \frac{7.4 \times 10^{-8} \left ( 2.6 \cdot 18 \right )^{1/2}}{\left ( 59.2 \right )^{0.6}} = 8.9 \times 10^{-6} cm^{2} /s \)

 

For comparison, from Appendix J, Table J.2, \( D_{AB} = 8.3 \times 10^{-6} cm^{2}/s, \) which is within 10% of the estimated value. Now consider that water is the solute with \( V_{B} = 18.9 cm^{3} /gmole, \) and ethanol is the solvent with \( \Phi = 1.5 \) and \( \mu_{A} = 1.306 cP \). In context to equation ( \( \frac{D_{AB} \mu_{B}}{T} = \frac{7.4 \times 10^{-8} \left ( \Phi_{B} M_{B} \right )^{1/2}}{V_{A}^{0.6}} \) ), \( D_{BA} \) is now given by

 

\( D_{BA} = \frac{T}{\mu_{A}} \frac{7.4 \times 10^{-8} \left ( \Phi_{A} M_{A} \right )^{1/2}}{V_{B}^{0.6}} = \frac{\left ( 283 \right )}{\left (1.306 \right )} \frac{7.4 \times 10^{-8} \left ( 1.5 \cdot 46 \right )^{1/2}}{\left ( 18.9 \right )^{0.6}} = 2.28 \times 10^{-5} cm^{2} /s \)

 

This result shows that \( D_{AB} \neq D_{BA} \) for liquids.
