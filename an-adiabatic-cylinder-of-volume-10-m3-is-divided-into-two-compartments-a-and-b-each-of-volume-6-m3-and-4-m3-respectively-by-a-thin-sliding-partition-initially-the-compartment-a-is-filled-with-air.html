Guest Question.

An adiabatic cylinder of volume 10 \(\textbf{m}^{3}\) is divided into two compartments A and B each of volume 6 \(\textbf{m}^{3}\) and 4 \(\textbf{m}^{3}\) respectively, by a thin sliding partition. Initially the compartment A is filled with air at 6 bar and 600 K, while there is a vacuum in the compartment B. Air expands and fills both the compartments. Calculate the loss in available energy. Assume atmosphere is at 1 bar and 300 K. [U.P.S.C. 1997]

Admin
Solution
\(\begin{aligned}\text{Here}\qquad \qquad \qquad T_{0}&= 300 K, P_{0} = 1 bar \\V_{A}&= 6 m^{3}, V_{B} = 4 m^{3}\\P_{1}&= 6 bar, T_{1} = 600 K\\\text{Initially, }V_{1}&= V_{A} = 6 m^{3},\text{ and finally, } V_{2} = V_{A} + V_{B} = 10 m^{3}\\\end{aligned}\\\text{Expansion occurs in adiabatic conditions.}\\\text{Temperature after expansion can be obtained by considering adiabatic expansion.}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \quad \qquad\qquad \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}&= \left\lgroup\frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}} \right\rgroup^{γ – 1} \\T_{2}&= 600 \left\lgroup\frac{6}{10} \right\rgroup^{(1.4 – 1)} = 489.12 K\\\text{Mass of air, }m&= \frac{P_{1}V_{1}}{RT_{1}} = \frac{6 × 10^{5} × 6}{287 × 600} = 20.91 kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Change in entropy of control system, }(S_{2} – S_{1}) = mc_{v} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}} + mR \ln \frac{V_{2}}{V_{1}}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad ΔS_{S} = (S_{2} – S_{1}) = 20.91 \left\{0.718 \ln \left\lgroup\frac{489.12}{600} \right\rgroup + 0.287 \ln \left\lgroup\frac{10}{6} \right\rgroup \right\} \\\qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad = – 2.01 × 10^{–3} kJ/K\\\text{Here, there is no change in entropy of environment, }ΔS_{e} = 0\\\text{Total entropy change of combined system = }ΔS_{c} = ΔS_{s} + ΔS_{e}\\\begin{aligned}&= – 2.01 × 10^{–3} kJ/K\\\text{Loss of available energy}&= \text{Irreversibility = }T_{0} · ΔS_{c}\\&= 300 × ( – 2.01 × 10^{–3})\\&= – 0.603 kJ\\\text{Loss of available energy}&= \textbf{0.603 kJ}\end{aligned}\)
