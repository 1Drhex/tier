Guest Question.

Double line-to-ground short-circuit calculations using sequence networks

Calculate (a) the subtransient fault current in each phase, (b) neutral fault current, and (c) contributions to the fault current from the motor and from the transmission line, for a bolted double line-to-ground fault from phase b to c to ground at bus 2 in . Neglect the Δ–Y transformer phase shifts.

Admin
Solution

a. The zero-, positive-, and negative-sequence networks in Figure 9.5 are connected in parallel at the fault terminals in Figure 9.13. From (9.4.12) with \(Z_{F} \) = 0,

\(I_{1} =\frac{V_{F} }{Z_{1}+\left[Z_{2}//\left(Z_{0}+3Z_{F} \right) \right] } =\frac{V_{F}}{Z_{1}+\left[\frac{Z_{2}\left(Z_{0}+3Z_{F}\right) }{Z_{2}+Z_{0}+3Z_{F}} \right] } \) (9.4.12)

\(I_{1} =\frac{1.05\angle 0°}{j\left[0.13893+\frac{\left(0.14562\right)\left(0.25\right) }{0.14562+0.25} \right] } =\frac{1.05\angle 0°}{j0.23095}=-j4.5464 \) per unit

From (9.4.13) and (9.4.14),

\(I_{2} =\left(-I_{1} \right) \left(\frac{Z_{0}+3Z_{F} }{Z_{0}+3Z_{F}+Z_{2}} \right) \) (9.4.13)

\(I_{0} =\left(-I_{1} \right) \left(\frac{Z_{2} }{Z_{0}+3Z_{F}+Z_{2}} \right) \) (9.4.14)

\(I_{2} = \left(+j4.5464\right) \left(\frac{0.25}{0.25+0.14562} \right) =j2.8730\) per unit

\(I_{0} = \left(+j4.5464\right) \left(\frac{0.14562}{0.25+0.14562} \right) =j1.6734\) per unit

Transforming to the phase domain, the subtransient fault currents are:

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} I^{\prime \prime } _{a} \\ I^{\prime \prime }_{b} \\ I^{\prime \prime }_{c} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & a^{2} & a \\ 1 & a & a^{2} \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} +j1.6734 \\ -j4.5464 \\ +j2.8730 \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 6.8983\angle 158.66° \\ 6.8983\angle 21.34° \end{matrix} \right ] \) per unit

Using the base current of 4.1837 kA at bus 2,

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} I^{\prime \prime } _{a} \\ I^{\prime \prime }_{b} \\ I^{\prime \prime }_{c} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 6.8983\angle 158.66° \\ 6.8983\angle 21.34° \end{matrix} \right ]\left(4.1837\right) =\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 28.86\angle 158.66° \\ 28.86\angle 21.34° \end{matrix} \right ] \) kA

b. The neutral fault current is

\(I_{n}=\left(I^{\prime \prime }_{b}+I^{\prime \prime }_{c} \right) =3I_{0} =j5.0202 \) per unit

\(=\left(j5.0202\right) \left(4.1837\right) =21.00\angle 90°\) kA

c. Neglecting Δ–Y transformer phase shifts, the contributions to the fault current from the motor and transmission line can be obtained from Figure 9.4. From the zero-sequence network, Figure 9.4(a), the contribution to the zero-sequence fault current from the line is zero, due to the transformer connection. That is,

\(I_{line 0} \)= 0

\(I_{motor 0} =I_{0} =j1.6734\) per unit

From the positive-sequence network, Figure 9.4(b), the positive terminals of the internal machine voltages can be connected, since \(E^{\prime \prime }_{g} \) =\(E^{\prime \prime }_{m} \) E0m . Then, by current division,

\(I_{line1} =\frac{X^{\prime \prime }_{m} }{X^{\prime \prime }_{m}+\left(X^{\prime \prime }_{g}+X_{T1}+X_{line 1}+X_{T2}\right) } I_{1}=\frac{0.20}{0.20+\left(0.455\right) } \left(-j4.5464\right) =-j1.3882\) per unit

\(I_{motor 1 } =\frac{0.455}{0.20+0.455}\left(-j4.5464\right)=-j3.1582\) per unit

From the negative-sequence network, Figure 9.4(c), using current division,

\(I_{line 2} =\frac{0.21}{0.21+0.475}\left(-j2.8730\right)=j0.8808 \) per unit

\(I_{motor 2} =\frac{0.475}{0.21+0.475}\left(-j2.8730\right)=j1.9922 \) per unit

Transforming to the phase domain with base currents of 0.41837 kA for the line and 4.1837 kA for the motor,

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} I^{\prime \prime } _{line a} \\ I^{\prime \prime } _{line b} \\ I^{\prime \prime }_{line c} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & a^{2} & a \\ 1 & a & a^{2} \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ -j1.3882 \\ j0.8808 \end{matrix} \right ]= \left [ \begin{matrix} 0.5074\angle -90° \\ 1.9813\angle 172.643° \\ 1.9813\angle 7.357° \end{matrix} \right ]\) per unit

\(=\left [ \begin{matrix} 0.2123\angle -90° \\ 0.8289\angle 172.643° \\ 0.8289\angle 7.357° \end{matrix} \right ] \) kA

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} I^{\prime \prime }_{motor a} \\ I^{\prime \prime }_{ motor b} \\ I^{\prime \prime }_{motor c} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & a^{2} & a \\ 1 & a & a^{2} \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} j1.6734 \\ -j3.1582 \\ j1.9922 \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} 0.5074\angle 90° \\ 4.9986\angle 153.17° \\ 4.9986\angle 26.83° \end{matrix} \right ]\) per unit

\(=\left [ \begin{matrix} 2.123\angle 90° \\ 20.91\angle 153.17°\\ 20.91\angle 26.83° \end{matrix} \right ]\) kA

The double line-to-line fault results for this example can be shown in PowerWorld Simulator by repeating the procedure, with the exception that the Fault Type field value should be Double Line-to Ground.
