Guest Question.

The Sum of Two Vectors

Find the sum of two displacement vectors \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) lying in the x y plane and given by

\(\vec{A}=(2.0 \hat{i}+2.0 \hat{j}) \mathrm{m} \quad \text { and } \quad \vec{B}=(2.0 \hat{i}-4.0 \hat{j}) \mathrm{m}\)

Admin
Solution

Conceptualize You can conceptualize the situation by drawing the vectors on graph paper. Draw an approximation of the expected resultant vector.

Categorize We categorize this example as a simple substitution problem. Comparing this expression for \(\overrightarrow{A}\) with the general expression \(\overrightarrow{A}=A_{x} \hat{i}+A_{y} \hat{j}+A_{z} \hat{k}\), we see that \(A_{x}=2.0 \mathrm{~m}, A_{y}=2.0 \mathrm{~m}\), and \(A_{z}=0 .\) Likewise, \(B_{x}=2.0 \mathrm{~m}\), \(B_{y}=-4.0 \mathrm{~m}\), and \(B_{z}=0 .\) We can use a two-dimensional approach because there are no \(z\) components.

Use Equation \(3.14\) to obtain the resultant vector \(\overrightarrow{R}\):

\(\overrightarrow{R}=\left(A_{x}+B_{x}\right) \hat{i}+\left(A_{y}+B_{y}\right) \hat{j}\) (3.14)

\(\overrightarrow{R}=\overrightarrow{A}+\overrightarrow{B}=(2.0+2.0) \hat{i} \mathrm{m}+(2.0-4.0) \hat{j} \mathrm{m}\)

Evaluate the components of \(\overrightarrow{R}\)

\(R_{x}=4.0 \mathrm{~m} \quad R_{y}=-2.0 \mathrm{~m}\)

Use Equation \(3.16\) to find the magnitude of \(\overrightarrow{\mathbf{R}}\):

\(R=\sqrt{R_{x}^{2}+R_{y}^{2}}=\sqrt{\left(A_{x}+B_{x}\right)^{2}+\left(A_{y}+B_{y}\right)^{2}}\) (3.16)

\(\quad R=\sqrt{R_{x}^{2}+R_{y}^{2}}=\sqrt{(4.0 \mathrm{~m})^{2}+(-2.0 \mathrm{~m})^{2}}=\sqrt{20} \mathrm{~m}=4.5 \mathrm{~m}\)

Find the direction of \(\overrightarrow{R}\) from Equation \(3.17\)

\(\tan \theta=\frac{R_{y}}{R_{x}}=\frac{A_{y}+B_{y}}{A_{x}+B_{x}}\) (3.17)

\(\tan \theta=\frac{R_{y}}{R_{x}}=\frac{-2.0 \mathrm{~m}}{4.0 \mathrm{~m}}=-0.50\)

Your calculator likely gives the answer \(-27^{\circ}\) for \(\theta=\tan ^{-1}(-0.50)\). This answer is correct if we interpret it to mean \(27^{\circ}\) clockwise from the \(x\) axis. Our standard form has been to quote the angles measured counterclockwise from the \(+x\) axis, and that angle for this vector is \(\theta=333^{\circ}\).
