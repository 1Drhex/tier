Guest Question.

Calculation of Extent of Gas-Phase Reaction with \(\Delta h_{ rxn }^{0}=\) Constant

Consider the production of ethylene from unimolecular decomposition of ethane as shown in the following reaction:

\(C _{2} H _{6} \rightleftarrows C _{2} H _{4}+ H _{2}\)

At a temperature of 1000°C and pressure of 1 bar, what is the equilibrium composition of the system? Assume \(\Delta h_{ rxn }^{0}=\) Constant.

Admin
Solution

From Appendix A.3, we can construct Table E9.6. The bottom row of Table E9.6 provides the enthalpy and Gibbs energy of reaction by summing the species’ values times the stoichiometric coefficient. Only ethane is present in the feed; hence, \(n_{ C _{2} H _{6}}^{o}=1\). We can write the number of moles and mole fractions in terms of the extent of reaction according to Equations (9.8) and ( 9.10), respectively:

 

\(n_{i}=n_{i}^{o}+v_{i} \xi\) (9.8)

 

\(y_{i}=\frac{n_{i}}{n^{v}}=\frac{n_{i}^{o}+v_{i} \xi}{n^{o}+v \xi}\) (9.10)

 

 

\(\begin{aligned}n_{ C _{2} H _{6}} &=1-\xi \\n_{ C _{2} H _{4}} &=\xi \\n_{ H _{2}} &=\xi \\\hline n^{v} &=1+\xi\end{aligned}\) \(\begin{aligned}y_{ C _{2} H _{6}} &=\frac{n_{ C _{2} H _{6}}}{n^{v}}=\frac{1-\xi}{1+\xi} \\y_{ C _{2} H _{4}} &=\frac{n_{ C _{2} H _{4}}}{n^{v}}=\frac{\xi}{1+\xi} \\y_{ H _{2}} &=\frac{n_{ H _{2}}}{n^{v}}=\frac{\xi}{1+\xi}\end{aligned}\)

 

We have simply summed the number of moles of the individual species to obtain the total number of moles in the vapor, \(n^{v}\). Alternatively we could have used Equation (9.9).

 

\(n^{v}=\sum_{\text {vapor }} n_{i}=n^{o}+v \xi\) (9.9)

 

TABLE E9.6 Summary of Thermochemical Data from Appendix A.3
Species \(\nu_{i}\) \(\Delta h_{f}^{o}\) \(\Delta g_{f}^{o}\)
\(C _{2} H _{6}\) -1 \(-84.68 k J / mol\) \(-32.84 kJ / mol\)
\(C _{2} H _{4}\) 1 52.26 68.15
\(H _{2}\) 1 0 0
\(\sum v_{i}()_{i}\) 1 136.94 100.99

 

Since we are at low pressure, ideal gas behavior is assumed. Thus, the equilibrium constant can be written using Equation (9.27):

 

\(K=\prod\left(y_{i}\right)^{v_{i}} P^{v} \quad \text { (ideal gas) }\) (9.27)

 

\(K=\prod\left(y_{i}\right)^{v_{i}} P^{v}=\frac{y_{ C _{2} H _{4}} y_{ H _{2}}}{y_{ C _{2} H _{6}}} P\)

 

where \(v=(-1+1+1)=1\). Expressing K in terms of extent of reaction gives:

 

\(K=\frac{\xi^{2}}{(1-\xi)(1+\xi)} P=\frac{\xi^{2}}{\left(1-\xi^{2}\right)} P\)

 

rearranging, \(\xi=\sqrt{\frac{K}{K+P}}\) (E9.6)

 

Equation (E9.6) expresses the extent of reaction in terms of P and T (through K). To numerically evaluate Equation (E9.6), K must be determined from the thermochemical data in Table E9.6. First, we calculate K at 25°C:

 

\(K_{298}=\exp \left(-\frac{\Delta g_{ rxn }^{o}}{R T}\right)=\exp \left(-\frac{100,990}{[8.314][298.2]}\right)=2.04 \times 10^{-18}\)

 

If we assume \(\Delta h_{ rxn }^{o}=\) constant, we can correct for T using Equation (9.21):

 

\(\ln \frac{K_{T}}{K_{298}}=-\frac{\Delta h_{ rxn }^{o}}{R}\left(\frac{1}{T}-\frac{1}{298}\right)\) (9.21)

 

\(\ln \frac{K_{1273}}{K_{298}}=-\frac{\Delta h_{\text {rхn }}^{o}}{R}\left(\frac{1}{T}-\frac{1}{298}\right)=-\frac{136,940}{8.314}\left(\frac{1}{1273}-\frac{1}{298}\right)=42.33\)

 

Solving for the equilibrium constant:

 

\(K_{1273}=4.95\)

 

Using this value in Equation (E9.6) gives:

 

\(\xi=0.91\)

 

or, in terms of number of moles,

 

\(n_{ C _{2} H _{6}}=0.09, \quad n_{ C _{2} H _{4}}=0.91, \quad n_{ H _{2}}=0.91\)

 

We see that under these conditions, it is possible to convert 91% of the ethane in the feed to ethylene.
