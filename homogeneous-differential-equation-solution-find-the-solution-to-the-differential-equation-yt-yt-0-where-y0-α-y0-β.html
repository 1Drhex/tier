Guest Question.

Homogeneous Differential Equation Solution

Find the solution to the differential equation

\( \ddot{y}(t) + y(t) = 0, \) where \( y(0) = α, \dot{y}(0) = β.\)

Admin
Solution

Using Eq. (3.34), the Laplace transform of the differential equation is

\( L\left\{\ddot{f}\right\} = s^2 F(s) – sf(0^-) – \dot{f}(0^-). \) (3.34)

\( s^2 Y(s) -αs – β + Y(s) = 0, \)

\( (s^2 +1) Y(s) = αs + β, \)

\( Y(s) = \frac{αs}{s^2 +1} + \frac{β}{s^2 +1} . \)

After looking up in the transform tables (Table A.2, Appendix A) the two terms on the right side of the preceding equation, we get

\( y(t) = [α \cos t + β \sin t ] 1(t) ,\)

where \( 1(t) \) denotes a unit step function. We can verify that this solution is correct by substituting it back into the differential equation.
