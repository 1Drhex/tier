Guest Question.

Consider the circuit of Fig. 8.7 with Re= 0 (grounded emitter), Rc= 2K , Rb =100 K ,Vcc = 10 V. Assume that the circuit uses a silicon transistor with β = 50 and ICO = 0.

(a) Calculate IB , IC and VCE .

(b) Recalculate the values of part (a) by taking into account of the base-spreading resistance of 690 Ω.

(c) Specify a value of Rb in part (a) so that VCE = 7

Admin
Solution

(a) Using IC = βIB and Re = 0, Rc = 2 K, Rb = 100 K,Vcc = 10 V,VBE = 0.7 V and β = 50 in Eq. (8.26), \(I_{B}=\frac{V_{CC}-V_{BE}}{R_{e}+R_{c}+R_{b}}-\left\lgroup\frac{R_{e}+R_{c}}{R_{e}+R_{c}+R_{b}} \right\rgroup I_{C} \) , the base current can be calculated as

\(I_{B}=\frac{V_{CC}-V_{BE}}{(\beta +1)R_{c}+R_{b}}=\frac{10V-0.7V}{(1+50)(2K)+100K}=46.04 \mu A\approx 46\mu A \)

Using Re = 0, Rc = 2 K, Rb = 100 K,Vcc = 10 V, VBE = 0.7 V, β = 50 and ICO = 0 in Eq. (8.29), \(I_{C}\approx \frac{(V_{CC}-V_{BE})+(R_{e}+R_{c}+R_{b})I_{CO}}{R_{e}+R_{c}+R_{b}/\beta } \) , the collector current can be obtained as

\(I_{C}=\frac{50\times (10V-0.7V)}{(1+50)(0+2K)+100K}=\frac{465V}{202K} =2.30 \) mA

Applying KVL in the collector-emitter circuit, the collector-emitter voltage can be obtained as

VCE = VCC – (IC + IB )(Rc + Re )

= 10 V – (2.30 mA + 46 μA) × (2 K + 0) = 5.31 V

(b) Since base-spreading resistance r′bb = 690Ω appears effectively in series with the base circuit, the net value of resistance in the base circuit becomes

R′b =Rb + r′bb= 100K +690 Ω =100.69 KΩ

Now, replacing Rb = 100 K by R′b = 100.69 K in the calculations of part (a), we obtain

\(I_{B}=\frac{V_{CC}-V_{BE}}{(\beta +1)R_{c}+R^{′}_{b}}=\frac{10V-0.7V}{(1+50)(2K)+100.69K}=46.04 \mu A\approx 46\mu A \)

\(I_{C}=\frac{50\times (10V-0.7V)}{(1+50)(0+2K)+100.69K}=\frac{465V}{202.69K} =2.30 \) mA

VCE = VCC – (IC + IB )(Rc + Re )

= 10 V – (2.29 mA + 46 μA) × (2 K + 0) ≈ 5.31 V

Clearly, the base-spreading resistance has negligible effect on the quiescent point of the transistor circuit for Rb >>r′bb .

(b) For VCE = 7 V and Re = 0, application of the KVL in the collector-emitter circuit of part (a) gives

IC + IB =β IB + IB = (50+1) IB =\(\frac{V_{CC}-V_{CE}}{R_{c}}=\frac{10V-7V}{2K}=1.5 \) mA

which gives the base and collector currents as

\(I_{B}=\frac{1.5mA}{51}=0.0294 \) mA and IC = βIB =50 × 0.0294 mA = 1.47 mA

Using Re= 0 , the required value of Rb to produce IC = 1.47 mA can be obtained from Eq. (8.29) as

\(R_{b}=\frac{\beta (V_{CC}-V_{BE})}{I_{C}}-(1+\beta )R_{c} \)

\(=\frac{50\times (10V -0.7V)}{1.47 mA} -(1+50)\times 2K \)

= 316.32 K – 102 K = 214.32 K
