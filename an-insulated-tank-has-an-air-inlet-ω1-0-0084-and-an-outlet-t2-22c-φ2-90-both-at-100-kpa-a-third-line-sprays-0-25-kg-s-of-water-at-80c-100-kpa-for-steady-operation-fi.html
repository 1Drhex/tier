Guest Question.

An insulated tank has an air inlet, \(\omega_{1}\) = 0.0084, and an outlet, \(T_{2}\) = 22°C, \(\phi_{2}\) = 90% both at 100 kPa. A third line sprays 0.25 kg/s of water at 80°C, 100 kPa. For steady operation find the outlet specific humidity, the mass flow rate of air needed and the required air inlet temperature, \(T_{1}\).

Admin
Solution

Take CV tank in steady state. Continuity and energy equations are:

Continuity Eq. water: \(\dot{ m }_{3}+\dot{ m }_{ a } w _{1}=\dot{ m }_{ a } w _{2}\)

Energy Eq.: \(\dot{ m }_{3} h _{ f }+\dot{ m }_{ a } \tilde{ h }_{1}=\dot{ m }_{ a } \tilde{ h }_{2}\)

All state properties are known except \(T _{1}\).

From the psychrometric chart we get

State 2: \(w _{2}= 0 . 0 1 5 , \quad \tilde{ h }_{2}=79.5\) State 3: \(h _{ f }=334.91(\text { steam tbl })\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{ m }_{ a }=\dot{ m }_{3} /\left( w _{2}- w _{1}\right)=0.25 /(0.015-0.0084)= 3 7 . 8 8 kg / s \\&\tilde{ h }_{1}=\tilde{ h }_{2}-\left( w _{2}- w _{1}\right) h _{ f }=79.5-0.0066 \times 334.91=77.3 \\&\text { Chart }\left( w _{1}, \tilde{ h }_{1}\right) \Rightarrow \quad T _{1}= 3 6 . 5 ^{\circ} C\end{aligned}\)

Using the tables and formulas we get

State 2:

\(\begin{aligned}& P _{ g 2}=2.671 ; P _{ v 2}=0.9 \times 2.671=2.4039 kPa \\& w _{2}=0.622 \times 2.4039 /(100-2.4039)= 0 . 0 1 5 3\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\dot{ m }_{ a }=\dot{ m }_{3} /\left( w _{2}- w _{1}\right)=0.25 /(0.0153-0.0084)= 3 6 . 2 3 kg / s\)

 

To avoid iterations on \(T _{1}\) we use specific heat values also for water vapor by writing \(h _{ v 1}= h _{ v 2}+ C _{ p h 2 o }\left( T _{1}- T _{2}\right)\) so the energy equation is

\(C _{ p a } T _{1}+ w _{1} C _{ p h 2 o }\left( T _{1}- T _{2}\right)+ w _{1} h _{ v 2}= C _{ p a } T _{2}+ w _{2} h _{ v 2}-\left( w _{2}- w _{1}\right) h _{ f }\)

The equation now becomes

\(\begin{gathered}(1.004+0.0084 \times 1.872) T _{1}=(0.0084 \times 1.872+1.004) 22 \\+(0.0153-0.0084)(2541.7-334.91)=37.219 \\T _{1}= 3 6 . 5 ^{\circ} C\end{gathered}\)
