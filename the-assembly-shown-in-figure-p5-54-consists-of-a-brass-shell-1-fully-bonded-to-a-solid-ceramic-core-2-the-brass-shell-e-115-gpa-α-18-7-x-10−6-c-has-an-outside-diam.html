Guest Question.

The assembly shown in Figure P5.54 consists of a brass shell (1) fully bonded to a solid ceramic core (2). The brass shell [E = 115 GPa, \(\alpha=18.7 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} C\)] has an outside diameter of 50 mm and an inside diameter of 35 mm. The ceramic core [E = 290 GPa, \(\alpha=3.1 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} C\)] has a diameter of 35 mm. At a temperature of 15°C, the assembly is unstressed. Determine the largest temperature increase that is acceptable for the assembly if the normal stress in the longitudinal direction of the brass shell must not exceed 80 MPa.

Admin
Solution

Section properties: The cross-sectional areas of brass shell (1) and ceramic core (2) are:

\(A_{1}=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[\begin{array}{ll}\left.(50 mm )^{2}-(35 mm )^{2}\right]=1,001.3827 mm ^{2} & A_{2}=\frac{\pi}{4}(35 mm )^{2}=962.1128 mm ^{2}\end{array}\right.\)

Equilibrium
Consider a FBD cut through the assembly. Sum forces in the horizontal direction to obtain:

\(\Sigma F_{x}=-F_{1}-F_{2}=0 \quad \therefore F_{2}=-F_{1}\) (a)

Geometry of Deformations Relationship
For this configuration, the deformations of both members will be equal; therefore,

\(\delta_{1}=\delta_{2}\) (b)

Force-Temperature-Deformation Relationships
The relationship between internal force, temperature change, and deformation of an axial member can be stated for members (1) and (2):

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\alpha_{1} \Delta T L_{1} \quad \delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}+\alpha_{2} \Delta T L_{2}\) (c)

Compatibility Equation
Substitute the force-deformation relationships (c) into the geometry of deformation relationship (b) to derive the compatibility equation:

\(\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\alpha_{1} \Delta T L_{1}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}+\alpha_{2} \Delta T L_{2}\) (d)

Solve the Equations
Since a limiting stress is specified for brass shell (1), express Eq. (d) in terms of normal stress:

\(\sigma_{1} \frac{L_{1}}{E_{1}}+\alpha_{1} \Delta T L_{1}=\sigma_{2} \frac{L_{2}}{E_{2}}+\alpha_{2} \Delta T L_{2}\) (e)

Based on Eq. (a), the normal stress \(\sigma_{2}\) can be expressed in terms of \(\sigma_{1}\) as:

\(\sigma_{2}=\frac{F_{2}}{A_{2}}=-\frac{F_{1}}{A_{2}}=-\frac{F_{1}}{A_{2}} \frac{A_{1}}{A_{1}}=-\sigma_{1} \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\)

Substitute this expression into Eq. (e) to obtain

\(\sigma_{1} \frac{L_{1}}{E_{1}}+\alpha_{1} \Delta T L_{1}=-\sigma_{1} \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \frac{L_{2}}{E_{2}}+\alpha_{2} \Delta T L_{2}\)

Rearrange terms

\(\sigma_{1}\left[\frac{L_{1}}{E_{1}}+\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \frac{L_{2}}{E_{2}}\right]=\alpha_{2} \Delta T L_{2}-\alpha_{1} \Delta T L_{1}=\left(\alpha_{2} L_{2}-\alpha_{1} L_{1}\right) \Delta T\)

and solve for ΔT, recognizing that both the shell and the core have the same length:

\(\Delta T=\frac{\sigma_{1}\left[\frac{L_{1}}{E_{1}}+\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \frac{L_{2}}{E_{2}}\right]}{\alpha_{2} L_{2}-\alpha_{1} L_{1}}=\frac{\sigma_{1}\left[\frac{1}{E_{1}}+\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \frac{1}{E_{2}}\right]}{\alpha_{2}-\alpha_{1}}\) (f)

Substitute the problem data into Eq. (f) to compute ΔT that will produce a normal stress of 80 MPa in the brass shell:

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta T &=\frac{(\pm 80 MPa )\left[\frac{1}{115,000 MPa }+\frac{1,001.3827 mm ^{2}}{962.1128 mm ^{2}} \frac{1}{290,000 MPa }\right]}{3.1 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} C -18.7 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} C } \\&=\mp 62.9983^{\circ} C\end{aligned}\)

Since the problem asks for the largest temperature increase,

\(\Delta T_{\max }=63.0^{\circ} C\)
