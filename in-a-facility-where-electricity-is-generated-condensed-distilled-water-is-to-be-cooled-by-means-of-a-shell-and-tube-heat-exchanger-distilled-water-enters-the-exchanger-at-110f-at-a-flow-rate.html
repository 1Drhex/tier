Guest Question.

In a facility where electricity is generated, condensed (distilled) water is to be cooled by means of a shell and tube heat exchanger. Distilled water enters the exchanger at 110°F at a flow rate of 170,000 lbm/hr, and it is desired to cool the water to 95°F. Heat will be transferred to raw water (from a nearby lake), which is available at 65°F and 150,000 lbm/hr. Preliminary calculations indicate that it may be appropriate to use a heat exchanger that has a 17-1/4-in.-inside-diameter shell, and 3/4-in.-OD, 18 BWG tubes that are 16 ft long. The tubes are laid out on a 1-in. triangular pitch, and the tube fluid will make two passes. The exchanger contains baffles that are spaced 1 ft apart. Analyze the proposed heat exchanger to determine its suitability. Route the distilled water through the tubes

Admin
Solution

The flow rates are greater here than with double pipe heat exchangers. It is essential in this problem to evaluate properties at the average of inlet and outlet temperatures. This has been done in the results that follow. Accordingly, numbers in this example were obtained after several iterations.
Assumptions

  1. Steady-state conditions exist.
  2. Raw and distilled water properties can be obtained from the same property table.
  3. Raw water properties are evaluated at 68°F initially, and after several iterations, properties are evaluated at 76.7°F.
  4. Distilled water properties are evaluated initially at \(104\)°F, and after several iterations, at 99.8°F.
  5. All heat lost by the distilled water is transferred to the raw water.

Nomenclature

  1. T refers to the temperature of the warmer fluid.
  2. t refers to the temperature of the cooler fluid.
  3. w subscript refers to the warmer fluid.
  4. h subscript refers to hydraulic diameter.
  5. c subscript refers to the cooler fluid.
  6. s subscript refers to the shell flow area or dimension.
  7. t subscript refers to the tubular flow area or dimension.
  8. 1 subscript refers to an inlet condition.
  9. 2 subscript refers to an outlet condition.
  10. e subscript refers to equivalent diameter.

 

A. Fluid Properties

\(\begin{matrix} Dist\ water &\dot{m}_w= 170,000 lbm/hr& T_1 = 110°F \\ at\ 99.8°F&ρ = 62.1 lbm/ft^3& C_p = 0.998 BTU/lbm·°R\\& k_f = 0.361 BTU/ (hr·ft·°R)& α =5.82\times10^{-3} ft^2/hr\\ & ν = 0.759 \times 10^{-5} ft^2/s& Pr =4.69\end{matrix}\) \(\begin{matrix} Raw \ water & \dot{m}_c =150,000 lbm/hr & t_1 = 65°F \\ at 76.7°F & ρ = 62.3 lbm/ft^3 &C_p = 0.9985 BTU/lbm·°R\\ &k_f = 0.350 BTU/ (hr·ft·°R) & α = 5.62\times 10^{-3} ft^2/hr \\ & ν = 0.986 \times 10^{-5} ft^2/s & Pr =6.32\end{matrix}\)

B.tubing Sizes

\(ID_t = 0.0543\) ft \(OD_t = 0.06255\) ft
\(N_t\) =no. of tubes=196 ft \(N_p\)=no. of passes=2 ft

C. Shell Data
\(D_s\) = shell inside diameter = 17.25 in.=\(1.438 ft^2\)
B= baffle Spacing= 1 ft
\(N_b\)=Number of baffles=15
\(p_T\)=tube pitch=1 in.=0.08333 ft

\(C=\begin{pmatrix} clearance between \\ adjacent tubes \end{pmatrix}=P_T-OD_t\)=0.02078 ft

D.Flow Areas

\(A_t=N_t\pi (ID_t^2)/4N_p=0.2269 ft^2\)
\(A_s=D_sCB/P_T=0.3586 ft^2\) \(A_s>A_t\)

E.Fluid Velocities
Dist Water \(V_t = \dot{m}/ρA = 3.35\) ft/s \(G_t=\dot{m}/A=208 lbm/ft^2.s\)
Raw Water \(V_s = \dot{m}/ρA = 1.87\) ft/s \(G_s=\dot{m}/A=117 lbm/ft^2.s\)

F. shell Equivalent Diameters
\(D_e=\frac{3.46P_T^2-\pi OD^2_t}{\pi OD_t}=0.05901\) ft \(\begin{pmatrix} triangular \\ pitch \end{pmatrix}\)

G. Reynolds Numbers
Dist Water \(Re_t = V_tID_t/ν = 23,995\)
Raw water \(Re_s = V_sD_e/ν =11,163\)

H. Nusselt Numbers
Dist Water \(Nu_t = 117\)
Raw water \( Nu_s = 127\)

I. Convection Coefficients
Dist Water \(h_i = Nu_pk_f/ID_p = 775\) \(h_t = h_iID_t/OD_t = 673\)
Raw water \(h_0 = Nu_sk_f/D_e = 751 BTU/(hr.ft^2.°R)\)

J. Exchanger Coefficient
\(\frac{1}{U_o}= \frac{1}{h_t}+\frac{1}{h_0}\) \(U_o = 355 BTU/(hr·ft^2·°R)\)
K. Outlet Temperature Calculations (Exchanger length L = 16 ft)

\( R=\frac{\dot{m}_cC_{pc}}{\dot{m}_wC_{pw}} =0.8831\) \(A _0=N_t\pi OD_tL=616.2 ft^2\)

\(\frac{U_0A_0}{(\dot{m}C_{pc})_min} =1.460\) S=0.546 (Equation 9.19b \([S= \frac{2(1 – C_1)}{C_2 – C_1C_3}]\))

Dist Water \(T_2 =T_1-R(t_2-t_1)= 88.3°\)F
Raw water \(t_2 = S(T_1-t_1)+t_1=89.6°\)F

L. Log Mean Temperature Difference
Counterflow LMTD =\(\frac{ (T_1 – t_2) – (T_2 – t_1)}{\ln [(T_1 – t_2)/(T_2 – t_1)]} = 21.8°\)F
M. Heat Balance for Fluids
Dist Water \(q_w = \dot{m}_wC_{pw}(T_1 – T_2) = 3.67\times 10^6\) BTU/hr
Raw Water \(q_c = \dot{m}_cC_{pc}(t_2 – t_1) = 3.67\times 10^6\) BTU/hr

N. Fouling Factors and Design Coefficient (assumed for the oil)
F=0.772 (Equation9.4 \([F=\frac{\sqrt{R^2+1}\ln[(1-S)/(1-RS)] }{(R-1)\ln\left[\frac{2-S(R+1-\sqrt{R^2+1})}{2-S(R+1+\sqrt{R^2+1})} \right] } ]\))

\(q = U_oA_oF(LMTD) = 3.67\times 10^6\) BTU/hr

O. Fouling Factors and Design Coefficient

\(R_{di} = 0.00075 hr.ft^2.°R\) \(R_{do} = 0.00075\)
\(\frac{1}{U} = \frac{1}{U_o}+ R_{di} + R_{do}\) \(U =232 BTU/(hr·ft^2·°R)\)
P. Outlet Temperatures for the Design Coefficient
Without detailing the calculations, the outlet temperatures for \(U = 232 BTU/(hr·ft^2·°R)\) are calculated to be
Dist Water \(T_2 =T_1-R(t_2-t_1)= 91.3°\)F (after 1 year)
Raw water \(t_2 = S(T_1-t_1)+t_1=86.2°\)F (after 1 year)
Q. Friction Factors
Dist Water \(\left. \begin{matrix}Re_t= 2.4\times 10^4 \\ \frac{ε}{ID_t}= smooth \end{matrix} \right\}\) \(f_t = 0.025\)
Raw water \(Re_s= 1.11 \times 10^4\) \(f_s = 0.303\)

R. Pressure Drop Calculations
Dist Water \(∆p_t = \frac{ρV_t^2}{2g_c}\left\lgroup\frac{f_tL}{ID_t}+4\right\rgroup N_p= 1.7\) psi

Raw water \(∆p_s =\frac{ρV_s^2}{2g_c}\frac{ D_s}{D_e}f_s(N_b+ 1)= 2.76 \) psi

S. Summary of Performance

\(T_2=95°\)F (required)
\(T_1=110°\)F(distilled) \(t_1=65°F\) (raw)
Δp=1.7 psi (distilled) Δp=2.8psi (raw)
\(R_d=0.00075\)(distilled) \(R_d=0.00075\) (raw)
New After 1 year
\(U_0=355 BTU/(hr·ft^2·°R)\) U=232BTU/\((hr·ft^2·°R)\)
\(T_2=88.3°\)F(distilled) \(T_2=9.13°\)F(distilled)
\(t_2=89.6 °\)F(raw) \(t_2=86.2°\)F(raw)
\(q=3.67 ×10^6\) BTU/hr \(q=3.17 ×10^6\) BTU/hr

 

The required outlet temperature is 95°F (\(=T_2\)), and this exchanger exceeds that requirement when fouled. The exchanger is suitable: both pressure drops are less than 10 psi, and F is greater than 0.75
