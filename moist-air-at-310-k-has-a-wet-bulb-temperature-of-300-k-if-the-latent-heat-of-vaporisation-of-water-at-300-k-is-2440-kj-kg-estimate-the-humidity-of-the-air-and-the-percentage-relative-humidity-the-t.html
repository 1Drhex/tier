Guest Question.

Moist air at 310 K has a wet-bulb temperature of 300 K. If the latent heat of vaporisation of water at 300 K is 2440 kJ/kg, estimate the humidity of the air and the percentage relative humidity. The total pressure is \( 105 kN/m^{2} \) , and the vapour pressure of water vapour at 300 K is 3.60 and 6.33 \( kN/m^{2} \) at 310 K.

Admin
Solution

The humidity of air saturated at the wet-bulb temperature is given by.
\( H_{w} = \frac{P_{w0}}{P0p_{w0}}\frac{M_{w}}{M_{A}} \)
\( = (\frac{3.6}{105.0-3.6} )(\frac{18}{29} ) = 0.0220kg/kg \)
Therefore, taking \( (h/h_{D}\rho _{A}) \) as 1.0kj/kg K, in Equation
\( (H – H_{w}) = -\frac{h}{h_{D}\rho _{A}\lambda }(\theta -\theta _{w}) \),
\( (0.0220-H) = (\frac{1.0}{2440} )(310-300) \)
H=0.018 kg/kg
or
at 310K, \( P_{w0} = 6.33kN/m^{2}\)
In Equation
\( H_{0}= \frac{P_{w0}}{P-P_{w0}}(\frac{M_{w}}{M_{A}} ) \),
\( 0.0780=\frac{18P_{w}}{(105.0-P_{w})29} \)
\( \therefore P_{w} = 2.959kN/m^{2} \)
and the percentage relative humidity
\( = \frac{(100\times 2.959)}{6.33} = 46.7 \) %.
