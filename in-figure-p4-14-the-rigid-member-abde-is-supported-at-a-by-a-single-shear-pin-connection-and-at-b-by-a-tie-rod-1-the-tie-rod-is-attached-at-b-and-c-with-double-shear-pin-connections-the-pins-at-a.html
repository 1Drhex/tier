Guest Question.

In Figure P4.14, the rigid member ABDE is supported at A by a single shear pin connection and at B by a tie rod (1). The tie rod is attached at B and C with double shear pin connections. The pins at A, B, and C each have an ultimate shear strength of 80 ksi, and tie rod (1) has a yield strength of 60 ksi. A concentrated load of P = 24 kips is applied perpendicular to DE, as shown. A factor of safety of 2.0 is required for all components. Determine:
(a) the minimum required diameter for the tie rod.
(b) the minimum required diameter for the pin at B.
(c) the minimum required diameter for the pin at A.

Admin
Solution

Member (1) is a two-force member that is oriented at θ with respect to the horizontal axis:

\(\tan \theta=\frac{9 ft }{6 ft }=1.5 \quad \therefore \theta=56.310^{\circ}\)

From a FBD of rigid structure ABDE, the following equilibrium equations can be written:

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma F_{x}=P \cos 70^{\circ}-F_{1} \cos 56.310^{\circ}+A_{x}=0 (a) \\&\Sigma F_{y}=-P \sin 70^{\circ}-F_{1} \sin 56.310^{\circ}+A_{y}=0 (b) \\&\Sigma M_{A}=\left(F_{1} \cos 56.310^{\circ}\right)(9 ft )-\left(P \cos 70^{\circ}\right)\left[13 ft +(8 ft ) \sin 20^{\circ}\right] \\&\quad-\left(P \sin 70^{\circ}\right)\left[(8 ft ) \cos 20^{\circ}\right]=0 (c)\end{aligned}\)

From Eq. (c):

\(\begin{aligned}F_{1} &=P \frac{\left(\cos 70^{\circ}\right)\left[13 ft +(8 ft ) \sin 20^{\circ}\right]+\left(\sin 70^{\circ}\right)\left[(8 ft ) \cos 20^{\circ}\right]}{\left(\cos 56.310^{\circ}\right)(9 ft )} \\&=(24 kips ) \frac{5.382084 ft +7.064178 ft }{4.992293 ft }=59.834286 kips (d)\end{aligned}\)

Substitute \(F_{1}\) into Eq. (a) to obtain \(A_{x}\):

\(\begin{aligned}A_{x} &=F_{1} \cos 56.310^{\circ}-P \cos 70^{\circ} \\&=(59.834286 kips ) \cos 56.310^{\circ}-(24 kips ) \cos 70^{\circ} \\&=24.981548 kips\end{aligned}\)

and substitute \(F_{1}\) into Eq. (b) to obtain \(A_{y}\):

\(\begin{aligned}A_{y} &=F_{1} \sin 56.310^{\circ}+P \sin 70^{\circ} \\&=(59.834286 kips ) \sin 56.310^{\circ}+(24 kips ) \sin 70^{\circ} \\&=72.337797 kips\end{aligned}\)

The resultant pin force at A is found from \(A_{x} \text { and } A_{y} \text { : }\)

\(|A|=\sqrt{A_{x}^{2}+A_{y}^{2}}=\sqrt{(24.981548 kips )^{2}+(72.337797 kips )^{2}}=76.529959 \text { kips }\) (e)

(a) The allowable normal stress for tie rod (1) is

\(\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\sigma_{Y}}{ FS }=\frac{60 ksi }{2.0}=30.0 ksi\)

The minimum cross-sectional area required for the tie rod is

\(A_{\min } \geq \frac{F_{1}}{\sigma_{\text {allow }}}=\frac{59.834286 kips }{30.0 ksi }=1.994476 in .^{2}\)

Therefore, the minimum tie rod diameter is

\(\frac{\pi}{4} d_{1}^{2} \geq 1.994476 in .{ }^{2} \quad \therefore d_{1} \geq 1.593564 in .=1.594 in.\)

 

(b) The allowable shear stress for the pins at A, B, and C is

\(\tau_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\tau_{U}}{ FS }=\frac{80 ksi }{2.0}=40.0 ksi\)

The double-shear pin connection at B and C must support a load of \(F_{1}\) = 59.834286 kips. The shear area \(A_{V}\) required for these pins is

\(A_{V} \geq \frac{F_{1}}{\tau_{\text {allow }}}=\frac{59.834286 kips }{40.0 ksi }=1.495857 in .^{2}\)

The pin diameter can be computed from

\(\text { (2 surfaces) } \frac{\pi}{4} d_{ pin }^{2} \geq 1.495857 in .^{2} \quad \therefore d_{ pin } \geq 0.975855 in .=0.976 in.\)

 

(c) The pin at A is a single shear connection; therefore, \(A_{V}=A_{ pin }\). The shear area AV required for this pin is

\(A_{V} \geq \frac{|A|}{\tau_{\text {allow }}}=\frac{76.529959 kips }{40.0 ksi }=1.913249 in. ^{2}\)

The pin diameter can be computed from

\((1 \text { surface }) \frac{\pi}{4} d_{\text {pin }}^{2} \geq 1.913249 \text { in. }^{2} \quad \therefore d_{\text {pin }} \geq 1.560777 \text { in. }=1.561 in.\)
