Guest Question.

(a) Determine the lower cutoff frequency for the network of Fig. 11.32 using the following parameters:
\(C_{G} = 0.01 \mu F , C_{C} = 0.5 \mu F , C_{S} = 2 \mu F R_{sig} = 10 k \Omega , R_{G} = 1 M \Omega , R_{D} = 4.7 k \Omega , R_{S} = 1 k \Omega , R_{L} = 2.2 k \Omega I_{DSS} = 8mA , V_{P} = - 4 V r_{d} =\infty \Omega , V_{DD} = 20 V\)
(b) Sketch the frequency response using a Bode plot.

Admin
Solution

(a) DC Analysis: Plotting the transfer curve of \(I_{D} = I_{DSS}(1 – V_{GS}/V_{P})^{2}\) and superimposing
the curve defined by \(V_{GS} = – I_{D}R_{S}\) will result in an intersection at \(V_{GS_{Q}} = – 2 V\) and \(I_{D_{Q}} = 2 mA\). In addition,
\(g_{mo} = \frac{2I_{DSS}}{\left|V_{P}\right|} = \frac{2(8 mA)}{4 V} = 4 mS\)

\(g_{m} = g_{mo}\left(1 – \frac{V_{GS_{Q}}}{V_{P}}\right) = 4 mS\left(1 – \frac{- 2 V}{- 4 V}\right) = 2 mS\)

\(C_{G}\)
Eq. (11.34): \(f_{L_{G}} = \frac{1}{2 \pi(R_{sig} + R_{i})C_{G}}\)

 

\(f_{L_{G}} = \frac{1}{2 \pi(10 k \Omega + 1 M \Omega)(0.01 \mu F)} \cong 15.8 Hz\)

 

\(C_{C}\)

Eq. (11.36): \(f_{L_{C}} = \frac{1}{2 \pi (R_{o} + R_{L})C_{C}}\)

 

\(f_{L_{C}} = \frac{1}{2 \pi (4.7 k \Omega + 2.2 k \Omega)(0.5 \mu F)} \cong 46.13 Hz\)

 

\(C_{S}\)

 

\(R_{eq} = R_{S} \parallel \frac{1}{g_{m}} = 1 k \Omega \parallel \frac{1}{2 m S} = 1 k \Omega \parallel 0.5 k \Omega = 333.33 \Omega\)

Eq. (11.38): \(f_{LS} = \frac{1}{2 \pi R_{eq}C_{s}}\)

 

\(f_{LS} = \frac{1}{2 \pi (333.33 \Omega)(2 \mu F)} = 238.73 Hz\)

Since \(f_{LS}\) is the largest of the three cutoff frequencies, it defines the low cutoff frequency for the network of Fig. 11.32.

(b) The midband gain of the system is determined by \(A_{v_{mid}} = \frac{V_{o}}{V_{i}} = – g_{m}(R_{D}\parallel R_{L}) =- (2 m S)(4.7 k \Omega) \parallel 2.2 k \Omega) = -(2 mS)(1.499 k \Omega) \cong – 3 \) Using the midband gain to normalize the response for the network of Fig. 11.32 will result in the frequency plot of Fig. 11.36. Using PSpice Windows, the network will appear as shown in Fig. 11.37, with the JFET parameters Beta set at \(0.5 m A/V^{2}\) and Vto at – 4 V (all others set to zero) and
the frequency of interest at a midband value of 10 kHz. The resulting dc levels confirm that \(V_{GS}\) is – 2 V and place \(V_{D}\) at 10.60 V, which should be right in the middle
of the linear active region since\(V_{GS} = 1/2(V_{D} = – 4 V)\) and \(V_{DS} = 1/2(V_{DD} = 2 V)\).
The 0-V levels clearly reveal that the capacitors have isolated the transistor for the dc biasing. The ac response results in an ac level of 2.993 mV across the load for a gain of 2.993, which is essentially equal to the calculated gain of 3.
Returning to Analysis and choosing Automatically run Probe after simulation followed by Setup-AC Sweep-Decade-Pts/Decade = 1000, Start Freq.: 10Hz, and End Freq.: 10 kHz will setup Simulation-Trace-Add-Trace Expression: DB (V(RL:1)/2.993mV)-OK, which will result in the plot of Fig. 11.38, with a low cutoff frequency of 227.5 Hz primarily determined by the source capacitance.
