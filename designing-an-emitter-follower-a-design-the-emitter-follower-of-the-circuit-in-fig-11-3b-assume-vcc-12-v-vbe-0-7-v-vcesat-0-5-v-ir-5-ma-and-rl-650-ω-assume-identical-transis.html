Designing an emitter follower

(a) Design the emitter follower of the circuit in Fig. 11.3(b). Assume \( V_{ CC }=12 V , V_{ BE }=0.7 V , V_{ CE ( sat )}=0.5 V \), \( I_{ R }=5 mA , \text { and } R_{ L }=650 \Omega \). Assume identical transistors of current gain \( h_{ fe }=\beta_{ F }=100 \). Note that \( h_{ fe } \) is the hybrid parameter and both \( h_{ fe } \text { and } \beta_{ F } \) represent the current gain of a BJT.

(b) Determine the critical value of load resistance to avoid clipping (or distortion).
(c) Calculate the peak-to-peak output voltage swing if \( R_{ L }=650 \Omega \).

(d) Calculate the peak-to-peak output voltage swing and the power efficiency \( \eta \text { if } R_{ L }=2.5 k \Omega \).
(e) Use PSpice/SPICE to plot the transfer function of the emitter follower for the values in part (a). The PSpice model parameters for the diode are

IS=100E-15 RS=16 BV=100 IBV=100E-15

and those for the transistors are

BF=100 VA=100

Admin
Solution

(a) Determine the value of \( R_{1} \) from Eq. (11.3):

\( I_{ R }=\frac{V_{ CC }-V_{ BE }}{R_{1}} \) (11.3)

\( R_{1}=\frac{V_{ CC }-V_{ BE }}{I_{ R }}=\frac{12 V -0.7 V }{5 mA }=2260 \Omega \)

(b) From Eq. (11.7), we find the critical value of load resistance to avoid clipping:

\(R_{ L } \geq R_{1}\) (11.7)

 

\( R_{ L \text { (crit) }}=R_{1}=2260 \Omega \)

(c) For \( R_{ L }=650 \Omega \), the negative peak output voltage is

\( -V_{ O (\max )}=-I_{ R } R_{ L }=-5 m A \times 650 \Omega=-3.25 V \)

The positive peak output voltage is

\( +V_{ O (\max )}=V_{ CC }-V_{ CE ( sat )}=12 V -0.5 V =11.5 V \)

Therefore, the peak-to-peak output voltage swing will be from 11.5 V to -3.25 V .

(d) For \( R_{ L }=2.5 k \Omega \) (which is greater than \( R_{ L \text { (crit) }}=2260 \Omega \)), the negative peak output voltage will be limited to

\( -V_{ O (\max )}=-V_{ CC }+V_{ CE ( sat )}=-12 V +0.5 V =-11.5 V \)

Therefore, the peak-to-peak output voltage swing will be from -11.5 V to +11.5 V. Thus, Eq. (11.13) gives

\( \eta=\frac{V_{ p }^{2}}{4 R_{ L } V_{ CC } I_{ R }}=\frac{1}{4}\left(\frac{V_{ p }}{R_{ L } I_{ R }}\right)\left(\frac{V_{ p }}{V_{ CC }}\right) \) (11.13)

 

\( \eta=\frac{(11.5 V )^{2}}{4 \times 2.5 k \Omega \times 12 V \times 5 mA }=22 \% \)

(e) The emitter-follower circuit for PSpice simulation is shown in Fig. 11.5.

The transfer characteristic is shown in Fig. 11.6(a). For \( R_{ L }=650 \Omega \text {, it gives }+V_{ O (\max )}=12 V \) (expected value is 11.5 V) and \( -V_{O(\max )}=-3.08 V \) (expected value is -3.25 V). For \( R_{ L }=2.5 k \Omega \text {, it gives }+V_{ O (\max )}= 13.26 V \) (expected value is 11.5 V) and \( -V_{ O (\max )}=-10.25 V \) (expected value is -11.5 V). The output voltage, shown in Fig. 11.6(b), has an offset of 0.8 V (expected value is 0.7 V) and is clamped to a certain value, thereby introducing distortion.

The Fourier analysis (.FOUR) gives the following results (from the PSpice output file).
For \( R_{ L }=650 \Omega \),

DC COMPONENT=1.300972E+00
TOTAL HARMONIC DISTORTION=3.131888E+01 PERCENT

For \( R_{ L }=2.5 k \Omega ,\)

DC COMPONENT=7.972313E-01
TOTAL HARMONIC DISTORTION=1.895552E-01 PERCENT
