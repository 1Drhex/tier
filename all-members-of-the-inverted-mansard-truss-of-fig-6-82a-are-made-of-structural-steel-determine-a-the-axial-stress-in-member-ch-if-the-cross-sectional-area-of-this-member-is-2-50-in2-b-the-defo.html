Guest Question.

All members of the inverted Mansard truss of Fig. 6-82a are made of structural steel. Determine
(a) The axial stress in member CH if the cross-sectional area of this member is 2.50 in \(^2\).

(b) The deformation of member BH if the cross-sectional area of this member is 2.50 in\(^2\).

Admin
Solution

First find the support reactions by drawing a free-body diagram of the entire truss (Fig. 6-82b) and writing the equilibrium equations:

\( +\searrow \sum{M_A}=0 : \quad\quad F_y(18)-10(6)-8(12)-4(18)-2(24)=0 \)

 

\( F_y=15.333 kip =15.333kip\uparrow \)

 

\( +\rightarrow \sum{F_x}=0 \quad \quad A_x=0 \)

 

\( A_x=0kip \)

 

\( +\uparrow \sum{F_y}=0 \quad \quad A_y-10-8-4-2+F_y=0 \)

 

\( A_y=8.667kip=8.667kip\uparrow \)

(a) Section aâ€“a of Fig. 6-82b passes through members BC, CH, and GH. A free-body diagram for the part of the truss to the left of this section is shown in Fig. 6-82c. The force in member CH can be found by summing forces in the y-direction. Thus,

\( \phi =tan^{-1}\frac{8}{6}=53.13^\circ \)

 

\( +\uparrow \sum{F_y}=0 \quad\quad T_{CH}sin53.13^\circ +8.667-10=0 \)

 

\( T_{CH}=1.6663kip=1666.3lb \quad T \)

The axial stress in member CH is given by

\( \sigma _{avg}=\frac{F}{A} \)

as

\( \sigma =\frac{T_{CH}}{A_{CH}}=\frac{1666.3}{2.5}psi\cong 667psi\quad T \)

(b) The force in member BH can be determined from a free-body diagram of
the pin at B (Fig. 6-82d) and the equilibrium equation\( \sum{F_y=0} \). Thus,

\( +\uparrow \sum{F_y}=0 :\quad\quad -T_{BH}-10=0 \)

 

\( T_{BH}=-10.00kip=10,000lb\quad C \)

The deformation of member BH is given by

\( \delta =\frac{PL}{EA} \)

as

\( \delta =\frac{T_{BH}L_{BH}}{E_{BH}A_{BH}} =\frac{-10,000(8)(12)}{29,000,000(2.5)}=-0.01324in \)
