Guest Question.

The 9 m long simply supported beam-column shown in Figure 7.19b has a factored design axial compression force of 200 kN and a design concentrated load of 20 kN (which includes an allowance for self-weight) acting in the major principal plane at mid-span. The beam-column is the 254 × 146 UB 37 of S275 steel shown in Figure 7.19a. The beam-column is continuously braced against lateral deflections v and twist rotations φ. Check the adequacy of the beam-column.

Admin
Solution

Simplified approach for cross-section resistance.

\(t_{f} = 10.9 mm, f_{y} = 275 N/mm^2\)

 

\(ε = (235/275)^{0.5} = 0.924\)

 

\(c_{f} /(t_{f} ε) = ((146.4 − 6.3 − 2 × 7.6)/2)/(10.9 × 0.924) = 6.20 < 9\) and the flange is Class 1.

 

\(c_{w} = 256.0 − (2 × 10.9) − (2 × 7.6) = 219.0 mm.\)

 

The compression proportion of web is

\(α =\left(\frac{h}{2}-(t_{f} + r) +\frac{1}{2}\frac{N_{Ed}}{t_{w}f_{y}} \right) /c_{w}\)

 

\(=\left(\frac{256}{2} − (10.9 + 7.6) +\frac{1}{2}\times \frac{200 × 10^3}{6.3 × 275} \right)/ 219.0\)

 

\(= 0.76 > 0.5\)

 

\(c_{w}/t_{w} = 219.0/6.3 = 34.8 < 41.3 =\frac{396ε}{13α − 1}\) and the web is Class 1.

 

\(M_{c,y,Rd} = 275 × 483 × 10^3/1.0 Nmm = 132.8 kNm.\)

 

\(M_{y,Ed} = 20 × 9/4 = 45.0 kNm.\)

 

\(\frac{200 × 10^3}{47.2 × 10^2 × 275/1.0} +\frac{45.0}{132.8} = 0.493 ≤ 1\)

and the cross-section resistance is adequate.

 

Alternative approach for cross-section resistance.

Because the section is Class 1, Clause 6.2.9.1 can be used. No reduction in plastic moment resistance is required provided both

\(N_{Ed} = 200 kN < 324.5 kN = (0.25 × 47.2 × 10^2 × 275/1.0)/10^3 = 0.25 N_{pl,Rd}\) and

 

\(N_{Ed} = 200 kN < 202.9 kN = \frac{0.5 × (256.0 − 2 × 10.9) × 6.3 × 275}{1.0 × 10^3} =\frac{0.5 h_{w}t_{w}f_{y}}{γ_{M0}}\)

 

and so no reduction in the plastic moment resistance is required.

Thus \(M_{N,y,Rd} = M_{pl,y,Rd} = 132.8 kNm > 45.0 kNm = M_{y,Ed}\) and the cross-section resistance is adequate.

Compression member buckling resistance.

Because the member is continuously braced, beam lateral buckling and column minor axis buckling need not be considered.

\(\bar{\lambda }_{y} =\sqrt{\frac{Af_{y}}{N_{cr,y}} }=\frac{L_{cr,y}}{i_{y}} \frac{1}{\lambda _{1}} =\frac{9000}{(10.8 × 10)}\frac{1}{93.9 × 0.924}= 0.960\)

 

For a rolled UB section (with h/b > 1.2 and \(t_{f} ≤ 40 mm)\), buckling about the y-axis, use buckling curve (a) with α = 0.21

\(Φ_{y} = 0.5[1 + 0.21(0.960 − 0.2) + 0.960^2] = 1.041\)

 

\(\chi _{y} = 1/(1.041 +\sqrt{1.041^2 − 0.960^2}) = 0.693\)

 

\(N_{b,y,Rd} = χ_{y}Af_{y}/γ_{M1} = 0.693 × 47.2 × 10^2 × 275/1.0 N = 900 kN > 200 kN = N_{Ed}\)

 

Beam-column member resistance – simplified approach (Annex B).

 

\(α_{h} = M_{h}/M_{s} = 0, ψ = 0, C_{my} = 0.90 + 0.1α_{h} = 0.90\)

 

 

\(C_{my}\left(1 + (\bar{\lambda }_{y} − 0.2)\frac{N_{Ed}}{\chi _{y}N_{Rk}/γ_{M1}} \right)= 0.90 ×\left(1 + (0.960 − 0.2) ×\frac{200 × 10^3}{0.693 × 47.2 × 10^2 × 275/1.0} \right) = 1.052\)

 

\(< C_{my}\left(1 + 0.8\frac{N_{Ed}}{\chi _{y}N_{Rk}/γ_{M1}} \right) = 0.90\times \left(1 + 0.8 ×\frac{200 × 10^3}{0.693 × 47.2 × 10^2 × 275/1.0} \right) = 1.060\)

 

so that \(k_{yy} = 1.052\)

 

\(\frac{N_{Ed}}{N_{b,y,Rd}} + k_{yy}\frac{M_{y,Ed}}{M_{c,y,Rd}} =\frac{200}{900} + 1.052 ×\frac{45.0}{132.8} = 0.222 + 0.356 = 0.579 < 1 \)

 

and the member resistance is adequate.

Beam-column member resistance – more exact approach (Annex A).

\(\bar{\lambda}_{max} = \bar{\lambda}_{y} = 0.960\)

\(N_{cr, y} = π^2EI_{y}/L^2_{cr} = π^2 × 21 0000 × 5537 × 10^4/9000^2 = 1417 kN.\)

 

Since there is no lateral buckling, \(\bar{\lambda}_{0} = 0, b_{LT} = 0, C_{mLT} = 1.0\)

 

\(C_{my} = C_{my,0} = 1 − 0.18N_{Ed} /N_{cr, y} = 1 − 0.18 × 200/1417 = 0.975\)

 

\(w_{y} =\frac{W{pl,y}}{W_{el,y}}=\frac{483}{433}= 1.115,\)

 

\(n_{pl} =\frac{N_{Ed}}{N_{Rk}/γ_{M1}}=\frac{200 × 10^3}{47.2 × 10^2 × 275/1.0}= 0.154\)

 

\(C_{yy} = 1 + \left(w_{y} – 1 \right) \left[\left(2 −\frac{1.6}{w_{y}}C^2_{my}\bar{\lambda}_{max} −\frac{1.6}{w_{y}} C^2_{my}\bar{\lambda}^2_{max} \right) n_{pl} – b_{LT} \right] ≥ \frac{W{el,y}}{W_{pl,y}}\)

 

\(= 1 + (1.115 − 1) \left[\left(2 −\frac{1.6}{ 1.115} × 0.975^2 × 0.960 − \frac{1.6}{ 1.115} ×0.975^2 × 0.960^2 \right) × 0.154 – 0 \right] = 0.990 > 0.896 = 1/1.115\)

 

\(µ_{y} =\frac{ 1− N_{Ed} /N_{cr, y}}{ 1 – χ_{y}N_{Ed} /N_{cr, y} }=\frac{ 1 − 200/1417}{ 1− 0.693 × 200/1417} = 0.952\)

 

\(k_{yy} = C_{my}C_{mLT}\frac{ µ_{y}}{ (1 – N_{Ed} /N_{cr, y})}\frac{1}{ C_{yy}} = 0.975 × 1.0 × \frac{ 0.952}{ 1 − 200/1417} ×\frac{1}{0.990} = 1.091\)

 

\(\frac{ N_{Ed}}{N_{b,y,Rd} }+k_{yy}\frac{ M_{y,Ed} }{ M_{c,y,Rd} }=\frac{200}{900} + 1.091 ×\frac{ 45.0}{ 132.8} = 0.222 + 0.370 = 0.592 < 1\)

and the member resistance is adequate.
