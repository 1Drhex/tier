VELOCITY ANALYSIS OF A SYSTEM WITH A TWO DEGREE OF FREEDOM PAIR

Link 2 of the mechanism shown in Figure 3.4 has a circular shape with center at C, and executes full rotations about base pivot O\(_2\). Links 2 and 3 remain in direct contact as the mechanism moves. Using the instantaneous centers, determine
(a) the magnitude and direction of rotational speed of link 3 when θ\(_2\) = 0
(b) the magnitude and direction of rotational speed of link 3 when θ\(_2\) = 135°
(c) all values of θ2 in the range 0 ≤ θ\(_2\) ≤ 360° for which the rotational speed of link 3 is zero

\( r_{O_2O_3}\) =10. cm; \( r_{O_2C}\)= 2.0 cm;ρ=3.0 cm ; \(\dot{θ}_2\)=12.0 rad/sec CCW (constant)

Admin
Solution

(a) The mechanism has three instantaneous centers all of which lie on the same straight line. There may be relative sliding and turning between links 2 and 3 and therefore the instantaneous center between these links lies on the common normal that passes through the point of contact. Since link 2 is circular, the common normal always passes through point C, the center of the circle. When θ\(_2\) = 0 (see Figure 3.5(a)),

\(r_{p_{1,2}C}=r_{p_{1,2}p_{2,3}}=2.0\) cm

\(r_{p_{1,3}p_{2,3}}=r_{p_{1,2}p_{1,3}}-r_{p_{1,2}p_{2,3}} =8.0\)cm

Using Equation (3.2-1b), the rotational speed of link 3 is

\(\left| \overline{v}_{p_{1,i}} \right| =r_{p_{1,i}p_{i,j}}\left|\dot{\theta}_i\right|=r_{p_{1,j}p_{i,j}} \left|\dot{\theta}_j\right|\) (3.2-1b)

\(\left|\dot{\theta}_3\right| =\frac{r_{p_{1,2}p_{2,3}}}{r_{p_{1,3}p_{2,3}}}\left|\dot{\theta}_2\right|=\frac{2.0}{8.0}12.0=3.0\frac{rad}{sec}\)

\(\dot{\theta}_3=3.0 \frac{rad}{sec}\)CW

b) When θ\(_2\) = 135° (see Figure 3.5(b)), we apply the cosine law to triangle \(O_2O_3C\)

\(r^2_{p_{1,3}C}=r^2_{p_{1,2}C}+r^2_{p_{1,2}p_{1,3}}-2r_{p_{1,2}C}r_{p_{1,3}}\cos\theta_2\); Solving \(r_{p_{1,3}C}=11.5\) cm

and apply the sine law

\(\frac{r_{p_{1,2}C}}{\sin\alpha }=\frac{r_{p_{1,3}C}}{\sin\theta_2 }\); solving \(\alpha =7.1°\)

For triangle \(BO_3C \) we have

\(\sin\beta =\frac{\rho }{r_{p_{1,3}C}}\);solving \(\beta =15.1°\)

and

\(\cos \beta =\frac{r_{p_{1,3}B}}{r_{p_{1,3}C}}\); Solving \(r_{p_{1,3}B}=11.1\) cm

For triangle \(BP_{1,3}P_{2,3}\) we have

\(\cos( \alpha+\beta) =\frac{r_{p_{1,3}B}} {r_{p_{1,3}p_{2,3}}}\) ;Solving \(r_{p_{1,3}p_{2,3}}=12.0\) cm

From Figure 3.5(b) we obtain

\(r_{p_{1,2}p_{2,3}}=r_{p_{1,3}p_{2,3}}-r_{p_{1,2}p_{2,3}}\); Solving \(r_{p_{1,2}p_{2,3}}=2.0\) cm

Using Equation (3.2-1b), the rotational speed of link 3 is

\(\left|\dot{\theta}_3\right| =\frac{r_{p_{1,2}p_{2,3}}}{r_{p_{1,3}p_{2,3}}}\left|\dot{\theta}_2\right|=\frac{2.0}{12.0}12.0=2.0\frac{rad}{sec}\)

\(\left|\dot{\theta}_3\right| =2.0\frac{rad}{sec}\)CCW

(c) By inspection of Equation (3.2-1b), for the rotational speed of link 3 to be zero we require

\(r_{p_{1,2}p_{2,3}}=0\)

The two corresponding geometries of the mechanism are shown in Figure 3.5(c). For limit geometry 1, we have

\(r_{p_{2,3}B_{1}}=\rho +r_{p_{2,3}C_1}=5.0\) cm

\((\theta _2)_1=\cos^{-1}\left\lgroup\frac{r_{p_{2,3}B_1}}{r_{p_{2,3}p_{1,3}}}\right\rgroup =60°\)

and for limit geometry 2, we have

\(r_{p_{2,3}B_{2}}=\rho-r_{p_{2,3}C_2}=1.0 cm\)

\((\theta _2)_2=180°+\cos^{-1}\left\lgroup \frac{r_{p_{2,3}B_2}}{r_{p_{2,3}p_{1,3}}} \right\rgroup =264.2°\)
