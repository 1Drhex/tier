Guest Question.

The rigid bar in Figure P3.25/26 is supported by axial bar (1) and by a pin connection at C. Axial bar (1) has a crosssectional area of \(A_{1}=275 mm ^{2}\) , an elastic modulus of E = 200 GPa, and a coefficient of thermal expansion of α = 11.9 × \(10^{-6}\) /°C. The pin at C has a diameter of 25 mm. After load P has been applied and the temperature of the entire assembly has been increased by 20°C, the total strain in bar (1) is measured as 925 με (elongation). Determine:
(a) the magnitude of load P.
(b) the average shear stress in pin C.

Admin
Solution

The total strain in bar (1) consists of thermal strain as well as normal strain caused by normal stress:

\(\varepsilon=\varepsilon_{\sigma}+\varepsilon_{T}\)

The normal strain due to the increase in temperature is:

\(\varepsilon_{T}=\alpha \Delta T=\left(11.9 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(20^{\circ} C \right)=0.000238 mm / mm\)

Therefore, the normal stress in bar (1) causes a normal strain of:

\(\varepsilon_{\sigma}=\varepsilon-\varepsilon_{T}=0.000925 mm / mm -0.000238 mm / mm =0.000687 mm / mm\)

From Hooke’s law, the normal stress in bar (1) can be calculated as:

\(\sigma_{1}=E \varepsilon_{\sigma}=(200,000 MPa )(0.000687 mm / mm )=137.4 MPa\)

and thus the axial force in bar (1) must be:

\(F_{1}=\sigma_{1} A_{1}=\left(137.4 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(275 mm ^{2}\right)=37,785 N\)

Next, consider a free-body diagram of the rigid bar. Write a moment equilibrium equation about pin C:

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{C} &=(260 mm ) F_{1}-(540 mm ) P \\&=(260 mm )(37,785 N )-(540 mm ) P=0 \\& \therefore P=18,192.8 N =18.19 kN\end{aligned}\)

Now that P is known, the horizontal and vertical reactions at C can be calculated:

\(\begin{array}{ll}\Sigma F_{x}=C_{x}-F_{1}=0 & \therefore C_{x}=F_{1}=37,785 N \\\Sigma F_{y}=C_{y}-P=0 & \therefore C_{y}=P=19,192.8 N\end{array}\)

The resultant force acting on pin C is:

\(|C|=\sqrt{C_{x}^{2}+C_{y}^{2}}=\sqrt{(37,785 N )^{2}+(18,192.8 N )^{2}}=41,936.659 N\)

Since the pin at C is a double shear connection, the shear force acting on one shear plane is half of the resultant force: V = 20,968.330 N. The area of one shear plane of the 25-mm-diameter pin at C (in other words, the cross-sectional area of the pin) is:

\(A_{ pin }=\frac{\pi}{4}(25 mm )^{2}=490.874 mm ^{2}\)

and thus the average shear stress in pin C is:

\(\tau_{C}=\frac{V}{A_{V}}=\frac{20,968.330 N }{490.874 mm ^{2}}=42.716 N / mm ^{2}=42.7 MPa\)
