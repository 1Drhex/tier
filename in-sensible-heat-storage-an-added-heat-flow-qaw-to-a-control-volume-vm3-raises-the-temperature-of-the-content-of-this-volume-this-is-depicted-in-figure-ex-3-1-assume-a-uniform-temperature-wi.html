Guest Question.

In sensible heat storage, an added heat flow \(Q\mid _{A}(W)\) to a control volume \(V(m^{3})\) raises the temperature of the content of this volume. This is depicted in Figure . Assume a uniform temperature within this volume (i.e., treating the volume as a lumped-capacitance volume) and allow for no heat losses. Determine the rate of rise in the temperature when the following materials are used (properties evaluated at 300K).

(i) Air (gas) \(\rho =1.161Kg/m^{3}, c_{p}=1,007 J/Kg-^{\circ }C \) at T = 300 K
(ii) Water (liquid) \(\rho =997Kg/m^{3}, c_{p}=4,179 J/Kg-^{\circ }C \) at T = 300 K
(iii) Aluminum (solid) \( \rho =2,702Kg/m^{3} ,c_{p}=903 J/Kg-^{\circ }C \) at T = 300 K

The heat flow rate \(Q \mid _{A} = âˆ’1,000 W \) is negative, because heat is flowing into the
control volume, and \(V = 0.1 m^{3} \).

Admin
Solution

The volumetric energy equation for a uniform temperature within the control volume with no energy conversion is the simplified form of the integral-volume energy equation, i.e.,

\( Q\mid _{A}=-\int_{V}{\frac{d}{dt}\rho c_{p}TdV }=-\rho c_{p}V\frac{dT}{dt} \) integral-volume energy equation,

where we have assumed that the product \(\rho c_{p}\) is constant. Solving for dT/dt, we have

\(\frac{dT}{dt} (^{\circ }C/s)=\frac{-Q\mid _{A}(W)}{V(m^{3})\rho (Kg/m^{3})c_{p}(J/Kg-^{\circ }C)} \)

Note that (J) = (W-s).

Substituting the numerical values of the parameters on the right-hand side, we have

(i) \( \frac{dT}{dt} (air)=\frac{-(-1,000)(J/s)}{1.161(Kg/m^{3})\times 1,007 (J/Kg-^{\circ }C)\times 0.1(m^{3})}=8.553^{\circ }C/s \)

(ii) \( \frac{dT}{dt} (water)=\frac{-(-1,000)(J/s)}{997(Kg/m^{3})\times 4,179 (J/Kg-^{\circ }C)\times 0.1(m^{3})} =0.002400^{\circ }C/s \)

(iii) \( \frac{dT}{dt} (aluminum)=\frac{-(-1,000)(J/s)}{2,702(Kg/m^{3})\times 903 (J/Kg-^{\circ }C)\times 0.1(m^{3})} =0.04099^{\circ }C/s \)
