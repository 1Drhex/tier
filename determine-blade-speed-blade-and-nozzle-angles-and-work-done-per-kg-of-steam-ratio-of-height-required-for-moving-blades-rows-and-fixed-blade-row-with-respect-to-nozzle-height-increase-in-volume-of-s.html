Guest Question.

Determine blade speed, blade and nozzle angles and work done per kg of steam, ratio of height required for moving blades rows and fixed blade row with respect to nozzle height. Increase in volume of steam during expansion in velocity compounded impulse turbine having two moving blade rows may be neglected. Further data required for analysis is given as under,
Velocity of steam leaving nozzle = 500 m/s
Angle at exit of second moving blade = 30° from plane of rotation of blades
Velocity coefficient = 0.90 for each blade.
There is no axial thrust on any of moving blade row and the discharge from stage is axial. The axial velocity in first moving blade is twice that of second moving blade. Solve using velocity diagram.

Admin
Solution

Let us draw the velocity diagram for the stages in backward manner starting from exit of second moving blade and coming gradually to inlet of Ist moving Blade.\(\\\begin{aligned}\text{Given,}\qquad C_{1}&= 500 m/s, β_{4} = 30°, \frac{V_{2}}{V_{1}} = \frac{V_{4}}{V_{3}} = \frac{C_{3}}{C_{2}} = 0.90\\α_{4}&= 90°, C_{2a} = C_{1a} and C_{2a} = 2 · C_{3a}\\C_{3a}&= C_{4a}\end{aligned}\\\quad\textit{Steps:}\\\text{(i) Start with any length AB designating U, say AB = 2 cm}\\\text{(ii) Draw line BG at angle }β_{4}\text{ (= 30°) such that G lies vertically below A, there is axial discharge from stage.}\\\text{Measure BG = 2.7 cm which gives V}_{4}\text{. Estimate V}_{3}, as V_{3} = \frac{V_{4}}{0.9} = \text{3 cm}\\\text{(iii) Draw a line GF parallel to AB as }C_{3a} = C_{4a}.\text{ Using compass for 3 cm width mark state F with compass needle at B.}\\\text{ Join B and F. Measure AF (= 4.8 cm) which gives C}_{3}.\text{ Estimate C}_{2} = \frac{C_{3}}{0.9} = \text{5.33 cm}\\\text{(iv) Draw a line ED parallel to AB as }C_{2a} = 2 · C_{3a}\text{ i.e. 3.5 cm below AB and using compass for 5.33 cm width mark}\\\text{state E with compass needle at A. Join E and A to show C}_{2}.\text{Join E and B which shows V}_{2}.\\\text{Measure BE (= 7 cm) which gives V}_{2}\text{. Estimate V}_{1} = \frac{V_{2}}{0.9} = \text{7.78 cm}\\\text{(v) Using compass open for 7.78 cm mark point D with compass needle at B. Join line BD and AD which show}\\V_{1} and C_{1}\text{ respectively. Measure length AD = 9.6 cm. Thus C}_{1}\text{ of 500 m/s velocity is shown on length of 9.6 cm.}\\\text{Scale comes out, 9.6 cm = 500 m/s, 1cm = 52.08 m/s}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Hence using scale, 1 cm}&= 52.08 m/s.\\\text{So AB}&= 2 cm ⇒ U = 2 × 52.08 m/s, U = 104.16 m/s\\ΔC_{\text{wI moving}}&= HI = 13.05 cm = 679.64 m/s\\ΔC_{\text{wII moving}}&= 4.55 cm or 236.96 m/s\\AG&= C_{4} = C_{3a} = 1.75 cm = 91.14 m/s\\HE&= C_{2a} = C_{1a} = 3.5 cm = 182.28 m/s\\\text{Angles }\quad \qquad \angle BAD&= α_{1} = 21°\\\angle IBD&= β_{1} = 26°\\\angle ABE&= β_{2} = 28.5°\\\angle HAE&= α_{2} = 39°\\\angle BAH&= α_{3} = 20°\\\angle ABF&= β_{3} = 33°\\\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Blade speed}&= 104.16 m/s\\\text{Ist moving blade inlet and exit angle}&= 26°, 28.5°\\\text{Fixed blade inlet and exit angle}&= 39°, 20°\\\text{IInd moving blade inlet angle}&= 33°\\\text{Nozzle angle}&= 21°\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Total work done}&= U · ΔC_{\text{wI moving}} + U · ΔC_{\text{wII moving}}\\\text{per kg of steam }&\\&= 104.16 (679.64 + 236.96)\\&= 95473.056 J/kg \approx 95.47 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Using continuity equation, }\frac{\text{Area × Flow velocity or axial velocity}}{\text{Specific volume}} = \text{=constant,}\\\text{Let height of nozzle first moving, fixed blade and second moving blades be }l_{n}, l_{m1}, l_{f}, l_{m2}\text{ respectively}\\\begin{aligned}l_{n} × C_{a1}&= l_{m1} × C_{a2}\\\frac{l_{m1}}{l_{n}} = \frac{C_{a1}}{C_{a2}} = \frac{182.28}{182.28}&= 1\\\text{Ratio of height of Ist moving blade to nozzle}&= 1\\\text{Ratio of height of fixed blade to nozzle}&= \frac{l_{f}}{l_{n}} = \frac{C_{a2}}{C_{a2}} = 1\\\text{Ratio of height of IInd moving blade to nozzle,}&\\\frac{l_{m2}}{l_{n}}&= \frac{C_{a1}}{C_{a3}} = \frac{182.28}{91.14} = 2\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Ratio of height of Ist moving blade to nozzle}&= 1\\\text{Ratio of height of fixed blade to nozzle}&= 1\\\text{Ratio of height of IInd moving blade to nozzle}&= 2\end{aligned}\)
