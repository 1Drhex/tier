Guest Question.

Prove that the quaternion product is expressed by (2.37)

\(Q_{1} \ast Q_{2} \) = \(\left\{\eta _{1} \eta _{2} - \epsilon^{T}_{1} \epsilon _{2}, \eta _{1} \epsilon _{2} + \eta _{2} \epsilon _{1} + \epsilon _{1} \times \epsilon _{2} \right\}\) (2.37)

Admin
Solution

Using the expression of the rotation matrix as a function of the unit quaternion in (2.33), the product \(R _{1}\) \(R _{2}\) gives rise to a rotation matrix which is a function of {\(\eta _{1} \) , \(\epsilon _{1}\) } and {\(\eta _{2}\) , \(\epsilon _{2}\) }. The diagonal elements of such matrix are

\( R\left(\eta ,\epsilon \right) = \begin{bmatrix} 2\left(\eta ^{2} + \epsilon ^{2}_{x}\right) – 1 & 2\left(\epsilon _{x} \epsilon _{y} – \eta \epsilon _{z}\right) & 2\left(\epsilon _{x} \epsilon _{z} + \eta \epsilon _{y}\right) \\ 2\left(\epsilon _{x} \epsilon _{y} + \eta \epsilon _{z}\right) & 2\left(\eta ^{2} + \epsilon ^{2}_{y}\right) – 1 & 2\left(\epsilon _{y} \epsilon _{z} – \eta \epsilon _{x}\right) \\ 2\left(\epsilon _{x} \epsilon _{z} – \eta \epsilon _{y}\right) & 2\left(\epsilon _{y}\epsilon _{z} + \eta \epsilon _{x}\right) – 1 & 2\left(\eta ^{2} + \epsilon ^{2}_{z}\right) – 1 \end{bmatrix}. \) (2.33)

\(r_{11} = \left(2\left(\eta ^{2}_{1} + \epsilon ^{2}_{1x} \right) – 1 \right) + \left(2\left(\eta^{2} _{2} + \epsilon ^{2} _{2x} \right) – 1 \right) + 4\left(\epsilon _{1x}\epsilon _{1y} -\eta _{1} \epsilon _{1z}\right) \left(\epsilon _{2x}\epsilon _{2y} + \eta _{2}\epsilon _{2z} \right) + 4\left(\epsilon _{1x}\epsilon _{1z} + \eta _{1}\epsilon _{1y}\right)\left(\epsilon _{2x}\epsilon _{2z} – \eta _{2}\epsilon _{2y}\right)\)

 

\(r_{22}= \left(2\left(\eta ^{2}_{1} + \epsilon ^{2}_{1y}\right) – 1 \right) + \left(2\left(\eta^{2} _{2} + \epsilon ^{2} _{2y} \right) – 1 \right) + 4\left(\epsilon _{1y}\epsilon _{1z} -\eta _{1} \epsilon _{1x}\right) \left(\epsilon _{2y}\epsilon _{2z} + \eta _{2}\epsilon _{2x} \right) + 4 \left(\epsilon _{1x}\epsilon _{1y} + \eta _{1}\epsilon _{1z}\right) + \left(\epsilon _{2x}\epsilon _{2y} – \eta _{2}\epsilon _{2z}\right)\)

 

\(r_{33} = \left(2\left(\eta ^{2}_{1} + \epsilon ^{2}_{1z}\right) – 1 \right) + \left(2\left(\eta^{2} _{2} + \epsilon ^{2} _{2z} \right) – 1 \right) + 4\left(\epsilon _{1x}\epsilon _{1z} -\eta _{1} \epsilon _{1y}\right) \left(\epsilon _{2x}\epsilon _{2z} + \eta _{2}\epsilon _{2y} \right) + 4 \left(\epsilon _{1y}\epsilon _{1z} + \eta _{1}\epsilon _{1x}\right) \left(\epsilon _{2y}\epsilon _{2z} – \eta _{2}\epsilon _{2x}\right).\)

 

Then, compose these terms as follows:

\(r_{11} + r_{22} + r_{33} + 1 = 4 (\epsilon ^{2}_{1x} \epsilon ^{2}_{2x} + \epsilon ^{2}_{1y}\epsilon ^{2}_{2y} + \epsilon ^{2}_{1z}\epsilon ^{2}_{2z} \\ \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt} + 2\epsilon _{1x}\epsilon _{2x}\epsilon _{1y}\epsilon_{2y} + 2\epsilon_{1x}\epsilon_{2x}\epsilon_{1z}\epsilon_{2z} +2\epsilon_{1y}\epsilon_{2y}\epsilon_{1z}\epsilon_{2z}) \\ \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt} -8\eta_{1}\eta_{2}\left(\epsilon_{1x}\epsilon_{2x} + \epsilon_{1y}\epsilon_{2y} + \epsilon_{1z}\epsilon_{2z}\right) \\ \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt} + \left(4\eta ^{2}_{1} – 2 \right) \left(\epsilon ^{2}_{2x} + \epsilon ^{2}_{2y} + \epsilon ^{2}_{2z}\right) + \left(4\eta ^{2} _{2} – 2 \right) \left(\epsilon ^{2}_{1x} + \epsilon ^{2}_{1y} + \epsilon ^{2}_{1z}\right) \\ \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt} + 12 \eta ^{2}_{1}\eta ^{2}_{2} -6\eta ^{2}_{1} -6\eta ^{2}_{2} + 4 \)

 

Using the constraint in (2.32) yields

\(\eta ^{2} + \epsilon ^{2}_{x} + \epsilon ^{2}_{y} + \epsilon ^{2}_{z} = 1\) (2.32)

\(\frac{1}{4} \left(r_{11} + r_{22} + r_{33} + 1\right) = \left(\eta _{1}\eta _{2} – \epsilon^{T} _{1}\epsilon _{2}\right)^{2}\)

 

which, in view of (2.34), coincides with the square of the scalar part of the quaternion product in (2.37). A similar argument can be pursued to show the equivalence between the vector part of the quaternion that can be extracted from the product \(R _{1}\) \(R _{2}\) using (2.33) and the vector part of the quaternion product in (2.37).

\(\eta \) = \(\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{r_{11} + r_{22} + r_{33} + 1 } \) (2.34)
