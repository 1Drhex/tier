Guest Question.

Derivation of the frequency of oscillation for Hartley oscillator circuit and Colpitts oscillator circuits using h-parameter transistor equivalent circuit. The basic configuration of LC oscillator circuits is shown in the following Fig. 1.

Admin
Solution

The h-parameter equivalent circuit for transistor Hartley oscillator circuit is shown in the following Fig.2 using the concepts of the equivalent circuit of Fig.1. In obtaining this equivalent circuit\( (h_{oe})^{-1}\) is neglected, as it is very large compared to \(R_L\) at the output port of the equivalent circuit. At the input port \(h_{re}.V_{out}\) is neglected as \(h_{re}\) is negligibly small.

The impedances \(Z_1\), \(Z_2\) are inductive reactances and \(Z_3\) is a capacitor for Hartley oscillator circuit. For Colpitts oscillator circuit, \(Z_1 and Z_2\) are capacitors and \(Z_3\) is an inductor elements. So, \(Z_1=jX_1; Z_2=jX_2 and Z_3=jX_3 \) substitutions in the expression for the Barkhausen condition for oscillations AÎ² = 1 are used in general for obtaining the expression for condition for oscillations and the nature of reactive elements used in the oscillator circuits.

Voltage gain of CE transistor amplifier without feedback

\(A=\frac{-h_{fe} .Z_L}{h_{ie}} \) and \(\beta =\frac{V_f}{V_{out}}=\frac{Z_1}{\left[Z_1+Z_3\right] } \) (1)

\(Z_L = Z_2 \parallel \left[Z_3+Z_1\parallel h_{ie}\right]=\frac{Z_2\left[h_{ie} (Z_1 + Z_3 ) + Z_1. Z_3\right] }{h_{ie} (Z_1 + Z_2 + Z_3 ) + Z_1Z_2 + Z_1Z_3} \) (2)

\(V_{OUT}=\left[Z_3+\frac{Z_1.h_{ie}}{Z_1 + h_{ie}} \right].I_F=\left[\frac{Z_1. Z_3+h_{ie} (Z_1 + Z_3 )}{(Z_1 + h_{ie})} \right].I_F \) (3)

\(V_{F}=\left[\frac{Z_1.h_{ie}}{Z_1 + h_{ie}} \right].I_F \) (4)

â´ Feedback factor \(\beta =\frac{V_F}{V_{OUT}}=\frac{Z_1.h_{ie}}{Z_1.Z_3+h_{ie} (Z_1 + Z_3 ) } \) (5)

using AÎ² = 1 for Barkhausen condition for oscillations

â´ \(A\beta =\frac{h_{fe}. Z_1.Z_2}{\left[h_{ie}(Z_1+Z_2+Z_3)+Z_1 .Z_2 + Z_1 Z_3\right] } \)

If the impedances are pure reactances (either inductive or capacitive reactances); then \(Z_1 = jX_1; Z_2 = jX_2; Z_3 = jX_3.\)

(For an inductor \( X_L = wL\) and for a capacitor \(X_C =\frac{1}{wC}\)

\(-A\beta =\frac{h._{fe} X_1X_2}{\left[h_{ie}(X_1+X_2+X_3)-X_2 ( X_1+ X_3)\right] } \)

In order for loop gain AÎ²= 1, that is, for loop gain to be real, the phase shift over the total loop is zero.
Therefore, \((X_1+X_2+X_3)\)=0

\(-A\beta =\frac{h._{fe} X_1X_2}{X_2 ( X_1+ X_3) }=\frac{-h._{fe} X_1}{ ( X_1+ X_3) } \)

Using the equation \((X_1+X_2+X_3)\)=0; that is,\((X_1+X_3)=-X_2\) in the above equation results in \(-A\beta =\frac{h._{f.e} X_1 }{X_2} \) Since – AÎ² must be positive and at least unity in magnitude to satisfy the Barkhausen condition for oscillations, \(X_1 and X_2\) must be of the same type of reactance, either inductive or capacitive. Then, from the equation \((X_1+X_2+X_3)=0\); \(X_3=-(X_1+X_2)\). These expressions clearly suggest the nature of reactive elements for \(X_1;X_2 and X_3\).
If \(X_1 and X_2\) are inductors; then \(X_3\) is a capacitor in the Hartley oscillator circuit; whereas \(X_1 and X_2\) are capacitors and \(X_3\) is an inductor in the Colpitts oscillator circuit.

For Hartley oscillator circuit

\(X_1 = jw (L_1 + M ); X_2 = jw (L_2 + M ) and X_3 = \frac{-j}{wC}\) ; where M = Mutual inductance between the two inductors \(L_1 and L_2\).

Substituting above values of \(X_1;X_2 and X_3\) in equation \(X_1+X_2+X_3=0\) ;

\(w(L_1 + M) + w (L_2 + M )-\frac{1}{wC} = 0\) ;

Therefore, \(w (L_1 + L_2 + 2M ) = wL_{eq} \) ; where \( L_{eq} = (L_1 + L_2 + 2M )\)

At resonant frequency \( wL = \frac{1}{wC}\)

Therefore, frequency of oscillations \( f_0=\frac{1}{2\pi \sqrt{L_{eq}C} } \) for Hartley oscillator circuit.

For Colpitts oscillator circuit

\(X_1=\frac{-j}{wC_1}; X_2=\frac{-j}{wC_2} and X_3=jwL \)

Substituting above values of \(X_1;X_2 and X_3\) in equation \((X_1+X_2+X_3)=0\) ;

\(\frac{-1}{wC_1}+\frac{-1}{wC_2} +wL =0\) ;

Therefore, \(\frac{1}{wC_1}+\frac{1}{wC_2} =wL \) and hence, \( w^2L=\frac{1}{C_1}+\frac{1}{C_2} \)

At resonant frequency \( wL = \frac{1}{wC}\)

Frequency of oscillations \( f_0=\frac{1}{2\pi \sqrt{LC_{eq}} } \) ,where \(C_{eq}=\left\lgroup\frac{1}{\frac{1}{C_1}+\frac{1}{C_2} } \right\rgroup=\frac{C_1C_2}{C_1+C_2}\).
