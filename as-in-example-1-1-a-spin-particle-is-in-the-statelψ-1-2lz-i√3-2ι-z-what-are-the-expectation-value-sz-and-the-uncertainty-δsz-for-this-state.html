Guest Question.

As in , a spin \(-\frac{1}{2}\) particle is in the state

\( \mid \psi \rangle=\frac{1}{2}\mid+Z\rangle+\frac{i\sqrt{3}}{ 2}\mid-Z\rangle\)

What are the expectation value \( \left\langle S_{z}\right\rangle \) and the uncertainty \( \Delta S_{z}\) for this state?

Admin
Solution
\( \left\langle S_{z}\right\rangle =\left|\left\langle+z\mid \psi \right\rangle \right| ^{2}+\left|\left\langle- z\mid \psi \right\rangle \right| ^{2}\left(-\frac{h}{2} \right)\) \( =\frac{1}{4}\left(\frac{h}{2} \right)+\frac{3}{4}\left(-\frac{h}{2} \right)=-\frac{h}{4}\)

and

\( \left\langle S_{z} ^{2}\right\rangle=\left|\left\langle+z\mid \psi \right\rangle \right| ^{2}\left(\frac{h}{2} \right) ^{2} +\left|\left\langle-z\mid \psi \right\rangle \right| ^{2}\left(-\frac{h}{2} \right) ^{2}\)

 

\( =\frac{1}{4}\left(\frac{h^{2} }{4} \right)+\frac{3}{4}\left(\frac{h^{2} }{4} \right)=\frac{h^{2} }{4}\)

Consequently

\( \Delta S_{z}=\sqrt{\left\langle S_{z} ^{2} \right \rangle-\left\langle S_{z} \right\rangle ^{2} }\)

 

\( =\sqrt{\frac{h^{2} }{4}-\left(-\frac{h}{4} \right) ^{2} }=\frac{\sqrt{3} }{4}h=0.43h\)

The uncertainty\(\Delta S_{z}\)is \(0.43\hbar\) for the state\(\mid \psi \rangle \), which is smaller than the value \(0.50h\) for the state \(\mid +x \rangle\), reflecting the fact that there is a \(75\) percent probability of obtaining \(\hbar/2\) if a measurement of \(S_{z} \) is carried out for the state \(\mid \psi \rangle \) as compared with 50 percent probability for the state \(\mid +x \rangle \).Of course, if the state of the particle were\(\mid +z \rangle\), then there would be a 100 percent probability of obtaining \(\hbar/2\) if a measurement of \(S_{z}\) is carried out. Correspondingly, \(\Delta S_{z}\) vanishes in this case.
