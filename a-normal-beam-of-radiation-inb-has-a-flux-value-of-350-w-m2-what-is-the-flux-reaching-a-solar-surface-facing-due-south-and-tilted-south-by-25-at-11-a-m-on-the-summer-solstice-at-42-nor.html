Guest Question.

A normal beam of radiation I\(_{n,b}\) has a flux value of 350 W/m\(^2\). What is the flux reaching a solar surface facing due south and tilted south by 25° at 11 a.m. on the summer solstice at 42° north latitude?

Admin
Solution

The surface faces south so the value of the surface azimuth is γ = 0. On the summer solstice, the declination is δ = 23.45°, and at 11 a.m. the hour angle is −15°. Solving for solar altitude gives 67.6°. Solving for solar azimuth on the basis of ω, δ, and a gives γ\(_s\) = −38.6°. Solving in turn for angle of incidence as a function of α, β, γ, and γ\(_s\) gives θ\(_i\) = 15.5°. Thus the energy flux onto the device is 350 w/m\(^2\) × cos(15.5°) = 337 w/m\(^2\).
