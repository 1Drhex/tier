Guest Question.

Designing a summing op-amp circuit for a certain resistance \( R _{ A } \)

Design an inverting and a noninverting summing amplifier of the configuration shown in Fig. 3.37 to give an output voltage of the form

\( v_{ O }=4 v_{ a }+6 v_{ b }+3 v_{ c }-7 v_{1}-v_{2}-5 v_{3} \)

The equivalent resistance \( R_{ A } \) is to be set to 15 k立.

Admin
Solution

The coefficients are \( A_{1}=4, A_{2}=6, A_{3}=3, B_{1}=7, B_{2}=1, \text { and } B_{3}=5 \). Let us follow the design steps just described.

Step 1. A = 4 + 6 + 3 = 13.
Step 2. B = 7 + 1 + 5 = 13.
Step 3. C = A – B – 1 = 13 – 13 – 1 = -1.
Step 4. Since \( C<0, R_{ x }=-R_{ F } / C=R_{ F } \text { and } R_{ y }=\infty \) .
Step 5. The design can be completed by choosing a value of \( R_{ F } \). For the given value of \( R_{ A }=15 k \Omega, R_{ F }=M R_{ A } \).

In this case, M = B + 1 = 13 + 1 = 14. Thus, the values are as follows:

\(\begin{aligned}&R_{ F }=R_{ y }=M R_{ A }=14 \times 15=210 k \Omega \\&R_{ a }=\frac{R_{ F }}{A_{1}}=\frac{210 k }{4}=52.5 k \Omega \\&R_{ b }=\frac{R_{ F }}{A_{2}}=\frac{210 k }{6}=35 k \Omega \\&R_{ c }=\frac{R_{ F }}{A_{3}}=\frac{210 k }{3}=70 k \Omega \\&R_{ x }=-\frac{R_{ F }}{C}=210 k \Omega \\&R_{1}=\frac{R_{ F }}{B_{1}}=\frac{210 k }{7}=30 k \Omega\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&R_{2}=\frac{R_{ F }}{B_{2}}=\frac{210 k }{1}=210 k \Omega \\&R_{3}=\frac{R_{ F }}{B_{3}}=\frac{210 k }{5}=42 k \Omega \\&R_{ y }=\infty\end{aligned}\)

 

Check:

From Eq. (3.104),

\( R_{ A }=\left(R_{ a }\left\|R_{ b }\right\| R_{ c } \| R_{ x }\right) \) (3.104)

 

\( R_{ A }=(52.5 k \Omega\|35 k \Omega\| 70 k \Omega \| 210 k \Omega)=15 k \Omega \)

 

From Eq. (3.105),

\( R_{ B }=\left(R_{1}\left\|R_{2}\right\| R_{3} \| R_{ y }\right) \) (3.105)

 

\( R_{ B }=(30 k \Omega\|210 k \Omega\| 42 k \Omega)=16.15 k \Omega \)

 

From Eq. (3.106),

 

\( \left(R_{ B } \| R_{ F }\right)=R_{ A } \) (3.106)

 

\( R_{ B } \| R_{ F }=(16.15 k \Omega \| 210 k \Omega)=15 k \Omega \)

 

Thus, the condition of \( R_{ A }=\left(R_{ B } \| R_{ F }\right) \) is satisfied.
