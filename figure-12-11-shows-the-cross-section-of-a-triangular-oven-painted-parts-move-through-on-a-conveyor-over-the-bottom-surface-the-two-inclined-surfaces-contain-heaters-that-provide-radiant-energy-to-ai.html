Guest Question.

Figure 12.11 shows the cross section of a triangular oven. Painted parts move through on a conveyor over the bottom surface. The two inclined surfaces contain heaters that provide radiant energy to aid in the drying of the paint. Determine the heat transferred to the conveyed parts for the conditions shown..

Admin
Solution

The heaters provide energy to the bottom surface, and as parts there become warm, they radiate energy and lose energy by convection to the surrounding air as well as by conduction to the conveyor. The temperatures shown will exist only at one section within the system. The surfaces are real surfaces and do not exhibit black-surface behavior.
Assumptions
1. Conduction and convection effects are negligible
2. The system is at steady state
3. The oven length is very long compared to the dimensions of the cross section (so that no energy is radiated out the ends)
4. The surfaces are all black with respect to radiation heat transfer

The energy exchanges of interest are from \(A_{2} to A_{1}\) and \(A_{3} to A_{1}\). The net energy exchanged between\( A_{2} and A_{1}\) is

 

\( q_{1 \rightarrow 2}=\sigma\left(T_{2}^{4}-T_{1}^{4}\right) A_{2} F_{2-1}=\sigma\left(T_{2}^{4}-T_{1}^{4}\right) A_{1} F_{1-2}\)

Between \(A_{3} and A_{1}\) we have

\( q_{3 \rightarrow 1}=\sigma\left(T_{3}^{4}-T_{1}^{4}\right) A_{3} F_{3-1}=\sigma\left(T_{3}^{4}-T_{1}^{4}\right) A_{1} F_{1-3} \)

Due to symmetry, we know that these heat-transfer rates are equal. We now evaluate each of the view factors. We write Equation 12.15 for surface \(A_{1}\) as

\(F_{1-2}+F_{1-3}=1 \) (12.15)

 

\(A_{1} F_{1-2}+A_{1} F_{1-3}=A_{1}\) (i)

For surfaces \(A_{2} and A_{3}\)

 

\(A_{2} F_{2-1}+A_{2} F_{2-3}=A_{2} \) (ii)

 

\(A_{3} F_{3-1}+A_{3} F_{3-2}=A_{3}\) (iii)

By using reciprocity on Equation ii, we get

\(A_{1} F_{1-2}+A_{2} F_{2-3}=A_{2}\) (iv)

 

Likewise, for Equation iii

\(A_{1} F_{1-3}+A_{2} F_{2-3}=A_{3}\) (v)

Equations i, iv, and v are three relations with three unknowns, which we now solve simultaneously. Subtracting Equation v from Equation iv gives

\(A_{1} F_{1-2}-A_{1} F_{1-3}=A_{2}-A_{3}\)

 

Adding this result to Equation i, we obtain

\(A_{1} F_{1-2}+A_{1} F_{1-2}=A_{1} +A_{2}-A_{3}\)

 

Solving for the view factor, we get

\(F_{1-2}=\frac{A_{1} +A_{2}-A_{3}}{2A_{1}} =\frac{L_{1} +L_{2}-L_{3}}{2L_{1}} \)

 

where L is the width of area \(A_{i}\). Each side of the triangular cross section has the same length (into the page).
Substituting into Equation i and solving for \(F_{1-3}\) gives

 

\(A_{1} \left\lgroup\frac{A_{1} +A_{2}-A_{3}}{2A_{1} } \right\rgroup +A_{1} F_{1-3}=A_{1} \)

or

 

\( F_{1-3}=\frac{A_{1}-A_{2}+A_{3}}{2 A_{1}}=\frac{L_{1}-L_{2}+L_{3}}{2 L_{1}}\)

 

Using reciprocity, we finally get

\(A_{2} F_{2-1}=A_{1} F_{1-2}=\frac{A_{1}+A_{2}-A_{3}}{2}\)

 

\(A_{3} F_{3-1}=A_{1} F_{1-3}=\frac{A_{1}-A_{2}+A_{3}}{2}\)

 

The heat-transfer equation \(\left(A_{2}\right. to \left.A_{1}\right) \)becomes

\(q_{2 \rightarrow 1}=\sigma\left(T_{2}^{4}-T_{1}^{4}\right) \frac{A_{1}+A_{2}-A_{3}}{2}\)

 

Because the length (into the page) is unknown, we will calculate the heat flux

\(\frac{q_{2 \rightarrow 1}}{A_{2}}=\sigma\left(T_{2}^{4}-T_{1}^{4}\right) \frac{A_{1} / A_{2}+1-A_{3} / A_{2}}{2}\)

 

or in terms of width of each side

\(\frac{q_{2 \rightarrow 1}}{A_{2}}=\sigma\left(T_{2}^{4}-T_{1}^{4}\right) \frac{L_{1} / L_{2}+1-L_{3} / L_{2}}{2}\)

 

The widths are

\(L_{1}=1m\)

 

\(L_{2} =L_{3} =\left(\sin 45^{\circ } \right) \left(1m\right) =0.707m\)

 

Substituting

\(\frac{q_{2 \rightarrow 1}}{A_{2}}=\left(5.67\times 10^{-8} \right) \left(473^{4}-303^{4} \right) \left\lgroup\frac{1/0.707+1-0.707/0.707}{2} \right\rgroup\)

 

\(\frac{q_{2 \rightarrow 1}}{A_{3}}=1.67\times 10^{3} W/m^{2}\)

 

and

\(\frac{q_{3 \rightarrow 1}}{A_{3}}=1.67\times 10^{3} W/m^{2}\)
