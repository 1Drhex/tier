Guest Question.

A furnace is in the shape of a sphere and is 0.6 m in diameter. It is filled with a gas mixture at a temperature of 1500 K and a pressure of 1.8 atmosphere. The gas mixture consists of \(H_2O\) at a partial pressure of 0.8 atm and \(CO_2\) with a partial pressure of 0.4 atm. The cavity wall is black and is maintained at 600 K. Determine the heat transfer to the wall.

Heat transfer to the wall having a temperature of 600 K,

\(q_1=J_1-G_1\)

 

\(Q_1=(J_1-G_1)4\pi R^2\)

 

\(\varepsilon _1=1\)

 

\(J_1=\varepsilon _1\sigma T_1^4 +(1-\varepsilon _1)G_1=\sigma T_1^4\)

 

\(G_1=\varepsilon _g\sigma T_g^4+\tau _g(\sigma T_1^4)\)

 

\(\alpha _g+\tau _g+\rho _g=1;\rho _g=0\)

 

\(\alpha _g+\tau _g=1;\tau _g=1-\alpha _g\)

 

\(G_1=\varepsilon _g\sigma T_g^4+(1-\alpha _g)(\sigma T_1^4)\)

 

\(q_1=J_1-G_1=\sigma T_1^4-\varepsilon _g\sigma T_g^4-\sigma T_1^4+\alpha _g\sigma T_1^4\)

 

\(q_1=\alpha _g\sigma T_1^4-\varepsilon _g\sigma T_g^4\)

For the problem under consideration,

\(q_1=0.393\times 5.67\times 10^{-8}\times 600^4-0.326\times 5.67\times 10^{-8}\times 1500^4\)
