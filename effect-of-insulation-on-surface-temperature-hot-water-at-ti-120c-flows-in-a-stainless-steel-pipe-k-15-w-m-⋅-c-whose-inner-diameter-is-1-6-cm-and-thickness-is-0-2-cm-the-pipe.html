Guest Question.

Effect of Insulation on Surface Temperature

Hot water at \( T_{i} = 120 ^{\circ} C\) flows in a stainless steel pipe \( \left ( k = 15 W/m \cdot ^{\circ}C \right )\) whose inner diameter is 1.6 cm and thickness is 0.2 cm. The pipe is to be covered with adequate insulation so that the temperature of the outer surface of the insulation does not exceed \( 40^{\circ} C\) when the ambient temperature is \(T_{o} = 25^{\circ} C \). Taking the heat transfer coefficients inside and outside the pipe to be \(h_{i} = 70 W/m^{2} \cdot ^{\circ}C \) and \(h_{o} = 20 W/m^{2} \cdot ^{\circ} C \), respectively, determine the thickness of fiberglass insulation \( \left ( k = 0.038 W/m \cdot ^{\circ}C\right )\) that needs to be installed on the pipe.

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION A steam pipe is to be covered with enough insulation to reduce the exposed surface temperature. The thickness of insulation that needs to be installed is to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Heat transfer is steady since there is no indication of any change with time. 2 Heat transfer is one-dimensional since there is thermal symmetry about the centerline and no variation in the axial direction. 3 Thermal conductivities are constant. 4 The thermal contact resistance at the interface is negligible.

Properties The thermal conductivities are given to be \( k = 15 W/m \cdot ^{\circ} C\) for the steel pipe and \( k = 0.038 W/m \cdot ^{\circ}C\) for fiberglass insulation.

Analysis The thermal resistance network for this problem involves four resistances in series and is given in Figure. The inner radius of the pipe is \( r_{1} = 0.8 cm \) and the outer radius of the pipe and thus the inner radius of the insulation is \( r_{2} = 1.0 cm \). Letting \( r_{3} \) represent the outer radius of the insulation, the areas of the surfaces exposed to convection for an L = 1-m-long section of the pipe become

\( A_{1} = 2 \pi r_{1} L = 2 \pi \left ( 0.008 m \right )\left ( 1 m \right) = 0.0503 m^{2} \)

 

\( A_{3} = 2 \pi r_{3} L = 2 \pi r_{3} \left ( 1 m \right) = 6.28 r_{3} m^{2} \)

 

Then the individual thermal resistances are determined to be

\( R_{i} = R_{conv, 1} = \frac{1}{h_{i}A_{1}} = \frac{1}{\left ( 70 W/m^{2} \cdot ^{\circ}C \right ) \left ( 0.0503 m^{2} \right )} = 0.284 ^{\circ}C/W \)

 

\( R_{1} = R_{pipe} = \frac{ln\left ( r_{2}/r_{1} \right )}{2 \pi k_{1}L} = \frac{ln \left ( 0.01/0.008 \right )}{2\pi \left ( 15 W/m \cdot ^{\circ}C \right )\left ( 1 m \right )} = 0.0024^{\circ} C/W \)

 

\( R_{2} = R_{insulation} = \frac{ln\left ( r_{3}/r_{2} \right )}{2 \pi k_{2}L} = \frac{ln \left ( r_{3}/0.01 \right )}{2\pi \left ( 0.038 W/m \cdot ^{\circ}C \right )\left ( 1 m \right )} \)

 

\( = 4.188 ln \left ( r_{3} /0.01 \right )^{\circ} C/W \)

 

\( R_{o} = R_{conv, 2} = \frac{1}{h_{o}A_{3}} = \frac{1}{\left ( 20 W/m^{2} \cdot ^{\circ}C \right ) \left ( 6.28 r_{3} m^{2} \right )} = \frac{1}{125.6 r_{3}} ^{\circ}C/W \)

 

Noting that all resistances are in series, the total resistance is determined to be

\( R_{total} = R_{i} + R_{1} + R_{2} + R_{0}\)

 

\( = \left [ 0.284 + 0.0024 + 4.188 ln\left ( r_{3}/0.01 \right ) + 1/125.6 r_{3} \right ] ^{\circ} C/W \)

 

Then the steady rate of heat loss from the steam becomes

\( \dot{Q} = \frac{T_{i} – T_{o}}{R_{total}} \)

 

\( = \frac{\left ( 120 – 125 \right )^{\circ}C}{\left [ 0.284 + 0.0024 + 4.188 ln\left ( r_{3}/0.01 \right ) + 1/125.6 r_{3} \right ] ^{\circ}C/W} \)

 

Noting that the outer surface temperature of insulation is specified to be \( 40^{\circ} C\), the rate of heat loss can also be expressed as

\( \dot{Q} = \frac{T_{3} – T_{o}}{R_{o}} = \frac{\left ( 40 – 25 \right )^{\circ} C}{\left ( 1 /125.6 r_{3} \right )^{\circ}C/W} = 1884 r_{3} \)

 

Setting the two relations above equal to each other and solving for \( r_{3} \) gives \( r_{3} = 0.0170 m \). Then the minimum thickness of fiberglass insulation required is

\( t = r_{3} – r_{2} = 0.0170 – 0.0100 = 0.0070 m = 0.70 cm \)

 

Discussion Insulating the pipe with at least 0.70-cm-thick fiberglass insulation will ensure that the outer surface temperature of the pipe will be at \( 40^{\circ} C\) or below.
