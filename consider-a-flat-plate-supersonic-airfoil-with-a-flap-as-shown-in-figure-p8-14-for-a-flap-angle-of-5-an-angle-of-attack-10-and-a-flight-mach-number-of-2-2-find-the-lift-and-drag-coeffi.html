Guest Question.

Consider a flat-plate supersonic airfoil with a flap, as shown in Figure P8.14. For a flap angle of 5°, an angle of attack 10°, and a flight Mach number of 2.2, find the lift and drag coefficients of the airfoil.

Admin
Solution

Except for the trailing edge phenomena, there will be two expansion fans on the top of the plate and two oblique shocks on the lower portion. The regions for the calculations are numbered as follows

 

From this and the angle of attack we can find \(v_{2}\), which will lead to \(M_{2}\)

 

\(v_{\infty}+\alpha_{\infty}=v_{2}+\alpha_{2}\)

 

\(\therefore v_{2}=v_{\infty}+\alpha_{\infty}-\alpha_{2}=31.7325+0-(-10.0000)=41.7325^{\circ}\)

 

and so \(M _{2}=2.6142\). This process is repeated in passing through the expansion fan at the corner of the flat plate and the flap

 

\(v_{2}+\alpha_{2}=v_{4}+\alpha_{4}\)

 

\(\therefore v_{4}=v_{2}+\alpha_{2}-\alpha_{4}=41.7325-10.0000-(-15.0000)=46.7325^{\circ}\)

 

and so \(M _{4}=2.8478 \text {. Furthermore using } M _{\infty}, M _{2}, M _{4}\), we can use the isentropic relations to determine the corresponding static to stagnation pressure ratios. Since the flows from the freestream into region 2 and from region 2 to 4 are isentropic

 

\(\frac{ p _{2}}{ p _{\infty}}=\frac{ p _{2}}{ p _{o2}} \frac{ p _{ o 2}}{ p _{o \infty}} \frac{ p _{o \infty}}{ p _{\infty}}=(0.04903)(1.0)\left(\frac{1}{0.09352}\right)=0.5242\)

 

\(\frac{ p _{4}}{ p _{\infty}}=\frac{ p _{4}}{ p _{o4}} \frac{ p _{ o 4}}{ p _{ o 2}} \frac{ p _{ o 2}}{ p _{o \infty}} \frac{ p _{o \infty}}{ p _{\infty}}=(0.03426)(1.0)(1.0)\left(\frac{1}{0.09352}\right)=0.3663\)

 

Lower Surface

The freestream flow is turned through 10º as it passes through the first oblique shock. Therefore,

 

\(\left.\begin{array}{l} M _{\infty}=2.2 \\\delta=10^{\circ}\end{array}\right\} \begin{aligned}&\theta=35.7855^{\circ}, \\& M _{1}=1.8228 \quad \frac{ p _{1}}{ p _{\infty}}=1.7641\end{aligned}\)

 

The stream in region 1 is turned through 5º as it passes through the second oblique shock as it flows into region 3. Therefore,

 

\(\left.\begin{array}{l} M _{1}=1.8228 \\\delta=5^{\circ}\end{array}\right\} \begin{aligned}&\theta=37.9098^{\circ}, \\& M _{3}=1.6502 \quad \frac{ p _{3}}{ p _{1}}=1.2967\end{aligned}\)

 

And so

 

\(\frac{p_{3}}{p_{\infty}}=\frac{p_{3}}{p_{1}} \frac{p_{1}}{p_{\infty}}=(1.2967)(1.7641)=2.2875\)

 

\(C _{ L }=\frac{\left( p _{1}- p _{2}\right) c \cos (10)}{\frac{1}{2} \gamma p _{\infty} M _{\infty}^{2} c }+\frac{\left( p _{3}- p _{4}\right)( c / 3) \cos (15)}{\frac{1}{2} \gamma p _{\infty} M _{\infty}^{2} c }\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&=\frac{(1.7641-0.5242)(0.9848)}{(3.3880)}+\frac{(2.2875-0.3663)(0.9659)}{(3.3880)(3)} \\&=0.3604+0.1826=0.5430\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}C_{D} &=\frac{\left(p_{1}-p_{2}\right) c \sin (10)}{\frac{1}{2} \gamma p_{\infty} M_{\infty}^{2} c}+\frac{\left(p_{3}-p_{4}\right)(c / 3) \sin (15)}{\frac{1}{2} \gamma p_{\infty} M_{\infty}^{2} c} \\&=\frac{\left(p_{1}-p_{2}\right) c \cos (10)}{\frac{1}{2} \gamma p_{\infty} M_{\infty}^{2} c} \tan (10)+\frac{\left(p_{3}-p_{4}\right)(c / 3) \cos (15)}{\frac{1}{2} \gamma p_{\infty} M_{\infty}^{2} c} \tan (15) \\&=(0.3604)(0.1763)+(0.1826)(0.2679)=0.0635+0.0489 \\&=0.1125\end{aligned}\)
