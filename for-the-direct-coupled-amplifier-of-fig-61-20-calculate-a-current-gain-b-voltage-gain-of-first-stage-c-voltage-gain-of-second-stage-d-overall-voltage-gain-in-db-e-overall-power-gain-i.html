Guest Question.

For the direct-coupled amplifier of \(Fig. 61.20\), calculate

\((a)\) current gain , \((b)\) voltage gain of first stage,

\((c)\) voltage gain of second stage, \((d)\) overall voltage gain in dB,

\((e)\) overall power gain in dB, \((f)\) input resistance.

Neglect \(V_{BE}\) and use \(r_e = 50 mV/I_E\) .

Admin
Solution

\((a) A_i= \beta_1 \beta_2=100 \times 50 = 5000\) \((b) A_{v.1} = 1\) — Art 61.11

\((c) A_{v.2}=\frac{r_{0.2} }{r_{e.2} }\) Now, \(r_{e.2}= \frac{50 }{I_{E.2} }\)

Let us find the value of \(I_{E.2}\) starting from the value of \(I_{B.1}\). As seen from \(Fig. 61.20\) ,

\(I_{B.1} = 12/1.2M = 10 \mu A\)

 

\(I_{C.1} = \beta_1. 1_{\beta 1} = 100 \times 10\)

 

\(=1000 \mu A\)

 

\(I_{E.1} = I_{C.1} = 1000 \mu A\)

 

\(= 1 mA\)

 

\(I_{B.2} = I_{C.1} = 1 mA\)

 

\(I_{C.2} = \beta_2. I_{C1} = 50 \times 1\)

 

\(= 50 mA\)

 

\(\therefore\) \(I_{E.2} = 50 mA\)

and \(r_{e.2} = 50/50 = 1 \Omega\)

Also, \(r_{e.2} = R_2 = 200 \Omega\)

 

\(\therefore\) \( A_{v.2}=\frac{200 }{1 }=200 \)

 

\((d) A_n =1 \times 200 = 200\) ; \(G_v = 20 \log_{10} 200 = 46 dB\)

 

\((e) A_p =A_v . A_i = 200 \times 5,000 = 106\) ; \(Gp = 20 \log_{10} 106 = 60 dB\)

 

\((f) r_i =R_1 \left|\right| \beta_1.r_{e.1}\) — Fig. 61.18

\(r_{e.1}= \frac{50 }{I_{E.1} }=\frac{50 }{1 }=50 \Omega\) \(\therefore\) \(r_i = 1.2 M \left|\right| (50 \times 100) \cong 5 K\)
