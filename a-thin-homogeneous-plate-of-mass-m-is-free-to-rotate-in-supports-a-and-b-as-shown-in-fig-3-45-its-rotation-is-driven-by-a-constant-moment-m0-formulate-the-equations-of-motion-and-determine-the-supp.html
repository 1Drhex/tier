Guest Question.

A thin homogeneous plate of mass m is free to rotate in supports A and B as shown in Fig. 3.45. Its rotation is driven by a constant moment \(M_{0} \). Formulate the equations of motion and determine the support reactions.

Admin
Solution

To describe the motion of the plate it would be sufficient to apply the principle of angular momentum about the fixed axis. However, to determine the support forces, the principles of angular momentum about the axes perpendicular to the rotation axis and the principle of linear momentum are needed.

We free the plate from the supports and choose a body-fixed (rotating) coordinate system x, y, z whose origin A is at rest (fixed in space). The center of mass rotates on a circle (Fig. 3.45b).With centripetal acceleration \(a_{cx}=-c\omega ^{2}/3 \) and tangential acceleration \(a_{cy}=c\dot{\omega} /3 \) the components of the principle of linear momentum in the x- and in y-direction read

\(m a_{cx}=A_{x} +B_{x} \rightarrow -\frac{mc\omega ^{2} }{3}=A_{x} +B_{x}\) , (a)

\(m a_{cy}=A_{y} +B_{y} \rightarrow \frac{mc\dot{\omega} }{3}=A_{y} +B_{y} \) . (b)

To set up the components of the principle of angular momentum, the moments and products of inertia are needed. With dm = ρ t dA and m = \(\frac{1}{2} \) ρ t c b they are calculated as (cf. Fig. 3.45c)

\(\Theta _{z} =\rho t\int x^{2} dA=\rho t\int\limits_{0}^{b}\left\{\int\limits_{0}^{cz/b}x^{2}dx \right\}dz=\frac{mc^{2} }{6}\) ,

\(\Theta _{xz} =-\rho t\int xz dA=-\rho t\int\limits_{0}^{b}\left\{\int\limits_{0}^{cz/b}xdx \right\}z dz=-\frac{mcb }{4}\) ,

\(\Theta _{yz}=0 \) .

Thus, from (3.61) we obtain

\(\Theta _{xz}\dot{\omega } -\Theta _{yz} \omega ^{2} =M_{x}\) ,

\(\Theta _{yz}\dot{\omega } +\Theta _{xz} \omega ^{2} =M_{y} \) ,

\(\Theta _{z}\dot{\omega } =M_{z} \) . (3.61)

\(M_{x}= \Theta _{xz} \dot{\omega } \rightarrow -bB_{y}=-\frac{\dot{\omega }mcb }{4}\) , (c)

\(M_{y}= \Theta _{xz} \omega ^{2} \rightarrow bB_{x}=-\frac{\omega^{2} mcb }{4}\) , (d)

\(M_{z}= \Theta _{z} \dot{\omega } \rightarrow M_{0} =\frac{\dot{\omega }mc^{2} }{6}\) . (e)

The last equation represents the equation of motion. Assuming the initial condition ω(0) = 0 leads to

\(\dot{\omega } =\frac{6M_{0} }{mc^{2} }\rightarrow \omega =\frac{6M_{0} }{mc^{2} } t\) .

Insertion into (a) – (d) finally yields

\(A_{x} =-\frac{\omega ^{2}mc }{12}, A_{y}=\frac{M_{0} }{2c}, B_{x}=-\frac{\omega ^{2} mc}{4}, B_{y}=\frac{3M_{0} }{2c}\) .
