Guest Question.

It is required to design a double-start screw with square threads for the C-clamp shown in Fig. 6.15(a). The maxi mum force exerted by the clamp is 5 kN. It is assumed that the operator will exert a force of 250 N at the ball handle of the hand wheel. The screw is made of plain carbon steel 45C8 \( \left(S_{v t}=330 N / mm ^{2}\right) \), while the nut is made of grey
cast iron FG 200. The dimensions of the collar are given in Fig. 6.15(b). The factor of safety is 2.
Determine the dimensions of the screw and the nut and calculate the radius \( R_{m} \) of the ball handle.

Admin
Solution

Given W = 5 kN operator force = 250 N.

\( \text { For screw, No. of starts }=2 \quad S_{y t}=330 N / mm ^{2} \).

(fs) = 2

\( \text { For collar, } D_{o}=17 mm \quad D_{i}=6 mm \).

\( \text { For nut, } \quad S_{u t}=200 N / mm ^{2} \).

Step I Selection of diameter and pitch of screw The lower portion of the screw between the nut and the object is subjected to compressive stress.
Assuming,

\( S_{y c}=S_{y t}=330 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \sigma_{c}=\frac{S_{y c}}{(f s)}=\frac{330}{2}=165 N / mm ^{2} \).

Since

\( \sigma_{c}=\frac{W}{\left(\frac{\pi}{4} d_{c}^{2}\right)} \quad \therefore \quad 165=\frac{5000}{\left(\frac{\pi}{4} d_{c}^{2}\right)} \).

or \( d_{c}=6.21 \text { or } 7 mm \).

There are additional stresses due to the collar friction torque. At this stage, it is not possible to calculate the additional stresses in the lower and upper parts of the screw. To account for these additional stresses, the diameter should be increased.
As a first trial, a screw with a 22 mm nominal diameter and 5 mm pitch is selected for this clamp.
Step II Screw and collar friction torques

\( d_{m}=d-0.5 p=22-0.5(5)=19.5 mm \).

l = 2p = 2(5) = 10 mm.

\( \tan \alpha=\frac{l}{\pi d_{m}}=\frac{10}{\pi(19.5)} \quad \text { or } \quad \alpha=9.271^{\circ} \).

Assuming the coefficient of friction to be 0.15,

\( \tan \phi=\mu=0.15 \text { or } \phi=8.531^{\circ} \).

∴ \( M_{t}=\frac{W d_{m}}{2} \tan (\phi+\alpha) \).

\( =\frac{(5000)(19.5)}{2} \tan (8.531+9.271) \).

= 15 653.79 N-mm
The coefficient of friction at the collar is taken
as 0.17. Thus,

\( \left(M_{t}\right)_{c}=\frac{\mu_{c} W}{4} \cdot\left(D_{0}+D_{i}\right) \).

\( =\frac{(0.17)(5000)}{4}(17+6) \).

= 4887.5 N-mm.
Step III Check for stresses in the screw

\( d_{c}=d-p=22.5=17 mm \).

Stresses at the section AA
At the section AA, the body of the screw is subjected to two types of stresses:
(i) torsional shear stress due to the total torque \( \left(M_{t}\right)_{t} \) ; and
(ii) bending stress due to a hand force of 250 N.

\( \tau=\frac{16\left(M_{t}\right)_{t}}{\pi d_{c}^{3}}=\frac{16(15653.79+4887.5)}{\pi(17)^{3}} \).

\( =21.29 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \sigma_{b}=\frac{32 M_{b}}{\pi d_{c}^{3}}=\frac{32(250 \times 275)}{\pi(17)^{3}} \).

= 142.54 N/mm².

\( \tau_{\max .}=\sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{b}}{2}\right)^{2}+(\tau)^{2}} \).

\( =\sqrt{\left(\frac{142.54}{2}\right)^{2}+(21.29)^{2}} \).

= 74.38 N/mm².

∴ \( (f s)=\frac{S_{s y}}{\tau_{\max }}=\frac{0.5 S_{y t}}{\tau_{\max }}=\frac{0.5(330)}{74.38} \).

= 2.22.

The recommended factor of safety is 2.
Therefore, the screw with a 22 mm nominal diameter is justified.
Stresses at the section BB
At the section BB, the body of the screw is subjected to two types of stresses:
(i) torsional shear stress due to the collar friction torque; and
(ii) direct compressive stress due to the clamping force.

\( \tau=\frac{16\left(M_{t}\right)_{c}}{\pi d_{C}^{3}}=\frac{16(4887.5)}{\pi(17)^{3}}=5.07 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \sigma_{c}=\frac{W}{\left(\frac{\pi}{4} d_{c}^{2}\right)}=\frac{5000}{\left(\frac{\pi}{4}(17)^{2}\right)}=22.03 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \tau_{\max }=\sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{c}}{2}\right)^{2}+(\tau)^{2}} \).

\( =\sqrt{\left(\frac{22.03}{2}\right)^{2}+(5.07)^{2}}=12.13 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \therefore \quad(f s)=\frac{S_{s y}}{\tau_{\max }}=\frac{0.5(330)}{12.13}=13.6 \).

Therefore, the stresses at the section BB are not critical.
Step IV Design of the nut
The unit bearing pressure for the cast iron nut and the soft steel screw is 13 to 17 N/mm². Using the mean value of 15 N/mm²,

\( z=\frac{4 W}{\pi S_{b}\left(d^{2}-d_{c}^{2}\right)}=\frac{4(5000)}{\pi(15)\left[(22)^{2}-(17)^{2}\right]} \).

= 2.18 or 3
∴ length of the nut = zp = 3(5) = 15 mm.
Step V Radius of ball handle
The total torque is given by,

\( \left(M_{t}\right)_{t}=M_{t}+\left(M_{t}\right)_{c}=15653.79+4887.5 \) (i).

The operator exerts a force of 250 N on the ball handle. Therefore,

\( \left(M_{t}\right)_{t}=250 \times R_{m} \) (ii).

Equating (i) and (ii),

\( R_{m}=82.17 mm \).
