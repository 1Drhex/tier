Guest Question.

Estimate the propagation delay for three stages of CMOS driving a 50-pF off-chip load, with a scaling factor of five applied at each stage of buffering, as depicted in Figure 9.26. Assume quarter micron CMOS technology using a 1.8-V supply for all gates, including the output drivers, and ±0.5-V thresh-old voltages. For this technology, the inverters dimensions of 2.2 μm/0.25 μm (p-MOSFET) and 0.9 μm/0.25 μm (n-MOSFET) and the oxide thickness is 7 nm.

Admin
Solution

From one stage to the next, both gate widths have been scaled up by a factor of five. For the first stage, the device transconductance parameters are

\(K_P=k´_P\frac{W_{p1}}{L_{p1}}=0.113 mA/V^2\left\lgroup \frac {2.2}{0.25} \right\rgroup =0.99mA/V^2\)

and

\(K_N=k´_N\frac{W_{N1}}{L_{N1}}=0.28 mA/V^2\left\lgroup \frac{0.9}{0.25} \right\rgroup =1.01 mA/V^2\)

The load capacitance for the first stage is equal to the input capacitance of the second stage,

\(C_{L1}=\frac{\varepsilon _{OX}W_{P2} L_{P2}}{t_{ox}} +\frac{\varepsilon _{OX}W_{N2} L_{N2}}{t_{OX}} =19.1 fF\)

therefore, the propagation delay for the first stage is

\(t_{p1} \approx\frac{19.1\times 10^{-15}F}{0.99 m A/V^2}\left[\frac{1.0V}{(1.3V)^2}+\frac{2}{1.3 V} \ln \left\lgroup \frac{1.3V}{0.9V} \right\rgroup \right] =23 \) ps

For the second stage,

\(K_{P2}=k´_{p}\frac{W_{P2}}{L_{P2}} =0.113 m A/V^2\left\lgroup\frac{11}{0.25} \right\rgroup =5.0m A/V^2 \)

\(K_{N2}=k´_{N}\frac{W_{N2}}{L_{N2}} =0.28 m A/V^2\left\lgroup\frac{4.5}{0.25} \right\rgroup =5.0m A/V^2 \).

\(C_{L2}=\frac{\varepsilon _{ox}W_{P3}L_{P3} }{t_{ox}} +\frac{\varepsilon _{ox}W_{N3}L_{N3}}{t_{ox}} =96 fF\),

and

\(t_{p2} \approx\frac{96\times 10^{-15}F}{5.0 mA/V^2}\left[\frac{1.0V}{(1.3V)^2}+\frac{2}{1.3 V} \ln \left\lgroup \frac{1.3V}{0.9V} \right\rgroup \right] =23 ps\),

For the third stage,

\(K_{P3}=k´_{p}\frac{W_{P3}}{L_{P3}} =0.113 m A/V^2\left\lgroup\frac{55}{0.25} \right\rgroup =25 m A/V^2 \)

\(K_{N3}=k´_{N}\frac{W_{N2}}{L_{N2}} =0.28 m A/V^2\left\lgroup\frac{22}{0.25} \right\rgroup =25m A/V^2 \).

\(C_{L3}=C_L=50\) pF

and

\(t_{p3} \approx\frac{50\times 10^{-12}F}{25m A/V^2}\left[\frac{1.0V}{(1.3 V)^2}+\frac{2}{1.3 V} \ln \left\lgroup \frac{1.3 V}{0.9V} \right\rgroup \right] =2.3 ns\).

The total propagation delay for the case of three stages is \(t_{P} = t_{P1} + t_{P2} +t_{P3} = 23\) ps \(+ 23\) ps \(+ 2.3\) ns = 2.34 ns. Therefore, the use of two buffer stages reduced the propagation delay by a factor of approximately 1/25. This is because the buffer stages increased the current-driving capability by 25 without adding appreciable propagation delays of their own.
