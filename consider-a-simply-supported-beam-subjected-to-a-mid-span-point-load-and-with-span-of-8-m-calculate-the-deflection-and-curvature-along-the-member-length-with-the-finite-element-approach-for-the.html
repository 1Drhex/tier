Guest Question.

Consider a simply-supported beam subjected to a mid-span point load and with span of 8 m. Calculate the deflection and curvature along the member length with the finite element approach for the following two applied mid-span loads: (1) P = 405 kN and (2) P = 475 kN. Use the Newton–Raphson method and, for the convergence criteria, take \( norm_{tol2}\) equal to \( 10^{–5}\) (Equation 15.59b). Consider the rectangular cross-section (width of 100 mm and height of 360 mm) and the elastic–perfectly plastic material properties (E = 200 GPa and \( f_y = 300\) MPa) of . Compare the calculated results with those obtained using the analytical solutions in Section 15.3.2 and plotted in Figure 15.15. For the numerical integrations, use three Gauss integration points.

\( norm_{tol2} = \frac{\left|r ^{(i+1)}_R\right| }{\left|r_e\right| } \) (15.59b).

Admin
Solution

(1) The load P = 405 kN is applied in one load step and is included in the load vector Q.

In the first iteration, we assume that the beam is undeformed, therefore described by a nil vector of displacements. Based on this, we can calculate the internal actions resisted by the beam \( K(D^{(1)}_e) \) and substitute it in Equation 15.62 for the calculation of the residual loads to be used in the analysis: \( Q^{(1)}_R = Q-K(D^{(1)}_e) \) . In particular, \( K(D^{(1)}_e) \) is obtained by collecting the contribution of each element \( k_e(x,d^{(1)} \) from Equation 15.82a

\( Q^{(i)}_R = Q-K(D^{(i)}_e) \) (15.62).

\( k_e\Bigl(x , d^{(i)}_e\Bigr) = \frac{L}{2}\sum\limits_{k=1}^{n_G}{w_k B^T(x_k)r^{(i)} \Bigl(x_k,d^{(i)}\Bigr) } = \frac{L}{2} \sum\limits_{k=1}^{n_G}{w_k}\begin{bmatrix} N^{\prime }_{u1} (x_k) & 0 \\ 0 & N^{\prime \prime}_{ν1} (x_k) \\ 0 & N^{\prime \prime}_{ν2} \\ N^{\prime }_{u2} (x_k) & 0 \\ N^{\prime }_{u3} (x_k) & 0 \\ 0 & N^{\prime \prime}_{ν3} (x_k) \\ 0 & N^{\prime \prime}_{ν4} (x_k) \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} N\Bigl(x_k,d^{(i)}_e\Bigr) \\ M\Bigl(x_k,d^{(i)}_e\Bigr) \end{bmatrix} \) (15.82a).

We then calculate the tangent stiffness matrices for each element from Equations 15.89 to 15.92 and assemble these in \( K_t(D^{(1)}_e) \) . At this point, we can evaluate the unknown displacement increment by solving Equation 15.61 for \( Δ D^{(1)}_e \).

\( k_{et}\Bigl(x , d^{(i)}_e\Bigr) = \frac{L}{2}\sum\limits_{k=1}^{n_G}{w_k} \begin{bmatrix} N^{\prime }_{u1} (x_k) & 0 \\ 0 & N^{\prime \prime}_{ν1} (x_k) \\ 0 & N^{\prime \prime}_{ν2} \\ N^{\prime }_{u2} (x_k) & 0 \\ N^{\prime }_{u3} (x_k) & 0 \\ 0 & N^{\prime \prime}_{ν3} (x_k) \\ 0 & N^{\prime \prime}_{ν4} (x_k) \end{bmatrix} × \begin{bmatrix} \frac{∂N^{(i)}_k}{∂d_{e1}} & \frac{∂N^{(i)}_k}{∂d_{e2}} & \frac{∂N^{(i)}_k}{∂d_{e3}} & \frac{∂N^{(i)}_k}{∂d_{e4}} & \frac{∂N^{(i)}_k}{∂d_{e5}} & \frac{∂N^{(i)}_k}{∂d_{e6}} & \frac{∂N^{(i)}_k}{∂d_{e7}} \\ \frac{∂M^{(i)}_k}{∂d_{e1}} & \frac{∂M^{(i)}_k}{∂d_{e2}} & \frac{∂M^{(i)}_k}{∂d_{e3}} & \frac{∂M^{(i)}_k}{∂d_{e4}} & \frac{∂M^{(i)}_k}{∂d_{e5}} & \frac{∂M^{(i)}_k}{∂d_{e6}} & \frac{∂M^{(i)}_k}{∂d_{e7}}\end{bmatrix} \) (15.89).

\( \frac{\partial \varepsilon ^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e1}} = \frac{\partial \left\{\bigl[1-y_i\bigr] B (x_k)d^{(i)}_e\right\} }{\partial d_{e1} } = – \frac{3}{L} +\frac{4x_k}{L^2} \) (15.90a).

\( \frac{\partial \varepsilon ^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e2}} = -y_i \Bigl(\frac{12x_k}{L^3} – \frac{6}{L^2} \Bigr) \), \( \frac{\partial \varepsilon ^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e3}} = -y_i \Bigl(\frac{6x_k}{L^2} – \frac{4}{L} \Bigr) \) (15.90b,c).

\( \frac{\partial \varepsilon ^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e4}} = \frac{4}{L} – \frac{8x_k}{L^2} \) , \( \frac{\partial \varepsilon ^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e5}} = -\frac{1}{L}+ \frac{4x_k}{L^2} \) (15.90d,e).

\( \frac{\partial \varepsilon ^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e6}} = -y_i \Bigl(\frac{6}{L^2} – \frac{12x_k}{L^3} \Bigr) \), \( \frac{\partial \varepsilon ^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e7}} = -y_i \Bigl(\frac{6x_k}{L^2} – \frac{2}{L} \Bigr) \) (15.90f,g).

\( \frac{\partial N^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e1}} \approx \sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{A_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon } \frac{\partial \varepsilon ^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e1}} = \sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{A_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon }\frac{\partial \left\{\bigl[1-y_i\bigr] B (x_k)d^{(i)}_e\right\} }{\partial d_{e1} } = \sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{A_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon } \Bigl(-\frac{3}{L} – \frac{4x_k}{L^2} \Bigr) \) (15.91a).

\( \frac{\partial N^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e2}} \approx \sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{A_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon } \frac{\partial \varepsilon ^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e2}} = \sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{A_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon } \biggl[-y_i \Bigl( \frac{12x_k}{L^3}- \frac{6}{L^2} \Bigr)\biggr] \) (15.91b).

\( \frac{\partial N^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e3}} \approx \sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{A_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon } biggl[-y_i \Bigl( \frac{6x_k}{L^2}- \frac{4}{L} \Bigr)\biggr] \) (15.91c).

\( \frac{\partial N^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e4}} \approx \sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{A_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon } \Bigl( \frac{4}{L^2}- \frac{8x_k}{L^2} \Bigr) \) (15.91d).

\( \frac{\partial N^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e5}} \approx \sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{A_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon } \Bigl( – \frac{1}{L}+ \frac{4x_k}{L^2} \Bigr) \) (15.91e).

\( \frac{\partial N^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e6}} \approx \sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{A_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon } biggl[-y_i \Bigl( \frac{6}{L^2}- \frac{12x_k}{L^3} \Bigr)\biggr] \) (15.91f).

\( \frac{\partial N^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e7}} \approx \sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{A_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon } biggl[-y_i \Bigl( \frac{6x_k}{L^2}- \frac{2}{L} \Bigr)\biggr] \) (15.91g).

\( \frac{\partial M^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e1}} \approx- \sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{y_jA_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon } \Bigl( – \frac{3}{L}+ \frac{4x_k}{L^2} \Bigr) \) (15.92a).

\( \frac{\partial M^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e2}} \approx -\sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{y_jA_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon } biggl[-y_i \Bigl( \frac{12x_k}{L^3}- \frac{6}{L^2} \Bigr)\biggr] =\sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{y^2_jA_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon } \Bigl( \frac{12x_k}{L^3}- \frac{6}{L^2} \Bigr) \) (15.92b).

\( \frac{\partial M^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e3}} \approx \sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{y^2_jA_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon } \Bigl( \frac{6x_k}{L^2}- \frac{4}{L} \Bigr) \) (15.92c).

\( \frac{\partial M^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e4}} \approx -\sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{y_jA_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon } \Bigl( \frac{4}{L}- \frac{8x_k}{L^2} \Bigr) \) (15.92d).

\( \frac{\partial M^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e5}} \approx -\sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{y_jA_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon } \Bigl( – \frac{1}{L}+ \frac{4x_k}{L^2} \Bigr) \) (15.92e).

\( \frac{\partial M^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e6}} \approx \sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{y^2_jA_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon } \Bigl( \frac{6x_k}{L^2}- \frac{2}{L} \Bigr) \) (15.92f).

\( \frac{\partial M^{(i)}_k}{\partial d_{e7}} \approx \sum\limits_{j=1}^{n_j}{y^2_jA_j} \frac{\partial \sigma \bigl(x_k,y_j,d^{(i)}_e\bigr) }{\partial \varepsilon } \Bigl( \frac{6x_k}{L^2}- \frac{2}{L} \Bigr) \) (15.92g).

\( K_t \Bigl(D^{(i)}_e\Bigr) ΔD^{(i)}_e = Q^{(i)}_R \) (15.61).

We then assign \( D^{(2)}_e = D^{(1)}_e + ΔD^{(1)}_e = ΔD^{(1)}_e \) and recalculate the internal actions \( K \Bigl(D^{(i)}_e\Bigr) \) so that we can verify the acceptance criteria of the convergence on the basis of \( norm_{tol2} = \left|Q ^{(2)}_R\right| / \left|Q\right| \), where \( Q ^{(2)}_R = Q – K \Bigl(D^{(2)}_e\Bigr) \). If \( norm_{tol2} \) is less than \( 10^{-5} \), then convergence is reached and the solution can be post-processed; otherwise, an additional iteration is required. The actual number of iterations depends on the number of layers and mesh discretisation specified in the solution process.

Comparisons between the results obtained at mid-span with the analytical solutions derived in Section 15.3.2 (referred to in the figure as ‘CFS’ for closed-form solution) and the finite element results (referred to in the figure as ‘FEA’) are plotted in Figure 15.21 for different levels of discretisation to provide better insight into the convergence behaviour. The number of layers used at the cross-section has been varied between 40, 80 and 200 to highlight how the results obtained with the discretisation of 40 layers (selected in for this particular cross-section) provide an acceptable prediction of the structural response. As expected, the convergence of the curvature is slower than convergence of the deflection because the former is based on the second derivative of the latter.

(2) A similar procedure is followed for the calculation of the solution when the beam is subjected to the load P = 475 kN. Comparisons between analytical (CFS) and numerical (FEA) values for the mid-span results of deflection and curvature are illustrated in Figure 15.22, which show similar trends to those observed at point 1. Because of the higher level of nonlinearity developed in the beam, a larger number of elements are required, when compared to point 1, to approach the values calculated with the analytical solution.

The variations along the member length are presented in Figure 15.23 for both deflection and curvature using the values plotted in Figure 15.15 as reference. Good agreement between the analytical and finite element results (calculated with a highly refined mesh) is evident.
