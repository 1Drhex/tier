Guest Question.

The double pendulum consists of 4 bars of equal length \(l\) and mass \(m\). The spring is unstrained in the vertical position of the hanging bars.

a) Determine the equations of motion by using the Lagrange equations.

b) Linearize the equations for small amplitudes.

Admin
Solution

a) The position of the conservative system is uniquely described by the angles \(\alpha \) and \(\beta \) . Accordingly, it has two degrees of freedom. To formulate the potential and kinetic energies, we introduce the \(x , y\)-coordinate system and determine first the coordinates and velocities of points ①, ② as well as the length change \(\Delta \) of the spring:

\(x_{1}=l\sin \alpha \) , \(x_{2}=l\left(\sin \alpha +\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{2}\sin \beta \right) \) ,

\(\dot{x}_{1}=l\dot{\alpha }\cos \alpha \) , \(\dot{x}_{2}=l\left(\dot{\alpha } \cos \alpha +\frac{1}{2}\dot{\beta } \cos \beta \right)\) ,

\(y_{1}=l\cos \alpha \) , \(y_{2}=l\left(\cos \alpha+\frac{1}{2}\cos \beta \right) \) ,

\(\dot{y}_{1}=-l\dot{\alpha }\sin \alpha \) , \(\dot{y}_{2}=-l\left( \dot{\alpha }\sin \alpha +\frac{1}{2}\dot{\beta } \sin \beta \right)\) ,

\(v^{2}_{1}=\dot{x}^{2}_{1}+\dot{y}^{2}_{1}=l^{2}\dot{\alpha }^{2}\left( \cos ^{2}\alpha +\sin^{2}\alpha \right)=l^{2}\dot{\alpha }^{2} \) ,

\(v^{2}_{2}=\dot{x}^{2}_{2}+\dot{y}^{2}_{2}=l^{2}\left(\dot{\alpha }^{2} \cos ^{2}\alpha +\dot{\alpha }\dot{\beta }\cos \alpha \cos \beta +\frac{1}{4}\dot{\beta }^{2}\cos^{2}\beta +\dot{\alpha }^{2} \sin^{2}\alpha +\dot{\alpha } \dot{\beta }\sin \alpha \sin \beta +\frac{1}{4}\dot{\beta }^{2} \sin^{2}\beta \right)\)

 

\(=l^{2}\left[\dot{\alpha }^{2}+\dot{\alpha } \dot{\beta }\cos \left(\alpha -\beta \right)+\frac{1}{4}\dot{\beta }^{2} \right] \) ,

\(\Delta =\sqrt{\left(l\sin \alpha +l \right)^{2}+l^{2}\cos ^{2}\alpha }-l\sqrt{2} =l\sqrt{2}\left(\sqrt{1+\sin \alpha } -1\right) \) ,

\(\Delta =\sqrt{\left(l\sin \alpha +l \right)^{2}+l^{2}\cos ^{2}\alpha }-l\sqrt{2} =l\sqrt{2}\left(\sqrt{1+\sin \alpha } -1\right) \) .

Herewith, the potential energy is obtained as

\(V=-2mg\frac{y_{1} }{2}-mgy_{1}-mgy_{2} +\frac{1}{2}k\Delta ^{2} \)

 

\(=-mgl\left(3\cos\alpha +\frac{1}{2}\cos\beta \right)+kl^{2} \left(2+\sin \alpha -2\sqrt{1+\sin \alpha } \right) \) .

When determining the kinetic energy, we consider that the motion of the upper bars is a pure rotation (angular velocity \(\dot{\alpha } \)) about the pins, the motion of the horizontal bar is a pure translation (velocity \(v_{1} \)) and the motion of the lower bar is a combination of translation (velocity \(v_{2}\) ) plus rotation (angular velocity \(\dot{\beta } \)). With the moment of inertia \(\Theta _{1}=ml^{2}/3\) of an upper bar with respect to the pin and the moment of inertia \(\Theta _{2}=ml^{2}/12 \) of the lower bar with respect to its center of mass ②, it follows

\(T=2\frac{1}{2}\Theta _{1}\dot{\alpha }^{2} +\frac{1}{2}mv^{2}_{1}+\left(\frac{1}{2}mv^{2}_{2} +\frac{1}{2}\Theta _{2}\dot{\beta }^{2} \right) \)

 

\(=ml^{2}\left[\frac{4}{3}\dot{\alpha }^{2}+\frac{1}{6}\dot{\beta }^{2}+\frac{1}{2}\dot{\alpha } \dot{\beta } \cos\left(\alpha -\beta \right) \right] \) .

With \(L = T − V\) and the derivatives

\(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \alpha }=-\frac{ml^{2} }{2}\dot{\alpha } \dot{\beta }\sin \left(\alpha -\beta \right)-3mgl\sin \alpha -kl^{2}\cos\alpha \left(1-\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+\sin\alpha } } \right) \) ,

\(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \beta }=\frac{ml^{2} }{2}\dot{\alpha } \dot{\beta }\sin \left(\alpha -\beta \right)-\frac{1}{2} mgl\sin \beta \) ,

\(\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\alpha } } \right)=ml^{2} \left[\frac{8}{3}\ddot{\alpha } +\frac{1}{2}\ddot{\beta }\cos \left(\alpha -\beta \right)-\frac{1}{2}\dot{\beta } \left(\dot{\alpha } -\dot{\beta } \right)\sin \left(\alpha -\beta \right) \right] \) ,

\(\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\beta } } \right)=ml^{2} \left[\frac{1}{6}\ddot{\beta } +\frac{1}{2}\ddot{\alpha }\cos \left(\alpha -\beta \right)-\frac{1}{2}\dot{\alpha } \left(\dot{\alpha } -\dot{\beta } \right)\sin \left(\alpha -\beta \right) \right] \) ,

we obtain from the LAGRANGE equations

\(\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial\dot{\alpha } } \right)-\frac{\partial L}{\partial \alpha }=0 \) , \(\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial\dot{\beta } } \right)-\frac{\partial L}{\partial \beta }=0 \)

the equations of motion:

\(\underline{\underline{\frac{8}{3}\ddot{\alpha }+\frac{1}{2}\ddot{\beta }\cos \left(\alpha -\beta \right)+\frac{1}{2}\dot{\beta }^{2}\sin \left(\alpha -\beta \right)+3\frac{g}{l}\sin\alpha +\frac{k}{m}\cos\alpha \left(1-\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+\sin\alpha } } \right)=0 } } \) ,

\(\underline{\underline{\frac{1}{6}\ddot{\beta }+\frac{1}{2}\ddot{\alpha }\cos \left(\alpha -\beta \right)-\frac{1}{2}\dot{\alpha }^{2}\sin \left(\alpha -\beta \right)+\frac{1}{2} \frac{g}{l}\sin\beta =0 } } \) .

b) For small amplitudes \(\alpha \ll 1\) , \(\beta \ll 1\) and \(\dot{\alpha }\ll 1 \) , \(\dot{\beta }\ll 1 \) the following linearizations apply:

\(\sin \alpha \approx \alpha \) , \(\cos \alpha \approx 1 \) , \(\sin \left( \alpha-\beta \right) \approx \left(\alpha -\beta \right) \) , \(\cos \left( \alpha-\beta \right) \approx 1\) ,

\(\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+\sin \alpha } } \approx \frac{1}{\sqrt{1+ \alpha } }\approx 1-\alpha\) , \(\dot{\beta }^{2}\sin \left(\alpha -\beta \right)\approx 0 \) , \(\dot{\alpha }^{2}\sin \left(\alpha -\beta \right)\approx 0 \) .

Herewith, the equations of motion simplify to

\(\underline{\underline{\ddot{\alpha }+\frac{3}{16}\ddot{\beta }+\left(\frac{9g}{8l}+\frac{3k}{8m} \right)\alpha =0 } } \) , \(\underline{\underline{\ddot{\beta }+3\ddot{\alpha }+3\frac{g}{l}\beta =0} } \) .
