Guest Question.

A steam power plant installation has reheating and regenerative feed water heating employing a surface type feed heater and other contact type feed heater on high pressure side and low pressure side respectively. Steam enters HP turbine at 100 bar, 803 K and leaves high pressure turbine at 25 bar from where some steam is bled for feed heating in high pressure surface type heater and remaining is reheated up to 823 K and then expanded in low pressure turbine up to 0.05 bar pressure. The contact type feed heater is supplied with steam bled at 6 bar from LP steam turbine. There occurs throttling pressure loss of 3 bar in reheater. Surface type feed heater sends the drain to contact type feed heater from where the total feed is sent to surface type feed heater employing a boiler feed water pump as saturated water at pressure of 100 bar. Determine the amounts of steam bled off, overall thermal efficiency and specific steam consumption in kg/kwh. Considering tubring efficiency pump efficiency, generator efficiency, and mechanical efficiency as 0.85, 0.90 & 0.95 respectively and plant output as 120 MW. Consider discharges of drains at saturated liquid state at respective pressures in feed heaters. Also show how the processes on T-s and h-s diagrams along with line sketch of arrangement.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{From steam table,}\\\text{At 100 bar, 803 K the state of inlet steam}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad h_{1} = 3450.02 kJ/kg, s_{1} = 6.6923 kJ/kg.K\\\text{At inlet to LP steam turbine at 22 bar, 823K}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad h_{3} = 3576.99 kJ/kg, s_{3} = 7.52411 kJ/kg.K\\\text{For exit from HP turbine, }s_{1} = s_{2}\\\text{Using Mollier diagram}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad h_{2} = 3010, kJ/kg.K.\\\text{Let us consider 1 kg of steam generated in boiler and bled fractions be }m_{1} \& m_{2}.\\ \begin{aligned}\text{Considering turbine efficiency, }η_{turb}&= \frac{h_{1} – h_{2}^{ \prime}}{h_{1} – h_{2}}\\0.85&= \frac{3450.02 – h_{2}^{ \prime}}{(3450.02 – 3010)}\\⇒ \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad h_{2}^{ \prime}&= 3076.003 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{From Mollier diagram, considering isentropic expansion in LP turbine}\\\begin{aligned}h_{9}&= 3175 kJ/kg\\h_{4}&= 2300 kJ/kg\\\text{Considering turbine efficiency, }0.85&= \frac{h_{3} – h_{9}^{ \prime}}{h_{3} – h_{9}}\\0.85&= \frac{(3576.99 – h_{9}^{ \prime})}{(3576.99 – 3175)}\\⇒ \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad\quad h_{9}^{ \prime}&= 3235.29 kJ/kg\\\text{Also} \qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad 0.85&= \frac{h_{3} – h_{4}^{ \prime}}{h_{3} – h_{4}} = \frac{(3576.99 – h_{4}^{ \prime})}{(3576.99 – 2300)}\\⇒ \qquad\qquad\qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad h_{4}^{ \prime}&= 2491.5 kJ/kg\\\text{From steam table} \quad\qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad\qquad &\\h_{5}&= h_{\text{f at 0.05 bar}} = 137.82 kJ/kg,\\v_{s}&= v_{\text{f at 0.05 bar}} = 0.001005 m^{3}/kg\\h_{8}&= h_{\text{f at 25 bar}} = 962.11 kJ/kg,\\h_{12}&= h_{\text{f at 100 bar}} = 1407.56 kJ/kg,\end{aligned}\\\text{Energy balance at surface type feed heater yields;}\\\begin{aligned}m_{1} × h_{7} + h_{11}^{ \prime}&= h_{12} + m_{1} h_{8} ⇒ m_{1} = \frac{(h_{11}^{ \prime} – h_{12})}{(h_{8} – h_{7})}\\\text{In this arrangement, }\qquad \quad \qquad\qquad \qquad h_{2}&= h_{7} ⇒ m_{1} = \frac{(h_{11}^{ \prime} – h_{12})}{(h_{8} – h_{2}^{ \prime})}\\\text{Energy balance at contact type heater yields}&\\m_{1} × h_{8} + m_{2} × h_{9} + (1 – m_{1} – m_{2})h_{6}^{ \prime}&= 1 × h_{10}\end{aligned}\\\text{for process 5–6 in condensate extraction pump,}\\\begin{aligned}h_{6} – h_{5}&= v_{5} . Δp = v_{5} × (p_{6} – p_{5})\\h_{6} – 137.82&= 0.001005 × (6 – 0.05) × 10^{2}\\⇒\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad\quad h_{6}&= 138.42 kJ/kg\\\text{Considering pump efficiency }η_{pump}&= \frac{(h_{6} – h_{5})}{(h_{6}^{ \prime} – h_{5})}\\0.90&= \frac{(138.42 – 137.82)}{(h_{6}^{ \prime} – 137.82)}\\h_{6}^{ \prime}&= 138.49 kJ/kg\\\text{From steam tables, }\qquad \qquad \qquad&\\h_{10}&= h_{\text{f at 6 bar}} = 670.56 kJ/kg\\v_{10}&= v_{\text{f at 6 bar}} = 0.001101 m^{3}/kg\\h_{8}&= h_{\text{f at 25 bar}} = 962.11 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Substituting in heat balance of contact type feed heater,}\\(m_{1} × 962.11) + (m_{2} × 3175) + ((1 – m_{1} – m_{2}) × 138.49) = 670.56\\(m_{1} × 823.62) + (m_{2} × 3036.51) = 532.07\\\text{For process 10-11 in boiler feed pump;}\\\begin{aligned}(h_{11} – h_{10}) &= v_{10} . Δp = 0.001101 × (100 – 6) × 10^{2}\\h_{11}&= 670.56 + (0.001101 × 94 × 10^{2})\\h_{11}&= 680.91\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Considering pump efficiency, 0.90}&= \frac{(h_{11} – h_{10})}{(h_{11}^{ \prime} – h_{10})}\\h_{11}^{ \prime}&= 682.06 kJ/kg\\\text{Substituting values, }m_{1}&= \frac{(682.06 – 1407.56)}{(962.11 – 3076.003)}\\&= \text{0.343 kg/kg of inlet steam to HP turbine}\end{aligned}\\\text{Also substitution yields;}\\(0.343 × 823.62) + (m_{2} × 3036.51) = 532.07\\\qquad \qquad m_{2} = \text{0.082 kg/kg of inlet steam to HP turbine}\\\text{Work from HP turbine, }W_{HP} = (h_{1} – h_{2}^{ \prime})\\\qquad \qquad W_{HP} = (3450.02 – 3076.003) = 374.017 KJ/kg\\\text{Work from LP turbine,}\\\begin{aligned}W_{LP}&= (1 – m_{1}) (h_{3} – h_{9}^{ \prime}) + (1 – m_{1} – m_{2}) (h_{9}^{ \prime} – h_{4}^{ \prime})\\W_{LP}&= (1 – 0.343)) (3576.99 – 3235.29) + (1 – 0.343 – 0.082) × (3235.29 – 2491.5)\\W_{LP}&= 652.18 kJ/kg\\\text{Pump work}&= W_{CEP} + W_{BFP} = (1 – m_{1} – m_{2}) (h_{6}^{ \prime} – h_{5}) + ( h_{11}^{ \prime} – h_{10})\\W_{pump}&= (1 – 0.343 – 0.082) (138.49 – 137.82) + (682.06 – 670.56)\\&= 0.38525 + 11.5 = 11.885 kJ/kg\\\text{Net work, }W_{net}&= W_{HP} + W_{LP} – W_{pump}\\&= 374.017 + 652.18 – 11.885\\&= 1014.312 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Specific steam consumption}&= \frac{3600}{W_{net} × η_{gen} × η_{mech}}\\&= \frac{3600}{(1014.312 × 0.95 × 0.95)}\\\text{Specific steam consumption}&= 3.93 kg/kw.h\\\text{Overall thermal efficiency}&= \frac{W_{net} × η_{mech} × η_{gen}}{(h_{1} – h_{12}) + (1 – m_{1})(h_{3} – h_{2}^{ \prime})}\\&= \frac{1014.312 × 0.95 × 0.95}{(345.02 – 1407.56) + (1 – 0.343)(3576.99 – 3076.003)}\\\text{Overall thermal efficiency, }η_{overall}&= \text{0.3656 or 36.56 }\%\\\text{Mass of steam required}&= \text{specific steam consumption × 120 × 103}\\&= 471600 kg/hr\\\text{Mass of steam bled from HP turbine}&= 0.343 × 471600 = 161758.8 kg/hr\\\text{Mass of steam held from LP turbine}& = 0.082 × 471600 = 38671.2 kg/hr\end{aligned}\)
