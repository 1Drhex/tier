Guest Question.

A solid 1.50-in.-diameter brass [G = 5,600 ksi] shaft [segments (1), (2), and (3)] has been stiffened between B and C by the addition of a cold-rolled stainless steel tube (4) (Figure P6.80a). The tube (Figure P6.80b) has an outside diameter of 3.50 in., a wall thickness of 0.12 in., and a shear modulus of G = 12,500 ksi. The tube is attached to the brass shaft by means of rigid flanges welded to the tube and to the shaft. (The thickness of the flanges can be neglected for this analysis.) If a torque of 400 lb-ft is applied to the shaft as shown in Figure P6.80a, determine:
(a) the maximum shear stress magnitude in segment (1) of the brass shaft.
(b) the maximum shear stress magnitude in segment (2) of the brass shaft (i.e., between flanges B and C)
(c) the maximum shear stress magnitude in the stainless steel tube (4).
(d) the rotation angle of end D relative to end A.

Admin
Solution

Section Properties: The polar moments of inertia for the brass shaft and the stainless steel tube are:

\(\begin{aligned}&J_{1}=J_{2}=J_{3}=\frac{\pi}{32}(1.50 \text { in. })^{4}=0.497010 in .^{4} \\&J_{4}=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[(3.50 in .)^{4}-(3.26 in .)^{4}\right]=3.643915 in. ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

(a) Shear Stress Magnitude in Brass Segment (1):

\(\tau_{1}=\frac{T_{1} c_{1}}{J_{1}}=\frac{(400 lb – ft )(1.500 in . / 2)(12 in . / ft )}{0.497010 in .^{4}}=7,243.3 psi =7,240 psi\)

Statically Indeterminate Portion of the Shaft: The portion of the shaft between B and C is statically indeterminate.

Equilibrium: Consider a free-body diagram that cuts through the shaft between B and C and includes the free end of the shaft at A:

\(\Sigma M_{x}=T_{2}+T_{4}+400 lb – ft =0\)

Geometry-of-Deformation Relationship: Since the tube and shaft are securely connected by the rigid flanges, the angles of twist in both members must be equal; therefore,

\(\phi_{2}=\phi_{4}\) (b)

Torque-Twist Relationships:

\(\phi_{2}=\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}} \quad \phi_{4}=\frac{T_{4} L_{4}}{J_{4} G_{4}}\) (c)

Compatibility Equation: Substitute the torque-twist relationships [Eqs. (c)] into the Geometry-ofdeformation relationship [Eq. (b)] to obtain the compatibility equation:

\(\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}=\frac{T_{4} L_{4}}{J_{4} G_{4}}\) (d)

Solve the Equations: Solve Eq. (d) for \(T_{2}\):

\(T_{2}=T_{4} \frac{L_{4}}{L_{2}} \frac{J_{2}}{J_{4}} \frac{G_{2}}{G_{4}}=T_{4}\left(\frac{20 in. }{20 in. }\right)\left(\frac{0.497010 in. ^{4}}{3.643915 in. ^{4}}\right)\left(\frac{5,600 ksi }{12,500 ksi }\right)=0.0611047 T_{4}\)

and substitute this result into Eq. (a) to compute the torque \(T_{4}\) in the stainless steel tube (4):

\(\begin{aligned}T_{2}+T_{4} &=0.0611047 T_{4}+T_{4}=1.0611047 T_{4}=-400 lb – ft \\& \therefore T_{4}=-376.9656 lb – ft\end{aligned}\)

The torque in brass shaft segment (2) is therefore:

\(T_{2}=-400 lb – ft -T_{4}=-400 lb – ft -(-376.9656 lb – ft )=-23.0344 lb – ft\)

(b) Maximum Shear Stress in Brass Segment (2): The maximum shear stress in segment (2) is:

\(\tau_{2}=\frac{T_{2} c_{2}}{J_{2}}=\frac{(23.0344 lb – ft )(1.50 in . / 2)(12 in. / ft )}{0.497010 in .^{4}}=417.1 psi =417 psi\)

(c) Maximum Shear Stress in Stainless Steel Tube (4): The maximum shear stress in tube (4) is:

\(\tau_{4}=\frac{T_{4} c_{4}}{J_{4}}=\frac{(376.9656 lb – ft )(3.50 in . / 2)(12 in . / ft )}{3.643915 in .{ }^{4}}=2,172.5 psi =2,170 psi\)

(d) Rotation Angle of End D Relative to End A: The angle of twist of segment (1) is:

\(\phi_{1}=\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}=\frac{(-400 lb – ft )(12 in .)(12 in. / ft )}{\left(0.497010 in .^{4}\right)(5,600,000 psi )}=-0.020695 rad\)

The angle of twist in brass segment (3) has the same value:

\(\phi_{3}=-0.020695 rad\)

The angle of twist in brass segment (2) is:

\(\phi_{2}=\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}=\frac{(-23.0344 lb – ft )(20 in. )(12 in. / ft )}{\left(0.497010 in .^{4}\right)(5,600,000 psi )}=-0.0019863 rad\)

The rotation angle of end D relative to end A is the sum of these three angles of twist:

\(\begin{aligned}\phi_{D} &=\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}+\phi_{3} \\&=-0.020695 rad +(-0.0019863 rad )+(-0.020695 rad ) \\&=-0.0433763 rad =-0.0434 rad\end{aligned}\)
