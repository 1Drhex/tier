Guest Question.

For the frame shown in Figure 6.31, calculate the support reactions caused by the applied loads and draw the axial force, shear force and bending moment diagrams.

Admin
Solution

The deflected shape of the frame is shown in Figure 6.32, with sidesway occurring because the applied loading is non-symmetric. Both columns AB and CD suffer the same transverse displacement Δ at the top, as shown, and with the axis of both columns rotating in a clockwise sense, Δ/L is negative for both columns.

Member AB: As this member is not subjected to external loads, the fixed-end moments are
zero. With \( θ_A = 0 \), the slope-deflection equations for member AB are (Equations 6.17):

\( M_A = \frac{EI}{L} \biggl(4 \theta _A + 2 \theta _B – \frac{6\Delta }{L} \biggr) + M_{FE.A} \) \( M_B = \frac{EI}{L} \biggl(2 \theta _A + 4 \theta _B – \frac{6\Delta }{L} \biggr) + M_{FE.B} \) (6.17a,b)

\( (M_A)_{AB} = \frac{EI}{L} \biggl(2 \theta _B – \frac{6\Delta }{L} \biggr) = \frac{EI}{5} \biggl(2 \theta _B – \frac{6\Delta }{5} \biggr) = EI (0.4 θ_B – 0.24 \Delta) \) (1).

\( (M_B)_{AB} = \frac{EI}{L} \biggl(4 \theta _B – \frac{6\Delta }{L} \biggr) = \frac{EI}{5} \biggl(4 \theta _B – \frac{6\Delta }{5} \biggr) = EI (0.8 θ_B – 0.24 \Delta) \) (2).

Member BC: From Appendix B, the fixed-end moments are:

\( M_{FE.B} = – \frac{-100 × 3 × 6^2}{9^2} – \frac{-60 ×6 × 3^2}{9^2} = + 173.3\) kNm.

\( M_{FE.C} = + \frac{-100 × 3^2 × 6}{9^2} + \frac{-60 ×6^2 × 3}{9^2} = – 164.4\) kNm.

and, for member BC, Δ = 0. The slope-deflection equations are:

\( (M_B)_{BC} = \frac{EI}{L} \biggl(4 \theta _B + 2 \theta _C – \frac{6\Delta }{L} \biggr) + M_{FE.B} = \frac{EI}{9} (4 \theta _B + 2 \theta _C ) +173.3 \)

\( = EI(0.444 \theta _B + 0.222 \theta _C ) +173.3 \) (3) .

\( (M_C)_{BC} = \frac{EI}{L} \biggl(2 \theta _B + 4 \theta _C – \frac{6\Delta }{L} \biggr) + M_{FE.C} = \frac{EI}{9} (2 \theta _B + 4 \theta _C ) -146.6 \)

\( = EI(0.222 \theta _B + 0.444 \theta _C ) -146.6 \) (4) .

Member CD: As the member is not subjected to external loads, the fixed-end moments are zero. With \( θ_D = 0\), the slope-deflection equations for member CD are:

\( (M_C)_{CD} = \frac{EI}{L} \biggl(4 \theta _C – \frac{6\Delta }{L} \biggr) = \frac{EI}{5} \biggl(4 \theta _C – \frac{6\Delta }{5} \biggr) = EI (0.8 θ_C – 0.24 \Delta) \) (5).

\( (M_D)_{CD} = \frac{EI}{L} \biggl(2 \theta _C – \frac{6\Delta }{L} \biggr) = \frac{EI}{5} \biggl(2 \theta _C – \frac{6\Delta }{5} \biggr) = EI (0.4 θ_C – 0.24 \Delta) \) (6).

Equations 1 through 6 contain nine unknowns. We get two more equations by considering moment equilibrium of corners B and C:

\( (M_B)_{AB} + (M_B)_{BC} = 0 \) (7).

\( (M_C)_{BC} + (M_C)_{CD} = 0 \) (8).

and the ninth equation can be obtained by considering horizontal equilibrium of the frame. Figure 6.33a shows free-body diagrams of column AB. By considering moment equilibrium about B, we can express the reaction \( H_A \) in terms of the end moments \( (M_A)_{AB} \) and \( (M_B)_{AB} \) :

\( (H_A × 5) + (M_A)_{AB} + (M_B)_{AB} = 0 \) ∴ \( H_A = – \frac{(M_A)_{AB} + (M_B)_{AB}}{5} \) .

Similarly, moment equilibrium about C on a free-body of CD gives (Figure 6.33b):

– \( (H_D × 5) + (M_D)_{CD} + (M_C)_{CD} = 0 \) ∴ \( H_D = – \frac{(M_D)_{CD} + (M_C)_{CD}}{5} \) .

The horizontal force equilibrium equation for the frame is:

\( 50 + H_A – H_D = 0 \) ∴ \( 50 – \frac{(M_A)_{AB} + (M_B)_{AB}}{5} – \frac{(M_D)_{CD} + (M_C)_{CD}}{5} = 0 \) (9).

By substituting Equations 2 and 3 into Equation 7, we get:

\( 1.244 θ_B + 0.222θ_C – 0.24 Δ = − 173.3 /EI \) (10).

and substituting Equations 4 and 5 into Equation 8, we get:

\( 0.222 θ_B + 1.244 θ_C – 0.24 Δ = + 146.6 /EI \) (11).

Finally, substituting Equations 1, 2, 5 and 6 into Equation 9 gives:

–\( 0.24 θ_B – 0.24 θ_C + 0.192 Δ = -50 /EI \) (12).

Equations 10, 11 and 12 represent three simultaneous equations in three unknowns (\( θ_B, θ_C \) and Δ).
Solving, we get:

\( θ_B = −244.0/EI \); \( θ_C = +69.0/EI \); and Δ = −479.2/EI.

and with these values, Equations 1 through 6 give:

\( (M_A)_{AB} = +17.39 \) kNm; \( (M_B)_{AB} = −80.20 \) kNm; \( (M_B)_{BC} = +80.23 \) kNm;

\( (M_C)_{BC} = −170.2 \) kNm; \( (M_C)_{CD} = +170.2 \) kNm; \( (M_D)_{CD} = +142.6 \) kNm.

Equilibrium: The free-body diagram in Figure 6.34a shows the moment reactions at A and D (\( (M_A)_{AB} \) and \( (M_D)_{DC} \) , respectively), together with the other four force reactions, \( H_A, V_A, H_D \) and \( V_D \) . Free-body diagrams of columns AB and CD are shown in Figures 6.34b and c, respectively.

Summing the moments about C in Figure 6.34c:

\( (H_D × 5) − 170.2 − 142.6 = 0 \) ∴ \( H_D = +62.56 \) kN (i.e. ←).

Summing the moments about B in Figure 6.34b:

\( 80.2 − 17.39 − (H_A × 5) = 0 \) ∴ \( H_A = 12.56 \) kN (i.e. →).

Summing the moments about D in Figure 6.34a:

\( (V_A × 9) − 17.39 − 142.6 + (50 × 5) − (100 × 6) − (60 × 3) = 0 \) ∴ \( V_A = 76.67 \) kN (i.e. ↑).

Summing the moments about A in Figure 6.34a:

\( −(V_D × 9) − 17.39 − 142.6 + (50 × 5) + (100 × 3) + (60 × 6) = 0 \) ∴ \( V_D = 83.33 \) kN (i.e. ↑).

The axial force, shear force and bending moment diagrams are readily determined from statics and are shown in Figure 6.35.
