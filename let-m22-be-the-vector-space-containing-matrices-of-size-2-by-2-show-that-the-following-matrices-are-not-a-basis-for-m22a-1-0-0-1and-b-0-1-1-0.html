Guest Question.

Let \(M_{22}\) be the vector space containing matrices of size 2 by 2 . Show that the following matrices are not a basis for \(M_{22}\) :

\(\mathbf{A}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}1 & 0 \\0 & 1\end{array}\right) \text { and } \mathbf{B}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}0 & 1 \\1 & 0\end{array}\right)\)
Admin
Solution

How do we show this result?

We need to show either that the matrices \(\mathrm{A}\) and \(\mathrm{B}\) do not span \(M_{22}\) or that they are linearly dependent. To show that matrices \(\mathbf{A}\) and \(\mathrm{B}\) do not span \(M_{22}\), we only need to select a matrix in \(M_{22}\) and prove that a linear combination of \(\mathrm{A}\) and \(\mathrm{B}\) do not generate the selected matrix.

Consider the matrix \(\mathbf{C}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4\end{array}\right)\). Let \(k_{1}\) and \(k_{2}\) be scalars then

\(\begin{aligned}k_{1} \mathbf{A}+k_{2} \mathbf{B} &=k_{1}\left(\begin{array}{cc}1 & 0 \\0 & 1\end{array}\right)+k_{2}\left(\begin{array}{cc}0 & 1 \\1 & 0\end{array}\right) \\&=\left(\begin{array}{cc}k_{1} & 0 \\0 & k_{1}\end{array}\right)+\left(\begin{array}{cc}0 & k_{2} \\k_{2} & 0\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{ll}k_{1} & k_{2} \\k_{2} & k_{1}\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{ll}1 & 2 \\3 & 4\end{array}\right)\end{aligned}\)

By equating entries we have

\(\left| \begin{array}{l}\hline k_{1}=1, k_{2}=2, k_{2}=3 \text { and } k_{1}=4 \\\hline\end{array}\right|\)

This is inconsistent because we have two different values for \(k_{1}, 1\) and 4 , which means that we cannot obtain the matrix \(\left(\begin{array}{ll}1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4\end{array}\right)\). Hence the given matrices \(\mathbf{A}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1\end{array}\right)\) and \(\mathbf{B}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0\end{array}\right)\) do not span \(M_{22}\) because we cannot generate the matrix \(\mathrm{C}\), therefore these matrices cannot form a basis for \(M_{22}\) The scalar \(k_{2}\) also has two different values; 2 and 3 .

[Note that matrices \(\mathrm{A}\) and \(\mathrm{B}\) are linearly independent but that is not enough to be a basis.] In fact, the given matrices only produce matrices of the form \(\left(\begin{array}{ll}a & b \\ b & a\end{array}\right)\), which means that we cannot obtain the matrix \(\left(\begin{array}{cc}a & b \\ c & d\end{array}\right)\) where \(a \neq d\) or \(b \neq c\)
