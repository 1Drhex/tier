Guest Question.

Determine the joint displacements, member end forces, and support reactions for the three-span continuous beam shown in Fig. 5.17(a), using the matrix stiffness method.

Admin
Solution

Analytical Model: See Fig. 5.17(b). The beam has two degrees of freedom—the rotations of joints 2 and 3—which are numbered 1 and 2, respectively. The six restrained coordinates of the beam are numbered 3 through 8.
Structure Stiffness Matrix and Fixed-Joint Force Vector:
Member 1 By substituting E = 29,000 ksi, I = 510 \(in.^{4}\), and L = 240 in. into Eq. (5.53) \(k=\frac{EI}{L^{3}}=\left[\begin{matrix} 12 & 6L & -12 & 6L\\ 6L & 4L^{2} & -6L &2L^{2} \\ -12 & -6L &12 &-6L \\6L&2L^{2} &-6L &4L^{2} \end{matrix} \right]\) , we obtain

(1)

Using the fixed-end force equations given inside the front cover, we evaluate the fixedend forces due to the 30 k concentrated load as

\(FS_{b}=\frac{30(120)^{2}}{(240)^{3}}=[3(120)+120]=15 k\)

\(FM_{b}=\frac{30 (120)(120)^{2}}{(240)^{2}}=900 k-in\) .

\(FS_{e}=\frac{30(120)^{2}}{(240)^{3}}[120+3(120)]=15k\)

\(FM_{e}=-\frac{30 (120)^{2}(120)}{(240)^{2}}=-900 k-in\) .

Thus, the fixed-end force vector for member 1 is

\(Q_{f1}=\left[\begin{matrix} 15 \\900 \\ \underset{……..}{15} \\-900 \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 3 \\ 4 \\5 \\1 \end{matrix}\) (2)

From Fig. 5.17(b), we observe that the code numbers for member 1 are 3, 4, 5, 1.
Using these code numbers, the pertinent elements of \(k_{1}\) and \(Q_{f1}\) are stored in their proper positions in the 2 × 2 structure stiffness matrix S and the 2 × 1 structure fixed-joint force vector \(P_{f}\), respectively, as shown in Fig. 5.17(c).
Member 2 E = 29,000 ksi, I = 510 \(in.^{4}\), and L = 180 in.

(3)

Since this member is not subjected to any external loads, its fixed-end force vector is 0; that is,

\(Q_{f2}=0\) (4)

Using the code numbers 5, 1, 6, 2 for this member (see Fig. 5.17(b)), the relevant elements of \(k_{2}\) are stored into S, as shown in Fig. 5.17(c).
Member 3 E = 29,000 ksi, I = 510 \(in.^{4}\) , and L = 240 in.

(5)

The fixed-end forces due to the 0.125 k/in. (=1.5 k/ft) uniformly distributed load are

\(FS_{b}=\frac{0.125(240)}{2}=15 k\)

\(FM_{b}=\frac{0.125(240)^{2}}{12}= 600 k-in\)

\(FS_{e}=\frac{0.125(240)}{2}=15 k\)

\(FM_{e}=-\frac{0.125(240)^{2}}{12}= -600 k-in\)

Thus,

\(Q_{f1}=\left[\begin{matrix} \underset{……….}{15} \\ \underset{……..}{600} \\15\\-600 \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 6 \\ 2 \\7 \\8 \end{matrix}\) (6)

The relevant elements of \(k_{3}\) and \(Q_{f3}\) are stored in S and Pf, respectively, using the member code numbers 6, 2, 7, 8.
The completed structure stiffness matrix S and structure fixed-joint force vector \(P_{f}\) are given in Fig. 5.17(c). Note that the S matrix is symmetric.
Joint Load Vector: Since no external loads (i.e., moments) are applied to the beam at joints 2 and 3, the joint load vector is 0; that is,

P = 0

Joint Displacements: By substituting the numerical values of P, Pf, and S into Eq. (5.109), we write the stiffness relations for the entire continuous beam as

\(P − P_{f} = Sd\)

\(\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\0 \end{matrix} \right]- \left[\begin{matrix} -900 \\600 \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} 575,167 & 164,333 \\ 164,333 & 575,167 \end{matrix} \right]\left[\begin{matrix}d_{1} \\ d_{2} \end{matrix} \right]\)

or

\(\left[\begin{matrix} 900 \\-600 \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} 575,167 & 164,333 \\ 164,333 & 575,167 \end{matrix} \right]\left[\begin{matrix}d_{1} \\ d_{2} \end{matrix} \right]\)

By solving these equations simultaneously, we determine the joint displacements to be

\(d=\left[\begin{matrix}2.0284 \\ -1.6227 \end{matrix} \right] \times 10^{-3} rad\)

To check the foregoing solution, we substitute the numerical values of d back into the
structure stiffness relationship to obtain

\(P − P_{f} = Sd = \left[\begin{matrix} 575,167 & 164,333 \\ 164,333 & 575,167 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix}2.0284 \\ -1.6227 \end{matrix} \right] \times 10^{-3}=\left[\begin{matrix}900.01 \\ -599.99 \end{matrix} \right]\)

Member End Displacements and End Forces:

Member 1 The member end displacements u can be obtained simply by comparing the member’s degree of freedom numbers with its code numbers, as follows:

\(u_{1}=\left[\begin{matrix}u_{1} \\ u_{2} \\ u_{3}\\u_{4} \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 3 \\ 4 \\ 5\\1 \end{matrix} = \left[\begin{matrix}0\\ 0 \\0\\d_{1} \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix}0\\ 0 \\0\\ 2.0284 \end{matrix} \right]\times 10^{-3}\) (7)

Note that the member code numbers (3, 4, 5, 1), when written on a side of u as shown in Eq. (7), define the compatibility equations for the member. Since the code numbers corresponding to \(u_{1}, u_{2}\), and u3 are the restrained coordinate numbers 3, 4, and 5, respectively, this indicates that \(u_{1} = u_{2}=u_{3}=0\),. Similarly, the code number 1 corresponding to \(u_{4}\) indicates that \(u_{4}\) = \(d_{1}\). The foregoing compatibility equations can be easily verified by a visual inspection of the beam’s line diagram, given in Fig. 5.17(b).
The member end forces can now be calculated, using the member stiffness relationship
Q = ku + \(Q_{f}\) (Eq. (5.4)). Using \(k_{1}\) and \(Q_{f1}\) from Eqs. (1) and (2), respectively,we write

\(Q_{1}=\left[\begin{matrix} 12.839 & 1,540.6 &- 12.839 & 1,540.6\\ 1,540.6 & 246,500 & -1,540.6 & 123,250 \\- 12.839 & -1,540.6 & 12.839 &-1,540.6 \\1,540.6 & 123,250&-1,540.6 & 246,500 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0\\2.0284 \end{matrix} \right]\times 10^{-3} + \left[\begin{matrix} 15 \\900 \\ 15\\-900 \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix}18.125k \\ 1,150 k-in \\\underset{………}{11.875k} \\-400 k-in \end{matrix} \right]\) (8)

The end forces for member 1 are shown in Fig. 5.17(d). We can check our calculation of end forces by applying the equilibrium equations , \(∑ F_{y} = 0 and ∑ M=0\) , to the free body of member 1 to ensure that it is in equilibrium. Thus,

\(+\uparrow \sum{F_{y}}=0 18.125-30+11.875=0\)

\(+\hookrightarrow \sum{M_{1}}=0 1,150-30(120) -400+11.875 (240)=0\)

Next, to generate the support reaction vector R, we write the member code numbers (3, 4, 5, 1) on the right side of \(Q_{1}\) , as shown in Eq. (8), and store the pertinent elements of \(Q_{1}\) in their proper positions in R by matching the code numbers on the side of \(Q_{1}\) to the restrained coordinate numbers on the right side of R (see Fig. 5.17(e)).
Member 2 The member end displacements are given by

\(u_{1}=\left[\begin{matrix}u_{1} \\ u_{2} \\ u_{3}\\u_{4} \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 5 \\ 1 \\ 6\\2 \end{matrix} = \left[\begin{matrix}0\\ d_{1} \\0\\d_{2} \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix}0\\ 2.0284 \\0\\ -1.6227 \end{matrix} \right]\times 10^{-3}\)

By using \(k_{2}\) from Eq. (3) and \(Q_{f2}\) = 0, we compute member end forces as

Q = ku + \(Q_{f}\)

\(Q_{2}=\left[\begin{matrix} 30.432 & 2,738.9 &- 30.432 & 2,738.9\\ 2,738.9 & 328.667 & -2,738.9 & 164,333 \\- 30.432 & -2,738.9 & 30.432 &-2,738.9 \\2,738.9 & 164,333&-2,738.9 & 328.667 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 2.0284 \\ 0\\-1.6227 \end{matrix} \right]\times 10^{-3}\)

\(=\left[\begin{matrix} \underset{……}{1.1111k} \\ \underset{……}{400k-in} \\ \underset{……}{-1.1111k} \\\underset{……}{-200k-in} \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 5 \\ 1 \\ 6\\2 \end{matrix}\)

The foregoing member end forces are shown in Fig. 5.17(d). To check our calculations, we apply the equations of equilibrium to the free body of member 2 as

\(+\uparrow \sum{F_{y}}=0 1.1111-1.1111=0\)

\(+\hookrightarrow \sum{M_{2}}=0 400-200-1.1111(180) =0.002\approx 0\)

Next, we store the pertinent elements of \(Q_{2}\) in their proper positions in the reaction vector R, using the member code numbers (5, 1, 6, 2), as shown in Fig. 5.17(e).

Member 3

\(u_{3}=\left[\begin{matrix}u_{1} \\ u_{2} \\ u_{3}\\u_{4} \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 6 \\ 2 \\ 7\\8 \end{matrix} = \left[\begin{matrix}0\\ d_{2} \\0\\0 \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix}0\\-1.6227\\ 0\\0 \end{matrix} \right]\times 10^{-3}\)

By substituting \(k_{3}\) and \(Q_{f3}\) from Eqs. (5) and (6), respectively, into the member stiffness relationship Q = ku + \(Q_{f}\), we determine the end forces for member 3 to be

\(Q_{3}=\left[\begin{matrix} 12.839 & 1,540.6 &- 12.839 & 1,540.6\\ 1,540.6 & 246,500 & -1,540.6 & 123,250 \\- 12.839 & -1,540.6& 12.839 &-1,540.6 \\1,540.6 & 123,250 &-1,540.6 & 246,500 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix}0\\-1.6227\\ 0\\0 \end{matrix} \right]\times 10^{-3} + \left[\begin{matrix} 15 \\600 \\ 15\\-600 \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix}\underset{………}{12.5k} \\ \underset{………}{200k-in} \\{17.5k} \\-800 k-in \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 6 \\2 \\ 7 \\8 \end{matrix}\)

These member end forces are shown in Fig. 5.17(d). To check our calculations, we apply the equilibrium equations:

\(+\uparrow \sum{F_{y}}=0 12.5-0.125(240)+17.5=0\)

\(+\hookrightarrow \sum{M_{3}}=0 200-0.125(240)(120)-800+17.5(240) = 0\)

Next, by using the code numbers (6, 2, 7, 8) for member 3, we store the relevant elements
of \(Q_{3}\) in their proper positions in R.

Support Reactions: The completed reaction vector R is shown in Fig. 5.17(e), and the support reactions are depicted on a line diagram of the structure in Fig. 5.17(f ).

Equilibrium Check: Finally, applying the equilibrium equations to the free body of the entire beam (Fig. 5.17(f )), we write

\(+\uparrow \sum{F_{y}}=0\)
\(18.125 − 30 + 12.986 + 11.389 − 0.125(240) + 17.5 = 0\)

\(+\hookrightarrow \sum{M_{1}}=0\)
\(1,150 − 30(120) + 12.986(240) + 11.389(420) − 0.125(240)(540)\)
\(+ 17.5(660) − 800 = 0.02 \approx 0\)
