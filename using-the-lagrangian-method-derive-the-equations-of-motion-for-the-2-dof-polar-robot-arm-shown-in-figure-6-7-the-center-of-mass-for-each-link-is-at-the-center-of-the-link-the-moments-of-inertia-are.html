Guest Question.

Using the Lagrangian method, derive the equations of motion for the 2-DOF polar robot arm shown in Figure 6.7. The center of mass for each link is at the center of the link. The moments of inertia are \( I_{1}\) and \( I_{2}\) .

Admin
Solution

Note that in this case, we have a prismatic joint. We denote the distance from the base of the robot to the center of the outer arm as r, which serves as one of the two variables. The total length of the arm is \(r + (l_{2}/2)\) . As before, we derive the Lagrangian and take the proper derivatives as follows:

\(K = K_{1} + K_{2}\)

 

\(K_{1} = \frac{1}{2} I_{1,A} \dot{\theta } ^{2} =\frac{1}{2} \left(\frac{1}{3}m_{1}l^{2}_{1} \dot{\theta }^{2} \right) =\frac{1}{6} m_{1} l^{2}_{1} \dot{\theta }^{2}\)

 

\(x_{D} =rC\theta \rightarrow \dot{x}_{D}=\dot{r} C\theta -r\dot{\theta }S\theta\)

 

\(y_{D} =rS\theta \rightarrow \dot{y}_{D}=\dot{r} S\theta +r\dot{\theta }C\theta\)

 

and

\(v^{2}_{D} =\dot{r}^{2} +r^{2} \dot{\theta }^{2}\)

 

\(K_{2} = \frac{1}{2} I_{2,D}\dot{\theta }^{2} +\frac{1}{2} m_{2} v^{2}_{D}=\frac{1}{2} \left(\frac{1}{12}m_{2}l^{2}_{2}\dot{\theta }^{2} \right)+\frac{1}{2} m_{2}(\dot{r}^{2}+r^{2}\dot{\theta }^{2})\)

 

\(K=\left(\frac{1}{6} m_{1}l^{2}_{1}+\frac{1}{24} m_{2}l^{2}_{2} +\frac{1}{2}m_{2} r^{2}\right) \dot{\theta }^{2} +\frac{1}{2}m_{ 2} \dot{r}^{2}\)

 

\(P= m_{1} g \frac{l_{1} }{2} S\theta +m_{2}grS\theta\)

 

\(L= \left(\frac{1}{6}m_{1} l^{2}_{1}+\frac{1}{24} m_{2} l^{2}_{2} +\frac{1}{2} m_{2} r^{2} \right) \dot{\theta }^{2}+\frac{1}{2} m_{2} \dot r^{2}- \left(m_{1}g \frac{l_{1}}{2} +m_{2}gr\right) S\theta\)

 

\(\frac{d}{dt} \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\theta } } =\left(\frac{1}{3}m_{1} l^{2}_{1}+\frac{1}{12} m_{2} l^{2}_{2}+m_{2} r^{2} \right)\ddot{\theta }+2m_{2}\dot{r} \dot{\theta }\)

 

\(\frac{\partial L}{\partial{\theta } } =-\left( m_{1} g \frac{l_{1} }{2} +m_{2} gr \right)C\theta\)

 

\(T=\left(\frac{1}{3}m_{1} l^{2}_{1}+\frac{1}{12} m_{2} l^{2}_{2}+m_{2} r^{2} \right)\ddot{\theta }+2m_{2}r\dot{r} \dot{\theta }+\left( m_{1} g \frac{l_{1} }{2} +m_{2} gr \right)C\theta\) (6.14)

 

\(\frac{d}{dt} \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{r} } =m_{2} \ddot{r}\)

 

\(\frac{\partial L}{\partial {r} } =m_{2}r\dot{\theta }^{2} -m_{2} gS\theta\)

 

\(F=m_{2} \ddot{r} -m_{2} r\dot{\theta }^{2}+m_{2}gS\theta\) (6.15)

 

Writing these two equations in matrix form, we get:

\(\left [ \begin{matrix}T \\ F \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} \left(\frac{1}{3}m_{1} l^{2}_{1}+\frac{1}{12} m_{2} l^{2}_{2}+m_{2} r^{2} \right) & 0 \\ 0 & m_{2} \end{matrix} \right ]\left [ \begin{matrix} \ddot{\theta } \\ \ddot{r} \end{matrix} \right ] +\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & 0 \\ -m_{2}r & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} \dot{\theta }^{2} \\ \dot{r }^{2} \end{matrix} \right ]\)

 

\(+\left [ \begin{matrix} m_{2}r & m_{2}r \\0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ]\left [ \begin{matrix} \dot{r } \dot{\theta } \\ \dot{\theta } \dot{r } \end{matrix} \right ] +\left [ \begin{matrix}\left( m_{1} g(l_{1}/{2}) +m_{2} gr \right)C\theta \\m_{2} gS\theta \end{matrix} \right ]\) (6.16)
