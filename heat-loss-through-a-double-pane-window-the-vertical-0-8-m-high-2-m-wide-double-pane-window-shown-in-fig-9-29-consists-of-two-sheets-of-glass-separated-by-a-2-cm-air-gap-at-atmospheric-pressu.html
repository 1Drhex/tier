Heat Loss through a Double-Pane Window

The vertical 0.8-m-high, 2-m-wide double-pane window shown in Fig. 9–29 consists of two sheets of glass separated by a 2-cm air gap at atmospheric pressure. If the glass surface temperatures across the air gap are measured to be \( 12^{\circ}C \) and \( 2^{\circ}C \), determine the rate of heat transfer through the window.

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION Two glasses of a double-pane window are maintained at specified temperatures. The rate of heat transfer through the window is to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Steady operating conditions exist. 2 Air is an ideal gas. 3 Radiation heat transfer is not considered.

Properties The properties of air at the average temperature of \( T_{ave} = \left ( T_{1} + T_{2 } \right )/2 = \left ( 12 + 2 \right )/2 = 7^{\circ}C \) and 1 atm pressure are (Table A-15)

\( k = 0.02416 W/m \cdot ^{\circ}C Pr = 0.7344\)

 

\( \nu = 1.399 \times 10^{-5} m^{2}/s \beta = \frac{1}{T_{ave}} = \frac{1}{280 K}\)

 

Analysis We have a rectangular enclosure filled with air. The characteristic length in this case is the distance between the two glasses, L = 0.02 m. Then the Rayleigh number becomes

\( Ra_{L} = \frac{g \beta \left ( T_{1} – T_{2} \right )L^{3}}{\nu ^{2}} \)

 

\( = \frac{\left ( 9.81 m/s^{2} \right ) \left [ 1/\left ( 280 K \right ) \right ]\left ( 12 – 2 K \right )\left ( 0.02 m \right )^{3} }{\left ( 1.399 \times 10^{-5} m^{2}/s \right )^{2}}\left (0.7344 \right) = 1.051 \times 10^{4}\)

 

The aspect ratio of the geometry is H/L = 0.8/0.02 = 40. Then the Nusselt number in this case can be determined from Eq. to be

\( Nu = 0.42 Ra_{L}^{1/4} Pr^{0.012} \left ( \frac{H}{L} \right )^{-0.3}\)

 

\( = 0.42 \left ( 1.051 \times 10^{4} \right )^{1/4} \left ( 0.7344 \right )^{0.012} \left ( \frac{0.8}{0.02} \right )^{- 0.3} = 1.401\)

 

Then,

\( A_{s} = H \times W = \left ( 0.8 m \right )\left ( 2 m \right ) = 1.6 m^{2} \)

 

and

\( \dot{Q} = h A_{s} \left ( T_{1} – T_{2} \right ) = h Nu A_{s} \frac{T_{1} – T_{2}}{L} \)

 

\( = \left ( 0.02416 W/m \cdot ^{\circ}C \right ) \left ( 1.401 \right )\left ( 1.6 m^{2} \right ) \frac{\left ( 12 – 2 \right )^{\circ}C}{0.02 m} = 27.1 W \)

 

Therefore, heat will be lost through the window at a rate of 27.1 W.

 

Discussion Recall that a Nusselt number of Nu = 1 for an enclosure corresponds to pure conduction heat transfer through the enclosure. The air in the enclosure in this case remains still, and no natural convection currents occur in the enclosure. The Nusselt number in our case is 1.32, which indicates that heat transfer through the enclosure is 1.32 times that by pure conduction. The increase in heat transfer is due to the natural convection currents that develop in the enclosure.
