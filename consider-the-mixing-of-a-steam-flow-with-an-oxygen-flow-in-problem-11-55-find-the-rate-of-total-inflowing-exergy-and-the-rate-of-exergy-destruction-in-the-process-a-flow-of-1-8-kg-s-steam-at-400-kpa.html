Guest Question.

Consider the mixing of a steam flow with an oxygen flow in . Find the rate of total inflowing exergy and the rate of exergy destruction in the process.
A flow of 1.8 kg/s steam at 400 kPa, 400째C is mixed with 3.2 kg/s oxygen at 400 kPa, 400 K in a steady flow mixing-chamber without any heat transfer. Find the exit temperature and the rate of entropy generation.

Admin
Solution

Exergy Flow: \(\dot{\Phi}_{\text {in }}=\dot{ m } \psi_{\text {in }}=\dot{ m }_{ H 2 O } \psi_{1}+\dot{ m }_{ O 2} \psi_{2}\)

\(\begin{aligned}\psi_{1} &= h _{1}- h _{ o }- T _{o}\left( s _{1}- s _{ o }\right) \\&= C _{ P H 2 O }\left( T _{1}- T _{ o }\right)- T _{ o }\left[ C _{ P H 2 O } \ln \left( T _{1} / T _{ o }\right)- R \ln \left( P _{1} / P _{ o }\right)\right] \\&=1.872(400-25)-298.15\left[1.872 \ln \frac{673.15}{298.15}-0.4615 \ln \frac{400}{100}\right] \\&=702-298.15(1.5245-0.63978)=438.2 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\psi_{2} &= h _{2}- h _{ o }- T _{ o }\left( s _{2}- s _{ o }\right) \\&= C _{ P O 2}\left( T _{2}- T _{ o }\right)- T _{ o }\left[ C _{ P O 2} \ln \left( T _{2} / T _{ o }\right)- R \ln \left( P _{2} / P _{ o }\right)\right] \\&=0.922(126.85-25)-298.15\left[0.922 \ln \frac{400}{298.15}-0.2598 \ln \frac{400}{100}\right] \\&=93.906-298.15(0.27095-0.36016)=120.5 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\dot{\Phi}_{\text {in }}=\dot{ m }_{ H 2 O } \psi_{1}+\dot{ m }_{ O 2} \psi_{2}=1.8438 .2+3.2120 .5= 1 1 7 4 . 4 ~ k W\)

 

C.V. Mixing chamber, steady flow, no work, no heat transfer. To do the entropies we need the mole fractions.

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ n }_{ H 2 O }=& \frac{\dot{ m }_{ H 2 O }}{ M _{ H 2 O }}=\frac{1.8}{18.015}=0.1 kmol / s ; \quad \dot{ n }_{ O 2}=\frac{\dot{ m }_{ O 2}}{ M _{ O 2}}=\frac{3.2}{31.999}=0.1 kmol / s \\& y _{ H 2 O }= y _{ O 2}=0.5\end{aligned}\)

Energy Eq.: \(\dot{ m }_{ H 2 O } h _{1}+\dot{ m }_{ O 2} h _{2}=\dot{ m }_{ H 2 O } h _{3 H 2 O }+\dot{ m }_{ O 2} h _{3 O 2} \)

Entropy Eq.: \(\dot{ m }_{ H 2 O } s _{1}+\dot{ m }_{ O 2} s _{2}+\dot{ S }_{ gen }=\dot{ m }_{ H 2 O } s _{3 H 2 O }+\dot{ m }_{ O 2} s _{3 O 2} \)

Solve for T from the energy equation

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{ m }_{ H 2 O }\left( h _{3 H 2 O }- h _{1}\right)+\dot{ m }_{ O 2}\left( h _{3 O 2} – h _{2}\right)=0 \\&\dot{ m }_{ H 2O} C _{ P H 2 O }\left( T _{3}- T _{1}\right)+\dot{ m }_{ O 2} C _{ P O 2}\left( T _{3}- T _{2}\right)=0 \\&1.8 \times 1.872\left( T _{3}-400-273.15\right)+3.2 \times 0.922\left( T _{3}-400\right)=0\end{aligned}\)

 

\(T _{3}= 5 4 5 . 6 K\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ S }_{ gen }=& \dot{ m }_{ H 2 O }\left( s _{3 H 2 O }- s _{1}\right)+\dot{ m }_{ O 2}\left( s _{3 O2} – s _{2}\right) \\=& \dot{ m }_{ H 2 O }\left[ C _{ P H 2 O } \ln \frac{ T _{3}}{ T _{1}}- R \ln y _{ H 2 O }\right]+\dot{ m }_{ O 2}\left[ C _{ P O 2} \ln \frac{ T _{3}}{ T _{2}}- R \ln y _{ O 2}\right] \\=& 1.8\left[1.872 \ln \frac{545.6}{673.15}-0.4615 \ln 0.5\right] \\& \quad+3.2\left[0.922 \ln \frac{545.6}{400}-0.2598 \ln 0.5\right] \\=&-0.132+1.492=1.36 k W / K\end{aligned}\)

 

The exergy destruction is proportional to the entropy generation

\(\dot{\Phi}_{\text {in }}= T _{ o } \dot{ S }_{ gen }=298.15 \times 1.36= 4 0 5 . 5 ~ k W\)
