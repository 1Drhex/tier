Guest Question.

The controlling force \(\left(F_{ C }\right)\) in newtons and the radius of rotation (r) in metres for a spring controlled governor is given by the expression
\(F_{ C }=2800 r -76\)
The mass of the ball is 5 kg and the extreme radii of rotation of the balls are 100 mm and 175 mm. Find the maximum and minimum speeds of equilibrium. If the friction of the governor mechanism is equivalent to a force of 5 N at each ball, find the coefficient of insensitiveness of the governor at the extreme radii.

Admin
Solution

Given : m = 5 kg ; \(r_{1}=100 mm =0.1 m ; r_{2}=175 mm =0.175 m\)

Maximum and minimum speeds of equilibrium

Let \(N_{2} \text { and } N_{1}\) = Maximum and minimum speeds of equilibrium respectively.

The controlling force is given by the expression,

\(F_{C}=2800 r-76\)

\(\therefore \) Controlling force at the minimum radius of rotation (i.e. at \(r_{1}\) = 0.1 m),

\(F_{ Cl }=2800 \times 0.1-76=204 N\)

and controlling force at the maximum radius of rotation (i.e. at \(r_{2}\) = 0.175 m),

\(F_{ C 2}=2800 \times 0.175-76=414 N\)

We know that \(F_{ Cl }=m\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2} r_{1}=m\left(\frac{2 \pi N_{1}}{60}\right)^{2} r_{1}\)

or \(204=5\left(\frac{2 \pi N_{1}}{60}\right)^{2} 0.1=0.0055\left(N_{1}\right)^{2}\)

\(\therefore \) \(\left(N_{1}\right)^{2}=204 / 0.0055=37091 \quad \text { or } \quad N_{1}=192.6 \text { r.p.m. }\)

Similarly \(F_{ C 2}=m\left(\omega_{2}\right)^{2} r_{2}=m\left(\frac{2 \pi N_{2}}{60}\right)^{2} r_{2}\)

or \(414=5\left(\frac{2 \pi N_{2}}{60}\right)^{2} 0.175=0.0096\left(N_{2}\right)^{2}\)

\(\therefore \) \(\left(N_{2}\right)^{2}=414 / 0.0096=43125 \quad \text { or } \quad N_{2}=207.6 \text { r.p.m. }\)

 

Coefficient of insensitiveness

Let \(N_{1}^{\prime} \text { and } N_{2}^{\prime}\) = Minimum and maximum speeds of the governor considering friction.

We know that frictional force at each ball

= 5 N …(Given)

\(\therefore \) \(F_{ Cl }-F=m\left(\omega_{1}^{\prime}\right)^{2} r_{1}=m\left(\frac{2 \pi N_{1}^{\prime}}{60}\right)^{2} r_{1}\)

 

\(204-5=5\left(\frac{2 \pi N_{1}^{\prime}}{60}\right)^{2} 0.1=0.0055\left(N_{1}^{\prime}\right)^{2}\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(\left(N_{1}^{\prime}\right)^{2}=\frac{204-5}{0.0055}=36182 \text { or } N_{1}^{\prime}=190.2 \text { r.p.m. }\)

 

Similarly \(F_{ C 2}+F=m\left(\omega_{2}^{\prime}\right)^{2} r_{2}=m\left(\frac{2 \pi N_{2}^{\prime}}{60}\right)^{2} r_{2}\)

 

\(414+5=5\left(\frac{2 \pi N_{2}^{\prime}}{60}\right)^{2} 0.175=0.0096\left(N_{2}^{\prime}\right)^{2}\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(\left(N_{2}^{\prime}\right)^{2}=\frac{414+5}{0.0096}=43646 \text { or } N_{2}^{\prime}=209 \text { r.p.m. }\)

 

We know that coefficient of insensitiveness

\(=\frac{2\left(N_{2}^{\prime}-N_{1}^{\prime}\right)}{N_{2}^{\prime}+N_{1}^{\prime}}=\frac{2(209-190.2)}{209+190.2}=0.094 \text { or } 9.4 \%\)
