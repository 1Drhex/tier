Guest Question.

Warning: Attempt this problem only if you are familiar with vector calculus. Define the (three-dimensional) probability current by generalization of :

\( J \equiv \frac{i \hbar}{2 m}\left(\Psi \nabla \Psi^{*}-\Psi^{*} \nabla \Psi\right) \). (4.220).

(a) Show that J satisfies the continuity equation

\( \nabla \cdot J =-\frac{\partial}{\partial t}|\Psi|^{2} \), (4.221)

which expresses local conservation of probability. It follows (from the divergence theorem) that

\( \oint_{ S } J \cdot d a =-\frac{d}{d t} \int_{ V }|\Psi|^{2} d^{3} r \) (4.222).

where V is a (fixed) volume and S is its boundary surface. In words: The flow of probability out through the surface is equal to the decrease in probability of finding the particle in the volume.

(b) Find J for hydrogen in the state n=2, l = 1 ,m=1. Answer:

\( \frac{\hbar}{64 \pi m a^{5}} r e^{-r / a} \sin \theta \hat{\phi} \).

(c) If we interpret m J as the flow of mass, the angular momentum is

\( L =m \int( r \times J ) d^{3} r \).

Use this to calculate \( L_z \) for the state \( ψ_{211} \), and comment on the result.

Admin
Solution

(a)

\( \nabla \cdot J =\frac{i \hbar}{2 m}\left[\nabla \Psi \cdot \nabla \Psi^{*}+\Psi\left(\nabla^{2} \Psi^{*}\right)-\nabla \Psi^{*} \cdot \nabla \Psi-\Psi^{*}\left(\nabla^{2} \Psi\right)\right]=\frac{i \hbar}{2 m}\left[\Psi\left(\nabla^{2} \Psi^{*}\right)-\Psi^{*}\left(\nabla^{2} \Psi\right)\right] \).

But the Schrödinger equation says \( i \hbar \frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial t}=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \nabla^{2} \Psi+V \Psi \), so

\( \nabla^{2} \Psi=\frac{2 m}{\hbar^{2}}\left(V \Psi-i \hbar \frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial t}\right), \quad \nabla^{2} \Psi^{*}=\frac{2 m}{\hbar^{2}}\left(V \Psi^{*}+i \hbar \frac{\partial \Psi^{*}}{\partial t}\right) \). Therefore

\( \nabla \cdot J =\frac{i \hbar}{2 m} \frac{2 m}{\hbar^{2}}\left[\Psi\left(V \Psi^{*}+i \hbar \frac{\partial \Psi^{*}}{\partial t}\right)-\Psi^{*}\left(V \Psi-i \hbar \frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial t}\right)\right] \).

\( =\frac{i}{\hbar} i \hbar\left(\Psi \frac{\partial \Psi^{*}}{\partial t}+\Psi^{*} \frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial t}\right)=-\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\left(\Psi^{*} \Psi\right)=-\frac{\partial}{\partial t}|\Psi|^{2} \).

(b) From (b), \( \Psi_{211}=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi a}} \frac{1}{8 a^{2}} r e^{-r / 2 a} \sin \theta e^{i \phi} e^{-i E_{2} t / \hbar} \), In spherical coordinates,

\( \nabla \Psi=\frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial r} \hat{r}+\frac{1}{r} \frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial \theta} \hat{\theta}+\frac{1}{r \sin \theta} \frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial \phi} \hat{\phi} \), so

\( \nabla \Psi_{211}=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi a}} \frac{1}{8 a^{2}}\left[\left(1-\frac{r}{2 a}\right) e^{-r / 2 a} \sin \theta e^{i \phi} e^{-i E_{2} t / \hbar} \hat{r}+\frac{1}{r} r e^{-r / 2 a} \cos \theta e^{i \phi} e^{-i E_{2} t / \hbar} \hat{\theta}\right. \)

\( \left.+\frac{1}{r \sin \theta} r e^{-r / 2 a} \sin \theta i e^{i \phi} e^{-i E_{2} t / \hbar} \hat{\phi}\right]=\left[\left(1-\frac{r}{2 a}\right) \hat{r}+\cot \theta \hat{\theta}+\frac{i}{\sin \theta} \hat{\phi}\right] \frac{1}{r} \Psi_{211} \).

Therefore

\( J =\frac{i \hbar}{2 m}\left[\left(1-\frac{r}{2 a}\right) \hat{r}+\cot \theta \hat{\theta}-\frac{i}{\sin \theta} \hat{\phi}-\left(1-\frac{r}{2 a}\right) \hat{r}-\cot \theta \hat{\theta}-\frac{i}{\sin \theta} \hat{\phi}\right] \frac{1}{r}\left|\Psi_{211}\right|^{2} \).

\( =\frac{i \hbar}{2 m} \frac{(-2 i)}{r \sin \theta}\left|\Psi_{211}\right|^{2} \hat{\phi}=\frac{\hbar}{m} \frac{1}{\pi a} \frac{1}{64 a^{4}} \frac{r^{2} e^{-r / a} \sin ^{2} \theta}{r \sin \theta} \hat{\phi}=\sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{64 \pi m a^{5}} r e^{-r / a} \sin \theta \hat{\phi}} \).

(c) \( \text { Now } r \times J =\frac{\hbar}{64 \pi m a^{5}} r^{2} e^{-r / a} \sin \theta(\hat{r} \times \hat{\phi}), \text { while }(\hat{r} \times \hat{\phi})=-\hat{\theta} \text { and } \hat{z} \cdot \hat{\theta}=-\sin \theta, \text { so } \)

\( r \times J _{z}=\frac{\hbar}{64 \pi m a^{5}} r^{2} e^{-r / a} \sin ^{2} \theta \), and hence

\( L_{z}=m \frac{\hbar}{64 \pi m a^{5}} \int\left(r^{2} e^{-r / a} \sin ^{2} \theta\right) r^{2} \sin \theta d r d \theta d \phi \).

\( =\frac{\hbar}{64 \pi a^{5}} \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{4} e^{-r / a} d r \int_{0}^{\pi} \sin ^{3} \theta d \theta \int_{0}^{2 \pi} d \phi=\frac{\hbar}{64 \pi a^{5}}\left(4 ! a^{5}\right)\left(\frac{4}{3}\right)(2 \pi)=\hbar \).

as it should be, since (Eq. 4.133) \( L_{z}=\hbar m, \text { and } m=1 \) for this state.

\( H \psi=E \psi, \quad L^{2} \psi=\hbar^{2} \ell(\ell+1) \psi, \quad L_{z} \psi=\hbar m \psi \) (4.133).
