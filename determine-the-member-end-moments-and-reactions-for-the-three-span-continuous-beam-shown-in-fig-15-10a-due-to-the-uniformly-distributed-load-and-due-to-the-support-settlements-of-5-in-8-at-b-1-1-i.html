Guest Question.

Determine the member end moments and reactions for the three-span continuous beam shown in Fig. \( 15.10 (\mathrm{a})\) due to the uniformly distributed load and due to the support settlements of \( \frac{5}{8}\) in. at \( B, 1 \frac{1}{2}\) in. at \( C\) , and \( \frac{3}{4}\) in. at \( D\) . Use the slope-deflection method.

Admin
Solution

Degrees of Freedom. Although all four joints of the beam are free to rotate, we will eliminate the rotations of the simple supports at the ends \( A\) and \( D\) from the analysis by using the modified slope-deflection equations for members \( A B\) and \( C D\) , respectively. Thus, the analysis will involve only two unknown joint rotations, \( \theta_{B}\) and \( \theta_{C}\) .

Fixed-End Moments.

\( \begin{array}{l} \mathrm{FEM}_{A B}=\mathrm{FEM}_{B C}=\mathrm{FEM}_{C D}=\frac{2(30)^{2}}{12}=150 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowleft \quad \text { or } \quad+150 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \\ \mathrm{FEM}_{B A}=\mathrm{FEM}_{C B}=\mathrm{FEM}_{D C}=150 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowright \quad \text { or } \quad-150 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \end{array} \)

Chord Rotations. The specified support settlements are depicted in Fig. 15.10(b) using an exaggerated scale. The inclined dashed lines in this figure indicate the chords (not the elastic curves) of the members in the deformed positions. It can be seen from this figure that since support \( A\) does not settle but support \( B\) settles by \( \frac{5}{8} \mathrm{in}\) , the relative settlement between the two ends of member \( A B\) is \( \frac{5}{8}\) in. \( =0.0521 \mathrm{ft}\) . Because the length of member \( A B\) is \( 30 \mathrm{ft}\) , the rotation of the chord of member \( A B\) is

\( \psi_{A B}=-\frac{0.0521}{30}=-0.00174 \)

in which the negative sign has been assigned to the value of \( \psi_{A B}\) to indicate that its direction is clockwise, as shown in Fig. 15.10(b). The chord rotation for member \( B C\) can be computed in a similar manner by using the settlement of supports \( B\) and \( C\) . From Fig. 15.10(b), we observe that the relative settlement between the ends of member \( B C\) is \( 1 \frac{1}{2}\) in. \( -\frac{5}{8}\) in. \( =0.875 \mathrm{in} .=0.0729 \mathrm{ft}\) , and so

\( \psi_{B C}=-\frac{0.0729}{30}=-0.00243 \)

Similarly, the chord rotation for member \( C D\) is

\( \psi_{C D}=\frac{1.5-0.75}{(12)(30)}=0.00208 \)

Slope-Deflection Equations.

\( \begin{aligned}M_{A B} &=0 \\\end{aligned} \)

\( \begin{aligned} M_{B A} &=\frac{3 E I}{30}\left(\theta_{B}+0.00174\right)-225=0.1 E I \theta_{B}+0.000174 E I-225 \\\end{aligned} \) (1)

\( \begin{aligned} M_{B C} &=\frac{2 E I}{30}\left[2 \theta_{B}+\theta_{C}-3(-0.00243)\right]+150 \\ &=0.133 E I \theta_{B}+0.0667 E I \theta_{C}+0.000486 E I+150 \\\end{aligned} \) (2)

\( \begin{aligned} M_{C B} &=\frac{2 E I}{30}\left[2 \theta_{C}+\theta_{B}-3(-0.00243)\right]-150 \\ &=0.0667 E I \theta_{B}+0.133 E I \theta_{C}+0.000486 E I-150 \\\end{aligned} \) (3)

\( \begin{aligned} M_{C D} &=\frac{3 E I}{30}\left(\theta_{C}-0.00208\right)+225=0.1 E I \theta_{C}-0.000208 E I+225 \\\end{aligned} \) (4)

\( \begin{aligned}M_{D C} &=0 \end{aligned} \)

Equilibrium Equations. See Fig. 15.10(c).

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{B A}+M_{B C}=0 \\\end{array} \) (5)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{C B}+M_{C D}=0 \end{array} \) (6)

Joint Rotations. By substituting the slope-deflection equations (Eqs. (1) through (4)) into the equilibrium equations (Eqs. (5) and (6)), we obtain

\( \begin{array}{l} 0.233 E I \theta_{B}+0.0667 E I \theta_{C}=-0.00066 E I+75 \\ 0.0667 E I \theta_{B}+0.233 E I \theta_{C}=-0.000278 E I-75 \end{array} \)

Substituting \( E I=(29,000)(7,800) /(12)^{2} \mathrm{k}\) -ft \( ^{2}\) into the right sides of the previous equations yields

\( \begin{array}{l} 0.233 E I \theta_{B}+0.0667 E I \theta_{C}=-961.75 \\\end{array} \) (7)

\( \begin{array}{l} 0.0667 E I \theta_{B}+0.233 E I \theta_{C}=-511.69 \end{array} \) (8)

By solving Eqs. (7) and (8) simultaneously, we determine the values of \( E I \theta_{B}\) and \( E I \theta_{C}\) to be

\( \begin{array}{l} E I \theta_{B}=-3,811.3 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}^{2} \\ E I \theta_{C}=-1,105 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}^{2} \end{array} \)

Member End Moments. To compute the member end moments, we substitute the numerical values of \( E I \theta_{B}\) and \( E I \theta_{C}\) back into the slope-deflection equations (Eqs. (1) through (4)) to obtain

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{B A}=-332.8 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \quad \text { or } \quad 332.8 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowright\\ M_{B C}=332.8 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowleft\\ M_{C B}=212.2 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowleft\\ M_{C D}=-212.2 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft} \quad \text { or } \quad 212.2 \mathrm{k}-\mathrm{ft}\curvearrowright \end{array} \)

Member End Shears and Support Reactions. See Fig. 15.10(d) and (e).

Equilibrium Check. The equilibrium equations check.

We previously analyzed the continuous beam considered here in by the method of consistent deformations. Theoretically, the slope-deflection method and the method of consistent deformations should yield identical results for a given structure. The small differences between the results determined here and those obtained in are due to the roundoff errors.
