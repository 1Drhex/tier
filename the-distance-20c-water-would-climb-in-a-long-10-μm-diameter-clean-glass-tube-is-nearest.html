Guest Question.

The distance 20°C water would climb in a long 10- μm-diameter, clean glass tube is nearest:
(A) 50 cm
(B) 100 cm
(C) 200 cm
(D) 300 cm

Admin
Solution

(D) Using Eqn. (1.5.16), \(h=\frac{4\sigma \cos \beta }{\gamma D} \) , β = 0 (for clean glass tube), and σ = 0.0736 N/m for water (Table B.1 in Appendix B) we write:

\(h=\frac{4\sigma \cos \beta }{\gamma g D} =\frac{4 \times 0.0736 N/m\times 1}{1000 kg/m^3\times 9.81 m/s^2\times 10\times 10^{-6} m} =3 m or \underline{300cm} \)

Where we used \(N= kg\times m/s^2 \)
