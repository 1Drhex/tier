Guest Question.

Air at 25 C flows over a sphere at a velocity of 2 m/s. The sphere has a diameter of 20 cm and internal heat generation at a rate of 5000 \(W /m^{3}\) . The thermal conductivity of the sphere is 25 W/m C.

(a) What is the surface temperature of the sphere?

(b) What is the center temperature of the sphere?

Admin
Solution

The first thing to do is to determine the convective coefficient h at the surface of the sphere. An appropriate equation for forced convection over a sphere is Equation (6.111):

\(Nu=\frac{hD}{k} =2+\left(0.4Re^{1/2}_{D} +0.06Re^{2/3}_{D} \right) Pr^{0.4} \left(\frac{\mu }{\mu _{s} } \right) ^{1/4}\) (6.111)

 

\(Nu=\frac{hD}{k} =2+\left(0.4Re^{1/2}_{D} +0.06Re^{2/3}_{D} \right) Pr^{0.4} \left(\frac{\mu }{\mu _{s} } \right) ^{1/4}\) (12.68)

 

The properties in this equation, except for \(\mu _{s}\) , are taken at the fluid temperature, which is 25 C. For air at 25 C ,

k=0.026W/mC , \(v =1.55\times 10^{-5} m^{2}/s\) , Pr= 0.729 , and \(\mu =1.84\times 10^{-5} kg/ms\)

\(R_{eD} =\frac{\mu _{\infty } D}{v} =\frac{(2)(0.2)}{1.55\times 10^{-5} } =25800\)

 

We will assume that \(\mu _{s} \approx \mu\) . Then, Eq. (12.68) is

 

\(Nu=\frac{hD}{k} =2+\left(0.4Re^{1/2}_{D} +0.06Re^{2/3}_{D} \right) Pr^{0.4}\)

 

Putting values into this equation, we get Nu =104.78 and

\(h=\frac{k}{D} Nu=\frac{0.026}{0.2}(104.78)=13.6W/m^{2} C\)

 

The surface temperature of the sphere is given by Eq. (12.55):

\(T(r_{0}) =T_{0}= T_{\infty } +\frac{r_{0} }{3h} q_{gen}\) (12.55)

 

\(T(r_{0}) =25+\frac{0.1}{(3)(13.6)}(5000) =37.3C\)

 

The center temperature of the sphere is given by Eq (12.56):

\(T_{max} =T_{\infty } +q_{gen} \left(\frac{r^{2}_{0} }{6k}+\frac{r_{0} }{3h} \right)\) (12.56)

 

\(T_{max} =25+(5000)\left(\frac{\left(0.1\right) ^{2} }{6(25)} +\frac{0.1}{(3)(13.6)} \right) =37.6C\)

 

(a) The surface temperature of the sphere is 37.3 C.

(b) The center temperature of the sphere is 37.6 C.

Note: Our assumption that \(\mu _{s} \approx \mu\) was appropriate. There is only a 12 C difference between fluid and surface temperatures and \(\mu /\mu _{s} =0.99 .\)
