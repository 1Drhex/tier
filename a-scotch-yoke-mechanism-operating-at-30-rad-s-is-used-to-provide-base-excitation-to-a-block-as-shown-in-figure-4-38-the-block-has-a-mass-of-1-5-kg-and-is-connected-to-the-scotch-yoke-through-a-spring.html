Guest Question.

A Scotch yoke mechanism operating at 30 rad/s is used to provide base excitation to a block as shown in Figure 4.38. The block has a mass of 1.5 kg and is connected to the Scotch yoke through a spring of stiffness 500 N/m. The coefficient of friction between the block and the surface is 0.13. Approximate the steady-state response of the block.

Admin
Solution

The differential equation governing the motion of the block is

\( m\ddot{x}+kx= kl \sin \omega t\mp \mu mg\) (a)

The amplitude of the excitation is kl. Thus

\( \iota =\frac{\mu mg}{kl}=\frac{(0.13)(1.5 kg)(9.81 m/s^{2})}{(500 N/m)(0.1m)}=0.038\) (b)

The systemâ€™s natural frequency and frequency ratio are

\( \omega _{n}=\sqrt{\frac{k}{m} }=18.26 rad/s\) (c )

The Coulomb damping magnification factor is

\( M_{c}(1.64,0.038)=\sqrt{\frac{1-\left[\frac{4(0.038)}{2\pi} \right]^{2} }{\left[1- (1.64)^{2}\right]^{2} } }= 0.587\) (d)

The steady-state response is calculated from

\( \frac{m\omega _{n}^{2}X}{kl} =\frac{X}{l}=M_{c}(1.64, 0.038)\) (e )

\( X=(0.1 m)(0.587)=0.0588m\) (f)

The phase angle is calculated from Equation (4.185b) as

\( \phi _{c}= -\tan^{-1}\left[\frac{\frac{4(0.034)}{\pi} }{1-\left(\frac{4(0.038)}{\pi} \right)^{2} } \right]= -0.0488\) (g)

\( \phi _{c}=\tan^{-1}\left[\frac{\frac{4\iota }{\pi} }{\sqrt{1-\left(\frac{4\iota }{\pi} \right)^{2} } } \right] r\gt 1\) (4.185b)

The response of the system is

\( x(t)= 0.0588 \sin (18.26t+0.0488)m\) (h)
