A Reversible Absorption Refrigerator

A reversible absorption refrigerator consists of a reversible heat engine and a reversible refrigerator (Fig. 11–25). The system removes heat from a cooled space at –15°C at a rate of 70 kW. The refrigerator operates in an environment at 25°C. If the heat is supplied to the cycle by condensing saturated steam at 150°C, determine (a) the rate at which the steam condenses and (b) the power input to the reversible refrigerator. (c) If the COP of an actual absorption chiller at the same temperature limits has a COP of 0.8, determine the second-law efficiency of this chiller.

Admin
Solution

A reversible absorption refrigerator consists of a reversible heat engine and a reversible refrigerator. The rate at which the steam condenses, the power input to the reversible refrigerator, and the second-law efficiency of an actual chiller are to be determined.

Properties The enthalpy of vaporization of water at 150°C is \(h_{fg} = 2113.8 kJ / kg\) .

Analysis (a) The thermal efficiency of the reversible heat engine is

\(\eta_{\text {th,rev }}=1-\frac{T_{0}}{T_{s}}=1-\frac{(25+273.15) \mathrm{K}}{(150+273.15) \mathrm{K}}=0.2954\)

The COP of the reversible refrigerator is

\(\mathrm{COP}_{\mathrm{R}, \mathrm{rev}}=\frac{T_{L}}{T_{0}-T_{L}}=\frac{(-15+273.15) \mathrm{K}}{(25+273.15 \mathrm{~K})-(-15+273.15 \mathrm{~K})}=6.454\)

The COP of the reversible absorption refrigerator is

\(\mathrm{COP}_{\mathrm{abs}, \mathrm{rev}}=\eta_{\mathrm{th}, \mathrm{rev}} \mathrm{COP}_{\mathrm{R}, \mathrm{rev}}=(0.2954)(6.454)=1.906\)

The heat input to the reversible heat engine is

\(\dot{Q}_{\mathrm{in}}=\frac{\dot{Q}_{L}}{\mathrm{COP}_{\mathrm{abs}, \mathrm{rev}}}=\frac{70 \mathrm{~kW}}{1.906}=36.72 \mathrm{~kW}\)

Then, the rate at which the steam condenses becomes

\(\dot{m}_{s}=\frac{\dot{Q}_{\text {in }}}{h_{f g}}=\frac{36.72 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{s}}{2113.8 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}}=\mathbf{0 . 0 1 7 4} \mathrm{kg} / \mathrm{s}\)

(b) The power input to the refrigerator is equal to the power output from the heat engine

\(\dot{W}_{\mathrm{in}, \mathrm{R}}=\dot{W}_{\mathrm{out}, \mathrm{HE}}=\eta_{\mathrm{th}, \mathrm{rev}} \dot{Q}_{\mathrm{in}}=(0.2954)(36.72 \mathrm{~kW})=10.9 \mathrm{k} \mathrm{W}\)

(c) The second-law efficiency of an actual absorption chiller with a COP value of \(0.8\) is

\(\eta_{\mathrm{II}}=\frac{\mathrm{COP}_{\text {actual }}}{\mathrm{COP}_{\mathrm{abs}, \mathrm{rev}}}=\frac{0.8}{1.906}=0.420 \text { or } \mathbf{4 2 . 0} \%\)
