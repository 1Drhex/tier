Guest Question.

The Collision Rate of NO with \(O_{3}\)

Objective Find the collision rate of NO with \(O_{3}\) at 300 K if the abundances at 1 atm total pressure are each 0.2 ppm and if the molecular diameters are 300 and 375 pm, respectively. Reactive collisions between these two species are important in photochemical smog formation.

Method Use equation 1.44 \([Z_{12}=Z_{2n^{*}_{1}}=\pi b^{2}_{max}<v_{r}>n^{*}_{1}n^{*}_{2}]\), remembering to convert the abundances to number densities at 300 K and calculating the average relative velocity by use of equation 1.46 \(<v_r> =\sqrt{2}<v>=[\sqrt{2}\left(\frac{8kT}{\pi m} \right) ^{1/2}=\left(\frac{8kT}{\pi (m/2)} \right) ^{1/2}=\left(\frac{8kT}{\pi \mu } \right) ^{1/2}]\).

Admin
Solution

First find the total number density n* at 1 atm:

n* = \((n/V)N_{A} = (p/RT)N_{A} = (1 atm) (6.02 × 10^{23} molec /mole)/ [( 0.082 L atm mol^{-1} K^{-1})(300 K)] = 2.45 \times 10^{22}\) molec/L. Next determine the number densities of NO and \(O_{3}\), each being the total density times \(0.2 \times 10^{-6}\): \(n^{*}(NO) = n^{*}(O_{3}) = (0.2 \times 10^{-6})(2.45 \times 10^{22})= 4.9 \times 10^{15} molec/L\). The average relative velocity is \(<v_r>= (8kT/\pi \mu)^{1/2} = [8(1.38 \times 10^{-23} J K^{-1})(300 K)(6.02 × 10^{23} amu/g)(1000 g/kg)/ (\pi(48 \times 30/78) amu)]^{1/2}= 586\) m/s. The average diameter is (300 + 375 pm)/2 = 337.5 pm. Then \(Z_{12} = \pi(337.5 \times 10^{-12} m)^{2} (586 m/s)(4.9 \times 10^{15} molec/L)^{2}(1 L/10^{-3} m^{3})^{2} = 5.0 \times 10^{21}\) collisions \(s^{-1} m^{-3}\). If every collision resulted in a reaction, this would be the number of reactions per unit second per cubic meter.
