Guest Question.

A grade 30 gray cast iron is subjected to a load F applied to a 1 by \(\frac{3}{8}\)-in cross-section link with a \(\frac{1}{4}\)-in-diameter hole drilled in the center as depicted in Fig. 6–31a. The surfaces are machined. In the neighborhood of the hole, what is the factor of safety guarding against failure under the following conditions:
(a) The load F = 1000 lbf tensile, steady.
(b) The load is 1000 lbf repeatedly applied.
(c) The load fluctuates between 21000 lbf and 300 lbf without column action.
Use the Smith-Dolan fatigue locus.

Admin
Solution

Some preparatory work is needed. From Table A–24, \(S_{u t}=31 kpsi , S_{u c}=109 kpsi\) \(k_{a} k_{b} S_{e}^{\prime}=14 \text { kpsi. }\) Since \(k_{c}\) for axial loading is 0.9, then \(S_{e}=\left(k_{a} k_{b} S_{e}^{\prime}\right) k_{c}=14(0.9)=\)\(12.6 kpsi \text { . }\) From Table A–15–1, \(A=t(w-d)=0.375(1-0.25)=0.281 \text { in }^{2}\), \(d / w=0.25 / 1=0.25\), \(K_{t} = 2.45\). The notch sensitivity for cast iron is 0.20 , so

 

\(K_{f}=1+q\left(K_{t}-1\right)=1+0.20(2.45-1)=1.29\)

 

(a) Since the load is steady, \(\sigma_{a}=0\) the load is static. Based on the discussion of cast iron, \(K_{t},\) and consequently \(K_{f}\) need not be applied. Thus, \( \sigma_{m}=F_{m} / A=\)\(1000\left(10^{-3}\right) / 0.281=3.56 kpsi , \text { and }\)

 

\(n=\frac{S_{u t}}{\sigma_{m}}=\frac{31.0}{3.56}=8.71\)

 

(b) \(F_{a}=F_{m}=\frac{F}{2}=\frac{1000}{2}=500 lbf\)

 

\(\sigma_{a}=\sigma_{m}=\frac{K_{f} F_{a}}{A}=\frac{1.29(500)}{0.281}\left(10^{-3}\right)=2.30 kpsi\)

 

\(r=\frac{\sigma_{a}}{\sigma_{m}}=1\)

 

From Eq. (6–52),

 

\(S_{a}=\frac{r S_{u t}+S_{e}}{2}\left[-1+\sqrt{1+\frac{4 r S_{u t} S_{e}}{\left(r S_{u t}+S_{e}\right)^{2}}}\right]\) (6–52)

 

\(S_{a}=\frac{(1) 31+12.6}{2}\left[-1+\sqrt{1+\frac{4(1) 31(12.6)}{[(1) 31+12.6]^{2}}}\right]=7.63 kpsi\)

 

\(n=\frac{S_{a}}{\sigma_{a}}=\frac{7.63}{2.30}=3.32\)

 

(c) \(F_{a}=\frac{1}{2}|300-(-1000)|=650 lbf \quad \sigma_{a}=\frac{1.29(650)}{0.281}\left(10^{-3}\right)=2.98 kpsi\)

 

\(F_{m}=\frac{1}{2}[300+(-1000)]=-350 lbf \quad \sigma_{m}=\frac{1.29(-350)}{0.281}\left(10^{-3}\right)=-1.61 kpsi\)

 

\(r=\frac{\sigma_{a}}{\sigma_{m}}=\frac{3.0}{-1.61}=-1.86\)

 

From Eq. (6–53), \(S_{a}=S_{e}+\left(S_{e} / S_{u t}-1\right) S_{m} \text { and } S_{m}=S_{a} / r . \text { It follows that }\)

 

\(S_{a}=S_{e}+\left(\frac{S_{e}}{S_{u t}}-1\right) S_{m} \quad-S_{u t} \leq S_{m} \leq 0 \quad \text { (for cast iron) }\) (6–53)

 

\(S_{a}=\frac{S_{e}}{1-\frac{1}{r}\left(\frac{S_{e}}{S_{u t}}-1\right)}=\frac{12.6}{1-\frac{1}{-1.86}\left(\frac{12.6}{31}-1\right)}=18.5 kpsi\)

 

\(n=\frac{S_{a}}{\sigma_{a}}=\frac{18.5}{2.98}=6.20\)

 

Figure 6–31b shows the portion of the designer’s fatigue diagram that was constructed.
