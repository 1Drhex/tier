Guest Question.

Calculate the surface temperature in a circular solid cylinder of radius 25 mm with a volumetric heat generation of \( 35.3 MW / m ^{3} . \) The external surface of the cylinder is exposed to a liquid at a temperature of \( 20^{\circ} C\) with a surface heat transfer coefficient of \( 4000 W / m ^{2}{ }^{\circ} C . \) The thermal conductivity of the material is \( 21 W / m ^{\circ} C . \)

 

 

Admin
Solution

Let us divide the region into four elements, each of width 6.25 cm . On substituting the given data into Equation (4.77),

\([ K ]_{\varepsilon}=\frac{2 \pi k}{l}\left(\frac{r_{i}+r_{j}}{2}\right)\left[\begin{array}{cc}1 & -1 \\-1 & 1\end{array}\right]\)

the stiffness matrix of the four elements may be calculated as follows:

\([ K ]_{1}=2 \pi\left[\begin{array}{rr}10.5 & -10.5 \\-10.5 & 10.5\end{array}\right]\) (4.81)

\([ K ]_{2}=2 \pi\left[\begin{array}{rr}31.5 & -31.5 \\-31.5 & 31.5\end{array}\right]\) (4.82)

\([ K ]_{3}=2 \pi\left[\begin{array}{rr}52.5 & -52.5 \\-52.5 & 52.5\end{array}\right]\) (4.83)

And

\([ K ]_{4}=2 \pi\left[\begin{array}{rr}73.5 & -73.5 \\-73.5 & 73.5\end{array}\right]+2 \pi\left[\begin{array}{rr}0 & 0 \\0 & 100\end{array}\right]\) (4.84)

\(\text { Similarly the forcing vectors for all the four elements can be calculated as }\)

\(\{ f \}_{1}=2 \pi\left\{\begin{array}{l}229.82 \\459.63\end{array}\right\}\) (4.85)

\(\{ f \}_{2}=2 \pi\left\{\begin{array}{c}919.27 \\1149.09\end{array}\right\}\) (4.86)

\(\{ f \}_{3}=2 \pi\left\{\begin{array}{l}1608.18 \\1838.54\end{array}\right\}\) (4.87)

and

\(\{ f \}_{4}=2 \pi\left\{\begin{array}{c}2298.18 \\2528.00\end{array}\right\}+2 \pi\left\{\begin{array}{c}0 \\2000\end{array}\right\}\) (4.88)

Assembly gives

\(\left[\begin{array}{rrrrr}10.5 & -10.5 & 0.0 & 0.0 & 0.0 \\-10.5 & 42.0 & -31.5 & 0.0 & 0.0 \\0.0 & -31.5 & 84.0 & -52.5 & 0.0 \\0.0 & 0.0 & -52.5 & 126.0 & -73.5 \\0.0 & 0.0 & 0.0 & -73.5 & 173.5\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}T_{1} \\T_{2} \\T_{3} \\T_{4} \\T_{5}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{l}229.82 \\1378.9 \\2757.81 \\4136.72 \\4528.00\end{array}\right\} .\) (4.89)

The solution obtained by solving the above system of equations is tabulated in Table 4.3 We can see that the surface temperature, \( T_{5}\), is predicted very well but the deviation from the exact solution increases as we proceed towards the center. If two linear elements replaces the one element near the center, then the solution for the maximum temperature is improved to \( 398.43^{\circ} C . \) It is also possible to improve the accuracy of the temperature solution by using quadratic elements. The analytical solution for the outer wall temperature \( T_{o}\) is computed using the energy balance per unit cylinder length at the outer surface, that is, \( -k(d T / d r)=h\left(T_{o}-T_{a}\right) \) and Equation (4.76)

\(\frac{d T}{d r}=-\frac{G r}{2 k}\)
