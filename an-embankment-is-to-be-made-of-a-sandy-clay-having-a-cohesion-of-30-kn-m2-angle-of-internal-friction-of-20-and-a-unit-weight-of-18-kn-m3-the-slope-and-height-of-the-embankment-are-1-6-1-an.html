Guest Question.

An embankment is to be made of a sandy clay having a cohesion of 30 \(kN / m ^{2}\), angle of internal friction of 20째 and a unit weight of 18 \(kN / m ^{3}\). The slope and height of the embankment are 1.6 : 1 and 10 m respectively. Determine the factor of safety by using the trial circle given in Fig. Ex. 10.11 by the method of slices.

Admin
Solution

Consider the embankment as shown in Fig. Ex.10.11. The center of the trial circle O is selected by taking \(\alpha_{A}=26^{\circ} \text { and } \alpha_{B}=35^{\circ}\) from Table 10.1. The soil mass above the slip circle is divided into 13 slices of 2 m width each. The weight of each slice per unit length of embankment is given by \(W=h_{a} b \gamma_{l}, \text { where } h_{a}=\) average height of the slice, b = width of the slice, \(\gamma_{t}=\) unit weight of the soil.

The weight of each slice may be represented by a vector of height \(h_{a} \text { if } b \text { and } \gamma_{t}\) remain the same for the whole embankment. The vectors values were obtained graphically. The height vectors may be resolved into normal components \(h_{n}\) and tangential components \(h_{t} \text {. The values of } h_{a}, h_{n}\) and \(h_{t}\) for the various slices are given below in a tabular form.

The sum of these components \(h_{n} \text { and } h_{t}\) may be converted into forces \(\Sigma N \text { and } \Sigma T\) respectively by multiplying them as given below

 

\(\Sigma h_{n}=81.57 m , \quad \Sigma h_{t}=24.87 m\)

 

Therefore, \(\Sigma N=81.57 \times 2 \times 18=2937 kN\)

 

\(\Sigma T=24.87 \times 2 \times 18=895 kN\)

 

Length of arc \(=L=31.8 m\)

 

Factor of safety \(=\frac{c^{\prime} L+\tan \phi^{2} \Sigma N}{\Sigma T}=\frac{30 \times 31.8+0.364 \times 2937}{895}=2.26\)
