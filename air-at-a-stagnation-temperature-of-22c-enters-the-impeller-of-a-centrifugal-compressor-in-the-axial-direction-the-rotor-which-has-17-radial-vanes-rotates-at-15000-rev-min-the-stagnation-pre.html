Guest Question.

Air at a stagnation temperature of 22°C enters the impeller of a centrifugal compressor in the axial direction. The rotor, which has 17 radial vanes, rotates at 15,000 rev/min. The stagnation pressure ratio between diffuser outlet and impeller inlet is 4.2 and the overall efficiency (total-to-total) is 83%. Determine the impeller tip radius and power required to drive the compressor when the mass flow rate is 2 kg/s and the mechanical efficiency is 97%. Given that the air density at impeller outlet is \(2 kg/m^{3} \) and the axial width at entrance to the diffuser is 11 mm, determine the absolute Mach number at that point. Assume that the slip factor σ = 1-2/Z, where Z is the number of vanes. (For air, take \(\gamma =1.4 \) and R = 0.287 kJ/(kg K).)

Admin
Solution

From Eq. (7.3), the specific work is

\(\Delta W=h_{02}-h_{01}=U_{2}c_{\theta 2}\)

as \(c_{\theta 1}=0\). For a radial impeller, \(\beta ^{\prime }_{2}=0 \), so \(c_{\theta 2}=\sigma U_{2}\). With Eq. (7.28) and some rearranging:

 

\(\frac{w_{s}-w_{p}}{DC}=2\omega \sin \gamma \cos \beta -w\left(\frac{d\beta }{dm}-\frac{\cos \beta \sin \beta }{\rho b} \left\langle\frac{d\left(\rho b\right)}{dm} \right\rangle \right) \) (7.28)

 

\(U^{2}_{2}=\frac{C_{p} T_{01}\left({p_{03} }/{p_{02} }^{\left(\gamma -1\right)/\gamma} -1\right)}{\sigma \eta _{c}}\)

 

where \({p_{03} }/{p_{01} }=4.2 ; C_{p}=\gamma R/\left(\gamma -1\right)=1.005 kJ/kg K, \sigma_{s}=1-2/17=0.8824\). Therefore,

 

\(U^{2}_{2}=\frac{1005\times 295\left(4.2^{0.286} -1\right) }{0.8824\times 0.83} =20.5\times 10^{4}\)

and \(U_{2}=452 m/s\)

The rotational speed is \(\Omega =15,000\times 2\pi /60=1570 rad/s\) and the impeller tip radius is

\( r_{t}= U_{2}/\Omega =452/1570=0.288 m\)

The actual shaft power is obtained from

\(\dot{W}_{act} =\dot{W}_{c}/\eta _{m} =\dot{m}\Delta W /\eta _{m}=2\times 0.8824\times 452^{2}/0.97=373 kW \)

Although the absolute Mach number at the impeller tip can be obtained almost directly from Eq. (7.46a), it may be instructive instead to find it from its basic definition:

 

\(\left(\frac{a_{02} }{a_{01} } \right) ^{2}=\frac{T_{02} }{T_{01} } =1+\frac{\Delta T_{0} }{T_{01} }=1+\frac{\Delta W }{C_{p} T_{01}}=1+\frac{1}{\eta _{1} } \left(p^{\left(\gamma -1\right)\gamma}_{R} -1\right) \) (7.46)

 

\(M_{2}=\frac{c_{2}}{a_{2}}=\frac{c_{2}}{\left(\gamma R T_{2}\right)^{1/2} }\)

where

\(c_{2}=\left(c^{2}_{\theta 2} +c^{2}_{r2} \right) ^{1/2} \)

\(c_{r2}=\dot{m}/\left(\rho _{2} 2\pi r_{t}b_{2}\right)=2/\left(2\times 2\pi \times 0.288\times 0.011\right)=50.3 m/s\)

\(c_{\theta 2}=\sigma U_{2} =400 m/s\)

Therefore,

\(c_{2}=\sqrt{400^{2}+50.3^{2} } =402.5 m/s \)

Since

\(h_{02}=h_{01}+\Delta W\)

\(h_{2}=h_{01}+\Delta W-\frac{1}{2}c^{2}_{2} \)

Therefore,

\( T_{2} =T_{01}+\left(\Delta W-\frac{1}{2}c^{2}_{2} \right) /C_{p}=295+\left(18.1-8.1\right) 10^{4}/1005=394.5 K \)

Hence,

\(M_{2}=\frac{402.5}{\sqrt{402\times 394.5} } =1.01 \)
