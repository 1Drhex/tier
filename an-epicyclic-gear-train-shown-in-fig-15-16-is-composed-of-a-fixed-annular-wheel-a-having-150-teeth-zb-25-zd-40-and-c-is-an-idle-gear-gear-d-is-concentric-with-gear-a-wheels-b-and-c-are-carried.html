Guest Question.

An epicyclic gear train shown in Fig.15.16 is composed of a fixed annular wheel A having 150 teeth. \( z_{B}=25, z_{D}=40 \) and C is an idle gear. Gear D is concentric with gear A. Wheels B and C are carried on an arm E which revolves clockwise at 120 rpm about the axis of A. Find the number of teeth of gear C and its speed and sense of rotation.

Admin
Solution

\( \frac{d_{A}}{2}=d_{B}+d_{C}+\frac{d_{D}}{2} \).

\( \frac{z_{A}}{2}=z_{B}+z_{C}+\frac{z_{D}}{2} \).

\( \frac{150}{2}=25+z_{C}+\frac{40}{2} \).

\( z_{C}=75-25-20=30 \).

Table 15.13 can be used to find the speed of gear C.

y = -120.

\( x+y=0 ; \quad x=120 \).

\( N_{C}=-5 x+y=-5 \times 120-120=-720 \text { rpm. i.e } 720 rpm cw \).
