Guest Question.

Determine for the shaded area:

a) \(I_{y}, I_{z}, I_{yz},\) .

b) the direction of the principal axes,

c) the principal moments of iner-tia.

Admin
Solution

a) The area is devided into five partial areas. Since the
area is point-symmetric with re-spect to C, the values of the mo-ments of inertia of the partial are-as I and \(\overline{I}\) are the same as is for II and \(\overline{II} .\)

\(\underline{\underline{I_{y}} }=\overbrace{2\left[\frac{a^{4}}{3}+\left(\frac{a}{2} \right)^{2}2a^{2} \right] }^{I and \overline{I} }+\)

 

\(+\overbrace{2\left[\frac{a^{4}}{36}+\left(-\frac{a}{6} \right)^{2}\frac{a^{2}}{2} \right] }^{II and \overline{II} }+\overbrace{\frac{2a^{4}}{12} }^{III}=\underline{\underline{\frac{23}{12}a^{4} } } ,\)

 

\(\underline{\underline{I_{z}} }=\overbrace{2\left[\frac{a^{4}}{3}+(2a)^{2}2a^{2} \right] }^{I and \overline{I} }+\overbrace{2\left[\frac{a^{4}}{36}+\left(\frac{4a}{3} \right)^{2}\frac{a^{2}}{2} \right] }^{II and \overline{II} }+\overbrace{\frac{a(2a)^{3}}{12} }^{III}\)

 

\(=\underline{\underline{\frac{115}{6}a^{4} } } , \)

 

 

\(\underline{\underline{I_{yz}} }=\overbrace{2\left[0-(2a)\left(\frac{a}{2} \right)2 a^{2} \right] }^{I and \overline{I} }+\overbrace{2\left[\frac{a^{4}}{72}-\left(\frac{4a}{3} \right)\left(-\frac{a}{6} \right)\frac{a^{2}}{2} \right] }^{II and {\overline{II} } }+\overbrace{0}^{III}\)

 

\(=\underline{\underline{-\frac{15}{4}a^{4} } } .\)

b) With these results, the directions of the principal axes can be found as

\(\tan 2\varphi ^{*}=\frac{2I_{yz}}{I_{y}- I_{z}}=\frac{90}{207} \rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{\varphi ^{*}_{1}=11.75^{\circ } } } , \underline{\underline{\varphi ^{*}_{2}=101.75^{\circ } } } .\)

c) One obtains for the principal moments of inertia

\(I_{1,2}=\left[\frac{23+230}{24}\pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{23-230}{24} \right)^{2}+\left(-\frac{15}{4} \right)^{2} } \right]a^{4}\)

 

\(=\left[\frac{235}{24}+ \sqrt{\frac{5661}{64} } \right]a^{4}\)

 

\(\rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{I_{1}\approx 19.95 a^{4}} } , \underline{\underline{I_{2}\approx 1.14 a^{4}} } .\)

Remark: The match of the principal axes to their respective principal moments of inertia can be derived by the transformation equations. In the present case, it is clear that the smaller principal moment of inertia \(I_{2}\) corresponds to the direction with angle \(\varphi ^{*}_{1}.\) The areas have in average a much smaller distance to the axis with direction \(\varphi ^{*}_{1} \) than to the axis with direction \(\varphi ^{*}_{2}\)
