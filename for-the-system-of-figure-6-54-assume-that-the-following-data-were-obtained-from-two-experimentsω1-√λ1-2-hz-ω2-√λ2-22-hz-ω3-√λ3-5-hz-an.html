Guest Question.

For the system of Figure 6.54, assume that the following data were obtained from two experiments,

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\omega_{1}=\sqrt{\lambda_{1}}=2 Hz \\&\omega_{2}=\sqrt{\lambda_{2}}=22 Hz \\&\omega_{3}=\sqrt{\lambda_{3}}=5 Hz\end{aligned}\)

 

and that \(m_{1}\) + \(m_{2}\) = 11 kg. (The first experiment provided \(\omega_{1}\) and\(\omega_{2}\), and the second experiment, corresponding to the case of Figure 6.55, provided \(\omega_{3}\).) Solve for \(k_{1}\), \(k_{2}\), \(m_{1}\), and \(m_{2}\).

Admin
Solution

Solution: Note that \(\omega_{3}\) is the natural frequency of the structure when the second mass is fixed, as per Equation 6.145. Using Equations 6.146 to 6.148, we find

 

\(\lambda_{3}=\frac{k_{1}+k_{2}}{m_{1}}\) (6.145)

 

\(R_{1} \equiv \frac{k_{2}}{m_{2}}=\lambda_{1}+\lambda_{2}-\lambda_{3}\) (6.146)

 

\(R_{2} \equiv \frac{k_{1}}{m_{1}}=\frac{\lambda_{1} \lambda_{2}}{\lambda_{1}+\lambda_{2}-\lambda_{3}}\) (6.147)

 

\(R_{3} \equiv \frac{k_{2}}{m_{1}}=\frac{\left(\lambda_{3}-\lambda_{1}\right)\left(\lambda_{2}-\lambda_{3}\right)}{\lambda_{1}+\lambda_{2}-\lambda_{3}}\) (6.148)

 

\(R_{1}=463, \quad R_{2}=4.18, \quad R_{3}=20.8\)

 

all of which have units of (rad/s)^2, and using Equations 6.150 to 6.153, the physical parameters are found to be

 

\(k_{1}=\frac{R_{1} R_{2}}{R_{1}+R_{3}} m\) (6.150)

 

\(k_{2}=\frac{R_{1} R_{3}}{R_{1}+R_{3}} m\) (6.151)

 

\(m_{1}=\frac{R_{1}}{R_{1}+R_{3}} m\) (6.152)

 

\(m_{2}=\frac{R_{3}}{R_{1}+R_{3}} m\) (6.153)

 

\(\begin{gathered}k_{1}=44.0 N / m , \quad k_{2}=219 N / m \\m_{1}=10.5 kg , \quad m_{2}=0.473 kg\end{gathered}\)

 

These results make sense physically since we expect a large disparity between mass and/or stiffness properties if there is a significant difference between the natural
frequencies.
