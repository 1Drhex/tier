Guest Question.

A cantilever beam supports a uniformly distributed load, q. Use Castigliano’s second theorem to determine the deflections at points A and B (E and l are constant).

Admin
Solution

To use Castigliano’s second theorem, imaginary forces \(F_{A}\) and \(F_{B}\) are applied at points A and B, respectively, in the calculation of strain energy. To remove them, these imaginary forces will be replaced by zeros after the derivatives have been taken.

The strain energy for a beam is (Table 11.1):

 

\(U=\frac{1}{2} \int_{0}^{L} \frac{M^{2}(x)}{E l} d x\)

 

The bending moment due to the applied distributed load and the two imaginary forces is (see Section 6.2.3):

 

\(\begin{aligned}M(x) &=-F_{ A }\langle x-0\rangle-F_{ B }\left\langle x-\frac{L}{2}\right\rangle-\frac{1}{2} q\langle x-0\rangle^{2} \\&=-F_{ A } x-F_{ B }\left\langle x-\frac{L}{2}\right\rangle-\frac{1}{2} q x^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

By Castigliano’s second theorem (Equation (11.6)), the vertical deflections at A and B are,
respectively:

 

\(\delta_{i}=\frac{\partial U}{\partial F_{i}}=\int_{0}^{L} \frac{N(x)}{E A} \frac{\partial N(x)}{\partial F_{i}} d x+\int_{0}^{L} \frac{M(x)}{E l} \frac{\partial M(x)}{\partial F_{i}} d x+\int_{0}^{L} \frac{T(x)}{G J} \frac{\partial T(x)}{\partial F_{i}} d x\) (11.6)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\delta_{ A } &=\frac{\partial U}{\partial F_{ A }}=\int_{0}^{L} \frac{M(x)}{E l} \frac{\partial M(x)}{\partial F_{ A }} d x \\&=\int_{0}^{L} \frac{-P_{ A } x-P_{ B }\langle x-L / 2\rangle-q x^{2} / 2}{E l}(-x) d x \\&=\int_{0}^{L} \frac{q x^{2} / 2}{E l} x d x=\frac{q L^{4}}{8 E l}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\delta_{ B } &=\frac{\partial U}{\partial F_{ B }}=\int_{0}^{L} \frac{M(x)}{E l} \frac{\partial M(x)}{\partial F_{ B }} d x \\&=\int_{0}^{L} \frac{-P_{ A } x-P_{ B }\langle x-L / 2\rangle-q x^{2} / 2}{E l} \times(-1)\left\langle x-\frac{L}{2}\right) d x \\&=\int_{0}^{L} \frac{q x^{2} / 2}{E l}\langle x-L / 2\rangle d x \\&=\int_{L / 2}^{L} \frac{q x^{2} / 2}{E l}(x-L / 2) d x=\frac{17 q L^{4}}{384}\end{aligned}\)

 

The above deflections can be verified by the formulas in Table 6.2.
