Guest Question.

Ideal Operational Amplifier. Consider the circuit shown in Figure 6.3.2a. Suppose the operational amplifier is a μA741 operational amplifier. Model the operational amplifier as an ideal operational amplifier. Determine how the output voltage \(v_o\) is related to the input voltage \(v_s\).

Admin
Solution

Figure 6.3-2b shows the circuit when the operational amplifier of Figure 6.3-2a is modeled as an ideal operational amplifier.
1. The inverting input node and output node of the operational amplifier are connected by a short circuit, so the node voltages at these nodes are equal:
\(v_1 = v_o\)

2. The voltages at the inverting and noninverting nodes of an ideal op amp are equal:
\(v_2 = v_1 = v_o\)

3. The currents into the inverting and noninverting nodes of an operational amplifier are zero, so
\(i_1 = 0 and i_2 = 0\)

4. The current in resistor \(R_s is i_2 = 0\), so the voltage across \(R_s is 0 V\). The voltage across \(R_s is v_s – v_2 = v_s – v_o\); hence,
\(v_s – v_o = 0\)
or \(v_s = v_o\)

Does this solution satisfy the requirements of Eqs. 6.3-1 and 6.3-2? The output current of the operational amplifier must be calculated. Apply KCL at the output node of the operational amplifier to get
\(i_1 + i_o + \frac{v_o}{R_L} = 0\)

\(\left|v_o\right| \leq v_{sat}\)

 

\(\left|i_o\right| \leq i_{sat}\)

 

\(\left|\frac{dv_o(t)}{dt}\right| \leq SR\) (6.3-1)

\(v_{sat} = 14 V, i_{sat} = 2 mA, and SR = 500,000 \frac{V}{s}\) (6.3-2)

Because \(i_1 = 0, i_o = – \frac{v_o}{R_L}\)

Now Eqs. 6.3-1 and 6.3-2 require
\(\left|v_s\right| \leq 14 V\)

 

\(\left|\frac{v_s}{R_L}\right| \leq 2 mA\)

 

\(\left|\frac{d}{dt} v_s\right| \leq 500,000 \frac{V}{s}\)

For example, when \(v_s = 10 V and R_L = 20 kΩ\), then

\(\left|v_s\right|=10 V \lt 14 V\)

 

\(\left|\frac{v_s}{R_L}\right|=\frac{10}{20,000}=\frac{1}{2} mA \lt 2 mA\)

 

\(\left|\frac{d}{dt} v_s\right|=0 \lt 500,000 \frac{V}{s}\)

This is consistent with the use of the ideal operational amplifier. On the other hand, when \(v_s = 10 V and R_L = 2 kΩ\), then

\(\frac{v_s}{R_L} = 5 mA \gt 2 mA\)

so it is not appropriate to model the μA741 as an ideal operational amplifier when \(v_s = 10 V and R_L = 2 kΩ\). When \(v_s = 10 V\), we require \(R_L \gt 5 kΩ\) to satisfy Eq. 6.3-1.
