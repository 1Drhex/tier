Guest Question.

Two single phase alternators having induced emf \(E_{1}\) and \(E_{2}\) and impedance \(Z_{1}\) and \(Z_{2}\) are connected in parallel and supplying a load of impedance \(Z\).

(i) Represent the terminal voltage in terms of alternators emfs \(E_{1}\) and \(E_{2}\) and admittances \(Y\), \(Y_{1}\) and \(Y_{2}\).

(ii) If \(\overline{E}_{1}=220 V,\overline{E}_{2}=230 V, \overline{Z}= 3 + j4 \) and \(\overline{Z}_{1}=\overline{Z}_{2}=\left(0.2 + j0.8\right) ohm \), determine the terminal voltage, circulating current at no-load and power delivered by each alternator in kW.

Admin
Solution

We know, terminal voltage in given by the relation

\(\overline{V}=\frac{\frac{\overline{E}_{1} }{\overline{Z}_{S1} } + \frac{\overline{E}_{2} }{\overline{Z}_{S2} } }{\frac{1}{\overline{Z} } + \frac{1}{\overline{Z}_{S1} } + \frac{1}{\overline{Z}_{s2} }} \)

 

We also know that admittance, \(Y=\frac{1}{\overline{Z} } \)

\(\therefore \frac{1}{\overline{Z}_{S1} } = \overline{Y}_{S1}; \frac{1}{\overline{Z}_{S2} } = \overline{Y}_{S2} \) and \( \frac{1}{\overline{Z} } = \overline{Y} \)

 

\( \therefore \) Terminal voltage, \(\overline{V}=\frac{\overline{E}_{1}\overline{Y}_{S1} + \overline{E}_{2}\overline{Y}_{S2} }{\overline{Y} + \overline{Y}_{S1} + \overline{Y}_{S2}} \)

Give that \(\overline{Z}= \left(3+ j4\right) ohm; \overline{Z}_{S1}=\overline{Z}_{S2}=\left(0.2+ j0.8\right) ohm; \overline{E}_{1}=220 V; \overline{E}_{2}=230 V \)

\(\overline{Y}=\frac{1}{\overline{Z}} =\frac{1}{3+ j4}\times \frac{3- j4}{3- j4}=\frac{3- j4}{3^{2} + 4^{2}}=\left(0.12-j0.168\right) mho \)

 

\(\overline{Y}_{S1}=\overline{Y}_{S2}=\frac{1}{\overline{Z}_{S1}} =\frac{1}{0.2+ j0.8}= \frac{1}{0.2+ j0.8}\times \frac{0.2- j0.8}{0.2- j0.8}=\left(0.2425-j0.97\right) mho \)

 

\(\overline{V}=\frac{\overline{E}_{1}\overline{Y}_{S1} + \overline{E}_{2}\overline{Y}_{S2} }{\overline{Y} + \overline{Y}_{S1} + \overline{Y}_{S2}}=\frac{\left(220+ 230\right)\left(0.2425 – j0.97\right)}{\left(0.12 – j0.168\right)+ 2\left(0.2425 – j0.97\right) } =\frac{109.125 – j436.5}{(0.605 – j2.108)} =\frac{450\angle -76^{\circ }}{2.193\angle -74^{\circ }} = 205.2\angle -2^{\circ } volt= \left(205.075- j7.16\right) V\)

 

Circulating current at no-load

\(\overline{I}_{C}=\frac{\overline{E}_{2}-\overline{E}_{1}}{\overline{Z}_{S1} + \overline{Z}_{S2}}=\frac{230-220}{ 2\left(0.2 + j0.8\right) } =\frac{10}{0.4+ j1.6}=\frac{10}{1.65\angle 76^{\circ }} = \mathbf{6.06\angle -76^{\circ }A} \)

 

Current supplied to the load by alternator I

\(\overline{I}_{1}=\frac{\overline{E}_{1}-\overline{V}}{\overline{Z}_{S1}}=\left(\overline{E}_{1}-\overline{V}\right)\overline{Y}_{S1}=\left(220-205.075+j7.16\right)\left(0.2425-j0.97\right)=\left(14.925+j7.16\right)\left(0.2425-j0.97\right)=\left(16.55\angle +25.63^{\circ }\right)\left(1\angle -76^{\circ }\right)=16.55\angle -50.37^{\circ }A\)

 

Current supplied to the load by alternator II

\(\overline{I}_{2}=\frac{\overline{E}_{2}-\overline{V}}{\overline{Z}_{S2}}=\left(\overline{E}_{2}-\overline{V}\right)\overline{Y}_{S2}=\left(230-205.075+j7.16\right)\left(0.2425-j0.97\right)=\left(24.925+j7.16\right)\left(0.2425-j0.97\right)=\left(25.93\angle +16^{\circ }\right)\left(1\angle -76^{\circ }\right)=25.93\angle -50^{\circ }A\)

 

Power delivered by alternator I

\( P_{1}=\overline{V}\overline{I}_{1}=\left(205.2\angle-2^{\circ }\right)\left(16.55\angle -50.37^{\circ }\right)=3396\angle -52.37=\left(2073.5-j2.69\right)VA=2073.5W= \mathbf{2.0735 kW} \)

 

Power delivered by alternator II

\( P_{2}=\overline{V} \overline{I}_{2}=\left(205.2\angle-2^{\circ }\right)\left(25.93\angle -50^{\circ }\right)=5321\angle -52^{\circ }=\left(3276-j4.193\right)VA=3276W= \mathbf{3.276 kW} \)
