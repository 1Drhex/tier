Guest Question.

The Frobenius norm \(\|A\|_{F}\) of a matrix \(A\) is obtained by stringing out the entries of the matrix into a vector and then taking the Euclidean norm. In other words, \(\|A\|_{F}\) is just the square root of the sum of the squares of the entries of \(A . \mathrm{So}\), if \(A=\left[a_{i j}\right]\), then

\(\|A\|_{F}=\sqrt{\sum_{i, j=1}^{n} a_{i j}^{2}}\)

(a) Find the Frobenius norm of

\(A=\left[\begin{array}{rr}3 & -1 \\2 & 4\end{array}\right]\)

(b) Show that the Frobenius norm is compatible with the Euclidean norm.

(c) Show that the Frobenius norm is a matrix norm.

Admin
Solution

(a) \(\|A\|_{F}=\sqrt{3^{2}+(-1)^{2}+2^{2}+4^{2}}=\sqrt{30}\)

Before we continue, observe that if \(\mathrm{A}_{1}=\left[\begin{array}{ll}3 & -1\end{array}\right]\) and \(\mathrm{A}_{2}=\left[\begin{array}{ll}2 & 4\end{array}\right]\) are the row vectors of \(A\), then \(\left\|\mathrm{A}_{1}\right\|_{E}=\sqrt{3^{2}+(-1)^{2}}\) and \(\left\|\mathrm{A}_{2}\right\|_{E}=\sqrt{2^{2}+4^{2}} .\) Thus,

\(\|A\|_{F}=\sqrt{\left\|\mathrm{A}_{1}\right\|_{E}^{2}+\left\|\mathrm{A}_{2}\right\|_{E}^{2}}\)

Similarly, if \(\mathbf{a}_{1}=\left[\begin{array}{l}3 \\ 2\end{array}\right]\) and \(\mathbf{a}_{2}=\left[\begin{array}{r}-1 \\ 4\end{array}\right]\) are the column vectors of \(A\), then

\(\|A\|_{F}=\sqrt{\left\|\mathbf{a}_{1}\right\|_{E}^{2}+\left\|\mathbf{a}_{2}\right\|_{E}^{2}}\)

It is easy to see that these facts extend to \(n \times n\) matrices in general. We will use these observations to solve parts (b) and (c).

(b) Write

\(A=\left[\begin{array}{c}\mathrm{A}_{1} \\\vdots \\\mathrm{A}_{n}\end{array}\right]\)

Then

\(\begin{aligned}\|A \mathrm{x}\|_{E} &=\left\|\left[\begin{array}{c}A_{1} \mathbf{x} \\\vdots \\A_{n} \mathbf{x}\end{array}\right]\right\|_{E} \\&=\sqrt{\left\|\mathbf{A}_{1} \mathbf{x}\right\|_{E}^{2}+\cdots+\left\|\mathbf{A}_{n} \mathbf{x}\right\|_{E}^{2}} \\& \leq \sqrt{\left\|\mathbf{A}_{1}\right\|_{E}^{2}\|\mathbf{x}\|_{E}^{2}+\cdots+\mathrm{A}_{n}\left\|_{E}^{2}\right\| \mathbf{x} \|_{E}^{2}} \\&=\left(\sqrt{\left.\left\|\mathrm{A}_{1}\right\|_{\mathrm{E}}^{2}+\cdots+\left\|\mathrm{A}_{\mathrm{n}}\right\|_{\mathrm{E}}^{2}\right)}\|\mathrm{x}\|_{\mathrm{E}}\right.\\&=\|\mathrm{A}\|_{F}\|\mathbf{x}\|_{E}\end{aligned}\)

where the inequality arises from the Cauchy-Schwarz Inequality applied to the dot products of the row vectors \(\mathrm{A}_{i}\) with the column vector \(\mathrm{x}\). (Do you see how CauchySchwarz has been applied?) Hence, the Frobenius norm is compatible with the Euclidean norm.

(c) Let \(\mathrm{b}_{i}\) denote the \(i\) th column of \(B\). Using the matrix-column representation of the product \(A B\), we have

\(\begin{aligned}\|A B\|_{F} &=\left\|\left[A \mathbf{b}_{1} \cdots A \mathbf{b}_{n}\right]\right\|_{F} \\&=\sqrt{\left\|A \mathbf{b}_{1}\right\|_{E}^{2}+\cdots+\left\|A \mathbf{b}_{n}\right\|_{E}^{2}} \\& \leq \sqrt{\|A\|_{F}^{2}\left\|\mathbf{b}_{1}\right\|_{\mathrm{E}}^{2}+\cdots+\|A\|_{F}^{2}\left\|\mathbf{b}_{n}\right\|_{E}^{2}} \quad \text { by part (b) } \\&=\|A\|_{F} \sqrt{\left\|\mathbf{b}_{1}\right\|_{E}^{2}+\cdots+\left\|\mathbf{b}_{n}\right\|_{E}^{2}} \\&=\|A\|_{F}\left\|_{B}\right\|_{F}\end{aligned}\)

which proves property (4) of the definition of a matrix norm. Properties (1) through (3) are true, since the Frobenius norm is derived from the Euclidean norm, which satisfies these properties. Therefore, the Frobenius norm is a matrix norm.
