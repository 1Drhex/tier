Guest Question.

A strain rosette, composed of three electrical resistance strain gages making angles of 0°, 60°, and 120° with the x-axis (see Fig. 10-32a), was mounted on the free surface of a steel (E = 30,000 ksi and v = 0.30) machine component. Under load, the following strains were measured:
\(ε_a =+1000 μin./in. \quad\quad ε_b =+750 μin./in.\quad\quad ε_c =-650 μin./in.\)
(a) Determine the principal strains and the maximum shearing strain at the point. Show the directions of the in-plane principal strains on a sketch.
(b) Determine the principal stresses and the maximum shearing stress at the point. Show the principal stresses and the maximum shearing stress on a sketch.

Admin
Solution

(a) When the given data are substituted into \(\epsilon _n=\epsilon_xcos^2\theta +\epsilon _ysin^2\theta +\gamma _{xy}sin\theta cos\theta \), the following equations are obtained

\(\epsilon _a=\epsilon _0=\epsilon _x=+1000\mu in/in.(measured)\)

 

\(\epsilon _c=\epsilon _{60}=-650\mu=1000\mu cos^260^\circ +\epsilon _ysin^260^\circ +\gamma _{xy}sin60^\circ cos60^\circ \)

 

\(\epsilon _b=\epsilon _{120}=+750\mu=1000\mu cos^2120^\circ +\epsilon _ysin^2120^\circ +\gamma _{xy}sin120^\circ cos120^\circ \)

from which

\(\epsilon _y=-266.7\mu in./in. \quad\quad \gamma _{xy}=-1616.6\mu rad\)

The in-plane principal strains \(\epsilon_{p1}\) and \(\epsilon_{p2}\) are given by \(\) as

\((\epsilon _{p1},\epsilon _{p2})=\frac{\epsilon _x+\epsilon _y}{2}\pm \sqrt{(\frac{\epsilon _x-\epsilon _y}{2} )^2+(\frac{\gamma _{xy}}{2} )^2} \)

 

\((\epsilon _{p1},\epsilon _{p2})(10^6)=\frac{1000+(-266.7)}{2}\pm \sqrt{(\frac{1000-(-266.7)}{2} )^2+(\frac{-1616.6}{2} )^2}=366.7\pm 1026.9 \)

 

\(\epsilon _{p1}=(366.7+1026.9)(10^{-6})=1393.6(10^{-6})\cong 1394\mu in./in.\)

 

\(\epsilon _{p2}=(366.7-1026.9)(10^{-6})=-660.2(10^{-6})\cong -660\mu in./in.\)

The principal strain perpendicular to the surface, \(\epsilon_{p3} =\epsilon_ z\) is given by

\(\epsilon _{z}=-\frac{v}{E}(\sigma _x+\sigma _y)=-\frac{v}{E}\left[ \frac{E}{1-v^2} \right][(\epsilon _x+v\epsilon _y)+(\epsilon _y+v\epsilon _x)]=-\frac{v}{1-v}(\epsilon _x+\epsilon _y) \) as

 

\(\epsilon _{p3}=\epsilon _z=-\frac{v}{1-v}(\epsilon _x+\epsilon _y) \)

 

\(=-\frac{0.3}{1-0.3}(1000-266.7)(10^{-6})\)

 

\(=-314.3(10^{-6})≅-314μin./in.\)

Since \(\epsilon_{p3}\) is less compressive than the in-plane principal strain \(\epsilon_{ p2}\), the maximum shearing strain at the point is the maximum in-plane shearing strain \(γ_p\). Thus,

\(\gamma _{max}=\gamma _p=\pm (1394\mu +660\mu )=\pm 2054\mu \cong \pm 2050\mu rad\)

The in-plane principal strain directions are obtained using \(\)

\(tan2\theta _p=\frac{\gamma _{xy}}{\epsilon _x-\epsilon _y}=\frac{(-1616.6)}{1000-(-266.7)}=-1.2763 \)

 

\(\theta _p=-25.96^\circ =25.96^\circ \circlearrowright \)

The required sketch is given in Fig. 10-32b.
(b) The principal stresses and the maximum shearing stress at the point can be
obtained from the strain results of part a by using \(\sigma _{x}=\frac{E}{1-v^2}(\epsilon _x+v\epsilon _y)\) , \(\sigma _{y}=\frac{E}{1-v^2}(\epsilon _y+v\epsilon _x)\)and \(\tau _{xy}=G\gamma _{xy}=\frac{E}{2(1+v)}\gamma _{xy} \) ,

\(\tau _{yz}=G\gamma _{yz}=\frac{E}{2(1+v)}\gamma _{yz} \),

\(\tau _{zx}=G\gamma _{zx}=\frac{E}{2(1+v)}\gamma _{zx} \). Thus,

\(\sigma _{max}=\frac{30(10^6)}{1-(0.30)^2}[1394+0.30(-660)](10^{-6}) \)

 

\(=+39,429psi\cong 39.4ksi \quad T\)

 

\(\sigma _{min}=\frac{30(10^6)}{1-(0.30)^2}[-660+0.30(1394)](10^{-6}) \)

 

\(=-7971psi\cong 7.97ksi \quad C\)

 

\(\tau _{max}=\frac{30(10^6)}{2(1+0.30)}(2054)(10^{-6})=23.7(10^3)psi=23.7ksi \)

Note that the value for \(τ_{max}\) checks with that given by \(\tau _{max}=\frac{\sigma _{max}-\sigma _{min}}{2}\); that is,

\(\tau _{max}=\frac{\sigma _{p1}-\sigma _{p2}}{2}=\frac{39,429-(-7971)}{2}=23.7(10^3)psi=23.7ksi \)

The directions of the principal stresses will be the same as those for the principal strains, \(θ_p= -25.96°\). The required sketch is shown in Fig. 10-32c.
ALTERNATE SOLUTION
The Cartesian components of strain obtained from the original rosette data were

\(\epsilon _x=+1000\mu in./in.\quad\quad \epsilon _y=-266.7\mu in./in. \quad\quad \gamma _{xy}=-1616.6\mu rad\)

The Cartesian components of stress at the point can be obtained from these strains by using \(\sigma _{x}=\frac{E}{1-v^2}(\epsilon _x+v\epsilon _y)\) , \(\sigma _{y}=\frac{E}{1-v^2}(\epsilon _y+v\epsilon _x)\)and \(\tau _{xy}=G\gamma _{xy}=\frac{E}{2(1+v)}\gamma _{xy} \) ,

\(\tau _{yz}=G\gamma _{yz}=\frac{E}{2(1+v)}\gamma _{yz} \),

\(\tau _{zx}=G\gamma _{zx}=\frac{E}{2(1+v)}\gamma _{zx} \). Thus,

\(\sigma _x=\frac{30(10^6)}{1-(0.30)^2}[1000+0.30(-266.7)](10^{-6})=30,330psi \)

 

\(\sigma _y=\frac{30(10^6)}{1-(0.30)^2}[(-266.7)+0.30(1000)](10^{-6})=1097.8psi \)

 

\(\tau _{xy}=\frac{30(10^6)}{2(1+0.30)}(-1616.6)(10^{-6})=-18,653psi \)

Then the principal stresses and the maximum in-plane shear stress are obtained using \(\sigma _{p1,p2}=\frac{\sigma _x+\sigma _y}{2}\pm \sqrt{(\frac{\sigma _x-\sigma _y}{2} )^2+\tau _{xy}^2} \) and \(\tau _p=\pm \sqrt{(\frac{\sigma _x-\sigma _y}{2} )^2+\tau _{xy}^2} \).

\((\sigma _{p1},\sigma _{p2})=\frac{\sigma _x+\sigma _y}{2}\pm \sqrt{(\frac{\sigma _x-\sigma _y}{2} )^2+(\tau _{xy})^2} \)

 

\(=\frac{30,330+1097.8}{2}\pm \sqrt{(\frac{30,330-1097.8}{2} )^2+(-18,653)^2} \)

 

\(=15,714\pm 23,697psi\)

 

\(\sigma _{p1}=15,714+23,697=39.411psi\cong 39.4 ksi \quad T\)

 

\(\sigma _{p2}=15,714-23,697=-7983psi\cong 7.98 ksi \quad C\)

 

\(\tau _p=\sqrt{(\frac{\sigma _x-\sigma _y}{2} )^2+(\tau _{xy})^2}=\sqrt{(\frac{30,330-1097.8}{2} )^2+(-18,653)^2} \)

 

\(\tau _p=\tau _{max}=23,697psi=23.7ksi\)

The principal directions are obtained using \(tan2\theta _p=\frac{2\tau _{xy}}{\sigma _x-\sigma _y}\)

\(tan2\theta _p=\frac{2(-18,653)}{30,330-1097.8}=-1.2762 \)

 

\(\theta _p=-25.96^\circ =25.96^\circ \circlearrowright \)

Any differences in numerical values found in part b of the solution and in the alternate solution is due to rounding off.
