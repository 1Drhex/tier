Guest Question.

Select a pump to deliver 500 gal/min of water with a pressure rise of 65 psi. Assume a rotational speed not to exceed 3600 rpm.

Admin
Solution

To estimate the specific speed we need the following:

\(\begin{array}{c}\omega=3600 \times \frac{\pi}{30}=377 \mathrm{~rad} / \mathrm{sec} \\\\H_{P}=\frac{\Delta p}{\rho g}=\frac{65 \times 144}{1.94 \times 32.2}=150 \mathrm{~ft} \\\\Q=\frac{500}{7.48 \times 60}=1.11 \mathrm{~ft}^{3} / \mathrm{sec}\end{array}\)

Use Eq. \(12.3 .13\) to find the specific speed to be

\(\Omega_{P}=\frac{C_{Q}^{1 / 2}}{C_{H}^{3 / 4}}=\frac{\omega Q^{1 / 2}}{\left(g H_{P}\right)^{3 / 4}}\) (12.3.13)

\(\begin{aligned}\Omega_{P} &=\frac{\omega \sqrt{Q}}{\left(g H_{P}\right)^{3 / 4}} \\\\&=\frac{377 \sqrt{1.11}}{(32.2 \times 150)^{3 / 4}}=0.69\end{aligned}\)

From Eq. 12.3.14, this would indicate a radial-flow pump. The pump of Fig. \(12.12\) could be used, even though the specific speed \((0.61)\) would result in a lower efficiency. With \(\Omega_{P} \simeq 0.61\) (Fig. 12.12), the speed is estimated with Eq. \(12.3 .13\) as

\(\begin{array}{l}\Omega_{P}<1 \quad\quad\quad {\text{ radial-flow pump}}\\\\1<\Omega_{P}<4 \quad {\text{ mixed-flow pump}}\\\\\Omega_{P}>4 \quad\quad\quad {\text{axial-flow pump}}\end{array} (12.3.14)\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\omega &=\frac{\Omega_{P}\left(g H_{P}\right)^{3 / 4}}{\sqrt{Q}} \\\\&=\frac{0.61 \times(32.2 \times 150)^{3 / 4}}{\sqrt{1.11}}=335 \mathrm{~rad} / \mathrm{sec}\end{aligned}\)

Hence the required speed is \(335 \times 30 / \pi=3200 \mathrm{~rpm}\), which does not exceed \(3600 \mathrm{~rpm}\). The required diameter is determined with use of Fig. 12.12, where at maximum efficiency, \(C_{Q}=0.0165\). This is substituted into Eq. \(12.3 .4\) to determine the diameter:

\(C_{Q}=\frac{Q}{\omega D^{3}} \quad \text { flow rate coefficient }\) (12.3.4)

\(\begin{aligned}D &=\left(\frac{Q}{C_{Q} \omega}\right)^{1 / 3} \\\\&=\left(\frac{1.11}{0.0165 \times 335}\right)^{1 / 3}=0.59 \mathrm{ft}\end{aligned}\)
