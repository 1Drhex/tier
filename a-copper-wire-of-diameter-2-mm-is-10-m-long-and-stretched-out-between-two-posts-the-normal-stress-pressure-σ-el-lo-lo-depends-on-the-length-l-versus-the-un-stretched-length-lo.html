Guest Question.

A copper wire of diameter 2 mm is 10 m long and stretched out between two posts. The normal stress (pressure) \(\sigma= E \left( L - L _{o}\right) / L _{o}\) , depends on the length L versus the un-stretched length \(L _{ o }\) and Young’s modulus \(E =1.1 \times 10^{6} kPa\). The force is \(F = A \sigma\) and measured to be 110 N. How much longer is the wire and how much work was put in?

Admin
Solution
\(F = As = AE \Delta L / L _{ o } \quad \text { and } \quad \Delta L = FL _{ o } / AE\)

 

\(A =\frac{\pi}{4} D ^{2}=\frac{\pi}{4} \times 0.002^{2}=3.142 \times 10^{-6} m ^{2}\)

 

\(\Delta L =\frac{110 N \times 10 m }{3.142 \times 10^{-6} m ^{2} \times 1.1 \times 10^{6} \times 10^{3} Pa }=0.318 m\)

 

\({ }_{1} W _{2}=\int F dx =\int A s dx =\int AE \frac{ x }{ L _{0}} dx\)

 

\(=\frac{ AE }{ L _{ o }} 1 / 2 x ^{2} \quad \text { where } x = L – L _{ o }\)

 

\(=\frac{3.142 \times 10^{-6} \times 1.1 \times 10^{6} \times 10^{3}}{10} \times 1 / 2 \times 0.318^{2}= 1 7 . 4 7 J\)
