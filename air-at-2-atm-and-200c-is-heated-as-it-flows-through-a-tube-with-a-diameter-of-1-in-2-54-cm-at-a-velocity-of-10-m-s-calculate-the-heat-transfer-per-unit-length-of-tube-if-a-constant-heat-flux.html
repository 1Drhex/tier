Guest Question.

Air at 2 atm and \(T =200^{◦} C\) is heated as it flows through a tube with a diameter of 1 in (2.54 cm) at a velocity of 10 m/s. Calculate the heat transfer per unit length of tube if a constant-heat-flux condition is maintained at the wall and the wall temperature is \(T =20^{◦} C\) above the air temperature, all along the length of the tube. How much would the bulk temperature increase over a 3-m length of
the tube?

Admin
Solution

We first calculate the Reynolds number to determine if the flow is laminar or turbulent, and then select the appropriate empirical correlation to calculate the heat transfer. The properties of air at a bulk temperatur of \(T =200^{◦} C\) are
\(p=\frac{P}{RT}=\frac{(2)(1.0132×10^{5} )}{(287)(473)}=1.493 \> kg/m^{3} \enspace [0.0932 \; lb_{m} /ft^{3} ] \)

Pr = 0.681

\(μ=2.57×10^{-5} \; kg/m· s \enspace [0.0622\enspace lb_{m} /h · ft] \)

 

\(k = 0.0386\; W/m·^{◦} C \qquad [0.0223 \enspace Btu/h · ft · ^{◦} F] \)

 

\(cp = 1.025\quad kJ/kg ·^{◦} C \)

 

\(Re_{d} =\frac{ρu_{m} d}{μ}=\frac{(1.493)(10)(0.0254)}{2.57×10^{-5} } =14,756 \)

so that the flow is turbulent. We therefore use Equation (6-4a)

\(Nu_{d}=0.023Re^{0.8}_{d} Pr^{n}\hspace{30 pt} [6-4a] \)

to calculate the heat-transfer
coefficient.

\(Nu_{d}=\frac{hd}{k}=0.023Re^{0.8}_{d} Pr^{0.4}=(0.023)(14,756)^{0.8} (0.681)^{0.4} =42.67 \)

 

\(h=\frac{k}{d} Nu_{d}=\frac{(0.0386)(42.67)}{0.0254}=64.85 \; W/m^{2} ·^{◦} C \enspace [11.42\enspace Btu/h · ft^{2} ·^{◦} F] \)

The heat flow per unit length is then

\(\frac{q}{L} =hπd(T_{w} −T_{b} )=(64.85)π(0.0254)(20)=103.5 \enspace W/m \quad [107.7 \enspace Btu/ft] \)

We can now make an energy balance to calculate the increase in bulk temperature in a 3.0-m length
of tube:

\(q=\dot{m} c_{p}\Delta T_{b}= L (\frac{q}{L} ) \)

We also have

\(\dot{m}=ρu_{m}\frac{πd^{2} }{4}= (1.493)(10)π =\frac{(0.0254)^{2} }{4}=7.565×10^{-3} \; kg/s \enspace [0.0167\enspace lb_{m} /s] \)

so that we insert the numerical values in the energy balance to obtain

\((7.565×10^{-3} )(1025)\Delta T_{b} =(3.0)(103.5) \)

and

\(\Delta T_{b} =40.04^{ ◦}C \enspace [\enspace 104.07^{◦} F] \)
