Calculating the Efficiency of a Steam Power Cycle

A Rankine power generation cycle using steam operates at a temperature of 100°C in the condenser, a pressure of 3.0 MPa in the evaporator, and a maximum temperature of 600°C. Assuming the pump and turbine operate reversibly, plot the cycle on a \(T-\hat{S}\) diagram for steam and compute the efficiency of the cycle.

Admin
Solution

We start by plotting the cycle on the temperature-entropy diagram for steam (Fig. 3.3-1b, repeated below). Then we construct the table of thermodynamic properties (which follows) for each point in the cycle using the known operating conditions and the path from each point in the cycle to the next point. Based on this information, we can then compute the efficiency of the cycle

\(\eta=\frac{-\left(\dot{W}_{ T }+\dot{W}_{ P }\right)}{\dot{Q}_{ B }}=\frac{-(-947.6+3.03)}{3260.2}=\frac{944.3}{3260.2}=0.290 \quad \text { or } 29.0 \text { percent }\)
