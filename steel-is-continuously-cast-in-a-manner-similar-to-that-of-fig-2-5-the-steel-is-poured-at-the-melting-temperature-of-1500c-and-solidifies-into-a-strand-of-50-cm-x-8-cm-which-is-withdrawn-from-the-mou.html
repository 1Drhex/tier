Guest Question.

Steel is continuously cast in a manner similar to that of Fig. 2.5. The steel is poured at the melting temperature of \( 1500^{\omicron }C\) and solidifies into a strand of 50 cm x 8 cm which is withdrawn from the mould at 280 cm/min. The solid skin at the mould exit is to be 1.5 cm thick and the film heat transfer coefficient between the metal and the mould is \( 1500W/m ^{2} .^{\omicron }C\) The mould is maintained at \( 20^{\omicron }C\) Calculate the mould length required.

Fig. 2.5 Diagram showing detail of liquid-solid transformation during continuous casting. (Reproduced courtesy of The AISE Steel Foundation.)

Admin
Solution

From Table 2.3, assuming steel constants are the same as for iron,

\( H_{f} = 280 kJ/kg, k_{s}= 73 W/m^{\omicron }C , \rho =7900 kg/m^{3}, C_{s} = 0.70kJ/kg^{\omicron }C\)

From the question it is known that when y = L (the mould length), thenÎ´ =0.015 m

\( S^{*}=\frac{h_{f}\delta }{K_{s}} =\frac{1500 x 0.015}{73}=0.31\)

also

\( H^{*}= \frac{H_{f}}{C_{s}(T_{M}-T_{\omicron })} =\frac{280}{0.70 \times 1480} =0.27\)

With y = L, u = 0.047 m/s and, using Fig. 2.23,

\( Y^{*}= \frac{h^{2}_{f}L }{uK_{s}\rho _{m}C_{s}} =0.12\)

Hence
\( L= \frac{0.12\times 0.047 x 73 x 7900 x 700}{1500^{2}} =1 m\)

The mould length required is about 1 m.
