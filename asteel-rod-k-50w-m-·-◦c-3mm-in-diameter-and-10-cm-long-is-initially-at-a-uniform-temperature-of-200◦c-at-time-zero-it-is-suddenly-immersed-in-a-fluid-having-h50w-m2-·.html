Guest Question.

Asteel rod \([k =50W/m · {◦} C]\) 3mm in diameter and 10 cm long is initially at a uniform temperature of \(200^{◦} C\). At time zero it is suddenly immersed in a fluid having \(h =50\; W/m^{2} · {◦} C\) and \(T_{∞}= 40^{◦} C\) while one end is maintained at \(200^{◦} C\). Determine the temperature distribution in the rod after 100 s. The properties of steel are\(ρ=7800 kg/m^{3} \enspace and\enspace c=0.47\; kJ/kg · {◦} C \).

Admin
Solution

The selection of increments on the rod is as shown in the Figure Example 4-11. The cross-sectional area of the rod is \(A=π(1.5)^{2} =7.069\enspace mm^{2} \) The volume element for nodes 1, 2, and 3 is:

\(\Delta V =A\Delta x=(7.069)(25)=176.725 \; mm^{3} \)

Node 4 has a ΔV of half this value, or \(88.36 \enspace mm^{3} \) We can now tabulate the various resistances and capacities for use in an explicit formulation. For nodes 1, 2, and 3 we have:

\(R_{m+}=R_{m-} =\frac{\Delta x}{kA} =\frac{0.025}{(50)(7.069×10^{-6} )}= 70.731^{◦} C/W \)

and:

\(R_{∞}=\frac{1}{h(πd \Delta x)} =\frac{1}{(50)π(3×10^{-3} )(0.025)} =84.883^{◦} C/W \)

 

\(C=ρc \Delta V =(7800)(470)(1.7673×10^{-7} )=0.6479 \; J/^{◦} C\)

 

For node 4 we have:

\(R_{m+}=\frac{1}{hA}=2829^{◦} C/W\hspace{30 pt} R_{m-}=\frac{\Delta X}{kA}=70.731^{◦} C/W \)

 

\(C=\frac{ρc \Delta V}{2}=0.3240^{◦} J/C \hspace{30 pt}R_{∞} =\frac{2}{hπd\Delta x}=169.77^{◦} C/W \)

To determine the stability requirement we form the following table:

Thus node 4 is the most restrictive, and we must select Δτ<15.9 s. Since we wish to find the temperature distribution at 100 s, let us use Δτ =10 s and make the calculation for 10 time increments using Equation (4-47) \(T^{p+1}_{i}=\frac{\Delta τ}{C_{i} }\left[q_{i}+\sum\limits_{j}{\frac{T^{p}_{j} -T^{p}_{i}}{R_{ij} } } \right]+T^{p}_{i} \)for the computation. We note, of course, that \(q_{i} =0 \)because there is no heat generation. The calculations are shown in the following table.

We can calculate the heat-transfer rate at the end of 100 s by summing the convection heat
losses on the surface of the rod. Thus:

\(q=\sum\limits_{i}{\frac{T_{i}-T_{∞} }{R_{i∞} } } \)

and:

\(q=\frac{200-40}{(2)(84.883)} +\frac{112.08+78.51+66.57−(3)(40)}{84.883}+(\frac{1}{169.77} +\frac{1}{2829} )(63.37−40)= 2.704 W \)
