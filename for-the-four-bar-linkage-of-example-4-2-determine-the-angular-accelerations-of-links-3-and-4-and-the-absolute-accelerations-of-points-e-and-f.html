Guest Question.

For the four-bar linkage of , determine the angular accelerations of links 3 and 4, and the absolute accelerations of points E and F.

Admin
Solution

Recall that the first-order kinematic coefficients from Eq. (8) in are

\(\theta_{3}^{\prime}=+0.2718 rad / rad \quad \text { and } \quad \theta_{4}^{\prime}=+0.5265 rad / rad.\) (8)

\(\theta_{3}^{\prime}=0.2718 rad / rad \quad \text { and } \quad \theta_{4}^{\prime}=0.5265 rad / rad \text {. }\) (1)

Substituting Eqs. (1) and the known data into Eqs. (4.36) and substituting the results into Eq. (4.38), the second-order kinematic coefficients of links 3 and 4 are

\(B_{1}=r_{2} \cos \theta_{2}+r_{3} \cos \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime 2}-r_{4} \cos \theta_{4} \theta_{4}^{\prime 2}\)

 

\(B_{2}=r_{2} \sin \theta_{2}+r_{3} \sin \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime 2}-r_{4} \sin \theta_{4} \theta_{4}^{\prime 2} \text {, }\) (4.36)

 

\(\theta_{3}^{\prime \prime}=\frac{B_{1} \cos \theta_{4}+B_{2} \sin \theta_{4}}{r_{3} \sin \left(\theta_{4}-\theta_{3}\right)} \text { and } \theta_{4}^{\prime \prime}=\frac{B_{1} \cos \theta_{3}+B_{2} \sin \theta_{3}}{r_{4} \sin \left(\theta_{4}-\theta_{3}\right)} .\) (4.38)

 

\(\theta_{3}^{\prime \prime}=0.0131 rad / rad ^{2} \quad \text { and } \quad \theta_{4}^{\prime \prime}=-0.2030 rad / rad ^{2}.\) (2)

 

Substituting the constant angular velocity, ω2 = 94.25 rad/s ccw, and Eqs. (1) and (2) into Eqs. (4.39), the angular accelerations of links 3 and 4 are

\(\alpha_{3}=\theta_{3}^{\prime \prime} \omega_{2}^{2}+\theta_{3}^{\prime} \alpha_{2} \quad \text { and } \quad \alpha_{4}=\theta_{4}^{\prime \prime} \omega_{2}^{2}+\theta_{4}^{\prime} \alpha_{2}.\) (4.39)

 

\(\alpha_{3}=116 rad / s ^{2} ccw \quad \text { and } \quad \alpha_{4}=1801 rad / s ^{2} cw.\)

These answers are in good agreement with the results obtained from the graphic method of —that is, α3 = 113 rad/s2 ccw and α4 = 1 822 rad/s2 cw.

To obtain the acceleration of point E, we differentiate Eqs. (13) in with respect to the input angle θ2. Therefore, the second-order kinematic coefficients for point E are

\(x_{E}^{\prime}=\frac{d x_{E}}{d \theta_{2}}=-r_{2} \sin \theta_{2}-r_{E B} \sin \left(\theta_{3}-\phi\right) \theta_{3}^{\prime},\)

 

\(y_{E}^{\prime}=\frac{d y_{E}}{d \theta_{2}}=r_{2} \cos \theta_{2}+r_{E B} \cos \left(\theta_{3}-\phi\right) \theta_{3}^{\prime} .\) (13)

\(x_{E}^{\prime \prime}=-r_{2} \cos \theta_{2}-r_{E B} \cos \left(\theta_{3}-\phi\right) \theta_{3}^{\prime 2}-r_{E B} \sin \left(\theta_{3}-\phi\right) \theta_{3}^{\prime \prime},\)

 

\(y_{E}^{\prime \prime}=-r_{2} \sin \theta_{2}-r_{E B} \sin \left(\theta_{3}-\phi\right) \theta_{3}^{\prime 2}+r_{E B} \cos \left(\theta_{3}-\phi\right) \theta_{3}^{\prime \prime} .\) (3)

 

Substituting Eqs. (1) and (2) and the known data into Eqs. (3) gives

\(x_{E}^{\prime \prime}=1.2067 in / rad ^{2} \quad \text { and } \quad y_{E}^{\prime \prime}=-3.3108 in / rad ^{2}.\) (4)

Differentiating the velocity of point E, from Eq. (15) in , with respect to time, the acceleration of point E can be written as

\(V _{E}=\left(x_{E}^{\prime} \hat{ i }+y_{E}^{\prime} \hat{ j }\right) \omega_{2}\) (15)

\(A _{E}=\left(x_{E}^{\prime \prime} \omega_{2}^{2}+x_{E}^{\prime} \alpha_{2}\right) \hat{ i }+\left(y_{E}^{\prime \prime} \omega_{2}^{2}+y_{E}^{\prime} \alpha_{2}\right) \hat{ j }.\) (5)

Recall that the first-order kinematic coefficients for point E, from Eq. (14) in , are

\(x_{E}^{\prime}=-3.4191 in / rad \quad \text { and } \quad y_{E}^{\prime}=+0.9269 in / rad \text {. }\) (14)

\(x_{E}^{\prime}=-3.4191 in / rad \quad \text { and } \quad y_{E}^{\prime}=0.9269 in / rad .\) (6)

Substituting Eqs. (4) and (6), and the angular velocity and acceleration of the input link into Eq. (5) gives

\(A _{E}=892.32 \hat{ i }-2448.24 \hat{ j } ft / s ^{2}.\)

Therefore, the magnitude of the acceleration of point E is AE = 2 606 ft/s2. This result is in good agreement with the answer in —that is, AE = 2 580 ft/s2.

The acceleration of point F can be obtained in a similar manner. The first-order and second-order kinematic coefficients for point F are

\(x_{F}^{\prime}=-2.6262 in / rad , y_{F}^{\prime}=3.0686 in / rad,\)

 

\(x_{F}^{\prime \prime}=-0.5860 in / rad ^{2}, y_{F}^{\prime \prime}=-2.5517 in / rad ^{2}.\) (7)

Similar to Eq. (5), the acceleration of point F can be written as

\(A _{F}=\left(x_{F}^{\prime \prime} \omega_{2}^{2}+x_{F}^{\prime} \alpha_{2}\right) \hat{ i }+\left(y_{F}^{\prime \prime} \omega_{2}^{2}+y_{F}^{\prime} \alpha_{2}\right) \hat{ j }.\) (8)

Substituting Eqs. (7) and the angular velocity and acceleration of the input into Eq. (8), the acceleration of point F is

\(A _{F}=-433 \hat{ i }-1886 \hat{ j } ft / s ^{2}.\)

Therefore, the magnitude of the acceleration of point F is AF = 1 935 ft/s2. This result is in good agreement with the answer of —that is, AF = 1 960 ft/s2.
