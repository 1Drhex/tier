Guest Question.

Find the smallest positive, nonzero root of \(f (x) = e^{x} cos x − 1\) within five significant digits. Use (1) Newton’s method; and (2) the secant method.

Admin
Solution

To obtain an approximate value of the desired root, we plot f (x) in increments of
Δx = 0.5, starting at x = 0 and ending after f (x) reverses its sign. The results are shown in the figure. By inspection we estimate the root to be approximately x = 1.3.

Part 1: Newton’s Method

Newton’s method requires the derivative of f (x), which is

\(\acute{f}(x)=\frac{d}{dx}(e^{x}\cos x-1)=e^{x}(\cos x-\sin x)\)

The following table shows the computations for the root, based on the algorithm given in Eq. (B.1):

estimate x

do until \(\left|\Delta x\right|\lt \epsilon\)

\(\Delta x\longleftarrow -\frac{f(x)}{\acute{f}(x) }\)

 

\(x\longleftarrow x+\Delta x\)

end do. The starting value was x = 1.3.

 

Δx \(\acute{f}(x)\) f(x) x
−0.007 23 −2.554 05 −0.018 47 1.3
−0.000 07 −2.503 16 −0.000 18 1.292 77
0.000 00 −2.502 64 0.000 00 1.292 70

The final result, x = 1.2927, has been obtained in only two iterations because of the accuracy of our initial estimate.

Part 2: Secant Method

We chose \(x_{1} = 1.2\) and \(x_{2} = 1.3\) as the starting values of the root. The algorithm in Eq. (B.2):

estimate \(x_{1}\) and \(x_{2}\)

 

do until \(\left|\Delta x\right|\lt \epsilon\)

 

\(\Delta x\longleftarrow -f(x_{2})\frac{x_{1}-x_{2}}{f(x_{1})-f(x_{2})}\)

 

\(x_{3}\longleftarrow x_{2}+\Delta x\)

 

\(x_{1}\longleftarrow x_{2}\)

 

\(x_{2}\longleftarrow x_{3}\)

end do ,leads to the following sequence of computations.

\(x_{1}\) \(x_{2}\) \(f(x_{1})\) \(f(x_{2})\) Δx \(x_{3}=x_{2}+ Δx\)
1.2 1.3 0.203 07 −0.018 47 −0.008 34 1.291 66
1.3 1.291 66 −0.018 47 0.002 58 0.001 03 1.292 69
1.291 66 1.292 69 0.002 58 0.000 03 0.000 01 1.292 70
1.292 69 1.292 70 0.000 03 0.000 00 0.000 00 1.292 70

 

Referring to the first row in the table, note that \(x_{1}\) and \(x_{2}\) are entered first, and then the remaining entries in the row are computed using the algorithm. In the second row, the values for \(x_{1}\), \(x_{2}\), and f (\(x_{1}\)) are simply copied from the appropriate columns in the first row. This pattern is repeated in subsequent rows.

Once again, the final result (\(x_{3}\)) is 1.2927, with only three iterations required. In general, the secant method converges a little more slowly than Newton’s method.
