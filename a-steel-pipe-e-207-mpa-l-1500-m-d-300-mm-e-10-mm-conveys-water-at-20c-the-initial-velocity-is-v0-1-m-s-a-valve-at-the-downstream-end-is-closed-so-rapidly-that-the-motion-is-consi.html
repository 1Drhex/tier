Guest Question.

A steel pipe (E = 207 MPa, L = 1500 m, D = 300 mm, e = 10 mm) conveys water at 20°C.The initial velocity is \(V_{0} = 1 m/s\). A valve at the downstream end is closed so rapidly that the motion is considered to be instantaneous, reducing the velocity to zero. Determine the pressure pulse wave speed in the pipe, the speed of sound in an unbounded water medium, the pressure rise at the valve, the time it takes for the wave to travel from the valve to the reservoir at the upstream end, and the period of oscillation.

Admin
Solution

The density and bulk modulus of water at \(20^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\) are found in Table B.1: \(\rho=998 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\) and \(B=220 \times 10^{7} \mathrm{~Pa}\). The pressure wave speed, \(a\), is given by Eq. 11.5.16:

\(a=\sqrt{\frac{B / \rho}{1+(D / e)(B / E)}} (11.5.16)\)

\(a=\sqrt{\frac{220 \times 10^{7} / 998}{1+\frac{0.3}{0.01} \times \frac{220 \times 10^{7}}{207 \times 10^{9}}}}=1290 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\)

The speed of sound in an unbounded water medium is found by use of Eq. \(1.5 .12\) to be

\(c=\sqrt{\left.\frac{\Delta p}{\Delta \rho}\right|_{T}}=\sqrt{\frac{B}{\rho}} (1.5.12)\)

\(a=\sqrt{\frac{220 \times 10^{7}}{998}}=1485 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\)

Note that the sound speed is about \(15 \%\) larger than the pressure wave speed. To compute the pressure rise at the valve, we recognize that the reduction in velocity is \(-V_{0}=-1 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\). Using Eq. 11.5.12, the increase in pressure upstream of the valve is

\(\Delta p = – \rho a \Delta V (11.5.12)\)

\(\Delta p=-998 \times 1290 \times(-1)=1.29 \times 10^{6} \mathrm{~Pa} \quad \text { or } \quad 1290 \mathrm{kPa}\)

The wave travel time from the valve to the reservoir is \(L l a=1500 / 1290=1.16 \mathrm{~s}\), and the period of oscillation is \(4 L / a=4 \times 1.16=4.65 \mathrm{~s}\).
