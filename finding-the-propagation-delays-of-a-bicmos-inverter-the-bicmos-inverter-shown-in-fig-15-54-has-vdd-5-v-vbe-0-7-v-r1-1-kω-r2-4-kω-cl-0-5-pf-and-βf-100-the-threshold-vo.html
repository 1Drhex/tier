Guest Question.

Finding the propagation delays of a BiCMOS inverter The BiCMOS inverter shown in Fig. 15.54 has \( V_{ DD }=5 V , V_{ BE }=0.7 V , R_{1}=1 k \Omega, R_{2}=4 k \Omega, C_{ L }=0.5 pF \text {, and } \beta_{ F }=100 \). The threshold voltages are \( V_{ tP }=-1 V , V_{ tN }=1 V \text {, and } K_{ p }=20 \mu A / V ^{2} \text {. Assume } V_{ OL }=0 \text { and } V_{ OH }=5 V \).

(a) Calculate \( t_{ pLH }, t_{ pHL }, \text { and } t_{ pd } \).

(b) Use PSpice/SPICE to plot the VTC and transient response.

Admin
Solution

(a) Using Eq. (15.96), we have

\( t_{ pLH } \approx \frac{\left(V_{ OL }+V_{ OH }\right) C_{ L }}{2 I_{ E }}=\frac{V_{ DD } C_{ L }}{2 \beta_{ F } K_{ p }\left(V_{ DD }-V_{ BE }-\left|V_{ tP }\right|\right)^{2}} \) (15.96)

 

\(\begin{aligned}t_{ pLH } &=\frac{5 V \times 0.5 pF }{2 \times 100 \times 20 \mu A / V ^{2} \times(5 V -0.7 V -1 V )^{2}}=0.06 ns \\t_{ pd } &=t_{ pHL }=t_{ pLH }=0.06 ns\end{aligned}\)

Although \( t_{ pLH } \) is independent of the L ⁄ W ratio of the MOSFETs, in PSpice its value will depend on the ratio L ⁄ W. This causes the difference between the calculated value in part (a) and the PSpice value in part (b).

(b) The PSpice schematic is shown in Fig. 15.55. The PSpice plot of the VTC, shown in Fig. 15.56(a), gives \( V_{ IL }=2.125 V , V_{ IH }=2.875 V , V_{ OL }=0 \text { at } V_{ OH }=5 V , \text { and } V_{ I (\operatorname{tran} 1)}=2.5 V \) (expected value is 2.5 V). The transient response is shown in Fig. 15.56(b), which gives \( t_{ pHL }=2.93 ns , t_{ pLH }=0.65 ns \text {, and } t_{ pd }=1.79 ns \), which is lower than \( t_{ pd }=3.91 \) ns for the CMOS in .

NOTE: Figure 15.56 illustrates the very sharp rise and fall times that occur while the BJTs are conducting. The output signal is nearly \( V_{ DD } \), and the noise margins for this circuit are high, both being more than 2 V.
