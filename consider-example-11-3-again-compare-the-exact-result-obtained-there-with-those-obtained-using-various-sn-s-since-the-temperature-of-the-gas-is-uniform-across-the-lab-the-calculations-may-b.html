Guest Question.

Consider again. Compare the exact result obtained there with those obtained using various \(S_n’s\). Since the temperature of the gas is uniform across the lab, the calculations may be made by treating the entire slab as a single elemental slab. We shall calculate the flux leaving at the right using the \(S_4\) method. Similar calculations may be performed for other \(S_n ’s\) also.

The intensity of radiation leaving the black wall at the left is

\(I\left(\tau =0\right)=\frac{5.67 \times 10 ^{−8} \times 400^4}{\pi}=462.03 W/m^2sr\)

The black body intensity corresponding to gas temperature is given by

\(I_g=\frac{5.67 \times 10 ^{−8} \times 350^4}{\pi}=270.84 W/m^2sr\)

Along the slant path \(μ_1 = 0.295876\), the intensity leaving at the right is given by

\(I_{\mu 1}\left(\tau =0.1\right)=462.03e^{-\frac{0.1}{0.295876}}+270.84\left(1-e^{-\frac{0.1}{0.295876}}\right) =407.20 W/m^2 sr\)

Along the slant path \(μ_2 = 0.908248\), the intensity leaving at the right is given by

\(I_{\mu 2}\left(\tau =0.1\right)=462.03e^{-\frac{0.1}{0.908248}}+270.84\left(1-e^{-\frac{0.1}{0.908248}}\right) =442.10 W/m^2 sr\)

With these two values of leaving intensities, the forward flux leaving the gas slab is given by

\(q^+\left(\tau =0.1\right)=4.188790 \times 0.295876 \times 407.20 + 2.094395\times\)

\(\times 0.908248 \times 442.10 = 1345.64 W/m^2\)

This compares pretty well with the exact value obtained in of \(q^+\left(\tau =0.1, exact\right)1352.57 W/m^2\). The following table compares the exact and the various \(S_n\) results .
