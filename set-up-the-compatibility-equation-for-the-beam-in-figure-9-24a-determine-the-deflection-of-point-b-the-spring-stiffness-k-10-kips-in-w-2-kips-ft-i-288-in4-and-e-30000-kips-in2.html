Guest Question.

Set up the compatibility equation for the beam in Figure 9.24a. Determine the deflection of point B. The spring stiffness \( K=10\frac{kips}{in.}\), \( w=2\frac{kips}{ft}\) , \( I=288in.^{4} \) ,and \( E=30000\frac{kips}{in.^{2}}\)

Admin
Solution

Figure 9.24b shows the released structure loaded with the applied load and the redundant. For clarity the spring is displaced laterally to the right, but the displacement is labeled zero to indicate that the spring is actually located directly under the tip of the beam. Following the previously established sign convention (i.e., the direction of the redundant establishes the positive direction for the displacements), displacements of the right end of the beam are positive when up and negative when down. Deflection of the spring is positive downward. Because the tip of the beam and the spring are connected, they both deflect the same amount \( \Delta _{B}\), that is,

 

\( \Delta _{B,beam}=\Delta _{B,spring}\) (1)

 

Using Equation 9.13, we can write \( \Delta _{B}\) of the spring as

 

\( \Delta = \frac{P}{K}\) (9.13 )

 

\( \Delta _{B,spring}=\frac{X_{B}}{K} \) (2)

 

and substituting Equation 2 into Equation 1 gives us

 

\( \Delta _{B,beam}=-\frac{X_{B}}{K} \) (3)

 

The minus sign is added to the right side of Equation 3 because the end of the beam displaces downward. If \( \Delta _{B,beam} \) (the left side of Equation 3) is evaluated by superimposing the displacements of the B end of the beam in Figure 9.24c and d, we can write Equation 3 as

 

\( \Delta _{B0}+\delta _{1}X_{B}=-\frac{X_{B}}{K} \) (4)

 

Using Figure 9.3 to evaluate \( \Delta _{B0} \) and \( \delta _{1} \) in Equation 4, we compute \( X_{B} \)

 

\( -\frac{wL^{4}}{8EI}+\frac{L^{3}}{3EI}X_{B}=-\frac{X_{B}}{K} \)

 

Substituting the specified values of the variables into the equation above,
we obtain

 

\( -\frac{2\left ( 18 \right )^{4}\left ( 1728 \right )}{8\left ( 30000 \right )\left ( 288 \right )}+\frac{\left ( 18 \right )^{3}\left ( 1728 \right )}{3\left ( 30000 \right )\left ( 288 \right )}X_{B}=-\frac{X_{B}}{10}\)

 

\( X_{B}=10.71 \) kips

 

If support B had been a roller and no settlement had occurred, the right side of Equation 4 would equal zero and \( X_{B} \) would increase to 13.46 kips and

 

\( \Delta _{B,spring}=-\frac{X_{B}}{K} =-\frac{10.71}{10}=1.071 \) in.
