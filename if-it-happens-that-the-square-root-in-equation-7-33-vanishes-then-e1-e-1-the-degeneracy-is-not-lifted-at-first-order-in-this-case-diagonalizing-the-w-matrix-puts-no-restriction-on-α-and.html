Guest Question.

If it happens that the square root in Equation 7.33 vanishes, then \( E_{+}^{1}=E_{-}^{1} \) ; the degeneracy is not lifted at first order. In this case, diagonalizing the W matrix puts no restriction on α and β and you still don’t know what the “good” states are. If you need to determine the “good” states— for example to calculate higher-order corrections—you need to use second -order degenerate perturbation theory.

\( E_{\pm}^{1}=\frac{1}{2}\left[W_{a a}+W_{b b} \pm \sqrt{\left(W_{a a}-W_{b b}\right)^{2}+4\left|W_{a b}\right|^{2}}\right] \) (7.33).

(a) Show that, for the two-fold degeneracy studied in Section 7.2.1, the first-order correction to the wave function in degenerate perturbation theory is

\( \psi^{1}=\sum_{m \neq a, b} \frac{\alpha V_{m a}+\beta V_{m b}}{E^{0}-E_{m}^{0}} \psi_{m}^{0} \).

(b) Consider the terms of order λ² (corresponding to Equation 7.8 in the nondegenerate case) to show that α and β are determined by finding the eigenvectors of the matrix W² (the superscript denotes second order, not W squared) where

\( \left[ W ^{2}\right]_{i j}=\sum_{m \neq a, b} \frac{\left\langle\psi_{i}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{m}^{0}\right\rangle\left\langle\psi_{m}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{j}^{0}\right\rangle}{E^{0}-E_{m}^{0}} \).

\( H^{0} \psi_{n}^{2}+H^{\prime} \psi_{n}^{1}=E_{n}^{0} \psi_{n}^{2}+E_{n}^{1} \psi_{n}^{1}+E_{n}^{2} \psi_{n}^{0} \) (7.8).

and that the eigenvalues of this matrix correspond to the second-order energies E².

(c) Show that second-order degenerate perturbation theory, developed in , gives the correct energies to second order for the three-state Hamiltonian in .

Solution

(a) In Equation 7.10, let \( \psi_{n} \rightarrow \psi \equiv \alpha \psi_{a}+\beta \psi_{b} \):

\( \left(H^{0}-E_{n}^{0}\right) \psi_{n}^{1}=-\left(H^{\prime}-E_{n}^{1}\right) \psi_{n}^{0} \) (7.10).

\( \left(H^{0}-E^{0}\right) \psi^{1}=-\left(H^{\prime}-E^{1}\right) \psi^{0} \).

Now take the inner product with \( \psi_{m}^{0}, \text { where } m \neq a, b \):

\( E_{m}^{0}\left\langle\psi_{m}^{0} \mid \psi^{1}\right\rangle-E^{0}\left\langle\psi_{m}^{0} \mid \psi^{1}\right\rangle=-\left\langle\psi_{m}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi^{0}\right\rangle+ E^1 \cancel{〈ψ^0_m | ψ^0〉} \),

so

\( \left\langle\psi_{m}^{0} \mid \psi^{1}\right\rangle=\frac{\left\langle\psi_{m}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi^{0}\right\rangle}{E^{0}-E_{m}^{0}}=\frac{\alpha H_{m a}^{\prime}+\beta H_{m b}^{\prime}}{E^{0}-E_{m}^{0}} \), and (Equation 7.13) \( \psi^{1}=\sum_{m \neq a, b} \frac{\alpha H_{m a}^{\prime}+\beta H_{m b}^{\prime}}{E^{0}-E_{m}^{0}} \psi_{m}^{0} \).

\( \psi_{n}^{1}=\sum_{m \neq n} \frac{\left\langle\psi_{m}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{n}^{0}\right\rangle}{\left(E_{n}^{0}-E_{m}^{0}\right)} \psi_{m}^{0} \) (7.13).

(b) Similarly, write Equation 7.8 for \( \psi_{n} \rightarrow \psi \):

\( H^{0} \psi^{2}+H^{\prime} \psi^{1}=E^{0} \psi^{2}+E^{1} \psi^{1}+E^{2} \psi^{0} \).

\( H^{0} \psi_{n}^{2}+H^{\prime} \psi_{n}^{1}=E_{n}^{0} \psi_{n}^{2}+E_{n}^{1} \psi_{n}^{1}+E_{n}^{2} \psi_{n}^{0} \) (7.8).

and take the inner product with \( \psi_{a}^{0} \text { and } \psi_{b}^{0} \):

\( \cancel {E^0 〈ψ^0_a|ψ^2〉} + \left\langle\psi_{a}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi^{1}\right\rangle=\cancel {E^0 〈ψ^0_a|ψ^2〉} + E^{1}\left\langle\psi_{a}^{0} \mid \psi^{1}\right\rangle+E^{2}\left\langle\psi_{a}^{0} \mid \psi^{0}\right\rangle \).

As before (Equation 7.11) we might as well choose \( \left\langle\psi_{a}^{0} \mid \psi^{1}\right\rangle=0 . \text { And }\left\langle\psi_{a}^{0} \mid \psi^{0}\right\rangle=\alpha \). so

\( \left\langle\psi_{a}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi^{1}\right\rangle=E^{2} \alpha, \text { and similarly }\left\langle\psi_{b}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi^{1}\right\rangle=E^{2} \beta \).

\( \psi_{n}^{1}=\sum_{m \neq n} c_{m}^{(n)} \psi_{m}^{0} \) (7.11).

Putting in the result of part (a):

\( \sum_{m \neq a, b} \frac{\alpha H_{m a}^{\prime}+\beta H_{m b}^{\prime}}{E^{0}-E_{m}^{0}} H_{a m}^{\prime}=E^{2} \alpha, \quad \sum_{m \neq a, b} \frac{\alpha H_{m a}^{\prime}+\beta H_{m b}^{\prime}}{E^{0}-E_{m}^{0}} H_{b m}^{\prime}=E^{2} \beta \).

This has the form of an eigenvalue equation:

\( \underbrace{\sum_{m \neq a, b} \frac{1}{E^{0}-E_{m}^{0}}\left(\begin{array}{cc} \left|H_{m a}^{\prime}\right|^{2} & H_{a m}^{\prime} H_{m b}^{\prime} \\ H_{b m}^{\prime} H_{m a}^{\prime} & \left|H_{m b}^{\prime}\right|^{2} \end{array}\right)}_{ W ^{2}}\left(\begin{array}{l} \alpha \\ \beta \end{array}\right)=E^{2}\left(\begin{array}{c} \alpha \\ \beta \end{array}\right) \).

(c) In the case of the matrix is

\( W ^{2}=\frac{1}{\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{c}}\left(\begin{array}{ll} |V|^{2} & |V|^{2} \\ |V|^{2} & |V|^{2} \end{array}\right) \).

Its (normalized) eigenvectors are

\( \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ \pm 1 \end{array}\right), \quad \text { so the "good" states are } \psi_{\pm}^{0}=\frac{\psi_{a}^{0} \pm \psi_{b}^{0}}{\sqrt{2}} \).

and the second-order correction to the energies are the eigenvalues

\( E_{+}^{2}=2 \frac{|V|^{2}}{\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{c}}, \quad E_{-}^{2}=0 \).

This agrees precisely with the result in (b), obtained by Taylor expansion of the exact energies.
