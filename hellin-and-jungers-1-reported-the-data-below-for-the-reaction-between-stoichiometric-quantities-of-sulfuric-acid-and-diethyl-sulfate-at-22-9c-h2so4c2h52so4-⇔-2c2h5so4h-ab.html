Guest Question.

Hellin and Jungers (1) reported the data below for the reaction between stoichiometric quantities of sulfuric acid and diethyl sulfate at \(22.9^{\circ}\)C:

\(H_{2}SO_{4}+(C_{2}H_{5})_{2}SO_{4}\leftrightarrow 2C_{2}H_{5}SO_{4}H\)

A+B\(\leftrightarrow\) 2R

The initial concentrations of the reactants are each 5.50 kmol/\(m^{3}\)

Time \(C_{2}H_{5}SO_{4}H\)
(s) (kmol/\(m^{3}\))
0 0
1,680 0.69
2,880 1.38
4,500 2.24
5,760 2.75
7,620 3.31
9,720 3.81
10,800 4.11
12,720 4.45
16,020 4.86
19,080 5.15
22,740 5.35
24,600 5.42

After 11 days the concentration of \(C_{2}H_{5}SO_{4}H\) is equal to approximately 5.80 kmol/ \(m^{3}\).

Hellin and Jungers determined the following rate expression for this reaction:

\(r=k_{f}[(C_{2}H_{5})_{2}SO_{4}][H_{2}SO_{4}]-k_{r}[C_{2}H_{5}SO_{4}H]^{2}\)

\(=k_{f}(A)(B)-k_{r}(R)^{2}\) (A)

Is the rate expression proposed consistent with the data above? If so, what are the values of the rate constants at this temperature?

Admin
Solution

Because the rate expression simplifies when stoichiometric quantities of reactants are used, the equations developed earlier in this chapter cannot be applied directly in the solution of this problem. Thus, we will have to derive appropriate relations in the course of our analysis. Each of the reactant concentrations is given by

 

\(A=B=A_{0}-\xi ^{\ast }\)

 

while the concentration of the product is given by

 

\(R=2\xi ^{\ast }\)

 

The long-time data may be used to determine the equilibrium concentrations of all species and the equilibrium constant for the reaction:

 

\(R_{e}=2\xi _{e}^{\ast }=5.80\) kmol/\(m^{3}\)

 

Hence,

 

\(\xi _{e}^{\ast }=2.90\) kmol/\(m^{3}/\)

 

and

 

\(A_{e}=B_{e}=5.50-2.90=2.60\) kmol/\(m^{3}\)

 

The equilibrium constant for this reaction is given by

 

\(K=\frac{k_{f}}{k_{r}}=\frac{R_{e}^{2}}{A_{e}B_{e}}=\frac{(5.80)^{2}}{(2.60)^{2}}=4.98\) (B)

 

Hence,

 

\(k_{f}=4.98k_{r}\) (C)

 

Equations of the form of (5.1.26) may now be written for each species:

 

\(B=A=A_{e}-(\xi ^{\ast }-\xi _{e}^{\ast }),R=R_{e}+2(\xi ^{\ast }-\xi _{e}^{\ast })\) (D)

 

Substitution of these relations into equation (A) gives

 

\(\frac{d\xi ^{\ast }}{dt}=k_{f}[A_{e}-(\xi ^{\ast }-\xi _{e}^{\ast })]^{2}-k_{r}[R_{e}+2(\xi ^{\ast }-\xi _{e}^{\ast })]^{2}\)

 

Expansion and use of equation (5.1.30) gives

 

\(\frac{d(\xi ^{\ast }-\xi _{e}^{\ast })}{dt}=k_{f}A_{e}^{2}-k_{r}R_{e}^{2}-2k_{f}A_{e}(\xi ^{\ast }-\xi _{e}^{\ast })-4k_{r}R_{e}(\xi ^{\ast }-\xi _{e}^{\ast })+k_{f}(\xi ^{\ast }-\xi _{e}^{\ast })^{2}-4k_{r}(\xi ^{\ast }-\xi _{e}^{\ast })^{2}\)

 

The first two terms on the right represent the net rate of reaction at equilibrium, which must be zero. Hence,

 

\(\frac{d(\xi ^{\ast }-\xi _{e}^{\ast })}{dt}=-(2k_{f}A_{e}+4k_{r}R_{e})(\xi ^{\ast }-\xi _{e}^{\ast })+(k_{f}-4k_{r})(\xi ^{\ast }-\xi _{e}^{\ast })^{2}\)

 

Separation of variables and integration give

 

\(t=\left ( \frac{1}{2k_{f}A_{e}+4k_{r}R_{e}} \right )\times\)

 

\(\left \{ \ln \left [ \frac{(k_{f}-4k_{r})(\xi ^{\ast }-\xi _{e}^{\ast })-(2k_{f}A_{e}+4k_{r}R_{e})}{\xi ^{\ast }-\xi _{e}^{\ast }} \right ] \right.\)

 

\(\left. -\ln \left [ \frac{(k_{f}-4k_{r})(-\xi _{e}^{\ast })-(2k_{f}A_{e}+4k_{r}R_{e})}{-\xi _{e}^{\ast }} \right ] \right \}\) (E)

 

Combination of equations (B) and (E) and rearrangement gives

 

\(t=\frac{1}{k_{r}(2KA_{e}+4R_{e})}\left \{ \ln \left [ \frac{\xi _{e}^{\ast }}{\xi _{e}^{\ast }-\xi ^{\ast }} \right ] \right.\)

 

\(+\ln \left. \left [ \frac{(K-4)(\xi ^{\ast }-\xi_{e}^{\ast } )-(2KA_{e}+4R_{e})}{(K-4)(-\xi _{e}^{\ast }-(2KA_{e}+4R_{e}))} \right ] \right \}\) (F)

 

Now

 

\(2KA_{e}+4R_{e}=2(4.98)(2.60)+4(5.80)\)

 

= 49.10 kmol/\(m^{3}\)

 

Substitution of numerical values into equation (F) gives

 

\(t=\frac{1}{49.10k_{r}}\left \{ \ln \left ( \frac{2.90}{2.90-\xi ^{\ast }} \right ) \right.\)

 

\(\left. +\ln \left [ \frac{(4.98-4)(\xi ^{\ast }-2.90)-49.10}{(4.98-4)(-2.90)-49.10} \right ] \right \}\)

 

or

 

\(49.1k_{r}t=\ln \left [ \frac{2.90}{51.94}\left ( \frac{51.94+0.98\xi ^{\ast }}{2.90-\xi ^{\ast }} \right ) \right ]\)

 

\(=\ln \left [ \frac{1}{18.28} \left ( \frac{53.00+\xi ^{\ast }}{2.90-\xi ^{\ast }} \right )\right ]\)

 

Hence, a plot of \(\ln [(53.00+\xi ^{\ast })/(2.90-\xi ^{\ast })]\) versus time should be linear if the proposed rate expression provides an accurate representation of the experimental results. The data were worked up in this manner and plotted in Figure I5.1. The linearity of the plot indicates that the proposed rate expression is consistent with the data. The slope of the plot is equal to \(1.154\times 10^{-4}s^{-1}\). Because this value is also equal to \(49.1k_{r}\) kmol/\(m^{3}\), \(k_{r}1.154\times 10^{-4}/49.1=2.35\times 10^{-6}m_{3}/(kmol\cdot s)\). From equation (C),

 

\(k_{f}=4.98(2.35\times 10^{-6})=1.17\times 10^{-5}m^{3}/(Kmol\cdot s)\)
