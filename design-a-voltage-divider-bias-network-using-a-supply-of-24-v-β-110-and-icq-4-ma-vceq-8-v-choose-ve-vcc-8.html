Guest Question.

Design a voltage divider bias network using a supply of 24 V, β= 110 and \(I_{CQ}\) = 4 mA \(V_{CEQ}\) = 8 V. Choose \(V_{E}\) = \(V_{CC}\) / 8.

Admin
Solution

Given: \(I_{CQ} = 4 mA, V_{CEQ} = 8 V, V_{E} = V_{CC} / 8, V_{CC} = 24 V, \beta = 110\)

(a) To determine \(I_{B}, I_{E} and V_{E} \):

\(I_{B}=\frac{I_{CQ} }{\beta }=\frac{4\times 10^{3} }{110}=36.36 \mu A \)

\(I_{E} = I_{B} + I_{C} = 36.36 × 10 ^{– 6} + 4 × 10^{ – 3} = 4.03636 mA\)

\(V_{E}=\frac{V_{CC} }{8}=\frac{24}{8} =3 V \)

(b) To determine \(R_{E} and R_{2}:\)

\(R_{E}=\frac{V_{E} }{I_{E} }=\frac{0.3}{4.03636\times 10^{-3} }=743.244 \Omega \)

Applying KVL to the collector circuit,

\(V_{CC} – I_{C}R_{C} – V_{CE} – V_{E} = 0\)

Therefore, \(R_{C}=\frac{V_{CC}-V_{CE}-V_{E}}{I_{C}}=\frac{24-8-3}{4\times 10^{-3} }=3.25 K \Omega \)

(c) To determine \(R_{1} and R_{2}\)

\(V_{B} = V_{E} + V_{BE} = 3 + 0.7 = 3.7 V\)

Referring to Fig. 6.38 consider the current through \(R_{1}\) be I + \(I_{B}\) and that through \(R_{2}\) be I. Resistors \(R_{1}\) and \(R_{2}\) forms the potential divider. For proper operation of potential divider, current I should be atleast ten times the \(I_{B}\). i.e. I ≥ 10 \(I_{B}.\) Therefore,

\(I = 10 I_{B} = 10 × 36.36 × 10^{ – 6} = 363.6 \mu A\)

 

\(R_{2}=\frac{V_{B} }{I}=\frac{3.7}{363.6\times 10^{-6} }=10.176 K \Omega \)

 

\(R_{1}=\frac{V_{CC}-V_{B} }{I+I_{B} } =\frac{24-3.7}{(363.6+.36.36)\times 10^{-6}} =50.755 K \Omega \)
