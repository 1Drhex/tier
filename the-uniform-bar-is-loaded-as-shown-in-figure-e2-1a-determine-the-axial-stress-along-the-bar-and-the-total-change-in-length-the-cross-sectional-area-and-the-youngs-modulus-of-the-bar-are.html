Guest Question.

The uniform bar is loaded as shown in Figure E2.1(a). Determine the axial stress along the bar and the total change in length. The cross-sectional area and the Young’s modulus of the bar are, respectively, \(A=1 cm ^{2} \text { and } E=140 GPa\).

Admin
Solution

This is a uniform bar with forces applied between the two ends. The internal forces along the bar are, therefore, not constant. The stresses and elongation between A and B, B and C, and C and D must be calculated individually and the total change in length is equal to the sum of all the elongations. The method of section is used to determine the internal forces.

Between A and B:

From the equilibrium of Figure E2.1(b):

 

\(\begin{aligned}&N_{ AB }-5 kN =0 \\&N_{ AB }=5 kN\end{aligned}\)

 

The normal stress between A and B (Equation (2.1)):

 

\(\sigma=\frac{F}{A}\) (2.1a)

 

\(\varepsilon=\frac{\sigma}{E}=\frac{F}{E A}\) (2.1b)

 

\(\sigma_{ AB }=\frac{N_{ AB }}{A}=\frac{5 kN }{0.0001 m ^{2}}=5 \times 10^{4} kN / m ^{2}=50 MPa\)

 

The elongation between A and B (Equation (2.5)):

 

\(\Delta l=l_{1}-l=\frac{N l}{E A}\) (2.5)

 

\(\Delta_{ AB }=\frac{N_{ AB } L_{ AB }}{E A}=\frac{5 kN \times 0.5 m }{140 \times 10^{6} kN / m ^{2} \times 0.0001 m ^{2}}=178.57 \times 10^{-6} m\)

 

Between B and C:

 

From the equilibrium of Figure E2.1(c):

 

\(\begin{aligned}&N_{A B}+8 k N-5 k N=0 \\&N_{A B}=-8 k N+5 k N=-3 k N \text { (compression) }\end{aligned}\)

 

The normal force should be in the opposite direction of the assumed \(N_{B C}\).

The normal stress between B and C (Equation (2.1)):

 

\(\sigma_{ BC }=\frac{N_{ BC }}{A}=\frac{-3 kN }{0.0001 m ^{2}}=-3 \times 10^{4} kN / m ^{2}=-30 MPa\)

 

The elongation between B and C (Equation (2.5)):

 

\(\Delta_{ BC }=\frac{N_{ BC } L_{ BC }}{E A}=\frac{-3 kN \times 0.75 m }{140 \times 10^{6} kN / m ^{2} \times 0.0001 m ^{2}}=-160.71 \times 10^{-6} m\)

 

Between C and D:

From the equilibrium of Figure E2.1(d):

 

\(\begin{aligned}&N_{ CD }+8 kN +4 kN -5 kN =0 \\&N_{ AB }=-8 kN -4 kN +5 kN =-7 kN \text { (compression) }\end{aligned}\)

 

The normal force should be in the opposite direction of the assumed \(N_{CD}\)

The normal stress between C and D (Equation (2.1)):

 

\(\sigma_{ CD }=\frac{N_{ CD }}{A}=\frac{-7 kN }{0.0001 m ^{2}}=-7 \times 10^{4} kN / m ^{2}=-70 MPa\)

 

The elongation between C and D (Equation (2.5)):

 

\(\Delta_{ CD }=\frac{N_{ CD L_{C D}}}{E A}=\frac{-7 kN \times 1.0 m }{140 \times 10^{6} kN / m ^{2} \times 0.0001 m ^{2}}=-500 \times 10^{-6} m\)

 

The total change in length of the bar is:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta_{\text {Total }} &=\Delta_{ AB }+\Delta_{ BC }+\Delta_{ CD }=178.57 \times 10^{-6} m -160.71 \times 10^{-6} m -500 \times 10^{-6} m \\&=-482.14 \times 10^{-6} m =-0.482 mm\end{aligned}\)

 

The negative sign means the change in length is contraction.
