Guest Question.

Two large parallel plates are exchanging radiation. One plate has a temperature of 1000 K and an emissivity of 0.8. The other plate is at 350 K and has an emissivity of 0.4.
(a) What is the radiant heat flux between the plates?
(b) It is desired to reduce the heat transfer between the plates by inserting a thin radiation shield between them. The shield has an emissivity of 0.1 on one side and 0.05 on the other. What is the new rate of heat transfer after the shield is in place, and what is the temperature of the shield?
(c) An additional shield like the one of Part (b) is placed in the system to further reduce the heat transfer. What is the resulting heat flux, and what are the temperatures of the two shields?

Admin
Solution

(a) \(ε_{1} = 0.8 ; ε_{2} = 0.4 ; T_{1} = 1000 K ; T_{2} = 350 K.\)
Using Eq. (9.51),

 

\(q_{1} = \frac{\sigma A (T^4_{1} – T^4_{2})}{\frac{1}{\varepsilon _{1}} + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{2}} – 1 }\) (9.51)

 

\(\frac{q_{1}}{A} = \frac{\sigma A (T^4_{1} – T^4_{2})}{\frac{1}{\varepsilon _{1}} + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{2}} – 1 } = \frac{5.67 \times 10^{- 8} (1000^4 – 350^4)}{\frac{1}{0.8} + \frac{1}{0.4} – 1 } = 20310 W / m^2\)

 

(b) Consider Fig. 9.13. For this problem, \(ε_{1} = 0.8 ; ε_{2} = 0.4 ; ε_{31} = 0.1 ; and ε_{32} = 0.05.\)

\(T_{1} = 1000 K and T_{2} = 350 K.\)

Using Eq. (9.70),

 

\(T_{3} = \left[\frac{\left(\frac{1}{\varepsilon _{32} } + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{2}} – 1 \right)T^4_{1} + \left(\frac{1}{\varepsilon _{1} } + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{31}} – 1 \right)T^4_{2} }{\frac{1}{\varepsilon _{1}} + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{2}} + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{31}} + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{32}} – 2 } \right]^{0.25} = \left[\frac{\left(\frac{1}{0.05} + \frac{1}{0.4} – 1 \right) 1000^4 + \left(\frac{1}{0.8} + \frac{1}{0.1} – 1 \right) 350^4 }{\frac{1}{0.8} + \frac{1}{0.4} + \frac{1}{0.1} + \frac{1}{0.05} – 2 } \right]^{0.25}\)

 

\(T_{3} = 908.8 K\)

Now that the shield temperature is known, one of the relations in Eq. (9.69) may be used to get the heat flux.

 

\(q_{1 \rightarrow 3} = \frac{\sigma A (T^4_{1} – T^4_{3})}{\frac{1}{\varepsilon _{1}} + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{31}} – 1 } = q_{3 \rightarrow 2} = \frac{\sigma A (T^4_{3} – T^4_{2})}{\frac{1}{\varepsilon _{32}} + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{2}} – 1 }\) (9.69)

 

\(\frac{q_{1 \rightarrow 2}}{A} = \frac{\sigma (T^4_{1} – T^4_{3})}{\frac{1}{\varepsilon _{1}} + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{31}} – 1 } = \frac{\sigma (T^4_{3} – T^4_{2})}{\frac{1}{\varepsilon _{32}} + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{2}} – 1 }\)

 

Using the first relation, we have

 

\(\frac{q_{1 \rightarrow 2}}{A} = \frac{5.67 \times 10^{- 8} (1000^4 – 908.8^4)}{\frac{1}{0.8} + \frac{1}{0.1} – 1 } = 1760 W / m^2\)

 

Alternatively, Eq.(9.72) , which was obtained from the circuit diagram, could have been used to get the heat flux without having to first find the shield temperature.

 

\(q_{1 \rightarrow 2} = \frac{\sigma A (T^4_{1} – T^4_{2})}{\left({\frac{1}{\varepsilon _{1}} + \frac{1} {\varepsilon _{2}}} – 1\right) + \left({\frac{1}{\varepsilon _{31}} + \frac{1} {\varepsilon _{32}}} – 1\right) } \) (9.72)

 

\(\frac{q_{1 \rightarrow 2}}{A} = \frac{\sigma (T^4_{1} – T^4_{2})}{\left({\frac{1}{\varepsilon _{1}} + \frac{1} {\varepsilon _{2}}} – 1\right) + \left({\frac{1}{\varepsilon _{31}} + \frac{1} {\varepsilon _{32}}} – 1\right) } = \frac{5.67 \times 10^{- 8} (1000^4 – 350^4)}{\left(\frac{1}{0.8} + \frac{1}{0.4} – 1 \right) + \left(\frac{1}{0.1} + \frac{1}{0.05} – 1 \right)} = 1760 W / m^2 \)

 

The temperature of the shield is 908.8 K and the heat flux is 1760 W/m².

(c) A two-shield system is shown in Fig. 9.15 and the circuit diagram for the system is shown in Fig. 9.16. Let us look at Fig. 9.15.
For this problem, \(ε_{1} = 0.8 ; ε_{2} = 0.4 ; ε_{31} = 0.1 ; ε_{32} = 0.05 ; ε_{41} = 0.1 ; and ε_{42} = 0.05.\)

\(T_{1} = 1000 K and T_{2} = 350 K.\)

We can use the circuit diagram of Fig. 9.16 to get equations to solve for \(q_{1 → 2 } , T_{3} , and T_{4}\). The heat flow is the difference in overall \(E_{b}\) divided by the resistances between the end \(E_{b}s\). That is,

 

\(q_{1 \rightarrow 2} = \frac{\sigma (T^4_{1} – T^4_{2})}{\frac{1 – \varepsilon _{1}}{\varepsilon _{1}A_{1}} + \frac{1}{A_{1} F_{1 – 3}} + \frac{1 – \varepsilon _{31}}{\varepsilon _{31}A_{3}} + \frac{1 – \varepsilon _{32}}{\varepsilon _{32}A_{3}} + \frac{1}{A_{3}F_{3 – 4}} + \frac{1 – \varepsilon _{41}}{\varepsilon _{41}A_{4}} + \frac{1 – \varepsilon _{42}}{\varepsilon _{42}A_{4}} + \frac{1}{A_{4}F_{4 – 2}} + \frac{1 – \varepsilon _{2}}{\varepsilon _{2}A_{2}} } \) (9.76)

 

For this problem, \(A_{1} = A_{2} = A_{3} = A_{4} = A\) and all the shape factors are unity. Eq. (9.76) then reduces to

 

\(q_{1 \rightarrow 2} = \frac{\sigma A(T^4_{1} – T^4_{2})}{\frac{1 }{\varepsilon _{1}} + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{31}} + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{32}} + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{41}} + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{42}} + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{2}} – 3 }\) (9.77)

 

Putting in values for the parameters of Eq. (9.77), we get \(\frac{q_{1 \rightarrow 2}}{A} = 919 W / m^2 .\)

We can also use Fig. 9.16 to get the shield temperatures. For \(T_{3}\) , we can write an equation either between nodes \(E_{b1} and E_{b3}\) or between nodes \(E_{b3} and E_{b2}\). The equation between nodes \(E_{b1} and E_{b3} \) is

 

\(q_{1 \rightarrow 2} = \frac{\sigma (T^4_{1} – T^4_{3})}{\frac{1 – \varepsilon _{1}}{\varepsilon _{1}A_{1}} + \frac{1}{A_{1} F_{1 – 3}} + \frac{1 – \varepsilon _{31}}{\varepsilon _{31}A_{3}} }\) (9.78)

 

With equal areas and unity shape factors, Eq. (9.78) becomes

 

\(\frac{q_{1 \rightarrow 2}}{A} = \frac{\sigma (T^4_{1} – T^4_{3})}{\frac{1 }{\varepsilon _{1}} + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{31}} – 1}\) (9.79)

 

Using the values we have for \(\frac{q_{1 \rightarrow 2}}{A} , T_{1} , ε_{1} , and ε_{31}\) , we can solve Eq. (9.79) for shield temperature \(T_{3}\) . It is found that \(T_{3} = 955.6 K.\)
For shield temperature T_{4} , we can write an equation between nodes \(E_{b4} and E_{b2}\) of Fig. 9.16. This equation is

 

\(q_{1 \rightarrow 2} = \frac{\sigma (T^4_{4} – T^4_{2})}{\frac{1 – \varepsilon _{42}}{\varepsilon _{42}A_{4}} + \frac{1}{A_{4} F_{4 – 2}} + \frac{1 – \varepsilon _{2}}{\varepsilon _{2}A_{2}} }\) (9.80)

 

With equal areas and unity shape factors, Eq. (9.80) becomes

 

\(\frac{q_{1 \rightarrow 2}}{A} = \frac{\sigma (T^4_{4} – T^4_{2})}{\frac{1 }{\varepsilon _{42}} + \frac{1}{\varepsilon _{2}} – 1}\) (9.81)

 

Using the values we have for \(\frac{q_{1 \rightarrow 2}}{A} , T_{2} , ε_{42} , and ε_{2} \), we can solve Eq. (9.81) for shield temperature \(T_{4}\). It is found that \(T_{4} = 776.5 K.\) For the two-shield system, the heat flux is 919 W/m² and the shield temperatures are 955.6 and 776.5 K.
To summarize:
Without radiation shields, the heat flux between the two plates is 20310 W/m². With one shield, the heat flux is 1760 W/m². With two shields, the heat flux is 919 W/m².
It is seen that use of one shield greatly decreases the heat flux. Use of an additional shield further decreases the heat flux but not to as great an extent as achieved by the first shield.
