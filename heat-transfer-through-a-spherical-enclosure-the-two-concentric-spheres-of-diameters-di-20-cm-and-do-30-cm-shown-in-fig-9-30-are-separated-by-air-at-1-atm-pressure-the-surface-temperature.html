Heat Transfer through a Spherical Enclosure

The two concentric spheres of diameters \( D_{i} = 20 cm \) and \( D_{o} = 30 cm \) shown in Fig. 9–30 are separated by air at 1 atm pressure. The surface temperatures of the two spheres enclosing the air are \( T_{i} = 320 K \) and \( T_{o} = 280 K \), respectively. Determine the rate of heat transfer from the inner sphere to the outer sphere by natural convection.

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION Two surfaces of a spherical enclosure are maintained at specified temperatures. The rate of heat transfer through the enclosure is to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Steady operating conditions exist. 2 Air is an ideal gas. 3 Radiation heat transfer is not considered.

Properties The properties of air at the average temperature of \( T_{ave} = \left ( T_{i} + T_{o} \right )/2 = \left ( 320 + 280 \right )/2 = 300 K = 27^{\circ}C \) and 1 atm pressure are (Table A-15)

\( k = 0.02566 W/m \cdot ^{\circ}C Pr = 0.7290\)

 

\( \nu = 1.580 \times 10^{-5} m^{2}/s \beta = \frac{1}{T_{ave}} = \frac{1}{300 K}\)

 

Analysis We have a spherical enclosure filled with air. The characteristic length in this case is the distance between the two spheres,

\( L_{c} = \left ( D_{o} – D_{i} \right )/2 = \left ( 0.3 – 0.2 \right )/2 = 0.05 m \)

 

The Rayleigh number is

\( Ra_{L} = \frac{g \beta \left ( T_{i} – T_{o} \right )L^{3}}{\nu ^{2}} Pr \)

 

\( = \frac{\left ( 9.81 m/s^{2} \right ) \left [ 1/\left ( 300 K \right ) \right ]\left ( 320 – 280 K \right )\left ( 0.05 m \right )^{3} }{\left ( 1.58 \times 10^{-5} m^{2}/s \right )^{2}}\left (0.729 \right) = 4.776 \times 10^{5}\)

 

The effective thermal conductivity is

\( F_{sph} = \frac{L_{c}}{\left ( D_{i}D_{o} \right )^{4}\left ( D_{i}^{-7/5} + D_{o}^{-7/5} \right )^{5}} \)

 

\( = \frac{0.05 m}{ \left [ \left ( 0.2 m \right )\left ( 0.3 m \right ) \right ]^{4} \left [\left ( \left (0.2 m \right )^{-7/5} + \left ( 0.3 m \right )^{-7/5} \right ) \right ]^{5}} = 0.005229 \)

 

\( k_{eff} = 0.74 k \left ( \frac{Pr}{0.861 + Pr} \right )^{1/4} \left ( F_{sph} Ra_{L} \right )^{1/4} = \)

 

\( 0.74 \left ( 0.02566 W/m \cdot ^{\circ}C \right ) \left ( \frac{0.729}{0.861 + 0.729} \right )\left ( 0.005229 \times 4.776 \times 10^{5} \right )^{1/4} \)

 

\( = 0.1104 W/m \cdot ^{\circ}C \)

 

Then the rate of heat transfer between the spheres becomes

\( \dot{Q} = k_{eff} \pi \left ( \frac{D_{i} – D_{o}}{L_{c}} \right ) \left ( T_{i} – T_{o} \right ) \)

 

\( = \left ( 0.1104 W/m \cdot ^{\circ}C \right )\pi \left ( \frac{\left ( 0.2 m \right )\left ( 0.3 m \right )}{0.05 m} \right )\left ( 320 – 280 \right ) K = 16.7 W \)

 

Therefore, heat will be lost from the inner sphere to the outer one at a rate of 16.7 W.

 

Discussion Note that the air in the spherical enclosure will act like a stationary fluid whose thermal conductivity is \(k_{eff} /k = 0.1104/0.02566 = 4.3 \) times that of air as a result of natural convection currents. Also, radiation heat transfer between spheres is usually very significant, and should be considered in a complete analysis.
