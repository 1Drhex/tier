Guest Question.

Compute the inverse z-transform of the functions:

\(F_{1}(z)=\frac{z^{-1}}{(1-\alpha z^{-1})(1-bz^{-1})}=\frac{z^{2}}{(z-\alpha )^{2}(z-b)}\)
\(F_{2}(z)=\frac{2(z^{2}-5z+6.5)}{(z-2)(z-3)^{2}}\), for 2<|z|<3
\(F_{3}(z)=\frac{2z^{2}+3z}{(z+2)(z-4)}\)
\(F_{4}(z)=\frac{z^{3}-20z}{(z-2)^{3}(z-4)} \)

Admin
Solution

a. It is true that :\(f_{1}[n]=\sum{residues[\frac{z^{2}}{(z-a)^{2}(z-b)}z^{n-1} ]}\) (1)

The residue of the simple pole z = b is:

\(\frac{z^{2}z^{n-1}}{(z-a)^{2}}|_{z=b}=\frac{z^{n+1}}{(z-a)^{2}}|_{z=b}=\frac{b^{n+1}}{(b-a)^{2}}\)(2)

The residue of the double pole z = a is:

\(\frac{d}{dz} (\frac{z^{2}}{z-b}z^{n-1} )|_{z=\alpha }=\frac{[n+1]z^{n}(z-b)-z^{n+1}}{(z-b)^{2}}|_{z=\alpha }=a^{n}\frac{[n+1](a-b)-a}{(a-b)^{2}}\) (3)

Hence:

\(f[n]=\frac{a^{n}[n(a-b)-b]+b^{n+1}}{(a-b)^{2}}\) (4)

b. We expand the function \(F_{2} (z)\) in a sum of fractions:

\(F_{2}(z)=\frac{2(z^{2}-5z+6.5)}{(z-2)(z-3)^{2}}=\frac{A}{z-2}+\frac{B}{z-3}+\frac{C}{(z-3)^{2}}\) where A=B=C=1
\(\Rightarrow F_{2}(z)=\frac{1}{z-2}+\frac{1}{z-3}+\frac{1}{(z-3)^{2}}\)
\(=\frac{1}{2}(1-\frac{2}{z} )^{-1}-\frac{1}{3}(1-\frac{z}{3} )^{-1}+\frac{1}{9}(1-\frac{z}{3} )^{-2}\) so that 2/z<1 and z/3<1
\(=\frac{1}{z}(1+\frac{2}{z}+\frac{4}{z^{2}}+\frac{8}{z^{3}}+… )-\frac{1}{3}(1+\frac{z}{3}+\frac{z^{2}}{9}+\frac{z^{3}}{27}+… )+\frac{1}{9}(1+\frac{2z}{3}+\frac{3z^{2}}{9}+\frac{4z^{3}}{27}+… )\) where 2<|z|<3

\(=(\frac{1}{2}+\frac{2}{z^{2}}+\frac{2^{2}}{z^{3}}+\frac{2^{3}}{z^{4}}+… )-(\frac{1}{3}+\frac{z}{3^{2}}+\frac{z^{2}}{3^{3}}+\frac{z^{3}}{3^{4}}+… )+(\frac{1}{3^{2}}+\frac{2z}{3^{3}}+\frac{3z^{2}}{3^{4}}+\frac{4z^{3}}{3^{5}}+… )\)
\(=\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty }{2^{n-1}z^{-n}}- \sum\limits_{n=0}^{\infty }{(\frac{1}{3} )^{n-1}z^{n}}+\sum\limits_{n=0}^{\infty }{(n+1)(\frac{1}{3} )^{n+2}z^{n}}\)

or inversely, we have \(u_{n}=2^{n-1}\), n≥1 and \(u_{n}=-(n+2)3^{n-2}\),n≤0.
c. \(F_{3}(z)=\frac{2z^{2}+3z}{(z+2)(z-4)}\) can be written as:
\(\frac{F_{3}(z)}{z}=\frac{2z+3}{(z+2)(z-4)}=\frac{A}{z+2}+\frac{B}{z-4}\) when A=1/6 and B=11/6

Thus,
\(F_{3}(z)=\frac{1}{6}\frac{z}{z+2}+\frac{11}{6}\frac{z}{z-4}\)

and, by applying the inverse z-transform, we get:

\(f_{3}[n]=\frac{1}{6} (-2)^{-n}+\frac{11}{6} (4)^{n}\)

d.\(F_{4}(z)=\frac{z^{3}-20z}{(z-2)^{3}(z-4)}\) can be written as:
\(\frac{F_{4}(z)}{z}=\frac{z^{2}-20}{(z-2)^{3}(z-4)}=\frac{A+Bz+Cz^{2}}{(z-2)^{3}}+\frac{D}{z-4}\)

Multiplication with \((z – 2)^{3}(z – 4) \)yields:

\(z^{2}-20=(A+Bz+Cz^{2})(z-4)+D(z-2)^{3}\)

We then compare the coefficients of z in the last equation, which yields:

A = 6 ,B = 0, C = 1/2 and D = −1/2.

Thus,\( \frac{F_{4}(z)}{z}=\frac{z^{2}-20}{(z-2)^{3}(z-4)}=\frac{6+0z+1/2z^{2}}{(z-2)^{3}}-\frac{1/2}{z-4}\)
\(F_{4}(z)=\frac{1}{2}.\left\{\frac{12z+z^{3}}{(z-2)^{3}} \right\}-\frac{1}{2}(\frac{z}{z-4} ) =\frac{1}{2}\left\{\frac{12z+4z^{2}-4z^{2}+z^{3}}{(z-2)^{3}} \right\}-\frac{1}{2}(\frac{z}{z-4} )\)
\(=\frac{1}{2}\left\{\frac{(8z+4z)+4z^{2}-4z^{2}+z^{3}}{(z-2)^{3}} \right\} -\frac{1}{2}(\frac{z}{z-4} )\)
\(=\frac{1}{2}\left\{\frac{(z^{3}-4z^{2}+4z)+8z+4z^{2}}{(z-2)^{3}} \right\}-\frac{1}{2}(\frac{z}{z-4} )\)
\(=\frac{1}{2}\left\{\frac{z(z-2)^{2}+4z^{2}+8z}{(z-2)^{3}} \right\} -\frac{1}{2}(\frac{z}{z-4} )=\frac{1}{2}\left\{\frac{z}{z-2}+2\frac{2z^{2}+4z}{(z-2)^{3}} \right\} -\frac{1}{2}(\frac{z}{z-4} )\)

The inverse z-transform yields:

\(f_{4}[n]=\frac{1}{2}[(2^{n}+2.n^{2}2^{n})-4^{n}]=2^{n-1}+n^{2}2^{n}-2^{2n-1}\)
