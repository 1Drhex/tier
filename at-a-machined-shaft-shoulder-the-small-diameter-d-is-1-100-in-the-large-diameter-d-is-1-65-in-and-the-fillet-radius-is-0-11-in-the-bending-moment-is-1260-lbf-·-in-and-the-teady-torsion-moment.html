Guest Question.

At a machined shaft shoulder the small diameter \(d\) is \(1.100\) in, the large diameter \(D\) is \(1.65\) in, and the fillet radius is \(0.11\) in. The bending moment is \(1260 \mathrm{lbf}\). in and the steady torsion moment is \(1100 \mathrm{lbf} \cdot\) in. The heat-treated steel shaft has an ultimate strength of \(S_{u t}=105 \mathrm{kpsi}\) and a yield strength of \(S_{y}=82 \mathrm{kpsi}\). The reliability goal is \(0.99\).

(a) Determine the fatigue factor of safety of the design using each of the fatigue failure criteria described in this section.

(b) Determine the yielding factor of safety.

Admin
Solution

(a) \(D / d=1.65 / 1.100=1.50, r / d=0.11 / 1.100=0.10, K_{t}=1.68\) (Fig. A-15-9), \(K_{t s}=1.42\) (Fig. A-15-8), \(q=0.85\) (Fig. 6-20), \(q_{\text {shear }}=0.88\) (Fig. 6-21).

From Eq. (6-32),

\(\begin{array}{r}K_{f}=1+0.85(1.68-1)=1.58 \\K_{f s}=1+0.88(1.42-1)=1.37\end{array}\)

Eq. (6-8):

\(S_{e}^{\prime}=0.5(105)=52.5 \mathrm{kpsi}\)

Eq. (6-19):

\(k_{a}=2.70(105)^{-0.265}=0.787\)

Eq. (6-20):

\(\begin{aligned}&k_{b}=\left(\frac{1.100}{0.30}\right)^{-0.107}=0.870 \\&k_{c}=k_{d}=k_{f}=1\end{aligned}\)

Table 6-6:

\(\begin{aligned}&k_{e}=0.814 \\&S_{e}=0.787(0.870) 0.814(52.5)=29.3 \mathrm{kpsi}\end{aligned}\)

For a rotating shaft, the constant bending moment will create a completely reversed bending stress.

\(M_{a}=1260 \mathrm{lbf} \cdot \mathrm{in} \quad T_{m}=1100 \mathrm{lbf} \cdot \text { in } \quad M_{m}=T_{a}=0\)

Applying Eq. (7-7) for the DE-Goodman criteria gives

\(\begin{gathered}\frac{1}{n}=\frac{16}{\pi(1.1)^{3}}\left\{\frac{\left[4(1.58 \cdot 1260)^{2}\right]^{1 / 2}}{29300}+\frac{\left[3(1.37 \cdot 1100)^{2}\right]^{1 / 2}}{105000}\right\}=0.615 \\n=1.63 \quad \text { DE-Goodman }\end{gathered}\)

Similarly, applying Eqs. \((7-9),(7-11)\), and \((7-13)\) for the other failure criteria,

\(n=1.87 \quad\) DE-Gerber

\(n=1.88 \quad\) DE-ASME Elliptic

\(n=1.56 \quad\) DE-Soderberg

For comparison, consider an equivalent approach of calculating the stresses and applying the fatigue failure criteria directly. From Eqs. (7-5) and (7-6),

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{a}^{\prime}=\left[\left(\frac{32 \cdot 1.58 \cdot 1260}{\pi(1.1)^{3}}\right)^{2}\right]^{1 / 2}=15235 \mathrm{psi} \\&\sigma_{m}^{\prime}=\left[3\left(\frac{16 \cdot 1.37 \cdot 1100}{\pi(1.1)^{3}}\right)^{2}\right]^{1 / 2}=9988 \mathrm{psi}\end{aligned}\)

Taking, for example, the Goodman failure critera, application of Eq. (6-46) gives

\(\begin{aligned}&\frac{1}{n}=\frac{\sigma_{a}^{\prime}}{S_{e}}+\frac{\sigma_{m}^{\prime}}{S_{u t}}=\frac{15235}{29300}+\frac{9988}{105000}=0.615 \\&n=1.63\end{aligned}\)

which is identical with the previous result. The same process could be used for the other failure criteria.

(b) For the yielding factor of safety, determine an equivalent von Mises maximum stress using Eq. (7-15).

\(\begin{gathered}\sigma_{\max}^{\prime}=\left[\left(\frac{32(1.58)(1260)}{\pi(1.1)^{3}}\right)^{2}+3\left(\frac{16(1.37)(1100)}{\pi(1.1)^{3}}\right)^{2}\right]^{1 / 2}=18220 \mathrm{psi} \\n_{y}=\frac{S_{y}}{\sigma_{\max }^{\prime}}=\frac{82000}{18220}=4.50\end{gathered}\)

For comparison, a quick and very conservative check on yielding can be obtained by replacing \(\sigma_{\max }^{\prime}\) with \(\sigma_{a}^{\prime}+\sigma_{m}^{\prime}\). This just saves the extra time of calculating \(\sigma_{\max }^{\prime}\) if \(\sigma_{a}^{\prime}\) and \(\sigma_{m}^{\prime}\) have already been determined. For this example,

\(n_{y}=\frac{S_{y}}{\sigma_{a}^{\prime}+\sigma_{m}^{\prime}}=\frac{82000}{15235+9988}=3.25\)

which is quite conservative compared with \(n_{y}=4.50\).

Eq. (6–32),

\(K_{f}=1+q\left(K_{t}-1\right) \quad \text { or } \quad K_{f s}=1+q_{\text {shear }}\left(K_{t s}-1\right)\)

Eq. (6–8):

\(S_{e}^{\prime}= \begin{cases}0.5 S_{u t} & S_{u t} \leq 200 \mathrm{kpsi}(1400 \mathrm{MPa}) \\ 100 \mathrm{kpsi} & S_{u t}>200 \mathrm{kpsi} \\ 700 \mathrm{MPa} & S_{u t}>1400 \mathrm{MPa}\end{cases}\)

Eq. (6–19):

\(k_{a}=a S_{u t}^{b}\)

Eq. (6–20):

\(k_{b}= \begin{cases}(d / 0.3)^{-0.107}=0.879 d^{-0.107} & 0.11 \leq d \leq 2 \text { in } \\ 0.91 d^{-0.157} & 2<d \leq 10 \text { in } \\ (d / 7.62)^{-0.107}=1.24 d^{-0.107} & 2.79 \leq d \leq 51 \mathrm{~mm} \\ 1.51 d^{-0.157} & 51<d \leq 254 \mathrm{~mm}\end{cases}\)
