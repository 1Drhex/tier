Guest Question.

A pair of straight bevel gears is mounted on shafts, which are intersecting at right angles. The number of teeth on the pinion and gear are 21 and 28 respectively. The pressure angle is 20°. The pinion shaft is connected to an electric motor developing 5 kW rated power at 1440 rpm. The service factor can be taken as 1.5. The pinion and the gear are made of steel \( \left(S_{u t}=750\right.\left. N / mm ^{2}\right) \) and heat-treated to a surface hardness of 380 BHN. The gears are machined by a manufacturing process, which limits the error between the meshing teeth to 10 mm. The module and face width are 4 mm and 20 mm respectively.
Determine the factor of safety against bending as well as against pitting failure.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } kW =5 \quad n_{p}=1440 rpm \quad z_{p}=21 \quad z_{g}=28 \).

\( S_{u t}=750 N / mm ^{2} \quad BHN =380 \quad m=4 mm \).

\( b=20 mm \quad C_{s}=1.5 \).

error between meshing teeth = 10 mm α = 20°
Step I Beam strength

\( D_{p}=m z_{p}=4(21)=84 mm \).

\( D_{g}=m z_{g}=4(28)=112 mm \).

\( A_{o}=\sqrt{\left(\frac{D_{p}}{2}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{D_{g}}{2}\right)^{2}}=\sqrt{\left(\frac{84}{2}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{112}{2}\right)^{2}} \).

= 70 mm.

\( \tan \gamma=\frac{z_{p}}{z_{g}}=\frac{21}{28} \quad \text { or } \quad \gamma=36.87^{\circ} \).

\( z_{p}^{\prime}=\frac{z_{p}}{\cos \gamma}=\frac{21}{\cos (36.87)}=26.25 \).

From Table 17.3,

\( Y=0.344+\frac{(0.348-0.344)(26.25-24)}{(27-26)} \).

= 0.345.

\( \sigma_{b}=\frac{S_{u t}}{3}=\frac{750}{3}=250 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( S_{b}=m b \sigma_{b} Y\left[1-\frac{b}{A_{o}}\right] \).

\( =4(20)(250)(0.345)\left[1-\frac{20}{70}\right] \).

\( =4928.57 N \).

Step II Wear strength

\( Q=\frac{2 z_{g}}{z_{\sigma}+z_{p} \tan \gamma}=\frac{2(28)}{28+21 \tan (36.87)}=1.28 \).

\( K=0.16\left(\frac{ BHN }{100}\right)^{2}=0.16\left(\frac{380}{100}\right)^{2} \).

\( =2.3104 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( S_{w}=\frac{0.75 b Q D_{p} K}{\cos \gamma}=\frac{0.75(20)(1.28)(84)(2.3104)}{\cos (36.87)} \).

\( =4657.77 N \).

Step III Tangential force due to rated torque

\( M_{t}=\frac{60 \times 10^{6}( kW )}{2 \pi n_{p}}=\frac{60 \times 10^{6}(5)}{2 \pi(1440)} \).

\( =33157.28 N – mm \).

\( P_{t}=\frac{2 M_{t}}{D_{p}}=\frac{2(33157.28)}{84}=789.46 N \).

Step IV Dynamic load by Buckingham’s equation

\( v=\frac{\pi D_{p} n_{p}}{60 \times 10^{3}}=\frac{\pi(84)(1440)}{60 \times 10^{3}}=6.3335 m / s \).

\( \text { Also, } \quad C=11400 N / mm ^{2}, \quad b=20 mm \text {, } \)

\( P_{t}=789.46 N , \quad e=0.01 mm \).

From Eq. (19.21),

\( P_{d}=\frac{21 v\left( Ceb +P_{t}\right)}{21 v+\sqrt{\left( Ceb +P_{t}\right)}} \). (19.21).

\( P_{d}=\frac{21 v\left( Ceb +P_{t}\right)}{21 v+\sqrt{\left( Ceb +P_{t}\right)}} \).

\( =\frac{21(6.3335)[11400(0.01)(20)+789.46]}{21(6.3335)+\sqrt{[11400(0.01)(20)+789.46]}} \).

\( =2166.84 N \).

Step V Effective load
From Eq. (19.22),

\( P_{ eff }=C_{s} P_{t}+P_{d} \). (19.22).

\( P_{\text {eff }}=C_{s} P_{t}+P_{d}=1.5(789.46)+2166.84 \).

= 3351.03 N.

Step VI Factor of safety
For bending consideration,

\( (f s)=\frac{S_{b}}{P_{ eff }}=\frac{4928.57}{3351.03}=1.47 \).

For wear consideration,

\( \left(f_{S}\right)=\frac{S_{w}}{P_{ eff }}=\frac{4657.77}{3351.03}=1.39 \).
