Guest Question.

Consider the circuit in Fig. 13.16. Determine the coupling coefficient. Calculate the energy stored in the coupled inductors at time \(t = 1 s\) if \(v = 60 \cos \left(4 t + 30^{\circ}\right) V\).

Admin
Solution

The coupling coefficient is

\(k=\frac{M}{\sqrt{L_{1} L_{2}}}=\frac{2.5}{\sqrt{20}}=0.56\)

indicating that the inductors are tightly coupled. To find the energy stored, we need to calculate the current. To find the current, we need to obtain the frequency-domain equivalent of the circuit.

\(\begin{aligned}60 \cos \left(4 t + 30^{\circ}\right) & \Rightarrow \quad 60 \angle 30^{\circ}, \quad \omega=4 rad / s \\5 H & \Rightarrow \quad j \omega L_{1}=j 20 \Omega \\2.5 H & \Rightarrow \quad j \omega M=j 10 \Omega \\4 H & \Rightarrow \quad j \omega L_{2}=j 16 \Omega \\\frac{1}{16} F & \Rightarrow \quad \frac{1}{j \omega C}=-j 4 \Omega\end{aligned}\)

The frequency-domain equivalent is shown in Fig. 13.17. We now apply mesh analysis. For mesh 1,

\((10 + j 20) I _{1} + j 10 I _{2}=60 \angle 30^{\circ}\) (13.3.1)

For mesh 2,

\(j 10 I _{1} + (j 16 – j 4) I _{2}=0\)

or

\(I _{1}=-1.2 I _{2}\) (13.3.2)

Substituting this into Eq. (13.3.1) yields

\(I _{2}(-12 – j 14)=60 \angle 30^{\circ} \quad \Rightarrow \quad I _{2}=3.254 \angle 160.6^{\circ} A\)

and

\(I _{1}=-1.2 I _{2}=3.905 \angle -19.4^{\circ} A\)

In the time-domain,

\(i_{1}=3.905 \cos \left(4 t – 19.4^{\circ}\right), \quad i_{2}=3.254 \cos \left(4 t + 160.6^{\circ}\right)\)

At time \(t = 1 s , 4t = 4 rad = 229.2^{\circ}\), and

\(\begin{aligned}&i_{1}= 3.905 \cos\left(229.2^{\circ} – 19.4^{\circ}\right)=-3.389 A \\&i_{2}=3.254 \cos\left(229.2^{\circ} + 160.6^{\circ}\right)=2.824 A\end{aligned}\)

The total energy stored in the coupled inductors is

\(w=\frac{1}{2} L_{1} i_{1}^{2} + \frac{1}{2} L_{2} i_{2}^{2} + M i_{1} i_{2}\)

\(=\frac{1}{2} (5) (-3.389)^{2} + \frac{1}{2}(4)(2.824)^{2} + 2.5 (-3.389) (2.824)= 20.73 J\)
