Guest Question.

Solve the Poisson equation \(-\bigtriangledown ^{2}u=2\) in \(\Omega \) , u = 0 on \(\Gamma _{1}\) , \(\partial u/\partial n=0\) on \(\Gamma _{2}\) , where \(\Omega \) is the first quadrant bounded by the parabola y = 1 – \(x^{2}\) and the coordinate axes (see Fig. P8.25), and \(\Gamma _{1}\) and \(\Gamma _{2}\) are the boundaries shown in Fig. P8.25.

Admin
Solution

The coefficient matrix associated with the Laplace operator over a rightangle triangle is given in Eq. ( 8.2.9) with \(k_{e}\) = 1. The element coefficent matrices and source vectors for each element are listed below. (\(L_{12}\equiv a\), \(L_{13}\equiv b\)):

Elements 1 and 2: (a = 0.6, b = 0.64, \(\beta \) = a/b = 0.9375, \(\alpha \) = b/a = 1.06667)

\([K^{1}]=[K^{2}]=\begin{bmatrix}1.0021 &-0.5333 &-0.4688 \\ -0.5333 &0.5333 &0.0000 \\ -0.4688 &0.0000 &0.4688 \end{bmatrix}\); \(\left \{ f^{1} \right \}=\left \{ f^{2} \right \}=\begin{Bmatrix}0.128\\ 0.128\\ 0.128\end{Bmatrix}\)

Element 3: (a = 0.4, b = 0.64, \(\beta \) = a/b = 0.625, \(\alpha \) = b/a = 1.6)

\([K^{3}]=\begin{bmatrix}1.1125 &-0.800 &-0.3125 \\ -0.8000 &0.800 &0.0000 \\ -0.3125 &0.000 & 0.3125\end{bmatrix}\) ; \(\left \{ f^{3} \right \}=\begin{Bmatrix}0.0853\\ 0.0853\\ 0.0853\end{Bmatrix}\)

Element 4: (a = 0.6, b = 0.36, \(\beta \) = a/b = 1.6667, \(\alpha \) = b/a = 0.6)

\([K^{4}]=\begin{bmatrix}1.1333 &-0.3000 &-0.8333 \\ -0.3000 &0.3000 &0.0000 \\ -0.8333 &0.0000 &0.8333 \end{bmatrix}\) ; \(\left \{ f^{1} \right \}=\begin{Bmatrix}0.072\\ 0.072\\ 0.072\end{Bmatrix}\)

The coefficients of the assembled coefficient matrix are \((K_{IJ}=K_{JI})\)

\(K_{11}=K_{11}^{1}\) , \(K_{12}=K_{12}^{1}\) , \(K_{13}=0\) , \(K_{14}=K_{13}^{1}\) , \(K_{15}=0\) , \(K_{16}=0\)

\(K_{22}=K_{22}^{1}+K_{33}^{2}+K_{11}^{3}\) , \(K_{23}=K_{12}^{3}\) , \(K_{24}=K_{23}^{1}+K_{32}^{2}\)

\(K_{25}=K_{31}^{2}+K_{13}^{3}\) , \(K_{26}=0\) , \(K_{33}=K_{22}^{3}\) , \(K_{34}=0\) , \(K_{35}=K_{23}^{3}\) , \(K_{36}=0\)

\(K_{44}=K_{33}^{1}+K_{22}^{2}+K_{11}^{4}\) , \(K_{45}=K_{12}^{2}+K_{12}^{4}\) , \(K_{46}=K_{13}^{4}\)

\(K_{55}=K_{11}^{2}+K_{33}^{3}+K_{22}^{4}\) , \(K_{56}=K_{23}^{4}\), \(K_{66}=K_{33}^{4}\)

The coefficients of the assembled source vector are \((F_{i}=f_{i}+Q_{i})\)

\(F_{1}=F_{1}^{1}\) , \(F_{2}=F_{2}^{1}+F_{3}^{2}+F_{1}^{3}\) , \(F_{3}=F_{2}^{3}\) , \(F_{4}=F_{3}^{1}+F_{2}^{2}+F_{1}^{4}\)

\(F_{5}=F_{1}^{2}+F_{3}^{3}+F_{2}^{4}\) , \(F_{6}=F_{3}^{4}\)

For a constant source, \(f=f_{0}^{e}\) , over an element, the source vector components are \(f_{i}^{e}=f_{0}^{e}A^{e}/3\) , where \(A^{e}\) is the area of the e-th linear triangular element. Note that \(f_{0}^{e}=2\) for all elements.

For the mesh of triangular elements shown in Figure P8.34, the boundary conditions on the primary variables are: \(U_{3}\) = 0, \(U_{5}\) = 0, \(U_{6}\) = 0. Hence, the unknown primary nodal variables are: \(U_{1}\), \(U_{2}\), and \(U_{4}\). The known secondary variables are:

\(Q_{1}=Q_{1}^{1}=0\) , \(Q_{2}=Q_{2}^{1}+Q_{3}^{2}+Q_{1}^{3}=0\) , \(Q_{4}=Q_{3}^{1}+Q_{2}^{2}+Q_{1}^{4}=0\)

The condensed equations are given by

\(\begin{bmatrix}K_{11} &K_{12} &K_{14} \\ K_{21} &K_{22} &K_{24} \\ K_{41} &K_{42} &K_{44} \end{bmatrix}\begin{Bmatrix}U_{1}\\ U_{2}\\ U_{4}\end{Bmatrix}=\begin{Bmatrix}f_{1}^{1}\\ f_{2}^{1}+f_{3}^{2}+f_{1}^{3}\\ f_{3}^{1}+f_{2}^{2}+f_{1}^{4}\end{Bmatrix}\)

or

\(\begin{bmatrix}1.0021 &-0.5333 &-0.4688 \\ -0.5333 &2.1146 &0.0000 \\ -0.4688 &0.0000 &2.1354 \end{bmatrix}\begin{Bmatrix}U_{1}\\ U_{2}\\ U_{4}\end{Bmatrix}=\begin{Bmatrix}0.1280\\ 0.3413\\ 0.3280\end{Bmatrix}\)

The solution of these equations is (obtained with the help of a computer)

\(U_{1}\) = 0.37413, \(U_{2}\) = 0.25578, \(U_{4}\) = 0.23573

Eq. ( 8.2.9): \(q_{n}\equiv n_{x}\left ( a_{11}\frac{\partial u}{\partial x}+a_{12}\frac{\partial u}{\partial y} \right )+n_{y}\left ( a_{21}\frac{\partial u}{\partial x}+a_{22}\frac{\partial u}{\partial y} \right )\)
