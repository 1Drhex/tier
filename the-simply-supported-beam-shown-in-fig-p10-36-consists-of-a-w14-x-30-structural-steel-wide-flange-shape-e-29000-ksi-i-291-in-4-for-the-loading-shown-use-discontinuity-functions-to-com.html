Guest Question.

The simply supported beam shown in Fig. P10.36 consists of a W14 × 30 structural steel wide-flange shape [E = 29,000 ksi; I = 291 \(\text { in. }^{4}\)]. For the loading shown, use discontinuity functions to compute (a) the slope of the beam at A and (b) the deflection of the beam at midspan.

Admin
Solution

Support reactions: A FBD of the beam is shown to the right.

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{A}=&-(2.5 kips / ft )(12 ft )(12 ft )+D_{y}(24 ft )=0 \\& \therefore D_{y}=15 kips \\\Sigma F_{y}=& A_{y}+D_{y}-(2.5 kips / ft )(12 ft )=0 \\& \therefore A_{y}=15 kips\end{aligned}\)

Load function w(x):

\(w(x)=15 kips \langle x-0 ft \rangle^{-1}-2.5 kips / ft \langle x-6 ft \rangle^{0}+2.5 kips / ft \langle x-18 ft \rangle^{0}+15 kips \langle x-24 ft \rangle^{-1}\)

Shear-force function V(x) and bending-moment function M(x):

\(\begin{aligned}&V(x)=15 kips \langle x-0 ft \rangle^{0}-2.5 kips / ft \langle x-6 ft \rangle^{1}+2.5 kips / ft \langle x-18 ft \rangle^{1}+15 kips \langle x-24 ft \rangle^{0} \\&M(x)=15 kips \langle x-0 ft \rangle^{1}-\frac{2.5 kips / ft }{2}\langle x-6 ft \rangle^{2}+\frac{2.5 kips / ft }{2}\langle x-18 ft \rangle^{2}+15 kips \langle x-24 ft \rangle^{1}\end{aligned}\)

Equations for beam slope and beam deflection:
From Eq. (10.1), we can write:

\(\begin{aligned}E I \frac{d^{2} v}{d x^{2}} &=M(x)=15 kips \langle x-0 ft \rangle^{1}-\frac{2.5 kips / ft }{2}\langle x-6 ft \rangle^{2} \\&+\frac{2.5 kips / ft }{2}\langle x-18 ft \rangle^{2}+15 kips \langle x-24 ft \rangle^{1}\end{aligned}\)

Integrate the moment function to obtain an expression for the beam slope:

\(\begin{aligned}E I \frac{d v}{d x} &=\frac{15 \text { kips }}{2}\langle x-0 ft \rangle^{2}-\frac{2.5 kips / ft }{6}\langle x-6 ft \rangle^{3} \\&+\frac{2.5 kips / ft }{6}\langle x-18 ft \rangle^{3}+\frac{15 kips }{2}\langle x-24 ft \rangle^{2}+C_{1} (a)\end{aligned}\)

Integrate again to obtain the beam deflection function:

\(\begin{aligned}E I v &=\frac{15 kips }{6}\langle x-0 ft \rangle^{3}-\frac{2.5 kips / ft }{24}\langle x-6 ft \rangle^{4} \\&+\frac{2.5 kips / ft }{24}\langle x-18 ft \rangle^{4}+\frac{15 kips }{6}\langle x-24 ft \rangle^{3}+C_{1} x+C_{2} (b)\end{aligned}\)

Evaluate constants using boundary conditions: Boundary conditions are specific values of deflection v or slope dv/dx that are known at particular locations along the beam span. For this beam, the deflection v is known at the pin support (x = 0 ft) and at the roller support (x = 24 ft). Substitute the boundary condition v = 0 at x = 0 ft into Eq. (b) to obtain:

\(C_{2}=0\)

Next, substitute the boundary condition v = 0 at x = 24 ft into Eq. (b) to obtain:

\(\begin{aligned}0 &=\frac{15 kips }{6}(24 ft )^{3}-\frac{2.5 kips / ft }{24}(18 ft )^{4}+\frac{2.5 kips / ft }{24}(6 ft )^{4}+C_{1}(24 ft ) \\& \therefore C_{1}=-990 kip – ft ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

The beam slope and elastic curve equations are now complete:

\(\begin{aligned}E I \frac{d v}{d x} &=\frac{15 kips }{2}\langle x-0 ft \rangle^{2}-\frac{2.5 kips / ft }{6}\langle x-6 ft \rangle^{3} \\&+\frac{2.5 kips / ft }{6}\langle x-18 ft \rangle^{3}+\frac{15 kips }{2}\langle x-24 ft \rangle^{2}-990 kip – ft ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}E I v &=\frac{15 kips }{6}\langle x-0 ft \rangle^{3}-\frac{2.5 kips / ft }{24}\langle x-6 ft \rangle^{4} \\&+\frac{2.5 kips / ft }{24}\langle x-18 ft \rangle^{4}+\frac{15 kips }{6}\langle x-24 ft \rangle^{3}-\left(990 kip – ft ^{2}\right) x\end{aligned}\)

 

(a) Beam slope at A: For the W14 × 30 structural steel wide-flange shape, EI = 58,604.164 \(\text { kip-ft }^{2}\) . The beam slope at A is:

\(\begin{aligned}\left.E I \frac{d v}{d x}\right|_{A} &=-990 kip – ft ^{2} \\&\left.\therefore \frac{d v}{d x}\right|_{A}=-\frac{990 kip – ft ^{2}}{58,604.164 kip – ft ^{2}}=-0.016893 rad =-0.01689 rad\end{aligned}\)

(b) Beam deflection at midspan: At midspan where x = 12 ft, the beam deflection is:

\(\begin{gathered}E I v_{\text {midspan }}=\frac{15 \text { kips }}{6}(12 ft )^{3}-\frac{2.5 kips / ft }{24}(6 ft )^{4}-\left(990 kip – ft ^{2}\right)(12 ft )=-7,695 kip – ft ^{3} \\\therefore v_{\text {midspan }}=-\frac{7,695 kip – ft ^{3}}{58,604.164 \text { kip-ft }^{2}}=-0.131305 ft =1.576 in . \downarrow\end{gathered}\)
