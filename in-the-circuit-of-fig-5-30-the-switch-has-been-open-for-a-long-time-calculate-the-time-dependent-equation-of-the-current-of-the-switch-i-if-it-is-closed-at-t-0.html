Guest Question.

In the circuit of Fig. 5.30, the switch has been open for a long time. Calculate the time dependent equation of the current of the switch (I), if it is closed at t = 0.

Admin
Solution

6.30. The status of the circuit for t = \(0^{-}\) is illustrated in Fig. 6.30.2. As can be seen, the capacitor and the inductors are open circuit and short circuits, respectively, since the circuit has reached its steady state condition.

KVL in the indicated loop:

\(-10+I_{L2}(0^{-})×1+4×I_{L2}(0^{-})=0\Rightarrow I_{L2}(0^{-})=\frac{10}{5} =\) 2 A ……..(1)

Using Ohm’s law for the 4 Ω resistor:

\(V_{C}(0^{-})=4I_{L2}(0^{-}) \)= 4 × 2 = 8V …….(2)

As can be noticed from the circuit of Fig. 6.30.2, the current of the first inductor is zero.

\(I_{L1}(0^{-})\) = 0 A …….(3)

Due to the current continuity of the inductor in lack of a power source with an infinite size (e.g., impulse power source):

\(I_{L1}(0^{+}) = I_{L1}(0^{-})\) = 0 A …….(4)

\(I_{L2}(0^{+}) = I_{L2}(0^{-})\) = 2 A …….(5)

Due to the voltage continuity of the capacitor in lack of a power source with an infinite size (e.g., impulse power source):

\(V_{C}(0^{+})=V_{C}(0^{-})\) = 8 V……..(6)

Applying KCL in the indicated supernode of Fig. 6.30.3:

\(-I_{0}(t) +I_{L1}(t)+ I_{L2}(t)+I(t) =0\Rightarrow I(t) =I_{0}(t)-I_{L1}(t)-I_{L2}(t)\) ……..(7)

Therefore, we need to calculate the value of \(I_{0}(t)\), \(I_{L1}(t)\), and \(I_{L2}(t)\). As can be noticed from Fig 6.30.3, due to the existence of the short-circuit branch, the circuit includes three separate circuits including zero-order circuit, first-order circuit, and second-order circuit.
Zero-order circuit: The zero-order circuit includes the series combination of the voltage source and the 1 Ω resistor, as can be seen in Fig. 6.30.4.

Applying Ohm’s law for the 1 Ω resistor:

\(I_{0}(t)=\frac{10-0}{1}=10\) A ……..(8)

First-order circuit: The first-order circuit includes the series combination of the 4 Ω resistor and the 1 H inductor, as can be seen in Fig. 6.30.5.
The primary current of the inductor:

\(I_{L2}(0^{+})\) = 2 A ……..(9)

The final current of the inductor is zero, since its circuit does not include any power source.

\(I_{L2}(∞)=\) 0 A …….(10)

The time constant of the first-order circuit:

\(τ=\frac{L}{R_{Th}}=\frac{1}{4}Ω \)…….(11)

\(I_{L2}(t)=I_{L2}(∞)+(I_{L2}(0^{+})- I_{L2}(∞))e^{-\frac{t}{τ}}\Rightarrow I_{L2}(t)=0+(2-0)e^{-4t}=2e^{-4t}\) ……(12)

Second-order circuit: The second-order circuit includes the series combination of the 1 H inductor and the capacitor, as can be seen in Fig. 6.30.6.
Applying KVL in the mesh:

\(I_{L1}(t)+V_{C}(t)=0 \)……(13)

\(\Rightarrow L_{1}\frac{d}{dt}I_{L1}(t)+V_{C}(t) =0\Rightarrow 1×\frac{d}{dt}I_{L1}(t)+V_{C}(t)\) =0

\(\frac{d}{dt}\Rightarrow \frac{d²}{dt²}I_{L1}(t)+\frac{d}{dt}V_{C}(t)\) =0 …….(14)

KCL in the indicated node:

\(-I_{L}(t)+I_{C}(t)=0\Rightarrow I_{L}(t)=I_{C}(t) \)…….(15)

Using the voltage-current relation of the capacitor:

\(I_{C}(t)=C\frac{d}{dt}V_{C}(t)=0.25\frac{d}{dt}V_{C}(t)\Rightarrow \frac{d}{dt}V_{C}(t)=4I_{C}(t)\)

Using (15)\(\Rightarrow \frac{d}{dt}V_{C}(t)=4I_{L}(t) \)……(16)

Solving (14) and (16):

\(\frac{d²}{dt²}I_{L1}(t)+4I_{L}(t)\) =0 …….(17)

Equation (17) is a second-order homogeneous differential equation; therefore, it only has a common solution. Its characteristic equation and the related roots are:

s²+4=0\(\Rightarrow \) s = ± 2j ……(18)

Therefore, the general form of the solution of equation (17) is:

\(I_{L1}(t)\)= Acos(2t) + Bsin(2t) ……..(19)

To determine the value of the parameters (A and B), we need to apply the primary value of the variables (\(I_{L1}(t)\) and \(V_{C}(t)\), that is, \(I_{L1}(0^{+}\)) = 0 A and \(V_{C}(0^{+}\)) = 8 V.

Solving (4) and (19):

\(Acos(2×0^{+}) + Bsin(2×0^{+}) = 0 \Rightarrow A ×1 +B ×0=0\Rightarrow A=0 \)…….(20)

Solving (13) and (6):

\(V_{L1}(0^{+})=-V_{C}(0^{+} \)= – 8 V …….(21)

Using the current-relation of the inductor:

\(V_{L1}(t)=L_{1}\frac{d}{dt}I_{L1}(t)=1×\frac{d}{dt}I_{L1}(t)=\frac{d}{dt}I_{L1}(t)\)

Using\( (19)\Rightarrow V_{L1}(t)=\frac{d}{dt}(Acos(2t) + Bsin(2t) )= -2Asin(2t) + 2Bcos(2t) \)……(22)

Solving (21) and (22):

\(-2Asin(2×0^{+}) + 2Bcos(2×0^{+}) = -8 \Rightarrow -2A×0+2B×1=-8 \Rightarrow\) B= -4 ……(23)

Solving (19), (20), and (23):

\(I_{L1}(t) = -4sin(2t)\) …….(24)

\(I(t)=I_{0}(t)-I_{L1}(t)-I_{L2}(t)=10-(-4sin(2t))-(2e^{-4t})\) \(I(t)= 10-2e^{-4t}+4sin(2t)\)
