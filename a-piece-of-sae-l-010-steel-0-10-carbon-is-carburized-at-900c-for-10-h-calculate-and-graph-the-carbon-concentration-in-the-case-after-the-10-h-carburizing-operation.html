Guest Question.

A piece of SAE l 010 steel (0.10% carbon) is carburized at\( 900^{o}C \)for 10 h. Calculate and graph the carbon concentration in the case after the 10 h carburizing operation.

Admin
Solution
\(t=10 h =3.6 \times 10^{4} s , C_{0}=0.1 \% \text { carbon, } T=900^{\circ} C =1173 K\)

From Table 9.2, for carbon diffusing in austenite (fcc iron), Q = 142kJ/mol, Do = 0.21 cm2/s, R = 8.314J/mol. Using the Fe-C phase diagram (Fig. 2.32) at \( 900^{ o}\) the maximum solubility of carbon in iron (austenite) is about 1.3%, so \( C_{s}\) = 1.3%. Using equation 9.13, calculate the diffusion constant:

\( D= D_{o}exp (-\frac{Q}{RT} )\)

 

\( = 0.21 cm^{2}/s \times exp (-\frac{142 000 J/mol}{8.314J/mol.K \times 1173K} )= 1.0 \times 10^{-7}cm^{2}/s\)

Use equation 9.12 to calculate carbon concentration for various depths:

\( c(x)=c_{s}+(c_{\omicron }- c_{s})erf (\frac{x}{2\sqrt{Dt} } )\)

 

\( =1.3-1.2 erf (\frac{x}{2\sqrt{1\times 10^{-7}cm^{2}/s\times 3.6 \times 10^{4}s} } )\)

 

\( =1.3 – 1.2 erf(8.3x)\)

Using Table 2.2 the following can be calculated:

x c(x)
0 1.3
0..1 1.18
0.05 0.76
0.1 0.38
0.2 0.124
0.3 0.1

Plotting these data yields the profile shown in Fig. E9.1.
