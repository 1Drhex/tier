Guest Question.

A tank having a cross-sectional area of 0.2 \(m^{2}\) is operating at a steady state with an inlet flowrate of \(10^{-3}\) \(m^{3}\)/s. Between the liquid heads of 0.3 m and 0.09 m the flow-head characteristics are given by:
\(Q_{2}\) = 0.002Z + 0.0006
where \(Q_{2}\) is the outlet flowrate and Z is the liquid level. Determine the transfer functions relating (a) inflow and liquid level, (b) inflow and outflow.
If the inflow increases from \(10^{-3}\) to 1.1 Ã— \(10^{-3}\) \(m^{3}\)/s according to a step change, calculate the liquid level 200 s after the change has occurred.

Admin
Solution

(a) A mass balance gives:

\(Q_{1}\rho – Q_{2}\rho =\frac{d}{dt}(\rho V)=\frac{d}{dt}(\rho AZ)\)

where \(Q_{1}\) is the inlet flow, \(\rho \) the fluid density, V the volume of fluid in the tank and A the area of the base (\(\rho \) and A are constants).

Thus: \(Q_{1}-Q_{2}=A\frac{dZ}{dt}\) (i)

At steady-state: \(Q'_{1}-Q'_{2}=0\) (ii)

Subtracting equation (ii) from equation (i), then:

\((Q_{1}-Q'_{1})-(Q_{2}-Q'_{2})=A\frac{dZ}{dt}=A\frac{dZ}{dt}\)

\(Q_{1}-Q_{2}=A\frac{dZ}{dt}\) (iii)

where: \(Q_{1}=Q_{1}-Q'_{1}\) , \(Q_{2}=Q_{2}-Q'_{2}\), Z=Z-Z’

But: \(Q_{2}\) = 0.002Z + 0.0006

Thus: \(Q_{2}\) = 0.002Z (iv)

From equations (iii) and (iv): \(Q_{1}\)– 0.002Z=A\(\frac{dZ}{dt}\)

Transforming: \(\overline{Q}_{1}-0.002\overline{Z}=A\frac{d\overline{Z}}{dt}\)

Hence, the transfer function, \(G_{A}=\overline{Z}/\overline{Q}_{1}=\frac{500}{1+100 s}\) (v)

(b) From equations (iv) and (v): \(G_{B}=\overline{Q}_{2}/\overline{Q}_{1}=\frac{500}{1+100 s}\) (vi)

For a step change in \(Q_{1}\) of \(10^{-4}\) \(m^{3}\)/s:

\(\overline{Q}_{1}=\frac{10^{-4}}{s}\)

Thus, from equation (v): \(\overline{Z}=\frac{10^{-4}}{s}.\frac{500}{1+100 s}=\frac{5\times 10^{-4}}{s(0.01+s)}\)

=0.05\(\left ( \frac{1}{s}-\frac{1}{s+0.01} \right )\)

Thus: Z = 0.05\((1-e^{-0.01t})\)

At t = 200 s: Z = 0.05(1 – e^{-2} ) = 0.043 m.

At the original steady-state: \(Q'_{2}\)= 0.002Z’+ 0.006 =\(Q'_{1}\)

Thus: 0.002Z’+ 0.0006 = 0.001

and: original steady-state level \(Z'_{1}\)=0.2 m

Hence, level at t = 200 s is (0.2 + 0.043) = 0.243 m
