Guest Question.

Extending , let’s assume that point S is on the screw axis and is described as \( \overline{OS} = \left[0,1,0.5\right] ^{T}\) , while \(\overline{SP_{1}} = \left[0,1,1\right] ^{T}\) remains the same as shown in Figure 3.5, and we add a translation of 0.3 units along the screw axis. Find the location of \(P_{3}\).

Admin
Solution

To find the location of \(P_{3}\), we use Eqs. (3.13)–(3.15) as follows:

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} P_{3x} \\ P_{3y} \\ P_{3z} \end{matrix} \right ] =^{P_{1} } \left[T\right] _{P_{3} } \left [ \begin{matrix} P_{1x} \\ P_{1y} \\ P_{1z} \end{matrix} \right ]\) (3.13)

 

\(T_{14} =ts_{x} -OS_{x} (T_{11}-1 )-OS_{y} T_{12}-OS_{z}T_{13}\)

 

\(T_{24} =ts_{y} -OS_{x} T_{21}-OS_{y} (T_{22}-1)-OS_{z}T_{23}\) (3.15)

 

\(T_{34} =ts_{z} -OS_{x} T_{31}-OS_{y} T_{32}-OS_{z}T_{23}(T_{33}-1)\)

 

\(\overline{OP_{1} } =\left [ \begin{matrix} P_{1x} \\ P_{1y} \\ P_{1z} \end{matrix} \right ]=\overline{OS }+\overline{SP_{1} }=\left [ \begin{matrix}0 \\1 \\0.5 \end{matrix} \right ]+\left [ \begin{matrix}0 \\1 \\1 \end{matrix} \right ]=\left [ \begin{matrix}0 \\2 \\1.5 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

 

\(^{P_{1} }\left[ T\right]_{P_{3} } = \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & -0.866 & 0.5 & 0.616 \\ 0.866 & 0.25 & 0.433 & 0.684 \\-0.5 & 0.433 & 0.75 & -0.0482 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

 

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} P_{3x} \\ P_{3y} \\ P_{3z} \\1 \end{matrix} \right ]=^{P_{1} }\left[ T\right]_{P_{3} } \left [ \begin{matrix} P_{1x} \\ P_{1y} \\ P_{1z} \\1 \end{matrix} \right ]=\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & -0.866 & 0.5 & 0.616 \\ 0.866 & 0.25 & 0.433 & 0.684 \\-0.5 & 0.433 & 0.75 & -0.0482 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\2 \\ 1.5 \\1 \end{matrix} \right ]=\left [ \begin{matrix}-0.366 \\ 1.833 \\ 1.9428 \\1 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

 

For comparison, we can say that point \(P_{3}\) can be reached by the following transformations:

\(P_{3} = Trans (0,1,0.5) Rot (n,60) Trans (0,1.366,0) Rot (o,90) Trans (0,0, −0.366) ^{2 }P_{3}\)

 

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} P_{3x} \\ P_{3y} \\ P_{3z} \\1 \end{matrix} \right ]=\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & 0 & 1 & -0.366 \\ 0.866 & 0.5 & 0 & 1.683 \\-0.5 & 0.866 & 0 & 1.683 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\0 \\ 0.3 \\1 \end{matrix} \right ]=\left [ \begin{matrix} -0.366 \\1.833 \\ 1.9428 \\1 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

 

which once again is the same as earlier. Notice that the orientations do not appear to be the same because we have not included the orientation of a frame we might attach to point \(P_{1}\). Doing so, we would get:

\(\left[P_{3} \right] =^{P_{1} }\left[T\right]_{P_{3} } \left[P_{1} \right] = \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & -0.866 & 0.5 & 0.616 \\ 0.866 & 0.25 & 0.433 & 0.684 \\-0.5 & 0.433 & 0.75 & -0.0482 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0 &0 \\ 0 & 0.5 &-0.866 & 2\\ 0 & 0.866 & 0.5 & 1.5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

 

\(=\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & 0 & 1 & -0.366 \\ 0.866 & 0.5 & 0 & 1.833\\ -0.5 & 0.866 &0 & 1.9428 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\)
