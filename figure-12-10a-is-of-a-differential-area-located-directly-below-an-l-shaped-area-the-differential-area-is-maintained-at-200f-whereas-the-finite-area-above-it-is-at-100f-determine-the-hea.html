Guest Question.

Figure 12.10a is of a differential area located directly below an L-shaped area. The differential area is maintained at 200°F, whereas the finite area above it is at 100°F. Determine the heat transferred by radiation from\( dA_{1} to A_{2} \).

Admin
Solution

We have an equation for heat transferred between two black surfaces in terms of the view factor.
Assumptions
1. Both areas are diff use emitters and absorbers
2. Both surfaces are black
The heat-transfer rate is written as

\(dq_{1\rightarrow 2} =\sigma \left(T^{4}_{1}-T^{4}_{2} \right) dA_{1} F_{d1\rightarrow 2}\)

 

With the size of \(dA_{1}\) unknown, we write

\(\frac{dq_{1\rightarrow 2}}{dA_{1}} =\sigma \left(T^{4}_{1}-T^{4}_{2} \right) F_{d1\rightarrow 2} \)

 

All terms on the right-hand side are known except the view factor. We can evaluate the view factor by applying Figure 12.3.

Figure 12.10b shows the same areas, but with \(dA_{2}\) divided into overlapping areas \(dA_{3} and dA_{4}\), \(dA_{5}\) being common to both \(dA_{3} and dA_{4}\). The view factor is

\(F_{d1- 2} =F_{d1- 3}+F_{d1- 4}+F_{d1- 5}\)

 

From Figure 12.3 we can evaluate each term as

\(F_{d1- 3}\) a=100cm b=250cm c=100cm
X=a/c=1.0 Y=b/c=2.5 \(F_{d1- 3}=0.17\)
\(F_{d1- 4}\) a=300cm b=50cm c=100cm
X=3.0 Y=0.5 \(F_{d1- 4}=0.11\)
\(F_{d1- 5}\) a=100cm b=50cm c=100cm
X=1.0 Y=0.5 \(F_{d1- 5}=0.09\)

Thus

 

\(F_{d1- 2}=0.17+0.11+0.09=0.19\)

So only 0.19 or 19% of the energy leaving \(dA_{1}\) reaches \(dA_{2}\). The net heat transferred then is

 

\(\frac{dq_{d1\rightarrow 2}}{dA_{1}}=\left(0.1714\times 10^{-8}BTU/hr.ft^{2}.°R^{4} \right)\left(660^{4}-560^{4} \right) °R^{4} \left(0.19\right)\)

 

where temperature is expressed in absolute units. Solving

 

\(\frac{dq_{d1\rightarrow 2}}{dA_{1}}=29.7BTU/\left(hr.ft^{2} \right) \)
