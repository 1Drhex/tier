Guest Question.

Determine the moments at each joint of the frame shown in Fig 2.7. E = 29000 ksi, A = 16 \( in^{2}\), and I = 446 \(in^{4}\) for all members.

Admin
Solution

Step 1

Since the loading is only on the span BC there will only be fixed-end moments in members BC and CB.\(M_{FBC}=-\frac{WL^{2} }{12}=-\frac{4\left(8\right) ^{2} }{12}=-21.33^{K-ft}\)

 

\(M_{FCB}=\frac{WL^{2} }{12}=\frac{4\left(8\right) ^{2} }{12}=21.33^{K-ft}\)

Step 2

\(KI=2\)There are two unknown displacements in this problem, which are \(\theta _{B}\) and \(\theta _{C}\). They are unknown because these frame joints will rotate as the members bend due to the applied loading. The rotations\(\theta _{A}\) and \(\theta _{D}\)are zero because of the fixed supports at A and D. Due to symmetrical loading, there will be no side sway in the frame, therefore Î¨= 0.

Step 3

The slope-deflection equations are formulated below using Eq. 2.2 and Eq.2.3 as,\( M_{AB}=M_{FAB}+\frac{2EI}{L}\left(2\theta _{A}+\theta _{B} \right)\)

 

\(M_{BA}=M_{FBA}+\frac{2EI}{L}\left(2\theta _{B}+\theta _{A} \right) \)

 

\(M_{AB}=2E\frac{1}{10}\left[2\theta _{A}+\theta _{B} \right]=\frac{EI}{5}\theta _{B}\)

 

\(M_{BA}=2E\frac{1}{10}\left[2\theta _{B}+\theta _{A} \right]=\frac{EI}{5}\theta _{B}\)

 

\(M_{BC}=-21.33+2E\frac{1}{8}\left[2\theta _{B}+\theta _{C} \right]=-21.33+\frac{EI}{4}\left(2\theta _{B}+\theta _{C} \right)\)

 

\(M_{CB}=21.33+2E\frac{1}{8}\left[2\theta _{C}+\theta _{B} \right]=21.33+\frac{EI}{4}\left(\theta _{B}+2\theta _{C} \right)\)

 

\(M_{CD}=2E\frac{1}{10}\left[2\theta _{C}+\theta _{D} \right]=\frac{2EI}{5}\theta _{C}\)

 

\(M_{DC}=2E\frac{1}{10}\left[2\theta _{D}+\theta _{C} \right]=\frac{EI}{5}\theta _{C}\)

Step 4

The corresponding joint equilibrium equations are written as,\(M_{BA}+M_{BC}=0 \)and \(M_{CB}+M_{CD}=0\)

Step 5

Substituting the expressions for\(M_{BA},M_{BC},M_{CB},M_{BC}\)and\(M_{CD}\) from step 3, we
have,\(\frac{2EI}{5}\theta _{B}-21.33+\frac{EI}{4}\left(2\theta _{B}+\theta _{C} \right)=0\)

 

\(21.33+\frac{EI}{4} \left(\theta _{B}+2\theta _{C} \right)+\frac{2EI}{5}\theta _{C}=0 \)Simplifying these equations to isolate\(\theta _{B}\) and\(\theta _{C}\) gives:From\(\left(1\right)\rightarrow 0.9EI\theta _{B}+0.25EI\theta _{C}=21.33\)

 

From\(\left(2\right)\rightarrow 0.25EI\theta _{B}+0.9EI\theta _{C}=-21.33\)Solving (1) and (2) yields:\(\theta _{B}=\frac{32.815}{EI}\)and\(\theta _{C}=-\frac{32.815}{EI}\)This step of solving the simultaneous equations can be greatly simplified by using a calculator with this capability. Otherwise, hand calculations can be used, but these will not be shown in the text.

Step 6

Substituting the value of the rotation back into expressions for end moments calculated in step 3, the end moments can be expressed as,\(M_{AB}=6.563^{K-ft}\)

 

\(M_{BA}=13.126^{K-ft}\)

 

\(M_{BC}=-13.126^{K-ft}\)

 

\(M_{CB}=13.126^{K-ft}\)

 

\(M_{CD}=-13.126^{K-ft}\)

 

\(M_{DC}=-6.563^{K-ft}\)Based on the moments of each joints, we can easily compute the reactions
at support:Alternatively, the relative stiffness factors could have been used to solve this problem. If one were to use this concept, the relative stiffness factors for AB and BC would be as follows:\(K_{AB-rel} =\frac{2*1}{10}\left(20\right)=4\)

 

\(K_{BC-rel}=\frac{2*1}{8}\left(20\right)=5\) Here, the relative stiffness factor\(\left(\frac{2I}{L} \right)\)have been multiplied by the LCM (Least Common Multiple) to simplify the calculations. Also, E and I are not included in the relative stiffness factors because they must be constant in all members to use\(K_{rel}\)The rotations obtained using this
concept are different than those found using the actual stiffness factors because they are modified according to the LCM. The point to be noted is that, the final end moments remain the same and calculation is facilitated. If one was to use these relative stiffness factors and modified slope-deflection equations,2.2a and 2.2b \( M_{AB}= M_{FAB}+K_{AB}\left(2\theta _{A}+\theta _{B} \right)\)

 

\(M_{BA}=M_{FBA}+K_{BA}\left(2\theta _{B}+\theta _{A} \right)\)the value of \(\theta _{B}\)comes out to be 1.6408. However, the actual end moments remain the same.\(M_{AB}=\frac{EI}{5}\theta _{B}=\frac{EI}{5}*\frac{32.815}{EI}=6.563^{K-ft}\)

 

\(M_{AB-rel}=4\left[\theta _{B} \right]=4*1.6408=6.563^{K-ft}\)
