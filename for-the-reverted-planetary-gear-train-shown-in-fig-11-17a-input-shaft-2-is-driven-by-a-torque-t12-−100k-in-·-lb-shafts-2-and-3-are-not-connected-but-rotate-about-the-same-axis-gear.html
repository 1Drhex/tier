Guest Question.

For the reverted planetary gear train shown in Fig. 11.17a, input shaft 2 is driven by a torque \( T _{12}=-100 \hat{ k } \text { in } \cdot lb\). Shafts 2 and 3 are not connected but rotate about the same axis. Gear 6 is fixed to frame 1 (not shown). All gears have a diametral pitch of 10 teeth per in and a pressure angle of 20°. Perform a force analysis on the parts of the train and compute the magnitude and direction of the output torque delivered by shaft 3.

ASSUMPTIONS

The forces act in a single plane, and gravitational forces and centrifugal forces on the planet gears can be neglected.

Admin
Solution

The pitch radii of the gears are R2 = (20 teeth)/(2 · 10 teeth/in) = 1.00 in, R4 = 1.50 in, R5 = 0.80 in, and R6 = 1.70 in. The distance between centers of meshing gear pairs is (R2 +R4) = (1.00 in+1.50 in) = 2.50 in = (R5 +R6). The transmitted force is \( F_{42}^{t}= T_{12} / R_{2}=(100 in \cdot lb ) /(1 in )=100 lb \). Therefore, \( F_{42}=F_{42}^{t} / \cos \phi=100 / \cos 20^{\circ}= 106 lb. \) The free-body diagram of gear 2 is shown in Fig. 11.17b. In vector form, the results are

\(F _{12}=- F _{42}=106 lb \angle 20^{\circ}\)

Figure 11.17b also shows the free-body diagram of gear 4. The forces are

\(F _{24}=- F _{34}=106 lb \angle 20^{\circ}\)

where F34 is the force of planet arm 3 onto gear 4. Gears 4 and 5 are connected to each other but turn freely on the planet arm shaft. Thus, T54 is the torque exerted by gear 5 onto gear 4. This torque is \( T_{54}=\left(R_{4}\right) F_{24}^{t}=(1.50 in )(100 lb )=150 in \cdot lb cw \).

Turning next to the free-body diagram of gear 5 in Fig. 11.17c, we first find \(F_{65}^{t}= T_{45} / R_{5}=(150 in \cdot lb ) /(0.800 in )=188 lb \text {. Therefore, } F_{65}=(188 lb ) / \cos 20^{\circ}=200 lb.\)

In vector form, the results for gear 5 are summarized as

\(F _{65}=- F _{35}=200 lb \angle 160^{\circ}, T _{45}=150 \hat{ k } \text { in } \cdot lb\)

For gear 6, shown in Fig. 11.17c, we have

\(F _{16}=- F _{56}=200 lb \angle 160^{\circ}\)

 

\(T _{16}=R_{6} F_{56}^{t} \hat{ k }=(1.70 in )\left(200 \cos 20^{\circ} lb \right) \hat{ k }=319 \hat{ k } \text { in } \cdot lb\)

 

Note that F16 and T16 are the force and torque, respectively, exerted by the frame on gear 6.

The free-body diagram of arm 3 is shown in Fig. 11.17d. As noted earlier, the forces are assumed to act in a single plane. The two forces F43 and F53 are

\(F _{43}=- F _{34}=106 lb \angle 20^{\circ}, \quad F _{53}=- F _{35}=200 lb \angle 160^{\circ}\)

and can be summed to

\(F _{43}+ F _{53}=137 lb \angle 130.2^{\circ}\)

We now find the output shaft reaction to be

\(F _{13}=-\left( F _{43}+ F _{53}\right)=1371 b \angle-49.8^{\circ}\)

 

Using \(R _{A O}=2.50 \hat{ j }\) in and the equation

\(\sum M _{O}= T _{13}+ R _{A O} \times\left( F _{43}+ F _{53}\right)= 0\)

 

we find \(T _{13}=-221 \hat{ k } \text { in } \cdot lb.\) Therefore, the output shaft torque is

\(T _{13}=221 \hat{ k } in \cdot lb\)
