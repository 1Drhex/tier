Guest Question.

Work out a good design for \(85 \%\) conversion of a pure A feed to two packed beds.

Admin
Solution

First determine the slope of the adiabatic line and draw it lightly on Fig. \(18.11 .\)

\(\text { slope }=\frac{\mathbf{C}_{p}}{-\Delta H_{r}}=\frac{40}{80000}=\frac{1}{2000}\)

This gives a very shallow adiabatic, as sketched in Fig. E19.2a. The rate continually increases as you move along this adiabatic, thus use a mixed flow reactor operating at the optimum. To minimize the amount of catalyst needed , use the method of maximization of rectangles, so tabulate \(X_{\mathrm{A}}\) versus \(1 /\left(-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime}\right)_{\text {opt }}\)

 

\(X_A\) \(\left(-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime}\right)_{\mathrm{opt}}\) \(1 /\left(-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime}\right)_{\mathrm{opt}}\)
0.85 0.05 20
0.785 0.1 10
0.715 0.2 5
0.66 0.28 3.6
0.58 0.5 2
0.46 1 1

 

And use the method of maximization of rectangles as shown in Fig. E19.2b. Then from the performance cquation

\(\frac{W}{F_{0}}=\left(X_{\mathrm{A}}\right) \frac{1}{\left(-r_{\mathrm{A}}\right)_{\mathrm{opt}}}=\left(\begin{array}{c}\text { shaded area } \\\text { in Fig. E19.2b }\end{array}\right)\)

we have

\(W_{1}=F_{\mathrm{A} 0}(\text { area })_{1}=100(2.376)=\underline{237.6 \mathrm{~kg}}\)

and

\(W_{2}=F_{\mathrm{A} 9}(\text { area })_{2}=100(3.819)=381.9 \mathrm{~kg}\)

Now to heat exchange:

For the first reactor. If we want to cool the feed before introducing it into the first reactor we’d have to cool it to

\(820-2000(0.6)=-380 \mathrm{~K}\)

which is well below absolute zero. This is impossible. So we have to cool it somewhere inside the recycle reactor loop as shown in Fig. E19.2c. But wherever you put the exchanger the amount of heating or cooling needed is the same. So to go to \(66 \%\) conversion at \(820^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\) the amount of heat needed per mole of \(\mathrm{A}\) is

\(\underset{heat\: in}{(820-300)}40+\underset{heat\: out}{0.66(-80000)}=-32000\: J/mol .\)

But for \(100 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{s}\) of feed

\(Q_{1}=(32000 \mathrm{~J} / \mathrm{mol})(100 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{s})=-3.2 \mathrm{MW}(\text { cooling })\)

For the second reactor. To go from \(X_{\mathrm{A}}=0.66\) at \(820 \mathrm{~K}\) to \(X_{\mathrm{A}}=0.85\) at 750 \(\mathrm{K}\) requires, per mole

\((750-820) 40+(0.85-0.66)(-80000)=-18000 \mathrm{~J} / \mathrm{mol}\)

So for \(100 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{s}\)

\(Q_{2}=(-18000)(100)=-1.8 \mathrm{MW} \text { (cooling) }\)

Similarly for the exchanger needed to cool the exit stream from \(750 \mathrm{~K}\) to \(300 \mathrm{~K}\)

\(Q_{3}=100(40)(300-750)=-1.8 \mathrm{MW}\)

So our recommended design is shown in Fig. E19.2c.
