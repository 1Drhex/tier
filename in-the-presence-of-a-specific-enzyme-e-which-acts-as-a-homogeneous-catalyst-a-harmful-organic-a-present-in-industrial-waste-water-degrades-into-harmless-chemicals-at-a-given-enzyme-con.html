Guest Question.

In the presence of a specific enzyme \(\mathrm{E}\), which acts as a homogeneous catalyst, a harmful organic A present in industrial waste water degrades into harmless chemicals. At a given enzyme concentration \(C_{\mathrm{E}}\) tests in a laboratory mixed flow reactor give the following results:

\(\begin{array}{r|crrrrrrr}C_{\mathrm{A} 0}, \mathrm{mmol} / \mathrm{m}^{3} & 2 & 5 & 6 & 6 & 11 & 14 & 16 & 24 \\C_{\mathrm{A}}, \mathrm{mmol} / \mathrm{m}^{3} & 0.5 & 3 & 1 & 2 & 6 & 10 & 8 & 4 \\\tau, \min & 30 & 1 & 50 & 8 & 4 & 20 & 20 & 4\end{array}\)

We wish to treat \(0.1 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{min}\) of this waste water having \(C_{\mathrm{A} 2}=10 \mathrm{mmol} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\) to \(90 \%\) conversion with this enzyme at concentration \(C_{\mathrm{E}}\).

(a) One possibility is to use a long tubular reactor (assume plug flow) with possible recycle of exit fluid. What design do you recommend? Give the size of the reactor, tell if it should be used with recycle, and if so determine the recycle flow rate in cubic meters per minute \(\left(\mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{min}\right) .\) Sketch your recommended design.

(b) Another possibility is to use one or two stirred tanks (assume ideal). What two-tank design do you recommend, and how much better is it than the one-tank arrangement?

(c) What arrangement of plug flow and mixed flow reactors would you use to minimize the total volume of reactors needed? Sketch your recommended design and show the size of units selected. We should mention that separation and recycle of part of the product stream is not allowed.

 

Admin
Solution

First calculate and tabulate \(1 /-r_{\mathrm{A}}\) at the measured \(C_{\mathrm{A}} .\) This is shown as the last line of Table E6.3. Next, draw the \(1 /-r_{\mathrm{A}}\) vs. \(C_{\mathrm{A}}\) curve. This is seen to be Ushaped so we must prepare to deal with an autocatalytic type reacting system.

Part (a) Solution. From the \(-1 / r_{\mathrm{A}}\) vs. \(C_{\mathrm{A}}\) curve we see that we should use plug flow with recycle. From Fig. E6.3a we find

\(\begin{aligned}C_{\text {Ain }} &=6.6 \mathrm{mmol} / \mathrm{m}^{3} \\\qquad \begin{aligned}\boldsymbol{R} &=\frac{10-6.6}{6.6-1}=0.607\end{aligned} \\\qquad \begin{aligned}V &=\tau v_{0}=\operatorname{area}\left(v_{0}\right)=[(10-1)(1.2)](0.1)=\underline{1.08 \mathrm{~m}^{3}} \\v_{R} &=v_{0} \boldsymbol{R}=0.1(0.607)=\underline{0.0607 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{min}}\end{aligned}\end{aligned}\)

Part (b) Solution. Drawing slopes and diagonals according to the method of maximization of rectangles we end up with Fig. E6.3b.

Part (c) Solution. Following the reasoning in this chapter we should use a mixed flow reactor followed by a plug flow reactor. So with Fig. E6.3c we find

\(\begin{aligned}&\left.\begin{array}{ll}\text { For the MFR } & V_{m}=v \tau_{m}=0.1(1.2)=0.12 \mathrm{~m}^{3} \\\text { For the PFR } & V_{p}=v \tau_{p}=0.1(5.8)=0.58 \mathrm{~m}^{3}\end{array}\right\} V_{\text {total }}=\underline{\underline{0.7 \mathrm{~m}^{3}}}\\&\text { Note which scheme (a) or (b) or (c) gives the smallest size of reactors. }\end{aligned}\)
