Guest Question.

Loan Repayment Under Continuous Compounding

You need $30,000 immediately in order to make a down payment on a new home. Suppose that you can borrow the money from your insurance company. You will be required to repay the loan in equal amounts made every 6 months over the next five years. The nominal interest rate being charged is 8% compounded continuously. What is the amount of each payment?

Admin
Solution

Given: P = $30,000, r = 8% per year, and N = 5 years (or 10 semiannual periods).
Find: A.

Note that 8% APR is stated over a year, so the APR over a 6-month period is simply 8%/2 = 4%.

\(i = e^{r/K} -1 = e^{0.08/2} -1= 4.0811\%\) per semiannual period.

N = 5 \(\times\) 2 = 10
A = (A/P,4.0811%,10)
= $3,713.68

Or, using the formula in Table 4.3,

Table 4.3 Summary of Selected Continuous Compounding Formulas with Discrete Payments
Flow Type Find Given Factor Notation Formula
Compound-Amount F P (F/P, r, N) \(F=P \left(e^{rN} \right)\)
Present-Worth P F (P/F, r, N) \(P=F \left(e^{-rN} \right)\)
Compound-Amount F A (F/A, r, N) \(F = A \left[\frac{\left(e^{r} \right) ^{N}-1 }{e^{r}-1 } \right]\)
Sinking-Fund A F (A/F, r, N) \(A = F \left[\frac{e^{r}-1 }{\left(e^{r} \right) ^{N}-1 } \right]\)
Present-Worth P A (P/A, r, N) \(P = A \left[\frac{1-e^{-rN} }{e^{r}-1 } \right]\)
Capital Recovery A P (A/P, r, N) \(A = P \left[\frac{e^{r}-1 }{1-e^{-rN} } \right]\)

\(A=\$30,000 \left[\frac{e^{0.04}-1 }{1-e^{-0.04\left(10\right) } } \right]\)

= $30,000(0.123789)
= $3,713,67
