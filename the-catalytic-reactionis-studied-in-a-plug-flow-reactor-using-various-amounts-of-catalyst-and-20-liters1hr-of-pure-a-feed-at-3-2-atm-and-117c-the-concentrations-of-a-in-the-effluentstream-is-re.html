Guest Question.

The catalytic reaction

\(\mathrm{A} \rightarrow 4 \mathrm{R}\)

is studied in a plug flow reactor using various amounts of catalyst and 20 liters/ hr of pure A feed at 3.2 atm and \(117^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\). The concentrations of \(\mathrm{A}\) in the effluent stream is recorded for the various runs as follows.

4 3 2 1 Run
0.16 0.08 0.04 0.02 Catalyst used ,Kg
0.029 0.044 0.06 0.074 \(C_{Aout}\) mol/liter

(a) Find the rate equation for this reaction, using the integral method of analysis

(b) Repeat part (a), using the differential method of analysis.

Admin
Solution

(a) Integral Analysis. we have for all experimental runs

\(C_{\mathrm{A} 0}=0.1 \mathrm{~mol} / \text { liter }, \quad F_{\mathrm{A} 0}=2 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{hr}, \quad \varepsilon_{\mathrm{A}}=3\)

Since the concentration varies significantly during the runs, the experimental reactor should be considered to be an integral reactor.

As a first guess try a first-order rate expression. Then for plug flow Eq.44 gives:

\({k}'{\tau }'={{k}''}'{{\tau }''}'=(1+\varepsilon _{A})In\frac{1}{1-X_{Aout}}-\varepsilon _{A}X_{Aout}\: \: [-]\) (Eq.44)

 

\(k^{\prime} \frac{C_{\mathrm{A} 0} W}{F_{\mathrm{A} 0}}=\left(1+\varepsilon_{\mathrm{A}}\right) \ln \frac{1}{1-X_{\mathrm{A}}}-\varepsilon_{\mathrm{A}} X_{\mathrm{A}}\)and with \(\varepsilon_{\mathrm{A}}, C_{\mathrm{A} 0}\), and \(F_{\mathrm{A} 0}\) replaced by numerical values this becomes

\(\left(4 \ln \frac{1}{1-X_{\mathrm{A}}}-3 X_{\mathrm{A}}\right)=k^{\prime}\left(\frac{W}{20}\right)\)

(b) Differential Analysis. Equation 54 shows that the rate of reaction is given by the slope of the \(X_{\mathrm{A}}\) versus \(W / F_{\mathrm{A} 0}\) curve. The tabulation (Table \(\mathrm{E} 18.3 \mathrm{~b}\) ) based on the measured slopes in Fig. E18.3b shows how the rate of reaction is found at various \(C_{\mathrm{A}}\). The linear relation between \(-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime}\) and \(C_{\mathrm{A}}\) in Fig. E18.3c then gives for the rate equation:

\(-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime}=\left(93 \frac{\text { liters }}{\mathrm{hr} \cdot \mathrm{kg} \text { cat }}\right)\left(C_{\mathrm{A}}, \frac{\mathrm{mol}}{\text { liter }}\right)\)
