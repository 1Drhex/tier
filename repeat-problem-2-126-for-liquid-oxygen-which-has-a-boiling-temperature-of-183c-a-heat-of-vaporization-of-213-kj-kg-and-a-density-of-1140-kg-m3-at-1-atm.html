Guest Question.

Repeat for liquid oxygen, which has a boiling temperature of -183°C, a heat of vaporization of 213 kJ/kg, and a density of \( 1140 kg / m ^{3} \) at 1 atm.

Admin
Solution

A spherical liquid oxygen container is subjected to specified temperature on the inner surface and convection on the outer surface. The mathematical formulation, the variation of temperature, and the rate of evaporation of oxygen are to be determined for steady one-dimensional heat transfer.

Assumptions 1 Heat conduction is steady and one-dimensional since there is no change with time and there is thermal symmetry about the midpoint. 2 Thermal conductivity is constant. 3 There is no heat generation.

Properties The thermal conductivity of the tank is given to be \( k = 18 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \). Also, \(h_{ fg } = 213 kJ / kg \) for oxygen.

Analysis (a) Noting that heat transfer is one-dimensional in the radial r direction, the mathematical formulation of this problem can be expressed as

\( \frac{d}{d r}\left(r^{2} \frac{d T}{d r}\right)=0 \)

 

and \( T\left(r_{1}\right)=T_{1}=-183^{\circ} C \)

 

\( -k \frac{d T\left(r_{2}\right)}{d r}=h\left[T\left(r_{2}\right)-T_{\infty}\right] \)

 

(b) Integrating the differential equation once with respect to r gives

\( r^{2} \frac{d T}{d r}=C_{1} \)

 

Dividing both sides of the equation above by r to bring it to a readily integrable form and then integrating,

\( \frac{d T}{d r}=\frac{C_{1}}{r^{2}} \quad \rightarrow \quad T(r)=-\frac{C_{1}}{r}+C_{2} \)

 

where \( C_{1} \) and \( C_{2} \) are arbitrary constants. Applying the boundary conditions give

\( r=r_{1} \) :

\( T\left(r_{1}\right)=-\frac{C_{1}}{r_{1}}+C_{2}=T_{1} \)

 

\( r=r_{2} \) :

\( -k \frac{C_{1}}{r_{2}^{2}}=h\left(-\frac{C_{1}}{r_{2}}+C_{2}-T_{\infty}\right)\)

 

Solving for \( C_{1} \) and \( C_{2} \) simultaneously gives

\( C_{1}=\frac{r_{2}\left(T_{1}-T_{\infty}\right)}{1-\frac{r_{2}}{r_{1}}-\frac{k}{h r_{2}}} \text { and } C_{2}=T_{1}+\frac{C_{1}}{r_{1}}=T_{1}+\frac{T_{1}-T_{\infty}}{1-\frac{r_{2}}{r_{1}}-\frac{k}{h r_{2}}} \frac{r_{2}}{r_{1}} \)

 

Substituting \( C_{1} \) and \( C_{2} \) into the general solution, the variation of temperature is determined to be

\( T(r)=-\frac{C_{1}}{r}+T_{1}+\frac{C_{1}}{r_{1}}=C_{1}\left(\frac{1}{r_{1}}-\frac{1}{r}\right)+T_{1}=\frac{T_{1}-T_{\infty}}{1-\frac{r_{2}}{r_{1}}-\frac{k}{h r_{2}}}\left(\frac{r_{2}}{r_{1}}-\frac{r_{2}}{r}\right)+T_{1}\)

 

\( =\frac{(-183-20)^{\circ} C }{1-\frac{2.1}{2}-\frac{18 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C }{\left(25 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)(2.1 m )}}\left(\frac{2.1}{2}-\frac{2.1}{r}\right)+(-183){ }^{\circ} C =516.7(1.05-2.1 / r)-183 \)

 

(c) The rate of heat transfer through the wall and the rate of evaporation of nitrogen are determined from

\( \dot{Q}=-k A \frac{d T}{d x}=-k\left(4 \pi r^{2}\right) \frac{C_{1}}{r^{2}}=-4 \pi k C_{1}=-4 \pi k \frac{r_{2}\left(T_{1}-T_{\infty}\right)}{1-\frac{r_{2}}{r_{1}}-\frac{k}{h r_{2}}} \)

 

\( =-4 \pi\left(18 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right) \frac{(2.1 m )(-183-20)^{\circ} C }{1-\frac{2.1}{2}-\frac{18 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C }{\left(25 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)(2.1 m )}} = – 2 4 5 , 4 5 0 W\) (to the tank since negative)

 

\( \dot{m}=\frac{\dot{Q}}{h_{f g}}=\frac{245,450 J / s }{213,000 J / kg }= 1 . 1 5 k g / s\)
