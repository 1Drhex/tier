Guest Question.

Calculate the loads in the members of the singly redundant pin-jointed framework shown in Fig. 5.9.The members AC and BD are 30 \(mm ^{2}\) in cross-section, and all other members are 20 \(mm ^{2}\) in cross-section. The members AD, BC, and DC are each 800 mm long. E = 200,000 \(N / mm ^{2}\).

Repeat using MATLAB and columns 1–4 of Table 5.3.

Admin
Solution

From the geometry of the framework \(A \hat{ B } D = C \hat{ B } D =30^{\circ} \text { ; }\) therefore, \(BD = AC =800 \sqrt{3} mm\). Choosing CD as the redundant member and proceeding from Eq. (ii) of , we have

\(\frac{1}{E} \sum_{i=1}^{k} \frac{F_{i} L_{i}}{A_{i}} \frac{\partial F_{i}}{\partial R}=0\) (i)

From Table 5.3, we have

\(\sum_{i=1}^{k} \frac{F_{i} L_{i}}{A_{i}} \frac{\partial F_{i}}{\partial R}=-268+129.2 R=0\)

 

Hence, R = 2.1 N and the forces in the members are tabulated in column ➆ of Table 5.3.
