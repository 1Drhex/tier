Guest Question.

Check the adequacy of the 8 – in reinforced concrete wall depicted in Fig . 9.3 for the following loads :

Dead load= 120 psf
Roof live load =20 psf
Wind load= 20 psf

The wall is reinforced with a single layer of No. 3 bars spaced at 10 in on center and located at the middle of the wall . Assume that the gravity loads act through the centroid of the section and that the ends of the wall are pinned . Also assume normal-weight concrete with \(f '_{c}= 4,000\) psi and Grade 60 reinforcement ( \(f_{y}= 60,000\) psi ) and that the frame is nonsway.

 

Admin
Solution

Step 1 : Determine the axial loads and bending moments on the wall. The axial loads and bending moments are determined for a 1-ft design strip.

\(P_{D}= 0.120 × \frac{30}{2} =1.8\) kips / ft
\(P_{L,r}= 0.020 × \frac{30}{ 2}= 0.3\) kips / ft

The wind load does not cause any axial load on the wall, but it does cause the following bending
moment that acts at the midheight section of the wall:

\(M_{W} = 0.020 × \frac{14^{2}}{8} = 0.5\) ft kips/ft = 6.0 in kips/ft

A summary of the factored load combinations is given in Table 9.3. The axial loads and bending moments are expressed in kips and inch-kips, respectively, per foot length of wall.
Step 2: Determine if slenderness effects need to be considered. Because the frameis a nonsway
frame, slenderness effects need not be considered where Eq. (8.19) is satisfied:

\(\frac{ k\ell_{u}}{r} \leq 34-12 \left(\frac {M_{1} }{M_{2}} \right)\leq 40\)

For this 8-in wall, in the direction of analysis (i.e., bending about the minor axis) r = 0.3 × 8 = 2.4 in.
Thus,

\(\frac{ k\ell_{u}}{r}=\frac{1.0 × 14 × 12}{2.4}= 70 > 40\)

Thus, slenderness effects must be considered in the design of this wall in this direction.
Step 3: Determine the magnified moments in the column using the moment magnification procedure. In lieu of an elastic or a nonlinear second-order analysis, the moment magnification procedure of ACI 10.10.6 is used to determine the magnified factored moments due to second-order effects.

Calculations are provided for load combination number 4 of Table 9.3.
From Eq. (8.22),

\(M_{c} = δ_{ns} M_{2}\)

where

\(δ_{ns} = \frac{C_{m}}{1-(P_{u}/0.75 P_{c} })≥1.0\)

Because there are transverse loads between the supports of this wall, Cm = 1.0 (ACI 10.10.6.4)
The critical buckling load Pc is determined by Eq. (8.25):

\(P_{c}=\frac{\pi ^{2}EI}{(K\ell_{u})^{2}}\)

Because the wall has one layer of vertical reinforcement, use Eq. (9.3) to determine the stiffness
EI:

\(\frac{0.1E_{c}I_{g}}{\beta}≤EI =\frac{E_{c} I_{g}} {\beta}\left(0.5 -\frac{e}{h}\right)≤ \frac{0.4 E_{c} I_{g}}{\beta}\)

The modulus of elasticity of the concrete is determined by ACI 8.5.1:

\(E_{c}=w^{1.5}_{c}33\sqrt {f '_{c}}=(150)^{1.5} \times 33\sqrt{4,000}=3,834,254\) psi

For a 1-ft design strip,

\(I_{g}=\frac{12 \times 8^{3}}{12}=512 in^{4}\)

Because the dead load is the only sustained load, \(β_{d}\) is determined as follows:

\(β_{d}=\frac{1.2D}{1.2D+1.6W+0.5L_{r}} =0.94\)

The eccentricity \(e = M_{u}/P_{u} = 9.6/2.3 = 4.2\) in.
Therefore, the EI from Eq. (9.3) is

\(EI=\frac{E_{c}I_{g}}{\beta}\left(0.5 -\frac{e}{h}\right)=\frac{E_{c}I_{g}}{\beta}\left(0.5 -\frac {4.2} {8.0}\right)=-0.03 \frac{E_{c}I_{g}}{\beta}<0.1 \frac{E_{c}I_{g}}{\beta}\); use\( 0.1 \frac{ E_{c} I_{g}}{\beta}\)

\(\rho=\frac{0.11\times (12/10)}{8 \times 12}= 0.0014 >0.0012 \)

\(\beta =0.9 +0.5 \beta^{2}_{d}-12\rho=0.9+(0.5 \times 0.94^{2})-(12 \times 0.0014)=1.33\)

Thus,

\(EI=0.1 \frac{ E_{c} I_{g}}{\beta}=\frac{0.1 \times 3,834 \times 512}{1.33}=147.6 \times 10^{3} kip in^{2}\)

Determine the critical buckling load \(P_{c}\) :

\(P_{c}=\frac{\pi^{2}EI}{(K\ell_{u})^{2}} =\frac {\pi ^{2}\times 147.6\times 10^{3}}{(1.0\times 14\times 12)^{2}}=51.6\) kips

The moment magnification factor δns is equal to the following:

\(δ_{ns} = \frac{C_{m}}{1-(P_{u}/0.75P_{c})}= \frac{1.0}{1-[2.3/(0.75\times51.6)]} =1.06≥1.0\)

Thus,

\(M_{c} = δ_{ns} M_{2} = 1.06 × 9.6 = 10.2 \) in kips

Check the minimum factored bending moment requirement [see Eq. (8.29)]:

\(M_{2,min} = P_{u}(0.6 + 0.03h) = 2.3 × [0.6 + (0.03 × 8)] = 1.9\) in kips < \(M_{c}\)

Therefore, the factored axial load and moment in this load combination become \(P_{u} = 2.3\) kips and \(M_{u} = M_{c} = 10.2\) in kips.
Similar calculations can be performed for the other load combinations.
