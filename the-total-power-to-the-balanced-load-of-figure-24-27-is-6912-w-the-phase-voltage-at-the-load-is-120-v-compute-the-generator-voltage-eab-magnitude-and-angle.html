Guest Question.

The total power to the balanced load of Figure 24–27 is 6912 W. The phase voltage at the load is 120 V. Compute the generator voltage \(E_{AB}\), magnitude and angle.

Admin
Solution

Consider the single-phase equivalent in Figure 24–28. \(I_{a}\) is computed as follows:

\(P_{an}=\frac{P_{T}}{3} =\frac{1}{3} (6912)=2304 W\)

 

\(V_{an}=120 V\)

 

\(\theta _{an}=\cos^{-1}(0.8)=36.87^{\circ}\)

 

\(P_{an}=V_{an}I_{a}\cos\theta _{an}\)

Therefore,

\(I_{a}=\frac{P_{an}}{V_{an}\cos\theta _{an}} =\frac{2304}{(120)(0.8)} =24.0A\)

Select \(V_{an}\) as reference. \(V_{an} = 120 V\angle 0^{\circ}\). Thus, \(I_{a} = 24 A\angle -36.87^{\circ}\) (since power factor was given as lagging).

\(E_{AN}=I_{a}\times Z_{line}+V_{an}\)

 

\(=(24\angle -36.87)(0.12+j0.09)+120\angle0^{\circ}=123.6V\angle0^{\circ}\)

 

\(E_{AB}=\sqrt{3} E_{AN}\angle 30^{\circ}=214.1 V\angle 30^{\circ}\)
