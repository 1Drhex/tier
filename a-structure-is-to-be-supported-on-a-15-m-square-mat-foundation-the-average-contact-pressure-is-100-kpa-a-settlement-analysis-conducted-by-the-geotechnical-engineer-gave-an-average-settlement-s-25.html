Guest Question.

A structure is to be supported on a 15-m square mat foundation. The average contact pressure is \( 100 kPa \). A settlement analysis conducted by the geotechnical engineer gave an average settlement \( S = 25 mm \). Recommend design values for the modulus of subgrade reaction KS which may be used in a pseudo-coupled analysis.

Admin
Solution

Step 1. Estimate \( K_{s,av} \)
Equation : \( K_{s} = \frac{q}{\delta } \)

Where

\( q = \) bearing pressure

\( \delta = \) deflection ( settlement )

 

Average modulus of subgrade reaction \( = K_{s, av} = \frac{100}{0.025} = 4000 kN/m^{3} \)

Step 2. Divide the mat area into three zones and compute the area of each zone. Note that the innermost (central) zone shall be half as wide and half as long as the mat.

\( Area of zone A \left ( central \right ) : A_{A} = 7.5 \times 7.5 = 56.25 m^{2} \)

 

\( Area of zone B \left ( interior \right ) : A_{B} = 11.25 \times 11.25 – 7.5 \times 7.5 = 70.313 m^{2} \)

 

\( Area of zone C \left ( exterior \right ) : A_{C} = 15 \times 15 – 11.25 \times 11.25 = 98.438 m^{2} \)

 

Step 3.Assign a \( K_{S} \) value to each zone.
The \( K_{S} \) values should progressively increase from the centre such that the outermost zone (exterior zone) has a \( K_{S} \) about twice as large as the innermost zone (central zone). In this case, let us use:

\( K_{s,C} = 2 K_{s,A}; K_{s,B} = 1.5 K_{s,A} \)

 

Step 4. Compute the design \( K_{S} \) values.
Summation of the product of each zone area and its \( K_{S} \) should equal to the product of the mat area and \( K_{s,av} \).

\( A_{A} \left ( K_{s,A} \right ) + A_{B} \left ( K_{s,B} \right ) + A_{C} \left ( K_{s,C} \right ) = \left ( A_{A} + A_{B} + A_{C} \right ) K_{s,av}\)

 

\( 56.25 \left ( K_{s,A} \right ) + 70.313 \left ( 1.5 \right ) \left ( K_{s,A} \right ) + 98.438 \left ( 2 \right ) \left ( K_{s,A} \right ) = \left ( 15^{2} \right ) K_{s,av}\)

 

\( K_{s,A} = \frac{225}{358.6} = 0.627 K_{s,av} \)

 

\( K_{s,A} = 0.627 \times 4000 = 2508 kN/m^{3} \)

 

\( K_{s,B} = 1.5 K_{s,A} = 1.5 \times 2508 = 3762 kN/m^{3} \)

 

\( K_{s,V} = 2 K_{s,A} = 2 \times 2508 = 5016 kN/m^{3} \)
