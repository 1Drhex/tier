Guest Question.

In Figure P5.16, aluminum [E = 70 GPa] links (1) and (2) support rigid beam ABC. Link (1) has a cross-sectional area of 300 \(mm ^{2}\) and link (2) has a crosssectional area of 450 \(mm ^{2}\) . For an applied load of P = 55 kN, determine the rigid beam deflection at point B.

Admin
Solution

From a FBD of the rigid beam, write two equilibrium equations:

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma F_{y}=F_{1}+F_{2}-55 kN =0 (a)\\&\Sigma M_{A}=(2,200 mm ) F_{2}-(1,400 mm )(55 kN )=0 (b)\end{aligned}\)

Solve Eq. (b) for \(F_{2} : \)

\(F_{2}=\frac{(1,400 mm )(55 kN )}{2,200 mm }=35 kN\)

and backsubstitute into Eq. (a) to obtain \(F_{1} : \)

\(F_{1}=55 kN -F_{2}=55 kN -35 kN =20 kN\)

Next, determine the deformations in links (1) and (2):

\(\begin{aligned}&\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}=\frac{(20,000 N )(2,500 mm )}{\left(300 mm ^{2}\right)\left(70,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=2.380952 mm \\&\delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}=\frac{(35,000 N )(4,000 mm )}{\left(450 mm ^{2}\right)\left(70,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=4.444444 mm\end{aligned}\)

Since the connections at A and C are perfect, the rigid beam deflections at these joints are equal to the deformations of links (1) and (2), respectively:

\(v_{A}=\delta_{1}=2.380952 mm \quad \text { and } \quad v_{C}=\delta_{2}=4.444444 mm\)

The deflection at B can be determined from similar triangles:

\(\frac{v_{C}-v_{A}}{2,200 mm }=\frac{v_{B}-v_{A}}{1,400 mm }\)

Solve this expression for \(v_{B}\):

\(\begin{aligned}v_{B} &=\frac{1,400 mm }{2,200 mm }\left(v_{C}-v_{A}\right)+v_{A} \\&=\frac{1,400 mm }{2,200 mm }(4.444444 mm -2.380952 mm )+2.380952 mm \\&=(0.636364)(2.053492 mm )+2.380952 mm \\&=3.69 mm \downarrow\end{aligned}\)
