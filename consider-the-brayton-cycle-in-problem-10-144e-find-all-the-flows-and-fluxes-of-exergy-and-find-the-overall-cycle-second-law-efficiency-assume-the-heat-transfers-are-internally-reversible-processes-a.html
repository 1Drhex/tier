Guest Question.

Consider the Brayton cycle in . Find all the flows and fluxes of exergy and find the overall cycle second-law efficiency. Assume the heat transfers are internally reversible processes and we then neglect any external irreversibility.

Admin
Solution

Efficiency is from Eq.10.1

\(\eta=\dot{ W }_{ NET } / \dot{ Q }_{ H }=\frac{ w _{ net }}{ q _{ H }}=1- r _{ p }^{-( k -1) / k }=1-16^{-0.4 / 1.4}= 0 . 5 4 7\)

from the required power we can find the needed heat transfer

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{ Q }_{ H }=\dot{ W }_{ net } / \eta=14000 / 0.547=25594 Btu / s \\&\dot{ m }=\dot{ Q }_{ H } / q _{ H }=25594( Btu / s ) / 400 Btu / lbm = 6 3 . 9 9 lbm / s\end{aligned}\)

Temperature after compression is

\(T _{2}= T _{1} r _{ p }^{( k -1) / k }=519.67 \times 16^{0.4 / 1.4}=1148 R\)

The highest temperature is after combustion

\(T _{3}= T _{2}+ q _{ H } / C _{ p }=1148+\frac{400}{0.24}= 2 8 1 5 ~ R\)

For the exit flow I need the exhaust temperature

\(T _{4}= T _{3} r _{ p }^{-( k -1) / k }=2815 \times 16^{-0.2857}=1274.8 R\)

The high T exergy input from combustion is

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{\Phi}_{ H } &=\dot{ m }\left(\psi_{3}-\psi_{2}\right)=\dot{ m }\left[ h _{3}- h _{2}- T \left( s _{3}- s _{2}\right)\right] \\&=63.99\left[400-536.67 \times 0.24 \ln \left(\frac{2815}{1148}\right)\right]= 1 7 8 9 5 Btu / s\end{aligned}\)

Since the low T exergy flow out is lost the second law efficiency is

\(\eta_{ II }=\dot{ W }_{ NET } / \dot{\Phi}_{ H }=14000 / 17895= 0 . 7 8 2\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{\Phi}_{\text {flow out }}=\dot{ m }\left(\psi_{4}-\psi_{o}\right)=\dot{ m }\left[ h _{4}- h _{ o }- T \left( s _{4}- s _{ o }\right)\right] \\&\quad=63.99\left[0.24(1274.8-536.7)-536.7 \times 0.24 \ln \left(\frac{1274.8}{536.7}\right)\right]= 4 2 0 5 Btu / s\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{\Phi}_{\text {flow in }}=\dot{ m }\left(\psi_{1}-\psi_{ o }\right)=\dot{ m }\left[ h _{1}- h _{ o }- T \left( s _{1}- s _{ o }\right)\right] \\&\quad=63.99\left[0.24(60-77)-536.7 \times 0.24 \ln \left(\frac{519.7}{536.7}\right)\right]= 4 . 2 Btu / s\end{aligned}\)
