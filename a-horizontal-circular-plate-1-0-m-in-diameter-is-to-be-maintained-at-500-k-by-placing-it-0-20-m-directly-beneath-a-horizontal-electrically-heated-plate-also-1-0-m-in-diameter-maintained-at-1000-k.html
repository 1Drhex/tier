Guest Question.

A horizontal circular plate, 1.0 m in diameter, is to be maintained at 500 K by placing it 0.20 m directly beneath a horizontal electrically heated plate, also 1.0 m in diameter, maintained at 1000 K. The assembly is exposed to black surroundings at 300K and convection heat transfer is negligible. Estimate the electrical input to the heater and the net rate of heat transfer to the plate if the emissivity of the heater is 0.75 and the emissivity of the plate is 0.5.

Admin
Solution

For surface 1:
For a plane surface:
\( F_{11}=0 \space and \space A_{1}F_{11}=0\)
Using the nomenclature of Figure below:
For coaxial parallel discs with \(r_{1}=r_{2}=0.5m \space and \space L=0.2m\):
\(R_{1}=r_{1}/L=(0.5/0.20)=2.5\)
\(R_{2}=r_{2}/L=(0.5/0.20)=2.5\)
and:
\(S=1+(1+R^{2}_{2})/R^{2}_{1} =1+(1+2.5^{2})/2.5^{2}=2.16\)
From Figure below(ii):
\(F_{12}=0.5\left\{S-[S^{2}-4(r_{2}/r_{1})^{2}]^{0.5}\right\}\)
\(=0.5\times \left\{2.16-[(2.16)^{2}-4(0.5/0.5)^{2}]^{0.5}\right\}=0.672\)
\(A_{1}F_{12}=(1.07\times 0.672)=0.719m^{2}\)
and, from the summation rule:
\(A_{1}F_{13}=A_{1}-(A_{1}F_{11}+A_{1}F_{12})=1.07-(0+0.719)=0.350m^{2}\)
For surface 2:
For a plane surface:
\(A_{2}F_{22}=0\)
and by the reciprocity rule:
\(A_{2}F_{21}=A_{1}F_{12}=0.719m^{2}\)
By symmetry:
\(A_{2}F_{23}=A_{1}F_{13}=0.350m^{2}\)
For surface 3:
By the reciprocity rule:
\(A_{3}F_{31}=A_{1}F_{13}=0.350m^{2}\)
and:
\(A_{3}F_{32}=A_{2}F_{23}=0.350m^{2}\)
From the summation rule:
\(A_{3}F_{33}=A_{3}-(A_{3}F_{31}+A_{3}F_{32})=0.785-(0.350+0.350)=0.085m^{2}\)
From Equation
\(E_{b}=\sigma T^{4}\) :
\(E_{b1}=\sigma T^{4}_{1}=(5.67\times 10^{-8}\times 1000^{4})=5.67\times 10^{4}W/m^{2} \space or \space 56.7kW/m^{2}\)
\(E_{b2}=\sigma T^{4}_{2}=(5.67\times 10^{-8}\times 500^{4})=3.54\times 10^{3}W/m^{2} \space or \space 3.54kW/m^{2}\)
\(E_{b3}=\sigma T^{4}_{3}=(5.67\times 10^{-8}\times 300^{4})=0.459\times 10^{3}W/m^{2} \space or \space 0.459kW/m^{2}\)
Since surface 3 is a black body,
\(q_{O3}=E_{b3}=0.459kW/m^{2}\)
From Equations
\(A_{2}(q_{O2}-e_{2}E_{b2})/r_{2}=A_{1}F_{12}q_{O1}+A_{2}F_{22}q_{O2}+A_{3}F_{32}q_{O3}+…+A_{j}F_{j2}q_{Oj}+…A_{n}F_{n2}q_{On}\) ,
\(A_{1}F_{12}q_{O1}+[A_{2}F_{22}-(A_{2}/r_{2})]q_{O2}+A_{3}F_{32}q_{O3}+…+A_{j}F_{j2}q_{Oj}+…+A_{n}F_{n2}q_{On}=(A_{2}e_{2}/r_{2})E_{b2}\) :
\((A_{1}F_{11}-A_{1}/r_{1})q_{O1}+A_{2}F_{21}q_{O2}+A_{3}F_{31}q_{O3}\)
\(=-E_{b1}A_{1}e_{1}/r_{1}\times (0-4.28)q_{O1}+0.719q_{O2}+(0.350\times 0.459)\)
\(=-(56.7\times 1.07\times 0.75)/0.25\)
or:
\(0.719q_{O2}-4.28q_{O1}=-182\) (1)
and:
\((A_{1}F_{12}q_{O1})+(A_{2}F_{22}-A_{2}/r_{2})q_{O2}=-E_{b2}A_{2}e_{2}/r_{2}\)
\(0.719q_{O1}+(0-1.07/0.5)q_{O2}=-(3.54\times 1.07\times 0.5)/0.5\)
or:
\(0.719q_{O1}-2.14q_{O2}=-3.79\) (2)
Solving Equations. (1), (2) simultaneously gives:
\(q_{O1}=45.42kW/m^{2} \space and \space q_{O2}=17.16kW/m^{2}\)
power input to the heater = rate of heat transfer from the heater
From Equation
\(Q_{i}=A_{i}e_{i}E_{bi}/r_{i}+(A_{i}/r_{i} )[q_{Oi} (1-e_{i} ) -q_{Oi} ] = (A_{i}e_{i}/r_{i})(E_{bi}-q_{Oi})\) :
\(Q_{1}=(A_{1}e_{1}/r_{1})(E_{b1}-q_{O1})=(1.07\times 0.75/0.25)(56.7-45.42)=36.2kW\)
Again, from last equation , the rate of heat transfer to the plate is:
\(Q_{2}=(A_{2}e_{2}/r_{2})(E_{b2}-q_{O2})=(1.07\times 0.5/0.25)(3.54-17.16)=-14.57kW\)
where the negative sign indicates heat transfer to the plate.
