Guest Question.

As an angular momentum, a particle’s spin must flip under time reversal (). The action of time-reversal on a spinor (Section 4.4.1) is in fact

\( \hat{\Theta}\left(\begin{array}{l} a \\ b \end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{c} -b^{*} \\ a^{*} \end{array}\right) \) , (6.84).

so that, in addition to the complex conjugation, the up and down components are interchanged.

(a) Show that \( \hat{\Theta}^{2}=-1 \) for a spin-1/2 particle.

(b) Consider an eigenstate \( \left|\psi_{n}\right\rangle \) of a time-reversal invariant Hamiltonian (Equation 6.83) with energy \( E_{n} . \text { We know that }\left|\psi_{n}^{\prime}\right\rangle=\hat{\Theta}\left|\psi_{n}\right\rangle \) is also an eigenstate of \( \hat{H} \) with the same energy \( E_{n} \). There two possibilities: either \( \left|\psi_{n}^{\prime}\right\rangle \text { and }\left|\psi_{n}\right\rangle \) are the same state (meaning \( \left|\psi_{n}^{\prime}\right\rangle=c\left|\psi_{n}\right\rangle \) for some
complex constant or they are distinct states. Show that the first case leads to a contradiction in the case of a spin -1/2 particle, meaning the energy level must be (at least) two-fold degenerate in that case.

\( [\hat{\Theta}, \hat{H}]=0 \) (6.83).

Comment: What you have proved is a special case of Kramer’s degeneracy: for an odd number of spin-1/2 particles (or any half-integer spin for that matter), every energy level (of a time-reversal-invariant Hamiltonian) is at least twofold degenerate. This is because—as you just showed—for half-integer spin a state and its time-reversed state are necessarily distinct.

Solution

(a) Acting on a generic spinor

\( \hat{\Theta}^{2}\left(\begin{array}{l} a \\ b \end{array}\right)=\hat{\Theta}\left(\begin{array}{c} -b^{*} \\ a^{*} \end{array}\right) \equiv \hat{\Theta}\left(\begin{array}{l} a^{\prime} \\ b^{\prime} \end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{c} -\left(b^{\prime}\right)^{*} \\ \left(a^{\prime}\right)^{*} \end{array}\right) \).

\( =\left(\begin{array}{c} -\left(a^{*}\right)^{*} \\ \left(-b^{*}\right)^{*} \end{array}\right)=-\left(\begin{array}{l} a \\ b \end{array}\right) \).

where I defined \( a^{\prime} \text { and } b^{\prime} \) to make it easier to follow the manipulations.

(b) Lets assume that they are the same state. Then

\( \hat{\Theta}^{2}\left|\psi_{n}\right\rangle=\hat{\Theta}\left|\psi_{n}^{\prime}\right\rangle=\hat{\Theta} c\left|\psi_{n}\right\rangle=c^{*} \hat{\Theta}\left|\psi_{n}\right\rangle=c^{*}\left|\psi_{n}^{\prime}\right\rangle \).

\( =c^{*} c\left|\psi_{n}\right\rangle=|c|^{2}\left|\psi_{n}\right\rangle \).

but we know from part (a) that \( \Theta^{2}=-1 \) and this is a contradiction since, of course, \( |c|^{2}>0 \).
