Guest Question.

A particle moves in straight line with a velocity-time diagram shown in Fig. 11.33(a). Knowing that s = -25 m and t = 0, draw s-t and a-t diagram for 0 < t < 24.

Admin
Solution

(i) Acceleration-Time diagram

Acceleration = Slope of ν-t diagram.

(a) 0 < t < 10 seconds ;

Slope \( = a = \frac{30}{10} = 3 m/s^2 \) Ans.

(b) 10 sec < t < 16 seconds ;

Slope \( = a = – \frac{30}{3} = -10 m/s^2\) Ans.

(By geometry, slope is intersecting as a mid-point between t = 10 seconds to t = 16 seconds, i.e., t = 13 seconds).

(c) From t = 16 seconds onwards ;

Slope = a = 0 Ans.

(ii) Method for finding displacement

Change in displacement = Area under ν-t diagram.

(a) At t = 10 seconds

\( s_{10} – s_0 = \frac{1}{2} × 10 × 30 \) (\( \because s _0 = -25 \) m ).

\( s_{10} = -25 + \frac{1}{2}× 10 × 30 \) .

\( s_{10} = 125 \) m.

(b) At t = 13 seconds (t-axis intercept)

\( s_{13} – s_{10} = \frac{1}{2}× 3 × 30 \) .

\( s_{13} = 125 + 45 \) .

\( s_{13} = 170 \) m Ans.

(c) At t = 16 seconds.

\( s_{16} – s_{13} = \frac{1}{2}× 3 × (-30) \) .

\( s_{16} = 170 – 45 \).

\( s_{16} = 125 \) m.

(d) Due to negative velocity, the particle has reversed its direction and will reach again to its origin. Let t be the time taken by particle to reach the origin from t = 16 seconds.

\( s_0 – s_{16} = (-30) × t \) \( (s_0 = 0) \) .

0 – 125 = -30 t.

t = 4.17 seconds Ans.

∴ At t = 16 + 4.17 = 20.17 second , the particle will again pass through origin.

(e) At t = 24 seconds

\( s_{24} – s_{16} = 8 × (-30) \).

\( s_{24} = 125 – 240 \).

\( s_{24} = -115 \) m.

(f) Initially particle is having negative displacement, s = -25 m.

Let t be the time taken to cross origin, we know.

\( 25 = \frac{1}{3} × t × 30 \).

∴ t = 1.67 seconds Ans.
