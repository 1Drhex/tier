Guest Question.

AN EFFECTIVE MAGNETIC FIELD

To six significant figures, the wavelengths of the two spectral lines that make up the sodium doublet are \(λ_1\) = 588.995 nm and \(λ_2\) = 589.592 nm. Calculate the effective magnetic field experienced by the electron in the \(3 p\) levels of the sodium atom.

Admin
Solution

IDENTIFY and SET UP:

The two lines in the sodium doublet result from transitions from the two \(3 p\) levels, which are split by spin-orbit coupling, to the \(3 s\) level, which is not split because it has \(L\) = 0. We picture the spin-orbit coupling as an interaction between the electron spin magnetic moment and an effective magnetic field due to the nucleus. This example is like in reverse: There we were given \(B\) and found the difference between the energies of the two spin states, while here we use the energy difference to find the target variable \(B\). The difference in energy between the two 3p levels is equal to the difference in energy between the two photons of the sodium doublet. We use this relationship and the results of to determine \(B\).

EXECUTE:

The energies of the two photons are \(E_1 = hc/λ_1\) and \(E_2 = hc/λ_2\). Here \(E_1 > E_2\) because \(λ_1 < λ_2\), so the difference in their energies is

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta E=& \frac{h c}{\lambda_{1}}-\frac{h c}{\lambda_{2}}=hc\left(\frac{\lambda_{2}-\lambda_{1}}{\lambda_{2} \lambda_{1}}\right) \\=&\left(4.136\times 10^{-15} \mathrm{eV} \cdot \mathrm{s}\right)\left(2.998 \times 10^{8}\mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\right) \\& \times \frac{\left(589.592 \times 10^{-9} \mathrm{~m}\right)-\left(588.995 \times 10^{-9} \mathrm{~m}\right)}{\left(589.592 \times 10^{-9}\mathrm{~m}\right)\left(588.995 \times 10^{-9} \mathrm{~m}\right)} \\=& 0.00213\mathrm{eV}=3.41 \times 10^{-22} \mathrm{~J}\end{aligned}\)

This equals the energy difference between the two 3p levels. The spin-orbit interaction raises one level by 1.70 × \(10^{-22}\) J (one-half of 3.41 × \(10^{-22}\) J) and lowers the other by 1.70 × \(10^{-22}\) J.
From , the amount each state is raised or lowered is \(|U|=(1.00116) \mu_{\mathrm{B}} B\), so

\(B=\left|\frac{U}{(1.00116) \mu_{\mathrm{B}}}\right|=\frac{1.70 \times 10^{-22} \mathrm{~J}}{9.28 \times 10^{-24} \mathrm{~J} / \mathrm{T}}=18.0 \mathrm{~T}\)

 

EVALUATE: The electron experiences a very strong effective magnetic field. To produce a steady, macroscopic field of this magnitude in the laboratory requires state-of-the-art electromagnets.
