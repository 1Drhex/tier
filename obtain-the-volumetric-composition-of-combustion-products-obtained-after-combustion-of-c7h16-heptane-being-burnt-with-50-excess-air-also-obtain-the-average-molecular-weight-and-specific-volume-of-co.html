Guest Question.

Obtain the volumetric composition of combustion products obtained after combustion of \(C_{7}H_{16}\) (heptane) being burnt with 50% excess air. Also obtain the average molecular weight and specific volume of combustion products at S.T.P. Consider the volume per kg mol. at S.T.P. to be 22.4 \(\textbf{m}^{3}\) and air to have 21% \(\textbf{O}_{2}\) and 79% \(\textbf{N}_{2}\) by volume.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{Here excess air supplied shall result in unutilized }O_{2}\text{ with combustion products.} \\ \begin{aligned}^{*}\text{Excess O}_{2}\text{ supplied} &= 0.5 × 73.33 = 36.665 m^{3}\\\text{Total O}_{2}\text{ supplied} &= 73.33 + 36.665 = 109.995 m^{3}\\^{**}\text{Total N}_{2}\text{ supplied} &= (109.995) ×\frac{79}{21} = 413.79 m^{3}\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Total volume of combustion products}&= \left\{46.67 + 53.33 + 36.665 + 413.79\right\} \\&= 550.455 m^{3}\end{aligned}\\\text{Average molecular weight of combustion products can be estimated by summation of mass of constituents per mol. of}\\\text{ combustion products.}\)
Constituents of combustion products Fraction of constituents per unit products \(b = \frac{a}{Σ(a)}\) Molecular weight (c) Mass of constituents per mol. of combustion (d = b × c) Average molecular weight of combustion products c = Σ(d)
CO2(= 46.67) \(\frac{46.67}{550.455}= 0.0848 \) 44 3.7312
H2O(= 53.33) \(\frac{53.33}{550.455}= 0.0969 \) 18 1.7442 28.65
O2(= 36.665) \(\frac{36.665}{550.455}= 0.0666\) 32 2.1312
N2(= 413.79) \(\frac{413.79}{550.455}= 0.7517\) 28 21.0476
