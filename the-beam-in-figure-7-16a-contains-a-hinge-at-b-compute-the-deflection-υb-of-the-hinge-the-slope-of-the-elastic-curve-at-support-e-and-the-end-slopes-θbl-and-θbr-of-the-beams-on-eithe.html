The beam in Figure 7.16a contains a hinge at B. Compute the deflection\( v_{B}\)of the hinge, the slope of the elastic curve at support E, and the end slopes \( \Theta _{BL}\) and \( \Theta _{BR}\) of the beams on either side of the hinge (Figure 7.16d ). Also locate the point of maximum deflection in span BE. EI is constant. The elastomeric pad at E is equivalent to a roller.

Admin
Solution

The deflection of the hinge at B, denoted by \( v_{B}\) , equals \( t_{BA}\) , the tangential deviation of B from the tangent to the fixed support at A. Deflection \( t_{BA} \) equals the moment of the area under the \( \frac{M}{EI} \) curve between A and B about B (Figure 7.16b).

 

\( v_{B}=t_{BA}=area . \bar{x}=\frac{1}{2}\left ( -\frac{108}{EL} \right )\left ( 9 \right )\left ( 6 \right )=-\frac{2916}{EL}\)

 

Compute \( \Theta _{BL}\) , the slope of the B end of cantilever AB

 

\( \Theta _{BL}=\Theta _{A}+\Delta \Theta _{AB}\)

 

\( =0+\frac{1}{2}\left ( 9 \right )\left ( -\frac{108}{EI} \right )=\frac{-486}{EI} \) rad

 

where \( \Delta \Theta _{AB}\) is equal to the triangular area under the \( \frac{M}{EI}\) curve between A and B and \( \Theta _{A}=0\) because the fixed support at A prevents rotation.

Compute \( \Theta _{E}\) , the slope of the elastic curve at E (Figure 7.16b)

 

\( \Theta _{E}=\frac{v_{B}+t_{BE}}{18}=\left ( \frac{2916}{EI}+\frac{7776}{EI} \right )\left ( \frac{1}{18} \right )=\frac{594}{EI}\) rad

 

where\( t_{BE} \) equals the moment of the area under the \( \frac{M}{EI} \) curve between B and E about B. This computation is simplified by dividing the trapezoidal area into two triangles and a rectangle (see the dashed lines in Figure 7.16c)

 

\( t_{BE}=\frac{1}{2}\left ( 6 \right )\left ( \frac{72}{EI} \right )\left ( 4 \right )+\left ( 6 \right )\left ( \frac{72}{EI} \right )\left ( 9 \right )+\frac{1}{2}\left ( 6 \right )\left ( \frac{72}{EI} \right )\left ( 14 \right )=\frac{7776}{EI}\) rad

 

Locate the point of maximum deflection in span BE. The point of maximum deflection, labeled point F, is located at the point in span BE where the tangent to the elastic curve is zero. Between F and support E, a distance x, the slope goes from 0 to\( \Theta _{E}\) . Since the change in slope is given by the area under the \( \frac{M}{EI}\) curve between these two points, we can write

 

\( \Theta _{E}=\Theta _{F}+\Delta \Theta _{EF} \) (1)

 

where \( \Theta _{F}=0 \) and \( \Theta _{E}=\frac{594}{EI} \) rad. Between points D and E the change in slope produced by the area under the\( \frac{M}{EI} \) curve equals \( \frac{216}{EI}\) . Since this value is less than \( \Theta _{E}\) , the slope at D has a positive value of

 

\( \Theta _{D}=\Theta _{E}-\Delta \Theta _{ED}=\frac{594}{EI}-\frac{216}{EI} = \frac{378}{EI} \) rad (2)

 

Between D and C the area under the \( \frac{M}{EI}\) curve equals \( \frac{432}{EI}\) . Since this value of change in slope exceeds \( \frac{378}{EI}\) , the point of zero slope must lie between C and D. We can now use Equation 1 to solve for distance x.
