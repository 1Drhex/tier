Guest Question.

Present Worth with Assumed Residual Values
For the brass and stainless steel pumps in through 9.5, use a 10-year study period. Compare the EACs with those calculated for a 12-year study period and those for 3-, 4-, and 5-year periods.

Admin
Solution

The residual value for the brass pump was assumed to be $6K after 1 year of use, $2K after 2 years, and $0 after 3 years. Similarly, the residual values for the stainless steel pump are assumed to be $9K, $4.5K, $1.5K, and $0 after 1, 2, 3, and 4 years, respectively.
The PW values calculated in are converted to EACs (shown in the following table) by multiplying them by the (A/P) factors for 3, 4, and 5 years. The EACs for 10 years are calculated directly as follows:

\(\mathrm{EAC}_{\text {brass }}=\left[12 \mathrm{~K}+12 \mathrm{~K} /(1.08)^{3}+12 \mathrm{~K} /(1.08)^{6}+12 \mathrm{~K} /(1.08)^{9}-6 \mathrm{~K} /(1.08)^{10}\right](A / P, 8 \%, 10)\)

= (35.09 – 2.78)(.1490) = $4815

\(\mathrm{EAC}_{\text {stainless }}=\left[15 \mathrm{~K}+15 \mathrm{~K} /(1.08)^{4}+15 \mathrm{~K} /(1.08)^{8}-4.5 \mathrm{~K} /(1.08)^{10}\right](A / P, 8 \%, 10)\)

= (34.13 – 2.08)(.1490) = $4776

Thus, with these assumed residual values, the brass pump is best with 3 years (where the stainless steel pump has a negligible residual value). The stainless steel pump is best with 4, 5, 10, 12, or a long but indefinite horizon.
