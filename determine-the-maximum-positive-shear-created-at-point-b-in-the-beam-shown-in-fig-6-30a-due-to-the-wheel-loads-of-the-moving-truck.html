Guest Question.

Determine the maximum positive shear created at point \(B\) in the beam shown in Fig. \(6-30 (\mathrm{a})\) due to the wheel loads of the moving truck.

Admin
Solution

The influence line for the shear at \(B\) is shown in Fig. 6–30b.

3-ft Movement of 4-k Load. Imagine that the 4-k load acts just to the right of point \(B\) so that we obtain its maximum positive influence. Since the beam segment \(B C\) is \(10 \mathrm{ft}\) long, the 10 -k load is not as yet on the beam. When the truck moves \(3 \mathrm{ft}\) to the left, the 4-k load jumps downward on the influence line 1 unit and the \(4-\mathrm{k}, 9-\mathrm{k}\), and \(15-\mathrm{k}\) loads create a positive increase in \(\Delta V_{B}\), since the slope is upward to the left. Although the \(10-\mathrm{k}\) load also moves forward \(3 \mathrm{ft}\), it is still not on the beam. Thus,

\( \Delta V_{B}=4(-1)+(4+9+15)\left(\frac{0.5}{10}\right) 3=+0.2 \mathrm{k} \)

6-ft Movement of 9-k Load. When the 9-k load acts just to the right of \(B\), and then the truck moves \(6 \mathrm{ft}\) to the left, we have

\( \Delta V_{B}=9(-1)+(4+9+15)\left(\frac{0.5}{10}\right)(6)+10\left(\frac{0.5}{10}\right)(4)=+1.4 \mathrm{k} \)

Note in the calculation that the \(10-\mathrm{k}\) load only moves \(4 \mathrm{ft}\) on the beam.

6-ft Movement of 15-k Load. If the \(15-k\) load is positioned just to the right of \(B\) and then the truck moves \(6 \mathrm{ft}\) to the left, the \(4-\mathrm{k}\) load moves only \(1 \mathrm{ft}\) until it is off the beam, and likewise the 9-k load moves only \(4 \mathrm{ft}\) until it is off the beam. Hence,

\( \begin{aligned} \Delta V_{B} &=15(-1)+4\left(\frac{0.5}{10}\right)(1)+9\left(\frac{0.5}{10}\right)(4)+(15+10)\left(\frac{0.5}{10}\right)(6) \\ &=-5.5 \mathrm{k} \end{aligned} \)

Since \(\Delta V_{B}\) is now negative, the correct position of the loads occurs when the \(15-\mathrm{k}\) load is just to the right of point \(B\), Fig. 6-30c. Consequently,

\( \begin{aligned} \left(V_{B}\right)_{\max } &=4(-0.05)+9(-0.2)+15(0.5)+10(0.2) \\ &=7.5 \mathrm{k} \end{aligned} \)

In practice one also has to consider motion of the truck from left to right and then choose the maximum value between these two situations.
