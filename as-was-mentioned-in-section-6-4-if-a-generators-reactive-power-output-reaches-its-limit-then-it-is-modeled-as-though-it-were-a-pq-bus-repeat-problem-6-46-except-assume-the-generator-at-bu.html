Guest Question.

As was mentioned in Section 6.4, if a generator’s reactive power output reaches its limit, then it is modeled as though it were a PQ bus. Repeat , except assume the generator at bus 2 is operating with its reactive power limited to a maximum of 50 Mvar. Then verify your solution by solving the case with PowerWorld Simulator. To increase the reactive power output of the bus 2 generator, select Tools, Play to begin the power flow simulation, then click on the up arrow on the bus 2 magenta voltage setpoint field until the reactive power output reaches its maximum.

Admin
Solution

First, convert values to per-unit

\(Q_{2}=50 \text { Mvar }=0.5 \text { p.u. }\)

\(Y_{\text {bus }}\) stays the same as in .

Since bus 2 is now a PQ bus, we introduce a fourth equation in order to solve for \(V_{2}\).

Iteration 0
Step 1

Start with \(x(0)=\left[\begin{array}{l}\delta_{2}(0) \\\delta_{3}(0) \\V_{2}(0) \\V_{3}(0)\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}0 \\0 \\1 \\1\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&P(x(0))=\left[\begin{array}{l}P_{2}(x(0)) \\P_{3}(x(0))\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}0 \\0\end{array}\right] \\&Q(x(0))=\left[\begin{array}{l}Q_{2}(x(0)) \\Q_{3}(x(0))\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}0 \\0\end{array}\right]\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\Delta y(0)=\left[\begin{array}{c}P_{2}-P_{2}(x(0)) \\P_{3}-P_{3}(x(0)) \\Q_{2}-Q_{2}(x(0) \\Q_{3}-Q_{3}(x(0))\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{r}0.8 \\-1.8 \\0.5 \\0\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 2

\(J(0)=\left[\begin{array}{ll|ll}16 & -8 & 8 & -4 \\-8 & 16 & -4 & 8 \\\hline-8 & 4 & 16 & -8 \\4 & -8 & -8 & 16\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 3

\(\begin{aligned}J(0) \Delta x(0) &=\Delta y(0) \\\Delta x(0) &=\left[\begin{array}{c}-0.023333 \\-0.10167 \\1.03 \\0.97\end{array}\right]\end{aligned}\)

 

Step 4

\(x(1)=x(0)+\Delta x(0)=\left[\begin{array}{c}-0.023333 \\-0.10167 \\1.03 \\0.97\end{array}\right]\)

 

Iteration 1
Step 1

\(\begin{aligned}&P(x(1))=\left[\begin{array}{r}0.8174 \\-1.7299\end{array}\right] \\&Q(x(1))=\left[\begin{array}{l}0.5517 \\0.0727\end{array}\right]\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\Delta y(1)=\left[\begin{array}{l}-0.01739 \\-0.070052 \\-0.051746 \\-0.072698\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 2

\(J(1)=\left[\begin{array}{cccc}16.4227 & -8.28102 & 9.03358 & -3.46256 \\-7.65556 & 14.9817 & -4.47535 & 5.97655 \\-7.66981 & 3.35868 & 17.0157 & -8.53714 \\4.60961 & -9.25715 & -7.43258 & 15.5949\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 3

\(\Delta x(1)=\left[\begin{array}{c}-0.00057587 \\-0.0032484 \\-0.0077284 \\-0.010103\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 4

\(x(2)=\left[\begin{array}{c}-0.023904 \\-0.10492 \\1.0223 \\0.9599\end{array}\right]\)

 

Iteration 2
Step 1

\(\begin{aligned}&P(x(2))=\left[\begin{array}{r}0.7999 \\-1.7989\end{array}\right] \\&Q(x(2))=\left[\begin{array}{l}0.5005 \\0.0007\end{array}\right]\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\Delta y(2)=\left[\begin{array}{c}0.00013185 \\-0.0011097 \\-0.00048034 \\-0.00072284\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 2

\(J(2)=\left[\begin{array}{cccc}16.2201 & -8.14207 & 8.96061 & -3.41392 \\-7.50685 & 14.7417 & -4.44838 & 5.80513 \\-7.56045 & 3.27701 & 16.8459 & -8.48223 \\4.54745 & -9.17011 & -7.34331 & 15.3591\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 3

\(\Delta x(2)=\left[\begin{array}{cc}-6.3502 E -7 \\-5.3289 E -5 \\-7.6468 E -5 \\-0.00011525\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 4

\(x(3)=\left[\begin{array}{c}-0.02391 \\-0.10497 \\1.0222 \\0.95978\end{array}\right]\)

 

Iteration 3
Step 1

\(\begin{aligned}&P(x(3))=\left[\begin{array}{c}0.8000 \\-1.8000\end{array}\right] \\&Q(x(3))=\left[\begin{array}{l}0.5000 \\0.0000\end{array}\right]\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{gathered}\Delta y(3)=\left[\begin{array}{cc}6.1267 E -8 \\-2.0161 E -7 \\-6.5136 E -8 \\-8.9553 E -8\end{array}\right] \\\Delta y(3)<1 E -4\end{gathered}\)

∴ Newton-Raphson has converged in 3 iterations to

\(\begin{aligned}&V_{1}=1.0 \angle 0 \\&V_{2}=1.0222 \angle-0.02391 rad =1.0222 \angle-1.370^{\circ} \\&V_{3}=0.95978 \angle-0.10497 rad =0.95978 \angle-6.014^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

Using the voltages, we can calculate the powers at bus 1 with Eqs. (6.6.2) and (6.6.3).

\(\begin{aligned}&P_{1}=1.0944 p . u .=109.44 MW \\&Q_{1}=-0.3112 p . u .=-31.12 Mvar\end{aligned}\)

Using PowerWorld Simulator, we get

\(\begin{aligned}&P_{1}=109.4 MW \\&Q_{1}=-31.1 Mvar \\&V_{2}=1.025 p . u \\&V_{3}=0.960 p . u\end{aligned}\)

Which agrees with our solution.
