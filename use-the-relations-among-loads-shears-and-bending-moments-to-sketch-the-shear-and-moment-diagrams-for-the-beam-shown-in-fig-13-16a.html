Guest Question.

Use the relations among loads, shears, and bending moments to sketch the shear and moment diagrams for the beam shown in Fig. 13.16(a).

Admin
Solution

The free-body diagram is shown in Fig. 13.16(b). From the usual equations of equilibrium,

\(ΣM_{B} = 0\) \(30(1) – 40(1.5) – 75(2.25) + D_{y}3 = 0\)

\(D_{y}\) = 66.25 kN

\(ΣM_{D} = 0\) \(30(4) – B_{y}3 + 40(1.5) + 75(0.75) = 0\)

\(B_{y}\) = 78.75 kN

Check:

\(ΣF_{y} = 0\) \(-30 + B_{y} – 40 – 75 + D_{y} = 0\)

-30 + 78.75 – 40 – 75 + 66.25 = 0 OK

From Eq. (13.5) \(V_{2}-V_{1}=external\ load\ between\ 1\ and\ 2\) or \(\Delta V_{1 \xrightarrow[]{} 2 }=load_{1 \xrightarrow[]{} 2 }\) and the free-body diagram, we calculate the shears. The value of the shear to the left of point A is zero. That is,

V (left of point A) = 0

ΔV (left of A to right of A) = -30 kN

V (right of A) = -30 kN

ΔV (from A to B) = 0

V (left of B) = -30 kN

ΔV (left of B to right of B) = +78.75 kN

V (right of B) = 78.75 – 30 = 48.75 kN

ΔV (from B to C) = 0

V (left of C) = 48.75 kN

ΔV (left of C to right of C) = -40 kN

V (right of C) = -40 + 48.75 = 8.75 kN

ΔV (right of C to left of D) = -75 kN

V (left of D) = 8.75 – 75 = -66.25 kN

ΔV (left of D to right of D) = +66.25

V (right of D) = -66.25 + 66.25 = 0 OK

To find the location of E where the shear is zero, we write

V (from right of C to E) = \(-50\acute{x} = -8.75\) or \(\acute{x} = 0.175\ m\)

The section where the shear is zero is at x = 2.5 + 0.175 = 2.675 m. The shear diagram is drawn in Fig. 13.16(c). From Eq. (13.6) and the shear diagram, we calculate the following values for bending moments:

\(M_{A} = 0\)

\(\Delta M_{A \xrightarrow[]{} B }\) = -30(1) = -30 kN.m

\(M_{B}\) = -30 kN.m

\(\Delta M_{B \xrightarrow[]{} C }\) = 48.75(1.5) = 73.125 kN.m

\(M_{C}\) = -30 + 73.125 = 43.125 kN.m

\(\Delta M_{C \xrightarrow[]{} E } =\frac{(0.175)(8.75)}{2}=+0.766\ KN.m\)

\(M_{E}\) = 43.125 + 0.766 = 43.891 kN.m

\(\Delta M_{E \xrightarrow[]{} D } =\frac{(1.5-0.175)(-66.25)}{2}=-43.891\ KN.m\)

\(M_{D}\) = 43.891 – 43.891 = 0 OK

The moment diagram is drawn in Fig. 13.16(d).
