Guest Question.

Beginning with the virial expansion Eq. (20.70) and the fact that one obtains an ideal gas if all \(B_{r} (T) = 0\) for r ≥ 2, determine series expansions for the following: the Helmholtz free energy per particle, f ; the entropy per particle,s; the internal energy per particle, u; the heat capacity (at constant volume) per particle, c; and the chemical potential, μ.

\(\frac{p}{nk_{B}T} = 1 + \sum\limits_{v=2}^{\infty }{B_{v}(T)n^{v-1}} . \) (20.70)

Admin
Solution

We begin with

\(df = -sdT – pdv = -sdT + \left(p/n^{2}\right) dn \) (20.89)

and integrate \(p/n^{2}\) over n at constant T. This yields

\(f = k_{B}T \ln n + w(T) + k_{B}T \sum\limits_{r=2}^{\infty }{B_{r}(T)\frac{n^{r – 1}}{r – 1} } , \) (20.90)

where w(T) is a function of integration that depends only on T.

We determine w(T) by recognizing that \(k_{B}T ln n + w(T)\) must be the value \(f^{ideal} = (\mu − pv)^{ideal}\) for an ideal gas. Thus

\(f = k_{B}T\left[\ln \left(n / n_{Q}\right) – 1\right] + k_{B}T \sum\limits_{r=2}^{\infty }{B_{r}(T)\frac{n^{r – 1}}{r – 1} } , \) (20.91)

where \(n_{Q} = \left(mk_{B}T / 2\pi \hbar^{2} \right)^{3/2} \) is the quantum concentration. Thus,

\(s = -\left(\partial f / \partial T \right)_{n} = k_{B}\left[\ln \left(n_{Q} / n\right) + 5/2 \right] – k_{B} \sum\limits_{r=2}^{\infty }{\frac{d\left[TB_{r}(T)\right] }{dT} \frac{n^{r – 1}}{r – 1} } ,\) (20.92)

 

\(u = f + T_{s} = (3/2)k_{B}T – k_{B}T^{2} \sum\limits_{r=2}^{\infty }{\frac{dB_{r}(T) }{dT} \frac{n^{r – 1}}{r – 1} } , \) (20.93)

 

\(c = \left(\partial u / \partial T \right)_{n} = (3/2)k_{B} – k_{B}T \sum\limits_{r=2}^{\infty }{\frac{d^{2}\left[TB_{r}(T)\right] }{dT^{2}} \frac{n^{r – 1}}{r – 1}} , \) (20.94)

 

\(\mu = f + p/n = k_{B}T \ln \left(n / n_{Q}\right) + k_{B}T \sum\limits_{r=2}^{\infty }{B_{r}(T) \frac{rn^{r – 1}}{r – 1}} . \) (20.95)
