Guest Question.

Calculate the pressure drop across a fully open globe valve placed in a 4-in Schedule 40 steel pipe carrying 400 gal/min of oil (sg = 0.87).

Admin
Solution

A sketch of the installation is shown in Fig. 10.25. To determine the pressure drop, the energy equation should be written for the flow between points 1 and 2:

\( \frac{p_1}{\gamma} + z_1 +\frac{v^2_1}{2g} -h_L= \frac{p_2}{\gamma} + z_2 +\frac{v^2_2}{2g} \)

The energy loss \( h_L \) is the minor loss due to the valve only. The pressure drop is the difference between \( p_1 \) and \( p_2 \). Solving the energy equation for this difference gives

\( p_1 -p_2 = \gamma \left[\left(z_2 – z_1\right)+\frac{v^2_2 – v^2_1}{2g} +h_L \right] \)

But \( z_1 = z_2 \) and \( v_1 = v_2 \). Then we have

\( p_1 – p_2 = \gamma h_L \)

Equation (10â€“1) is used to determine \( h_L\) :

\( h_L = K\times \frac{v^2}{2g} = f_T \times \frac{L_e}{D} \times \frac{v^2}{2g}\)

The velocity \( v\) is the average velocity of flow in the 4-in pipe. For the pipe, D = 0.3355 ft and \(A = 0.0884 ft^2\). Then, we have

\( v=\frac{Q}{A} = \frac{400gal/min}{0.0884ft^2} \times \frac{1ft^3/s}{449gal/min} = 10.08 ft/s \)

From Table 10.5 we find \( f_T = 0.016 \) for a 4-in pipe. For the globe valve, \( L_e/D =340 \). Then,

\( K = f_T \frac{L_e}{D} =\left(0.016\right)\left(340\right) =5.44 \)

\( h_L = K \times \frac{v^2}{2g} = \left(5.44\right) \frac{\left(10.8\right)^2 }{\left(2\right)\left(32.2\right) } ft = 8.58ft\)

For the oil, \( \gamma = \left(0.870\right) \left(62.4lb/ft^3\right) \). Then, we have

\( p_1 – p_2 = \gamma h_L = \frac{\left(0.870\right)\left(62.4\right)lb }{ft^3} \times 8.58ft \times \frac{1ft^2}{144in^2} \)

\( p_1 – p_2 = 3.24 psi \)

Therefore, the pressure in the oil drops by 3.24 psi as it flows through the valve. Also, an energy loss of 8.58 lb-ft is dissipated as heat from each pound of oil that flows through the valve.
