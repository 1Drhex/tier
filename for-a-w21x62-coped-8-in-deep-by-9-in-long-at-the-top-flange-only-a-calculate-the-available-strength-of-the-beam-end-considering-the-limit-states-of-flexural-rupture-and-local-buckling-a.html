Guest Question.

For a W21×62 coped 8-in. deep by 9-in. long at the top flange only:
A. calculate the available strength of the beam end, considering the limit states of flexural rupture and local buckling. Assume e = 9½ in.
B. choose an alternate W21 shape to eliminate the need for stiffening for an end reaction of\(R_{D}=16.5 \text { kips and } R_{L}=47 \text { kips. }\).
C. determine the size of doubler plate needed to stiffen the W21×62 for the given end reaction.
D. determine the size of longitudinal stiffeners needed to stiffen the W21×62 for the given end reaction.
Material Properties:
Beam W21×62 ASTM A992 \(F_{y}=50 ksi \quad F_{u}=65 ksi\)
Plate ASTM A36 \(F_{y}=36 ksi \quad F_{u}=58 ksi\)

Part 9

\(c \leq 2 d\)

\(\frac{c}{d}=\frac{9 in .}{21.0 in .}=0.429 ; \frac{c}{h_{0}}=\frac{9 in .}{13.0 in .}=0.693\)

 

\(\text { Since } \frac{c}{d} \leq 1.0\)

 

\(f=2\left(\frac{c}{d}\right)=2(0.429)=0.858\)

 

\(\text { Since } \frac{c}{h_{0}} \leq 1.0\)

 

\(k=2.2\left(\frac{h_{0}}{c}\right)^{1.65}=2.2\left(\frac{13.0 in .}{9.0 in .}\right)^{1.65}=4.03\)

 

For a top cope only, the critical buckling stress is

 

\(F_{c r}=26,210\left(\frac{t_{w}}{h_{0}}\right)^{2} f k \leq F_{y}\)

C

 

LRFD ASD
Design doubler plate

 

Doubler plate must provide a required strength of

 

95 kips – 84.3 kips = 10.7 kips.

 

\(\begin{aligned}S_{r e q} &=\frac{\left(R_{u}-\phi R_{n \text { beam }}\right) e}{\phi F_{y}} \\&=\frac{(95 \text { kips }-84.3 \text { kips })(91 / 2 \text { in. })}{0.90(50 ksi )} \\&=2.26 in .^{3}\end{aligned}\)

 

For an 8-in. deep plate,

 

\(t_{r e q}=\frac{6 S_{r e q}}{d^{2}}=\frac{6\left(2.26 in .^{3}\right)}{(8 in .)^{2}}=0.212 in .\)

 Design doubler plate

 

Doubler plate must provide a required strength of

 

63.5 kips – 56.1 kips = 7.40 kips.

 

\(\begin{aligned}S_{r e q} &=\frac{\left(R_{a}-R_{n \text { beam }} / \Omega\right) e \Omega}{F_{y}} \\&=\frac{(63.5 \text { kips }-56.1 kips )(91 / 2 in .)(1.67)}{50 ksi } \\&=2.35 in .^{3}\end{aligned}\)

 

For an 8-in. deep plate,

 

\(t_{r e q}=\frac{6 S_{r e q}}{d^{2}}=\frac{6\left(2.35 in ^{3}\right)}{(8 in .)^{2}}=0.220 in .\)

Manual
Part 9

Note: ASTM A572 grade 50 plate is recommended in order to match the beam yield strength

Thus, since the doubler plate must extend at least \(d_{c}\) beyond the cope, use a PL 5/16 in.×8 in. ×1’-5” with ¼ in welds.

Design longitudinal stiffeners

From section property calculations for the neutral axis and moment of inertia, the neutral axis is located 4.40 in. from the bottom flange (8.84 in. from the top of the stiffener) and the elastic section modulus of the reinforced section is as follows:

\(I_{0}\left(\text { in. }^{4}\right)\) \(A d^{2}\left( in .^{4}\right)\) \(I_{0}+A d^{2}\left(\text { in. }^{4}\right)\)
Stiffener 0.00521 76 76
W21×62 web 63.2 28.6 91.8
W21×62 bottom flange 0.16 84.9 85.1
\(\Sigma=I_{x}=253 in ^{4}\)
\(S_{n e t}=\frac{I_{x}}{c}=\frac{253 in ^{4}}{8.84 in .}=28.6 in .^{3}\)

and the nominal strength of the reinforced section is

\(R_{n}=\frac{F_{y} S_{n e t}}{e}=\frac{(50 ksi )\left(28.6 in ^{3}\right)}{91 / 2 in. }=151 kips\)
