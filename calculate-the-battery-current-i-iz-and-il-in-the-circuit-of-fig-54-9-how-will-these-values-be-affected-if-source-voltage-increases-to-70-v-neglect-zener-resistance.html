Guest Question.

Calculate the battery current \(I , I_{z}\) and \(I_{L}\) in the circuit of \(Fig. 54.9.\) How will these values be affected if source voltage increases to \(70 V\) ? Neglect Zener resistance.

Admin
Solution

When \( V_{in} = 40 V\)

Now, \( V_{AB} = V_{z} = 10 V\)

\(\therefore \) \(I = 30/3 K = 10 mA\)

 

\(\therefore \) drop across \(3K\) series (or line) resistor is \(= 40 − 10 = 30 V\)

 

\(I_{L} = V_{z} /R_{L} = V_{AB}/R_{L}= 10/2 K = 5 mA\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(I_{z} = I − I_{L} = 10 − 5 = 5 mA\)

Now, \(P_{max}=V_{z}.I_{z\left(max\right) } \) or \(0.5 = 10 \times I_{z\left(max\right) }\)

or \(I_{z\left(max\right) } = 0.5/10 = 0.05 A = 50 mA\)

Obviously, diode current of \(5 mA\) is very much within the current range of the diode.

(b) When, \(V_{in}= 70 V\)

Drop across \(R = 70 − 10 = 60 V\) \(\therefore \) \(I = 60/3 K = 20 mA\)

 

\(I_{L}= 5 mA \)(as before); \(I_{z}= I −I_{L} = 20 − 5 = 15 mA\)
