Inductance and inductive reactance: single-phase line

A single-phase line operating at 60 Hz consists of two 4/0 12-strand copper conductors with 5 ft spacing between conductor centers. The line length is 20 miles. Determine the total inductance in H and the total inductive reactance in Ω.

Admin
Solution

The GMD between conductor centers is \(D_{xy} =5ft\). Also, from Table A.3, the GMR of a 4/0 12-strand copper conductor is \(D_{xx} =D_{yy} =0.01750ft\) . From (4.6.5) and (4.6.8),

\(L_{x}=2\times 10^{-7}\ln \frac{D_{xy} }{D_{xx} } \) Hym per conductor (4.6.5)

\(L_{y}=2\times 10^{-7}\ln \frac{D_{xy} }{D_{yy} } \) Hym per conductor (4.6.8)

\(L_{x}=L_{y}=2\times 10^{-7}\ln \left(\frac{5}{0.01750} \right) \frac{H}{m} \times 1609\frac{m}{mi} \times 20mi=0.03639 \) H per conductor

The total inductance is

\(L=L_{x}+L_{y}=2\times 0.03639=0.07279 \) H per circuit

and the total inductive reactance is

\(X_{L}=2\pi fL=\left(2\pi \right) \left(60\right) \left(0.07279\right) =27.44\) Ω per circuit
