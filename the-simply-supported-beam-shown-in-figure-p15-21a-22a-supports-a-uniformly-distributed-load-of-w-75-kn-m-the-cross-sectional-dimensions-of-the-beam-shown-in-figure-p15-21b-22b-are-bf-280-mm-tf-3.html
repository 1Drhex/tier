Guest Question.

The simply supported beam shown in Figure P15.21a/22a supports a uniformly distributed load of w = 75 kN/m. The cross-sectional dimensions of the beam shown in Figure P15.21b/22b are \(b_{f}\) = 280 mm, \(t_{f}\) = 20 mm, d = 460 mm, \(t_{w}\) = 12 mm, and \(y_{H}\) = 110 mm. Determine the principal stresses and the maximum shear stress acting at point H. Show these stresses on an appropriate sketch.

Admin
Solution

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

\(\begin{array}{lcccccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Width } b \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Height } h \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 280 & 20 & 186,666.7 & 220.0 & 271,040,000.0 & 271,226,666.7 \\\text { web } & 12 & 420 & 74,088,000.0 & 0.0 & 0.0 & 74,088,000.0 \\\text { bottom flange } & 280 & 20 & 186,666.7 & -220.0 & 271,040,000.0 & 271,226,666.7 \\\hline {\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( mm ^{4}\right)=} & 616,541,333.3\end{array}\)

 

Shear-force and bending-moment diagram:

Shear force at H:
V = 126.04 kN
Bending moment at H:
M = 189.06 kN-m

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Bending stress at point H: (y = 110 mm)

\(\sigma_{x}=-\frac{M y}{I_{z}}=-\frac{(189.06 kN – m )(110 mm )(1,000 N / kN )(1,000 mm / m )}{616,541,333.3 mm ^{4}}=-33.731 MPa\)

Transverse and horizontal shear stress at point H:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q=(280 mm )(20 mm )(220 mm )+(12 mm )(100 mm )(160 mm )=1,424,000.000 mm ^{3} \\&\tau=\frac{V Q}{I t}=\frac{(126.04 N )\left(1,424,000 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(1,424,000.000 mm ^{4}\right)(9 mm )}=24.259 MPa \text { (Sense to be determined by inspection) }\end{aligned}\)

Stress element at H:

Principal stress calculations:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq. (12.12):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}}{2}\right)^{2}+\tau_{x y}^{2}} \\&=\frac{(-33.731)+(0)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(-33.731)-(0)}{2}\right)^{2}+(-24.259)^{2}} \\&=-16.866 \pm 29.546\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{p 1}=12.68 MPa \text { and } \quad \sigma_{p 2}=-46.4 MPa \\&\tau_{\max }=29.5 MPa \text { (maximum in-plane shear stress) } \\&\sigma_{\text {avg }}=16.87 MPa ( C ) \quad \text { (normal stress on planes of maximum in-plane shear stress) }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan 2 \theta_{p} &=\frac{\tau_{x y}}{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) / 2}=\frac{-24.259}{[(-33.731)-(0)] / 2}=\frac{-24.259}{-16.866}=1.4384 \\&\left.\therefore \theta_{p}=27.60^{\circ} \quad \text { (counterclockwise from the } x \text { axis to the direction of } \sigma_{p 2}\right)\end{aligned}\)
