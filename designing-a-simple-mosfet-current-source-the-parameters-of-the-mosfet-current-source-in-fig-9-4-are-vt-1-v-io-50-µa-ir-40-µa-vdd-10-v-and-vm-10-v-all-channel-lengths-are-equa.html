Guest Question.

Designing a simple MOSFET current source The parameters of the MOSFET current source in Fig. 9.4 are \( V_{ t }=1 V , I_{ O }=50 \mu A , I_{ R }=40 \mu A , V_{ DD }=10 V \text {, and } V_{ M }=10 V \). All channel lengths are equal, \( L_{1}=L_{2}=L_{3}=L=10 \mu m , \text { and } K_{ x }=20 \mu A / V ^{2} \). Calculate the required values of (a) \( K_{ n 1}, W_{1} \), (b) \( K_{ n 2}, W_{2} \), (c) \( K_{ n 3}, W_{3} \), and (d) the output resistance \( R_{ o } \) of the current source. Assume \( V_{ GS 1}=1.5 V \text { and } V_{ DS 1}=5 V \).

Admin
Solution

(a) From Eq. (9.22),

\(I_{ D 1}=I_{ O }=K_{ n 1}\left(V_{ GS 1}-V_{ t 1}\right)^{2}\left(1+\lambda V_{ DS 1}\right)\) (9.22)

 

\(50 \times 10^{-6}=K_{ n 1}(1.5-1)^{2}\left(1+\frac{5}{10}\right)\)

 

which gives \( K_{ n 1}=133.3 \mu A / V ^{2} \). From Eq. (9.21),

\(K_{ m }=\frac{W K_{ x }}{L}=2\left(\frac{W K_{ x }}{2 L}\right)=2 K_{ n }\) (9.21)

 

\(133.3 \times 10^{-6}=\frac{W_{1} \times 20 \times 10^{-6}}{10 \times 10^{-6}}\)

 

which gives \( W_{1}=66.65 \mu m \).

(b) \( V_{ DS 2}=V_{ GS 2}=V_{ GS 1}=1.5 V \). From Eq. (9.28),

\(I_{ D 2}=I_{ R }=K_{ n 2}\left(V_{ GS 2}-V_{ t 2}\right)^{2}\left(1+\lambda V_{ GS 2}\right)\) (9.28)

 

\( 40 \times 10^{-6}=K_{ n 2}(1.5-1)^{2}\left(1+\frac{1.5}{10}\right) \)

 

which gives \( K_{ n 2}=139.1 \mu A / V ^{2} \). From Eq. (9.21),

 

\( 139.1 \times 10^{-6}=\frac{W_{2} \times 20 \times 10^{-6}}{10 \times 10^{-6}} \)

 

which gives \( W_{2}=69.55 \mu m \) .

(c) \( V_{ GS 3}=V_{ DS 3}=V_{ DD }-V_{ DS 2}=V_{ DD }-V_{ GS 1}=10-1.5=8.5 V \). From Eq. (9.29),

\(\begin{aligned}I_{ D 3} &=I_{ R }=K_{ n 3}\left(V_{ GS 3}-V_{ t 3}\right)^{2}\left(1+\lambda V_{ DS 3}\right) \\&=K_{ n 3}\left(V_{ DD }-V_{ GS 2}-V_{ t 3}\right)^{2}\left[1+\lambda\left(V_{ DD }-V_{ GS 2}\right)\right]\end{aligned}\) (9.29)

 

\(40 \times 10^{-6}=K_{ n 3}(8.5-1)^{2}\left(1+\frac{8.5}{10}\right)\)

 

which gives \(K_{ n 3}=0.384 \mu A / V ^{2}\). From Eq. (9.21),

 

\(0.384 \times 10^{-6}=\frac{W_{3} \times 20 \times 10^{-6}}{10 \times 10^{-6}}\)

 

which gives \( W_{3}=0.192 \mu m \). In practice, because of manufacturing limitations, the minimum value of L or W is 10 µm. Since the value of \( W_{3} \) is smaller than 10 µm, \( V_{M} \) must be increased to make \( W_{3} \) at least 10 µm. (d) From Eq. (9.32), the output resistance \( R_{ o } \) is

\(R_{ o }=r_{ ds 1}=\frac{V_{ M }}{I_{ D 1}}=\frac{1}{\lambda I_{ D 1}}=r_{ ol }\) (9.32)

 

\( R_{ o }=r_{ ds 1}=\frac{V_{ M }}{K_{ n 1}\left(V_{ GS 1}-V_{ t }\right)^{2}}=\frac{10}{133.3 \times 10^{-6} \times(1.5-1)^{2}}=300 k \Omega \)
