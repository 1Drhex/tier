Guest Question.

Consider a classical harmonic oscillator with spring constant k in one dimension x for a particle of mass m having linear momentum p. Its energy E is given by

\(E = \frac{p^{2} }{2m} + \frac{1}{2} kx^{2} = \frac{p^{2} }{2m} + \frac{m\omega ^{2} }{2} x^{2} ,\) (17.22)

where \(\omega = \sqrt{k/m}\) is its angular frequency.

The well-known solution to this equation has the form x = A sin(ωt + φ), where t is the time and A and φ are constants.

Show that the trajectory of the particle orbit in phase space is an ellipse and determine the sizes of its semiaxes.

Then compute the area of the shell in phase space that lies between energies E and E − ΔE and compare with the corresponding energy ΔE for a quantum harmonic oscillator having quantized energies \(E = \left(n + 1/2\right) \hbar \omega .\)

From your result, determine the number of quantum states per area of phase space.

Admin
Solution

Equation (17.22) can be written in the form

\(\frac{p^{2}}{a^{2}} + \frac{x^{2}}{b^{2}} = 1,\) (17.23)

which is the equation of an ellipse with semiaxes \(a = \sqrt{2mE} and b = \sqrt{2E/mw^{2} }.\)

The momentum \(p = mdx/dt = mA\omega \cos (\omega t + \varphi ), with A =\sqrt{2E/m\omega ^{2}} ,\) so an elliptical trajectory is traversed periodically as time increases.

The area of the ellipse is πab = 2πE/ω. The phase space area in a shell between E and E − ΔE is therefore

 

2πE/ω − 2π(E − ΔE)/ω = 2πΔE/ω. (17.24)

For a quantum oscillator, the corresponding energy increment is

\(\Delta E = \Delta \left(n + 1/2\right) \hbar \omega = \hbar \omega \Delta n.\) (17.25)

The number of quantum states per area of phase space is therefore

\(\frac{\Delta n}{2\pi \Delta E/\omega } = \frac{1}{2\pi \hbar } = \frac{1}{h} . \) (17.26)
