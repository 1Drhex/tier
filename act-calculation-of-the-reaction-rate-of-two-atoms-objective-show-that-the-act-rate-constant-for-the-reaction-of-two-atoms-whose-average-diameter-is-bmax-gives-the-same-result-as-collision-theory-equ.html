ACT Calculation of the Reaction Rate of Two Atoms
Objective Show that the ACT rate constant for the reaction of two atoms whose average diameter is \(b_{max}\), gives the same result as collision theory, equation 3.7 \([k(T)=\pi b^{2}_{max}v_{r} \exp\left(\frac{-\epsilon^{*}}{kT} \right)]\) .

Method Use equation 3.17 \([k(T)=\frac{kT}{h}\frac{\acute{q^{\ddagger} } }{q_{A}q_{B}}\exp\left(-\frac{\epsilon^* }{kT} \right) ]\) with column 2 of Table 3.1. Note \(\acute{q ^{\ddagger}}\) is the partition function of the activated complex, but without the degree of freedom corresponding to the reaction coordinate. For the reaction of two atoms, the reaction coordinate corresponds to the vibration of the diatomic activated complex.

Type of Motion Partition Function Order of Magnitude
Translational, three-dimensional, per unit volume \(z _{r}/V = (2\pi mkT)^{3/2}/h^{3}\) \(10^{24} cm^{-3}\)
Rotational, linear molecule \(\tau _{r}= 8\pi ^{2}IkT/h^{2}\sigma \) \(10^{2}\)
Rotational, nonlinear molecule \(z _{r} = [\pi^{1/2}(8\pi^{2} kT) ^{3/2} /h^{3} \sigma] \times (I_{A}I_{B} I_{c})^{1/2}\) \(10^{3}\)
Vibrational, each degree of freedom, measured from the lowest (zero point) vibrational leve \(z _{v}= [1 - \exp(-hv/kT)]^{-1}\)

1 (high v)

10 (low v)
Electronic \(z _{e}= \sum g_i\exp(-\epsilon^{8}_{i}/kT)\) 1

Solution

The exponential factors, \(\exp(-\epsilon^{*}/kT)\), are the same for ACT and collision theory, so that our task is to show that the pre-exponential factor in ACT, (kT/h)\((\acute{q ^{\ddagger}}/ q_{A}q_{B})\), is equivalent to the preexponential factor in equation 3.7. Using Table 3.1 and recalling that the moment of inertia in this case is \(\mu b_{max}^2\), we find that the ACT preexponential factor is

\(\frac{kT}{h}\frac{\acute{q ^{\ddagger}}}{q_{A}q_{B}} =\frac{kT}{h}\frac{[2\pi (m_{A}+m_{B})kT/h^{2}]^{3/2}[2lKT/\hslash^{2}]}{[2\pi m_{A}KT/h^{2}]^{3/2}[2\pi m_{B}KT/h^{2}]^{3/2}}\)

\(=\frac{kT}{h}\frac{[2IKT/\hslash^{2}]}{[2\pi( m_{A} m_{B}/(m_{A}+ m_{B})KT/h^{2}]^{3/2}}\) (3.22)

\(=\frac{kT}{h}\frac{[2(\mu b^{2}_{max})KT/ \hslash ^{2}]}{[2\pi \mu KT/h^{2}]^{3/2}} =\frac {(KT) ^{1/2}(2 \pi)^{1/2} 2b^{2}_{max}}{ \mu^{1/2}}\)

\(=\left(\frac{8kT }{\pi \mu } \right)^{1/2}\pi b^{2}_{max}=v_{r}\pi b^{2}_{max}\)

This is just the result of equation 3.7.
