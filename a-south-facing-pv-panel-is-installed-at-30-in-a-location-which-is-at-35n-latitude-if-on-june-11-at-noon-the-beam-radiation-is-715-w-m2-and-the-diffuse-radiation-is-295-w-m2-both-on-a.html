Guest Question.

A south-facing PV panel is installed at 30° in a location which is at 35°N latitude. If, on June 11 at noon, the beam radiation is 715 \(W/m^2\) and the diffuse radiation is 295 \(W/m^2\), both on a horizontal surface, estimate the absorbed solar radiation on the PV panel. The thickness of the glass cover on PV is 2 mm, the extinction coefficient K is \(4m^{-1}\), and ground reflectance is 0.2.

Admin
Solution

From Table 2.1,

Month Day number Average day of the month
Date N δ (deg)
January i 17 17 20.92
February 31 + i 16 47 – 12.95
March 59 + i 16 75 – 2.42
April 90 + i 15 105 9.41
May 120 + i 15 135 18.79
June 151 + i 11 162 23.09
July 181 + i 17 198 21.18
August 212 + i 16 228 13.45
September 243 +i 15 258 2.22
October 273 + i 15 288 – 9.60
November 304 + i 15 318 – 18.91
December 334 + i 10 344 – 23.05

on June 11, δ = 23.09°. First, the effective incidence angles need to be calculated. For the beam radiation, the incidence angle is required, estimated from Eq. (2.20):

cos(θ) = sin(L-β) sin(δ) + cos(L-β) cos(δ) cos(h)

=sin(35-30) sin(23.09) +cos(35-30) cos(23.09)cos(0)

= 0.951 or θ = 18.1°

For the diffuse and ground-reflected components, Eq. (3.4) can be used:

\(θ_{e,D}\)=59.68 – 0.1388β + 0.001497\(β^2\)

=59.68 – 0.1388(30) + 0.001497\((30)^2\)

=56.7°

\(θ_{e,G}\)=90 – 0.5788β + 0.002693\(β^2\)

=90 – 0.5788(30) +0.002693\((30)^2\)

=75.1°

Next, we need to estimate the three incidence angle modifiers. At an incidence angle of 18.1°, the refraction angle from Eq. (2.44), \(n=\frac{n_{2}}{n_{1}} = \frac{sinθ_{1}}{sinθ_{2}}\) is

sin(\(θ_{r}\)) = sin(θ)/1.526 = sin(18.1)/1.526 = 0.204 or \(θ_{r}\) = 11.75°

Using Eq. (9.30) with K = 4\(m^{-1}\)and L = 0.002 m,

\((\tau \alpha)_{B} =e^{-[KL/cos(θ_{r})]}\left\{1-\frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{sin^2(θ_{r}-θ)}{sin^2(θ_{r}+θ)}+\frac{tan^2(θ_{r}-θ)}{tan^2(θ_{r}+θ)} \right] \right\}\)

\( =e^{-[0.008/cos(11.75)]}\left\{1-\frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{sin^2(11.75-18.1)}{sin^2(11.75+18.1)}+\frac{tan^2(11.75-18.1)}{tan^2(11.75+18.1)} \right] \right\}\)

= 0.9487

At normal incidence, as shown in Chapter 2, Section 2.3.3, Eq. (2.49), \(r_{(0)}=\left\lgroup\frac{n-1}{n+1} \right\rgroup ^2\) the term in the square bracket of Eq. (9.30)

is replaced with 1 – [(n – 1)/ (n + 1)\( ]^2\) . Therefore,

\((\tau \alpha)_{n} =e^{-KL} \left[1-\left\lgroup\frac{n-1}{n+1}\right\rgroup^2 \right]\)

\(=e^{-0.008} \left[1-\left\lgroup\frac{1.526-1}{1.526+1}\right\rgroup^2 \right]\)

= 0.9490

And from Eq. (9.28),

\(K_{θ,B}=\frac{(\tau \alpha )_{B}}{(\tau \alpha )_{n}} =\frac{0.9487}{0.9490} =0.9997\)

For the diffuse radiation,

sin(\(θ_{r}\)) = sin(\(θ\))/1.526 = sin(56.7)/1.526 = 0.5477 or \(θ_{r}\) = 33.21°

Using Eq. (9.30),

\((\tau \alpha)_{D} =e^{-[KL/cos(θ_{r})]}\left\{1-\frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{sin^2(θ_{r}-θ_{e,D})}{sin^2(θ_{r}+θ_{e,D})}+\frac{tan^2(θ_{r}-θ_{e,D})}{tan^2(θ_{r}+θ_{e,D})} \right] \right\}\)

\((\tau \alpha)_{D} =e^{-[0.008/cos(33.21)]}\left\{1-\frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{sin^2(33.21-56.7)}{sin^2(33.21+56.7)}+\frac{tan^2(33.21-56.7)}{tan^2(33.21+56.7)} \right] \right\}\)

= 0.9111

And from Eq. (9.28),

\(K_{θ,D}=\frac{(\tau \alpha )_{D}}{(\tau \alpha )_{n}} =\frac{0.9111}{0.9490} =0.9601\)

Using Eq. (9.31) \( K_{θ}=b_{0} +b_{1}θ+b_{2}θ^2+b_{3}θ^3+b_{4}θ^4+b_{5}θ^5\) for monocrystalline cells gives \(K_{θ,D}\) = 0.9679; and for polycrystalline cells, \(K_{θ,D}\) = 0.9674. Both values are close to the value just obtained, so even if the exact type of PV cell is not known, acceptable values can be obtained from Eq. (9.31) using either type of cell.

For the ground-reflected radiation,

sin(\(θ_{r}\)) = sin(\(θ\))/1.526 = sin(75.1)/1.526 = 0.6333 or \(θ_{r}\) = 39.29°

Using Eq. (9.30),

\((\tau \alpha)_{G} =e^{-[KL/cos(θ_{r})]}\left\{1-\frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{sin^2(θ_{r}-θ_{e,G})}{sin^2(θ_{r}+θ_{e,G})}+\frac{tan^2(θ_{r}-θ_{e,G})}{tan^2(θ_{r}+θ_{e,G})} \right] \right\}\)

\( =e^{-[0.008/cos(39.29)]}\left\{1-\frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{sin^2(39.29-75.1)}{sin^2(39.29+75.1)}+\frac{tan^2(39.29-75.1)}{tan^2(39.29+75.1)} \right] \right\}\)

=0.7325

And from Eq. (9.28),

\(K_{θ,G}=\frac{(\tau \alpha )_{G}}{(\tau \alpha )_{n}} =\frac{0.7325}{0.9490} =0.7719\)

Using Eq. (9.31) for monocrystalline cells gives \(K_{θ,G}\) = 0.7752, and for polycrystalline cells, \(K_{θ,G}\) = 0.7665. Both values, again, are close to the value obtained previously.

For the estimation of the air mass, the zenith angle is required, obtained from Eq. (2.12):

cos(\(\Phi\) ) = sin(L) sin(δ) + cos(L) cos(δ) cos(h)

= sin(35) sin(23.09) + cos(35) cos(23.09) cos(0)

=0.9785 or \(\Phi\) = 11.91°

The air mass is obtained from Eq. (9.27):

\(m=\frac{1}{cos(\Phi)+ 0.5050(96.08-\Phi)^{-1.634}}\)

\(=\frac{1}{cos(11.91 )+ 0.5050(96.08-11.91)^{-1.634}}\)

= 1.022

It should be noted that the same result is obtained using Eq. (2.81):

\(m=\frac{1}{cos(\Phi )}\) = 1.022

From Eq. (9.26),

\(M= \alpha _{0}+ \alpha _{1}m + \alpha _{2}m^2 + \alpha _{3}m^3 + \alpha _{4}m^4\)

=0.935823 + 0.054289 × (1.022) – 0.008677 × (1.022\()^2\)+ 0.000527 ×(1.022\()^3\) – 0.000011 × (1.022\()^4\)

= 0.9823

From Eq. (2.88),

\(R_{B}=\frac{cos(θ)}{cos(Φ)} =\frac{cos(18.1)}{cos(11.91)} \)=0.971

Now, using Eq. (9.29),

\(S =(\tau \alpha )_{n}M\left\{G_{B}R_{B}K_{\theta ,B}+G_{D}K_{\theta ,D}\left[\frac{1+cos(\beta )}{2} \right]+G_{\rho G}K_{\theta ,G}\left[\frac{1-cos(\beta )}{2} \right] \right\}\)

\( =0.9490× 0.9828\left\{715×0.971×0.9997+295×0.9601 +\left[\frac{1+cos(30)}{2} \right]+1010×0.2×0.7719\left[\frac{1-cos(30 )}{2} \right] \right\}\)

= 0903.5 W/\(m^2\)
