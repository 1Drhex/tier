Guest Question.

Calculate the deflection at the middle of the 12-ft span of a simply supported beam with a concentrated load of 10 kip, 9 ft from one of the supports, as shown in Fig. 15.13(a).

Admin
Solution

The deflection curve is shown in Fig. 15.13(b). To find the value of \(t_{B/A}\), we draw the M/EI diagram by parts in Fig. 15.13(c). The calculations are shown in Table (A). From the sum in column three,

\(t_{B/A}=\frac{675\ kip-ft^{3}}{EI}\)
\(A^{*}\) (from A to B) \((kip-ft^{2})\)/EI \(\bar{x}_{B}\)(ft) \(\bar{x}_{B}A^{*}\) (A to B) \((kip-ft^{3})\)/EI
(1) \(\frac{12(30)}{2EI}=\frac{180}{EI}\) 4 \(\frac{720}{EI}\)
(2) \(\frac{3(-30)}{2EI}=-\frac{45}{EI}\) 1 \(-\frac{45}{EI}\)
\(\Sigma \frac{675}{EI}\)
\(t_{B/A}^{\curvearrowleft }\)

 

To determine the value of \(t_{M/A}\), we draw the M/EI diagram by parts in Fig. 15.13(d). Calculating the value of \(t_{M/A}\), we have

\(t_{M/A} = \bar{x}_{M}A^{*}\) (from A to M) = \(2\left(\frac{6}{2}\right) \frac{15}{EI}\)

=\(\frac{90\ kip-ft^{3}}{EI}\)

Therefore, from Eq. (15.8),

\(\delta _{M}=-\left(\frac{t_{B/A}}{2}-t_{M/A} \right)=-\left(\frac{675}{2EI}-\frac{90}{EI} \right)\)

=\(-\frac{247.5\ kip-ft^{3}}{EI}=-\frac{4.277(10^{8})\ lb-in.^{3}}{EI}\)

The formula for the maximum deflection [Table A.14(e) of the Appendix] is

\(\delta _{max}=-\frac{Pb(L^{2}-b^{2})^{3/2}}{9\sqrt{3}LEI }\)

For P = 10,000 lb, L = 144 in., and b = L/4 = 36 in., the maximum deflection

\(\delta _{max}=-\frac{4.348(10^{8})lb-in.^{3}}{EI}\)

The deflection at the middle of the beam and the maximum deflection differ by approximately 1.6 percent.
