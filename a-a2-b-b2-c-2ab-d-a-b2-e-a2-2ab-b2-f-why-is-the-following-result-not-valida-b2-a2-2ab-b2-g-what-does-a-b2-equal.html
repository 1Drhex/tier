Guest Question.

Let \(\mathbf{A}=\left(\begin{array}{rr}-3 & 7 \\ 1 & 8\end{array}\right)\) and \(\mathbf{B}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}5 & 9 \\ 2 & 3\end{array}\right)\). Determine:

(a)\(\mathrm{A}^{2}\)(b) \(B^{2}\) (c) \(2 \mathrm{AB}\) (d) \((\mathrm{A}+\mathrm{B})^{2}\) (e) \(\mathrm{A}^{2}+2 \mathrm{AB}+\mathrm{B}^{2}\)

(f) Why is the following result not valid \((\mathrm{A}+\mathbf{B})^{2}=\mathbf{A}^{2}+2 \mathbf{A B}+\mathbf{B}^{2}\) ?

(g) What does ( \(\mathrm{A}+\mathrm{B})^{2}\) equal?

Admin
Solution

(a) Remember, \(\mathrm{A}^{2}=\mathrm{A} \times \mathrm{A}\) so we have

\(\mathrm{A}^{2}=\left(\begin{array}{rr}-3 & 7 \\1 & 8\end{array}\right) \times\left(\begin{array}{rr}-3 & 7 \\1 & 8\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{rr}16 & 35 \\5 & 71\end{array}\right)\)

(b) Similarly we have

\(\mathbf{B}^{2}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}5 & 9 \\2 & 3\end{array}\right) \times\left(\begin{array}{ll}5 & 9 \\2 & 3\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{ll}43 & 72 \\16 & 27\end{array}\right)\)

(c) How do we work out \(2 \mathrm{AB}\) ?

Multiply the matrices \(\mathbf{A}\) and \(\mathrm{B}\) and then multiply our result by 2 :

\(2 \mathrm{AB}=2\left(\begin{array}{rr}-3 & 7 \\1 & 8\end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{ll}5 & 9 \\2 & 3\end{array}\right)=2\left(\begin{array}{rr}-1 & -6 \\21 & 33\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{rr}-2 & -12 \\42 & 66\end{array}\right)\)

(d) How do we find \((\mathrm{A}+\mathrm{B})^{2} ?\)

Remember, \((\mathrm{A}+\mathrm{B})^{2}=(\mathrm{A}+\mathrm{B}) \times(\mathrm{A}+\mathbf{B})\). First, we add the matrices \(\mathrm{A}\) and \(\mathrm{B}\) and then square the result:

\(\begin{gathered}\mathbf{A}+\mathbf{B}=\left(\begin{array}{rr}-3 & 7 \\1 & 8\end{array}\right)+\left(\begin{array}{ll}5 & 9 \\2 & 3\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{ll}2 & 16 \\3 & 11\end{array}\right) \\(\mathrm{A}+\mathbf{B})^{2}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}2 & 16 \\3 & 11\end{array}\right) \times\left(\begin{array}{ll}2 & 16 \\3 & 11\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{ll}52 & 208 \\39 & 169\end{array}\right)\end{gathered}\)

(e) To find \(\mathrm{A}^{2}+2 \mathrm{AB}+\mathrm{B}^{2}\) we add the above parts (a), ( \(\left.\mathbf{b}\right)\) and \((\mathbf{c})\) :

\(\begin{aligned}\mathbf{A}^{2}+2 \mathbf{A} \mathbf{B}+\mathbf{B}^{2}=& \underbrace{\left(\begin{array}{rr}16 & 35 \\5 & 71\end{array}\right)}_{=\mathbf{A}^{2}}+\underbrace{\left(\begin{array}{rr}-2 & -12 \\42 & 66\end{array}\right)}_{=2 \mathrm{AB}}+\underbrace{\left(\begin{array}{ll}43 & 72 \\16 & 27\end{array}\right)}_{=\mathbf{B}^{2}} \\&=\left(\begin{array}{lr}16-2+43 & 35-12+72 \\5+42+16 & 71+66+27\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{rr}57 & 95 \\63 & 164\end{array}\right)\end{aligned}\)

Note, that parts (d) and (e) are not equal. That is

\((\mathrm{A}+\mathrm{B})^{2} \neq \mathrm{A}^{2}+2 \mathrm{~A} \mathbf{B}+\mathbf{B}^{2} \quad[\text { not equal }]\)

Why not?

(f) Because matrix multiplication is not commutative that means order of multiplication matters. Remember, the matrix multiplication \(\mathrm{AB}\) does not equal BA.What is \((\mathrm{A}+\mathrm{B})^{2}\) equal to?

\(\begin{aligned}(\mathrm{A}+\mathrm{B})^{2} &=(\mathrm{A}+\mathrm{B}) \times(\mathrm{A}+\mathrm{B}) \\&=\mathrm{A}^{2}+\mathrm{AB}+\mathrm{BA}+\mathrm{B}^{2}\end{aligned}\)\(\mathrm{AB} \neq \mathrm{BA}\) [not equal],

therefore \(\mathrm{AB}+\mathrm{BA} \neq 2 \mathrm{AB}\) [not equal]. Let’s check the result for this particular example.We know what \(\mathrm{AB}\) is by part (c) above but what is BA equal to?

\(\mathrm{BA}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}5 & 9 \\2 & 3\end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{rr}-3 & 7 \\1 & 8\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{rr}-6 & 107 \\-3 & 38\end{array}\right)\)

(g) Next we work out \(\mathrm{A}^{2}+\mathrm{AB}+\mathrm{BA}+\mathrm{B}^{2}\). By the above parts we have

\(\begin{aligned}\mathbf{A}^{2}+\mathrm{A} \mathrm{B}+\mathrm{B} \mathrm{A}+\mathrm{B}^{2}=& \underbrace{\left(\begin{array}{rr}16 & 35 \\5 & 71\end{array}\right)}_{=\mathrm{A}^{2}}+\underbrace{\left(\begin{array}{rr}-1 & -6 \\21 & 33\end{array}\right)}_{=\mathrm{AB}}+\underbrace{\left(\begin{array}{rr}-6 & 107 \\-3 & 38\end{array}\right)}_{=\mathrm{BA}}+\underbrace{\left(\begin{array}{ll}43 & 72 \\16 & 27\end{array}\right)}_{=\mathrm{B}^{2}} \\&=\left(\begin{array}{rr}16-1-6+43 35-6+107+72\\5+21-3+16 71+33+38+27\end{array}\right) \\&=\left(\begin{array}{ll}52 & 208 \\39 & 169\end{array}\right)\end{aligned}\)

This is the same answer as to part \((d)\). Therefore we have \((A+B)^{2}=A^{2}+A B+B A+B^{2}\).
