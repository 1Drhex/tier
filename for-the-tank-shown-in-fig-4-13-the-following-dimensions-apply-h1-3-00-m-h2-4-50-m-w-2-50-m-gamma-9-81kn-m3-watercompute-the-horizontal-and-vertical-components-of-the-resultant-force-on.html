Guest Question.

For the tank shown in Fig. 4.13, the following dimensions apply:

\(h_1 = 3.00 m\)

\(h_2 = 4.50 m\)

\(w = 2.50 m\)

\(\gamma = 9.81{kN}/{m^3} \left(water\right) \)

Compute the horizontal and vertical components of the resultant force on the curved surface and the resultant force itself. Show these force vectors on a sketch.

Admin
Solution

Using the steps outlined above:

1. The volume above the curved surface is shown in Fig. 4.15.

2. The weight of the isolated volume is the product of the specific weight of the water times the volume. The volume is the product of the area times the length w. The total area is the sum of a rectangle and a quarter circle.

\(Area =A_1+A_2=h_1\cdot R+\frac{1}{4} \left(\pi R^2\right) \)

\( Area = \left(3.00m\right) \left(1.50m\right) +\frac{1}{4}\left[\pi\left(1.50m\right)^2 \right] =4.50 m^2 +1.767 m^2 \)

\(Area = 6.267 m^2\)

\(volume = area \cdot w = \left(6.267m^2\right) \left(2.50m\right) = 15.67m^3\)

\(weight = \gamma V =\left(9.81{kN}/{m^3}\right) \left(15.67m^3\right) =153kN\)

3. Then \(F_V=153.7kN,\) acting upward through the centroid of the volume. The location of the centroid is found using the composite-area technique. Refer to Fig. 4.15 for the data. Each value should be obvious except \(X_2\), the location of the centroid of the quadrant. From Appendix L,

\(X_2 = 0.424R=0.424\left(1.50m\right)=0.636m \)

Then, the location of the centroid for the composite area is

\(\overline{X} =\frac{A_1 x_1+A_2 x_2}{A_1+A_2} =\frac{\left(4.50\right)\left(0.75\right)+\left(1.767\right)\left(0.636\right) }{4.50+1.767} =0.718m\)

4. The vertical projection of the curved surface is shown in Fig. 4.15. The height s equals 1.50 m.

5. The depth to the centroid of the projected area is

\(h_c = h_1+ s/2 =3.00m +{\left(1.50m\right) }/{2}=3.75m\)

6. The magnitude of the horizontal force is

\(F_H = \gamma sw\left(h_1+s/2\right) =\gamma swh_c\)

\(F_H = \left(9.81 {kN}/{m^3}\right) \left(1.50m\right) \left(2.50m\right) \left(3.75m\right) =138.0kN\)

7. The depth to the line of action of the horizontal component is found from

\(h_p=h_c + {s^2}/{12h_c}\)

\(h_p = 3.75m +{\left(1.50\right)^2 }/{\left[\left(12\right) \left(3.75\right) \right] }=3.80m\)

8. The resultant force is computed from

\(F_R =\sqrt{{F_V}^2+{F_H}^2} \)

\(F_R=\sqrt{\left(153.7kN\right)^2 + \left(138.0kN\right)^2 } =206.5kN\)

9. The angle of inclination of the resultant force relative to the horizontal is computed from

\(\phi = tan^{-1}\left({F_V}/{F_H}\right) \)

\(\phi = tan^{-1}\left({153.7}/{138.0}\right) =48.1°\)

10. The horizontal component, the vertical component, and the resultant force are shown in Fig. 4.16. Note that the line of action of \(F_R\)is through the center of curvature of the surface. Also note that the vertical component is acting through the centroid of the volume of liquid above the surface. The horizontal component is acting through the center of pressure of the projected area at a depth \(h_p\)from the level of the free surface of the fluid.
