Guest Question.

A bracket, subjected to a force of 5 kN inclined at an angle of 60° with the vertical, is shown in Fig. 7.29. The bracket is fastened by means of four identical bolts to the structure. The bolts are made of plain carbon steel 30C8 \( \left(S_{y t}=400 N / mm ^{2}\right) \) and the factor of safety is 5 based on maximum shear stress. Assume maximum shear stress theory and determine the size of the bolts.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } P=5 kN \quad S_{y t}=400 N / mm ^{2} \quad(f s)=5 \).

Step I Permissible shear stress

\( \tau_{\max }=\frac{S_{s y}}{(f s)}=\frac{0.5 S_{y t}}{(f s)}=\frac{0.5(400)}{5}=40 N / mm ^{2} \).

Step II Tensile and shear stresses in bolts
The force P is resolved into horizontal component \( P_{h} \text { and vertical component } P_{v} \). These components are given by,

\( P_{h}=P \sin 60^{\circ}=5000 \sin 60^{\circ}=4330.13 N \).

\( P_{v}=P \cos 60^{\circ}=5000 \cos 60^{\circ}=2500 N \).

\( \text { Effect of } P_{h} \).

The horizontal component \( P_{h} \) is parallel to the axis of the bolts. It has following two effects:
(i) a direct tensile force on each bolt; and
(ii) a turning moment about the centre of gravity of four bolts (G) in clockwise direction.
The direct tensile force on each bolt is given by,

\( P_{1}=P_{2}=\frac{P_{h}}{\text { (No. of bolts) }}=\frac{4330.13}{4} \).

= 1082.53 N (i).

As shown in Fig. 7.29, the centre of gravity of four bolts is at a distance (60 + 100) or 160 mm from the lower edge. The point of application of the force Ph is at a distance of 200 mm from the lower edge. Therefore, the eccentricity of the force \( P_{h} \) is (200–160) or 40 mm about the centre of gravity. The turning moment \( \left(M_{h}\right) \) due to horizontal component is given by,

\( M_{h}=P_{h} \times 40=4330.13 \times 40 \).

= 173 205.2 N-mm (ii)
The moment \( M_{h} \) acts in the clockwise direction.
Effect of \( P_{v} \)
The vertical component \( P_{v} \) is perpendicular to
the axis of the bolts. It has following two effects:
(i) a direct shear force on each bolt; and
(ii) a turning moment about the tilting edge C in the clockwise direction.
The direct shear force on each bolt is given by,

\( P_{s}=\frac{P_{v}}{(\text { No. of bolts })}=\frac{2500}{4}=625 N \) (iii).

The distance of the vertical component \( P_{v} \) from the tilting edge C is 240 mm. The turning moment \( \left(M_{v}\right) \) due to the vertical component is given by,

\( M_{v}=P_{v} \times 240=2500 \times 240 \).

= 600 000 N-mm (iv).

The moment \( M_{v} \) acts in the clockwise direction.
It is observed that turning moments due to horizontal and vertical components are clockwise and the resultant turning moment is obtained by their addition. The resisting forces set up in bolts 1 and 2, are proportional to their distances from the tilting edge C. Two bolts, denoted by 1, are at the farthest distance from the edge C. Therefore, the tensile force due to the tendency of the bracket to tilt about the edge C is maximum for two bolts denoted by 1.

From Eq. (7.10),

\( P_{1}^{\prime \prime}=\frac{P e l_{1}}{2\left(l_{1}^{2}+l_{2}^{2}\right)} \) (7.10).

\( P_{1}^{\prime \prime}=\frac{(P e) l_{1}}{2\left(l_{1}^{2}+l_{2}^{2}\right)}=\frac{\left(M_{h}+M_{v}\right) l_{1}}{2\left(l_{1}^{2}+l_{2}^{2}\right)} \).

\( =\frac{(173205.2+600000)(260)}{2\left(260^{2}+60^{2}\right)} \).

= 1411.75 N (v).

From (i) and (v), the tensile force acting on each of the two bolts, denoted by 1, is given by,

\( P_{t}=P_{1}+P_{1}^{\prime \prime}=1082.53+1411.75 \).

= 2494.28 N (vi)
The tensile stress in the bolt is given by,

\( \sigma_{t}=\frac{P_{t}}{A}=\frac{2494.28}{A} N / mm ^{2} \) (a).

From (iii), the shear stress in the bolt is given by,

\( \tau=\frac{P_{s}}{A}=\frac{625}{A} N / mm ^{2} \) (b).

Step III Maximum shear stress in bolts
From Eq. (7.14),

\( t_{\max }=\sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{t}}{2}\right)^{2}+\tau^{2}}=\sqrt{\left(\frac{2494.28}{2 A}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{625}{A}\right)^{2}} \).

\( =\left(\frac{1394.99}{A}\right) N / mm ^{2} \).

Step IV Size of bolts

\( \tau_{\max }=\frac{1394.99}{A} \quad \text { or } \quad 40=\frac{1394.99}{A} \).

∴ A = 34.87 mm²
From Table 7.1, the standard size of the bolts is M 8 (A = 36.6 mm²).
