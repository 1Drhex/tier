Solving for Remaining Balances on Loans

A petroleum engineer needs to purchase new equipment when he is working in the oil fields. The equipment cost $50,000.00 and he has to borrow the funds to pay for the equipment. He agrees to repay the loan over four years by making monthly payments at a yearly interest rate of 10%. Calculate the monthly payments and fill in the spreadsheet shown in Table 4.3

TABLE 4.3
Format for a Spreadsheet for Tracking Payments and Balances on Loans

Period Remaining Principal Balance Payment This Period Interest Paid This Period Principal Paid This Period New Principal Balance
1 Original principal \( A = P_0\left[\frac{i(1 +i)^n}{(1+i)^n - 1} \right]\) Original Principal × Period interest rate Payment – Interest paid this period Remaining principal balance – Principal paid this period
2 New principal balance from previous year \( A = P_0\left[\frac{i(1 +i)^n}{(1+i)^n - 1} \right]\) Remaining principal balance × Period interest rate Payment – Interest paid this period Remaining principal balance – Principal paid this period
3 New principal balance from previous year \( A = P_0\left[\frac{i(1 +i)^n}{(1+i)^n - 1} \right]\) Remaining principal balance × Period interest rate Payment – Interest paid this period Remaining principal balance – Principal paid this period
Admin
Solution

Procedure

First, calculate the period interest rate and the total number of periods.

Second, calculate the amount of the monthly annuity using the uniform series capital recovery factor formula Equation 4.8.

\(A = F_n\left[\frac{i}{(1+i)^n – 1} \right]\) (4.8)

Third, use Equations 4.13 and 4.14 to perform the calculations for the values required in the spreadsheet format shown in Table 4.3.

Principal paid per period = Payment – Interest paid in that period (4.13)

Remaining balance = Starting principal for period – Principal paid that period (4.14)

Part I

\(n = \frac{Number of compounding periods}{Year}\times Total number of years\)

 

\(= \frac{12 periods}{Year}\times 4 years\)

= 48 periods

\(i = \frac{i_n}{m}\)

 

\(i = \frac{10\%/year}{12 periods/year}\)

= 0.83% per period

Part II

Calculate the monthly equivalent uniform series of the present value:

\(A = P_0\left[\frac{i(1 + i)^n}{(1 + i)^n – 1} \right]\)

 

\(A = \$50,000.00\left[\frac{0.0083(1 + 0.0083)^{48}}{(1 + 0.0083)^{48}- 1} \right]\)

 

\(= \$ 50,000.00\left\lgroup\frac{0.012342}{0.486993} \right\rgroup\)

= $ 50,000.00(0.025343)

= $ 1,267.16

Part III

The spreadsheet in Table 4.4 is developed using Equations 4.13 and 4.14 and the format provided in Table 4.3.

TABLE 4.4
Spreadsheet for Solving for Payments and Remaining Balances

Period Remaining Principal Balance Payment This Period Interest Paid This Period Principal Paid This Period New Principal Balance
1 $50,000.00 $1,267.16 $50,000.00 × 0.0083 = $415.00 $1,267.16−$415.00 = $852.16 $50,000.00−$852.16 = $49,147.84
2 $49,147.84 $1,267.16 $49,147.84 × 0.0083 = $407.93 $1,267.16−$407.93 = $859.23 $49,147.84−$859.23 = $488,288.61
3 $48,288.61 $1,267.16 $49,288.61 × 0.0083 = $409.10 $1,267.16−$409.10 =$858.06 $48,288.61−$858.06 = $47,430.55
