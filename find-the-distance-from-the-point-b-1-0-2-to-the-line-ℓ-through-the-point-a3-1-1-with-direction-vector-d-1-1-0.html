Guest Question.

Find the distance from the point \(B=(1,0,2)\) to the line \(\ell\) through the point \(A=(3,1,1)\) with direction vector \(\mathbf{d}=\left[\begin{array}{r}-1 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{array}\right]\)

Admin
Solution

As we have already determined, we need to calculate the length of \(\overrightarrow{P B}\), where \(P\) is the point on \(\ell\) at the foot of the perpendicular from \(B\). If we label \(\mathbf{v}=\overrightarrow{A B}\), then \(\overrightarrow{A P}=\operatorname{proj}_{\mathrm{d}}(\mathbf{v})\) and \(\overrightarrow{P B}=\mathbf{v}-\operatorname{proj}_{\mathrm{d}}(\mathbf{v})\) (see Figure 1.64). We do the necessary calculations in several steps.

Step 1: \(\quad \mathbf{v}=\overrightarrow{A B}=\mathbf{b}-\mathbf{a}=\left[\begin{array}{l}1 \\ 0 \\ 2\end{array}\right]-\left[\begin{array}{l}3 \\ 1 \\ 1\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{r}-2 \\ -1 \\ 1\end{array}\right]\)

Step 2: The projection of \(\mathbf{v}\) onto \(\mathrm{d}\) is

\( \begin{aligned} \operatorname{proj}_{\mathrm{d}}(\mathrm{v}) &=\left(\frac{\mathrm{d} \cdot \mathrm{v}}{\mathrm{d} \cdot \mathrm{d}}\right) \mathrm{d} \\ &=\left(\frac{(-1) \cdot(-2)+1 \cdot(-1)+0 \cdot 1}{(-1)^{2}+1+0}\right)\left[\begin{array}{l} -1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] \\ =& \frac{1}{2}\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] \\ &=\left[\begin{array}{rl} -\frac{1}{2} \\ \frac{1}{2} \\ 0 \end{array}\right] \end{aligned} \)

Step 3: The vector we want is

\( \mathbf{v}-\operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{d}}(\mathbf{v})=\left[\begin{array}{r} -2 \\ -1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]-\left[\begin{array}{r} -\frac{1}{2} \\ \frac{1}{2} \\ 0 \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{r} -\frac{3}{2} \\ -\frac{3}{2} \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\)

Step 4: The distance \(\mathrm{d}(B, \boldsymbol{\ell})\) from \(B\) to \(\ell\) is

\( \left\|\mathbf{v}-\operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{d}}(\mathbf{v})\right\|=\left\|\left[\begin{array}{r} -\frac{3}{2} \\ -\frac{3}{2} \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\right\| \)

Using Theorem \(1.3(\mathrm{~b})\) to simplify the calculation, we have

\( \begin{aligned} \left\|\mathbf{v}-\operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{d}}(\mathbf{v})\right\| &=\frac{1}{2}\left\|\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\right\| \\ &=\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{9+9+4} \\ &=\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{22} \end{aligned}\)

Theorem 1.3 Let \(\mathbf{v}\) be a vector in \(\mathbb{R}^{n}\) and let \(c\) be a scalar. Then

(a) \(\|\mathbf{v}\|=0\) if and only if \(\mathbf{v}=\mathbf{0}\)

(b)\(\|c \mathbf{v}\|=|c|\|\mathbf{v}\|\)
