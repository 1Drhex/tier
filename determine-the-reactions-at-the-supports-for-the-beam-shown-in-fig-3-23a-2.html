Guest Question.

Determine the reactions at the supports for the beam shown in Fig. \(3.23(\mathrm{a})\).

Admin
Solution

Free-Body Diagram. See Fig. \(3.23(\mathrm{b})\).

Static Determinacy. The beam is internally unstable. It is composed of three rigid members, \(A B, B E\), and \(E F\), connected by two internal hinges at \(B\) and \(E\). The structure has \(r=5\) and \(e_{c}=2\); because \(r=3+e_{c}\), the structure is statically determinate.

Support Reactions.

\( \begin{array}{r} +\rightarrow \sum F_{x}=0 \\ A_{x}=0 \end{array} \)

Next, we apply the equation of condition, \(\sum M_{B}^{A B}=0\), which involves the summation of moments about \(B\) of all the forces acting on the portion \(A B\).

\( \begin{aligned} +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{B}^{A B} &=0 \\ -A_{y}(20)+[5(20)](10) &=0 \\ A_{y} &=50 \mathrm{kN} \\ A_{y} &=50 \mathrm{kN} \uparrow \end{aligned} \)

Similarly, by applying the equation of condition \(\sum M_{E}^{E F}=0\), we determine the reaction \(F_{y}\) as follows:

\( \begin{aligned} +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{E}^{E F} &=0 \\ -[3(20)](10)+F_{y}(20) &=0 \\ F_{y} &=30 \mathrm{kN} \\ F_{y} &=30 \mathrm{kN} \uparrow \end{aligned} \)

The remaining two equilibrium equations can now be applied to determine the remaining two unknowns, \(C_{y}\) and \(D_{y}\) :

\( \begin{aligned} +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{D} &=0 \\ -50(90)+[5(40)](70)-C_{y}(50)+[3(90)](5)+30(40) &=0 \\ C_{y} &=241 \mathrm{kN} \\ C_{y} &=241 \mathrm{kN} \uparrow \end{aligned} \)

It is important to realize that the moment equilibrium equations involve the moments of all the forces acting on the entire structure, whereas the moment equations of condition involve only the moments of those forces that act on the portion of the structure on one side of the internal hinge.

Finally, we compute \(D_{y}\) by using the equilibrium equation,

\( \begin{aligned} +\uparrow \sum F_{y} &=0 \\ 50-5(40)+241-3(90)+D_{y}+30 &=0 \\ D_{y} &=149 \mathrm{kN} \\ D_{y} &=149 \mathrm{kN} \uparrow \end{aligned} \)

Alternative Method. The reactions of the beam can be determined alternatively by applying the three equations of equilibrium to each of the three rigid portions \(A B, B E\), and \(E F\) of the beam. The free-body diagrams of these rigid portions are shown in Fig. \(3.23(\mathrm{c})\).These diagrams show the internal forces being exerted through the internal hinges at \(B\) and \(E\) in addition to the applied loads and support reactions. Note that the internal forces acting at each end \(B\) of portions \(A B\) and \(B E\) and at each end \(E\) of portions \(B E\) and \(E F\) have the same magnitudes but opposite senses, according to Newton’s law of action and reaction.

The total number of unknowns (including the internal forces) is nine. Since there are three equilibrium equations for each of the three rigid portions, the total number of equations available is also nine \(\left(r+f_{i}=3 n_{r}=9\right) .\) Therefore, all nine unknowns (reactions plus internal forces) can be determined from the equilibrium equations, and the beam is statically determinate.

Applying the three equations of equilibrium to portion \(A B\), we obtain the following:

\( \begin{array}{rrr} +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{A}^{A B}=0 & -[5(20)](10)+B_{y}(20)=0 & B_{y}=50 \mathrm{kN} \\ +\uparrow \sum F_{y}^{A B}=0 & A_{y}-5(20)+50=0 & B_{y}=50 \mathrm{kN} \\ +\rightarrow \sum F_{x}^{A B}=0 & A_{x}-B_{x}=0 & \end{array} \) (1)

Next, we consider the equilibrium of portion \(E F\) :

\( \begin{array}{lrl} +\rightarrow \sum F_{x}^{E F} & =0 & & E_{x}=0 \\ +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{F}^{E F} & =0 & -E_{y}(20)+[3(20)](10)=0 & E_{y} & =30 \mathrm{kN} \\ +\uparrow \sum F_{y}^{E F} & =0 & 30-3(20)+F_{y}=0 & F_{y} & =30 \mathrm{kN} \end{array} \)

Considering the equilibrium of portion \(B E\), we write

\( +\rightarrow \sum F_{x}^{B E}=0 \quad B_{x}=0 \)

From Eq. (1), we obtain

\( \begin{array}{c} A_{x}=0 \\ +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{C}^{B E}=0 \quad 50(20)+[5(20)](10)-[3(70)](35)+D_{y}(50)-30(70)=0 \\ D_{y}=149 \mathrm{kN} \\ +\uparrow \sum F_{y}^{B E}=0 \quad-50-5(20)+C_{y}-3(70)+149-30=0 \\ C_{y}=241 \mathrm{kN} \end{array} \)
