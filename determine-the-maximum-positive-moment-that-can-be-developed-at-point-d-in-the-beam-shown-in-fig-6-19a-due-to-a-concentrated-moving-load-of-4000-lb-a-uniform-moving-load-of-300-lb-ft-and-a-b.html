Guest Question.

Determine the maximum positive moment that can be developed at point \(D\) in the beam shown in Fig. \(6-19(\mathrm{a})\) due to a concentrated moving load of \(4000 \mathrm{lb}\), a uniform moving load of \(300 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{ft}\), and a beam weight of \(200 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{ft}\).

Admin
Solution

A hinge is placed at \(D\) and positive moments \(\mathbf{M}_{D}\) are applied to the beam. The deflected shape and corresponding influence line are shown in Fig. 6-19b. Immediately one recognizes that the concentrated moving load of \(4000 \mathrm{lb}\) creates a maximum positive moment at \(D\) when it is placed at \(D\), i.e., the peak of the influence line. Also, the uniform moving load of \(300 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{ft}\) must extend from \(C\) to \(E\) in order to cover the region where the area of the influence line is positive. Finally, the uniform weight of \(200 \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{ft}\) acts over the entire length of the beam. The loading is shown on the beam in Fig. 6-19c. Knowing the position of the loads, we can now determine the maximum moment at \(D\) using statics. In Fig. 6-19d the reactions on \(B E\) have been computed. Sectioning the beam at \(D\) and using segment \(D E\), Fig. \(6-19 e\), we have

\( \begin{array}{ll} \curvearrowleft +\Sigma M_{D}=0 ; & -M_{D}-5000(5)+4750(10)=0 \\ & M_{D}=22500 \mathrm{lb} \cdot \mathrm{ft}=22.5 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \end{array} \)

This problem can also be worked by using numerical values for the influence line as in Sec. 6-1. Actually, by inspection of Fig. 6-19b, only the peak value \(h\) at \(D\) must be computed. This requires placing a unit load on the beam at \(D\) in Fig. 6-19a and then solving for the internal moment in the beam at \(D\). Show that the value obtained is \(h=3.33\). By proportional triangles, \(h^{\prime} /(10-5)=3.33 /(15-10)\) or \(h^{\prime}=3.33\). Hence, with the loading on the beam as in Fig. 6-19c, using the areas and peak values of the influence line, Fig. 6-19b, we have

\( \begin{aligned} M_{D} &=500\left[\frac{1}{2}(25-10)(3.33)\right]+4000(3.33)-200\left[\frac{1}{2}(10)(3.33)\right] \\ &=22500 \mathrm{lb} \cdot \mathrm{ft}=22.5 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \end{aligned} \)
