Guest Question.

Given the system

\( \mathbf{\dot{x}} = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 3 & 1 \\ 2 & 8 & 1\\ -10 & -5 & -2\end{bmatrix} \mathbf{x} + \begin{bmatrix}10\\ 0\\ 0\end{bmatrix} u\) (6:33a)

\(y =\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix} \mathbf{x}\) (6:33b)

find out how many poles are in the left half-plane, in the right half-plane, and on the j\(\omega \)-axis.

Admin
Solution

First form \(\left ( s\mathbf{I – A} \right )\) :

\(\left ( s\mathbf{I – A} \right ) = \begin{bmatrix}s & 0 & 0\\ 0 & s & 0\\ 0 & 0 & s\end{bmatrix} – \begin{bmatrix}0 & 3 & 1 \\ 2 & 8 & 1\\ -10 & -5 & -2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}s & -3 & -1 \\ -2 & s-8 & -1\\ 10 & 5 & s+ 2\end{bmatrix}\) (6:34)

Now find the \(det \left ( s\mathbf{I – A} \right )\) :

\(det \left ( s\mathbf{I – A} \right ) = s^{3}- 6s^{2} -7s – 52\) (6:35)

Using this polynomial, form the Routh table of Table 6.18.

TABLE 6.18 Routh table for Example

\(s^{3}\) 1 -7
\(s^{2}\) \(\begin{matrix}\not{-6} & -3 \end{matrix}\) \(\begin{matrix}\not{-52} & -26 \end{matrix}\)
\(s^{1}\) \(\begin{matrix}\not{-\frac{47}{3}} & -1 \end{matrix}\) \(\begin{matrix}\not{0} & 0 \end{matrix}\)
\(s^{0}\) -26

Since there is one sign change in the first column, the system has one right–half-plane pole and two left–half-plane poles. It is therefore unstable. Yet, you may question the possibility that if a nonminimum-phase zero cancels the unstable pole, the system will be stable. However, in practice, the nonminimum-phase zero or unstable pole will shift due to a slight change in the system’s parameters. This change will cause the system to become unstable.

We can use MATLAB to determine the stability of a system represented in state space by using the command eig (A) to find the eigenvalues of the system matrix, A.
