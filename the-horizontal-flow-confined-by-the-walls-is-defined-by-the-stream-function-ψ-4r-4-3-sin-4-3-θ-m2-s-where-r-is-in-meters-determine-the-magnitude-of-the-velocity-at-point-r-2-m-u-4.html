Guest Question.

The horizontal flow confined by the walls is defined by the stream function \(\psi = 4r^{4/3} sin (4/3)\Theta m^2/s \), where r is in meters. Determine the magnitude of the velocity at point r = 2 m, u = 45°. Is the flow rotational or irrotational? Can the Bernoulli equation be used to determine the difference in pressure between the two points A and B?

Admin
Solution

We consider ideal fluid flow.

\(v_r = \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial \psi }{\partial θ } = \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial }{\partial θ } (4r^{\frac{4}{3}} sin\frac{4}{3}θ ) = \frac{1}{r}[4r^{\frac{4}{3}}(\frac{4}{3} cos \frac{4}{3}θ )] = \frac{16}{3} r^{\frac{1}{3}}cos\frac{4}{3}θ \)

 

\(v_θ = -\frac{\partial \psi }{\partial r} = – \frac{\partial }{\partial r}(4 r^{\frac{4}{3}} sin \frac{4}{3} θ ) = – \frac{16}{3} r^{\frac{1}{3}} sin \frac{4}{3} θ \)

At the point \(r = 2 m, θ = 45°.\)

 

\(v_r = \frac{16}{3}(2^{\frac{1}{3}}) cos[\frac{4}{3}(45^{\circ})] =3.360 m/s\)

 

\(v_θ =- \frac{16}{3}(2^{\frac{1}{3}}) cos[\frac{4}{3}(45^{\circ})] =- 5.819 m/s\)

Thus, the magnitude of the velocity is

\(V = \sqrt{v^2_r + v_θ ^2 }= \sqrt{(3.360 m/s)^2 + ( -5.819 m/s)^2}\)

= 6.72 m/s

\(v_r = \frac{\partial \phi }{\partial r}; \frac{16}{3}r^{\frac{1}{3}} cos \frac{4}{3} θ= \frac{\partial \phi }{\partial r}\)

Integrating with respect to r,

\(\phi = 4r^{\frac{4}{3}} cos\frac{4}{3}θ + f(θ )\)

Substituting this result into,

\(v_θ = \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial \phi }{\partial }; – \frac{16}{3}r^{\frac{1}{3}} sin\frac{4}{3}θ = \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial }{\partial θ }[4r^{\frac{4}{3}} cos \frac{4}{3} θ + f(θ )]\)

 

\(– \frac{16}{3}r^{\frac{1}{3}} sin \frac{4}{3}θ = (4r^{\frac{1}{3}})[-\frac{4}{3} sin \frac{4}{3} θ ] + \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial }{\partial θ } [f(0)]\)

 

\(\frac{\partial }{\partial θ }[f(θ )] =0\)

Integrating with respect to u,

\(f(θ ) = C\)

Setting the constant equal to zero,

\(\phi = 4r^{\frac{4}{3}} cos \frac{4}{3}θ \)

Since the potential function can be established, the flow is irrotational. Therefore,

the Bernoulli equation is applicable between any two points in the flow, including points A and B.
