Guest Question.

Member 2 of the truss shown in Fig. 14-16 is subjected to an increase in temperature of \(150^{\circ} \mathrm{F}\). Determine the force developed in member 2 . Take \(\alpha=6.5\left(10^{-6}\right) /{ }^{\circ} \mathrm{F}, E=29\left(10^{6}\right) \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{in}^{2}\). Each member has a crosssectional area of \(A=0.75 \mathrm{in}^{2}\).

Admin
Solution

Since there is a temperature increase, \(\Delta T=+150^{\circ} \mathrm{F}\). Applying Eq. 14-25 to member 2, where \(\lambda_{x}=0.7071, \lambda_{y}=0.7071\), we have

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\left(Q_{N x}\right)_{0} \\ \left(Q_{N y}\right)_{0} \\ \left(Q_{F x}\right)_{0} \\ \left(Q_{F y}\right)_{0}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{cc}\lambda_{x} & 0 \\ \lambda_{y} & 0 \\ 0 & \lambda_{x} \\ 0 & \lambda_{y}\end{array}\right] A E \alpha \Delta T\left[\begin{array}{r}1 \\ -1\end{array}\right]=A E \alpha \Delta T\left[\begin{array}{r}\lambda_{x} \\ \lambda_{y} \\ -\lambda_{x} \\ -\lambda_{y}\end{array}\right]\) (14-25)

\( \left[\begin{array}{l} \left(Q_{1}\right)_{0} \\ \left(Q_{2}\right)_{0} \\ \left(Q_{7}\right)_{0} \\ \left(Q_{8}\right)_{0} \end{array}\right]=A E(6.5)\left(10^{-6}\right)(150)\left[\begin{array}{r} 0.7071 \\ 0.7071 \\ -0.7071 \\ -0.7071 \end{array}\right]=A E\left[\begin{array}{rr} 0.000689325 \\ 0.000689325\\ -0.000689325 \\ -0.000689325\\ \end{array}\right] \begin{array}{rr} 1 \\ 2 \\ 7\\ 8 \end{array}\)

The stiffness matrix for this truss has been developed in .

Expanding to determine the equations of the unknown displacements, and solving these equations simultaneously, yields

\( \begin{array}{l} D_{1}=-0.002027 \mathrm{ft} \\ D_{2}=-0.01187 \mathrm{ft} \\ D_{3}=-0.002027 \mathrm{ft} \\ D_{4}=-0.009848 \mathrm{ft} \\ D_{5}=-0.002027 \mathrm{ft} \end{array} \)

Using Eq. 14-30 to determine the force in member 2, we have

\(\begin{aligned} q_{F}=\frac{A E}{L}\left[-\lambda_{x}-\lambda_{y}\right.& \lambda_{x} &\left.\lambda_{y}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}D_{N x} \\ D_{N y} \\ D_{F x} \\ D_{F y}\end{array}\right]+\left(\begin{array}{ll}\left.q_{F}\right)_{0} & (14-30)\end{array}\right.\\ \end{aligned}\) (14-30)

\(\begin{aligned}q_{2}&=\frac{0.75\left[29\left(10^{6}\right)\right]}{10 \sqrt{2}}[-0.707 \quad-0.707 \quad 0.707 \quad 0.707]\left[\begin{array}{c}-0.002027 \\ -0.01187 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{array}\right]-0.75\left[29\left(10^{6}\right)\right]\left[6.5\left(10^{-6}\right)\right](150) \\&=-6093 \mathrm{lb}=-6.09 \mathrm{k} \end{aligned}\)

Note that the temperature increase of member 2 will not cause any reactions on the truss since externally the truss is statically determinate. To show this consider the matrix expansion of Eq. (1) for determining the reactions. Using the results for the displacements, we have

\(\begin{aligned} Q_{6}=A E[-& 0.1(-0.002027)+0-0.035(-0.002027) \\+&0.035(-0.009848)-0.035(-0.002027)]+A E[0]=0 \end{aligned}\) \(\begin{aligned} Q_{7}=A E[&-0.035(-0.002027)-0.035(-0.01187) \\ &\quad-0.1(-0.002027)+0+0]+A E[-0.000689325]=0 \end{aligned}\) \(\begin{aligned} Q_{8}=A E[-0.035(&-0.002027)-0.035(-0.01187)+0 \\ &+0-0.1(-0.002027)]+A E[-0.000689325]=0 \end{aligned}\)
