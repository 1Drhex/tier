Guest Question.

Determine for the given structure all reaction forces as well as all extreme values of the internal forces (normal, she-ar force, and bending moments) and the sketch of the internal for-ce diagrams for the beam A − B.

Admin
Solution

With the help of the free-body sketch, we can determine the reaction forces as follows. Consideration of the equilibrium of sub system \(⑤\) yields

\(\underline{\underline{C_{H}=0} }.\)

Therefore, the hori-zontal reaction force \(E_{H}\) vanishes, since the system does not have any horizontal loading. In order to calculate the remaining vertical reaction forces \(C_{V} , D_{V} , \) and \(E_{V} ,\) we determine the internal forces of the sub system \(①\) . A han-dy choice of the equilibrium equations leads directly to the respective forces:

\(\overset{\curvearrowright }{B}: -2aW-aR_{1}-\frac{\sqrt{2} }{2}S_{1}a=0 \rightsquigarrow S_{1}=-2\sqrt{2}(qa+W),\)

 

\(\overset{\curvearrowright }{F}: B_{V}a-Wa=0 \rightsquigarrow B_{V}=W,\)

 

\(\rightarrow : \frac{\sqrt{2} }{2}S_{1}+B_{H}=0 \rightsquigarrow B_{H}=2(qa+W).\)

Equilibrium in vertical direction of sub system \(②\) leads to

\(\uparrow : E_{V}+B_{V}-R_{2}=0.\)

Thus, the reaction force \(E_{V}\) follow as

\(\underline{\underline{E_{V}=2\sqrt{2}q a-W } }.\)

The remaining reaction forces \(C_{V}\) and \(D_{V}\) can be determined by

\(\overset{\curvearrowright }{D}:-aE_{V}+aC_{V}-3aW-2aR_{2}=0 \rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{C_{V}=qa(4+2\sqrt{2} )-2W} }, \)

 

\(\uparrow :D_{V}+E_{V}+C_{V}-W-R_{1}-R_{2}=0 \rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{D_{V}=-qa(2\sqrt{2}+2 )} }.\)

The substructure A−B can be idealized as a beam with two supports.The support reactions \(B_{V} , B_{H}\) and \(S_{1}\) have already been determined. The bending moment in point F can be computed by

\(M_{F}=-Wa-\frac{qa^{2}}{2}.\)
