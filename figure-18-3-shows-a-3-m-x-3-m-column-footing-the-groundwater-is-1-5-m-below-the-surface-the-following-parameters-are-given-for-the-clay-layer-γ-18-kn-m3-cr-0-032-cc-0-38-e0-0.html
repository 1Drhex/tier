Guest Question.

Figure 18.3 shows a \(3 m \times 3 m\) column footing. The groundwater is 1.5 m below the surface. The following parameters are given for the clay layer: \(\gamma = 18 kN/m^{3}, C_{r} = 0.032, C_{c} = 0.38, e_{0} = 0.85, p_{c}^{\prime } = 50 kPa, c_{v} = 0.011 m^{2}/day, C_{a} = 0.013.\)

Find the total settlement after 10 years.

Admin
Solution

\(\Delta H,\) total primary consolidation settlement; H, thickness of the drainage layer; \(C_{c},\) compression index of the clay layer in the virgin zone;\( C_{r}, \)recompression index of the clay layer in the recom-pression zone; \( e_{0},\) void ratio of the clay layer at midpoint of the clay layer prior to loading; \(p_{o}^{\prime },\) effective stress at the midpoint of the clay layer prior to loading; \(p_{c}^{\prime },\) preconsolidation pressure; \(p_{f}^{\prime },\) pressure after the footing is placed; \(c_{v},\) consolidation coefficient \(= 0.011 m^{2}/day; C_{a},\) secondary compression coefficient.

Step 1: Find the total settlement due to consolidation.

\(\Delta H=H\frac{C_{r}}{1+e_{0}}\log \frac{p_{0}^{\prime }+\Delta p_{1}}{p_{0}^{\prime }}+H\frac{C_{c}}{1+e_{0}}\log \frac{p_{c}^{\prime }+\Delta p_{2}}{p_{c}^{\prime }}\)

The clay layer is 7 m thick and the footing is placed 1 m below the surface. The top 1 m is not subjected to consolidation. The compressible portion of the clay layer is 6 m thick. Find the effective stress at the mid layer of the compressible clay stratum; \((p_{0}^{\prime });\)

\(p_{0}^{\prime }=1.5\times \gamma _{clay}+2.5\times (\gamma _{clay}-\gamma _{w})\)

 

\(p_{0}^{\prime }=1.5\times 18+2.5\times (18-9.807)=47.5kN/m^{2}.\)

Step 2: Find \(\Delta p.\)
\(\Delta p =\) Increase of stress at the midpoint of the clay layer due to the footing.

Total load of 350 kN is distributed at a larger area at the mid section of the clay layer.

Area of the mid section of the clay layer \(= (3 + 1.5 + 1.5) \times (3 + 1.5 + 1.5) = 36 m^{2}\)

\(\Delta p = 350/36 = 9.72 kN/m^{2}.\)

Step 3: Find the final pressure after application of the footing load.
\(p_{f}^{\prime}=\) Initial pressure + pressure increase due to footing load at mid point
\(p_{f}^{\prime} = p_{o}^{\prime } + \Delta p = 47.5 + 9.72 = 57.22 kN/m^{2}.\)

Step 4: Apply values in the consolidation equation.

\(\Delta H=H\frac{C_{r}}{1+e_{0}}\log \frac{p_{0}^{\prime }+\Delta p_{1}}{p_{0}^{\prime }}+H\frac{C_{c}}{1+e_{0}}\log \frac{p_{c}^{\prime }+\Delta p_{2}}{p_{c}^{\prime }}\)

\((\Delta p_{1}) =\) Stress increase from initial stress \( (p_{o}^{\prime })\) to preconsolidation pressure \(p_{c}^{\prime }.\)

Since \(p_{o}^{\prime }\) is 47.5 and \(p_{c}^{\prime }\) was given to be \(50 kN/m^{2}, Δp_{1}\) would be \((50 − 47.5) kN/m^{2}.\)

\(\Delta p_{1}= 2.5 kN/m ^{2}.\)

\((Δp_{2}) =\) Stress increase from preconsolidation pressure \(p_{c}^{\prime }\) to final pressure \(p_{f}^{\prime }.\)
Since \(p_{c}^{\prime }\) is \(50 kN/m^{2}\) and \(p_{f}^{\prime }\) is found to be \(57.2, Δp_{2}\) would be \((57.22 − 50) kN/m^{2}.\)

 

\(\Delta p_{2}= 7.22 kN/m^{2}.\)

 

\(\Delta H=6\frac{0.032}{1+0.85}\log \frac{p_{0}^{\prime }+\Delta p_{1}}{p_{0}^{\prime }}+6\frac{0.38}{1+0.85}\log \frac{p_{c}^{\prime }+\Delta p_{2}}{p_{c}^{\prime }}\)

 

\(\Delta H=0.1037\log \frac{47.5+2.5}{47.5}+1.23\log \frac{50+7.22}{50}\)

 

\(\Delta H=0.0023+0.072\)

 

\(\Delta H=0.0743 m\) (2.92in.).

Step 5: Find the time required for the completion of the primary consolidation;
Typically, \(90\%\) consolidation is assumed to be the end of primary consolidation.

\(t=\frac{H^{2}T_{v}}{c_{v}}\)

\(U = 90\%\);
From Table 17.1, find the \(T_{v}\) value.
\(T_{v} = 0.933\)
H = Thickness of the drainage layer. In this case, drainage is possible from both top and bottom.
Sand layer is seen below the clay layer. This is a double drainage situation. Hence,\( H = 3 m.\)
Therefore, \(t = 3^{2} \times 0.933/0.011 = 763.4\) days
Since \(C_{v}\) is given in \(m^{2}/day,\) t will be in days.

Step 6: Find the settlement due to secondary compression.

\(S=C_{a}/(1+e_{0})L\times \log (t/t_{p})\)

S, secondary compression; \(C_{a},\) secondary compression coefficient \(= 0.013; e_{0},\) initial void
ratio = 0.85; L, thickness of the clay layer \(= 6 m; t_{p},\) time for completion of primary consoli-dation \(= 763.4 \)days (found in Step 5); t, time elapsed \(= 10\) years \(− 763.4\) days \(= 10 \times 365 − 763.4 = 2886.6\) days.
In this problem, we need to find the settlement after 10 years.
Primary consolidation starts from day 0 and ends on day\( 763.4\)

Secondary compression starts from day 763.4.

\(S=C_{a}/(1+e_{0})L\times \log (t/t_{p})\)

 

\(S=0.013/(1+0.85)6\log (2886.6/763.4)=0.0243 m\)

Total settlement = Total settlement due to primary consolidation + Total settlement due to second-ary compression
Total settlement \(= 0.0743 + 0.0243 = 0.0986 m = 9.86 cm.\)
