Guest Question.

A homeowner fills his pool after cleaning it. The water initially has no oxygen in it. However, oxygen is gained from the atmospheric air by diffusion. What will be the mole fraction of oxygen at a depth of 1 cm from the surface 10 hours after the pool filling?

Admin
Solution

Atmospheric air contains about 21% oxygen. We will assume the air and water are at 300 K and the air is at standard atmospheric pressure of 101.325 kPa. The saturation pressure of water at 300 K is 3.57 kPa, so the partial pressure of the air is 101.325-3.57=97.76 kPa. The partial pressure of the oxygen in the air is

\(P_{O_{2} } =0.21(97.76kPa)=20.53kPa=0.2053bar\) (11.58)

Using Eq. (11.55)

\(y_{A} -liquid=\frac{P_{A}-gas }{H}\) (11.55)

and Henry’s constant from Table 11.7

 

Table 11.7 Henry’s constant H for gases in water. [The table entries are pressure (bar)].

Air 6.2\(\times 10^{4}\) 7.4\(\times 10^{4}\) 8.4\(\times 10^{4}\) 9.2\(\times 10^{4}\)
\(H_{2}\) 6.7\(\times 10^{4}\) 7.2\(\times 10^{4}\) 8.6\(\times 10^{4}\) 7.9\(\times 10^{4}\)
\(N_{2}\) 7.6\(\times 10^{4}\) 8.9\(\times 10^{4}\) 1.0\(\times 10^{5}\) 1.1\(\times 10^{5}\)
\(O_{2}\) 3.8\(\times 10^{4}\) 4.5\(\times 10^{4}\) 5.2\(\times 10^{4}\) 5.7\(\times 10^{4}\)
CO 5.1\(\times 10^{4}\) 6.0\(\times 10^{4}\) 6.7\(\times 10^{4}\) 7.4\(\times 10^{4}\)
\(CO_{2}\) 1.3\(\times 10^{3}\) 1.7\(\times 10^{3}\) 2.2\(\times 10^{3}\) 2.7\(\times 10^{3}\)

 

, we can get the mole fraction of oxygen on the liquid side of the
pool surface .

\(y_{O_{2} } -liquid=\frac{P_{O_{2}}-gas }{H}=\frac{0.2053}{45000}=4.56\times 10^{-6}\) (11.59)

 

This is the mole fraction at the surface of the water. To determine the mole fraction at a depth, we can use Eq. (11.52):

\(\frac{C_{A} (x,t)-C_{A,i}}{C_{A,o} -C_{A,i} } =erfc\left(\frac{x}{2\sqrt{D_{AB}t } } \right)\) (11.52)

 

, \(\frac{y_{O_{2} } (x,t)-y_{O_{2} ,i}}{y_{O_{2} ,o} -y_{O_{2} ,i} } =erfc\left(\frac{x}{2\sqrt{D_{AB}t } } \right)\) (11.60)

\(D_{AB}\) for gases in water is given in Table 11.4

For oxygen in water at about 300 K, \(D_{AB}\) is about \(2.4\times 10^{-9} m^{2} /s\) .

The time is \(t=10h\times \left(3600s/h\right) =36000s\).

Putting values in Eq. (11.60) , we have

\(\frac{y_{O_{2} } (1cm,10hour)-0}{4.56\times 10^{-6} -0 } =erfc\left(\frac{0.01}{2\sqrt{\left(2.4\times 10^{-9} \right)(36000)} } \right)\)

 

which reduces to \(y_{O_{2} } (1cm,10hour) =\left(4.56\times 10^{-6}\right) \left[erfc(0.538)\right] =2.03\times 10^{-6}\)

The mole fraction of oxygen at a depth of 1 cm after 10 hours is \(2.03\times 10^{-6}\)
