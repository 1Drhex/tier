Guest Question.

An ice cube is 2 cm on a side. How much energy is needed to melt ten ice cubes if their initial temperature is 0°C?

Admin
Solution

First, let’s determine the mass of the ice cubes. In Table C.5 at 0°C, we observe that vi = 0.0010908 m³/kg. The total mass of the ice cubes is then

\( m=\frac{V}{v_{i}}=\frac{(10)\left(0.02^{3}\right) m ^{3}}{0.0010908 m ^{3} / kg }=0.0733 kg \).

The energy to melt a kilogram of ice, that is, the heat of fusion for ice, is 320 kJ/kg. So, the energy needed to melt the ice cubes is found to be

\( m h_{i f}=0.0733 kg \times 320 kJ / kg =23.5 kJ \).
