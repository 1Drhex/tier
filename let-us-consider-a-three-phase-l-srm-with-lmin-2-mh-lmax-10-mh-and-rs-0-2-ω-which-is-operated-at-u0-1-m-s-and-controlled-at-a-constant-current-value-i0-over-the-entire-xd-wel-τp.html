Guest Question.

Let us consider a three-phase L-SRM with \(L_{\min }\) = 2 mH, \(L_{\max}\) = 10 mH, and \(R_{s}\) = 0.2 Ω, which is operated at \(U_{0}\) = 1 m/s and controlled at a constant current value \(I_0\) over the entire \(x_{d wel}=\tau _{p}/2\)= τp/2 = 10 mm, which corresponds to equal pole and interpole primary lengths, for voltage \(V_{0}\) = 42 Vdc.

Calculate
1. Current \(I_{0}\), peak flux linkage \(ψ _{\max}\), average thrust \(F_{xav}\), and load force \(F_{load}\) at \(U_{0}\) = 1 m/s.
2. For constant load and a 10% increase in average voltage \(V_{0}(\Delta V=+0.1V_{0})\) indicate the way for calculating the eigen values and current and speed transients using the small signal approach, with \(\tau _{1}=G_{m}\)/B = 3 s, B = 5.6 Ns.

Admin
Solution

1. The conducting time/phase \(t_{on}\) is

\(t_{on}=\frac{x_{d wel}}{U_{0}}=\frac{10\times 10^{-3} }{1}=0.01s\) (11.44)

Using voltage equation yields (at constant speed)

\(V_{0}t_{on}=R_{s}I_{0}t_{on}+(L_{\max}-L_{\min })I_{0}\) (11.45)

\(I_{0}=\frac{V_{0}t_{on}}{R_{s}t_{on}+(L_{\max}-L_{\min })}=\frac{42\times 0.01}{0.2\times 0.01+(10-2)\times 10^{-3} } =42A \) (11.46)

The average thrust \(F_{xav}\)/phase is in fact total average thrust as only one phase is active at any time:

\(F_{xav}=\frac{1}{2}i^{2}_{0} \frac{\partial L}{\partial x}=\frac{i^{2}_{0}}{2}\cdot \frac{(L_{\max}-L_{\min })}{x_{d well}}=\frac{42^{2}}{2} \cdot \frac{(10-2)10^{-3} }{10\times 10^{-3}}=705.6N \) (11.47)

Now the load force \(F_{load}\) is

\(F_{load}=F_{xav}-BU=705.6-5.6\times 1=700N\) (11.48)

2. The inductance variation may be written as

\(L(x)=L_{\min }+(L_{\max}-L_{\min })\frac{x<br /> }{x_{d well}}; 0\leq x\leq x_{d well}\) (11.49)

So,

\(K_{b}=\frac{\partial L}{\partial X}I_{0}=\frac{(L_{\max}-L_{\min })}{x_{d well}}\cdot I_{0}=\frac{(10-2)\times 10^{-3} }{10\times 10^{-3}}\times 42=33.6 Wb/m \) (11.50)

\(R_{e}=R_{s}+\frac{\partial L}{\partial x}\cdot U_{0}=0.2+\frac{(10-2)\times 10^{-3} }{10\times 10^{-3}}\times 1=0.2+0.8=1\Omega \) (11.51)

The mover mass \(M_{G}=\tau _{m}\cdot B=3\times 5.6=16.8Kg \) and the equivalent electrical time constant

\(\tau _{e}=\frac{L_{av} }{R_{e} }=\frac{6\times 10^{-3} }{1}=6\times 10^{-3}s \) (11.52)

The two small signal equations (11.43) write

\(\left\lgroup s+\frac{1}{\tau _{e} } \right\rgroup \Delta i+\frac{k_{b} }{L_{av} }\Delta U=\frac{\Delta V}{L_{av}}; R_{e}=R_{s}+\frac{\partial L}{\partial x}U_{0}; L_{av}\approx \frac{(L_{\max}+L_{\min })}{2} \)

\(-\frac{k_{b} }{G_{m} }\Delta i+ \left\lgroup s+\frac{1}{\tau _{m} } \right\rgroup \Delta U=-\frac{\Delta F_{l} }{G_{m}}; \tau _{e}=\frac{L_{av}}{R_{e}}; k_{b}=\frac{\partial L}{\partial x}i_{0}; \tau _{m}=\frac{G_{m}}{B} \) (11.43)

\(\left\lgroup s+\frac{1}{6\times 10^{-3} } \right\rgroup \Delta i+\frac{33.6}{6\times 10^{-3}}\Delta U=\frac{\Delta V}{6\times 10^{-3}} \)

\(-\frac{33.6}{16.8}\Delta i+ \left\lgroup s+\frac{1}{3} \right\rgroup \Delta U=\frac{-\Delta F_{L} }{16.8}\) (11.53)

Now solving the characteristic equation (11.53) for ΔV = 0.1\(V_{0}\) = 4.2 V and \(\Delta F_{L} \) = 0, the two eigen values \(s_{1,2} \) are obtained. Once the eigen values are calculated (most probably in our case both are real and negative, say, \(-\tau _{1},-\tau _{2}\)), the speed U is

\(U(t)=U_{0}+\Delta U(t)=U_{0}+A_{1}e^{-\frac{t}{\tau 1} } +A_{2}e^{-\frac{t}{\tau 2} } +(U_{final}-U_{0})\) (11.54)

The new conducting time \(t^{'}_{on}\) (at the new steady-state speed) is

\((V_{0}+\Delta V )t^{'}_{on}=R_{s}I_{0}t^{'}_{on}+(L_{\max}-L_{\min })I_{0}\) (11.55)

\(U_{final}=U_{0}\cdot \frac{t_{on} }{t^{'}_{on} } \) (11.56)

Now also at t = 0, U = \(U_{0}\) = 1 m/s, and\((dU/dt)_{t=0}\) = 0 and thus from (11.54), both \(A_{1}\) and \(A_{2} \) constants are obtained. The current variation Δi may be obtained from (11.42)

\(i=i_{0}+\Delta i; U=U_{0}+\Delta U; V=V_{0}+\Delta V; F_{load}=F_{Lo}+\Delta F_{load} \) (11.42)

\(\Delta i=\left\lgroup\frac{\Delta U(t)}{\tau _{m} }+\frac{d\Delta U(t)}{dt} \right\rgroup \frac{G_{m} }{K_{b} }\) (11.57)

As expected, the final speed increase is +10% as is voltage increase from constant load; and the current increases and then decreases to its initial value \(I_{0}\), because the load is unchanged. The structural diagram with its reduced form may be used directly in the design of control system (Figure 11.14).
