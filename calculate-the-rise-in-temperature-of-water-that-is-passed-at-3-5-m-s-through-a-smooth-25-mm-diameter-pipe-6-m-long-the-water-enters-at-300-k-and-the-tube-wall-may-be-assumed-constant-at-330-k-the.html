Guest Question.

Calculate the rise in temperature of water that is passed at 3.5 m/s through a smooth 25 mm diameter pipe, 6 m long. The water enters at 300 K, and the tube wall may be assumed constant at 330 K. The following methods may be used:
(a) The simple Reynolds analogy (Equation \(\frac{h}{C_{p}\rho u} =0.032Re^{-1/4}\))
(b) The Taylor–Prandtl modification (Equation \(\frac{h}{C_{p}\rho u} =\frac{0.032Re^{-1/4}}{1+2.0Re^{-1/8}(Pr-1)} \))
(c) The universal velocity profile (Equation \(\frac{h}{C_{p}\rho u} =\frac{0.032Re^{-1/4}}{1+0.817Re^{-1/8}[(Pr-1)+\ln (\frac{5}{6}Pr+\frac{1}{6} )]} \))
(d) \(Nu=0.023Re^{0.8}Pr^{0.33}\)

Admin
Solution

Taking the fluid properties at 310 K and assuming that fully developed flow exists, an approximate solution will be obtained neglecting the variation of properties with temperature:
\(Re=\frac{0.025×3.5×1000}{0.7×10^{-3}}=1.25×10^{5} \)
\(Pr=\frac{4.18×10^{3}×0.7×10^{-3}}{0.65}=4.50\)
(a) Reynolds analogy
\(\frac{h}{C_{p}\rho u}=0.032Re^{-0.25} \)
\(h=[4.18×1000×1000×3.5×0.032(1.25×10^{5})^{-0.25}]=24,902W/m^{2}K \ or \ 24.9kW/m^{2}K\)
Heat transferred per unit time in length dL of pipe=hπ 0.025 dL (330-θ) kW, where θ is the temperature at a distance L m from the inlet.
Rate of increase of heat content of fluid = \((\frac{\pi }{4} 0.025^{2}×3.5×1000×4.18)d\theta kW\)
The outlet temperature \(\theta ^{\prime }\) is then given by
\(\int_{300}^{\theta ^{\prime }}{\frac{d\theta }{(330-\theta )} } =0.0109h\int_{0}^{6}{dL} \)
where h is in \(kW/m^{2}K\)
\(\therefore \)
\(\log _{10}(330-\theta ^{\prime })=\log _{10}30-(\frac{0.0654h}{2.303} )=1.477-0.0283h\)
In this case,
\(h=24.9kW/m^{2}K\)
\(\therefore \)
\(\log _{10}(330-\theta ^{\prime })=(1.477-0.75)=0.772\)
and
\(\theta ^{\prime }=324.1K\)
(b) Taylor–Prandtl equation
\(\frac{h}{C_{p}\rho u} =0.032Re^{-1/4}[1+2.0Re^{-1/8}(Pr-1)]^{-1}\)
\(\therefore \)
\(h=\frac{24.9}{(1+2.0×3.5/4.34)}=9.53kW/m^{2}K \)
and
\(\log _{10}(330-\theta ^{\prime })=1.477-(0.0283×9.53)=1.207\)
\(\theta ^{\prime }=313.9K\)
(c) Universal velocity profile equation
\(\frac{h}{C_{p}\rho u} =0.032Re^{-1/4}\left\{1+0.82Re^{-1/8}[(Pr-1)+\ln (0.83Pr+0.17 )]\right\} ^{-1}\)
\(=\frac{24.9}{1+(0.82/4.34)(3.5+2.303×0.591)}=12.98kW/m^{2}K \)
\(\therefore \)
\(\log _{10}(330-\theta ^{\prime })=1.477-(0.0283×12.98)=1.110\)
and
\(\theta ^{\prime }=317.1K\)
(d) \(Nu=0.023Re^{0.8}Pr^{0.33}\)
\(h=\frac{0.023×0.65}{0.0250}(1.25×10^{5})^{0.8}(4.50)^{0.33} \)
\(=0.596×1.195×10^{4}×1.64=1.168×10^{4}W/m^{2}K \ or \space 11.68kW/m^{2}K\)
and
\(\log _{10}(330-\theta ^{\prime })=1.477-(0.0283×11.68)=1.147\)
\(\theta ^{\prime }=316.0K\)
