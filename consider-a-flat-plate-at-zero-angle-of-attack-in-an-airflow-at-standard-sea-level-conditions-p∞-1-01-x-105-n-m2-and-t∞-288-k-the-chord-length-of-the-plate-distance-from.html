Guest Question.

Consider a flat plate at zero angle of attack in an airflow at standard sea level conditions \(\left(p_{\infty}=1.01 \times 10^{5} N / m ^{2} \text { and } T_{\infty}=288 K \right)\). The chord length of the plate (distance from the leading edge to the trailing edge) is 2 m. The planform area of the plate is \(40 m ^{2}\). At standard sea level conditions, \(\mu_{\infty}=1.7894 \times 10^{-5} kg /( m )( s )\). Assume the wall temperature is the adiabatic wall temperature \(T_{a w}\). Calculate the friction drag on the plate when the freestream velocity is (a) 100 m/s, (b) 1000 m/s.

Admin
Solution

(a) The freestream density is

\(\rho_{\infty}=\frac{p_{\infty}}{R T_{\infty}}=\frac{1.01 \times 10^{5}}{(287)(288)}=1.22 kg / m ^{3}\)

The speed of sound is

\(a_{\infty}=\sqrt{\gamma R T_{\infty}}=\sqrt{(1.4)(287)(288)}=340.2 m / s\)

The Mach number is \(M_{\infty}=100 / 340.2=0.29 . \text { Hence, } M_{\infty}\) is low enough to assume incompressible flow, and we can use Equation (18.22),

\(C_{f}=\frac{1.328}{\sqrt{\operatorname{Re}_{c}}} (18.22)\)

Please note that for the flow over a flat plate at zero angle of attack, the freestream velocity and density,\(V_{\infty}\) and \(ρ_{\infty}\), are the same as the velocity and density at the outer edge of the boundary layer, \(u_{e}\)and \(ρ_{e}\). Hence, these quantities can be used interchangeably. Thus,

\(\operatorname{Re}_{c}=\frac{\rho_{\infty} V_{\infty} c}{\mu_{\infty}}=\frac{(1.22)(100)(2)}{1.7894 \times 10^{-5}}=1.36 \times 10^{7}\)

Hence, \(C_{f}=\frac{1.328}{\sqrt{\operatorname{Re}_{c}}}=\frac{1.328}{\sqrt{1.36 \times 10^{7}}}=3.60 \times 10^{-4}\)

The friction drag on one surface of the plate is given by

\(D_{f}=\frac{1}{2} \rho_{\infty} V_{\infty}^{2} S C_{f}=\frac{1}{2}(1.22)(100)^{2}(40)\left(3.6 \times 10^{-4}\right)=87.8 N\)

The total drag due to friction is generated by the shear stress acting on both the top and bottom of the plate. Since \(D_{f}\) above is the friction drag on only one surface, we have

Total friction drag \(=D=2 D_{f}=2(87.8)=175.6 N\)

(b) For\(V_{\infty}= 1000 m/s,\) we have

\(M_{\infty}=\frac{V_{\infty}}{a_{\infty}}=\frac{1000}{340.2}=2.94\)

Clearly, the flow is compressible, and we have to use Equation (18.44), or more directly,Figure 18.8. From Figure 18.8, we have for \(M_{\infty}= M_{e} = 2.94\) and an adiabatic wall,

\(C_{f}=\frac{1.328}{\sqrt{\operatorname{Re}_{c}}} F\left(M_{e}, \operatorname{Pr}, \frac{T_{w}}{T_{e}}\right) (18.44)\)

\(C_{f} \sqrt{\operatorname{Re}_{c}}=1.2\)

or \(C_{f}=\frac{1.2}{\sqrt{ Re _{C}}}\)

\(\operatorname{Re}_{c}=\frac{\rho_{\infty} V_{\infty} c}{\mu_{\infty}}=\frac{(1.22)(1000)(2)}{1.7894 \times 10^{-5}}=1.36 \times 10^{8}\)

Thus, \(C_{f}=\frac{1.2}{\sqrt{1.36 \times 10^{8}}}=1.03 \times 10^{-4}\)

The friction drag on one surface is

\(D_{f}=\frac{1}{2} \rho_{\infty} V_{\infty}^{2} S C_{f}=\frac{1}{2}(1.22)(1000)^{2}(40)\left(1.03 \times 10^{-4}\right)=2513 N\)

Taking into account both the top and bottom surfaces,

Total friction drag \(1=D=2 D_{f}=2(2513)=5026 N\)
