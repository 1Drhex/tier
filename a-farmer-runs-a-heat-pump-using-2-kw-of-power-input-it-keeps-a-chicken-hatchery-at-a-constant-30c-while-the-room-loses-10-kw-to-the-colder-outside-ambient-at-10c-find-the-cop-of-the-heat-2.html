Guest Question.

A farmer runs a heat pump using 2 kW of power input. It keeps a chicken hatchery at a constant 30°C while the room loses 10 kW to the colder outside ambient at 10°C. Find the COP of the heat pump, the rate of exergy destruction in the heat pump and its heat exchangers, and the rate of exergy destruction in the heat loss process.

Admin
Solution

C.V. Hatchery, steady state at 30°C for the hatchery

Energy Eq.:

\(\begin{aligned}&0=\dot{ Q }_{ H }-\dot{ Q }_{\text {Loss }} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \dot{ Q }_{ H }=\dot{ Q }_{\text {Loss }}=10 kW \\& COP =\dot{ Q }_{ H } / \dot{ W }=10 kW / 2 kW = 5\end{aligned}\)

C.V. Heat pump, steady state

Energy eq.: \(0=\dot{ Q }_{ L }+\dot{ W }-\dot{ Q }_{ H } \quad \Rightarrow \quad \dot{ Q }_{ L }=\dot{ Q }_{ H }-\dot{ W }=8 kW\)

Entropy Eq.: \(0=\frac{\dot{ Q }_{ L }}{ T _{ L }}-\frac{\dot{ Q }_{ H }}{ T _{ H }}+\dot{ S }_{ gen HP }\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{ S }_{ gen HP }=\frac{\dot{ Q }_{ H }}{ T _{ H }}-\frac{\dot{ Q }_{ L }}{ T _{ L }}=\frac{10}{273+30}-\frac{8}{273+10}=0.00473 kW / K \\&\dot{\Phi}_{\text {destr. } HP }= T _{ o } \dot{ S }_{ gen HP }=283.15 K \times 0.00473 kW / K =1.34 k W\end{aligned}\)

 

C.V. From hatchery at 30°C to the ambient 10°C. This is typically the walls and the outer thin boundary layer of air. Through this goes \(\dot{ Q }_{\text {Loss }}\).

 

Entropy Eq.: \(0=\frac{\dot{ Q }_{\text {Loss }}}{ T _{ H }}-\frac{\dot{ Q }_{\text {Loss }}}{ T _{ amb }}+\dot{ S }_{\text {gen walls }}\)

 

\(\dot{ S }_{\text {gen walls }}=\frac{\dot{ Q }_{\text {Loss }}}{ T _{ amb }}-\frac{\dot{ Q }_{\text {Loss }}}{ T _{ H }}=\frac{10}{283}-\frac{10}{303}=0.00233 kW / K\)

 

\(\dot{\Phi}_{\text {destr. walls }}= T _{o} \dot{ S }_{\text {gen } HP }=283.15 K \times 0.00233 kW / K = 0 . 6 6 k W\)

 

Comment: Notice the two exergy destruction terms exactly equals work input. It was necessary to use \(T _{ o }\) as ambient T otherwise the flux of exergy associated with the heat transfers \(\dot{ Q }_{ L } \text { and } \dot{ Q }_{\text {Loss }}\) would be non-zero and a storage effect present.
