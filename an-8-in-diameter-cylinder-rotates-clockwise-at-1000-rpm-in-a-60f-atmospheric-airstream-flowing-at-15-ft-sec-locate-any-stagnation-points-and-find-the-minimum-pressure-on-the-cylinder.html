Guest Question.

An 8-in.-diameter cylinder rotates clockwise at 1000 rpm in a 60째F-atmospheric airstream flowing at 15 ft/sec. Locate any stagnation points and find the minimum pressure on the cylinder.

Admin
Solution

The circulation is calculated (see Eq. 8.5.19) to be

\(\Gamma=\oint_{L} \mathbf{V} \cdot d \mathbf{s} (8.5.19)\)

\(\begin{aligned}\Gamma &=\oint_{L} \mathbf{V} \cdot d \mathbf{s} \\&=2 \pi r_{c}^{2} \omega=2 \pi \times\left(\frac{4}{12}\right)^{2} \times \frac{1000 \times 2 \pi}{60}=73.1 \mathrm{ft}^{2} / \mathrm{sec}\end{aligned}\)

This is greater than \(4 \pi U_{\infty} r_{c}=4 \pi \times 15 \times 4 / 12=62.8 \mathrm{ft}^{2} / \mathrm{sec} ;\) hence the stagnation point is off the cylinder (see Fig. 8.20b) at \(\theta=270^{\circ} .\) The cylinder’s radius is

\(r_{0}=-\frac{\Gamma}{4 \pi U_{\infty} \sin 270^{\circ}}=-\frac{73.1}{4 \pi \times 15 \times(-1)}=0.388 \mathrm{ft}\)

Only one stagnation point exists.

\(\quad\quad\)The minimum pressure is located on the top of the cylinder where \(\theta=90^{\circ} .\) Using Bernoulli’s equation from the free stream to that point, we have, letting \(p_{\infty}=0\),

\(\begin{array}{c}\qquad p_{\infty}+\rho \frac{U_{\infty}^{2}}{2}=p_{\min }+\frac{\rho}{2}\left(v_{\theta}\right)_{\max }^{2} \\\\\therefore p_{\min }=\frac{\rho}{2}\left[U_{\infty}^{2}-\left(v_{\theta}\right)_{\max }^{2}\right]=\frac{\rho}{2}\left[U_{\infty}^{2}-\left(-2 U_{\infty} \sin 90^{\circ}-\frac{\Gamma}{2 \pi r_{c}}\right)^{2}\right] \\\\\quad=\frac{0.0024 \operatorname{slug} / f t^{3}}{2}\left[15^{2}-\left(2 \times 15+\frac{73.1}{2 \pi \times 4 / 12}\right)^{2}\right] \mathrm{ft}^{2} / \sec ^{2}=-4.78 \mathrm{psf}\end{array}\)
