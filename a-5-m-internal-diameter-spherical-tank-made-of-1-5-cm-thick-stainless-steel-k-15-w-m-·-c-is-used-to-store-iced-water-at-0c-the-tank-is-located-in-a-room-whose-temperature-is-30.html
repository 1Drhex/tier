Guest Question.

A 5-m-internal-diameter spherical tank made of 1.5-cm-thick stainless steel (\(k=15 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\)) is used to store iced water at 0°C. The tank is located in a room whose temperature is 30°C. The walls of the room are also at 30°C. The outer surface of the tank is black (emissivity \(\varepsilon = 1 \)), and heat transfer between the outer surface of the tank and the surroundings is by natural convection and radiation. The convection heat transfer coefficients at the inner and the outer surfaces of the tank are \(80 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\) and \(10 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\), respectively. Determine (a) the rate of heat transfer to the iced water in the tank and (b) the amount of ice at 0°C that melts during a 24-h period. The heat of fusion of water at atmospheric pressure is \( h_{i f}=333.7 kJ / kg\).

Admin
Solution

A spherical container filled with iced water is subjected to convection and radiation heat transfer at its outer surface. The rate of heat transfer and the amount of ice that melts per day are to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Heat transfer is steady since the specified thermal conditions at the boundaries do not change with time. 2 Heat transfer is one-dimensional since there is thermal symmetry about the midpoint. 3 Thermal conductivity is constant.

Properties The thermal conductivity of steel is given to be \(k=15 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\). The heat of fusion of water at 1 atm is \(h_{i f}=333.7 kJ / kg\). The outer surface of the tank is black and thus its emissivity is ε = 1.

Analysis (a) The inner and the outer surface areas of sphere are

\( A_{i}=\pi D_{i}^{2} = \pi(5 m )^{2} = 78.54 m ^{2} A_{o}=\pi D_{o}^{2}=\pi(5.03 m )^{2}=79.49 m ^{2} \)

 

We assume the outer surface temperature \(T_{2}\) to be 5°C after comparing convection heat transfer coefficients at the inner and the outer surfaces of the tank. With this assumption, the radiation heat transfer coefficient can be determined from

\( h_{r a d}=\varepsilon \sigma\left(T_{2}^{2}+T_{s u r r}^{2}\right)\left(T_{2}+T_{s u r r}\right) \)

 

\( \left.=1\left(5.67 \times 10^{-8} W / m ^{2} \cdot K ^{4}\right)\left[(273+5 K )^{2}+(273+30 K )^{2}\right](273+30 K )(273+5 K )\right]=5.570 W / m ^{2} \cdot K\)

 

The individual thermal resistances are

\( R_{\text {conv }, i}=\frac{1}{h_{i} A}=\frac{1}{\left(80 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(78.54 m ^{2}\right)}=0.000159^{\circ} C / W \)

 

\( R_{1}=R_{\text {sphere }}=\frac{r_{2}-r_{1}}{4 \pi k r_{1} r_{2}}=\frac{(2.515-2.5) m }{4 \pi\left(15 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)(2.515 m )(2.5 m )}=0.000013{ }^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\( R_{\text {conv }, o}=\frac{1}{h_{o} A}=\frac{1}{\left(10 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(79.49 m ^{2}\right)}=0.00126^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\( R_{r a d}=\frac{1}{h_{r a d} A}=\frac{1}{\left(5.57 W / m ^{2} .{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(79.54 m ^{2}\right)}=0.00226^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\( \frac{1}{R_{e q v}}=\frac{1}{R_{c o n v, o}}+\frac{1}{R_{r a d}}=\frac{1}{0.00126}+\frac{1}{0.00226} \longrightarrow R_{e q v}=0.000809^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\( R_{\text {total }} = R_{\text {conv }, i}+R_{1}+R_{e q v} = 0.000159+0.000013+0.000809 = 0.000981^{\circ} C / W\)

 

Then the steady rate of heat transfer to the iced water becomes

\( \dot{Q}=\frac{T_{\infty 1}-T_{\infty 2}}{R_{\text {total }}}=\frac{(30-0)^{\circ} C }{0.000981^{\circ} C / W }= 3 0 , 5 8 1 W\)

 

(b) The total amount of heat transfer during a 24-hour period and the amount of ice that will melt during this period are

\( Q=\dot{Q} \Delta t=(30.581 kJ / s )(24 \times 3600 s )=2.642 \times 10^{6} kJ\)

 

\( m_{i c e}=\frac{Q}{h_{i f}}=\frac{2.642 \times 10^{6} kJ }{333.7 kJ / kg }= 7 9 1 8 kg\)

 

Check: The outer surface temperature of the tank is

\( \dot{Q}=h_{\text {conv+rad }} A_{o}\left(T_{\infty 1}-T_{s}\right) \rightarrow T_{s}=T_{\infty 1}-\frac{\dot{Q}}{h_{\text {conv+rad }} A_{o}} \)

 

\( = 30^{\circ} C -\frac{30,581 W }{\left(10+5.57 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(79.54 m ^{2}\right)}=5.3^{\circ} C\)

 

which is very close to the assumed temperature of 5°C for the outer surface temperature used in the evaluation of the radiation heat transfer coefficient. Therefore, there is no need to repeat the calculations.
