Guest Question.

}COMPARISON WITH EXPERIMENT. Biot reported data (Table E5.1) in his investigations of heat conduction and Newton’s “Law” of cooling by placing thermometers along a bar, with one end heated to \(82 ^{◦}C\) and the bar exposed to air at \(13 ^{◦}C.\) In Biot’s experiment, the bar was much longer (27.342 dm) such that the free end was found to be at ambient temperature. Biot successfully analyzed his data by assuming an exponential decay; show if this follows from the prior analysis.

Solution

We again assume the Biot number is sufficiently large such that a lumped analysis applies. The derivation leading to Eqs. (E5.2) is modified because the bar is essentially infinitely long for this experiment such that the heat never propagates to the far end.We can obtain a solution either by assuming that the parameter Γ →∞ or by replacing the second boundary condition such that

\(\frac{d^{2}\hat{T} }{d\hat{Z} ^{2}}=\frac{Bi}{\hat{R}^{2} }(\hat{T} )\) (E5.2)

\(\hat{Z}→∞ \), \(\frac{d\hat{T} }{d\hat{Z} } \)=0

The solution becomes simply \(\hat{T} (\hat{Z}) = e^{−\hat{Z}},\) which was the functional form assumed by Biot. Figure E5.4 shows a best fit of this expression to the data, with the parameter value of

\(\Gamma=\frac{1}{2}\)

confirming Biot’s calculations (see Table E5.1).
