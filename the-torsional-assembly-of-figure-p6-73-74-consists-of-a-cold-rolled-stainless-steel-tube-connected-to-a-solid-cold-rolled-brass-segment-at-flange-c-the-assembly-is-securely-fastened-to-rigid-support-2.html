Guest Question.

The torsional assembly of Figure P6.73/74 consists of a cold-rolled stainless steel tube connected to a solid cold-rolled brass segment at flange C. The assembly is securely fastened to rigid supports at A and D. Stainless steel tube (1) and (2) has an outside diameter of 3.50 in., a wall thickness of 0.120 in., and a shear modulus of G = 12,500 ksi. The solid brass segment (3) has a diameter of 2.00 in. and a shear modulus of G = 5,600 ksi. A concentrated torque of \(T_{B}\) = 6 kip-ft is applied to the stainless steel pipe at B. Determine:
(a) the maximum shear stress magnitude in the stainless steel tube.
(b) the maximum shear stress magnitude in brass segment (3).
(c) the rotation angle of flange C.

Admin
Solution

Section Properties: The polar moments of inertia for the stainless steel tube and the solid brass segment are:

\(\begin{aligned}&J_{1}=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[(3.500 \text { in. })^{4}-(3.260 \text { in } .)^{4}\right]=3.643915 \text { in. }^{4}=J_{2} \\&J_{3}=\frac{\pi}{32}(2.0 \text { in. })^{4}=1.570796 \text { in. }^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Equilibrium: Consider a free-body diagram cut around joint B, where the external torque \(T_{B}\) is applied:

\(\Sigma M_{x}=-T_{1}+T_{2}+T_{B}=0\) (a)

and also consider a FBD cut around joint C. Although there is not an external torque applied at joint C, the section properties of the torsion structure change at C.

\(\Sigma M_{x}=-T_{2}+T_{3}=0 \quad \therefore T_{2}=T_{3}\) (b)

Geometry-of-Deformation Relationship: Since the two ends of the torsion structure are securely attached to fixed supports at A and D, the sum of the angles of twist in the three shafts must equal zero:

\(\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}+\phi_{3}=0\) (c)

Torque-Twist Relationships:

\(\phi_{1}=\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}} \quad \phi_{2}=\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}} \quad \phi_{3}=\frac{T_{3} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}\) (d)

Compatibility Equation: Substitute the torque-twist relationships [Eqs. (d)] into the Geometry-ofdeformation relationship [Eq. (c)] to obtain the compatibility equation:

\(\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}+\frac{T_{3} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}=0\) (e)

Solve the Equations: The approach used here will be to reduce the variables in Eq. (e) by replacing \(T_{1}\) and \(T_{3}\) with equivalent expressions involving \(T_{2}\). From Eq. (a):

\(T_{1}=T_{2}+T_{B}\) (f)

Substitute Eqs. (b) and (f) into Eq. (e) and simplify to derive an expression for \(T_{2}\):

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{\left(T_{2}+T_{B}\right) L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}+\frac{T_{2} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}} &=0 \\T_{2}\left[\frac{L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}+\frac{L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}\right] &=-\frac{T_{B} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}} \\T_{2} &=-\frac{\frac{T_{B} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}}{\frac{L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}+\frac{L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}} (e)\end{aligned}\)

Note that \(G_{1}=G_{2} \text { and } J_{1}=J_{2}\). Compute \(T_{2}\):

\(\begin{aligned}T_{2}=&-\frac{\frac{(6 \text { kip }- ft )(42 in .)(12 in. / ft )}{\left(3.643915 in .{ }^{4}\right)(12,500 ksi )}}{\frac{42 in. +30 in .}{\left(3.643915 in .^{4}\right)(12,500 ksi )}+\frac{24 in .}{\left(1.570796 in. ^{4}\right)(5,600 ksi )}} \\=&-15.4070 kip \text {-in. }\end{aligned}\)

Substitute this result into Eq. (f) to compute internal torque \(T_{1}\):

\(T_{1}=T_{2}+T_{B}=-15.4070 \text { kip-in. }+(6 \text { kip }- ft )(12 in . / ft )=56.5930 \text { kip-in. }\)

and from Eq. (b):

\(T_{3}=T_{2}=-15.4070 \text { kip-in. }\)

Maximum Shear Stress: The maximum shear stress magnitudes in the three members are:

\(\begin{aligned}&\tau_{1}=\frac{T_{1} c_{1}}{J_{1}}=\frac{(56.5930 kip – in .)(3.500 in . / 2)}{3.643915 in .^{4}}=27.2 ksi \\&\tau_{2}=\frac{T_{2} c_{2}}{J_{2}}=\frac{(15.4070 kip – in .)(3.500 in . / 2)}{3.643915 in .^{4}}=7.40 ksi \\&\tau_{3}=\frac{T_{3} c_{3}}{J_{3}}=\frac{(15.4070 kip – in .)(2.000 in . / 2)}{1.570796 in. ^{4}}=9.81 ksi\end{aligned}\)

(a) Maximum Shear Stress Magnitude in Stainless Steel Tube:

\(\tau_{1}=27.2 ksi\)

(b) Maximum Shear Stress Magnitude in Brass Shaft:

\(\tau_{3}=9.81 ksi\)

(c) Rotation Angle of Flange C Relative to Support A: The angle of twist in member (1) can be defined by the difference in rotation angles at the two ends:

\(\phi_{1}=\phi_{B}-\phi_{A} \quad \therefore \phi_{B}=\phi_{A}+\phi_{1}\) (f)

Similarly, the angle of twist in member (2) can be defined by:

\(\phi_{2}=\phi_{C}-\phi_{B} \quad \therefore \phi_{C}=\phi_{B}+\phi_{2}\) (g)

To derive an expression for \(\phi_{C}\), substitute Eq. (f) into Eq. (g), and note that joint A is restrained from rotating; therefore, \(\phi_{A}=0\) .

\(\phi_{C}=\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}\)

The angle of twist in member (1) is:

\(\phi_{1}=\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}=\frac{(56.5930 kip – in .)(42 in .)}{\left(3.643915 in .^{4}\right)(12,500 ksi )}=0.052184 rad\)

The angle of twist in member (2) is:

\(\phi_{2}=\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}=\frac{(-15.4070 kip – in .)(30 in .)}{\left(3.643915 in .^{4}\right)(12,500 ksi )}=-0.010148 rad\)

The rotation angle of flange C is thus:

\(\phi_{C}=\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}=0.052184 rad +(-0.010148 rad )=0.042036 rad =0.0420 rad\)
