Guest Question.

(a) Determine the natural frequencies for the three degree-of-freedom system shown in Figure 8.13.
(b) Calculate and graphically illustrate the normalized mode shape vectors.
(c) Demonstrate mode shape orthogonality

Admin
Solution

The differential equations governing the system may be formulated using Newton’s law or Lagrange’s equations (the full set or via influence coefficients as)

\(\begin{bmatrix} m & 0 & 0 \\0 & m & 0 \\0 & 0 & 2m \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} \ddot{x}_{1}\\ \ddot{x}_{2}\\ \ddot{x}_{3} \end{bmatrix} +\begin{bmatrix} 2k & -2k & 0 \\-2k & 3k & -k \\ 0 & -k & k \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x_{1}\\x_{2}\\x_{3} \end{bmatrix} =\begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0\\0 \end{bmatrix} \) (a)

(a) The natural frequencies are the square roots of the eigenvalues of \( \pmb{M^{-1}K}\),

\(\det(\pmb{M^{-1}K}-\lambda \pmb{I})=\pmb{0}\Rightarrow \begin{bmatrix}\frac{1}{m} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \frac{1}{m} & 0 \\0 & 0 & \frac{1}{2m} \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 2k & -2k & 0 \\ -2k & 3k & -k \\ 0 & -k & k \end{bmatrix} – \lambda \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 0\\0 & 1 & 0\\0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \)

\( =\begin{bmatrix} 4\phi -\lambda & -4\phi & 0 \\-4\phi & 6\phi -\lambda & -2\phi \\0 & -\phi & \phi -\lambda \end{bmatrix} \) (b)

where \(\phi =\frac{k}{2m} \). Evaluation of the determinant in Equation (b) leads to

\(-\lambda ^{3}+11\phi \lambda ^{2}-16\phi ^{2}\lambda =0 \) (c)

The smallest toot of Equation (c) is \(\lambda=0\). The system is unrestrained. The other roots are obtained by solving

\(\lambda ^{2}-11\phi \lambda +16\phi ^{2}=0 \) (d)

The solutions of Equation (b) are

\(\lambda = 0,1.725 \phi ,9.275\phi \) (e)

\(\omega _{1}=0 \) \( \omega _{2}=\sqrt{\frac{1.725k}{2m} }=0.928\sqrt{\frac{k}{m} } \) \(\omega _{3}=\sqrt{\frac{9.275k}{2m} } =2.15\sqrt{\frac{k}{m} } \) (f)

(b) The mode shape vectors are determined from

\(\begin{bmatrix} 4\phi -\lambda & -4\phi & 0 \\-4\phi & 6\phi -\lambda & -2\phi \\0 & -\phi &\phi -\lambda \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}X_{1}\\X_{2}\\X_{3}\end{bmatrix} =\begin{bmatrix} 0\\0\\0 \end{bmatrix} \) (g)

The first equation gives

\(X_{1}=\frac{4\phi }{4\phi -\lambda }X_{2} \) (h)

while the third equation gives

\( X_{3}=\frac{\phi }{\phi -\lambda }X_{2} \) (i)

When evaluated for the values of which are the eigenvalues of \(\pmb{M^{-1}K}.\) and keeping \(X_{2}=C \), arbitrary Equations (h) and (i) yield

\(\pmb{X}_{1}=C\begin{bmatrix} 1\\1\\1 \end{bmatrix} \) \(\pmb{X}_{2}=C\begin{bmatrix} 1.758 \\ 1 \\-1.379 \end{bmatrix} \) \(\pmb{X}_{3}=C\begin{bmatrix} -0.758 \\ 1 \\ -0.121 \end{bmatrix} \) (j)

The mode shapes are normalized by requiring \(\pmb{(X_{i,X_{i}})_{M}=X^{T}_{i}MX_{i}} =1\). For example, the normalization of \(\pmb{X_{2}} \) chooses C such that

\(\pmb{X^{T}_{2}MX_{2}}=1\Rightarrow 1=C\begin{bmatrix} 1.758 &1 &-1.3679 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} m &0&0 \\0&m&0\\ 0&0&2m\end{bmatrix} C\begin{bmatrix} 1.758 \\1\\ -1.379 \end{bmatrix} \)

 

\(=C^{2}\begin{bmatrix} 1.758 & 1& -1.3679 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1.75m\\m\\-2.758m \end{bmatrix} \)

\( =C^{2}\begin{bmatrix} 1.758(1.758m)+1(m)-1.479(-2.758m)=C^{2}(8.939m) \end{bmatrix} \) (k)

or \( C=\frac{0.334}{\sqrt{m} } \). Similar calculations are performed yielding the normalized mode-shape vectors as

\(\pmb{X}_{1}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{m} }\begin{bmatrix} 0.5 \\ 0.5 \\ 0.5 \end{bmatrix} \) \( \pmb{X}_{2}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{m} }\begin{bmatrix} 0.587 \\ 0.334 \\-0.461 \end{bmatrix} \) \( \pmb{X}_{3}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{m} }\begin{bmatrix} -.599 \\0.790 \\-0.096\end{bmatrix} \) (l)

The normalized mode shapes are illustrated in Figure 8.12(b). The first mode is a rigidbody mode corresponding to the natural frequency of zero. There is a node for the second mode in the spring connecting the second and third masses. Two nodes mark the third mode. One is in the spring connecting the first two masses; the second is in the spring connecting the second and third mass, but not in the same location as the node for the second mode.
(a) Mode-shape orthogonality implies \(\pmb{(X_{i},X_{j})}_{M}=\pmb{X}^{T}_{j}\pmb{MX}_{i}=0 \) for \( i\neq j\). The demonstration of this relation follows

\(\pmb{(X_{1},X_{2})}_{M}=\pmb{X}^{T}_{2}\pmb{MX}_{1}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{m} }\begin{bmatrix} 0.587&0.334 &-0.461 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} m & 0 & 0 & \\0 & m & 0\\0 & 0 & 2m\end{bmatrix} \frac{1}{\sqrt{m} } \begin{bmatrix}0.5\\0.5\\0.5 \end{bmatrix} \) \(= \frac{1}{m }\begin{bmatrix} 0.587 & 0.334 &-0.461 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0.5\\0.5\\m \end{bmatrix} \)

\( =0.587(0.5)+0.334(0.5)=0.461(1)=0\) (m)

\(\pmb{(X_{2},X_{3})}_{M}=\pmb{X}^{T}_{3}\pmb{MX}_{2}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{m} }\begin{bmatrix} 0.599 & 0.790 & -0.096 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} m & 0 & 0 & \\0 & m & 0\\0 & 0 & 2m \end{bmatrix} \frac{1}{\sqrt{m} } \begin{bmatrix} -0.587\\0.334\\ -0.461\end{bmatrix} \)

 

\( =\frac{1}{m}\begin{bmatrix} -0.599 & 0.790 & 0.096 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0.587m \\ 0.334 m\\0.922m \end{bmatrix} \)

\(=-0599(0.587)+0.790(0.334)-0.096(-0.922)=0\) (n)

\(\pmb{(X_{3},X_{1})}_{M}=\pmb{X}^{T}_{2}\pmb{MX}_{1}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{m} }\begin{bmatrix} 0.5 & 0.5 & -0.5 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} m & 0 & 0 & \\0 & m & 0\\0 & 0 & 2m \end{bmatrix} \frac{1}{\sqrt{m} } \begin{bmatrix} -00.599\\00.790\\-0.096 \end{bmatrix} \)

 

\(=\frac{1}{m}\begin{bmatrix} 0.5 & 0.5 & -0.5 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} -0.599m\\0.790m\\-0.192m \end{bmatrix} \)

\(=0.5(-0.599)+0.5(0.790)+0.5(-0.192)=0 \) (o)
