Guest Question.

A current passing through a resistor is recorded by 10 different observers and the readings obtained are as 100.1 A, 101.7 A, 100.9 A, 102.1 A, 101.5 A, 101.0 A,100.0 A, 102.1 A, 102.3 A, and 101.3 A. Calculate:

  1. arithmetic mean
  2. standard deviation
  3. variance

Let us represent this data in tabular form to avoid any confusion.

Given readings (in amperes)

X

 Deviation from mean,

\(d_{n}=X_{n}-\overline{x}\)

 Square of deviation,

\(d_{n}^{2}\)
100.1 -1.2 1.44
101.7 0.4 0.16
100.9 -0.4 0.16
102.1 0.8 0.64
101.5 0.2 0.04
101.0 -0.3 0.09
100.0 -1.3 1.69
102.1 0.8 0.64
102.3 1.0 1.0
101.3 0 0
\(\sum X_{n}=1013.0\) \(\sum \left | d_{n} \right |=6.4\) \(\sum \left | d_{n}^{2} \right |5.86\)

 

Admin
Solution

a) Arithmetic mean\( \overline{x}\) is obtained as:

\(\overline{x}=\frac{\sum x_{n}}{n}=\frac{1013.0}{10}=101.3 A\)

b) Standard deviation \sigma for a finite number of observations is given by Equation (9)

\(\sigma =\sqrt{\frac{\sum d^{2}_{n}}{n-1}}=\sqrt{\frac{5.86}{6}}=0.806\)

c) Variance \(\sigma^{2} \)is calculated as:

Variance =\(\sigma^{2}=(0.806)^{2}=0.649 A^{2}\)
