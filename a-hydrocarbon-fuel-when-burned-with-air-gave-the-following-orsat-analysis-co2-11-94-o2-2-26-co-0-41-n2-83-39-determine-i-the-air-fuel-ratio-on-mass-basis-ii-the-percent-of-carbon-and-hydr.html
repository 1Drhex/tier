Guest Question.

A hydrocarbon fuel when burned with air gave the following Orsat analysis, \(CO_{2}: 11.94\%, O_{2}: 2.26\%, CO: 0.41\%, N_{2}: 83.39\%\) Determine,
(i) the air-fuel ratio on mass basis
(ii) the percent of carbon and hydrogen in the fuel on mass basis, and
(iii) percentage of theoretical air supplied.
Assume air to have 21% oxygen.

Admin
Solution
\(\begin{aligned}\text{Orsat analysis gives, }CO_{2}&= 11.94\%, O_{2} = 2.26\%, CO = 0.41\%, N_{2} = 83.39\%\\H_{2}O&= 100 – (11.94 + 2.26 + 0.41 + 83.39)\\H_{2}O&= 2\%\end{aligned}\\\text{Let hydrogen fuel be }‘C_{a}H_{b}’.\\C_{a}H_{b} + d . O_{2} + \left\lgroup\frac{79}{21} . d \right\rgroup N_{2}→ e . CO + f . CO_{2} + \frac{b}{2} H_{2}O + g .O_{2} + h . N_{2}\\\text{On mass basis,}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad → 28e + 44f + 9b + 32g + 28h\\\text{Mass of constituents of exhaust gas, CO}_{2} = 44f, CO = 28e, H_{2}O = 9b, O_{2} = 32g, N_{2} = 28h\\\qquad \text{Exhaust gas mass} = (28e + 44f + 9b + 32g + 28h)\\\text{As per analysis,}\\\begin{aligned}44f&= 0.1194 (28e + 44f + 9b + 32g + 28h)\\28e&= 0.0041 (28e + 44f + 9b + 32g + 28h)\\9b&= 0.02 (28e + 44f + 9b + 32g + 28h)\\32g&=0.0226 (28e + 44f + 9b + 32g + 28h)\\28b&=0.8339 (28e + 44f + 9b + 32g + 28h)\\\text{Also}\qquad \qquad &\\12 . a&= 12e + 12f, or, a = e + f\\\frac{79}{21} . d . 28&= 28h\\h&= 3.76 d\end{aligned}\\\text{Solving above we get}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad \qquad a&= 19.53e\\b&= 15.18e\\d&= 54.09e\\f&= 18.53e\\g&= 4.82e\\h&= 203.37e\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Air-fuel ratio}&= \frac{\left\lgroup32 \times d + \frac{79}{21}\times d \times 28 \right\rgroup }{\left(12 \times a + b\right) } = 29.77\\\text{Air-fuel ratio}&= 29.77\\\text{Carbon fraction}&= \frac{12 a}{(12 a + b)} = 0.9392\\\text{Hydrogen fraction}&= \frac{b}{(12 a + b)} = 0.0608\\\%\text{Carbon}&= 93.92\%\\\%\text{Hydrogen}&= 6.08\%\\\text{Percentage theoretical air supplied}&= \frac{\left\lgroup 32 d + \frac{79}{21}\times d\times 28 \right\rgroup \times 100}{\left\lgroup 32 d + \frac{79}{21} d \times 28 + 12 a +b \right\rgroup } \\&= 96.75\\\%\text{Theoretical air supplied}&= 96.75\%\end{aligned}\)
