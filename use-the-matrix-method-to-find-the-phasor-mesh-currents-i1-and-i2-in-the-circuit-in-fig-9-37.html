Guest Question.

Use the matrix method to find the phasor mesh currents \(I_{1}\) and \( I_{2}\) in the circuit in Fig.9.37.

Admin
Solution

Summing the voltages around mesh \(1\) generates the equation

\(\left(1+j2\right) I_{1} +\left(12-j16\right) \left(I_{1}-I_{2}\right) =150\angle 0°.\) (A.79)

Summing the voltages around mesh \(2\) produces the equation

\(\left(12-j16\right) \left(I_{1}-I_{2}\right) +\left(1+j3\right)-I_{2}+39I_{x}=0.\) (A.80)

The current controlling the dependent voltage source is

\(I_{x}=\left(I_{1}-I_{2}\right).\) (A.81)

After substituting Eq. A.81 into Eq. A.80, the equations are put into a matrix format by first collecting, in each equation, the coefficients of \(I_{1}\) and \(I_{2}\); thus

\(\left(13-j14\right)I_{1}-\left(12-j16\right)I_{2}=150\angle 0°,\) (A.82)

 

\(\left(27+j16\right)I_{1}-\left(26+j13\right)I_{2}=0.\)

Now, using matrix notation, Eq.A.82 is written

\(AI=V,\) (A.83)

where

\(A=\left[\begin{matrix} 13-j14 & -\left(12-j16\right) \\ 27+j16 &- \left(26+j13\right)\end{matrix} \right] ,\)

\(I=\left[\begin{matrix}I_{1}\\ I_{2}\end{matrix} \right] ,\) and \(V=\left[\begin{matrix}150\angle 0°\\ 0\end{matrix} \right] .\)

It follows from Eq.A.83 that

\(I=A^{-1} V.\) (A.84)

The inverse of the coefficient matrix \(A\) is found using Eq.A.73. In this case, the cofactors of \(A\) are

\(\Delta _{11}= \left(-1\right)^{2} \left(-26-j13\right)=-26-j13,\)

 

\(\Delta _{12}= \left(-1\right)^{3} \left(27+j16\right)=-27-j16,\)

 

\(\Delta _{21}= \left(-1\right)^{3} \left(-12+j16\right)=12-j16,\)

 

\(\Delta _{22}= \left(-1\right)^{4} \left(13-j14\right)=13-j14.\)

The cofactor matrix \(B\) is

\(B=\left[\begin{matrix} \left(-26-j13\right) & \left(-27-j16\right)\\\left(12-j16\right)&\left(13-j14\right)\end{matrix} \right] .\) (A.85)

The adjoint of \(A\) is

\(adj A=B^{T}=\left[\begin{matrix} \left(-26-j13\right) &\left(12-j16\right)\\ \left(-27-j16\right)&\left(13-j14\right)\end{matrix} \right] .\) (A.86)

The determinant of \(A\) is

\(det A=\left|\begin{matrix}\left(13-j14\right)& -\left(12-j16\right)\\ \left(27+j16\right) & -\left(26+j13\right) \end{matrix} \right|\)

 

\(=-\left(13-j14\right)\left(26+j13\right)+\left(12-j16\right)\left(27+j16\right)\)

 

\(=60-j45.\) (A.87)

The inverse of the coefficient matrix is

\(A^{-1}=\frac{\left[\begin{matrix} \left(-26-j13\right) &\left(12-j16\right)\\ \left(-27-j16\right)&\left(13-j14\right)\end{matrix} \right] }{\left(60-j45\right)} .\) (A.88)

Equation A.88 can be simplified to

\(A^{-1}=\frac{60+j45}{5625}\left[\begin{matrix} \left(-26-j13\right) &\left(12-j16\right)\\ \left(-27-j16\right)&\left(13-j14\right)\end{matrix} \right] \)

 

\(=\frac{1}{375} \left[\begin{matrix} -65-j130 &96-j28\\ -60-j145&94-j17\end{matrix} \right] .\) (A.89)

Substituting Eq.A.89 into A.84 gives us

\(\left[\begin{matrix} I_{1} \\ I_{2}\end{matrix} \right]=\frac{1}{375}\left[\begin{matrix}\left(-65-j130 \right) &\left(96-j28 \right) \\ \left( -60-j145 \right)&\left( 94-j17 \right)\end{matrix} \right]\left[\begin{matrix}150\angle 0°\\0 \end{matrix} \right] \)

 

\(=\left[\begin{matrix}\left(-26-j52\right)\\ \left(-24-j58\right) \end{matrix} \right] .\) (A.90)

It follows from Eq.A.90 that

\(I_{1}=\left(-26-j52\right)=58.14 \angle -116.57° A,\)

 

\(I_{2}=\left(-24-j58\right)=62.77 \angle -122.48° A.\) (A.91)
