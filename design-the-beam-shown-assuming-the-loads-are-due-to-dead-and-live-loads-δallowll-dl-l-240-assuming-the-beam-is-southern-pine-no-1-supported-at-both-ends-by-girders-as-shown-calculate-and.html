Guest Question.

Design the beam shown assuming the loads are due to dead and live loads.

\(\Delta_{allow_{(LL + DL)}} = L/240\)

Assuming the beam is Southern Pine No. 1 supported at both ends by girders as shown, calculate and check the bearing stress developed between the 6 ×_ beam and a 6 × 12 girder.

Fb = 1,550 psi; Fv = 110 psi;

E = 1.6 × 106 psi; Fc⊥ = 410 psi

Admin
Solution

Vmax = 2000lb.

 

\(A_{req'd} = \frac{1.5V}{F_{v}} = \frac{1.5(2000lb.)}{110lb./in.^{2}} = 27.3in.^{2}\)

 

Mmax = 12,000lb. – ft.

 

\(S_{req'd} = \frac{M}{F_{b}} = \frac{(12,000lb.-ft.)(12in./ft.)}{1550lb./in.^{2}} = 92.9in.^{3}\)

 

Try : 4 × 14 S4S
(A = 46.4 in.2, S = 102.4 in.3, Ix = 678.5 in.4, ω = 12 lb./ft.)

 

\(M_{add.} = \frac{\omega _{bm}L^{2}}{8} = \frac{(12lb./ft.)(16ft.)^{2}}{8} = 384lb.-ft.\)

 

\(S_{add.} = \frac{M_{add.}}{F_{b}} = \frac{(384lb.-ft.)(12in./ft.)}{1550lb./in.^{2}} = 3in.^{3}\)

 

\(S_{total} = S_{req'd} + S_{add.} = 92.9in.^{3} + 3in.^{3}\)

 

\(S_{total} = 95.9in.^{3} \lt 102.4in.^{3}\) ∴ OK

 

\(\Delta_{allow} = \frac{L}{240} = \frac{(16^{'})(12in./ft.)}{240} = 0.8^{"}\)

 

\(\Delta_{actual} = \frac{PL^{3}}{20.1EI} + \frac{PL^{3}}{48EI} + \frac{5\omega L^{4}}{384EI}\)

 

\(\Delta_{act} = \frac{(1k)(16^{'})^{3}(1728)}{20.1(1.6\times 10^{3})(678.5)} + \frac{(1k)(16^{'})^{3}(1728)}{48(1.6\times 10^{3})(678.5)} + \frac{5(.012)(16^{'})^{4}(1728)}{384(1.6\times 10^{3})(678.5)}\)

 

Δact = 0.324″ + 0.136″ + 0.016″ = 0.48″ < 0.80″ ∴ OK

Check the bearing stress.

 

\(f_{p} = \frac{P}{A_{brg}} = \frac{\left\lgroup2000lb. + \underset{(bm.wt)}{96lb.} \right\rgroup }{(5.5^{"}\times 3.5^{"})} = 109 psi \lt F_{c\bot} = 410 psi\)

 

∴ OK Use: 4 × 14 S4S
