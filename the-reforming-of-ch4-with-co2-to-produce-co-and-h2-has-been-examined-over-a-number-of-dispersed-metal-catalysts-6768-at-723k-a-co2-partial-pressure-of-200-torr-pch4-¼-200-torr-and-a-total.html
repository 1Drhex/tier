Guest Question.

The reforming of \(CH_{4}\) with \(CO_{2}\) to produce CO and \(H_{2}\) has been examined over a number of dispersed metal catalysts [67,68]. At 723K, a \(CO_{2}\) partial pressure of 200 Torr, \(P_{CH4}\)= 200 Torr, and a total pressure of 1 atm (1 atm = 760 Torr), the catalysts listed below produced the rates listed for \(CO_{2}\) consumption. The catalysts used were sieved to a 120/70 mesh fraction, thus the largest particles had a diameter of 0.020 cm. Assume a catalyst density of 1 g \(cm^{-3}\) , which provides a conservative overestimate. The pore diameter, \(\delta \),was obtained from pore size distribution measurements. Determine the W-P number for: a) \(Ni/SiO_{2}\). b) \(Pt/ZrO_{2}\), c) \(Rh/TiO_{2}\), d) \(Ru/Al_{2} O_{3}\). Should one be concerned about pore diffusion limitations with any of these catalysts?Why?

Admin
Solution

\(N_{W-P}=\Re R^{2}_{p} / C_{s}D_{eff} \cong r_{CO_2} \rho_{cat} R^{2}_{p}/\left(C_{s}D_{Kn}\right)\) because \(D_{Kn}\lt D_{eff}\)

1)\(C_{CO_2}= \frac{n}{V}=\frac{P}{RT}= \frac{\left(200 \ Torr\right)\left\lgroup\frac{1 \ atm}{760Torr} \right\rgroup }{\left\lgroup82.06 \frac{atm \cdot cm^{3} }{g \ mole \ K} \right\rgroup \left(723 \ K\right) }= 4.4 \times 10^{-6} \frac{mole \ CO_{2} }{cm^{3} }\)

2)\(\bar{v}= \left\lgroup\frac{8 \ k_{B}T }{\pi \ m} \right\rgroup ^{1/2} = \left[\frac{8\left(1.38 \times 10^{-6} erg/K\right)\left(723 \ K\right) }{\pi \left(44 \ amu \right)\left(1.66 \times 10^{-24} \ g/amu \right) } \right] ^{1/2}= 5.9\times 10^{4} cm/s\) (Eq.4.76)

a)

\(D_{Kn}= 1/3 \bar{v} d_{p}= 1/3 \left(5.9 \times 10^{4} cm/s \right) \left(18 \ nm\right)\left(1 \ cm /10^{7}nm\right)= 3.5\times 10^{-2} cm^{2}/s\) (Eq.4.78)

\(N_{W-P}=\frac{\left(42.6 \times 10^{-6} mole/s\cdot g \right)\left(1 \ g/cm^{3}\right)\left (0.01 \ cm\right) ^{2}}{\left(4.4 \times 10^{-6} mole/cm^{3}\right)\left(3.5 \times 10^{-2}cm^{2}/s \right)} = \underline{\underline{0.028}} \) (No problem)

b)

\(D_{Kn}= 1/3 \left(5.9 \times 10^{4} cm/s \right ) \left(14 \ nm\right)\left(1 \ cm /10^{7}nm\right)= 2.8\times 10^{-2} cm^{2}/s\)

\(N_{W-P}=\frac{\left(9.6 \times 10^{-6} mole/s \cdot g \right)\left(1 \ g/cm^{3}\right)\left(0.01 \ cm\right) ^{2}}{\left(4.4 \times 10^{-6} mole/cm^{3} \right)\left(2.8 \times 10^{-2}cm^{2}/s \right)} = \underline{\underline{7.8 \times 10^{-3}}} \) (No problem)

c)

\(D_{Kn}= 1/3 \left(5.9 \times 10^{4} cm/s \right ) \left(20 \ nm\right)\left(1 \ cm /10^{7} nm \right)= 3.9\times 10^{-2} cm^{2}/s\)

\(N_{W-P}=\frac{\left(118 \times 10^{-6} mole/s \cdot g \right)\left(1 \ g/cm^{3}\right)\left(0.01 \ cm \right) ^{2}}{\left(4.4 \times 10^{-6} mole/cm^{3} \right)\left(3.9 \times 10^{-2}cm^{2}/s \right)} = \underline{\underline{6.9 \times 10^{-3}}}\) (No problem)

d)

\(D_{Kn}= 1/3 \left(5.9 \times 10^{4} cm/s \right) \left(20 \ nm\right)\left(1 \ cm /10^{7} nm \right)= 3.9 \times 10^{-2} cm^{2}/s\)

\(N_{W-P}=\frac{\left(237 \times 10^{-6} mole/s\cdot g \right)\left(1 \ g/cm^{3}\right)\left (0.01 \ cm\right) ^{2}}{\left(4.4 \times 10^{-6} mole/cm^{3}\right)\left(3.9 \times 10^{-2}cm^{2}/s \right)} = \underline{\underline{01.4}} \) (Possible concern)
