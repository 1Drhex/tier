Guest Question.

The distributed mass system shown in Fig. 15.12 has been tested for unbalance by rotating the rotor at an angular velocity of 100 rad/s. The bearing reactions on the frame at two bearings A and B are FA = 40 lb and FB = 35 lb, respectively. Determine the magnitudes and orientations of the correcting masses to be removed in the specified planes 1 and 2 to achieve dynamic balance. The correcting masses are to be located at radii r1 = r2 = r = 4 in.

Admin
Solution

The x and y components of the forces acting on frame at bearings A and B are

\(F_{A_{21}}^{x}=40 \cos 240^{\circ} lb =-20 lb\) (1a)

 

\(F_{A_{21}}^{y}=40 \sin 240^{\circ} lb =-20 \sqrt{3} lb\) (1b)

 

\(F_{B_{21}}^{x}=35 \cos \left(-45^{\circ}\right) lb =17.5 \sqrt{2} lb\) (1c)

 

\(F_{B_{21}}^{y}=35 \sin \left(-45^{\circ}\right) lb =-17.5 \sqrt{2} lb\) (1d)

Equations (15.8) and (15.9) can be written as

\(F_{A_{21}}^{x}+F_{B_{21}}^{x}+\omega^{2} \sum\limits_{j=1}^{2} m_{c j} r_{c j} \cos \phi_{c j}=0\) (15.8a)

 

\(F_{A_{21}}^{y}+F_{B_{21}}^{y}+\omega^{2} \sum\limits_{j=1}^{2} m_{c j} r_{c j} \sin \phi_{c j}=0\) (15.8b)

 

\(z_{A} F_{A_{21}}^{x}+z_{B} F_{B_{21}}^{x}+\omega^{2} \sum\limits_{j=1}^{2} z_{j} m_{c j} r_{c j} \cos \phi_{c j}=0\) (15.9a)

 

\(z_{A} F_{A_{21}}^{y}+z_{B} F_{B_{21}}^{y}+\omega^{2} \sum\limits_{j=1}^{2} z_{j} m_{c j} r_{c j} \sin \phi_{c j}=0\) (15.9b)

 

\(m_{1} r \omega^{2} \cos \phi_{1}+m_{2} r \omega^{2} \cos \phi_{2}=-F_{A_{21}}^{x}-F_{B_{21}}^{x}\) (2a)

 

\(m_{1} r \omega^{2} \sin \phi_{1}+m_{2} r \omega^{2} \sin \phi_{2}=-F_{A_{21}}^{y}-F_{B_{21}}^{y}\) (2b)

 

\(z_{1} m_{1} r \omega^{2} \cos \phi_{1}+z_{2} m_{2} r \omega^{2} \cos \phi_{2}=-z_{A} F_{A_{21}}^{x}-z_{B} F_{B_{21}}^{x}\) (3a)

 

\(z_{1} m_{1} r \omega^{2} \sin \phi_{1}+z_{2} m_{2} r \omega^{2} \sin \phi_{2}=-z_{A} F_{A_{21}}^{y}-z_{B} F_{B_{21}}^{y}\) (3b)

Using the direct method, we take moments about one of the two balancing planes. Here we take moments about plane 2 and note that the distances are

\(z_{1}=+40 \text { in, } z_{2}=0, z_{A}=+45 \text { in, and } z_{B}=-5 \text { in }\) (4)

Substituting Eqs. (4) and the known data into Eqs. (2) and (3) gives

\((4 \text { in })(100 rad / s )^{2}\left(m_{1} \cos \phi_{1}+m_{2} \cos \phi_{2}\right)=-4.749 lb\) (5a)

 

\((4 \text { in })(100 rad / s )^{2}\left(m_{1} \sin \phi_{1}+m_{2} \sin \phi_{2}\right)=59.390 lb\) (5b)

 

\((40 in )(4 in )(100 rad / s )^{2} m_{1} \cos \phi_{1}=1023.744 in \cdot lb\) (6a)

 

\((40 in )(4 in )(100 rad / s )^{2} m_{1} \sin \phi_{1}=1435.102 in \cdot lb\) (6b)

Dividing Eq. (6b) by Eq. (6a), the location of the first correcting mass is

\(\phi_{1}=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{1435.102 in \cdot lb }{1023.744 in \cdot lb }\right)=54.5^{\circ}\) (7a)

Substituting Eq. (7a) into Eq. (6a) or (6b), the first correcting mass is

\(m_{1}=\frac{(1435.102 in \cdot lb )\left(386 in / s ^{2}\right)}{\left(1600000 in ^{2} / s ^{2}\right) \sin 54.5^{\circ}}=0.425 lb\) (7b)

Substituting Eqs. (7) into Eqs. (5), rearranging, and dividing Eq. (5b) by Eq. (5a), the location of the second correcting mass is

\(\phi_{2}=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{23.535 lb }{-30.324 lb }\right)=142.2^{\circ}\) (8a)

Substituting Eq. (8a) into Eq. (5a) or (5b), the second correcting mass is

\(m_{2}=\frac{(-30.342 lb )\left(386 in / s ^{2}\right)}{\left(40000 in / s ^{2}\right) \cos 142.2^{\circ}}=0.370 lb\) (8b)

Note that the answers given by Eq. (7a) and (8a) are for adding mass to the system. To balance the rotating system by removing mass, we use:

\(\phi_{\text {remove }}=\phi_{\text {add }} \pm 180^{\circ}\)

Therefore, using this equation the mass to be removed from plane 1 is

\(m_{1}=0.425 lb \text { at the angle } 54.5^{\circ}+180^{\circ}=234.5^{\circ}=-125.5^{\circ} \text {. }\)

Also, the mass to be removed from plane 2 is

\(m_{2}=0.370 lb \text { at the angle } 142.2^{\circ}+180^{\circ}=322.2^{\circ}=-37.8^{\circ} .\)
