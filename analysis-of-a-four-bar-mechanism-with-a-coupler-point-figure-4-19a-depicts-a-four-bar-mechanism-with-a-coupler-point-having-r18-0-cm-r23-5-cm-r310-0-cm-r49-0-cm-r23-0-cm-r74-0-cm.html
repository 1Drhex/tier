ANALYSIS OF A FOUR-BAR MECHANISM WITH A COUPLER POINT

Figure 4.19(a) depicts a four-bar mechanism with a coupler point having

r\(_1\)=8.0 cm; r\(_2\)=3.5 cm; r\(_3\)=10.0 cm;

r\(_4\)=9.0 cm; r\(_2\)=3.0 cm; r\(_7\)=4.0 cm;

θ\(_2\)=15°; \(\dot θ_2\)=36.7 rad/sec(constant)

Determine the displacement, velocity, and acceleration of coupler point C with respect to the given coordinate system.

Admin
Solution

Some of the required quantities were determined in . Using Equations (4.4-44) \([r_8=r_2\cos\theta_2+r_6\cos\theta_2-r_7\sin\theta_3]\) and (4.4-46)\([x_C=r_8 ,y_C=r_9]\) , we obtain

\(x_C=r_2\cosθ_2+r_6\cosθ_3-r_7\sinθ_3=(3.5×\cos15° +3.0×\cos35°-4.0×\sin53°)\)cm=1.99 cm

Similarly,

y\(_C\)=5.71 cm; \(\dot x_C\)=86.6 cm/sec; \(\dot y_C\)=159 cm/sec
\(\ddot x_C\)=-8050 cm/sec²; \(\ddot y_C\)=-5470 cm/sec²

From Equations (4.4-19), (4.4-22)\([\overline v_C=\dot x_C\overline i+\dot y_C \overline j]\), and (4.4-25)\([\overline a_C=\ddot x_C\overline i+\ddot y_C \overline j]\) we have

\(\overline r_C=x_C\overline i+y_C \overline j=(1.99 \overline i+5.71\overline j)\)cm=6.05 °

\(\overline v_C=(86.6 \overline i+159\overline j\))cm/sec=181 cm/°

\(\overline a_C=(-8050\overline i-5470\overline j)\) cm/sec²=9730 cm/sec² @214°

Figure 4.19(b) shows plots of functions of components of displacement, velocity, and acceleration of the coupler point throughout one cycle of motion.
