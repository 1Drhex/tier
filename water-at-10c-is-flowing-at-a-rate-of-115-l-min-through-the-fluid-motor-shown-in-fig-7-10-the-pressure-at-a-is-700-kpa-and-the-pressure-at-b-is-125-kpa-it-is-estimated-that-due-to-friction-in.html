Guest Question.

Water at 10°C is flowing at a rate of 115 L/min through the fluid motor shown in Fig. 7.10. The pressure at A is 700 kPa and the pressure at B is 125 kPa. It is estimated that due to friction in the tubing there is an energy loss of 4.0 N·m / N of water flowing. At A the tubing entering the fluid motor is a standard steel hydraulic tube having an OD = 25 mm and a wall thickness of 2.0 mm. At B, the tube leaving the motor has OD = 80 mm and wall thickness of 2.8 mm. See Appendix G.2. (a) Calculate the power delivered to the fluid motor by the water. (b) If the mechanical efficiency of the fluid motor is 85 percent, calculate the power output.

Start the solution by writing the energy equation.

Admin
Solution

Choosing points A and B as our reference points, we get

\( \frac{p_A}{\gamma}+z_A+\frac{v^2_A}{2g}-h_R-h_L= \frac{p_B}{\gamma}+z_B+\frac{v^2_B}{2g} \)

The value of \( h_R \) is needed to determine the power output. Solve the energy equation for this term.

Compare this equation with your result:

\( h_R = \frac{p_A – p_B}{\gamma}+\left(z_A – z_B\right) + \frac{v^2_A – v^2_B}{2g} -h_L \) (7-10)

Before looking at the next panel, solve for the value of each term in this equation using the unit of N · m/N or m.

The correct results are as follows:

1. \( \frac{p_A – p_B}{\gamma}=\frac{\left(700-125\right) \left(10^3\right)N }{m^2} \times \frac{m^3}{9.81 \times 10^3 N} =58.6m \)

2. \( z_A – z_B = 1.8m \)

3. Solving for \( \left(v^2_A -v^2_B \right)/2g \), we obtain

\(Q = 115 L/min \times \frac{1.0 m^3/s}{60000 L/min}= 1.92 \times 10^{-3} m^3/s \)

\( v_A = \frac{Q}{A_A} =\frac{1.92 \times 10^{-3}m^3}{s} \times \frac{1}{3.464 \times 10^{-4}m^2}=5.543m/s \)

\( v_A = \frac{Q}{A_B} =\frac{1.92 \times 10^{-3}m^3}{s} \times \frac{1}{4.347 \times 10^{-3}m^2}=0.442m/s \)

\( \frac{v^2_A – v^2_B}{2g}= \frac{\left(5.543\right)^2 – \left(0.442\right)^2 }{\left(2\right)\left(9.81\right) } \frac{m^2}{s^2} \frac{s^2}{m}=1.56m \)

4. \( h_L = 4.0 m \) (given)

Complete the solution of Eq. (7–10) for \( h_R \) now.

The energy delivered by the water to the turbine is

\( h_R \) = (58.6 + 1.8 + 1.56 – 4.0) m = 57.96 m

To complete part (a) of the problem, calculate \( P_R \).

Substituting the known values into Eq. (7–8), we get

\( P_R = h_R \gamma Q \)

\( P_R = 57.96 \times \frac{9.81 \times 10^3 N}{m^3} \times \frac{1.92 \times 10^{-3}m^3}{s} =1092W\)

\( P_R = 1.092kW \)

This is the power delivered to the fluid motor by the water. How much useful power can the motor put out?

Because the efficiency of the motor is 85 percent, we get 0.928 kW of power out. Using Eq. (7–09),

\( e_M = P_0/P_R \), we get

\(e_M = \frac{power output from motor}{power delivered by fluid} =\frac{P_0}{P_R} \) (7–09)

 

\(P_{0}=e_{M}P_{R}\)

 

\( = \left(0.85\right) \left(1.092kW\right) \)

\( P_0 = 0.928 kW \)

This completes the programmed example problem.
