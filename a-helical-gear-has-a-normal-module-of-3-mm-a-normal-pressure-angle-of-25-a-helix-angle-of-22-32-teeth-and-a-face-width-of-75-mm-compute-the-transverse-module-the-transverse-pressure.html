Guest Question.

A helical gear has a normal module of 3 mm, a normal pressure angle of 25°, a helix angle of 22°, 32 teeth, and a face width of 75 mm. Compute the transverse module, the transverse pressure angle, and the pitch diameter. Then, if the gear is rotating at 650 rpm while transmitting 5.0 kW of power, compute the pitch line speed, the tangential force, the axial force, and the radial force.

Admin
Solution

Transverse Module:
\(\begin{aligned}m_{n} &=m \cos \psi \\m &=m_{n} / \cos \psi=3.00 \mathrm{~mm} / \cos 22^{\circ}=3.236 \mathrm{~mm}\end{aligned}\)
Transverse Pressure Angle: [Equation (10-1)]
\(\phi_{\mathrm{t}}=\tan ^{-1}\left(\tan \phi_{\mathrm{n}} / \cos \psi\right)=\tan ^{-1}\left[\tan \left(25^{\circ}\right) / \cos \left(22^{\circ}\right)\right]=23.38^{\circ}\)
Pitch Diameter: \(D=m N=(3.236 \mathrm{~mm})(32)=103.54 \mathrm{~mm}\)
Pitch Line Speed: [Equation (10-5M)]
\(v_{t}=\pi D n /(60000)=\pi(103.54 \mathrm{~mm})(650 \mathrm{rpm}) /(60000)=3.524 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\)
Tangential Force: [Equation (10-6M)]
\(W_{t}=(1000)(P) / v_{t}=(1000)(5.0 \mathrm{~kW}) /(3.524 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s})=1419 \mathrm{~N}\)
Axial Force: [Equation (10-9)]
\(W_{x}=W_{t} \tan \psi=(1419 \mathrm{~N}) \tan \left(22^{\circ}\right)=573.3 \mathrm{~N}\)
Radial Force: [Equation (10-8)]
\(W_{r}=W_{t} \tan \phi_{t}=(1419 \mathrm{~N}) \tan \left(23.38^{\circ}\right)=613.5 \mathrm{~N}\)
