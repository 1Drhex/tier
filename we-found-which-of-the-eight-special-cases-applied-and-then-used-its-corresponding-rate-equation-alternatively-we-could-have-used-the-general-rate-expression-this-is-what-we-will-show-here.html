Guest Question.

In Example 24.2 we found which of the eight special cases (see Fig. 23.3) applied and then used its corresponding rate equation (it was Eq. 23.16). Alternatively we could have used the general rate expression (Eq. 23.5). This is what we will show here.

Eq(23.5): \(-{{r}''}''_{A}=\frac{1}{\frac{1}{k_{Ag}a}+\frac{H_{A}}{k_{Al}Ae}+\frac{H_{A}}{kC_{B}f_{l}}}P_{A}\)

Admin
Solution

a material balance gives the tower end conditions, as shown in Fig. E24.5. Now the rate of reaction at any point in the tower is, from Eq. 23.5

\(-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{m \prime}=\left(\frac{1}{\frac{1}{0.32}+\frac{12.5}{0.1 E}+\frac{12.5}{\infty\left(C_{\mathrm{B}}\right)}}\right) p_{\mathrm{A}}=\left(\frac{1}{3.125+\frac{125}{E}}\right) p_{\mathrm{A}}\) (i)

Evaluate \(E\) at various points in the tower. For this we need to first evaluate \(M_{H}\) and \(E_{i}\)

At the Top of the Tower. From Fig. 23.4

\(M_{H}=\frac{\sqrt{\mathscr{D}_{\mathrm{B}} C_{\mathrm{B}} k}}{k_{\mathrm{A} l}}=\infty\)

because \(k=\infty\)

\(E_{i}=1+\frac{\mathscr{D}_{\mathrm{B}} C_{\mathrm{B}} H_{\mathrm{A}}}{\mathscr{D}_{\mathrm{A}} p_{\mathrm{A} i}}=1+\frac{800(12.5)}{p_{\mathrm{A} i}}=\frac{10^{4}}{p_{\mathrm{A} i}}\) (ii)

We have to guess the value of \(p_{\mathrm{Ai}} .\) It can be anywhere between \(0 \mathrm{~Pa}\) (gas film controls) up to 20 Pa (liquid film controls). Let us guess no gas-phase resistance. Then \(p_{\mathrm{A} i}=p_{\mathrm{A}},\) in which case

\(E_{i}=\frac{10^{4}}{20}=500\)

and from Fig. \(23.4,\) for \(M_{H}=\infty, E_{i}=500,\) we see that\(E=500\)

Replacing in Eq. (i) we find

\(-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime \prime \prime \prime}=\frac{1}{3.125+\frac{125}{500}} p_{\mathrm{A}}=\frac{1}{3.125+0.25} p_{\mathrm{A}}=0.296 p_{\mathrm{A}}\)

Our guess was wrong, so let’s try again. Let us guess the other extreme, \(p_{\mathrm{A} i}=\) \(0,\) meaning that the total resistance is in the gas film. Then from Eq.

(ii) we see that \(E_{i}=\infty, E=\infty\) and the rate equation becomes

\(-r_{\mathrm{A}}^{\prime \prime \prime \prime}=\frac{1}{3.125+0} p_{\mathrm{A}}=0.32 p_{\mathrm{A}}\) (iii)

Thus our guess was correct.

At the Bottom of the Tower. We follow the same procedure and find the same result. Thus the rate at all points in the tower is given by Eq. (iii). The height of the tower, from Eq. 10

\(\underline{h}=5.03 \mathrm{~m}\)

Suggestion. Whenever \(M_{H}>E_{i}\) we end up having to guess \(p_{\mathrm{A} i},\) and that is tedious. In those cases try to use the special case expressions.

In other cases (and this is what we usually find) the general rate equation is easier to use.
