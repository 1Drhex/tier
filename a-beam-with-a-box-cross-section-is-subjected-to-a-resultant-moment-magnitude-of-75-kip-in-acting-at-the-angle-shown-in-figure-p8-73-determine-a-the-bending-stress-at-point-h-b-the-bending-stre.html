Guest Question.

A beam with a box cross section is subjected to a resultant moment magnitude of 75 kip-in. acting at the angle shown in Figure P8.73. Determine:
(a) the bending stress at point H.
(b) the bending stress at point K.
(c) the maximum tension and the maximum compression bending stresses in the beam.
(d) the orientation of the neutral axis relative to the +z axis. Show its location on a sketch of the cross section.

Admin
Solution

Section properties

\(\begin{aligned}&I_{y}=\frac{(4 \text { in. })(6 in .)^{3}}{12}-\frac{(3.25 in .)(5.25 in .)^{3}}{12}=32.8096 in .{ }^{4} \\&I_{z}=\frac{(6 in .)(4 in .)^{3}}{12}-\frac{(5.25 in .)(3.25 in .)^{3}}{12}=16.9814 in .{ }^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Moment components

\(\begin{aligned}M_{y} &=(75 \text { kip-in. }) \cos 20^{\circ}=70.4769 \text { kip-in } \\M_{z} &=(75 \text { kip-in. }) \sin 20^{\circ}=25.6515 kip \text {-in. }\end{aligned}\)

(a) Bending stress at H
For a shape having at least one axis of symmetry, Eq. (8-24) can be used to determine bending stresses.
To compute the normal stress at H, use the (y, z) coordinates y = −2.0 in. and z = −3.0 in.:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\frac{M_{y} z}{I_{y}}-\frac{M_{z} y}{I_{z}} \\&=\frac{(70.4769 \text { kip-in. })(-3.0 \text { in. })}{32.8096 \text { in. }^{4}}-\frac{(25.6515 \text { kip-in. })(-2.0 \text { in. })}{16.9814 \text { in } .^{4}} \\&=-6.4442 ksi +3.0211 ksi \\&=-3.4231 ksi =3.42 ksi ( C )\end{aligned}\)

(b) Bending stress at K
To compute the normal stress at K, use the (y, z) coordinates y = 2.0 in. and z = 3.0 in.:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\frac{M_{y} z}{I_{y}}-\frac{M_{z} y}{I_{z}} \\&=\frac{(70.4769 \text { kip-in. })(3.0 \text { in. })}{32.8096 \text { in. }^{4}}-\frac{(25.6515 \text { kip-in. })(2.0 in .)}{16.9814 \text { in. }{ }^{4}} \\&=6.4442 ksi -3.0211 ksi \\&=3.4231 ksi =3.42 ksi ( T )\end{aligned}\)

(c) Maximum bending stresses
The maximum tension normal stress occurs at the (y, z) coordinates y = −2.0 in. and z = 3.0 in.:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\frac{M_{y} z}{I_{y}}-\frac{M_{z} y}{I_{z}} \\&=\frac{(70.4769 \text { kip-in. })(3.0 \text { in. })}{32.8096 \text { in. }^{4}}-\frac{(25.6515 \text { kip-in. })(-2.0 in .)}{16.9814 in .{ }^{4}} \\&=6.4442 ksi +3.0211 ksi \\&=9.4653 ksi =9.47 ksi ( T )\end{aligned}\)

The maximum compression normal stress occurs at the (y, z) coordinates y = 2.0 in. and z = −3.0 in.:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\frac{M_{y} z}{I_{y}}-\frac{M_{z} y}{I_{z}} \\&=\frac{(70.4769 kip – in .)(-3.0 in .)}{32.8096 in .{ }^{4}}-\frac{(25.6515 kip – in .)(2.0 in .)}{16.9814 in. ^{4}} \\&=-6.4442 ksi -3.0211 ksi \\&=-9.4653 ksi =9.47 ksi ( C )\end{aligned}\)

(d) Orientation of neutral axis
For a shape having at least one axis of symmetry, Eq. (8-25) can be used to determine the orientation of the neutral axis:

\(\begin{aligned}\tan \beta &=\frac{M_{y} I_{z}}{M_{z} I_{y}}=\frac{(70.4769 kip – in. )\left(16.9814 in .^{4}\right)}{(25.6515 kip – in .)\left(32.8096 in .^{4}\right)}=1.4220 \\&\left.\therefore \beta=54.88^{\circ} \quad \text { (i.e., } 54.88^{\circ} CW \text { from }+z \text { axis }\right)\end{aligned}\)
