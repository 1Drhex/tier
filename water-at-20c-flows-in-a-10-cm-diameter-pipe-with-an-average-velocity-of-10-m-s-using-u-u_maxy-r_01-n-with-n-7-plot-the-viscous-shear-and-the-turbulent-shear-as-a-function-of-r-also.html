Guest Question.

Water at 20°C flows in a 10-cm-diameter pipe with an average velocity of 10 m/s. Using \(u = u_{max}(y/r_0)^{1/n}\) with n = 7, plot the viscous shear and the turbulent shear as a function of r. Also, find \(d\overline{p}\) /dx.

Admin
Solution

With n = 7, Eq. 7.6.21 gives \(f =\frac{1}{n^2}=\frac{1}{49}= 0.0204\) .

\(n=\frac{1}{\sqrt{f} } (7.6.21)\)

∴\(τ_0 =\frac{1}{8} ρV^2 f =\frac{1}{2}\times 11000 \times 10^2 \times 0.0204 = 1020 Pa\).

Since τ varies linearly with r and is zero at r = 0,

\(\overline{τ} =τ_0\frac{r}{r_0} =\frac{1020}{0.05}r = 20400 r \).

\(τ_{lam}=\mu \frac{\partial \overline{u} }{\partial y} =10^{-3}\left[u_{max}\frac{1}{7}\frac{1}{r_0^{1/7}}y^{-6/7} \right] =\frac{10^{-3}\times 12.24}{7\times 0.05^{1/7}} y^{-6/7}=0.00268y^{-6/7}\).

\(τ_{turb} =\overline{τ}- τ_{lam}= 20 400r- 0.00268 y^{-6/7} where y+r= 0.05\) .

\(τ_{lam}\)(y) is good away from y = 0 (the wall).

\(τ_{lam} (.00625) = .21, τ_{lam}(.003125) = .38, τ_{lam}(.00156) = .68, τ_{lam} (.00078)=1.24\) .

\(\frac{d\overline{p} }{dx} =-\frac{2τ_0}{r_0} =-\frac{2\times 1020}{0.05}=\underline{-40 800 Pa/m}\) .
