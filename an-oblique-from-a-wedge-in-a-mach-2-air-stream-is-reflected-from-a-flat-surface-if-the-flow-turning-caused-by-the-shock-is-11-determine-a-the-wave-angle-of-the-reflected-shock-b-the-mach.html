Guest Question.

An oblique from a wedge in a Mach 2 air stream is reflected from a flat surface. If the flow turning caused by the shock is \(11^{\circ}\), determine (a) the wave angle of the reflected shock, (b) the Mach number downstream of the reflected shock, and (c) the strength of reflection.

Admin
Solution

(a) For the incident shock, \(M_{1}=2 \text { and } \theta=11^{\circ} \text { . }\) For this Mach number and \(\theta,\) from the oblique shock table (), we get

\(\beta_{1}=40.42^{\circ}, M_{2}=1.6, \frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}=1.795\)

 

For \(M_{2}=1.6 \text { and } \theta=11^{\circ},\) from the oblique shock chart (Figure A.1), we have

\(\beta_{2}=52.88^{\circ}\)

 

The normal component of the Mach number upstream of the reflected shock is

\(M_{2 n}=M_{2} \sin \beta_{2}=1.6 \times \sin 52.88\)

 

\(= 1.276\)

 

For \(M_{2 n}=1.276,\) from the normal shock table (),

\(M_{3 n}=0.808, \frac{p_{3}}{p_{2}}=1.732\)

 

(b) The Mach number downstream of the reflected shock is

\(M_{3}=\frac{M_{3 n}}{\sin \left(\beta_{2}-\theta_{2}\right)}\)

 

\(=\frac{0.808}{\sin (52.88-11)}\)

 

\(= 1.213\)

 

(c) The strength of reflection is

\(\frac{p_{3}}{p_{1}}=\frac{p_{3}}{p_{2}} \frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}\)

 

\(= 1.732 × 1.795\)

 

\(= 3.11\)
