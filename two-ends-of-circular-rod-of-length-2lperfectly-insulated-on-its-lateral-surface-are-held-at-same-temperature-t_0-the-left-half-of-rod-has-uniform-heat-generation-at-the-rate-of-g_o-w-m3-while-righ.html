Guest Question.

Two ends of circular rod of length 2L,perfectly insulated on its lateral surface are held at same temperature \(T_0\). The left half of rod has uniform heat generation at the rate of \(g_o W/m^3\), while right half portion has no heat generation. Thermal conductivity of the rod material is constant (independent of temperature). In steady state conditions (a) Develop the expressions for the temperature distribution in the left and right portion of the end. (b) Find the location of maximum temperature.

Admin
Solution

Given :
Analysis : (a) The governing equation in left half of the rod.
\(\frac{d^{2}T_{L}(x)}{dx^{2}} + \frac{g_o}{k}=0\)
and temperature distribution in left portion of rod
\(T_L(x) = −\frac{g_o x^2}{2k}+ C_1x + C_2 …(i)\)
The boundary condition
At x = 0, \(T_L(x) = T_0,\)
It gives \(C_2 =T_0\)
Hence \(T_L(x) = − g\frac{g_o x^2}{2k}+ C_1x + T_0 …(ii)\)
The governing differential equation in right portion of the rod is reduced to
\(\frac{d^{2}T_{R}(x)}{dx^{2}}=0\)

and temperature distribution,
\(T_R(x) = C_3x + C_4 …(iii)\)
With boundary condition
At x = 2L, \(T_R(x) = T_0\)
It gives \( C_4 =T_0 – 2LC_3\)
Hence \(T_R(x) = C_3(x – 2L) + T_0 …(iv)\)
Due to symmetry, the temperature at mid-point
At x = L, \(T_R(x) = T_L(x)\)
Therefore, equating eqns. (ii) and (iv),
\(\left [ – \frac{g_o x^{2}}{2k} +C_1x + T_0 \right ]_{x=L}=\left [ C_3 (x -2L) +T_0 \right ]_{x=L}\)
\(\frac{g_o L^{2}}{2k}+ C_1L + T_0 = – C_3L + T_0\)
or \(C_1 + C_3 = \frac{g_o L}{2k}\) …(v)
Also at section x = L.
Heat transfer rate from left = Heat flow rate to right
\(Q_L =Q_R\)
or \(\left [ \frac{d T_{L}(x)}{dx} \right ]_{x = L} – \left [ \frac{d T_{R}(x)}{dx} \right ]_{x = L}\)

\(\frac{g_o L}{k} + C_1 =C_3\)
or\( C_1 – C_3 = \frac{g_o L}{k} …(vi)\)
Adding eqns. (v) and (vi), we get
\(2C_1 = \frac{3g_o L}{2k}\)

It gives \(C_1 = \frac{3g_o L}{4k}\)

Subtracting eqns. (v) and (vi), we get
\(2C_3 = − \frac{g_o L}{2k}\)

or\( C_3 = − \frac{g_o L}{4k}\)

Substituting the values of \(C_1\) and \(C_3\) in eqns. (ii) and (iv), respectively, we get
\(T_L(x) = − \frac{g_o x^2}{2k}+\frac{3g_o L}{4k}+ T_0…(vii)\)
and\( T_R(x) = −\frac{g_o L}{4k} (x – 2L) + T_0.\)

(b) The location of maximum temperature, the left portion will have maximum temperature, therefore, differentiating equation (vii) w.r.t. x and equating it to zero.
\(\frac{d T_{L}(x)}{dx} = \frac{g_o x}{k} + \frac{3g_o L}{4k}\)
\(x = \frac{3}{4}L\)
