Guest Question.

A 1.50-in.-diameter solid steel shaft supports the loads shown in Figure P15.51. The load magnitudes are \(P_{x}\) = 190 lb, \(P_{y}\) = 370 lb, and \(P_{z}\) = 220 lb. The length dimensions are a = 5.60 in., b = 3.70 in., c = 4.80 in., and d = 2.50 in. Determine
(a) the normal and shear stresses on the top of the shaft at point H.
(b) the normal and shear stresses on the side of the shaft at point K.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{array}{ll}A=\frac{\pi}{4}(1.5 in. )^{2}=1.767146 in .^{2} & J=\frac{\pi}{32}(1.5 in .)^{4}=0.497010 in .{ }^{4} \\Q=\frac{(1.5 in .)^{3}}{12}=0.281250 in .^{3} & I_{y}=I_{z}=\frac{\pi}{64}(1.5 in .)^{4}=0.248505 in .^{4}\end{array}\)

Equivalent forces at H and K:

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{x}=190 lb \\&F_{y}=-370 lb \\&F_{z}=-220 lb\end{aligned}\)

 

 

Equivalent moments at H and K:

\(\begin{aligned}M_{x} &=(370 lb )(4.8 in .)-(220 lb )(2.5 in .) \\&=1,226 lb \text {-in. } \\M_{y} &=(220 lb )(9.3 in .)=2,046 lb – in . \\M_{z} &=-(190 lb )(2.5 in .)-(370 lb )(5.6 in .) \\&=-2,547 lb – in .\end{aligned}\)

Each of the non-zero forces and moments will be evaluated to determine whether stresses are created at the point of interest.

 

(a) Consider point H.
Force \(F _{x}\) creates an axial stress at H. The magnitude of this normal stress is:

\(\sigma_{x}=\frac{190 lb }{1.767146 in. ^{2}}=107.518 psi\)

Force \(F _{y}\) does not cause either a normal stress or a shear stress at H.

Force \(F _{z}\) creates a transverse shear stress in the xz plane at H. The magnitude of this shear stress is:

\(\tau_{x z}=\frac{(220 lb )\left(0.281250 in .^{3}\right)}{\left(0.248505 in .^{4}\right)(1.5 in .)}=165.993 psi\)

Moment \(M _{x}\), which is a torque, creates a torsion shear stress in the xz plane at H. The magnitude of this shear stress is:

\(\tau_{x z}=\frac{M_{x} c}{J}=\frac{(1,226 lb – in .)(1.5 in . / 2)}{0.497010 in .^{4}}=1,850.064 psi\)

Moment \(M _{y}\) does not create bending stress at H because H is located on the neutral axis for bending about the y axis.

Moment \(M _{z}\) creates bending stress at H. The magnitude of this stress is:

\(\sigma_{x}=\frac{M_{z} y}{I_{z}}=\frac{(2,547 lb – in .)(1.5 in. / 2)}{0.248505 in. ^{4}}=7,686.972 psi\)

Summary of stresses at H:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=107.518 psi +7,686.972 psi \\&=7,794.490 psi =7,790 psi ( T ) \\\sigma_{z} &=0 psi \\\tau_{x z} &=-165.993 psi +1,850.064 psi \\&=-1,684.072 psi =-1,680 psi\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Consider point K.
Force \(F _{x}\) creates an axial stress at K. The magnitude of this normal stress is:

\(\sigma_{x}=\frac{190 lb }{1.767146 in .^{2}}=107.518 psi\)

Force \(F _{y}\) creates a transverse shear stress in the xy plane at K. The magnitude of this shear stress is:

\(\tau_{x y}=\frac{(370 lb )\left(0.281250 in .^{3}\right)}{\left(0.248505 in. ^{4}\right)(1.5 in .)}=279.170 psi\)

Force \(F _{z}\) does not cause either a normal stress or a shear stress at K.

Moment \(M _{x}\), which is a torque, creates a torsion shear stress in the xy plane at K. The magnitude of this shear stress is:

\(\tau_{x y}=\frac{M_{x} c}{J}=\frac{(1,226 lb – in .)(1.5 in . / 2)}{0.497010 in .^{4}}=1,850.064 psi\)

Moment \(M _{y}\) creates bending stress at K. The magnitude of this stress is:

\(\sigma_{x}=\frac{M_{y} z}{I_{y}}=\frac{(2,046 lb – in .)(1.5 in . / 2)}{0.248505 in .{ }^{4}}=6,174.929 psi\)

Moment \(M _{z}\) does not create bending stress at K because K is located on the neutral axis for bending about the z axis.

Summary of stresses at K:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=107.518 psi +6,174.929 psi \\&=6,282.447 psi =6,280 psi ( T ) \\\sigma_{y} &=0 psi \\\tau_{x y} &=-279.170 psi -1,850.064 psi \\&=-2,129.234 psi =-2,130 psi\end{aligned}\)
