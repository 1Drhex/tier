Guest Question.

A mixture of fuels is E85, which is 85% ethanol and 15% gasoline (assume octane) by mass. Assume we put the fuel and air, both at \(T _{ o }, P _{ o }\), into a carburetor and vaporize the fuel as we mix it with stoichiometric air before it flows to an engine. Assume the engine has an efficiency as work divided by the lower heating value of 30%, and we want it to deliver 40 kW. We use heat from the exhaust flow (500 K) for the carburetor. Find the lower heating value of this fuel (kJ/kg), the rate of fuel consumption, the heating rate needed in the carburetor, and the rate of entropy generation in the carburetor.

Admin
Solution

The heating value of the liquid fuel blend becomes

\(\begin{aligned}\text { LHV } &=0.85 \text { LHV }_{\text {ethanol }}+0.15 \text { LHV }_{\text {octane }} \\&=0.85 \times 26811+0.15 \times 44425 \quad kJ / kg = 2 9 4 5 3 ~ k J / k g\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\dot{ W }=0.3 \dot{ m }_{\text {fuel }} LHV\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ m }_{\text {fuel }} &=\dot{ W } /(0.3 \times LHV )=40 kW /(0.3 \times 29453 kJ / kg ) \\&= 0 . 0 0 4 5 2 7 kg / s =16.3 kg / h\end{aligned}\)

 

The difference in heating value based on liquid versus vapor fuel is
ΔLHV = 0.85 (27 731 – 26 811) + 0.15 (44 788 – 44 425)
= 0.85 × 920 + 0.15 × 363 = 836.45 kJ/kg

\(\dot{ Q }=\dot{ m }_{\text {fuel }} \Delta LHV =0.004527 \times 836.45=3.79 kW\)

Entropy generation is from vaporizing the fuel by transfer of heat from 500 K exhaust gas to the intake system at To and then mixing it with air. The mole fractions are

Ethanol: M = 46.069; Octane: M = 114.232
DIV = [0.85 / 46.069] + [0.15 /114.232] = 0.01845 + 0.001313 = 0.019764

\(\begin{aligned}& y _{\text {ethanol }}=0.01845 / 0.019764=0.9336 \\& y _{\text {octane }}=1- y _{\text {ethanol }}=0.06644\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{gathered}0.93356 C _{2} H _{5} OH +0.06644 C _{8} H _{18}+3.63118\left( O _{2}+3.76 N _{2}\right) \Rightarrow \\2.39864 CO _{2}+3.39864 H _{2} O +13.65324 N _{2}\end{gathered}\)

 

C balance: 0.93356 × 2 + 0.06644 × 8 = 2.39864 = \(v_{ CO 2}\)
H balance: 0.93356 × 6 + 0.06644 × 18 = 6.79728 = \(2 v_{ H 2 O }\)

 

So the A/F ratio on a mole and mass basis are

A/F-mole = 3.63118 × 4.76 / 1 = 17.2844;
A/F-mass = 17.2844 × 28.97/50.5996 = 9.896

The fuel was mixed before the carburetor so afterwards we have a 1: 17.2844 mix.

\(\begin{aligned}& s _{ fg \text { ethanol }}=(282.444-160.554) / 46.069=2.6458 kJ / kg – K \\& s _{ fg \text { octane }}=(466.514-360.575) / 114.232=0.927402 kJ / kg – K \\& s _{ fg \text { fuel mix }} =0.85 \times 2.6458+0.15 \times 0.927=2.38798 kJ / kg – K \\& M _{\text {fuel }}=0.9336 \times 46.069+0.06644 \times 114.232=50.5996\end{aligned}\)

Entropy Eq.:

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ S }_{ gen }=& out – in =\dot{ m }_{\text {fuel }}\left[ s _{ fg }- R \ln \left( y _{\text {fuel }}\right)\right]+\dot{ m }_{\text {air }}\left[- R \ln \left( y _{ air }\right)\right]-\dot{ Q } / T _{\text {exhaust }} \\=& 0.004527 \times\left[2.38798-\frac{8.3145}{50.5996} \ln \left(\frac{1}{18.2844}\right)\right] \\&-0.004527 \times 9.896 \times 0.287 \ln \left(\frac{17.2844}{18.2844}\right)-\frac{3.79}{500} kW / K \\=& 0.01297+0.000723-0.00758= 0 . 0 0 6 1 kW / K\end{aligned}\)
