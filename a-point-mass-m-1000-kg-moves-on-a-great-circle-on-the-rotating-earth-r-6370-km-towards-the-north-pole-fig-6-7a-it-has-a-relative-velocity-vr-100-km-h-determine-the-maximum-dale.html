Guest Question.

A point mass (m = 1000 kg) moves on a great circle on the rotating earth (R = 6370 km) towards the north pole (Fig. 6.7a). It has a relative velocity \(\upsilon _{r} \) = 100 km/h. Determine the maximum d’Alembert’s inertial force and the maximum Coriolis force. Neglect the motion of the earth around the sun.

Admin
Solution

The earth performs one revolution about its north-south axis in 24 hours. Thus, the angular velocity of its rotation is given by

\(\omega =\frac{2\pi }{24.3600} =7.27.10^{-5}s^{-1}\) .

We introduce the rotating ξ, η, ζ-coordinate system which is fixed to the earth (Fig. 6.7b). Then the angular velocity vector is \(\omega =\omega e_{\zeta } \) .

The fixed frame acceleration is equal to the acceleration of the point of the earth at the instantaneous location of the point mass. It is obtained from (6.17b) with \(a_{0}=0 and \dot{\omega }=0 \) :

\(a_{f}=a_{0}+\dot{\omega }\times r_{0P} \times \omega \times \left(\omega \times r_{0P} \right) , a_{r}=\frac{d^{*}\upsilon _{r} }{dt}=\frac{d^{2*}r_{0P} }{dt^{2} } , a_{c}=2\omega \times \upsilon _{r}\) . (6.17b)

\(a_{f} =\omega \times \left(\omega \times r_{0P} \right) =-R\cos \varphi \omega ^{2} e_{\eta } \) .

It is orthogonal to the rotation axis of the earth. The Coriolis acceleration follows from (6.17a,b):

\(a_{a}=a_{f}+a_{r} +a_{c}\) . (6.17a)

\(a_{c}=2\omega \times \upsilon _{r} =2\omega \upsilon _{r}\sin \varphi e_{\xi }\) .

(compare Fig. 6.7c). It is tangential to the small circle and points westwards. D’Alembert’s inertial force and the Coriolis force are thus

\(F_{f}=mR\omega ^{2}\cos \varphi e_{\eta } , F_{c}=-2m\omega \upsilon _{r}\sin \varphi e_{\xi }\) .

D’Alembert’s inertial force reaches its maximum value at the equator (φ = 0):

\(E_{f\max }=mR\omega ^{2}\approx 34N \) .

It is small as compared with the weight of the point mass (W = mg ≈ \(10^{4} \) N). The Coriolis force has its maximum value at the North Pole (φ = π/2):

\(F_{c\max } =2m\omega \upsilon _{r}\approx 4N\) .

It has to be exerted on the mass otherwise the mass would deviate from the great circle.
