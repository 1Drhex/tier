Guest Question.

A compound shaft (Figure P6.21) consists of an aluminum alloy [G = 26 GPa] tube (1) and a solid bronze [G = 45 GPa] shaft (2). Tube (1) has a length of \(L_{1}\) = 900 mm, an outside diameter of \(D_{1}\) = 35 mm, and a wall thickness of \(t_{1}\) = 4 mm. Shaft (2) has a length of \(L_{2}\) = 1,300 mm and a diameter of \(d_{2}\) = 25 mm. If an external torque of \(T_{B}\) = 420 N-m acts at pulley B in the direction shown, calculate the torque \(T_{C}\) required at pulley C so that the rotation angle of pulley C relative to A is zero.

Admin
Solution

Equilibrium: From the two FBD’s shown, derive the following equilibrium equations:

\(\begin{gathered}\Sigma M_{x}=T_{C}-T_{B}-T_{1}=0 \\\therefore T_{1}=T_{C}-T_{B} \\\Sigma M_{x}=T_{C}-T_{2}=0 \\\therefore T_{2}=T_{C}\end{gathered}\)

Section properties: The polar moments of inertia for the shaft segments will be needed for this calculation.

\(\begin{aligned}&J_{1}=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[(35 mm )^{4}-(27 mm )^{4}\right]=95,149.42 mm ^{4} \\&J_{2}=\frac{\pi}{32}(25 mm )^{4}=38,349.52 mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Geometry-of-deformation relationship: From the problem statement, the rotation angle of pulley C relative to A must equal zero. The rotation angle \(\phi_{C}\) is simply the sum of the two angles of twist.
Therefore:

\(\phi_{C}=\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}=0\)

Torque-twist relationships: The torque-twist relationship for each shaft can be written as:

\(\phi_{1}=\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}} \quad \phi_{2}=\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}\)

Compatibility equation: Substitute the torque-twist relationships into the geometry relationship to derive:

\(\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}=0\)

Solve: Substitute the equilibrium equations into the compatibility equation.

\(\frac{\left(T_{C}-T_{B}\right) L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{T_{C} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}=0\)

and solve for \(T_{C}\):

\(\begin{aligned}T_{C}\left[\frac{L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}\right] &=\frac{T_{B} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}} \\& \therefore T_{C}=T_{B} \frac{\frac{L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}}{\frac{L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}}=T_{B} \frac{L_{1}}{L_{1}+L_{2}\left(\frac{J_{1}}{J_{2}}\right)\left(\frac{G_{1}}{G_{2}}\right)}\end{aligned}\)

Calculate the torque required at C:

\(\begin{aligned}T_{C} &=(420 N – m ) \frac{0.9 m }{0.9 m +(1.3 m )\left(\frac{95,149.42 mm ^{4}}{38,349.52 mm ^{4}}\right)\left(\frac{26 GPa }{45 GPa }\right)} \\&=(420 N – m )(0.325663)=136.7785 N – m =136.8 N – m\end{aligned}\)
