Guest Question.

In the sudden enlargement shown below in Figure, the pressure acting at section (1) is considered uniform with value \( P_{1} \). Find the change in internal energy between stations (1) and (2) for steady, incompressible flow. Neglect shear stress at the walls and express \( u_{2} - u_{1} \) in terms of \( \upsilon_{1}, A_{1}, \) and \(A_{2} \). The control volume selected is indicated by the dotted line.

Admin
Solution

Conservation of Mass

\( \iint_{c.s.}^{} \rho \left ( v \cdot n \right ) dA + \frac{\partial }{\partial t} \int \int \int_{c.v.}^{} \rho d \upsilon = 0 \)

 

If we select station (2), a considerable distance downstream from the sudden enlargement, the continuity expression, for steady, incompressible flow, becomes

\( \rho_{1} \upsilon_{1} A_{1} = \rho_{2} \upsilon_{2} A_{2} \)

 

or

\( \upsilon_{2} = \upsilon_{1} \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\)

 

Momentum

\( \sum F = \iint_{c.s.}^{} \rho v \left ( v \cdot n \right ) dA + \frac{\partial }{\partial t} \int \int \int_{c.v.}^{} \rho v d \upsilon \)

 

and thus

\( P_{1} A_{2} – P_{2} A_{2} = \rho \upsilon_{2}^{2} A_{2} – \rho \upsilon_{1}^{2} A_{1} \)

 

or

\( \frac{ P_{1} – P_{2}}{\rho} = \upsilon_{2}^{2} – \upsilon_{1}^{2} \left ( \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \right ) \)

 

Energy

\( \frac{\delta Q}{\partial t} – \frac{\delta W_{s}}{dt} = \iint_{c.s.}^{} \rho \left ( e + \frac{P}{\rho } \right ) \left ( v \cdot n \right ) dA + \frac{\partial }{\partial t} \int \int \int_{c.v.}^{} \rho e d \upsilon + \frac{\delta W_{\mu }}{dt} \)

 

Thus,

\( \left ( e_{1} + \frac{P_{1}}{\rho } \right )\left ( \rho \upsilon_{1} A_{1} \right ) = \left ( e_{2} + \frac{P_{2}}{\rho } \right )\left ( \rho \upsilon_{2} A_{2} \right ) \)

 

or, since \( \rho \upsilon_{1} A_{1} = \rho \upsilon_{2} A_{2}. \)

\( e_{1} + \frac{P_{1}}{\rho} = e_{2} + \frac{P_{2}}{\rho} \)

 

The specific energy is

\( e = \frac{\upsilon^{2}}{2} + gy + u \)

 

Thus, our energy expression becomes

\( \frac{\upsilon_{1}^{2}}{2} + gy_{1} + u_{1} + \frac{P_{1}}{\rho } = \frac{\upsilon_{2}^{2}}{2} + gy_{2} + u_{2} + \frac{P_{2}}{\rho } \)

 

The three control-volume expressions may now be combined to evaluate \( u_{2} – u_{1} \). From equation ( \( \frac{\upsilon_{1}^{2}}{2} + gy_{1} + u_{1} + \frac{P_{1}}{\rho } = \frac{\upsilon_{2}^{2}}{2} + gy_{2} + u_{2} + \frac{P_{2}}{\rho } \) ), we have

 

\( u_{2} – u_{1} = \frac{P_{1} – P_{2}}{\rho } + \frac{\upsilon_{1}^{2} – \upsilon_{2}^{2}}{2} + g \left ( y_{1} – y_{2} \right ) \)

 

Substituting equation (\( \frac{P_{1} – P_{2}}{\rho } = \upsilon_{2}^{2} – \upsilon_{1}^{2} \left ( \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \right ) \)) for \( \left ( P_{1} – P_{2} \right )/\rho \) and equation ( \( \upsilon_{2} = \upsilon_{1} \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \) ) for \( \upsilon_{2} \) and noting that \( y_{1} = y_{2}, \) we obtain

 

\( u_{2} – u_{1} = \upsilon_{1}^{2} \left ( \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \right )^{2} – \upsilon_{1}^{2} \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} + \frac{ \upsilon_{1}^{2}}{2} – \frac{ \upsilon_{1}^{2}}{2} \left ( \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \right )^{2} \)

 

\( = \frac{\upsilon_{1}^{2}}{2} \left [ 1 – 2 \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} + \left ( \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \right )^{2} \right ] = \frac{\upsilon_{1}^{2}}{2} \left [ 1 – \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \right ]^{2} \)

 

Equation shows that the internal energy increases in a sudden enlargement. The temperature change corresponding to this change in internal energy is insignificant, but from equation ( \( u_{2} – u_{1} = \frac{P_{1} – P_{2}}{\rho } + \frac{\upsilon_{1}^{2} – \upsilon_{2}^{2}}{2} + g \left ( y_{1} – y_{2} \right ) \) ) it can be seen that the change in total head,

 

\( \left ( \frac{P_{1}}{\rho } + \frac{\upsilon_{1}^{2}}{2} + gy_{1} \right ) – \left ( \frac{P_{2}}{\rho } + \frac{ \upsilon_{2}^{2}}{2} + gy_{2} \right ) \)

 

is equal to the internal energy change. Accordingly, the internal energy change in an incompressible flow is designated as the head loss, \( h_{L} \), and the energy equation for steady, adiabatic, incompressible flow in a stream tube is written as

 

\( \frac{P_{1}}{\rho g} + \frac{ \upsilon_{1}^{2}}{2 g} + y_{1} = h_{L} \frac{P_{2}}{\rho g} + \frac{\upsilon_{2}^{2}}{2 g} + y_{2}\)

 

Note the similarity to equation ( \( g y_{1} + \frac{V_{1}^{2}}{2} + \frac{P_{1}}{\rho_{1}} = g y_{2} + \frac{V_{2}^{2}}{2} + \frac{P_{2}}{\rho_{2}} \) )
