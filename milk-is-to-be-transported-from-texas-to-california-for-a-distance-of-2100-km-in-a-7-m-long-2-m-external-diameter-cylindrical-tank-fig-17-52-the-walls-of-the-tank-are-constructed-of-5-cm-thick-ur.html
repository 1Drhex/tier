Guest Question.

Milk is to be transported from Texas to California for a distance of 2100 km in a 7-m-long, 2-m-external-diameter cylindrical tank (Fig. 17-52). The walls of the tank are constructed of 5 -cm-thick urethane insulation \( k= (0.029 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C ) \) sandwiched between two metal sheets. The milk is precooled to \( 3^{\circ} C\) before loading and its temperature is not to exceed \( 7^{\circ} C\) on arrival. The average ambient temperature can be as high as \( 30^{\circ} C\), and the effect of solar radiation and the radiation from hot pavement surfaces can be taken to be equivalent to a \( 4^{\circ} C\) rise in ambient temperature. If the average velocity during transportation is 60 km / h, determine if the milk can be transported without any refrigeration.

 

 

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION Milk is to be transported in a cylindrical tank by a truck. It is to be determined if the milk can be transported without any refrigeration.

Assumptions Thermal properties of milk and insulation are constant.

Properties The thermal conductivity of urethane is given to be \( k=0.029 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C . \) The density and specific heat of refrigerated milk at temperatures near \( O ^{\circ} C\) are \( \rho_{ milk }=\rho_{\text {water }}=1000 kg / m ^{3}\) and \( c_{p, \text { milk }}=3.79 kJ / kg \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\) (Table A-7).

Analysis Problems of this kind that involve “checking” are best solved by performing the calculations under the worst conditions, with the understanding the if the performance is satisfactory under those conditions, it will surely be satisfactory under any conditions. We take the average ambient temperature to be \( 30^{\circ} C\), which is the highest possible, and raise it to \( 34^{\circ} C\) to account for the radiation from the sun and the pavement. We also assume the metal sheets to offer no resistance to heat transfer and assume the convection resistances on the inner and outer sides of the tank wall to be negligible. Under these conditions, the inner and outer surface temperatures of the insulation will be equal to the milk and ambient temperatures, respectively. Further, we take the milk temperature to be\( 3^{\circ} C\) during heat transfer calculations (to maximize the temperature difference) and the heat transfer area to be the outer surface area of the tank (instead of the smaller inner surface area), which is determined to be

\( A =2 A_{\text {base }}+A_{\text {side }}=2\left(\pi D_{o}^{2} / 4\right)+\left(\pi D_{o}\right) L_{o} \) \( =2 \pi(2 m )^{2} / 4+\pi(2 m )(7 m )=50.3 m ^{2}\)

Then the rate of heat transfer through the insulation into the milk becomes

\( \dot{Q}=k_{ ins } A \frac{\Delta T_{ ins }}{L_{\text {ins }}}=\left(0.029 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(50.3 m ^{2}\right) \frac{(34-3)^{\circ} C }{0.05 m }=904.4 W\)

This is the highest possible rate of heat transfer into the milk since it is determined under the most favorable conditions for heat transfer.At an average velocity of 60 km / h, transporting the milk 2100 km will take

\( \Delta t=\frac{\text { Distancetraveled }}{\text { Average velocity }}=\frac{2100 km }{60 km / h }=35 h\)

Then the total amount of heat transfer to the milk during this long trip becomes

\( Q=Q \Delta t=(904.4 kJ / s )(35 \times 3600 s )=113,950,000 J =113,950 kJ\)

The volume and mass of the milk in a full tank is determined to be

\( V_{\text {milk }}=\left(\pi D_{i}^{2} / 4\right) L_{i}=\left[\pi(1.9 m )^{2} / 4\right](6.9 m )=19.56 m ^{3} \) \( m_{\text {milk }}=\rho V_{\text {rmilk }}=\left(1000 kg / m ^{3}\right)\left(19.56 m ^{3}\right)=19,560 kg\)

The transfer of 113,950 kJ of heat into the milk will raise its temperature to

\( Q=m c_{p}\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right) \rightarrow T_{2}= T_{1}+\frac{Q}{m c_{p}} \) \( =3^{\circ} C +\frac{113,950 kJ }{(19,560 kg )\left(3.79 kJ / kg \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)}=4.5^{\circ} C\)

That is, the temperature of the milk will rise from 3 to \( 4.5^{\circ} C\) during this long trip under the most adverse conditions, which is well below the \( 7^{\circ} C\) limit. Therefore, the milk can be transported even longer distances without any refrigeration.
