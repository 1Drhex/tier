Guest Question.

Determine pressure of steam flowing through a steam pipe when the U-tube manometer connected to it indicates as shown in figure 1.16. During pressure measurement some steam gets condensed in manometer tube and occupies a column of height 2 cm (AB) while mercury gets raised by 10 cm (CD) in open limb. Consider barometer reading as 76 cm of Hg, density of mercury and water as 13.6 × \(\textbf{10}^{3}\) kg/\(\textbf{m}^{3}\) and 1000 kg/\(\textbf{m}^{3}\) respectively.

Admin
Solution

Let us make pressure balance at plane BC.

\(\begin{aligned}p_{steam} + p_{water, AB} &= p_{atm} + p_{Hg , CD}\\p_{steam} &= p_{atm} + p_{Hg, CD} – p_{water, AB}\\p_{atm} &= (13.6 × 10^{3} × 76 × 10^{–2} × 9.81)\\p_{atm} &= 101396.16 N/m^{2}\\p_{water, AB} &= (1000 × 2 × 10^{–2} × 9.81)\\p_{water, AB} &= 196.2 N/m^{2}\\p_{Hg, CD} &= (13.6 × 10^{3} × 10 × 10^{–2} × 9.81)\\p_{Hg, CD} &= 13341.6 N/m^{2}\end{aligned}\\\text{Substituting for getting steam pressure,}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad p_{steam} &= 101396.16 + 13341.6 – 196.2\\p_{steam} &= 114541.56 N/m^{2}\\p_{steam} &= \textbf{114.54 kPa}\end{aligned}\)
