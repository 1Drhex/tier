Guest Question.

A process stream is heated using a shell and tube heat exchanger. The exit temperature is controlled by adjusting the steam control valve shown in Fig. E12.5. During an open-loop experimental test, the steam pressure \(P_{s}\) was suddenly changed from 18 to 20 psig and the temperature data shown below were obtained. At the nominal conditions, the control valve and current-to-pressure transducers have gains of \(K_{v}=0.9 psi / psi\) and \(K_{I P}=0.75 psi / mA\), respectively. Determine appropriate PID controller settings using the following approaches:

(a) Internal Model Control (select a reasonable value of \(\tau_{c}\) )

(b) AMIGO (with \(\beta=1\) and \(\gamma=0\) )

(c) ITAE (disturbance)

Which controller is the most aggressive?

Solution

Assume that the process can be modeled adequately by the first-order-plus-time delay-model in Eq. 12-10. The step response data and the tangent line at the inflection point for the slope-intercept identification method of Chapter 7 are shown in Fig. S12.5.

\(\tilde{G}(s)=\frac{K e^{-\theta s}}{\tau s+1}\) (12-10)

This estimated model parameters are:

\(\begin{aligned}&K=K_{I P} K_{v}\left(K_{p} K_{m}\right)=\left(0.75 \frac{ psi }{ mA }\right)\left(0.9 \frac{ psi }{ psi }\right)\left(\frac{16.9-12.0}{20-18} \frac{ mA }{ psi }\right)=1.65 \\&\theta=1.7 min \\&\theta+\tau=7.2 min \Rightarrow \tau=5.5 min\end{aligned}\)

a) Since \(\theta / \tau>0.25\), a conservative choice of \(\tau_{c}=\frac{1}{2} \tau\) is used. Thus, \(\tau_{c}=2.75\) min. From Case \(H\) in Table 12.1:

\(\begin{aligned}&K_{c}=\frac{1}{K} \frac{\tau+\frac{\theta}{2}}{\tau_{c}+\frac{\theta}{2}}=1.76 \\&\tau_{I}=\tau+\frac{\theta}{2}=6.35 min , \quad \tau_{D}=\frac{\tau \theta}{2 \tau+\theta}=0.736 min\end{aligned}\)

b) From Table \(12.6\), the AMIGO tuning parameters are:

\(\begin{aligned}K_{c} &=\frac{1}{K}\left(0.2+0.45 \frac{\tau}{\theta}\right)=\frac{1}{1.65}\left(0.2+0.45 \frac{5.5}{1.7}\right)=1 \\\tau_{I} &=\frac{0.4 \theta+0.8 \tau}{\theta+0.1 \tau} \theta=\frac{0.4(1.7)+0.8(5.5)}{1.7+0.1(5.5)}(1.7)=3.8 min \\\tau_{D} &=\frac{0.5 \theta \tau}{0.3 \theta+\tau}=\frac{0.5(1.7)(5.5)}{0.3(1.7)+5.5}=0.78 min\end{aligned}\)

c) From Table 12.4, the ITAE PID settings for a step disturbance are

\(\begin{array}{lll}K K_{c}=1.357(\theta / \tau)^{-0.947} & \text { or } & K_{c}=2.50 \\\tau / \tau_{I}=0.842(\theta / \tau)^{-0.738} &\text { or } & \tau_{I}=2.75 min & \\\tau_{D} / \tau=0.381(\theta / \tau)^{0.995} & \text { or } & \tau_{D}=0.65 min\end{array}\)

d) The most aggressive controller is the one from part \(c\), which has the highest value of \(K_{c}\) and smallest value of \(\tau_{ I }\)
