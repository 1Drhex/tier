Guest Question.

A double acting compound steam engine has two cylinders. Steam is admitted at 1.4 MPa and is exhausted at 25 kPa. Engine runs at 240 rpm. Low pressure cylinder has bore of 60 cm and stroke of 60 cm. Diagram factor referred to LP cylinder is 0.8 while expansion is hyperbolic throughout. Expansion is complete in high pressure cylinder and the clearance volume may be neglected. Total expansion ratio is 8 throughout the engine. Considering equal work to be produced by each cylinder determine,
(i) the indicated power output
(ii) the diameter of high pressure cylinder considering same stroke length for both cylinders.
(iii) the intermediate pressure.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{Overall expansion ratio, r = 8}\\\text{Hypothetical mep referred to LP cylinder}\\\begin{aligned}&= \frac{P_{1}}{r}(1 + \ln r) – P_{4}\\&= \frac{1.4 × 10^{3}}{8}(1 + \ln 8) – 25\\&= 513.9 kPa\\\text{Actual mep}&= \text{Hypothetical mep × d.f. referred to LP}\\\text{mep}_{actual}&= 513.9 × 0.8 = 411.12 kPa\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Indicated power output}&= \text{mep}_{actual} × L × A × N\\&= 411.12 × 0.6 × \frac{π}{4} (0.6)^{2} × \frac{240}{60} × 2\\&= 557.96 kW\\\text{Total area of diagram}&= \text{mep}_{actual} × \text{Volume of LP cylinder}\end{aligned}\\\text{Since each HP and LP perform equal work so work done in HP cylinder}\\\begin{aligned}&= \frac{\text{Total area of diagram}}{2} = \frac{411.12 × π × (0.6)^{2} × 0.6}{4 × 2}\\&= 34.87 kJ\\\text{HP cylinder work}&= P_{1} V_{1} \ln \left\lgroup\frac{V_{2}}{V_{1}} \right\rgroup = 34.87 kJ\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Also from expansion ratio, }\frac{V_{3}}{V_{1}} = 8 ⇒ V_{1} &= \frac{V_{3}}{8} = \frac{\frac{π}{4} × (0.6)^{2} × 0.6}{8}\\V_{1}&= 0.0212 m^{3}\end{aligned}\\\text{Substituting }P_{1} = 1.4 × 10^{3}\text{ kPa and V}_{1} = 0.0212 m^{3},\text{ we get}\\\begin{aligned}(1.4 × 10^{3} × 0.0212) \ln \left\lgroup\frac{V_{2}}{0.0212} \right\rgroup &= 34.87\\V_{2}& = 0.0686 m^{3}\end{aligned}\\\text{Thus, the volume of HP cylinder shall be 0.0686 m}^{3}.\text{ Let diameter of HP cylinder be ‘d’ so.}\\\begin{aligned}0.0686 &= \frac{π}{4} × (d^{2}) × 0.6\\d &= \text{0.3815 m or 38.15 cm}\end{aligned}\\\text{From hyperbolic process consideration on process 1–2.}\\\begin{aligned}P_{1} V_{1}&= P_{2} V_{2}\\(1.4 × 10^{3} × 0.0212)&= P_{2} × 0.0686\\⇒ \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad P_{2}&= 432.65 kPa\\\text{Indicated power}&= 557.96 kW\\\text{Diameter of HP cylinder}&= 38.15 cm\\\text{Intermediate pressure}&= 432.65 kPa\end{aligned}\)
