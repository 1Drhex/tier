Guest Question.

Solve each of the following overdetermined systems:

(a) \(x_{1} +x_{2} =1\)

\( x_{1} -x_{2} =3 \)

\( -x_{1} +2x_{2} =-2 \)

 

(b) \( x_{1} +2x_{2} +x_{3} =1\)

\( 2x_{1} -x_{2} +x_{3} =2 \)

\( 4x_{1} +3x_{2} +3x_{3} =4\)

\( 2x_{1} -x_{2} +3x_{3} =5 \)

 

(c) \( x_{1} +2x_{2} +x_{3} =1 \)

\( 2x_{1} -x_{2} +x_{3} =2 \)

\( 4x_{1} +3x_{2} +3x_{3} =4 \)

\( 3x_{1} +x_{2} +2x_{3} =3 \)

Admin
Solution

By now the reader should be familiar enough with the elimination process that we can omit the intermediate steps in reducing each of these systems. Thus, we may write

System (a): \( \left\lgroup\begin{matrix} 1 & 1 &| & 1 \\ 1 & -1 & | & 3 \\ -1 & 2 & | & -2 \end{matrix} \right\rgroup \rightarrow \left\lgroup\begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & | & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & | & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & | & 1 \end{matrix} \right\rgroup \)

It follows from the last row of the reduced matrix that the system is inconsistent. The three equations in system (a) represent lines in the plane. The first two lines intersect
at the point (2, âˆ’1). However, the third line does not pass through this point. Thus, there are no points that lie on all three lines (see Figure 1.2.1).

System (b): \( \left\lgroup\begin{matrix} 1 & 2 & 1 &| & 1 \\ 2 & -1 & 1 & | & 2 \\ 4 & 3 & 3 & | & 4 \\ 2 & -1 & 3 & | & 5 \end{matrix} \right\rgroup \rightarrow \left\lgroup\begin{matrix} 1 & 2 & 1 & | & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{1}{5} & | & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & | & \frac{3}{2} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & | & 0 \end{matrix} \right\rgroup \)

Using back substitution, we see that system (b) has exactly one solution: (0.1, âˆ’0.3, 1.5). The solution is unique because the nonzero rows of the reduced

matrix form a strictly triangular system.

System (c): \( \left\lgroup\begin{matrix} 1 & 2 & 1 &| & 1 \\ 2 & -1 & 1 & | & 2 \\ 4 & 3 & 3 & | & 4 \\ 3 & 1 & 2 & | & 3 \end{matrix} \right\rgroup \rightarrow \left\lgroup\begin{matrix} 1 & 2 & 1 & | & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{1}{5} & | & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & | & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & | & 0 \end{matrix} \right\rgroup \)

Solving for x2 and x1 in terms of x3, we obtain

\( x_{2} = -0.2x_{3} \) \( x_{1} = 1-2x_{2}-x_{3} =1-0.6x_{3} \)

It follows that the solution set is the set of all ordered triples of the form
(1 âˆ’ 0.6 \( (1-0.6\alpha ,-0.2\alpha ,\alpha ) \), where \( \alpha \) is a real number. This system is consistent and has infinitely many solutions because of the free variable \( x_{3} \).
