Guest Question.

Evaluate the source vector components \(f_{i}^{e}\) and coefficients \(K_{i j}^{e}\) over a master prism element when f is a constant, \(f_{0}, \text { and } k_{1}=k_{2}=k_{3}\) = constant in (14.3.5b).

 

Eq.(14.3.5b):

\(K_{i j}^{e}=\int_{\Omega_{\varphi}}\left(k_{x} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial x} \frac{\partial\psi_{j}^{e}}{\partial x}+k_{y} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial y} \frac{\partial \psi_{j}^{e}}{\partial y}+k_{z} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial z} \frac{\partial \psi_{j}^{e}}{\partial z}\right) d x+\oint_{\Gamma^{e}} \beta \psi_{i}^{e} \psi_{j}^{e} d s\) ,

\(f_{i}^{e}=\int_{\Omega_{e}} g \psi_{i}^{e} d x , \quad Q_{i}^{e}=\oint_{\Gamma^{e}}\left(q_{n}+\beta T_{\infty}\right) \psi_{i}^{e} d s\)
Admin
Solution

For a cube of sides a × b × c, the coordinate transformation become

\(x=\frac{a}{2}(1+\xi), \quad y=\frac{b}{2}(1+\eta), \quad x=\frac{c}{2}(1+\zeta)\)

and the Jacobian matrix and its inverse are

\(J =\left[\begin{array}{lll}\frac{\partial x}{\partial \xi} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial \xi} & \frac{\partial z}{\partial \xi} \\\frac{\partial x}{\partial \eta} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial \eta} & \frac{\partial z}{\partial \eta} \\\frac{\partial x}{\partial \zeta} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial \zeta} & \frac{\partial z}{\partial \zeta}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}\frac{a}{2} & 0 & 0 \\0 & \frac{b}{2} & 0 \\0 & 0 & \frac{c}{2}\end{array}\right], \quad J ^{-1}=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}\frac{2}{a} & 0 & 0 \\0 & \frac{2}{b} & 0 \\0 & 0 & \frac{2}{c}\end{array}\right]\)

Then the derivatives of the interpolation functions with respect to the global coordinates can be expressed in terms of the interpolation functions with respect to the natural coordinates as

\(\left\{\begin{array}{l}\frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial x} \\\frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial y} \\\frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial z}\end{array}\right\}= J ^{-1}\left\{\begin{array}{l}\frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial \xi} \\\frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial \eta} \\\frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial \zeta}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{l}\frac{2}{a} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial \xi} \\\frac{2}{b} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial \eta} \\\frac{2}{c} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial \zeta}\end{array}\right\}\)

Hence, the coefficients \(K_{i j}^{e}\) can be expressed as

\(K_{i j}^{e}=\int_{0}^{a} \int_{0}^{b} \int_{0}^{c}\left(k_{x} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial x} \frac{\partial \psi_{j}^{e}}{\partial x}+k_{y} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial y} \frac{\partial \psi_{j}^{e}}{\partial y}+k_{z} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial z} \frac{\partial \psi_{j}^{e}}{\partial z}\right) d x\)

 

\(=k_{x} S_{i j}^{11}+k_{y} S_{i j}^{22}+k_{z} S_{i j}^{33}\)

where \(S_{i j}^{\alpha \beta}\) are defined as

\(S_{i j}^{11}=\int_{0}^{a} \int_{0}^{b} \int_{0}^{c} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial x} \frac{\partial \psi_{j}^{e}}{\partial x} d x d y d z\)

 

\(S_{i j}^{22}=\int_{0}^{a} \int_{0}^{b} \int_{0}^{c} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial y} \frac{\partial \psi_{j}^{e}}{\partial y} d x d y d z\)

 

\(S_{i j}^{33}=\int_{0}^{a} \int_{0}^{b} \int_{0}^{c} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial z} \frac{\partial \psi_{j}^{e}}{\partial z} d x d y d z\)

The matrices \(S_{i j}^{\alpha \beta}\) can now be evaluated using the Gauss quadrature:

\(S_{i j}^{11}=\int_{0}^{a} \int_{0}^{b} \int_{0}^{c} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial x} \frac{\partial \psi_{j}^{e}}{\partial x} d x d y d z=\frac{b c}{2 a} \int_{-1}^{1} \int_{-1}^{1} \int_{-1}^{1} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial \xi} \frac{\partial \psi_{j}^{e}}{\partial \xi} d \xi d \eta d \zeta\)

 

\(S_{i j}^{22}=\int_{0}^{a} \int_{0}^{b} \int_{0}^{c} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial y} \frac{\partial \psi_{j}^{e}}{\partial y} d x d y d z=\frac{a c}{2 b} \int_{-1}^{1} \int_{-1}^{1} \int_{-1}^{1} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial \eta} \frac{\partial \psi_{j}^{e}}{\partial \eta} d \xi d \eta d \zeta\)

 

\(S_{i j}^{33}=\int_{0}^{a} \int_{0}^{b} \int_{0}^{c} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial z} \frac{\partial \psi_{j}^{e}}{\partial z} d x d y d z=\frac{a b}{2 c} \int_{-1}^{1} \int_{-1}^{1} \int_{-1}^{1} \frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial \zeta} \frac{\partial \psi_{j}^{e}}{\partial \zeta} d \xi d \eta d \zeta\)

The coefficients \(S_{i j}^{\alpha \beta}\) can be evaluated using the interpolation functions listed in Eq.(14.3.31)

\(S ^{11}=\frac{b c}{36 a}\left[\begin{array}{rrrrrrrr}4 & -4 & -2 & 2 & 2 & -2 & -1 & 1 \\-4 & 4 & 2 & -2 & -2 & 2 & 1 & -1 \\-2 & 2 & 4 & -4 & -1 & 1 & 2 & -2 \\2 & -2 & -4 & 4 & 1 & -1 & -2 & 2 \\2 & -2 & -1 & 1 & 4 & -4 & -2 & 2 \\-2 & 2 & 1 & -1 & -4 & 4 & 2 & -2 \\-1 & 1 & 2 & -2 & -2 & 2 & 4 & -4 \\1 & -1 & -2 & 2 & 2 & -2 & -4 & 4\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(S ^{22}=\frac{a c}{36 b}\left[\begin{array}{rrrrrrrr}4 & 2 & -2 & -4 & 2 & 1 & -1 & -2 \\2 & 4 & -4 & -2 & 1 & 2 & -2 & -1 \\-2 & -4 & 4 & 2 & -1 & -2 & 2 & 1 \\-4 & -2 & 2 & 4 & -2 & -1 & 1 & 2 \\2 & 1 & -1 & -2 & 4 & 2 & -2 & -4 \\1 & 2 & -2 & -1 & 2 & 4 & -4 & -2 \\-1 & -2 & 2 & 1 & -2 & -4 & 4 & 2 \\-2 & -1 & 1 & 2 & -4 & -2 & 2 & 4\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(S ^{33}=\frac{a b}{36 c}\left[\begin{array}{rrrrrrrr}4 & 2 & 1 & 2 & -4 & -2 & -1 & -2 \\2 & 4 & 2 & 1 & -2 & -4 & -2 & -1 \\1 & 2 & 4 & 2 & -1 & -2 & -4 & -2 \\2 & 1 & 2 & 4 & -2 & -1 & -2 & -4 \\-4 & -2 & -1 & -2 & 4 & 2 & 1 & 2 \\-2 & -4 & -2 & -1 & 2 & 4 & 2 & 1 \\-1 & -2 & -4 & -2 & 1 & 2 & 4 & 2 \\-2 & -1 & -2 & -4 & 2 & 1 & 2 & 4\end{array}\right]\)

Similarly, the source vector \(f ^{e}\) can be computed

\(F ^{e}=\frac{a b c}{8}\left\{\begin{array}{l}1 \\1 \\1 \\1 \\1 \\1 \\1 \\1\end{array}\right\}\)

 

 

Eq. (13.3.31) : \(\left\{\Psi^{e}\right\}=\frac{1}{8}\left\{\begin{array}{l}(1-\xi)(1-\eta)(1-\zeta) \\(1+\xi)(1-\eta)(1-\zeta) \\(1+\xi)(1+\eta)(1-\zeta) \\(1-\xi)(1+\eta)(1-\zeta) \\(1-\xi)(1-\eta)(1+\zeta) \\(1+\xi)(1-\eta)(1+\zeta) \\(1+\xi)(1+\eta)(1+\zeta) \\(1-\xi)(1+\eta)(1+\zeta)\end{array}\right\}\)
