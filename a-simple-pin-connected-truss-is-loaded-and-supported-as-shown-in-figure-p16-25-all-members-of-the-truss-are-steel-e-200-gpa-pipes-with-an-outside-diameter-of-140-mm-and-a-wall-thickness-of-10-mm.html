Guest Question.

A simple pin-connected truss is loaded and supported as shown in Figure P16.25. All members of the truss are steel [E = 200 GPa] pipes with an outside diameter of 140 mm and a wall thickness of 10 mm. The yield strength of the aluminum is 250 MPa. Determine the maximum value of P that may be applied to the structure if a factor of safety of 2.0 with respect to failure by yielding and a factor of safety of 3.0 with respect to failure by buckling are specified.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&d=140 mm -2(10 mm )=120 mm \quad A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[(140 mm )^{2}-(120 mm )^{2}\right]=4,084.070 mm ^{2} \\&I=\frac{\pi}{64}\left[(140 mm )^{4}-(120 mm )^{4}\right]=8,678,659.7 mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Euler buckling load:

\(P_{c r}=\frac{\pi^{2} E I}{L^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}\left(200,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(8,678,659.7 mm ^{4}\right)}{L^{2}}=\frac{17.1310 \times 10^{3} kN – m ^{2}}{L^{2}}\) (a)

Yield load:

\(P_{Y}=\left(250 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(4,084.070 mm ^{2}\right)=1,021,018 N =1,021.018 kN\) (b)

Truss analysis results:

\(\begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c|c|}\hline \text { Member } & \begin{array}{c}\text { Member } \\\text { axial force }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Member } \\\text { length } L\end{array} & P _{c r} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Corresponding } \\\text { truss load } P \text { based } \\\text { on buckling } \\\text { (with FS =3.0) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Corresponding } \\\text { truss load } P \\\text { based on yielding } \\\text { (with FS = 2.0) }\end{array} \\& & ( m ) & ( kN ) & ( kN ) & ( kN ) \\\hline A B & -0.750 P & 6 & 475.860 & 211.493 & 680.679 \\\hline A E & 1.250 P & 10 & \text { N.A. } & \text { N.A. } & 408.407 \\\hline B C & -3.000 P & 6 & 475.860 & 52.873 & 170.170 \\\hline B E & -1.000 P & 8 & 267.672 & 89.224 & 510.509 \\\hline B F & 3.750 P & 10 & \text { N.A. } & \text { N.A. } & 136.136 \\\hline C D & -6.750 P & 6 & 475.860 & 2 3 . 4 9 9 & 75.631 \\\hline C F & -3.000 P & 8 & 267.672 & 29.741 & 170.170 \\\hline C G & 6.250 P & 10 & \text { N.A. } & \text { N.A. } & 81.681 \\\hline D G & 0 & 8 & \text { N.A. } & \text { N.A. } & \text { N.A. } \\\hline E F & 0.750 P & 6 & \text { N.A. } & \text { N.A. } & 680.679 \\\hline F G & 3.000 P & 6 & \text { N.A. } & \text { N.A. } & 170.170 \\\hline\end{array}\)

Maximum allowable truss load P:

\(P_{\max }=23.5 kN \quad 2 P_{\max }=47.0 kN\)
