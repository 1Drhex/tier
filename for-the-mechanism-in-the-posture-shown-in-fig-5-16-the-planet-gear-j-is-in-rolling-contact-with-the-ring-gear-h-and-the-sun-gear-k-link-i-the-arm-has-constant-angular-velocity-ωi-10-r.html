Guest Question.

For the mechanism in the posture shown in Fig. 5.16, the planet gear, j, is in rolling contact with the ring gear, h, and the sun gear, k. Link i (the arm) has constant angular velocity ωi = 10 rad/s ccw, and gear h has angular velocity ωh = 5 rad/s cw and angular acceleration αh = 15 rad/s2 ccw. Determine: (a) the first- and second-order kinematic coefficients of gears j and k; and (b) the angular velocity and acceleration of gears j and k.

Admin
Solution

For the rolling contact between planet gear j and ring gear h, the constraint equation can be written in the form

\(\rho_{j}\left(\Delta \theta_{j}-\Delta \theta_{i}\right)=+\rho_{h}\left(\Delta \theta_{h}-\Delta \theta_{i}\right).\) (1)

Similarly, for the rolling contact between planet gear j and sun gear k, the constraint
equation can be written as

\(\rho_{j}\left(\Delta \theta_{j}-\Delta \theta_{i}\right)=-\rho_{k}\left(\Delta \theta_{k}-\Delta \theta_{i}\right).\) (2)

These two equations can be put into matrix form, with the independent variables on the right, as follows

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc}\rho_{j}&0\\\rho_{j}&\rho_{k}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}\Delta\theta_{j}\\\Delta\theta_{k}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{cc}\rho_{h} & \rho_{h}-\rho_{j}\\0&\rho_{j}+\rho_{k}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{r} \Delta \theta_{h} \\\Delta \theta_{i}\end{array}\right]\) (3)

 

(a) Taking partial derivatives of Eqs. (3) with respect to the first independent variable, we find equations for the following first-order kinematic coefficients:

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc}\rho_{j} & 0 \\\rho_{j} & \rho_{k}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}\theta_{j h}^{\prime}\\\theta_{k h}^{\prime}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{cc}\rho_{h} & \rho_{h}-\rho_{j}\\0&\rho_{j}+\rho_{k}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}1 \\0\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}\rho_{h}\\0\end{array}\right].\) (4)

The determinant of the Jacobian matrix is

\(\Delta=\rho_{j} \rho_{k}=8 \text { in }^{2},\)

and Cramer’s rule gives the solution to Eqs. (4) as

\(\theta_{j h}^{\prime}=\rho_{h} / \rho_{j}=4.0 rad / rad \quad \text { and } \quad \theta_{k h}^{\prime}=-\rho_{h} / \rho_{k}=-2.0 rad / rad.\) (5)

Taking partial derivatives of Eqs. (3) with respect to the other independent variable, we find equations for the remaining first-order kinematic coefficients:

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc}\rho_{j} & 0 \\\rho_{j} &\rho_{k}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}\theta_{j i}^{\prime}\\\theta_{k i}^{\prime}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{cc}\rho_{h} & \rho_{h}-\rho_{j} \\0 &\rho_{j}+\rho_{k}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}0 \\1\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}\rho_{h}-\rho_{j} \\\rho_{j}+\rho_{k}\end{array}\right]\) (6)

 

Cramer’s rule gives the solutions to Eqs. (6) as

\(\theta_{j i}^{\prime}=\left(\rho_{h}-\rho_{j}\right) / \rho_{j}=3.0 rad / rad \quad \text { and } \quad \theta_{k i}^{\prime}=\left(-\rho_{h}+2 \rho_{j}+\rho_{k}\right) / \rho_{k}=0.0 .\) (7)

The second-order kinematic coefficients can be found by taking partial derivatives of Eqs. (5) and (7) with respect to each of the independent variables. Since the right-hand sides of these equations are constants, we find the second-order kinematic coefficients to be

\(\theta_{j h h}^{\prime \prime}=\theta_{k h h}^{\prime \prime}=\theta_{j h i}^{\prime \prime}=\theta_{k h i}^{\prime \prime}=\theta_{j i i}^{\prime \prime}=\theta_{k i i}^{\prime \prime}=0 .\)

(b) The angular velocities of links j and k are

\(\omega_{j}=\theta_{j h}^{\prime} \omega_{h}+\theta_{j i}^{\prime} \omega_{i}\)

 

\(=(4.0 rad / rad )(-5.0 rad / s )+(3.0 rad / rad )(10.0 rad / s )=10.0 rad / s ( ccw ),\)

 

\(\omega_{k}=\theta_{k h}^{\prime} \omega_{h}+\theta_{k i}^{\prime} \omega_{i}\)

 

\(=(-2.0 rad / rad )(-5.0 rad / s )+(0.0 rad / rad )(10.0 rad / s )=10.0 rad / s ( ccw ).\)

 

The angular accelerations of links j and k are

\(\alpha_{j}=\theta_{j h h}^{\prime \prime} \omega_{h}^{2}+2 \theta_{j h i}^{\prime \prime} \omega_{h} \omega_{i}+\theta_{j i i}^{\prime \prime} \omega_{i}^{2}+\theta_{j h}^{\prime} \alpha_{h}+\theta_{j i}^{\prime} \alpha_{i}\)

 

\(=0+0+0+(4.0 rad / rad )\left(15 rad / s ^{2}\right)+(3.0 rad / rad )(0)=60.0 rad / s ^{2}( ccw ),\)

 

\(\alpha_{k}=\theta_{k h h}^{\prime \prime} \omega_{h}^{2}+2 \theta_{k h i}^{\prime \prime} \omega_{h} \omega_{i}+\theta_{k i i}^{\prime \prime} \omega_{i}^{2}+\theta_{k h}^{\prime} \alpha_{h}+\theta_{k i}^{\prime} \alpha_{i}\)

 

\(=0+0+0+(-2.0 rad / rad )\left(15 rad / s ^{2}\right)+0=-30.0 rad / s ^{2}( cw ).\)
