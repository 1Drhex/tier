Guest Question.

Compute the required size of new clean Schedule 40 pipe that will carry \( 0.50 ft^3/s\) of water at 60°F and limit the pressure drop to 2.00 psi over a length of 100 ft of horizontal pipe.

Admin
Solution

We first calculate the limiting energy loss. Note that the elevation difference is zero. Write

\( h_L = \left(p_1-p_2\right) /\gamma +\left(z_1 – z_2\right) = \left(2.00lb/in^2\right) \left(144in^2/ft^2\right) \left(62.4lb/ft^3\right) +0 = 4.62ft\)

The following data are needed in Eq. (11–8):

\( Q = 0.50 ft^3/s \) L = 100 ft \( g=32.2ft/s^2 \)

\( h_L = 4.62ft \) \( \epsilon = 1.5 \times 10^{-4}ft \) \( v = 1.21 \times 10^{-5}ft^2/s \)

Now we can enter these data into Eq. (11–8):

\( D = 0.66\left[\epsilon^{1.25}\left(\frac{LQ^2}{gh_L} \right)^{4.75}+\nu Q^{9.4}\left(\frac{L}{gh_L} \right)^{5.2} \right]^{0.04} \) (11–8)

\( D = 0.66\left[\left(1.5 \times 10^{-4}\right) ^{1.25}\left[\frac{\left(100\right) \left(0.50\right) ^2}{\left(32.2\right) \left(4.62\right) } \right]^{4.75}+\left(1.21 \times 10^{-5}\right) \left(0.50\right) ^{9.4}\left[\frac{100}{\left(32.2\right) \left(4.62\right) } \right]^{5.2} \right]^{0.04} \)

D = 0.309 ft

The result shows that the pipe should be larger than D = 0.309 ft. The next larger standard pipe size is a 4-in Schedule 40 steel pipe having an ID of D = 0.3355 ft.
