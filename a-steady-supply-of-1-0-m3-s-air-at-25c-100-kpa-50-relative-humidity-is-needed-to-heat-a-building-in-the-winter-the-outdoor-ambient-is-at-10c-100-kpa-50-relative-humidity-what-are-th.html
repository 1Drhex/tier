Guest Question.

A steady supply of 1.0 \(m ^{3} / s\) air at 25°C, 100 kPa, 50% relative humidity is needed to heat a building in the winter. The outdoor ambient is at 10°C, 100 kPa, 50% relative humidity. What are the required liquid water input and heat transfer rates for this purpose?

Admin
Solution

Air: \(R _{ a }=0.287 kJ / kg K , \quad C _{ p }=1.004 kJ / kg – K\)

State 1:

\(\begin{aligned}& T _{1}=10^{\circ} C , \quad \phi_{1}=50 \%, P _{1}=100 kPa \\& P _{ g 1}=1.2276 kPa , \quad P _{ v1 }=\phi_{1} P _{ g 1}=0.6138 kPa \\& P _{ a 1}= P _{1}- P _{ v 1}=99.39 kPa \Rightarrow \omega_{1}=0.622 P _{ v 1} / P _{ a 1}=0.003841\end{aligned}\)

State 2:

\(\begin{aligned}& T _{2}=25^{\circ} C , P _{2}=100 kPa , \quad \phi_{2}=50 \%, \quad \dot{ V }_{2}=1 m ^{3} / s \\& P _{ g 2}=3.169 kPa , \quad P _{ v 2}=\phi_{2} P _{ g 2}=1.5845 kPa\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{array}{r}P _{ a 2}= P _{2}- P _{ v 2}=98.415 kPa , \quad \omega_{2}=0.622 P _{ v 2} / P _{ a 2}=0.010014 \\\dot{ m }_{ a 2}= P _{ a 2} \dot{ V }_{2} / R _{ a } T _{2}=98.415 \times 1 /(0.287 \times 298.15)=1.15 kg / s\end{array}\)

 

Steam tables B.1.1: \(h _{ v 1}=2519.7 kJ / kg , \quad h _{ v 2}=2547.2 kJ / kg\)

State 3: Assume liq. water at \(T _{3}=25^{\circ} C , \quad h _{ f 3}=104.9 kJ / kg\)

Conservation of Mass: \(\dot{ m }_{ a 1}=\dot{ m }_{ a 2}, \quad \dot{ m }_{ f 3}=\dot{ m }_{ v 2}-\dot{ m }_{ v 1}\)

\(\dot{ m }_{ f 3}=\dot{ m }_{ a 2}\left(\omega_{2}-\omega_{1}\right)=1.15 \times 0.006173= 0 . 0 0 7 1 kg / s\)

Energy Eq.: \(\dot{ Q }+\dot{ m }_{ a 1} h _{ a 1}+\dot{ m }_{ v 1} h _{ v 1}+\dot{ m }_{ f 3} h _{ f 3}=\dot{ m }_{ a 2} h _{ a 2}+\dot{ m }_{ v 2} h _{ v 2}\)

\(\begin{aligned}\tilde{ q } &=\frac{\dot{ Q }}{\dot{ m }_{ a }}= C _{ p }\left( T _{2}- T _{1}\right)+\omega_{2} h _{ v 2}-\omega_{1} h _{ v 1}-\frac{\dot{ m }_{ f 3}}{\dot{ m }_{ a }} h _{ f 3} \\&=1.004(25-10)+0.010014 \times 2547.2-0.003841 \times 2519.7 \\& \quad-0.006173 \times 104.9 \\&=30.24 kJ / kg \text { dry air }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\Rightarrow \quad \dot{Q}=\dot{m}_{a 1} \tilde{q}=1.15 kg / s \times 30.24 kJ / kg =34.78 k W\)
