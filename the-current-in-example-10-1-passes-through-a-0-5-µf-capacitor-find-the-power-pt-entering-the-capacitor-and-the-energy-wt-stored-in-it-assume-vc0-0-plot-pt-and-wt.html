Guest Question.

The current in passes through a 0.5-µF capacitor. Find the power p(t) entering the capacitor and the energy w(t) stored in it. Assume \( v_{C}(0)=0 \). Plot p(t) and w(t).

Admin
Solution

Figure 10-2(a) indicates that the current in the capacitor is a periodic function with a period T = 2 ms. During one period the current is given by

\(i=\left\{\begin{aligned}1 mA \quad (0<t<1 ms ) \\-1 mA \quad (1<t<2 ms )\end{aligned}\right.\)

The voltage across the capacitor is also a periodic function with the same period T [Fig. 10-3(a)]. During one period the voltage is

\(v(t)=\frac{1}{C} \int_{0}^{t} i d t=\left\{\begin{aligned}2000 t( V ) \qquad (0<t<1 m s ) \\4-2000 t( V ) \qquad (1<t<2 ms )\end{aligned}\right.\)

Finally, the power entering the capacitor and the energy stored in it (both also periodic with period T) are

\( p(t)=v i=\begin{cases} 2000 t( mW ) \qquad (0<t<1 ms ) \\ 2000 t-4( mW ) \quad (1<t<2 ms )\end{cases} \qquad [\text { Fig. 10-3(b)]}\)

 

\(w(t)=\frac{1}{2} C v^{2}= \begin{cases}t^{2}( J ) & (0<t<1 ms ) \\t^{2}+4 \times 10^{-6}-4 \times 10^{-3} t( J ) & (1<t<2 ms )\end{cases} \quad[\text { Fig. } 10-3(c)]\)

 

Alternatively, w(t) may be obtained by integrating p(t). Power entering the capacitor during one period is equally positive and negative [see Fig. 10-3(b)]. The energy stored in the capacitor is always positive as shown in Fig. 10-3(c). The maximum stored energy is \( W_{\max }=10^{-6} J =1 \mu J \text { at } t=1,3,4, \ldots ms \).
