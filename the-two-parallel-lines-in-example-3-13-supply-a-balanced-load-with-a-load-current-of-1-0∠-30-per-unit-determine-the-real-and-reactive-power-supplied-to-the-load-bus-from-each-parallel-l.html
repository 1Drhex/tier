Guest Question.

The two parallel lines in supply a balanced load with a load current of 1.0∠-30° per unit. Determine the real and reactive power supplied to the load bus from each parallel line with (a) no regulating transformer, (b) the voltage-magnitude-regulating transformer in (a), and (c) the phase-angle-regulating transformer in (b). Assume that the voltage at bus abc is adjusted so that the voltage at bus \(a^{\prime} b^{\prime} c^{\prime}\) remains constant at 1.0∠0° per unit. Also assume positive sequence. Comment on the effects of the regulating transformers.

Admin
Solution
\(P_{\text {Load }}+j Q_{\text {Load }}=\bar{V}^{\prime} \bar{I}_{\text {Load }}^{*}=\left(1.0 \angle 0^{\circ}\right)\left(1.0 \angle-30^{\circ}\right)^{*}=1.0 \angle 30^{\circ}=0.866+j 0.50 \text { per unit }\)

 

(a) No regulating transformer, \(\bar{C}=1.0\)

Using current division:

\(\bar{I}_{L 1}=\left(\frac{X_{L 2}}{X_{L 1}+X_{L 2}}\right) \bar{I}_{\text {Load }}=\left(\frac{0.25}{0.45}\right)\left(1.0 \angle-30^{\circ}\right)=0.5556 \angle-30^{\circ} \text { per unit }\)

 

\(P_{L 1}+j Q_{L 1}=\bar{V}^{\prime} \bar{I}_{L 1}^{*}=0.5556 \angle+30^{\circ}=0.4811+j 0.2778 \text { per unit }\)

 

\(\begin{gathered}P_{L 2}+j Q_{L 2}=\left(P_{\text {Load }}+j Q_{\text {Load }}\right)-\left(P_{L 1}+j Q_{L 1}\right)=(0.866+j 0.5)-(.4811+j .2778) \\=0.3849+j 0.2222 \text { per unit }\end{gathered}\)

 

 

(b) Voltage magnitude regulating transformer, C = 0.9524
Using the admittance parameters from (a)

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I} \\-1.0 \angle-30^{\circ}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{I} \\-\bar{I}_{\text {Load }}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{cc}\bar{Y}_{11} & \bar{Y}_{12} \\\bar{Y}_{21} & \bar{Y}_{22}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{V} \\\bar{V}^{1}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{cc}-j 9.0 & j 8.810 \\j 8.810 & -j 8.628\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{V} \\1.0 \angle 0^{\circ}\end{array}\right]\)

 

Solving the second equation above for \(\bar{V}\) :

\(\begin{aligned}-1.0 \angle-30^{\circ} &=(j 8.810) \bar{V}-(j 8.628)\left(1.0 \angle 0^{\circ}\right) \\\bar{V} &=\frac{8.628 \angle 90^{\circ}-1.0 \angle-30^{\circ}}{j 8.810}=\frac{-.866+j 9.128}{j 8.810}=1.041 \angle 5.42^{\circ} \text { per unit }\end{aligned}\)

Then:

\(\bar{I}_{L 1}=\frac{\bar{V}-\bar{V}^{\prime}}{j X_{L 1}}=\frac{1.041 \angle 5.42^{\circ}-1.0 \angle 0^{\circ}}{j 0.20}=\frac{0.0361+j 0.0983}{j 0.20}=0.5235 \angle-20.14^{\circ}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&P_{L 1}+j Q_{L 1}=\bar{V}^{\prime} \bar{I}_{L 1}^{*}=0.5235 \angle 20.14^{\circ}=\underline{\underline{0.4915+j 0.1802}} \text { per unit } \\&P_{L 2}+j Q_{L 2}=\left(P_{\text {Load }}+j Q_{\text {Load }}\right)-\left(P_{L 1}+j Q_{L 1}\right)=\underline{\underline{0.3745+j 0.3198}} \text { per unit }\end{aligned}\)

 

The voltage magnitude regulating transformer increases the reactive power delivered by line L-2 43.970 (from 0.2222 to 0.3198) with a relatively small change in the real power delivered by line L2.

 

(c) Phase angle regulating transformer, \(\bar{C}=1.0 \angle-3^{\circ}\)

Using \(\bar{Y}_{21}=-0.2093+j 8.9945\) and \(\bar{Y}_{22}=-j 9.0\) per unit from (b):

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{V} &=\frac{-\bar{Y}_{22} \bar{V}^{\prime}-\bar{I}_{\text {Load }}}{\bar{Y}_{21}}=\frac{(j 9.0)\left(1.0 \angle 0^{\circ}\right)-1.0 \angle-30^{\circ}}{-0.2093+j 8.9945} \\&=\frac{-0.8660+j 9.50}{-0.2093+j 8.9945}=\frac{9.539 \angle 95.21^{\circ}}{8.997 \angle 91.33^{\circ}}=1.060 \angle 3.89^{\circ} \text { per unit }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{I}_{L 1} &=\frac{\bar{V}-\bar{V}^{\prime}}{j X_{L 1}}=\frac{1.060 \angle 3.879^{\circ}-1.0 \angle 0^{\circ}}{j 0.20}=\frac{0.0578+j 0.717}{j 0.20} \\&=0.4606 \angle-38.87^{\circ} \text { per unit }\end{aligned}\) \(\begin{aligned}&P_{L 1}+j Q_{L 1}=\bar{V}^{\prime} \bar{I}_{L 1}^{*}=0.4606 \angle +38.87^{\circ}=\underline{\underline{0.3586+j 0.2890}} \\&P_{L 2}+j Q_{L 2}=\left(P_{\text {Load }}+j Q_{\text {Load }}\right)-\left(P_{L 1}+j Q_{L 1}\right)=\underline{\underline{0.5074+j 0.2110}}\end{aligned}\)

The phase-angle regulating transformer increases the real power delivered by line L2 31.8% (from 0.3849 to 0.5074) with a relatively small change in the reactive power delivered by line L2.
