Guest Question.

A tractor is stuck in mud, and to free it, a cable applying a force with magnitude F and direction \(\vec{r} _{1} \) is attached to the front of the tractor as shown. The operator estimates that a 400 lb force applied in the direction of the tractor’s chassis, which is \(\vec{r} _{2} \) , will be sufficient to free it. Determine the cable force F that should be applied and the component of this force perpendicular to the direction of the tractor’s chassis. Note: This problem is similar to except here the force and position vectors are not in a horizontal plane.

Admin
Solution

Road Map The direction of the cable force is known, but its magnitude is unknown. Thus, \( \vec{F} = F\left({\vec{r} _{1}}/{\left|\vec{r} _{2}\right| }\right) \) where F is to be determined. We will use the dot product to determine the component of the cable force that acts in the direction of the tractor, and according to the problem statement, this must equal 400 lb.

Governing Equations & Computation The dot product between the cable force \( \vec{F}\) and a unit vector in the direction of the tractor’s chassis must equal 400 lb:

\(F_{\parallel } = \vec{F} \cdot \frac{\vec{r} _{2} }{\left|\vec{r} _{2} \right| }= F \frac{\vec{r} _{1} }{\left|\vec{r} _{1} \right| } \cdot \frac{\vec{r} _{2} }{\left|\vec{r} _{2} \right| }\)

\(400 lb = F\frac{\left(200\hat{\imath} + 600\hat{\jmath} + 150 \hat{k} \right) ft }{650 ft} \cdot \frac{\left(-2 \hat{\imath} + 4\hat{\jmath} – \hat{k} \right) ft }{\sqrt{21}ft }\)

\(= F\frac{1850}{650 \sqrt{21} }\)

\(= F\left(0\cdot 6211\right)\) (1)

Solving the above equation provides the cable force

\(F = 644 \cdot 0 lb.\) (2)

Determination of only the magnitude of the perpendicular component of force is straightforward using the Pythagorean theorem, Eq. (2.44):

\(F_{\bot } = \sqrt{F^{2} – F^{2}_{\parallel } }\) (2.44)

\(F_{\bot } = \sqrt{F^{2} – F^{2}_{\parallel } } = \sqrt{\left(644\cdot 0 lb\right) ^{2} – \left(400 lb\right) ^{2} } = 504\cdot 8 lb\) (3)

If the direction of the perpendicular component is also desired, then we may use vector addition to obtain it, as expressed by Eq. (2.46):

\(\vec{F} _{\bot } = \vec{F} – \vec{F} _{\parallel }\) (2.46)

\(\vec{F} _{\bot } = \vec{F} – \vec{F} _{\parallel } = F \frac{\vec{r} _{1} }{\left| \vec{r} _{1} \right| } – F_{\parallel } \frac{\vec{r} _{2} }{\left| \vec{r} _{2} \right| }\)

\(= \left(644 lb\right) \frac{\left(200\hat{\imath} + 600\hat{\jmath} + 150\hat{k} \right) ft }{650 ft} – \left(400 lb\right) \frac{\left(-2\hat{\imath} + 4\hat{\jmath} – \hat{k} \right) ft }{\sqrt{21} ft}\)

\(= \left(373\hat{\imath} + 245\hat{\jmath} + 236\hat{k} \right)lb\) (4)

Discussion & Verification As a partial check of our solution, the magnitude of Eq. (4) is \(\left|\vec{F} _{\bot } \right| = \sqrt{\left(373\right) ^{2} + \left(245\right) ^{2} + \left(236\right) ^{2}} lb = 505 lb\), which agrees with the value found in Eq. (3).
