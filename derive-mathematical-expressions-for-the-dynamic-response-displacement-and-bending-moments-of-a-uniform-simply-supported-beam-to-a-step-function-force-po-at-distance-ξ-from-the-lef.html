Guest Question.

Derive mathematical expressions for the dynamic response—displacement and bending moments—of a uniform simply supported beam to a step-function force \(p_{o} \) at distance \(\xi \) from the left end (Fig. E17.1). Specialize the results for the force applied at midspan.

Admin
Solution

1. Determine the natural vibration frequencies and modes.

\(\omega _{n}=\frac{n^{2}\pi ^{2}}{L^{2}}\sqrt{\frac{EI}{m}}\)

\(\phi _{n}\left ( x \right )= sin \frac{n\pi x}{L} \)(a)

2. Set up the modal equations. Substituting \(\phi _{n}(x)\) gives \(M_{n}\), which issubstituted in Eq. (17.5.5) together with\( \omega _{n}^{2}\) to get \(K_{n}:\)

\(M_{n}=\frac{mL}{2}\)
\(K_{n} = \frac{n^{4}\pi ^{4}EI}{2L^{3}}\) (b)

Substituting \(p(x, t) = p_{o}\delta (x − \xi ),\) where \(\delta (x − \xi )\) is the Dirac delta function centered at \(\xi\) , gives

\(P_{n}\left ( t \right )=p_{o}\phi _{n}\left ( \xi \right )\)(c)

Then the nth modal equation is

\(M_{n}\ddot{q}_{n}\left ( t \right )+K_{n}q_{n}\left ( t \right )=p_{o}\phi _{n}\left ( \xi \right )\)(d)

3. Solve the modal equations. Eq describes the response of an SDF system to a step force. We will adapt this solution to Eq. (d) by changing the notation u(t) to \(q_{n}(t)\) and noting that \((u_{st})_{o} =p_{o}\phi _{n}\left ( \xi \right )/K_{n}.\) Thus

\(q_{n}\left ( t \right )= \frac{p_{o}\phi _{n}\left ( \xi \right )}{K_{n}}\left ( 1- cos \omega _{n}t \right )=\frac{2p_{o}L^{3}}{\pi ^{4}EI}\frac{\phi _{n}\left ( \xi \right )}{n^{4}}\left ( 1- cos \omega _{n}t \right )\)(e)

Substituting Eq. (e) in Eq. (17.5.7) and noting that \(\phi _{n}(x)\) is known from Eq. (a), we obtain the displacement response u(x, t).
4. Specialize for \(\xi = L/2.\) Substituting \(\xi = L/2\) in Eq. (e) and the latter in Eq. (17.5.7) gives

\(u\left ( x,t \right )=\frac{2p_{o}L^{3}}{\pi ^{4}EI}\sum_{n=1}^{\infty }\frac{\phi _{n}\left ( L/2 \right )}{n^{4}}\left ( 1-cos \omega _{n}t \right )sin \frac{n\pi x}{L}\)(f)

where

\(\phi _{n}\left ( \frac{L}{2} \right )=\left\{\begin{matrix} 0 & n = 2, 4, 6, . . . \\ 1 & n = 1, 5, 9, . . . \\ -1 & n = 3, 7, 11, . . . \end{matrix}\right.\)(g)

from Eq. (a) and Fig. 17.3.1. Substituting Eq. (g) in Eq. (f) gives

\(u\left ( x,t \right )=\frac{2p_{o}L^{3}}{\pi ^{4}EI}\left ( \frac{1-cos \omega _{1}t}{1}sin \frac{\pi x}{L}-\frac{1-cos \omega _{3}t}{81}sin \frac{3\pi x}{L} +\frac{1-cos \omega _{5}t}{625}sin \frac{5\pi x}{L}-\frac{1-cos \omega _{7}t}{2401}sin \frac{7\pi x}{L} +… \right )\) (h)

The displacement at midspan is

\(u\left ( \frac{L}{2},t \right )=\frac{2p_{o}L^{3}}{\pi ^{4}EI}\left ( \frac{1-cos \omega _{1}t}{1}+\frac{1-cos \omega _{3}t}{81} +\frac{1-cos \omega _{5}t}{625}+\frac{1-cos \omega _{7}t}{2401}+… \right )\) (i)

The coefficients 1, 81, 625, 2401, and so on, in the denominator suggest that the first-mode contribution is dominant and that the series converges rapidly.
The bending moments are obtained by substituting Eq. (h) in Eq. (17.5.9a):

\(M\left ( x,t \right )=-\frac{2p_{o}L}{\pi ^{2}}\left ( \frac{1-cos \omega _{1}t}{1}sin \frac{\pi x}{L}-\frac{1-cos \omega _{3}t}{9}sin \frac{3\pi x}{L} +\frac{1-cos \omega _{5}t}{ 25}sin \frac{5\pi x}{L}-\frac{1-cos \omega _{7}t}{49}sin \frac{7\pi x}{L} +… \right )\)(j)

The bending moment at midspan is

\(M\left ( \frac{L}{2},t \right )=-\frac{2p_{o}L}{\pi ^{2}}\left ( \frac{1-cos \omega _{1}t}{1}+\frac{1-cos \omega _{3}t}{9} +\frac{1-cos \omega _{5}t}{ 25}+\frac{1-cos \omega _{7}t}{49}+… \right )\) (k)

This series with \(n^{2}\) in the denominator converges slowly compared to Eq. (i) with \(n^{4}\) in the denominator. This difference implies that higher modes contribute more significantly to forces than to displacements, a result consistent with the conclusions of Chapters 12 and 13 for discretized systems.
