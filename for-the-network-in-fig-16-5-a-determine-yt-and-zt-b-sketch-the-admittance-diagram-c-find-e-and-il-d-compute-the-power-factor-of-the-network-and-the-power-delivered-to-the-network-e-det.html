Guest Question.

For the network in Fig. 16.50.

a. Determine \(Y_T and Z_T\).

b. Sketch the admittance diagram.

c. Find E and \(I_L\).

d. Compute the power factor of the network and the power delivered to the network.

e. Determine the equivalent series circuit as far as the terminal characteristics of the network are concerned.

f. Using the equivalent circuit developed in part (e), calculate E, and compare it with the result of part (c).

g. Determine the power delivered to the network, and compare it with the solution of part (d).

h. Determine the equivalent parallel network from the equivalent series circuit, and calculate the total admittance \(Y_T\). Compare the result with the solution of part (a).

Admin
Solution

a. Combining common elements and finding the reactance of the inductor and capacitor, we obtain

\(R_T=10 Ω\parallel 40 Ω = 8 Ω\\[0.5cm] L_T=6 mH\parallel 12 mH = 4 mH\\[0.5cm] C_T = 80 \mu F + 20 \mu F = 100 \mu F\\[0.5cm] X_L = \omega L = (1000 rad/s)(4 mH) = 4 Ω\\[0.5cm] X_C=\frac{1}{\omega C}=\frac{1}{(1000 rad/s)(100 \mu F)}=10 Ω \)

The network is redrawn in Fig. 16.51 with phasor notation. The total admittance is

\(Y_T=Y_R+Y_L+Y_C\\[0.5cm] =G ∠0° + BL ∠-90° + BC ∠+90°\\[0.5cm] =\frac{1}{8 Ω}∠0° +\frac{1}{4 Ω}∠-90° +\frac{1}{10 Ω}∠+90°\\[0.5cm] =0.125 S ∠0° + 0.25 S ∠-90° + 0.1 S ∠+90°\\[0.5cm] =0.125 S – j 0.25 S + j 0.1 S=0.125 S – j 0.15 S =0.195S\angle -50.194°\\[0.5cm] Z_T=\frac{1}{Y_T}=\frac{1}{0.195 S ∠-50.194°}=5.13\Omega\angle 50.19° \)

 

b. See Fig. 16.52.

c. \(E=IZ_T=\frac{I}{Y_T}=\frac{12 A ∠0°}{0.195 S ∠-50.194°}=61.54V\angle 50.19°\\[0.5cm] I_L=\frac{V_L}{Z_L}=\frac{E}{Z_L}=\frac{61.538 V∠50.194°}{4 Ω ∠90°}=15.39A\angle -39.81° \)

 

d. \(F_p=cos\theta=\frac{G}{Y_T}=\frac{0.125 S}{0.195 S}=0.641\; lagging (E \; leads\; I) \\[0.5cm] P=EIcos\theta=(61.538 V)(12 A) cos 50.194°=472.75W\)

 

e. \(Z_T=\frac{1}{Y_T}=\frac{1}{0.195 S ∠-50.194°}=5.128 Ω ∠+50.194°=3.28 Ω + j 3.94 Ω=R + jX_L\\[0.5cm] X_L=3.94 Ω = \omega L\\[0.5cm] L=\frac{3.94 Ω}{\omega}=\frac{3.94 Ω}{1000 rad/s}=3.94mH \)

The series equivalent circuit appears in Fig. 16.53.

 

f. \(E=IZ_T=(12 A ∠0°)(5.128 Ω ∠50.194°)=61.54V\angle 50.194° \; (as\; above)\\[0.5cm] \)

 

g. \(P=I^2R=(12A)^2(3.28 Ω) =472.32W\; (as\; above)\)

 

h. \(R_p=\frac{R^{2}_{s}+X^{2}_{s}}{R_s}=\frac{(3.28 Ω)^2+(3.94 Ω)^2}{3.28 Ω} =8 Ω \\[0.5cm] X_p=\frac{R^{2}_{s}+X^{2}_{s}}{X_s}= \frac{(3.28 Ω)^2+(3.94 Ω)^2}{3.94 Ω}=6.67 Ω\)

The parallel equivalent circuit appears in Fig. 16.54.

\(Y_T=G ∠0° + BL ∠-90° =\frac{1}{8 Ω}∠0° +\frac{1}{6.675 Ω}∠-90°\\[0.5cm] = 0.125 S ∠0° + 0.15 S ∠-90°=0.125 S – j 0.15 S=0.195S\angle -50.194°\quad (as\; above) \)
