Guest Question.

For the device shown in Fig. 3.31, some of the links are visible, whereas other links are enclosed in a housing. However, the location of instant center I25 is known, as shown in the figure. If the velocity of point C of link 6 is VC = 10 m/s to the right, determine the angular velocity of link 2.

Admin
Solution

Since \(V _{C_{5} / 1}= V _{C}\), and we are required to find ω2/1, then we need to use the instant centers I15, I12, and I25. Note that ω2/1 is the same as ω2, but the additional subscript has been written to emphasize the presence of the third link (the frame). The primary instant center I16 is at infinity, as shown. The instant center I15 lies on line I12 I25 and on line I16 I56, and since these two lines are parallel, then I15 is also at infinity.

Considering I25 a point of link 5, we wish to find the velocity of this point from the given VC. We have difficulty in locating point C′ on the line of centers at the same radius from I15 as point C, since I15 is at infinity. How can we proceed?

Recalling the discussion of Sec. 3.14 and Eq. (3.21), since I15 is at infinity, the relative motion between links 5 and 1 is translation, and ω5/1 = 0. Therefore, every point of link 5 has the same absolute velocity, including \(V _{I_{25}}= V_{C}\). Thus, we lay out \(V _{I_{25}}\) on Fig. 3.31. Next, we turn our attention to link 2. We treat I25 as a point of link 2 rotating about I12, and solve for the angular velocity of link 2:

 

\(\left| R _{I_{34} A}\right|=\left|\frac{ \omega _{3 / 4} \times V _{A_{3} / 4}}{\omega_{3 / 4}^{2}}\right|=\left|\hat{\omega}_{3 / 4} \times\left(\frac{ V _{A_{3} / 4}}{\omega_{3 / 4}}\right)\right|=\infty\) (3.21)

 

\(\omega_{2}=\frac{V_{I_{25}}}{R_{I_{25} I_{12}}}=\frac{10 m / s }{0.25 m }=40 rad / s ccw\)

Noticing the apparent paradox between the directions of VC and ω2, we may speculate on the validity of our solution. This is resolved, however, by opening the enclosed housing and discovering that the linkage is as shown in Fig. 3.32.
