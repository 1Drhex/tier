Guest Question.

Consider the partial decomposition of gaseous \(P_4\) according to

\(P_{4(g)}=2P_{2(g)}\)

Calculate

a. The temperature at which \(X_{P_4}=X_{P_2}=0.5\) at a total pressure of 1 atm

b. The total pressure at which \(X_{P_4}=X_{P_2}=0.5\) at 2000 K

given that, for the reaction \(P_{4(g)}=2P_{2(g)}\),

ΔG° = 225,400 + 7.90T lnT − 209.4T J

Admin
Solution

a. Since Δ G° = 225,400 + 7.90T ln T – 209.4T J,

\(\ln K_p=\frac{-27,109}{T} -0.95\ln T+25.18\) (i)

Partial decomposition of 1 mole of \(P_4\) produces (1 – x ) moles of \(P_4\) and 2x moles of \(P_2\). Thus, for \(X_{P_4}=(1-x)/(1+x)=0.5,x=1/3\), we can write

\(P_{P4}=\left\lgroup\frac{1-\frac{1}{3} }{1+\frac{1}{3} } \right\rgroup P=0.5P\)

and

\(P_{P2}=\left\lgroup\frac{2\times \frac{1}{3} }{1+\frac{1}{3} } \right\rgroup P=0.5P\)

Thus, with \(X_{P_4}=X_{P_2}=0.5\) and the pressure P ,

\(K_p=\frac{p^{2}_{P_2} }{p_{P_4}}=0.5P \) (ii)

With P = 1 atm, Equation (i) gives

\(\ln K_p=\ln (0.5)=\frac{-27,109}{T}-0.95\ln T+25.18 \)

which has the solution T = 1429 K. Thus, at P = 1 atm and T = 1429 K, \(X_{P_2}=X_{P_4}=0.5\) in a \(P_4-P_2\) mixture.

b. From Equations (i) and (ii):

\(K_{P,2000 K}=81.83=0.5P\)

which gives P = 163.6 atm. Thus, at P = 163.6 atm and T = 2000 K, \(X_{P_4}=X_{P_2}=0.5\) in the mixture.

The variations of \(X_{P_4}\) and \(X_{P_2}\) with temperature in an equilibrated \(P_4-P_2\) mixture at P = 1 atm and at P = 163.3 atm are shown in Figure 11.5. Since the dissociation of \(P_4\) is endothermic, increasing the temperature at constant pressure shifts the equilibrium toward \(P_2\), and since the dissociation increases the number of moles present, increasing the pressure at constant temperature shifts the equilibrium toward \(P_4\).
