Guest Question.

The kinematic diagram of one of the cylinders of a rotary engine is shown in Fig. 8.34. The crank OA which is vertical and fixed , is 50 mm long. The length of the connecting rod AB is 125 mm. The line of the stroke OB is inclined at \(50^{\circ}\) to the vertical.
The cylinders are rotating at a uniform speed of 300 r.p.m., in a clockwise direction, about the fixed centre O. Determine: 1. acceleration of the piston inside the cylinder, and 2. angular acceleration of the connecting rod.

Admin
Solution

Given: A B = 125 mm = 0.125 m ; \(N_{ CO }=300 \text { r.p.m }\) or \(\omega_{ CO }=2 \pi \times 300 / 60=31.4 rad / s\)

First of all draw the space diagram, as shown in Fig. (a), to some suitable scale. By measurement from the space diagram, we find that

OC = 85 mm = 0.085 m

\(\therefore \) Velocity of C with respect to O,

\(v_{ CO }=\omega_{ CO } \times O C=31.4 \times 0.85=2.7 m / s\) …(Perpendicular to CO)

Now the velocity diagram, as shown in Fig. (b), is drawn as discussed below:

1. Since O and A are fixed points, therefore these are marked at the same place in the velocity diagram. Draw vector oc perpendicular to OC to represent the velocity of C with respect to O i.e. \(v_{ CO }\), such that

\(\text { vector } o c=v_{ CO }=v_{ C }=2.7 m / s\)

2. From point c, draw vector cb parallel to the path of motion of the piston B (which is along CO) to represent the velocity of B with respect to C i.e. \(v_{ BC }\) , and from point a draw vector ab perpendicular to A B to represent the velocity of B with respect to A i.e. \(v_{ BA } \text { or } v_{ B }\).

By measurement, we find that velocity of piston B with respect to coincident point C,

\(v_{ BC }=\text { vector } c b=0.85 m / s\)

and velocity of piston B with respect to A,

\(v_{ BA }=v_{ B }=\text { vector } a b=2.85 m / s\)

 

1. Acceleration of the piston inside the cylinder

We know that the radial component of the acceleration of the coincident point C with respect to O,

\(a_{ CO }^{r}=\frac{v_{ CO }^{2}}{O C}=\frac{(2.7)^{2}}{0.085}=85.76 m / s ^{2}\)

Coriolis component of acceleration of the piston B with respect to the cylinder or coincident point C,

\(a_{ BC }^{c}=2 \omega_{ CO } \times v_{ BC }=2 \times 31.4 \times 0.85=53.4 m / s ^{2}\)

Radial component of acceleration of B with respect to A,

\(a_{ BA }^{r}=\frac{v_{ BA }^{2}}{A B}=\frac{(2.85)^{2}}{0.125}=65 m / s ^{2}\)

The acceleration diagram, as shown in Fig.(d), is drawn as discussed below:

1. Since O and A are fixed points, therefore these are marked as one point in the acceleration diagram. Draw vector o’c’ parallel to OC, to some suitable scale, to represent the radial component of the acceleration of C with respect to O i.e., \(a_{ CO }^{r}\) such that

\(\text { vector } o^{\prime} c^{\prime}=a_{ CO }^{r}=85.76 m / s ^{2}\)

2. The acceleration of piston B with respect to coincident point C has the following two components:

(i) Coriolis component of the acceleration of B with respect to C i.e. \(a_{ BC }^{c}\), and

(ii) Radial component of the acceleration of B with respect to C i.e. \(a_{ BC }^{r}\)

These two components are mutually perpendicular. Therefore from point c’, draw vector c’x perpendicular to CO to represent \(a_{ BC }^{c}=53.4 m / s ^{2}\) in a direction as shown in Fig. (c). The direction of \(a_{ BC }^{c}\) is obtained by rotating vBC (represented by vector cb in velocity diagram) through \(90^{\circ}\) in the same sense as that of \(\omega_{ CO }\) which rotates in the clockwise direction. Now from point x, draw vector xb’ perpendicular to vector c’x (or parallel to OC) to represent \(a_{ BC }^{c}=53.4 m / s ^{2}\) whose magnitude is yet unknown.

3. The acceleration of B with respect to A has also the following two components:

(i) Radial component of the acceleration of B with respect to A i.e. \(a_{ BA }^{r}\), and

(ii) Tangential component of the acceleration of B with respect to A i.e.\(a_{ BA }^{t}\).

These two components are mutually perpendicular. Therefore from point a’, draw vector a’y parallel to A B to represent \(a_{ BA }^{r}=65 m / s ^{2}\) and from point y draw vector yb’ perpendicular to vector a’y to represent \(a_{ BA }^{t}\). The vectors xb’ and yb’ intersect at b’.

4. Join c’b’ and a’b’. The vector c’b’ represents the acceleration of B with respect to C (i.e. acceleration of the piston inside the cylinder).

By measurement, we find that acceleration of the piston inside the cylinder,

\(a_{ BC }=\text { vector } c^{\prime} b^{\prime}=73.2 m / s ^{2}\)

 

2. Angular acceleration of the connecting rod

By measurement from acceleration diagram, we find that the tangential component of the acceleration of B with respect to A,

\(a_{ BA }^{t}=\text { vector } y b^{\prime}=37.6 m / s ^{2}\)

\(\therefore \) Angular acceleration of the connecting rod A B,

\(\alpha_{ AB }=\frac{a_{ BA }^{t}}{A B}=\frac{37.6}{0.125}=301 rad / s ^{2}(\text { Clockwise })\)
