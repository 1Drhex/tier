Guest Question.

Let \( \left\{X(t)\right\} \) denote the response of the nonlinear oscillator of and with the equation of motion of

\( m\ddot{X}(t)+c\dot{X}(t)+k_{1}X(t)+k_{3}X^{3}(t)=F(t) \)

but with \( \left\{F(t)\right\} \) being a mean-zero, stationary Gaussian process with a nonwhite autospoctral density of

\( S_{FF}(\omega )=S_{0}U(0.7\omega _{0}-\left|\omega \right| ) \)

in which \(ω_{0}\) is defined as \( \omega _{0}=(k_{1}/m)^{1/2} \) . That is, \( S_{FF}(\omega )=S_{0} \) for \( \left|\omega \right| \lt 0.7\omega _{0} \) and equals zero otherwise. Using statistical linearization, estimate the variance of the stationary \( \left\{X(t)\right\} \) and \( \left\{\dot{X}(t)\right\} \) responses for the special case of

\( k_{3}=2ck^{2}_{1}/(\pi S_{0}) \) and \( \zeta _{0}\equiv c/(2m\omega _{0})=0.05 \)

Admin
Solution

For this excitation we are not able to use simple closed-form analytical solutions of the linearized system. Thus, we use iteration in finding a solution. We can directly use the linearized model found in :

\( m\ddot{X}(t)+c\dot{X}(t)+b_{1}X(t)=F(t) \)

with \( b_{1}=k_{1}+k_{3}E(X^{4})/\sigma ^{2}_{X} \) . We also use the assumption that \( X(t) \) is approximately Gaussian to convert this into \( b_{1}=k_{1}+3k_{3}\sigma ^{2}_{X} \) . We do not have a closed-form solution for the response variance for this linearized problem, so we use the harmonic transfer function of \( H_{x}(\omega )=[b_{1}-m\omega ^{2}+2ic\omega ]^{-1} \) and integrate this to obtain

\( \sigma ^{2}_{X}=S_{0}\int_{-0.7\omega _{0}}^{0.7\omega _{0}}{\left|H_{x}(\omega )\right|^{2} } d\omega =2S_{0}\int_{0}^{0.7\omega _{0}}{\frac{d\omega }{(b_{1}-m\omega ^{2})^{2}+(2c\omega )^{2}}} \)

For convenience, we use a nondimensional frequency of \( \eta =\omega /\omega _{0} \) to obtain

\( \sigma ^{2}_{X}=\frac{2S_{0}\omega _{0}}{b^{2}_{1}}\int_{0}^{0.7}{\left(\left[1-\frac{k_{1}\eta ^{2}}{b_{1}} \right]^{2}+\left[\frac{2k_{1}\xi _{0}\eta }{b_{1}} \right]^{2} \right)^{-1} } d\eta \)

To begin the iterative process we simply neglect the \( k_{3} \) term, taking \( b_{1}=k_{1} \) and numerically evaluating the integral to obtain \( \sigma ^{2}_{X}=(2S_{0}\omega _{0}/k^{2}_{1})(1.114) \) . Using this value along with the given value of \( k_{3} \) gives an estimate of \( b_{1}=k_{1}+3k_{3}\sigma ^{2}_{X}=k_{1}(1.142) \) . Using this value in a new evaluation of the frequency integral gives an improved estimate of the response variance as \( \sigma ^{2}_{X}=(2S_{0}\omega _{0}/k^{2}_{1})(1.029) \) . This, in turn, gives an improved estimate of the linearization parameter of \( b_{1}=k_{1}+3k_{3}\sigma ^{2}_{X}=k_{1}(1.131) \) . Carrying out two more steps of the iteration gives \( \sigma ^{2}_{X}=(2S_{0}\omega _{0}/k^{2}_{1})(1.034) \) and \( b_{1}=k_{1}+3k_{3}\sigma ^{2}_{X}=k_{1}(1.132) \) then \( \sigma ^{2}_{X}=(2S_{0}\omega _{0}/k^{2}_{1})(1.033) \) and \( b_{1}=k_{1}+3k_{3}\sigma ^{2}_{X}=k_{1}(1.132) \) . This represents convergence, so our estimate of the response variance is

\( \sigma ^{2}_{X}=\frac{2S_{0}\omega _{0}}{k^{2}_{1}}(1.033)=\frac{2.067 S_{0}}{(k^{3}_{1}m)^{1/2}} \)

For this linear system we know that \( \sigma ^{2}_{\dot{X}}=(a_{1}/m)\sigma ^{2}_{X} \) , so our estimate of \( \sigma ^{2}_{\dot{X}} \) . is

\( \sigma ^{2}_{\dot{X}}=\left(\frac{1.132k_{1}}{m} \right)\left( \frac{2.067 S_{0}}{(k^{3}_{1}m)^{1/2}}\right)=\frac{2.340S_{0}}{(k_{1}m^{3})^{1/2}} \)
