Guest Question.

A small cube (edge length 2a) is removed from a large cube (edge length 4a), as shown in Fig. 4.5a.
a) Locate the center of gravity of the remaining body.
b) Determine the center of gravity if the small cube is replaced by a cube with the same size but of different material (density \(\varrho _{2} = 2 \varrho _{1})\), as shown in Fig. 4.5b.

Admin
Solution

In both cases the body can be considered to be composed of several parts. In Problem a), the material is homogeneous (constant density \(\varrho _{1}\)). Therefore, the position of the center of gravity can be determined from (4.10). In Problem b), the densities of the two parts of the body are different. Therefore, Equations (4.9) have to be used.

\(x_{c}=\frac{x_{i}\varrho _{i}V_{i}}{\sum \varrho _{i}V_{i}},\)

\(y_{c}=\frac{y_{i}\varrho _{i}V_{i}}{\sum \varrho _{i}V_{i}},\) (4.9)

\(z_{c}=\frac{z_{i}\varrho _{i}V_{i}}{\sum \varrho _{i}V_{i}}\).

The body as shown in Fig. 4.5a is chosen as the first part. Its center of gravity is known from Problem a). The second part is the small cube, having the density \(\varrho _{2} = 2 \varrho_{1}\):
