Guest Question.

The composite shape with dimensions in millimeters shown in Fig. 12.4 consists of a rectangular solid, less a circular through hole, plus a triangular plate of a different thickness. Determine the center of mass of this shape, which is made of a homogeneous material with density of ρ kg/(mm)3.

Admin
Solution

We can find the masses and the centers of mass of each of the three subshapes with the help of the formulae in Appendix A. For the rectangular solid subshape, we obtain*

\(m_{1}=(200 mm )(40 mm )(80 mm )\left(\rho kg /( mm )^{3}\right)=640000 \rho kg\)

\(R _{G_{1}}=100 \hat{ i }+20 \hat{ j }-40 \hat{ k } mm\)

For the hole, we treat the mass as negative, giving

\(m_{2}=\pi(20 mm )^{2}(40 mm )\left(-\rho kg /( mm )^{3}\right)=-50265 \rho kg\)

 

\(R _{G_{2}}=160 \hat{ i }+20 \hat{ j }-40 \hat{ k } mm\)

Finally, for the triangular subshape, we find

\(m_{3}=0.5(60 mm )(90 mm )(20 mm )\left[\rho kg /( mm )^{3}\right]=54000 \rho kg\)

 

\(R _{G_{3}}=30 \hat{ i }+10 \hat{ j }+20 \hat{ k } mm\)

Now since we are completing a process of integration, we can combine the values of the subshapes; that is, from Eq. (12.5) we can write

\(R _{G}=\frac{m_{1} R _{1}+m_{2} R _{2}+m_{3} R _{3}+m_{4} R _{4}}{m_{1}+m_{2}+m_{3}+m_{4}}=\frac{\sum m_{i} R _{i}}{\sum m_{i}}\) (12.5)

\(R _{G}=\frac{m_{1} R _{1}+m_{2} R _{2}+m_{3} R _{3}}{m_{1}+m_{2}+m_{3}}\)

This gives the following result:

\(R _{G}=89.4 \hat{ i }+19.2 \hat{ j }-35.0 \hat{ k } mm\)

* Note that in this example, we use SI units. US customary units, pounds, or other units might have been used. However, this is not emphasized here, because the density cancels in the use of Eq. (12.5).
