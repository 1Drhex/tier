Guest Question.

A compound shaft drives several pulleys, as shown in Figure P6.19. Segments (1) and (2) of the compound shaft are hollow aluminum [G = 4,000 ksi] tubes, which have an outside diameter of 3.00 in. and a wall thickness of 0.125 in. Segments (3) and (4) are solid 1.50-in.-diameter steel [G = 12,000 ksi] shafts. The bearings shown allow the shaft to turn freely.
(a) Determine the maximum shear stress in the compound shaft.
(b) Determine the rotation angle of flange C with respect to pulley A.
(c) Determine the rotation angle of pulley E with respect to pulley A.

Admin
Solution

(a) Equilibrium

\(\Sigma M_{x}=95 lb – ft -T_{4}=0 \quad \therefore T_{4}=95 lb – ft\)

\(\Sigma M_{x}=95 lb – ft -270 lb – ft -T_{3}=0 \quad \therefore T_{3}=-175 lb – ft\)

Note: The internal torque in shaft segment (2) is the same as
in segment (3); therefore, \(T_{2}=-175 lb – ft\).

\(\Sigma M_{x}=T_{1}-650 lb – ft =0 \quad \therefore T_{1}=650 lb – ft\)

Polar moments of inertia in the shaft segments will be needed for this calculation. Segments (1) and (2) are hollow tubes with an outside diameter of 3.00 in. and an inside diameter of 3.00 in. â€“ 2(0.125 in.) = 2.75 in. The polar moment of inertia of segments (1) and (2) is:

\(J_{1}=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[D_{1}^{4}-d_{1}^{4}\right]=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[(3.00 in .)^{4}-(2.75 in .)^{4}\right]=2.33740 in. ^{4}=J_{2}\)

Segments (3) and (4) are solid 1.50-in.-diameter shafts, which have a polar moment of inertia of:

\(J_{3}=\frac{\pi}{32} d_{3}^{4}=\frac{\pi}{32}(1.50 in .)^{4}=0.49701 in .{ }^{4}=J_{4}\)

Shear stresses: The shear stresses in each segment can be calculated by the elastic torsion formula:

\(\begin{aligned}&\tau_{1}=\frac{T_{1} c_{1}}{J_{1}}=\frac{(650 lb – ft )(1.50 in .)(12 in. / ft )}{2.33740 in .^{4}}=5,005.56 psi =5,010 psi \\&\tau_{2}=\frac{T_{2} c_{2}}{J_{2}}=\frac{(-175 lb – ft )(1.50 in .)(12 in. / ft )}{2.33740 in .^{4}}=-1,347.6 psi =-1,348 psi \\&\tau_{3}=\frac{T_{3} c_{3}}{J_{3}}=\frac{(-175 lb – ft )(0.75 in .)(12 in. / ft )}{0.49701 in .{ }^{4}}=-3,169.0 psi =-3,170 psi \\&\tau_{4}=\frac{T_{4} c_{4}}{J_{4}}=\frac{(95 lb – ft )(0.75 in .)(12 in. / ft )}{0.49701 in .^{4}}=1,720.3 psi =1,720 psi\end{aligned}\)

The maximum shear stress in the compound shaft is \(\tau_{\max }=\tau_{1}=5,010 psi\)

Angles of twist:

\(\begin{aligned}&\phi_{1}=\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}=\frac{(650 lb – ft )(72 in .)(12 in . / ft )}{\left(2.33740 in .^{4}\right)\left(4,000,000 lb / in .^{2}\right)}=0.060067 rad \\&\phi_{2}=\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}=\frac{(-175 lb – ft )(36 in .)(12 in. / ft )}{\left(2.33740 in .^{4}\right)\left(4,000,000 lb / in .{ }^{2}\right)}=-0.008086 rad \\&\phi_{3}=\frac{T_{3} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}=\frac{(-175 lb – ft )(54 in .)(12 in . / ft )}{\left(0.49701 in .^{4}\right)\left(12,000,000 lb / in .^{2}\right)}=-0.019014 rad \\&\phi_{4}=\frac{T_{4} L_{4}}{J_{4} G_{4}}=\frac{(95 lb – ft )(54 in .)(12 in. / ft )}{\left(0.49701 in .^{4}\right)\left(12,000,000 lb / in .^{2}\right)}=0.010322 rad\end{aligned}\)

Rotation angles: The angles of twist can be defined in terms of the rotation angles at the ends of each segment:

\(\phi_{1}=\phi_{B}-\phi_{A} \quad \phi_{2}=\phi_{C}-\phi_{B} \quad \phi_{3}=\phi_{D}-\phi_{C} \quad \phi_{4}=\phi_{E}-\phi_{D}\)

The origin of the coordinate system is located at pulley A. We will arbitrarily define the rotation angle at pulley A to be zero \(\left(\phi_{A}=0\right)\). The rotation angle at B can be calculated from the angle of twist in segment (1):

\(\begin{aligned}&\phi_{1}=\phi_{B}-\phi_{A} \\&\therefore \phi_{B}=\phi_{A}+\phi_{1}=0+0.060067 rad =0.0601 rad\end{aligned}\)

Similarly, the rotation angle at C is determined from the angle of twist in segment (2) and the rotation angle of pulley B:

\(\begin{aligned}&\phi_{2}=\phi_{C}-\phi_{B} \\&\therefore \phi_{C}=\phi_{B}+\phi_{2}=0.060067 rad +(-0.008086 rad )=0.051981 rad =0.0520 rad\end{aligned}\)

The rotation angle at D is:

\(\begin{aligned}&\phi_{3}=\phi_{D}-\phi_{C} \\&\therefore \phi_{D}=\phi_{C}+\phi_{3}=0.051981 rad +(-0.019014 rad )=0.032967 rad =0.0330 rad\end{aligned}\)

and the rotation angle at E is:

\(\begin{aligned}&\phi_{4}=\phi_{E}-\phi_{D} \\&\therefore \phi_{E}=\phi_{D}+\phi_{4}=0.032967 rad +0.010322 rad =0.043289 rad =0.0433 rad\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Rotation angle of flange C with respect to pulley A: Using the rotation angles determined for the system, the rotation angle of flange C with respect to pulley A is simply:

\(\phi_{C}=0.051981 rad =0.0520 rad\)

 

(c) Rotation angle of pulley E with respect to pulley A: Using the rotation angles determined for the system, the rotation angle of flange C with respect to pulley A is simply:

\(\phi_{E}=0.043289 rad =0.0433 rad\)
