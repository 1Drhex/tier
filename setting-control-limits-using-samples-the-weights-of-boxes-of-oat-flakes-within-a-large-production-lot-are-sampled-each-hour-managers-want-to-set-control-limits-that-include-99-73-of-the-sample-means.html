Guest Question.

SETTING CONTROL LIMITS USING SAMPLES. The weights of boxes of Oat Flakes within a large production lot are sampled each hour. Managers want to set control limits that include 99.73% of the sample means.
APPROACH \(\blacktriangleright\) Randomly select and weigh nine (n = 9) boxes each hour. Then find the overall mean and use Equations (S6-3) and (S6-4) to compute the control limits. Here are the nine boxes chosen for Hour 1 in Figure 6.S1a.

Upper control limit (UCL) = \(\bar{\bar{x}} + z \sigma_{\bar{x}}\) (S6-3)
Lower control limit (LCL) = \(\bar{\bar{x}} - z \sigma_{\bar{x}}\) (S6-4)

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION \(\blacktriangleright\)

The average weight in the first hourly sample = \(\frac{17 + 13 + 16 + 18 + 17 + 16 + 15 + 17 + 16}{9}\) = 16.1 ounces.

Also, the population (process) standard deviation (σ) is known to be 1 ounce. We do not show each of the boxes randomly selected in hours 2 through 12, but here are all 12 hourly samples in Figure 6.S1b.

The average mean \(\bar{\bar{x}}\) of the 12 samples is calculated to be exactly 16 ounces \(\left[\bar{\bar{x}} = \frac{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{12}{(Avg. of 9 Boxes)}}{12}\right]\).

We therefore have \(\bar{\bar{x}}\) = 16 ounces, σ = 1 ounce, n = 9, and z = 3. The control limits are:

\(UCL_{\bar{x}} = \bar{\bar{x}} + z \sigma_{\bar{x}} = 16 + 3\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{9}}\right) = 16 + 3\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)\) = 17 ounces

\(LCL_{\bar{x}} = \bar{\bar{x}} – z \sigma_{\bar{x}} = 16 – 3\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{9}}\right) = 16 – 3\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)\) = 15 ounces

The 12 samples are then plotted on the following control chart in Figure 6.S1c.

INSIGHT \(\blacktriangleright\) Because the means of recent sample averages fall outside the upper and lower control limits of 17 and 15, we can conclude that the process is becoming erratic and is not in control.

LEARNING EXERCISE \(\blacktriangleright\) If Oat Flakes’s population standard deviation (σ) is 2 (instead of 1), what is your conclusion? [Answer: LCL = 14, UCL = 18; the process would be in control.]
