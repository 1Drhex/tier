Guest Question.

Material-removal rate and cutting force in turning

A 125 mm-long, 10 mm-diameter 304 stainless-steel rod is being reduced in diameter to 9 mm by turning on a lathe. The spindle rotates at N = 360 rpm, and the tool is traveling at an axial speed of 1.75 mm/min. Calculate the cutting speed, material-removal rate, cutting time, power dissipated, and cutting force.

Admin
Solution

The cutting speed is the tangential speed of the workpiece. The maximum
cutting speed is at the outer diameter, \( D_{o}\), and is obtained from the expression

\(V = \pi D_{\omicron }N.\)

Thus

\(=(\pi )(10)(1000)(360) = 11.3\) m/min.

The cutting speed at the machined diameter is

\(=\pi \left\lgroup\frac{9}{1000} \right\rgroup (360)=10.2\) m /min.

From the information given, note that the depth of cut is

\(d=\frac{(10-9)}{2}=0.5 \) mm

and the feed is

\(f=\frac{175}{360} =0.49\) mm/ rev.

Thus, according to Eq. (8.38),

\(MRR=\pi D_{avg}dfN.\) (8.38)

the material-removal rate is

MRR =\(\pi (9.5)(0.5)(0.49)(360)= 2610.08\) \(mm^{3}\)/min.

The actual time to cut, according to Eq. (8.39)

\(t=\frac{l}{fN}. \) (8.39)

is

\(t=\frac{125}{(0.5) (360)}=0.70 \) min.

We can calculate the power required by referring to Table 8.3

Table 8.3,

Approximate Specific-Energy Requirements in Machining Operations
Material Specific energy* (W-s/mm\(^{3}\))
Aluminum alloys 0.4–1.1
Cast irons 1.6–5.5
Copper alloys 1.4–3.3
High-temperature alloys 3.3–8.5
Magnesium alloys 0.4–0.6
Nickel alloys 4.9–6.8
Refractory alloys 3.8–9.6
Stainless steels 3.0–5.2
Steels 2.7–9.3
Titanium alloys 3.0–4.1
*At drive motor, corrected for 80% efficiency; multiply the energy by 1.25 for dull tools.

and taking an average value for stainless steel as 4 W-s/\(mm^3\). Therefore, the power dissipated is

Power = \((4)\left\lgroup\frac{2610}{60} \right\rgroup=178 \) N-m s,

and since the power dissipated is 178 N-m/s. The cutting force, \(F_{c}\), is the tangential force exerted by the tool. Since power is the product of torque, T, and rotational speed in radians per unit time, we have

\(T=\frac{(178)}{\left\lgroup\frac{360}{60} \right\rgroup (2\pi )}= 4.72 \) N-m.

Since \(T=(F_{c})(D_{avg}/2)\), we obtain

\(F_{c}=\frac{(4.72)(2)\times 1000}{9.5}=994 \) N.
