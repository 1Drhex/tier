Guest Question.

Air enters the diffuser of a compressor with a velocity of 300 m/s at a stagnation pressure of 200 kPa and a stagnation temperature of 200°C and leaves the diffuser with a velocity of 50 m/s. Using compressible flow relations and assuming the diffuser efficiency, \(\eta _{D} =0.9\), determine
a. the static temperatures at inlet and outlet of the diffuser and the inlet Mach number;
b. the static pressure at diffuser inlet;
c. the increase in entropy caused by the diffusion process.
Take \(\gamma =1.4\) and \(C_{p} =1005 J/kg K\)

Admin
Solution

Note: When solving diffuser problems it is advisable and always useful to make a sketch of the
Mollier diagram of the diffusion process. In this case, we refer to Figure 7.25.
The expression for the diffuser efficiency, which is most useful for this example, is Eq. (7.53):

\(\eta _{D} =\left(T_{2s}/T_{1}-1\right)/\left(T_{2}/T_{1}-1\right) \)

From the energy equation, \(h_{01}-h_{1} =\left(1/2\right)c^{2}_{1}\) we obtain,

 

\( \frac{T_{1}}{T_{01} }=1-\frac{c^{2}_{1}}{2C_{p}T_{01} } =1-\frac{300^{2}}{2\times 1050\times 473} =0.90533\)

Therefore,

\(T_{1}=428.2 K \) as \(T = 473 K \)

The Mach number at diffuser entry is

\(M_{1} =c_{1}/a_{1} \) where \(a_{1}=\sqrt{\gamma R T_{1}} =\sqrt{1.4\times 287\times 428.2}=414.8 m/s\)

Therefore,

\(M_{1} =0.7233.\)

Again, from the energy equation, \(h_{02}-h_{2} =\left(1/2\right)c^{2}_{2}\) , hence,

 

\( \frac{T_{2}}{T_{02} }=1-\frac{c^{2}_{2}}{2C_{p}T_{02} } =1-\frac{50^{2}}{2\times 1050\times 473} =0.9974\)

and

\(T_{2} =471.7 K\)

From the diffuser efficiency definition above, we get

 

\( \frac{T_{2s}}{T_{1} }=\eta _{D}\left(\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1} }-1\right) +1=1+0.9\left(\frac{471.7}{428.2} -1\right)=1.0915\)

 

\( \frac{p_{2}}{p_{1} }=\left(\frac{T_{2s}}{T_{1} }\right)^{\gamma /\left(\gamma -1\right) } =1.0915^{3.5} =1.3588 \)

 

\( \frac{p_{01}}{p_{1} }=\left(\frac{T_{02}}{T_{1} }\right)^{\gamma /\left(\gamma -1\right) } =\left(\frac{473}{428.2}\right)^{3.5} =1.4166 \)

Therefore,

\( p_{1}=141.2 kPa\)

and

\( p_{2}=1.3588 \times 141.2 =191.8 kPa\)

From the thermodynamic relation, namely \(T ds = dh – \left(1/\rho \right) dp\) , we obtain

 

\( s_{2}-s_{1}= C_{p}\ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}- R \ln \frac{p_{2}}{p_{1} }=1005\ln \frac{471.7}{428.2}-287\ln 1.3588=97.2-88.0=9.2 J/kg K\)
