Guest Question.

A system of three pin connected bars takes the depicted equilibri-um position under the given loading with the forces \(F_{1}\) and \(F_{2}\). a) Determine the required ratio of \(F_{1}/F_{2}\) for this case. b) Calculate the forces \(S_{1}, S_{2}\) and \(S_{3}\) in the bars?

Admin
Solution

We isolate the joints \(G_{1}\) and \(G_{2}\) and obtain two central sys-tems of forces, for which we have two equilibrium conditions each. From the free-body diagram of \(G_{1}\) we obtain

\(\rightarrow : -S_{1}\cos \alpha +S_{2}\cos \beta =0,\)

 

\(\uparrow : S_{1}\sin \alpha -S_{2}\sin \beta-F_{1} =0.\)

This results in the relations

\(S_{1}=S_{2}\frac{\cos \beta }{\cos \alpha }, \)

 

\(S_{2}\cos \beta \frac{\sin \alpha }{\cos \alpha }-S_{2}\sin \beta -F_{1}=0 \rightsquigarrow S_{2}=\frac{F_{1}}{\cos \beta \tan \alpha -\sin \beta }.\)

From the equilibrium conditions at \(G_{2}\)

\(\rightarrow : -S_{2}\cos \beta +S_{3}\cos \delta =0,\)

 

\(\uparrow : S_{2}\sin \beta +S_{3}\sin \delta -F_{2}=0,\)

it follows

\(S_{3}=S_{2}\frac{\cos \beta }{\cos \delta },\)

 

\(S_{2}\sin \beta +S_{2}\cos \beta \frac{\sin \delta }{\cos \delta }-F_{2}=0 \rightsquigarrow S_{2}=\frac{F_{2}}{\sin \beta +\cos \beta \tan \delta }.\)

Thus, we have two solutions for \(S_{2},\) one given as a function of the force \(F_{1}\) and one as a function of \(F_{2}.\) The ratio of the forces \(F_{1}/F_{2}\) results

from equating the two solutions:

\(\frac{F_{1}}{\cos \beta \tan \alpha -\sin \beta }=\frac{F_{2}}{\sin \beta +\cos \beta \tan \delta },\)

 

\( \rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{\frac{F_{1}}{F_{2}} } }=\frac{\cos \beta \tan \alpha -\sin \beta }{\sin \beta +\cos \beta \tan \delta }=\underline{\underline{\frac{\tan \alpha -\tan \beta }{\tan \beta +\tan \delta }. } }\)

From the geometry of the equilibrium position, we obtain for the angles:

\(\tan \alpha =\frac{3a}{2a}=\frac{3}{2}, \sin \alpha =\frac{3}{\sqrt{13} }, \cos \alpha =\frac{2}{\sqrt{13} },\)

 

\(\tan \beta =\frac{a}{3a}=\frac{1}{3}, \sin \beta =\frac{1}{\sqrt{10} }, \cos \beta =\frac{3}{\sqrt{10} },\)

 

\(\tan \delta =\frac{4a}{2a}=2, \sin \delta=\frac{2}{\sqrt{5} }, \cos \delta =\frac{1}{\sqrt{5} }.\)

This results in

\(\underline{\underline{\frac{F_{1}}{F_{2}} } }=\frac{\frac{3}{2}-\frac{1}{3} }{\frac{1}{3}-2 }=\frac{9-2}{2+12}=\underline{\underline{\frac{1}{2} } }.\)

For the forces in the bars, it follows

\(\underline{\underline{S_{2}} }=\frac{F_{1}}{\frac{3}{\sqrt{10} }\frac{3}{2}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{10} } }=\frac{2\sqrt{10} } {7}F_{1}=\underline{\underline{0.903F_{1}} },\)

 

\(\underline{\underline{S_{1}} }=\frac{3}{\sqrt{10} }\frac{\sqrt{13} } {2}S_{2}=\underline{\underline{1.545F_{1}} }, \underline{\underline{S_{3}} }=\frac{3}{\sqrt{10} }\frac{\sqrt{5} } {1}S_{2}=\underline{\underline{1.916F_{1}} }.\)

The solution of the problem can also be obtained graphically. First we sketch the closed triangle of forces with \(F_{1}, S_{1}\) and \(S_{2}\) for \(G_{1}\) with the given angles. Then we sketch the according triangle of forces for \(G_{2}\) with the same force scaling (the magni-tude of \(S_{2}\) has to be equal and the direction of rotation must be opposite). From both tri-angles of forces we obtain by inspection the ratio of the magnitudes of the forces \(F_{1}\) and \(F_{2}:\)

\(\underline{\underline{\frac{F_{1}}{F_{2}}\approx 0.5 } }.\)
