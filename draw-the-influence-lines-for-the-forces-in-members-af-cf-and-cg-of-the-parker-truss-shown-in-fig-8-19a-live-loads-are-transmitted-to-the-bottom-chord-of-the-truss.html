Guest Question.

Draw the influence lines for the forces in members \(AF\), \(CF\), and \(CG\) of the Parker truss shown in Fig. \( 8.19 (\mathrm{a})\). Live loads are transmitted to the bottom chord of the truss.

Admin
Solution

Influence Lines for Reactions. The influence lines for the reactions \(A_{y}\) and \(E_{y}\) (Fig. \(8.19(\) b \(\left.)\right)\), obtained by applying the equilibrium equations, \(\sum M_{E}=0\) and \(\sum M_{A}=0\), respectively, to the free body of the entire truss, are shown in Fig. \(8.19(\mathrm{c})\) and (d).

Influence Line for \( \boldsymbol F_{A F} .\) The expressions for \(F_{A F}\) can be determined by applying the equilibrium equation \(\sum F_{y}=0\) to the freebody diagram of joint \(A\) shown in Fig. 8.19(e). When the \(1 \mathrm{k}\) load is located at joint \(A\), we write

\( +\uparrow \sum F_{y}=0 \quad A_{y}-1+\frac{3}{5} F_{A F}=0 \)

Because \(A_{y}=1 \mathrm{k}\) (see Fig. \(8.19(\mathrm{c})\) ), we obtain

\( F_{A F}=0 \quad \text { for } x=0 \)

When the \(1 \mathrm{k}\) load is located to the right of joint \(B\), we write

\( \begin{aligned} +\uparrow \sum F_{y}=0 \quad A_{y}+\frac{3}{5} F_{A F} &=0 \\ F_{A F} &=-1.667 A_{y} \quad 20 \mathrm{ft} \leq x \leq 80 \mathrm{ft} \end{aligned} \)

Thus, the segment of the influence line for \(F_{A F}\) between \(B\) and \(E\) is obtained by multiplying the corresponding segment of the influence line for \(A_{y}\) by \(-1.667\), as shown in Fig. 8.19(f). The ordinates at \(A\) and \(B\) are then connected by a straight line to complete the influence line as shown in the figure.

Influence Line for \( \boldsymbol F_{C F}\). The expressions for \(F_{C F}\) can be determined by passing a section \(a a\) through the members \(B C, C F\), and \(F G\) as shown in Fig. \(8.19\) (b). The free-body diagrams of the two portions of the truss thus obtained are shown in Fig. 8.19(g). The lines of action of \(F_{F G}\) and \(F_{B C}\) intersect at point \(O\), so the equilibrium equation \(\sum M_{o}=0\) will contain only one unknown, namely, \(F_{C F}\). Because the slope of member \(F G\) is \(1: 4\), the distance \(O B=4(F B)=4(15)=60 \mathrm{ft}\). Thus, the distance \(O A=O B-A B=60-20=40 \mathrm{ft}\) as shown in Fig. \(8.19(\mathrm{~g})\). When the \(1 \mathrm{k}\) load is located to the left of \(B\), we apply the equilibrium equation \(\sum M_{o}=0\) to the free body of the right portion \(C E\) of the truss to obtain

\( \begin{aligned} +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{o} &=0 \\ \frac{3}{5} F_{C F}(80)+E_{y}(120) &=0 \\ F_{C F} &=-2.5 E_{y} \quad 0 \leq x \leq 20 \mathrm{ft} \end{aligned} \)

When the \(1 \mathrm{k}\) load is located to the right of \(C\), we consider the equilibrium of the left portion \(A B\) to obtain

\( \begin{aligned} +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{o} &=0 \\ A_{y}(40)-\frac{4}{5} F_{C F}(15)-\frac{3}{5} F_{C F}(60) &=0 \\ F_{C F} &=0.833 A_{y} \quad 40 \mathrm{ft} \leq x \leq 80 \mathrm{ft} \end{aligned} \)

The segments of the influence line for \(F_{C F}\) between \(A\) and \(B\) and between \(C\) and \(E\) are constructed by using the influence lines for \(E_{y}\) and \(A_{y}\), respectively, in accordance with the preceding expressions. The ordinates at \(B\) and \(C\) are then connected by a straight line to complete the influence line, as shown in Fig. 8.19(h).

Influence Line for \( \boldsymbol F_{C G} .\) We will determine the influence line for \(F_{C G}\) by considering the equilibrium of joint \(G\). By applying the equations of equilibrium to the free-body diagram of joint \(G\) (Fig. 8.19(i)), we write

\( \begin{aligned} +\uparrow \sum F_{y} &=0 \\ -F_{C G}-\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{17}}\right) F_{F G}-\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{17}}\right) F_{G H} &=0 \\ \end{aligned} \)

\( \begin{aligned} F_{C G} &=-\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{17}}\right)\left(F_{F G}+F_{G H}\right) \\ \end{aligned} \) (1)

\( \begin{aligned} +\rightarrow \sum F_{x} &=0 \\ -\left(\frac{4}{\sqrt{17}}\right) F_{F G}+\left(\frac{4}{\sqrt{17}}\right) F_{G H} &=0 \\ F_{G H} &=F_{F G} \end{aligned} \) (2)

By substituting Eq. (2) into Eq. (1), we obtain

\( F_{C G}=-\left(\frac{2}{\sqrt{17}}\right) F_{F G}=-0.485 F_{F G} \) (3)

Note that Eq. (3), which is valid for any position of the unit load, indicates that the influence line for \(F_{C G}\) can be obtained by multiplying the influence line for \(F_{F G}\) by \(-0.485\). Thus we will first construct the influence line for \(F_{F G}\) by using section \(a a\) (Fig. \(8.19(\mathrm{~g}))\) and then apply Eq. (3) to obtain the desired influence line for \(F_{C G}\).

It can be seen from Fig. \(8.19(\mathrm{~g})\) that when the \(1 \mathrm{k}\) load is located to the left of \(B\), the expression for \(F_{F G}\) can be determined by applying the equilibrium equation \(\sum M_{C}=0\) to the free body of the right portion \(C E\) of the truss. Thus,

\(\begin{aligned}+\curvearrowleft \sum M_{C} &=0 \\\left(\frac{4}{\sqrt{17}}\right) F_{F G}(20)+E_{y}(40) &=0 \\ F_{F G} &=-2.062 E_{y} \quad 0 \leq x \leq 20 \mathrm{ft} \end{aligned}\)

When the \(1 \mathrm{k}\) load is located to the right of \(C\), we consider the equilibrium of the left portion \(A B\) to obtain

\( \begin{aligned} +\curvearrowleft \sum M_{C} &=0 \\ -\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{17}}\right) F_{F G}(20)-\left(\frac{4}{\sqrt{17}}\right) F_{F G}(15)-A_{y}(40) &=0 \\ F_{F G} &=-2.062 A_{y} \quad 40 \mathrm{ft} \leq x \leq 80 \mathrm{ft} \end{aligned} \)

The influence line for \(F_{F G}\), constructed by using the preceding expressions, is shown in Fig. 8.19(j).

The desired influence line for \(F_{C G}\) can now be obtained by multiplying the influence line for \(F_{F G}\) by \(-0.485\), in accordance with Eq. (3). The influence line for \(F_{C G}\) thus obtained is shown in Fig. 8.19(k).

The influence line for \(F_{C G}\) can also be constructed by considering the section \(b b\) shown in Fig. 8.19(b). By summing moments about the point of intersection of the axes of members \(B C\) and \(G H\), we can determine the expressions for \(F_{C G}\) in terms of \(F_{C F}\) and \(A_{y}\) or \(E_{y}\), whose influence lines are known. The influence line for \(F_{C G}\) can then be constructed by plotting these expressions. The reader is encouraged to check the influence line for \(F_{C G}\) shown in Fig. 8.19(k) by employing this alternative approach.
