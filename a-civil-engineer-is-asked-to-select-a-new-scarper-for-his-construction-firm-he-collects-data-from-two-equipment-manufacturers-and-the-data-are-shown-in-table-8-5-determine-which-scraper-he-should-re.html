Guest Question.

A civil engineer is asked to select a new scarper for his construction firm. He collects data from two equipment manufacturers and the data are shown in Table 8.5. Determine which scraper he should recommend based on equivalent uniform annual worth analysis using an interest rate of 9%. Figures 8.10 and 8.11 are the cash flow diagrams for the two scarpers.

TABLE 8.5
Data for Scraper Alternatives

Cost or Disbursements Scraper 1 Scraper 2
Initial cost $1,600,000.00 $1,200,000.00
Cost to operate per hour $120.00 per hour $150.00 per hour
Number of hours used per year 2,000 hours 2,000 hours
Increase in operating cost $1,000.00 per year starting in year 7 until year 12 $1,500.00 per year starting in year 7 until year 10
Repairs $15,000.00 years 6 through 9 $20,000.00 years 6 through 9
Major overhaul $200,000.00 at year 5 $270,000.00 at year 5
Salvage value $260,000.00 $200,000.00
Life in years 12 10
Admin
Solution

Scraper 1

The yearly operating cost is the cost per hour times the number of hours per year the scraper is operated during the year:

Operating cost = \(\frac{\$120.00 }{h0ur}\times \frac{2,000 hour}{year} = \$240,000.00\) per year

\(EUAW_1=P_0(A/P ,i ,n ) +A_1 +A_2 (P/A ,i ,n )(P/F ,i ,n )(A/P ,i ,n ) + G(F/G ,i ,n )(A/F ,i ,n ) +F_5(P/F ,i ,n )(A/P ,i ,n ) + F_{12}(A/F ,i ,n )\)

= – $1,160,000.00(A/P ,9 ,12 ) – $ 240,000.00 – $ 15,000.00 (P/A ,9 ,4 )(P/F ,9,5 )(A/P ,9 ,12 ) – $ 1,000.00(F/G ,9 ,7 )(A/F ,9,12 ) – $ 200,000.00(P/F ,9 ,5 )(A/P ,9 ,12 ) + $260,000.00(A/F ,9 ,12)

= – $1,160,000.00(0.13965 ) – $ 240,000.00 – $ 15,000.00 (3.2397 )(0.64993 )(0.13965 ) – $ 1,000.00(24.449 )(0.04965 ) – $ 200,000.00(0.64993 )(0.13965 ) + $260,000.00(0.04965)

= – 161,994.00 -$240,000.00 -$4,410.66 -$1,213.89 -$ 181,152.55 + $ 12,909.00

= – $ 412,862.10

Scraper 2

The yearly operating cost is the cost per hour times the number of hours per year the scraper is operated during the year:

Operating cost = \(\frac{\$150.00 }{h0ur}\times \frac{2,000 hour}{year} = \$300,000.00\) per year

\(EUAW_2=P_0(A/P ,i ,n ) +A_1 +A_2 (P/A ,i ,n )(P/F ,i ,n )(A/P ,i ,n ) + G(F/G ,i ,n )(A/F ,i ,n ) +F_5(P/F ,i ,n )(A/P ,i ,n ) +F_{10}(A/F ,i ,n )\)

= – $1,200,000.00(A/P ,9 ,10 ) – $ 3,000,000.00 – $ 20,000.00 (P/A ,9 ,4 )(P/F ,9,5 )(A/P ,9 ,10 ) – $ 1,500.00(F/G ,9 ,5 )(A/F ,9,10 ) – $ 270,000.00(P/F ,9 ,5 )(A/P ,9 ,10 ) + $200,000.00(A/F ,9 ,10)

= – $1,200,000.00(0.15582 ) – $ 3,000,000.00 – $ 20,000.00 (3.2397 )(0.64993)(0.15582) – $ 1,500.00(10.941)(0.06582) – $ 270,000.00(0.64993 )(0.15882 ) + $200,000.00(0.06582)

= -$ 186,984.00 – $300,000.00 – $ 6,561.82 – $ 27,869.91 + 13,164.00

= – $ 509,331.93

Therefore, select scraper 1 since it has a lower equivalent uniform annual cost

-$412,862.10 < -$509,331.93
