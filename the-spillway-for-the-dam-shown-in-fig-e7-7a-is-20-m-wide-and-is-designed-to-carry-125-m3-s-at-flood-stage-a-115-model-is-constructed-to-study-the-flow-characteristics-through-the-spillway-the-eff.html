Guest Question.

The spillway for the dam shown in Fig. E7.7a is 20 m wide and is designed to carry 125 \({m^{3}}/{s} \) at flood stage. A 1:15 model is constructed to study the flow characteristics through the spillway. The effects of surface tension and viscosity are to be neglected.
FIND
(a) Determine the required model width and flowrate.
(b) What operating time for the model corresponds to a 24-hr period in the prototype?

Admin
Solution

The width, \(w_{m}\), of the model spillway is obtained from the length scale, \(\lambda _{l}\), so that

\(\frac{w_{m}}{w}=\lambda _{l} =\frac{1}{15} \)

and

\(w_{m}=\frac{20 m}{15}=1.33 m \)

Of course, all other geometric features (including surface roughness) of the spillway must be scaled in accordance with the same length scale.

With the neglect of surface tension and viscosity, \(Dependent pi term=\phi (\frac{l_{i}}{l}, \frac{\epsilon }{l},\frac{\rho Vl}{\mu },\frac{V}{\sqrt{gl} },\frac{\rho V^{2}l}{\sigma } )\) indicates that dynamic similarity will be achieved if the Froude numbers are equal between model and prototype. Thus,

\(\frac{V_{m}}{\sqrt{g_{m}l_{m}} } =\frac{V}{\sqrt{gl} } \)

and for \( g_{m}=g\)

\(\frac{V_{m}}{V } =\sqrt{\frac{l_{m}}{l} } \)

Since the flowrate is given by Q = VA, where A is an appropriate cross sectional area, it follows that

\(\frac{Q_{m}}{Q}=\frac{V_{m}A_{m}}{VA}=\sqrt{\frac{l_{m}}{l} }(\frac{l_{m}}{l} )^{2}=(\lambda _{l})^{{5}/{2}} \)

where we have made use of the relationship \({A_{m}}/{A}=({l_{m}}/{l})^{2}\).

For \(\lambda _{l}=\frac{1}{15} \) and \(Q=125{m^{3}}/{s}\)

\(Q_{m}=(\frac{1}{15} )^{{5}/{2}}(125{m^{3}}/{s})=0.143{m^{3}}/{s}\)

The time scale can be obtained from the velocity scale, since the velocity is distance divided by time (V = l/t), and therefore

\(\frac{V}{V_{m}}=\frac{l}{t}\frac{t_{m}}{l_{m}} \)

or

\(\frac{t_{m}}{t} =\frac{V}{V_{m}}\frac{l_{m}}{l}=\sqrt{\frac{l_{m}}{l} } =\sqrt{\lambda _{l}} \)

This result indicates that time intervals in the model will be smaller than the corresponding intervals in the prototype if \(\lambda _{l}\lt 1\). For \(\lambda _{l}=\frac{1}{15} \) and a prototype time interval of 24 hr

\(t_{m}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{15} }(24hr)=6.20 hr \)

COMMENT As indicated by the foregoing analysis, the time scale varies directly as the square root of the length scale. Thus, as shown in Fig. E7.7b, the model time interval, \(t_{m}\), orresponding to a 24-hr prototype time interval can be varied by changing the length scale, \(\lambda _{l}\). The ability to scale times may be very useful, since it is possible to “speed up” events in the model that may occur over a relatively long time in the prototype. There is, of course, a practical limit to how small the length scale (and the corresponding time scale) can become. For example, if the length scale is too small, then surface tension effects may become important in the model whereas they are not in the prototype. In such a case the present model design, based simply on Froude number similarity, would not be adequate.
