Guest Question.

Vertical Stress Increase Due to a Point Load.
A pole carries a vertical load of 200 kN. Determine the vertical total stress increase at a depth 5 m (a) directly below
the pole and (b) at a radial distance of 2 m.

Strategy: The first step is to determine the type of surface load. The load carried by the pole can be approximated to a point load. You can then use the equation for the vertical stress increase for a point load.

 

Admin
Solution

Step 1: Determine the load type.
Assume the load from the pole can be approximated by a point load.

Step 2: Use the equation for a point load. Use Equation (7.57):

Equation(7.57): \(\Delta \sigma _{z}=\frac{3Q}{z^{2}}I\)

z = 5 m, Q = 200 kN; Under load, r = 0, \(\therefore \frac{r}{z}=0\)

Equation (7.58): \(I=\frac{3}{2\pi }\frac{1}{\begin{bmatrix}1+(\frac{r}{z})^{2}\end{bmatrix}^{5/2}}\)

From Equation (7.58): \(\frac{r}{z}=0,\: \: I=\frac{3}{2\pi }=0.48\)

\(\Delta \sigma _{z}=\frac{Q}{z^{2}}I=\frac{200}{5^{2}}*0.48=3.8\, kPa\)

Step 3: Determine the vertical stress at the radial distance.

\(r=2\, m,\: \: \frac{r}{z}=\frac{2}{5}=0.4,\: \: I=\frac{3}{2\pi }\frac{1}{\begin{bmatrix}1+(0.4)^{2}\end{bmatrix}^{5/2}}=0.33\) \(\Delta \sigma _{z}=\frac{200}{5^{2}}*0.33=2.6\, kPa\)
