Guest Question.

A horizontal cylindrical heater is boiling water at 1 atm pressure. The heater has a diameter of 2 cm and a length of 0.8 m. Its surface temperature is 460 C, and the emissivity of its surface is 0.6. W

hat is the heat transfer rate to the water?

Admin
Solution

Looking at Fig. 12.17, with an excess temperature of 360 C, the boiling is certainly in the film boiling region. The boiling heat transfer coefficient is given by Eq. (12.102).

\(h_{boil} =0.62\left[\frac{\rho _{v}(\rho -\rho _{v} )gh^{\prime }_{fg}k^{3}_{v} }{\mu _{v}D(T_{s}-T_{sat} ) } \right] ^{1/4}\) (12.102)

 

\(T_{s}=460C\) and \(T_{sat}=100C\)

The vapor properties are taken at the film temperature \(T_{f} =\frac{T_{s}+T_{sat} }{2}=(460+100)/2 =280C\) .

For water vapor at 280 C,

\(\rho _{v} =33.15kg/m^{3}\)

 

\(k_{v}=0.0605W/mC\)

 

\(\mu _{v} =1.870\times 10^{-5} kg/ms\)

 

\(c_{pv}=4835J/kgC \)

 

For water at 100 C,

\(\rho =958kg/m^{3}\)

 

\(h_{fg}=2257kJ/kg\)

 

From Eq. (12.103)

\(h^{\prime }_{fg}=h_{fg}+0.4c_{pv}(T_{s}-T_{sat} )\) (12.103)

, the corrected latent heat of vaporization is

\(h^{\prime }_{fg}=2257\times 10^{3} +0.4(4835)(460-100 )=2953\times 10^{3} J/kg\)

Putting values into Eq. (12.102), we have

\(h_{boil} =0.62\left[\frac{\rho _{v}(\rho -\rho _{v} )gh^{\prime }_{fg}k^{3}_{v} }{\mu _{v}D(T_{s}-T_{sat} ) } \right] ^{1/4}\)

 

\(=0.62\left[\frac{(33.15)(958-33.15)(9.807)(2953\times 10^{3} )(0.0605)^{3} }{(1.870\times 10^{-5} )(0.02)(460-100) } \right] ^{1/4}=682W/m^{2}C\)

 

For the radiation, from Eq. (12.104)

\(h_{rad}=\frac{\varepsilon \sigma (T^{4}_{s}-T^{4}_{sat} )}{T_{s}-T_{sat}}\) (12.104)

 

, \(h_{rad}=\frac{(0.6)(5.67\times 10^{-8} ) \left[(460+273.15)^{4}-(100+273.15)^{4} \right] }{460-100} =25.5W/m^{2}C\)

 

The total heat transfer coefficient is given by Eq. (12.105).

\(h_{total}=h_{boil}\left(\frac{h_{boil}}{h_{total}} \right) ^{1/3} +h_{rad}\) (12.105)

 

Putting in values, we get

\(h_{total}=682\left(\frac{682}{h_{total}} \right) ^{1/3} +25.5\) (12.107)

 

Rearranging Eq. (12.107) for a solution by Excel’s Goal Seek program, we have

\(h_{total}-682\left(\frac{682}{h_{total}} \right) ^{1/3} -25.5=0\) (12.108)

 

Solving Eq. (12.108) with Excel’s Goal Seek, we got \(h_{total}=701W/m^{2}C\) .

The heat flow to the water is \(q=h_{total}A(T_{s}-T_{sat} )=(701)\left[(\pi )(0.02)(0.8)\right](460-100)=12685W\) .

The heat flow to the water is 12700 W.
