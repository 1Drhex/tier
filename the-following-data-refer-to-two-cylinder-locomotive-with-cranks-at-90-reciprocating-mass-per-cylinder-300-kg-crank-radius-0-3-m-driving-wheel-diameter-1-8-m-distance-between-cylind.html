Guest Question.

The following data refer to two cylinder locomotive with cranks at \(90^{\circ}\) : Reciprocating mass per cylinder = 300 kg ; Crank radius = 0.3 m ; Driving wheel diameter = 1.8 m ; Distance between cylinder centre lines = 0.65 m ; Distance between the driving wheel central planes = 1.55 m.
Determine : 1. the fraction of the reciprocating masses to be balanced, if the hammer blow is not to exceed 46 kN at 96.5 km. p.h. ; 2. the variation in tractive effort ; and 3. the maximum swaying couple.

Admin
Solution

Given : m = 300 kg ; r = 0.3 m ; D = 1.8 m or R = 0.9 m ; a = 0.65 m ; Hammer blow = 46 kN = 46 \(\times 10^{3} N ; v=96.5 km / h =26.8 m / s\)

1. Fraction of the reciprocating masses to be balanced

Let c = Fraction of the reciprocating masses to be balanced, and

B = Magnitude of balancing mass placed at each of the driving wheels at radius b.

We know that the mass of the reciprocating parts to be balanced

\(=c \cdot m=300 c kg\)

The position of planes of the wheels and cylinders is shown in Fig. 22.9 (a), and the position of cranks is shown in Fig 22.9 (b). Assuming the plane of wheel A as the reference plane, the data may be tabulated as below :

Plane (1) Mass (m) kg (2) Radius (r) m (3) \(\text { Cent.force } \div \omega^{2}\)(m.r) kg-m (4) Distance from Plane x(l) m (5) \(\text { Couple } \div \omega^{2} (m . r . l) k g-m^{2}\)(6)
A(R.P.) B b B.b 0 0
B 300 c 0.3 90 c 40.5 c 40.5 c
C 300 c 0.3 90 c 99 c 99 c
D B b B.b 1,55 B.b 1.55 B.b
Table 22.3

Now the couple polygon, to some suitable scale, may be drawn with the data given in Table 22.3 (column 6), as shown in Fig. 22.10. The closing side of the polygon (vector c′ o′) represents the balancing couple and is proportional to 1.55 B.b.

From the couple polygon,

\(1.55 B \cdot b=\sqrt{(40.5 c)^{2}+(99 c)^{2}}=107 c\)

 

\(\therefore \quad B \cdot b=107 c / 1.55=69 c\)

We know that angular speed,

\(\omega=v / R=26.8 / 0.9=29.8 rad / s\)

 

\(\therefore\) Hammer blow,

 

\(46 \times 10^{3}=B \cdot \omega^{2} \cdot b\)

 

\(=69 c(29.8)^{2}=61275 c\)

 

\(\therefore \quad c=46 \times 10^{3} / 61275=0.751\)

 

2. Variation in tractive effort

We know that variation in tractive effort

\(=\pm \sqrt{2}(1-c) m \cdot \omega^{2} \cdot r=\pm \sqrt{2}(1-0.751) 300(29.8)^{2} 0.3\)

 

\(=28140 N =28.14 kN\)

 

Maximum swaying couple

We know the maximum swaying couple

\(=\frac{a(1-c)}{\sqrt{2}} \times m \cdot \omega^{2} \cdot r=\frac{0.65(1-0.751)}{\sqrt{2}} \times 300(29.8)^{2} 0.3=9148 N – m\)

 

\(=9.148 kN – m\)
