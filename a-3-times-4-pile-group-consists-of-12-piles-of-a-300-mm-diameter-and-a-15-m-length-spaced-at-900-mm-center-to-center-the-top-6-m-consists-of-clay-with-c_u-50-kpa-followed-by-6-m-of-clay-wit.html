Guest Question.

A 3 \(\times \)4 pile group consists of 12 piles of a 300 mm-diameter and a 15 m-length, spaced at 900 mm, center-to-center. The top 6 m consists of clay with \(c_{u}\) = 50 kPa, followed by 6 m of clay with \( c_{u}\) = 65 kPa, which was underlain by stiff clay with\(c_{u}\) = 90 kPa. Estimate the allowable load carrying capacity of the pile group.

 

Admin
Solution

B = 2.1 m, L = 3.0 m, and D = 15.0 m.
Adhesion factors :
\(\alpha =0.21+0.26\frac{P_{a}}{C_{u}}< 1.0\).
\(c_{u} = 50 kPa \rightarrow \alpha = 0.74; c_{u} = 65 kPa \rightarrow \alpha = 0.62; c_{u} = 90 kPa \rightarrow \alpha = 0.50\).
Single pile:\( Q_{s,ult} = \pi \times 0.3 \times [6 \times 0.74 \times 50 + 6 \times 0.62 \times 65 + 3 \times 0.50 \times 90] = 564.4 kN\).
\(Q_{t,ult} = (\pi /4) \times 0.32 \times 90 \times 9 = 57.3 kN\).
\(\therefore Q_{ult} =564.4 + 57.3 = 621.7 kN\).
\(\therefore Q_{ult , group}= 12 \times 621.7 = 7460 kN\).

Block: \(Q_{sides, ult} = 2 \times (2.1 + 3.0) \times [ 6 \times 0.74 \times 50 + 6 \times 0.62 \times 65 + 3 \times 0.50 \times 90] = 6108 kN\)
\(D/B = 15.0/2.1 = 7.1 , B/L = 2.1/3.0 = 0.7 \rightarrow N_{c} = 8.6\) (Figure (b))
\(Q_{base,ult} = 90 \times 8.6 \times 2.1 \times 3 = 4876 kN\)
\(\therefore Q_{ult , group}= 6108 + 4876 = 10,984 kN\)
We will take the lowest of the two values (i.e., 7460 kN and 10984 kN).
The allowable load on the group is 7460/2.5 = 2984 kN
