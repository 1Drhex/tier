Guest Question.

Consider the I-shaped column of Figure 14.21 and calculate its buckling load. The column can buckle in either xy or xz planes. Different support conditions are specified in these two planes as shown in Figures 14.21b and c. The column is 4 m long and its cross-sectional properties are A = 8000 mm², \( I_z = 100 × 10^6 mm^4 \) and \( I_y = 10 × 10^6 mm^4 \). The material is linear–elastic with E = 200 GPa. Comment on how the results would change if \( I_y \) doubles (i.e. \( I_y = 20 × 10^6 mm^4 \)).

Admin
Solution

\( P_E \) is calculated by considering the slenderness about each axis.

The radius of gyration \( r (=\sqrt{I/A} ) \) is calculated about the y- and z-axes as:

\( r_z = \sqrt{\frac{I_z}{A}} = \sqrt{\frac{100 × 10^6}{8000}} = 111.8 \) mm .

\( r_y = \sqrt{\frac{I_y}{A}} = \sqrt{\frac{10 × 10^6}{8000}} = 35.35 \) mm .

The support conditions are those of a cantilever beam in the xy plane (with respect to the z-axis) and those of a propped cantilever in the xz plane (with respect to the y-axis). From Table 14.1:

Table 14.1 Summary of effective length factors
Structural static configuration ψ \( k_e = 1/\sqrt{ψ} \)
1 1
0.25 2
2.046 0.7

\( k_{ez} = 2 \), \( k_{ey} = 0.7 \).

and the effective lengths become:

\( L_{ez} = k_{ez} × L = 2 × 4000 = 8000 \) mm.

\( L_{ey} = k{ey} × L = 0.7 × 4000 = 2800 \) mm.

The slenderness can then be calculated with Equation 14.81:

\( λ = k_e L \sqrt{\frac{A}{I}} = \frac{L_e}{r} \) (14.81).

\( λ_z = \frac{L_{ez}}{r_z} = \frac{8000}{111.8} = 71.55 \) .

\( λ_y = \frac{L_{ey}}{r_y} = \frac{2800}{35.35} =79.20 \) .

Buckling will occur in the xz plane because \( λ_z < λ_y \) . Its corresponding buckling stress and load are:

Equation 14.80: \( σ_E = π^2 \frac{E}{λ^2} \) (14.80)

\( σ_E = π^2 \frac{E}{λ^2_y} = π^2 \frac{200000}{79.20^2} = 314 7 \) MPa.

Equation 14.79: \( σ_E = \frac{P_E}{A} = π^2 \frac{EI}{AL^2_e} \) (14.79).

\( P_E = σ_E A = 314 7 × 8000 = 2518 \) kN.

The previous calculations are now repeated in the case \( I_y = 20 × 10^6 mm^4 \) (only for values related to the y-axis because properties with respect to z remained unchanged):

\( r_y = \sqrt{\frac{I_y}{A}} = \sqrt{\frac{20 × 10^6}{8000}} = 50 \) mm .

\( L_{ey} = 2800 \) mm (unchanged).

\( λ_y = \frac{L_{ey}}{r_y} = \frac{2800}{50} =56 \) .

In this case, buckling will occur in the xy plane because \( λ_z(= 71.55) > λ_y(= 56) \) .

\( σ_E = π^2 \frac{E}{λ^2_z} = π^2 \frac{200000}{71.55^2} = 385.5 \) MPa.

\( P_E = σ_E A = 385.5× 8000 = 3084 \) kN.

Note that if the yield stress of the steel was 350 MPa, the steel would have yielded before buckling.
