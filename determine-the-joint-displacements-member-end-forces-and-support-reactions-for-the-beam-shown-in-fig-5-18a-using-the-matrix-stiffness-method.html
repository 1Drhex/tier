Guest Question.

Determine the joint displacements, member end forces, and support reactions for the beam shown in Fig. 5.18(a), using the matrix stiffness method.

Admin
Solution

Analytical Model: See Fig. 5.18(b). The beam has four degrees of freedom (numbered 1 through 4) and four restrained coordinates (numbered 5 through 8).

Structure Stiffness Matrix and Fixed-Joint Force Vector:

Member 1 By substituting \(E = 28(106) kN/m^{2}, I = 8,700(10−6) m^{4}\), and L = 6 m into Eq. (5.53) \(k=\frac{EI}{L^{3}}=\left[\begin{matrix} 12 & 6L & -12 & 6L\\ 6L & 4L^{2} & -6L &2L^{2} \\ -12 & -6L &12 &-6L \\6L&2L^{2} &-6L &4L^{2} \end{matrix} \right]\) , we write

Using the fixed-end force expressions given inside the front cover, we obtain \(FS_{b} = 63 kN, FM_{b} = 54 kN · m, FS_{e} = 27 kN, and FM_{e} = −36 kN · m\). Thus,

\(Q_{f1}=\left[\begin{matrix} 63 \\ \underset{……..}{54} \\27\\-36 \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 5 \\ 6 \\1 \\2 \end{matrix}\)

Using the code numbers (5, 6, 1, 2) for member 1, we store the pertinent elements of \(k_{1}\) and \(Q_{f1}\) in their proper positions in the S matrix and the \(P_{f}\) vector, respectively, as shown in Fig. 5.18(c).
Member 2 \(E = 28(10^{6}) kN/m^{2}, I = 5,800(10^{−6}) m^{4}, and L = 4 m\). Thus,

As this member is not subjected to any loads,

\(Q_{f2} = 0\)

Using the member code numbers 1, 2, 7, 3, the relevant elements of \(k_{2}\) are stored in S in Fig. 5.18(c).

Member 3 \(E = 28(10^{6}) kN/m^{2}, I = 5,800(10^{−6}) m^{4}, and L = 10 m\).

The fixed-end forces are determined to be \(FS_{b} = 75 kN, FM_{b} = 187.5 kN · m, FS_{e} = 75 kN, and FM_{e} = −187.5 kN · m.\) Thus,

\(Q_{f3}=\left[\begin{matrix} \underset{……..}{75} \\ \underset{……..}{187.5} \\\underset{……..}{75}\\\underset{……..}{-187.5} \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 7 \\ 3 \\8 \\4 \end{matrix}\)

The relevant elements of \(k_{3}\) and \(Q_{f3}\) are stored in S and \(P_{f}\) respectively, using the member code numbers 7, 3, 8, 4. The completed structure stiffness matrix S and structure fixed-joint force vector Pf are given in Fig. 5.18(c).

Joint Load Vector: By comparing Figs. 5.18(a) and (b), we realize that \(P_{1} = −200 kN, P_{2} = 0, P_{3} = −90 kN · m, and P_{4} = 0\). Thus, the joint load vector can be expressed as

\(P=\left[\begin{matrix}-200 \\ 0\\ -90 \\0 \end{matrix} \right]\)

Joint Displacements: The stiffness relations for the entire beam can be expressed as

\(P − P_{f }= Sd\)

By substituting the numerical values of P, \(P_{f}\), and S, we obtain

\(P=\left[\begin{matrix}-200 \\ 0\\ -90 \\0 \end{matrix} \right] – \left[\begin{matrix}27 \\-36\\ 187.5 \\-187.5 \end{matrix} \right]= \left[\begin{matrix} 43,983 &20,300& 60,900 & 0\\ 20,300 & 324,800 & 81,200 & 0 \\60,900 & 81,200& 227,360 &32,480 \\0 & 0 &32,480 & 64,960 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix}d_{1} \\ d_{2}\\ d_{3} \\d_{4} \end{matrix} \right]\)

or

\(\left[\begin{matrix}-227 \\ 36\\-277.5\\187.5 \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix} 43,983 &20,300& 60,900 & 0\\ 20,300 & 324,800 & 81,200 & 0 \\60,900 & 81,200& 227,360 &32,480 \\0 & 0 &32,480 & 64,960 \end{matrix} \right]\left[\begin{matrix}d_{1} \\ d_{2}\\ d_{3} \\d_{4} \end{matrix} \right]\)

By solving the foregoing system of simultaneous equations, we determine the joint displacements to be

\(d=\left[\begin{matrix}-4.4729m \\ 0.56143 rad\\ -0.68415 rad \\ 3.2285 rad \end{matrix} \right]\times 10^{-3}\)

Back substitution of the foregoing numerical values of d into the structure stiffness relationship \(P − P_{f} = Sd\) indicates that the solution of the simultaneous equations has indeed been carried out correctly.

Member End Displacements and End Forces:

Member 1

\(u_{1}=\left[\begin{matrix}u_{1} \\ u_{2}\\ u_{3} \\u_{4} \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 5 \\ 6 \\1\\2 \end{matrix} =\left[\begin{matrix}0 \\ 0\\ d_{1} \\ d_{2} \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix}0 \\ 0\\ -4.4729 \\0.56143 \end{matrix} \right]\times 10^{-3}\)

\(Q_{1}=k_{1}u_{1}+Q_{f1}=\left[\begin{matrix}146.33 kN \\ 281.19 kN\cdot m\\ -56.33 kN \\236.78 kN\cdot \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 5 \\ 6 \\1\\2 \end{matrix}\)

Member 2

\(u_{2} =\left[\begin{matrix}-4.4729 \\ 0.56143\\ 0 \\−0.68415 \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix}1 \\ 2 \\ 7 \\3\end{matrix} \times 10^{-3} ;Q_{2}=k_{2}u_{2}+Q_{f2}=\left[\begin{matrix}-143.67 kN \\ -236.78 kN\cdot m\\ 143.67 kN \\-337.92 kN\cdot \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 2 \\7\\3 \end{matrix}\)

Member 3

\(u_{3} =\left[\begin{matrix}-0 \\-0.68415\\ 0 \\3.2285 \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 7 \\ 3 \\ 8 \\ 4\end{matrix} \times 10^{-3} ;Q_{3}=k_{3}u_{3}+Q_{f3}=\left[\begin{matrix}99.79 kN \\ 247.92 kN\cdot m\\ 50.21 kN \\0 kN\cdot \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 7 \\3 \\8\\4 \end{matrix}\)

The member end forces are shown in Fig. 5.18(d).
