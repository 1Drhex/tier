Guest Question.

A 12-m-long and 5-m-high wall is constructed of two layers of 1-cm-thick sheetrock (\(k = 0.17 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\)) spaced 12 cm by wood studs (\(k = 0.11 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\)) whose cross section is \(12 cm \times 5 cm\). The studs are placed vertically 60 cm apart, and the space between them is filled with fiberglass insulation (\(k = 0.034 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\)). The house is maintained at 20°C and the ambient temperature outside is – 5°C. Taking the heat transfer coefficients at the inner and outer surfaces of the house to be 8.3 and \(34 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\), respectively, determine (a) the thermal resistance of the wall considering a representative section of it and (b) the rate of heat transfer through the wall.

Admin
Solution

A wall is constructed of two layers of sheetrock spaced by 5 cm × 12 cm wood studs. The space between the studs is filled with fiberglass insulation. The thermal resistance of the wall and the rate of heat transfer through the wall are to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Heat transfer is steady since there is no indication of change with time. 2 Heat transfer through the wall is one-dimensional. 3 Thermal conductivities are constant. 4 Heat transfer coefficients account for the radiation heat transfer.

Properties The thermal conductivities are given to be \(k=0.17 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\) for sheetrock, \(k = 0.11 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\) for wood studs, and \(k=0.034 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C\) for fiberglass insulation.

Analysis (a) The representative surface area is \( A = 1 \times 0.65 = 0.65 m ^{2}\). The thermal resistance network and the individual thermal resistances are

\( R_{i}=\frac{1}{h_{i} A}=\frac{1}{\left(8.3 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(0.65 m ^{2}\right)}=0.185^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\( R_{1}=R_{4}=R_{\text {sheetrock }}=\frac{L}{k A}=\frac{0.01 m }{\left(0.17 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(0.65 m ^{2}\right)}=0.090{ }^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\( R_{2}=R_{\text {stud }}=\frac{L}{k A}=\frac{0.12 m }{\left(0.11 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(0.05 m ^{2}\right)}=21.818^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\( R_{3}=R_{\text {fiberglass }}=\frac{L}{k A}=\frac{0.12 m }{\left(0.034 W / m \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(0.60 m ^{2}\right)}=5.882^{\circ} C / W \)

 

\( R_{o}=\frac{1}{h_{o} A}=\frac{1}{\left(34 W / m ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(0.65 m ^{2}\right)}=0.045^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\( \frac{1}{R_{\text {mid }}}=\frac{1}{R_{2}}+\frac{1}{R_{3}}=\frac{1}{21.818}+\frac{1}{5.882} \longrightarrow R_{\text {mid }}=4.633^{\circ} C / W\)

 

\( R_{\text {total }}=R_{i}+R_{1}+R_{\text {mid }}+R_{4}+R_{o}\)

 

\(\left.=0.185+0.090+4.633+0.090+0.045 = 4.858^{\circ} C / W \text { (for a } 1 m \times 0.65 m \text { section }\right)\)

 

\( \dot{Q}=\frac{T_{\infty 1}-T_{\infty 2}}{R_{\text {total }}}=\frac{[20-(-5)]^{\circ} C }{4.858^{\circ} C / W } = 5.15 W\)

 

(b) Then steady rate of heat transfer through entire wall becomes

\( \dot{Q}_{\text {total }}=(5.15 W ) \frac{(12 m )(5 m )}{0.65 m ^{2}}= 4 7 5 W\)
