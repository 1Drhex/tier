Guest Question.

The reaction takes place between two gases that mix at the entrance of the PFR reactor. The first gas A enters at a flow rate of 14.2\(m^{3}/min\) and the second gas contains 50% B and balance in inert I, entering with a total flow rate of 7.1\(m^{3}/min\). Both are instantaneously mixed at the entrance of the reactor at 86◦C and 1 atm. The gases are ideal and the product obtained is R according to the stoichiometric reaction:
A + B→R
The rate constant is given by the following expression:
\(ln k_{p}=−\frac{5470}{T} + 12.5(mol/(atm^{2} L min))\)
Calculate the reactor volume operating isothermally to achieve 90% conversion. Calculate the molar flow and space velocity.

Admin
Solution

Taking into account 1 mol as reference, we obtain the following molar or volumetric fractions, with temperature and pressure constant:
\(y_{A0}=\frac{V_{10}}{\nu _{0}}=\frac{14.2}{21.5}=0.67\)
\(y_{B0}=\frac{V_{20}}{\nu _{0}}=\frac{0.5\times 7.1}{21.5}=0.165\)
\(y_{1}=y_{B0}=0.165\)

The component B is the limiting one. In this case, the rate expression as a function of product R or reactant B will be:
\((-r_{B})=kC_{B0}^{2}\frac{(1-X_{B})(M-X_{B})}{(1+\varepsilon _{B}X_{B})^{2}}\) (14.50)

The ideal PFR equation is:
\(\tau =\frac{V}{\nu _{0}}=C_{B0}\int_{0}^{X_{B}}\frac{dX_{A}}{(-r_{B})}\)

Substituting the rate expression 14.50 into PFR equation we obtain the following solution:
\(\tau =\frac{1}{kC_{B0}}[(\frac{(1+\varepsilon _{B}M)^{2}}{(M-1)}) \ln \frac{(M-X_{B})}{M}-\frac{(1+\varepsilon _{B})^{2}}{(M-1)}\ln (1-X_{B})-\varepsilon _{B}^{2}X_{B}]\)

Determining \(\varepsilon _{B}\):

A B R Inert Total
Initial 0.67 0.165 0 0.165 1
Final 0.5 0 0.165 0.165 0.83
\(\varepsilon _{B}=−0.17\)

Calculating \(M=\frac{C_{A0}}{C_{B0}}\)

where:
\(C_{B0}=\frac{y_{B0}}{RT}=\frac{0.165\times 1.0}{0.082\times 359}=0.00564 (mol/L)\)

and
\(C_{A0}=\frac{y_{A0}}{RT}=\frac{0.67\times 1.0}{0.082\times 359}=0.0228 (mol/L)\)

Thus,
\(M=\frac{C_{A0}}{C_{B0}}=4.0\)

Calculating the rate constant at T =359 K:
\(ln k_{p}=−\frac{5470}{T}+ 12.5=−2.736\)
\(k_{p} =0.0648 (mol/(atm^{2}L min)\)

Thus,
\(k=k_{p}(RT)^{2} =0.0648(0.082 × 359)^{2} =56.1L/(mol min)\)
\(k=56.1L/(mol min)\)

Substituting \(X_{B}\) =0.90 (limiting), we obtain:
\(\tau KC_{B0} =0.486\)
\(\tau =1.537 min\)

The reactor volume will be:
\(V =\tau V_{0} =1.537 × 21.3=32.7 m^{3}\)
\(V =32.7m^{3}\)

And the space velocity:
\(\nu _{e}=\frac{1}{\tau }=\frac{\nu _{0}}{V}=0.65 min^{-1}\)
