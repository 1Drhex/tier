Guest Question.

A twin cylinder uncoupled locomotive has its cylinders 0.6 m apart and balance weights are 60° apart. The planes are symmetrically placed about the centre line. For each cylinder, the revolving masses are 300 kg at crank pin radius of 320 mm and reciprocating parts 285 kg. All the revolving and 2/3rd of the reciprocating masses are balanced. The driving wheels are 1.8 m diameter. When the engine runs at 60 km/h, find (a) the swaying couple, (b) the variation in tractive effort, and (c) the hammer blow.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given: } M=300 kg , r=320 mm , R=285 kg , c=\frac{2}{3}, l=0.6 m , d_{ w }=1.8 m , v=60 km / h \).

\( \text { Total mass to be balanced }=M+c R=300+2 \times \frac{285}{3}=490 kg \).

\( v=60 \times \frac{1000}{3600}=16.67 m / s , \omega=\frac{2 v}{d_{w}}=2 \times \frac{16.67}{1.8}=18.52 rad / s \).

From Table 12.27, we have

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} \cos \theta_{i}=156.8 \).

\( \sum M_{i} r_{i} \sin \theta_{i}=156.8 \).

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}=156.8 a \).

\( \Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}=156.8 a+94.08 \).

\( \tan \theta_{4}=\frac{-\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}}{-\Sigma M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}} \).

\( =\frac{-156.8 a+94.08}{-156.8 a} \).

\( =\frac{a+0.6}{a} \).

Taking 4 as the reference plane, we get

\( \tan \theta_{1}=\frac{a}{a+0.6} \).

\( \tan \theta_{1} \times \tan \theta_{4}=1 \).

\( \theta_{4}=\theta_{1}+60^{\circ} \).

\( \tan \left(\theta_{1}+60^{\circ}\right) \times \tan \theta_{1}=1 \).

\( \tan ^{2} \theta_{1} 2 \sqrt{3} \tan \theta_{1}-1=0 \).

\( \tan \theta_{1}=0.268,-4.732 \).

\( \theta_{1}=195^{\circ}, 285^{\circ} \).

\( 0.268=\frac{a}{a+0.6} \).

\( a=0.22 m \).

\( \left.M_{4} r_{4} l_{4}=\left[\sum M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \cos \theta_{i}\right)^{2}+\left(\sum M_{i} r_{i} l_{i} \sin \theta_{i}\right)^{2}\right]^{0.5} \).

\( 0.32(2 a+0.6) M_{4}=\left[(156.8 a)^{2}+(156.8 a+94.08)^{2}\right]^{0.5} \).

\( 0.32(0.44+0.6) M_{4}=\left[(156.8 \times 0.22)^{2}+(156.8 \times 0.22+94.08)^{2}\right]^{0.5} \).

\( 0.3328 M_{4}=134.123 \).

\( M_{4}=400 kg \).

By symmetry, \( M_{1}=400 kg \).

\( B b=\frac{400 \times 0.32 \times(213) \times 285}{490}=49.63 Nm \).

\( \text { Hammer blow }=B b \omega^{2}=49.63 \times(18.53)^{2}=17023 N \).

\( \text { Swaying couple }=(1-c) \frac{\operatorname{Rr} \omega^{2} l}{\sqrt{2}}=\frac{(1-2 / 3) \times 285 \times 0.32(18.52)^{2} 0.6}{\sqrt{2}} \).

\( =4424.76 N m \).

\( \text { Variation of tractive effort }=\pm \sqrt{2}(1-c) R \omega^{2} r \).

\( =\pm \sqrt{2}\left(1-\frac{2}{3}\right) \times 285 \times(18.52)^{2} \times 0.32 \).

\( =\pm 14745.87 N \).
