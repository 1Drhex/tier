Determine the moment of inertia about the centroidal x and y axes for the composite area shown.

Solution

Usually the first step in determining the moment of inertia about the major or centroidal axis requires the determination of the centroid (particularly in unsymmetrical cross-sections).

\(\overline{y}= \frac{ \sum{y\Delta A}}{A}=\frac{61\space in.^3}{26 \space in.^2 } = 2.35^{\prime \prime }\)

 

\(d_{y1} =y_{1} -\overline{y} \)=4.5″ – 2.35″ = 2.15″

 

\(d_{y2}=\overline{y}-y_{2}\)=2.35″ – 1″ = 1.35″
