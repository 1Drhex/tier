Guest Question.

Show that the molar temperature coefficients of energy and of enthalpy (the specific heats) of a mixture can be calculated as the sum of the products of molar fractions and the coefficients for each component. Also, give the formulas for the entropy capacities of the mixture.

Admin
Solution

The temperature coefficient of energy is the derivative of the energy with respect to temperature. Applying this definition to Equ.(6.57)2 yields

\(\bar{c}_{V}=\frac{d \bar{e}}{d T}=\frac{d}{d T} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \bar{x}_{i} \bar{e}_{i}=\sum_{i=1}^{N} \bar{x}_{i} \frac{d}{d T} \bar{e}_{i}=\sum_{i=1}^{N} \bar{x}_{i} \bar{c}_{V i}\)

 

The molar temperature coefficient of enthalpy of the ideal gas is defined as the derivative of the enthalpy with respect to temperature. We first have to show that the molar enthalpy can be calculated similarly to Equ.(6.57):

\(n \bar{h}=E+P V=\sum_{i=1}^{N} E_{i}+\left(\sum_{i=1}^{N} P_{i}\right) V=\sum_{i=1}^{N}\left(E_{i}+P_{i} V\right)=\sum_{i=1}^{N} n_{i}\left(\bar{e}_{i}+P_{i} \bar{v}_{i}\right)=\sum_{i=1}^{N} n_{i} \bar{h}_{i}\)

 

Therefore, the derivative of the molar enthalpy of the mixture is equal to

\(\bar{c}_{P}=\sum_{i=1}^{N} \bar{x}_{i} \bar{c}_{P i}\)

 

The entropy capacitances are related to the temperature coefficients through the temperature of the mixture. Since every component has the same temperature, the equations just derived apply to the entropy as well:

\(\bar{\kappa}_{P}=\sum_{i=1}^{N} \bar{x}_{i} \bar{\kappa}_{P i}\)

 

The equation holds also for the entropy capacitance at constant volume.

\(\bar{s}=\sum_{i=1}^{N} \bar{x}_{i} \bar{s}_{i} \quad, \quad \bar{e}=\sum_{i=1}^{N} \bar{x}_{i} \bar{e}_{i} \quad\quad \)(6.57)
