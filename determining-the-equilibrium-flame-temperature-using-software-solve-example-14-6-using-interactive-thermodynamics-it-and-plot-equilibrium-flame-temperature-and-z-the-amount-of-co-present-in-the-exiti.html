Analysis Equation (a) of provides the point of departure for the IT solution

 

\(K(T)=\frac{z(z / 2)^{1 / 2}}{(1-z)}\left[\frac{p / p_{ ref }}{(5.76+z) / 2}\right]^{1 / 2}\) (a)

 

For a given pressure, this expression involves two unknowns: z and T.

Also, from , we use the energy balance, Eq. (c)

 

\(\bar{h}_{ R }=\bar{h}_{ P }\) (c)

 

where

 

\(\bar{h}_{ R }=\left(\bar{h}_{ CO }\right)_{ R }+\frac{1}{2}\left(\bar{h}_{ O _{2}}\right)_{ R }+1.88\left(\bar{h}_{ N _{2}}\right)_{ R }\)

 

and

 

\(\bar{h}_{ P }=z\left(\bar{h}_{ CO }\right)_{ P }+(z / 2)\left(\bar{h}_{ O _{2}}\right)_{ P }+(1-z)\left(\bar{h}_{ CO _{2}}\right)_{ P }+1.88\left(\bar{h}_{ N _{2}}\right)_{ P }\)

 

where the subscripts R and P denote reactants and products, respectively, and z denotes the amount of CO in the products, in kmol per kmol of CO entering.
