Guest Question.

The truss shown in Fig. 6.19 carries the forces \(F_{1} = 10 KN\) and \(F_{2}=20 KN\)(given: a = 3 m).

Calculate the forces in the members 6 and 8.

Admin
Solution

The truss represents a three-hinged arch. In a first step we calculate the support reactions at B. We isolate the two parts

of the truss and write down the moment equilibrium conditions about point A of the complete (frozen) system and point G of the right-hand part:

\(\overset{\curvearrowright }{A} : a F_{1} +2a F_{2} -3a B_{V} +a B_{H} =0\) ,

\(\overset{\curvearrowright }{G} : aF_{2} -2aB_{V} +2a B_{H} =0 \rightarrow B_{H}=B_{V} -F_{2} /2\) .

Solving these two equations yields

\(B_{H} = 10 KN\) , \(B_{V}=20 KN\).

Now we pass a cut through the members 4 and 6 and apply the equilibrium conditions in the vertical and horizontal directions to the free-body diagram:

\(\uparrow : \sqrt{2} /2S_{6}-F_{2} +B_{V} =0 \rightarrow \underline{\underline{S_{6}=0} }\) ,

\(\leftarrow :S_{4}+B_{H} =0 \rightarrow S_{4}=-10KN\) .

Equilibrium in the horizontal direction at the isolated joint I leads to

\(\rightarrow : S_{5}=S_{4} =-10KN\) .

Finally, we isolate joint II and write down the equilibrium condition in the horizontal direction:

\(\leftarrow :S_{5} +\sqrt{2} /2 S_{8} =0 \rightarrow \underline{\underline{S_{8}=-\sqrt{2}S_{5}=14.1 KN } }\) .

Note that the forces in the members are independent of the value of a.
