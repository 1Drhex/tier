Guest Question.

A spherical water droplet at a temperature of \(10^{\circ } C\) forms with a diameter of 0.5 cm during a rainfall at an elevation of 1km above sea level. (a) Determine the internal pressure acting on the spherical water droplet (refer to Figure 1.8).

Admin
Solution

(a) The surface tension for water at \(10^{\circ } C\) is read from Table A.2 in Appendix A, while the atmospheric pressure at an elevation of 1 km above sea level is read from Table A.1 in Appendix A.The internal pressure acting on the spherical water droplet is determined by applying Equation 1.93 \((p_{i} -p_{e})=\frac{2\sigma }{r} \) as follows:

\((p_{i} -p_{e})=\frac{2\sigma }{r} \)

\( N:=kg\frac{m}{sec^{2} } \) \(\sigma :=0.0742\frac{N}{m} \) \(P_{atm} :=89.976KPa=8.998\times 10^{4} \frac{N}{m^{2} } \)

\(Pe:=P_{atm} =8.998\times 10^{4} \frac{N}{m^{2} } \)

\(D:=0.5cm=5\times 10^{-3} m\) \(r:=\frac{D}{2} =2.510^{-3} m\)

Guess value: \(P_{i} :=1\frac{N}{m^{2} } \)

Given

\((P_{i} – P_{e})=\frac{2.\sigma }{r} \)
\(P_{i}:=Find(P_{i})=9.004\times 10^{4} \frac{N}{m^{2} } \)
