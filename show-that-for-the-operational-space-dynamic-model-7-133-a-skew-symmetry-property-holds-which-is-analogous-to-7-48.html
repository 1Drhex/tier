Guest Question.

Show that for the operational space dynamic model (7.133) a skew-symmetry property holds which is analogous to (7.48).

\(B_{A} \left(x_{e} \right)\ddot{x}_{e} +C_{A}\left(x_{e}, \dot{x}_{e}\right)\dot{x}_{e} + g_{A} \left(x_{e} \right) \) = \( \gamma_{A} - h_{A} \) (7.133)

\(w^{T} N \left(q, \dot{q}\right) w\) = 0 (7.48)

Admin
Solution

In order to differentiate matrix \(B_{A} \) in (7.130) with respect to time, it is worth recalling the formula for the derivative of the inverse of a matrix A

\(B_{A} \) = \(\left(J_{A}B^{-1} J^{T}_{A}\right)^{-1}\) (7.130)

\(\frac{d}{dt} A^{-1}\left(t\right) \) = \(– A^{-1}\left(t\right) \dot{A} \left(t\right)A^{-1}\left(t\right) \)

Therefore, the time derivative of \(B_{A} \) can be expressed as

\(\dot{B}_{A} \) = \(-B_{A} \left(\dot{J}_{A}B^{-1} J^{T}_{A} – J_{A}B^{-1}\dot{B}B^{-1} J^{T}_{A} + J_{A}B^{-1}\dot{J}^ {T}_{A} \right) B_{A}\)

As regards \(C_{A}\) , in view of (3.62), the relation in (7.131) can be written as

\(\dot{x}_{e} = \left[\begin{matrix} \dot{p}_{e} \\ \dot{\phi }_{e} \end{matrix} \right] \)= \(\left[\begin{matrix} J_{p}\left(q\right) \\ J_{\phi }\left(q\right) \end{matrix} \right] \dot{q} \) = \( J_{A}\left(q\right)\dot{q} \) (3.62)

\(C_{A}\dot{x}_{e} \) = \(B_{A}J_{A}B^{-1}C\dot{q} – B_{A}\dot{J}_{A}\dot{q}\) (7.131)

\(C_{A} J_{A}\dot{q} \) = \(B_{A} J_{A}B^{-1} C \dot{q} – B_{A}\dot{J}_{A}\dot{q}\)

and using a right pseudo-inverse of \(J_{A}\) , e.g., the one in (7.138), leads to

\(\bar{J}_{A} \left(q\right) \) = \( B^{-1}\left(q\right)J^{T}_{A}\left(q\right) B_{A}\left(q\right) \) (7.138)

\(C_{A}\) = \(B_{A} J_{A}B^{-1}C \bar{J}_{A} – B_{A} \dot{J}_{A}\bar{J}_{A} \)

Hence, the matrix \(\dot{B_A} -2C_{A}\) can be expressed as

\(\dot{B}_{A} – 2 C_{A}\) = \(\bar{J}^{T}_{A} \left( \dot{B} – 2 C\right) \bar{J}_{A} + B_{A} \dot{J}_{A} \bar{J}_{A} – \bar{J}^{T}_{A}\dot{J}^{T}_{A} B_{A} \)

From the skew-symmetry of \(\dot{B} − 2 C_{A} \) it follows that the first term represents a skew-symmetric matrix. Also, by observing that the second term on the right-hand side is the transpose of the third term, the matrix resulting from the difference of those terms is skew-symmetric too. It can be concluded that the whole matrix \(\dot{B}_{A} − 2 C_{A} \) is skew-symmetric.
