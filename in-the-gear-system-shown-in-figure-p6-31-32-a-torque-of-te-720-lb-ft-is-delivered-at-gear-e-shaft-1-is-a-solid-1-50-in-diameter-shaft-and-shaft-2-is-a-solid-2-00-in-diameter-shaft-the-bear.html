Guest Question.

In the gear system shown in Figure P6.31/32, a torque of \(T_{E}\) = 720 lb-ft is delivered at gear E. Shaft (1) is a solid 1.50-in.-diameter shaft, and shaft (2) is a solid 2.00-in.-diameter shaft. The bearings shown allow free rotation of the shafts.
(a) Determine the torque provided by the motor to gear A.
(b) Determine the maximum shear stresses in shafts (1) and (2).

Admin
Solution

(a) Torque relationship: The torque on gear E creates a torque in shaft (2) of \(T_{2}\) = 720 lb-ft.
Accordingly, the torque applied to gear D is also equal to 720 lb-ft. The torque on gear C is found with the gear ratio:

\(T_{C}=T_{D} \frac{30}{60}=0.5 T_{D}=0.5(720 lb – ft )=360 lb – ft\)

Shaft (1) has the same torque as gear C; therefore, \(T_{1}\) = 360 lb-ft and \(T_{B}\) = 360 N-m. The torque on gear A is found from:

\(T_{A}=T_{B} \frac{24}{72}=\frac{T_{C}}{3}=\frac{360 lb – ft }{3}=120 lb – tt\)

To summarize:

\(\begin{array}{|ll|}\hline T_{1}=360 lb – ft & T_{2}=720 lb – ft \\\hline\end{array}\)

(b) Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&J_{1}=\frac{\pi}{32}(1.50 \text { in. })^{4}=0.497010 in .^{4} \\&J_{2}=\frac{\pi}{32}(2.00 in .)^{4}=1.570796 in .{ }^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Maximum shear stress in shafts (1) and (2):

\(\begin{aligned}&\tau_{1}=\frac{T_{1} c_{1}}{J_{1}}=\frac{(360 lb – ft )(1.50 in . / 2)(12 in . / ft )}{0.497010 in .^{4}}=6,519.98 psi =6,520 psi \\&\tau_{2}=\frac{T_{2} c_{2}}{J_{2}}=\frac{(720 lb – ft )(2.00 in. / 2)(12 in. / ft )}{1.570796 in .^{4}}=5,500.396 psi =5,500 psi\end{aligned}\)
