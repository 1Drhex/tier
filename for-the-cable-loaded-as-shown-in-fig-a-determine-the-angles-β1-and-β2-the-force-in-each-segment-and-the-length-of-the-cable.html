Guest Question.

For the cable loaded as shown in Fig. (a), determine the angles \(β_{1}\) and \(β_{2}\), the force in each segment, and the length of the cable.

Admin
Solution

Method of Analysis

The free-body diagram of the entire cable is shown in Fig. (b), where the labeling of the variables is consistent with the notation used in Fig. 6.12 \(s(x)=\frac{x}{2}\sqrt{1+\left(\frac{w_{0}x}{T_{0}} \right)^{2} }+\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{T_{0}}{w_{0}}\right) \ln\left[\left(\frac{w_{0}x}{T_{0}} \right)+\sqrt{1+\left(\frac{w_{0}x}{T_{0}} \right)^{2} } \right]\) (recall that the positive direction for \(θ_{1}\), \(θ_{2}\), and \(θ_{3}\) is counterclockwise from the horizontal, and positive h is measured downward from end B).

We note that the information given in Fig. (a) includes the horizontal spacing of the loads and the angle \(β_{3}\). Therefore, according to the discussion in Art. 6.6, the problem is statically determinate, and a solution can be obtained by writing and solving Eqs. (6.18) \(T_{i}\cos \theta _{i}=T_{0}\) i = 1, 2, . . . , n–(6.20) \(h=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{L_{i}\tan \theta _{i}}\) . In this problem, however, the difficulty of solving these simultaneous, nonlinear equations can be avoided.

Examination of the FBD in Fig. (b) reveals that \(T_{3}\) can be calculated from the equation \(ΣM_{A}\) = 0. Equilibrium equations for joints 2 and 1 will then determine the other unknowns without having to solve the equations simultaneously.

Mathematical Details

From the FBD of the entire cable, Fig. (b), we obtain

\(\Sigma M_{A}=0\underset{+ }{\curvearrowleft }T_{3} \sin 35^{\circ }(24)+T_{3} \cos 35^{\circ }(6)-1600(6)-2000(17)=0\)

which gives

\(T_{3}\) = 2334 N

The constant horizontal component \(T_{0}\) of the cable tension can now be found by computing the horizontal component of \(T_{3}\).

\(T_{0} = T_{3} \cos θ_{3} = 2334 \cos 35^{\circ } = 1912 N\)

Substituting i = 2 into Eqs. (6.19) \(T_{0}(\tan \theta _{i+1}-\tan \theta _{i})=W_{i}\) i = 1, 2, . . . , n − 1 , we obtain the equilibrium equation for joint 2:

\(T_{0}(\tan \theta _{3}-\tan \theta _{2})=W_{2}\)

 

\(1912(\tan 35^{\circ } − \tan θ_{2}) = 2000\)

which gives

\(θ_{2} = −19.08^{\circ }\) or \(β_{2} = 19.08^{\circ }\)

With i = 1, Eqs. (6.19) give the equilibrium equation for joint 1:

\(T_{0}(\tan \theta _{2}-\tan \theta _{1})=W_{1}\)

 

\(1912[\tan(−19.08^{\circ }) − tan θ_{1}] = 1600\)

which gives

\(θ_{1} = −49.78^{\circ }\) or \(β_{1} = 49.78^{\circ }\)

The tensions in the first and second segments can now be found from Eqs. (6.18):

\(T_{1}=\frac{T_{0}}{\cos \theta _{1}}=\frac{1912}{\cos (-49.78^{\circ })}=2961 N\)

 

\(T_{2}=\frac{T_{0}}{\cos \theta _{2}}=\frac{1912}{\cos (-19.08^{\circ })}=2023 N\)

The total length s of the cable is

\(s = s_{1} + s_{2} + s_{3}\)

 

\(=\frac{L_{1}}{\cos \beta _{1}}+ \frac{L_{2}}{\cos \beta _{2}}+\frac{L_{3}}{\cos \beta _{3}}\)

 

\(=\frac{6}{\cos 49.78^{\circ }}+ \frac{11}{\cos 19.08^{\circ }}+\frac{7}{\cos 35^{\circ }}\)

= 9.29 + 11.64 + 8.55 = 29.48 N
