Guest Question.

One thousand specimens of 1020 steel were tested to rupture, and the ultimate tensile strengths were reported as follows:

71.5 70.5 69.5 68.5 67.5 66.5 64.5 63.5 62.5 61.5 60.5 59.5 58.5 57.5 56.5 \(\begin{aligned}&\text { Range midpoint }\\&S_{u t}( kpsi )\end{aligned}\)
2 4 11 28 49 82 130 151 138 109 83 31 23 18 2 \(\text { Frequency, } f_{i}\)
Admin
Solution

The worksheet for the data is in

From Eq. (1–6),

 

\(\bar{x}=\frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{k} f_{i} x_{i}\) (1–6)

 

\(\bar{x}=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{k} f_{i}x_{i}=\frac{1}{1000}(63625)=63.625 kpsi\)

 

From Eq. (1–7),

 

\(s_{x}=\sqrt{\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{k} f_{i} x_{i}^{2}-N \bar{x}^{2}}{N-1}}\) (1–7)

 

\(s_{x}=\sqrt{\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{k} f_{i} x_{i}^{2}-N \bar{x}^{2}}{N-1}}=\sqrt{\frac{4054864-1000\left(63.625^{2}\right)}{1000-1}}\)

 

\(=2.594245=2.594 kpsi\)

 

From Eq. (1–4), with \(\mu_{x}=\bar{x} \text { and } \hat{\sigma}_{x}=s_{x}\), the PDF for a normal density function is

 

\(f(x)=\frac{1}{\hat{\sigma}_{x} \sqrt{2 \pi}} \exp \left[-\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{x-\mu_{x}}{\hat{\sigma}_{x}}\right)\right]\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{2.594245 \sqrt{2 \pi}} \exp \left[-\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{x-63.625}{2.594245}\right)^{2}\right]\)

 

For example, f(63.5) 5 0.1536.
