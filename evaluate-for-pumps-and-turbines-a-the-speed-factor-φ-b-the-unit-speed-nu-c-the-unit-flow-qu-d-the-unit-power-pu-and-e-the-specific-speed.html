Guest Question.

Evaluate for pumps and turbines (a) the speed factor Φ (b) the unit speed Nu , (c) the unit flow Qu, (d) the unit power Pu, and (e) the specific speed.

Admin
Solution

(a) By definition , \(\phi =\frac{u}{\sqrt{2gH} } \) . But \(u=r\omega =r\frac{2\pi N}{60}=\frac{\pi DN}{60}=\frac{\pi D_{1}N}{720} \) , where D1 is the diameter in inches and N is the speed in revolutions per minute. Finally,

\(\phi =\frac{\pi D_{1}N}{720}\times \frac{1}{\sqrt{2gH} } =\frac{D_{1}N}{1840\sqrt{H} } \) (for g = 32.2 ft/sec2) (1a)

(b) If D1 = 1 in and H = 1 ft, we obtain from equation (1a) the unit speed Nu. Thus

Nu = 1840 Φ (1b)

which is constant for all wheels of like design if Φ relates to best speed. Also, from (1a),

\(N_{u}=\frac{D_{1}N}{\sqrt{H} } \) in rpm (2)

Thus, for homologous rotating elements, the best speed N varies inversely as the diameter and directly as the square root of H.

(c) For the tangential turbine, the flow Q through the unit can be expressed as

\(Q=cA\sqrt{2gH}=c\frac{\pi d^2_{1}}{4\times 144}\sqrt{2gH}=\frac{c\pi \sqrt{2g} }{576}\left(\frac{d_{1}}{D_{1}} \right)^2 D^2_{1}\sqrt{H} \)

\(=(factor) D^2_{1} \sqrt{H} =Q_{u}D^2_{1}\sqrt{H} \) (3)

For D1 = 1 in and H = 1 ft, the factor is defined as the unit flow Qu .

For reaction turbines and pumps, flow Q can be expressed as the product

c x A x ( velocity component )

The velocity component depends upon the square root of H and the sine of angle α1 (see Fig. 14-1). Thus flow Q can be written in the form of (3), above.

(d) Using expression (3),

power \(P=\frac{\gamma QH}{550}= \frac{\gamma (Q_{u}D^2_{1}\sqrt{H} )H}{550} \) hp

For D1 = 1 in and H = 1 ft, power = γQu/550 = (factor). When the efficiency is included in the power output for turbines and the water horsepower for pumps, the factor becomes the unit power Pu. Then

power \(P=P_{u}D^2_{1}H^{3/2} \) (4)

(e) In equation (4) we may substitute for D1 its value in (2), obtaining

power \(P=P_{u}\frac{N^2_{u}H}{N^2} H^{3/2} \)

Also , \(P_{u}N^2_{u}=\frac{PN^2}{H^{5/2}} \) or \(N_{u}\sqrt{P_{u}}=\frac{N\sqrt{P} }{H^{5/4}} \) (5)

The term \(N_{u}\sqrt{P_{u}} \) called the specific speed NS . Expression (5) then becomes

\(N_{S}=\frac{N\sqrt{P} }{H^{5/4}} \) ( for turbines) (6)

If P is replaced by Q eliminating D1 in equations (2) and (3), we obtain

\(N^2_{u}Q_{u}=\frac{QN^2}{H^{3/2}} \)

and \(N_{S}=\frac{N\sqrt{Q} }{H^{3/4}} \) ( for pumps) (7)

where this specific speed refers to the speed at which 1 cfs would discharge against a 1-ft head. These are the common expressions for pumps and water wheels. For homologous rotating elements in which different fluids may be used, see expressions (9b) : \(P= \frac{\gamma (C^{′}_{Q}D^3N)}{550e}\times \frac{D^2N^2}{g(C^{′}_{N})^2} =C^{′}_{p}\rho D^5N^3 \) , (11a) :\(N_{S}=\frac{N\sqrt{P} }{\sqrt{\rho }(gH)^{5/4} } \) , and (12a) : \(N_{S}=\frac{N\sqrt{Q} }{(gH)^{3/4} } \) , at the beginning of this chapter.
