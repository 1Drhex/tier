Enrollment in Business Statistics Courses (Confidence Interval)

Suppose that there are 1,395 colleges in the United States. From a simple random sample of 400 of these schools, it was found that the sample mean enrollment during the past year in business statistics courses was 320.8 students, and the sample standard deviation was found to be 149.7 students. Estimate the total number of students enrolled in business statistics courses in the previous year, and find a 99% confidence interval.

Admin
Solution

If the population mean is m, an estimate of Nm includes the following:

N = 1,395 n = 400 \( \overline{x }= 320.8 \) s = 149.7

Our point estimate for the total is

\( N \overline{x }= (1,395)(320.8) = 447,516 \)

We estimate that a total of 447,516 students are enrolled in business statistics courses. To obtain interval estimates, Equation 7.15 is used to obtain

\( N \hat{\sigma } _{\overline{x }} = \frac{Ns}{\sqrt{n} }\sqrt{\frac{N-n}{N-1}} = \frac{(1,395)(149,7)}{\sqrt{400} }\sqrt{\frac{995}{1,394}} = 8,821.6 \)

Since the sample size is large, the 99% confidence interval for the population total, Nm, is found by Equation 7.16 and the central limit theorem with \( z_{a/2} = 2.58 \) :

\( N {\overline{x }} \pm z_{a/2} N \hat{\sigma } _{\overline{x }} \)

 

\( 447,516 \pm 2.58(8,821.6) \)

 

\( 447,516 \pm 22,760 \)

Thus, our interval runs from 424,756 to 470,276 students.
