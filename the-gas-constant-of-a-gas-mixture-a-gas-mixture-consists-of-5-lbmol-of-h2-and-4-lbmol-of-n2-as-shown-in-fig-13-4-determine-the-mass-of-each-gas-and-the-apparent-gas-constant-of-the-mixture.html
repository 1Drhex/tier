Guest Question.

The Gas Constant of a Gas Mixture

A gas mixture consists of 5 lbmol of \(H_{2}\) and 4 lbmol of \(N_{2}\), as shown in Fig. 13–4. Determine the mass of each gas and the apparent gas constant of the mixture.

Admin
Solution

The mole numbers of the constituents of a gas mixture are given. The mass of each gas and the apparent gas constant are to be determined.

Properties The molar masses of \(H_{2}\) and \(N_{2}\) are 2.0 and 28.0 lbm/lbmol, respectively .

Analysis The mass of each component is determined from

\(N_{\mathrm{H}_{2}}=5 \mathrm{lbmol} \rightarrow m_{\mathrm{H}_{2}}=N_{\mathrm{H}_{2}} M_{\mathrm{H}_{2}}=(5 \mathrm{lbmol})(2.0 \mathrm{lbm} / \mathrm{lbmol})=10 \mathrm{lbm}\)

\(N_{\mathrm{N}_{2}}=5 \mathrm{lbmol} \rightarrow m_{\mathrm{H}_{2}}=N_{\mathrm{H}_{2}} M_{\mathrm{H}_{2}}=(4 \mathrm{lbmol})(28 \mathrm{lbm} / \mathrm{lbmol})=112 \mathrm{lbm}\)

The total mass and the total number of moles are

\(\begin{array}{l}m_{m}=m_{\mathrm{H}_{2}}+m_{\mathrm{N}_{2}}=10 \mathrm{lbm}+112 \mathrm{lbm}=122 \mathrm{lbm} \\N_{m}=N_{\mathrm{H}_{2}}+N_{\mathrm{N}_{2}}=5 \mathrm{lbmol}+4 \mathrm{lbmol}=9 \mathrm{lbmol}\end{array}\)

The molar mass and the gas constant of the mixture are determined from their definitions,

\(M_{m}=\frac{m_{m}}{N_{m}}=\frac{122 \mathrm{lbm}}{9 \mathrm{lbmol}}=13.56 \mathrm{lbm} / \mathrm{lbmol}\)

and

\(R_{m}=\frac{R_{u}}{M_{m}}=\frac{1.986 \mathrm{Btu} / \mathrm{lbmol} \cdot \mathrm{R}}{13.56 \mathrm{lbm} / \mathrm{lbmol}}=0.1465 \mathrm{Btu} / \mathrm{lbm} \cdot \mathrm{R}\)

Discussion The mole fractions of \(\mathrm{H}_{2}\) and \(\mathrm{N}_{2}\) can be calculated as \(0.556\) and \(0.444\), and the corresponding mass fractions are \(0.082\) and \(0.918\), respectively.
