Guest Question.

A medium office building has 800 luminaires equipped with four 40-W lamps and standard magnetic ballasts. The manager of the building agreed with an energy service company to replace these luminaires with four 32-W lamps and electronic ballasts. In the agreement, a specific calculation procedure was defined to verify the energy savings due to the lighting retrofit. In particular, it was agreed that the lighting system is operated 8 hours per day,5 days per week,and 50 weeks per year. In addition, the wattage rating is set to be 192 W for the standard luminaire and 140 W for the energy-efficient luminaire. Estimate the annual energy savings stipulated by the agreement. Determine the cost savings due to the lighting retrofit if the electricity cost is $0.07/kWh.

Admin
Solution

The reduction in the electrical energy input for all the luminaires is first determined:

\(ΔkW_{light}\) =[(0.192 − 0.140)kW]* 800 = 41.6 kW

The total number of hours for operating the lighting system during one year can be estimated as follows

\(Δt_{light}\) = 8hrs/day *5days/week *50weeks/year = 2,000hrs/yr

Thus, the annual energy savings due to the lighting retrofit are:

\(ΔkWh_{light}\)= 41.6kW* 2,000hrs/yr = 83,200kWh/yr

Therefore, the annual cost savings that resulted from the stipulated savings are:

\(ΔC_{light}\)= $0.07/kWh*83,200kWh/yr = $5,824/yr
