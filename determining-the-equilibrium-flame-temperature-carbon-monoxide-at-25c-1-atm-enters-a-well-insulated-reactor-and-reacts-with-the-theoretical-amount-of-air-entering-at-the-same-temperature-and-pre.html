Determining the Equilibrium Flame Temperature

Carbon monoxide at 25°C, 1 atm enters a well-insulated reactor and reacts with the theoretical amount of air entering at the same temperature and pressure. An equilibrium mixture of \(CO _{2}, CO , O _{2}\), and \(N _{2}\) exits the reactor at a pressure of 1 atm. For steady-state operation and negligible effects of kinetic and potential energy, determine the composition and temperature of the exiting mixture, in K.

Admin
Solution

Known Carbon monoxide at 25°C, 1 atm reacts with the theoretical amount of air at 25°C, 1 atm to form an equilibrium mixture of \(CO _{2}, CO , O _{2}, \text { and } N _{2}\) at temperature T and a pressure of 1 atm.

Find Determine the composition and temperature of the exiting mixture.

Schematic and Given Data:

Engineering Model

1. The control volume shown on the accompanying sketch by a dashed line operates at steady state with \(\dot{Q}_{ cv }=0, \dot{W}_{ cv }=0\), and negligible effects of kinetic and potential energy.

2. The entering gases are modeled as ideal gases.

3. The exiting mixture is an ideal gas mixture at equilibrium wherein \(N _{2}\) is inert.

Analysis The overall reaction is the same as in the solution to :

 

\(CO +\frac{1}{2} O _{2}+1.88 N _{2} \rightarrow z CO +\frac{z}{2} O _{2}+(1-z) CO _{2}+1.88 N _{2}\)

 

By assumption 3, the exiting mixture is an equilibrium mixture. The equilibrium constant expression developed in the solution to is

 

\(K(T)=\frac{z(z / 2)^{1 / 2}}{(1-z)}\left(\frac{p / p_{ ref }}{(5.76+z) / 2}\right)^{1 / 2}\) (a)

 

Since p = 1 atm, Eq. (a) reduces to

 

\(K(T)=\frac{z}{(1-z)}\left(\frac{z}{5.76+z}\right)^{1 / 2}\) (b)

 

This equation involves two unknowns: z and the temperature T of the exiting equilibrium mixture.

Another equation involving the two unknowns is obtained from an energy rate balance of the form Eq. 13.12b, which reduces with assumption 1 to

 

\(\frac{\dot{Q}_{ cv }}{\dot{n}_{ F }}-\frac{\dot{W}_{ cv }}{\dot{n}_{ F }}=\bar{h}_{ P }-\bar{h}_{ R }\) (13.12b)

 

\(\bar{h}_{ R }=\bar{h}_{ P }\) (c)

 

where

 

\(\bar{h}_{ R }=\left(\bar{h}_{ f }^{\circ}+\Delta \bar{h}^{\nearrow0}\right)_{ CO }+\frac{1}{2}\left(\bar{h}_{ f }^{\circ \nearrow0}+\Delta \bar{h}^{\nearrow0}\right)_{ O _{2}}+1.88\left(\bar{h}_{ f }^{\circ \nearrow0}+\Delta \bar{h}^{\nearrow0}\right)_{ N _{2}}\)

 

and

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{h}_{ P }=& z\left(\bar{h}_{ f }^{\circ}+\Delta \bar{h}\right)_{ CO }+\frac{z}{2}\left(\bar{h}_{ f }^{\circ\nearrow0}+\Delta \bar{h}\right)_{ O _{2}}+(1-z)\left(\bar{h}_{ f }^{\circ}+\Delta \bar{h}\right)_{ CO _{2}} \\&+1.88\left(\bar{h}_{ f }^{\circ\nearrow0}+\Delta \bar{h}\right)_{ N _{2}}\end{aligned}\)

 

The enthalpy of formation terms set to zero are those for oxygen and nitrogen. Since the reactants enter at 25°C, the corresponding \(\Delta \bar{h}\) terms also vanish. Collecting and rearranging, we get

 

\(\begin{aligned}&z(\Delta \bar{h})_{ CO }+\frac{z}{2}(\Delta \bar{h})_{ O _{2}}+(1-z)(\Delta \bar{h})_{ CO _{2}}+1.88(\Delta \bar{h})_{ N _{2}} \\&+(1-z)\left[\left(\bar{h}_{ f }^{\circ}\right)_{ CO _{2}}-\left(\bar{h}_{ f }^{\circ}\right)_{ CO }\right]=0\end{aligned}\) (d)

 

Equations (b) and (d) are simultaneous equations involving the unknowns z and T. When solved iteratively using tabular data, the results are z = 0.125 and T = 2399 K, as can be verified. The composition of the equilibrium mixture, in kmol per kmol of CO entering the reactor, is then \(0.125 CO , 0.0625 O _{2}, 0.875 CO _{2}, 1.88 N _{2}\).
