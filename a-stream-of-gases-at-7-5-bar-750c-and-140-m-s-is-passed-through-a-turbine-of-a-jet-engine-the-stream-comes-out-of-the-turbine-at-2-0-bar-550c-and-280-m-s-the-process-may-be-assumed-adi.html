Guest Question.

A stream of gases at 7.5 bar, 750°C and 140 m/s is passed through a turbine of a jet engine. The stream comes out of the turbine at 2.0 bar, 550°C and 280 m/s. The process may be assumed adiabatic. The enthalpies of gas at the entry and exit of the turbine are 950 kJ/kg and 650 kJ/kg of gas respectively.Determine the capacity of the turbine if the gas flow is 5 kg/s.

Admin
Solution

Refer Fig. 2.46.

 

Conditions at ‘1’ :

Pressure, \(p_{1}\) = 7.5 bar = 7.5 × \(10^{5} N/m^{2}\), 750°C

Velocity, \(C_{1}\) = 140 m/s

Enthalpy, \(h_{1}\) = 950 kJ/kg

 

Conditions at ‘2’ :

Pressure, \( p_{2}\) = 2.0 bar = 2 × \(10^{5} N/m^{2}\) , 550°C

Velocity, \(C_{2}\) = 280 m/s

Enthalpy, \(h_{2} \)= 650 kJ/kg

Gas flow, m = 5 kg/s.

 

Capacity of the turbine :

Considering the flow of gas as 1 kg and neglecting the change in potential energy, we can write the steady flow energy equation for the turbine as

 

\(h_{1}+\frac{C_{1}^{2}}{2}\pm Q=h_{2}+\frac{C_{2}^{2}}{2}\pm W\)

 

Q = 0 as the system is adiabatic and W should be taken as +ve since it develops work.

 

\(\therefore\) \( h_{1}+\frac{C_{1}^{2}}{2}=h_{2}+\frac{C_{2}^{2}}{2}W\)

 

\(\therefore\) \( W=\left ( h_{1}-h_{2} \right )+\frac{C_{1}^{2}-C_{2}^{2}}{2}=10^{3}\left ( 950-650 \right )+\frac{140^{2}-280^{2}}{2}\)

 

\(= 10^{3} × 300 – 29.4 × 10^{3}\)

 

\(= 270.6 × 10^{3} \) J/kg = 270.6 kJ/kg.

 

Power capacity of the turbine

= m W = 5 × 270.6 = 1353 kJ/s

= 1353 kW.
