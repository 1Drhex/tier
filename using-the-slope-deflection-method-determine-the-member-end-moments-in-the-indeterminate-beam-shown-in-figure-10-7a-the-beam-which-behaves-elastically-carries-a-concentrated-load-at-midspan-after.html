Guest Question.

Using the slope-deflection method, determine the member end moments in the indeterminate beam shown in Figure 10.7a. The beam, which behaves elastically, carries a concentrated load at midspan. After the end moments are determined, draw the shear and moment curves. If \( I=240 in.^{4}\) and \( E=30000 \frac{kips}{in.^{2}}\) , compute the magnitude of the slope at joint B.

Admin
Solution

Since joint A is fixed against rotation, \( \Theta _{A}=0\) ; therefore, the only unknown displacement is \( \Theta _{B}\) , the rotation of joint B (\( \psi _{AB} \) is, of course, zero since no support settlements occur). Using the slope-deflection equation

 

\( M_{NF}=\frac{2EI}{L}\left ( 2\Theta _{N}+\Theta _{F}-3\psi _{NF} \right )+FEM_{NF}\) (10.16)

 

and the values in Appendix Table A.4a for the fixed-end moments produced by a concentrated load at midspan, we can express the member end moments shown in Figure 10.7b as

 

\( M_{AB}=\frac{2EI}{L}\left ( \Theta _{B} \right )-\frac{PL}{8} \) (1)

 

\( M_{BA}=\frac{2EI}{L}\left (2 \Theta _{B} \right )+\frac{PL}{8} \) (2)

 

To determine \( \Theta _{B}\) , we next write the equation of moment equilibrium at joint B (Figure 10.7c).

 

\( ⟳+\sum M_{B}=0\)

 

\( M_{BA}=0 \) (3)

 

Substituting the value of \( M_{BA} \) given by Equation 2 into Equation 3 and solving for \( \Theta _{B}\) give

 

\( \frac{4EI}{L}\Theta _{B}+\frac{PL}{8}=0\)

 

\( \Theta _{B}=-\frac{PL^{2}}{32EI} \) (4)

 

where the minus sign indicates both that the B end of member AB and joint B rotate in the counterclockwise direction. To determine the member end moments, the value of \( \Theta _{B}\) given by Equation 4 is substituted into Equations 1 and 2 to give

 

\( M_{AB}=\frac{2EI}{L}\left ( \frac{-PL^{2}}{32EI} \right )-\frac{PL}{8}=-\frac{3PL}{16}=-54 \) kip.ft

 

\( M_{BA}=\frac{4EI}{L}\left ( \frac{-PL^{2}}{32EI} \right )-\frac{PL}{8}=0\)

 

Although we know that \( M_{BA}\) is zero since the support at B is a pin, the computation of \( M_{BA} \) serves as a check.
To complete the analysis, we apply the equations of statics to a free body of member AB (Figure 10.7d).

 

\( ⟳+ \sum M_{A}=0\)

 

\( 0=\left ( 16kips \right )\left ( 9ft \right )-V_{BA}\left ( 18ft \right )-54\) kip.ft

 

\( V_{BA}=5 \) kips

 

\( +↑\sum F_{y}=0\)

 

\( 0=V_{BA}+V_{AB}-16\)

 

\( V_{AB}=11 \) kips

 

To evaluate \( \Theta _{B}\) , we express all variables in Equation 4 in units of inches and kips.

 

\( \Theta _{B}=-\frac{PL^{2}}{32EI}=-\frac{16\left ( 18\times 12 \right )^{2}}{32\left ( 30000 \right )140}=-0.0032 \) rad

 

Expressing \( \Theta _{B} \) in degrees, we obtain

 

\( \frac{2\pi rad}{360^{\circ}}=\frac{-0.0032}{\Theta _{B}}\)

 

\( \Theta _{B}=-0.183^{\circ} \)

 

where the slope \( \Theta _{B}\) is very small and not discernible to the naked eye. Note that when you analyze a structure by the slope-eflection method, you must follow a rigid format in formulating the equilibrium equations. There is no need to guess the direction of unknown member end moments since the solution of the equilibrium equations will automatically produce the correct direction for displacements and moments. For example, in Figure 10.7b we show the moments\( M_{AB} \) and \( M_{BA} \) clockwise on the ends of member AB even though intuitively we may recognize from a sketch of the deflected shape in Figure 10.7a that moment \( M_{AB} \) must act in the counterclockwise direction because the beam is bent concave downward at the left end by the load. When the solution indicates \( M_{AB} \) is −54 kip·ft, we know from the negative sign that \( M_{AB} \) actually acts on the end of the member in the counterclockwise direction.
