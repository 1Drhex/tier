Guest Question.

Derive the equation of motion for the system in Figure 5.11, consisting of two masses connected by an inextensible string around a frictionless, massless pulley.

Admin
Solution

The free-body forces are superimposed in Figure 5.11. To apply d’Alembert’s principle, the system must undergo a virtual displacement compatible with the constraints that guide the motion (string and inclines on which the masses move). The assumption that the connecting string is inextensible means that only one coordinate is needed to specify the system configuration. (An elastic string would require two coordinates for the system configuration.) \(F_{1}\) and \(F_{2}\) are friction forces acting in the directions opposite to that of the motion.

Assume mass \(m_{2}\) is displaced in a virtual displacement \(\delta r _{2}\) as shown. The string constraint equation is its length \(l=\left| r _{1}\right|+\left| r _{2}\right|+p\), where ρ is the length of string in contact with the pulley. Since \(l\) is constant, \(\delta r _{1}+\delta r _{2}=\) 0. Applying d’Alembert’s principle, Equation 5.10, for \(i=1,2\), we have

\(\left[\left(m_{1} g + F _{1}\right)-m_{1} \ddot{ r }_{1}\right] \cdot \delta r _{1}+\left[\left(m_{2} g + F _{2}\right)-m_{2} \ddot{ r }_{2}\right] \cdot \delta r _{2}=0\)

Expanding the dot products we find

\(\begin{gathered}m_{1} g\left(\sin \theta_{1}+\mu \cos \theta_{1}\right) \delta r_{1}+m_{2} g\left(\sin \theta_{2}-\mu \cos \theta_{2}\right) \delta r_{2} \\-\left(m_{1} \ddot{r}_{1} \delta r_{1}+m_{2} \ddot{r}_{2} \delta r_{2}\right)=0\end{gathered}\)

where \(\mu\) is the dynamic coefficient of friction between each block and its surface. We use the relations\(\delta r _{1}\) = – \(\delta r _{2}\) and \(\ddot{r}_{1}=-\ddot{r}_{2}\) to find

\(\begin{gathered}{\left[-m_{1} g\left(\sin \theta_{1}+\mu \cos \theta_{1}\right)+m_{2} g\left(\sin \theta_{2}-\mu \cos \theta_{2}\right)\right.} \\\left.-\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right) \ddot{r}_{2}\right] \delta r_{2}=0\end{gathered}\)

Since the variation \(\delta r _{2}\) is arbitrary, the expression in the square brackets must be equal to zero,

\(\begin{aligned}\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right) \ddot{r}_{2}=&-m_{1} g\left(\sin \theta_{1}+\mu \cos \theta_{1}\right) \\&+m_{2} g\left(\sin \theta_{2}-\mu \cos \theta_{2}\right)\end{aligned}\)

This is the equation of motion for the single degree-offreedom system in terms of coordinate \(r_{2}\). If \(\ddot{r}_{2}=0\), we recover the equation for static equilibrium,

\(m_{1}\left(\sin \theta_{1}+\mu \cos \theta_{1}\right)=m_{2}\left(\sin \theta_{2}-\mu \cos \theta_{2}\right)\)

which is a nonlinear relation linking the masses, the coefficient of friction, and the two angles of inclination.
