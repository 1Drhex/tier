Guest Question.

A pipe with an outside diameter of 8.50 in. and a wall thickness of 0.25 in. is subjected to the 3-kip load shown in Figure P15.65. The internal pressure in the pipe is 320 psi.
(a) Determine the normal and shear stresses on the side of the pipe at point K.
(b) Determine the principal stresses and maximum inplane shear stress at point K and show the orientation of these stresses on an appropriate sketch.
(c) Compute the absolute maximum shear stress at K.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&d=8.50 \text { in. }-2(0.25 in .)=8.00 in . \\&A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[(8.50 in .)^{2}-(8.00 in .)^{2}\right]=6.479535 in. ^{2} \\&J=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[(8.50 in .)^{4}-(8.00 in .)^{4}\right]=110.354578 in .{ }^{4} \\&I_{y}=I_{z}=\frac{\pi}{64}\left[(8.50 \text { in. })^{4}-(8.00 \text { in. })^{4}\right]=55.177289 in .{ }^{4} \\&Q=\frac{1}{12}\left[(8.50 in .)^{3}-(8.00 in .)^{3}\right]=8.510417 in .^{3}\end{aligned}\)

Vector expression for the 3-kip load:
The 3-kip load can be expressed in vector form as:

\(F =-(3 kips ) \sin 55^{\circ} j +(3 kips ) \cos 55^{\circ} k\)

The equivalent forces acting at H and K are thus

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{x}=0 kips \\&F_{y}=-2.45746 kips \\&F_{z}=1.72073 kips\end{aligned}\)

The position vector from the section of interest to a point on the line of action of F is:

\(r =(24 \text { in. }) i +(48 \text { in. }) k\)

The equivalent moments are found from the cross-product r Ã— F.

\(\begin{aligned}r \times F &=\left|\begin{array}{ccc} i & j & k \\24 & 0 & 48 \\0 & -2.45746 & 1.72073\end{array}\right| \\&=117.958 kip \text {-in. } i -41.298 \text { kip-in. } j -58.979 \text { kip-in. } k\end{aligned}\)

Equivalent moments:

\(\begin{aligned}M_{x} &=117.958 kip – in. \\M_{y} &=-41.298 kip – in. \\M_{z} &=-58.979 kip – in.\end{aligned}\)

Each of the non-zero forces and moments will be evaluated to determine whether stresses are created at the point of interest

(a) Consider point K.
Force \(F _{y}\) creates a transverse shear stress in the xy plane at K. The magnitude of this shear stress is:

\(\tau_{x y}=\frac{(2.45746 kips )\left(8.510417 in .{ }^{3}\right)}{\left(55.177289 in. ^{4}\right)[(8.50 in .)-(8.00 in .)]}=0.758 ksi\)

Force \(F _{z}\) does not cause either a normal stress or a shear stress at K.

Moment \(M _{x}\), which is a torque, creates a torsion shear stress in the xy plane at K. The magnitude of this shear stress is:

\(\tau_{x y}=\frac{M_{x} c}{J}=\frac{(117.958 kip – in .)(8.50 in . / 2)}{110.354578 in .^{4}}=4.543 ksi\)

Moment \(M _{y}\) creates bending stress at K. The magnitude of this stress is:

\(\sigma_{x}=\frac{M_{y} z}{I_{y}}=\frac{(41.298 kip – in. )(8.50 in . / 2)}{55.177289 in .^{4}}=3.181 ksi\)

Moment \(M _{z}\) does not create bending stress at K because K is located on the neutral axis for bending about the z axis.

Stresses due to internal pressure:
The 320-psi internal fluid pressure creates tension normal stresses in the 0.25-in.-thick wall of the pipe.
The longitudinal stress (which acts in the x direction) in the pipe wall is:

\(\sigma_{\text {long }}=\frac{p d}{4 t}=\frac{(320 psi )(8.00 in .)}{4(0.25 \text { in. })}=2,560 psi =2.56 ksi ( T )\)

and the circumferential stress is:

\(\sigma_{\text {hoop }}=\frac{p d}{2 t}=\frac{(320 psi )(8.00 in .)}{2(0.25 in .)}=5,120 psi =5.12 ksi ( T )\)

The hoop stress acts in the y direction at K.

Summary of stresses at K:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=-3.181 ksi +2.56 ksi \\&=-0.621 ksi =0.621 ksi ( C ) \\\sigma_{y} &=5.12 ksi ( T ) \\\tau_{x y} &=-0.758 ksi -4.543 ksi \\&=-5.301 ksi =-5.30 ksi\end{aligned}\)

 

 

(b) Principal stresses at K:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq. (12.12).

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{(-0.621)+(5.12)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(-0.621)-(5.12)}{2}\right)^{2}+(-5.301)^{2}} \\&=2.250 \pm 6.028\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{p 1}=8.28 ksi \text { and } \quad \sigma_{p 2}=-3.78 ksi\\&\tau_{\max }=6.03 ksi \quad \text{(maximum in-plane shear str ) } \\&\sigma_{\text {avg }}=2.25 ksi ( T ) \quad \text { (normal stress on planes of maximum in-plane shear stress) }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan 2 \theta_{p} &=\frac{-5.301}{[(-0.621)-(5.12)] / 2}=\frac{-5.301}{-2.870}=1.846703 \\&\left.\therefore \theta_{p}=30.78^{\circ} \quad \text { (counterclockwise from the } x \text { axis to the direction of } \sigma_{p 2}\right)\end{aligned}\)

 

(c) Absolute maximum shear stress at K:

\(\tau_{\text {abs } \max }=\frac{8.278 ksi -3.779 ksi }{2}=6.028 ksi =6.03 ksi\)
