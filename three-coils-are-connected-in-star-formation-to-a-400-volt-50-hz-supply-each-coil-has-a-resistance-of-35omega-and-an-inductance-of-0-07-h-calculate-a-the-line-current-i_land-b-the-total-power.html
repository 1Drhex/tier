Guest Question.

Three coils are connected in star formation to a 400 volt 50 Hz supply, each coil has a resistance of 35\(\Omega\) and an inductance of 0.07 H. Calculate (a) the line current \(I_{L}\)and (b) the total power dissipated.

Admin
Solution

Step 1
Calculate inductive reactance
\(X_{L} = 2\pi f L\)
= 2 × 3.142 × 50 × 0.07
= 22 \(\Omega \)
An impedance triangle could be drawn if required as follows: Draw to scale a line representing resistance on the horizontal (opposite), at right angles to line R. Draw a line representing inductive reactance (adjacent). The length of the hypotenuse will represent the impedance Z.
By calculation
\(Z^{2} = X^{2}_{L} + R^{2}\)
\(Z^{2}= 22^{2} + 35^{2}\)
Z = \(\sqrt{22^{2} + 35^{2}}\)
= 41.34 \(\Omega \)
Impedance (Z) is 41.34\(\Omega\) per phase.
Because the circuit has inductive reactance and resistance, the load will have a power factor, this must now be calculated:
Power factor \(=\frac{R}{Z}\)
=\(\frac{35}{41.34}\)
= 0.84
Line current can now be calculated
\(I_{L} = \frac{U_{L}}{Z \times \sqrt{3}}\) (\(U_{L}\)}is line voltage)
= \(\frac{400}{41.34\times 1.732\times 0.84}\)
= 5.59A (in star \(I_{L}=I_{P} \)Remember this is the current per phase)
Power can now be calculated
P =\( \sqrt{3}\times U_{L}\times I_{L}\times \cos \Phi\)
= 1.732 × 400 × 5.59 × 0.84
= 3250 watts (3.25 kW)
