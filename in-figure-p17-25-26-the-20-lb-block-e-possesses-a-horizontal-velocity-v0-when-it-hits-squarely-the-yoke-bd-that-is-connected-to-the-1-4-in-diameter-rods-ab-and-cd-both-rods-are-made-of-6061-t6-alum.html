Guest Question.

In Figure P17.25/26, the 20-lb block E possesses a horizontal velocity \(v_{0}\) when it hits squarely the yoke BD that is connected to the 1/4-in.-diameter rods AB and CD. Both rods are made of 6061-T6 aluminum that has a yield strength of \(\sigma_{Y}\) = 40 ksi and an elastic modulus of E = 10,000 ksi, and both have a length of L = 30 in. Yoke BD may be assumed to be rigid. Calculate the maximum allowable velocity \(v_{0}\) of block E if factor of safety of 3 with respect to yield is required for the maximum normal stress in the rods.

Admin
Solution
\(A=\frac{\pi}{4}(0.25 in .)^{2}=0.049087 in .^{2}\)

Allowable dynamic stress:

\(\sigma_{\max }=\frac{\sigma_{Y}}{ FS }=\frac{40 ksi }{3}=13.3333 ksi\)

Allowable dynamic force:

\(F_{\max }=\sigma_{\max } A=(13.3333 ksi )\left(0.049087 in .^{2} \times 2\right)=1.3090 \text { kips }=1,308.997 lb\)

Strain energy in rod at impact from Equation (17.12):

\(U=\frac{F_{\max }^{2} L}{2 A E}=\frac{(1,308.997 lbs )^{2}(30 in. )(1 ft / 12 in .)}{2\left(0.049087 in .{ }^{2} \times 2\right)(10,000 ksi )}=2.1817 lb – ft\)

Equate kinetic energy and strain energy:

\(\frac{1}{2} m v^{2}=\frac{F_{\max }^{2} L}{2 A E}\)

and compute the maximum velocity of block E.

\(v=\sqrt{\frac{2(2.1817 lb – ft )}{\left(\frac{20 lb }{32.1740 ft / s ^{2}}\right)}}=2.6494 ft / s =2.65 ft / s\)
