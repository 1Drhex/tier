Guest Question.

Design a straight-bevel gear mesh for shaft centerlines that intersect perpendicularly, to deliver 6.85 hp at 900 rev/min with a gear ratio of 3:1, temperature of 300°F, normal pressure angle of 20°, using a design factor of 2. The load is uniform-uniform. Although the minimum number of teeth on the pinion is 13, which will mesh with 31 or more teeth without interference, use a pinion of 20 teeth. The material is to be AGMA grade 1 and the teeth are to be crowned. The reliability goal is 0.995 with a pinion life of \(10^{9}\) revolutions.

Admin
Solution

First we list the a priori decisions and their immediate consequences.

Function: 6.85 hp at 900 rev/min, gear ratio \(m_{G}\) = 3, 300°F environment, neither gear straddle-mounted, \(K_{mb}\) = 1.25 [Eq. (15–11)], R = 0.995 at \(10^{9}\) revolutions of the pinion,

\(K_{Hβ} = K_{mb} + 5.6(10−6)b^{2}\) (SI units) (15–11)

Eq. (15–14):

\(C_{L} =\begin{cases} 2&10^{3} ≤ N_{L} < 10^{4}\\3.4822N^{−0.0602}_{L}&10^{4} ≤ N_{L} ≤ 10^{10}\end{cases}\)

\(Z_{NT}=\begin{cases} 2&10^{3} ≤ n_{L} < 10^{4}\\3.4822n^{−0.0602}_{L}&10^{4} ≤ n_{L} ≤ 10^{10}\end{cases}\) (15-14)

\((C_{L} )_{G} = 3.4822(10^{9}/3)^{−0.0602} = 1.068\)
\((C_{L} )_{P} = 3.4822(10^{9})^{−0.0602} = 1\)

Eq. (15–15):

\(K_{L} =\begin{cases} 2.7&10^{2} ≤ N_{L} < 10^{3}\\6.1514N^{−0.1182}_{L}&10^{3} ≤ N_{L} < 3(10^{6})\\1.6831N^{−0.0323}_{L}&3(10^{6}) ≤ N_{L} ≤ 10^{10} general\\1.3558N^{−0.0178}_{L}&33(10^{6}) ≤ N_{L} ≤ 10^{10} critical\end{cases}\)

\(Y_{NT} =\begin{cases} 2.7&10^{2} ≤ n_{L} < 10^{3}\\6.1514n^{−0.1182}_{L}&10^{3} ≤ n_{L} < 3(10^{6})\\1.6831n^{−0.0323}_{L}&3(10^{6}) ≤ n_{L} ≤ 10^{10} general\\1.3558n^{−0.0323}_{L}&33(10^{6}) ≤ n_{L} ≤ 10^{10} critical\end{cases}\) (15-15)

\((K_{L} )_{G} = 1.683(10^{9}/3)^{−0.0323} = 0.8929\)
\((K_{L} )_{P} = 1.683(10^{9})^{−0.0323} = 0.8618\)

Eq. (15–19):

\(Y_{Z} = K_{R} =0.50 − 0.25 log(1 − R) 0.99 ≤ R ≤ 0.999\) (15.19)

\(K_{R} = 0.50 − 0.25 log(1 − 0.995) = 1.075\)
\(C_{R} = \sqrt{K_{R}} = \sqrt{1.075} = 1.037\)

Eq. (15–18):

\(K_{T} =\begin{cases}1&32°F ≤ t ≤ 250°F\\(460 + t)/710&t > 250°F\end{cases}\)

\(K_{θ} =\begin{cases}1&0°C ≤ θ ≤ 120°C\\(273 + θ)/393&θ > 120°C\end{cases}\) (14-18)

\(K_{T} = C_{T} = (460 + 300)/710 = 1.070\)

Design factor: \(n_{d} = 2, S_{F} = 2, S_{H} = \sqrt{2} = 1.414\).

Tooth system: crowned, straight-bevel gears, normal pressure angle 20°,

Eq. (15–13):

\(K_{x} = Y_{β} = 1 \) (15–13)

\(K_{x} = 1\)

Eq. (15–12):

\(C_{xc} = Z_{xc} =\begin{cases}1.5&properly crowned teeth\\2.0&or larger uncrowned teeth\end{cases}\) (15-12)

\(C_{xc} = 1.5\).

With \(N_{P}\) = 20 teeth, \(N_{G}\) = (3)20 = 60 teeth and from Fig. 15–14,

\( γ= tan^{−1}(N_{P}/N_{G}) = tan^{−1}(20/60) = 18.43° Γ = tan^{−1}(60/20) = 71.57°\)

From Figs. 15–6 and 15–7, I = 0.0825,\( J_{P}\) = 0.248, and \(J_{G}\) = 0.202. Note that \(J_{P} > J_{G}\).

Decision 1: Trial diametral pitch, \(P_{d}\) = 8 teeth/in.

Eq. (15–10):

\(K_{s} =\begin{cases} 0.4867 + 0.2132/P_{d}&0.5 ≤ P_{d} ≤ 16 in^{−1}\\ 0.5&Pd > 16 in^{−1}\end{cases}\) (U.S. customary units)

\(Y_{x} =\begin{cases} 0.5&m_{et} < 1.6 mm\\ 0.4867 + 0.008 339m_{et}&1.6 ≤ m_{et} ≤ 50 mm\end{cases}\) (SI units) (15–10)

\(K_{s} = 0.4867 + 0.2132/8 = 0.5134\)
\(d_{P} = N_{P}/P_{d} = 20/8 = 2.5 in\)
\(d_{G} = 2.5(3) = 7.5 in\)
\(v_{t} = πd_{P}n_{P}/12 = π(2.5)900/12 = 589.0 ft/min\)
\(W^{t} = 33 000 hp/v_{t} = 33 000(6.85)/589.0 = 383.8 lbf\)

Eq. (15–25):

\(A_{0} =\frac{d_{P}}{2 sin γ} =\frac{d_{G}}{2 sin Γ}\) (15–25)

\(A_{0} = d_{P}/(2 sin γ ) = 2.5/(2 sin 18.43°) = 3.954 in\)

Eq. (15–24):

\(F = min(0.3A_{0}, 10/P_{d} )\) (15–24)

\(F = min(0.3A_{0}, 10/P_{d} ) = min[0.3(3.954), 10/8] = min(1.186, 1.25) = 1.186 in\)

Decision 2: Let F = 1.25 in. Then,

Eq. (15–9):

\(C_{s} =\begin{cases} 0.5&F < 0.5 in\\0.125F + 0.4375&0.5 ≤ F ≤ 4.5 in\\1&F > 4.5 in \end{cases}\) (U.S. customary units)

\(Z_{x} =\begin{cases} 0.5& b < 12.7 mm\\0.004 92b + 0.4375 &12.7 ≤ b ≤ 114.3 mm\\1& b > 114.3 mm\end{cases}\) (SI units) (15.9)

\(C_{s} = 0.125(1.25) + 0.4375 = 0.5937\)

Eq. (15–11):

\(K_{m} = 1.25 + 0.0036(1.25)^{2} = 1.256\)

Decision 3: Let the transmission accuracy number be 6. Then, from Eq. (15–6),

\(A = 50 + 56(1 − B)\)
\(B = 0.25(12 − Q_{v})^{2/3}\)
(15–6)

\(B = 0.25(12 − 6)^{2/3} = 0.8255\)

\(A = 50 + 56(1 − 0.8255) = 59.77\)

Eq. (15–5):

\(K_{v} =\left(\frac{A + \sqrt{v_{t}}}{A}\right)^{B}\) (U.S. customary units)
\(K_{v} =\left(\frac{A + \sqrt{200v_{et}}}{A}\right)^{B}\) (SI units) (15–5)

\(K_{v} =\left( \frac{59.77 + \sqrt{589.0}}{59.77}\right)^{0.8255}= 1.325\)

Decision 4: Pinion and gear material and treatment. Carburize and case-harden grade ASTM 1320 to

Core 21 HRC (\(H_{B}\) is 229 Brinell)
Case 55-64 HRC (\(H_{B}\) is 515 Brinell)

From Table 15–4, \(s_{ac}\) = 200 000 psi and from Table 15–6,\( s_{at}\) = 30 000 psi.

Gear bending: From Eq. (15–3), the bending stress is

\(s_{t} =\frac{W^{t}}{F}P_{d}K_{o}K_{v} \frac{K_{s}K_{m}}{K_{x} J}\) (U.S. customary units)
\(σ_{F} =\frac{1000W^{t}}{b} \frac{K_{A}K_{v}}{m_{et}} \frac{Y_{x} K_{Hβ}}{Y_{β}Y_{J}}\) (SI units)
(15–3)

\((s_{t} )_{G} =\frac{W^{t}}{F}P_{d}K_{o}K_{v} \frac{K_{s}K_{m}}{K_{x} J_{G}} =\frac{383.8}{1.25} 8(1)1.325 \frac{0.5134(1.256)}{(1)0.202}\)

= 10 390 psi

The bending strength, from Eq. (15–4), is given by

\(s_{wt} =\frac{s_{at} K_{L}}{S_{F} K_{T} K_{R}}\) (U.S. customary units)
\(σ_{F P} =\frac{σ_{F lim}Y_{NT}}{S_{F} K_{θ}Y_{z}}\) (SI units)
(15–4)

\((s_{wt} )_{G} =\left(\frac{s_{at} K_{L}}{S_{F} K_{T} K_{R}}\right)_{G} =\frac{30 000(0.8929)}{2(1.070)1.075} = 11 640 psi\)

The strength exceeds the stress by a factor of 11640/10390 = 1.12, giving an actual factor of safety of \((S_{F} )_{G}\) = 2(1.12) = 2.24.

Pinion bending: The bending stress can be found from

\((s_{t} )_{P} = (s_{t} )_{G} \frac{J_{G}}{J_{P}} = 10 390 \frac{0.202}{0.248 }= 8463 psi\)

The bending strength, again from Eq. (15–4), is given by

\((s_{wt} )_{P} =\left(\frac{s_{at} K_{L}}{S_{F} K_{T} K_{R}}\right)_{P} =\frac{30 000 (0.8618)}{2(1.070)1.075} = 11 240 psi\)

The strength exceeds the stress by a factor of 11 240/8463 = 1.33, giving an actual factor of safety of \((S_{F} )_{P}\) = 2(1.33) = 2.66.

Gear wear: The load-induced contact stress for the pinion and gear, from Eq. (15–1), is

\(s_{c} = σ_{c} = C_{p} \left( \frac{W^{t}}{Fd_{P} I}K_{o}K_{v}K_{m}C_{s}C_{xc}\right)^{1/2}\) (U.S. customary units)
\(σ_{H} = Z_{E} \left(\frac{1000W^{t}}{bd Z_{1}}K_{A}K_{v}K_{Hβ} Z_{x} Z_{xc}\right)^{1/2}\) (SI units)
(15–1)

\(s_{c} = C_{p} \left( \frac{W^{t}}{Fd_{P} I}K_{o}K_{v}K_{m}C_{s}C_{xc}\right)^{1/2}\)

\(= 2290 \left( \frac{383.8}{1.25(2.5)0.0825}(1)1.325(1.256)0.5937(1.5)\right)^{1/2}\)
= 107 560 psi

From Eq. (15–2) the contact strength of the gear is

\(s_{wc} = (σ_{c})_{all} =\frac{s_{ac}C_{L}C_{H}}{S_{H} K_{T}C_{R}}\) (U.S. customary units)
\(σ_{H P} =\frac{σ_{H lim}Z_{NT} Z_{W}}{S_{H} K_{θ} Z_{Z}}\) (SI units)
(15–2)

\((s_{wc})_{G} =\left(\frac{s_{ac}C_{L}C_{H}}{S_{H} K_{T}C_{R}}\right)_{G} =\frac{200 000(1.068)(1)}{\sqrt{2}(1.070)1.037} = 136 120 psi\)

The strength exceeds the stress by a factor of 136 120/107 560 = 1.266, giving an actual factor of safety of \((S_{H})^{2}_{G} = 1.266^{2}(2) = 3.21\).

Pinion wear: From Eq. (15–2) the contact strength of the pinion is

\((s_{wc})_{P} =\left(\frac{s_{ac}C_{L}C_{H}}{S_{H} K_{T}C_{R}}\right)_{P} =\frac{200 000(1)(1)}{\sqrt{2}(1.070)1.037 }= 127 450 psi\)

The strength exceeds the stress by a factor of 136 120/127 450 = 1.068, giving an actual factor of safety of \((S_{H})^{2}_{P} = 1.068^{2}(2) = 2.28\).

The actual factors of safety are 2.24, 2.66, 3.21, and 2.28. Making a direct comparison of the factors, we note that the threat from gear bending and pinion wear are practically equal. We also note that three of the ratios are comparable. Our goal would be to make changes in the design decisions that drive the factors closer to 2. The next step would be to adjust the design variables. It is obvious that an iterative process is involved. We need a figure of merit to order the designs. A computer program clearly is desirable.
