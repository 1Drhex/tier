Guest Question.

Deduce the condition for minimum uncertainty in \( S_x \) and \( S_y \)(that is, equality in the expression \( \sigma_{S_{x}} \sigma_{S_{y}} \geq(\hbar / 2)\left|\left\langle S_{z}\right\rangle\right| \)) , for a particle of spin 1/2 in the generic state (Equation 4.139). Answer: With no loss of generality we can pick a to be real; then the condition for minimum uncertainty is that b is either pure real or else pure imaginary.

\( \chi=\left(\begin{array}{l} a \\ b \end{array}\right)=a \chi_{+}+b \chi \) (4.139).

Admin
Solution

From we know that in the generic state \( \chi=\left(\begin{array}{l} a \\ b \end{array}\right) \text { (with }|a|^{2}+|b|^{2}=1 )\),

\( \left\langle S_{z}\right\rangle=\frac{\hbar}{2}\left(|a|^{2}-|b|^{2}\right), \quad\left\langle S_{x}\right\rangle=\hbar \operatorname{Re}\left(a b^{*}\right), \quad\left\langle S_{y}\right\rangle=-\hbar \operatorname{Im}\left(a b^{*}\right) ; \quad\left\langle S_{x}^{2}\right\rangle=\left\langle S_{y}^{2}\right\rangle=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{4} \).

Writing \( a=|a| e^{i \phi_{a}}, \quad b=|b| e^{i \phi_{b}}, \text { we have } a b^{*}=|a||b| e^{i\left(\phi_{a}-\phi_{b}\right)}=|a||b| e^{i \theta}, \text { where } \theta \equiv \phi_{a}-\phi_{b} \) is the phase difference between a and b. Then

\( \left\langle S_{x}\right\rangle=\hbar \operatorname{Re}\left(|a||b| e^{i \theta}\right)=\hbar|a||b| \cos \theta, \quad\left\langle S_{y}\right\rangle=-\hbar \operatorname{Im}\left(|a||b| e^{i \theta}\right)=-\hbar|a||b| \sin \theta \).

\( \sigma_{S_{x}}^{2}=\left\langle S_{x}^{2}\right\rangle-\left\langle S_{x}\right\rangle^{2}=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{4}-\hbar^{2}|a|^{2}|b|^{2} \cos ^{2} \theta ; \quad \sigma_{S_{y}}^{2}=\left\langle S_{y}^{2}\right\rangle-\left\langle S_{y}\right\rangle^{2}=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{4}-\hbar^{2}|a|^{2}|b|^{2} \sin ^{2} \theta \).

We want \( \sigma_{S_{x}}^{2} \sigma_{S_{y}}^{2}=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{4}\left\langle S_{z}\right\rangle^{2} \), or

\( \frac{\hbar^{2}}{4}\left(1-4|a|^{2}|b|^{2} \cos ^{2} \theta\right) \frac{\hbar^{2}}{4}\left(1-4|a|^{2}|b|^{2} \sin ^{2} \theta\right)=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{4} \frac{\hbar^{2}}{4}\left(|a|^{2}-|b|^{2}\right)^{2} \).

\( 1-4|a|^{2}|b|^{2}\left(\cos ^{2} \theta+\sin ^{2} \theta\right)+16|a|^{4}|b|^{4} \sin ^{2} \theta \cos ^{2} \theta=|a|^{4}-2|a|^{2}|b|^{2}+|b|^{4} \).

\( 1+16|a|^{4}|b|^{4} \sin ^{2} \theta \cos ^{2} \theta=|a|^{4}+2|a|^{2}|b|^{2}+|b|^{4}=\left(|a|^{2}+|b|^{2}\right)^{2}=1 \quad \Rightarrow|a|^{2}|b|^{2} \sin \theta \cos \theta=0 \).

So either θ = 0 or π, in which case a and b are relatively real, or else \( \theta=\pm \pi / 2 \), in which case a and b are relatively imaginary (these two options subsume trivially the solutions a = 0 and b = 0).
