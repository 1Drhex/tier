Guest Question.

Expand the vector

\(\pmb{y}=\begin{bmatrix} 1\\4\\-2 \end{bmatrix} \) (a)

using the normalized mode shapes of .

Admin
Solution

The general mode shapes of are

\(\pmb{X}_{1}=B_{1}\begin{bmatrix} 0.908\\1\\0.384 \end{bmatrix} \) \(\pmb{X}_{1}=B_{2}\begin{bmatrix} -1.375\\1\\1.294 \end{bmatrix} \) \( \pmb{X}_{3}=B_{3}\begin{bmatrix}-0.534 \\1 \\-2.677 \end{bmatrix}\) (b)

where \(B_{1},B_{2} \) and \( B_{3}\), are arbitrary constants. The normalization of the first mode shape proceeds as follows

\(1=(\pmb{X}_{1},\pmb{X}_{1})_{M}=B^{2}_{1} \begin{bmatrix} 0.908 &1 & 0.384 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}m & 0 & 0\\ \\ 0 & m & 0 \\ \\0& &0 \frac{m}{2} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}0.908 \\ 1 \\0.384 \end{bmatrix} \) (c)

which yields \(B_{1}=0.726/\sqrt{m} \) and

\( \pmb{X}_{1}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{m} } \begin{bmatrix} 0.659 \\ 0.726 \\ 0.279 \end{bmatrix} \) (d)

The other mode shapes are normalized in the same manner yielding

\(\pmb{X}_{2}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{m} } \begin{bmatrix}-0.712 \\ 0.518 \\ 0.670 \end{bmatrix} \) \( \pmb{X}_{3}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{m} } \begin{bmatrix}-0.242 \\ 0.453 \\ -1.213 \end{bmatrix}\) (e)

The first coefficient in the expansion is calculated by

\(C_{1}=(\pmb{X}_{1},\pmb{y})_M=\frac{1}{\sqrt{m} } \begin{bmatrix} 0.659 & 0.726 & 0.279 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0.908 &1 & 0.384 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}m & 0 & 0\\ \\ 0 & m & 0 \\ \\0& &0 \frac{m}{2} \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} =3.284\sqrt{m}\) (f)

The other coefficients are calculated in a similar manner, yielding \(c_{2}=0,690\sqrt{m}, C_{3}=2.777\sqrt{m} \). Thus,

\(\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} =3.284\begin{bmatrix} 0.659 \\ 0.725 \\ 0.279 \end{bmatrix} +0.690 \begin{bmatrix} -0.712 \\ 0.518\\0.670 \end{bmatrix} +2.777\begin{bmatrix}-0.242 \\ 0.453 \\ -1.213 \end{bmatrix} \) (g)
