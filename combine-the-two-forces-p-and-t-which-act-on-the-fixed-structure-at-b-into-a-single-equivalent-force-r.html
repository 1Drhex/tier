Guest Question.

Combine the two forces P and T, which act on the fixed structure at B, into a single equivalent force R

Admin
Solution

Graphical solution. The parallelogram for the vector addition of forces T and P is constructed as shown in Fig. a. The scale used here is 1 in. = 800 lb; a scale of 1 in. = 200 lb would be more suitable for regular-size paper and would give greater accuracy. Note that the angle a must be determined prior to construction of the parallelogram. From the given figure

\( \tan{\alpha} = \frac{\overline{BD} }{\overline{AD} } = \frac{6 \sin{60}^{\circ}} {3+6\cos{60}^{\circ}} = 0.866 \)

 

\( \alpha = {40.9}^{\circ} \)

Measurement of the length R and direction \( \theta \) of the resultant force R yields the approximate results

R= 525 Ib

\( \theta = {49}^{\circ} \)

Geometric solution. The triangle for the vector addition of T and P is shown in Fig. b. The angle \(x \alpha \) is calculated as above. The law of cosines gives

\( R^2 =(600)^{2} + (800)^{2} – 2(600)(800) \cos{40.9}^{\circ} = 274,300 \)

R = 524 Ib

From the law of sines, we may determine the angle \( \theta \) which orients R. Thus

\( \frac{600}{\sin{\theta}} = \frac{524}{\sin{40.9}^{\circ}} \)

 

\( \sin{\theta} = 0.750 \)

 

\( \theta = {48.6}^{\circ} \)

Algebraic solution. By using the x-y coordinate system on the given figure, we may write

\( R_{x} = \Sigma F_{x} = 800 – 600 \cos{40.9}^{\circ} = 346 Ib \)

 

\( R_{y} = \Sigma F_{y} = – 600 \sin{40.9}^{\circ} = – 393 Ib \)

The magnitude and direction of the resultant force R as shown in Fig. c are then

\( R=\sqrt{R_{x}^{2} – R_{y}^{2}} =\sqrt{(346)^{2}+ (-393)^{2}} = 524 Ib\)

 

\( \theta = \tan^{-1} \frac{\left|R_{y}\right|}{\left|R_{x}\right| } =\tan^{-1} \frac{393}{346} = {48.6}^{\circ }\)

The resultant R may also be written in vector notation as

\( R = R_{x} i + R_{y}j = 346i- 393 j Ib \)
