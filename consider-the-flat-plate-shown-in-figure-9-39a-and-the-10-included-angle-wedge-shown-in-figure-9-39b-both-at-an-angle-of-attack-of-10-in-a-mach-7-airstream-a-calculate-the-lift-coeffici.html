Guest Question.

Consider the flat plate shown in Figure 9.39a and the 10° included angle wedge shown in Figure 9.39b, both at an angle of attack of 10° in a Mach 7 airstream. (a) Calculate the lift coefficient of the flat plate. (b) Calculate the lift coefficient of the wedge.

Admin
Solution

Solution (a) First, consider the expansion wave over the top of the plate. From Appendix C, for \( M_{1}=7, v_{1}=90.97^{\circ}\). From Equation (9.43)

\(\theta=v\left(M_{2}\right)-v\left(M_{1}\right)(9.43)\)

\(v_{2}=v_{1}+\alpha=90.97^{\circ}+10^{\circ}=100.97^{\circ}\)

Interpolating to obtain \(M_{2}\) from Appendix C,

\(M_{2}=9+\frac{100.97-99.32}{102.3-99.32}(1)=9.56\)

Going to the isentropic flow tables in Appendix A, and interpolating for \(p_{o} / p\) between entries, we have \(p_{o_{2}} / p_{2}=0.33 \times 10^{5}\). Also from Appendix A, for \(M_{1} = 7\), we have \(p_{o_{1}} / p_{1}=0.14 \times 10^{4}.\) Since \(p_{o}\) is constant across the expansion wave, then

\(\frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}=\frac{p_{o_{1}} / p_{1}}{p_{o_{2}} / p_{2}}=\frac{0.414 \times 10^{4}}{0.33 \times 10^{5}}=0.1255\)

Now consider the shock under the bottom of the plate in Figure 9.39a. From the \(θ-β-M\) diagram in Figure 9.9, for \(M_{1}= 7\) and \(α = 10° , β = 16.5°,\)

\(M_{n, 1}=M_{1} \sin \beta=7 \sin 16.5^{\circ}=1.99\)

From Appendix B, for \(M_{n, 1} = 1.99\), interpolating, we have

\(\frac{p_{3}}{p_{1}}=4.407+(0.093)(0.5)=4.45\)

The lift coefficient for a supersonic or hypersonic flat plate was derived in as

\(c_{\ell}=\frac{2}{\gamma M_{1}^{2}}\left(\frac{p_{3}}{p_{1}}-\frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}\right) \cos \alpha\)

\(=\frac{2}{(1.4)(7)^{2}}(4.45-0.1255)=0.126\)

(b) First consider the expansion wave over the top of the wedge.

\(\nu_{2}=v_{1}+5^{\circ}=90.97^{\circ}+5^{\circ}=95.97^{\circ}\)

From Appendix C, interpolating,

\(M_{2}=8+\frac{95.97-96.62}{99.32-95.62}(1)=8.1\)

From Appendix A, interpolating,

\(\frac{p_{o_{2}}}{p_{2}}=0.9763 \times 10^{4}+\left(0.211 \times 10^{5}-0.9763 \times 10^{4}\right)(1)=1.0897 \times 10^{4}\)

The relation between the chord length c and the length of the face of the wedge \(\ell \) is

\(\ell=\frac{c}{\cos 5^{\circ}}=\frac{c}{0.996}=1.004 c\)

The force per unit span,\(F_{2}^{\prime}\), acting on the top surface of the wedge, is

\(F_{2}^{\prime}=p_{2} \ell=\left(\frac{p_{o_{1}} / p_{1}}{p_{o_{2}} / p_{2}}\right) p_{1} \ell\)

For \(M_{1}= 7\), from Appendix A, \(p_{o_{1}} / p_{1}=0.414 \times 10^{4}\). Thus,

\(M_{n, 1}=M_{1} \sin \beta=7 \sin 23.5^{\circ}=2.79\)

From Appendix B, interpolating,

\(\frac{p_{3}}{p_{1}}=8.656+(8.98-8.656)(0.8)=8.915\)

Thus, the force per unit span, \(F_{3}^{\prime}\) , acting on the bottom surface of the wedge, is

\(F_{3}^{\prime}=p_{3} \ell=\left(\frac{p_{3}}{p_{1}}\right) p_{1} \ell=8.915 p_{1} \ell\)

The lift per unit span is the combination of the components of \(F_{2}^{\prime}\) and\(F_{3}^{\prime}\) perpendicular to the freestream. Examining Figure 9.39b, we see that

\(L^{\prime}=F_{3}^{\prime} \cos 15^{\circ}-F_{2}^{\prime} \cos 5^{\circ}=0.9659 F_{3}^{\prime}-0.9962 F_{2}^{\prime}\)

\(L^{\prime}=(0.9659)(8.915) p_{1} \ell-(0.9962)(0.38) p_{1} \ell\)

\(L^{\prime}=8.232 p_{1} \ell\)

However,\( \ell=1.004 c.\) Thus,

\(L^{\prime}=8.232 p_{1}(1.004 c)=8.265 p_{1} c\)

The lift coefficient is

\(c_{\ell}=\frac{L^{\prime}}{q_{1} c}=\frac{L^{\prime}}{(\gamma / 2) p_{1} M_{1}^{2} c}=\frac{2 L^{\prime}}{\gamma p_{1} M_{1}^{2} c}\)

Since \(L^{\prime}=8.265 p_{1} c\), we have

\(c_{\ell}=\frac{2(8.265) p_{1} c}{(1.4) p_{1}(7)^{2} c}=0.241\)
