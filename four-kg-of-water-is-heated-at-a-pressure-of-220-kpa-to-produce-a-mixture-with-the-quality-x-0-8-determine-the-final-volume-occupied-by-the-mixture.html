\( v_{g}=\left(\frac{220-200}{300-200}\right)(0.6058-0.8857)+0.8857=0.8297 m ^{3} / kg . \quad v_{f}=0.0011 m ^{3} / kg \).

Note that no interpolation is necessary for \( v_{f} \text {, since for both pressures } v_{f} \) is the same to four decimal places. Using (2.3), we now find

\( v=v_{f}+x\left(v_{g}-v_{f}\right) \) (2.3).

\( v=v_{f}+x\left(v_{g}-v_{f}\right)=0.0011+(0.8)(0.8297-0.0011)=0.6640 m ^{3} / kg \).

The total volume occupied by 4 kg is V = mv = (4 kg)(0.6640 m³/kg) = 2.656 m³.
