Guest Question.

Check the divergence theorem for the function

\(v = r^ 2 \cos θ \hat{r} + r ^2 \cos \phi \hat{\pmb{\theta} } − r^ 2 \cos θ \sin \phi \hat{\pmb{\phi} } ,\)

using as your volume one octant of the sphere of radius R (Fig. 1.48). Make sure you include the entire surface. [Answer: \(π R^4/4]\)

Admin
Solution
\(∇\cdot v=\frac{1}{r^2}\frac{\partial}{\partial r}\left(r^2r^2\cos \theta \right)+\frac{1}{r\sin \theta } \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta }\left(\sin \theta r^2\cos \phi \right)+\frac{1}{r\sin \theta }\frac{\partial}{\partial \phi } \left(-r^2\cos \theta \sin \phi \right) \)

=\(\frac{1}{r^2}4r^3\cos \theta +\frac{1}{r\sin \theta } \cos \theta r^2\cos \phi +\frac{1}{r\sin \theta } \left(-r^2\cos \theta \cos \phi \right) \)

=\(\frac{r\cos \theta }{\sin \theta }\left[4\sin \theta +\cos \phi -\cos \phi \right]=4r\cos \theta \) .

\(\int{}\left(∇\cdot v\right)d\tau =\int{}\left(4r\cos \theta \right)r^2\sin \theta drd\theta d\phi =4\int\limits_{0}^{R}{}r^3dr\int\limits_{0}^{\pi /2}{}\cos \theta \sin \theta d\theta \int\limits_{0}^{\pi /2}{}d\phi \)

=\(\left(R^4\right)\left(\frac{1}{2} \right) \left(\frac{\pi }{2} \right) =\frac{\pi R^4}{4} \) .

Surface consists of four parts:

(1) Curved: \(da =R^2\sin \theta d\theta d\phi \hat{r}; r=R. v\cdot da = \left(R^2\cos \theta \right) \left(R^2\sin \theta d\theta d\phi \right) \) .

\(\int{}v\cdot da = R^4\int\limits_{0}^{\pi /2}{} \cos \theta \sin \theta d\theta \int\limits_{0}^{\pi /2}{} d\phi = R^4\left(\frac{1}{2} \right) \left(\frac{\pi }{2} \right)=\frac{\pi R^4}{4} \) .

(2) Left: \(da = −r dr d\theta \hat{\pmb{\phi} } ; \phi =0. v · da =\left(r^2\cos \theta \sin \phi \right) \left(r dr d\theta \right)=0.\int{}v · da = 0 \) .

(3) Back: \(da = r dr d\theta \hat{\pmb{\phi} } ; \phi =\pi /2. v · da =\left(-r^2\cos \theta \sin \phi \right)\left(r dr d\theta \right)=−r^3 \cos \theta dr d\theta \) .

\(\int{} v · da =\int\limits_{0}^{R}{}r^3 dr\int\limits_{0}^{\pi /2}{}\cos \theta d\theta =-\left(\frac{1}{4}R^4 \right) (+1) = −\frac{1}{4} R^4 \) .

(4) Bottom: \(da = r\sin \theta dr d\phi \hat{\pmb{\theta} }; \theta =\pi /2. v · da =\left(r^2\cos \phi \right) \left(r dr d\phi \right) \) .

\(\int{}v · da =\int\limits_{0}^{R}{}r^3 dr \int\limits_{0}^{\pi /2}{}\cos \phi d\phi =\frac{1}{4}R^4 \).

Total: \(\oint{}v · da = \pi R^4/4 + 0 − \frac{1}{4}R^4 + \frac{1}{4} R^4 =\frac{\pi R^4}{4} \).
