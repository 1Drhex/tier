Guest Question.

Two blocks \( W_1 \) and \( W_2 \) which are connected by a horizontal bar AB are supported on rough planes as shown in Fig. 8.6. The coefficient of friction for the block A = 0.4. The angle of friction for the block B is 20°. Find the smallest weight \( W_1 \) of the block A for which the equilibrium can exist, if \( W_2 \) = 2250 N.

Admin
Solution

(i) Consider the FBD of Block B

\( ∑ F_y = 0 \).

\( N_1 \sin 30° + μ_1N_1 \sin 60° – 2250 = 0 \).

\( N_1 (\sin 30° + \tan 20°\sin 60° ) = 2250 \).

\( N_1 = 2760.03 \) N.

\( ∑ F_x = 0 \).

\( F_{AB} + μ_1N_1 \cos 60° – N_1 \cos 30° = 0 \).

\( F_{AB} = 2760.03 \cos 30° – \tan 20° × 2760.03 \cos 60° \).

\( F_{AB} = 1887.97 \) N.

(ii) Consider the FBD of Block A

\( ∑ F_x = 0 \).

\( μ_2N_2 -F_{AB} = 0 \).

\( 0.4 N_2 = 1887.97 \).

\(N_2 = 4719.93 \) N.

\( ∑ F_y = 0 \).

\( N_2 – W_1 = 0 \).

∴ \( W_1 = 4719.93 \) N Ans.
