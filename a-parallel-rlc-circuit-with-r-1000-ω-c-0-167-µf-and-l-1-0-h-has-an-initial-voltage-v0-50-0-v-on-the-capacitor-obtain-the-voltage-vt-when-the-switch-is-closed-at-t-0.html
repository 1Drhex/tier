Guest Question.

A parallel RLC circuit, with R = 1000 Ω, C = 0.167 µF, and L = 1.0 H, has an initial voltage \( V_{0}=50.0 V \) on the capacitor. Obtain the voltage v(t) when the switch is closed at t = 0.

Admin
Solution

We have

\( \alpha=\frac{1}{2 R C}=2994 \quad \alpha^{2}=8.96 \times 10^{6} \quad \omega_{0}^{2}=\frac{1}{L C}=5.99 \times 10^{6} \)

Since \( \alpha^{2}>\omega_{0}^{2} \), the circuit is overdamped and from (2) we have

\(v=A_1e^{s_1t}+A_2e^{s_2t}\) (2)

\( s_{1}=-\alpha+\sqrt{\alpha^{2}-\omega_{0}^{2}}=-1271 \quad \text { and } \quad s_{2}=-\alpha-\sqrt{\alpha^{2}-\omega_{0}^{2}}=-4717 \)

At t = 0, \( V_{0}=A_{1}+A_{2} \quad \text { and }\left.\quad \frac{d v}{d t}\right|_{t=0}=s_{1} A_{1}+s_{2} A_{2} \)

From the nodal equation (1), at t = 0 and with no initial current in the inductance L,

\(\frac{v}{R}+\frac{1}{L} \int_{0}^{t}{vdt} +C\frac{dv}{dt} = 0\) (1)

\( \frac{V_{0}}{R}+C \frac{d v}{d t}=0 \quad \text { or }\left.\quad \frac{d v}{d t}\right|_{t=0}=-\frac{V_{0}}{R C} \)

Solving for \( A_{1} \),

\( A_{1}=\frac{V_{0}\left(s_{2}+1 / R C\right)}{s_{2}-s_{1}}=155.3 \quad \text { and } \quad A_{1}=V_{0}-A_{1}=50.0-155.3=-105.3 \)

Substituting into (2)

\( v=155.3 e^{-1271 t}-105.3 e^{-4717 t} (V) \)

See Fig. 8-8.

Underdamped (Oscillatory) Case \( \left(\omega_{0}^{2}>\alpha^{2}\right) \)

The oscillatory case for the parallel RLC circuit results in an equation of the same form as that of the underdamped series RLC circuit. Thus,

\( v=e^{-\alpha t}\left(A_{1} \cos \omega_{d} t+A_{2} \sin \omega_{d} t\right) \) (3)

where \( \alpha=1 / 2 R C \text { and } \omega_{d}=\sqrt{\omega_{0}^{2}-\alpha^{2}} . \quad \omega_{d} \) is a radian frequency just as was the case with sinusoidal circuit analysis. Here it is the frequency of the damped oscillation. It is referred to as the damped radian frequency.
