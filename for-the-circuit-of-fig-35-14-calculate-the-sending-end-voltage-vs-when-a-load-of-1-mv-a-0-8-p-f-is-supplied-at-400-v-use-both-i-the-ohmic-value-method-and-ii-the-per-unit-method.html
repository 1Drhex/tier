Guest Question.

For the circuit of Fig. 35.14 calculate the sending-end voltage \(V_{S}\), when a load of 1 MV A, 0.8 p.f. is supplied at 400 V. Use both (i) the ohmic value method and (ii) the per-unit method.

Admin
Solution

(i) Ohmic value method

Load current is obtained from \(V A = \sqrt{3}V_{L}I_{L}\)

 

\(∴ I_{L}=\frac{1\cdot 10^6}{\sqrt{3}\cdot 400 }= 1443 A \)

 

If 400 V is taken as the reference phasor then I = 1443(0.8 − j0.6) A.
The answer expected for \(V_{S}\) will be around 33 kV, therefore refer all values to 33 kV by multiplying by the turns ratio:

 

\(∴ I_{33}=1443(0.8 – j 0.6)\cdot \frac{0.400}{33} \\ = 17.5 (0.8 – j 0.6 ) A \)

 

2 MV A transformer

\(Z_{Ω} = j 6.05 Ω\) referred to 11 kV

 

\(Z_{\Omega }(referred to 33 kV)= j 6.05 \times \left(\frac{33}{11} \right)^2= j 54.45 \Omega\)

 

10 MV A transformer

\(Z_{Ω} ( referred to 33 kV) = j 10.89 Ω\)

 

To calculate \(V_{S}\), use equation [35.1]:

 

\(V_{S}=V_{R}+Z I_{R}\)

 

\(V_{S} = V_{R} ∠ 0 + I ∠ \phi ⋅ Z_{total}\)

 

\(Z_{t} = j 54.45 + j 10.89\)

 

\(V_{S}=\frac{33000}{\sqrt{3} }+17.5 (0.8 – j 0.6) j 65.3 \\ =19052 + j 1143 (0.8 – j 0.6) \\ =19052 + 686 + j 915 \\ =19759 V (phase voltage) \\ = 34.22 k V (line-to-line voltage)\)

 

(ii) Per-unit method

We begin by choosing the base value of MV A, SB or MV AB. This can be any value, but it would be sensible to choose a base MV A of either 1 MV A, 2 MV A or 10 MV A to minimize work!
Choose 2 MV A as the base power.
The per-unit impedance (reactance) of the 2 MV A transformer remains at j0.1 pu as the voltage drop within the transformer, when the base power flows through it, will be 0.1 pu.
What is the voltage drop within the 10 MV A transformer when the base power flows through it?
Since its voltage drop is 0.1 pu when the load is 10 MV A then when the load is 2 MV A the voltage drop will be 0.02 pu

 

or \( Z_{pu}=Z_{pu} actual \times \frac{M V A_{basic}}{M V A_{actual}} \\ =j 0.1\cdot \frac{2}{10} \\ =j 0.02 pu \)

 

Similarly, a load of 2 MV A absorbs a current of 1 pu. Therefore the actual load of 1 MV A must absorb a current of 0.5 pu. Consequently, the load current is also 0.5 pu but the phase angle should not be forgotten. In this case

 

\(I_{pu} = 0.5(0.8 – j 0.6)\)

 

The per-unit network for a base of 2 MV A is thus as shown in Fig. 35.15. We are now in a position to complete the example using per-unit techniques:

Circuit for base power of 2 MV A:

\(∴ V_{S} = V_{R} + IZ \\ = 1 ∠ 0 + 0.5(0.8 – j 0.6) j 0.12 \\ =1 + j 0.06(0.8 – j 0.6) \\ = 1.036 + j 0.048 = 1.037 pu \)

 

\(V_{actual} = V_{pu} \times V_{B} \\ = 1.037 \times 33 kV \\ = 34.22 kV (as before)\)
