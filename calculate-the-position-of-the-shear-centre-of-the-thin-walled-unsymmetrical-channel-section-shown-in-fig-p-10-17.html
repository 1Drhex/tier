Guest Question.

Calculate the position of the shear centre of the thin-walled unsymmetrical channel section shown in Fig. P.10.17.

Admin
Solution

The section is unsymmetrical so that both horizontal and vertical positions of the shear centre are unknown. To find the horizontal position apply a vertical shear load through the shear centre and then to find the vertical position apply a horizontal shear load through the shear centre. Initially the position of the centroid of area must be found so that the second moments of area can be determined.
The cross-sectional area of the flange DC is equal to the cross-sectional area of the flange AB so that the centroid of area is at the mid-depth of the section.
The area of cross section is given by

\( A=10 \times 50+10 \times 100+5 \times 100=2000 mm ^{2} \)

Referring to Fig. S.10.17 and taking moments of area about the web BC

\( 2000 \bar{z}=10 \times 50 \times 25+5 \times 100 \times 50\)

from which

\( \bar{z}=18.75 mm \)

Then

\(\begin{aligned}I_{z}=& 10 \times 5 \times 50^{2}+5 \times 100 \times 50^{2}+\frac{10 \times 100^{3}}{12}=3.33 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4} \\I_{y}=& \frac{10 \times 50^{3}}{12}+10 \times 50 \times 6.25^{2}+\frac{5 \times 100^{3}}{12}+5 \times 100 \times 31.25^{2} \\&+10 \times 100 \times 18.75^{2} \\=& 1.38 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4} \\I_{z y}=& 10 \times 50(6.25)(50)+5 \times 100(31.25)(-50)=-0.625 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

The numerator in both terms on the right hand side of Eq. (10.22) is then

\( q_{s}=\frac{\left(S_{y} I_{z y}-S_{z} I_{z}\right)}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t z d s+\frac{\left(S_{z} I_{z y}-S_{y} I_{y}\right)}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s \) (10.22)

 

\( I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}=\left(3.33 \times 1.38-0.625^{2}\right) \times 10^{12}=4.205 \times 10^{12} \)

Since only the position of the shear centre is required it is not necessary to calculate the shear flow distribution in the complete cross section; if moments are taken about, say, the corner C the shear flows in the walls BC and CD will not contribute to the moment equation so that only the shear flow distribution in the flange AB is needed. First consider the application of \( S_{y} \). Equation (10.22) becomes

\( q_{s}=\frac{S_{y}\left[I_{z y} \int_{0}^{s} t z d s-I_{y} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s\right]}{4.205 \times 10^{12}} \) (i)

In the flange AB, \( z=81.25-s \text { and } y=-50 mm \). Therefore

\( q_{ AB }=S_{y}\left[-0.149 \times 10^{-6} \int_{0}^{s} 5(81.25-s) d s-0.328 \times 10^{-6} \int_{0}^{s} 5(-50) d s\right] \)

i.e.

\( q_{ AB }=S_{y}\left[21.47 \times 10^{-6} s+0.37 \times 10^{-6} s^{2}\right] \) (ii)

Taking moments about C

\( S_{y} z_{S}=\int_{0}^{100} q_{ AB } \times 100 d s \)

Substituting for \( q_{ AB } \) from Eq. (ii) gives

\( z_{ S }=23.1 mm \)

Now apply \( S_{z} \) through the shear centre. Eq. (10.22) becomes

\( q_{ AB }=\frac{S_{z}\left[-I_{z} \int_{0}^{s} t z d s+I_{z y} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s\right]}{4.205 \times 10^{12}} \) (iii)

The expressions for z and y in terms of s are identical to those above so that Eq. (iii) simplifies to

\( q_{ AB }=S_{ z }\left[-0.792 \times 10^{-6} \int_{0}^{s} 5(81.25-s) d s-0.149 \times 10^{-6} \int_{0}^{s} 5(-50) d s\right] \)

i.e.

\( q_{ AB }=S_{ z }\left[-284.5 \times 10^{-6} s+1.98 \times 10^{-6} s^{2}\right] \) (iv)

Taking moments about C

\(S_{z} y_{ S }=-\int_{0}^{100} q_{ AB } \times 100 d s \)

Again substituting for \( q_{ AB } \) from Eq. (iv) and carrying out the integration

\( y_{ S }=76.3 mm \)
