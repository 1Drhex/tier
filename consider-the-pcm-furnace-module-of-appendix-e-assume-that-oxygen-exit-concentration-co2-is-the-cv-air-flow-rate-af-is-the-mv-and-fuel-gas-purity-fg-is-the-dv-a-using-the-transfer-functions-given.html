Guest Question.

Consider the PCM furnace module of Appendix E.

Assume that oxygen exit concentration \(c_{O_{2}}\) is the \(CV\), air flow rate \(A F\) is the MV, and fuel gas purity \(F G\) is the DV.

(a) Using the transfer functions given below, design a feedforward control system.

(b) Design a PID controller based on IMC tuning and a reasonable value of \(\tau_{c}\).

(c) Simulate the FF, FB, and combined FF-FB controllers for a sudden change in \(d\) at \(t=10 min\), from 1 to \(0.9\). Which controller is superior? Justify your answer.

\(\begin{aligned}&\frac{c_{O_{2}}}{A F}=G_{p}=\frac{0.14 e^{-4 s}}{4.2 s+1} \\&\frac{c_{O_{2}}}{F G}=G_{d}=-\frac{2.82 e^{-4 s}}{4.3 s+1}, \quad G_{v}=G_{m}=G_{t}=1\end{aligned}\)

Admin
Solution

Note: The disturbance transfer function is incorrect in the first printing. It should be:

\(\frac{c_{O_{2}}}{F G}=-\frac{-2.82 e^{-4 s}}{4.3 s+1}\)

(a) The feedforward controller design equation is \((15-21)\) :

\(G_{f}=-\frac{G_{d}}{G_{t} G_{v} G_{p}}\) (15-21)

\(\begin{aligned}&G_{f}=\frac{-G_{d}}{G_{v} G_{t} G_{p}}=-\frac{\frac{-2.82 e^{-4 s}}{4.3 s+1}}{(1)(1)\left(\frac{0.14 e^{-4 s}}{4.2 s+1}\right)}=20.1 \frac{4.2 s+1}{4.3 s+1} \\&G_{f} \approx 20.1 m ^{3} / min\end{aligned}\)

b) Using Item \(G\) in Table \(12.1\), a PI controller is obtained from for \(G=G_{v} G_{p} G_{m}\), Assume that \(\tau_{ c }=\tau / 2=2.1 min\)

\(\begin{aligned}&K_{c}=\frac{1}{K} \frac{\tau+\theta / 2}{\tau_{c}+\theta / 2}=\frac{1}{0.14} \frac{4.2+2}{(2.1+2)}=10.8 \\&\tau_{ I }=\tau+\theta / 2=4.2+2=6.2 min \\&\tau_{ D }=\frac{\tau \theta}{2 \tau+\theta}=\frac{(4.2)(4)}{2(4.2)+4}=1.35 min \\&\tau_{i}=\tau=95\end{aligned}\)

c) As shown in Fig.S15.12a, the FF-FB controller provides the best control with a small maximum deviation and no offset. The oscillation due to the feedback controller can be damped by using a larger value of design parameter, \(\tau_{ c }\)
