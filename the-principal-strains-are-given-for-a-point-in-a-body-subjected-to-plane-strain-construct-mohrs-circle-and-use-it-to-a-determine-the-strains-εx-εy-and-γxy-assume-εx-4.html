Guest Question.

The principal strains are given for a point in a body subjected to plane strain. Construct Mohr’s circle and use it to
(a) determine the strains \(\varepsilon_{x}, \varepsilon_{y}, \text { and } \gamma_{x y}\). (Assume \(\varepsilon_{x}>\varepsilon_{y}\))
(b) determine the maximum in-plane shear strain and the absolute maximum shear strain.
(c) draw a sketch showing the angle \(\theta_{p}\), the principal strain deformations, and the maximum in-plane shear strain distortions.
\(\varepsilon_{p 1}=1,590 \mu \varepsilon \quad \varepsilon_{p 2}=-540 \mu \varepsilon \quad \theta_{p}=-23.55^{\circ}\)

Admin
Solution

The center of Mohr’s circle is at

\(C=\frac{\varepsilon_{p 1}+\varepsilon_{p 2}}{2}=\frac{(1,590 \mu \varepsilon)+(-540 \mu \varepsilon)}{2}=525 \mu \varepsilon\)

and the radius of Mohr’s circle is equal to

\(R=\frac{\varepsilon_{p 1}-\varepsilon_{p 2}}{2}=\frac{(1,590 \mu \varepsilon)-(-540 \mu \varepsilon)}{2}=1,065 \mu\)

The angle \(\theta_{p}\) is given as −23.55°, and we are told to assume that \(\varepsilon_{x}>\varepsilon_{y}\). From this information, we know that the \(\varepsilon_{p 1}\) principal plane is rotated 23.55° in a clockwise direction from the x face. Thus, to locate point x on Mohr’s circle, begin at \(\varepsilon_{p 1}\) and rotate 2(23.55°) = 47.10° in a counterclockwise direction. The Mohr’s circle is shown.

 

(a) The normal strain in the x direction is computed as:

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{x} &=C+R \cos 2 \theta_{p} \\&=525 \mu+(1,065 \mu) \cos \left(47.10^{\circ}\right) \\&=1,250.0000 \mu \varepsilon=1,250 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)

and the shear strain \(\gamma_{x y}\) is computed from:

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{\gamma_{x y}}{2} &=R \sin 2 \theta_{p} \\&=(1,065 \mu) \sin \left(47.10^{\circ}\right) \\&=780.1582 \mu rad \\& \therefore \gamma_{x y}=1,560.3164 \mu rad =-1,560 \mu rad \quad \text { (negative since point } x \text { plots above the }\varepsilon \text { axis) }\end{aligned}\)

The normal strain in the y direction is computed from:

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{y} &=C-R \cos 2 \theta_{p} \\&=525 \mu-(1,065 \mu) \cos \left(47.10^{\circ}\right) \\&=-199.9677 \mu \varepsilon=-200 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) The maximum shear strain is simply two times the radius of Mohr’s circle:

\(\gamma_{\max }=2 R=2(1,065 \mu)=2,130 \mu rad\)

Since \(\varepsilon_{p 1} \text { and } \varepsilon_{p 2}\) are of different signs, the absolute maximum shear strain is equal to the maximum inplane shear strain:

\(\gamma_{ abs \max }=\gamma_{\max }=2,130 \mu rad\)
