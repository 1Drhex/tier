Guest Question.

A circular steel disk, called a swashplate, is fastened at its center at an angle of 30째 to a steel shaft, as shown in Fig. 12.28. Using 0.282 lb/in3 as the density of steel, find the mass moments and products of inertia of the combined disk and shaft about the combined center of mass and aligned along the axes of the shaft.

Admin
Solution

First, we calculate the mass of the disk and the principal mass moments of inertia of the disk using the equations of Table 5, Appendix A. These are

\(m=\frac{0.282 lb / in ^{3}}{386 in / s ^{2}} \pi(8 in )^{2}(1.5 in )=0.220 lb \cdot s ^{2} / in\)

 

\(I^{x x}=\frac{\left(0.220 lb \cdot s ^{2} / in \right)(8 in )^{2}}{2}=7.04 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\)

 

\(I^{y y}=I^{z z}=\frac{\left(0.220 lb \cdot s ^{2} / in \right)(8 in )^{2}}{4}=3.52 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\)

From Fig. 12.28 we now find the direction cosines between the coordinate systems. These are

 

\(\begin{array}{lll}\cos \theta_{i^{\prime} i}=0.866, & \cos \theta_{i^{\prime} j}=0.500, & \cos \theta_{i^{\prime} k}=0.000 \\\cos \theta_{j^{\prime} i}=-0.500, & \cos \theta_{j^{\prime} j}=0.866, & \cos \theta_{j^{\prime} k}=0.000 \\\cos \theta_{k^{\prime} i}=0.000, & \cos \theta_{k^{\prime} j}=0.000, & \cos \theta_{k^{\prime} k}=1.000\end{array}\)

 

Substituting these results into Eqs. (12.106) and (12.107) gives the mass moments and products of inertia of the disk aligned with the axes of the shaft. These are

 

\(\begin{aligned}I^{x^{\prime} x^{\prime}} &=\cos ^{2} \theta_{i^{\prime} i} I^{x x}+\cos ^{2} \theta_{i^{\prime} j} I^{y y}+\cos ^{2} \theta_{i^{\prime} k} I^{z z} \\&+2 \cos \theta_{i^{\prime} i} \cos \theta_{i^{\prime} j} I^{x y}+2 \cos \theta_{i^{\prime} i} \cos \theta_{i^{\prime} k} I^{x z}+2 \cos \theta_{i^{\prime} j} \cos \theta_{i^{\prime} k} I^{y z} \\I^{y^{\prime} y^{\prime}} &=\cos ^{2} \theta_{j^{\prime} i} I^{x x}+\cos ^{2} \theta_{j^{\prime} j} I^{y y}+\cos ^{2} \theta_{j^{\prime} k} I^{z z} \\&+2 \cos \theta_{j^{\prime} i} \cos \theta_{j^{\prime} j} I^{x y}+2 \cos \theta_{j^{\prime} i} \cos \theta_{j^{\prime} k} I^{x z}+2 \cos \theta_{j^{\prime} j} \cos \theta_{j^{\prime} k} I^{y z} \\I^{z^{\prime} z^{\prime}} &=\cos ^{2} \theta_{k^{\prime} i} I^{x x}+\cos ^{2} \theta_{k^{\prime} j} I^{y y}+\cos ^{2} \theta_{k^{\prime} k} I^{z z} \\&+2 \cos \theta_{k^{\prime} i} \cos \theta_{k^{\prime} j} I^{x y}+2 \cos \theta_{k^{\prime} i} \cos \theta_{k^{\prime} k} I^{x z}+2 \cos \theta_{k^{\prime} j} \cos \theta_{k^{\prime} k} I^{y z}\end{aligned}\) (12.106)

 

\(\begin{aligned}I^{x^{\prime} y^{\prime}}=&-\cos \theta_{i^{\prime} i} \cos \theta_{j^{\prime} i} I^{x x}-\cos \theta_{i^{\prime} j} \cos \theta_{j^{\prime} j} I^{y y}-\cos \theta_{i^{\prime} k} \cos \theta_{j^{\prime} k} I^{z z} \\&+\left(\cos \theta_{i^{\prime} i} \cos \theta_{j^{\prime} j}+\cos \theta_{i^{\prime} j} \cos \theta_{j^{\prime} i}\right) I^{x y} \\&+\left(\cos \theta_{i^{\prime} j} \cos \theta_{j^{\prime} k}+\cos \theta_{i^{\prime} k} \cos \theta_{j^{\prime} j}\right) I^{y z} \\&+\left(\cos \theta_{i^{\prime} k} \cos \theta_{j^{\prime} i}+\cos \theta_{i^{\prime} i} \cos \theta_{j^{\prime} k}\right) I^{z x}\end{aligned}\) (12.107a)

 

\(\begin{aligned}I^{y^{\prime} z^{\prime}}=&-\cos \theta_{j^{\prime} i} \cos \theta_{k^{\prime} i} I^{x x}-\cos \theta_{j^{\prime} j} \cos \theta_{k^{\prime} j} I^{y y}-\cos \theta_{j^{\prime} k} \cos \theta_{k^{\prime} k} I^{z z} \\&+\left(\cos \theta_{j^{\prime} i} \cos \theta_{k^{\prime} j}+\cos \theta_{j^{\prime} j} \cos \theta_{k^{\prime} i}\right) I^{x y} \\&+\left(\cos \theta_{j^{\prime} j} \cos \theta_{k^{\prime} k}+\cos \theta_{j^{\prime} k} \cos \theta_{k^{\prime} j}\right) I^{y z} \\&+\left(\cos \theta_{j^{\prime} k} \cos \theta_{k^{\prime} i}+\cos \theta_{j^{\prime} i} \cos \theta_{k^{\prime} k}\right) I^{z x}\end{aligned}\) (12.107b)

 

\(\begin{aligned}I^{z^{\prime} x^{\prime}}=&-\cos \theta_{k^{\prime} i} \cos \theta_{i^{\prime} i} I^{x x}-\cos \theta_{k^{\prime} j} \cos \theta_{i^{\prime} j} I^{y y}-\cos \theta_{k^{\prime} k} \cos \theta_{i^{\prime} k} I^{z z} \\&+\left(\cos \theta_{k^{\prime} i} \cos \theta_{i^{\prime} j}+\cos \theta_{k^{\prime} j} \cos \theta_{i^{\prime} i}\right) I^{x y} \\&+\left(\cos \theta_{k^{\prime} j} \cos \theta_{i^{\prime} k}+\cos \theta_{k^{\prime} k} \cos \theta_{i^{\prime} j}\right) I^{y z} \\&+\left(\cos \theta_{k^{\prime} k} \cos \theta_{i^{\prime} i}+\cos \theta_{k^{\prime} i} \cos \theta_{i^{\prime} k}\right) I^{z x}\end{aligned}\) (12.107c)

 

\(I^{x^{\prime} x^{\prime}}=(0.866)^{2}\left(7.04 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\right)+(0.500)^{2}\left(3.52 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\right)=6.16 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\)

 

\(I^{y^{\prime} y^{\prime}}=(-0.500)^{2}\left(7.04 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\right)+(0.866)^{2}\left(3.52 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\right)=4.40 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\)

 

\(I^{z^{\prime} z^{\prime}}=I^{z z}=3.52 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}I^{\prime^{\prime} y^{\prime}}=&-(0.866)(-0.500)\left(7.04 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\right) \\&-(0.500)(0.866)\left(3.52 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\right)=1.52 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2} \\I^{y^{\prime} z^{\prime}}=& I^{z^{\prime} x^{\prime}}=0\end{aligned}\)

 

Next, we find the mass of the shaft and the principal mass moments of inertia of the shaft using the equations of Table 5, Appendix A. These are

 

\(\begin{aligned}&m=\frac{0.282 lh / in ^{3}}{386 in / s ^{2}} \pi(0.5 in )^{2}(24 in )=0.0138 lb \cdot s ^{2} / in \\&I^{x x}=\frac{\left(0.0138 lb \cdot s ^{2} / in \right)(0.5 in )^{2}}{2}=0.00172 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2} \\&I^{y y}=I^{z z}=\frac{\left(0.0138 lb \cdot s ^{2} / in \right)\left[3(0.5 in )^{2}+(24 in )^{2}\right]}{12}=0.662 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

The mass products of inertia of both the disk and the shaft are zero because the axes used are principal axes.
Translating the values for the shaft to the same axes as the disk, Eq. (12.7) gives

 

\(\begin{aligned}&I^{x x}=\int(\hat{ i } \times R ) \cdot(\hat{ i } \times R ) dm=\int\left[\left(R^{y}\right)^{2}+\left(R^{z}\right)^{2}\right] d m \\&I^{y y}=\int(\hat{ j } \times R ) \cdot(\hat{ j } \times R ) d m=\int\left[\left(R^{z}\right)^{2}+\left(R^{x}\right)^{2}\right] d m \\&I^{z z}=\int(\hat{ k } \times R ) \cdot(\hat{ k } \times R ) d m=\int\left[\left(R^{x}\right)^{2}+\left(R^{y}\right)^{2}\right] d m\end{aligned}\) (12.7)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&I^{x^{\prime} x^{\prime}}=0.00172 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2} \\&I^{y^{\prime} y^{\prime}}=I^{z^{\prime} z^{\prime}}=\left(0.662 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\right)+\left(0.0138 lb \cdot s ^{2} / in \right)(2 in )^{2}=0.717 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

The values of the disk and the shaft are now both in the same coordinate system and are combined to give

 

\(\begin{aligned}&I^{x^{\prime} x^{\prime}}=\left(6.16 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\right)+\left(0.00172 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\right)=6.16 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}, \\&I^{y^{\prime} y^{\prime}}=\left(4.40 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\right)+\left(0.717 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\right)=5.12 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}, \\&I^{z z^{\prime}}=\left(3.52 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\right)+\left(0.717 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\right)=4.24 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}, \\&I^{x^{\prime} y^{\prime}}=\left(1.52 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\right)+0=1.52 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}, \\&I^{y^{\prime} z^{\prime}}=I^{z^{\prime} x^{\prime}}=0 .\end{aligned}\)

 

Finally, all values are translated to the combined center of mass using Eqs. (12.109):

 

\(\begin{aligned}&I^{x^{\prime \prime} x^{\prime \prime}}=I^{x^{\prime} x^{\prime}}+2 m R_{G}^{y^{\prime}} d^{y^{\prime}}+2 m R_{G}^{z^{\prime}}d^{z^{\prime}}+m\left(d^{y^{\prime}}\right)^{2}+m\left(d^{z^{\prime}}\right)^{2}\\&I^{y^{\prime \prime} y^{\prime \prime}}=I^{y^{\prime} y^{\prime}}+2 m R_{G}^{z^{\prime}} d^{z^{\prime}}+2 m R_{G}^{x^{\prime}}d^{x^{k}}+m\left(d^{z^{\prime}}\right)^{2}+m\left(d^{x^{\prime}}\right)^{2}\\&I^{z^{\prime \prime} z^{\prime \prime}}=I^{z^{\prime} z^{\prime}}+2 m R_{G}^{x^{\prime}} d^{x^{\prime}}+2 m R_{G}^{y^{\prime}} d^{y^{\prime\prime}}+m\left(d^{x^{\prime}}\right)^{2}+m\left(d^{y^{\prime}}\right)^{2}\\&I^{x^{\prime \prime} y^{\prime \prime}}=I^{x^{\prime} y^{\prime}}+m R_{G}^{x^{\prime}} d^{y^{\prime}}+m R_{G}^{y^{\prime}} d^{x^{\prime}}+m d^{x^{\prime}} d^{y^{\prime}} \text {, }\\&I^{y^{\prime \prime} z^{\prime \prime}}=I^{y^{\prime} z^{\prime}}+m R_{G}^{y^{\prime}} d^{z^{\prime}}+m R_{G}^{z^{\prime}} d^{y^{\prime}}+m d^{y^{\prime}} d^{z^{\prime}} \text {, }\\&I^{z^{\prime \prime} x^{\prime \prime}}=I^{2^{\prime} x^{\prime}}+m R_{G}^{z^{\prime}} d^{x^{\prime}}+m R_{G}^{x^{\prime}} d^{z^{\prime}}+m d^{z^{\prime}} d^{x^{\prime}} \text {. }\end{aligned}\) (12.109)

 

\(m=\left(0.220 lb \cdot s ^{2} / in \right)+\left(0.0138 lb \cdot s ^{2} / in \right)=0.234 lb \cdot s ^{2} / in\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}R_{G}^{x^{\prime}} &=\frac{\left(0.220 lb \cdot s ^{2} / in \right)(0)+\left(0.0138 lb \cdot s ^{2} / in \right)(2.0 in )}{0.235 lb \cdot s ^{2} / in }=0.127 in \\R_{G}^{y^{\prime}} &=R_{G}^{z^{\prime}}=0 \\d^{x^{\prime}} &=-0.127 in \\d^{y^{\prime}} &=d^{z^{\prime}}=0 \\I^{x^{\prime \prime} x^{\prime \prime}} &=6.16 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}I^{y^{\prime \prime} y^{\prime \prime}} &=\left(5.12 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\right)+2\left(0.235 lb \cdot s ^{2} / in \right)(0.118)(-0.118)+(0.234 in )(-0.118 in )^{2} \\&=5.12 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2},\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}I^{z^{\prime \prime} z^{\prime \prime}}=&\left(4.24 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\right)+2\left(0.234 lb \cdot s ^{2} / in \right)(0.118 in )(-0.118 in ) \\&+\left(0.234 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\right)(-0.118 in )^{2} \\=& 4.24 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&I^{x^{\prime \prime} y^{\prime \prime}}=1.52 in \cdot lb \cdot s ^{2} \\&I^{y^{\prime \prime} z^{\prime \prime}}=I^{z^{\prime \prime} x^{\prime \prime}}=0\end{aligned}\)
