Guest Question.

A 50 Hz, 400 V, 4-pole cylindrical synchronous generator has 36 slots, two-layer winding with full-pitch coils of 8 turns each. The mean air-gap diameter is 0.16 m, axial length 0.12 m and a uniform air-gap of 2 mm. Calculate the value of the resultant AT/pole and the peak air-gap flux density. The machine is developing an electromagnetic torque of 60 Nm as a generator at a torque angle 26Â°. What should be the rotor AT/pole? What is the stator AT and the angle it makes with the resultant AT? Also find the stator current.

Admin
Solution
\( N_{ph}(series)=\frac{36\times 8\times 2}{2\times 3} =96\)

 

\( \gamma =\frac{4\times 180^{\circ }}{36} =20^{\circ }\)

 

\( m=\frac{36}{4\times 3} =3\)

 

\( K_{b}=\frac{\sin(3\times 20^{\circ }/2)}{3\times \sin(20^{\circ }/2)} 0.96\)

 

\( V\approx E=4.44K_{d}f\Phi _{r}N_{ph}\)

 

\( \frac{400}{\sqrt{3} } =4.44\times 0.96\times 50\times \Phi _{r}\times 96\)

 

\( \Phi _{r}=0.0113 Wb/pole\)

 

\( D= 0.16 m, l = 0.12 m\)

 

Pole area \( =\frac{\pi \times 0.16\times 0.12}{4} =0.0151 m^{2}\)

 

\( B_{r}(av)=\frac{0.0113}{0.0151} = 0.749 T\)

 

\( B_{r}(peak)=\frac{\pi }{2} \times 0.749=1.18T\)

 

\( F_{r}=\frac{gB_{r}(peak)}{\mu _{0}} =\frac{2\times 10^{-3}\times 1.18}{4\pi \times 10^{-7}}\)

 

\( = 1878 AT/pole (peak)\)

 

Torque developed, \( T=\frac{\pi }{2} \left(\frac{P}{2} \right) \Phi _{r}F_{2}\sin \delta\)

 

\( 60 =\frac{\pi}{2} \times \left(\frac{4}{2} \right) ^{2}\times 0.0113 F_{2}\sin 26^{\circ }\)

 

or \( F_{2}=1928 AT/pole (peak)\)

 

Mmf phasor diagram is drawn in Fig. 5.40 with \( \vec{F} _{2}\) leading \( \vec{F} _{r} \) by \( \delta =26^{\circ }\). This is the case in a generating machine. From the phasor diagram we find

 

\( F_{1}^{2}= (1928)^{2} + (1878)^{2} â 2\times 1928\times 1878 \sin 26^{\circ }\)

 

or \( F_{1} = 858 AT/pole (peak)\)

 

\( \cos \Psi =\frac{(858)^{2}+(1878)^{2}-(1928)^{2}}{2\times 858\times 1878}\)

 

or \( \Psi=80^{\circ}\)

 

\( \vec{F}_{1}lags \vec{F} _{r}\) by \( 80^{\circ }\)

 

\( F_{1}=\frac{3}{2} \times \frac{4\sqrt{2} }{\pi } k_{w}\left(\frac{N_{ph}(series)}{P} \right) I_{a}\)

 

\( K_{w} = K_{b} = 0.96; N_{ph}(series) = 96\)

 

\( 858=\frac{3}{2} \times \frac{4\sqrt{2} }{\pi } \times 0.96\frac{96}{4} \times I_{a}\)

 

\( I_{a} = 13.8 A\)
