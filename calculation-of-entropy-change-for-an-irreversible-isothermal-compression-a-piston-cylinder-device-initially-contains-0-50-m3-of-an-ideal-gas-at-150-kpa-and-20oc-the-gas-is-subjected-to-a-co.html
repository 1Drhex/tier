Calculation of Entropy Change for an Irreversible, Isothermal Compression

A piston–cylinder device initially contains 0.50 \(m ^{3}\) of an ideal gas at 150 kPa and 20ºC. The gas is subjected to a constant external pressure of 400 kPa and compressed in an isothermal process. Assume the surroundings are at 20ºC. Take \(c_{P}=25 R\) and assume the ideal gas model holds.

(a) Determine the heat transfer (in kJ) during the process.

(b) What is the entropy change of the system, surroundings, and universe?

(c) Is the process reversible, irreversible, or impossible?

Admin
Solution

(a) To determine the heat transfer during the process, we can apply the first law. The change in internal energy of an ideal gas undergoing an isothermal process is zero:

 

\(\Delta U=Q+W=0\)

 

Solving for Q gives:

 

\(Q=-W=P_{\text {ext }}\left(V_{2}-V_{1}\right)=P_{2}\left(\frac{P_{1} V_{1}}{P_{2}}-V_{1}\right)=V_{1}\left(P_{1}-P_{2}\right)\)

 

Finally, plugging in numerical values:

 

\(Q=\left(0.50\left[ m ^{3}\right]\right)(-250[ kPa ])=-125[ kJ ]\)

 

(b) To fi nd the entropy change of the system, we can apply Equation (3.23):

 

\(\Delta s=c_{P} \ln \left(\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}\right)-R \ln \left(\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}\right)\left(c_{P} \text { is constant }\right)\) (3.23)

 

The first term is zero, since the process is at constant temperature. Thus,

 

\(\Delta S_{ sys }=n \Delta s_{ sys }=\left(\frac{P V}{T}\right)_{1}\left(-\ln \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}\right)\)

 

Plugging in numerical values gives:

 

\(\Delta S_{s y s}=\left(\frac{150[ kPa ] 0.5\left[ m ^{3}\right]}{293[ K ]}\right)\left(-\ln \frac{400}{150}\right)=-0.25\left[\frac{ kJ }{ K }\right]\)

 

The entropy change of the surroundings is given by:

 

\(\Delta S_{\text {surr }}=\frac{Q_{\text {surr }}}{T}=\frac{-Q}{T}=\frac{125[ kJ ]}{293[ K ]}=0.43\left[\frac{ kJ }{ K }\right]\)

 

The entropy change of the universe is equal to that of the system plus the surroundings:

 

\(\Delta S_{\text {univ }}=\Delta S_{\text {sys }}+\Delta S_{\text {surr }}=0.18[ kJ / K ]\)

 

(c) Since entropy of the universe increases, this process is irreversible. The irreversibility arises from the fi nite pressure difference.
