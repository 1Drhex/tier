Guest Question.

Calculate the effective inductance of the circuit shown in Fig. 4.43 across AB.

Admin
Solution

The inductance matrix is

\(\begin{bmatrix} L_{11} &L_{12} &L_{13} \\ L_{21} &L_{22} &L_{23} \\ I_{31} &I_{32} &I_{33} \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix} 5 &0 &-2 \\ 0 &6 &-3 \\ -2 &-3 &17 \end{bmatrix}\)

Magnetic Circuits 4.41

From KVL \(\nu =\nu _{1}+\nu _{2} (4.35)\)

and \(\nu _{2}=\nu _{3} (4.36)\)

From KCL \(i_{1}=i_{2}+i_{3} (4.37)\)

\(\begin{bmatrix}\nu _{1}\\ \nu _{2}\\ \nu _{3}\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}5 &0 &-2 \\ 0 &6 &-3 \\ -2 &-3 &17 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} di_{1}/dt\\ di_{2}/dt\\ di_{3}/dt \end{bmatrix}\)

\(\nu _{1}=5\frac{di_{1}}{dt}-2\frac{di_{3}}{dt} (4.38)\)

and \(\nu _{2}=6\frac{di_{2}}{dt}-3\frac{di_{3}}{dt} (4.39)\)

\(\nu _{3}=-2\frac{di_{1}}{dt}-3\frac{di_{2}}{dt}+17\frac{di_{3}}{dt} (4.40)\)

From Eq. 4.35, we have

\(\nu =\nu _{1}+\nu _{2}\)

\(=5\frac{di_{1}}{dt}-2\frac{di_{3}}{dt}+6\frac{di_{2}}{dt}-3\frac{di_{3}}{dt}\)

\(\nu =5\frac{di_{1}}{dt}+6\frac{di_{2}}{dt}-5\frac{di_{3}}{dt} (4.41)\)

From Eq. 4.37

\(\frac{di_{1}}{dt}=\frac{di_{2}}{dt}+\frac{di_{3}}{dt}\)

Substituting Eq. 4.42 in Eq. 4.43, we have

\(\nu _{3}=-2\left [ \frac{di_{2}}{dt}+\frac{di_{3}}{dt} \right ]-3\left [ \frac{di_{2}}{dt} \right ]+17\left [\frac{di_{3}}{dt} \right ]\)

or \(-5\frac{di_{2}}{dt}+15\frac{di_{3}}{dt}=\nu _{3} (4.43)\)

Multiplying Eq. 4.39 by 5, we get

\(30\frac{di_{2}}{dt}+15\frac{di_{3}}{dt}=5\nu _{2}\) (4.44)

Adding Eqs (4.43) and (4.44), we get

\(25\frac{di_{2}}{dt}=\nu _{3}+5\nu _{2}\)

\(25\frac{di_{2}}{dt}=6\nu _{2}\)

4.42 Electrical Circuit Analysis-1

\(=6\nu _{3}, since \nu _{2}=\nu _{3}\)

or \(\nu _{2}=\frac{25}{6}\frac{di_{2}}{dt}\)

From Eq. 4.41

\(\frac{25}{6}\frac{di_{2}}{dt}=6\frac{di_{2}}{dt}-3\frac{di_{3}}{dt}\)

from which \(\frac{di_{2}}{dt}=\frac{18}{11}\frac{di_{3}}{dt}\)

From Eq. 4.44

\(\frac{di_{2}}{dt}=\frac{di_{2}}{dt}+\frac{11}{18}\frac{di_{2}}{dt}=\frac{29}{18}\frac{di_{2}}{dt}\)

Substituting the values of \(\frac{di_{2}}{dt} \)and \(\frac{di_{3}}{dt}\) in Eq. 4.41 yields

\(\nu =5\frac{di_{1}}{dt}+6\frac{18}{29}\frac{di_{1}}{dt}-5\frac{11}{18}\frac{di_{2}}{dt}\)

\(=5\frac{di_{2}}{dt}+\frac{108}{29}\frac{di_{1}}{dt}-\frac{55}{18}\frac{di_{1}}{dt}\)

\(\nu =\frac{198}{29}\frac{di_{1}}{dt}=6.827\frac{di_{1}}{dt}\)

\(\therefore\) equivalent inductance across \(AB=6.827 H\)
