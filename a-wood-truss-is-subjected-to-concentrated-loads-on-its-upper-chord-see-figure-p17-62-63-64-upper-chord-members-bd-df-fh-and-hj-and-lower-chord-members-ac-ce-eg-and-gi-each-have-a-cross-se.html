A wood truss is subjected to concentrated loads on its upper chord (see Figure P17.62/63/64). Upper chord members (BD, DF, FH, and HJ) and lower chord members (AC, CE, EG, and GI) each have a cross-sectional area of A = 8.00 \(\text { in. }^{2}\) , with web members (AB, AD, CD, CF, EF, FG, GH, HI, and IJ) each having a cross-sectional area of A = 5.25 \(\text { in. }^{2}\) . The elastic modulus for all member is E = 1,080 ksi. Assume all joints behave as pin joints. Make the assumption that P = 2.5 kips and calculate the vertical displacement of joint C employing the virtual-work method.
