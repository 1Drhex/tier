Guest Question.

Find the dynamic model of the two-link planar arm with a prismatic joint and a revolute joint in Fig. 7.16 with the Lagrange formulation. Then, consider the addition of a concentrated tip payload of mass \(m_{L}\) , and express the resulting model in a linear form with respect to a suitable set of dynamic parameters as in (7.81).

\(\tau \) = \(Y\left(q,\dot{q},\ddot{q} \right)\pi\) (7.81).

Admin
Solution

Consider the planar arm in Fig. S7.2, for which the vector of generalized coordinates is q = \(\left[d_{1} \vartheta _{2}\right]^{T}\)

Let \(m_{l_{1}}\) , \(m_{l_{2}}\) be the masses of the two links, \( l_{2}\), \(l_{2} \) the distances of the centres of mass from the respective joint axes, and \(m_{m_{1}}\) , \(m_{m_{2}}\) the masses of the rotors of the two joint motors. Let also \(I_{l_{2}}\) be the inertia tensor of Link 2 about Joint 2 axis, and \(I_{m_{1}}\) , \(I_{m_{2}}\) the moments of inertia about the axes of the two rotors. It is assumed that \(p_{m_{i}}\) = \(p_{i – 1}\) and \(z_{m_{i}}\) = \(z_{i – 1}\) , for i = 1, 2, i.e., the motors are located on the joint axes with centres of mass located at the origins of the respective frames.

With the choice of the frames in Fig. S7.2, it is \(z_{1} \) = \(\left[ 0 1 0\right]^{T}\) \(p_{1} \) = \(p_{m_{2}}\) = \(\left[ 0 0 d_{1}\right]^{T}\) , \(p_{l_{2}}\) = \(\left[ -l_{2}c_{2} 0 d_{1} + l_{2}s_{2}\right]^{T}\) Computation of the Jacobians in (7.16), (7.18) yields

\(J^{\left(l_{i}\right)}_{P}\) = \(\left[J^{\left(l_{i}\right) }_{P1} . . . J^{\left(l_{i}\right) }_{Pi} 0 . . . . 0\right]\) (7.16)

\(j^{\left(l_{i} \right) }_{P_{j} }\) = \(\begin{cases}z_{j – 1} & for a prismatic joint \\z_{j – 1}\times \left(p_{l_{i} } – p_{j – 1} \right) & for a revolute joint \end{cases}\) (7.18)

\(J^{\left(l_{1}\right)}_{P}\) = \(\left [ \begin{matrix}0 & 0 \\ 0& 0 \\ 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \) \(J^{\left(l_{2}\right)}_{P}\) = \(\left [ \begin{matrix}0 & l_{2}s_{2} \\ 0 & 0 \\ 1 & l_{2}s_{2} \end{matrix} \right ] \)

whereas computation of the Jacobians in (7.17), (7.19) yields

\(J^{\left(l_{i}\right) }_{O}\) = \(\left[J^{\left(l_{i}\right) }_{O1} . . . J^{\left(l_{i}\right) }_{Oi} 0 . . . . 0\right]\) (7.17)

\(j^{\left(l_{i} \right) }_{O_{j} }\) = \(\begin{cases}0 & for a prismatic joint \\z_{j – 1} & for a revolute joint \end{cases}\) (7.19)

\(J^{\left(l_{1}\right)}_{o}\) = \(\left [ \begin{matrix}0 & 0 \\ 0& 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \) \(J^{\left(l_{2}\right)}_{O}\) = \(\left [ \begin{matrix}0 & 0 \\ 0& 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Computation of the Jacobians in (7.25), (7.26)

\(J^{\left(m_{i}\right)}_{P}\) = \(\left[j^{\left(m_{i}\right) }_{P1} . . . j^{\left(m_{i}\right) }_{P,i – 1} 0 . . . . 0\right]\) (7.25)

\(j^{\left(m_{i} \right) }_{P_{j} }\) = \(\begin{cases}z_{j – 1} & for a prismatic joint \\z_{j – 1}\times \left(p_{m_{i} } – p_{j – 1} \right) & for a revolute joint \end{cases}\) (7.26)

\(J^{\left(m_{1}\right)}_{P}\) = \(\left [ \begin{matrix}0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \) \(J^{\left(m_{2}\right)}_{P}\) = \(\left [ \begin{matrix}0 & 0 \\ 0& 0 \\ 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \),

whereas computation of the Jacobians in (7.28), (7.29) yields

\(J^{\left(m_{i}\right)}_{O}\) = \(\left[j^{\left(m_{i}\right) }_{O1} . . . j^{\left(m_{i}\right) }_{O,i – 1} j^{\left(m_{i}\right) }_{Oi} 0 . . . . 0\right]\) (7.28)

\( j^{\left(m_{i} \right) }_{O{j} }\) =\(\begin{cases}j^{\left(l_{i} \right) }_{O{j} }& j = 1, . . . , i – 1 \\ k_{ r{i} }z_{ m_{i} }\ & j = i \end{cases}\) (7.29)

\(J^{\left(m_{1}\right)}_{O}\) = \(\left [ \begin{matrix}0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \\ k_{r{1}} & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \) \(J^{\left(m_{2}\right)}_{O}\) = \(\left [ \begin{matrix}0 & 0 \\ 0 & k_{r{2}} \\ 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

where \( k_{r{i}} \) is the gear reduction ratio of motor i.

From (7.32), the inertia matrix is

\(B(q)=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{\left(m_{l_i}J_P^{(l_i)T}J_P^{(l_i)}+J_O^{(l_i)T}R_i I_{l_i}^iR_i^TJ_O^{(l_i)}+m_{m_i}J_P^{\left(m_i\right)T }J_P^{(m_i)}+J_O^{(m_i)T}R_{m_i} I_{m_i}^{m_i}R_{m_i}^TJ_O^{(m_i)}\right) } \) (7.32)

B(q) = \(\left[\begin{matrix} b_{11} & b_{12}\left(\vartheta _{2} \right) \\ b_{12} \left(\vartheta _{2} \right)& b_{22} \end{matrix} \right] \)

\(b_{11} \) = \(m_{l_{1}} + m_{m_{2}} + m_{l_{2}} + k^{2}_{r1} I_{m_{1}}\)

\(b_{12} \) = \(m_{l_{2}}l_{2}c_{2}\)

\(b_{22}\) = \(I_{l_{2}} + m_{l_{2}} l^{2}_{2} + k^{2}_{r1} I_{m_{2}}\)

where \( I_{l_{2}}\) = \(I_{l_{2zz}} \)is the constant moment of inertia about Joint 2 axis.
Computation of Christoffel symbols as in (7.45) gives:

\(c_{ijk} \) = \(\left(\frac{\partial b_{ij} }{\partial q_{k}} + \frac{\partial b_{ik} }{\partial q_{j}} – \frac{\partial b_{jk} }{\partial q_{i}}\right)\) (7.45)

\(c_{111} \) = \(c_{112} \) = \(c_{121} \) = \(c_{211} \) = \(c_{221} \) = \(c_{222} \) = 0

\(c_{122} \) = \(\frac{\partial b_{12}}{\partial q_{2}} – \frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial b_{22}}{\partial q_{1}} \) = \(– m_{l_{2}} l_{2} s_{2}\)

leading to the matrix

\(C\left(q,\dot{q} \right)\) = \(\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & -m_{l_{2} } l_{2} s_{2} \dot{\vartheta } _{2} \\ 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right]\)

Computing the matrix N in (7.47) gives

\(N\left(q, \dot{q}\right) \) = \( \dot{B}\left(q\right) – 2C \left(q, \dot{q}\right) \) (7.47)

= \(\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & m_{l_{2} } l_{2} s_{2} \dot{\vartheta } _{2} \\ -m_{l_{2} } l_{2} s_{2} \dot{\vartheta } _{2} & 0 \end{matrix} \right]\)

which is skew-symmetric.

As for the gravitational terms, with \(g_{0}\) = \( \left[ -g 0 0 \right]^{T}\) (7.39) gives :

\(\frac{\partial u }{\partial q_{i} } = – \sum\limits_{ j = 1}^{m}{\left(m_{l_{j} } g^{T}_{o}\frac{\partial p_{l_{j} } }{\partial q_{i}} + m_{m_{j} } g^{T}_{o}\frac{\partial p_{m_{j} } }{\partial q_{i}} \right) } \\[0.5cm] \quad = – \sum\limits_{ j = 1}^{m}{\left(m_{l_{j} } g^{T}_{o}j_{P{i} }^{(l_j)} (q) + m_{m_{j} } g^{T}_{o}j_{P{i} }^{(m_j)} (q) \right) } = g_i(q)\) (7.39)

\(g_{1}\) = 0

\(g_{2}\) = \( m_{l_{2} } l_{2} gs_{2}\)

In the absence of friction and tip contact forces, the resulting equations of motion are

\(\left(m_{l_{1} } + m_{l_{2} } + m_{m_{2} } + k^{2}_{r1} I_{m_{1} } \right) \ddot{d}_{1} + m_{l_{2} } l_{2} c_{2} \ddot{\vartheta _{2} } – m_{l_{2} } l_{2} s_{2} \dot{\vartheta } ^{2}_{2}\) = \(\tau_{1} \) (S7.4)

\(m_{l_{2} } l_{2} c_{2} \dot{d _{1} } + \left(I_{l_{2} } + m_{l_{2} } l^{2}_{2} + k^{2}_{r2} I_{m_{2} }\right)\ddot{\vartheta _{2} } + m_{l_{2} }l_{2}g s_{2}\) = \(\tau_{2} \) (S7.5)

where \(τ_1\) and \(τ_2\) respectively denote the force applied to Joint 1 and the torque applied to Joint 2. The presence of a concentrated tip payload of mass \(m_{L}\) alters Link 2 parameters as follows:

\(m^{\prime }_{l_{2}}\) = \( m_{l_{2} } + m_{L}\) \(l^{\prime }_{2} \) = \(\frac{m_{l_{2} } l_{2} + m_{L} a_{2}} {m^{\prime }_{l_{2}} } \)

\(I^{\prime }_{l_{2}}\) = \(I_{l_{2} } + m_{l_{2} } \left(l^{\prime }_{2} – l_{2}\right)^{2} + m_{L} \left(a_{2} – l^{\prime }_{2} \right)^{2}\)

and the same formal dynamic model can be written as above in terms of the new parameters.

A minimum parameterization of the dynamic model can be obtained in terms of the following parameter vector:

\(\pi ^{\prime }\) = \(\left[\pi^{\prime }_{1} \pi^{\prime }_{2} \pi^{\prime }_{3}\right]^{T}\)

\(\pi^{\prime }_{1}\) = \(m_{l_{2} } + m_{m_{2} } + m^{\prime }_{l_{2}} + k^{2}_{r1} I_{m_{1} } \)

\(\pi^{\prime }_{2}\) = \(m^{\prime }_{l_{2}} l^{\prime }_{2} \)

\(\pi^{\prime }_{3}\) = \( l^{\prime }_{l_{2}} + m^{\prime }_{l_{2}} l^{\prime 2}_{2} + k^{2}_{r2} I_{m_{2} } \)

Accordingly, the elements of the regressor are

\( y^{\prime }_{11}\) = \(\ddot{d_{1}} \)

\( y^{\prime }_{12}\) = \( c_{2} \ddot{\vartheta } _{2} – s_{2} \dot{\vartheta } ^{2}_{2}\)

\( y^{\prime }_{13}\) = 0

\( y^{\prime }_{21}\) = 0

\( y^{\prime }_{22}\) = \( c_{2} \ddot{d_{1}} + gs_{2}\)

\( y^{\prime }_{23}\) = \(\ddot{\vartheta } _{2} \)
