Guest Question.

A compacted fill of 8.5 m height is to be placed over a large area. The soil profile under the filled area is shown in the scheme below. Oedometer tests on samples from points A and B produced the following results:

 

\(C_{c} \) \(C_{r} \) \(e_{o} \) \({\sigma}'_{c} \left (kPa \right)\)
Sample A 0.25 0.08 0.66 101
Sample B 0.2 0.06 0.45 510

 

Compute \(S_{oed}\) and make an estimate of \(S_{i}\) and \(S_{c}\).

Admin
Solution

The loaded area is large and, therefore, B/z and L/z values will be large too (say three or above). Assume the same vertical pressure q will be transmitted to any point located in the concerned subsurface layers below centre of the filled area. Accordingly,

\( {\sigma}'_{z} = q = 20.3 \times 8.5 = 172.6 kPa \)

At location of sample A:

\( {\sigma}'_{o} = 2 \times 18.3 + 2 \left ( 19 – 10 \right) = 54.6 kPa < {\sigma}'_{c} = 101 kPa. \) Hence, the soil is overconsolidated.

\( {\sigma}'_{1} = {\sigma}'_{o} + {\sigma}'_{z} = 54.6 + 172.6 = 227.2 kPa > {\sigma}'_{c} \rightarrow Case II\)

 

Equation : \( S_{c} = \frac{C_{r} H}{ 1 + e_{o}} log \frac{{\sigma}'_{o} + {\sigma}'_{z}}{{\sigma}'_{o}}\)

In order to compute settlements more accurately, the stiff silty clay is divided into three layers and the very stiff clay into four layers, as shown in the scheme above and as indicated in Table.

Calculate \( {\sigma}'_{o}\), \( {\sigma}'_{c}\) and \( \left ({\sigma}'_{o} + {\sigma}'_{z} \right )\) at midpoint of each layer and determine Soed using Equations as applicable.

For the Case II overconsolidation, it is required to determine the \( {\sigma}'_{c}\) value at midpoint of each layer, as follows:

Layer I; \( {\sigma}'_{c} = 101 – \left [ 1 \times 18.3 + 2\left ( 19 – 10 \right) \right] = 64.7 kPa\)

Layer II; \( {\sigma}'_{c} = 101 – \left [ 0.5 \left ( 19 – 10 \right) \right] = 96.5 kPa\)

Layer III; \( {\sigma}'_{c} = 101 + \left [ 3 \left ( 19 – 10 \right) \right] = 128.0 kPa\)

The clays are stiff and overconsolidated; hence, according to Burland et al. (1977), the immediate settlement \( S_{i}\) and the consolidation settlement \( S_{c}\) may be estimated, approximately, as

\( S_{i} = 0.5 S_{oed} = 0.5 \times 834 = 417 mm \) \( S_{c} = 0.5 S_{oed} = 0.5 \times 834 = 417 mm \)

 

Also, the \( S_{c}\) may be estimated, approximately, using Equation as follows:

According to Craig (1995), value the settlement ratio K for lightly over-consolidated clays is typically between 0.5 and 0.7. Values of \( {\sigma }'_{c} \) and \( {\sigma }'_{o} \) indicate that the clays are lightly overconsolidated \( \left ( OCR < 3 \right )\). Hence, an average value of K = 0 6 may be used to estimate, approximately,

\(S_{c} = 0.6 S_{oed} = 0.6 \times 834 = 500 mm\)
