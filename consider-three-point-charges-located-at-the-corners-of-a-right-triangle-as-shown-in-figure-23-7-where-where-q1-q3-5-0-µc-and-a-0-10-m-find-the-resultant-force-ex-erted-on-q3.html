Guest Question.

Consider three point charges located at the corners of a right triangle as shown in Figure 23.7, where where q1 = q3 = 5.0 µC and a = 0.10 m. Find the resultant force ex- erted on q3 .

 

Admin
Solution

First, note the direction of the individual forces exerted by q1 and q2 on q3 . The force F23 exerted by q2 on q3 is attractive because q2 and q3 have opposite signs. The force F13 exerted by q1 on q3 is repulsive because both charges are positive.

The magnitude of \( \mathbf{F}_{23}\) is

The magnitude of \(\mathbf{F}_{23}\) is

\(F_{23} =k_{e} \frac{\left|q_{2}\right|\left|q_{3}\right|}{a^{2}} =\left(8.99 \times 10^{9} \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{2}}{\mathrm{C}^{2}}\right) \frac{\left(2.0 \times 10^{-6} \mathrm{C}\right)\left(5.0 \times 10^{-6} \mathrm{C}\right)}{(0.10 \mathrm{~m})^{2}}=9.0 \mathrm{~N}\)

Note that because \( q_{3}\) and\( q_{2}\) have oppasite signs, \( \mathbf{F}_{23}\) is to the left, as shown in Figure 23.7 . The magnitude of the force exerted by \( q_{1}\) on \( q_{3}\) is

\( F_{13}=k_{e} \frac{\left|q_{1}\right|\left|q_{3}\right|}{(\sqrt{2} a)^{2}}\) \( =\left(8.99 \times 10^{9} \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{2}}{\mathrm{C}^{2}}\right) \frac{\left(5.0 \times 10^{-6} \mathrm{C}\right)\left(5.0 \times 10^{-6} \mathrm{C}\right)}{2(0.10 \mathrm{~m})^{2}} \) \( =11 \mathrm{~N}\)

The force \( \mathbf{F}_{13}\) is repulsive and makes an angle of \( 45^{\circ}\) with the x axis. Therefore, the x and y components of \( \mathbf{F}_{13}\) are equal, with magnitude given by \( F_{13} \cos 45^{\circ}=7.9 \mathrm{~N}\)

The force \( \mathbf{F}_{23}\) is in the negative x direction. Hence, the x and y components of the resultant force acting on \( q_{3}\) are

\( F_{3 x}=F_{13 x}+F_{23}=7.9 \mathrm{~N}-9.0 \mathrm{~N}=-1.1 \mathrm{~N}\) \( F_{3 y}=F_{13 y}=7.9 \mathrm{~N}\)

We can also express the resultant force acting on \( q_{3}\) in unitvector form as

\( \mathbf{F}_{3}=(-1.1 \mathbf{i}+7.9 \mathbf{j}) \mathrm{N}\)
