Guest Question.

A large machine has a mass of 200 kg and is mounted on an undamped elastic foundation of stiffness \(2.5\times 10^{6} N/m \) as shown in Figure 6.36(a). During operation at 110 r/s, the machine is subject to a harmonic force of magnitude 2200 N.
(a) Determine the steady-state amplitude of the machine as it operates.
(b) Determine the required stiffness of an undamped vibration absorber of mass 20 kg
such that steady-state vibrations of the machine are eliminated during operation.

(c) Determine the amplitude of the absorber mass when the vibration absorber of part (b) is used.
(d) What are the natural frequencies of the resulting two degree-of-freedom system?
(e) When this absorber is used, what is the frequency range such that the machine’s steadystate amplitude is less than 1.2 mm?

Admin
Solution

(a) The natural frequency of the machine mounted on the elastic foundation is

\(\omega_{n} =\sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} =\sqrt{\frac{2.5 \times 10^{6}N/m}{200kg} }=111.8 rad/s \) (a)

The frequency ratio is

\(r=\frac{\omega }{\omega _{n}}=\frac{110 rad/s}{1118.8 rad/s}=0.984 \) (b)

The steady-state amplitude of the machine is

\(X=\frac{F_{0}}{m\omega ^{2}_{n}}M(0.984,0)=\frac{2200 N}{2.5\times 10^{6}N/m} \frac{1}{1-(0.984)^{2}} =2.75 cm \) (c)

To eliminate steady-state vibrations at the excitation speed, the absorber is tuned to the excitation speed

\( \omega _{22}=\sqrt{\frac{k_{2}}{m_{2}} }=\omega \) (d)

Thus

\(k_{2}=m_{2}\omega ^{2}=(20kg)(110 rad/s)^{2}= 2.42 \times 10^{5}N/m\) (e)

(c) The steady-state amplitude of the absorber when the system operates at the frequency to which the absorber is tuned is

\( X_{2}=\frac{F_{0}}{k_{2}}=\frac{2200 N}{2.42\times 10^{5}N/m} =9.1 mm\) (f)

The absorber attached to the machine is illustrated in Figure 6.36(b).
(d) The ratio of the absorber mass to the mass of the machine is \(\mu = (20 kg)/(200 kg) = 0.1\). The ratio of the tuned frequency to the natural frequency of the machine is the same as the original frequency ratio q = 0.984. Natural frequencies of the two degree-of-freedom system with the absorber in place are

\(\omega _{1,2}=\frac{\omega _{11}}{\sqrt{2} }\sqrt{1+q^{2}(1+\mu )\pm \sqrt{q^{4}(1+\mu ^{2})+2(\mu -1)q^{2}+1} } \)

\( =\frac{111.8\frac{r}{s} }{\sqrt{2} }\sqrt{1+(0.984)^{2}(1+0.1)\pm \sqrt{(0.984)^{4}(1+0.1)^{2}+2(0.1-1)(0.984)^{2}+1} } \)

\(=94.8 rad/s, 129.7 rad/s \) (g)

(e) Let be a varying frequency. Define \(r_{1 }=\frac{\omega }{111.8 rad/s}\) and \(r_{2 }=\frac{\omega }{110 rad/s}\). The frequency
response of the machine is given by

\(X_{1}=\frac{F_{0}}{k_{1}}\left|\frac{1-r^{2}_{2}}{r^{2}_{1}r^{2}_{2}-r^{2}_{2}-(1+\mu )r^{2}_{1}+1} \right| \)

\( =\frac{2200 N}{2.5\times 10^{6 } N/m} \left|\frac{1-\left(\frac{\omega }{110 rad/s}^{2} \right) }{\left(\frac{\omega }{111.8 rad/s} \right)^{2}\left(\frac{\omega }{110 rad/s} \right)^{2} – \left(\frac{\omega }{110rad/s} \right)^{2}-(1+0.1) \left(\frac{\omega }{111.8 rad/s} \right)^{2} +1 } \right| \) (h)

The values of \( \omega\) for which the steady-state amplitude of the machine is less 1.2 mm are
obtained by setting \(X1 \lt 0.0012\) m in Equation (h) and solving for \( \omega\). There are two values of which satisfy \(X1\lt 0.0012 m\): one value less than 22 and one value greater than \( \omega_{22}\). In performing the calculations, note that the numerator is positive for \( \omega \lt\omega_{22}\) and negative for \( \omega \gt\omega_{22}\), but the denominator is always positive in the operating range. The equation can be rearranged into a quadratic equation in \( \omega^{2}\), resulting in an operating range of

\( 104.3 rad/s \lt \omega \lt 117.0 rad/s \) (i)
