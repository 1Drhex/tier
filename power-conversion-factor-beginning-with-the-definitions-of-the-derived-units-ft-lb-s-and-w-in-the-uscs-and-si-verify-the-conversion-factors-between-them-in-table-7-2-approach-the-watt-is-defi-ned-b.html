Power Conversion Factor

Beginning with the definitions of the derived units (ft.lb)/s and W in the USCS and SI, verify the conversion factors between them in Table 7.2.

Table 7.2 Conversion Factors Between Various Units for Power in the USCS and SI

(ft . lb)/s W hp
1 1.356 \(1.818×10^{-3}\)
0.7376 1 \(1.341×10^{-3}\)
550 745.7 1
Admin
Solution

The conversion factor is given by

\(1\frac{ft.lb}{s} =\left(1\frac{ft.lb}{s}\right)\left(0.3048\frac{m}{ft} \right) \left(4.448\frac{N}{lb} \right) \)

\(=1.356\left(\frac{\cancel{ft}.\cancel{lb}}{s}\right) \left(\frac{m}{\cancel{ft}} \right) \left(\frac{N}{\cancel{lb}} \right) \)

\(=1.356\frac{N.m}{s} \)

\(=1.356\frac{J}{s} \)

\(=1.356 W\)

where we have used the definition of the derived unit joule from Table 3.2.

Table 3.2 Certain Derived Units in the SI

Quantity SI Derived Unit Abbreviation Definition
Length micrometer or micron \(\mu m\) \(1\mu m = 10^{-6} m\)
Volume liter L \(1 L = 0.001 m^{3}\)
Force newton N \(1 N = 1 (kg . m)/s^{2}\)
Torque, or moment of a force newton-meter N . m —
Pressure or stress pascal Pa \(1 Pa = 1 N/m^{2}\)
Energy, work, or heat joule J 1 J = 1 N . m
Power watt W 1 W = 1 J/s
Temperature degree Celsius °C °C = K – 273.15
Although a change in temperature of 1 Kelvin equals a change of 1 degree Celsius, numerical values are converted using the formula.

Discussion
The conversion factor between the dimensions watt and (ft .lb)/s is the reciprocal of the conversion factor from (ft . lb)/s to watts. Thus, \(1 W=(1.356)^{-1}(ft.lb)/s= 0.7376(ft.lb)/s.\)

\(1\frac{ft.lb}{s} =1.356 W \)

\(1 W=0.7376\frac{ft.lb}{s} \)
