Guest Question.

A block with weight G resting on a rough inclined plane (angle of slope α, coefficient of static friction \(\mu _{0} \)) is subjected to an external force F (Fig. 9.5a). Specify the range of F such that the block stays at rest.

Admin
Solution

If F is a sufficiently large positive force, the block would move upwards without static friction. The static friction force H then is oriented downwards (Fig. 9.5b). From the equilibrium conditions

\(\nearrow : F-G\sin \alpha -H=0 , \nwarrow :N-G\cos \alpha =0 \)

the static friction force and the normal force can be determined:
H = F − G sinα , N = G cosα.

Inserting these forces into (9.4a) , \(H\leq H_{0} = \mu _{0}N \). establishes the range of F fulfilling the static friction condition:

\( F-G\sin \alpha \leq \mu _{0}G\cos \alpha \rightarrow F\leq G(\sin \alpha+\mu _{0}\cos \alpha)\)

Using (9.5), \(\tan \varrho _{0}=\mu _{0}\). we can reformulate the preceding relation in terms of the static friction angle \( \varrho _{0}\):

\(F\leq G(\sin \alpha +\tan \varrho _{0}\cos \alpha )=G\frac{\sin (\alpha +\varrho _{0})}{\cos \varrho _{0}} \). (a)

On the other hand, if F is too small, the block may slip downwards due to its weight. The static friction force preventing this motion is then oriented upwards according to Fig. 9.5c. In this case, from the equilibrium equations

\(\nearrow : F-G\sin \alpha +H=0 , \nwarrow :N-G\cos \alpha =0 \)

and the static friction condition

\(H\leq \mu _{0}N \) ,

we obtain the following inequality:

\(G\sin \alpha -F\leq \mu _{0}G\cos \alpha \)

This result can be formulated in terms of the static friction angle:

\(F\geq G(\sin \alpha-\mu _{0}G\cos \alpha)=G\frac{(\sin \alpha-\varrho _{0})}{\cos\varrho _{0} } \). (b)

Summarizing the results (a) and (b) yields the following admissible range for the force F:

\(\underline{\underline{G\frac{(\sin \alpha-\varrho _{0})}{\cos\varrho _{0} }\leq F\leq G\frac{(\sin \alpha+\varrho _{0})}{\cos\varrho _{0} }} } \). (c)

Assuming, for example, the case “steel on steel”, friction coefficient \(\mu _{0}=0.15 \) from Table 9.1 yields \(\varrho _{0} \) = arctan 0.15 = 0.149.

coefficient of static friction \(\mu _{0}\) coefficient of kinetic friction \(\mu \)
steel on ice 0.03 0.015
steel on steel 0.15 . . . 0.5 0.1 . . . 0.4
steel on Teflon 0.04 0.04
leather on metal 0.4 0.3
wood on wood 0.5 0.3
car tire on snow 0.7 . . . 0.9 0.5 . . . 0.8
ski on snow 0.1 . . . 0.3 0.04 . . . 0.2

Choosing furthermore α = 10º \(\overset{\wedge }{=} \)0.175 rad, we obtain from (c)

\(G\frac{\sin (0.175-0.149)}{\cos0.149 }\leq F\leq G\frac{\sin (0.175+0.149)}{\cos0.149}\)

or

0.026G ≤ F ≤ 0.32G.

In this numerical example, the block stays at rest provided that F is in the range between approximately 3% and 30% of its weight G. If α < \(\varrho _{0}\) , F can also take on negative values according to (c). In the case α =\(\varrho _{0}\) , the lower limit of the range of F equals zero. Therefore, the slope of the inclined plane is a direct measure of the coefficient of static friction. A body under the action of only its own weight (i.e., F = 0) stays at rest on an inclined plane as long as α ≤ \(\varrho _{0}\) holds.
