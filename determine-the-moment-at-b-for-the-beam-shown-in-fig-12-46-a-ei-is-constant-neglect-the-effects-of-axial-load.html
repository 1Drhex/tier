Guest Question.

Determine the moment at B for the beam shown in Fig. 12–46 a . EI is constant. Neglect the effects of axial load.

Admin
Solution

Principle of Superposition. Since the axial load on the beam is neglected, there will be a vertical force and moment at A and B . Here there are only two available equations of equilibrium \((∑M = 0, ∑F_y = 0)\) and so the problem is indeterminate to the second degree. We will assume that \(B_y\) and \(M_B\) are redundant, so that by the principle of superposition, the beam is represented as a cantilever, loaded separately by the distributed load and reactions \(B_y\) and \(M_B\) , Figs. 12–46 b , 12–46 c , and 12–46 d .

Compatibility Equations. Referring to the displacement and slope at B , we require

\((\nearrow +) \quad\quad 0=\theta _B+\theta ^\prime _B+\theta _B^{\prime \prime }\) (1)

 

\((+\downarrow ) \quad\quad 0=v _B+v^\prime _B+v _B^{\prime \prime }\) (2)

Load-Displacement Equations. Using the table in Appendix C to calculate the slopes and displacements, we have

\(\theta _B=\frac{wL^3}{48EI}=\frac{3kip/ft(12ft)^3}{48EI}=\frac{108kip\cdot ft^2}{EI}\swarrow \)

 

\(v_B=\frac{7wL^4}{384EI}=\frac{7(3kip/ft)(12ft)^4}{384EI}=\frac{1134kip\cdot ft^3}{EI}\downarrow \)

 

\(\theta ^\prime _B=\frac{PL^2}{2EI}=\frac{B_y(12ft)^2}{2EI}=\frac{72B_y}{EI}\swarrow \)

 

\(v ^\prime _B=\frac{PL^3}{3EI}=\frac{B_y(12ft)^3}{3EI}=\frac{576B_y}{EI}\downarrow \)

 

\(\theta ^{\prime \prime } _B=\frac{ML}{EI}=\frac{M_B(12ft)}{EI}=\frac{12M_B}{EI}\swarrow \)

 

\(v ^{\prime \prime } _B=\frac{ML^2}{2EI}=\frac{M_B(12ft)^2}{2EI}=\frac{72M_B}{EI}\downarrow \)

Substituting these values into Eqs. 1 and 2 and canceling out the common factor EI , we get

\((\nearrow +) \quad\quad 0=108+72B_y+12M_B\)

 

\((+\downarrow ) \quad\quad 0=1134+576B_y+72M_B\)

Solving these equations simultaneously gives

\(B_y=-3.375kip\)

 

\(M_B=11.25kip·ft\)

NOTE: The reactions at A can now be determined from the equilibrium equations.
