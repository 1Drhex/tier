Guest Question.

Consider the plant of Eqs. (12.116):

\(\mathbf{\dot{x}} =\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1\\ -3 & -5\end{bmatrix}\mathbf{x} + \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}u\) (12:116a)

\(y=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\) (12:116b)

a. Design a controller without integral control to yield a 10% overshoot and a settling time of 0.5 second. Evaluate the steady-state error for a unit step input.

b. Repeat the design of (a) using integral control. Evaluate the steady-state error for a unit step input .

Admin
Solution

a. Using the requirements for settling time and percent overshoot, we find that the desired characteristic polynomial is

\( s^{2} + 16s + 183.1\) (12:117)

Since the plant is represented in phase-variable form, the characteristic polynomial for the controlled plant with state-variable feedback is

\(s^{2}+ \left ( 5+ k_{2} \right )s+ \left ( 3+ k_{1} \right )\) (12:118)

Equating the coefficients of Eqs. (12.117) and (12.118), we have

\(\mathbf{K} = \begin{bmatrix}k_{1} & k_{2} \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}180.1 & 11 \end{bmatrix}\) (12:119)

From Eqs .\(\mathbf{x = Ax + Bu = Ax + B(-Kx + r) = (A – BK) x + Br} , y = \mathbf{Cx}\)(12.3),the controlled plant with state-variable feedback represented in phase-variable form is

\(\mathbf{\dot{x}} = \mathbf{\left ( A-BK \right )x +Br} =\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -183.1 & -16 \end{bmatrix}\mathbf{x} + \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1\end{bmatrix}r\) (12:120a)

\(y = \mathbf{Cx} =\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\mathbf{x}\) (12:120b)

Using Eq. \(e(\infty ) = 1 -y_{ss} = 1-\mathbf{CV} = 1 + \mathbf{CA^{-1}B}\) (7.96), we find that the steady-state error for a step input is

\(e\left ( \infty \right ) = 1+\mathbf{C\left ( A-BK \right )^{-1}B}\) \(=1+ \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}0 & 1\\ -183.1 & -16\end{bmatrix}^{-1}\begin{bmatrix}0 \\1 \end{bmatrix}\)

=0.995 (12:121)

b. We now use Eqs.\(\begin{bmatrix}\mathbf{\dot{x}}\\ \dot{x}_{N}\end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix}\mathbf{(A – BK)} & \mathbf{BK_{e}} \\ \mathbf{-C} & 0 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}\mathbf{x}\\ x_{N}\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}0\\ 1\end{bmatrix} r , y = \begin{bmatrix}\mathbf{C} & 0\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}\mathbf{x}\\ x_{N}\end{bmatrix} \) (12.115) to represent the integral-controlled plant as follows :

\(\begin{bmatrix} \dot{x}_{1} \\ \dot{x}_{2} \\ \dot{x}_{N} \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}\left ( \begin{bmatrix} 0 &1 \\ -3 & -5 \end{bmatrix}-\begin{bmatrix} 0\\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} k_{1} & k_{2} \end{bmatrix} \right ) & \begin{bmatrix} 0\\ 1 \end{bmatrix} k_{e}\\ -\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x_{1}\\ x_{2}\\ x_{N}\end{bmatrix} +\begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} r\)

\(=\begin{bmatrix}0 & 1 & 0 \\ -\left ( 3+k_{1} \right ) & -\left ( 5+k_{2} \right ) & k_{e} \\ -1 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}x_{1}\\ x_{2}\\ x_{N}\end{bmatrix} +\begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} r\) (12:122a)

\(y=\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_{1}\\ x_{2} \\ x_{N} \end{bmatrix}\) (12:122b)

Using Eq. \(T(s) =\frac{Y(s)}{U(s)} = \mathbf{C}(s\mathbf{I-A)^{-1}B+D}\) (3.73) and the plant of Eqs. (12.116), we find that the transfer function of the plant is \(G(s)= 1/(s^{2} + 5s + 3)\) . The desired characteristic polynomial for the closed-loop integral-controlled system is shown in Eq. (12.117). Since the plant has no zeros ,we assume no zeros for the closed-loop system and augment Eq. (12.117) with a third pole, (s + 100) , which has a real part greater than five times that of the desired dominant second-order poles. The desired third-order closed-loop system characteristic polynomial is

\(( s + 100)( s^{2} + 16s + 183.1) = s^{3} + 116s^{2} + 1783.1s + 18,310\) (12:123)

The characteristic polynomial for the system of Eqs. \(\mathbf{\dot{x} = Ax + Bu} , \dot{x}_{N} = \mathbf{- Cx +r} ,y = \mathbf{Cx}\) (12.112) is

\(s^{3} + ( 5 + k_{2}) s^{2} + (3+ k_{1} )s + K_{e}\) (12:124)

Matching coefficients from Eqs. (12.123) and (12.124), we obtain

\(k_{1} = 1780.1\) (12:125a)

\( k_{2} = 111 \)(12:125b)

\( k_{e} = 18,310 \)(12:125c)

Substituting these values into Eqs. (12.122) yields this closed-loop integral-controlled system:

\(\begin{bmatrix} \dot{x}_{1} \\ \dot{x}_{2} \\ \dot{x}_{N} \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0\\ -1783.1 & -116 & 18,310 \\ -1 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{N} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} r \) (12:126a)

\(y =\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{N} \end{bmatrix}\) (12:126b)

In order to check our assumption for the zero, we now apply Eq. (3.73) to Eqs. (12.126) and find the closed-loop transfer function to be

\(T\left ( s \right )=\frac{18.310}{s^{3}+ 116s^{2}+ 1783.1 s + 18.310}\) (12:127)

Since the transfer function matches our design, we have the desired transient response.

Now let us find the steady-state error for a unit step input. Applying Eq. (7.96) to Eqs. (12.126), we obtain

\(e\left ( \infty \right ) = 1+ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}0 & 1 & 0 \\ -1783.1 & -116 & 18.310\\ -1 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}^{-1}\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}=0\) (12:128)

Thus, the system behaves like a Type 1 system.
