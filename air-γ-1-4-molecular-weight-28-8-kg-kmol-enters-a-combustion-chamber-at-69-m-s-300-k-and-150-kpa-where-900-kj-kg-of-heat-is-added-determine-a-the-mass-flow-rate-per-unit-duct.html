Guest Question.

Air (\(\gamma\) = 1.4‚ molecular weight = 28.8 kg/kmol), enters a combustion chamber at 69 m/s, 300 K and 150 kPa, where 900 kJ/kg of heat is added. Determine (a) the mass flow rate per unit duct area, (b) exit properties and (c) inlet Mach number if the heat added is 1825 kJ/kg.

Admin
Solution

Given \(P_{1}=150 kPa,\) \(T_{1} = 300 K,\) \(u_{1} = 69 m/s.\)

\(M_{1} =u_{1} / \sqrt{\gamma RT_{1} } =0.2,\)

 

\(T_{0,1} =T_{1} \left(1+ \frac{\gamma -1}{2}M^{2}_{1} \right) =302K\)

and

\(P_{0,1} =P_{1} \left(\frac{T_{0,1} }{T_{1} } \right) ^{\frac{\gamma }{\gamma -1} } =154kPa .\)

(a) \(\dot{m} =\rho _{1} u_{1} A=\frac{P_{1} }{RT_{1} } u_{1} A=0.12\times 10^{2} kg/ s .\)

(b) From the table for heat addition, for \(M_{1} =0.2,\) we get

\(\frac{T_{0,1} }{T^{\ast }_{0} }\thickapprox 0.1736\) and \(\frac{P_{0,1} }{P^{\ast }_{0} }\thickapprox 1.235.\)

Therefore, \({T^{\ast }_{0} }=1740K\) and \({P^{\ast }_{0} }=125kPa.\) It follows that

\(T_{0,2} =T_{0,1} + \frac{q}{C_{p} } =1193K.\)

Since \(T_{0,2} / T^{\ast }_{0} =0.6857,\) from the table for heat addition, we get \(M_{2} \approx 0.49\) and \(P_{0.2} / P^{\ast }_{0}=1.118 . \) Hence \(P_{0.2}=140kPa,\)

\(T_{2} =\frac{T_{0,2} }{\left(1+ \frac{\gamma – 1}{2} M^{2}_{2} \right) } =1138K\)

and

\(P_{2} =\frac{T_{0,2} }{\left(1+ \frac{\gamma – 1}{2} M^{2}_{2} \right) } ^{\frac{\gamma }{\gamma -1} } =119kPa .\)

(c) For the given inlet conditions, \(q^{\ast } =C_{p} =\left(T^{\ast }_{0}- T_{0,1} \right) 1453 kJ/kg.\) Since the heat to be added is greater than 1453 kJ/kg, we must find an inlet state (1′) for which \(q^{\ast } =1825 kJ/kg.\) Inlet stagnation conditions remain the
same. Hence,

\(T_{0}^{*}=\frac{q^{*}}{C_{p}}+T_{0,1^{\prime}}=2108 \mathrm{~K}\)
Thus,
\(\frac{T_{0,1^{\prime}}}{T_{0}^{*}}=0.1432 \Rightarrow \mathbf{M}_{\mathbf{1}^{\prime}}=\mathbf{0 . 1 8}\)
