Guest Question.

If the central vertical plane of the box beam of Exercise 20.2, i.e., the vertical plane through booms 2 and 5, has a sweep of 20° as shown in Fig. 20.9(a) calculate the forces in the booms and the shear flow distribution in the walls at a section BB, 2 m distant from the built-in end. Note that the span of the beam has been retained.

Admin
Solution

At the section 2 m from the built-in end the internal force system and second moment of area \(I_{x x}\) are identical to those in , i.e.,

 

\(S_{y}=100 kN , \quad S_{x}=0, \quad M_{x}=-200 kNm , \quad M_{y}=0, \quad I_{x x}=5.4 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\)

 

Also \(P_{z, r}=-0.37 y_{r} B_{r}\) as in . The values of \(\partial x_{r} / \partial z\) are obtained by elementary trigonometry and are listed in Table 20.2 as are the values of \(\partial y_{r} / \partial z\) which are identical to those in . The final boom loads \(P_{r}\) are listed in column 7. From Table 20.2 \(\sum_{r=1}^{n} P_{x, r}=0, \sum_{r=1}^{n} P_{y, r}=33.4 kN , \sum_{r=1}^{n} P_{y, r} \eta_{r}=-74.0 kNm\) (i.e., anticlockwise), \(\sum_{r=1}^{n} P_{y, r} \xi_{r}=0 . \text { Then } S_{x, w}=0, \quad S_{y, w}=100-33.4=66.6 kN\). The \(q_{ b }\) shear flows are identical to those in . Therefore, taking moments about the center of symmetry and noting that the line of action of the externally applied load is 0.33 m to the right of the center of symmetry (again obtained from elementary trigonometry) we have
