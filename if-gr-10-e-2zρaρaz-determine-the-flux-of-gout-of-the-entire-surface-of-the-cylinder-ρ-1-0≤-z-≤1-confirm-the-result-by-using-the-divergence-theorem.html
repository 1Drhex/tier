Guest Question.

If \(G(r)=10e^{-2z}(\rho a_{\rho }+a_{z})\), determine the flux of Gout of the entire surface of the cylinder ρ= 1, 0≤ z ≤1. Confirm the result by using the divergence theorem.

Admin
Solution

If ψ is the flux of G through the given surface, shown in Figure 3 .18, then

\(\psi =\oint\limits_{s}^{}{G.ds}=\psi _{t}+\psi _{b}+\psi _{s}\)

where \(\psi_{t}, \psi_{b},\) and \(\psi_{s}\) are the fluxes through the top, bottom, and sides (curved surface) of the cylinder as in Figure 3.18.

For \(\psi _{t},z=1,dS=\rho d\rho d\phi a_{z}.\)Hence,

\(\psi _{t}=\iint{G.dS}=\int\limits_{\rho =0}^{1}{}\int\limits_{\phi =0}^{2\pi }{10e^{-2}\rho d\rho d\phi }=10e^{-2}(2\pi )\frac{\rho ^{2}}{2}|_{0}^{1}\)
\(=10\pi e^{-2}\)

F0r \(\psi _{b},z=0\) and \(dS=\rho d\rho d\phi (-a_{z})\).Hence,

\(\psi _{b}=\int\limits_{b}^{}{G.dS}= \int\limits_{\rho =0}^{1}{}\int\limits_{\phi =0}^{2\pi }{10e^{0}\rho d\rho d\phi }=-10(2\pi )\frac{\rho ^{2}}{2}|_{0}^{1}\)

\(=-10\pi \)

For \(\psi _{s},\rho =1\) and \(dS=\rho dz d\phi a_{\rho }\).Hence,

\(\psi _{s}=\int\limits_{s}^{}{G.dS}= \int\limits_{z =0}^{1}{}\int\limits_{\phi =0}^{2\pi }{10e^{-2z}\rho ^{2}dz d\phi }=10(1)^{2}(2\pi )\frac{e^{-2z}}{-2}|_{0}^{1}\)
\(=10\pi (1-e^{-2})\)

Thus,

\(\psi =\psi_{t}+ \psi_{b}+ \psi_{s}=10\pi e^{-2}-10\pi +10\pi (1-e^{-2})=0\)

Alternatively, since S is a closed surface, we can apply the divergence theorem:

\(\psi =\oint\limits_{s}^{}{G.ds}=\int\limits_{v}^{}{(\triangledown .G)}dv \)

But

\(\triangledown .G=\frac{1}{\rho } \frac{\partial}{\partial \rho }(\rho G_{\rho })+ \frac{1}{\rho } \frac{\partial}{\partial \phi }G_{\phi }+\frac{\partial}{\partial z} G_{z}\)
\(=\frac{1}{\rho } \frac{\partial}{\partial \rho }(\rho ^{2}10e^{-2z})-20e^{-2z}\)
\(=\frac{1}{\rho } (20\rho e^{-2z})-20e^{-2z}=0\)

showing that G has no outward flux. Hence,

\(\psi =\int\limits_{v}^{}{(\triangledown .G)dv}=0\)
