Guest Question.

Consider the reaction:

\(A \rightarrow B , k=0.15 min ^{-1}\)

in a batch reactor. The shifting (loading, preparation, unloading and cleaning of the reactor) requires 10 h for each batch of production. A costs $2/mole, and B sells for $5/mole. The cost of operating the reactor (including the shifting time) is $0.03 per liter per hour. We need to produce 100 moles of B/h using \(C _{ A 0} \) = 2 moles/L. Assume no value or cost of disposal of unreacted A (i.e., separation or recovery cost to A is identical to the fresh A cost).

1. Perform a mole balance on the reactor to relate the conversion with reactor size.

2. What is the optimum conversion and reactor size?

3. What is the cash flow (or profit per mole of A feed to the reactor) from the process?

4. At what operating cost do we break even? What is the corresponding conversion?

Admin
Solution

Let the reaction time be t, and the shifting time (reactor loading, preparation, unloading and cleaning) be \(t_{ s } \). The total time required for each batch is then \(t_{ B }=t+t_{ s }\).

1. Mole balance of A in the reactor leads to

\(\overset{\overset{0}{\nwarrow } }{In} – \overset{\overset{0}{ \nearrow } }{Out}+Generation=Accumulation\)

\(0-0+r_{A} V=\frac{d n_{A}}{d t} \). (E4.5.1)

The rate law gives

\(r_{ A }=v_{ A } r=-k C_{ A }\). (E4.5.2)

It is understood that the reaction mixture density is not changing. We have

\(C_{A}=\frac{n_{A}}{V}=\frac{n_{A 0}\left(1-f_{A}\right)}{V}=C_{A 0}\left(1-f_{A}\right)\). (E4.5.3)

Substituting Eqs. (E4.5.2) \(r_{ A }=v_{ A } r=-k C_{ A }\). and (E4.5.3) \(C_{A}=\frac{n_{A}}{V}=\frac{n_{A 0}\left(1-f_{A}\right)}{V}=C_{A 0}\left(1-f_{A}\right)\). into Eq. (E4.5.1) \(0-0+r_{A} V=\frac{d n_{A}}{d t} \). and integrating with the initial point of \(f_{ A }\) = 0 at t = 0, we obtain

\(t=-\frac{\ln \left(1-f_{A}\right)}{k}\). (E4.5.4)

We know the production rate of B, \(F_{ BO }\) = 100 moles/h. From stoichiometry,

\(F_{A 0}=\frac{F_{B}}{f_{A}} .\) (E4.5.5)

Therefore the reactor volume can be obtained from Eq. (4.24) \(V=t_{ B } Q=\left(t_{ p }+t_{ f }+t_{ U }\right) Q\). as

\(V=t_{B} Q=\left(t_{s}+t\right) \frac{F_{B}}{C_{A 0} f_{A}}=\left[t_{s}-\frac{\ln \left(1-f_{A}\right)}{k}\right] \frac{F_{B}}{C_{A0} f_{A}} .\) (E4.5.6)

2. Based on the economic information at hand, we can estimate the gross profit for the reaction process as

\(GP \$=\text { Value } \text { from } B -\text { Cost of } A -\text { Operating Cost }\)

 

\(GP \$=\$_{ B } F_{ B }-\$_{ A } F_{ A 0}-\$_{ V } V\) (E4.5.7)

where \(\$_{B}\), \(\$_{ A }\) and \(\$_{ V }\) are the molar value of product B, molar cost of reactant A, and the unit operating cost of reactor based on the reactor volume and time; respectively.

Substituting Eqs. (E4.5.5) \(F_{A 0}=\frac{F_{B}}{f_{A}} .\) and (E4.5.6) \(V=t_{B} Q=\left(t_{s}+t\right) \frac{F_{B}}{C_{A 0} f_{A}}=\left[t_{s}-\frac{\ln \left(1-f_{A}\right)}{k}\right] \frac{F_{B}}{C_{A0} f_{A}} .\) into Eq. (E5.4.7) \(GP \$=\$_{ B } F_{ B }-\$_{ A } F_{ A 0}-\$_{ V } V\) , we obtain

\(GP \$=\$_{B} F_{B}-\$_{A} \frac{F_{B}}{f_{A}}-\$_{V}\left[t_{ s }-\frac{\ln \left(1-f_{A}\right)}{k}\right] \frac{F_{B}}{C_{A 0} f_{A}}\). (E4.5.8)

To find the optimum conversion, we maximize the gross profit. That is starting by setting

\(0=\frac{d G P \$}{d f_{A}}\). (E4.5.9)

Since the flow rate of product B is fixed, not a function of the conversion \(f_{ A }\), we have,

\(0=\frac{d G P \$}{d f_{A}}=0+\$_{A} \frac{F_{B}}{f_{A}^{2}}-\$_{V} \frac{1}{k\left(1-f_{A}\right)} \frac{F_{B}}{C_{A 0} f_{A}}+\$_{V}\left[t_{s}-\frac{\ln \left(1-f_{A}\right)}{k}\right] \frac{F_{B}}{C_{A 0} f_{A}^{2}}\), (E4.5.10)

which can be reduced to,

\(0=\frac{\$_{A} k C_{A 0}}{\$_{V}}-\frac{f_{A}}{1-f_{A}}+k t_{s}-\ln \left(1-f_{A}\right)\). (E4.5.11)

\(\frac{\$_{A} k C_{A 0}}{\$_{V}}+k t_{s}=\frac{2(\$ / m o l) \times 0.15\left(\min ^{-1}\right) \times 2(m o l / l)}{0.03 \$ /(I \cdot h)}+0.15\left( min ^{-1}\right) \times 10 h=1290\).

Eq. (E-5.11) \(0=\frac{\$_{A} k C_{A 0}}{\$_{V}}-\frac{f_{A}}{1-f_{A}}+k t_{s}-\ln \left(1-f_{A}\right)\). is reduced to

\(1290=\frac{f_{A}}{1-f_{A}}+\ln \left(1-f_{A}\right) .\) (E4.5.12)

This nonlinear equation can be solved to give

\(f_{ A }=0.9992297.\)

Thus the optimum conversion is \(f_{ A }=0.9992297.\) The reactor size can be computed from Eq. (E4.5.6) \(V=t_{B} Q=\left(t_{s}+t\right) \frac{F_{B}}{C_{A 0} f_{A}}=\left[t_{s}-\frac{\ln \left(1-f_{A}\right)}{k}\right] \frac{F_{B}}{C_{A0} f_{A}} .\)

 

\( V=\left[t_{s}-\frac{\ln \left(1-f_{A}\right)}{k}\right] \frac{F_{B}}{C_{A 0} f_{A}}=\left[10-\frac{\ln (1-0.9992297)}{0.15 \times 60}\right] \frac{100}{2 \times 0.9992297} L \).

 

\(=540.24 L\)

3. The cash flow based on the optimum conversion:

\(\frac{G P \$}{F_{A 0}}=\frac{\$_{B} F_{B}}{F_{A 0}}-\$_{A} \frac{F_{B}}{F_{A 0} f_{A}}-\$_{V}\left[t_{s}-\frac{\ln \left(1-f_{A}\right)}{k}\right] \frac{F_{B}}{F_{A 0} C_{A 0} f_{A}}\)

 

\(=\$_{B} f_{A}-\$_{A}-\left[t_{s}-\frac{\ln \left(1-f_{A}\right)}{k}\right] \frac{\$_{V}}{C_{A 0}}\)

 

\(=5 \times 0.9992297-2-\left[10-\frac{\ln (1-0.9992297)}{0.15 \times 60}\right] \frac{0.03}{2} \$ / mol – A\),

 

\(\frac{G P \$}{F_{A 0}}=\$ 2.8342 / mol – A .\)

4. At breaking even point, the net profit (or in this case the gross profit) is zero. Therefore, at the break-even point, operating cost and the value of product are equal.

The value of product is,

\(\$_{B} F_{B}=5 \times 100 \$ / h=500 \$ / h\),

which is also the operating cost at break-even.

The operating conditions for the break-even point can be obtained by setting the gross profit to zero.

\(0= GP \$=\$_{B} F_{B}-\$_{A} \frac{F_{B}}{f_{A}}-\$_{V}\left[t_{s}-\frac{\ln \left(1-f_{A}\right)}{k}\right] \frac{F_{B}}{C_{A 0} f_{A}}\) , (E4.5.13)

which can be rearranged to

\(f_{A}-\frac{\$_{A}}{\$_{B}}-\frac{\$_{V}}{\$_{B}}\left[t_{s}-\frac{\ln \left(1-f_{A}\right)}{k}\right] \frac{1}{C_{A 0}}=0 \) (E4.5.14)

or

\(f_{A}-0.43-\frac{0.001}{3} \ln \left(1-f_{A}\right)=0\). (E4.5.15)

Solving Eq. (E4.5.15) \(f_{A}-0.43-\frac{0.001}{3} \ln \left(1-f_{A}\right)=0\). , we obtain

\(f_{ A }=0.430187\).
