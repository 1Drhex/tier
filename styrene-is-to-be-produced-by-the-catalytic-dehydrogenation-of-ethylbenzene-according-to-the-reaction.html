Guest Question.

Styrene is to be produced by the catalytic dehydrogenation of ethylbenzene according to the reaction:

\(C_{6}H_{5}.CH_{2}.CH_{3}\rightleftharpoons C_{6}H_{5}.CH:CH2+H_{2}\)

The rate equation for this reaction takes the form:

\(R=K\left ( P_{Et}-\frac{1}{K_{p}}P_{St}P_{H} \right )\)

where \(P_{Et},P_{St}\) and \(P_{H}\) are partial pressures of ethylbenzene, styrene and hydrogen respectively.
The reactor will consist of a number of tubes each of 80 mm diameter packed with catalyst with a bulk density of 1440 kg/\(m^{3}\) .The ethylbenzene will be diluted with steam, the feed rates are ethylbenzene 1.6 × \(10^{-3}\) kmol/\(m^{2}\)s and steam 29 × \(10^{-3}\) kmol/\(m^{2}\)s. The reactor will be operated at a mean pressure of 120 kN/\(m^{2}\) (1.2 bar) and the temperature will be maintained at 833 K (\(560^{\circ}C\)) throughout. If the fractional conversion of ethylbenzene is to be 0.45, estimate the length and number of tubes required to produce 0.231 kg/s (20 tonne styrene/day).

At 833 K, k = 6.6 × \(10^{-11}\) kmol/(N/\(m^{2}\)) s kg catalyst

(= 6.6 × \(10^{-6}\) kmol (kg catalyst bar s) and \(K_{p}\) = 1.0 × \(10^{4}\) N/\(m^{2}\).

Admin
Solution

The fractional conversion of ethylbenzene is 0.45 or 1 kmol of ethylbenzene feed produces 0.45 kmol styrene. Taking the molecular mass of styrene as 104 kg/kmol, then:

1 kmol ethylbenzene feed produces (0.45 × 104) = 46.8 kg styrene.

Hence, the feed rate of ethylbenzene required = 1 × (0.231/46.8)

= 0.00495 kmol/s.

The feed rate of ethylbenzene per unit cross-sectional area = 1.6 × \(10^{-3}\) kmol/\(m^{2}\) s

Thus:

cross-sectional area of all the tubes in the reactor = (0.00495/0.0016) = 3.09 \(m^{2}\)

cross-sectional area of one tube =\((\pi \times 0.080^{2})/4\) = 0.00503 \(m^{2}\)

and: number of tubes required = (3.09/0.00503) = 615

In estimating the length of the tubes, the mass of catalyst, W, is calculated from the design equation for a tubular reactor as:

\(\frac{W}{F_{A}}=\int_{0}^{\alpha _{f}}\frac{d\alpha }{R_{A}^{m}}\) (equation 1.35)

although, as a first stage, the rate of reaction, \(R_{A}^{m}\) , must be expressed in terms of the fractional conversion, \(\alpha \).

Basis: 1 kmol ethylbenzene entering the reactor at a total pressure of P bar.

In (kmol) (kmol) Out (mole fraction) (partial pressure)
ethylbenzene 1 1-\(\alpha \) \(\frac{(1-\alpha)}{(19.1+\alpha )} \) \(\frac{(1-\alpha )P}{(19.1+\alpha )}\)
styrene __ \(\alpha \) \(\alpha /(19.1+\alpha )\) \(\alpha P/(19.1+\alpha )\)
hydrogen __ \(\alpha \) \(\alpha /(19.1+\alpha )\) \(\alpha P/(19.1+\alpha )\)
steam 18.1 18.1 \(18.1/(19.1+\alpha )\) \(18.1P/(19.1+\alpha )\)
TOTAL 19.1 19.1+\(\alpha \) 1 P

Thus: \(R^{m}=\mathbf{K\left [ \frac{(1-\alpha )P}{(19.1+\alpha )}-\frac{\alpha ^{2}}{(19.1+\alpha )^{2}} \frac{P^{2}}{K_{p}}\right ]}\)

Noting that P = 1.2 bar and \(K_{p}\) = 0.1 bar, then:

\(R^{m}=(1.2K/(19.1+\alpha )^{2})(19.1-18.1\alpha -13\alpha ^{2})\)

In equation 1.35:

\(\mathbf{\frac{W}{F_{A}}=\frac{1}{1.2K}\int_{0}^{\alpha _{f}}\frac{(19.1+\alpha )^{2}}{(19.1-18.1\alpha -13\alpha ^{2})}d\alpha} \)

where \(\alpha _{f} \) = 0.45.

Thus: \(\mathbf{\frac{W}{F_{A}}=\frac{1}{1.2K}\int_{0}^{\alpha _{f}}\left [ -0.0769+\frac{28.18+2.832\alpha }{1.469-1.392\alpha -\alpha ^{2}} \right ].d\alpha }\)

\(=\mathbf{\frac{1}{1.2K}\int_{0}^{\alpha _{f}}\left [ -0.0769+\frac{10.79}{(0.702-\alpha )} +\frac{7.96}{(2.094+\alpha )}\right ].d\alpha }\) \(=\frac{1}{1.2K}[-0.0769\alpha -10.79\ln (0.702-\alpha )+7.96\ln (2.094+\alpha )]_{0}^{0.45}\)

= 12.57/1.2k

Noting that \(F_{A}\) = 0.00495 kmol/s and k = 6.6 × \(10^{-6}\) kmol/kg catalyst bar s, then:

(W/0.00495) = 12.57/(1.2 × 6.6 × \(10^{-6}\))

and: W = 7856 kg

Total volume of catalyst required = (7856/1440) = 5.46 \(m^{3}\)

Volume of catalyst in each tube = (5.46/615) = 0.00887 \(m^{3}\)

Thus: Length of each tube = 0.00887/\([(\pi \times 0.080^{2})/4]\)= 1.76 m
