Guest Question.

Develop the nonlinear finite element model of the Timoshenko beam theory. use \(-(d / d x)(b d \Psi / d x)+G A k(d w / d x+\Psi)\) and add the following additional equation for w:
\(-\frac{d}{d x}\left[G A k\left(\frac{d w}{d x}+\Psi\right)\right]=q\)

Admin
Solution

The equations of equilibrium of the Timoshenko beam theory for the nonlinear case are

\(-\frac{d}{d x}\left\{A_{x x}\left[\frac{d u}{d x}+\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{d w}{d x}\right)^{2}\right]\right\}=f\) (a)

 

\(-\frac{d}{d x}\left[S_{x x}\left(\frac{d w}{d x}+\Psi\right)\right]\)

 

\(-\frac{d}{d x}\left\{A_{x x} \frac{d w}{d x}\left[\frac{d u}{d x}+\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{d w}{d x}\right)^{2}\right]\right\}=q\) (b)

 

\(-\frac{d}{d x}\left(D_{x x} \frac{d \Psi}{d x}\right)+S_{x x}\left(\frac{d w}{d x}+\Psi\right)=0\) (c)

where \(A_{x x}=E A, S_{x x}=K_{s} G A \text { and } D_{x x}=E I\) .

The weaks forms of the three equations are

 

\(0=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\left\{A_{x x} \frac{d \delta u}{d x}\left[\frac{d u}{d x}+\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{d w}{d x}\right)^{2}\right]+f \delta u_{0}\right\} d x\)

 

\(-Q_{1}^{e} \delta u\left(x_{a}\right)-Q_{4}^{e} \delta u\left(x_{b}\right)\) (d)

 

\(0=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}} \frac{d \delta w}{d x}\left\{S_{x x}^{e}\left(\frac{d w}{d x}+\Psi\right)+A_{x x}^{e} \frac{d w}{d x}\left[\frac{d u}{d x}+\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{d w}{d x}\right)^{2}\right] \delta w q\right\} d x\)

 

\(-Q_{2}^{e} \delta w\left(x_{a}\right)-Q_{5}^{e} \delta w\left(x_{b}\right)\) (e)

 

\(0=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\left[D_{x x}^{e} \frac{d \delta \Psi}{d x} \frac{d \Psi}{d x}+S_{x x}^{e} \delta \Psi\left(\frac{d w}{d x}+\Psi\right)\right] d x\)

 

\(-Q_{3}^{e} \delta \Psi\left(x_{a}\right)-Q_{6}^{e} \delta \Psi\left(x_{b}\right)\) (f)

where \(\delta u, \delta w, \text { and } \delta \Psi\) are the virtual displacements. The \(Q_{i}^{e}\) have the same physical meaning as in the Euler—Bernoulli beam element, and their relationship to the horizontal displacement u, transverse deflection \(w_{0}\) , and rotation \(\Psi \), is

\(Q_{1}^{e}=-N_{x x}\left(x_{a}\right), \quad Q_{4}^{e}=N_{x x}\left(x_{b}\right)\)

 

\(Q_{2}^{e}=-\left[Q_{x}+N_{x x} \frac{d w}{d x}\right]_{x=x_{a}}, \quad Q_{5}^{e}=\left[Q_{x}+N_{x x} \frac{d w}{d x}\right]_{x=x_{b}}\)

 

\(Q_{3}^{e}=-M_{x x}\left(x_{a}\right), \quad Q_{6}^{e}=M_{x x}\left(x_{b}\right)\) (g)

Suppose that the displacements are approximated as

\(u(x)=\sum_{j=1}^{m} u_{j}^{e} \psi_{j}^{(1)}, \quad w(x)=\sum_{j=1}^{n} w_{j}^{e} \psi_{j}^{(2)}, \Psi(x)=\sum_{j=1}^{p} s_{j}^{e} \psi_{j}^{(3)}\) (h)

where \(\psi_{j}^{(\alpha)}(x)(\alpha=1,2,3)\) are Lagrange interpolation functions of degree (m − 1),(n − 1), and (p − 1), respectively. At the moment, the values of m, n, and p arearbitrary, that is, arbitrary degree of polynomial approximations of \(u_{0}, w_{0}, \text { and } \Psi\) may be used. Substitution of (h) for u, w, and \(\Psi \) \(\Psi \) \(\delta u=\psi_{i}^{(1)}, \delta w=\psi_{i}^{(2)}\) , and \(\delta \Psi=\psi_{i}^{(3)}\) into Eqs. (d)—(f) yields the finite element model

 

\(0=\sum_{j=1}^{m} K_{i j}^{11} u_{j}^{e}+\sum_{j=1}^{n} K_{i j}^{12} w_{j}^{e}+\sum_{j=1}^{p} K_{i j}^{13} s_{j}^{e}-F_{i}^{1}\) (i)

 

\(0=\sum_{j=1}^{m} K_{i j}^{21} u_{j}^{e}+\sum_{j=1}^{n} K_{i j}^{22} w_{j}^{e}+\sum_{j=1}^{p} K_{i j}^{23} s_{j}^{e}-F_{i}^{2}\) (j)

 

\(0=\sum_{j=1}^{m} K_{i j}^{31} u_{j}^{e}+\sum_{j=1}^{n} K_{i j}^{32} w_{j}^{e}+\sum_{j=1}^{p} K_{i j}^{33} s_{j}^{e}-F_{i}^{3}\) (k)

where

 

\(K_{i j}^{11}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}} A_{x x} \frac{d \psi_{i}^{(1)}}{d x} \frac{d \psi_{j}^{(1)}}{d x} d x, K_{i j}^{12}=\frac{1}{2} \int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}} A_{x x} \frac{d w_{0}}{d x} \frac{d \psi_{i}^{(1)}}{d x} \frac{d \psi_{j}^{(2)}}{d x} d x\)

 

\(K_{i j}^{21}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}} A_{x x} \frac{d w_{0}}{d x} \frac{d \psi_{i}^{(2)}}{d x} \frac{d \psi_{j}^{(1)}}{d x} d x, \quad K_{i j}^{13}=0, \quad K_{i j}^{31}=0\)

 

\(K_{i j}^{22}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}} S_{x x} \frac{d \psi_{i}^{(2)}}{d x} \frac{d \psi_{j}^{(2)}}{d x} d x+\frac{1}{2} \int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}} A_{x x}\left(\frac{d w_{0}}{d x}\right)^{2} \frac{d \psi_{i}^{(2)}}{d x} \frac{d \psi_{j}^{(2)}}{d x} d x\)

 

\(K_{i j}^{23}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}} S_{x x} \frac{d \psi_{i}^{(2)}}{d x} \psi_{j}^{(3)} d x=K_{j i}^{32}\)

 

\(K_{i j}^{33}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\left(D_{x x} \frac{d \psi_{i}^{(3)}}{d x} \frac{d \psi_{j}^{(3)}}{d x}+S_{x x} \psi_{i}^{(3)} \psi_{j}^{(3)}\right) d x\)

 

\(F_{i}^{1}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}} \psi_{i}^{(1)} f d x+Q_{1}^{e} \psi_{i}^{(1)}\left(x_{a}\right)+Q_{4}^{e} \psi_{i}^{(1)}\left(x_{b}\right)\)

 

\(F_{i}^{2}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}} \psi_{i}^{(2)} q d x+Q_{2}^{e} \psi_{i}^{(2)}\left(x_{a}\right)+Q_{5}^{e} \psi_{i}^{(2)}\left(x_{b}\right)\)

 

\(F_{i}^{3}=Q_{3}^{e} \psi_{i}^{(3)}\left(x_{a}\right)+Q_{6}^{e} \psi_{i}^{(3)}\left(x_{b}\right)\) (l)

The element equations (i)—(k) can be expressed in matrix form as

\(\left[\begin{array}{lll}{\left[K^{11}\right]} & {\left[K^{12}\right]} & {\left[K^{13}\right]} \\{\left[K^{21}\right]} & {\left[K^{22}\right]} & {\left[K^{23}\right]} \\{\left[K^{31}\right]} & {\left[K^{32}\right]} & {\left[K^{33}\right]}\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}\{u\} \\\{w\} \\\{s\}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{l}\left\{F^{1}\right\} \\\left\{F^{2}\right\} \\\left\{F^{3}\right\}\end{array}\right\}\) (m)

The choice of the approximation functions \(\psi_{i}^{(\alpha)}\) dictates different finite element models. The choice of linear polynomials \(\psi_{i}^{(1)}=\psi_{i}^{(2)}\) is known to yield a stiffness matrix that is nearly singular. This will be discussed further in the next section. When \(\psi_{i}^{(1)}\) are quadratic and \(\psi_{i}^{(2)}\) are linear, the stiffness matrix is 5 × 5. It is possible to eliminate the interior degree of freedom for \(w_{0}\) and obtain 4 × 4 stiffness matrix. This element behaves well. When \(\psi_{i}^{(1)}\) are cubic and \(\psi_{i}^{(2)}\) are quadratic, the stiffness matrix is 7 × 7. If the interior nodal degrees of freedom are eliminated, one obtains 4 × 4 stiffness matrix that is known to yield the exact solution at the nodes in the linear case when the shear stiffness and bending stiffnesses are element-wise constant.
