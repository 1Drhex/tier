Guest Question.

Determine the constant-volume heat capacity of aluminum given the following data: At 20° C, aluminum has the following properties:

\(c_p=24.36\) J / mole.K

 

\(\alpha =7.05\times 10^{-5}K^{-1} \)

 

\(\beta _T=1.20\times 10^{-6}atm^{-1}\)

 

density, \(\rho =2.70 g/cm^3\)

Admin
Solution

The atomic weight of aluminum is 26.98, and thus, at 20° C, the molar volume of aluminum, V , is

\(V=\frac{26.98}{2.70\times 1000} =0.010\) liters/mole

and thus, the difference between \(c_p\) and \(c_v\) is (see Qualitative Problem 1):

\(c_p-c_v=\frac{VT\alpha ^2}{\beta _T} \)

 

\(\frac{0.010\times 293\times (7.05\times 10^{-5} )^2}{1.20\times 10^{-6} }=0.0121 \) liter.atm/mole.K

 

\(0.0121\times \frac{8.3144}{0.08206} =1.23 \) J/mole.K

The constant-volume molar heat capacity of aluminum at 20° C is thus

24.36 – 1.23 = 23.13 J/mole.K
