Guest Question.

Design a Southern Pine No. 1 beam to carry the loads shown (roof beam, no plaster). Assume the beam is supported at each end by an eight inch block wall.

\(F_{ b }\)= 1,550 psi; \(F_{v }\)= 110 psi; E = 1.6 × \(10^{6}\) psi

Admin
Solution

Bending

\(S_{\text {required }}=\frac{M_{\max }}{F_{ b }}=\frac{12.8 k – ft . \times 12 in. / ft. }{1.55 ksi }=99.1 in .^{3}\)

Shear

\(A_{\text {required }}=\frac{1.5 V_{\max }}{F_{ v }}=\frac{1.5 \times(2,750 \#)}{110 psi }=37.5 in .^2\)

Deflection (Allowable)

\(Δ_{\text {allow }}=\frac{L}{240}=\frac{15^{\prime} \times 12 \text { in. } / ft }{240}=0.75^{\prime \prime}\)

Try 6 × 12 S4S.

(A = 63.3 in.²; \(S_{x}\) = 121 in.³; \(I_{x}=697 \text { in. }^{4}\) )

 

\(ω_{\text {beam }} \approx\) 0.252 × 63.3 = 16 #/ft.

Bending

\(M_{\text {add. }}=\frac{\omega_{\text {beam }} L^{2}}{8}=\frac{16 \# / ft \cdot\left(15^{\prime}\right)^{2}}{8}=450 \#- ft.\)

 

\(S_{\text {add. }}=\frac{M_{\text {add. }}}{F_{ b }}=\frac{450 \# \text { -ft. } \times 12 in . / ft. }{1,550 psi }=3.5 in .^{3}\)

 

\(S_{\text {total }}=99.1 in .^{3}+3.5 in .^{3}=102.5 in .{ }^{3}<121 in .^{3} \therefore OK \)

Note: \(S_{\text {add. }} \) is usually approximately 2% to 5% of \(S_{\text {required }}\).

Shear

\(V_{\text {add. }}=\frac{ω_{\text {beam }} L}{2}=\frac{16 \# / \text { ft. }\left(15^{\prime}\right)}{2}=120 \#\)

 

\(A_{\text {add. }}=\frac{1.5 V_{\text {add. }}}{F_{ v }}=\frac{1.5 \times 120 \#}{110 psi }=1.6 in .{ }^{2}\)

 

\(A_{\text {total }}\)= 37.5 in.² + 1.6 in.² = 39.1 in.² \(<\) 63.3 in.² \(\therefore OK\)

 

Actual Deflection

Using superposition (the combination, or superimposing, of one load condition onto another),

\(\Delta_{\text {actual }}=\frac{5 \omega_{ LL } L^{4}}{384 E I}+\frac{23 P L^{3}}{648 EI }(\text { @ centerline })\)

 

\(\therefore \Delta_{\text {actual }}=\frac{5(100+16)\left(15^{\prime}\right)^{4}(1,728)}{384\left(1.6 \times 10^{6}\right)\left(697 in .^{4}\right)}+\frac{23(2,000 \#)\left(15^{\prime}\right)^{3}(1,728)}{684\left(1.6 \times 10^{6}\right)\left(697 in .^{4}\right)}\)

 

\(\Delta_{\text {actual }}\)= 0.12″ + 0.35″ = 0.47″ \(<\) 0.75″ \(\therefore OK\)

Check the bearing stress between the beam and the block wall support.

\(f_{p}=\frac{P}{A_{\text {bearing }}}=\frac{2,870 \#}{44 in.^{2}}=65.2 psi\)

The allowable bearing stress perpendicular to the grain for Southern Pine No. 1 is:

\(F_{ c \perp}=440 psi \therefore OK\)

Use 6 × 12 S4S.
