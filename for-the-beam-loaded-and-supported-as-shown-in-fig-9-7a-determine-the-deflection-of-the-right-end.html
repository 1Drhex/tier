Guest Question.

For the beam loaded and supported as shown in Fig. 9-7a, determine the deflection of the right end.

Admin
Solution

From the free-body diagram of Fig. 9-7b, the equations of equilibrium give the support reactions

\(+\uparrow \sum{F_y}=0: \quad\quad V_A-w(L/3)=0 \quad\quad V_A=wL/3\uparrow \)

 

\(+\circlearrowleft \sum{M_A}=0: \quad\quad M_A-(5L/6)[w(L/3)]=0 \quad\quad M_A=5wL^2/18\circlearrowleft \)

Therefore, with the sign conventions established in Section 8-6, the shear force and bending moment at x = 0 are

\(V(0)=+\frac{wL}{3} \quad \quad and \quad\quad M(0)=-\frac{5wL^2}{18} \)

In Fig. 9-7b there are two intervals to be considered, namely, the loaded portion of the beam and the unloaded portion of the beam. The loaded portion of the beam must be used because it contains the point where the deflection is required. However, a quick check reveals the absence of boundary conditions in this interval. It therefore becomes necessary to use both intervals as well as matching and boundary conditions; hence, the origin of coordinates is selected at the left end of the beam, as shown. The intervals are

\(0\leq x\leq 2L/3 \quad\quad and \quad\quad 2L/3\leq x\leq L\)

The available boundary conditions are

\(\frac{dy}{dx}=0 \quad\quad when \quad\quad x=0\)

 

\(y=0 \quad\quad when \quad\quad x=0\)

The available matching conditions are when x = 2L/3

\(\frac{dy}{dx}\) from the left equation \(=\frac{dy}{dx}\) from the right equation
y from the left equation = y from the right equation
where the left equation is for the interval 0 ≤ x ≤ 2L/3 and the right equation is for the interval 2L/3 ≤ x ≤ L. Four conditions (two boundary and two matching) are sufficient for the evaluation of the four constants of integration (two in each of the two elastic curve differential equations); therefore, the problem can be solved in the following manner.
From the free-body diagram of Fig. 9-7c where the beam is sectioned in the unloaded interval, \(\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=\frac{d\theta }{dx}=\frac{1}{\rho }=\frac{M}{EI} \quad\quad or \quad\quad EI\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=M \) yields for 0 ≤ x ≤ 2L/3

\(EI\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=M(x)=\frac{wL}{3}x-\frac{5wL^2}{18} \) (a)

From the free-body diagram of Fig. 9-7d where the beam is sectioned in the loaded interval, \(\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=\frac{d\theta }{dx}=\frac{1}{\rho }=\frac{M}{EI} \quad\quad or \quad\quad EI\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=M \) yields for 2L/3 ≤ x ≤ L

\(EI\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=M(x)=\frac{wL}{3}x-\frac{5wL^2}{18}-w(x-\frac{2L}{3} )(\frac{x-2L/3}{2} )\) (b)

Integration of Equations (a) and (b) gives

\(EI\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{wL}{6}x^2-\frac{5wL^2}{18}x+C_1 \quad\quad 0\leq x\leq 2L/3\) (c)

 

\(EI\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{wL}{6}x^2-\frac{5wL^2}{18}x-\frac{w}{6}(x-\frac{2L}{3} )^3 +C_3 \quad\quad 2L/3\leq x\leq L\) (d)

Substitution of the boundary condition \(\frac{dy}{dx}= 0\) when x = 0 into Eq. (c) gives

\(C_1=0\)

Since the beam has a continuous slope at x = 2L/3
\(\frac{dy}{dx}\) [from Eq. (c) at x = 2L/3] \(= \frac{dy}{dx}\) [from Eq. (d) at x = 2L/3]
which gives
\(C_3 = 0\)
Integration of the resulting differential equations gives

\(EIy=\frac{wL}{18}x^3 -\frac{5wL^2}{36}x^2+C_2 \quad\quad 0\leq x\leq 2L/3\)

 

\(EIy=\frac{wL}{18}x^3 -\frac{5wL^2}{36}x^2-\frac{w}{24}(x-\frac{2L}{3} )^4 +C_4 \quad\quad 2L/3\leq x\leq L \)

Use of the remaining boundary condition yields \(C_2 = 0\). Use of the final matching condition yields \(C_4 = 0\).
The deflection of the right end of the beam can now be obtained from the elastic curve equation for the right interval by replacing x by its value L. The result is

\(EIy=\frac{wL}{18}(L^3) -\frac{5wL^2}{36}(L^2)-\frac{w}{24}(L-\frac{2L}{3} )^4 \)

from which

\(y=-\frac{163}{1944}\frac{wL^4}{EI}=\frac{163}{1944}\frac{wL^4}{EI}\downarrow \)
