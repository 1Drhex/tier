Guest Question.

Consider the circuit of Fig. 3.2a with R=200 Ω, L=2 mH, C=0.01 μF, and \(\ V_{dc} = 20 V \) . Derive the expressions for \(\ i_{L} \left(t\right) and v_{C} \left(t\right) \) for t>0. Assume the initial inductor current and the initial capacitor voltage are zeros, i.e., \(i_{L} \left(0^{-} \right) =0 and v_{C} \left(0^{-} \right) =0 \) .

Admin
Solution

Here we have \(\ R/ L =100 \times 10^{3} rad/ s and 1/ LC = 50\times 10^{9} rad^{2} / s^{2} . since \left(R/2L\right) ^{2} \lt 1/ LC \) , the circuit is underdamped; hence the roots of the characteristic equation are \(\ s_{1} = \left(-50 \times 10^{3} -j218 \times 10^{3} \right) rad/ s and s_{2} = \left(-50 \times 10^{3} -j218 \times 10^{3} \right) rad/ s \) . The general solution for the inductor current is given by:

\(\ i_{L} \left(t\right) =e^{-50 \times 10^{3}t} \left(A_{1} \cos \left(218\times 10^{3} t \right) + A_{2} \sin \left(218\times 10^{3} t \right) \right)ِِِA \) (3.12)

The constants \(\ A_{1} and A_{2} \) can be obtained from Table 3.1. However, for illustration purposes we will show how to ﬁnd \(\ A_{1} and A_{2} \) .

Table 3.1 Three possible cases and their response constants for the series resonant RLC circuit
Circuit type \(\ A_{1} \) \(\ A_{2} \)
Case I: critically damped \(\ i_{L} \left(0^{-}\right) \) \(\ \frac{V_{dc} -v_{C} \left(0^{-}\right) }{L} +\frac{R}{2L} i_{L} \left(0^{-}\right) \)
Case II: overdamped \(\ \frac{V_{dc}-v_{C} \left(0^{-}\right) }{L \left(s_{1} – s_{2}\right) } +\frac{s_{1} i_{L} (0^{-})}{\left(s_{1} – s_{2}\right)} \) \(\ \frac{V_{dc} -v_{C} \left(0^{-}\right) }{L\left(s_{1} – s_{2}\right)} +\frac{s_{2} i_{L}\left(0^{-}\right) }{s_{1} – s_{2}} \)
Case III: underdamped \(\ i_{L} \left(0^{-}\right) \) \(\ \frac{V_{dc} -v_{C} \left(0^{-}\right) }{\omega _{d} L} -\frac{R}{2\omega _{d} L} i_{L} \left(0^{-}\right) \)
\(\ s_{1} = -\frac{R}{2L} +\sqrt{\left(\frac{R}{2L}\right) ^{2} -\frac{1}{LC^{,} } } s_{2} = -\frac{R}{2L}+\sqrt{\left(\frac{R}{2L}\right) ^{2} -\frac{1}{LC^{,} } } \omega _{d} =\sqrt{\omega ^{2}_{0} -\alpha ^{2} } , \alpha =\frac{R}{2L^{,} } \omega _{0} =\sqrt{\frac{1}{LC} } \)

 

Since \(\ i_{L} \left(0^{+} \right) =i_{L} \left(0^{-} \right) =0 \) , then \(\ A_{1} =0 \) . To solve for\(\ A_{2} \) , we use the capacitor initial condition. Applying KVL to the circuit, we obtain:

\(\ -V_{dc} +L\frac{di_{L}}{dt} +v_{c} +Ri_{L} =0 \)

Evaluating this equation at \(\ t=0^{+} \) , we obtain the ﬁrst derivative at \(\ t=0^{+} \) :

\(\ \frac{di_{L} \left(0^{+}\right)}{dt} =\frac{V_{dc} v_{c} \left(0^{+}\right) }{L} =10 A/ ms \)

Taking the ﬁrst derivative of Eq. (3.12) and evaluating it at \(\ t=0^{+} \) and setting it to 10 A/ms, we obtain the following equation:

\(\ i_{L} \left(t\right) =e^{-50 \times 10^{3}t} \left(A_{1} \cos \left(218\times 10^{3} t \right) + A_{2} \sin \left(218\times 10^{3} t \right) A \right) \) (3:12)

\(\ \frac{di_{L} \left(0^{+}\right)}{dt}\mid _{t=0^{+}} = i_{L} \left(t\right) = -50 e^{-50\times 10^{3} } A_{2}\left(sin 218 \times 10^{3} t \right) +218 \times 10^{3}e^{-50 t} A_{2}\left(\cos 218 t\right) {\mid _{t=0} } = 10 A/ ms \)

Solving for \(\ A_{2}, we obtain A_{2} \) =0.046.

The inductor current and capacitor voltage expressions for t>0 are given below:

\(\ i_{L} \left(t\right) =0.046 e^{-50\times 10^{3} } \sin \left(218\times 10^{3} t \right) A \)

 

\(\ v_{C} \left(t\right) =V_{dc} -Ri_{L} -L frac{di_{L}}{dt} \)

Substituting for \(\ i_{L} \) (t), the capacitor voltage is given by:

\(\ v_{C} \left(t\right) 20 -e^{-50\times 10^{3}} \left(4.6\sin \left(218 \times 10^{3} t \right)- 20.056 \cos \left(218 \times 10^{3} t \right) \right) V \)

The diode switches off when \(\ i_{L} \left(t\right) \)=0 which occurs when \(\ 218 \times 10^{3} t = \pi \) , or\(\ t=\pi / \left(218 \times 10^{3}\right) =14.4 \mu s \) , at which the capacitor voltage equals 29.76 V.
