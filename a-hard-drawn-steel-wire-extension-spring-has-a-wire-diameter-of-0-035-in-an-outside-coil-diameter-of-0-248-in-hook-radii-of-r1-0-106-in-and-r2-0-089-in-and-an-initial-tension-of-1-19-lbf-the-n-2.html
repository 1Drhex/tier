Guest Question.

A hard-drawn steel wire extension spring has a wire diameter of 0.035 in, an outside coil diameter of 0.248 in, hook radii of \(r_1 = 0.106 \ in \ and \ r_2 = 0.089 \ in\), and an initial tension of 1.19 lbf. The number of body turns is 12.17. From the given information:
(a) Determine the physical parameters of the spring.
(b) Check the initial preload stress conditions.
(c) Find the factors of safety under a static 5.25-lbf load.

Admin

(a) \(D=OD-d=0.248-0.035=0.13 \ in \\\\ C=\frac{D}{d} =\frac{0.213}{0.035} =6.086 \\\\ K_B=\frac{4C+2}{4C-3} =1.234\)

\(N_a=N_b+{G}/{E}=12.17+{11.6}/{28.7}=12.57\)

Eq. (10–9): \(k= \frac{d^4 G}{8 D^3 N_a} =\frac{0.035^4\left(11.6\right)10^6 }{8\left(0.213^3\right)12.57 } =17.91 \ lbf/in\)

Eq. (10–39): \(L_0=\left(2C-1+N_b\right) d=\left[2\left(6.086\right)-1+12.17 \right] 0.035=0.817 \ in\)

The deflection under the service load is

\(y_{max}=\frac{F_{max}-F_i}{k} =\frac{5.25-1.19}{19.91} =0.227 \ in \)

where the spring length becomes \(L = L_0 + y = 0.817 + 0.227 = 1.044 \ in\).
(b) The uncorrected initial stress is given by Eq. (10–2)

\(\tau =K_s\frac{8FD}{\pi d^3} \)

without the correction factor.
That is,

\(\left(\tau_i\right) _{uncorr}=\frac{8F_i D}{\pi d^3} =\frac{8\left(1.19\right)0.213\left(10^{-3}\right) }{\pi \left(0.035^3\right) } =15.1 \ kpsi\)

The preferred range is given by Eq. (10–41) and for this case is

\(\left(\tau _i\right) _{pref}=\frac{33500}{exp\left(0.105C\right) } \pm 1000\left(4-\frac{C-3}{6.5} \right) \\ =\frac{33500}{exp\left[0.105\left(6.086\right) \right] } \pm 1000\left(4-\frac{6.086}{6.5} \right) \\ 17681\pm 3525=21.2,14.2 \ kpsi\)

Thus, the initial tension of 15.1 kpsi is in the preferred range.

\(S_{ut}=\frac{A}{d^m} \)

\(S_{ut}=\frac{A}{d^m}=\frac{140}{0.035^{0.190}}=264.7 \ kpsi \)

\(S_{sy}=0.45S_{ut}=0.45\left(264.7\right) =119.1 \ kpsi\)

The shear stress under the service load is
\(\tau_{max}= \frac{8K_B F_{max}D}{\pi d^3}= \frac{8\left(1.234\right)5.25 \left(0.213\right)}{\pi \left(0.035^3\right)}\left(10^{−3}\right) = 82.0 \ kpsi\)
Thus, the factor of safety is
\(n = \frac{S_{sy}}{\tau_{max}}=\frac{119.1}{82.0}= 1.45\)
For the end-hook bending at A,
\(C_1 = {2r_1}/{d} = {2\left(0.106\right)}/{0.0.035} = 6.057\)
From Eq. (10–35)
\(\left(K\right)_A = \frac{4C^2_1-C_1 − 1}{4C_1\left(C1 − 1\right)} \ C_1=\frac{2r_1}{d}\)
\(\left(K\right)_A = \frac{4C^2_1-C_1 − 1}{4C_1\left(C1 − 1\right)}=\frac{4\left(6.057^2\right) − 6.057 − 1}{4\left(6.057\right)\left(6.057 − 1\right)}= 1.14\)
From Eq. (10–34)
\(\sigma _A=F\left[\left(K\right)_A\frac{16D}{\pi d^3}+\frac{4}{\pi d^2} \right] \\\\ =5.25\left[1.14\frac{16\left(0.213\right)}{\pi \left(0.035^3\right) }+\frac{4}{\pi \left(0.035^2\right) } \right] \left(10^{-3}\right) =156.9 \ kpsi\)
The yield strength, from Table 10–7, is given by
\(S_y = 0.75S_{ut} = 0.75\left(264.7\right) = 198.5 \ kpsi\)
The factor of safety for end-hook bending at A is then
\(n_A = \frac{S_y}{\sigma_A}=\frac{198.5}{156.9}= 1.27\)
For the end-hook in torsion at B, from Eq. (10–37)
\(\left(K\right)_B=\frac{4C_2-1}{4C_2-4} \ C_2=\frac{2r_2}{d} \)
\(C_2={2r_2}/{d}= {2\left(0.089\right)}/{0.035} = 5.086 \\\\ \left(K\right)_B=\frac{4C_2-1}{4C_2-4}=]\frac{4\left(5.086\right) − 1}{4\left(5.086\right) − 4}= 1.18\)
and the corresponding stress, given by Eq. (10–36), is
\(\tau_B = \left(K\right)_B \frac{8FD}{\pi d^3}\)
\(\tau_B = \left(K\right)_B \frac{8F_{max}D}{\pi d^3}= 1.18\frac{8\left(5.25\right)0.213}{\pi \left(0.035^3\right)}\left(10^{−3}\right) = 78.4 \ kpsi\)
Using Table 10–7 for yield strength, the factor of safety for end-hook torsion at B is
\(n_B = \frac{\left(S_{sy}\right)_B}{\tau_B}= \frac{0.4\left(264.7\right)}{78.4}= 1.35\)
Yield due to bending of the end hook will occur first.
