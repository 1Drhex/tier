Guest Question.

Copper tubing is used to convey methyl alcohol in a lubricating system. The system consists of 2 m of 3/4 std type M tubing attached in series to 3 m of 1/2 std type M copper tubing, as indicated in Figure 3.12. The pressure drop over the 5 m length is 100 kPa. Determine the flow rate through the system.

Admin
Solution

This type of problem (flow rate Q unknown) is somewhat more involved than the other type (∆p unknown). The procedure is familiar,
however

methyl alcohol \(ρ = 0.789(1 000) kg/m^3\) \(µ = 0.56 \times 10^{-3} N·s/m^2\) [App. Table B.1]
3/4 std type M D = 2.060 cm \(A = 3.333 \times 10^{-4} m^2\)
1/2 std type M D = 1.446 cm \(A= 1.642 \times 10^{-4} m^2\) [App. Table D.2]
drawn tubing ε = 0.000 15 cm [Table 3.1]

The modified Bernoulli equation is

\(\frac{p_1g_c}{ρg} +\frac{V^2_1}{2g} + z_1 = \frac{p_2g_c}{ρg} +\frac{V^2_2}{2g} + z_2 + \left\lgroup \frac{f L}{D} + ∑K\right\rgroup \frac{V^2_1}{2g} +\left\lgroup \frac{f L}{D}+\sum K \right\rgroup \frac{V^2_2}{2g} \)

Evaluating properties, we write

\(p_1 – p_2 = 100 000 N/m^2\) \(z_1 = z_2\)
\(∑K_{3/4 std} = 0 ∑K_{1/2 std} = K_{contraction} + 4K_{90°ells}\)

The pressure drop in the contraction (reducing bushing) can be evaluated after the ratio of diameters is calculated:

\(\frac{D_2}{D_1} = \frac{1.642}{2.060} = 0.702\)

From Table 3.2, the loss equation for a reducing bushing is

\(K = 0.5 – 0.167(D_2/D_1) – 0.125(D_2/D_1)^2 – 0.208(D_2/D_1)^3\)
\(K_{contraction} = 0.5 – 0.167(0.702) – 0.125(0.702)^2 – 0.208(0.702)^3\)
\(K_{contraction} = 0.25\)

so

\(∑K_{1/2 std} = 0.25 + 4(0.31) = 1.490\)

This loss will be based on the downstream kinetic energy of the flow.
Applying the continuity equation, we get

\(A_{1}V_1 = A_2V_2\)
\(V_1 = \frac{A_2}{A_1}V_2 = \frac{1.642}{3.333} V_2\)
\(V_1 = 0.493V_2\) (i)

After rearranging and substituting for \(V_1\), the modified Bernoulli equation becomes

\(\frac{(p_1 – p_2)g_c}{ρg} = \left\lgroup\frac{f L}{D} + ∑K – 1\right\rgroup \frac{V^2_1}{2g}+ \left\lgroup\frac{f L}{D} + ∑K + 1\right\rgroup \frac{V^2_2}{2g}\)
\(\frac{(p_1 – p_2)g_c}{ρg}=\left\lgroup\frac{f L}{D} + ∑K – 1\right\rgroup \frac{0.243V^2_2}{2g}+\left\lgroup\frac{f L}{D} + ∑K + 1\right\rgroup \frac{V^2_2}{2g}\)

Factoring the kinetic energy term gives

\(\frac{(p_1 – p_2)g_c}{ρg} =\frac{V^2_2}{2g} \left\{0.243\frac{f_1 L_1 }{D_1} + ∑K_1-0.243+\frac{f_2L_2}{D_3} + ∑K_2 + 1\right\}\)

Substituting known quantities

\(\frac{30 000}{789(9.81)} = \frac{V^2_2}{2(9.81)} \left\{0.243 \frac{f_1(2)}{0.020 60} + 0 – 0.243 +\frac{ f_2(3) }{0.013 40} + 1.490 + 1\right\}\)

which reduces to

\(253.5 = V^2_2(17.37f_1 + 223.9f_2 + 2.311)\)
or \(V_2 =\left\lgroup\frac{ 253.5}{17.37f_1 + 223.9f_2 + 2.311}\right\rgroup^{1/2}\) (ii)

The Reynolds number for each line is

\(Re_{3/4 std} = \frac{ρVD}{µg_c} =\frac{ 789V_1(0.020 6)}{0.56 \times 10^{-3}}\)
\(Re_{3/4 std} = 2.9 \times 10^4V_1\)

We also calculate

\(\frac{\epsilon}{D} = \frac{0.000 15}{2.060} = 0.000 073\)

Similarly,

\(Re_{1/2 std} = 2.04 \times 10^4V_2\)

and

\(\frac{ε}{D} = 0.000 1\)

1st trial: Assuming the fully turbulent values for each friction factor gives

\(f_1 = 0.011 5\) (3/4 std)
\(f_2 = 0.012\) (1/2 std)

Substituting into Equation ii of this example gives

or \(V_2 =\left\lgroup\frac{253.5}{17.37(0.011 5) + 223.9(0.012) + 2.311}\right\rgroup^{1/2}\)
\(V_2 = 7.1\) m/s

Substituting into Equation i, we get

\(V_1 = 0.493(7.1) = 3.5\) m/s

The Reynolds numbers and friction factors become

\(\left.\begin{matrix}Re_{3/4 std} = 2.9 \times 10^4V_1 = 2.9 \times 10^4 \\ \\ \frac{ε}{D}= 0.000073 \end{matrix}\right\}f_1 = 0.016\)

and,

\(\left.\begin{matrix}Re_{1/2 std} = 2.04 \times 10^4 \\ \\ \frac{ε}{D}= 0.0001 \end{matrix}\right\}f_2= 0.02\)

2nd trial: Using these friction factors, we calculate new velocities:

\(V_2 = 6.11\) m/s and \(V_1 = 3.01\) m

With these new values, we repeat the calculations to get

\(\left.\begin{matrix}Re_{3/4 std} = 2.9 \times 10^4V_1 = 7.28 \times 10^4 \\ \\ \frac{ε}{D}= 0.000073 \end{matrix}\right\}f_1 = 0.0163\)

and,

\(\left.\begin{matrix}Re_{1/2 std} = 6.14 \times 10^4 \\ \\ \frac{ε}{D}= 0.0001 \end{matrix}\right\}f_2= 0.02\)

3rd trial: The velocities are now found as

\(V_2 = 6.11\) m/s and \(V_1 = 3.01\) m/s (close enough)

The volume flow rate then is

\(Q = A_1V_1 = A_2V_2\)
\(Q = 3.333 \times 10^{-4}(3.01)\)
or \(Q = 1.0 \times 10^{-3} m^3/s\)
