Guest Question.

At the beginning of compression in a diesel cycle T = 540 R, P = 30 \(lbf / in .^{2}\) and the state after combustion (heat addition) is 2600 R and 1000 \(lbf / in .^{2}\). Find the compression ratio, the thermal efficiency and the mean effective pressure.

Admin
Solution

Compression process (isentropic) from Eq.6.28 or p295

\(\begin{gathered}P _{2}= P _{3}=1000 lbf / in ^{2} \Rightarrow v _{1} / v _{2}=\left( P _{2} / P _{1}\right)^{1 / k }=(1000 / 30)^{0.7143}= 1 2 . 2 4 \\T _{2}= T _{1}\left( P _{2} / P _{1}\right)^{( k -1) / k }=540(1000 / 30)^{0.2857}=1470.6 R\end{gathered}\)

Expansion process (isentropic) first get the volume ratios

\(\begin{aligned}& v _{3} / v _{2}= T _{3} / T _{2}=2600 / 1470.6=1.768 \\& v _{4} / v _{3}= v _{1} / v _{3}=\left( v _{1} / v _{2}\right)\left( v _{2} / v _{3}\right)=12.24 / 1.768=6.923\end{aligned}\)

The exhaust temperature follows from state 3 and constant s, Eq.6.24

\(\begin{aligned}& T _{4}= T _{3}\left( v _{3} / v _{4}\right)^{ k -1}=2600 ^{* }6.923^{-0.4}=1199 R \\& q _{ L }= C _{ V }\left( T _{4}- T _{1}\right)=0.171(1199-540)=112.7 Btu / lbm \\& q _{ H }= h _{3}- h _{2}= C _{ P }\left( T _{3}- T _{2}\right)=0.24(2600-1470.6)=271.1 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\)

Overall performance

\(\begin{aligned}&\eta=1- q _{ L } / q _{ H }=1-112.7 / 271.1= 0 . 5 8 4 3 \\& w _{\text {net }}= q _{\text {net }}=271.1-112.7=158.4 Btu / lbm \\& v _{\max }= v _{1}= RT _{1} / P _{1}=53.34 \times 540 /(30 \times 144)=6.6675 ft ^{3} / lbm \\& v _{\min }= v _{\max }\left( v _{1} / v _{2}\right)=6.6675 / 12.24=0.545 ft ^{3} / lbm \\& P _{ meff }=\frac{ w _{ net }}{ v _{\max }- v _{\min }}=\frac{158.4}{6.6675-0.545} \times \frac{778}{144}=139.8 lbf / in ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Remark: This is a too low compression ratio for a practical diesel cycle.
