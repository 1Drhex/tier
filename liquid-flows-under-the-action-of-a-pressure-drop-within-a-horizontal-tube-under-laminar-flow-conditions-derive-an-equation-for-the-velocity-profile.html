Guest Question.

Liquid flows under the action of a pressure drop within a horizontal tube. Under laminar-flow conditions, derive an equation for the velocity profile.

Admin
Solution

Figure 5.9 is a sketch of the system, with an expected velocity profile. The pressure drop imposed can be written as Δp/L or, more generally, as will be seen in the equations, ∂p/∂z. The r, θ, and z axes are indicated in the figure.

 

Assumptions

 

1. The flow is laminar.
2. Liquid properties are constant.
3. The liquid is Newtonian.
4. Steady flow exists.
5. Gravitational forces are neglected.

 

As before, we start by writing

 

\(\tilde{V}=\tilde{e_{x}}V_{r}+\tilde{e_{\theta }}V_{\theta }+\tilde{e_{z}}V_{z}\)

 

We conclude that \(V_{r} \,=\, V_{\theta }\, = \,0\), with \(V_{z}\) the only nonzero velocity to consider. Next, we write

 

\(V_{z}=V_{z}(r,\theta ,z,t )\) (i)

 

Based on the assumptions and an analysis of the flow, we get

 

\(V_{z}=V_{z}(r)\)

 

The continuity equation in cylindrical coordinates reduces to

 

\(\frac{\partial V_{z}}{\partial z}=0\)

 

which states that \(V_{z}\) is independent of z. The Navier–Stokes equations 5.14a through 5.14c of Table 5.2 in Section 5.10 become

 

r component \(0=\frac{\partial p}{\partial r}\) (p is not a function of r)

 

θ component \(0=\frac{\partial p}{\partial \theta}\) ( is not a function of θ)

 

z component \(0=-\frac{\partial p}{\partial z}+\frac{\mu}{r}\frac{\partial}{\partial r}\left ( r\frac{\partial V_{z}}{\partial r} \right )\)

 

The boundary conditions are

 

BC1. r = R, \(V_{z} = 0\) (nonslip condition)

 

BC2. r = 0, \(\frac{dV_{z}}{dr}=0\) (velocity is a maximum at the centerline due to symmetry)

 

The solution to the differential equation as found by direct integration is

 

\(V_{z}=\frac{R^{2}}{4\mu }\left ( -\frac{dp}{dz} \right )\left ( 1-\frac{r^{2}}{R^{2}} \right )\) (5.16)

 

The velocity profile is thus parabolic. It is important to note that as z increases, pressure p decreases. Therefore the pressure gradient (−dp/dz) is actually a positive quantity.

 

\(\frac{\rho }{g_{c}}\left ( \frac{\partial V_{x}}{\partial t}+V_{x}\frac{\partial V_{x}}{\partial x}+V_{y}\frac{\partial V_{x}}{\partial y}+V_{z}\frac{\partial V_{x}}{\partial z} \right )=-\frac{\partial \rho }{\partial x}+\mu \left ( \frac{\partial ^{2}V_{x}}{\partial x^{2}}+\frac{\partial ^{2}V_{x}}{\partial y^{2}}\frac{\partial ^{2}V_{x}}{\partial z^{2}} \right )+\frac{\rho g_{x} }{g_{c}}\) (5.14a)
