Guest Question.

During trial of single cylinder, double acting steam engine of condensing type following observations were made;
Bore: 24 cm, Stroke: 34 cm; Engine speed: 150 rpm, Piston rod diameter: 5cm, Brake load: 120kg. Spring balance reading: 100N.
Brake wheel drum diameter: 100cm;
Steam inlet state: 15 bar, 0.98 dry,
Mean effective pressure at cover end: 1.8 bar
Mean effective pressure at crank end: 1.6 bar
Cooling water flow through condenser: 42 kg/min
Rise in temperature of cooling water: 20°C
Condensate discharged from condensor: 4 kg/min
Temperature of condensate: 50°C
Determine brake power, indicated power, mechanical efficiency, brake thermal efficiency, indicated steam consumption and energy balance sheet on minute basis.

Admin
Solution
\(\begin{aligned}\text{Brake power}&= \text{2πN(Brake load – Spring balance reading) × Drum radius}\\\text{Brake power}&= \frac{2π × 150(120 × 9.81 – 100) × (100/2) × 10^{2}}{1000 × 60}\\&= 8.46 kW\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Indicated power, cover end}&= \text{MEP}_{\text{cover}} × L × A_{\text{cover}} × N\\&= 1.8 × 10^{2} × 0.34 × \frac{\pi}{4} × (0.24)^{4} × \frac{150}{60}\\&= 6.92 kW\\\text{Indicated power, crank end}&= \text{MEP}_{\text{crank}} × L × A_{\text{crank}} × N\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}&= 1.6 × 10^{2} × 0.34 × \frac{\pi}{4} × \left\{(0.24)^{2} – (0.05)^{2}\right\} × \frac{150}{60}\\&= 5.89 kW\\\text{Total indicated power}&= 6.92 + 5.89 = 12.81 kW\\\text{Mechanical efficiency}&= \frac{\text{Brake power}}{\text{Indicated power}} = \frac{8.46}{12.81} = \text{0.6604 or 66.04}\%\\\text{Enthalpy of steam at inlet, }\qquad h_{s,in}&= h_{\text{f at 15 bar}} + 0.98 . h_{\text{fg}} \text{ at 15 bar}\\&= 844.89 + (0.98 × 1947.3)\\h_{s,in}&= 2753.24 kJ/kg\\\text{Enthalpy of condensate, }\quad\qquad h_{cond}&= h_{\text{f at 50°C}} = 209.33 kJ/kg\\\text{Energy supplied by steam}&= h_{s,in} – h_{cond}\\&= 2753.24 – 209.33 = 2543.91 kJ/kg\\\text{Steam consumption rate}&= 4 kg/min = 240 kg/hr\\\text{Brake thermal efficiency}&= \frac{3600}{\frac{m_{steam}}{BP} × (h_{s,in} – h_{cond})} = \frac{3600 × 8.46}{240 × 2543.91}\\&= \text{0.0498 or 4.98}\%\\\text{Indicated steam consumption}&= \frac{m_{steam}}{IP} = \frac{240}{12.81}\\&= 18.74 kg/kWh\\\text{Energy balance sheet on per minute b}&\text{asis.}\\\text{Energy supplied per minute, }\qquad Q_{in}&= 4(h_{s,in} – h_{cond})\\&= 4 × 2543.91 = 10175.64 kJ/min\\\text{Energy consumed by brake or availab}&\text{le at shaft}\\Q_{1}&= 8.46 × 60 = 507.6 kJ/min\\\text{Energy consumed by cooling water in}&\text{ condenser,}\\Q_{2}&= m_{water} × C_{\text{p,water}} × ΔT\\&= 42 × 4.18 × 20 = 3511.2 kJ/min\\\text{Energy going alongwith condensate, }&\\Q_{3}&= m_{steam }× C_{p} × ΔT\\&= 4 × 4.18 × 50 = 836 kJ/min\\\text{Engery loss to surroundings, }\qquad Q_{4}&= Q_{in} – (Q_{1} + Q_{2} + Q_{3})\\&= 10175.64 – (507.6 + 3511.2 + 836) = 5320.84 kJ/min\\\end{aligned}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \text{Energy Balance sheet on per minute basis}\)
