Guest Question.

A W10 × 49 is used as a column and is supported by a concrete pier as shown in Figure 5.44. The top surface of the pier is 18 inches by 18 inches. Design an A36 base plate for a column dead load of 98 kips and a live load of 145 kips. The concrete strength is \(f ^{′}_{c}= 3000\) psi.

 

 

Admin
Solution

The factored load is

\(P_{u}=1.2D+1.6L=1.2(98) + 1.6(145)=349.6\) kips

Compute the required bearing area. From AISC Equation J8-2,

\(P_{p}=0.85f ^{′}_{c}A_{1}\sqrt{ \frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}}} ≤1.7f ^{′}_{c}A_{1}\)

For \(\phi_{c}P_{p} ≥ P_{u}\),

\(0.65[0.85(3)A_{1}\sqrt{ \frac{18(18)}{A_{1}}}]≥349.6\)

\(A_{1}≥137.3\) \(in.^{2}\)

Check upper limit:

\(\phi_{c}1.7f ^{′}_{c} A_{1 }= 0.65(1.7)(3)(137.3) = 455\) kips \(> 349.6\) kips (OK)

Also, the plate must be at least as large as the column, so

\(b_{f} d = 10.0(10.0) = 100\) \(in.^{2}\) \(< 137.3\) \(in.^{2}\) (OK)

For \(B = N = 13\) in., \(A_{1 }\) provided \(= 13(13) = 169\) \(in.^{2}\)

The dimensions of the cantilever strips are

\(m=\frac{N-0.95d}{2}=\frac{13-0.95(10)}{2}=1.75\) in.

\(n=\frac{B-0.8b_{f}}{2}=\frac{13-0.8(10)}{2}=2.5\) in.

From Equation 5.17,

\(n ^{′}=\frac{1}{4}\sqrt{db_{f}}=\frac{1}{4}\sqrt{10.0(10.0)}=2.5\) in.

As a conservative simplification, let \(\lambda = 1.0\), giving

\(\ell\)= max(m, n, \(\lambda n^{′}\)) = max(1.75, 2.5, 2.5) = 2.5 in.

From Equation 5.18, the required plate thickness is

\(t=\ell\sqrt{\frac{2P_{u}}{0.90F_{y}BN}}=2.5\sqrt{\frac{2(349.6)}{0.90(36)(13)(13)}}=0.893\) in.

 

Use a PL 1 × 13 × 13.

The applied load is \(P_{a} = D + L = 98 + 145 = 243\) kips.

Compute the required bearing area. Using AISC Equation J8-2,

\(\frac{P_{p}}{Ω_{c}}≥P_{a}\)

 

\(\frac{1}{2.31}\left[0.85(3)A_{1}\sqrt{\frac{18(18)}{A_{1}}}\right] ≥243\)

\(A_{1}≥150\) \(in.^{2}\)

The upper limit is

\(\frac{1}{Ω_{c}}(1.7f ^{′}_{c}A_{1})=\frac{1}{2.31}[(1.7)(3)(150)]=331\) kips \(>243\) kips (OK)

The plate must be as large as the column:

\(b_{f}d = 10.0(10.0) = 100\) \(in.^{2}\) \(< 150\) \(in.^{2}\) (OK)

Try \(B = N = 13\) in., with \(A_{1}\) provided \(= 13(13) = 169\) \(in.^{2}\) \(>150\) \(in.^{2}\)

 

\(m=\frac{N-0.95d}{2}=\frac{13-0.95(10.0)}{2}=1.75\) in.

\(n=\frac{B-0.8b_{f}}{2}=\frac{13-0.8(10)}{2}=2.5\) in

\(n ^{′}=\frac{1}{4}\sqrt{db_{f}}=\frac{1}{4}\sqrt{10.0(10.0)}=2.5\) in.

 

Conservatively, let \(\lambda = 1.0\), resulting in

\(\ell\)= max(m, n, \(\lambda n^{′}\)) = max(1.75, 2.5, 2.5) = 2.5 in.

From Equation 5.20,

\(t≥\ell\sqrt{\frac{2P_{a}}{F_{y}BN/1.67}}=2.5\sqrt{\frac{2(243)}{36(13)(13)/1.67}}=0.913\) in.

 

Use a PL 1 × 13 × 13.
