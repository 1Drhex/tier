Guest Question.

Rod Stretching

A round rod is made from the steel alloy having the stress–strain characteristics shown in Figure 5.12. (See Figure 5.15.) When it is subjected to 3500 lb of tension (approximately equal to the weight of an automobile sedan), calculate (a) the stress and strain in the rod, (b) the amount that it stretches, (c) its change in diameter, and (d) its stiffness. (e) If the force was only 1000 lb, by what amount would the rod have stretched? Use the value \(ν\) = 0.3 for the Poisson’s ratio.

Approach
We will first calculate the stress by applying Equation (5.2).

\(\sigma =\frac{F}{A} \) (5.2)

Then, we will calculate the strain, change in length, change in diameter, and stiffness by applying Equations (5.5), (5.3), (5.8), and (5.7) respectively.

σ = Eε (5.5)

\(\varepsilon =\frac{\Delta L}{L}\) (5.3)

\(\Delta d=-\nu d\frac{\Delta L}{L}\) (5.8)

\(k=\frac{EA}{L} \) (5.7)

Admin
Solution

(a) The rod’s cross-sectional area is

\(A=\frac{\pi (0.5in.)^2}{4} \longleftarrow\left[A=\frac{\pi d^2}{4} \right] \)

\(= 0.1963 in^2\)

and the tensile stress is

\(\sigma =\frac{3500lb}{0.1963 in^ 2} \longleftarrow \left[\sigma =\frac{F}{A} \right] \)

\(=1.783\times 10^4\frac{lb}{in^2} \)
\(=1.783\times10^4psi\)

The USCS dimension ksi is next used to represent the factor of 1000 psi:

\(\sigma =(1.783\times10^4psi) \left(10^{-3}\frac{ksi}{psi} \right) \)

\(=17.83(\cancel {psi})(\frac{ksi}{\cancel {psi}} )\)

\(=17.83ksi\)

The strain within the rod is given by

\(\varepsilon =\frac{1.783\times 10^4psi}{30.6\times 10^6psi} \longleftarrow \left[\varepsilon =\frac{\sigma }{E} \right] \)

\(=5.825\times 10^{-4}(\frac{\cancel {psi}}{\cancel {psi}})\)

\(=5.825\times 10^{-4}\)

or 0.05825%. Because stress and elastic modulus have the same units, their dimensions cancel when strain is calculated by using Equation (5.5).

(b) The 3500-lb load elongates the rod by

\(ΔL = (5.825 \times 10 ^{-4} )(12 in.) \longleftarrow \left[Δd=εL\right]\)

\(= 6.990 \times 10^{-3} in.\)

(c) Using ν =0.3 for steel, the diameter changes by the amount

\(Δd = -(0.3)(0.5 in.)\left(\frac{6.990\times 10^{-3}in.}{12in.} \right) \longleftarrow \left[Δd=-\nu d\frac{\Delta L}{L} \right]\)

\(= – 8.738 \times 10^{-5} (in.)(\frac{\cancel{in}}{\cancel{in}} )\)

\(=- 8.738 × 10 ^{-5} in.\)

and the negative sign convention indicates that the diameter contracts.
(d) The rod’s stiffness is determined from the material’s elastic modulus, cross-sectional area, and length:

\(k=\frac{(30.6\times 10^6psi)(0.196 in ^2 )}{12 in.} \longleftarrow \left[k=\frac{EA}{L} \right] \)

\(=5.007 \times 10^5(\frac{lb}{{\cancel{in}^2} })(\cancel{in}^2) \left(\frac{1}{in.} \right) \)

\(= 5.007 \times 10^5\frac{lb}{in.} \)

In canceling the dimensions, we have expanded the stress unit psi as lb/in^2.

(e) With a force of only 1000 lb, the rod would stretch by only

\(\Delta L=\frac{1000lb}{5.007\times 10^5{lb}/{in.}} \longleftarrow [ΔL=\frac{F}{k} ] \)

\(= 1.997\times 10 ^{-3}(\cancel{lb})(\frac{in.}{\cancel{lb}} )\)

\(= 1.997 \times 10 ^{-3} in.\)
