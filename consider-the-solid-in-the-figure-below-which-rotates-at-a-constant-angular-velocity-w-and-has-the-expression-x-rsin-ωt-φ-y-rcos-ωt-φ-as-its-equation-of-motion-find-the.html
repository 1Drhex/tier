Guest Question.

Consider the solid in the figure below, which rotates at a constant angular velocity w and has the expression

\(\begin{cases} x= R\sin (\omega t+\phi ) \\ y= R\cos (\omega t+\phi ) \end{cases}\)

as its equation of motion. Find the velocity and acceleration of the motion described both in material and spatial forms.

Admin
Solution

The equation of motion can be rewritten as

\(\begin{cases} x= R\sin (\omega t+\phi )= R\sin(\omega t)\cos\phi + R\cos(\omega t)\sin \phi \\ y= R\cos (\omega t+\phi )= R\cos(\omega t)\cos \phi – R\sin(\omega t)\sin\phi \end{cases}\)

and, since for t = 0, X = RsinΦ and Y = RcosΦ, the canonical form of the equation of motion and its inverse equation result in

\(\begin{cases} x= X\cos(\omega t)+ Y\sin (\omega t) \\ y= -X\sin(\omega t)+ Y\cos (\omega t ) \end{cases}\) and \(\begin{cases} X= x\cos(\omega t)-y\sin (\omega t) \\ Y= -x\sin(\omega t)+ y\cos (\omega t ) \end{cases}\)

Velocity in material description:

\(V(X,t)= \frac{\partial x(X,t)}{\partial t} \overset{not}{\equiv } \left[\begin{matrix} \frac{\partial x}{\partial t}= -X\omega \sin (\omega t)+Y\omega \cos (\omega t) \\ \frac{\partial y}{\partial t}= -X\omega \cos (\omega t)-Y\omega \sin (\omega t) \end{matrix} \right]\)

Velocity in spatial description:

Replacing the canonical form of the equation of motion into the material description of the velocity results in

\(v(x,t)= V(X,(x,t)t)\overset{not}{\equiv } \left[\begin{matrix} \omega y \\ -\omega x \end{matrix} \right]\)

Acceleration in material description:

\(A(X,t)= \frac{\partial V(X,t)}{\partial t}\)

 

\(A(X,t)\overset{not}{\equiv } \left[\begin{matrix} \frac{\partial v_{x} }{\partial t}= -X\omega^{2} \cos (\omega t)-Y\omega^{2} \sin (\omega t) \\ \frac{\partial v_{y} }{\partial t}= X\omega^{2} \sin (\omega t)-Y\omega^{2} \cos (\omega t) \end{matrix} \right]=\)

 

\(=-\omega ^{2} \left[\begin{matrix}X \cos (\omega t)+ Y \sin (\omega t) \\ -X \sin (\omega t)+ Y \cos (\omega t) \end{matrix} \right]\)

Acceleration in spatial description:

Replacing the canonical form of the equation of motion into the material description of the acceleration results in

\(a(x,t)= A(X(x,t),t)\overset{not}{\equiv } \left[\begin{matrix}-\omega^{2}x \\ -\omega^{ 2}y \end{matrix} \right]\)

This same expression can be obtained if the expression for the velocity v(x; t) and the definition of material derivative in (1.15) are taken into account,

\(a(x,t)= \frac{dv(x,t)}{dt} = \frac{\partial v(x,t)}{\partial t} + v(x,t)\cdot \nabla v(x,t)= \\ \overset{not}{\equiv }\frac{\partial}{\partial t} \left[\begin{matrix} \omega y \\ -\omega x \end{matrix} \right]+ [\omega y, -\omega x]\left[\begin{matrix} \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial y} \end{matrix} \right] [\omega y, -\omega x]\)

 

\(a(x,t) \overset{not}{\equiv }\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right] + [ \omega y, -\omega x]\left[ \begin{matrix} \frac{\partial}{\partial x} (\omega y) & \frac{\partial}{\partial x} (-\omega x) \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial y}(\omega y) & \frac{\partial}{\partial y}(-\omega x) \end{matrix} \right] = \left[\begin{matrix} -\omega ^{2}x \\ -\omega ^{2} y \end{matrix} \right]\)

Note that the result obtained using both procedures is identical.
