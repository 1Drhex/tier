Guest Question.

Explain how the moments in each joint of the two-story frame shown in Fig. \(11-21 (\mathrm{a})\) are determined. \(EI\) is constant.

Admin
Solution

Slope-Deflection Equation. Since the supports at \(A\) and \(F\) are fixed, Eq. 11-8 applies for all six spans of the frame. No FEMs have to be calculated, since the applied loading acts at the joints. Here the loading displaces joints \(B\) and \(E\) an amount \(\Delta_{1}\), and \(C\) and \(D\) an amount \(\Delta_{1}+\Delta_{2}\). As a result, members \(A B\) and \(F E\) undergo rotations of \(\psi_{1}=\Delta_{1} / 5\) and \(B C\) and \(E D\) undergo rotations of \(\psi_{2}=\Delta_{2} / 5\).

Applying Eq. 11-8 to the frame yields

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=2 E k\left(2 \theta_{N}+\theta_{F}-3 \psi\right)+(\text { FEM })_{N}\\ \text For Internal Span or End Span with Far End Fixed \end{array} \) (11-8)

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{A B}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{5}\right)\left[2(0)+\theta_{B}-3 \psi_{1}\right]+0 \\ \end{array}\) (1)

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{B A}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{5}\right)\left[2 \theta_{B}+0-3 \psi_{1}\right]+0 \\ \end{array}\) (2)

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{B C}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{5}\right)\left[2 \theta_{B}+\theta_{C}-3 \psi_{2}\right]+0 \\ \end{array}\) (3)

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{C B}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{5}\right)\left[2 \theta_{C}+\theta_{B}-3 \psi_{2}\right]+0 \\ \end{array}\) (4)

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{C D}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{7}\right)\left[2 \theta_{C}+\theta_{D}-3(0)\right]+0 \\ \end{array}\) (5)

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{D C}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{7}\right)\left[2 \theta_{D}+\theta_{C}-3(0)\right]+0 \end{array}\) (6)

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{B E}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{7}\right)\left[2 \theta_{B}+\theta_{E}-3(0)\right]+0 \\ \end{array}\) (7)

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{E B}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{7}\right)\left[2 \theta_{E}+\theta_{B}-3(0)\right]+0 \\ \end{array}\) (8)

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{E D}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{5}\right)\left[2 \theta_{E}+\theta_{D}-3 \psi_{2}\right]+0 \\ \end{array}\) (9)

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{D E}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{5}\right)\left[2 \theta_{D}+\theta_{E}-3 \psi_{2}\right]+0 \\ \end{array}\) (10)

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{F E}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{5}\right)\left[2(0)+\theta_{E}-3 \psi_{1}\right]+0 \\ \end{array}\) (11)

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{E F}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{5}\right)\left[2 \theta_{E}+0-3 \psi_{1}\right]+0 \end{array}\) (12)

These 12 equations contain 18 unknowns.

Equilibrium Equations. Moment equilibrium of joints \(B, C, D\), and \(E\), Fig. 11-21 \(b\), requires

\(\begin{aligned} M_{B A}+M_{B E}+M_{B C} &=0 \\ \end{aligned}\) (13)

\(\begin{aligned} M_{C B}+M_{C D} &=0 \\ \end{aligned}\) (14)

\(\begin{aligned} M_{D C}+M_{D E} &=0 \\ \end{aligned}\) (15)

\(\begin{aligned} M_{E F}+M_{E B}+M_{E D} &=0 \end{aligned}\) (16)

As in the preceding examples, the shear at the base of all the columns for any story must balance the applied horizontal loads, Fig. 11-21c. This yields

\(\begin{array}{r} :^{+} \sum F_{x}=0 ; \quad 40-V_{B C}-V_{E D}=0 \\ 40+\frac{M_{B C}+M_{C B}}{5}+\frac{M_{E D}+M_{D E}}{5}=0 \end{array}\) (17)

\(\begin{array}{r} :^{+} \sum F_{x}=0 ; \quad 40+80-V_{A B}-V_{F E}=0 \\ 120+\frac{M_{A B}+M_{B A}}{5}+\frac{M_{E F}+M_{F E}}{5}=0 \end{array}\) (18)

Solution requires substituting Eqs. (1)-(12) into Eqs. (13)-(18), which yields six equations having six unknowns, \(\psi_{1}, \psi_{2}, \theta_{B}, \theta_{C}, \theta_{D}\), and \(\theta_{E}\). These equations can then be solved simultaneously. The results are resubstituted into Eqs. (1)-(12), which yields the moments at the joints.
