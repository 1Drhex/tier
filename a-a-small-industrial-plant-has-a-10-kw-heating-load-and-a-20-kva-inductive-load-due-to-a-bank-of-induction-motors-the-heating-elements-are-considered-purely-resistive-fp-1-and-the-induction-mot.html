Guest Question.

a. A small industrial plant has a 10 kW heating load and a 20 kVA inductive load due to a bank of induction motors. The heating elements are considered purely resistive \((F_p = 1)\), and the induction motors have a lagging power factor of 0.7. If the supply is 1000 V at 60 Hz, determine the capacitive element required to raise the power factor to 0.95.
b. Compare the levels of current drawn from the supply.

Admin
Solution

a. For the induction motors,

\(S = VI = 20 kVA\\[0.5cm] P = S cos \theta = (20 \times 10^3 VA)(0.7) = 14 kW\\[0.5cm] \theta = cos^{-1} 0.7 ≅ 45.6°\\[0.5cm] and\\[0.5cm] Q_L = VI sin \theta = (20 kVA)(0.714) = 14.28 kVAR(L)\)

The power triangle for the total system appears in Fig. 20.36. Note the addition of real powers and the resulting \(S_T\):

\(S_T=\sqrt{(24kW)^2+(14.28 kVAR)^2}=27.93 kVA\\[0.5cm] and\hspace{30 pt} I_T=\frac{S_T}{E}=\frac{27.93 kVA}{1000 V}=27.93 A \)

The desired power factor of 0.95 results in an angle between S and P of

\(\theta = cos^{-1} 0.95 = 18.91°\)

changing the power triangle to that in Fig. 20.37:

with \(tan \ \theta=\frac{Q^{\prime}_{L}}{P_T}\longrightarrow Q^{\prime}_{L}=P_Ttan\ \theta=(24 kW)(tan 18.19°)=(24 kW)(0.329) = 7.9 kVAR(L) \)

The inductive reactive power must therefore be reduced by

\(Q_L-Q^{\prime}_{L}=14.28 kVAR(L) – 7.9 kVAR(L) = 6.38 kVAR(L)\)

Therefore, \(Q_C = 6.38kVAR\), and using

\(Q_C=\frac{E^2}{X_C}\\[0.5cm] we\ obtain \\[0.5cm] X_C=\frac{E^2}{Q_C}=\frac{(10^3V)^2}{6.38 kVAR}=156.74 Ω\\[0.5cm] and\hspace{30 pt} C=\frac{1}{2\pi fX_C}=\frac{1}{(2\pi)(60 Hz)(156.74 Ω)}=16.93 \mu F\)

 

b. \(S_T=\sqrt{(24 kW)^2+[7.9 kVAR(L)]^2}=25.27 kVA\\[0.5cm] I_T=\frac{S_T}{E}=\frac{25.27 kVA}{1000 V}=25.27 \)

The new \(I_T\) is

\(I_T = 25.27 A \lt 27.93 A\quad (original)\)
