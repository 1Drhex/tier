Guest Question.

A simple steam power plant operates between pressures of 10 kPa and 4 MPa with a maximum temperature of 400°C. The power output from the steam turbine is 100 MW. A gas turbine provides the energy to the boiler; it accepts air at 100 kPa and 25°C, has a pressure ratio of 5, and a maximum temperature of 850°C. The exhaust gases exit the boiler at 350 K. Determine the thermal efficiency of the combined Brayton-Rankine cycle.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { If we neglect the work of the pump, the enthalpy remains unchanged across the pump. Hence, } h_{2} \cong h_{1}= \) \( 192 kJ / kg \text {. At } 400^{\circ} C \text { and } 4 MPa \text {, we have } h_{3}=3214 kJ / kg \text { and } s_{3}=6.7698 kJ / kg \cdot K \text {. State } 4 \text { is located by noting that } s_{4}= \) \( s_{3} \text { so that the quality is } \)

\( x_{4}=\frac{s_{4}-s_{f}}{s_{f g}}=\frac{6.798-0.6491}{7.5019}=0.8159 \) \( \text { Thus, } h_{4}=h_{f}+x_{4} h_{f g}=192+(0.8159)(2393)=2144 kJ / kg \text {. The steam mass flux is found using the turbine output as follows: } \)

\( \dot{W}_{ ST }=\dot{m}_{s}\left(h_{3}-h_{4}\right) \quad 100000=\dot{m}_{s}(3214-2144) \quad \dot{m}_{s}=93.46 kg / s \).

Considering the gas-turbine cycle,

\( T_{6}=T_{5}\left(\frac{P_{6}}{P_{5}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}=(298)(5)^{0.2857}=472.0 K \).

Also,

\( T_{8}=T_{7}\left(\frac{P_{8}}{P_{7}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}=(1123)\left(\frac{1}{5}\right)^{0.2857}=709.1 K \).

Thus, we have for the boiler,

\( \dot{m}_{s}\left(h_{3}-h_{2}\right)=\dot{m}_{a} C_{ p }\left(T_{8}-T_{9}\right) \quad(93.46)(3214-192)=\left(\dot{m}_{a}\right)(1.00)(709.1-350) \).

\( \dot{m}_{a}=786.5 kg / s \).

\( \text { The output of the gas turbine is (note that this is not } \dot{W}_{G T} \text { ) } \).

\( \dot{W}_{\text {turb }}=\dot{m}_{a} C_{p}\left(T_{7}-T_{8}\right)=(786.5)(1.00)(1123-709.1)=325.5 MW \).

The energy needed by the compressor is

\( \dot{W}_{\text {comp }}=\dot{m}_{a} C_{p}\left(T_{6}-T_{5}\right)=(786.5)(1.00)(472-298)=136.9 MW \).

\( \text { Hence, the net gas turbine output is } \dot{W}_{ GT }=\dot{W}_{\text {turb }}-\dot{W}_{\text {comp }}=325.5-136.9=188.6 MW . \text { The energy input by the combustor is } \)

\( Q_{\text {in }}=\dot{m}_{a} C_{p}\left(T_{7}-T_{6}\right)=(786.5)(1.00)(1123-472)=512 MW \).

The above calculations allow us to determine the combined cycle efficiency as

\( \eta=\frac{\dot{W}_{ ST }+\dot{W}_{ GT }}{\dot{Q}_{ in }}=\frac{100+188.6}{512}=0.564 \quad \text { or } \quad 56.4 \% \).

Note that this efficiency is 59.3 percent higher than the Rankine cycle (see ) and 52.8 percent higher than the Brayton cycle (see ). Cycle efficiency could be increased even more by using steam reheaters, steam regenerators, gas intercoolers, and gas reheaters.
