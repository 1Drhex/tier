Guest Question.

A Parson’s reaction turbine has mean diameter of blades as 1.6 m and rotor moving at 1500 rpm. The inlet and outlet angles are 80° and 20° respectively. Turbine receives steam at 12 bar, 200°C and has isentropic heat drop of 26 kJ/kg. 5% of steam supplied is lost through leakage. Determine the following considering horse power developed in stage to be 600 hp.
(a) the stage efficiency
(b) the blade height.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{Given: }50\%\text{ reaction turbine with }β_{1} = 80° \& β_{2} = 20°\\\text{N = 1500 rpm, d = 1.6 m }⇒ α_{2} = 80° and α_{1} = 20°\\U = r ω = \frac{1.6 × 2 × \pi × 1500}{2 × 60} = 125.66 m/s\\\text{Velocity diagram for turbine stage is drawn as below using above data.}\\\text{From velocity diagram, Length AD = C}_{1}\text{ = 168.75 m/s}\\\text{Length FG }= ΔC_{w} = 170 m/s\\\text{Length FE }= C_{2a} = 52.5 m/s = C_{1a}\\\text{Work done }= U · ΔC_{w} = \text{ 125.66 × 170 = 21362.2 J/kg or 21.36 kJ/kg per kg of steam.}\\\text{Since 5}\%\text{ of steam gets leaked so only 95}\%\text{ of steam supplied will be used for production of work}\\\text{Net work per kg of steam = 0.95 × 21.36 = 20.29 kJ/kg}\\\text{Stage efficiency} = \frac{\text{Work done}}{\text{Heat supplied}} = \frac{20.29}{26} = \text{0.7804 or 78.04}\%\\\text{Let mass of steam supplied be m kg per second to produce 600 hp.}\\\begin{aligned}0.7457 × 600&= m × 20.29\\m&= 22.05 kg/s\end{aligned}\\\text{Assuming heat drop per stage to be small so that the specific volume remains nearly constant throughout in the stage.}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Specific volume at inlet from steam table, }v_{1}&= v_{\text{at 12 bar, 200°c}}\\v_{1}&= 0.16930 m^{3}/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Let height of blades be ‘h’.}\\\text{Volume of steam flow rate = π × d × h × C}_{1a}\\22.05 × 0.16930 = π × 1.2 × h × 52.5\\\qquad\qquad \text{ h = 0.01886 m or 1.89 cm}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad\quad\text{Stage efficiency}&= 78.04\%\\\text{Height of blade}&= 1.89 cm\end{aligned}\)
