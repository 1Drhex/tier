Guest Question.

Per-Phase Equivalent Circuit. Figure 12.6-1a shows a balanced Y-to-Δ three-phase circuit. The phase voltages of the Y-connected source are \(V_a\) = 110 ∠0° V rms, \(V_b\) = 110 ∠-120° V rms, and \(V_c\) = 110 ∠120° V rms. The line impedances are each \(Z_L\) = 10 + j5 Ω. The impedances of the Δ-connected load are each \(Z_Δ\) = 75 + j225 Ω. Determine the phase currents in the Δ-connected load.

Admin
Solution

Convert the Δ-connected load to a Y-connected load using the Δ-to-Y transformation summarized in Table 12.4-1. The impedances of the balanced equivalent Y-connected load are

\(Z_Y = \frac{75 + j225}{3} = 25 + j75 \Omega\)

The per-phase equivalent circuit for the Y-to-Y circuit is shown in Figure 12.6-1c. The line current is given by

\(I_{aA} = \frac{V_a}{Z_L + Z_Y} = \frac{110 \angle 0^\circ}{(10 + j5) + (25 + j75)} = 1.26 \angle -66^\circ\) A rms (12.6-1)

The line current \(I_{aA}\), calculated using the per-phase equivalent circuit, is also the line current \(I_{aA}\) in the Y-to-Y circuit, as well as the line current \(I_{aA}\) in the Y-to-Δ circuit. The other line currents in the balanced Y-to-Y circuit have the same magnitude but differ in phase angle by 120°. These line currents are

\(I_{bB} = 1.26 \angle -186^\circ\) A rms and \(I_{cC} = 1.26 \angle 54^\circ\) A rms

(To check the value of \(I_{bB}\), apply KVL to the loop in the Y-to-Y circuit that starts at node n, passes through nodes b, B, N, and returns to node n. The resulting KVL equation is

\(V_b = Z_L I_{bB} + Z_Y I_{bB} + V_{Nn}\)

Because the circuit is balanced, \(V_{Nn} = 0\). Solving for \(I_{bB}\) gives

\(I_{bB} = \frac{V_b}{Z_L + Z_Y} = \frac{110 \angle -120^\circ}{(10 + j5) + (25 + j75)} = 1.26 \angle -186^\circ\) A rms (12.6-2)

Comparing Eqs. 12.6-1 and 12.6-2 shows that the line currents in the balanced Y-to-Y circuit have the same magnitude but differ in phase angle by 120°.

The line currents of the Y-to-Δ circuit in Figure 12.6-1a are equal to the line currents of the Y-to-Y circuit in Figure 12.6-1b because the Y-to-Δ and Y-to-Y circuits are equivalent.

The voltage \(V_{AN}\) in the per-phase equivalent circuit is

\(V_{AN} = I_{aA}Z_Y = (1.26 \angle -66^\circ)(25 + j75) = 99.6 \angle 5^\circ\) V rms

The voltage \(V_{AN}\) calculated using the per-phase equivalent circuit is also the phase voltage \(V_{AN}\) of the Y-to-Y circuit. The other phase voltages of the balanced Y-to-Y circuit have the same magnitude but differ in phase angle by 120°. These phase voltages are

\(V_{BN} = 99.6 \angle -115^\circ\) V rms and \(V_{CN} = 99.6 \angle 125^\circ\) V rms

The line-to-line voltages of the Y-to-Y circuit are calculated as

\(V_{AB} = V_{AN} – V_{BN} = 99.5 \angle 5^\circ – 99.5 \angle -115^\circ = 172 \angle 35^\circ\) V rms

\(V_{BC} = V_{BN} – V_{CN} = 99.5 \angle -115^\circ – 99.5 \angle 125^\circ = 172 \angle -85^\circ\) V rms

\(V_{CA} = V_{CN} – V_{AN} = 99.5 \angle 125^\circ – 99.5 \angle 5^\circ = 172 \angle 155^\circ\) V rms

The phase voltages of a Δ-connected load are equal to the line-to-line voltages. The phase currents are

\(I_{AB} = \frac{V_{AB}}{Z_Δ} = \frac{172 \angle 35^\circ}{75 + j225} = 0.727 \angle -36^\circ\) A rms

\(I_{BC} = \frac{V_{BC}}{Z_Δ} = \frac{172 \angle -85^\circ}{75 + j225} = 0.727 \angle -156^\circ\) A rms

\(I_{CA} = \frac{V_{CA}}{Z_Δ} = \frac{172 \angle 155^\circ}{75 + j225} = 0.727 \angle -84^\circ\) A rms
