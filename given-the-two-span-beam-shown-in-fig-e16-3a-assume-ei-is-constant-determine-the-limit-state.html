Guest Question.

Given: The two-span beam shown in Fig. E16.3a. Assume EI is constant.

Determine: The limit state.

Admin
Solution

The moments at section B and C are relative maxima; the value at B is the largest, so yielding will occur first at this section.

 

\(M_{ B }=\frac{13}{64} P L\)

 

\(M_{ C }=-\frac{6}{64} P L\)

 

Load level 1:
We set \(M_{ B }\) equal to \(M_{ y }\) and insert a hinge at B.

 

\(\frac{13}{64} P_{1} L=M_{ y }\)

 

\(\Downarrow\)

 

\(P_{1}=\frac{64}{13 L} M_{ y }\)

 

At this load level, the modified structure is

 

Load level 2:
We apply an incremental load, \(\Delta P\), to the modified structure leading to an incremental moment at C.

 

\(\Delta M_{ C }=\frac{L}{2} \Delta P\)

 

The total negative moment at C is

 

\(\left\{M_{ C }=\frac{6}{64} P_{1} L+\frac{\Delta P L}{2}\right\}\)

 

Setting \(M_{ C }=M_{ y }\) leads to the limiting value for \(\Delta P\)

 

\(\frac{6}{64} P_{1} L+\Delta P \frac{L}{2}=M_{ y }\)

 

Substituting for \(P_{1}\) , one obtains

 

\(\Delta P=\frac{14}{13 L} M_{ y }\)

 

Finally

 

\(P_{\max }=P_{1}+\Delta P=\frac{78}{13 L} M_{ y }=\frac{6}{L} M_{ y }\)

 

The limit state has hinges at B and C.
