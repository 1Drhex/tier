Guest Question.

Suppose two spin-1/2 particles are known to be in the singlet configuration (Equation 4.176). Let \( S_{a}^{(a)} \) be the component of the spin angular momentum of particle number 1 in the direction defined by the vector a. Similarly, let \( S_{b}^{(2)} \) be the component of 2’s angular momentum in the direction b. Show that

\( \left\langle S_{a}^{(1)} S_{b}^{(2)}\right\rangle=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{4} \cos \theta \) (4.225).

where θ is the angle between a and b.

Admin
Solution

We may as well choose axes so that \( \hat{a} \) lies along the z axis and \( \hat{b} \) is in the xz plane. Then \( S_{a}^{(1)}=S_{z}^{(1)}, \text { and } S_{b}^{(2)}= \cos \theta S_{z}^{(2)}+\sin \theta S_{x}^{(2)} \cdot\left\langle 00\left|S_{a}^{(1)} S_{b}^{(2)}\right| 00\right\rangle \) is to be calculated.

\( S_{a}^{(1)} S_{b}^{(2)}|00\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left[S_{z}^{(1)}\left(\cos \theta S_{z}^{(2)}+\sin \theta S_{x}^{(2)}\right)\right](|\uparrow \downarrow\rangle-|\downarrow \uparrow\rangle) \).

\( =\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left[\left(S_{z}|\uparrow\rangle\right)\left(\cos \theta S_{z}|\downarrow\rangle+\sin \theta S_{x}|\downarrow\rangle\right)-\left(S_{z}|\downarrow\rangle\right)\left(\cos \theta S_{z}|\uparrow\rangle+\sin \theta S_{x}|\uparrow\rangle\right)\right] \).

\( =\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left\{\left(\frac{\hbar}{2}|\uparrow\rangle\right)\left[\cos \theta\left(-\frac{\hbar}{2}|\downarrow\rangle\right)+\sin \theta\left(\frac{\hbar}{2}|\uparrow\rangle\right)\right]-\left(-\frac{\hbar}{2}|\downarrow\rangle\right)\left[\cos \theta\left(\frac{\hbar}{2}|\uparrow\rangle\right)+\sin \theta\left(\frac{\hbar}{2}|\downarrow\rangle\right)\right]\right\} \), (using Eq. 4.145)

\( S _{z}=\frac{\hbar}{2}\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{array}\right) \) (4.145).

\( =\frac{\hbar^{2}}{4}\left[\cos \theta \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(-|\uparrow \downarrow\rangle+|\downarrow \uparrow\rangle)+\sin \theta \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|\uparrow \uparrow\rangle+|\downarrow \downarrow\rangle)\right]=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{4}\left[-\cos \theta|00\rangle+\sin \theta \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|11\rangle+|1-1\rangle)\right] \).

so \( \left\langle S_{a}^{(1)} S_{b}^{(2)}\right\rangle=\left\langle 00\left|S_{a}^{(1)} S_{b}^{(2)}\right| 00\right\rangle=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{4}\left\langle0 0 \left|\left[-\cos \theta|00\rangle+\sin \theta \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|11\rangle+|1-1\rangle)\right]=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{4} \cos \theta\langle 00 \mid 00\rangle\right.\right. \).

(by orthogonality), and hence \( \left\langle S_{a}^{(1)} S_{b}^{(2)}\right\rangle=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{4} \cos \theta \). QED
