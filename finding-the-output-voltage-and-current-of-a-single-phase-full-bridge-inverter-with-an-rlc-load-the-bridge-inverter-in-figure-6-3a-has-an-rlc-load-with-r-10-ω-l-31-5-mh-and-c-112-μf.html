Finding the Output Voltage and Current of a Single-Phase Full-Bridge Inverter with an RLC Load

The bridge inverter in Figure 6.3a has an RLC load with \(R = 10 Ω\), \(L = 31.5 mH\), and \(C= 112 \mu F\). The inverter frequency is \(f_0 = 60 Hz\) and dc input voltage is \(V_s = 220 V\). (a) Express the instantaneous load current in Fourier series. Calculate (b) the rms load current at the fundamental frequency \(I_{o1}\), (c) the THD of the load current, (d) the power absorbed by the load \(P_0\) and the fundamental power \(P_{01}\), (e) the average current of dc supply \(I_s\), and (f) the rms and peak current of each transistor. (g) Draw the waveform of fundamental load current and show the conduction intervals of transistors and diodes. Calculate the conduction time of (h) the transistors, (i) the diodes, and (j) the effective load angle θ.

Admin
Solution

\(V_s = 220 V, f_0 = 60 Hz, R = 10 Ω , L = 31.5 mH, C = 112 µF, and = 2π ×60 = 377 rad/s\). The inductive reactance for the nth harmonic voltage is

\(X_L = j_n \omega L = j2n\pi \times 60 \times 31.5 \times 10^{-3} = j11.87 n \Omega\)

The capacitive reactance for the nth harmonic voltage is

\(X_c=\frac{j}{n\omega C}=\frac{j10^6}{2n\pi \times 60 \times 112}=\frac{-j23.68}{n} \Omega\)

The impedance for the nth harmonic voltage is

\(|Z_n|=\sqrt{R^2+\left(n\omega L – \frac{1}{n\omega C} \right)^2 }=[10^2+(11.87n – 23.68/n)^2]^{1/2}\)

and the load impedance angle for the nth harmonic voltage is

\(\theta _n=tan^{-1}\frac{11.87n – 23.68/n}{10}=tan^{-1}\left(1.187n-\frac{2.368}{n} \right)\)

a. From Eq. (6.16),

\(\upsilon _0=\sum\limits_{n=1,3,5,. . . }^{\infty }{\frac{4V_s}{n\pi } \sin n \omega t }\) (6.16)

the instantaneous output voltage can be expressed as

\(\upsilon _0(t)=280.1 \sin(377t) + 93.4 \sin(3 × 377t) + 56.02 \sin(5 × 377t) + 40.02 \sin(7 × 377t) + 31.12 \sin(9 × 377t) + . . .\)

Dividing the output voltage by the load impedance and considering the appropriate delay due to the load impedance angles, we can obtain the instantaneous load current as

\(i_o(t) = 18.1 \sin(377t + 49.72º) + 3.17 \sin(3 \times 377t – 70.17º)+ \sin(5 \times 377t – 79.63º) + 0.5 \sin(7 \times 377t – 82.85º)+ 0.3 \sin(9 \times 377t – 84.52º) + . . .\)

b. The peak fundamental load current is \(I_{m1} = 18.1 A\). The rms load current at fundamental frequency is \(I_{o1}=18.1/\sqrt{2}=12.8 A\)

c. Considering up to the ninth harmonic, the peak load current,

\(I_m = (18.1^2 + 3.17^2 + 1.0^2 + 0.5^2 + 0.3^2)^{1/2} = 18.41 A\)

The rms harmonic load current is

\(I_h=\frac{(I^2_m-I^2_{m1})^{1/2}}{\sqrt{2}}=\frac{\sqrt{18.41^2-18.1^2} }{\sqrt{2} }=2.3789 A\)

Using Eq. (6.6),the THD of the load current is

\(THD=\frac{(I^2_m-I^2_m)^{1/2}}{I_{m1}}=\left[\left(\frac{18.41}{18.1} \right)^2 -1\right]^{1/2}= 18.59\%\)

d. The rms load current is \(I_o\cong I_m/\sqrt{2}=18.41/\sqrt{2}=18.41 A \), and the load power is \(P_o = 13.02^2 ×10 = 1695 W\). Using Eq. (6.13), the fundamental output power is

\(P_{o1}=V_{o1}I_{01}\cos \theta _1 = I_{o1}^2 R \) (6.13)

\(P_{o1} = I_{o1}^2 R = 12.82 × 10 = 1638.4 W\)

e. The average supply current \(I_s = P_o/V_s = 1695/220 = 7.7 A\).

f. The peak transistor current \(I_o\cong I_m= 18.41 A\). The maximum permissible rms current
of each transistor is \(I_{Q(max)}=I_o/\sqrt{2}=I_p/2= 18.41/2 = 9.2 A\).

g. The waveform for fundamental load current \(i_1(t) \) is shown in Figure 6.4.

h. From Figure 6.4, the conduction time of each transistor is found approximately from

\(\omega t_0=180 – 49.72 = 130.28º or t_0 = 130.28 \times \pi (180 \times 377) = 6031 \mu s\).

i. The conduction time of each diode is approximately

\(t_d = (180 – 130.28)\times \frac{\pi }{180\times 377}=2302 \mu s\)

j. The effective load angle can be found from

\(V_oI_o \cos θ = P_o or 220 × 13.02 × \cos θ = 1695\)

which gives \(θ = 53.73º\)

Notes:
1. To calculate the exact values of the peak current, the conduction time of transistors and diodes, the instantaneous load current \(i_o(t)\) should be plotted as shown in Figure 6.4. The conduction time of a transistor must satisfy the condition \(i_o(t = t_0) = 0\), and a plot of \(i_o(t)\) by a computer program gives \(I_p = 21.14 A, t_0 = 5694 s\), and \(t_d = 2639 µs\).

2. This example can be repeated to evaluate the performance of an inverter with R, RL, or RLC load with an appropriate change in load impedance \(Z_L\) and load angle \(θ_n\).
