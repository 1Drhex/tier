Guest Question.

An oil is cooled to 100°C in a concurrent heat exchanger by transferring its heat to cooling water, that leaves the exchanger at 30°C. However, it is now required that the oil must be cooled down to 75°C by increasing the length of heat exchanger, while oil and water flow rates, their inlet temperatures and other dimensions of the exchanger keeping constant. The inlet temperatures of water and oil being 15°C and 150°C, respectively. If the original cooler was 1 m long, calculate
(i) Outlet temperature of water in new cooler,
(ii) Length of new cooler.

Admin
Solution

Given : A concurrent (parallel) flow heat exchanger.
Case I. \(L_1 = 1 m\)
Hot fluid : Oil
\(T_{h, i} = 150°C, T_{h, o} = 100°C\)
Cold fluid :Water
\(T_{c, i} = 15° C, T_{c, o} = 30°C\)
Case II. \(L_2 =?\)
Hot fluid : Oil
\(T_{h, i} = 150°C, T_{h, o} = 75°C\)
Cold fluid \(T_{c, i} = 15°C, T_{c, o} = ?\)
To find :
(i) Outlet temperature of water in new cooler, and
(ii) Length of new cooler.
Analysis : Case I. Length of new heat exchanger :
Before increasing the length of cooler, the energy balance on exchanger
\(\dot{m}_hC_{p, h}(T_{h, i} – T_{h, o}) = \dot{m}_cC_{p, c}(T_{c, o} – T_{c, i})\)
\(\dot{m}_hC_{p, h}(150 – 100) = \dot{m}_cC_{p, c}(30 – 15)\)
or \(C =\frac{C_h}{C_c} =\frac{30 – 15}{150 – 100}=\frac{15}{50}\)
= 0.3
and \(C_{min} =C_h, C_{max} = C_c\)
Effectiveness of heat exchanger
\(ε_1=\frac{ C_h(T_{h, i} – T_{h, o})}{ C_{min }(T_{h, i} – T_{c, i}) }=\frac{150 – 100}{150 – 15} = 0.37\)
Further, for parallel flow heat exchanger,
\(ε_1 = \frac{1 – exp [ – NTU_1 (1 + C)]}{1 + C}\)
\(0.37 =\frac{1 – exp [ – NTU_1 (1 + 0.3)]}{1 + 0.3}\)

\(exp [– 1.3 NTU_1] = 1 – 1.3 × 0.37 = 0.519\)
or \(NTU_1 = \frac{ln (0.519)}{− 1.3} = 0.50\)

Further, \(NTU_1 = \frac{UA_1}{C_{min}}=\frac{U(\pi d L_1)}{C_{min}}\)
or \(\frac{U \pi d}{C_{min}}= \frac{NTU_1}{L_1}= \frac{0.50}{1} =0.5\)
The quantity \(\frac{U \pi d}{C_{min}}\) is a constant quantity for heat exchanger.
Case II. After increasing the length of heat exchanger,
(a) Flow rates are same,
(b) Properties are unchanged.
(c) Dimension other than length are also unchanged.
Therefore,
\(C = 0.3, C_{min} = C_h and C_{max} = C_c\)
The effectiveness of heat exchanger in new case
\(ε_2 = \frac{ C_h(T_{h, i} – T_{h, o})}{ C_{min }(T_{h, i} – T_{c, i}) }=\frac{150 – 75}{150 – 15} =\frac{75}{135}= 0.37\)
=0.55
and \(0.55 = =\frac{1 – exp [ – NTU_2 (1 + 0.3)]}{1 + 0.3}\)
or \(NTU_2 = 0.965\)
Further,
\(NTU_2 = \frac{UA_2}{ C_{min}}=\frac{0.965}{0.5}= 0.5 L_2\)
or \(L_2 = \frac{NTU_2}{0.5}=\frac{0.965}{0.5}=1.93 m.\)
The outlet temperature of water :
\(ε_2=\frac{ C_c(T_{c, o} – T_{c, i})}{ C_{min }(T_{h, i} – T_{c, i}) }\)
or \(T_{c, o} = T_{c, i} + ε_2 \frac{C_{min}}{C_c}(T_{h, i} – T_{c, i})\)
= 15 + 0.55 × 0.3 × (150 – 15)
= 37.27°C.
