Guest Question.

The elementary reaction

\(A + R \rightarrow 2 R\)

with a rate constant k = 0.02 L·\(mol ^{-1} \cdot s ^{-1}\) is to be carried out isothermally in a plug-flow reactor having a working volume of 200 L. We wish to process 120 L/min of a feed containing 5 mol/L of A to the highest conversion possible in the reactor with any feed arrangement. Find the optimum conversion achievable.

Admin
Solution

As discussed in , this is an autocatalytic reaction that requires the product in the reactor to proceed. Stoichiometry leads to

\(C_{A}+C_{R}=C_{A 0}+C_{R 0}=C_{A 0} \) (E5.6.1)

The reaction rate is given by

\(r_{A}=v_{A} r=-k C_{A} C_{R}=-k C_{A}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A}\right)\) (E5.6.2)

When PFR is used, one must recycle the final product stream back to the feed in order for the reaction to proceed in the reactor.
Fig. E5.6.1 shows a sketch of the reactor system.

A simple split at ➁ is used for recycle the effluent stream to the feed point ➀. Mass balance around point ➀ (mixing for feed into the reactor) leads to

\(Q_{\text {PFR Feed }}=(1+R) Q\) (E5.6.3)

and the concentration of A in the feed into PFR is given by

\(C_{A i}=\frac{Q C_{A 0}+R Q C_{A e}}{(1+R) Q}=\frac{C_{A 0}+R C_{A e}}{1+R}\) (E5.6.4)

Mole balance of A in a differential volume within the PFR (V, V + dV) leads to

\(– d \left[(1+R) Q C_{A}\right]+r_{ A } d V=0\) (E5.6.5)

Since Q is constant, substituting Eq. (E5.6.2) \(r_{A}=v_{A} r=-k C_{A} C_{R}=-k C_{A}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A}\right)\) into (E5.6.5) \(– d \left[(1+R) Q C_{A}\right]+r_{ A } d V=0\) and rearranging, we obtain

\(k d V=\frac{(1+R) Q d C_{A}}{C_{A}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A}\right)}=-\left\lgroup \frac{1}{C_{A}}-\frac{1}{C_{A 0}-C_{A}} \right\rgroup \frac{(1+R) Q d C_{A}}{C_{A 0}}\) (E5.6.6)

Integrating Eq. (E5.6.6) \(k d V=\frac{(1+R) Q d C_{A}}{C_{A}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A}\right)}=-\left\lgroup \frac{1}{C_{A}}-\frac{1}{C_{A 0}-C_{A}} \right\rgroup \frac{(1+R) Q d C_{A}}{C_{A 0}}\) around the PFR,

\(\int_{0}^{v} k d V=\int_{C_{A 1}}^{C_{Ae}}-\left\lgroup \frac{1}{C_{A}}-\frac{1}{C_{A 0}-C_{A}}\right\rgroup \frac{(1+R) Q d C_{A}}{C_{A 0}}\) (E5.6.7)

we obtain

\(k V=\frac{(1+R) Q}{C_{A 0}} \ln \frac{C_{A 1}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A e}\right)}{C_{A e}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A 1}\right)}\) (E5.6.8)

Substituting Eq. (E5.6.4) \(C_{A i}=\frac{Q C_{A 0}+R Q C_{A e}}{(1+R) Q}=\frac{C_{A 0}+R C_{A e}}{1+R}\) into (E5.6.8) \(k V=\frac{(1+R) Q}{C_{A 0}} \ln \frac{C_{A 1}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A e}\right)}{C_{A e}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A 1}\right)}\) , we obtain

\(k V=\frac{(1+R) Q}{C_{A 0}} \ln \frac{C_{A 0}+R C_{A e}}{R C_{A e}}\) (E5.6.9)

Eq. (E5.6.9) \(k V=\frac{(1+R) Q}{C_{A 0}} \ln \frac{C_{A 0}+R C_{A e}}{R C_{A e}}\) can be rearranged to give

\(C_{A e}=\frac{C_{A 0}}{\operatorname{Rexp}\left[\frac{k V C_{A 0}}{(1+R) Q}\right]-R}\) (E5.6.10)

or

\(\frac{C_{A 0}}{C_{A e}}=\operatorname{Rexp}\left[\frac{k V C_{A 0}}{(1+R) Q}\right]-R\) (E5.6.11)

The optimum recycle ratio is one that minimizes \(C_{ Ae }\) or maximizes \(C_{ A0 }\) / \(C_{ Ae }\). Substituting in the numbers in Eq. (E5.6.11), we have

\(\frac{C_{A 0}}{C_{A e}}=\operatorname{Rexp}\left[\frac{0.02 \times 200 \times 5}{(1+R) \times 120 / 60}\right]-R=\operatorname{Rexp}\left(\frac{10}{1+R}\right)-R\) (E5.6.12)

Maximizing the right-hand-side, we obtain R = 0.126994 and \(C_{ Ae }\) = 0.005517 mol/L. The optimum conversion is thus

\(f_{A e}=\frac{Q C_{A 0}-Q C_{A e}}{Q C_{A 0}}=\frac{C_{A 0}-C_{A e}}{C_{A 0}}=\frac{5-0.005517}{5}=0.9989\)

The conversion is at 99.89%.

Fig. E5.6.2 illustrate the recycle plug-flow reactor on the \(\left(-r_{ A }\right)^{-1}\) versus CA plot. The recycle of the effluent stream into the reactor caused the concentration of A entering the reactor to be lower than CA0 and thus ensures a finite value of \(\left(-r_{ A }\right)^{-1}\). Since the volume of the PFR is proportional to the area under the rate curve, the finite value of \(\left(-r_{ A }\right)^{-1}\) ensures a finite reactor volume requirement. For the fixed volume reactor, this ensures the concentration of the effluent to be lower than the feed concentration.
