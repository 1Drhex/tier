Finding the Performance Parameters of a Three-Phase Star Rectifier

A three-phase star rectifier has a purely resistive load with R ohms. Determine (a) the efficiency, (b) the FF, (c) the RF, (d) the TUF factor, (e) the PIV of each diode, and (f) the peak current through a diode if the rectifier delivers \(I_{dc} = 30 A \)at an output voltage of \(V_{dc }= 140 V\).

Admin
Solution

For a three-phase rectifier q = 3 in Eqs. (3.25) to (3.27)

\(V_{dc}=\frac{2}{2\pi /q}\int_{0}^{\pi /q}{V_m cos ωt d(ωt)}= V_m \frac{q}{\pi } \sin \frac{\pi }{q}\) (3.25)

 

\(V_{rms}= V_m\left[\frac{q}{2\pi }\left(\frac{\pi }{q}+\frac{1}{2} \sin\frac{2\pi }{q} \right) \right]^{1/2}\) (3.26)

 

\(I_s= I_m\left[\frac{1}{2\pi }\left(\frac{\pi }{q}+\frac{1}{2} \sin \frac{2\pi }{q} \right) \right]^{1/2} =\frac{V_{rms}}{R}\) (3.27)

a. From Eq. (3.25), \(V_{dc} = 0.827 V_m\) and \(I_{dc} = 0.827 V_m/R\). From Eq. (3.26), \(V_{rms }= 0.84068 V_m\). and \(I_{rms} = 0.84068 V_m/R\). From Eq. (3.1),

\(P_{dc} = V_{dc} I_{dc}\) (3.1)

\(P_{dc} = (0.827 V_m)^2/R;\) from Eq. (3.2),

\(P_{ac} = V_{rms} I_{rms}\) (3.2)

\(P_{ac} = (0.84068 V_m)^2/R;\) and from Eq. (3.3), the efficiency

\(\eta =\frac{P_{dc}}{P_{ac}}\) (3.3)

\(\eta =\frac{(0.827V_m)^2} {(0.84068V_m)^2}=96.77\%\)

b. From Eq. (3.5),

\(FF=\frac{V_{rms}}{V_{dc}}\) (3.5)

the \(FF = 0.84068/0.827 = 1.0165 or 101.65\%\).

c. From Eq. (3.7),

\(RF=\sqrt{\left(\frac{V_{rms}}{V_{dc}} \right)^2-1 }=\sqrt{FF^2 -1}\) (3.7)

the \(RF=\sqrt{1.0165^2 -1}=0.1824=18.24\%.\)
d. The rms voltage of the transformer secondary, \(V_s=V_m/\sqrt{2}=0.707 V_m.\) From Eq. (3.27), the rms current of the transformer secondary,
\(I_s=0.4854I_m=\frac{0.4854V_m}{R}\)
The VA rating of the transformer for q = 3 is

\(V_A=3V_sI_s=3\times 0.707V_m\times \frac{0.4854V_m}{R}\)

From Eq. (3.8),

\(TUF=\frac{P_dc}{V_sI_s}\) (3.8)

\(TUF=\frac{0.827^2}{3 \times 0.707 \times 0.4854}=0.6643\)

 

\(PF=\frac{0.84068^2}{3 \times 0.707 \times 0.4854}= 0.6644\)

e. The peak inverse voltage of each diode is equal to the peak value of the secondary line-to-line voltage. Three-phase circuits are reviewed in Appendix A. The line-to-line voltage is \(\sqrt{3}\) times the phase voltage and thus \(PIV = \sqrt{3} V_m.\)

f. The average current through each diode is

\(I_{D(av)}=\frac{2}{2\pi }\int_{0}^{\pi /q}{I_m cos ωt d(ωt)} = I_m\frac{1}{\pi } \sin \frac{\pi }{q}\) (3.28)

For q = 3, \(I_{D(av)} = 0.2757 I_m\). The average current through each diode is \(I_{D(av)} = 30/3 = 10 A\) and this gives the peak current as \(I_m = 10/0.2757 = 36.27 A.\)
