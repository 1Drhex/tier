Guest Question.

An L-shaped cantilever retaining wall constructed of reinforced concrete, with a density of 150 pcf, is eight feet tall from the base to the top of the soil. Assuming a soil density of 120 pcf and an equivalent fluid density of 40 pcf, determine the overturning stability of the wall. Check the bearing pressure beneath the toe at point A.

Admin
Solution

Pmax = ω’ × h

P = 1/2Pmax × h × 1′

Pmax = (40#/ft3)(8′) = 320#/ft2

P = 1/2 (320#/ft2)(8′)(1′) = 1280#

W1 =(1′)(8′)(1′)(150#/ft3) = 1200#

W2=(3′)(1′)(1′)(150#/ft3) = 450#

W3 =(3′)(7′)(1′)(150#/ft3) = 2520#

WT = 1200# + 450# + 2520# = 4170#

R = \(\sqrt{(4170)^{2} + (1280)^{2}} \) = 4362#

MRM = MA = W1 (0.5′) + W2(2.5′) + W3(2.5′)

MA = (1200#)(0.5′) + (450#)(2.5′) + (2520#) = 8025#-ft.

MA = WT × b = 8025#-ft.

b = \(\frac{8025\#-ft.}{4170\#}\) = 1.92′

MOTM = P × h/3 = (1280#)(2.67′) = 3418#-ft.

S.F. = \(\frac{M_{RM}}{M_{OTM}} = \frac{8025\#-ft.}{3418\#-ft}\) = 2.34 > 1.5 ∴ OK

a/3 = 4’/3 = 1.33′ 2a/3 = 2.67′

x = \(\frac{M_{RM} – M_{OTM}}{W_{T}} = \frac{(8025 – 3418)}{4170}\) = 1.1′

x < a/3

Pmax = \(\frac{2W}{3x} = \frac{2(4170)}{3(1.1')}\) = 2527#/ft2 < 3000PSf ∴ OK
