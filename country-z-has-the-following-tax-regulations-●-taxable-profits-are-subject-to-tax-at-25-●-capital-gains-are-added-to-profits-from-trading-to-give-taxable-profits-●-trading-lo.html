Guest Question.

Country Z has the following tax regulations:
● Taxable profits are subject to tax at 25%.
● Capital gains are added to profits from trading to give taxable profits.
● Trading losses can be carried forward indefinitely but cannot be carried back to previous years.
● Capital gains/losses cannot be offset against trading gains/losses or visa versa.
LL started trading in 2002 and has the following profits/losses

Trading profit/(loss) $000 Capital profit/(loss) $000
2002 (300) 400
2003 550 0
2004 700 (150)

Calculate the tax payable by LL in each year.

Admin
Solution
Trading profit/(loss) Capital profit/(loss) Taxable profit Tax due at 25%
2002 400 400 100
Loss carried forward (300)
2003 \( \left(550 – 300 \right) = 250 \) 0 250 62.50
Loss carried forward 0 0
2004 700 (150) 700 175
Loss carried forward 0 (150)

Note that in 2002 the trading loss cannot be offset against the capital gain and in 2004 the capital loss cannot be
offset against the trading profit. This is a very common situation that applies in many countries.
