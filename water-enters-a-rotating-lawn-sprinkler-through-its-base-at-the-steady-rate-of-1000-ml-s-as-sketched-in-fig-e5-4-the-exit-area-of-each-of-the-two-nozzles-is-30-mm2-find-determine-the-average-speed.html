Guest Question.

Water enters a rotating lawn sprinkler through its base at the steady rate of 1000 ml/s as sketched in Fig. E5.4. The exit area of each of the two nozzles is 30\( mm^{2} \).

FIND Determine the average speed of the water leaving the nozzle, relative to the nozzle, if
(a) the rotary sprinkler head is stationary,
(b) the sprinkler head rotates at 600 rpm, and
(c) the sprinkler head accelerates from 0 to 600 rpm.

Admin
Solution

(a) We specify a control volume that contains the water in the rotary sprinkler head at any instant. This control volume is nondeforming, but it moves (rotates) with the sprinkler head.

The application of \(\frac{\partial}{\partial t} \int_{cv}{\rho dV } +\sum{\rho _{out}A_{out}W_{out}} -\sum{\rho _{in}A_{in}W_{in}} =0 \) to the contents of this control volume for situation (a), (b), or (c) of the problem results in the same expression, namely,

\(\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\int\limits_{cv}{\rho dV}=0\) ( flow is steady or the control volume is filled with an incompressible fluid)

\(\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\int\limits_{cv}{\rho dV}+\sum{\rho _{out}A_{out}W_{out}}-\sum{\rho _{in}A_{in}W_{in}} =0\) (1)

The time rate of change of the mass of water in the control volume is zero because the flow is steady and the control volume is filled with water.

Because there is only one inflow [at the base of the rotating arm, section (1)] and two outflows [the two nozzles at the tips of the arm, sections (2) and (3), each with the same area and fluid velocity], Eq. 1 becomes

\( \rho _{2}A_{2}W_{2}+\rho _{3}A_{3}W_{3}-\rho _{1}A_{1}W_{1}=0 \) (2)

Hence, for incompressible flow with \( \rho _{1}=\rho _{2}=\rho _{3} \), Eq. 2 becomes

\( A_{2}W_{2}+A_{3}W_{3}-A_{1}W_{1}=0 \)

With \(Q=A_{1}W_{1}, A_{2}=A_{3}, and W_{2}=W_{3} \) it follows that

\( W_{2}=\frac{Q}{2A_{2}} \)

or

\( W_{2}=\frac{(1000{ml}/{s})(0.001{m^{3}}/{liter})(10^{6}{mm^{2}}/{m^{2}})}{(1000{ml}/{liter})(2)(30mm^{2})} =16.7{m}/{s} \)

(b), (c) The value of \( W_{2} \) is independent of the speed of rotation of the sprinkler head and represents the average velocity of the water exiting from each nozzle with respect to the nozzle for cases (a), (b), and (c).

COMMENT The velocity of water discharging from each nozzle, when viewed from a stationary reference (i.e., \( V_{2} \)), will vary as the rotation speed of the sprinkler head varies since from \(V=W+V_{cv}\)

\( V_{2}=W_{2}-U \)

where U = ωR is the speed of the nozzle and ω and R are the angular velocity and radius of the sprinkler head, respectively.
