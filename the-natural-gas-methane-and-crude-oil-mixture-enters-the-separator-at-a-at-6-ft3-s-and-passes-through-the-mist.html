Guest Question.

The natural gas (methane) and crude oil mixture enters the separator at A at \( 6 ft^3/s \)and passes through the mist extractor at B. Crude oil flows out at 800 gal/min through the pipe at C, and natural gas leaves the 2-in-diameter pipe at D at \(V_D = 300 ft/s \). Determine the specific weight of the mixture that enters the separator at A. The process takes place at a constant temperature of 68°F. Take \(\rho o = 1.71 slug/ft^3, \rho _me = 1.291(10^{-3}) slug/ft^3. Note 1 ft^3 = 7.48 gal. \)

Admin
Solution

The control volume is fixed which is the volume of the crude oil and natural gas contained in the tank. Here, the flow is steady. Thus, no local changes take place. Also, the densities of the gas oil mixture, gas and oil separation are constant, and the average velocities will be used.

\(\frac{\partial }{\partial t}+\int_{cv} \rho dV + \int_{cs} \rho = 0\)

 

\(0 – \rho _{mix}V_AA_A + \rho _{CD}V_C A_C + \rho _{me}V_D A_D = 0 \) (1)

From Appendix A, \(\rho_{CD} = 1.71 slug/ft^3 and \rho m = 1.29(10^{-3}) slug/ft^3 \) when

\(T = 68°F. Also, Q_C = V_CA_C =(800\frac{gal}{min})(\frac{1 ft^3}{7.48 gal})(\frac{1 min}{60 s}) = 1.783 ft^3/s \) and

\(Q_A = V_AA_A = 6 ft^3/s.\) Substituting these results into Eq. (1),

\(-\rho _{mix}(6 ft^3/s) + (1.71 slug/ft^3)(1.783 ft^3/s) \)

 

\(+ [1.29(10^{-3}) slug/ft^3] (300 ft/s) [ \pi (\frac{1}{12} ft)^2] =0 \)

 

\(\rho _{mix} = 0.5094 slug/ft^3 \)

 

\(\gamma _{mix} = \rho _{mix} g = (0.5094 slug/ft^3)(32.2 ft/s^2) = 16.4 lb/ft^3\)
