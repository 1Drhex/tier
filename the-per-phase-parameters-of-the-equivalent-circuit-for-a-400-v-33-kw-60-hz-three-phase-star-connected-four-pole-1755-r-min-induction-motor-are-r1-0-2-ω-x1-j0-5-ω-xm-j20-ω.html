Guest Question.

The per-phase parameters of the equivalent circuit for a 400 V, 33 kW, 60 Hz, three-phase, star-connected, four-pole, 1755 r/min induction motor are: \(R_{1} = 0.2 Ω , X_{1} = j 0.5 Ω , X_{m} = j 20 Ω , R^{′}_{2} = 0.1 Ω , X^{′}_{2} = 0.2 Ω\). If the mechanical losses (windage and friction) are 650 W and iron losses are 300 W, determine the following when the motor operates at full load at rated speed:

(a) the synchronous speed;
(b) the slip;
(c) the input current and power factor;
(d) the input power;
(e) the useful mechanical output power;
(f) the output torque;
(g) the efficiency of the motor.

Admin
Solution

(a) Synchronous speed:

 

\(n_{s}=120\frac{f}{P}=120\frac{60}{4}=1800 r/ min\)

 

(b) Slip:

 

\(s=\frac{n_{s}-n_{r}}{n_{s}}=\frac{1800-1755}{1800} =0.025\)

 

(c) The calculation of current requires the impedance of the motor at a slip of 0.025 to be found from the per-phase equivalent circuit. The simplified equivalent circuit of Fig. 33.6 is used here, in Fig. 33.7. (The magnetizing reactance, \(j 20 Ω\), may alternatively, for improved accuracy, be placed between \(X_{1} and X^{′}_{2}\).)

 

The rotor impedance at a slip of 0.025 is:

 

\(Z_{r}=R^{\prime }_{2}+j X^{\prime }_{2}+\frac{1-s}{s} R^{\prime }_{2} \\ = \frac{R^{\prime }_{2}}{s}+ j X^{\prime }_{2} =\left(\frac{0.1}{0.025}+j 0.2 \right) \\ =(4+j 0.2 ) \Omega \)

 

This is now added to the stator impedance:

 

\(Z_{s}+Z_{r}=0.2 +j 0.5+4+j0.2 \Omega \\ =4.2 +j 0.7 \Omega \)

 

This impedance is now combined with the parallel magnetizing branch impedance, \(j 20 Ω\) , by addition of admittances:

 

\(Y_{r+s}=\frac{1}{Z_{r}+Z_{s}}=\frac{1}{4.2+j 0.7} \\ = \frac{4.2-j 0.7}{4.2^2+0.7^2}\\ = (0.232-j 0.039 )\Omega ^{-1}\)

 

Magnetizing admittance:

 

\(Y_{m}=\frac{1}{jX_{m}}=\frac{1}{j 20}= – j 0.05 \Omega ^{-1}\)

 

Total admittance:

 

\(Y_{t} = Y_{r + s} + Y_{m} = (0.232 – j 0.089) Ω^{- 1}\)

 

Equivalent impedance:

 

\(Z_{t}=\frac{1}{Y_{t}}=\frac{1}{(0.232-j 0.089)} \\ = \frac{1}{0.248\angle -21^\circ } = 4.03 \angle 21^\circ \\ = (3.76+j 1.44) \Omega\)

 

As the machine is star connected, the per-phase voltage is

 

\(\frac{400}{\sqrt{3} }= 230.9 V\)

 

Taking the voltage as the reference phasor the current can be found:

 

\(I=\frac{V}{Z_{t}}=\frac{230.9}{4.03\angle 21^\circ } =57.3\angle (-21^\circ ) A= (53.5-j 20.5) A\)

 

The load current is therefore 57.3 A at a power factor of 0.93 lagging.

 

(d) Input power is

 

\(P_{in}=\sqrt{3}V_{L}I_{L}\cos \theta =\sqrt{3}\times 400\times 57.3\times 0.93=36918 W\)

 

(e) The useful output power is found by calculating the losses and subtracting these from the input power:

 

Stator copper loss \(= 3 × I^{2}_{1} R_{1} = 3 × 57.3^{2} × 0.2 = 1970 W\)

 

Core iron loss \(= 300 W\).

 

Airgap power \(= (P_{in} – stator losses ) = (36 918 – 2270) W = 34.65 kW\)

 

Rotor copper loss \(= 3 × I^{2}_{1} R^{′}_{2} = 3 × 57.3^2 × 0.1 = 985 W\)

 

The useful output power is further reduced by windage and friction loss of 650 W.

 

The useful power output is therefore

 

\(P_{o} = 36 918 – (1970 + 300 + 985 + 650) = 33 kW\)

 

(f) The mechanical speed in rad/s is

 

\(\omega _{m}= 1755 \times \frac{2\pi }{60}=183.78 rad / s \)

 

Therefore, useful output torque is

 

\(M= \frac{P_{o}}{\omega _{m}} =\frac{33000}{183.78}= 179.6 N m\)

 

(g) Efficiency , \(\eta =\frac{P_{o}}{P_{in}}=\frac{33000}{36918}\times 100 per cent = 89.4 per cent \)
