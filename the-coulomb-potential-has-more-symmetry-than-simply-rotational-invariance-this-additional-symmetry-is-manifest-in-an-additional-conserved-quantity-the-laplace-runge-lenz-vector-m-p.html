Guest Question.

The Coulomb potential has more symmetry than simply rotational invariance. This additional symmetry is manifest in an additional conserved quantity, the Laplace–Runge–Lenz vector

\( \hat{ M }=\frac{\hat{ p } \times \hat{ L }-\hat{ L } \times \hat{ p }}{2 m}+V(r) r \),

where V(r) is the potential energy, \( V(r)=-e^{2} / 4 \pi \epsilon_{0} r \). The complete set of commutators for the conserved quantities in the hydrogen atom is

\( \text { (i) }\left[\hat{H}, \hat{M}_{i}\right]=0 \),

\( \text { (ii) } \quad\left[\hat{H}, \hat{L}_{i}\right]=0 \),

\( \text { (iii) } \quad\left[\hat{L}_{i}, \hat{L}_{j}\right]=i \hbar \epsilon_{i j k} \hat{L}_{k} \),

\( \text { (iv) } \quad\left[\hat{L}_{i}, \hat{M}_{j}\right]=i \hbar \epsilon_{i j k} \hat{M}_{k} \),

\( \text { (v) } \quad\left[\hat{M}_{i}, \hat{M}_{j}\right]=\frac{\hbar}{i} \epsilon_{i j k} L_{k} \frac{2}{m} \hat{H} \).

The physical content of these equations is that (i) M is a conserved quantity, (ii) L is a conserved quantity, (iii) L is a vector, and (iv) M is a vector ((v) has no obvious interpretation). There are two additional relations between the quantities \( \hat{ L }, \hat{ M }, \text { and } \hat{H} \). They are

\( \text { (vi) } \quad \hat{M}^{2}=\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)^{2}+\frac{2}{m} \hat{H}\left(\hat{L}^{2}+\hbar^{2}\right) \),

\( \text { (vii) } \quad \hat{ M } \cdot \hat{ L }=0 \).

(a) From the result of , and the fact that \( \hat{ M } \) is a conserved
quantity, we know that \( \hat{M}_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}=c_{n \ell} \psi_{n(\ell+1)(\ell+1)} \) for some constants \( c_{n} \ell \text {. Apply (vii) to the state } \psi_{n \ell \ell} \text { to show that } \)

\( \hat{M}_{z} \psi_{n \ell \ell}=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \frac{1}{\sqrt{\ell+1}} c_{n \ell} \psi_{n(\ell+1) \ell} \).

(b) Use (vi) to show that

\( \hat{M}_{-} \hat{M}_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}=\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)^{2}\left[1-\left(\frac{\ell+1}{n}\right)^{2}\right] \psi_{n \ell \ell}-\hat{M}_{z}^{2} \psi_{n \ell \ell} \).

(c) From your results to parts (a) and (b), obtain the constants \( c_{n \ell} . \) You
should find that \( c_{n \ell} \) is nonzero unless \( \ell=n-1 \). Hint: Consider \( \int\left|M_{+} \psi_{n \ell m}\right|^{2} d^{3} r \) and use the fact that M ± re Hermitian conjugates. Figure 6.9 shows how the degenerate states of hydrogen are related by the generators \( \hat{ L } \text { and } \hat{ M } \).

Solution

(a) We first write

\( M \cdot L =M_{x} L_{x}+M_{y} L_{y}+M_{z} L_{z} \).

\( =\frac{M_{+}+M_{-}}{2} \frac{L_{+}+L_{-}}{2}+\frac{M_{+}-M_{-}}{2 i} \frac{L_{+}-L_{-}}{2 i}+M_{z} L_{z} \).

\( =\frac{M_{+} L_{-}+M_{-} L_{+}}{2}+M_{z} L_{z} \).

Now we apply this to \( \psi_{n \ell \ell} \) to get

\( \frac{1}{2} M_{+} L_{-} \psi_{n \ell \ell}+\frac{1}{2} M_{-} L_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}+M_{z} L_{z} \psi_{n \ell \ell}=0 \).

\( \frac{1}{2}\left(L_{-} M_{+}+\left[M_{+}, L_{-}\right]\right) \psi_{n \ell \ell}+0+\ell \hbar M_{z} \psi_{n \ell \ell}=0 \).

\( \frac{1}{2} L_{-} c_{n \ell} \psi_{n \ell+1 \ell+1}+\frac{1}{2}\left[M_{+}, L_{-}\right] \psi_{n \ell \ell}+\ell \hbar M_{z} \psi_{n \ell \ell}=0 \).

The commutator (remember M is a vector operator) is given in Equation 6.54.

\( \left[\hat{L}_{\pm}, \hat{V}_{\mp}\right]=\pm 2 \hbar \hat{V}_{z} \) (6.54).

\( \frac{1}{2} B_{\ell+1}^{\ell} c_{n \ell} \psi_{n \ell+1 \ell}+\hbar M_{z} \psi_{n \ell \ell}+\ell \hbar M_{z} \psi_{n \ell \ell}=0 \).

\( \sqrt{\frac{\ell+1}{2}} \hbar c_{n \ell} \psi_{n \ell \ell-1}=-(\ell+1) \hbar M_{z} \psi_{n \ell \ell} \).

so that

\( \hat{M}_{z} \psi_{n \ell \ell}=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2(\ell+1)}} c_{n \ell} \psi_{n \ell+1 \ell} \).

(b) Note that, just as for M. L in part (a),

\( M \cdot M =\frac{M_{+} M_{-}+M_{-} M_{+}}{2}+M_{z}^{2} \).

Then from vi,

\( M^{2} \psi_{n \ell \ell}=\left[\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)^{2}+\frac{2}{m} \hat{H}\left(L^{2}+\hbar^{2}\right)\right] \psi_{n \ell \ell} \).

\( \frac{1}{2} M_{+} M_{-} \psi_{n \ell \ell}+\frac{1}{2} M_{-} M_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}+M_{z}^{2} \psi_{n \ell \ell}=\left\{\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)^{2}+\frac{2}{m} E_{n}[\ell(\ell+1)+1] \hbar^{2}\right\} \psi_{n \ell \ell} \).

\( \frac{1}{2}\left[M_{+}, M_{-}\right] \psi_{n \ell \ell}+M_{-} M_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}+M_{z}^{2} \psi_{n \ell \ell}=\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)^{2}\left[1-\frac{\ell^{2}+\ell+1}{n^{2}}\right] \psi_{n \ell \ell} \).

We can work out the commutator from v,

\( \left[M_{+}, M_{-}\right]=\left[M_{x}, M_{x}\right]+i\left[M_{y}, M_{x}\right]-i\left[M_{x}, M_{y}\right]-\left[M_{y}, M_{y}\right] \).

\( =-2 i\left[M_{x}, \mid M_{y}\right]=-2 \hbar L_{z} \frac{2}{m} \hat{H} \).

Therefore we have

\( -\hbar L_{z} \frac{2}{m} \hat{H} \psi_{n \ell \ell}+M_{-} M_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}+M_{z}^{2} \psi_{n \ell \ell}=\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)^{2}\left[1-\frac{\ell^{2}+\ell+1}{n^{2}}\right] \psi_{n \ell \ell} \).

\( -\hbar \ell \hbar \frac{2}{m} E_{n} \psi_{n \ell \ell}+M_{-} M_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}+M_{z}^{2} \psi_{n \ell \ell}=\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)^{2}\left[1-\frac{\ell^{2}+\ell+1}{n^{2}}\right] \psi_{n \ell \ell} \).

so that

\( \hat{M}_{-} \hat{M}_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}=\left\{\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)^{2}\left[1-\left(\frac{\ell+1}{n}\right)^{2}\right]-\hat{M}_{z}^{2}\right\} \psi_{n \ell \ell} \).

(c) Finally we note that

\( \left|c_{n \ell}\right|^{2}=\int\left(M_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}\right)^{*}\left(M_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell}\right) d^{3} r \).

\( =\int \psi_{n \ell \ell}^{*} M_{-} M_{+} \psi_{n \ell \ell} d^{3} r \).

From part (b) this is

\( \left|c_{n \ell}\right|^{2}=\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)^{2}\left[1-\left(\frac{\ell+1}{n}\right)^{2}\right]-\int \psi_{n \ell \ell}^{*} M_{z}^{2} \psi_{n \ell \ell} d^{3} r \).

\( =\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)^{2}\left[1-\left(\frac{\ell+1}{n}\right)^{2}\right]-\int\left(M_{z} \psi_{n \ell \ell}\right)^{*} M_{z} \psi_{n \ell \ell} d^{3} r \).

and from part (a) this is

\( \left|c_{n \ell}\right|^{2}=\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)^{2}\left[1-\left(\frac{\ell+1}{n}\right)^{2}\right]-\frac{1}{2(\ell+1)}\left|c_{n \ell}\right|^{2} \underbrace{\int \psi_{n \ell+1 \ell}^{*} \psi_{n \ell+1 \ell} d^{3} r }_{1} \).

Finally, solving for \( c_{n \ell} \text { gives } \)

\( \left|c_{n \ell}\right|^{2}=\frac{2 \ell+3}{2 \ell+2}\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)^{2}\left[1-\left(\frac{\ell+1}{n}\right)^{2}\right] \).

As we see, this nonzero except when \(\ell=n-1\).
