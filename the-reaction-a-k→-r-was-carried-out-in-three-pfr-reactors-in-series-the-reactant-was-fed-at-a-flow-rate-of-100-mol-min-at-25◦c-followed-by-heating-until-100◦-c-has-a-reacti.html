Guest Question.

The reaction A \(\overset{k}{\rightarrow} R\) was carried out in three PFR reactors in series. The reactant was fed at a flow rate of 100 mol/min at \(25^{\circ} C\), followed by heating until \(100^{\circ} C\). Has a reaction taken place in the first reactor during this heating phase?
Then, the reactor operated adiabatically until a temperature of \(650^{\circ} C\). When \(650^{\circ} C\) was reached, the reactor operated isothermally releasing heat. The final conversion was 85%.
Considerations and questions

1. The system comprises three PFR reactors in series, the first is nonisothermal, the second is adiabatic, and the third is isothermal. What are the inlet and outlet conversions in the second reactor (adiabatic)?

2. Calculate the volume of the isothermal reactor.

3. Calculate the energy required to heat the first reactor and also the heat required to maintain the third reactor isothermal.

Data:
\(k= 4 × 10^{6} e^{−8000/T}min^{−1}\)
\(\Delta H_{R} =−150 kJ/mol\)
\(\bar{C}_{PA} =\bar{C}_{PR}= 180 J/(molK)\)
\(Heat\, exchange = 300 J/(m^{2}s)\)
\(Heat\, exchange\, area = 1 m^{2}\)

Admin
Solution

1. First reactor–Heating from \(25^{\circ}C\) to \(100^{\circ}C\) The energy balance in PFR yields:

\(\sum F_{i}\bar{C}_{Pi}(T − T_{0}) + \Delta H_{R}F_{A0}X_{A} = Q = UA × \Delta T\) (17.34)
But

\(\sum F_{i}\bar{C}_{Pi} =F_{A}\bar{C}_{PA} + F_{R}\bar{C}_{PR} =F_{A0}(1 − X_{A})\bar{C}_{PA} +F_{A0}X_{A}\bar{C}_{PR}\) (17.35)
Since \(\bar{C}_{PA} = \bar{C}_{PR} =180 J/mol\)

\(\sum F_{i}\bar{C}_{Pi} =F_{A0}\bar{C}_{P} =1.8 × 10^{4} J/min\)
The second term:

\(\Delta H_{R}F_{A0} =150 × 10^{3} × 100= 1.5 × 10^{7}J/min\)
The third term:
\(Q=UA · \Delta T =300 × 60 × 1 × 100=1.8 × 10^{6}\)

\(Q=UA × \Delta T =300.60.1.100=1.8.10^{6}\)

Substituting the values into Equation 17.35, we calculate the outlet conversion in the
first reactor. In this case,
\(X_{A1} =0\)
Therefore, no conversion occurred during the heating.
In the second reactor, operated adiabatically, the temperature increases but does
not exceed \(650^{\circ}C= 923K.\)
With the energy balance in an adiabatic system, we deduce:
\(\frac{T}{T_{0}} =1 + \beta · X_{A}\) (17.36)
where:
\(\beta =\frac{-\Delta H_{R}. F_{A0}}{\sum F_{i}\bar{C_{Pi}}T_{0}} =\frac{1.5 × 10^{7}}{1.8 × 10^{4} × 393} =2.12\) (17.37)
Then,
\(\frac{T}{T_{0}} =1 + 2.12 X_{A}\)
If the maximum temperature is \(650^{\circ}C=923 K\) , we obtain:
\(\frac{T}{T_{0}} =2.34\)

Therefore, the outlet conversion in the adiabatic reactor is:
\(X_{A2} = 0.636\)
The third reactor in series operates isothermally and its inlet and outlet conversions have been calculated and specified above. Then, by the molar balance in a PFR:
\(\tau = C_{A0}\int_{0}^{X_{A}}\frac{dX_{A}}{(-r_{A})}\) (17.38)
where:
\((-r_{A})=k · C_{A} =k · C_{A0}(1 − X_{A})\)
Substituting the rate expression into Equation 17.38 and integrating between conversion limits in the third reactor:
\(\tau _{3} =-\frac{1}{k}\left [ \ln \left ( \frac{1- X_{Afinal}}{1-X_{A2}} \right ) \right ]\) (17.39)
Since the third reactor operates isothermally at 923 K, we need to calculate k (specific reaction rate):
\(k=4.10^{6}.e^{−8000/T}\)
Thus,
\(k=0.688 min^{−1}\)
Therefore, with Equation 17.29, we calculate:

Eq (17.29): \((-r_{A})=kC^{2}_{A}=kC^{2}_{A0}\frac{(1-X_{A})^{2}}{(1+\varepsilon _{A}X_{A})^{2}} \)

\(\tau _{3} =1.31 min\)
Obtaining the initial concentration:
\(F_{A0} =C_{A0} · \upsilon _{0} =100\)
and
\(C_{A0} = \frac{P}{RT} =4.09 × 10^{−2}\)
and
\(\upsilon _{0} =2.44 × 10^{3} L/min\)
Therefore, the volume of the third reactor will be:
\(V_{3} =3.2m^{3}\)
