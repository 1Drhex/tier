Guest Question.

A generator is supplying power to a load centre through a transmission line as shown in Fig. 1. The power output of the generator is increased slowly while maintaining the magnitudes of the voltages \(V_{1}\) and \(V_{2}\)constants at 1.0 p.u. by manual control (of both gene ator excitation and infinite bus voltage). Find the steady state stability limit of power that can be transmitted. Assume \(x_{t}=0.1, x_{l}=0.4, Z_{T}=j0.1, x_{g}=0.3\).

Admin
Solution

The equivalent circuit for the system is shown in Fig. 1 where

\(x_{1}=x_{g}, x=x_{t}+x_{l}, x_{2}=\wp \left [ Z_{T} \right ]\)

Note that the system is lossless. The expression for power \(p_{e}\) is given by

\(p_{e}=\frac{E_{g}E_{b}\sin \delta }{\left ( x_{1}+x+x_{2} \right )}\)

However, the above expression cannot be used directly as \(E_{g}\) and \(E_{b}\) are unknown. The phasor diagram at the stability limit \(\left ( \delta =90^{\circ} \right )\)is shown in Fig.2.It is easy to see that at the stability limit,

\(P_{e}=\frac{E_{g}V_{1}}{x_{1}}\sin \left ( 90-\theta _{1} \right )=\frac{E_{g}V_{2}}{\left ( x+x_{1} \right )}\sin \left ( 90-\theta _{2} \right )\)

from which,

\(\frac{V_{1}}{x_{1}}\cos \theta _{1}=\frac{V_{2}}{\left ( x+x_{1} \right )}\cos \theta _{2} \left ( A\right )\)

Also,

\(P_{e}=\frac{E_{b}V_{1}\sin \theta _{1}}{\left ( x+x_{2} \right )}=\frac{E_{b}V_{2}\sin \theta _{2}}{x_{2}}\)

from which,

\(\frac{V_{1}\sin \theta _{1}}{\left ( x+x_{2} \right )}=\frac{V_{2}\sin \theta _{2}}{x_{2}} \left ( B \right )\)

From the two relations (A) and (B) we have

\(\left. \begin{matrix}\sin \theta _{1}=a\sin \theta _{2}\\\cos \theta _{1}=b\cos\theta _{2}\end{matrix} \right \} \left ( C \right )\)

where

\(a=\frac{V_{2}}{V_{1}}\frac{\left ( x+x_{2} \right )}{x_{2}},b=\frac{V_{2}}{V_{1}}\frac{x_{1}}{\left ( x+x_{1} \right )}\)

Substituting (C) in the following equation

\(\sin ^{2}\theta _{1}+\cos ^{2}\theta _{1}=1 \left ( D \right )\)

we get,

\(a^{2}\sin ^{2}\theta _{2}+b^{2}\cos ^{2}=1\left ( a^{2}-b^{2} \right )\sin ^{2}\theta _{2}=1-b^{2}\)
In the example, \(x_{1}=0.3, x_{2}=0.1, x=0.5, V_{1}=V_{2}=1.0\)

\(a=\frac{0.5+0.1}{0.1}=6.0, b=\frac{0.3}{0.8}=0.375\) \(\sin \theta _{2}=\sqrt{\frac{\left ( 1-b^{2} \right )}{\left ( a^{2}-b^{2} \right )}}=0.1548, \theta _{2}=8.91^{\circ}\sin \theta _{1}=6\sin \theta _{2}=0.9288, \theta _{1}=68.25^{\circ}\)

The stability limit is also given by

\(P_{emax}=\frac{V_{1}V_{2}}{x}\sin \left ( \theta _{1}-\theta _{2 } \right )\) \(=\frac{1.0}{0.5}\sin\left ( 68.25^{\circ}-8.91^{\circ} \right )=1.7204 p.u\)

\(E_{g}\) and \(E_{b}\) can be calculated from

\(P_{emax}=\frac{E_{g}V_{1}}{x_{1}}\cos \theta _{1}=\frac{E_{b}V_{1}\sin \theta _{1}}{\left ( x+x_{2} \right )}\)

The values are \(E_{g}=1.393, E_{b}=1.111\). It can be verified that

\(P_{emax}=\frac{E_{g}E_{b}}{\left ( x_{1}+x+x_{2} \right )}\)
