Guest Question.

In a flat belt drive the initial tension is 2000 N. The coefficient of friction between the belt and the pulley is 0.3 and the angle of lap on the smaller pulley is \(150^{\circ}\). The smaller pulley has a radius of 200 mm and rotates at 500 r.p.m. Find the power in kW transmitted by the belt.

Admin
Solution

Given : \(T_{0}=2000 N ; \mu_{0}=0.3 ; \theta=150^{\circ}=150^{\circ} \times \pi / 180=2.618 rad ; r_{2}=200 mm\) or \(d_{2}=400 mm =0.4 m ; N_{2}=500 r.p.m\).

We know that velocity of the belt,

\(v=\frac{\pi d_{2} \cdot N_{2}}{60}=\frac{\pi \times 0.4 \times 500}{60}=10.47 m / s\)

Let \(T_{1}\) = Tension in the tight side of the belt, and

\(T_{2}\) = Tension in the slack side of the belt.

We know that initial tension \(\left(T_{0}\right)\),

\(2000=\frac{T_{1}+T_{2}}{2} \quad \text { or } \quad T_{1}+T_{2}=4000 N\) …(i)

We also know that

\(2.3 \log \left(\frac{T_{1}}{T_{2}}\right)=\mu . \theta=0.3 \times 2.618=0.7854\)

 

\(\log \left(\frac{T_{1}}{T_{2}}\right)=\frac{0.7854}{2.3}=0.3415\)

or

\(\frac{T_{1}}{T_{2}}=2.2\) …(ii) …(Taking antilog of 0.3415)

From equations (i) and (ii),

\(T_{1}=2750 N\)

and \(T_{2}=1250 N\)

\(\therefore \) Power transmitted, \(P=\left(T_{1}-T_{2}\right) v\)

= (2750 – 1250) 10.47

= 15 700 W =15.7 kW
