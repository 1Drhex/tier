Guest Question.

For the case of double-circuit, bundle-conductor lines, the same method indicated in applies with \(r^{\prime}\) replaced by the bundle’s GMR in the calculation of the overall GMR.
Now consider a double-circuit configuration shown in Figure 4.36 that belongs to a 500-kV, three-phase line with bundle conductors of three subconductors at 21 in. spacing. The GMR of each subconductor is given to be 0.0485 ft.
Determine the inductive reactance of the line in ohms per mile per phase. You may use

\(X _{ L }=0.2794 \log \frac{ GMD }{ GMR } \Omega / mi / phase\)

 

 

Admin
Solution

With \(N=3, S=21^{\prime \prime}, A=\frac{S}{2 \sin 60^{\circ}}=\frac{21 / 12}{2 \times 0.866}=1.0104 ft\)

Conductor \(G M R=0.0485 ft\)

Bundle \(G M R=\left[3(0.0485)(1.0104)^{2}\right]^{1 / 3}=0.5296 ft\)

Then

\(\begin{aligned}&r_{A}^{\prime}=\left[\left(G M R_{b}\right)\left(D_{A A^{\prime}}\right)\right]^{1 / 2}=\left(0.5296 \times \sqrt{32^{2}+36^{2}}\right)^{1 / 2}=5.05 ft \\&r_{B}^{\prime}=\left(G M R_{b} \cdot D_{B B^{\prime}}\right)^{1 / 2}=(0.5296 \times 96)^{1 / 2}=7.13 ft \\&r_{C}^{\prime}=\left(G M R_{b} \cdot D_{C C^{\prime}}\right)^{1 / 2}=\left[0.5296 \times \sqrt{32^{2}+36^{2}}\right]^{1 / 2}=5.05 ft\end{aligned}\)

Overall Phase \(G M R=\left(r_{A}^{\prime} r_{B}^{\prime} r_{C}^{\prime}\right)^{1 / 3}=5.67 ft\)

\(\begin{aligned}D_{A B_{e q}} &=\left[\sqrt{32^{2}+36^{2}} \cdot \sqrt{64^{2}+36^{2}} \cdot(64)(32)\right]^{1 / 4}=51.88 ft \\D_{B C_{e q}} &=\left[(32) \sqrt{64^{2}+36^{2}} \cdot 64 \cdot \sqrt{32^{2}+36^{2}}\right]^{1 / 4}=51.88 ft \\D_{A C_{e q}} &=[(36)(32)(36)(32)]^{1 / 4}=33.94 ft \\& \therefore G M D=[51.88 \times 51.88 \times 33.94]^{1 / 3}=45.04 ft\end{aligned}\)

Then \(X_{L}=0.2794 \log \left(\frac{45.04}{5.67}\right)=0.2515 \Omega / mi / phase \leftarrow\)
