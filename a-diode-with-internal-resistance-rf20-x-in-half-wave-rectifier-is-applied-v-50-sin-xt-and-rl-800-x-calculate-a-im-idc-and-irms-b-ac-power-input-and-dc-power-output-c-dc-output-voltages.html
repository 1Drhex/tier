Guest Question.

A diode with internal resistance \(r_f=20 \Omega \) in half-wave rectifier, is applied \(V = 50 \sin \omega t\) and \(R_L =800 \Omega\). Calculate (a) \(I_m\), \(I_{dc}\) and \(I_{rms}\), (b) ac power input and dc power output, (c) dc output voltages and (d) efficiency of rectifier.

Admin
Solution

\(V=50\sin\omega t\)
\(V_m=50\)volt
(a) \(I_m=\frac{V_m}{r_f+R_L}=\frac{50}{20+800}=0.061A =61mA\)
\(I_{dc}=\frac{I_m}{\pi}=\frac{61}{\pi}=19.4mA\)
\(I_{rms}=\frac{I_m}{2}=\frac{61}{2}=30.5mA\)
(b) ac power input\(=I_{rms}^2(r_f+R_L)\)
\(=(30.5\times 10^{-3})^2(20+800)\)
\(=0.763W\)
d.c. power output\(=(I_{dc})^2 R_L\)
\(=(19.4\times 10^{-3})^2\times 800\)
\(=0.301W\)
(c) d.c. output voltage \(=I_{dc}R_L=19.4mA\times 800\Omega\)
\(=15.2V\)
(d) Efficiency\(=\frac{P_{dc}}{P_{ac}}\times 100\)%\(=\frac{0.301}{0.763}\times 100\)%=39.5%
