Guest Question.

Calculate the available strength of a W14×90 with a strong axis unbraced length of 30 ft and weak axis and torsional unbraced lengths of 15 ft. The material properties and loads are as given in .

Admin
Solution

Geometric Properties:

\(\text { W14x90 } \quad A_{g}=26.5 \text { in. }^{2} \quad r_{x}=6.14 \text { in. } \quad r_{y}=3.70 \text { in. }\) Manual Table 1-1

Check both slenderness ratios

K = 1.0 Commentary Table C-C2.2

\(\frac{K L_{x}}{r_{x}}=\frac{1.0(30.0 ft )}{6.14 in .} \frac{12 in }{ ft }=58.6\) governs

 

\(\frac{K L_{y}}{r_{y}}=\frac{1.0(15.0 ft )}{3.70 in .} \frac{12 in .}{ ft }=48.6\)

The available critical stresses may be interpolated from Manual Table 4-22 or calculated directly as follows.

Calculate the elastic critical buckling stress, \(F_{e}\)

\(F_{e}=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{\left(\frac{K L}{r}\right)^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2} 29,000 ksi }{(58.6)^{2}}=83.3 ksi\) Eqn. E3-4

Calculate flexural buckling stress, \(F_{c r}\)

Check limi

\(4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{F_{y}}}=4.71 \sqrt{\frac{29,000 ksi }{50 ksi }}=113>58.6\)

 

Because \(\frac{K L}{r}<4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{F_{y}}}\)

 

\(F_{c r}=\left[0.658^{\frac{F_{y}}{F_{e}}}\right] F_{y}=\left[0.658^{\frac{50.0 ksi }{83.3 ksi }}\right] 50.0 ksi =38.9 ksi\) Eqn. E3-2

 

\(P_{n}=F_{c r} A_{g}=38.9 ksi \left(26.5 in ^{2}\right)=1030 kips\) Eqn. E3-1

LRFD ASD
\(\phi_{c}=0.90\) \(\Omega_{c}=1.67\)
\(\phi_{c} P_{n}=0.90(1030 \text { kips })\)

= 928 kips > 840 kips o.k.

 \(P_{n} / \Omega_{c}=(1030 kips ) / 1.67\)

= 618 kips > 560 kips o.k.
