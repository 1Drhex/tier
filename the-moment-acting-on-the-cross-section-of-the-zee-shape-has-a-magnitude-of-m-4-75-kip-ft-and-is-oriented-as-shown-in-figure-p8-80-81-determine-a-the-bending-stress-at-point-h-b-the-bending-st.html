Guest Question.

The moment acting on the cross section of the zee shape has a magnitude of M = 4.75 kip-ft and is oriented as shown in Figure P8.80/81.
Determine:
(a) the bending stress at point H.
(b) the bending stress at point K.
(c) the maximum tension and the maximum compression bending stresses in the cross section.
(d) the orientation of the neutral axis relative to the +z axis. Show its location on a sketch of the cross section.

Admin
Solution

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

 

\(\begin{array}{lccccc}\text { Shape } & I_{C} & d=y_{i}-\bar{y} & \text { Area } A_{i} & d^{2} A & I_{C}+d^{2} A \\& \left( in. ^{4}\right) & ( in .) & \left( in. ^{2}\right) & \left( in. ^{4}\right) & ( in .4) \\\hline \text { top flange } & 0.0260 & 2.75 & 1.25 & 9.4531 & 9.4792 \\\text { web } & 3.6458 & 0 & 1.75 & 0 & 3.6458 \\\text { bottom flange } & 0.0260 & -2.75 & 1.25 & 9.4531 & 9.4792 \\\hline {\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)=} & 22.6042\end{array}\)

Moment of inertia about the y axis:

 

\(\begin{array}{lccccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( in ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=z_{i}-\bar{z} \\( in .)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left( in .^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 0.6510 & 1.075 & 1.25 & 1.4445 & 2.0956 \\\text { web } & 68,266.7 & 0 & 1.75 & 0 & 0.0179 \\\text { bottom flange } & 0.6510 & -1.075 & 1.25 & 1.4445 & 2.0956 \\\hline {\text { Moment of inertia about the } y \text { axis }\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)=} & 4.2091\end{array}\)

Product of inertia about the centroidal axes:

 

\(\begin{array}{lccccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}y_{c} \\(\text { in. })\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}z_{c} \\(\text { in. })\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\text { (in. } \left.^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{c} z_{c} A_{i} \\\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{y z} \\\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 2.75 & 1.075 & 1.25 & 3.6953 & 3.6953 \\\text { web } & 0 & 0 & 1.75 & 0 & 0 \\\text { bottom flange } & -2.75 & -1.075 & 1.25 & 3.6953 & 3.6953 \\\hline & & & {\text { Product of inertia }\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)=} & 7.3906\end{array}\)

 

(a) Bending stress at H

Since the zee shape has no axis of symmetry, Eq. (8.21) or Eq. (8.22) must be used to determine the bending stresses. Equation (8.21) will be used here.

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=-\frac{\left(M_{z} I_{y}+M_{y} I_{y z}\right) y}{I_{y} I_{z}-I_{y z}^{2}}+\frac{\left(M_{y} I_{z}+M_{z} I_{y z}\right) z}{I_{y} I_{z}-I_{y z}^{2}} \\&=-\left[\frac{(-4.75 kip – ft )(12 in. / ft )\left(4.2091 in.{ }^{4}\right)}{\left(4.2091 in. ^{4}\right)\left(22.6042 in .{ }^{4}\right)-\left(7.3906 in .{ }^{4}\right)^{2}}\right] y+\left[\frac{(-4.75 kip – ft )(12 in. / ft )\left(7.3906 in. ^{4}\right)}{\left(4.2091 in .^{4}\right)\left(22.6042 in. ^{4}\right)-\left(7.3906 in. ^{4}\right)^{2}}\right] z \\&=\left(5.92065 kips / in. ^{3}\right) y-\left(10.39584 kips / in. ^{3}\right) z\end{aligned}\)

 

To compute the normal stress at H, use (y, z) coordinates of y = 3 in. and z = 2.325 in.:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\left(5.92065 kips / in .^{3}\right)(3 in .)-\left(10.39584 kips / in .{ }^{3}\right)(2.325 in .) \\&=-6.4084 ksi =6.41 ksi ( C )\end{aligned}\).

(b) Bending stress at K
To compute the normal stress at K, use (y, z) coordinates of y = −2.50 in. and z = −2.325 in.:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\left(5.92065 kips / in. ^{3}\right)(-2.50 in .)-\left(10.39584 kips / in .{ }^{3}\right)(-2.325 in .) \\&=9.3687 ksi =9.37 ksi ( T )\end{aligned}\)

(d) Orientation of neutral axis
Since the zee shape has no axis of symmetry, it is helpful to determine the orientation of the neutral axis from Eq. (8.23) to help identify points of maximum stress.

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan \beta &=\frac{M_{y} I_{z}+M_{z} I_{y z}}{M_{z} I_{y}+M_{y} I_{y z}} \\&=\frac{(-4.75 kip – ft )\left(7.3906 in.^{4}\right)}{(-4.75 kip – ft )\left(4.2091 in.^{4}\right)} \\&=1.7559 \\&\left.\therefore \beta=60.34^{\circ} \quad \text { (i.e., } 60.34^{\circ} CW \text { from }+z \text { axis }\right)\end{aligned}\)

(c) Maximum tension and compression bending stresses
Sketch the orientation of the neutral axis. By inspection, the points on the zee cross section that are farthest from the neutral axis are on the top and bottom surfaces at the outside corners of the web. To compute bending stresses at the upper point, use (y, z) coordinates of y = 3 in. and z = −0.175 in.:

 

\(\begin{array}{rlr}\sigma_{x} & =\left(5.92065 kips / in. ^{3}\right)(3 \text { in. })-\left(10.39584 kips / in. ^{3}\right)(-0.175 in .) \\& =19.5812 ksi =19.58 ksi ( T ) \quad \text { Maximum tension bending stress }\end{array}\)

To compute bending stresses at the lower point, use (y, z) coordinates of y = −3 in. and z = 0.175 in.:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\left(5.92065 kips / in. ^{3}\right)(-3 in .)-\left(10.39584 kips / in .{ }^{3}\right)(0.175 in .) \\&=-19.5812 ksi =19.58 ksi ( C ) \quad \text { Maximum compression bending stress }\end{aligned}\)
