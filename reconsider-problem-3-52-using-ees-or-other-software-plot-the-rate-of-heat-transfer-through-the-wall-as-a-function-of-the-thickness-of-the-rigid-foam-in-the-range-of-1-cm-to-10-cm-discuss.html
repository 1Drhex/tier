Guest Question.

Reconsider . Using EES (or other) software, plot the rate of heat transfer through the wall as a function of the thickness of the rigid foam in the range of 1 cm to 10 cm. Discuss the results.

Admin
Solution

“GIVEN”

A=4*6 “[m^2]”

L_brick=0.18 “[m]”

L_plaster_center=0.18 “[m]”

L_plaster_side=0.02 “[m]”

“L_foam=2 [cm], parameter to be varied”

k_brick=0.72 “[W/m-C]”

k_plaster=0.22 “[W/m-C]”

k_foam=0.026 “[W/m-C]”

T_infinity_1=22 “[C]”

T_infinity_2=-4 “[C]”

h_1=10 “[W/m^2-C]”

h_2=20 “[W/m^2-C]”

 

“ANALYSIS”

R_conv_1=1/(h_1*A_1)

A_1=0.33*1 “[m^2]”

R_foam=(L_foam*Convert(cm, m))/(k_foam*A_1) “L_foam is in cm”

R_plaster_side=L_plaster_side/(k_plaster*A_2)

A_2=0.30*1 “[m^2]”

R_plaster_center=L_plaster_center/(k_plaster*A_3)

A_3=0.015*1 “[m^2]”

R_brick=L_brick/(k_brick*A_2)

R_conv_2=1/(h_2*A_1)

 

1/R_mid=2*1/R_plaster_center+1/R_brick

R_total=R_conv_1+R_foam+2*R_plaster_side+R_mid+R_conv_2

Q_dot=(T_infinity_1-T_infinity_2)/R_total

Q_dot_total=Q_dot*A/A_1
