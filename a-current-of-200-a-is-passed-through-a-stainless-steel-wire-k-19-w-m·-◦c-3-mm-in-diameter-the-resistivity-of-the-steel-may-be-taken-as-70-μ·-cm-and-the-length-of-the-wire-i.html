Guest Question.

A current of 200 A is passed through a stainless-steel wire \([K=19W/m\cdot^{◦} C ]\) 3 mm in diameter. The resistivity of the steel may be taken as 70 μ· cm, and the length of the wire is 1 m. The wire is submerged in a liquid at \(110{◦} C \) and experiences a convection heat-transfer coefficient of \(4KW/m^{2} \cdot {◦} C \) . Calculate the center temperature of the wire.

Admin
Solution

All the power generated in the wire must be dissipated by convection to the liquid:

\(p=I^{2}R=q=hA(T_{w}-T_{∞} ) \qquad [a]\)

The resistance of the wire is calculated from

\(R=ρ\frac{L}{A} =\frac{(70\times 10^{-6} )(100)}{\pi (0.15 )^{2} } =0.099\Omega\)

where ρ is the resistivity of the wire. The surface area of the wire is πdL, so from Equation (a),

\((200)^{2} (0.099)=4000\pi (3\times 10^{-3} )(1)(T_{w}-110 )=3960\; W\)

and\(\quad T_{w}=215^{◦} C\quad [419^{◦}F]\)

The heat generated per unit volume \dot{q} is calculated from:

\(p=\dot{q} V=\dot{q} \pi r^{2} L\)

so that:

 

\(\dot{q} =\frac{3960}{\pi (1.5\times 10^{-3} )^{2}(1)} =560.2MW/m^{3} \enspace [5.41\times 10^{7}Btu/h\cdot ft^{3} ]\)

 

Finally, the center temperature of the wire is calculated from Equation (2-26):

\(T_{0} =\frac{qR^{2} }{4k} +T_{w}\)

 

\(T_{0} =\frac{\dot{q} r^{2}_{0} }{4k} +T_{w}=\frac{(5.602\times 10^{8} )(1.5\times 10^{-3} )^{2} }{(4)(19)} +215=231.6^{◦} C \quad [449^{◦} F]\)
