Guest Question.

The eyebolt is fastened to a thick base plate, and it supports three steel cables with tensions 150 lb, 350 lb, and 800 lb. Determine the resultant force that acts on the eyebolt by using the vector algebra approach. The unit vectors i and j are oriented with the x-y coordinates as shown. (See Figure 4.6)

We are tasked to find the resultant force on the eyebolt. By using Equations (4.1) and (4.2), we will break each force down into its horizontal and vertical components and write them in vector form. Then we will add the respective components of the three forces to find the resultantâ€™s components. Given those, the magnitude and angle of action of R follow from Equation (4.7).

 

\(F =F_{x} i +F_{y} j\) (Equation 4.1)

 

\(F_{x}=F \cos \theta \quad \text { (polar to rectangular) }\)

\(F_{y}=F \sin \theta\) (Equation 4.2)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&R=\sqrt{R_{x}^{2}+R_{y}^{2}} \\&\theta=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{R_{y}}{R_{x}}\right)\end{aligned}\) (Equation 4.7)

Solution

The components of the 800 lb force are

 

\(\begin{aligned}F_{x, 1} &=(800 lb ) \cos 45^{\circ} \quad \leftarrow\left[F_{x}=F \cos \theta\right] \\&=565.7 lb \\F_{y, 1} &=(800 lb ) \sin 45^{\circ} \quad \leftarrow\left[F_{y}=F \sin \theta\right] \\&=565.7 lb\end{aligned}\)

 

and \(F _{1}\) is written in vector form as

 

\(F _{1}=565.7 i +565.7 j lb \quad \leftarrow\left[ F =F_{x} i +F_{y} j \right]\)

 

By using the same procedure for the other two forces,

 

\(\begin{aligned}F _{2} &=-\left(350 \sin 20^{\circ}\right) i +\left(350 \cos 20^{\circ}\right) j lb \\&=-119.7 i +328.9 j lb \\F _{3} &=-150 i lb\end{aligned}\)

 

To calculate the components of the resultant, the horizontal and vertical components of the three forces are summed separately:

 

\(\begin{aligned}R_{x} &=565.7-119.7-150 lb \quad \leftarrow\left[R_{x}=\sum_{i=1}^{N} F_{x, i}\right] \\&=296.0 lb\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}R_{y} &=565.7+328.9 lb \quad \leftarrow\left[R_{y}=\sum_{i=1}^{N} F_{y, i}\right] \\&=894.6 lb\end{aligned}\)

 

The magnitude of the resultant force is

 

\(\begin{aligned}R &=\sqrt{(296.0 lb )^{2}+(894.6 lb )^{2}} \leftarrow\left[\sqrt{R_{x}^{2}+R_{y}^{2}}\right] \\&=942.3 lb\end{aligned}\)

 

and it acts at the angle

 

\(\theta=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{894.6 lb }{296.0 lb }\right) \quad \leftarrow\left[\theta=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{R_{y}}{R_{x}}\right)\right]\) \(\begin{aligned}&=\tan ^{-1}\left(3.022 \frac{ lb }{ lb }\right) \\&=\tan ^{-1}(3.022) \\&=71.69^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

 

which is measured counterclockwise from the x-axis.
