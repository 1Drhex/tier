Guest Question.

In a countercurrent packed column, n-butanol flows down at a rate of \( 0.25 kg/m^{2}\) s and is cooled from 330 to 295 K. Air at 290 K, initially free of n-butanol vapour, is passed up the column at the rate of \( 0.7 m^{3}/m^{2}\) s. Calculate the required height of tower and the condition of the exit air.
Data :
Mass-transfer coefficient per unit volume, \( h_{D}a = 0.1 s^{-1}\).
Psychrometric ratio, \( h_{G}/(h_{D}ρ_{A}s) = b = 2.34\).
Heat-transfer coefficients, \( h_{L} = 3h_{G} \) .
Latent heat of vaporisation of n-butanol, λ=590 kJ/kg.
Specific heat of liquid n-butanol, \( C_{L}=2.5 kJ/kg K\).
Humid heat of gas, s = 1.05 kJ/kg K.

Solution

The first stage is to calculate the enthalpy of the saturated gas by way of the saturated humidity, \( H_{0} \) given by
\( H_{0} = \frac{P_{w0}}{P-P_{w0}}\frac{M_{w}}{M_{A}} = \frac{P_{w0}}{(101.3-P_{w0})}(\frac{74}{29} ) \)
The enthalpy is then
\( H_{f} = \frac{1}{(1+H_{0})} × 1.001(\theta _{f}-273)+H_{0}[2.5(\theta _{f}-273)+590]kJ/kg \)
where 1.001 kJ/kg K is the specific heat of dry air.
Thus,
\( H_{f} = \frac{1.001\theta _{f}-273.27}{(1+H_{0})}+H_{0}(2.5\theta _{f}-92.5)kJ/kgmoistair \)
The results of this calculation are presented in the following table, and \( H_{f} \) is plotted against \( θ_{f} \) in Fig. 5.21.

The modified enthalpy at saturation \( H^{\prime }_{f} \) is given by

\( H^{\prime }_{f} = \frac{(1.001\theta _{f}-273.27)}{(1+H_{0})}+H_{0}[2.5(\theta _{f}-273)+\lambda ^{\prime }] \)
where from Equation
\( b = \frac{\lambda }{\lambda ^{\prime }} \),
\( \lambda ^{\prime } = \lambda /b = (590/2.34)\) or 252 kJ/kg.
\( \therefore H^{\prime }_{f} = \frac{(1.001\theta _{f}-273.27)}{(1+H_{0})} + H_{0}(2.5\theta _{f}-430.5)kJ/kg\space moistair \)
These results are also given in the following table and plotted as \( H^{\prime}_{f} \) against \( θ_{f} \) in Fig. 5.21:

The bottom of the operating line (point a) has coordinates \(\theta _{L1}=295K \ and \ H_{G1} \), where
\(H_{G1}=1.05(290-273)=17.9kJ/kg\).
At a mean temperature of, say, 310 K, the density of air is
\((\frac{29}{22.4} )(\frac{273}{310} ) =1.140kg/m^{3}\)
and
\(G^{\prime }=(0.70×1.140)=0.798kg/m^{2}s\)
Thus, the slope of the operating line becomes
\(\frac{L^{\prime} C_{L}}{G^{\prime }}=\frac{(0.25×2.5)}{0.798}=0.783kJ/kgK \)
and this is drawn in as AB in Fig. 5.22, and at \(\theta _{L2}=330K,H_{G2}=46kJ/kg\).
From Equation
\(H^{\prime }_{G}=\frac{1}{b}[H_{G}+(b-1)s(\theta _{G}-\theta _{0})] \),
\(H^{\prime }_{G}=(H_{G}+(b-1)s(\theta _{G}-\theta _{0}))/b\)
\(\therefore H^{\prime }_{G1}=\frac{17.9+(2.34-1)1.05(290-273)}{2.34} =17.87kJ/kg\)
Point a coincides with the bottom of the column.
\(-\frac{h_{L}}{h_{D}\rho b}=-(\frac{3h_{G}}{h_{D}\rho b} ) \cdot (\frac{h_{D}\rho s}{h_{G}} )\)
A line is drawn through a of slope
= -3s= -3.15kJ/kgK
This line meets curve RS at c (\(\theta _{f1} \ and \ H^{\prime }_{f1}) \) to give the interface conditions at the bottom of the column. The corresponding air enthalpy is given by point C whose coordinates are
\(\theta _{f1} =293K \space H_{f1}=29.0kJ/kg\)
The difference between the ordinates of c and a gives the driving force in terms of the modified enthalpy at the bottom of the column or
\((H^{\prime} _{f1}-H^{\prime} _{G1})=(23.9-17.9)=6.0kJ/kg\)

Value of integral = 2.379

from which
\(\int_{H_{G1}}^{H_{G2}}{\frac{dH_{G}}{H^{\prime}_{f}-H^{\prime }_{G}} } =2.379\)
Substituting in Equation
\(\int_{H_{G1}}^{H_{G2}}{\frac{dH_{G}}{(H^{\prime}_{f}-H^{\prime }_{G})} } =\frac{bh_{D}a\rho }{G^{\prime }} z\),
\(\frac{bh_{D}\rho a z }{G^{\prime }} =2.379\)
and
\(z=\frac{(2.379×0.798)}{(2.34×0.1)}=8.1m \)
It remains to evaluate the change in gas conditions.
Point e, (\(\theta _{G1}=290K \space and \space H_{G1}=17.9kJ/kg\)) represents the condition of the inlet gas. ec is now drawn in, and from Equation
\(\frac{(H^{\prime }_{G}-H^{\prime }_{f})}{(\theta _{G}-\theta _{f})}=\frac{dH_{G}}{d\theta _{G}} \),
this represents \(dH_{G}/d\theta _{G}\). As for the air–water system, this construction is continued until the gas enthalpy reaches \(H_{G2}\). The final point is given by D at
which \(\theta _{G2}=308K\).
It is fortuitous that, in this problem,\(H^{\prime }_{G}=H_{G}\). This is not always the case, and reference should be made to Section 5.7 for elaboration of this point.
