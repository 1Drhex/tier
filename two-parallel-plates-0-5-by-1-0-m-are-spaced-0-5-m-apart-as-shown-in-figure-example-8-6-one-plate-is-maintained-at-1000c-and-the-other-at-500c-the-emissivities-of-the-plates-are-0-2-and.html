Guest Question.

Two parallel plates 0.5 by 1.0 m are spaced 0.5 m apart, as shown in Figure Example 8-6. One plate is maintained at \( 1000^{◦} C\) and the other at \( 500^{◦} C\). The emissivities of the plates are 0.2 and 0.5, respectively. The plates are located in a very large room, the walls of which are maintained at \( 27^{◦} C\). The plates exchange heat with each other and with the room, but only the plate surfaces facing each other are to be considered in the analysis. Find the net transfer to each plate and to the room.

Admin
Solution

This is a three-body problem, the two plates and the room, so the radiation network is shown in Figure 8-27. From the data of the problem

\( T_{1} = 1000^{◦} C=1273 \; K \qquad A_{1} =A_{2} =0.5\> m^{2} \)

 

\( T_{2} = 500^{◦} C=773\; K\hspace{30 pt} \epsilon _{1} =0.2 \)

 

\( T_{3} = 27^{◦} C=300 \; K \hspace{30 pt} \epsilon _{2} =0.5 \)

Because the area of the room \(A_3\) is very large, the resistance \( (1-\epsilon _{3} )/\epsilon _{3} A_{3} \) may be taken as zero and we obtain \( E_{b3} =J_{3} . \) . The shape factor \( F_{12 } \) was given in:

\( F_{12} = 0.285=F_{21} \)

 

\(F_{13} = 1−F_{12} =0.715 \)

 

\(F_{23} = 1−F_{21} =0.715\)

The resistances in the network are calculated as

\(\frac{1−\epsilon _{1} }{\epsilon _{1} A_{1} }=\frac{1−0.2}{(0.2)(0.5)}=8.0\hspace{30 pt} \frac{1−\epsilon _{2} }{\epsilon _{12} A_{2} }=\frac{1−0.5}{(0.5)(0.5)}=2.0 \)

 

\(\frac{1 }{ A_{1}F_{12} }=\frac{1}{(0.5)(0.285)}=7.018\hspace{30 pt} \frac{1 }{ A_{1}F_{13} }=\frac{1}{(0.5)(0.715)}=2.797 \)

 

\(\frac{1 }{ A_{2}F_{23} }=\frac{1}{(0.5)(0.715)}=2.797 \)

Taking the resistance \( (1-\epsilon _{3} )/\epsilon _{3} A_{3} \) as zero, we have the network as shown. To calculate the heat lows at each surface we must determine the radiosities \( J_{1} \) and \( J_{2} \) . The network is solved by setting the sum of the heat currents entering nodes \( J_{1} \) and \( J_{2} \) to zero:

node \( J_{1} \) :

 

\(\frac{E_{b1} −J_{1} }{8.0} +\frac{J_{_{2} } −J_{1} }{7.018}+\frac{E_{b3} −J_{1} }{2.797}=0\hspace{30 pt} [a] \)

node \( J_{2} \) :

\(\frac{J_{_{1} } −J_{2} }{7.018}+\frac{E_{b3} −J_{2} }{2.797} +\frac{E_{b2} −J_{2} }{2.0}=0\hspace{30 pt} [b] \)

Now

\(E_{b1} = σT ^{4} _{1} =148.87\quad kW/m^{2} \quad [47,190 \enspace Btu/h · ft^{2} ]\)

 

\(E_{b2} = σT ^{4} _{2} =20.241\quad kW/m^{2} \quad [6416 \enspace Btu/h · ft^{2} ]\)

 

\(E_{b3} = σT ^{4} _{3} =0.4592\quad kW/m^{2} \quad [145.6 \enspace Btu/h · ft^{2} ]\)

Inserting the values of \( E_{b1} , E_{b2}\enspace and \quad E_{b3} \) into Equations (a) and (b), we have two equations and two unknowns \( J_{1} \) and \( J_{2} \) that may be solved simultaneously to give

\(J_{1} =33.469 \quad kW/m^{2} \hspace{30 pt} J_{2} =15.054 \enspace kW/m^{2} \)

 

The total heat lost by plate 1 is

\(q_{1}=\frac{E_{b1} −J_{1} }{(1-\epsilon _{1} )/\epsilon _{1} A_{1} }=\frac{148.87−33.469}{8.0}=14.425\quad kW \)

and the total heat lost by plate 2 is

\(q_{2}=\frac{E_{b2} −J_{2} }{(1-\epsilon _{2} )/\epsilon _{2} A_{2} }=\frac{20.241−15.054}{2.0}=2.594\quad kW \)

The total heat received by the room is

\(q_{3}=\frac{J_{1} −J_{3} }{1/A_{1} F_{13} }+ \frac{J_{2} −J_{3} }{1/A_{2} F_{23} }=\frac{33.469−0.4592}{2.797}+\frac{15.054−0.4592}{2.797}=17.020 \enspace kW \quad [58,070 \quad Btu/h] \)

From an overall-balance standpoint we must have

\(q_{3} =q_{1} +q_{2} \)

because the net energy lost by both plates must be absorbed by the room.
