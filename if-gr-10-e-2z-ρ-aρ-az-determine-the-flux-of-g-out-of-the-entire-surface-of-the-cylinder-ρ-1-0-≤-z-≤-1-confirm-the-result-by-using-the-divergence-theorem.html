Guest Question.

If \(G\left(r\right)=10e^{-2z}\left(\rho a_{\rho}+a_{z}\right)\), determine the flux of G out of the entire surface of the cylinder ρ = 1, 0 ≤ z ≤ 1. Confirm the result by using the divergence theorem.

Admin
Solution

If \(\Psi\) is the flux of G through the given surface, shown in Figure 3.18, then

\(\Psi=\oint_{s}G\cdot dS=\Psi_{t}+\Psi_{b}+\Psi_{s}\)

where \(\Psi_{t}\), \(\Psi_{b}\), and \(\Psi_{s}\) are the fluxes through the top, bottom, and sides (curved surface) of the cylinder as in Figure 3.18. For

\(\Psi_{t}, z =1, dS=\rho d\rho d\phi a_{z}\)

Hence

\(\Psi_{t}=\iint{G}\cdot dS=\int_{\rho=0}^{1}\int_{\phi=0}^{2\pi}10e^{-2}\rho d\rho d\phi=10e^{-2}\left(2\pi\right)\frac{\rho^{2}}{2}|_{0}^{1}=10\pi e^{-2}\)

For

\(\Psi_{b}, z=0\) and \(dS=\rho d\rho d\phi\left(-a_{z}\right)\)

Hence

\(\Psi_{b}=\int_{b}G\cdot dS=\int_{\rho=0}^{1}\int_{\phi=0}^{2\pi}10e^{0}\rho d\rho d\phi=-10\left(2\pi\right)\frac{\rho^{2}}{2}|_{0}^{1}=-10\pi\)

For

\(\Psi_{s}, \rho=1\) and \(dS=\rho dzd\phi a_{\rho}\)

Hence

\(\Psi_{s}=\int_{s}G\cdot dS=\int_{z=0}^{1}\int_{\phi=0}^{2\pi}10e^{-2z}\rho^{2} dzd\phi=10\left(1\right)^{2} \left(2\pi\right)\frac{e^{-2z}}{-2}|_{0}^{1}=10\pi\left(1-e^{-2}\right)\)

Thus

\(\Psi=\Psi_{t}+\Psi_{b}+\Psi_{s}=10\pi e^{-2}-10\pi+10\pi\left(1-e^{-2}\right)=0\)

Alternatively, since S is a closed surface, we can apply the divergence theorem:

\(\Psi =\oint_{s}G\cdot dS=\int_{v}\left(\nabla\cdot G\right) dv\)

But

\(\nabla\cdot G=\frac{1}{\rho}\frac{\partial}{\partial \rho} \left(\rho G_{\rho}\right) +\frac{1}{\rho}\frac{\partial}{\partial\phi }G_{\phi } +\frac{\partial}{\partial z} G_{z}\)

\(=\frac{1}{\rho}\frac{\partial}{\partial \rho} \left(\rho^{2}10e^{-2z}\right)-20e^{-2z}\)

\(=\frac{1}{\rho}\left(20\rho e^{-2z}\right)-20e^{-2z}=0\)

showing that G has no outward flux. Hence

\(\Psi =\int_{v}\left(\nabla\cdot G\right)dv=0\)
