Guest Question.

The truss in Figure P17.105 is subjected to concentrated loads of P = 130 kN and 2P = 260 kN. All members are made of steel [E = 200 GPa], and each has a cross-sectional area of A = 4,200 \(mm ^{2}\) . Use Castigliano’s second theorem to determine:
(a) the horizontal displacement of joint A.
(b) the vertical displacement of joint D.

Admin
Solution

(a) Horizontal Displacement of Joint A

Add a horizontal dummy load at A and designate it as Q.

Truss Reaction Forces

\(\begin{aligned}&B_{x}=1.0000 Q \rightarrow \\&B_{y}=0.375 Q+520.0000 kN \uparrow \\&F_{y}=-0.375 Q+520.0000 kN \uparrow\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{array}{cc|cccc}\hline \text { Member } & F & \frac{\partial F}{\partial Q} & \begin{array}{c}F \\F (Q=0 kN )\end{array} & L & \left(\frac{\partial F}{\partial Q}\right) F L \\& ( kN ) & & ( kN ) & ( mm ) & ( kN – mm ) \\\hline A B & -216.6667 & 0.0000 & -216.6667 & 10,000.0000 & 0.0000 \\A C & 1.0000 Q+173.3333 & 1.0000 & 173.3333 & 8,000.0000 & 1,386,666.4000 \\B C & -0.3750 Q-390.0000 & -0.3750 & -390.0000 & 6,000.0000 & 877,500.0000 \\B D & -1.0000 Q-173.3333 & -1.0000 & -173.3333 & 8,000.0000 & 1,386,666.4000 \\C D & 0.6250 Q+216.6667 & 0.6250 & 216.6667 & 10,000.0000 & 1,354,166.8750 \\C E & 0.5000 Q & 0.5000 & 0.0000 & 8,000.0000 & 0.0000 \\D E & -260.0000 & 0.0000 & -260.0000 & 6,000.0000 & 0.0000 \\D F & -173.3333 & 0.0000 & -173.3333 & 8,000.0000 & 0.0000 \\D G & -0.6250 Q+216.6667 & -0.6250 & 216.6667 & 10,000.0000 & -1,354,166.8750 \\E G & 0.5000 Q & 0.5000 & 0.0000 & 8,000.0000 & 0.0000 \\F G & 0.3750 Q-390.0000 & 0.3750 & -390.0000 & 6,000.0000 & -877,500.0000 \\F H & -216.6667 & 0.0000 & -216.6667 & 10,000.0000 & 0.0000 \\G H & 173.3333 & 0.0000 & 173.3333 & 8,000.0000 & 0.0000 \\\hline & & & & \sum\left(\frac{\partial F}{\partial Q}\right) F L= & 2,773,332.8000 \\\hline\end{array}\)

 

Apply Equation (17.39)

\(\Delta=\frac{1}{A E} \sum\left(\frac{\partial F}{\partial Q}\right) F L\)

to calculate the deflection from the tabulated results:

\(\Delta_{A}=\frac{(2,773,332.8 kN – mm )(1,000 N / kN )}{\left(4,200 mm ^{2}\right)\left(200,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=3.30 mm \leftarrow\)

 

(b) Vertical Displacement of Joint D
Add a vertical dummy load at D and designate it as PD.

Truss Reaction Forces

\(\begin{aligned}B_{x} &=0.0 \\B_{y} &=0.5000 P_{D}+520.0000 kN \uparrow \\F_{y} &=0.5000 P_{D}+520.0000 kN \uparrow\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{array}{cc|cccc}\hline \text { Member } & F & \frac{\partial F}{\partial P_{D}} &F \left(P_{D}=0 kN \right) & L & \left(\frac{\partial F}{\partial P_{D}}\right) F L \\& ( kN ) & & ( kN ) & ( mm ) & ( kN – mm ) \\\hline A B & -216.6667 & 0.0000 & -216.6667 & 10,000.0000 & 0.0000 \\A C & 173.3333 & 0.0000 & 173.3333 & 8,000.0000 & 0.0000 \\B C & -0.5000 P_{D}-390.0000 & -0.5000 & -390.0000 & 6,000.0000 & 1,170,000.0000 \\B D & -173.3333 & 0.0000 & -173.3333 & 8,000.0000 & 0.0000 \\C D & 0.8333 P_{D}+216.6667 & 0.8333 & 216.6667 & 10,000.0000 & 1,805,483.6111 \\C E & -0.6667 P_{D} & -0.6667 & 0.0000 & 8,000.0000 & 0.0000 \\D E & -260.0000 & 0.0000 & -260.0000 & 6,000.0000 & 0.0000 \\D F & -173.3333 & 0.0000 & -173.3333 & 8,000.0000 & 0.0000 \\D G & 0.8333 P_{D}+216.6667 & 0.8333 & 216.6667 & 10,000.0000 & 1,805,483.6111 \\E G & -0.6667 P_{D} & -0.6667 & 0.0000 & 8,000.0000 & 0.0000 \\F G & -0.5000 P_{D}-390.0000 & -0.5000 & -390.0000 & 6,000.0000 & 1,170,000.0000 \\F H & -216.6667 & 0.0000 & -216.6667 & 10,000.0000 & 0.0000 \\G H & 173.3333 & 0.0000 & 173.3333 & 8,000.0000 & 0.0000 \\\hline & &&& \sum\left(\frac{\partial F}{\partial P_{D}}\right) F L= & 5,950,967.2222 \\\hline\end{array}\)

 

Apply Equation (17.39)

\(\Delta=\frac{1}{A E} \sum\left(\frac{\partial F}{\partial P_{D}}\right) F L\)

to calculate the deflection from the tabulated results:

\(\Delta_{D}=\frac{(5,950,967.2222 kN – mm )(1,000 N / kN )}{\left(4,200 mm ^{2}\right)\left(200,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=7.08 mm \downarrow\)
