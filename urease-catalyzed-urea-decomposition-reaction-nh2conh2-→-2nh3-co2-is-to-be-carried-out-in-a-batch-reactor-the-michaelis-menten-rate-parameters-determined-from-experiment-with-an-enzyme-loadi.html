Guest Question.

Urease catalyzed urea decomposition reaction

\(NH _{2} CONH _{2} \rightarrow 2 NH _{3}+ CO _{2}\)

is to be carried out in a batch reactor. The Michaelis-Menten rate parameters determined from experiment with an enzyme loading of 5 g/L are given by \(r_{\max }\) = 1.35 \(mol \cdot L ^{-1} \cdot s ^{-1}\) and \(K_{ m }\) = 0.0265 mol/L. Determine the time needed for 90% conversion of urea to ammonia and carbon dioxide of a solution containing 0.2 mol/L of urea in a 2 L reactor. The enzyme loading is 0.001 g/L.

Admin
Solution

The Michaelis-Menten rate law is given by

\(r_{P}=\frac{r_{\max } S}{K_{m}+S}\) (E7.3.1)

We know that \(K_{ m }\) = 0.0265 mol/L. Since \(r_{\max }\) is proportional to the enzyme loading, we have \(r_{\max }\) = 1.35 × 0.001 / 5 \( mol \cdot L ^{-1} \cdot s ^{-1}=2.7 \times 10^{-4} mol \cdot L ^{-1} \cdot s ^{-1} \)

Mole balance of substrate (urea) in the reactor gives

\(0-0+r_{S} V=\frac{d n_{S}}{d t}\) (E7.3.2)

The reactor volume is constant (isothermal liquid/suspended-phase reaction). Eq. (E7.3.2) \(0-0+r_{S} V=\frac{d n_{S}}{d t}\) is reduced to

\(-\frac{d S}{d t}=\frac{r_{\max } S}{K_{m}+S}\) (E7.3.3)

Separation of variable leads to,

\(\left\lgroup \frac{K_{m}}{S}+1 \right\rgroup d S=-r_{\max } d t\) (E7.3.4)

Integrating Eq. (E7.3.4) \(\left\lgroup \frac{K_{m}}{S}+1 \right\rgroup d S=-r_{\max } d t\) from t = 0 and \(S=S_{0}\), we obtain

\(K_{m} \ln \frac{S}{S_{0}}+S-S_{0}=-r_{\max } t\) (E7.3.5)

The conversion of substrate is defined as

\(f_{S}=\frac{S_{0}-S}{S_{0}}\) (E7.3.6)

Substitute Eq. (E7.3.6) \(f_{S}=\frac{S_{0}-S}{S_{0}}\) into Eq. (E7.3.5) \(K_{m} \ln \frac{S}{S_{0}}+S-S_{0}=-r_{\max } t\) , we obtain

\(t=\frac{S_{0} f_{S}-K_{m} \ln \left(1-f_{S}\right)}{r_{\max }}\) (E7.3.7)

Thus, the time required for 90% conversion is

\(t=\frac{S_{0} f_{S}-K_{m} \ln \left(1-f_{S}\right)}{r_{\max }}=\frac{0.1 \times 0.9-0.0265 \ln (1-0.9)}{2.7 \times 10^{-4}} s=559.3 s=9.322 min\)
