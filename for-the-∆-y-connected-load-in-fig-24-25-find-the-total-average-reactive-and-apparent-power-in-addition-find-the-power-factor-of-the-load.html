Guest Question.

For the ∆-Y connected load in Fig. 24.25, find the total average, reactive, and apparent power. In addition, find the power factor of the load.

Admin
Solution

Consider the ∆ and Y separately.

For the ∆:

\(Z_\Delta =6 Ω – j 8 Ω = 10 Ω ∠-53.13°\\[0.5cm] I_\phi=\frac{E_L}{Z_\Delta}=\frac{200V}{10 Ω}=20A\\[0.5cm] P_{T_\Delta}=3I^2_{\phi}R_\phi =(3)(20 A)^2 (6 Ω) = 7200 W \\[0.5cm] Q_{T_\Delta}=3I^2_{\phi}X_\phi=(3)(20 A)^2 (8 Ω) = 9600\enspace VAR \enspace (C)\\[0.5cm] S_{T_\Delta}=3V_\phi I_\phi=(3)(200 V)(20 A) = 12,000 VA\)

For the Y:

\(Z_Y=4 Ω + j 3 Ω = 5 Ω ∠36.87°\\[0.5cm] I_\phi=\frac{E_L/\sqrt{3} }{Z_Y}=\frac{200V/\sqrt{3} }{5 Ω}=\frac{116V}{5 Ω}=23.12 A\\[0.5cm] P_{T_Y}=3I^2_\phi R_\phi=(3)(23.12 A)^2 (4 Ω) = 6414.41 W \\[0.5cm] Q_{T_Y}=3I^2_\phi X_\phi=(3)(23.12 A)^2 (3 Ω) = 4810.81 \enspace VAR\enspace (L)\\[0.5cm] S_{T_Y}=3V_\phi I_\phi=(3)(116 V)(23.12 A) = 8045.76 VA\)

For the total load:

\(P_T=P_{T_\Delta}+P_{T_Y}=7200 W + 6414.41 W = 13,614.41 W\\[0.5cm] Q_T=Q_{T_\Delta}-Q_{T_Y}=9600 \enspace VAR \enspace (C) – 4810.81\enspace VAR\enspace (L)=4789.19\enspace VAR \enspace(C)\\[0.5cm] S_T=\sqrt{P^2_T+Q^2_T}=\sqrt{(13,614.41 W)^2 + (4789.19\enspace VAR)^2}=14,432.2\enspace VA \\[0.5cm] F_p=\frac{P_T}{S_T}=\frac{13,614.41 W}{14,432.20 VA} =0.943\; leading \)
