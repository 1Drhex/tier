Guest Question.

Determine the moments at each joint of the frame shown in Fig. \(11-16 (\mathrm{a})\). \(EI\) is constant

Admin
Solution

Slope-Deflection Equations. Three spans must be considered in this problem: \(A B, B C\), and \(C D\). Since the spans are fixed supported at \(A\) and \(D\), Eq. 11-8 applies for the solution.

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=2 E k\left(2 \theta_{N}+\theta_{F}-3 \psi\right)+(\text { FEM })_{N}\\ \text For Internal Span or End Span with Far End Fixed \end{array} \) (11-8)

From the table on the inside back cover, the FEMs for \(B C\) are

\( \begin{array}{l} (\mathrm{FEM})_{B C}=-\frac{5 w L^{2}}{96}=-\frac{5(24)(8)^{2}}{96}=-80 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ (\mathrm{FEM})_{C B}=\frac{5 w L^{2}}{96}=\frac{5(24)(8)^{2}}{96}=80 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \end{array} \)

Note that \(\theta_{A}=\theta_{D}=0\) and \(\psi_{A B}=\psi_{B C}=\psi_{C D}=0\), since no sidesway will occur.

Applying Eq. 11-8, we have

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=2 E k\left(2 \theta_{N}+\theta_{F}-3 \psi\right)+(\text { FEM })_{N}\\ \text For Internal Span or End Span with Far End Fixed \end{array} \) (11-8)

\( \begin{aligned} M_{N} &=2 E k\left(2 \theta_{N}+\theta_{F}-3 \psi\right)+(\mathrm{FEM})_{N} \\ \end{aligned} \)

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{A B}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{12}\right)\left[2(0)+\theta_{B}-3(0)\right]+0 \\ M_{A B}=0.1667 E I \theta_{B} \end{array} \) (1)

\(\begin{array}{l}M_{B A}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{12}\right)\left[2 \theta_{B}+0-3(0)\right]+0 \\ M_{B A}=0.333 E I \theta_{B} \\ \end{array}\) (2)

\(\begin{array}{l}M_{B C}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{8}\right)\left[2 \theta_{B}+\theta_{C}-3(0)\right]-80 \\ M_{B C}=0.5 E I \theta_{B}+0.25 E I \theta_{C}-80 \\ \end{array}\) (3)

\(\begin{array}{l}M_{C B}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{8}\right)\left[2 \theta_{C}+\theta_{B}-3(0)\right]+80 \\ M_{C B}=0.5 E I \theta_{C}+0.25 E I \theta_{B}+80 \\ \end{array}\) (4)

\(\begin{array}{l}M_{C D}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{12}\right)\left[2 \theta_{C}+0-3(0)\right]+0 \\ M_{C D}=0.333 E I \theta_{C} \\ \end{array}\) (5)

\(\begin{array}{l}M_{D C}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{12}\right)\left[2(0)+\theta_{C}-3(0)\right]+0 \\ M_{D C}=0.1667 E I \theta_{C} \end{array}\) (6)

Equilibrium Equations. The preceding six equations contain eight unknowns. The remaining two equilibrium equations come from moment equilibrium at joints \(B\) and \(C\), Fig. 11-16b. We have

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{B A}+M_{B C}=0 \\ \end{array}\) (7)

\(\begin{array}{l}M_{C B}+M_{C D}=0 \end{array}\) (8)

To solve these eight equations, substitute Eqs. (2) and (3) into Eq. (7) and substitute Eqs. (4) and (5) into Eq. (8). We get

\( \begin{array}{l} 0.833 E I \theta_{B}+0.25 E I \theta_{C}=80 \\ 0.833 E I \theta_{C}+0.25 E I \theta_{B}=-80 \end{array} \)

Solving simultaneously yields

\( \theta_{B}=-\theta_{C}=\frac{137.1}{E I} \)

which conforms with the way the frame deflects as shown in Fig. 11-16a. Substituting into Eqs. (1)-(6), we get

\( \begin{array}{ll} M_{A B} & =22.9 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ M_{B A} & =45.7 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ M_{B C} & =-45.7 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ M_{C B} & =45.7 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ M_{C D} & =-45.7 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ M_{D C} & =-22.9 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \end{array} \)

Using these results, the reactions at the ends of each member can be determined from the equations of equilibrium, and the moment diagram for the frame can be drawn, Fig. 11-16c.
