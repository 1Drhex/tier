Guest Question.

A charged particle having thrice the charge and mass twice that of an electron is accelerated through a potential difference \(V_A\) of 50 volts, before it enters a uniform magnetic field of flux density B of magnitude 0.02 Webers/\(m^2\) normally with the field. Find
1. Velocity of charged particle before entering the field
2. Radius of the path
3. Time of one revolution
4. Repeat the above calculations when an electron enters at an angle θ of 25°.

Admin
Solution

1) Velocity

\(v=\sqrt{2\frac{Q.V_A}{m} } \) m/sec

Velocity of charged particle before entering the field
As per the given data, Q = 3Q and m = 2m and \(V_a\) = 50 volts

\(\therefore v=\sqrt{3\frac{3Q}{2m}\times 50 }=\sqrt{\left\lgroup\frac{3q}{m} \right\rgroup \times 50} \) \(\because \frac{e}{m}=1.76\times 10^{11}=\frac{Q}{m} \)

\(v=\sqrt{3\times 1.76\times 10^{11}\times 50 }=\sqrt{26.4\times 10^{12}}=5.138\times 10^6 \) m/sec

2) Radius R of the path, when charge particle enters normal to the magnetic field
Data \(\frac{e}{m}=1.76\times 10^{11} , B=0.02 wb/m^2 =2\times 10^{-2}wb/m^2\) ;and \(v=5.138\times 10^6\)m/sec
\(f_m=Bev=\frac{mv^2}{R} \therefore Be=\frac{mv}{R} \therefore R=\frac{m}{e}.\frac{v}{B} \)
R=\(\frac{m}{e}.\frac{v}{B} =\frac{5.138\times 10^6}{1.76\times 10^{11} \times 2\times 10^{-2} }=\frac{10^{-11}\times 5.138\times 10^8}{3.52}=1.46\times 10^{-3} \) metres

If the particle enters at an angle θ of 25° with the field; θ = 25° ∴ \(v_{y}\) = v sin θ

using \(Bev=\frac{mv^2}{R} ; Be=\frac{mv\sin \theta }{R} \therefore R=\left\lgroup\frac{m}{e} \right\rgroup\frac{v\sin \theta}{B} \)
R=\(\frac{5.138\times 10^6}{1.76\times 10^{11}\times 2\times 10^{-2}} \times \sin 25^\circ =1.46\times 10^{-3}\times \sin 25^\circ\)
R=\(1.46\times 10^{-3}\times \sin 25^\circ =0.617\times 10^{-3}\) metres.

3) Time for one revolution
Angular velocity in radian/sec; \(w=\frac{v}{R}\) where \(R=\left\lgroup\frac{m}{e} \right\rgroup\frac{v}{B} \therefore w=\frac{eB}{m} \) rad/sec

Time in seconds for one complete revolution is called period

\(T=\frac{2\pi }{w}=\frac{2\pi m }{eB} =\left\lgroup\frac{2\pi}{B} \right\rgroup\frac{m}{e} \)
For an electron, \(T=\frac{2\pi }{B}\times \frac{1}{1.76\times 10^{11}}=\frac{3.57\times 10^{-11}}{2\times 10^{-2}}=1.785\times 10^{-9} \) sec\(=1.785\) secs

Time for one revolution when θ= 25º

\(w=\frac{v\sin \theta }{R}\therefore 2\pi f=\frac{v\sin \theta }{R} \)
T=\(\frac{1}{f}=\frac{2\pi R}{v\sin \theta }=\frac{2\pi \times 1.46\times 10^{-3}\sin 25^\circ }{5.13\times 10^6\sin 25^\circ }=1.788\times 10^{-9} \)sec
\(T=1.788\times 10^{-9}\)sec
