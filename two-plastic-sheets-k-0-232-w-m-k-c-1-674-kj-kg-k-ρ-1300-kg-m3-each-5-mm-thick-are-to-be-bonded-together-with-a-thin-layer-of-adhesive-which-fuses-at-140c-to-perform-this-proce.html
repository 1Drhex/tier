Guest Question.

Two plastic sheets \((k = 0.232 W/m.K, C = 1.674 kJ/kg.K, ρ = 1300 kg/m^3),\) each 5 mm thick are to be bonded together with a thin layer of adhesive, which fuses at 140°C. To perform this process, they are pressed between two surfaces at 250°C. Find the time required for which the sheets should be pressed together to complete the process. The initial temperature of sheets is 30°C. Assume perfect contact and neglect resistance of adhesive. Derive the formula used.

Admin
Solution

Given : Two plastic sheets to be bonded ;
L = 5 mm, k = 0.232 W/m.K,
\(T_i= 30°C, T_L = 250°C\)
\(T = 140°C, ρ = 1300 kg/m^3,\)
C = 1.674 kJ/kg.K = 1674 J/kg.K.
To find : The time required to reach the centre temperature 140°C.
Assumptions :
1. Infinite long plastic sheets.
2. Internal temperature gradients are negligible.
3. No radiation heat exchange.
4. Constant properties.
Analysis : Consider the two plastic sheets as shown in Fig. 6.22.

The thickness of the two sheet
2L = 5 mm + 5 mm = 10 mm = 0.01 m.
The energy balance equation for the two sheets together :
Rate of heat inflow = Rate of internal energy increase
or \(\frac{2 kA(T_L – T)}{L} = ρVC \frac{dT}{dt}\)

where T is the temperature, function of time.
Let \(θ =T_L – T\)
\(\frac{dθ}{dt} = – \frac{dT}{dt}\)

Substituting and rearranging,
\(\frac{2kA dt}{ρVC L} = – \frac{dθ}{θ}\)

Integrating both sides within the limits, we get
\(\frac{2kA }{ρVC L} \int_{0}^{t} dt = -\int_{θ_i}^{θ_c}\)

or \(\frac{2kAt }{ρVC L} = – ln\left ( \frac{\theta _c}{\theta _i} \right ) = – ln \left [ \frac{T_L – T_c}{T_L – T_i} \right ]\)

\(t = \frac{ρ(AL) CL}{2kA} × ln \left [ \frac{T_L – T_i}{T_L – T_c} \right ]\) \(= \frac{1300 × 1674 ×(0.005)^2}{2 × 0.232} × ln \left [ \frac{250 – 30}{250 – 140} \right ]\)

= 81.2 s = 1.35 min.
