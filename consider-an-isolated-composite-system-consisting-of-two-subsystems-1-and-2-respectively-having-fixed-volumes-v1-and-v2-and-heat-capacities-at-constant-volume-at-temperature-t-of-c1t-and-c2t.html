Guest Question.

Consider an isolated composite system consisting of two subsystems, (1) and (2) respectively, having fixed volumes \(V_{1} and V_{2}\) and heat capacities at constant volume at temperature \(T of C_{1}(T) and C_{2}(T)\). Suppose that the subsystems are separated initially by an insulating wall and are at equilibrium with initial temperatures \(T_{1} \lt T_{2}\). Then let very small amounts of energy pass very slowly through the wall by heat transfer so that each subsystem passes through a series of equilibrium states until the system comes to a final equilibrium state. Calculate the temperatures of the subsystems at each stage of the process and study the total entropy change until a maximum entropy has been reached.

Admin
Solution

At some intermediate stage of the process, the changes in energy and entropy will be given by

\(0 = \Delta\left(U\right) = \int_{T_{1} }^{T^{*}_{1} }{C_{1}\left(T\right) dT } + \int_{T_{2} }^{T^{*}_{2} }{C_{2}\left(T\right) dT }\) (3.37)

and

\(\Delta \left(S\right) = \int_{T_{1} }^{T^{*}_{1} }{\frac{C_{1}\left(T\right)}{T} dT } + \int_{T_{2} }^{T^{*}_{2} }{\frac{C_{2}\left(T\right)}{T} dT } \geq 0.\) (3.38)

Then take differentials of these expressions to obtain

\(0 = C_{1} \left(T^{*}_{1} \right) dT^{*}_{1} + C_{2} \left(T^{*}_{2} \right) dT^{*}_{2}\) (3.39)

and

\(d\Delta \left(S\right) = \frac{C_{1} \left(T^{*}_{1} \right)}{T^{*}_{1}} dT^{*}_{1} + \frac{C_{2} \left(T^{*}_{2} \right)}{T^{*}_{2}} dT^{*}_{2} \geq 0.\) (3.40)

Substitution of Eq. (3.39) into Eq. (3.40) gives

\(d\Delta \left(S\right) = C_{1} \left(T^{*}_{1} \right) \left(\frac{1}{T^{*}_{1}} – \frac{1}{T^{*}_{2}}\right) dT^{*}_{1} \geq 0,\) (3.41)

which for positive \(dT^{*}_{1} requires \left(1/T^{*}_{1} – 1/T^{*}_{2}\right) \geq 0.\) In view of Eq. (3.37), this requires \(T_{1} \lt T^{*}_{1} \leq T^{*}_{2} \lt T_{2}\) at each stage of the process. When \(T^{*}_{1} increases to T^{*}_{2}, d\Delta \left(S\right) = 0\) and S will reach its maximum value at some new equilibrium temperature \(T^{*}_{1} = T^{*}_{2} = T_{eq}.\) This can be seen in principle by integrating Eq. (3.41) from \(T_{1} to T_{eq},\) but that would require specification of \(C_{1}(T) and C_{2}(T)\) to enable \(T^{*}_{2} to be expressed as a function of T^{*}_{1} .\) Nevertheless, the final result will satisfy

\(0 = \Delta\left(U\right) = \int_{T_{1} }^{T_{eq} }{C_{1}\left(T\right) dT } + \int_{T_{2} }^{T_{eq} }{C_{2}\left(T\right) dT }\) (3.42)

and

\(\Delta \left(S\right) = \int_{T_{1} }^{T_{eq} }{\frac{C_{1}\left(T\right)}{T} dT } + \int_{T_{2} }^{T_{eq} }{\frac{C_{2}\left(T\right)}{T} dT } \gt 0.\) (3.43)

For the simple case when \(C_{1} and C_{2}\) are independent of T, the reader is invited to carry out these calculations explicitly.
