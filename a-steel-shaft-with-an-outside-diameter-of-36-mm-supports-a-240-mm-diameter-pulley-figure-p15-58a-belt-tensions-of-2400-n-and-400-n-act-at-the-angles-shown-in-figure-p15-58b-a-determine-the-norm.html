Guest Question.

A steel shaft with an outside diameter of 36 mm supports a 240-mm-diameter pulley (Figure P15.58a). Belt tensions of 2,400 N and 400 N act at the angles shown in Figure P15.58b.
(a) Determine the normal and shear stresses on the side of the shaft at point K.
(b) Determine the principal stresses and maximum in-plane shear stress at point K and show the orientation of these stresses on an appropriate sketch.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{array}{ll}A=\frac{\pi}{4}(36 mm )^{2}=1,017.876 mm ^{2} & J=\frac{\pi}{32}(36 mm )^{4}=164,895.915 mm ^{4} \\Q=\frac{(36 mm )^{3}}{12}=3,888 mm ^{3} & I_{x}=I_{y}=\frac{\pi}{64}(36 mm )^{4}=82,447.958 mm ^{4}\end{array}\)

Vector expressions for the belt tensions:

Denote the 2,400-N belt tension as \(F _{1}\) and express this force vector as:

\(F _{1}=-(2,400 N ) \cos 15^{\circ} i -(2,400 N ) \sin 15^{\circ} j\)

The position vector from the section of interest to a point on the line of action of \(F _{1}\) is:

\(r _{1}=-(120 mm ) \sin 15^{\circ} i +(120 mm ) \cos 15^{\circ} j +(160 mm ) k\)

Denote the 400-N belt tension as \(F _{2}\) and express this force vector as:

\(F _{2}=-(400 N ) \cos 15^{\circ} i +(400 N ) \sin 15^{\circ} j\)

The position vector from the section of interest to a point on the line of action of \(F _{2}\) is:

\(r _{2}=-(120 mm ) \sin 15^{\circ} i -(120 mm ) \cos 15^{\circ} j +(160 mm ) k\)

The equivalent forces acting at H and K are found from the sum of \(F _{1} \text { and } F _{2}\).

\(\begin{aligned}F_{x} &=-(2,400 N ) \cos 15^{\circ}-(400 N ) \cos 15^{\circ} \\&=-2,318.222 N -386.370 N =-2,704.592 N \\F_{y} &=-(2,400 N ) \sin 15^{\circ}+(400 N ) \sin 15^{\circ} \\&=-621.166 N +103.528 N =-517.638 N \\F_{z} &=0 N\end{aligned}\)

 

The equivalent moments are found from the sum of the cross-products \(r _{1} \times F _{1} \text { and } r _{2} \times F _{2}\).

\(\begin{aligned}r _{1} \times F _{1} &=\left|\begin{array}{ccc} i & j & k \\-31.058 & 115.911 & 160 \\-2,318.222 & -621.166 & 0\end{array}\right| \\&=99,386.513 N – mm i -370,915.517 N – mm j +288,000 N – mm k\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}r _{2} \times F _{2} &=\left|\begin{array}{ccc} i & j & k \\-31.058 & -115.911 & 160 \\-386.370 & 103.528 & 0\end{array}\right| \\&=-16,564.419 N – mm i -61,819.253 N – mm j -48,000 N – mm k\end{aligned}\)

Summing these results gives the equivalent moments:

\(\begin{aligned}M_{x} &=99,386.513 N – mm -16,564.419 N – mm \\&=82,822.094 N – mm \\M_{y} &=-370,915.517 N – mm -61,819.253 N – mm \\&=-432,734.770 N – mm \\M_{z} &=288,000 N – mm -48,000 N – mm \\&=240,000 N – mm\end{aligned}\)

 

Each of the non-zero forces and moments will be evaluated to determine whether stresses are created at the point of interest.

 

(a) Consider point K.
Force \(F _{ x }\) does not cause either a normal stress or a shear stress at K.

Force \(F _{ y }\) creates a transverse shear stress in the yz plane at K. The magnitude of this shear stress is:

\(\tau_{y z}=\frac{(517.638 N )\left(3,888 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(82,447.958 mm ^{4}\right)(36 mm )}=0.678 MPa\)

Moment \(M _{x}\) does not create bending stress at K because K is located on the neutral axis for bending about the x axis.

Moment \(M _{y}\) creates bending stress at K. The magnitude of this stress is:

\(\sigma_{z}=\frac{M_{y} x}{I_{y}}=\frac{(432,734.770 N – mm )(36 mm / 2)}{82,447.958 mm ^{4}}=94.474 MPa\)

Moment \(M _{z}\), which is a torque, creates a torsion shear stress in the yz plane at K. The magnitude of this shear stress is:

\(\tau_{y z}=\frac{M_{z} c}{J}=\frac{(240,000 N – mm )(36 mm / 2)}{164,895.915 mm ^{4}}=26.198 MPa\)

Summary of stresses at K:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{y} &=0 MPa \\\sigma_{z} &=94.474 MPa =94.5 MPa ( T ) \\\tau_{y z} &=-0.678 MPa +26.198 MPa \\&=25.520 MPa =25.5 MPa\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Principal stress calculations:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq. (12.12). For use in this equation, the negative z axis will be taken as the x axis, which causes the shear stress value to change sign.

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{(94.474)+(0)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(94.474)-(0)}{2}\right)^{2}+(-25.520)^{2}} \\&=47.237 \pm 53.690\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1} &=100.9 MPa \text { and } \quad \sigma_{p 2}=-6.45 MPa \\\tau_{\max } &=53.7 MPa (\text { maximum in-plane shear stress }) \\\sigma_{\text {avg }} &=47.2 MPa ( T ) \text { (normal stress on planes of maximum in-plane shear stress) }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan 2 \theta_{p} &=\frac{-25.520}{[(94.474)-(0)] / 2}=\frac{-25.520}{47.237}=-0.540258 \\&\left.\therefore \theta_{p}=-14.19^{\circ} \quad \text { (clockwise from the } z \text { axis to the direction of } \sigma_{p 1}\right)\end{aligned}\)
