Guest Question.

A mercury thermometer having first-order dynamics with a time constant of 60 s is placed in a bath at 308 K (\(35^{\circ}\)C). After the thermometer reaches a steady state it is suddenly placed in a bath at 313 K (\(40^{\circ}\)C) at t = 0 and left there for 60 s, after which it is immediately returned to the bath at 308 K (\(35^{\circ}\)C).
(a) Draw a sketch showing the variation of the thermometer reading with time.
(b) Calculate the thermometer reading at t = 30 s and at t = 120 s.
(c) What would be the reading at t = 6 s if the thermometer had only been immersed in the 313 K bath for less than 1 s before being returned to the 308 K bath?

Admin
Solution

(a) At t = 0 the thermometer is subjected to a step change of 5 deg K.

\(\frac{\overline{\vartheta}_{t_{1}}}{\overline{\vartheta}_{b}}=\frac{1}{1+60s}\)

\(\overline{\vartheta}_{b}=\frac{5}{s}\) at t = 0

Thus: \(\overline{\vartheta}_{t1}=\left (\frac{5}{s} \right )\left ( \frac{1}{1+60 s} \right )\)

\(=\frac{A}{s}+\frac{B}{1+60 s}\)

Thus: A = 5, B = -300

\(\overline{\vartheta }_{t_{1}}=\frac{5}{s}-\frac{300}{1+60 s}=\frac{5}{s}-\frac{5}{1/60+s}\) \(\overline{\vartheta }_{t_{1}}=5-5e^{-t/60}\)

=\(5(1-e^{-t/60})\)

After 60 s,\(\overline{\vartheta }_{t_{1}}=5(1-e^{-1})\)= 3.16 deg K

Hence after 60 s, the thermometer will read (308 + 3.16) = 311.16 K.

At t = 60 s, a further negative step change is imposed of 3.16 deg K. The thermometer will respond immediately to this as it has only first order dynamics.

Thus: \(\overline{\vartheta }_{t_{2}}=3.16(1-e^{-t/60})\)

(b) At t = 30 s, the thermometer reading is:

\(\vartheta_{t_{1}}=5(1-e^{-30/60})\)= 1.97 deg K

and: thermometer reading = 309.97 K

At t = 120 s, since a step decrease is applied at t = 60 s, then:

\(\vartheta_{t_{2}}=3.16(1-e^{-60/60})\)= 2 deg K

Thus: Thermometer reading at 120 s = (311.16 – 2)

= 309.16 K

(c) At t = 0, the thermometer is immersed for less than 1 s in the 313 K bath, hence assuming that an impulse applied is:

\(\frac{\overline{\vartheta }_{t_{1}}}{\overline{\vartheta }_{b}}=\frac{1}{1+60 s}\) \(F(t)_{Impulse}=\frac{d}{dt}\left \{ F(t) \right \}_{step}\)

Thus: \(=\frac{d}{dt}\left \{ 5(1-e^{-t/60}) \right \}=\frac{1}{12}e^{-t/60}\)

Thus at t = 6 s \(\vartheta _{t_{1}}=\frac{1}{12}e^{-0.1}=0.075\) deg K

and the thermometer will read 308.075 K
