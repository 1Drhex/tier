MASS FLOW RATE IN BOUNDARY LAYER

The fluid in direct contact with a stationary solid boundary has zero velocity; there is no slip at the boundary. Thus the flow over a flat plate adheres to the plate surface and forms a boundary layer, as depicted below. The flow ahead of the plate is uniform with velocity \(\vec{V}=U \hat{i} ; U=30 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\). The velocity distribution within the boundary layer \((0 \leq y \leq \delta)\) along \(c d\) is approximated as \(u/U=2(y / \delta)-(y / \delta)^{2}\).

The boundary-layer thickness at location \(d\) is \(\delta=5 \mathrm{~mm}\). The fluid is air with density \(\rho=1.24 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\). Assuming the plate width perpendicular to the paper to be \(w=0.6 \mathrm{~m}\), calculate the mass flow rate across surface \(b c\) of control volume \(a b c d\).

Admin
Solution

Given: Steady, incompressible flow over a flat plate, \(\rho=1.24 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\). Width of plate, \(w=0.6 \mathrm{~m}\).
Velocity ahead of plate is uniform: \(\vec{V}=U \hat{i}, U=30 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\).
\( \begin{aligned}\text { At } \quad x &=x_{d} \\\delta &=5 \mathrm{~mm} \\\frac{u}{U} &=2\left(\frac{y}{\delta}\right)-\left(\frac{y}{\delta}\right)^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Find: Mass flow rate across surface \(b c\).

Solution: The fixed control volume is shown by the dashed lines.
Governing equation: The general control volume equation is Eq. 4.12 (\(\frac{\partial}{\partial t} \int_{\mathrm{CV}} \rho d \forall+\int_{\mathrm{CS}} \rho \vec{V} \cdot d \vec{A}=0\)), but we can go immediately to Eq. 4.15a
because of assumption (1) below,

\(\int_{\mathrm{CS}} \rho \vec{V} \cdot d \vec{A}=0\)

Assumptions:

(1) Steady flow (given).
(2) Incompressible flow (given).
(3) Two-dimensional flow, given properties are independent of \(z\).
Assuming that there is no flow in the \(z\) direction, then

\(\int_{A_{a b}} \rho \vec{V} \cdot d \vec{A}+\int_{A_{b c}} \rho \vec{V} \cdot d \vec{A}+\int_{A_{c d}} \rho \vec{V} \cdot d \vec{A}+\int_{A_{d a}} \rho \cancel{\vec V}^{\text{(no flow across da)}} \cdot d \vec{A}=0 \)

 

\(\therefore \dot{m}_{b c}=\int_{A_{b c}} \rho \vec{V} \cdot d \vec{A}=-\int_{A_{a b}} \rho \vec{V} \cdot d \vec{A}-\int_{A_{c d}} \rho \vec{V} \cdot d \vec{A}\) (1)
We need to evaluate the integrals on the right side of the equation.

For depth \(w\) in the \(z\) direction, we obtain
\(\begin{aligned}\int_{A_{a b}} \rho \vec{V} \cdot d \vec{A} &=-\int_{A_{a b}} \rho u d A=-\int_{y_{a}}^{y_{b}} \rho u w d y \\&=-\int_{0}^{\delta} \rho u w d y=-\int_{0}^{\delta} \rho U w d y \\\int_{A_{a b}} \rho \vec{V} \cdot d \vec{A} &=-[\rho U w y]_{0}^{\delta}=-\rho U w \delta \end{aligned}\)

\(\begin{gathered}\left\{\begin{array}{c}\vec{V} \cdot d \vec{A} \text { is negative } \\d A=w d y\end{array}\right\} \\\{u=U \text { over area } a b\}\end{gathered}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\int_{A_{c d}} \rho \vec{V} \cdot d \vec{A} &=\int_{A_{c d}} \rho u d A=\int_{y_{d}}^{y_{c}} \rho u w d y \\&=\int_{0}^{\delta} \rho u w d y=\int_{0}^{\delta} \rho w U\left[2\left(\frac{y}{\delta}\right)-\left(\frac{y}{\delta}\right)^{2}\right] d y \\\int_{A_{c d}} \rho \vec{V} \cdot d \vec{A} &=\rho w U\left[\frac{y^{2}}{\delta}-\frac{y^{3}}{3 \delta^{2}}\right]_{0}^{\delta}=\rho w U \delta\left[1-\frac{1}{3}\right]=\frac{2 \rho U w \delta}{3}\end{aligned}\)

\(\{\begin{array}{c}\vec{V} \cdot d \vec{A} \text { is positive } \\d A=w d y\end{array}\}\)

Substituting into Eq. 1, we obtain

\(\begin{aligned}\therefore \dot{m}_{b c} &=\rho U w \delta-\frac{2 \rho U w \delta}{3}=\frac{\rho U w \delta}{3} \\&=\frac{1}{3} \times 1.24 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}} \times 30 \frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}} \times 0.6 \mathrm{~m} \times 5 \mathrm{~mm} \times \frac{\mathrm{m}}{1000 \mathrm{~mm}}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\dot{m}_{b c} = 0,0372 kg/s\) \(\xleftarrow[]{{\left\{\begin{array}{l}\text { Positive sign indicates flow } \\\text { out across surface } b c .\end{array}\right\}\dot{m}_{b c}}}\)

 

This problem demonstrates use of the conservation of mass equation when we have nonuniform flow at a section.
