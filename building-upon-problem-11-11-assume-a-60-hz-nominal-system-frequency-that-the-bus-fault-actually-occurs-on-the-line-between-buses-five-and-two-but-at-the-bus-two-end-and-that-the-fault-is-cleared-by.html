Guest Question.

Building upon , assume a 60 Hz nominal system frequency, that the bus fault actually occurs on the line between buses five and two but at the bus two end, and that the fault is cleared by opening breakers B21 and B52. Again, neglecting the loads, assume that the generator at bus three is modeled with the classical generator model having a per unit value (on its 800 MVA base) of \(X _{d}^{\prime}=0.24, \text { and } H =3\) p.u.-s. Before the fault occurs the generator is delivering 300 MW into the infinite bus at unity power factor (hence its terminal voltage is not 1.05 as was assumed in ). Further, assume the fault is cleared after 3 cycles. Determine: (a) the initial generator one power angle, (b) the power angle when the fault is cleared, and (c) the maximum value of the power angle using the equal area criteria.

Admin
Solution

If gen 3 \(X_{d}^{\prime}=0.24\) on an 800 MVA base, then it is 0.03 on a 100 MVA base.

 

(a)

\(\begin{aligned}X_{e q} &=0.02+[0.025 \| 0.15]+0.04=0.0814 \\I &=\left(\frac{3.00}{1 \angle 0}\right)^{*}=3 \angle 0 \quad E^{\prime}=1.0+j 0.0814 .3 \angle 0 \\E^{\prime} &=1+j 0.2442=1.0294 \angle 13.7^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) The faulted network is

\(\begin{aligned}X_{e q} &=0.03+0.01+\{0.1 \|[0.025+(0.02 \| 0.05)]\} \\&=0.0682 \\V_{e q} &=1 \angle 0 \cdot \frac{0.0357}{0.0557} \cdot \frac{0.1}{0.125}=0.5130 \angle 0\end{aligned}\)

\(\begin{aligned}P_{e 2} &=\frac{1.0294 \cdot 0.513}{0.0682} \sin \delta \\&=7.742 \sin \delta\end{aligned}\)

Once the fault is cleared, the network becomes

\(\begin{aligned}P_{e 3} &=\frac{1.0294 \cdot 1}{0.085} \sin \delta \\&=12.11 \sin \delta\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\delta_{0}=13.7^{\circ}=0.239 rad \\&\left(\frac{4}{2 \pi 60}\right) \frac{d^{2} \delta(t)}{d t^{2}}=p_{m p . u .}-p_{\text {ep.u. }}=1-12.11 \sin \delta(t) \\&\left(\frac{4}{2 \pi 60}\right) \frac{d \delta(t)}{d t}=t+12.11 \cos \delta(t)+0 \\&\left(\frac{4}{2 \pi 60}\right) \delta(t)=\frac{1}{2} t^{2}+12.11 \sin \delta(t)+0.239 \\&\left(\frac{4}{2 \pi 60}\right) \delta(0.05)=\frac{1}{2}(0.05)^{2}+12.11 \sin \delta(0.05)+0.239 \\&0.011 \cdot \delta(0.05)=0.0013+12.11 \sin \delta(0.05)+0.239 \\&0.011 \cdot \delta(0.05)=0.2402+12.11 \sin \delta(0.05)\end{aligned}\)

Solving iteratively, the power angle when the fault is cleared is:

\(\begin{aligned}&0.011 \cdot \delta(0.05)=0.2402+12.11 \sin \delta(0.05) \\&\delta(0.05)==0.020 rad =-1.15^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

 

(c) Using the equal area criteria, we can solve for the maximum value of the power angle.

\(\begin{aligned}&\left(\frac{2 H}{2 \pi 60}\right) \frac{d^{2} \delta(t)}{d t^{2}}=p_{m p . u .} \\&\delta(0)=\delta_{0}=0.239 r a d \\&\frac{d \delta(0)}{d t}=0 \\&\left(\frac{d \delta(t)}{d t}\right)=\frac{2 \pi 60 \cdot p_{m p . u} \cdot t}{2 H}+0\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\delta(t)=\frac{2 \pi 60 \cdot p_{m p . u .} \cdot t^{2}}{4 H}+\delta_{0} \\&\delta_{1}=\frac{2 \pi 60}{12}(0.05)^{2}+0.239=0.3175\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&A_{1}=\int_{\delta_{0}}^{\delta_{1}} p_{m p . u } d \delta=\int_{\delta_{0}}^{\delta_{1}} 1 d \delta=0.3175-0.239=0.0785 \\&A_{2}=\int_{\delta_{1}}^{\delta_{2}} p_{\max } \sin \delta-p_{m p . u .} d \delta=\int_{0.3175}^{\delta_{2}} 12.11 \sin \delta-1 d \delta=A_{1}=0.0785\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&12.11\left[\cos (0.3175)-\cos \delta_{2}\right]-\left(\delta_{2}-0.3175\right)=0.0785 \\&12.11 \cos \delta_{2}+\delta_{2}=11.744\end{aligned}\)

 

Solving iteratively, the maximum power angle is:

\(\delta_{2}=-0.177 rad =-10.148^{\circ}\)
