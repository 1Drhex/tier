Guest Question.

The grain-size distribution curve for a sand is shown in Figure 5.10. Estimate the hydraulic conductivity using Eq (5.26). \(k=1.99 \times 10^{4}\left[\frac{100 \%}{\sum \frac{f_{i}}{D_{l i}^{0.404} \times D_{s i}^{0.595}}}\right]^{2}\left(\frac{1}{S F}\right)^{2}\left(\frac{e^{3}}{1+e}\right)\)

Given: the void ratio of the sand is 0.6. Use SF = 7.

Admin
Solution

From Figure 5.10, the following table can be prepared.

\(\textbf{Sieve no.}\) \(\textbf{Sieve opening (cm)}\) \(\textbf{Percent passing}\) \(\textbf{Fraction of particles between two consecutive sieves }(\%)\)
30 0.06 100
40 0.0425 96 4
60 0.02 84 12
100 0.015 50 34
200 0.0075 0 50

For fraction between Nos. 30 and 40 sieves:

\(\frac{f_{i}}{D^{0.404}_{li} \times D^{0.595}_{si}} = \frac{4}{(0.06)^{0.404} \times (0.0425)^{0.595}} = 81.62\)

For fraction between Nos. 40 and 60 sieves:

\(\frac{f_{i}}{D^{0.404}_{li} \times D^{0.595}_{si}} = \frac{12}{(0.0425)^{0.404} \times (0.02)^{0.595}} = 440.76\)

Similarly, for fraction between Nos. 60 and 100 sieves:

\(\frac{f_{i}}{D^{0.404}_{li} \times D^{0.595}_{si}} = \frac{34}{(0.02)^{0.404} \times (0.015)^{0.595}} = 2009.5\)

And, for between Nos. 100 and 200 sieves:

\(\frac{f_{i}}{D^{0.404}_{li} \times D^{0.595}_{si}} = \frac{50}{(0.015)^{0.404} \times (0.0075)^{0.595}} = 5013.8\\\) \(\frac{100 \%}{\sum{\frac{f_{i}}{D^{0.404}_{li} \times D^{0.595}_{si}}}} = \frac{100}{81.62 + 440.76 + 2009.5 + 5013.8} \approx 0.0133\)

From Eq. (5.26),

\(k = (1.99 \times 10^{4})(0.0133)^{2}(\frac{1}{7})^{2} (\frac{0.6^{3}}{1 + 0.6}) = \textbf{0.0097 cm/s}\)
