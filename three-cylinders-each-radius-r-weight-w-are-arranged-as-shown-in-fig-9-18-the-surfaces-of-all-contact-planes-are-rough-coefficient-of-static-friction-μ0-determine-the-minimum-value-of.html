Guest Question.

Three cylinders (each radius r, weight W) are arranged as shown in Fig. 9.18. The surfaces of all contact planes are rough (coefficient of static friction\(\mu _{0}\)).

Determine the minimum value of \(\mu _{0}\) in order to prevent slipping.

Admin
Solution

First we separate the cylinders and draw the freebody diagram (note the symmetry of the system; the normal force between the two lower cylinders is zero). Moment equilibrium shows that all the forces of static friction are equal:

\(H_{A} = H_{B} =H_{C}=H_{D} =H\) .

They are thus simply denoted by H. Then we write down the force equilibrium at the upper cylinder

\(\rightarrow : N_{A} \sin 30^{\circ } – N_{B} \sin 30^{\circ } =0 \rightarrow N_{A}= N_{B} =N\) ,

\(\uparrow : 2N \cos 30^{\circ } +2H \sin 30^{\circ }=W\)

and at the lower cylinder

\(\rightarrow : -N \sin 30^{\circ }+H \cos 30^{\circ } +H =0\),

\(\uparrow : N_{C} -W -N \cos 30^{\circ } -H \sin 30^{\circ } =0\) .

Solving these equations yields

\(N =\frac{W}{2} , N_{C}=\frac{3}{2} W , H=\frac{\sin 30^{\circ }}{2(1+ \cos 30^{\circ } )} W=\frac{W}{2(2+\sqrt{3} )}\) .

The conditions of static friction between the cylinders

\(\mu _{0} \gt \frac{H}{N_{A} } =\frac{1}{2+\sqrt{3} } =0.268\)

and between the lower cylinders and the base

\(\mu _{0} \gt \frac{H}{N_{C} } = \frac{1}{3(2+\sqrt{3}) }=0.089\)

have to be satisfied in order to avoid slipping. Thus, the minimum value of \(\mu _{0}\) is obtained as

\(\underline{\underline{\mu _{0} =0.268} }\) .
