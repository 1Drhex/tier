Guest Question.

A rope hangs between two bearings at the sa-me height and is loaded by a triangular line load with the maximal amplitude \(q_{0}\) in the middle of the rope. Determine the force in the ro-pe at the lowest point of the rope and the shape of the rope curve.

Admin
Solution

Choosing the coordinate system in the middle between the bearings, the load is given by

\(q(x)=q_{0}(1-\frac{x}{a} ), 0\leq x\leq a.\)

Integrating the differential equation of the rope curve twice yields

\(\frac{H}{q_{0}}z^{\prime \prime }=\frac{x}{a}-1 ,\)

 

\(\frac{H}{q_{0}}z^{\prime }=\frac{x^{2}}{2a}-x+C_{1},\)

 

\(\frac{H}{q_{0}}z =\frac{x^{3}}{6a}-\frac{x^{2}}{2}+C_{1}x+C_{2}.\)

We obtain the integration constants from the boundary conditions:

\(z^{\prime }(0)=0 \rightsquigarrow C_{1}=0, z(a)=0 \rightsquigarrow C_{2}=\frac{a^{2}}{3}.\)

The curve of the rope then reads (symmetry!)

\(\underline{\underline{z=\frac{q_{0}}{H}\left(\frac{x^{3}}{6a}-\frac{x^{2}}{2}+\frac{a^{2}}{3} \right) } } , 0\leq x\leq a.\)

The horizontal force H is calculated from the boundary condition

\(z(0)=h \rightsquigarrow h=\frac{q_{0}}{H}\frac{a^{2}}{3} \rightsquigarrow H=\frac{q_{0}a^{2}}{3h}.\)

Then the force in the rope at the lowest point follows as

\(S=H\sqrt{1+(z^{\prime })^{2}}\) mit \(z^{\prime }(0)=0 \rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{S=H=\frac{q_{0}a^{2}}{3h} } }.\)
