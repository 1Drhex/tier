Guest Question.

Let \( W = \left \{ \begin{matrix} \left [ \begin{matrix} a \\ b \\ c \end{matrix} \right ] : a - 3b - c = 0 \end{matrix} \right \} \) . Show in two different ways that W is a subspace of \( \mathbb{R} ^{3} \). (Use two theorems.)

Admin
Solution

First method: W is a subspace of \( \mathbb{R} ^{3} \) by Theorem 2 because W is the set of all solutions to a system of homogeneous linear equations (where the system has only one equation). Equivalently, W is the null space of the 1 × 3 matrix A = [ 1 -3 -1 ].

Second method: Solve the equation a -3b –c = 0 for the leading variable a in terms of the free variables b and c. Any solution has the form \( \left [ \begin{matrix} 3b + c \\ b \\ c \end{matrix} \right ] \), where b and c are arbitrary, and

\( \left [ \begin{matrix} 3b + c \\ b \\ c \end{matrix} \right ] = b \underset{\overset{\uparrow}{v_{1}}}{\left [ \begin{matrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ] } + c \underset{\overset{\uparrow}{v_{2}}}{\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ] } \)

This calculation shows that W = Span {\( v_{1} \), \( v_{2} \)}. Thus W is a subspace of \( \mathbb{R} ^{3} \) by Theorem 1. We could also solve the equation a -3b –c = 0 for b or c and get alternative descriptions of W as a set of linear combinations of two vectors.

THEOREM 1 :

If \( v_{1}, . . . ,v_{p} \) are in a vector space V , then Span {\( v_{1}, . . . ,v_{p} \)} is a subspace of V .

THEOREM 2 :

The null space of an m × n matrix A is a subspace of \( \mathbb{R} ^{n} \). Equivalently, the set of all solutions to a system Ax = 0 of m homogeneous linear equations in n unknowns is a subspace of \( \mathbb{R} ^{n} \).
