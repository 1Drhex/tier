Guest Question.

On a hot and sunny day, the concrete deck surrounding a swimming pool is at a temperature of \(T_{d}=55^{\circ}C\). A swimmer walks across the dry deck to the pool. The soles of the swimmer’s dry feet are characterized by an \(L_{sf}=3-mm-\) thick skin/fat layer of thermal conductivity \(k_{sf}=0.3W/m\cdot K\). Consider two types of concrete decking; (i) a dense stone mix and (ii) a lightweight aggregate characterized by density, specific heat, and thermal conductivity of \(\rho _{1w}=1495 kg/m^{3},c_{1w}=880J/kg\), and \(k_{1w}=0.28W/m\cdot K\), respectively. The density and specific heat of the skin/fat layer may be approximated to be those of liquid water, and the skin/fat layer is at an initial temperature of \(T_{sf,i}=37^{\circ}C\). What is the temperature of the bottom of the swimmer’s feet after an elapsed time of t = 1 s?

Admin
Solution

Known: Concrete temperature, initial foot temperature, and thickness of skin/fat layer on the sole of the foot. Skin/fat and lightweight aggregate concrete properties.

Find: The temperature of the bottom of the swimmer’s feet after 1 s.

Assumptions:
1. One-dimensional conduction in the x-direction.
2. Constant and uniform properties.
3. Negligible contact resistance.

 

Properties: stone mix concrete

\((T=300 K):\rho _{sm}=2300 kg/m^{3}, k_{sm}=1.4W/m\cdot K, c_{sm}=880 J/kg\cdot K\). water \((T=310 K):\rho _{sf}=993 kg/m^{3}, c_{sf}= 4178 J/kg\cdot K\).

 

Analysis: If the skin/fat layer and the deck are both semi-infinite media, from Equation 5.66 the surface temperature \(T_{s}\) is constant when the swimmer’s foot is in contact with the deck. For the lightweight aggregate concrete decking, the thermal penetration depth at t = 1 s is

 

\(T_{s}=\frac{(k\rho c)_{A}^{1/2}T_{A,i}+(k\rho c)_{B}^{1/2}T_{B,i}}{(k\rho c)_{A}^{1/2}+(k\rho c)_{B}^{1/2}}\) (5.66)

 

\(\delta _{p,lw}=2.3\sqrt{\alpha _{lw}t}=2.3\sqrt{\frac{k_{1w}t}{\rho _{1w}c_{1w}}}=2.3\sqrt{\frac{0.28 W/m\cdot K\times 1 s}{1495 kg/m^{3}\times 880 J/kg\cdot K}}\)

 

\(=1.06\times 10^{-3}m=1.06mm\)

Since the thermal penetration depth is relatively small, it is reasonable to assume that the lightweight aggregate deck behaves as a semi-infinite medium. Similarly, the thermal penetration depth in the stone mix concrete is \(\delta _{p,sm}=1.91 mm\), and the thermal penetration depth associated with the skin/fat layer of the foot is \(\delta _{p,sf} = 0.62 mm\). Hence, it is reasonable to assume that the stone mix concrete deck responds as a semi-infinite medium, and, since \(\delta _{p,sf}< L_{sf},\) it is also correct to assume that the skin/fat layer behaves as a semi-infinite medium. Therefore, Equation 5.66 may be used to determine the surface temperature of the swimmer’s foot for exposure to the two types of concrete decking. For the lightweight aggregate,

 

\(T_{s,lw}=\frac{(k\rho c)_{1w}^{1/2}T_{d,i}+(k\rho c)_{sf}^{1/2}T_{sf,i}}{(k\rho c)_{1w}^{1/2}+(k\rho c)_{sf}^{1/2}}\)

 

\(=\frac{\left [ \begin{matrix} (0.28W/m\cdot K\times 1495kg/m^{3}&\times 880 J/kg\cdot K)^{1/2}\times 55^{\circ}C\\ + (0.3W/m\cdot K\times 993 kg/m^{3}&\times 4178 J/kg\cdot K)^{1/2}\times 37^{\circ}C \end{matrix} \right ]}{\left [ \begin{matrix} (0.28W/m\cdot K\times 1495kg/m^{3}&\times 880 J/kg\cdot K)^{1/2}\\+ (0.3W/m\cdot K\times 993 kg/m^{3}&\times 4178 J/kg\cdot K)^{1/2} \end{matrix} \right ]}=43.3C^{\circ}\)

 

Repeating the calculation for the stone mix concrete gives \(T_{s,sm}=47.8^{\circ}C\).

 

Comments:
1. The lightweight aggregate concrete feels cooler to the swimmer, relative to the stone mix concrete. Specifically, the temperature rise from the initial skin/fat temperature that is associated with the stone mix concrete is

 

 

\(\Delta T_{sm}=T_{sm}-T_{sf,i}=47.8^{\circ}C-37^{\circ}C=10.8^{\circ}C,\) whereas the temperature rise associated with the lightweight aggregate is \(\Delta T_{1w}=T_{1w}-T_{sf,i}=43.3^{\circ}C- 37^{\circ}C=6.3^{\circ}C\)

.

2. The thermal penetration depths associated with an exposure time of t = 1 s are small. Stones and air pockets within the concrete may be of the same size as the thermal penetration depth, making the uniform property assumption somewhat questionable. The predicted foot temperatures should be viewed as representative values.
