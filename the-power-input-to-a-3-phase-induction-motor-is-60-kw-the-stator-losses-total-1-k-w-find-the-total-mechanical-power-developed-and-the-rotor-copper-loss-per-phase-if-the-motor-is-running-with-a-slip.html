Guest Question.

The power input to a 3-phase induction motor is 60 kW. The stator losses total 1 k W. Find the total mechanical power developed and the rotor copper loss per phase if the motor is running with a slip of 3%.

Admin
Solution

Total power input to the stator 60 kW

 

Total stator losses = 1 kW

 

Therefore total input to the rotor = 59 kW

 

Using equation 8.15 we have \(\frac{3{I_{2}}^{'2}{R_{2}}^{'}}{s}=59\times 1000 \) watts

 

Hence \(\frac{3{I_{2}}^{'2}{R_{2}}^{'}}{0.03}=59000\)

 

Hence rotor copper loss \(={I_{2}}^{'2}{R_{2}}^{'}=\frac{59000\times 0.03}{3}=590 \) watt per phase

 

and mechanical power developed = rotor input – rotor copper loss

 

\(=59000-590\times 3=57.23 \) KW.

 

It is to be noted that the parameters of the induction motor \((R_{1}R_{2}^{'},X_{1}X_{2}^{'},R_{0},X_{0}) \) can be obtained on the similar lines as in case of a transformer by performing short circuit (blocked rotor) and open circuit (light running) tests.
