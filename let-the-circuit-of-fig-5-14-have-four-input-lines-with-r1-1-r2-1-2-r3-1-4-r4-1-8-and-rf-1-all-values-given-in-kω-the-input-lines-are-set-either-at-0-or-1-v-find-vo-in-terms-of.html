Guest Question.

Let the circuit of Fig. 5-14 have four input lines with \( R_{1}=1, R_{2}=\frac{1}{2}, R_{3}=\frac{1}{4}, R_{4}=\frac{1}{8} \text {, and } R_{f}=1 \), all values given in k立. The input lines are set either at 0 or 1 V. Find \( v_{o} \text { in terms of } v_{4}, v_{3}, v_{2}, v_{1} \), given the following sets of inputs:

(a) \( v_{4}=1 V \quad v_{3}=0 \quad v_{2}=0 \quad v_{1}=1 V \)

(b) \( v_{4}=1 V \quad v_{3}=1 V \quad v_{2}=1 V \quad v_{1}=0 \)

Admin
Solution

From (11)

\( v_{o}=-\left(8 v_{4}+4 v_{3}+2 v_{2}+v_{1}\right) \)

Substituting for \( v_{1} \text { to } v_{4} \) we obtain

(a) \( v_{o}=-9 V \)

(b) \( v_{o}=-14 V \)

The set \( \left\{v_{4}, v_{3}, v_{2}, v_{1}\right\} \) forms a binary sequence containing four bits at high (1 V) or low (0 V) values. Input sets given in (a) and (b) correspond to the binary numbers \( (1001)_{2}=(9)_{10} \text { and }(1110)_{2}=(14)_{10} \), respectively. With the inputs at 0 V (low) or 1 V (high), the circuit converts the binary number represented by the input set \( \left\{v_{4}, v_{3}, v_{2}, v_{1}\right\} \) to a negative voltage which, when measured in V, is equal to the base 10 representation of the input set. The circuit is a digital-to-analog converter.
