Guest Question.

The production manager of a manufacturing firm has tracked the average repair and maintenance cost of a machine for 10 years. Cost averages were steady at ₹20,000 for the first five years, but have increased consistently by ₹5,000 for each of the last 5 years. Calculate the equivalent present worth and annual worth of the costs, if the rate of interest is 6% per year.

Admin
Solution

The cash flow diagram is shown in Fig. 4.41.
It can be seen from Fig. 4.41 that the base amount is ₹20,000 and the arithmetic gradient G = ₹5,000 starting from the end of year 6. Figure 4.42 (a) & (b) partitions these two series.

The present worth of the cash flow diagram shown in Fig. 4.42(a) i.e. \(P_{A}\) is obtained as:

\(P_{A}\) = A(P/A, i%, n)
 = ₹20,000(P/A, 6%, 10)
 = ₹20,000(7.3601)
 = ₹1,47,202

In order to calculate the present worth of the shifted arithmetic gradient series shown in Fig. 4.42(b), first present worth of the arithmetic gradient G = ₹5,000 is calculated as:

\(P_{G}\) = G(P/G, i%, 6)
 = ₹5,000(P/G, 6%, 6)
 = ₹5,000(11.4594)
 = ₹57,297

The \(P_{G}\) will be located two periods before gradient starts i.e. at the end of period 4 (Fig. 4.42(b)).
Considering \(P_{G}\) calculated above as future worth, the present worth \(P_{G}^{\prime}\) is calculated as;

\(P_{G}^{\prime}\) = F(P/F, i%, n)
 = ₹57,297(P/F, 6%, 4)
 = ₹57,297(0.7921)
 = ₹45,384.95

The equivalent present worth of the cash flow diagram shown in Fig. 4.41 i.e. \(P_{T}\) is obtained as:

\(P_{T}\) = \(P_{A}\) + \(P_{G}^{\prime}\)
= ₹1,47,202 + ₹45,384.95
 = ₹1,92,586.95

The equivalent annual worth \(A_{T}\) for the above calculated \(P_{T}\) is obtained as:

\(A_{T}\) = \(P_{T}\)(A/P, i%, n)
 = ₹1,92,586.95(A/P, 6%, 10)
 = ₹1,92,586.95(0.13587)
 = ₹26,166.79
