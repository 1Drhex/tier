Guest Question.

Determine the speed, size, and required power for the axial-flow pump of Fig. \(12.13\) to deliver \(0.65 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{s}\) of water at a head of \(2.5 \mathrm{~m}\).

Admin
Solution

The pump data are obtained from Fig. 12.13. Reading from the figure we find that at the design, or maximum, efficiency of \(\eta_{P}=0.75\)

\(C_{Q}=0.048 \quad C_{H}=0.018 \quad C_{W}=0.0012\)

Equations \(12.3 .4\) and \(12.3 .6\) are employed to determine the two unknowns \(\omega\) and \(D\). Rearrange Eq. \(12.3 .6\) to solve for \(\omega D\) :

\(C_{Q}=\frac{Q}{\omega D^{3}} \) flow rate coefficient (12.3.4)

\(C_{H}=\frac{g H}{\omega^{2} D^{2}} \quad \) head coefficient (12.3.6)

\(\omega D=\sqrt{\frac{g H_{P}}{C_{H}}}\)\(=\sqrt{\frac{9.81 \times 2.5}{0.018}}=36.9 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\)

Equation 12.3.4 is rearranged to include \(\omega D\) as a known quantity to provide \(D\) :

\(D=\sqrt{\frac{Q}{C_{Q} \omega D}}=\sqrt{\frac{0.65}{0.048 \times 36.9}}=0.61 \mathrm{~m}\)

Substituting \(D=0.61 \mathrm{~m}\) into the relation \(\omega D=36.9\), one finds that

\(\omega=\frac{36.9}{D}=\frac{36.9}{0.61}=60.5 \mathrm{~rad} / \mathrm{s} \quad \text { or } \quad 578 \mathrm{~rpm}\)

The density for water is \(\rho=1000 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\). Then the pump power is determined by using Eq. \(12.3 .2:\)

\(C_{\dot{W}}=\frac{\dot{W}}{\rho \omega^{3} D^{5}} \quad \text { power coefficient }\) (12.3.2)

\(\dot{W}_{P}=C_{W} \rho \omega^{3} D^{5}=0.0012 \times 1000 \times 60.5^{3} \times 0.61^{5}=2.2 \times 10^{4} \mathrm{~W}\)

Thus the speed, size, and required power are approximately \(580 \mathrm{~rpm}, 610 \mathrm{~mm}\), and \(22 \mathrm{~kW}\), respectively.
