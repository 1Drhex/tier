Solution

1: With a 50-hp motor driving at 1725 rpm, Figure 11.4 suggests using a 5V belt.

2: The respective shaft speeds are as follows:

\(\omega _1=1725\ rev/min(\frac{2\pi \ rad}{1\ rev} )=10,838\ rev/min\)

\(\omega _2=600\ rev/min(\frac{2\pi \ rad}{1\ rev} )=3770\ rev/min\)

A belt speed of 4000 to 5000 fpm is optimal. Rewriting equation \(VR=\frac{\omega _1}{\omega _2}=\frac{r_2}{r_1}=\frac{d_2}{d_1}\) yields the following:

\(d_1=2(\frac{v_b}{\omega _1} )=2(\frac{4500\ ft/min}{10,838\ rad/min} )\)

\(=0.83\ ft=9.96\ in.\)

3: Selecting a driver sheave of 10.20 in. from Table 11.1 yields a belt speed of

\(v_b=\frac{d_1}{2}\omega _1=\frac{10.20\ in.}{2}(10,838\ rad/min)=55,274\ in./min=4606\ fpm\)

4: From equation \(v_b=r_1\omega _2=\frac{d_1}{2}\omega _1=r_2\omega =\frac{d_2}{2}\omega _2 \), the desired velocity ratio is determined by

\(VR=\frac{\omega _1}{\omega _2}=\frac{10,838\ rad/min}{3770\ rad/min}=2.87\)

And the resulting driven sheave diameter is calculated as follows:

\(d_2\) = (VR)(\(d_1\)) = 2.87(10.2 in.) = 29.3 in.

The closest stock sheave of 27.9 in. is selected. Rewriting equation \(v_b=r_1\omega _2=\frac{d_1}{2}\omega _1=r_2\omega =\frac{d_2}{2}\omega _2 \), the actual grinding wheel speed is

\(\omega _2=\frac{\omega _1d_1}{d_2}=\frac{(1725\ rpm)(10.2\ in.)}{27.9\ in.}=630\ rpm\)

5: The suggested center distance for belt drives is within the following range

\(d_2 < C < 3(d_1 + d_2)\)

27.9 in. < C < 114.3 in.

A mid-value of 72 in. is tentatively selected. Substituting into equation \(L=2C+\frac{\pi }{2}(d_2+d_1)+\frac{(d_2-d_1)^2}{4C}\) gives

\(L=2C+\frac{\pi }{2}(d_2+d_1)+\frac{(d_2-d_1)^2}{4C}\)

\(=2(72\ in.)+\frac{\pi }{2}(27.9+10.2)+\frac{(27.9-10.2)^2}{4(72)}=204.9\ in.\)

Because a standard length of belt is desired, a 212-in. belt will be selected from Table 11.2. Equations \(B=4L-2\pi (d_2+d_1)\) and \(\theta _1=180^\circ -2sin^{-1}\left\{\frac{d_2-d_1}{2C} \right\}\) are used to calculate the required, actual center distance.
