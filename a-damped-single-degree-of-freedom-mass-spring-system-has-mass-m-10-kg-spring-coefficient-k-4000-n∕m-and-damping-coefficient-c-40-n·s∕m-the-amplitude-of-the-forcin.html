Guest Question.

A damped single degree of freedom mass–spring system has mass m = 10 kg, spring coefficient k = 4000 N∕m, and damping coefficient c = 40 N·s∕m. The amplitude of the forcing function \(F_0\) = 60 N, and the frequency \(\omega_f\) = 40 rad∕s. Determine the displacement of the mass as a function of time. Determine also the transmissibility and the amplitude of the force transmitted to the support.

Admin
Solution

The circular frequency of the system is

\( \omega =\sqrt{\frac{k}{m} } = \sqrt{\frac{4000}{10} } = 20\) rad/s

The frequency ratio r is given by

\( r=\frac{\omega _f}{\omega } =\frac{40}{20} =2\)

The critical damping coefficient \(C_c\) is defined as

\( C_c = 2m\omega =2(10)(20)= 400\) N·s∕m

The damping factor ξ is then given by

\( \xi =\frac{c}{C_c}=\frac{40}{400} = 0.1 \)

which is the case of an underdamped system. One can then write the complete solution in the following form:

\( x(t) = x_h + x_p\)

 

\( = Xe^{-\xi \omega t}\sin (\omega _dt+\phi )+X_0\beta \sin (\omega _ft-\psi )\)

where \(ω_d\) is the damped circular frequency

\( \omega _d=\omega \sqrt{1-\xi ^2} = 20\sqrt{1-(0.1)^2} = 19.8997 \) rad∕s

The constants \(X_0\), β, and ψ are

\(X_0=\frac{F_0}{k} = \frac{60}{4000} =0.015 \) m
\(\beta =\frac{1}{\sqrt{(1-r)^2+(2r\xi )^2} }=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\left(1-(2)^2\right)^2 + (2\times 2\times 0.1)^2} } =0.3304\)
\(\psi =\tan ^{-1}(\frac{2r\xi }{1-r^2} )= \tan ^{-1}(\frac{2(2)(0.1) }{1-(2)^2} )= \tan ^{-1} (0.13333) = 0.13255\) rad

The displacement can then be written as a function of time as

\( x(t) = Xe^{-2 t}\sin (19.8997t+\phi )+0.004956 \sin (40t-0.13255 )\)

The constants X and ϕ can be determined using the initial conditions. The transmissibility \( \beta _t\) is defined as

\(\beta_t =\frac{\sqrt{1 + (2r\xi )^2} }{\sqrt{(1-r)^2+(2r\xi )^2} }=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\left[1-(2)^2\right]^2 + (2\times 2\times 0.1)^2} } =0.35585\)

The amplitude of the force transmitted is given by

\(\left|F_t\right| = F_0\beta _t= (60)(0.35585) = 21.351 \)N
