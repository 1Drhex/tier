Guest Question.

The bearing pressure will be checked for the gravity retaining wall found in . Assume that the allowable bearing pressure is 2,000 psf.

Admin
Solution

Wall weights and the horizontal fluid force \(P_{3}\) are as shown in Figure 3.88. The location of the resultant vertical force \(W_{total}\) is obtained by writing a moment equation in which

\(M_{A} =W_{1}\times (1 \space ft.)+ W_{2}\times (2\space ft.)\)

 

\(M_{A} =(506\# )(1 \space ft.)+ (675\# ) (2\space ft.)=1,856\# -ft.\)

From Figure 3.89, a moment equation due to the wall weight can also be written as

\(M_{A}=(W_{total}) \times (b)\)

where

\(W_{total}=W_{1}+W_{2}=(506\# )+(675\# )=1,181\# \)

Equating the two equations,

\(M_{A}= W_{total} \times (b)=(1,181\# )\times (b)=1,856\# -ft.\)

 

\(\therefore b=1.57 \space ft.\)

The resultant force \(R\) of the vertical and horizontal components is equal to:

\( R= \sqrt{( W_{total}^{2}+ P_{3}^{2} )} =\sqrt{(1,181^{2} +405^{2} )} =1,249\#\)

Locating the point where the resultant intersects the base of the footing utilizes Varignonâ€™s theorem, in which the moment caused by the vertical and horizontal forces about the toe at \(A\) is the same as the moment resulting from \(R_{y} \) (which is equal to \( W_{total}\) ) times the distance \(x\) from point A (Figure 3.90).

\(x=\frac{M_{RM}-M_{OTM}}{W_{total}} \)

Values for the overturning moment (\(M_{OTM}\)) and the righting moment (\(M_{RM}\)) are obtained from work done in

\(x=\frac{ (1,856\# -ft.)-(608\# -ft.)}{(1,181\# )} =1.06 \space ft.\)

The dimension \(x=1.06 \space ft.\)falls within the middle third of the base dimension.

\( \frac{a}{3} \leq x=1.06 \space ft. \leq \frac{2a}{3}\)

Because the resultant is within the middle third of the base dimension, the equations for the maximum and minimum bearing pressures are

\(p_{max}=\frac{W}{a^{2} } (4a – 6x) =\frac{(1,181\# )}{(2.5^{2})} (4\times 2.5 \space ft. – 6 \times 1.06 \space ft.)\)

 

\( p_{max}=\left(189 \frac{\# }{ft.^{2} } \right) (10-6.36)=688 \frac{\# }{ft.^{2}}\)

 

\(p_{min} =\frac{W}{a^{2} }( 6x-2a ) =\left(\frac{1181\# }{2.5^{2} }\right) (6\times 1.06 -2\times 2.5)\)

 

\(=257 \frac{\# }{ft.^{2}}\)

The maximum bearing pressure is well within the allowable bearing stress limit of \( 2,000 \space psf\).
