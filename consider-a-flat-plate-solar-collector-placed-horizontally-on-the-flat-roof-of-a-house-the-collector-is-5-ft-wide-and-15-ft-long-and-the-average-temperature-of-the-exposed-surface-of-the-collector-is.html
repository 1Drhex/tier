Guest Question.

Consider a flat plate solar collector placed horizontally on the flat roof of a house. The collector is 5 ft wide and 15 ft long, and the average temperature of the exposed surface of the collector is 100°F. The emissivity of the exposed surface of the collector is 0.9. Determine the rate of heat loss from the collector by convection and radiation during a calm day when the ambient air temperature is 70°F and the effective sky temperature for radiation exchange is 50°F. Take the convection heat transfer coefficient on the exposed surface to be \( 2.5 Btu / h \cdot ft ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} F \).

Admin
Solution

A flat plate solar collector is placed horizontally on the roof of a house. The rate of heat loss from the collector by convection and radiation during a calm day are to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Steady operating conditions exist. 2 The emissivity and convection heat transfer coefficient are constant and uniform. 3 The exposed surface, ambient, and sky temperatures remain constant.

Properties The emissivity of the outer surface of the collector is given to be 0.9.

Analysis The exposed surface area of the collector is

\( A_{s}=(5 ft )(15 ft )=75 ft ^{2} \)

 

Noting that the exposed surface temperature of the collector is 100°F, the total rate of heat loss from the collector the environment by convection and radiation becomes

\( \dot{Q}_{\text {conv }}=h A_{s}\left(T_{\infty}-T_{s}\right)=\left(2.5 Btu / h \cdot ft ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} F \right)\left(75 ft ^{2}\right)(100-70)^{\circ} F \)

 

\( = 5625 Btu / h \)

 

\( \dot{Q}_{ rad }=\varepsilon A_{s} \sigma\left(T_{\text {surr }}^{4}-T_{s}^{4}\right)=(0.9)\left(75 ft ^{2}\right)\left(0.1714 \times 10^{-8} Btu / h \cdot ft ^{2} \cdot R ^{4}\right) \)

 

\( \left[(100+460 R )^{4}-(50+460 R )^{4}\right] = 3551 Btu/h \)

 

and

\( \dot{Q}_{\text {total }}=\dot{Q}_{\text {conv }}+\dot{Q}_{ rad } = 5625 + 3551 = 9176 Btu / h \)
