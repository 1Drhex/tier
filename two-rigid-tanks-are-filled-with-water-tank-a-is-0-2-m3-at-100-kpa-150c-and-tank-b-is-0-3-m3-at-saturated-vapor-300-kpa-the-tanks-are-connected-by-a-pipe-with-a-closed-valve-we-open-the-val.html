Guest Question.

Two rigid tanks are filled with water. Tank A is 0.2 \(m^{3}\) at 100 kPa, 150°C and tank B is 0.3 \(m^{3}\) at saturated vapor 300 kPa. The tanks are connected by a pipe with a closed valve. We open the valve and let all the water come to a single uniform state while we transfer enough heat to have a final pressure of 300 kPa. Give the two property values that determine the final state and find the heat transfer.

Admin
Solution

State A1: \(u =2582.75 kJ / kg , v =1.93636 m ^{3} / kg\)

 

\(\Rightarrow m _{ A 1}= V / v =0.2 / 1.93636=0.1033 kg\)

 

State B1: \(u =2543.55 kJ / kg , v =0.60582 m ^{3} / kg\)

 

\(\Rightarrow m_{B 1}=V / v=0.3 / 0.60582=0.4952 kg\)

 

The total volume (and mass) is the sum of volumes (mass) for tanks A and B.

 

\(\begin{array}{l}m _{2}= m _{ A 1}+ m _{ B 1}=0.1033+0.4952=0.5985 kg \\V _{2}= V _{ A 1}+ V _{ B 1}=0.2+0.3=0.5 m ^{3}\end{array}\)

 

\(=>v_{2}=V_{2} / m_{2}=0.5 / 0.5985=0.8354 m ^{3} / kg\)

 

State 2: \(\left[ P _{2}, v _{2}\right]=\left[300 kPa , 0.8354 m ^{3} / kg \right]\)

 

\(\Rightarrow T _{2}=274.76^{\circ} C \text { and } u _{2}=2767.32 kJ / kg\)

 

The energy equation is (neglecting kinetic and potential energy)

\(m _{2} u _{2}- m _{ A } u _{ A 1}- m _{ B } u _{ B 1}={ }_{1} Q _{2}-{ }_{1} W _{2}={ }_{1} Q _{2}\)

 

\({ }_{1} Q _{2}\) = (0.5985 × 2767.32 – 0.1033 × 2582.75 – 0.4952 × 2543.55) kg ×kJ/kg

= 129.9 kJ
