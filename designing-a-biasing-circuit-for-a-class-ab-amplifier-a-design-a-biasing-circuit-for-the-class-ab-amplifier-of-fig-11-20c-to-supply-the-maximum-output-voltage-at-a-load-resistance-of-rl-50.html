Designing a biasing circuit for a class AB amplifier

(a) Design a biasing circuit for the class AB amplifier of Fig. 11.20(c) to supply the maximum output voltage at a load resistance of \( R_{ L }=50 \Omega \). The quiescent biasing current \( I_{ Q } \) is 2 mA. Assume a DC supply voltage of 12 V. The diode parameters are \( I_{ S }=10^{-13} A , V_{ D 1}=V_{ D 2}=0.7 V , \text { and } I_{ D (\min )}=1 mA \) to ensure conduction. The transistor parameters are \( \beta_{ F }=h_{ fe }=50, V_{ BE }=0.7 V , I_{ S }=10^{-14} A \text {, and } V_{ CE ( sat )}=0.2 V .\)

(b) Find the biasing voltage \( V_{ BB } \text { for } v_{ O }=0 \text { and } 11.8 V \).

Admin
Solution

(a) The maximum peak load voltage is

\( V_{ p ( max )}=V_{ CC }-V_{ CE ( sat )}=12-0.2=11.8 V \)

 

The maximum peak load current is

\( I_{ p (\max )}=\frac{V_{ p (\max )}}{R_{ L }}=\frac{11.8}{50}=236 mA \)

From Eq. (11.71), the maximum DC power from the supply source is

\(P_{ S }=\frac{2 I_{ p } V_{ CC }}{\pi}+I_{ Q } V_{ CC }=V_{ CC }\left(I_{ Q }+\frac{2 I_{ p }}{\pi}\right)\) (11.71)

 

\( P_{ S } \approx \frac{2 I_{ p (\max )} V_{ CC }}{\pi}+I_{ Q } V_{ CC }=\frac{2 \times 236 mA \times 12 V }{\pi}+2 mA \times 12 V =1.83 W \)

 

From Eq. (11.17), the output power is

\( P_{ L }=\left(\frac{V_{ p }}{\sqrt{2}}\right)\left(\frac{I_{ p }}{\sqrt{2}}\right)=\frac{V_{ p } I_{ p }}{2} \) (11.17)

\( P_{ L (\max )}=\frac{I_{ p (\max )} V_{ p (\max )}}{2}=236 mA \times \frac{11.8 V }{2}=1.39 W \)

 

Thus, the maximum power efficiency is

\( \eta_{\max }=\frac{P_{ L (\max )}}{P_{ S }}=\frac{1.39}{1.83}=76.1 \% \)

 

\( \eta_{\max }=\frac{P_{ L (\max )}}{P_{ S }}=\frac{1.39}{1.83}=76.1 \% \)

 

The power dissipation of each transistor can be found from

\( P_{ C }=\frac{P_{ S }-P_{ L (\max )}}{2}=\frac{1.83 W -1.39 W }{2}=220 mW \)

 

From Eq. (11.72), we get

\( R_{1}=R_{2}=\frac{V_{ CC }-V_{ D 1}\left(=V_{ D 2}=V_{ BB } / 2\right)}{I_{ D 1(\min )}+\left(I_{ Q }+i_{ O (\max )}\right) /\left(1+h_{ fe }\right)} \) (11.72)

 

\( R_{1}=R_{2}=\frac{12 V -0.7 V }{1 mA +(2 mA +236 mA ) /(1+50)}=1.99 k \Omega \)

 

(b) We have

\( I_{ R }=I_{ D (\min )}+\frac{I_{ Q }+I_{ p (\max )}}{1+h_{ fe }}=1 mA +\frac{2 mA +236 mA }{1+50}=5.667 mA \)

 

For \( v_{ O }=0, i_{ N }=I_{ Q }=2 mA \). Thus, the base current of the npn transistor is

\( I_{ BN }=\frac{I_{ N }}{1+h_{ fe }}=\frac{2 mA }{1+50}=0.039 mA \)

 

and \( I_{ D 1}=I_{ R }-I_{ BN }=5.667 mA -0.039 mA =5.628 mA \)

 

Therefore, the biasing voltage \( V_{ BB } \) becomes

\( V_{ BB }=2 V_{ T } \ln \left(\frac{I_{ D 1}}{I_{ S }}\right)=2 \times 25.8 mV \times \ln \left(\frac{5.628 mA }{10^{-13} A }\right)=1.277 V \)

 

For \( v_{ O }=11.8, i_{ N }=I_{ Q }+I_{ p (\max )}=2 mA +236 mA =238 mA .\) Thus,

\( I_{ BN }=\frac{i_{ N }}{1+h_{ fe }}=\frac{238 mA }{1+50}=4.67 mA \)

 

and \( I_{ D 1}=I_{ R }-I_{ BN }=5.667 mA -4.67 mA =1 mA \)

Therefore, for \( I_{ D 1}=1 mA \), the biasing voltage \( V_{ BB } \text { becomes } V_{ BB }=1.19 V \).
