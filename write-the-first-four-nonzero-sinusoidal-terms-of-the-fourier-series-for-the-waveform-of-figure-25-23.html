Guest Question.

Write the first four nonzero sinusoidal terms of the Fourier series for the waveform of Figure 25–23.

Admin
Solution

The waveform of Figure 25–23 is made up of a combination of waves as illustrated in Figure 25–24.

The Fourier series of each of the waveforms is determined from Table 25–1 as

\(\nu _{1}(t)=\frac{4}{\pi } \sin\omega t+\frac{4}{3\pi } \sin3\omega t+\frac{4}{5\pi } \sin5\omega t+\cdot \cdot \cdot \)

and

\(\nu _{2}(t)=\frac{1}{2}- \frac{4}{\pi^{2} } \cos\omega t-\frac{4}{3^{2}\pi ^{2} } \cos3\omega t-\frac{4}{5^{2}\pi^{2} } \cos5\omega t-\cdot \cdot \cdot \)

When these series are added algebraically, we get

\(\nu (t)=\nu _{1}(t)+\nu _{2}(t)\)

 

\(=0.5+1.27\sin\omega t-0.41\cos\omega t\)

 

\(+0.42\sin3\omega t-0.05\cos3\omega t\)

 

\(+0.25\sin5\omega t-0.02\cos5\omega t\)

 

\(+0.18\sin7\omega t-0.01\cos7\omega t\)

The preceding series may be further simplified by using Equations 25–6 and 25–7 to provide a single coefficient and phase shift for each frequency. The resultant waveform is written as

\(c_{n}=\sqrt{a^{2}_{n}+b^{2}_{n}} \) (25-6)

\(\theta =\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{a_{n}}{b_{n}} \right)\) (25-7)

\(\nu (t) =0.5+1.34\sin(\omega t-17.7^{\circ})+0.43\sin(3\omega t-6.1^{\circ})\)

 

\(+0.26\sin(5\omega t-3.6^{\circ})+0.18\sin(7\omega t-2.6^{\circ})\)
