Guest Question.

IDEAL ANALYSIS OF A REACTION TURBINE

In a vertical-shaft Francis turbine the available head at the inlet flange of the turbine is 500 ft and the vertical distance between the runner and the tailrace is 6.5 ft. The runner tip speed is 115 ft/s, the velocity of the water entering the runner is 130 ft/s, and the velocity of the water exiting the runner is constant and equal to 35 ft/s. The flow velocity at the exit of the draft tube is 11.5 ft/s. The hydraulic energy losses estimated from the turbine are equal to 20 ft at the volute, 3.5 ft at the draft tube, and 33.0 ft at the runner. Determine the pressure head (with respect to the tailrace) at the inlet and exit of the runner, the flow angle at the runner inlet, and the efficiency of the turbine.

Admin
Solution

Given:

Flow through a vertical shaft Francis turbine
Head at entrance: \(500 \mathrm{ft}\)
Distance between runner and tailrace: \(6.5 \mathrm{ft}\)
Runner tip speed: \(115 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s}\)
Velocity at runner entrance: \(130 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s}\)
Velocity at runner exit: \(35 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s}\)
Flow velocity at draft tube exit: \(11.5 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s}\)
Losses: \(20 \mathrm{ft}\) at volute, \(3.5 \mathrm{ft}\) at draft tube, \(33 \mathrm{ft}\) at runner
Find:

(a) Pressure head at inlet and exit of runner.
(b) Flow angle at runner inlet.
(c) Turbine efficiency.
Solution: Apply the energy and Euler turbomachine equations to the control volume.
Governing equations:

\(\begin{gathered}H=\frac{\dot{W}_{m}}{\dot{m} g}=\frac{1}{g}\left(U_{2} V_{t_{2}}-U_{1} V_{t_{1}}\right)\quad\quad\quad\quad (10.2c) \\\quad\\\eta_{t}=\frac{\dot{W}_{m}}{\dot{W}_{h}}=\frac{\omega T}{\rho Q g H_{t}}\quad\quad\quad\quad (10.4c)\end{gathered}\)

 

\(\frac{p_{1}}{\rho g}+\alpha_{1} \frac{\bar{V}_{1}^{2}}{2 g}+z_{1}+H_{a}=\frac{p_{2}}{\rho g}+\alpha_{2} \frac{\bar{V}_{2}^{2}}{2 g}+z_{2}+\frac{h_{l_{T}}}{g}\) (10.28b)

Assumptions:

(1) Steady flow
(2) Uniform flow at each station
(3) Turbulent flow; \(\alpha=1\)
(4) Reservoir and tailrace are at atmospheric pressure
(5) Reservoir is at stagnation condition; \(\bar{V}_{1}=0\)
(a) If we apply the energy equation between the runner exit and the tailrace:

\(\begin{aligned}&H_{3}=\frac{p_{3}-p_{\mathrm{atm}}}{\rho g}=\frac{\bar{V}_{4}^{2}-\bar{V}_{3}^{2}}{2 g}+\Delta H_{D T}+z_{4} \\\quad\\&H_{3}=\frac{1}{2} \times\left[\left(11.5 \frac{\mathrm{ft}}{\mathrm{s}}\right)^{2}-\left(35 \frac{\mathrm{ft}}{\mathrm{s}}\right)^{2}\right] \times \frac{1}{32.2} \frac{\mathrm{s}^{2}}{\mathrm{ft}}+3.5 \mathrm{ft}-6.5 \mathrm{ft}=-19.97 \mathrm{ft} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad H_{3}}\end{aligned}\)

(negative sign indicates suction)
Next we apply the energy equation between the runner entrance and the tailrace:

\(\begin{aligned}&H_{2}=\frac{p_{2}-p_{\mathrm{atm}}}{\rho g}=H_{E}-\Delta H_{R}-\frac{\bar{V}_{2}^{2}}{2 g} \\\quad\\&H_{2}=500 \mathrm{ft}-33.0 \mathrm{ft}-\frac{1}{2} \times\left(130 \frac{\mathrm{ft}}{\mathrm{s}}\right)^{2} \times \frac{1}{32.2} \frac{\mathrm{s}^{2}}{\mathrm{ft}}=205 \mathrm{ft} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad H_{2}}\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Applying the energy equation across the entire system provides the work extraction through the turbine:

 

\(\frac{p_{1}}{\rho g}+\alpha_{1} \frac{\bar{V}_{1}^{2}}{2 g}+z_{1}+H_{a}=\frac{p_{4}}{\rho g}+\alpha_{4} \frac{\bar{V}_{4}^{2}}{2 g}+z_{4}+\frac{h_{l_{T}}}{g}\)

If we simplify the expression based on assumptions and solve for the head extracted at the turbine:

\(H_{a}=\frac{\bar{V}_{4}^{2}}{2 g}-z_{1}+z_{4}+\sum \Delta H=\frac{\bar{V}_{4}^{2}}{2 g}-\left(H_{E}+z\right)+\left(\Delta H_{V}+\Delta H_{R}+\Delta H_{D T}\right)\)

Since station 1 is higher than station 4 , we will take the negative of \(H_{a}\) and call that \(H_{T}\), the head extracted at the turbine:

\(\begin{aligned}H_{T} &=-\frac{\bar{V}_{4}^{2}}{2 g}+\left(H_{e}+z\right)-\left(\Delta H_{V}+\Delta H_{R}+\Delta H_{D T}\right) \\\quad\\&=-\frac{1}{2} \times\left(11.5 \frac{\mathrm{ft}}{s}\right)^{2} \times \frac{1}{32.2} \frac{\mathrm{s}^{2}}{\mathrm{ft}}+(500 \mathrm{ft}+6.5 \mathrm{ft})-(20 \mathrm{ft}+33 \mathrm{ft}+3.5 \mathrm{ft})=448 \mathrm{ft}\end{aligned}\)

Applying the Euler turbomachine equation to this system:

\(-H_{T}=\frac{U_{3} V_{t_{3}}-U_{2} V_{t_{2}}}{g}\)

Solving for the tangential velocity at 2 :

\(V_{t_{2}}=\frac{g H_{T}}{U_{2}}=32.2 \frac{\mathrm{ft}}{\mathrm{s}^{2}} \times 448 \mathrm{ft} \times \frac{1}{115} \frac{\mathrm{s}}{\mathrm{ft}}=125.4 \frac{\mathrm{ft}}{\mathrm{s}}\)

 

Setting up the velocity triangle:

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\beta_{2}=\tan ^{-1} \frac{V_{t_{2}}-U_{2}}{V_{n_{2}}}=\tan ^{-1} \frac{125.4-115}{35}=16.58^{\circ} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad \beta_{2}} \\\quad\\&\alpha_{2}=\tan ^{-1} \frac{V_{t_{2}}}{V_{n_{2}}}=\tan ^{-1} \frac{125.4}{10.5}=85.2^{\circ} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad \alpha_{2}}\end{aligned}\)

(c) To calculate the efficiency:

\(\eta_{t}=\frac{\dot{W}_{m}}{\dot{W}_{h}}=\frac{g H_{T}}{g H_{E}}=\frac{448}{500}=89.6 \% \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad \eta_{t}}\)

 

This problem demonstrates the analysis of a hydraulic turbine with head losses and quantifies those effects in terms of a turbine efficiency. In addition, since the head at the turbine exit is below atmospheric, care must be taken to ensure that cavitation does not occur.
