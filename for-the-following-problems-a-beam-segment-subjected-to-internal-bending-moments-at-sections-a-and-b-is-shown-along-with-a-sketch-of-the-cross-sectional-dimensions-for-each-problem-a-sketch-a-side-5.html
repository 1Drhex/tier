Guest Question.

For the following problems, a beam segment subjected to internal bending moments at sections A and B is shown along with a sketch of the cross-sectional dimensions. For each problem:
(a) Sketch a side view of the beam segment and plot the distribution of bending stresses acting at sections A and B. Indicate the magnitude of key bending stresses on the sketch.
(b) Determine the resultant forces acting in the x direction on the specified area at sections A and B and show these resultant forces on the sketch.
(c) Is the specified area in equilibrium with respect to forces acting in the x direction? If not, determine the horizontal force required to satisfy equilibrium for the specified area and show the location and direction of this force on the sketch.
Consider area (1) of the 500-mm-long beam segment, which is subjected to internal bending moments of \(M_{A}=-5.8 kN - m \text { and } M_{B}=-3.2 kN - m\).

Admin
Solution

Centroid location in y direction: (reference axis at bottom of shape)

\(\begin{array}{lccccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Width } b \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Height } h \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} \text { (from bottom) } \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 160 & 30 & 4,800 & 285 & 1,368,000 \\\text { left stem } & 20 & 270 & 5,400 & 135 & 729,000 \\\text { right stem } & 20 & 270 & 5,400 & 135 & 729,000 \\\hline & & & 15,600 & & 2,826,000\end{array}\)

 

\(\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{2,826,000 mm ^{3}}{15,600 mm ^{2}}=181.154 mm\) (measured upward from bottom edge of shape)

 

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

\(\begin{array}{lcccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 360,000 & 103.846 & 51,763,160.3 & 52,123,160.3 \\\text { left stem } & 32,805,000 & -46.154 & 11,503,035.3 & 44,308,035.3 \\\text { right stem } & 32,805,000 & -46.154 & 11,503,035.3 & 44,308,035.3 \\\hline {\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( mm ^{4}\right)=} 140,739,231\end{array}\)

 

(a) Bending stress distribution

(b) Resultant forces acting on area (1)
On section A, the resultant force on area (1) in the x direction is

\(F_{A}=\frac{1}{2}(4.898 MPa +3.661 MPa )(160 mm )(30 mm )=20,542 N =20.5 kN ( T )\)

and on section B, the horizontal resultant force on area (1) is

\(F_{B}=\frac{1}{2}(2.702 MPa +2.020 MPa )(160 mm )(30 mm )=11,334 N =11.33 kN ( T )\)

(c) Equilibrium of area (1)

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma F_{x} &=-20,542 N +11,334 N =-9,209 N \neq 0 \\& \therefore F_{H}=9.21 kN\end{aligned}\)

The horizontal shear force is directed from section A toward section B at the interface between area (1) and the stems.
