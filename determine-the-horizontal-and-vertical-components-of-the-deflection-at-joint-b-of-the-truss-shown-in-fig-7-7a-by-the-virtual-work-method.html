Guest Question.

Determine the horizontal and vertical components of the deflection at joint \( B \) of the truss shown in Fig. \( 7.7 (\mathrm{a})\) by the virtual work method.

Admin
Solution

Real System. The real system and the corresponding member axial forces \((F)\) are shown in Fig. \(7.7(\mathrm{~b})\).

Horizontal Deflection at \(\boldsymbol{B}, \boldsymbol{\Delta}_{B H} .\) The virtual system used for determining the horizontal deflection at \(B\) consists of a \(1 \mathrm{kN}\) load applied in the horizontal direction at joint \(B\), as shown in Fig. \(7.7\) (c). The member axial forces \(\left(F_{v 1}\right)\) due to this virtual load are also shown in this figure. The member axial forces due to the real system \((F)\) and this virtual system \(\left(F_{v 1}\right)\) are then tabulated, and the virtual work expression given by Eq. (7.23) is applied to determine \(\Delta_{B H}\), as shown in Table \(7.3\).

Vertical Deflection at \(\boldsymbol{B}, \boldsymbol{\Delta}_{B V} .\) The virtual system used for determining the vertical deflection at \(B\) consists of a \(1 \mathrm{kN}\) load applied in the vertical direction at joint \(B\), as shown in Fig. \(7.7(\mathrm{~d})\). The member axial forces \(\left(F_{v 2}\right)\) due to this virtual load are also shown in this figure. These member forces are tabulated in the sixth column of Table \(7.3\), and \(\Delta_{B V}\) is computed by applying the virtual work expression (Eq. (7.23)), as shown in the table.

\( \begin{aligned} 1(\Delta)=& \sum F_{v}\left(\frac{F L}{A E}\right) \\ \end{aligned} \) (7.23)

\( \begin{aligned} 1\left(\Delta_{B H}\right) &=\frac{1}{E A} \sum F_{v 1}(F L) & 1\left(\Delta_{B V}\right) &=\frac{1}{E A} \sum F_{v 2}(F L) \\ (1 \mathrm{kN}) \Delta_{B H} &=\frac{84}{200\left(10^{6}\right)(0.0012)} \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} &(1 \mathrm{kN}) \Delta_{B V} &=\frac{796.91}{200\left(10^{6}\right)(0.0012)} \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ \Delta_{B H} &=0.00035 \mathrm{~m} & \Delta_{B V} &=0.00332 \mathrm{~m} \\ \Delta_{B H} &=0.35 \mathrm{~mm} \rightarrow & \Delta_{B V} &=3.32 \mathrm{~mm} \downarrow \end{aligned} \)
