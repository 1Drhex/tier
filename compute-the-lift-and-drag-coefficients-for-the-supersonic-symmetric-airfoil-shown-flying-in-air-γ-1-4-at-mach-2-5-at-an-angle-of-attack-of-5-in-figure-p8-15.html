Guest Question.

Compute the lift and drag coefficients for the supersonic, symmetric airfoil shown flying in air (γ = 1.4) at Mach 2.5 at an angle of attack of 5° in Figure P8.15.

Admin
Solution

Because the angle of attack and the wedge angle have the same value, the flow will experience only one expansion fan on the upper surface where the slope changes and an oblique shock on the bottom at the leading edge.

Upper Surface

 

\(v_{\infty}+\alpha_{\infty}=v_{2}+\alpha_{2}\)

 

\(\therefore v_{2}=v_{\infty}+\alpha_{\infty}-\alpha_{2}=39.1236+0-(-10.0000)=49.1236^{\circ}\)

 

and so \(M _{2}=2.9674\).

 

\(\frac{ p _{2}}{ p _{\infty}}=\frac{ p _{2}}{ p _{o2}} \frac{ p _{ o 2}}{ p _{o \infty}} \frac{ p _{o \infty}}{ p _{\infty}}=(0.02859)(1.0)\left(\frac{1}{0.05853}\right)=0.4885\)

 

Lower Surface

The freestream flow is turned through 5º as it passes through the forward oblique shock. Therefore,

 

\(\left.\begin{array}{l} M _{\infty}=2.5 \\\delta=5^{\circ}\end{array}\right\} \begin{aligned}&\theta=27.4227^{\circ}, \\& M _{1}=2.2915 \quad \frac{ p _{1}}{ p _{\infty}}=1.3799\end{aligned}\)

 

\(C_{L}=\frac{p_{1} c \cos (5)-\frac{p_{\infty} c}{2 \cos (5)}-\frac{p_{2} c \cos (10)}{2 \cos (5)}}{\frac{1}{2} \gamma p_{\infty} M_{\infty}^{2} c}\)

 

\(=\frac{(1.3799)(0.9962)-\frac{0.5}{(0.9962 .)}-\frac{(0.4885)(0.9848)}{2(0.9962)}}{(4.3750)}=\frac{1.3747-0.5019-0.2415}{4.3750}\)

 

\(=0.1443\)

 

\(C_{D}=\frac{p_{1} c \sin (5)-\frac{p_{2} c \sin (10)}{2 \cos 5^{\circ}}}{\frac{1}{2} \gamma p_{\infty} M_{\infty}^{2} c}\)

 

\(=\frac{(1.3747)(0.0875)-(0.2415)(0.1763)}{4.3750}=0.0178\)
