Guest Question.

Figure 4.34 shows double-circuit conductors’ relative positions in segment 1 of transposition of a completely transposed three-phase overhead transmission line. The inductance is given by

\(L =2 \times 10^{-7} \ln \frac{ GMD }{ GMR } H / m / phase\)

Where \(GMD =\left( D _{ AB _{ eq }} D _{ BC _{ eq }} D _{ AC _{ eq }}\right)^{1 / 3}\)

With mean distances defined by equivalent spacings

\(\begin{aligned}& D _{ AB _{ eq }}=\left( D _{12} D _{1^{\prime} 2^{\prime}} D _{12^{\prime}} D _{1^{\prime} 2}\right)^{1 / 4} \\& D _{ BC _{ eq }}=\left( D _{23} D _{2^{\prime} 3^{\prime}} D _{2^{\prime} 3} D _{23^{\prime}}\right)^{1 / 4} \\& D _{ AC _{ eq }}=\left( D _{13} D _{1^{\prime} 3^{\prime}} D _{13^{\prime}} D _{1^{\prime} 3}\right)^{1 / 4}\end{aligned}\)

And \(GMR =\left[( GMR )_{ A }( GMR )_{ B }( GMR )_{ C }\right]^{1 / 3}\) with phase GMRs defined by \(( GMR )_{ A }=\left[r^{\prime} D _{11^{\prime}}\right]^{1 / 2}\) ; \(( GMR )_{ B }=\left[r^{\prime} D _{22^{\prime}}\right]^{1 / 2}\) ; \(( GMR )_{ C }=\left[r^{\prime} D _{33^{\prime}}\right]^{1 / 2}\) and \(r^{\prime}\) is the GMR of phase conductors.

Now consider a 345-kV, three-phase, double-circuit line with phaseconductor’s GMR of 0.0588 ft and the horizontal conductor configuration shown in Figure 4.35.

(a) Determine the inductance per meter per phase in Henries (H).
(b) Calculate the inductance of just one circuit and then divide by 2 to obtain the inductance of the double circuit.

Admin
Solution

(a)

\(\begin{aligned}&D_{A B_{e q}}=[30 \times 30 \times 60 \times 120]^{1 / 4}=50.45 ft \\&D_{B C_{e q}}=[30 \times 30 \times 60 \times 120]^{1 / 4}=50.45 ft \\&D_{A C_{e q}}=[60 \times 60 \times 150 \times 30]^{1 / 4}=63.44 ft \\&\therefore GMD =(50.45 \times 50.45 \times 63.44)^{1 / 3}=54.46 ft\end{aligned}\)

Equivalent \(GMR =\left[(0.0588)^{3}(90)^{3}\right]^{1 / 6}=2.3 ft\)

\(\therefore L=2 \times 10^{-7} \ln \left(\frac{54.46}{2.3}\right)=0.633 \times 10^{-6} H / m \leftarrow\)

 

(b) Inductance of one circuit is calculated below:

\(\begin{gathered}D_{e q}=[30 \times 30 \times 60]^{1 / 3}=37.8 ft ; \quad r^{\prime}=0.0588 ft \\\therefore L=2 \times 10^{-7} \ln \left(\frac{37.8}{0.0588}\right)=1.293 \times 10^{-6} H / m\end{gathered}\)

Inductance of the double circuit \(=\frac{1.293 \times 10^{-6}}{2}=0.646 \times 10^{-6} H / m \leftarrow\)

Error percent \(=\left(\frac{0.633-0.646}{0.633}\right) \times 100=-2.05 \% \leftarrow\)
