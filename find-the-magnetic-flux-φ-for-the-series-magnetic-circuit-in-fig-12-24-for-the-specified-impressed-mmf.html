Guest Question.

Find the magnetic flux Φ for the series magnetic circuit in Fig. 12.24 for the specified impressed mmf.

Admin
Solution

Assuming that the total impressed mmf NI is across the air gap, we obtain

\(NI=H_gI_g\\[0.5cm] or\qquad H_g=\frac{NI}{l_g}=\frac{400At}{0.001m}=4\times10^{5}At/m \\[0.5cm] and\qquad B_g=\mu_oH_g=(4\pi \times10^{-7})(4\times10^5At/m)=0.50T\)

The flux is given by

\(\phi_g=\phi_{core}=B_gA\\[0.5cm] =(0.50T)(0.003m^2)\\[0.5cm] \phi_{core}=1.5\times10^{-3}Wb\)

Using this value of Φ, we can find NI. The core and gap data are inserted in Table 12.7.

 

TABLE 12.7
Section \(\phi (Wb)\) \(A(m^2)\) B (T) H (At/m) l (m) Hl (At)
Core \(1.51\times10^{-3}\) 0.003 0.50 1500(B-H curve) 0.16
Gap \(1.51\times10^{-3}\) 0.003 0.50 \(4\times10^{5}\) 0.001 400

 

\(H_{core}l_{core}=(1500At/m)(0.16m)=240At\)

Applying Ampère’s circuital law results in

\(NI=H_{core}l_{core}+H_gl_g\\[0.5cm] =240At+400At\\[0.5cm] 400At\neq 640At\)

Since we neglected the reluctance of all the magnetic paths but the air gap, the calculated value is greater than the specified value. We must therefore reduce this value by including the effect of these reluctances. Since approximately (640 At – 400 At)/640 At = 240 At/640 At ≅ 37.5% of our calculated value is above the desired value, let us reduce Φ by 30% and see how close we come to the impressed mmf of 400 At:

\(\phi=(1-0.3)(1.5\times10^{-3}Wb)\\[0.5cm] =1.05\times10^{-3}Wb\)

See Table 12.8. We have

TABLE 12.8
Section \(\phi (Wb)\) \(A(m^2)\) B (T) H (At/m) l (m) Hl (At)
Core \(1.05\times10^{-3}\) 0.003 0.16
Gap \(1.05\times10^{-3}\) 0.003 0.001

 

\(B=\frac{\phi}{A}=\frac{1.05\times10^{-3}Wb}{0.003m^3}\cong 0.35T\\[0.5cm] H_gI_g=(7.96\times10^5)B_gI_g\\[0.5cm] = (7.96\times10^5)(0.35T)(0.001m)\\[0.5cm] \cong 278.6 At\)

From the B-H curves,

\(H_{core}\cong 850 At/m\\[0.5cm] H_{core}l_{core}=(850 At/m)(0.16 m) = 136 At\)

Applying Ampère’s circuital law yields

\(NI=H_{core}l_{core}+H_gl_g\\[0.5cm] =136 At + 278.6 At\\[0.5cm] 400 At \cong 414.6 At\) (but within ±5% and therefore acceptable)

The solution is, therefore,

\(\phi≅ 1.06\times10^{-3}Wb\)
