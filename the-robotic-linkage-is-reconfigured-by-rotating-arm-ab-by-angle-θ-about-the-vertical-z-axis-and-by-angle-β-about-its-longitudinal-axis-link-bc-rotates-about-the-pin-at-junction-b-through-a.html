Guest Question.

The robotic linkage is reconfigured by rotating arm AB by angle θ about the vertical Z axis, and by angle β about its longitudinal axis. Link BC rotates about the pin at junction B through angle γ. The pin is horizontal when β = 0. The system is given a set of rotations consisting of θ = 50°, β = 30°, γ = 60°. The length L of link BC changes from 0.5 m to 1.5 m in the course of this motion. Determine the coordinates of end C with respect to the fixed XYZ coordinate after these rotations.

Admin
Solution

This example serves to illustrate the versatility of the general procedure for evaluating displacements, and the ease with which it may be extended. It should be noted that the sequence in which the rotations are applied is not specified. It will become apparent in the course of the solution that such information is irrelevant for this system. We define reference frames \(x_{1} y_{1} z_{1}\) fixed to arm AB, and \(x_{2} y_{2} z_{2}\) fixed to arm BC, with the x axis for each aligned with the corresponding arm. The \(z_{1}\) axis is vertical when β = 0 and the\(y_{2}\) axis is aligned with the pin at connection B, so that all rotations occur about coordinate axes.

The general approach is to use Eq. (3.2.7) to express the displacement of point B in terms of the rotation of \(x_{1} y_{1} z_{1}\) and then to describe the displacement of point C in terms of the displacement of point B and the rotation of \(x_{2} y_{2} z_{2}\) . We use\(\bar{I}\bar{J}\bar{K}\) components to facilitate relating vector quantities for each body. Thus,

\(\begin{aligned}\left\{\begin{array}{c}\Delta \bar{r}_{P} \cdot \bar{I} \\\\ \Delta \bar{r}_{P} \cdot \bar{J} \\\\ \Delta \bar{r}_{P} \cdot \bar{K}\end{array}\right\}=&\left\{\begin{array}{l}\Delta \bar{r}_{O^{\prime}} \cdot \bar{I} \\\\ \Delta \bar{r}_{O^{\prime}} \cdot \bar{J} \\\\ \Delta \bar{r}_{O^{\prime}} \cdot \bar{K}\end{array}\right\}+[R]_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{T}}\left\{\begin{array}{l}\left(\Delta \bar{r}_{P}\right)_{x y z} \cdot \bar{i} \\\\ \left(\Delta \bar{r}_{P}\right)_{x y z} \cdot \bar{j} \\\\ \left(\Delta \bar{r}_{p}\right)_{x y z} \cdot \bar{k}\end{array}\right\} \\\\ &+\left[[R]_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{T}}- [R]_{\mathrm{o}}^{\mathrm{T}}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{c}\left(x_{P}\right)_{\mathrm{o}} \\\\ \left(y_{P}\right)_{\mathrm{o}} \\\\ \left(z_{P}\right)_{\mathrm{o}} \end{array}\right\} . \end{aligned}\) (3.2.7)

 

\( \begin{aligned} \left\{\begin{array}{c} \Delta \bar{r}_{B} \cdot \bar{I} \\\\ \Delta \bar{r}_{B} \cdot \bar{J} \\\\ \Delta \bar{r}_{B} \cdot \bar{K} \end{array}\right\}=&\left\{\begin{array}{l} \Delta \bar{r}_{A} \cdot \bar{I} \\\\ \Delta \bar{r}_{A} \cdot J \\\\ \Delta \bar{r}_{A} \cdot \bar{K} \end{array}\right\}+\left[R_{1}\right]_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{T}}\left\{\begin{array}{c} \left(\Delta \bar{r}_{B}\right)_{x_{1} y_{1} z_{1}} \cdot \bar{i} \\\\ \left(\Delta \bar{r}_{B}\right)_{x_{1} y_{1} z_{1}} \cdot \bar{j} \\\\ \left(\Delta \bar{r}_{B}\right)_{x_{1} y_{1} z_{1}} \cdot \bar{k} \end{array}\right\} \\\\ &+\left[\left[R_{1}\right]_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{T}}- \left[R_{1}\right]_{\mathrm{o}}^{\mathrm{T}}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l} \left(x_{1 B}\right)_{\mathrm{o}} \\\\ \left(y_{1 B}\right)_{0} \\\\ \left(z_{1 B}\right)_{\mathrm{o}} \end{array}\right\}, \\\\ \left\{\begin{aligned} \Delta \bar{r}_{C} \cdot \bar{I} \\\\ \Delta \bar{r}_{C} \cdot \bar{J} \\\\ \Delta \bar{r}_{C} \cdot \bar{K} \end{aligned}\right\}=&\left\{\begin{array}{l} \Delta \bar{r}_{B} \cdot \bar{I} \\\\ \Delta \bar{r}_{B} \cdot \bar{J} \\\\ \Delta \bar{r}_{B} \cdot \bar{K} \end{array}\right\}+\left[R_{2}\right]_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{T}}\left\{\begin{array}{l} \left(\Delta \bar{r}_{C}\right)_{x_{2} y_{2} z_{2}} \cdot \bar{i} \\\\ \left(\Delta \bar{r}_{C}\right)_{x_{2} y_{2} z} \cdot \bar{j} \\\\ \left(\Delta \bar{r}_{C}\right)_{x_{2} y_{2} z_{2}} \cdot \bar{k} \end{array}\right\} \\\\ &+\left[\left[R_{2}\right]_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{T}}- \left[R_{2}\right]_{0}^{\mathrm{T}}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l} \left(x_{2 C}\right)_{0} \\\\ \left(y_{2 C}\right)_{0} \\\\ \left(z_{2 C}\right)_{0} \end{array}\right\} . \end{aligned}\)

We derive the required rotation transformations as a sequence of simple rotations. In the initial state, like axes were parallel, so

\(\left[R_{1}\right]_{0}=\left[R_{2}\right]_{\mathrm{o}}=[U].\)

For the transformation from XYZ to the final orientation of \(x_{1} y_{1} z_{1}\) we may consider arm AB to rotate first about the Z axis by θ, followed by the β rotation about the \(x_{1}\) axis, which corresponds to a sequence of body-fixed rotations. Alternatively, we may consider the β rotation to occur about the X axis, followed by the θ rotation about the Z axis, which is a space-fixed sequence. Either way, we find that

\(\left[R_{1}\right]_{\mathrm{f}}=\left[R_{\beta}\right]\left[R_{\theta}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \cos \beta & \sin \beta \\ 0 & -\sin \beta & \cos \beta \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{ccc} \cos \theta & \sin \theta & 0 \\ -\sin \theta & \cos \theta & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right]\).

When γ = 0, like axes of \(x_{1} y_{1} z_{1}\) and \(x_{2} y_{2} z_{2}\) are parallel. Thus the final orientation of \(x_{2} y_{2} z_{2}\) may be considered to be attained by rotating it about the negative \(y_{2}\) axis by γ relative to \(x_{1} y_{1} z_{1}\) . This is a body-fixed rotation, so that

\(\left[R_{2}\right]_{\mathrm{f}}=\left[R_{\gamma}\right]\left[R_{1}\right]_{\mathrm{f}}=\left[\begin{array}{ccc} \cos \gamma & 0 & \sin \gamma \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -\sin \gamma & 0 & \cos \gamma \end{array}\right]\left[R_{1}\right]_{\mathrm{f}}.\)

Next we describe the relative positions and displacements. Because point A is stationary, \(\Delta \bar {r}_{A}=\bar{0}\) Also, because point B remains at 2 m along the \(x_{1}\) axis throughout the motion, \(\left(\bar{r}_{B / A}\right)_{\mathrm{o}}=2 \bar{i}_{1}\) and \(\left(\Delta \bar{r}_{B}\right)_{x_{1} y_{1} z_{1}}=\bar{0}\) In contrast, point C remains on the \(x_{2}\) axis, but its distance to the origin of \(x_{2} y_{2} z_{2}\) increases from 0.5 m to 1.5 m, so it displaces 1 m in the \(x_{2}\) direction relative to \(x_{2} y_{2} z_{2}\). Thus \(\left(\bar{r}_{C / B}\right)_{0}=0.5 \bar{i}_{2}\) and \(\left(\Delta \bar{r}_{C}\right)_{x_{2} y_{2} z_{2}}=1 \bar{i}_{2}\) Substituting the given values of θ, β, and γ into the earlier expressions for \(\left(\Delta \bar{r}_{B}\right)\) and \(\left(\Delta \bar{r}_{C}\right)\) leads to

\(\begin{array}{l} \left\{\begin{array}{c} \Delta \bar{r}_{B} \cdot \bar{I} \\\\ \Delta \bar{r}_{B} \cdot \bar{J} \\\\ \Delta \bar{r}_{B} \cdot \bar{K} \end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{c} -0.7144 \\\\ 1.5321 \\\\ 0 \end{array}\right\} \mathrm{m}, \\\\ \left\{\begin{array}{c} \Delta \bar{r}_{C} \cdot \bar{I} \\\\ \Delta \bar{r}_{C} \cdot \bar{J} \\\\ \Delta \bar{r}_{C} \cdot \bar{K} \end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{c} -0.2348 \\\\ 1.6891 \\\\ 1.1250 \end{array}\right\} \mathrm{m} . \end{array}\)
