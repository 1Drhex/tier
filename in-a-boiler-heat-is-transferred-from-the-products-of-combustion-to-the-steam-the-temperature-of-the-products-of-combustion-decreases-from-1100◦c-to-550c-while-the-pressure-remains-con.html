Guest Question.

In a boiler, heat is transferred from the products of combustion to the steam. The temperature of the products of combustion decreases from 1100◦C to 550°C, while the pressure remains constant at 0.1 MPa. The average constant-pressure specific heat of the products of combustion is 1.09 kJ/kg K. The water enters at 0.8 MPa, 150°C, and leaves at 0.8 MPa, 250°C. Determine the second-law efficiency for this process and the irreversibility per kilogram of water evaporated.
Control volume: Overall heat exchanger.
Sketch: Fig. 8.12.
Inlet states: Both known, given in Fig. 8.12.
Exit states: Both known, given in Fig. 8.12.
Process: Overall, adiabatic.
Diagram: Fig. 8.13.
Model: Products—ideal gas, constant specific heat. Water—steam tables.

For the products, the entropy change for this constant-pressure process is

\(\left(s_{e}-s_{i}\right)_{\text {prod }}=C_{p o} \ln \frac{T_{e}}{T_{i}}\)

For this control volume we can write the following governing equations: Continuity equation:

\(\left(\dot{m}_{i}\right)_{ H _{2} O }=\left(\dot{m}_{e}\right)_{ H _{2} O } (a)\)

\(\left(\dot{m}_{i}\right)_{\text {prod }}=\left(\dot{m}_{e}\right)_{\text {prod }} (b)\)

Energy equation (a steady-state process):

\(\left(\dot{m}_{i} h_{i}\right)_{ H _{2} O }+\left(\dot{m}_{i} h_{i}\right)_{ prod }=\left(\dot{m}_{e} h_{e}\right)_{ H _{2} O }+\left(\dot{m}_{e} h_{e}\right)_{ prod } (c)\)

Entropy equation (the process is adiabatic for the control volume shown):

\(\left(\dot{m}_{e} s_{e}\right)_{ H _{2} O }+\left(\dot{m}_{e} s_{e}\right)_{ prod }=\left(\dot{m}_{i} s_{i}\right)_{ H _{2} O }+\left(\dot{m}_{i} s_{i}\right)_{ prod }+\dot{S}_{ gen }\)

Solution

From Eqs. a, b, and c, we can calculate the ratio of the mass flow of products to the mass flow of water.

From Eqs. a, b, and c, we can calculate the ratio of the mass flow of products to the mass
flow of water.

\(\dot{m}_{\text {prod }}\left(h_{i}-h_{e}\right)_{\text {prod }}=\dot{m}_{ H _{2} O }\left(h_{e}-h_{i}\right)_{ H _{2} O }\)

\(\frac{\dot{m}_{\text {prod }}}{\dot{m}_{ H _{2} O }}=\frac{\left(h_{e}-h_{i}\right)_{ H _{2} O }}{\left(h_{i}-h_{e}\right)_{\text {prod }}}=\frac{2950-632.2}{1.09(1100-550)}=3.866\)

The increase in exergy of the water is, per kilogram of water,

\(\psi_{2}-\psi_{1}=\left(h_{2}-h_{1}\right)-T_{0}\left(s_{2}-s_{1}\right)\)

\(=(2950-632.2)-298.15(7.0384-1.8418)\)

\(=768.4 kJ / kg H _{2} O\)

The decrease in exergy of the products, per kilogram of water, is

\(\frac{\dot{m}_{\text {prod }}}{\dot{m}_{ H _{2} O }}\left(\psi_{3}-\psi_{4}\right)=\frac{\dot{m}_{\text {prod }}}{\dot{m}_{ H _{2} O }}\left[\left(h_{3}-h_{4}\right)-T_{0}\left(s_{3}-s_{4}\right)\right]\)

\(=3.866\left[1.09(1100-550)-298.15\left(1.09 \ln \frac{1373.15}{823.15}\right)\right]\)

\(=1674.7 kJ / kg H _{2} O\)

Therefore, the second-law efficiency is, from Eq. 8.32,

\(\eta_{2 \text { nd law }}=\frac{\dot{m}_{1}\left(\psi_{2}-\psi_{1}\right)}{\dot{m}_{3}\left(\psi_{3}-\psi_{4}\right)} (8.32)\)

\(\eta_{2 \text { nd law }}=\frac{768.4}{1674.7}=0.459\)

From Eq. 8.30, \(I=\dot{W}^{ rev }\), and Eq. 8.29, the process irreversibility per kilogram of water is

\(\dot{W}_{ c.v. ac }=\dot{W}^{ rev }-\dot{Q}_{0}^{ rev }=\dot{W}^{ rev }-\dot{I} (8.30)\)

\(\dot{W}^{ rev }=\dot{\Phi}_{q}+\sum \dot{m}_{i} \psi_{i}-\sum \dot{m}_{e} \psi_{e}-\dot{\Phi}_{ c.v. }+P_{0} \dot{V} (8.29)\)

\(\frac{\dot{I}}{\dot{m}_{ H _{2} O }}=\sum_{i} \frac{\dot{m}_{i}}{\dot{m}_{ H _{2} O }} \psi_{i}-\sum_{2} \frac{\dot{m}_{e}}{\dot{m}_{ H _{2} O }} \psi_{e}\)

\(=\left(\psi_{1}-\psi_{2}\right)+\frac{\dot{m}_{\text {prod }}}{\dot{m}_{ H _{2} O }}\left(\psi_{3}-\psi_{4}\right)\)

\(=(-768.4+1674.7)=906.3 kJ / kg H _{2} O\)

It is also of interest to determine the net change of entropy. The change in the entropy of the water is

\(\left(s_{2}-s_{1}\right)_{ H _{2} O }=7.0384-1.8418=5.1966 kJ / kg H _{2} O K\)

The change in the entropy of the products is

\(\frac{\dot{m}_{\text {prod }}}{\dot{m}_{ H _{2} O }}\left(s_{4}-s_{3}\right)_{ prod }=-3.866\left(1.09 \ln \frac{1373.15}{823.15}\right)=-2.1564 kJ / kg H _{2} O K\)

Thus, there is a net increase in entropy during the process. The irreversibility could also have been calculated from Eqs. 8.6 and 8.13:

\(\frac{d S_{ c.v }}{d t}=\sum \frac{\dot{Q}_{j}}{T_{j}}+\sum \dot{m}_{i} s_{i}-\sum \dot{m}_{e} s_{e}+\dot{S}_{ gen ac } (8.6)\)

\(I=\dot{W}^{ rev }-\dot{W}_{ c.v.ac }=\dot{Q}_{0}^{ rev }=T_{0} \dot{S}_{ genac } (8.13)\)

\(I=\sum \dot{m}_{e} T_{0} s_{e}-\sum \dot{m}_{i} T_{0} s_{i}=T_{0} \dot{S}_{ gen }\)

\(\frac{I}{\dot{m}_{ H _{2} O }}=T_{0}\left(s_{2}-s_{1}\right)_{ H _{2} O }+T_{0} \frac{\dot{m}_{ prod }}{\dot{m}_{ H _{2} O }}\left(s_{4}-s_{3}\right)_{ prod }\)

\(=298.15(5.1966)+298.15(-2.1564)\)

\(=906.3 kJ / kg H _{2} O\)

These two processes are shown on the T–s diagram of Fig. 8.13. Line 3–4 represents the process for the 3.866 kg of products. Area 3–4–c–d–3 represents the heat transferred from the 3.866 kg of products of combustion, and area 3–4–e–f –3 represents the decrease in exergy of these products. Area 1–a–b–2–h–c–1 represents the heat transferred to the water, and this is equal to area 3–4–c–d–3, which represents the heat transferred from the products of combustion. Area 1–a–b–2–g–e–1 represents the increase in exergy of the water. The difference between area 3–4–e–f –3 and area 1–a–b–2–g–e–1 represents the net decrease in exergy. It is readily shown that this net change is equal to area f –g–h–d–f , or \(T_{0}(\Delta s)_{\text {net }}\). Since the actual work is zero, this area also represents the irreversibility, which agrees with our calculation.
