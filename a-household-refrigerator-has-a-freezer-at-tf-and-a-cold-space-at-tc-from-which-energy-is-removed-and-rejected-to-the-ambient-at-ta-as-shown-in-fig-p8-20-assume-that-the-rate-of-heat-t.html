Guest Question.

A household refrigerator has a freezer at \(T _{ F }\) and a cold space at \(T _{ C }\) from which energy is removed and rejected to the ambient at \(T _{ A }\) as shown in Fig. P8.20.

Assume that the rate of heat transfer from the cold space, \(\dot{ Q }_{ C }\), is the same as from the freezer, \(\dot{ Q }_{ F }\) , find an expression for the minimum power into the heat pump.

Evaluate this power when \(T _{ A }=20^{\circ} C , T _{ C }=5^{\circ} C , T _{ F }=-10^{\circ} C , \text { and } \dot{ Q }_{ F }=3 kW\)

Admin
Solution

C.V. Refrigerator (heat pump), Steady, no external flows except heat transfer.

Energy Eq.: \(\dot{ Q }_{ F }+\dot{ Q }_{ c }+\dot{ W }_{ in }=\dot{ Q }_{ A }\)

(amount rejected to ambient)

 

Reversible gives maximum work (minimum work in) as from Eq. 8.1 or 8.11 on rate form (standard sign notation for work).

\(\begin{array}{l}\begin{aligned}\dot{ W }^{ rev } &=\dot{ Q }_{ F }\left[1-\frac{ T _{ A }}{ T _{ F }}\right]+\dot{ Q }_{ c }\left[1-\frac{ T _{ A }}{ T _{ C }}\right]-\dot{ Q }_{ A }\left[1-\frac{ T _{ A }}{ T _{ A }}\right] \\&=3\left[1-\frac{293.15}{263.15}\right]+3\left[1-\frac{293.15}{278.15}\right]-0\end{aligned}\\=- 0 . 5 0 4 k W \quad \text { (negative so work goes in) }\end{array}\)
