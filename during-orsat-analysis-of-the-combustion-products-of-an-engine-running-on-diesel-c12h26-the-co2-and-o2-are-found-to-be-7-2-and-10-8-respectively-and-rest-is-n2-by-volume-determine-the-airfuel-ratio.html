Guest Question.

During Orsat analysis of the combustion products of an engine running on diesel \((C_{12}H_{26})\) the \(CO_{2}\) and \(O_{2}\) are found to be 7.2% and 10.8% respectively and rest is \(N_{2}\) by volume. Determine the air-fuel ratio and percentage excess air considering air to have \(O_{2}\) and \(N_{2}\) in proportion of 23.2% and 76.8% respectively by mass.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{There are two approaches for getting the air-fuel ratio and subsequently excess air. One is by carbon-balancing and other by}\\\text{balancing of oxygen-hydrogen.}\\\text{Minimum air required for complete combustion of 1 kg of fuel can be obtained considering the composition of fuel i.e. }C_{12}H_{26}.\)
Mass of constituent per kg of fuel, \(C_{12}H_{26} \\\quad (a)\) Oxygen required per kg of constituents \(\\\quad (b)\) Oxygen required per kg of fuel \(\\(c) = a × b\) Minimum air required per kg of fuel for complete combustion
\(\begin{aligned}C&= \frac{12 × 12}{(12 × 12 + 26 × 1)}\\&= 0.847\end{aligned}\) \(\frac{8}{3}\) \(\frac{8}{3} × 0.847 = 2.259\) \(\frac{100}{23.2} × 3.483 \)
\(\begin{aligned}H_{2}&= \frac{26× 1}{(12 × 12 + 26 × 1)}\\&= 0.153\end{aligned}\) 8 \(8 × 0.153 = 1.224\) = 15 kg
Total = 3.483
\(\text{Dry flue gases contain }7.2\% of CO_{2}, 10.8\% O_{2} and 82\% N_{2}\text{ by volume. This volumetric composition is to be converted to}\\\text{ gravimetric (mass basis) i.e. mol fractions may be estimated as mol is unit of volume.} \\\text{Mass of constituent in one mol of dry flue gases = Volume fraction × Mol. wt. of constituent}\\\text{Mass of CO}_{2}\text{ in one mol of dry flue gases = 0.072 × 44 = 3.168 kg}\\\text{Mass of N}_{2}\text{ in one mol of dry flue gases = 0.82 × 28 = 22.96 kg}\\\text{Corresponding to N}_{2}\text{ the mass of air per mol of dry flue gases can be obtained as,}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad = \frac{22.96 × 100}{76.8} = 29.89 kg\\\text{Carbon present in one mol of dry flue gases = }\left\lgroup 3.168 ×\frac{12}{44} \right\rgroup = 0.864 kg\\\text{Therefore, from the carbon present in one mol of dry flue gases the amount of fuel containing this much of carbon can}\\\text{ be calculated.}\\\begin{aligned}\text{So, air fuel ratio}&= \frac{29.89}{0.964} = 31.0\\\%\text{excess air}&= \frac{(31 – 15)}{15} × 100= 106.67\%\end{aligned}\\\text{Air-fuel ratio and }\%\text{ excess air calculated differ from those estimated using balancing of carbon.}\\\text{Generally, carbon balancing is used for getting accurate results.}\)
