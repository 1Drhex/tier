Guest Question.

The 400 V, 50 kVA, 0.8 power factor leading delta connected synchronous motor has synchronous reactance of 3 ohm, resistance neglected. It is supplying a 12 kW load with initial power factor of 0.86 lagging. The windage and friction losses are 2.0 kW and the core losses are 1.5 kW. Determine the line current, armature current and excitation voltage. If the flux of the motor is increased by 20 percent determine the excitation voltage, armature current and the new power factor.

Admin
Solution
\(P_{i} = P_{0} + all losses; P_{i} = P_{o} + P_{mech} + P_{core} + P_{elec} = 12 + 2 + 1.5 + 0 = 15.5 kW\)

 

Now, \(P_{i} = 3 V I_{1} \cos \phi \)

\(\therefore I_{1}= \frac{P_{i}}{3 V \cos \phi} = \frac{15.5\times 10^{3}}{3\times 400\times 0.86} = 15 A\)

 

Since the power factor of the motor is 0.86 lagging, the phasor armature current is given by

\(\overline{I}_{1}= {I}_{1} \angle -\cos ^{-1}0.86= 15\angle -30.68^{\circ } A\)

 

Line current \(I_{L}= \sqrt{3} I_{1}=\sqrt{3} \times 15= 25.98 A\)

The excitation voltage E is given by

\(\overline{E}_{1}=\overline{V} -\overline{I}_{1} \overline{Z}_{s} = V- j I_{1}X_{s} \\[0.5cm] \quad \, =400 \angle 0^{\circ } – j 3 \left(15\angle -30.68^{\circ }\right) \\[0.5cm] \quad \; =400 \angle 0^{\circ } – 45\angle 90^{\circ } -30.68^{\circ } =400 – 45 \angle 59.32^{\circ } \\[0.5cm] \quad \; =400- \left(22.96 + j38.7\right) = 377.04 = j38.7 = 379 \angle -5.86^{\circ } V\)

 

If the flux is increased by 20%, then E will also increase by 20%.

\(\therefore E_{2}= 1.2 E_{1}= 1.2 \times 379=454.8 V\)

 

When flux is increased by 20%, the power supplied to the load remains constant,

\(\therefore E_{1} \sin \delta_{1} = E_{2} \sin \delta_{2} \)

 

\(\sin \delta_{2}= \frac{ E_{1}}{E_{2}} \sin \delta_{1} = \frac{1}{1.2} \sin \left(-5.86^{\circ }\right)= -0.085 \)

 

\(\delta_{2}= \sin^{-1} \left(-0.085\right)= -4.88^{\circ }\)

 

The new armature current is given by

\(\overline{I} _{2}= \frac{\overline{V} – E_{2}}{j X_{s}} = \frac{400\angle 0^{\circ } – 454.8\angle -4.88^{\circ }}{j3} \\[0.5cm] \quad \; =\frac{1}{j3} \left[400- \left(453.2- j 38.69\right) \right] \\[0.5cm] \quad \; =\frac{1}{j3} \left(-53.2+ j 38.69\right)= \frac{65.78\angle 148^{\circ }}{3\angle 90^{\circ }}= 21.93\angle 58^{\circ } A \)

 

New power factor of the motor, \(\cos \phi_{2}=\cos 58^{\circ }=\mathbf{0.53 leading } \)
