Guest Question.

The unsymmetrical I beam shown in Fig. 22.24 carries an effective prestress force of 288 kips and supports a superimposed dead load of 345 lb/ft and service live load of 900 lb/ft, in addition to its own weight of 255 lb/ft, on a 50 ft simple span. At the maximum moment section, the effective depth to the main steel is 24.5 in. (eccentricity 11.4 in.). The strands are deflected upward starting 15 ft from the support, and eccentricity is reduced linearly to zero at the support. If concrete with \(f_c^\prime\) = 5000 psi and stirrups with \(f_{yt}\) = 60,000 psi are used, and if the prestressed strands have strength \(f_{pu}\) = 270 ksi, what is the required stirrup spacing at a point 10 ft from the support?

Admin
Solution

For a cross section with the given dimensions, it is easily confirmed that \(I_c = 24,200 in^4\), \(A_c = 245 in^2\), and \(r^2 = I_c/A_c = 99 in^2\). At a distance 10 ft from the support centerline, the tendon eccentricity is

\(e = 11.4 \times \frac{10}{15} = 7.6\) in.

corresponding to an effective depth d from the compression face of 20.7 in. According to the ACI Code, the larger value of d = 0.80 \(\times\) 29 = 23.2 in. will be used. Calculation of \(V_{ci}\) is based on Eqs. (22.43) and (22.44). The bottom-fiber stress due to effective prestress acting alone is

\(f_{pe}=f_{2pe}=-\frac{P_e}{A_c}\left(1+\frac{ec_2}{r^2}\right)=-\frac{288,000}{245}\left(1+\frac{7.6\times15.9}{99}\right)=-2600\) psi

\(M_{cre} = \frac{I_c}{y_t} (6 λ \sqrt{f_c^\prime} + f_{pe} − f_o)\) (22.43)

\(V_{ci} = 0.6 λ \sqrt{f_c^\prime} b_w d_p + V_o + \frac{V_{d + l}}{M_{d+ l}} M_{cre}\) (22.44)

The moment and shear at the section due to beam load alone are, respectively,
\(M_{o, 10} = \frac{w_o x}{2} (ℓ − x ) = 0.255 \times 5 \times 40 = 51\) ft-kips

\(V_{o, 10} = w_o\left(\frac{ℓ}{2}-x\right)= 0.255 \times 15 = 3.8\) kips

and the bottom-fiber stress due to this load is
\(f_{2o} = \frac{51 \times 12,000 \times 15.9}{24,200} = 402\) psi

Then, from Eq. (22.43),
\(M_{cre} = \frac{24,200(425 + 2600 − 402)}{15.9 \times 12} = 333,000\) ft-lb

The ratio of superimposed load shear to moment at the section is
\(\frac{V_{d+ l}}{M_{d+ l}} = \frac{ℓ − 2x}{x (ℓ − x)} = \frac{30}{400} = 0.075 ft^{−1}\)

Equation (22.44) is then used to determine the shear force at which flexure-shear cracks can be expected to form.
\(V_{ci} = [ 0.6 \times 1 \sqrt{5000} ( 5 \times 23.2) + 3800 + 0.075 \times 330,000 ] \times \frac{1}{1000} = 33.5\) kips

The lower limit of \(1.7 \times 1 \sqrt{5000} ( 5 \times 23.2)/1000 = 13.9\) kips does not control.

Calculation of \(V_{cw}\) is based on Eq. (22.45). The slope θ of the tendons at the section under consideration is such that sin θ ≈ tan θ = 11.4/(15 \(\times\) 12) = 0.063. Consequently, the vertical component of the effective prestress force is \(V_p = 0.063 \times 288 = 18.1\) kips. The concrete compressive stress at the section centroid is

\(f_{pc} = \frac{P_e}{A_c} = \frac{288,000}{245} = 1170\) psi

\(V_{cw} = (3.5 λ \sqrt{f_c^\prime} + 0.3 f_{pc}) b_w d_p + V_p\) (22.45)

Equation (22.45) can now be used to find the shear at which web-shear cracks should occur.
\(V_{cw} = [ ( 3.5 \times 1 \sqrt{5000} + 0.3 \times 1170 ) 5 \times 23.2 + 18,100 ] \times \frac{1}{1000} = 87.5\) kips

Thus, in the present case,
\(V_c = V_{ci} = 33.5\) kips

At the section considered, the total shear force at factored loads is
\(V_u = 1.2 \times 0.600 \times 15 + 1.6 \times 0.900 \times 15 = 32.4\) kips

When No. 3 (No. 10) U stirrups are used, for which \(A_v = 2 \times 0.11 = 0.22 in^2\), the required spacing is found from Eq. (22.51) to be

\(s = \frac{ϕ A_v f_{yt} d}{V_u − ϕ V_c} = \frac{0.75 \times 0.22 \times 60,000 \times 23.2}{32,400 − 0.75 \times 33,500} = 32\) in.

Equation (22.53) is then applied to establish a maximum spacing criterion.

\(A_{v, min} = \frac{A_{ps}}{80} \frac{f_{pu}}{f_{yt}} \frac{s}{d} \sqrt{\frac{d}{b_w}}\) (22.53)

\(0.22 = \frac{1.75}{80} \times \frac{270}{60} \times \frac{s}{23.2} \sqrt{\frac{23.2}{5}} = 0.0091 s\)
s = 24.1 in.

The other criteria for maximum spacing, \(\frac{3}{4} \times 29\) = 22 in. and 24 in., however, control here. Open U stirrups will be used, at a spacing of 22 in.

For comparison, the concrete shear will be calculated on the basis of Eq. (22.46). The ratio \(V_u / M_u\) is 0.075, and

\(V_c=\left(0.6\lambda \sqrt{f_c^\prime}+700\frac{V_u d_p}{M_u}\right)b_w d\) (22.46)

\(V_c=\left(0.6\times1\sqrt{5000}+700\times \frac{0.075}{12}\times23.2\right)(5\times23.2)\times \frac{1}{1000}=16.7\) kips

The lower and upper limits, \(2 \times 1 \sqrt{5000} (5 \times 23.2)/1000 = 16.4\) kips and \(5 \times 1 \sqrt{5000} (5 \times 23.2)/1000 = 41.0\) kips, do not control. Thus, on the basis of \(V_c\) obtained from Eq. (22.46), the required spacing of No. 3 (No. 10) U stirrups is

\(s = \frac{0.75 \times 0.22 \times 60,000 \times 23.2}{32,400 − 0.75 \times 16,700} = 11.6\) in.

For the present case, an I-section beam of intermediate span, nearly 2 times the web steel is required at the location investigated if the alternative expression giving \(V_c\) directly is used.
