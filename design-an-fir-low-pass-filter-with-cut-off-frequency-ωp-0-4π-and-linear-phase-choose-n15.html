Guest Question.

Design an FIR low-pass filter with cut-off frequency \(ω_{p} = 0.4π\) and linear phase. (Choose N=15.)

Admin
Solution

According to the specifications, the magnitude and phase of the low-pass filter are:

For N=15 (odd),

\(|H(k)|=|H(15-k)|, 1\leq k\leq 7\) (1)
\(\Phi (k)=\begin{cases} -\frac{\pi k14}{15}, & 0\leq k\leq 7\\ \pi -\frac{\pi k14}{15}=\frac{\pi k}{15}, & 7\leq k\leq 14 \end{cases}\) (2)

The sampling intervals of \(H(e^{jω})\) are \(\frac{2\pi }{N}=\frac{2\pi }{15}\) .The following figure illustrates the ideal magnitude and phase specifications for N=15.

According to Equation 6.54, the impulse response h[n] of the FIR filter is:

\(h[n]=\frac{1}{N} (….), n=0,1,….N-1\) (6.54)

\(h[n]=\frac{H(0)}{15}+\frac{1}{15}\sum\limits_{k=1}^{7}{2(-1)^{k}|H(k)|\cos \frac{(\pi k+2\pi k n)}{15} } ,0\leq n\leq 14\) (3)

\(\begin{matrix} H(0)=H(1)=H(2)=H(3)=1 \\ H(4)=H(5)=H(6)=H(7)=0 \end{matrix}\) . So, Equation (3) becomes:

\(h[n]=\frac{1}{15}+\frac{1}{15}\sum\limits_{k=1}^{3}{2(-1)^{k}|H(k)|\cos \frac{(\pi k+2\pi n)}{15} } ,0\leq n\leq 14\) (4)
\(h[0]=\frac{1}{15}+\frac{1}{15}(2(-1)\cos \frac{\pi }{15}+2(-1)^{2}\cos \frac{2\pi }{15}+2(-1)^{3}\cos \frac{3\pi }{15} )\Rightarrow \)
\(h[0]=\frac{1}{15}+\frac{1}{15}(-1.95629+1.827091-1.618034)=-0.0498155\) (5)

Likewise,

\(h[1]=0.0412022 , h[2]=0.0666667 , h[3]=−0.0364878 ,h[4]=-0.107868, h[5]=0.034078 ,h[6]=0.318892 ,h[7]=0.466667, h[8]=0.318892, h[9]=0.0340779, h[10]=-0.10787, h[11]=-0.036488, h[12]=0.066667, h[13]=0.0412022, h[14]=-0.0498159\)
