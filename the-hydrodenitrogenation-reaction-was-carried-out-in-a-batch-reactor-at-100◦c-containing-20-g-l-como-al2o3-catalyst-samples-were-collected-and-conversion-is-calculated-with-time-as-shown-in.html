Guest Question.

The hydrodenitrogenation reaction was carried out in a batch reactor at 100◦C, containing 20 g/L \(CoMo/Al_{2}O_{3}\) catalyst. Samples were collected and conversion is calculated with time as shown in the table below:

t(min) 0 5 10 20 30 40 50
\(X_{A}\) 0 0.3 0.5 0.73 0.85 0.93 0.97

Determine the reaction order and calculate the specific rate constant. If the observed reaction rate at 100◦C is four times the rate at 80◦C and the catalyst concentration is doubled, calculate the reaction rate at 90◦C. Calculate the conversion after 10 min.

Admin
Solution

Assuming a first-order reaction rate:
\((-r_{A})=kC_{A}=kC_{A0}(1-X_{A})\)

Then:
\(t=-\frac{1}{k}\ln (1-X_{A})\)

Thus, from the date in table 11.9 we calculate:
Table 11.9

t(min) \(X_{A}\) K
0 0 _
5 0.3 0.0713
10 0.5 0.069
20 0.73 0.065
30 0.85 0.063
40 0.93 0.066
50 0.97 0.07
Mean value 0.067

The mean specific constant value k is:
\(k=0.0676 min^{−1}\)

However:
\(\frac{r_{100}}{r_{80}}=4\)

Then:
\(\frac{k_{100}}{k_{80}}=4\)

Thus:
\(k_{80}=\frac{0.0067}{4}=0.0167min^{-1}\)

From these values, we calculate the activation energy:
\(E=R\frac{\ln (k_{100}/k_{80})}{(1/T_{80})-(1/T_{100})}=1.80\times 10^{4} cal/mol\)

Calculate \(k_{0}\):
\(k_{0}=ke^{E/RT}=2.83\times 10^{9}\)

Therefore:
\(k=2.83\times 10^{9}e^{-(1.8\times 10^{4}/RT)}\)

The specific rate constant at 90◦C (363 K) is:
\(k_{90} =0.0341 min^{−1}\)

How the catalyst concentration affects the rate?
\(\frac{(-r_{A})1}{(-r_{A})2}=\frac{k_{1}C_{cat}^{1}}{k_{2}C_{cat}^{2}}=\frac{k_{100}}{k_{90}\times 2}=\frac{0.067}{2\times 0.034}=1\)

In fact, the rate was not affected.
The conversion after 10 min at 90◦C is:
\((1-X_{A})=e^{-kt}=e^{-0.034\times 10}=0.711\)
\(X_{A}=0.288\)
