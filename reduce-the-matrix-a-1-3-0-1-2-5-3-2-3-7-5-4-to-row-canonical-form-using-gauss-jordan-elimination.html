Guest Question.

Reduce the matrix

\( A = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 3 & 0 & -1 \\ 2 & 5 & 3 & -2 \\ 3 & 7 & 5 & -4 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

to row canonical form using Gauss-Jordan elimination.

Admin
Solution

We choose the entry in row 1 and column 1 as the first pivot. First we eliminate all entries below this number. To eliminate the entry in the second row, we use \(-2R_{1} + R_{2} \rightarrow R_{2}\)

This gives

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 3 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & -1 & 3 & 0 \\ 3 & 7 & 5 & -4 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

Next we use \( -3R_{1} + R_{3} \rightarrow R_{3} \) to eliminate the next entry below the first pivot, and we obtain

\( \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 3 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & -1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & -2 & 5 & -1 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Now we select the second entry in row 2 as the next pivot. We eliminate the entry directly below this value using \( -2R_{2} + R_{3} \rightarrow R_{3} \) and we have

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 3 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & -1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -1 & -1 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

In Gauss-Jordan elimination, we want to eliminate all entries above the pivot as well. So we use \(3 R_{2} + R_{1} \rightarrow R_{1}\) , which gives

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 9 & -1 \\ 0 & -1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -1 & -1 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Next we eliminate terms above the \( a_{33}\) entry. First we eliminate the term immediately above by carrying out \( 3R_{3} + R_{2} \rightarrow R_{2}\), which gives

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 9 & -1 \\ 0 & -1 & 0 & -3 \\ 0 & 0 & -1 & -1 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

Now we eliminate the term in the first row above the \( a_{33}\) entry using \( 9R_{3} + R_{1} \rightarrow R_{1} \) and we obtain

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & -10 \\ 0 & -1 & 0 & -3 \\ 0 & 0 & -1 & -1 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

To put the matrix in row canonical form, the leftmost entries in each row must be equal to 1. We divide rows 2 and 3 by âˆ’1 to obtain the row canonical form

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & -10 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ] \)
