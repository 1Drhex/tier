Guest Question.

The voltage wave shown in Fig. 17-35 is applied to a series circuit of R = 2 kΩ and L = 10 H. Use the trigonometric Fourier series to obtain the voltage across the resistor. Plot the line spectra of the applied voltage and \( v_{R} \)to show the effect of the inductance on the harmonics. ω = 377 rad/s.

Admin
Solution

The applied voltage has average value \( V_{\max } / \pi \), as in . The wave function is even and hence the series contains only cosine terms, with coefficients obtained by the following evaluation integral:

\( a_{n}=\frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi / 2}^{\pi / 2} 300 \cos \omega t \cos n \omega t d(\omega t)=\frac{600}{\pi\left(1-n^{2}\right)} \cos n \pi / 2 \quad V \)

Here, \( \cos n \pi / 2 \text { has the value }-1 \text { for } n=2,6,10, \ldots, \text { and }+1 \text { for } n=4,8,12, \ldots \). For n odd, cos nπ/2 = 0. However, for n = 1, the expression is indeterminate and must be evaluated separately.

\( a_{1}=\frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi / 2}^{\pi / 2} 300 \cos ^{2} \omega t d(\omega t)=\frac{300}{\pi}\left[\frac{\omega t}{2}+\frac{\sin 2 \omega t}{4}\right]_{-\pi / 2}^{\pi / 2}=\frac{300}{2} V \)

 

Thus, \( v=\frac{300}{\pi}\left(1+\frac{\pi}{2} \cos \omega t+\frac{2}{3} \cos 2 \omega t-\frac{2}{15} \cos 4 \omega t+\frac{2}{35} \cos 6 \omega t-\cdots\right) (V) \)

In Table 17-3, the total impedance of the series circuit is computed for each harmonic in the voltage expression. The Fourier coefficients of the current series are the voltage series coefficients divided by the \( Z_{n} \text {; } \) the current terms lag the voltage terms by the phase angles \( \theta_{n} \).

Then the current series is

\( \begin{aligned}i=& \frac{300}{2 \pi}+\frac{300}{(2)(4.26)} \cos \left(\omega t-62^{\circ}\right)+\frac{600}{3 \pi(7.78)} \cos \left(2 \omega t-75.1^{\circ}\right) \\&-\frac{600}{15 \pi(15.2)} \cos \left(4 \omega t-82.45^{\circ}\right)+\frac{600}{35 \pi(22.6)} \cos \left(6 \omega t-84.92^{\circ}\right)-\cdots \quad( m A )\end{aligned}\)

 

and the voltage across the resistor is

 

\(\begin{aligned}v_{R}=& R i=95.5+70.4 \cos \left(\omega t-62^{\circ}\right)+16.4 \cos \left(2 \omega t-75.1^{\circ}\right) \\&-1.67 \cos \left(4 \omega t-82.45^{\circ}\right)+0.483 \cos \left(6 \omega t-84.92^{\circ}\right)-\cdots (V)\end{aligned}\)
