Guest Question.

A steam pipe of 5 cm inside diameter and 6.5 cm outside diameter is covered with a 2.75 cm radial thickness of high temperature insulation (k = 1.1 W/m.K). The surface heat transfer coefficient for inside and outside surfaces are 4650 \(W/m^2.K\) and 11.5 \(W/m^2.K,\) respectively. The thermal conductivity of the pipe material is 45 W/m.K. If the steam temperature is 200°C and ambient air temperature is 25°C, determine :
(i) Heat loss per metre length of pipe.
(ii) Temperature at the interface.
(iii) Overall heat transfer coefficient.

Admin
Solution

Given : A steam pipe covered with high temperature insulation :
\(d_1 = 5 cm or r_1 = 0.025 m\)
\(d_2 = 6.5 cm r_2 = 0.0325 m\)
or \(k_1 = 45 W/m.K , k_2 = 1.1 W/m.K\)
\(r_3 = 0.0325 + 0.0275 = 0.06 m\)
\(h_1 = 4650 W/m^2.K, h_2 = 11.5 W/m^2.K\)
\(T_{∞_1} = 200°C, T_{∞_2} = 25°C\)

To find :
(i) Heat loss per metre length of the pipe.
(ii) Temperature \(T_2\) at the interface.
(iii) Overall heat transfer coefficient.
Assumptions :
(i) Steady state conditions.
(ii) Heat transfer in radial direction only.
(iii) 1 m length of the pipe.
(iv) Constant properties.
(v) No contact resistance at the interface.
Analysis : Using the electrical analogy for radial heat flow through composite cylinder.
\(Q = \frac{T_{∞_1} – T_{∞_2}}{ΣR_{th}}\)
where \(R_1 = \frac{1}{h_1 A_i}=\frac{1}{2πr_1 L h_1 }\)
\(=\frac{1}{2π ×0.025 ×1 ×4650 }\)
\(= 0.00137 m^2.K/W\)
\(R_2 =\frac{ln \left(\frac{r_2}{r_1} \right)}{2πL k_1}=\frac{ln \left(\frac{0.0325}{0.025} \right)}{2π ×1 × 45} = 9.28 × 10^{–4} m^2.K/W\)
\(R_3 =\frac{ln \left(\frac{r_3}{r_2} \right)}{2πL k_2}=\frac{ln \left(\frac{0.06}{0.0325} \right)}{2π ×1 × 1.1} = 0.088 m^2.K/W\)
\(R_4 = \frac{1}{h_2 A_o}=\frac{1}{2πr_3 L × h_2 } =\frac{1}{2π ×0.06 ×1 ×11.5 }\)
\(= 0.23 m^2.K/W\)
All resistances are in series, thus
\(ΣR_{th} =R_1 + R_2 + R_3 + R_4\)
\(= 0.00137 + 9.28 × 10^{–4} + 0.088 + 0.23\)
\(= 0.320 m^2.K/W\)
(i) Heat loss per metre length of the pipe
\(Q = \frac{200 – 25}{0.320} = 545.25 W/m.\)
(ii) The temperature \(T_2\) at the interface :
Considering first two resistances of thermal network, then heat flow rate
\(Q =\frac{T_{∞_1} – T_{∞_2}}{R_1 + R_2 }\)

or \(545.25 =\frac{200 – T_2}{0.00137 + 9.28 × 10^{–4}}\)

or \(200 – T_2 = 545.25 × 2.298 × 10^{–3} = 1.253\)
or \(T_2 = 200 – 1.138 = 198.75°C.\)

(iii) Overall heat transfer coefficient based on inner radius
\(U_i = \frac{1}{ A_i ΣR_{th}} = \frac{1}{ π d_1 L ΣR_{th}} \)
\(\frac{1}{ π × 0.05 × 1 × 0.320}\)

\(= 19.89 W/m^2.K.\)
Overall heat transfer coefficient based on outer radius
\(U_o = \frac{1}{ A_o ΣR_{th}} = \frac{1}{ 2π r_3 L ΣR_{th}} \)
\(\frac{1}{ 2π × 0.06 × 1 × 0.320}\)
\(= 8.29 W/m^2.K.\)
