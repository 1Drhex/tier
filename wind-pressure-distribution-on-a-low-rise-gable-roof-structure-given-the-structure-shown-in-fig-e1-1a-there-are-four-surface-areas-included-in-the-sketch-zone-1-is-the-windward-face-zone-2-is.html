Guest Question.

Wind Pressure Distribution on a Low-Rise Gable Roof Structure

Given: The structure shown in Fig. E1.1a. There are four surface areas included in the sketch. Zone (1) is the windward face, zone (2) is the leeward face, and zones (3) and (4) are on the gable roof.

Determine: The wind pressure distribution on the interior zone away from the ends. Assume

\(\overline{V} = 100\) mph and exposure C

Admin
Solution

Applying (1.5) leads to

 

\(P_{stag}=(0.00256)(10^{4})k(z)=25.6k(z)(lb/ft^{2})\)

 

Values of k(z) and the corresponding \(P_{stag}\) are listed below

 

z (ft) k (z) \(P_{stag}\, (lb/ft^{2})\)
15 0.85 21.8
20 0.9 23
25 0.94 24.1
30 0.98 25.1

 

We assume the structure is Category III and use I = 1.15. For low-rise buildings with h < 60 ft, the factors G and \(C_{P}\) are combined and specified as constant for each zone. Using data from ASCE 7-05, the values are

 

Zone \(GC_{P}\) \(IGC_{P}\)
1 0.53 0.609
2 -0.43 -0.495
3 -0.69 -0.794
4 -0.48 -0.552

 

 

Lastly, we compute the design pressure using (1.6). The ASCE 7-05 code assumes that \(P_{design}\) varies on the windward force (zone 1), but specifies constant distributions for the other zones. The details are listed below.

 

Zone 1 \(P_{design}=0.609(25.6)k(z)=15.59\)k(z)
Zone 2 \(P_{design}=-0.495(25.6)k(30)=-12.42\) Psf
Zone 3 \(P_{design}=-0.791(25.6)k(30)=-19.92\) Psf
Zone 4 \(P_{design}=-0.552(25.6)k(30)=-13.85\) Psf

 

Pressure distributions generated with (1.6) define the quasi-static wind load, which acts predominately in the horizontal (lateral) direction. For low-rise buildings, gravity loads are the dominant loads and generally control the structural dimensioning process for vertical members. Since the wind loads are horizontal, whereas the gravity loads are vertical, lateral structural bracing systems such as shown in Fig. 1.14 need to be incorporated in certain types of structures such as a braced frames. This topic is addressed further in Chaps. 11, 14, and 15.
