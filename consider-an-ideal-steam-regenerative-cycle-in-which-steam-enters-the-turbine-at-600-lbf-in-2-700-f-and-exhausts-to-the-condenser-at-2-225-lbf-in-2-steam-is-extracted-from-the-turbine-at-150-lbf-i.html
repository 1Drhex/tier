Guest Question.

Consider an ideal steam regenerative cycle in which steam enters the turbine at 600 \(lbf / in .^{2}\), 700 F, and exhausts to the condenser at 2.225 \(lbf / in .^{2}\). Steam is extracted from the turbine at 150 \(lbf / in .^{2}\) for an open feedwater heater. The feedwater leaves the heater as saturated liquid. The appropriate pumps are used for the water leaving the condenser and the feedwater heater. Calculate the thermal efficiency of the cycle and the net work per pound-mass of steam.

Admin
Solution

From Table F.7.2

\(\begin{aligned}& h _{5}=1350.62 Btu / lbm, \\& s _{5}=1.5871 Btu / lbm R \\& h _{1}=97.97 Btu / lbm, \\& v _{1}=0.01625 ft ^{3} / lbm\end{aligned}\)

Interpolate to get

\(\begin{aligned}& h _{3}=330.67 Btu / lbm , \\& v _{3}=0.01809 ft ^{3} / lbm\end{aligned}\)

C.V. Pump1:

\(\begin{aligned}w _{ P 12} &=0.01625(150-2.2) \frac{144}{778} \\&=0.44 Btu / lbm = h _{2}- h _{1} \\h _{2} &= h _{1}+ w _{ P 12}=98.41 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\)

C.V. Pump2:

\(\begin{aligned}& w _{ P 34}=0.01809(600-150) 144 / 778=1.507 Btu / lbm \\\Rightarrow \quad & h _{4}= h _{3}+ w _{ P 34}=332.18 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\)

C.V. Turbine (high pressure section)
Entropy Eq.: \(s _{6}= s _{5}=1.5871 Btu / lbm R \quad \Rightarrow h _{6}=1208.93 Btu / lbm\)

CV: feedwater heater, call the extraction fraction \(y =\dot{ m }_{6} / \dot{ m }_{3}\)

Continuity Eq.: \(\dot{ m }_{3}=\dot{ m }_{6}+\dot{ m }_{2}\) , Energy Eq.: \(\dot{ m }_{6} h _{6}+\dot{ m }_{2} h _{2}=\dot{ m }_{3} h _{3}\)

\(\begin{aligned}& yh _{6}+(1- y ) h _{2}= h _{3} \quad \Rightarrow \quad y _{6}=\left( h _{3}- h _{2}\right) /\left( h _{6}- h _{2}\right) \\&\Rightarrow \quad y =(330.67-98.41) /(1208.93-98.41)=0.2091\end{aligned}\)

CV: Turbine from 5 to 7

\(\begin{aligned}& s _{7}= s _{5} \Rightarrow x _{7}=(1.5871-0.1817) / 1.7292=0.8127 \\& h _{7}=97.97+0.8127 \times 1019.78=926.75 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\)

The HP turbine section has the full flow, the LP section has fraction (1-y)

\(\begin{aligned}w _{ T } &=\left( h _{5}- h _{6}\right)+(1- y )\left( h _{6}- h _{7}\right) \\&=(1350.62-1208.93)+0.7909(1208.93-926.75)=364.87 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\)

CV: pumps
P2 has the full flow and P1 has the fraction 1- y of the flow

\(\begin{aligned}& w _{ P }=(1- y ) w _{ P 12}+ w _{ P 34}=0.7909 \times 0.44+1 \times 1.507=1.855 Btu / lbm \\& w _{ NET }= w _{ T }- w _{ P }=364.87-1.855= 3 6 3 . 0 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\)

CV: steam generator

\(\begin{aligned}& q _{ H }= h _{5}- h _{4}=1350.62-332.18=1018.44 Btu / lbm \\&\eta_{ TH }= w _{ NET } / q _{ H }=363 / 1018.44= 0 . 3 5 6\end{aligned}\)
