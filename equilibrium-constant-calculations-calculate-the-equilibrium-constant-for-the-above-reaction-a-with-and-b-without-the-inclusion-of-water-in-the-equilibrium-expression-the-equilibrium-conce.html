Guest Question.

Equilibrium Constant Calculations . Calculate the equilibrium constant for the above reaction (a) with and (b) without the inclusion of water in the equilibrium expression. The equilibrium concentrations are as follows: \([NH_{3} ]= 0.0100 M [NH_{4}^+ ] = 0.000400 M; [OH^-] = 0.000400 M \).

Strategy : Set up the equilibrium constant expression with and without the concentration of water, then compare the two.

Admin
Solution

(a) Because molarity is based on 1 L, fi rst we fi nd the concentration of water in water—that is, how many moles of water are present in 1 L of water.

\([H_{2}O ]=\frac{1000 .\cancel{g H_{2}O}}{1 L H_{2}O } \times \frac{1 mol H_{2}O}{18.02 \cancel{g H_{2}O} } =\frac{55.49 mol H_{2}O}{1 L H_{2}O } = 55.49 M\)

We then use this value and the concentration of the other species to calculate the equilibrium constant for this reaction.

\(K=\frac{[0.000400 M] [0.000400 M]}{[0.0100 M] [55.49 M]} =2.88 \times 10^{-7}\)

(b) \(K=\frac{[0.000400 \cancel{ M}] [0.000400 M]}{[0.0100 \cancel{ M}] }= 1.60 \times 10^{-5}\)

he inclusion of the concentration of the solvent, water, gives an equilibrium constant two magnitudes smaller than without it. The equilibrium constant without the inclusion of the solvent is the accepted one.
