Guest Question.

At the beginning of compression in a diesel cycle T = 300 K, P = 200 kPa and after combustion (heat addition) is complete T = 1500 K and P = 7.0 MPa. Find the compression ratio, the thermal efficiency and the mean effective pressure.

Admin
Solution

Standard Diesel cycle. See P-v and T-s diagrams for state numbers.
Compression process (isentropic) from Eqs.6.24-25

\(\begin{aligned}& P _{2}= P _{3}=7000 kPa \Rightarrow v _{1} / v _{2}=\left( P _{2} / P _{1}\right)^{1 / k }=(7000 / 200)^{0.7143}= 1 2 . 6 7 \\& T _{2}= T _{1}\left( P _{2} / P _{1}\right)^{( k -1) / k }=300 K (7000 / 200)^{0.2857}=828.4 K\end{aligned}\)

Expansion process (isentropic) first get the volume ratios

\(\begin{aligned}& v _{3} / v _{2}= T _{3} / T _{2}=1500 / 828.4=1.81 \\& v _{4} / v _{3}= v _{1} / v _{3}=\left( v _{1} / v _{2}\right)\left( v _{2} / v _{3}\right)=12.67 / 1.81=7\end{aligned}\)

The exhaust temperature follows from Eq.6.24

\(\begin{aligned}& T _{4}= T _{3}\left( v _{3} / v _{4}\right)^{ k -1}=1500 K (1 / 7)^{0.4}=688.7 K \\& q _{ L }= C _{ vo }\left( T _{4}- T _{1}\right)=0.717 kJ / kg – K (688.7-300) K =278.5 kJ / kg \\& q _{ H }= h _{3}- h _{2} \approx C _{ po }\left( T _{3}- T _{2}\right)=1.004 kJ / kg – K (1500-828.4) K =674 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

Overall performance

\(\begin{aligned}&\eta=1- q _{ L } / q _{ H }=1-278.5 / 674= 0 . 5 8 7 \\& w _{\text {net }}= q _{ net }= q _{ H }- q _{ L }=674-278.5=395.5 kJ / kg \\& v _{\max }= v _{1}= R T _{1} / P _{1}=0.287 kJ / kg – K \times 300 K / 200 kPa =0.4305 m ^{3} / kg \\& v _{\min }= v _{\max } /\left( v _{1} / v _{2}\right)=0.4305 / 12.67=0.034 m ^{3} / kg \\& P _{ meff }=\frac{ w _{ net }}{ v _{\max }- v _{\min }}=395.5 /(0.4305-0.034)= 9 9 7 kPa\end{aligned}\)

Remark: This is a too low compression ratio for a practical diesel cycle.
