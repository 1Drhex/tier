Guest Question.

A vertical flat plate is 25 cm wide and 80 cm high. The plate is at 40 C and is exposed to saturated steam at 1 atm pressure. What is the rate of heat transfer and the amount of steam condensed in 1 hour?

Admin
Solution

Plate width = 0.25 m
Plate height = L = 0.8 m

Plate surface area = (0.25)(0.8)= \(0.2m^{2} \)

\(T_{s}=40CT_{v}=\) saturation temperature at 1 atm = 100 C

Condensate properties are at film temperature

\(T_{f}=\frac{T_{s}+T_{v} }{2} =\frac{40+100 }{2} =70C\) .

Properties of water at 70 C are

\(\rho =978kg/m^{3} \)

 

\(c_{p}=4190J/kgC \)

 

\(k=0.659W/mC\)

 

\(\mu =0.404\times 10^{-3} kg/ms\)

 

\(v=\mu /\rho =4.13\times 10^{-7} m^{2} /s\)

 

For the saturated steam at 100 C, we have

\(\rho _{v} =0.60kg/m^{3} \)

 

\(h_{fg}2260kJ/kg\)

 

The corrected heat of vaporization is

\(h^{\prime }_{fg}= h_{fg} +0.68c_{p}(T_{v}-T_{s} ) =2260\times 10^{3} +0.68(4190)(100-40 ) =2430\times 10^{3}J/kg\)

Let us first use Eq. (12.90) for the heat transfer coefficient.

\(h=1.13\left[\frac{\rho (\rho -\rho _{v} )gh^{\prime }_{fg}k^{3} }{\mu L(T_{v}-T_{s} )} \right] ^{1/4}\) (12.90)

 

\(h=1.13\left[\frac{978(978 -0.60 )(9.807)(2430\times 10^{3} )(0.659)^{3} }{(0.404\times 10^{-3} ) (0.8)(100-40 )} \right] ^{1/4}=4840W/m^{2}C\)

 

\(q=hA(T_{v}-T_{s} )=(4840)(0.2)(100-40) =58080W\)

 

For the condensate flow rate, we have

\(q=\dot{m}h^{\prime}_{fg} \)

 

\(\dot{m}= {\frac{q}{h^{\prime}_{fg}}} =\frac{58080}{2430\times 10^{3} } =0.024kg/s\times (3600s/h)=86.4kg/h\)

 

Let us check the Reynolds number from Eq. (12.89).

\(Re=\frac{4hL(T_{v}-T_{s} )}{\mu h^{\prime }_{fg} }\) (12.89)

 

\(Re=\frac{4(4840)(0.8)(100-40 )}{(0.404\times 10^{-3} )(2430\times 10^{3} ) } =947\)

 

This is for the wavy surface region, so let us see the results using Eqs. (12.91) and (12.92) .

\(h=\frac{(Re) (k )}{1.08Re^{1.22}-5.2 } \left(\frac{g}{v^{2} } \right) ^{1/3}\) for \(30\lt Re\lt 1800\) and \(\rho _{v}\ll \rho\) (12.91)

 

\(Re=\left[4.81+\frac{3.70kL(T_{v}-T_{s} )}{\mu h^{\prime }_{fg} }\left(\frac{g}{v^{2} } \right) ^{1/3} \right] ^{0.82}\) for \(\rho _{v}\ll \rho\) (12.92)

 

Using Eq. (12.92), the Reynolds number is

\(Re=\left[4.81+\frac{3.70kL(T_{v}-T_{s} )}{\mu h^{\prime }_{fg} }\left(\frac{g}{v^{2} } \right) ^{1/3} \right] ^{0.82}\)

 

\( =\left[4.81+\frac{3.70(0.659)(0.8)(100-40)}{(0.404\times 10^{-3} ) (2430\times 10^{3} ) }\left(\frac{9.807}{\left(4.13\times 10^{-7}\right) ^{2} } \right) ^{1/3} \right] ^{0.82}=1009\)

Using this Reynolds number in Eq. (12.91), we have

\(h=\frac{(Re) (k )}{1.08Re^{1.22}-5.2 } \left(\frac{g}{v^{2} } \right) ^{1/3}\)

 

\(h=\frac{(1009) (0.659 )}{1.08(1009)^{1.22}-5.2 } \left(\frac{9.807}{\left(4.13\times 10^{-7} \right) ^{2} } \right) ^{1/3}=5147W/m^{2}C\)

 

\(q=hA(T_{v}-T_{s} )=(5147)(0.2)(100-40)=61765W\)

 

For the condensate flow rate, we have

\(\dot{m}=\frac{q}{h^{\prime }_{fg} } =\frac{61765}{2430\times 10^{3} } =0.0254kg/s\times (3600s/h)=91.5kg/h\)

 

The rate of heat transfer is 61765 W, and the rate of steam condensation is 91.5 kg/h.

Note: There is only a 6% difference between the results of Eqs. (12.90) and (12.91). The laminar analysis with a 20% increase for the wavy surface region did a pretty good job of determining the heat transfer coefficient.
