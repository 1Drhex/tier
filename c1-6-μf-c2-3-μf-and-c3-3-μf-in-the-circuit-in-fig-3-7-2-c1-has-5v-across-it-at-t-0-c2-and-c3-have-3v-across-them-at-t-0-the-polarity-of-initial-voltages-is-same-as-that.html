Guest Question.

\(C_{1} = 6 \mu F\), \(C_{2} = 3 \mu F\) and \(C_{3} = 3 \mu F\) in the circuit in Fig. 3.7-2. \(C_{1} \) has \(5V\) across it at \(t = 0^{-}\). \(C_{2}\) and \(C_{3}\) have \(3V\) across them at \(t = 0-\). The polarity of initial voltages is same as that of voltage variables identified in the circuit. The current source function \(i_{1}(t)\) is zero after \(3 \mu s\). (i) Find the voltage across all the capacitors at \(5 \mu s\). (ii) Find the stored energy in the individual capacitors and total stored energy at \(4 \mu s.\) (iii) Is there any energy trapped in the system ? If yes, how much?

Admin
Solution

(i) The effective value of \(C_{2}\) and \(C_{3}\) in parallel is \(6\mu F\) and this \(6\mu F\) is in series with \(C_{1}\) which is also of \(6\mu F\).

Therefore, the effective capacitance of the entire circuit is \(3\mu F\).

The initial voltage across the equivalent capacitor is \(5V + 3V = 8V\).

The area under input current is \(9\mu C\) for all \(t ≥ 3\mu s\).

Therefore, the net \(A-s\) dumped into the equivalent capacitor at \(5\mu s\) is \(9\mu C\). This produces a change in voltage by \(9\mu C/3\mu F = 3V\).

Change in voltage in a series combination of capacitors is shared by the capacitors in inverse proportion to capacitance values. Therefore, change in voltage of \(C_{1} = (1/ C_{2}/C_{3})/(1/ C_{1} + 1/C_{2}/ C_{3})\) times \(3V = 1.5V\). Similarly change in \(v_{2}(t) = 1.5V\). Therefore, \(v_{1}(t)\) at \(5\mu s = 5V + 1.5V = 6.5V\) and \(v_{2}(t)\) at \(5\mu s = 3V + 1.5V = 4.5 V\)

(ii) The capacitor voltages at \(4\mu s\) are the same as at \(5\mu s\) since the current source value is zero after \(3\mu s\). Therefore, the stored energy in various capacitors can be calculated using the voltage values.

Stored energy in \(C_{1} = 0.5 × 6 × 6.52 \mu J = 126.75 \mu J\)

Stored energy in \(C_{2} = 0.5 × 3 × 4.52 \mu J = 30.375 \mu J\)

Stored energy in \(C_{3} = 0.5 × 3 × 4.52 \mu J = 30.375 \mu J\)

Total stored energy \(= 187.5 \mu J\)

(iii) The initial charge in \(C_{1}\) was \(30 \mu C\) and in \(C_{2} // C_{3}\) was \(18 \mu C\). Thus initial charges in the two capacitors in series combination were not equal. Hence there will be trapped energy in the system.

Initial stored energy \(= 0.5 × 6 × 52 + 0.5 × 3 × 32 + 0.5 × 3 × 32 = 102 \mu J.\)

Initial energy in the equivalent capacitor \(= 0.5 × 3 × 82 = 96 \mu J.\)
Therefore, trapped energy \(= 102 – 96 = 6 \mu J.\)
