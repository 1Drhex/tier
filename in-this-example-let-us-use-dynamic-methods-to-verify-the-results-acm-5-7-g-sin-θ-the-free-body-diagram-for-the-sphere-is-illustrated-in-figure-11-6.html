Guest Question.

In this example, let us use dynamic methods to verify the results of ( \( a_{C M}=\frac{5}{7} g \sin \theta\) ) The free-body diagram for the sphere is illustrated in Figure 11.6.

Admin
Solution

Newton’s second law applied to the center of mass gives

(1) \( \Sigma F_{x}=M g \sin \theta-f=M a_{\mathrm{CM}} \)

\(\Sigma F_{y}=n-M g \cos \theta=0\)

where x is measured along the slanted surface of the incline. Now let us write an expression for the torque acting on the sphere. A convenient axis to choose is the one that passes through the center of the sphere and is perpendicular to the plane of the ﬁgure.1 Because n and Mg go through the cen-ter of mass, they have zero moment arm about this axis and thus do not contribute to the torque. However, the force of static friction produces a torque about this axis equal to fR in the clockwise direction; therefore, because r is also in the clockwise direction,

\( \tau_{\mathrm{CM}}=f R=I_{\mathrm{CM}} \alpha \) \(\text { Because } I_{\mathrm{CM}}=\frac{2}{5} M R^{2} \text { and } \alpha=a_{\mathrm{CM}} / R, \text { we obtain } \) \(\text { (2) } f=\frac{I_{\text {CM }} \alpha}{R}=\left(\frac{\frac{2}{5} M R^{2}}{R}\right) \frac{a_{\mathrm{CM}}}{R}=\frac{2}{5} M a_{\mathrm{CM}}\)

Substituting Equation (2) in to Equation (1) gives

\( a_{\mathrm{CM}}=\frac{5}{7} g \sin \theta\)

Which agrees with the result of Example 11.1 Note that \(\Sigma \mathbf{F}=m \mathbf{a}\) applies only if \(\Sigma \mathbf{F}\) is the net external force exerted on the sphere and \(\mathbf{a}\) is the acceleration of its center of mass. In the case of our sphere rolling down an incline, even though the frictional force does not change the total kinetic energy of the sphere, it does contribute to \(\Sigma \mathbf{F}\) and thus decreases the acceleration of the center of mass. As a result, the final translational kinetic energy is less than it would be in the absence of friction. As mentioned in Example 11.1 , some of the initial potential energy is converted to rotational kinetic energy.
