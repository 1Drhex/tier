Guest Question.

If you combine three spin-1/2 particles, you can get a total spin of 3/2 or 1/2 (and the latter can be achieved in two distinct ways). Construct the quadruplet and the two doublets, using the notation of Equations 4.175 and 4.176:

\( \left\{\begin{array}{l} |11\rangle=|\uparrow \uparrow\rangle \\ |10\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|\uparrow \downarrow\rangle+|\downarrow \uparrow\rangle) \\ |1-1\rangle=|\downarrow \downarrow\rangle \end{array}\right\} \quad s=1 \text { (triplet) } \) (4.175).

\( \left\{|00\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|\uparrow \downarrow\rangle-|\downarrow \uparrow\rangle)\right\} \quad s=0 \text { (singlet) } \) (4.176).

\( \left\{\begin{array}{ll} \left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{3}{2}\right\rangle & =? ? \\ \left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle & =? ? \\ \left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{-1}{2}\right\rangle & =? ? \\ \left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{-3}{2}\right\rangle & =? ? \end{array}\right\} \quad s=\frac{3}{2} \text { (quadruplet) } \).

\( \left\{\begin{array}{ll} \left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle_{1} & =? ? \\ \left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{-1}{2}\right\rangle_{1} & =? ? \end{array}\right\} \quad s=\frac{1}{2}(\text { doublet } 1) \).

\( \left\{\begin{array}{ll} \left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle_{2} & = & ? ? \\ \left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{-1}{2}\right\rangle_{2} & =&? ? \end{array}\right\} \quad s=\frac{1}{2}(\text { doublet } 2) \).

Hint: The first one is easy: \( \left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{3}{2}\right\rangle=|\uparrow \uparrow \uparrow\rangle \) ; apply the lowering operator to get theother states in the quadruplet. For the doublets you might start with the first two in thesinglet state, and tack on the third:

\( \left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle_{1}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|\uparrow \downarrow\rangle-|\downarrow \uparrow\rangle)|\uparrow\rangle \).

Take it from there make sure \( \left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle_{2} \) is orthogonal to \( \left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle_{1} \text { and to }\left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle \). Note: the two doublets are not uniquely determined—any linear combination of them would still carry spin 1/2. The point is to construct two independent doublets.

Solution

Quartet. \( \left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{3}{2}\right\rangle=|\uparrow \uparrow \uparrow\rangle \). For one particle (Equation 4.146),

\( S _{+}=\hbar\left(\begin{array}{ll} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{array}\right), \quad S _{-}=\hbar\left(\begin{array}{ll} 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \end{array}\right) \) (4.146).

\( S_{-}|\uparrow\rangle=\hbar|\downarrow\rangle, S_{-}|\downarrow\rangle=0 \). For all three, \( S = S _{1}+ S _{2}+ S _{3} \Rightarrow S_{-}=S_{1-}+S_{2-}+S_{3-} \). So (using Equations 4.120 and 4.121):

\( L_{+} f_{\ell}^{m}=\left(A_{\ell}^{m}\right) f_{\ell}^{m+1}, \quad L_{-} f_{\ell}^{m}=\left(B_{\ell}^{m}\right) f_{\ell}^{m-1} \) (4.120).

\( A_{\ell}^{m}=\hbar \sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)-m(m+1)}=\hbar \sqrt{(\ell-m)(\ell+m+1)} \),

\( B_{\ell}^{m}=\hbar \sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)-m(m-1)}=\hbar \sqrt{(\ell+m)(\ell-m+1)} \) (4.121).

\( S_{-}\left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{3}{2}\right\rangle=S_{-}|\uparrow \uparrow \uparrow\rangle=\hbar(|\downarrow \uparrow \uparrow\rangle+|\uparrow \downarrow \uparrow\rangle+|\uparrow \uparrow \downarrow\rangle)=B_{3 / 2}^{3 / 2}\left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle=\sqrt{3} \hbar\left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle \).

\( \Rightarrow\left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(|\downarrow \uparrow \uparrow\rangle+|\uparrow \downarrow \uparrow\rangle+|\uparrow \uparrow \downarrow\rangle) \).

\( S_{-}\left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle=\frac{\hbar}{\sqrt{3}}(|\downarrow \downarrow \uparrow\rangle+|\downarrow \uparrow \downarrow\rangle+|\downarrow \downarrow \uparrow\rangle+|\uparrow \downarrow \downarrow\rangle+|\downarrow \uparrow \downarrow\rangle+|\uparrow \downarrow \downarrow\rangle)=\frac{2 \hbar}{\sqrt{3}}(|\downarrow \downarrow \uparrow\rangle+|\downarrow \uparrow \downarrow\rangle+|\uparrow \downarrow \downarrow\rangle) \).

\( =B_{3 / 2}^{1 / 2}\left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{-1}{2}\right\rangle=2 \hbar\left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{-1}{2}\right\rangle \Rightarrow\left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{-1}{2}\right\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(|\downarrow \downarrow\rangle+|\downarrow \uparrow \downarrow\rangle+|\uparrow \downarrow \downarrow\rangle) \).

\( S_{-}\left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{-1}{2}\right\rangle=\frac{\hbar}{\sqrt{3}}(|\downarrow \downarrow \downarrow\rangle+|\downarrow \downarrow \downarrow\rangle+|\downarrow \downarrow \downarrow\rangle)=\sqrt{3} \hbar|\downarrow \downarrow \downarrow\rangle=B_{3 / 2}^{-1 / 2}\left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{-3}{2}\right\rangle=\sqrt{3} \hbar\left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{-3}{2}\right\rangle \).

\( \left.\Rightarrow | \frac{3-3}{2}\right\rangle=|\downarrow \downarrow \downarrow\rangle \).

Doublet 1. \( \left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle_{1}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|\uparrow \downarrow \uparrow\rangle-|\downarrow \uparrow \uparrow\rangle) \).

\( S_{-}\left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle_{1}=\frac{\hbar}{\sqrt{2}}(|\downarrow \downarrow \uparrow\rangle+|\uparrow \downarrow \downarrow\rangle-|\downarrow \downarrow \uparrow\rangle-|\downarrow \uparrow \downarrow\rangle)=\frac{\hbar}{\sqrt{2}}(|\uparrow \downarrow \downarrow\rangle-|\downarrow \uparrow \downarrow\rangle) \).

\( =B_{1 / 2}^{1 / 2}\left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{-1}{2}\right\rangle_{1}=\hbar\left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{-1}{2}\right\rangle_{1} \Rightarrow \left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{-1}{2}\right\rangle_{1}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|\uparrow \downarrow \downarrow\rangle-|\downarrow \uparrow \downarrow\rangle) \).

Doublet 2. \( \left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle_{2}=a|\uparrow \uparrow \downarrow\rangle+b|\uparrow \downarrow \uparrow\rangle+c|\downarrow \uparrow \uparrow\rangle \). Make it orthogonal to \( \left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle_{1} \text { and }\left|\frac{3}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle \):

\( { }_{1}\left\langle\frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{2} \mid \frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle_{2}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\left\langle\uparrow \downarrow \uparrow|-\langle\downarrow \uparrow \uparrow|)(a|\uparrow \uparrow \downarrow\rangle+b|\uparrow \downarrow \uparrow\rangle+c|\downarrow \uparrow \uparrow\rangle)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(b-c)=0 \Rightarrow c=b\right.\right. \).

\( \left\langle\frac{3}{2} \frac{1}{2} \mid \frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle_{2}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\left(\left\langle\downarrow \uparrow \uparrow \left|+\left\langle\uparrow \downarrow \uparrow|+\langle\uparrow \uparrow \downarrow|)(a|\uparrow \uparrow \downarrow\rangle+b|\uparrow \downarrow \uparrow\rangle+c|\downarrow \uparrow \uparrow\rangle)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(a+b+c)=0 \Rightarrow a=-2 b\right.\right.\right.\right. \).

Normalization: \( |a|^{2}+|b|^{2}+|c|^{2}=1 \Rightarrow 4|b|^{2}+|b|^{2}+|b|^{2}=6|b|^{2}=1 \Rightarrow b=\frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}, c=\frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}, a=-\frac{2}{\sqrt{6}} \).

\( \left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle_{2}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}(-2|\uparrow \uparrow \downarrow\rangle+|\uparrow \downarrow \uparrow\rangle+|\downarrow \uparrow \uparrow\rangle) \).

\( S_{-}\left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{2}\right\rangle_{2}=\frac{\hbar}{\sqrt{6}}(-2|\downarrow \uparrow \downarrow\rangle-2|\uparrow \downarrow \downarrow\rangle+|\downarrow \downarrow \uparrow\rangle+|\uparrow \downarrow \downarrow\rangle+|\downarrow \downarrow \uparrow\rangle+|\downarrow \uparrow \downarrow\rangle) \).

\( =\frac{\hbar}{\sqrt{6}}(-|\downarrow \uparrow \downarrow\rangle-|\uparrow \downarrow \downarrow\rangle+2|\downarrow \downarrow \uparrow\rangle)=B_{1 / 2}^{1 / 2}\left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{-1}{2}\right\rangle_{2}=\hbar\left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{-1}{2}\right\rangle_{2} \).

\( \left|\frac{1}{2} \frac{-1}{2}\right\rangle_{2}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}(-|\downarrow \uparrow \downarrow\rangle-|\uparrow \downarrow \downarrow\rangle+2|\downarrow \downarrow \uparrow\rangle) \).
