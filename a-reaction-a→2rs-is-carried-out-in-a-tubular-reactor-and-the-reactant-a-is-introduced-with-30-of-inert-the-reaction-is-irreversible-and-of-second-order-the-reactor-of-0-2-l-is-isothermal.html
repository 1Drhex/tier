Guest Question.

A reaction A→2R+S is carried out in a tubular reactor, and the reactant A is introduced with 30% of inert. The reaction is irreversible and of second order. The reactor of 0.2 L is isothermal, and the reaction occurs at 800K and pressure of 10 atm.
It is known that the outflow of the product R is 0.034 mol/s and the conversion was
10%. Calculate the reaction rate constant. If this reaction would be further carried out in a batch reactor, calculate the reaction time for the previous conditions.

Admin
Solution

Since we have a second-order irreversible reaction, the rate is:
\(\left ( -r_{A} \right )=kC_{A}^{2}\)

and consequently as a function of the conversion we have:
\(\left ( -r_{A} \right )=kC_{A0}^{2}\frac{\left ( 1-X_{A} \right )^{2}}{\left ( 1+\varepsilon _{A}X_{A} \right )^{2}}\)

Since the reactor is tubular, we have:
\(\tau C_{A0}=\int_{0}^{X_{A}}\frac{\left ( 1+\varepsilon _{A}X_{A} \right )^{2}dX_{A}}{k\left ( 1-X_{A} \right )^{2}}dX_{A}\)

whose solution was:
\(\left ( 1+\varepsilon _{A} \right )^{2}\frac{X_{A}}{\left ( 1-X_{A} \right )}+\varepsilon _{A}^{2}X_{A}+2\varepsilon _{A}\left ( 1+\varepsilon _{A} \right )\ln \left ( 1-X_{A} \right )=\tau kC_{A0}\)

We can calculate the conversion through the outflow of the product R:
\(\frac{F_{A0}-F_{A}}{F_{A0}}=\frac{F_{R}-F_{R0}}{2F_{A0}}=\frac{F_{S}-F_{S0}}{F_{A0}}\)

Where \(F_{R0} =F_{S0} =0\)

Therefore:
\(F_{R}=2F_{A0}.X_{A}\)
\(0.034=2F_{A0} × 0.1\)
\(F_{A0} =0.170 mol/s\)

But:
\(\tau =\frac{V}{\nu _{0}}=\frac{VC_{A0}}{\nu _{0}C_{A0}}=\frac{VC_{A0}}{F_{A0}}=\frac{0.2\times 1.06\times 10^{1}}{0.170}=0,124s^{-1}\)

Calculation of \(\varepsilon _{A}\):

Total Inert S 2R A
1 0.3 0 0 0.7 Initial
2.4 0.3 0.7 1.4 0 Final
\(\varepsilon _{A}=\frac{2.4-1}{1}=1.4\)

with \(X_{A} =0.1\), we have:
=9.73L/mol · s

(b) If the reaction is conducted in a batch system at constant volume, we have that \(\varepsilon _{A} =0\).
Thus, we calculate the time necessary to reach the same previous conversions, by using the expression for the batch system:
\(\frac{X_{A}}{\left ( 1-X_{A} \right )}=kC_{A0}t\)

Thus,
\(\tau =0.107 s\)

When comparing with \(\tau =0.124s^{-1}\), we can observe a difference due to the expansion of the gas-phase continuous system.
