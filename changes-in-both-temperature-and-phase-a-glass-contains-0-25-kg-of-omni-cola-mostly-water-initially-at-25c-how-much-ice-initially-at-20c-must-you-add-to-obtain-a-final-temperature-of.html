Guest Question.

CHANGES IN BOTH TEMPERATURE AND PHASE

A glass contains 0.25 kg of Omni-Cola (mostly water) initially at 25°C. How much ice, initially at -20°C, must you add to obtain a final temperature of 0°C with all the ice melted? Ignore the heat capacity of the glass.

Admin
Solution

IDENTIFY and SET UP:

The Omni-Cola and ice exchange heat. The cola undergoes a temperature change; the ice undergoes both a temperature change and a phase change from solid to liquid. We use subscripts C for cola, I for ice, and W for water. The target variable is the mass of ice, \(m_I\). We use Eq. (17.13) to obtain an expression for the amount of heat involved in cooling the drink to T = 0°C and warming the ice to T = 0°C, and Eq. (17.20) to obtain an expression for the heat required to melt the ice at 0°C.

We have \(T_{0C}\) = 25°C and \(T_{0I}\) = -20°C, Table 17.3 gives \(c_W\) = 4190 J/kg . K and \(c_I\) = 2100 J/kg . K, and Table 17.4 gives \(L_{\mathrm{f}}=3.34 \times 10^{5} \mathrm{~J} / \mathrm{kg}\).

Q = mc ∆T (17.13)

EXECUTE:

From Eq. (17.13), the (negative) heat gained by the Omni-Cola is \(Q_{\mathrm{C}}=m_{\mathrm{C}} c_{\mathrm{W}} \Delta T_{\mathrm{C}}\). The (positive) heat gained by the ice in warming is \(Q_{\mathrm{I}}=m_{\mathrm{I}} c_{\mathrm{I}} \Delta T_{\mathrm{I}}\). The (positive) heat required to melt the ice is \(Q_{2}=m_{\mathrm{I}} L_{\mathrm{f}} . \text { We set } Q_{\mathrm{C}}+Q_{\mathrm{I}}+Q_{2}=0\), insert \(\Delta T_{\mathrm{C}}=T-T_{0 \mathrm{C}} \text { and } \Delta T_{\mathrm{I}}=T-T_{0 \mathrm{I}}\), and solve for \(m_I\):

\(\begin{aligned}m_{\mathrm{C} c_{\mathrm{W}}} \Delta T_{\mathrm{C}}+m_{\mathrm{I}} c_{\mathrm{I}}\Delta T_{\mathrm{I}}+m_{\mathrm{I}} L_{\mathrm{f}} &=0 \\m_{\mathrm{C}} c_{\mathrm{W}}\left(T-T_{0\mathrm{C}}\right)+m_{\mathrm{I}} c_{\mathrm{I}}\left(T-T_{0 \mathrm{I}}\right)+m_{\mathrm{I}}L_{\mathrm{f}} &=0 \\m_{\mathrm{I}}\left[c_{\mathrm{I}}\left(T-T_{0 \mathrm{I}}\right)+L_{\mathrm{f}}\right] &=-m_{\mathrm{C}} c_{\mathrm{W}}\left(T-T_{0 \mathrm{C}}\right) \\m_{\mathrm{I}} &=m_{\mathrm{C}} \frac{c_{\mathrm{W}}\left(T_{0 \mathrm{C}}-T\right)}{c_{\mathrm{I}}\left(T-T_{0 \mathrm{I}}\right)+L_{\mathrm{f}}}\end{aligned}\)

Substituting numerical values, we find that \(m_I\) = 0.070 kg = 70 g.

EVALUATE: Three or four medium-size ice cubes would make about 70 g, which seems reasonable given the 250 g of Omni-Cola to be cooled.
