Finding the Switching Loss of a Transistor

The waveforms of the transistor switch in Figure 4.31 are shown in Figure 4.35. The parameters are \(V_{CC} = 250 V, V_{B_E(sat)} = 3 V, I_B = 8 A, V_{CS(sat)} = 2 V, I_{CS} = 100 A, t_d = 0.5 μs, t_r = 1 μs,t_s = 5 μs, t_f = 3 μs\),and \(f_s = 10 kHz\). The duty cycle is k = 50%. The collector-to-emitter leakage current is \(I_{CEO} = 3 mA\). Determine the power loss due to collector current (a) during
turn-on \(t_{on }= t_d + t_r\), (b) during conduction period tn, (c) during turn-off \(t_o = t_s + t_f\), (d) during off-time to, and (e) total average power losses \(P_T\). (f) Plot the instantaneous power due to collector current \(P_c(t)\).

Admin
Solution

\(T = 1/f_s = 100 μs, k = 0.5, kT = t_d + t_r + t_n = 50 μs, t_n = 50 – 0.5 – 1 = 48.5 μs, (1 – k)T = t_s + t_f + t_o = 50 μs\), and \(t_o = 50 – 5 – 3 = 42 μs\).

a. During delay time, \(0≤ t ≤ t_d\):

\(i_c(t) = I_{CEO}\)

 

\(v_{CE}(t) = V_{CC}\)

The instantaneous power due to the collector current is

\(P_c(t) = i_cv_{CE }= I_{CEO} V_{CC}\)

 

\(= 3 × 10^{-3} × 250 = 0.75 W\)

The average power loss during the delay time is

\(P_d=\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^{t_d}{P_c(t)dt}= I_{CEO} V_{CC} t_d f_s\) (4.35)

\(= 3 × 10^{-3} × 250 × 0.5 × 10^{-6} × 10 × 10^3 = 3.75 mW\)

During rise time, \(0 ≤t ≤t_r\):

\(i_c(t)=\frac{I_{CS}}{t_r}t\)

 

\(\upsilon _{CE}(t)=V_{CC}+(V_{CE(sat)}-V_{CC})\frac{t}{t_r}\)

 

\(P_c(t)=i_c\upsilon _{CE}=I_{CS}\frac{t}{t_r}\left[V_{CC}+(V_{CE(sat)}-V_{CC})\frac{t}{t_r} \right]\) (4.36)

The power \(P_c(t)\) is maximum when \(t = t_m\), where

 

\(t_m=\frac{t_rV_{CC}}{2[V_{CC}-V_{CE(sat)}]}\) (4.37)

 

\(=1\times \frac{250}{2(250 – 2)}= 0.504 μs\)

and Eq. (4.36) yields the peak power

\(P_p= \frac{V_{CC}^2I_{CS}}{4[V_{CC} – V_{CE(sat)}]}\) (4.38)

 

\(=250^2\times \frac{100}{4(250 -2)}=6300 W\)

 

\(P_r=\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^{t_r}{P_c(t)dt}= f_s I_{CS} t_r \left[\frac{V_{CC}}{2}+\frac{V_{CE(sat)}- V_{CC}}{3} \right]\) (4.39)

 

\(= 10 \times 10^3 \times 100 \times 1 \times 10^{-6}\left[\frac{250}{2}+\frac{2 -250}{3} \right]=42.33 W\)

The total power loss during the turn-on is

\(P_{on} = P_d + P_r\) (4.40)

= 0.00375 + 42.33 = 42.33 W

b. The conduction period, \(0 ≤t ≤t_n\):

\(i_c(t) = I_{CS}\)

 

\(\upsilon _{CE(sat)}(t)=V_{CE(sat)}\)

 

\(P_c(t) = i_cv_{CE} = V_{CE(sat)} I_{CS}\)

= 2 ×100 = 200 W

\(P_n=\frac{1}{T} \int_{0}^{t_n}{P_c(t)dt} = V_{C E(sat)} I_{CS} t_n f_s\) (4.41)

 

\(= 2 × 100 × 48.5 × 10^{-6} × 10 × 10^3 = 97 W\)

c. The storage period, \(0 ≤ t ≤ t_s\):

\(i_c(t) = I_{CS}\)

 

\(\upsilon _{CE}(t)= V_{CE(sat)}\)

 

\(P_c(t) =i_c\upsilon _{CE}= V_{CE(sat)}I_{CS}\)

= 2 × 100 = 200 W

\(P_s=\frac{1}{T} \int_{0}^{t_s}{P_c(t)dt} = V_{C E(sat)} I_{CS} t_s f_s\) (4.42)

 

\(= 2 × 100 × 5 × 10^{-6} × 10 × 10^3 = 10 W\)

The fall time, \(0 ≤ t ≤ t_f\) :

\(i_c(t)=I_{CS}\left(1-\frac{t}{t_f} \right) \), neglecting \(I_{CEO}\)

\(\upsilon _{CE}(t)=\frac{V_{CC}}{t_f}t\), neglecting \(I_{CEO}\) (4.43)

 

\(P_C(t)= i_c\upsilon _{CE}=V_{CC}I_{CS}\left[\left(1 -\frac{t}{t_f} \right)\frac{t}{t_f} \right]\)

This power loss during fall time is maximum when \(t = t_f /2 = 1.5 μs \)and Eq. (4.43) gives the peak power,

\(P_m =\frac{V_{CCI+{CS}}}{4}\) (4.44)

 

\(=250 \times \frac{100}{4}=6250 W\)

 

\(P_f=\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^{t_f}{P_c(t)dt}=\frac{V_{CC}I_{CS}t_ff_s}{6}\) (4.45)

 

\(=\frac{250 \times 100 \times 3 \times 10^{-6} \times 10 \times 10^3}{6} =125 W\)

The power loss during turn-off is

\(P_{off}=P_s + P_f=I_{CS}f_s\left(t_sV_{CE(sat)}+\frac{V_{CC}t_f}{6} \right)\) (4.46)

= 10 + 125 = 135 W

d. Off-period, \(0 ≤ t ≤ t_o\):

\(i_c(t) = I_{CEO}\)

 

\(\upsilon _{CE}(t)=V_{CC}\)

 

\(P_c(t)=i_v\upsilon _{CE}=I_{CEO }V_{CC}\) (4.47)

 

\(= 3 × 10^{-3 }× 250 = 0.75 W\)

 

\(P_0=\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^{t_0}{P_c(t)dt}=I_{CEO}V_{CC}t_0f_s\)

 

\(= 3 × 10^{-3} × 250 × 42 × 10^{-6} × 10 × 10^3 = 0.315 W\)

e. The total power loss in the transistor due to collector current is

\(P_T = P_{on }+ P_n + P_{off} + P_0\) (4.48)

= 42.33 + 97 + 135 + 0.315 = 274.65 W

f. The plot of the instantaneous power is shown in Figure 4.36

Note: The switching losses during the transition from on to off and vice versa are much more than the on-state losses. The transistor must be protected from breakdown due to a high junction temperature.
