Guest Question.

An aircraft P takes off at A with a velocity \(v_{0} \) of 250 km / h and climbs in the vertical \(\acute{y} -\acute{z} \) plane at the constant 15° angle with an acceleration along its flight path of 0.8 \({m}/{s^{2} }\) . Flight progress is monitored by radar at point O. (a) Resolve the velocity of P into cylindrical-coordinate components 60 seconds after takeoff and find \(\dot{r} \) , \(\dot{\theta } \) , and \(\dot{z} \) for that instant. (b) Resolve the velocity of the aircraft P into spherical-coordinate components 60 seconds after takeoff and find \(\dot{R} \), \(\dot{\theta } \), and \(\dot{\phi } \) for that instant.

Admin
Solution

(a) The accompanying figure shows the velocity and acceleration vectors in the \(\acute{y} -\acute{z} \) plane. The takeoff speed is

\(v_{0}=\frac{250}{3.6} =69.4{m}/{s}\)

and the speed after 60 seconds is

\(v=v_{0}+at=69.4+0.8(60)=117.4{m}/{s}\)

The distance s traveled after takeoff is

\(s=s_{0}+v_{0}t+\frac{1}{2}at^{2} =0+69.4(60)+\frac{1}{2}0.8(60)^{2} =5610m\)

The y-coordinate and associated angle Θ are

\(y=5610\cos 15^{\circ } =5420m\)

\(\Theta =\tan ^{-1} \frac{5420}{3000} =61.0^{\circ }\)

From the figure (b) of x-y projections, we have

\(r=\sqrt{3000^{2}+5420^{2} } =6190m\)

\(v_{xy} =v\cos 15^{\circ } =117.4\cos 15^{\circ } =113.4{m}/{s}\)

\(v_{r}=\dot{r} =v_{xy}\sin \Theta =113.4\sin 61.0^{\circ }=99.2 {m}/{s}\)

\(v_{\Theta }=r\dot{\Theta } =v_{xy}\cos \Theta =113.4\cos 61.0^{\circ }=55.0 {m}/{s}\)

So \(\dot{\Theta }=\frac{55.0}{6190} =8.88(10^{-3} ){rad}/{s}\)

Finally \(\dot{z }=v_{z}=v\sin 15^{\circ }=117.4\sin 15^{\circ }=30.4{m}/{s}\)

(b) Refer to the accompanying figure (c), which shows the x-y plane and various velocity components projected into the vertical plane containing r and R. Note that

\(z=v_{z}=y\tan 15^{\circ }=5420\tan 15^{\circ }=1451m\)

\(\phi =\tan ^{-1} \frac{z}{r} =\tan ^{-1} \frac{1451}{6190}=13.19^{\circ }\)

\(R=\sqrt{r^{2} +z^{2} } =\sqrt{6190^{2} +1451^{2} } =6360m\)

From the figure,

\(v_{R} =\dot{R} =99.2\cos 13.19^{\circ } =103.6{m}/{s}\)

\(\dot{\Theta }=8.88(10^{-3} ){rad}/{s}\), , as in part (a)

\(v_{\phi } =R\dot{\phi } =30.4\cos 13.19^{\circ } -99.2\sin 13.19^{\circ }=6.95{m}/{s}\)

\(\dot{\phi }=\frac{6.95}{6360} =1.093(10^{-3}){rad}/{s}\)
