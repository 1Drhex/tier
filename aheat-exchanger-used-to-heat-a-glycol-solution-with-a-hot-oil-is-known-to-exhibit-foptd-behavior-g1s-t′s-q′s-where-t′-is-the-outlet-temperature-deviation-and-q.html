Guest Question.

A heat exchanger used to heat a glycol solution with a hot oil is known to exhibit FOPTD behavior, \(G_{1}(s)=T^{\prime}(s) / Q^{\prime}(s)\), where \(T^{\prime}\) is the outlet temperature deviation and \(Q^{\prime}\) is the hot oil flow rate deviation. A thermocouple is placed \(3 m\) downstream from the outlet of the heat exchanger. The average velocity of the glycol in the outlet pipe is \(0.5 m / s\). The thermocouple also is known to exhibit first-order behavior; however, its time constant is expected to be considerably smaller than the heat exchanger time constant.

(a) Data from a unit step test in \(Q^{\prime}\) on the complete system are shown in Fig. E7.7. Using a method of your choice, calculate the time constants of this process from the step response.

(b) From your empirical model, find transfer functions for the heat exchanger, pipe, and thermocouple. Think of the model as the product of three transfer functions: process, pipe flow, and sensor. What assumptions do you have to make to obtain these individual transfer functions from the overall transfer function?

Admin
Solution

a) From the plot, time delay \(\theta=4.0 min\)

Using Smith’s method,

from the graph, \(t_{20}+\theta \approx 5.6, \quad t_{60}+\theta \approx 9.1\)

\(t_{20}=1.6, t_{60}=5.1, t_{20} / t_{60}=1.6 / 5.1=0.314\)

From Fig.7.7, \(\zeta=1.63, t_{60} / \tau=3.10 \quad, \quad \tau=1.645\)

Using Eqs. 5-45 and \(5-46, \tau_{1}=4.81 min , \tau_{2}=0.56 min\)

\(\tau_{2}=\frac{\tau}{\zeta+\sqrt{\zeta^{2}-1}} \quad(\zeta \geq 1)\) (5-45)

\(Y(s)=\frac{K M}{s\left(\tau^{2} s^{2}+2 \zeta \tau s+1\right)}\) (5-46)

b) Overall transfer function

\(G(s)=\frac{10 e^{-4 s}}{\left(\tau_{1} s+1\right)\left(\tau_{2} s+1\right)} \quad, \quad \tau_{1}>\tau_{2}\)

Assuming plug-flow in the pipe with constant-velocity,

\(G_{\text {pipe }}(s)=e^{-\theta_{p} s} \quad, \quad \theta_{p}=\frac{3}{0.5} \times \frac{1}{60}=0.1 min\)

Assuming that the thermocouple has unit gain and no time delay

\(G_{T C}(s)=\frac{1}{\tau_{2} s+1} \quad \text { since } \quad \tau_{2}<<\tau_{1}\)

Then

\(G_{H E}(s)=\frac{10 e^{-3 s}}{\tau_{1} s+1}\)

so that,

\(G(s)=G_{H E}(s) G_{\text {pipe }}(s) G_{T C}(s)=\left(\frac{10 e^{-3 s}}{\tau_{1} s+1}\right)\left(e^{-0.1 s}\right)\left(\frac{1}{\tau_{2} s+1}\right)\)
