Guest Question.

A single-strand roller chain drive connects a 10-hp engine to a lawn waste chipper/shredder, as shown in Figure 11.13. As the engine operates at 1200 rpm, the shredding teeth should rotate at 240 rpm. The drive sprocket has 18 teeth. Indicate the number of links in a suitable chain. 1.Select an Appropriate Chain Pitch 2.Determine the Number of Teeth on the Driven Sprocket 3.Determine the Pitch Diameter of the Sprockets 4.Calculate the Chain Speed 5.Determine an Appropriate Center Distance.

Admin
Solution

1: With a 10-hp engine driving a sprocket at 1200 rpm, Figure 11.10 specifies that a 40-pitch, single-strand chain is appropriate.

2: By rewriting equation \(VR=\frac{\omega _{driver}}{\omega _{driven}}=\frac{\omega _1}{\omega _2}=\frac{d_2}{d_1}=\frac{N_2}{N_1} \), the number of teeth needed on the driven sprocket can be determined.

\(N_2=N_1(\frac{\omega _1}{\omega _2} )=18\left\{\frac{1200\ rpm}{240\ rpm} \right\}=90\ teeth\)

Notice from Table 11.5 that a 90-tooth sprocket is commercially available for a No. 40 chain.

3: A No. 40 roller chain has a pitch of

\(p=\frac{40}{80}=0.5\ in.\)

From equation \(d=\frac{p}{sin\ (180^\circ /N)} \), the pitch diameters of the sprockets are

\(d_1=\frac{p}{sin(\frac{180^\circ }{N_1} )}=\frac{0.5\ in.}{sin(\frac{180^\circ }{18\ teeth} )}=2.88\ in.\)

\(d_2=\frac{p}{sin(\frac{180^\circ }{N_2} )}=\frac{0.5\ in.}{sin(\frac{180^\circ }{90\ teeth} )}=14.33\ in.\)

4: The chain speed can be calculated from equation \(v_c=\frac{d_1}{2}\omega _1=\frac{d_2}{2}\omega _2 \).

\(\omega _1=1200\ rev/min(\frac{2\pi \ rad}{1\ rev} )=10,838\ rad/min\)

\(v_c=(\frac{d_1}{2} )\omega _1=(\frac{2.88\ in.}{2} )10,833\ rad/min=15,603\ in./min=1300\ fpm\)

With a chain speed of 1300 fpm, a bath lubrication system for the chain is desired.

5: The suggested center distance for a chain drive is

30p < C < 50p

15 in. < C < 25 in.

A mid-value of 20 in. is tentatively selected. Substituting into equation \(L=\frac{2C}{p}+\frac{(N_2+N_1)}{2}=\left\{\frac{p(N_2-N_1)^2}{4\pi ^2C} \right\}\) gives

\(L=\frac{2C}{p}+\frac{(N_2+N_1)}{2}=\left\{\frac{p(N_2-N_1)^2}{4\pi ^2C} \right\}\)

\(=\frac{2(20\ in.)}{(0.5\ in.)}+\frac{(90+18)}{2}+\left\{\frac{0.5\ in.(90-18)^2}{4\pi ^2(20\ in.)} \right\}=137.28\ links\)

An even 138 links will be specified. The corresponding, actual center distance is computed from equation \(c=\frac{p}{4}[L-\frac{(N_2+N_1)}{2}+\sqrt{\left\{L-\frac{(N_2+N_1)}{2} \right\}^2-\frac{8(N_2-N_1)^2}{4\pi ^2} } ] \).

\(c=\frac{p}{4}[L-\frac{(N_2+N_1)}{2}+\sqrt{\left\{L-\frac{(N_2+N_1)}{2} \right\}^2-\frac{8(N_2-N_1)^2}{4\pi ^2} } ]\)

\(=\frac{(0.5\ in.)}{4}[(138)-\frac{(90+18)}{2}+\sqrt{\left\{(138)-\frac{(90+18)}{2} \right\}^2-\frac{8(90-18)^2}{4\pi ^2} } ] =20.187\ in.\)
