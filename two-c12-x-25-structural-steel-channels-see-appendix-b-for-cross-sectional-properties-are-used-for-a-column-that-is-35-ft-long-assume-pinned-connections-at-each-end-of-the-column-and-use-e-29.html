Guest Question.

Two C12 × 25 structural steel channels (see Appendix B for cross-sectional properties) are used for a column that is 35-ft long. Assume pinned connections at each end of the column and use E = 29,000 ksi for the steel. Determine the total compressive load required to buckle the two members if:
(a) they act independently of each other.
(b) they are latticed back-to-back as shown in Figure P16.7.

Admin
Solution

The following section properties for a standard steel C12 × 25 shape are given in Appendix B:

\(A=7.34 \text { in. }^{2}, I_{x}=144 in .^{4}, I_{y}=4.45 in .^{4}, \bar{x}=0.674 in.\)

(a) Independent channels:
Consider buckling about horizontal cross-sectional axis:

\(\begin{aligned}&I=2\left(144 in. ^{4}\right)=288 in .{ }^{4} \\&P_{c r}=\frac{\pi^{2} E I}{L^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(29,000 ksi )\left(288 in .^{4}\right)}{[(35 ft )(12 in . / ft )]^{2}}=467.296 kips (b)\end{aligned}\)

Consider buckling about vertical cross-sectional axis:

\(\begin{aligned}&I=2\left(4.45 in. ^{4}\right)=8.90 in .{ }^{4} \\&P_{c r}=\frac{\pi^{2} E I}{L^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(29,000 ksi )\left(8.90 in .^{4}\right)}{[(35 ft )(12 in . / ft )]^{2}}=14.44 kips \end{aligned}\)

Euler buckling load for independent channels:

\(P_{c r}=14.44 kips\)

 

(b) Latticed channels:
Consider buckling about horizontal cross-sectional axis:

\(P_{c r}=467.296 kips\) calculated previously in Eq. (a)

Consider buckling about vertical cross-sectional axis:

\(\begin{aligned}&I=2\left[4.45 in .^{4}+(3 in .+0.674 \text { in. })^{2}\left(7.34 in .^{2}\right)\right]=207.055 in .^{4} \\&P_{c r}=\frac{\pi^{2} E I}{L^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(29,000 ksi )\left(207.055 in. ^{4}\right)}{[(35 ft )(12 in. / ft )]^{2}}=335.957 kips\end{aligned}\)

Euler buckling load for latticed channels:

\(P_{c r}=336 kips\)
