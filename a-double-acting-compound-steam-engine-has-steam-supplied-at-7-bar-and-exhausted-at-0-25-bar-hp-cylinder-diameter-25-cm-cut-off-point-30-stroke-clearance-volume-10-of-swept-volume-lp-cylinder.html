Guest Question.

double acting compound steam engine has steam supplied at 7 bar and exhausted at 0.25 bar.
HP cylinder: diameter = 25 cm
cut-off point = 30% stroke
clearance volume = 10% of swept volume
LP cylinder: diameter = 50 cm
cut-off point = 45% stroke
clearance volume = 5% of swept volume
Diagram factors of HP and LP cylinder are 0.8 and 0.7 respectively. Considering hyperbolic expansion and neglecting cushioning effect estimate mep in each cylinder and total hp developed for engine running at 100 rpm. Take account of clearance volumes.

Admin
Solution

Let the common stroke of cylinders be ‘L’ m. Assuming the expansion curve to be continuous the P-V diagram is shown here.\(\\\begin{aligned}\text{Volume of HP cylinder}&= \frac{π}{4} × (0.25)^{2} × L = 0.04908 L m^{3}\\\text{Clearance volume}&= 0.1 × 0.04908 L = 0.004908 L m^{3} = V_{9}\\\text{Total volume of HP}&= 0.04908 L + 0.004908 L = 0.053988 L m^{3} = V_{2}\\\text{Volume at cut-off in HP cylinder}&\\&= (0.004908 · L) + (0.3 × 0.04908 · L)\\&= 0.019632 L m^{3} = V_{1}\\\text{For LP cylinder}\qquad \quad\qquad \qquad&\\\text{Volume of LP cylinder}&= \frac{π}{4} × (0.5)^{2} · L\\&= 0.1963 L m^{3}\\\text{Clearance volume of LP cylinder}&= 0.05 × 0.1963 L\\&= 9.815 × 10^{–3} L m^{3} = V_{7}\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Total volume of}&\text{ LP cylinder}\\&= (0.1963 L + 9.815 × 10^{–3} L)\\&= 0.206115 L m^{3} = V_{5}\\\text{Volume at cut-}&\text{off of LP cylinder}\\&= 9.815 × 10^{–3} L + (0.45 × 0.1963 L)\\&= 0.09815 L m^{3} = V_{3}\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Expansion ratio for HP cylinder, r}_{HP}&= \frac{0.053988 L}{0.019632 L} = 2.75\\\text{Expansion ratio for LP cylinder, r}_{LP}&= \frac{0.206115 L}{0.09815 L} = 2.1\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}P_{1} V_{1} &= P_{2} V_{2}= P_{3} V_{3} = P_{4} V_{4}\\⇒\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad P_{1} V_{1}&= P_{3} V_{3}\\\text{or, } (7 × 10^{2} × 0.019632 L)&= (P_{3} × 0.09815 L)\\⇒ \quad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad p_{3}&= 140.01 kPa\end{aligned}\\\text{Actual m.e.p. for HP cylinder}\\\begin{aligned}&= 0.8 \frac{\left[P_{1} V_{1} (1 + \ln r_{HP}) – (P_{3} · V_{2}) – (P_{1} – P_{3}) × V_{9}\right]}{\text{Stroke volume of HP}}\\&= 0.8 \frac{\left[7 × 10^{2} × 0.019632 (1 + \ln 2.75) – (140.01 × 0.053988) – \left(\left(7 × 10^{2} – 140.01\right) × 0.004908 L\right) \right] }{0.04908 · L}\\&= 282.59 kPa\end{aligned}\\\text{Actual mep of LP cylinder}\\\begin{aligned}&= 0.7 × \frac{\left[P_{3} V_{3} (1 + \ln r_{LP}) – (P_{3} – P_{5}) × V_{7}\right] }{\text{Stroke volume of LP}}\\&= 0.7 \frac{\left[(140.01 × 0.09815(1 + \ln 2.1)) – (25 × 0.206115 L) – (140.01 – 25)× 9.815 × 10^{-3} L \right] }{0.1963 L}\\&= 62.96 kPa\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Actual mep of HP referred to LP cylinder}&= \frac{282.59 × 0.04908 L}{0.1963 L}\\&= 70.65 kPa\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Total mep}&= 62.96 + 70.65 = 133.61 kPa\\\text{Total output}&= \text{Total mep × L × A × N}\\&= \frac{133.61 × L × \frac{π}{4} × (0.5)^{2} × 100}{60} = 43.72 L kW\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{mep of HP referred to LP}&= 70.65 kPa\\\text{mep of LP}&= 62.96 kPa\\\text{Total output}&= \text{43.72 L kW where L is stroke length}\end{aligned}\)
