Guest Question.

Economic analysis

We wish to produce R isothermally from the reaction

\(A \rightarrow R , r_{1}=k_{1} C _{ A } \) (E3.8.1)

Unfortunately, A also decomposes into Q at the condition where R is formed:

\(A \rightarrow Q , r_{2}=k_{2} C _{ A }\) (E3.8.2)

A costs $0.05 /lb, R sells for $0.10 /lb, and Q costs $0.03 /lb to dispose. Assume that cost to the reactor and operation is negligible.

1. What relation between the k‘s will give a positive cash flow?

2. What relation between the k‘s will give a 30% profit on sales?

Admin
Solution

This is a system of first-order parallel reactions. The instantaneous selectivity of R is given by, Eq. (3.103) \(s_{D / A}=\frac{ d (\# \text { moles of A converted to } D )}{ d (\# \text { moles of } A \text { reacted })}=\frac{-\left.r_{A}\right|_{\text {formang } D }}{-r_{A}}\) ,

 

\(s_{R / A}=\frac{d(\# \text { moles of A converted to } R)}{d(\text { \# moles of A reacted })}=\frac{-\left.r_{A}\right|_{\text {forming } R}}{-r_{A}}=\frac{r_{1}}{r_{1}+r_{2}}=\frac{k_{1}}{k_{1}+k_{2}}\) (E3.8.3)

which is constant. Therefore, one can conclude that the conversion of A into R is always a fraction of the total A reacted. This can simplify our computations (before we even know how to perform reactor analysis). The fraction of A converted to R is \(s_{ R / A }\) and the fraction of A converted to Q is 1 – \(s_{ R / A }\) . Therefore, for every pound of A reacted,

Profit per lb of A reacted = \(\$ 0.10 \times \frac{k_{1}}{k_{1}+k_{2}}-\$ 0.03 \times \left\lgroup1-\frac{k_{1}}{k_{1}+k_{2}}\right\rgroup -\$ 0.05\) (E3.8.4)

1. For the process to give a positive cash flow, it is equivalent to require:

\(\$ 0.10 \times \frac{k_{1}}{k_{1}+k_{2}}-\$ 0.03 \times \left\lgroup 1-\frac{k_{1}}{k_{1}+k_{2}} \right\rgroup -\$ 0.05>0\) (E3.8.5)

That is to say

\(\frac{10 k_{1}}{k_{1}+k_{2}}-3 \times\left\lgroup 1-\frac{k_{1}}{k_{1}+k_{2}}\right\rgroup >5\) (E3.8.6)

or

\(\frac{13 k_{1}}{k_{1}+k_{2}}>8\) (E3.8.7)

which can be reduced to

\(\frac{k_{2}}{k_{1}}<\frac{5}{8}=0.625\) (E3.8.8)

2. To make 30% profit on sales is equivalent to say that the profit is 30% of the value of R produced. Thus,

\(\$ 0.10 \times \frac{k_{1}}{k_{1}+k_{2}}-\$ 0.03 \times\left\lgroup 1-\frac{k_{1}}{k_{1}+k_{2}}\right\rgroup -\$ 0.05=30 \% \times \$ 0.10 \times \frac{k_{1}}{k_{1}+k_{2}}\) (E3.8.9)

which can be reduced to

\(\frac{10 k_{1}}{k_{1}+k_{2}}=8\) (E3.8.10)

or

\(\frac{k_{2}}{k_{1}}=\frac{2}{8}=0.25\) (E3.8.11)

This concludes the example.
