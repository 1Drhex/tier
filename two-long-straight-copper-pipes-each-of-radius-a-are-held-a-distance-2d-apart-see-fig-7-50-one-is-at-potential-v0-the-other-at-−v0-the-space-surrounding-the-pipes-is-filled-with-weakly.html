Guest Question.

Two long, straight copper pipes, each of radius a, are held a distance 2d apart (see Fig. 7.50). One is at potential \(V_{0}\), the other at \(-V_{0}\). The space surrounding the pipes is filled with weakly conducting material of conductivity σ . Find the current per unit length that flows from one pipe to the other. [Hint: Refer to .]

Admin
Solution

Begin with a different problem: two parallel wires carrying charges \( +\lambda \text { and }-\lambda\) as shown.

Field of one wire: \(E =\frac{\lambda}{2 \pi \epsilon_{0} s} \hat{ s } ; \text { potential: } V=-\frac{\lambda}{2 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \ln (s / a)\) .

Potential of combination: \(V=\frac{\lambda}{2 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \ln \left(s_{-} / s_{+}\right)\) , or \(V(y, z)=\frac{\lambda}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \ln \left\{\frac{(y+b)^{2}+z^{2}}{(y-b)^{2}+z^{2}}\right\} \) .

Find the locus of points of fixed V (i.e. equipotential surfaces):

\(e^{4 \pi \epsilon_{0} V / \lambda} \equiv \mu=\frac{(y+b)^{2}+z^{2}}{(y-b)^{2}+z^{2}} \Longrightarrow \mu\left(y^{2}-2 y b+b^{2}+z^{2}\right)=y^{2}+2 y b+b^{2}+z^{2}\) ;

\(y^{2}(\mu-1)+b^{2}(\mu-1)+z^{2}(\mu-1)-2 y b(\mu+1)=0 \Longrightarrow y^{2}+z^{2}+b^{2}-2 y b \beta=0 \quad\left(\beta \equiv \frac{\mu+1}{\mu-1}\right)\) ;

\((y-b \beta)^{2}+z^{2}+b^{2}-b^{2} \beta^{2}=0 \Longrightarrow\left(y-b \beta^{2}\right)+z^{2}=b^{2}\left(\beta^{2}-1\right)\) .

This is a circle, with center at \(y_{0}=b \beta=b\left(\frac{\mu+1}{\mu-1}\right) \text { and radius }=b \sqrt{\beta^{2}-1}=b \sqrt{\frac{\left(\mu^{2}+2 \mu+1\right)-\left(\mu^{2}-2 \mu+1\right)}{(\mu-1)^{2}}}=\frac{2 b \sqrt{\mu}}{\mu-1}\) .

This suggests an image solution to the problem at hand. We want \( y_{0}=d, \text { radius }=a, \text { and } V=V_{0}\) .

These determine the parameters b, µ, and λ of the image solution:

\(\frac{d}{a}=\frac{y_{0}}{\text { radius }}=\frac{b\left(\frac{\mu+1}{\mu-1}\right)}{\frac{2 b \sqrt{\mu}}{\mu-1}}=\frac{\mu+1}{2 \sqrt{\mu}} . \quad \text { Call } \frac{d}{a} \equiv \alpha\) .

\(4 \alpha^{2} \mu=(\mu+1)^{2}=\mu^{2}+2 \mu+1 \Longrightarrow \mu^{2}+\left(2-4 \alpha^{2}\right) \mu+1=0\) ;

\(\mu=\frac{4 \alpha^{2}-2 \pm \sqrt{4\left(1-2 \alpha^{2}\right)^{2}-4}}{2}=2 \alpha^{2}-1 \pm \sqrt{1-4 \alpha^{2}+4 \alpha^{4}-1}=2 \alpha^{2}-1 \pm 2 \alpha \sqrt{\alpha^{2}-1}\) .

\(\frac{4 \pi \epsilon_{0} V_{0}}{\lambda}=\ln \mu \Longrightarrow \lambda=\frac{4 \pi \epsilon_{0} V_{0}}{\ln \left(2 \alpha^{2}-1 \pm 2 \alpha \sqrt{\alpha^{2}-1}\right)}\) . That’s the line charge in the image problem.

\(I=\int J \cdot d a =\sigma \int E \cdot d a =\sigma \frac{1}{\epsilon_{0}} Q_{ enc }=\frac{\sigma}{\epsilon_{0}} \lambda l\) .

The current per unit length is \(i=\frac{I}{l}=\frac{\sigma \lambda}{\epsilon_{0}}=\frac{4 \pi \sigma V_{0}}{\ln \left(2 \alpha^{2}-1 \pm 2 \alpha \sqrt{\alpha^{2}-1}\right)}\) .

Which sign do we want? Suppose the cylinders are far apart, \(d \gg a \text {, so that } \alpha \gg 1\).

\(( )=2 \alpha^{2}-1 \pm 2 \alpha^{2} \sqrt{1-1 / \alpha^{2}}=2 \alpha^{2}-1 \pm 2 \alpha^{2}\left[1-\frac{1}{2 \alpha^{2}}-\frac{1}{8 \alpha^{4}}+\cdots\right]\) .

\(=2 \alpha^{2}(1 \pm 1)-(1 \pm 1) \mp \frac{1}{4 \alpha^{2}} \pm \cdots= \begin{cases}4 \alpha^{2}-2-1 / 2 \alpha^{2}+\cdots \approx 4 \alpha^{2} & (+\operatorname{sign}), \\ -1 / 4 \alpha^{2} & (-\operatorname{sign}) .\end{cases}\)

The current must surely decrease with increasing α, so evidently the + sign is correct:

\(i=\frac{4 \pi \sigma V_{0}}{\ln \left(2 \alpha^{2}-1+2 \alpha \sqrt{\alpha^{2}-1}\right)}, \text { where } \alpha=\frac{d}{a}\) .
