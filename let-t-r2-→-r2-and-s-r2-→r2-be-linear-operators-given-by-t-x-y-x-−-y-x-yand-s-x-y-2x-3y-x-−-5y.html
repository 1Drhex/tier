Guest Question.

Let \(T: \mathbb{R}^{2} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{2}\) and \(S: \mathbb{R}^{2} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{2}\) be linear operators given by

\( T\left(\left[\begin{array}{l} x \\ y \end{array}\right]\right)=\left(\begin{array}{c} x-y \\ x+y \end{array}\right) \text { and } S\left(\left[\begin{array}{l} x \\ y \end{array}\right]\right)=\left(\begin{array}{c} 2 x+3 y \\ x-5 y \end{array}\right) \)

Determine \((S \circ T)\left(\left[\begin{array}{l}2 \\ 3\end{array}\right]\right)\) and \((T \circ S)\left(\left[\begin{array}{l}2 \\ 3\end{array}\right]\right)\).

Admin
Solution

\((S \circ T)\left(\left[\begin{array}{l}2 \\ 3\end{array}\right]\right)\) means apply the transform \(T\) to the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{l}2 \\ 3\end{array}\right]\) and then apply transform \(S\) to this result. By using the above Definition (5.32) we have

\( \begin{aligned} (S \circ T)\left(\left[\begin{array}{l} 2 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\right)=S\left(T\left(\left[\begin{array}{l} 2 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\right)\right) \\ =S\left(\left[\begin{array}{l} 2-3 \\ 2+3 \end{array}\right]\right) \quad\left[\text { applying } T\left(\left[\begin{array}{l} x \\ y \end{array}\right]\right)=\left(\begin{array}{c} x-y \\ x+y \end{array}\right)\right] \\ =S\left(\left[\begin{array}{r} -1 \\ 5 \end{array}\right]\right)=\left(\begin{array}{c} 2(-1)+3(5) \\ -1-5(5) \end{array}\right) \quad\left[\text { applying } S\left(\left[\begin{array}{l} x \\ y \end{array}\right]\right)=\left(\begin{array}{c} 2 x+3 y \\ x-5 y \end{array}\right)\right] \\ =\left(\begin{array}{r} 13 \\ -26 \end{array}\right) \end{aligned} \)

Similarly, carrying out the transformations the other way, first \(S\) then \(T\), we have [verify this]:

\((T \circ S)\left(\left[\begin{array}{l}2 \\3\end{array}\right]\right)=T\left(S\left(\left[\begin{array}{l}2 \\3\end{array}\right]\right)\right)=\left(\begin{array}{c}26 \\0\end{array}\right)\)

These vectors are illustrated in Fig. 5.35.

\(\text { Note that }(S \circ T)\left(\left[\begin{array}{l}2 \\3\end{array}\right]\right)=\left(\begin{array}{r}13 \\-26\end{array}\right) \text { does not equal }(T \circ S)\left(\left[\begin{array}{l}2 \\3\end{array}\right]\right)=\left(\begin{array}{c}26 \\0\end{array}\right) \text {. }\)

Definition (5.32). Let \(T: U \rightarrow V\) and \(S: V \rightarrow W\) be linear transforms and \(\mathbf{u}\) and \(T(\mathbf{u})\) be in the domain (start vector spaces) of \(U\) and \(V\) respectively. Then the composition of these two transforms, \(S\) and \(T\), denoted by \(S \circ T\) is defined by \(S \circ T: U \rightarrow W\) (from \(U\) to \(W\) ) and

\((S \circ T)(\mathbf{u})=S(T(\mathbf{u}))\)
