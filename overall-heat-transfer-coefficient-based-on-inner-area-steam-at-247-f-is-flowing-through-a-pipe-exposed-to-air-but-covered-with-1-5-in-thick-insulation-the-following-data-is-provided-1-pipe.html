Guest Question.

Overall Heat Transfer Coefficient based on Inner Area. Steam at 247 °F is flowing through a pipe exposed to air but covered with 1.5 in. thick insulation. The following data is provided:

1. Pipe diameter, inside = 0.825 in.

2. Pipe diameter, outside = 1.05 in.

3. Surrounding air temperature = 60 °F

4. Thermal conductivity, pipe = 26 Btu/hr∙\(ft^2\)∙ °F

5. Thermal conductivity, insulation = 0.037 Btu/hr∙\(ft^2\)∙ °F

6. Steam film coefficient = 800 Btu/hr∙\(ft^2\)∙ °F

7. Air film coefficient = 2.5 Btu/hr∙\(ft^2\)∙ °F

Calculate the overall heat transfer coefficient based on the inside area of the pipe.

Admin
Solution

Obviously, cylindrical coordinates are to be employed here. Assume a basis 1.0ft of pipe length. The inside (i) and outside (o) areas of the pipe (P) plus the insulation (I) areas are

\(A_{i}=0.2157 ft ^{2}\)

 

\(A_{o}=0.2750 ft ^{2}\)

 

\(A_{I}=1.060 ft ^{2}\)

The log mean area for the steel pipe (P) and insulation (I) are, therefore,

\(A_{p, I M}=\frac{A_{o}-A_{i}}{\ln \left(A_{o} / A_{i}\right)}=\frac{0.2750-0.2157}{\ln (0.2750 / 0.2157)}=0.245 ft ^{2}\)

 

\(A_{I, L M}=\frac{A_{I}-A_{o}}{\ln \left(A_{I} / A_{o}\right)}=\frac{1.060-0.2750}{\ln (1.060 / 0.2750)}=0.582 ft ^{2}\)

A slightly modified form of Equation (5.51) and (5.56) is employed to calculate the overall heat coefficient based on the inside area:

\(\frac{Q}{\Delta T}=\frac{1}{\frac{1}{h_{i} A_{i}}+\frac{\Delta D}{2 k A_{ LM }}+\frac{1}{h_{o} A_{o}}} ; \quad \Delta D=D_{o}-D_{i}=2\left(r_{o}-r_{i}\right)\) (5.51)

\(U_{i}=\frac{1}{\frac{1}{h_{i}}+\frac{A_{i} \ln \left(D_{o} / D_{i}\right)}{2 \pi Z k}+\frac{A_{i}}{h_{o} A_{o}}}\) (5.56)

\(U_{i}=\frac{1}{A_{i} \Sigma R}=\frac{1}{A_{i}\left(R_{i}+R_{p}+R_{o}+R_{I}\right)}\) \(=\frac{1}{A_{i}\left(\frac{1}{h_{i} A_{i}}+\frac{r_{o}-r_{i}}{k_{p} A_{p, LM }}+\frac{1}{h_{o} A_{I}}+\frac{r_{1}-r_{o}}{k_{I} A_{I, LM }}\right)}\) \(\begin{array}{r}=\frac{1}{0.2157\left(\frac{1}{(800)(0.2157)}+\frac{(0.525-0.412)}{(26)(0.245)}+\frac{1}{(2.5)(1.060)}\right.} \\\left.+\frac{(2.025-0.525) / 12}{(0.037)(0.582)}\right)\end{array}\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{0.2157(0.005795+0.001478+0.37736+5.80477)}\)

 

\(=0.749 Btu / hr \cdot ft ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} F\)

Note that the resistance from the insulation is much higher than the resistances of other materials, as one can see in the denominator of the resistance values: \(0.005795,0.001475, \text { and } 0.37736 \lll 5.80477 .\)
