Guest Question.

Compute the z-transform and the region of convergence for the function:

\(x[n]=(\frac{1}{3} )^{n}u[n]+2^{n}u[-n-1].\)

Admin
Solution

The given function can be considered the sum of \(x_{1}[n]\) and \(x_{2}[n]\), where \(x_{1}[n]=(\frac{1}{3} )^{n}u[n] \) and \(x_{2}[n]=2^{n}u[-n-1].\)

The transform of \(x_{1}[n]\) directly results from the transform (5) of Table 3.1, which is \(x_{1}[n]=(\frac{1}{3} )^{n}u[n]\leftrightarrow \frac{z}{z-\frac{1}{3} } =X_{1}(z) \) with a region of convergence \(|z|>\frac{1}{3}.\)

Region of convergence \(\frac{1}{3}<|z|<2\) \(x[n]=(\frac{1}{3} )^{n}u[n]+2^{n}u[-n-1]\)

Based on transform (6), we have \(-2^{n}u[-n-1]\leftrightarrow -\frac{z}{z-2}.\)

From the property of linearity, if y[n] ↔ Y(z) then ay[n] ↔ aY(z), hence \(x_{2}[n]=2^{n}u[-n-1]\leftrightarrow \frac{z}{z-2}\) with a region of convergence |z|< 2.

Consequently, \(X(z)=\frac{z}{z-\frac{1}{3} } +\frac{z}{z-2}\)

\(=\frac{z^{2}-2z-z^{2}+\frac{1}{3}z }{(z-\frac{1}{3} )(z-2)}=\frac{-\frac{5}{3}z }{(z-\frac{1}{3} )(z-2)}=-5\frac{z}{3z^{2}-7z+2}.\)

The poles of X(z) are \(z_{1} = 2\) and \(z_{2}=\frac{1}{3} \). Since x[n] is bilateral, the region of convergence will be the ring between the two poles, i.e., it will be defined by the relation\(\frac{1}{3}<|z|<2,\) which is the intersection of the regions of convergence of \(X_{1}(z)\) and \(X_{2}(z).\)
