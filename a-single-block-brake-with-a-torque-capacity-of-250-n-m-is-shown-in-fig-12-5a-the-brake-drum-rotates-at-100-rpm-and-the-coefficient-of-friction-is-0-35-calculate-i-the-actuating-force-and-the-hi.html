Guest Question.

A single block brake with a torque capacity of 250 N-m is shown in Fig. 12.5(a). The brake drum rotates at 100 rpm and the coefficient of friction is 0.35. Calculate
(i) the actuating force and the hinge-pin reaction for clockwise rotation of the drum;
(ii) the actuating force and hinge-pin reaction for anticlockwise rotation of the drum;
(iii) the rate of heat generated during the braking action; and

(iv) the dimensions of the block, if the intensity of pressure between the block and brake drum is 1 N/mm². The length of the block is twice its width.
State whether the brake is self-locking.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } M_{t}=250 N – m \quad n=100 rpm \quad \mu=0.35 \).

\( p=1 N / mm ^{2} \quad l=2 w \).

Step I Actuating force and hinge-pin reaction for clockwise rotation
From Eq. (12.6),

\( M_{t}=\mu N R \) (12.6).

\( N=\frac{M_{t}}{\mu R}=\frac{\left(250 \times 10^{3}\right)}{0.35(200)}=3571.43 N \).

The free-body diagram of forces for clockwise rotation of the drum is shown in Fig. 12.5(b).
Taking moments about the hinge-pin,

\( \mu N(50)+P(500)-N(200)=0 \).

or 0.35(3571.43)(50) + P(500) – 3571.43(200) = 0.

∴ P = 1303.57 N.

\( R_{x}=\mu N=0.35(3571.43)=1250 N \).

\( R_{y}=N-P=3571.43-1303.57=2267.86 N \).

\( R=\sqrt{(1250)^{2}+(2267.86)^{2}}=2589.53 N \) (i).

Step III Rate of heat generated during braking action Initial velocity of the drum

\( =\omega r=\frac{2 \pi(100)}{60}(0.2)=2.094 m / s \).

Final velocity of the drum = 0.

\( \text { Average velocity of the drum }=\frac{2.094+0}{2} \).

= 1.047 m/s.

The rate of heat generated during the braking period is equal to the rate of work done by the frictional force.
Rate of heat generated = frictional force × average velocity.

\( =\mu N(1.047) \).

= 0.35 (3571.43) (1.047)
= 1308.75 N-m/s or W (iii)
Step IV Dimensions of the block
From Eq. (12.7),

N = plw (12.7).

N = plw
3571.43 = (1)(2w)(w)
∴ w = 42.26 mm or 45 mm.

l = 2w = 90 mm (iv)
Step V Self-locking property
Referring to Fig. 12.5 (a),
a = 200 mm c = 50 mm μ = 0.35.

\( \left(\frac{a}{c}\right)=4 \quad \therefore\left(\frac{a}{c}\right)>\mu \).

The brake is not self-locking.
