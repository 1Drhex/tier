Guest Question.

To design a heating system for the purpose of preventing ice formation on an aircraft wing, it is necessary to know the heat transfer coefficient over the outer surface of the leading edge. The leading-edge contour can be approximated by a half-cylinder of 30-cm diameter, as shown in Fig. 7.10. The ambient air is at -34°C, and the surface temperature is to be no less than 0°C. The plane is designed to fly at 7500-m altitude at a speed of 150 m/s. Calculate the distribution of the convection heat transfer coefficient over the forward portion of the wing.

Admin

The heat transfer coefficient at the stagnation point (θ = 0) is, according to Eq. (7.2),

\(Nu(\theta )= \frac{h_{c}(\theta )D }{k}=1.14\left(\frac{\rho U_\infty D}{\mu } \right)^{0.5} Pr^{0.4 }\left[1 – \left(\frac{\theta }{90} \right)^3 \right]\) (7.2)

 

\(h_c(\theta = 0) = 1.14\left(\frac{\rho U_\infty D}{\mu } \right)^{0.5}Pr^{0.4}\frac{k}{D}\)

 

\( = (1.14)\left(\frac{(0.566 kg/m^3) \times (150 m/s) \times (0.30 m)}{1.74 \times 10^{-5} kg/m s} \right)^{0.5}(0.72)^{0.4}\left(\frac{0.024 W/m K}{0.30 m} \right)\)

 

\( = 96.7 W/m^2 °C\)

The variation of \(h_c\) with θ is obtained by multipling the value of the heat transfer coefficient at the stagnation point by \(1 – (θ/90)^3\) . The results are tabulated below.
