Guest Question.

Consider a horizontal circular plate of diameter D = 0.2m losing heat by natural convection to surrounding ambient air at \(T_{\infty}=20^{\circ} C\). The plate is maintained at a temperature of \(T_{w}=20^{\circ} C\). Compare the heat loss from this plate with a square plate of the same area, all other data remaining the same.

Admin
Solution

Case (a): Circular plate

We use the thermo-physical properties of air at the mean temperature of \(T_{m}=\frac{T_{w}+T_{\infty}}{2}=\frac{60+20}{2}=40^{\circ} C\)

Kinematic viscosity: \(v_{m}=17.07 \times 10^{-6} m ^{2} / s\)

Thermal conductivity: \(k_{m}=0.0274 W / m ^{\circ} C\)

Prandtl number: \(P r_{m}=0.699\)

Isobaric expansion coefficient: \(\beta_{m}=\frac{1}{T_{m}}=\frac{1}{273+40}=0.0032 K ^{-1}\)

The Rayleigh number may now be calculated with \(L_{c h}=D=0.2 m\).

\(R a_{D}=\frac{g \beta_{m}\left(T_{w}-T_{\infty}\right) D^{3}}{v_{m}^{2}} P r_{m}\) \(=\frac{9.81 \times 0.0032(60-20) 0.2^{3}}{\left(17.07 \times 10^{-6}\right)^{2}} 0.699=2.41 \times 10^{7}\)

Using Eq. 16.55, we than have

\(\overline{N u}_{L_{c h}}=0.27 R a_{L_{c h}}^{\frac{1}{4}}\) (16.55)

\(\overline{N u}_{D}=0.27 R a_{D}^{\frac{1}{4}}=0.27 \times\left(2.41 \times 10^{7}\right)^{\frac{1}{4}}=18.92\)

Correspondingly the mean heat transfer coefficient is

\(\bar{h}=\frac{\overline{N u}_{D} k_{m}}{D}=\frac{18.92 \times 0.0274}{0.2}=2.59 W / m ^{2 \circ} C\)

Heat loss from the plate to the ambient air is

\(Q=\bar{h} \frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}\left(T_{w}-T_{\infty}\right)=2.59 \times \frac{\pi \times 0.2^{2}}{4}(60-20)=3.26 W\)

Case (b): Square plate of equal area

The only change in this case is that the characteristic dimension changes from D to\(\frac{W+L}{2}=a\) where a is the side of the square plate. Since the square plate has the same area as that of the circular plate, we have

\(a=\sqrt{\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}}=\sqrt{\frac{\pi \times 0.2^{2}}{4}}=0.177 m\)

The Rayleigh number in this case is

\(R a_{a}=\frac{g \beta_{m}\left(T_{w}-T_{\infty}\right) a^{3}}{v^{2}} P r_{m}\) \(=\frac{9.81 \times 0.0032(60-20) 0.177^{3}}{\left(17.07 \times 10^{-6}\right)^{2}} 0.699=1.67 \times10^{7}\)

Using Eq. 16.55, we than have

\(\overline{N u}_{L_{c h}}=0.27 R a_{L_{c h}}^{\frac{1}{4}}\) (16.55)

\(\overline{N u}_{a}=0.27 R a_{a}^{\frac{1}{4}}=0.27 \times\left(1.67 \times 10^{7}\right)^{\frac{1}{4}}=17.26\)

Correspondingly the mean heat transfer coefficient is

\(\bar{h}=\frac{\overline{N u}_{a} k_{m}}{a}=\frac{17.26 \times 0.0274}{0.177}=2.672 W / m ^{2}{ }^{\circ} C\)

Heat loss from the plate to the ambient air is

\(Q=\bar{h} a^{2}\left(T_{w}-T_{\infty}\right)=2.672 \times 0.177^{2}(60-20)=3.35 W\)

The square plate loses marginally more heat than the circular plate.
