Guest Question.

In the text I asserted that the first-order corrections to an n-fold degenerate energy are the eigenvalues of the W matrix, and I justified this claim as the “natural” generalization of the case n = 2. Prove it, by reproducing the steps in Section 7.2.1, starting with

\( \psi^{0}=\sum_{j=1}^{n} \alpha_{j} \psi_{j}^{0} \).

(generalizing Equation 7.17), and ending by showing that the analog to Equation 7.27 can be interpreted as the eigenvalue equation for the matrix .

\( \psi^{0}=\alpha \psi_{a}^{0}+\beta \psi_{b}^{0} \) (7.17).

\( \alpha W_{a a}+\beta W_{a b}=\alpha E^{1} \) (7.27).

Admin
Solution

Given a set of orthonormal states \( \left\{\psi_{j}^{0}\right\} \) that are degenerate eigenfunctions of the unperturbed Hamiltonian:

\( H \psi_{j}^{0}=E^{0} \psi_{j}^{0}, \quad\left\langle\psi_{j}^{0} \mid \psi_{l}^{0}\right\rangle=\delta_{j l} \).

construct the general linear combination,

\( \psi^{0}=\sum_{j=1}^{n} \alpha_{j} \psi_{j}^{0} \).

It too is an eigenfunction of the unperturbed Hamiltonian, with the same eigenvalue:

\( H^{0} \psi^{0}=\sum_{j=1}^{n} \alpha_{j} H^{0} \psi_{j}^{0}=E^{0} \sum_{j=1}^{n} \alpha_{j} \psi_{j}^{0}=E^{0} \psi^{0} \).

We want to solve the Schrödinger equation \( H \psi=E \psi \) for the perturbed Hamiltonian \( H=H^{0}+\lambda H^{\prime} \).

Expand the eigenvalues and eigenfunctions as power series in λ:

\( E=E^{0}+\lambda E^{1}+\lambda^{2} E^{2}+\ldots, \quad \psi=\psi^{0}+\lambda \psi^{1}+\lambda^{2} \psi^{2}+\ldots \).

Plug these into the Schrödinger equation and collect like powers:

\( \left(H^{0}+\lambda H^{\prime}\right)\left(\psi^{0}+\lambda \psi^{1}+\lambda^{2} \psi^{2}+\ldots\right)=\left(E^{0}+\lambda E^{1}+\lambda^{2} E^{2}+\ldots\right)\left(\psi^{0}+\lambda \psi^{1}+\lambda^{2} \psi^{2}+\ldots\right) \Rightarrow \)

\( H^{0} \psi^{0}+\lambda\left(H^{0} \psi^{1}+H^{\prime} \psi^{0}\right)+\ldots=E^{0} \psi^{0}+\lambda\left(E^{0} \psi^{1}+E^{1} \psi^{0}\right)+\ldots \).

The zeroth-order terms cancel; to first order

\( H^{0} \psi^{1}+H^{\prime} \psi^{0}=E^{0} \psi^{1}+E^{1} \psi^{0} \).

Take the inner product with \( \psi_{j}^{0} \):

\( \left\langle\psi_{j}^{0} \mid H^{0} \psi^{1}\right\rangle+\left\langle\psi_{j}^{0} \mid H^{\prime} \psi^{0}\right\rangle=E^{0}\left\langle\psi_{j}^{0} \mid \psi^{1}\right\rangle+E^{1}\left\langle\psi_{j}^{0} \mid \psi^{0}\right\rangle \).

But \( \left\langle\psi_{j}^{0} \mid H^{0} \psi^{1}\right\rangle=\left\langle H^{0} \psi_{j}^{0} \mid \psi^{1}\right\rangle=E^{0}\left\langle\psi_{j}^{0} \mid \psi^{1}\right\rangle \), so the first terms cancel, leaving

\( \left\langle\psi_{j}^{0} \mid H^{\prime} \psi^{0}\right\rangle=E^{1}\left\langle\psi_{j}^{0} \mid \psi^{0}\right\rangle \).

Now use \( \psi^{0}=\sum_{l=1}^{n} \alpha_{l} \psi_{l}^{0} \), and exploit the orthonormality of \( \left\{\psi_{l}^{0}\right\} \):

\( \sum_{l=1}^{n} \alpha_{l}\left\langle\psi_{j}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{l}^{0}\right\rangle=E^{1} \sum_{l=1}^{n} \alpha_{l}\left\langle\psi_{j}^{0} \mid \psi_{l}^{0}\right\rangle=E^{1} \alpha_{j} \).

or, defining

\( W_{j l} \equiv\left\langle\psi_{j}^{0}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{l}^{0}\right\rangle \), \( \sum_{l=1}^{n} W_{j l} \alpha_{l}=E^{1} \alpha_{l} \).

This (the generalization of Eq. 7.27 for the case of n-fold degeneracy) is the eigenvalue equation for the matrix W (whose \( \left.j l^{\text {th }} \text { element, in the }\left\{\psi_{j}^{0}\right\} \text { basis, is } W_{j l}\right) ; E^{1} \) is the eigenvalue, and the eigenvector (in the \( \left\{\psi_{j}^{0}\right\} \) basis) is \( \chi_{j}=\alpha_{j} \). Conclusion: The first-order corrections to the energy are the eigenvalues of W. QED

\( \alpha W_{a a}+\beta W_{a b}=\alpha E^{1} \) (7.27).
