Guest Question.

A home in Los Angeles has a roof area of 100 \(m^{2}\), and the total cost of installing a photovoltaic system is $8000/kW. The cost of energy is $0.15/kWh. Estimate:

a. The total power produced.

b. The payback period.

c. The payback period if the utility buys the energy produced at $0.60/kWh.

d. Repeat (c) above when the installation is equipped with a tracking system whose cost is $6000 and provides an increase in output energy of 50%.

Admin
Solution

a. Since 1 kW of PV power requires \(10 m^{2}\) of roof space, then the total power produced is

\( P = \frac{1(100)}{10} = \underline{\underline{10kW} } \)

b. The installation cost is 10(8000) = $80,000.
The potential output energy from Table 1 is
10(1485) = 14,850 kWh/year

and the corresponding savings \((C_{1})\) are

\(C_{1}\) = 14,850 (0.15) = $2227.50/year
The payback period (\(T_{1}\)) is

\( T_{1}= \frac{80,000}{2227.5}= \underline{\underline{35.9 years} } \)

c. The savings \((C_{2})\) at $0.60/kWh are
14,850(0.60) = $8910/year
and the payback period \((Y_{2})\) is

\( Y_{2} = \frac{80,000}{8910} = \underline{\underline{8.98 years} } \)

d. When a tracking system is installed, the output energy is
1.5(1485)(10) = 22,275.00 kWh/year
The corresponding savings \((C_{2})\) are
\(C_{2}\) = 22,275.00(0.60) = $13,365.00/year
and the payback period \((T_{2})\) is

\( T_{2}= \frac{86,000}{13,365.0}= \underline{\underline{6.44 years} } \)
