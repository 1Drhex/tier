Guest Question.

What is complex power? Explain in detail. [JNTU May/June 2006]

Admin
Solution

Single-Phase ac Circuits 2.93

Complex power

Active power (P):

The active power or real power in an a.c. circuit is given by the product of voltage, current and cosine of the phase angle. It is always positive

\(P=VI\cos \Theta\) Watts

Reactive power (Q):

The reactive power in an a.c. circuit is given by the product of voltage, current and sine of the phase angle \(\Theta .\)

If \(\Theta\) is leading then reactive power is taken as 1ve and it is capacitive.

If \(\Theta\) is lagging then reactive power is taken as 2ve and it is inductive

\(\mathbb{Q}=VI\sin \Theta VARs\)

Apparent power:
The apparent power in an a.c. circuit is the product of voltage and current. It is measured in voltamps.

\(S=VI\) Volt amps

The component \(I\cos \Theta = Active\) component or real component or in phase component of a current.

The product of voltage and the above component (active component) gives active power. The component \(I\sin \Theta =\) Reactive component or quadrature component of current.

The produce of this component with voltage V gives the reactive power.

Power factor \(cos \phi =\frac{Real power}{Apparent power}\)

The factor sin u is called the reactive factor.
Complex power=(Active power) + j (Reactive power)
