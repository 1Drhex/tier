Guest Question.

A second-order irreversible reaction A→2R is carried out in gas phase in a PFR reactor. A reactant (MW=40) with 50% by weight and the rest with an inert (MWinert =20) are introduced into the PFR. It is desired to produce 20 kmol/h of a product R, knowing that the final conversion was 35%. Calculate the volume of the reactor.
Data: rate constant=500m3/(kmol ks); pressure=4.7 atm; temperature=60.C (constant).

Admin
Solution

The reaction has second-order kinetics,
\(-r_{A}=kC^{2}_{A}=kC^{2}_{A0}\frac{(1-X_{A})^{2}}{(1+\varepsilon _{A}X_{A})^{2}}\) (20.18)

Substituting Equation 20.18 into PFR equation gives:
\(\tau =\frac{V}{\nu _{0}}=C_{A0}\int_{0}^{X_{A}}\frac{dX_{A}}{r}\) (20.19)
\( \tau =\frac{1}{kC_{A0}}[(1+\varepsilon _{A})^{2}\frac{X_{A}}{(1-X_{A})}+\varepsilon _{A}^{2}X_{A}+2\varepsilon _{A}(1+\varepsilon _{A})\ln (1-X_{A})]\)

Calculation basis: 1.0 g
\(n_{A}=\frac{m_{A}}{M_{A}}=\frac{0.5}{40}=0.0125 mol\)
\(n_{1}=\frac{m_{1}}{M_{1}}=\frac{0.5}{20}=0.025 mol\)

Calculation of \(\varepsilon _{A}\):

A 2R Inert Total
Initial 0.0125 0 0.025 0.0373
Final 0 0.025 0.025 0.05
\(\varepsilon _{A} =0.33\)

Calculation of initial concentration:
\(C_{A0}=\frac{\gamma _{A0}P}{RT}=\frac{0.33\times 4.7}{0.082\times 333}=5.68\times 10^{-2} mol/L(kmol/m^{3})\)

Substituting these values into Equation 20.19, we get:
\(\tau =0.041 ks=41.15 s=0.01143 h\)

From R output stream, we determine the flow rate:
\(F_{R}=C_{R}\nu _{0}=2C_{A0}\nu _{0}X_{A}\)
\(20=2C_{A0}\nu _{0}X_{A}\)
\(\nu _{0}=503 m^{3}/h\)

Thus, the volume of the reactor is:
\(V =\tau \nu _{0}=0.01143 × 503=5.75m^{3}\)
