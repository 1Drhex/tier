Guest Question.

In a Hartung governor, the length of the ball and sleeve arms are 80 mm and 120 mm, respectively. The total travel of the sleeve is 25 mm. In the mid-position, each spring is compressed by 50 mm and the radius of rotation of the balls is 140 mm. The weight of each ball is 40 N and the spring has a stiffness of 10 N/mm. The equivalent weight of the governor gear at the sleeve is 160 N. Neglecting the moment due to the revolving masses when the arms are inclined, determine the ratio of range of speed to the mean speed of the governor. Also find the speed in the mid position.

Admin
Solution

Refer to Fig.9.20.

\( \text { Here } a=80 mm , b=120 mm , h=25 mm , r=140 mm , w=40 N \).

W=160 N, k=10 N/mm, initial compression=50 mm.

\( F_{c}=(w / g) \omega^{2} r=(40 / 9.81) \omega^{2} \times 0.14=0.57 \omega^{2} N \).

\( \text { Spring force, } S=10 \times 50=500 N \).

Taking moments about the fulcrum A of the lever (Fig.9.20), we have

\( F_{c} \times a=S \times a+W \times b / 2 \).

\( 0.57 \omega^{2} \times 80=500 \times 80+160 \times 120 / 2 \).

\( \omega^{2}=1088.72 \).

\( \omega=32.98 rad / s \).

Mean speed of the governor, N=314.9 rpm
At maximum position:

\( \left(r_{1}-r\right) / h_{1}=a / b \).

or \( r_{1}=r+h_{1} a / b \).

\( =140+(25 \times 80) /(2 \times 120) \).

= 148.3 mm.

\( F_{c 1}=(w / g) \omega_{1}^{2} r_{1} \).

\( =(40 / 9.81) \omega_{1}^{2} \times 0.1483 \).

\( =0.6047 \omega_{1}^{2} N \).

\( \text { Spring force, } S_{1}=\left[\text { initial compression }+\left(r_{1}-r\right)\right] \times \text { stiffness } \).

\( =[50+(148.3-140)] \times 10 \).

= 583 N.

Taking moments about the fulcrum of the lever, we have

\( F_{c 1} a=S_{1} a+W b / 2 \).

\( 0.6047 \omega_{1}^{2} \times 80=583 \times 80+160 \times 120 / 2 \).

\( 48.376 \omega_{1}^{2}=56240 \).

= 1162.56.

\( \omega_{1}=34.1 rad / s \).

\( N_{1}=325.63 rpm \).

At minimum position:

\( \left(r-r_{2}\right) / h_{2}=a / b \).

or \( r_{2}=r-h_{2} a / b \).

\( =140-(25 \times 80) /(2 \times 120) \).

= 131.67 mm.

\( F_{c 2}=(w / g) \omega_{1}^{2} r_{1} \).

\( =(40 / 9.81) \omega_{1}^{2} \times 0.13167 \).

\( =0.537 \omega_{1}^{2} N \).

\( S_{2}=\left[\text { initial compression }-\left(r-r_{2}\right)\right] \times \text { stiffness } \).

\( =[50-(140-131.67)] \times 10 \).

= 416.7 N.

Taking moments about the fulcrum of the lever, we have

\( F_{c 2} a=S_{2} a+W b / 2 \).

\( 0.537 \omega_{2}^{2} \times 80=416.7 \times 80+160 \times 120 / 2 \).

\( 42.96 \omega_{2}^{2}=42936 \).

\( \omega_{2}^{2}=999.44 \).

\( \omega^{2}=31.614 rad / s \).

\( N_{2}=301.9 rpm \).

\( \text { Range of speed }=N_{1}-N_{2}=325.63-301.9=23.73 rpm \).

\( \text { Ratio of range of speed }=\left(N_{1}-N_{2}\right) / N=23.73 / 314.9=0.07536 \text { or } 8.536 \% \).
