Guest Question.

A WT10.5 \(\times \)31 is used as a bracket to transmit a 60-kip service load to a W14 \(\times \)90 column, as previously shown in Figure . The load consists of 15 kips dead load and 45 kips live load. Four 7⁄8-inch-diameter Group A bolts are used. The column is of A992 steel, and the bracket is A36. Assume all spacing and edge-distance requirements are satisfied, including those necessary for the use of the nominal strength in bearing deformation (i.e., \(2.4dtF_u)\)* , and determine the adequacy of the bolts for the following types of connections: (a) bearing-type connection with the threads in shear and (b) slip-critical connection with the threads in shear.

 

Admin
Solution

(The following values are used in both the LRFD and ASD solutions.)
Compute the nominal bearing strength (flange of tee controls):
\(R_n = 2.4dtF_u = 2.4\left ( \frac{7}{8} \right )(0.615)(58) = 74.91 kips\)
Nominal shear strength:
\(A_{b}=\frac{\pi (7/8)^{2}}{4}= 0.6013 in.^{2}\)

\(R_n = F_{nv}A_b = 54(0.6013) = 32.47 kips\)
\(P_u = 1.2D + 1.6L = 1.2(15) + 1.6(45) = 90 kips\)

a. The total shear/bearing load is
\(V_u = \frac{3}{5}(90) = 54 kips\)

The shear/bearing force per bolt is
\(V_{u bolt} = \frac{54}{4} = 13.5 kips\)
The design bearing strength is
\(\phi R_n = 0.75(74.91) =56.2 kips > 13.5 kips (OK)\)
The design shear strength is
\(\phi R_n = 0.75(32.47) = 24.4 kips > 13.5 kips (OK)\)
The total tension load is
\(T_u = \frac{4}{5}(90) = 72 kips\)
The tensile force per bolt is
\(T_{u bolt} = \frac{72}{4} = 18 kips\)
To determine the available tensile strength, use AISC Equation J3-3a:
\(F'_{nt}=1.3F_{nt} – \frac{F_{nt}}{\phi F_{nv} }f_{rv}\leq F_{nt}\)
where
\(F_{nt} = \)nominal tensile stress in the absence of shear = 90 ksi
\(F_{nv} = \)nominal shear stress in the absence of tension = 54 ksi
\(f_{rv} = \frac{V_{u bolt}}{A_b}= \frac{13.5}{0.6013} = 22.45 ksi\)

Then
\(F'_{nt} = 1.3(90) – \frac{90}{0.75(54)}(22.45) = 67.11 ksi < 90 ksi\)
The nominal tensile strength is
\(R_n = F'_{nt} A_b = 67.11(0.6013) = 40.35 kips\)
and the available tensile strength is
\(\phi R_n = 0.75(40.35) = 30.3 kips > 18 kips (OK)\)
The connection is adequate as a bearing-type connection. (In order not to obscure the combined loading features of this example, prying action has not been included in the analysis.)
b. From part (a), the shear, bearing, and tensile strengths are satisfactory. From
AISC Equation J3-4, the slip-critical strength is
\(R_n = \mu D_{u}h_{f}T_{b}n_{s}\)
From AISC Table J3.1, the prescribed tension for a 7⁄8-inch-diameter Group A bolt is
\(T_b = 39 kips\)
If we assume Class A surfaces, the slip coefficient is \(\mu = 0.30, \)and for four bolts,
\(R_n =\mu D_{u}h_{ f}T_{b}n_{s} \times 4 = 0.30(1.13)(1.0)(39)(1) \times 4 = 52.88 kips\)
\(\phi R_n = 1.0(61.70) = 61.70 kips\)
Since there is a tensile load on the bolts, the slip-critical strength must be reduced by a factor of
\(k_{sc} = 1 – \frac{T_u}{D_{u}T_{b}n_{b}}= 1 – \frac{72}{1.13(39)(4)}= 0.5916\)
The reduced strength is therefore
\(k_{sc}(52.88) = 0.5916(52.88) = 31.3 kips < 54 kips (N.G.)\)
The connection is inadequate as a slip-critical connection.
\(P_a = D + L = 15 + 45 = 60 kips\)

a. The total shear/bearing load is
\(V_a = \frac{3}{5}(60) = 36 kips\)

The shear/bearing force per bolt is
\(V_{a bolt} = \frac{36}{4} = 9.0 kips\)
The allowable bearing strength is
\(\frac{R_n}{\Omega} = \frac{74.91}{2.00} = 37.5 kips > 9.0 kips (OK)\)
The allowable shear strength is
\(\frac{R_n}{\Omega} = \frac{32.47}{2.00} = 16.24 kips > 9.0 kips (OK)\)
The total tension load is
\(T_a = \frac{4}{5}(60) = 48 kips\)
The tensile force per bolt is
\(T_{a bolt} = \frac{48}{4} = 12 kips\)
To determine the available tensile strength, use AISC Equation J3-3b:
\(F'_{nt}=1.3F_{nt} – \frac{\Omega F_{nt}}{F_{nv} }f_{rv}\leq F_{nt}\)
where
\(F_nt = \)nominal tensile stress in the absence of shear = 90 ksi
\(F_nv =\) nominal shear stress in the absence of tension = 54 ksi
\(f_{rv} = \frac{V_{a bolt}}{A_b}= \frac{9.0}{0.6013} = 14.97 ksi\)
Then
\(F'_{nt}= 1.3(90) – \frac{2.00(90)}{54 (14.97)} = 67.10 ksi < 90 ksi\)
The nominal tensile strength is
\(R_n = F'_{nt}A_b = 67.10(0.6013) = 40.35 kips\)
and the available tensile strength is
\(\frac{R_n}{\Omega} = \frac{ 40.35}{2.00} = 20.2 kips > 12 kips (OK)\)

The connection is adequate as a bearing-type connection. (In order not to obscure the combined loading features of this example, prying action has not been included in the analysis.)
b. From part (a), the shear, bearing, and tensile strengths are satisfactory. From AISC Equation J3-4, the slip-critical strength is
\(R_n = \mu D_{u}h_{f}T_{b}n_{s}\)
From AISC Table J3.1, the prescribed tension for a 7⁄8-inch-diameter Group A bolt is
Tb 5 39 kips
If we assume Class A surfaces, the slip coefficient is \(\mu = 0.30, \)and for four bolts,
\(R_n =\mu D_{u}h_{ f}T_{b}n_{s} \times 4 = 0.30(1.13)(1.0)(39)(1) \times 4 = 52.88 kips\)
\(\frac{R_n}{\Omega} = \frac{ 52.88}{1.50} = 35.25 kips\)
Since there is a tensile load on the bolts, the slip-critical strength must be reduced by a factor of
\(k_{sc} = 1 – \frac{T_a}{D_{u}T_{b}n_{b}}= 1 – \frac{72}{1.13(39)(4)}= 0.5916\)

The reduced strength is therefore
\(k_{sc}(35.25) = 0.5916(35.25) = 20.9 kips < 36 kips (N.G.)\)
The connection is inadequate as a slip-critical connection.
