Guest Question.

A 100 kW, 460 V shunt generator was run as a motor on no load at its rated voltage and speed. The total current taken was 9.8 A, including a shunt current of 2.7 A. The resistance of the armature circuit (including compoles) at normal working temperature was 0.11 Ω. Calculate the effciencies at

(a) full load;
(b) half load.

Admin
Solution

(a) Output current at full load \(= \frac{100\times 1000}{460}= 217.5 A \)

 

∴ Armature current at full load \(= 217.5 + 2.7 = 220.2 A\)

The \(I^2R\) loss in armature circuit at full load is

\((220)^2 × 0.11 = 5325 W\)

Loss in shunt circuit \(= 2.7 × 460 = 1242 W\)

Armature current on no load \(= 9.8 – 2.7 = 7.1 A\)

 

Thus input power to armature on no load is

\(7.1 × 460 = 3265 W\)

But loss in armature circuit on no load is

\((7.1)^2 × 0.11 = 5.5 W\)

This loss is less than 0.2 per cent of the input power and could therefore have been neglected.

Hence, core, friction and windage losses \(= 3260 W,\) and

 

Total losses at full load \(= 5325 + 1242 + 3260 \\ = 9827 W = 9.83 kW\)

 

Input power at full load \(= 100 + 9.83 = 109.8 kW \)

 

and Efficiency at full load \(= \frac{100}{109.8}= 0.911 per unit \\ = 91.1 per cent \)

 

(b) Output current at half load \(= 108.7 A,\) therefore armature current at half load is

\(108.7 + 2.7 = 111.4 \)

and \(I^2R\) loss in armature circuit at half load is

\((111.4)^2 × 0.11 = 1365 W\)

 

∴ Total losses at half load \(= 1365 + 1242 + 3260 \\ = 5867 W = 5.87 kW\)

 

Input power at half load \(= 50 + 5.87 = 55.87 kW \)

 

and Efficiency at half load \(=\frac{50}{55.87}= 0.895 per unit \\ = 89.5 per cent \)
