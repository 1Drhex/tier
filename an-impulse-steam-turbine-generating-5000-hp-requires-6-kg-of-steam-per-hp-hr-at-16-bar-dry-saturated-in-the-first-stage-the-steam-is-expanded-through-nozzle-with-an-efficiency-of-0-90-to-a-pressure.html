Guest Question.

An impulse steam turbine generating 5000 hp requires 6 kg of steam per hp-hr at 16 bar, dry saturated. In the first stage the steam is expanded through nozzle with an efficiency of 0.90 to a pressure of 8 bar. These nozzles are placed so as to extend over approximately \(\large{\frac{1}{3}}\) rd of the circumference with the pitch circle diameter of 600 mm and pitch of 50 mm. Considering nozzle angle as 12° to the plane of wheel and plates dividing being 3 mm thick, determine total length of nozzle arc and radial height of nozzles.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{At inlet to nozzle, from steam table, }h_{1} = h_{\text{g at 16 bar}} = 2794 kJ/kg\\\qquad \qquad\quad s_{1} = s_{\text{g at 16 bar}} = 6.4218 kJ/kg·K\\\text{At exit of nozzle,}\\\qquad \qquad\quad s_{1} = s_{2} = 6.4218 kJ/kg·K\\\text{Since }s_{2} < s_{\text{g at 8 bar}}\text{ so the state at the end of expansion lies in wet region. Let dryness fraction at state 2 be }x_{2}\text{, then,}\\\begin{aligned}s_{2}&= s_{\text{f at 8 bar}} + x_{2} · s_{\text{fg at 8 bar}}\\6.4218&= 2.0462 + x_{2} · 4.6166\\\text{or,}\qquad \qquad x_{2}&= 0.9478\\h_{2}&= h_{\text{f at 8 bar}} + x_{2} · h_{\text{fg at 8 bar}}\\&= 721.11 + (0.9478 × 2048)\\h_{2}&= 2662.2 kJ/kg\\\text{Theoretically,}&\text{ change in enthalpy, }Δh_{12} = 2794 – 2662.2\\Δh_{12}&= 131.8 kJ/kg\\\frac{Δ h_{12 actual}}{Δ h_{12}}&= 0.90 or Δ h_{12 actual} = 0.9 × 131.8 = 118.62 kJ/kg\\h_{2 actual}&= 2794 – 118.62 = 2675.38 kJ/kg\\h_{2, actual}&= 2675.38 = h_{\text{f at 8 bar}} + x_{2 actual} · h_{\text{fg at 8 bar}}\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}2675.38&= 721.11 + (x_{2 actual} × 2048)\\x_{2 actual}&= 0.9542\\\text{Specific volume,} \qquad \qquad v_{2 actual}&= v_{\text{f at 8 bar}} + x_{2 actual} × v_{\text{fg at 8 bar}}\\v_{2 actual}&= 0.001115 + (0.9542 × (0.2404 – 0.001115))\\v_{2 actual}&= 0.2294 m^{3}/kg\\\text{Neglecting the velocity at inlet to }&\text{nozzle,}\\C_{2}&= \sqrt{2 × Δ h_{12 actual}}\\C_{2}&= 487.1 m/s\\\text{By continuity equation,} \qquad \qquad &\\m&= \frac{A_{2} C_{2}}{v_{2 actual}}\\\frac{5000 × 6}{3600}&= \frac{A_{2} × 487.1}{0.2294}\\A_{2}&= 3.925 × 10^{–3} m^{2}\\\text{Area at exit of nozzle}&\approx 39.25 cm^{2}\\\text{Approximate length of nozzle arc,}&= \frac{60 × π}{3} = 62.83 cm\\\text{Number of nozzles}&= \frac{\text{Length of nozzle arc}}{\text{Pitch}}\\&= \frac{62.83}{5} \approx 13 nozzles.\\\text{Correct length of nozzle arc } \qquad&= 13 × 5 = 65 cm\\\text{Flow area at exit of each nozzle }&= (5 \sin 12 – 0.3) × h\\39.25&= (5 \sin 12 – 0.3) × h × 13\\\text{or, }\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad h&= 4.08 cm\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Length of nozzle}&= 65 cm\\\text{Radial height of nozzle}&= 4.08 cm\end{aligned}\)
