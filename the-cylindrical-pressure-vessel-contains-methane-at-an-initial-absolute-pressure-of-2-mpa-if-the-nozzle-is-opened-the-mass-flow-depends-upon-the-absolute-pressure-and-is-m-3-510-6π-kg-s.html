Guest Question.

The cylindrical pressure vessel contains methane at an initial absolute pressure of 2 MPa. If the nozzle is opened, the mass flow depends upon the absolute pressure and is \(m = 3.5(10^{-6})p kg/s \), where P is in PASCALS. Assuming the temperature remains constant at 20°C, determine the pressure in the tank as a function of time. Plot this pressure (vertical axis) versus the time for \(0 \leq t \leq 15 s \). Give values for increments of \( \Delta t = 3 s. \)

Admin
Solution

From Appendix A, the gas constant for Methane is \(R = 518.3 J/kg \cdot K \)using the ideal gas law with T = 20°C + 273 = 293 K, which is constant throughout,

\(p = \rho RT; p = \rho (518.3 J/kg \cdot k)(293 k) \)

 

\(p = 151861.9\rho \)

 

\(r = 6.5849(10^{-6})p \) (1)

The control volume considered is the volume of tank which contains Methane. Since the tank is fully filled at all times, the control volume can be classified as fixed.

\(\frac{d}{dt}\int_{cv} \rho dV + \int_{cs} \rho V \cdot dA =0 \)

Here \(\int_{cv} dV = V \)(fixed control volume)[/latex] and \(m = \int_{cv} \rho V \cdot A \)

\(V \frac{d \rho }{d t} + m =0 \) (2)

Since, \(V = \pi (1 m)^2(6 m) = 6\pi m^3 and m = [3.5(10^{-6})\pi ] kg/s, \) then Eq (1),

 

\(\frac{d\rho }{dt} =6.5849(10^{-6}) \frac{dp}{dt} \)

Substitute these results into Eq. (2),

\(6\pi [6.5849(10^{-6}) \frac{dp}{dt}] + 3.5(10^{-6})p = 0 \)

 

\(\frac{dp}{dt} = -0.02820p \)

 

\(\int_{p_0}^{p}\frac{dp}{p} = -0.02820 \int_{0}^{t} dt \)

 

\(\ln (\frac{p}{p_0})= -0.02820t \)

 

\(\frac{p}{p_0} = e^{-0.02820t} \)

 

\(p = p_0 e^{-0.02820t} \)

Here \(p_0 = 2MPa, \)then

\(p = (2e^{-0.02820t})Mpa,\) where t is in seconds .
