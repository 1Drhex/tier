Guest Question.

Verify the local strength of the HSS tension member subject to the transverse loadings given below, applied through a 4 in. wide plate. The HSS6.000×0.375 is in tension with \(P_{D}\) = 4 kips and \(P_{L}\) = 12 kips.

Admin
Solution

Material Properties:

Column ASTM A500 Gr. B \(F_{y}=42 ksi \quad F_{u}=58 ksi\)

Plate ASTM A36 \(F_{y p}=36 ksi \quad F_{u}=58 ksi\)

Manual
Table 2-3
Table 2-4

Geometric Properties:

Column HSS6.000×0.375 D = 6.00 in. t = 0.349 in.

Plate N = 4.00 in.

Manual
Table 1-13

Check the limits of applicability of Specification Section K1

1) Strength: \(F_{y}\) less than or equal to 52 ksi for HSS \(F_{y}=42 ksi\) ksi o.k.

2) Ductility: \(F_{y} / F_{u} \leq 0.8\) for HSS \(F_{y} / F_{u}=0.72\) o.k.

3) \(D / t \leq 50\) for T-connections D/t=17.2 o.k.

Section K1.2
Section
K1.4a

Calculate the required strength

LRFD ASD
\(\begin{aligned}P_{u} &=1.2(4.00 kips )+1.6(12.0 kips ) \\&=24.0 \text { kips }\end{aligned}\) \(\begin{aligned}P_{a} &=4.00 kips +12.0 kips \\&=16.0 kips\end{aligned}\)

Check the limit state of chord plastification

\(R_{n}=5.5 F_{y} t^{2}\left(1+\frac{0.25 N}{D}\right) Q_{f}\)

Eqn. K1-8

Since the column is in tension, \(Q_{f}\) = 1.0

\(R_{n}=5.5(42 ksi )(0.349 in .)^{2}\left(1+\frac{0.25(4.00 in .)}{6.00 in .}\right) 1.0=32.8 kips\)

Eqn. K1-8

LRFD ASD
\(\begin{array}{l}\phi R_{n}=0.90(32.8 kips )=29.5 kips \\29.5 \text { kips }>24.0 kips \quad \text { o.k. }\end{array}\) \(\begin{array}{l}R_{n} / \Omega=\frac{32.8 kips }{1.67}=19.6 kips \\19.6 \text { kips }>16.0 kips \quad \text { o.k. }\end{array}\)
