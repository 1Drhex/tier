Guest Question.

When 100 \(cm^{3} \)of an aqueous solution of nitric acid, \(HNO_{3}(1.0 mol dm^{-3})\) is mixed with 100 \(cm^{3}\) of an aqueous solution of sodium hydroxide, \(NaOH (1.0 mol dm^{-3})\) in a calorimeter of the type shown in Figure 2.2, a temperature rise of 6.9K is recorded.

(a) Is the reaction exothermic or endothermic?

(b)What is the value of\( \Delta H\) for this reaction in kJ per mole of \(HNO_{3}\)?

[Data: density of water = 1.00 \(g cm^{-3} : C_{water} = 4.18 JK^{-1}g^{-1}\)]

Admin
Solution

(a) A rise in temperature is observed. Therefore, the reaction is exothermic.

(b) Total volume of solution = 100:0 + 100:0 = 200:0 \(cm^{3}\):

Assume that the density of the aqueous solution \(\approx \) density of water

Mass of solution in g =\( (Volume in cm^{3})\times (Density in g cm^{-3})\)

= \((200.0 cm^{3})\times (1.00 g cm^{-3})\)

= 200 g

The heat change can now be found:

Heat change = (mg)\(\times (CJK^{-1}g^{-1})\times (\Delta TK)\)

=\((200 g)\times (4.18 K^{-1}g^{-1} )\times (6.9 K)\)

= 5800 J

To find\(\Delta H\) per mole of \(HNO_{3}\), first determine how many moles of \(HNO_{3}\)

are involved in the reaction:

Amount of \(HNO_{3}\) in moles = \((Volume in dm^{3}\))

\(\times (Concentration in moldm^{-3})\).

\(\times (100.0\times 10^{-3} moldm^{-3})\times (1.0 moldm^{-3} )\).

=0.10 mol

When 0.10 moles of \(HNO_{3}\) react, the heat released is 5800 J. Therefore when 1.0 mole of \(HNO_{3}\) reacts.

Heat released= \(\frac{5800}{0.10}\)=58000 J

The reaction is carried out at constant pressure, and therefore the heat change equals the enthalpy change. The reaction is exothermic and \(\Delta H\) is negative.

\(\Delta H= 58000 J mol^{-1}= -58 KJ mol ^{-1}\)
