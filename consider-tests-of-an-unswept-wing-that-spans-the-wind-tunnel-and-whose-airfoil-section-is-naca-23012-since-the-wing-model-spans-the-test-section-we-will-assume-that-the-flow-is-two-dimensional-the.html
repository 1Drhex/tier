Guest Question.

Consider tests of an unswept wing that spans the wind tunnel and whose airfoil section is NACA 23012. Since the wing model spans the test section, we will assume that the flow is two dimensional. The chord of the model is 1.3 m. The test section conditions simulate a density altitude of 3 km. The velocity in the test section is 360 km/h. What is the lift per unit span (in N/m) that you would expect to measure when the angle of attack is \( 4^{\infty } \)? What would be the corresponding section lift coefficient?

Admin
Solution

-First, we need to calculate the section lift coefficient. We will assume that the lift is a linear function of the angle of attack and that it is independent of the Reynolds number (i.e., the viscous effects are negligible) at these test conditions. These are reasonable assumptions as can be seen by referring to Fig. 5.13 . Therefore, the section lift coefficient from equation (5.9) is:

\( C_{l}=C_{l_{\alpha }}\left ( \alpha -\alpha _{0l} \right ) \)

Using the values presented in the discussion associated with Fig. 5.13 ,

\( C_{l}=\left ( 0.1041/deg \right )\left ( 4.0-\left ( -1.2 \right ) \right )=0.541 \)

At an angle of attack of 4, the experimental values of the section lift coefficient for an NACA 23012 airfoil section range from 0.50 to 0.57, as shown in Fig.5.13 . To calculate the corresponding lift force per unit span, we rearrange equation (5.8) to obtain

\( l=C_{l}q_{\infty }c \)

To calculate the dynamic pressure \( \left ( q_{\infty } \right ) \) , we need the velocity in m/s and the density in \( kg/ m^{3} \) , in order for the units to be consistent

\( U_{\infty }=360\tfrac{km}{h}\frac{1000m}{km}\frac{h}{3600s}=100\tfrac{m}{s} \)

Given the density altitude is 3 km , we can refer to Appendix B to find that :

\( \rho =0.9093kg/m^{3} \)

( Note: The fact that we are given the density altitude as 3 km does not provide specific information either about the temperature or pressure.) So now the lift per unit span is:

\( l=\left ( 0.541 \right )\left [ \frac{1}{2}\left ( 0.9093\tfrac{kg}{m^{3}} \right )\left ( 100\tfrac{m}{s} \right )^{2} \right ]\left ( 1.3m \right ) \) \( l=\left ( 0.541 \right )\left [ 4546.5\frac{N}{m^{2}} \right ]\left ( 1.3m \right )=3197.6\tfrac{N}{m} \)
