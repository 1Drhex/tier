Guest Question.

A single line railway track over a road is supported by two trusses as shown in elevation in Fig. P.3.21(a). The load on the bridge produced by a train may be regarded as comprising two uniformly distributed loads, one representing the engine and one the line of freight cars as shown in Fig. P.3.21(b). With the front of the engine at mid-span, determine the distribution of shear force and bending moment in one of the trusses.

Admin
Solution

With the head of the engine at mid-span, the load on a truss is as shown in Fig. S.3.21(a).

Taking moments about A

\( R_{ D } \times 12-100 \times 4 \times 8-25 \times 2 \times 11=0 \)

which gives

\( R_{ D }=312.5 kN\)

Then, at any section between D and C, a distance x from D

\(S_{ DC }=312.5-25 x\)

When \( x=0, S_{ D }=312.5 kN , \text { and when } x=2 m , S_{ C }=262.5 kN\). At any section between C and B, a distance x from D

\(S_{ CB }=312.5-25 \times 2-100(x-2)\)

i.e.

\(S_{ CB }=462.5-100 x\)

When \( x=2 m , S_{ C }=262.5 kN \text { and when } x=6 m , S_{ B }=-137.5 kN \text {. Note that } S_{ CB }=0 \) when x =4.625 m. In BA, the shear force is constant and equal to -137.5 kN and the complete shear force distribution is as shown in Fig. S.3.21(b). The bending moment at any section between D and C is given by

\(M_{ DC }=R_{ D } x-\frac{25}{2} x^{2}=312.5 x-12.5 x^{2}\)

Then, when \( x=0, M_{ D }=0 \text { and when } x=2 m , M_{ C }=575 kNm \). Note that there is no turning value for MDC between D and C. At any section between C and B, the bending moment is given by

\(M_{ CB }=312.5 x-25 \times 2(x-1)-\frac{100}{2}(x-2)^{2}\)

i.e.

\(M_{ CB }=462.5 x-50 x^{2}-150\)

When \( x=2 m , M_{ C }=575 kNm \text { (as before) and when } x=6 m , M_{ B }=825 kNm \). Further,

\( \frac{ d M_{ CB }}{ d x}=462.5-100 x=0 \text { for a turning value } \)

i.e. when x =4.625 m. Substituting this value of x gives

\( M_{ CB (\max )}=919.5 kNm \)

Between B and A, the bending moment distribution is linear and \( M_{ A }=0 \). The complete distribution of bending moment is as shown in Fig. S.3.21(c).
