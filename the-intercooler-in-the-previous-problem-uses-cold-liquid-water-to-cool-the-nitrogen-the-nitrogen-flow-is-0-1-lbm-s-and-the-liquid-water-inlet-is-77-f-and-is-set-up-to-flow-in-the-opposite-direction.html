Guest Question.

The intercooler in uses cold liquid water to cool the nitrogen. The nitrogen flow is 0.1 lbm/s, and the liquid water inlet is 77 F and is set up to flow in the opposite direction from the nitrogen, so the water leaves at 105 F. Find the flow rate of the water.

Admin
Solution

C.V. Heat exchanger, steady 2 flows in and two flows out.

Continuity eq.: \(\dot{ m }_{1}=\dot{ m }_{2}=\dot{ m }_{ H 2 O } ; \quad \dot{ m }_{3}=\dot{ m }_{4}=\dot{ m }_{ N 2}\)

Energy eq.: \(0=\dot{ m }_{ H 2 O }\left( h _{3}- h _{4}\right)+\dot{ m }_{ N 2}\left( h _{2}- h _{1}\right)\)

Due to the lower range of temperature we will use constant specific heats from F.4 and F.3 so the energy equation is approximated as

\(0=\dot{ m }_{ H 2 O } C _{ pH 2 O }\left( T _{3}- T _{4}\right)+\dot{ m }_{ N 2} C _{ p }\left( T _{2}- T _{1}\right)\)

 

Now solve for the water flow rate

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ m }_{ H 2 O } &=\dot{ m }_{ N 2} C _{ pN 2}\left( T _{2}- T _{1}\right) /\left[ C _{ pH 2 O }\left( T _{4}- T _{3}\right)\right] \\&=0.1 lbm / s \times \frac{0.249 Btu / lbm – R \times(800-580) R }{1.0 Btu / lbm – R \times(105-77) R } \\&= 0 . 1 9 6 \mathrm { lbm } / \mathrm { s }\end{aligned}\)
