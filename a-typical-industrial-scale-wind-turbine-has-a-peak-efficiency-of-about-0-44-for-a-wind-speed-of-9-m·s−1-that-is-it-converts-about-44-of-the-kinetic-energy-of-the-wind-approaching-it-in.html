Guest Question.

A typical industrial-scale wind turbine has a peak efficiency of about 0.44 for a wind speed of \(9 m\cdot s^{−1}.\) That is, it converts about \(44\%\) of the kinetic energy of the wind approaching it into usable electrical energy. The total air flow impinging on such a turbine with a rotor diameter of 43 m is about \(15,000 kg\cdot s^{−1}\) for the given wind speed.

(a) How much electrical energy is produced when 1 kg of air passes through the turbine?
(b) What is the power output of the turbine?
(c) If there is no heat transferred to the air, and if its temperature remains unchanged, what is its change in speed upon passing through the turbine?

Admin
Solution

(a) The kinetic energy of the wind on the basis of 1 kg of air is:

\( E_{K_{1}}=\frac{1}{2}mu^{2}=\frac{(1 kg)(9 m\cdot s^{-1})^{2}}{2}=40.5 kg\cdot m^{2}\cdot s^{-2}=40.5 J\)

Thus, the electrical energy produced per kilogram of air is \(0.44 \times 40.5 = 17.8 J.\)
(b) The power output is:

\( 17.8 J\cdot kg^{-1}\times 15,000 kg\cdot s^{-1}=267,000 J\cdot s^{-1}=267 kW\)

(c) If the temperature and pressure of the air are unchanged, then its internal energy is
unchanged. Changes in gravitational potential energy can also be neglected. Thus, with no heat transfer, the first law becomes

\( \Delta\)(Energy of the system)\(=\Delta E_{K}=E_{K_{2}}-E_{K_{1}}=W=-17.8 J\cdot kg^{-1}\)

 

\( E_{K_{2}}=40.5-17.8=22.7 J\cdot kg^{-1}=22.7 N\cdot m\cdot kg^{-1}=22.7 m^{2}\cdot s^{-2}\)

 

\( E_{K_{2}}=\frac{u^{2}_{2}}{2}=22.7 m^{2}\cdot s^{-2}\) and \(u_{2}=6.74 m\cdot s^{-1}\)

The decrease in air speed is: \(9.00 − 6.74 = 2.26 m\cdot s^{−1}.\)
