Guest Question.

In a cogenerating steam power plant the turbine receives steam from a highpressure steam drum (h = 3445.9 kJ/kg, s = 6.9108 kJ/kgK) and a low-pressure steam drum (h = 2855.4 kJ/kg, s = 7.0592 kJ/kgK) as shown in Fig. P9.80. For the turbine calculation assume a mass weighted average entropy and neglect entropy generation by mixing. The condenser is made as two closed heat exchangers used to heat water running in a separate loop for district heating. The high-temperature heater adds 30 MW and the low-temperature heater adds 31 MW to the district heating water flow. Find the power cogenerated by the turbine and the temperature in the return line to the deaerator.

Admin
Solution

Inlet states from Table B.1.3

\(\begin{array}{ll}h _{1} & =3445.9 kJ / kg , & s _{1}=6.9108 kJ / kg K \\h _{2} & =2855.4 kJ / kg , & s _{2}=7.0592 kJ / kg K\end{array}\)

 

\(\dot{ m }_{ TOT }=\dot{ m }_{1}+\dot{ m }_{2}=27 kg / s\)

 

Assume a reversible turbine and the two flows can mix without s generation.

Energy Eq.4.10: \(\dot{ m }_{1} h _{1}+\dot{ m }_{2} h _{2}=\dot{ m }_{3} h _{3}+\dot{ m }_{4} h _{4}+\dot{ W }_{ T }\)

Entropy Eq.7.7: \(\dot{ m }_{1} s _{1}+\dot{ m }_{2} s _{2}=\dot{ m }_{ TOT } s _{\text {mix }} \Rightarrow s _{ MIX }=6.9383 kJ / kg K\)

State 3: \(s _{3}= s _{ MIX } \Rightarrow h _{3}=2632.4 kJ / kg , x _{3}=0.966\)

State 4: \(s _{4}= s _{ MIX } \Rightarrow h _{4}=2413.5 kJ / kg , x _{4}=0.899\)

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ W }_{ T } &=22 \times 3445.9+5 \times 2855.4-13 \times 2632.4-14 \times 2413.5 \\&=22077 kW = 2 2 M W\end{aligned}\)

District heating line \(\dot{ Q }_{ TOT }=\dot{ m }\left( h _{95}- h _{60}\right)=60935 kW\)

OK, this matches close enough

C.V. Both heaters: \(\dot{ m }_{3} h _{3}+\dot{ m }_{4} h _{4}-\dot{ Q }_{ TOT }=\dot{ m }_{ TOT } h _{ EX }\)

\(\begin{aligned}&13 \times 2632.4-14 \times 2413.5-60935=7075.2=27 \times h _{ EX } \\& h _{ EX }=262 \approx h _{ f } \Rightarrow T _{ EX }= 6 2 . 5 ^{\circ} C\end{aligned}\)

Remark: We could have computed the expansion from state 1 to \(P _{2}\) followed by a mixing process to find a proper state 2a from which we expand down to \(P _{3} \text { and } P _{4}\)

Eq.4.10 : \(\dot{Q}_{ C.V. }+\sum \dot{m}_{i}\left(h_{i}+\frac{ V _{i}^{2}}{2}+g Z_{i}\right)=\sum \dot{m}_{e}\left(h_{e}+\frac{ V _{e}^{2}}{2}+g Z_{e}\right)+\dot{W}_{ C . V. }\)

Eq.7.7 : \(\sum \dot{m}_{e} s_{e}-\sum \dot{m}_{i} s_{i}=\sum_{ c.s. } \frac{\dot{Q}_{ c.v. }}{T}+\dot{S}_{ gen }\)
