Guest Question.

Let \(\mathbf{u}=\left(\begin{array}{l}1 \\ 2\end{array}\right)\) and \(\mathbf{v}=\left(\begin{array}{l}3 \\ 4\end{array}\right)\) be in \(\mathbb{R}^{2}\). Determine the following with respect to the dot product:

(i) \(|\langle\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}\rangle|\) (ii) \(\|\mathbf{u}\|\|\mathbf{v}\|\) (iii) \(\|\mathbf{u}\|+\|\mathbf{v}\|\) (iv) \(\|\mathbf{u}+\mathbf{v}\|\)

Admin
Solution

(i) We have \(\langle\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}\rangle=\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v}=\left(\begin{array}{l}1 \\ 2\end{array}\right) \cdot\left(\begin{array}{l}3 \\ 4\end{array}\right)=(1 \times 3)+(2 \times 4)=11\). Hence \(|\langle\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}\rangle|=|11|=11\)

(ii) Remember, the symbol \(\|\)\(\|\) means the length of the vector. By Pythagoras we have

\(\|\mathbf{u}\|=\left\|\left(\begin{array}{l}1 \\2\end{array}\right)\right\|=\sqrt{1^{2}+2^{2}}=\sqrt{5} \text { and } \quad\|\mathbf{v}\|=\left\|\left(\begin{array}{l}3 \\4\end{array}\right)\right\|=\sqrt{3^{2}+4^{2}}=5\)

Multiplying these we have \(\|\mathbf{u}\|\|\mathbf{v}\|=\sqrt{5} \times 5=11.1803(4 \mathrm{dp})\).

(iii) Adding both the above results in part (ii) we have

\( \|\mathbf{u}\|+\|\mathbf{v}\|=\sqrt{5}+5=7.2361(4 \mathrm{dp}) \)

(iv) Similarly by using Pythagoras we have

\( \|\mathbf{u}+\mathbf{v}\|=\left\|\left(\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right)+\left(\begin{array}{l} 3 \\ 4 \end{array}\right)\right\|=\left\|\left(\begin{array}{l} 4 \\ 6 \end{array}\right)\right\|=\sqrt{4^{2}+6^{2}}=\sqrt{52}=7.2111(4 \mathrm{dp}) \)
