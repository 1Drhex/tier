Guest Question.

In the Otto cycle all the heat transfer \(q_{ H }\) occurs at constant volume. It is more realistic to assume that part of \(q_{ H }\) occurs after the piston has started its downward motion in the expansion stroke. Therefore, consider a cycle identical to the Otto cycle, except that the first two-thirds of the total \(q_{ H }\) occurs at constant volume and the last one-third occurs at constant pressure. Assume that the total \(q_{ H }\) is 2100 kJ/kg, that the state at the beginning of the compression process is 90 kPa, 20Â°C, and that the compression ratio is 9. Calculate the maximum pressure and temperature and the thermal efficiency of this cycle. Compare the results with those of a conventional Otto cycle having the same given variables.
