Guest Question.

For horizontal flexural member AB, determine the principal stresses and the maximum shear stress acting at points H and K, as shown on Figures P15.28a and P15.28b. Show these stresses on an appropriate sketch for each point.

Admin
Solution

Centroid location in y direction: (reference axis at bottom of shape)

\(\begin{array}{lccccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Width } b \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Height } h \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} \text { (from bottom) } \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 60 & 16 & 960 & 108 & 103,680 \\\text { stem } & 16 & 100 & 1,600 & 50 & 80,000 \\\hline & & & 2,560 & & 183,680\end{array}\)

 

\(\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{183,680 mm ^{3}}{2,560 mm ^{2}}=71.75 mm\) (measured upward from bottom edge of stem)

 

Moment of inertia about the z axis: (i.e., horizontal axis)

\(\begin{array}{lcccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 20,480.00 & 36.25 & 1,261,500.00 & 1,281,980.00 \\\text { stem } & 1,333,333.33 & -21.75 & 756,900.00 & 2,090,233.33 \\\hline & {\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( mm ^{4}\right)=} & 3,372,213.33\end{array}\)

 

Equilibrium:

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma F_{x}=& B_{x}+F_{A C} \cos \left(32.667^{\circ}\right)=0 \\\Sigma F_{y}=& B_{y}-F_{A C} \sin \left(32.667^{\circ}\right)-6,000 N \\&-(30 N / mm )(800 mm )=0 \\\Sigma M_{B}=& F_{A C}(1,700 mm ) \sin \left(32.667^{\circ}\right) \\&+F_{A C}(40 mm ) \cos \left(32.667^{\circ}\right) \\&+(6,000 N )(1,700 mm ) \\&+(30 N / mm )(800 mm )(400 mm )=0\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\therefore F_{A C}=-20,814.557 N \quad B_{x}=17,552.124 N \quad B_{y}=18,765.226 N\)

 

Internal forces at H:

\(\Sigma F_{x}=B_{x}-F_{H}=0 \quad \therefore F_{H}=17,552.124 N\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma F_{y}=& V_{H}+B_{y}-(30 N / mm )(800 mm )=0 \\& \therefore V_{H}=5,234.774 N \\\Sigma M_{H}=&-M_{H}+B_{y}(1,100 mm ) \\&-(30 N / mm )(800 mm )(700 mm )=0 \\& \therefore M_{H}=3,841,749.089 N – mm\end{aligned}\)

 

Axial stress at H:

\(\sigma_{\text {axial }}=\frac{17,522.124 N }{2,560 mm ^{2}}=6.845 MPa ( T )\)

Shear stress at H:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q_{H}=(16 mm )(50 mm )(71.75 mm -25 mm )=37,400 mm ^{3} \\&\tau_{H}=\frac{(5,234.774 N )\left(37,400 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(3,372,213.33 mm ^{4}\right)(16 mm )}=3.629 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Bending stress at H:

\(\sigma_{\text {bend }}=-\frac{(3,841,749.089 N – mm )(-71.75 mm +50 mm )}{3,372,213.33 mm ^{4}}=24.778 MPa ( T )\)

 

Summary of stresses at H:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=6.845 MPa +24.778 MPa =31.623 MPa \\\sigma_{y} &=0 MPa \\\tau_{x y} &=-3.629 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Principal stress calculations:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq.
(12.12):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}}{2}\right)^{2}+\tau_{x y}^{2}}=\frac{(31.623)+(0)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(31.623)-(0)}{2}\right)^{2}+(-3.629)^{2}} \\&=15.811 \pm 16.223\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1} &=32.0 MPa \text { and } \quad \sigma_{p 2}=-0.411 MPa \\\tau_{\max } &=16.22 MPa \text { (maximum in-plane shear stress) } \\\sigma_{\text {avg }} &=15.81 MPa ( T ) \text { (normal stress on planes of maximum in-plane shear stress) }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan 2 \theta_{p} &=\frac{\tau_{x y}}{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) / 2}=\frac{-3.629}{[(31.623)-(0)] / 2}=\frac{-3.629}{15.811}=-0.229489 \\&\left.\therefore \theta_{p}=-6.46^{\circ} \quad \text { (clockwise from the } x \text { axis to the direction of } \sigma_{p 1}\right)\end{aligned}\)

 

 

Internal forces at K:

\(\begin{array}{ll}\Sigma F_{x}=B_{x}-F_{K}=0 & \therefore F_{K}=17,552.124 N \\\Sigma F_{y}=V_{K}+B_{y}-(30 N / mm )(300 mm )=0 & \therefore V_{K}=-9,765.226 N\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{K}=&-M_{K}+B_{y}(300 mm )-(30 N / mm )(300 mm )(150 mm )=0 \\& \therefore M_{K}=4,279,567.933 N – mm\end{aligned}\)

Axial stress at K:

\(\sigma_{\text {axial }}=\frac{17,522.124 N }{2,560 mm ^{2}}=6.845 MPa ( T )\)

Shear stress at K:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q_{K}=37,400 mm ^{3} \\&\tau_{K}=\frac{(9,765.226 N )\left(37,400 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(3,372,213.33 mm ^{4}\right)(16 mm )}=6.769 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Bending stress at K:

\(\sigma_{\text {bend }}=-\frac{(4,279,567.933 N – mm )(-71.75 mm +50 mm )}{3,372,213.33 mm ^{4}}=27.602 MPa ( T )\)

 

Summary of stresses at K:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=6.845 MPa +27.602 MPa =34.447 MPa \\\sigma_{y} &=0 MPa \\\tau_{x y} &=6.769 MPa\end{aligned}\)

 

Principal stress calculations:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq.
(12.12):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}}{2}\right)^{2}+\tau_{x y}^{2}}=\frac{(34.447)+(0)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(34.447)-(0)}{2}\right)^{2}+(6.769)^{2}} \\&=17.223 \pm 18.506\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1} &=35.7 MPa \text { and } \quad \sigma_{p 2}=-1.282 MPa \\\tau_{\max } &=18.51 MPa \quad \text { (maximum in-plane shear stress) } \\\sigma_{\text {avg }} &=17.22 MPa ( T ) \quad \text { (normal stress on planes of maximum in-plane shear stress) }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan 2 \theta_{p} &=\frac{\tau_{x y}}{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) / 2}=\frac{6.769}{[(34.447)-(0)] / 2}=\frac{6.769}{17.223}=0.393007 \\&\left.\therefore \theta_{p}=10.73^{\circ} \quad \text { (counterclockwise from the } x \text { axis to the direction of } \sigma_{p 1}\right)\end{aligned}\)
