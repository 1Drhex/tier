Magnetic Field on the Axis of a Circular Current Loop

Consider a circular wire loop of radius a located in the yz plane and carrying a steady current I as in Figure 30.5. Calculate the magnetic field at an axial point P a distance x from the center of the loop.

Admin
Solution

Conceptualize Compare this problem to for the electric field due to a ring of charge. Figure \(30.5\) shows the magnetic field contribution \(d \overrightarrow{ B }\) at \(P\) due to a single current element at the top of the ring. This field vector can be resolved into components \(d B_{x}\) parallel to the axis of the ring and \(d B_{\perp}\) perpendicular to the axis. Think about the magnetic field contributions from a current element at the bottom of the loop. Because of the symmetry of the situation, the perpendicular components of the field due to elements at the top and bottom of the ring cancel. This cancellation occurs for all pairs of segments around the ring, so we can ignore the perpendicular component of the field and focus solely on the parallel components, which simply add.

Categorize We are asked to find the magnetic field due to a simple current distribution, so this example is a typical problem for which the Biot-Savart law is appropriate.

Analyze In this situation, every length element \(d \overrightarrow{ S }\) is perpendicular to the vector \(\hat{ r }\) at the location of the element. Therefore, for any element, \(|d \overrightarrow{ s } \times \hat{ r }|=(d s)(1) \sin 90^{\circ}=d s .\) Furthermore, all length elements around the loop|are at the same distance \(r\) from \(P\), where \(r^{2}=a^{2}+x^{2}\)

Use Equation \(30.1\) to find the magnitude of \(d \overrightarrow{ B }\) due to the current in any length element \(d \overrightarrow{ s }\) :

\(d \overrightarrow{ B }=\frac{\mu_{0}}{4 \pi} \frac{I d \overrightarrow{ s } \times \hat{ r }}{r^{2}}\) (30.1)

\(d B=\frac{\mu_{0} I}{4 \pi} \frac{|d \overrightarrow{ s } \times \hat{ r }|}{r^{2}}=\frac{\mu_{0} I}{4 \pi} \frac{d s}{\left(a^{2}+x^{2}\right)}\)

Find the \(x\) component of the field element:

\(d B_{x}=\frac{\mu_{0} I}{4 \pi} \frac{d s}{\left(a^{2}+x^{2}\right)} \cos \theta\)

Integrate over the entire loop:

\(B_{x}=\oint d B_{x}=\frac{\mu_{0} I}{4 \pi} \oint \frac{d s \cos \theta}{a^{2}+x^{2}}\)

From the geometry, evaluate \(\cos \theta\) :

\(\cos \theta=\frac{a}{\left(a^{2}+x^{2}\right)^{1 / 2}}\)

Substitute this expression for \(\cos \theta\) into the integral and note that \(x\) and \(a\) are both constant:

\(B_{x}=\frac{\mu_{0} I}{4 \pi} \oint \frac{d s}{a^{2}+x^{2}}\left[\frac{a}{\left(a^{2}+x^{2}\right)^{1 / 2}}\right]=\frac{\mu_{0} I}{4 \pi} \frac{a}{\left(a^{2}+x^{2}\right)^{3 / 2}} \oint d s\)

Integrate around the loop:

\(B_{x}=\frac{\mu_{0} I}{4 \pi} \frac{a}{\left(a^{2}+x^{2}\right)^{3 / 2}}(2 \pi a)=\frac{\mu_{0} I a^{2}}{2\left(a^{2}+x^{2}\right)^{3 / 2}}\) (30.7)

Finalize To find the magnetic field at the center of the loop, set \(x=0\) in Equation 30.7. At this special point,

\(B=\frac{\mu_{0} I}{2 a} \quad(\text { at } x=0)\) (30.8)
