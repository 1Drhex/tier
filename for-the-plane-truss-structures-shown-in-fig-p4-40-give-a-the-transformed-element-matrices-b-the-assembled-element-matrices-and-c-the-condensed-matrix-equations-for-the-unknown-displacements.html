Guest Question.

For the plane truss structures shown in Fig. P4.40, give (a) the transformed element matrices, (b) the assembled element matrices, and (c) the condensed matrix equations for the unknown displacements and forces.

Admin
Solution

This problem involves 5 members and it is hard to be solved by hand. The main point should be to give the specified displacements and forces on the structure. We have

\(U_{1}=U_{2}=U_{8}\) = 0, \(F_{5}\) = 8, 000 lbs. , \(F_{6}\) = -8, 000 lbs.

The connectivity of the elements is defined by the matrix

\([B]=\begin{bmatrix}1&3 \\ 1&2 \\ 2&3 \\ 2&4 \\ 4&3\end{bmatrix}\)

The angle of orientation of each member are (CCW):

\(\theta _{1}=45^{\circ}\), \(\theta _{2}=0^{\circ}\), \(\theta _{3}=90^{\circ}\), \(\theta _{4}=0^{\circ}\), \(\theta _{5}=315^{\circ}\)

The element stiffness matrices are

\([K^{1}]=10^{6}\begin{bmatrix}0.2652 &0.2652 &-0.2652 &-0.2652 \\ 0.2652 &0.2652 &-0.2652 &-0.2652 \\ -0.2652 &-0.2652 &0.2652 &0.2652 \\ -0.2652 &-0.2652 &0.2652 &0.2652 \end{bmatrix}\)

 

\([K^{2}]=10^{6}\begin{bmatrix}0.7500 &0.0000 &-0.7500 &0.0000 \\ 0.0000 &0.0000 &0.0000 &0.0000 \\ -0.7500 &0.0000 &0.7500 &0.0000 \\ 0.0000 &0.0000 &0.0000 &0.0000 \end{bmatrix}\)

 

\([K^{3}]=10^{6}\begin{bmatrix}0.0000 &0.0000 &0.0000 &0.0000 \\ 0.0000 &0.7500 &0.0000 &-0.7500 \\ 0.0000 &0.0000 &0.0000 &0.0000 \\ 0.0000 &-0.7500 &0.0000 &0.7500 \end{bmatrix}\)

 

\([K^{4}]=10^{6}\begin{bmatrix}0.7500 &0.0000 &-0.7500 &0.0000 \\ 0.0000 &0.0000 &0.0000 &0.0000 \\ -0.7500 &0.0000 &0.7500 &0.0000 \\ 0.0000 &0.0000 &0.0000 &0.0000 \end{bmatrix}\)

 

\([K^{5}]=10^{6}\begin{bmatrix}0.2652 &-0.2652 &-0.2652 & 0.2652\\ -0.2652 &0.2652 &0.2652 &-0.2652 \\ -0.2652 &0.2652 &0.2652 &-0.2652 \\ 0.2652 &-0.2652 &-0.2652 &0.2652 \end{bmatrix}\)

 

The generalized displacements calculated using FEM1D are

\(U_{3}\) = 0.0107 in., \(U_{4}\) = -0.0257 in., \(U_{5}\) = 0.0257 in.

\(U_{6}\) = -0.0257 in., \(U_{7}\) = 0.0213 in.

The axial forces in the members are (subscripts denote the global node numbers; i.e., \(F_{ij}\) denotes the tensile force in the member connecting global nodes i and j):

\(F_{12}\) = 8 kips, \(F_{13}\) = 0 kips, \(F_{23}\) = 0 kips, \(F_{24}\) = -8 kips, \(F_{34}\) = \(\sqrt{2}\) × 8 kips

These can be easily verified using the “method of sections” for this determinate structure.
