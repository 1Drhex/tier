Guest Question.

A hockey puck (mass \(m\) ) in an ideally smooth ice field (no friction) is shot with speed \(v_{0}\) into the half-circular part of the boards and slides along the boards. At the end of the curved part, the speed is measured to be \(0.6v_{0}\) .

Determine the friction coefficient \(\mu \)of the boards.

Admin
Solution

With aid of the free body diagram, we obtain the equations of motion

\(\swarrow :ma_{t}=-R\) , \(\searrow :ma_{n}=N\) .

Introducing the kinematic relations \(a_{t}=\dot{v}\) , \(a_{n}=v^{2}/r\) and the friction law \(R=\mu N\) yields

\(m\dot{v}=-\mu m\frac{v^{2} }{r} \) .

Separation of variables and integration leads to the velocity \(v\left(t\right) \) :

\(\int_{v_{0} }^{v}{\frac{d\bar{v} }{\bar{v}^2 } } =-\int_{0}^{t}{\frac{\mu }{r} d\bar{t} } \rightsquigarrow v\left(t\right)= \frac{v_{0} }{1+\frac{\mu v_{0} }{r}t } \) .

From repeated integration, the path \(s\left(t\right) \) is determined:

\(\int_{0}^{s}{d\bar{s} }=v_{0}\int_{0}^{t}{\frac{d\bar{t} }{1+\mu \frac{v_{0} }{r}\bar{t} } } \rightsquigarrow s\left(t\right)=\frac{r}{\mu } \ln \left(1+\frac{ \mu v_{0} }{r}t\right) \) .

The time \(t_{1}\), until the speed is reduced to \(0.6v_{0}\), is calculated as

\(0.6v_{0}=\frac{v_{0} }{1+\frac{\mu v_{0} }{r}t_{1} } \rightsquigarrow t_{1}=\frac{2}{3}\frac{r}{\mu v_{0} } \) .

Thus, we obtain for the corresponding path length at the end of the curved boards

\(s_{1}=s\left(t_{1} \right)=\frac{r}{\mu }\ln \left(1+\frac{\mu v_{0}}{r}t_{1} \right)=\frac{r}{\mu } \ln \frac{5}{3} \) .

Equalizing it with the length of the half-circle yields

\(s_{1}=r\pi \rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{\mu =\frac{\ln \left(5/3\right) }{\pi }=0.16 } } \) .
