Guest Question.

Determine the reactions and the member end moments produced in the frame shown in Figure 11.23a by a load of 5 kips at joint B. Also determine the horizontal displacement of girder BC. Given:\( E=30000\frac{kips}{in.^{2}}\) . Units of I are in \( in.^{4}\) .

Admin
Solution

We first displace the frame 1 in. to the right with all joints clamped against rotation (Figure 11.23b) and introduce a temporary roller at B to provide horizontal restraint. The column moments in the restrained structure are computed using Equation 11.21.

 

\( M_{NF}=-\frac{6EI}{L^{2}}\) (11.21)

 

\( M_{AB}=M_{BA}=-\frac{6EI}{L^{2}}=-\frac{6\left ( 30000 \right )\left ( 100 \right )}{\left ( 20\times 12 \right )^{2}}=-312 \) kip.in.

 

\( =-26 \) kip.ft

 

\( M_{CD}=M_{DC}=-\frac{6EI}{L^{2}}=-\frac{6\left ( 30000 \right )\left ( 200 \right )}{\left ( 40\times 12 \right )^{2}}=-166 \) kip.in.

 

\( =-13 \) kip.ft

 

The clamps are now removed (but the roller remains) and the column moments distributed until all joints are in equilibrium. Details of the analysis are shown in Figure 11.23c. The distribution factors at joints B and C are computed below.

Joint B:

\( K_{AB}=\frac{3}{4}\left ( \frac{I}{L} \right )=\frac{3}{4}\left ( \frac{100}{20} \right )=\frac{15}{4}\)

 

\( K_{BC}=\frac{I}{L}=\frac{200}{40}=\frac{20}{4}\)

 

\( \sum K=\frac{35}{4}\)

 

Distribution factors

 

\( \frac{K_{AB}}{\sum K}=\frac{3}{7}\)

 

\( \frac{K_{BC}}{\sum K}=\frac{4}{7}\)

 

Joint C:

\( K_{CB}=\frac{I}{L}=\frac{200}{40}=5\)

 

\( K_{CD}=\frac{I}{L}=\frac{200}{40}=5\)

 

\( \sum K=10\)

 

Distribution factors

 

\( \frac{5}{10}=\frac{1}{2}\)

 

\( \frac{5}{10}=\frac{1}{2}\)

 

We next compute the column shears by summing moments about an axis through the base of each column (Figure 11.23d).

 

Compute \( V_{1} \) .

 

\( ⟳+ \sum M_{A}=0\) \( 20V_{1}-8.5=0\) \( V_{1}=0.43 \) kip

 

Compute \( V_{2} \) .

 

\( ⟳+ \sum M_{D}=0\) \( 40V_{2}-8.03-10.51=0\) \( V_{2}=0.46 \) kip

 

Considering horizontal equilibrium of the free body of the girder (in Figure 11.23d), compute the roller reaction at B.

 

\( \rightarrow +\sum F_{x}=0 S-V_{1}-V_{2}=0\)

 

\( S=0.46+0.43=0.89 \) kip

 

At this stage we have produced a solution for the forces and reactions produced in the frame by a lateral load of 0.89 kip at joint B. (The results of the analysis in Figure 11.23c and d are summarized in Figure 11.23e.) To compute the forces and displacements produced by a 5-kip load,
we scale up all forces and displacements by the ratio of \( \frac{P}{S}=\frac{5}{0.89}=5.62\) . Final results are shown in Figure 11.23 f. The displacement of the girder \( =\left ( \frac{P}{S} \right )\left ( 1in. \right )=5.62 \) in.
