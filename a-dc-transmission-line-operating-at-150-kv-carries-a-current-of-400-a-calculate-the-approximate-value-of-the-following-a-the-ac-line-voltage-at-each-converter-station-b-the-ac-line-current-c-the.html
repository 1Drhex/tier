Guest Question.

A dc transmission line operating at 150 kV carries a current of 400 A. Calculate the approximate value of the following:


a. The ac line voltage at each converter station
b. The ac line current
c. The active power absorbed by the rectifier
d. The reactive power absorbed by each converter

Admin
Solution

Using the scale model of Fig. 4 and multiplying by the appropriate ratios, we find the following:

 

a. The ac line voltages are

 

\(\begin{aligned}E_{1} &=(150 kV / 100 kV ) \times 82 \\&=123 kV \\E_{2} &=(150 kV / 100 kV ) \times 90 \\&=135 kV\end{aligned}\)

 

b. The effective value of the line current is

 

\(\begin{aligned}I_{1} &=I_{2}=(400 / 1000) \times 820 \\&=328 A\end{aligned}\)

 

c. The active power absorbed by the rectifier is

 

\(\begin{aligned}P_{1} &=150 kV \times 400 A \\&=60000 kW \\&=60 MW\end{aligned}\)

 

d. The reactive power absorbed by each converter is

 

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{1} &=(60 / 100) \times 47 \\&=28 Mvar \\Q_{2} &=(60 / 100) \times 70 \\&=42 Mvar\end{aligned}\)
