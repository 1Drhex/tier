Guest Question.

In a chemostat with cell recycle, as shown in Fig. 14.15, the feed flow rate and culture volumes are Q = 1 mL/min and V = 1000 mL, respectively. The system is operated under glucose limitation, and the yield factor, \(Y F_{ X/S }\), is 0.5 g-cells/g-substrate. Glucose concentration in the feed is \(S_{0}\) = 50 g glucose/L. The kinetic constants of the organisms are Î¼max = \(\mu_{\max }=0.2 h ^{-1}\), \(K_{ S }\) = 1 g glucose/L. The value of \(c_{ R }\) is 2.5, and the recycle ratio is R = 0.25. The system is at steady state

1. Find the substrate concentration in the recycle stream (S).
2. Find the specific growth rate (\(\mu_{\text {net }}\)) of the organisms.
3. Find the cell (biomass) concentration in the recycle stream.
4. Find the cell concentration in the centrifuge effluent (\(X_{ e }\))

Admin
Solution

In this solution, we will use the derivations already shown in the proceeding section. For students, you are expected to follow the steps from the beginning with mass balances. Using Eq. (14.52) \(\mu_{ net }=\left(1+R-R c_{ R }\right) D=\left[\left(1+R\left(1-c_{ R }\right)\right] D\right.\) , we determine \(\mu_{\text {net }}\)

 

\(\mu_{\text {net }}=\left(1+R-R c_{R}\right) D=(1+0.25-0.25 \times 2.5) \times 1 / 1000 min ^{-1}\)

 

\(=0.000625 min ^{-1}=0.0375 h ^{-1}\)

Since the endogenous maintenance is negligible, we have

\(\mu_{\text {net }}=\mu_{G}=\frac{\mu_{\max } S}{K_{S}+S}\)

which can be rearranged to give

\(S=\frac{K_{S} \mu_{\max }}{\mu_{m a x}-\mu_{n e t}}=\frac{1 \times 0.0375}{0.2-0.0375} \frac{ g }{ L }=0.23077 g / L\)

Mass balance of the substrate leads to

\(X=\frac{D}{\mu_{G}} Y F_{X / S}\left(S_{0}-S\right)=\frac{1 \div 1000}{0.000625} \times 0.25 \times(50-0.23077) \frac{ g }{ L }=39.815 g / L\)

which is the biomass concentration in the recycle stream.

A biomass balance around the separator yields

\((1+R) Q X=R c_{R} Q X+Q X_{e}\)

Thus,

\(X_{ e }=(1+R) X-R c_{ R } X=(1+0.25-0.25 \times 2.5) \times 39.815 g / L\)

= 24.8846g/L

which is the biomass concentration in the effluent stream of the chemostat system.
