Guest Question.

A closed rigid container is filled with 1.5 kg water at 100 kPa, 55°C, 1 kg of stainless steel and 0.5 kg of PVC (polyvinyl chloride) both at 20°C and 0.1 kg of air at 400 K, 100 kPa. It is now left alone with no external heat transfer and no water vaporizes. Find the final temperature and air pressure.

Admin
Solution

CV. Container.

Process: V = constant => \({ }_{1} W _{2}=0\) and also given \({ }_{1} Q _{2}=0\)

Energy Eq.: \(U _{2}- U _{1}=\sum m _{ i }\left( u _{2}- u _{1}\right)_{ i }={ }_{1} Q _{2}-{ }_{1} W _{2}=0\)

For the liquid and the metal masses we will use the specific heats (Tbl A.3, A.4)
so

\(\sum m _{ i }\left( u _{2}- u _{1}\right)_{ i }=\sum m _{ i } C _{ v i }\left( T _{2}- T _{1}\right)_{ i }= T _{2} \sum m _{ i } C _{ v i }-\sum m _{ i } C _{ v i } T _{1 i }\)

noticing that all masses have the same \(T _{2}\) but not same initial T.

\(\sum m _{ i } C _{ v i }\) = 1.5 × 4.18 + 1 × 0.46 + 0.5 × 0.96 + 0.1 × 0.717 = 7.282 kJ/K

The T for air must be converted to °C like the others.

Energy Eq.: \(T _{2} \sum m _{ i } C _{ v i }=\sum m _{ i } C _{ v i } T _{1 i }\)

\(7.282 T _{2}\) = 1.5 × 4.18 × 55 + (1 × 0.46 + 0.5 × 0.96) × 20 + 0.1 × 0.717 × (400-273.15) = 372.745 kJ

\(T _{2}=\) 51.2°C

The volume of the air is constant so from PV = mRT it follows that P varies with T

\(P _{2}= P _{1} T _{2} / T _{1 \text { air }}=100 kPa \times 324.34 K / 400 K = 8 1 kPa\)
