Declining Balance, \(B_N \lt S\)

Compute the double-declining-balance \((DDB)\) depreciation schedule for the data used in , this time with the asset having a salvage value of \(\$2,000\). Then

Cost basis of the asset, \(I = \$10,000\),

Useful life, \(N = 5\) years,

Salvage value, \(S = \$2,000\),

\(α = (\frac{1}{5})(2)=40\%\).

Admin
Solution

Given: \(I = \$10,000, S = \$2,000, N = 5\) years, and \(\alpha = 40\%\).

Find: \(D_n\) and \(B_n\) for \(n=1\) to \(5\).

The given data result in the accompanying table.

Note that \(B_4\) would be less than \(S = \$2,000\) if the full deduction \((\$864)\) had been taken. Therefore, we adjust \(D_4\) to \(\$160\), making \(B_4 = \$2,000\). \(D_5\) is zero and \(B_5\) remains at \(\$2,000\). Year \(4\) is equivalent to \(n^{′′}\) in \(Figure 9.4(b)\).

End of Year \(D_n\) \(B_n\)
1 0.4($10,000) = $4,000 $10,000 – $4,000 = $6,000
2 0.4(6,000) = 2,400 6,000 – 2,400 = 3,600
3 0.4(3,600) = 1,440 3,600 – 1,440 = 2,160
4 0.4(2,160) = 864 > 160 2,160 – 160 = 2,000
