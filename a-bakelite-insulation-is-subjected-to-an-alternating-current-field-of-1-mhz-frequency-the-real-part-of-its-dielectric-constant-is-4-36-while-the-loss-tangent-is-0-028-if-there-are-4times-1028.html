Guest Question.

A bakelite insulation is subjected to an alternating current field of 1 MHz frequency. The real part of its dielectric constant is 4.36, while the loss tangent is 0.028. If there are \(4\times 10^{28}\) atoms/\(m^{3}\) in it, deter-mine the complex polarizability of bakelite. Assume that the Lorentz field exists in insulator. Take \(\varepsilon _{0}=8.854\times 10^{-12}\) F/m.

Admin
Solution

As the Lorentz’s fi eld exists in insulator, the relationship between dielectric constant \(\varepsilon _{r}\) and polarizability p will be similar to as given by Clausius-Mossotti relation. Hence for A.C. field, Eqn. 9.38 may be expressed as

 

\(\frac{Np_{e}^{\star }}{3\varepsilon _{0}}=\frac{(\varepsilon _{r}^{\star }-1)}{(\varepsilon _{r}^{\star }+2)}\) (i)

 

where \(\varepsilon _{r}^{\star }=\varepsilon _{r}^{'}-j\varepsilon _{r}^{''}\) (ii)

 

and the loss tangent is given by

 

\(\tan \delta =\frac{\varepsilon _{r}^{''}}{\varepsilon _{r}^{'}}\) (iii)

 

in which \(\delta\) is loss angle. From Eqn. (iii), \(\varepsilon _{r}^{''}=\varepsilon _{r}^{'}\tan \delta\) . On substituting it in Eqn. (ii), we get

 

\(\varepsilon _{r}^{\star }=\varepsilon _{r}^{'}-j\varepsilon _{r}^{'}\tan \delta\) (iv)

 

i.e. \(\varepsilon _{r}^{'}\) is real and \(\varepsilon _{r}^{'}\tan \delta\) is imaginary.

 

As in the case of insulator under alternating conditions, the real part of complex quantity is a measure of ‘dielectric constant’ and the imaginary part depicts ‘dielectric losses’ of the material. Therefore [Re] quantity \(\varepsilon _{r}^{'}\) and [Im] quantity \(\varepsilon _{r}^{'}\tan \delta\) are

 

\(\varepsilon _{r}^{'}=4.36 and \varepsilon _{r}^{'}\tan \delta =0.028\)

 

i.e. \(\varepsilon _{r}^{\star }=4.36-j0.028\)

 

Now on substituting the appropriate values in Eqn. (ii), we find

 

\(\frac{4\times 10^{28}\times p_{e}^{\star }}{3\times (8.854\times 10^{-12})}=\frac{(4.36-j4.36\times 0.028)-1}{(4.36-j4.36\times 0.028)+2}\)

 

\(=\frac{3.36-j0.122}{6.36-j0.122}\)

 

\(=\frac{(3.36-j0.122)}{(6.36-j0.122)}\times \frac{(6.36+j0.122)}{(6.36+j0.122)}\)

 

\(=\frac{3.36(6.36+j0.122)-j0.122(6.36+j0.122)}{40.449+0.0140}\)

 

\(=\frac{21.369+j0.41-j0.777+0.0149}{40.464}\)

 

\(=\frac{21.384-j0.366}{40.464}\)

 

\(=0.528-j40.464\)

 

\(P_{e}^{\star }=\frac{(0.528-j40.464)\times 3\times 8.854\times 10^{-12}}{4\times 10^{28}}\)

 

\(=(3.509\times 10^{-40})-j(0.06\times 10^{-40})\)

 

or \(=(3.509-j0.06)\times 10^{-40}Fm^{2}\)
