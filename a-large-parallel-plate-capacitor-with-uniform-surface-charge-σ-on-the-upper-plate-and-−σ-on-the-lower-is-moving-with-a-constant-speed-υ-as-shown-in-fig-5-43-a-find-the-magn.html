Guest Question.

A large parallel-plate capacitor with uniform surface charge σ on the upper plate and –σ on the lower is moving with a constant speed υ, as shown in Fig. 5.43.

(a) Find the magnetic field between the plates and also above and below them.

(b) Find the magnetic force per unit area on the upper plate, including its direction.

(c) At what speed υ would the magnetic force balance the electrical force\(?^{15}\)

Admin
Solution

From , the top plate produces a field \( \mu_{0} K / 2\) (aiming out of the page, for points above it, and into the page, for points below). The bottom plate produces a field \( \mu_{0} K / 2\) (aiming into the page, for points above it, and out of the page, for points below). Above and below both plates the two fields cancel; between the plates they add up to \(\mu_{0} K\) , pointing in.

(a) \(B=\mu_{0} \sigma \upsilon \text { (in) }\) betweem the plates, B = 0 elsewhere.

(b) The Lorentz force law says \(F =\int( K \times B ) d a\) , so the force per unit area is \(f = K \times B . \text { Here } K=\sigma \upsilon \) , to the right, and B (the field of the lower plate) is \( \mu_{0} \sigma \upsilon / 2\) , into the page. So \(f_{m}=\mu_{0} \sigma^{2} \upsilon ^{2} / 2(\text { up })\) .

(c) The electric field of the lower plate is \(\sigma / 2 \epsilon_{0}\) ; the electric force per unit area on the upper plate is \( f_{e}=\sigma^{2} / 2 \epsilon_{0} \text { (down) } \) . They balance if \(\mu_{0} v^{2}=1 / \epsilon_{0}, \text { or } v=1 / \sqrt{\epsilon_{0} \mu_{0}}=c\) (the speed of light), as in .
