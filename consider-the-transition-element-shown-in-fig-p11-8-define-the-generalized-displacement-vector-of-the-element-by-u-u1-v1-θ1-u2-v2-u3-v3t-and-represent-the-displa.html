Guest Question.

Consider the (“transition”) element shown in Fig. P11.8. Define the generalized displacement vector of the element by
\(\{u\}=\left\{u_{1}, v_{1}, \Theta_{1}, u_{2}, v_{2}, u_{3}, v_{3}\right\}^{T}\)
and represent the displacement components u and v by
\(u=\psi_{1} u_{1}+\psi_{2} u_{2}+\psi_{3} u_{3}+\frac{b}{2} \eta \psi_{1} \theta_{1}, \quad v=\psi_{1} v_{1}+\psi_{2} v_{2}+\psi_{3} v_{3}\)
where \(\psi _{1}\) is the interpolation function for the beam, and \(\psi _{2}\) and \(\psi _{3}\) are the interpolation functions for nodes 2 and 3:
\(\psi_{1}=\frac{1}{2}(1-\xi), \quad \psi_{2}=\frac{1}{4}(1+\xi)(1-\eta), \quad \psi_{3}=\frac{1}{4}(1+\xi)(1+\eta)\)
Derive the stiffness matrix for the element.

Admin
Solution

The individual beam and plane elasticity (plane stress) element are shown in the figure below. Both the plan stress and beam elements have the same height b (not depicted in the figure below). Let us introduce the following nodal displacement vector:

\(\Delta =\left\{u_{1} v_{1}=w_{2} \theta_{2}=\Theta_{1} u_{2} v_{2} u_{3} v_{3}\right\}^{ T }\)

The interpolation functions associated with nodes 2 and 3 are those of the plane stress element and they are

\(\psi_{2}(\xi, \eta)=\frac{1}{4}(1+\xi)(1-\eta), \quad \psi_{3}(\xi, \eta)=\frac{1}{4}(1+\xi)(1+\eta)\) (1)

Node 3 of Fig. P11.8 is a beam node that is connected to the plane stress element. Its interpolation function is

\(\psi_{1}(\xi, \eta)=\frac{1}{2}(1-\xi)\)

The finite element approximation of the displacements \(\left(u_{x}, u_{y}\right)\) of the transitionelement are of the form

\(u_{x}=u_{1} \psi_{1}+\frac{b}{2} \eta \psi_{1} \Theta_{1}+u_{2} \psi_{2}+u_{3} \psi_{3}, \quad u_{y}=v_{1} \psi_{1}+v_{2} \psi_{2}+v_{3} \psi_{3}\) (2)

Then \(\Psi \) of Eq. (11.4.2) becomes

\(\Psi =\left[\begin{array}{ccccccc}\psi_{1} & 0 & 0.5 b \eta \psi_{1} & \psi_{2} & 0 & \psi_{3} & 0 \\0 & \psi_{1} & 0 & 0 & \psi_{2} & 0 & \psi_{3}\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(\begin{array}{lllllll} \Delta & =\left\{\begin{array}{llllllll}u_{1} & v_{1} & \Theta_{1} & u_{2} & v_{2} & u_{3} & v_{3}\end{array}\right\}^{ T }\end{array}\) (3)

The coordinate transformation is given by the usual expression, with the coordinates \(x_{1}=x_{4}=0, x_{2}=x_{3}=h, y_{1}=y_{2}=0, \text { and } y_{3}=y_{4}=b:\)

 

\(x=\sum_{i=1}^{4} x_{i} \psi_{i}=\frac{h}{2}(1+\xi), \quad y=\sum_{i=1}^{4} y_{i} \psi_{i}=\frac{b}{2}(1+\eta)\) (4)

Note that the Jacobian matrix and its inverse are

\(J =\left[\begin{array}{ll}\frac{\partial x}{\partial \xi} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial \xi} \\\frac{\partial x}{\partial \eta} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial \eta}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{cc}\frac{h}{2} & 0 \\0 & \frac{b}{2}\end{array}\right], \quad J ^{-1}=\left[\begin{array}{cc}\frac{2}{h} & 0 \\0 & \frac{2}{b}\end{array}\right]\)

and the transformation between (x, y) and \((\xi, \eta)\) is given by

\(\left\{\begin{array}{l}\frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial x} \\\frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial y}\end{array}\right\}=[J]^{-1}\left\{\begin{array}{l}\frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial \xi} \\\frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial \eta}\end{array}\right\}=\left[\begin{array}{ll}\frac{2}{h} & 0 \\0 & \frac{2}{b}\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}\frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial \xi} \\\frac{\partial \psi_{i}^{e}}{\partial \eta}\end{array}\right\}\) (5)

Finally, matrix B required to evaluate the stiffness matrix in (11.4.9) can be computed using Eq. (11.4.4):

\(B = D \Psi=\left[\begin{array}{cc}\frac{\partial}{\partial x} & 0 \\0 & \frac{\partial}{\partial y} \\\frac{\partial}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial}{\partial x}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{ccccccc}\psi_{1} & 0 & 0.5 b \eta \psi_{1} & \psi_{2} & 0 & \psi_{3} & 0 \\0 & \psi_{1} & 0 & 0 & \psi_{2} & 0 & \psi_{3}\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(=\left[\begin{array}{cc}\frac{2}{h} \frac{\partial}{\partial \xi} & 0 \\0 & \frac{2}{b} \frac{\partial}{\partial \eta} \\\frac{2}{b} \frac{\partial}{\partial \eta} & \frac{2}{h} \frac{\partial}{\partial \xi}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{ccccccc}\psi_{1} & 0 & 0.5 b \eta \psi_{1} & \psi_{2} & 0 & \psi_{3} & 0 \\0 & \psi_{1} & 0 & 0 & \psi_{2} & 0 & \psi_{3}\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(=\left[\begin{array}{ccccccc}-\frac{1}{h} & 0 & -\frac{b}{2 h} \eta & \frac{1}{2 h}(1-\eta) & 0 & \frac{1}{2 h}(1+\eta) & 0 \\0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & -\frac{1}{2 b}(1+\xi) & 0 & \frac{1}{2 b}(1+\xi) \\0 & -\frac{1}{h} & \frac{1}{2}(1-\xi) & -\frac{1}{2 b}(1+\xi) & \frac{1}{2 h}(1-\eta) & \frac{1}{2 b}(1+\xi) & \frac{1}{2 h}(1+\eta)\end{array}\right]\) (6)

 

Eq. (11.4.2) : \(\Psi=\left[\begin{array}{ccccccc}\psi_{1} & 0 & \psi_{2} & 0 & \ldots & \psi_{n} & 0 \\0 & \psi_{1} & 0 & \psi_{2} & \ldots & 0 & \psi_{n}\end{array}\right]\)

\(\Delta=\left\{\begin{array}{lllllll}u_{x}^{1} & u_{y}^{1} & u_{x}^{2} & u_{y}^{2} & \ldots & u_{x}^{n} & u_{y}^{n}\end{array}\right\}^{ T }\)

 

Eq. (11.4.4) : \(B = D \Psi=\left[\begin{array}{ccccccc}\frac{\partial \psi_{1}}{\partial x} & 0 & \frac{\partial \psi_{2}}{\partial x} & 0 & \cdots & \frac{\partial \psi_{n}}{\partial x} & 0 \\0 & \frac{\partial \psi_{1}}{\partial y} & 0 & \frac{\partial \psi_{2}}{\partial y} & \cdots & 0 & \frac{\partial \psi_{n}}{\partial y} \\\frac{\partial \psi_{1}}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial \psi_{1}}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial \psi_{2}}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial \psi_{2}}{\partial x} & \cdots & \frac{\partial \psi_{n}}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial \psi_{n}}{\partial x}\end{array}\right]\)

 

Eq. (11.4.9) : \(K ^{e}=\int_{\Omega_{e}} h_{e} B ^{ T } C B d x , \quad M ^{e}=\int_{\Omega_{e}} \rho h_{e} \Psi^{ T } \Psi d x\)

\(F ^{e}=\int_{\Omega_{e}} h_{e} \Psi^{ T } f d x , \quad Q ^{e}=\oint_{\Gamma_{e}} h_{e} \Psi^{ T } t d s\)
