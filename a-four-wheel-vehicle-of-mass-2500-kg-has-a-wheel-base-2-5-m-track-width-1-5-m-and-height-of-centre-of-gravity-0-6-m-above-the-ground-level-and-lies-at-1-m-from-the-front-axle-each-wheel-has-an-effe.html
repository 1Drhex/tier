Guest Question.

A four-wheel vehicle of mass 2500 kg has a wheel base 2.5 m, track width 1.5 m, and height of centre of gravity 0.6 m above the ground level and lies at 1 m from the front axle. Each wheel has an effective diameter of 0.8 m and a moment of inertia of 0.8 kg · m². The drive shaft, engine flywheel and transmission are rotating at four times the speed of road wheels, in clockwise direction when viewed from the front, and is equivalent to a mass of 80 kg having a radius of gyration of 100 mm. If the vehicle is taking a right turn of 60 m radius at 60 km/h, find the load on each wheel.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given: } M=2500 kg , \ell=2.5 m , a=1.5 m , h=0.6 m , \ell=1 m , r_{w}=0.4 m , I_{w}=0.8 kg \cdot m ^{2}, i=4 \),

\( M_{e}=80 kg , K_{e}=100 mm , R=60 m , v=60 km / h \).

\( \text { Let } W_{f}, W_{r}=\text { weight on the front and rear wheels, respectively. }\).

Taking moments about the front wheels (Fig.13.13), we have

\( W_{r} \times \ell=m g \ell_{1} \).

\( 2.5 W_{r}=2500 \times 9.81 \times 1 \).

\( W_{f}=9810 N \).

\( W_{f}=M g-W_{2}=2500 \times 9.81-9810=14715 N \).

\( \text { Weight on each of the front wheels } \frac{W_{f}}{2}=7357.5 N \).

\( \text { Weight on each of the rear wheels } \frac{W_{r}}{2}=4905 N \).

Gyroscopic Effect

\( v=\frac{60 \times 1000}{3600}=16.67 m / s \).

\( I_{e}=M_{e} K_{e}^{2}=80 \times(0.1)^{2}=0.8 kg \cdot m ^{2} \).

\( \omega_{w}=\frac{v}{r_{w}}=\frac{16.67}{0.4}=41.675 rad / s \).

\( \omega_{p}=\frac{v}{R}=\frac{16.67}{60}=0.2778 rad / s \).

\( C_{w}=4 I_{w} \cdot \omega_{w} \cdot \omega_{p} \).

\( =4 \times 0.8 \times 41.675 \times 0.2778=37.05 Nm \).

This gyroscopic couple tends to lift the inner wheels and depress the outer wheels. In other words, the reaction will be vertically downward on the inner wheels and vertically upward on the outer wheels.
The magnitude of this reaction at each of the inner or outer wheels,

\( \frac{P}{2}=\frac{C_{w}}{2 a}=\frac{37.05}{3}=12.35 N \).

\( C_{e}=I_{e} \cdot i \omega_{w} \cdot \omega_{p} \).

\( =0.8 \times 4 \times 41.675 \times 0.2778=37.05 Nm \).

This gyroscopic couple tends to lift the front wheels and depress the rear wheels. In other words, the reaction will be vertically downwards on the front wheels and vertically upwards on the rear wheels. The magnitude of this reaction at each of the front or rear wheels,

\( \frac{F}{2}=\frac{C_{e}}{2 \ell}=\frac{37.05}{5}=7.01 N \).

Centrifugal couple

Centrifugal force, \( F_{c}=\frac{M v^{2}}{R}=\frac{2500 \times(16.67)^{2}}{60}=11578.7 N \).

\( C_{c}=F_{c} \cdot h=11578.7 \times 0.6=6947.2 Nm \).

The reactions due to this couple are vertically downwards on the inner wheels and vertically upwards on the outer wheels. The magnitude of this reaction on each of the inner and outer wheels,

\( \frac{Q}{2}=\frac{C_{c}}{2 a}=\frac{6947.2}{3}=2315.7 N \).

Load on the inner front wheel \( \frac{W_{f}}{2}-\frac{P}{2}-\frac{F}{2}-\frac{Q}{2} \).

\( =7357.5-12.35-7.01-2315.7=5022.44 N \).

\( \text { Load on the front outer wheel }=\frac{W_{f}}{2}+\frac{P}{2}-\frac{F}{2}+\frac{Q}{2}\).

\( =7357.5+12.35-7.01+2315.7=9678.54 N \).

\( \text { Load on the rear inner wheel } \frac{W_{r}}{2}-\frac{P}{2}-\frac{F}{2}-\frac{Q}{2} \).

\( =4905-12.35+7.01-2315.7=25814.96 N \).

\( \text { Load on the rear outer wheel }=\frac{W_{r}}{2}+\frac{P}{2}+\frac{F}{2}+\frac{Q}{2} \).

\( =4905+12.35+7.01+2315.7=7240.06 N \).
