Guest Question.

AVERAGE VELOCITIES OF A SLIDER CRANK MECHANISM

For the slider crank mechanism shown in Figure 2.20(a) with dimensions and crank angular speed we have
r\(_1\)=2.0cm r\(_2\)=3.5 cm r\(_3\)=10.0cm \(\dot θ_2\)=20.0 rad/sec CCW (constant)
determine the average speed of the slider to the right, and to the left.

Admin
Solution

From Equation (2.8-2) we obtain

\(\alpha _1=\sin ^{-1}\left\lgroup \frac{r_1}{r_2+r_3} \right\rgroup , \ \alpha _2=\sin ^{-1}\left\lgroup \frac{r_1}{r_3-r_2} \right\rgroup\) (2.8-2)

\(\alpha _1 =\sin ^{-1}\left\lgroup \frac{2.0}{3.5+10.0} \right\rgroup =8.52°\)

\(\alpha _2=17.92°\)

From Equation (2.8-1)

\(∆θ_2 = 180° +α_2 − α_1\) (2.8-1)

\( = 189.40° = 3.31\) rad

Employing Equation (2.8-4), the stroke of the slider is

\(stroke=s_1-s_2=[(r_2+r_3)^2-r_1^2]^{1/2}-[(r_3-r_2)-r_1^2]^{1/2}\) (2.8-4)

\(=7.17\) cm

Employing Equations (2.8-8) and (2.8-9) yields

\((v_{4,avg})_{left}=\frac{strok}{\Delta t_1}=\frac{strok}{\left\lgroup\frac{\Delta \theta _2}{\dot \theta _2}\right\rgroup }\) (8.2-8)

\(=\frac{7.17}{\left\lgroup\frac{3.31}{20.0}\right\rgroup }=43.35\) cm/sec

\((v_{4,avg})_{right}=\frac{strok}{\Delta t_2}=\frac{strok}{\left\lgroup\frac{2 \pi -\Delta \theta _2}{\dot \theta _2}\right\rgroup }\) (2.8-9)

\((v_{4,avg})_{right}=48.14\) cm/sec
