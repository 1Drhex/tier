Guest Question.

The condenser in a power plant cools 10 kg/s water at 10 kPa, quality 90% so it comes out as saturated liquid at 10 kPa. The cooling is done by ocean-water coming in at ambient 15°C and returned to the ocean at 20°C. Find the transfer out of the water and the transfer into the ocean-water of both energy and exergy (4 terms).

Admin
Solution

C.V. Water line. No work but heat transfer out.

Energy Eq.: \(\dot{Q}_{\text {out }}=\dot{ m }\left( h _{1}- h _{2}\right)=10(2345.35-191.81)= 2 1 5 3 5 k W\)

C.V. Ocean water line. No work but heat transfer in equals water heattransfer out

Energy Eq.: \(q = h _{4}- h _{3}=83.94-62.98=20.96 kJ / kg\)

\(\dot{ m }_{\text {ocean }}=\dot{Q}_{\text {out }} / q =21535 / 20.96=1027.4 kg / s\)

Exergy out of the water follows from a flow term in Eq.8.38

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{\Phi}_{\text {out }} &=\dot{m}\left(\psi_{1}-\psi_{2}\right)=\dot{ m }\left[ h _{1}- h _{2}- T _{ o }\left( s _{1}- s _{2}\right)\right] \\&=10[2345.35-191.81-288.15(7.4001-0.6492)] \\&= 2 0 8 2 . 3 k W\end{aligned}\)

Exergy into the ocean water

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{\Phi}_{\text {ocean }}=\dot{ m }_{\text {ocean }}\left(\psi_{4}-\psi_{3}\right)=\dot{ m }_{\text {ocean }}\left[ h _{4}- h _{3}- T _{o}\left( s _{4}- s _{3}\right)\right] \\&=1027.4[20.96-288.15(0.2966-0.2245)] \\&= 1 8 9 . 4 k W\end{aligned}\)

Notice there is a large amount of energy exchanged but very little exergy.

\(\frac{d \Phi}{d t}=\sum\left(1-\frac{T_{0}}{T}\right) \dot{Q}_{ c.v . }\) Transfer by heat at T
