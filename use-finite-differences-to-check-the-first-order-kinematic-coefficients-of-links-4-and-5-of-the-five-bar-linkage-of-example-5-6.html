Guest Question.

Use finite differences to check the first-order kinematic coefficients of links 4 and 5 of the five-bar linkage of .

Admin
Solution

By Eq. (5.23), the finite change in the angular displacement of link 4 can be written as

\(\left[\left(\theta_{i}\right)_{A}-\left(\theta_{i}\right)_{B}\right] \approx \theta_{i j}^{\prime}\left[\left(\theta_{j}\right)_{A}-\left(\theta_{j}\right)_{B}\right]+\theta_{i k}^{\prime}\left[\left(\theta_{k}\right)_{A}-\left(\theta_{k}\right)_{B}\right] .\) (5.23)

 

\(\Delta \theta_{4}=\left[\left(\theta_{4}\right)_{A}-\left(\theta_{4}\right)_{B}\right] \approx \theta_{42}^{\prime}\left[\left(\theta_{2}\right)_{A}-\left(\theta_{2}\right)_{B}\right]+\theta_{43}^{\prime}\left[\left(\theta_{3}\right)_{A}-\left(\theta_{3}\right)_{B}\right]\) (1)

and the finite change in the angular displacement of link 5 can be written as

\(\Delta \theta_{5}=\left[\left(\theta_{5}\right)_{A}-\left(\theta_{5}\right)_{B}\right] \approx \theta_{52}^{\prime}\left[\left(\theta_{2}\right)_{A}-\left(\theta_{2}\right)_{B}\right]+\theta_{53}^{\prime}\left[\left(\theta_{3}\right)_{A}-\left(\theta_{3}\right)_{B}\right]\) (2)

 

For the input angles θ2 = 120° and θ3 = 45° (row nine of Table 5.1), the first-order kinematic coefficients are

\(\theta_{42}^{\prime}=-0.237, \quad \theta_{43}^{\prime}=0.705, \quad \theta_{52}^{\prime}=0.455, \quad \text { and } \quad \theta_{53}^{\prime}=-0.109 .\)

Substituting these values into Eqs. (1) and (2), the finite change in the angular displacements of links 4 and 5, respectively, are

\(\Delta\theta_{4}=\left[30.526^{\circ}-42.360^{\circ}\right]\approx-0.237\left[130^{\circ}-110^{\circ}\right]+0.705\left[40^{\circ}-50^{\circ}\right]\) (3)

 

\(\Delta\theta_{5}=\left[156.510^{\circ}-146.315^{\circ}\right]\approx0.455\left[130^{\circ}-110^{\circ}\right]-0.109\left[40^{\circ}-50^{\circ}\right] .\) (4)

Note that the answers given by the two separate calculations in Eqs. (3) and (4) are in reasonable agreement with each other. If the increments of the two input displacements are decreased, then even closer agreement between the two sets of calculations is obtained. The general conclusion, therefore, is that the first-order kinematic coefficients of the five-bar linkage as indicated by Table 5.1 are correct.
