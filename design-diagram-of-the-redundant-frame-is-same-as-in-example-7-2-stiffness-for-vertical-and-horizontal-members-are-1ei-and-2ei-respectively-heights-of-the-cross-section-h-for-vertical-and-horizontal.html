Guest Question.

Design diagram of the redundant frame is same as in. Stiffness for vertical and horizontal members are 1EI and 2EI, respectively. Heights of the cross section h for vertical and horizontal members are 0.60 m. The frame is subjected to temperature changes as presented in Fig. 7.20a. Construct the internal force diagrams and calculate reactions of supports.

Admin
Solution

Let us accept a primary system as shown in Fig. 7.20b.

Canonical equations of the force method are

\( \delta _{11}X_1 + \delta _{12}X_2 + \Delta _{1t}= 0,\)

 

\( \delta _{21}X_1 + \delta _{22}X_2 + \Delta _{2t}= 0,\) (a)

where \(\delta _{ik}\) are unit displacements and \(Δ_{1t}\) and \(Δ_{2t}\) are displacements in a primary system in the direction of primary unknowns \(X_1\) and \(X_2\), respectively, due to change of temperature.

The unit states and corresponding bending moment and axial force diagrams caused by unit primary unknowns are presented in Fig. 7.20c. All normal forces due to \(\bar{X}_2 = 1\) are zero, i.e., \(\bar{N}_2 =0\).

The unit displacements \(\delta _{ik}\) for this primary system have been obtained in ; they are

\(\delta _{11}= \frac{666.67}{EI}, \delta _{12}=\delta _{21}=\frac{275}{EI}, \delta _{22}= \frac{170:67}{EI}.\) (b)

For calculation of free terms we will use expressions (7.18a).

\(\Delta _{it}=\sum{\alpha t_{av}}\int{\bar{N}_ids }+\sum{\alpha \frac{\Delta t}{h} }\int{\bar{M}_ids. }\) (7.18a)

These expressions contain the average temperature \(t_{av}\) and temperature gradient Δt for each member;
they are

For portion 1-3

\(t_{av}=\frac{20 +(- 10)}{2} = 5°; \Delta t = | – 10 -(+20)|=30°,\)

for portion 4-5

\(t_{av}=\frac{0 +(- 10)}{2} = – 5° \Delta t = | – 10 -0|=10°,\) (c)

for portion 6-8

\(t_{av}=\frac{0 +20}{2} = – 10° \Delta t = | 0- (+20)|=+20°,\)

These parameters for each element of the frame are shown on the primary system in Fig. 7.20b. Detailed calculation of free terms is presented in Table 7.13.

The first term in (7.18a) is positive if normal force in the unit state and temperature of the neutral fiber \(t_{av}\) has the same sign (for example, for element 1-3, normal force in the first unit state equals +1 and average temperature equals +5). The second term in (7.18a) is positive if the bending moment diagram in the unit state is located on the side of more heated fibers. For member 1-3, the first component is \(\Delta _{1t} =\alpha \times 5\times 1\times 5,\) where 1 ×5 presents the area of the axial force diagram along this member.

Canonical equations become

\(666.67X_1 + 275X_2 + 4, 191.7\alpha \cdot EI = 0,\)

 

\(275X_1 + 170.67X_2 + 1,450\alpha \cdot EI = 0.\) (d)

Kinematical verification The summary unit bending moment diagram \(\bar{M}_\Sigma\) is shown in Fig. 7.20e.

The expression (7.21)

\(\sum{\int{\frac{M_t\cdot \bar{M_\Sigma } }{EI} ds} }+\sum{\Delta _{it}}=0,\) (7.21)

leads to the following result:

\(\sum{\int{\frac{M_t\cdot \bar{M_\Sigma } }{EI} ds} }+\sum{\Delta _{it}}= -\frac{5}{1EI\times 6} \left(18\times 43.99+4\times \frac{13+18}{2}\times \frac{43.99+68.37}{2}+13\times 68.37 \right)\cdot \alpha EI – \underbrace{\frac{1}{1EI}\times \frac{1}{2}\times 3\times 3\times \frac{2}{3}\times (- 14.628)\alpha EI }_{portion 4-5} – \underbrace{\frac{1}{2EI}\times \frac{1}{2 }\times 10\times 10\times \frac{2}{3} \times 82.99\alpha EI }_{portion 6-8}+\underbrace{4,191.7\alpha }_{\Delta _{1t}} + \underbrace{1,450\alpha }_{\Delta _{2t}}= – 5,686.32\alpha + 5,685.32\cong 0.\)

Summary: Distribution of internal forces (bending moment, shear force and normal
force) as well as reactions depends on both relative and absolute stiffnesses, as well as on coefficient of thermal expansion. This is the property of any statically indeterminate structure subjected to change of temperature.
