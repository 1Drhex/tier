Guest Question.

You want to supplement your retirement income through IRA contributions. You have 15 years left until retirement and you are going to make 15 equal annual deposits into your IRA until you retire with the first deposit being made at the end of year 1. You need to save enough so that you can make 10 annual withdrawals that will begin at the end of year 16. The first withdrawal will be $10,000, and each subsequent withdrawal will increase at a rate of 4% over the previous year’s withdrawal in line with expected increase in cost‐of‐living. Your last withdrawal will be at the end of year 25. What is the amount of the equal annual deposit amount ( C ) for the first 15 years? Assume the interest rate is 8% compounded annually before and after you retire.

Given: i = 8% per year, deposit and withdrawal series shown in Figure 2.34a .

SOLUTION

First, compute the equivalent worth of the total deposit series at n = 0.

\(P_{deposit}\) = C(P/A, 8%, 15) = 8.5595C.

Find the equivalent single lump‐sum withdrawal now in two steps by finding the equivalent amount at n = 15 and then bring this value back to n = 0:

Discounting factor

\(P_{withdrawal} = \overbrace{\$ 10,000\underbrace{(P/A_{1},4 \% , 8 \% , 10)}_{7.8590} }^{V_{15}} \underbrace{(P/F, 8 \% , 15)}_{0.3152}\)

Since the two amounts are equivalent, by equating, we obtain C .

8.5595C = $24,771.60
C = $2,894.

2) First, compute the accumulated balance at the end of 15 years. Call this amount \(F_{15}\).

\(F_{15}\)= C(F/A, 8%, 15) = $27.1521C.

Then find the equivalent lump‐sum withdrawal at the end of retirement
contribution, n = 15. Call this \(V_{15}\) :

\(V_{15}\) = $10,000(P/\(A_{1}\),4%,8%,15) = $78,590.

Since the two amounts must be the same, \(F_{15}\) = \(V_{15}\) , we obtain

27.1521C = $78,590
C = $2,894.

COMMENTS: In general, Method 2 is the more efficient way to obtain an equivalence solution to this type of decision problem as it requires fewer interest factors.
