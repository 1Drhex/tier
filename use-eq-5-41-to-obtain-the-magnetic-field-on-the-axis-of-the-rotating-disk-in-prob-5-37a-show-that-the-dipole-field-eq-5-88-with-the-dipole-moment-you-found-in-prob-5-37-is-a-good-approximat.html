Guest Question.

Use Eq. 5.41 to obtain the magnetic field on the axis of the rotating disk in . Show that the dipole field (Eq. 5.88), with the dipole moment you found in , is a good approximation if z ≫ R.

\(B(z)=\frac{\mu_{0} I}{4 \pi}\left(\frac{\cos \theta}{ᴫ^{2}}\right) 2 \pi R=\frac{\mu_{0} I}{2} \frac{R^{2}}{\left(R^{2}+z^{2}\right)^{3 / 2}} \) (5.41)

\(B _{ dip }( r )= \nabla \times A =\frac{\mu_{0} m}{4 \pi r^{3}}(2 \cos \theta \hat{ r }+\sin \theta \hat{ \theta })\) (5.88)

Admin
Solution

The total charge on the shaded ring is \(d q=\sigma(2 \pi r) d r\) . The time for one revolution is \(d t=2 \pi / \omega\) . So the current in the ring is \(I=\frac{d q}{d t}=\sigma \omega r d r\) . From Eq. 5.41, the magnetic field of this ring (for points on the axis) is \(d B =\frac{\mu_{0}}{2} \sigma \omega r \frac{r^{2}}{\left(r^{2}+z^{2}\right)^{3 / 2}} d r \hat{ z }\) , and the total field of the disk is

\(B =\frac{\mu_{0} \sigma \omega}{2} \int_{0}^{R} \frac{r^{3} d r}{\left(r^{2}+z^{2}\right)^{3 / 2}} \hat{ z } . \quad \text { Let } u \equiv r^{2}, \text { so } d u=2 r d r\) . Then

\(=\frac{\mu_{0} \sigma \omega}{4} \int_{0}^{R^{2}} \frac{u d u}{\left(u+z^{2}\right)^{3 / 2}}=\left.\frac{\mu_{0} \sigma \omega}{4}\left[2\left(\frac{u+2 z^{2}}{\sqrt{u+z^{2}}}\right)\right]\right|_{0} ^{R^{2}}=\frac{\mu_{0} \sigma \omega}{2}\left[\frac{\left(R^{2}+2 z^{2}\right)}{\sqrt{R^{2}+z^{2}}}-2 z\right] \hat{ z }\) .

When z ≫ R, the term in square brackets is

\([ ]=\frac{2 z^{2}\left(1+R^{2} / 2 z^{2}\right)}{z\left[1+(R / z)^{2}\right]^{-1 / 2}}-2 z \approx 2 z\left[\left(1+\frac{R^{2}}{2 z^{2}}\right)\left(1-\frac{1}{2} \frac{R^{2}}{z^{2}}+\frac{3}{8} \frac{R^{4}}{z^{4}}\right)-1\right]\)

 

\(\approx 2 z\left(1-\frac{R^{2}}{2 z^{2}}+\frac{3}{8} \frac{R^{4}}{z^{4}}+\frac{R^{2}}{2 z^{2}}-\frac{R^{4}}{4 z^{4}}-1\right)=2 z\left(\frac{1}{8} \frac{R^{4}}{z^{4}}\right)=\frac{R^{4}}{4 z^{4}}\) ,

so

\(B \approx \frac{\mu_{0} \sigma \omega}{2} \frac{R^{4}}{4 z^{4}} \hat{ z }=\frac{\mu_{0} \sigma \omega R^{4}}{8 z^{3}} \hat{ z }\) .

Meanwhile, from Eq. 5.88, the dipole field is

\(B _{\text {dip }}=\frac{\mu_{0} m}{4 \pi r^{3}}(2 \cos \theta \hat{ r }+\sin \theta \hat{ \theta })\) ,

and for points on the \(+z \text { axis } \theta=0, r=z, \hat{ r }=\hat{ z }, \text { so } B _{\text {dip }}=\frac{\mu_{0} m}{2 \pi z^{3}} \hat{ z } \) . In this case (Problem 5.37a) \(m=\pi \sigma \omega R^{4} / 4\) ,

so \(B _{ dip }=\frac{\mu_{0} \sigma \omega R^{4}}{8 z^{3}} \hat{ z }\) , in agreement with the approximation.
