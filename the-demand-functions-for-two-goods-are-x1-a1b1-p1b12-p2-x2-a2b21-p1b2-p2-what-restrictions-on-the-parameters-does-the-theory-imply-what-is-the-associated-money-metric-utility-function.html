Guest Question.

The demand functions for two goods are

\( x_1 = a_1+b_1 p_1+b_{12} p_2 \) ,

\( x_2 = a_2+b_{21} p_1+b_2 p_2 \),

What restrictions on the parameters does the theory imply? What is the associated money metric utility function?

Admin
Solution

Now the budget constraint is given by \( z + p_1x_1 + p_2x_2 = m \). The symmetry of the substitution effects implies

\( \frac{∂x_1}{∂p_2} \frac{∂x_2}{∂p_1} ⇒ b_{12} = b_{21} \).

The negative semidefiniteness of the substitution matrix implies \( b_1 < 0 \) and \( b_1b_2−b^2 > 0 \). (Prove that these two conditions together imply that \( b_2 < 0 \) must also hold.)
We have to solve the following system of partial differential equations

\( \frac{∂ μ (P;q,m)}{∂ p_1} = a_1 + b_1p_1 + bp_2 \),

and

\( \frac{∂ μ (P;q,m)}{∂ p_2} = a_2 + bp_1 + b_2p_2 \),

The first equation implies

\( μ (P;q,m) = a_1p_1 + \frac{b_1}{2} p^2_1 +bp_1p_2 + C_1 \),

where \( C_1 \) is a constant of integration. The second implies

\( μ (P;q,m) = a_2p_2 + \frac{b_2}{2} p^2_2 +bp_1p_2 + C_2 \),

Therefore, we must have

\( μ (P;q,m) = a_1p_1 + \frac{b_1}{2} p^2_1 +bp_1p_2 + a_2p_2 + \frac{b_2}{2} p^2_2 + C \).

\( = [p_1,p_2] \biggl[\begin{matrix} a_{1} \\ a_{2} \end{matrix} \biggr] + \frac{1}{2} [p_1,p_2] \biggl[\begin{matrix} b_{1} & b\\ b & b_2 \end{matrix} \biggr] \biggl[\begin{matrix} p_1 \\ p_2 \end{matrix} \biggr] +C \).

Using μ(q; q,m) = m, we have

\( μ (P;q,m) = m + a_1 (p_1 +q_1) + \frac{b_1}{2} (p^2_1 -q^2_1) + b (p_1p_2 −q_1q_2) +a_2(p_2 −q_2) + \frac{b_2}{2} (p^2_2 -q^2_2) \).

The indirect utility function is given by

\( υ(q,m) =m−a_1q_1 − \frac{b_1}{2} q^2_1 −bq_1q_2 − a_2q_2 −\frac{b_2}{2} q^2_2 \).

\( = m – [q_1,q_2] \biggl[\begin{matrix} a_{1} \\ a_{2} \end{matrix} \biggr] -\frac{1}{2} [q_1,q_2] \biggl[\begin{matrix} b_{1} & b\\ b & b_2 \end{matrix} \biggr] \biggl[\begin{matrix} q_1 \\ q_2 \end{matrix} \biggr] \).
