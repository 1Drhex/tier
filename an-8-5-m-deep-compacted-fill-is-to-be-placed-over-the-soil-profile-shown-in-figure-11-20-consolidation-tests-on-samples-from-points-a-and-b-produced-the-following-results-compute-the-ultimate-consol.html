Guest Question.

An 8.5 m deep compacted fill is to be placed over the soil profile shown in Figure 11.20. Consolidation tests on samples from points A and B produced the following results:

Sample A Sample B
\(C_{c}\) 0.25 0.20
\(C_{r}\) 0.08 0.06
\(e_{0}\) 0.66 0.45
\(\sigma ^{\prime }_{c}\) 101 kPa 510 kPa
Admin
Solution

Using Equation 11.2:

\(\sigma ^{\prime }_{zf}=\sigma ^{\prime }_{z0}+\gamma _{fill}H_{fill}=\sigma ^{\prime }_{z0}+\left(20.3 kN/m^{3}\right) \left(8.5 m\right) =\sigma ^{\prime }_{z0}+172.6 kPa\)

Applying Equation 10.34 at sample A:

\(\sigma ^{\prime }_{z0}=\sum{\gamma H – u} =\left(18.3 kN/m^{3}\right) \left(2.0 m\right) +\left(19.0 kN/m^{3}\right) \left(2.0 m\right) – \left(9.8 kN/m^{3}\right) \left(2.0 m\right) =55.0 kPa\)

 

\(\sigma ^{\prime }_{zf}=\sigma ^{\prime }_{z0}+172.6 kPa=55.0 kPa+172.6 kPa=227.6 kPa\)

\(\sigma ^{\prime }_{z0}<\sigma ^{\prime }_{c}≤\sigma ^{\prime }_{zf}\) ∴ overconsolidated case II

\(\sigma ^{\prime }_{m}=\sigma ^{\prime }_{c}-\sigma ^{\prime }_{z}=101 – 55=46 kPa\)

Therefore, \(\sigma ^{\prime }_{c}\) at any depth in the stiff silty clay stratum is equal to \(\sigma ^{\prime }_{z}+46 kPa\).

At sample B:

\(\sigma ^{\prime }_{z0}=\sum{\gamma H – u} =\left(18.3 kN/m^{3}\right) \left(2.0 m\right) +\left(19.0 kN/m^{3}\right) \left(7.0 m\right) +\left(19.5 kN/m^{3}\right) \left(10.0 m\right) – \left(9.8 kN/m^{3}\right) \left(17.0 m\right) =198.0 kPa\)

 

\(\sigma ^{\prime }_{zf}=\sigma ^{\prime }_{z0}+\gamma _{f}H_{f}=198.0 kPa+172.6 kPa=370.6 kPa\)

\(\sigma ^{\prime }_{z0}<\sigma ^{\prime }_{c}\) and \(\sigma ^{\prime }_{zf}≤\sigma ^{\prime }_{c}\) ∴ overconsolidated case I

Notice how most of the compression occurs in the upper stratum, which is overconsolidated case II (i.e., some of the compression occurs along the virgin curve). The lower stratum, which is overconsolidated case II. has much less compression even though it is twice as thick because it is overconsolidated case I and all of the compression occurs on the recompression curve.
