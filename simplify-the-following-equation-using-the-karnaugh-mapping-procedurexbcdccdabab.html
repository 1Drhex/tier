Guest Question.

Simplify the following equation using the Karnaugh mapping procedure:

\( X = \overline{B} \left(CD+\overline{C} \right) +C\overline{D} \left(\overline{A+B}+AB \right) \)
Admin
Solution

Before filling in the K-map, an SOP expression must be formed:

\( X =\overline{B} CD+\overline{B} \overline{C} +C\overline{D} \left(\overline{A}\overline{B} +AB \right)\)

 

\(=\overline{B} CD+ \overline{B} \overline{C} +\overline{A} \overline{B} C\overline{D} +ABC\overline{D} \)

The group of four 1’s can be encircled to form \( \overline{A} \overline{B} \), as shown in Figure 93. Another group of four can be encircled using wraparound to form \( \overline{B} \overline{C} \). That leaves two 1’s that are not combined with any others. The unattached 1 in the bottom row can be combined within a group of four, as shown, to form \( \overline{B} D \). The last 1 is not adjacent to any other, so it must be encircled by itself to form \( ABC\overline{D} \). The final simplified equation is \( X=\overline{A} \overline{B} +\overline{B} \overline{C} +\overline{B} D+ABC\overline{D} \)
