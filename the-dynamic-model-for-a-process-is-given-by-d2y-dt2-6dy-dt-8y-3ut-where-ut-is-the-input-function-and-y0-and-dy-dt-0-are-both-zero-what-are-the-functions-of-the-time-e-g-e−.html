Guest Question.

The dynamic model for a process is given by

 

\(\frac{d^{2} y}{d t^{2}}+6 \frac{d y}{d t}+8 y=3 u(t)\)

 

where u(t) is the input function and y(0) and dy/dt (0) are both zero.

What are the functions of the time \(\left(\text { e.g.}, e^{-t / \tau}\right)\) in the solution for each of the following cases?

(a) \(u(t)=b e^{-2 t}\)

 

(b) u(t) = ct

b and c are constants.

Note: You do not have to find y(t) in these cases. Just determine the functions of time that will appear in y(t).

Admin
Solution

(a) Laplace transform on the ODE gives:

 

\(\mathcal{L} \left(\frac{d^{2} y}{d t^{2}}\right)+6 \mathcal{L} \left(\frac{d y}{d t}\right)+8 \mathcal{L} (y)=3 b \mathcal{L} \left(e^{-2 t}\right)\)

 

\(s^{2} Y(s)-s y(0)-y^{\prime}(0)+6 s Y(s)-6 y(0)+8 Y(s)=3 b \frac{1}{s+2}\)

 

\(s^{2} Y(s)+6 s Y(s)+8 Y(s)=\frac{3 b}{s+2}\)

 

Thus:

 

\(Y(s)=\frac{3 b}{(s+2)\left(s^{2}+6 s+8\right)}=\frac{3 b}{(s+2)^{2}(s+4)}=\frac{a_{1}}{(s+2)^{2}}+\frac{a_{2}}{(s+2)}+\frac{a_{3}}{(s+4)}\)

 

Regardless of the numerical values of \(a_{1}, a_{2}\) and \(a_{3}\), the inverse Laplace transform indicates that y(t) includes \(e^{-2 t}, t e^{-2 t}\), and \(e^{-4 t}\).

(b) When u = ct, Laplace transform gives:

 

\(s^{2} Y(s)+6 s Y(s)+8 Y(s)=\frac{3 c}{s^{2}}\)

 

\(Y(s)=\frac{3 c}{s^{2}(s+2)(s+4)}=\frac{a_{1}}{s}+\frac{a_{2}}{s^{2}}+\frac{a_{3}}{(s+2)}+\frac{a_{4}}{(s+4)}\)

 

Regardless of the numerical values of \(a_{1}, a_{2}, a_{3}\), and \(a_{4}\), the inverse Laplace transform indicates y(t) includes \(a_{1}, t, e^{-2 t}\), and \(e^{-4 t}\).
