Guest Question.

A 230V, 50Hz, 5kW electric motor is fed from a distribution board containing BS 88 Part 2 fuses. The wiring between the d.f.b. and the motor starter which is 20m distant is p.v.c.-insulated single-core cables drawn into steel conduit.
Assume that the:
(i) starter affords overload protection;
(ii) motor has a power factor of 0.75 and an efficiency of 80\(\%\);
(iii) ambient temperature is 40°C;
(iv) fuse in the d.f.b. may have a rating up to twice the rating of
the circuit cables;
(v) volts drop in the motor circuit cables must not exceed 6V;
(vi) resistance of metal conduit is 0.1\(\Omega\) per metre;
(vii) ‘worst’ conduit run is 8m with two 90° bends;
(viii) \(I_{p}\) at d.f.b. is 2kA;
(ix) value of Ze is 0.19\(\Omega \).
Establish the:
(a) design current\( (I_{b} )\);
(b) rating of circuit fuse;
(c) minimum cable rating \((I_{n}) \)between d.f.b. and starter;
(d) minimum cable cross-sectional area;
(e) actual voltage drop in cables;
(f ) prospective short circuit current;
(g) short circuit disconnection time;
(h) whether BS 7671 requirement 434-03-03, etc. is satisfied;
(i) whether BS 7671 requirement 413-02-04, etc. is satisfied;
( j) minimum conduit size.

Admin
Solution

(a) Design current \(I_{b}=\frac{5000}{230\times 0.75\times 0.8} \)= 36.2A
(b) Rating of circuit fuse may be 80A.
(c) Minimum cable rating may be 40A.
(d) Minimum cable c.s.a. = \(\frac{40}{0.87} \)=46A
from Table 4D1A select \(10mm^{2} \)cable (57A).
(e) Actual voltage drop:
from Table 4D1BmV/A/m value for \(10mm^{2} \) is 4.4 thus
volts drop = \(\frac{36.2\times 20\times 4.4}{1000} \) =3.19V
(f ) Impedance of supply cables to d.f.b.
= \(\frac{230}{2000}=0.115\Omega \)
Using BS 7671 Tables 9A and 9C, resistance of circuit cables
= \(\frac{20\times 3.66\times 1.2}{1000} = 0.09\Omega \)
thus total circuit impedance = 0.115 + 0.09 = 0.205 \(\Omega \)
Prospective short circuit current = \(\frac{230}{0.205} \)=1122 A
(g) Disconnection time from Figure 3.3A is 0.1 s
(h) Cable thermal capacity t = \(\frac{k^{2}\times S^{2}}{I^{2}}=\frac{115^{2}\times 10}{1122^{2}} \)= 1.05 s
Thus 10 \(mm^{2} \) cables are thermally safe.
(i) Now Zs = Ze + R1 + R2
Using BS 7671 Tables 9A and 9C,
Resistance of R1 = \(\frac{20\times 1.83}{1000}=0.0366\Omega \)
Resistance of conduit R2 = 20 × 0.01 = 0.2 \(\Omega \)
Thus Zs = 0.19 + 0.0366 + 0.2 = 0.4266 \(\Omega \)
\(I_{ef}=\frac{230}{0.4366} \)= 539A
From BS 7671, Figure 3.3A, disconnection time is 1.4s; this
being less than 5s, protection is satisfactory.
( j) From Table 5C,
cable factor for 2 ×10 \(mm^{2} \)cables = 2 × 105 = 210 From Table 5D select 25mm conduit (factor 292).
