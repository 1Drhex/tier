Guest Question.

Find the temperature \(T_{c},\) pressure \(p_{c},\) and volume \(V_{c}\) at the critical point of a van der Waals gas, and calculate the ratio \(p_{c}V_{c}/RT_{c}.\)

Admin
Solution

The equation of state for one mole of van der Waals gas can be rewritten with p as the subject as follows:

\( p=\frac{RT}{V-b}-\frac{a}{V^{2}}.\) (26.12)

The point of inflection can be found by using

\( \left(\frac{\partial p}{\partial V} \right)_{T}=-\frac{RT}{(V-b)^{2}}+\frac{2a}{V^{3}}=0\) (26.13)
and
\( \left(\frac{\partial ^{2} p}{\partial V^{2}} \right)_{T}=\frac{2RT}{(V-b)^{3}}-\frac{6a}{V^{4}}=0.\) (26.14)

Equation 26.13 implies that

\( RT=\frac{2a(V-b)^{2}}{V^{3}},\) (26.15)

while eqn 26.14 implies that

\( RT=\frac{3a(V-b)^{3}}{V^{4}},\) (26.16)

and equating these last two equations gives

\( \frac{3(V-b)}{V}=2,\) (26.17)

which implies that \(V = V_{c},\) where \(V_{c}\) is the critical volume given by

\( V_{c} = 3b.\) (26.18)

Substituting this back into eqn 26.14 yields \(RT = 8a/27b\) and hence \(T = T_{c}\) where \(T_{c}\) is the critical temperature given by

\( T_{c}=\frac{8a}{27Rb}.\) (26.19)

Substituting our expressions for \(V_{c}\) and \(T_{c}\) back into the equation of state for a van der Waals gas gives the critical pressure \(p_{c}\) as

\( p_{c}=\frac{a}{27b^{2}}.\) (26.20)
We then have that
\( \frac{p_{c}V_{c}}{RT_{c}}=\frac{3}{8}=0.375,\) (26.21)

independent of both a and b. At the critical point,

\( \left(\frac{\partial p}{\partial V} \right)_{T_{c}}=0,\) (26.22)

and hence the isothermal compressibility diverges since

\( k_{T}=-\frac{1}{V}(\partial V/\partial p )_{T}\rightarrow \infty .\) (26.23)
