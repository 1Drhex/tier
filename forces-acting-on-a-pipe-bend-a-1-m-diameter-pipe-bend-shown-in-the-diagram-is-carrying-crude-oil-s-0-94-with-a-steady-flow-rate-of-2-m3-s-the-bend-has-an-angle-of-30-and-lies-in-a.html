Guest Question.

FORCES ACTING ON A PIPE BEND

A 1 m–diameter pipe bend shown in the diagram is carrying crude oil (S = 0.94) with a steady flow rate of 2 \(m ^{3} / s\). The bend has an angle of 30° and lies in a horizontal plane. The volume of oil in the bend is 1.2 \(m ^{3}\), and the empty weight of the bend is 4 kN. Assume the pressure along the centerline of the bend is constant with a value of 75 kPa gage. Find the net force required to hold the bend in place.

Sketch:

Problem Definition

Situation: Crude oil flows through a 30° pipe bend.

Find: Force (in kN) required to hold bend in place.

Assumptions: Pressure is constant through bend.

Properties: \(S_{\text {oil }}=0.94\).

Plan

From the continuity equation, the inlet and outlet mass flows are the same.

1. Select a control volume that accommodates the pressure forces and reaction forces at the flanges.

2. Sketch the force diagram.

3. Sketch the momentum diagram.

4. Use the vector form of the momentum equation, Eq. (6.6).

\(\sum F =\frac{d}{d t} \int_{ cv } \rho v d V+\sum_{ cs } \dot{m}_{o} v _{o}-\sum_{ cs } \dot{m}_{i} v _{i}\) (6.6)

5. Evaluate the sum of the forces.

6. Evaluate the momentum terms.

7. Calculate the reaction force.

 

Admin
Solution

1. The control volume selected is shown. The control volume is stationary. The z-direction is outward from the page.

2. The force diagram shows pressure forces and the component reaction forces.

3. Vector form of momentum equation

 

\(\sum F =\frac{d}{d t} \int_{ cv } \rho v d V+\sum_{ cs } \dot{m}_{o} v _{o}-\sum_{ cs } \dot{m}_{i} v_{i}\)

 

4. Sum of the forces: The weight of the pipe and fluid therein is W and acts in the negative z-direction.

 

\(\sum F =\left(R_{x}+p A-p A \cos 30^{\circ}\right) i +\left(R_{y}+p A \sin 30^{\circ}\right) j\)

 

\(+\left(R_{z}-W\right) k\)

 

5. Momentum terms

• Accumulation term for stationary control volume is

 

\(\frac{d}{d t} \int_{ cv } \rho v d V=0\).

 

• Momentum outflow is

 

\(\sum_{\text {cs }} \dot{m}_{o} v_{o}=\left(\dot{m} v \cos 30^{\circ}\right) i -\left(\dot{m} v \sin 30^{\circ}\right) j\).

 

• Momentum inflow is \(\sum_{c s} \dot{m}_{i} v_{i}=(\dot{m} v) i\).

 

6. Reaction force

 

\(\left(R_{x}+p A-p A \cos 30^{\circ}\right) i +\left(R_{y}+p A \sin 30^{\circ}\right) j +\left(R_{z}-W\right) k\)

 

\(=[m v(\cos 30-1)] i -\left(\dot{m} v \sin 30^{\circ}\right) j\)

 

• Equating components

 

\(R_{x}+p A-p A \cos 30^{\circ}=\dot{m} v \cos 30^{\circ}-\dot{m} v\)

 

\(R_{y}+p A \sin 30^{\circ}=-\dot{m} v \sin 30^{\circ}\)

 

\(R_{z}-W=0\)

 

• Pressure force

 

\(p A=\left(75 kN / m ^{2}\right)\left(\pi \times 0.5^{2} m ^{2}\right)=58.9\) KN

 

• Fluid speed

 

\(v=Q / A=\frac{\left(2 m ^{3} / s \right)}{\left(\pi \times 0.5^{2} m ^{2}\right)}=2.55\) m/s

 

• Momentum flux

 

\(\dot{m} v=\rho Q v=\left(0.94 \times 1000 kg / m ^{3}\right)\left(2 m ^{3} / s \right)(2.55 m / s )\)

 

= 4.79 kN

 

Reaction force in x-direction

 

\(R_{x}=-(p A+\dot{m} v)\left(1-\cos 30^{\circ}\right)\)

 

\(=-(58.9+4.79)( kN )\left(1-\cos 30^{\circ}\right)=-8.53\) KN

 

Reaction force in y-direction

 

\(R_{y}=-(p A+\dot{m} v) \sin 30^{\circ}\)

 

\(=-(58.9+4.79)( kN )\left(\sin 30^{\circ}\right)=-31.8\) KN

 

Reaction force in z-direction. (The bend weight includes the oil plus the empty pipe).

 

\(W=\gamma V +4 kN\)

 

\(=\left(0.94 \times 9.81 kN / m ^{3}\right)\left(1.2 m ^{3}\right)+4 kN =15.1\) KN

 

Reaction force vector

 

\(R =(-8.53 kN ) i +(-31.8 kN ) j +(15.1 kN ) k\)
