Guest Question.

(a) Two columns A and B are to be supported on a trapezoidal combined footing, as shown in the scheme below. The footing is 0.7 m thick. The top of the footing is flush with the ground surface, and the ground water table is located at a great depth. The vertical dead load on columns A and B is 500 and 1400 kN, respectively. Determine dimension \( B_{2}\) so that the resultant of the column loads acts through the centroid of the footing. (b) In addition to the dead loads, columns A and B also can carry vertical live loads of up to 800 and 1200 kN, respectively. The live load on each column is independent of that on the other column; that is, one could be carrying the full live load while the other does not. Using the dimensions obtained in (a), find the worst possible combination of the columns live load.

Admin
Solution

(a) Centroid of the trapezoid is located at distance m from \( B_{2}\), as shown. The trapezoid rule gives

\( m = \frac{L}{3}\left ( \frac{2B_{1} + B_{2} }{B_{1} + B_{2} }\right ) = \frac{4.5}{3}\left ( \frac{2 \times 1.5 + B_{2}}{B_{1} + B_{2}} \right) = \frac{4.5 + 1.5 B_{2}}{B_{2} + 1.5} \cdots \cdots \left (1 \right) \)

 

\( W_{f} = 24 \times 0.7 \times 4.5 \left ( \frac{1.5 + B_{2}}{2} \right) = 56.7 + 37.8 B_{2} \)

 

\( R = W_{f} + 500 + 1400 = 56.7 + 37.8 B_{2} + 1900 = 1956.7 + 37.8 B_{2} \)

 

Moment of R about \( B_{2} \) equals to \(\sum \) moment of components:

\(m \left ( 1956.7 + 37.8 B_{2} \right)= m \left ( 56.7 + 37.8 B_{2} \right) + 4 \times 500 + 1 \times 1400 \)

 

\(m = \frac{3400}{1900} = 1.79 m \cdots \cdots \left ( 2 \right ) \)

 

Hence,

\( 1.79 = \frac{4.5 + 1.5 B_{2}}{B_{2} + 1.5} \rightarrow 1.79 B_{2} + 2.69 = 4.5 + 1.5 B_{2} \rightarrow 0.29 B_{2} = 1.81 \rightarrow \)

 

\( B_{2} = \frac{1.81}{0.29} = 6.241 m \rightarrow \) Use \( B_{2} = 6.3 m \)

 

\( m = \frac{4.5 + 1.5 B_{2}}{B_{2} + 1.5} = \frac{4.5 + 1.5 \times 6.3}{6.3 + 1.5} = 1.79 m \rightarrow unchanged. \)

 

(b) In order to find the worst possible combination of the columns live load, it is necessary to find which combination produces the greatest maximum contact pressure \( q_{max}\). Therefore the following possible loading conditions may be examined:

(1) The two column live loads are present at the same time.

\( A = \left ( 4.5 \right)\left ( \frac{1.5 + 6.3}{2} \right) = 17.55 m^{2}, W_{f} = 0.7 \times 17.55 \times 24 = 294.84 kN \)

 

\( R = 294.84 + \left ( 500 + 800 \right) + \left ( 1400 + 1200 \right ) = 4194.84 kN \)

 

Moment of R about \( B_{2} \) equals to \( \sum \) moment of components:

\( 4194.84 {x}' = 294.84 \times 1.79 + 1300 \times 4 + 2600 \times 1 = 8327.76 \)

 

\( {x}' = \frac{8327.76}{4194.84} = 1.99 m > m; e_{x} = {x}' – m = 1.99 – 1.79 = 0.2m \)

 

Equation : \( q = \frac{R}{A} \pm \frac{M_{y}}{I_{y}}x \pm \frac{M_{x}}{I_{x}}y \)

 

\( M_{x} = 0; x_{max} = L – m = 4.5 – 1.79 = 2.71 m. \)

 

\( I_{y} = \frac{B^{2}_{1} + 4 B_{1}B_{2} + B^{2}_{2}}{36 \left (B_{1} + B_{2} \right)} L^{3} \)

 

For a trapezoid area

\( I_{y} = \frac{1.5^{2} + 4 \times 1.5 \times 6.3 + 6.3^{2}}{36 \left (1.5 + 6.3 \right)} \left ( 4.5^{3} \right) = 25.88 m^{4} \)

 

\( M_{y} = Re_{x} = 4194.84 \times 0.2 = 838.97 kN.m \)

 

\( q_{max} = \frac{4194.84}{17.55} + \frac{838.97 \times 2.71}{25.88} = 239.02 + 87.85 = 326.87 kPa \)

 

It may be noticed that \( q_{min} \) is also positive. Therefore, the supporting soil is totally in compression.

(2) Live load of column A is present only.

\( R = 294.84 + \left ( 500 + 800 \right) + \left ( 1400 \right) = 2994.84 Kn \)

 

Moment of R about \( B_{2} \) equals to \(\sum \) moment of components:

\( 2994.84 {x}' = 294.84 \times 1.79 + 1300 \times 4 + 1400 \times 1 = 7127.76 \)

 

\( {x}' = \frac{7127.76}{2994.84} = 2.38 m > m; e_{x} = {x}' – m = 2.38 – 1.79 = 0.59 m \)

 

\( q_{max} = \frac{R}{A} + \frac{M_{y}x_{max}}{I_{y}},x_{max} = L – m = 4.5 – 1.79 = 2.71 m \)

 

\( M_{y} = Re_{x} = 2994.84 \times 0.59 = 1766.96 kN.m \)

 

\( q_{max} = \frac{2994.84}{17.55} + \frac{1766.96 \times 2.71}{25.88} = 170.65 + 185.03 = 355.68 kPa \)

 

However, in this case, \( q_{min}\) is negative which indicates tension between the soil and a part of the footing bottom. Because soil cannot take any tension, separation between the footing and the soil underlying it will take place. This condition tends to overload the base soil, that is to increase \( q_{max}\). Therefore, the maximum contact pressure needs to be recomputed, using the same concept as outlined for rectangular footings (see Figure).

The soil reaction resultant \( R_{s}\) remains equal and opposite in direction to the resultant R, as shown below. Let \( {L}'\) represent length of the base area under which the soil is in compression, and \( {m}'\) equals the distance of its centroid from side B, as shown in the scheme below.

\( {L}' – \left ( L – {x}' \right) = {m}' = \frac{{L}'}{3} \left ( \frac{2B_{1} + B}{ B_{1} + B} \right)\)

 

\( {L}' – \left ( 4.5 – 2.38 \right) = \frac{{L}'}{3} \left ( \frac{2 \times 1.5 + B}{ 1.5 + B} \right)\)

 

\( {L}' – 2.12 = \frac{{L}'}{3} \left ( \frac{2 \times 1.5 + B}{ 1.5 + B} \right) \cdots \cdots \cdots \left ( 1 \right )\)

 

\( B = B_{1} + 2 \left [ {L}' \left ( \frac{B_{2} – B_{1}}{2} L\right )\right ] = 1.5 + {L}'\left ( \frac{6.3 – 1.5}{4.5} \right ) \)

 

\( B = 1.5 + 1.07 {L}' \cdots \cdots \cdots \left ( 2 \right ) \)

 

Hence,

\( {L}' – 2.12 = \frac{{L}'}{3}\left ( \frac{2 \times 1.5 + 1.5 + 1.07 {L}'}{1.5 + 1.5 + 1.07 {L}'} \right ) = \frac{{L}'}{3} \left ( \frac{4.5 + 1.07 {L}'}{3 + 1.07 {L}'} \right ) \)

 

\( 3 {L}' + 1.07\left ( {L}' \right)^{2} – 6.36 – 2.27 {L}' = 1.5{L}' + 0.36 \left ( {L}' \right )^{2} \)

 

\( \left ( {L}' \right )^{2} – 1.08 \left ( {L}' \right ) – 8.96 = 0 \)

 

\( {L}' = \frac{ – \left ( – 1.08 \right) \pm \sqrt{\left ( – 1.08 \right)^{2} – 4 \left ( 1 \right) \left ( – 8.96 \right )}}{2 \left ( 1 \right )} = \frac{1.08 \pm 6.08}{2} = 3.58 m \)

 

\( R_{s} = R = 2994.84 kN \)

 

\( R_{s} = \frac{q_{max} {L}'}{2} \left ( \frac{B_{1} + B}{2} \right) = \frac{q_{max} \left ( 3.58 \right )}{2} \left ( \frac{1.5 + 1.5 + 1.07 \times 3.58}{2} \right ) = 6.11 q_{max} \)

 

\( R_{s} = \frac{2994.84}{6.11} = 490.15 kPa > 355.68 kPa \)

 

(3) Live load of column B is present only.

\( R = 294.84 + \left ( 500 \right) + \left ( 1400 + 1200 \right) = 3394.84 Kn \)

 

Moment of R about \( B_{2} \) equals to \( \sum \) moment of components:

\( 3394.84 {x}' = 294.84 \times 1.79 + 500 \times 4 + 2600 \times 1 = 5127.76 \)

 

\( {x}' = \frac{5127.76}{3394.84} = 1.51 m < m; e_{x} = m – {x}' = 1.79 − 1.51 = 0.28 \)

 

\( q_{max} = \frac{R}{A} + \frac{M_{y}x_{max}}{I_{y}},x_{max} = m = 1.79 m \)

 

\( M_{y} = Re_{x} = 3394.84 \times 0.28 = 950.56 kN.m \)

 

\( q_{max} = \frac{3394.84}{17.55} + \frac{950.56 \times 1.79}{25.88} = 193.44 + 65.75 = 259.19 kPa \)

Therefore, the worst possible combination of the columns live load occurs when the live load of column A is present only.
