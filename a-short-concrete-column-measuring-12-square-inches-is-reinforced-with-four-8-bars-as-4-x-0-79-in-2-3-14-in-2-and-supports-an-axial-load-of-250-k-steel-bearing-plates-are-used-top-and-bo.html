Guest Question.

A short concrete column measuring 12 square inches is reinforced with four #8 bars (\(A_{s} =4\times 0.79 \space in.^2=3.14 \space in.^2\)) and supports an axial load of 250 k. Steel bearing plates are used top and bottom to ensure equal deformations of steel and concrete. Calculate the stress developed in each material if

\(E_{c} =3\times 10^6 \space psi\) and

 

\(E_{s} =29\times 10^6 \space psi\)
Admin
Solution

From equilibrium,

 

\(\left[\sum{F_{y}=0 }\right] – 250 \space k + f_{s} A_{s} +f_{c} A_{c} =0\)

 

\( A_{s}=3.14 \space in.^2\)

 

\(A_{c}= (12^{\prime \prime }\times 12^{\prime \prime })-3.14 \space in.^2\cong 141\space in.^2\)

 

\(3.14 \space f_{s}+141 \space f_{c}=250 \space k\)

 

From the deformation relationship,

 

\( \delta _{s} = \delta _{c} \space ; \space L_{s}=L_{c}\)

 

\(\therefore \frac{ \delta _{s}}{L} =\frac{ \delta _{c}}{L} \)

 

and

 

\(\varepsilon_{s}=\varepsilon_{c}\)

 

Because

 

\(E=\frac{f}{\varepsilon} \)

 

and

 

\(\frac{f_{s}}{E_{s}} =\frac{f_{c}}{E_{c}} \)

 

\(f_{s}=f_{c}\frac{E_{s}}{E_{c}}=\frac{29\times 10^3(f_{c})}{3\times 10^3} =9.67 \space f_{c}\)

 

Substituting into the equilibrium equation

 

\(3.14(9.76f_{c})+141f_{c}=250\)

 

\(30.4f_{c}+141f_{c}=250\)

 

\(171.4f_{c}=250\)

 

\(f_{c}=1.46 \space ksi\)

 

\(\therefore f_{s}=9.67(1.46) \space ksi \)

 

\( f_{s}=14.1 \space ksi\)
