Guest Question.

A wood post of rectangular cross section (Figure P16.73) consists of Select Structural grade Douglas fir lumber (\(F_{c}\) = 1,700 psi; E = 1,900,000 psi). The finished dimensions of the post are b = 3.5 in. and h = 5.5 in. Assume pinned connections at each end of the post. Determine the allowable axial load \(P_{\text {allow }}\) that may be supported by the post for the following column lengths: (a) L = 6 ft, (b) L = 10 ft, and (c) L = 14 ft. Use the NFPA NDS column design formula.

Admin
Solution

(a) L = 6 ft

\(\begin{aligned}&\frac{K L}{d}=\frac{(1.0)(6 ft )(12 in . / ft )}{3.5 in .}=20.5714 \\&F_{c E}=\frac{K_{c E} E}{(K L / d)^{2}}=\frac{(0.3)(1,900,000 psi )}{(20.5714)^{2}}=1,346.9329 psi \quad \frac{F_{c E}}{F_{c}}=\frac{1,346.9329 psi }{1,700 psi }=0.7923\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{\text {allow }} &=F_{c}\left\{\frac{1+\left(F_{c E} / F_{c}\right)}{2 c}-\sqrt{\left[\frac{1+\left(F_{c E} / F_{c}\right)}{2 c}\right]^{2}-\frac{F_{c E} / F_{c}}{c}}\right\} \\&=(1,700 psi )\left\{\frac{1+(0.7923)}{2(0.8)}-\sqrt{\left[\frac{1+(0.7923)}{2(0.8)}\right]^{2}-\frac{0.7923}{0.8}}\right\}=1,030.118 psi \\P_{\text {allow }} &=(1,030.118 psi )(3.5 in .)(5.5 in .)=19,829.77 lb =19,830 lb\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) L = 10 ft

\(\begin{aligned}&\frac{K L}{d}=\frac{(1.0)(10 ft )(12 in . / ft )}{3.5 in .}=34.2857 \\&F_{c E}=\frac{K_{c E} E}{(K L / d)^{2}}=\frac{(0.3)(1,900,000 psi )}{(34.2857)^{2}}=484.8958 psi \quad \frac{F_{c E}}{F_{c}}=\frac{484.8958 psi }{1,700 psi }=0.2852\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{\text {allow }} &=F_{c}\left\{\frac{1+\left(F_{c E} / F_{c}\right)}{2 c}-\sqrt{\left[\frac{1+\left(F_{c E} / F_{c}\right)}{2 c}\right]^{2}-\frac{F_{c E} / F_{c}}{c}}\right\} \\&=(1,700 psi )\left\{\frac{1+(0.2852)}{2(0.8)}-\sqrt{\left[\frac{1+(0.2852)}{2(0.8)}\right]^{2}-\frac{0.2852}{0.8}}\right\}=452.1322 psi \\P_{\text {allow }} &=(452.1322 psi )(3.5 in .)(5.5 in. )=8,703.54 lb =8,700 lb\end{aligned}\)

 

(c) L = 14 ft

\(\begin{aligned}&\frac{K L}{d}=\frac{(1.0)(14 ft )(12 in . / ft )}{3.5 in .}=48.0 \\&F_{c E}=\frac{K_{c E} E}{(K L / d)^{2}}=\frac{(0.3)(1,900,000 psi )}{(48.0)^{2}}=247.3958 psi \quad \frac{F_{c E}}{F_{c}}=\frac{247.3958 psi }{1,700 psi }=0.1455\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{\text {allow }} &=F_{c}\left\{\frac{1+\left(F_{c E} / F_{c}\right)}{2 c}-\sqrt{\left[\frac{1+\left(F_{c E} / F_{c}\right)}{2 c}\right]^{2}-\frac{F_{c E} / F_{c}}{c}}\right\} \\&=(1,700 psi )\left\{\frac{1+(0.1455)}{2(0.8)}-\sqrt{\left[\frac{1+(0.1455)}{2(0.8)}\right]^{2}-\frac{0.1455}{0.8}}\right\}=239.5382 psi \\P_{\text {allow }} &=(239.5382 psi )(3.5 in .)(5.5 in .)=4,611.11 lb =4,610 lb\end{aligned}\)
