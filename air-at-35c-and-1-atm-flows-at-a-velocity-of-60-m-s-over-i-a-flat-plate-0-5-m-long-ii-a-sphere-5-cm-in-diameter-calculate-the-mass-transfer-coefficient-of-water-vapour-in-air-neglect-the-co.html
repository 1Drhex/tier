Guest Question.

Air at 35°C and 1 atm flows at a velocity of 60 m/s over (i) a flat plate 0.5 m long (ii) a sphere 5 cm in diameter. Calculate the mass transfer coefficient of water vapour in air. Neglect the concentration of vapour in air.

Admin
Solution

Given : Flow of air over the bodies with
\(p = 1 atm = 1.01325 × 10^5 N/m^2\)
\(T_∞ = 35°C = 308 K,\)
\(u_∞ = 60 m/s.\)

To find : Mass transfer coefficients.
Analysis : The diffusion coefficient of water vapour in air
\(D_{AB} = 0.256 × 10^{–4} m^2/s\)
(From Appendix Table A-11)
The properties of air at 35°C from Table A-4
Density \(C_p = 1.006 kJ/kg.K,\)
\(µ = 2 × 10^{–5} kg/ms, Pr = 0.706\)
\(ρ = \frac{1.01325 × 10^5}{287 × 308} =1.146 kg/m^3\)
Schmidt number
\(Sc = \frac{µ}{ρD_{AB}}= \frac{2× 10^{-5}}{1.146 × 0.256 × 10^{–4}}= 0.682\)
(i) Flow over flat plate,
\(L_c = 0.5 m\)
\(Re = \frac{ρ u_∞L}{µ}=\frac{ 1.146 × 60 × 0.5}{2 × 10^{-5}}= 1.719 × 10^6\)
greater than \(5 × 10^5\), thus flow is turbulent. Using eqn. (15.94)
\(Sh = 0.0296 Re^{0.8} Sc^{1/3}\)
or \( \frac{h_m × 0.5}{ 0.256 × 10^{–4}} = 0.0296 × (1.719 × 10^6)^{0.8} × (0.682)^{1/3}\)
= 2472.2
or \(h_m = 0.1265 m/s. \)
(ii) Flow over a sphere,
\(L_c = d = 0.05 m\)
\(Re = \frac{ρ u_∞L}{µ}=\frac{ 1.146 × 60 × 0.05}{2 × 10^{-5}}= 1719000\)
Using correlation, eqn. (15.99)
\(Sh = 2 [1 + 0.276 Re^{1/2} Sc^{1/3}]\)
\( \frac{h_m × 0.5}{ 0.256 × 10^{–4}} = 2 × [1 + 0.276 × (171900)^{1/2} × (0.632)^{1/3}]\)
= 198.4
or \(h_m = 0.101 m/s.\)
