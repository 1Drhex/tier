Guest Question.

Consider a Zener regulator circuit as shown in Fig. 6.21 b where \(V_{s}\) = 45 V, \(R_{S}\) = 1 kΩ and \(D_{3}\) is an ideal Zener diode with \(V_{Z}\) = 10 V and maximum current rating \(I_{Z max}\) = 30 mA.

(a) Determine the range of \(R_{L}\) and \(I_{L}\) that will result in \(V_{L}\) being maintained at 10

(b) Determine maximum wattage rating of the Zener diode as a voltage

(c) If the diode is not ideal and has a dynamic resistance of 20 Ω, calculate Zener current and the load current in the circuit for \(R_{L}\) = 1 kΩ.

Admin
Solution

(a) Using Eq. (6.74), \(R_{L min}=\frac{R_{S}V_{Z}}{V_{S}-V_{Z}} \) , the minimum value of \(R_{L} \) is given by

\(R_{L min}=\frac{(1\times 10^3\Omega )\times 10V}{(45-10)V} =285.71\Omega \)

The minimum load current can be calculated from Eq. (6.76) , \(I_{L min} =\left\lgroup\frac{V_{S}-V_{Z}}{R_{S}} \right\rgroup -I_{Z max} \) , as

\(I_{L min}=\frac{(45-10)V}{1\times 10^3\Omega }-30 \) mA

= 35 mA – 30 mA

= 5 mA

Now, the maximum load resistance can be obtained by using Eq. (6.77) , \(R_{L max}=\frac{V_{Z}}{I_{L min}} \) :

\(R_{L max}=\frac{V_{Z}}{I_{L min}}=\frac{10V}{5\times 10^{-3}A}=2 k\Omega \)

Clearly, \(R_{L} \) must be in the range of \(R_{L min} \) = 285.71 Ω and \(R_{L max} \) = 2 kΩ, i.e. 285.71 Ω ≤ \(R_{L} \) ≤ 2 kΩ.

Since minimum load resistance corresponds to the maximum load current, \(I_{L max} \) can be obtained from Eq. (6.75) , \(I_{L}\leq \frac{V_{Z}}{R_{L min}}=\frac{V_{S}-V_{Z}}{R_{S}}=I_{L max} \) , as

\(I_{L max} =\frac{V_{Z}}{R_{L min}}=\frac{10V}{285.71\Omega } =35 \) mA

Thus, for the load current in the range of 5 mA and 35 mA, the \(V_{L} \) will be maintained at 10 V.

(b) The maximum power rating \(P_{max} \) of the Zener diode is the product of the maximum limiting current \(I_{Z max} \) and breakdown voltage \(V_{Z} \):

\(P_{max}=V_{Z}I_{Z max}=10V\times 30 mA=300 mW\)

(c) The Zener current in this case can be obtained from Eq. (6.70) as

\(I_{Z}=\frac{\left\lgroup\frac{V_{S}-V_{Z}}{R_{S}} \right\rgroup -\frac{V_{Z}}{R_{L}} }{1+\left\lgroup\frac{r}{R_{S}}+\frac{r}{R_{L}} \right\rgroup }=\frac{\left\lgroup\frac{45-10}{1\times 10^{3}}-\frac{10}{1\times 10^3} \right\rgroup A}{1+\left\lgroup\frac{20}{1\times 10^3}+\frac{20}{1\times 10^3} \right\rgroup }=\frac{25mA}{1.04}=24.04 \) mA

Combining Eqs (6.66) , \(I_{L}=\frac{V_{L}}{R_{L}} \), and (6.67), \(V_{L}=V_{Z}+rI_{Z} \) , we can obtain the load current as

\(I_{L}=\frac{V_{Z}+\gamma I_{Z}}{R_{L}}=\frac{10V+20\times 24.04\times 10^{-3}V}{1\times 10^3\Omega } =10.48 \) mA

Note that for the ideal diode, the load current is \(\frac{10}{1\times 10^3} =10 \) mA which is slightly less than the non-ideal case due to the effect of the dynamic resistance of the diode.
