Guest Question.

Design a single stage RC coupled BJT amplifier circuit shown in Fig. 9.63. Assume that \(V_{CC}\) = 10 V, \(I_{C}\) = 4 mA, \(h_{fe}\) = 100, \(h_{ie}\) = 1 kΩ , \(R_{L}\) = 100 kΩ and \(f_{L}\) = 100 Hz.

Admin
Solution

Refer to Fig. 9.63.

(a) To determine bias resistors \(R_{1} and R_{2} and R_{C} and R_{E}\)

We know that \(V_{E}= \frac{V_{CC}}{10}= \frac{10}{10} = 1 V, V_{CE} = \frac{V_{CC}}{2}= 5V\)

\(V_{CC} = I_{C}R_{C} + V_{CE} + V_{E}\)

Therefore, \(I_{C}R_{C} = V_{CC} – V_{CE} – V_{E} = 10 – 5 – 1 = 4V\)

i.e \(R_{C}= \frac{4}{4\times 10^{-3} }= 1 K \Omega \)

Here, \(V_{E} = I_{E}R_{E} \approx I_{C}R_{E}\)

Therefore,

\(R_{E}= \frac{V_{E}}{I_{C}}= \frac{1}{4\times 10^{-3} } = 250 \Omega \)

\(V_{B} = V_{BE} + V_{E} = 0.7 + 1 = 1.7\)

\(V_{B}= (\frac{R_{2}}{R_{1}+R_{2}} )V_{CC}\)

\(\frac{R_{2}}{R_{1}+R_{2}}= \frac{V_{B}}{V_{CC}}= \frac{1.7}{10}= 0.17\)

\(R_{2} = 0.17(R_{1} + R_{2})\)

\(5.88R_{2} = R_{1} + R_{2}\)

\(4.88R_{2} = R_{1}\)

The value of \(R_{2}\) can be selected to satisfy β \(R_{E} >> R_{2}\). Hence \(R_{2}\) is selected as 2 kΩ. Therefore, \(R_{1} = 9.76 k \Omega \approx 10 k \Omega\)

(b) To determine the bypass capacitor \(C_{E}\)

\(X_{C_{E} }= \frac{h_{ie}}{1+h_{fe}}= \frac{1\times 10^{3} }{1+100}= 9.9\)

\(C_{E}= \frac{1}{2\pi f_{L}X_{C_{E} } }= \frac{1}{2\pi \times 100\times 9.9}= \frac{1}{6217.2}= 160.8 \mu F\)

(c) To determine coupling capacitors \(C_{1} and C_{2}\)

\(X_{C_{1} }= \frac{Z_{i}}{10}= \frac{(R_{1}\parallel R_{2}\parallel h_{ie} )}{10}= \frac{(10\times 10^{3}\parallel 2\times 10^{3}\parallel 1\times 10^{3} )}{10}\)

= 246.154

\(C_{1}= \frac{1}{2\pi f_{L}X_{C_{1} }}= \frac{1}{2\pi \times 100\times 246.154}= 6.47 \mu F \)

\(X_{C_{2} }= \frac{Z_{O} }{10}= \frac{R_{C}\parallel R_{L}}{10}= \frac{1\times 10^{3} \parallel 100\times 10^{3} }{10}= 99.001 \)

\(C_{2}= \frac{1}{2\pi f_{L}X_{C_{2} } }= \frac{1}{2\pi \times 100\times 99.001}= \frac{1}{62172.628}= 0.1608 \mu F\)
