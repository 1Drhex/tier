Guest Question.

Draw the schematic and derive the equations for a 24-bit adder using 3-bit stages. Express the equations in both Boolean form and in ABEL.

Admin
Solution

The figure below is a 24-bit adder using 3-bit stages. The equations for \(S_0\) and \(S_1\) are the same as the two-bit adder in , that is,

\(S_0 = A_0 ⊕ (B_0 ⊕ C_{IN0}) = A_0 ⊕ (B_0\bar{C}_{IN0} + \bar{B}_0C_{IN0})\)

SUM0 = A0 $ ((B0 & !CIN0) # (!B0 & CIN0))

\(S_1 = A_1 ⊕ [(\bar{A}_0\bar{B}_0B_1 + \bar{A}_0B_1\bar{C}_{IN0} + \bar{B}_0B_1\bar{C}_{IN0}) + (A_0B_0\bar{B}_1 + A_0\bar{B}_1C_{IN0} + B_0\bar{B}_1C_{IN0})]\)

SUM1 = A1 $ ((!A0 & !B0 & B1) # (!A0 & B1 & !CIN0) # (!B0 & B1 & !CIN0)
# (A0 & B0 & !B1) # (A0 & !B1 & CIN0) # (B0 & !B1 & CIN0))

The equations for the sum and \(S_2\) are:

\(S_2=A_2⊕[(\bar{A}_1\bar{B}_1B_2)+(\bar{A}_0\bar{B}_0\bar{B}_1B_2)+(\bar{A}_0\bar{B}_1\bar{C}_{IN0}B_2)+(\bar{B}_0\bar{B}_1\bar{C}_{IN0}B_2)\)

\(+(\bar{A}_0\bar{A}_1\bar{B}_0B_2)+(\bar{A}_0\bar{A}_1\bar{C}_{IN0}B_2)+(\bar{A}_1\bar{B}_0\bar{C}_{IN0}B_2)\)

\(+(A_1B_1\bar{B}_2)+(A_0B_0B_1\bar{B}_2)+(A_0B_1C_{IN0}\bar{B_2})+(B_0B_1C_{IN0}\bar{B}_2)\)

\(+(A_0A_1B_0\bar{B}_2)+(A_0A_1C_{IN0}\bar{B}_2)+(A_1B_0C_{IN0}\bar{B}_2)]\)

SUM2 = A2 $ ((!A1 & !B1 & B2) # ( !A0 & !B0 & !B1 & B2 )
# (!A0 & !B1 & !CIN0 & B2) # (!B0 & !B1 & !C IN0 & B2)
# (!A0 & !A1 & !B0 & B2) # (!A0 & !A1 & !C IN0 & B2)
# (!A1 & !B0 & !CIN0 & B2) # (A1 & B1 & !B2)
# (A0 & B0 & B1 & !B2) # (A0 & B1 & C IN0 & !B2)
# (B0 & B1 & CIN0 & !B2) # (A0 & A1 & B0 & !B2)
# (A0 & A1 & CIN0 & !B2) # (A1 & B0 & CIN0 & !B2)

The generated equations for \(G_1\) and \(G_2\) are the same as in , that is,

\(G_0 = A_0B_0\)
\(G_1 = A_1B_1 + A_0B_0A_1 + A_0B_0B_1\)

G0 = A0 & B0
G1 = A1 & B1 # A0 & B0 & A1 # A0 & B0 & B1

The generated equation for \(G_2\) is satisfied when:

(1) both \(A_2\) and \(B_2\) are a logical 1 or

(2) at least one of the addends is a logical 1 and there is a carry being generated in the middle stage; or

(3) at least one of the addends is a logical 1 and there is a carry being generated in the first stage and the middle stage propagates the carry to the third stage. Thus,

\(G_2 = A_2B_2 + A_2A_1B_1 + A_2A_1A_0B_0 + A_2B_1A_0B_0 + B_2A_1B_1 + B_2A_1A_0B_0 + B_2B_1A_0B_0\)

G2 = (A2 & B2) # (A2 & A1 & B1) # (A2 & A1 & A0 & B0)
# (A2 & B1 & A0 & B0) # (B2 & A1 & B1)
# (B2 & A1 & A0 & B0) # (B2 & B1 & A0 & B0)

As in , for the propagated equation we can obtain a simpler expression if we consider the case where \(P_1\) is logical 0. This occurs when both A and B are logical 0 in one stage of the three-bit adder. Thus,

\(\bar{p}_1=\overline{(A_0 + B_0) ⋅ (A_1 + B_1) ⋅ (A_2 + B_2)} = \bar{A}_0\bar{B}_0 + \bar{A}_1\bar{B}_1 + \bar{A}_2\bar{B}_2 \)

!P1 = (!A0 & !B0) # (!A1 & !B1) # (!A2 & !B2)

Note: We can derive the four-bit equations for implementing a 24-bit adder but the number of product terms would probably exceed the CPLD capacity.
