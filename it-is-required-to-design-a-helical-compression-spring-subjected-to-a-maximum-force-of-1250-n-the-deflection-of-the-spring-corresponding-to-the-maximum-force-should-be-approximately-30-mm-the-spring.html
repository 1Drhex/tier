Guest Question.

It is required to design a helical compression spring subjected to a maximum force of 1250 N. The deflection of the spring corresponding to the maximum force should be approximately 30 mm. The spring index can be taken as 6. The spring is made of patented and cold-drawn steel wire. The ultimate tensile strength and modulus of rigidity of the spring material are 1090 and 81 370 N/mmÂ² respectively. The permissible shear stress for the spring wire should be taken as 50% of the ultimate tensile strength. Design the spring and calculate:
(i) wire diameter;
(ii) mean coil diameter;
(iii) number of active coils;
(iv) total number of coils;

(v) free length of the spring; and
(vi) pitch of the coil.
Draw a neat sketch of the spring showing various dimensions.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } P=1250 N \quad \delta=30 mm \quad C=6 \).

\( S_{u t}=1090 N / mm ^{2} \quad G=81370 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \tau=0.5 S_{u t} \).

Step I Wire diameter
The permissible shear stress is given by,

\( \tau=0.5 S_{u t}=0.5(1090)=545 N / mm ^{2} \).

From Eq. (10.7),

\( K=\frac{4 C-1}{4 C-4}+\frac{0.615}{C} \) (10.7).

\( K=\frac{4 C-1}{4 C-4}+\frac{0.615}{C}=\frac{4(6)-1}{4(6)-4}+\frac{0.615}{6}=1.2525 \).

From Eq. (10.13),

\( \tau=K\left(\frac{8 P C}{\pi d^{2}}\right) \) (10.13).

\( \tau=K\left(\frac{8 P C}{\pi d^{2}}\right) \quad \text { or } \quad 545=(1.2525)\left\{\frac{8(1250)(6)}{\pi d^{2}}\right\} \).

\( \therefore \quad d=6.63 \text { or } 7 mm \) (i).

Step II Mean coil diameter
D = Cd = 6(7) = 42 mm (ii)
Step III Number of active coils
From Eq. (10.8),

\( \delta=\frac{8 P D^{3} N}{G d^{4}} \) (10.8).

\( \delta=\frac{8 P D^{3} N}{G d^{4}} \quad \text { or } \quad 30=\frac{8(1250)(42)^{3} N}{(81370)(7)^{4}} \).

\( \therefore \quad N=7.91 \text { or } 8 \text { coils } \) (iii).

Step IV Total number of coils
It is assumed that the spring has square and ground ends. The number of inactive coils is 2. Therefore,

\( N_{t}=N+2=8+2=10 \text { coils } \) (iv).

Step V Free length of spring
The actual deflection of the spring is given by,

\( \delta=\frac{8 P D^{3} N}{G d^{4}}=\frac{8(1250)(42)^{3}(8)}{(81370)(7)^{4}}=30.34 mm \).

\( \text { solid length of spring }=N_{t} d=10(7)=70 mm \).

It is assumed that there will be a gap of 1 mm between consecutive coils when the spring is subjected to the maximum force. The total number of coils is 10.
The total axial gap between the coils will be (10â1) Ã 1 = 9 mm.

\( \text { Free length }=\text { Solid length }+\text { Total axial gap }+\delta \).

= 70 + 9 + 30.34
= 109.34 or 110 mm (v)
Step VI Pitch of the coil

\( \text { Pitch of coil }=\frac{\text { Free length }}{\left(N_{t}-1\right)}=\frac{109.34}{(10-1)}=12.15 mm \). (vi).
