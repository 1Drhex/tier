Guest Question.

The block diagram of Figure 22–5 represents the first three stages of a typical AM or FM receiver.

Find the following quantities:
a. \(A_{P1(dB)}, A_{P2(dB)}\), and \(A_{P3(dB)}\)
b. \(A_{PT(dB)}\)
c. \(P_{1}, P_{2}\), and \(P_{out}\)
d. \(P_{in(dBm)}, P_{1(dBm)}, P_{2(dBm)}\), and \(P_{out(dBm)}\)

Admin
Solution

a. \(A_{P1(dB)}=10\log _{10}A_{P1}=10\log _{10}100=20 dB \)

 

\(A_{P2(dB)}=\log _{10}A_{P2}=10\log _{10}0.2= -7.0 dB\)

 

\(A_{P3(dB)}=10\log _{10}A_{P3}=10\log _{10}(10 000)= 40 dB\)

b. \(A_{PT(dB)}=A_{P1(dB)}+A_{P2(dB)}+A_{P3(dB)}\)

 

\(=20 dB-7.0 dB+40 dB\)

 

\(=53.0 dB\)

c. \(P_{1}=A_{P1}P_{in}=(100)(1 pW)=100 pW\)

 

\(P_{2}=A_{P2}P_{1}=(100 pW)(0.2)=20 pW\)

 

\(P_{out}=A_{P3}P_{2}=(10 000)(20 pW)=0.20 \mu W\)

d. \(P_{in(dBm)}=10\log _{10}\frac{P_{in}}{1 mW} =10\log _{10}\frac{1 pW}{1mW} =-90 dBm\)

 

\(P_{1(dBm)}=10\log _{10}\frac{P_{1}}{1 mW} =10\log _{10}\frac{100 pW}{1 mW} =-70 dBm\)

 

\(P_{2(dBm)}=10\log _{10}\frac{P_{2}}{1 mW} =10\log _{10}\frac{20 pW}{1 mW} =-77.0 dBm\)

 

\(P_{out(dBm)}=10\log _{10}\frac{P_{out}}{1 mW} =10\log _{10}\frac{0.20 \mu W}{1 mW} =-37.0 dBm\)

Notice that the power level (in dBm) at the output of any stage is easily determined as the sum of the input power level (in dBm) and the gain of the stage (in dB). It is for this reason that many communication circuits express power levels in decibels rather than in watts.
