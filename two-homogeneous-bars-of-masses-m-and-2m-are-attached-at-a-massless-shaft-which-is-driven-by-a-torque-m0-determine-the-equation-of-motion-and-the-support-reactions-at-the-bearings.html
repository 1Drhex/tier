Guest Question.

Two homogeneous bars of masses \(m\) and \(2m\) are attached at a massless shaft which is driven by a torque \(M_{0} \) .

Determine the equation of motion and the support reactions at the bearings.

Admin
Solution

We first determine the axial moment of inertia and products of inertia for the \(body-fixed\) system \(\xi , \eta , \zeta \)

 

\(\Theta _{\zeta }=\frac{2m}{3}\left(2a\right)^{2}+\frac{m}{3}a^{2}=3ma^{2} \) ,

\(\Theta _{\xi \zeta } =-m2a\frac{-a}{2}=ma^{2}\) ,

\(\Theta _{\eta \zeta }=0 \) .

With the external moments

\(M_{\xi }=2aB_{\eta }-4aA_{\eta } \) , \(M_{\eta }=4aA_{\xi }-2aB_{\xi } \) , \(M_{\zeta }=M_{0}\) ,

it follows from the priciple of angular momentum with respect to the body-fixed system

\( \overset{\curvearrowright }{\xi } :2a\left(B_{\eta }-2A_{\eta } \right) =-\dot{\omega } ma^{2} \rightsquigarrow B_{\eta }-2A_{\eta }=-\frac{ma\dot{\omega } }{2} \) ,

\( \overset{\curvearrowright }{\eta } :2a\left(2A_{\xi }-B_{\xi } \right) =\omega^{2} ma^{2} \rightsquigarrow 2A_{\xi }-B_{\xi }=\frac{ma\omega^{2} }{2} \) ,

\( \overset{\curvearrowright }{\zeta } : M_{0}=3ma^{2}\dot{\omega } \rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{\dot{\omega } } }=\underline{\underline{\frac{M_{0} }{3ma^{2} } } } \) .

The last equation is the equation of motion of the shaft. To determine the support reactions, the principle of linear momentum must be applied. Since the motion of the mass centers \( C_{1} \) and \( C_{2}\) is circular, the acceleration components of e.g. \( C_{1} \) are given by \( \xi _{1}\dot{\omega } \) in \(\eta \)-direction and \(-\xi _{1}\omega ^{2} \) in ξ-direction. Thus, we obtain in \(\xi \)– and in \(\eta \)-direction

\(-2ma\omega^{2}+m\left(a/2\right)\omega^{2}=A_{\xi }+B_{\xi } \) , \(2ma\dot{\omega } +m\left(a/2\right)\dot{\omega} =A_{\eta }+B_{\eta } \)

and finally

\(\underline{\underline{A_{\xi }=-\frac{1}{3}ma\omega ^{2} } } \) , \(\underline{\underline{A_{\eta } =\frac{2}{9}\frac{M_{0} }{a} } } \) , \(\underline{\underline{ B_{\xi }=-\frac{5}{6}ma\omega ^{2} } } \) , \(\underline{\underline{B_{\eta }=\frac{5}{18}\frac{M_{0 } }{a} } }\) .
