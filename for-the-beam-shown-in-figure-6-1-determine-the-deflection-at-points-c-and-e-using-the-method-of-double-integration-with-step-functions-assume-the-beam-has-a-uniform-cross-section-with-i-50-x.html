Guest Question.

For the beam shown in Figure 6.1, determine the deflection at points C and E using the method of double integration with step functions. Assume the beam has a uniform cross-section with \( I = 50 × 10^6 mm^4 \) and take \( E = 2 × 10^5 \) MPa.

Admin
Solution

Using step functions, the expression for the moment at any point along the beam in Figure 6.1 is given by Equation 6.5 (reproduced here for convenience):

\( M = 24x − 24< x-2 > – \frac{8}{2}< x-4>^2 + 48 <x-8> \) (6.5).

\( M = EI \frac{d^2 v}{dx^2} = 24x − 24< x-2 > – \frac{8}{2}< x-4>^2 + 48 <x-8> \) .

Integrating twice gives:

\( EI \frac{d v}{dx} = 12x^2 – 12 < x-2>^2 -\frac{4}{3}<x-4>^3 +24 <x-8>^2 + C_1 \) .

\( EIv = 4x^3 – 4 < x-2 >^3 -\frac{1}{3}<x-4>^4 + 8<x-8>^3 + C_1 x + C_2 \) (1).

Enforcing the boundary condition that v = 0 at x = 0 and with <x – 2> = <x – 4> = <x – 8> = 0, \( C_2 is calculated using Equation 1 as C_2 = 0 \) .

At x = 8, we have v = 0, <x − 2> = x − 2, <x − 4> = x − 4 and <x − 8> = 0, and from Equation 1: \( C_1 = −137.3 \) .
At Point C, where x = 4 m, <x − 2> = x − 2, <x − 4> = 0 and <x − 8> = 0, and with EI = 10 × 13³ kNm², the deflection is obtained from Equation 1:

\(v_C = \frac{1}{10 × 10^3 } (4 × 4^3 – 4 × (4-2)^3 – 137.3×4 ) \)= -0.0325 m = -32.5 mm.

which represents a downward deflection (on the basis of our sign convention).

At Point E, where x = 10 m, <x − 2> = x − 2, <x − 4> = x − 4, <x − 8> = x − 8 and with EI = 10 × 10³ kNm², the deflection is calculated from Equation 1 as:

\( v_E = \frac{1}{10 × 10^3 }\Bigl(4 × 10^3 -4 × (10-2)^3 -\frac{1}{3}× 8 (10-8)^3 – 137.3×10 \Bigr) \)= + 0.0211 m = + 21.1 mm (i.e. upward).
