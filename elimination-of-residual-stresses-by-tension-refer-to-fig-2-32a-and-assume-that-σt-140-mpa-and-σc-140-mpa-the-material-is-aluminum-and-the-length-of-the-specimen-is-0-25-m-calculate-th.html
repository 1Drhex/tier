Guest Question.

Elimination of residual stresses by tension

Refer to Fig. 2.32a and assume that \( σ_{t}\)= 140 MPa and \(σ_{c}\)= -140 MPa. The material is aluminum and the length of the specimen is 0.25 m. Calculate the length to which this specimen should be stretched so that, when unloaded, it will be free from residual stresses. Assume that the yield stress of the material is 150 MPa.

Admin
Solution

Stretching should be to the extent that \(σ_{c}\) reaches the yield stress in tension, Y. Therefore, the total strain should be equal to the sum of the strain required to bring the compressive residual stress to zero and the strain required to bring it to the tensile yield stress. Hence,

\(\epsilon_{\text {total }}=\frac{\sigma_{c}}{E}+\frac{Y}{E}\) (2.32)

For aluminum, let \(E=70 \mathrm{GPa}\), as obtained from Table 2.1. Thus,

\(\epsilon_{\text {total }}=\frac{140}{70 \times 10^{3}}+\frac{150}{70 \times 10^{3}}=0.00414\)

Hence, the stretched length should be

\(\ln \left(\frac{l_{f}}{0.25}\right)=0.00414 \quad \text { or } \quad l_{f}=0.2510 \mathrm{~m}\)

As the strains are very small, we may use engineering strains in these calculations. Hence,

\(\frac{l_{f}-0.25}{0.25}=0.00414 \text { or } l_{f}=0.2510 \mathrm{~m}\)
