Guest Question.

Consider a 2-km-deep gas well containing a gas mixture of methane and ethane at a uniform temperature of 30°C. The pressure at the top of the well is 14 MPa, and the composition on a mole basis is 90% methane, 10% ethane. Each component is in equilibrium (top to bottom) with dG + g dZ = 0 and assume ideal gas, so for each component Eq.14.10 applies. Determine the pressure and composition at the bottom of the well.

Admin
Solution

\(\left( Z _{1}- Z _{2}\right)=2000 m\) , Let \(A = CH _{4}, \quad B = C _{2} H _{6}\)

\(P _{1}=14 MPa , \quad y _{ A1 }=0.90, \quad y _{ B 1}=0.10\)

T = 30 °C = constant

From section 14.1, for A to be at equilibrium between

1 and 2: \(W _{ REV }=0= n _{ A }\left(\overline{ G }_{ A1}-\overline{ G }_{ A 2}\right)+ n _{ A } M _{ A } g \left( Z _{1}- Z _{2}\right)\)

Similarly, for B: \(W _{ REV }=0= n _{ B }\left(\overline{ G }_{ B 1}-\overline{ G }_{ B 2}\right)+ n _{ B } M _{ B } g \left( Z _{1}- Z _{2}\right)\)

Using eq. 14.10 for A: \(\overline{ R } T \ln \left( P _{ A 2} / P _{ A 1}\right)= M _{ A } g \left( Z _{1}- Z _{2}\right)\)

with a similar expression for B. Now, ideal gas mixture, \(P _{ A1 }= y _{ A1 } P , \text { etc }\).

Substituting: \(\ln \frac{ y _{ A 2} P _{2}}{ y _{ A 1} P _{1}}=\frac{ M _{ A } g \left( Z _{1}- Z _{2}\right)}{\overline{ R } T } \quad \) and \(\ln \frac{ y _{ B 2} P _{2}}{ y _{ B 1} P _{1}}=\frac{ M _{ B } g \left( Z _{1}- Z _{2}\right)}{\overline{ R } T }\)

\(\ln \left( y _{ A 2} P _{2}\right)=\ln (0.9 \times 14)+\frac{16.04 \times 9.807(2000)}{1000 \times 8.3145 \times 303.2}=2.6585\)

=> \(y _{ A 2} P _{2}=14.2748\)

\(\ln \left( y _{ B 2} P _{2}\right)=\ln (0.1 \times 14)+\frac{30.07 \times 9.807(2000)}{1000 \times 8.3145 \times 303.2}=0.57043\)

=> \(y _{ B 2} P _{2}=\left(1- y _{ A 2}\right) P _{2}=1.76903\)

Solving: \(P _{2}= 1 6 . 0 4 4 MPa \& y _{ A 2}= 0 . 8 8 9 7\)
