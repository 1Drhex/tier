Guest Question.

A rectangular traffic sign of weight W is attached to a wall via two wires in A and B. It is held perpendicular to the wall by a joint in point C and a bar in D. All lengths are given in meters (m). Determine the forces at the joint, in the wires and the bar.

Admin
Solution

We isolate the traffic sign and sketch a free-body diagram with the components of all forces. Thus, the six spatial equilibrium conditions yield:

\(\sum{F_{x}}=0 -A_{x}-B_{x}-C_{x}=0, \)

 

\(\sum{F_{y}}=0: -A_{y}+B_{y}+C_{y}+D=0, \)

 

\(\sum{F_{z}}=0: A_{z}+B_{z}+C_{z}- W=0\)

 

\(\sum{M^{(0)}_{x} }=0: 1C_{y}=0, \)

 

\(\sum{M^{(0)}_{y} }=0: -4A_{z}-2B_{z}+2W+1C_{x}=0,\)

 

\(\sum{M^{(0)}_{z} }=0: -4A_{y}-2B_{y}=0.\)

This provides six equations for 10 unknowns. Another \(2 Ã— 2 = 4\) equa-tions follow from the decomposition of the wire forces A and B in-to components (the components are related to eachother according to their respective lengths!):

\(\frac{A_{x}}{A_{y}}=\frac{4}{1.6}, \frac{A_{x}}{A_{z}}=\frac{4}{2}, \frac{B_{x}}{B_{y}}=\frac{2}{1.6}, \frac{B_{x}}{B_{z}}=\frac{2}{2}.\)

by solving for the forces, we obtain:

\(\underline{\underline{A_{x}=B_{x}=\frac{W}{3} } }, \underline{\underline{C_{x}=-\frac{2}{3}W } }, \underline{\underline{A_{y}=\frac{2}{15}W } }, \underline{\underline{B_{y}=\frac{4}{15}W } },\)

 

\(\underline{\underline{C_{y}=0} }, \underline{\underline{A_{z}=\frac{W}{6} } }, \underline{\underline{B_{z}=\frac{W}{3} } }, \underline{\underline{C_{z}=\frac{W}{2} } }, \underline{\underline{D=-\frac{2}{15}W } }.\)

With the components of A and B, the forces in the ropes result as:

\(\underline{\underline{S_{A}=0.4W} }, \underline{\underline{S_{B}=0.34W} }.\)
