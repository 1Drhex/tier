Guest Question.

On a compact disc, audio information is stored in a series of pits and ﬂat areas on the surface of the disc. The information is stored digitally, and the alternations between pits and ﬂat areas on the surface represent binary ones and zeroes to be read by the compact disc player and converted back to sound waves. The pits and ﬂat areas are detected by a system consist-ing of a laser and lenses. The length of a certain number of ones and zeroes is the same everywhere on the disc, whether the information is near the center of the disc or near its outer edge. In order that this length of ones and zeroes al- ways passes by the laser – lens system in the same time period, the linear speed of the disc surface at the location of the lens must be constant. This requires, according to Equation 10.10, that the angular speed vary as the laser – lens system moves ra- dially along the disc. In a typical compact disc player, the disc spins counterclockwise (Fig. 10.6), and the constant speed of the surface at the point of the laser – lens system is 1.3 m/s.Find the angular speed of the disc in revolutions per minute when information is being read from the innermost ﬁrst track (r = 23 mm) and the outermost ﬁnal track (r = 58 mm).

 

Admin
Solution

we can find the angular speed; this will give us the required linear speed at the position of the inner track,

\(\omega_{i}=\frac{v}{r_{i}}=\frac{1.3 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}}{2.8 \times 10^{-2} \mathrm{~m}}=56.5 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}\) \(=(56.5 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s})\left(\frac{1}{2 \pi} \mathrm{rev} / \mathrm{rad}\right)(60 \mathrm{~s} / \mathrm{min}) \) \(=5.4 \times 10^{2} \mathrm{rev} / \mathrm{min}\)

For the outer track,

\(w_{f} =\frac{v}{r_{f}}=\frac{1.3 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}}{5.8 \times 10^{-2} \mathrm{~m}}=22.4 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s} \) \(=2.1 \times 10^{2} \mathrm{rev} / \mathrm{min}\)

The player adjusts the angular speed \(\omega\) of the disc within this range so that in formation moves past the objective lens at a constant rate. These angular velocity values are positive because the direction of rotation is counterclockwise.

(b) The maximum playing time of a standard music CD is 74 minutes and 39 seconds. How many revolutions does the disc make during that time?

We know that the angular speed is always decreasing, and we assume that it is decreasing steadily, with \(\alpha\) constant. The time interval t is \( (74 min) (60 \mathrm{~s} / \mathrm{min})+39 s=4479 s. \) We are looking for the angular position \(\theta_{f},\) Where we set the initial angular position \(\theta_{i}=0\). We can use Equation 10.3 , replacing the average angular speed \(\bar{w}\) with its mathematical equivalent \(\left(\omega_{i}+\omega_{f}\right) / 2\)

\(\theta_{f}= \theta_{i}+\frac{1}{2}\left(\omega_{i}+\omega_{f}\right) t\) \(= 0+\frac{1}{2}(540 \mathrm{rev} / \mathrm{min}+210 \mathrm{rev} / \mathrm{min})\) \( (1 \mathrm{~min} / 60 \mathrm{~s})(4479 \mathrm{~s}) \) \(=2.8 \times 10^{4} \mathrm{rev}\)

(c) What total length of track moves past the objective lens during this time?

Because we know the (constant) linear velocity and the time interval, th is is a straightforward calculation:

\(x_{f}=v_{i} t=(1.3 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s})(4479 \mathrm{~s})=5.8 \times 10^{3} \mathrm{~m}\)

More than 3.6 miles of track spins past the objective lens!

(d) What is the angular acceleration of the CD over the 4479 s time interval? Assume that \(\alpha\) is constant.

We have several choices for approaching this problem. Let us use the most direct approach by utilizing Equation 10.5, which is based on the definition of the term We are seeking. We should obtain a negative number for the angular acceleration because the disc spins more and more slowly in the pasitive direction as time goes on. Our answer should also be fairly small because it takes such a long time more than an hour – for the change in angular speed to be accomplished:

\(\alpha =\frac{w_{f}-\omega_{i}}{t}=\frac{22.4 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}-56.5 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}}{4479 \mathrm{~s}} \) \(=-7.6 \times 10^{-3} \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}^{2}\)

The disc experiences a very gradual decrease in its rotation rate, as expected.
