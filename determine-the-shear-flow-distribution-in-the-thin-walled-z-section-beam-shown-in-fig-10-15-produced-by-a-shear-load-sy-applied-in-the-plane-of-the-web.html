Guest Question.

Determine the shear flow distribution in the thin-walled Z-section beam shown in Fig. 10.15 produced by a shear load \( S_{y} \) applied in the plane of the web.

Admin
Solution

The origin for our system of reference axes coincides with the centroid of the section at the mid-point of the web. The centroid is also the centre of antisymmetry of the section so that the shear load, applied through this point, causes no twisting of the section and the shear flow distribution is given by Eq. (10.22) in which \( S_{z}=0 \), i.e

\(q_{s}=\frac{\left(S_{y} I_{z y}-S_{z} I_{z}\right)}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t z d s+\frac{\left(S_{z} I_{z y}-S_{y} I_{y}\right)}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s\) (10.22)

 

\(q_{s}=\frac{S_{y} I_{z y}}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t z d s-\frac{S_{y} I_{y}}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s\) (i)

The second moments of area of the section about the z and y axes have previously been calculated in and are

\(I_{z}=\frac{h^{3} t}{3} I_{y}=\frac{h^{3} t}{12} I_{z y}=\frac{h^{3} t}{8}\)

Substituting these values in Eq. (i) we obtain

\(q_{s}=\frac{S_{y}}{h^{3}} \int_{0}^{s}(10.29 z-6.86 y) d s\)

On the upper flange \(AB , y=+h / 2 \text { and } z=h / 2-s_{ A } \text { where } 0 \leq s_{ A } \leq h / 2 \). Therefore

\(q_{ AB }=\frac{S_{y}}{h^{3}} \int_{0}^{s_{ A }}\left(1.72 h-10.29 s_{ A }\right) d s_{ A }\)

which gives

\(q_{ AB }=\frac{S_{y}}{h^{3}}\left(1.72 h s_{ A }-5.15 s_{ A }^{2}\right)\) (ii)

Thus at \( A \left(s_{ A }=0\right), q_{ A }=0 \text { and at } B \left(s_{ A }=h / 2\right), q_{ B }=-0.43 s _{ y } / h \).Note that the order of the suffixes of q in Eq. (ii) denotes the positive direction of q (and \( s_{ A } \)). An examination of Eq. (ii) shows that the shear flow distribution on the upperflange is parabolic with a change of sign (i.e. direction) at \( s_{ A }=0.33 h \). For values of \( s_{ A }<0.33 h, q_{ AB } \) is positive and is therefore in the same direction as \( s_{ A } \). Furthermore, \( q_{ AB } \) has a turning value between \( s_{ A }=0 \text { and } s_{ A }=0.33 h \text { at a value of } s_{ A } \) given by

\(\frac{ d q_{ AB }}{ d s_{A}}=1.72 h-10.29 s_{A}=0\)

i.e. at \( s_{ A }=0.17 h \). The corresponding value of \( q_{ AB } \) is then, from Eq. (ii), \( q_{ AB }=0.14 S_{y} / h \). In the web BC, \( y=+h / 2-s_{ B } \text { where } 0 \leq s_{ B } \leq h \text { and } z=0 \). Thus

\(q_{ BC }=\frac{S_{y}}{h^{3}} \int_{0}^{s_{ B }}\left(6.86 s_{ B }-3.43 h\right) d s_{ B }+q_{ B }\) (iii)

Note that in Eq. (iii), \( q_{ BC } \text { is not zero when } s_{ B }=0 \) but equal to the value obtained by inserting \( s_{ A }=h / 2 \) in Eq. (ii), i.e. \( q_{ B }=-0.43 S_{y} / h \). Integrating the first two terms on the right-hand side of Eq. (iii) we obtain

\(q_{ BC }=\frac{S_{y}}{h^{3}}\left(3.43 s_{ B }^{2}-3.43 h s_{ B }-0.43 h^{2}\right)\) (iv)

Equation (iv) gives a parabolic shear flow distribution in the web, symmetrical about Gz and with a maximum value at \( s_{ B }=h / 2 \text { equal to }-1.29 S_{y} / h ; q_{ AB } \) is negative at all points in the web.

The shear flow distribution in the lower flange may be deduced from antisymmetry; the complete distribution is shown in Fig. 10.16.
