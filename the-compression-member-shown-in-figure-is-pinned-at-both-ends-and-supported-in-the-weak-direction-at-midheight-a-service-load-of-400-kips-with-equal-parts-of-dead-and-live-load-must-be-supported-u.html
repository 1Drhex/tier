Guest Question.

The compression member shown in Figure is pinned at both ends and supported in the weak direction at midheight. A service load of 400 kips, with equal parts of dead and live load, must be supported. Use \(F_{y}\) = 50 ksi and select the lightest W-shape.

Admin
Solution

Factored load =\( P_{u}\) = 1.2(200) + 1.6(200) = 560 kips

Assume that the weak direction controls and enter the column load table (Table 4-1a,for \(F_{y}\) = 50 ksi) with \(L_{c}\) = 9 feet. Beginning with the smallest shapes, the first one foundthat will work is a W8 \(\times\) 58 with a design strength of 634 kips.
\(\textbf{Check the strong axis:}\)
\(\frac{L_{cx}}{r_{x}/r_{y}}=\frac{18}{1.74}=10.34 ft > 9 ft\)
\(\therefore L_{cx}\) controls for this shape.

Enter the table with \(L_{c}\) = 10.34 feet. A W8 \(\times\) 58 has an interpolated strength of

\(\phi_{c}P_{n}\) = 596 kips > 560 kips (OK)
Next, investigate the W10 shapes. Try a W10 \(\times\) 49 with a design strength of 568 kips.
\(\textbf{Check the strong axis:}\)
\(\frac{L_{cx}}{r_{x}/r_{y}}=\frac{18}{1.71}=10.53 ft > 9 ft\)
\(\therefore L_{cx}\) controls for this shape.
Enter the table with \(L_{c} = 10.53 \)feet. A W10 \(\times\) 54 is the lightest W10, with an interpolated design strength of 594 kips.
Continue the search and investigate a W12 \(\times\) 53 (\(\phi_{c}P_{n}\) = 611 kips for \(L_{c} = 9 ft\)):

\(\frac{L_{cx}}{r_{x}/r_{y}}=\frac{18}{2.11}=8.53 ft < 9 ft \)
\(\therefore L_{cy}\) controls for this shape, and \(\phi_{c}P_{n}\) = 611 kips.
Determine the lightest W14. The lightest one with a possibility of working is a W14 \(\times\) 61. It is heavier than the lightest one found so far, so it will not be considered.
Use a W12\( \times \)53.
The required load capacity is P = 400 kips. Assume that the weak direction controls and enter the column load tables with \(L_{c}\) = 9 feet. Beginning with the smallest shapes, the first one found that will work is a W8 \(\times\) 58 with an allowable strength of 422 kips.
\(\textbf{Check the strong axis:}\)
\(\frac{L_{cx}}{r_{x}/r_{y}}=\frac{18}{1.74}=10.34 ft > 9 ft\)
\(\therefore L_{cx} \)controls for this shape.
Enter the table with \(L_{c}\) = 10.34 feet. A W8 \(\times\) 58 has an interpolated strength of
\(\frac{P_{n}}{\Omega_{c}}\) = 397 kips < 400 kips (N.G.)
The next lightest W8 that will work is a W8 \(\times\) 67.
\(\frac{L_{cx}}{r_{x}/r_{y}}=\frac{18}{1.75}=10.29 ft > 9 ft\)
The interpolated allowable strength is
\(\frac{P_{n}}{\Omega_{c}}= 460 kips > 400 kips (OK)\)
Next, investigate the W10 shapes. Try a W10 \(\times\) 60.
\(\frac{L_{cx}}{r_{x}/r_{y}}=\frac{18}{1.71}=10.53 ft > 9 ft\)
The interpolated strength is
\(\frac{P_{n}}{\Omega_{c}} = 444 kips > 400 kips (OK)\)
\(\textbf{Check the W12 shapes:}\) Try a W12 \(\times\) 53 (\(P_{n}/\Omega_{c}\) = 407 kips for \(L_{c}\) = 9 ft).
\(\frac{L_{cx}}{r_{x}/r_{y}}=\frac{18}{2.11}\)=8.53 ft < 9 ft
\(\therefore L_{cy}\) controls for this shape, and \(P_{n}/\Omega_{c}\) = 407 kips.
Find the lightest W14. The lightest one with a possibility of working is a W14 \(\times\) 61.Since it is heavier than the lightest one found so far, it will not be considered.
Use a W12 \(\times\) 53.
