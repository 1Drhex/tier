Guest Question.

In Figure P3.15, rigid bar ABC is supported by axial member (1), which has a cross-sectional area of 400 \(mm ^{2}\), an elastic modulus of E = 70 GPa, and a coefficient of thermal expansion of α = 22.5 × \(10^{-6}\) /°C. After load P is applied to the rigid bar and the temperature rises 40°C, a strain gage affixed to member (1) measures a strain increase of 2,150 με. Determine:
(a) the normal stress in member (1).
(b) the magnitude of applied load P.
(c) the deflection of the rigid bar at C.

Admin
Solution

(a) The strain measured in member (1) is due to both the internal force in the member and the temperature change. The strain caused by the temperature change is

\(\varepsilon_{T}=\alpha \Delta T=\left(22.5 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} C \right)\left(40^{\circ} C \right)=0.000900 mm / mm\)

Since the total strain is \(\varepsilon=2,150 \mu \varepsilon=0.002150 mm / mm\) , the strain caused by the internal force in member (1) must be

\(\varepsilon_{\sigma}=\varepsilon-\varepsilon_{T}=0.002150 mm / mm -0.000900 mm / mm =0.001250 mm / mm\)

The elastic modulus of member (1) is E = 70 GPa; thus, from Hooke’s Law, the stress in the member is:

\(\sigma_{1}=E \varepsilon_{\sigma}=(70,000 MPa )(0.001250 mm / mm )=87.5 MPa\)

 

(b) If the normal stress in member (1) is 87.5 MPa, the axial force in the member is

\(F_{1}=\sigma_{1} A_{1}=\left(87.5 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(400 mm ^{2}\right)=35,000 N\)

Consider moment equilibrium of rigid bar ABC about joint A to determine the magnitude of P:

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma M_{A}=(1.75 m )(35,000 N )-(3.0 m ) P=0 \\&\therefore P=20,417 N =20.4 kN\end{aligned}\)

 

(c) The strain in member (1) was measured as \(\varepsilon=2,150 \mu \varepsilon=0.002150 mm / mm\) ; therefore, the total elongation of member (1) is

\(\delta_{1}=\varepsilon_{1} L_{1}=(0.002150 mm / mm )(3,750 mm )=8.0625 mm\)

The deflection of the rigid bar at B is equal to this elongation; therefore, \(v_{B}=\delta_{1}=8.0625 mm\) (downward). By similar triangles, the deflection of the rigid bar at C is given by:

\(\begin{aligned}&\frac{v_{B}}{1.75 m }=\frac{v_{C}}{3.0 m } \\&\therefore v_{C}=v_{B} \frac{3.0 m }{1.75 m }=(8.0625 mm ) \frac{3.0}{1.75}=13.821429 mm =13.82 mm \downarrow\end{aligned}\)
