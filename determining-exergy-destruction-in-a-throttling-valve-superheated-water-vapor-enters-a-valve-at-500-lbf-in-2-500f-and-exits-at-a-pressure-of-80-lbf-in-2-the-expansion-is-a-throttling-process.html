Determining Exergy Destruction in a Throttling Valve

Superheated water vapor enters a valve at 500 \(lbf / in .^{2}\), 500°F and exits at a pressure of 80 \(lbf / in .^{2}\) The expansion is a throttling process. Determine the exergy destruction per unit of mass flowing, in Btu/lb. Let \(T_{0}=77^{\circ} F , p_{0}=1\) atm.

Admin
Solution

Known Water vapor expands in a throttling process through a valve from a specified inlet state to a specified exit pressure.

Find Determine the exergy destruction per unit of mass flowing.

Schematic and Given Data:

Engineering Model

1. The control volume shown in the accompanying figure is at steady state.

2. For the throttling process, \(\dot{Q}_{ cv }=\dot{W}_{ cv }=0\), and the effects of motion and gravity can be ignored.

3. \(T_{0}=77^{\circ} F , p_{0}=1\) atm.

Analysis The state at the inlet is specified. The state at the exit can be fixed by reducing the steady-state mass and energy rate balances to obtain Eq. 4.22:

 

\(h_{2}=h_{1} \quad\left(p_{2}<p_{1}\right)\) (4.22)

 

\(h_{2}=h_{1}\) (a)

 

Thus, the exit state is fixed by \(p_{2} \text { and } h_{2}\). From , \(h_{1}=1231.5\) Btu/lb, \(s_{1}=1.4923\) Btu/lb ⋅ °R. Interpolating at a pressure of 80 \(lbf / in .^{2}\) with \(h_{2}=h_{1}\), the specific entropy at the exit is \(s_{2}=1.680\) Btu/lb ⋅ °R.

With assumptions listed, the steady-state form of the exergy rate balance, Eq. 7.17, reduces to

 

\(0=\sum_{j}\left(1-\frac{T_{0}}{T_{j}}\right) \dot{Q}_{j}-\dot{W}_{ cv }+\dot{m}\left( e _{ f 1}- e _{ f 2}\right)-\dot{ E }_{ d }\) (7.17)

 

\(0=\sum_{j}\left(1-\frac{T_{0}}{T_{j}}\right) \dot{Q}_{j}^{\nearrow0}-\dot{W}_{ cv }^{\nearrow0}+\dot{m}\left( e _{ f 1}- e _{ f 2}\right)-\dot{ E }_{ d }\)

 

Dividing by the mass flow rate \(\dot{m}\) and solving, the exergy destruction per unit of mass flowing is

 

\(\frac{\dot{ E }_{ d }}{\dot{m}}=\left( e _{ f 1}- e _{ f 2}\right)\) (b)

 

Introducing Eq. 7.18, using Eq. (a), and ignoring the effects of motion and gravity

 

\(e _{ f 1}- e _{ f 2}=\left(h_{1}-h_{2}\right)-T_{0}\left(s_{1}-s_{2}\right)+\frac{ V _{1}^{2}- V _{2}^{2}}{2}+g\left(z_{1}-z_{2}\right)\) (7.18)

 

\(e _{ f 1}- e _{ f 2}=\left(h_{1} – h_{2}\right)^{\nearrow0}-T_{0}\left(s_{1}-s_{2}\right)+\frac{ V _{1}^{2}-V _{2}^{2}}{2}^{\nearrow0} +g\left(z_{1} – z_{2}\right)^{\nearrow0}\)

 

Eq. (b) becomes

 

1 \(\frac{\dot{ E }_{ d }}{\dot{m}}=T_{0}\left(s_{2}-s_{1}\right)\) (c)

 

Inserting values,

 

2 \(\frac{\dot{ E }_{ d }}{\dot{m}}=537^{\circ} R (1.680-1.4923) \frac{ Btu }{ lb \cdot{ }^{\circ} R }=100.8 Btu / lb\)

 

1 Equation (c) can be obtained alternatively beginning with the relationship \(\dot{ E }_{ d }=T_{0} \dot{\sigma}_{ cv }\) and then evaluating the rate of entropy production \(\dot{\sigma}_{ cv }\) from an entropy balance. The details are left as an exercise.

2 Energy is conserved in the throttling process, but exergy is destroyed. The source of the exergy destruction is the uncontrolled expansion that occurs.
