Guest Question.

If the beam shown in Figure 5.32 is subjected to the temperature change described in Figures 5.29b and c and in Equations 5.45 and 5.46 over its entire length, calculate the deflected shape using:
(i) the method of double integration
(ii) the system of differential equations in terms of displacements
Use the expressions derived in Reflection Activity 5.2 in your solution. Assume the beam is uniform throughout, with area A, second moment of area I, elastic modulus E and coefficient of thermal expansion α.

\( ε_T = ε_{T,r} − y_{κ_T} \) (5.45).

\( \varepsilon _{T.r} =\biggl[T_1 + (T_2 - T_1)\frac{y_t}{h}\biggr] \) \( κ_T = α \frac{T_2 - T_1}{h} \) (5.46a,b).

Admin
Solution

(i) Method of double integration

The expression for the internal moment is M = 0 as no external loads are applied to the cantilever beam. Based on Equation 5.50b, the curvature along the length of the beam can be written as:

\( κ = \frac{M}{EI} + κ_T \) (5.50 b).

\( κ = \frac{0}{EI} + κ_T = κ_T \) (5.54a).

from which the expressions for the rotation and deflection become

\( \theta = \int{\kappa _T} dx + C_{v1 }= κ_T x + C_{v1} \) (5.54b).

\( v = \iint{\kappa dx } dx + C_{v1}x + C_{v2} = \frac{\kappa _T}{2} x^2 + C_{v1}x + C_{v2} \) (5.54c).

Constants of integration \( C_{v1} \) and \( C_{v2} \) are obtained by applying the following boundary conditions: v(x = 0) = 0 and \( v^{\prime }(x = 0) = 0 \), which lead to \( C_{v1} = 0 \) and \( C_{v2} = 0 \). Based on these, the expression for deflection becomes:

\( v = \frac{κ_T}{2} x^2 \) (5.55).

(ii) System of differential equations expressed in terms of displacements
The six constants of integration included in the system of differential equation (Equations 5.41) are determined applying the following boundary conditions at the ends of the beam (using Equations 5.42 and 5.43) and recalling that the member is unloaded, i.e. w = n = 0 (see Figures 5.30 and 5.31):

\( EIν^{IV} − w = 0 \) (5.41a).

\( EAu^{\prime \prime } + n = 0 \) (5.41b).

\( ν^{IV} = \frac{w}{EI} \) (5.42a).

\( \nu ^{\prime \prime \prime } = \int{\frac{w}{EI} } dx + C_{\nu 1} \) (5.42b).

\( \nu ^{\prime \prime } = \iint{ \frac{w}{EI}dx} dx + C_{\nu 1}x + C_{\nu 2} \) (5.42c).

\( \nu ^{\prime } = \iiint{ \frac{w}{EI}dx dx} dx + C_{\nu 1}\frac{x^2}{2} + C_{\nu 2}x + C_{\nu 3} \) (5.42d).

\( \nu = ∫∫∫∫\frac{w}{EI} dx dx dx dx + C_{\nu 1}\frac{x^3}{6} + C_{\nu 2}\frac{x^2}{2}+ C_{\nu 3}x + C_{\nu 4} \) (5.42e).

\( u^{\prime \prime } = -\frac{n}{EA} \) (5.43a).

\( u ^{\prime } = -\int{\frac{n}{EA} } dx + C_{u 1} \) (5.43b).

\( u = – \iint{ \frac{n}{EA}dx} dx + C_{u 1}x + C_{u 2} \) (5.43c).

\( u(x = 0) = C_{u2} = 0 \); \( u ^{\prime }(x = L) = C_{u1} = ε_{T,r} \).

\( v (x = 0) = C_{v4} = 0 \); \( v^{\prime }(x = 0) = C_{v3} = 0\).

\( v ^{\prime \prime \prime } (x = L)= C_{v1} L = 0 \);

\( v ^{\prime \prime } (x = L)= C_{v1} L + C_{v2} = κ_T \).

from which \( C_{v1} = C_{v3} = C_{v4} = C_{u2} = 0, C_{u1} = ε_{T,r} \) and \( C_{v2} = κ_T \) and the expressions for u and v become:

\( u = ε_{T,r} x \), \( v = \frac{κ_T}{2} x^2 \) (5.56a,b).

As expected, the results obtained for v from the two methods are identical, as observed by comparing Equations 5.55 and 5.56b.
