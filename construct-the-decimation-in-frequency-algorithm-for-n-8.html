Guest Question.

Construct the decimation-in-frequency algorithm for N = 8.

Admin
Solution

From Eqs. (7.52a) and (7.52b), we have

 

\(X(2 k)=\sum_{n=0}^{N / 2-1} x_{10}(n) W_{N / 2}^{-k n}=X_{10}(k)\) (7.52a)

 

\(X(2 k+1)=\sum_{n=0}^{N / 2-1} x_{11}(n) W_{N / 2}^{-k n}=X_{11}(k)\) (7.52b)

 

\(X_{10}(k)=X(2 k) \quad \text { and } \quad X_{11}(k)=X(2 k+1)\)

 

for k = 0, 1, 2, 3. Hence

 

\(X_{10}(k)=\{X(0), X(2), X(4), X(6)\}\) (7.58a)

 

\(X_{11}(k)=\{X(1), X(3), X(5), X(7)\}\) (7.58b)

 

From Eqs. (7.54a)–(7.54d), we have

 

\(X(4 k)=\sum_{n=0}^{N / 4-1} x_{20}(n) W_{N / 4}^{-k n}=X_{20}(k)\) (7.54a)

 

\(X(4 k+2)=\sum_{n=0}^{N / 4-1} x_{21}(n) W_{N / 4}^{-k n}=X_{21}(k)\) (7.54b)

 

\(X(4 k+1)=\sum_{n=0}^{N / 4-1} x_{22}(n) W_{N / 4}^{-k n}=X_{22}(k)\) (7.54c)

 

\(X(4 k+3)=\sum_{n=0}^{N / 4-1} x_{23}(n) W_{N / 4}^{-k n}=X_{23}(k)\) (7.54d)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&X_{20}(k)=X(4 k) \quad X_{21}(k)=X(4 k+2) \\&X_{22}(k)=X(4 k+1) \quad X_{23}(k)=X(4 k+3)\end{aligned}\)

 

for k = 0, 1. Thus Eqs. (7.58a) and (7.58b), give

 

\(X_{20}(k)=\{X(0), X(4)\} \quad X_{21}(k)=\{X(2), X(6)\}\) (7.59a)

 

\(X_{22}(k)=\{X(1), X(5)\} \quad X_{23}(k)=\{X(3), X(7)\}\) (7.59b)

 

Finally, from Eqs. (7.56a) and (7.56b) or Eqs. (7.57a)–(7.57c), the elements of the 3rd cycle can be generated by replacing k in the 2nd cycle first by 2k and then by 2k + 1, that is,

 

\(X\left(2^{m} k\right)=\sum_{n=0}^{N_{1}-1} x_{m 0}(n) W_{N_{1}}^{-k n}=X_{m 0}(k)\) (7.56a)

 

\(X\left(2^{m} k+2^{m-1}\right)=\sum_{n=0}^{N_{1}-1} x_{m 1}(n) W_{N_{1}}^{-k n}=X_{m 1}(k)\) (7.56b)

 

\(X(\stackrel{\leftarrow}0)=x_{r 0}(0)\) (7.57a)

 

\(X(\stackrel{\leftarrow}1)=x_{r 1}(0)\) (7.57b)

 

\(X(\stackrel{\leftarrow}p)=x_{r p}(0)\) (7.57c)

 

\(\begin{array}{lc}X_{30}(k)=X[4(2 k)] \quad X_{31}(k)=X[4(2 k+1)] \\\left.X_{32}(k)=X[4(2 k)+2)\right] \quad X_{33}(k)=X[4(2 k+1)+2] \\X_{34}(k)=X[4(2 k)+1] \quad X_{35}(k)=X[4(2 k+1)+1] \\X_{36}(k)=X[4(2 k)+3] \quad X_{37}(k)=X[4(2 k+1)+3]\end{array}\)

 

for k = 0. Hence from Eqs. (7.59a) and (7.59b), we obtain

 

\(\begin{array}{ll}X_{30}(0)=X(0)=x(0) & X_{31}(0)=X(4)=x(4) \\X_{32}(0)=X(2)=x(2) & X_{33}(0)=X(6)=x(6) \\X_{34}(0)=X(1)=x(1) & X_{35}(0)=X(5)=x(5) \\X_{36}(0)=X(3)=x(3) & X_{37}(0)=X(7)=x(7)\end{array}\)

 

The complete algorithm is illustrated in Fig. 7.27.
