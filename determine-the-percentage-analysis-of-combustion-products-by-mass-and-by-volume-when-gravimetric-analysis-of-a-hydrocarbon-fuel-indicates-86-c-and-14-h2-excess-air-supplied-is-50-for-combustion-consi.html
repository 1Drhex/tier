Solution
\(\text{Theoretical mass of oxygen required per kg of fuel depending upon its’ constituents}\\\begin{aligned}C + O_{2}&→ CO_{2}\\12 kg + 32 kg&→ 44 kg\\\text{or,} \qquad \qquad \qquad 1 kg + \frac{8}{3} kg& → \frac{11}{3} kg\\\text{or,}\qquad \qquad 0.86 kg + 2.29 kg&→ 3.15 kg\\\text{For hydrogen,} \qquad\qquad \qquad &\\2H_{2} + O_{2}&→ 2H_{2}O\\4 kg + 32 kg&→ 36 kg\\1 kg + 8 kg&→ 9 kg\\0.14 kg + 1.12 kg&→ 1.26 kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Hence, minimum mass of oxygen required kg per kg of fuel = }\left\lgroup0.86 × \frac{8}{3} + 0.14 × 8 \right\rgroup = 3.41 kg\\\text{As it is given that 50}\%\text{ excess air is supplied so the excess oxygen = }3.41 × 0.5 = 1.705 kg\\\text{Total oxygen supplied }= 3.41 + 1.705 = 5.115 kg\\\text{Constituents of exhaust gases per kg of fuel, CO}_{2} = \frac{11}{3} × 0.86 = 3.15 kg\\\begin{aligned}H_{2}O&= 9 × 0.14 = 1.26 kg\\N_{2}&= \left\lgroup\frac{5.115 \times 76.8}{23.2} \right\rgroup = 16.932 kg\\\text{Excess }O_{2}&= 1.705 kg\end{aligned}\\\text{For one kg of fuel the composition of combustion products is as under,}\)
