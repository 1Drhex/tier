Guest Question.

A music wire helical compression spring is needed to support a 20-lbf load after being compressed 2 in. Because of assembly considerations the solid height cannot exceed 1 in and the free length cannot be more than 4 in. Design the spring.

From Fig. 10–3 and Table 10–6,

\(S_{sy} = 0.45 \frac{201 000}{0.080^{0.145}}= 130 455 \ psi\)
From Fig. 10–3 or Eq. (10–23)

\(C=\frac{2\alpha-\beta}{4 \beta}+\sqrt{\left(\frac{2 \alpha – \beta}{4 \beta}\right)^2-\frac{3 \alpha}{4 \beta}} \) \(\alpha = \frac{S_{sy}}{n_s}=\frac{ 130 455}{1.2}= 108 713 \ psi \\ \beta = \frac{8 \left(1 + \xi\right)F_{max}}{\pi d^2}= \frac{8 \left(1 + 0.15\right)20}{\pi \left(0.080^2\right)}= 9151.4 \ psi \\ C = \frac{2 \left(108 713\right) − 9151.4}{4 \left(9151.4\right)}+\sqrt{\left[\frac{2\left(108713\right)-9151.4 }{4\left(9151.4\right) } \right]^2 – \frac{3 \left(108 713\right)}{4\left(9151.4\right)}} = 10.53 \)

Continuing with Fig. 10–3:
\(D = Cd = 10.53\left(0.080\right) = 0.8424 \ in \\ K_B = \frac{4(10.53) + 2}{4\left(10.53\right) − 3}= 1.128 \\ \tau_s = 1.128\frac{8 \left(1 + 0.15\right)20\left(0.8424\right)}{\pi \left(0.080\right)^3}= 108 700 psi \\ n_s = \frac{130 445}{108 700}= 1.2 \\ OD = 0.843 + 0.080 = 0.923 \ in \\ N_a = \frac{11.75 \left(10^6\right)0.080^4 \left(2\right)}{8 \left(0.843\right)^3 20}= 10.05 \ turns \\ N_t = 10.05 + 2 = 12.05 \ total \ turns \\ L_s = 0.080\left(12.05\right) = 0.964 \ in \\ L_0 = 0.964 + \left(1 + 0.15\right)2 = 3.264 \ in \\ \left(L\right)_{cr} = 2.63\left({0.843}/{0.5}\right) = 4.43 \ in \\ fom = {−2.6\pi^2 \left(0.080\right)^2 12.05\left(0.843\right)}/{4} = −0.417 \)

Now examine the table and perform the adequacy assessment. The shading of the table indicates values outside the range of recommended or specified values. The spring index constraint \(4 \leq C \leq 12\) rules out diameters larger than 0.085 in. The constraint \(3 \leq N_a \leq 15 \) rules out wire diameters less than 0.075 in. The \(L_s \leq 1\) constraint rules out diameters less than 0.080 in. The \(L0 \leq 4\) constraint rules out diameters less than 0.071 in. The buckling criterion rules out free lengths longer than \(\left(L_0\right)_{cr}\), which rules out diameters less than 0.075 in. The factor of safety \(n_s\) is exactly 1.20 because the mathematics forced it. Had the spring been in a hole or over a rod, the helix diameter would be chosen without reference to \(\left(n_s\right)_d\). The result is that there are only two springs in the feasible domain, one with a wire diameter of 0.080 in and the other with a wire diameter of 0.085. The figure of merit decides and the decision is the design with 0.080 in wire diameter.
