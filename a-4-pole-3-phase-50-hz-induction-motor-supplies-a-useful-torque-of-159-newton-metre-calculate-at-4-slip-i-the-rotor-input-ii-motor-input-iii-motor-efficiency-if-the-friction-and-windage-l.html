Guest Question.

A 4-pole, 3-phase, 50 Hz induction motor supplies a useful torque of 159 Newton-metre. Calculate at 4% slip: (i) The rotor input; (ii) Motor input; (iii) Motor efficiency, if the friction and windage losses total 500 watt and stator losses are 1000 watt.

Admin
Solution

No. of poles, \(P = 4\); Frequency, \(f = Hz\)

Torque at shaft, \(T_{m} = 159 Nm\); Slip, \(S = 4\% = 0.04\)

\(Mechanical losses = 500 W\); \(Stator losses = 1000 W\)

 

Synchronous speed, \(N_{s} = \frac{120 f}{P} = \frac{120 \times 50}{4} = 1500 r.p.m.\)

Rotor speed, \(N = N_{s} \left(1 – S\right) = 1500 \left(1 – 0.04\right) = 1440 rpm\)

Angular speed, \(\omega = \frac{2 \pi N}{60} \)

\(Rotor output = \omega T_{m} = \frac{2 \pi N}{60} T_{m} = \frac{2\pi \times 1440}{60}\times 159 = 23977 W \)

 

\( Mech. power developed in rotor = Rotor output + Mech. losses = 23977 + 500 = 24477 W \)

 

\( Rotor Cu loss = \frac{S}{1 – S} mech. power developed = \frac{0.04}{1 – 0.04} \times 24477 = 1020 W \)

 

\(\therefore Rotor input = Mech. power developed + Rotor Cu loss \\[0.5cm] \hspace{30 pt} \quad \quad \quad \quad = 24477 + 1020 = \mathbf{25497 W}\)

 

\(Motor input = Rotor input + Stator losses = 25497 + 1000 = \mathbf{26497 W }\)

 

Motor efficiency, \( \eta = \frac{Output}{Input} \times 100 = \frac{23977}{26497} \times 100 =\mathbf{90.49 \%} \)
