Guest Question.

In a double pipe heat exchanger, \(\dot{m}_hC_{p, h} = 0.5\dot{m}_c C_{p,c}\). The inlet temperatures of hot and cold fluids are \(T_{h, i}\) and \(T_{c, i}.\) Deduce an expression in terms of \(T_{h, i}, T_{c, i}\) and \(T_{h, o}\) for the ratio of area of counter flow heat exchanger to that of parallel flow heat exchanger, which will give same hot fluid outlet temperature \(T_{h, o}\). Find the ratio, if \(T_{h, i} = 150°C, T_{c, i} = 30°C\) and \(T_{h, o} = 90°C.\)

Admin
Solution

Given : A double pipe heat exchanger, with
\(T_{h, i} = 150°C, T_{h, o} = 90°C and T_{c, i} = 30°C\)
\(\dot{m}_hC_{p, h} = 0.5\dot{m}_c C_{p,c} and T_{h, o, parallel }= T_{h, o, counter}\)
Analysis : The heat capacity rates for two fluids are related as :
\(\dot{m}_hC_{p, h} = 0.5\dot{m}_c C_{p,c} \)
which yields to
\(C_{min }= \dot{m}_hC_{p, h} = C_h\)
\(C_{max} = \dot{m}_c C_{p,c} = C_c\)
and capacity ratio C = 0.5 The effectiveness of heat exchanger
\(ε = \frac{C_h (T_{h, i} – T_{h, o})}{C_{min }(T_{h, i} – T_{c, i})}= \frac{C_c (T_{c, o} – T_{c, i})}{C_{min }(T_{h, i} – T_{c, i})}\)
Using \(C_h = 0.5C_c,\) or \(C_c = 2C_h\), then
\(ε = \frac{T_{h, i} – T_{h, o}}{T_{h, i} – T_{c, i}}= \frac{2 (T_{c, o} – T_{c, i})}{C_{min }(T_{h, i} – T_{c, i})}\)
or \(T_{c, o} = T_{c, i} + 0.5 (T_{h, i} – T_{h, o}) …(i)\)
Let \(A_{PF} \)= area of parallel flow arrangement
\(A_{CF}\) = area of counter flow arrangement.
As, \(T_{h, o, parallel} = T_{h, o, counter},\) therefore, the heat lost by hot fluid in both case be same.
Further, the heat transfer rate is also expressed
as\(Q = UA_{PF} ∆T_{lm, parallel} = UA_{CF} ∆T_{lm, counter}\)
or \(\frac{A_{CF}}{A_{PF}} =\frac{∆T_{lm, parallel}}{∆T_{lm, counter}}…(ii)\)
(∵ U is constant in both cases)
(a) For counter flow arrangement
\(∆T_1 =T_{h, i} – T_{c, o} = T_{h, i} – T_{c, i} – 0.5(T_{h, i} – T_{h, o})\)
\(= 0.5 T_{h, i} + 0.5 T_{h, o} – T_{c, i}\)
\(∆T_2 =T_{h, o} – T_{c, i}\)

 

\(∆T_{lm, counter} = \frac{∆T_1 – ∆T_2}{ln\left(\frac{∆T_1}{∆T_2} \right)}\)

 

\(=\frac{0.5T_{h, i} +0.5 T_{h, o} – T_{c, i} – T_{h, o} + T_{c, i}}{ln\left \{ \frac{0.5(T_{h, i} + T_{h, o}) – T_{c, i}}{T_{h, o} – T_{c, i}} \right \}}\)

 

\(=\frac{0.5(T_{h, i} + T_{h, o}) }{ln\left \{ \frac{0.5(T_{h, i} + T_{h, o}) – T_{c, i}}{T_{h, o} – T_{c, i}} \right \}}…(iii)\)
(b) For parallel flow arrangement
\(∆T_1 =T_{h, i} – T_{c,i} \)

 

\(∆T_2 =T_{h, o} – T_{c, o} = T_{h, o} – T_{c, i} – 0.5(T_{h, i} – T_{h, o})\)

 

\(= 1.5 T_{h, o} – 0.5 T_{h, i} – T_{c, i}\)

 

\(∆T_{lm, parallel} = \frac{∆T_1 – ∆T_2}{ln\left(\frac{∆T_1}{∆T_2} \right)}\)

 

\(=\frac{T_{h, i} – T_{c, i} – 1.5T_{h, o} + 0.5T_{h, i}+T_{c, i}}{ln\left \{ \frac{T_{h, i} – T_{c, i}}{1.5T_{h, o} – 0.5T_{h, i} – T_{c, i}} \right \}}\)

 

\(=\frac{1.5[T_{h, i} + T_{h, o}] }{ln\left [ \frac{T_{h, i} – T_{c, i}}{1.5T_{h, o} – 0.5T_{h, i} – T_{c, i}} \right ]}…(iv)\)
Substituting eqns. (iii) and (iv) in eqn. (ii),

 

\(\frac{A_{CF}}{A_{PF}} =\frac{1.5(T_{h, i} + T_{h, o}) }{ln\left [ \frac{T_{h, i} – T_{c, i}}{1.5T_{h, o} – 0.5T_{h, i} – T_{c, i}} \right ]} \times \frac{ln \left[ \frac{0.5(T_{h, i} + T_{h, o}) – T_{c, i} }{T_{h, o} – T_{c, i}} \right] }{0.5(T_{h, i} + T_{h, o})} \)

 

\(=3 \left [\frac{ln\left \{ \frac{0.5(T_{h, i} + T_{h, o})- T_{c, i}}{T_{h, o} – T_{c, i}} \right\}}{ln\left \{ \frac{T_{h, i} – T_{c, i}}{1.5T_{h, o} – 0.5T_{h, i} – T_{c, i}} \right\}} \right]\)

For given data :
\(T_{h, i} = 150°C, T_{c, i} = 30°C\) and

\(T_{h, o} = 90°C\)

 

\(\frac{A_{CF}}{A_{PF}} =3 \left [\frac{ln\left \{\frac{0.5(150 + 90)- 30}{90 – 30} \right\}}{ln\left \{\frac{150 – 30}{1.5 ×90 – 0.5 × 150 – 30} \right\}}\right]\)

 

\(= 3 × \frac{ln(1.5)}{ln(4)} =0.877.\)
