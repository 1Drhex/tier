Guest Question.

Composition of a fuel by volume is as follows:
\(\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad H_{2}& – 52\%\\CH_{4}& – 20\%\\CO& – 16\%\\CO_{2}& – 3\%\\O_{2}& – 2\%\\N_{2}& – 7\%\end{aligned}\)
Higher calorific value of fuel constituents \(\textbf{H}_{2}\), CO and \(\textbf{CH}_{4}\) may be taken as 28424 kJ/\(\textbf{m}^{3}\), 27463 kJ/\(\textbf{m}^{3}\), 87780 kJ/\(\textbf{m}^{3}\) at 1 bar and 0°C respectively. The latent heat of one kg of water for transformation to steam at 1 bar, 0°C may be taken as 2445 kJ. Air may be considered to have 21% \(\textbf{O}_{2}\) by volume. Determine
(a) the volume of air required for complete combustion of 1 \(\textbf{m}^{3}\) of fuel.
(b) the volumetric analysis of dry flue gases considering 20% excess air.
(c) the lower calorific value of fuel.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{Combustion reactions for constituents are,}\\\begin{aligned}2H_{2} + O_{2}&→ 2H_{2}O,\\\text{1 mol 0.5 mol}& \text{ 1 mol}\\CH_{4} + 2O_{2}&→ 2H_{2}O + CO_{2},\\\text{1 mol 2 mol}&\text{ 2 mol 1 mol}\\2CO + O_{2}&→ 2CO_{2},\\\text{1 mol 0.5 mol}&\text{ 1 mol}\end{aligned}\\\text{Minimum air required = 0.72 × }\frac{100}{21} = 3.43 m^{3}\\\text{Volume of air required for complete combustion }= 3.43 m^{3}\)
Constituents per m3 of fuel ( a) O2 required per m3 of constituents of fuel (b) O2 required per m3 of fuel

(c) = a × b

CH4 = 0.20 2 0.40 0.20 0.40 — —
CO = 0.16 0.5 0.08 0.16 — — —
CO2 = 0.03 — — 0.03 — — —

N2 = 0.07 — — — — — 3.322
\(\Sigma\)CO2 = 0.39 \(\Sigma\)H2O = 0.92 \(\Sigma\)O2 = 0.144 \(\Sigma\)N2 = 3.322
\(\qquad \qquad\text{Total combustion product per }m^{3}\text{ of fuel = 4.776 m}^{3}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \text{Total dry flue gases per }m^{3}\text{ of fuel = 3.856 m}^{3}\\\text{Excess air supplied is 20 }\% \text{ so total volume of air supplied = Minimum air required + excess air supplied.}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Excess air supplied}&= 0.2 × 3.43 = 0.686 m^{3}\\\text{Total air supplied}&= 3.43 + 0.686 = 4.116 m^{3}\text{ air per m}^{3}\text{ of fuel}\\^{**}\text{Oxygen present in excess air}&= 0.72 × 0.2 = 0.144 m^{3}, \text{ This will be with combustion products}\\\text{Nitrogen present in total air}&= \frac{4.116 × 79}{21} = 3.252 m^{3}\\^{\ast \ast }\text{Since 0.07 m}^{3} N_{2}\text{ per m}^{3}\text{ of fuel is}& \text{ already present in fuel so total nitrogen available in combustion products}\end{aligned}\\ = 3.252 + 0.07 = 3.322 m^{3} per m^{3}\text{ of fuel.}\\\begin{aligned}\text\%\text{by volume of CO}_{2}&= \frac{(0.20 + 0.16 + 0.03) × 100}{3 856} = 10.12\%\\\%\text{by volume of O}_{2}&= \frac{0.144 × 100}{3 856} = 3.73\%\\\%\text{by volume of N}_{2}&= \frac{3.322 × 100}{3.856} = 86.15\%\\\text{Volumetric composition of dry flue gases}& = 10.12\% CO_{2}\\& 3.73\% O_{2}, 86.15\% N_{2}\end{aligned}\\\text{Higher calorific value may be obtained from the values given for constituents }H_{2}, CH_{4}\text{ and CO.}\\\begin{aligned}&= \left\{ (0.52 × 28424) + (0.20 × 27463) + (0.16 × 87780)\right\} \\&= 34317.88 kJ/m^{3}\\\text{Steam produced}&\text{ during combustion of 1 m}^{3}\text{ of fuel.}\\m_{steam}&= \frac{0.92}{22.4} = 0.0411 kg\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Lower calorific value}&= \text{Higher calorific value} – (m_{steam} × \text{latent heat})\\&= \left\{ 34317.88 – (0.0411 × 2445)\right\} \\&= 34217.39 kJ/m^{3}\\\text{Lower calorific value}& = 34217.39 kJ/m^{3}\end{aligned}\)
