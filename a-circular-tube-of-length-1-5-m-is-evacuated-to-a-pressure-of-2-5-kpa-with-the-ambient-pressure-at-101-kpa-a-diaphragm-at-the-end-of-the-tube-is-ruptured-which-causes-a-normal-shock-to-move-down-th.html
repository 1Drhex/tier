Guest Question.

A circular tube of length 1.5 m is evacuated to a pressure of 2.5 kPa, with the ambient pressure at 101 kPa. A diaphragm at the end of the tube is ruptured, which causes a normal shock to move down the tube. Determine the velocity of the initial shock that moves down the tube, the velocity and Mach number of the air (γ = 1.4, R = 287 J/kg·K) behind the shock, and the velocity of the shock that reflects from the closed end. Initial air temperature before diaphragm rupture is 300 K. A test object is located midway along the tube. Determine the time that this object is subjected to the pressure and temperature conditions behind the initial shock (before arrival of the reflected shock). Find the static pressure and temperature behind the initial shock.

Admin
Solution

Initial Shock: Fix the shock by redefining the coordinate system

 

To begin we can calculate \(a_{1}=\sqrt{(1.4)(287)(300)}=347.1887\) m/ s . Since we are given \(p _{2}=101 kPa \text { and } p _{1}=2.5 kPa\), then

 

\(\frac{ p _{2}}{ p _{1}}=\frac{101}{2.5}=40.40\)

 

From Eq,(4.12)

 

\(\frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}=\frac{2 \gamma M_{1}^{2}}{\gamma+1}-\frac{\gamma-1}{\gamma+1}\) (4.12)

 

\(M _{1}=\sqrt{\left(\frac{\gamma+1}{2 \gamma}\right)\left(\frac{ p _{2}}{ p _{1}}+\frac{\gamma-1}{\gamma+1}\right)}=\sqrt{\frac{6}{7}\left(40.4+\frac{1}{6}\right)}=5.8967\)

 

\(S = M _{1} a _{1}=(5.8967) 347.1887=2047.2781 m / s\)

 

Now at \(M _{1}\) we can also find the density ratio and temperature ratio across the shock. The first ratio will give the velocity behind the shock, \(V _{2}\), and the second will produce \(a _{2}\).

 

\(\frac{\rho_{2}}{\rho_{1}}=5.2457, \frac{ T _{2}}{ T _{1}}=7.7016\)

 

Hence,

 

\(\frac{\rho_{2}}{\rho_{1}}=\frac{ S }{ S – V _{2}}=\frac{2047.2781}{2047.2781- V _{2}}=5.2457\)

 

From which we find, \(V _{2}=1,657.0007\) m/s (the velocity of the air behind shock). Also, \(T _{2}=\left(\frac{ T _{2}}{ T _{1}}\right) T _{1}=7.7016(300)=2,310.48 K\) (the temperature of the air behind the initial shock). Thus, \(a_{2}=\sqrt{(1.4)(287)(2310.48)}=963.5097 m / s\)

 

Mach number of the air behind the initial shock \(=\frac{ V _{2}}{ a _{2}}=\frac{1,657.0007}{963.5097}=1.7198\). The pressure behind the shock is \(p _{2}=101 kPa\).

 

Reflected Shock: Define a moving coordinate system for the reflected wave as usual.

 

Replace S and V in Eq.(5.10) with \(S _{ R }+ V _{2} \text { and } V _{2}\), respectively and rewrite the expression as

 

\(S=\left(\frac{\gamma+1}{4}\right) V+\sqrt{\left[\left(\frac{\gamma+1}{4}\right) V\right]^{2}+a_{1}^{2}}\) (5.10)

 

\(\begin{aligned}S _{ R } &=\left(\frac{\gamma-3}{4}\right) V _{2}+\sqrt{\left[\left(\frac{\gamma+1}{4}\right) V _{2}\right]^{2}+ a _{2}^{2}} \\&=-(0.4) 1657.0007+\sqrt{[(0.6) 1657.0007]^{2}+963.5097^{2}} \\&=721.6799 m / s\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\Delta t =\frac{0.75}{2047.2781}+\frac{0.75}{721.6799}=3.663 \times 10^{-4}+10.392 \times 10^{-4}=0.001406 s\)
