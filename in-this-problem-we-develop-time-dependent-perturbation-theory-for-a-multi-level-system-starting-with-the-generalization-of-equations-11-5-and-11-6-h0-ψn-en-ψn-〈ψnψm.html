Guest Question.

In this problem we develop time-dependent perturbation theory for a multi-level system, starting with the generalization of Equations 11.5 and 11.6:

\( \hat{H}^{0} \psi_{a}=E_{a} \psi_{a}, \quad \text { and } \quad \hat{H}^{0} \psi_{b}=E_{b} \psi_{b} \) (11.5).

\( \left\langle\psi_{i} \mid \psi_{j}\right\rangle=\delta_{i j}, \quad(i, j=a, b) \) (11.6).

\( \hat{H}_{0} \psi_{n}=E_{n} \psi_{n}, \quad\left\langle\psi_{n} \mid \psi_{m}\right\rangle=\delta_{n m} \). (11.113)

At time t = 0 we turn on a perturbation \( H^{\prime}(t) \) so that the total Hamiltonian is

\( \hat{H}=\hat{H}_{0}+\hat{H}^{\prime}(t) \) (11.114).

(a) Generalize Equation 11.10 to read

\( \Psi(t)=c_{a}(t) \psi_{a} e^{-i E_{a} t / \hbar}+c_{b}(t) \psi_{b} e^{-i E_{b} t / \hbar} \) (11.10).

\( \Psi(t)=\sum_{n} c_{n}(t) \psi_{n} e^{-i E_{n} t / \hbar} \) (11.115).

and show that

\( \dot{c}_{m}=-\frac{i}{\hbar} \sum_{n} c_{n} H_{m n}^{\prime} e^{i\left(E_{m}-E_{n}\right) t / \hbar} \) (11.116).

where

\( H_{m n}^{\prime} \equiv\left\langle\psi_{m}\left|\hat{H}^{\prime}\right| \psi_{n}\right\rangle \) (11.117).

(b) If the system starts out in the state \( \psi_{N} \), show that (in first-order perturbation theory)

\( c_{N}(t) \approx 1-\frac{i}{\hbar} \int_{0}^{t} H_{N N}^{\prime}\left(t^{\prime}\right) d t^{\prime} \) (11.118).

and

\( c_{m}(t) \approx-\frac{i}{\hbar} \int_{0}^{t} H_{m N}^{\prime}\left(t^{\prime}\right) e^{i\left(E_{m}-E_{N}\right) t^{\prime} / \hbar} d t^{\prime}, \quad(m \neq N) \) (11.119).

(c) For example, suppose \( \hat{H}^{\prime} \) is constant (except that it was turned on at t = 0 , and switched off again at some later time . Find the probability of transition from state N to state M \( (M \neq N) \), as a function of T. Answer:

\( 4\left|H_{M N}^{\prime}\right|^{2} \frac{\sin ^{2}\left[\left(E_{N}-E_{M}\right) T / 2 \hbar\right]}{\left(E_{N}-E_{M}\right)^{2}} \) (11.120).

(d) Now suppose \( \hat{H}^{\prime} \) is a sinusoidal function of time: \( \hat{H}^{\prime}=V \cos (\omega t) \) Making the usual assumptions, show that transitions occur only to states with energy \( E_{M}=E_{N} \pm \hbar \omega \), and the transition probability is

\( P_{N \rightarrow M}=\left|V_{M N}\right|^{2} \frac{\sin ^{2}\left[\left(E_{N}-E_{M} \pm \hbar \omega\right) T / 2 \hbar\right]}{\left(E_{N}-E_{M} \pm \hbar \omega\right)^{2}} \) (11.121).

(e) Suppose a multi-level system is immersed in incoherent electromagnetic radiation. Using Section 11.2.3 as a guide, show that the transition rate for stimulated emission is given by the same formula (Equation 11.54) as for a two-level system.

\( R_{b \rightarrow a}=\frac{\pi}{3 \epsilon_{0} \hbar^{2}}| \wp |^{2} \rho\left(\omega_{0}\right) \) (11.54).

Solution

(a)

\( \Psi(t)=\sum c_{n}(t) e^{-i E_{n t} / \hbar} \psi_{n} . \quad H \Psi=i \hbar \frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial t} ; \quad H=H_{0}+H^{\prime}(t) ; \quad H_{0} \psi_{n}=E_{n} \psi_{n} \). So

\( \sum c_{n} e^{-i E_{n} t / \hbar} E_{n} \psi_{n}+\sum c_{n} e^{-i E_{n} t / \hbar} H^{\prime} \psi_{n}=i \hbar \sum \dot{c}_{n} e^{-i E_{n} t / \hbar} \psi_{n}+i \hbar\left(-\frac{i}{\hbar}\right) \sum c_{n} E_{n} e^{-i E_{n} t / \hbar} \psi_{n} \).

The first and last terms cancel, so

\( \sum c_{n} e^{-i E_{n} t / \hbar} H^{\prime} \psi_{n}=i \hbar \sum \dot{c}_{n} e^{-i E_{n} t / \hbar} \psi_{n} \) . Take the inner product with \( \psi_{m}:\)

\( \sum c_{n} e^{-i E_{n} t / \hbar}\left\langle\psi_{m}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{n}\right\rangle=i \hbar \sum \dot{c}_{n} e^{-i E_{n} t / \hbar}\left\langle\psi_{m} \mid \psi_{n}\right\rangle \).

Assume orthonormality of the unperturbed states, \( \left\langle\psi_{m} \mid \psi_{n}\right\rangle=\delta_{m n}, \quad \text { and define } \quad H_{m n}^{\prime} \equiv\left\langle\psi_{m}\left|H^{\prime}\right| \psi_{n}\right\rangle \).

\( \sum c_{n} e^{-i E_{n} t / \hbar} H_{m n}^{\prime}=i \hbar \dot{c}_{m} e^{-i E_{m} t / \hbar} \), or \( \dot{c}_{m}=-\frac{i}{\hbar} \sum_{n} c_{n} H_{m n}^{\prime} e^{i\left(E_{m}-E_{n}\right) t / \hbar} \).

(b) Zeroth order: \( c_{N}(t)=1, \quad c_{m}(t)=0 \quad \text { for } m \neq N \). Then in first order:

\( \dot{c}_{N}=-\frac{i}{\hbar} H_{N N}^{\prime}, \quad \text { or } \quad c_{N}(t)=1-\frac{i}{\hbar} \int_{0}^{t} H_{N N}^{\prime}\left(t^{\prime}\right) d t^{\prime} \), whereas for \( m \neq N: \)

\( \dot{c}_{m}=-\frac{i}{\hbar} H_{m N}^{\prime} e^{i\left(E_{m}-E_{N}\right) t / \hbar}, \quad \text { or } \quad c_{m}(t)=-\frac{i}{\hbar} \int_{0}^{t} H_{m N}^{\prime}\left(t^{\prime}\right) e^{i\left(E_{m}-E_{N}\right) t^{\prime} / \hbar} d t^{\prime} \).

(c)

\( c_{M}(t)=-\frac{i}{\hbar} H_{M N}^{\prime} \int_{0}^{t} e^{i\left(E_{M}-E_{N}\right) t^{\prime} / \hbar} d t^{\prime}=-\left.\frac{i}{\hbar} H_{M N}^{\prime}\left[\frac{e^{i\left(E_{M}-E_{N}\right) t^{\prime} / \hbar}}{i\left(E_{M}-E_{N}\right) / \hbar}\right]\right|_{0} ^{t}=-H_{M N}^{\prime}\left[\frac{e^{i\left(E_{M}-E_{N}\right) t / \hbar}-1}{E_{M}-E_{N}}\right] \).

\( =-\frac{H_{M N}^{\prime}}{\left(E_{M}-E_{N}\right)} e^{i\left(E_{M}-E_{N}\right) t / 2 \hbar} 2 i \sin \left(\frac{E_{M}-E_{N}}{2 \hbar} t\right) \).

\( P_{N \rightarrow M}=\left|c_{M}\right|^{2}=\frac{4\left|H_{M N}^{\prime}\right|^{2}}{\left(E_{M}-E_{N}\right)^{2}} \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{E_{M}-E_{N}}{2 \hbar} t\right) \).

(d)

\( c_{M}(t)=-\frac{i}{\hbar} V_{M N} \frac{1}{2} \int_{0}^{t}\left(e^{i \omega t^{\prime}}+e^{-i \omega t^{\prime}}\right) e^{i\left(E_{M}-E_{N}\right) t^{\prime} / \hbar} d t^{\prime} \).

\( =-\left.\frac{i V_{M N}}{2 \hbar}\left[\frac{e^{i\left(\hbar \omega+E_{M}-E_{N}\right) t^{\prime} / \hbar}}{i\left(\hbar \omega+E_{M}-E_{N}\right) / \hbar}+\frac{e^{i\left(-\hbar \omega+E_{M}-E_{N}\right) t^{\prime} / \hbar}}{i\left(-\hbar \omega+E_{M}-E_{N}\right) / \hbar}\right]\right|_{0} ^{t} \).

\( \text { If } E_{M}>E_{N} \), the second term dominates, and transitions occur only for \( \omega \approx\left(E_{M}-E_{N}\right) / \hbar: \)

\( c_{M}(t) \approx-\frac{i V_{M N}}{2 \hbar} \frac{1}{(i / \hbar)\left(E_{M}-E_{N}-\hbar \omega\right)} e^{i\left(E_{M}-E_{N}-\hbar \omega\right) t / 2 \hbar} 2 i \sin \left(\frac{E_{M}-E_{N}-\hbar \omega}{2 \hbar} t\right) \), so

\( P_{N \rightarrow M}=\left|c_{M}\right|^{2}=\frac{\left|V_{M N}\right|^{2}}{\left(E_{M}-E_{N}-\hbar \omega\right)^{2}} \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{E_{M}-E_{N}-\hbar \omega}{2 \hbar} t\right) \).

\( \text { If } E_{M}<E_{N} \) the first term dominates, and transitions occur only for \( \omega \approx\left(E_{N}-E_{M}\right) / \hbar \):

\( c_{M}(t) \approx-\frac{i V_{M N}}{2 \hbar} \frac{1}{(i / \hbar)\left(E_{M}-E_{N}+\hbar \omega\right)} e^{i\left(E_{M}-E_{N}+\hbar \omega\right) t / 2 \hbar} 2 i \sin \left(\frac{E_{M}-E_{N}+\hbar \omega}{2 \hbar} t\right) \), and hence

\( P_{N \rightarrow M}=\frac{\left|V_{M N}\right|^{2}}{\left(E_{M}-E_{N}+\hbar \omega\right)^{2}} \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{E_{M}-E_{N}+\hbar \omega}{2 \hbar} t\right) \).

Combining the two results, we conclude that transitions occur to states with energy \( E_{M} \approx E_{N} \pm \hbar \omega \), and

\( P_{N \rightarrow M}=\frac{\left|V_{M N}\right|^{2}}{\left(E_{M}-E_{N} \pm \hbar \omega\right)^{2}} \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{E_{M}-E_{N} \pm \hbar \omega}{2 \hbar} t\right) \).

(e) For light, \( V_{b a}=-\wp E_{0} \) (Eq. 11.41). The rest is as before (Section 11.2.3), leading to Eq. 11.54:

\( V_{b a}=-\wp E_{0} \) (11.41).

\( R_{b \rightarrow a}=\frac{\pi}{3 \epsilon_{0} \hbar^{2}}| \wp |^{2} \rho\left(\omega_{0}\right) \) (11.54).

\( R_{N \rightarrow M}=\frac{\pi}{3 \epsilon_{0} \hbar^{2}}|\wp|^{2} \rho(\omega), \text { with } \omega=\pm\left(E_{M}-E_{N}\right) / \hbar \)

\( (+\operatorname{sign} \Rightarrow \text { absorption, }-\operatorname{sign} \Rightarrow \text { stimulated emission) } \).
