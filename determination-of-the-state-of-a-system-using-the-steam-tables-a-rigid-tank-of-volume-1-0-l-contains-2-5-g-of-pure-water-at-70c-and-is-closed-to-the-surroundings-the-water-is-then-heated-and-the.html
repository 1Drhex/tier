Guest Question.

Determination of the State of a System using the Steam Tables

A rigid tank of volume 1.0 L contains 2.5 g of pure water at 70°C and is closed to the surroundings. The water is then heated and the temperature rises.

(a) Determine the initial state of the system.

(b) Determine the temperature at which the water in the tank is all vaporized.

Admin
Solution

(a) We label the initial state, state 1, and the final state, state 2. We need two independent intensive properties to constrain the state of the system. In addition to temperature, we can fi nd the specific volume of water in the tank:

 

\(\hat{v}_{1}=\frac{V_{1}}{m_{1}}=\frac{1 L }{2.5 g } \times \frac{1000 g }{ kg } \times \frac{0.001 m ^{3}}{ L }=0.4 \frac{ m ^{3}}{ kg }\)

 

where we have put the specific volume in units consistent with data in the steam tables. From the steam tables (Appendix B, Table B.1), we see that at a temperature of 70°C, the saturated vapor volume is \(\hat{v}_{v}=5.042\left[\frac{ m ^{3}}{ kg }\right]\), and the saturate liquid volume is \(\hat{v}_{l}=0.001023\left[\frac{ m ^{3}}{ kg }\right]\).

 

Because the volume of the system is in between these two values, we have a mixture of saturated liquid and saturated vapor at 70°C. To constrain the system, we need to determine the quality (i.e., the proportion in the vapor):

From Equation (1.15)

 

\(v=(1-x) v_{l}+x v_{v}\) (1.15)

 

\(x_{1}=\frac{\hat{v}_{1}-\hat{v}_{l}}{\hat{v}_{v}-\hat{v}_{l}}=0.079\)

 

Approximately 8% of the mass of water is in the vapor.

 

(b) Because this process occurs in a closed system and the tank is rigid, the specific volume remains constant (i.e., \(\hat{v}_{2}=\hat{v}_{1}\)). We therefore wish to fi nd the state at which the specific volume of saturated vapor is 0.4 \(m ^{3} / kg\).

 

From Table B.1, the temperature table for saturated water from the steam tables, we see that this value of specific volume is between temperatures of 145°C and 150°C:

 

T [°C] \(\hat{v}_{v}\left[\frac{ m ^{3}}{ kg }\right]\)
145 0.44632
150 0.39278

 

By linear interpolation, we get:

 

\(T_{2}=T_{\hat{v}=0.4}=T_{\hat{v}=0.44632}+\left(T_{\hat{v}=0.39278}-T_{\hat{v}=0.44632}\right)\left(\frac{0.4-0.44632}{0.39278-0.44632}\right)=145.8\left[{ }^{\circ} C \right]\)
