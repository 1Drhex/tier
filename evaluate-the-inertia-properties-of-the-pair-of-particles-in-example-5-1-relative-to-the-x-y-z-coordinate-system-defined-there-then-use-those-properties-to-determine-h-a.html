Guest Question.

Evaluate the inertia properties of the pair of particles in relative to the \(x^{\prime} y^{\prime} z^{\prime}\) coordinate system defined there. Then use those properties to determine \(\bar{H}_{A}\).

Admin
Solution

This example uses a simple system to illustrate the significance of the inertia properties. It also provides some insight that will be useful to later developments concerning the rate of change of \(\bar{H}_{A}\). The two particles and their massless connecting rod form the rigid body of interest here. Each particle may be considered to be an element of mass. Correspondingly, the integral reduces to a single term for each particle. The \(z^{\prime}\) axis in was defined to be vertical, and the \(x^{\prime}\) axis is aligned with the motor’s rotation axis. The position of the particles in terms of components relative to \(x^{\prime} y^{\prime} z^{\prime}\) was found previously to be

\(\bar{r}_{1 / O}=L \bar{i}^{\prime}+R \sin \theta~ \bar{j}^{\prime}+R \cos \theta~ \bar{k}^{\prime}, \quad \bar{r}_{2 / O}=L \bar{i}^{\prime}-R \sin \theta~ \bar{j}^{\prime}-R \cos \theta ~\bar{k}^{\prime}.\)

Because the components are the respective values of \(x^{\prime}, y^{\prime}\), and \(z^{\prime}\) for each particle, Eqs. (5.2.4) for the present situation reduce to

\(\begin{array}{l} I_{x x}=\iiint\left(y^{2}+z^{2}\right) d m, \quad I_{y y}=\iiint\left(x^{2}+z^{2}\right) d m, \quad I_{z z}=\iiint\left(x^{2}+y^{2}\right) d m, \\\\ I_{x y}=I_{y x}=\iiint x y d m, \quad I_{x z}=I_{z x}=\iiint x z d m, \quad I_{y z}=I_{z y}=\iiint y z d m. \end{array} (5.2.4)\)

 

\( \begin{array}{c} I_{x^{\prime} x^{\prime}}=m\left(y_{1}^{2}+z_{1}^{2}\right)+m\left(y_{2}^{2}+z_{2}^{2}\right)=2 m R^{2}, \\\\ I_{y^{\prime} y^{\prime}}=m\left(x_{1}^{2}+z_{1}^{2}\right)+m\left(x_{2}^{2}+z_{2}^{2}\right)=2 m L^{2}+2 m R^{2}(\cos \theta)^{2}, \\\\ I_{z^{\prime} z^{\prime}}=m\left(x_{1}^{2}+y_{1}^{2}\right)+m\left(x_{2}^{2}+y_{2}^{2}\right)=2 m L^{2}+2 m R^{2}(\sin \theta)^{2}, \\\\ I_{x^{\prime} y^{\prime}}=I_{y^{\prime} x^{\prime}}=m x_{1} y_{1}+m x_{2} y_{2}=0, \\\\ I_{x^{\prime} z^{\prime}}=I_{z^{\prime} x^{\prime}}=m x_{1} z_{1}+m x_{2} z_{2}=0, \\\\ I_{y^{\prime} z^{\prime}}=I_{z^{\prime} y^{\prime}}=m y_{1} z_{1}+m y_{2} z_{2}=2 m R^{2} \sin \theta \cos \theta=m R^{2} \sin (2 \theta). \end{array}\)

The products of inertia indicate that there is a balanced mass distribution relative to the \(x^{\prime} y^{\prime}\) and \(x^{\prime} z^{\prime}\) quadrants, and the mass is situated in the two quadrants where \(y^{\prime} z^{\prime}\) are positive whenever \(\sin (2 \theta)\) is positive, that is, \(0<\theta<\pi / 2\) and \(\pi<\theta<3 \pi\).

To evaluate \(\bar{H}_{A}\) we recall from the previous example that the angular velocity of the assembly of the two particles and the connecting rod is the sum of the rotation rates \(\omega_{2}\) about the vertical and \(\omega_{1}\) of the motor. We previously found that

\(\bar{\omega}=-\omega_{1} \bar{i}^{\prime}+\omega_{2} \bar{k}^{\prime}. \)

The components are \(\omega_{x^{\prime}}=-\omega_{1}, \omega_{y^{\prime}}=0, \omega_{z^{\prime}}=\omega_{2} .\) Correspondingly, Eq. (5.2.3) gives

\(\begin{aligned} \bar{H}_{A}=&\left(I_{x x} \omega_{x}-I_{x y} \omega_{y}-I_{x z} \omega_{z}\right) \bar{i}+\left(I_{y y} \omega_{y}-I_{y x} \omega_{x}-I_{y z} \omega_{z}\right) \bar{j} \\\\ &+\left(I_{z z} \omega_{z}-I_{z x} \omega_{x}-I_{z y} \omega_{y}\right) \bar{k}, \end{aligned} (5.2.3)\)

 

\(\begin{aligned} \bar{H}_{A}=&\left[2 m R^{2}\left( \omega_{1}\right)-0(0)-0\left(\omega_{2}\right)\right] \bar{i}^{\prime} \\\\ &+\left\{\left[2 m L^{2}+2 m R^{2}(\cos \theta)^{2}\right](0)-0\left(-\omega_{1}\right)-\left(2 m R^{2} \sin \theta \cos \theta\right)\left(\omega_{2}\right)\right\} \bar{j}^{\prime} \\\\ &+\left\{\left[2 m L^{2}+2 m R^{2}(\sin \theta)^{2}\right]\left(\omega_{2}\right)-0-\left(2 m R^{2} \sin \theta \cos \theta\right)(0)\right\} \bar{k}^{\prime}. \end{aligned}\)

The preceding exemplifies a general observation that many terms in the standard formula for angular momentum usually vanish because either a product of inertia or a component of \(\bar{\omega}\) is zero. The present result reduces to

\(\bar{H}_{A}=-2 m R^{2}~\omega_{1}~\bar{i}^{\prime}-2 m R^{2} ~\omega_{2}~ \sin \theta ~\cos \theta ~\bar{j}^{\prime}+2 m\left[L^{2}+R^{2}(\sin \theta)^{2}\right] \omega_{2} ~\bar{k}^{\prime}.\)

This is the same as the expression we derived in by actually evaluating moments of the relative momenta.
