Guest Question.

A container, which is wrapped in a fabric that is continually moistened with a highly volatile liquid, may be used to keep beverages cool in hot, arid regions. Suppose that the container is placed in dry ambient air at \(40^{\circ}C,\) with heat and mass transfer between the wetting agent and the air occurring by forced convection. The wetting agent is known to have a molecular weight of 200 kg/kmol and a latent heat of vaporization of 100 kJ/kg. Its saturated vapor pressure for the prescribed conditions is approximately \(5000 N/m^{2},\) and the diffusion coefficient of the vapor in air is \(0.2\times 10^{-4} m^{2}/s.\) What is the steady-state temperature of the beverage?

Admin
Solution

Known: Properties of wetting agent used to evaporatively cool a beverage container.

Find: Steady-state temperature of beverage.

Assumptions:
1. Heat and mass transfer analogy is applicable.
2. Vapor displays ideal gas behavior.
3. Radiation effects are negligible.
4. Air properties may be evaluated at a mean boundary layer temperature assumed to be \(T_{f}=300 K.\)

Properties: , air \((300 K): \rho =1.16 kg/m^{3}, c_{p}=1.007 kJ/kg\cdot K, \alpha =22.5\times 10^{-6} m^{2}/s.\)

 

Analysis: Subject to the foregoing assumptions, the evaporative cooling effect is given by Equation 6.65.

 

\((T_{\infty }-T_{s})=\frac{M_{A}h_{fg}}{R\rho c_{p}Le^{2/3}}\left [ \frac{P_{A,sat}(T_{s})}{T_{s}} -\frac{P_{A,\infty }}{T_{\infty }}\right ]\) (6.65)

 

Setting \(P_{A,\infty } = 0\)

 

\(T_{s}^{2}-T_{\infty }T_{s}+B=0\)

 

where the coefficient B is

 

\(B=\frac{M_{A}h_{fg}P_{A,sat}}{R\rho c_{p}Le^{2/3}}\)

 

or

 

\(B=[200 kg/kmol \times 100 kJ/kg\times 5000 N/m^{2}\times 10^{-3}kJ/N\cdot m]\)

 

\(\div \left [ 8.314kJ/kmol \cdot K\times 1.16 kg/m^{3}\times 1.007 kJ/kg\cdot K \times \left ( \frac{22.5\times 10^{-6}m^{2}/s}{20\times 10^{-6}m^{2}s} \right )^{2/3} \right ] = 9520K^{2}\)

 

Hence

 

\(T_{s}=\frac{T_{\infty }\pm \sqrt{T_{\infty }^{2}-4B}}{2}=\frac{313 K\pm \sqrt{(313)^{2}-4(9520)K}}{2}\)

 

Rejecting the minus sign on physical grounds \((T_{s}\) must equal \(T_{\infty }\) if there is no evaporation, in which case \(P_{A,sat}=0\) and B=0), it follows that

 

\(T_{s}=278.9 K=5.9^{\circ}C\)

 

Comments: The result is independent of the shape of the container as long as the heat and mass transfer analogy may be used.
