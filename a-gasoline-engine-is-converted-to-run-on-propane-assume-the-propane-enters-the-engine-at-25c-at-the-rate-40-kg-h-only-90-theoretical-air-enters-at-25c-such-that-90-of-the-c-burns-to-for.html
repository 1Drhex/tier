Guest Question.

A gasoline engine is converted to run on propane. Assume the propane enters the engine at 25°C, at the rate 40 kg/h. Only 90% theoretical air enters at 25°C such that 90% of the C burns to form \(CO _{2}\), and 10% of the C burns to form CO. The combustion products also include \(H _{2} O , H _{2}\) and \(N _{2}\), exit the exhaust at 1000 K. Heat loss from the engine (primarily to the cooling water) is 120 kW. What is the power output of the engine? What is the thermal efficiency?

Admin
Solution

Propane: \(T _{1}=25^{\circ} C , \dot{ m }=40 kg / hr , M =44.094 kg / kmol\)

Air: \(T _{2}=25^{\circ} C\) , 90% theoretical Air produces 90% \(CO _{2}\) , 10% \(CO \)

Products: \(T _{3}=1000 K , CO _{2}, CO , H _{2} O , H _{2}, N _{2}\)

\(C _{3} H _{8}+4.5 O _{2}+16.92 N _{2} \rightarrow 2.7 CO _{2}+0.3 CO +3.3 H _{2} O +0.7 H _{2}+16.92 N _{2}\)

 

\(\dot{ n }_{ C 3 H 8}=\dot{ m } /( M \times 3600)=0.000252 kmol / s\)

 

Energy eq.: \(\dot{ Q }+ H _{ R }= H _{ P }+\dot{ W } ; \quad \dot{ Q }=-120 kW\)

\(H _{ R }= n _{ C 3 H 8} \overline{ h }_{ f }^{ o }=-103900 kJ\)

Products:

\(\begin{array}{ll}CO _{2} & – & n _{ CO 2}\left(\overline{ h }_{ f }^{ o }+\Delta \overline{ h }\right)=2.7(-393522+33397)=-972337.5 kJ \\CO & – & n _{ CO }\left(\overline{ h }_{ f }^{ o }+\Delta \overline{ h }\right)=0.3(-110527+21686)=-26652 kJ \\H _{2} O & – & n _{ H 2 O }\left(\overline{ h }_{ f }^{ o }+\Delta \overline{ h }\right)=3.3(-241826+26000)=-712226 kJ \\H _{2} & – & n _{ H 2}\left(\overline{ h }_{ f }^{ o }+\Delta \overline{ h }\right)=0.7(0+20663)=14464.1 kJ \\N _{2} & – & n _{ N 2}\left(\overline{ h }_{ f }^{ o }+\Delta \overline{ h }\right)=16.92(0+21463)=363154 kJ\end{array}\)
