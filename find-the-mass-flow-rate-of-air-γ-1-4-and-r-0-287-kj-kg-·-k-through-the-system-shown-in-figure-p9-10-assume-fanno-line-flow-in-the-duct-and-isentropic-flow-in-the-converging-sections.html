Guest Question.

Find the mass flow rate of air (γ = 1.4 and R = 0.287 kJ/kg · K) through the system shown in Figure P9.10. Assume Fanno line flow in the duct and isentropic flow in the converging sections; f = 0.01.

Admin
Solution

Assume that the system is choked so that \(M _{3}=1, A _{3}= A ^{*} . \text { So }\left( A / A ^{*}\right)=\left( A _{2} / A _{3}\right)=(2 / 1.75)^{2}=1.3061, \text { which yields } M _{2}=0.5184 . \text { In turn, }\left( fL _{\max } / D \right)_{2}=0.9287 . \text { Also, }( fL / D )=(0.01)(15) /(0.02)=7.5\); therefore,

 

\(\left(\frac{ fL _{\max }}{ D }\right)_{1}=\left(\frac{ fL _{\max }}{ D }\right)_{2}+\frac{ fL }{ D }=0.9287+7.5=8.4287\)

 

From this, we find that \(M _{1}=0.2506 . \text { Consequently, with } M _{1}, M _{2} \text { and } M _{3}\) we are able to compute the following static pressures

 

\(p _{1}=\left(\frac{ p _{1}}{ p _{ o1 }}\right) p _{ o1 }=(0.95725) 101=96.6823 kPa\)

 

\(p _{2}=\left(\frac{ p _{2}}{ p ^{*}}\right)\left(\frac{ p ^{*}}{ p _{1}}\right) p _{1}=(2.0585)\left(\frac{1}{4.3441}\right) 96.6823=45.8140 kPa\)

 

\(p _{3}=\left(\frac{ p _{3}}{ p _{o3}}\right)\left(\frac{ p _{o3}}{ p _{o2}}\right)\left(\frac{ p _{o2}}{ p _{2}}\right) p _{2}=(0.5283)(1.0)\left(\frac{1}{0.83257}\right) 45.8140=29.0708 kPa\)

 

Since \(p _{3}= p _{ e }\) and since \(p _{ e }\) must be equal to or greater than \(p _{ b }\), the nozzle is not choked as assumed. Therefore, \(p_{3}=p_{e}=30 kPa \text { and } M_{3}=M_{e}<1\).

 

Assume \(M _{3}=0.9\) and from the isentropic relations we find

 

\(\frac{ A _{3}}{ A ^{*}}=1.0089, \frac{ p _{3}}{ p _{ o 3}}=0.59126\)

 

Now

 

\(\frac{ A _{2}}{ A ^{*}}=\frac{ A _{2}}{ A _{3}} \frac{ A _{3}}{ A ^{*}}=\left(\frac{2.0}{1.75}\right)^{2} 1.0089=1.3177\)

 

From this we find \(M _{2}=0.5119\), from which we obtain

 

\(\left(\frac{ fL _{\max }}{ D }\right)_{2}=0.9761, \frac{ p _{2}}{ p _{ o 2}}=0.8363, \frac{ p _{2}}{ p ^{*}}=2.0860\)

 

\(\left(\frac{ fL _{\max }}{ D }\right)_{1}=\left(\frac{ fL _{\max }}{ D }\right)_{2}+\frac{ fL }{ D }=0.9761+7.5=8.4761\)

 

From this, we find that \(M_{1}=0.2501\). Consequently, with \(M _{1}, M _{2} \text { and } M _{3}\) we are able to compute the following static pressures

 

\(p _{1}=\left(\frac{ p _{1}}{ p _{ o1 }}\right) p _{ o1 }=(0.9574) 101=96.6974 kPa\)

 

\(p _{2}=\left(\frac{ p _{2}}{ p ^{*}}\right)\left(\frac{ p ^{*}}{ p _{1}}\right) p _{1}=(2.0860)\left(\frac{1}{4.3531}\right) 96.6974=46.3373 kPa\)

 

\(p_{3}=\left(\frac{p_{3}}{p_{o3}}\right)\left(\frac{p_{o3}}{p_{o2}}\right)\left(\frac{p_{o2}}{p_{2}}\right) p_{2}=(0.59126)(1.0)\left(\frac{1}{0.8363}\right) 46.6974=32.7602 kPa\)

 

Too large; therefore \(M _{3}\) needs to be increased. After a few tries \(M _{3}= 0.973\) and from the isentropic relations we find

 

\(\frac{A_{3}}{A^{*}}=1.0006, \frac{p_{3}}{p_{o 3}}=0.5450\)

 

Now

 

\(\frac{ A _{2}}{ A ^{*}}=\frac{ A _{2}}{ A _{3}} \frac{ A _{3}}{ A ^{*}}=\left(\frac{2.0}{1.75}\right)^{2} 1.0006=1.3069\)

 

From this we find \(M _{2}=0.5180\), from which we obtain

 

\(\left(\frac{ fL _{\max }}{ D }\right)_{2}=0.9316, \frac{ p _{2}}{ p _{ o 2}}=0.8328, \frac{ p _{2}}{ p ^{*}}=2.0602\)

 

\(\left(\frac{ fL _{\max }}{ D }\right)_{1}=\left(\frac{ fL _{\max }}{ D }\right)_{2}+\frac{ fL }{ D }=0.9316+7.5=8.4316\)

 

From this, we find that \(M_{1}=0.2506\). Consequently, with \(M_{1}, M_{2} \text { and } M_{3}\) we are able to compute the following static pressures

 

\(p _{1}=\left(\frac{ p _{1}}{ p _{ o1 }}\right) p _{ o1 }=(0.9572) 101=96.6772 kPa\)

 

\(p _{2}=\left(\frac{ p _{2}}{ p ^{*}}\right)\left(\frac{ p ^{*}}{ p _{1}}\right) p _{1}=(2.0602)\left(\frac{1}{4.3438}\right) 96.6772=45.8521 kPa\)

 

\(p _{3}=\left(\frac{ p _{3}}{ p _{ o 3}}\right)\left(\frac{ p _{ o 3}}{ p _{ o 2}}\right)\left(\frac{ p _{ o 2}}{ p _{2}}\right) p _{2}=(0.5450)(1.0)\left(\frac{1}{0.8328}\right) 45.8521=30.0082 kPa\)

 

Slightly too high but close enough. Note from the isentropic relations at \(M _{1},\left( T / T _{ o }\right)_{1}=(0.9876)293 = 289.3668K\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ m } &=\rho_{1} AV _{1}=\left(\frac{ p _{1}}{ RT _{1}}\right) AM _{1} \sqrt{\gamma RT _{1}} \\&=\left[\frac{(96.6772)}{(0.287) 289.3668}\right]\left(\frac{\pi}{4} 0.02^{2}\right) 0.2506 \sqrt{(1.4)(287)(289.3668)} \\&=0.0313 kg / s\end{aligned}\)
