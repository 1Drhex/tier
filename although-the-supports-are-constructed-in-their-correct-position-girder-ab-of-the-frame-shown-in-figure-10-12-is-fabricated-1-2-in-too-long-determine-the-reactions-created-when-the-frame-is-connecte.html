Guest Question.

Although the supports are constructed in their correct position, girder AB of the frame shown in Figure 10.12 is fabricated 1.2 in. too long. Determine the reactions created when the frame is connected into the supports. Given: EI is a constant for all members, \( I=240 in.^{4} \) , and \( E=29000\frac{kips}{in.^{2}}\) .

Admin
Solution

The deflected shape of the frame is shown by the dashed line in Figure 10.12a. Although internal forces (axial, shear, and moment) are created when the frame is forced into the supports, the deformations producedby these forces are neglected since they are small compared to the 1.2-in.
fabrication error; therefore, the chord rotation \( \psi _{BC} \) of column BC equals

 

\( \psi _{BC}=\frac{\Delta }{L}=\frac{1.2}{9\left ( 12 \right )}=\frac{1}{90}\) rad

 

Since the ends of girder AB are at the same level,\( \psi _{AB} = 0\) . The unknown displacements are\( \Theta _{B}\) and \( \Theta _{C}\) . Using the slope-deflection equation (Equation 10.16), we express member end moments in terms of the unknown displacements. Because no loads are applied to the members, all fixed-end moments equal zero.

 

\( M_{NF}=\frac{2EI}{L}\left ( 2\Theta _{N}+\Theta _{F}-3\psi _{NF} \right )+FEM_{NF}\) (10.16)

 

\( M_{AB}=\frac{2E\left ( 240 \right )}{18\left ( 12 \right )}\left ( \Theta _{B} \right )=2.222E\Theta _{B}\) (1)

 

\( M_{BA}=\frac{2E\left ( 240 \right )}{18\left ( 12 \right )}\left ( 2\Theta _{B} \right )=4.444E\Theta _{B}\) (2)

 

\( M_{BC}=\frac{2E\left ( 240 \right )}{9\left ( 12 \right )}\left [ 2\Theta _{B}+\Theta _{C}-3\left ( \frac{1}{90} \right ) \right ]\)

 

\( =8.889E\Theta _{B}+4.444E\Theta _{C}-0.1481E \) (3)

 

\( M_{CB}=\frac{2E\left ( 240 \right )}{9\left ( 12 \right )}\left [ 2\Theta _{C}+\Theta _{B}-3\left ( \frac{1}{90} \right ) \right ]\)

 

\( =8.889E\Theta _{C}+4.444E\Theta _{B}-0.1481E \) (4)

 

Writing equilibrium equations gives

Joint C: \( M_{CB}=0\) (5)

Joint B: \( M_{BA}+M_{BC}=0\) (6)

 

Substituting Equations 2 to 4 into Equations 5 and 6 solving for \( \Theta _{B} \) and \( \Theta _{C}\) yield

 

\( 8.889E\Theta _{C}+4.444E\Theta _{B}-0.1481 =0\)

 

\( 8.889E\Theta _{B}+8.889E\Theta _{C}+4.444E\Theta _{C}-0.1481E=0\)

 

\( \Theta _{B}=0.00666 \) rad (7)

 

\( \Theta _{c}=0.01332\) rad (8)

 

Substituting \( \Theta _{c} \) and \( \Theta _{B}\) into Equations 1 to 3 produces

 

\( M_{AB}=35.76 \) kip.ft \( M_{BA}=71.58\) kip.ft

 

\( M_{BC}=-71.58 \) kip.ft \( M_{CB}=0\)

 

The free-body diagrams used to compute internal forces and reactions are shown in Figure 10.12b, which also shows moment diagrams.
