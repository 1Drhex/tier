Guest Question.

Determine the natural frequencies and modal amplitude vectors for the 3 degrees-of freedom system depicted in Figure 10.6a.

Admin
Solution

The ﬁnite element model is shown in Figure 10.6b, with node and element numbers as indicated. Assembly of the global stiffness matrix results in

\(\ \left[K\right] = \left [ \begin{matrix} K & -K & 0 & 0 \\ -K & 3K & -2K & 0 \\ 0 & -2K & 3K & -K \\ 0 & 0 & -K & K \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Similarly, the assembled global mass matrix is

\(\ \left[M\right] = \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & m & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & m & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 2m \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Owing to the constraint \(\ U_{1}=0 \) , we need consider only the last three equations of motion, given by

\(\ \left [ \begin{matrix} m & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & m & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 2m \end{matrix} \right ] \left \{ \begin{matrix} \ddot{U} _{2} \\ \ddot{U} _{3} \\ \ddot{U} _{4} \end{matrix} \right \} + \left [ \begin{matrix} 3k & -2k & 0 \\ -2k & 3k & -k \\ 0 & -k & k \end{matrix} \right ] \left \{ \begin{matrix} U_{2} \\ U_{3} \\ U_{4} \end{matrix} \right \} = \left \{ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right \} \)

Assuming sinusoidal response as \(\ U_{i} = A_{i}sin(\omega t+\phi ) \) ,i =2, 4 and substituting into the equations of motion leads to the frequency equation

\(\ \left | \begin{matrix} 3k-\omega ^{2} m & -2k & 0 \\ -2k & 3k-\omega ^{2} m & -k \\ 0 & -k & k-2\omega ^{2} m \end{matrix} \right | =0 \)

Expanding the determinant and simplifying gives

\(\ \omega ^{6}-6.5\frac{k}{m}\omega ^{4} + 7.5(\frac{k}{m} )^{2} \omega ^{2} -(\frac{k}{m} )^{3} =0 \)

which will be treated as a cubic equation in the unknown ω² . Setting ω² = C(k/m), the frequency equation becomes

\(\ (C^{3}-6.5 C^{2}+7.5 C-1 )(\frac{k}{m} )^{3} = 0 \)

which has the roots

\(\ C_{1} = 0.1532 C_{2} = 1.2912 C_{3} = 5.0556 \)

The corresponding natural circular frequencies are

\(\ \omega _{1}= 0.3914\sqrt{\frac{k}{m} } \)

\(\ \omega _{2}= 1.1363\sqrt{\frac{k}{m} } \)

\(\ \omega _{3}= 2.2485\sqrt{\frac{k}{m} } \)

To obtain the amplitude ratios, we substitute the natural circular frequencies into the amplitude equations one at a time while setting (arbitrarily) \(\ A^{(i)}_{2} = 1 \) , i = 1, 2, 3andsolve for the amplitudes \(\ A^{(i)}_{3} \) and \(\ A^{(i)}_{4} \) . Using \(\ \omega _{1} \) results in

\(\ (3k-\omega ^{2}_{1}m )A^{(1)}_{2}-2kA^{(1)}_{3}=0 \)

\(\ -2kA^{(1)}_{2}+(3k-\omega ^{2}_{1}m )A^{(1)}_{3}-kA^{(1)}_{4}=0 \)

\(\ -kA^{(1)}_{3}+(k-2\omega ^{2}_{1}m )A^{(1)}_{4}=0 \)

Substituting \(\ \omega _{1} =0.3914 \sqrt{{k}/{m}} \) , we obtain

\(\ 2.847A^{(1)}_{2} -2A^{(1)}_{3}=0 \)

\(\ -2A^{(1)}_{2} +2.847A^{(1)}_{3}-A^{(1)}_{4} =0 \)

\(\ -A^{(1)}_{3}+0.694A^{(1)}_{4} =0 \)

As discussed, the amplitude equations are homogeneous; explicit solutions cannot be obtained. We can, however, determine the amplitude ratios by setting \(\ A^{(1)}_{2} = 1 \) to obtain

\(\ A^{(1)}_{3}=1.4235 \)

\(\ A^{(1)}_{4}=2.0511 \)

The amplitude vector corresponding to the fundamental mode \(\ \omega _{1} \) is then represented as

\(\ \left\{A^{(1)} \right\}=A^{(1)}_{2}\left \{ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 1.4325 \\ 2.0511 \end{matrix} \right \} \)

and this is the eigenvector corresponding to the eigenvalue \(\ \omega _{1} \) . Proceeding identically with the values for the other two frequencies, \(\ \omega _{2} \) and \(\ \omega _{3} \) , the resulting amplitude vectors are

\(\ \left\{A^{(2)} \right\}=A^{(2)}_{2}\left \{ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 0.8544 \\ −0.5399 \end{matrix} \right \} \)

\(\ \left\{A^{(3)} \right\}=A^{(3)}_{2}\left \{ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ −1.0279 \\ 0.1128 \end{matrix} \right \} \)
