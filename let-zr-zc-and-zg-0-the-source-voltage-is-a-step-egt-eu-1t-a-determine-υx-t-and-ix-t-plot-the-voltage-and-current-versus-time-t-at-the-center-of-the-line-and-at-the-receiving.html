Single-phase lossless-line transients: step-voltage source at sending end, matched load at receiving end

Let \(Z_{R}= Z_{c}\) and \(Z_{G}=0\). The source voltage is a step, \(e_{G}\left(t\right)=Eu_{-1}\left(t\right) \). (a) Determine υ(x, t) and i(x, t). Plot the voltage and current versus time t at the center of the line and at the receiving end.

Admin
Solution

a. From (13.2.12) with \(Z_{R} =Z_{c}\) and \(Z_{G} =0\),

\(\Gamma _{S} \left(s\right)=\frac{\frac{Z_{G}\left(s\right) }{Z_{c} } -1}{\frac{Z_{G}\left(s\right) }{Z_{c}} +1}\) per unit (13.2.12)

\(\Gamma _{R} \left(s\right)=\frac{1-1}{1+1} =\) 0 \(\Gamma _{S} \left(s\right)=\frac{0-1}{0+1} = -1\)

The Laplace transform of the source voltage is \(E_{G}\left(s\right)=E/s \) Then, from (13.2.10) and (13.2.11),

\(V\left(x,s\right) =E_{G}\left(s\right) \left[\frac{Z_{c} }{Z_{G}\left(s\right)+Z_{c} } \right]\left[\frac{e^{-sx/\nu}+\Gamma _{R}\left(s\right)e^{s\left[\left(x/\nu \right) -2\tau \right] } }{1-\Gamma _{R}\left(s\right) \Gamma _{S} \left(s\right)e^{-2s\tau } } \right] \) (13.2.10)

\(I\left(x,s\right) = \left[\frac{E_{G}\left(s\right) }{Z_{G}\left(s\right)+Z_{c} } \right]\left[\frac{e^{-sx/\nu}-\Gamma _{R}\left(s\right)e^{s\left[\left(x/\nu \right) -2\tau \right] } }{1-\Gamma _{R}\left(s\right) \Gamma _{S} \left(s\right)e^{-2s\tau } } \right]\) (13.2.11)

\(V\left(x,s\right) =\left(\frac{E}{s} \right)\left(1\right)\left(e^{-sx/\nu } \right)=\frac{Ee^{-sx/\nu } }{s}\)

 

\( I\left(x,s\right)=\frac{\left(E/Z_{c} \right) }{s}e^{-sx/\nu }\)

Taking the inverse Laplace transform,

\(\upsilon \left(x,t\right) =Eu_{-1}\left(t-\frac{x}{\nu } \right) \)

 

\(i\left(x,t\right) =\frac{E}{Z_{c} }u_{-1}\left(t-\frac{x}{\nu } \right) \)

b. At the center of the line, where x = l/2,

\(\upsilon \left(\frac{l}{2^{,} }t \right) =Eu_{-1}\left(t-\frac{\tau }{2} \right) \quad\quad i\left(\frac{l}{2^{,} } t\right)=\frac{E}{Z_{c} } u_{-1} \left(t-\frac{\tau }{2} \right) \)

At the receiving end, where x = I,

\(\upsilon \left(l,t\right) =Eu_{-1}\left(t-\tau \right)\) i\(\left(l,i\right)=\frac{E}{Z_{c} }u_{-1}\left(t-\tau \right)\)

These waves, plotted in Figure 13.4, can be explained as follows. At t = 0 the ideal step voltage of E volts, applied to the sending end, encounters \(Z_{c}\), the characteristic impedance of the line. Therefore, a forward traveling step voltage wave of E volts is initiated at the sending end. Also, since the ratio of the forward traveling voltage to current is \(Z_{c}\), a forward traveling step current wave of \(\left(E/Z_{c} \right) \) amperes is initiated. These waves travel in the positive x direction, arriving at the center of the line at \(t=\tau /2\), and at the end of the line at \(t=\tau \). The receiving-end load is matched to the line; that is, \(Z_{R}=Z_{c}\) for a matched load, \(\Gamma _{R}=0 \), and therefore no backward traveling waves are initiated. In steady-state, the line with matched load is energized at E volts with current \(E/Z_{c} \) amperes.
