Guest Question.

Find the transmission coefficient, for the potential in .

Admin
Solution

\( \psi=\left\{\begin{array}{ll} A e^{i k x}+B e^{-i k x} & (x<-a) \\ C e^{i k x}+D e^{-i k x} & (-a<x<a) \\ F e^{i k x} & (x>a) \end{array}\right\} \). Impose boundary conditions:

(1) Continuity at -a : \( A e^{-i k a}+B e^{i k a}=C e^{-i k a}+D e^{i k a} \Rightarrow \beta A+B=\beta C+D, \text { where } \beta \equiv e^{-2 i k a} \).

(2) Continuity at +a : \( C e^{i k a}+D e^{-i k a}=F e^{i k a} \Rightarrow F=C+\beta D \).

(3) Discontinuity in ψ′ at -a : \( i k\left(C e^{-i k a}-D e^{i k a}\right)-i k\left(A e^{-i k a}-B e^{i k a}\right)=-\frac{2 m \alpha}{\hbar^{2}}\left(A e^{-i k a}+B e^{i k a}\right) \) ⇒ \( \Rightarrow \beta C-D=\beta(\gamma+1) A+B(\gamma-1) \), where \( \gamma \equiv i 2 m \alpha / \hbar^{2} k \).

(4) Discontinuity in ψ′ at +a : \( i k F e^{i k a}-i k\left(C e^{i k a}-D e^{-i k a}\right)=-\frac{2 m \alpha}{\hbar^{2}}\left(F e^{i k a}\right) \) ⇒ \( C-\beta D=(1-\gamma) F \).

To solve for C and D, \( \left\{\begin{array}{l} \operatorname{add}(2) \text { and }(4): \quad 2 C=F+(1-\gamma) F \Rightarrow 2 C=(2-\gamma) F \\ \text { subtract }(2) \text { and }(4): 2 \beta D=F-(1-\gamma) F \Rightarrow 2 D=(\gamma / \beta) F \end{array}\right. \).

\( \left\{\begin{array}{l} \operatorname{add}(1) \text { and }(3): \quad 2 \beta C=\beta A+B+\beta(\gamma+1) A+B(\gamma-1) \Rightarrow 2 C=(\gamma+2) A+(\gamma / \beta) B \\ \text { subtract (1) and }(3): 2 D=\beta A+B-\beta(\gamma+1) A-B(\gamma-1) \Rightarrow 2 D=-\gamma \beta A+(2-\gamma) B \end{array}\right. \)

 

\( \left\{\begin{array}{l} \text { Equate the two expressions for } 2 C:(2-\gamma) F=(\gamma+2) A+(\gamma / \beta) B \\ \text { Equate the two expressions for } 2 D:(\gamma / \beta) F=-\gamma \beta A+(2-\gamma) B \end{array}\right. \).

Solve these for F and B, in terms of A. Multiply the first by \( \beta(2-\gamma) \), the second by γ and subtract:

\( \left[\beta(2-\gamma)^{2} F=\beta\left(4-\gamma^{2}\right) A+\gamma(2-\gamma) B\right] ; \quad\left[\left(\gamma^{2} / \beta\right) F=-\beta \gamma^{2} A+\gamma(2-\gamma) B\right] \).

\( \Rightarrow\left[\beta(2-\gamma)^{2}-\gamma^{2} / \beta\right] F=\beta\left[4-\gamma^{2}+\gamma^{2}\right] A=4 \beta A \Rightarrow \frac{F}{A}=\frac{4}{(2-\gamma)^{2}-\gamma^{2} / \beta^{2}} \).

Let \( g \equiv i / \gamma=\frac{\hbar^{2} k}{2 m \alpha} ; \phi \equiv 4 k a \), \( so \gamma=\frac{i}{g}, \beta^{2}=e^{-i \phi}\) . Then: \( \frac{F}{A}=\frac{4 g^{2}}{(2 g-i)^{2}+e^{i \phi}} \).

Denominator: \( 4 g^{2}-4 i g-1+\cos \phi+i \sin \phi=\left(4 g^{2}-1+\cos \phi\right)+i(\sin \phi-4 g) \).

\( \mid \text { Denominator }\left.\right|^{2}=\left(4 g^{2}-1+\cos \phi\right)^{2}+(\sin \phi-4 g)^{2} \).

\( =16 g^{4}+1+\cos ^{2} \phi-8 g^{2}-2 \cos \phi+8 g^{2} \cos \phi+\sin ^{2} \phi-8 g \sin \phi+16 g^{2} \).

\( =16 g^{4}+8 g^{2}+2+\left(8 g^{2}-2\right) \cos \phi-8 g \sin \phi \).

\( T=\left|\frac{F}{A}\right|^{2}= \frac{8 g^{4}}{\left(8 g^{4}+4 g^{2}+1\right)+\left(4 g^{2}-1\right) \cos \phi-4 g \sin \phi}, \text { where } g \equiv \frac{\hbar^{2} k}{2 m \alpha} \text { and } \phi \equiv 4 k a \).
