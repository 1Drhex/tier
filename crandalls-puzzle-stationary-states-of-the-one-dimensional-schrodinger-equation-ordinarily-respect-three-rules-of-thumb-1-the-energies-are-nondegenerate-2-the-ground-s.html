Guest Question.

Crandall’s Puzzle. Stationary states of the one-dimensional Schrödinger equation ordinarily respect three “rules of thumb”: (1) the energies are nondegenerate, (2) the ground state has no nodes, the first excited state has one node, the second has two, and so on, and (3) if the potential is an even function of x, the ground state is even, the first excited state is odd, the second is even, and so on. We have already seen that the “bead-on-a-ring” () violates the first of these; now suppose we introduce a “nick” in at the origin:

\( H^{\prime}=-\alpha \delta(x) \),

(If you don’t like the delta function, make it a gaussian, as in .) This lifts the degeneracy, but what is the sequence of even and odd wave functions, and what is the sequence of node numbers? Hint: You don’t really need to do any calculations, here, and you’re welcome to assume that α is small, but by all means solve the Schrödinger equation exactly if you prefer.

Admin
Solution

In the absence of the perturbation, the ground state is

\( \psi_{0}(x)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{L}} \).

and the remaining stationary states come in degenerate pairs,

\( \psi_{n, e}(x)=\sqrt{\frac{2}{L}} \cos \left(\frac{2 n \pi x}{L}\right) \).

\( \psi_{n, o}(x)=\sqrt{\frac{2}{L}} \cos \left(\frac{2 n \pi x}{L}\right) \).

The unperturbed energies are

\( E_{n}^{0}=\frac{2 n^{2} \pi^{2} \hbar^{2}}{m L^{2}} \).

We now calculate the first-order energy corrections due to the perturbation. The correction to the ground state is

\( E_{0}^{1}=\int_{0}^{L}\left[\psi_{0}(x)\right]^{2}[-\alpha \delta(x)] \psi_{0}(x) d x=-\frac{\alpha}{L} \).

The degenerate states are already the good states (calculate the matrix W if you wish) since they are eigenstates of a reflection about x = 0 with distinct eigenvalues (±1), a symmetry of the Hamiltonian. The corrections to their energies are then

\( E_{n, e}^{1}=\int_{0}^{L}\left[\psi_{n}(x)\right]^{2}[-\alpha \delta(x)] \psi_{n}(x) d x=-\frac{2 \alpha}{L} \).

\( E_{n, o}^{1}=\int_{0}^{L}\left[\psi_{n}(x)\right]^{2}[-\alpha \delta(x)] \psi_{n}(x) d x=0 \).

These results are shown on the next page fro the first seven states. The sequence of node numbers is 0, 2, 2, 4, 4, 6, 6, ….
