Guest Question.

A two-bladed wind turbine is designed with the following characteristics. The blades are 13 m long, and the pitch at the tip is –1.970°. The turbine has a design TSR of λ = 8. Lift coefficient is a function of a as follows (note, α is in degrees): For α <21°: \(C_L\) = 0.42625 + 0.11628α − 0.00063973α\(^2\) − 8.712 × 10\(^{−5}\) α\(^3\) – 4.2576 × 10\(^{−6}\) α\(^4\). For α >21: \(C_L\) = 0.95. For the entire blade, the drag coefficient is \(C_D\) = 0.011954 + 0.00019972a + 0.00010332 α\(^2\). For the midpoint of section 6 (r/R = 0.55) find the following for operation at a tip speed ratio of 8: (a) angle of attack α; (b) angle of relative wind θ; (c) \(C_L\) and \(C_D\); (d) the local contribution to \(C_P\). Ignore the effects of tip losses. The chord at that point is 0.72 m and the twist is 3.400°.

Admin
Solution

First calculate factors needed for calculating α: r = 0.55(13) = 7.15 m; σ′ = Bc/2πr = 2(0.72)/ 2π(7.15) = 0.0321; x = rλ/R = 0.55(8) = 4.4; β = \(θ_T\) + \(β_0\) = 3.400 – 1.970 = 1.430. We can now solve for α using the lift coefficient equation for the airfoil design and Eq. (12-38). The exact solution using a computer solver is α = 7.844°, which gives \(C_L\) = 1.241 from both equations. In other words, given the values of r, σ′, x, and β, and the derived value of φ = α + β = 7.844 + 1.430 = 9.274 degrees, the following hold:

\(C_{L}=4 \sin \phi(\cos \phi-x \sin \phi) /\left[\sigma^{\prime}(\sin \phi-x \cos \phi)\right] \) (12-38)

\(\begin{aligned} &C_{L}=4 \sin \phi(\cos \phi-x \sin \phi) /\left[\sigma^{\prime}(\sin \phi-x \cos \phi)\right]=1.241 \\ &C_{L}=0.42625+0.11628 \alpha-0.00063973 \alpha^{2}-8.712 \times 10^{-5} \alpha^{3}-4.2576 \times 10^{-6} \alpha^{4}=1.24 \end{aligned}\)

Thus, answer to part (a) is α = 7.844°. Part (b), \(φ\) = α + β = 7.844 + 1.43 = 9.274. Part (c), \(C_L\) = 1.241, \(C_D\) = 0.011954 + 0.00019972α + 0.00010332α\(^2\) = 0.01988. Part (d), for the contribution to \(C_{P,i}\), we first calculate Δλ = x\(_i\) – x\(_{i − 1}\) = 4.4 – 3.6 = 0.8. Then solve for \(C_{P,i}\) using Δλ, λ, \(φ\), x, \(C_L\), and \(C_D\) in Eq. (12-42), which gives \(C_{P,i}\) = 0.0567.

\(C_{P, i}=\left(\frac{8 \Delta \lambda}{\lambda^{2}}\right) \sin ^{2} \phi_{i}\left(\cos \phi_{i}-x \sin \phi_{i}\right)\left(\sin \phi_{i}+x \cos \phi_{i}\right)\left(1-\left(C_{d} / C_{l}\right) \cot \phi_{i}\right) x^{2}\) (12-42)
