Guest Question.

A balloon behaves so the pressure is \(P = C _{2} V ^{1 / 3}, C _{2}=100 kPa / m\). The balloon is blown up with air from a starting volume of 1 \(m ^{3}\) to a volume of 4 \(m ^{3}\). Find the final mass of air assuming it is at 25째C and the work done by the air.

Admin
Solution

The process is polytropic with exponent n = -1/3.

\(P _{1}= C _{2} V ^{1 / 3}=100 \times 1^{1 / 3}=100 kPa\)

 

\(P _{2}= C _{2} V ^{1 / 3}=100 \times 4^{1 / 3}=158.74 kPa\)

 

\({ }_{1} W _{2}=\int P dV =\frac{ P _{2} V _{2}- P _{1} V _{1}}{1- n }\)

 

\(=\frac{158.74 \times 4-100 \times 1}{1-(-1 / 3)} kPa – m ^{3}=401.2 kJ\)

 

\(m _{2}=\frac{ P _{2} V _{2}}{ RT _{2}}=\frac{158.74 \times 4}{0.287 \times 298} \frac{ kPa – m ^{3}}{ kJ / kg }=7.424 kg\)
