Guest Question.

Determine the reactions and draw the shear and bending moment diagrams for the two-span continuous beam shown in Fig. \( 16.9 (\mathrm{a})\) by using the moment-distribution method.

Admin
Solution

Distribution Factors. From Fig. 16.9(a), we can see that joints \(B\) and \(C\) of the continuous beam are free to rotate. The distribution factors at joint \(B\) are

\( \begin{array}{l} \mathrm{DF}_{\mathrm{BA}}=\frac{K_{\mathrm{BA}}}{K_{\mathrm{BA}}+K_{\mathrm{BC}}}=\frac{1.5 I / 10}{(1.5 I / 10)+(I / 10)}=0.6 \\ \mathrm{DF}_{\mathrm{BC}}=\frac{K_{\mathrm{BC}}}{K_{\mathrm{BA}}+K_{\mathrm{BC}}}=\frac{I / 10}{(1.5 I / 10)+(I / 10)}=0.4 \end{array} \)

Similarly, at joint \(C\),

\( \mathrm{DF}_{C B}=\frac{K_{C B}}{K_{C B}}=\frac{0.1 I}{0.1 I}=1 \)

Fixed-End Moments.

\( \begin{array}{l} \mathrm{FEM}_{A B}=+\frac{80(10)}{8}=+100 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ \mathrm{FEM}_{B A}=-100 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ \mathrm{FEM}_{B C}=+\frac{40(10)}{8}=+50 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ \mathrm{FEM}_{C B}=-50 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \end{array} \)

Moment Distribution. After recording the distribution factors and the fixed-end moments in the moment-distribution table shown in Fig. 16.9(b), we begin the moment-distribution process by balancing joints \(B\) and \(C\). The unbalanced moment at joint \(B\) is equal to \(-100+50=-50 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}\). Thus the distributed moments at the ends \(B\) of members \(A B\) and \(B C\) are

\( \begin{array}{l} \mathrm{DM}_{\mathrm{BA}}=\mathrm{DF}_{\mathrm{BA}}\left(-\mathrm{UM}_{\mathrm{B}}\right)=0.6(+50)=+30 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ \mathrm{DM}_{B C}=\mathrm{DF}_{{B C}}\left(-\mathrm{UM}_{B}\right)=0.4(+50)=+20 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \end{array} \)

Similarly, noting that the unbalanced moment at joint \(C\) is \(-50 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}\), we determine the distributed moment at end \(C\) of member \(B C\) to be

\( \mathrm{DM}_{C B}=\mathrm{DF}_{C B}\left(-\mathrm{UM}_{C}\right)=1(+50)=+50 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \)

One-half of these distributed moments are then carried over to the far ends of the members, as shown on the third line of the moment-distribution table in Fig. 16.9(b). This process is repeated, as shown in the figure, until the unbalanced moments are negligibly small.

Final Moments. The final member end moments, obtained by summing the moments in each column of the moment-distribution table, are recorded on the last line of the table in Fig. 16.9(b).

Alternative Method. Because the end support \(C\) of the continuous beam is a simple support, the analysis can be simplified by using the reduced relative bending stiffness for member \(B C\), which is adjacent to the simple support \(C\) :

\( K_{B C}=\frac{3}{4}\left(\frac{I}{10}\right) \)

Note that the relative bending stiffess of member \(A B\) remains the same as before. The distribution factors at joint \(B\) are now given by

\( \begin{array}{l} \mathrm{DF}_{B A}=\frac{K_{B A}}{K_{B A}+K_{B C}}=\frac{1.5 I / 10}{(1.5 I / 10)+(3 I / 40)}=\frac{2}{3} \\ \mathrm{DF}_{B C}=\frac{K_{B C}}{K_{B A}+K_{B C}}=\frac{3 I / 40}{(1.5 I / 10)+(3 I / 40)}=\frac{1}{3} \end{array} \)

At joint \(C, \mathrm{DF}_{C B}=K_{C B} / K_{C B}=1\). These distribution factors, and the fixed-end moments that remain the same as before, are recorded in the moment-distribution table, as shown in Fig. 16.9(c).

Since we are using the reduced relative bending stiffess for member \(B C\), joint \(C\) needs to be balanced only once in the moment-distribution process. Thus joints \(B\) and \(C\) are balanced and the distributed moments are computed in the usual manner, as indicated on the second line of the moment-distribution table (Fig. 16.9(c)). However, as shown on the third line of the table in Fig. 16.9(c), no moment is carried over to end \(C\) of member \(B C\). Joint \(B\) is balanced once more, and the moment is carried over to the end \(A\) of member \(A B\) (lines 4 and 5 ). Because both joints \(B\) and \(C\) are now balaced, we can end the moment-distribution process and determine the final moments by summing the moments in each column of the moment-distribution table.

Member End Shears. The member end shears, obtained by considering the equilibrium of each member, are shown in Fig. 16.9(d).

Support Reactions. See Fig. 16.9(e).
