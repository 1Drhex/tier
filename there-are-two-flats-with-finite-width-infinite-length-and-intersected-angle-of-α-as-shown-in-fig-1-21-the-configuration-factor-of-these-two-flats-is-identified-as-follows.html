Guest Question.

There are two flats with finite width, infinite length, and intersected angle of α, as shown in Fig. 1.21. The configuration factor of these two flats is identified as follows.

Admin
Solution

The solution can be divided into two steps: first, calculating the configuration factor from the narrow strip dx to the surface with the width of s, \(\phi_{dx,s'}\); then obtaining \(\phi_{s',s}\), by integrating x.

Draw the auxiliary chart as shown in Fig. 1.21b, then, according to Eq. (1.81):

\(\begin{aligned}\phi_{d_{1}, d_{2}} &=\frac{s^{3} \cos \theta d \theta}{\pi} \int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} \frac{d x}{\left(s^{2}+x^{2}\right)^{2}} \\&=\frac{s^{3} \cos \theta d \theta}{\pi}\left[\frac{x}{2 s^{2}\left(s^{2}+x^{2}\right)}+\frac{1}{2 s^{2}} \arctan \frac{x}{s}\right]_{-\infty}^{+\infty} \\&=\frac{\cos \theta d \theta}{2} \\&=\frac{1}{2} d(\sin \theta)\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\varphi_{dx,dz} = \frac{1}{2}d(\sin\theta)\)

where angle θ is in the normal plane of the two differential narrow strips, which is positive in the clockwise direction of the normal line of dx.
The configuration factor from dx to the surface with the width of s is:

\(\begin{aligned}\varphi_{d x, s} &=\int_{z=0}^{s} \varphi_{d x, d z}=\int_{\theta=-\frac{\pi}{2}}^{0} \frac{1}{2} d(\sin \theta)+\int_{0}^{\theta^{\prime}} \frac{1}{2} d(\sin \theta) \\&=\left.\frac{\sin \theta}{2}\right|_{\theta=-\frac{\pi}{2}} ^{0}+\left.\frac{\sin \theta}{2}\right|_{\theta=0} ^{\theta^{\prime}}=\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{2} \sin \theta^{\prime}\end{aligned}\)

The expression of function sinu9 from Fig. 1.21b is identified as:

\(\sin\theta^\prime = \frac{b}{c} = \frac{scos\alpha – x}{(x^{2} + s^{2} – 2xscos\alpha )^{1/2}} \)

Thus,

\(\varphi_{dx,s} = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{scos\alpha – x}{2(x^{2} + s^{2} – 2xscos\alpha )^{1/2}}\)

According to interchangeability, another configuration factor is:

\(\varphi_{s,dx} = \frac{dx}{s}\varphi_{dx,s} = \left[\frac{1}{2s} + \frac{cos\alpha – \frac{x}{s} }{2(x^{2} + s^{2} – 2xscos\alpha )^{1/2}} \right]dx\) (1.88)

From Eq. (1.87):

\( \varphi_{21} = \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}} \varphi_{12} = \frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\frac{1}{A_{1}}\int_{A_{1}}^{}{\varphi_{d_{1},2}}dA_{1}\)

 

\(= \frac{1}{A_{2}}\int_{A_{2}}^{}{A_{2}\varphi_{2,d_{1}}} = \int_{A_{1}}^{}{\varphi_{2,d_{1}}}\)

 

\(\varphi_{s,s^\prime} =\int_{x=0}^{s^\prime}{\varphi_{s,dx}}= \int_{0}^{s^\prime}{\left[\frac{1}{2s} + \frac{cos\alpha – \frac{x}{s} }{2(x^{2} + s^{2} – 2xscos\alpha )^{1/2}} \right]dx}\)

According to subject meaning,\(s=s^{\prime} .\). Define a dimensionless variable \(\frac{x}{s}= X\), then the above equation can be rewritten as follows:

\(\varphi_{s, s^{\prime}}=\int_{0}^{1}\left[\frac{1}{2}+\frac{\cos \alpha-X}{2\left(X^{2}+1-2 X \cos \alpha\right)^{1 / 2}}\right] d X\)

Integrate the above equation, then:

\(\varphi_{s, s^{\prime}}=1-\left(\frac{1-\cos \alpha}{2}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}}=1-\sin \frac{\alpha}{2}\) (1.89)

Eq. (1.89) suggests that the configuration factor of flats of the same width is only related to the intersected angle. Because the areas of two flats are the same, according to interchangeability:

\(\varphi_{s, s^{\prime}}=\varphi_{s^{\prime}, s}\)

The above derivations exist under the premise that the surfaces are isothermal blackbody surfaces. Their applicability will be discussed in detail in Section 1.7.
