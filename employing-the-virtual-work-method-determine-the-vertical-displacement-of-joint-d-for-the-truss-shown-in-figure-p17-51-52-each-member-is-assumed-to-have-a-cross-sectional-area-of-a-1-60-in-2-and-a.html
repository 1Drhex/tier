Guest Question.

Employing the virtual-work method, determine the vertical displacement of joint D for the truss shown in Figure P17.51/52. Each member is assumed to have a cross-sectional area of A = 1.60 \(\text { in. }^{2}\) and an elastic modulus of E = 29,000 ksi. The loads acting on the truss are P = 20 kips and Q = 30 kips.

Admin
Solution

Real Truss Reaction Forces

\(A_{x}=30.000 \text { kips } \leftarrow \quad A_{y}=36.667 \text { kips } \downarrow \quad C_{y}=76.667 \text { kips } \uparrow\)

Virtual Load
Assume that a 1-kip virtual load is applied in the â€“y direction at D. The reaction forces for the virtual load are:

\(A_{x}=0.00 \text { kips } \quad A_{y}=0.667 kips \downarrow \quad C_{y}=1.667 kips \uparrow\)

 

\(\begin{array}{ccccc}\hline \text { Member } & \begin{array}{c}L \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}F \\\text { (kips) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}f \\\text { (kips) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}f(F L) \\\text { (kips } \left.^{2} \text {-in. }\right)\end{array} \\\hline A B & 259.5997 & 44.0679 & 0.8012 & 9,166.0714 \\A C & 216.0000 & 5.5556 & -0.4444 & -533.3284 \\B C & 227.6840 & -59.7319 & -0.7027 & 9,557.1278 \\B D & 216.0000 & 43.3333 & 0.6667 & 6,240.0307 \\C D & 259.5997 & -24.0370 & -1.20185 & 7,499.5445 \\\hline & & & \sum f(F L)= & 31,929.4460 \\\hline\end{array}\)

 

Apply Equation (17.30)

\(1 \cdot \Delta=\sum_{j} f_{j}\left(\frac{F_{j} L_{j}}{A_{j} E_{j}}\right)=\frac{1}{A E} \sum_{j} f_{j}\left(F_{j} L_{j}\right)\)

to get the deflection at B from the tabulated results:

\(\begin{aligned}(1 kip ) \cdot \Delta_{D} &=\frac{\left(31,929.4460 kips ^{2}- in .\right)}{\left(1.60 in .^{2}\right)(29,000 ksi )} \\\Delta_{D} &=0.688 in . \downarrow\end{aligned}\)

Since the virtual load was applied in a downward direction at D, the positive value of the result confirms that joint D does displace downward.
