Guest Question.

One of the growing applications of the power electronics is light dimming, where the average power applied to the light is varied to change its brightness. Figure 2.8a shows a simpliﬁed circuit for conventional incandescent lamp with dimming functionality. Figure 2.8b shows the typical input and output voltage waveforms for such dimming circuit of Fig. 2.8a. By varying the phase angle α, it can be shown that average power delivered to the lamp may be varied from 0% to 100% of the available rated power. Hence dimming is achieved theoretically for the entire range. Assume that the incandescent lamp acts like constant pure resistance at it maximum rated power, with \(\ v_{s}(t) = V_{p} \sin \omega t =120\sqrt{2} \sin \left(120\pi t\right) V \) , and the maximum rated lamp power,\(\ P_{L,max} =100W \) .

(a) Discuss the type of switch is required to input this circuit functionality.

(b) Determine the equivalent rated lamp resistance.

(c) Derive the expression for the rms value for the output voltage, \(\ \left(V_{o} \left(t\right)\right) \) , as a function of the control angle α. What are the rms values for \(\ \alpha = \frac{\pi }{3} ,\frac{\pi }{2},\frac{3\pi }{2} and \pi \) .

(d) Derive the expression for the average output power, \(\ P_{o,ave} \) , as a function of α. What are the values at for \(\ \alpha = \frac{\pi }{3} ,\frac{\pi }{2},\frac{3\pi }{2} and \pi \).

(e) The percentages of the power delivered to the lamp with respect to its maximum rated value for \(\ \alpha = \frac{\pi }{3} ,\frac{\pi }{2},\frac{3\pi }{2} and \pi \) .

Solution

(a) Since the load (incandescent lamp) is resistive, then the load current ﬂows in both directions given theinput sourceis sinusoidal.This meanstheswitchshould allow bidirectional current ﬂow. Moreover, when the switch is not conducting, it should be able to block positive and negative voltages. Fortunately, there is one device in the market that can support bidirectional current ﬂow and block the voltages bidirectionally as well. This device is known as the Triac and is discussed in the next section.

(b) Since the maximum rated power is given and load assumed resistive, the equivalent load resistance, RL, is obtained from:

\(\ P_{o,max} =\frac{V^{2}_{o,rms,max} }{R_{L} } \)

\(\ V_{o,rms,max} \) is the maximum available rms output voltage across the lamp. So the load resistance is given by:

\(\ R_{L}=\frac{V^{2}_{o,rms,max}}{P_{o,max}}=\frac{V^{2}_{s,rms}}{P_{o,max}} =\frac{120^{2} }{100} =144\Omega \)

(c) The output voltage \(\ V_{s} \) (t) is obtained by inspection, as follows:

\(\ V_{o} (t)=\begin{cases} 0 & 0\lt \omega t\leq \alpha and \pi \lt \omega t\leq \pi +\alpha \\ V_{s} \left(t\right) & \alpha \lt \omega t\leq \pi and \pi+ \alpha \lt \omega t\leq 2\pi \end{cases} \)

Because of the symmetry of \(\ V_{o} \) (t), it can be shown that its rms is given by the following equation:

\(\ V_{o,rms} =\sqrt{\frac{1}{\pi } \int_{\alpha }^{\pi }{V^{2}_{s} \left(t\right) d\omega t}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{\pi } \int_{\alpha }^{\pi }{V^{2}_{p}\sin ^{2} \omega t d\omega t }}} \)

Students are invited to verify that \(\ V_{o,rms} \) is given by:

\(\ V_{o,rms} = V_{p} \sqrt{\frac{1}{2} \left(1-\frac{\alpha }{\pi }+\frac{\sin 2\alpha }{ 2\pi } \right) } = V_{s,rms} \sqrt{1-\frac{\alpha }{\pi }+\frac{\sin 2\alpha }{ 2\pi }} \)

For \(\ \alpha = \frac{\pi }{3} ,\frac{\pi }{2},\frac{3\pi }{2} and \pi \), we have :

\(\ V_{o,rms}\left(\frac{\pi }{3} \right) = 107.632 V \) \(\ V_{o,rms}\left(\frac{\pi }{2} \right)=84.85 V \)
\(\ V_{o,rms}\left(\frac{3\pi }{2} \right) =84.85j V \) \(\ V_{o,rms}\left(\pi \right) =0 V \)

(d) The average output power, \(\ P_{o,ave} \) , is given by:

\(\ P_{o,ave} =\frac{V_{o,rms}^{2} }{R_{L} } =\frac{V_{s,rms}^{2}}{R_{L}} \left(1-\frac{\alpha }{\pi } + \frac{\sin 2\alpha }{ 2\pi } \right) \)

Notice when α=0, no dimming, the output power is at its maximum ratio.

For \(\ \alpha = \frac{\pi }{3} ,\frac{\pi }{2},\frac{3\pi }{2} and \pi \), the average output power is given by :

\(\ P_{o,ave}\left(\frac{\pi }{3} \right) = 80.45 W \) \(\ P_{o,ave}\left(\frac{\pi }{2} \right)=50 W \)
\(\ P_{o,ave}\left(\frac{3\pi }{2} \right) =- 50 W \) \(\ P_{o,ave}\left(\pi \right) =0 W \)

(e) The percentage of the power delivered of the lamp with respect to the maximum rated value is given by:

%P = \(\ \frac{P_{o,ave}}{P_{o,max}} \times 100\)%\( = \left(1-\frac{\alpha }{\pi } + \frac{\sin 2\alpha }{ 2\pi } \right) \times 100\)%

For \(\ \alpha = \frac{\pi }{3} ,\frac{\pi }{2},\frac{3\pi }{2} and \pi \), the average output power is given by :
