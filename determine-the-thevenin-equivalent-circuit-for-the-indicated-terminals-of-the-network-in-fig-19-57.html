Guest Question.

Determine the Thévenin equivalent circuit for the indicated terminals of the network in Fig. 19.57.

Admin
Solution

Apply the second method introduced in this section.

\(E_{Th}\)

 

\(E_{oc}=V_2\\[0.5cm] I=\frac{V_i-k_1V_2}{R_1}=\frac{V_i-k_1E_{oc}}{R_1}\\[0.5cm] and\hspace{30 pt} E_{oc}=-k_2IR_2=-k_2R_2\left(\frac{V_i-k_1E_{oc}}{R_1} \right)\\[0.5cm] =\frac{-k_2R_2V_i}{R_1}+\frac{k_1k_2R_2E_{oc}}{R_1} \\[0.5cm] or\hspace{30 pt} E_{oc}=\left(1-\frac{k_1k_2R_2}{R_1} \right)=\frac{-k_2R_2V_i}{R_1}\\[0.5cm] and \hspace{30 pt} E_{oc}\left(\frac{R_1 – k_1k_2R_2}{R_1} \right)=\frac{-k_2R_2V_i}{R_1}\\[0.5cm] so\hspace{30 pt} E_{oc}=\frac{−k_2R_2V_i}{R_1 − k_1k_2R_2}=E_{Th}\hspace{30 pt} (19.5) \)

 

\(I_{sc}\) For the network in Fig. 19.58, where

\(V_2 = 0 \qquad k_1V_2 = 0 \qquad I =\frac{V_i}{R_1}\\[0.5cm] and\hspace{30 pt} I_{sc}=-k_2I=\frac{-k_2V_i}{R_1}\\[0.5cm] so\hspace{30 pt} Z_{Th}=\frac{E_{oc}}{I_{sc}}=\frac{\frac{-k_2R_2V_i}{R_1 – k_1k_2R_2} }{\frac{-k_2V_i}{R_1} }\\[0.5cm] and\hspace{30 pt} Z_{Th}=\frac{R_1R_2}{R_1 – k_1k_2R_2}\hspace{30 pt} (19.6) \)

Frequently, the approximation \(k_1 ≅ 0\) is applied. Then the Thévenin voltage and impedance are, respectively,

\(E_{Th}=\frac{−k_2R_2V_i}{R_1}\qquad k_1=0\hspace{30 pt} (19.7)\\[0.5cm] Z_{Th}=R_2\qquad \quad \qquad k_1=0\hspace{30 pt} (19.8) \)

Apply \(Z_{Th} = E_g/I_g\) to the network in Fig. 19.59, where

\(I=\frac{-k_1V_2}{R_1} \\[0.5cm] But\hspace{30 pt} V_2=E_g\\[0.5cm] so\hspace{30 pt} I=\frac{-k_1E_g}{R_1} \)

Applying Kirchhoff’s current law, we have

\(I_g=k_2I+\frac{E_g}{R_2}=k_2\left(-\frac{k_1E_g}{R_1} \right)+\frac{E_g}{R_2}=E_g\left(\frac{1}{R_2}-\frac{k_1k_2}{R_1} \right) \\[0.5cm] and\hspace{30 pt} \frac{I_g}{E_g}=\frac{R_1 – k_1k_2R_2}{R_1R_2}\\[0.5cm] or\hspace{30 pt} Z_{Th}=\frac{E_g}{I_g}=\frac{R_1R_2}{R_1 − k_1k_2R_2} \)

as obtained above.
