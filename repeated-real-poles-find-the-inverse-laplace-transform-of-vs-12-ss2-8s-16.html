Guest Question.

Repeated Real Poles. Find the inverse Laplace transform of

\(V(s) = \frac{12}{s(s^2 + 8s + 16)}\)
Admin
Solution

First, we will do this problem without using MATLAB. Noticing that \(s^2 + 8s + 16 = (s + 4)^2\), we begin the partial fraction expansion:

\(V(s) = \frac{12}{s(s^2 + 8s + 16)} = \frac{12}{s(s + 4)^2} = \frac{k}{s + 4} + \frac{-3}{(s + 4)^2} + \frac{\frac{3}{4}}{s}\)

Next, the constant k is evaluated by multiplying both sides of the last equation by \(s(s + 4)^2\).

\(12 = ks(s + 4) – 3s + \frac{3}{4} (s + 4)^2 = \left(\frac{3}{4} + k\right)s^2 + (3 + 4k)s + 12 \Rightarrow k = – \frac{3}{4}\)

Finally,

\(v(t) = \mathscr{L}^{-1} \left[\frac{-\frac{3}{4}}{s + 4} + \frac{-3}{(s + 4)^2} + \frac{\frac{3}{4}}{s}\right] = \left(\frac{3}{4} â€“ e^{-4t} \left(\frac{3}{4} + 3t\right)\right)u(t) V\)

Next, we perform the partial fraction expansion, using the MATLAB function residue:

>>num = [12 ];

>>den = [1 8 16 0 ];

>>[r, p] = residue(num, den)

MATLAB responds

r =
-0.7500
-3.0000
0.7500

P =
-4
-4
0

A repeated pole of multiplicity m is listed m times corresponding to the m terms

\(\frac{r_1}{s – p}, \frac{r_2}{(s – p)^2}, . . . \frac{r_m}{(s – p)^m}\)

listed in order of increasing powers of s – p. The constants, \(r_1, r_2 . . ., r_m\) are the corresponding residues, again listed in order of increasing powers of s – p. In our present case, the pole p = -4 has multiplicity 2, and the first two terms of the partial fraction expansion are

\(\frac{-0.75}{s – (-4)} + \frac{-3}{(s – (-4))^2} = \frac{-0.75}{s + 4} + \frac{-3}{(s + 4)^2}\)

The entire partial fraction expansion is

\(\frac{-0.75}{s – (-4)} + \frac{-3}{(s – (-4))^2} + \frac{0.75}{s – (0)} = \frac{-0.75}{s + 4} + \frac{-3}{(s + 4)^2} + \frac{0.75}{s}\)

Finally, as before,

\(v(t) = \mathscr{L}^{-1} \left[\frac{-0.75}{s + 4} + \frac{-3}{(s + 4)^2} + \frac{0.75}{s}\right] = (0.75 – e^{-4t}(0.75 + 3t))u(t) V\)
