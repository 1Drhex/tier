Guest Question.

Use the WKB approximation in the form of Equation 9.52,

\( \int_{x_{1}}^{x_{2}} p(x) d x=\left(n-\frac{1}{2}\right) \pi \hbar, \quad \text { with } n=1,2,3, \ldots \) (9.52).

\( \int_{r_{1}}^{r_{2}} p(r) d r=\left(n^{\prime}-1 / 2\right) \pi \hbar \), (9.56).

to estimate the bound state energies for hydrogen. Don’t forget the centrifugal term in the effective potential (Equation 4.38). The following integral may help:

\( V_{ eff }=V+\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \frac{\ell(\ell+1)}{r^{2}} \) (4.38).

\( \int_{a}^{b} \frac{1}{x} \sqrt{(x-a)(b-x)} d x=\frac{\pi}{2}(\sqrt{b}-\sqrt{a})^{2} \). (9.57)

Answer:

\( E_{n^{\prime} \ell} \approx \frac{-13.6 eV }{\left[n^{\prime}-(1 / 2)+\sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)}\right]^{2}} \) (9.58).

I put a prime on n′, because there is no reason to suppose it corresponds to the n in the Bohr formula. Rather, it orders the states for a given \( \ell \), counting the number of nodes in the radial wave function. In the notation of Chapter 4, \( n^{\prime}=N=n-\ell \), (Equation 4.67). Put this in, expand the square root \( \left(\sqrt{1+\epsilon}=1+\frac{1}{2} \epsilon-\frac{1}{8} \epsilon^{2}+\cdots\right) \), and compare your result to the Bohr
formula.

\( n \equiv N+\ell \) (4.67).

Solution

\( \left(n^{\prime}-\frac{1}{2}\right) \pi \hbar=\int_{r_{1}}^{r_{2}} \sqrt{2 m\left(E+\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \frac{1}{r}-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \frac{\ell(\ell+1)}{r^{2}}\right)} d r=\sqrt{-2 m E} \int_{r_{1}}^{r_{2}} \sqrt{-1+\frac{A}{r}-\frac{B}{r^{2}}} d r \).

\( \text { where } A \equiv-\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \frac{1}{E} \quad \text { and } \quad B \equiv-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \frac{\ell(\ell+1)}{E} \). are positive constants, since E is negative.

\( \left(n^{\prime}-\frac{1}{2}\right) \pi \hbar=\sqrt{-2 m E} \int_{r_{1}}^{r_{2}} \frac{\sqrt{-r^{2}+A r-B}}{r} d r \).

\( \text { Let } r_{1} \text { and } r_{2} \text { be the roots of the polynomial in the numerator: } \) \( -r^{2}+A r-B=\left(r-r_{1}\right)\left(r_{2}-r\right) \).

\( \left(n^{\prime}-\frac{1}{2}\right) \pi \hbar=\sqrt{-2 m E} \int_{r_{1}}^{r_{2}} \frac{\sqrt{\left(r-r_{1}\right)\left(r_{2}-r\right)}}{r} d r=\sqrt{-2 m E} \frac{\pi}{2}\left(\sqrt{r_{2}}-\sqrt{r_{1}}\right)^{2} \).

\( 2\left(n^{\prime}-\frac{1}{2}\right) \hbar=\sqrt{-2 m E}\left(r_{2}+r_{1}-2 \sqrt{r_{1} r_{2}}\right) . \quad \text { But }-r^{2}+A r-B=-r^{2}+\left(r_{1}+r_{2}\right) r-r_{1} r_{2} \)

\( \Longrightarrow r_{1}+r_{2}=A ; r_{1} r_{2}=B \) Therefore

\( 2\left(n^{\prime}-\frac{1}{2}\right) \hbar=\sqrt{-2 m E}(A-2 \sqrt{B})=\sqrt{-2 m E}\left(-\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \frac{1}{E}-2 \sqrt{-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \frac{\ell(\ell+1)}{E}}\right) \).

\( =-\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \sqrt{-\frac{2 m}{E}}-2 \hbar \sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)} \).

\( -\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \sqrt{-\frac{2 m}{E}}=2 \hbar\left[n^{\prime}-\frac{1}{2}+\sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)}\right] ; \quad-\frac{E}{2 m}=\frac{\left(e^{2} / 4 \pi \epsilon_{0}\right)^{2}}{4 \hbar^{2}\left[n^{\prime}-\frac{1}{2}+\sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)}\right]^{2}} \).

\( E_{n^{\prime} \ell}=\frac{-\left(m / 2 \hbar^{2}\right)\left(e^{2} / 4 \pi \epsilon_{0}\right)^{2}}{\left[n^{\prime}-\frac{1}{2}+\sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)}\right]^{2}}= \frac{-13.6 eV }{\left(n^{\prime}-\frac{1}{2}+\sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)}\right)^{2}} \).

\( \sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)}=\ell \sqrt{1+\frac{1}{\ell}}=\ell\left(1+\frac{1}{2 \ell}-\frac{1}{8 \ell^{2}}+\ldots\right) \approx \ell+\frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{8 \ell} \).

so

\( \left[n^{\prime}-(1 / 2)+\sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)}\right]=[n-\ell-(1 / 2)+\sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)}] \approx[n-\ell-(1 / 2)+\ell+(1 / 2)-(1 / 8 \ell)]=n-(1 / 8 \ell) \)

and hence

\( E_{n \ell} \approx \frac{-13.6 eV }{[n-(1 / 8 \ell)]^{2}} \approx \frac{-13.6 eV }{n^{2}} \).

which is the Bohr formula.
