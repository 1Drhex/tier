Guest Question.

Given: A state of stress referenced to a x−y−z coordinate is known to be

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} \sigma _{xx} & \sigma _{xy} & \sigma _{xz} \\ \sigma _{yx} & \sigma _{yy} & \sigma _{yz} \\\sigma _{zx} & \tau _{zy} & \sigma _{zz} \end{matrix} \right ]= \left [ \begin{matrix} 50 & - 10 & 15 \\ - 10 & 25 & 30 \\ 15 & 30 & - 5 \end{matrix} \right ]\)(Ksi)

It is desired to express this state of stress in an x″−y″−z″ coordinate system, generated by the following two sequential rotations:

i. Rotation of θ = 20° about the original z-axis (which defines an intermediate x′−y′−z′ coordinate system), followed by ii. Rotation of β = 35° about the x′-axis (which defines the final x″−y″−z″ coordinate system)

PROBLEM

a. Rotate the stress tensor to the x″−y″−z″ coordinate system.

b. Calculate the first, second, and third invariants of the stress tensor using (i) elements of the stress tensor referenced to the x−y−z coordinate system, \(σ_{ij}\), and (ii) elements of the stress tensor referenced to the x″−y″−z″ coordinate system,\( σ_{i^{\prime \prime} j^{\prime \prime}}\).

Admin
Solution

a. General expressions for direction cosines relating the x−y−z and x″−y″−z″ coordinate systems were determined as a part of . The direction cosines were found to be

\(c_{x^{\prime \prime}x} = \cos θ\)

 

\(c_{x^{\prime \prime}y} = \sin θ\)

 

\(c_{x^{\prime \prime}z} = 0\)

 

\(c_{y^{\prime \prime}x} = -\cos β \sin θ\)

 

\(c_{y^{\prime \prime}y} =\cos β \sin θ\)

 

\(c_{y^{\prime \prime}z} = \sin β \)

 

\(c_{z^{\prime \prime}x} = \sin β \sin θ\)

 

\(c_{z^{\prime \prime}y} = -\sin β \cos θ\)

 

\(c_{z^{\prime \prime}z} = \cos β \)

 

As in this problem θ = 20° and β = 35°, the numerical values of the direction cosines are

\(c_{x^{\prime \prime}x} = \cos (20°) = 0.9397\)

 

\(c_{x^{\prime \prime}y} = \sin (20°) = 0.3420\)

 

\(c_{x^{\prime \prime}z} = 0\)

 

\(c_{y^{\prime \prime}x} =- \cos(35°) \sin (20°) = 0.2802\)

 

\(c_{y^{\prime \prime}y} = \cos(35)\cos (20°) = 0.7698\)

 

\(c_{y^{\prime \prime}z} = \sin (35°) = 0.5736\)

 

\(c_{z^{\prime \prime}x} = \sin (35°)\sin(20°) = 0.1962\)

 

\(c_{z^{\prime \prime}y} = – \sin (35°)\cos(20°) = – 0.5390\)

 

\(c_{z^{\prime \prime}z} = – \cos (35°) = 0.8192\)

 

Each component of the transformed stress tensor is now found through application of either Equation 2.12a or 2.12b.

\(\sigma _{i^′j^′}= c_{i^′k}c_{j^′l}\sigma _{kl}\) where i,j,k,l =x,y,z (2.12a)

 

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} \sigma _{x^′x^′} & \sigma _{x^′y^′} & \sigma _{x^′z^′} \\ \sigma _{y^′x^′} & \sigma _{y^′y^′} & \sigma _{y^′z^′} \\ \sigma _{z^′x^′} & \sigma _{z^′y^′} & \sigma _{z^′z^′} \end{matrix} \right ]= \left [ \begin{matrix} c _{x^′x} & c _{x^′y} & c _{x^′z} \\ c _{y^′x} & c _{y^′y} & c _{y^′z} \\ c _{z^′x} & c _{z^′y} & c _{z^′z} \end{matrix} \right ]\times \left [ \begin{matrix} \sigma _{xx} & \sigma _{xy} & \sigma _{xz} \\ \sigma _{yx} & \sigma _{yy} & \sigma _{yz} \\ \sigma _{zx} & \sigma _{zy} & \sigma _{zz} \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} c _{x^′x} & c _{y^′x} & c _{z^′x} \\ c _{x^′y} & c _{y^′y} & c _{z^′y} \\ c _{x^′z} & c _{y^′z} & c _{z^′z} \end{matrix} \right ] \) (2.12b)

For example, if indicial notation is used stress component \(σ_{x^{\prime \prime} z^{\prime \prime}}\)can be found using Equation 2.13:

\(σ_{x^′z^′} = c_{x^′x} c_{z^′x}σ_{ xx} + c_{x^′x} c_{z^′y }σ_{xy} + c_{x^′x} c_{z^′z}σ_{ xz} +c_{x^′y} c_{z^′x }σ_{yx} + c_{x^′y} c_{z^′y }σ_{yy} +c_{x^′y} c_{z^′z }σ_{yz}+ c_{x^′z} c_{z^′x }σ_{zx} +c_{x^′z} c_{z^′y }σ_{zy} + c_{x^′z} c_{x^′z }σ_{zz}\) (2.13)

\(σ_{x^{\prime \prime} z^{\prime \prime}} = c_{x^{\prime \prime}x} c_{z^{\prime \prime}x}σ_{ xx} + c_{x^{\prime \prime}x} c_{z^{\prime \prime}y }σ_{xy} + c_{x^{\prime \prime}x} c_{z ^{\prime \prime}z}σ_{ xz} +c_{x^{\prime \prime}y} c_{z^{\prime \prime}x }σ_{yx} + c_{x^{\prime \prime}y} c_{z^{\prime \prime}y }σ_{yy} +c_{x^{\prime \prime}y} c_{z^{\prime \prime}z }σ_{yz}+ c_{x^{\prime \prime}z} c_{z^{\prime \prime}x }σ_{zx} +c_{x^{\prime \prime}z} c_{z^{\prime \prime}y }σ_{zy} + c_{x^{\prime \prime}z} c_{x^{\prime \prime}z }σ_{zz}\)

 

\(σ_{x^{\prime \prime} z^{\prime \prime}} = ( 0.9397)(0.1962)(50 Ksi) +(0.9397)(- 0.5390)( – 10 Ksi) + (0.9397)(0.8192)( 15 Ksi) + (0.3420) (0.1962)( -10Ksi) +(0.3420)( – 0,5390)( 25 Ksi) +(0.3420)(0.8192)( 30 Ksi) +(0)(0.1962)( 15 Ksi) + (0)(−0.5390)( 30 Ksi) + (0)(0.8192)( – 5 Ksi) \)

 

\(σ_{x^{\prime \prime}z^{\prime \prime}} = 28.95 Ksi\)

Alternatively, if matrix notation is used, then Equation 2.12b becomes:

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} \sigma _{x^{\prime \prime}x^{\prime \prime}} & \sigma _{x^{\prime \prime}y^{\prime \prime}} & \sigma _{x^{\prime \prime}z^{\prime \prime}} \\ \sigma _{y^{\prime \prime}x^{\prime \prime}} & \sigma _{y^{\prime \prime}y^{\prime \prime}} & \sigma _{y^{\prime \prime}z^{\prime \prime}} \\ \sigma _{z^{\prime \prime}x^{\prime \prime}} & \sigma _{z^{\prime \prime}y^{\prime \prime}} & \sigma _{z^{\prime \prime}z^{\prime \prime}} \end{matrix} \right ]= \left [ \begin{matrix} 0.9397& 0.3420 & 0 \\ -0.2802 & 0.7698 & 0.5736 \\ 0.1962 & -0.5390 & 0.8192 \end{matrix} \right ]\left [ \begin{matrix} 50& – 10 &15\\ -10 & 25 & 30 \\ 15 & 30 & – 5 \end{matrix} \right ]\times\left [ \begin{matrix} 0.9397& -0.2802 & 0.1962 \\ 0.3420 & 0.7698 &-0.5390 \\ 0 & 0.5736 & 0.8192 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

Completing the matrix multiplication indicated, there results:

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} \sigma _{x^{\prime \prime}x^{\prime \prime}} & \sigma _{x^{\prime \prime}y^{\prime \prime}} & \sigma _{x^{\prime \prime}z^{\prime \prime}} \\ \sigma _{y^{\prime \prime}x^{\prime \prime}} & \sigma _{y^{\prime \prime}y^{\prime \prime}} & \sigma _{y^{\prime \prime}z^{\prime \prime}} \\ \sigma _{z^{\prime \prime}x^{\prime \prime}} & \sigma _{z^{\prime \prime}y^{\prime \prime}} & \sigma _{z^{\prime \prime}z^{\prime \prime}} \end{matrix} \right ]=\left [ \begin{matrix} 40.65 & 1.113 & 28.95 \\ 1.113 & 43.08 & -10.60 \\ 28.95 & – 10.60 & – 13.72 \end{matrix} \right ]\)( Ksi)

Notice that the value of \(σ_{x^{\prime \prime} z^{\prime \prime}}\) determined through matrix multiplication is identical to that obtained using indicial notation, as previously described. The stress element is shown in the original and final coordinate systems in Figure 2.11.

b. The first, second, and third stress invariants will now be calculated using components of both \(σ_{ij}\) and \(σ_{i^{\prime \prime}j^{\prime \prime}}\). It is expected that identical values will be obtained, as the stress invariants are independent of coordinate system.

First Stress Invariant:

x−y−z coordinate system:

\(\Theta =\sigma _{ii}= \sigma _{xx} + \sigma _{yy} +\sigma _{zz}\)

\(\Theta\) =(50 + 25 – 5)°Ksi

\(\Theta\)= 70°Ksi

x″−y″−z″ coordinate system:

\(\Theta =\sigma _{i^{\prime \prime}i^{\prime \prime}}= \sigma _{x^{\prime \prime}x^{\prime \prime}} + \sigma _{y^{\prime \prime}y^{\prime \prime}} +\sigma _{z^{\prime \prime}z^{\prime \prime}}\)

\(\Theta\) =(40.65 + 43.08 – 13.72)

\(\Theta\)= 70Ksi

As expected, the first stress invariant is independent of coordinate system.

Second Stress Invariant:

x−y−z coordinate system:

\(\Phi =\frac{1}{2} (\sigma _{i^{\prime \prime}i^{\prime \prime}}\sigma _{j^{\prime \prime}j^{\prime \prime}}- \sigma _{i^{\prime \prime}j^{\prime \prime}}\sigma _{i^{\prime \prime}j^{\prime \prime}})= \sigma _{xx} \sigma _{yy} +\sigma _{xx}\sigma _{zz} + \sigma _{yy}\sigma _{zz} – (\sigma _{xy}^2 +\sigma _{xz}^2 + \sigma _{yz}^2)\)

 

\(\Phi = \left\{(50)(25)+(50)(- 5)+(25)(- 5)-[(-10)^2 + (15)^2 + (30)^2]\right\}(Ksi)^2\)

 

\(\Phi = -350(Ksi)^2\)

x′′ −y′′ −z′′ coordinate system:

\(\Phi =\frac{1}{2} (\sigma _{i^{\prime \prime }i^{\prime \prime }}\sigma _{j^{\prime \prime }j^{\prime \prime }}- \sigma _{i^{\prime \prime }j^{\prime \prime }}\sigma _{i^{\prime \prime }j^{\prime \prime }})\)

 

\(\Phi =\sigma _{x^{\prime \prime }x^{\prime \prime }}\sigma _{y^{\prime \prime }y^{\prime \prime }}+ \sigma _{x^{\prime \prime }x^{\prime \prime }}\sigma _{z^{\prime \prime }z^{\prime \prime }}+ \sigma _{y^{\prime \prime }y^{\prime \prime }}\sigma _{z^{\prime \prime }z^{\prime \prime }}-( \sigma^{2} _{x^{\prime \prime }y^{\prime \prime }}+\sigma^{2} _{x^{\prime \prime }z^{\prime \prime }}+ \sigma^2 _{y^{\prime \prime }z^{\prime \prime }})\)

 

\(\Phi = \left\{(40.65)(43.08)+(40.65)(-13.72)+(43.08)(-13.72)-[(1.113)^2 + (28.95)^2 + (-10.60)^2]\right\}(Ksi)^2\)

 

\(\Phi = -350(Ksi)^2\)

 

As expected, the second stress invariant is independent of coordinate system.

Third Stress Invariant:

x−y−z coordinate system:

\(\Psi =\frac{1}{6} (\sigma _{ii}\sigma _{jj}\sigma _{kk}- 3\sigma _{ii}\sigma _{jk}\sigma _{jk}+ 2\sigma _{ij}\sigma _{jk}\sigma _{ki})\)

 

\(\Psi = \sigma _{xx}\sigma _{yy}\sigma _{zz}- \sigma _{xx}\sigma _{yz}^2 -\sigma _{yy}\sigma _{xz}^2 -\sigma _{zz}\sigma _{xy}^2 + 2\sigma _{xy}\sigma _{xz}\sigma _{yz}\)

 

\(\Psi= \left[(50)(25)( -5)-(50)(30)^2-(25)(15)^2-(-5)(- 10)^2 + 2( -10)(15)(30)\right] (Ksi)^3\)

 

\(\Psi = -65375 (Ksi)^3\)

x″−y″−z″ coordinate system:

\(\Psi =\frac{1}{6} (\sigma _{i^{\prime \prime}i^{\prime \prime}}\sigma _{j^{\prime \prime}j^{\prime \prime}}\sigma _{k^{\prime \prime}k^{\prime \prime}}- 3\sigma _{i^{\prime \prime}i^{\prime \prime}}\sigma _{j^{\prime \prime}k^{\prime \prime}}\sigma _{j^{\prime \prime}k^{\prime \prime}}+ 2\sigma _{i^{\prime \prime}j^{\prime \prime}}\sigma _{j^{\prime \prime}k^{\prime \prime}}\sigma _{k^{\prime \prime}i^{\prime \prime}})\)

 

\(\Psi = \sigma _{x^{\prime \prime}x^{\prime \prime}}\sigma _{y^{\prime \prime}y^{\prime \prime}}\sigma _{z^{\prime \prime}z^{\prime \prime}}- \sigma _{x^{\prime \prime}x^{\prime \prime}}\sigma _{y^{\prime \prime}z^{\prime \prime}}^2 -\sigma _{y^{\prime \prime}y^{\prime \prime}}\sigma _{x^{\prime \prime}z^{\prime \prime}}^2 -\sigma _{z^{\prime \prime}z^{\prime \prime}}\sigma _{x^{\prime \prime}y^{\prime \prime}}^2 + 2\sigma _{x^{\prime \prime}y^{\prime \prime}}\sigma _{x^{\prime \prime}z^{\prime \prime}}\sigma _{y^{\prime \prime}z^{\prime \prime}}\)

 

\(\Psi = [(40.65)(43.08)(-13.72)-(40.65)(-10.60)^2 – (43.08)(28.95)^2 -(-13.72)(1.113)^2 +2(1.113)(28.95)(-10.60)](Ksi)^3\)

 

\(\Psi= -65375(Ksi)^3\)

As expected, the third stress invariant is independent of coordinate system.
