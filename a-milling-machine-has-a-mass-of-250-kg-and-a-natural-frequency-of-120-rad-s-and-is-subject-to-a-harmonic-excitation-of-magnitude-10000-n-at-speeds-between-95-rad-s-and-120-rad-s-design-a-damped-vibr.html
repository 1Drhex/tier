Guest Question.

A milling machine has a mass of 250 kg and a natural frequency of 120 rad/s and is subject to a harmonic excitation of magnitude 10,000 N at speeds between 95 rad/s and 120 rad/s. Design a damped vibration absorber of mass 50 kg such that the steady-state amplitude is no greater than 15 mm at all operating speeds.

Admin
Solution

The mass ratio is

\(\mu =\frac{50kg}{250kg}=0.2 \) (a)

Since a wide operating range is required, the optimum absorber design is tried. From Equations (6.108) and (6.109),

\(q=\frac{1}{1.2}=0.833 \) \(\zeta =\sqrt{\frac{3(0.2)}{8(1.2)} }=0.25 \) (b)

\(q=\frac{1}{1+\mu } \) (6.108)

\(\zeta _{opt}=\sqrt{\frac{3\mu }{8(1+\mu )} } \) (6.109)

The steady-state amplitude at any operating speed for this absorber design is calculated by Equations (6.100) and (6.101). The results are used to generate the frequency response curve of Figure 6.26.

The fixed-points are calculated from Equation (6.106) as

\(r_{1}=\sqrt{\frac{1+(1+0.2)(0.833)^{2}\pm \sqrt{1-2(0.833)^{2}+(1+0.2)^{2}(0.833)^{4}} }{2+0.2} } \)

\( =0.7629,1.0414\) (c)

\(\frac{k_{1}X_{1}}{F_{0}}=M_{1d}(r_{1},q,\mu ,\zeta ) \)

\(-\sqrt{\frac{(2\zeta r_{1}q)^{2}+(r^{2}_{1}-q^{2})^{2}}{\left\{r^{4}_{1}-\left[1+(1+\mu )q^{2}r^{2}_{1}\right] +q^{2}\right\}^{2}+(2\zeta r_{1}q)^{2} \left[1-r^{2}_{1}(1+\mu )\right]^{2} } } \) (6.100)

 

\(\frac{k_{1}X_{2}}{F_{0}}=M_{2d}(r-{1,q,\mu ,\zeta }) \)

\( =\sqrt{\frac{q^{4}+(2\zeta q)^{2}}{\left\{r^{4}_{1}-\left[1+(1+\mu )q^{2}r^{2}_{1}\right] +q^{2}\right\}^{2}+(2\zeta r_{1}q)^{2}\left[1-r^{2}_{1}(1+\mu )\right]^{2} } } \) (6.101)

 

\( r_{1}=\sqrt{\frac{1+(1+\mu )q^{2}\pm \sqrt{1-2q^{2}+(1+\mu )^{2}q^{4}} }{2+\mu } } \) (6.106)

which leads to \(\omega =91.5 rad/s, 125.0 rad/s \).

Since the extremes of the operating range lie between the peaks and the steady-state amplitudes at the extremes are

\(X(\omega =95 rad/s) =10.1mm\)
\( X(\omega =120 rad/s)=12.7mm \) (d)

and both are less than 15 mm, the optimum design is acceptable. The absorber stiffness and damping ratio are calculated as

\( k_{2}=m_{2}\omega ^{2}_{22}-\mu q^{2}k_{1}=(0.2)(0.833)^{2}(3.6\times10^{6}N/m)= 5.08 \times 10^{5}N/m\) (e)

\( c=2\zeta \sqrt{k_{2}m_{2}}=2500 N\cdot/m \) (f)
