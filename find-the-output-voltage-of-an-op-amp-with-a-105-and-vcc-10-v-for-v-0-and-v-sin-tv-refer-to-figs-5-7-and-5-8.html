Guest Question.

Find the output voltage of an op amp with \(A=10^{5} \text { and } V_{c c}=10 V \text { for } v^{-}=0 \text { and } v^{+}=\sin t( V )\). Refer to Figs. 5-7 and 5-8.

Admin
Solution

Because of high gain, saturation occurs quickly at

\(\left|v_{2}\right|=10^{5}\left|v_{d}\right|=10 V \quad \text { or } \quad\left|v_{d}\right|=10^{-4} V\)

We may ignore the linear interval and write

\(v_{2}= \begin{cases}+10 V & v_{d}>0 \\ -10 V & v_{d}<0\end{cases}\)

 

where \(v_{d}=v^{+}-v^{-}=\sin t( V )\). One cycle of the output is given by

\(v_{2}= \begin{cases}+10 V & 0<t<\pi \\ -10 V & \pi<t<2 \pi\end{cases}\)

For a more exact v2, we use the transfer characteristic of the op amp in Fig. 5-7.

\(v_{2}=\left\{\begin{array}{rr}-10 & v_{d}<-10^{-4} V \\10^{5} v_{d} & -10^{-4}<v_{d}<10^{-4} V \\+10 & v_{d}>10^{-4} V\end{array}\right.\)

 

Saturation begins at \(\left|v_{d}\right|=|\sin t|=10^{-4} V\). Since this is a very small range, we may replace sin t by t. The output v2 is then given by

\(\begin{array}{lr}v_{2}=10^{5} t & -10^{-4}<t<10^{-4} s \\v_{2}=10 & 10^{-4}<t<\pi-10^{-4} s \\v_{2}=-10^{5}(t-\pi) & \pi-10^{-4}<t<\pi+10^{-4} s \\v_{2}=-10 & \pi+10^{-4}<t<2 \pi-10^{-4} s\end{array}\)

To appreciate the insignificance of error in ignoring the linear range, note that during one period of 2π s the interval of linear operation is only 4 × 10-4 s, which gives a ratio of 64 × 10-6.
