Guest Question.

Locate the center of mass of the trapezoidal parallelepiped. Then determine the moments and products of inertia a corresponding to centroidal coordinate system xyz whose axes are parallel to XYZ defined in the sketch.

Admin
Solution

This is an illustration of the procedures for evaluating the inertia properties of a body that is a composite of basic shapes. The decomposition we use is a rectangular parallelepiped and a rectangular prism, both of whose properties are described in the Appendix. We begin with a sketch that shows the location of the center of mass for each constituent shape.

The first moment of mass is used to locate the center of mass. We may perform this calculation in one step by writing the positions of the respective centers of mass vectorially. The masses are

 

\(m_{1}=\rho(1.5)(2)(1.2), \quad m_{2}=\rho \frac{1}{2}(1.5)(2)(1.2), \quad m=m_{1}+m_{2}=5.4 \rho.\)

 

Relative to \(X Y Z\) the centers of mass are located at

 

\(\bar{r}_{G 1 / O}=0.75 \bar{I}+1 \bar{J}+0.6 \bar{k}, \quad \bar{r}_{G 2 / O}=2 \bar{I}+1 \bar{J}+0.4 \bar{k} \mathrm{~m}. \)

 

The corresponding moment of mass is

 

\(\begin{array}{c} m \bar{r}_{G / O}=m_{1} \bar{r}_{G 1 / O}+m_{2} \bar{r}_{G 2 / O}, \\\\ 5.4 \rho \bar{r}_{G / O}=3.6 \rho(0.75 \bar{I}+1 \bar{J}+0.6 \bar{k})+1.8 \rho(2 \bar{I}+1 \bar{J}+0.4 \bar{K}). \end{array}\)

from which we find that

 

\(\bar{r}_{G / O}=1.1667 \bar{I}+1 \bar{J}+0.5333 \bar{K} m.\)

 

Obviously we could have identified the \(Y\) coordinate of the center of mass by symmetry.

 

Let \(x y z\) be the coordinate system that is parallel to \(X Y Z\) with origin at point \(G\). The \(x z\) plane cuts the body in half, so it is a plane of symmetry. Because \(y\) is the axis perpendicular to this plane, we have

 

\(I_{x y}=I_{y z}=0.\)

 

Thus we need to evaluate each moment of inertia, as well as \(I_{x z}.\)

 

For each basic shape let \(x_{j} y_{j} z_{j}\) denote the centroidal axis that is aligned parallel to \(X Y Z\). For body 1 we find from the Appendix that

 

\(\begin{array}{c}\left(I_{x_{1} x_{1}}\right)_{1}=\frac{1}{12} m_{1}\left(2^{2}+1.2^{2}\right)=1.6320 \rho, \\\\ \left(I_{y_{1} y_{1}}\right)_{1}=\frac{1}{12} m_{1}\left(1.5^{2}+1.2^{2}\right)=1.1070 \rho, \\\\ \left(I_{z_{1} z_{1}}\right)_{1}=\frac{1}{12} m_{1}\left(1.5^{2}+2^{2}\right)=1.8750 \rho, \\\\ \left(I_{x_{1} z_{1}}\right)_{1}=0. \end{array}\)

 

The coordinates of point \(G\) with respect to \(x_{1} y_{1} z_{1}\) are the components of the position of the relative position vector,

 

\(\bar{r}_{G / G 1}=\bar{r}_{G / O}-\bar{r}_{G 1 / O}=0.4167 \bar{I}-0.0667 \bar{K}.\)

 

The parallel axis transformation for shape 1 therefore is

 

\(\begin{array}{c}\left(I_{x x}\right)_{1}=\left(I_{x_{1} x_{1}}\right)_{1}+m_{1}(-0.0667)^{2}=1.6480 \rho, \\\\ \left(I_{y y}\right)_{1}=\left(I_{y_{1} y_{1}}\right)_{1}+m_{1}\left[(0.4167)^{2}+(-0.0667)^{2}\right]=1.7481 \rho, \\\\ \left(I_{z z}\right)_{1}=\left(I_{z_{1} z_{1}}\right)_{1}+m_{1}(0.4167)^{2}=2.500 \rho, \\\\ \left(I_{x z}\right)_{1}=\left(I_{x_{1} z_{1}}\right)_{1}+m_{1}(0.4167)(-0.0667)=-0.100 \rho.\end{array}\)

For shape 2 care must be taken to properly permute the axis labels from those depicted in the Appendix. The centroidal inertia properties of shape 2 are thereby found to be

 

\(\begin{array}{c} \left(I_{x_{2} x_{2}}\right)_{2}=\frac{1}{36} m_{2}\left[3\left(2^{2}\right)+2\left(1.2^{2}\right)\right]=0.7440 \rho, \\\\ \left(I_{y_{2} y_{2}}\right)_{2}=\frac{1}{18} m_{2}\left(1.5^{2}+1.2^{2}\right)=0.3690 \rho, \\\\ \left(I_{z_{2} z 2}\right)_{2}=\frac{1}{36} m_{2}\left[3\left(2^{2}\right)+2\left(1.5^{2}\right)\right]=0.8250 \rho, \\\\ \left(I_{x_{2} z_{2}}\right)_{1}=-\frac{1}{36} m_{2}(1.5)(1.2)=-0.0900 \rho. \end{array}\)

The position vector for point \(G\) relative to the centroid of shape 2 is

 

\(\bar{r}_{G / G 2}=\bar{r}_{G / O}-\bar{r}_{G 2 / O}=-0.8333 \bar{I}+0.1333 \bar{K}.\)

The corresponding parallel axis transformation is

 

\(\begin{array}{c} \left(I_{x x}\right)_{2}=\left(I_{x_{2} x_{2}}\right)_{2}+m_{2}(0.1333)^{2}=0.7760 \rho, \\\\ \left(I_{y y}\right)_{2}=\left(I_{y_{2} y_{2}}\right)_{2}+m_{2}\left[(-0.8333)^{2}+(0.1333)^{2}\right]=1.6510 \rho,\\\\ \left(I_{z z}\right)_{2}=\left(I_{z_{1} z_{1}}\right)_{2}+m_{2}(-0.8333)^{2}=2.0750 \rho, \\\\ \left(I_{x z}\right)_{2}=\left(I_{x_{2} z_{2}}\right)_{2}+m_{2}(-0.8333)(0.1333)=-0.2900 \rho. \end{array}\)

Now that the inertia properties of each shape are known with respect to \(x y z\) their contributions may be added. Thus,

 

\(\begin{aligned}I_{x x} &=\left(I_{x x}\right)_{1}+\left(I_{x x}\right)_{2}=2.4240 \rho, \\\\ I_{y y} &=\left(I_{y y}\right)_{1}+\left(I_{y y}\right)_{2}=3.3990 \rho, \\\\ I_{z z} &=\left(I_{z z}\right)_{1}+\left(I_{z z}\right)_{2}=4.5750 \rho, \\\\ I_{x z} &=\left(I_{x z}\right)_{1}+\left(I_{x z}\right)_{2}=-0.3900 \rho. \end{aligned}\)
