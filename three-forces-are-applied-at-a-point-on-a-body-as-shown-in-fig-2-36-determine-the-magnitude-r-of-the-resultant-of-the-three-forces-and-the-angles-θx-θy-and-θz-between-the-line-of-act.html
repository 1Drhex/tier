Guest Question.

Three forces are applied at a point on a body as shown in Fig. 2-36. Determine the magnitude R of the resultant of the three forces and the angles \(θ_x, θ_y\), and \(θ_z\) between the line of action of the resultant and the positive x-, y-, and z-coordinate axes.

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION (USING SCALAR COMPONENTS)
The magnitude R of the resultant will first be determined by using the rectangular components \(F_x, F_y\), and \(F_z\) of each of the forces. Thus

\(F_{1x}=25cos26^\circ cos120^\circ =-11.235kN\)

 

\(F_{1y}=25cos26^\circ sin120^\circ =+19.459kN\)

 

\(F_{1z}=25sin26^\circ =+10.959kN\)

 

\(F_{2x}=10cos60^\circ cos(-60^\circ) =+2.500kN\)

 

\(F_{2y}=10cos60^\circ sin(-60^\circ) =-4.330kN\)

 

\(F_{2z}=10sin60^\circ =+8.660kN\)

 

\(F_{3x}=15cos16^\circ cos50^\circ =+9.268kN\)

 

\(F_{3y}=15cos16^\circ sin50^\circ =+11.046kN\)

 

\(F_{3z}=15sin16^\circ =+4.135kN\)

Once the rectangular components of the forces are known, the components \(R_x, R_y\), and \(R_z\) of the resultant are obtained from the expressions

\(R_x=\sum{F_x}=F_{1x}+F_{2x}+F_{3x}\)

 

\(=-11.235+2.500+9.268=+0.533kN\)

 

\(R_y=\sum{F_y}=F_{1y}+F_{2y}+F_{3y}\)

 

\(=+19.459-4.330+11.046=+26.175kN\)

 

\(R_z=\sum{F_z}=F_{1z}+F_{2z}+F_{3z}\)

 

\(=+10.959+8.660+4.135=+23.754kN\)

The magnitude R of the resultant is

\(R=\sqrt{R_x^2+R_y^2+R_z^2} \)

 

\(R=\sqrt{(0.533)^2+(26.175)^2+(23.754)^2}=35.35\cong 35.4kN \)

The angles \(θ_x, θ_y\), and \( θ_z\) are obtained from the expressions

\(\theta _x=cos^{-1}\frac{R_x}{R}=cos^{-1}\frac{+0.533}{35.35}=89.1^\circ \)

 

\(\theta _y=cos^{-1}\frac{R_y}{R}=cos^{-1}\frac{+26.175}{35.35}=42.2^\circ \)

 

\(\theta _z=cos^{-1}\frac{R_z}{R}=cos^{-1}\frac{+23.754}{35.35}=47.8^\circ \)

SOLUTION (USING VECTOR NOTATION)
The solution can also be obtained using vector notation. Unit vectors \(e_1, e_2\), and \(e_3\), along the lines of action of forces \(F_1, F_2\), and \(F_3\), respectively, are

\(e_1=(cos26^\circ cos120^\circ )i+(cos26^\circ cos30^\circ )j+(cos64^\circ )k\)

 

\(=-0.4494i+0.7784j+0.4384k\)

 

\(e_2=(cos60^\circ cos60^\circ )i+(cos60^\circ cos150^\circ )j+(cos30^\circ )k\)

 

\(=0.2500i-0.4330j+0.8660k\)

 

\(e_3=(cos16^\circ cos50^\circ )i+(cos16^\circ cos40^\circ )j+(cos74^\circ )k\)

 

\(=0.6179i+0.7364j+0.2756k\)

The forces are then written in Cartesian vector form as

\(F_1=F_1e_1=25(-0.4494i+0.7784j+0.4384k)\)

 

\(=-11.235i+19.460j+10.960k\) kN

 

\(F_2=F_2e_2=10(0.2500i+0.4330j+0.8660k)\)

 

\(=2.500i-4.330j+8.660k\) kN

 

\(F_3=F_3e_3=15(0.6179i+0.7364j+0.2756k)\)

 

\(=9.269i+11.046j+4.134k\) kN

The resultant R of the three forces is

\(R=F_1+F_2+F_3=\sum{F_xi}+\sum{F_yj}+\sum{F_zk} \)

 

\(=0.533i+26.175j+23.754k\) kN

Once the scalar components of the resultant are determined, the magnitude of the resultant and the direction angles are determined as in the first part of the example.
