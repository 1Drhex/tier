Guest Question.

A long, steel cylinder having a diameter of 5 cm has an initial uniform temperature of \(250^{\circ}C\) and is suddenly exposed to a convection environment at \(80^{\circ}C \) with \(h = 500 W/m^{2} ·^{\circ}C\). Calculate the temperature at a radius of 1.2 cm after a time of 1 min and the heat lost per unit length during this period. Take the properties of steel as \(\rho = 7800 kg/m^{3}, c = 0.48 kJ/kg · ^{\circ}C, \) and \(k = 35 W/m · ^{\circ}C\).

Admin
Solution

We first calculate the thermal diffusivity as

\( \alpha = \frac{k}{\rho c} =\frac{ 35}{(7800)(480)}= 9.35 × 10^{−6} m^{2}/s\)

and the Biot number as

\( Bi = \frac{hr0}{k} = \frac{(500)(0.025)}{35} = 0.357\)

From Table C-2

\( A_{B} = 0.8064 C_{B} = 1.0837\)

From Equation \(\frac{\theta_{ 0}}{\theta _{i}}≈ C_{B} exp \left(−A^{2}_{ B}\frac{\alpha \tau }{s^{2}} \right) \)

\(\frac{\theta_{ 0}}{\theta _{i}}= (1.0837) exp\left[\frac{−(0.8064)^{2}(9.35 × 10^{−6})(60)}{(0.025)^{2}} \right]\)

= 0.6045

For the off-centerline temperature we must use Equation \(\frac{\theta}{\theta _{0}}= J_{0} \left(\frac{A_{B}r }{r_{0}} \right)\) to obtain

\( \frac{\theta}{\theta _{0}}= J_{0} \left[ \frac{(0.8064)(0.012) }{0.025}\right] = J_{0}(0.387) = 0.9623 \)

Then

\( \frac{\theta}{\theta _{i}}=(0.6045)(0.9623) = 0.5817 \)

and

\(T = (0.5817)(250 − 80) + 80 = 178.9^{\circ}C\)

To obtain the heat loss we employ Equation \(\frac{Q}{Q_{0}}= 1-\left(\frac{2\theta _{0}}{A_{B}\theta _{i}} \right) J_{1}(A_{B})\)

\( \frac{Q}{Q_{0}}= 1-\left[\frac{(2)(0.6045)}{0.8064} \right] J_{1}(0.8064) \)

From Table C-1, \(J_{1}(0.8064) = 0.37112\) so that

\(\frac{Q}{Q_{0}}= 0.4436\)

We have

\(Q_{0} = \rho cV θ_{i} = (7800)(480)π(0.025)^{2}(250 − 80) = 1.25 × 10^{6} J\)

so that

\(Q = (0.4436)(1.25 × 10^{6}) = 5.54 × 10^{5} J/m length\)
