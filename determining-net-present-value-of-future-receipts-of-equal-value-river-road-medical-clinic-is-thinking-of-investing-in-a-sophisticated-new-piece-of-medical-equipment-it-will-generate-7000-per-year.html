Guest Question.

DETERMINING NET PRESENT VALUE OF FUTURE RECEIPTS OF EQUAL VALUE. River Road Medical Clinic is thinking of investing in a sophisticated new piece of medical equipment. It will generate $7,000 per year in receipts for 5 years.
APPROACH \(\blacktriangleright\) Determine the present value of this cash flow; assume an interest rate of 6%.

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION \(\blacktriangleright\) The factor from Table S7.3 (4.212) is obtained by finding that value when the interest rate is 6% and the number of years is 5 (alternatively using the Excel formula =-PV (.06,5,1)):
S = RX = $7,000(4.212) = $29,484

INSIGHT \(\blacktriangleright\) There is another way of looking at this example. If you went to a bank and took a loan for $29,484 today, your payments would be $7,000 per year for 5 years if the bank used an interest rate of 6% compounded yearly. Thus, $29,484 is the present value.

LEARNING EXERCISE \(\blacktriangleright\) If the interest rate is 8%, what is the present value? [Answer: $27,951.]
