Guest Question.

Design a rocker arm for the exhaust valve of a four-stroke engine using the following data: Effective length of each arm = 180 mm
Angle between two arms = 135°
Diameter of valve head = 75 mm
Lift of valve = 25 mm
Mass of valve = 0.5 kg
Engine speed = 600 rpm
Back pressure when the exhaust valve opens = 0.4 MPa
Maximum suction pressure = 0.02 MPa below atmosphere
The valve opens 33° before the outer dead centre and closes 1º after the inner dead centre. The motion of the valve is SHM without dwell in the fully opened condition. Assume suitable data and state the assumptions you make.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } a=180 mm \quad \theta=135^{\circ} \quad d_{v}=75 mm \).

\( m=0.5 kg \quad p_{c}=0.4 MPa =0.4 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( p_{s}=0.02 MPa =0.02 N / mm ^{2} \quad h=25 mm \).

N = 600 rpm

Step I Total force on rocker arm

\( \text { The gas load } P_{g} \text { is given by, } \).

\( P_{g}=\left(\frac{\pi d_{v}^{2}}{4}\right) p_{c}=\left(\frac{\pi(75)^{2}}{4}\right)(0.4) \).

= 1767.15 N (a).

\( \text { The initial spring force } P_{i} \text { is given by, } \)

\( P_{i}=\left(\frac{\pi d_{v}^{2}}{4}\right) p_{s}=\left(\frac{\pi(75)^{2}}{4}\right)(0.02) \).

= 88.36 N (b)
The acceleration of the valve is calculated by using the following steps:
(i) Speed of camshaft
For a four-stroke engine,

\( \text { speed of camshaft }=\frac{1}{2} \text { (speed of crankshaft) } \)

\( =\frac{N}{2}=\frac{600}{2}=300 rpm \).

(ii) Angle turned by camshaft per second
Since, 1 revolution = 360°
Angle turned by camshaft per second = (Number of revolutions/s)(360°)

\( =\left(\frac{300}{60}\right)(360)=1800^{\circ} \text { per } s \).

(iii) Total crankshaft angle when the valve is open = 33 + 180 + 1 = 214°
(iv) Total angle of cam action
For a four-stroke engine,

\( \text { Angle of camshaft }=\frac{1}{2} \text { (angle of crankshaft) } \)

\( =\frac{1}{2}(214)=107^{\circ} \).

(v) Time taken by valve to open and close Since, 1800° of cam rotation = 1 second
Time taken by the valve to open

\( =\frac{\text { Angle of cam action }}{\text { Angle turned by camshaft per second }} \).

\( =\frac{107}{1800}=0.059 s \).

\( \text { (vi) } \omega=\frac{2 \pi}{t}=\left(\frac{2 \pi}{0.059}\right) \).

\( \text { (vii) } r=\frac{h}{2}=\frac{25}{2}=12.5 mm =\left(12.5 \times 10^{-3}\right) m \).

\( \text { (vii) } \alpha=\omega^{2} r=\left(\frac{2 \pi}{0.059}\right)^{2}\left(12.5 \times 10^{-3}\right) \).

\( =141.76 m / s ^{2} \).

\( \text { The inertia force } P_{a} \text { is given by, } \)

\( P_{a}=m \alpha=0.5(141.76)=70.88 N \) (c)

\( \text { Adding (a), (b) and (c), the total force }\left(P_{e}\right) \text { on the } \) rocker arm of the exhaust valve is given by,

\( P_{e}=P_{g}+P_{a}+P_{i}=1767.15+70.88+88.36 \).

= 1926.39 N
Assumption 1 The permissible bending stress is 70 N/mm².
Assumption 2 The permissible shear stress is 42 N/mm².
Assumption 3 The permissible compressive stress is 50 N/mm²2.
Assumption 4 The permissible bearing pressure is 5 MPa.

Step II Design of fulcrum pin
The reaction at the fulcrum pin is given by,

\( R_{f}=\sqrt{\left(P_{e}\right)^{2}+\left(P_{c}\right)^{2}-2 P_{e} P_{c} \cos \theta} \).

When arms are equal,

\( a=b \quad P_{e}=P_{c}=1926.39 N \quad \text { and } \quad \theta=135^{\circ} \).

\( R_{f}=\sqrt{(1926.39)^{2}+(1926.39)^{2}-2(1926.39)(1926.39) \cos \left(135^{\circ}\right)} \).

\( =1926.39 \sqrt{1+1-2 \cos (135)}=3559.5 N \).

Suppose,

\( d_{1}=\text { diameter of fulcrum } \operatorname{pin}( mm ) \).

\( l_{1}=\text { length of fulcrum pin }( mm ) \).

Considering the bearing at the fulcrum pin,

\( p_{b}=\frac{R_{f}}{d_{1} l_{1}} \quad\left(p_{b}=5 N / mm ^{2}\right) \)

The (l/d) ratio for fulcrum pin is assumed as 1.25.
Therefore,

\( 5=\frac{3559.5}{d_{1}\left(1.25 d_{1}\right)} \quad d_{1}^{2}=569.52 \)

\( d_{1}=23.86 \text { or } 25 mm \).

\( l_{1}=1.25 d_{1}=1.25(25)=31.25 \text { or } 32 mm \).

The pin should be checked for double shear stress. The shear stress is given by,

\( \tau=\frac{R_{f}}{2\left(\frac{\pi d_{1}^{2}}{4}\right)}=\frac{3559.5}{2\left(\frac{\pi(25)^{2}}{4}\right)} \).

\( =3.63 N / mm ^{2} \quad \tau<42 N / mm ^{2} \).

The outside diameter of the boss of the rocker arm at the fulcrum pin is assumed as twice the diameter of the fulcrum pin.
Outside diameter of the boss at the fulcrum pin = \( D_{1}=2 d_{1}=2(25)=50 mm \).

A phosphor-bronze bush of 3-mm thickness is press fitted in the pinhole of the rocker arm.
Inside diameter of hole at the boss

\( =d_{1}+3 \times 2=25+3 \times 2=31 mm \)

The cross-section of the rocker arm passing through the fulcrum pin is shown in Fig. 25.40. Let us check this cross-section for bending stress.

\( M_{b}=P_{e} \times a=1926.39 \times 180 \).

\( =346.75 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

\( I=\frac{(32) \times(50)^{3}}{12}-\frac{(32) \times(31)^{3}}{12}=253.89 \times 10^{3} mm ^{4} \).

\( y=\frac{50}{2}=25 mm \).

\( \sigma_{b}=\frac{M_{b} y}{I}=\frac{\left(346.75 \times 10^{3}\right)(25)}{\left(253.89 \times 10^{3}\right)}=34.14 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \sigma_{b}<70 N / mm ^{2} \).

Step III Design of forked end
The forked end carries a roller that revolves on the pin. The force acting on the roller pin is \( P_{c} \).

\( P_{c}=P_{e}=1926.39 N \).

Suppose,

\( d_{2}=\text { diameter of roller pin }( mm ) \).

\( l_{2}=\text { length of roller pin }( mm ) \).

Considering the bearing at the roller pin,

\( p_{b}=\frac{P_{c}}{d_{2} l_{2}} \quad\left(p_{b}=5 N / mm ^{2}\right) \).

\( d_{2}=17.56 \text { or } 20 mm \).

\( l_{2}=1.25 d_{2}=1.25(20)=25 mm \).

The shear stress is given by,

\( \tau=\frac{P_{c}}{2\left(\frac{\pi d_{2}^{2}}{4}\right)}=\frac{1926.39}{2\left(\frac{\pi(20)^{2}}{4}\right)}=3.07 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \tau<42 N / mm ^{2} \).

The roller pin is fixed in the eye. The dimensions of the eye are as follows:

\( \text { Thickness of each eye }=l_{2} / 2=25 / 2=12.5 mm \)

\( \text { Outer diameter of eye }=D_{2}=2 d_{2}=2(20) \) = 40 mm.

The diameter of the roller should be more than the outer diameter of the eye by at least 5 mm.
Diameter of roller = 40 + 5 = 45 or 50 mm When the roller pin is tight in the two eyes of the fork, failure occurs due to shear. On the other hand, when the pin is loose, it is subjected to bending moment. It acts as a beam as shown in Fig. 25.38. It is assumed that the load acting on the pin is uniformly distributed in the portion that is in contact with the roller. It is uniformly varying in two parts of the fork.
The bending moment is maximum at the central plane of the pin. From Eq. 25.89,

\( M_{b}=\left(\frac{5}{24}\right) P_{c} l_{2} \). (25.89)

\( M_{b}=\left(\frac{5}{24}\right) P_{c} l_{2}=\left(\frac{5}{24}\right)(1926.39)(25) \).

= 10 033.28 N-mm.

\( Z=\frac{\pi d_{2}^{3}}{32}=\frac{\pi(20)^{3}}{32}=785.4 mm ^{3} \).

\( \sigma_{b}=\frac{M_{b}}{Z}=\frac{10033.28}{785.4}=12.77 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \sigma_{b}<70 N / mm ^{2} \).

Step IV Design of cross-section of rocker arm
As shown in Fig. 25.39(a), the arm is subjected to bending moment under the action of the force \( P_{e} \).
We will consider the cross-section of arm near the boss of the fulcrum pin. The outside diameter of the boss of rocker arm at the fulcrum pin is twice the diameter of fulcrum pin \( \left(2 d_{1}\right) \). Therefore,

\( M_{b}=P_{e}\left[a-d_{1}\right]=1926.39[180-25] \).

\( =298.59 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

The proportions of cross-section for the rocker arm are shown in Fig. 25.39(b).
From Eq. 25.91,

\( I=\left(37 t^{4}\right) \). (25.91).

\( I=\left(37 t^{4}\right) \quad \text { and } \quad y=(3 t ) \).

\( \sigma_{b}=\frac{M_{b} y}{I} \quad \text { or } \quad 70=\frac{298.59 \times 10^{3}(3 t)}{\left(37 t^{4}\right)} \).

\( t^{3}=345.86 \).

t = 7.02 or 8 mm
The dimensions of cross-section as shown in
Fig. 25.39(b) are as follows:
Width of flange = 2.5t = 2.5(8) = 20 mm
Depth of section = 6t = 6(8) = 48 mm.

Step V Design of tappet
A tappet is a stud which is subjected to compressive force \( \left(P_{e}\right) \). Suppose,
\( d_{c}= \) core diameter of stud (mm).

\( \sigma_{c}=\frac{P_{e}}{\left(\frac{\pi d_{c}^{2}}{4}\right)} \quad \text { or } \quad 50=\frac{1926.39}{\left(\frac{\pi d_{c}^{2}}{4}\right)} \).

\( d_{c}^{2}=49.06 \).

\( d_{c}=7 mm \).

The nominal diameter (d) of the stud is given by,

\( d=\frac{d_{c}}{0.8}=\frac{7}{0.8}=8.75 \text { or } 10 mm \).

The diameter of the circular end of the rocker arm \( \left(D_{3}\right) \text { and its depth }\left(t_{3}\right) \) are calculated by the following relationships:

\( D_{3}=2 d=2(10)=20 mm \).

\( t_{3}=2 d=2(10)=20 mm \).
