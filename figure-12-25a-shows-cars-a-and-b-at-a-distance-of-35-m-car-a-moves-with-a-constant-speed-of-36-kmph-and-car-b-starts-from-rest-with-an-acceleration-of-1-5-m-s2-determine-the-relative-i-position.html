Guest Question.

Figure 12.25(a) shows cars A and B at a distance of 35 m. Car A moves with a constant speed of 36 kmph and car B starts from rest with an acceleration of 1.5 m/s². Determine the relative (i) position, (ii) velocity and (iii) acceleration of car B w.r.t. car A 5 seconds after car A crosses the intersection.

Admin
Solution

Car A (Uniform velocity)

\( u_A = 10 \) m/s, t = 5 seconds.

\( a_A = 0 \).

\( s_A = u_A t \).

\( s_A = 10 × 5 \).

\( s_A = 50 \) m.

Car B (Uniform acceleration)

\( u_B = 0, a_B = 1.5 \) m/s² , t = 5 seconds.

Displacement of car B

\( s = u_B t + \frac{1}{2} a_B t^2 \).

\( s = 0 + \frac{1}{2} × 1.5 × 5^2 = 18.75 \) m.

Initial distance from O is 35 m

Position of car B w.r.t. O after 5 seconds

\( s_B = 35 – 18.75 \).

\( s_B = 16.25 \) m.

\( ν_B = u_B + a_B t = 0 + 1.5 × 5 \).

\( ν_B = 7.5 \) m/s.

(i) Relative position of car B w.r.t. car A

Consider the triangle law.

By cosine rule, we have

\( s_{B/A} = \sqrt{s^2_A + s^2_B -2(s_A)(s_B) \cos 53.13°} \).

\( s_{B/A} = \sqrt{50^2 + 16.25^2 -2(50)(16.25) \cos 53.13°} \).

\( s_{B/A} = 42.3 \) m.

By sine rule, we have

\( \frac{s_A}{\sin θ} = \frac{s_{B/A}}{\sin 53.13°} \) .

\( \frac{16.25}{\sin θ} = \frac{42.3}{\sin 53.13°} \) ∴ θ = 17.9°

∴ Relative position of car B w.r.t. car A will be \( s_{B/A} = 42.3 \) m \( (17.9°\measuredangle ) \) Ans.

(ii) Relative velocity of car B w.r.t. car A

Consider the triangle law.

By cosine rule, we have

\( ν_{B/A} = \sqrt{ν^2_A + ν^2_B -2(ν_A)(ν_B) \cos 126.87°} \).

\( ν_{B/A} = \sqrt{10^2 + 7.5^2 -2(10)(7.5) \cos 126.87°} \).

\( ν_{B/A} = 15.69 \) m/s.

By sine rule, we have

\( \frac{ ν_{B/A}}{\sin 126.87°} = \frac{ν_B}{\sin \phi } \) .

\( \frac{ 15.69}{\sin 126.87°} = \frac{7.5}{\sin \phi } \) ∴ \( \phi = 22.48°\) .

∴ Relative velocity of car B w.r.t. car A will be \( ν_{B/A} = 15.69 \) m/s \( (22.48°\measuredangle ) \) Ans.

(iii) Relative acceleration of car B w.r.t. car A

\( a_{ B/A} = a_B – a_A = 1.5 – 0 \) .

\( a_{ B/A} = 1.5 \) m/s² \( (53.13°\measuredangle ) \) Ans.

Alternate Method

(i) Relative position of car B w.r.t. car A

\( s_A = 50 \) i and \( s_B = 16.25 \cos 53.13° i + 16.25 \sin 53.13° j \) .

\( s_{B/A} = s_B – s_A \).

\( s_{ B/A} = (16.25 \cos 53.13° i + 16.25 \sin 53.13° j) – 50 i = -40.25 i + 13 j \).

Magnitude

\( s_{B/A} = \sqrt{(40.25)^2 +(13)^2} \) .

\( s_{B/A} = 42.3 \) m.

Direction

\( \tan θ = \frac{13}{40.25} \) ∴ θ = 17.9°.

∴ \( s_{B/A} = 42.3 m \) (17.9°\measuredangle ) Ans.

(ii) Relative velocity of car B w.r.t. car A

\( ν_A = 10 i and ν_B = -7.5 \cos 53.13° i - 7.5 \sin 53.13° j \) .

\( ν_B = -4.5 i - 6 j \) .

\( ν_{B/A} = ν_B - ν_A \) .

\( ν_{B/A} = (-4.5 i - 6 j) - (10 i) = -14.5 i - 6 j \) .

Magnitude

\( ν_{B/A} = \sqrt{(14.5)^2 +(6)^2} \) .

\( ν_{B/A} = 15.69 \) m/s.

Direction

\( \tan \phi = \frac{6}{14.5} \) ∴ \( \phi = 22.48° \) .

∴ \( ν_{B/A} = 15.69 \) m/s \( (22.48°\measuredangle ) \) Ans.
