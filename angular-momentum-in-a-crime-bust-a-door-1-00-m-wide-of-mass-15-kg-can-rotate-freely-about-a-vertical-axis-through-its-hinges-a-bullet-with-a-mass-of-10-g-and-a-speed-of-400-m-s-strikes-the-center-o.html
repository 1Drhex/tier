ANGULAR MOMENTUM IN A CRIME BUST

A door 1.00 m wide, of mass 15 kg, can rotate freely about a vertical axis through its hinges. A bullet with a mass of 10 g and a speed of 400 m/s strikes the center of the door, in a direction perpendicular to the plane of the door, and embeds itself there. Find the door’s angular speed. Is kinetic energy conserved?

Admin
Solution

IDENTIFY and SET UP:

We consider the door and bullet as a system. There is no external torque about the hinge axis, so angular momentum about this axis is conserved. Figure 10.31 shows our sketch. The initial angular momentum is that of the bullet, as given by Eq. (10.25) (\(L=m v r \sin \phi=m v l\)). The final angular momentum is that of a rigid body composed of the door and the embedded bullet. We’ll equate these quantities and solve for the resulting angular speed ω of the door and bullet.

EXECUTE:

From Eq. (10.25) (\(L=m v r \sin \phi=m v l\)), the initial angular momentum of the bullet is

\(L=m v l=(0.010 \mathrm{~kg})(400 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s})(0.50 \mathrm{~m})=2.0 \mathrm{~kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{2} / \mathrm{s}\)

The final angular momentum is \(I \omega, \text { where } I=I_{\mathrm{door}}+I_{\mathrm{bullet}}\).

From Table 9.2, case (d), for a door of width d = 1.00 m,

\(I_{\text {door }}=\frac{M d^{2}}{3}=\frac{(15 \mathrm{~kg})(1.00 \mathrm{~m})^{2}}{3}=5.0 \mathrm{~kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{2}\)

The moment of inertia of the bullet (with respect to the axis along the hinges) is

\(I_{\text {bullet }}=m l^{2}=(0.010 \mathrm{~kg})(0.50 \mathrm{~m})^{2}=0.0025 \mathrm{~kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{2}\)

Conservation of angular momentum requires that \(m v l=I \omega\), or

\(\omega=\frac{m v l}{I}=\frac{2.0 \mathrm{~kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{2} / \mathrm{s}}{5.0 \mathrm{~kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{2}+0.0025 \mathrm{~kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{2}}=0.40 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}\)

The initial and final kinetic energies are

\(\begin{aligned}&K_{1}=\frac{1}{2} m v^{2}=\frac{1}{2}(0.010 \mathrm{~kg})(400 \mathrm{~m}/\mathrm{s})^{2}=800 \mathrm{~J} \\&K_{2}=\frac{1}{2} I \omega^{2}=\frac{1}{2}\left(5.0025\mathrm{~kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{2}\right)(0.40 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s})^{2}=0.40 \mathrm{~J}\end{aligned}\)

 

EVALUATE: The final kinetic energy is only \(\) of the initial value! We did not expect kinetic energy to be conserved: The collision is inelastic because nonconservative friction forces act during the impact. The door’s final angular speed is quite slow: At 0.40 rad/s, it takes 3.9 s to swing through 90° (π/2 radians).
