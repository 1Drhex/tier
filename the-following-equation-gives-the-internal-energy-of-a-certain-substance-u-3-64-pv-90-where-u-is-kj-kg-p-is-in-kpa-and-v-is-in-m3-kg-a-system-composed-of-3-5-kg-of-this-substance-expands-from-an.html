Guest Question.

The following equation gives the internal energy of a certain substance u = 3.64 pv + 90 where u is kJ/kg, p is in kPa and v is in \(\frac{m^{3}}{kg}\) .A system composed of 3.5 kg of this substance expands from an initial pressure of 500 kPa and a volume of 0.25 m3 to a final pressure 100 kPa in a process in which pressure and volume are related by \( pv^{1.25}\) = constant.
(i) If the expansion is quasi-static, find Q, ΔU and W for the process.
(ii) In another process, the same system expands according to the same pressure-volume relationship as in part (i), and from the same initial state to the same final state as in part (i), but the heat transfer in this case is 32 kJ. Find the work transfer for this process.
(iii) Explain the difference in work transfer in parts (i) and (ii).

Admin
Solution

Internal energy equation : \(u = 3.64 pv + 90\)

 

Initial volume, \(V_{1} = 0.25 m^{3}\)

 

Initial pressure, \(P_{1} = 500 \) kPa

 

Final pressure, \(P_{2} = 100 \) kPa

 

Process : \( pv^{1.25}\) = constant.

 

(i) Now, \(u = 3.64 pv + 90\)

 

\( Δu = u_{2}-u_{1}\)

 

\( = 3.64 (p_{2}v_{2}-p_{1}v_{1}) \) …per kg

 

\( \therefore\) \( ΔU = 3.64 (p_{2}V_{2}-p_{1}V_{1})\) …for 3.5 kg

 

Now, \( p_{1}V_{1}^{1.25}=p_{2}V_{2}^{1.25}\)

 

\( V_{2}=V_{1}\left ( \frac{p_{1}}{p_{2}} \right )^{\frac{1}{1.25}}=0.25\left ( \frac{500}{100} \right )^{\frac{1}{1.25}}\)

 

\( = 0.906 m^{3}\)

 

\(\therefore\) \( \Delta U=3.64\left ( 100\times 10^{3}\times 0.906-500\times 10^{3}\times 0.25 \right )\) J \(\left [ \because 1Pa=1\frac{N}{m^{2}} \right ]\)

 

\( = 3.64 × 10^{5} (0.906 – 5 × 0.25) \) J

 

\( = – 3.64 × 10^{5} × 0.344 J = – 125.2 \) kJ

 

i.e., \( ΔU = – 125.2 \) kJ.

 

For a quasi-static process

 

\( W=\int pdV=\frac{P_{1}V_{1}-p_{2}V_{2}}{n-1}\)

 

\(=\frac{\left ( 500\times 10^{3}\times 0.25-100\times 10^{3} \times 0.906\right )}{\left ( 1.25-1 \right )}=\frac{125-90.6}{0.25} \) kJ = \( 137.6 \) kJ

 

\( \therefore\) \( Q = ΔU + W\)

 

\( = – 125.2 + 137.6 = 12.4 \) kJ

 

i.e., \( Q = 12.4 \) kJ.

 

(ii) Here Q = 32 kJ

 

Since the end states are the same, ΔU would remain the same as in (i)

 

\( \therefore\) \( W = Q – ΔU = 32 – (– 125.2)\)

 

\( = 157.2 \) kJ.

 

(iii) The work in (ii) is not equal to ∫ p dV since the process is not quasi-static.
