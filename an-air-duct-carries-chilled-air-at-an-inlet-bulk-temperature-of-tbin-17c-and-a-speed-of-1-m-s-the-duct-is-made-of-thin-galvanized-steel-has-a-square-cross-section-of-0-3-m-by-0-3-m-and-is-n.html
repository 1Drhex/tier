Guest Question.

An air duct carries chilled air at an inlet bulk temperature of \(T_{b_{in}}\) = 17°C and a speed of 1 m/s. The duct is made of thin galvanized steel, has a square cross-section of 0.3 m by 0.3 m, and is not insulated. A length of the duct 15 m long runs outdoors through warm air at \(T_{∞}\) = 37°C. The heat transfer coefficient on the outside surface, due to natural convection and thermal radiation, is 5 W/m²K. Find the bulk temperature change of the air over this length.

Admin
Solution

The hydraulic diameter, from eqn. (7.61 a)

\(D_{h}=\frac{4ab}{2a+2b}=\frac{2ab}{a+b}\)

with a = b, is simply

\(D_{h}=a=0.3 m\)

Using properties of air at the inlet temperature (290 K), the Reynolds number is

\(Re_{D_{h}}=\frac{u_{av}D_{h}}{v}=\frac{(1)(0.3)}{(1.578\times 10^{-5})}=19,011\)

The Reynolds number for turbulent transition in a noncircular duct is typically approximated by the circular tube value of about 2300, so this flow is turbulent. The friction factor is obtained from eqn. (7.42)

\(f=\frac{1}{(1.82 log_{10} Re_{D}-1.64)^2}\)

 

\(f = [{(1.82 log_{10}(19,011)-1.64]^{-2}}=0.02646\)

and the Nusselt number is found with Gnielinski’s equation, (7.43)

\(Nu_{D}=\frac{(f/8)(Re_{D}-1000)Pr}{1+12.7\sqrt{f/8}(Pr^{2/3}-1) } for 2300\leq Re_{D} \leq 5\times 10^{6}\)

 

\(Nu_{D}=\frac{(0.02646/8)(19,011-1,000)(0.713)}{1+12.7\sqrt{0.02646/8}((0.713)^{2/3}-1) }=49.82\)

The heat transfer coefficient is

\(h=Nu_{D_{h}}\frac{k}{D_{h}}=\frac{(49.82)(0.02523)}{0.3}=4.371 W/m^2 K\)

The remaining problem is to find the bulk temperature change. The thin metal duct wall offers little thermal resistance, but convection resistance outside the duct must be considered. Heat travels first from the air at \(T_{∞}\) through the outside heat transfer coefficient to the duct wall, through the duct wall, and then through the inside heat transfer coefficient to the flowing air— effectively through three resistances in series from the fixed temperature \(T_{∞}\) to the rising temperature \(T_{b}\). Here, with \(A_{inside} \simeq A_{outside}\), we find U based on inside area to be

\(U=\frac{1}{A_{inside}}[\frac{1}{(hA)_{inside}}+\underbrace{R_{t_{wall}}}_{neglect}+\frac{1}{(hA)_{outside}}]^{-1}=(\frac{1}{4.371}+\frac{1}{5} )^{-1}=2.332 W/m^2K\)

We then adapt eqn. (7.59b) by replacing \(\bar{h}\) by U and \(T_{w}\) by \(T_{∞}:\)

\(\frac{T_{b_{out}}-T_{b_{in}}}{T_{\infty }-T_{b_{in}}}=1-exp\biggl(-\frac{U}{\rho u_{av}c_{p}}\frac{4L}{D_{h}} \biggr) = 1-exp[-\frac{2.332}{(1.217)(1)(1007)}\frac{4(15)}{0.3}]=0.316\)

The outlet bulk temperature is therefore

\(T_{b_{out}}=[17+(37-17)(0.3165)]^\circ C=23.3^\circ C\)
