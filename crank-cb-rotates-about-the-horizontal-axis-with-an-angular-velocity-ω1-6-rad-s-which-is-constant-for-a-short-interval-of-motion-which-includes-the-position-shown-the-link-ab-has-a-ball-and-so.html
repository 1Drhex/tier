Guest Question.

Crank CB rotates about the horizontal axis with an angular velocity \(\omega_{1}=6\) rad /s which is constant for a short interval of motion which includes the position shown. The link AB has a ball-and-socket fitting on each end and connects crank DA with CB. For the instant shown, determine the angular velocity \(\omega_{2}\) of crank DA and the angular velocity \(\omega_{n}\) of link AB.

Admin
Solution

The relative-velocity relation, Eq. 7/4, will be solved first using 1 translating reference axes attached to B. The equation is

 

\(\begin{aligned}& v _{A}= v _{B}+\omega \times r _{A / B} \\& a _{A}= a _{B}+\dot{\omega} \times r _{A / B}+\omega \times\left(\omega \times r _{A / B}\right)\end{aligned}\) (7/4)

 

\(v _{A}= v _{B}+\omega_{n} \times r _{A / B}\)

 

2 where \(\omega_{n}\) is the angular velocity of link AB taken normal to AB. The velocities of A and B are

 

\([v=r \omega] \quad v _{A}=50 \omega_{2} j \quad v _{B}=100(6) i =600 i\) mm/s

 

Also \(r _{A / B}=50 i +100 j +100 k\) mm. Substitution into the velocity relation gives

 

\(50 \omega_{2} j =600 i +\left|\begin{array}{ccc} i & j & k \\\omega_{n_{x}} & \omega_{n_{y}} & \omega_{n_{z}} \\50 & 100 & 100\end{array}\right|\)

 

Expanding the determinant and equating the coefficients of the i, j, k terms give

 

\(\begin{array}{rlr}-6 & = & +\omega_{n_{y}} & -\omega_{n_{z}} \\\omega_{2} & =-2 \omega_{n_{x}} && +\omega_{n_{z}} \\0 & = 2 \omega_{n_{x}}&-\omega_{n_{y}}\end{array}\)

 

These equations may be solved for \(\omega_{2}\), which becomes

 

\(\omega_{2}=6 rad / s\)

 

As they stand, the three equations incorporate the fact that \(\omega_{n}\) is normal to \(v _{A / B}\), but they cannot be solved until the requirement that \(\omega_{n}\) be normal to \(r _{A / B}\) is 3 included. Thus,

 

\(\left[\omega_{n} \cdot r _{A / B}=0\right] \quad 50 \omega_{n_{x}}+100 \omega_{n_{y}}+100 \omega_{n_{z}}=0\)

 

Combination with two of the three previous equations yields the solutions

 

\(\omega_{n_{x}}=-\frac{4}{3} rad / s \quad \omega_{n_{y}}=-\frac{8}{3} rad / s \quad \omega_{n_{z}}=\frac{10}{3} rad / s\)

 

Thus,

 

\(\omega_{n}=\frac{2}{3}(-2 i -4 j +5 k ) rad / s\)

 

Helpful Hints

1 We select B as the reference point since its motion can easily be determined from the given angular velocity \(\omega_{1}\) of CB.

2 The angular velocity \(\omega \) of AB is taken as a vector \(\omega_{n}\) normal to AB since any rotation of the link about its own axis AB has no influence on the behavior of the linkage.

3 The relative-velocity equation may be written as \(v _{A}- v _{B}= v _{A / B}=\omega_{n} × r _{A / B}\), which requires that \(v _{A / B}\) be perpendicular to both \(\omega_{n} \text { and } r _{A / B}\). This equation alone does not incorporate the additional requirement that \(\omega_{n}\) be perpendicular to \(r _{A / B}\). Thus, we must also satisfy \(\omega_{n} \cdot r _{A / B}=0\).
