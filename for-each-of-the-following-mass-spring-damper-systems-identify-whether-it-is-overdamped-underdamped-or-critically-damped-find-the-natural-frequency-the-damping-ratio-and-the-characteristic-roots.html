Guest Question.

For each of the following mass-spring-damper systems, identify whether it is overdamped, underdamped, or critically damped. Find the natural frequency, the damping ratio, and the characteristic roots. If the system is underdamped, find the damped natural frequency and the characteristic time for the decay envelope:

(a) \(\ddot{x} +4\dot{x}+5x =0\)

(b) \(2\ddot{x} +5\dot{x}+3x =0\)

(c) \(4\ddot{x} +4\dot{x}+x =0\)

Admin
Solution

(a) For\(\ddot{x} +4\dot{x}+6x =0\), m = 1, c = 4, and k = 6, with appropriate units. The natural frequency and the damping ratio are then

\(\omega_{n}=\sqrt{\frac{k}{m} }=\sqrt{5}=2.236 rad/s\)

\(\varsigma =\frac{c}{2\sqrt{mk} } =\frac{4}{2} =0.894\).

Since ζ < 1 the system is underdamped. The characteristic
equation is given by

\(r^{2}+4r+5=0\),

which can be determined by inspection of the differential
equation. The characteristic roots are r = −2 ± i. The
damped natural frequency is the imaginary part of the
characteristic root, \(\omega_{d}=1rad/s\), and the decay envelope has a characteristic time that is the inverse of the absolute value of the real part, or τ = 1/2 s.

(b) For \(2\ddot{x} +5\dot{x}+3x =0\), the natural frequency and

\(\omega_{n}=\sqrt{\frac{k}{m} }\sqrt{\frac{3}{2} }=1.225 rad/s\) the damping ratio are

\(\varsigma =\frac{c}{2\sqrt{mk} }=\frac{5}{2\sqrt{6} }=1.021\).

Since ζ > 1 the system is overdamped. The characteristic
equation is given by

\(2r^{2}+5r+3=0\)

giving the characteristic roots r = −1 and −1.5.
(c) For \(4\ddot{x} +4\dot{x}+x =0\), the natural frequency and the damping ratio are

\(\omega_{n}=\sqrt{\frac{k}{m} }=\frac{1}{2}=0.5 rad/s\)

\(\varsigma =\frac{c}{2\sqrt{mk} }=1\).

Since ζ = 1 the system is critically damped. The characteristic equation is

\(4r^{2}+4r+1=0\),

giving the repeated characteristic roots r = −0.5.
