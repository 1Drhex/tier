Guest Question.

DETERMINING THE MEAN ABSOLUTE DEVIATION (MAD). During the past 8 quarters, the Port of Baltimore has unloaded large quantities of grain from ships. The port’s operations manager wants to test the use of exponential smoothing to see how well the technique works in predicting tonnage unloaded. He guesses that the forecast of grain unloaded in the first quarter was 175 tons. Two values of a are to be examined: α = .10 and α = .50.
APPROACH \(\blacktriangleright\) Compare the actual data with the data we forecast (using each of the two α values) and then find the absolute deviation and MADs.

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION \(\blacktriangleright\) The following table shows the detailed calculations for α = .10 only:

QUARTER ACTUAL TONNAGE UNLOADED FORECAST WITH α = .10 FORECAST WITH α = .50
1 180 175 175
2 168 175.50 = 175.00 + .10(180 – 175) 177.50
3 159 174.75 = 175.50 + .10(168 – 175.50) 172.75
4 175 173.18 = 174.75 + .10(159 – 174.75) 165.88
5 190 173.36 = 173.18 + .10(175 – 173.18) 170.44
6 205 175.02 = 173.36 + .10(190 – 173.36) 180.22
7 180 178.02 = 175.02 + .10(205 – 175.02) 192.61
8 182 178.22 = 178.02 + .10(180 – 178.02) 186.30
9 ? 178.59 = 178.22 + .10(182 – 178.22) 184.15

Sum of absolute deviations:

\(MAD =\frac{\sum{|Deviations|}}{n}\)

INSIGHT \(\blacktriangleright\) On the basis of this comparison of the two MADs, a smoothing constant of α = .10 is preferred to α = .50 because its MAD is smaller.
LEARNING EXERCISE \(\blacktriangleright\) If the smoothing constant is changed from α = .10 to α = .20, what is the new MAD? [Answer: 10.21.]
