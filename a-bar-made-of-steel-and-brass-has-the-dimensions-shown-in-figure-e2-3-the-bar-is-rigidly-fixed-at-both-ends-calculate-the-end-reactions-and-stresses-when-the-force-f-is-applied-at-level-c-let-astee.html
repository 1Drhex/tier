Guest Question.

A bar made of steel and brass has the dimensions shown in Figure E2.3. The bar is rigidly fixed at both ends. Calculate the end reactions and stresses when the force F is applied at level C. Let \(A_{\text {steel }}=2 \times 10^{4} mm ^{2}, E_{\text {steel }}=200 GPa , A_{ br }=1 \times 10^{4} mm ^{2}, E_{ br }=100\) GPa and \(F=1 \times 10^{3}\) kN.

Admin
Solution

This is a statically indeterminate system subjected to only vertical forces. The equilibrium in the vertical direction must be first established. Due to the two fixed ends, the total elongation of the bar is zero. This observation provides the additional equation in terms of the two unknown reactions.

From the vertical equilibrium of Figure E2.3(b):

 

\(R_{1}+R_{2}-F=0\) (2.3a)

 

Since the total deformation between A and D is zero:

 

\(\Delta l_{ AB }+\Delta l_{ BC }-\Delta l_{ CD }=0\)

 

where \(\Delta l_{A B}, \Delta l_{B C} \text { and } \Delta l_{C D}\) are the respective deformations between A and B, B and C, and C and D. The negative sign before \(\Delta l_{C D}\) denotes compressive deformation between C and D. From Equation (2.5):

 

\(\Delta l=l_{1}-l=\frac{N l}{E A}\) (2.5)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\Delta l_{ AB }=\frac{R_{2} \times 2 a}{E_{\text {steel }} A_{\text {steel }}}, \\&\Delta l_{ BC }=\frac{R_{2} a}{E_{ br } A_{ br }}, \\&\Delta l_{ CD }=\frac{R_{1} a}{E_{ br } A_{ br }}\end{aligned}\)

 

Thus:

 

\(\frac{R_{2} \times 2 a}{E_{\text {steel }} A_{\text {steel }}}+\frac{R_{2} a}{E_{ br } A_{ br }}-\frac{R_{1} a}{E_{ br } A_{ br }}=0\)

 

or

 

\(\frac{2 R_{2}}{200 \times 10^{9} Pa \times 100 \times 10^{-4} m ^{2}}+\frac{R_{2}-R_{1}}{100 \times 10^{9} Pa \times 200 \times 10^{-4} m ^{2}}=0\)

 

or

 

\(3 R_{2}-R_{1}=0\) (2.3b)

 

From Equations (E2.3a) and (E2.3b):

 

\(R_{1}=\frac{3}{4} F=750 kN \quad R_{2}=\frac{1}{4} F=250 kN\)

 

Stresses in the bar:

 

Stress between A and B \(\sigma_{ AB }=\frac{R_{2}}{A_{\text {steel }}}=\frac{250 kN }{100 \times 10^{-4} m ^{2}}=25 MPa \text { (tension) }\)

 

Stress between B and C \(\sigma_{ BC }=\frac{R_{2}}{A_{ br }}=\frac{250 kN }{200 \times 10^{-4} m ^{2}}=12.5 MPa \text { (tension) }\)

 

Stress between C and D \(\sigma_{ CD }=\frac{R_{1}}{A_{ br }}=\frac{750 kN }{200 \times 10^{-4} m ^{2}}=37.5 MPa \text { (compression) }\)
