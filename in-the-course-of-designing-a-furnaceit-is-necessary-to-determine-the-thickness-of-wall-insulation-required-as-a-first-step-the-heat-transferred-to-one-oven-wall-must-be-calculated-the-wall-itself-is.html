Guest Question.

In the course of designing a furnace,it is necessary to determine the thickness of wall insulation required. As a first step, the heat transferred to one oven wall must be calculated. The wall itself is 1-ft tall by 2-ft wide. It is determined that the wall temperature is roughly a constant and equal to \(120^{\circ }\) F when the inside air temperature is \(400^{\circ }\) F. Determine the heat transferred to the wall.

Admin
Solution

Air near the wall becomes cooler and descends under the action of gravity. For no air movement, conduction is the primary mode of heat transfer.

Assumptions

1. Air properties are constant and evaluated at the film temperature of \(\left(400+120\right) /2=260^{\circ } F=720^{\circ } R.\)

2. The system is at steady state.
3. Only natural-convection heat transfer takes place.
4. Air behaves as an ideal gas.

From Appendix Table D.1 for air at \(720^{\circ } R\)

 

\(\rho =0.551lbm/ft^{3}\) \(k_{f}=0.01944 BTU/\left(hr.ft.R^{\circ } \right) \)

\(c_{p} =0.242 BTU/\left(lbm.R^{\circ } \right) \) \(\alpha =1.457ft^{2} /hr\)

\(\nu =27.88\times 10^{-5}ft^{2} /s \) Pr=0.689

 

Also, because the fluid is a gas (assumed ideal),

 

\(\beta =\frac{1}{T_{\infty } } =\frac{1}{400+460} =0.00116/R^{\circ }\)

 

The heat transferred is calculated with

\(q_{w} =h_{L}A\left(T_{w}-T_{\infty } \right)\)

 

and the convection coefficient is found with Equation 8.18. We first determine the Grash of number.

 

\(Gr_{L} =\frac{g_{x}\beta \left(T_{w}-T_{\infty } \right) L^{3} }{\nu ^{2} } =\frac{32.2\left(0.00116\right)\left(400-120\right)\left(1\right) ^{3} }{\left(27.88\times 10^{-5} \right) ^{2} } \)

 

\(Gr_{L}=1.34\times 10^{8}\)

 

The convection coefficient is found with

\(\frac{\overline{h} _{L} L}{k_{f} } =\frac{4}{3} \left\lgroup\frac{Gr_{L} }{4} \right\rgroup ^{1/4} \left\lgroup -\frac{d\theta }{d\eta } \right\rgroup \mid _{\eta=0} \)

 

Substituting gives

\(\overline{h} _{L} =\frac{0.01944}{1} \frac{4}{3}\left\lgroup\frac{1.34\times 10^{8} }{4} \right\rgroup ^{1/4} \left(0.505\right)\)

 

where the slope of the temperature profile has been evaluated from Table 8.1 at Pr = 0.72. The convection coefficient is

\(\overline{h} _{L}=1.00 BTU/\left(hr.ft^{2}.^{\circ }R \right)\)

 

This is a small value compared to the forced-convection results of the previous two chapters. The heat transferred is

\(q_{w} =1.00\left(1\right) \left(2\right) \left(400-120\right)\)

 

\(q_{w} =558 BTU/hr\)
