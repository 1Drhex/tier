CHOKED FLOW IN A CONVERGING NOZZLE

Air flows isentropically through a converging nozzle. At a section where the nozzle area is 0.013 ft\(^2\), the local pressure, temperature, and Mach number are 60 psia, 40째F, and 0.52, respectively. The back pressure is 30 psia. The Mach number at the throat, the mass flow rate, and the throat area are to be determined.

Admin
Solution

Given: Air flow through a converging nozzle at the

conditions shown:

\(\begin{aligned}M_{1} &=0.52 \\\quad\\T_{1} &=40^{\circ} \mathrm{F} \\\quad\\p_{1} &=60 \mathrm{psia} \\\quad\\A_{1} &=0.013 \mathrm{ft}^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Find:

(a) \(M_{t}\).
(b) \(\dot{m}\).
(c) \(A_{t}\).

Solution:
First we check for choking, to determine if flow is isentropic down to \(p_{b}\). To check, we evaluate the stagnation conditions.

\(p_{0}=p_{1}\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{1}^{2}\right]^{k /(k-1)}=60 \mathrm{psia}\left[1+0.2(0.52)^{2}\right]^{3.5}=72.0 \mathrm{psia}\)

 

The back pressure ratio is

 

\(\frac{p_b}{p_0}=\frac{30.0}{72.0}=0.417<0.528\)

 

so the flow is choked! For choked flow,

\(M_t=1.0\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad M_t}\)

 

The \(Ts\) diagram is

The mass flow rate may be found from conditions at section (1), using \(\dot{m}=\rho_{1} V_{1} A_{1}\).

\(\begin{aligned}V_{1} &=M_{1} c_{1}=M_{1} \sqrt{k R T_{1}} \\\quad\\&=0.52\left[1.4 \times 53.3 \frac{\mathrm{ft} \cdot \mathrm{lbf}}{\mathrm{lbm}^{\circ} \mathrm{R}} \times(460+40)^{\circ} \mathrm{R} \times 32.2 \frac{\mathrm{lbm}}{\mathrm{slug}} \times \frac{\mathrm{slug} \cdot \mathrm{ft}}{\mathrm{lbf} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}\right]^{1 / 2} \\\quad\\V_{1} &=570 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s} \\\quad\\\rho_{1} &=\frac{p_{1}}{R T_{1}}=60 \frac{\mathrm{lbf}}{\mathrm{in} .^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{lbm} \cdot \mathrm{R}}{53.3 \mathrm{ft} \cdot \mathrm{lbf}} \times \frac{1}{500^{\circ} \mathrm{R}} \times 144 \frac{\mathrm{in} \cdot{ }^{2}}{\mathrm{ft}^{2}}=0.324 \mathrm{lbm} / \mathrm{ft}^{3} \\\quad\\\dot{m} &=\rho_{1} V_{1} A_{1}=0.324 \frac{\mathrm{lbm}}{\mathrm{ft}^{3}} \times 570 \frac{\mathrm{ft}}{\mathrm{s}} \times 0.013 \mathrm{ft}^{2}=2.40 \mathrm{lbm} / \mathrm{s}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad \dot m}\end{aligned}\)

 

From Eq. 13.6,

\(\frac{A_{1}}{A^{*}}=\frac{1}{M_{1}}\left[\frac{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{1}^{2}}{\frac{k+1}{2}}\right]^{(k+1) / 2(k-1)}=\frac{1}{0.52}\left[\frac{1+0.2(0.52)^{2}}{1.2}\right]^{3.00}=1.303\)

 

For choked flow, \(A_{t}=A^{*}\). Thus,

\(\begin{aligned}&A_{t}=A^{*}=\frac{A_{1}}{1.303}=\frac{0.013 \mathrm{ft}^{2}}{1.303} \\\quad\\&A_{t}=9.98 \times 10^{-3} \mathrm{ft}^{2}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad A_t}\end{aligned}\)

 

This problem illustrates use of the isentropic equations, Eqs. 13.7, for a flow that is choked.

\(\begin{gathered}\frac{p_{0}}{p}=\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\right]^{k /(k-1)} \\\quad\\\frac{T_{0}}{T}=1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\\\quad\\\frac{\rho_{0}}{\rho}=\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\right]^{1 /(k-1)} \\\quad\\\frac{A}{A^{*}}=\frac{1}{M}\left[\frac{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}}{\frac{k+1}{2}}\right]^{(k+1) / 2(k-1)}\end{gathered}\) (13.7 a,b,c,d)

Because the flow is choked, we could also have used Eq.13.9a for \(\dot m\) (after finding \(T_0\)).

\(\dot{m}_{\text {choked }}=A_{e} p_{0} \sqrt{\frac{k}{R T_{0}}}\left(\frac{2}{k+1}\right)^{(k+1) / 2(k-1)}\) (13.9a)
