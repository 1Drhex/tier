Guest Question.

For the five-bar linkage in the posture of , Fig. 5.5, suppose that links 2 and 3 are rotating with constant angular velocities ω2 = 10 rad/s ccw and ω3 = 5 rad/s cw, respectively. Using the method of instant centers, determine the angular velocities of coupler links 4 and 5, and the velocity of coupler point P.

Admin
Solution

The five primary instant centers for the five-bar linkage, denoted I12,I24,I45,I35, and I13, are the centers of the five pin joints, and are shown in Fig. 5.8.

From the Aronhold-Kennedy theorem (Sec. 3.14), we know that secondary instant center I23, which relates the two known velocities, ω2 and ω3, must lie on the line containing instant centers I12 and I13.

Furthermore, from the angular velocity ratio theorem, Eq. (3.28), we know that the ratio of the angular velocity of link 2 to the angular velocity of link 3 can be written as

\(V _{P}=\left(x_{P}^{\prime} \hat{ i }+y_{P}^{\prime} \hat{ j }\right) \dot{\psi}\) (3.28)

 

\(\frac{\omega_{2}}{\omega_{3}}=\frac{R_{I_{23} I_{13}}}{R_{I_{23} I_{12}}}=\frac{10.0 rad / s }{-5.0 rad / s }=-2.0.\) (1)

From Sec. 3.17 we know that, if instant center I23 is located between the two absolute instant centers, I12 and I13 , then the angular velocity ratio is negative. Similarly, if instant center I23 is outside the two absolute instant centers, I12 and I13 , then the angular velocity ratio is positive. Note, however, that a sign convention is not necessary in Eq. (1), since a directed line is used for measuring the locations of the instant centers. In this example, Eq. (1) gives

\(R_{I_{23} I_{13}}=-2.0 R_{I_{23} I_{12}} .\) (2)

But we also know from Fig. 5.8, using positive distances to the right, that

\(R_{I_{23} I_{13}}=R_{I_{23} I_{12}}+R_{I_{12} I_{13}}=R_{I_{23} I_{12}}-6.0 \text { in, }\) (3)

and solving Eqs. (2) and (3) simultaneously gives

\(-2.0 R_{I_{23} I_{12}}=R_{I_{23} I_{12}}-6.0 \text { in, }\)

 

\(R_{I_{23} I_{12}}=2.0 \text { in. }\) (4)

Therefore, instant center I23 is located 2.0 in to the right of instant center I12 , as shown on the scaled drawing of the linkage in Fig. 5.9.

The remaining secondary instant centers can be obtained directly from the Aronhold-Kennedy theorem. The Kennedy circle, used to help locate the secondary instant centers, is also shown in this figure. Secondary instant center I14 , for example, must lie on the line containing instant centers I12 and I24 . Similarly, secondary instant center I15 must lie on the line containing instant centers I13 and I35 . The locations of all secondary instant centers that lie within the limits of the page are shown in Fig. 5.9. The location of instant center I34 lies outside of the page, but it is indicated by two dashed lines. Note that it is not necessary to find the locations of all secondary instant centers in order to determine the unknown angular velocities ω4 and ω5.

Choosing the upward direction along each line of centers as positive, the distances between instant centers I12 and I24 , and between instant centers I13 and I35 are known from the lengths of links 2 and 3, respectively; these are

\(R_{I_{24} I_{12}}=2.50 \text { in and } R_{I_{35} I_{13}}=4.00 \text { in. }\) (5)

Distances between other instant centers are also measured from the scaled drawing as

\(R_{I_{24} I_{14}}=-4.24 \text { in, } R_{I_{25} I_{12}}=-13.41 \text { in, } R_{I_{25} I_{15}}=-26.29 \text { in, and } R_{I_{35} I_{15}}=-3.92 \text { in. }\)

Substituting these distances into Eq. (3.28), angular velocity ratios can be written as

\(\frac{\omega_{4}}{\omega_{2}}=\frac{R_{I_{24} I_{12}}}{R_{I_{24} I_{14}}}=\frac{2.50 \text { in }}{-4.24 \text { in }}=-0.590,\) (6)

 

\(\frac{\omega_{5}}{\omega_{2}}=\frac{R_{I_{25} I_{12}}}{R_{I_{25} I_{15}}}=\frac{-13.41 in }{-26.29 in }=0.510,\) (7)

 

\(\frac{\omega_{5}}{\omega_{3}}=\frac{R_{I_{35} I_{13}}}{R_{I_{35} I_{15}}}=\frac{4.00 \text { in }}{-3.92 \text { in }}=-1.020.\) (8)

Then, substituting the angular velocity of link 2 into Eqs. (6) and (7), the angular velocities of links 4 and 5 are

\(\omega_{4}=-0.590 \omega_{2}=-0.590(10 rad / s )=-5.90 rad / s ( cw )\),

 

\(\omega_{5}=0.510 \omega_{2}=0.510(10 rad / s )=5.10 rad / s ( ccw ).\)

As a check, we can substitute the angular velocity of link 3 into Eq. (8) to verify that the angular velocity of link 5 is

\(\omega_{5}=-1.020 \omega_{3}=-1.020(-5 rad / s )=5.10 rad / s ( ccw ).\)

Using the method of instant centers, the magnitude of the velocity of point P can be written as

\(V_{P}=\omega_{4} R_{P I_{14}}.\) (9)

From the scaled drawing (Fig. 5.10), the distance from the absolute instant center of link 4 (I14 ) to point P is measured as \(R_{P I_{14}}=6.46 \text { in. }\)Substituting this value into Eq. (9), the magnitude of the velocity of point P is

\(V_{P}=(|-5.90 rad / s |)(6.46 in )=38.11 in / s.\)

The direction of the velocity of point P is measured from Fig. 5.10 as

Φ = −55.5°.

Note that these results are in good agreement with those given in .
