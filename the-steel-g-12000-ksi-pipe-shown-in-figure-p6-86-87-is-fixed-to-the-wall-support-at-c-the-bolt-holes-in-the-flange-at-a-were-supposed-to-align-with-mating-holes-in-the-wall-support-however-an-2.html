Guest Question.

The steel [G = 12,000 ksi] pipe shown in Figure P6.86/87 is fixed to the wall support at C. The bolt holes in the flange at A were supposed to align with mating holes in the wall support; however, an angular misalignment of 4° was found to exist. To connect the pipe to its supports, a temporary installation torque \( T_{B}^{\prime}\) must be applied at B to align flange A with the mating holes in the wall support. The outside diameter of the pipe is 2.875 in. and its wall thickness is 0.203 in.
(a) Determine the temporary installation torque \( T_{B}^{\prime}\) that must be applied at B to align the bolt holes at A.
(b) Determine the maximum shear stress \(\tau_{\text {initial }}\) in the pipe after the bolts are connected and the temporary installation torque at B is removed.
(c) Determine the magnitude of the maximum shear stress in segments (1) and (2) if an external torque of \(T_{B}\) = 80 kip-in. is applied at B after the bolts are connected.

Admin
Solution

Section Properties: The polar moment of inertia for the pipe is:

\(J_{1}=J_{2}=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[(2.875 in .)^{4}-(2.469 in .)^{4}\right]=3.059108 in .^{4}\)

(a) Temporary Installation Torque \(T _{B}^{\prime}\): The shaft, which is fixed to the support at C, must be rotated 4° so that the connection at A can be completed. Consequently, segment (2) must be twisted 4° (or 0.069813 rad) by the temporary torque \(T _{B}^{\prime}\). From the torque-twist relationship for shaft (2)

\(\phi_{2}=\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}\)

the temporary torque \(T _{B}^{\prime}\) is:

\(T_{B}^{\prime}=\frac{\phi_{2} J_{2} G_{2}}{L_{2}}=\frac{(0.069813 rad )\left(3.059108 in. ^{4}\right)(12,000 ksi )}{(15 ft )(12 in. / ft )}=14.24 kip \text {-in. }\)

(b) Maximum Shear Stress after Temporary Torque is Removed: After \(T_{B}^{\prime}\) is removed, the 4° angle of twist now applies to the total pipe length, i.e., 25 ft. The internal torque magnitude in the pipe due to the 4° misfit is thus:

\(T_{\text {inital }}=\frac{\phi_{\text {misfit }} J G}{\left(L_{1}+L_{2}\right)}=\frac{(0.069813 rad )\left(3.059108 in .{ }^{4}\right)(12,000 ksi )}{(25 ft )(12 in. / ft )}=8.5426 \text { kip-in. }\)

and the maximum shear stress magnitude in the pipe due to this internal torque is:

\(\tau_{\text {initial }}=\frac{T_{\text {initial }} c}{J}=\frac{(8.5426 \text { kip-in. })(2.875 in . / 2)}{3.059108 in .^{4}}=4.01 ksi\)

After Bolts Installed: After the bolts are installed, the pipe is analyzed as a statically indeterminate torsion structure.

Equilibrium:

\(\Sigma M_{x}=-T_{1}+T_{2}+T_{B}=0\) (a)

Note: By inspection, the internal torque \(T_{2}\) will end up having a negative value.

Geometry-of-Deformation Relationship: The two shafts are securely attached to fixed supports at A and C; however, in this instance, the sum of the angles of twist in the two members must equal the misfit angle. Note: If the rotation angle at A is a positive 4° (as can be inferred from the problem sketch), then the pipe must twist in a negative sense to reach a zero rotation angle at C.

\(\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}=-0.069813 rad\) (b)

Torque-Twist Relationships:

\(\phi_{1}=\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}} \quad \phi_{2}=\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}\) (c)

Compatibility Equation: Substitute the torque-twist relationships [Eqs. (c)] into the Geometry-ofdeformation relationship [Eq. (b)] to obtain the compatibility equation:

\(\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}=-0.069813 rad\) (d)

Solve the Equations: Solve Eq. (d) for \(T_{1}\):

\(\begin{aligned}T_{1} &=(-0.069813 rad ) \frac{J_{1} G_{1}}{L_{1}}-T_{2} \frac{L_{2}}{L_{1}} \frac{J_{1}}{J_{2}} \frac{G_{1}}{G_{2}} \\&=(-0.069813 rad ) \frac{\left(3.059108 in. ^{4}\right)(12,000 ksi )}{(10 ft )(12 in. / ft )}-T_{2}\left(\frac{15 ft }{10 ft }\right) \\&=-21.356600 kip – in .-1.5 T_{2}\end{aligned}\)

and substitute this result into Eq. (a) to compute the torque \(T_{2}\):

\(\begin{gathered}-T_{1}+T_{2}=-\left(-21.356600 \text { kip-in. }-1.5 T_{2}\right)+T_{2}=21.356600 \text { kip-in. }+2.5 T_{2}=-80 \text { kip-in. } \\\therefore T_{2}=\frac{-101.3566 \text { kip-in. }}{2.5}=-40.5426 \text { kip-in. }\end{gathered}\)

The torque in member (1) is therefore:

\(T_{1}=T_{2}+80 \text { kip-in. }=-40.5426 \text { kip-in. }+80 \text { kip-in. }=39.4574 \text { kip-in. }\)

(c) Maximum Shear Stress: The maximum shear stress magnitude in member (1) is:

\(\tau_{1}=\frac{T_{1} c_{1}}{J_{1}}=\frac{(39.4574 kip – in .)(2.875 in . / 2)}{3.059108 in.{ }^{4}}=18.54 ksi\)

The maximum shear stress magnitude in member (2) is:

\(\tau_{2}=\frac{T_{2} c_{2}}{J_{2}}=\frac{(40.5426 kip – in .)(2.875 in . / 2)}{3.059108 in. ^{4}}=19.05 ksi\)
