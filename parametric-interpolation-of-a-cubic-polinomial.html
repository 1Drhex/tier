Guest Question.

Parametric interpolation of a cubic polinomial.

Admin
Solution

Let us consider the polynomial \(y=x^{3}-2 x^{2}-x+4\) plotted in Figure 3.3. Such a function can represent, for instance, the geometry of a curved beam or the boundary of a curved 2D element. We will assume that the coordinates of the three points at \(x_{1}=0, x_{2}=1, \text { and } x_{3}=2\) are known.

The coordinates of the three points will be used to derive a quadratic approximation using a 3-noded 1D element. The relationship between the cartesian (x; y) coordinates and the natural coordinate Î¾ is obtained as a particular case of Eq.(3.17), i.e.

 

\(x=\hat{N}_{1}(\xi) x_{1}+\hat{N}_{2}(\xi) x_{1}+\cdots+\hat{N}_{m}(\xi) x_{m}\) (3.17)

 

\(x=\sum_{i=1}^{3} N_{i}(\xi) x_{i}=\frac{1}{2} \xi(\xi-1) x_{1}+\left(1-\xi^{2}\right) x_{2}+\frac{1}{2} \xi(\xi+1) x_{3}=1-\xi\)

 

\(y=\sum_{i=1}^{3} N_{i}(\xi) y_{i}=\frac{1}{2} \xi(\xi-1) y_{1}+\left(1-\xi^{2}\right) y_{2}+\frac{1}{2} \xi(1+\xi) y_{3}=\xi^{2}-\xi+2\)

 

Figure 3.3 shows the approximating quadratic function. Note the error with respect to the “exact” cubic function. Also note that this error is much larger outside the interval [0,2] which includes the three points selected.

The accuracy can be dramatically improved by using a cubic approximation in terms of the coordinates of four known points at \(x_{1}=0, x_{2}=2 / 3, x_{3}=4/3\), and \(x_{4}=2.0, \text { with } y\left(x_{1}\right)=4.0, y\left(x_{2}\right)=74 / 27, y\left(x_{3}\right)=40 / 27\), and \(y\left(x_{4}\right)=2.0\), respectively. A cubic 1D element is now used giving

 

\(x=\sum_{i=1}^{4} N_{i}(\xi) x_{i} \quad ; \quad y=\sum_{i=1}^{4} N_{i}(\xi) y_{i}\)

 

where \(N_{i}\) are the cubic shape function of Eq.(3.12). After some easy algebra,
the following is obtained

 

\(\begin{aligned}&N_{1}=\frac{\left(\xi-\xi_{2}\right)\left(\xi-\xi_{3}\right)\left(\xi-\xi_{4}\right)}{\left(\xi_{1}-\xi_{2}\right)\left(\xi_{1}-\xi_{3}\right)\left(\xi_{1}-\xi_{4}\right)}=-\frac{9}{16}\left(\xi^{2}-\frac{1}{9}\right)(\xi-1) \\&N_{2}=\frac{\left(\xi-\xi_{1}\right)\left(\xi-\xi_{3}\right)\left(\xi-\xi_{4}\right)}{\left(\xi_{2}-\xi_{1}\right)\left(\xi_{2}-\xi_{3}\right)\left(\xi_{2}-\xi_{4}\right)}=\frac{27}{16}\left(\xi-\frac{1}{3}\right)\left(\xi^{2}-1\right) \\&N_{3}=\frac{\left(\xi-\xi_{1}\right)\left(\xi-\xi_{2}\right)\left(\xi-\xi_{4}\right)}{\left(\xi_{3}-\xi_{1}\right)\left(\xi_{3}-\xi_{2}\right)\left(\xi_{3}-\xi_{4}\right)}=-\frac{27}{16}\left(\xi+\frac{1}{3}\right)\left(\xi^{2}-1\right) \\&N_{4}=\frac{\left(\xi-\xi_{1}\right)\left(\xi-\xi_{2}\right)\left(\xi-\xi_{3}\right)}{\left(\xi_{4}-\xi_{1}\right)\left(\xi_{4}-\xi_{2}\right)\left(\xi_{4}-\xi_{3}\right)}=\frac{9}{16}(\xi+1)\left(\xi^{2}-\frac{1}{9}\right)\end{aligned}\) (3.12)

 

\(x=1+\xi \quad ; \quad y=(1+\xi)^{3}-2(1+\xi)^{2}-(1+\xi)+4\)

 

The reader can verify that the cubic field chosen exactly approximates the original cubic function, as expected.
