Guest Question.

Multiple Steady States
The oxidation of carbon monoxide is carried out in a “fluidized” CSTR containing catalyst particles impregnated with platinum:

\(CO +\frac{1}{2} O _{2} \stackrel{ Pt }{\rightarrow} CO _{2}\) \(\space \space \space \) \(r=\frac{k_{S} K_{A} K_{B}^{1 / 2} C_{A} C_{B}^{1 / 2}}{\left(1+K_{A} C_{A}+K_{B} C_{B}^{1 / 2}\right)^{2}}\)

where \(C_{ A }\) is the concentration of carbon monoxide and \(C_{B}\) is the concentration of oxygen. When the oxygen is in excess, the rate law can be reduced to

\(r=\frac{k C_{A}}{\left(1+K C_{A}\right)^{2}}\) (E15.1.1)

Determine the region (values of feed concentration \(C_{ A0 }\) and space time \(\tau\)) where MSS exits.

Admin
Solution

A schematic of the fluidized bed reactor is shown in Fig. E15.1.1. Since oxygen is in excess, the concentration change of oxygen is negligible. Mole balance on Carbon monoxide over the entire reactor (assuming well mixed and at steady state) leads to

\(Q_{0} C_{A 0}-Q C_{A}+r_{A} V=\frac{d\left(V C_{A}\right)}{d t}=0\) (E15.1.2)

Furthermore, we assume that the volumetric rate remains constant. Eq. (E15.1.2) is reduced to

\(C_{ A 0}-C_{ A }+r_{ A } \tau=0\) (E15.1.3)

Substituting in the rate law, we obtain

\(C_{A 0}-C_{A}-k \tau \frac{C_{A}}{\left(1+K C_{A}\right)^{2}}=0\) (E15.1.4)

In Eq. (E15.1.4), we have two feed parameters that can vary, \(\tau \text { and } C_{ A 0}\). In order for MSS to exist, there exist bifurcation points in the solution for CA. That is, the derivative of Eq. (E15.1.4) with respect to \(C_{ A }\) also holds,

\(C_{A 0}-C_{A}^{*}-k \tau \frac{C_{A}^{*}}{\left(1+K C_{A}^{*}\right)^{2}}=0\) (E15.1.5)

\(-1-k \tau \frac{1+K C_{A}^{*}-2 K C_{A}^{*}}{\left(1+K C_{A}^{*}\right)^{3}}=0\) (E15.1.6)

Eqs. (E15.1.6) can be rearranged to give

\(k \tau=\frac{\left(K C_{A}^{*}+1\right)^{3}}{K C_{A}^{*}-1}\) (E15.1.7)

Substitute Eq. (E15.1.7) into Eq. (E15.1.5), we obtain

\(K C_{A 0}=K C_{A}^{*}+\frac{K C_{A}^{*}+1}{K C_{A}^{*}-1} K C_{A}^{*}=\frac{2\left(K C_{A}^{*}\right)^{2}}{K C_{A}^{*}-1}\) (E15.1.8)

Therefore, the region where MSS exists can be determined by Eqs. (E15.1.7) and (E15.1.8). Table E15.1.1 shows the values of feed parameters where bifurcation points exit (computed from Eq. E15.1.7 and (E15.1.8). Fig. E15.1.2 plotted out the bifurcation points (solid line) and mapped out the region of MSS.
