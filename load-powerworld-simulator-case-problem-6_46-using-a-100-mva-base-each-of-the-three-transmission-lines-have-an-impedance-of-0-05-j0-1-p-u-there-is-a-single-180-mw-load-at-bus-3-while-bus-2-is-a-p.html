Load PowerWorld Simulator case Problem 6_46. Using a 100 MVA base, each of the three transmission lines have an impedance of 0.05 + j0.1 p.u. There is a single 180 MW load at bus 3, while bus 2 is a PV bus with generation of 80 MW and a voltage setpoint of 1.0 p.u. Bus 1 is the system slack with a voltage setpoint of 1.0 p.u. Manually solve this case using the Newton-Raphson approach with a convergence criteria of 0.1 MVA. Show all your work. Then verify your solution by solving the case with PowerWorld Simulator.

Admin
Solution

First, convert all values to per-unit.

\(\begin{aligned}&P_{ G 2}=80 MW =0.8 p . u. \\&P_{ L 3}=180 MW =1.8 p . u.\end{aligned}\)

For convergence, \(\Delta y_{\max }=0.1 MVA =1 E -4 p . u.\)

Second, find \(Y_{\text {bus }}\).

\(Y_{\text {bus }}=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}8-j 16 & -4+j 8 & -4+j 8 \\-4+j 8 & 8-j 16 & -4+j 8 \\-4+j 8 & -4+j 8 & 8-j 16\end{array}\right]\)

To solve the case, we follow the steps outlined in section 6.6.
Iteration 0
Step 1

Start with \(x(0)=\left[\begin{array}{l}\delta_{2}(0) \\\delta_{3}(0) \\V_{3}(0)\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}0 \\0 \\1\end{array}\right]\)

\(P(x(0))=\left[\begin{array}{l}P_{2}(x(0)) \\P_{3}(x(0))\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}0 \\0\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(Q(x(0))=Q_{3}(x(0))=0\)

 

\(\Delta y(0)=\left[\begin{array}{c}P_{2}-P_{2}(x(0)) \\P_{3}-P_{3}(x(0)) \\Q_{3}-Q_{3}(x(0))\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}0.8 \\-1.8 \\0\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 2

\(\begin{array}{lr}J 1=\left[\begin{array}{rr}16 & -8 \\-8 & 16\end{array}\right] & J 2=\left[\begin{array}{c}-4 \\8\end{array}\right] \\J 3=\left[\begin{array}{ll}4 & -8\end{array}\right] \quad J 4=[16]\end{array}\)

 

\(J(0)=\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}16 & -8 & -4 \\-8 & 16 & 8 \\\hline 4 & -8 & 16\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 3

\(\begin{aligned}&J(0) \Delta x(0)=\Delta y(0) \\&{\left[\begin{array}{rrr}16 & -8 & -4 \\-8 & 16 & 8 \\4 & -8 & 16\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}\Delta \delta_{2}(0) \\\Delta \delta_{3}(0) \\\Delta V_{3}(0)\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}0.8 \\-1.8 \\0\end{array}\right]}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\Delta x(0)=\left[\begin{array}{l}-0.0083333 \\-0.094167 \\-0.045\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 4

\(x(1)=x(0)+\Delta x(0)=\left[\begin{array}{c}-0.0083333 \\-0.094167 \\0.955\end{array}\right]\)

 

Iteration 1
Step 1

\(\begin{gathered}P(x(1))=\left[\begin{array}{r}0.7825 \\-1.6861\end{array}\right] \\Q(x(1))=0.0610 \\\Delta y(1)=\left[\begin{array}{c}0.017502 \\-0.11385 \\-0.06104\end{array}\right]\end{gathered}\)

 

Step 2

\(J(1)=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}15.9057 & -7.93935 & -3.29945 \\-7.28439 & 14.5314 & 5.8744 \\4.4609 & -8.98235 & 15.3439\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 3

\(\Delta x(1)=\left[\begin{array}{c}-0.0038007 \\-0.0069372 \\-0.0069342\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 4

\(x(2)=\left[\begin{array}{c}-0.012134 \\-0.1011 \\0.94807\end{array}\right]\)

 

Iteration 2
Step 1

\(\begin{aligned}&P(x(2))=\left[\begin{array}{r}0.7999 \\-1.7990\end{array}\right] \\&Q(x(2))=6.484 E -4\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\Delta y(2)=\left[\begin{array}{cc}1.3602 & E -4 \\-1.0458 & E -4 \\-6.484 & E -4\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 2

\(J(2)=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}15.8424 & -7.89148 & -3.27336 \\-7.21758 & 14.3806 & 5.68703 \\4.45117 & -8.98958 & 15.1697\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 3

\(\Delta x(2)=\left[\begin{array}{ll}-3.7748 E -5 \\-6.412 E -5 \\-6.9664 E -5\end{array}\right]\)

 

Step 4

\(x(3)=\left[\begin{array}{c}-0.012172 \\-0.10117 \\0.948\end{array}\right]\)

 

Iteration 3
Step 1

\(\begin{aligned}&P(x(3))=\left[\begin{array}{r}0.8000 \\-1.8000\end{array}\right] \\&Q(x(3))=6.4875 E -8\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\Delta y(3)=\left[\begin{array}{rl}1.1294 & E -8 \\-9.7437 & E -8 \\-6.4875 & E -8\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(\Delta y(3)<1 E -4\)

∴ Newton-Raphson has converged in 3 iterations to

\(\begin{aligned}&V_{1}=1.0 \angle 0 \\&V_{2}=1.0 \angle-0.012172 rad =1.0 \angle-0.6974^{\circ} \\&V_{3}=0.948 \angle-0.10117 rad =0.948 \angle-5.797^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

 

Using the voltages, we can calculate the powers at buses 1 and 2 with Eqs. (6.6.2) and (6.6.3).

\(\begin{aligned}&P_{1}=1.0910 p . u .=109.10 MW \\&Q_{1}=0.0237 p . u .=2.37 Mvar \\&Q_{2}=0.1583 p . u .=15.83 Mvar\end{aligned}\)

Using PowerWorld simulator, we get

\(\begin{aligned}&P_{1}=109.1 MW \\&Q_{1}=2.4 Mvar \\&Q_{2}=15.8 Mvar \\&V_{3}=0.948 p . u.\end{aligned}\)

which agrees with our solution.
