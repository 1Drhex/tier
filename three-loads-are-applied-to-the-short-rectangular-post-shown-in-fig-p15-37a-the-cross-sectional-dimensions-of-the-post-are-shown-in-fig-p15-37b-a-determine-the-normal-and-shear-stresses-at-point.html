Guest Question.

Three loads are applied to the short rectangular post shown in Fig. P15.37a. The cross-sectional dimensions of the post are shown in Fig. P15.37b.
(a) Determine the normal and shear stresses at point H.
(b) Determine the principal stresses and maximum in-plane shear stress at point H and show the orientation of these stresses on an appropriate sketch.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&A=(120 mm )(160 mm )=19,200 mm ^{2} \\&I_{x}=\frac{(120 mm )(160 mm )^{3}}{12}=40.960 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4} \\&I_{z}=\frac{(160 mm )(120 mm )^{3}}{12}=23.040 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Equivalent forces at H:

\(\begin{aligned}&F=210 kN =210,000 N \\&V_{x}=-65 kN =-65,000 N \\&V_{z}=-95 kN =-95,000 N \\&M_{x}=-(95 kN )(150 mm )-(210 kN )(50 mm )=-24,750 kN – mm =-24.750 \times 10^{6} N – mm \\&M_{z}=(65 kN )(150 mm )=9,750 kN – mm =9.750 \times 10^{6} N – mm\end{aligned}\)

Axial stress at H due to F:

\(\sigma_{\text {axial }}=\frac{210,000 N }{19,200 mm ^{2}}=10.938 MPa ( C )\)

Shear stress at H due to \(V _{x}\):

\(\begin{aligned}&Q_{H}=(160 mm )(60 mm )(30 mm )=288,000 mm ^{3} \\&\tau_{H}=\frac{(65,000 N )\left(288,000 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(23.040 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\right)(160 mm )}=5.078 MPa\end{aligned}\)

Shear stress at H due to \(V _{z}\):

\(Q_{H}=0 mm ^{3} \quad \therefore \tau_{H}=0 MPa\)

Bending stress at H due to \(M_{x}\):

\(\sigma_{\text {bend } x}=\frac{M_{x} z}{I_{x}}=\frac{\left(24.750 \times 10^{6} N – mm \right)(80 mm )}{40.960 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}}=48.340 MPa ( T )\)

Bending stress at H due to \(M_{z}\):

\(\sigma_{\text {bend } z}=\frac{M_{z} x}{I_{z}}=\frac{\left(9.750 \times 10^{6} N – mm \right)(0 mm )}{23.040 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}}=0 MPa\)

 

Summary of stresses at H:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=0 MPa \\\sigma_{y} &=-10.938 MPa +48.340 MPa =37.402 MPa \\\tau_{x y} &=-5.078 MPa\end{aligned}\)

 

Principal stress calculations:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq. (12.12):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}}{2}\right)^{2}+\tau_{x y}^{2}}=\frac{(0)+(37.402)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(0)-(37.402)}{2}\right)^{2}+(5.078)^{2}} \\&=18.701 \pm 19.378\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1} &=38.1 MPa \text { and } \quad \sigma_{p 2}=-0.677 MPa \\\tau_{\max } &=19.38 MPa \quad(\text { maximum in-plane shear stress }) \\\sigma_{\text {avg }} &=18.70 MPa ( T ) \quad \text { (normal stress on planes of maximum in-plane shear stress) }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan 2 \theta_{p} &=\frac{\tau_{x y}}{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right) / 2}=\frac{-5.078}{[(0)-(37.402)] / 2}=\frac{-5.078}{-18.701}=0.271540 \\&\left.\therefore \theta_{p}=7.60^{\circ} \quad \text { (counterclockwise from the } x \text { axis to the direction of } \sigma_{p 2}\right)\end{aligned}\)
