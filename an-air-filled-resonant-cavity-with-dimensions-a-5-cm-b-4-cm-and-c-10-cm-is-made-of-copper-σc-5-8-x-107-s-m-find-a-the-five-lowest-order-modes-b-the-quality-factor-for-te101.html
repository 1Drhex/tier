Guest Question.

An air-filled resonant cavity with dimensions \(a=5cm, b=4cm\), and \(c=10cm\) is made of copper \((\sigma_{c}=5.8\times 10^{7} S/m)\). Find

(a) The five lowest-order modes

(b) The quality factor for \(TE_{101}\) mode

Admin
Solution

(a) The resonant frequency is given by

\(f_{r}=\frac{u^{'}}{2}\sqrt{\left[\frac{m}{a}\right]^{2}+\left[\frac{n}{b}\right]^{2}+\left[\frac{p}{c}\right]^{2}}\)

where

\(u^{'}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu\varepsilon}}=c\)

Hence

\(f_{r}=\frac{3\times10^{8}}{2}\sqrt{\left[\frac{m}{5\times10^{-2}}\right]^{2}+\left[\frac{n}{4\times10^{-2}}\right]^{2}+\left[\frac{p}{10\times10^{-2}}\right]^{2}}\)

\(=15\sqrt{0.04m^{2}+0.0625n^{2}+0.01p^{2}}GHz\)

Since \(c\gt a\gt b\) or \(1/c\lt 1/a\lt 1/b\), the lowest order mode is \(TE_{101}\). Notice that \(TM_{101}\) and \(TE_{100}\) do not exist because \(m =1, 2, 3,… , n=1, 2, 3,…\), and \(p=0, 1, 2, 3,…\) for the TM modes, and \(m=0, 1, 2,…, n=0, 1, 2,…\), and \(p=1, 2, 3,…\) for the TE modes. The resonant frequency for the \(TE_{101}\) mode is

\(f_{r_{101}}=15\sqrt{0.04+0+0.01}=3.354 GHz\)

The next higher mode is \(TE_{011}(TM_{011}\) does not exist), with

\(f_{r_{011}}=15\sqrt{0+0.0625+0.01}=4.04 GHz\)

The next mode is \(TE_{102}(TM_{102}\) does not exist), with

\(f_{r_{102}}=15\sqrt{0.04+0+0.04}=4.243 GHz\)

The next mode is \(TM_{110}(TE_{110}\) does not exist), with

\(f_{r_{110}}=15\sqrt{0.04+0.0625+0}=4.8 GHz\)

The next two modes are \(TE_{111}\) and \(TM_{111}\) (degenerate modes), with

\(f_{r_{111}}=15\sqrt{0.04+0.0625+0.01}=5.031 GHz\)

The next mode is \(TM_{103}\) with

\(f_{r_{103}}=15\sqrt{0.04+0+0.09}=5.408 GHz\)

Thus the five lowest order modes in ascending order are

\(TE_{101} (3.35 GHz) \\ TE_{011} (4.04 GHz) \\ TE_{102} (4.243 GHz) \\ TM_{110} (4.8 GHz) \\ TE_{111} or TM_{111} (5.031 GHz)\)

(b) The quality factor for \(TE_{101}\) is given by

\(Q_{TE_{101}}=\frac{(a^{2}+c^{2})abc}{\delta[2b(a^{3}+c^{3})+ac(a^{2}+c^{2})]}=\frac{(25+100)200\times10^{-2}}{\delta[8(125+1000)+50(25+100)]}\)

\(=\frac{1}{61\delta}=\frac{\sqrt{\pi f_{101}\mu _{o}\sigma_{c}}}{61}=\frac{\sqrt{\pi(3.35\times10^{9})4\pi\times10^{-7}(5.8\times10^{7})}}{61}=14,358\)
