Guest Question.

A rectangular steel tube is used as a 36 foot column. It has pinned ends, and its weak axis is braced at mid-height by a masonry infill wall as shown. Determine the column’s critical buckling load. E = 29 × 10³ ksi.

Admin
Solution

4″ × 2″ × \(\frac{1}{4}\)” rectangular tube

\(\left(A=2.59 in .^{2} ; I_{x}=4.69 in .^{4} ; r_{x}=1.35^{\prime \prime} ; I_{y}=1.54 in .^{4}; r_{y}=0.77^{\prime \prime}\right)\)

Again, the first step in the solution must involve the determination of the critical slenderness ratio.

Weak Axis

K = 0.7

KL = 0.7 × 18′ = 12.6′ = 151.2″

\(\frac{K L}{r_{y}}=\frac{151.2^{\prime \prime}}{0.77^{\prime \prime}}=196.4\)

Strong Axis

K = 1.0

KL = 1.0 × 36′ = 36′ = 432″

\(\frac{K L}{r_{x}}=\frac{432^{\prime \prime}}{1.35^{\prime \prime}}=320 \leftarrow\) Governs

Because \(\frac{K L}{r_{x}}>\frac{K L}{r_{y}} \) , buckling is more critical about the
strong axis.

\(P_{\text {critical }}=\frac{\pi^{2} E I_{x}}{\left(K L_{x}\right)^{2}}=\frac{3.14^{2}\left(29 \times 10^{3} ksi \right)\left(4.69 in .^{4}\right)}{\left(432^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2}}\)

= 7.19 k

\(f_{\text {critical }}=\frac{P_{\text {critical }}}{A}=\frac{7.19 k }{2.59 in .^{2}}=2.78 ksi\)
