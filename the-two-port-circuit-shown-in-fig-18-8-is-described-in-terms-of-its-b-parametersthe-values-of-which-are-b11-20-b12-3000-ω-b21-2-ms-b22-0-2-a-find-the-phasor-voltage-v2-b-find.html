Guest Question.

The two-port circuit shown in Fig. 18.8 is described in terms of its \(b\) parameters, the values of which are

\( b_{11}=-20,\) \( b_{12} -3000 \Omega,\)

 

\( b_{21}=-2 mS,\) \( b_{22}=-0.2.\)

a) Find the phasor voltage \( V_{2}.\)

b) Find the average power delivered to the \(5 \Omega\) load.

c) Find the average power delivered to the input port.

d) Find the load impedance for maximum average power transfer.

e) Find the maximum average power delivered to the load in \(\left(d\right) \).

Admin
Solution

a) To find \( V_{2},\) we have two choices from the entries in Table 18.2. We may choose to find \( I_{2}\) and then find \( V_{2}\) from the relationship \( V_{2}=-I_{2}Z_{L},\) or we may find the voltage gain \(V_{2}/V_{g}\) and calculate \( V_{2}\) from the gain. Let’s use the latter approach. For the b-parameter values given, we have

From Table 18.2,

\(\frac{V_{2}}{V_{g}} \)\(=\frac{\Delta bZ_{L}}{b_{12}+b_{11}Z_{g}+b_{22}Z_{L}+b_{21}Z_{g}Z_{L}}\)

 

\(=\frac{\left(-2\right) \left(5000\right) }{-3000+\left(-20\right)500+ \left(-0.2\right)5000+\left[-2\times 10^{-3}\left(500\right)\left(5000\right) \right] }\)

 

\(=\frac{10}{19} .\)

Then,

\(V_{2}\)\(=\left(\frac{10}{19} \right) 500=263.16\angle 0° V.\)

b) The average power delivered to the \(5000 \Omega\) load is

\(P_{2}\)\(=\frac{263.16^{2} }{2\left(5000\right) } =6.93 W.\)

c) To find the average power delivered to the input port, we first find the input impedance \(Z_{in}.\) From Table 18.2,

\(Z_{in}\)\(=\frac{ b_{22}Z_{L}+b_{12}}{b_{21}Z_{L} +b_{11}} \)

 

\(=\frac{\left(-0.2\right)\left(5000\right)-3000 }{-2\times 10^{-3}\left(5000\right)-20} \)

 

\(=\frac{400}{3}=133.33 \Omega.\)

Now \(I_{1}\) follows directly:

\(I_{1}\)\(=\frac{500}{500+133.33}=789.47 mA\)

The average power delivered to the input port is

\(P_{1} \)\(=\frac{0.78947^{2} }{2} \left(133.33\right)=41.55 W.\)

d) The load impedance for maximum power transfer equals the conjugate of the Thévenin impedance seen looking into port \(2\). From Table 18.2,

\(Z_{Th}=\frac{ b_{11}Z_{g}+b_{12}}{b_{21}Z_{g} +b_{22}} \)

 

\(=\frac{\left(-20\right)\left(500\right)-3000 }{\left(-2\times 10^{-3}\right)\left(500\right)-0.2} \)

 

\(=\frac{13,000}{1.2}=10,833.33 \Omega.\)

Therefore \(Z_{L}=Z^{\ast }_{Th} =10,833.33 \Omega.\)

e) To find the maximum average power delivered to \(Z_{L},\) we first find \(V_{2}\) from the voltage-gain expression \(V_{2}/V_{g}.\) When \(Z_{L}\) is \(10,833.33 \Omega,\) this gain is

\(\frac{V_{2}}{V_{g}}\)\(=0.8333.\)

Thus

\(V_{2}\)\(=\left(0.8333\right)\left(500\right)=416.67 V,\)

and

\(P_{L}\left(maximum\right)=\frac{1}{2}\frac{416.67^{2}}{10,833.33}\)

 

\(=8.01 W.\)
