Finding the Inductive Reactance and the Delay Angle of TCR

The particulars of a transmission line with a TCR, as shown in Figure 12.3a, are \(V = 220 V, f = 60 Hz, X = 1.2 Ω\), and \(P_p = 56 kW\). The maximum current of the TCR is \(I_{L(mx) }= 100 A\). Find (a) the phase-angle δ , (b) the line current I, (c) the reactive power \(Q_p\) of the shunt compensator, (d) the current through the TCR, (e) the inductance reactance \(X_L\) , and (f) the delay angle of the TCR if the \(I_L\) is 60% of the maximum current.

Admin
Solution
\(V = 220, f = 60 Hz, X = 1.2 Ω, ω = 2 πf = 377 rad/s, P_p = 56 kW, I_{L(mx)} = 100 A, k = 0.6\)

a. Using Eq. (12.8),

\(P_p=\frac{2V^2}{X}sin\frac{\delta }{2}\) (12.8)

 

\(\delta =2 sin^{-1}\left(\frac{XP_p}{2V^2} \right)=2 sin^{-1}\left(\frac{1.2\times 56\times 10^3}{2\times 220^2} \right)=887.93°\).

b. Using Eq. (12.7b),

\(I_{sm}=I_{mr}=I=\frac{4V}{X}sin\frac{\delta }{4}\) (12.7b)

 

\(I=\frac{4V}{X}sin\frac{\delta }{4}=\frac{4\times 220}{1.2}\times sin\frac{87.93}{4}=274.5 A\)

c. Using Eq. (12.10),\(Q_p=\frac{4V^2}{X}\left(1-cos\frac{\delta }{2} \right)=\frac{4\times 220^2}{1.2}\times \left(1-cos\frac{87.93}{2} \right)=45.21× 10^3 A \)

d. The current through TCR, \(I_Q=\frac{Q_p}{V}=\frac{45.21\times 10^3}{220}=205.504 A\).

e. The inductance reactance \(X_L=\frac{V}{I_L(max)}=\frac{220}{100}=2.2 \Omega\) .

f. \(I_L = kI_L(max) = 0.6 × 100 = 60 A\).

Using Eq. (12.12),

\(I_{LF}(\alpha )=\frac{V}{\omega L} \left(1-\frac{2}{\pi }\alpha -\frac{1}{\pi }sin 2\alpha \right)\) (12.12)

 

\(60=220/2.2\times \left(1-\frac{2}{\pi }\alpha -\frac{1}{\pi }sin 2\alpha \right)\) , which by using Mathcad gives the delay angle \(α= 18.64°\).
