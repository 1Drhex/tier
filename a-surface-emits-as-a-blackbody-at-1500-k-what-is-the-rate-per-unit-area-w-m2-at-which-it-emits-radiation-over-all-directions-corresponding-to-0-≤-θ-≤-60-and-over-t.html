Guest Question.

A surface emits as a blackbody at 1500 K. What is the rate per unit area \(\left( W / m ^{2}\right)\) at which it emits radiation over all directions corresponding to \(0^{\circ} \leq \theta \leq 60^{\circ}\) and over the wavelength interval \(2 \mu m \leq \lambda \leq 4 \mu m\)?

Admin
Solution

Known: Temperature of a surface that emits as a blackbody.

Find: Rate of emission per unit area over all directions between \(\theta=0^{\circ} \text { and } 60^{\circ}\) and over all wavelengths between \(\lambda=2 \text { and } 4 \mu m\).

Assumptions: Surface emits as a blackbody.

Analysis: The desired emission may be inferred from Equation 12.15, with the limits of integration restricted as follows:

 

\(E=\int_{0}^{\infty} \int_{0}^{2 \pi} \int_{0}^{\pi / 2} I_{\lambda, e}(\lambda, \theta, \phi) \cos \theta \sin \theta d \theta d \phi d \lambda\) (12.15)

 

\(\Delta E=\int_{2}^{4} \int_{0}^{2 \pi} \int_{0}^{\pi / 3} I_{\lambda, b} \cos \theta \sin \theta d \theta d \phi d \lambda\)

 

or, since a blackbody emits diffusely,

 

\(\Delta E=\int_{2}^{4} I_{\lambda, b}\left(\int_{0}^{2 \pi} \int_{0}^{\pi / 3} \cos \theta \sin \theta d \theta d \phi\right) d \lambda\)

 

\(\Delta E=\int_{2}^{4} I_{\lambda, b}\left(\left.2 \pi \frac{\sin ^{2} \theta}{2}\right|_{0} ^{\pi / 3}\right) d \lambda=0.75 \int_{2}^{4} \pi I_{\lambda, b} d \lambda\)

 

Substituting from Equation 12.16 and multiplying and dividing by \(E_{b}\), this result may be put in a form that allows for use of Table 12.2 in evaluating the spectral integration. In particular,

 

\(E_{\lambda}(\lambda)=\pi I_{\lambda, e}(\lambda)\) (12.16)

\(\Delta E=0.75 E_{b} \int_{2}^{4} \frac{E_{\lambda, b}}{E_{b}} d \lambda=0.75 E_{b}\left[F_{(0 \rightarrow 4)}-F_{(0 \rightarrow 2)}\right]\)

 

where from Table 12.2

 

\(\lambda_{1} T=2 \mu m \times 1500 K =3000 \mu m \cdot K : \quad F_{(0 \rightarrow 2)}=0.273\)

 

\(\lambda_{2} T=4 \mu m \times 1500 K =6000 \mu m \cdot K : \quad F_{(0 \rightarrow 4)}=0.738\)

 

Hence

\(\Delta E=0.75(0.738-0.273) E_{b}=0.75(0.465) E_{b}\)

 

From Equation 12.31, it then follows that

 

\(\lambda_{\max } T=C_{3}\) (12.31)

 

\(\Delta E=0.75(0.465) 5.67 \times 10^{-8} W / m ^{2} \cdot K ^{4}(1500 K )^{4}=10^{5} W / m ^{2}\)

 

Comments: The total, hemispherical emissive power is reduced by 25% and 53.5% due to the directional and spectral restrictions, respectively.
