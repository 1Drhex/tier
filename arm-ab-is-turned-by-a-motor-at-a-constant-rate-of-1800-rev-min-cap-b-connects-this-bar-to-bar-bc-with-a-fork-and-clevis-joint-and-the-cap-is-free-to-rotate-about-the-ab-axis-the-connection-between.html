Guest Question.

Arm \(A B\) is turned by a motor at a constant rate of 1800 rev/min. Cap \(B\) connects this bar to bar \(B C\) with a fork-and-clevis joint, and the cap is free to rotate about the \(A B\) axis. The connection between bar \(B C\) and collar \(C\) is a balland-socket joint. Determine the velocity and acceleration of collar \(C\) and the angular velocity and angular acceleration of bar \(B C\) when the system is in the position shown.

Admin
Solution

This example applies the general procedures to a system that is sometimes referred to as a four-bar linkage: rotating arm \(A B\), connecting rod \(B C\), slider \(C\), and the fixed guide bar for the slider. The process is readily extended to multiple links. The global coordinate system we use has a vertical \(Z\) axis, and \(Y\) is aligned with the rotation axis of arm \(A B\), with \(\bar{J}\) pointing rightward. We begin by expressing the constraints on the motion of the connection points. Point \(A\) is stationary, collar \(C\) follows a straight path parallel to the \(X\) axis at an unknown speed \(v_{C}\), and cap \(B\) is attached to both arm \(A B\) and connecting \(\operatorname{rod} C D .\) Thus we have

\(\large\begin{aligned} \bar{v}_{B} &=\bar{\omega}_{A B} \times \bar{r}_{B / A}=v_{C} \bar{I}+\bar{\omega}_{B C} \times \bar{r}_{B / C}, \\\\ \bar{a}_{B} &=\bar{\alpha}_{A B} \times \bar{r}_{B / A}+\bar{\omega}_{A B} \times\left(\bar{\omega}_{A B} \times \bar{r}_{B / A}\right) \\\\ &=\dot{v}_{C} I+\bar{\alpha}_{B C} \times \bar{r}_{B / C}+\bar{\omega}_{B C} \times\left(\bar{\omega}_{B C} \times \bar{r}_{B / C}\right).\end{aligned} (1)\)

The position vectors at the instant depicted in the diagram are

\( \large\begin{aligned} \bar{r}_{B / A} &=0.3\left(-\sin 30^{\circ} \bar{I}+\cos 30^{\circ} \bar{K}\right)=-0.15 \bar{I}+0.2598 \bar{K} \mathrm{~m}, \\\\ \bar{r}_{B / C} &=-\left[0.75^{2}-0.4^{2}-\left(0.3 \cos 30^{\circ}\right)^{2}\right]^{1 / 2} \bar{I}-0.4 \bar{J}+0.3 \cos 30^{\circ} \bar{K} \\\\ &=-0.5788 \bar{I}-0.4 \bar{J}+0.2598 \bar{K} \mathrm{~m}. \end{aligned} (2) \)

Next, we describe the constraints on angular motion. It is given that

\(\large\bar{\omega}_{A B}=1800\left(\frac{2 \pi}{60}\right)(-\bar{J}) \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}, \quad \bar{\alpha}_{A B}=\overline{0}. (3) \)

Cap \(C\) rotates at an unknown rate \(\dot{\psi}\) about axis \(A B\) relative to arm \(A B\). Relative to the cap, the fork-and-clevis joint allows bar \(B C\) only to rotate at an unknown rate \(\dot{\theta}\) about the axis of that joint’s pin. Thus we have

\(\large\bar{\omega}_{\text {cap }}=\bar{\omega}_{A B}+\dot{\psi} \bar{e}_{B / A}, \quad \bar{\omega}_{B C}=\bar{\omega}_{\text {cap }}+\dot{\theta} \bar{e}_{\text {pin }}=\bar{\omega}_{A B}+\dot{\psi} \bar{e}_{B / A}+\dot{\theta} \bar{e}_{\text {pin }}. (4) \)

To describe the angular acceleration we observe that \(\bar{e}_{B / A}\) rotates at \(\bar{\omega}_{A B}\) while \(\bar{e}_{\mathrm{pin}}\) rotates at \(\bar{\omega}_{\text {cap }} .\) Because \(\bar{\alpha}_{A B}=\overline{0}\), we have

\(\large\bar{\alpha}_{B C}=\ddot{\psi} \bar{e}_{B / A}+\dot{\psi}\left(\bar{\omega}_{A B} \times \bar{e}_{B / A}\right)+\ddot{\theta} \bar{e}_{\mathrm{pin}}+\dot{\theta}\left(\bar{\omega}_{\mathrm{cap}} \times \bar{e}_{\mathrm{pin}}\right). (5) \)

We obtain \(\bar{e}_{B / A}\) from \(\bar{r}_{B / A}\) and evaluate \(\bar{e}_{\mathrm{pin}}\) as the normal to the plane formed by \(\bar{r}_{B / A}\) and \(\bar{r}_{B / C}\), where both position vectors are given by Eqs. (2). Thus,

\(\large\begin{array}{c} \bar{e}_{B / A}=\frac{\bar{r}_{B / A}}{\left|\bar{r}_{B / A}\right|}=-0.5 \bar{I}+0.866 \bar{K}, \\\\\bar{e}_{\mathrm{pin}}=\frac{\bar{r}_{B / A} \times \bar{r}_{B / C}}{\left|\bar{r}_{B / A} \times \bar{r}_{B / C}\right|}=0.6347 \bar{I}-0.6804 \bar{J}+0.3664 \bar{K}. \end{array} (6)\)

Now that we have characterized the constraint conditions, we proceed to analyze the velocities. This will yield the unknown rotation rates, which we will need to know in order to evaluate the angular acceleration. The first of Eqs. (1) gives

\(\large\begin{aligned} \bar{v}_{B}=&(-60 \pi \bar{J}) \times(-0.15 \bar{I}+0.2598 \bar{K}) \\\\ =& v_{C} \bar{I}+[-60 \pi \bar{J}+\dot{\psi}(-0.5 \bar{I}+0.866 \bar{K})+\dot{\theta}(0.6347 \bar{I}-0.6804 \bar{J}\\\\ &+0.3664 \bar{K})] \times(-0.5788 \bar{I}-0.4 \bar{J}+0.2598 \bar{K}). \end{aligned} \)

The component equations obtained from this vector equation are

\(\large\begin{array}{c} \bar{v}_{B} \cdot \bar{I}=-48.97=v_{C}+0.3464 \dot{\psi}-0.0302 \dot{\theta}-48.97, \\\\ \bar{v}_{B} \cdot \bar{J}=0=-0.3713 \dot{\psi}-0.3770 \dot{\theta}, \\\\ \bar{v}_{B} \cdot \bar{K}=-28.27=0.200 \dot{\psi}-0.6477 \dot{\theta}-109.10 . \end{array} \)

The solution of this set of linear equations is

\(\large v_{C}=-36.28 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}, \dot{\psi}=96.46 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}, \dot{\theta}=-95.01 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}. (7) \)

The corresponding angular velocity obtained from the second of Eqs. (4) is

\(\large\bar{\omega}_{B C}=-108.53 \bar{I}-123.86 \bar{J}+48.72 \bar{K} \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}. (8) \)

We may now proceed to analyze the acceleration. We begin by using the parameters obtained thus far to form \(\bar{\alpha}_{B C}\). From the value of \(\dot{\psi}\) we know that

\(\large\bar{\omega}_{\text {cap }}=\bar{\omega}_{A B}+96.45 \bar{e}_{B / A}=-48.23 \bar{I}-188.50 \bar{J}+83.53 \bar{K}. \)

we substitute into Eq. (5) this expression, the unit vectors in Eqs. (6), and the results for \(\dot{\psi}\) and \(\dot{\theta}\), which gives

\(\large\begin{aligned} \bar{\alpha}_{B C}=& \ddot{\psi} \bar{e}_{B / A}+96.45\left(\bar{\omega}_{A B} \times \bar{e}_{B / A}\right)+\ddot{\theta} \bar{e}_{\mathrm{pin}}-95.01\left(\bar{\omega}_{\text {cap }} \times \bar{e}_{\mathrm{pin}}\right) \\\\ =&(-0.50 \ddot{\psi}+0.6347 \ddot{\theta}-14582) \bar{I}+(-0.6804 \ddot{\theta}-6716) \bar{J} \\\\ &+(0.8660 \ddot{\psi}+0.3664 \ddot{\theta}-23575) \bar{K} \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}^{2}. \end{aligned} (9)\)

Substitution of Eqs. (8) and (9), as well as the position vectors in Eqs. (2), into the second of Eqs. (1) governing the acceleration of cap \( B\) gives

\(\large\begin{aligned} \bar{a}_{B}=&(-60 \pi \bar{J}) \times[(-60 \pi \bar{J}) \times(-0.15 \bar{I}+0.2598 \bar{K})] \\\\ =& \dot{v}_{C} \bar{I}+[(-0.50 \ddot{\psi}+0.6347 \ddot{\theta}-14582) \bar{I}+(-.6804 \ddot{\theta}-6716) \bar{J}\\\\ &+(0.8660 \ddot{\psi}+0.3664 \ddot{\theta}-23575) \bar{K}] \times(-0.5788 \bar{I}-0.4 \bar{J}+0.2598 \bar{K}) \\\\ &+(-108.53 \bar{I}-123.86 \bar{J}+48.72 \bar{K}) \times[(-108.53 \bar{I}-123.86 \bar{J}\\\\ &+48.72 \bar{K}) \times(-0.5788 \bar{I}-0.4 \bar{J}+0.2598 \bar{K})].\end{aligned}\)

The corresponding component equations are

\(\large\begin{array}{c} \bar{a}_{B} \cdot \bar{I}=5330=\dot{v}_{C}+0.3464 \ddot{\psi}-0.0302 \ddot{\theta}-7673, \\\\ \bar{a}_{B} \cdot \bar{J}=0=-0.3713 \ddot{\psi}-0.3770 \ddot{\theta}+13747, \\\\ \bar{a}_{B} \cdot \bar{K}=-9231=0.200 \ddot{\psi}-0.6477 \ddot{\theta}+373.6, \end{array} (10)\)

whose solution is

\(\large\dot{v}_{C}=7813 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}, \ddot{\psi}=16722 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}^{2}, \ddot{\theta}=19994 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}^{2}. \)

The angular acceleration corresponding to these acceleration rates is

\(\large\bar{\alpha}_{B C}=(-10.253 \bar{I}+20.319 \bar{J}-1.77 \bar{K})\left(10^{3}\right) \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}^{2}. \)

In closing, it should be noted that the operations we performed are readily implemented in mathematical software.
