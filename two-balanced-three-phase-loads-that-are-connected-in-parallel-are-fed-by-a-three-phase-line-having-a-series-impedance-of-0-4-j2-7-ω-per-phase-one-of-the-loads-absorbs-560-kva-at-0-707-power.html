Guest Question.

Two balanced three-phase loads that are connected in parallel are fed by a three-phase line having a series impedance of (0.4 + j2.7) 立 per phase. One of the loads absorbs 560 kVA at 0.707 power factor lagging, and the other 132 kW at unity power factor. The line-to-line voltage at the load end of the line is \(2200 \sqrt{3} V\). Compute (a) the line-to-line voltage at the source end of the line, (b) the total real and reactive power losses in the three-phase line, and (c) the total three-phase real and reactive power supplied at the sending end of the line. Check that the total three-phase complex power delivered by the source equals the total three-phase complex power absorbed by the line and loads.

Admin
Solution

(a) The per-phase equivalent circuit for the problem is shown below:

Phase voltage at the load terminals is \(V_{2}=\frac{2200 \sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}}=2200 V\) taken as Ref.

Total complex power at the load end or receiving end is

\(\bar{S}_{R(3 \phi)}=560.1(0.707+j 0.707)+132=528+j 396=660 \angle 36.87^{\circ} kVA\)

With phase voltage \(\bar{V}_{2}\) as reference,

\(\bar{I}=\frac{\bar{S}_{R(3 \phi)}^{*}}{3 \bar{V}_{2}^{*}}=\frac{660,000 \angle-36.87^{\circ}}{3\left(2200 \angle 0^{\circ}\right)}=100 \angle-36.87^{\circ} A\)

Phase voltage at sending end is given by

\(\bar{V}_{1}=2200 \angle 0^{\circ}+(0.4+j 2.7)\left(100 \angle-36.87^{\circ}\right)=2401.7 \angle 4.58^{\circ} V\)

The magnitude of the line to line voltage at the sending end of the line is

\(\left(V_{1}\right)_{L-L}=\sqrt{3} V_{1}=\sqrt{3}(2401.7)=4160 V\)

 

(b) The three-phase complex-power loss in the line is given by

\(\begin{gathered}\bar{S}_{L(3 \phi)}=3 R I^{2}+j 3 \times I^{2}=3(0.4)\left(100^{2}\right)+j 3(2.7)(100)^{2} \\=12 kW +j 81 kVAR\end{gathered}\)

 

(c) The three-phase sending power is

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{S}_{S(3 \phi)}=3 \bar{V}_{1} \bar{I}^{*} &=3\left(2401.7 \angle 4.58^{\circ}\right)\left(100 \angle 36.87^{\circ}\right) \\&=540 kW +j 477 kVAR\end{aligned}\)

Note that \(\bar{S}_{S(3 \phi)}=\bar{S}_{R(3 \phi)}+\bar{S}_{L(3 \phi)}\)
