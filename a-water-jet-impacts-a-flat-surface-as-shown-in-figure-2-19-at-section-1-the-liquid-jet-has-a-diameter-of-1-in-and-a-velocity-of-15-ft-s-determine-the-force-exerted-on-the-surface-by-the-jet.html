Guest Question.

A water jet impacts a flat surface, as shown in Figure 2.19. At section 1, the liquid jet has a diameter of 1 in. and a velocity of 15 ft/s. Determine the force exerted on the surface by the jet.

Admin
Solution

The equation that relates forces to property changes of a fluid is the momentum equation. Before applying it, however, it is necessary to select a coordinate system, which is shown in the figure. It is also necessary to select a control volume for analysis. This too is shown in the figure. Note that where mass crosses the control surface, it does so at a right angle. If the surface is stationary, then it exerts a force F on the jet, as indicated. In this case, the one-dimensional momentum equation applies in the z direction:

\(\Sigma F_{z} = \frac {\dot{m}}{g_{c}} (V_{out}-V_{in})|_{z}\)

The only force exerted on the control volume is the restraining force F acting in the negative z direction. This force is equal in magnitude but opposite in direction to the force exerted on the flat plate by the liquid stream. The \(V_{out}\) term is zero because there is no mass leaving the control volume in the z- direction. The \(V_{in}\) term is given as 15 ft/s. The mass flow rate is

\(\dot{m} = \rho AV\)

applied anywhere that the properties are known. With \(D_{1}\) given,

\(A_{1} = \frac {\pi D^{2}_{1}}{4} = \frac {\pi (1/12)^{2}}{4} = 5.45 \times 10^-3 ft^2\)

The density of water is taken to be 1.94 slug/\(ft^3\). Thus, we calculate

\(\dot{m} = (1.94)(5.45 \times 10^-3)(15) = 0.159\) slug/s

The momentum equation after substitution becomes

– F = 0.159(0 – 15) = – 2.38 slug·\(ft/s^2\)

or F = 2.38 lbf

where by definition, 1 lbf = 1 slug·\(ft/s^2\) .

The result is positive, which indicates that the direction we assumed for F was correct.
