Guest Question.

A metal plate is subjected to tensile stresses of \(\sigma_{x}\) = 21 ksi and \(\sigma_{y}\) = 17 ksi (Figure P13.51/52). The corresponding strains measured in the plate are \(\varepsilon_{x}=930 \mu \varepsilon \text { and } \varepsilon_{y}=620 \mu \varepsilon\). Determine Poisson’s ratio ν and the elastic modulus E for the material.

Admin
Solution

Rewrite Eqs. (13.21) as:

\(\begin{aligned}&E \varepsilon_{x}=\sigma_{x}-v \sigma_{y} \\&E \varepsilon_{y}=\sigma_{y}-v \sigma_{x}\end{aligned}\)

Substitute the stress and strain values, taking care to convert the strain values to dimensionless quantities.

\(\begin{aligned}&E\left(930 \times 10^{-6} \text { in./in. }\right)=21 ksi -v(17 ksi ) \\&E\left(620 \times 10^{-6} \text { in./in. }\right)=17 ksi -v(21 ksi )\end{aligned}\)

Rearrange the equations to the form:

\(\begin{aligned}&E\left(930 \times 10^{-6} in. / in .\right)+v(17 ksi )=21 ksi \\&E\left(620 \times 10^{-6} in . / in .\right)+v(21 ksi )=17 ksi\end{aligned}\)

and solve simultaneously for the unknowns E and ν:

\(\begin{aligned}&E=16,907.675 ksi =16,908 ksi \\&v=0.310345=0.310\end{aligned}\)
