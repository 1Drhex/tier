Guest Question.

Re-solve using transfer functions and flexibility coefficients.

Admin
Solution

As in Worked Example 8.6, the two reactions at D are selected as the two redundants, \( R_1 (= V_D) \) and \( R_2 (= H_D) \) , and the frame is reduced to a primary frame by removing the pin support at D.
The bending moment diagrams for the primary frame caused by the external loads \( (M_o) \) , by a unit vertical force at \( D (m_1) \) and unit horizontal forces at \( D (m_2) \) are shown in Figures 8.15 a, b and c, respectively.

In AB, \( m_1 = +8 \) and \( m_2 = + x \) , where x is measured upward from A. In BC, \( m_1 = +8 − x \) and \( m_2 = + 4 \) , where x is measured from B. In CD, \( m_1 = 0 \) and \( m_2 = + x \) , where x is measured upward from D.

Because of the external loads, in AB, \( M_0 = –400 + 100 x \) and, in BC and CD, \( M_0 \) is everywhere zero. The vertical displacement \( u_{10} \) at D (release 1) and the horizontal displacement \( u_{20} \) at D (release 2) caused by the external load are calculated from:

\( u_{10} = \int\limits_{0}^{4}{\frac{M_0}{EI}\overline{m}_1 } dx = \frac{1}{50 × 10^3} \int\limits_{0}^{4}{(-400 + 100 x)8} dx = \frac{1}{50 × 10^3}[ -3200 x + 400 x^2]^4_0 = – 0.128 \) m.

and

\( u_{20} = \int\limits_{0}^{4}{\frac{M_0}{EI}\overline{m}_2 } dx = \frac{1}{50 × 10^3} \int\limits_{0}^{4}{(-400 + 100 x)x} dx = \frac{1}{50 × 10^3}[ -200 x^2 + 100 x^3]^4_0 = – 0.0213 \) m.

The flexibility coefficients are:

\( f_{11} = \biggl[\int{\frac{m_1 \overline{m}_1 }{EI}}dx \biggr]_{AB} + \biggl[\int{\frac{m_1 \overline{m}_1 }{EI}}dx \biggr]_{BC} + \biggl[\int{\frac{m_1 \overline{m}_1 }{EI}}dx \biggr]_{CD} \) .

\( = \frac{1}{50 × 10^3} \biggl[\int\limits_{0}^{4}{64} dx +\int\limits_{0}^{8}{(64 – 16 x + x^2)}dx \int\limits_{0}^{4}{0}dx \biggr] \).

\( = \frac{1}{50 × 10^3} \Bigl[[64x]^4_0 + [64x -8 x^2 + x^3/3]^8_0\Bigr] \).

\( = 8.53 × 10^{-3}\) m/kN.

\( f_{12} = \biggl[\int{\frac{ \overline{m}_1 m_2 }{EI}}dx \biggr]_{AB} + \biggl[\int{\frac{ \overline{m}_1 m_2 }{EI}}dx \biggr]_{BC} + \biggl[\int{\frac{ \overline{m}_1 m_2}{EI}}dx \biggr]_{CD} \) .

\( = \frac{1}{50 × 10^3} \biggl[\int\limits_{0}^{4}{8x} dx +\int\limits_{0}^{8}{(32 -4 x^2 )}dx \int\limits_{0}^{4}{0}dx \biggr] \).

\( = \frac{1}{50 × 10^3} \Bigl[[4x^2]^4_0 + [32 x -2 x^2 ]^8_0\Bigr] \).

\( = 3.84 × 10^{-3} \) m/kN.

\( f_{21} = f_{12} = 3.84 × 10^{–3} \) m/kN.

and

\( f_{22} = \biggl[\int{\frac{m_2 \overline{m}_2 }{EI}}dx \biggr]_{AB} + \biggl[\int{\frac{m_2 \overline{m}_2 }{EI}}dx \biggr]_{BC} + \biggl[\int{\frac{m_2 \overline{m}_2 }{EI}}dx \biggr]_{CD} \) .

\( = \frac{1}{50 × 10^3} \biggl[\int\limits_{0}^{4}{x^2} dx +\int\limits_{0}^{8}{16}dx \int\limits_{0}^{4}{x^2}dx \biggr] \).

\( = \frac{1}{50 × 10^3} \Bigl[[x^3/3]^4_0 + [16x ]^8_0 + [x^3/3]^4_0\Bigr] \).

\( = 3.41 × 10^{-3} \) m/kN.

For compatibility at release 1 and 2, we have:

\( u_{10} + R_1f_{11} + R_2f_{12} = −0.128 + 0.00853 R_1 + 0.00384 R_2 = 0 \) .

\( u_{20} + R_1f_{21} + R_2f_{22} = −0.0213 + 0.00384 R_1 + 0.00341 R_2 = 0 \) .

and solving these two equations gives:

\( R_1 = 24.59 \) kN and \( R_2 − 21.36 \) kN.

As in , the other three reactions are obtained using the equations of statics, giving:

\( V_A = 24.59 \) kN (↓), \( H_A = 78.64 \) kN (←), \( M_A = 203.3 \) kNm \( (\curvearrowleft ) \) .

\( V_D = 24.59 \) kN (↑) and \( H_D = 21.36 \) kN (←).

We can now calculate the moments at A, B, C and D using Equation 8.17:

\( M = M_0 +m_1 R_1 +m_1 R_1 \) (8.17).

\( M_A = M_{0A} + m_{1A} R_1 + m_{2A} R_2 = -400 + 8 × 24.59 + 0 × (-21.36) = -202.6 \) kNm.

\( M_B = M_{0B} + m_{1B} R_1 + m_{2B} R_2 = 0 + 8 × 24.59 + 4 × (-21.36) = +111.4 \) kNm.

\( M_C = M_{0C} + m_{1C} R_1 + m_{2C} R_2 = 0 + 0 × 24.59 + 4 × (-21.36) = -86.1 \) kNm.

and the final reactions and bending moment diagram for the indeterminate frame are shown in Figure 8.16.
