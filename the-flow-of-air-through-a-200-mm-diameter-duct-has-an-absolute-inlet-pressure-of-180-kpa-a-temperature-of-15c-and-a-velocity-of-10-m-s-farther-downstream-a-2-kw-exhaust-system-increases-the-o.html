Guest Question.

The flow of air through a 200-mm-diameter duct has an absolute inlet pressure of 180 kPa, a temperature of 15°C, and a velocity of 10 m/s. Farther downstream a 2-kW exhaust system increases the outlet velocity to 25 m/s. Determine the density of the air at the outlet, and the change in enthalpy of the air. Neglect heat transfer through the pipe.

Admin
Solution

Ideal Gas Law. Referring to Appendix \( A, R = 286.9 J/kg \cdot K. \)

\(p_in = \rho _{in}RT_{in} \)

 

\(180(10^3) N/m^2 = \rho _{in}(286.9 J/kg \cdot K)(15° + 273) K \)

 

\(\rho _{in }= 2.178 kg/m^3 \)

 

\(m = \rho _{in}V_{in}A_{in} = (2.178 kg/m^3)(10 m/s) [\pi (0.1 m)^2] \)

 

\(= 0.6844 kg/s \)

Energy Equation. With \((\frac{dQ}{dt})_{in} = (\frac{d W_{turb}} {dt})= 0 and z_{in} = z_{out} = z, \)

 

\((\frac{dQ}{dt})_{in} – (\frac{dW_{turb}} {dt}) + (\frac{dW_{pump}}{dt}) = [(h_{out} + \frac{V_{ou t}^2}{ 2} + gz_{out}) -(h_{in} + \frac{V_{in}^2}{2} + gz_{in}) ] m \)

 

 

\(0 -0 + ( \frac{dW_{pump}}{dt}) = [((h_{out} + \frac{V_{out}^2}{2} + gz) – ((h_{in} + \frac{V_{i n}^2}{2} + gz)] \)

 

\(h = h_{out} – h_{in} = (\frac{V^2_{in}}{2} – \frac{V^2_{out}}{2}) + (\frac{dW_{pump}}{dt })\frac{1 }{m} \)

 

\(= [\frac{(10 m/s)^2}{2} – \frac{(25 m/s)^2}{2}] + \frac{2000 N\cdot m/s}{0.6844 kg/s} \)

 

\(= 2660 J/kg = 2.66 kJ/kg \)
