Guest Question.

The pipe and hot-water system of is exposed to steam at 1 atm and \(100^{\circ }C\). Calculate the overall heat-transfer coefficient for this situation based on the outer area of pipe.

Admin
Solution

We have already determined the inside convection heat-transfer coefficient in as

\(h_{i} = 1961 W/m^{2} ·^{\circ}C\)

The convection coefficient for condensation on the outside of the pipe is obtained by using Equation \(\overline{h} =0.725\left[\frac{\rho (\rho -\rho _{v})gh_{fg}k_{f}^{3}}{\mu _{f}d(T_{g}-T_{w})} \right] ^{1/4}\),

\(h_{o} =0.725\left[\frac{\rho (\rho -\rho _{v})gh_{fg}k_{f}^{3}}{\mu _{f}d(T_{g}-T_{o})} \right] ^{1/4}\) [a]

where \(T_{o}\) is the outside pipe-surface temperature. The water film properties are

\(\rho = 960 kg/m^{3} \mu _{f} = 2.82 × 10^{−4} kg/m ·s\)
\(k_{f} = 0.68 W/m ·^{\circ}C h_{fg} = 2255 kJ/kg\)

so Equation (a) becomes

\(h_{o} =0.725\left[\frac{(960)^{2}(9.8)(2.255 × 10^{6})(0.68)^{3}}{(2.82 × 10^{−4})(0.06033)(100-T_{o})} \right] ^{1/4}\)

 

\( = 17,960(100 − T_{o}) ^{−1/4} \) [b]

and the outside thermal resistance per unit length is

\( R_{o} = \frac{1}{ h_{o}A_{o}}= \frac{(100 − T_{o})^{1/4}}{ (17,960)π(0.06033)} =\frac{ (100 − T_{o})^{1/4}}{3403} \) [c]

The energy balance requires

\( \frac{100 − T_{o}}{ R_{o}} = \frac{T_{o} − T_{i}} {R_{s}} = \frac{T_{i} − T_{w}}{ R_{i}} \) [d]

From

\( R_{i} = 3.092 × 10^{−3} R_{s} = 4.097 × 10^{−4} T_{w} = 98^{\circ}C \)

and Equations (c) and (d) may be combined to give

\( 3403(100 − T_{o})^{3/4} =\frac {(T_{o} − T_{i})} {4.097 × 10^{−4}} \)

 

\( \frac {T_{o} − T_{i}} {4.097 × 10^{−4}} =\frac {T_{i} − 98} {3.092 × 10^{−3} }\)

This is a nonlinear set of equations that may be solved to give
\(T_{o} = 99.91^{\circ}C T_{i} = 99.69^{\circ}C\)
The exterior heat-transfer coefficient and thermal resistance then become

\(h_{o} = 17,960(100 − 99.91) ^{−1/4} = 32,790 W/m^{2} · ^{\circ}C \) [e]

\( R_{o} = \frac{(100 − 99.91)^{1/4}} {3403} = 1.610 × 10^{−4} \) [f]

Based on unit length of pipe, the overall heat-transfer coefficient is

\( U_{o} = \frac{1}{A_{o}(R_{i} + R_{s} + R_{o})} \)

 

\(= \frac{1}{π(0.06033)(3.092 × 10^{−3} + 4.097 × 10^{−4} + 1.610 × 10^{−4})}\) [g]

\(= 1441 W/^{\circ}C · Area\)

Since \(A_{o}\) and the R’s were both per unit length,

\(U_{o} = 1441 W/m^{2} · ^{\circ}C [254 Btu/h · ft^{2} · ^{\circ}F] \)

In this problem the water-side convection coefficient is the main controlling factor because \(h_{o}\) is so large for a condensation process. In fact, the outside thermal resistance is smaller than the conduction resistance of the steel. The approximate relative magnitudes of the resistances are

\( R_{o} ∼ 1 R_{s} ∼ 2.5 R_{i} ∼ 19 \)
