Guest Question.

A signal containing only two frequency components (3 kHz and 6 kHz) is sampled at the rate of 8 kHz, and then passed through a low pass filter with a cut-off frequency of 8 kHz. The filter output

(a) is an undistorted version of the original signal
(b) contains only 3 kHz component
(c) contains the 3 kHz component and a spurious component of 2 kHz
(d) contains both the components of the original signal and two spurious components of 2 kHz and 5 kHz

Admin
Solution

\(f_{s}= 9000\) samples/sec.

 

\(f_{m1}=3kH_{3},\,f_{m2}=6kH_{3}\)

 

Then spectrum of sampled signal would have \(nf_{s} \pm f_{m}\)

 

So, \(3kH_{3},\, 8 \pm 3,\, 16 \pm 3 … = 3kH_{3},\, 5kH_{3},\, 11kH_{3}, …\)

 

\(6kH_{3},\, 8 \pm 6,\, 16 \pm 6… = 6kH_{3},\, 2kH_{3},\, 14kH_{3}, …\)

 

Cut of frequency of low pass filter \(= 8kH_{3}\),
So filter output would have,
\(3kH_{3},\, 6kH_{3},\, 2kH_{3}\) and \(5kH_{3}\)

 

Hence, the correct option is (d).
