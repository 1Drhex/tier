Guest Question.

Find the solution to the previous example using the \(P_{1}\) -approximation.

Admin
Solution

As for the exact solution we find

\(G_{c}=q_{s}e^{-\tau /\mu_{0} }, q_{c}= G_{c}\widehat{s}_{0}\)

and we realize again that \(G_{d}\) and \(q_{d}=q_{d}\widehat{k}\) depend on Ï„ (optical distance perpendicular to the layer) only.
Thus, from equations (18.19) \(\triangledown _{\tau }\cdot q_{d}=(1-w)(4\pi I_{b}-G_{d})+wG_{c}\) and (18.20) \(\frac{1}{3}\triangledown _{\tau} G_{d}=-(1-\frac{A_{1}w}{3} )q_{d}+ \frac{A_{1}w}{3} q_{c}\) and their boundary conditions (18.23) \(r=r_{w}: 2q_{d}\cdot \widehat{n}=\frac{\epsilon (4\pi I_{bw}-G_{d})+ 4(1-\epsilon )H_{c}}{2-\epsilon }\) , we find that

\(\frac{dq_{d}}{d\tau }=-(1-w)G_{d}+wG_{c},\)
\(\frac{dG_{d}}{d\tau }=-3q_{d}.\)
\(\tau =0 : 2q_{d}=-G_{d},\)
\(\tau =\tau _{L}: -2q_{d}=-G_{d}.\)

Since the solution procedure for this equation is different for w = 1 (as opposed to w< 1), and since we would like to compare the present results with exact ones shown in Fig. 18-4, we shall limit the rest of our discussion to w = 1. Then

\(\frac{dq_{d}}{d\tau }= q_{s}e^{-\tau /\mu_{0} } , or q_{d}=-\mu _{0}q_{s}e^{-\tau /\mu_{0} }+ C_{1},\)
\(\frac{dG_{d}}{d\tau }=-3q_{d}, or G_{d}=-3\mu _{0}^{2}q_{s} e^{-\tau /\mu_{0} }-3C_{1}\tau +C_{2.}\)

It follows from the boundary conditions that

\(\tau =0 : 2C_{1}+C_{2}=(2+3\mu _{0})\mu _{0}q_{s},\)
\(\tau =\tau _{L}: (2+3\tau _{L})C_{1}-C_{2}=(2-3\mu _{0})\mu _{0}q_{s}e^{-\tau /\mu_{0} }\),

or

\(C_{1}=\frac{2+3\mu _{0}+(2-3\mu _{0})e^{-\tau_{L} /\mu_{0} }}{4+3\tau _{L}}\mu _{0}q_{s},\)
\(q_{d}= \left[\frac{2+3\mu _{0}+(2-3\mu _{0})e^{-\tau_{L} /\mu_{0} }}{4+3\tau _{L}} -e^{-\tau /\mu_{0}}\right] \mu _{0}q_{s}.\)

Finally,

\(\Psi = \frac{q_{c}\cdot \widehat{k} + q_{d}}{q_{s}}= \frac{2+3\mu _{0}+(2-3\mu _{0})e^{-\tau_{L} /\mu_{0} }}{4+3\tau _{L}}\mu _{0},\)

which, as discussed in the previous example, is constant across the layer. This nondimensional heat flux is compared with the exact result in Fig. 18-4. It is seen that the \(P_{1}\)-approximation gives good accuracy for all cases shown in that figure.
