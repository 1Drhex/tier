Guest Question.

The symmetrical sawbuck in Fig. 5.17a consists of two beams connected at hinge C and fixed by the rope S. It is
loaded with a frictionless cylinder of weight G. Determine the support reactions at A and B, the force S in the
rope and the joint reaction in C. The weight of the sawbuck can be neglected.

Admin
Solution

Since only three support reactions are present (see Fig. 5.17b), they can be determined by applying the equilibrium conditions to the complete system:

\(\rightarrow :A_{H}=0,\)

\(\overset{\curvearrowleft }{A}: -2aG+4aB=0 \rightarrow B=G/2,\) (a}

\(\overset{\curvearrowleft }{B}: -4aA_{V}+2aG=0 \rightarrow A_{V}=G/2.\)

In order to determine the forces in the rope and the hinge, we separate the structure into its two parts (n = 2). In the hinge C and rope S, in total \(v = 2 + 1 = 3\) forces are transferred (Fig. 5.17c). With r = 3, the necessary condition (5.3) for statical determinacy is fulfilled: 3 + 3 = 3 · 2.

\(r+v=3n (5.3)\)

Since the surface of the cylinder is assumed to be frictionless, the contact forces \(N_{1}\) and \(N_{2}\) between the beams and the cylinder act in directions normal to the respective contact planes. Therefore, with sin 45◦ = √2/2, the equilibrium conditions for the cylinder read

\(\rightarrow : \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}N_{2}-\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}N_{1}=0 \rightarrow N_{1}=N_{2},\)

(b)

\(↑: -G+\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}N_{2}+\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}N_{1}=0 \rightarrow N_{1}=N_{2}=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}G.\)

From the equilibrium conditions for the beam ② we obtain with (a) and (b):

\(\overset{\curvearrowleft }{C}: \sqrt{2}aN_{2}-aS+2aB=0 \rightarrow S=2B+\sqrt{2}N_{2}=2G,\)

 

\(↑: -\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}N_{2}-C_{V}+B=0 \rightarrow C_{V}=B-\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}N_{2}=0,\)

 

\(\rightarrow : -\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}N_{2}-C_{H}-S=0 \rightarrow C_{H}=-\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}N_{2}-S=-\frac{5}{2}G.\)

The same result is obtained when the equilibrium conditions are applied to beam ① . By symmetry considerations, it can be concluded from Fig. 5.17c with no calculation that \(N_{1} = N_{2}\) and
\(C_{V} = 0.\)
