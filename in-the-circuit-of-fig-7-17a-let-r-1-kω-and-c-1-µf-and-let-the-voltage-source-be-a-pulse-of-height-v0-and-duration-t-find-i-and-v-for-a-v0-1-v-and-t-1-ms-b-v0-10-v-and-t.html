Guest Question.

In the circuit of Fig. 7-17(a), let R = 1 kΩ and C = 1 µF and let the voltage source be a pulse of height \( V_{0} \) and duration T. Find i and v for (a) \( V_{0}=1 V \text { and } T=1 ms ,(b) V_{0}=10 V \text { and } T=0.1 ms \), and (c) \( V_{0}=100 V \text { and } T=0.01 ms \).

Admin
Solution

We use (14) and (15) with the time constant of τ = RC = 1 ms. For convenience, time will be expressed in ms, voltages in V, and currents in mA. We also use the approximation \( e^{-t}=1-t \text { when } t \ll 1 .\)

\(v=V_0(1-e^{-t/RC})\qquad (0\lt t\lt T)\) (14a)

\(i=\frac{V_0}{R} e^{-t/RC}\qquad (0\lt t\lt T)\) (14b)

\(V_T=V_0(1-e^{-T/RC})\) (14c)

\(v=V_T e^{-(t-T)/RC}\qquad (t\gt T)\) (15a)

\(i=-\left(V_T /R\right) e^{-(t-T)/RC}\qquad (t\gt T)\) (15b)

(a) \( V_{0}=1 V , T=1 ms \)

For 0 < t < 1 ms,

\( v=\left(1-e^{-t}\right), i=e^{-t} \text {, and } V_{T}=\left(1-e^{-1}\right)=0.632 V \)

For t > 1 ms,

\( v=0.632 e^{-(t-1)}=1.72 e^{-t}, \text { and } i=-1.72 e^{-t} \)

 

(b) \( V_{0}=10 V , T=0.1 ms. \)

For 0 < t < 0.1 ms,

\( v=10\left(1-e^{-t}\right), i=10 e^{-t}, \text { and } V_{T}=10\left(1-e^{-0.1}\right)=0.95 V \)

For t > 0.1 ms,

\( v=0.95 e^{-(t-0.1)}=1.05 e^{-t}, \text { and } i=-1.05 e^{-i} \)

 

(c) \( V_{0}=100 V , T=0.01 ms. \)

For 0 < t < 0.01 ms,

\( v=100\left(1-e^{-t}\right) \approx 100 t, i=100 e^{-t} \approx 100(1-t), \text { and } V_{T}=100\left(1-e^{-0.01}\right)=0.995 V \)

For t > 0.01 ms,

\( v=0.995 e^{-(t-0.01)}=1.01 e^{-t} \text { and } i=-1.01 e^{-t} \)

As the input voltage pulse approaches an impulse, the capacitor voltage and current approach \( v=e^{-t} u(t)( V ) \) and \( i=\delta(t)-e^{-t} u(t) \).
