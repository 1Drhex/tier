Guest Question.

An annealed AISI 1018 steel (see Table A–22) is given 15 percent cold work. Find the new values for the yield strength and the ultimate strength.

Admin
Solution

From Table A–22, \( S_{y} = 32.0 kpsi, S_{u} = 49.5 kpsi, σ_{0} = 90 kpsi \) , m = 0.25. From Equation (2–28) \( \tilde{\epsilon }_{15} = ln (\frac{1}{1-W} )= ln (\frac{1}{1-0.15} )= 0.1625\) , the true strain that is induced by 15 percent cold work is

From Equation (2–23) \( m=\tilde{\epsilon } _{u} \), the true strain at the ultimate point is equal to m. That is,\( \tilde{\epsilon } _{u} = m = 0.25 \). Therefore, since\( \tilde{\epsilon } _{15}< \tilde{\epsilon } _{u}\) , the strain from 15 percent cold work has not reached the strain at the ultimate point, and Equations (2–30) and (2–32) are valid.

Equation (2–30): \( S^{\prime }_{y}= \sigma _{\omicron }\tilde{\epsilon } ^{m}_{15} = 90(0.1625)^{0.25} = 57.1 kpsi\)

Equation (2–32): \( S^{′}_{u}= \frac{S_{u}}{1-W}=\frac{49.5}{1 − 0.15} = 58.2 kpsi \)
