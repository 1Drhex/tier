Guest Question.

Determine the largest force that can be developed in member \(B C\) of the bridge truss shown in Fig. \(6-26 (\mathrm{a})\) due to a moving force of \(20 \mathrm{k}\) and a moving distributed load of \(0.6 \mathrm{k} / \mathrm{ft}\). The loading is applied at the top cord.

Admin
Solution

Tabulate Values. A table of unit-load position \(x\) at the joints along the top cord versus the force in member \(B C\) is shown in Fig. 6-26b. The method of sections can be used for the calculations. For example, when the unit load is at joint \(I(x=20 \mathrm{ft})\), Fig. 6-26a, the reaction \(\mathbf{E}_{y}\) is determined first \(\left(E_{y}=0.25\right)\). Then the truss is sectioned through \(B C, H B\), and \(H I\), and the right segment is isolated, Fig. 6-26c. One obtains \(\mathbf{F}_{B C}\) by summing moments about point \(H\) to eliminate \(\mathbf{F}_{H I}\) and \(\mathbf{F}_{H B}\). In a similar manner determine the other values in Fig. 6-26b.

Influence Line. A plot of the tabular values yields the influence line, Fig. 6-26d. By inspection, \(B C\) is a primary member. Why?

Concentrated Live Force. The largest force in member \(B C\) occurs when the moving force of \(20 \mathrm{k}\) is placed at \(x=40 \mathrm{ft}\). Thus,

\( F_{B C}=(1.33)(20)=26.7 \mathrm{k} \)

Distributed Live Load. The uniform live load must be placed over the entire deck of the truss to create the largest tensile force in \(B C\).* Thus,

\( F_{B C}=\left[\frac{1}{2}(80)(1.33)\right] 0.6=32.0 \mathrm{k} \)

Total Maximum Force

\( \left(F_{B C}\right)_{\max }=26.7 \mathrm{k}+32.0 \mathrm{k}=58.7 \mathrm{k} \)

*The largest tensile force in member \(G B\) of is created when the distributed load acts on the deck of the truss from \(x=0\) to \(x=8 \mathrm{~m}\), Fig. 6-24d.
