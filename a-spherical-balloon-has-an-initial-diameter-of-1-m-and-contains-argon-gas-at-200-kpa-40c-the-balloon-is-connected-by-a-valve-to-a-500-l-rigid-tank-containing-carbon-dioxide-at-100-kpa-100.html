Guest Question.

A spherical balloon has an initial diameter of 1 m and contains argon gas at 200 kPa, 40°C. The balloon is connected by a valve to a 500-L rigid tank containing carbon dioxide at 100 kPa, 100°C. The valve is opened, and eventually the balloon and tank reach a uniform state in which the pressure is 185 kPa. The balloon pressure is directly proportional to its diameter. Take the balloon and tank as a control volume, and calculate the final temperature and the heat transfer for the process.

Admin
Solution

\(V _{ A1}=\frac{\pi}{6} 1^{3}=0.5236\),

 

\(m _{ A1 }=\frac{ P _{ A1 } V _{ A 1}}{ RT _{ A 1}}=\frac{200 \times 0.5236}{0.20813 \times 313.2}=1.606 kg\)

 

\(m _{ B 1}= P _{ B 1} V _{ B 1} / RT _{ B 1}=100 \times 0.50 / 0.18892 \times 373.2=0.709 kg\)

 

Process: \(P = C D = CV ^{1 / 3} \Rightarrow\) polytropic \(n =-1 / 3 \quad \Rightarrow\)

 

\(V _{ A 2}= V _{ A1 }\left(\frac{ P _{2}}{ P _{ A 1}}\right)^{3}=0.5236\left(\frac{185}{200}\right)^{3}=0.4144 m ^{3}\)

 

2: Uniform ideal gas mixture :

\(P _{2}\left( V _{ A 2}+ V _{ B }\right)=\left( m _{ A } R _{ A }+ m _{ B } R _{ B }\right) T _{2}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}T _{2} &= P _{2}\left( V _{ A 2}+ V _{ B }\right) /\left( m _{ A } R _{ A }+ m _{ B } R _{ B }\right) \\&=185(0.4144+0.50) /(1.606 \times 0.20813+0.709 \times 0.18892)= 3 6 1 . 3 K\end{aligned}\)

 

Boundary work in a polytropic process from Eq.6.29

\(\begin{aligned}W _{12} &=\frac{ P _{2} V _{ A 2}- P _{ A1} V _{ A1 }}{1-(-1 / 3)}=\frac{185 \times 0.4144-200 \times 0.5236}{(4 / 3)} kPa m ^{3} \\&=-21.0 kJ\end{aligned}\)

The heat transfer from the energy equation

\(\begin{aligned}Q &= m _{ A } C _{ V 0 A }\left( T _{2}- T _{ Al }\right)+ m _{ B } C _{ V0B }\left( T _{2}- T _{ B 1}\right)+ W _{12} \\&=1.606 \times 0.312(361.3-313.2)+0.709 \times 0.653(361.3-373.2)-21.0 \\&=18.6-21.0=-2.4 kJ\end{aligned}\)
