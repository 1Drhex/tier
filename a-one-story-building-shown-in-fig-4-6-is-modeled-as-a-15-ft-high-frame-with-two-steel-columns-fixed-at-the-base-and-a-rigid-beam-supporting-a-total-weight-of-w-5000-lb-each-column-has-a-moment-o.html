Guest Question.

A one-story building, shown in Fig. 4.6, is modeled as a 15-ft high frame with two steel columns fixed at the base and a rigid beam supporting a total weight of W = 5000 lb. Each column has a moment of inertia I = 69.2 \(in^{2}\) and a section modulus \(S=\frac{I}{c}=17in^{3}\) (\(E=30\times 10^{6}\) psi).

Determine the maximum response of the frame to a rectangular impulse of amplitude 3000 lb and duration \(t_{d}=0.1\) s applied horizontally to the top member of the frame. The response of interest is the horizontal displacement at the top of the frame and the bending stress in the columns.

Admin
Solution

1. Natural period.

\(k=\frac{12E(2I)}{L^{3}}=\frac{12\times 30\times 10^{6}\times 2\times 69.2}{(15\times 12)^{3}}=8544\) lb/in

 

\(m=\frac{5000}{386}=12.9534\) \(lb\cdot sec^{2}/in\)

 

\(T=2\pi \sqrt{\frac{m}{k}}=2\pi \sqrt{\frac{12.9534}{8544}}=0.2446\) sec

 

2. Maximum displacement.

\(\frac{t_{d}}{T}=\frac{0.1}{0.2446}=0.408\)

 

\(DLF_{max}=\frac{u_{max}}{u_{st}}=1.9\)

 

\(u_{st}=\frac{F_{0}}{k}=\frac{3000}{8544}=0.3511\) in

 

\(u_{max}=(1.9)(0.3511)=0.667\) in

 

3. Maximum bending stress.
The bending moment M in the columns is given by

\(M=V_{max}\frac{L}{2}=ku_{max}\frac{L}{2}\)

 

\(=\frac{6EI}{L^{2}}u_{max}=\frac{6\times 30\times 10^{6}\times 69.2}{(15\times 12)^{2}}0.667=256,424\) (lb.in)

 

and the maximum stress \(\sigma _{max}\) by

\(\sigma _{max}=\frac{M}{S}=\frac{256,424}{17}=15,083\) psi

Alternatively, the maximum response can be estimated using MATLAB file.
