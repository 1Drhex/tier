Guest Question.

R-410A at 120째C, 4 MPa is in an insulated tank and flow is now allowed out to a turbine with a backup pressure of 800 kPa. The flow continue to a final tank pressure of 800 kPa and the process stops. If the initial mass was 1 kg how much mass is left in the tank and what is the turbine work assuming a reversible process?

Admin
Solution

C.V. Total tank and turbine.
Continuity Eq.4.15: \(m _{2}- m _{1}=- m _{ ex }\)

Energy Eq.4.16: \(m _{2} u _{2}- m _{1} u _{1}=- m _{ ex } h _{ ex }+{ }_{1} Q _{2}-{ }_{1} W _{2}\)

Entropy Eq.7.12: \(m _{2} s _{2}- m _{1} s _{1}=- m _{ ex } s _{ ex }+\int dQ / T +{ }_{1} S _{2 gen} \)

Process: Adiabatic \({ }_{1} Q _{2}=0\); Reversible \({ }_{1} S _{2 \text { gen }} =0\)

This has too many unknowns (we do not know state 2 only \(P _{2}\) ).

C.V. \(m _{2}\) the mass that remains in the tank. This is a control mass.

Entropy Eq.7.3 (6.37): \(m _{2}\left( s _{2}- s _{1}\right)=\int d Q / T +{ }_{1} S _{2 gen} \)

Process: Adiabatic \({ }_{1} Q _{2}=0\); Reversible \({ }_{1} S _{2 \text { gen }} =0\)

\(\Rightarrow \quad s_{2}=s_{1}\)

State 1: \(v _{1}=0.00897 m ^{3} / kg , u _{1}=331.39 kJ / kg , s _{1}=1.1529 kJ / kg – K\)

State 2 (P,s): \(T _{2}=33.23^{\circ} C , \quad v _{2}=0.37182 m ^{3} / kg , \quad u _{2}=281.29 kJ / kg\)

State exit: \(s _{ ex }= s _{2}= s _{1}\) follows from entropy Eq. for first C.V. using the continuity eq., this is identical to state 2, \(h _{ ex }=312.85 kJ / kg\)

Tank volume constant so \(V = m _{1} v _{1}= m _{2} v _{2}\)

\(m _{2}= m _{1} v _{1} / v _{2}=1 kg \times 0.00897 / 0.37182= 0 . 0 2 4 1 kg\)

From energy eq.

\(\begin{aligned}_{1}W _{2} &= m _{1} u _{1}- m _{2} u _{2}- m _{ ex } h _{ ex } \\&=1 \times 331.39-0.0241 \times 281.29-0.9759 \times 312.85[ kg kJ / kg ] \\&= 1 9 . 3 k J\end{aligned}\)
