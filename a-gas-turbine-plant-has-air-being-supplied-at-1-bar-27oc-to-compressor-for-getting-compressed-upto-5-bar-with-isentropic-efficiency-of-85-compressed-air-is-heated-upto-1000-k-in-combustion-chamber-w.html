Guest Question.

A gas turbine plant has air being supplied at 1 bar, 27ºC to compressor for getting compressed upto 5 bar with isentropic efficiency of 85%. Compressed air is heated upto 1000 K in combustion chamber where also occurs a pressure drop of 0.2 bar. Subsequently expansion occurs to 1 bar in turbine. Determine isentropic efficiency of turbine, if thermal efficiency of plant is 20%. Neglect the air property variation throughout cycle. Take \(\pmb{\gamma}\) = 1.4

Admin
Solution
\(T_{1} = 300 K, P_{1} = 1 bar, P_{2} = 5 bar, η_{c} = 0.85, T_{3} = 1000 K\\\text{For process 1–2}^{\prime}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad \qquad\quad\frac{T_{2^{\prime}}}{T_{1}}&= \left\lgroup\frac{P_{2^{\prime}}}{P_{1}} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{\gamma – 1}{\gamma} } ⇒ T_{2^{\prime}} = 300 (5)^{\frac{1.4 – 1}{1.4} }\\T_{2^{\prime}}&= 475.15 K\\η_{c}&= 0.85 = \frac{T_{2^{\prime}} – T_{1}}{T_{2} – T_{1}}\\T_{2}&= 506.06 K\end{aligned}\\\text{For process 3–4}^{\prime}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad \qquad\quad\frac{T_{4^{\prime}}}{T_{3}}&= \left\lgroup\frac{P_{4}}{P_{3}} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{\gamma – 1}{\gamma} } = \left\lgroup\frac{1}{4.8} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{1.4 – 1}{1.4} }\\T_{4^{\prime}}&= 1000 × \left\lgroup\frac{1}{4.8} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{1.4 – 1}{1.4} }\\T_{4^{\prime}}&= 638.79 K\end{aligned}\\\text{Compressor work per kg }W_{C} = c_{p} (T_{2} – T_{1}) = 1.0032 × (506.06 – 300) = 206.72 kJ/kg\\\text{Turbine work per kg, }W_{T} = c_{p}(T_{3} – T_{4}) = 1.0032 × (1000 – T_{4}), kJ/kg\\\text{Net output, }W_{net} = W_{T} – W_{C} = \left\{206.72 – (1.0032 (1000 – T_{4}))\right\} , kJ/kg\\\text{Heat added, }q_{add} = c_{p} (T_{3} – T_{2}) = 1.0032 (1000 – 506.06) = 495.52 kJ/kg\\\text{Thermal efficiency, }η = \frac{W_{net}}{q_{add}} ⇒ 0.20 = \frac{206.71 – (1.0032 × (1000 – T_{4}))}{495.52}\\ \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad T_{4} = 892.73 K\\\text{Therefore, isentropic efficiency of turbine, }η_{T} = \frac{T_{3} – T_{4}}{T_{3} – T_{4^{\prime}}} = \frac{(1000 – 892.73)}{(1000 – 638.79)}\\\qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad η_{T} = 0.2969 or 29.69\%\\\qquad \text{Turbine isentropic efficiency} = \textbf{29.69}\pmb{\%}\)
