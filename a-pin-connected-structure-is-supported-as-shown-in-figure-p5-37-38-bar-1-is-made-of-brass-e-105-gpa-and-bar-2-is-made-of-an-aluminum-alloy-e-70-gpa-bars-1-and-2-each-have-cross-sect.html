Guest Question.

A pin-connected structure is supported as shown in Figure P5.37/38. Bar (1) is made of brass [E = 105 GPa], and bar (2) is made of an aluminum alloy [E = 70 GPa]. Bars (1) and (2) each have cross-sectional areas of 375 \(mm ^{2}\) . Rigid bar ABCD is supported by a pin in a double-shear connection at B. If the allowable shear stress for pin B is 130 MPa, calculate the minimum allowable diameter for the pin at B when P = 42 kN.

Admin
Solution

Equilibrium
Consider a FBD of the rigid bar. Assume tension forces in bars (1) and (2).

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma M_{B}=(260 mm ) F_{1}+(660 mm ) F_{2} \\&+(880 mm ) P=0 (a)\\&\Sigma F_{x}=F_{1}+F_{2}+B_{x}=0 (b)\\&\Sigma F_{y}=B_{y}-P=0 (c)\end{aligned}\)

Geometry-of-deformation relationship
Draw a deformation diagram of the rigid bar. The deflections of the rigid bar are related by similar triangles:

\(\frac{v_{A}}{880 mm }=\frac{v_{C}}{260 mm }=\frac{v_{D}}{660 mm }\) (d)

Since there are no gaps or clearances at either pin C or pin D, the deformations of bars (1) and (2) will equal the deflections of the rigid bar at C and D, respectively.

\(\frac{\delta_{1}}{260 mm }=\frac{\delta_{2}}{660 mm }\) (e)

Force-Deformation Relationships
The relationship between the internal force and the deformation of an axial member can be stated for bars (1) and (2) as:

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}} \quad \delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}\) (f)

Compatibility Equation

Substitute the force-deformation relationships (f) into the geometry-of-deformation relationship (e) to derive the compatibility equation:

\(\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}=\left(\frac{260 mm }{660 mm }\right) \frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}=0.393939 \frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}\) (g)

Solve the Equations
Solve Eq. (g) for \(F_{1}\):

\(F_{1}=0.393939\left(\frac{L_{2}}{L_{1}}\right)\left(\frac{A_{1}}{A_{2}}\right)\left(\frac{E_{1}}{E_{2}}\right) F_{2}=0.393939\left(\frac{430 mm }{720 mm }\right)\left(\frac{375 mm ^{2}}{375 mm ^{2}}\right)\left(\frac{105 GPa }{70 GPa }\right) F_{2}=0.352904 F_{2}\)

and substitute into Eq. (a) to solve for \(F_{2}\):

\(\begin{aligned}(260 mm ) F_{1}+(660 mm ) F_{2} &=-(880 mm ) P \\(260 mm )\left(0.352904 F_{2}\right)+(660 mm ) F_{2} &=-(880 mm ) P \\F_{2} &=-\left(\frac{880 mm }{(260 mm )(0.352904)+660 mm }\right) P \\&=-1.170594 P\end{aligned}\)

Given P = 42 kN, calculate the forces in bars (1) and (2):

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{2}=-1.170594(42 kN )=-49.1650 kN \\&F_{1}=0.352904(-49.1650 kN )=-17.3505 kN\end{aligned}\)

From Eqs. (b) and (c), calculate the reaction forces at B:

\(\begin{aligned}&B_{x}=-F_{1}-F_{2}=17.3505 kN +49.1650 kN =65.5155 kN \\&B_{y}=P=42 kN \\&|B|=\sqrt{(65.5155 kN )^{2}+(42 kN )^{2}}=78.6658 kN\end{aligned}\)

 

Minimum allowable diameter for pin B
The shear area required for the pin at B is:

\(A_{V}=\frac{78,665.8 N }{130 N / mm ^{2}}=605.1214 mm ^{2}\)

Since the pin at B is held in a double shear connection, the required pin cross-sectional area is

\(A_{ pin }=\frac{A_{V}}{2}=302.5607 mm ^{2}\)

and thus the minimum required bolt diameter is

\(\frac{\pi}{4} d_{ pin }^{2} \geq 302.5607 mm ^{2} \quad \therefore d_{ pin } \geq 19.63 mm\)
