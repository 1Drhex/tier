Designing a hard voltage limiter

(a) Design a hard limiter like the one in Fig. 16.15(a). The circuit should limit the negative output voltage to \( V_{ O ( min )}=-5 V \) and the positive output voltage to \( V_{ O (\max )}=5 V \). The magnitude of the slopes after the break points should be less than or equal to 1 ‚ÅÑ 20. The diode drop is \( V_{ D }=0.7 V \). The DC supplies are given by \( V_{ A }=V_{ B }=12 V \). Determine the values of \( R_{1}, R_{2}, R_{3}, R_{4}, \text { and } R_{5} \).

(b) Use PSpice/SPICE to plot the transfer characteristic. Assume \( V_{ CC }=12 V ,-V_{ EE }=-12 V , \text { and } v_{ S }=-6 V \) to 6 V.

Admin
Solution

(a) Choose \( R_{1}=10 k \Omega \text {. Since the slope }\left|S_{1}\right|=R_{3} / R_{1}=1 / 20 \),

 

\( R_{3}=\frac{R_{1}}{20}=\frac{10 k \Omega}{20}=500 \Omega \)

From Eq. (16.5),

\( V_{ O (\min )}=v_{ O }=-V_{ D }-\left(V_{ A }+V_{ D }\right) \frac{R_{3}}{R_{2}}=-V_{ D }\left(1+\frac{R_{3}}{R_{2}}\right)-V_{ A } \frac{R_{3}}{R_{2}} \) (16.5)

 

\(\begin{aligned}-5 &=-0.7-\frac{(12+0.7) R_{3}}{R_{2}} \\\frac{R_{3}}{R_{2}} &=0.3386 \\R_{2} &=1477 \Omega\end{aligned}\)

 

Since the slope \( \left|S_{2}\right|=R_{5} / R_{1}=1 / 20,\)

 

\( R_{5}=\frac{R_{1}}{20}=\frac{10 k \Omega}{20}=500 \Omega \)

From Eq. (16.10),

\(V_{ O (\max )}=v_{ O }=V_{ D }+\left(V_{ B }+V_{ D }\right) \frac{R_{5}}{R_{4}}=V_{ D }\left(1+\frac{R_{5}}{R_{4}}\right)+V_{ B } \frac{R_{5}}{R_{4}}\) (16.10)

 

\(\begin{aligned}5 &=0.7+\frac{(12+0.7) R_{5}}{R_{4}} \\\frac{R_{5}}{R_{4}} &=0.3386 \\R_{4} &=1477 \Omega\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) The hard limiter for PSpice simulation is shown in Fig. 16.17. The transfer characteristic is shown in Fig. 16.18, which gives \( V_{ S (\max )}=55.86 mV , V_{ S (\min )}=-27.93 mV , V_{ O (\max )}=4.50 V , \text { and } V_{ O (\min )}=-4.55 V \). Discrepancies between the design values and the PSpice results come from use of an ideal op-amp versus a macromodel and use of a specific diode versus a diode of type D1N4148.
