Guest Question.

A 50-kN load is transferred from a welded fitting into a 200-mm steel channel as illustrated in Fig. 9–14. Estimate the maximum stress in the weld.

Admin
Solution

(a) Label the ends and corners of each weld by letter. See Fig. 9–15. Sometimes it is desirable to label

each weld of a set by number.
(b) Estimate the primary shear stress \(\acute{\tau } \).

As shown in Fig. 9–14, each plate is welded to the channel by means of three 6-mm fillet welds. Figure 9–15 shows that we have divided the load in half and are considering only a single plate. From case 4 of Table 9–1

Table 9–1
Torsional Properties of Fillet Welds
Unit Second Polar
Weld Throat Area Location of G Moment of Area
\( A=0.707hd \) \( \bar{x} =0 \\ \bar{y} ={d}/{2} \) \( J_u={d^3}/{12} \)
\( A=1.414 hd \) \( \bar{x} ={b}/{2} \\ \bar{y} ={d}/{2} \) \( J_u=\frac{d \left(3b^2+d^2\right)}{6} \)
\( A=0.707h \left(b+d\right) \) \( \bar{x} =\frac{b^2}{2\left(b+d\right)} \\ \bar{y} =\frac{d^2}{2\left(b+d\right)} \) \( J_u=\frac{\left(b+d\right)^4-6b^2 d^2}{12\left(b+d\right)} \)
\( A=0.707h \left(2b+d\right) \) \( \bar{x} = \frac{b^2}{2b+d} \\ \bar{y} ={d}/{2} \) \( J_u=\frac{8b^3+6bd^2+d^3}{12}-\frac{b^4}{2b+d} \)
\( A=1.414h \left(b+d\right) \) \( \bar{x} = {b}/{2} \\ \bar{y} ={d}/{2} \) \( J_u=\frac{\left(b+d\right)^3}{6} \)
\(A=1.414 \pi hr \) \( J_u=2 \pi r^3 \)

we find the throat area as
\(A = 0.707 \left(6\right)\left[2\left(56\right)+190 \right] =1280 \ mm^2\)
Then the primary shear stress is

\(\acute{\tau } =\frac{V}{A} =\frac{25\left (10\right)^3 }{1280} =19.5 \ MPa\)

(c) Draw the \(\acute{\tau } \) stress, to scale, at

each lettered corner or end. See Fig. 9–16.
(d) Locate the centroid of the weld pattern. Using case 4 of

Table 9–1, we find

\(\bar{x} =\frac{\left(56\right)^2 }{2\left(56\right)+190 } =10.4 \ mm\)

This is shown as point O on Figs. 9–15 and 9–16.

(e) Find the distances ri(see Fig. 9–16):

\(r_A=r_B=\left[\left({190}/{2}\right)^2+\left(56-10.4\right)^2 \right] j^{{1}/{2}}=105 \ mm \\ r_C=r_D=\left[\left({190}/{2}\right)^2+\left(10.4\right)^2 \right] j^{{1}/{2}}=95.6 \ mm \)

These distances

can also be scaled from the drawing.

(f) Find J. Using case 4 of Table 9–1 again, with Eq. (9–6)

\(J= 0.707 h J_u\)

,we get

\(J=0.707\left(6\right) \left[\frac{8\left(56\right)^3+6\left(56\right)\left(190\right)^3 }{12}-\frac{\left(56\right)^4 }{2\left(56\right)+190 } \right] =\\7.07\left(10\right) ^6 \ mm^4\)

(g) Find M:

\(M=Fl=25\left(100+10.4\right) =2760 \ N.m\)

(h) Estimate the secondary shear stresses τ??at each lettered end or corner:

\(\begin{array}{l}\tau_{A}^{\prime \prime}=\tau_{B}^{\prime \prime}=\frac{M r}{J}=\frac{2760(10)^{3}(105)}{7.07(10)^{6}}=41.0 \mathrm{MPa} \\ \tau_{C}^{\prime \prime}=\tau_{D}^{\prime \prime}=\frac{2760(10)^{3}(95.6)}{7.07(10)^{6}}=37.3 \mathrm{MPa}\end{array}\)

(i) Draw the \(\tau ''\) stress at each corner and end.

See Fig. 9–16. Note that this is a free-body diagram of one of the side plates, and therefore the\(\tau '\) and \(\tau ''\)

stresses represent what the channel is doing to the plate (through the welds) to hold the plate in equilibrium.
(j) At each point labeled, combine the two stress components as vectors (since they apply to the same area).

At point A, the angle that \(\tau ''_A\) makes with the vertical,

\(\alpha \), is also the angle \(r_A\) makes with the horizontal, which is

\(\alpha= \tan ^{-1}\left({45.6}/{95}\right) = 25.64◦\) .

This angle also applies to point B. Thus

\( \tau _A=\tau _B=\sqrt{\left(19.5-41.0 \sin 25.64◦\right)^2+\left(41.0 \cos 6.25◦\right) ^2 }=37.0 \ MPa \)

Similarly, for\( C \ and \ D \ , \ β = \tan^{−1}(10.4/95) = 6.25◦\) . Thus

\(\tau _C=\tau _D=\sqrt{\left(19.5+37.3 \sin 6.25◦\right)^2+\left(37.3 \cos 6.25◦\right) ^2 } =43.9 \ MPa\)

(k) Identify the most highly stressed point:

\(\tau_{\max }=\tau_{C}=\tau_{D}=43.9 \mathrm{MPa}\)
