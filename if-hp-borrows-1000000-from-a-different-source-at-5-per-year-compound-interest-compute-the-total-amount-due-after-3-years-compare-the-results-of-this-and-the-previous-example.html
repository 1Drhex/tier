Solution

The interest and total amount due each year are computed separately using Equation [1.4]. In $1000 units,

Interest = (principal + all accrued interest)(interest rate) [1.4]

Year 1 interest: $1000(0.05) = $50.00

Total amount due after year 1: $1000 + 50.00 = $1050.00

Year 2 interest: $1050(0.05) = $52.50

Total amount due after year 2: $1050 + 52.50 = $1102.50

Year 3 interest: $1102.50(0.05) = $55.13

Total amount due after year 3: $1102.50 + 55.13 = $1157.63

The details are shown in Table 1.2. The repayment plan is the same as that for the simple interest exampleâ€”no payment until the principal plus accrued interest is due at the end of year 3. An extra $1,157,630 – 1,150,000 = $7,630 of interest is paid compared to simple interest over the 3-year period.

Comment: The difference between simple and compound interest grows significantly each year. If the computations are continued for more years, for example, 10 years, the difference is $128,894; after 20 years compound interest is $653,298 more than simple interest.
