Guest Question.

Chlorine has two naturally occurring isotopes, \( Cl ^{35} \text { and } Cl ^{37} \). Show that the vibrational spectrum of HCl should consist of closely spaced doublets, with a splitting given by \( \Delta v=7.51 \times 10^{-4} v \) , where ν is the frequency of the emitted photon. Hint: Think of it as a harmonic oscillator, with \( \omega=\sqrt{k / \mu} \),where μ is the reduced mass (Equation 5.15) and k is presumably the same for both isotopes.

\( \mu \equiv \frac{m_{1} m_{2}}{m_{1}+m_{2}} \) (5.15).

Admin
Solution

The energy of the emitted photon, in a transition from vibrational state \( n_i \) to state \( n_f \) , is

\( \left.E_{p}=\left(n_{i}+\frac{1}{2}\right) \hbar \omega-\left(n_{f}+\frac{1}{2}\right) \hbar \omega=n \hbar \omega, \quad \text { (where } n \equiv n_{i}-n_{f}\right) \). The frequency of the photon is

\( \nu=\frac{E_{p}}{h}=\frac{n \omega}{2 \pi}=\frac{n}{2 \pi} \sqrt{\frac{k}{\mu}} \). The splitting of this line is given by

\( \Delta \nu=\left|\frac{n}{2 \pi} \sqrt{k}\left(-\frac{1}{2 \mu^{3 / 2}} \Delta \mu\right)\right|=\frac{1}{2} \frac{n}{2 \pi} \sqrt{\frac{k}{\mu}} \frac{\Delta \mu}{\mu}=\frac{1}{2} \nu \frac{\Delta \mu}{\mu} \).

Now

\( \mu=\frac{m_{h} m_{c}}{m_{h}+m_{c}}=\frac{1}{\frac{1}{m_{c}}+\frac{1}{m_{h}}} \Rightarrow \Delta \mu=\frac{-1}{\left(\frac{1}{m_{c}}+\frac{1}{m_{h}}\right)^{2}}\left(-\frac{1}{m_{c}^{2}} \Delta m_{c}\right)=\frac{\mu^{2}}{m_{c}^{2}} \Delta m_{c} \).

\( \Delta \nu=\frac{1}{2} \nu \frac{\mu \Delta m_{c}}{m_{c}^{2}}=\frac{1}{2} \nu \frac{\left(\Delta m_{c} / m_{c}\right)}{\left(1+\frac{m_{c}}{m_{h}}\right)} \).

Using the average value (36) for \( m_c \), we have \( \Delta m_{c} / m_{c}=2 / 36 \text {, and } m_{c} / m_{h}=36 / 1, \text { so } \).

\( \Delta \nu=\frac{1}{2} \frac{(1 / 18)}{(1+36)} \nu=\frac{1}{(36)(37)} \nu=7.51 \times 10^{-4} \nu \).
