Guest Question.

If the water table in occupies any of the positions (a) 1.25 m below ground level or (b) 1.25 m below the base level of the foundation, what will be the net safe bearing pressure?

Assume \(\gamma_{\text {sat }}=18.5 kN / m ^{3}, \gamma(\text { above WT })=17.5 kN / m ^{3}\). All the other data remain the same as given in .

Admin
Solution

Method 1—By making use of reduction factors \(R_{w 1} \text { and } R_{w 2}\) and using Eqs. (12.20) and (12.23), we may write

 

\(q_{n u}=\left(q_{u}-\gamma D_{f}\right)=c N_{c}+\gamma D_{f}\left(N_{q}-1\right)+\frac{1}{2} \gamma B N_{\gamma}\) (12.20)

 

\(q_{u}=c N_{c}+\gamma D_{f} N_{q} R_{w 1}+\frac{1}{2} \gamma B N_{\gamma} R_{w 2}\) (12.23)

 

\(q_{u}=c N_{c}+\gamma D_{f} N_{q} R_{w 1}+\frac{1}{2} \gamma B N_{\gamma} R_{w 2}\)

 

Given: \(N_{q}=41.4, N_{\gamma}=42.4 \text { and } N_{c}=57.8\)

 

Case I—When the WT is 1.25 m below the GL

From Eq. (12.24), we get \(R_{w 1}=0.813 \text { for } D_{w 1} / D_{f}=0.625, R_{w 2}=0.5 \text { for } D_{w 2} / B=0\).

By substituting the known values in the equation for \(q_{n u}\), we have

 

\(R_{w 1}=\frac{1}{2}\left(1+\frac{D_{w 1}}{D_{f}}\right)\) (12.24a)

 

\(R_{w 2}=\frac{1}{2}\left(1+\frac{D_{w 2}}{B}\right)\) (12.24b)

 

\(q_{n u}=30 \times 57.8+18.5 \times 2 \times 40.4 \times 0.813+\frac{1}{2} \times 18.5 \times 3 \times 42.4 \times 0.5=3538 kN / m ^{2}\)

 

\(q_{n a}=\frac{3538}{3}=1179 kN / m ^{2}\)

 

Case 2—When the WT is 1.25 m below the base of the foundation

 

\(R_{w 1}=1.0 \text { for } D_{w 1} / D_{f}=1, R_{w 2}=0.71 \text { for } D_{w} / B=0.42\).

 

Now the net bearing capacity is

 

\(q_{n u}=30 \times 57.8+18.5 \times 2 \times 40.4 \times 1+\frac{1}{2} \times 18.5 \times 3 \times 42.4 \times 0.71=4064 kN / m ^{2}\)

 

\(q_{n a}=\frac{4064}{3}=1355 kN / m ^{2}\)

 

Method 2— Using the equivalent effective unit weight method.

Submerged unit weight \(\gamma_{b}=18.5-9.81=8.69 kN / m ^{3}\).

Per Eq. (12.25)

The net ultimate bearing capacity is

 

\(q_{u}=c N_{c}+\gamma_{e 1} D_{f} N_{q}+\frac{1}{2} \gamma_{e 2} B N_{\gamma}\) (12.25)

 

\(q_{m u}=c N_{c}+\gamma_{e 1} D_{f}\left(N_{q}-1\right)+\frac{1}{2} \gamma_{e 2} B N_{\gamma}\)

 

Case I—When \(D_{w 1}=1.25\) m (Fig. Ex. 12.4)

From Eq. (12.26a)

 

\(\gamma_{e 1}=\gamma_{b}+\frac{D_{w 1}}{D_{f}}\left(\gamma_{m}-\gamma_{b}\right)\) (12.26a)

 

\(\gamma_{e 1}=\gamma_{b}+\frac{D_{w 1}}{D_{f}}\left(\gamma_{m}-\gamma_{b}\right)\)

 

where \(\gamma_{m}=\gamma_{\text {sat }}=18.5 kN / m ^{3}\)

 

\(\gamma_{e 1}=8.69+\frac{1.25}{2}(18.5-8.69)=14.82 kN / m ^{3}\)

 

\(\gamma_{e 2}=\gamma_{b}=8.69 kN / m ^{3}\)

 

\(q_{n u}=30 \times 57.8+14.82 \times 2 \times 40.4+\frac{1}{2} \times 8.69 \times 3 \times 42.4=3484 kN / m ^{2}\)

 

\(q_{n a}=\frac{3484}{3}=1161 kN / m ^{2}\)

 

Case 2—When \(D_{w 2}=1.25\) m (Fig. Ex. 12.4)

From Eq. (12.26b)

 

\(\gamma_{e 2}=\gamma_{b}+\frac{D_{w 2}}{B}\left(\gamma_{m}-\gamma_{b}\right)\) (12.26b)

 

\(\gamma_{e 1}=\gamma_{m}=18.5 kN / m ^{3}\)

 

\(\gamma_{e 2}=8.69+\frac{1.25}{3}(18.5-8.69)=12.78 kN / m ^{3}\)

 

\(q_{n u}=30 \times 57.8+18.5 \times 2 \times 40.4+\frac{1}{2} \times 12.78 \times 3 \times 42.4=4042 kN / m ^{2}\)

 

\(q_{n a}=\frac{4042}{3}=1347 kN / m ^{2}\)
