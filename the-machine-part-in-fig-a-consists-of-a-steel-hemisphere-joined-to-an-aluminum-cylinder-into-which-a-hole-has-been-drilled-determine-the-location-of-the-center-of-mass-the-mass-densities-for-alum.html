Guest Question.

The machine part in Fig. (a) consists of a steel hemisphere joined to an aluminum cylinder into which a hole has been drilled. Determine the location of the center of mass. The mass densities for aluminum and steel are \(2700 kg/m^{3}\) and \(7850 kg/m^{3}\), respectively.

Admin
Solution

By symmetry, we note that \(\bar{x} = \bar{z} = 0\). If the machine part were homogeneous, its center of mass would coincide with the centroid of the enclosing volume, and \(\bar{y}\) could be determined using the method of composite volumes described in Art. 8.3. Because the machine part is not homogeneous, \(\bar{y}\) must be determined by the method of composite bodies.

The part is composed of the four bodies shown in Fig. (b): the aluminum cylinder 1, plus the steel hemisphere 2, minus the aluminum cylinder 3, minus the aluminum cone 4. Because each of these bodies is homogeneous, each center of mass coincides with the centroid of the enclosing volume.

Aluminum Cylinder 1

\(m = ρV = ρπ R^{2}h = 2700π(0.030)^{2}(0.100) = 0.7634 kg\)

 

\(\bar{y}=\frac{100}{2}=50 mm\) (by symmetry)

 

\(m \bar{y} = (0.7634)(50) = 38.17 kg · mm\)

Steel Hemisphere 2

\(m=ρV=ρ\frac{2\pi }{3}R^{3}=7850\frac{2\pi }{3}(0.030)^{3}=0.4439 kg\)

 

\(\bar{y}=-\frac{3}{8}R=-\frac{3}{8}(30)=-11.25 mm\) (using Table 8.3)

 

\(m\bar{y} = (0.4439)(−11.25) = −4.994 kg · mm\)

Aluminum Cylinder 3 (to be subtracted)

\(m = −ρV = −ρπ R^{2}h = −2700π(0.0125)^{2}(0.050) = −0.066 27 kg\)

 

\(\bar{y}= 75 mm\) (by symmetry)

 

\(m \bar{y} = (−0.066 27)(75) = −4.970 kg · mm\)

Aluminum Cone 4 (to be subtracted)

\(m=-\rho V=-\rho \frac{\pi }{3}R^{2}h=-2700\frac{\pi }{3}(0.0125)^{2}(0.020)=-0.008 836 kg\)

 

\(\bar{y}=30+\frac{3}{4}(20)=45 mm\) (using Table 8.3)

 

\(m \bar{y} = (−0.008 836)(45) = −0.3976 kg · mm\)

Totals

Σm = 0.7634 + 0.4439 − 0.066 27 − 0.008 836 = 1.1322 kg

Σm \(\bar{y}\) = 38.17 − 4.994 − 4.970 − 0.3976 = 27.81 kg · mm

Therefore, the coordinates of the mass center of the machine part are

\(\bar{x}= \bar{z} = 0\) \(\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma m\bar{y}}{\Sigma m}=\frac{27.81}{1.1322}=24.6 mm\)
