Guest Question.

Determine the change in availability due to throttling of steam from 6 MPa and 400°C to 5 MPa when surroundings are at 100 kPa and 20°C. The changes in KE and PE may be considered negligible.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{From steam tables}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Initially at 6 MPa, 400°C, }h_{1} &= 3177.2 kJ/kg \\ s_{1} &= 6.5408 kJ/kg.K\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{After throttling at 5 Mpa,} \qquad & \\h_{2}&= h_{1}; \text{ in view of throttling process.}\\\text{Hence at 5 MPa and} \qquad\qquad &\\h_{2}&= 3177.2 kJ/kg\\\text{Superheated Steam table gives,}\quad & \\\text{at 5 MPa} , h_{\text{at 350°C}}&= 3068.4 kJ/kg\\\text{at 5 MPa }, h_{\text{at 400°C}}&= 3195.7 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Hence by interpolation at 5 MPa, enthalpy of 3177.2 kJ/kg will be at}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad T_{2}&= 350 + \frac{(400 – 350) × (3177.2 – 3068.4)}{(3195.7 – 3068.4)}\\T_{2}&= 392.7°C\end{aligned}\\\text{After throttling steam will be at 5 MPa, 392.7°C.}\\\text{By interpolation Entropy, }s_{2} = 6.6172 kJ/kg.K\\\text{At dead state of 20°C,}\\\begin{aligned}h_{\text{f at 20°C}}&= h_{0} = 83.96 kJ/kg\\s_{\text{f at 20°C}}&= s_{0} = 0.2966 kJ/kg\\\text{Hence availability at state 1,}\qquad\qquad &\\A_{1}&= (h_{1} – h_{0}) – T_{0} (s_{1} – s_{0}) + \frac{1}{2} (C^{2}_{1}) + g (z_{1} – z_{0})\\⇒\qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad A_{1}&= (3177.2 – 83.96) – 293(6.5408 – 0.2966) + 0 + 0\\A_{1}&= 1263.68 kJ/kg\\\text{Availability of steam after throttling,} &\\A_{2}&= (h_{2} – h_{0}) – T_{0} (s_{2} – s_{0}) + \frac{1}{2} (C^{2}_{2}) + g (z_{2} – z_{0})\\A_{2}&= (3177.2 – 83.96) – 293 (6.6172 – 0.2966)\\A_{2}&= 1241.30 kJ/kg\\\text{Change in availability = }A_{2} – A_{1}&\\&= (1241.30 – 1263.68)\\&= – 22.5 kJ/kg\\&= \textbf{22.5 kJ/kg, decrease.}\end{aligned}\)
