Assigning formal charges

The line-bond structure of acetate ion (Table 3.2) is shown below. Assign formal charges to the atoms in this ion.

Strategy
For each atom, subtract the number of electrons that surround the atom from the number of valence electrons for the neutral atom.

 

TABLE 3.2 COMMON POLYATOMIC IONS
Formula Name Formula Name
Cations \( H_{3}O^{+} \) Hydronium ion \( NH_{4}^{+} \) Ammonium ion
Anions \( OH^{-} \) Hydroxide ion \( HSO_{4}^{-} \) Hydrogensulfate (bisulfate) ion
\( CO_{3}^{2-} \) Carbonate ion \( PO_{4}^{3-} \) Phosphate ion
\( HCO_{3}^{-} \) Hydrogencarbonate (bicarbonate) ion \( HPO_{4}^{2-} \) Hydrogenphosphate ion
\( NO_{2}^{-} \) Nitrite ion \( H_{2}PO_{4}^{-} \) Dihydrogenphosphate ion
\( NO_{3}^{-} \) Nitrate ion \( Cr_{2}O_{7}^{2-} \) Dichromate ion
\( SO_{3}^{2-} \) Sulfite ion \( CH_{3}CO_{2}^{-} \) Acetate ion
\( SO_{4}^{2-} \) Sulfate ion \( CN^{-} \) Cyanide ion
Admin
Solution

Each carbon atom sees four electrons and has a formal charge of 0.

\( \text{Formal charge on C} = \ \begin{matrix}\text{number of valence electrons} \\ \text{for a neutral atom} \end{matrix} \ – \ \begin{matrix}\text{number of electrons around} \\ \text{the atom in the compound} \end{matrix} \\ \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \ \ = 4 – 4 = 0\)

Each hydrogen atom sees one electron and has a formal charge of 0.

\( \text{Formal charge on H} = \ \begin{matrix}\text{number of valence electrons} \\ \text{for a neutral atom} \end{matrix} \ – \ \begin{matrix}\text{number of electrons around} \\ \text{the atom in the compound} \end{matrix} \\ \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \ \ = 1 – 1 = 0\)

The double-bonded oxygen atom sees six electrons and has a formal charge of 0.

\( \text{Formal charge on O} = \ \begin{matrix}\text{number of valence electrons} \\ \text{for a neutral atom} \end{matrix} \ – \ \begin{matrix}\text{number of electrons around} \\ \text{the atom in the compound} \end{matrix} \\ \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \ \ = 6-6=0 \)

The remaining oxygen atom sees seven electrons and has a formal charge of 1−.

\( \text{Formal charge on O} = \ \begin{matrix}\text{number of valence electrons} \\ \text{for a neutral atom} \end{matrix} \ – \ \begin{matrix}\text{number of electrons around} \\ \text{the atom in the compound} \end{matrix} \\ \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \ \ = 6 – 7 = 1 – \)
