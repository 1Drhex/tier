Guest Question.

Estimate the viscosity of an oil contained in the annulus between two 25-cm-long cylinders, as shown in Fig. 7.6.The outer stationary cylinder is 8 cm in diameter. The 7.8-cm-diameter inner cylinder rotates at 3800 rpm when a torque of 0.12 Nm is applied. The specific gravity of the oil is 0.85. Neglect any torque due to the cylinder ends.

Admin
Solution

Assuming that the Reynolds number is less than 1700 , Eq. 7.5.19 provides

\(\begin{aligned} T &=\tau_{1} A_{1} r_{1} \\ &=\frac{2 \mu r_{2}^{2} \omega_{1}}{r_{2}^{2}-r_{1}^{2}} 2 \pi r_{1} L r_{1}=\frac{4 \pi \mu r_{1}^{2} r_{2}^{2} L \omega_{1}}{r_{2}^{2}-r_{1}^{2}} \end{aligned} (7.5.19)\)

\(\begin{aligned}\mu &=\frac{T\left(r_{2}^{2}-r_{1}^{2}\right)}{4 \pi r_{1}^{2} r_{2}^{2} L \omega_{1}} \\&=\frac{0.12 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m}\left(0.04^{2}-0.039^{2}\right) \mathrm{m}^{2}}{4 \pi \times 0.04^{2} \mathrm{~m}^{2} \times 0.039^{2} \mathrm{~m}^{2} \times 0.25 \mathrm{~m} \times(3800 \times 2 \pi / 60) \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}}=0.00312 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{s} / \mathrm{m}^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Check the Reynolds number using \(\nu=\mu / \rho\) :

\(\operatorname{Re}=\frac{\omega_{1} r_{1} \delta}{\nu}=\frac{(3800 \times 2 \pi / 60) \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s} \times 0.039 \mathrm{~m} \times 0.002 / 2 \mathrm{~m}}{0.0013 /(1000 \times 0.85) \mathrm{m}^{2} / \mathrm{s}}=845\)

This is less than 1700 so the calculation is acceptable.
