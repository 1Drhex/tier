Guest Question.

Verify an ASTM A992 W12 × 30 simply supported beam (Figure E7.2.1) in accordance with AISC, loaded by a uniform dead load of 0.40 kip/ft (5.8 kN/m) (first load case) and a uniform live load (second load case) of 0.70 kip/ft (10.2 kN/m). Loads are applied at the shear centre or on the top flange. The beam is not braced against lateral-torsional buckling along its entire length. Warping and lateral rotation free at both ends; lateral displacement and torsion prevented at both ends
Geometrical properties:
d=12.3 in (312 mm) \(A_{g} =7.79 in.^{2}(56.7) cm^{2}\)
\(b_{f}\)=6.52 in (166 mm) \(Z_{x} =43.1 in.^{3}(706.3) cm^{3})\)
\(t_{f}\)=0.44 in (11.2 mm) \(S_{x} =37.6 in.^{3}(623.5) cm^{3})\)
\(t_{w}\)=0.26 in (6.6 mm) \(Z_{y} =9.56 in.^{3}(156.7) cm^{3})\)
k=0.74 in (18.8 mm) \(S_{y} =6.24 in.^{3}(102.3) cm^{3})\)
L=17 ft (5.18 m) \(I_{x} =238 in.^{4}(9906 cm^{4})\)
\(L_{x}\)=17 ft \(I_{y} =20.3 in.^{4}( 845 cm^{4})\)
\(L_{y}\)=17 ft \(J =0.457 in.^{4}( 19 cm^{4})\)
\(L_{b}\)=17 ft \(C_{w} =720 in.^{6}( 193300 cm^{6})\)
\(r_{x}\) =5.21 in.(13.2 cm)
\(r_{y}\) =1.52 in.(3.9 cm)
Material properties:
Steel : ASTM A992 \(F_{y}\) = 50 ksi (345 MPa) \(F_{u}\) = 65 ksi (448 MPa)

Admin
Solution

Limit the live load deflection to L/360.
Loads:
\(w_{D}\) = 0.40 kip/ft (5.8 kN/m); \(w_{L}\) = 0.70 kip/ft (10.2 kN/m)
LRFD: \(w_{u}\) = 1.2 × 0.40 + 1.6 × 0.70 = 1.6 kip/ft (23.4 kN/m)
ASD: \(w_{a}\) = 0.40 + 0.70 = 1.1 kip/ft (16.1 kN/m)
The required shear strength is:
LRFD : \(V_{u} =w_{u}\)L/2=1 6×17/2 = 13.6 kips (60.5 kN)
ASD : \(V_{a}= w_{a}\)L/2=1 1×17/2 = 9.4 kips (41.8 kN)
The required flexural strength is:
LRFD :\(M_{u}=\frac{w_{u}L^{2}}{8}=\frac{1.6\times 17^{2}}{8}\)= 57.8 kip-ft (78.4 kNm)
ASD :\(M_{a}=\frac{w_{a}L^{2}}{8}=\frac{1.1\times 17^{2}}{8}\) = 39.7 kip-ft (58.9 kNm)
Section classification for local buckling.
Flange:\(b/t\)= (0.5×6.52)/0.44 = 7.41 < 0.38 \(\sqrt{E/F_{y}}\)=0.38 × \(\sqrt{29000/50}=9.15\rightarrow\) compact
Web:\(h/t_{w}\)= (12.3-2×0.74)/0.26 = 41.6 < 3.76 \(\sqrt{E/F_{y}}\)=3.76 × \(\sqrt{29000/50}=90.6\rightarrow\) compact
ASTM A992 steel W12 × 30, subjected to bending moment, is a compact section.
Check for deflection.
Compute deflection at midspan:
\(f=\frac{5}{384}\frac{w_{L}L^{4}}{EI_{x}}=\frac{5}{384}\frac{0.7/12\times (17\times 12)^{4}}{29000\times 238}=0.19 in.(0.49 cm)\)<
=17 × 12/360 = 0.57 in (1.45 cm) OK
Verification of shear.
Shear area:\(A_{w} = d.t_{w} =12.3×0.26 = 3.2 in.^{2} (20.6 cm^{2})\)
\(h/t_{w}\)= (12.3−2×0.74)/ 0.26 = 41.6 ≤ 2.24\(\sqrt{E/F_{y}}\) =2.24 × \(\sqrt{29000/50}\)=53.9
Apply Method 1 (AISC 360-10 Section G2.1).
\(C_{v}\) = 1.0
Nominal shear strength \(V_{n}\):
\(V_{n} =0.6F_{y}A_{w}C_{v} \)=0.6×50×3.2×1.0=96.0 kips (427 kN)
Compute the available shear strength.
LRFD: \(\Phi_{b}M_{n}\) =1.00 × 96.0 = 96.0 kips (427 kN) > \(V_{u}\) = 13.6 kips
ASD: \(V_{n}/\Omega_{v}\) =96.0 /1.50 = 64.0 kips (285 kNm) > \(V_{a}\) = 9.4 kips
Verification of bending.
Apply verifications of case (a):
(a) Doubly symmetrical compact I-shaped members and channels bent about their major axis.
Compute plastic moment
\(M_{p} = F_{y} × Z_{x}\)=50×43.1/12 = 179.6 kip-ft (243.5 kNm)
Flexural strength corresponding to lateral torsional buckling limit state \(M_{LT}\)):
Compute the modification factor \(C_{b}\).
\(M_{max} =M_{B}\) =57.8 kip-ft
\(M_{A}=M_{C}=\left ( \frac{wL}{2} \right ).\frac{L}{4}-\left ( \frac{wL}{4} \right )\left ( \frac{1}{2}.\frac{L}{4} \right )=\frac{3}{32}wL^{2}=\frac{3}{32}\times 1.6\times 17^{2}\)=43.4 kip-ft(58.8kNm)
\(C_{b}=\frac{12.5M_{max}}{2.5M_{max}+3M_{A}+4M_{B}+3M_{C}}=\frac{12.5\times 57.8}{2.5\times 57.8+3\times 43.4+4\times 57.8+3\times 43.4}=1.136\)
\(L_{p}=1.76r_{y}\sqrt{\frac{E}{F_{y}}}=1.76\times 1.52\times \sqrt{\frac{29000}{50}}=64.4in./12\)=5.37ft(1.64m)
\(r_{ts}=\sqrt{\frac{\sqrt{I_{y}C_{w}}}{S_{x}}}=\sqrt{\frac{\sqrt{20.3\times 720}}{38.6}}\)=1.770 in.(4.5cm)
c = 1 (doubly symmetrical I-shape)
\(h_{o} = d – t_{f}\) =12.3 – 0.44 = 11.86 in.( 301.2 mm)
\(L_{r}=1.95r_{ts}\frac{E}{0.7F_{y}}\sqrt{\frac{J.c}{S_{x}h_{0}}+\sqrt{\left ( \frac{J.c}{S_{x}h_{0}} \right )^{2}+6.76\left ( \frac{0.7F_{y}}{E} \right )^{2}}}\)
\(=1.95\times 1.770\times \frac{29000}{0.7\times 50}\sqrt{\frac{0.457\times 1}{38.6\times 11.86}+\sqrt{\left ( \frac{0.457\times 1}{38.6\times 11.86} \right )^{2}+6.76\left ( \frac{0.7\times 50}{29000} \right )^{2}}}\)
=187.3 in/12 = 15.6 ft (4.76 m)
\(L_{b}\) =17 ft > \(L_{r}\) =15.6 ft.
Hence:
(a) Consider loads applied at the shear centre.
\(F_{cr}=\frac{C_{b}\pi ^{2}E}{\left ( \frac{L_{b}}{r_{ts}} \right )^{2}}\sqrt{1+0.078\frac{J.c}{S_{x}h_{0}}\left ( \frac{L_{b}}{r_{ts}} \right )^{2}}\)
\(\frac{1.136\times \pi ^{2}\times 29000}{\left ( \frac{17.12}{1.770} \right )^{2}}\sqrt{1+0.078\frac{0.457\times 1}{38.6\times 11.86}\left ( \frac{17.12}{1.770} \right )^{2}}\)=34.9 ksi (241 MPa)
\(M_{LTB} = F_{cr}S_{x}\) =34.9 × 37.6/12 = 112.3 kip-ft (152.3kNm)
Compute the nominal flexural strength (\(M_{n}\)).
\(M_{n}=min (M_{p};M_{LTB}\) =min (179.6; 112.3) = 112.3 kip-ft (152.3 kNm)
Compute the available strength.
LRFD:\(\Phi _{b}M_{n}\)=0.90 × 112.3 = 101.1 kip-ft (137.1 kN)
ASD:\(M_{n}/\Omega _{b}\)=112.3/1.67 = 67.3 kip-ft (91.2 kN)
(b) Consider now loads applied on the top flange.
\(F_{cr}=\frac{C_{b}\pi ^{2}E}{\left ( \frac{L_{b}}{r_{ts}} \right )^{2}}=\frac{1.136\times \pi ^{2}\times 29000}{\left ( \frac{17.12}{1.770} \right )^{2}}\)=24.5 ksi (169 MPa)
\(M_{LTB} = F_{cr}S_{x}\) =24.5 × 38.6/12 = 77.7 kip-ft (106.8 kNm)
Compute the nominal flexural strength
\(M_{n} =min(M_{p};M_{LTB})\) =min (179.6; 77.7) =77.7 kip-ft (106.8 kNm)
Compute the available strength.
LRFD:\(\Phi _{b}M_{n}\)=0.90 × 78.7 = 70.9 kip-ft (96.1 kN)
ASD:\(M_{n}/\Omega _{b}\)=78.7/1.67 = 47.1 kip-ft (63.9 kN)
