Guest Question.

Consider the simple planetary gear train shown in Fig. 7.40 in which the ring gear h is fixed (that is, the angular velocity of the ring gear is ωh = 0) and the input is the planet carrier (the arm). The dimensions of ring gear h, planet gear j, sun gear k, and arm A are Rh = 200 mm, Rj = 50 mm, Rk = 100 mm, and RA = 150 mm, respectively. If the input angular velocity is ωA = ωi = 10 rad/s ccw, determine the angular velocities of the planet gear, j, and the sun gear, k.

Admin
Solution

Using Eq. (7.25), the first-order kinematic coefficient of planet gear j, in rolling contact with ring gear h, can be written as

\(\theta_{L F / A}^{\prime}=\frac{\omega_{L}-\omega_{A}}{\omega_{F}-\omega_{A}}.\) (7.25)

 

\(\theta_{j h / A}^{\prime}=\pm \frac{R_{h}}{R_{j}}=\frac{\omega_{j}-\omega_{A}}{\omega_{h}-\omega_{A}}\) (1a)

 

Similarly, the first-order kinematic coefficient of planet gear j, in rolling contact with sun gear k, can be written as

\(\theta_{j k / A}^{\prime}=\pm \frac{R_{k}}{R_{j}}=\frac{\omega_{j}-\omega_{A}}{\omega_{k}-\omega_{A}}\) (1b)

Note that if the ring gear is removed, and the arm is fixed (that is, ωA = 0), then the planetary gear train reduces to an ordinary gear train, that is, Eq. (1b) reduces to

\(\theta_{j k / A}^{\prime}=\pm \frac{R_{k}}{R_{j}}=\frac{\omega_{j}}{\omega_{k}}\) (2)

Since there is internal contact between the planet gear and the fixed ring gear, then the positive sign is used in Eq. (1a). Then, rearranging the equation and substituting ωh = 0 gives

\(R_{j} \omega_{j}=-\left(R_{h}-R_{j}\right) \omega_{A}=-R_{A} \omega_{A}\) (3)

Therefore, the angular velocity of the planet gear is

\(\omega_{j}=-\frac{R_{A} \omega_{A}}{R_{j}}=-\frac{(150 mm )(10 rad / s )}{50 mm }=-30 rad / s.\)

The negative sign indicates that the direction of the angular velocity of the planet gear is opposite to the direction of the arm and, therefore, clockwise.
Similarly, since there is external contact between the planet gear and the sun gear, the negative sign is used in Eq. (1b), and the equation can be written as

\(R_{k} \omega_{k}=-R_{j} \omega_{j}+\left(R_{k}+R_{j}\right) \omega_{A}\) (4a)

The angular velocity of the sun gear can be expressed in terms of the input angular velocity of the arm by substituting Eq. (3) into Eq. (4a) and simplifying; that is,

\(R_{k} \omega_{k}=\left(R_{k}+R_{h}\right) \omega_{A}=2 R_{A} \omega_{A}\) (4b)

Therefore, the angular velocity of the sun gear is

\(\omega_{k}=\frac{2 R_{A} \omega_{A}}{R_{k}}=\frac{2(150 mm )(10 rad / s )}{100 mm }=30 rad / s.\)

The positive result indicates that the direction of the angular velocity of the sun gear is the same as the arm and is, therefore, counterclockwise.
