Guest Question.

A flow out of a clothes dryer of 0.1 kg/s dry air is at 60°C and relative humidity 60%. It flows through a heat exchanger where it exits at 20°C. After the heat exchanger the flow combines with another flow of 0.03 kg/s dry air at 30°C and relative humidity 40%. Find the dew point of state 1, see Fig. P11.120, the heat transfer per kg dry air and the final exit state humidity ratio and relative humidity.

Admin
Solution

Use the psychrometric chart to solve the problem except for state 1.

State 1:

\(\begin{gathered}P _{ g 1}=19.941 kPa, \\P _{ v 1}=\Phi_{1} P _{ g 1}=11.965 kPa \\T _{ dew }= T _{\text {sat }}\left( P _{ v 1}\right)= 4 9 . 3 ^{ \circ } C\end{gathered}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\omega_{1} &=0.622 \frac{11.965}{100-11.965}=0.084534 \\\tilde{ h }_{1} &= h _{ a 1}- h _{ a -20 C }+\omega_{1} h _{ a 1} \\&=333.81-253.45+0.084534 \times 2609.59 \\&=301 kJ / kg \text { air } ,\end{aligned}\)

 

State 2: \(20^{\circ} C < T _{\text {dew }}\) so it is saturated

\(\omega_{2}=0.0148, \tilde{ h }_{2}=77.8 kJ / kg \text { air }\)

State 3: \(\omega_{3}=0.0078, \tilde{ h }_{3}=69.8 kJ / kg \text { air }\) ,

\(\dot{ m }_{ liq } / \dot{ m }_{ a }=\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}=0.06973 kg / kg \text { dry air }\)

Energy Eq.:

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ Q } / \dot{ m }_{ a } &=\tilde{ h }_{1}-\tilde{ h }_{2}-\left(\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}\right) h _{ f } \\&=301-77.8-0.06973 \times 83.94= 2 1 7 . 3 5 kJ / kg \text { dry air }\end{aligned}\)

Do CV around the junction where flow 2 and 3 combines to give exit at 4.

Continuity water: \(\dot{ m }_{ a1 } \omega_{2}+\dot{ m }_{ a 3} \omega_{3}=\dot{ m }_{ a 4} \omega_{4}\)

\(\begin{aligned}\omega_{4} &=\left(\dot{ m }_{ a 1} / \dot{ m }_{ a 4}\right) \omega_{2}+\left(\dot{ m }_{ a 3} / \dot{ m }_{ a 4}\right) \omega_{3} \\&=\frac{0.05}{0.08} \times 0.0148+\frac{0.03}{0.08} \times 0.0078= 0 . 0 1 2 1 7 5\end{aligned}\)

Energy Equation: \(\dot{ m }_{ a 1} \tilde{ h }_{2}+\dot{ m }_{ a 3} \tilde{ h }_{3}=\dot{ m }_{ a 4} \tilde{ h }_{4}\)

\(\begin{aligned}\tilde{ h }_{4} &=\left(\dot{ m }_{ a 1} / \dot{ m }_{ a4 }\right) \tilde{ h }_{2}+\left(\dot{ m }_{ a 3} / \dot{ m }_{ a 4}\right) \tilde{ h }_{3} \\&=\frac{0.05}{0.08} \times 77.8+\frac{0.03}{0.08} \times 69.8=74.8 kJ / kg \text { air }\end{aligned}\)

From chart given \(\left(\omega_{4}, \tilde{ h }_{4}\right)\) we get: \(\Phi_{4}= 6 5 \% \text { and } T _{4}=24^{\circ} C\)
