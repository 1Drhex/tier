Guest Question.

For the system in Fig. 20.25,

a. Find the average power, apparent power, reactive power, and \(F_p\) for each branch.

b. Find the total number of watts, volt-amperes reactive, volt-amperes, and power factor of the system. Sketch the power triangle.

c. Find the source current I.

Admin
Solution

a. Bulbs:

Total dissipation of applied power

\(P_1=12(60 W) = 720 W\\[0.5cm] Q_1=0 VAR\\[0.5cm] S_1 = P_1 = 720 VA\\[0.5cm] F_{p_1}=1\)

Heating elements:

Total dissipation of applied power

\(P_2=6.4 kW\\[0.5cm] Q_2 = 0 VAR\\[0.5cm] S_2 = P_2 = 6.4 kVA\\[0.5cm] F_{p_2}=1\)

Motor:

\(\eta =\frac{P_o}{P_i}\longrightarrow P_i=\frac{P_o}{\eta} =\frac{5(746 W)}{0.82}=4548.78 W = P_3\\[0.5cm] F_p = 0.72\; lagging \\[0.5cm] P_3 = S_3 cos \theta\longrightarrow S_3=\frac{P_3}{cos\theta}=\frac{4548.78 W}{0.72}= 6317.75 VA\\[0.5cm] Also, \theta=cos^{-1}0.72=43.95°, so\; that \\[0.5cm] Q_3 = S_3 sin \theta = (6317.75 VA)(sin 43.95°)=(6317.75 VA)(0.694) = 4384.71\; VAR (L)\)

Capacitive load:

\(I=\frac{E}{Z}=\frac{208 V ∠0°}{9 Ω – j 12 Ω} =\frac{9 Ω – j 12 Ω}{15 Ω ∠-53.13°}=13.87 A ∠53.13°\\[0.5cm] P_4=I^2R=(13.87 A)^2\cdot 9 Ω = 1731.39 W \\[0.5cm] Q_4=I^2X_C=(13.87 A)^2\cdot 12 Ω = 2308.52\; VAR(C)\\[0.5cm] S_4=\sqrt{P^{2}_{4}+Q^{2}_{4}}=\sqrt{(1731.39 W)^2+(2308.52 VAR)^2} = 2885.65 VA\\[0.5cm] F_p=\frac{P_4}{S_4}=\frac{1731.39 W}{2885.65 VA}=0.6 \; leading \)

 

b. \(P_T = P_1 + P_2 + P_3 + P_4\\[0.5cm] =720 W + 6400 W + 4548.78 W + 1731.39 W\\[0.5cm] =13,400.17 W\\[0.5cm] QT = \pm Q1 \pm Q2 \pm Q3 \pm Q4\\[0.5cm] =0 + 0 + 4384.71 \; VAR(L) – 2308.52\; VAR(C)\\[0.5cm] =2076.19\; VAR(L)\\[0.5cm] S_T=\sqrt{P^{2}_{T}+Q^{2}_{T}}= \sqrt{(13,400.17 W)^2+(2076.19 \; VAR)^2}\\[0.5cm] =13,560.06 \; VA \\[0.5cm] \\[0.5cm] F_p=\frac{P_T}{S_T}=\frac{13.4 kW}{13,560.06 VA}=0.988 \; lagging \\[0.5cm] \theta=cos^{-1}0.988=8.89° \)

Note Fig. 20.26.

c. \(S_T=EI\longrightarrow I=\frac{S_T}{E}=\frac{13,559.89 VA}{208 V}=65.19 A \)

Lagging power factor: E leads I by 8.89°, and

\(I = 65.19 A \angle −8.89°\)
