Guest Question.

A vertical plane with a width of 4.5 ft and a height of 9ft is submerged in two layers of fluid, as illustrated in Figure EP 2.37. The first fluid is oil with a specific gravity of 0.8 andadepthof3 ft, and it is open to the atmosphere. The second fluid is water and has a depth of 6ft. (a) Determine the magnitude and location of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the vertical plane in the first fluid. (b) Determine the magnitude and location of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the vertical plane in the second fluid. (c) Determine the magnitude and location of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the vertical plane in the multilayer fluid.

Admin
Solution

(a) The magnitude of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the vertical plane in the first fluid is determined by applying Equation 2.236 \( F_{1} = \int{d F_{1}} = \int{ p_{1}d A_{1}} = \int_{0}^{L}{(\gamma _{1} h)(wdh)} \), integrating over the length of the first part of the plane using the top of the free surface as a reference point for integration as follows:

\( w:= 4.5 ft \) \( L:= 9 ft \) \(\gamma_{w} := 62.417 \frac{lb}{ft^{3} } \)
\( L_{1}:= 3 ft \) \( s_{1}:= 0.8\) \(\gamma_{1} := s_{1}\gamma_{w}= 49.934 \frac{lb}{ft^{3} } \)
\( F_{1}:= \int_{0}^{ L_{1}}{(\gamma_{1}h)w dh} = 1011.155 lb\)

Alternatively, the magnitude of the resultant hydrostatic force acting onthe vertical plane in the first fluid may be determined by applying Equation 2.237 \( F_{1} = p_{ca1} A_{1}= \gamma _{1} h_{ca1} A_{1} = gamma _{1} \left(\frac{L_{1}}{2} \right) (wL_{1} )= \frac{\gamma _{1} L^{2}_{1}w }{2} \) as follows:

\( A_{1}:= wL_{1}= 13.5 ft^{2} \) \(h_{cal}:= \frac{L_{1}}{2}=1.5ft \)
\( F_{1}:= \gamma_{1} h_{cal} A_{1}=1011.155 lb\)

The location of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the vertical plane in the first fluid is determined by applying Equation 2.239 \( h_{F1}= \frac{M_{1}}{F_{1}} \) as follows:

\(h_{F1}:= \frac{\int_{0}^{ L_{1}}{h(\gamma_{1} h )wdh} }{\int_{0}^{ L_{1}}{(\gamma_{1} h )wdh}} = 2ft \)

Alternatively, the location of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the vertical plane in the first fluid may be determined by applying Equation 2.242 \( h_{F1}= \frac{I_{x-ca_{1}}}{h_{ca_{1}}}+h_{ca_{1}}= \frac{w L^{3}_{1}/12 }{(L_{1}/2)(w L_{1})} + (L_{1}/2)\) as follows:

\(I_{xca} := \frac{w.L^{3} }{12} = 10.125b ft^{4} \) \( h_{F1}:= \frac{I_{xca} }{ h_{ca1}A_{1} }+ h_{ca1} = 2ft \)

(b) The magnitude of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the vertical plane in the second fluid is determined by applying Equation 2.243 \( F_{2}= \int{dF_{2}} = \int{p_{2}dA_{2}} = \int_{0}^{L_{2}}{(\gamma _{2} h(y)+ \gamma _{1}L_{1})(wdy)} = \int_{0}^{L_{2}}{(\gamma _{2} (L_{1}+y)+ \gamma _{1}L_{1})(wdy)} \), integrating over the length of the second part of the plane using the top of the second part of the plane as a reference point for integration as follows:

\( L_{2}:=6 ft \) \(\gamma_{2} := \gamma_{w}=62.417 \frac{lb}{ft^{3} } \) \(h(y):= L_{1}+ y \)
\( F_{2}:= \int_{0}^{ L_{2}}{(\gamma_{2}h(y)+\gamma_{1} L_{1} )w dh} = 14156.176 lb\)

Alternatively,the magnitude of the resultant hydrostatic force acting onthe vertical plane in the second fluid may bedetermined by applying Equation 2.244 \( F_{2} = (p_{ca2}+ \gamma _{1} L_{1} )A_{2}= (\gamma _{2}h_{ca2} + \gamma _{1} L_{1} )A_{2} = \left( \gamma _{2} \left(L_{1} + \frac{L_{2} }{2} \right) + \gamma _{1} L_{1} \right) (w L_{2} )= \frac{w L_{2}(2 \gamma _{2} L_{1}+ \gamma _{2} L_{2} + 2 \gamma _{1} L_{1})}{2} \) as follows:

\(A_{2}:= w L_{2}= 27 ft^{2}\) \(h_{ca2}:= L_{1}+ \frac{L_{2}}{3}=6 ft \)
\( F_{2}:= (\gamma_{2} h_{ca2}+ gamma_{1}L_{1})A_{2}=14156.176 lb \).

The location of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the vertical plane in the second fluid is determined by applying Equation 2.246 \(h_{F_{2} } = \frac{M_{2} }{F_{2} } \) as follows:

\(h_{F2}:= \frac{\int_{0}^{ L_{1}+ L_{2}}{h(\gamma_{2} h+ \gamma_{1}L_{1} )wdh} }{\int_{0}^{ L_{2}}{(\gamma_{2} h(y)+ \gamma_{1}L_{1} )wdh}} = 6.357 ft \)

Alternatively, the location of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the vertical plane in the second fluid may bedetermined by applying Equation 2.249 \(h_{F_{2} } = \frac{\gamma _{2} (w x^{3}_{2} /12+w L_{2}( L_{1}+ L_{2}/2)^{2} )+ \gamma _{1} L_{1}(L_{1}+L_{2}/2)}(wL_{2}){ (\gamma _{2} ( L_{1}+L_{2}/2)+\gamma _{1} L_{1} )(w L_{2})} \) as follows:

\(I_{xca2} := \frac{w.L^{3}_{2} }{12} = 81 ft^{4} \)
\(h_{F2}:= \frac{\gamma_{2} (I_{xca2} + A_{2} h^{2}_{ca2} )+ \gamma_{1}L_{1}h_{ca2}A_{2} }{( \gamma_{2} h_{ca2}+ \gamma_{1}L_{1} )A_{2} } = 6.357 ft \)

(c) The magnitude of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the vertical plane in the multilayer fluid is computed as the vector sum of \( = F_{1} \) and \( = F_{2} \) and is determined by applying Equation 2.250 \(F = F_{1} + F_{2} \) as follows:

\(F:= F_{1}+ F_{2} = 15167.331 lb \)

The location of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the vertical plane in the multilayer fluid is located at the center of gravity (center of area) of the entire pressure prism for both fluids and is computed as the weighted average of the center of pressures (moment arms) for the individual forces, \( = F_{1} \) and \( = F_{2} \) measured from the top of the free surface and is determined by applying Equation 2.251 \( h_{F}= \frac{h_{F_{1}} F_{1} +h_{F_{2}} F_{2}}{F} \) as follows:

\(h_{F}:= \frac{h_{F1}F_{2} + h_{F2}F_{2}}{F} = 0.067 ft \)
