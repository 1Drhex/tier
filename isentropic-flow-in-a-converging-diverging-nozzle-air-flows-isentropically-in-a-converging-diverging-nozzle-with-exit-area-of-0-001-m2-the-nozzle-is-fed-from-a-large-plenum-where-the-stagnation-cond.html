ISENTROPIC FLOW IN A CONVERGING-DIVERGING NOZZLE

Air flows isentropically in a converging-diverging nozzle, with exit area of 0.001 m\(^2\). The nozzle is fed from a large plenum where the stagnation conditions are 350K and 1.0 MPa (abs). The exit pressure is 954 kPa (abs) and the Mach number at the throat is 0.68. Fluid properties and area at the nozzle throat and the exit Mach number are to be determined.

Admin
Solution

Given: Isentropic flow of air in C-D nozzle as shown:

\(\begin{aligned}T_{0} &=350 \mathrm{~K} \\p_{0} &=1.0 \mathrm{MPa}(\mathrm{abs}) \\p_{b} &=954 \mathrm{kPa}(\mathrm{abs}) \\M_{t} &=0.68 \quad A_{e}=0.001 \mathrm{~m}^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Find:

(a) Properties and area at nozzle throat.
(b) \(M_{e}\).

Solution:
Stagnation temperature is constant for isentropic flow. Thus, since

\(\frac{T_{0}}{T}=1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\)

 

then

\(T_{t}=\frac{T_{0}}{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{t}^{2}}=\frac{350 \mathrm{~K}}{1+0.2(0.68)^{2}}=320 \mathrm{~K}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad T_t}\)

 

Also, since \(p_{0}\) is constant for isentropic flow, then

\(\begin{aligned}&p_{t}=p_{0}\left(\frac{T_{t}}{T_{0}}\right)^{k /(k-1)}=p_{0}\left[\frac{1}{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{t}^{2}}\right]^{k /(k-1)} \\\quad\\&p_{t}=1.0 \times 10^{6} \mathrm{~Pa}\left[\frac{1}{1+0.2(0.68)^{2}}\right]^{3.5}=734 \mathrm{kPa}(\mathrm{abs})\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad p_t}\end{aligned}\)

 

so

\(\rho_{t}=\frac{p_{t}}{R T_{t}}=7.34 \times 10^{5} \frac{\mathrm{N}}{\mathrm{m}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}}{287 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m}} \times \frac{1}{320 \mathrm{~K}}=7.99 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad \rho_t}\)

and

\(\begin{aligned}&V_{t}=M_{t} c_{t}=M_{t} \sqrt{k R T_{t}} \\\quad\\&V_{t}=0.68\left[14 \times 287 \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}} \times 320 \mathrm{~K} \times \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}\right]^{1 / 2}=244 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad V_{t}}\end{aligned}\)

 

From Eq. 13.7 d we can obtain a value of \(A_{t} / A^{*}\)

 

\(\frac{A_{t}}{A^{*}}=\frac{1}{M_{t}}\left[\frac{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{t}^{2}}{\frac{k+1}{2}}\right]^{(k+1) / 2(k-1)}=\frac{1}{0.68}\left[\frac{1+0.2(0.68)^{2}}{1.2}\right]^{3.00}=1.11\)

 

but at this point \(A^{*}\) is not known.
Since \(M_{t}<1\), flow at the exit must be subsonic. Therefore, \(p_{e}=p_{b} .\) Stagnation properties are constant, so

\(\frac{p_{0}}{p_{e}}=\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{e}^{2}\right]^{k /(k-1)}\)

 

Solving for \(M_{e}\) gives

 

\(M_{e}=\left\{\left[\left(\frac{p_{0}}{p_{e}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}-1\right] \frac{2}{k-1}\right\}^{1 / 2}=\left\{\left[\left(\frac{1.0 \times 10^{6}}{9.54 \times 10^{5}}\right)^{0.286}-1\right](5)\right\}^{1 / 2}=0.26\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad M_{e}}\)

 

The Ts diagram for this flow is


Since \(A_{e}\) and \(M_{e}\) are known, we can compute \(A^{*}\). From Eq. 13.7 d

\(\frac{A_{e}}{A^{*}}=\frac{1}{M_{e}}\left[\frac{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{e}^{2}}{\frac{k+1}{2}}\right]^{(k+1) / 2(k-1)}=\frac{1}{0.26}\left[\frac{1+0.2(0.26)^{2}}{1.2}\right]^{3.00}=2.317\)

Thus,

\(A^{*}=\frac{A_{e}}{2.317}=\frac{0.001 \mathrm{~m}^{2}}{2.317}=4.32 \times 10^{-4} \mathrm{~m}^{2}\)

 

and

\(\begin{aligned}A_{t} &=1.110 A^{*}=(1.110)\left(4.32 \times 10^{-4} \mathrm{~m}^{2}\right) \\\quad\\&=4.80 \times 10^{-4} \mathrm{~m}^{2}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad A_{t}}\end{aligned}\)

 

This problem illustrates use of the isentropic equations, Eqs. 13.7, for flow in a C-D nozzle that is not choked.

\(\begin{gathered}\frac{p_{0}}{p}=\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\right]^{k /(k-1)} \\\quad\\\frac{T_{0}}{T}=1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\\\quad\\\frac{\rho_{0}}{\rho}=\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\right]^{1 /(k-1)} \\\quad\\\frac{A}{A^{*}}=\frac{1}{M}\left[\frac{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}}{\frac{k+1}{2}}\right]^{(k+1) / 2(k-1)}\end{gathered}\) (13.7 a,b,c,d)

  • Note that use of Eq. 13.7d allowed us to obtain the throat area without needing to first compute other properties.

The Excel workbook for this Example is convenient for performing the calculations (using either the isentropic equations or the basic equations). (The Excel add-ins for isentropic flow, on the Web site, also make calculations much easier.)
