Guest Question.

A liquid surge tank similar to the one described by Equation
\( A_{1}\frac{dh_{1}}{dt}=q_{i}-\frac{1}{R_{1}}h_{1} \)
has the transfer function model form of Equation
\( \frac{H^{\prime}_{1}(s)}{Q^{\prime}_{1}(s)}=\frac{R_{1}}{A_{1}R_{1}s+1}=\frac{K_{1}}{\tau _{1}s+1} \) :
\( \frac{H^{\prime}(s)}{Q^{\prime}_{i}(s)}=\frac{10}{50s+1} \)
where h is the tank level (m), \( q_{i} \) is the flow rate \( (m^{3}/s), \) the gain is \( 10m/m^{3}/s, \space or \space 10 s/m^{2} \), and the time constant is 50 s. The system is operating at steady state with \( \overline{q}=0.4m^{3}/s \) and \( \overline{h}=4m \) when a sinusoidal perturbation in inlet flow rate begins with amplitude \( = 0.1 m^{3} /s \) and a cyclic frequency of 0.002 cycles/s. What are the maximum and minimum values of the tank level after the flow rate disturbance has occurred for 6 min or more? What are the largest level perturbations expected as a result of this sinusoidal variation in flow rate? What is the effect of high-frequency variations, say, 0.2 cycles/s?

Admin
Solution

Note that the actual input signal q(t) is given by Equation
\( q(t) = 0.4\frac{m^{3}}{s}+(0.1 \frac{m^{3}}{s})\sin wt \),
but only the amplitude of the input deviation \( (0.1m^{3}/s) \) is required. From Equation
\( y(t) = \frac{KAw\tau}{w^{2}\tau ^{2}+1}e^{-t/\tau }+ \frac{KA}{\sqrt{w^{2}\tau ^{2}+1}}\sin( wt+Φ) \), (1)
the value of the exponential term 6 min after the start of sinusoidal forcing is
\( e^{-360/50}=e^{-7.2}<10^{-3}. \)
Thus, the effect of the exponential transient term is less than 0.1% of the disturbance amplitude and can be safely neglected. Consequently, from Equation (1) the amplitude of the output (level) perturbation is
\( \frac{KA}{\sqrt{w^{2}\tau ^{2}+1} } \)
where A is the input amplitude and ω is the frequency (in radians)=(2π) (cyclic frequency)=(6.28)(0.002) radians/s. The amplitude of the perturbation in the liquid level is

\( \frac{10(s/m^{2})(0.1m^{3}/s)}{\sqrt{[(6.28\space rad/cycles)(0.002\space cycles/s)(50s)]^{2}+1} } \)

or 0.85 m. Hence, the actual tank level varies from a minimum of 3.15 m to a maximum of 4.85 m.
The largest output deviations that can result from sinusoidal variations of amplitude \( 0.1m^{3} /s \) occur for ω → 0— that is, for very low frequencies. In this case, the deviations would be \( ±KA=±(10 s/m^{2}) (0.1 m^{3}/s)=±1 m. \) Hence, the minimum and maximum values of liquid level would be 3 and 5 m, respectively.
For high-frequency variations (0.2 cycles/s), the output amplitude approaches zero. This occurs because the rapid variations of flow rate are averaged in the tank when the residence time is sufficiently large, giving a relatively constant level.
