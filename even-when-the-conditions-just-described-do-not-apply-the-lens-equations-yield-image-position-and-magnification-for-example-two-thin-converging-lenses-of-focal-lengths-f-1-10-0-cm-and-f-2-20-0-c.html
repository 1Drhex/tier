Guest Question.

Even when the conditions just described do not apply, the lens equations yield image position and magnification. For example, two thin converging lenses of focal lengths f 1 = 10.0 cm and f 2 = 20.0 cm are separated by 20.0 cm, as illustrated in Figure 36.29. An object is placed 15.0 cm to the left of lens 1. Find the position of the ﬁnal image and the magniﬁcation of the system.

Admin
Solution

Solution First we locate the image fomed by lens 1 while ignoring lens 2 :

\( \frac{1}{t_{1}}+\frac{1}{q_{1}} =\frac{1}{f_{1}} \) \( \frac{1}{15.0 \mathrm{~cm}}+\frac{1}{q_{1}} =\frac{1}{10.0 \mathrm{~cm}}\) \( q_{1} =30.0 \mathrm{~cm}\)

where \( q_{1}\) is measured from lens 1 . A positive value for q1 means that this first image is in back of lens 1 Because \( q_{1}\) is greater than the separation between the two lenses, this image formed bylens 1 lies \( 10.0 \mathrm{~cm}\) to the right of lens 2. We take this as the object distance for the second lens, so \( t_{2}=-10.0 \mathrm{~cm},\) where distances are now measured from lens 2 :

\( \frac{1}{t_{2}}+\frac{1}{q^{2}}=\frac{1}{f_{2}}\) \( \frac{1}{-10.0 \mathrm{~cm}}+\frac{1}{q_{2}} =\frac{1}{20.0 \mathrm{~cm}}\) \( q_{2} =6.67 \mathrm{~cm}\)

The final image lies \( 6.67 \mathrm{~cm}\) to the right of lens 2 . The individual magnifications of the images are

\( M_{1}=-\frac{g_{1}}{t_{1}}=-\frac{30.0 \mathrm{~cm}}{15.0 \mathrm{~cm}}=-2.00 \) \( M_{2}=-\frac{92}{t_{2}}=-\frac{6.67 \mathrm{~cm}}{-10.0 \mathrm{~cm}}=0.667\)

The total magnification M is equal to the product \( M_{1} M_{2}=(-2.00)(0.667)=-1.33\). The final image is real because \( q_{2}\) is positive. The image is also inverted and enlarged.
