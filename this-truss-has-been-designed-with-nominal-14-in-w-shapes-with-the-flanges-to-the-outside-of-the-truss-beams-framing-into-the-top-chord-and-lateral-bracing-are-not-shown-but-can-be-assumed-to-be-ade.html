Guest Question.

This truss has been designed with nominal 14 in. W-shapes, with the flanges to the outside of the truss. Beams framing into the top chord and lateral bracing are not shown but can be assumed to be adequate.
Based on multiple load cases, the critical dead and live load forces for this connection were determined. A typical top chord connection and the dead load and live load forces are shown below in detail A. Design this typical connection using 1-in. diameter ASTM A325 slipcritical bolts in oversized holes with a class-A faying surface
Note: slip critical bolts in oversized holes were selected for this truss to facilitate field erection.

Material Properties:
\(\begin{array}{llll}\text { Beam W-shapes } & \text { ASTM A992 } & F_{y}=50 ksi & F_{u}=65 ksi \\\text { Gusset Plates } & \text { ASTM A36 } & F_{y}=36 ksi & F_{u}=58 ksi\end{array}\)

\(d_{\text {bolt }}\) = 1 in.
ASTM A325-SC bolts Section J3.8
Class A faying surface
\(\mu\) = 0.35 (class A)
\(d_{ hg }\) = 1.25 in. (diameter of holes at gusset plates)
\(h_{ sc }\) = 0.85 (oversized and short-slotted holes)
\(T_{ b }\) = 51.0 kips
\(D_{ u }\) = 1.13 Table J3.1

Slip prevention up to the required strength level is selected in this case to prevent truss deflections that could permit a significant ponding condition for which the truss was not designed.

\(R_{ n }=\mu D_{ u } h_{ sc } T_{ b } N_{s}=0.35(1.13)(0.85)(51.0 kips )(1)=17.1 kips\)
