Replacing a Heat Pipe by a Copper Rod

A 30-cm-long cylindrical heat pipe having a diameter of 0.6 cm is dissipating heat at a rate of 180 W, with a temperature difference of \( 3^{\circ}C \) across the heat pipe, as shown in Figure 10–38. If we were to use a 30-cm-long copper rod in-stead to remove heat at the same rate, determine the diameter and the mass of the copper rod that needs to be installed.

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION A cylindrical heat pipe dissipates heat at a specified rate. The diameter and mass of a copper rod that can conduct heat at the same rate are to be determined.

Assumptions Steady operating conditions exist.

Properties The properties of copper at room temperature are \( \rho = 8950 kg/m^{3} \) and \( k = 386 W/m \cdot ^{\circ}C. \)

Analysis The rate of heat transfer \( \dot{Q} \) through the copper rod can be expressed as

\( \dot{Q} = kA \frac{\Delta T}{L} \)

 

where k is the thermal conductivity, L is the length, and \(\Delta T\) is the temperature difference across the copper bar. Solving for the cross-sectional area A and substituting the specified values gives

\( A = \frac{L}{k \Delta T} \dot{Q} = \frac{0.3 m}{\left ( 386 W/m \cdot ^{\circ}C \right )\left ( 3 ^{\circ}C \right )} \left ( 180 W \right ) \)

 

\( = 0.04663 m^{2} = 466.3 cm^{2} \)

 

Then the diameter and the mass of the copper rod become

\( A = \frac{1}{4} \pi D^{2} \rightarrow D = \sqrt{4 A/\pi } = \sqrt{4 \left ( 466.3 cm^{2} \right )/\pi } = 24.4 cm\)

 

\( m = \rho V = \rho AL = \left ( 8590 kg/m^{3} \right )\left ( 0.04663 m^{2} \right )\left ( 0.3 m \right )\)

 

\( = 125.2 kg \)

 

Therefore, the diameter of the copper rod needs to be almost 25 times that of the heat pipe to transfer heat at the same rate. Also, the rod would have a mass of 125.2 kg, which is impossible for an average person to lift.
