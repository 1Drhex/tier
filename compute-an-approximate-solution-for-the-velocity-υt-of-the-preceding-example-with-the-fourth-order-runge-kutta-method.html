Guest Question.

Compute an approximate solution for the velocity υ(t) of the preceding example with the fourth-order Runge-Kutta method.

Admin
Solution

We divide the time interval into 6 equidistant increments:

\(t_{i}\) = iΔ t with i = 0, …, 6 \(\rightarrow \Delta t=\frac{120-0}{6}=20\) .

At time t = 0 (i = 0) we set the initial condition \(\upsilon _{0}=\upsilon_{ s}=10 \). Subsequently we compute the values \(k_{1} ,k_{2},k_{3},k_{4}\):

\(k_{1}=f\left[t_{0},\upsilon _{0} \right]=2-0.0005\cdot 10^{2}=1.950\) ,

\(k_{2}=f\left[t_{0}+\frac{\Delta t}{2},\upsilon _{0} +\frac{\Delta t}{2}k_{1} \right]= 2− 0.0005 · (10 + 20/2 · 1.950)^{2} = 1.5649 \) ,

\(k_{3}=f\left[t_{0}+\frac{\Delta t}{2} ,x_{0}+\frac{\Delta t}{2} k_{2} \right]= 2− 0.0005 · (10 + 20/2 · 1.5649)^{2} = 1.6711 \) ,

\(k_{4}=f\left[t_{i}+\Delta t,x_{i}+\Delta t k_{3} \right]= 2− 0.0005 · (10 + 20 · 1.6711)^{2} = 1.0573 \) .

Thus the k-value is

\(k=\frac{1}{6}\left(k_{1}+2k_{2}+2k_{3}+k_{4} \right)= 1.5799\) ,

and we obtain at t = 20 (i = 1) the velocity

\(\upsilon _{1}=\upsilon _{0}+k\Delta t\rightarrow \upsilon _{1}=10+1.5799 · 20 = 41.597\) .

This procedure has to be repeated until the end of the time interval is reached. We summarize the computed values for a discretization of the time interval \([t_{s},t_{e} ]\) with n = 6 equidistant time increments in Table 7.4.

A comparison of the approximate solution with the exact one at the discrete times t = 40 s and t = 120 s reveals that the error is less than 1% in each case.
