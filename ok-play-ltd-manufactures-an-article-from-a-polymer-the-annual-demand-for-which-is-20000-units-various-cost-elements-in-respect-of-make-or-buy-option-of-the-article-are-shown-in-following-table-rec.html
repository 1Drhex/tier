Guest Question.

OK Play Ltd manufactures an article from a polymer, the annual demand for which is 20,000 units. Various cost elements in respect of make or buy option of the article are shown in following table. Recommend the best option.

Buy

Make
Unit cost of item (₹)

85

52
Procurement cost/order (₹)

750
Set-up cost/set-up (₹)

1,000
Carrying cost/per item/per year (₹)

25

12
Annual rate of production

35,000
Admin
Solution

Make option:
\(C_{s}\) = ₹1,000/set-up
\(C_{c}\) = ₹12/unit/year
D = 20,000 units/year
r = 35,000 units/year
P = ₹52/unit

 

\(Q_{P} =\sqrt{\frac{2C_{s} D}{C_{c} (1 – D/r)} } \)

\(Q_{P} = \sqrt{\frac{2 × 1,000 × 20,000 }{12(1 – 20,000/ 35,000)} } = 2,788.867 ≈ 2,789 units (approximately) \)

\( TC_{P} = D × P + \frac{D C_{o}}{Q_{P}} + C_{c} (r – D) \frac{Q_{P}}{2 × r} \\= 20,000 × 52 +\frac{20,000 × 12}{2,789} + 12 × (35,000 – 20,000) × \frac{2,789}{2 × 35,000} \\= ₹10,47,257.77\)

Buy option:

\(C_{o} = ₹750/order\)
\(C_{c} = ₹25/unit/year\)
D = 20,000 units/year
P = ₹85/unit

\(Q_{o} = \sqrt{\frac{2C_{o} D }{C_{c}} } \)

\(Q_{o} = \sqrt{\frac{2 × 750× 20,000}{25} } = 1,095.45 ≈ 1,096 units (approximately)\)

\( TC_{o} = D × P + \frac{D C_{o}}{Q_{o}} + \frac{Q_{o} × C_{c}}{2} \\=20,000 × 85 +\frac{20,000 × 750}{1,096} + \frac{1,096 × 25}{2}\\=₹17,27,386.131\)

From the above calculations, it can be seen that the cost of making is less than the cost of buying.
Therefore, the company should opt for making the item in-house rather than buying it from outside vendor.
