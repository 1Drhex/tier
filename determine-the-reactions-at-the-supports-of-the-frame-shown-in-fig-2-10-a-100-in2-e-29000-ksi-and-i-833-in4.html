Guest Question.

Determine the reactions at the supports of the frame shown in Fig. 2.10. A = 100 \(in^{2}\), E = 29000 ksi and I = 833 \(in^{4}\).

Admin
Solution

Step 1

The fixed-end moments are calculated using Table 1A found in the appendix

\(M_{FAB}=-\frac{4*8^{2} }{12}=-21.33^{K-ft} \)

 

\(M_{FBA}=\frac{4*8^{2} }{12}=21.33^{K-ft}\)

Step 2

The unknown displacements are\(\theta _{B},\theta _{C}\)and\(\Psi _{BC}\). KI=3

Step 3

The slope-deflection equations for this structure can be written as

\(M_{AB}=2E\frac{I}{8}\left[2\left(0\right)+\theta _{B} \right]-21.33=\frac{EI}{4}\theta _{B}-21.33 \) (1)

(Note:\( \theta _{B} = 0\) due to fixed support at A)

\(M_{BA}=2E\frac{I}{8}\left[2\theta _{B} +0 \right]+21.33=\frac{EI}{4}\theta _{B}+21.33\) (2)

 

\(M_{BC}=2E\frac{I}{10}\left[2\theta _{B}+\theta _{C}-\Psi _{BC} \right] \) (3)

 

\(M_{CB}=2E\frac{I}{10}\left[2\theta _{C}+\theta _{B}-\Psi _{BC} \right]\) (4)

Step 4

Moment equilibrium required: \(M_{BA}+M_{BC}=0 \) (5)

Roller support at \(C\rightarrow M_{CB}=0\) (6)

Due to symmetrical loading there is no moment in member BC, but the structure will still sidesway. Hence,

\(M_{BC}=0\) (7)

Step 5

From Equations (5) and(7)\(\rightarrow\)

\(M_{BA}=0\)

Substitute in (2) \(\rightarrow\)

\(\frac{EI}{2}\theta _{B}+21.33=0\rightarrow \theta _{B}=-\frac{42.66}{EI}\)

Step 6

Substituting the value of the rotation back into expressions for end moments calculated in step 3, the end moments can be expressed as,

\(M_{AB}=-32^{K-ft} \)

Step 7 and Step 8

The reactions at A\(\left(A_{x}andA_{y} \right)\)and\(C_{y}\)are calculated from principles of statics:

\(\overset{+}{\rightarrow }\sum{F_{X} }=0\rightarrow A_{X}=0\)

 

\(\sum{M_{A} }=0\)

 

\(-32+32*4-Cy*8=0\rightarrow C_{y}=12K\left(\uparrow \right)\)

 

\(+\uparrow \sum{F_{y} }=0:\)

 

\(A_{y}+12-32=0\rightarrow A_{y}=20K\left(\uparrow \right) \)

After the reactions are obtained, the Bending Moment Diagram (BMD) and the Shear Force Diagram (SFD) can be drawn as needed.
