A gas mixture at 20째C, 125 kPa is 50% \(N _{2}\), 30% \(H _{2}O\) and 20% \(O _{2}\) on a mole basis. Find the mass fractions, the mixture gas constant and the volume for 5 kg of mixture.

Admin
Solution

The conversion follows the definitions and identities:

From Eq.11.3: \(c _{ i }= y _{ i } M _{ i } / \sum y _{ j } M _{ j }\)

From Eq.11.5:

\(\begin{aligned}M _{\operatorname{mix}}=\sum y _{ j } M _{ j }=& 0.5 \times 28.013+0.3 \times 18.015+0.2 \times 31.999 \\&=14.0065+5.4045+6.3998=25.811\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}& c _{ N 2}=14.0065 / 25.811=0.5427, \quad c _{ H 2 O }=5.4045 / 25.811=0.2094 \\& c _{ O 2}=6.3998 / 25.811=0.2479, \quad \text { sums to } 1 \quad OK\end{aligned}\)

From Eq.11.14:

\(\begin{aligned}& R _{\operatorname{mix}}=\overline{ R } / M _{\operatorname{mix}}=8.3145 kJ / kmol – K / 25.811 kg / kmol = 0 . 3 2 2 1 ~ k J / k g K \\&V=m R_{\operatorname{mix}} T / P=5 kg \times 0.3221 kJ / kg – K \times 393.15 K / 125 kPa = 5 . 0 6 5 ~ m ^{3}\end{aligned}\)

Eq.11.3: \(c_{i}=\frac{m_{i}}{m_{ tot }}=\frac{n_{i} M_{i}}{\sum n_{j} M_{j}}=\frac{n_{i} M_{i} / n_{ tot }}{\sum n_{j} M_{j} / n_{ tot }}=\frac{y_{i} M_{i}}{\sum y_{j} M_{j}}\)

Eq.11.5: \(M_{\operatorname{mix}}=\frac{m_{ tot }}{n_{ tot }}=\frac{\sum n_{i} M_{i}}{n_{ tot }}=\sum y_{i} M_{i}\)

Eq.11.14: \(R_{\operatorname{mix}}=\frac{1}{m}\left(\frac{P V}{T}\right)=\frac{1}{m}(n \bar{R})=\bar{R} / M_{\operatorname{mix}}\)
