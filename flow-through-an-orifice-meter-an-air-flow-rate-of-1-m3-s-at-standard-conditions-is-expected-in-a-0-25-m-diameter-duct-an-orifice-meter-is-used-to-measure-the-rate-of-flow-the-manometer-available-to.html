FLOW THROUGH AN ORIFICE METER

An air flow rate of 1 m\(^3\)/s at standard conditions is expected in a 0.25-m diameter duct. An orifice meter is used to measure the rate of flow. The manometer available to make the measurement has a maximum range of 300 mm of water. What diameter orifice plate should be used with corner taps? Analyze the head loss if the flow area at the vena contracta is \(A_2 = 0.65 A_t\). Compare with data from Fig. 8.24.

Admin
Solution

Given: Flow through duct and orifice as shown.

Find:

(a) \(D_t\).
(b) Head loss between sections 1 and 2 .
(c) Degree of agreement with data from Fig. 8.24.
Solution:
The orifice plate may be designed using Eq. 8.56 and data from Fig. 8.21.
Governing equation:

\(\dot m_{actual}=KA_t\sqrt{2\rho(P_1-P_2)}\) (8.56)

Assumptions:

(1) Steady flow.
(2) Incompressible flow.

Since \(A_{t} / A_{1}=\left(D_{t} / D_{1}\right)^{2}=\beta^{2}\),

\(\dot{m}_{\text {actual }}=K \beta^{2} A_{1} \sqrt{2 \rho\left(p_{1}-p_{2}\right)}\)
or

\(\begin{aligned}K \beta^{2} &=\frac{\dot{m}_{\text {actual }}}{A_{1} \sqrt{2 \rho\left(p_{1}-p_{2}\right)}}=\frac{\rho Q}{A_{1} \sqrt{2 \rho\left(p_{1}-p_{2}\right)}}=\frac{Q}{A_{1}} \sqrt{\frac{\rho}{2\left(p_{1}-p_{2}\right)}} \\&=\frac{Q}{A_{1}} \sqrt{\frac{\rho}{2 g \rho_{\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}} \Delta h}} \\&=1 \frac{\mathrm{m}^{3}}{\mathrm{~s}} \times \frac{4}{\pi} \frac{1}{(0.25)^{2} \mathrm{~m}^{2}}\left[\frac{1}{2} \times 1.23 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}} \times \frac{\mathrm{s}^{2}}{9.81 \mathrm{~m}} \times \frac{\mathrm{m}^{3}}{999 \mathrm{~kg}} \times \frac{1}{0.30 \mathrm{~m}}\right]^{1 / 2} \end{aligned}\)

 

\(K \beta^{2} =0.295 \quad \text { or } \quad K=\frac{0.295}{\beta^{2}}\) (1)

Since \(K\) is a function of both \(\beta\) (Eq. 1) and \(\operatorname{Re}_{D_{1}}\) (Fig. 8.21), we must iterate to find \(\beta\). The duct Reynolds number is

\(\begin{aligned}&\operatorname{Re}_{D_{1}}=\frac{\rho \bar{V}_{1} D_{1}}{\mu}=\frac{\rho\left(Q / A_{1}\right) D_{1}}{\mu}=\frac{4 Q}{\pi \nu D_{1}} \\&\operatorname{Re}_{D_{1}}=\frac{4}{\pi} \times 1 \frac{\mathrm{m}^{3}}{\mathrm{~s}} \times \frac{\mathrm{s}}{1.46 \times 10^{-5} \mathrm{~m}^{2}} \times \frac{1}{0.25 \mathrm{~m}}=3.49 \times 10^{5}\end{aligned}\)

Guess \(\beta=0.75\). From Fig. 8.21, \(K\) should be 0.72. From Eq. 1,

\(K=\frac{0.295}{(0.75)^{2}}=0.524\)

 

Thus our guess for \(β\) is too large. Guess \(β = 0.70\). From Fig. 8.21, \(K\) should be 0.69. From Eq. 1,

\(K=\frac{0.295}{(0.70)^2}=0.602\)

 

Thus our guess for \(\beta\) is still too large. Guess \(\beta=0.65\). From Fig. 8.21, \(K\) should be 0.67. From Eq. 1,

\(K=\frac{0.295}{(0.65)^{2}}=0.698\)

There is satisfactory agreement with \(\beta \simeq 0.66\) and

\(D_{t}=\beta D_{1}=0.66(0.25 \mathrm{~m})=0.165 \mathrm{~m}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad D_t}\)

 

To find the permanent head loss for this device, we could simply use the diameter ratio \(\beta \approx 0.66\) in Fig. 8.24; but instead we will find it from the given data. To evaluate the permanent head loss, apply Eq. 8.29 between sections (1) and (3).
Governing equation:

\(\left(\frac{p_{1}}{\rho}+\alpha_{1} \cancel{\frac{\bar{V}_{1}^{2}}{2}}+\cancel{g z_{1}}\right)-\left(\frac{p_{3}}{\rho}+\alpha_{3} \cancel{\frac{\bar{V}^{2}_3}{2}}+\cancel{g z_{3}}\right)=h_{l_{T}}\)

Assumptions:
(3) \(\alpha_{1} \bar{V}_{1}^{2}=\alpha_{3} \bar{V}_{3}^{2}\).
(4) Neglect \(\Delta z\).
Then

\(h_{l_{T}}=\frac{p_{1}-p_{3}}{\rho}=\frac{p_{1}-p_{2}-\left(p_{3}-p_{2}\right)}{\rho}\)

Equation 2 indicates our approach: We will find \(p_{1}-p_{3}\) by using \(p_{1}-p_{2}=300 \mathrm{~mm} \mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}\), and obtain a value for \(p_{3}-p_{2}\) by applying the \(x\) component of the momentum equation to a control volume between sections (2) and (3).

Governing equation:

\(F_{S_{x}}+\cancel{F_{B_{x}}}=\cancel{\frac{\partial}{\partial t}} \int_{\mathrm{CV}} u \rho d \forall+\int_{\mathrm{CS}} u \rho \vec{V} \cdot d \vec{A}\) (4.18a)

Assumptions:

(5) \(F_{B_{x}}=0\)
(6) Uniform flow at sections (2) and (3).
(7) Pressure uniform across duct at sections (2) and (3).
(8) Neglect friction force on CV.
Then, simplifying and rearranging,

\(\left(p_{2}-p_{3}\right) A_{1}=u_{2}\left(-\rho \bar{V}_{2} A_{2}\right)+u_{3}\left(\rho \bar{V}_{3} A_{3}\right)=\left(u_{3}-u_{2}\right) \rho Q=\left(\bar{V}_{3}-\bar{V}_{2}\right) \rho Q\)

or

\(p_{3}-p_{2}=\left(\bar{V}_{2}-\bar{V}_{3}\right) \frac{\rho Q}{A_{1}}\)

 

Now \(\bar{V}_{3}=Q / A_{1}\), and

\(\bar{V}_{2}=\frac{Q}{A_{2}}=\frac{Q}{0.65 A_{t}}=\frac{Q}{0.65 \beta^{2} A_{1}}\)

Thus,

\(\begin{aligned}&p_{3}-p_{2}=\frac{\rho Q^{2}}{A_{1}^{2}}\left[\frac{1}{0.65 \beta^{2}}-1\right] \\&p_{3}-p_{2}=1.23 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}} \times(1)^{2} \frac{\mathrm{m}^{6}}{\mathrm{~s}^{2}} \times \frac{4^{2}}{\pi^{2}} \frac{1}{(0.25)^{4} \mathrm{~m}^{4}}\left[\frac{1}{0.65(0.66)^{2}}-1\right] \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}{\mathrm{~kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}} \\&p_{3}-p_{2}=1290 \mathrm{~N} / \mathrm{m}^{2}\end{aligned}\)

The diameter ratio, \(\beta\), was selected to give maximum manometer deflection at maximum flow rate.

Thus

\(p_{1}-p_{2}=\rho_{\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}} g \Delta h=999 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}} \times 9.81 \frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}^{2}} \times 0.30 \mathrm{~m} \times \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}{\mathrm{~kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}}=2940 \mathrm{~N} / \mathrm{m}^{2}\)

Substituting into Eq. 2 gives

\(\begin{aligned}&h_{l_{T}}=\frac{p_{1}-p_{3}}{\rho}=\frac{p_{1}-p_{2}-\left(p_{3}-p_{2}\right)}{\rho} \\&h_{l_{T}}=(2940-1290) \frac{\mathrm{N}}{\mathrm{m}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{m}^{3}}{1.23 \mathrm{~kg}}=1340 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m} / \mathrm{kg} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad {h}_{l_{T}}}\end{aligned}\)

To compare with Fig. 8.24, express the permanent pressure loss as a fraction of the meter differential

 

\(\frac{P_1-P_3}{P_1-P_2}=\frac{(2940 – 1290) N/m^2}{2940 N/m^2}=0.561\)

 

The fraction from Fig. 8.24 is about 0.57. This is satisfactory agreement!

 

This problem illustrates flow meter calculations and shows use of the momentum equation to compute the pressure rise in a sudden expansion.
