Guest Question.

A long copper rod of thermal diffusivity λ is heated at one end with a flame passing underneath it periodically, while the other end is located so far away that it remains at room temperature \( T_0 \). Consider the rod as a one-dimensional system with a periodic temperature variation of amplitude ΔT at x = 0, given by,

\( T (0, t) = T_0 +ΔT \cos (ω t) \).

where x is the the spatial coordinate along the wire. Once the rod has reached a regime at which every point of the rod has a periodic temperature variation, show that the temperature profile is given by,

\( T (x, t) = T_0 +ΔT \exp\bigl(-\frac{x}{d}\bigr) \cos \exp\bigl(ω t-\frac{x}{d}\bigr) \) where \( d =\sqrt{\frac{2\lambda }{\omega } } \).

The temperature oscillation at position x is dephased by an angle −x/d with respect to the oscillation at position x = 0. The amplitude of oscillation is attenuated by a factor exp (−x/d).

Admin
Solution

To show that the temperature profile T (x, t) is a solution of the heat diffusion equation (11.37), we compute the partial derivatives of this function. The partial derivative of the temperature T with respect to t is given by,

\( \frac{\partial T}{\partial t} T (x, t) = λ \frac{\partial ^2}{\partial x^2} T (x, t) \). (11.37)

\( \frac{\partial T}{\partial t} = \Delta T\exp \Bigl(-\frac{x}{d}\Bigr) \frac{\partial}{\partial t} \biggl(\cos \Bigl(\omega t -\frac{x}{d} \Bigr) \biggr) \).

\( = −ω ΔT \exp \Bigl(-\frac{x}{d}\Bigr) \sin \Bigl(\omega t -\frac{x}{d} \Bigr) \).

\( = – \frac{2 λ ΔT}{d^2}\exp \Bigl(-\frac{x}{d}\Bigr) \sin \Bigl(\omega t -\frac{x}{d} \Bigr) \).

The first-order partial derivative of the temperature T with respect to x yields,

\( \frac{\partial T}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \biggl(\Delta T \exp \Bigl(-\frac{x}{d}\Bigr) \cos \Bigl(\omega t -\frac{x}{d}\Bigr) \biggr) \).

\( = – \frac{\Delta T}{d} \exp \Bigl(-\frac{x}{d}\Bigr) \biggl(\cos \Bigl(\omega t -\frac{x}{d} \Bigr)- \sin \Bigl(\omega t-\frac{x}{d}\Bigr) \biggr) \).

which implies that the product of λ and the second-order partial derivative of the temperature T with respect to x is given by,

\(\lambda \frac{\partial^2 T}{\partial x^2} = \lambda \frac{\partial }{\partial x}\Bigl(\frac{\partial T }{\partial x }\Bigr) \).

\( = – \frac{\lambda \Delta T}{d} \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \Biggl(\exp \Bigl(-\frac{x}{d} \Bigr) \biggl(\cos \bigl(\omega t -\frac{x}{d}\bigr) -\sin \bigl(\omega t -\frac{x}{d}\bigr) \biggr) \Biggr) \).

\( = \frac{\lambda \Delta T}{d^2} \exp \bigl(-\frac{x}{d}\bigr) \Bigl(\cos \bigl(\omega t – \frac{x}{d}\bigr) -\sin \bigl(\omega t -\frac{x}{d}\bigr) -\sin \bigl(\omega t -\frac{x}{d}\bigr) -\cos \bigl(\omega t -\frac{x}{d}\bigr)\Bigr) \).

\( = – \frac{2\lambda \Delta T}{d^2} \exp \bigl(-\frac{x}{d}\bigr) \sin \bigl(\omega t -\frac{x}{d}\bigr) \).

Thus, we find that the expressions for ∂T/∂t and \( λ∂^2T/∂x^2 \) are identical, which establishes that the temperature profile T (x, t) is a solution of the heat diffusion equation.
