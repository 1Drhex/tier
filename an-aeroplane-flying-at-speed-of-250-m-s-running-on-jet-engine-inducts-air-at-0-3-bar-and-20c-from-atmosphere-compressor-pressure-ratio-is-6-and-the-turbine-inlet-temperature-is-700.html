Guest Question.

An aeroplane flying at speed of 250 m/s, running on jet engine inducts air at 0.3 bar and –20°C from atmosphere, compressor pressure ratio is 6 and the turbine inlet temperature is 700 °C. Velocity at exit of turbine is 180 m/s, turbine and compressor efficiencies are 0.85 each and jet engine efficiency is 90%. Thurst power produced is 800kW, and calorific value of fuel is 43000 kJ/kg. Considering following properties for air and gas determine overall efficiency, rate of air consumption, power developed by turbine, and exit area of jet.
\(\text{For air, }C_{p, air} = 1.005 kJ/kg·K, γ_{air} = 1.4, R = 0.287 kJ/kg·K\\\text{For gas,} C_{p, gas} = 1.087 kJ/kg·K, γ_{gas} = 1.33\)

Admin
Solution

\(\text{Let mass flow rate of air and fuel be }m_{air} \& m_{fuel} kg/s\\\text{Writing down energy balance on combustion chamber;}\\(m_{fuel} × CV) + (m_{air} · C_{pair} · T_{2^{′}}) = (m_{fuel} + m_{air}) · C_{pgas} . T_{3}\\\text{In diffuser}\\\begin{aligned}T_{1}&= T_{0} + \frac{C_{0}^{2}}{2C_{pair}} = 253 + \frac{(250)^{2}}{2 × 1005} = 284.09 K\\p_{1}&= p_{0} \left\lgroup\frac{T_{1}}{T_{0}} \right\rgroup^{γ/(γ – 1)} = 0.31 bar\\\text{Let, }&\left\lgroup\frac{m_{fuel}}{m_{air}} = FA\right\rgroup \end{aligned} \\\text{Thus energy balance on combustion chamber;}\\FA × CV = (1 + FA)C_{p, gas} · T_{3} – C_{p, air} T_{2^{′}}\\\text{In compressor, }T_{2} = T_{1}(6)^{0.286} = 474.25 K\\\begin{aligned}T_{2^{′}}&= T_{1} + \frac{(T_{2} – T_{1})}{η_{compr.}}\\&= \frac{284.09 + (474.25 – 284.09)}{0.85}\\T_{2^{′}}&= 507.81 K, T_{3} = 973 K\\p_{2} = p_{1} × \text{pressure ratio} = 0.31& × 6 = 1.86 bar = p_{3}\\\text{Substituting in energy balance }&\text{on combustion chamber;}\\FA × 43000&= \left\{(1 + FA) ×
