Guest Question.

The mass of an electric motor is 120 kg and it runs at 1500 r.p.m. The armature mass is 35 kg and its C.G. lies 0.5 mm from the axis of rotation. The motor is mounted on five springs of negligible damping so that the force transmitted is one-eleventh of the impressed force. Assume that the mass of the motor is equally distributed among the five springs.
Determine : 1. stiffness of each spring; 2. dynamic force transmitted to the base at the operating speed; and 3. natural frequency of the system.

Admin
Solution

Given \(m_{1}=120 kg ; m_{2}=35 kg ; \quad r=0.5 mm =5 \times 10^{-4} m ; \quad \varepsilon=1 / 11\) ; N = 1500 r.p.m. or \(\omega=2 \pi \times 1500 / 60=157.1 rad / s\) ;

1. Stiffness of each spring

Let s = Combined stiffness of the spring in N-m, and

\(\omega_{n}\) = Natural circular frequency of vibration of the machine in rad/s.

We know that transmissibility ratio \((\varepsilon)\),

\(\frac{1}{11}=\frac{1}{\left(\frac{\omega}{\omega_{n}}\right)^{2}-1}=\frac{\left(\omega_{n}\right)^{2}}{\omega^{2}-\left(\omega_{n}\right)^{2}}=\frac{\left(\omega_{n}\right)^{2}}{(157.1)^{2}-\left(\omega_{n}\right)^{2}}\)

 

or \((157.1)^{2}-\left(\omega_{n}\right)^{2}=11\left(\omega_{n}\right)^{2} \quad \text { or } \quad\left(\omega_{n}\right)^{2}=2057 \text { or } \omega_{n}=45.35 rad / s\)

 

We know that \(\omega_{n}=\sqrt{s / m_{1}}\)

 

\(s=m_{1}\left(\omega_{n}\right)^{2}=120 \times 2057=246840 N / m\)

 

Since these are five springs, therefore stiffness of each spring

\(=246840 / 5=49368 N / m\)

 

2. Dynamic force transmitted to the base at the operating speed (i.e. 1500 r.p.m. or 157.1 rad/s)

We know that maximum unbalanced force on the motor due to armature mass,

\(F=m_{2} \omega^{2} \cdot r=35(157.1)^{2} 5 \times 10^{-4}=432 N\)

\(\therefore \) Dynamic force transmitted to the base,

\(F_{ T }=\varepsilon \cdot F=\frac{1}{11} \times 432=39.27 N\)

 

3. Natural frequency of the system

We have calculated above that the natural frequency of the system,

\(\omega_{n}=45.35 rad / s\)
