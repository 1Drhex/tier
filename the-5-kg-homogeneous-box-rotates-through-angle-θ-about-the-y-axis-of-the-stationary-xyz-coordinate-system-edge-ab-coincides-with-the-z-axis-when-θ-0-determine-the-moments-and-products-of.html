Guest Question.

The 5-kg homogeneous box rotates through angle \(\theta\) about the \(Y\) axis of the stationary \(X Y Z\) coordinate system. Edge \(A B\) coincides with the \(Z\) axis when \(\theta=0 .\) Determine the moments and products of inertia with respect to \(X Y Z\) as functions of \(\theta\).

Admin
Solution

This problem has the obvious purpose of illustrating the transformation of inertia properties, but the results also help us understand some general aspects. In the second drawing \(x^{\prime} y^{\prime} z^{\prime}\) is a body-fixed coordinate system having origin \(A\) whose axes are aligned with the edges of the box. It is parallel to the centroidal system of a rectangular parallelepiped described in the Appendix.

The coordinates of corner \(A\) relative to the parallel centroidal coordinate system are the components of the relative position vector,

\(\bar{r}_{A / G}=-0.75 \bar{i}^{\prime}-1 \bar{j}^{\prime}-0.5 \bar{k}^{\prime} \mathrm{m}.\)

Applying the parallel axis transformation to the tabulated properties gives

\(\begin{array}{c}I_{x^{\prime} x^{\prime}}=\frac{1}{12}(5)\left(2^{2}+1^{2}\right)+5\left[(-1)^{2}+(-0.5)^{2}\right]=8.3333, \\\\ I_{y^{\prime} y^{\prime}}=\frac{1}{12}(5)\left(1.5^{2}+1^{2}\right)+5\left[(-0.75)^{2}+(-0.5)^{2}\right]=5.4167, \\\\ I_{z^{\prime} z^{\prime}}=\frac{1}{12}(5)\left(1.5^{2}+2^{2}\right)+5\left[(-0.75)^{2}+(-1)^{2}\right]=10.4167, \\\\ I_{x^{\prime} y^{\prime}}=I_{y^{\prime} x^{\prime}}=0+5(-0.75)(-1)=3.7500, \\\\ I_{x^{\prime} z^{\prime}}=I_{z^{\prime} x^{\prime}}=0+5(-0.75)(-0.5)=1.8750,\\\\ I_{y^{\prime} z^{\prime}}=I_{z^{\prime} y^{\prime}}=0+5(-1)(-0.5)=2.5 \mathrm{kg}\text{-}\mathrm{m}^{2}.\end{array}\)

The rotation transformation from \(x^{\prime} y^{\prime} z^{\prime}\) to \(X Y Z\) is a simple rotation by \(\beta=\) \(\tan ^{-1}(1.5 / 2)=36.87^{\circ}\) about the negative \(z^{\prime}\) axis:

\(\left\{\begin{array}{l}X \\\\ Y \\\\ Z \end{array}\right\}=\left[R_{1}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}x^{\prime} \\\\ y^{\prime} \\\\ z^{\prime} \end{array}\right\}, \quad\left[R_{1}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc} 0.8 & -0.6 & 0 \\\\ 0.6 & 0.8 & 0 \\\\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right].\)

Let us define the body-fixed coordinate system \(x y z\) such that it coincides with \(X Y Z\) when \(\theta=0\). Thus, transforming the inertia matrix from \(x^{\prime} y^{\prime} z^{\prime}\) to \(X Y Z\) gives the constant properties relative to \(x y z\). We designate this as \(\left[I_{\theta=0}\right] .\) Using the preceding rotation matrix gives

\(\begin{aligned}\left[I_{\theta=0}\right] &=\left[R_{1}\right]\left[\begin{array}{ccc} 8.3333 & -3.7500 & -1.8750 \\\\ -3.7500 & 5.4167 & -2.5 \\\\ -1.8750 & -2.5 & 10.4167 \end{array}\right]\left[R_{1}\right]^{\mathrm{T}} \\\\ &=\left[\begin{array}{ccc} 10.8833 & 0.3500 & 0 \\\\ 0.3500 & 2.8667 & -3.1250 \\\\ 0 & -3.1250 & 10.4167 \end{array}\right]. \end{aligned} \)

Another rotation transformation is needed to find the properties relative to space-fixed \(X Y Z\) axes when \(\theta\) is not zero. Because \(x y z\) rotates about the \(Y\) axis relative to \(X Y Z\), the transformation from \(X Y Z\) to \(x y z\) is

\(\left\{\begin{array}{l}x \\\\ y \\\\ z \end{array}\right\}=\left[R_{2}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l} X \\\\ Y \\\\ Z \end{array}\right\}, \quad\left[R_{2}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc} \cos \theta & 0 & -\sin \theta \\\\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\\\ \sin \theta & 0 & \cos \theta\end{array}\right].\)

The transformation from \(x y z\) to \(X Y Z\) is described by \(\left[R_{2}\right]^{\mathrm{T}}\), so the inertia matrix for the \(X Y Z\) axes is given by

\([I]=\left[R_{2}\right]^{\mathrm{T}}\left[I_{\theta=0}\right]\left[R_{2}\right] .\)

The diagonal elements are the moments of inertia relative to \(X Y Z\), and the offdiagonal elements are the negative of the products of inertia:

\(\begin{array}{c} I_{X X}=0.4667(\cos \theta)^{2}+10.4167, \quad I_{Z Z}=0.4667(\sin \theta)^{2}+10.4167, \\\\ I_{Y Y}=2.8667, \\\\ I_{X Y}=I_{Y X}=-0.3500 \cos \theta+3.1250 \sin \theta, \\\\ I_{X Z}=I_{Z X}=0.4667(\sin \theta)(\cos \theta), \\\\ I_{Y Z}=I_{Z Y}=0.3500 \sin \theta+3.1250 \cos \theta.\end{array}\)

The moment of inertia about the \(Y\) axis does not depend on \(\theta\) because the distance from this axis to each mass point is unaffected by the rotation. Both \(I_{X X}\) and \(I_{Z Z}\) are periodic in \(\Delta \theta=\pi\), but they vary little as \(\theta\) changes, essentially because the dimensions of the box in both directions transverse to the \(Y\) axis are comparable. The products of inertia \(I_{X Y}\) and \(I_{Y Z}\) are periodic in \(\Delta \theta=2 \pi\), whereas \(I_{X Z}\) is periodic in \(\Delta \theta=\pi .\) The periodic nature of the inertia properties stems from the fact that a rotation by \(\Delta \theta=\pi\) changes the sign of the \(X\) and \(Z\) coordinates of each mass element, whereas the \(Y\) coordinate remains constant. An important observation is that there are values of \(\theta\) for which each product of inertia vanishes, but the angle is different for each term.
