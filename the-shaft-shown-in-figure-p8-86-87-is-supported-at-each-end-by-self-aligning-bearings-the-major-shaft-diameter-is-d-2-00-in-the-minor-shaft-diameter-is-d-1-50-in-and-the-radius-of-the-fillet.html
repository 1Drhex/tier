Guest Question.

The shaft shown in Figure P8.86/87 is supported at each end by self-aligning bearings. The major shaft diameter is D = 2.00 in., the minor shaft diameter is d = 1.50 in., and the radius of the fillet between the major and minor diameter sections is r = 0.125 in. The shaft length is L = 24 in. and the fillets are located at x = 8 in. and x = 16 in. Determine the maximum load P that may be applied to the shaft if the maximum normal stress must be limited to 24,000 psi.

Admin
Solution

From Figure 8.20

\(\frac{r}{d}=\frac{0.125 in .}{1.50 in .}=0.083 \quad \frac{D}{d}=\frac{2.00 in .}{1.50 in. }=1.33 \quad \therefore K \cong 1.78\)

Moment of inertia at minimum diameter section:

\(I=\frac{\pi}{64}(1.50 in .)^{4}=0.248505 in .{ }^{4}\)

Maximum allowable bending moment:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{\text {allow }} & \geq K \frac{M y}{I} \\& \therefore M_{\max } \leq \frac{\sigma_{\text {allow }} I}{K y}=\frac{(24,000 psi )\left(0.248505 in. ^{4}\right)}{(1.78)(1.50 in . / 2)}=4,467.50 lb – in .\end{aligned}\)

Bending moment at x = 8 in.:

\(M=\frac{P}{2} x=\frac{P}{2}(8 \text { in. })=P(4 \text { in. })\)

Maximum load P:

\(\begin{aligned}P(4 \text { in. }) & \leq 4,467.50 lb – in .\\& \therefore P \leq 1,116.88 lb =1,117 lb\end{aligned}\)

Check stress at midspan:

\(\begin{aligned}M_{\text {midspan }} &=\frac{P L}{4}=\frac{(1,116.88 lb )(24 \text { in. })}{4}=6,701.28 lb – in . \\I &=\frac{\pi}{64}(2.00 in .)^{4}=0.785398 in. ^{4} \\\sigma_{\text {midspan }} &=\frac{M y}{I}=\frac{(6,701.28 lb – in .)(2.00 in . / 2)}{0.785398 in. ^{4}}=8,532 psi \leq 24,000 psi \quad OK\end{aligned}\)
