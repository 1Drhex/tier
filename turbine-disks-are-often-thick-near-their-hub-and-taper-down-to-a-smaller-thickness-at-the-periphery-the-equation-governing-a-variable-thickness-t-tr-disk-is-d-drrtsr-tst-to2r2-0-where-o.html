Guest Question.

Turbine disks are often thick near their hub and taper down to a smaller thickness at the periphery. The equation governing a variable-thickness t = t(r) disk is
\(\frac{\mathrm{d} }{\mathrm{d} r}(rt\sigma _{r})-t\sigma _{\theta }+t\rho \omega ^{2}r^{2}=0\)
where \(\omega ^{2}\) is the angular speed of the disk and
\(\sigma _{r}=c\left ( \frac{\mathrm{d} u}{\mathrm{d} r}+\nu \frac{u}{r} \right )\) , \(\sigma _{\theta }=c\left ( \frac{u}{r}+\nu \frac{\mathrm{d} u}{\mathrm{d} r} \right )\) , \(c=\frac{E}{1-\nu ^{2}}\)
(a) Construct the weak integral form of the governing equation such that the bilinear form is symmetric and the natural boundary condition involves specifying the quantity tr\(\sigma _{r}\).
(b) Develop the finite element model associated with the weak form derived in part (a).

 

Admin
Solution

(a) The weak form is given by

0 = \(\int_{r_{a}}^{r_{b}}w\left [ -\frac{1}{r}\frac{\mathrm{d} }{\mathrm{d} r}(tr\sigma _{r})+\frac{t\sigma _{\theta }}{r}-f_{0} \right ]rdrd\theta \)

= \(2\pi \int_{r_{a}}^{r_{b}}\left ( tr\frac{\mathrm{d} w}{\mathrm{d} r}\sigma _{r}+wt\sigma _{\theta }-wf_{0}r \right )dr-Q_{a}w(r_{a})-Q_{b}w(r_{b})\) (1a)

where

\(f_{0}=t\rho \omega ^{2}r\) , \(Q_{a}\equiv 2\pi (-tr\sigma _{r})_{a}\) , \(Q_{b}\equiv 2\pi (tr\sigma _{r})_{b}\) (1b)

 

(b) The finite element model is given by

\([K^{e}]\left \{ u^{e} \right \}=\left \{ F^{e} \right \}\) or \(K^{e}u^{e}=F^{e}\) (2a)

where

\(K_{ij}^{e}=2\pi \int_{r_{a}}^{r_{b}}ct \left [ r\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{i}}{\mathrm{d} r}\left ( \frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{j}}{\mathrm{d} r}+\frac{\nu }{r}\psi _{j} \right ) +\psi _{i}\left ( \frac{1}{r}\psi _{j}+\nu \frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{j}}{\mathrm{d} r} \right )\right ]dr\)

\(F_{i}^{e}=2\pi \int_{r_{a}}^{r_{b}}f_{0}\psi _{i}rdr +Q_{a}\psi _{i}(r_{a})+Q_{b}\psi _{i}(r_{b})\) (2b)
