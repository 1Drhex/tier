Guest Question.

Determine the minimum slab thickness that satisfies the deflection criteria of ACI 9.5.3 for the flat-slab system depicted in Fig. 7.13. Assume Grade 60 reinforcement.

Admin
Solution

For slabs without interior beams spanning between the supports, the provisions of ACI 9.5.3.2 must be used to determine the minimum slab thickness.
Check if the plan dimensions of the drop panel meet the minimum requirements of ACI 13.2.5(b):
Minimum extension from centerline of support \(=\frac{\ell}{6}=\frac{23.5}{6}=3.9\) ft
Minimum width of drop panel = 2 × 3.9 = 7.8 ft
Note that the largest center-to-center distance between columns was used to determine the minimum drop panel extension.
The requirements of ACI 13.2.5(b) are satisfied for the plan dimensions of the drop panels because 8 × 8 ft drop panels are provided. Once a slab thickness has been determined, the thickness of the drop panel below the slab must be equal to at least one-quarter of the slap thickness in order to satisfy the requirements of ACI 13.2.5(a).

The minimum slab thickness is determine using the information given in ACI Table 9.5(c) for slab system with drop panels and edge beams and with Grad 60 reinforcment (see fig 7.9) .The minimum thickness values in the table for exterior panels with edge beams are on stiffness ratio \(\alpha_{f}≥0.8\) . Becouse the slab thickness is not known at this stage, \(\alpha_{f}\) cannot be determined, In this assumption will be checked after the slab thickness has been calculated

  • Exterior panel : longest \(\ell_{n}=(23.5 \times 12)-20=262\) in

\(h_{min}=\frac{\ell_{n}}{36} = \frac {262} {36}=7.3\) in

  • Interior panel : longest \(\ell_{n}=(23.5 \times 12)-24=258\) in

\(h_{min}=\frac{\ell_{n}}{36} = \frac {258} {36}=7.2\) in

Thus, the minimum slab thickness for an exterior panel governs.
Try a 7.5-in slab thickness, and calculate αf for an edge beam, using Eq. (7.1):

\(\alpha _{f}=\frac{E_{cb}I_{b}}{E_{cs}I_{s}}\)

Effective width \(b_{e}=\begin{cases} b_{w} + h_{b} = 20 + (24 − 7.5) = 36.5 in (governs)\\ b_{w} + 4h_{f} = 20 + (4 × 7.5) = 50.0 in\end{cases}\).

(see Fig. 7.10)

Using Eq. (7.4)

\(y_{b}=\frac{b_{e}h_{f}[h_{b}+(h_{f}/2)]+(b_{w}h^{2} _ {b} /2) } {b _{e} h_{f} +b_{w}h_{b}} =\frac {( 36.5 \times 7.5)(16.5 +3.75)+(0.5 \times 20 \times 16.5^{2})}{(36.5 \times 7.5)+(20 \times 16.5)}=13.7\) in

Using Eq. (7.3)

\(I_{b}=\frac{1}{12} b_{w} h_{b}^{3}+b_{w} h_{b}\left(y_{b}-\frac{h_{b}}{2}\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{12} b_{e} h_{f}^{3}+b_{e} h_{f}\left(h_{b}+\frac{h_{f}}{2}-y_{b}\right)^{2}\).

\(I_{b}=\frac{1}{12} b_{w} h_{b}^{3}+b_{w} h_{b}\left(y_{b}-\frac{h_{b}}{2}\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{12} b_{e} h_{f}^{3}+b_{e} h_{f}\left(h_{b}+\frac{h_{f}}{2}-y_{b}\right)^{2}\).

\(=\frac{1}{12} b_{w} h_{b}^{3}+b_{w} h_{b}\left(y_{b}-\frac{h_{b}}{2}\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{12} b_{e} h_{f}^{3}+b_{e} h_{f}\left(h_{b}+\frac{h_{f}}{2}-y_{b}\right)^{2}\)

 

\(I_{b}=\left(\frac{1}{12}\times 20\times 16.5 ^{3} \right)+ \left (20 \times 16.5 \right)\left(13.7-\frac{16.5 }{2} \right ) ^{2} + \left (\frac{1}{12} \times 36.5\times 7.5^{3} \right) \left (16.5 \times 7.5\right)\left(16.5 +\frac {7.5}{2}-13.7 \right)^{2} = 30,317 in ^ {4} \)

Also, from Eq. (7.2),

\(I_{s}=\frac{1}{12}\ell_{2}h^{3}_{f}= \frac{1}{12}\times \left (\frac{23.5 \times 12}{2}+\frac{20}{2} \right)\times 7.5 ^{3}=5,309 in^{4}\)

Note that \(I_{s}\) was calculated using the largest \(\ell_{2}\); this results in the maximum \(I_{s}\), which is required in order to obtain the minimum \(α_{f}\) .
Therefore,

\(\alpha _{f}=\frac{E_{cb}I_{b}}{E_{c s}I_{s}}= \frac {30,317} {5,309} =5.7 >0.8\)

Thus, the assumption that \(α_{f} ≥ 0.8\) is correct.
Use a 7.5-in-thick slab. This thickness is greater than the minimum thickness of 4 in prescribed in ACI 9.5.3.2(b) for slabs with drop panels.
The minimum thickness of the projection of the drop panel below the slab must be 0.25 × 7.5 =1.9 in (see Fig. 7.4). From Fig. 7.6, use a 2.25-in drop panel projection. The overall thickness of the drop panel and the slab adjacent to the drop panel need to be checked for two-way shear.
