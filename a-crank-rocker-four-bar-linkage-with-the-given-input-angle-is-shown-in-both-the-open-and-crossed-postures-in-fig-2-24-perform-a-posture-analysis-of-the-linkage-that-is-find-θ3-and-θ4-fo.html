Guest Question.

A crank-rocker four-bar linkage with the given input angle, is shown in both the open and crossed postures in Fig. 2.24. Perform a posture analysis of the linkage; that is, find θ3 and θ4 for both postures.

Admin
Solution

Note that the line connecting the ground pins, O2 and O4 (that is, the ground link), is not horizontal. In the previous examples, the ground link was chosen to be coincident with the x axis; that is, θ1 = 0. In this example, however, the angle θ1 = 15º and must be accounted for when using Eqs. (2.25) through (2.30)

\(S=\sqrt{r_{1}^{2}+r_{2}^{2}-2 r_{1} r_{2} \cos \theta_{2}}\) (2.25)

 

\(\theta_{4}=\pi-\beta \mp \lambda\) (2.30)

 

For the input angle θ2 = 30º, we observe that

\(R_{A O_{2}}=0.200 m \angle 30^{\circ} \quad \text { and } \quad R _{O_{4} O_{2}}=0.400 m \angle 15^{\circ}\)

 

Therefore,

 

\(S = R _{O_{4} O_{2}}- R _{A O_{2}}=0.400 m \angle 15^{\circ}-0.200 m \angle 30^{\circ}=0.213 m \angle 0.95^{\circ}\)

 

Note that the vectors in triangle ABO4 are related by the equation

 

\(\overset{\sqrt{}\sqrt{}}{S} = \overset{\sqrt{}? }{R_{BA}} – \overset{\sqrt{}? }{R_{BO_{4}}}.\) (1)

 

 

There are two unknown orientations in this equation, and so we identify this as case 4 (Table 2.2).

Table 2.2 Unknowns in Planar Vector Equations
Case Unknowns
1 C, θC Fig. 2.10 Eq. (2.14)
2 A, θB Fig. 2.11 Eq. (2.17)
3 A,B Fig. 2.12 Eq. (2.18)
4 θA, θB Fig. 2.13 Eq.(2.19)

C = A+B = B+A. (2.14)

\(\overset{\sqrt{}\sqrt{}}{C} = \overset{?\sqrt{}}{A} + \overset{\sqrt{}?}{B}.\) (2.17)

 

\(\overset{\sqrt{}\sqrt{}}{C} = \overset{?\sqrt{}}{A} + \overset{?\sqrt{}}{B}.\) (2.18)

 

\(\overset{\sqrt{}\sqrt{}}{C} = \overset{\sqrt{}?}{A} + \overset{\sqrt{}?}{B}.\) (2.19)

 

(a) Graphic approach When Fig. 2.24 is drawn at a chosen scale, then, by direct measurement of the figure in the open posture, we find

θ3 = 60º and θ4 = 97º,

and in the crossed posture, we find

\(\theta_{3}^{\prime}=-57^{\circ} \quad \text { and } \quad \theta_{4}^{\prime}=-95^{\circ} .\)

 

(b) Analytic approach Comparing the notation in Fig. 2.24 with Fig. 2.19, we set \(r_{1}=R_{O_{4} O_{2}}=0.400 m , r_{2}=R_{A O_{2}}=0.200 m , r_{3}=R_{B A}=0.350 m , \text { and } r_{4}=R_{B O_{4}}= 0.300 m. \) Also, note that the ground link of Fig. 2.19 must be rotated by θ1 = 15º ccw, and
θ2 = 30º −15º = 15º; therefore, Eqs. (2.25) through (2.30) give

\(S=\sqrt{(0.400 m )^{2}+(0.200 m )^{2}-2(0.400 m )(0.200 m ) \cos \left(30^{\circ}-15^{\circ}\right)} = 0.213 m,\)

 

\(\beta=\cos ^{-1} \frac{(0.400 m )^{2}+(0.213 m )^{2}-(0.200 m )^{2}}{2(0.400 m )(0.213 m )}=14.05^{\circ},\)

 

\(\psi=\cos ^{-1} \frac{(0.350 m )^{2}+(0.213 m )^{2}-(0.300 m )^{2}}{2(0.350 m )(0.213 m )}=58.51^{\circ}\)

 

\(\lambda=\cos ^{-1} \frac{(0.300 m )^{2}+(0.213 m )^{2}-(0.350 m )^{2}}{2(0.300 m )(0.213 m )}=84.19^{\circ}\)

 

Then, in the open posture, remembering that θ1 = 15º,

θ3 = 15º −14.05º +58.51º = 59.46º,

θ4 = 15º +180º −14.05º −84.19º = 96.76º,

and, in the crossed posture,

\(\theta_{3}^{\prime}=15^{\circ}-14.05^{\circ}-58.51^{\circ}=-57.56^{\circ}\)

 

\(\theta_{4}^{\prime}=15^{\circ}+180^{\circ}-14.05^{\circ}+84.19^{\circ}=-94.86^{\circ} \text {. }\)

 

(c) Complex-algebraic approach Substituting S for C, \(R_{B A} \text { for } A,-R_{B O_{4}} \text { for } B \text {, }\) θS for θC, and θ4 for θB, into Eq. (2.49) gives

 

\(\theta_{B}=\theta_{C} \pm \cos ^{-1} \frac{C^{2}+B^{2}-A^{2}}{2 B C}\) (2.49)

 

\(\theta_{4}=\theta_{S} \pm \cos ^{-1} \frac{S^{2}+R_{B O_{4}}^{2}-R_{B A}^{2}}{2\left(-R_{B O_{4}}\right) S}=\left(\theta_{1}-\beta\right) \pm \cos ^{-1} \frac{S^{2}+R_{B O_{4}}^{2}-R_{B A}^{2}}{2\left(-R_{B O_{4}}\right) S}\)

 

\(=0.95^{\circ} \pm \cos ^{-1} \frac{(0.213 m )^{2}+(-0.300 m )^{2}-(0.350 m )^{2}}{2(-0.300 m )(0.213 m )}\)

 

\(=0.95^{\circ} \pm 95.81^{\circ}=96.76^{\circ} \text { or }-94.86^{\circ} .\)

 

In addition, substituting θ3 for θA into Eq. (2.50) gives

 

\(\theta_{A}=\theta_{C} \pm \cos ^{-1} \frac{C^{2}+A^{2}-B^{2}}{2 A C}\) (2.50)

 

\(\theta_{3}=\theta_{S} \pm \cos ^{-1} \frac{S^{2}+R_{B A}^{2}-R_{B O_{4}}^{2}}{2 R_{B A} S}\)

 

\(=0.95^{\circ} \pm \cos ^{-1} \frac{(0.213 m )^{2}+(0.350 m )^{2}-(-0.300 m )^{2}}{2(0.350 m )(0.213 m )}\)

 

\(=0.95^{\circ} \pm 58.51^{\circ}=59.46^{\circ} \text { or }-57.56^{\circ}\)

 

where the first result corresponds to the open posture and the second result is for the crossed posture. We note that the answers in Parts (b) and (c) are in good agreement with those of Part (a), but are of higher accuracy than measurements taken from the scaled drawing.

(d) Vector-algebraic approach The vector loop equation for the four-bar-linkage can be written as

\(\overset{\sqrt{}? }{R_{BA}} = \overset{\sqrt{} \sqrt{} }{R_{O_{4}O_{2}}} + \overset{\sqrt{}? }{R_{BO_{4}}} – \overset{\sqrt{}I }{R_{AO_{2}}}.\) (2)

 

Separating this equation into horizontal and vertical components,

\(R_{B A} \cos \theta_{3}=R_{O_{4} O_{2}} \cos \theta_{1}+R_{B O_{4}} \cos \theta_{4}-R_{A O_{2}} \cos \theta_{2}\) (3a)

 

\(R_{B A} \sin \theta_{3}=R_{O_{4} O_{2}} \sin \theta_{1}+R_{B O_{4}} \sin \theta_{4}-R_{A O_{2}} \sin \theta_{2}\) (3b)

 

By squaring and adding these two equations, we can eliminate the variable θ3 :

 

\(R_{B A}^{2}=R_{O_{4} O_{2}}^{2}+R_{B O_{4}}^{2}+R_{A O_{2}}^{2}+2 R_{O_{4}O_{2}} R_{B O_{4}}\left[\cos \theta_{1} \cos \theta_{4}+\sin \theta_{1} \sin \theta_{4}\right] \)

 

\(-2 R_{O_{4} O_{2}} R_{A O_{2}}\left[\cos \theta_{1} \cos \theta_{2}+\sin \theta_{1} \sin \theta_{2}\right]-2 R_{B O_{4}} R_{A O_{2}}\left[\cos \theta_{4} \cos \theta_{2}+\sin \theta_{4} \sin \theta_{2}\right]\)

 

Then we can write this equation as

A cos θ4 +B sin θ4 = C, (4)

where

\(A=2 R_{O_{4}O_{2} } R_{B O_{4}} \cos \theta_{1}-2 R_{B O_{4}} R_{A O_{2}} \cos \theta_{2},\) (5a)

 

\(B=2 R_{O_{4} O_{2}} R_{B O_{4}} \sin \theta_{1}-2 R_{B O_{4}} R_{A O_{2}} \sin \theta_{2},\) (5b)

 

\(C=R_{B A}^{2}-R_{O_{4} O_{2}}^{2}-R_{B O_{4}}^{2}-R_{A O_{2}}^{2}+2 R_{O_{4} O_{2}} R_{A O_{2}} \cos \left(\theta_{2}-\theta_{1}\right)\) (5c)

 

Equation (4) is one form of Freudenstein’s equation (Sec. 9.11 shows another variation that is more suitable for kinematic synthesis).

Substituting the known data into Eqs. (5) gives

A = 2(0.400 m)(0.300 m) cos 15º −2(0.300 m)(0.200 m) cos 30º= 0.127 90 m2,

B = 2(0.400 m)(0.300 m)sin 15º −2(0.300 m)(0.200 m)sin 30º = 0.002 12 m2,

C = (0.350 m)2 −(0.400 m)2 −(0.300 m)2 −(0.200 m)2

+2(0.400 m)(0.200 m) cos(30º −15º) = −0.012 95 m2.

Therefore, Eq. (4) can be written as

(0.127 90 m2) cos θ4 +(0.002 12 m2)sin θ4 = −0.012 95 m2. (6)

To determine the output angle θ4, this transcendental equation can be written as a quadratic equation. The procedure is to use the half-angle relationships; that is, to define

Z = tan(θ4 /2), (7a)

which gives

\(\sin \theta_{4}=\frac{2 Z}{1+Z^{2}} \quad \text { and } \quad \cos \theta_{4}=\frac{1-Z^{2}}{1+Z^{2}} \text {. }\) (7b)

 

Substituting Eqs. (7b) into Eq. (6), multiplying through by (1 + Z2), and rearranging, gives the quadratic equation

(0.114 95 m2)Z2 +(−0.004 24 m2)Z +(−0.140 85 m2) = 0.

The solutions to this equation can be written as

\(Z=\frac{-(-0.00424 m ) \pm \sqrt{(-0.00424 m )^{2}-4(0.11495 m )(-0.14085 m )}}{2(0.11495 m )}\)

 

\(=0.01841 \pm 1.10680=1.12521 \text { or }-1.08839 .\) (8)

 

Substituting Eq. (8) into Eq. (7a), gives the two output angles corresponding to the open and crossed postures, namely

 

\(\theta_{4}=96.76^{\circ} \quad \text { and } \quad \theta_{4}^{\prime}=-94.86^{\circ} \text {. }\)

 

Substituting these results into Eqs. (3a) and (3b) gives the corresponding coupler angles

 

\(\theta_{3}=59.46^{\circ} \quad \text { and } \theta_{3}^{\prime}=-57.56^{\circ} \text {. }\)

 

(e) Numeric approach Rearrange the vector loop equation, Eq. (2), into the form

 

\(f = R _{A O_{2}}+ R _{B A}- R _{O_{4} O_{2}}- R _{B O_{4}}= 0,\) (9)

 

and separate the horizontal and vertical components:

 

\(f^{x}=R_{A O_{2}} \cos \theta_{2}+R_{B A} \cos \theta_{3}-R_{O_{4} O_{2}} \cos \theta_{1}-R_{B O_{4}} \cos \theta_{4}=0,\) (10a)

 

\(f^{y}=R_{A O_{2}} \sin \theta_{2}+R_{B A} \sin \theta_{3}-R_{O_{4} O_{2}} \sin \theta_{1}-R_{B O_{4}} \sin \theta_{4}=0 .\) (10b)

 

We recognize these as two equations in two unknowns, θ3 and θ4 , and we solve them by the Newton-Raphson method introduced in Sec. 2.9 to an accuracy of 0.01º. To do this, we expand Eqs. (10) to first-order in Taylor series in the two unknowns as follows:

 

\(R_{A O_{2}} \cos \theta_{2}+R_{B A} \cos \theta_{3}-R_{B A} \sin \theta_{3} \Delta \theta_{3}-R_{O_{4} O_{2}} \cos \theta_{1}-R_{B O_{4}} \cos \theta_{4}+R_{B O_{4}} \sin \theta_{4} \Delta \theta_{4}=0,\)

 

\(R_{A O_{2}} \sin \theta_{2}+R_{B A} \sin \theta_{3}+R_{B A} \cos \theta_{3} \Delta \theta_{3}-R_{O_{4} O_{2}} \sin \theta_{1}-R_{B O_{4}} \sin \theta_{4}-R_{B O_{4}} \cos \theta_{4} \Delta \theta_{4}=0.\)

 

Then, writing these equations in matrix form,

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{ll} R_{B A} \sin \theta_{3} & – R_{B O_{4}} \sin \theta_{4} \\ -R_{B A} \cos \theta_{3} & R_{B O_{4}} \cos \theta_{4} \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} \Delta \theta_{3} \\ \Delta \theta_{4} \end{array}\right]\)

 

\(=\left[\begin{array}{l} R_{A O_{2}} \cos \theta_{2}+R_{B A} \cos \theta_{3}-R_{O_{4} O_{2}} \cos \theta_{1}-R_{B O_{4}} \cos \theta_{4} \\ R_{A O_{2}} \sin \theta_{2}+R_{B A} \sin \theta_{3}-R _{O_{4} O_{2}} \sin \theta_{1}-R_{B O_{4}} \sin \theta_{4} \end{array}\right].\)

 

Substituting the given data, these matrices become

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc} (0.350 m ) \sin \theta_{3} & -(0.300 m ) \sin \theta_{4} \\ -(0.350 m ) \cos \theta_{3} & (0.300 m ) \cos \theta_{4} \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c} \Delta \theta_{3} \\ \Delta \theta_{4} \end{array}\right]\)

 

\(=\left[\begin{array}{c} (-0.21316 m )+(0.350 m ) \cos \theta_{3}-(0.300 m ) \cos \theta_{4} \\ (-0.00353 m )+(0.350 m ) \sin \theta_{3}-(0.300 m ) \sin \theta_{4} \end{array}\right].\) (11)

 

Note that Δθ3 and Δθ4 are called the corrections and are dimensionless values, meaning values in radians. For the iteration procedure, we must be careful when combining the coorections with θ3 and θ4 if the two angles have units of degrees; therefore,

 

\(\left(\theta_{3}^{\circ}\right)_{n e w}=\left(\theta_{3}^{\circ}\right)_{o l d}+\frac{180^{\circ}}{\pi rad } \Delta \theta_{3},\) (12a)

 

\(\left(\theta_{4}^{\circ}\right)_{n e w}=\left(\theta_{4}^{\circ}\right)_{o l d}+\frac{180^{\circ}}{\pi rad } \Delta \theta_{4} .\) (12b)

 

To use the iteration procedure, we begin with initial estimates of the unknowns θ3 and θ4 . From a scale drawing of the linkage, such that, for the open posture, our starting estimates are

θ3 = 60º and θ4 = 100º. (13a)

Inserting these estimates into Eq. (11) gives

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc} 0.303 11 m & -0.295 44 m \\ -0.175 00 m & -0.052 09 m \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} \Delta \theta_{3} \\ \Delta \theta_{4} \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{cc} 0.013 93 m \\ 0.004 14 m \end{array}\right].\)

 

Using Cramer’s rule, the corrections are

Δθ3 = −0.007 371 5 rad and Δθ4 = −0.005 471 rad.

Substituting these corrections into Eqs. (12) gives

θ3 = 59.58º and θ4 = 96.87º. (13b)

Inserting these improved estimates into Eq. (11) gives

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc} 0.301 82 m & -0.297 85 m \\ -0.177 22 m & -0.035 89 m \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} \Delta \theta_{3} \\ \Delta \theta_{4} \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{cc} -0.577 13(10)^{-4} m \\ 0.441 91(10)^{-3} m \end{array}\right].\)

 

Solving again by Cramer’s rule, the updated corrections are

Δθ3 = −0.002 101 6 rad and Δθ4 = −0.001 94 rad.

Substituting these corrections into Eqs. (12), gives

θ3 = 59.46º and θ4 = 96.76º. (13c)

Inserting these improved estimates into Eq. (11) gives us

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc} 0.30145 m & -0.297 91 m \\ -0.17785 m & -0.035 31 m \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} \Delta \theta_{3} \\ \Delta \theta_{4} \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{cc} 0.213 51(10)^{-5} m \\ 0.173 94(10)^{-5} m \end{array}\right].\)

Solving again by Cramer’s rule, the updated corrections are

Δθ3 = −0.000 069 59 rad and Δθ4 = −0.000 014 2 rad.

Substituting the corrections into Eqs. (12), gives

θ3 = 59.46º and θ4 = 96.76º.

Since the corrections are now both smaller than the desired accuracy of 0.00017 rad = 0.01º, we accept these values as final results for the open posture.

For the crossed posture, we begin with initial estimates from our drawing of θ3 = −60º and θ4 = −100º. Then, using these as starting estimates with Eq. (11), we repeat the same process and iterate until we converge to a result of

θ3 = −57.56º and θ4 = −94.86º.
