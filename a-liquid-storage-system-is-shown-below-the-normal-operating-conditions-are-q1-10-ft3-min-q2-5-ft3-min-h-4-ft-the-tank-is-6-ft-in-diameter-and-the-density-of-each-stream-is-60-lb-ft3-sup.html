Guest Question.

A liquid storage system is shown below. The normal operating conditions are \(\bar{q}_{1}=10 ft ^{3} / min , \bar{q}_{2}=5 ft ^{3} / min , \bar{h}=4 ft\). The tank is \(6 ft\) in diameter, and the density of each stream is \(60 lb / ft ^{3}\). Suppose that a pulse change in \(q_{1}\) occurs as shown in Fig. E5.8.

(a) What is the transfer function relating \(H^{\prime}\) to \(Q_{1}^{\prime} ?\)

(b) Derive an expression for \(h(t)\) for this input change.

(c) What is the new steady-state value of liquid level \(h\) ?

(d) Repeat (b) and (c) for a change in \(q_{1}\) from 10 to 20 (at \(t=0\) ) to \(15 ft ^{3} / min\) at \(t=12\).

Admin
Solution

a) Assume that q is constant. Material balance over the tank,

\(A \frac{d h}{d t}=q_{1}+q_{2}-q\)

Writing in deviation variables and taking Laplace transform

\(\begin{aligned}&A s H^{\prime}(s)=Q_{1}^{\prime}(s)+Q_{2}^{\prime}(s) \\&\frac{H^{\prime}(s)}{Q_{1}^{\prime}(s)}=\frac{1}{A s}\end{aligned}\)

b)

\(\begin{aligned}&q_{1}^{\prime}(t)=5 S(t)-5 S(t-12) \\&Q_{1}^{\prime}(s)=\frac{5}{s}-\frac{5}{s} e^{-12 s} \\&H^{\prime}(s)=\frac{1}{A s} Q_{1}^{\prime}(s)=\frac{5 / A}{s^{2}}-\frac{5 / A}{s^{2}} e^{-12 s} \\&h^{\prime}(t)=\frac{5}{A} t S(t)-\frac{5}{A}(t-12) S(t-12)\end{aligned}\)

\(h(t)= \begin{cases}4+\frac{5}{A} t=4+0.177 t & 0 \leq t \leq 12 \\ 4+\left(\frac{5}{A} \times 12\right)=6.122 & 12<t\end{cases}\)

c)

\(\bar{h}=6.122 ft \text { at the new steady state } t \geq 12\)

d)

\(\begin{aligned}&q_{1}^{\prime}(t)=10 S(t)-5 S(t-12) \\&Q_{1}^{\prime}(s)=\frac{10}{s}-\frac{5}{s} e^{-12 s} \\&H^{\prime}(s)=\frac{10 / A}{s^{2}}-\frac{5 / A}{s^{2}} e^{-12 s}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(h(t)=\left\{\begin{array}{cc}4+\frac{10}{A} t=4+0.354 t & 0 \leq t \leq 12 \\6.122+0.177 t & t \geq 12\end{array}\right.\)

The liquid level will keep increasing and there will be no steady-state value of liquid level \(\bar{h}\).
