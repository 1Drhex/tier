Guest Question.

Determine annual cost, average hourly cost, and the burden markup for the secretary in using , , , , , , and. Assume the same person fills the secretary position during the entire year.

Solution

From , , , ,, ,, and , we calculated the following costs:

Wages = $21,195/year
Allowances = $1,200/year
Social Security Tax = $1,277/year
Medicare Tax = $299/year
SUTA = $180/year FUTA = $56/year
Workers’ Compensation = $278/year

Retirement = $672/year

The total cost of burden is $5,276 ($1,200 + $1,277 + $299 + $180 + $56 + $278 + $114 + $1,200 + $672) per year. The annual cost of the secretary is $26,471 ($21,195 +
$5,276) per year.

From , the secretary worked 2,220 hours. The hourly cost is calculated as follows: Hourly Costs =\(\frac{Total Cost}{Hours Worked}=\frac{\$26,471/year}{2,220 hours} =\$11.92\)

The burden markup is calculated by taking the total cost of the employee and dividing it by the wages paid for work performed and subtracting 1. The work performed equals the wages paid to the employee less the wages paid for time off and bonuses. This allows us to include the cost of vacation, holidays, sick leave, and bonuses in the markup. The wages in must be re-duced by $1,020 (120 hours × $8.50/hour) for time off and $200 for the bonus. The burden markup is calculated
as follows:

Burden Markup =\(\frac{Total Cost}{Wages_{Total} -Wages_{Time Off}-Bonus } -1 \)

Burden Markup =\(\frac{\$26,471/year }{\$21,195/year – \$1,020/year – \$200/year } -1 \)

Burden Markup = 0.325 or 32.5%

It is important to note that we cannot take the base hourly rate of $8.50 and add the burden markup to get the hourly cost because for five hours a week the secretary is earning 1.5 times the base rate. The overtime rate is not included in the burden markup.
