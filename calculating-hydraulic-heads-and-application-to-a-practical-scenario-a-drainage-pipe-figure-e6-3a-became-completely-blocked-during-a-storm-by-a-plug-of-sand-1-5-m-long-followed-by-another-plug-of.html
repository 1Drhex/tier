Guest Question.

Calculating Hydraulic Heads and Application to a Practical Scenario.

A drainage pipe (Figure E6.3a) became completely blocked during a storm by a plug of sand 1.5 m long, followed by another plug of a mixture of clays, silts, and sands 0.5 m long. When the storm was over, the water level above ground was 1 m. The hydraulic conductivity of the sand is 2 times that of the mixture of clays, silts, and sands.

(a) Plot the variation of pressure, elevation, and total head over the length of the pipe.
(b) Calculate the porewater pressure at (1) the center of the sand plug and (2) the center of the mixture of clays, silts, and sands.
(c) Find the average hydraulic gradients in the sand and in the mixture of clays, silts, and sands.

Strategy: You need to select a datum. From the information given, you can calculate the total head at A and B. The difference in head is the head loss over both plugs, but you do not know how much head is lost in the sand and in the mixture of clays, silts, and sands. The continuity equation provides the key to finding the head loss over each plug.

Admin
Solution

Step 1: Select a datum.
Select the exit at B along the centerline of the drainage pipe as the datum.

Step 2: Determine heads at A and B.

\((h_{z})_{A}=0\, m,\: \: (h_{p})_{A}=0.3+2+1=3.3\, m,\: \: H_{A}=0+3.3=3.3\, m\) \((h_{z})_{B}=0\, m,\: \: (h_{p})_{B}=0\, m,\: \: H_{B}=0\, m\)

Step 3: Determine the head loss in each plug.

Head loss between A and B = \(|H_{B}-H_{A}|=3.3\, m\)(decrease in head taken as positive). Let \(\Delta H_{1},L_{1},k_{1}\) and \(q_{1}\) be the head loss, length, hydraulic conductivity, and flow in the sand;

let \(\Delta H_{2},L_{2},k_{2}\) and \(q_{2}\) be the head loss, length, hydraulic conductivity, and flow in the mixture of clays, silts, and sands.

Now,

\(q_{1}=Ak_{1}\frac{\Delta H_{1}}{L_{1}}=A*2k_{2}\frac{\Delta H_{1}}{L_{1}}\) \(q_{2}=Ak_{2}\frac{\Delta H_{2}}{L_{2}}=A*2k_{2}\frac{\Delta H_{2}}{L_{2}}\)

From the continuity equation, \(q_{1} = q_{2}\)

\(\therefore A*2k_{2}\frac{\Delta H_{1}}{L_{1}}=Ak_{2}\frac{\Delta H_{2}}{L_{2}}\)

Solving, we get

\(\frac{\Delta H_{1}}{\Delta H_{2}}=\frac{L1}{2L_{2}}=\frac{1.5}{2*0.5}=1.5\)

\(\Delta H_{1}=1.5\Delta H_{2}\) (1)

However, we know that

\(\Delta H_{1}+\Delta H_{2}=\Delta H=3.3\, m\) (2)

Solving for \(\Delta H_{1}\) and \(\Delta H_{2}\) 2 from Equations (1) and (2), we obtain

\(\Delta H_{1} = 1.98 m\) and \(\Delta H_{2} = 3.3 – 1.98 = 1.32 m\)

Step 4: Calculate heads at the junction of the two plugs.

Total head at \(C=H_{C}=H_{A}-\Delta H_{1}=3.3-1.98=1.32\, m\)

\((h_{z})_{c}=0\) \((h_{z})_{C}=H_{C}-(h_{z})_{C}=1.32\, m\)

Step 5: Plot distribution of heads.
See Figure E6.3b.

Step 6: Calculate porewater pressures.
Let D be the center of the sand.

\((h_{p})_{D}=\frac{(h_{p})_{A}+(h_{p})_{C}}{2}=\frac{3.3+1.32}{2}=2.31\, m\) \(u_{D}=2.31*\gamma _{w}=2.31*9.8=22.6\, kPa\)

Let E be the center of the mixture of clays, silts, and sands.

\((h_{p})_{E}=\frac{(h_{p})_{C}+(h_{p})_{B}}{2}=\frac{1.32+0}{2}=0.66\, m\) \(u_{E}=0.66*9.8=6.5\, kPa\)

Step 7: Find the average hydraulic gradients.

\(i_{1}=\frac{\Delta H_{1}}{L_{1}}=\frac{1.98}{1.5}=1.32\) \(i_{2}=\frac{\Delta H_{2}}{L_{2}}=\frac{1.32}{0.5}=2.64\)
