Guest Question.

The \(\max\) norm \(\|\mathbf{v}\|_{m}\) of a vector \(\mathbf{v}\) in \(\mathbb{R}^{n}\) is the largest number among the absolute values of its components. That is, if \(\mathbf{v}=\left[\begin{array}{lll}v_{1} & \cdots & v_{n}\end{array}\right]^{T}\), then

\(\|\mathbf{v}\|_{m}=\max \left\{\left|v_{1}\right|, \ldots,\left|v_{n}\right|\right\}\)

Show that the max norm is a norm.

Admin
Solution

Again, it is clear that \(\|\mathbf{v}\|_{m} \geq 0\). If \(\|\mathbf{v}\|_{m}=0\), then the largest of \(\left|v_{1}\right|, \ldots,\left|v_{n}\right|\) is zero, and so they all are. Hence, \(v_{1}=\cdots=v_{n}=0\), so \(\mathbf{v}=0\). This verifies property (1). Next, we observe that for any scalar \(c\),

\(\|c \mathbf{v}\|_{m}=\max \left\{\left|c v_{1}\right|, \ldots,\left|c v_{n}\right|\right\}=|c| \max \left\{\left|v_{1}\right|, \ldots,\left|v_{n}\right|\right\}=|c|\|\mathbf{v}\|_{m}\)

Finally, for \(\mathbf{u}=\left[\begin{array}{lll}u_{1} & \cdots & u_{n}\end{array}\right]^{T}\), we have

\(\begin{aligned}\|\mathbf{u}+\mathbf{v}\|_{m} &=\max \left\{\left|u_{1}+v_{1}\right|, \ldots,\left|u_{n}+v_{n}\right|\right\} \\& \leq \max \left\{\left|u_{1}\right|+\left|v_{1}\right|, \ldots,\left|u_{n}\right|+\left|v_{n}\right|\right\} \\& \leq \max \left\{\left|u_{1}\right|, \ldots,\left|u_{n}\right|\right\}+\max \left\{\left|v_{1}\right|, \ldots,\left|v_{n}\right|\right\}=\|\mathbf{u}\|_{m}+\|\mathbf{v}\|_{m}\end{aligned}\)

(Why is the second inequality true?) This verifies the Triangle Inequality.
