Guest Question.

A small dam has a pipe carrying liquid water at 150 kPa, 20°C with a flow rate of 2000 kg/s in a 0.5 m diameter pipe. The pipe runs to the bottom of the dam 15 m lower into a turbine with pipe diameter 0.35 m. Assume no friction or heat transfer in the pipe and find the pressure of the turbine inlet. If the turbine exhausts to 100 kPa with negligible kinetic energy what is the rate of work?

 

Admin
Solution

C.V. Pipe. Steady flow no work, no heat transfer.
States: compressed liquid B.1.1 \(v _{2} \approx v _{1} \approx v _{ f }=0.001002 m ^{3} / kg\)

Continuity Eq.4.3: \(\dot{ m }=\rho AV = AV / v\)

 

\(V _{1}=\dot{ m } v _{1} / A _{1}=2000 kg / s \times 0.001002 m ^{3} / kg /\left(\frac{\pi}{4} 0.5^{2} m ^{2}\right)=10.2 m s ^{-1}\)

 

\(V _{2}=\dot{ m } v _{2} / A _{2}=2000 kg / s \times 0.001002 m ^{3} / kg /\left(\frac{\pi}{4} 0.35^{2} m ^{2}\right)=20.83 m s ^{-1}\)

 

From Bernoulli Eq.7.16 for the pipe (incompressible substance):

\(v \left( P _{2}- P _{1}\right)+\frac{1}{2}\left( V _{2}^{2}- V _{1}^{2}\right)+ g \left( Z _{2}- Z _{1}\right)=0 \Rightarrow\)

 

\(P _{2}= P _{1}+\left[\frac{1}{2}\left( V _{1}^{2}- V _{2}^{2}\right)+ g \left( Z _{1}- Z _{2}\right)\right] / v\)

 

\(=150 kPa +\frac{\frac{1}{2} \times\left(10.2^{2}-20.83^{2}\right)+9.80665 \times 15}{1000 \times 0.001002} \frac{ m ^{2} s ^{-2}}{ J / kJ \times m ^{3} / kg }\)

 

= 150 – 17.8 = 132.2 kPa

Note that the pressure at the bottom should be higher due to the elevation difference but lower due to the acceleration. Now apply the energy equation Eq.7.13 for the total control volume

\(\begin{aligned}w &=-\int v dP +\frac{1}{2}\left( V _{1}^{2}- V _{3}^{2}\right)+ g \left( Z _{1}- Z _{3}\right) \\&=-0.001002(100-150)+\left[\frac{1}{2} \times 10.2^{2}+9.80665 \times 15\right] / 1000=0.25 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\dot{ W }=\dot{ m } w =2000 kg / s \times 0.25 kJ / kg = 5 0 0 kW\)
