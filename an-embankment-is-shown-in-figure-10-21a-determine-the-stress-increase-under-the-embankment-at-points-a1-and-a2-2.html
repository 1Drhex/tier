Guest Question.

An embankment is shown in Figure 10.21a. Determine the stress increase under the embankment at points\( A_{1}\) and \( A_{2}\)

Admin
Solution

γH = (17.5)(7)= 122.5 kN/\( m^{2} \)

Stress Increase at \( A_{1}\)

The left side of Figure 10.21b indicates that \( B_{1}=2.5m\) and \( B_{2}=14m\) .so

\( \frac{B_{1} }{z} =\frac{2.5}{5} =0.5;\frac{B_{2} }{z}=\frac{14}{5} =2.8\)

According to Figure 10.20, in this case, \( I_{2}=0.445\).Because the two sides in Figure 10.21b are symmetrical, the value of \( I_{2}\) for the right side will also be 0.445. So,

\( \Delta \sigma _{z} =\Delta \sigma _{z(1)}+\Delta \sigma _{z(2)}=q_{o} [I_{2(Left)}+ I_{2(Right)}]=122.5[0.445+0.445]=109.03 kN/m^{2}\)

Stress Increase at\( A_{2}\)

Refer to Figure 10.21c. For the left side,\( B_{2}=5m\) and \( B_{1}=0\) .so ,

\(\frac{B_{2} }{z} =\frac{5}{5} =1;\frac{B_{1} }{z} =\frac{0}{5} =0\)

According to Figure 10.20, for these values of \(B_{2} /z\) and \(B_{1} /z\), \( I_{2}=0.24\).so\( \Delta \sigma _{z(1)} =43.75(0.24)=10.5 kN/m^{2}\)

For the middle section,

\(\frac{B_{2} }{z} =\frac{14}{5} =2.8;\frac{B_{1} }{z}=\frac{14}{5} =2.8\)

Thus,\( I_{2}=0.495\).so,

\(\Delta \sigma _{z(2)}=0.495(122.5)=60.64 kN/m^{2}\)

For the right side,

\(\frac{B_{2} }{z} =\frac{9}{5} =1.8;\frac{B_{1} }{z}=\frac{0}{5} =0\)

and,\( I_{2}=0.335\).so,

\(\Delta \sigma _{z(3)}=(78.75)(0.335)=26.38 kN/m^{2}\)

Total stress increase at point \( A_{2}\) is

\(\Delta \sigma _{z}=\Delta \sigma _{z(1)}+\Delta \sigma _{z(2)}+\Delta \sigma _{z(3)}=10.5+60.64-26.38=44.76 kN/m^{2}\)
