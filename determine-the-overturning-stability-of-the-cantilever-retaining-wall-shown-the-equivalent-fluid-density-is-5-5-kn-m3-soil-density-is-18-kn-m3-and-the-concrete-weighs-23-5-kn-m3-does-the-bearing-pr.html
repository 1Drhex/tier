Guest Question.

Determine the overturning stability of the cantilever retaining wall shown. The equivalent fluid density is 5.5 kN/m3, soil density is 18 kN/m3, and the concrete weighs 23.5 kN/m3. Does the bearing pressure beneath the toe remain within the allowable bearing pressure limit?

Admin
Solution

Pmax = ω’ × h = (5.5kN/m3)(5.5m) = 30.25kN/m2

P = 1/2 Pmax (h)(1′) = 1/2 (30.25kN/m2)(5.5m )(1′) = 83.2kN

W1 = (0.5m)(5m)(1m)(23.5kN/m3) = 58.8kN

W2 = (0.5m)(3m)(1m)(23.5kN/m3) = 35.3kN

W3 = (2m)(5m)(1m)(18kN/m3) = 180kN

WT = 58.8kN + 35.3kN + 180kN = 274kN

R = \(\sqrt{W^{2}_{T} + P^{2}} = \sqrt{(274)^{2} + (83.3)^{2}}\) = 286kN

MRM = MA = W1 (0.75m) + W2 (1.5m) + W3 (2m)

MA = (58.8kN)(0.75m) + (35.3kN)(1.5m) + (180kN)(2m)

MA = 457kN – m

MA = WT × b = 457kN – m

b = \(\frac{457kN – m}{274kN}\) = 1.67m

MOTM = P × h/3 = (83.2kN)(1.83m) = 152kN – m

S.F. = \(\frac{M_{RM}}{M_{OTM}} = \frac{457kN – m}{152kN – m}\) = 3.0 > 1.5 ∴ OK

a/3 = 3m/3 = 1m 2a/3 = 2m

x = \(\frac{M_{RM} – M_{OTM}}{W_{T}} = \frac{(457 – 152)kN – m}{274kN}\) = 1.11m

a/3 < x = 1.11m < 2a/3

∴ Pmax = \(\frac{W_{T}}{a^{2}}(4a – 6x) = \frac{(274kN)}{(3m)^{2}}(4 × 3m – 6 × 1.11m)\)

Pmax = 163kN/m2 (3400psf) > 143.6kN/m2 (3000psf)

Overstressed in bearing
