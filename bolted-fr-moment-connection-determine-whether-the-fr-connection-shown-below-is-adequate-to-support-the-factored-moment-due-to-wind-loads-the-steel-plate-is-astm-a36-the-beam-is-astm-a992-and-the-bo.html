Guest Question.

Bolted FR Moment Connection

Determine whether the FR connection shown below is adequate to support the factored moment due to wind loads. The steel plate is ASTM A36, the beam is ASTM A992, and the bolts are 3⁄4-in. A325N in standard (STD) holes.

Admin
Solution

From AISCM, Table 1-1,

Table 1-1 Structural steel material properties

Steel Type ASTM Designation or Grade of Structural Steel \(F_{y}\) (ksi) \(F_{y}\) (ksi)
Carbon Steel A36 36 58–80
A53 Grade B 35 60
A500 Grade B 42 or 46 58
A500 Grade C 46 or 50 62
High-Strength, Low-Alloy A913 50–70 60–90
A992 50–65 65
A572 Grade 50 50 65
Corrosion-Resistant, High-Strength, Low-Alloy A242 50 70
A588 50 70

W16 × 45

d = 16.1 in.

\(b_{f}\) = 7.04 in.

\(t_{f}\) =0.565 in.

\(S_{x}= 72.7 in.^{3}\)

Flange force:

\(P_{uf}=\frac{M}{d}=\frac{150}{16.1/12}=112\) kips

When the flanges of moment connections are bolted, the flexural strength of the beam is reduced due to the presence of the holes at the connection. The following provisions apply (AISC specification, Section F13):

When \(F_{u}A_{fn}\geq Y_{t}F_{y}A_{fg}\), (11-12)

the reduced flexural strength does not need to be checked. When \(F_{u}A_{fn}\lt Y_{t}F_{y}A_{fg},\) the design flexural strength at the moment connection is

\(\phi _{b}M_{n}=\phi _{b}F_{u}S_{x}\left(\frac{A_{fn} }{A_{fg} } \right),\) (11-13)

where

\(\phi _{b} = 0.9,\)

\(F_{u}\) = Tensile rupture strength,

\(S_{x}\) = Section modulus,

\(A_{fg}\) = Gross area of tension flange,

\(A_{fn}\) = Net area of tension flange, and

\(Y_{t} =1.0 for F_{y}/F_{u}≤0.8\)

= 1.1 for all other cases.

Check the reduced flexural strength of the W16 × 45:

\(F_{y}/F_{u} =50/65 =0.77< 0.080 ∴ Y_{t} =1.0\)

 

\(A_{fg}= (7.04)(0.565) = 3.98 in.^{2}\)

 

\(A_{fn} =A_{fg} – \sum{A_{holes} }\)

 

\(=3.98 – \left[(2)\left(\frac{3}{4}+\frac{1}{8}(0.565) \right) \right]=2.99 in.^{2}\)

 

\(F_{u} A_{fn}\)= (65)(2.99) = 194 kips

\(Y_{t}F_{y}A_{fg}\) = (1.0)(50)(3.98) = 199 kips

194 k < 199 k → Reduced flexural strength must be checked.

\(\phi _{b}M_{n}=\phi _{b}F_{u}S_{x}\left(\frac{A_{fn} }{A_{fg} } \right)=(0.9)(65)(72.7)\left(\frac{2.99}{3.90} \right)= 3195 in.-kips = 266 ft.-kips \gt 150\) ft.-kips OK

Check shear on bolts:

From AISCM, Table 7-1, \(\phi _{v}r_{n} =15.9\) kips/bolt.

Table 7-1 Reduction coefficients \(R_{g} \) and \(R_{p} \)

Framing Condition \(R_{g} \) \(R_{p} \) Notes
No deck interference (or no deck for formed concrete slabs) 1·0 1·0 Shear connectors are welded directly to the beam flange; miscellaneous deck fillers can only be placed over less than 50% of the beam flange
Deck ribs oriented parallel to beam (i.e., girders) \(\frac{w_{r} }{h_{r} }\geq 1.5 \) 1·0 0.75
\(\frac{w_{r} }{h_{r} }\lt 1.5\) 0.85 0.75 Value for \(R_{g} \)= 0.85 only applies for a single stud
Deck ribs oriented perpendicular to beam 1 stud per rib 1·0 0.6 Values for \(R_{p} \) may be increased to 0.75 when \(e_{mid-ht}\geq 2\) in. (i.e., when studs are placed in the (“strong” position, see Figure 7-6)
2 studs per rib 0.85 0.6
3 or more shear studs per rib 0.7 0.6

\(N_{b,required}= \frac{P_{uf} }{\phi _{v}r_{n} }=\frac{112}{15.9} = 7.05\lt 8\) bolts provided OK

Check bolt bearing:

Since the thickness of the plate is less than the flange thickness, and since \(F_{u}\) for the plate is less than \(F_{u}\) for the beam, the bolt bearing on the plate will control. Figure 11-18 illustrates the failure modes for bearing, tension, and block shear.

\(L_{c1}= 2 in. – 0.5\left(\frac{3}{4}+\frac{1}{8} \right)= 1.56\) in.

\(L_{c2}=L_{c3}=L_{c4}= 3 in. -\left(\frac{3}{4}+\frac{1}{8} \right) = 2.13\) in.

For bolt 1, \(\phi R_{n} = \phi 1.2L_{c}tF_{u}\leq \phi 2.4dtF_{u}\)

= (0.75)(1.2)(1.56 in.)(0.5 in.)(58 ksi) < (0.75)(2.4)(0.75)(0.5 in.)(58 ksi)

=40.7 kips > 39.2 kips

\(\phi R_{n} =39.2\) K.

For bolts 2, 3, and 4,

\(\phi R_{n}\) = (0.75)(1.2)(2.13 in.)(0.5 in.)(58 ksi) < (0.75)(2.4)(0.75)(0.5 in.)(58 ksi)

=55.4 kips > 39.2 kips

= 39.2 kips

\(\phi R_{n}\) = [(2)(39.2 kips)]+[(6)(39.2 kips)]=313 kips > \(P_{uf} = 112\) kips OK for bearing

Check tension on gross and net area of the flange plate:

\(A_{g} = (0.5 in.)(8 in.) = 4in.^{2}\)

 

\(A_{n} =A_{g} -\sum{A_{holes} } = 4 – \left[(2)\left(\frac{3}{4}+\frac{1}{8} \right)(0.5) \right]= 3.13 in.^{2} \)

 

\(A_{n} ≤ 0.85 A_{g}=(0.85)(4) 3.4 in.^{2}\) OK

\(A_{e}=A_{n}U =(3.13 in.^{2})(1.0) 3.13 in.^{2}\)

 

Strength based on gross area is

\(\phi P_{n}=\phi F_{y}A_{g}= (0.9)(36)(4 in.^{2}) = 129 kips \gt P_{uf} = 112\) kips OK

Strength based on effective area is

\(\phi P_{n}=\phi F_{u}A_{e}= (0.75)(58)(3.13 in.^{2}) = 136 kips \gt P_{uf} = 112\) kips OK

Block shear:

\(A_{gv}=(2)(3 in. + 3 in. + 3 in. + 2 in.)(0.5 in.) = 11 in.^{2}\)

 

\(A_{gt}=(1.75 in. + 1.75 in.)(0.5 in.) =1.75 in.^{2}\)

 

\(A_{nv}=A_{gv}-\sum{A_{holes} } = 11 – \left[(3.5)(2)\left(\frac{3}{4}+\frac{1}{8} \right)(0.5) \right]= 7.93 in.^{2}\)

 

\(A_{nt}=A_{gt}-\sum{A_{holes} } = 1.75 – \left[\left(\frac{3}{4}+\frac{1}{8} \right)(0.5) \right]= 1.31 in.^{2}\)

 

The available block shear strength is found from equation (4-11)(\(U_{bs}\) = 1.0):

\(\phi P_{n} =\phi (0.60F_{u}A_{nu}+U_{bs}F_{u}A_{nt} )\leq \phi (0.60F_{y}A_{gv}+U_{bs}F_{u}A_{nt})\) (4-11)

=0.75[(0.60)(58)(7.93) + (1.0)(58)(1.31)]

≤ 0.75[(0.60)(36)(11) + (1.0)(58)(1.31)]

263 kips > 235 kips

\(\phi P_{n} =235 kips > P_{uf}= 112\) kips OK

Compression:

K = 0.65 (Figure 5-3)

L = 3 in.

\(r_{y}= \frac{t}{\sqrt{12} }=\frac{0.5}{\sqrt{12} }= 0.144\) in.

\(\frac{KL}{r}=\frac{(0.65)(3)}{0.144} =13.5\)

 

Since KL/r < 25, \(F_{cr} = F_{y}\) (AISC specification, Section J4.4).

\(\phi P_{n}= \phi F_{cr}A_{g}= (0.9)(36)(0.5)(8) = 129 kips\gt P_{uf} = 112\) kips OK

The connection is adequate for the applied moment. Note that the column should also be checked for the need of stiffeners to support the concentrated flange force (see ).
