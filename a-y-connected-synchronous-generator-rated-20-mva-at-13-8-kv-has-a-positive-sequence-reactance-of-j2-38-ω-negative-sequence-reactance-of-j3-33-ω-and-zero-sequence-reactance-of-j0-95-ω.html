Guest Question.

A Y-connected synchronous generator rated 20 MVA at 13.8 kV has a positive-sequence reactance of j2.38 Ω, negative-sequence reactance of j3.33 Ω, and zero-sequence reactance of j0.95 Ω. The generator neutral is solidly grounded. With the generator operating unloaded at rated voltage, a so-called single line-to-ground fault occurs at the machine terminals. During this fault, the line-to-ground voltages at the generator terminals are \(V_{a g}=0, V_{b g}=8.071 \angle-102.25^{\circ}, \text { and } V_{c g}=8.071 \angle 102.25^{\circ} kV\). Determine the sequence components of the generator fault currents and the generator fault currents. Draw a phasor diagram of the prefault and postfault generator terminal voltages. (Note: For this fault, the sequence components of the generator fault currents are all equal to each other.)

Admin
Solution

The line-to-ground fault on phase a of the machine is shown below, along with the corresponding sequence networks:

With the base voltage to neutral \(\frac{13.8}{\sqrt{3}} kV\),

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{V}_{a}=0 ; \quad \bar{V}_{b} &=1.013 \angle-102.25^{\circ} ; & \bar{V}_{c}=& 1.013 \angle 102.25^{\circ} pu . \\&=(-0.215-j 0.99) pu & &=(-0.215+j 0.99) pu\end{aligned}\)

 

with \(Z_{\text {base }}=\frac{(13.8)^{2}}{20}=9.52 \Omega, \bar{Z}_{1}=\frac{j 2.38}{9.52}=j 0.25 ; \bar{Z}_{2}=\frac{j 3.33}{9.52}=j 0.35\);

\(\bar{Z}_{g 0}=\frac{j 0.95}{9.52}=j 0.1 ;\bar{Z}_{n}=0 ; \bar{Z}_{0}=j 0.1 pu\)

The symmetrical components of the voltages at the fault point are

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\bar{V}_{a 0} \\\bar{V}_{a 1} \\\bar{V}_{a 2}\end{array}\right]=\frac{1}{3}\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\1 & a & a^{2} \\1 & a^{2} & a\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}0 \\-0.215-j 0.99 \\-0.215+j 0.99\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}-0.143+j 0 \\0.643+j 0 \\-0.500+j 0\end{array}\right] pu\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{I}_{a 0}=-\frac{\bar{V}_{a 0}}{\bar{Z}_{g 0}}=-\frac{(-0.143+j 0)}{j 0.1}=-j 1.43 pu \\&\bar{I}_{a 1}=\frac{\bar{E}_{a n}-\bar{V}_{a 1}}{\bar{Z}_{1}}=\frac{(1+j 0)-(0.643+j 0)}{j 0.25}=-j 1.43 pu \\&\bar{I}_{a 2}=-\frac{\bar{V}_{a 2}}{\bar{Z}_{2}}=-\frac{(-0.5+j 0)}{j 0.35}=-j 1.43 pu\end{aligned}\)

 

∴ Fault current into the ground \(\bar{I}_{a}=\bar{I}_{a 0}+\bar{I}_{a 1}+\bar{I}_{a 2}=3 \bar{I}_{a 0}=-j 4.29 pu\)

With base current \(\frac{20,000}{\sqrt{3} \times 13.8}=837 A\) , the subtransient current in line a is

\(I_{a}=4.29 \times 837=3590 A\)

Line-to-line voltages during the fault are: (on base voltage to neutral)

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{V}_{a b}=\bar{V}_{a}-\bar{V}_{b}=0.215+j 0.99=1.01 \angle 77.7^{\circ} pu =8.05 \angle 77.7^{\circ} kV \\&\bar{V}_{b c}=\bar{V}_{b}-\bar{V}_{c}=0-j 1.98=1.98 \angle 270^{\circ} pu =15.78 \angle 270^{\circ} kV \\&\bar{V}_{c a}=\bar{V}_{c}-\bar{V}_{a}=-0.215+j 0.99=1.01 \angle 102.3^{\circ} pu =8.05 \angle 102.3^{\circ} kV\end{aligned}\)
