Guest Question.

Steam at 800 kPa and 200°C in a rigid vessel is to be condensed by cooling. Determine pressure and temperature corresponding to condensation.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{Here steam is kept in rigid vessel, therefore its’ specific volume shall remain constant.}\\\text{Specific volume at initial state, 800 kPa, 200°C, }v_{1} ,\\\text{It is superheated steam as }T_{sat} = \text{170.43°C at 800 kPa.}\\\text{From superheated steam table; }v_{1} = 0.2404 m^{3}/kg.\\\text{At the begining of condensation, specific volume }= 0.2404 m^{3}/kg.\\\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad v_{2} = 0.2404 m^{3}/kg\\\text{This }v_{2}\text{ shall be specific volume corresponding to saturated vapour state for condensation.}\\\text{Thus }v_{2} = v_{g} = 0.2404 m^{3}/kg\\\text{Looking into steam table }v_{g} = 0.2404 m^{3}/kg\text{ shall lie between temperatures 175°C }(v_{g} = 0.2168 m^{3}/kg)\\\text{and 170°C }(v_{g} = 0.2428 m^{3}/kg)\text{ and pressures 892 kPa (175°C) and 791.7 kPa (170°C).}\\\text{By interpolation, temperature at begining of condensation}\\\begin{aligned}T_{2}&= 175 – \frac{(175 – 170) × (0.2404 – 0.2168)}{(0.2428 – 0.2168)}\\T_{2}&= 170.46°C\\\text{Similarly, pressure, }p_{2}&= 892 – \frac{(892 – 791.7) × (0.2404 – 0.2168)}{(0.2428 – 0.2168)}\\p_{2}&= 800.96 kPa\\\text{Pressure and temperature at condensation}&= \textbf{800.96 kPa and 170.46°C}\end{aligned}\)
