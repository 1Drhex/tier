Guest Question.

Consider a 5 m high and 8 m long and 0.22 m thick wall whose representation is shown in Fig. 3.22(a). The thermal conductivity of various materials used are \(k_A = k_F = 2, k_B = 8, k_C = 20, k_D = 15,\) and \(k_E = 35 W/m.K.\) The left surface of the wall is maintained at uniform temperatures of 300°C. The right surface is exposed to convection environment at 50°C with h = 20 \(W/m^2.K. \)Determine (a) one dimensional heat transfer rate through the wall, (b) temperature at the point where section B, D and E meet, and (c) temperature drop across the section F.

Admin
Solution

Given : A composite wall
\(A_{wall} = 5 m × 8 m = 40 m^2\)
For representative cross-section of wall
\(k_A = kF = 2 W/m.K,\)
\(k_B = 8 W/m.K,\)
\(k_C = 20 W/m.K,\)
\(k_D = 15 W/m.K\)
\(k_E = 35 W/m.K,\)
\(L_A = 1 cm = 0.01 m,\)
\(L_B =L_C = 5 cm = 0.05 m\)
\(L_D =L_E = 10 cm = 0.1 m,\)
\(L_F = 6 cm = 0.06 m\)
\(z = 6 cm + 6 cm = 12 cm = 0.12 m,\)
\(z_B = z_C = 4 cm = 0.04 m\)
w = 100 cm = 1 m,
\(z_D = z_E = 6 cm = 0.06 m,\)
\(T_1 = 300°C,\)
\(T_∞ = 50°C,\)
\(h = 20 W/m^2.K.\)

To find :
(i) Heat transfer rate through composite wall,
(ii) Temperature \(T_2\) at the point where section B, D and E meet, and
(iii) Temperature drop across the section F.
Assumptions :
(i) Steady state heat conduction.
(ii) No contact resistance at interfaces.
(iii) No radiation heat transfer in the system.
Analysis : (i) Equivalent thermal network for given composite wall is shown in Fig. 3.22 (b).
The cross-section area of representative portion of wall
\(A_1 = w × z = (1 m) × (0.12 m) = 0.12 m^2\)
Area for section B and C,
\(A_2 = w × z_B = 1 m × 0.04 = 0.04 m^2\)
Area for section B and D,
\(A_3 = w × z_D = 1 m × 0.06 m = 0.06 m^2\)

Individual thermal resistances of thermal
network
\(R_A = \frac{L_A}{k_A A_1}=\frac{0.01}{2 ×0.12} = 0.04167 K/W\)
\(R_B = \frac{L_B}{k_B A_2}=\frac{0.05}{8 ×0.04} = 0.15625 K/W\)
\(R_C = \frac{L_C}{k_C A_2}=\frac{0.05}{20 ×0.04}= 0.0625 K/W\)
\(R_D = \frac{L_D}{k_D A_3}=\frac{0.1}{15 ×0.06}= 0.111 K/W\)
\(R_E = \frac{L_E}{k_E A_3}=\frac{0.1}{35 ×0.06}= 0.0476 K/W\)
\(R_F = \frac{L_F}{k_AF A_1}=\frac{0.06}{2 ×0.12}= 0.25 K/W\)
\(R_{conv} = \frac{1}{h A_1}=\frac{1}{20 ×0.12}= 0.4167 K/W\)
The resistance \(R_B \) and \(R_C \) are parallel as shown in thermal network Fig. 3.32(b), their equivalent resistance
\(\frac{1}{R_{eq_1}}=\frac{1}{R_{C}}+\frac{1}{R_{B}}+\frac{1}{R_{C}}\)
\(=\frac{1}{0.0625} + \frac{1}{0.15625} + \frac{1}{0.0625}\)

\(R_{eq_1} = \frac{1}{38.4} = 0.0260 K/W\)
Further, the resistance \(R_D\) and \(R_E\) are parallel as shown and their equivalent resistance :
\(\frac{1}{R_{eq_2}}=\frac{1}{R_{D}}+\frac{1}{R_{E}}\)
\(=\frac{1}{0.111} + \frac{1}{0.0476} = 30.017 W/K\)
\(∴ R_{eq_2} = \frac{1}{30.017} = 0.0333 K/W\)
Now, resistances \(R_A, R_{eq_1}, R_{eq_2} , R_F\) and \(R_{conv}\) are in series as shown in Fig. 3.22(c)

Heat flow rate in representative section of wall
\(Q =\frac{T_{s} – T_{∞}}{\sum R_{th}}=\frac {300 – 50}{0.7677} = 325.65 W\)
Heat transfer rate from total surface of the wall
\(= Q ×\frac{Wall area}{ Representative area}\)

\(= Q × \frac{A_{wall}}{A_1}= 325.65 ×\frac{40}{0.12}\)
\(= 108.55 × 10^3 W = 108.55 kW. \)
(ii) Temperature \(T_2 :\)
Considering the heat flow through resistances \(R_A \)and \(R_{eq_1} ,\)
\(Q =\frac{T_{s} – T_{2}}{R_A + R_{eq _1}}\)

or \(T_2 =T_s – Q × (R_A + R_{eq_1} )\)
\(= 300 – 325.65 × (0.04167 + 0.0260)\)
\(= 300 – 22.03 ≈ 278°C. \)
(iii) Temperature drop across section F :
The heat flow through section F can be expressed as :
\(Q = \frac{T_{3} – T_{4}}{R_F} or T3_ – T_4 = Q R_F\)
= 325.65 × 0.25 = 81.41°C.
