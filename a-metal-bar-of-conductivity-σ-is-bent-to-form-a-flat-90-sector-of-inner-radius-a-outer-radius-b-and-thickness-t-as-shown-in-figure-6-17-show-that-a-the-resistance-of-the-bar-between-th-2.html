Guest Question.

A metal bar of conductivity σ is bent to form a flat 90° sector of inner radius a, outer radius b, and thickness t as shown in Figure 6.17. Show that (a) the resistance of the bar between the vertical curved surfaces at ρ= a and ρ= b is

\(R=\frac{2\ln \frac{b}{a} }{\sigma \pi t}\)

and (b) the resistance between the two horizontal surfaces at z = 0 and z = t is

\(R'=\frac{4t}{\sigma \pi (b^{2}-a^{2})}\)

Admin
Solution

(a) Between the vertical curved ends located at ρ= a and ρ= b, the bar has a nonuniform cross section and hence eq. (5.16) does not apply. We have to use eq. (6.16). Let a potential difference \(V_{0}\) be maintained between the curved surfaces at ρ= a and p = b so that V(ρ= a) = 0 and V(ρ= b) =\( V_{0}\) • We solve for V in Laplace’s equation\( ∇^{2}V = 0 \)in cylindrical coordinates. Since V = V(ρ),

\(R=\frac{V}{I}=\frac{\int\limits_{L}^{}{E.dl} }{\int\limits_{S}^{}{\sigma E.dS} } \)(6.16)
\(R=\frac{V}{I}=\frac{\ell }{\sigma S}\) (5.16)

\(\triangledown ^{2}V=\frac{1}{\rho }\frac{d}{d\rho }(\rho \frac{dV}{d\rho } ) =0\)

As ρ= 0 is excluded, upon multiplying by ρ and integrating once, this becomes
\(\rho \frac{dV}{d\rho } =A\)
or
\(\frac{dV}{d\rho } =\frac{A}{\rho }\)

Integrating once again yields
\(V=A\ln \rho +B\)

where A and B are constants of integration to be determined from the boundary conditions
\(V(\rho =a)=0\rightarrow 0=A\ln a+B\) or \( B=-A\ln a\)
\(V(\rho =b)=V_{0}\rightarrow V_{0}=A\ln b+B=A\ln b-A\ln a=A\ln \frac{b}{a}\) or \(A=\frac{V_{0}}{\ln \frac{b}{a}}\)
Hence,
\(V=A\ln \rho -A\ln a=A\ln \frac{\rho }{a} =\frac{V_{0}}{\ln \frac{b}{a}}\ln \frac{\rho }{a}\)
\(E=-\triangledown V=-\frac{dV}{d\rho }a_{\rho }=-\frac{A}{\rho }a_{\rho } =- \frac{V_{0}}{\rho \ln \frac{b}{a}}a_{\rho }\)
\(J=\sigma E, dS=-\rho d\phi dz a_{\rho }\)
\(I=\int\limits_{S}^{}{J.dS}=\int\limits_{\phi =0}^{\pi /2}{}\int\limits_{z=0}^{t}{\frac{V_{0}\sigma }{\rho \ln \frac{b}{a}} } dz \rho d\phi =\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{tV_{0}\sigma }{\ln \frac{b}{a}}\)

Thus
\(R=\frac{V_{0}}{I}=\frac{2\ln \frac{b}{a}}{\sigma \pi t}\)

as required.
(b) Let \(V_{0}\) be the potential difference between the two horizontal surfaces so that V(z = 0) = 0 and\( V(z = t) = V_{0}\). V = V(z), so Laplace,s equation \( ∇^{2}V = 0 \) becomes
\(\frac{d^{2}V}{dz^{2}}=0\)

Integrating twice gives
V=Az+B

We apply the boundary conditions to determine A and B:
\(V(z=0)=0\rightarrow 0=0+B\) or \(B=0\)
\(V(z=t)=V_{0}\rightarrow V_{0}=At \)or \(A=\frac{V_{0}}{t}\)
Hence,
\(V=\frac{V_{0}}{t}z\)
\(E=-\triangledown V=-\frac{dV}{dz}a_{z }=-\frac{V_{0}}{t }a_{z}\)
\(J=\sigma E=-\frac{\sigma V_{0}}{t}a_{z} , dS=-\rho d\phi d\rho a_{z }\)
\(I=\int\limits_{S}^{}{J.dS}=\int\limits_{\rho =0}^{b}{}\int\limits_{\phi =0}^{\pi /2}{\frac{V_{0}\sigma }{t} } \rho d\phi d\rho = \frac{V_{0}\sigma }{t}.\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\rho ^{2}}{2}|_{a}^{b}=\frac{V_{0}\sigma \pi (b^{2}-a^{2})}{4t}\)
Thus
\(R'=\frac{V_{0}}{I}=\frac{4t}{\sigma \pi (b^{2}-a^{2})}\)

Alternatively, for this case, the cross section of the bar is uniform between the horizon – tal surfaces at z = 0 and z = t and eq. (5.16) holds. Hence,

\(R'=\frac{\ell }{\sigma S}=\frac{t}{\sigma \frac{\pi}{4}(b^{2}-a^{2}) }\)
\(=\frac{4t}{\sigma \pi (b^{2}-a^{2}) }\)

as required.
