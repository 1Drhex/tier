Guest Question.

In the op amp circuit of Fig. 8.112, determine \(v_{0}\)(t) for t>0 . Let \(v_{in}\)=u(t) V, \(R_{1}=R_{2}=10k\Omega\) , \(C_{1}=C_{2}=100 \mu F\)

Admin
Solution

At node 1 ,

\(\frac{\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{in}}-\mathrm{v}_{1}}{\mathrm{R}_{1}}=\mathrm{C}_{1} \frac{\mathrm{d}\left(\mathrm{v}_{1}-\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}\right)}{\mathrm{dt}}+\mathrm{C}_{2} \frac{\mathrm{d}\left(\mathrm{v}_{1}-0\right)}{\mathrm{dt}} (1)\)

At node 2 ,

\(\mathrm{C}_{2} \frac{\mathrm{d}\left(\mathrm{v}_{1}-0\right)}{\mathrm{dt}}=\frac{0-\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{R}_{2}}, \text { or } \frac{\mathrm{d} \mathrm{v}_{1}}{\mathrm{dt}}=\frac{-\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{C}_{2} \mathrm{R}_{2}} (2)\)

From (1) and (2)

\(\mathrm{v}_{\text {in }}-\mathrm{v}_{1}=-\frac{\mathrm{R}_{1} \mathrm{C}_{1}}{\mathrm{C}_{2} \mathrm{R}_{2}} \frac{\mathrm{d} \mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{dt}}-\mathrm{R}_{1} \mathrm{C}_{1} \frac{\mathrm{d} \mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{dt}}-\mathrm{R}_{1} \frac{\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{R}_{2}} \\\)

 

\(\mathrm{v}_{1}=\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{in}}+\frac{\mathrm{R}_{1} \mathrm{C}_{1}}{\mathrm{C}_{2} \mathrm{R}_{2}} \frac{\mathrm{d} \mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{dt}}+\mathrm{R}_{1} \mathrm{C}_{1} \frac{\mathrm{d} \mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{dt}}+\mathrm{R}_{1} \frac{\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{R}_{2}} (3)\)

 

From (2) and (3)

\(-\frac{\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{C}_{2} \mathrm{R}_{2}}=\frac{\mathrm{d} \mathrm{v}_{1}}{\mathrm{dt}}=\frac{\mathrm{d} \mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{in}}}{\mathrm{dt}}+\frac{\mathrm{R}_{1} \mathrm{C}_{1}}{\mathrm{C}_{2} \mathrm{R}_{2}} \frac{\mathrm{d} \mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{dt}}+\mathrm{R}_{1} \mathrm{C}_{1} \frac{\mathrm{d}^{2} \mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{dt}^{2}}+\frac{\mathrm{R}_{1}}{\mathrm{R}_{2}} \frac{\mathrm{d} \mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{dt}} \\\)

 

\(\frac{\mathrm{d}^{2} \mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{dt}^{2}}+\frac{1}{\mathrm{R}_{2}}\left(\frac{1}{\mathrm{C}_{1}}+\frac{1}{\mathrm{C}_{2}}\right) \frac{\mathrm{d} \mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{dt}}+\frac{\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{C}_{1} \mathrm{C}_{2} \mathrm{R}_{2} \mathrm{R}_{1}}=-\frac{1}{\mathrm{R}_{1} \mathrm{C}_{1}} \frac{\mathrm{d} \mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{in}}}{\mathrm{dt}} \\\)

 

\(\text { But } \mathrm{C}_{1} \mathrm{C}_{2} \mathrm{R}_{1} \mathrm{R}_{2}=10^{-4} \times 10^{-4} \times 10^{4} \times 10^{4}=1\)

 

\(\frac{1}{\mathrm{R}_{2}}\left(\frac{1}{\mathrm{C}_{1}}+\frac{1}{\mathrm{C}_{2}}\right)=\frac{2}{\mathrm{R}_{2} \mathrm{C}_{1}}=\frac{2}{10^{4} \times 10^{-4}}=2 \\\)

 

\(\frac{\mathrm{d}^{2} \mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{dt}^{2}}+2 \frac{\mathrm{d} \mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{dt}}+\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}=-\frac{\mathrm{d} \mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{in}}}{\mathrm{dt}}\)

Which leads to \(\mathrm{s}^{2}+2 \mathrm{s}+1=0\) or \((\mathrm{s}+1)^{2}=0\) and \(\mathrm{s}=-1,-1\)

\(\text { Therefore, } \quad \mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}(\mathrm{t})=\left[(\mathrm{A}+\mathrm{Bt}) \mathrm{e}^{-t}\right]+\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{f}}\)

 

As t approaches infinity, the capacitor acts like an open circuit so that

\(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{f}}=\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}(\infty)=0\)

\(\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{in}}=10 \mathrm{u}(\mathrm{t}) \mathrm{mV}\) and the fact that the initial voltages across each capacitor is 0
means that \(\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}(0)=0\) which leads to \(\mathrm{A}=0\)

\(\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}(\mathrm{t})=\left[\mathrm{Bte}^{-\mathrm{t}}\right] \)

 

\(\frac{\mathrm{dv}_{\mathrm{o}}}{\mathrm{dt}}=\left[(\mathrm{B}-\mathrm{Bt}) \mathrm{e}^{-\mathrm{t}}\right] (4)\)

From (2)

\(\frac{\mathrm{dv}_{\mathrm{o}}(0+)}{\mathrm{dt}}=-\frac{\mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}(0+)}{\mathrm{C}_{2} \mathrm{R}_{2}}=0\)

From (1) at t=0+ \(\frac{1-0}{\mathrm{R}_{1}}=-\mathrm{C}_{1} \frac{\mathrm{dv}_{\mathrm{o}}(0+)}{\mathrm{dt}}\) which leads to \(\frac{\mathrm{d} \mathrm{v}_{\mathrm{o}}(0+)}{\mathrm{dt}}=-\frac{1}{\mathrm{C}_{1} \mathrm{R}_{1}}=-1\)
Substituting this into (4) gives B =-1
Thus,

\(\mathrm{v}(\mathrm{t})={-\mathrm{te}^{-\mathrm{t}} {u}({t}) {V}}\)
