Guest Question.

In the gear system shown in Figure P6.28/29, the motor applies a torque of 400 N-m to the gear at A. A torque of \(T_{C}\) = 700 N-m is removed from the shaft at gear C, and the remaining torque is removed at gear D. Segments (1) and (2) are solid steel [G = 80 GPa] shafts, and the bearings shown allow free rotation of the shaft.
(a) Determine the minimum permissible diameters for segments (1) and (2) of the shaft if the maximum shear stress must not exceed 40 MPa.
(b) If the same diameter is to be used for segments (1) and (2), determine the minimum permissible diameter that can be used for the shaft if the maximum shear stress must not exceed 40 MPa and the rotation angle of gear D relative to gear B must not exceed 3.0Â°.

Admin
Solution

Torque relationship:

\(\frac{T_{A}}{100 mm }=\frac{T_{B}}{300 mm }\) hence \(T_{B}=T_{A} \frac{300 mm }{100 mm }=3 T_{A}=3(400 N – m )=1,200 N – m\)

Shaft (1) has the same torque as gear B; therefore, \(T_{1}\) = 1,200 N-m. A torque of \(T_{C}\) = 700 N-m is removed from the shaft at gear C. The torque in shaft (2) must satisfy equilibrium:

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{x} &=-T_{1}+T_{C}+T_{2}=0 \\& \therefore T_{2}=T_{1}-T_{C}=1,200 N – m -700 N – m =500 N – m\end{aligned}\)

To summarize:

\(\begin{array}{|ll|}\hline T_{1}=1,200 N – m & T_{2}=500 N – m \\\hline\end{array}\)

(a) Determine minimum diameters: The elastic torsion formula can be rearranged and rewritten in terms of the unknown diameter d:

\(\tau=\frac{T c}{J} \quad \therefore \frac{J}{c}=\frac{\frac{\pi}{32} d^{4}}{d / 2}=\frac{\pi}{16} d^{3}=\frac{T}{\tau}\)

Using this expression, solve for the minimum acceptable diameter of each shaft:

\(\begin{aligned}d_{1}^{3} & \geq \frac{16}{\pi} \frac{T_{1}}{\tau_{\text {allow }}}=\frac{16(1,200 N – m )(1,000 mm / m )}{\pi\left(40 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=152,788.745 mm ^{3} \\& \therefore d_{1} \geq 53.460 mm =53.5 mm\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}d_{2}^{3} & \geq \frac{16}{\pi} \frac{T_{2}}{\tau_{\text {allow }}}=\frac{16(500 N – m )(1,000 mm / m )}{\pi\left(40 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=63,661.977 mm ^{3} \\& \therefore d_{2} \geq 39.929 mm =39.9 mm\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) The rotation angle of gear D with respect to gear B is equal to the sum of the angles of twist in shaft segments (1) and (2):

\(\phi_{D}-\phi_{B}=\phi_{1}+\phi_{2} \leq\left(3^{\circ}\right)\left(\pi / 180^{\circ}\right)=0.052360 rad\)

Since the same solid steel shaft is to be used for both segments (1) and (2):

\(0.052360 rad \geq \frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}=\frac{1}{J G}\left[T_{1} L_{1}+T_{2} L_{2}\right]\)

or rearranging:

\(\begin{gathered}J=\frac{\pi}{32} d^{4} \geq \frac{(1,200 N – m )(1.5 m )(1,000 mm / m )^{2}+(500 N – m )(1.0 m )(1,000 mm / m )^{2}}{\left(80,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)(0.052360 rad )} \\=549,083.270 mm ^{4} \\\therefore d \geq 48.6 mm\end{gathered}\)

Since the shaft has already been designed for stresses and since the diameter that is required to satisfy the rotation angle requirement is smaller than \(d_{1}\) determined in part (a), the minimum diameter required for a constant diameter shaft between gears B and D is:

\(d_{\min }=53.5 mm\)
