Solution

Assuming the Prandtl number is reasonably constant,

\(r=\sqrt{\operatorname{Pr}^{*}}=\sqrt{\operatorname{Pr}}=\sqrt{0.71}=0.843\)

Also, because the flat plate is at the adiabatic wall temperature, from ,

\(\frac{T_{w}}{T_{e}}=\frac{T_{a w}}{T_{e}}=2.467\)

So the Meador-Smart equation becomes

\(\frac{T^{*}}{T_{e}}=0.45+0.55(2.467)+0.16(0.843)(0.2) M_{e}^{2}\)

or \(\frac{T^{*}}{T_{e}}=1.807+0.027 M_{e}^{2}\)

For \(M_{e}=2.94\), we have

\(\frac{T^{*}}{T_{e}}=1.807+0.027(2.94)^{2}=2.04\)

\(T^{*}=2.04 T_{e}=2.04(288)=587.5 K\)

\(\rho^{*}=\frac{p^{*}}{R T^{*}}=\frac{1.01 \times 10^{5}}{(287)(587.5)}=0.599 kg / m ^{3}\)

\(\frac{\mu^{*}}{\mu_{0}}=\left(\frac{T^{*}}{T_{0}}\right)^{3 / 2} \frac{T_{0}+110}{T^{*}+110}=\left(\frac{587.5}{288}\right)^{3 / 2} \frac{288+110}{587.5+110}=1.664\)

\(\mu^{*}=1.664 \mu_{0}=1.664\left(1.7894 \times 10^{-5}\right)=2.978 \times 10^{-5}\)

\(\operatorname{Re}_{c}^{*}=\frac{\rho^{*} u_{e} c}{\mu^{*}}=\frac{(0.500)(1000)(2)}{2.978 \times 10^{-5}}=4.02 \times 10^{7}\)

\(C_{f}^{*}=\frac{1.328}{\sqrt{ Re _{c}^{*}}}=\frac{1.328}{\sqrt{4.02 \times 10^{7}}}=2.09 \times 10^{4}\)

\(D_{f}=\frac{1}{2} \rho^{*} V_{\infty}^{2} S C_{f}^{*}=\frac{1}{2}(0.599)(1000)^{2}(40)\left(2.09 \times 10^{-4}\right)=2504 N\)

\(D=2(2504)=5008 N\)
