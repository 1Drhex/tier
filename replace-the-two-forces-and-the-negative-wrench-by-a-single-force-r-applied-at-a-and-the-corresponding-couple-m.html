Guest Question.

Replace the two forces and the negative wrench by a single force R applied at A and the corresponding couple M.

Admin
Solution

The resultant force has the components

\([R_{x}=\sum{F_{x} } ] R_{x}=500\sin40^{\circ } +700\sin60^{\circ } =928\) N

\([R_{y}=\sum{F_{y} } ] R_{y}=600+500\cos 40^{\circ }\cos 45^{\circ } =817\) N

\([R_{z}=\sum{F_{z} } ] R_{z}=700\cos 60^{\circ } +500\cos 40^{\circ }\sin45^{\circ } =621\) N

Thus, \(R=928i+871j+621k\) N

and \(R=\sqrt{(928)^{2}+(817)^{2}+(621)^{2} } =1416 \) N

The couple to be added as a result of moving the 500-N force is

\([M=r\times F]\) \(M_{500}=(0.08i+0.12j+0.05k)\times 500(i\sin40^{\circ }+j\cos 40^{\circ }\cos 45^{\circ }+k\cos 40^{\circ }\sin45^{\circ })\)

where r is the vector from A to B.

The term-by-term, or determinant, expansion gives

\(M_{500}=18.95i-5.59j-16.90k\) \(N\cdot m\)

The moment of the 600-N force about A is written by inspection of its x- and z-components, which gives

\(M_{600}=(600)(0.060)i+(600)(0.040)k\)

\(=36.0i+24.0k\) \(N\cdot m\)

The moment of the 700-N force about A is easily obtained from the moments of the x- and z-components of the force. The result becomes

\(M_{700}=(700\cos 60^{\circ })(0.030)i-[(700\sin60^{\circ })(0.060)+(700\cos 60^{\circ })(0.100)]j-(700\sin60^{\circ })(0.030)k\)

\(=10.5i-71.4j-18.19k\) \(N\cdot m\)

Also, the couple of the given wrench may be written

\(M^{\prime } =25.0(-i\sin40^{\circ }-j\cos 40^{\circ }\sin45^{\circ }-k\cos 40^{\circ }\sin45^{\circ })\)

\(=-16.07i-13.54j-13.54k\) \(N\cdot m\)

Therefore, the resultant couple on adding together the i-, j-, and k-terms of the four Mâ€™s is

\(M=49.4i-90.5j-24.6k\) \(N\cdot m\)

and \( M=\sqrt{(49.4)^{2}+(90.5)^{2}+(24.6)^{2} }=106.0\) \(N\cdot m\)

Helpful Hints
1) Suggestion: Check the cross-product results by evaluating the moments about A of the components of the 500-N force directly from the sketch.
2) For the 600-N and 700-N forces it is easier to obtain the components of their moments about the coordinate directions through A by inspection of the figure than it is to set up the cross-product relations.
3) The \(25-N\cdot m\)couple vector of the wrench points in the direction opposite to that of the 500-N force, and we must resolve it into its x-, y-, and z-components to be added to the other couple-vector components.
4) Although the resultant couple vector M in the sketch of the resultants is shown through A, we recognize that a couple vector is a free vector and therefore has no specified line of action.
