Guest Question.

Given the following empirical equation,

\(\frac{\bar{H} _{D}}{\bar{H}} = 1.390 - 4.027 \bar{K}_{T} +5.531\bar{K}^{2}_{T} - 3.108 \bar{K}^{3}_{T} \)

where \( \bar{H} _{D}\) is the monthly average daily diffuse radiation on horizontal surface— see Eq. (2.105a)—estimate the average total radiation and the average diffuse radiation between 11:00 am and 12:00 pm solar time in the month of July on a horizontal surface located at 35°N latitude. The monthly average daily total radiation on a horizontal surface, \( \bar{H}\), in July at the surface location is 23.14 MJ/\(m^2\)-d.

Admin

Therefore,

\(\bar{K} _{T} = \frac{\bar{H}}{\bar{H} _{o}} =\frac{23.14}{40.6} = 0.570 \)

Therefore,

\(\frac{\bar{H}_{D}}{\bar{H}} =1.390 – 4.027(0.57)+5.531(0.57)^2 – 3.108(0.57)^3 = 0.316\)

and

\(\bar{H}_{D}= 0.316\bar{H} = 0.316(23.14)= 7.31\)MJ/\(m^2\)

From Table 2.5, the recommended average day for the month is July 17 (N = 199). The solar declination is calculated from Eq.(2.5) as

\(\delta = 23.45 \sin\left[\frac{360}{365}(284+N) \right] = 23.45 \sin\left[\frac{360}{365}(284+199) \right]=21.0^{\circ } \)

The sunset hour angle is calculated from Eq. (2.15) as

\(\cos(h_{ss})=- \tan(L)\tan(\delta ) \rightarrow h_{ss} = \cos^{-1}\left[- \tan(35)\tan(21)\right] =106^\circ \)

The middle point of the hour from 11:00 am to 12:00 pm is 0.5 h from solar noon, or hour angle is –7.5°. Therefore, from Eqs. (2.84b), (2.84c), and (2.84a), we have

\(\alpha = 0.409 + 0.5016 \sin(h_{ss}- 60) = 0.409 +0.5016 \sin(106 – 60) = 0.77 \)

\(\beta = 0.6609 + 0.4767 \sin(h_{ss}- 60) = 0.6609 – 0.4767 \sin(106 – 60) = 0.318\)

\(r= \frac{\pi }{24} (\alpha + \beta \cos(h))\frac{\cos(h)-\cos(h_{ss})}{\sin(h_{ss})- \left\lgroup\frac{2\pi h_{ss} }{360} \right\rgroup \cos(h_{ss})}\)

\(= \frac{\pi }{24} (0.77 + 0.318 \cos(-7.5))\frac{\cos(-7.5)-\cos(106)}{\sin(106)- \left\lgroup\frac{2\pi (106) }{360} \right\rgroup \cos(106)}\)

\(= 0.123 \)

From Eq. (2.83), we have

\(r_{d} = \left\lgroup\frac{\pi }{24}\right\rgroup \frac{\cos(h)-\cos(h_{ss})}{\sin(h_{ss})- \left\lgroup\frac{2\pi h_{ss} }{360} \right\rgroup \cos(h_{ss})} \)

\(= \left\lgroup\frac{\pi }{24}\right\rgroup \frac{\cos(-7.5)-\cos(106)}{\sin(106)- \left\lgroup\frac{2\pi (106) }{360} \right\rgroup \cos(106)}\)

Finally,

Average hourly total radiation = 0.123 (23.14) = 2.85 MJ/\(m^2\) or 2850 kJ/\(m^2\)

Average hourly diffuse radiation = 0.113 (7.31) = 0.826 MJ/\(m^2\) or 2850 kJ/\(m^2\)
