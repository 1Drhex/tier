Guest Question.

The synchronous motor shown in the one-line diagram of Fig. 5-36(a) draws rated current at a power factor of 0.95 leading. Its direct- and quadrature-axis reactances are 0.8 per unit and 0.5 per unit, respectively. Neglecting losses,

1.a.The approximate torque angle in electrical and mechanical degrees.

b.The excitation voltage.

c.The maximum torque that the machine can develop when the excitation voltage, as determined in (b), is constant.

2.Repeat part 1 by using the cylindrical rotor theory.

Admin
Solution

1.a. The torque angle is found by using Eq. (5.76)\( V_{q} \angle −\delta ° = V_{t} \angle 0° − I_{a}R_{a} − jI_{a}X_{q} \).

\( V_{q} \angle −\delta = V_{t} − jI_{a}X_{q} \)

 

\( V_{q} \angle −\delta = 1.0 \angle 0° − j1.0 \angle 18.2°(0.5) = 1.25 \angle −22.3° pu \)

Thus, the torque angle in electrical degrees is

\( \delta = \underline{\underline{−22.3°} } \)

In mechanical degrees, it is

\( \delta _{m} =\frac{2}{10}(22.3) =\underline{\underline{ 4.5°} } \)

b.The phasor diagram, as shown in Fig. 5-36(b), is drawn by following the procedure outlined in Section 5.3.2. Then, from basic trigonometry,

\( I_{q} = I_{a} \cos (\theta + \delta ) \angle −\delta \)

=\( \cos (18.2° + 22.3°) \angle −22.3° = 0.76 \angle −22.3° pu \)

And

\( I_{d} = I_{a} \sin (18.2° + 22.3°) \angle90°−22.3° \)

=\( 0.65 \angle 67.7° pu \)

Using Eq. (5.73)\( V_{f} \angle ±\delta ° = V_{t} \angle 0° ± I_{a}R_{a} ± jI_{q}X_{q} ± jI_{d}X_{d} \) , we obtain

\( V_{f}\angle -\delta = 1.0 \angle 0° – j0.76 \angle -22.3° (0.5) – j0.65 \angle 67.7°(0.8) \)

=\( \underline{\underline{ 1.45 \angle -22.3° pu} } \)

c.The torque angle for maximum power is found from Eq. (5.97)\( \delta = arccos\left(-\frac{K_{1}}{8K_{2}}\pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{K_{1}}{8K_{2}} \right)^{2} +\frac{1}{2}} \right) \):

\( K_{1}=\frac{1.45(1)}{0.8}= \)1.81

\( K_{2}=\frac{(1.0)^{2}(0.8-0.5)}{2(0.8)(0.5)}= \)0.375

\( \delta = arccos\left(-\frac{1.81}{8(0.375)}\pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{1.81}{8(0.375)} \right)^{2} +\frac{1}{2}} \right) \)

from which

\( \delta = 70.9° \)

The maximum torque is

\( T=\frac{maximum power in perunit}{speed perunit} \)

T=\( \frac{1.45(1)}{0.8}\sin 70.9°+\frac{(1.0)^{2} (0.8 – 0.5)}{2(0.8)(0.5)} \sin 141.9° \)

=\( \underline{\underline{1.94 pu} } \)

2.For a cylindrical rotor,

\( X_{d} = X_{q} = X_{s} = 0.8 pu \)

a.Using the per-phase equivalent circuit of Fig. 5-36(c), from KVL, we have

\( V_{f} \angle -\delta = 1.0 \angle 0° − j1.0 \angle +18.2° (0.8) = 1.46 \angle −31.3° pu \)

Thus,

\( \delta = −31.3° \)

And

\( \delta_{m}=\frac{2}{10}(31.3)=\underline{\underline{6.3°} } \)

b.The excitation voltage is

\( V_{f}=\underline{\underline{1.46 pu} } \)

c.For maximum torque,

\( \delta = 90° \)

And

\( T=\left(\frac{1}{1.0} \right)\frac{1.46(1)}{0.8}=\underline{\underline{1.83 pu} } \)
