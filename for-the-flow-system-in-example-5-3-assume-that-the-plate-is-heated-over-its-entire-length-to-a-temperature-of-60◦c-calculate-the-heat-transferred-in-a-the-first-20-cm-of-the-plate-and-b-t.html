Guest Question.

For the flow system in assume that the plate is heated over its entire length to a
temperature of \(60^{◦} C\). Calculate the heat transferred in (a) the first 20 cm of the plate and (b) the first 40 cm of the plate.

Admin
Solution

The total heat transfer over a certain length of the plate is desired; so we wish to calculate average heat-transfer coefficients. For this purpose we use Equations (5-44)\(Nu_{x}=0.332Pr^{1/3}Re^{1/2}_{x} \) and (5-45) \(\bar{h} =\frac{\int_{0}^{L}{h_{x} dx} }{\int_{0}^{L}{dx} }=2h_{x=L} \),

\(\frac{\bar{h}_{X0-L} }{h_{X=L} } =2L\frac{1-(x_{0}/L )^{3/4} }{L−x_{0} } \)

 

\(q_{total } =\bar{h} _{x0-L}(L-x_{0} ) (T_{w}-T_{∞} ) \)

evaluating the properties at the film temperature:

\(T_{f}= \frac{27+60}{2} =43.5^{◦} C=316.5\; K\; [110.3^{◦} F] \)

 

From Appendix A the properties are:

\(ν=17.36×10^{-6} m^{2} /s \enspace [1.87×10^{-4} ft^{2} /s] \)

 

\(k =0.02749\; W/m· ^{◦} C \enspace [0.0159 \; Btu/h · ft ·^{◦} F] \)

 

\(Pr =0.7\)

 

\(c_{p} =1.006\; kJ/kg · ^{◦} C\enspace [0.24\; Btu/lb_{m} ·^{◦} F]\)

At x=20 cm:

\(Re_{x}=\frac{u_{∞} x}{v} =\frac{(2)(0.2)}{17.36×10^{-6} } =23,041\)

 

\(Nu_{x}=\frac{h_{x} x}{k} =0.332Re^{1/2}_{x} \> Pr^{1/3}=(0.332)(23,041)^{1/2}(0.7)^{1/3} =44.74 \)

 

\(h_{x}= Nu_{x}(\frac{k}{x} )=\frac{(44.74)(0.02749)}{0.2} =6.15\; W/m^{2} · ^{◦} C \> [1.083\; Btu/h · ft^{2} ·^{◦} F] \)

The average value of the heat-transfer coefficient is twice this value, or:

\(\bar{h}=(2)(6.15)=12.3\> W/m^{2} ·^{◦} C [2.17 \; Btu/h · ft^{2} · ^{◦} F] \)

The heat flow is:

\(q=\bar{h}A(T_{w}-T_{∞} ) \)

If we assume unit depth in the z direction,

\(q=(12.3)(0.2)(60−27)=81.18\; W \; [277\; Btu/h] \)

At x=40 cm

\(Re_{x}=\frac{u_{∞} x}{v} =\frac{(2)(0.4)}{17.36×10^{-6} } =46,082\)

 

\(Nu_{x} =(0.332)(46,082)^{1/2}(0.7)^{1/3} =63.28 \)

 

\(h_{x}=\frac{(63.28)(0.02749)}{0.4} =4.349\; W/m^{2} · ^{◦} C \)

 

\(\bar{h}=(2)(4.349)=8.698\> W/m^{2} ·^{◦} C [1.53 \; Btu/h · ft^{2} · ^{◦} F] \)

 

\(q=(8.698)(0.4)(60−27)=114.8\> W\; [392\> Btu/h] \).
