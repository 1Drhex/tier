Guest Question.

A synchronous generator is connected to an infinite bus of voltage 1 pu through an external reactance. The reactance values are as under.

Saturated reactance of generator

\( X_{d} = 1.48 pu\) \( X_{q} = 1.24 pu \)

External reactance \( X_{e} = 0.1 pu\)

The generator is feeding rated MVA to the bus at 0.9 pf lagging measured at generator terminals.

(a) Draw the complete phasor diagram showing bus voltage \( (V_{b})\), generator terminal voltage \( (V_{t})\), armature current \( (I_{a})\) and power angle \( \delta\) with respect to bus voltage.

(b) Calculate the generator terminal voltage, excitation emf and power angle.

Admin
Solution

The one-line diagram of the system is drawn in Fig. 8.83(a)

\( X_{d}(\text { total })=X_{d t}=X_{d}+X_{e}=1.48+0.1=1.58 pu\)

 

\( X_{q} \text { (total) }=X_{q t}=X_{q}+X_{e}=1.24+0.1=1.34 pu\)

(a) The phasor diagram is drawn in Fig. 8.83(b).

\( \phi=\) = pf angle between \( \bar{V}_{t}\) and \( \bar{I}_{a}\) , generator terminals

\( \psi=\) angle between \( \bar{E}_{f}\) and \( \bar{I}_{a}\)

 

\( \delta=\) power angle between \( \bar{E}_{f}\) and \( \bar{V}_{b}\). \( \bar{E}_{f}\) leads \( \bar{V}_{b} \) as generator is feeding bus

\( \phi^{\prime}= pf\) angle between \( \bar{V}_{b}\) and \( \bar{I}_{a}\) at bus

(b) MVA (fed to bus) = 1 pu

Bus voltage \( V_{b}=1 pu\)

Current fed to bus = generator current, \( I_{a}=1 pu\)

Generator pf = 0.9 lagging

\( \phi=\cos ^{-1} 0.9=25.8^{\circ}\left(I_{a} \text { lags } V_{t}\right)\)

 

\( I_{a} X_{e}=1 \times 0.1=0.1 pu\)

Part of the phasor diagram in is sketched in Fig. 8.84 (not to scale)

Form the geometry of the phase diagram

\( \left[\left(V_{t} \cos \phi\right)+\left(V_{t} \sin \phi-0.1\right)^{2}\right]^{1 / 2}=V_{b}=1\)

 

\( V_{b}=1, \phi=25.8^{\circ}\)

Solving we find,

\( V_{t}=1.04 V , \phi^{\prime}=20^{\circ}\)

Second solution

\( V_{t}=0.796<V_{b}=1 \text { is rejected }\)

 

\( V_{t}=1.04 pu\)

 

\( \phi^{\prime}=20^{\circ}\left(I_{a} \text { lags } V_{b}\right)\)

 

\( \tan \psi=\frac{V_{b} \sin \phi^{\prime}+I_{a} X_{q t}}{V_{t} \cos \phi^{\prime}}=\frac{\sin 20^{\circ}+1 \times 1.34}{\cos 20^{\circ}}\), Eq. (8.111(a))

Or \( \tan \psi=1.79 \quad \psi=60.8^{\circ}\)

 

\( \psi=\delta+\phi^{\prime}, \quad \delta=60.8^{\circ}-20^{\circ}=40.8^{\circ}\)

 

\( E_{f}=V_{t} \cos \delta+I_{d} X_{d} ; \text { Eq. (8.113(a)) }\)

 

\( I_{d}=I_{ a } \sin \psi=1 \times \sin 60.8^{\circ}=0.873\)

 

Substituting values

\( E_{f}=2.136 pu\)

Alternative method

Taking \( \bar{V}_{b}\) as reference

\( \bar{I}_{a}=1 \angle-20^{\circ}\)

 

\( j \bar{I}_{a} X_{q t}=j 1 \angle-20^{\circ} \times 1.34=1.34 \angle 70^{\circ}\)

 

\( \bar{E}_{f}^{\prime}=\bar{V}_{b}+j \bar{I}_{a} X_{q t}=1+1.34 \angle 70^{\circ}\)

 

\( =1.458+j 1.259=1.926 \angle 40.8^{\circ}\)

 

\(E_{f}^{\prime}=1.926 \quad \delta=40.8^{\circ}\)

 

\( \psi=40.8^{\circ}+20^{\circ}=60.8^{\circ}\)

 

\( I_{d}=1 \sin \psi=\sin 60.8^{\circ}=0.873\)

 

\( I_{d}\) lags \( I_{q}\), so \( I_{d}\) is positive

Thus \( E_{f}=E_{f}^{\prime}+I_{d}\left(X_{d}-X_{q}\right)=1.926+0.873(1.58-1.34)\)

\( E_{f}=2.135 pu\)
