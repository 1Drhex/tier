Guest Question.

A small utility gasoline engine of 250 cc runs at 1500 RPM with a compression ratio of 7:1. The inlet state is 75 kPa, 17°C and the combustion adds 1500 kJ/kg to the charge. This engine runs a heat pump using R-410A with high pressure of 4 MPa and an evaporator operating at 0°C. Find the rate of heating the heat pump can deliver.

Admin
Solution

Overall cycle efficiency is from Eq.10.12, \(r _{ v }= v _{1} / v _{2}=7\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\eta_{ TH }=1- r _{1- k }=1-7^{-0.4}=0.5408 \\& w _{ net }=\eta_{ TH } \times q _{ H }=0.5408 \times 1500=811.27 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

We also need specific volume to evaluate Eqs.10.9 to 10.11

\(\begin{aligned}& v _{1}= RT _{1} / P _{1}=0.287 kJ / kg – K \times 290 K / 75 kPa =1.1097 m ^{3} / kg \\& v _{2}= v _{1} / CR =0.15853 m ^{3} / kg \\& P _{ meff }=\frac{ w _{ net }}{ v _{1}- v _{2}}=\frac{811.27}{1.097-0.15853} \frac{ kJ / kg }{ m ^{3} / kg }=852.9 kPa\end{aligned}\)

Now we can find the power from Eq.10.11 (assume 4 stroke engine)

\(\dot{ W }= P _{ meff } V _{ displ } \frac{ RPM }{60} \frac{1}{2}=852.9 \times 2.5 \times 10^{-4} \times \frac{1500}{60} \times \frac{1}{2}=2.665 kW\)

For the refrigeration cycle we have:

State 1: \(h _{1}=279.12 kJ / kg , s _{1}=1.0368 kJ / kgK\)

State 2: \(4 MPa , s _{2}= s _{1} \quad \text { interpolate } \Rightarrow h _{2}=323.81 kJ / kg\)

State 3: \(\text { 4 MPa interpolate } h _{3}=171.62 kJ / kg\)

\(\beta_{ HP }=\frac{ q _{ H }}{ w _{ C }}=\frac{ h _{2}- h _{3}}{ h _{2}- h _{1}}=\frac{323.81-171.62}{323.81-279.12}=3.405\)

The work out of the heat engine equals the input to the heat pump

\(\dot{ Q }_{ H }=\beta_{ HP } \dot{ W }=3.405 \times 2.665 kW = 9 . 0 7 k W\)
