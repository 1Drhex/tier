Guest Question.

In an I.C. engine operating on the dual cycle (limited pressure cycle), the temperature of the working fluid (air) at the beginning of compression is 27ºC. The ratio of the maximum and minimum pressures of the cycle is 70 and the compression ratio is 15. The amount of heat added at constant volume and at constant pressure are equal. Compute the air standard thermal efficiency of the cycle. State three main reasons why the actual thermal efficiency is different from the theoretical value. [U.P.S.C. 1993]

Admin
Solution

\(\begin{aligned}T_{1}&= 27ºC = 300 K\\\frac{P_{4}}{P_{1}}&= \frac{P_{3}}{P_{1}} = 70\\\text{Compression ratio, }\frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}}&= 15 = \frac{V_{1}}{V_{3}}\\\text{Heat added at constant }&\text{volume = Heat added at constant pressure}\\Q_{23}&= Q_{34}\\m · c_{v}(T_{3} – T_{2})&= m · c_{p} · (T_{4} – T_{3})\\(T_{3} – T_{2})&= γ · (T_{4} – T_{3})\\\text{For process 1–2;}\qquad\quad &\\\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}&= \left\lgroup\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} \right\rgroup^{\frac{γ – 1}{γ} }\\\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}&= \left\lgroup\frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}} \right\rgroup^{γ – 1}\\\frac{T_{2}}{300}&= (15)^{0.4}\\T_{2}&= 886.25\\\text{and} \qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad&\\\left\lgroup\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} \right\rgroup&= \left\lgroup\frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}} \right\rgroup^{γ} = (15)^{1.4}\\P_{2}&= P_{1} . (15)^{1.4}\\P_{2}&= 44.3 P_{1}\\\text{For process 2–3}\qquad\quad &\\\frac{P_{2}}{P_{3}}&= \frac{T_{2}}{T_{3}}\\\frac{44.3 P_{1}}{70 P_{1}}&= \frac{886.25}{T_{3}}\\\text{or,}\qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad T_{3}&= 1400.39\\\text{Using equal heat additi}&\text{ons for processes 2–3 and 3–4,}\\(T_{3} – T_{2})&= γ (T_{4} – T_{3})\\(1400.39 – 886.25)&= 1.4(T_{4} – 1400.39)\\T_{4}&= 1767.63\\\text{For process 3–4,}\qquad\quad &\\\frac{V_{3}}{V_{4}}&= \frac{T_{3}}{T_{4}}\\\frac{V_{3}}{V_{1}} × \frac{V_{1}}{V_{4}}&= \frac{T_{3}}{T_{4}}\\\frac{1}{15} × \left\lgroup\frac{V_{1}}{V_{4}} \right\rgroup&= \left\lgroup\frac{1400.39}{1767.63} \right\rgroup\\\frac{V_{1}}{V_{4}}&= 11.88\\\frac{V_{5}}{V_{4}}&= 11.88\\\text{For process 4–5,}\qquad\quad &\\\frac{P_{4}}{P_{5}}&= \left\lgroup\frac{V_{5}}{V_{4}} \right\rgroup^{\gamma} or, \frac{T_{4}}{T_{5}} = \left\lgroup\frac{V_{5}}{V_{4}} \right\rgroup^{\gamma – 1} \\\frac{P_{4}}{P_{5}}&= (11.88)^{1.4} or, \frac{T_{4}}{T_{5}} = (11.88)^{0.4}\\T_{5}&= \frac{1767.63}{(11.88)^{0.4}} = 656.85\\\text{Air standard thermal}&\text{ efficiency = 1 – }\frac{\text{Heat rejected}}{\text{Heat added}}\\&=1 – \frac{m c_{v} (T_{5} – T_{1})}{m c_{P} (T_{4} – T_{3}) + m c_{v} (T_{3} – T_{2})}\\&= 1 – \frac{(T_{5} – T_{1})}{γ . (T_{4} – T_{3}) + (T_{3} – T_{2})}\\&= 1 – \frac{(656.85 – 300)}{1.4 × (1767.63 – 1400.39) + (1400.39 – 886.25)}\end{aligned}\\\text{Air standard thermal efficiency}= 0.6529\\\text{Air standard thermal efficiency}= \textbf{65.29}\pmb{\%}\\\)Actual thermal efficiency may be different from theoretical efficiency due to following reasons;
(a) Air standard cycle analysis considers air as the working fluid while in actual cycle it is not air throughout the cycle. Actual working fluid which are combustion products do not behave as perfect gas.
(b) Heat addition does not occur isochorically in actual process. Also combustion is accompanied by inefficiency such as incomplete combustion, dissociation of combustion products, etc.
(c) Specific heat variation occurs in actual processes where as in air standard cycle analysis specific heat variation is neglected. Also during adiabatic process theoretically no heat loss occurs while actually these processes are accompanied by heat losses.
