Guest Question.

Figure P.26.5 shows the doubly symmetrical idealized cross-section of a uniform box beam of length l. Each of the four corner booms has area B and Young’s modulus E, and they constitute the entire direct stress carrying area. All the thin walls have the same shear modulus G. The beam transmits a torque T from one end to the other, and at each end, warping is completely suppressed. Between the ends, the shape of the cross-section is maintained without further restriction of warping. Obtain an expression for the distribution of the end load along the length of one of the corner booms. Assuming \( bt_{1} > at_{2}\), indicate graphically the relation between torque direction and tension and compression in the boom end loads.

Admin
Solution

\(p=\frac{\mu BET}{8abGt_{1}t_{2}}(bt_{1}-at_{2})[-\sinh\mu z+\frac{(\cosh \mu l-1 )}{\sinh\mu l}\cosh \mu z]\)
where\(\mu^{2} =8Gt_{1}t_{2}=BE(at_{2} + bt_{1}):\)
Using the results of Problem P.26.5 and assuming \(bt_{1} > bt_{}\), use MATLAB to graphically show the relationship between the torque direction and tension and compression in the boom end loads using the variable values that follow. Also, calculate the value and location of the maximum and minimum end load for \(\pm T\).
\(G = 36,400 N=mm^{2} , t_{1} = 2 mm , t_{2} = 1 mm , B = 1,290 mm^{2} , E = 20,000 N/mm^{2}\)
\(a = 20 mm , b = 30 mm , l = 1 m , T =\pm 1 N mm\)
\(T < 0: P_{max} = 0:0496 N(z = 1 m); P_{min} = -0.0496 N(z = 0 m)\)
\(T > 0: P_{max} = 0:0496 N(z = 0 m); P_{min} = -0.0496 N(z = 1 m)\)
