Guest Question.

A metal–semiconductor junction is made of silver and silicon with \(N _{D} = 4 × 10^{17} cm^{–3}\). Calculate the barrier height and the built-in potential.

Admin
Solution

The work function \((Φ_{M})\) and electron affinity \((\chi )\) for silver and silicon are 4.26 V and 4.01 V, respectively (from Table 5.1). The barrier height for N type material, from Eq. (5.1), is given by

Table 5.1

\(\Phi _{BN}=\Phi _{M} -\chi =4.26-4.01=0.25V\)

The built-in potential is given by Eq. (5.7) i.e.,

\(\Phi _{IN}=\Phi _{BN} -\frac{E_{C}-E_{F} }{q}= \Phi _{BN} -\frac{KT}{q}\ln \frac{N_{C} }{N_{D} }\) (5.7)

\(\Phi _{IN}=\Phi _{BN} -\frac{KT}{q}\ln \frac{N_{C} }{N_{D} } =0.25-\frac{1.38\times 10^{-23}\times 300 }{1.602\times 10^{-19} } \ln \frac{2.8\times 10^{25} }{4\times 10^{17} }=0.47V\)

(The density of state at conduction band edge, \(N_{C} = 2.8 × 10^{25}\) from Table 4.1)
