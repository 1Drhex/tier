Guest Question.

The depicted beam is partially loaded by \(q_{0} \). Determine the shear force and bending moment diagrams.

Admin
Solution

Due to the discontinuity of the loading we separate the inte-gration into two parts:

\(0\leq x\leq a: a\leq x\leq l:\)
\(q=0, q=q_{0},\)
\(V=C_{1}, V=-q_{0}x+C_{3},\)
\(M=C_{1}x+C_{2}, M=-\frac{1} {2}q_{0}x^{2}+C_{3}x+C_{4}.\)

The 4 integration constants follow from the boundary conditions and the transition condition at x = a:

\(M(0)=0 \rightsquigarrow C_{2}=0, M(l)=0 \rightsquigarrow -\frac{1}{2}q_{0}l^{2}+C_{3}l +C_{4}=0.\)

Here V and M have to be continuous (no jumps, since no concentrated force and no single moment occur)

\(V(a^{-})=V(a^{+}) \rightsquigarrow C_{1}=-q_{0}a+C_{3},\)

 

\(M(a^{-})=M(a^{+}) \rightsquigarrow C_{1}a=-\frac{1}{2}q_{0}a^{2}+C_{3}a+C_{4}.\)

It follows

\(C_{1}=\frac{q_{0}l}{2}\frac{(l-a)^{2}} {l^{2}}, C_{2}=0, C_{3}=\frac{q_{0}l} {2}\frac{l^{2}+a^{2}}{l^{2}}, C_{4}=- \frac{q_{0}a^{2}}{2}.\)

We obtain for the first part \(0\leq x\leq a\)

\(\underline{\underline{V=\frac{q_{0}l}{2}\frac{(l-a)^{2}}{l^{2}} } }, \underline{\underline{M=\frac{q_{0}l^{2}}{2}\frac{(l-a)^{2}}{l^{3}}x } }\)

and for the second part \(0\leq x\leq l\)

\(\underline{\underline{V=\frac{q_{0}}{2}\left[\frac{(l-a)^{2}}{l}-2(x-a) \right] } }, \underline{\underline{M=\frac{q_{0}}{2}\left[\frac{(l-a)^{2}}{l}x-(x-a)^{2} \right] } }.\)

For a = l/2, the shear-force and bending moment diagrams follow as

Remark:
• Instead of considering the parameter x for the complete length of
the beam, we may also introduce separated parameters \(x_{1}, x_{2}\)
• For the special case of a = 0, the first part vanishes, such that we
obtain for the shear force and the bending moment

\(V=\frac{1}{2}q_{0}(l-2x), M=\frac{1}{2}q_{0}(lx-x^{2}).\)

Alternative: The diagrams can be determined by use of the \(\ddot{F}OPPL\) sym-bol. Therefore we represent the discontinuous loading for the complete beam as

\(q=q_{0}\lt x-a\gt ^{0} f\ddot{u}r 0\leq x\leq l.\)

With application of the integration rules for the \(\ddot{F}OPPL\) symbol, we obtain

\(V=-q_{0}\lt x-a\gt ^{1}+C_{1},\)

 

\(M=-\frac{q_{0}}{2}\lt x-a\gt ^{2}+C_{1}x+C_{2}.\)

Due to the boundary conditions, this leads to (the transition conditions are directly fulfilled)

\(M(0)=0\rightsquigarrow C_{2}=0\)

 

\(M(l)=0\rightsquigarrow 0=-\frac{q_{0}}{2}(l-a)^{2}+C_{1}l \rightsquigarrow C_{1}=\frac{q_{0}}{2}\frac{(l-a)^{2}}{l}.\)

We obtain the solution for the entire beam as

\(\underline{\underline{V=\frac{q_{0}}{2}\left[\frac{(l-a)^{2}}{l}-2\lt x-a\gt ^{1} \right] } },\)

 

\(\underline{\underline{M=\frac{q_{0}}{2}\left[\frac{(l-a)^{2}x}{l}-\lt x-a\gt ^{2} \right] } }.\)
