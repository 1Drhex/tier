Guest Question.

The transfer matrix The S-matrix () tells you the outgoing amplitudes (B and F ) in terms of the incoming amplitudes (A and G) —Equation 2.180. For some purposes it is more convenient to work with the transfer matrix, , which gives you the amplitudes to the right of the potential (F and G) in terms of those to the left (A and B):

\( \left(\begin{array}{l} B \\ F \end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{ll} S_{11} & S_{12} \\ S_{21} & S_{22} \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} A \\ G \end{array}\right) \) (2.180).

\( \left(\begin{array}{l} F \\ G \end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{ll} M_{11} & M_{12} \\ M_{21} & M_{22} \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{l} A \\ B \end{array}\right) \) (2.183).

(a) Find the four elements of the M-matrix, in terms of the elements of the S-matrix, and vice versa. Express \( R_{l}, T_{l}, R_{r} \text { and } T_{r} \) (Equations 2.181 and 2.182) in terms of elements of the M-matrix.

\( R_{l}=\left.\frac{|B|^{2}}{|A|^{2}}\right|_{G=0}=\left|S_{11}\right|^{2}, \quad T_{l}=\left.\frac{|F|^{2}}{|A|^{2}}\right|_{G=0}=\left|S_{21}\right|^{2} \) (2.181).

\( R_{r}=\left.\frac{|F|^{2}}{|G|^{2}}\right|_{A=0}=\left|S_{22}\right|^{2}, \quad T_{r}=\left.\frac{|B|^{2}}{|G|^{2}}\right|_{A=0}=\left|S_{12}\right|^{2} \) (2.182).

(b) Suppose you have a potential consisting of two isolated pieces (Figure 2.22). Show that the M-matrix for the combination is the product of the two M-matrices for each section separately:

\( M = M _{2} M _{1} \) (2.184).

(This obviously generalizes to any number of pieces, and accounts for the usefulness of the M-matrix.)

(c) Construct the M-matrix for scattering from a single delta-function potential at point a:

\( V(x)=-\alpha \delta(x-a) \).

(d) By the method of part (b), find the M-matrix for scattering from the double delta-function

\( V(x)=-\alpha[\delta(x+a)+\delta(x-a)] \).

What is the transmission coefficient for this potential?

Solution

(a)

\( B=S_{11} A+S_{12} G \Rightarrow G=\frac{1}{S_{12}}\left(B-S_{11} A\right)=M_{21} A+M_{22} B \Rightarrow M_{21}=-\frac{S_{11}}{S_{12}}, M_{22}=\frac{1}{S_{12}} \).

\( F=S_{21} A+S_{22} G=S_{21} A+\frac{S_{22}}{S_{12}}\left(B-S_{11} A\right)=-\frac{\left(S_{11} S_{22}-S_{12} S_{21}\right)}{S_{12}} A+\frac{S_{22}}{S_{12}} B=M_{11} A+M_{12} B \).

\( \Rightarrow M_{11}=-\frac{\operatorname{det} S}{S_{12}}, M_{12}=\frac{S_{22}}{S_{12}} . \quad M=\frac{1}{S_{12}}\left(\begin{array}{cc} -\operatorname{det}( S ) & S_{22} \\ -S_{11} & 1 \end{array}\right) \). Conversely:

\( G=M_{21} A+M_{22} B \Rightarrow B=\frac{1}{M_{22}}\left(G-M_{21} A\right)=S_{11} A+S_{12} G \Rightarrow S_{11}=-\frac{M_{21}}{M_{22}} ; S_{12}=\frac{1}{M_{22}} \).

\( F=M_{11} A+M_{12} B=M_{11} A+\frac{M_{12}}{M_{22}}\left(G-M_{21} A\right)=\frac{\left(M_{11} M_{22}-M_{12} M_{21}\right)}{M_{22}} A+\frac{M_{12}}{M_{22}} G=S_{21} A+S_{22} G \).

\( \Rightarrow S_{21}=\frac{\operatorname{det} M}{M_{22}} ; S_{22}=\frac{M_{12}}{M_{22}} . \quad S =\frac{1}{M_{22}}\left(\begin{array}{cc} -M_{21} & 1 \\ \operatorname{det}( M ) & M_{12} \end{array}\right) \).

[It happens that the time-reversal invariance of the Schrödinger equation, plus conservation of probability, requires \( M_{22}=M_{11}^{*}, M_{21}=M_{12}^{*}, \text { and } \operatorname{det}( M )=1 \), but I won’t use this here. See Merzbacher’s Quantum Mechanics. Similarly, for even potentials \( S_{11}=S_{22}, S_{12}=S_{21} \) ().]

\( R_{l}=\left|S_{11}\right|^{2}=\left|\frac{M_{21}}{M_{22}}\right|^{2} \) , \( T_{l}=\left|S_{21}\right|^{2}=\left|\frac{\operatorname{det}(M)}{M_{22}}\right|^{2} \) , \( R_{r}=\left|S_{22}\right|^{2}= \left|\frac{M_{12}}{M_{22}}\right|^{2} \) , \( T_{r}=\left|S_{12}\right|^{2}= \frac{1}{\left|M_{22}\right|^{2}} \).

(b)

\( \left(\begin{array}{l} F \\ G \end{array}\right)= M _{2}\left(\begin{array}{l} C \\ D \end{array}\right),\left(\begin{array}{l} C \\ D \end{array}\right)= M _{1}\left(\begin{array}{l} A \\ B \end{array}\right) \) , so \( \left(\begin{array}{l} F \\ G \end{array}\right)= M _{2} M _{1}\left(\begin{array}{l} A \\ B \end{array}\right)= M \left(\begin{array}{l} A \\ B \end{array}\right) \), with \( M = M_2M_1 \). QED.

(c)

\( \psi(x)=\left\{\begin{array}{l} A e^{i k x}+B e^{-i k x}(x<a) \\ F e^{i k x}+G e^{-i k x}(x>a) \end{array}\right\} \).

\( \left\{\begin{array}{l} \text { Continuity of } \psi: \quad A e^{i k a}+B e^{-i k a}=F e^{i k a}+G e^{-i k a} \\ \text { Discontinuity of } \psi^{\prime}: i k\left(F e^{i k a}-G e^{-i k a}\right)-i k\left(A e^{i k a}-B e^{-i k a}\right)=-\frac{2 m \alpha}{\hbar^{2}} \psi(a)=-\frac{2 m \alpha}{\hbar^{2}}\left(A e^{i k a}+B e^{-i k a}\right)<br /> \end{array}\right. \).

(1) \( F e^{2 i k a}+G=A e^{2 i k a}+B \).

(2) \( F e^{2 i k a}-G=A e^{2 i k a}-B+i \frac{2 m \alpha}{\hbar^{2 k}}\left(A e^{2 i k a}+B\right) \).

Add (1) and (2):

\( 2 F e^{2 i k a}=2 A e^{2 i k a}+i \frac{2 m \alpha}{\hbar^{2} k}\left(A e^{2 i k a}+B\right) \Rightarrow F=\left(1+i \frac{m \alpha}{\hbar^{2} k}\right) A+i \frac{m \alpha}{\hbar^{2} k} e^{-2 i k a} B=M_{11} A+M_{12} B \).

So \( M_{11}=(1+i \beta) ; M_{12}=i \beta e^{-2 i k a} ; \beta \equiv \frac{m \alpha}{\hbar^{2} k} \).

Subtract (2) from (1):

\( 2 G=2 B-2 i \beta e^{2 i k a} A-2 i \beta B \Rightarrow G=(1-i \beta) B-i \beta e^{2 i k a} A=M_{21} A+M_{22} B \).

So \( M_{21}=-i \beta e^{2 i k a} ; M_{22}=(1-i \beta) \). \( M=\left(\begin{array}{cc} (1+i \beta) & i \beta e^{-2 i k a} \\ -i \beta e^{2 i k a} & (1-i \beta) \end{array}\right) \).

(d)

\( M_{2}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} (1+i \beta) & i \beta e^{-2 i k a} \\ -i \beta e^{2 i k a} & (1-i \beta) \end{array}\right) \); to get \( M_1 \), just switch the sign of a: \( M_{1}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} (1+i \beta) & i \beta e^{2 i k a} \\ -i \beta e^{-2 i k a} & (1-i \beta) \end{array}\right) \).

\( M = M _{2} M _{1}= \left(\begin{array}{l} {\left[1+2 i \beta+\beta^{2}\left(e^{-4 i k a}-1\right)\right] 2 i \beta[\cos 2 k a-\beta \sin 2 k a]} \\ -2 i \beta[\cos 2 k a-\beta \sin 2 k a]\left[1-2 i \beta+\beta^{2}\left(e^{4 i k a}-1\right)\right] \end{array}\right) \).

\( T=T_{l}=T_{r}=\frac{1}{\left|M_{22}\right|^{2}} \Rightarrow \).

\( T^{-1}=\left[1+2 i \beta+\beta^{2}\left(e^{-4 i k a}-1\right)\right]\left[1-2 i \beta+\beta^{2}\left(e^{4 i k a}-1\right)\right] \).

\( =1-2 i \beta+\beta^{2} e^{4 i k a}-\beta^{2}+2 i \beta+4 \beta^{2}+2 i \beta^{3} e^{4 i k a}-2 i \beta^{3}+\beta^{2} e^{-4 i k a} \).

\( -\beta^{2}-2 i \beta^{3} e^{-4 i k a}+2 i \beta^{3}+\beta^{4}\left(1-e^{-4 i k a}-e^{4 i k a}+1\right) \).

\( =1+2 \beta^{2}+\beta^{2}\left(e^{-4 i k a}+e^{4 i k a}\right)-2 i \beta^{3}\left(e^{-4 i k a}-e^{4 i k a}\right)+2 \beta^{4}-\beta^{4}\left(e^{-4 i k a}+e^{4 i k a}\right) \).

\( =1+2 \beta^{2}+2 \beta^{2} \cos 4 k a+2 i \beta^{3} 2 i \sin 4 k a+2 \beta^{4}-2 \beta^{4} \cos 4 k a \).

\( =1+2 \beta^{2}(1+\cos 4 k a)-4 \beta^{3} \sin 4 k a+2 \beta^{4}(1-\cos 4 k a) \).

\( =1+4 \beta^{2} \cos ^{2} 2 k a-8 \beta^{3} \sin 2 k a \cos 2 k a-4 \beta^{4} \sin ^{2} 2 k a \).

\( T=\frac{1}{1+4 \beta^{2}(\cos 2 k a-\beta \sin 2 k a)^{2}} \).
