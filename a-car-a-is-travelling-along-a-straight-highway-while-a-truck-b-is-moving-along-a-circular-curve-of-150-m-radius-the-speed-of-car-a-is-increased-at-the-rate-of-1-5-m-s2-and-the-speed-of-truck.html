Guest Question.

A car A is travelling along a straight highway, while a truck B is moving along a circular curve of 150 m radius. The speed of car A is increased at the rate of 1.5 m/s² and the speed of truck B is being decreased at the rate of 0.9 m/s². For the position shown in Fig. 12.26(a), determine the velocity of A relative to B and the acceleration of A relative to B. At this instant the speed of A is 75 km/hr and that of B is 40 km/hr.

Admin
Solution

(i) Motion of car A

\( ν_A = 75 \) km/hr = 20.83 m/s.

\( ν_A = 20.83 \) i.

\( a_A = 1.5 \) i.

Motion of truck B

\( ν_B = 40 \) km/hr = 11.11 m/s (\( \measuredangle 30° \)).

\( ν_B = 11.11 \cos 30° i – 11.11 \sin 30° j \).

\( ν_B = 9.622 i – 5.55 j \).

Tangential component of acceleration,

\( a_t = 0.9 \) m/s² (\(30° \measuredangle \)).

Normal component of acceleration,

\( a_n = \frac{ν^2}{ρ} = \frac{11.11^2}{150} \) .

\( a_n = 0.823\) m/s² (\(60° \measuredangle \)).

\( a_B = (-0.9 \cos 30° – 0.823 \cos 60° ) i + (0.9 \sin 30° – 0.823 \sin 60° ) j \) .

\( a_B = -1.190 i – 0.267 j \) .

(ii) Relative velocity of A w.r.t. B

\( ν_{A/B} = ν_A – ν_B \) .

\( ν_{A/B} = 20.83 i – (9.622 i – 5.55 j) = 11.21 i + 5.55 j \) .

Magnitude

\( ν_{A/B} = \sqrt{(11.21)^2 + (5.55)^2} \) .

\( ν_{A/B} = 12.51 \) m/s.

Direction

\( \tan θ = \frac{5.55}{11.21} \) ∴ θ = 26.36°.

∴ \( ν_{A/B} = 12.51 \) m/s (\( \measuredangle 26.36° \)) Ans.

(iii) Relative acceleration of A w.r.t. B

\( a_{A/B} = a_A – a_B \) .

\( a_{A/B} = 1.5 i – (-1.190 i – 0.267 j) = 2.69 i + 0.2627 j \) .

Magnitude

\( a_{A/B} = \sqrt{(2.69)^2 + (0.2627)^2} \) .

\( a_{A/B} = 2.7 \) m/s².

Direction

\( \tan \phi = \frac{0.2627}{2.69} \) ∴ \( \phi = 5.58° \).

∴ \( a_{A/B} = 2.7 \) m/s² (\( \measuredangle 5.58° \)) Ans.
