Guest Question.

A W18 × 55 structural steel [E = 29,000 ksi] wide-flange shape is loaded and supported as shown in Fig. P11.61. The beam is supported at C by a ¾-in.-diameter aluminum [E = 10,000 ksi] rod, which has no load before the distributed load is applied to the beam. After a distributed load of 4 kips/ft is applied to the beam, determine:
(a) the force carried by the aluminum rod.
(b) the maximum bending stress in the steel beam.
(c) the deflection of the beam at C.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

Beam: \(I_{\text {beam }}=890 \text { in. }^{4} \quad d=18.1 \text { in. } \quad E_{\text {beam }}=29,000 ksi\)

\(S_{\text {beam }}=98.3 \text { in. }^{3}\)

Rod (1): \(A_{1}=\frac{\pi}{4}(0.75 in. )^{2}=0.4417865 in .^{2} \quad E_{1}=10,000 ksi\)

 

(a) Force in the aluminum rod.

The reaction force from rod (1) will be taken as the redundant, leaving a cantilever beam as the released beam.

For this analysis, a tension force is assumed to exist in axial member (1).

 

Downward deflection of W18 × 55 beam at C due to 4 kips/ft uniformly distributed load.
[Appendix C, Cantilever beam with uniformly distributed load.]
Relevant equations from Appendix C:

\(v_{B}=-\frac{w L^{4}}{8 E I} \quad \text { and } \quad \theta_{B}=-\frac{w L^{3}}{6 E I}\)

Values:

\(w=4 kips / ft , L=11 ft , E I=25.81 \times 10^{6} \text { kip-in. }{ }^{2}\)

Calculation:
\(\begin{aligned}&v_{B}=-\frac{w L^{4}}{8 E I}=-\frac{(4 kips / ft )(11 ft )^{4}(12 in. / ft )^{3}}{8\left(25.81 \times 10^{6} kip – in .{ }^{2}\right)}=-0.490113 in . \\&\theta_{B}=-\frac{w L^{3}}{6 E I}=-\frac{(4 kips / ft )(11 ft )^{3}(12 in . / ft )^{2}}{6\left(25.81 \times 10^{6} \text { kip-in. }^{2}\right)}=-4.950639 \times 10^{-3} rad \\&v_{C}=-0.490113 in .+(5 ft )(12 in . / ft )\left(-4.950639 \times 10^{-3} rad \right)=-0.787152 in .\end{aligned}\)

 

Upward deflection of W18 × 55 beam at C due to force \(F_{1}\) in rod (1).
[Appendix C, Cantilever beam with concentrated load at tip.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{C}=-\frac{P L^{3}}{3 E I}\)

Values:

\(P=-F_{1}, L=16 ft , E I=25.81 \times 10^{6} \text { kip-in. }{ }^{2}\)

Calculation:
\(v_{C}=-\frac{P L^{3}}{3 E I}=-\frac{\left(-F_{1}\right)(16 ft )^{3}(12 in . / ft )^{3}}{3\left(25.81 \times 10^{6} kip – in. ^{2}\right)}=\left[\left(91.41015 \times 10^{-3} in . / kip \right) F_{1}\right.\)

Elongation of aluminum rod (1) due to force \(F_{1}\).

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}=\frac{(14 ft )(12 in . / ft )}{\left(0.4417865 in .{ }^{2}\right)(10,000 ksi )} F_{1}=\left(38.02742 \times 10^{-3} in . / kip \right) F_{1}\)

The deflection of the W18 × 55 beam at C will not equal zero in this instance because the rod that supports the beam at C elongates, thus permitting the W18 × 55 beam to deflect downward. Therefore,

\(v_{C}=-\delta_{1}=-\left(38.02742 \times 10^{-3} \text { in. } / kip \right) F_{1}\)

Compatibility equation at C:
The sum of the downward deflection caused by the uniformly distributed load and the upward deflection caused by the force in rod (1) must equal the elongation of the aluminum rod. Elongation of the aluminum rod will produce a downward (i.e., negative) deflection of the W18 × 55 beam at C.

\(\begin{gathered}-0.787152 in .+\left(91.41015 \times 10^{-3} in . / kip \right) F_{1}=-\left(38.02742 \times 10^{-3} in . / kip \right) F_{1} \\\therefore F_{1}=\frac{0.787152 in .}{129.4376 \times 10^{-3} \text { in. } / kip }=6.081323 kips =6.08 kips ( T )\end{gathered}\)

(b) Determine maximum bending stress in W18 × 55 beam:

Maximum bending moment magnitude
\(M_{\max }=144.70 kip – ft (\text { at } A)\)

Bending stress at maximum moment

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\frac{(144.70 kip – ft )(18.1 in . / 2)(12 in. / ft )}{890 in. ^{4}} \\&=17.66 ksi\end{aligned}\)

or using the tabulated value for the section modulus of the W18 × 55 beam:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\frac{(144.70 kip – ft )(12 in. / ft )}{98.3 in. ^{3}} \\&=17.66 ksi\end{aligned}\)

 

(c) Beam deflection at C.
The beam deflection at C is equal to the elongation of the aluminum rod:

\(v_{C}=-\delta_{1}=-\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}=-\frac{(6.081323 kips )(14 ft )(12 in. / ft )}{\left(0.4417865 in. ^{2}\right)(10,000 ksi )}=-0.231257 in. =0.231 in . \downarrow\)
