Guest Question.

(a) Work out all of the canonical commutation relations for components of the operators r and p: \( [x, y],\left[x, p_{y}\right],\left[x, p_{x}\right],\left[p_{y}, p_{z}\right] \), and so on. Answer:

\( \left[r_{i}, p_{j}\right]=-\left[p_{i}, r_{j}\right]=i \hbar \delta_{i j}, \quad\left[r_{i}, r_{j}\right]=\left[p_{i}, p_{j}\right]=0 \), (4.10).

where the indices stand for x, y, or z, and \( r_{x}=x, r_{y}=y \text {, and } r_{z}=z \).

(b) Confirm the three-dimensional version of Ehrenfest’s theorem,

\( \frac{d}{d t}\langle r \rangle=\frac{1}{m}\langle p \rangle, \quad \text { and } \quad \frac{d}{d t}\langle p \rangle=\langle-\nabla V\rangle \) (4.11).

(Each of these, of course, stands for three equations—one for each component.) Hint: First check that the “generalized” Ehrenfest theorem, Equation 3.73, is valid in three dimensions.

\( \frac{d}{d t}\langle Q\rangle=\frac{i}{\hbar}\langle[\hat{H}, \hat{Q}]\rangle+\left\langle\frac{\partial \hat{Q}}{\partial t}\right\rangle \) (3.73).

(c) Formulate Heisenberg’s uncertainty principle in three dimensions.
Answer

\( \sigma_{x} \sigma_{p_{x}} \geq \hbar / 2, \quad \sigma_{y} \sigma_{p_{y}} \geq \hbar / 2, \quad \sigma_{z} \sigma_{p_{z}} \geq \hbar / 2 \) (4.12).

but there is no restriction on, say, \( \sigma_{x} \sigma_{p_{y}} \).

Admin
Solution

(a)

[x; y] = xy – yx = 0; etc., so \( \left[r_{i}, r_{j}\right]=0 \).

\( \left[p_{x}, p_{y}\right] f=\frac{\hbar}{i} \frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left(\frac{\hbar}{i} \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\right)-\frac{\hbar}{i} \frac{\partial}{\partial y}\left(\frac{\hbar}{i} \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\right)=-\hbar^{2}\left(\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial x \partial y}-\frac{\partial^{2} f}{\partial y \partial x}\right)=0 \).

(by the equality of cross-derivatives), so \( \left[p_{i}, p_{j}\right]=0 \).

\( \left[x, p_{x}\right] f=\frac{\hbar}{i}\left(x \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}-\frac{\partial}{\partial x}(x f)\right)=\frac{\hbar}{i}\left(x \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}-x \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}-f\right)=i \hbar f \).

\( \text { so } \left.\left[x, p_{x}\right]=i \hbar \text { (likewise }\left[y, p_{y}\right]=i \hbar \text { and }\left[z, p_{z}\right]=i \hbar\right) \).

\( \left.\left[y, p_{x}\right] f=\frac{\hbar}{i}\left(y \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}-\frac{\partial}{\partial x}(y f)\right)=\frac{\hbar}{i}\left(y \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}-y \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\right)=0 \text { (since } \frac{\partial y}{\partial x}=0\right) \text {. So }\left[y, p_{x}\right]=0 \).

and same goes for the other \mixed” commutators. Thus \( \left[r_{i}, p_{j}\right]=-\left[p_{j}, r_{i}\right]=i \hbar \delta_{i j} \).

(b) The derivation of Eq. 3.73 is identical in three dimensions, so \( \frac{d\langle x\rangle}{d t}=\frac{i}{\hbar}\langle[H, x]\rangle \);

\( \frac{d}{d t}\langle Q\rangle=\frac{i}{\hbar}\langle[\hat{H}, \hat{Q}]\rangle+\left\langle\frac{\partial \hat{Q}}{\partial t}\right\rangle \) (3.73).

\( [H, x]=\left[\frac{p^{2}}{2 m}+V, x\right]=\frac{1}{2 m}\left[p_{x}^{2}+p_{y}^{2}+p_{z}^{2}, x\right]=\frac{1}{2 m}\left[p_{x}^{2}, x\right] \)

\( =\frac{1}{2 m}\left(p_{x}\left[p_{x}, x\right]+\left[p_{x}, x\right] p_{x}\right)=\frac{1}{2 m}\left[(-i \hbar) p_{x}+(-i \hbar) p_{x}\right]=-i \frac{\hbar}{m} p_{x} \).

\( \therefore \frac{d\langle x\rangle}{d t}=\frac{i}{\hbar}\left(-i \frac{\hbar}{m}\left\langle p_{x}\right\rangle\right)=\frac{1}{m}\left\langle p_{x}\right\rangle \). The same goes for y and z, so: \( \frac{d\langle r \rangle}{d t}=\frac{1}{m}\langle p \rangle \).

\( \frac{d\left\langle p_{x}\right\rangle}{d t}=\frac{i}{\hbar}\left\langle\left[H, p_{x}\right]\right\rangle ; \quad\left[H, p_{x}\right]=\left[\frac{p^{2}}{2 m}+V, p_{x}\right]=\left[V, p_{x}\right]=i \hbar \frac{\partial V}{\partial x} \) (Eq. 3.65).

\( [\hat{A} \hat{B}, \hat{C}]=\hat{A}[\hat{B}, \hat{C}]+[\hat{A}, \hat{C}] \hat{B} \) (3.65).

\( =\frac{i}{\hbar}(i \hbar)\left\langle\frac{\partial V}{\partial x}\right\rangle=\left\langle-\frac{\partial V}{\partial x}\right\rangle \). Same for y and z, so: \( \frac{d\langle p \rangle}{d t}=\langle-\nabla V\rangle \).

(c) From Eq. 3.62: \( \sigma_{A}^{2} \sigma_{B}^{2} \geq\left(\frac{1}{2 i}\langle[\hat{A}, \hat{B}]\rangle\right)^{2} \) (3.62).

\( \sigma_{x} \sigma_{p_{x}} \geq\left|\frac{1}{2 i}\left\langle\left[x, p_{x}\right]\right\rangle\right|=\left|\frac{1}{2 i} i \hbar\right|=\frac{\hbar}{2} \). Generally, \( \sigma_{r_{i}} \sigma_{p_{j}} \geq \frac{\hbar}{2} \delta_{i j} \).
