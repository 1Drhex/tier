\( s_{2}^{o}=s_{1}^{o}-R \ln \frac{P_{1}}{P_{2}}=2.968-0.286 \ln 5=2.506 kJ / kg \cdot K \).

Thus

\( T_{2}=657.5 K \quad h_{2}=667.8 kJ / kg \quad h_{1}=1046.1 kJ / kg \quad h_{0}=298.2 kJ / kg \).

By the first law,

\( h_{1}=h_{2}+\frac{V_{2}^{2}}{2} \quad \text { or } \quad V_{2}=\sqrt{2}\left(h_{1}-h_{2}\right)^{0.5}=\sqrt{2}\left[(1046.1-667.8)\left(10^{3}\right)\right]^{0.5}=1230 m / s \).

To evaluate the second-law effectiveness, we need the availability produced:

\( \psi_{2}=h_{2}-h_{0}+\frac{V_{2}^{2}}{2}-T_{0}\left(s_{2}^{\circ}-s_{1}^{\circ}-R \ln \frac{P_{2}}{P_{0}}\right) \).

\( =667.8-298.2+\frac{1230^{2}}{(2)(1000)}-(298)[2.506-1.695-(0.287)(0)]=884 kJ / kg \).

\( \text { where } P_{2}=P_{0}=0.1 MPa \text {. The availability supplied is } \)

\( \psi_{1}=h_{1}-h_{0}-T_{0}\left(s_{1}^{ o }-s_{0}^{ o }-R \ln \frac{P_{1}}{P_{0}}\right)=1046.1-298.2-(298)(2.968-1.695-0.287 \ln 5)=506 kJ / kg \).

Since there is no work or heat transfer, (7.19) gives

\( \varepsilon_{\text {II }}=\frac{\text { (availability produced) }+\text { (work produced) }+\text { (adjusted heat produced) }}{\text { (availability supplied) }+(\text { work used })+(\text { adjusted heat used })} \) (7.19).

\( \varepsilon_{\Pi}=\frac{\psi_{2}}{\psi_{1}}=\frac{884}{506}=1.75 \)

Note that second-law effectiveness is not bounded by 1 (much like the COP for a refrigeration cycle).
