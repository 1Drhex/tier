Guest Question.

Consider the balanced three-phase system shown in Figure 2.34. Determine \(v_{1}(t) \text { and } i_{2}(t)\). Assume positive phase sequence.

Admin
Solution

Replace delta by the equivalent WYE:

 

\(\bar{Z}_{Y}=-j \frac{2}{3} \Omega\)

 

Per-phase equivalent circuit is shown below:

 

Noting that \(\left(j 1.0 \|-j \frac{2}{3}\right)=-j 2\) , by voltage-divider law,

 

\(\begin{gathered}\bar{V}_{1}=\frac{-j 2}{-j 2+j 0.1}\left(100 \angle 0^{\circ}\right)=105 \angle 0^{\circ} \\\therefore v_{1}(t)=105 \sqrt{2} \cos \left(\omega t+0^{\circ}\right)=148.5 \cos \omega t V \leftarrow\end{gathered}\)

In order to find \(i_{2}(t)\) in the original circuit, let us calculate \(\bar{V}_{A^{\prime} B^{\prime}}\)

 

 

\(\bar{V}_{A^{\prime}B^{\prime}}=\bar{V}_{A^{\prime} N^{\prime}}-\bar{V}_{B^{\prime} N^{\prime}}=\sqrt{3} e^{j 30^{\circ}} \bar{V}_{A^{\prime} N^{\prime}}=173.2 \angle 30^{\circ}\)

 

Then \(\bar{I}_{A^{\prime} B^{\prime}}=\frac{173.2 \angle 30^{\circ}}{-j 2}=86.6 \angle 120^{\circ}\)

\(\begin{aligned}\therefore i_{2}(t) &=86.6 \sqrt{2} \cos \left(\omega t+120^{\circ}\right) \\&=122.5 \cos \left(\omega t+120^{\circ}\right) A \leftarrow\end{aligned}\)
