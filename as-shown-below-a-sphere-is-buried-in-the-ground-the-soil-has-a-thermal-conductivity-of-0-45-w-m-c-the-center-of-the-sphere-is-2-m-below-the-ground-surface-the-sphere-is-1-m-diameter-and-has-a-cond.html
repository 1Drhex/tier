Guest Question.

As shown below, a sphere is buried in the ground. The soil has a thermal conductivity of 0.45 W/m C. The center of the sphere is 2 m below the ground surface. The sphere is 1 m diameter and has a conductivity of 15 W/m C. It generates heat uniformly at a rate of 3000 W/m³. The surface of the ground is at 30 C. What is the maximum temperature in the sphere?

Admin
Solution

\(S = \frac{2 \pi D}{1 – \frac{D}{4 d} }\) (12.64)

 

where D is the diameter of the sphere and d is the depth of the center of the sphere.
For our problem, D = 1 m and d = 2 m. Therefore, the shape factor is

 

\(S = \frac{2 \pi D}{1 – \frac{D}{4 d} } = \frac{2 \pi (1)}{1 – \frac{1}{4 (2)} } = 7.181 m\)

\(q=kS(T_1 – T_2 ​) \) (3.106)

The heat transfer is given by Equation (3.106), which for this problem is

 

\(q = k_{soil} S (T_{o} – T_{ground})\) (12.65)

For our problem, \(S = 7.181 m , T_{ground} = 30 C , and k_{soil} = 0.45 W / m C.\) Putting these values into Eq. (12.65), we have

\(q = 0.45 (7.181 )(T_{o} – 30) = 3.231(T_{o} – 30) \) (12.66)

Now let us look at the heat generation in the sphere. The heat generation is the heat generation rate times the volume of the sphere or

 

\(q = q_{gen}(Volume) = q_{gen} \left(\frac{4}{3} \pi r^3_{o} \right) = (3000) (4 / 3) \pi (0.5)^3 = 1570.8 W\) (12.67)

 

For steady state, the heat flows given by Eqs. (12.66) and (12.67) are equal. That is \(3.231(T_{o} – 30) = 1570.8\) and the surface temperature of the sphere is \(T_{o} = 516.2 C\)
The maximum temperature in the sphere is given by Eq. (12.50):

 

\(T_{max} = T_{o} + \frac{q_{gen} r^2_{o}}{6 k} = 516.2 + \frac{3000(0.5)^2}{6 (15)} = 524.5 C\)

 

The maximum temperature in the sphere is at the center and is 525 C.
