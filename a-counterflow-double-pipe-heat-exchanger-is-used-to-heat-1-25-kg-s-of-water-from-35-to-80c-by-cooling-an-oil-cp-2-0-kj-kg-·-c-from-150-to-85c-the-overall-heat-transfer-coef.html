Guest Question.

A counterflow double-pipe heat exchanger is used to heat 1.25 kg/s of water from 35 to \(80^{\circ}C\) by cooling an oil \([c_{p} = 2.0 kJ/kg ·^{\circ} C]\) from 150 to \(85^{\circ}C\). The overall heat-transfer coefficient is \(150 Btu/h · ft^{2} · ^{\circ}F\). A similar arrangement is to be built at another plant location, but it is desired to compare the performance of the single counterflow heat exchanger with two smaller counterflow heat exchangers connected in series on the water side and in parallel on the oil side, as shown in Figure. The oil flow is split equally between the two exchangers, and it may be assumed that the overall heat-transfer coefficient for the smaller exchangers is the same as for the large exchanger. If the smaller exchangers cost 20 percent more per unit surface area, which would be the most economical arrangement—the one large exchanger or two equal-sized small exchangers?

Admin
Solution

We calculate the surface area required for both alternatives and then compare costs. For the one large exchanger

\(q =\dot{m}_{c}c_{c} \Delta T_{c} =\dot{m}_{h}c_{h} \Delta T_{h}\)
\(= (1.25)(4180)(80 − 35) = \dot{m}_{h}c_{h}(150 − 85)\)
\(= 2.351 × 10^{5} W [8.02 × 10^{5} Btu/h]\)
\(\dot{m}_{ c}c_{c} = 5225 W/^{\circ}C \dot{m}_{ h}c_{h} = 3617 W/^{\circ}C\)

so that the oil is the minimum fluid:

\(\epsilon _{h} =\frac{ \Delta T_{h}}{150 − 35} = \frac{150 − 85}{150 − 35} = 0.565\)

 

\( \frac{C_{min}}{C_{max}}=\frac{ 3617}{5225} = 0.692 \)

From Figure or Table, \( NTU_{max} = 1.09\), so that

\( A = NTU_{max} \frac{C_{min}}{U} =\frac{ (1.09)(3617)}{850} = 4.649 m^{2} [50.04 ft^{2}] \)

We now wish to calculate the surface-area requirement for the two small exchangers shown in the sketch. We have

\( \dot{m}_{ h}c_{h} = \frac{3617}{2} = 1809 W/^{\circ}C \)

 

\( \dot{m}_{c}c_{c} = 5225 W/^{\circ}C \)

 

\( \frac{C_{min}}{C_{max}}=\frac{ 1809}{5225} = 0.347 \)

The number of transfer units is the same for each heat exchanger because UA and \(C_{min}\) are the same for each exchanger. This requires that the effectiveness be the same for each exchanger. Thus,

\( \epsilon_{1} =\frac{ T_{oi} − T_{oe,1}}{T_{oi} − T_{w,1}}= \epsilon_{2 } = \frac{T_{oi} − T_{oe,2}}{T_{oi} − T_{w,2}} \)

 

\( \epsilon_{1} = \frac{150 − T_{oe,1}}{150 − 35} = \epsilon_{2} = \frac{150 − T_{oe,2}}{150 − T_{w,2}} \) [a]

where the nomenclature for the temperatures is indicated in the sketch. Because the oil flow is the same in each exchanger and the average exit oil temperature must be \(85^{\circ}C\), we may write

\( \frac{T_{oe,1} + T_{oe,2}}{2} = 85 \) [b]

An energy balance on the second heat exchanger gives

\( (5225)(T_{w3} − T_{w2}) = (1809)(T_{oi} − T_{oe,2})\)
\((5225)(80 − T_{w2}) = (1809)(150 − T_{oe,2}) \) [c]

We now have the three equations (a), (b), and (c) that may be solved for the three unknowns \(T_{oe,1}, T_{oe,2} , and T_{w2}\). The solutions are

\( T_{oe,1} = 76.98^{\circ}C\)
\(T_{oe,2} = 93.02^{\circ}C\)
\(T_{w2} = 60.26^{\circ}C \)

The effectiveness can then be calculated as

\( \epsilon_{1} = \epsilon_{2} = \frac{150 − 76.98}{150 − 35} = 0.635 \)

From Figure or Table, we obtain \(NTU_{max}= 1.16\) , so that

\( A = NTU_{max}\frac{C_{min}}{U} = \frac{(1.16)(1809)}{850 }= 2.47 m^{2} \)

We thus find that \(2.47 m^{2}\) of area is required for each of the small exchangers, or a total of \(4.94 m^{2}\). This is greater than the \(4.649 m^{2}\) required in the one larger exchanger; in addition, the cost per unit area is greater so that the most economical choice would be the single larger exchanger. It may be noted, however, that the pumping costs for the oil would probably be less with the two smaller exchangers, so that this could precipitate a decision in favor of the smaller exchangers if pumping costs represented a sizable economic factor.

\(C = C_{min}/C_{max}\) \( \epsilon = effectiveness\) \( N = NTU = UA/C_{min} \)
Flow geometry Relation
Double pipe:
Parallel flow \(N = \frac{− ln[1 − (1 + C)\epsilon ]}{1 + C}\)
Counterflow \(N = \frac{1}{C – 1}ln\left(\frac{\epsilon -1}{C\epsilon-1 } \right) \)
Counterflow, C = 1 \(N = \frac{\epsilon }{1 – \epsilon }\)
Cross flow:
\(C_{max}\) mixed, \(C_{min}\) unmixed \(N = − ln\left[ 1 + \frac{1}{ C} ln(1 − C\epsilon )\right] \)
\(C_{max}\) unmixed, \(C_{min}\) mixed \(N = \frac{−1}{C} ln\left[ 1 + C ln(1 − \epsilon )\right] \)
Shell and tube:
One shell pass, 2, 4, 6, tube passes \(N = −(1 + C^{2})^{−1/2}× ln\left[ \frac{2/\epsilon − 1 − C − (1 + C^{2})^{1/2}}{ 2/\epsilon − 1 − C + (1 + C^{2})^{1/2}} \right] \)
All exchangers, C = 0 \(N = − ln(1 − \epsilon )\)
