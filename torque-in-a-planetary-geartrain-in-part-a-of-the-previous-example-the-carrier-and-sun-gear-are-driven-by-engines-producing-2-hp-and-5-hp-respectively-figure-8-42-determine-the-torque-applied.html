Torque in a Planetary Geartrain

In part (a) of the previous example, the carrier and sun gear are driven by engines producing 2 hp and 5 hp, respectively (Figure 8.42). Determine the torque applied to the shaft of the output ring gear.

To calculate the torque applied to the output shaft, we recognize that, to balance the power supplied to the geartrain, the output shaft must transfer a total of 7 hp to a mechanical load. To apply Equation (8.5) in dimensionally consistent units, first convert the units for power from horsepower to (ft · lb)/s with the conversion factor in Table 7.2.

 

\(P=T \omega \text { (torque) }\) (Equation 8.5)

 

Table 7.2 Conversion Factors between Various Unit for Power in USCS and SI
(ft . lb)/s W hp
1 1.356 1.818 × 10-3
0.7376 1 1.341 × 10-3
550 745.7 1
Admin
Solution

In dimensionally consistent units, the speed of the ring gear’s output shaft is

 

\(\begin{aligned}\omega_{ r } &=(4800 rpm )\left(0.1047 \frac{ rad / s }{ rpm }\right) \\&=502.6( rpm )\left(\frac{ rad / s }{ rpm }\right) \\&=502.6 \frac{ rad }{ s }\end{aligned}\)

 

The total power supplied to the geartrain is P = 7 hp, and, in dimensionally consistent units, this becomes

 

\(\begin{aligned}P &=(7 hp )\left(550 \frac{( ft \cdot lb ) / s }{ hp }\right) \\&=3850( hp )\left(\frac{( ft \cdot lb ) / s }{ hp }\right) \\&=3850 \frac{ ft \cdot lb }{ s }\end{aligned}\)

 

The output torque is therefore

 

\(\begin{aligned}T &=\frac{3850( ft \cdot lb ) / s }{502.6 rad / s } \leftarrow[P=T \omega] \\&=7.659\left(\frac{ ft \cdot lb }{ s }\right)\left(\frac{ s }{ rad }\right) \\&=7.659 ft \cdot lb\end{aligned}\)
