Guest Question.

Estimate the wall superheat at the onset of departure from nucleate boiling (DNB) or critical heat ﬂux for saturated pool boiling of water on a horizontal surface at atmospheric pressure. Assume the constant \(C_{sf}\) to be 0.013 in the Rohsenow correlation.

Admin
Solution

The properties of saturated water at atmospheric pressure are

\(T_{sat}=100^\circ C, \rho _{_{f}}=958kg/m^3, \rho _{_{g}}=0.598kg/m^3, h_{_{fg}}=2257kJ/kg\),

\(\sigma =0.05878N/m, \mu _{_{f}}=283.1\times 10^{-6}Ns/m^2, c_{_{pf}}=4.218kJ/kg K\),

\(Pr_{_{f}}=1.75, C_{_{sf}}=0.013\)

Using Eq. (11.22) ,

\(q_{_{crit}}=q_{_{max}}=Kh_{_{fg}}\rho _{_{g}}^{^{1/2}}\left[\sigma g(\rho _{_{f}}-\rho _{_{g}})\right] ^{1/4}\)

 

\(q_{_{crit}}=0.149h_{_{fg}}\rho _{_{g}}^{^{1/2}}\left[\sigma g(\rho _{_{f}}-\rho _{_{g}})\right] ^{1/4}\)

 

\(=0.149\times 2257\times 10^3\times (0.598)^{1/2}[0.05878\times 9.81\times (958-0.598)]^{1/4}\)

 

\(=1.26\times 10^6W/m^2\)

According to the Rohsenow correlation (this is very widely used and is the workhorse in pool boiling calculations for obtaining the frst cut estimates in an engineering problem), Eq. (11.20),

\(\frac{c_{_{pf}}\Delta T_{_{sup}}}{h_{_{fg}}} =C_{_{sf}}\left[\frac{q}{h_{_{fg}}\mu _{_{f}}}\left\lgroup\frac{\sigma }{g(\rho _{_f}-\rho _{_g})} \right\rgroup^{\frac{1}{2} } \right]^n \left\lgroup\frac{\mu _{_f}c_{_{pf}}}{k_{_f}} \right\rgroup^{m+1} \)

 

\(\Delta T_{_{sup}} =\left\lgroup\frac{q}{\mu _{f}h_{_{fg}}} \right\rgroup^n \left\lgroup\frac{\sigma }{g(\rho _{_f}-\rho _{_g})} \right\rgroup^{_{n/2}} \frac{h_{{fg}}C_{sf}Pr_{f}^{^{m+1}}}{c_{_{pf}}} \)

 

\(=\left\lgroup\frac{1.26\times 10^{6}}{283.1\times 10^{-6}\times 2257\times 10^3} \right\rgroup ^n\)×\( \left\lgroup\frac{0.05878}{9.81\times (958-0.598)} \right\rgroup^{n/2} \) ×

\(\frac{2257\times 10^3\times 0.013\times (1.75)^{m+1}}{4.218\times 10^3} \)

 

\(=(1971.96)^n\times (6.258\times 10^{-6})^{n/2}\times 6.955\times (1.75)^{m+1}\)

 

\(=4.933^n\times 6.955\times 1.75^{m+1}\)

The original Rohsenow correlation had values of n = 0.33 and m = 0.7.

\(\Delta T_{_{sup}}=4.933^{0.33}\times 6.955\times 1.75^{1.7}\)

=30.49 K

For n = 0.33 and m = 0 (recommended by Rohsenow, later for only water),

\(\Delta T_{_{sup}}=4.933^{0.33}\times 6.955\times 1.75\)

=20.60 K
