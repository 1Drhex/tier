Guest Question.

Determine the moments at each joint of the frame shown in Fig. \(12-19(\mathrm{a})\). The moment of inertia of each member is indicated in the figure.

Admin
Solution

The frame is first held from sidesway as shown in Fig. 12-19b. The internal moments are computed at the joints as indicated in Fig. 12-19d. Here the stiffness factor of \(C D\) was computed using \(3 E I / L\) since there is a pin at \(D\). Calculation of the horizontal reactions at \(A\) and \(D\) is shown in Fig. 12-19e. Thus, for the entire frame,

\(\Sigma F_{x}=0 ; \quad R=2.89-1.00=1.89 \mathrm{k}\)

The opposite force is now applied to the frame as shown in Fig. 12-19c. As in the previous example, we will consider a force \(\mathbf{R}^{\prime}\) acting as shown in Fig. 12-19f. As a result, joints \(B\) and \(C\) are displaced by the same amount \(\Delta^{\prime} .\) The fixed-end moments for \(B A\) are computed from

\( (\mathrm{FEM})_{A B}=(\mathrm{FEM})_{B A}=-\frac{6 E I \Delta}{L^{2}}=-\frac{6 E(2000) \Delta^{\prime}}{(10)^{2}} \)

However, from the table on the inside back cover, for \(C D\) we have

\( (\mathrm{FEM})_{C D}=-\frac{3 E I \Delta}{L^{2}}=-\frac{3 E(2500) \Delta^{\prime}}{(15)^{2}} \)

Assuming the FEM for \(A B\) is \(-100 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft}\) as shown in Fig. 12-19f, the corresponding FEM at \(C\), causing the same \(\Delta^{\prime}\), is found by comparison, i.e.,

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta^{\prime}=-\frac{(-100)(10)^{2}}{6 E(2000)} &=-\frac{(\mathrm{FEM})_{C D}(15)^{2}}{3 E(2500)} \\ (\mathrm{FEM})_{C D} &=-27.78 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \end{aligned} \)

Moment distribution for these FEMs is tabulated in Fig. 12-19g. Computation of the horizontal reactions at \(A\) and \(D\) is shown in Fig. 12-19h. Thus, for the entire frame,

\(\sum F_{x}=0; \quad R^{\prime}=11.0+1.55=12.55 \mathrm{k}\)

The resultant moments in the frame are therefore

\(\begin{array}{l}M_{A B}=9.58+\left(\frac{1.89}{12.55}\right)(-69.91)=-0.948 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ M_{B A}=19.34+\left(\frac{1.89}{12.55}\right)(-40.01)=13.3 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ M_{B C}=-19.34+\left(\frac{1.89}{12.55}\right)(40.01)=-13.3 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ M_{C B}=15.00+\left(\frac{1.89}{12.55}\right)(23.31)=18.5 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ M_{C D}=-15.00+\left(\frac{1.89}{12.55}\right)(-23.31)=-18.5 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \end{array}\)
