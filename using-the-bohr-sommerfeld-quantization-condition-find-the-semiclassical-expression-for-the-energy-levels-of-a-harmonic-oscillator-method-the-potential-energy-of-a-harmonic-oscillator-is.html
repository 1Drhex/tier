Guest Question.

Using the Bohr–Sommerfeld quantization condition
Find the semiclassical expression for the energy levels of a harmonic oscillator.
Method The potential energy of a harmonic oscillator is \(½k_{f} x^{2} \). At the classical turning points, \(E=½k_{f} x^{2}_{tp}\). Evaluate the integral in eqn 6.9 by using \(p(x)=\left\{2m(E-V)\right\} ^{1/2}\) and the integral

\(\int{(a^{2}-x^{2} )^{1/2} dx} =\frac{1}{2}\left[x(a^{2}-x^{2} )^{1/2}+a^{2}\arcsin \frac{x}{\left|a\right| } \right]\)

and then set the resulting expression for E equal to the right-hand side of eqn 6.9.

\(\int_{x_{tp1} }^{x_{tp2} }{p(x)dx} =(n+½)\pi \hbar \) n=0,1,2 . . . (6.9)

Admin
Solution

With \(x_{tp1}=-a\) and \(x_{tp2}=+a\), the left-hand side of eqn 6.9 gives

\(\int_{-a}^{a}{(2m(E-½k_{f}x^{2} ))^{1/2} dx}=(mk_{f} )^{1/2} \int_{-a}^{a}{(2E/k_{f}-x^{2}) )^{1/2} } dx\)

 

\(=(mk_{f} )^{1/2} \int_{-a}^{a}{(x^{2}_{tp} -x^{2} )^{1/2} } dx\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{2} (mk_{f} )^{1/2} \left[a^{2}\arcsin \left(\frac{a}{a} \right)-a^{2} \arcsin \left(\frac{-a}{a} \right) \right] \)

 

\(=\frac{1}{2} (mk_{f} )^{1/2} \pi a^{2} =\pi E(m/k_{f} )^{1/2} \)

Equating this with the right-hand side of eqn 6.9 results in

\(\pi E(m/k_{f} )^{1/2} =(n+\frac{1}{2} )\pi \hbar\) or \(E=(n+\frac{1}{2} )\hbar (k_{f}/m )^{1/2}\)

For the harmonic oscillator, the semiclassical expression matches the exact quantum mechanical energy levels (eqn 2.40).

\(E_{v} =(v+\frac{1}{2} )\hbar w\) where \(w=\left(\frac{k_{f} }{m}\right)^{1/2}\) \(v=0,1,2, . . .\) (2.40)
