Guest Question.

A beam is to be made of steel that has an allowable bending stress of \( σ_{allow} = 24 ksi\) and an allowable shear stress of \(τ_{allow} = 14.5 ksi\). Select an appropriate W shape that will carry the loading shown in Fig. 1 1–7 a .

Admin
Solution

Shear and Moment Diagrams. The support reactions have been calculated, and the shear and moment diagrams are shown in Fig. 11–7 b . From these diagrams, \(V_{max} = 30 kip\) and \(M_{max} = 120 kip·ft\).

Bending Stress. The required section modulus for the beam is determined from the flexure formula,

\(S_{req’d}=\frac{M_{max}}{\sigma _{allow}}=\frac{120kip\cdot ft.(12in./ft)}{24kip/in^2}=60in^3\)

Using the table in Appendix B , the following beams are adequate:

\(W18\times 40 \quad\quad S=68.4in^3\)

 

\(W16\times 45 \quad\quad S=72.7in^3\)

 

\(W14\times 43 \quad\quad S=62.7in^3\)

 

\(W12\times 50 \quad\quad S=64.7in^3\)

 

\(W10\times 54 \quad\quad S=60.0in^3\)

 

\(W8\times 67 \quad\quad S=60.4in^3\)

The beam having the least weight per foot is chosen, i.e.,

\(W18\times 40\)

The actual maximum moment \(M_{max}\), which includes the weight of the beam, can be calculated and the adequacy of the selected beam can be checked. In comparison with the applied loads, however, the beam’s weight, \((0.040 kip/ft)(18 ft)= 0.720 kip\), will only slightly increase \(S_{req’d}\). In spite of this,

\(S_{req’d}=60in^3<68.4in^3 \quad\quad\quad OK\)

Shear Stress. Since the beam is a wide-flange section , the average shear stress within the web will be considered. (See .) Here the web is assumed to extend from the very top to the very bottom of the beam. From Appendix B , for a \(W18\times 40, d = 17.90 in., t_w = 0.315 in\). Thus,

\(\tau _{avg}=\frac{V_{max}}{A_w} =\frac{30kip}{(17.90in.)(0.315in.)}=5.32ksi<14.5ksi \quad\quad OK \)

Use a\( W18 \times 40\).
