Guest Question.

The engine cylinder of a motorcycle is constructed of 2024-T6 aluminum alloy and is of height H = 100 mm and outer diameter \(D = 2r_{1} = 50 mm\). Under typical operating conditions, \(q_{t} = 2 kW\) of heat is transferred from the cylinder to ambient air at 300 K, with a convection coefficient of \(75 W/m^{2} ⋅ K\). Annular fins are integrally cast with the cylinder to reduce the cylinder temperature. Consider ten equally-spaced fins, each of which are of thickness t = 4 mm and length L = 20 mm. What reduction in the cylinder temperature can be achieved by use of the fins?

Admin
Solution

Known: Operating conditions of a finned motorcycle cylinder.

Find: Reduction in cylinder temperature associated with using fins.

 

Assumptions:
1. Steady-state conditions.
2. Temperature is uniform across the fin thickness.

3. Constant properties.
4. Negligible radiation exchange with surroundings.
5. Uniform convection coefficient over surface (with or without fins).

 

Properties: Table A.1, 2024-T6 aluminum (T ≈ 550 K): k = 186 W/m ⋅ K.

 

Analysis: With the fins in place, Equation 3.106 can be rearranged to determine an
expression for the cylinder temperature

 

\(q_{t}=h[N{\eta }_{f}A_{f}+(A_{t}-NA_{f})]\theta _{b}=hA_{t}\left [ 1-\frac{NA_{f}}{A_{t}}(1-\eta _{f}) \right ]\theta _{b}\) (3.106)

 

\(T_{b}=T_{\infty }+\frac{q_{t}}{hA_{t}\left [ 1-\frac{NA_{f}}{A_{t}}(1-n_{f}) \right ]}\)

 

where \(A_{f}=2\pi (r_{2c}^{2}-r_{1}^{2})=2\pi (r_{2c}^{2}-r_{1}^{2})=2\pi [(0.047m)]=0.00995m^{2}\) and, from Equation 3.104, \(A_{t}=NA_{f}+2\pi r_{1}(H-Nt)=0.0995m^{2}+2\pi (0.025m)[0.10m-0.04m]=0.109m^{2}\). With \(r_{2c}/r_{1}=1.88, L_{c}=0.022m,A_{p}=8.8\times 10^{-5}m^{2}\) we obtain \(L_{c}^{3/2}(h/kA_{p})^{1/2}=0.221\). Hence, from Figure 3.20 (or Equation 3.96), the fin efficiency is \(\eta _{f}\approx 0.96\). With the fins, the cylinder temperature is

 

\(A_{t}=NA_{f}+A_{b}\) (3.104)

 

\(mL_{c}=\left ( \frac{2h}{kA_{p}} \right )^{1/2}L_{c}^{3/2}\) (3.96)

 

\(T_{b}=27^{\circ}C+\frac{2000W}{75W/m^{2}\cdot K\times 0.109m^{2}\left [ 1-\frac{10\times 0.00995m^{2}}{0.109m^{2}}(1-0.96) \right ]}=282^{\circ}C\)

 

Without the fins, the cylinder temperature would be

 

\(T_{b,wo}=T_{\infty }+\frac{q_{t}}{h(2\pi r_{1}H)}=27^{\circ}C+\frac{2000W}{75W/m^{2}\cdot K(2\pi \times 0.025m\times 0.10m)}=1725^{\circ}C\)

 

Therefore, the reduction in cylinder temperature is

 

\(\Delta T_{b}=T_{b,wo}-T_{b}=1725^{\circ}C-282^{\circ}C=1443C^{\circ}\)

 

Comments:
1. From Table A.1, the melting temperature of 2024-T6 aluminum is 775 K = 502°C. The engine must be equipped with fins to avoid failure.

 

2. The fins are of high efficiency. Assuming isothermal fins \((\eta _{f}=1)\), the cylinder temperature would be \(T_{b}=T_{\infty }+q_{t}/hA_{t}=272^{\circ}C\), which is only slightly lower than the
predicted temperature.

3. Further reduction in the cylinder temperature could be achieved by adding more fins. Prescribing a fin clearance of 2 mm at each end of the array and a minimum fin gap of 4 mm, the maximum allowable number of fins is \(N_{max}= H/S= 0.10m/(0.004+0.004)m=12.5\), which we round down to \(N_{max}=12\). Use of 12 fins reduces the cylinder temperature to \(T_{b}=245^{\circ}C\).

4. The assumed temperature of 550 K (277°C) that was used to evaluate the thermal conductivity is reasonable.

5. The Models/Extended Surfaces option in the Advanced section of IHT provides readyto- solve models for straight, pin, and annular fins, as well as for fin arrays. The models include the efficiency relations of Figures 3.19 and 3.20 and Table 3.5.
