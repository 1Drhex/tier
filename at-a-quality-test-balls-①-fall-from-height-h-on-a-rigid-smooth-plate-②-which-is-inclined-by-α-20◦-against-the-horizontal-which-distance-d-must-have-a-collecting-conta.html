Guest Question.

At a quality test, balls ① fall from height \(H\) on a rigid smooth plate ② which is inclined by \(\alpha =20 °\) against the horizontal.

Which distance d must have a collecting container ③, so that only the balls with a coefficient of restitution \(e\geq 0.8 \) reach the container?

Admin
Solution

That balls with \(e\geq 0.8\) reach the container, the distance \(d\) must exactly be equal to the flight distance of balls with the coefficient
of restitution \(e=0.8 \) . The balls ① hit plate ② with the velocity

\(v_{1 }=\sqrt{2gH } \) .

Using the displayed coordinate system (\(x\widehat{=} \) contact normal), its components are

\(v_{1x}=-v_{1}\cos \alpha \) ,

\(v_{1y}=-v_{1}\sin \alpha \) .

Since the impulse in y-direction is zero (smooth plate), \(\overline{v} _{1y}= v_{1y} \) holds. The impact hypothesis leads then with \(v_{2x} =\overline{v} _{2x}=0\) (rigid plate) to

\(e=-\frac{\overline{v}_{1x} }{v_{1x} } \rightsquigarrow \overline{v}_{1x} =-ev_{1x}=ev_{1}\cos \alpha \) .

Thus, for the limit case \(e = 0,8\) follows for the angle \(\beta \)

 

\(\tan \beta=\frac{\left|\overline{v}_{1y } \right| }{\left|\overline{v}_{1x } \right|} =\frac{\left|v_{1y} \right| }{ev_{1x} }=\frac{1}{e}\tan \alpha \rightsquigarrow \beta =24.46 °\)

and for the launching angle γ to the horizontal

\(\gamma =90°-\alpha -\beta =45.54° \) .

With the equations for the projectile motion we obtain the flight distance \(d\) of balls with \(e = 0.8\) :

\(\underline{\underline{d} }=\frac{1}{g}\overline{v}^{2}_{1}\sin 2\gamma =\frac{1}{g}\left(\overline{v}^{2}_{1x}+\overline{v}^{2}_{1y} \right) \sin 2\gamma \)

 

\(=2H\left(e^{2}\cos^{2}\alpha +\sin^{2}\alpha \right)\sin 2\gamma =\underline{\underline{1.364H} } \) .
