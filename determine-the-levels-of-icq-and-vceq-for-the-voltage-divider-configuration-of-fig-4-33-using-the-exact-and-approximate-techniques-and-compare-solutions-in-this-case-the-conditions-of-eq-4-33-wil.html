Guest Question.

Determine the levels of \(I_{C_{Q}}\) and \(V_{CE_{Q}}\) for the voltage-divider configuration of Fig. 4.33 using the exact and approximate techniques and compare solutions. In this case, the conditions of Eq. (4.33) will not be satisfied but the results will reveal the difference in solution if the criterion of Eq. (4.33) is ignored.

Admin
Solution

Exact Analysis

Eq. (4.33): \(\beta R_{E} \geq 10R_{2}\)

\((50)(1.2 K\Omega) \geq 10(22 K\Omega)\)

 

\(60 K\Omega \ngeq 220 K\Omega (not satisfied) \)

 

\(R_{Th} = R_{1} \parallel R_{2} = 82 K\Omega \parallel 22 K\Omega = 17.35 K\Omega\)

 

\(E_{Th} = \frac{R_{2} V_{CC}}{R_{1} + R_{2}} = \frac{22 K\Omega (18 V)}{82 K\Omega + 22 K\Omega} = 3.81 V\)

 

\(I_{B} = \frac{E_{Th} – V_{BE}}{R_{Th} + (\beta + 1 )R_{E}} = \frac{3.81 V – 0.7 V}{17.35 K\Omega + (51)(1.2 K\Omega)} = \frac{3.11 V}{78.55 K\Omega}\)

 

\( I_{C_{Q}} = \beta I_{B} = (50)(39.6 \mu A ) = 1.98 mA\)

 

\(V_{CE_{Q}} = V_{CC} – I_{C}(R_{C} + R_{E}) = 18 V – (1.98 mA)(5.6 K\Omega + 1.2 K\Omega) =4.54 V\)

Approximate Analysis

\(V_{B} = E_{Th} = 3.81 V \)

 

\(V_{E} = V_{B} – V_{BE} = 3.81 V – 0.7 V = 3.11 V\)

 

\(I_{C_{Q}}\cong I_{E} = \frac{V_{E}}{R_{E}} = \frac{3.11 V}{1.2 K\Omega} = 2.59 mA\)

 

\(V_{CE_{Q}} = V_{CC} – I_{C}(R_{C} + R_{E}) = 18 V – (2.59 mA)(5.6 K\Omega + 1.2 K\Omega) = 3.88 V\)

Tabulating the results, we have:

\(I_{C_{Q}}(mA)\) \(V_{CE_{Q}}(V)\)
Exact 1.98 4.54
Approximate 2.59 3.88

 

The results reveal the difference between exact and approximate solutions. \(I_{C_{Q}}\) is about 30% greater with the approximate solution, while \(V_{CE_{Q}}\) is about 10% less. The results
are notably different in magnitude, but even though \(\beta R_{E}\) is only about three times larger than \(R_{2}\), the results are still relatively close to each other. For the future, however, our analysis will be dictated by Eq. (4.33) to ensure a close similarity between exact and approximate solutions.
