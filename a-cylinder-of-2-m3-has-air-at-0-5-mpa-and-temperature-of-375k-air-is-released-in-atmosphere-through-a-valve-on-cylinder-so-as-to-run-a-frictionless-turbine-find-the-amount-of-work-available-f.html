Guest Question.

A cylinder of 2 \(\textbf{m}^{\textbf{3}}\) has air at 0.5 MPa and temperature of 375째K. Air is released in atmosphere through a valve on cylinder so as to run a frictionless turbine. Find the amount of work available from turbine assuming no heat loss and complete kinetic energy being used for running turbine. Take \(\textbf{C}_{\textbf{p , air}}\) = 1.003 kJ/kg . K, \(\textbf{C}_{\textbf{v , air}}\) = 0.716 kJ/kg . K, \(\textbf{R}_{\textbf{air}}\) = 0.287 kJ/kg . K.

Admin
Solution

Let initial states and final states of air inside cylinder be given by

 

\(m_{1}, p_{1}, V_{1}, T_{1}\),

 

and

 

\(m_{2}, p_{2}, V_{2}, T_{2}\)

 

respectively. It is a case of emptying of cylinder.

Initial mass of air,

 

\(\quad m_{1}=\frac{p_{1} V_{1}}{P_{\mathrm{aur}} \cdot T_{1}}=9.29 \mathrm{~kg} .\)

 

For adiabatic expansion during release of air through valve from

 

\(0.5 \mathrm{MPa}\)

to atmospheric pressure.

 

\(\begin{aligned}T_{2} &=T_{1}\left(\frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}\right)^{\frac{\gamma-1}{\gamma}} \\&=375\left(\frac{1.013 \times 10^{5}}{0.5 \times 10^{6}}\right)^{\frac{1.4-1}{1.4}}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}T_{2} &=237.65 \mathrm{~K} \text {, } \\& n \cdot \mathrm{V} \mathrm{V}\end{aligned}\)

Final mass of air left in tank,

 

\(\quad m_{2}=\frac{p_{2} V_{2}}{R T_{2}}\) \(m_{2}=\frac{1.013 \times 10^{5} \times 2}{\left(0.287 \times 10^{3} \times 237.65\right)}=2.97 \mathrm{~kg}\)

 

Writing down energy equation for unsteady flow system

 

\(\begin{aligned}\left(m_{1}-m_{2}\right)\left(h_{2}+\frac{C^{2}}{2}\right) &=m_{1} u_{1}-m_{2} u_{2} \\\left(m_{1}-m_{2}\right) \frac{C^{2}}{2} &=\left(m_{1} u_{1}-m_{2} u_{2}\right)-\left(m_{1}-m_{2}\right) h_{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

Kinetic energy available for running turbine

 

\(=\left(m_{1} C_{v} T_{1}-m_{2} C_{v} T_{2}\right)-\left(m_{1}-m_{2}\right) \cdot C_{p} \cdot T_{2}\) \(=\left(9.29 \times 0.716 \times 10^{3} \times 375\right)-\left(2.97 \times 0.716 \times 10^{3} \times 237.65\right)-\{(9.29-2.97) \times 1.003\) \(\left.\quad \times 10^{3} \times 237.65\right\}\) \(=482.54 \times 10^{3} \mathrm{~J}\) Amount of work available \(=482.54 \mathrm{~kJ}\) .
