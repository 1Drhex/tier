Guest Question.

Laboratory compaction test results for a clayey silt are given in the following table.

Moisture content (%) Dry unit weight \((kN/m^3)\)
6 14.80
8 17.45
9 18.52
11 18.9
12 18.5
14 16.9

Following are the results of a field unit-weight determination test performed on thesame soil by means of the sand cone method:
• Calibrated dry density of Ottawa sand = 1570 \(kg/m^3\)
• Calibrated mass of Ottawa sand to fill the cone = 0.545 kg• Mass of jar + cone + sand (before use) = 7.59 kg
• Mass of jar + cone + sand (after use) = 4.78 kg
• Mass of moist soil from hole = 3.007 kg
• Moisture content of moist soil = 10.2%

Determine:
a. Dry unit weight of compaction in the field
b. Relative compaction in the field

Admin
Solution

Part a
In the field,

Mass of sand used to fill the hole and cone = 7.59 kg – 4.78 kg = 2.81 kg

Mass of sand used to fill the hole = 2.81 kg – 0.545 kg = 2.265 kg

Volume of the hole (V) \(= \frac{2.265 kg}{Dry density of Ottawa sand} \)

=\(\frac{2.265 kg}{1570 kg/m^3}=0.0014426 m^3 \)

Moist density of compacted soil = \(\frac{Mass of moist soil}{Volume of hole}\)

=\(\frac{3.007}{0.0014426}= 2.084.4 kg/m^3\)

Moist unit weight of compacted soil = \(\frac{(2084.4)(9.81)}{1000}= 20.45 kN/m^3\)

Hence,

\(\gamma _d=\frac{\gamma }{1+\frac{w(\%)}{100} } =\frac{20.45}{1+\frac{10.2}{100} }=18.56 kN/m^3 \)

Part b
The results of the laboratory compaction test are plotted in Figure 6.27. From the plot, we see that \(\gamma _{d(max)} =19 kN/m^3 \). Thus, from Eq. (6.19),

\(R(\%)=\frac{\gamma _{d(field)}}{\gamma _{d(max – lab)}}\times 100 \)

R = \(\frac{\gamma _{d(field)}}{\gamma _{d(max)}} = \frac{18.56}{19.0} =\) 97.7%
