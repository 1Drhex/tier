Guest Question.

Determine the higher and lower calorific values of coal for which following observations are made in bomb calorimeter.

Mass of coal sample = 1 gm
Mass of water in bomb calorimeter = 2.5 kg
Initial temperature of water = 20ºC
Maximum recorded temperature of water = 22.6ºC
Water equivalent of apparatus = 750 gm
Cooling correction = + 0.018ºC
Consider coal to have 5% H2 in it.
Admin
Solution
\(=(22.6-20)+0.018=2.618^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\)

 

Heat supplied to calorimeter and water

 

\(=(2500+750) \times 2.618 \\ =8508.5 \mathrm{Cal} / \mathrm{gm}\)

 

Higher calorific value of coal

 

\(=8508.5 \mathrm{Cal} / \mathrm{gm} \)

 

In order to get lower calorific value the heat carried by the steam formation is to be excluded from higher calorific value.

 

\(\begin{aligned}2 \mathrm{H}_{2}+\mathrm{O}_{2} & \rightarrow 2 \mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O} \\1 \mathrm{~kg} \mathrm{H}_{2}+8 \mathrm{~kg} \mathrm{O}_{2} & \rightarrow 9 \mathrm{~kg} \mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}\end{aligned}\)

(Mass of steam formed per kg of coal)

 

\(=0.05 \times 9\)

 

\(=0.45 \mathrm{gm}\) steam per gm of coal.

 

From steam table

\(h_{f g}\)

at \(20^{\circ} \mathrm{C}=586 \mathrm{kcal} / \mathrm{kg}\)

 

Lower heating value of coal

 

\(=8508.5-\left(0.45 \times \frac{586 \times 10^{3}}{10^{3}}\right) \\ =8244.8 \mathrm{cal} / \mathrm{gm} \)

 

Higher heating value

 

\(=8508.5 \mathrm{kcal} / \mathrm{kg}\)

 

{ Lower heating value

 

\(=8244.8 \mathrm{kcal} / \mathrm{kg}\)
