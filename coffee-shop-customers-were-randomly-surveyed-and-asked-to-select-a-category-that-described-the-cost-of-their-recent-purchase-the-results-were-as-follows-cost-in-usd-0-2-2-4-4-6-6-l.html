Guest Question.

Coffee shop customers were randomly surveyed and asked to select a category that
described the cost of their recent purchase. The results were as follows:
Cost (in USD) \(\quad\quad\)0 < 2\(\quad\) 2 < 4\(\quad\) 4 < 6\( \quad\) 6 < 8 \(\quad \) 8 < 10
Number of Customers 2\(\quad\) 3\(\quad\quad\quad\) 6\( \quad\quad\) 5\(\quad\quad \) 4
Find the sample mean and standard deviation of these costs.

Admin
Solution

The frequencies are the number of customers for each cost category. The
computations for the mean and the standard deviation are set out in Table 2.9.

\(n=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{K}{f_{i} } =20\) and \(\sum\limits_{i=1}^{K}{f_{i}m_{i} } =112\)

The sample mean is estimated by

\(\overline{x} =\frac{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{K}{f_{i}m_{i} } }{n} =\frac{112}{20} =5.6\)

Since these are sample data, the variance is estimated by

\(s^{2} =\frac{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{K}{f_{i}\left(m_{i}-\overline{x} \right)^2 } }{n-1} =\frac{120.8}{19} =6.3579\)

Hence, the sample standard deviation is estimated as

\(s=\sqrt{s^{2} } =\sqrt{6.3579} =2.52\)

Therefore, the mean coffee shop purchase price is estimated as $5.60, and the sample
standard deviation is estimated to be $2.52.
