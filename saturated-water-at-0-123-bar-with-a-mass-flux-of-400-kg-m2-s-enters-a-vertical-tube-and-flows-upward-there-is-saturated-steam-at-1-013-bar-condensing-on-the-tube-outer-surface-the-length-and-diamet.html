Guest Question.

Saturated water at 0.123 bar with a mass flux of \(400 kg/m^2\) s enters a vertical tube and flows upward. There is saturated steam at 1.013 bar condensing on the tube outer surface. The length and diameter of the tube are 0.6 m and 0.025 m, respectively. Assume laminar film condensation. Determine
(a) The average heat transfer coefficient on the condensation side.
(b) The average heat transfer coefficient on the boiling side, which can be assumed to be approximately equal to the mean of the local heat transfer coefficients at the inlet and outlet of the tube. You may use the Chen correlation.
(c) The average tube wall temperature.
(d) The rate of condensation (total), kg/s.
(e) The exit quality on the boiling side.

Admin
Solution

The average heat transfer coefficient relationship for laminar film condensation on a vertical plate, given by Eq. (11.63), can also be used for condensation on a vertical tube.

\(h_{NcB}=0.00122[\frac{k_f^{0.79}C_{pf}^{0.45}\rho_f^{0.49}}{\sigma^{0.5}\mu_f^{0.29}h_{fg}^{0.24}\rho_g^{0.24}}](\Delta T_e)^{0.24}(\Delta p_e)^{0.75}\) (11.36)

\(\bar{h_{f}} =0.943[\frac{\rho _{f}(\rho _{f}-\rho _{g})g h_{fg} K_{f}^{3}}{\mu _{f}z(T_{sat}-T_{w})} ]^{1/4}\)

Though all liquid properties in the above expression correspond to the film temperature \((T_{w} + T_{sat})/2\), and \(h_{fg}\)corresponds to \(T_{sat}\), let all the properties be evaluated at \(T_{sat}\)since \(T_{w}\)is unknown

\(\bar{h_{f}}=0.943[\frac{958 \times (958-0.598) \times9.81 \times2257 \times 10^{3} \times0.681^{3}}{283.1 \times 10^{-6} \times 0.6 \times (100-T_{w})} ]^{1/4}=0.943 \times13939.67 \times\frac{1}{(100-T_{w})^{1/4}} \)

The two-phase heat transfer coefficient for saturated boiling is obtained using Eq. (11.30),

\(h_{TP}=h_{c}+h_{NcB}\)

Using Eq. (11.34) and the saturated fluid properties at 0.123 bar,

\(x_{tt}=(\frac{\rho _{g}}{\rho _{f}} )^{0.5}(\frac{\mu _{f}}{\mu _{g}} )^{0.1}(\frac{1-x}{x} )^{0.9}\) (11.34)

 

\(\frac{1}{x_{tt}}=(\frac{\rho _{f}}{\rho _{g}} )^{0.5}(\frac{\mu _{g}}{\mu _{f}} )^{0.1}(\frac{x}{1-x} )^{0.9}=(\frac{988}{0.083} )^{0.5}\times(\frac{10.01\times10^{-6}}{547.8 \times 10^{-6}} )^{0.1}\times (\frac{x}{1-x} )^{0.9}=73.17 (\frac{x}{1-x} )^{0.9}\)

From Eq. (11.35),

F=1 for \((\frac{1}{x_{tt}})\leq 0.1\)

\(=2.35(\frac{1}{x_{tt}}+0.213)^{0.736}\)for \(\frac{1}{x_{tt}} \gt 0.1\)

From Eq. (11.32),

\(h_{c}=0.023(\frac{G(1-x)D}{\mu _{f}})^{0.8}(\frac{\mu c_{p}}{k})^{0.4}(\frac{K_{f}}{D})(F)=0.023(\frac{400\times 0.025}{547.8 \times 10^{-6}})^{0.8}\times(1-x)^{0.8}\times(3.56)^{0.4}\times(\frac{0.643}{0.025})(F)=2521.53\times(1-x)^{0.8}\times F\)

From Eq. (11.40),

\(h_{NcB}=0.00122[\frac{k_{f}^{0.79} c_{pf}^{0.45}\rho _{f}^{0.49}}{\sigma ^{0.5}\mu_{f}^{0.29} h_{fg}^{0.24}\rho _{g}^{0.24}}](\Delta T_{sup})^{0.24}(\Delta p_{sup})^{0.75}(S)\) (11.40)

\(h_{NcB}=0.00122[\frac{k_{f}^{0.79} c_{pf}^{0.45}\rho _{f}^{0.49}}{\sigma ^{0.5}\mu_{f}^{0.29} h_{fg}^{0.24}\rho _{g}^{0.24}}](T_{w}-T{sat})^{0.24}(P_{sat}(T_{W})-P_{sat}(T_{sat}))^{0.75}(s)=0.00122[\frac{0.7055\times42.16\times29.34}{0.2606\times0.1133\times33.92\times0.55}]\times\Delta T_{sup}^{0.24}\times \Delta P_{sup}^{0.75}\times S\)

From Eq. (11.33),

\(Re_{TP}=Re_{f} \times F^{1.25}=\frac{G(1-x)D}{\mu _{f}} \times F^{1.25}=18254.8 \times (1-x) \times F^{1.25} \)

From Eq. (11.41),

\(S=\frac{1}{1+2.53\times 10^{-6}Re_{TP}^{1.17}}\)

 

\(h_{NcB}=1.953\times (T_{w}-T{sat})^{0.24}\times (P_{sat}(T_{W})-P_{sat}(T_{sat}))^{0.75}\times S\)

 

\(\bar{h}_{TP}\approx \frac{h_{TP,in}+h_{TP,out}}{2}\)

 

\(Q_{condens}=\bar{h}_{f} A(T_{sat}-T_{w})=\bar{h}_{f} A(100- T_{W})=13145.11\times \frac{1}{(100-T_{w})^{1/4}}\times(100-T_{w})\times A\)

 

\(Q_{boil}=\bar{h}_{TP}A(T_{w}-T_{sat})=\bar{h}_{TP}A(T_{w}-50)\)

[Note that the saturation temperature on the boiling side is 50 °C and that on the condensation side is 100 °C.]
Please note that in this problem too, \(T_{w}\) is unknown, and consequently, “tedious” iterations to solve the problem are unavoidable. Discerning readers will note that problems on boiling and condensation are eminently solvable with a computer code or tools like MATLAB or SCILAB.
Initial guess:

Let \(T_{w}=\frac{100+50}{2}=75 °C\)

\(Q_{condens}=146967 A J/s=146967 \times \pi Dz=146967\times \pi \times 0.025\times 0.6=6925.45 w\)

At inlet, x = 0 (no two-phase) and hence \(h_{NcB}\)= 0. Also, at inlet, F = 1 as \(\frac{1}{x_{tt}}\)=0 for x=0 Therefore

\(h_{TP,in}=h_{c}=2521.35 w/m^{2}K\) \(G\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}h_{fg}x_{out}=Q_{condens}\) \(x_{out}=\frac{6925.45}{400 \times \frac{\pi }{4} \times 0.025^{2}\times 2382.7\times 10^{3}}=0.0148\)

\(\frac{1}{x_{tt}}\)=1.6726

F=3.748

\(h_{c}=9338.63 W/m^{2}K\) \(Re_{TP}=93788.79\)

S=0.375

\(h_{NcB}=1.953 \times (75-50)^{0.24} \times (38.59 \times 10^{3}-12.33\times 10^{3})^{0.75}\times 0.375=3271.2 W/m^{2}K\)

 

\(h_{TP,out}=h_{c,out}+h_{NcB,out}=9338.63+3271.2=12609.83 w/m^{2} K\)

 

\(\bar{h}_{TP}=\frac{2521.53+12609.83}{2}=7565.68 w/m^{2} K\)

 

\(Q_{boil}=\bar{h}_{TP}\pi Dz(T_{w}-T{sat})=7565.68\times\pi \times 0.025\times0.6\times(75-50)=8912.84 w Q_{condens}=6925.45 w\)

Since there is a large difference between \(Q_{boil}\)and \(Q_{condens}\), the assumed \(T_{w}\)is
incorrect.

Let \(T_{w}\)= 70 °C. Repeating the above calculations for \(T_{w}\)= 70 °C,

\(Q_{boil}\)=7021.49 w

\(Q_{condens}\)=7940.24 w

Now \(Q_{condens} \gt Q_{boil} \) , but the difference is smaller

Let \(T_{w}\)= 71 °C. Repeating the above calculations for \(T_{w}\)= 71 °C,

\(Q_{boil}\)=7483.4w

\(Q_{condens}\)=7740.8w

\(Q_{boil}\) and \(Q_{condens}\) are quite close now.

Let \(T_{w}\)= 71 .5°C. Repeating the above calculations for \(T_{w}\)= 71 .5°C,

\(Q_{boil}\)=7565.67 w

\(Q_{condens}\)=7640.57 w

 

Now the difference between \(Q_{boil}\) and \(Q_{condens}\)is very small, and it is approximately 1%.
The corresponding average heat transfer coefficients on the boiling side and the condensation side are 7467.6 W\(/m^{2}\)K and 5689 W\(/m^{2}\) K, respectively. Therefore,

(a) \(\bar{h}_{f}=5689 w/m^{2} K\)

(b) \(\bar{h}_{TP}=7467 w/m^{2} K\)

(c) \(T_{w}=71.5\mathring{c}\)

(d)\(m_{condens}=\frac{Q}{h_{fg}}=\frac{7640.57}{2257\times 10^{3}}=3.385\times 10^{-3} kg/s\)

(e)\(x_{out}=\frac{Q}{G\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}h_{fg}}=\frac{7640.57}{400 \times \frac{\pi }{4}\times 0.025^{2} \times 2382.7 \times10^{3}}=0.0163\)

Note
The calculation can be repeated taking liquid properties (other than \(h_{fg}\)) in the expression for laminar film heat transfer coefficient corresponding to (\(T_{w}\)+ \(T_{sat}\))/2. The calculation process needs to be repeated until the obtained \(T_{w}\)matches the value used for evaluating the liquid properties. The final Tw is expected to be slightly different
from the one obtained above (71.5 °C) and so are the values in (a), (b), (d), and (e).
As the quality continuously changes (and hence the heat transfer coefficient) along the flow direction, accurate design of heat exchangers involving flow boiling or flow condensation needs numerical methods. This is because the local heat transfer coefficient is a complex function of quality and moreover the local heat transfer coefficient also depends on the wall superheat (for boiling) or wall subcooling (for condensation), which is unknown. Hence, for accuracy, it needs to be solved numerically (and by iteration) taking small control volumes and using the energy balance and the heat convected.
