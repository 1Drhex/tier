Guest Question.

For a certain incident proton energy the reaction \(p + ^{56} Fe \rightarrow n + ^{56} Co\) has a cross section of 0.40 b. If we bombard a target in the form of a 1.0-cm-square, 1.0-μm-thick iron foil with a beam of protons equivalent to a current of 3.0 μA, and if the beam is spread uniformly over the entire surface of the target, at what rate are the neutrons produced?

Admin
Solution

We first calculate the number of nuclei in the target. The volume of the target is V = \((1.0 cm)^{2}(1.0 μm) = 1.0 \times 10^{−4} cm^{3}\), and (using the density of iron of 7.9 \(g/cm^{3}\)) its mass is \( m = \rho V = (7.9 g/cm^{3})(1.0 \times 10^{-4} cm^{3}) = 7.9 \times 10^{−4} g\). The number of atoms (or nuclei) is then

\( N = \frac{mN_{A}}{M} =\frac{(7.9 \times 10^{-4} g) (6.02 \times 10^{23} atoms/mole)}{56 g/mole} \)
\(= 8.5 \times 10^{18} atoms\)

Next we need to find the number of particles per second in the incident beam. We are given that the current is \( 3.0 \times 10^{-6} A = 3.0 \times 10^{-6} C/s\), and with each proton having a charge of \(1.6 \times 10^{-19} C\), the beam intensity is

\( I_{0} = \frac{3.0 \times 10^{-6} C/s}{1.6 \times 10^{-19} C/particle} = 1.9 \times 10^{13} particles/s \)

From Eq. 13.1 we can now find R:

\(R = \frac{N \sigma I_{0}}{S}\)
\(= (8.5 \times 10^{18} nuclei)(0.40 \times 10^{-24} cm^{2}/nucleus) \times (1.9 \times 10^{13} particles/s) (1 cm^{2})^{-1}\)

\(= 6.5 \times 10^{7} particles/s\)

About \(10^{8}\) neutrons per second are emitted from the target.
