Guest Question.

MOLECULAR KINETIC ENERGY AND \(v_{rms}\)

(a) What is the average translational kinetic energy of an ideal-gas molecule at 27°C? (b) What is the total random translational kinetic energy of the molecules in 1 mole of this gas? (c) What is the rms speed of oxygen molecules at this temperature?

Admin
Solution

IDENTIFY and SET UP:

This problem involves the translational kinetic energy of an ideal gas on a per-molecule and per-mole basis, as well as the root-mean-square molecular speed \(v_{rms}\). We are given T = 27°C = 300 K and n = 1 mol; we use the molecular mass m for oxygen. We use Eq. (18.16) to determine the average kinetic energy of a molecule, Eq. (18.14) to find the total molecular kinetic energy \(K_{tr}\) of 1 mole, and Eq. (18.19) to find \(v_{rms}\).

\(K_{\mathrm{tr}}=\frac{3}{2} n R T\) (18.14)

\(\frac{1}{2} m\left(v^{2}\right)_{\mathrm{av}}=\frac{3}{2} k T\) (18.16)

EXECUTE:

(a) From Eq. (18.16),

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{1}{2} m\left(v^{2}\right)_{\mathrm{av}}=\frac{3}{2} k T &=\frac{3}{2}\left(1.38 \times 10^{-23} \mathrm{~J} / \mathrm{K}\right)(300 \mathrm{~K}) \\&=6.21\times10^{-21} \mathrm{~J}\end{aligned}\)

(b) From Eq. (18.14), the kinetic energy of one mole is

\(K_{\mathrm{tr}}=\frac{3}{2} n R T=\frac{3}{2}(1 \mathrm{~mol})(8.314 \mathrm{~J} / \mathrm{mol} \cdot \mathrm{K})(300 \mathrm{~K})=3740 \mathrm{~J}\)

(c) We found the mass per molecule m and molar mass M of molecular oxygen in . Using Eq. (18.19) (\(v_{\mathrm{rms}}=\sqrt{\left(v^{2}\right)_{\mathrm{av}}}=\sqrt{\frac{3 k T}{m}}=\sqrt{\frac{3 R T}{M}}\) in two ways:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{\mathrm{rms}} &=\sqrt{\frac{3 k T}{m}}=\sqrt{\frac{3\left(1.38 \times10^{-23} \mathrm{~J} / \mathrm{K}\right)(300 \mathrm{~K})}{5.31 \times 10^{-26}\mathrm{~kg}}} \\&=484 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}=1740 \mathrm{~km} / \mathrm{h}=1080\mathrm{mi} / \mathrm{h} \\v_{\mathrm{rms}} &=\sqrt{\frac{3 R T}{M}}=\sqrt{\frac{3(8.314 \mathrm{~J} / \mathrm{mol} \cdot \mathrm{K})(300 \mathrm{~K})}{32.0\times 10^{-3} \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{mol}}}=484 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\end{aligned}\)

 

EVALUATE: The answer in part (a) does not depend on the mass of the molecule. We can check our result in part (b) by noting that the translational kinetic energy per mole must be equal to the product of the average translational kinetic energy per molecule from part (a) and Avogadro’s number \(N_{\mathrm{A}}: K_{\mathrm{tr}}=\left(6.21 \times 10^{-21} \mathrm{~J} / \text { molecule }\right) \times\)\(\left(6.022 \times 10^{23} \text { molecules }\right)=3740 \mathrm{~J}\).
