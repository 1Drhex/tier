Guest Question.

Two single-phase transformers I and II rated at 250 kVA, each are connected in parallel on both sides. Resistance and reactance drops for I and II are 1% and 6% and 1.2% and 4.8%, respectively. Calculate the load shared by each and the respective power factors, if the total load is 500 kVA at 0.8 power factor lagging. Their no-load emf’s are equal.

Admin
Solution
\(\overline{Z_{1}} = \left(1 + j6\right)\%;= 6.083\angle 80.54^{\circ } \)

 

\(\overline{Z_{2}} = \left(1.2 + j4.8\right)\%= 4.948\angle 75.96^{\circ } \)

 

\(\overline{Z_{1}} + \overline{Z_{2}} = \left(1 + j6\right)\% + \left(1.2 + j4.8\right)\% = \left(2.2 + j10.8\right)\% = 11.08\angle 78.48^{\circ } \)

 

\(kVA = 500 \angle \cos ^{-1}0.8 = 500\angle -36.87^{\circ } \)

 

\(\therefore kVA_{1} = \frac{\overline{Z_{2}}}{\overline{Z_{1}} + \overline{Z_{2}}} \overline{kVA} = \frac{4.948\angle 75.96^{\circ }}{11.08\angle 78.48^{\circ }} \times 500\angle -36.87^{\circ } = 223.3\angle -39.39^{\circ }\)

 

\( kVA_{1} = \mathbf{223.3 kVA} \)

 

Power factor, \(\cos \phi _{1} = \cos \left(–39.39^{\circ }\right) = \mathbf{0.773 lag} \)

\(\overline{kVA_{2}} = \frac{\overline{Z_{1}}}{\overline{Z_{1}} + \overline{Z_{2}}} \times kVA = \frac{6.083\angle 80.54^{\circ }}{11.08\angle 78.48^{\circ }} \times 500\angle -36.87^{\circ } = 274.5\angle -34.81^{\circ }\)

 

\( kVA_{2} = \mathbf{274.5 kVA} \)

 

Power factor, \(\cos \phi _{2} = \cos \left(–34.81^{\circ }\right) = \mathbf{0.821 lag} \)
