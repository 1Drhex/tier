Guest Question.

At a point in a stressed body, the known stresses are \(\sigma_{x}=0, \sigma_{y}=0, \sigma_{z}=0\), \(\tau_{x y}\) = +6 ksi, \(\tau_{y z}\) = +10 ksi, and \(\tau_{z x}\) = +8 ksi. Determine:
(a) the normal and shear stresses on a plane whose outward normal makes equal angles with the x, y, and z axes.
(b) the principal stresses and the absolute maximum shear stress at the point.

Admin
Solution

The known stresses are

\(\begin{array}{lll}\sigma_{x}=0 ksi & \sigma_{y}=0 ksi & \sigma_{ z }=0 ksi \\\tau_{x y}=6 ksi & \tau_{y z}=10 ksi & \tau_{z x}=8 ksi\end{array}\)

(a) The plane of interest is defined by its direction cosines, and in this case, all three angles are the same.
Therefore, l = m = n. The direction cosines are related by the identity:

\(l^{2}+m^{2}+n^{2}=1\)

Since l = m = n in this instance,

\(l^{2}+m^{2}+n^{2}=3 l^{2}=1 \quad \therefore l=m=n=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}=0.5774\)

The three orthogonal components of the resultant stress are:

\(\begin{aligned}&S_{x}=\sigma_{x} \cdot l+\tau_{x y} \cdot m+\tau_{z x} \cdot n=(0)(0.5774)+(6)(0.5774)+(8)(0.5774)=8.0836 ksi \\&S_{y}=\tau_{x y} \cdot l+\sigma_{y} \cdot m+\tau_{y z} \cdot n=(6)(0.5774)+(0)(0.5774)+(10)(0.5774)=9.2384 ksi \\&S_{z}=\tau_{z x} \cdot l+\tau_{y z} \cdot m+\sigma_{z} \cdot n=(8)(0.5774)+(10)(0.5774)+(0)(0.5774)=10.3932 ksi\end{aligned}\)

The normal component \(\sigma_{n}\) of the resultant stress is

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{n} &=S_{x} \cdot l+S_{y} \cdot m+S_{z} \cdot n \\&=(8.0836)(0.5774)+(9.2384)(0.5774)+(10.3932)(0.5774) \\&=16.0028 ksi =16.00 ksi ( T )\end{aligned}\)

The shear stress \(\tau_{n t}\) on the oblique plane can be obtained from the relation \(S^{2}=\sigma_{n}^{2}+\tau_{n t}^{2}\) .

\(S^{2}=S_{x}^{2}+S_{y}^{2}+S_{z}^{2}=(8.0836)^{2}+(9.2384)^{2}+(10.3932)^{2}=258.7112\)

and thus;

\(\tau_{n t}=\sqrt{\left(S^{2}-\sigma_{n}^{2}\right)}=\sqrt{258.7112-(16.0028)^{2}}=1.620 ksi\)

(b) The principal stresses can be obtained from the roots of the cubic equation [Eq. (12.27)]

\(\sigma_{p}^{3}-I_{1} \sigma_{p}^{2}+I_{2} \sigma_{p}-I_{3}=0\)

The three invariants have values of

\(\begin{aligned}I_{1} &=\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}+\sigma_{z} \\&=(0)+(0)+(0)=0 \\I_{2} &=\sigma_{x} \sigma_{y}+\sigma_{y} \sigma_{z}+\sigma_{z} \sigma_{x}-\tau_{x y}^{2}-\tau_{y z}^{2}-\tau_{z x}^{2} \\&=(0)(0)+(0)(0)+(0)(0)-(6)^{2}-(10)^{2}-(8)^{2}=-200 \\I_{3} &=\sigma_{x} \sigma_{y} \sigma_{z}+2 \tau_{x y} \tau_{y z} \tau_{z x}-\left(\sigma_{x} \tau_{y z}^{2}+\sigma_{y} \tau_{z x}^{2}+\sigma_{z} \tau_{x y}^{2}\right) \\&=(0)(0)(0)+2(6)(10)(8)-\left[(0)(10)^{2}+(0)(8)^{2}+(0)(6)^{2}\right]=960\end{aligned}\)

therefore, Eq. (12.27) is

\(\sigma_{p}^{3}-(0) \sigma_{p}^{2}+(-200) \sigma_{p}-(960)=\sigma_{p}^{3}-200 \sigma_{p}-960=0\)

The three roots of this cubic equation are the principal stresses:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1} &=16.11 ksi ( T ) \\\sigma_{p 2} &=5.75 ksi ( C ) \\\sigma_{p 3} &=10.36 ksi ( C )\end{aligned}\)

The absolute maximum shear stress at the point is found from

\(\tau_{ abs \max }=\frac{\sigma_{\max }-\sigma_{\min }}{2}=\frac{16.1116-(-10.3605)}{2}=13.24 ksi\)
