Guest Question.

A gas turbine unit receives air at 1 bar, 300 K and compresses it adiabatically to 6.2 bar. The compressor efficiency is 88%. The fuel has a heating value of 44186 kJ/kg and the fuel-air ratio is 0.017 kg fuel/kg of air. The turbine internal efficiency is 90%. Calculate the work of turbine and compressor per kg of air compressed and thermal efficiency. For products of combustion \(\textbf{c}_{p}\) = 1.147 kJ/ kg K, \(\pmb{\gamma}\) = 1.33. [U.P.S.C. 1992]

Admin
Solution
\(\text{Gas turbine cycle is shown by 1–2–3–4 on T-S diagram,}\\\text{Given:}\\P_{1} = 1 bar, P_{2} = P_{3} = 6.2 bar, F/A ratio = 0.017\\T_{1} = 300 K, η_{compr.} = 88\%, η_{turb} = 90\%\\\text{Heating value of fuel = 44186 kJ/kg}\\\text{For process 1–2 being isentropic,}\\\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad \qquad\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}&= \left\lgroup\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{\gamma}{\gamma – 1} }\\T_{2}&= 505.26 K\end{aligned}\\\text{Considering compressor efficiency, }η_{compr} = \frac{T_{2} – T_{1}}{T_{2^{\prime}} – T_{1}} , 0.88 = \frac{(505.26 – 300)}{(T_{2^{\prime}} – 300)}\\\text{Actual temperature after compression, }T^{\prime}_{2} = 533.25 K\\\text{During process 2–3 due to combustion of unit mass of air compressed the energy balance shall be as under,}\\\text{Heat added = }m_{f} × \text{Heating value}\\\begin{aligned}&= ((m_{a} + m_{f}) · c_{\text{p, comb}} · T_{3}) – (m_{a} · c_{\text{p, air}} · T_{2^{\prime}})\\\text{or}\qquad \left\lgroup\frac{m_{f}}{m_{a}} \right\rgroup × 44186&= \left\lgroup\left\lgroup1 + \frac{m_{f}}{m_{a}} \right\rgroup.c_{\text{p , comb}} . T_{3} \right\rgroup – (c_{\text{p, air}} × 533.25)\\\text{Here,} \qquad \qquad\qquad \frac{m_{f}}{m_{a}}&= 0.017, c_{\text{p, comb}} = 1.147 kJ/kg · K, c_{\text{p, air}} = 1.005 kJ/kg · K\\\text{Upon substitution} \qquad &\\(0.017 × 44186)&= ((1 + 0.017) × 1.147 × T_{3}) – (1.005 × 533.25)\\T_{3}&= 1103.37 K\\\text{For expansion 3–4 bein}&\text{g}\\\frac{T_{4}}{T_{3}}&= \left\lgroup\frac{P_{4}}{P_{3}} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{n – 1}{n} }\\T_{4}&= 1103.37 × \left\lgroup\frac{1}{6.2} \right\rgroup ^{\frac{0.33}{1.33} }\\T_{4} & = 701.64 K\\\text{Actual temperature at }&\text{turbine inlet considering internal efficiency of turbine,}\\η_{turb}&= \frac{T_{3} – T_{4^{\prime}}}{T_{3} – T_{4}} ; 0.90 = \frac{(1103.37 – T_{4^{\prime}})}{(1103.37 – 701.64)}\\T_{4^{\prime}}&= 741.81 K\\\text{Compressor work, per}&\text{ kg of air compressed = } c_{\text{p, air}} · (T_{2^{\prime}} – T_{1})\\W_{C}&= 1.005 × (533.25 – 300)\\W_{C}&= 234.42 \text{ kJ/kg or air}\\\text{Compressor work}&= \textbf{234.42 kJ/kg of air}\\\text{Turbine work, per kg}&\text{ of air compressed = }c_{\text{p, comb}} · (T_{3} – T_{4^{\prime}})\\&= 1.147 × (1103.37 – 741.81)\\\text{Turbine work}&= \textbf{414.71 kJ/kg of air}\\W_{T}&= \text{414.71, kJ/kg of air}\\\text{Net work}&= W_{T} – W_{C} = (414.71 – 234.42)\\W_{net}&= \text{180.29, kJ/kg of air}\\\text{Heat supplied}&= \text{0.017 × 44186 = 751.162 kJ/kg of air.}\\\text{Thermal efficiency}&= \frac{W_{net}}{\text{Heat supplied}} = \frac{180.29}{751.162} = 24\%\\\text{Thermal efficiency}&= \textbf{24}\pmb{\%}\end{aligned}\)
