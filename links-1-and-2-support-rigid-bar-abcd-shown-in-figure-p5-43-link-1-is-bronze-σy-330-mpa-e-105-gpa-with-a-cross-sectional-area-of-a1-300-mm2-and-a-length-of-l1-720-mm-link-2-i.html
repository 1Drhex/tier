Guest Question.

Links (1) and (2) support rigid bar ABCD shown in Figure P5.43. Link (1) is bronze [\(\sigma_{Y}\) = 330 MPa; E = 105 GPa] with a cross-sectional area of \(A_{1}=300 mm ^{2}\) and a length of \(L_{1}\) = 720 mm. Link (2) is cold-rolled steel [\(\sigma_{Y}\) = 430 MPa; E = 210 GPa] with a cross-sectional area of \(A_{2}=200 mm ^{2}\) and a length of \(L_{2}\) = 940 mm. A factor of safety of 2.5 with respect to yield is specified for the normal stresses in links (1) and (2). Furthermore, the maximum horizontal displacement of the rigid bar at end D may not exceed 2.0 mm. Calculate the magnitude of the maximum load P that can be applied to the rigid bar at D. Use a = 420 mm, b = 420 mm, and c = 510 mm.

Admin
Solution

Equilibrium
Consider a FBD of the rigid bar. Assume tension forces in members (1) and (2). A moment equation about pin A gives the best information for this situation:

\(\Sigma M_{A}=F_{1}(a+b)-F_{2} a-P(a+b+c)=0\) (a)

Geometry-of-deformations relationship
Draw a deformation diagram of the rigid bar. The deflections of the rigid bar are related by similar triangles:

\(\frac{v_{B}}{a}=\frac{v_{C}}{a+b}=\frac{v_{D}}{a+b+c}\) (b)

There are no gaps, clearances, or other misfits at pins B and C; therefore, Eq. (b) can be rewritten in terms of the member deformations as:

\(\frac{\delta_{1}}{a+b}=\frac{-\delta_{2}}{a}\) (c)

Note: To understand the negative sign associated with \(\delta_{2}\), see Section 5.5 for discussion of statically indeterminate rigid bar configurations with opposing members.

Force-Deformation Relationships

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}} \quad \delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}\) (d)

Compatibility Equation

\(\frac{1}{a+b} \frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}=-\frac{1}{a} \frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}\) (e)

Allowable Stresses
The allowable stress magnitudes for bars (1) and (2) can be calculated from the yield stresses and the factor of safety:

\(\sigma_{\text {allow }, 1}=\frac{330 MPa }{2.5}=132 MPa \quad \sigma_{\text {allow }, 2}=\frac{430 MPa }{2.5}=172 MPa\)

Solve for stresses in links (1) and (2)
Since allowable stresses are specified, it is convenient to express the compatibility equation (e) in terms of stresses

\(\frac{1}{a+b} \frac{\sigma_{1} L_{1}}{E_{1}}=-\frac{1}{a} \frac{\sigma_{2} L_{2}}{E_{2}}\) (f)

Solve for \(\sigma_{1}\):

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{1} &=-\left(\frac{a+b}{a}\right)\left(\frac{L_{2}}{L_{1}}\right)\left(\frac{E_{1}}{E_{2}}\right) \sigma_{2} \\&=-\left(\frac{420 mm +420 mm }{420 mm }\right)\left(\frac{940 mm }{720 mm }\right)\left(\frac{105 GPa }{210 GPa }\right) \sigma_{2} \\&=-1.305556 \sigma_{2}\end{aligned}\)

Substitute the allowable stress for bar (2) and calculate the corresponding stress in bar (1):

\(\sigma_{1}=-1.305556(172 MPa )=-224.556 MPa\) N.G.

This result demonstrates that it is the stress in bar (1) that actually controls here. Revise the calculation to find the stress magnitudes in bars (1) and (2):

\(\sigma_{2}=-\frac{132 MPa }{1.305556}=-101.106 MPa\)

Deformation of link (1)
We’ve established the stresses that are acceptable; however, we need to check whether or not end D of the rigid bar will deflect more than 2 mm. Begin by calculating the deformation of link (1):

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}=\frac{\sigma_{1} L_{1}}{E_{1}}=\frac{(132 MPa )(720 mm )}{105,000 MPa }=0.905143 mm\)

The deflection of the rigid bar at C is equal to the deformation of link (1). Knowing this, the deflection of the rigid bar at D can be found from:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{D} &=\frac{a+b+c}{a+b} v_{C} \\&=\frac{(420 mm +420 mm +510 mm )}{(420 mm +420 mm )}(0.905143 mm ) \\&=1.454694 mm \leq 2.0 mm \quad OK\end{aligned}\)

From this result, we can conclude that deflections at D are acceptable for the link normal stresses previously determined.

Link Forces and Allowable Load P
Based on the stresses calculated previously, the forces in each link are:

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{1}=\sigma_{1} A_{1}=\left(132 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(300 mm ^{2}\right)=39,600 N \\&F_{2}=\sigma_{2} A_{2}=\left(-101.106 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(200 mm ^{2}\right)=-20,221.277 N\end{aligned}\)

The allowable load P can now be calculated from the equilibrium equation (a):

\(\begin{aligned}P &=\frac{F_{1}(a+b)-F_{2} a}{(a+b+c)} \\&=\frac{(39,600 N )(420 mm +420 mm )-(-20,221.277 N )(420 mm )}{(420 mm +420 mm +510 mm )} \\&=30,931.064 N =30.9 kN\end{aligned}\)
