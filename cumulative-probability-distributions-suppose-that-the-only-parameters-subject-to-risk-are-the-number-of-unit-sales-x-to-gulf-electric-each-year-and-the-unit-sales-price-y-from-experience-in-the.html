Solution

Given: Discrete probability distributions for \(X\) and Y.
Find: Cumulative distributions for \(X\) and Y.

Consider the demand probability distribution (\(X\)) given in Table 12.5 for BMCâ€™s transmission-housings project:

Unit Demand (\(X\)) Probability P(\(X\) = x)

If we want to know the probability that demand will be less than or equal to any particular value, we can use the following cumulative probability function:

 

 

 

\(F\left(x\right) = P\left(X\leq x\right) =\)

 \(0 x \lt 1,600\)
\(0.2 1,600\leq x\lt 2,000\)
\(0.8 2,000\leq x\lt 2,400\)
\(1.0 2,400\leq x\)

For example, if we want to know the probability that the demand will be less than or equal to 2,000, we can examine a single value, namely x = 2,100, and we will find that the probability is 80%.
