Guest Question.

A cryogenic fluid flows through a long tube of 20 mm diameter, the outer surface of which is diffuse and gray (\(ε_1 = 0.02\)) at 77 K. This tube is concentric with a larger tube of 50 mm diameter, the inner surface of which is diffuse and gray (\(ε_2 = 0.05\)) and at 300 K. The space between the surfaces is evacuated. Calculate the heat gain by cryogenic fluid per unit length of tubes. If a thin radiation shield of 35 mm diameter (\(ε_3 = 0.02\)) both sides is inserted midway between the inner and outer surfaces, calculate the percentage change in heat gain per unit length of the tube.

 

Admin
Solution

Given : Concentric tube arrangement with diffuse and gray surfaces.
To find :
(i) Heat gain by cryogenic fluid passing through the inner tube.
(ii) Percentage change in heat gain with radiation shield inserted midway between inner and outer pipe.
Assumptions :
1. Space between tubes is evacuated.
2. No conduction and convection involve.
3. Infinite long concentric tubes.
Analysis : For long concentric cylinders
\(F_{1–2} =F_{1–3} = F_{3–2} = 1\)
(i) The radiation exchange without radiation
shield can be calculated as
\(Q = \frac{A_1 σ (T_1^4 – T_2^4)}{\frac{1}{ε_1} +\left ( \frac{1}{ε_{2}} – 1 \right )\frac{A_1}{A_2}}\)

where \(\frac{A_1}{A_2}=\left ( \frac{D_1}{D_2} \right )=\left ( \frac{0.02}{0.05} \right )=0.4\)

\(Q = \frac{π ×0.02 ×1 ×5.67 ×10^{-8} ×(77^4 – 300^4)}{\frac{1}{0.02} +\left ( \frac{1}{0.05} – 1 \right )×0.4}\)

= – 50 W/m.
(ii) When radiation shield is placed in midway between two tubes,
\(Q_2 = \frac{E_{b1} – E_{b2} }{\sum R_{th}}\)
where \(ΣR_{th }= \frac{1 – ε_1 }{ε_1A_1} +\frac{1}{A_1F_{1 – 3}}+\frac{2(1 – ε_1 )}{ε_3A_{3}} +\frac{1}{A_3F_{3 – 2}}+\frac{1 – ε_2 }{ε_2A_2}\)
For 1 m length of tube
\(ΣR_{th} = \frac{1 – 0.02}{0.02 × \pi ×0.02}+\frac{1}{0.02 × \pi ×1}+\frac{2 ×(1 – 0.02)}{0.02 × \pi ×0.035} +\frac{1 }{ \pi × 0.035 ×1} \frac{1 -0.05 }{ 0.05 × \pi ×0.05}\)
\(ΣR_{th} = 779.9 + 15.9 + 891.3 + 121.0 = 1817 m^{–2}\)
The heat gain with radiation shield
\(Q_2 =\frac{ π×0.02 ×1 ×5.67 ×10^{-8} × (77^4 – 300^4)}{1817}\)

= – 25 W/m
The percentage reduction in heat gain
\( = \frac{(Q_1 – Q_2 )}{Q_1}=\frac{(-50 +25)}{-50} = 50%.\)
