Guest Question.

A force F of magnitude 840 N acts at a point as shown in Fig. 5-14. Determine
(a) The moment of the force about point B.
(b) The direction angles associated with the unit vector e along the axis of the moment.
(c) The perpendicular distance d from point B to the line of action of the force.

Admin
Solution

Scalar Analysis

(a) \(d_{OA}= \sqrt{ (x_A)^2+(y_A)^2+(z_A)^2 } \)

 

\(= \sqrt{(200)^2+(275)^2+(400)^2 } =525mm\)

 

\(F_x=\frac{200}{525}F=\frac{200}{525}(840)=320N \)

 

\(F_y=\frac{275}{525}F=\frac{275}{525}(840)=440N \)

 

\(F_z=\frac{400}{525}F=\frac{400}{525}(840)=640N \)

At point B (positive moments counterclockwise when viewing in the negative coordinate direction):

\(M_{Bx}=-F_yz_B-F_zy_B=-440(0.150)-640(0.250)=-226N.m\)

 

\(M_{By}=+F_zx_B+F_xz_B=+640(0.375)+320(0.150)=+288N.m\)

 

\(M_{Bz}=+F_xy_B-F_yx_B=+320(0.250)-440(0.375)=-85.0N.m\)

 

\(M_B=(-226i+288j-85.0k)N.m\)

Vector Analysis

The force F and the position vector r from point B to point A can be written in Cartesian vector form a

\(F=840[(200/525)i+(275/525)j+(440/525)k]\)

 

\(=(320i+440j+640k)N\)

 

\(r_{A/B}=(-0.175i+0.025j+0.550k)m\)

The moment \(M_B\) is given by \(M_O=r\times F=\left|\begin{matrix} i & j & k \\ r_x & r_y & r_z \\F_x & F_y & F_z \end{matrix} \right| =(r_yF_z-r_zF_y)i+(r_zF_x-r_xF_z)j+(r_xF_y-r_yF_x)k=M_{Ox}i+M_{Oy}j+M_{Oz}k\) as

\(M_B=r_{A/B}\times F=\left|\begin{matrix} i & j & k \\ r_x & r_y & r_z \\F_x & F_y & F_z \end{matrix} \right| =\left|\begin{matrix} i & j & k \\ -0.175 & 0.025 & 0.550 \\320 & 440 & 640 \end{matrix} \right|\)

 

\(=(-226i+288j-85.0k)N.m\)

Alternatively, the position vector r can be written from point B to point O as

\(r_{O/B}=(-0.375i-0.250j+0.150k)m\)

The moment \(M_B\) is then given by \(M_O=r\times F=\left|\begin{matrix} i & j & k \\ r_x & r_y & r_z \\F_x & F_y & F_z \end{matrix} \right| =(r_yF_z-r_zF_y)i+(r_zF_x-r_xF_z)j+(r_xF_y-r_yF_x)k=M_{Ox}i+M_{Oy}j+M_{Oz}k\) as

\(M_B=r_{O/B}\times F=\left|\begin{matrix} i & j & k \\ r_x & r_y & r_z \\F_x & F_y & F_z \end{matrix} \right| =\left|\begin{matrix} i & j & k \\ -0.375 & -0.250 & 0.150 \\320 & 440 & 640 \end{matrix} \right|\)

 

\(=(-226i+288j-85.0k)N.m\)

(b) The magnitude of moment \(M_B\) is obtained by using \(\left|M_O\right| =\sqrt{M_{Ox}2+M_{Oy}^2+M_{Oz}^2} \). Thus

\(\left|M_B\right| =\sqrt{(-226)^2+(288)^2+(-85.0)^2}=375.8N.m \)

The direction angles are obtained by using \(cos\theta _x=\frac{M_{Ox}}{\left|M_O\right| } \quad\quad cos\theta _y=\frac{M_{Oy}}{\left|M_O\right| } \quad\quad cos\theta _z=\frac{M_{Oz}}{\left|M_O\right| } \). Thus

\(\theta _x=cos^{-1}\frac{M_x}{\left|M_B\right| }=cos^{-1}\frac{-226}{375.8}=127.0^\circ \)

 

\(\theta _y=cos^{-1}\frac{M_y}{\left|M_B\right| }=cos^{-1}\frac{288}{375.8}=40.0^\circ \)

 

\(\theta _z=cos^{-1}\frac{M_z}{\left|M_B\right| }=cos^{-1}\frac{-85.0}{375.8}=103.1^\circ \)

(c) The distance d is obtained by using the definition of a moment. Thus

\(d=\frac{M}{F}=\frac{375.8}{840}=0.447m=447mm \)
