Guest Question.

Develop the interaction diagram for a shear wall that has the following properties: \(d_{v} = 24 ft 8 in. = 296 in.\), height \(= h = 14 ft\) \(0 in., tsp = 7.625 in\)., #5 bars at \(48 in\). centers \({f}'_{m}= 1500 psi\), \(f_{y}= 60 ksi\)

Admin
Solution

This wall will have seven bars, equally spaced, with the first bar at \(4 in.\) from each end. Hence, according to the notation in Figure 20.5,

\(d_{1}=d_{v}-4 in. = 296 in. – 4 in. = 292 in.\)

 

\(d_{2}=d_{1}-48 in. = 292 in. – 48 in. = 244 in.\)

 

\(d_{3}== 196 in., d_{4}= 148 in., d_{5}= 100 in.,d_{6}= 52 in., d_{7}= 4 in.\)

 

Assume \(c = 146 in.\)

Then \(a = 0.8(146 in.) = 116.8 in.\)

\(C = 0.64{f}'_{m} ct_{sp}= 0.64(1500 psi) (146 in.) (7.625 in.)\)

 

\(= 1,068,720 lb = 1069k\)

 

\(\frac{\epsilon _{t}}{d_{i}-c}=\frac{\epsilon _{mu}}{c}. \epsilon _{1}=\frac{(d_{1}-c)\epsilon _{mu}}{c}=\frac{(292- 146) (0.0025)}{146}= 0.0025 >\epsilon _{y}\)

 

\(\therefore f_{s2}=\epsilon _{2}E_{s}= (0.001678) (29,000 ksi) = 48.66 ksi\)

 

\(\epsilon _{3}=\frac{(d_{3}-c)\epsilon _{mu}}{c}=\frac{(196 – 146) (0.0025)}{146}= 0.00086 <\epsilon _{y}\)

 

\(\therefore f_{s2}=\epsilon _{4}E_{s}= (0.000034) (29,000 ksi) = 0.99 ksi\)

 

The remaining bars are in compression, and therefore their contribution to the forces in the section must be neglected. The tension force in each bar is \(T_{i}=A_{si}f_{si}\) Since all of the bars arethe same size (they usually are in a shear wall), \(A_{si}\) is constant for all bars, and the equations are simplified as follows:

\(P_{n}= C-\sum T_{i}= 0.64{f}'_{m}ct_{sp}-A_{si}\sum f_{si}\)

 

\(= (0.64) (1.500 ksi) (146 in.) (7.625 in.) – (0.31 in.^{2})(60 k + 48.66 k + 24.83k + 0.99k)\)

 

\(= 1027k\)

 

\(\phi P_{n}= 0.9(1027 k) = 924 k\)

 

\(M_{n}= C\frac{d_{y}-a}{2}\sum T_{i}(d_{i}-\frac{d_{y}}{2})\)

 

\(= (1069 k)\frac{296 in. – 116.8 in.}{2}\)

 

\(= (1069 k)\frac{296 in. – 116.8 in.}{2} +(0.31)[(60 ksi)(292 in.-\frac{296}{2}in.)+ (48.66 ksi)(60 ksi)(244 in-\frac{296}{2}in.)+ (24.83 ksi)(196 in.-\frac{296}{2}in.)+ (0.99 ksi)(148 in.-\frac{296}{2}in.)]\)

 

\(= 100,253 in-k = 8354 ft-k\)

 

\(\phi M_{n}= 0.9(8354 ft-k) = 7519 ft-k\)

 

The coordinates of one point of the interaction diagram \(\phi P_{n},\phi M_{n}\) are \((924 k, 7521 ft-k)\). This single point is shown in Table 20.3, along with numerous other points. The points are calculated from assumed values of \(c/d_{1}\) that give a wide range of values for this parameter. Also included is the axial load associated with ACI 530 . This is the horizontal line shown at a value of \(1232 k\). This value is the only one that depends on the slenderness reduction factor.
