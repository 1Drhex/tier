Guest Question.

Two moist air streams with 85% relative humidity, both flowing at a rate of 0.1 kg/s of dry air are mixed in a steady setup. One inlet flowstream is at 32.5°C and the other at 16°C. Find the exit relative humidity.

Admin
Solution

CV mixing chamber.

Continuity Eq. water: \(\dot{ m }_{\text {air }} w _{1}+\dot{ m }_{\text {air }} w _{2}=2 \dot{ m }_{\text {air }} w _{ ex }\);

Energy Eq.: \(\dot{ m }_{\text {air }} \tilde{ h }_{1}+\dot{ m }_{\text {air }} \tilde{ h }_{2}=2 \dot{ m }_{\text {air }} \tilde{ h }_{ ex }\)

Properties from the tables and formulas

\(\begin{aligned}& P _{ g 32.5}=4.937 kPa ; P _{ v 1}=0.85 \times 4.937 kPa =4.196 kPa \\& w _{1}=0.622 \times 4.196 /(100-4.196)=0.0272 \\& P _{ g 16}=1.831 kPa ; P _{ v 2}=0.85 \times 1.831 kPa =1.556 kPa \\& w _{2}=0.622 \times 1.556 /(100-1.556)=0.00983\end{aligned}\)

Continuity Eq. water: \(w _{ ex }=\left( w _{1}+ w _{2}\right) / 2=0.0185\);

For the energy equation we have \(\tilde{ h }= h _{ a }+ wh _{ v }\) so:

\(2 \tilde{ h }_{ ex }-\tilde{ h }_{1}-\tilde{ h }_{2}=0=2 h _{ a ex }- h _{ a 1}- h _{ a 2}+2 w _{ ex } h _{ v ex }- w _{1} h _{ v 1}- wh _{ v 2}\)

we will use constant specific heat to avoid an iteration on \(T _{ ex }\).

\(C _{ p \text { air }}\left(2 T _{ ex }- T _{1}- T _{2}\right)+ C _{ p H 2 O }\left(2 w _{ ex } T _{ ex }- w _{1} T _{1}- w _{2} T _{2}\right)=0\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}T _{ ex } &=\left[ C _{ p \text { air }}\left( T _{1}+ T _{2}\right)+ C _{ p H 2 O }\left( w _{1} T _{1}+ w _{2} T _{2}\right)\right] /\left[2 C _{ p \text { air }}+2 w _{ ex } C _{ p H 2 O }\right] \\&=[1.004(32.5+16)+1.872(0.0272 \times 32.5+0.00983 \times 16] / 2.0773\\&=24.4^{\circ} C\end{aligned}\)

 

\(P _{ v ex }=\frac{ w _{ ex }}{0.622+ w _{ ex }} P _{ tot }=\frac{0.0185}{0.622+0.0185} 100=2.888 kPa\),

 

\(P _{ g ex }=3.069 kPa \quad \Rightarrow \quad \phi=2.888 / 3.069= 0 . 9 4 \text { or } 94 \%\)

 

Properties taken from the psychrometric chart

State 1: \(w _{1}=0.0266, \quad \tilde{ h }_{1}=120\) State 2: \(w _{2}=0.0094, \quad \tilde{ h }_{2}=60\)

Continuity Eq. water: \(w _{ ex }=\left( w _{1}+ w _{2}\right) / 2=0.018\);

Energy Eq.: \(\tilde{ h }_{ ex }=\left(\tilde{ h }_{1}+\tilde{ h }_{2}\right) / 2=90 kJ / kg \text { dry air }\)

exit: \(w_{e x}, \tilde{h}_{e x} \Rightarrow T_{e x}=24.5^{\circ} C , \quad \phi=94 \%\)

Notice how the energy in terms of temperature is close to the average of the two flows but the relative humidity is not.
