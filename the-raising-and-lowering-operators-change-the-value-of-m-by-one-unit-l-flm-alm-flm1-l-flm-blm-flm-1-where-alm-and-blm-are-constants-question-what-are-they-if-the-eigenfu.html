Guest Question.

The raising and lowering operators change the value of m by one unit:

\( L_{+} f_{\ell}^{m}=\left(A_{\ell}^{m}\right) f_{\ell}^{m+1}, \quad L_{-} f_{\ell}^{m}=\left(B_{\ell}^{m}\right) f_{\ell}^{m-1} \). (4.120).

where \( A_{\ell}^{m} \text { and } B_{\ell}^{m} \) are constants. Question: What are they, if the eigenfunctions are to be normalized? Hint: First show that \( L_{\mp} \) is the hermitian conjugate of \( L_{\mp} \) (since \( L_{x} \text { and } L_{y} \) are observables, you may assume they are hermitian…but prove it if you like); then use Equation 4.112. Answer:

\( A_{\ell}^{m}=\hbar \sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)-m(m+1)}=\hbar \sqrt{(\ell-m)(\ell+m+1)}\),

\( B_{\ell}^{m}=\hbar \sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)-m(m-1)}=\hbar \sqrt{(\ell+m)(\ell-m+1)} \). (4.121).

\( L^{2}=L_{\pm} L_{\mp}+L_{z}^{2} \mp \hbar L_{z} \) (4.112).

Note what happens at the top and bottom of the ladder (i.e. when you apply \(L_+ \) to \( f_{\ell}^{\ell} \text { or } L_{-} \text {to } f_{\ell}^{-\ell} \).

Admin
Solution

\( \left\langle f \mid L_{\pm} g\right\rangle=\left\langle f \mid L_{x} g\right\rangle \pm i\left\langle f \mid L_{y} g\right\rangle=\left\langle L_{x} f \mid g\right\rangle \pm i\left\langle L_{y} f \mid g\right\rangle=\left\langle\left(L_{x} \mp i L_{y}\right) f \mid g\right\rangle=\left\langle L_{\mp} f \mid g\right\rangle, \text { so }\left(L_{\pm}\right)^{\dagger}=L_{\mp} \).

Now, using Eq. 4.112, in the form \( L_{\mp} L_{\pm}=L^{2}-L_{z}^{2} \mp \hbar L_{z} \).

\( L^{2}=L_{\pm} L_{\mp}+L_{z}^{2} \mp \hbar L_{z} \) (4.112).

\( \left\langle f_{\ell}^{m} \mid L_{\mp} L_{\pm} f_{\ell}^{m}\right\rangle=\left\langle f_{\ell}^{m} \mid\left(L^{2}-L_{z}^{2} \mp \hbar L_{z}\right) f_{\ell}^{m}\right\rangle=\left\langle f_{\ell}^{m} \mid\left[\hbar^{2} \ell(\ell+1)-\hbar^{2} m^{2} \mp \hbar^{2} m\right] f_{\ell}^{m}\right\rangle \).

\( =\hbar^{2}[\ell(\ell+1)-m(m \pm 1)]\left\langle f_{\ell}^{m} \mid f_{\ell}^{m}\right\rangle=\hbar^{2}[\ell(\ell+1)-m(m \pm 1)]=\left\langle L_{\pm} f_{\ell}^{m} \mid L_{\pm} f_{\ell}^{m}\right\rangle \).

Upper signs:

\( \hbar^{2}[\ell(\ell+1)-m(m+1)]=\left\langle L_{+} f_{\ell}^{m} \mid L_{+} f_{\ell}^{m}\right\rangle=\left\langle A_{\ell}^{m} f_{\ell}^{m+1} \mid A_{\ell}^{m} f_{\ell}^{m+1}\right\rangle=\left|A_{\ell}^{m}\right|^{2} \Rightarrow A_{\ell}^{m}=\hbar \sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)-m(m+1)} \).

Lower signs:

\( \hbar^{2}[\ell(\ell+1)-m(m-1)]=\left\langle L_{-} f_{\ell}^{m} \mid L_{-} f_{\ell}^{m}\right\rangle=\left\langle B_{\ell}^{m} f_{\ell}^{m-1} \mid B_{\ell}^{m} f_{\ell}^{m-1}\right\rangle=\left|B_{\ell}^{m}\right|^{2} \Rightarrow B_{\ell}^{m}=\hbar \sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)-m(m-1)} \).

At the top of the ladder \( (m=\ell) \text { we get } A_{\ell}^{\ell}=0 \), so there is no higher rung; at the bottom of the ladder \( (m=\ell) \) we get \( B_{\ell}^{-\ell}=0 \), so there is no lower rung.
