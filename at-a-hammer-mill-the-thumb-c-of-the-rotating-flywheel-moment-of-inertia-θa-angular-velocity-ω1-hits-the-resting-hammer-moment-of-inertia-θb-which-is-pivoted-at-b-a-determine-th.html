Guest Question.

At a hammer mill, the thumb \(C\) of the rotating flywheel (moment of inertia \(\Theta _{A} \) , angular velocity \(\omega _{1}\) ) hits the resting hammer (moment of inertia \(\Theta _{B}\) ) which is pivoted at \(B\).

a) Determine the angular velocities \(\overline{\omega}_{1} \) of the flywheel and \(\overline{\omega}_{1} \) of the hammer shortly after impact (coefficient of restitution \(e\))

b) Calculate the energy loss for the case \(\Theta _{B}/\Theta _{A}= a^{2} /r^{2}\) .

Admin
Solution

a) The principles of angular impulse and momentum for ① and ② yield

\(\overset{\curvearrowright }{A}=\Theta _{A}\left(\overline{\omega} _{1}-\omega _{1} \right)=-r\widehat{F} \) ,

\(\overset{\curvearrowleft }{B}: \Theta _{B}\overline{\omega } _{2}=a\widehat{F}\) .

With the impact hypothesis

\(e=-\frac{\overline{v} _{1C}-\overline{v} _{2C} }{v_{1C}-v_{2C} }\)

and the kinematic relations

\(v_{1C}=r\omega _{1} \) , \(\overline{v} _{1C}=r\overline{\omega} _{1} \) , \(v_{2C} =0\) , \(\overline{v} _{2C}=a\overline{\omega} _{2}\)

we obtain

\(\underline{\underline{\overline{\omega}_{1}=\omega _{1} \frac{1-e\frac{r^{2}\Theta _{B} }{a^{2}\Theta _{A} } }{1+ \frac{r^{2}\Theta _{B} }{a^{2}\Theta _{A} }} } } \) , \(\underline{\underline{\overline{\omega}_{2}=\omega _{1} \frac{\left(1+e\right)\frac{r}{a} }{1+\frac{r^{2} \Theta _{B} }{a^{2} \Theta _{A} } } } } \) .

b) The energy loss is calculated from the difference of kinetic energies before and after impact. With \(r^{2}\Theta _{B}=a^{2}\Theta _{A} \) follows

\(\underline{\underline{\Delta T} }=\frac{1}{2}\Theta _{A}\omega ^{2}_{1}-\left[\frac{1}{2}\Theta _{A}\overline{\omega}^{2}_{1}+\frac{1}{2}\Theta _{B}\overline{\omega} ^{2}_{2} \right] \)

 

\(\frac{1}{8}\Theta _{A}\omega ^{2}_{1}\left[4-\left(1-e\right)^{2} -\left(1+e\right)^{2} \right] =\underline{\underline{\frac{1}{4}\left(1-e^{2} \right)\Theta _{A}\omega ^{2}_{1} } } \) .

Remark: In case of an ideal elastic impact (\(e = 1\)) we would obtain for the given datai \(\overline{\omega } _{1} =0\) and \(\overline{\omega } _{2} =\omega _{1}r/a \).
