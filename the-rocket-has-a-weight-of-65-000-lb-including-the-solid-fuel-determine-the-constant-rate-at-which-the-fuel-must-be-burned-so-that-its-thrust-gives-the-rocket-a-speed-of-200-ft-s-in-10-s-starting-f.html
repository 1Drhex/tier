Guest Question.

The rocket has a weight of 65 000 lb, including the solid fuel. Determine the constant rate at which the fuel must be burned, so that its thrust gives the rocket a speed of 200 ft/s in 10 s starting from rest. The fuel is expelled from the rocket at a speed of 3000 ft/s relative to the rocket. Neglect air resistance and the variation of gravity with altitude.

Admin
Solution

The control volume considered consists of the rocket and its contents as shown in Fig. a, which is accelerating upwards. We consider steady flow of an ideal fluid relative to the control volume. The mass of the control volume as a function of time t is

\(M = \DOT{M}_o – \dot{m} _f t = \frac{65000}{32.2 ft/s^2} – \dot{m}_ft = (2018.63 – \dot{m}_ft) slug \)

Referring to the FBD of the control volume, Fig. a with \(\dot{m} _a = 0 and V_e = 3000 ft/s, \)

\(+ c\sum F_y = m \frac{dV_{cv}}{dt} + \dot{m}_aV_{cv} – (\dot{m}_a + \dot{m}_f)V_e \)

 

\(– (2018.63 – \dot{m}_ft)(32.2) = (2018.63 – \dot{m}_f t) \frac{dV}{dt} + 0 – (0 + \dot{m}_f)(3000 ft/s) \)

 

\(\frac{dV}{dt} = \frac{3000 \dot{m}_f}{2018.63 – \dot{m}_ft} – 32.2 \)

Integrating this equation with the initial condition V = 0 at t = 0 and the requirement \( V = 200 ft/s at t = 10 s, \)

\(\int_{0}^{200 ft/s} dV = \int_{0}^{10 s} (\frac{3000 \dot{m}_f}{2018.63 – \dot{m}_ft} – 32.2)dt \)

 

\( 200 = [-3000 ln (2018.63 – \dot{m}_f t) – 32.2 t]_{0}^{10 s} \)

 

\(200 = 3000 ln (\frac{2018.63}{2018.63 – 10\dot{m}_f}) – 322 \)

 

\( = ln(\frac{2018.63}{2018.63 – 10\dot{m}_f}) = 0.174 \)

 

\(\frac{2018.63}{2018.63 – 10\dot{m}_f} = e^{0.174} \)

 

\(\dot{m}_f = 32.2 slug/s \)
