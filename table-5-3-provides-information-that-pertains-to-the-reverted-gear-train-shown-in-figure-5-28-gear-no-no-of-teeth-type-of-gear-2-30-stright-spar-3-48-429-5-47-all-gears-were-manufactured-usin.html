Guest Question.

Table 5.3 provides information that pertains to the reverted gear train shown in Figure 5.28.

 

Gear No. No. of Teeth Type of Gear
2 30 stright spar
3 48 —
4 29 —
5 47 Helical spar
Admin
Solution

By inspection, gear 3 is a straight spur gear, and gear 4 is a helical spur gear.
(a) For gears 2 and 3 we have

\(p_{c2}=p_{c3}=0.375 in =p_{h}\)

From Equation (5.6-3)\([Pp_c=\pi]\) we get

\(P_2=P_3=\frac{\pi}{p_{c3}}=\frac{\pi}{0.375 in}=8.38 in^{-1}\)

From Equations (5.5-4)\([r_{bj}=r_j\cos φ]\) and (5.6-2)\([P=\frac Nd=\frac{N}{2r}]\) we obtain

\(r_{b3}=r_3\cos φ=\frac{d_3}{2}\cosφ=\frac{N}{2P_3}\cosφ=\frac{48}{2\times 8.38 in ^{-1}}\cos 20°=2.69\) in

(b) From Equations (5.8-1)\([c=\frac{d_2}{2}+\frac{d_3}{2}=\frac{d_4}{2}+\frac{d_5}{2}]\) and (5.6-2)\([P=\frac Nd=\frac{N}{2r}]\) we have

\(c=\frac{d_2}{2}+\frac{d_3}{2}=\frac{N_2}{2P_2}+\frac{N_3}{2P_3}=\frac{30}{2\times 8.38 in ^{-1}}+\frac{48}{2\times 8.38 in^{-1}}=4.65\) in

(c) Employing the result from part (b) and then combining Equations (5.8-1) and (5.6-1), we obtain

\(c=4.65 in \frac{d_4}{2}+\frac{d_5}{2}=\frac{N_4p_{c4}}{2\pi}+\frac{N_5p_{c5}}{2\pi}=\frac{29p_{c4}}{2\pi}+\frac{47p_{c5}}{2\pi}\)

Noting that

\(p_{c4}=p_{c5}\)

we obtain

\(p_{c4}=0.384\) in

(d) For gears 4 and 5, we recognize that

\(p_{n4}=p_{n5}\)

From Equation (5.9-2)\([p_{n}=p_h=p_{c}\cos Ψ_4]\) we have

\(p_{n4}=p_{c4}\cos Ψ_4=p_h\)

\(0.375=0.384 \cos Ψ_4\)

and therefore

\(Ψ_4 = 12.7°=ψ_5\)

(e) From Equation (5.6-1)\([p_c=\frac{\pi d}{N}=\frac{2\pi r}{N}]\) we get

\(d_4=\frac{N_4p_{c4}}{\pi}=\frac{29\times 0.384 in}{\pi}=3.54\) in

(f) For a given hob, the addendum obtained for a helical spur gear is the same as that for a straight spur gear. Therefore

\(a_4=a_2=\frac{1}{P_2}=\frac{1}{8.38}\) in

Using the results of part (e), the addendum circle diameter of gear 4 is

\(d_4+2a_4=\left\lgroup 3.45+\frac{2}{8.38}\right\rgroup\) in =3.78 in
