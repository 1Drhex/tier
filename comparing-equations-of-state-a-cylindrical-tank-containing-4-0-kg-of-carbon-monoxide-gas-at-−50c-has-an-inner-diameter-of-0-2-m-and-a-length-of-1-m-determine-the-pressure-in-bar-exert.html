Comparing Equations of State

A cylindrical tank containing 4.0 kg of carbon monoxide gas at −50°C has an inner diameter of 0.2 m and a length of 1 m. Determine the pressure, in bar, exerted by the gas using (a) the generalized compressibility chart, (b) the ideal gas equation of state, (c) the van der Waals equation of state, (d) the Redlich–Kwong equation of state. Compare the results obtained.

Admin
Solution

Known A cylindrical tank of known dimensions contains 4.0 kg of CO gas at −50°C.

Find Determine the pressure exerted by the gas using four alternative methods.

Schematic and Given Data:

Engineering Model

1. As shown in the accompanying figure, the closed system is taken as the gas.

2. The system is at equilibrium.

Analysis The molar specific volume of the gas is required in each part of the solution. Let us begin by evaluating it. The volume occupied by the gas is

 

\(V=\left(\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}\right) L=\frac{\pi(0.2 m )^{2}(1.0 m )}{4}=0.0314 m ^{3}\)

 

The molar specific volume is then

 

\(\bar{v}=M v=M\left(\frac{V}{m}\right)=\left(28 \frac{ kg }{ kmol }\right)\left(\frac{0.0314 m ^{3}}{4.0 kg }\right)=0.2198 \frac{ m ^{3}}{ kmol }\)

 

a. From for CO, \(T_{ c }=133 K , p_{ c }=35\) bar. Thus, the reduced temperature \(T_{ R }\) and pseudoreduced specific volume \(v_{ R }^{\prime}\) are, respectively,

 

\(T_{ R }=\frac{223 K }{133 K }=1.68\)

 

\(v_{ R }^{\prime}=\frac{\bar{v} p_{ c }}{\bar{R} T_{ c }}=\frac{\left(0.2198 m ^{3} / kmol \right)\left(35 \times 10^{5} N / m ^{2}\right)}{(8314 N \cdot m / kmol \cdot K )(133 K )}=0.696\)

 

Turning to Fig. A-2, \(Z \approx 0.9 . \text { Solving } Z=p \bar{v} / \bar{R} T\) for pressure and inserting known values

 

\(p=\frac{Z \bar{R} T}{\bar{v}}=\frac{0.9(8314 N \cdot m / kmol \cdot K )(223 K )}{\left(0.2198 m ^{3} / kmol \right)}\left|\frac{1 bar }{10^{5} N / m ^{2}}\right|\)

 

= 75.9 bar

 

b. The ideal gas equation of state gives

 

\(p=\frac{\bar{R} T}{\bar{v}}=\frac{(8314 N \cdot m / kmol \cdot K )(223 K )}{\left(0.2198 m ^{3} / kmol \right)}\left|\frac{1 bar }{10^{5} N / m ^{2}}\right|=84.4 bar\)

 

c. For carbon monoxide, the van der Waals constants a and b given by Eqs. 11.4 can be read directly from . Thus,

 

\(a=\frac{27}{64} \frac{\bar{R}^{2} T_{ c }^{2}}{p_{ c }}\) (11.4a)

 

\(b=\frac{\bar{R} T_{ c }}{8 p_{ c }}\) (11.4b)

 

\(\bar{v}_{ c }=\frac{3}{8} \frac{\bar{R} T_{ c }}{p_{ c }}\) (11.4c)

 

\(a=1.474 \text { bar }\left(\frac{ m ^{3}}{ kmol }\right)^{2} \quad \text { and } \quad b=0.0395 \frac{ m ^{3}}{ kmol }\)

 

Substituting into Eq. 11.2

 

\(p=\frac{\bar{R} T}{\bar{v}-b}-\frac{a}{\bar{v}^{2}}\) (11.2)

 

\(p=\frac{\bar{R} T}{\bar{v}-b}-\frac{a}{\bar{v}^{2}}\)

 

\(=\frac{(8314 N \cdot m / kmol \cdot K )(223 K )}{(0.2198-0.0395)\left( m ^{3} / kmol \right)}\left|\frac{1 bar }{10^{5} N / m ^{2}}\right|\)

 

\(-\frac{1.474 \operatorname{bar}\left( m ^{3} / kmol \right)^{2}}{\left(0.2198 m ^{3} / kmol \right)^{2}}\)

 

= 72.3 bar

 

Alternatively, the values for \(v_{ R }^{\prime} \text { and } T_{ R }\) obtained in the solution of part (a) can be substituted into Eq. 11.5, giving Z = 0.86. Then, with \(p=Z \bar{R} T / \bar{v}, p=72.5\) bar. The slight difference is attributed to roundoff.

 

\(Z=\frac{v_{ R }^{\prime}}{v_{ R }^{\prime}-1 / 8}-\frac{27 / 64}{T_{ R } v_{ R }^{\prime}}\) (11.5)

 

d. For carbon monoxide, the Redlich–Kwong constants given by Eqs. 11.8 can be read directly from . Thus

 

\(a=a^{\prime} \frac{\bar{R}^{2} T_{ c }^{5 / 2}}{p_{ c }} \quad \text { and } \quad b=b^{\prime} \frac{\bar{R} T_{ c }}{p_{ c }}\) (11.8)

 

\(a=\frac{17.22 \operatorname{bar}\left( m ^{6}\right)( K )^{1 / 2}}{( kmol )^{2}} \quad \text { and } \quad b=0.02737 m ^{3} / kmol\)

 

Substituting into Eq. 11.7

 

\(p=\frac{\bar{R} T}{\bar{v}-b}-\frac{a}{\bar{v}(\bar{v}+b) T^{1 / 2}}\) (11.7)

 

\(p=\frac{\bar{R} T}{\bar{v}-b}-\frac{a}{\bar{v}(\bar{v}+b) T^{1 / 2}}\)

 

\(=\frac{(8314 N \cdot m / kmol \cdot K )(223 K )}{(0.2198-0.02737) m ^{3} / kmol }\left|\frac{1 bar }{10^{5} N / m ^{2}}\right|\)

 

\(-\frac{17.22 \text { bar }}{(0.2198)(0.24717)(223)^{1 / 2}}\)

 

= 75.1 bar

 

Alternatively, the values for \(v_{ R }^{\prime} \text { and } T_{ R }\) obtained in the solution of part (a) can be substituted into Eq. 11.9, giving Z = 0.89. Then, with \(p=Z \bar{R} T / \bar{v}, p=75.1\) bar. In comparison to the value of part (a), the ideal gas equation of state predicts a pressure that is 11% higher and the van der Waals equation gives a value that is 5% lower. The Redlich–Kwong value is about 1% less than the value obtained using the compressibility chart.

 

\(Z=\frac{v_{ R }^{\prime}}{v_{ R }^{\prime}-b^{\prime}}-\frac{a^{\prime}}{\left(v_{ R }^{\prime}+b^{\prime}\right) T_{ R }^{3 / 2}}\) (11.9)
