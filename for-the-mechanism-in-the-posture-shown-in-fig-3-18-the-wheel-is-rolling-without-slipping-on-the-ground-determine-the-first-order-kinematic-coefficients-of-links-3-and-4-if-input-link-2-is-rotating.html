Guest Question.

For the mechanism in the posture shown in Fig. 3.18, the wheel is rolling without slipping on the ground. Determine the first-order kinematic coefficients of links 3 and 4. If input link 2 is rotating with a constant angular velocity of 10 rad/s ccw, then determine: (a) the angular velocities of links 3 and 4 and (b) the velocity of the center of the wheel, point G.

Admin
Solution

The loop-closure equation for the mechanism is

\(\overset{\sqrt{}I}{r_{2}} + \overset{\sqrt{}?}{r_{3}} + \overset{\sqrt{}?}{r_{4}} + \overset{\sqrt{}C1}{r_{7}} – \overset{C2\sqrt{}}{r_{9}} = 0,\) (1)

where the first constraint, C1, is θ7 = θ9 + 270° and the second constraint, C2, designates the rolling contact constraint defined later by Eq. (5).

The horizontal and vertical components of Eq. (1) give the scalar equations

\(r_{2} \cos \theta_{2}+r_{3} \cos \theta_{3}+r_{4} \cos \theta_{4}+r_{7} \cos \theta_{7}-r_{9} \cos \theta_{9}=0\) (2a)

 

\(r_{2} \sin \theta_{2}+r_{3} \sin \theta_{3}+r_{4} \sin \theta_{4}+r_{7} \sin \theta_{7}-r_{9} \sin \theta_{9}=0\) (2b)

 

Since the input is the angular displacement of link 2, we differentiate Eqs. (2) with respect to θ2 and, by setting θ9 = 0°, we obtain

\(-r_{2} \sin \theta_{2}-r_{3} \sin \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}-r_{4} \sin \theta_{4} \theta_{4}^{\prime}-r_{9}^{\prime}=0\) (3a)

and

\(r_{2} \cos \theta_{2}+r_{3} \cos \theta_{3} \theta_{3}^{\prime}+r_{4} \cos \theta_{4} \theta_{4}^{\prime}=0\) (3b)

where the first-order kinematic coefficients of links 3 and 4 and the change in position of contact point C, respectively, are

\(\theta_{3}^{\prime}=\frac{d \theta_{3}}{d \theta_{2}}, \quad \theta_{4}^{\prime}=\frac{d \theta_{4}}{d \theta_{2}}, \quad \text { and } \quad r_{9}^{\prime}=\frac{d r_{9}}{d \theta_{2}} .\) (4)

 

Also, note that the rotation of the wheel, θ4 , and the change in the distance, r9, are not independent, since the wheel is rolling on the ground without slipping. This rolling contact (no-slip) condition is constraint C2 and can be written as shown in Sec. 2.18:

−Δr9 = ρ (Δθ4 − Δθ7 ),

where ρ is the radius of the wheel and, from constraint C1, we note that Δθ7 = 0.
Therefore, the rolling contact condition is

-Δr9 = ρΔθ4. (5)

The negative sign on the left-hand side of this constraint equation is the result of the decreasing magnitude of r9 for a positive change in the input variable—that is, Δθ2.

In the limit, for infinitesimal displacements, constraint Eq. (5) can be written in terms of first-order kinematic coefficients as

\(-r_{9}^{\prime}=\rho \theta_{4}^{\prime}\) (6)

Substituting Eq. (6) into Eqs. (3), and writing the resulting equations in matrix form, gives

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc}-r_{3} \sin \theta_{3} & \rho-r_{4} \sin \theta_{4} \\r_{3} \cos \theta_{3} & r_{4} \cos \theta_{4}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}\theta_{3}^{\prime}\\\theta_{4}^{\prime}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}+r_{2} \sin \theta_{2} \\-r_{2} \cos \theta_{2}\end{array}\right]\) (7)

 

The determinant of the (2×2) coefficient matrix in Eq. (7) can be written as

\(\Delta=r_{3}\left[r_{4} \sin \left(\theta_{4}-\theta_{3}\right)-\rho \cos \theta_{3}\right]\) (8)

From Eq. (7), using Cramer’s rule, the symbolic equations for the first-order kinematic coefficients of links 3 and 4 are

 

\(\theta_{3}^{\prime}=\frac{r_{2}\left[\rho \cos \theta_{2}-r_{4} \sin \left(\theta_{4}-\theta_{2}\right)\right]}{\Delta}\) (9a)

 

\(\theta_{4}^{\prime}=\frac{r_{2} r_{3} \sin \left(\theta_{3}-\theta_{2}\right)}{\Delta}\) (9b)

 

For the posture shown in Fig. 3.18, we have θ2 = 90°, θ3 = 0°, and θ4 = −90°.
Therefore, the determinant in Eq. (8) can be written as

Δ = −r3 (r4 +ρ). (10)

Substituting the known data and Eq. (10) into Eqs. (9), and simplifying, gives

 

\(\theta_{3}^{\prime}=0 \quad \text { and } \quad \theta_{4}^{\prime}=\frac{r_{2}}{r_{4}+\rho}=1 rad / rad \text {. }\) (11)

 

Therefore, the angular velocity of link 3 is

\(\omega_{3}=\theta_{3}^{\prime} \omega_{2}=0\)

and the angular velocity of the wheel, link 4, is

\(\omega_{4}=\theta_{4}^{\prime} \omega_{2}=10 rad / s ccw\)

Note that these results are in agreement with our intuition; in this posture, link 3 is not rotating, and link 4 is rotating at the same angular velocity as the input.
The velocity of the center of the wheel, point G, can be written as

\(V _{G}=r_{9}^{\prime} \omega_{2} \angle \theta_{9}\) (12)

Substituting Eq. (11) into Eq. (6) gives \(r_{9}^{\prime}=-\rho=-100 mm,\) and substituting this into Eq. (12) gives

\(V _{G}=r_{9}^{\prime} \omega_{2} \angle \theta_{9}=(-100 mm / rad )(10 rad / s ) \angle 0^{\circ}=-1.0 m / s \angle 0^{\circ} .\)

This result can be verified by direct calculation by noting that

\(V _{G}= \not V _{ C _{4}}^{0}+ \omega _{4} \times(\rho \hat{ j })=(10 rad / s ) \hat{ k } \times(100 mm ) \hat{ j }=-1.0 \hat{ i } m / s.\)
