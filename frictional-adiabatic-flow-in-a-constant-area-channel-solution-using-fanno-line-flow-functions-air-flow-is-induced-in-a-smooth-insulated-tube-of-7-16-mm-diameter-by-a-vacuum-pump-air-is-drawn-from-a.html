FRICTIONAL ADIABATIC FLOW IN A CONSTANT-AREA CHANNEL: SOLUTION USING

FANNO-LINE FLOW FUNCTIONS

Air flow is induced in a smooth insulated tube of 7.16 mm diameter by a vacuum pump. Air is drawn from a room, where \(p_0 = 760\) mm Hg (abs) and \(T_0 = 23\)°C, through a smoothly contoured converging nozzle. At section (1), where the nozzle joins the constant-area tube, the static pressure is 218.9 mm Hg (gage). At section (2), located some distance downstream in the constant-area tube, the static pressure is -412 mm Hg (gage). The duct walls are smooth; assume the average friction factor, f, is the value at section (1). Determine the length of duct required for choking from section (1), the Mach number at section (2), and the duct length, \(L_{12}\), between sections (1) and (2) . Sketch the process on a \(Ts\) diagram.

Admin
Solution

Given:


Air flow (with friction) in an insulated constant-area tube.
Gage pressures: \(p_1 = -18.9\) mm Hg, and \(p_2 = -412\) mm Hg.
\(M_3 = 1.0\)

Find:

(a) \(L_{13}\). (b) \(M_2\). (c) \(L_{12}\). (d) Sketch the \(Ts\) diagram.
Solution:
Flow in the constant-area tube is frictional and adiabatic, a Fanno-line flow. To find the friction factor, we need to know the flow conditions at section (1). If we assume flow in the nozzle is isentropic, local properties at the nozzle exit may be computed using isentropic relations. Thus

 

\(\frac{p_{0_{1}}}{p_{1}}=\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{1}^{2}\right)^{k /(k-1)}\)

 

Solving for \(M_{1}\), we obtain

\(\begin{aligned}M_{1} &=\left\{\frac{2}{k-1}\left[\left(\frac{p_{0_{1}}}{p_{1}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}-1\right]\right\}^{1 / 2}=\left\{\frac{2}{0.4}\left[\left(\frac{760}{760-18.9}\right)^{0.286}-1\right]\right\}^{1 / 2}=0.190 \\\quad\\T_{1} &=\frac{T_{0_{1}}}{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M_{1}^{2}}=\frac{296 \mathrm{~K}}{1+0.2(0.190)^{2}}=294 \mathrm{~K} \\\quad\\V_{1} &=M_{1} c_{1}=M_{1} \sqrt{k R T_{1}}=0.190\left[1.4 \times 287 \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}} \times 294 \times \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}\right]^{1 / 2} \\\quad\\V_{1} &=65.3 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\end{aligned}\)

 

Using the density of mercury at room temperature \(\left(23^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\right)\),

\(\begin{aligned}p_{1} &=g \rho_{\mathrm{Hg}} h_{1}=g \mathrm{SG} \rho_{\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}} h_{1} \\\quad\\&=9.81 \frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}^{2}} \times 13.5 \times 1000 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}} \times(760-18.9) \times 10^{-3} \mathrm{~m} \times \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}{\mathrm{~kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&p_{1}=98.1 \mathrm{kPa}(\mathrm{abs}) \\\quad\\&\rho_{1}=\frac{p_{1}}{R T_{1}}=9.81 \times 10^{4} \frac{\mathrm{N}}{\mathrm{m}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}}{287 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m}} \times \frac{1}{294 \mathrm{~K}}=1.16 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\end{aligned}\)

 

At \(T=294 \mathrm{~K}\left(21^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\right), \mu=1.82 \times 10^{-5} \mathrm{~kg} /(\mathrm{m} \cdot \mathrm{s})\) from Table A.10, Appendix A. Thus

\(R e_{1}=\frac{\rho_{1} V_{1} D_{1}}{\mu_{1}}=1.16 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}} \times 65.3 \frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}} \times 0.00716 \mathrm{~m} \times \frac{\mathrm{m} \cdot \mathrm{s}}{1.82 \times 10^{-5} \mathrm{~kg}}=2.98 \times 10^{4}\)

 

From Eq. 8.37 (turbulent flow), for smooth pipe, \(f=0.0235\).

\(\frac{1}{\sqrt{f}}=-2.0 \log \left(\frac{e / D}{3.7}+\frac{2.51}{\operatorname{Re} \sqrt{f}}\right)\) (8.37)

From Appendix E.3 at \(M_{1}=0.190, p / p^{*}=5.745\) (Eq. 13.34d), and \(\bar{f} L_{\max } / D_{h}=16.38\) (Eq. 13.34a). Thus, assuming \(\bar{f}=f_{1}\),

\(L_{13}=\left(L_{\max }\right)_{1}=\left(\frac{\bar{f} L_{\max }}{D_{h}}\right)_{1} \frac{D_{h}}{f_{1}}=16.38 \times \frac{0.00716 \mathrm{~m}}{0.0235}=4.99 \mathrm{~m} \quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad L_{13}}\)

Since \(p^{*}\) is constant for all states on the same Fanno line, conditions at section (2) can be determined from the pressure ratio, \(\left(p / p^{*}\right)_{2}\). Thus

\(\left(\frac{p}{p^{*}}\right)_{2}=\frac{p_{2}}{p^{*}}=\frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}} \frac{p_{1}}{p^{*}}=\frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}\left(\frac{p}{p^{*}}\right)_{1}=\left(\frac{760-412}{760-18.9}\right) 5.745=2.698\)

where we used Eq. 13.34 d to obtain the value of \(p / p^{*}\) at section (1). For \(p / p^{*}=2.698\) at section (2), Eq. 13.34d yields \(M_{2}=0.400\) (after obtaining an initial guess value from the plot in Appendix E.3, and iterating).

\(M_{2}=0.400\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad M_{2}}\)

The \(Ts\) diagram for this flow is

At \(M_{2}=0.400, \quad \bar{f} L_{\max } / D_{h}=2.309\) (Eq. 13.34a, Appendix E.3). Thus

 

\(L_{23}=\left(L_{\max }\right)_{2}=\left(\frac{\bar{f} L_{\max }}{D_{h}}\right)_{2} \frac{D_{h}}{f_{1}}=2.309 \times \frac{0.00716 \mathrm{~m}}{0.0235}=0.704 \mathrm{~m}\)

 

Finally,

 

\(L_{12}=L_{13}-L_{23}=(4.99-0.704) \mathrm{m}=4.29 \mathrm{~m}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad L_{12}}\)

 

This problem illustrates use of the Fanno-line equations, Eqs.13.34.

\(\begin{gathered}\frac{\bar{f} L_{\max }}{D_{h}}=\frac{1-M^{2}}{k M^{2}}+\frac{k+1}{2 k} \ln \left[\frac{(k+1) M^{2}}{2\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\right)}\right] \\\quad\\\frac{T}{T^{*}}=\frac{\left(\frac{k+1}{2}\right)}{\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\right)} \\\quad\\\frac{V}{V^{*}}=\frac{\rho^{*}}{\rho}=\left[\frac{\left(\frac{k+1}{2}\right) M^{2}}{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}}\right]^{1 / 2} \\\quad\\\frac{p}{p^{*}}=\frac{1}{M}\left[\frac{\left(\frac{k+1}{2}\right)}{1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}}\right]^{1 / 2} \\\quad\\\frac{p_{0}}{p_{0}^{*}}=\frac{1}{M}\left[\left(\frac{2}{k+1}\right)\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\right)\right]^{(k+1) / 2(k-1)}\end{gathered}\) (13.34 a, b, c, d, e)

 

  • These equations give the same results as the basic equations, Eqs. 13.24, as can be seen by comparing,
    for example, the value of \(M_2\) obtained in this Example and in .

\(\begin{gathered}\rho_{1} V_{1}=\rho_{2} V_{2}=\rho V \equiv G=\frac{\dot{m}}{A}=\text { constant } \\\quad\\R_{x}+p_{1} A-p_{2} A=\dot{m} V_{2}-\dot{m} V_{1}\\\quad\\h_{0_{1}}=h_{1}+\frac{V_{1}^{2}}{2}=h_{2}+\frac{V_{2}^{2}}{2}=h_{0_{2}}=h_{0} \\\quad\\s_{2}>s_{1} \\\quad\\p=\rho R T \\\quad\\\Delta h=h_{2}-h_{1}=c_{p} \Delta T=c_{p}\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right) \\\quad\\\Delta s=s_{2}-s_{1}=c_{p} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-R \ln \frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}\end{gathered}\) (13.24 a, b, c, d, e, f, g)

  • The computations can be quite laborious without using preprogrammed Fanno-line relations (available, for example, in the Excel add-ins on the Web site)!

The Excel workbook for this Example is convenient for performing the calculations, either using the Fanno-line relations or the basic equations.
