Guest Question.

The simply supported beam shown in Fig. P10.75 consists of a rectangular structural steel tube shape [E = 200 GPa; I = 350 × \(10^{6} mm ^{4}\)]. For the loading shown, determine:
(a) the beam deflection at point C.
(b) the beam deflection at point E.

Admin
Solution

(a) Beam deflection at point C
Consider 315 kN-m concentrated moment.
[Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated moment at one end.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{C}=-\frac{M x}{6 L E I}\left(2 L^{2}-3 L x+x^{2}\right) \text { (elastic curve) }\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&M=-315 kN – m , L=9 m , x=6 m, \\&E I=7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{C} &=-\frac{M x}{6 L E I}\left(2 L^{2}-3 L x+x^{2}\right) \\&=-\frac{(-315 kN – m )(6 m )}{6(9 m )\left(7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\right)}\left[2(9 m )^{2}-3(9 m )(6 m )+(6 m )^{2}\right]=0.018000 m\end{aligned}\)

 

Consider 120 kN/m uniformly distributed load.
[Appendix C, SS beam with uniformly distributed load over a portion of the span.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{C}=-\frac{w a^{2}}{24 L E I}\left(2 x^{3}-6 L x^{2}+a^{2} x+4 L^{2} x-a^{2} L\right)\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&w=120 kN / m , L=9 m , a=3 m , x=6 m , \\&E I=7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{C} &=-\frac{w a^{2}}{24 L E I}\left(2 x^{3}-6 L x^{2}+a^{2} x+4 L^{2} x-a^{2} L\right) \\&=-\frac{(120 kN / m )(3 m )^{2}}{24(9 m )\left(7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\right)}\left[2(6 m )^{3}-6(9 m )(6 m )^{2}+(3 m )^{2}(6 m )+4(9 m )^{2}(6 m )-(3 m )^{2}(9 m )\right] \\&=-0.028929 m\end{aligned}\)

 

Consider 100-kN concentrated load. [Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated load not at midspan.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{C}=-\frac{P a b}{6 L E I}\left(L^{2}-a^{2}-b^{2}\right)\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&P=100 kN , L=9 m , a=6 m , b=3 m , \\&E I=7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{C} &=-\frac{P a b}{6 L E I}\left(L^{2}-a^{2}-b^{2}\right) \\&=-\frac{(100 kN )(6 m )(3 m )}{6(9 m )\left(7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\right)}\left[(9 m )^{2}-(6 m )^{2}-(3 m )^{2}\right]=-0.017143 m\end{aligned}\)

 

Consider 60 kN/m uniformly distributed load on overhang DE.
[Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated moment.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{C}=-\frac{M x}{6 L E I}\left(2 L^{2}-3 L x+x^{2}\right) \text { (elastic curve) }\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&M=-(60 kN / m )(3 m )(1.5 m )=-270 kN – m, \\&L=9 m , x=3 m , E I=7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}v_{C} &=-\frac{M x}{6 L E I}\left(2 L^{2}-3 L x+x^{2}\right) \\&=-\frac{(-270 kN – m )(3 m )}{6(9 m )\left(7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\right)}\left[2(9 m )^{2}-3(9 m )(3 m )+(3 m )^{2}\right]=0.019286 m\end{aligned}\)

Beam deflection at C

\(v_{C}=0.018000 m -0.028929 m -0.017143 m +0.019286 m =-0.008786 m =8.79 mm \downarrow\)

 

(b) Beam deflection at point E
Consider 315 kN-m concentrated moment.
[Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated moment at one end.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(\theta_{D}=\frac{M L}{6 E I} \text { (slope magnitude) }\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&M=-315 kN – m , L=9 m ,\\&E I=7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}\theta_{D} &=\frac{M L}{6 E I}=\frac{(-315 kN – m )(9 m )}{6\left(7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\right)}=-0.0067500 rad \\v_{E} &=(3 m )(-0.0067500 rad )=-0.020250 m\end{aligned}\)

 

Consider 120 kN/m uniformly distributed load.
[Appendix C, SS beam with uniformly distributed load over a portion of the span.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(\theta_{D}=\frac{w a^{2}}{24 L E I}\left(2 L^{2}-a^{2}\right) \text { (slope magnitude) }\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&w=120 kN / m , L=9 m , a=3 m , \\&E I=7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}&\theta_{D}=\frac{w a^{2}}{24 L E I}\left(2 L^{2}-a^{2}\right)=\frac{(120 kN / m )(3 m )^{2}}{24(9 m )\left(7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\right)}\left[2(9 m )^{2}-(3 m )^{2}\right]=0.0109286 rad \\&v_{E}=(3 m )(0.0109286 rad )=0.032786 m\end{aligned}\)

 

Consider 100-kN concentrated load. [Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated load not at midspan.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(\theta_{D}=\frac{P a\left(L^{2}-a^{2}\right)}{6 L E I} \text { (slope magnitude) }\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&P=100 kN , L=9 m , a=6 m ,\\&E I=7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}&\theta_{D}=\frac{P a\left(L^{2}-a^{2}\right)}{6 L E I}=\frac{(100 kN )(6 m )}{6(9 m )\left(7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\right)}\left[(9 m )^{2}-(6 m )^{2}\right]=0.0071429 rad \\&v_{E}=(3 m )(0.0071429 rad )=0.021429 m\end{aligned}\)

 

Consider 60 kN/m uniformly distributed load on overhang DE.
[Appendix C, SS beam with concentrated moment.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(\theta_{D}=\frac{M L}{3 E I} \text { (slope magnitude) }\)

Values:

\(\begin{aligned}&M=-(60 kN / m )(3 m )(1.5 m )=-270 kN – m, \\&L=9 m , E I=7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

Computation:

\(\begin{aligned}\theta_{D} &=\frac{M L}{3 E I}=\frac{(270 kN – m )(9 m )}{3\left(7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\right)}=0.0115714 rad \\v_{E} &=-(3 m )(0.0115714 rad )=-0.034714 m\end{aligned}\)

 

Determine cantilever deflection due to 60 kN/m uniformly distributed load on overhang DE.
[Appendix C, Cantilever beam with concentrated load.]
Relevant equation from Appendix C:

\(v_{E}=-\frac{w L^{4}}{8 E I} \quad(\text { assuming fixed support at } D \text { ) }\)

Values:

\(w=60 kN / m , L=3 m , E I=7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\)

Computation: \(v_{E}=-\frac{w L^{4}}{8 E I}=-\frac{(60 kN – m )(3 m )^{4}}{8\left(7.0 \times 10^{4} kN – m ^{2}\right)}=-0.008679 m\)

Beam deflection at E

\(\begin{aligned}v_{E} &=-0.020250 m +0.032786 m +0.021429 m -0.034714 m -0.008679 m \\&=-0.009429 m =9.43 mm \downarrow\end{aligned}\)
