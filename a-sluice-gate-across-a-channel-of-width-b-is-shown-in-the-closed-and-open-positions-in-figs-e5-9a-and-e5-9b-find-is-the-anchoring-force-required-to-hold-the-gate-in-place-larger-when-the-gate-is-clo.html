Guest Question.

A sluice gate across a channel of width b is shown in the closed and open positions in Figs. E5.9a and E5.9b.
FIND Is the anchoring force required to hold the gate in place larger when the gate is closed or when it is open?

Admin
Solution

We will answer this question by comparing expressions for the horizontal reaction force, \(R_{x} \), between the gate and the water when the gate is closed and when the gate is open. The control volume used in each case is indicated with dashed lines in Figs. E5.9a and E5.9b.

When the gate is closed, the horizontal forces acting on the contents of the control volume are identified in Fig. E5.9c. Application of \(\sum{V_{out}\rho _{out}A_{out}V_{out}}-\sum{V_{in}\rho _{in}A_{in}V_{in}} =\sum{F_{contents of the control volume}} \) to the contents of this control volume yields

\(A_{out}=0\)

\(A_{in}=0\) (no flow)

\(\sum{u_{out}\rho A_{out}V_{out}}-\sum{u_{in}\rho A_{in}V_{in}} =\frac{1}{2}\gamma H^{2}b-R_{x} \) (1)

Note that the hydrostatic pressure force, \({\gamma H^{2}b}/{2} \), is used. From Eq. 1, the force exerted on the water by the gate (which is equal to the force necessary to hold the gate stationary) is

\(R_{x}=\frac{1}{2} \gamma H^{2}b \) (2)

which is equal in magnitude to the hydrostatic force exerted on the gate by the water.

When the gate is open, the horizontal forces acting on the contents of the control volume are shown in Fig. E5.9d. Application of \(\sum{V_{out}\rho _{out}A_{out}V_{out}}-\sum{V_{in}\rho _{in}A_{in}V_{in}} =\sum{F_{contents of the control volume}} \) to the contents of this control volume leads to

\(\sum{u_{out}\rho A_{out}V_{out}}-\sum{u_{in}\rho A_{in}V_{in}} =\frac{1}{2} \gamma H^{2}b-R_{x}-\frac{1}{2} \gamma h^{2}b-F_{f} \) ( 3)

Note that we have assumed that the pressure distribution is hydrostatic in the water at sections (1) and (2). (See Section 3.4.) Also the frictional force between the channel bottom and the water is specified as \(F_{f} \). With the assumption of uniform velocity distributions

\( \sum{u_{out}\rho A_{out}V_{out}}-\sum{u_{in}\rho A_{in}V_{in}} =u_{2}\rho hbu_{2}-u_{1}\rho Hbu_{1}\) (4)

Equations 3 and 4 can be combined to form

\(\rho u^{2}_{2} hb-\rho u^{2}_{1} Hb=\frac{1}{2}\gamma H^{2}b-R_{x}- \frac{1}{2}\gamma H^{2}b-F_{f} \) (5)

Solving Eq. 5 for the reaction force, \( R_{x}\), we obtain

\(R_{x}= \frac{1}{2}\gamma H^{2}b- \frac{1}{2}\gamma h^{2}b-F_{f}-\rho u^{2}_{2} hb+\rho u^{2}_{1} Hb \) (6)

By using the continuity equation, \(\dot{m} =\rho bHu_{1}=\rho bhu_{2}\), Eq. 6 can be rewritten as

\( R_{x}=\frac{1}{2}\gamma H^{2}b-\frac{1}{2}\gamma h^{2}b -F_{f}-\dot{m}(u_{2}-u_{1}) \) (7)

Hence, since \( u_{2}\gt u_{1}\), by comparing the expressions for \(R_{x}\) (Eqs. 2 and 7) we conclude that the reaction force between the gate and the water (and therefore the anchoring force required to hold the gate in place) is smaller when the gate is open than when it is closed.
