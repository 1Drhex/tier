Guest Question.

Consider a large isothermal enclosure that is maintained at a uniform temperature of 2000 K. Calculate the emissive power of the radiation that emerges from a small aperture on theenclosure surface. What is the wavelength \(\lambda_{1}\) below which 10% of the emission is concentrated? What is the wavelength \(\lambda_{2}\) above which 10% of the emission is concentrated? Determine the maximum spectral emissive power and the wavelength at which this emission occurs. What is the irradiation incident on a small object placed inside the enclosure?

Admin
Solution

Known: Large isothermal enclosure at uniform temperature.

Find:
1. Emissive power of a small aperture on the enclosure.
2. Wavelengths below which and above which 10% of the radiation is concentrated.
3. Spectral emissive power and wavelength associated with maximum emission.
4. Irradiation on a small object inside the enclosure.

Assumptions: Areas of aperture and object are very small relative to enclosure surface.

Analysis:
1. Emission from the aperture of any isothermal enclosure will have the characteristics of blackbody radiation. Hence, from Equation 12.32,

 

\(E_{b}=\sigma T^{4}\) (12.32)

 

\(E=E_{b}(T)=\sigma T^{4}=5.670 \times 10^{-8} W / m ^{2} \cdot K ^{4}(2000 K )^{4}\)

 

\(E=9.07 \times 10^{5} W / m ^{2}\)

 

2. The wavelength \(\lambda_{1}\) corresponds to the upper limit of the spectral band \(\left(0 \rightarrow \lambda_{1}\right)\) containing 10% of the emitted radiation. With \(F_{\left(0 \rightarrow \lambda_{1}\right)}=0.10\) it follows from Table 12.2 that \(\lambda_{1} T=2195 \mu m \cdot K\) . Hence

\(\lambda_{1}=1.1 \mu m\)

 

The wavelength \(\lambda_{2}\) corresponds to the lower limit of the spectral band \(\left(\lambda_{2} \rightarrow \infty\right)\) containing 10% of the emitted radiation. With

 

\(F_{\left(\lambda_{2} \rightarrow \infty\right)}=1-F_{\left(0 \rightarrow \lambda_{2}\right)}=0.1\)

 

\(F_{\left(0 \rightarrow \lambda_{2}\right)}=0.9\)

 

it follows from Table 12.2 that \(\lambda_{2} T=9382 \mu m \cdot K\). Hence

 

\(\lambda_{2}=4.69 \mu m\)

 

3. From Wien’s displacement law, Equation 12.31, \(\lambda_{\max } T=2898 \mu m \cdot K\) Hence

 

\(\lambda_{\max } T=C_{3}\) (12.31)

 

\(\lambda_{\max }=1.45 \mu m\)

 

The spectral emissive power associated with this wavelength may be computed from Equation 12.30 or from the third column of Table 12.2. For \(\lambda_{\max } T=2898 \mu m \cdot K\) it follows from Table 12.2 that

 

\(E_{\lambda, b}(\lambda, T)=\pi I_{\lambda, b}(\lambda, T)=\frac{C_{1}}{\lambda^{5}\left[\exp \left(C_{2} / \lambda T\right)-1\right]}\) (12.30)

 

\(I_{\lambda, b}(1.45 \mu m , T)=0.722 \times 10^{-4} \sigma T^{5}\)

 

Hence

\(I_{\lambda, b}(1.45 \mu m , 2000 K )=0.722 \times 10^{-4}(\mu m \cdot K \cdot sr )^{-1} \times 5.67\)

 

\(\times 10^{-8} W / m ^{2} \cdot K ^{4}(2000 K )^{5}\)

 

\(I_{\lambda, b}(1.45 \mu m , 2000 K )=1.31 \times 10^{5} W / m ^{2} \cdot sr \cdot \mu m\)

 

Since the emission is diffuse, it follows from Equation 12.16 that

\(E_{\lambda}(\lambda)=\pi I_{\lambda, e}(\lambda)\) (12.16)

 

\(E_{\lambda, b}=\pi I_{\lambda, b}=4.12 \times 10^{5} W / m ^{2} \cdot \mu m\)

 

4. Irradiation of any small object inside the enclosure may be approximated as being equal to emission from a blackbody at the enclosure surface temperature. Hence \(G=E_{b}(T)\), in which case

 

\(G=9.07 \times 10^{5} W / m ^{2}\)
