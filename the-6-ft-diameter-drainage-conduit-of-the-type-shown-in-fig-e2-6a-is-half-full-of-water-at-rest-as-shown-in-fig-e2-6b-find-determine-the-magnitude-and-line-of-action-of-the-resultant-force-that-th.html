Guest Question.

The 6-ft-diameter drainage conduit of the type shown in Fig. E2.6a is half full of water at rest, as shown in Fig. E2.6b.
FIND Determine the magnitude and line of action of the resultant force that the water exerts on the curved portion BC for a section of the conduit that is 1 ft long

Admin
Solution

We first isolate a volume of fluid bounded by the curved section BC, the horizontal surface AB, and the vertical surface AC, as shown in Fig. E2.6c. The volume has a length of 1 ft. Forces acting on the volume are the horizontal force,\(F_{1} \), which acts on the vertical surface AC, the weight, w, of the fluid contained within the volume, and the horizontal and vertical components of the force of the conduit wall on the fluid, \(F_{H} \) and \(F_{V} \) ,respectively.

The magnitude of\(F_{1} \) is found from the equation

\(F_{1} =\gamma h_{c} A=(62.4{Ib}/{ft^{3} })(\frac{3}{2}ft )(3ft^{2} )=281Ib\)

and this force acts 1 ft above C as shown. The weight, w, is

\(w=\gamma vol=(62.4{Ib}/{ft^{3} })({9\pi }/{4ft^{2} })(1 ft)=441Ib\)

and acts through the center of gravity of the mass of fluid, which according to Fig. 2.13 is located 1.27 ft to the right of AC as shown. Therefore, to satisfy equilibrium

\(F_{H} =F_{1} =281Ib\)

\(F_{V} =W =441Ib\)

and the magnitude of the resultant force is

\(F_{R} =\sqrt{(F_{H} )^{2}+(F_{V})^{2} } =\sqrt{(281Ib)^2+(441Ib)^2} =523Ib\)

The force the water exerts on the conduit wall is equal, but opposite in direction, to the forces\(F_{H}\) and \(F_{V} \) shown in Fig. E2.6c. Thus, the resultant force on the conduit wall is shown in Fig. E2.6d. This force acts through the point O at the angle shown.

COMMENT An inspection of this result will show that the line of action of the resultant force passes through the center of the conduit. In retrospect, this is not a surprising result, as at each point on the curved surface of the conduit, the elemental force due to the pressure is normal to the surface, and each line of action must pass through the center of the conduit. It therefore follows that the resultant of this concurrent force system must also pass through the center of concurrence of the elemental forces that make up the system.
