FRICTIONLESS FLOW IN A CONSTANT-AREA DUCT WITH HEAT ADDITION:

SOLUTION USING RAYLEIGH-LINE FLOW FUNCTIONS

Air flows with negligible friction in a constant-area duct. At section (1), properties are \(T_1 = 60\)°C, \(p_1 = 135\) kPa (abs), and \(V_1 = 732\) m/s. Heat is added between section (1) and section (2), where \(M_2 = 1.2\). Determine the properties at section (2), the heat exchange per unit mass, and the entropy change, and sketch the process on a \(Ts\) diagram.

Admin
Solution

Given: Frictionless flow of air as shown:


\(T_1 = 333\) K \(M_2 = 1.2\)
\(p_1 = 135\) kPa (abs)
\(V_1 = 732\) m/s
Find:

(a) Properties at section (2) .
(b) \(δQ/dm\).
(c) \(s_2 – s_1\).
(d) \(Ts\) diagram.

Solution:
To obtain property ratios, we need both Mach numbers.

\(\begin{aligned}&c_{1}=\sqrt{k R T_{1}}=\left[1.4 \times 287 \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}} \times 333 \mathrm{~K} \times \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}}\right]^{1 / 2}=366 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s} \\\quad\\&M_{1}=\frac{V_{1}}{c_{1}}=732 \frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}} \times \frac{\mathrm{s}}{366 \mathrm{~m}}=2.00\end{aligned}\)

 

From the Rayleigh-line flow functions of Appendix E-4 we find the following:

\(M\) \(T_0/T_0^*\) \(p_0/p_0^*\) \(T/T^*\) \(p/p^*\) \(V/V^*\)
2.00 0.7934 1.503 0.529 0.364 1.455
1.20 0.9787 1.019 0.9119 0.796 1.146

 

Using these data and recognizing that critical properties are constant, we obtain

\(\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}=\frac{T_{2} / T^{*}}{T_{1} / T^{*}}=\frac{0.9119}{0.5289}=1.72;\quad\quad T_{2}=1.72 T_{1}=(1.72) 333 \mathrm{~K}=573 \mathrm{~K}\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad T_2}\)

 

\(\frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}=\frac{p_{2} / p^{*}}{p_{1} / p^{*}}=\frac{0.7958}{0.3636}=2.19 ; \quad\quad p_{2}=2.19 p_{1}=(2.19) 135 \mathrm{kPa}\)

 

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad p_2 = 296 \mathrm{kPa} \mathrm{(abs)}\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad p_2}\)

 

\(\frac{V_{2}}{V_{1}}=\frac{V_{2} / V^{*}}{V_{1} / V^{*}}=\frac{1.146}{1.455}=0.788 ; \quad\quad V_{2}=0.788 V_{1}=(0.788) 732 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\)

 

\(\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad V_2 = 577 m/s\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad V_2}\)

 

\(\rho_{2}=\frac{p_{2}}{R T_{2}}=2.96 \times 10^{5} \frac{\mathrm{N}}{\mathrm{m}^{2}} \times \frac{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}}{287 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m}} \times \frac{1}{573 \mathrm{~K}}=1.80 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad \rho_2}\)

 

The heat addition may be determined from the energy equation, Eq. 13.43c,

 

\(\frac{\delta Q}{d m}=h_{0_{2}}-h_{0_{1}}=c_{p}\left(T_{0_{2}}-T_{0_{1}}\right)\)

 

From the isentropic-stagnation functions (Eq. 12.21b) at \(M=2.0\),

\(\frac{T_{0}}{T}=1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\) (12.21b)

 

\(\frac{T}{T_{0}}=\frac{T_{1}}{T_{0_{1}}}=0.5556 ; \quad\quad T_{0_{1}}=\frac{T_{1}}{0.5556}=\frac{333 \mathrm{~K}}{0.5556}=599 \mathrm{~K}\)

 

and at \(M=1.2\),

 

\(\frac{T}{T_{0}}=\frac{T_{2}}{T_{0_{2}}}=0.7664 ; \quad\quad T_{0_{2}}=\frac{T_{2}}{0.7764}=\frac{573 \mathrm{~K}}{0.7764}=738 \mathrm{~K}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad T_{0_{2}}}\)

Substituting gives

\(\frac{\delta Q}{d m}=c_{p}\left(T_{0_{2}}-T_{0_{1}}\right)=1.00 \frac{\mathrm{kJ}}{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}} \times(738-599) \mathrm{K}=139 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad {\delta Q}/{d m}}\)

For the change in entropy (Eq. 13.43g),

\(\begin{aligned}s_{2}-s_{1} &=c_{p} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-R \ln \frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}} \\\quad\\&=1.00 \frac{\mathrm{kJ}}{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}} \times \ln \left(\frac{573}{333}\right)-287 \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}} \times \ln \left(\frac{2.96 \times 10^{5}}{1.35 \times 10^{5}}\right) \times \frac{\mathrm{kJ}}{1000 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m}} \\\quad\\s_{2}-s_{1} &=0.137 \mathrm{~kJ} /(\mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K})\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad s_{2}-s_{1}}\end{aligned}\)

 

Finally, check the effect on \(p_{0}\). From the isentropic-stagnation function (Eq. 12.21a), at \(M=2.0\),

\(\frac{p_{0}}{p}=\left[1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\right]^{k /(k-1)}\) (12.21a)

\(\frac{p}{p_{0}}=\frac{p_{1}}{p_{0_{1}}}=0.1278 ; \quad p_{0_{1}}=\frac{p_{1}}{0.1278}=\frac{135 \mathrm{kPa}}{0.1278}=1.06 \mathrm{MPa}(\mathrm{abs})\)

and at \(M=1.2\),

\(\frac{p}{p_{0}}=\frac{p_{2}}{p_{0_{2}}}=0.4124 ; \quad\quad p_{0_{2}}=\frac{p_{2}}{0.4124}=\frac{296 \mathrm{kPa}}{0.4124}=718 \mathrm{kPa}(\mathrm{abs})\quad\quad\xleftarrow[]{\quad\quad\quad\quad p_{0_{2}}}\)

 

Thus, \(p_{0_{2}}<p_{0_{1}}\), as expected for a heating process.

The process follows the supersonic branch of a Rayleigh line:

This problem illustrates the use of the Rayleigh-line equations, Eqs. 13.44, for analyzing frictionless flow of an ideal gas in a duct with heat exchange.

\(\begin{gathered}\frac{p}{p^{*}}=\frac{1+k}{1+k M^{2}} \\\quad\\\frac{T}{T^{*}}=\left[M\left(\frac{1+k}{1+k M^{2}}\right)\right]^{2} \\\quad\\\frac{\rho^{*}}{\rho}=\frac{V}{V^{*}}=\frac{M^{2}(1+k)}{1+k M^{2}} \\\quad\\\frac{T_{0}}{T_{0}^{*}}=\frac{2(k+1) M^{2}\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\right)}{\left(1+k M^{2}\right)^{2}}\\\quad\\\frac{p_{0}}{p_{0}^{*}}=\frac{1+k}{1+k M^{2}}\left[\left(\frac{2}{k+1}\right)\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} M^{2}\right)\right]^{k / k-1}\end{gathered}\) (13.44 a, b, c, d, e)
