Guest Question.

Frequency of a Floor System

For the floor system shown in Figure 12-1, calculate the natural frequency of the floor system. The dead load of the floor is 40 psf. The moment of inertia of the beam is \(I_{b}= 371 in.^4\) and the moment of inertia of the girder is \(I_{g} = 500 in.^{4}\) Neglect the column deformation.

Admin
Solution

The free-body diagram of the beam is shown in Figure 12-2.

where

w = (40 psf)(6 ft.) = 240 lb./ft.(20 lb./in.)

\(R = (240 lb./ft.)\left(\frac{20 ft.}{2} \right) = 2400\) lb.

The beam deflection is

\(\Delta =\frac{5wL^{4} }{384EI}\)

 

\(= \frac{(5)(20)(240)^{4} }{(384)(29 \times 10^{6} )(371)}= 0.0803\) in.

The natural frequency of the beam is

\(f_{b}= 0.18\sqrt{\frac{g}{\Delta } }\)

 

\(= 0.18\sqrt{\frac{386}{0.0803 } } = 12.48\) Hz.

Using the beam reactions calculated previously, the free-body diagram of the girder is as shown in Figure 12-3.

the maximum deflection in the girder is

\(\Delta _{g}=\frac{Pa}{24EI}(3L^{2} – 4a^{2} )\)

 

\(= \frac{(2400)(72)}{(24)(29 \times 10^{6} )(500)}[(3)(216)^{2} – (4)(72)^{2} ]= 0.0592\) in.

The natural frequency of the girder is

\(f_{g}=0.18\sqrt{\frac{g}{\Delta } }\)

 

\( =0.18\sqrt{\frac{386}{0.0592} }=14.53\) Hz.

The natural frequency of the combined system is

\(\frac{1}{f _{n}^{2} }= \frac{1}{f _{b}^{2} }+\frac{1}{f _{g}^{2} }\)

 

\( = \frac{1}{(12.48)^{2} }+\frac{1}{(14.53)^{2} }\)

 

\(f _{n}\) = 9.47 Hz.

Alternatively, the natural frequency can be calculated as follows:

\(f _{n} =0.18\sqrt{\frac{g}{\Delta _{b}+\Delta _{g} } }\)

 

\( =0.18\sqrt{\frac{386}{ 0.0803 + 0.0592 } }= 9.47\) Hz.

It should be noted that the above calculation illustrates the application of the natural frequency equations to an isolated floor. The effects of continuity and other factors will be considered later.
