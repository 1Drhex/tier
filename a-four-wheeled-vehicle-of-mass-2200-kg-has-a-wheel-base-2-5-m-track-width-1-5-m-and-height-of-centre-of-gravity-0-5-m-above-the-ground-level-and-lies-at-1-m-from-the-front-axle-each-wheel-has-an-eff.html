Guest Question.

A four wheeled vehicle of mass 2200 kg has a wheel base 2.5 m, track width 1.5 m and height of centre of gravity 0.5 m above the ground level and lies at 1 m from the front axle. Each wheel has an effective diameter of 0.8 m and a moment of inertia of 0.85 kg.mÂ². The drive shaft, engine flywheel and transmission are rotating at 4 times the speed of road wheel, in a clockwise direction when viewed from the front, and is equivalent to a mass of 80 kg having a radius of gyration of 0.1 m. If the vehicle is taking a right turn of 60 m radius at 60 km/h, calculate the load on each wheel.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given: } M=2200 kg , l=2.5 m , a=1.5 m , h=0.5 m , l_{1}=1 m , r_{w}=0.4 m , I_{w}=0.85 kg \cdot m ^{2}, i=4 \).

\( M_{e}=80 kg , K_{e}=0.1 m , R=60 m , v=\frac{60 \times 10^{3}}{3600}=16.67 m / s \).

\( W=M g=2200 \times 9.81=21582 N \).

\( W_{f}=\frac{21582 \times 1.5}{2.5}=12949.2 N \).

\( W_{r}=21582-12949.2=8632.8 N \).

Weight on each front wheel \( =\frac{W_{f}}{2}=\frac{12949.2}{2}=6474.6 N \).

Weight on each rear wheel \( =\frac{W_{r}}{2}=\frac{8632.8}{2}=4316.4 N \).

\( \omega_{w}=\frac{v}{r_{w}}=\frac{16.67}{0.4}=41.675 rad / s \).

\( \omega_{p}=\frac{v}{R}=\frac{16.67}{60}=0.278 rad / s \).

\( \text { Gyro-couple due to four wheels, } C_{w}=4 I_{w} \omega_{w} \omega_{p} \).

\( =4 \times 0.85 \times 41.675 \times 0.278=39.39 Nm \).

\( I_{e}=M_{e} K_{e}^{2}=80 \times(0.1)^{2}=0.8 kg \cdot m ^{2} \).

\( \text { Gyro-couple due to engine etc., } C=I \omega \cdot \omega=I \times i \omega \times \omega \).

\( =0.8 \times 4 \times 41.675 \times 0.278=37.07 Nm \).

The gyro-couple due to wheels tends to lift the inner wheels and to press the outer wheels. The reaction will be vertically downwards on the inner wheels and vertically upwards on the outer wheels.

Reaction at each of the inner and outer wheel, \( \frac{P}{2}=\frac{C_{w}}{2 a}=\frac{39.39}{2 \times 1.5}=13.13 N \).

The gyro-couple due to engine etc. tends to lift the front wheels and to press the rear wheels. The reaction will be vertically downwards on the front wheels and vertically upwards on the rear wheels.

\( \frac{F}{2}=\frac{C_{e}}{2 l}=\frac{37.07}{2 \times 2.5}=7.41 N \).

\( \text { Centrifugal force, } F_{c}=\frac{M v^{2}}{R}=\frac{2200 \times(16.67)^{2}}{60}=10189 N \).

\( \text { Centrifugal couple, } C_{c}=F_{c} \times h=10189 \times 0.5=5094.6 Nm \).

The centrifugal couple tends to reduce the pressure on the inner wheels and to increase on the outer wheels. The reactions are vertically downwards on the inner wheels and vertically upwards on the outer wheels.

\( \frac{Q}{2}=\frac{C_{c}}{2 a}=\frac{5094.6}{2 \times 1.5}=1698.2 N \).

The forces acting on the wheels are shown in Fig.13.22.

\( \text { Load on front wheel } 1=\frac{W_{f}}{2}-\frac{P}{2}-\frac{F}{2}-\frac{Q}{2} \).

\( =6474.6-13.13-7.41-1698.2=4755.86 N \).

\( \text { Load on front wheel } 2=\frac{W_{f}}{2}+\frac{P}{2}-\frac{F}{2}+\frac{Q}{2} \).

\( =6474.6+13.13-7.41+1698.2=8178.52 N \).

\( \text { Load on rear wheel } 3=\frac{W_{r}}{2}-\frac{P}{2}+\frac{F}{2}-\frac{Q}{2} \).

\( =4316.4-13.13+7.41-1698.2=2612.48 N \).

\( \text { Load on rear wheel } 4=\frac{W_{r}}{2}+\frac{P}{2}+\frac{F}{2}+\frac{Q}{2} \).

\( =4316.4+13.13+7.41+1698.2=6035.14 N \).
