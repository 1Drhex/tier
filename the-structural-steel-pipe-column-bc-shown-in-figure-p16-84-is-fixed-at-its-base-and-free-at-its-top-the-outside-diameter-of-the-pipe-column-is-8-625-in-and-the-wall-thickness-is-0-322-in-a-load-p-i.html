Guest Question.

The structural steel pipe column BC shown in Figure P16.84 is fixed at its base and free at its top. The outside diameter of the pipe column is 8.625 in. and the wall thickness is 0.322 in. A load P is applied to beam AB, which is connected to the upper end of the column. Use the AISC equations given in Section 16.5 and assume that E = 29,000 ksi, \(\sigma_{Y}\) = 36 ksi, and \(\left(\sigma_{\text {allow }}\right)_{b}\) = 24 ksi. Using the interaction equation method, determine:
(a) whether column BC is safe for a load of P = 2.5 kips. Report the value of the interaction equation.
(b) the magnitude of the largest load P that may be applied to the column.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{array}{ll}A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[(8.625 \text { in. })^{2}-(7.981 \text { in. })^{2}\right]=8.399255 in .^{2} \quad d=8.625 \text { in. }-2(0.322 \text { in. })=7.981 \text { in. } \\I=\frac{\pi}{64}\left[(8.625 \text { in. })^{4}-(7.981 \text { in. })^{4}\right]=72.489241 \text { in. }^{4} \quad r=\sqrt{\frac{72.489241 in .^{4}}{8.399255 in .^{2}}}=2.937760 \text { in. }\end{array}\)

Limiting slenderness ratio

\(\begin{aligned}&4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{Y}}}=4.71 \sqrt{\frac{29,000 ksi }{36 ksi }}=133.681 \\&\frac{K L}{r}=\frac{(2)(16 ft )(12 in . / ft )}{2.937760 in .}=130.712\end{aligned}\)

Elastic critical buckling stress

\(\sigma_{e}=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{\left(\frac{K L}{r}\right)^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(29,000 ksi )}{(130.712)^{2}}=16.752 ksi\)

Critical and allowable buckling stresses

Since \(\frac{K L}{r} \leq 4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{y}}}\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{c r}=\left[0.658^{\left(\frac{\sigma_{Y}}{\sigma_{e}}\right)}\right] \sigma_{Y}=\left[0.658^{\left(\frac{36 ksi }{16.752 ksi }\right)}\right](36 ksi )=14.644 ksi \\&\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\sigma_{c r}}{1.67}=\frac{14.644 ksi }{1.67}=8.769 ksi\end{aligned}\)

(a) Interaction method for P = 2.5 kips:

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{\frac{P}{A}}{\left(\sigma_{\text {allow }}\right)_{a}}+\frac{\frac{M c}{I}}{\left(\sigma_{\text {allow }}\right)_{b}}&=\frac{2.5 kips }{8.399255 in. ^{2}}+\frac{(2.5 kips )(10 ft )(12 in . / ft )(8.625 in. / 2)}{72.489241 in .{ }^{4}} \\&=\frac{0.298 ksi }{8.769 ksi }+\frac{17.847 ksi }{24 ksi }=0.778 \quad \therefore Safe\end{aligned}\)

(b) Determine largest P:

\(\frac{\frac{P}{A}}{\left(\sigma_{\text {allow }}\right)_{a}}+\frac{\frac{M c}{I}}{\left(\sigma_{\text {allow }}\right)_{b}}=P\left[\frac{\frac{1}{8.399255 in. ^{2}}}{8.769 ksi }+\frac{\frac{(10 ft )(12 in . / ft )(8.625 in . / 2)}{72.489241 in .^{4}}}{24 ksi }\right] \leq 1\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&P\left[\frac{1}{73.653946 kips }+\frac{1}{3.361820 kips }\right] \leq 1 \\&\therefore P \leq 3.2151 kips =3.22 kips\end{aligned}\)
