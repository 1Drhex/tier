Guest Question.

A concrete sidewalk \((k = 0.604 {BTU}/{(hr · ft · °R)})\) is receiving heat from the ground. During nighttime the sidewalk loses heat by radiation to space and by convection to the surroundings. Ground temperature is such that the temperature at the bottom of the cement sidewalk is \(80°F\). Ambient air is at \(20°F\). For the system as sketched in Figure 1.12, determine the upper-surface temperature of the sidewalk if steady-state conditions exist.

Admin
Solution

An energy balance performed for the sidewalk would include

Heat flow in = energy stored + heat flow out

With no energy stored, we have

\(q_{z}=q_{c}+ q_{r}\)

where \(q_{z}\) is the heat conducted into the sidewalk, and \(q_{c}\) and \(q_{r}\) combined represent the heat flowing
out by convection and radiation, respectively.

Assumptions

1. One-dimensional conduction exists through the material.
2. The convective heat-transfer coefficient is a constant and applies to the entire surface.
3. The shape factor between the surface and space is \(1.0\) because all energy radiated is intercepted
in space.
4. The space temperature is \(0°R\).

Evaluating each term in the heat-balance equation brings

\(q_{z}=\frac{KA(T_{1}-T_{w})}{L}\)

\(q_{c}=\bar{h} _{c}A(T_{w}-T_{\infty })\)

\(q_{r}=\sigma AF_{w-r}\varepsilon _{w}(T^{4}_{w}-T^{4}_{r})\)

Substituting, we obtain

\(\frac{KA}{L} (T_{1}-T_{w})=\bar{h} _{c}A(T_{w}-T_{\infty })+ \sigma AF_{w-r}\varepsilon _{w}(T^{4}_{w}-T^{4}_{r})\)

 

Dividing area out and rearranging gives

\(\frac{K}{L} T_{w}+ \bar{h} _{c}T_{w}+ \sigma F_{w-r}\varepsilon _{w}T^{4}_{w}=\frac{K}{L} T_{1}+ \bar{h} _{c}T_{\infty }+ \sigma F_{w-r}\varepsilon _{w}T^{4}_{r}\)

We now evaluate each variable. From Table 1.2

The shape factor we take as \(F_{w−r} = 1\), assuming that all energy leaving by radiation is intercepted in space.

The Stefan–Boltzmann constant is \(0.1714 × 10^{–8} {BTU}/{(hr · ft 2 · °R)}\).

Physical parameters—\(L = {4}/{12} = 0.333 ft\) , \(T_{1} = 80 + 460 = 540°R\), \(T_{\infty }= 20 + 460 = 480°R\), and
\(T_{r} = 0°R\).

Substituting gives

\(\frac{0.604}{0.333} T_{w}+3.0T_{w}+\frac{0.1714}{10^8} (1)(0.93)T^{4}_{w}=\frac{0.604}{0.333} (540)+3.0(480)+0\)

Simplifying, we get

\(4.814T_{w}+\frac{0.1594}{10^8} T^{4}_{w}=2419\)
