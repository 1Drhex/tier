Guest Question.

A consulting engineer is currently evaluating four different projects for the Department of Housing and Urban Development. The future worth of costs, benefits, disbenefits, and cost savings is shown. The interest rate is \(10\)% per year, compounded continuously. Determine which of the projects, if any, should be selected, if the projects are \(( a )\) independent and \(( b )\) mutually exclusive.

Project ID
Good Better Best Best of All
FW of fi rst costs, $ 10,000 8,000 20,000 14,000
FW of benefi ts, $ 15,000 11,000 25,000 42,000
FW of disbenefi ts, $ 6,000 1,000 20,000 32,000
FW of cost savings, $ 1,500 2,000 16,000 3,000
Admin
Solution

\((a)\) \(B/{ C }_{ Good } = (15,000 – 6,000)/(10,000 – 1,500)\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad\quad \quad \quad = 1.06\)

 

\(B/{ C }_{ Better } = (11,000 – 1,000)/(8,000 – 2,000)\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad\quad = 1.67\)

 

\(B/{ C }_{ Best } = (25,000 – 20,000)/(20,000 – 16,000)\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad \quad = 1.25\)

 

\(B/{ C }_{ Best of all } = (42,000 – 32,000)/(14,000 – 3,000)\)

 

\(\quad \quad \quad \quad\quad = 0.91\)

 

Select Good, Better, and Best

 

\((b)\) Rank acceptable alternatives in terms of increasing \(FW\) of net cost

 

Best: \(20,000 – 16,000 =\) $\(4,000\)

 

Better: \(8,000 – 2,000 =\) $\(6,000\)

 

Good: \(10,000 – 1,500 =\) $\(8,500\)

 

Better vs. Best:

\(\Delta B/C = [(11,000 – 1,000) – (25,000 – 20,000)]/(6,000 – 4,000)\)

 

\( \quad \quad \quad \quad = 2.5 \quad \quad \) Eliminate Best

 

Good vs. Better:

\(\Delta B/C = [(15,000 – 6,000) – (11,000 – 1,000)]/(8,500 – 6,000)\)

\(< 0 \quad \quad\) Eliminate Good

Select Better
