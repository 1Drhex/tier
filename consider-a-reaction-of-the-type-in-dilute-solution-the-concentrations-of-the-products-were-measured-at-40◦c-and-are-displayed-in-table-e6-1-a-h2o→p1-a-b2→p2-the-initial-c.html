Consider a reaction of the type in dilute solution. The concentrations of the products were measured at 40◦C and are displayed in Table E6.1.
\(A + H_{2}O→P_{1}\)
\(A + B_{2}→P_{2}\)
The initial concentrations of A and \(B_{2}\) are 1 mole/L. Determine the reaction rate constants \(k_{1}\) and \(k_{2}\).

Table E6.1

43 19.4 7.6 2.9 1.41 t(min)
0.49 0.46 0.35 0.2 0.117 \([p_{2}]\) (mol/L)

Solution

The data allow us to calculate \(\kappa\) through Equation 6.60:

\(k=R\frac{In(1-\frac{\varphi p_{2}}{N})}{(\varphi p_{2}-1)}\) (6.60)

The concentration of \([P_{2}]\) for \(t\rightarrow \infty\) will be in the dimensionless form as follows:
\(\varphi _{P_{2}}=0.5\)
N=1
R=1

Thus, by using Equation 6.60:
\(\kappa =1.42\)

Integrating Equation 6.61 ,we have,

\(C_{A0^{\theta }}=-\int_{0}^{\varphi _{B}}\frac{d\varphi _{B_{2}}}{k\varphi B_{2}[(1-N)+\varphi B2+(R/k1)In(\varphi B2/N)]}\) (6.61)
\(C_{A0}.k_{1}=2.57\)
\(k_{1}=2.57min^{-1}\)
\(k_{2}=\kappa k_{1}=3.65L\left ( mol^{-1}min^{-1} \right )\)
