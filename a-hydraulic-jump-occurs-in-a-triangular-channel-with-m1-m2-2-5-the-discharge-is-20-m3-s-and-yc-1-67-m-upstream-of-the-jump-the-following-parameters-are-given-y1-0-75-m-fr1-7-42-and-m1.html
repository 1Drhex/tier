Guest Question.

A hydraulic jump occurs in a triangular channel with \(m_{1} = m_{2} = 2.5\). The discharge is \(20 m^3/s\) and \(y_{c} = 1.67 m\). Upstream of the jump the following parameters are given: \(y_{1} = 0.75 m\), \(F_{r_{1}} = 7.42 \), and \(M_{1} = 29.35 m^3\). Determine the conjugate depth \(y_{2}\) downstream of the jump.

Admin
Solution

Use Eq. 10.5.16 with \(F=0\) :

\(\frac{y_{2}^{2}}{6}\left(2 m y_{2}+3 b\right)+\frac{Q^{2}}{g\left(b y_{2}+m y_{2}^{2}\right)}-M_{1}+\frac{F}{\gamma}=0 (10.5.16)\)

\(\frac{y_{2}^{2}}{6} \times 2 \times 2.5 y_{2}+\frac{20^{2}}{9.81 \times 2.5 y_{2}^{2}}-29.35=0\)

The relation reduces to

\(f\left(y_{2}\right)=y_{2}^{3}+\frac{19.58}{y_{2}^{2}}-35.23=0\)

The false position method is chosen to find \(y_{2}\). The first step is to set the upper and lower limits, termed \(y_{u}\) and \(y_{l}\). Since \(\mathrm{Fr}_{1}>1\), and \(y_{2}>y_{c}\), an appropriate lower limit is \(y_{l}=y_{c}=1.67 \mathrm{~m} .\) The upper limit is assumed to be \(5 \mathrm{~m}\)

The solution is tabulated above. In each iteration a new estimate \(y_{r}\) of the root is made:

\(y_{r}=\frac{y_{u} f\left(y_{l}\right)-y_{l} f\left(y_{u}\right)}{f\left(y_{l}\right)-f\left(y_{u}\right)}\)

The product \(f\left(y_{l}\right) \times f\left(y_{r}\right)\) is formed to determine on which subinterval the root will be found. If \(f\left(y_{l}\right) \times f\left(y_{r}\right)<0\), then \(y_{u}=y_{r} ;\) otherwise, \(y_{l}=y_{r} .\) It is required that initially \(f\left(y_{u}\right)\) and \(f\left(y_{l}\right)\) be of opposite sign. Iterations continue until a relative error \(\varepsilon\), defined by

\(\varepsilon=\left|\frac{y_{r}^{\text {new }}-y_{r}^{\text {old }}}{y_{r}^{\text {old }}}\right|\)

is less than a specified value, which in the example is \(0.0005 .\) The result after nine iterations is \(y_{2}=3.22 \mathrm{~m}\), rounded off to three significant figures. Using Mathcad or MaTLAB, one finds the solution to be less time consuming; see Appendix E, Figs. E.2 and E.3.
