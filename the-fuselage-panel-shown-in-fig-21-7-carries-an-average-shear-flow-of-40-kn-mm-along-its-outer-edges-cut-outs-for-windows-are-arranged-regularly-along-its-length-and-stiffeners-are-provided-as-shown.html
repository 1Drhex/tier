Guest Question.

The fuselage panel shown in Fig. 21.7 carries an average shear flow of 40 kN/mm along its outer edges. Cut-outs for windows are arranged regularly along its length and stiffeners are provided as shown. Determine the shear flows in the panels and the distribution of axial load along the stiffeners bordering a cut-out.

Admin
Solution

In this example the shear flows in the outer shear panels cannot be assumed to be equal to the externally applied shear flow since there are no intermediate frames and stiffeners between the cut-outs and the outer edges of the panel. Again we consider the equilibrium of a series of free-body diagrams. From Fig. 21.8(a) and resolving forces horizontally

 

\(600 q_{1}=900 q_{ av }\)

 

so that

 

\(\left.q_{1}=1.5 \times 40=60 kN / m \quad \text { (or } N / mm \right)\)

 

From Fig. 21.8(b) and resolving forces vertically

 

\(2 q_{2} \times 600=q_{ av } \times 1500\)

 

which gives

 

\(\left.q_{2}=1.25 \times 40=50 kN / m \quad \text { (or } N / mm \right)\)

 

From Fig. 21.8(c) and resolving forces vertically

 

\(2 \times 600 q_{3}+300 q_{1}=1500 q_{ av }\)

 

from which

 

\(\left.q_{3}=35 kN / m \quad \text { (or } N / mm \right)\)

 

From Fig. 21.8(d) and resolving forces horizontally

 

\(2 q_{5} \times 600+600 q_{1}=2400 q_{\text {av }}\)

 

so that

 

\(1200 q_{5}+600 \times 60=2400 \times 40\)

 

which gives

 

\(q_{5}=50 kN / mm \quad(\text { or } N / mm )\)

 

Now from Fig. 21.8(c) and resolving forces vertically

 

\(2 q_{4} \times 600+300 q_{5}=1500 q_{ av }\)

 

or

 

\(1200 q_{4}+300 \times 50=1500 \times 40\)

 

from which

 

\(\left.q_{4}=37.5 kN / m \quad \text { (or } N / mm \right)\)

 

The shear flows acting on the stringer 7 8 9 10 11 12 are shown in Fig. 21.9(a) and the variation of axial load follows from simple statics. For example, at 8

 

\(\text { Axial load }=50 \times 0.6-37.5 \times 0.6=7.5 kN\)

 

and so on. The complete distribution is shown in Fig. 21.9(b).
