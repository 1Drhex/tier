Guest Question.

It is desired to bias the amplifier stage of the previous example for zero drain-current drift. If Rd = 10 K, find (a) ID for zero drift, (b) VGS, (c) Rs, (d) the voltage gain, with Rs bypassed with a very large capacitance Cs.

Admin
Solution

(a) From Eq. (12.39), \( I_{D} = I_{DSS}\left\lgroup\frac{0.63}{V_{P}} \right\rgroup ^{2}\) ,

\( I_{D} = 1.65\left\lgroup\frac{0.63}{2} \right\rgroup ^{2}=0.165 mA =165\mu A\)

(b) From Eq. (12.38), \( |V_{P}|-|V_{GS}|=0.63\) V,

VGS = – 1.37 V

(c) Since VGS = − IdRs ,

\( R_{s} = \left\lgroup\frac{1.37}{0.165} \right\rgroup K=8.3 K\)

(d) From (12.40), \( g_{m}=g_{mo}\frac{0.63}{|V_{P}|} \) , we have

\( g_{m} = 1.60\left\lgroup\frac{0.63}{2} \right\rgroup =0.50 \) mA/V

Hence \(A_{V}\approx g_{m}R_{d}=0.50\times 10=5.0 \) .

We thus see that zero drift has been obtained at the expense of gm and voltage gain, which are now one-half their values in the previous example.
