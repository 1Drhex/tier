Guest Question.

The power plant using ammonia in has a flow of liquid water at 120°C, 300 kPa as a heat source, the water leaves the heat exchanger at 90°C. Find the second law efficiency of this heat exchanger.

Admin
Solution

C.V. The liquid water source – ammonia boiler heat exchanger.

\(\begin{aligned}&\Delta h _{ liq }=503.69-376.9=126.79 kJ / kg \quad \text { (sat. liquid at } 120^{\circ} C \text { and } 90^{\circ} C \text { ) }\\&\Delta s _{ liq }=1.5275-1.1924=0.3351 kJ / kgK\end{aligned}\)

The energy equation establishes the ratio of the mass flow rates

\(\dot{ Q }_{ H }=\dot{ m }_{\text {water }} \Delta h _{\text {liq }}=\dot{ m }_{ NH 3} q _{ H }\)

 

\(\Rightarrow \dot{ m }_{\text {water }} / \dot{ m }_{ NH 3}= q _{ H } / \Delta h _{ liq }=1442 / 126.79=11.373\)

 

Now the second law efficiency is the ratio of exergy pick-up over exergy source

\(\eta_{I I}=\frac{\dot{m}_{ NH 3} \Delta \psi}{\dot{ m }_{\text {water }} \Delta \psi}=\frac{\dot{ m }_{ NH 3}\left[ q _{ H }- T _{ o }\left( s _{5}- s _{3}\right)\right]}{\dot{ m }_{\text {water }}\left(\Delta h _{ liq }- T _{ o } \Delta s _{ liq }\right)}\)

From the power plant cycle we have state 3:

State 3: \(x _{3}=0, h _{3}=171.65 kJ / kg , s _{3}=0.6793 kJ / kgK , v _{3}=0.00156 m ^{3} / kg\)

State 5: \(h _{5}=1614.6 kJ / kg , s _{5}=5.4971 kJ / kg K\)

C.V Pump P2: \(w _{ P 2}= h _{4}- h _{3}= v _{3}\left( P _{4}- P _{3}\right)=0.00156(1000-400)=0.936 kJ / kg\)

\(\begin{gathered}q _{ H }= h _{5}- h _{4}=1614.6-(171.65+0.936)=1442 kJ / kg \\s _{5}- s _{3}=5.4971-0.6793=4.81775 kJ / kgK\end{gathered}\)

 

\(\eta_{ II }=\frac{1}{11.373} \frac{1442-253.15 \times 4.81775}{126.79-253.15 \times 0.3351}= 0 . 4 6 6\)
