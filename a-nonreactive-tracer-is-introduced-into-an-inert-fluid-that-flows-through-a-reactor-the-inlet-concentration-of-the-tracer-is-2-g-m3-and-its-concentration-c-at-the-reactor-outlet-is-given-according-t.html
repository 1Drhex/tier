Guest Question.

A nonreactive tracer is introduced into an inert fluid that flows through a reactor. The inlet concentration of the tracer is 2 \(g/m^{3}\) and its concentration C at the reactor outlet is given according to the following table:

t (min) 0.1 0.2 1 2 5 10 20 30
C\((g/m^{3})\) 1.96 1.93 1.642 1.344 0.736 0.286 0.034 0.004

The reactor volume is 1 \(m^{3}\) and the feed volumetric flow is 0.2 \(m^{3}/min\). Determine the cumulative residence time distribution function (F) and the mean residence time.

Admin
Solution

Space time:
\(\tau =\frac{V}{\nu _{0}}(h)=\frac{1}{0.2}=5 min\)

Calculation the function F:
\(F(t)=\frac{G(t)-G_{1}}{(G_{2}-G_{1})}=\frac{2-C(t)}{2}\)

t (min) 0.1 0.2 1 2 5 10 20 30
C \((g/m^{3})\) 1.96 1.93 1.642 1.344 0.736 0.286 0.034 0.004
F(t) 0.02 0.035 0.179 0.328 0.632 0.866 0.983 0.088

The mean residence time can be determined by the graph assuming equal areas \(A_{1}\) and \(A_{2}\) according to the dashed line corresponding to t =5 min. The cumulative distribution function as a function of time is shown in the above table. Longer times indicate a fraction of molecules that leaves the reactor at a time longer than the mean residence time.
