Guest Question.

A rope without any bending stiffness (mass \(m\), length \(l\)), moves freely on a frictionless plane, such that its bend \(K\) changes its position continuously (similar as it does at a horsewhip). In the initial state, \(K\) is in the middle of the rope; the lower part ① has the velocity \(v_{10}=v_{0}\) and the upper part ② the velocity \(v_{20} = 0\).

a) Determine the velocities \(v_{2}\) and \(v_{1}\) in dependence on the distance u of the ends of the rope.

b) What are the velocities of the parts ① and ② at the instant when the bend \(K\) passes the end of the rope? Sketch the functions \(v_{1}\left(u\right) \) and \(v_{2}\left(u\right) \).

Admin
Solution

Because no external forces are acting, the linear momentum and the total energy at initial state must be conserved. Thus, with the lengths \(\left(l+u\right)/ 2 \) and \(\left(l-u\right)/ 2 \)of the parts ① and ② of the rope, it follows

momentum conservation: \(\frac{m}{2}v_{0}=\frac{m}{2}\frac{l+u}{l}v_{1}+ \frac{m}{2}\frac{l-u}{l}v_{2} \) ,

energy conservation: \(\frac{1}{2} \frac{m}{2} v^{2} _{0}=\frac{1}{2}\frac{m}{2}\frac{l+u}{l}v^{2} _{1}+ \frac{1}{2} \frac{m}{2}\frac{l-u}{l}v^{2} _{2} \)

respectively with \(y=u/l \)

 

\(v_{0}=\left(1+y\right)v_{1}+\left(1-y\right)v_{2}\) , \((a)\)

 

\(v^{2} _{0}=\left(1+y\right)v^{2} _{1}+\left(1-y\right)v^{2} _{2}\) .

Solving for \(v_{2}\) leads to the quadratic equation

\(2y\left(1-y\right)v^{2}_{2}-2\left(1-y\right)v_{0}v_{2}-yv^{2}_{0}=0 \)

with the solution

\(v_{2}=\frac{v_{0} }{2y}\left[1\pm \sqrt{1+\frac{2y^{2} }{1-y} } \right]\) .

Introducing this result into \((a)\) , yields

\(v_{1}=\frac{v_{0} }{2y\left(1+y\right) }\left[3y-1\mp \left(1-y\right)\sqrt{1+\frac{2y^{2} }{1-y} } \right] \) .

To find the correct sign in front of the root, we consider the initial state \(v_{1}=v_{0} \) , \(v_{2}=0\) By determining the limit values for \(y\longrightarrow 0\) it can be seen that the ’lower’ sign leads to the correct initial state. Thus, the solution reads

\(\underline{\underline{v_{1}=\frac{v_{0} }{2y\left(1+y\right) }\left[3y-1+ \left(1-y\right)\sqrt{1+\frac{2y^{2} }{1-y} } \right] } } \) ,

\(\underline{\underline{v_{2}=\frac{v_{0} }{2y}\left[1-\sqrt{1+\frac{2y^{2} }{1-y} } \right] } }\) .

b) The bend \(K\) reaches the end of the rope for \(u\longrightarrow l\) or for \(y\longrightarrow 1 \) , respectively. Determining the limit values leads to the velocities

\(\underline{\underline{\underset{y\rightarrow 1 }{\lim}v_{1}\left(y\right)=\frac{v_{0} }{2} } } \) , \(\underline{\underline{\underset{y\rightarrow 1 }{\lim}v_{2}\left(y\right)=-\infty }}\) .

c) In order to sketch \(v_{1}\left(y\right)\) and \(v_{2}\left(y\right)\) , it is advisable first to approximate the functions near \(y = 0\) and \(y = 1\) . By series expansion and neglecting

higher order terms, we find

\(y\ll 1 :v_{1}\left(y\right)\approx v_{0}\left(1-y/2\right) \) , \(v_{2}\left(y\right)\approx -v_{0}y/2\) ,

\(\left(1-y\right) \ll 1 : v_{1}\left(y\right)\approx \frac{v_{0} }{2}\left(1+\frac{\sqrt{1-y} }{\sqrt{2} } \right)\) , \(v_{2}\left(y\right)\approx -\frac{v_{0} }{2}\frac{\sqrt{2} }{\sqrt{1-y} }\) .
