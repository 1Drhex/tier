Guest Question.

An axial-flow ventilating fan driven by a motor that delivers 0.4 kW of power to the fan blades produces a 0.6-mdiameter axial stream of air having a speed of 12 m/s. The flow upstream of the fan involves negligible speed.

FIND Determine how much of the work to the air actually produces a useful effect, that is, a rise in available energy, and estimate the fluid mechanical efficiency of this fan.

Admin
Solution

We select a fixed and nondeforming control volume as is illustrated in Fig. E5.15. The application of \(\frac{p_{out}}{\rho } +\frac{V^{2}_{out} }{2}+gz_{out}= \frac{p_{in}}{\rho } +\frac{V^{2}_{in} }{2}+gz_{in}+W_{shaft net in}-loss\) to the contents of this control volume leads to

\(\frac{p_{2}}{\rho }=\frac{p_{1}}{\rho }=0\) (atmospheric pressures cancel)

\( gz_{2}=gz_{1}=0\)( no elevation change )

\(\frac{V^{2}_{1}}{2}=0\) (\(V_{1}\approx 0 \))

\(w_{shaft net in}-loss=(\frac{p_{2}}{\rho }+\frac{V^{2}_{2}}{2}+gz_{2} )-(\frac{p_{1}}{\rho }+\frac{V^{2}_{1}}{2}+gz_{1} )\) (1)

where \(w_{shaft net in}-loss\) is the amount of work added to the air that produces a useful effect. Equation 1 leads to

\(w_{shaft net in}-loss=\frac{V^{2}_{2} }{2}=\frac{(12 {m}/{s})^{2}}{2[1{(kg\cdot m)}/{(N\cdot s^{2})}]} =72.0{N\cdot m}/{kg}\) (2)

A reasonable estimate of efficiency, \(\eta \), would be the ratio of amount of work that produces a useful effect, Eq. 2, to the amount of work delivered to the fan blades. That is,

\(\eta =\frac{W_{shaft net in}-loss}{W_{shaft net in}} \) (3)

To calculate the efficiency, we need a value of \(w_{shaft net in}\), which is related to the power delivered to the blades, \(\dot{W}_{shaft net in} \). We note that

\(w_{shaft net in}=\frac{\dot{W}_{shaft net in} }{\dot{m} } \) (4)

where the mass flowrate, \(\dot{m}\) is (from \( \dot{m} =\rho AV=\rho Q\))

\(\dot{m}=\rho AV=\rho \frac{\pi D^{2}_{2} }{4} V_{2}\) (5)

For fluid density,\(\rho \), we use \(1.23{kg}/{m^{3}}\) (standard air) and, thus, from Eqs. 4 and 5 we obtain

\(w_{shaft net in}=\frac{\dot{W}_{shaft net in} }{({\rho \pi D^{2}_{2} }/{4})V_{2}} =\frac{(0.4 kW)[1000{(N\cdot m)}/{(s\cdot kW)}]}{(1.23{kg}/{m^{3}})[{(\pi )(0.6 m)^{2}}/{4}](12{m}/{s})} \)

or

\( w_{shaft net in}=95.8{N\cdot m}/{kg}\) (6)

From Eqs. 2, 3, and 6 we obtain

\(\eta =\frac{72.0{N\cdot m}/{kg}}{95.8{N\cdot m}/{kg}} =0.752\)

COMMENT Note that only 75% of the power that was delivered to the air resulted in a useful effect and, thus, 25% of the shaft power is lost to air friction.
