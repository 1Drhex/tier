Guest Question.

American Vending Inc. (AVI) supplies vended food to a large university. Because students often kick the machines out of anger and frustration, management has a constant repair problem. The machines break down on an average of three per hour, and the breakdowns are distributed in a Poisson manner. Downtime costs the company $25 per hour per machine, and each maintenance worker gets $16 per hour. One worker can service machines at an average rate of five per hour, distributed exponentially; two workers working together can service seven per hour, distributed exponentially; and a team of three workers can do eight per hour, distributed exponentially.
What is the optimal maintenance crew size for servicing the machines?

Admin
Solution

Case I—One worker:
λ = 3/hour Poisson, μ = 5/hour exponential
The average number of machines in the system is

\( L_{s} = \frac{\lambda}{\mu – \lambda}=\frac{3}{5-3}= \frac{3}{2}=1 \)½ machines

Downtime cost is $25 × 1.5 = $37.50 per hour; repair cost is $16.00 per hour; and total cost per hour for 1 worker is $37.50 + $16.00 = $53.50.

Downtime cost is 1.5 × $25 = $37.50

Labor (1 worker × $16) = \(\underline{16.00} \\\hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt}\quad\underline{ \underline{\$53.50}} \)

Case II—Two workers:
λ = 3, μ = 7

\( L_{s} = \frac{\lambda}{\mu – \lambda}=\frac{3}{7-3}= 0.75 \) machines

Downtime (0.75 × $25) = $18.75

Labor (2 workers × $16) = \(\underline{32.00} \\ \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt}\quad \underline{\underline{\$50.75} } \)

Case III—Three workers:
λ = 3, μ = 8

\( L_{s} = \frac{\lambda}{\mu – \lambda}=\frac{3}{8-3}=\frac{3}{5} = 0.60 \) machines

Downtime (0.60 × $25) = $15.00

Labor (3 workers × $16)= \(\underline{48.00} \\\hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt}\quad\underline{ \underline{\$63.00}} \)

Comparing the costs for one, two, or three workers, we see that Case II with two workers is the optimal decision.
