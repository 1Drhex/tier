Guest Question.

A box beam is fabricated from two plywood webs that are secured to dimension lumber boards at its top and bottom flanges (Figure P9.47b). The beam supports a concentrated load of P = 5,000 lb at the center of a 15-ft span (Figure P9.47a). Bolts (⅜-in. diameter) connect the plywood webs and the lumber flanges at a spacing of s = 12 in. along the span. Supports A and C can be idealized as a pin and a roller, respectively. Determine:
(a) the maximum horizontal shear stress in the plywood webs.
(b) the average shear stress in the bolts.
(c) the maximum bending stress in the lumber flanges.

Admin
Solution

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

\(\begin{array}{lcccc}{\text { Shape }} & I_{C} & d=y_{i}-\bar{y} & d^{2} A & I_{C}+d^{2} A \\& \left(\text { in. }^{4}\right) & (\text { in. }) & \left(\text { in. }^{4}\right) & \left(\text { in. }^{4}\right) \\\hline \text { left web } & 576 & 0 & 0 & 576 \\\text { top flange } & 16 & 10 & 1,200 & 1,216 \\\text { bottom flange } & 16 & -10 & 1,200 & 1,216 \\\text { right web } & 576 & 0 & 0 & 576 \\\hline {\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)=} 3,584\end{array}\)

 

Maximum shear force:
For P = 5,000 lb, V = P/2 = 2,500 lb

Maximum bending moment:
For P = 5,000 lb, M = PL/4 = 18,750 lb-ft = 225,000 lb-in.

(a) Maximum horizontal shear stress (in plywood webs):

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{\max }=&(3 \text { in. })(4 in. )(10 in .) \\&+2(0.5 in .)(12 in .)(6 \text { in. })=192 in .{ }^{3}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\tau_{\max }=\frac{V Q}{I t}=\frac{(2,500 lb )\left(192 in .^{3}\right)}{\left(3,584 in .^{4}\right)(2 \times 0.5 in .)}=133.9 psi\)

 

(b) Bolt shear stress:
Consider the dimension lumber boards that comprise the top flange.

\(\begin{aligned}&Q_{\text {flange }}=(3 \text { in. })(4 \text { in } .)(10 in .)=120 in .{ }^{3} \\&q=\frac{V Q_{\text {flange }}}{I}=\frac{(2,500 lb )\left(120 in .^{3}\right)}{3,584 in .^{4}}=83.7054 lb / in.\end{aligned}\)

Determine the force carried by one bolt:

\(\begin{aligned}&q s \leq n_{f} V_{f} \\&\therefore V_{f} \geq \frac{q s}{n_{f}}=\frac{(83.7054 lb / in .)(12 in. )}{1 bolt }=1,004.4643 lb / bolt\end{aligned}\)

The bolt cross-sectional area is:

\(A_{\text {bolt }}=\frac{\pi}{4}(0.375 \text { in. })^{2}=0.110447 \text { in. }^{2}\)

Each bolt acts in double shear; therefore, the shear stress in each bolt is:

\(\tau_{ bolt }=\frac{1,004.4643 lb / bolt }{2\left(0.110447 in. ^{2}\right)}=4,547.284 psi =4,550 psi\)

(c) Maximum bending stress in lumber flanges:

\(\sigma=\frac{M c}{I}=\frac{(225,000 lb – in .)(12 in. )}{3,584 in. ^{4}}=753 psi\)
