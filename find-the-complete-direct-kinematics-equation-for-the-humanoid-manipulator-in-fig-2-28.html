Guest Question.

Find the complete direct kinematics equation for the humanoid manipulator in Fig. 2.28

Admin
Solution

The torso can be modelled as an anthropomorphic arm, corresponding to the first three DOFs. Therefore \(T^{0}_{3}\) in (2.78) and (2.79) has the expression (2.66).

The constant matrices \(T^{t}_{l} \) and \(T^{t}_{r}\) can be computed in terms of the angle Î² and of the lengths \(d _{l}\) and \(d _{r}\) of segments \(O _{t} O _{l} \) and \(O _{t} O _{r}\), being \(O _{t} \) , \(O _{l} \) and \(O _{r}\) the origins of frames t, l and r , respectively. In detail

\( T^{0}_{rh} = T^{0}_{3} T^{3}_{t} T^{t}_{r} T^{r}_{rh} \) (2.78)

\(T^{0}_{lh} = T^{0}_{3} T^{3}_{t} T^{t}_{l} T^{l}_{lh}\) (2.79)

\(T^{0}_{3 }\left(q\right)=A^{0}_{1 } A^{1}_{2 } A^{2}_{3 } \) = \(\left[ \begin{matrix} c_{1}c_{23} & -c_{1}s_{23} & s_{1} & c_{1}\left(a_{2} c_{2} + a_{3} c_{23}\right) \\ s_{1}c_{123} & -s_{1}s_{23} & -c_{1} & s_{1}\left(a_{2} c_{2} + a_{3} c_{23}\right) \\ s_{23} & c_{23} & 0 & a_{2} s_{2} + a_{3} s_{23} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right] \) (2.66)

\(T^{t}_{l} = \left[\begin{matrix} R^{t}_{l} & o^{T}_{t,l} \\ 0^{t} & 1 \end{matrix} \right]\) \(T^{t}_{r} = \left[\begin{matrix} R^{t}_{r} & o^{t}_{t,r} \\ 0^{T} & 1 \end{matrix} \right]\)

 

where

\(o^{t}_{t,l} = \left[\begin{matrix} d_{l}c_{\beta } \\ 0 \\d_{l}s_{\beta } \end{matrix} \right]\) \(o^{t}_{t,r} =\left[\begin{matrix} d_{r}c_{\beta } \\ 0 \\-d_{r}s_{\beta } \end{matrix} \right]\)

 

are the positions of the origins of frames l and r with respect to frame t and

\(R^{t}_{l} = \left[\begin{matrix} 0 & -s_{\beta } & c_{\beta } \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & c_{\beta } & s_{\beta } \end{matrix} \right]\) \(R^{t}_{r} = \left[\begin{matrix} 0 & s_{\beta } & c_{\beta } \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & c_{\beta } & -s_{\beta } \end{matrix} \right]\)

 

are the corresponding rotation matrices

Finally, matrices \(T^{r}_{rh}\) = \(T^{l}_{lh}\) can be computed using (2.72) with (2.77) in place of (2.73).

\(A^{i – 1}_{i} = \left[ \begin{matrix} c_{i} & 0 & s_{i} & 0 \\ s_{i} & 0 & -c_{i} & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & d_{i} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right] \) i = 1,…, 6 (2.72)

\(A^{6}_{7} = \left[ \begin{matrix} c_{7} & -s_{7} & 0 & 0 \\ s_{7} & c_{7} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & d_{7} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix}\right]\) (2.73)

\(A^{6}_{7} = \left[ \begin{matrix} c_{7} & 0 & s_{7} & a_{7}c_{7}\\ s_{7} & 0 & -c_{7} & a_{7}s_{7}\\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix}\right] \) (2.77)
