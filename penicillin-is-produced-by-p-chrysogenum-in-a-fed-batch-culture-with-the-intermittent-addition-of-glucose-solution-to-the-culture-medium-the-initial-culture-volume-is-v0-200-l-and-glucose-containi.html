Guest Question.

Penicillin is produced by P. chrysogenum in a fed-batch culture with the intermittent addition of glucose solution to the culture medium. The initial culture volume is \(V _{0}\) = 200 L, and glucose-containing nutrient solution is added with a flow rate of Q = 30 L/h. Glucose concentration in the feed solution, and the initial concentrations in the reactor are:\(S_{ F }=300 g / L , S_{0}=1 g / L\) and \(X_{0}=20 g / L\). The kinetic and yield factors of the organism are: \(\mu_{\max }=0.2 h ^{-1}, K_{ S }=0.5 g / L , \text { and } Y F_{ X / S }\) = 0.3 g-dw/g-glucose.

Using pseudosteady state approximation to determine

a. the culture volume at t = 24 h.
b. the concentration of glucose at t = 24 h.
c. the concentration and total amount of cells when t = 24 h.

d. the product (penicillin) concentration in the vessel at 24 h, if \(\mu_{p}=0.05 \text { g-product/(g-cells.h) and } P_{0}=0.1 g / L\).

Admin
Solution

Initially in the reactor, the amount of glucose, cells, and products are:

\(\begin{aligned}&S_{0} V_{0}=1 \times 200 g =200 g \\&X_{0} V_{0}=20 \times 200 g =4000 g \\&P_{0} V_{0}=0.1 \times 200 g =20 g\end{aligned} \)

(a) Overall mass balance of the medium in the fermentor yields:

\(\rho_{F} Q-0+0=\frac{d(\rho V)}{d t}\)

Assuming that the density of the culture is the same as that of the feed, \(\rho_{F}=\rho \), integration and rearrangement of the overall mass balance equation lead to

\(V=V_{0}+Q_{t}\)

 

\(V =200+30 \times 24 L =920 L\)

(b) At pseudo-steady state, the concentration of cells remain constant. From the mass balance of cells in the reactor, we obtain Eq. (14.119) \( \frac{d X}{d t}=\left\lgroup \mu_{G}-k_{d}-\frac{Q}{V} \right\rgroup X \) ,

\( 0= \frac{d X}{d t}=\left\lgroup \mu_{G}-k_{d}-\frac{Q}{V} \right\rgroup X \)

Combining with the Monod growth equation, the above equation can be rearranged to give Eq. (14.134). When \(k_{ d }\) = 0, it gives the pseudosteady state glucose concentration

\(S=\frac{K_{S} \frac{Q}{V}}{\mu_{\max }-\frac{Q}{V}}\)

Therefore, \(S=\frac{K_{S}}{\mu_{\max } V / Q-1}=\frac{0.5}{0.2 \times(920 \div 30)-1} \frac{ g }{ L }=0.0974 g / L\)

(c) The concentration of cells can be computed using an appropriate equation from Table 14.6, which is derived by mass balance of glucose in the reactor. Alternatively, as there is no cell death, we can directly use yield factor from all the glucose converted in the reactor. The total amount of glucose fed to the reactor is

\(Q S_{F} t=30 \times 300 \times 24 g =216000 g\)

Glucose in the reactor at 24 hours = V S = 920 × 0.09740 g = 89.61 g

Therefore the amount of glucose converted is:

\(S_{0} V_{0}+Q S_{F} t-V S=200+216000-89.61 g =216110.39 g\)

which produces 0.3 \(\left(Y F_{ X / S }\right) \times 216110.39 g =64833.12 g \text { cells. }\)

Therefore, the total cells in the reactor is

\(X V=4000\left(X_{0} V_{0}\right)+64833.12 g =68833.12 g.\)

Finally, the concentration of cells is

\(X=\frac{X V}{V}=\frac{68833.12 g }{920 L }=74.8186 g / L\)

(d) Mass balance on the extracellular products,

\(0-0+r_{P} V-0+0=\frac{d(P V)}{d t}\)

Which leads to

\(\frac{d(P V)}{d t}=\mu_{P} X\left(V_{0}+Q t\right)\)

Thus, upon integration and rearrangement,

\(P=\frac{P_{0} V_{0}+\mu_{P} X\left\lgroup V_{0}+\frac{1}{2} Q t \right\rgroup t}{V}=\frac{P_{0} V_{0}+\frac{1}{2} \mu_{P} X\left(V_{0}+V\right) t}{V}\)

Substituting in the numbers, we have

\(P=\frac{P_{0} V_{0}+\frac{1}{2} \mu_{P} X\left(V_{0}+V\right) t}{V}\)

 

\(=\frac{0.1 \times 200+\frac{1}{2} \times 0.05 \times 74.8186 \times(200+920) \times 24}{920} g / L\)

= 54.67 g/L
