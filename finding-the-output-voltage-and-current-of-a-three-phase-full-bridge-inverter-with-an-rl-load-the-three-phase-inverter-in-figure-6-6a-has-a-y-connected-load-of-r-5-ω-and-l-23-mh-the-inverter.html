Finding the Output Voltage and Current of a Three-Phase Full-Bridge Inverter with an RL load

The three-phase inverter in Figure 6.6a has a Y-connected load of \(R = 5 Ω\) and \(L = 23 mH\). The inverter frequency is \(f_0 = 60 Hz\) and the dc input voltage is \(V_s = 220 V\). (a) Express the instantaneous line-to-line voltage \(v_{ab(t)}\) and line current \(i_a(t)\) in a Fourier series. Determine (b) the rms line voltage \(V_L\), (c) the rms phase voltage \(V_p\), (d) the rms line voltage \(V_{L1}\) at the fundamental frequency, (e) the rms phase voltage at the fundamental frequency \(V_{p1}\), (f) the THD, (g) the DF, (h) the HF and DF of the LOH, (i) the load power \(P_o\), (j) the average transistor current \(I_{Q(av)}\), and (k) the rms transistor current \( I_{Q}(rms)\) .

Admin
Solution

\(V_s = 220 V, R = 5 Ω, L = 23 mH, f_0 = 60 Hz, and ω= 2π ×60 = 377 rad/s\).

a. Using Eq. (6.20a),

\(\upsilon _{ab}=\sum\limits_{n=1,3,5,. . .}^{\infty }{\frac{4V_s}{n\pi }\sin\left(\frac{n\pi }{2} \right)\sin n\left(\omega t+\frac{\pi }{6} \right) }\) (6.20a)

the instantaneous line-to-line voltage \(v_{ab}(t)\) can be written for a positive sequence as

\(v_{ab}(t) = 242.58 \sin(377t + 30º) – 48.52 \sin 5(377t + 30º)- 34.66 \sin 7(377t + 30º) + 22.05 \sin 11(377t + 30º)+ 18.66 \sin 13(377t + 30º) – 14.27 \sin 17(377t + 30º) + . . .\) \(Z_L=\sqrt{R^2+(n\omega L)^2}\angle tan^{-1}(n\omega L/R)=\sqrt{52+(8.7)^2}\angle tan^{-1}(8.67n/5)\)

Using Eq. (6.26),

\(i_0=\sum\limits_{n=1,3,5,. . .}^{\infty }{\left[\frac{4V_s}{\sqrt{3}[n\pi \sqrt{R^2+(n\omega L)^2} ] }\sin\left(\frac{n\pi }{3} \right) \sin\frac{n\pi }{3} \right] }\sin\left[n\left(\omega t+\frac{\pi }{6} \right)\pm \frac{\pi }{6}-\theta _n \right]\) (6.26)

the instantaneous line (or phase) current for a positive sequence is given by

\(i_a(t) = 14 \sin(377t – 60º)- 0.64 \sin(5 ×377t + 36.6º)- 0.33 \sin(7 × 377t + 94.7º) + 0.13 \sin(11 × 377t + 213º) + 0.10 \sin(13 × 377t + 272.5º) – 0.06 \sin(17 × 377t + 391.9º) – . . .\)

b. From Eq. (6.21),

\(V_L=\left[\frac{2}{2\pi} \int_{0}^{2\pi /3}{V_s^2d(\omega t)} \right]^{1/2}= \sqrt{\frac{2}{3} } V_s=0.8165V_s\) (6.21)

\(V_L = 0.8165 ×220 = 179.63 V\).

c. From Eq. (6.24),

\(V_p=\frac{V_L}{\sqrt{3} }=\frac{\sqrt{2}V_s }{3}= 0.4714 V_s\) (6.24)

\(V_P = 0.4714 × 220 = 103.7 V\).

d. From Eq. (6.23),

\(V_{L1}=\frac{4V_s \sin 60°}{\sqrt{2}\pi }=0.7797 V_s\) (6.23)

 

\(V_{L1} = 0.7797 V_s\)

 

\(\left(\sum\limits_{n=5,7,11,. . .}^{\infty }{V_{Ln}^2} \right)^{1/2}=(V^2_L-V^2_{L1})^{1/2}= 0.24236 V_s\)

From Eq. (6.6),

\(THD=\frac{1}{V{o1}}\left(\sum\limits_{n=2,3,. . .}^{\infty }{V^2_{on}} \right)^{1/2}\) (6.6)

\(THD = 0.24236V_s/(0.7797 V_s) = 31.08\%\). The rms harmonic line voltage is

g. \(V_{Lh}=\left[\sum\limits_{n=5,7,11,. . .}^{\infty }{\left(\frac{V_{Ln}}{n^2} \right) }^{2} \right]^{1/2}= 0.00941 V_s\)

From Eq. (6.7),

\(DF=\frac{1}{V_{o1}} \left[\sum\limits_{n=2,3,. . .}^{\infty }{\left(\frac{V_{on}}{n^2} \right) }^{2} \right]^{1/2}\) (6.7)

 

\(DF = 0.00941V_s/(0.7797V_s2)= 1.211\%\).

h. The LOH is the fifth, \(V_{L5} = V_{L1}/5\). From Eq. (6.5),

\(HF_n=\frac{V_{on}}{V_{o1}}\) for \(n \gt 1\) (6.5)

 

\(HF_5 = V_{L5}/V_{L1} = 1/5 = 20\%\),

and from Eq. (6.8),

\(DF_n=\frac{V_{on}}{V_{o1}n^2}\) for \(n \gt 1\)

 

\(DF_5 = (V_{L5}/5^2)/V_{L1} = 1/125 = 0.8\%\).

i. For Y-connected loads, the line current is the same as the phase current and the rms line current,

\(L=\frac{(14^2 + 0.64^2 + 0.33^2 + 0.13^2 + 0.10^2 + 0.06^2)^{1/2}}{\sqrt{2} } =9.91 A\)

The load power \(P_0 = 3I_L^ 2 R = 3 × 9.91^2 × 5 = 1473 W\).

j. The average supply current \(I_s = P_o/220 = 1473/220 = 6.7 A\) and the average transistor current \(I_{Q(av)} = 6.7/3 = 2.23 A\).

k. Because the line current is shared by three transistors, the rms value of a transistor current is \(I_{Q(rms)}=I_L/\sqrt{3}=9.91/\sqrt{3} =5.72 A \)
