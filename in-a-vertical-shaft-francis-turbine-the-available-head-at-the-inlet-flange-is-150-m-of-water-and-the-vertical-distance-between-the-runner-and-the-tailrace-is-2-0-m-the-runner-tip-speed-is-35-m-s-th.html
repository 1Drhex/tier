Guest Question.

In a vertical-shaft Francis turbine, the available head at the inlet flange is 150 m of water and the vertical distance between the runner and the tailrace is 2.0 m. The runner tip speed is 35 m/s, the meridional velocity of the water through the runner is constant at 10.5 m/s, the flow leaves the runner without whirl and the velocity at exit from the draft tube is 3.5 m/s.
The hydraulic losses for the turbine are as follows:
\(\Delta H_{N}=6.0 m , \Delta H_{R}=10 m , \Delta H_{DT}=1.0 m\)
Determine
a. the specific work, Î”W, and the hydraulic efficiency, \(\eta _{h}\), of the turbine;
b. the absolute velocity, \(c _{2}\), at runner entry;
c. the pressure head (relative to the tailrace) at inlet to and exit from the runner;
d. the absolute and relative flow angles at runner inlet; the flow discharged by the turbine is \(20 m^{3}/s \) and the power specific speed is 0.8 (rad), the speed of rotation and diameter of the runner.

Admin
Solution

a. From Eqs (9.18) and (9.20), we can find the specific work:

 

\( g\left( H_{E}-\Delta H_{N}-\Delta H_{R}\right)-\Delta W=\frac{1}{2} c^{2}_{3}+p_{3}/\rho-p_{a}/\rho+gz_{3}\) (9.18)

 

\(p_{3}/\rho + \frac{1}{2} c^{2}_{3} +gz_{3}-g\Delta H_{DT}=\frac{1}{2} c^{2}_{4}+ pa/\rho \) (9.20)

 

\(\Delta W=g\left( H_{E}-\Delta H_{N}-\Delta H_{R}-\Delta H_{DT}\right) -\frac{1}{2} c^{2}_{4} \)

\(=9.81\times \left(150-6-10-1\right) -3.5^{2}/2=1298.6 m^{2}/s^{2} \)

The hydraulic efficiency, \(\eta _{h} =\Delta W/\left(g H_{E}\right) =0.8825 \) .

b. As \(c_{\theta 3} =0 \) , then \(\Delta W=U_{2} c_{\theta 2}\) and \( c_{\theta 2}=\Delta W/U_{2}=1298.6/35=37.1 m/s\) , thus,

\( c_{2}=\sqrt{c^{2}_{\theta 2}+c^{2}_{m}} =\sqrt{37.1^{2}+10.5^{2} }=38.56 m/s \)

c. From Eq. (9.17), the pressure head at inlet to the runner is

\(g\left(H_{E}-\Delta H_{N}\right) =\frac{p_{2}-p_{a}}{\rho } +\frac{1}{2} c^{2}_{2}+gz_{2} \) (9.17)

\(H_{2}=H_{E}-\Delta H_{N}-c^{2}_{2}/\left(2g\right)=150-6-38.56^{2}/\left(2\times 9.81\right)=68.22 m\)

Again, using Eq. (9.20), the pressure head (relative to the tailrace) at runner exit is

\(H_{3}=\left(p_{3}-p_{a}\right)/\left(\rho g\right) =\left(c^{2}_{4}-c^{2}_{3}\right)/ \left(2 g\right)+\Delta H_{DT}-z_{3} =\left(3.5^{2}-10.5^{2}\right) /\left(2\times 9.81\right) +1-2=-6.0 m \)

Note: The minus sign for \(H_{3} \) indicates that the pressure is below the atmospheric level.
This is a matter of considerable importance in the design and operation of hydraulic turbomachinery and is considered in further detail under Section 9.8 later in this chapter.

d. The flow angles at runner inlet are now obtained as follows:

\(\alpha _{2} =\tan ^{-1}\left(c_{\theta 2}/c_{r2}\right) =\tan ^{-1}\left(37.1/10.5\right)=74.2Â°\)

\(\beta _{2}=\tan ^{-1}\left[\left(c_{\theta 2}-U_{2} \right)/c_{r2}\right] =\tan ^{-1}\left[\left(37.1-35\right)/10.5\right] =11.31Â°\)

From the definition of power specific speed, Eq. (9.1), and using \(P/\rho =Q\Delta W\),

 

\(\Omega _{sp}=\frac{\Omega \sqrt{P/\rho } }{\left(gH_{E}\right)^{5/4}} \) (9.1)

 

\(\Omega =\frac{\Omega _{SP}\left(gH_{E}\right)^{5/4} }{\sqrt{Q\Delta W} } =\frac{0.8\times 9114}{\sqrt{20\times 1298.7} } =45.24 rad/s\)

 

Thus, the rotational speed \(\Omega= 432 rpm\) and the runner diameter is

\(D_{2}=2U_{2} /\Omega =70/45.24=1.547 m\)
