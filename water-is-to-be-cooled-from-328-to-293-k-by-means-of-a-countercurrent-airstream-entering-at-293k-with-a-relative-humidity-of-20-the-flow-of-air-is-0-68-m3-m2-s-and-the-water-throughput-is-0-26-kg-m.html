Guest Question.

Water is to be cooled from 328 to 293 K by means of a countercurrent airstream entering at 293K with a relative humidity of 20%. The flow of air is \(0.68 m^{3}/m^{2} s, \) and the water throughput is \( 0.26 kg/m^{2} s. \) The whole of the resistance to heat and mass transfer may be assumed to be in the gas phase , and the product, \( (h_{D}a) \), may be taken as \( 0.2(m/s)(m^{2}/m^{3}), \) that is, \( 0.2 s^{-1} \).
What is the required height of packing and the condition of the exit airstream?

Admin
Solution

Assuming the latent heat of water at 273 K= 2495 kJ/kg,
specific heat of air = 1.003 kJ/kg K.
and specific heat of water vapour = 2.006 kJ/kg K.
The enthalpy of the inlet airstream:
\( H_{G1} = 1.003(293-273)+H[2495+2.006(293-273)] \)
From Figure. 5.4,

at θ= 293 K and 20% RH , H=0.003 kg/kg, and hence
\( H_{G1} = (1.003\times 20)+0.003[2495+(2.006\times 20)] = 27.67kJ/kg \)
In the inlet air, water vapour = 0.003 kg/kg dry air. or
\( \frac{(0.003/18)}{(1/29)} = 0.005kmol/kmol \) dry air
Thus, flow of dry air = \((1-0.005)0.68 = 0.677 m^{3}/m^{2} s \)
Density of air at 293K = \( (\frac{29}{22.4})(\frac{273}{293} ) = 1.206kg/m^{3} \)
and
Mass flow of dry air = \( (1.206×0.677) = 0.817 kg/m^{2} s \)
Slope of operating line:
\( (L^{\prime }C_{L}/G^{\prime }) = \frac{(0.26×4.18)}{0.817}=1.33 \)
The coordinates of the bottom of the operating line are
\( \theta _{L1} = 293K, H_{G1} = 27.67kJ/kg \)

Hence, on an enthalpy-temperature diagram, the operating line of slope 1.33 is drawn through the point
(293 and 27.67)= \( (\theta _{L1} \space and \space H_{G1}). \)

The top point of the operating line is given by \( \theta _{L2} = 328K, \space and \space H_{G2} \) is found to be 76.5 kJ/kg (Figure. 5.19).

From Figs 5.4 and 5.5, the curve representing the enthalpy of saturated air as a function of temperature is obtained and drawn in. Alternatively, this plot may be calculated from
\( H_{F} = C_{a}(\theta _{f}-273)+H_{0}[C_{w}(\theta _{f}-273)+\lambda ]kj/kg \)

The curve represents the relation between enthalpy and temperature at the interface, that is, \( H_{f} \) as a function of \( θ_{f}. \)

It now remains to evaluate the integral \( \int\limits{dH_{G}/(H_{f}-H_{G})} \) between the limits, \( H_{G1} = 27.7kJ/kg \)
and \( H_{G2} = 76.5kJ/kg \). Various values of \( H_{G} \) between these limits are selected and the value of 9 obtained from the operating line. At this value of \( θ_{1}, \space now \space θ_{f} \), the corresponding value of \( H_{f} \) is obtained from the curve for saturated air. The working is as follows:

A plot of \(1/(H_{f}-H_{G}) \ and \ H_{G}\) is now made as shown in Fig. 5.20 from which the area under the curve = 0.65. This value may be checked using the approximate solution of Carey and Williamson.
At the bottom of the column,
\(H_{G1}=27.7kJ/kg, \ H_{f1}=57.7kJ/kg, \ \therefore \Delta H_{1}=30kJ/kg\)
At the top of the column,
\(H_{G2}=76.5kJ/kg, \ H_{f2}=355kJ/kg, \ \therefore \Delta H_{2}=279kJ/kg\)
At the mean water temperature of 0.5(328 + 293) = 310.5 K,
\(H_{Gm}=52kJ/kg, \ H_{f}=145kJ/kg, \ \therefore \Delta H_{m}=93kJ/kg\)
\(\frac{\Delta H_{m}}{\Delta H_{1}} =3.10, \ \frac{\Delta H_{m}}{\Delta H_{2}}=0.333,\)
and from Fig. 5.16, f = 0.79.
Thus,
\(\frac{(H_{G2}-H_{G1})}{f\Delta H_{m}}=\frac{(76.5-27.7)}{(0.79×93)} =0.66\)
which agrees well with the value (0.65) obtained by graphical integration.
Thus, in Equation
\(z=\int_{1}^{2}{dz}=\frac{G^{\prime }}{h_{D}a\rho }\int_{1}^{2}{\frac{dH_{G}}{(H_{f}-H_{G})} } \)
Height of packing,
\(z=\int_{H_{G1}}^{H_{G2}}{\frac{dH_{G}}{(H_{f}-H_{G})}\frac{G^{\prime }}{h_{D}a\rho } } =\frac{(0.65×0.817)}{(0.2×1.206)}=2.20m \)
Assuming that the resistance to mass transfer lies entirely within the gas phase, the lines connecting \(\theta _{L} \ and \ \theta _{f} \) are parallel with the enthalpy axis.
In Fig. 5.18, a plot of \(H_{G} \ and \ \theta _{G}\) is obtained using the construction given in Section 5.6.4 and shown in Fig. 5.15. From this curve, the value of \(\theta _{G2}\) corresponding to \(H_{G2}=76.5kJ/kg\) is 300 K.
From Fig. 5.5, under these conditions, the exit air has a humidity of 0.019 kg/kg that from Fig. 5.4 corresponds to a relative humidity of 83%.
