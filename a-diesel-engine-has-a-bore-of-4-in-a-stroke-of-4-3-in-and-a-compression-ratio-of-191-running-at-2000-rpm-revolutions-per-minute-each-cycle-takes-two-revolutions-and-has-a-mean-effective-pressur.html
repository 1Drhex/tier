Guest Question.

A diesel engine has a bore of 4 in., a stroke of 4.3 in. and a compression ratio of 19:1 running at 2000 RPM (revolutions per minute). Each cycle takes two revolutions and has a mean effective pressure of 200 \(lbf / in .^{2}\). With a total of 6 cylinders find the engine power in Btu/s and horsepower, hp.

Admin
Solution

Work from mean effective pressure, Eq.10.9.

\(P _{ meff }= w _{\text {net }} /\left( v _{\max }- v _{ min }\right) \quad -> w _{\text {net }}= P _{\operatorname{meff}}\left( v _{\max }- v _{ min }\right)\)

The displacement is

\(\Delta V =\pi \text { Bore }^{2} \times 0.25 \times S =\pi \times 4^{2} \times 0.25 \times 4.3=54.035 in ^{3}\)

Work per cylinder per power stroke, Eq.10.10

\(W = P _{ meff }\left( V _{\max }- V _{\min }\right)=200 \times 54.035 /(12 \times 778)=1.1575 Btu / cycle\)

Only every second revolution has a power stroke so we can find the power, see also Eq.10.11

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ W } &= W \times N _{ cyl } \times RPM \times 0.5\left(\frac{\text { cycles }}{\min }\right)\left(\frac{\min }{60 s }\right)\left(\frac{ Btu }{ cycle }\right) \\&=1.1575 \times 6 \times 2000 \times 0.5 \times(1 / 60)=115.75 Btu / s \\&=115.75 \times 3600 / 2544.43 hp = 1 6 4 hp\end{aligned}\)

The conversion factor from Btu/s to hp is from Table A.1 under power.

Eq. 10.11 : \(\dot{W}=N_{ cyl } m w_{ net } \frac{ RPM }{60}=P_{ meff } V_{ displ } \frac{ RPM }{60}\)
