Guest Question.

Determine the free vibration responses of the two-story shear frame of Fig. E14.2a with \(c = \sqrt{km}\) due to two sets of initial displacements: (1) \(u(0) = \phi _{1}\) and (2) \(u(0) = \phi _{2}\), where \(\phi _{n}\) is the nth natural vibration mode of the associated undamped system; \(\phi _{1}= \langle \begin{matrix} 1/2 & 2 \end{matrix} \rangle ^{T} \). and \(\phi _{2}= \langle \begin{matrix} -1 & 1 \end{matrix} \rangle ^{T}\).

Admin
Solution

Part 1 Substituting the first u(0) together with m, c, \(\lambda _{n}\), and \(\psi _{n}\) determined gives

\(B_{1} = \frac{\lambda _{1} \psi _{1} ^{T} \mathbf{mu (o)} + \psi _{1} ^{T}\mathbf{cu (o)} + \psi _{1} ^{T} \mathbf{m\dot{u (o)}} }{2 \lambda _{1} \psi _{1} ^{T} \mathbf{m} \psi _{1}+ \lambda _{1} \psi _{1} ^{T} \mathbf{c} \psi _{1} } = 0.5103 − 0.0200i \) (a.1)

\(B_{2} = \frac{\lambda _{2} \psi _{2} ^{T} \mathbf{mu (o)} + \psi _{2} ^{T}\mathbf{cu (o)} + \psi _{2} ^{T} \mathbf{m\dot{u (o)}} }{2 \lambda _{2} \psi _{2} ^{T} \mathbf{m} \psi _{2}+ \lambda _{2} \psi _{2} ^{T} \mathbf{c} \psi _{2} } = −0.0103 − 0.0200i\) (a.2)

Using the Bn in Eq. (a) and ψn, βn and γn are determined as follows:

\(\beta _{1} = Re (2 B_{1}\psi _{1}) = \begin{Bmatrix} 0.5002 \\ 1.0207 \end{Bmatrix}\)

\(\beta _{2} = Re (2 B_{2}\psi _{2}) = \begin{Bmatrix} −0.0002 \\ −0.0207 \end{Bmatrix}\) (b.1)

\(\gamma _{1} = Im (2 B_{1}\psi _{1}) = \begin{Bmatrix} −0.1448 \\ −0.0401 \end{Bmatrix}\)

\(\gamma _{2} = Im (2 B_{2}\psi _{2}) = \begin{Bmatrix} 0.0442 \\ −0.0401 \end{Bmatrix}\) (b.2)

Substituting the βn and γn from Eq. (b) into Eq. gives the free-vibration response:
\(\mathbf{u (o)} = e^{-0.1186 \omega _{1} t} [ \begin{Bmatrix} 0.5002 \\ 1.0207 \end{Bmatrix} cos \omega _{1D} t+ \begin{Bmatrix} 0.1448 \\ 0.0401 \end{Bmatrix} sin \omega _{1D} t]\)

\(+ e^{-0.1186 \omega _{2} t} [ \begin{Bmatrix} -0.0002 \\ −0.0207 \end{Bmatrix} cos \omega _{2D} t+ \begin{Bmatrix} 0.0442 \\ −0.0401 \end{Bmatrix} sin \omega _{2D} t\) (c)

Part 2 Proceeding as in Part 1, the free vibration response due to the second u(0) can be determined:

\(\mathbf{u (o)} = e^{-0.1186 \omega _{1} t} [ \begin{Bmatrix} 0.0815 \\ -0.0004 \end{Bmatrix} cos \omega _{1D} t- \begin{Bmatrix} 0.3304 \\ 0.6675 \end{Bmatrix} sin \omega _{1D} t]\)

\(+ e^{-0.1186 \omega _{2} t} [ \begin{Bmatrix} −1.0815 \\ 1.0004 \end{Bmatrix} cos \omega _{2D} t+ \begin{Bmatrix} 0.0476 \\0.4664 \end{Bmatrix} sin \omega _{2D}t \)(d)

Observations

The displacements u(t) are expressed as a linear combination of φn, the natural vibration modes of the associated undamped system:

\(\mathbf{u}(t)=\sum_{n=1}^{2} \phi_{n} q_{n}(t)\) (e)

where the modal coordinates are given by

\( q_{n}(t) = \phi ^{T}_{n} \mu(t) \phi ^{T}_{n} m\phi _{n}\) (f)

which is a generalization of Eq. Substituting the known φn and Eq. (c) for u(t) in Eq. (f) leads to

\(q_{1}(t)=e^{-0.1186 \omega_{1} t}\left(1.0139 \cos \omega_{1 D} t+0.1232 \sin \omega_{1 D} t\right) \)
\(+e^{-0.1186 \omega_{2} t}\left(0.0068 \cos \omega_{2 D} t-0.0832 \sin \omega_{2 D} t\right)\) (g)

\(q_{2}(t)= e^{-0.1186 \omega_{1} t}\left(-0.0139 \cos \omega_{1 D} t-0.0028 \sin \omega_{1 D} t\right)\)
\(+e^{-0.1186 \omega_{2} t}\left(-0.0068 \cos \omega_{2 D} t+0.0428 \sin \omega_{2 D} t\right) \) (h)

These are the \(q_{n}(t)\) associated with Eq. (c), the free vibration response due to the first set of initial displacements. Similarly, substituting the known φn and Eq. (d) for u(t), Eq. (f) leads to

\(q_{1}(t)= e^{-0.1186 \omega_{1} t}\left(0.0541 \cos \omega_{1 D} t-0.6652 \sin \omega_{1} D t\right) \)
\(+e^{-0.1186 \omega_{2} t}\left(-0.0545 \cos \omega_{2 D} t-0.0023 \sin \omega_{2 D} t\right)\) (i)

 

\(q_{2}(t)= e^{-0.1186 \omega_{1} t}\left(-0.0541 \cos \omega_{1 D} t+0.3427 \sin \omega_{1 D} t\right)\)
\(+e^{-0.1186 \omega_{2} t}\left(1.0545 \cos \omega_{2 D} t+0.1237 \sin \omega_{2 D} t\right)\)
(k)

These are the\( q_{n}(t)\) associated with Eq. (d), the free vibration response to the second set of initial displacements. These results, presented in Figs. Eqs , are for a system disturbed from its equilibrium position by imposing initial displacements that are proportional to a natural vibration mode φn of the associated undamped system. The solutions for \(q_{n}(t)\) are presented in part (c) of these figures; the floor displacements in part (d); and the deflected shapes at selected time instants—a, b, c, d, and e—in part (b). These results permit three observations that contrast with what we observed earlier for undamped systems and for classically damped systems: First, \(q2(t) \neq 0\) in Fig. E14.5.1c and \(q1(t) \neq 0\) in Fig. E14.5.2c. Second, the initial deflected shape is not maintained in free vibration; see Figs. E14.5.1b and E14.5.2b. Third, all floors (or DOFs) of the system do not vibrate in the same phase; they do not pass through zero, maximum, or minimum positions at the same instant of time.
