Guest Question.

The Clebsch–Gordan coefficients are defined by Equation 4.183. Adding together two states with angular momentum \( j_{1} \text { and } j_{2} \) produces a state with total angular momentum J according to

\( |s m\rangle=\sum_{m_{1}+m_{2}=m} C_{m_{1} m_{2} m}^{s_{1} s_{2} s}\left|s_{1} s_{2} m_{1} m_{2}\right\rangle \) (4.183).

\( |J M\rangle=\sum_{m_{1}, m_{2}} C_{m_{1} m_{2} M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}\left|j_{1} j_{2} m_{1} m_{2}\right\rangle \). (6.64).

(a) From Equation 6.64, show that the Clebsch–Gordan coefficients satisfy

\( C_{m_{1} m_{2} M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}=\left\langle j_{1} j_{2} m_{1} m_{2} \mid J M\right\rangle \) (6.65).

(b) Apply \( \hat{J}_{\pm}=\hat{J}_{\pm}^{(1)}+\hat{J}_{\pm}^{(2)} \) to Equation 6.64 to derive the recursion relations for Clebsch–Gordan coefficients:

\( A_{J}^{M} C_{m_{1} m_{2} M+1}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}=B_{j_{1}}^{m_{1}} C_{m_{1}-1 m_{2} M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}+B_{j_{2}}^{m_{2}} C_{m_{1} m_{2}-1 M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J} \),

\( B_{J}^{M} C_{m_{1} m_{2} M-1}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}=A_{j_{1}}^{m_{1}} C_{m+1 m_{2} M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}+A_{j_{2}}^{m_{2}} C_{m_{1} m_{2}+1 M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J} \). (6.66).

Admin
Solution

(a) This is nothing more than Fourier’s trick. Taking an inner product with an arbitrary state from the sum and using the orthonormality,

\( \left\langle j_{1} j_{2} m_{1}^{\prime} m_{2}^{\prime} \mid J M\right\rangle=\sum_{m_{1}, m_{2}} C_{m_{1} m_{2} M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J} \underbrace{\left\langle j_{1} j_{2} m_{1}^{\prime} m_{2}^{\prime} \mid j_{1} j_{2} m_{1} m_{2}\right\rangle}_{\delta_{m_{1}, m_{1}}^{\prime} \delta_{m_{2}, m_{2}}} \).

\( =C_{m_{1}^{\prime} m_{2}^{\prime} M}^{j_{1} j_{2}, J} \).

(b) Applying \( J_{\pm} \) to Equation 6.64 we get

\( J_{\pm}|J M\rangle=\sum_{m_{1}, m_{2}} C_{m_{1} m_{2} M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}\left[J_{\pm}^{(1)}\left|j_{1} j_{2} m_{1} m_{2}\right\rangle+J_{\pm}^{(2)}\left|j_{1} j_{2} m_{1} m_{2}\right\rangle\right] \).

(a) For the plus case, Equation 4.121 gives

\( A_{\ell}^{m}=\hbar \sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)-m(m+1)}=\hbar \sqrt{(\ell-m)(\ell+m+1)} \),

\( B_{\ell}^{m}=\hbar \sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)-m(m-1)}=\hbar \sqrt{(\ell+m)(\ell-m+1)} \). (4.121).

\( A_{J}^{M}|J M+1\rangle=\sum_{m_{1} m_{2}} C_{m_{1} m_{2} M}^{j_{1 j 2} J}\left[A_{j_{1}}^{m_{1}}\left|j_{1} j_{2} m_{1}+1 m_{2}\right\rangle+A_{j_{2}}^{m_{2}}\left|j_{1} j_{2} m_{1} m_{2}+1\right\rangle\right] \).

Next we take the inner product with \( \left\langle j_{1} j_{2} m_{1}^{\prime} m_{2}^{\prime}\right| \) to get

\( A_{J}^{M} C_{m_{1}^{\prime} m_{2}^{\prime} M+1}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}=\sum_{m_{1} m_{2}} C_{m_{1} m_{2} M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}\left[A_{j_{1}}^{m_{1}} \delta_{m_{1}+1, m_{1}^{\prime}} \delta_{m_{2}, m_{2}^{\prime}}+A_{j_{2}}^{m_{2}} \delta_{m_{1}, m_{1}^{\prime}} \delta_{m_{2}+1, m_{2}^{\prime}}\right] \).

\( =A_{j_{1}}^{m_{1}^{\prime}-1} C_{m_{1}^{\prime}-1 m_{2} M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}+A_{j_{2}}^{m_{2}^{\prime}-1} C_{m_{1}^{\prime} m_{2}^{\prime}-1 M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J} \).

Now \( A_{j}^{m-1}=B_{j}^{m} \) and dropping the primes we have

\( A_{J}^{M} C_{m_{1} m_{2} M+1}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}=B_{j_{1}}^{m_{1}} C_{m_{1}-1 m_{2} M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}+B_{j_{2}}^{m_{2}} C_{m_{1} m_{2}-1 M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J} \).

which is the first desired equality.

(b) For the minus case, Equation 4.121 gives

\( A_{\ell}^{m}=\hbar \sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)-m(m+1)}=\hbar \sqrt{(\ell-m)(\ell+m+1)} \),

\( B_{\ell}^{m}=\hbar \sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)-m(m-1)}=\hbar \sqrt{(\ell+m)(\ell-m+1)} \). (4.121).

\( B_{J}^{M}|J M-1\rangle=\sum_{m_{1} m_{2}} C_{m_{1} m_{2} M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}\left[B_{j_{1}}^{m_{1}}\left|j_{1} j_{2} m_{1}-1 m_{2}\right\rangle+B_{j_{2}}^{m_{2}}\left|j_{1} j_{2} m_{1} m_{2}-1\right\rangle\right] \).

Next we take the inner product with \( \left\langle j_{1} j_{2} m_{1}^{\prime} m_{2}^{\prime}\right| \text { to get } \)

\( B_{J}^{M} C_{m_{1}^{\prime} m_{2}^{\prime} M-1}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}=\sum_{m_{1} m_{2}} C_{m_{1} m_{2} M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}\left[B_{j_{1}}^{m_{1}} \delta_{m_{1}-1, m_{1}^{\prime}} \delta_{m_{2}, m_{2}^{\prime}}+B_{j_{2}}^{m_{2}} \delta_{m_{1}, m_{1}^{\prime}} \delta_{m_{2}-1, m_{2}^{\prime}}\right] \).

\( =B_{j_{1}}^{m_{1}^{\prime}+1} C_{m_{1}^{\prime}+1 m_{2} M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}+B_{j_{2}}^{m_{2}^{\prime}+1} C_{m_{1}^{\prime} m_{2}^{\prime}+1 M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J} \).

\( \text { Now } B_{j}^{m+1}=A_{j}^{m} \) and dropping the primes we have

\( B_{J}^{M} C_{m_{1} m_{2} M-1}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}=A_{j_{1}}^{m_{1}} C_{m_{1}+1 m_{2} M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J}+A_{j_{2}}^{m_{2}} C_{m_{1} m_{2}+1 M}^{j_{1} j_{2} J} \).

which is the second desired equality.
