Guest Question.

Calculation of the Power and Efficiency of a Rankine Power Cycle

Steam enters the turbine in a power plant at 600ºC and 10 MPa and is condensed at a pressure of 100 kPa. Assume the plant can be treated as an ideal Rankine cycle. Determine the power produced per kg of steam and the efficiency of the cycle. How does the efficiency compare to a Carnot cycle operating between these two temperatures?

 

 

Admin
Solution

We can refer to Figure 3.7 to identify the states of water as it goes through the cycle. It is useful to refer to this fi gure as we are solving the problem. Examining Equation (3.34), we see that we need to determine the enthalpies in the four states to solve for the effi ciency. Steam enters the turbine at 600ºC and 10 MPa. Looking up values from the steam tables (Appendix B), we get:

 

\(\eta_{\text {Rankine }}=\frac{\left|\dot{W}_{s}\right|-\dot{W}_{c}}{\dot{Q}_{ H }}=\frac{\left|\left(\hat{h}_{2}-\hat{h}_{1}\right)\right|-\left(\hat{h}_{4}-\hat{h}_{3}\right)}{\left(\hat{h}_{1}-\hat{h}_{4}\right)}\) (3.34)

 

\(\hat{h}_{1}=3625.3[ kJ / kg ]\)

 

and, \(\hat{s}_{1}=6.9028[k J /(k g K )]=\hat{s}_{2}\)

 

Since the entropy at state 1 is equal to the entropy at state 2. We also know the pressure at state 2, \(P_{2}=100\) kPa. Thus, state 2 is completely constrained, and we can determine its enthalpy from the steam tables. Since it exists as a liquid–vapor mixture, we must determine the quality, x, of the steam as follows:

 

\(\hat{s}_{2}=6.9028[ k J /( kg K )]=(1-x) \hat{s}_{l}+x \hat{s}_{v}=(1-x)(1.3025[ kJ / kg K ])+x(7.3593[ kJ / kg K ])\)

 

Solving for x gives:

 

\(x=0.925\)

 

Therefore, the enthalpy in state 2 is given by:

 

\(\hat{h}_{2}=(1-x) \hat{h}_{l}+x \hat{h}_{v}=(0.075)(417.44[ kJ / kg ])+0.925(2675.5[ kJ / kg ])=2505.3[ kJ / kg ]\)

 

The power generated by the turbine is found by the enthalpy difference between state 2 and state 1:

 

\(\hat{w}_{s}=\hat{h}_{2}-\hat{h}_{1}=2505.3-3625.3=-1120.0[ kJ / kg ]\)

 

The enthalpy of state 3 is a saturated liquid at 100 kPa:

 

\(\hat{h}_{3}=\hat{h}_{1}=417.44[ kJ / kg ]\)

 

The enthalpy at state 4 can be determined from Equation (3.33):

 

\(\dot{W}_{c}=\dot{m}\left(\hat{h}_{4}-\hat{h}_{3}\right)=\dot{m} \hat{v}_{1}\left(P_{4}-P_{3}\right)\) (3.33)

 

\(\hat{h}_{4}=\hat{h}_{3}+\hat{v}_{l}\left(P_{4}-P_{3}\right)=427.34[ kJ / kg ]\)

 

Note that since we are increasing the pressure of water in the liquid state, the work required is only 9.9 kJ/kg. This value is less than 1% of the work produced by expansion of vapor through the turbine (1120 kJ/kg). The net work is given by:

 

\(\hat{w}_{ net }=\hat{w}_{s}+\hat{w}_{c}=-1120.0+9.9=-1110.1[ kJ / kg ]\)

 

Solving for the effi ciency from Equation (3.34), we get:

 

\(\eta_{\text {Rankine }}=\left(\left|\dot{W}_{s}\right|-\dot{W}_{c}\right) / \dot{Q}_{ H }=\left[\left|\left(\hat{h}_{2}-\hat{h}_{1}\right)\right|-\left(\hat{h}_{4}-\hat{h}_{3}\right)\right] /\left(\hat{h}_{1}-\hat{h}_{4}\right)=0.347\)

 

The 34.7% effi ciency is the best-case scenario for this cycle, since we assumed reversible processes. In reality, we would not even achieve this value!

The Carnot effi ciency is given by Equation (3.9):

 

\(\eta=1-\frac{T_{ C }}{T_{ H }}\) (3.9)

 

\(\eta_{\text {Carnot }}=1-\frac{T_{ C }}{T_{ H }}=1-\frac{373}{873}=0.573\)

 

The Rankine effi ciency is lower than the Carnot effi ciency. We can see the basis if we compare the net work graphically, as we did in Figure 3.8. The box representing net work for each cycle is shown in Figure E3.14. Recall that the steam that enters the turbine of the Rankine cycle is superheated to eliminate wear and corrosion on the turbine blades. In modifying the cycle in this way, we “crop off” a signifi cant portion of the rectangle that represents the Carnot cycle.
