Guest Question.

In a crystal, the electric field of neighboring ions perturbs the energy levels of an atom. As a crude model, imagine that a hydrogen atom is surrounded by three pairs of point charges, as shown in Figure 7.14. (Spin is irrelevant to this problem, so ignore it.)

(a) Assuming that \( r \ll d_{1}, r \ll d_{2} \text {, and } r \ll d_{3} \), show that

\( H^{\prime}=V_{0}+3\left(\beta_{1} x^{2}+\beta_{2} y^{2}+\beta_{3} z^{2}\right)-\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}+\beta_{3}\right) r^{2} \),

where

\( \beta_{i} \equiv-\frac{e}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \frac{q_{i}}{d_{i}^{3}}, \quad \text { and } V_{0}=2\left(\beta_{1} d_{1}^{2}+\beta_{2} d_{2}^{2}+\beta_{3} d_{3}^{2}\right) \).

(b) Find the lowest-order correction to the ground state energy.

(c) Calculate the first-order corrections to the energy of the first excited states ( n = 2). Into how many levels does this four-fold degenerate system split, (i) in the case of cubic symmetry, \( \beta_{1}=\beta_{2}=\beta_{3} \); (ii) in the case of tetragonal symmetry, \( \beta_{1}=\beta_{2} \neq \beta_{3} \); (iii) in the general case of orthorhombic symmetry (all three different)? Solution

(a) The potential energy of the electron (charge -e) at (x, y, z) due to q’s at x = ±d alone is:

\( V=-\frac{e q}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\left[\frac{1}{\sqrt{(x+d)^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}}+\frac{1}{\sqrt{(x-d)^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}}\right] \). Expanding (with \( d \gg x, y, z) \):

\( \frac{1}{\sqrt{(x \pm d)^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}}=\left(x^{2} \pm 2 d x+d^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}\right)^{-1 / 2}=\left(d^{2} \pm 2 d x+r^{2}\right)^{-1 / 2}=\frac{1}{d}\left(1 \pm \frac{2 x}{d}+\frac{r^{2}}{d^{2}}\right)^{-1 / 2} \).

\( \approx \frac{1}{d}\left(1 \mp \frac{x}{d}-\frac{r^{2}}{2 d^{2}}+\frac{3}{8} \frac{4 x^{2}}{d^{2}}\right)=\frac{1}{d}\left[1 \mp \frac{x}{d}+\frac{1}{2 d^{2}}\left(3 x^{2}-r^{2}\right)\right] \).

\( V=-\frac{e q}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0} d}\left[1-\frac{x}{d}+\frac{1}{2 d^{2}}\left(3 x^{2}-r^{2}\right)+1+\frac{x}{d}+\frac{1}{2 d^{2}}\left(3 x^{2}-r^{2}\right)\right]=-\frac{2 e q}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0} d}-\frac{e q}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0} d^{3}}\left(3 x^{2}-r^{2}\right) \).

\( =2 \beta d^{2}+3 \beta x^{2}-\beta r^{2}, \quad \text { where } \quad \beta \equiv-\frac{e}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \frac{q}{d^{3}} \).

Thus with all six charges in place

\( H^{\prime}=2\left(\beta_{1} d_{1}^{2}+\beta_{2} d_{2}^{2}+\beta_{3} d_{3}^{2}\right)+3\left(\beta_{1} x^{2}+\beta_{2} y^{2}+\beta_{3} z^{2}\right)-r^{2}\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}+\beta_{3}\right) \). QED

(b) \( \left\langle 100\left|H^{\prime}\right| 100\right\rangle=\frac{1}{\pi a^{3}} \int e^{-2 r / a} H^{\prime} r^{2} \sin \theta d r d \theta d \phi \).

\( =V_{0}+\frac{3}{\pi a^{3}} \int e^{-2 r / a}\left(\beta_{1} x^{2}+\beta_{2} y^{2}+\beta_{3} z^{2}\right) r^{2} \sin \theta d r d \theta d \phi-\frac{\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}+\beta_{3}\right)}{\pi a^{3}} \int r^{2} e^{-2 r / a} r^{2} \sin \theta d r d \theta d \phi \).

\( I_{1} \equiv \int r^{2} e^{-2 r / a} r^{2} \sin \theta d r d \theta d \phi=4 \pi \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{4} e^{-2 r / a} d r=4 \pi 4 !\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^{5}=3 \pi a^{5} \).

\( I_{2} \equiv \int e^{-2 r / a}\left(\beta_{1} x^{2}+\beta_{2} y^{2}+\beta_{3} z^{2}\right) r^{2} \sin \theta d r d \theta d \phi \).

\( =\int r^{4} e^{-2 r / a}\left(\beta_{1} \sin ^{2} \theta \cos ^{2} \phi+\beta_{2} \sin ^{2} \theta \sin ^{2} \phi+\beta_{3} \cos ^{2} \theta\right) \sin \theta d r d \theta d \phi \).

But \( \int_{0}^{2 \pi} \cos ^{2} \phi d \phi=\int_{0}^{2 \pi} \sin ^{2} \phi d \phi=\pi, \quad \int_{0}^{2 \pi} d \phi=2 \pi \), so

\( =\int_{0}^{\infty} r^{4} e^{-2 r / a} d r \int_{0}^{\pi}\left[\pi\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}\right) \sin ^{2} \theta+2 \pi \beta_{3} \cos ^{2} \theta\right] \sin \theta d \theta \).

But \( \int_{0}^{\pi} \sin ^{3} \theta d \theta=\frac{4}{3}, \quad \int_{0}^{\pi} \cos ^{2} \theta \sin \theta d \theta=\frac{2}{3} . \) So

\( =4 !\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^{5}\left[\frac{4 \pi}{3}\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}\right)+\frac{4 \pi}{3} \beta_{3}\right]=\pi a^{5}\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}+\beta_{3}\right) \).

\( \left\langle 100\left|H^{\prime}\right| 100\right\rangle=V_{0}+\frac{3}{\pi a^{3}} \pi a^{5}\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}+\beta_{3}\right)-\frac{\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}+\beta_{3}\right)}{\pi a^{3}} 3 \pi a^{5}=V_{0}\).

(c) \( \text { The four states are }\left\{\begin{array}{ll} |200\rangle & =R_{20} Y_{0}^{0} \\ |211\rangle & =R_{21} Y_{1}^{1} \\ |21-1\rangle & =R_{21} Y_{1}^{-1} \\ |210\rangle & =R_{21} Y_{1}^{0} \end{array}\right\} \text { (functional forms in Problem 4.13). } \) ()

Diagonal elements: \( \left\langle n \ell m\left|H^{\prime}\right| n \ell m\right\rangle=V_{0}+3\left(\beta_{1}\left\langle x^{2}\right\rangle+\beta_{2}\left\langle y^{2}\right\rangle+\beta_{3}\left\langle z^{2}\right\rangle\right)-\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}+\beta_{3}\right)\left\langle r^{2}\right\rangle \).

\( \text { For }|200\rangle,\left\langle x^{2}\right\rangle=\left\langle y^{2}\right\rangle=\left\langle z^{2}\right\rangle=\frac{1}{3}\left\langle r^{2}\right\rangle\left(Y_{0}^{0} \text { does not depend on } \phi, \theta\right. \); this state has spherical symmetry), so \( \left\langle 200\left|H^{\prime}\right| 200\right\rangle=V_{0} \). (I could have used the same argument in (b).)

From (a), \( \left\langle r^{2}\right\rangle=\frac{n^{2} a^{2}}{2}\left[5 n^{2}-3 \ell(\ell+1)+1\right], \text { so for } n=2, \ell=1:\left\langle r^{2}\right\rangle=30 a^{2} \). Moreover, since \( \left\langle x^{2}\right\rangle=\{\ldots\} \int_{0}^{2 \pi} \cos ^{2} \phi d \phi=\{\ldots\} \int_{0}^{2 \pi} \sin ^{2} \phi d \phi=\left\langle y^{2}\right\rangle, \text { and }\left\langle x^{2}\right\rangle+\left\langle y^{2}\right\rangle+\left\langle z^{2}\right\rangle=\left\langle r^{2}\right\rangle \), it follows that

\( \left\langle x^{2}\right\rangle=\left\langle y^{2}\right\rangle=\frac{1}{2}\left(\left\langle r^{2}\right\rangle-\left\langle z^{2}\right\rangle\right)=15 a^{2}-\frac{1}{2}\left\langle z^{2}\right\rangle . \quad \text { So all we need to calculate is }\left\langle z^{2}\right\rangle \).

\( \left\langle 210\left|z^{2}\right| 210\right\rangle=\frac{1}{2 \pi a} \frac{1}{16 a^{4}} \int r^{2} e^{-r / a} \cos ^{2} \theta\left(r^{2} \cos ^{2} \theta\right) r^{2} \sin \theta d r d \theta d \phi \).

\( =\frac{1}{16 a^{5}} \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{6} e^{-r / a} d r \int_{0}^{\pi} \cos ^{4} \theta \sin \theta d \theta=\frac{1}{16 a^{5}} 6 ! a^{7} \frac{2}{5}=18 a^{2} ; \quad\left\langle x^{2}\right\rangle=\left\langle y^{2}\right\rangle=15 a^{2}-9 a^{2}=6 a^{2} \).

\( \left\langle 210\left|H^{\prime}\right| 210\right\rangle=V_{0}+3\left(6 a^{2} \beta_{1}+6 a^{2} \beta_{2}+18 a^{2} \beta_{3}\right)-30 a^{2}\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}+\beta_{3}\right) \).

\( =V_{0}-12 a^{2}\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}+\beta_{3}\right)+36 a^{2} \beta_{3} \).

\( \left\langle 21 \pm 1\left|z^{2}\right| 21 \pm 1\right\rangle=\frac{1}{\pi a} \frac{1}{64 a^{4}} \int r^{2} e^{-r / a} \sin ^{2} \theta\left(r^{2} \cos ^{2} \theta\right) r^{2} \sin \theta d r d \theta d \phi \).

\( =\frac{1}{32 a^{5}} \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{6} e^{-r / a} d r \int_{0}^{\pi}\left(1-\cos ^{2} \theta\right) \cos ^{2} \theta \sin \theta d \theta=\frac{1}{32 a^{5}} 6 ! a^{7}\left(\frac{2}{3}-\frac{2}{5}\right)=6 a^{2} \).

\( \left\langle x^{2}\right\rangle=\left\langle y^{2}\right\rangle=15 a^{2}-3 a^{2}=12 a^{2} \).

\( \left\langle 21 \pm 1\left|H^{\prime}\right| 21 \pm 1\right\rangle=V_{0}+3\left(12 a^{2} \beta_{1}+12 a^{2} \beta_{2}+6 a^{2} \beta_{3}\right)-30 a^{2}\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}+\beta_{3}\right) \).

\( =V_{0}+6 a^{2}\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}+\beta_{3}\right)-18 a^{2} \beta_{3} \).

Off-diagonal elements: We need \( \left\langle 200\left|H^{\prime}\right| 210\right\rangle,\left\langle 200\left|H^{\prime}\right| 21 \pm 1\right\rangle,\left\langle 210\left|H^{\prime}\right| 21 \pm 1\right\rangle, \text { and }\left\langle 21-1\left|H^{\prime}\right| 211\right\rangle \).

Now \( \left\langle n \ell m\left|V_{0}\right| n^{\prime} \ell^{\prime} m^{\prime}\right\rangle=0 \text {, by orthogonality, and }\left\langle n \ell m\left|r^{2}\right| n^{\prime} \ell^{\prime} m^{\prime}\right\rangle=0, \text { by orthogonality of } Y_{\ell}^{m} \), so all we need are the matrix elements of \( x^{2} \text { and } y^{2}\left(\left\langle\left|z^{2}\right|\right\rangle=-\left\langle\left|x^{2}\right|\right\rangle-\left\langle\left|y^{2}\right|\right\rangle\right) \text {. For }\left\langle 200\left|x^{2}\right| 21 \pm 1\right\rangle \) and \( \left\langle 210\left|x^{2}\right| 21 \pm 1\right\rangle \text { the } \phi \text { integral is } \int_{0}^{2 \pi} \cos ^{2} \phi e^{\pm i \phi} d \phi=\int_{0}^{2 \pi} \cos ^{3} \phi d \phi \pm i \int_{0}^{2 \pi} \cos ^{2} \phi \sin \phi d \phi=0 \) and the
same goes for y². So \( \left\langle 200\left|H^{\prime}\right| 21 \pm 1\right\rangle=\left\langle 210\left|H^{\prime}\right| 21 \pm 1\right\rangle=0 \).

For \( \left\langle 200\left|x^{2}\right| 210\right\rangle \text { and }\left\langle 200\left|y^{2}\right| 210\right\rangle \text { the } \theta \text { integral is } \int_{0}^{\pi} \cos \theta\left(\sin ^{2} \theta\right) \sin \theta d \theta=\sin ^{4} \theta /\left.4\right|_{0} ^{\pi}=0 \text {, } \) so \( \left\langle 200\left|H^{\prime}\right| 210\right\rangle=0 \). Finally:

\( \left\langle 21-1\left|x^{2}\right| 211\right\rangle=-\frac{1}{\pi a} \frac{1}{64 a^{4}} \int r^{2} e^{-r / a} \sin ^{2} \theta e^{2 i \phi}\left(r^{2} \sin ^{2} \theta \cos ^{2} \phi\right) r^{2} \sin \theta d r d \theta d \phi \).

\( =-\frac{1}{64 \pi a^{5}} \underbrace{\int_{0}^{\infty} r^{6} e^{-r / a} d r}_{6 ! a^{7}} \underbrace{\int_{0}^{\pi} \sin ^{5} \theta d \theta}_{16 / 15} \underbrace{\int_{0}^{2 \pi} e^{2 i \phi} \cos ^{2} \phi d \phi}_{\pi / 2} \).

\( =-\frac{1}{64 \pi a^{5}} 6 ! a^{7} \frac{16}{15} \frac{\pi}{2}=-6 a^{2} \).

\( \text { For } y^{2} \text {, the } \phi \text { integral is } \int_{0}^{2 \pi} e^{2 i \phi} \sin ^{2} \phi d \phi=-\pi / 2 \text {, so }\left\langle 21-1\left|y^{2}\right| 211\right\rangle=6 a^{2} \text {, and }\left\langle 21-1\left|z^{2}\right| 211\right\rangle=0 \).

\( \left\langle 21-1\left|H^{\prime}\right| 211\right\rangle=3\left[\beta_{1}\left(-6 a^{2}\right)+\beta_{2}\left(6 a^{2}\right)\right]=-18 a^{2}\left(\beta_{1}-\beta_{2}\right) \).

The perturbation matrix is:

21-1 211 210 200
0 0 0 \( V_{0} \) 200
0 0 \( V_{0}-12 a^{2}\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}\right)+24 a^{2} \beta_{3} \) 0 210
\( -18 a^{2}\left(\beta_{1}-\beta_{2}\right) \) \( V_{0}+6 a^{2}\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}\right)-12 a^{2} \beta_{3} \) 0 0 211
\( V_{0}+6 a^{2}\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}\right)-12 a^{2} \beta_{3} \) \( -18 a^{2}\left(\beta_{1}-\beta_{2}\right) \) 0 0 21-1

The 2× 2 block has the form \( \left(\begin{array}{cc} A & B \\ B & A \end{array}\right) \); its characteristic equation is \( (A-\lambda)^{2}-B^{2}=0 \text {, so } A-\lambda=\pm B \),

or

\( \lambda=A \mp B=V_{0}+6 a^{2}\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}\right)-12 a^{2} \beta_{3} \pm 18 a^{2}\left(\beta_{1}-\beta_{2}\right)=\left\{\begin{array}{l} V_{0}+24 a^{2} \beta_{1}-12 a^{2} \beta_{2}-12 a^{2} \beta_{3} \\ V_{0}-12 a^{2} \beta_{1}+24 a^{2} \beta_{2}-12 a^{2} \beta_{3} \end{array}\right. \).

The first-order corrections to the energy \( \left(E_{2}\right) \) are therefore:\( \begin{aligned} \epsilon_{1} &=V_{0} \\ \epsilon_{2} &=V_{0}-12 a^{2}\left(\beta_{1}+\beta_{2}-2 \beta_{3}\right) \\ \epsilon_{3} &=V_{0}-12 a^{2}\left(-2 \beta_{1}+\beta_{2}+\beta_{3}\right) \\ \epsilon_{4} &=V_{0}-12 a^{2}\left(\beta_{1}-2 \beta_{2}+\beta_{3}\right) \end{aligned} \) .

\( \text { (i) If } \beta_{1}=\beta_{2}=\beta_{3} \text {, then } \epsilon_{1}=\epsilon_{2}=\epsilon_{3}=\epsilon_{4}=V_{0}: \text { one level } \) (still 4-fold degenerate).

\( \text { (ii) If } \beta_{1}=\beta_{2} \neq \beta_{3}, \text { then } \epsilon_{1}=V_{0}, \epsilon_{2}=V_{0}-24 a^{2}\left(\beta_{1}-\beta_{3}\right), \epsilon_{3}=\epsilon_{4}=V_{0}+12 a^{2}\left(\beta_{1}-\beta_{3}\right) \): three levels (one remains doubly degenerate).

(iii) If all three β’s are different, there are four levels (no remaining degeneracy).
