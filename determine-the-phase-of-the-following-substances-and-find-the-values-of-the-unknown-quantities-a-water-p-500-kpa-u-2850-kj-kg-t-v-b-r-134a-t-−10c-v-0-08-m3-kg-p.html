Guest Question.

Determine the phase of the following substances and find the values of the unknown quantities.
a. Water, P = 500 kPa, u = 2850 kJ/kg, T = ?, v = ?
b. R-134a, T = −10°C, v = 0.08 \(m ^{3} / kg\), P = ?, u = ?
c. Ammonia, T = −20°C, u = 1000 kJ/kg, P = ?, x = ?

Admin
Solution

a. B.1.2 at 500 kPa: \(u > u _{ g }\) = 2561 kJ/kg so superheated vapor.

we locate it between 300 and 350°C and interpolate

x = (2850 – 2802.9)/(2882.6 – 2802.9) = 0.590966

T = 300 + x (350 – 300) = 329.55°C

v = 0.52256 + x (0.57012 – 0.52256) = 0.5507 \(m ^{3} / kg\)

 

b. B.5.1 at −10°C, \(v _{ f }=0.000755 m ^{3} / kg , v _{ g }=0.09921 m ^{3} / kg , v _{ f }< v < v _{ g }\)

saturated, P = 201.7 kPa,

\(x =\left( v – v _{ f }\right) / v _{ fg }\) = (0.08-0.000755)/0.09845 = 0.805,

\(u = u _{ f }+ x u _{ fg }\) = 186.57 + 0.805 × 185.7 = 336 kJ/kg

 

c. B.2.1 at −20°C: \(u < u _{ g }=1299.5 kJ / kg \quad \text { so, } \quad P = P _{ sat }=190.2 kPa\)

\(x =\left( u – u _{ f }\right) / u _{ fg }\) = (1000 – 88.76)/1210.7 = 0.7526
