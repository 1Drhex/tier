Guest Question.

Calculate the temperature at the nodes of the axisymmetric element shown in Figure 5.18 with heat generation of \( G=1.2 W / cm ^{3} . \) The coordinates of the nodes are given in cm as \( i(15,10), j(25,10) \) and k(20,12) .The heat transfer coefficient on the side ij is \( 1.2 W / cm ^{2} K\) and the ambient temperature is \( 30^{\circ} C . \) The heat flux on the side j k is equal to \( 1 W / cm ^{2} . \) Assume the thermal conductivities \( k_{r}=k_{z}=2 W / cm ^{\circ} C\)

 

 

Admin
Solution

The solution to this problem starts with the calculation of various terms in the stiffness matrix Equation (5.73)

\(\begin{array}{c}{[ K ]_{e}=\frac{2 \pi \bar{r} k_{r}}{4 A_{e}}\left[\begin{array}{ccc}b_{i}^{2} & b_{i} b_{j} & b_{i} b_{k} \\b_{i} b_{j} & b_{j}^{2} & b_{j} b_{k} \\b_{i} b_{k} & b_{j} b_{k} & b_{k}^{2}\end{array}\right]+\frac{2 \pi \bar{r} k_{z}}{4 A_{e}}\left[\begin{array}{ccc}c_{i}^{2} & c_{i} c_{j} & c_{i} c_{k} \\c_{i} c_{j} & c_{j}^{2} & c_{j} c_{k} \\c_{i} c_{k} & c_{j} c_{k} & c_{k}^{2}\end{array}\right]} \\+\frac{2 \pi h l_{i j}}{12}\left[\begin{array}{ccc}3 r_{i}+r_{j} & r_{i}+r_{j} & 0.0 \\r_{i}+r_{j} & r_{i}+3 r_{j} & 0.0 \\0.0 & 0.0 & 0.0\end{array}\right]\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}b_{i} &=z_{j}-z_{k}=-2.0 \\b_{j} &=z_{k}-z_{i}=2.0 \\b_{k} &=z_{i}-z_{j}=0.0 \\c_{i} &=r_{k}-r_{j}=-5.0 \\c_{j} &=r_{i}-r_{k}=-5.0 \\c_{k} &=r_{j}-r_{i}=10.0 .\end{aligned}\) (5.76)

From Equation (5.64),

\(2 A_{e}=\operatorname{det}\left|\begin{array}{lll}1 & r_{i} & z_{i} \\1 & r_{j} & z_{j} \\1 & r_{k} & z_{k}\end{array}\right|\)

the value of \( 2 A _{e}\) is \( 20 cm ^{2} . \) Similarly, \( \bar{r}\) from Equation (5.74)

\(\bar{r}=\frac{r_{i}+r_{j}+r_{k}}{3}\)

is calculated as being 20 cm (a reference axis at r=0.0 is assumed). Also, the coefficients used in the stiffness matrix can be calculated as

\(\frac{2 \pi \bar{r} k_{r}}{4 A_{\varepsilon}}=\frac{2 \pi \bar{r} k_{z}}{4 A_{\varepsilon}}=2 \pi\) (5.77)

Similarly,

\(\frac{2 \pi h l_{i j}}{12}=2 \pi\) (5.78)

 

\(\text { Note that length of the convective side } l_{i j} \text { is calculated as }\)

\(l_{i j}=\sqrt{\left(x_{i}-x_{j}\right)^{2}+\left(y_{i}-y_{j}\right)^{2}}=10 cm\) (5.79)

\(\text { Substituting into Equation (5.73) gives }\)

\([ K ]_{e}=2 \pi\left[\begin{array}{rrr}99 & 61 & -50 \\61 & 119 & -50 \\-50 & -50 & 100\end{array}\right]\) (5.80)

\(\text { Now, to calculate the loading vector, we need to determine the relevant coefficients, that is }\)

\(\frac{2 \pi h T_{a} l_{i j}}{6}=120 \pi\) (5.81)

Similarly,

\(\frac{2 \pi q l_{j k}}{6}=1.8 \pi\) (5.82)

\(\text { Substituting the coefficients and other values into Equation (5.75),}\)

 

\(\{ f \}_{e}=\int_{\Omega} G[ N ]^{T} r_{e} d \Omega-\int_{\Gamma_{q}} q[ N ]^{T} r_{e} d \Gamma+\int_{\Gamma_{h}} h T_{a}[ N ]^{T} r_{e} d \Gamma\) \(=\frac{2 \pi G A_{e}}{12}\left[\begin{array}{lll}2 & 1 & 1 \\1 & 2 & 1 \\1 & 1 & 2\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}r_{i} \\r_{j} \\r_{k}\end{array}\right\}-\frac{2 \pi q l_{j k}}{6}\left\{\begin{array}{c}0 \\2 r_{j}+r_{k} \\r_{j}+2 r_{k}\end{array}\right\}+\frac{2 \pi h T_{a} l_{i j}}{6}\left\{\begin{array}{c}2 r_{i}+r_{j} \\r_{i}+2 r_{j} \\0\end{array}\right\}\)

we obtain

\(\{ f \}_{e}=2 \pi\left\{\begin{array}{l}3375.0 \\3922.2 \\21.690\end{array}\right\}\) (5.83)

\(\text { The system of equation may be put together as }\)

\(\left[\begin{array}{rrr}99 & 61 & -50 \\61 & 119 & -50 \\-50 & -50 & 100\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{c}T_{i} \\T_{j} \\T_{k}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{l}3375.0 \\3922.2 \\21.690\end{array}\right\}\) (5.84)

Application of Gaussian elimination gives \( T_{k}=30.76^{\circ} C , T_{j}=29.87^{\circ} C and T_{i}=31.22^{\circ} C . \)

Total heat generated per unit thickness may be calculates as \( G \times\) volume \(=1.2 \times A_{e}=12 W\). The total heat flowing out of the element may be calculated by adding the heat leaving due to convection along the side \( i-j\left(h \times l_{i j} \times t \times\left[0.5 \times\left(T_{i}+T_{j}\right)-T_{a}\right]\right)=-6.54 W\), and heat leaving the side along j-k due to the flux leaving this side \( \left(q \times l_{j k} \times t\right)=-5.4 W\). As seen the total heat leaving the element is approximately -12 W, proving the energy balance.
