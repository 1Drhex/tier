Guest Question.

Determine the shear breakout capacity of the anchor group shown in Fig. 21.8. T he load is concentrically applied, the attachment has six \(\frac{3}{4} \)-in. diameter headed bolts cast in 5000 psi normalweight concrete that analysis indicates is uncracked during service load, and \(c_{a1}\) = 8 in.

Admin
Solution

From Fig. 21.8, \(s_1\) = 5.0 in. and \(s_2\) = 4.5 in. For normalweight concrete, \(λ and λ_a\) = 1.0. From Table 21.4 for uncracked concrete and cast-in anchors, \(ψ_{c,V}\) = 1.2, and because the value of \(c_{a2}\) is greater than \(c_{a1}\) by inspection, from Table 21.5 \(ψ_{ed,V}\) = 1.0. The load is concentric, so \(ψ_{ec,V}\) = 1.0, and the concrete thickness is greater than \(1.5 c_{a1}\) so \(ψ_{h,V}\) = 1.0.

Assume that one-half of the load is carried by the front row of anchors. The projected area of a single anchor is

\(A_{Vco} = 4.5 c_{a1}^2 = 4.5 \times 8^2 = 288 in^2\)

The projected area of the anchor group is
\(A_{Nc} = 1.5 c_{a1} (3 c_{a1} + 2 s_2 ) = 1.5 \times 8 \times (3 \times 8 + 2 \times 4.5) = 408 in^2\)

For a single anchor, \(d_a\) = 0.75 in. and \(ℓ_e = h_{ef}\). Then from Eq. (21.3), the shear strength of a single anchor is

\(V_b=\left(7\left(\frac{ℓ_e}{d_a} \right)^{0.2} \sqrt{d_a} \right)\lambda_a \sqrt{f_c^\prime}(c_{a1})^{1.5}=\left(7\left(\frac{4}{0.75} \right)^{0.2} \sqrt{0.75} \right)1.0 \sqrt{5000}(8)^{1.5}=13,570\) lb

The anchor group capacity is

\(\frac{V_{cbg}}{2}=\frac{A_{Vc}}{A_{Vco}}ψ_{ec,V} ψ_{ed,V} ψ_{c,V} ψ_{h,V} V_b=\frac{408}{288}1.0 \times 1.0 \times 1.2 \times 1.0 \times 13.57 = 23.1\) kips

Thus,
\(V_{cbg}\) = 46.1 kips

The strength of the anchor must also be checked for the case in which the back row of anchors carries the entire load. For this condition the edge distance \(c_{a1}\) increases to 8 + 5 in. = 13 in. With this change,

\(A_{Vco} = 4.5 c_{a1}^2 = 9 \times 13^2 = 760.5 in^2\)

 

\(A_{Vc} = 1.5 c_{a1} (3 c_{a1} + 2 s_2 ) = 1.5 \times 13 \times (3 \times 13 + 2 \times 4.5) = 955.5 in^2\)

\(V_b=\left(7\left(\frac{ℓ_e}{d_a} \right)^{0.2} \sqrt{d_a} \right)\lambda_a \sqrt{f_c^\prime}(c_{a1})^{1.5}=\left(7\left(\frac{4}{0.75} \right)^{0.2} \sqrt{0.75} \right)1.0 \sqrt{5000}(13)^{1.5}=28,080\) lb

\(V_{cbg}=\frac{A_{Vc}}{A_{Vco}}ψ_{ec,V} ψ_{ed,V} ψ_{c,V} ψ_{h,V} V_b=\frac{955.5}{760.5}\times 1.0 \times 1.0 \times 1.2 \times 1.0 \times 28.08 = 42.3\) kips

Thus, the second condition limits the nominal strength of the anchor group to \(V_{cbg}\) = 42.3 kips. The anchor group must be detailed with a No. 4 (No 13) bar or larger between the edge and the anchor to be consistent with the assumed value of \(ψ_{c,v}\).
