Guest Question.

A homogeneous bar (mass \(m\) ) on a frictionless horizontal base is accelerated by a force \(F\) acting at point \(A\) in length direction of the bar.

Determine the distribution of the normal force \(N\) along the bar.

Admin
Solution

The acceleration of the bar (in length direction) is \(a = F /m\) . To calculate the normal force, we first introduce the body-fixed coordinate \(x\) and then cut the bar at an arbitrary location \(x\) above and/or below the point A. For the lower cut (\(x\lt c\) ), the principle of linear momentum yields with \(\bar{m}=mx/l \)

 

\(\underline{\underline{N\left(x \right) } } =\bar{m}a=\frac{\bar{m} }{m}F=\underline{\underline{\frac{x}{l}F } } \) .

For the upper cut (\(x \gt c\) ), we obtain

\(N\left(x\right)+F=\bar{m}a= \bar{m}a=\frac{\bar{m} }{m}F \)

 

\(\rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{ N\left(x\right) =-F\left(1-\frac{x}{l} \right) } }\) .
