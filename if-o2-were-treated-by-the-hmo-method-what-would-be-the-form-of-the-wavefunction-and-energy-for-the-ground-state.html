Guest Question.

If \(O_{2}\) were treated by the HMO method, what would be the form of the wavefunction and energy for the ground state?

Admin
Solution

The ground state configuration for \(O_{2}\) is \(1σ^{2}_{g} 1σ^{2}_{u} 2σ^{2}_{g} 2σ^{2}_{u} 3σ^{2}_{g} 1π^{2}_{u,x} 1π^{2}_{u,y} ×1π_{g,x} 1π_{g,y}\) , where we have shown the degenerate members of π levels explicitly and in their real forms. The HMO wavefunction is simply a product of the pi MOs, one for each of the six pi electrons: \(1π_{u,x} (1)1π_{u,x} (2)1π_{u,y} (3)1π_{u,y} (4)1π_{g,x} (5)1π_{g,y} (6)\). The HMO energy is \(2E_{π,u,x} + 2E_{π,u,y} + E_{π,g,x} + E_{π,g,y}\) , which reduces to \(4E_{π,u} + 2E_{π,g}\). Note that, because \(O_{2}\) is linear, there is no unique molecular plane containing the internuclear axis. Therefore this molecule has two sets of π MOs, one pair pointing in the x direction, the other pair pointing along y. For a planar molecule, only one of these pairs would qualify as π MOs, as will be seen in the next section.
