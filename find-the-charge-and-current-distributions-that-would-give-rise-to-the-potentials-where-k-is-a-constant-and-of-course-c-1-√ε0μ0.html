Guest Question.

Find the charge and current distributions that would give rise to the potentials

\(V=0, A=\begin{cases} \frac{\mu_0k}{4c}(ct-\left|x\right| )^2\hat{Z}, for \left|x\right|\lt ct \\ 0, for \left|x\right|\gt ct\end{cases} \)

where k is a constant, and (of course) \(c=1/\sqrt{\epsilon _0\mu _0}. \)

Admin
Solution

First we’ll determine the electric and magnetic fields, using Eqs. 10.2 and 10.3:

\(B=\nabla\times A,\) (10.2)

\(E=-\nabla V-\frac{\partial A}{\partial t}. \) (10.3)

\(E=-\frac{\partial A}{\partial t} =-\frac{\mu_0k}{2}(ct-\left|x\right| )\hat{z},\\B=\nabla \times A =-\frac{\mu_0k}{4c}\frac{\partial }{\partial x}(ct-\left|x\right| )^2 \hat{y}=\pm \frac{\mu_0k}{2c}(ct-\left|x\right|)\hat{y}, \)

(plus, for x > 0; minus, for x < 0). These are for |x| < ct; when |x| > ct, E = B = 0 (Fig. 10.1). Calculating every derivative in sight, I find

\(\nabla . E =0 z; \nabla . B =0; \nabla \times E =\pm \frac{\mu_0k}{4c} \hat{y}; \nabla \times E =- \frac{\mu_0k}{2c}\hat{z},\\\frac{\partial E}{\partial t}=- \frac{\mu_0k}{2c}\hat{z}; \frac{\partial B}{\partial t}=± \frac{\mu_0k}{2c}\hat{y}\)

As you can easily check, Maxwell’s equations are all satisfied, with ρ and J both zero. Notice, however, that B has a discontinuity at x = 0, and this signals the presence of a surface current K in the yz plane; boundary condition (iv) in Eq. 7.64 gives

\(kt \hat{y} = K × \hat{x},\)

\(\begin{cases}(i)\epsilon _1E^\bot _1-\epsilon _2E^\bot _2=\sigma_f, (iii)E^\parallel _1-E^\parallel _2=0 \\(ii)B^\bot _1-B^\bot _2=0, (iv)\frac{1}{\mu_1}B^\parallel _1-\frac{1}{\mu_2}B^\parallel _2= K_f\times \hat{n}.\end{cases} \) (7.64)

and hence

\(K = kt \hat{z}.\)

Evidently we have here a uniform surface current flowing in the z direction over the plane x = 0, which starts up at t = 0, and increases in proportion to t. Notice that the news travels out (in both directions) at the speed of light: for points |x| > ct the message (“current is now flowing”) has not yet arrived, so the fields are zero.
