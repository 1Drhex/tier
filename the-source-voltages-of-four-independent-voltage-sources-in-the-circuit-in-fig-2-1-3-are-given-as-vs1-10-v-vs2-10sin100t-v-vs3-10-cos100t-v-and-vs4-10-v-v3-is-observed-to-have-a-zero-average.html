Guest Question.

The source voltages of four independent voltage sources in the circuit in Fig. 2.1-3 are given as \(v_{S1} =10 V\), \(v_{S2} = 10 sin100t V\), \(v_{S3} = 10 cos 100t V\) and \(v_{S4} = 10 V. v_{3}\) is observed to have a zero average value.The time-varying component of \(v_{1}\) is seen to be \(10 sin(100t-30°) V\). Find \(v_{1}\), \(v_{2}\) and \(v_{3}\) as functions of time.

Admin
Solution

\(v_{3}\) is stated to have a zero average. This implies that \(v_{3}\) has a zero \(DC\) content. Thus, \(v_{3}\) can be written as \(v_{3} = A sin(100t-\theta ) V\) where \(A\) and \(\theta\) are to be found. \(v_{1}\) is stated to have a time-varying component of \(10 sin(100t-30°) V\). It may have a \(DC\) content too. Thus, \(v_{1} = B + 10 sin(100t-30°) V\), where \(B\) is to be found. \(v_{2}\) may contain both \(DC\) and time-varying components. Thus, \(v_{2} = C + D sin(100t-\phi ) V\), where \(C, D\) and \(\phi\) have to be found.

Apply \(KVL\) in the first loop. The \(KVL\) equation is:

\(-v_{S1}+v_{1}+v_{2}+v_{3}+v_{S2}=0\)

 

\(i.e.,-10+(B+10sin(100t-30^{o}))+Asin(100t-\theta )+10 sin 100t=0\)

 

This equation involves some constants and some sinusoidal functions. This equation is the result of applying \(KVL\) to a loop in a circuit. Therefore, this equation has to be true at all \(t.\) Therefore, the equation can be split into two equations that have to be satisfied simultaneously. This is because a constant can not be met by a sinusoidal function in an equation for all \(t\).

\(−10 + B = 0 and 10sin(100t − 30°) + Asin(100t −q ) +10sin100t = 0\)

 

First equation yields \(B = 10 V\). Second equation is simplified by employing trigonometric identities as below:

\(5\sqrt{3}sin100t − 5cos100t + (Acos\theta ) sin100t − (Asin\theta ) cos100t +10sin100t = 0\)

 

This equation can be true for all \(t\) only if the coefficient of \(sin100t\) is zero and the coefficient of \(cos 100t\) is zero independently.

\(\therefore 5\sqrt{3}+A cos\theta +10=0\) and \(\theta=0\)

 

\(\therefore A=19 32\) and \(\theta =tan^{-1}\frac{-5}{-18.66}=180+tan^{-1}\frac{5}{18.66}=195^{o}\)

 

\(\therefore v_{1}=10+10 sin (100t-30^{o})V\) and \(v_{3}=19.32 sin (100t-195^{o})V\)

 

Now apply \(KVL\) in the outer loop to get,

\(-v_{S1}+v_{1}-v_{2}+v_{S3}+v_{S4}+v_{S2}=0\)

 

\(i.e.,-10+(10+10sin(100t-30^{o}))-C-Dsin(100t-\phi )+10cos(100t)+10+10sin100t=0\)

 

\((10+10sin(100t-30^{o}))-Dsin(100t-\phi )+10cos100t+10+10sin100t=0\)

 

\(8 66 sin100t-5cos100t-(Dcos\phi )sin100t+( Dsin\phi )cos100t+10 co s100t+10sin 100t=0\)

 

\(\therefore Dcos\phi =18.66;Dsin\phi=-5\Rightarrow D=19.32\) and \(\phi=tan^{-1}\frac{-5}{18.66}=-15^{o}\)

 

\(\therefore v_{2}=10+19.32 sin (100t+15^{o})V\)

 

Therefore, \(v_{1} = 10 + 10sin(100t – 30°) V, v t 2 = 10 +19.32sin(100 +15°)V\) and \(v_{3} = 19.32 sin(100t – 195°) V\) is the required answer.

The key to the solution of this problem is the point that \(KVL\) has to be satisfied at all time instants.
