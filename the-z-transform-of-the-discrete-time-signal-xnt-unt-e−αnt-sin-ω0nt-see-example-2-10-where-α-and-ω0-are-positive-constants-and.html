Guest Question.

The z transform of the discrete-time signal

\(x(n T)=u(n T) e^{-\alpha n T} \sin \omega_{0} n T\)

(see ) where α and ω0 are positive constants and

\(u(n T)= \begin{cases}1 & \text { for } n \geq 0 \\ 0 & \text { for } n<0\end{cases}\)

is the discrete-time unit-step function can be obtained as

\(X(z)=\frac{z e^{-\alpha T} \sin \omega_{0} T}{z^{2}-2 z e^{-\alpha T} \cos \omega_{0} T+e^{-2 \alpha T}}\)

(see Table 3.2). Deduce the frequency spectrum.

Admin
Solution

The given z transform can be expressed as

 

\(X(z)=\frac{a_{1} z}{z^{2}+b_{1} z+b_{0}}\)

 

where

 

\(a_{1}=e^{-\alpha T} \sin \omega_{0} T\)

 

\(b_{0}=e^{-2 \alpha T}\)

 

\(b_{1}=-2 e^{-\alpha T} \cos \omega_{0} T\)

 

The frequency spectrum of x(nT ) can be obtained by evaluating X(z) at \(z=e^{j \omega T}\), that is,

 

\(X\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)=\frac{a_{1} e^{j \omega T}}{e^{j 2 \omega T}+b_{1} e^{j \omega T}+b_{0}}\)

 

\(=\frac{a_{1} e^{j \omega T}}{\cos 2 \omega T+j \sin 2 \omega T+b_{1} \cos \omega T+j b_{1} \sin \omega T+b_{0}}\)

 

\(=\frac{a_{1} e^{j \omega T}}{b_{0}+b_{1} \cos \omega T+\cos 2 \omega T+j\left(b_{1} \sin \omega T+\sin 2 \omega T\right)}\)

 

\(=A(\omega) e^{j \phi(\omega)}\)

 

where

 

\(A(\omega)=\frac{\left|a_{1}\right| \cdot\left|e^{j \omega T}\right|}{\left|\left(b_{0}+b_{1} \cos \omega T+\cos 2 \omega T\right)+j\left(b_{1} \sin \omega T+\sin 2 \omega T\right)\right|}\)

 

\(=\frac{\left|a_{1}\right|}{\sqrt{\left(b_{0}+b_{1} \cos \omega T+\cos 2 \omega T\right)^{2}+\left(b_{1} \sin \omega T+\sin 2 \omega T\right)^{2}}}\)

 

\(=\frac{\left|a_{1}\right|}{\sqrt{1+b_{0}^{2}+b_{1}^{2}+2 b_{1}\left(1+b_{0}\right) \cos \omega T+2 b_{0} \cos 2 \omega T}}\)

 

(See Eq. (A.32b).) Since T > 0, we have

 

\(\begin{aligned}M(\omega) &=\left|\frac{N\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)}{D\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)}\right| \\&=\left\{\frac{\left[\Re e N\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)\right]^{2}+\left[\Im m N\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)\right]^{2}}{\left[\Re e D\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)\right]^{2}+\left[\Im m D\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)\right]^{2}}\right\}^{1 / 2}\end{aligned}\) (A.32b)

 

\(\phi(\omega)=\arg \left(a_{1}\right)+\arg e^{j \omega T}-\arg \left[b_{0}+b_{1} \cos \omega T+\cos 2 \omega T+j\left(b_{1} \sin \omega T+\sin 2 \omega T\right)\right]\)

 

\(=\arg a_{1}+\omega T-\tan ^{-1} \frac{b_{1} \sin \omega T+\sin 2 \omega T}{b_{0}+b_{1} \cos \omega T+\cos 2 \omega T}\)

 

where

 

\(\arg a_{1}=\left\{\begin{aligned}0 & \text { if } \geq 0 \\-\pi & \text { otherwise }\end{aligned}\right.\)

 

(See Eq. (A.32c).) The amplitude and phase spectrums of the discrete-time signal are illustrated in Fig. 3.10 for the case where \(\alpha=0.4 \text { and } \omega_{0}=2.0\) rad/s assuming a sampling frequency \(\omega_{s}=2 \pi / T=10\) rad/s.

 

\(\theta(\omega)=\arg H\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)=\arg N\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)-\arg D\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)\)

 

\(=\tan ^{-1} \frac{\Im m N\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)}{\Re e N\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)}-\tan ^{-1} \frac{\Im m D\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)}{\Re e D\left(e^{j \omega T}\right)}\) (A.32c)
