Guest Question.

In Cartesian coordinates, vector A points from the origin to point\(P_1=(2, 3, 3)\), and vector B is directed from \(P_1\) to point \(P_2=(1, −2, 2). \) Find:
(a) vector A, its magnitude A, and unit vector \(\hat{a} \),
(b) the angle between A and the y axis,
(c) vector B,
(d) the angle \(\theta _{AB}\) between A and B, and
(e) perpendicular distance from the origin to vector B

Admin
Solution

(a) Vector A is given by the position vector of P1 = (2, 3, 3) (Fig. 3-7). Thus,

\(A = \hat{X} 2 + \hat{Y} 3 + \hat{Z} 3 \)

 

\(A=\left|A\right| = \sqrt{2^2+3^2+3^2} =\sqrt{22} \)

 

\(\hat{a} \frac{A}{A} = (\hat{X} 2 + \hat{Y} 3 + \hat{a} \hat{Z} 3 ) /\sqrt{22} \)

(b) The angle β between A and the y axis is obtained from

\( A.\hat{Y} = \left|A\right|\left|\hat{Y}\right|\cos \beta = A\cos \beta\)

or

\( \beta =\cos ^{-1}(\frac{A.\hat{Y}}{A} )=\cos ^{-1}(\frac{3}{\sqrt{22} } )= 50.2^◦\)

(c)

\( B = \hat{X}(1 − 2) + \hat{Y}(−2 − 3) + \hat{Z}(2 − 3) = −\hat{X} − \hat{Y}5 − \hat{Z}.\)

(d)

\( θ_{AB} = cos^{−1}\left[\frac{A.B}{\left|A\right|\left|B\right|} \right]=cos^{−1}\left[\frac{-2-15-}{\sqrt{22}\sqrt{27} } \right]= 145.1^◦ \)

(e) The perpendicular distance between the origin and vector B
is the distance \( \left|\overrightarrow{OP_3} \right| \)
 shown in Fig. 3-7. From right triangle \(OP_1P_3 \),

\( \left|\overrightarrow{OP_3}\right|= |A| sin (180^◦ − θ_{AB}) =\sqrt{22} sin (180^◦ − 145.1^◦) = 2.68 \)
