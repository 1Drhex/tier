Guest Question.

Designing a simple inverter An inverter, as shown in Fig. 15.8, drives identical inverters and has \( V_{ OL }=0.3 V \), \( I_{ OL }=2 mA , V_{ OH }=2.4 V , I_{ OH }=100 \mu A , \text { and } V_{ CC }=5 V \). The input currents drawn by each load inverter are \( I_{ IH }=0.18 mA \text { (at logic high) and } I_{ IL }=10 \mu A \) (at logic low).

(a) If there are five load inverters, determine the value of pull-up resistance RP that will ensure a logic 1 output of \( V_{ OH }=2.4 V \).
(b) If \( R_{ P }=4 k \Omega ,\) find the fan-out N.

Admin
Solution

(a) At high output, all load inverters are connected to the driving inverter, and each draws 0.18 mA. Because of the resistance \( R_{ P } \), the output voltage will be lower than \( V_{ CC } \). Thus,

\( v_{ O }=V_{ CC }-R_{ P }\left(N I_{ IH }+I_{ OH }\right) \)

To ensure that \( v_{ O } \geq V_{ OH }=2.4 V \), we calculate the value of \( R_{ P }(\text { for } N=5) \) as

\( R_{ P } \leq \frac{V_{ CC }-V_{ OH }}{N I_{ IH }+I_{ OH }}=\frac{(5-2.4) V }{5 \times 0.18 mA +100 \mu A }=2.6 k \Omega \)

At low output, each load inverter draws \( I_{ IL }=10 \mu A \). Thus,

\( V_{ CC }=V_{ OL }+R_{ P }\left(I_{ OL }+N I_{ IL }\right) \)

To ensure that \( R_{ P } \geq I_{ OL }=2 mA \), we calculate the value of \( R_{ P } \) as

\( R_{ P } \geq \frac{V_{ CC }-V_{ OL }}{I_{ OL }+N I_{ IL }}=\frac{(5-0.3) V }{2 mA +5 \times 10 \mu A }=2.29 k \Omega \)

which depends mostly on the value of \( I_{ OL } \text { because } I_{ IL }<<I_{ OL } \). Therefore, the value of \( R_{ P } \) should be in the range \( 2.29 k \Omega \leq R_{ P } \leq 2.6 k \Omega \text {. Let us choose } R_{ P }=2.5 k \Omega \).

(b) For \( R_{ P }=4 k \Omega \), we get the value of N (for logic high) as

\( N=\frac{V_{ CC }-V_{ OH }}{R_{ P } I_{ IH }}-\frac{I_{ OH }}{I_{ IH }}=\frac{(5-2.4) V }{4 k \Omega \times 0.18 mA }-\frac{0.1 mA }{0.18 mA }=3.06 \)

Since fractional loads are not possible, the fan-out is N = 3. If we choose N = 4, \( v_{ O } \) will be less than \( V_{ OH }=2.4 V \), and the inverter will be in the transition (or logic 0) region.
