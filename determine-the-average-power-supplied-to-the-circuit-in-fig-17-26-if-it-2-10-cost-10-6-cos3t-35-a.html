Guest Question.

Determine the average power supplied to the circuit in Fig. 17.26 if \( i(t) = 2+ 10 \cos(t + 10°) + 6 \cos(3t + 35°) A \).

Admin
Solution

The input impedance of the network is

\( Z= 10 \parallel \frac{1}{j2\omega } = \frac{10(1/j2\omega )}{10+1/j2\omega } =\frac{10}{1+j20\omega } \)

Hence,

\( V=IZ = \frac{10I}{\sqrt{1+400\omega ^2} \angle \tan ^{-1}20\omega } \)

For the dc component, ω = 0.

I = 2 A \( \Rightarrow \) V = 10(2) = 20 V

This is expected, because the capacitor is an open circuit to dc and the entire 2-A current flows through the resistor. For ω = 1 rad/s,

\( I = 10\angle 10° \) \( \Rightarrow \) \( V =\frac{10(10 \angle 10^\circ )}{\sqrt{1+400}\angle \tan ^{-1} 20} = 5\angle -77.14° \)

For ω = 3 rad/s,

\( I = 6\angle 35° \) \( \Rightarrow \) \( V =\frac{10(6 \angle 35^\circ )}{\sqrt{1+3600}\angle \tan ^{-1} 60} = 1\angle -54.04° \)

Thus, in the time domain,

\( υ(t) = 20 + 5 \cos(t -77.14°) + 1 \cos(3t -54.04°) V \)

We obtain the average power supplied to the circuit by applying Eq. (17.46), as

\( P = V_{dc}I_{dc} + \frac{1}{2} \sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty }{V_n I_n \cos (\theta _n-\phi _n)} \) (17.46)

To get the proper signs of \( \theta _n \) and \( \phi _n \) we have to compare υ and i in this example with Eqs. (17.42) and (17.43). Thus,

\( \upsilon (t) = V_{dc} + \sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty }{V_n \cos (n\omega _0t-\theta _n)} \) (17.42)

\( i (t) = I_{dc} + \sum\limits_{m=1}^{\infty }{I_m \cos (m\omega _0t-\phi _m)} \) (17.43)

\( P=20(2)+\frac{1}{2} (5)(10)\cos [77.14°-(-10°)] + \frac{1}{2} (1)(6)\cos [54.04°-(-35°)] = 40+1.247+0.05=41.5 \)W

Alternatively, we can find the average power absorbed by the resistor as

\( P= \frac{V^2_{dc}}{R} +\frac{1}{2}\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty }{\frac{\left|V_n\right|^2 }{R} } = \frac{20^2}{10} +\frac{1}{2} .\frac{5^2}{10} +\frac{1}{2} .\frac{1^2}{10} =40 + 1.25 + 0.05 = 41.5 \)W.

which is the same as the power supplied, since the capacitor absorbs no average power.
