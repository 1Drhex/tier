Guest Question.

For the network in Fig. 17.14:
a. Compute I.
b. Find \(I_1, I_2, and I_3\).
c. Verify Kirchhoff’s current law by showing that \(I = I_1 + I_2 + I_3\)
d. Find the total impedance of the circuit.

Admin
Solution

a. Redrawing the circuit as in Fig. 17.15 reveals a strictly parallel network where

\(Z_1 = R_1 = 10 Ω ∠0°\\[0.5cm] Z_2 = R_2 + jX_{L_1} = 3 Ω + j 4 Ω\\[0.5cm] Z_3 = R_3 + jX_{L_2} – jX_C = 8 Ω + j 3 Ω – j 9 Ω = 8 Ω – j 6 Ω\)

The total admittance is

\( Y_T = Y_1 + Y_2 + Y_3\\[0.5cm] =\frac{1}{Z_1}+\frac{1}{Z_2}+\frac{1}{Z_3}=\frac{1}{10 Ω}+\frac{1}{3 Ω + j 4 Ω}+\frac{1}{8 Ω – j 6 Ω}\\[0.5cm] =0.1S+\frac{1}{ 5Ω ∠53.13°}+\frac{1}{10 Ω ∠-36.87°}\\[0.5cm] =0.1 S + 0.2 S ∠-53.13° + 0.1 S ∠36.87°\\[0.5cm] =0.1 S + 0.12 S – j 0.16 S + 0.08 S + j 0.06 S\\[0.5cm] =0.3 S – j 0.1 S = 0.316 S ∠-18.435°\)

Calculator The above mathematical exercise presents an excellent opportunity to demonstrate the power of some of today’s calculators. For the TI-89, the above operation is as shown in Fig. 17.16.

Be sure to use the negative sign for the complex number from the subtraction option and not the sign selection (-). The sign selection is used for negative angles in the polar form.

Converting to polar form requires the sequence shown in Fig. 17.17.

Convert to polar form:

The current I is given by

\( I = EY_T = (200 V ∠0°)(0.326 S ∠-18.435°)=63.2A∠-18.44°\)

 

b. Since the voltage is the same across parallel branches,

\(I_1=\frac{E}{Z_1}=\frac{200 V ∠0°}{10 Ω ∠0°}=20A\angle 0°\\[0.5cm] I_2=\frac{E}{Z_2}=\frac{200 V ∠0°}{5 Ω ∠ 53.13°}=40A\angle -53.13°\\[0.5cm] I_3=\frac{E}{Z_3}=\frac{200 V ∠0°}{10 Ω ∠-36.87°}=20A\angle +36.87° \)

 

c. \(I=I_1+I_2+I_3\\[0.5cm] 60 – j 20 = 20 ∠0° + 40 ∠-53.13° + 20 ∠+36.87°\\[0.5cm] =(20 + j 0) + (24 – j 32) + (16 + j 12)\\[0.5cm] 60 – j 20 = 60 – j 20\qquad (checks)\)

 

d. \(Z_T=\frac{1}{Y_T}=\frac{1}{0.316 S ∠-18.435°}=3.17\Omega\angle 18.44° \)
