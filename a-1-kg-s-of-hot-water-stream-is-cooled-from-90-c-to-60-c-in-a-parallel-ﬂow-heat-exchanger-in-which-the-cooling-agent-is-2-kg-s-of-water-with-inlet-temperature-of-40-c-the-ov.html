Guest Question.

A 1 kg/s of hot water stream is cooled from 90 \(^\circ C\) to 60 \(^\circ C\) in a parallel-ﬂow heat exchanger in which the cooling agent is 2 kg/s of water with inlet temperature of 40 \(^\circ C\). The overall heat transfer coeffcient U is 1000 W/ \(m^2\) K. Using the LMTD method, determine the required area of the heat exchanger. Take \(c_{_{ph}}=c_{_{pc}}=\)4182 J/kg K .

Admin
Solution
\(\dot{m}_hc_{ph}(T_{hi}-T_{ho})=\dot{m}_cc_{pc} (T_{co}-T_{ci})\)

1× 4182( 90-60) = 2× 4182 (\(T_{co}\) – 40)

\(T_{co}=55^\circ C\)

 

\(\Delta T_i=90-40=50^\circ C\)

 

\(\Delta T_o=60-55=5^\circ C\)

 

\(LMTD=\frac{50-5}{\ln \left\lgroup\frac{50}{5} \right\rgroup } =19.54^\circ C\)

Q = UA(LMTD)

1× 4182 × (90-60 ) = 1000 × A× 19.54

\(A_p=6.42m^2\)
