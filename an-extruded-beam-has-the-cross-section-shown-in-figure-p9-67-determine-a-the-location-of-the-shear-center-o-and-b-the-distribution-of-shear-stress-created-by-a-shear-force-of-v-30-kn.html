Guest Question.

An extruded beam has the cross section shown in Figure P9.67. Determine (a) the location of the shear center O, and (b) the distribution of shear stress created by a shear force of V = 30 kN.

Admin
Solution

(a) Moment of inertia about the neutral axis: Recognizing that the wall thickness is thin, the moment of inertia for the stiffened channel shape can be calculated as:

\(\begin{aligned}I_{N A} &=\frac{(3 mm )(80 mm )^{3}}{12}+2\left[(55 mm )(5 mm )\left(\frac{80 mm }{2}\right)^{2}\right] \\&+2\left[\frac{(3 mm )(30 mm )^{3}}{12}+(3 mm )(30 mm )(25 mm )^{2}\right] \\&=128,000 mm ^{4}+880,000 mm ^{4}+126,000 mm ^{4}=1,134,000 mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Shear Flow in Upper Stiffener: Derive an expression for Q for the upper stiffener as a function of a temporary variable v which originates at A and extends upward.

\(Q_{s}=\left(10 mm +\frac{v}{2}\right)[(3 mm ) v]=\left(30 mm ^{2}\right) v+(1.5 mm ) v^{2}\)

Express the shear flow q in the upper stiffener using \(Q_{s}\).

\(q_{s}=\frac{V Q_{s}}{I}=\frac{V}{1,134,000 mm ^{4}}\left[\left(30 mm ^{2}\right) v+(1.5 mm ) v^{2}\right]\)

Integrate with respect to the temporary variable v to determine the resultant force in the upper stiffener.

\(\begin{aligned}F_{s} &=\int_{0}^{30 mm } q_{s} d v \\&=\int_{0}^{30 mm } \frac{V}{1,134,000 mm ^{4}}\left[\left(30 mm ^{2}\right) v+(1.5 mm ) v^{2}\right] d v \\&=\frac{V}{1,134,000 mm ^{4}}\left[\frac{30 mm ^{2}}{2} v^{2}+\frac{1.5 mm }{3} v^{3}\right]_{0}^{30 mm }=0.0238095 V\end{aligned}\)

 

Shear Flow in Upper Flange: Derive an expression for Q for the upper flange as a function of a temporary variable u which originates at B and extends toward C in the upper flange.

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{f} &=(25 mm )(3 mm )(30 mm )+(40 mm )[(5 mm ) u] \\&=2,250 mm ^{3}+\left(200 mm ^{2}\right) u\end{aligned}\)

Express the shear flow q in the upper flange using \(Q_{f}\).

\(q_{f}=\frac{V Q_{f}}{I}=\frac{V}{1,134,000 mm ^{4}}\left[2,250 mm ^{3}+\left(200 mm ^{2}\right) u\right]\)

Integrate with respect to the temporary variable u to determine the resultant force in the upper flange.

\(\begin{aligned}F_{f} &=\int_{0}^{55 mm } q_{f} d u \\&=\int_{0}^{55 mm } \frac{V}{1,134,000 mm ^{4}}\left[2,250 mm ^{3}+\left(200 mm ^{2}\right) u\right] d u \\&=\frac{V}{1,134,000 mm ^{4}}\left[\left(2,250 mm ^{3}\right) u+\left(100 mm ^{2}\right) u^{2}\right]_{0}^{55 mm } \\&=\frac{V}{1,134,000 mm ^{4}}\left[123,750 mm ^{4}+302,500 mm ^{4}\right]=0.3758818 V\end{aligned}\)

 

Shear Flow in Web: Derive an expression for Q for the web as a function of a temporary variable v which originates at C and extends downward in the web.

\(\begin{gathered}Q_{w}=(25 mm )(3 mm )(30 mm )+(40 mm )(55 mm )(5 mm ) \\\quad+\left(40 mm -\frac{v}{2}\right)[(3 mm ) v] \\=2,250 mm ^{3}+11,000 mm ^{3}+\left(120 mm ^{2}\right) v-(1.5 mm ) v^{2}\end{gathered}\)

Express the shear flow q in the web using \(Q_{w}\).

\(q_{w}=\frac{V Q_{w}}{I}=\frac{V}{1,134,000 mm ^{4}}\left[13,250 mm ^{3}+\left(120 mm ^{2}\right) v-(1.5 mm ) v^{2}\right]\)

It is not required for this problem; however, for completeness, the resultant force in the web can be calculated as:

\(\begin{aligned}F_{w} &=2 \int_{0}^{40 mm } q_{w} d v \\&=2 \int_{0}^{40 mm } \frac{V}{1,134,000 mm ^{4}}\left[13,250 mm ^{3}+\left(120 mm ^{2}\right) v-(1.5 mm ) v^{2}\right] d v \\&=\frac{2 V}{1,134,000 mm ^{4}}\left[\left(13,250 mm ^{3}\right) v+\frac{120 mm ^{2}}{2} v^{2}-\frac{1.5 mm }{3} v^{3}\right]_{0}^{40 mm } \\&=\frac{2 V }{1,134,000 mm ^{4}}\left[530,000 mm ^{4}+96,000 mm ^{4}-32,000 mm ^{4}\right]=1.0476190 V\end{aligned}\)

 

Shear center: To determine the shear center, sum moments about E at the lower left corner of the channel web.

\(\begin{aligned}V e &=F_{f}(80 mm )+2 F_{s}(55 mm ) \\e &=\frac{F_{f}(80 mm )+2 F_{s}(55 mm )}{V} \\&=\frac{(0.3758818 V )(80 mm )+2(0.0238095 V )(55 mm )}{V} \\&=30.0705467 mm +2.6190476 mm =32.7 mm\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Shear Stress Distribution

\(\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|}\hline \text { Location } & \text { Element } & \begin{array}{c}q \\( N / mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}t \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\tau \\( MPa )\end{array} \\\hline \hline A & \text { Stiffener } & 0 & 3 & 0 \\\hline B & \text { Stiffener } & 59.5238 & 3 & 19.84 \\\hline B & \text { Flange } & 59.5238 & 5 & 11.90 \\\hline C & \text { Flange } & 350.5291 & 5 & 70.1 \\\hline C & \text { Web } & 350.5291 & 3 & 116.8 \\\hline D & \text { Web } & 414.0212 & 3 & 138.0 \\\hline\end{array}\)
