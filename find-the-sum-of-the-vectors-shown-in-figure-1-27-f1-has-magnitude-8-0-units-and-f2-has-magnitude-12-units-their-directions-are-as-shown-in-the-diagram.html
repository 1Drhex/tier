Guest Question.

Find the sum of the vectors shown in Figure 1.27. \(F_1\) has magnitude 8.0 units and \(F_2\) has magnitude 12 units. Their directions are as shown in the diagram.

Admin
Solution

Find the components of the two vectors:
\(F_{1x} = −F_1\) cos 42°
\(F_{1x}\) = −5.945
\(F_{1y} = F_1\) sin 42°
\(F_{1y}\) = 5.353
\(F_{2x} = F_2\) cos 28°
\(F_{2x}\) = 10.595
\(F_{2y} = F_2\) sin 28°
\(F_{2y}\) = 5.634
The sum \(F = F_1 + F_2\) then has components:
\(F_x = F_{1x} + F_{2x}\) = 4.650
\(F_y = F_{1y} + F_{2y}\) = 10.987
The magnitude of the sum is therefore:
F = \(\sqrt{4.650^2 + 10.987^2} \)
F = 11.9 ≈ 12
and its direction is:
φ = arctan \(\left(\frac{10.987}{4.65} \right) \)
φ = 67.1 ≈ 67°
