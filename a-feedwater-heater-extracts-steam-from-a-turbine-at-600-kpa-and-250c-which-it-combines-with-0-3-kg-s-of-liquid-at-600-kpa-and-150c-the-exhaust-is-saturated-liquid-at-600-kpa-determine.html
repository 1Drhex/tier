Guest Question.

A feedwater heater extracts steam from a turbine at 600 kPa and 250°C, which it combines with 0.3 kg /s of liquid at 600 kPa and 150°C. The exhaust is saturated liquid at 600 kPa. Determine the second-law effectiveness of the heater.

Admin
Solution

For data, see Table 7.5. By the conservation of mass, \( \dot{m}_{3}=\dot{m}_{1}+\dot{m}_{2} \text {. Then, the first law demands } \dot{m}_{3} h_{3}=\dot{m}_{1} h_{1}+\dot{m}_{2} h_{2} \).

Solving simultaneously for \( \dot{m}_{1} \text { and } \dot{m}_{3} \).

\( \dot{m}_{1}=0.00504 kg / s \quad \dot{m}_{3}=0.305 kg / s \).

Table 7.5

Exit State 3: Saturated Liquid Inlet State 2: Compressed Liquid Inlet State 1: Superheated Vapor
T = 158.9°C T = 150°C T = 250°C
P = 0.6 MPa P = 0.6 MPa P = 0.6 MPa
h = 670.6 kJ/ kg h = 632.2 kJ/ kg h = 2957.2
s = 1.9316 kJ/ kg⋅K s = 1.8422 kJ/ kg⋅K s = 7.1824 kJ/ kg⋅K

The second-law effectiveness is \( \varepsilon_{ II }=\dot{\Psi}_{3} /\left(\dot{\Psi}_{1}+\dot{\Psi}_{2}\right) \). Taking the dead state as liquid water at 25°C and 100 kPa, we have

\( h_{0}=105 kJ / kg \quad s_{0}=0.3672 kJ / kg \cdot K \).

Then

\( \dot{\Psi}_{3}=\dot{m}\left[h_{3}-h_{0}-T_{0}\left(s_{3}-s_{0}\right)\right]=(0.305)[670.6-105-298(1.9316-0.3672)]=30.33 kW \).

\( \left.\left.\dot{\Psi}_{1}=\dot{m}_{1}\left[h_{1}-h_{0}-T_{0}\left(s_{1}-s_{0}\right)\right]=(0.00504)\right] 2957.2-105-298(7.1824-0.3672)\right]=4.14 kW \).

\( \dot{\Psi}_{2}=\dot{m}_{2}\left[h_{2}-h_{0}-T_{0}\left(s_{2}-s_{0}\right)\right]=(0.30)[632.2-105-298(1.8422-0.3672)]=23.63 kW \).

and

\( \varepsilon_{ II }=\frac{30.33}{4.14+23.63}=1.09 \).
