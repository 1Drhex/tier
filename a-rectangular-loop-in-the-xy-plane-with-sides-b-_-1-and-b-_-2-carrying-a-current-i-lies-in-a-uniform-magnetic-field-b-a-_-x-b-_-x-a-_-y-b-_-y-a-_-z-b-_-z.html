Guest Question.

A rectangular loop in the \(xy\)-plane with sides \({ b }_{ 1 }\) and \({ b }_{ 2 }\) carrying a current \(I\) lies in a uniform magnetic field \(B={ a }_{ x }{ B }_{ x }+{ a }_{ y }{ B }_{ y }+{ a }_{ z }{ B }_{ z }\). Determine the force and torque on the loop.

Admin
Solution

Resolving \(B\) into perpendicular and parallel components \({ B }_{ \bot }\) and \({ B }_{ \parallel }\). we have

\({ B }_{ \bot }={ a }_{ z }{ B }_{ z };\)

 

\({ B }_{ \parallel }={ a }_{ x }{ B }_{ x }+{ a }_{ y }{ B }_{ y }\)

 

Assuming that the current now in a clockwise direction. a shown in Fig. 5-21. we find that the perpendicular c0mponent \({ a }_{ z }{ B }_{ z }\); result in forces \(I{ b }_{ 1 }{ B }_{ z }\). on sides \((1)\) and \((3)\) and forces \(I{ b }_{ 2 }{ B }_{ z }\). on side \((2)\) and \((4)\). all directed toward the center of the loop. The vector sum of these four contracting forces is zero. and no torque is produced .
The parallel component of the magnetic flux density, \({ B }_{ \parallel }\), produces the following force on the four side:

\({ F }_{ 1 }=I{ b }_{ 1 }{ a }_{ x }\times \left( { a }_{ x }{ B }_{ x }+{ a }_{ y }{ B }_{ y } \right)\)

 

\(\quad \quad ={ a }_{ z }I{ b }_{ 1 }{ B }_{ y }=-{ F }_{ 3 }\)

 

\({ F }_{ 2 }=I{ b }_{ 2 }\left( -{ a }_{ y } \right) \times \left( { a }_{ x }{ B }_{ x }+{ a }_{ y }{ B }_{ y } \right)\)

 

\(\quad \quad ={ a }_{ z }I{ b }_{ 2 }{ B }_{ x }=-{ F }_{ 4 }\)

 

Again. the net force on the loop. \({ F }_{ 1 } + { F }_{ 2 } + { F }_{ 3 } + { F }_{ 4 }\) , is zero. However. these forces result in a net torque that can be computed as follows. The torque \({ T }_{ 13 }\), due to force \({ F }_{ 1 }\) and \({ F }_{ 3 }\) on sides \(( 1)\) and \((3)\), is

\({ T }_{ 13 }={ a }_{ x }I{ b }_{ 1 }{ b }_{ 2 }{ B }_{ v }\)

 

the torque \({ T }_{ 24 }\), due to forces \({ F }_{ 2 }\) and \({ F }_{ 4 }\) on sides \((2)\) and \((4)\). is

\({ T }_{ 24 }={ a }_{ y }I{ b }_{ 1 }{ b }_{ 2 }{ B }_{ x }\)

 

The total torque on the rectangular loop is then

\(T={ T }_{ 13 }{ T }_{ 24 }=I{ b }_{ 1 }{ b }_{ 2 }\left( { a }_{ x }{ B }_{ y }-{ a }_{ y }{ B }_{ x } \right) \quad \left( N/m \right)\)
