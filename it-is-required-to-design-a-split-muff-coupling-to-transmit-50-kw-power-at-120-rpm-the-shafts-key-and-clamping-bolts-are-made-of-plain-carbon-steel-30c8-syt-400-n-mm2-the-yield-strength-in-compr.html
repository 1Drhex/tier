Guest Question.

It is required to design a split muff coupling to transmit 50 kW power at 120 rpm The shafts, key and clamping bolts are made of plain carbon steel 30C8 \( \left(S_{y t}=400 N / mm ^{2}\right) \). The yield strength in compression is 150% of the tensile yield strength. The factor of safety for shafts, key and bolts is 5. The number of clamping bolts is 8. The coefficient of friction between sleeve halves and the shaft is 0.3.
(i) Calculate the diameter of the input and output shafts.
(ii) Specify the length and outer diameter of the sleeve halves.
(iii) Find out the diameter of clamping bolts assuming that the power is transmitted by friction.
(iv) Specify bolt diameter using standard empirical relations.
(v) Specify the size of key and check the dimensions for shear and compression criteria.

Admin
Solution

Given kW = 50 n = 120 rpm μ = 0.3.

\( \text { For shafts, key and bolts } S_{y t}=400 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( (f s)=5 \quad n=8 \quad S_{y c}=1.5 S_{y t} \).

Step I Permissible stresses
For the material of shafts, key and bolt,

\( \sigma_{t}=\frac{S_{y t}}{(f s)}=\frac{400}{5}=80 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \sigma_{c}=\frac{S_{y c}}{(f s)}=\frac{1.5(400)}{5}=120 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \tau=\frac{S_{s y}}{(f s)}=\frac{0.5 S_{y t}}{(f s)}=\frac{0.5(400)}{5}=40 N / mm ^{2} \).

Step II Diameter of each shaft

\( M_{t}=\frac{60 \times 10^{6}( kW )}{2 \pi n} \).

\( =\frac{60 \times 10^{6}(50)}{2 \pi(120)}=3978873.58 N – mm \).

\( \tau=\frac{16 M_{t}}{\pi d^{3}} \quad \therefore 40=\frac{16(3978873.58)}{\pi d^{3}} \).

\( \therefore \quad d=79.72 \text { or } 80 mm \) (i).

Step III Length and outer diameter of sleeve halves
From Eqs (9.35) and (9.36),

D = 2.5d (9.35)
L = 3.5d (9.36)

D = 2.5 d = 2.5 (80) = 200 mm
L = 3.5 d = 3.5 (80) = 280 mm (ii)
Step IV Diameter of clamping bolts—Friction basis
From Eq. (9.40),

\( P_{1}=\frac{2 M_{t}}{f d n} \) (9.40).

\( P_{1}=\frac{2 M_{t}}{f d n}=\frac{2(3978873.58)}{0.3(80)(8)}=41446.6 N \).

From Eq. (9.39),

\( P_{1}=\frac{\pi}{4} d_{1}^{2} \sigma_{t} \) (9.39).

\( P_{1}=\frac{\pi}{4} d_{1}^{2} \sigma_{t} \quad \text { or } \quad 41446.6=\frac{\pi}{4} d_{1}^{2}(80) \).

\( \therefore \quad d_{1}=25.68 \text { or } 26 mm \).

Step V Diameter of clamping bolts—Empirical relation
From Eq. (9.38),

\( d_{1}=0.15 d+15 mm \) (9.38).

\( d_{1}=0.15 d+15=0.15(80)+15=27 mm \).

Step VI Dimensions of key
From Table 9.3, the standard cross-section of flat key for an 80 mm diameter shaft is 22 × 14 mm.

The length of the key in each shaft is one-half of the length of the sleeve half.
Therefore,

\( l=\frac{L}{2}=\frac{280}{2}=140 mm \).

The dimensions of the key are,
22 × 14 × 140 mm (v)

Step VII Check for stresses in key
From Eq. (9.27),

\( \tau=\frac{2 M_{t}}{d b l} \) (9.27).

\( \tau=\frac{2 M_{t}}{d b l}=\frac{2(3978873.58)}{80(22)(140)} \).

\( =32.3 N / mm ^{2} \quad \therefore \tau<40 N / mm ^{2} \).

From Eq. (9.28),

\( \sigma_{c}=\frac{4 M_{t}}{d h l} \) (9.28).

\( \sigma_{c}=\frac{4 M_{t}}{d h l}=\frac{4(3978873.58)}{80(14)(140)} \).

\( =101.5 N / mm ^{2} \quad \therefore \sigma_{c}<120 N / mm ^{2} \).

The design of key is safe from shear and compression considerations.
