Find the surface temperature at radial distances of (a) 3 mm and (b)12 mm from the axis of the jet.

\( \mu = 993 \times 10^{-6}N s/m^2\)

 

\( k =0.597 W/m K\)

 

 

\( Pr = 7.0\)

 

 

\(Re_d =\frac{\dot{m} }{\pi d\mu }=\frac{4 \times 0.008}{\pi \times 0.006 \times 993 \times 10^{-6}}= 1709\)

 

(a) For r = 3 mm, r/ d = 0.003/0.006 0.5 (<0.8).

 

From Eq. (7.45),

 

\(Pr\gt 3\) \( \overline{Nu} _{d}= 0.797 Re_d^{1/2}Pr^{1/3}\) (7.45)

 

\(\overline{Nu} _{d}= \frac{\overline{h} _{c}d }{k} = 0.797\times 1709^{1/2}\times 7.0^{1/3}=63.0\)

 

\(\overline{h} _{c}= \frac{63.0 \times 0.597}{0.006}=6269 W/m^2 °C\)

 

\(T_s =T_j +\frac{q″}{h_c} =20 +\frac{70,000}{6269} = 31.2 °C\)

 

(b) For r = 12 mm,\( r_v = 0.1773 \times 1709^{1/3} \times 0.006 = 0.013\) m and \(r \lt r_v\).

 

From Eq. (7.48) for Region II,

 

\(\overline{Nu} _{d}= 0.632 Re_d ^{1/2}Pr^{1/3}\left(\frac{d}{r} \right)^{1/2}\) (7.48)

 

 

\(\overline{Nu} _{d}= 0.632 \times 1709 ^{1/2}\times 7.0^{1/3}\left(\frac{0.006}{0.012} \right)^{1/2}= 35.3\)

 

\(\overline{h} _{c} =\frac{35.3 \times 0.597}{0.006}= 3512 W/m^2°C\) \( T_s = 20 + \frac{70,000}{3512}= 39.9 °C\)
