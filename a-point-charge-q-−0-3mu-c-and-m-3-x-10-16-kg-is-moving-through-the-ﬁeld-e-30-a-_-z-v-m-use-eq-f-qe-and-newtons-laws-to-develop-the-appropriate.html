Guest Question.

A point charge, \(Q = −0.3\mu C\) and m = 3 × \({ 10 }^{ -16 }\)kg, is moving through the ﬁeld E = 30 \({ a }_{ z }\) V/m. Use Eq. F = QE and Newton’s laws to develop the appropriate differential equations and solve them, subject to the initial conditions at t =0: \(v = 3 \times{ 10 }^{ 5 }{ a }_{ x }\) m/s at the origin. At t = \(3\mu S\), ﬁnd:(a) the position P(x, y, z) of the charge. (b) the velocity, v. (c) the kinetic energy of the charge.

Admin
Solution

a) The force on the charge is given by F = qE, and Newton’s second law becomes:
\(F= ma=m\frac { { d }^{ 2 }z }{ d{ t }^{ 2 } } =qE=(-0.3\times { 10 }^{ -6 })(30{ a }_{ z })\)

describing motion of the charge in the z direction. The initial velocity in x is constant, and so no force is applied in that direction. We integrate once:
\(\frac { dz }{ dt } ={ \upsilon }_{ z }=\frac { qE }{ m } t+{ C }_{ 1 }\)

The initial velocity along z, \(\upsilon z(0)\) is zero, and so \({ C }_{ 1 }\) = 0. Integrating a second time yields the z coordinate:
\(z=\frac { qE }{ 2m } { t }^{ 2 }+{ C }_{ 2 }\)

The charge lies at the origin at t =0, and so \({ C }_{ 2}\) =0. Introducing the given values, we ﬁnd
\(z=\frac { \left( -0.3\times { 10 }^{ -6 } \right) \left( 30 \right) }{ 2\times 3\times { 10 }^{ -16 } } { t }^{ 2 }=-1.5\times { 10 }^{ 10 }{ t }^{ 2 }m\)
At t = \(3\mu s, z = -(1.5\times { 10 }^{ 10 }){ (3\times { 10 }^{ -6 }) }^{ 2 }\) = -.135 cm. Now, considering the initial constant velocity in x, the charge in 3 \(\mu s\) attains an x coordinate of x = \(\upsilon t = (3\times { 10 }^{ 5 }) (3\times { 10 }^{ -6 })\) = .90 m. in summary, at t = 3 \(\mu s\) we have P(x, y, z) = (.90, 0, -.135).
b) After the ﬁrst integration in part a, we ﬁnd
\({ \upsilon }_{ z }=\frac { qE }{ m } t=-(3\times { 10 }^{ 10 })(3\times { 10 }^{ -6 })=-9\times { 10 }^{ 4 }m/s\)

Including the intial x-directed velocity, we ﬁnally obtain \(v = 3 \times { 10 }^{ 5 }{ a }_{ x } – 9 \times { 10 }^{ 4 }{ a }_{z } m/s\).

c) Have
\(K.E. =\frac { 1 }{ 2 } m{ \left| \upsilon \right| }^{ 2 }=\frac { 1 }{ 2 } (3\times { 10 }^{ -16 }){ \left( 1.13\times { 10 }^{ 5 } \right) }^{ 2 }=1.5\times { 10 }^{ -5 }J\)
