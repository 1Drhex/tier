Guest Question.

Analyzing the CMOS op-amp MC14573 The CMOS amplifier in Fig. 14.20 is operated at a biasing current of \( I_{ Q }=40 \mu A \). The parameters of the MOSFETs are \( K_{ x }=10 \mu A / V ^{2},\left|V_{ M ( NMOS )}\right|=V_{ M ( PMOS )}=70 V \), \( V_{ t }=0.5 V , \text { and } W=160 \mu m / 10 \mu m , \), except for \( Q_7 \), for which \( W / L=320 \mu m / 10 \mu m . \text { Assume } V_{ DD }= -V_{ SS }=5 V \).

(a) Find \( V_{ GS }, g_{ m }, \text { and } r_{ o } \) for all MOSFETs.
(b) Find the low-frequency voltage gain of the amplifier \( A_{\text {vo }} \).
(c) Find the value of the external resistance \( R_{\text {ref }} \).
(d) Find the value of compensation capacitance \( C_{ x } \) that gives a unity-gain bandwidth of 1 MHz and the corresponding slew rate.
(e) Find the value of resistance \( R_{ x } \) to be connected in series with \( C_{ x } \) in order to move the zero frequency to infinity.
(f) Find the common-mode input voltage range.
(g) Find the output voltage range.

Admin
Solution

(a) \( K_{ p }, V_{ GS }, g_{ m }, \text { and } r_{ o } \) are calculated using the following equations:

\( K_{ p }=\frac{K_{ x } W}{L} \) (14.68)

\(W / L(\text { in } \mu m / \mu m )\) 160⁄10 160⁄10 160⁄10 160⁄10 160⁄10 160⁄10 320 ⁄10 160⁄10
\(K_{ p }\left(\text { in } \mu A / V ^{2}\right)\) 160 160 160 160 160 160 320 160
\(I_{D}(i n \mu A)\) 20 20 20 20 40 40 40 40
\(V_{ GS }(\text { in } V )\) 0.854 0.854 0.854 0.854 1 1 0.854 1
\(g_{ m }(\text { in } \mu A / V )\) 113.1 113.1 113.1 113.1 160 160 226.3 160
\(r_{ o }(\text { in } M \Omega)\) 3.5 3.5 3.5 3.5 1.75 1.75 1.75 1.75

 

\( V_{ GS }=V_{ t }+\sqrt{\frac{I_{ D }}{K_{ p }}} \) (14.69)

 

\( g_{ m }=2 K_{ p }\left(V_{ GS }-V_{ t }\right)=2 \sqrt{K_{ p } I_{ D }} \) (14.70)

 

\( r_{ o }=\frac{\left|V_{ M }\right|}{I_{ D }} \) (14.71)

Their values are shown in Table 14.2.

(b) Using Eq. (7.34) the voltage gain of the first differential stage with the active load is given by

\( G_{ mo }=\frac{i_{ o }}{v_{ gs }}=-g_{ m } \) (7.34)

 

\(\begin{aligned}A_{ v 1} &=-g_{ m 4}\left(r_{ o 2} \| r_{ o 4}\right) \\&=-113.1 \mu \times(3.5 M \| 3.5 M )=-198\end{aligned}\) (14.72)

 

The voltage gain of the second stage with the active load is given by

\(\begin{aligned}A_{ v 2} &=-g_{ m 7}\left(r_{ o 7} \| r_{ o 8}\right) \\&=-226.3 \mu \times(1.75 M \| 1.75 M )=-198\end{aligned}\) (14.73)

 

Thus, the overall voltage gain is \( A_{ vo }=A_{ v 1} A_{ v 2}=198 \times 198=39,204(\text { or } 91.87 dB ).\)

(c) From Eq. (14.67), we get the value of the external resistance \( R_{\text {ref }} \) as

\( I_{ Q }=I_{ ref }=\frac{V_{ DD }+V_{ SS }-V_{ SG }}{R_{ ref }} \) (14.67)

 

\( R_{ ref }=\frac{V_{ DD }-V_{ SS }-V_{ GS 5}}{I_{ Q }}=\frac{(5+5-1) V }{40 \mu A }=225 k \Omega \)

 

(d) For \( f_{ u }=1 MHz \), Eq. (14.56) gives the value of compensation capacitance \( C_{ x } \) as

\( f_{ u }=\frac{G_{ m 1}}{2 \pi C _{ x }} \) (14.56)

 

\( C_{ x }=\frac{G_{ m 1}}{2 \pi f_{ u }}=\frac{G_{ m 2}}{2 \pi f_{ u }}=\frac{113.1 \mu A / V }{2 \pi \times 1 MHz }=18 pF \)

 

From Eq. (14.63), we get the slew rate as

\( SR =\left.\frac{d v_{ o }}{d t}\right|_{\max }=\frac{i_{ ol (\max )}}{C_{ x }}=\pm \frac{I_{ Q }}{C_{ x }} \) (14.63)

 

\( SR =\frac{I_{ Q }}{C_{ x }}=\frac{40 \mu A }{18 pF }=2.22 V / \mu s \)

 

(e) From Eq. (14.59), the value of resistance \( R_{ X } \text { for } \omega_{ z }=0 \) is

\( \omega_{ z }=\frac{1}{C_{ x }\left(1 / G_{ m 2}-R_{ x }\right)} \) (14.59)

 

\( R_{ x }=\frac{1}{G_{ m 2}}=\frac{1}{g_{ m 7}}=\frac{1}{226.3 \mu A / V }=4.42 k \Omega \)

 

(f) Transistor \( Q_{6} \) will leave saturation when the common-mode input voltage becomes

\( v_{ ic (\max )}=V_{ DD }-\left|V_{ GS 6}\right|+\left|V_{ t }\right|-\left|V_{ GS 1}\right|=5-1+0.5-0.854=3.646 V \)

 

Transistors \( Q _{1} \text { and } Q _{2} \) will leave saturation when the common-mode input voltage falls below the voltage at the drain of \( Q _{1} \text { by }\left|V_{ t }\right| \)—that is, when

\( v_{ ic (\min )}=-V_{ SS }+\left|V_{ GS 3}\right|-\left|V_{ t }\right|=-5+1-0.5=-4.5 V \)

Thus, the common-mode input voltage range is -4.5 V to 3.646 V.

(g) Transistor \( Q _{8} \) will leave saturation when the output voltage becomes

\( v_{ o (\max )}=V_{ DD }-\left|V_{ GS 8}\right|+\left|V_{ t }\right|=5-1+0.5=4.5 V \)

Transistor \( Q_7 \) will leave saturation when the output voltage becomes

\( v_{ o (\min )}=-V_{ SS }+\left|V_{ GS 7}\right|-\left|V_{ t }\right|=-5+0.854-0.5=-4.646 V \)

Thus, the output voltage range is -4.646 V to 4.5 V.
