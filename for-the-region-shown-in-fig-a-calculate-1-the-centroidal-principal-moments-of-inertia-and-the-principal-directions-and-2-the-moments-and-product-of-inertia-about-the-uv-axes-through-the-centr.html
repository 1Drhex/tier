Guest Question.

For the region shown in Fig. (a), calculate (1) the centroidal principal moments of inertia and the principal directions; and (2) the moments and product of inertia about the uv-axes through the centroid C.

Admin
Solution

Centroidal Properties

The table below lists the computations that have been used to determine the cen-troidal coordinates and the inertial properties with respect to the x- and y-axes.The region is considered as a composite of the two rectangles shown in Fig. (b). Their moments of inertia have been calculated using Table 9.1.

The centroidal coordinates, shown in Fig. (b), are computed from the results in Table 9.1 as follows:

\(\bar{x}=\frac{\Sigma A\bar{x} }{\Sigma A}=\frac{460.5\times 10^{3}}{9900}=46.52 mm\)

 

\(\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma A\bar{y} }{\Sigma A}=\frac{685.5\times 10^{3}}{9900}=66.52 mm\)

The parallel-axis theorem is then used to calculate the inertial properties about the centroidal axes.

\(\bar{I}_{x}=I_{x}-A\bar{y}^{2}=(81.17\times 10^{6})-(9900)(66.52)^{2}\)

 

\(= 37.36 × 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

 

\(\bar{I}_{y}=I_{y}-A\bar{x}^{2}=(42.49\times 10^{6})-(9900)(46.52)^{2}\)

 

\(= 21.07 × 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

 

\(\bar{I}_{xy}=I_{xy}-A\bar{x}\bar{y} =(14.56\times 10^{6})-(9900)(46.52)(66.52)\)

 

\(= −16.08 × 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

Part 1

Substituting the values for \(\bar{I}_{x} , \bar{I}_{y}\) , and \(\bar{I}_{xy}\) into Eq. (9.22) \(R=\sqrt{\left(\frac{I_{x}-I_{y}}{2}\right)^{2}+I_{xy}^{2} }\) ,yields

\(R=10^{6}\sqrt{\left(\frac{37.36-21.07}{2}\right)^{2}+(-16.08)^{2}}=18.03\times 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

Therefore Eq. (9.23) \(I_{1,2}=\frac{I_{x}+I_{y}}{2}\pm R\) ,becomes

\(I_{1,2}=\left(\frac{37.36 + 21.07}{2}\pm 18.03 \right)\times 10^{6}=(29.22\pm 18.03)\times 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

from which we obtain the principal moments of inertia

\(I_{1} = 47.3 × 10^{6} mm^{4}\) \(I_{2} = 11.2 × 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

For the principal directions, Eqs. (9.21) \(\sin 2\theta _{1.2}=\mp \frac{I_{xy}}{R}\),\(\cos 2\theta _{1.2}=\pm \frac{I_{x}-I_{y}}{2R}\) yield

\(\sin 2\theta _{1.2}=\mp \frac{\bar{I}_{xy}}{R}=\mp \frac{(-16.08)}{18.03}=\pm 0.8919\)

Because the upper sign goes with \(θ_{1}\), we have \(2θ_{1} = 63.11^{\circ}\)or \(116.9^{\circ}\), and \(2θ_{2} = 243.11^{\circ}\)or \(−63.11^{\circ}\). To determine the correct choices, we investigate the sign of \(\cos 2θ_{1}\). From Eqs. (9.21) we obtain

\(\cos 2θ_{1} = ( \bar{I}_{x} − \bar{I}_{y}) / (2R) = (37.36 − 21.07) / [2(18.03)]\)

which is positive. Therefore, \(2θ_{1} = 63.11^{\circ}\) and \(2θ_{2} = 243.11^{\circ}\) are the correct choices, which give

\(θ_{1} = 31.6^{\circ}\) \(θ_{2} = 121.6^{\circ}\)

The principal axes, labeled 1 and 2 in Fig. (c), correspond to the axes of \(I_{1}\) and \(I_{2}\), respectively.

Part 2

To compute the moments and product of inertia relative to the uv-axes in Fig. (a), we need only substitute \(\bar{I}_{x} = 37.36 × 10^{6} mm^{4}\), \(\bar{I}_{y} = 21.07 × 10^{6} mm^{4}\), \(\bar{I}_{xy} = −16.08 × 10^{6} mm^{4}\), and \(θ = 50^{\circ}\) into the transformation equations. From Eq. (9.17) \(I_{u}=\frac{1}{2}(I_{x}+I_{y})+\frac{1}{2}(I_{x}-I_{y})\cos 2\theta -I_{xy}\sin 2\theta\) we obtain

\(I_{u}\times 10^{-6}=\frac{37.36 + 21.07}{2}+\frac{37.36 + 21.07}{2}\cos 100^{\circ }-(-16.08)\sin 100^{\circ }\)

 

\(I_{u} = 43.6 × 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

Equation (9.18) \(I_{v}=\frac{1}{2}(I_{x}+I_{y})-\frac{1}{2}(I_{x}-I_{y})\cos 2\theta +I_{xy}\sin 2\theta\) yields

 

\(I_{v}\times 10^{-6}=\frac{37.36 + 21.07}{2}-\frac{37.36 + 21.07}{2}\cos 100^{\circ }+(-16.08)\sin 100^{\circ }\)

 

\(I_{v} = 14.8 × 10^{6} mm^{4}\)

From Eq. (9.19) \(I_{uv}=\frac{1}{2}(I_{x}-I_{y})\sin 2\theta +I_{xy}\cos 2\theta\) we have

 

\(I_{uv}\times 10^{-6}=\frac{37.36 – 21.07}{2}\sin 100^{\circ }+(-16.08)\cos 100^{\circ }\)

 

\(I_{uv} = 10.8 × 10^{6} mm^{4}\)
