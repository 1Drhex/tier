Guest Question.

A piston-cylinder contains 3 kg of wet steam at 1.4 bar. The initial volume is 2.25 \(\textbf{m}^{3}\). The steam is heated until its’ temperature reaches 400°C. The piston is free to move up or down unless it reaches the stops at the top. When the piston is up against the stops the cylinder volume is 4.65 \(\textbf{m}^{3}\). Determine the amounts of work and heat transfer to or from steam.[U.P.S.C. 1994]

Admin
Solution
\(\begin{aligned}\text{From steam table, specific volume of steam at 1.4 bar}&= 1.2455 m^{3}/kg\\&= v_{\text{g at 1.4 bar}}\\\text{Specific volume of wet steam in cylinder, }v_{1}&= \frac{2.25}{3} = 0.75 m^{3}/kg\\\text{Dryness fraction of initial steam, }x_{1}&= \frac{0.75}{1.2455} = 0.602\\\text{Initial enthalpy of wet steam, }h_{1}&= h_{\text{f at 1.4 bar}} + x_{1} · h_{\text{fg at 1.4 bar}}\\&= 457.99 + (0.602 × 2232.3) ⇒ h_{1} = 1801.83 kJ/kg\\\text{At 400°C specific volume of steam, }v_{2}&= \frac{4.65}{3} = 1.55 m^{3}/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{For specific volume of 1.55 }m^{3}\text{/kg at 400°C the pressure can be seen from the steam table. From superheated steam}\\\text{tables the specific volume of 1.55 }m^{3}\text{/kg lies between the pressure of 0.10 MPa(specific volume 3.103 }m^{3}\text{/kg at 400°C)}\\\text{and 0.20 MPa (specific volume 1.5493 }m^{3}\text{/kg at 400°C). Actual pressure can be obtained by interpolation;}\\\begin{aligned}P_{2}&= 0.10 + \left\lgroup\frac{0.20 – 0.10}{(1.5493 – 3.103)} \right\rgroup × (1.55 – 3.103)\\P_{2}&= 0.199 MPa ≈ 0.20 MPa\\\text{Saturation pressure at 0.20 MPa}&= 120.23°C\\\text{Finally the degree of superheat}&= 400 – 120.23\\&= 279.77°C\\\text{Final enthalpy of steam at 0.20 MPa and 400°C, }h_{2}&= 3276.6 kJ/kg\\\text{Heat added during process}&= m (h_{2} – h_{1})\\&= 3 × (3276.6 – 1801.83)\\ΔQ&= 4424.31 kJ\\\text{Internal energy of initial wet steam, }u_{1}&= u_{\text{f at 1.4 bar}} + x_{1} . u_{\text{fg at 1.4 bar}}\\u_{1}&= 457.84 + (0.607 × 2059.34)\\u_{1}&= 1707.86 kJ/kg\\\end{aligned}\\\text{Internal energy of final state,}\\\begin{aligned}u_{2}&= u_{\text{at 0.2 MPa, 400°C}}\\u_{2}&= 2966.7 kJ/kg\\\text{Change in internal energy ⇒ ΔU}&= m(u_{2} – u_{1})\\&= 3 × (2966.7 – 1707.86)\\ΔU&= 3776.52 kJ\\\text{From first law of thermodynamics,}&\\\text{Work done ΔW}&= ΔQ – ΔU\\&= 4424.31 – 3776.52\\\text{Work done , ΔW}&= 647.79 kJ\\\text{Heat transfer}&= \textbf{4424.31 kJ}\\\text{Work transfer}&= \textbf{647.79 kJ}\end{aligned}\)
