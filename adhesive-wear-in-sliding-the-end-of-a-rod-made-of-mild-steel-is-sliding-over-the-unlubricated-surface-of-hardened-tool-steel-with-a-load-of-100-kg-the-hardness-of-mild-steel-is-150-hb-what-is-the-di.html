Guest Question.

Adhesive wear in sliding

The end of a rod made of mild steel is sliding over the unlubricated surface of hardened tool steel with a load of 100 kg. The hardness of mild steel is 150 HB. What is the distance traveled to produce a wear volume of 1 m\(m^3\) by adhesive wear of the mild steel rod?

Admin
Solution

The parameters in Eq. (4.6) for adhesive wear are as follows:

\(V=k\frac{LW}{3p}\) (4.6)

\(\begin{aligned}V &=1 \mathrm{~mm}^{3} ; \\k &=10^{-2}(\text { from Table }(4.2);\\W&=100\mathrm{~kg}\\p&=150\mathrm{~kg}\mathrm{mm}^{2} .\end{aligned}\)

Therefore, the distance traveled is

\(L=\frac{3 V p}{k W}=\frac{(3)(1)(150)}{\left(10^{-2}\right)(100)}=450 \mathrm{~mm}\)
