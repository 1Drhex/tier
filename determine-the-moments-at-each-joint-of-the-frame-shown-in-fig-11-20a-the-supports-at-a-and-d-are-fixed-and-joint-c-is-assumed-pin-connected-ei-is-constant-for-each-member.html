Guest Question.

Determine the moments at each joint of the frame shown in Fig. \(11-20 (\mathrm{a})\). The supports at \(A\) and \(D\) are fixed and joint \(C\) is assumed pin connected. \(E I\) is constant for each member.

Admin
Solution

Slope-Deflection Equations. We will apply Eq. 11-8 to member \(A B\) since it is fixed connected at both ends. Equation 11-10 can be applied from \(B\) to \(C\) and from \(D\) to \(C\) since the pin at \(C\) supports zero moment. As shown by the deflection diagram, Fig. 11-20b, there is an unknown linear displacement \(\Delta\) of the frame and unknown angular displacement \(\theta_{B}\) at joint \(B *\) Due to \(\Delta\), the cord members \(A B\) and \(C D\) rotate clockwise, \(\psi=\psi_{A B}=\psi_{D C}=\Delta / 4\). Realizing that \(\theta_{A}=\theta_{D}=0\) and that there are no FEMs for the members, we have

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=2 E k\left(2 \theta_{N}+\theta_{F}-3 \psi\right)+(\text { FEM })_{N}\\ \text For Internal Span or End Span with Far End Fixed \end{array} \) (11-8)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=3 E k\left(\theta_{N}-\psi\right)+(\mathrm{FEM})_{N}\\ Only for End Span with Far End Pinned or Roller Supported \end{array} \) (11-10)

\( \begin{aligned} M_{N} &=2 E\left(\frac{I}{L}\right)\left(2 \theta_{N}+\theta_{F}-3 \psi\right)+(\mathrm{FEM})_{N} \\ \end{aligned} \)

\( \begin{aligned} M_{A B} &=2 E\left(\frac{I}{4}\right)\left[2(0)+\theta_{B}-3 \psi\right]+0 \\ \end{aligned} \) (1)

\( \begin{aligned} M_{B A} &=2 E\left(\frac{I}{4}\right)\left(2 \theta_{B}+0-3 \psi\right)+0 \\ \end{aligned} \) (2)

\( \begin{aligned} M_{N} &=3 E\left(\frac{I}{L}\right)\left(\theta_{N}-\psi\right)+(\mathrm{FEM})_{N} \end{aligned} \)

\(\begin{array}{l}M_{B C}=3 E\left(\frac{I}{3}\right)\left(\theta_{B}-0\right)+0 \\ \end{array}\) (3)

\(\begin{array}{l}M_{D C}=3 E\left(\frac{I}{4}\right)(0-\psi)+0 \end{array}\) (4)

Equilibrium Equations. Moment equilibrium of joint \(B\), Fig. 11-20c, requires

\( M_{B A}+M_{B C}=0 \) (5)

If forces are summed for the entire frame in the horizontal direction, we have

\(:^{+} \Sigma F_{x}=0 ; \quad 10-V_{A}-V_{D}=0\) (6)

As shown on the free-body diagram of each column, Fig. 11-20d, we have

\( \begin{array}{ll} \Sigma M_{B}=0 ; & V_{A}=-\frac{M_{A B}+M_{B A}}{4} \\ \Sigma M_{C}=0 ; & V_{D}=-\frac{M_{D C}}{4} \end{array} \)

*The angular displacements \(\theta_{C B}\) and \(\theta_{C D}\) at joint \(C\) (pin) are not included in the analysis since Eq. 11-10 is to be used.

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=3 E k\left(\theta_{N}-\psi\right)+(\mathrm{FEM})_{N}\\ Only for End Span with Far End Pinned or Roller Supported \end{array} \) (11-10)

Thus, from Eq. (6),

\( 10+\frac{M_{A B}+M_{B A}}{4}+\frac{M_{D C}}{4}=0 \) (7)

Substituting the slope-deflection equations into Eqs. (5) and (7) and simplifying yields

\( \begin{array}{l} \theta_{B}=\frac{3}{4} \psi \\ 10+\frac{E I}{4}\left(\frac{3}{2} \theta_{B}-\frac{15}{4} \psi\right)=0 \end{array} \)

Thus,

\( \theta_{B}=\frac{240}{21 E I} \quad \psi=\frac{320}{21 E I} \)

Substituting these values into Eqs. (1)-(4), we have

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{A B}=-17.1 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}, M_{B A}=-11.4 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ M_{B C}=11.4 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m}, M_{D C}=-11.4 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \end{array} \)

Using these results, the end reactions on each member can be determined from the equations of equilibrium, Fig. 11-20e. The moment diagram for the frame is shown in Fig. 11-20f.
