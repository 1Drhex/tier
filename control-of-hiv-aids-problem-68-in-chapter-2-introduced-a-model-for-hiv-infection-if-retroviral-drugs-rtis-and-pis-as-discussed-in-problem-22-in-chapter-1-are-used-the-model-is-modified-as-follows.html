Guest Question.

Control of HIV/AIDS. introduced a model for HIV infection. If retroviral drugs, RTIs and PIs as discussed in , are used, the model is modified as follows (Craig, 2004):

\( \begin{array}{l} \frac{d T}{d t}=s-d T-\left(1-u_{1}\right) \beta T v \\ \frac{d T^{*}}{d t}=\left(1-u_{1}\right) \beta T v-\mu T^{*} \\ \frac{d v}{d t}=\left(1-u_{2}\right) k T^{*}-c v \end{array} \)

where \( 0 \leq u_{1} \leq 1,0 \leq u_{2} \leq 1 \) represent the effectiveness of the RTI and PI medication, respectively.
a. Obtain a state-space representation of the HIV/ AIDS model by linearizing the equations about the

\( \left(T_{0}, T_{0}^{*}, v_{0}\right)=\left(\frac{c \mu}{\beta k}, \frac{s}{\mu}-\frac{c d}{\beta k}, \frac{s k}{c \mu}-\frac{d}{\beta}\right) \)

equilibrium with \(u_{10} = u_{20} = 0.\) This equilibrium represents the asymptomatic HIV-infected patient. Note that each one of the above equations is of the form\(\dot{x}_{i}=f_{i}\left(x_{i}, u_{1}, u_{2}\right) i=1,2,3\)

b. If Matrices A and B are given by

\(\mathbf{A}=\left[\begin{array}{lll} \frac{\partial f_{1}}{\partial x_{1}} & \frac{\partial f_{1}}{\partial x_{2}} & \frac{\partial f_{1}}{\partial x_{3}} \\ \frac{\partial f_{2}}{\partial x_{1}} & \frac{\partial f_{2}}{\partial x_{2}} & \frac{\partial f_{2}}{\partial x_{3}} \\\frac{\partial f_{3}}{\partial x_{1}} & \frac{\partial f_{3}}{\partial x_{2}} & \frac{\partial f_{3}}{\partial x_{3}} \end{array}\right]_{T_{0}, T_{0}^{*}, v_{0}} ; \quad \quad \mathbf{B}=\left[\begin{array}{ll}\frac{\partial f_{1}}{\partial u_{1}} & \frac{\partial f_{1}}{\partial u_{2}} \\\frac{\partial f_{2}}{\partial u_{1}} & \frac{\partial f_{2}}{\partial u_{2}} \\\frac{\partial f_{3}}{\partial u_{1}} & \frac{\partial f_{3}}{\partial u_{2}}\end{array}\right]_{T_{0}, T_{0}^{*}, v_{0}}\)

and we are interested in the number of free HIV viruses as the system’s output,

\(\mathbf{C}=\left[\begin{array}{lll}0 & 0 & 1\end{array}\right]\)

show that

\(\mathbf{A}=\left[\begin{array}{crr}-\left(d+\beta v_{0}\right) & 0 & -\beta T_{0} \\\beta v_{0} & -\mu & \beta T_{0} \\ 0 & k & -c\end{array}\right] ; \quad \mathbf{B}=\left[\begin{array}{cc}\beta T_{0} v_{0} & 0 \\-\beta T_{0} v_{0} & 0 \\0 & -k T_{0}^{*}\end{array}\right]\)

c. Typical parameter values and descriptions for the HIV/AIDS model are shown in the following table.
Substitute the values from the table into your model and write as

\(\begin{array}{l}\dot{\mathbf{x}}=\mathbf{A} \mathbf{x}+\mathbf{B u} \\\mathbf{y}=\mathbf{C x}\end{array}\)
t Time days
d Death of uninfected T cells 0.02/day
k Rate of free viruses produced pe infected T cell 100 counts/cell

uninfected T cells		 \(10/mm^3/day\)
\( \beta \) Infectivity rate of
free virus particles		 \(2.4\times 10 ^-5/mm3/day\)

Solution

a.

\(\underline{\frac{\frac{f_{1}=s-d T-\left(1-u_{1}\right) \beta T v}{f_{2}=\left(1-u_{1}\right) \beta T v-\mu T^{*}}}{f_{3}=\left(1-u_{2}\right) k T^{*}-c v}}\)

 

\(\underline{\left.\frac{\partial f_{1}}{\partial T}\right|_{0}=-d-\left.\left(1-u_{1}\right) \beta v\right|_{0}=-d-\left(1-u_{10}\right) \beta v_{0}}\)

 

\(\underline{\left.\frac{\partial f_{1}}{\partial T^{*}}\right|_{0}=0}\)

 

\(\underline{\left.\frac{\partial f_{1}}{\partial v}\right|_{0}=\left.\left(1-u_{1}\right) \beta v\right|_{0}=\left(1-u_{10}\right) \beta v_{0}}\)

 

\(\underline{\left.\frac{\partial f_{2}}{\partial T}\right|_{0}=\left.\left(1-u_{1}\right) \beta v\right|_{0}=\left(1-u_{10}\right) \beta v_{0}}\)

 

\(\underline{\left.\frac{\partial f_{2}}{\partial T^{*}}\right|_{0}=-\mu}\)

 

\(\underline{\left.\frac{\partial f_{2}}{\partial v}\right|_{0} =\left.\left(1-u_{1}\right) \beta T\right|_{0} = \left(1-u_{10}\right) \beta T_{0}}\)

 

\(\underline{\left.\frac{\partial f_{2}}{\partial v}\right|_{0}=\left.\left(1-u_{1}\right) \beta T\right|_{0}=\left(1-u_{10}\right) \beta T_{0}}\)

 

\(\underline{\left.\frac{\partial f_{3}}{\partial T}\right|_{0}=0}\)

 

\(\underline{\frac{\left.\frac{\partial f_{3}}{\partial T^{*}}\right|_{0}=\left(1-u_{20}\right) k}{\left.\frac{\partial f_{3}}{\partial v}\right|_{0}=-c}}\)

 

\(\underline{\frac{\partial f_{1}}{\left.\partial u_{1}\right|_{0}}=\beta T_{0} v_{0}}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\frac{\partial f_{1}}{\left.\partial u_{2}\right|_{0}}=0\\&\frac{\partial f_{2}}{\left.\partial u_{1}\right|_{0}=-\beta T_{0} v_{0}}\\&\begin{array}{l}\frac{\partial f_{2}}{\left.\partial u_{2}\right|_{0}=0} \\\frac{\partial f_{3}}{\left.\partial u_{1}\right|_{0}=0} \\\frac{\partial f_{3}}{\left.\partial u_{2}\right|_{0}=-k T_{0}^{*}}\end{array}\end{aligned}\)

 

Then just by direct substitution .

b. Substituting values one gets:

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\dot{T} \\\dot{T}^{*} \\\dot{v}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}-\left(d+\beta v_{0}\right) & 0 & -\beta T_{0} \\\beta v_{0} & -\mu & \beta T_{0} \\0 & k & -c\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c} T \\T^{*} \\v\end{array}\right]+\left[\begin{array}{cc} \beta T_{0} v_{0} & 0 \\-\beta T_{0} v_{0} & 0 \\0 & -k T_{0}^{*}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}u_{1} \\ u_{2}\end{array}\right]\)
