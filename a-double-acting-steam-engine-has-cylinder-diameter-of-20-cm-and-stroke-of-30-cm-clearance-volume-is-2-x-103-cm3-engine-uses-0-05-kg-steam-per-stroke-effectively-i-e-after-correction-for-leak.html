Guest Question.

A double acting steam engine has cylinder diameter of 20 cm and stroke of 30 cm. Clearance volume is 2 × 10\(^{3}\) cm\(^{3}\). Engine uses 0.05 kg steam per stroke effectively i.e. after correction for leakage. Compression starts at 80% of stroke with pressure of 1 bar and steam is dry at the point of starting of compression. Cut-off point is at 10% of stroke and release occurs at 90% of stroke. The pressures at cut off point and point of release are 15 bar and 3 bar. Determine total mass of steam present during expansion and dryness at cut-off and release. Consider the expansion to follow PV \(^{n}\) = constant and find heat leaking through cylinder walls per kg of steam during expansion.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{Clearance volume,}\\\begin{aligned}V_{6} = V_{5} &= 2 × 10^{3} cm^{3} = 2 × 10^{–3} m^{3}\\\text{Stroke volume, V}_{3}&- V_{6} = \frac{π}{4} × (0.2)^{2} × 0.3 = 9.42 × 10^{-3} m^{3}\\\text{Total volume}&= V_{3} = 2 × 10^{–3} + 9.42 × 10^{–3}\\&= 11.42 × 10^{–3} m^{3}\end{aligned}\\\text{At the point of beginning of compression, i.e. ‘4’}\\\quad V_{4} = V_{3} – 0.8 × (V_{3} – V_{6}) = 3.884 × 10^{–3} m^{3}\\\text{At the start of compression, v}_{4} = v_{\text{g at 1 bar, dry saturated}} = 1.6940 m^{3}/kg\\\text{Therefore, mass of steam at state 4, i.e. cushion steam }= \frac{V_{4}}{v_{4}} = 2.293 × 10^{–3} kg\\\text{Total mass of steam during expansion = Cushion steam + Steam used per stroke}\\\begin{aligned}&= 2.293 × 10^{3} + 0.05\\&= 0.052293 kg\\\text{Volume at cut-off point,} V_{1} = V_{6} + 0.1 (V_{3}&- V_{6}) \\V_{1}&= 2.942 × 10^{–3} m^{3}\\\text{Dryness fraction at cut-off point, }\qquad x_{1}&= \frac{\text{Volume}}{m · v_{\text{g at 15 bar}}} = \frac{2.49 × 10^{- 3}}{(0.052293 × 0.13177)}\\x_{1}&= 0.4269\\\text{Volume at point of release,}\qquad \qquad V_{2}&= V_{6} + 0.9 (V_{3} – V_{6}) = 0.010478 m^{3}\\\text{Dryness fraction at point of release, x}_{2}&= \frac{\text{Volume , V}_{2}}{m · v_{\text{g at 3 bar}}}\\&= \frac{0.010478}{(0.052293 × 0.6058)}\\x_{2}&= 0.3307\\\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Let us find out index of expansion n for pro}&\text{cess 1–2.}\\P_{1} V_{1}^{n}&= P_{2} V^{n}_{2}\\15 × (2.942 × 10^{–3})^{n}&= 3 × (0.010478)^{n}\\n&= 1.267\\\text{Work done in a stroke}&= \left\lgroup\frac{P_{1}V_{1} – P_{2}V_{2}}{n – 1} \right\rgroup \\&= 10^{2} × \left\lgroup\frac{15 × 2.942 × 10^{-3} – 3 × 0.010478}{1.267 – 1} \right\rgroup \\&= 4.755 kJ\\\text{Work done per kg of steam}&= \frac{4.755}{\text{Steam available per stroke}}\\&= \frac{4.755}{0.052293} = 90.93 kJ/kg\\\text{Change in internal energy during expansion}&= u_{1} – u_{2}\\u_{1} = u_{\text{f at 15 bar}} + x_{1} · u_{\text{fg at 15 bar}}&= 1590.79 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}u_{2}&= u_{\text{f at 3 bar}} + x_{2} · u_{\text{fg at 3 bar}}\\u_{2}&= 1216.73 kJ/kg\\\text{Applying first}&\text{ law of thermodynamics,}\\Δq&= Δu + Δw = (u_{2} – u_{1}) – Δw\\&= (1216.73 – 1590.79) – 90.93\\&= – 464.99 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Total mass of steam during expansion}&= 0.052293 kg\\\text{Dryness fraction at cut off and release}&= 0.4269, 0.3307\\\text{Heat leakage}&= \text{464.99 kJ/kg steam}\end{aligned}\)
