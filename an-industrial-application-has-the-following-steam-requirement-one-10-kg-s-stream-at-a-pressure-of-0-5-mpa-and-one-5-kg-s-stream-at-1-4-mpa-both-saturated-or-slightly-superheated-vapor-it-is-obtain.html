Guest Question.

An industrial application has the following steam requirement: one 10-kg/s stream at a pressure of 0.5 MPa and one 5-kg/s stream at 1.4 MPa (both saturated or slightly superheated vapor). It is obtained by cogeneration, whereby a highpressure boiler supplies steam at 10 MPa, 500°C to a reversible turbine. The required amount is withdrawn at 1.4 MPa, and the remainder is expanded in the low-pressure end of the turbine to 0.5 MPa providing the second required steam flow.
a. Determine the power output of the turbine and the heat transfer rate in the boiler.
b. Compute the rates needed were the steam generated in a low-pressure boiler without cogeneration. Assume that for each, 20°C liquid water is pumped to the required pressure and fed to a boiler.

Admin
Solution

a) With cogeneration
high-pressure turbine.

\(\begin{aligned}& s _{4}= s _{3}=6.5966 kJ / kg K \Rightarrow T _{4}=219.9{ }^{\circ} C , h _{4}=2852.6 kJ / kg \\& w _{ S HPT }= h _{3}- h _{4}=3373.7-2852.6=521.1 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

low-pressure turbine

\(s _{5}= s _{4}=6.5966=1.8607+ x _{5} \times 4.9606, x _{5}=0.9547\)

 

\(h _{5}=640.23+0.9547 \times 2108.5=2653.2 kJ / kg\)

 

\(w _{ S LPT }= h _{4}- h _{5}=2852.6-2653.2=199.4 kJ / kg\)

 

\(\dot{ W }_{ TURB }=15 \times 521.1+10 \times 199.4= 9 8 1 0 k W\)

 

\(\dot{ W }_{ P }=15 \times 0.001002(10000-2.3)=150.3 kW\)

 

\(h _{2}= h _{1}+ w _{ P }=83.96+10.02=94.0 kJ / kg\)

 

\(\dot{ Q }_{ H }=\dot{ m }_{1}\left( h _{3}- h _{2}\right)=15(3373.7-94.0)= 4 9 1 9 6 k W\)

 

b) Without cogeneration
This is to be compared to the amount of heat required to supply 5 kg/s of 1.4 MPa sat. vap. plus 10 kg/s of 0.5 MPa sat. vap. from 20°C water.

Pump 1 and boiler 1

\(w _{ P }=0.001002(1400-2.3)=14.0 kJ / kg\),

 

\(h _{2}= h _{1}+ w _{ P }=83.96+14.0=85.4 kJ / kg\)

 

\({ }_{2} \dot{ Q }_{3}=\dot{ m }_{1}\left( h _{3}- h _{2}\right)=5(2790.0-85.4)=13523 kW\)

 

\({ }_{2} \dot{ Q }_{3}=\dot{ m }_{1}\left( h _{3}- h _{2}\right)=5(2790.0-85.4)=13523 kW\)

 

\(\dot{ W }_{ P1 }=5 \times 14.0=7 kW\)

 

Pump 2 and boiler 2

\(h _{5}= h _{4}+ w _{ P 2}=83.96+0.001002(500-2.3)=84.5 kJ / kg\)

 

\({ }_{5} \dot{ Q }_{6}=\dot{ m }_{4}\left( h _{6}- h _{5}\right)=10(2748.7-84.5)=26642 kW\)

 

\(\dot{ W }_{ P 2}=10 \times 0.5=5 kW\)

 

\(\text { Total } \dot{Q}_{H}=13523+26642= 4 0 1 6 5 k W\)

 

Notice here that the extra heat transfer is about 9000 kW to run the turbines but that provides 9800 kW of work for electricity (a 100% conversion of the extra Q to W).
