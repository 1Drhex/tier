Guest Question.

Consider here again the soil profile shown in Figure 6.23, and compute the time required to obtain an average degree of consolidation equal to 90%, if the vertical drain pattern is such that \(d_w = 80mm\) and \(de = 2.00m\).

Admin
Solution

By using (6.145):

\(\begin{matrix}u=\frac{u_{o}}{r_{e}^2F(n)}\left[r_{e}^2\ln\left(\frac{r}{r_{w}}\right)-\frac{r^2-r_{w}}{2}\right] \cdot e^{\lambda} \\ U_{p}=1-e^{\lambda} \end{matrix}\),

and (6.146):

\(\begin{matrix} \lambda=-\frac{8T_{r}}{F(n)} \\F(n)=\frac{n^2}{n^2-1}\ln(n)-\frac{3n^2-1}{4n^2} \\ n=\frac{r_e}{r_w}\\ T_r=\frac{c_{r}t}{d_{e}^2}=\left(\frac{K_{r}}{\rho _{w}g\cdot m_{v}}\right) \frac{t}{d_{e}^2} \end{matrix}\)

, we obtain:

\(\begin{matrix} n=\frac{d_{e}}{d_{w}}=25 & F(n)=2.475 &\lambda =\ln(1-U_{p})=\ln(0.1)=-2.3\end{matrix}\).

The dimensionless time factor corresponding to the obtained value of Î» is equal to \(T_{r}={-\lambda F(n)}/{8}=0.712\), and assuming rather conservatively \(c_{r}=c_{v}\), he definition of the dimensionless time factor \(T_{r}={c_{r}t}/{d_{e}^{2}}\) allows us to obtain \(t\cong 110\) days. This result proves the effectiveness of vertical drains in reducing the time required for consolidation, because without their use the required time would have been equal to about 14 years.
