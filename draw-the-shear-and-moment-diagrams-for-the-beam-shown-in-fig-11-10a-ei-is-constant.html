Guest Question.

Draw the shear and moment diagrams for the beam shown in Fig. \(11-10 (\mathrm{a})\). \(EI\) is constant.

Admin
Solution

Slope-Deflection Equations. Two spans must be considered in this problem. Since there is no span having the far end pinned or roller supported, Eq. 11-8 applies to the solution. Using the formulas for the FEMs tabulated for the triangular loading given on the inside back cover, we have

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=2 E k\left(2 \theta_{N}+\theta_{F}-3 \psi\right)+(\text { FEM })_{N}\\ \text For Internal Span or End Span with Far End Fixed \end{array} \) (11-8)

\( \begin{array}{l} (\mathrm{FEM})_{B C}=-\frac{w L^{2}}{30}=-\frac{6(6)^{2}}{30}=-7.2 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ (\mathrm{FEM})_{C B}=\frac{w L^{2}}{20}=\frac{6(6)^{2}}{20}=10.8 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \end{array} \)

Note that \((\mathrm{FEM})_{B C}\) is negative since it acts counterclockwise on the beam at \(B\). Also, \((\mathrm{FEM})_{A B}=(\mathrm{FEM})_{B A}=0\) since there is no load on span \(A B\).

In order to identify the unknowns, the elastic curve for the beam is shown in Fig. 11-10b. As indicated, there are four unknown internal moments. Only the slope at \(B, \theta_{B}\), is unknown. Since \(A\) and \(C\) are fixed supports, \(\theta_{A}=\theta_{C}=0\). Also, since the supports do not settle, nor are they displaced up or down, \(\psi_{A B}=\psi_{B C}=0\). For span \(A B\), considering \(A\) to be the near end and \(B\) to be the far end, we have

\(\begin{aligned} M_{N} &=2 E\left(\frac{I}{L}\right)\left(2 \theta_{N}+\theta_{F}-3 \psi\right)+(\mathrm{FEM})_{N} \\ \end{aligned}\)

\(\begin{aligned} M_{A B} &=2 E\left(\frac{I}{8}\right)\left[2(0)+\theta_{B}-3(0)\right]+0=\frac{E I}{4} \theta_{B} \end{aligned}\) (1)

Now, considering \(B\) to be the near end and \(A\) to be the far end, we have

\( M_{B A}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{8}\right)\left[2 \theta_{B}+0-3(0)\right]+0=\frac{E I}{2} \theta_{B} \) (2)

In a similar manner, for span \(B C\) we have

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{B C}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{6}\right)\left[2 \theta_{B}+0-3(0)\right]-7.2=\frac{2 E I}{3} \theta_{B}-7.2 \\ \end{array}\) (3)

\(\begin{array}{l} M_{C B}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{6}\right)\left[2(0)+\theta_{B}-3(0)\right]+10.8=\frac{E I}{3} \theta_{B}+10.8 \end{array}\) (4)

Equilibrium Equations. The above four equations contain five unknowns. The necessary fifth equation comes from the condition of moment equilibrium at support \(B\). The free-body diagram of a segment of the beam at \(B\) is shown in Fig. 11-10c. Here \(\mathbf{M}_{B A}\) and \(\mathbf{M}_{B C}\) are assumed to act in the positive direction to be consistent with the slope-deflection equations.* The beam shears contribute negligible moment about \(B\) since the segment is of differential length. Thus,

\( \curvearrowleft +\sum M_{B}=0 ; \quad M_{B A}+M_{B C}=0 \) (5)

To solve, substitute Eqs. (2) and (3) into Eq. (5), which yields

\( \theta_{B}=\frac{6.17}{E I} \)

Resubstituting this value into Eqs. (1)-(4) yields

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{A B}=1.54 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ M_{B A}=3.09 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ M_{B C}=-3.09 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ M_{C B}=12.86 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \end{array} \)

The negative value for \(M_{B C}\) indicates that this moment acts counterclockwise on the beam, not clockwise as shown in Fig. 11–10b. Using these results, the shears at the end spans are determined from the equilibrium equations, Fig. 11–10d. The free-body diagram of the entire beam and the shear and moment diagrams are shown in Fig. 11–10e.

*Clockwise on the beam segment, but—by the principle of action, equal but opposite reaction—counterclockwise on the support.
