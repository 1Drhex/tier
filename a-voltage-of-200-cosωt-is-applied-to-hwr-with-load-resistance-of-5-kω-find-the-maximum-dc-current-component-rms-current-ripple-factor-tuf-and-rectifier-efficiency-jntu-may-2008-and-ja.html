Guest Question.

A voltage of 200 cosωt is applied to HWR with load resistance of 5 kΩ . Find the maximum dc current component, rms current, ripple factor, TUF and rectifier efficiency. [JNTU May 2008 and Jan 2010]

Admin
Solution

Given Applied Voltage = \(200\cos \omega t ,V_{m}=200V,R_{L}=5k\Omega\)

 

(a) To find dc current

\(I_{m}=\frac{V_{m} }{R_{L} }=\frac{200}{5\times 10^{3} } =40mA\)

 

Therefore, \(I_{dc}=\frac{I_{m} }{\pi }=\frac{40\times 10^{-3} }{\pi } =12.7\times 10^{-3} A=12.73mA\)

 

(b) To find rms current

\(I_{rms}=\frac{I_{m} }{2 }=\frac{40\times 10^{-3} }{2 } =20mA\)

 

(c) Ripple factor \(\Gamma =\sqrt{\left(\frac{I_{rms} }{I_{dc} } \right) ^{2}-1 } =\sqrt{\left(\frac{20\times 10^{-3} }{12.73\times 10^{-3} } \right) ^{2}-1 }=1.21\)

 

(d) To determine TUF

\(TUF=\frac{P_{dc} }{P_{ac(rated)} } \)

 

\(P_{dc} =I^{2}_{dc} R_{L} =(12.73\times 10^{-3} )^{2} \times 5\times 10^{3}=0.81W\)

 

\(P_{ac(rated)} =\frac{V_{m} }{\sqrt{2} }\times \frac{I_{m} }{2}= \frac{200 }{\sqrt{2} }\times \frac{40\times 10^{-3} }{2}=2.828\)

 

Therefore, \(TUF=\frac{P_{dc} }{P_{ac(rated)} }=\frac{0.81}{2.828}=0.2863\)

 

(e) Rectifier Efficiency, \(\eta =\frac{P_{dc} }{P_{ac} }\)

 

\(P_{dc}=0.81W \)

 

\(P_{ac}=I^{2}_{rms}R_{L} =(20\times 10^{-3} )^{2} \times 5\times 10^{3} =2W\)

 

Therefore, \(\eta =\frac{P_{dc} }{P_{ac} }\times 100=\frac{0.81}{2}\times 100 =40.5\%\)
