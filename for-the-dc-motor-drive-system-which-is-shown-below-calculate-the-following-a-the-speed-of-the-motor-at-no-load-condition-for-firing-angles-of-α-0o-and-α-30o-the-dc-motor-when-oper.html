Guest Question.

For the dc motor drive system, which is shown below, calculate the following:
a) The speed of the motor, at no-load condition, for firing angles of \(\alpha \)= 0° and \(\alpha \) = 30º.
The dc motor, when operating at full load absorbs 165 A and at no-load 16.5 A.
b) The firing angle a and the input power factor at which the motor develops nominal
speed of 1800 RPM at full load.
c) The motor speed regulation for the firing angle calculated in part (b).

 

Admin
Solution

Solution

a) The rms input phase voltage is \(\frac{480}{\sqrt{3}}\) p = 277 V.

According to the input voltage value, the small stator resistance, the stator inductor reactance (ωL = (2π × 60)(0.0065) = 2.45 Ω, ωL > R), and the low values of firing angle (a = 0° and a= 30°) it can be concluded that the output current is continuous. Consequently, the average output voltage is:

\(\bar{V}_{a}=\frac{3 \sqrt{6} 277}{\pi} \cos \alpha=648 \cos \alpha\)

For α = 0°,\(\bar{V}_{a}\) = 648 V.

Therefore, the counter EMF is:

\(E _{ g }=\overline{ V }_{ a }-\overline{ I }_{ a } R _{ a }=648(16.5 \times 0.0874)=646.6 V\)

Consequently, the speed of the motor at no-load mode, forα= 0° is:

\(N _{ o }=\frac{ E _{ g }}{ K _{ a } \Phi}=\frac{646.6}{0.33}=1959 RPM\)

For α= 30° and \(\bar{V}_{a}\)= 561:2 V the motor counter EMF is:

\(E _{ g }=\overline{ V }_{ a }-\overline{ I }_{ a } R _{ a }=561.2-(16.5 \times 0.0874)=559.8 V\)

Therefore, the speed of the motor at no-load for α= 30° is:

\(N _{ o }=\frac{559.8}{0.33}=1696 RPM\)

b) The motor counter EMF \(E _{ g }\) at 1800 RPM is:

\(E _{ g }= N _{ o } K _{ a } \Phi=(1800)(0.33)=594 V\)

The average voltage value across the motor terminals is:

\(\overline{ V }_{ a }=594+(165)(0.0874)=608.4 V =648 \cos \alpha \Rightarrow \alpha=20^{\circ}\)

When the motor operates at full load, the output current ripple is negligible. Therefore, the current ripple is a quasi-square-wave pulse of 165 A amplitude and pulse width of 120°as shown in Fig. 5.30.
The rms of this quasi-square-wave current pulse is:

\(\widetilde{I}_{i}=\left[\frac{1}{\pi} \int_{30^{\circ}}^{150^{\circ}}(165)^{2} d \omega t\right]^{1 / 2}=\left[\frac{1}{\pi}(165)^{2}\left(\frac{2 \pi}{3}\right)\right]^{1 / 2}=\sqrt{2 / 3} \times 165=134.6 A\)

The input power factor is:

\(\lambda=\frac{P_{ i }}{S_{ i }}=\frac{P_{ a }}{S_{ i }}=\frac{\bar{V}_{ a } \overline{ I }_{ a }}{3 \widetilde{ V }_{ i } \widetilde{ I }_{ i }}=\frac{608.4 \times 165}{3 \times 277 \times 134.6}=0.9\)

c) When the motor operates at full load and 1800 RPM, the output current is 165 A. If the motor load is disconnected, then the output current reduces to 16.5 A. Therefore, the counter EMF at no-load for firing angle α=20° is:

\(E _{ g }=\overline{ V }_{ a \left(\alpha=20^{\circ}\right)}-(165 \times 0.0874)=608.4-1.44=606.96 V\)

Furthermore, the speed of the motor at no-load condition andα=20° is

\(N _{ o }=\frac{ E _{ g \left(\alpha=20^{\circ}\right)}}{ K _{\alpha} \Phi}=\frac{606.6}{0.33}=1839.3 RPM\)

Therefore, the motor speed regulation is:

\(\frac{ N _{ o }(\text { no-load })- N _{ o }(\text { load })}{ N _{ o }(\text { load })} \times 100=\frac{1839.3-1800}{1800} \times 100=2.8 \%\)
