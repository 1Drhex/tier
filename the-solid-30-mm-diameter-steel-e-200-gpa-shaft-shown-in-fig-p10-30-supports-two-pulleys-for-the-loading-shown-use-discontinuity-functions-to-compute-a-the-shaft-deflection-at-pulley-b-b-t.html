Guest Question.

The solid 30-mm-diameter steel [E = 200 GPa] shaft shown in Fig. P10.30 supports two pulleys. For the loading shown, use discontinuity functions to compute:
(a) the shaft deflection at pulley B.
(b) the shaft deflection at pulley C.

Admin
Solution

Support reactions: A FBD of the beam is shown to the right.

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma F_{y}=& A_{y}-800 N -500 N =0 \\& \therefore A_{y}=1,300 N \\\Sigma M_{A}=&-(800 N )(250 mm )-(500 N )(600 mm )-M_{A}=0 \\& \therefore M_{A}=-500,000 N – mm\end{aligned}\)

Load function w(x):

\(\begin{aligned}w(x)=&-500,000 N – mm \langle x-0 mm \rangle^{-2}+1,300 N \langle x-0 mm \rangle^{-1} \\&-800 N \langle x-250 mm \rangle^{-1}-500 N \langle x-600 mm \rangle^{-1}\end{aligned}\)

Shear-force function V(x) and bending-moment function M(x):

\(\begin{gathered}V(x)=-500,000 N – mm \langle x-0 mm \rangle^{-1}+1,300 N \langle x-0 mm \rangle^{0} \\-800 N \langle x-250 mm \rangle^{0}-500 N \langle x-600 mm \rangle^{0} \\M(x)=-500,000 N – mm \langle x-0 mm \rangle^{0}+1,300 N \langle x-0 mm \rangle^{1} \\-800 N \langle x-250 mm \rangle^{1}-500 N \langle x-600 mm \rangle^{1}\end{gathered}\)

Equations for beam slope and beam deflection:
From Eq. (10.1), we can write:

\(\begin{array}{r}E I \frac{d^{2} v}{d x^{2}}=M(x)=-500,000 N – mm \langle x-0 mm \rangle^{0}+1,300 N \langle x-0 mm \rangle^{1} \\-800 N \langle x-250 mm \rangle^{1}-500 N \langle x-600 mm \rangle^{1}\end{array}\)

Integrate the moment function to obtain an expression for the beam slope:

\(\begin{aligned}E I \frac{d v}{d x} &=-500,000 N – mm \langle x-0 mm \rangle^{1}+\frac{1,300 N }{2}\langle x-0 mm \rangle^{2} \\&-\frac{800 N }{2}\langle x-250 mm \rangle^{2}-\frac{500 N }{2}\langle x-600 mm \rangle^{2}+C_{1} (a)\end{aligned}\)

Integrate again to obtain the beam deflection function:

\(\begin{aligned}E I v &=-\frac{500,000 N – mm }{2}\langle x-0 mm \rangle^{2}+\frac{1,300 N }{6}\langle x-0 mm \rangle^{3} \\&-\frac{800 N }{6}\langle x-250 mm \rangle^{3}-\frac{500 N }{6}\langle x-600 mm \rangle^{3}+C_{1} x+C_{2} (b)\end{aligned}\)

Evaluate constants using boundary conditions: Boundary conditions are specific values of deflection v or slope dv/dx that are known at particular locations along the beam span. For this beam, both the slope dv/dx and the deflection v are known at the fixed support (x = 0 mm). Substitute the boundary condition dv/dx = 0 at x = 0 mm into Eq. (a) to obtain:

\(C_{1}=0\)

Next, substitute the boundary condition v = 0 at x = 0 mm into Eq. (b) to obtain:

\(C_{2}=0\)

The beam slope and elastic curve equations are now complete:

\(\begin{aligned}E I \frac{d v}{d x} &=-500,000 N – mm \langle x-0 mm \rangle^{1}+\frac{1,300 N }{2}\langle x-0 mm \rangle^{2} \\&-\frac{800 N }{2}\langle x-250 mm \rangle^{2}-\frac{500 N }{2}\langle x-600 mm \rangle^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}E I v=&-\frac{500,000 N – mm }{2}\langle x-0 mm \rangle^{2}+\frac{1,300 N }{6}\langle x-0 mm \rangle^{3} \\&-\frac{800 N }{6}\langle x-250 mm \rangle^{3}-\frac{500 N }{6}\langle x-600 mm \rangle^{3}\end{aligned}\)

 

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&I=\frac{\pi}{64}(30 mm )^{4}=39,750.782 mm ^{4} \quad E=200 GPa =200,000 N / mm ^{2} \\&\therefore E I=7.9522 \times 10^{9} N – mm ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

(a) Beam deflection at B: The beam deflection at B where x = 250 mm is:

\(\begin{aligned}E I v_{B} &=-\frac{500,000 N – mm }{2}(250 mm )^{2}+\frac{1,300 N }{6}(250 mm )^{3} \\& \therefore v_{B}=-\frac{12.2396 \times 10^{9} N – mm ^{3}}{7.9522 \times 10^{9} N – mm ^{2}}=-1.5392 mm =1.539 mm \downarrow\end{aligned}\)

(b) Beam deflection at C: The beam deflection at C where x = 600 mm is:

\(\begin{gathered}E I v_{C}=-\frac{500,000 N – mm }{2}(600 mm )^{2}+\frac{1,300 N }{6}(600 mm )^{3}-\frac{800 N }{6}(350 mm )^{3} \\\therefore v_{C}=-\frac{48.9167 \times 10^{9} N – mm ^{3}}{7.9522 \times 10^{9} N – mm ^{2}}=-6.1514 mm =6.15 mm \downarrow\end{gathered}\)
