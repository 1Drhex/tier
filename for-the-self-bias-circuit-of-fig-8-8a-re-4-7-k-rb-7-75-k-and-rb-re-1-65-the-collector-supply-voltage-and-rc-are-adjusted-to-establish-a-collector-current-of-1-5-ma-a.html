Guest Question.

For the self-bias circuit of Fig. 8.8a, Re = 4.7 K, Rb = 7.75 K, and Rb /Re = 1.65. The collector supply voltage and Rc are adjusted to establish a collector current of 1.5 mA.

(a) Determine the variation of IC in the temperature range of − 65 to +175°C when the silicon transistor of Table 8.1 is used.

(b) Repeat (a) for the range − 65 to +75°C when the germanium transistor of Table 8.2 is used.

Table 8.2 Typical germanium transistor parameters
T, °C………………. − 65 +25 +75
ICO, μA…………… 1.95 × 10−3 1.0 32
β ……………………. 20 55 90
VBE, V…………….. 0.38 0.20 0.10
Admin
Solution

(a) since Re, Rb , and β are known, the stability factor S can be determined at +25°C from Eq. (8.34) , \((1+\beta _{2})\frac{1+R_{b}/R_{e}}{1+\beta _{2}+R_{b}/R_{e}} \) :

S(25°C) = \(\frac{(1+\beta )(1+\beta )(1+R_{b}/R_{c})}{1+\beta +R_{b}/R_{e}} =\frac{(56)(2.65)}{56+1.65}=2.57 \)

Similarly, S′ at +25°C can be determined from Eq. (8.43) , S′ = \(-\frac{S}{R_{b}+R_{e}}\frac{\beta }{1+\beta } \) :

S′(25°C) = \(\frac{-S}{R_{b}+R_{c}}\frac{\beta }{1+\beta }=-\left\lgroup\frac{2.57}{12.45} \right\rgroup \left\lgroup\frac{55}{56} \right\rgroup =-0.203 \) mA/V

The values of S and S′ are value for either a silicon or a germanium transistor operating in the circuit of Fig. 8.8a. Since the stability factor S′′ contains both β1 and β2, it must be determined individually for each transistor at each new temperature, using Eq. (8.51). Hence, for the silicon transistor at +175°C, we have, using Eq. (8.34),

S2(+175°C ) =\((1+\beta _{2})\frac{1+R_{b}/R_{e}}{1+\beta _{2}+R_{b}/R_{e}}=\frac{(101)(2.65)}{101+1.65}=2.61 \)

Then

S′′(+175°C ) =\(\frac{I_{C1}S_{2}}{\beta _{1}(1+\beta _{2})} =\frac{(1.5)(2.62)}{(55)(101)}=0.71\times 10^{-3} \) mA

Similarly,

S2(− 65°C ) =\(\frac{(26)(2.65)}{26+1.65}=2.49 \)

S′′(− 65°C) = \(\frac{(1.5)(2.49)}{(55)(26)}=2.61\times 10^{-3} \) mA

We are now in a position to calculate the change in IC, using Eq. (8.53) and Table 8.1.

Table 8.1 Typical silicon transistor parameters
T, °C………………. − 65 +25 +175
ICO, μA…………… 1.95 × 10−3 1.0 33,000
β ……………………. 25 55 100
VBE, V…………….. 0.78 0.60 0.225

ΔIC (+175°C) = S Δ ICO +S′ ΔVBE + S′′ Δβ

=(2.57) (33 × 10−3) +(− 0.203 ) (− 0.375) + ( 0.71 × 10−3)(45)

= 0.085 + 0.077 + 0.032 = 0.194 mA

and at −65°C,

ΔIC (− 65°C) = (2.57) (− 10−6) − ( 0.203 ) ( 0.18) + ( 2.61 × 10−3)(− 30)

= 0 − 0.036 − 0.078 = − 0.114 mA

Therefore, for the silicon transistor, the collector current will be approximately 1.69 mA at +175°C and 1.39 mA at −65°C.

(b) To calculate the change in collector current using the germanium transistor, we must compute S′′ +75 and

−65°C.

S2(+75°C )=\(\frac{(91)(2.65)}{91+1.65}=2.60 \)

S′′(+75°C) =\(\frac{I_{C1}S_{2}}{\beta _{1}(1+\beta _{2})} =\frac{(1.5)(2.60)}{(55)(91)}=0.78\times 10^{-3} \) mA

Similarly,

S2(− 65°C ) = \(\frac{(21)(2.65)}{21+1.65}=2.45 \)

S′′(− 65 °C) =\(\frac{(1.5)(2.45)}{(55)(21)}=3.18\times 10^{-3}\) mA

Hence the change in collector current is

ΔIC (+75°C) = (2.57) (31 × 10−3) + ( −0.203 ) ( − 0.10) + ( 0.78 × 10−3)(35)

= 0.080 + 0.020 + 0.027 = 0.127 mA

and at −65°C

ΔIC (− 65°C) = (2.57) (− 10−3) + ( +0.203 ) ( 0.18) + ( 3.18 × 10−3)(− 35)

= −0.002 − 0.0036 − 0.111 = −0.149 mA

Therefore, for the germanium transistor, the collector current will be approximately 1.63 mA at +75°C and 1.35 mA at −65°C.
