Guest Question.

In the half-wave rectifier circuit of \(Fig. 55.4\) , determine

\((i)\) maximum and rms values of load voltage,

\((ii)\) peak and rms values of load current,

\((iii)\) power absorbed by the load,

\((iv)\) PIV of the diode,

\((v)\) rms value of ripple voltage.

Neglect resistance of transformer secondary and that of the diode.

Admin
Solution

Here, \(K = N_{2}/N_{1} = 1/10\) . Peak primary voltage is

\(V_{pm}=220\sqrt{2} =310 V \)

Hence, \(V_{sm} = KV_{pm}\)

 

\(= 310/10 = 31 V\)

 

\((i)\) \( V_{LM} = V_{sm} = 31 V\)

 

\(V_{L} = V_{LM} /2 = 31/2 = 15.5 V\)

 

\((ii)\) \( I_{LM} = V_{LM}/R_{L} = 31/100 = 0.31 A\)

 

\(I_{L} = I_{LM}/2 = 0.31/2 = 0.155 A\)

 

\((iii)\) \( P_{L} = V_{L} I_{L} = 15.5 × 0.155 = 2.4 W\)

 

\((iv)\) \( P_{IV} = 2 V_{sm} = 2 × 31 = 62 V\)

 

\((v)\) \(V_{L\left(ac\right) }=\sqrt{V^{2}_{L}-V^{2}_{L\left(dc\right) } }\)

Now, \(V_{L}= V_{LM}/2\) and \(V_{L\left(dc\right) } = V_{LM}/\pi\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(V_{L\left(ac\right) }=\sqrt{\left(V_{LM}/2 \right)^{2}-\left(V_{LM}/\pi \right)^{2} } =0.385 V_{LM} \)

 

\(\therefore \) \(V_{r\left(rms\right) }=V_{L\left(ac \right) } =0.385\times 31=11.9 V\)

It represents the rms value of the ripple voltage.
