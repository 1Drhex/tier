Guest Question.

Determine the member end moments and reactions for the frame of due to a settlement of \(\frac{3}{4} in.\) at support \(B\). Use the slope-deflection method.

Admin
Solution

The frame is shown in Fig. 16.14(a).

Degrees of Freedom \(\theta_C\) and \(\theta_D\) are the degrees of freedom.

Chord Rotations Since the axial deformation of member BD is neglected,
the \(\frac{3}{4} in.\) settlement of support B causes the joint D to displace downward by the same amount, as shown in Fig. 16.14(b). The inclined dashed lines in this figure represent the chords (not the elastic curves) of members CD and DE in the deformed positions. The rotation of the chord of member CD is

\(\psi_{CD} = -\frac{\frac{3}{4}}{(12)(30)} = -0.00208\)

in which the negative sign has been assigned to the value of \(\psi_{CD}\) to indicate that its sense is clockwise. Similarly, for member DE,

\(\psi_{DE}=0.00208\)

Slope-Deflection Equations

\(M_{AC} = 0.1EI\theta_C\) (1)

\(M_{CA} = 0.2EI\theta_C\) (2)

\(M_{BD} = 0.1EI\theta_D\) (3)

\(M_{DB} = 0.2EI\theta_D\) (4)

\(M_{CD} =\frac{2E(2I)}{30}[2\theta_C + \theta_D – 3(-0.00208)]\)

 

\(= 0.267EI\theta_C + 0.133EI\theta_D + 0.000832EI\) (5)

\(M_{DE} =\frac{3E(2I)}{30}(\theta_D – 0.00208) = 0.2EI\theta_D – 0.000416EI\) (7)

\(M_{ED} = 0\) Ans.

Equilibrium Equations See Fig. 16.14(c).

\(M_{CA} + M_{CD} = 0\) (8)

\(M_{DB} + M_{DC} + M_{DE} = 0\) (9)

Joint Rotations By substituting the slope-deflection equations into the equilibrium equations, we obtain

\(0.467EI\theta_C + 0.133EI\theta_D = -0.000832EI\) \(0.133EI\theta_C + 0.667EI\theta_D = -0.000416EI\)

Substitution of \(EI = (29,000)(800)/(12)^2 k-ft^2\) into the right sides of the preceding equations yields

\(0.467EI\theta_C + 0.133EI\theta_D = -134\) (10)

\(0.133EI\theta_C + 0.667EI\theta_D = -67\) (11)

Solving Eqs. (10) and (11) simultaneously, we obtain

\(EI\theta_C = -273.883 k-ft^2\) \(EI\theta_D = -45.838 k-ft^2\)

Member End Moments By substituting the numerical values of \(EI\theta_C\) and \(EI\theta_D\) into the slope-deflection equations, we obtain

\(M_{AC} = -27.4 k-ft\) or \(27.4 k-ft \circlearrowright \) Ans.

\(M_{CA} = -54.8 k-ft\) or \(54.8 k-ft \circlearrowright \) Ans.

\(M_{BD} = -4.6 k-ft\) or \(4.6 k-ft \circlearrowright \) Ans.

\(M_{DB} = -9.2 k-ft\) or \(9.2 k-ft \circlearrowright \) Ans.

\(M_{CD} = 54.8 k-ft \circlearrowleft\) Ans.

\(M_{DC} = 85.4 k-ft \circlearrowleft\) Ans.

\(M_{DE} = -76.2 k-ft\) or \(76.2 k-ft \circlearrowright \) Ans.

Back substitution of the numerical values of member end moments into the equilibrium equations (Eqs. (8) and (9)) yields

\(M_{CA} + M_{CD} = -54.8 + 54.8 = 0\) Checks

\(M_{DB} + M_{DC} + M_{DE} = -9.2 + 85.4 – 76.2 = 0\) Checks

Member End Shears and Axial Forces See Fig. 16.14(d).

Support Reactions See Fig. 16.14(e).

Equilibrium Check The equilibrium equations check.
