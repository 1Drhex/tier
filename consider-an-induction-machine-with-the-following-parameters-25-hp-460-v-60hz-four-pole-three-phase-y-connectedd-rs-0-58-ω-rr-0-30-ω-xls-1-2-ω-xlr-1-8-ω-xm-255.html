Guest Question.

Consider an induction machine with the following parameters: 25 hp, 460 V, 60Hz, four pole, three phase, Y connectedd
\(r_{s} = 0.58 Ω, r_{r} = 0.30 Ω, X_{1s} = 1.2 Ω, X_{1r} = 1.8 Ω, X_{M} = 255.7 Ω \)The machine is operated under indirect vector control. The machine rotates at 1720 rpm and delivers 80% of its rated power to the load. Rated (peak) flux linkage is given to the controller. Determine the stator cur-rent and frequency

Admin
Solution

With a base frequency of\( ω_{b}\) = 120π, machine inductances are obtained as follows:

\(L_{ls} = 3.18 mΗ, L_{lr} = 4.78 mΗ, L_{M} = 68 mΗ\)

The electromagnetic torque on the shaft is calculated as follows

\(T_{e}=\frac{p_{owt}}{\omega_{m} }= \frac{0.8\times 25\times 746W}{1720\times \frac{2\pi }{60}rad/s }= 82.83 N.m \)

The peak of the rated flux linkage of the machine can be calculated
using the peak of the rated voltage (phase) and the magnetization reac-tance at the rated frequency, as follows:

\(\lambda _{rated}\left(peak\right)= L_{M}I_{m}= L_{M}\frac{V_{phase}\left(peak\right) }{X_{M}}= \frac{V_{phase}\left(peak\right)}{\omega _{b}}= \frac{460\frac{\sqrt{2} }{\sqrt{3} } }{120\pi }= 0.996 Wb.t \)

The calculated electromagnetic torque and flux linkage are now used to obtain the corresponding d- and q-components of the stator current, as follows:

\(i_{ds}=\frac{\lambda _{rated}}{L_{M}}=14.6 A\)
\(i_{qs}= T_{e}\left\lgroup\frac{4}{3p}\frac{L_{1r}+L_{M}}{L_{M}}\frac{1}{\lambda _{rated}} \right\rgroup= 29.65 A\)

The rms of the stator current will therefore be

\(I_{s} = (i^{2}_{ds} + i^{2}_{qs} )/ \sqrt{2} = 23.38 A.\)

The rotor speed and the angular frequency of the stator current are determined as follows:

\(\omega _{r} = 1720\times \frac{2\pi }{60}\times \frac{p}{2}= 360.24 rad/s \)(electrical)

\(\omega _{e}= \omega _{r}+\frac{r_{r}}{L_{1r}+L_{M} }\frac{i_{qs}}{i_{ds}}= 368.6 rad/s \left(electrical\right) \)

The corresponding frequency of the stator current is therefore 368.6/(2π) = 58.66 Hz
