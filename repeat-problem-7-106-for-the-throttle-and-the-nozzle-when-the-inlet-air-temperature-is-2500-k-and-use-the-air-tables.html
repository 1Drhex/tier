Guest Question.

Repeat for the throttle and the nozzle when the inlet air temperature is 2500 K and use the air tables.

 

Admin
Solution

C.V. Throttle, Steady single inlet and exit flow, no work or heat transfer.

Energy Eq.4.13: \(h _{ i }= h _{ e } \quad\left( Z _{ i }= Z _{ e } \text { and } V \text { 's are small }\right)\)

Entropy Eq.7.9: \(s _{ e }= s _{ i }+\int dq / T + s _{ gen }= s _{ i }+0+ s _{ gen }\)

Since it is air we have h = h(T) so same h means same \(T _{ e }= T _{ i }= 2 5 0 0 K\)

\(s _{ gen }= s _{ e }- s _{ i }= s _{ Te }^{ o }- s _{ Ti }^{ o }- R \ln \left( P _{ e } / P _{ i }\right)=0-0.287 \ln (1 / 2)= 0 . 2 k J / kg K\)

 

C.V. Nozzle, Steady single inlet and exit flow, no work or heat transfer.

Energy Eq.4.13: \(h _{ i }= h _{ e }+ V _{ e }^{2} / 2 \quad\left( Z _{ i }= Z _{ e }\right)\)

Entropy Eq.7.9: \(s _{ e }= s _{ i }+\int dq / T + s _{ gen }= s _{ i }+0+0\)

The isentropic process \(\left( s _{ e }= s _{ i }\right)\) gives Eq.6.19

\(0= s _{ e }- s _{ i }= s _{ Te }^{ o }- s _{ Ti }^{ o }- R \ln \left( P _{ e } / P _{ i }\right)\)

 

\(\Rightarrow \quad s _{ Te }^{ o }= s _{ Ti }^{ o }+ R \ln \left( P _{ e } / P _{ i }\right)=9.24781+0.287 \ln (100 / 200)=9.04888\)

 

\(T = 2 1 3 6 . 6 K , \quad h _{ e }=2422.86 kJ / kg\)

The energy equation becomes

\(V _{ e }^{2} / 2= h _{ i }- h _{ e }=2883.06-2422.86=460.2 kJ / kg\)

 

\(V _{ e }=\sqrt{2 \times 1000 \times 460.2}= 959 m / s\)
