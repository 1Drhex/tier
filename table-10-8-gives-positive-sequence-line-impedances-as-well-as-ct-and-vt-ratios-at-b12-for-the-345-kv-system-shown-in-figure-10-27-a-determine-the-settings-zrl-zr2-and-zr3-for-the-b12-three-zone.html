Three-zone impedance relay settings

Table 10.8 gives positive-sequence line impedances as well as CT and VT ratios at B12 for the 345-kV system shown in Figure 10.27. (a) Determine the settings \(Z_{r1}\), \(Z_{r2}\), and \(Z_{r3}\) for the B12 three-zone, directional impedance relays connected as shown in Figure 10.31. Consider only solid, three-phase faults. (b) Maximum current for line 1–2 during emergency loading conditions is 1500 A at a power factor of 0.95 lagging. Verify that B12 does not trip during normal andn emergency loadings.

Line Positive-Sequence Impedance Ω
1–2 8 +J50
2–3 8 +J50
2–4 5.3+J33
1–3 4.3+J27
Breaker CT Ratio VT Ratio
B12 1500:5 3000:1
Admin
Solution

a. Denoting \(V_{LN}\) as the line-to-neutral voltage at bus 1 and \(I_{L}\) as the line current through B12, the primary impedance Z viewed at B12 is

\(Z=\frac{V_{LN}}{I_{L}}\Omega\)

Using the CT and VT ratios given in Table 10.8, the secondary impedance viewed by the B12 impedance relays is

\( Z^{\prime }=\frac{V_{LN}/\left(\frac{3000}{1} \right) }{I_{L}\left(\frac{1500}{5} \right) } =\frac{Z}{10} \)

Setting the B12 zone 1 relay for 80% reach, that is, 80% of the line 1–2 (secondary) impedance:

\(Z_{r1}=0.80\left(8+j50\right) /10=0.64+j4=4.05\angle 80.9°\Omega\) secondary

Setting the B12 zone 2 relay for 120% reach:

\(Z_{r2}=1.2\left(8+j50\right) /10=0.96+j6=6.08\angle 80.9°\Omega \) secondary

From Table 10.8, line 2–4 has a larger impedance than line 2–3. Therefore, set the B12 zone 3 relay for 100% reach of line 1–2 plus 120% reach of line 2–4.

\(Z_{r3}=1.0\left(8+j50\right) /10+1.2\left(5.3+j33\right)/10 =1.44+j8.96=9.07\angle 80.9°\Omega \) secondary

b. The secondary impedance viewed by B12 during emergency loading, using

\(V_{LN} =345/\sqrt{3}\angle 0°=199.2\angle 0° kV\) and \(I_{L} =1500\angle -\cos ^{-1}\left(0.95\right) =1500\angle -18.19° A\) , is

\(Z^{\prime } =Z/10=\left(\frac{199.2\times 10^{3} }{1500\angle -18.19°} \right) /10=13.28\angle 18.19°\Omega \) secondary

Since this impedance exceeds the zone 3 setting of \(9.07\angle 80.9°\Omega \), the impedance during emergency loading lies outside the trip regions of the three-zone, directional impedance relay. Also, lower line loadings during normal operation will result in even larger impedances farther away from the trip regions. B12 will trip during faults but not during normal and emergency loadings.
