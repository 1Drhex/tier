Guest Question.

Derive the kinematic model of the tricycle robot towing N trailers con-sidered in . Denote by \(\ell\) the distance between the front wheel and rear wheel axle of the tricycle, and by \(\ell _{i}\) the joint-to-joint length of the i-th trailer.

Admin
Solution

With the configuration vector \( q \) =\(\left[x y \phi \theta _{0} \theta _{1} . . . \theta _{N}\right]^{T} \) deﬁned in the solution to , the N+ 2 kinematic constraints are

\(\dot{x} _{f} \sin \left(\theta _{0} + \phi \right) – \dot{y} _{f}\cos \left(\theta _{0} + \phi \right) \) = 0

\(\dot{x} \sin \theta _{0} – \dot{y} \cos \theta _{0} \) = 0

\(\dot{x} _{i} \sin \theta _{i} – \dot{y} _{i}\cos \theta _{i} \) = 0 i = 1, …, N,

where (\(x _{f}\) ,\( y _{f}\)) and (\(x _{i}\) , \(x _{i}\)) are the Cartesian coordinates of the centre of the tricycle front wheel and of the i-th trailer wheel axle midpoint, respectively (compare with (11.15), (11.16)). Denote by \(\ell\) the distance between the front wheel and the rear axle of the tricycle, and by \(\ell_{i}\) the hinge-to-hinge length of the i-th trailer. It is

\(\dot{x} _{f} \sin \left(\theta + \phi \right) – \dot{y} _{f}\cos \left(\theta + \phi \right) \) = 0 (11.15)

\(\dot{x} \sin \theta – \dot{y} \cos \theta \) = 0 (11.16)

\(x _{f}\) = x + \(\ell\cos \theta _{0} \)

\(y _{f}\) = y + \(\ell\sin \theta _{0} \)

and

\( x _{i}\) = x – \( \sum\limits_{j = 1}^{i}{\ell _{j} \cos\theta _{j} } \)

\( y _{i}\) = y – \( \sum\limits_{j = 1}^{i}{\ell _{j} \sin\theta _{j} } \)

so that the kinematic constraints become

\(\dot{x} \sin \left(\theta _{0} + \phi\right) – \dot{y} \cos \left(\theta _{0} + \phi\right) – \dot{\theta } _{0} \ell \cos\phi \) = 0

\(\dot{x} \sin \theta _{0} – \dot{y} \cos \theta _{0} \) = 0

\(\dot{x} \sin \theta _{i} – \dot{y} \cos \theta _{i} + \sum\limits_{j = 1}^{i}{\dot{\theta } _{j} \ell _{j}\cos \left( \theta _{i} – \theta _{j} \right) } \) = 0 i = 1, …, N

The null space of the constraint matrix is spanned by the two columns of

 

\(G(q)=\begin{bmatrix} \cos \theta _0 & 0 \\ \sin \theta _0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \\ \frac{1}{\ell}\tan \phi & 0 \\ -\frac{1}{\ell_1}\sin (\theta _1-\theta _0) & 0 \\ -\frac{1}{\ell_2}\cos (\theta _1-\theta _0)\sin (\theta _2-\theta _1) & 0 \\ \cdot & \cdot \\ \cdot & \cdot \\ \cdot & \cdot \\ -\frac{1}{\ell_i}\left(\prod_{j=1}^{i-1}{\cos \left(\theta_j – \theta _{j-1}\right) } \right)\sin \left(\theta_i – \theta _{i-1}\right) & 0 \\ \cdot & \cdot \\ \cdot & \cdot \\ \cdot & \cdot \\ -\frac{1}{\ell_N}\left(\prod_{j=1}^{N-1}{\cos \left(\theta_j – \theta _{j-1}\right) } \right)\sin \left(\theta_N – \theta _{N-1}\right) & 0 \end{bmatrix} \)

 

(compare with (11.19). The kinematic control system is then

\(\dot{q} \) = \(g_{1}\left(q\right) \upsilon + g_{2}\left(q\right) \omega\)

where \(\upsilon \) and ω are respectively the driving velocity of the rear wheels and the
steering velocity of the tricycle.

\(\left [ \begin{matrix}\dot{x} \\ \dot{y} \\ \dot{\theta } \\ \dot{\phi } \end{matrix} \right ] \) = \( \left [ \begin{matrix}\cos \theta \\\sin \theta \\ \tan {\phi }/{\ell } \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \upsilon + \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{matrix} \right ]\omega \) (11.19)
