Guest Question.

Figure 7.28 shows the bracket used in a jib crane to connect the tie rod. The maximum force in the tie rod is 5 kN, which is inclined at an angle of 30° with the horizontal. The bracket is fastened by means of four identical bolts, two at A and two at B. The bolts are made of plain carbon steel 30C8 \( \left(S_{y t}=400 N / mm ^{2}\right) \) and the factor of safety is 5. Assume maximum shear stress theory and determine the size of the bolts.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } P=5 kN \quad S_{y t}=400 N / mm ^{2} \quad(f s)=5 \).

Step I Permissible shear stress

\( \tau_{\max }=\frac{S_{s y}}{(f s)}=\frac{0.5 S_{y t}}{(f s)}=\frac{0.5(400)}{5}=40 N / mm ^{2} \).

Step II Tensile and shear stresses in bolts
The axial force in the tie rod (P) is resolved into vertical and horizontal components

\( \text { Vertical component }=P \sin \theta=5000 \sin (30) \).

= 2500 N.

\( \text { Horizontal component }=P \cos \theta=5000 \cos (30) \).

= 4330.13 N
The vertical component produces direct tensile force on each bolt. It is given by,

\( P_{1}=P_{2}=\frac{2500}{(\text { No. of bolts })}=\frac{2500}{4}=625 N \) (i).

The horizontal component produces direct shear force on each bolt. It is given by,

\( P_{1}^{\prime}=P_{2}^{\prime}=\frac{P}{\text { (No. of bolts) }}=\frac{4330.13}{4}=1082.53 N \) (ii).

In addition, the horizontal component produces tensile force on each bolt due to its moment about the tilting edge C. The bolts at A are denoted by 2 and the bolts at B by 1. The bolts at B are located at the farthest distance from the tilting edge C. From

Eq. (7.10),

\( P_{2}^{\prime \prime}=\frac{P e l_{2}}{2\left(l_{1}^{2}+l_{2}^{2}\right)} \) (7.10).

\( P_{1}^{\prime \prime}=\frac{P e l_{1}}{2\left(l_{1}^{2}+l_{2}^{2}\right)} \).

\( =\frac{(4330.13)(50+25)(175)}{2\left(25^{2}+175^{2}\right)} \).

= 909.33 N (iii).

From (i) and (iii),

\( \sigma_{ t }=\frac{\left(P_{1}+P_{1}^{\prime \prime}\right)}{A}=\frac{625+909.33}{A}=\frac{1534.33}{A} N / mm ^{2} \).

From (ii),

\( \tau=\frac{P_{1}^{\prime}}{A}=\frac{1082.53}{A} N / mm ^{2} \).

Step III Maximum shear stress in bolts
From Eq. (7.14),

\( \tau_{\max }=\sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{t}}{2}\right)^{2}+(\tau)^{2}} \) (7.14).

\( \tau_{\max }=\sqrt{\left(\frac{\sigma_{t}}{2}\right)^{2}+(\tau)^{2}} \).

\( =\sqrt{\left(\frac{1534.33}{2 A}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{1082.53}{A}\right)^{2}} \).

\( =\left(\frac{1326.81}{A}\right) N / mm ^{2} \).

Step IV Size of bolts

\( \tau_{\max .}=\frac{1326.81}{A} \quad \text { or } \quad 40=\frac{1326.81}{A} \).

∴ A = 33.17 mm²
From Table 7.1, the standard size of the bolts is M8 (A = 36.6 mm² ).
