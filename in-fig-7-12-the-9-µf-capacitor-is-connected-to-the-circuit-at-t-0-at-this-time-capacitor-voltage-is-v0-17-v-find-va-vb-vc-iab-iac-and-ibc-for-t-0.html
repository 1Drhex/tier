Guest Question.

In Fig. 7-12 the 9-µF capacitor is connected to the circuit at t = 0. At this time, capacitor voltage is \( v_{0}=17 V . \text { Find } v_{A}, v_{B}, v_{C}, i_{A B}, i_{A C}, \text { and } i_{B C} \text { for } t>0 .\)

Admin
Solution

Apply KCL at nodes A, B, and C for t > 0 to find voltages in term of i:

Node A: \( \left(\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{3}+\frac{1}{6}\right) v_{A}-\frac{1}{2} v_{B}-\frac{1}{6} v_{C}=0 \quad \text { or } \quad 6 v_{A}-3 v_{B}-v_{C}=0 \) (11)

 

Node B: \( -\frac{1}{2} v_{A}+\left(\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{4}\right) v_{B}-10^{3} i-\frac{1}{4} v_{C}=0 \quad \text { or } \quad-2 v_{A}+3 v_{B}-v_{C}=\left(4 \times 10^{3}\right) i \) (12)

Node C: \( -\frac{1}{6} v_{A}-\frac{1}{4} v_{B}+\left(\frac{1}{4}+\frac{1}{6}+\frac{1}{12}\right) v_{C}=0 \quad \text { or } \quad-2 v_{A}-3 v_{B}+6 v_{C}=0 \) (13)

Solving (11), (12), and (13) simultaneously,

\( v_{A}=\frac{7}{3}\left(10^{3}\right) i \quad v_{B}=\frac{34}{9}\left(10^{3}\right) i \quad v_{C}=\frac{8}{3}\left(10^{3}\right) i \)

The circuit as seen by the capacitor is equivalent to a resistor \( R=v_{B} / i=34 / 9 k \Omega \). The capacitor discharges its initial voltage \( V_{0} \)in an exponential form with a time constant \( \tau=R C=\frac{34}{9}\left(10^{3}\right)\left(9 \times 10^{-6}\right)=0.034 s \). For t > 0, the voltages and currents are

\(\begin{gathered}v_{B}=V_{0} e^{-t / \tau}=17 e^{-1000 t / 34} \quad( V ) \\i=-C \frac{d v_{B}}{d t}=\left(9 \times 17 \times 10^{-3} / 34 \right) e^{-1000 t / 34}=\left(4.5 \times 10^{-3}\right) e^{-1000 t / 34} \quad( A )\\v_{A}=\frac{7}{3}\left(10^{3}\right) i=10.5 e^{-1000 t / 34} \quad( V ) \quad v_{C}=\frac{8}{3}\left(10^{3}\right) i=12 e^{-1000 t / 34} \quad( V ) \\v_{A B}=v_{A}-v_{B}=-6.5 e^{-1000 t / 34} \quad( V ) \quad i_{A B}=v_{A B} / 2000=\left(-3.25 \times 10^{-3}\right) e^{-1000 t / 34}\quad( A ) \\v_{A C}=v_{A}-v_{C}=-1.5 e^{-1000 t / 34} \quad( V ) \quad i_{A C}=v_{A C} / 6000=\left(-0.25 \times 10^{-3}\right) e^{-1000 t / 34} \quad( A )\\v_{B C}=v_{B}-v_{C}=5 e^{-1000 t / 34} \quad( V ) \quad i_{B C}=v_{B C} / 4000=\left(1.25 \times 10^{-3}\right) e^{-1000 t / 34}\quad( A )\end{gathered}\)

All voltages and currents are exponential functions and have the same time constant. For simplicity, it is customary to use units of V, mA, kΩ, and ms for voltage, current, resistance, and time, respectively, so that the multipliers 1000 and \( 10^{-3} \) can be omitted from the equations as summarized below.

\(\begin{array}{rllll}v_{A} & =10.5 e^{-t / 34} & ( V ) & v_{A B}=-6.5 e^{-t / 34} & ( V ) & i_{A B}=-3.25 e^{-t / 34} & ( mA ) \\v_{B} & =17 e^{-i / 34} & ( V ) & v_{A C}=-1.5 e^{-t / 34} & ( V ) & i_{A C}=-0.25 e^{-t / 34} & ( mA ) \\ v_C & =12 e^{-t / 34} & ( V ) & v_{B C}=5 e^{-t / 34} & ( V ) & i_{B C}=1.25 e^{-t / 34}&( m A ) \\i & =4.5 e^{-t / 34} & ( mA )\end{array}\)
