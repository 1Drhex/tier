Maximum Dynamic Displacement:

Since \(f_n\) = 10.0 Hz > 5.0 Hz, use equation (12-40).

\(X_{max}= \frac{F_{m}\Delta _{p}f^{2} _{o} }{2f^{2} _{n}}\) for \(f_n\) > 5 Hz (12-40)

 

\( = \frac{(315)(9.42 \times 10^{- 6})(5.0)^2}{(2)(10.0)^2} = 371\mu\) -in(fast walking)

 

\( = \frac{(280)(9.42 \times 10^{- 6})(2.5)^2}{(2)(10.0)^2} = 82.4\mu\) -in. (moderate walking)

 

\( = \frac{(240)(9.42 \times 10^{- 6})(1.4)^2}{(2)(10.0)^2} = 22.2\mu\) -in. (slow walking)

The maximum floor velocity is then determined from equation (12-39):

\(V =2\pi f_{n}X_{max}\) (12-39)

= (2)(π)(10.0)(371) = 23,300 μ -in./s (fast walking)

= (2)(π)(10.0)(82.4) = 5170 μ -in./s (moderate walking)

= (2)(π)(10.0)(22.2) = 1390 μ -in./s (slow walking)

that this floor system could not support any of the equipment listed for fast walking. Reducing the criteria to moderate walking would allow the floor to support any of the equipment listed under 8000 μ-in./s and less. Further reducing the criteria to slow walking would allow equipment listed under 2000 μ-in./s and less.

Sensitive equipment located adjacent to a long, straight corridor would have a greater likelihood of being impacted by “fast walking.” If “slow walking” is desired in order to reduce the vibrational velocity, then short-length corridors with turns or bends should be used to reduce the walking speed, or the equipment should be relocated away from areas with heavy foot traffic.
