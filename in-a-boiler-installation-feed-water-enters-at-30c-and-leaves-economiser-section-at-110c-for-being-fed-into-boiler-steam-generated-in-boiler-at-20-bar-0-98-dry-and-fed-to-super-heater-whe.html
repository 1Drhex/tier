Guest Question.

In a boiler installation feed water enters at 30°C and leaves economiser section at 110°C for being fed into boiler. Steam generated in boiler at 20 bar, 0.98 dry and fed to super heater where its’ temperature is raised up to 300°C. For the coal with calorific value of 30,500 kJ/kg and steam generation rate of 10kg/kg of coal burnt determine the energy received per kg of water and steam in economiser, boiler and superheater section as fraction of energy supplied by coal. Take \(\textbf{C}_{\text{p,feed water}}\) = 4.18 kJ/kg·K, \(\textbf{C}_{\text{p,superheated steam}}\) = 2.093 kJ/kg·K.

Admin
Solution

\(\begin{aligned}\text{From steam table,}\qquad \qquad \qquad h_{4}& = 3023.5 kJ/kg\\h_{\text{f at 20 bar}}&= 908.79 kJ/kg\\h_{\text{fg at 20 bar}}&= 1890.7 kJ/kg\\h_{3}&= 908.79 + (0.98 × 1890.7) = 2761.7 kJ/kg\\\text{For feed water, }\quad\qquad \qquad \qquad h_{1}&= h_{\text{f at 30°C}} = 125.79 kJ/kg\\\text{Total heat supplied}&= (h_{4} – h_{1}) = (3023.5 – 125.79) = 2897.71 kJ/kg\\\text{Heat consumed in economiser}&= C_{\text{p, feed water}} × (110 – 30)\\&= \text{4.18 × (110 – 30) = 334.4 kJ/kg steam}\\&= \text{334.4 × 8 = 2678.4 kJ/kg coal}\\\text{Heat consumed in boiler}&= h_{3} – h_{2} = 2761.7 – (C_{\text{p feed water}} × 110)\\&= \text{2301.9 kJ/kg steam}\\&=\text{2301.9 × 8 = 18415.2 kJ/kg coal}\\\text{Heat consumed in super heater}&= h_{4} – h_{3} = 3023.5 – 2761.7\\&=\text{261.8 kJ/kg steam}\\&=\text{261.8 × 8 = 2094.4 kJ/kg coal}\\\text{Heat provided by burning of coal}&= \text{30,500 kJ/kg coal}\\\text{Fraction of energy consumed in ec}&\text{onomiser}\\&= \frac{2678.4}{30,500} = \text{0.0878 or 8.78}\%\end{aligned}\\\text{Fraction of energy consumed in boiler }= \frac{18415.2}{30500} = \text{0.6038 or 60.38}\%\\\text{Fracton of energy consumed in superheater }= \frac{2094.4}{30500} = \text{0.0687 or 6.87}\%\)
