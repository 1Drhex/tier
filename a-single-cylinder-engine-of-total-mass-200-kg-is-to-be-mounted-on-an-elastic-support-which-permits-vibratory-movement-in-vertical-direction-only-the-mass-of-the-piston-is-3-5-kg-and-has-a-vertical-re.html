Guest Question.

A single-cylinder engine of total mass 200 kg is to be mounted on an elastic support which permits vibratory movement in vertical direction only. The mass of the piston is 3.5 kg and has a vertical reciprocating motion which may be assumed simple harmonic with a stroke of 150 mm. It is desired that the maximum vibratory force transmitted through the elastic support to the foundation shall be 600 N when the engine speed is 800 r.p.m. and less than this at all higher speeds.
1. Find the necessary stiffness of the elastic support, and the amplitude of vibration at 800 r.p.m., and
2. If the engine speed is reduced below 800 r.p.m. at what speed will the transmitted force again becomes 600 N?

Admin
Solution

Given : \(m_{1}=200 kg ; m_{2}=3.5 kg ; l=150 mm =0.15 mm \text { or } r=l / 2=0.075 m\) ; \(F_{ T }=600 N ; N=800 \text { r.p.m. or } \omega=2 \pi \times 800 / 60=83.8 rad / s\)

We know that the disturbing force at 800 r.p.m.,

F = Centrifugal force on the piston

\(=m_{2} \cdot \omega^{2} \cdot r=3.5(83.8)^{2} 0.075=1843 N\)

1. Stiffness of elastic support and amplitude of vibration

Let s = Stiffness of elastic support in N/m, and

\(x_{\max }\) = Max. amplitude of vibration in metres.

Since the max. vibratory force transmitted to the foundation is equal to the force on the elastic support (neglecting damping), therefore

Max. vibratory force transmitted to the foundation,

\(F_{ T }\) = Force on the elastic support

= Stiffness of elastic support Ã— Max. amplitude of vibration

\(=s \times x_{\max }=s \times \frac{F}{m\left[\omega^{2}-\left(\omega_{n}\right)^{2}\right]}\)

 

\(=s \times \frac{F}{m\left(\omega^{2}-\frac{s}{m}\right)}=\frac{F \cdot s}{m \cdot \omega^{2}-s}\) \(\ldots\left[\because\left(\omega_{n}\right)^{2}=\frac{s}{m}\right]\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(600=\frac{1843 \times s}{200(83.8)^{2}-s}=\frac{1843 s}{1.4 \times 10^{6}-s}\) \(\left.\ldots \text { (Substituting } m=m_{1}\right)\)

 

or \(840 \times 10^{6}-600 s=1843 s\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(s=0.344 \times 10^{6}=344 \times 10^{3} N / m\)

 

and maximum amplitude of vibration,

 

\(x_{\max }=\frac{F}{m \cdot \omega^{2}-s}=\frac{1843}{200(83.8)^{2}-344 \times 10^{3}}=\frac{1843}{1056 \times 10^{3}} m\)

 

\(=1.745 \times 10^{-3} m =1.745 mm\)

 

2. Speed at the which the transmitted force again becomes 600 N

The transmitted force will rise as the speed of the engine falls and passes through resonance. There will be a speed below resonance at which the transmitted force will again equal to 600 N. Let this speed be \(\omega_{1} rad / s \text { (or } N_{1} \text { r.p.m.) }\).

\(\therefore \) Disturbing force, \(F=m_{2}\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2} r=3.5\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2} 0.075=0.2625\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2} N\)

Since the engine speed is reduced below \(N_{1}=800 \text { r.p.m. }\), therefore in this case, max, amplitude of vibration,

\(x_{\max }=\frac{F}{m\left[\left(\omega_{n}\right)^{2}-\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2}\right]}=\frac{F}{m\left[\frac{s}{m}-\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2}\right]}=\frac{F}{s-m\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2}}\)

 

and Force transmitted \(=s \times \frac{F}{s-m\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2}}\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(600=344 \times 10^{3} \times \frac{0.2625\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2}}{344 \times 10^{3}-200\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2}}=\frac{90.3 \times 10^{3}\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2}}{344 \times 10^{2}-200\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2}}\) \(\left.\ldots \text { (Substituting } m=m_{1}\right)\)

\(206.4 \times 10^{6}-120 \times 10^{3}\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2}=90.3 \times 10^{3}\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2} \quad \text { or } \quad\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2}=981\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(\omega_{1}=31.32 rad / s \text { or } \quad N_{1}=31.32 \times 60 / 2 \pi=299 r.p.m\)
