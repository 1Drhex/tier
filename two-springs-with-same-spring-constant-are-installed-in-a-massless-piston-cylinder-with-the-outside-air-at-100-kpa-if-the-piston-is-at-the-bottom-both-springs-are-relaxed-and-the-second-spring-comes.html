Guest Question.

Two springs with same spring constant are installed in a massless piston/cylinder with the outside air at 100 kPa. If the piston is at the bottom, both springs are relaxed and the second spring comes in contact with the piston at V = 2 \(m ^{3}\). The cylinder (Fig. P3.221) contains ammonia initially at −2°C, x = 0.13, V = 1 \(m ^{3}\), which is then heated until the pressure finally reaches 1200 kPa. At what pressure will the piston touch the second spring? Find the final temperature, the total work done by the ammonia and the heat transfer.

Admin
Solution

State 1: P = 399.7 kPa Table B.2.1

v = 0.00156 + 0.13×0.3106 = 0.0419 \(m ^{3}\)/kg

u = 170.52 + 0.13×1145.78 = 319.47 kJ/kg

m = V/v = 1/0.0419 = 23.866 kg

At bottom state 0: 0 \(m ^{3}\), 100 kPa

State 2: V = 2 \(m ^{3}\) and on line 0-1-2

Final state 3: 1200 kPa, on line segment 2.

Slope of line 0-1-2: \(\Delta P / \Delta V =\left( P _{1}- P _{0}\right) / \Delta V =(399.7-100) / 1=299.7 kPa / m ^{3}\)

 

\(P _{2}= P _{1}+\left( V _{2}- V _{1}\right) \Delta P / \Delta V =399.7+(2-1) \times 299.7= 6 9 9 . 4 kPa\)

 

State 3: Last line segment has twice the slope.

 

\(P _{3}= P _{2}+\left( V _{3}- V _{2}\right) 2 \Delta P / \Delta V \Rightarrow V _{3}= V _{2}+\left( P _{3}- P _{2}\right) /(2 \Delta P / \Delta V )\)

 

\(V _{3}=2+(1200-699.4) / 599.4=2.835 m ^{3}\)

 

\(v _{3}= v _{1} V _{3} / V _{1}=0.0419 \times 2.835 / 1=0.1188 \Rightarrow T _{3}= 5 1 ^{\circ} C\)

 

\(u _{3}=1384.39 kJ / kg =1383+(1404.8-1383) \frac{0.1188-0.11846}{0.12378-0.11846} \text { from B.2.2 }\)

 

\({ }_{1} W _{3}={ }_{1} W _{2}+{ }_{2} W _{3}=\frac{1}{2}\left( P _{1}+ P _{2}\right)\left( V _{2}- V _{1}\right)+\frac{1}{2}\left( P _{3}+ P _{2}\right)\left( V _{3}- V _{2}\right)\)

 

= 549.6 + 793.0 = 1342.6 kJ

 

The energy equation gives the heat transfer as

\(\begin{aligned}{ }_{1} Q _{3} &= m \left( u _{3}- u _{1}\right)+{ }_{1} W _{3}=23.866 kg (1384.39-319.47) kJ / kg +1342.6 kJ \\&= 2 6 7 5 8 ~ k J\end{aligned}\)
