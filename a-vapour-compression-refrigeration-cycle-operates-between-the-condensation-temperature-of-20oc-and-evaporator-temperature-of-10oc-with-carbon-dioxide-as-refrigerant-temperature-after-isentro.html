Guest Question.

A vapour compression refrigeration cycle operates between the condensation temperature of 20ºC and evaporator temperature of –10ºC with carbon dioxide as refrigerant. Temperature after isentropic compression is 40ºC and condensate leaves at 10ºC before being passed through expansion valve. Determine COP of system and mass flow rate of CO\(_{2}\) required to get refrigeration effect of 2 kW. Properties of CO\(_{2}\) are given as under:
Properties of CO\(_{2}\)

Temperature ºC Saturation Pressure, bar Specific Volume,v\(_{g}\) m3/kg Enthalpy, kJ/kg Entropy, kJ/kg·K Specific heat kJ/kg·K
h\(_{f}\) h\(_{g}\) s\(_{f}\) s\(_{g}\) C\(_{pf}\) C\(_{pg}\)
20 57.27 – 144.11 299.62 0.523 1.0527 2.889 2.135
– 10 26.49 0.014 60.78 322.28 0.2381 1.2324 – –
Admin
Solution
\(\text{Processes of vapour compression cycle are shown on T-s diagram}\\\begin{aligned}1 – 2&: \text{Isentropic compression process}\\2 – 3 – 4&: \text{Condensation process}\\4 – 5&: \text{Isenthalpic expansion process}\\5 – 1&: \text{Refrigeration process in evaporator}\end{aligned}\\\begin{aligned}h_{1}&= h_{\text{g at – 10ºC}} = 322.28 kJ/kg\\h_{2}&= h_{\text{g at 20ºC}} + C_{\text{pg at 20ºC}} × (40 – 20)\\&= 299.62 + (2.135 × 20)\\h_{2}&= 342.32 kJ/kg\\\text{Entropy at state 2, }s_{2}&= s_{\text{g at + 20ºC}} + C_{\text{pg at 20ºC}} × \ln\left\lgroup\frac{273 + 40}{273 + 20} \right\rgroup \\&= 1.0527 + 2.135 × \ln\left\lgroup\frac{313}{293} \right\rgroup \\s_{2}&= 1.1937 kJ/kg·K\end{aligned}\\\text{Entropy during isentropic process, }s_{1} = s_{2}\\\begin{aligned}1.1937&= s_{\text{f at – 10ºC}} + x_{1} × s_{\text{fg at – 10ºC}}\\1.1937&= 0.2381 + x_{1} × (1.2324 – 0.2381)\\x_{1}&= 0.961\\\text{Entropy at state 1, }h_{1}&= h_{\text{f at – 10ºC}} + x_{1} × h_{\text{fg at – 10ºC}}\\&= 60.78 + (0.961 × (322.28 – 60.78))\\h_{1}&= 312.08 kJ/kg\\h_{3}&= h_{\text{f at 20ºC}} = 144.11 kJ/kg\\\text{Since undercooling occu}&\text{rs up to 10ºC, so, }h_{4} = h_{3} – C_{pf} · ΔT\\&= 144.11 – (2.889 × (20 – 10))\\h_{4}&= 115.22 kJ/kg\\\text{Also, }h_{4}&= h_{5} = 115.22 kJ/kg\\\text{Refrigeration effect per }&\text{kg of refrigerant }= (h_{1} – h_{5}) = (312.08 – 115.22)\\&= 196.86 kJ/kg\\\text{Let refrigerant flow rate}&\text{ be m kg/s.}\\\text{Refrigeration effect ⇒ 2}&= m × 196.86\\m&= 0.1016 kg/s\\\text{Compressor work, }w_{c}&= h_{2} – h_{1}= 342.32 – 312.08\\w_{c}&= 30.24 kJ/kg\\COP&= \frac{\text{Refrigeration effect per kg}}{\text{Compressor work per kg}} = \frac{196.86}{30.24}\\COP&= \textbf{6.51}\text{, Mass flow rate = }\textbf{0.1016 kg/s}\end{aligned}\)
