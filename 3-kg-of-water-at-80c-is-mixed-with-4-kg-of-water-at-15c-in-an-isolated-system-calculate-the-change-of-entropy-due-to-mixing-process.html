Guest Question.

3 kg of water at 80°C is mixed with 4 kg of water at 15°C in an isolated system. Calculate the change of entropy due to mixing process.

Admin
Solution

Fig. 3.43 shows the isolated system before mixing. When barrier is removed, the water from two compartments mix each other. Let \(t_{m}\) is the final equilibrium temperature after mixing.

Applying first law of thermodynamics to the isolated system :

Total energy before mixing = Total energy after mixing

 

\(\therefore \quad 3 c_{p w}(80-0)+4 c_{p w}(15-0)=7 c_{p w}\left(t_{m}-0\right)\)

[\(c_{p w}\)= specific heat of water at constant pressure]

 

or \(240 c_{p w}+60 c_{p w}=7 c_{p w} t_{m}\)

 

or \(240+60=7 t_{m}\)

 

\(\therefore\) \(t_{m}=\frac{300}{7}=42.85^{\circ} C\)

 

Initial entropy of the system,

 

\(=3 c_{p w} \log _{e}\left(\frac{80+273}{273}\right)+4 c_{p w} \log _{e}\left(\frac{15+273}{273}\right)\)

 

\(=0.7709 c_{p w}+0.2139 c_{p w}=0.9848 c_{p w}\)

 

Final entropy of the system

 

\(=(3+4) c_{p w} \log _{e}\left(\frac{42.85+273}{273}\right)=1.0205 c_{p w}\)

 

Net change in entropy,


ΔS = Final entropy – Initial entropy

 

\(=1.0205 c_{p w}-0.9848 c_{p w}=0.0357 c_{p w}\)

 

\(=0.0357 \times 4.187 kJ / K \quad\left[\because \quad c_{p w}=4.187 kJ / kg K \right]\)

 

= 0.1495 kJ/K

 

Hence, net change in entropy = 0.1495 kJ/K.
