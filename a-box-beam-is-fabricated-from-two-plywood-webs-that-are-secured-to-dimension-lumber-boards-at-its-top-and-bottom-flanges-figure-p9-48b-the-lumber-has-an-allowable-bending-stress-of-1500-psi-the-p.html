Guest Question.

A box beam is fabricated from two plywood webs that are secured to dimension lumber boards at its top and bottom flanges (Figure P9.48b). The lumber has an allowable bending stress of 1,500 psi. The plywood has an allowable shear stress of 300 psi. The ⅜-in. diameter bolts have an allowable shear stress of 6,000 psi, and they are spaced at intervals of s = 9 in. The beam span is L = 15 ft (Figure P9.48a). Support A can be assumed to be pinned and support C can be idealized as a roller.
(a) Determine the maximum load P that can be applied to the beam at midspan.
(b) Report the bending stress in the lumber, the shear stress in the plywood, and the average shear stress in the bolts at the load P determined in part (a).

Admin
Solution

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

\(\begin{aligned}&\begin{array}{lcccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\(\text { in. })\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( in .^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left(\text { in. }^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { left web } & 576 & 0 & 0 & 576 \\\text { top flange } & 16 & 10 & 1,200 & 1,216 \\\text { bottom flange } & 16 & -10 & 1,200 & 1,216 \\\text { right web } & 576 & 0 & 0 & 576 \\\hline\end{array}\\&\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left(\text { in } .^{4}\right)=\quad 3,584\end{aligned}\)

 

Maximum shear force:
V = P/2

Maximum bending moment:
M = PL/4

(a) Determine maximum load P:
Consider maximum bending stress:

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma=\frac{M c}{I} \leq 1,500 psi \\&\therefore M \leq \frac{(1,500 psi )\left(3,584 in .^{4}\right)}{12 in .}=448,000 lb – in . \\&M=\frac{P L}{4} \leq 448,000 lb – in . \\&\therefore P_{\max } \leq \frac{4(448,000 lb – in .)}{(15 ft )(12 in . / ft )}=9,956 lb (a)\end{aligned}\)

Consider maximum horizontal shear stress (in plywood webs):

\(\begin{aligned}&Q=(3 in .)(4 in .)(10 in .)+2(0.5 in .)(12 in .)(6 in .)=192 in .^{3} \\&\tau=\frac{V Q}{I t} \leq 300 psi \\&\therefore V_{\max } \leq \frac{(300 psi )\left(3,584 in .^{4}\right)(2 \times 0.5 in .)}{192 in .^{3}}=5,600 lb \\&V_{\max }=\frac{P}{2} \\&\therefore P_{\max } \leq 2 V_{\max }=2(5,600 lb )=11,200 lb (b)\end{aligned}\)

Consider bolt shear stress:
The bolt cross-sectional area is:

\(A_{\text {bolt }}=\frac{\pi}{4}(0.375 \text { in. })^{2}=0.110447 \text { in. }^{2}\)

Each bolt acts in double shear; therefore, the maximum shear force that can be carried by one bolt is:

\(V_{\text {bolt }}=2\left(0.110447 in .^{2}\right)(6,000 psi )=1,325.364 lb\)

Determine the shear flow that can be allowed based on the bolt shear stress:

\(\begin{aligned}&q s \leq n_{f} V_{f} \\&\therefore q \geq \frac{n_{f} V_{f}}{s}=\frac{(1 \text { bolt })(1,325.364 lb / bolt )}{9 in .}=147.263 lb / in .\end{aligned}\)

Consider the dimension lumber boards that comprise the top flange.

\(\begin{aligned}&Q_{\text {fange }}=(3 \text { in. })(4 \text { in. })(10 \text { in. })=120 \text { in. }^{3} \\&q=\frac{V Q_{\text {nange }}}{I} \\&\therefore V_{\max }=\frac{q I}{Q_{\text {flange }}}=\frac{(147.263 lb / in .)\left(3,584 in .^{4}\right)}{120 in .{ }^{3}}=4,398.245 lb \\&V_{\max }=\frac{P}{2} \\&\therefore P_{\max } \leq 2 V_{\max }=2(4,398.245 lb )=8,796 lb (c)\end{aligned}\)

Compare the three values obtained for \(P_{\max }\) in Eqs. (a), (b), and (c) to find

\(P_{\max }=8,796 lb =8.80 kips\)

(b) Bending stress in lumber flanges for \(P_{\max }\):

\(\begin{aligned}&M=\frac{P L}{4}=\frac{(8,796 lb )(15 ft )}{4}=32,985 lb – ft \\&\sigma=\frac{M c}{I}=\frac{(32,985 lb – ft )(12 in. )(12 in . / ft )}{3,584 in .^{4}}=1,325 psi\end{aligned}\)

Maximum shear stress in plywood webs:

\(\begin{aligned}&V=\frac{P}{2}=\frac{8,796 lb }{2}=4,398 lb \\&\tau=\frac{V Q}{I t}=\frac{(4,398 lb )\left(192 in. ^{3}\right)}{\left(3,584 in .^{4}\right)(2 \times 0.5 in .)}=236 psi\end{aligned}\)

Bolt shear stress:

\(\tau_{\text {bolt }}=6,000 psi\)
