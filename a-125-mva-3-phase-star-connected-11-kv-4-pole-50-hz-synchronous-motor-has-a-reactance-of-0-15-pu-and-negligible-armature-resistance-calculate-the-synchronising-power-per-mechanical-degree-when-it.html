Guest Question.

A 125 MVA, 3-phase, star-connected 11 kV, 4-pole, 50 Hz synchronous motor has a reactance of 0.15 pu and negligible armature resistance. Calculate the synchronising power per mechanical degree when it supplies full load at 11 kV and 0.8 power factor leading.

Admin
Solution

Here, \(Rating = 125 MVA; V_{L} = 11 kV; X_{spu} = 0.15; P = 4; f = 50 Hz\)

\(I= \frac{125\times 10^{6}}{\sqrt{3} \times 11\times 10^{3}}= 6561 A\)

 

and \(V= \frac{V_{L}}{\sqrt{3}}= \frac{11\times 10^{3}}{\sqrt{3} } = 6351 V\)

\(X_{s pu }= \frac{X_{s} in ohm }{V / I}\)

 

or \(X_{s }= X_{s pu}\frac{V}{I} = 0.15 \times \frac{6351}{6561} = 0.14518 \Omega \)

\(\overline{E} = \overline{V} – \overline{I} \overline{Z}_{S} \\[0.5cm] \quad =V -\left(I\angle \phi \right)\left(X_{s}\angle 90^{\circ }\right) = V- IX_{s}\angle 90^{\circ } + \phi \\[0.5cm] \quad = V – IX_{s} \left[\cos \left(90 + \phi \right) + j \sin \left(90+ \phi \right) \right] \\[0.5cm] \quad =\left(V + I X_{s} \sin \phi \right) – j I X_{s}\cos \phi \\[0.5cm] \quad = 6350 + 6561 \times 0.14518 \times 0.6 – j 6561 \times 0.14518 \times 0.8 \\[0.5cm] \quad = 6921.5 – j762 = 6963.4 \angle 6.2828^{\circ } \)

 

\(E = 6963.4 V, \delta = 6.2826° \)

 

Synchronising power per mechanical degree

\(P_{syn} = \left(\frac{d}{d\delta} P \right) p \frac{\pi }{180} \) and \( P=\frac{3 V E}{X_{s}} \sin \delta \)

\(P_{syn} = \left\lgroup\frac{3 V E}{X_{s}} \cos \delta\right\rgroup p \frac{\pi }{180} \\[0.5cm] \qquad \, =\left(\frac{3 \times 6350 \times 6963.4}{0.14518} \cos 6.2826^{\circ } \right)\times \frac{6\pi }{180} \\[0.5cm] \qquad \, = 95109087 W – \mathbf{95.11 MW} \)
