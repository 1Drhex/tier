Guest Question.

Consider a three-level system with the unperturbed Hamiltonian

\( H ^{0}=\left(\begin{array}{ccc} \epsilon_{a} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \epsilon_{a} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & \epsilon_{c} \end{array}\right) \) (7.111).

\( \left(\epsilon_{a}>\epsilon_{c}\right) \) and the perturbation

\( H^{\prime}=\left(\begin{array}{ccc} 0 & 0 & V \\ 0 & 0 & V \\ V^{*} & V^{*} & 0 \end{array}\right) \) (7.112).

Since the ( 2 × 2 ) matrix is diagonal (and in fact identically in the basis of states (1,0,0) and (0,1,0), you might assume they are the good states, but they’re not. To see this:

(a) Obtain the exact eigenvalues for the perturbed Hamiltonian \( H = H ^{0}+ H ^{\prime} \).

(b) Expand your results from part (a) as a power series in |V| up to second order.

(c) What do you obtain by applying nondegenerate perturbation theory to find the energies of all three states (up to second order)? This would work if the assumption about the good states above were correct.

Moral: If any of the eigenvalues of W are equal, the states that diagonalize W are not unique, and diagonalizing W does not determine the “good” states.
When this happens (and it’s not uncommon), you need to use second-order degenerate perturbation theory (see ).

Solution

(a)

\( \left|\begin{array}{ccc} \epsilon_{a}-\lambda & 0 & V \\ 0 & \epsilon_{a}-\lambda & V \\ V^{*} & V^{*} & \epsilon_{c}-\lambda \end{array}\right|=\left(\epsilon_{a}-\lambda\right)^{2}\left(\epsilon_{c}-\lambda\right)-2 V V^{*}\left(\epsilon_{a}-\lambda\right)=0 \)

⇒ \( \lambda_{1}=\epsilon_{a}, \quad \lambda_{\pm}=\left(\frac{\epsilon_{a}+\epsilon_{c}}{2}\right) \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{c}}{2}\right)^{2}+2|V|^{2}} \).

(b)

\( \lambda_{\pm}=\left(\frac{\epsilon_{a}+\epsilon_{c}}{2}\right) \pm\left(\frac{\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{c}}{2}\right) \sqrt{1+\frac{8|V|^{2}}{\left(\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{c}\right)^{2}}} \approx\left(\frac{\epsilon_{a}+\epsilon_{c}}{2}\right) \pm\left(\frac{\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{c}}{2}\right)\left[1+\frac{4|V|^{2}}{\left(\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{c}\right)^{2}}\right] \).

\( =\left(\frac{\epsilon_{a}+\epsilon_{c}}{2}\right) \pm\left[\left(\frac{\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{c}}{2}\right)+\frac{2|V|^{2}}{\left(\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{c}\right)}\right] \).

\( \lambda_{1}=\epsilon_{a}, \quad \lambda_{+} \approx \epsilon_{a}+\frac{2|V|^{2}}{\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{c}}, \quad \lambda_{-} \approx \epsilon_{c}-\frac{2|V|^{2}}{\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{c}} \).

(c) Naively applying nondegenerate perturbation theory to the states (1,0,0), (0,1,0), and (0,0,1):

\( E_{1}=\epsilon_{a}+H_{11}^{\prime}+\sum_{n \neq 1} \frac{\left|H_{n 1}^{\prime}\right|^{2}}{\epsilon_{a}-E_{n}^{0}}+\cdots=\epsilon_{a}+\frac{\left|H_{21}^{\prime}\right|^{2}}{\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{a}}+\frac{\left|H_{31}^{\prime}\right|^{2}}{\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{c}}=\epsilon_{a}+\frac{0}{0}+\frac{|V|^{2}}{\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{c}} \).

\( E_{2}=\epsilon_{a}+H_{22}^{\prime}+\sum_{n \neq 2} \frac{\left|H_{n 2}^{\prime}\right|^{2}}{\epsilon_{a}-E_{n}^{0}}+\cdots=\epsilon_{a}+\frac{\left|H_{12}^{\prime}\right|^{2}}{\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{a}}+\frac{\left|H_{32}^{\prime}\right|^{2}}{\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{c}}=\epsilon_{a}+\frac{0}{0}+\frac{|V|^{2}}{\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{c}} \).

\( E_{3}=\epsilon_{c}+H_{33}^{\prime}+\sum_{n \neq 3} \frac{\left|H_{n 3}^{\prime}\right|^{2}}{\epsilon_{c}-E_{n}^{0}}+\cdots=\epsilon_{c}+\frac{\left|H_{13}^{\prime}\right|^{2}}{\epsilon_{c}-\epsilon_{a}}+\frac{\left|H_{23}^{\prime}\right|^{2}}{\epsilon_{c}-\epsilon_{a}}=\epsilon_{c}-2 \frac{|V|^{2}}{\epsilon_{a}-\epsilon_{c}} \).

The nondegenerate state comes out right \( \left(E_{3}=\lambda_{-}\right) \), but the other two are at best ambiguous (the 0/0 terms warning us, perhaps, that something is amiss).
