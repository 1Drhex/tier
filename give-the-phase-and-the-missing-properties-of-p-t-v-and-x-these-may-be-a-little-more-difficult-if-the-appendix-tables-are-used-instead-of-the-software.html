Guest Question.

Give the phase and the missing properties of P, T, v and x. These may be a little more difficult if the appendix tables are used instead of the software.

Admin
Solution

a) R-410A at T = 10°C, v = 0.02 \(m ^{3} / kg\): Table B.4.1 \(v > v _{ g }\) at 10°C

=> sup. vap. Table B.4.2 interpolate between sat. and sup. both at 10°C

\(P =680.7+(600-680.7) \frac{0.036-0.03471}{0.04018-0.03471}= 6 6 1 . 7 k P a\)

 

b) \(H _{2} O\) v = 0.2 \(m ^{3} / kg\), x = 0.5: Table B.1.1

sat. liq. + vap. \(v =(1- x ) v _{ f }+ x v _{ g } \Rightarrow v _{ f }+ v _{ g }=0.4 m ^{3} / kg\)

since \(v _{ f }\) is so small we find it approximately where \(v _{ g }=0.4 m ^{3} / kg\).

\(v _{ f }+ v _{ g }=0.39387 \text { at } 150^{\circ} C , \quad v _{ f }+ v _{ g }=0.4474 \text { at } 145^{\circ} C\)

An interpolation gives T ≅ 149.4°C, P ≅ 468.2 kPa

 

c) \(H _{2} O\) T = 60°C, v = 0.001016 \(m ^{3} / kg\): Table B.1.1 \(v < v _{ f }\) = 0.001017

=> compr. liq. see Table B.1.4

v = 0.001015 at 5 MPa so P ≅ 0.5(5000 + 19.9) = 2.51 MPa

 

d) \(NH _{3}\) T = 30°C, P = 60 kPa : Table B.2.1 \(P < P _{ sat }\)

=> sup. vapor interpolate in Table B.2.2 (50 kPa to 100 kPa)

\(v =2.9458+(1.4657-2.9458) \frac{60-50}{100-50}= 2 . 6 5 m ^{3} / kg\)

v is not linearly proportional to P (more like 1/P) so the computer table gives a more accurate value of 2.45 \(m ^{3} / kg\)

 

e) R-134a v = 0.005 \(m ^{3} / kg\) , x = 0.5: sat. liq. + vap. Table B.5.1

\(v =(1- x ) v _{ f }+ x v _{ g } \Rightarrow v _{ f }+ v _{ g }=0.01 m ^{3} / kg\) \(v _{ f }+ v _{ g }=0.010946 \text { at } 65^{\circ} C , \quad v _{ f }+ v _{ g }=0.009665 \text { at } 70^{\circ} C\)

An interpolation gives: T ≅ 68.7°C, P = 2.06 MPa

States shown are placed relative to the two-phase region, not to each other.
