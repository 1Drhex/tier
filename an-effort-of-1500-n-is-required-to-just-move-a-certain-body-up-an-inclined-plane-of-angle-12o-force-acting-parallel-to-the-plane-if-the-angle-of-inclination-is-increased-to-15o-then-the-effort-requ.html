Guest Question.

An effort of 1500 N is required to just move a certain body up an inclined plane of angle \(12^{\circ}\), force acting parallel to the plane. If the angle of inclination is increased to \(15^{\circ}\), then the effort required is 1720 N. Find the weight of the body and the coefficient of friction.

Admin
Solution

Given : \(P_{1}=1500 N ; \alpha_{1}=12^{\circ} ; \alpha_{2}=15^{\circ} ; P_{2}=1720 N\)

Let W = Weight of the body in newtons, and

\(\mu \) = Coefficient of friction.

First of all, let us consider a body lying on a plane inclined at an angle of \(12^{\circ}\) with the horizontal and subjected to an effort of 1500 N parallel to the plane as shown in Fig. 10.10 (a).

Let \(R_{ N _{1}}\) = Normal reaction, and

\(F_{1}\) = Force of friction.

We know that for the motion of the body up the inclined plane, the effort applied parallel to the plane \(\left(P_{1}\right)\),

\(1500=W\left(\sin \alpha_{1}+\mu \cos \alpha_{1}\right)=W\left(\sin 12^{\circ}+\mu \cos 12^{\circ}\right)\) …(i)

Now let us consider the body lying on a plane inclined at an angle of \(15^{\circ}\) with the horizontal and subjected to an effort of 1720 N parallel to the plane as shown in Fig. (b).

Let \(R_{ N _{2}}\) = Normal reaction, and

\(F_{2}\) = Force of friction.

We know that for the motion of the body up the inclined plane, the effort applied parallel to the plane \(\left(P_{2}\right)\),

\(1720=W\left(\sin \alpha_{2}+\mu \cos \alpha_{2}\right)=W\left(\sin 15^{\circ}+\mu \cos 15^{\circ}\right)\) …(ii)

 

Coefficient of friction

Dividing equation (ii) by equation (i),

\(\frac{1720}{1500}=\frac{W\left(\sin 15^{\circ}+\mu \cos 15^{\circ}\right)}{W\left(\sin 12^{\circ}+\mu \cos 12^{\circ}\right)}\)

 

\(1720 \sin 12^{\circ}+1720 \mu \cos 12^{\circ}=1500 \sin 15^{\circ}+1500 \mu \cos 15^{\circ}\)

 

\(\mu\left(1720 \cos 12^{\circ}-1500 \cos 15^{\circ}\right)=1500 \sin 15^{\circ}-1720 \sin 12^{\circ}\)

 

\(\therefore\) \(\mu=\frac{1500 \sin 15^{\circ}-1720 \sin 12^{\circ}}{1720 \cos 12^{\circ}-1500 \cos 15^{\circ}}=\frac{1500 \times 0.2588-1720 \times 0.2079}{1720 \times 0.9781-1500 \times 0.9659}\)

 

\(=\frac{388.2-357.6}{1682.3-1448.5}=\frac{30.6}{233.8}=0.131\)

 

Weight of the body

Substituting the value of µ in equation (i),

\(1500=W\left(\sin 12^{\circ}+0.131 \cos 12^{\circ}\right)\)

 

= W (0.2079 + 0.131 × 0.9781) = 0.336 W

\(\therefore\) W = 1500/0.336 = 4464 N
