Guest Question.

A series motor 120 Volt, \(2.2 \mathrm{KW}, 850 \mathrm{rpm}\), has \(\mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{a}}=0.5 \Omega\) and \(\mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{F}}=0.3 \Omega\). The field is linearly proportional to armature current. Find the required additional armature resistance to drive a load of \(20 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m}\) at speed of \(250 \mathrm{rpm}\).

Admin
Solution

Let \(k \phi=A \cdot I_{a}\),

where \(A=\) constant.

\( \begin{array}{c} \omega_{r}=\frac{2 \pi \cdot n_{r}}{60}=\frac{2 \pi \times 850}{60}=89.011 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{sec} \\ T_{m r}=\frac{P_{r}}{\omega_{r}}=\frac{2.2 \times 10^{3}}{89.011}=24.716 \mathrm{~N} . \mathrm{M} \\ T=K \Phi \cdot I_{a}=A \cdot I_{a}^{2} \\ \omega_{r}=\frac{V}{K \Phi}-\frac{R_{a}+R_{F}}{(K \Phi)^{2}} \cdot T_{r} \\ 89.011=\frac{120}{K \Phi}-\frac{0.5+0.3}{(K \Phi)^{2}} \cdot 24.716 . \end{array} \)

Solving:

\( \begin{array}{c} 89.011(K \Phi)^{2}-120(K \Phi)+19.7728=0 . \\ \text { Either } K \Phi=1.156 \\ \text { or }=0.192 . \\ \omega_{0}=\frac{V}{K \Phi} \\ \omega_{0}=\frac{120}{1.156}=103.8 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{sec} \\ \end{array} \)

or

\( \begin{array}{c} \omega_{o}=\frac{120}{0.192}=625 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{sec} . \\ \end{array} \)

By neglecting the high value of \(\omega_{\mathrm{o}}, \)

so

\( \begin{array}{c} \therefore k \phi=1.156=A \cdot I_{a} \\ T_{r}=A \cdot I_{a}{ }^{2}=24.716 \\ \therefore \frac{T_{r}}{K \Phi}=I_{a}=\frac{24.716}{1.156}=21.38 A \\ 1.156=\mathrm{A} \cdot 21.38, \\ \text { so } A=0.054 \end{array} \)

To find \(R_{d}\) required:

\( \begin{array}{c} 10=200.34-\frac{1+R_{d}}{(0.7487)^{2}} \cdot 15, \\ R_{d}=6.11 \Omega . \\ \text { For } R_{d}=0 \Omega \\ T_{L}=A \cdot I_{a}{ }^{2} \\ 20=0.054 \mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{a}}{ }^{2} \\ I_{a}=19.24 \mathrm{~A} . \\ \text { New } k \phi=A \cdot I_{a}=0.054 \times 19.24=1.039 \\ \omega_{L}=\frac{2 \pi n_{L}}{60} \\ \omega_{L}=\frac{2 \pi \times 250}{60}=26.18 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{sec} \\ \omega_{L}=\frac{V}{K \Phi}-\frac{R_{a}+R_{F}+R_{d}}{(K \Phi)^{2}} \cdot T_{L} \\ 26.18=\frac{120}{1.039}-\frac{0.5+0.3+R_{d}}{(1.039)^{2}} \cdot 20, \\ R_{d}=4.021 \Omega . \end{array} \)
