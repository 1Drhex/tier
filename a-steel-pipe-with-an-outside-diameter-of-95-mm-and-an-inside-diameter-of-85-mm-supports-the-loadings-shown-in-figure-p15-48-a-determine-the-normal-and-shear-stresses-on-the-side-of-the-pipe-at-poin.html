Guest Question.

A steel pipe with an outside diameter of 95 mm and an inside diameter of 85 mm supports the loadings shown in Figure P15.48.
(a) Determine the normal and shear stresses on the side of the pipe at point K.
(b) Determine the principal stresses and maximum inplane shear stress magnitude at point K and show the orientation of these stresses on an appropriate sketch.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left[(95 mm )^{2}-(85 mm )^{2}\right]=1,413.717 mm ^{2} \\&J=\frac{\pi}{32}\left[(95 mm )^{4}-(85 mm )^{4}\right]=2,871,612.035 mm ^{4} \\&I_{x}=I_{y}=\frac{\pi}{64}\left[(95 mm )^{4}-(85 mm )^{4}\right]=1,435,806.017 mm ^{4} \\&Q=\frac{1}{12}\left[(95 mm )^{3}-(85 mm )^{3}\right]=20,270.833 mm ^{3}\end{aligned}\)

 

Equivalent forces at H and K:

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{x}=-14 kN =-14,000 N \\&F_{y}=-10 kN =-10,000 N \\&F_{z}=-7 kN =-7,000 N\end{aligned}\)

 

 

 

Equivalent moments at H and K:

\(\begin{aligned}M_{x} &=(10 kN )(450 mm )-(7 kN )(240 mm ) \\&=2,820 kN – mm =2.820 \times 10^{6} N – mm \\M_{y} &=-(14 kN )(450 mm ) \\&=-6,300 kN – mm =-6.300 \times 10^{6} N – mm \\M_{z} &=(14 kN )(240 mm ) \\&=3,360 kN – mm =3.36 \times 10^{6} N – mm\end{aligned}\)

Each of the non-zero forces and moments will be evaluated to determine whether stresses are created at the point of interest.

 

(a) Consider point K.
Force \(F _{x}\) does not cause either a normal stress or a shear stress at K.

Force \(F _{y}\) creates a transverse shear stress in the yz plane at K. The magnitude of this shear stress is:

\(\tau_{y z}=\frac{(10,000 N )\left(20,270.833 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(1,435,806.017 mm ^{4}\right)[(95 mm )-(85 mm )]}=14.118 MPa\)

Force \(F _{z}\) creates an axial stress at K. The magnitude of this normal stress is:

\(\sigma_{z}=\frac{7,000 N }{1,413.717 mm ^{2}}=4.951 MPa\)

Moment \(M _{x}\) does not create bending stress at K because K is located on the neutral axis for bending about the x axis.

Moment \(M _{y}\) creates bending stress at K. The magnitude of this stress is:

\(\sigma_{z}=\frac{M_{y} x}{I_{y}}=\frac{\left(6.300 \times 10^{6} N – mm \right)(95 mm / 2)}{1,435,806.017 mm ^{4}}=208.420 MPa\)

Moment \(M _{z}\), which is a torque, creates a torsion shear stress in the yz plane at K. The magnitude of this shear stress is:

\(\tau_{y z}=\frac{M_{z} c}{J}=\frac{\left(3.360 \times 10^{6} N – mm \right)(95 mm / 2)}{2,871,612.035 mm ^{4}}=55.579 MPa\)

Summary of stresses at K:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{y} &=0 MPa \\\sigma_{z} &=-4.951 MPa +208.420 MPa \\&=203.468 MPa =203 MPa ( T ) \\\tau_{y z} &=-14.118 MPa +55.579 MPa \\&=41.460 MPa =41.5 MPa\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Principal stress calculations:
The principal stress magnitudes can be computed from Eq. (12.12). For use in this equation, the negative z axis will be taken as the x axis, which causes the shear stress value to change sign.

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1, p 2} &=\frac{(203.468)+(0)}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{(203.468)-(0)}{2}\right)^{2}+(-41.460)^{2}} \\&=101.734 \pm 109.858\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{p 1} &=212 MPa \text { and } \quad \sigma_{p 2}=-8.12 MPa \\\tau_{\max } &=109.9 MPa \text { (maximum in-plane shear stress) } \\\sigma_{\text {avg }} &=101.7 MPa ( T ) \text { (normal stress on planes of maximum in-plane shear stress) }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan 2 \theta_{p} &=\frac{-41.460}{[(203.468)-(0)] / 2}=\frac{-41.460}{101.734}=-0.407538 \\&\left.\therefore \theta_{p}=-11.09^{\circ} \quad \text { (clockwise from the } z \text { axis to the direction of } \sigma_{p 1}\right)\end{aligned}\)
