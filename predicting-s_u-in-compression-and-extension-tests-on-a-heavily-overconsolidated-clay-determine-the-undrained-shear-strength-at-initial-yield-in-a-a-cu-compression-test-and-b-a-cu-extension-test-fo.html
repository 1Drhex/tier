Guest Question.

Predicting \(s_u\) in Compression and Extension Tests on a Heavily Overconsolidated Clay
Determine the undrained shear strength at initial yield in (a) a CU compression test and (b) a CU extension test for a clay soil with \(R_o=5,\: {p}'_o=70\, kPa,\: and\: {\phi }'_{cs}=25^{\circ}\).

Strategy: Since you are given \(\: {\phi }'_{cs}\), you should use Equations 11.6 and 11.8 to fi nd \(M_c\) and \(M_e\). Use Equation (11.44) to solve the problem.
Equation (11.44) \((s_{u})_{y}=\frac{M}{2}{p}'_o\sqrt{R_{o}-1};\: R_{o}> 1\)

Admin
Solution

Step 1: Calculate \(M_c\) and \(M_e\).

\(M_c=\frac{6\, sin\, {\phi }'_{cs}}{3-sin\, {\phi }'_{cs}}=\frac{6\, sin\, 25^{\circ}}{3-sin\, 25^{\circ}}=0.98\)

 

\(M_e=\frac{6\, sin\, {\phi }'_{cs}}{3+sin\, {\phi }'_{cs}}=0.74\)

 

Step 2: Calculate \(s_u\).

Use Equation (11.44).
Compression: \((s_u)_y=\frac{0.98}{2}*70\sqrt{5-1}=68.6\, kPa\)

Extension: \((s_u)_y=\frac{0.74}{2}*70\sqrt{5-1}=51.8\, kPa\)

 

Or, by proportion,
Extension: \((s_u)_y=\frac{0.74}{0.98}*68.6=51.8\, kPa\)
