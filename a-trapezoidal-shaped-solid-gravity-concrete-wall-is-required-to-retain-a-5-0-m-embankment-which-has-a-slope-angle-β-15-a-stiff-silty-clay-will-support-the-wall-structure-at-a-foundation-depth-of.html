Guest Question.

A trapezoidal-shaped solid gravity concrete wall is required to retain a 5.0-m embankment which has a slope angle \( \beta = 15^{\circ} \). A stiff silty clay will support the wall structure at a foundation depth of about 1.0 m below the lowest ground level. Safety factors against bearing capacity failure, overturning and sliding should not be less than 3.0, 2.0 and 1.5, respectively. The following tentative dimensions for a gravity retaining wall may be used as a guide:
Base width \( B : 0.5 to 0.7 H \)
where H = total height of wall structure
Base thickness \( h : \frac{H}{8} to \frac{H}{6} \)
Toe projection \( m : 0.5 h to h \)
Stem thickness \( b_{1} \)(at top): \( 0.30 m to \frac{H}{12} \)
Minimum front face batter: 1 : 48
Design the retaining wall using the following soil and concrete data:

 

Base soil Backfill Concrete
\( γ = 18 kN/m^{3} \) \( γ = 17 kN/m^{3} \) \( γ = 23.5 kN/m^{3} \)
\( c_{u} = \frac{q_{u}}{2} = 105 kPa\) \( c_{u} = 0\) \( {f}'_{c} = 17 MPa \)
\( \varnothing = 0 \) \( \varnothing = 34^{\circ}\) —

Solution

Step 1. Select initial values for the base and stem cross-section dimensions.

Assume the following tentative dimensions:
Total height \( H = 6.00 m \)
Base thickness \( h = 0.75 m \)
Stem height \( Z = H − h = 6.00 − 0.75 = 5.25 m \)
Base width \( B = 3.50 m \)
Toe projection \( m = 0.60 m \)
Heel projection \( n = 0.60 m \)
Stem thickness at top \( b_{1} = 0.40 m \)
Stem thickness at bottom \( b_{2} = 2.30 m \)
Front face batter \( = 1 : 10.5 \)
Angle \( \alpha = \left ( 75.1 \right )^{\circ} \)
Angle \( \beta = 15^{\circ} \)
Foundation depth \( D = 1.00 m \)
Height of the vertical shear plane at heel is

\( {H}' = H + 2 \tan \beta = H + 2 \tan 15^{\circ} \)

 

\( = 6 + 2 \times 0.268 = 6.54 m \)

 

Step 2. Determine the total active earth pressure thrust \( P_{a} \) and its components \( P_{ah} \) and \( P_{av} \) using Rankine’s lateral earth pressure theory.

\( P_{a} = \frac{y {H}'^{2}}{2} K_{a} \)

 

Where \(K_{a} = \) Rankine’s coefficient of lateral active earth pressure.
The active thrust \(P_{a} \) acts on the vertical shear plane at a height of \( \frac{1}{3} {H}' \) and at \( \beta \) above the horizontal as shown in the scheme above.

For cohesionless soils, the coefficient \( K_{a} \) depends on the backfill slope angle \( \beta \) and friction angle \( \varnothing \), can be obtained from tables or may be calculated from the equation

\( K_{a} = \cos \beta \frac{ \cos \beta – \sqrt{\cos^{2} \beta – \cos^{2} \varnothing }}{\cos \beta + \sqrt{\cos^{2} \beta – \cos^{2} \varnothing }} \)

 

For \( \beta = 15^{\circ} \) and \( \varnothing = 34^{\circ} \) :

 

\( K_{a} = 0.966 \times \frac{ 0.966 – \sqrt{0.966^{2} – 0.829^{2} }}{ 0.966 + \sqrt{0.966^{2} – 0.829^{2}}} = 0.966 \times \frac{0.966 – 0.496}{0.966 + 0.496} = \frac{0.454}{1.462} = 0.311 \)

 

\( P_{a} = \frac{18 \times 6.54^{2}}{2} \times 0.311 = 119.72 kN/m \)

 

\( P_{ah} = P_{a} \cos 15^{\circ} = 119.72 \times 0.966 = 115.65 kN/m\)

 

\( P_{av} = P_{a} \sin 15^{\circ} = 119.72 \times 0.259 = 31.01 kN/m\)

For these computations refer to the scheme in Step 2. It is convenient to tabulate the load sources, loads, moment arms and moments, as in Table 5.13. Assume, conservatively, we neglect the toe soil and that covering the base in front of the wall stem.

Step 4. Check wall stability for overturning and sliding.
(a) Overturning stability.
Overturning moment:

\( M_{o} = P_{ah} \times 2.18 = 115.65 \times 2.18 \)

 

\( = 252.12 kN.m/m \)

 

Resisting moment:

\( M_{r} = M_{net} – M_{o} = 597.33 – \left ( – 252.12 \right ) \)

 

\( = 849.45 kN.m/m\)

 

Safety factor against overturning is

\( SF = \frac{M_{r}}{M_{o}} = \frac{849.45}{252.12} = 3.4 > 2 \) ( OK.)

 

(b) Sliding stability.
Resisting force against sliding (neglecting any passive resistance force) is

\( F_{r} = R \tan \delta + c_{a} A_{f} \)

 

In this case, \( \delta \) is zero since \( \varnothing = 0 \), and therefore \( F_{r} = c_{a} A_{f} \).
Assume \( c_{a} = 0.6 c_{u} = 0.6 \times 105 = 63 kPa \)

 

\( A_{f} = base effective area = {B}' \times 1 = \left ( B – 2 e \right ) \left ( 1 \right ) \)

 

\( = \left ( 3.5 − 2 \times 0.23 \right )\left ( 1 \right ) = 3.04 m^{2}/m \)

 

\( F_{r} = 63 \times 3.04 = 191.52 kN/m \)

 

Sliding force \( = P_{ah} = 115.65 kN/m \)

Safety factor against sliding is

\( SF = \frac{F_{r}}{P_{ah}} = \frac{191.52}{115.65} = 1.66 > 1.50 \) ( OK.)

 

Step 5. Check the foundation stability against bearing capacity failure.
Compute safe bearing capacity of the supporting soil using Hansen bearing capacity equation for the \( \varnothing = 0^{\circ} \) condition.

Table 4.2: \( gross q_{ult} = 5.14 s_{u} \left ( 1 + {s}'_{c} + {d}'_{c} – {i}'_{c} – {b}'_{c} – {g}'_{c} \right ) + \overline{q} \)

\( s_{u} = c_{u} = 105 kPa \)

 

Table 4.6: \( {s}'_{c} = 0.2 \frac{{B}'}{{L}'} = 0 \), since \(\frac{{B}'}{{L}'} \approx 0 \) for continuous foundations.

Table 4.7: \( {b}'_{c} = 0 \) since \( \eta^{\circ} = 0; {g}'_{c} = \frac{\beta^{\circ}}{147^{\circ}} = \frac{15}{147} = 0.102 \)

 

\( {d}'_{c} = 0.4 k; k = \frac{D}{B} for \frac{D}{B} \leq 1; \frac{D}{B} = \frac{1.0}{3.5} = 0.286; \) hence, \( {d}'_{c} = 0.114 \)

 

\( {i}'_{c} = 0.5 – 0.5 \sqrt{ 1 – \frac{H}{A_{f} c_{a}}} \).

 

Use \( c_{a} = 63 kPa; A_{f} = 3.04 m^{2}, \) as in Step 4(b).

 

\( {i}'_{c} = 0.5 – 0.5 \sqrt{ 1 – \frac{115.65}{3.04 \times 63}} = 0.184 \)

 

\( gross q_{ult} = 5.14 s_{u} \left ( 1 + {d}'_{c} – {i}'_{c} – {g}'_{c} \right ) + \overline{q} \)

 

Since the toe soil is neglected, the overburden pressure \( \overline{q} = 0 \).Hence, the safe bearing capacity is

\( gross q_{safe} = 5.14 s_{u} \left ( 1 + {d}'_{c} – {i}'_{c} – {g}'_{c} \right ) / SF \)

 

\( = 5.14 \left ( 105 \right ) \left ( 1 + 0.114 – 0.184 – 0.102 \right ) / 3 \)

 

\( = 464.87/3 = 155 kPa \)

 

Gross foundation pressure is

\( gross q = \frac{R}{{B}' \times 1} = \frac{391.75}{3.04 \times 1} = 128.87 kPa < gross q_{safe}\) ( OK.)

 

Check the maximum gross foundation pressure, \( gross q_{max}\):

\( gross q_{max} = \frac{R}{BL} \left ( 1 + \frac{6 e_{x}}{B} \right ) \)

 

\( = \frac{391.75}{3.5 \times 1} \left ( 1 + \frac{6 \times 0.23}{3.5} \right ) = 156 kPa \cong gross q_{safe} \) ( OK.)
