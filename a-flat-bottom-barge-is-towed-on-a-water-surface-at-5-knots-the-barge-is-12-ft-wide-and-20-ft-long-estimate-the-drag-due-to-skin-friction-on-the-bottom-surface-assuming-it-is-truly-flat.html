Guest Question.

A flat-bottom barge is towed on a water surface at 5 knots. The barge is 12 ft wide and 20 ft long. Estimate the drag due to skin friction on the bottom surface, assuming it is truly flat.

Admin
Solution

We can use Figure 7.11 to find the drag coefficient.

Assumptions

1. The system is at steady state.
2. The water temperature is \(68^{\circ }F \)

From Appendix Table C.11 for water at \(68^{\circ }F \) we read

 

\(\rho =1.000\left(62.4\right) lbm/ft^{3}\)

 

\(\nu =1.083\times 10^{-5}ft^{2}/s\)

 

Using conversion factors from Appendix Table A.1, the barge velocity is

\(V_{\infty } =5 knots\times \frac{5.144\times 10^{-1} }{3.048\times 10^{-1} } =8.44ft/s\)

 

The Reynolds number at plate end is

\(Re_{L}=\frac{V_{\infty }L }{\nu } =\frac{8.44\left(20\right) }{1.083\times 10^{-5} } =1.56\times 10^{7} \)

 

From Figure 7.13 we get

\(C_{D}=\frac{2D_{f}g_{c} }{\rho V^{2}_{\infty } bL}=0.003 \)

 

The drag force is

\(D_{f}=\frac{0.003\left(62.4\right)\left(8.44\right) ^{2}\left(12\right)\left(20\right) }{2\left(32.2\right) } \)

 

or

\(\underline{D_{f}=50 lbf}\)
