Guest Question.

Air flows with 30 m/s velocity through a tube of 2 cm dia. and 1 m length. The air inlet temperature is 20°C and its pressure is 101.3 kPa. The pressure loss in the tube is 80 mm of water column. How much heat is transferred from the wall to the air, when the wall is kept at 95°C. Use Chilton Colburn analogy.

Admin
Solution

Assumptions :
(i) Steady state conditions.
(ii) Fully developed flow.
(iii) Density of water as 1000 \(kg/m^3.\)
Analysis : The pressure drop in \(N/m^2 (Pa)\)
\(∆p = (ρgh )_{H_2O}\)
\(= 1000 × 9.81 ×\frac{80}{1000} = 784.8 Pa\)
The exit temperature is unknown, the mean fluid temperature cannot be obtained, and thus appropriate physical properties of air.
Assuming exit temperature of air to be 66°C
\(T_m = \frac{66+ 20}{2} = 43°C = 316 K\)

The physical properties of air (from Table A-7)
\(ρ = 1.112 kg/m^3, C_p = 1007 J/kg.K,\)
\(µ = 191.68 × 10^{–7} Ns/m^2\)
\(k_f= 27.41 × 10^{–3} W/m.K, Pr = 0.704\)
The friction factor is obtained by eqn. (9.21)
\(∆p = f \frac{L}{D}\frac{ρu_m^2}{2}\)

or \(f = \frac{2 ∆pD }{ρu_m^2L}=\frac{ 2 × 784.8 × 0.02}{1.112 × (30)^2×1}= 0.0313\)
The Reynolds number
\(Re_D =\frac{ρu_m D}{µ} – \frac{1.112 × 30 ×1}{191.68 ×10^{-7}}= 34808\)
which is greater than 2300, thus the flow is turbulent,using Chilton Colburn analogy
\(\frac{h}{ρC_p u_m} Pr^{2/3} = \frac{f}{8}\left \{where st_D=\frac{h}{ρC_p u_∞} \right \}\)
\(\frac{h}{1.112 × 1007 × 30}×(0.704)^{2/3}=\frac{0.0313}{8}\)
It gives \(h = 166 W/m^2.K.\)
Now calculating exit temperature of air by using eqn. (9.46) for constant wall temperature
\(T_o =Ts – (T_s – T_i) exp \left ( -\frac{hPL}{\dot{m}C_{p}} \right )\)

where \(\dot{m} = ρu_m\frac{\pi }{4}D^2\)

\(= 1.112 × 30 ×\frac{\pi }{4} × (0.02)^2 = 0.010 kg/s\)
Then \(T_o = 95 – (95 – 20) exp \left ( -\frac{166 ×(\pi × 0.02)×1}{0.010 ×1007} \right )\)

= 68.38°C
which is very close to assumed value of 66°C. Thus keeping it 68.38°C as exit temperature of air for any further calculations.
Heat transfer rate by pipe = Heat gain by air
\(Q = \dot{m} C_p(T_o – T_i)\)
= 0.010 × 1007 × (68.38 – 20)
= 487 W.
