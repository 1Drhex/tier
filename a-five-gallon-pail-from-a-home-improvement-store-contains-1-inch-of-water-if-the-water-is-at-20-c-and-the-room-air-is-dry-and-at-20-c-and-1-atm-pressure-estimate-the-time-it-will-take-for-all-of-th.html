Guest Question.

A five-gallon pail from a home improvement store contains 1 inch of water. If the water is at 20 C and the room air is dry and at 20 C and 1 atm pressure, estimate the time it will take for all of the water in the pail to evaporate .

Admin
Solution

From the website of a home improvement store, a 5-gallon pail has an inside diameter of 12 inches and a height of 14.5 inches

Diameter of pail \(=D=12 in\times \left(0.0254m/inch\right) =0.3048m\)

Cross – sectional area of pail = A = \(\frac{\pi }{4} D^{2} =\frac{\pi }{4} \left(0.3048\right) ^{2} =0.0730 m^{2}\)

Volume of water in pail = (A)(water height) = \(\left( 0.0730 m^{2} \right) \left(1 inch\right) \left(\frac{0.0254}{inch} \right) = 0.001853 m^{3}\) = 1.85 kg

Evaporation rate from Eq. (11.29):

\(\overset{.}{m_{A} } =D_{AB} A\left(\frac{M_{A} }{\overline{R} T} \right) \left(\frac{P}{L} \right) \ln \left[\frac{\left(P- P_{A} \right)_{x=L} }{\left(P- P_{A} \right) _{x=0} } \right]\) (11.29)

 

\(\overset{.}{m_{A} } =D_{AB} A\left(\frac{M_{A} }{\overline{R} T} \right) \left(\frac{P}{L} \right) \ln \left[\frac{\left(P- P_{A} \right)_{x=L} }{\left(P- P_{A} \right) _{x=0} } \right]\) (11.30)

 

Species A is the water vapor. Species B is the air.

At x=0 (water surface ) , \(P_{A}=P_{sat}\) at 20 C =2.339 kPa (from steam tables)

At x=L (pail top ) , \(P_{A}= 0\) kPa (dry air)

L = average height of air above water surface = \(14 inch \times \left(0.0254 m/inch\right) =0.3556 m .\)

P =1 atm = 101.325 kPa .\(M_{A}\) = molecular weight of water = 18 kg/kmol .

From Table 11.3

, \(D_{AB}\) for a water-air mixture at 300 K and 1 atm is \(0.26\times 10^{-4} m^{2} /s\) . Using Eq. (11.15)

\(\left(D_{AB} \right) _{2} =\left(D_{AB} \right) _{1} \left(\frac{P_{1} }{P_{2} } \right) \left(\frac{T_{2} }{T_{1} } \right) ^{1.75}\) (11.15)

, \(D_{AB}\) at 293.15 K and 1 atm is \(0.26\times 10^{-4} \left(\frac{293.15}{300} \right) ^{1.75} =0.25\times 10^{-4} m^{2} /s\)

 

From Eq. (11.30), the mass flow of water vapor out of the pail is

\(\overset{.}{m_{A} } =D_{AB} A\left(\frac{M_{A} }{\overline{R} T} \right) \left(\frac{P}{L} \right) \ln \left[\frac{\left(P- P_{A} \right)_{x=L} }{\left(P- P_{A} \right) _{x=0} } \right]\) \(=\left(0.25\times 10^{-4} \right) \left(0.0730\right) \left(\frac{18}{\left(8.31446\right)\left(293.15\right) } \right) \left(\frac{101.325}{0.3556} \right) \ln \left(\frac{101.325-0}{101.325-2.339} \right)\) \(8.97\times 10^{-8} kg/s\)

The time to evaporate the 1.85 kg of water in the pail is therefore

\(\Delta t= \frac{1.85kg}{8.97\times 10^{-8} kg/s}=\left(2.06\times 10^{7} s\right) \left(\frac{1 h}{3600s} \right)\left( \frac{1 day }{24 h} \right) = 238 days\)

 

It will take about 8 months to evaporate the 1 inch of water in the pail.
