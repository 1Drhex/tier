Guest Question.

Spherical particle 1 has a velocity \(v_{1}=6\) m /s in the direction shown and collides with spherical particle 2 of equal mass and diameter and initially at rest. If the coefficient of restitution for these conditions is e = 0.6, determine the resulting motion of each particle following impact. Also calculate the percentage loss of energy due to the impact.

Admin
Solution

1 The geometry at impact indicates that the normal n to the contacting surfaces makes an angle \(\theta=30^{\circ}\) with the direction of \(v _{1}\), as indicated in the figure. Thus, the initial velocity components are \(\left(v_{1}\right)_{n}=v_{1} \cos 30^{\circ}=\) \(6 \cos 30^{\circ}=5.20 m / s ,\left(v_{1}\right)_{t}=v_{1} \sin 30^{\circ}=6 \sin 30^{\circ}=3 m / s \text {, and }\left(v_{2}\right)_{n}=\left(v_{2}\right)_{t}=0\).

Momentum conservation for the two-particle system in the n-direction gives

 

\(m_{1}\left(v_{1}\right)_{n}+m_{2}\left(v_{2}\right)_{n}=m_{1}\left(v_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{n}+m_{2}\left(v_{2}^{\prime}\right)_{n}\)

 

or, with \(m_{1}=m_{2}\),

 

\(5.20+0=\left(v_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{n}+\left(v_{2}^{\prime}\right)_{n}\) (a)

 

The coefficient-of-restitution relationship is

 

\(e=\frac{\left(v_{2}^{\prime}\right)_{n}-\left(v_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{n}}{\left(v_{1}\right)_{n}-\left(v_{2}\right)_{n}} \quad 0.6=\frac{\left(v_{2}^{\prime}\right)_{n}-\left(v_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{n}}{5.20-0}\) (b)

 

2 Simultaneous solution of Eqs. a and b yields

 

\(\left(v_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{n}=1.039 m / s \quad\left(v_{2}^{\prime}\right)_{n}=4.16 m / s\)

 

Conservation of momentum for each particle holds in the t-direction because, with assumed smooth surfaces, there is no force in the t-direction. Thus for particles 1 and 2, we have

 

\(m_{1}\left(v_{1}\right)_{t}=m_{1}\left(v_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{t} \quad\left(v_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{t}=\left(v_{1}\right)_{t}=3 m / s\)

 

3 \(m_{2}\left(v_{2}\right)_{t}=m_{2}\left(v_{2}^{\prime}\right)_{t} \quad\left(v_{2}^{\prime}\right)_{t}=\left(v_{2}\right)_{t}=0\)

 

The final speeds of the particles are

 

\(v_{1}^{\prime}=\sqrt{\left(v_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{n}^{2}+\left(v_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{t}^{2}}=\sqrt{(1.039)^{2}+3^{2}}=3.17 m / s\) Ans.

 

\(v_{2}^{\prime}=\sqrt{\left(v_{2}^{\prime}\right)_{n}^{2}+\left(v_{2}^{\prime}\right)_{t}^{2}}=\sqrt{(4.16)^{2}+0^{2}}=4.16 m / s\) Ans.

 

The angle \(\theta^{\prime} \text { which } v _{1}^{\prime}\) makes with the t-direction is

 

\(\theta^{\prime}=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\left(v_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{n}}{\left(v_{1}^{\prime}\right)_{t}}\right)=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{1.039}{3}\right)=19.11^{\circ}\)

 

The kinetic energies just before and just after impact, with \(m=m_{1}=m_{2}\), are

 

\(T=\frac{1}{2} m_{1} v_{1}^{2}+\frac{1}{2} m_{2} v_{2}^{2}=\frac{1}{2} m(6)^{2}+0=18 m\)

 

\(T^{\prime}=\frac{1}{2} m_{1} v_{1}^{\prime 2}+\frac{1}{2} m_{2} v_{2}^{\prime 2}=\frac{1}{2} m(3.17)^{2}+\frac{1}{2} m(4.16)^{2}=13.68 m\)

 

The percentage energy loss is then

 

\(\frac{|\Delta E|}{E}(100)=\frac{T-T^{\prime}}{T}(100)=\frac{18 m-13.68 m}{18 m}(100)=24.0 \%\) Ans.

 

Helpful Hints

1 Be sure to set up n- and t-coordinates which are, respectively, normal to and tangent to the contacting surfaces. Calculation of the 30° angle is critical to all that follows.

2 Note that, even though there are four equations in four unknowns for the standard problem of oblique central impact, only one pair of the equations is coupled.

3 We note that particle 2 has no initial or final velocity component in the t-direction. Hence, its final velocity \(v _{2}^{\prime}\) is restricted to the n-direction.
