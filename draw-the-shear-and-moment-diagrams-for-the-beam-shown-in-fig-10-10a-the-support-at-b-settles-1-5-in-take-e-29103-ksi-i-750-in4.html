Guest Question.

Draw the shear and moment diagrams for the beam shown in Fig. \(10-10 (\mathrm{a})\). The support at \(B\) settles \(1.5\) in. Take \(E=29\left(10^{3}\right)\) ksi, \(I=750\) in \(^{4}\).

Admin
Solution

Principle of Superposition. By inspection, the beam is indeterminate to the first degree. The center support \(B\) will be chosen as the redundant, so that the roller at \(B\) is removed, Fig. 10-10b. Here \(\mathbf{B}_{y}\) is assumed to act downward on the beam.

Compatibility Equation. With reference to point \(B\) in Fig. 10-10b, using units of ft, we require

\((+\downarrow) \quad \frac{1.5}{12}=\Delta_{B}+B_{y} f_{B B}\) (1)

Here we will use the conjugate-beam method to compute \(\Delta_{B}\) and \(f_{B B}\) since the moment diagrams consist of straight line segments. For \(\Delta_{B}\), verify the calculations shown in Fig. 10-10c. Note that

\( \begin{array}{c} \curvearrowleft+\Sigma M_{B^{\prime}}=0 ; \quad &-M_{B^{\prime}}+\frac{1440}{E I}(8)-\frac{1800}{E I}(24)=0 \\ &M_{B^{\prime}}=-\frac{31680}{E I}=\frac{31680}{E I} \curvearrowleft \end{array} \)

Verify the calculations in Fig. 10-10d for calculating \(f_{B B}\). Note that

\( \begin{array}{c} \curvearrowleft+\Sigma M_{B^{\prime}}=0 ; \quad-m_{B^{\prime}}+\frac{144}{E I}(8)-\frac{144}{E I}(24)=0 \\ m_{B^{\prime}}=-\frac{2304}{E I}=\frac{2304}{E I} \curvearrowleft \end{array} \)

Substituting these results into Eq. (1), we have

\( \frac{1.5}{12}=\frac{31680}{E I}+B_{y}\left(\frac{2304}{E I}\right) \)

Expressing the units of \(E\) and \(I\) in terms of \(\mathrm{k}\) and \(\mathrm{ft}\), we have

\( \begin{array}{c} \left(\frac{1.5}{12} \mathrm{ft}\right)\left[29\left(10^{3}\right) \mathrm{k} / \mathrm{in}^{2}\left((12)^{2} \mathrm{in}^{2} / \mathrm{ft}^{2}\right)\right]\left[750 \mathrm{in}^{4}\left(\mathrm{ft}^{4} /(12)^{4} \mathrm{in}^{4}\right)\right] \\ =31680+B_{y}(2304) \\ B_{y}=-5.56 \mathrm{k} \end{array} \)

Equilibrium Equations. The negative sign indicates that \(\mathbf{B}_{y}\) acts upward on the beam. From the free-body diagram shown in Fig. 10-10e we have

\( \begin{array}{c} \curvearrowleft+\sum M_{A}=0 ; \quad-20(12)+5.56(24)+C_{y}(48)=0 \\ C_{y}=2.22 \mathrm{k} \\ +\uparrow \Sigma F_{y}=0 ; \quad A_{y}-20+5.56+2.22=0 \\ A_{y}=12.22 \mathrm{k} \end{array} \)

Using these results, verify the shear and moment diagrams shown in Fig. 10-10f.
