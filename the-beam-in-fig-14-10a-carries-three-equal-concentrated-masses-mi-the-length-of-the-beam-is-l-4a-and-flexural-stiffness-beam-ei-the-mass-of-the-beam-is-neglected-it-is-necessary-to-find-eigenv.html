Guest Question.

The beam in Fig. 14.10a carries three equal concentrated masses mi . The length of the beam is l = 4a, and flexural stiffness beam EI. The mass of the beam is neglected. It is necessary to find eigenvalues and mode shape vibrations.

Admin
Solution

The beam has three degrees of freedom. The bending moment diagrams caused by unit inertial forces are shown in Fig. 14.10b.

Multiplication of corresponding bending moment diagrams leads to the following results for unit displacements

\(\delta _{11}=\int{\frac{\overline{M}_1\overline{M}_1 }{EI}dx }= \frac{1}{EI}\left(\frac{1}{2}\frac{l}{4}\frac{3l}{16}\frac{2}{3}\frac{3l}{16} + \frac{1}{2}\frac{3l}{4}\frac{3l}{16}\frac{2}{3}\frac{3l}{16}\right)=\frac{9}{768}\frac{l^3}{EI},\)

 

\(\delta _{22}=\int{\frac{\overline{M}_2\overline{M}_2 }{EI}dx }= \frac{16}{768}\frac{l^3}{EI}, \delta _{33}=\int{\frac{\overline{M}_3\overline{M}_3 }{EI}dx }=\frac{9}{768}\frac{l^3}{EI},\)

 

\(\delta _{12}= \delta _{21}\int{\frac{\overline{M}_1\overline{M}_2 }{EI}dx }= \frac{11}{768}\frac{l^3}{EI} ,\)

 

\(\delta _{13}= \delta _{31}\int{\frac{\overline{M}_1\overline{M}_3 }{EI}dx }= \frac{7}{768}\frac{l^3}{EI}, \delta _{23}=\delta _{32}=\delta _{12}=\delta _{21}=\frac{11}{768}\frac{l^3}{EI}.\)

 

Let \(\delta _0 =l^3/768EI.\) Matrix of unit displacements F (Flexibility matrix) is

\(F =[\delta _{ik}]=\left [ \begin{matrix} \delta _{11} & \delta _{12} & \delta _{13} \\ \delta _{21} & \delta _{22} & \delta _{23} \\ \delta _{31} & \delta _{32} & \delta _{33} \end{matrix} \right ]=\delta _0\left [ \begin{matrix} 9 &11 & 7 \\ 11 &16 &11 \\ 7 & 11 &9 \end{matrix} \right ]=\delta _0F_0.\)

 

Equations (14.4)

\( \begin{matrix} (m_1\delta _{11}ω^2 -1)A_1 +m_2\delta _{12}ω^2A_2 + . . . + m_n\delta _{1n}ω^2A_n=0 , \\m_1\delta _{21}ω^2 A_1 +(m_2\delta _{22}ω^2-1)A_2 + . . . + m_n\delta _{2n}ω^2A_n=0 , \\ \cdot \cdot \cdot \cdot \cdot \cdot \cdot \\ m_1\delta _{31}ω^2A_1 +m_2\delta _{32}ω^2A_2 + . . . + (m_n\delta _{nn}ω^2-1)A_n=0 . \end{matrix} \) (14.4)

with unknown amplitudes \(A_i\) of mass \(m_i\) are

\(\begin{matrix} (m_1\delta _{11}ω^2 -1)A_1 +m_2\delta _{12}ω^2A_2 + m_3\delta _{13}ω^2A_3=0 , \\m_1\delta _{21}ω^2 A_1 +(m_2\delta _{22}ω^2-1)A_2 + m_3\delta _{23}ω^2A_3=0 , \\ m_1\delta _{31}ω^2A_1 +m_2\delta _{32}ω^2A_2 +(m_3\delta _{33}ω^2-1)A_3=0 . \end{matrix}\) (a)

In our case all masses \(m_i = m\). Divide by \(m\delta _0ω^2\) and denote \(\lambda = 1/m\delta _0ω^2\). Equations for amplitudes \(A_i\)

\(\begin{matrix} (9-\lambda )+11A_2 +7A_3=0, \\ 11A_1 + (16 -\lambda )A_2 +11A_3=0, \\ 7A_1 +11A_2 +(9 -\lambda )A_3=0. \end{matrix}\) (b)

Frequency equation becomes

\(\det [F_0 -\lambda I]=\det \left [ \begin{matrix} 9 -\lambda & 11 & 7 \\ 11 & 16 -\lambda & 11 \\ 7 & 11 & 9-\lambda \end{matrix} \right ] =0,\)

 

where I is unit matrix. Eigenvalues are in descending order

\(\begin{matrix} \lambda _1=31.5563, \\ \lambda _2 = 2.0, \\ \lambda _3 =0.44365. \end{matrix}\) (c)

Verification:

1. The sum of the eigenvalues is \(\lambda _1+\lambda _2+\lambda _3 = 31.5563+2.0+ 0.44365 = 34;\)

On the other hand, the trace of the matrix \(Tr(F_0) = 9 + 16 + 9 = 34.\)

2. The multiplication of the eigenvalues is \(\lambda _1.\lambda _2.\lambda _3 = 31.5563\cdot 2.0\cdot 0.4365 =28;\) On the other hand, det \(F_0 = 28\). (Note, that determinant of unit displacement matrix is strictly positive, i.e. det F > 0).

Frequencies of the free vibration in increasing order

\(ω_1^2 = \frac{1}{\lambda _1m\delta _0}=\frac{768}{31.5563}\frac{EI}{ml^3}= 24.337\frac{EI}{ml^3}\rightarrow ω_1 =4.9333\sqrt{\frac{EI}{ml^3} },\)

 

\(ω_2^2 = \frac{1}{\lambda _2m\delta _0}=\frac{768}{2.0}\frac{EI}{ml^3}= 384\frac{EI}{ml^3}\rightarrow ω_2 =19.5959\sqrt{\frac{EI}{ml^3} },\) (d)

 

\(ω_3^2 = \frac{1}{\lambda _3m\delta _0}=\frac{768}{0.44365}\frac{EI}{ml^3}= 1731.09\frac{EI}{ml^3}\rightarrow ω_3 =41.6064\sqrt{\frac{EI}{ml^3} },\)

 

For each i-th eigenvalue, the set of equation (b) for calculation of amplitudes is

\((9 -\lambda _i)A_1 +11A_2 +7A_3 =0,\)

 

\(11A_1 +(16 -\lambda _i)A_2 +11A_3 =0,\) (e)

 

\(7A_1 +11A_2 +(9 -\lambda _i)A_3 =0.\)

 

Equations (e) divide by \(A_1\). Let \(\rho _2=A_2/A_1, \rho _3 = A_3/A_1.\) Equations for modes become

\((9 -\lambda _i)+ 11\rho _{2i} +7\rho _{3i}= 0,\)

 

\(11 +(16 -\lambda _i)\rho _{2i}+ 11\rho _{3i}= 0,\) (f)

 

\(7 +11\rho _{2i}+(9 -\lambda _i)\rho _{3i}+ 11\rho _{3i}= 0.\)

 

Assuming \(A_1 = 1\) we can calculate \(ρ_2\) and \( ρ_3\) for each calculated eigenvalue. For their calculation we can consider set of any two equations.

1. Eigenvalue \(λ_1 = 31.5563\)

\((9 -31.5563)+ 11\rho _{2}+ 7\rho_3= 0,\)

 

\(11 +(16 -31.5563)\rho _2+ 11\rho _{3}= 0.\)

 

Solution of these equations is \(\rho_2 = 1.4142, \rho_ 3 = 1.0.\) Therefore, the first (principal)mode is defined as \(y_{11},y_{21}= \sqrt{2}y_{11} ,y_{31}=y_{11}.\) If we assume that \(y_{11} = 1\), then the eigenvector \(φ_1\) which corresponds to the frequency \(ω_1\) is \(\varphi _1 =\left\lfloor\begin{matrix} 1.0 & \sqrt{2} & 1\end{matrix} \right\rfloor^T.\)

Corresponding mode shape vibration is shown in Fig. 14.10c.

\( λ_1 =31.5563\)

 

\( \omega _1 = 4.9333\sqrt{\frac{EI}{ml^3} }\)

 

Note, that substitution of \(\rho _2\) and \(\rho _3\) into third equation (f) leads to the identity.

2. Eigenvalue \(λ_2 = 2.0.\) In this case

\( (9 -2.0) + 11\rho _2 +7\rho _3=0,\)

 

\( 11 +(16 -2.0)\rho_ 2 + 11\rho _3=0.\)

 

Solution is \(\rho _2 =0.0, \rho _3=- 1.0,\) and second eigenvector becomes \(\varphi _2=\left\lfloor\begin{matrix} 1.0 & 0.0 & – 1\end{matrix} \right\rfloor^T ;\) this mode shape vibration is shown in Fig. 14.10d.

\( \lambda _2 =2.0,\)

 

\( \omega _2 = 19.5959\sqrt{\frac{EI}{ml^3} }.\)

 

3. Eigenvalue \(\lambda _3 = 0.44365.\) In this case

\( (9 -0.44365)+ 11\rho _2 + 7\rho _3 =0,\)

 

\( 11 + (16 -0.44365)\rho _2 + 11\rho _3 =0.\)

 

Solution \(\rho _2 = -1.4142, \rho _3 = 1.0,\) and Eigenvector \(\varphi _3 = \left\lfloor\begin{matrix} 1.0 & – \sqrt{2} & 1\end{matrix} \right\rfloor ^T\). Third mode shape vibration is shown in Fig. 14.10e.

\( \lambda _3 = 0.44365,\)

 

\( \omega _3 = 41.6064\sqrt{\frac{EI}{ml^3} }.\)

 

We can see that a number of the nodal points of the mode of shape vibration one less than the number of the mode.

The modal matrix is defined as

\(\Phi = \left\lfloor\begin{matrix} \varphi _{1} & \varphi _{2} & \varphi _{3}\end{matrix} \right\rfloor=\left [ \begin{matrix} \varphi _{11} & \varphi _{12} & \varphi_{13} \\ \varphi_{21} & \varphi_{22} & \varphi_{23} \\ \varphi_{31} & \varphi_{32} & \varphi_{33} \end{matrix} \right ] = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 1 \\ \sqrt{2} & 0.0 & -\sqrt{2} \\ 1 &-1 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ],\) (g)

where the i -th and k-th indexes at φ mean the number of mass and number of frequency, respectively.
