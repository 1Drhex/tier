Guest Question.

Consider a water vapor-air mixture at 3 atm and 600 K, with 5% water vapor by volume. What is the most important \(H_{2}O\) band and what is its total band absorptance for a path of 10 cm ?

Admin
Solution

At 600 K the Planck function has its maximum around 5 μm. Since total emission will depend on the blackbody intensity [see equation (10.48) \(\int_{0}^{\infty }{}\kappa _{\eta }I_{(b)\eta } d\eta and \int_{0}^{\infty }{} I_{(b)\eta }\left[1-exp \left(-\int_{0}^{X}{}\kappa _{\eta }dX \right) \right]d\eta\) ], we seek a band with large a in the vicinity of 5 μm.
Inspection of Table 10.3 shows that the strongest vibration-rotation band for water vapor lies at 6.3 μm and is, therefore, the band we are interested in. From the table we find \(\alpha =\alpha _{0}=41.2 cm^{-1}/(g/m^{2}) , \beta =\gamma _{0}\sqrt{T_{0}/T}(\Phi /\Phi _{0})P_{e} with \gamma _{0}= 0.094, and w=w_{0}\sqrt{T/T_{0}}=56.4\sqrt{600/100}= 138.15 cm^{-1}\) To evaluate the effective broadening pressure we find n = 1 and b =\(8.6 \sqrt{100/600}+ 0.5 = 4.01 and with a volume fraction \)y=p_{a}/p the effective pressure becomes \(P_{e}=(p/1atm)[1+(b-1)y]^{n}=3[1+3.01\times 0.05]= 3.452\). Estimating the temperature dependence of the line overlap parameter from Fig. 10-18 leads to \(\gamma /\gamma _{0}\simeq 0.65 and \beta =0.094\times 0.65\times 3.452=0.211\) . For our water vapor with a partial pressure of \(p_{a}\) = 0.05 × 3 = 0.15 atm and a molecular weight of M= 18 g/mol, we get from the ideal gas law

\(\rho _{a}=\frac{Mp_{a}}{R_{a}T}=\frac{18g/mol\times 0.15 atm}{8.3145J/mol K\times 600K}\frac{1.0132\times 10^{5}J/m^{3}}{1 atm}=54.84 g/ m^{3}\)

and X = 54.84 × 0.1 = 5.48 \(g/m^{2}\) Finally, from \(\tau _{0}=\alpha X/w we get \tau _{0}=41.2\times 5.48/138.15=1.634\) . Since the value of \(\tau _{0}\) lies between the values of β and l/β we are in the square-root regime and

\(A^{*}=2\sqrt{\tau _{0}\beta }-\beta =2\sqrt{1.634\times 0.211}-0.211=0.964\)

or A=0.964 × 138.15 = 133 \(cm^{-1}\) .
