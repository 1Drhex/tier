Guest Question.

Calculate the lateral surface area cut from the cylinder \(y^{2}+z^{2}=9\) by the planes \(x=0\) and \(x=4\).

Admin
Solution

The surface is a right circular cylinder with height 4 and radius 3 whose axis lies along the \(x\)-axis. Thus

\(S=2 \pi r h=24 \pi\)

We now solve the problem by using formula (1).

\(\sigma=\iint_{\Omega} \sqrt{1+f_{x}^{2}(x, y)+f_{y}^{2}(x, y)} d A\)

The surface area consists of two equal parts, one for \(z>0\) and one for \(z<0\). We calculate the surface area for \(z>0\) and multiply the result by 2. We have, for \(z>0, z=f(x, y)=\sqrt{9-y^{2}}\), so \(f_{x}=0\), \(f_{y}=-y / \sqrt{9-y^{2}}\), and \(f_{y}^{2}=y^{2} /\left(9-y^{2}\right)\). Thus since \(0 \leq x \leq 4\) and \(-3 \leq y \leq 3\), we have

\(\begin{aligned}S &=2 \int_{0}^{4} \int_{-3}^{3} \sqrt{1+\frac{y^{2}}{9-y^{2}}} d y d x=4 \int_{0}^{4} \int_{0}^{3} \sqrt{\frac{9}{9-y^{2}}} d y d x \\&=12 \int_{0}^{4}\left(\int_{0}^{3} \frac{d y}{\sqrt{9-y^{2}}}\right) d x .\end{aligned}\)

Setting \(y=3 \sin \theta\), we have

\(S=12 \int_{0}^{4}\left(\int_{0}^{\pi / 2} \frac{3 \cos \theta}{3 \cos \theta} d \theta\right) d x=12 \int_{0}^{4} \frac{\pi}{2} d x=12(4)\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right)=24 \pi .\)
