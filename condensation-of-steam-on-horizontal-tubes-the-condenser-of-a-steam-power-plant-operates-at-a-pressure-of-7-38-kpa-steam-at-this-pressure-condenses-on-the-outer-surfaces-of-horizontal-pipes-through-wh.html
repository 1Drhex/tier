Condensation of Steam on Horizontal Tubes

The condenser of a steam power plant operates at a pressure of 7.38 kPa. Steam at this pressure condenses on the outer surfaces of horizontal pipes through which cooling water circulates. The outer diameter of the pipes is 3 cm, and the outer surfaces of the pipes are maintained at \( 30^{\circ}C \) (Fig. 10â€“32). Determine (a) the rate of heat transfer to the cooling water circulating in the pipes and (b) the rate of condensation of steam per unit length of a horizontal pipe.

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION Saturated steam at a pressure of 7.38 kPa (Table A-9) condenses on a horizontal tube at \( 30^{\circ}C \). The rates of heat transfer and condensation are to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Steady operating conditions exist. 2 The tube is isothermal.

Properties The properties of water at the saturation temperature of \( 40^{\circ}C \) corresponding to 7.38 kPa are \( h_{fg} = 2407 \times 10^{3} J/kg \) and \( \rho_{\nu } = 0.05 kg/m^{3} \).The properties of liquid water at the film temperature of \( T_{f} = \left ( T_{sat} + T_{s}\right )/2 = \left ( 40 + 30 \right )/2 = 35^{\circ}C \) are (Table A-9)

\( \rho_{l} = 994 kg/m^{3} C_{pl} = 4178 J/kg \cdot ^{\circ}C \)

 

\( \mu_{l} = 0.720 \times 10^{-3} kg/m \cdot s k_{l} = 0.623 W/m \cdot ^{\circ}C \)

 

Analysis (a) The modified latent heat of vaporization is

\( h_{fg}^{\ast } = h_{fg} + 0.68 C_{pl} \left ( T_{sat} – T_{s} \right ) \)

 

\( = 2407 \times 10^{3} J/kg + 0.68 \times \left ( 4178 J/kg \cdot ^{\circ}C \right ) \left ( 40 – 30 \right )^{\circ}C\)

 

\( = 2435 \times 10^{3} J/kg \)

 

Noting that \( \rho _{\nu } \ll \rho _{l} \) (since \( 0.05 \ll 994 \)), the heat transfer coefficient for condensation on a single horizontal tube is determined from Eq. to be

\( h = h_{horizontal} =\)

 

\( 0.729 \left [ \frac{g \rho _{l} \left ( _{l} – \rho _{\nu } \right ) h_{fg}^{\ast } k_{l}^{\ast }}{\mu \left ( T_{sat} – T_{s} \right ) D} \right ]^{1/4} \cong 0.729 \left [ \frac{g \rho _{l}^{2} h_{fg}^{\ast } k_{l}^{3 }}{\mu_{l} \left ( T_{sat} – T_{s} \right ) D} \right ]^{1/4} \)

 

\( = 0.729 \left [ \frac{\left ( 9.81 m/s^{2} \right )\left ( 994 kg/m^{3} \right )^{2}\left ( 2435 \times 10^{3} J/kg \right )\left ( 0.623 W/m \cdot ^{\circ}C \right )^{3}}{\left ( 0.720 \times 10^{-3} kg/m \cdot s \right )\left ( 40 – 30 \right )^{\circ}C \left ( 0.03 m \right )} \right ] \)

 

\( = 9292 W/m^{2} \cdot ^{\circ}C \)

 

The heat transfer surface area of the pipe per unit of its length is \( A_{s} = \pi DL = \pi \left ( 0.03 m \right )\left ( 1 m \right ) = 0.09425 m^{2} \). Then the rate of heat transfer during this condensation process becomes

\( \dot{Q} = h A_{s} \left ( T_{sat} – T_{s} \right )\)

 

\( = \left ( 9292 W/m^{2} \cdot ^{\circ}C \right )\left ( 0.09425 m^{2} \right )\left ( 40 – 30 \right )^{\circ}C = 8758 W \)

 

(b) The rate of condensation of steam is

\( \dot{m}_{condensation} = \frac{\dot{Q}}{h_{fg}^{\ast }} = \frac{8578 J/s}{2435 \times 10^{3} J/kg} = 0.00360 kg/s \)

 

Therefore, steam will condense on the horizontal tube at a rate of 3.6 g/s or 12.9 kg/h per meter of its length.
