Computing Income Taxes for Corporations
A small company is currently forecasting a taxable income of $50,000 for the year. The company’s owner is considering an investment that will increase taxable income by $45,000. If the investment is pursued and the anticipated return occurs, what will be the magnitude of the increase in income taxes caused by the new investment? What will it be if the company is currently forecasting a taxable income of $400,000 for the year?

KEY DATA Given: Base taxable income 1 = $50,000; Base taxable income 2 = $400,000; Increase in annual income with investment = $45,000
Find: Increase in income taxes for each scenario

Admin
Solution

From Table 9.1, with a base taxable income of $50,000, the federal income tax will be 0.15($50,000), or $7,500. The income tax for a taxable income of $95,000 will be $13,750 + 0.34($95,000 – $75,000) = $13,750 + 0.34($20,000) = $20,550. The magnitude increase in income tax caused by the new investment is therefore $20,550 – $7,500 = $13,050.
Again, from Table 9.1, with a base taxable income of $400,000, the federal income tax will be $113,900 + 0.34($400,000 – $335,000) = $113,900 + 0.34($65,000) = $136,000, or 34 percent of $400,000. In this case, every dollar of the additional $45,000 in taxable income will be taxed at 34 percent, so the increase in taxable income will be 0.34($45,000) = $15,300, for a total tax of $151,300.

75,000 < TI ≤ 100,000 0.34 13,750 + 0.34(TI – 75,000)

335,000 < TI ≤ 10,000,000 0.34 113,900 + 0.34(TI – 335,000)
10,000,000 < TI ≤ 15,000,000 0.35 3,400,000 + 0.35(TI – 10,000,000)
