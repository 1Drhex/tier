Guest Question.

A PMMC instrument with meter resistance \(1 k\Omega \) gives a full-scale deflection of 80 mA. It is to be used as a full-wave rectifier voltmeter to give FSD of 80 V (rms).

Determine the required value of multiplier resistance if silicon diodes are used in the circuit

Admin
Solution

Meter resistance Rm = \(1 k\Omega\)

The rms voltage of voltmeter \(V_{rms}\) = 80 V

At full-scale deflection, the average current flowing through the PMMC meter is:

\(I_{av} = 80 \mu A\)

The peak value of current Im can be obtained as:

\(I_{m}=\frac{I_{av}}{0.637}\)

Thus, we get:

\(I_{m}=\frac{80\times 10^{-6}}{0.637}=125.6 \mu A\)

Now, since the voltage drop for a full-wave rectifier voltmeter is \(2 V_{F}\) and silicon diodes are used in the circuit as per the given data, the voltage comes out as:

Voltage drop = 2 × 0.7 V = 1.4 V

We know that the amount of peak voltage \(V_{peak}\) applied to the voltmeter is obtained as:

\(V_{peak} = 1.414 × rms value of voltage = 1.414 × V_{rms} =\) 1.414 × 80 V = 113.12 V

Now, as the total circuit resistance is given as:

\(R_{T}=R_{s}+R_{m}\)

The multiplier resistance\( R_{s}\) can be determined using the relation for meter current

\(I_{m}\) as:

\(I_{m}=\frac{V_{peak}-2V_{F}}{R_{s}+R_{m}}\) \(R_{s}=\frac{V_{peak}-2V_{F}}{I_{m}}-R_{m}\)

On substituting the values, we get:

\(R_{S}=\frac{113.12-1.4}{125.6\times 10^{-6}}-1\times 10^{-6}=888.5 K\Omega\)
