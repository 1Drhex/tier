Guest Question.

Given: A state of strain referenced to an x−y−z coordinate is known to be

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} \varepsilon _{xx} & \varepsilon _{xy} & \varepsilon _{xz} \\ \varepsilon _{yx} & \varepsilon _{yy} & \varepsilon _{yz} \\ \varepsilon _{zx} & \varepsilon _{zy} & \varepsilon _{zz}\end{matrix} \right ]= \left [ \begin{matrix} 1000 \mu m/m & 500 \mu rad & 250 \mu rad \\ 500 \mu rad & 1500 \mu m/m & 750 \mu rad \\ 250 \mu rad & 750 \mu rad & 2000 \mu m/m\end{matrix} \right ]\)

It is desired to express this state of strain in a x″−y″−z″ coordinate system, generated by

i. Rotation of θ = 20° about the original z-axis (which defines an intermediate x′−y′−z′ coordinate system), followed by ii. Rotation of β = 35° about the x′-axis (which defines the final x″−y″−z″ coordinate system)

this coordinate transformation has been previously considered in , and is shown in Figure 2.2).

PROBLEM

a. Rotate the strain tensor to the x″−y″−z″ coordinate system. b. Calculate the first, second, and third invariants of the strain tensor using (i) elements of the strain tensor
referenced to the x−y−z coordinate system, \(ε_{ij}\), and (ii) elements of the strain tensor referenced to the x″−y″−z″ coordinate system, \(ε_{i″ j″}\).

Admin
Solution

a. General expressions for direction cosines relating the x−y−z and x″−y″−z″ coordinate systems were determined as a part of . Furthermore, numerical values for the particular rotation θ = 20° and β = 35° were determined in and found to be:

\(c_{x″x}\) =cos(20°) = 0.9397

\(c_{x″y}\) =sin(20°) = 0.3420

\(c_{x″z}\) =0

\(c_{y″x}\) =cos(35°)sin(20°) = -0.2802

\(c_{y″y}\) =cos(35°)cos(20°) = 0.7698

\(c_{y″z}\) =sin(35°) = 0.5736

\(c_{z″x}\) =cos(35°)sin(20°) =0.1962

\(c_{z″y}\) = – sin(35°)cos(20°) = – 0.5390

\(c_{z″z}\) =cos(35°)= 0.8192

Each component of the transformed strain tensor can now be found through application of Equation 2.36a

\(\varepsilon _{i^′j^′}= c_{i^′k}c_{j^′k}\varepsilon_{kl}\) where k , l = x , y ,z (2.36a)

or Equation 2.36b.

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} \varepsilon _{x^′x^′} & \varepsilon _{x^′y^′} & \varepsilon _{x^′z^′} \\ \varepsilon _{y^′x^′} & \varepsilon _{y^′y^′} & \varepsilon _{y^′z^′} \\ \varepsilon _{z^′x^′} & \varepsilon _{z^′y^′} & \varepsilon _{z^′z^′}\end{matrix} \right ]= \left [ \begin{matrix} c _{x^′x} & c_{x^′y} & c_{x^′z} \\c _{y^′x} & c _{y^′y} & c _{y^′z} \\ c _{z^′x} & c _{z^′y} & c _{z^′z}\end{matrix} \right ]\left [ \begin{matrix} \varepsilon _{xx} & \varepsilon _{xy} & \varepsilon _{xz} \\ \varepsilon _{yx} & \varepsilon _{yy} & \varepsilon _{yz} \\ \varepsilon _{zx} & \varepsilon _{zy} & \varepsilon _{zz}\end{matrix} \right ]\left [ \begin{matrix} c_{x^′x} & c _{y^x′} & c_{z^′x} \\ c_{x^′y} & c_{y^′y} & c _{z^′y} \\ c _{x^′z} & c _{y^′z} & c _{z^′z}\end{matrix} \right ]\) (2.36b)

For example, setting i′ = x″, j′ = x″ and expanding Equation 2.36a, strain component εx″ x″ is given by

\(\varepsilon _{x″x″}= c_{x″x}c_{x″x}\varepsilon _{xx}+c_{x″x}c_{x″y}\varepsilon _{xy} + c_{x″x}c_{x″z}\varepsilon _{xz}+ c_{x″y}c_{x″x}\varepsilon _{yx}+c_{x″y}c_{x″y}\varepsilon _{yy}+c_{x″y}c_{x″z}\varepsilon _{yz}+ c_{x″z}c_{x″x}\varepsilon _{zx}+c_{x″z}c_{x″y}\varepsilon _{zx}+c_{x″z}c_{x″y}\varepsilon _{zy}+c_{x″z}c_{x″z}\varepsilon _{zz}\)

 

\(\varepsilon _{x″x″}= (0.9397)(0.9397)(1000) + (0.9397)(0.3420)(500) + (0.9397 )(0)(250) +(0.3420)(0.9397)(500) + (0.3420)(0.3420)(1500) +(0.3420)(0)(750) +(0)(0.9397)(250) +(0)(0.3420)(750) + (0)(0)(2000)\)

 

\(\varepsilon _{x″x″}=1380 μm/m\)

 

Alternatively, if matrix notation is used, then Equation 2.36b becomes

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} \varepsilon _{x″x″} & \varepsilon _{x″y″} & \varepsilon _{x″z″} \\ \varepsilon _{y″x″} & \varepsilon _{y″y″} & \varepsilon _{y″z″} \\ \varepsilon _{z″x″} & \varepsilon _{z″y″} & \varepsilon _{z″z″}\end{matrix} \right ]= \left [ \begin{matrix} 0.9397 & 0.3420 & 0\\ – 0.2802 & 0.7698 & 0.5736 \\ 0.1962 & – 0.5390 & 0.8192\end{matrix} \right ]\left [ \begin{matrix} 1000 & 500 & 250 \\ 500 & 1500 & 750 \\ 250 & 750 & 2000\end{matrix} \right ]\times \left [ \begin{matrix} 0.9397 & – 0.2802 & 0.1962 \\ 0.3420 & 0.7698 & – 0.5390 \\ 0 & 0.5736 & 0.8192\end{matrix} \right ]\)

Completing the matrix multiplication indicated, there results:

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} \varepsilon _{x″x″} & \varepsilon _{x″y″} & \varepsilon _{x″z″} \\ \varepsilon _{y″x″} & \varepsilon _{y″y″} & \varepsilon _{y″z″} \\ \varepsilon _{z″x″} & \varepsilon _{z″y″} & \varepsilon _{z″z″}\end{matrix} \right ]= \left [ \begin{matrix} 1380 \mu m/m & 727 \mu rad & 91 \mu rad \\ 727 \mu rad & 1991 \mu m/m & 625 \mu rad \\ 91\mu rad & 625\mu rad & 1129 \mu m/m\end{matrix} \right ]\)

 

Notice that the value of \(ε_{x″ x″}\) determined through matrix multiplication is identical to that obtained using indicial notation, as expected.

b. The first, second, and third strain invariants will now be calculated using components of both \(ε_{ij}\) and \(ε_{i″ j″}\). It is expected that identical values will be obtained, as the strain invariants are independent of coordinate system.

First Strain Invariant

x−y−z coordinate system:

\(\Theta _\varepsilon =\varepsilon _{ii}= \varepsilon _{xx}+\varepsilon _{yy}+\varepsilon _{zz}\)

 

\(\Theta _\varepsilon= (1000 + 1500 + 2000) μm/m\)

 

\(\Theta _\varepsilon= 4500 μm/m = 0.004500m/m\)

 

x″−y″−z″ coordinate system

\(\Theta _\varepsilon =\varepsilon _{i″i″}= \varepsilon _{x″x″}+\varepsilon _{y″y″}+\varepsilon _{z″z″}\)

 

\(\Theta _\varepsilon =(1380 + 1991 + 1129) μm/m\)

 

\(\Theta _\varepsilon=4500 μm/m = 0.004500m/m\)

 

As expected, the first strain invariant is independent of coordinate system.

Second Strain Invariant

x−y−z coordinate system:

\(\Phi _\varepsilon = \frac{1}{2}(\varepsilon _{ii}\varepsilon _{jj} – \varepsilon _{ij}\varepsilon _{ij})= \varepsilon _{xx}\varepsilon _{yy} + \varepsilon _{xx}\varepsilon _{zz} + \varepsilon _{yy}\varepsilon _{zz} – (\varepsilon _{xy}^2 + \varepsilon _{xz}^2 + \varepsilon _{yz}^2)\)

 

\(\Phi _\varepsilon= \left\{(1000)(1500) + (1000)(2000) + (1500)(2000) -[(500)^2 + (250)^2 + (750)^2]\right\} (μm /m)^2\)

 

\(\Phi _\varepsilon = 5.625 × 10^6(μm /m)^2 = 5.625 × 10^{− 6} (m /m)^2\)

 

x″−y″−z″ coordinate system:

\(\Phi _\varepsilon = \frac{1}{2}(\varepsilon _{i″i″}\varepsilon _{j″j″} – \varepsilon _{i″j″}\varepsilon _{i″j″})\)

 

\(\Phi _\varepsilon = \varepsilon _{x″x″}\varepsilon _{y″y″} + \varepsilon _{x″x″}\varepsilon _{z″z″} + \varepsilon _{y″y″}\varepsilon _{z″z″} – (\varepsilon _{x″x″}^2 + \varepsilon _{x″z″}^2 + \varepsilon _{y″z″}^2)\)

 

\(\Phi _\varepsilon =\left\{(1380)(1991) + (1380)(1129) + (1991)(1129) – [(727)^2+(91)^2 + (625)^2]\right\}(μm/m)^2\)

 

\(\Phi _\varepsilon = 5.625 × 10^6 ( m/m)^2=5.625 × 10^{-6} (m/m)^2\)

 

As expected, the second strain invariant is independent of coordinate system.

Third Strain Invariant

x−y−z coordinate system:

\(\Psi _\varepsilon = \frac{1}{6}(\varepsilon _{ii}\varepsilon _{jj}\varepsilon _{kk} – 3\varepsilon _{ii}\varepsilon _{jk}\varepsilon _{jk}+2\varepsilon _{ij}\varepsilon _{jk}\varepsilon _{ki})\)

 

\(\Psi _\varepsilon = \varepsilon _{xx}\varepsilon _{yy}\varepsilon _{zz} – \varepsilon _{xx}\varepsilon _{yz}^2 – \varepsilon _{yy}\varepsilon _{xz}^2 -\varepsilon _{zz}\varepsilon _{xy}^2 + 2\varepsilon _{xy}\varepsilon _{xz}\varepsilon _{yz}\)

 

\(\Psi _\varepsilon =[(1000)(1500)(2000) − (1000)(750)^2 − (1500)(250)^2 – (2000)(500)^2 + 2 (500)(250)(750)](μm/m)^3\)

 

\(\Psi = 2.031 × 10^9 (μm/m)^3= 2.031 × 10^{− 9} (m/m)^3 \)

 

x″−y″−z″ coordinate system:

\(\Psi _\varepsilon = \frac{1}{6}(\varepsilon _{i″i″}\varepsilon _{j″j″}\varepsilon _{k″k″} – 3\varepsilon _{i″i″}\varepsilon _{j″k″}\varepsilon _{j″k″}+2\varepsilon _{i″j″}\varepsilon _{j″k″}\varepsilon _{k″i″})\)

 

\(\Psi _\varepsilon = \varepsilon _{x″x″}\varepsilon _{y″y″}\varepsilon _{z″z″} – \varepsilon _{x″x″}\varepsilon _{y″z″}^2 – \varepsilon _{y″y″}\varepsilon _{x″z″}^2 -\varepsilon _{z″z″}\varepsilon _{x″y″}^2 + 2\varepsilon _{x″y″}\varepsilon _{x″z″}\varepsilon _{y″z″}\)

 

\(\Psi _\varepsilon = [(1380)(1991)(1129) − (1380)(625)^2 − (1991)(91)^2 − (1129)(727)^2 + 2(727)(91)(625)](μm/m)^ 3\)

 

\(\Psi = 2.031 × 10^9 (μm/m)^3= 2 031 × 10^{− 9} (m/m)^3 \)

 

As expected, the third stress invariant is independent of coordinate system.
