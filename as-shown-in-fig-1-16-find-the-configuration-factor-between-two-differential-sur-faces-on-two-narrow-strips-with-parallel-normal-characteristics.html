Guest Question.

As shown in Fig. 1.16, find the configuration factor between two differential surfaces on two narrow strips with parallel normal characteristics.

Admin
Solution

According to the equation derived above, the configuration factor of two differential surfaces is:

\(\varphi_{ d_{1},d_{2}}=\frac{\cos\beta _{1} \cos\beta_{2} dA_{2} }{\pi I^{2}} =\cos\beta _{1}d\Omega _{1} \)

As Fig. 1.16 suggests:

I² =s² +x²

and,

\(\cos\beta _{1} =\frac{scos\theta}{I} = \frac{scos\theta}{(s^{2} +x^{2})^{1/2}} \)

Consider the solid angle of \(dA_{2}\) from \(dA_{1}\)

\(d\Omega _{1} = \frac{projected area of dA_{2}}{I^{2}} = \frac{projected width of dA_{2} × projected length of dA_{2}}{I^{2}}\)

\(= \frac{(sd\theta)(dx\cos\Psi )}{I^{2}} = \frac{(sd\theta dx\cos\Psi )}{I^{2}}\)

Because

\(\cos\Psi = \frac{s}{I}\)

\(d\Omega _{1} = \frac{s^{2}d\theta dx}{I^{3}} = \frac{s^{2}d\theta dx}{(s^{2} +x^{2})^{3/2}}\)

then,

\(\varphi_{ d_{1},d_{2}}=\frac{scos\theta}{(s^{2} +x^{2})^{1/2}} \frac{1}{\pi} \frac{s^{2}d\theta dx}{(s^{2} +x^{2})^{3/2}}\)

and finally,

\(\varphi_{d_{1},d_{2}}=\frac{s^{3}cos\theta d\theta dx}{\pi (s^{2} +x^{2})^{2}} \) (1.80)

This is the equation for the configuration factor from \(dA_{1}\) to \(dA_{2}\).

If \(dA_{2}\) is a narrow infinite strip, integrate x from –∞ to +∞, then the configuration factor from the differential surface \(dA_{1}\) to the narrow infinite strip \(ds_{2}\) can be calculated. Its geometric shape and relative position are shown in Fig. 1.17.

\(\varphi_{d_{1},d_{2}}=\frac{s^{3}cos\theta d\theta}{\pi}\int_{-\infty }^{+\infty }{\frac{dx}{(s^{2} +x^{2})^{2}}} \)

\(=\frac{s^{3}cos\theta d\theta}{\pi}\left[\frac{x}{2s^{2}(s^{2} +x^{2})} +\frac{1}{2s^{2}}\arctan\frac{x}{s} \right]_{-\infty }^{+\infty }\)

\(=\frac{\cos\theta d\theta}{2}\)

\(=\frac{1}{2}d(\sin\theta)\) (1.81)

where θ is on plane yz. Regardless of the way the position of \(dA_{1}\) varies on narrow strip \(ds_{1}\), Eq. (1.81) \(=\frac{1}{2}d(\sin\theta)\) can be used to calculate the coefficient. So the configuration factor between two infinite narrow strips with parallel normal is also \(=\frac{1}{2}d(\sin\theta)\), and:

\(\varphi_{d_{1},d_{2}}=\frac{1}{2}d(\sin\theta)\)
