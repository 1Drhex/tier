Guest Question.

Rigid beam ABC is supported by a pinned connection at C and by an inclined strut that is pinconnected at B and D, as shown in Figure P16.13a. The strut is fabricated from two steel [E = 200 GPa] bars, which are each 70 mm wide and 15 mm thick. Between B and D, the bars are separated and connected by two spacer blocks, which are 25 mm thick. The strut cross section is shown in Figure P16.13b. Determine:
(a) the compression force in strut BD that is created by the loads acting on the rigid beam.
(b) the slenderness ratios for the strut about its strong and weak axes.
(c) the minimum factor of safety in the strut with respect to buckling.

Admin
Solution

Compression force in strut:
Member (1) is a two-force member that is oriented at θ with respect to the horizontal axis:

\(\tan \theta=\frac{1.5 m }{2.0 m }=0.75 \quad \therefore \theta=36.870^{\circ}\)

From a FBD of rigid bar ABC, write the following equilibrium equation:

\(\Sigma M_{C}=(3 m )(90 kN )+\left(F_{1} \sin 36.870^{\circ}\right)(2 m )=0\)

and compute \(F_{1}\):

\(F_{1}=-\frac{(3 m )(90 kN )}{(2 m ) \sin 36.870^{\circ}}=-225 kN =225 kN ( C )\)

 

(b) Slenderness ratios:
Consider strong axis (i.e., buckling about horizontal cross-sectional axis):

\(\begin{aligned}&L=\sqrt{(1.5 m )^{2}+(2.0 m )^{2}}=2.5 m=2,500 mm \\&A=2(15 mm )(70 mm )=2,100 mm ^{2} \\&I=2\left[\frac{(15 mm )(70 mm )^{3}}{12}\right]=857,500 mm ^{4} \\&r=\sqrt{\frac{857,500 mm ^{4}}{2,100 mm ^{2}}}=20.207 mm \\&\frac{L}{r}=\frac{2,500 mm }{20.207 mm }=123.7\end{aligned}\)

Consider weak axis (i.e., buckling about vertical cross-sectional axis):

\(\begin{aligned}&I=2\left[\frac{(70 mm )(15 mm )^{3}}{12}+\left(\frac{25 mm }{2}+\frac{15 mm }{2}\right)^{2}(70 mm )(15 mm )\right]=879,375 mm ^{4} \\&r=\sqrt{\frac{879,375 mm ^{4}}{2,100 mm ^{2}}}=20.463 mm \\&\frac{L}{r}=\frac{2,500 mm }{20.463 mm }=122.2\end{aligned}\)

 

(c) Minimum factor of safety with respect to buckling:

\(\begin{aligned}&P_{c r}=\frac{\pi^{2} E I}{L^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}\left(200,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(857,500 mm ^{4}\right)}{(2,500 mm )^{2}}=270,822 N =270.8 kN \\& FS =\frac{270.8 kN }{225 kN }=1.204\end{aligned}\)
