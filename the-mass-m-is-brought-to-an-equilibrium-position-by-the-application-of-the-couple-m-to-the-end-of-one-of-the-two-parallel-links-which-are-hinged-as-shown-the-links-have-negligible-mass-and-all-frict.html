Guest Question.

The mass m is brought to an equilibrium position by the application of the couple M to the end of one of the two parallel links which are hinged as shown. The links have negligible mass, and all friction is assumed to be absent. Determine the expression for the equilibrium angle \(θ\) assumed by the links with the vertical for a given value of M. Consider the alternative of a solution by force and moment equilibrium.

Admin
Solution

The active-force diagram shows the weight mg acting through the center of mass G and the couple M applied to the end of the link. There are no other external active forces or moments which do work on the system during a change in the angle \(θ\).
The vertical position of the center of mass G is designated by the distance h below the fixed horizontal reference line and is \(h = b cos θ + c\). The work done by mg during a movement \(δh\) in the direction of mg is

\(+mg\delta h=mg\delta (b\cos \theta +c)\)
\(=mg (-b\sin \theta \delta \theta +0)\)
\(=-mgb\sin \theta \delta \theta\)

The minus sign shows that the work is negative for a positive value of \(δθ\). The constant c drops out since its variation is zero. With \( θ\) measured positive in the clockwise sense, \(δθ\) is also positive clockwise. Thus, the work done by the clockwise couple M is \(+M δθ\). Substitution into the virtual-work equation gives us

\([\delta U=0] M\delta \theta +mg\delta h=0\)

 

which yields

\(M\delta \theta =mgb\sin \theta \delta \theta\)

\(\theta =\sin ^{-1} \frac{M}{mgb}\)

Inasmuch as \(sin θ\) cannot exceed unity, we see that for equilibrium, M is limited to values that do not exceed \(mgb\).
The advantage of the virtual-work solution for this problem is readily seen when we observe what would be involved with a solution by force and moment equilibrium. For the latter approach, it would be necessary for us to draw separate free-body diagrams of all of the three moving parts and account for all of the internal reactions at the pin connections. To carry out these steps, it would be necessary for us to include in the analysis the horizontal position of G with respect to the attachment points of the two links, even though reference to this position would finally drop out of the equations when they were solved. We conclude, then, that the virtual-work method in this problem deals directly with cause and effect and avoids reference to irrelevant quantities.
