Guest Question.

Consider a gray gas with an absorption coefficient of \(k=0.5m^{-1}\) . The temperature of the gas varies linearly from x = 0 to x = L = 1 m according to the relation T = 400 − 100x where x is in m. Radiation emanates from a black wall at x = 0 maintained at a temperature of \(T_w = 400 K\). Determine the radiant flux leaving the gas slab at x = 1 m. Make use of the discrete ordinate method with \(S_8\) for solving this problem.

Admin
Solution

Step 1 With the given data, we may calculate the intensity of radiation leaving he black wall at x = 0 as \(I_{w}^{+}=\frac{\sigma T_{w}^{4}}{\pi}=\frac{5.67 \times 10^{-8} \times 400^4}{\pi}=462.0.3 {W}/{m^2sr}\).

Step 2 We divide the slab into 10 elemental slabs of thickness \(\Delta _{x}=\frac{L}{10}=\frac{1}{10}=0.1m\). The optical thickness of the slab is \(\tau _{L}=kL=0.5 \times 1=0.5\) and that of an elemental slab is \(\Delta \tau =kL=0.5 \times 0.1=0.05\).

Step 3 We associate the gas temperature with the mid-point of each elemental slab. The locations and temperature are calculated based on the linear temperature variation specified in the problem and are tabulated as given below.

x, m 0.05 0.15 0.25 0.35 0.45 0.55 0.65 0.75 0.85 0.95
\(T_{j}\), K 395 385 375 365 355 345 335 325 315 305

Note that the temperature profile is approximated by a staircase-type profile that is familiar to us from the finite difference method presented in conduction heat transfer.
Step 4 Consider the very first element next to the black wall. We calculate the intensities at Δx = 0.1 m along the four discrete ordinate directions using Eq. 11.81: \(I_{\Delta x}^{+}\left(\mu _{i}\right)=\frac{\sigma T_{w}^{4}}{\pi}e^{-\frac{k\Delta x}{\mu _{i}}}+\frac{\sigma T_{g,1}^{4}}{\pi}\left(1-e^{-\frac{k\Delta x}{\mu _{i}}}\right)\). For example, along \(\mu _{1}=0.14226\) we have

\(I_{\Delta x}^{+}\left(\mu _{1}\right)=\frac{\sigma T_{w}^{4}}{\pi}e^{-\frac{k\Delta x}{\mu _{1}}}+\frac{\sigma T_{g,1}^{4}}{\pi}\left(1-e^{-\frac{k\Delta x}{\mu _{1}}}\right) =462.03 \times e^{-\frac{0.5 \times 0.1}{0.14226}}+\frac{5.67 \times 10^{-8} \times 395^4}{\pi}\left(1-e^{-\frac{0.5 \times 0.1}{0.14226}}\right)=455.31 {W}/{m^2sr} \)

Similarly, we get the following values along the other discrete ordinates.

\(\mu _{2} = 0.57735\) \(I_{\Delta x}^{+}\left(\mu _{2}\right) = 460.15 W/m^2 sr\)
\(\mu _{3} = 0.80401\) \(I_{\Delta x}^{+}\left(\mu _{3}\right)= 460.67 W/m^2 sr\)
\(\mu _{4} = 0.97955\) \(I_{\Delta x}^{+}\left(\mu _{4}\right)= 460.90 W/m^2 sr\)

Step 5 We make use of these and Eq. 11.79: \(I_{x+\Delta x}^{+}\left(\mu _{i}\right)=I_{x}^{+}\left(\mu _{i}\right)e^{-\frac{k\Delta x}{\mu _{i}}}+\frac{\sigma T_{g,j^{(x)}}^{4}}{\pi}\left(1-e^{-\frac{k\Delta x}{\mu _{i}}}\right)\) to calculate the corresponding values at x = 2Δx. For example, along \(μ_1 = 0.14226\) we have

\(I_{2\Delta x}^{+}\left(\mu _{1}\right)=I_{\Delta x}^{+}\left(\mu _{1}\right)e^{-\frac{k\Delta x}{\mu _{1}}}+\frac{\sigma T_{g,2}^{4}}{\pi}\left(1-e^{-\frac{k\Delta x}{\mu _{1}}}\right) =455.31 \times e^{-\frac{0.5 \times 0.1}{0.14226}}+\frac{5.67 \times 10^{-8} \times 385^4}{\pi}\left(1-e^{-\frac{0.5 \times 0.1}{0.14226}}\right)=450.59{W}/{m^2sr} \)

Similarly, we calculate for the other ordinate directions also.

Step 7 The radiant flux leaving the slab at x = 1 m is obtained as a weighted sum of the intensities shown in the last row of the table.

\(q^+_{L}=\sum^4_{i=1}{I^+\left(\mu_{i}\right)\mu_{i}w_{i}}=241.55 \times 0.14226 \times 2.16371+ 361.17 \times 0.57735 \times 2.64070 + 384.26 \times 0.80401 \times 0.79382+ 396.03 \times 0.97955 \times 0.68494 = 1135.95 W/m^2\)
