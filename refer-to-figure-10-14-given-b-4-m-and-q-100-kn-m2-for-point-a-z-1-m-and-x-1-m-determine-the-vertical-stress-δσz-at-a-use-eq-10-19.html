Guest Question.

Refer to Figure 10.14. Given: B = 4 m and q 100 kN/\(m^{2}\). For point A, z = 1 m and x = 1 m. Determine the vertical stress \(\Delta \sigma _{z} \)at A. Use Eq. (10.19).

Admin
Solution

Since x=1 m < B/2 =2 m,

\(\Delta \sigma _{z}= \frac{q}{\pi } \left\{tan^{-1}[\frac{z}{x-(\frac{B}{2} )} ]+\pi -tan^{-1}[\frac{z}{x+(\frac{B}{2} )} ]-\frac{Bz[x^{2}z^{2} -(\frac{B^{2} }{4} )]}{ [x^{2}z^{2}-(\frac{B^{2} }{4} ) ]^{2}+B^{2} z^{2} } \right\}\)

 

\(tan^{-1} [\frac{z}{x-(\frac{B}{2} )} ]=tan^{-1}(\frac{1}{1-2} )=-45°=-0.785 rad\)

 

\(tan^{-1} [\frac{z}{x+(\frac{B}{2} )} ]=tan^{-1}(\frac{1}{1+2} )=18.43° = 0.322 rad\)

 

\(\frac{Bz[x^{2}-z^{2} -(\frac{B^{2} }{4} ) ]}{[x^{2}+z^{2} -(\frac{B^{2} }{4} )]^{2}+B^{2}z^{2} } = \frac{(4)(1) [(1)^{2}-(1)^{2}-(\frac{16}{4} ) ]}{[(1)^{2}+1)^{2}-(\frac{16}{4} ) ]^{2}+(16)(1) }=-0.8\)

Hence,

\(\frac{\Delta \sigma _{z}}{q} =\frac{1}{\pi } [-0.785+ \pi -0.322-(-0.8)]=0.902\)

Now, compare with Table 10.4. For this case, \(\frac{2x}{B} =\frac{(2)(1)}{4} =0.5\) and \(\frac{2z}{B} =\frac{(2)(1)}{4} =0.5\)

So,\( \frac{\Delta \sigma _{z} }{q} =0.902\) (Check)

\(\Delta \sigma _{z}=0.902q = (0.902)(100) = 90.2 kN/m^{2}\)
