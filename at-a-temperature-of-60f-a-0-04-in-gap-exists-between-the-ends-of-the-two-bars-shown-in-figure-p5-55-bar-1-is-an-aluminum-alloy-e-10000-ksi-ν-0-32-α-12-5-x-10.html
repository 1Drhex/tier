Guest Question.

At a temperature of 60°F, a 0.04-in. gap exists between the ends of the two bars shown in Figure P5.55. Bar (1) is an aluminum alloy [E = 10,000 ksi, ν = 0.32, α = 12.5 × \(10^{-6}\)/°F] bar with a width of 3 in. and a thickness of 0.75 in. Bar (2) is a stainless steel [E = 28,000 ksi, ν = 0.12, α = 9.6 × \(10^{-6}\)/°F] bar with a width of 2 in. and a thickness of 0.75 in. The supports at A and C are rigid. Determine:
(a) the lowest temperature at which the two bars contact each other.
(b) the normal stress in the two bars at a temperature of 250°F.
(c) the normal strain in the two bars at 250°F.
(d) the change in width of the aluminum bar at a temperature of 250°F.

Admin
Solution

(a) Lowest Contact Temperature
Before the gap is closed, only thermal strains and the associated axial elongations exist. Write expressions for the temperature-induced elongations and set this equal to the 0.04-in. gap:

\(\begin{aligned}&\alpha_{1} \Delta T L_{1}+\alpha_{2} \Delta T L_{2}=0.04 \text { in. } \\&\Delta T\left[\alpha_{1} L_{1}+\alpha_{2} L_{2}\right]=0.04 \text { in. } \\&\therefore \Delta T=\frac{0.04 \text { in. }}{\alpha_{1} L_{1}+\alpha_{2} L_{2}}=\frac{0.04 \text { in. }}{\left(12.5 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} F \right)(32 in .)+\left(9.6 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} F \right)(44 in .)}=48.6381^{\circ} F\end{aligned}\)

Since the initial temperature is 60°F, the temperature at which the gap is closed is 108.6°F.

(b) Equilibrium
From the results obtained for part (a), we know that the gap will be closed at 250°F, making this a statically indeterminate axial configuration. Knowing this, consider a FBD at joint B. Assume that both internal axial forces will be tension, even though we know intuitively that both \(F_{1} \text { and } F_{2}\) will turn out to be compression.

\(\Sigma F_{x}=-F_{1}+F_{2}=0\) (a)

Geometry of Deformations Relationship
For this configuration, the sum of the deformations of members (1) and (2) must equal the initial gap:

\(\delta_{1}+\delta_{2}=0.04 \text { in .}\) (b)

Force-Temperature-Deformation Relationships
The relationship between the internal force, temperature change, and the deformation of an axial member can be stated for members (1) and (2):

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\alpha_{1} \Delta T_{1} L_{1} \quad \delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}+\alpha_{2} \Delta T_{2} L_{2}\) (c)

Compatibility Equation
Substitute the force-temperature-deformation relationships (c) into the geometry of deformation relationship (b) to derive the compatibility equation:

\(\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\alpha_{1} \Delta T_{1} L_{1}+\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}+\alpha_{2} \Delta T_{2} L_{2}=0.04 \text { in. }\) (d)

Solve the Equations
This part is no fun, but it must be done. From Eq. (a), \(F_{1}=F_{2}\) . Substituting this into Eq. (d) gives:

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\alpha_{1} \Delta T_{1} L_{1}+\frac{F_{1} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}+\alpha_{2} \Delta T_{2} L_{2} &=0.04 \text { in. } \\\qquad \begin{aligned}\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\frac{F_{1} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}&=0.04 \text { in. }-\alpha_{1} \Delta T_{1} L_{1}-\alpha_{2} \Delta T_{2} L_{2} \\F_{1}\left[\frac{L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}\right] &=0.04 \text { in. }-\alpha_{1} \Delta T_{1} L_{1}-\alpha_{2} \Delta T_{2} L_{2} \\F_{1} &=\frac{0.04 \text { in. }-\alpha_{1} \Delta T_{1} L_{1}-\alpha_{2} \Delta T_{2} L_{2}}{\left[\frac{L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}\right]} (e)\end{aligned}\end{aligned}\)

For this structure, the lengths, areas, and elastic moduli are given below. The temperature change is the same for both members; therefore, \(\Delta T_{1}=\Delta T_{2}=\Delta T=190^{\circ} F\) :

\(\begin{aligned}&L_{1}=32 \text { in. } \\&A_{1}=(3 \text { in. })(0.75 \text { in. })=2.25 in .^{2} \\&E_{1}=10,000 ksi \\&\alpha_{1}=12.5 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} F\end{aligned}\) \(\begin{aligned}L_{2} &=44 \text { in. } \\A_{2} &=(2 \text { in. })(0.75 in .)=1.50 in .^{2} \\E_{2} &=28,000 ksi \\\alpha_{2} &=9.6 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} F\end{aligned}\)

Substitute these values into Eq. (e) and calculate \(F_{1}=-47.0702 \text { kips. }\) Backsubstitute into Eq. (a) to calculate \(F_{2}=-47.0702 \text { kips. }\) Note that the internal forces are compression, as expected.

Normal Stresses
The normal stresses in each axial member can now be calculated:

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{1}=\frac{F_{1}}{A_{1}}=\frac{-47.0702 \text { kips }}{2.25 in .^{2}}=-20.9201 ksi =20.9 ksi ( C ) \\&\sigma_{2}=\frac{F_{2}}{A_{2}}=\frac{-47.0702 kips }{1.50 in .^{2}}=-31.3801 ksi =31.4 ksi ( C )\end{aligned}\)

(c) Normal Strains
The force-temperature-deformation relationships were expressed in Eq. (b). By definition, \(\varepsilon=\delta /L\). Therefore, the normal strain for each axial member can be determined by dividing the relationships in Eq. (c) by the respective member lengths:

\(\varepsilon_{1}=\frac{F_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\alpha_{1} \Delta T_{1} \quad \varepsilon_{2}=\frac{F_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}+\alpha_{2} \Delta T_{2}\) (f)

Substitute the appropriate values to calculate the normal strains in each member:

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{1} &=\frac{F_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+\alpha_{1} \Delta T_{1} \\&=\frac{-47.0702 kips }{\left(2.25 in .^{2}\right)(10,000 ksi )}+\left(12.5 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} F \right)\left(190^{\circ} F \right) \\&=0.000283 in . / in .=283 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{2} &=\frac{F_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}+\alpha_{2} \Delta T_{2} \\&=\frac{-47.0702 kips }{\left(1.50 in .^{2}\right)(28,000 ksi )}+\left(9.6 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} F \right)\left(190^{\circ} F \right) \\&=0.000703 in . / in .=703 \mu \varepsilon\end{aligned}\)

 

(d) The change in width of the aluminum bar (1) is caused partly by the Poisson effect and partly by the temperature change. The longitudinal strain in the aluminum bar caused by the internal force \(F_{1}\) is:

\(\varepsilon_{\text {long }, \sigma}=\frac{F_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}=\frac{-47.0702 kips }{\left(2.25 in .^{2}\right)(10,000 ksi )}=-2,092.01 \times 10^{-6} \text { in./in. }\)

The accompanying lateral strain due to the Poisson effect is thus

\(\varepsilon_{ lat , \sigma}=-v \varepsilon_{\text {long } \sigma}=-(0.32)\left(-2,092.01 \times 10^{-6} in . / in .\right)=669.44 \times 10^{-6} in . / in\)

The lateral strain caused by the temperature change is

\(\varepsilon_{\text {lat } T}=\alpha_{1} \Delta T_{1}=\left(12.5 \times 10^{-6} /{ }^{\circ} F \right)\left(190^{\circ} F \right)=2,375 \times 10^{-6} in . / in .\)

Therefore, the total lateral strain in aluminum bar (1) is

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{\text {lat }} &=\varepsilon_{\text {lat, } \sigma}+\varepsilon_{\text {lat, } T} \\&=669.44 \times 10^{-6} \text { in. } /\text { in. }+2,375 \times 10^{-6} \text { in./in. }=3,044.44 \times 10^{-6} \text { in. } / \text { in. }\end{aligned}\)

The change is width of the aluminum bar is thus

\(\left.\Delta \text { width }=\varepsilon_{\text {lat }} \text { (width }\right)=\left(3,044.44 \times 10^{-6} \text { in./in. }\right)(3 \text { in. })=0.00913 \text { in. }\)
