Guest Question.

The specifications for the circuit shown below include the following:

\(v _{ i }=222 \sqrt{2} \sin 314 t , \alpha=45^{\circ}, R =20 \Omega, \text { and } L =10 mH\)

Find the relation of the circuit current and calculate the average output voltage and current.

 

Admin
Solution

Solution

Using Eq. (3.11) the circuit current is given by:

 

\(i _{ o }= i _{ i }= i _{ F }+ i _{ N }=\frac{\sqrt{2} \widetilde{ V }_{ i }}{\left| Z _{ o }\right|} \sin (\omega t -\varphi)+ Ae ^{-\left(\frac{\omega t }{\tau}\right)} \quad \text { for } \alpha \leq \omega t \leq \beta\) (3.11)

 

\(i _{0}=\frac{\sqrt{2} \widetilde{ V }_{ i }}{\left| Z _{ o }\right|}\left[\sin (\omega t -\varphi)-\sin (\alpha-\varphi) e ^{\left(\frac{\alpha-\omega t}{\omega \tau}\right)}\right]\) (1)

where

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\varphi=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{\omega L }{ R }\right)=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{314 \times 10 \times 10^{-3}}{20}\right)=9^{\circ} \\&\omega=314 rads / s \quad \alpha=45^{\circ} \text { or } 0.785 rads \\&\left| Z _{ o }\right|=\left[ R ^{2}+(\omega L )^{2}\right]^{1 / 2}=\left[20^{2}+\left(314 \times 10 \times 10^{-3}\right)^{2}\right]^{1 / 2}=20.24 \Omega \\&\tau=\frac{ L }{ R }=\frac{10 \times 10^{-3}}{20}=0.0005 s \quad \omega \tau=314 \times 0.0005=0.157 rads \quad \widetilde{ V }_{ i }=222 V\end{aligned}\)

 

Therefore, Eq. (1) becomes:

 

\(i _{o}=15.51\left[\sin \left(\omega t -9^{\circ}\right)-\sin \left(\alpha-9^{\circ}\right) e ^{\left(\frac{\alpha-\omega t}{\omega \tau}\right)}\right]\) (2)

 

Using the initial condition \(i _{0}(\omega t =\beta)=0\),

 

from Eq. (2) the following equation is found:

 

\(\sin \left(\beta-9^{\circ}\right)=\sin (\alpha-\varphi) e ^{\left(\frac{\alpha-\beta}{\omega \tau}\right)}\) (3)

 

Solving Eq. (3) with numerical methods, it is found that \(\beta=189^{\circ}\). The average output voltage and current are given by:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{V}_{o} &=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{\alpha}^{\beta} \sqrt{2} \widetilde{V}_{i} \sin (\omega t)(d \omega t)=\left.\frac{\sqrt{2} \widetilde{V}_{i}}{2 \pi}(-\cos \omega t)\right|_{\alpha} ^{\beta} \\&=\frac{222 \sqrt{2}}{2 \pi}\left(\cos 45^{\circ}-\cos 189^{\circ}\right)=84.7 V \quad \bar{I}_{o}=\frac{\bar{V}_{o}}{R}=\frac{84.7}{20}=4.23 A\end{aligned}\)
