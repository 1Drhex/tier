Guest Question.

Consider beam 6 of , which is a simply supported beam having a concentrated load F not in the center. Develop the deflection equations using singularity functions.

Admin
Solution

First, write the load intensity equation from the free-body diagram,
\(q = R1⟨x⟩^{−1} − F⟨x − a⟩^{−1} + R_{2}⟨x − l⟩^{−1}\) (1)

Integrating Equation (1) twice results in
\(V = R_{1}⟨x⟩^{0} − F⟨x − a⟩^{0} + R_{2}⟨x − l⟩^{0} \)(2)
\(M = R_{1}⟨x⟩^{1} − F⟨x − a⟩^{1} + R_{2}⟨x − l⟩^{1} \)(3)
Recall that as long as the q equation is complete, integration constants are unnecessary for V and M; therefore, they are not included up to this point. From statics, setting V = M = 0 for x slightly greater than l yields \(R_{1} = Fb∕l and R_{2} = Fa∕l.\) Thus Equation (3) becomes

\( M =\frac{Fb}{l}\left\langle x\right\rangle ^{1}- − F⟨x − a⟩^{1} +\frac{Fa}{l} ⟨x − l⟩^{1}\)

Integrating Equations (4–12) and (4–13) as indefinite integrals gives

\( EI\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{Fb}{2l} \left\langle x\right\rangle^{2} -\frac{F}{2} \left\langle x-1 \right\rangle ^{2}+\frac{Fa}{2l}\left\langle ⟨x − l\right\rangle ^{2}+ C_{1} \)

 

\( EI y=\frac{Fb}{6l} \left\langle x\right\rangle^{3} -\frac{F}{6} \left\langle x-1 \right\rangle ^{3}+\frac{Fa}{2l}\left\langle ⟨x − l\right\rangle ^{3}+ C_{1} x+ C_{2} \)

Note that the first singularity term in both equations always exists, so\( ⟨x⟩^{2} = x^{2} and ⟨x⟩^{3} = x^{3}\) . Also, the last singularity term in both equations does not exist until x = l, where it is zero, and since there is no beam for x > l we can drop the last term. Thus,

\( EI\frac{dy}{dx}= \frac{Fb}{2l} x^{2}-\frac{F}{2}\left\langle x-a\right\rangle^{2} +C_{1} \) (4)

 

\( EI y=\frac{Fb}{6l} x^{3} -\frac{F}{6} \left\langle x-a \right\rangle ^{3}+ C_{1}x+ C_{2}\) (5)

The constants of integration \(C_{1} and C_{2}\) are evaluated by using the two boundary conditions y = 0 at x = 0 and y = 0 at x = l. The first condition, substituted into Equation (5), gives \(C_{2} = 0\) (recall that\( ⟨0 − a⟩^{3} = 0\)). The second condition, substituted into Equation (5), yields

\( 0= \frac{Fb}{6l}l^{3} – \frac{F}{6}(l-a) ^{3}+ C_{1}l= \frac{Fbl^{2}}{6} -\frac{Fb^{3}}{6}+ C_{1}l \)

Solving for\( C_{1}\) gives

\( C_{1} = −\frac{Fb}{6!} (l^{2} − b^{2})\)

Finally, substituting \( C_{1} and C_{2}\) in Equation (5) and simplifying produces

\( y=\frac{F}{6EIl} \left[bx(x^{2} + b^{2} − l^{2})-l⟨x − a⟩^{3}\right] \)

Comparing Equation (6) with the two deflection equations for beam 6 in , we note that the use of singularity functions enables us to express the deflection equation with a single discontinuous equation. For x ≤ a, the singularity function in Equation (6) does not exist, and we see that Equation (6) perfectly agrees with \(y_{BC}\) from Table A–9. However, for x ≥ a, Equation (6) appears different from \(y_{BC}\) . This is because Equation (6) uses a, b, and l, whereas \(y_{BC}\) only uses a and l. Substituting b = l − a into Equation (6) and expanding, we get

\( y=-\frac{Fa}{6EIl} (x^{3} − 3lx^{2} + 2l ^{2}x + a^{2}x − la^{2})\)

Expanding \(y_{BC}\) from yields the same results
