Guest Question.

A staggered tube bank consists of seven rows of 3/4 standard type K copper tubes. The bank is used to
recover heat from water that flows to a nearby stream from an industrial plant. The bank is located
in an air duct, as illustrated in Figure 7.32. Air passing over the bank has a velocity of 12 ft /s and a
temperature of \(70^{\circ }\) F.The pitch spacings of the bank are \(S_{L} =1.5\) in and \(S_{T} =1.3\) in. Water flowing within the tubes elevates the tube outside-surface temperature to\(200^{\circ }\) F. Calculate the pressure drop for the air passing over the tubes and the heat transferred to the air. The tube lengths are all 2 ft .

Admin
Solution

The air receives heat from the tubes, and the air temperature increases as air passes through.

Assumptions

1. The system is at steady state.
2. Air properties are constant.
3. Radiation effects are negligible

From Appendix Table D.1, for air at \(70+460=530^{\circ }R\approx 540^{\circ } R\) ,we read

 

\(\rho =0.0735lbm/ft^{3}\)

 

\(c_{p}=0.240 BTU/\left(lbm.R^{\circ } \right)\)

 

\(\nu =16.88\times 10^{-5}ft^{2}/s\)

 

\(k_{f} =0.01516 BTU/\left(hr.ft.R^{\circ } \right)\)

 

\(\alpha =0.859ft^{2} /hr\)

 

Pr=0.708

 

From Appendix Table F.2, for 3/4 standard type K copper tubing

OD=0.875 in.

ID=0.06208 ft

\(A=0.003028 ft^{2}\)

 

It is necessary to evaluate \(V_{max}\) for the bank. Referring to Figure 7.33, we perform a mass flow balance for the flow entering the bank between two adjacent tubes. The inlet flow area is \(S_{T}\)(L), where

 

L is the tube length into the page. The velocity at this area is \(V_{\infty } =12 ft/s\). The area labeled \(A_{1}\) has dimensions of \(\left(S_{T}-D_{o} \right)L\). We therefore write for constant air density

\(V_{\infty } \left(S_{T}L \right)=V_{1} A_{1}=V_{1} \left(S_{T}-D_{o} \right) L\)

 

Canceling L and solving for \(V_{1}\) gives

\(V_{1} =\frac{S_{T}V_{\infty } }{S_{T}-D_{o} } =\frac{\left(1.3/12\right)\left(12\right) }{1.3/12-0.875/12}=36.7ft/s\)

 

Now the flow that enters between two tubes and travels through \(A_{1}\) is halved; that is, only half the flow at \(A_{1}\) passes through the area labeled \(A_{2}\) in Figure 7.33. The diagonal pitch is calculated as

 

\(S_{D} =\left[S^{2}_{L}+\left\lgroup\frac{S_{T} }{2} \right\rgroup ^{2} \right] ^{1/2} = \left[1.5^{2}+\left\lgroup\frac{1.3 }{2} \right\rgroup ^{2} \right] ^{1/2}=1.64 in.\)

 

A mass flow balance between \(A_{2}\) and the upstream conditions gives

 

\(\frac{1}{2}V_{\infty }\left(S_{T}L \right) =V_{2}\left(S_{D}-D_{o} \right)L\)

 

Solving for \(V_{2}\) gives

\(V_{2}=\frac{S_{T}V_{\infty } }{2\left(S_{D}-D_{o}\right) } =\frac{1.3\left(12\right) }{2\left(1.64-0.875\right) } =10.2 ft/s\)

 

Because \(V_{1} \gt V_{2}\),we use \(V_{1}\) as \(V_{max}\) in the equations. The Reynolds number is

 

\(Re_{D} =\frac{V_{max}D_{o} }{\nu } =\frac{36.7\left(0.875/12\right) }{16.88\times 10^{-5} } =1.59\times 10^{4}\)

 

With \(S_{T}/D_{o}=1.3/0.875=1.49\) ,Figure 7.31 shows, at the preceding Reynolds number

\(f_{1}=0.35\)

 

For \(S_{T}/S_{L}=1.3/1.5=0.87\) we also read

\(f_{2}=1.05\)

 

The pressure drop is calculated with Equation 7.44.

\(\Delta P=Nf_{1}f_{2}\left\lgroup\frac{\rho V^{2}_{max} }{2g_{c} } \right\rgroup\)

 

\(\Delta P=7\left(0.35\right)\left(1.05\right) \left(0.0735\right)\left(36.7\right) ^{2} /\left[2\left(32.2\right) \right]\)

 

\(\Delta P= 3.95 lbf/ft^{2} =0.0275psi\)

The convection coefficient is found with Equation 7.46, but we must first determine the constants \(C_{1}, C_{2}\) ,and m. Referring to Table 7.6, we obtain the following values for \(S_{T}/D_{o}=1.49 and S_{L}/D_{o}=1.7:\)

 

\(C_{1}\approx 0.438\) \(m\approx 0.565\) \(C_{2}=0.97\)

 

Equation 7.46 gives

 

\(\frac{\overline{h} _{c} D_{o} }{\overline{k} _{f} } =1.13C_{1}C_{2}Re^{m}_{D} Pr^{1/3}\)

 

\(\overline{h} _{c} =\frac{0.01516}{0.875/12} 1.13\left(0.438\right)\left(0.97\right) \left(1.59\times 10^{4} \right) ^{0.565} \left(0.708\right) ^{1/3} \)

 

or

\(\overline{h} _{c}=21.0 BTU/\left(hr.ft^{2}.R^{\circ } \right)\)

 

The heat transferred then becomes

\(q=\overline{h} _{c}A_{s}\left(T_{w}-T_{\infty } \right) \)

where \(A_{s}\) is the outside surface area of 70 tubes, evaluated as

 

\(A_{s}=70\pi D_{o}L=70\pi \left(0.875\right)\left(12\right) \left(2\right) =32.1ft^{2} \)

Thus

\(q=21.0\left(32.1\right) \left(200-70\right) \)

 

or

\(\underline{q=8.76\times 10^{4} BTU/hr }\)
