Guest Question.

Select an unequal-leg angle tension member 15 feet long to resist a service dead load of 35 kips and a service live load of 70 kips. Use A36 steel. The connection is shown in Figure 3.25.

Admin
Solution

The factored load is

\(P_{u} = 1.2D +1.6 L= 1.2(35) +1.6(70) = 154\) kips

Required \(A_{g} =\frac{P_{u} }{\phi _{t}F_{y} } =\frac{154}{0.90(36)} =4.75\) \(in.^{2} \)

 

Required \(A_{e} =\frac{P_{u} }{\phi _{t}F_{u} } =\frac{154}{0.75(58)} =3.54\) \(in.^{2} \)

The radius of gyration should be at least

\(\frac{L}{300} =\frac{15(12)}{300} =0.6\) in.

To find the lightest shape that satisfies these criteria, we search the dimensions and properties table for the unequal-leg angle that has the smallest acceptable gross area and then check the effective net area. The radius of gyration can be checked by inspection. There are two lines of bolts, so the connected leg must be at least 5 inches long (see the usual gages for angles in Figure 3.24). Starting at either end of the table, we find that the shape with the smallest area that is at least equal to 4.75 \(in.^{2}\) is an L6 × 4 × 1⁄2 with an area of 4.75 \(in.^{2}\) and a minimum radius of gyration of 0.864 in.

Try L6 × 4 × 1⁄2.

\(A_{n} = A_{g} − A_{holes} =4.75-2(\frac{3}{4}+\frac{1}{8} )(\frac{1}{2} )=3.875\) \(in.^{2}\)

Because the length of the connection is not known, Equation 3.1 \(U=1-\frac{\overline{x}}{\ell}\) cannot be used to compute the shear lag factor U. Since there are four bolts in the direction of the load, we will use the alternative value of U = 0.80.

\(A_{e} = A_{n}U = 3.875(0.80) = 3.10\) \(in.^{2}\) \(< 3.54\) \(in.^{2} \) (N.G.)

Try the next larger shape from the dimensions and properties tables.

Try L5 × 3 1⁄2 × 5⁄8 \((A_{g} = 4.93\) \(in.^{2}\) and \(r_{min} = 0.746\) in.)

 

\(A_{n} = A_{g} − A_{holes} =4.93-2(\frac{3}{4}+\frac{1}{8} )(\frac{5}{8} )=3.836\) \(in.^{2}\)

\(A_{e} = A_{n}U = 3.836(0.80) = 3.07\) \(in.^{2}\)\( < 3.54\) \(in.^{2} \) (N.G.)

(Note that this shape has slightly more gross area than that produced by the previous trial shape, but because of the greater leg thickness, slightly more area is deducted for the holes.) Passing over the next few heavier shapes,
Try L8 × 4 × 1⁄2 \((A_{g} = 5.80\) \(in.^{2}\) and \(r_{min} = 0.863\) in.)

\(A_{n} = A_{g} − A_{holes} =5.80-2(\frac{3}{4}+\frac{1}{8} )(\frac{1}{2} )=4.925\) \(in.^{2}\)

\(A_{e} = A_{n}U = 4.925(0.80) = 3.94\) \(in.^{2}\) \(> 3.54\) \(in.^{2}\) (OK)

 

This shape satisfies all requirements, so use an L8 × 4 × 1⁄2.
The total service load is

\(P_{a}=D+L=35+70=105\) kips

Required \(A_{g} =\frac{P_{a} }{F_{t} } =\frac{P_{a} }{0.6F_{y} }=\frac{105}{0.6(36)} =4.86\) \(in.^{2}\)

 

\(Required A_{e} =\frac{P_{a} }{0.5F_{u} } =\frac{105}{0.5(58)} =3.62\) \(in.^{2}\)

Required \( r_{min} =\frac{L }{300 } =\frac{15(12)}{300} =0.6\) \(in.^{2} \)

 

Try L8 × 4 × 1⁄2 \((A_{g} = 5.80\) \( in.^{2}\) and \(r_{min} = 0.863\) in.). For a shear lag factor U of 0.80,

\(A_{n} = A_{g} − A_{holes} =5.80-2(\frac{3}{4}+\frac{1}{8} )(\frac{1}{2} )=4.925\) \(in.^{2}\)

\( A_{e} = A_{n}U = 4.925(0.80) = 3.94\) \( in.^{2}\) \(> 3.62\) \( in.^{2}\) (OK)

This shape satisfies all requirements, so use an L8 × 4 × 1⁄2.
