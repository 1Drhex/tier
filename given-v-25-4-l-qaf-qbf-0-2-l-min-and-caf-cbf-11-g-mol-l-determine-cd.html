Guest Question.

Given V = 25.4 L, \( q_{AF}=q_{BF} \)= 0.2 L/min, and \( C_{AF}=C_{BF}\) = 11 gmol/L, determine \( C_{D} \) .

Admin
Solution

This example is the same as except that the flow rate
is doubled. Then, as before,

\(\Theta =\frac{V}{q_{AF} +q_{AF} } \)=63.5 min,

\(C_{A} =C_{B} \),

\(C_{D}=11-2C_{B} \).

The quadratic for \(C_{A}\) , Eq. (2.3.20)

\(0=q_{AF} +C_{AF} -qC_{A} -rV\) , is now

\(0=0.2\times 11-(0.2+0.2)C_{A}-(6.05\times 10^{-4} )C^{2}_{A} (25.4)\),

\(C_{A}=C_{B} =4.70 g-mol/L\),

\(C_{D}=1.60\).

This calculation can be easily repeated for various flow rates. Table 2.4 shows how \(C_{A}\) is affected by changing the flow \(q_{AF}+q_{BF} \) for \(q_{AF}=q_{BF}\) , \(C_{AF} =C_{BF} \) =11, V = 25.4. As we would expect, the amount of sulfuric acid converted to product
decreases as the reactor holding time \(\Theta \) decreases. Examination of the equations shows that the same effect is obtained if the flow rates are held constant and the feed concentrations of A and B are decreased.
