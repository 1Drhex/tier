Guest Question.

A one-story frame structure serves as a laboratory. The structure is composed of two beams and a rigid girder. The structure is modeled as a SDOF system with m = 1000 kg and \(k=\times10^{6}N/m (\omega _{n}=94.9rad/s)\). The force from an explosion is modeled by the pulse shown in Figure 5.27(a). Unfortunately, an explosion occurs, and that explosion triggers a second explosion at t = 0.07 s, later, which lasts twice as long. The force is approximately that of Figure 5.27(b). What is the maximum displacement of the structure?

Admin
Solution

The mathematical model for the dual explosions is

\(F(t)=50,000(1-20t)\left[u(t)-u(t-0.05)\right] \) (a)

\( +50,000(1.7-10t)\left[u(t-0.07)-u(t-0.17)\right] \)

The response of the system can be obtained using the convolution integral or Table 5.1 and the superposition formula

TABLE 5.1
Response of an undamped SDOF to common forms of
excitation
Delayed impulse
Excitation: \(F(t)=A\delta (t-t_{0})\)
Response: \(m_{eq}\omega ^{2}_{n}x(t)/ A=\omega _{n}\sin \omega _{n}(t-t_{0})u(t-t_{0})\)
Delayed step function
Excitation: \(F(t ) = Au(t – t_{0})\)
Response: \( m_{eq}\omega^{2}_{n}x(t)/A=[1-\cos \omega_{n} (t-t_{0})] u (t-t_{0})\)
Delayed ramp function
Excitation: \(F(t)=(At+B)u(t-t_{0})\)
Response: \(m_{eq}\omega^{2}_{n}x(t)/A=[t+B/A – (t_{0}+B/A)\cos \omega_{n} (t-t_{0})- \frac{1}{\omega _{n}}\sin \omega _{n}(t – t_{0})]u(t – t_{0})\)
Delayed exponential function
Excitation: \(F(t) = Ae ^{-\alpha (t – t_{0})}u(t – t_{0})\)
Response: \(m_{eq} \omega^{2}_{n}x(t)/A=[e^{-\alpha(t -t_{0})}+\alpha/\omega_{n}\sin \omega_{n}(t-t_{0})\\ -\cos\omega_{n}(t-t_{0})]/(1+\alpha^{2}/\omega^{2}_{n}) u (t-t_{0})\)
Delayed sine function:
Excitation:\(F(t) = A \sin[\omega (t – t_{0})]u(t – t_{0})\)
Response: \(\begin{matrix} \frac{m_{eq}\omega ^{2}_{n}xt}{A} =\frac{1}{2}\left\{\left(\frac{1}{\omega /\omega _{n}-1} \right)[\sin\omega (t-t_{0})-\sin\omega _{n}(t-t_{0})]\right. \\ \left. -\left(\frac{1}{\omega /\omega _{n}+1} \right)[\sin \omega (t-t_{0})+\sin\omega _{n}(t-t_{0})] \right\} u(t-t_{0}) \end{matrix} \)
This table provides the response of an undamped SDOF system to common forms of excitation. Man forms of excitation can be written as combinations of the excitations whose system responses are provided in the table. Superposition can be used to determine the response due to these excitations. In other cases, excitations can be approximated by combinations of excitations in this table. Then this table and superposition is used to approximate the response of an undamped SDOF system. The table provides the mathematical form of the excitation and response as well as graphical representations. In all cases, values of \(\omega_{n}= 10 rad/s\) and \(r_{0} = 0.5 s\) were used to generate the graphs. The values of specific parameters used for specific excitations are given.

\(x(t)=F_{0}\left[x_{a}(t)-x_{b}(t)+x_{c}(t)x-{d}(t)\right] \) (b)

Where \(F_{0}=50,000 N\) and \(x_{a}(t)\) is the response due to \((1-20t)u(t) \) or the response due to a delayed ramp function with A = -20, B = 1, and \(t_{0} \)

\(x_{a}(t)=\frac{-20}{m\omega ^{2}_{n}}\left(t-\frac{1}{20}+\frac{1}{20}\cos\omega _{n}t -\frac{1}{\omega _{n}}\sin\omega _{n}t \right)\) (c)

  • \(x_b(t)\) is the response due to (1 – 20t)u(t – 0.05) or the response due to a delayed ramp function with A = -20, B = 1, and \(t_{0} =0.05 \)

\( x_{c}(t)=\frac{-10}{m\omega ^{2}_{n}}\left[t- \frac{1.7}{10}-(0.07-0.17)\cos\omega _{n}(t-0.07)-\frac{1}{\omega _{n}}\sin\omega _{n}(t-0.07) \right]u(t-0.07) \) (e)

  • \( X_{d}(t)\) is the response due to (1.7 – 10t)u(t – 0.17) or the response due to a delayed ramp function with A = -10, B = 1.7, and \( t_{0}=0.07: \)

\(x_{d}(t)=\frac{-10}{m\omega ^{2}_{n}}\left[t-\frac{1.7}{10}-\frac{1}{\omega _{n}}\sin\omega _{n}(t-0.17) \right]u(t-0.17) \) (f)

Thus,

\( x(t)= -0.055\begin{cases}2(t-0.05+0.05\cos94.9t-0.0105\sin94.9)u(t)\end{cases} \)

\(\begin{matrix}-2\left[t-0.05-0.0105\sin(94.9t-4.745)\right. \\ \\\left. -0.0105\sin(94.9t-6.643) \right] u(t-0.07) \end{matrix} \)

\(-\left[t-0.17-0.0105\sin(94.9t-16.133)\right]u(t-0.17)\left. \right\} \)

(g)

The maximum of the absolute value of the displacement is determined as 16.0 mm, as shown in Figure 5.27(c).
