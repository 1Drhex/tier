Guest Question.

A gray, nonscattering medium with refractive index n = 1 is contained between two parallel, gray plates. The medium is isothermal at temperature \(T_{m}\) with constant absorption coefficient K. The two plates are both isothermal at temperature \(T_{w}\), have the same gray-diffuse emittance 6, and are spaced a distance L apart. Determine the radiative heat flux between the plates as well as its divergence.

Admin
Solution

For a nonscattering medium (\(σ_{s}\)=0 or w= 0), equation (13.59) \(\Phi (\tau )=(1-w)\frac{\pi I_{b}(\tau )-J_{2}}{J_{1}-J_{2}}+\frac{w}{2}\left[E_{2}(\tau )+\int_{0}^{\tau _{L}}{}\Phi (\tau )E_{1}(\left|\tau ^{\prime }-\tau \right| )d\tau ^{\prime } \right]\) becomes superfluous and the flux may be determined directly from equations (13.55) \(q(\tau )=2J_{1}E_{3}(\tau )-2_{2}E_{3}(\tau _{L}-\tau )+2\pi \int_{0}^{\tau }{}S(\tau ^{\prime })E_{2}(\tau -\tau ^{\prime })d \tau ^{\prime }-2\pi \int_{\tau }^{\tau_{L} }{}S(\tau ^{\prime })E_{2}(\tau ^{\prime }-\tau )d \tau ^{\prime }\) or (13.60) \(\Psi (\tau )=\frac{q(\tau )}{J_{1}-J_{2}}=2\left[E_{3}(\tau )+\int_{0}^{\tau }\Phi (\tau ^{\prime })E_{2}(\tau -\tau ^{\prime })d\tau ^{\prime }{} – \int_{\tau }^{\tau_{L} }\Phi (\tau ^{\prime })E_{2}( \tau ^{\prime }-\tau )d\tau ^{\prime }\right]\) . From equation (13.56) \(S(\tau )=(1-w)I_{b}(\tau )+\frac{w}{4\pi }G(\tau )\)\(\pi S(\tau )=\pi I_{bm}=\sigma T^{4}_{m}= const\). Therefore, from equation (13.55) with τ= κz ,

\(q(\tau )=2J_{w}E_{3}(\tau )-2J_{w}E_{3}(\tau _{L}-\tau )+2\sigma T^{4}_{m}\int_{0}^{\tau }{}E_{2}(\tau -\tau ^{\prime }) d\tau ^{\prime }-2\sigma T^{4}_{m}\int_{\tau }^{\tau _{L}}{}E_{2}(\tau ^{\prime }-\tau )d\tau ^{\prime }\)

\(=2J_{w}E_{3}(\tau )-2J_{w}E_{3}(\tau _{L}-\tau )+2\sigma T^{4}_{m}\left[E_{3}(\tau -\tau ^{\prime }|^{r}_{0})+E_{3}(\tau ^{\prime }-\tau )|^{\tau _{L}}_{\tau }\right]\)

\(=(J_{w}-\sigma T^{4}_{m})2[E_{3}(\tau )-E_{3}(\tau _{L}-\tau )]\)

where we have made use of the symmetry of the problem, i.e., \(J_{1}=J_{2}=J_{w}\) The necessary relationship between surface flux, temperature, and radiosity has been given by equation (13.44) \(q_{w}\cdot \widehat{n}=\frac{\varepsilon _{w}}{1-\varepsilon _{w}}(\pi I_{bw}-J_{w})\) , or for the plane slab at τ=0,

\(q(0)=\frac{\varepsilon }{1-\varepsilon }(\sigma T^{4}_{w}-J_{w})=(J_{w}-T^{4}_{m}) [1-2E_{3}(\tau _{L})]\).

Solving for \(J_{w}\) we find

\(J_{w}=\frac{\sigma T^{4}_{w}+(1/\varepsilon -1)[1-2E_{3}(\tau _{L})]\sigma T^{4}_{m}}{1+(1/\varepsilon -1)[1-2E_{3}(\tau _{L})]}\),

and

\(\frac{q(\tau )}{\sigma (T^{4}_{w}-T^{4}_{m})}=\frac{2[E_{3(\tau )}-E_{3}(\tau _{L}-\tau )]}{1+(1/\epsilon -1)[1-2E_{3}(\tau _{L})]}\)

The divergence of the flux may be evaluated by first calculating the incident radiation from equation (13.54) \(G(\tau )=2J_{1}E_{2}(\tau )+2J_{2}E_{2}(\tau _{L}-\tau )+2\pi \int_{0}^{\tau }{}S(\tau ^{\prime })E_{1}(\tau -\tau ^{\prime })d \tau ^{\prime }-2\pi \int_{\tau }^{\tau_{L} }{}S(\tau ^{\prime })E_{1}(\tau ^{\prime }-\tau )d \tau ^{\prime }\) and then using equation (13.57) \(\frac{dq}{d\tau }=(1-w)(4\pi I_{b}-G)\) . While this method is preferable for numerical and/or multidimensional calculations, it is more convenient here simply to differentiate the above expression for the heat flux. Thus,

\(\frac{dq}{d\tau }/\sigma (T^{4}_{w}-T^{4}_{m})=\frac{2[E_{2}(\tau )+E_{2}(\tau _{L}-\tau )]}{1+(1/\epsilon -1)[1-2E_{3}(\tau _{L})]}\) .

If \(T_{w} > T_{m}\), then dq/dτ is always negative: The flux is positive at τ = 0 (going into the medium), zero at the midplane, and turning more and more negative as the τ = \(τ _{L}\) plate is approached.
