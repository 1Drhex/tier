Guest Question.

A pass out turbine is fed with steam at 20 bar, 250°C. The pass out steam pressure is 5 bar and the exhaust pressure is 0.075 bar. In this installation the total power required is 4500 kW and heat load is 15000 kW. The efficiency of HP and LP turbine stages is 0.8 in each stage. Considering the reduction in total steam consumption to result in steam consumption rate of 10 kg/s, determine the new power output if the heat load remains same. Also assume the nozzle control governing and throttle governing at the inlets of HP turbine and LP turbine respectively.

Admin
Solution

\(\text{For first part of expanssion in HP stage.}\\\text{From steam table}\\ \qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad h_{1} = 3137 kJ/kg\\ \qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad s_{1} = 6.9563 kJ/kg.K.\\\text{It indicates that state of steam after expansion in HP stage lies in superheated region.}\\\text{From superheated steam table, degree of superheat may be estimated. At 5 bar,}\\\begin{aligned}s_{\text{g at 5 bar, 151.86°C}}&= 6.8213 kJ/kg.K\\s_{\text{at 5 bar, 200°C}}&= 7.0592 kJ/kg.K\\T_{2}&= \frac{(200 – 151.86) × (6.9563 – 6.8213)}{(7.0592 – 6.8213)} + 151.86\\T_{2}&= 179.18°C\\h_{\text{g at 5 bar, 151.86°C}}&= 2748.7 kJ/kg,\\h_{\text{at 5 bar, 200°C}}&= 2855.4 kJ/kg,\\h_{2}&= 2748.7 + \frac{(2885.4 – 2748.7)}{(200 – 151.86)} × (179.18 – 151.86)\\h_{2}&= 2813.41 kJ/kg.\\\text{Considering turbine efficiency, }&0.8= \frac{(h_{1} – h_{2^{\prime}})}{(h_{1} – h_{2})}\\0.8&= \frac{(3137 – h_{2^{\prime}})}{(3137 – 2813.41)}\\h_{2^{\prime}}&= 2878.13 kJ/kg\\\text{Heat available for process heat}&\text{ing}= h_{2^{\prime}} – h_{\text{f at 5 bar}}\\&= 2878.13 – 640.23\\&= 2237.9 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Let mass flow rate of steam be }m_{s, HP}\text{ to HP turbine and }m_{s, heat}\text{ for process heating after HP.}\\\text{Thus, mass flow rate of steam required for process heating of 15000 kW}\\\qquad \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad m_{s, heat} = \frac{15000}{2237.9} = 6.70 kg/s.\\\text{The h-s diagram shows two different arrangements. One arrangement shown by }(1 – 2^{\prime}– 3^{\prime})\\\text{indicates the case of no extraction in turbine. While }(1 –2^{\prime} – 3^{\prime\prime} – 4^{\prime})\text{ shows the case of passout turbine.}\\\textbf{For the first case}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Using turbine efficiency, 0.8}\quad &= \frac{(T_{1} – T_{2^{\prime}})}{(T_{1} – T_{2})}\\⇒ \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad T_{2}^{ \prime}&= T_{1} – 0.8 (T_{1} – T_{2}) = 350 – 0.8(350 – 179.18)\\T_{2}^{ \prime}&= 213.34°C\\⇒ \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad s_{2^{\prime}}&= 6.8213 + \frac{(7.0592 – 6.8213)}{(200 – 151.86)} × (213.34 – 151.86)\\s_{2}^{ \prime}&= 7.125 kJ.kg.K\\s_{3}&= s_{2}^{ \prime} = 7.125 kJ/kg.K\\\text{Let dryness fraction at 3 be }x_{3}& \\s_{3}&= s_{\text{f at 0.075 bar}} + x_{3} × s_{\text{fg at 0.075 bar}}\\7.125&= 0.5754 + (x_{3} × 7.675)\\⇒ \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad x_{3}&= 0.853\\⇒ \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad h_{3}&= h_{\text{f at 0.075 bar}} + x_{3} . h_{\text{fg at 0.075 bar}}\\&= 168.79 + (0.853 × 2406)\\h_{3}&= 2221.11 kJ/kg\\\text{Using LP turbine stage efficie}&\text{ncy,}\\0.8&= \frac{(h_{2^{\prime}} – h_{3^{\prime}})}{(h_{2^{\prime}} – h_{3})}\\0.8&= \frac{(2878.13 – h_{3^{\prime}})}{(2878.13 – 2221.11)}\\h_{3^{\prime}}&= 2352.5 kJ/kg\\\text{Power produced by HP stage,}&= m_{s,HP} × (h_{1} – h_{2^{\prime}})\\&= m_{s,HP} (3137 – 2878.13)\\P_{,HP}&= 258.87 m_{s,HP}\\\text{Power produced by LP stage,}&= (m_{s,HP} – m_{s,heat}) (h_{2^{\prime}} – h_{3^{\prime}})\\&= (m_{s,HP} – 6.7) (2878.13 – 2352.51)\\P_{,LP}&= (m_{s,HP} – 6.7)525.62\\\text{Total power produced}&=P_{HP} + P_{LP} \\&= 258.87 m_{s,HP} + 525.62 (m_{s,HP} – 6.7)\\4500&= (784.49 m_{s,HP} – 3521.65)\\m_{s,HP}&= 10.23 kg/s\end{aligned}\\\textbf{For the IInd case when steam consumption changes}\\\text{Since total steam consumption is reduced to 10 kg/s, i.e., }m_{s,HP}\text{ = 10 kg/s, but heat load remains save, so steam required for }\\\text{process heating would not change and }m_{s,heat}\text{ remains as 6.7 kg/s.}\\\text{Thus}\qquad \qquad \qquad m_{\text{s,HP, new}} = 10 kg/s \& m_{\text{s, heat, new}} = 6.7 kg/s.\\\text{Also it is given in problem that the nozzle control governing employed at entrance of HP turbine stage therefore pressure at}\\\text{inlet to turbine remains same as 20 bar.}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Hence power produced by HP turbine}&= 10 × (h_{1} – h_{2^{\prime}})\\&= 10 × (3137 – 2878.13)\\&= \text{Power,}_{\text{ HP, new}} = 2588.7 kW\end{aligned}\\\text{Also the problem says that LP steam turbine stage is having throttle control governing, so by symmetry of triangles on}\\\text{pressure – steam consumption graph.}\\\begin{aligned}\frac{p_{3^{\prime\prime}}}{M_{3^{\prime\prime}}}&= \frac{p_{2^{\prime}}}{M_{2^{\prime}}}\\\text{Here,}\qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad &\\M_{3}^{ \prime\prime}&= (m_{\text{s,HP,new}} – m_{\text{s,heat, new}}) = 10 – 6.7 = 3.3 kg/s.\\M_{2}^{ \prime}&= (m_{s,HP} – m_{s,heat}) = 10.23 – 6.7 = 3.53 kg/s.\\p_{3}^{ \prime\prime}&= \frac{5 × 3.3}{3.53} = 4.67 bar\\\text{Also, in view of throttling, }h_{2}^{ \prime}&= h_{3}^{ \prime\prime} = 2878.13 kJ/kg.\end{aligned}\\\)From steam table this state of steam at 4.67 bar pressure and enthalpy of 2878.13 kJ/kg can be located as it lies in super heated state by interpolation. Also it can be directly seen from mollier diagram, which would however yield approximate value. After locating this state on mollier diagram considering iseentropic expansion the state 4 can be located and enthalpy value be known.\(\\\text{At 4.67 bar, 2878.13 kJ/kg let temperature be }T_{3}^{ \prime\prime}\\\text{By interpolation,}\\\text{At 4.67 bar, 200°C,h = 2857.08 kJ/kg}\\\begin{aligned}s&= 7.096 kJ/kg.K\\\text{At 4.67 bar, 250°C, h = 2961.8}&\text{6 kJ/kg,}\\s&= 7.30654\\T_{3}^{ \prime\prime}&= \frac{(250 – 200) × (2878.13 – 2857.08)}{(2961.86 – 2857.08)}\\T_{3}^{ \prime\prime}&= 210.04°C\\⇒ \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad s_{3}^{ \prime\prime}&= 7.096 + \frac{(7.30654 – 7.096) × (210.04 – 200)}{(250 – 200)}\\s_{3}^{ \prime\prime}&= 7.138 kJ/kg.K.\end{aligned}\\\text{For isentropic expansion in LP turbine stage, }s_{3}^{ \prime\prime} = s_{4}\\\text{At 0.075 bar and entropy value of 7.138 kJ/kg.K the state of steam lies in wet region, hence let us consider dryness fraction}\\\text{at state 4 as }x_{4}.\\\begin{aligned}s_{4}&= 7.138 = s_{\text{f at 0.075 bar}} + x_{4} . s_{\text{fg at 0.075 bar}}\\7.138&= 0.5764 + (x_{4} . 7.6750)\\⇒ \quad\qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad x_{4}&= 0.855\\\text{Enthalpy at state 4 } \quad\qquad \qquad &\\h_{4}&= h_{\text{f at 0.075 bar}} + x_{4} . h_{\text{fg at 0.075 bar}}\\h_{4}&= 168.79 + (0.855 × 2406) = 2225.92 kJ/kg\\\text{Considering isentropic efficienc}&\text{y of LP turbine,} \\0.8&= \left\lgroup\frac{h_{3^{\prime\prime}} – h_{4^{\prime}}}{h_{3^{\prime\prime}} – h_{4} }\right\rgroup \\⇒ \qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad 0.8&= \left\lgroup\frac{2878.13 – h_{4^{\prime}}}{2878.13 – 2225.92} \right\rgroup ⇒ h_{4^{\prime}} = 2356.36 kJ/kg\\\text{Power produced by LP turbine}&= M_{3^{\prime\prime}} × (h_{3^{\prime\prime}} – h_{4^{\prime}})\\&= 3.3 × (2878.13 – 2356.36)\\\text{Power}_{\text{LP, new}}&= 1721.84 kW.\\\end{aligned}\\\text{Total power produced = Power, new = 2588.7 + 1721.84 = 4310.54 kw}\)
