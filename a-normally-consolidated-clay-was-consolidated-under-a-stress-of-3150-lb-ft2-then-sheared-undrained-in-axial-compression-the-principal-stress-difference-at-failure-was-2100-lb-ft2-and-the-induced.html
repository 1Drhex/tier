Guest Question.

A normally consolidated clay was consolidated under a stress of 3150 \(lb / ft ^{2}\), then sheared undrained in axial compression. The principal stress difference at failure was 2100 \(lb / ft ^{2}\), and the induced pore pressure at failure was 1848 \(lb / ft ^{2}\). Determine (a) the Mohr-Coulomb strength parameters, in terms of both total and effective stresses analytically, (b) compute \(\left(\sigma_{1} / \sigma_{3}\right)_{f}\) and \(\left(\sigma_{1}^{\prime} / \sigma_{3}^{\prime}\right)_{f}\). and (c) determine the theoretical angle of the failure plane in the specimen.

Admin
Solution

The parameters required are: effective parameters \(c^{\prime} \text { and } \phi^{\prime}\), and total parameters \(c \text { and } \phi\).

 

(a) Given \(\sigma_{3 f}=3150 lb / ft ^{2}, \text { and }\left(\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}\right)_{f}=2100 lb / ft ^{2}\). The total principal stress at failure \(\sigma_{1 f}\) is obtained from

 

\(\sigma_{1 f}=\left(\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}\right)_{f}+\sigma_{3 f}=2100+3150=5250 lb / ft ^{2}\)

 

Effective \(\sigma_{1 f}^{\prime}=\sigma_{1 f}-u_{f}=5250-1848=3402 lb / ft ^{2}\)

 

\(\sigma_{3 f}^{\prime}=\sigma_{3 f}-u_{f}=3150-1848=1302 lb / ft ^{2}\)

 

Now \(\sigma_{1}=\sigma_{3} \tan ^{2}\left(45^{\circ}+\phi / 2\right)+2 c \tan \left(45^{\circ}+\phi / 2\right)\)

 

Since the soil is normally consolidated, c = 0. As such

 

\(\frac{\sigma_{1}}{\sigma_{3}}=\tan ^{2}\left(45^{\circ}+\phi / 2\right)=\frac{1+\sin \phi}{1-\sin \phi}, \quad \text { or } \sin \phi=\frac{\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}}{\sigma_{1}+\sigma_{3}}\)

 

Total \(\phi=\sin ^{-1} \frac{2100}{5250+3150}=\sin ^{-1} \frac{2100}{8400}=14.5^{\circ}\)

 

Effective \(\phi^{\prime}=\sin ^{-1} \frac{2100}{3402+1302}=\sin ^{-1} \frac{2100}{4704}=26.5^{\circ}\)

 

(b) The stress ratios at failure are

 

\(\frac{\sigma_{1}}{\sigma_{3}}=\frac{5250}{3150}=1.67, \quad \frac{\sigma_{1}^{\prime}}{\sigma_{3}^{\prime}}=\frac{3402}{1302}=2.61\)

 

(c) From Eq. (8.18)

 

\(\alpha_{c}=45^{\circ}+\phi / 2\) (8.18)

 

\(\alpha_{f}=45^{\circ}+\frac{\phi^{\prime}}{2}=45^{\circ}+\frac{26.5}{2}=58.25^{\circ}\)

 

The above problem can be solved graphically by constructing a Mohr-Coulomb envelope.
