Guest Question.

Finding the performance of an inverting amplifier with shunt-shunt feedback The inverting amplifier shown in Fig. 10.9(b) has \( R_{ s }=2 k \Omega, R_{ L }=5 k \Omega, \text { and } R_{ F }=8 k \Omega \). The op-amp parameters are \( R_{ i }=2 M \Omega \text { and } R_{ o }=75 \Omega \text {, }\) and the open-loop voltage gain is \( \mu_{ g }=2 \times 10^{5} \).

(a) Determine the input resistance seen by the source \( R_{ if }=v_{ i } / i_{ s } \), the output resistance \( R_{\text {of }} \), the closed-loop transresistance \( A_{ f }=v_{ o } / i_{ S } \), and the voltage gain \( A_{ vf }=v_{ o } / v_{ s } \).
(b) Use PSpice/SPICE to verify your results.

Admin
Solution

\( R_{ L }=5 k \Omega, R_{ s }=2 k \Omega, R_{ F }=8 k \Omega, R_{ i }=2 M \Omega, R_{ o }=75 \Omega \text {, and } \mu_{ g }=2 \times 10^{5} \text {. } \) Replacing the op-amp in Fig. 10.9(b) by its equivalent circuit gives the amplifier shown in Fig. 10.36(a).

(a) The steps in analyzing the feedback network are as follows:

Step 1. \( R_{ F } \) constitutes the feedback network, and it produces a feedback current \( i_{ f } \) proportional to the output voltage \( v_{ o } \).

Step 2. The amplifier uses shunt-shunt feedback. Thus, A must be in V/A. Converting the voltage-controlled voltage source to a current-controlled voltage source, we get

\( v_{ o }=-\mu_{ g } v_{ e }=-\mu_{ g } R_{ i } i_{ e }=A i_{ e } \)

which gives the open-loop transresistance A:

\( A=-\mu_{ g } R_{ i }=-2 \times 10^{5} \times 2 \times 10^{6}=-4 \times 10^{11} V / A \)

Step 3. The effect of the feedback network is taken into account by shorting \( R_{ F } \) to the ground at sides 1 and 2. This arrangement is shown in Fig. 10.36(b). Then

\(\begin{array}{ll}& R_{ x }=R_{ F }=8 k \Omega \\\text { and } & R_{ y }=R_{ F }=8 k \Omega\end{array}\)

 

Step 4. If we represent the amplifier of Fig. 10.36(a) by an equivalent transresistance amplifier, as shown in Fig. 10.36(b), we have

\(\begin{array}{ll}R_{ ie }=R_{ s }\left\|R_{ i }\right\| R_{ F }=2 k \|2 M \| 8 k =1.6 k \Omega \\\text { and } R_{ oe }=R_{ o }\left\|R_{ F }\right\| R_{ L }=75\|8 k \| 5 k =73.2 \Omega\end{array}\)

From Eq. (10.86), we find that the modified open-loop transresistance gain \( A_{ e } \) is

\( A_{ e }=\frac{v_{ o }}{i_{ e 1}}=\frac{R_{ y } \| R_{ L }}{R_{ y } \| R_{ L }+R_{ o }} \times \frac{R_{ s } \| R_{ x }}{R_{ s } \| R_{ x }+R_{ i }} A \) (10.86)

 

\(\begin{aligned}A_{ e } &=\frac{v_{ o }}{i_{ e 1}}=\frac{-R_{ F } \| R_{ L }}{\left(R_{ F } \| R_{ L }\right)+R_{ o }} \times \frac{R_{ s } \| R_{ F }}{R_{ s } \| R_{ F }+R_{ i }} A \\&=\frac{-(8 k \| 5 k ) \times(2 k \| 8 k ) \times 4 \times 10^{11}}{(8 k \| 5 k +75)(2 k \| 8 k +2 M )}=-312.1 M \Omega\end{aligned}\)

 

Step 5. From Fig. 10.36(c), the feedback factor β is given by

\( \beta=\left.\frac{i_{ f }}{v_{ o }}\right|_{v_{ f }=0}=-\frac{1}{R_{ F }}=-125 \mu J \)

The loop gain is

\( T_{ L }=\beta A_{ e }=125 \mu \mho \times 312.1 \times 10^{6}=39.02 k \Omega \)

 

Step 6. The input resistance at the input side of the op-amp is

\(\begin{aligned}R_{ if } &=\frac{R_{ ie }}{1+\beta A_{ e }}=\frac{R_{ ie }}{1+T_{ L }} \\&=\frac{1.6 k \Omega}{1+39.02 \times 10^{3}}=41.0 m \Omega\end{aligned}\)

 

Step 7. The output resistance with feedback is

\( R_{ of }=\frac{R_{ oe }}{1+\beta A_{ e }}=\frac{R_{ oe }}{1+T_{ L }}=\frac{73.2 \Omega}{1+39.02 \times 10^{3}}=1.876 m \Omega \)

Step 8. The closed-loop transresistance gain \( A_{ f } \)is

\(\begin{aligned}A_{ f } &=\frac{v_{ o }}{i_{ s }}=\frac{A_{ e }}{1+\beta A_{ e }}=\frac{A_{ e }}{1+T_{ L }} \\&=\frac{-312.1 \times 10^{6}}{1+39.02 \times 10^{3}}=-8 k \Omega \\v_{ o } &=\left(\frac{v_{ o }}{v_{ s }}\right) v_{ s }=\left(\frac{v_{ o }}{i_{ s }}\right)\left(\frac{i_{ s }}{v_{ s }}\right) v_{ s }=\left(\frac{-8 k }{2 k }\right) v_{ s }=-4 v_{ s }\end{aligned}\)

Therefore, the overall voltage gain is \( v_{ o } / v_{ s }=-4 \).

NOTE: Substituting \( R_{1}=R_{ s } \) in Eq. (3.40) gives the voltage gain of the inverting amplifier as \( R_{ F } / R_{ s }= -8 k / 2 k =-4 \).

\(A_{ f }=\frac{v_{ O }}{v_{ S }}=-\frac{R_{ F }}{R_{1}}\) (3.40)

 

(b) The shunt-shunt feedback circuit for PSpice simulation is shown in Fig. 10.37. The results of the simulation (.TF analysis) are shown below, with hand calculations to the right:

\(\begin{array}{ll}V (5) / Is =-8.000 E +03=-8 k \Omega & A_{ f }=-8 k \Omega \\\text { INPUT RESISTANCE AT IS=4.097E-02=40.97 } m \Omega & R_{ if }=41.0 m \Omega \\\text { OUTPUT RESISTANCE AT V(5)=1.876E-03=1.876 } m \Omega & R_{ of }=1.876 m \Omega\end{array}\)

The PSpice results are very close to the hand-calculated values.
