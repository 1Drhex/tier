Guest Question.

The ABCD parameters of the two-port network in Fig. 19.34 are

\( \biggl[\begin{matrix} 4 & 20Ω \\ 0.1S & 2 \end{matrix} \biggr] \)

The output port is connected to a variable load for maximum power transfer. Find \( R_L \) and the maximum power transferred.

Admin
Solution

What we need is to find the Thevenin equivalent ( \( Z_{Th}\) and \(V_{Th}\)) at the load or output port. We find \(Z_{Th}\) using the circuit in Fig. 19.35(a). Our goal is to get \(Z_{Th}= V_2/I_2\). Substituting the given ABCD parameters into Eq. (19.22), we obtain

\( V_1 = AV_2 – BI_2 \)

\( I_1 = CV_2 – DI_2 \) (19.22)

\( V_1 = 4V_2 – 20I_2 \) (19.9.1)

\( I_1 = 0.1V_2 – 2I_2 \) (19.9.2)

At the input port,\( V_1 = -10I_1 \).Substituting this into Eq. (19.9.1) gives

\( -10I_1 = 4V_2 – 20I_2 \)

or

\( I_1 = -0.4V_2 + 2I_2 \) (19.9.3)

Setting the right-hand sides of Eqs. (19.9.2) and (19.9.3) equal,

\( 0.1V_2 – 2I_2 = -0.4V_2 + 2I_2 \) \( \Rightarrow \) \( 0.5V_2 = 4I_2 \)

Hence,

\( Z_{Th}= \frac{V_2}{I_2} = \frac{4}{0.5} = 8 Ω \)

To find \( V_{Th} \), we use the circuit in Fig. 19.35(b). At the output port \( I_2=0 \) and at the input port \( V_1=50-10I_1 \) Substituting these into Eqs. (19.9.1) and (19.9.2),

\( 50- 10I_1 =4V_2 \) (19.9.4)

\( I_1 = 0.1V_2 \) (19.9.5)

Substituting Eq. (19.9.5) into Eq. (19.9.4),

\( 50 – V_2 = 4V_2 \) \( \Rightarrow \) \( V_2=10 \)

Thus,

\( V_{Th} = V_2 = 10 V\)

The equivalent circuit is shown in Fig. 19.35(c). For maximum power transfer,

\( R_L= Z_{Th} = 8 Ω \)

From Eq. (4.24), the maximum power is

\( P_{max} = \frac{V^2_{Th}}{4R_{Th}} \) (4.24)

\( P =I^2R_L = \biggl(\frac{V_{Th}}{2R_L}\biggr)^2 R_L =\frac{V^2_{Th}}{4R_L} = \frac{100}{4×8} = 3.125 W \)
