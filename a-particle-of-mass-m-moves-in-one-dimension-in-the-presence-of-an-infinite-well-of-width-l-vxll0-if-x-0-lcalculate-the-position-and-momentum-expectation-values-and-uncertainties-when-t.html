Guest Question.

A particle of mass m moves in one dimension in the presence of an infinite well of width L:

\(V(x)=\left\{\begin{array}{ll}0 & \text { if } x \in[0, L] \\+\infty & \text { elsewhere. }\end{array}\right.\)

Calculate the position and momentum expectation values and uncertainties when the particle is in an energy eigenstate.Comment on these results in light of the uncertainty principle.

Admin
Solution

Remember that, for the potential well the energy eigenvalues are

\( E_{n}=\frac{n^{2} \pi^{2} h^{2}}{2 m L^{2}} \quad(n=1,2, \ldots)\) (2.11)

and the corresponding eigenfunctions in the region x âˆˆ [0, L] are given by

\( \psi_{n}(x)=\sqrt{\frac{2}{L}} \sin \frac{n \pi x}{L} \quad(n=1,2, \ldots)\) (2.12)

and zero elsewhere. They have symmetry \((-1)^{n+1}\) (with respect to \(x=\frac{L}{2}\)), thus the probability distributions are always symmetrical, so

\( \langle x\rangle=\frac{L}{2}\).

For a bound state, the momentum expectation value is always null.
This general property can easily be demonstrated in the present case. Indeed, as the eigenfunctions are real (we can set the arbitrary phase factor equal to 1), we have

\(\langle p\rangle=\int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} d x \psi^{*}(x) \frac{h}{l} \frac{d}{d x} \psi(x)=\frac{h}{l} \frac{1}{2} \int_{0}^{L} d x \frac{d}{d x} \psi^{2}(x)=\left.\frac{h}{2 \imath} \psi^{2}(x)\right|_{0} ^{L}=0\)

Let us calculate the momentum spread.\(\text { As }\langle p\rangle=0\).

\( (\Delta p)^{2}=\left\langle p^{2}\right\rangle=2 m\langle E\rangle=2 m E_{n}=\frac{n^{2} \pi^{2}h^{2}}{L^{2}}\)

The \(x^2\) expectation value is given by

\(\begin{aligned}\left\langle x^{2}\right\rangle &=\frac{2}{L} \int_{0}^{L} d x x^{2} \sin ^{2} \frac{n\pi x}{L}=\frac{2 L^{2}}{n^{3} \pi^{3}} \int_{0}^{n \pi} d y y^{2} \sin ^{2}y=\\&=\frac{2 L^{2}}{n^{3} \pi^{3}}\left(\frac{n^{3} \pi^{3}}{6}-\frac{1}{4} n \pi \cos (2n \pi)-\frac{1}{8}\left(1-6 n^{2} \pi^{2}\right) \sin (2 n \pi)\right)=\\&=L^{2}\left(\frac{1}{3}-\frac{1}{2 n^{2} \pi^{2}}\right)\end{aligned}\) (2.13)

where we integrated repeatedly by parts.

The position uncertainty is given by

\((\Delta x)^{2}=\left\langle x^{2}\right\rangle-\langle x\rangle^{2}=L^{2}\left(\frac{1}{3}-\frac{1}{2 n^{2} \pi^{2}}\right)-\frac{a^{2}}{4}=L^{2}\left(\frac{1}{12}-\frac{1}{2 n^{2} \pi^{2}}\right)\)

The two uncertainty product is

\( \Delta x \Delta p=h n \pi \sqrt{\frac{1}{12}-\frac{1}{2 n^{2} \pi^{2}}}\)

This product assumes its minimum value in the ground state (about 0.57 h, slightly higher than h/2) and grows as n increases.
