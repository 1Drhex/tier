Guest Question.

A W310 × 60 steel beam (see Appendix B) has a C250 × 45 channel bolted to the top flange, as shown in Figure P9.56. The beam is simply supported at its ends and carries a concentrated load of 100 kN at the center of a 6-m span. Pairs of 24-mm-diameter bolts are spaced at intervals of s along the beam. If the allowable average shear stress in the bolts must be limited to 125 MPa, determine the maximum spacing interval s for the bolts.

Admin
Solution

Centroid location in y direction:

\(\begin{array}{cccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} \text { (from bottom) } \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline W 310 \times 60 & 7,550 & 151 & 1,140,050 \\C 250 \times 45 & 5,680 & 302+17.1-16.5=302.6 & 1,718,768 \\\hline &13,230 & 2,858,818\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{2,858,818 mm ^{3}}{13,230 mm ^{2}} &=216.086 mm \text{(from bottom of shape to centroid)} \\&=103.014 mm \text{(from top of shape to centroid)}\end{aligned}\)

 

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

\(\begin{aligned}&\begin{array}{lcccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline W 310 \times 60 & 128,000,000 & -65.086 & 31,983,215 & 159,983,315 \\C 250 \times 45 & 1,640,000 & 86.5140 & 42,512,938 & 44,152,938 \\\hline\end{array}\\&\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( mm ^{4}\right)=204,136,153\end{aligned}\)

Maximum shear force:
V = P/2 = 100 kN/2 = 50 kN

Shear flow through the bolts
Consider the C250 × 45 shape, which is connected to the W310 × 60 shape with two bolts:

\(\begin{aligned}Q &=\left(5,680 mm ^{2}\right)(302.6 mm -216.086 mm ) \\&=491,399.5 mm ^{3} \\q &=\frac{V Q}{I} \\&=\frac{(50,000 N )\left(491,399.5 mm ^{3}\right)}{204,136,153 mm ^{4}} \\&=120.361 N / mm\end{aligned}\)

The cross-sectional area of a single 24-mm-diameter bolt is:

\(A_{ bolt }=\frac{\pi}{4}(24 mm )^{2}=452.389 mm ^{2}\)

Use Eq. (9.13) to determine the maximum spacing s:

\(\begin{aligned}q s & \leq n_{f} \tau_{f} A_{f} \\& \therefore s \leq \frac{n_{f} \tau_{f} A_{f}}{q}=\frac{(2 \text { bolts })\left(125 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(452.389 mm ^{2}\right)}{120.361 N / mm }=939.653 mm =940 mm\end{aligned}\)
