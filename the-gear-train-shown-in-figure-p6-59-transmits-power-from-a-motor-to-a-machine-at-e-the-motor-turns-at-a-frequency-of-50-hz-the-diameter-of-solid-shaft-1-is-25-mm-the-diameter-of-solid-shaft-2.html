Guest Question.

The gear train shown in Figure P6.59 transmits power from a motor to a machine at E. The motor turns at a frequency of 50 Hz. The diameter of solid shaft (1) is 25 mm, the diameter of solid shaft (2) is 32 mm, and the allowable shear stress for each shaft is 60 MPa. Determine:
(a) the maximum power that can be transmitted by the gear train.
(b) the torque provided at gear E.
(c) the rotation speed of gear E (in Hz).

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&J_{1}=\frac{\pi}{32} d_{1}^{4}=\frac{\pi}{32}(25 mm )^{4}=38,349.52 mm ^{4} \\&J_{2}=\frac{\pi}{32} d_{2}^{4}=\frac{\pi}{32}(32 mm )^{4}=102,943.71 mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Allowable torques in the shafts:

\(\begin{aligned}&T_{1, \text { allow }}=\frac{\tau_{\text {allow }} J_{1}}{c_{1}}=\frac{\left(60 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(38,349.52 mm ^{4}\right)}{25 mm / 2}=184,078 N – mm \\&T_{2, \text { allow }}=\frac{\tau_{\text {allow }} J_{2}}{c_{2}}=\frac{\left(60 N / mm ^{2}\right)\left(102,943.71 mm ^{4}\right)}{32 mm / 2}=386,039 N – mm\end{aligned}\)

Torque relationships at the gears: The magnitudes of the torques acting on the gears are related by:

\(T_{B}=T_{A} \frac{N_{B}}{N_{A} } \quad T_{D}=T_{C} \frac{N_{D}}{N_{C}}\) (a)

Equilibrium: The magnitudes of the torques in the gears and shafts are related by:

\(T_{B}=T_{1}=T_{C} \quad T_{D}=T_{2}=T_{E}\) (b)

Equations (a) and (b) express the relationship between the torque in shafts (1) and (2):

\(T_{2}=T_{1} \frac{N_{D}}{N_{C}}\) (c)

Controlling shaft torque: In Eq. (c), let \(T_{1}=T_{1, \text { allow }}\) and compare the result with the allowable torque for shaft (2):

check \( T_{1, \text { allow }} \frac{N_{D}}{N_{C}} \leq T_{2, \text { allow }}\)

\((184,078 N – mm )\left(\frac{60 \text { teeth }}{30 \text { teeth }}\right)=368,156 N – mm \leq 386,039 N – m\) O.K.

This calculation shows that the torque in shaft (1) controls the capacity of the gear train. Using this fact, the torques throughout the gear train can be determined:

\(\begin{array}{ll}T_{1}=184,078 N – mm & T_{2}=368,156 N – mm \\T_{B}=T_{C}=184,078 N – mm & T_{D}=T_{E}=368,156 N – mm \\T_{A}=T_{B} \frac{N_{A}}{N_{B}}=(184,078 N – mm )\left(\frac{24 \text { teeth }}{72 \text { teeth }}\right)=61,359 N – mm\end{array}\)

(a) Power transmission: The maximum power that can be transmitted by the gear train can be found from the torque and rotation speed at A:

\(P=T_{A} \omega_{A}=(61.359 N – m )\left(\frac{50 rev }{ s }\right)\left(\frac{2 \pi rad }{1 rev }\right)=19,276.571 N – m / s =19.28 kW\)

(b) Torque at gear E: Equations (a) and (b) can be combined to express the relationship between the torque supplied by the motor at A and the torque available at gear E:

\(T_{E}=T_{D}=T_{C} \frac{N_{D}}{N_{C}}=T_{B} \frac{N_{D}}{N_{C}}=\left(T_{A} \frac{N_{B}}{N_{A}}\right) \frac{N_{D}}{N_{C}}=T_{A}\left(\frac{N_{B}}{N_{A}}\right)\left(\frac{N_{D}}{N_{C}}\right)\) (d)

The torque available at gear E is thus:

\(T_{E}=T_{A}\left(\frac{N_{B}}{N_{A}}\right)\left(\frac{N_{D}}{N_{C}}\right)=(61.359 N – m )\left(\frac{72 \text { teeth }}{24 \text { teeth }}\right)\left(\frac{60 \text { teeth }}{30 \text { teeth }}\right)=368.154 N – m =368 N – m\)

This result could also be found from the torque in shaft (2), as calculated previously.

(c) Rotation speed of gear E: There are a couple of ways to compute this speed. Using the gear ratios, the speed of gear E can be found from:

\(\omega_{E}=\omega_{A}\left(\frac{N_{A}}{N_{B}}\right)\left(\frac{N_{C}}{N_{D}}\right)=(50 Hz )\left(\frac{24 \text { teeth }}{72 \text { teeth }}\right)\left(\frac{30 \text { teeth }}{60 \text { teeth }}\right)=8.33 Hz\)
