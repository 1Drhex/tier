Guest Question.

Chloroform flows at a rate of 0.01 \(m^{3}\)/s through a 4-nominal schedule 40 wrought iron pipe. The pipe is laid out horizontally and is 250 m long. Calculate the pressure drop of the chloroform.

Admin
Solution

From the property tables, we read

chloroform ρ = 1.47(1 000) kg/m3 µ = 0.53 x 10-3 N·s/m2 [App. Table B.1]
4-nom sch 40 ID = D = 10.23 cm A = 82.19 cm2 [App. Table D.1]
wrought iron ε = 0.004 6 cm [Table 3.1]

The continuity equation for incompressible steady flow through the pipe is

\(A_{1}V_{1} = A_{2}V_{2}\)

Because \(A_{1} = A_{2}\), then \(V_{1} = V_{2}\).

The Bernoulli equation applies:

\(\frac{p_1 g_{c}}{\rho g}+ \frac{{V_{1}}^{2}}{2g}+z_{1}=\frac{p_{2}g_{c}}{\rho g}+ \frac{{V_{2}}^{2}}{2g}+z_{2}+\frac{fL}{D_{h}}\frac{V^{2}}{2g} \)

where points 1 and 2 are L = 250 m apart, \(z_{1} = z_{2}\) for a horizontal pipe, and \(p_{1} – p_{2}\) is sought.

The above equation reduces to

\(p_{1}-p_{2}=\frac{fL}{D_{h}}\frac{\rho V^{2}}{2g_{c}} \)

The flow velocity is

V=\(\frac{Q}{A}=\frac{0.01}{82.19 \times 10^{-4}}\) = 1.22 m/s

The Reynolds number is calculated to be

Re=\(\frac{\rho VD}{\mu g_{c}}=\frac{960(1.22)(0.1023)}{650 \times 10^{-3}}\)

The Reynolds number is calculated to be

Re=\(\frac{\rho VD}{\mu g_{c}}=\frac{1.47(1000)(1.22)(0.1023)}{0.53 \times 10^{-3}}=3.46 \times 10^{5}\)

The flow is therefore turbulent. Thus,

\( \left. \begin{cases}Re=3.46 \times 10^{5}\\ Also\frac{\varepsilon }{D}=\frac{0.0046}{10.23}=0.00045 \end{cases} \right\} ƒ=0.018 (Figure 3.3)\)

 

The pressure loss is

\(p_{1}-p_{2}=\frac{fL}{D_{h}}\frac{\rho V^{2}}{2g_{c}} =\frac{0.018(250)}{0.1023}=\frac{1.47(1000)(1.22)^{2}}{2}\)

 

\(p_{1}-p_{2}=48120 N/m^{2}=48.1 KPa\)

 

This and are identical except for fluid properties. In, the flow is laminar and the pressure drop is 608 kPa. In this example, the flow is turbulent and the pressure drop is 48.1 kPa. The frictional loss in is large due to the high viscosity of the fluid.
