Guest Question.

Determine the time evolution of an osmotic cell by considering a system consisting of two subsystems labelled 1 and 2, separated by an osmotic wall of area A. Subsystem 1 is an osmotic cell that contains salt water with a low initial salt concentration, i.e. \(c\ll 1\). Subsystem 2 contains only pure water and constitutes a heat and work reservoir. The number of moles of pure water in the osmotic cell is \(N_1\) and the number of moles of salt is \(N_s\). The two subsystems are kept at constant temperature T and pressure p.

1. Determine the time evolution of the number of moles of salt \(N_s (t)\) in the osmotic cell.
2. Determine the time evolution of the number of moles of pure water \(N_1 (t)\) in the osmotic cell, assuming that the time derivative of this quantity is proportional to the chemical potential difference of water in the two subsystems.

Admin
Solution

1. The osmotic wall does not let salt pass. Thus, the number of moles of salt in the salt water is constant, i.e.

\(N_s = const\).

2. According to relation (3.45), the rate of change of the number of moles of pure water in the osmotic cell \(\dot{N_1}\) is given by,

\(\dot{N_1} =F\frac{A}{\ell }\Bigl(\mu _2(S_2, N_2 ) -μ_1(S_1, N_1) \Bigr)\).

\(\dot{N_1} =F\frac{A}{\ell }(\mu _2 -μ_1 )\).

where F is Fick’s diffusion constant and \(\ell\) is the characteristic diffusion length. The chemical potentials of water in the cell \(μ_1\) and in the reservoir \(μ_2\) are related by equation (8.82),

\(μ (T, p, 1 − c) = μ (T, p) + RT \ln (1 − c)\).

\(μ_1 = μ_2 + R T \ln (1 − c)\).

Since the salt concentration in the salt water is low, i.e. \(c \ll 1\), the following series expansion in terms of c can be used,

\(\ln (1 − c) = −c +\omicron (c^2)\).

It implies that,

\(μ_2 − μ_1 = R T c\).

and, for a low salt concentration,

\(c = \frac{N_s}{N_1}\).

Thus, the evolution equation can be recast as,

\(\dot{N_1} =\frac{1}{N_1\tau }\).

where the characteristic time \(\tau\) is given by,

\(\tau =\frac{\ell }{A F N_s R T}\).

The integral of the evolution equation reads,

\(\int_{N_1(0)}^{N_1(t)}{N_1 d N_1} = \frac{1}{\tau } \int_{0}^{1}{d\acute{t} }\).

which implies that the solution is given by,

\(\frac{1}{2} \Bigl(N_1 (t)^2 – N_1 (0)^2\Bigr) =\frac{t}{\tau }\).

Thus,

\(N_1 (t) = \sqrt{N_1 (0)^2 +\frac{2t}{\tau } }\).
