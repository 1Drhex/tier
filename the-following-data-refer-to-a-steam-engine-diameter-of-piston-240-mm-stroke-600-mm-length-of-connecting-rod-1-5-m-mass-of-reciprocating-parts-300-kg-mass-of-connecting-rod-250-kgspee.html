Guest Question.

The following data refer to a steam engine:
Diameter of piston = 240 mm; stroke = 600 mm ; length of connecting rod = 1.5 m ; mass of reciprocating parts = 300 kg; mass of connecting rod = 250 kg; speed = 125 r.p.m ; centre of gravity of connecting rod from crank pin = 500 mm ; radius of gyration of the connecting rod about an axis through the centre of gravity = 650 mm.
Determine the magnitude and direction of the torque exerted on the crankshaft when the crank has turned through \(30^{\circ}\) from inner dead centre.

Admin
Solution

Given : D = 240 mm = 0.24 m ; L = 600 mm or r = L/2 = 300 mm = 0.3 m ; l = 1.5 m ; \(m_{ R }=300 kg ; m_{ C }=250 kg\) N = 125 r.p.m. or \(\omega=2 \pi \times 125 / 60=13.1 rad / s\) ; GC = 500 mm = 0.5 m ; \(k_{ G }=650 mm =0.65 m ; \theta=30^{\circ}\);

The inertia torque on the crankshaft may be determined by graphical method or analytical method as discussed below:

 

1. Graphical method

First of all, draw the configuration diagram OCP, as shown in Fig. 15.25, to some suitable scale, such that

OC = r = 300 mm ; PC = l = 1.5 m ; and angle POC \(=\theta=30^{\circ}\).

Now draw the Klienâ€™s acceleration diagram OCQN, as shown in Fig. 15.25, and complete the figure in the similar manner

By measurement; NO = 0.28 m ; gO = 0.28 m ; IP = 1.03 m ; I X = 0.38 m ; I Y = 0.98 m, and IC = 1.7 m.

We know that inertia force of reciprocating parts,

\(F_{ I }=m_{ R } \times \omega^{2} \times N O=300 \times(13.1)^{2} \times 0.28=14415 N\)

and inertia force of connecting rod,

\(F_{ C }=m_{ C } \times \omega^{2} \times g O=250 \times(13.1)^{2} \times 0.28=12013 N\)

Let \(F_{T}\) = Force acting perpendicular to the crank OC.

Taking moments about point I,

\(F_{ T } \times I C=F_{ I } \times I P+W_{ C } \times I Y+F_{ C } \times I X\)

 

\(F_{ T } \times 1.7=14415 \times 1.03+250 \times 9.81 \times 0.98+12013 \times 0.38=21816\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(F_{ T }=2.816 / 1.7=12833 N\) \(\ldots\left(\because W_{ C }=m_{ C } \cdot g\right)\)

We know that torque exerted on the crankshaft

\(=F_{ T } \times r=12833 \times 0.3=3850 N – m\)

 

2. Analytical method

We know that the distance of centre of gravity (G) of the connecting rod from P, i.e.,

\(l_{1}=l-G C=1.5-0.5=1 m\)

\(\therefore \) Inertia force due to total mass of the reciprocating parts at P,

\(F_{ I }=\left(m_{ R }+\frac{l-l_{1}}{l} \times m_{ C }\right) \omega^{2} \cdot r\left(\cos \theta+\frac{\cos 2 \theta}{5}\right)\)

 

\(=\left(300+\frac{1.5-1}{1.5} \times 250\right) \times(13.1)^{2} \times .0 .3\left(\cos 30^{\circ}+\frac{\cos 60^{\circ}}{5}\right)=19064 N\) … \(\left[\because n=\frac{l}{r}=\frac{1.5}{0.3}=5\right]\)

 

\(\therefore \) Corresponding torque due to \(F_{ I }\) ,

 

\(T_{ I }=F_{ I } \times O M=F_{ I } \cdot r\left(\sin \theta+\frac{\sin 2 \theta}{2 \sqrt{n^{2}-\sin ^{2} \theta}}\right)\)

 

\(=19064 \times 0.3\left(\sin 30^{\circ}+\frac{\sin 60^{\circ}}{2 \sqrt{5^{2}-\sin ^{2} 30^{\circ}}}\right)\)

 

\(=5719.2 \times 0.587=3357 N – m \text { (anticlockwise) }\)

Equivalent length of a simple pendulum when swung about an axis through P,

\(L=\frac{\left(k_{ G }\right)^{2}+\left(l_{1}\right)^{2}}{l_{1}}=\frac{(0.65)^{2}+1^{2}}{1}=1.42 m\)

 

\(\therefore \) Correcting torque,

 

\(T_{ C }=m_{ C } \cdot l_{1}(l-L)\left[\frac{\omega^{2} \sin 2 \theta}{2 n^{2}}\right]\)

 

\(=250 \times 1(1.5-1.42)\left[\frac{(13.1)^{2} \sin 60^{\circ}}{2 \times 5^{2}}\right]=59.5 N – m \text { (anticlockwise) }\)

 

Torque due to the weight of the connecting rod at C,

 

\(T_{ W }=W_{ C } \times \frac{l_{1}}{n} \times \cos \theta=m_{ C } \times g \times \frac{l_{1}}{n} \times \cos \theta\)

 

\(=250 \times 9.81 \times \frac{1}{5} \times \cos 30^{\circ}=424.8 N – m \text { (anticlockwise) }\)

 

\(\therefore \) Total torque exerted on the crankshaft,

 

\(=T_{ I }+T_{ C }+T_{ W }\)

 

\(=3357+59.5+424.8=3841.3 N – m \text { (anticlockwise) }\)

 

Note: The slight difference in results arrived at by the above two methods is mainly due to error in measurement in graphical method.
