Guest Question.

Air with gaseous A bubbles through a tank containing aqueous B. Reaction occurs as follows:

\(\mathrm{A}(g \rightarrow l)+2 \mathrm{~B}(l) \rightarrow \mathrm{R}(l), \quad-r_{\mathrm{A}}=k C_{\mathrm{A}} C_{\mathrm{B}}^{2}, \quad k=10^{6} \mathrm{~m}^{6} / \mathrm{mol}^{2} \cdot \mathrm{hr}\)

For this system

\(k_{\mathrm{Ag}} a=0.01 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{hr} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} \cdot \mathrm{Pa} \quad f_{l}=0.98\)

\(k_{\mathrm{A}} a=20 \mathrm{hr}^{-1} \quad H_{\mathrm{A}}=10^{5} \mathrm{~Pa} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{mol}\), very low solubility

\(\mathscr{D}_{\mathrm{A} l}=\mathscr{D}_{\mathrm{B} l}=10^{-6} \mathrm{~m}^{2} / \mathrm{hr} \quad a=20 \mathrm{~m}^{2} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\)

For a point in the absorber-reactor where

\(p_{\mathrm{A}}=5 \times 10^{3} \mathrm{~Pa} \quad \text { and } \quad C_{\mathrm{B}}=100 \mathrm{~mol} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\)

(a) locate the resistance to reaction (what \(\%\) is in the gas film, in the liquid film, in the main body of liquid)

(b) locate the reaction zone

(c) determine the behavior in the liquid film (whether pseudo first-order reaction, instantaneous, physical transport, etc.)

(d) calculate the rate of reaction \(\left(\mathrm{mol} / \mathrm{m}^{3} \cdot \mathrm{hr}\right)\)

Admin
Solution

This chapter has only analyzed second-order reactions, however, this problem deals with a third-order reaction. Since no analysis is available for other than second-order reactions, let us replace our third-order reaction with a secondorder approximation. Thus,

\(k C_{\mathrm{A}} C_{\mathrm{B}}^{2} \Rightarrow\left(k C_{\mathrm{B}}\right) C_{\mathrm{A}} C_{\mathrm{B}}\)

To find the rate from the general expression (Eq. 5 ), we need to first evaluate \(E_{i}\) and \(M_{H} .\) Let us do this:

\({{-r}''}''_{A}=\frac{1}{\frac{1}{k_{Ag}a}+\frac{H_{A}}{k_{Al}aE}+\frac{H_{A}}{kC_{B}f_{l}}}P_{A}\) (Eq .5)

 

\(\left.\begin{array}{c}M_{H}=\frac{\sqrt{\mathscr{D}_{\mathrm{A}} k C_{\mathrm{B}}^{2}}}{k_{\mathrm{A} l}}=\frac{\sqrt{10^{-6} 10^{6} 100^{2}}}{1}=100 \\\left(E_{i}\right)_{\text {first guess }}=1+\frac{\mathscr{D}_{\mathrm{B}} C_{\mathrm{B}} H_{\mathrm{A}}}{b \mathscr{D}_{\mathrm{A}} p_{\mathrm{A} i}}=1+\frac{100 \times 10^{5}}{2\left(5 \times 10^{5}\right)}=10^{3} \\\text { guess } p_{\mathrm{A} i}=p_{\mathrm{A}}\end{array}\right\}\)

Since \(\left(E_{i}\right)_{\text {first guess }}>5 M_{H},\) then for any other smaller guess for \(p_{\mathrm{A} i}\) we will still have \(E_{i}>5 M_{H}\). Therefore, from Fig. 23.4 we have pseudo first-order reaction

Thus,

(a) \(2 / 3\) of the resistance is in the gas film, \(1 / 3\) is in the liquid film \(\leftarrow\)

(b) the reaction zone is in the liquid film \(\leftarrow\)

(c) reaction proceeds by a pseudo first-order reaction of \(\mathrm{A},\) at the interface
