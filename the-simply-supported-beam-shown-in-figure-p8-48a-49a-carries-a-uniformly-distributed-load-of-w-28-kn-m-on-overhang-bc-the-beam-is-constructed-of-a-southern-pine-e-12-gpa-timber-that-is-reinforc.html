Guest Question.

The simply supported beam shown in Figure P8.48a/49a carries a uniformly distributed load of w = 28 kN/m on overhang BC. The beam is constructed of a Southern pine [E = 12 GPa] timber that is reinforced on its upper surface by a steel [E = 200 GPa] plate (Figure P8.48b/49b). The beam spans are L = 5.5 m and a = 1.75 m. The wood beam has dimensions of \(b_{w}\) = 215 mm and \(d_{w}\) = 325 mm. The steel plate dimensions are \(b_{s}\) = 250 mm and \(t_{s}\) = 10 mm. (You may neglect the weight of the beam in your calculations.) At the location of the maximum bending moment for the beam, determine:
(a) the vertical distance from point K to the neutral axis of the composite beam.
(b) the bending stress in the steel at H.

Admin
Solution

Let the wood be denoted as material (1) and the steel as material (2). The modular ratio is:

\(n=\frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}=\frac{200 GPa }{12 GPa }=16.6667\)

Transform the steel plate into an equivalent amount of wood by multiplying its width by the modular ratio: \(b_{s, \text { trans }}\) = 16.6667(250 mm) = 4,166.667 mm. Thus, for calculation purposes, the 250 mm Ã— 10 mm steel plate is replaced by a wood board that is 4,166.667-mm wide and 10-mm thick.

Centroid location of the transformed section in the vertical direction

\(\begin{array}{lccccc} {\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Width } b \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Height } h \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} \text { (from bottom) } \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { wood beam } & 215 & 325 & 69,875 & 162.5 & 11,354,687.5 \\\text { transformed steel plate } & 4,166.667 & 10 & 41,666.667 & 330 & 13,750,000 \\\hline & & & 111,541.667 & & 25,104,687.5\end{array}\)

 

(a) Vertical distance from point K to the neutral axis of the composite beam.

\(\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{25,104,687.5 mm ^{3}}{111,541.667 mm ^{2}}=225.070 mm =225 mm\)

 

Moment of inertia about the horizontal centroidal axis

 

\(\begin{array}{ccccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { wood beam } & 615,045,572.9 & -62.570 & 273,560,967.4 & 888,606,540.3 \\\text { transformed steel plate } & 347,222.2 & 104.930 & 458,762,704.1 & 459,109,926.3 \\\hline {\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }=} & &&& 1,347,716,467 mm ^{4}\end{array}\)

 

(b) Bending stress in the steel at H.
A formula for the maximum bending moment in this beam can be easily derived using the process described in Chapter 7. For this simply supported beam with a uniformly distributed load on the overhang, the maximum bending moment occurs at support B and it has a value given by:

\(M_{\max }=\frac{w a^{2}}{2}\)

 

For a uniformly distributed load of 28 kN/m, the maximum bending moment is:

 

\(M_{\max }=-\frac{w a^{2}}{2}=-\frac{(28 kN / m )(1.75 m )^{2}}{2}=-42.875 kN – m =-42.875 \times 10^{6} N – mm\)

 

The bending stress in the transformed material must be multiplied by the modular ratio n. Therefore, the maximum bending stress in the steel at H is:

 

\(\sigma_{H}=-n \frac{M y}{I}=-(16.6667) \frac{\left(-42.875 \times 10^{6} N – mm \right)(335 mm -225.070 mm )}{1,347,716,467 mm ^{4}}=58.3 MPa ( T )\)
