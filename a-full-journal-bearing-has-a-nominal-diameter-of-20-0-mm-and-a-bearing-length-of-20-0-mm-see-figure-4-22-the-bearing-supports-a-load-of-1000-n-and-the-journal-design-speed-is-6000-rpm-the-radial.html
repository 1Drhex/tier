Guest Question.

A full journal bearing has a nominal diameter of 20.0mm and a bearing length of 20.0mm (see Figure 4.22). The bearing supports a load of 1000N, and the journal design speed is 6000rpm. The radial clearance has been specified as 0.02mm. An SAE 20 oil has been chosen to be compatible with requirements elsewhere in the machine and the lubricant supply temperature is 60°C.Using the approximate method of Reason and Narang find the temperature rise of the lubricant, the total lubricant flow rate, the minimum film thickness, the torque required to overcome friction and the heat generated in the bearing.

Admin
Solution

The primary design data are D = 20 mm, L = 20 mm, W = 1000 N, N = 6000 rpm, c = 0.02 mm, SAE 20, \( T_{1} = 60°C. \)

Guess ΔT =15°C. Hence:

\( T_{av}=T_{1}+\frac{\Delta T}{2}=60+\frac{15}{2} =67.5 ْC \)

For SAE 20, at 67.5°C, from Figure 4.7, μ=0.016 Pa s.

\( P = \frac{W}{LD}=\frac{1000}{0.02\times 0.02}=2.5 MPa \)

 

\( S= \frac{\downarrow r}{\rightarrow c}^{2} \frac{\mu N_{s} }{P} \)

 

\( =\frac{\downarrow 0.01 }{\rightarrow 0.00002} ^2 \frac{0.016 \times 100}{2.5\times 10^{6} }=0.16 \)

\( T_{av} = 73.6ْC , \) hence μ=0.013 Pa s, S=0.13, hence ε=0.5904,
Q/(rc \( N_{s} L)=4.492 \)

Using these values gives ΔT = 23.56°C. Repeating the procedure with this value for ΔT gives:

\( T_{av} = 71.78 ْC , hence μ = 0.014 Pa s, \)

S=0.14, hence ε=0.5738,

Q/(rc \( N_{s} L) \) = 4.454,

\( Q_{π} \) /(rc \( N_{s} L) \) = 1.585,

(r/c)f = 3.613.

Using these values gives ΔT = 24.83°C. Hence \( T_{av} = 72.4°C \). This is close enough to the previous value, indicating that the correct value for \( T_{av} \) has been determined.

f= 0.007226,
