Guest Question.

(a) Prove the three-dimensional virial theorem:

2 〈 T 〉 = 〈r.∇V〉 (4.217).

(for stationary states). Hint: refer to .

(b) Apply the virial theorem to the case of hydrogen, and show that

\( \langle T\rangle=-E_{n} ; \quad\langle V\rangle=2 E_{n} \) (4.218)

(c) Apply the virial theorem to the three-dimensional harmonic oscillator (), and show that in this case

\( \langle T\rangle=\langle V\rangle=E_{n} / 2 \) (4.219).

Admin
Solution

(a)

\( \frac{d}{d t}\langle r \cdot p \rangle=\frac{i}{\hbar}\langle[H, r \cdot p ]\rangle \).

\( [H, r \cdot p ]=\sum_{i=1}^{3}\left[H, r_{i} p_{i}\right]=\sum_{i=1}^{3}\left(\left[H, r_{i}\right] p_{i}+r_{i}\left[H, p_{i}\right]\right)=\sum_{i=1}^{3}\left(\frac{1}{2 m}\left[p^{2}, r_{i}\right] p_{i}+r_{i}\left[V, p_{i}\right]\right) \).

\( \left[p^{2}, r_{i}\right]=\sum_{j=1}^{3}\left[p_{j} p_{j}, r_{i}\right]=\sum_{j=1}^{3}\left(p_{j}\left[p_{j}, r_{i}\right]+\left[p_{j}, r_{i}\right] p_{j}\right)=\sum_{j=1}^{3}\left[p_{j}\left(-i \hbar \delta_{i j}\right)+\left(-i \hbar \delta_{i j}\right) p_{j}\right]=-2 i \hbar p_{i} \).

\( \left[V, p_{i}\right]=i \hbar \frac{\partial V}{\partial r_{i}} \). ((c)). \( [H, r \cdot p ]=\sum_{i=1}^{3}\left[\frac{1}{2 m}(-2 i \hbar) p_{i} p_{i}+r_{i}\left(i \hbar \frac{\partial V}{\partial r_{i}}\right)\right] \).

\( =i \hbar\left(-\frac{p^{2}}{m}+ r \cdot \nabla V\right) . \quad \frac{d}{d t}\langle r \cdot p \rangle=\left\langle\frac{p^{2}}{m}- r \cdot \nabla V\right\rangle=2\langle T\rangle-\langle r \cdot \nabla V\rangle \).

For stationary states \( \frac{d}{d t}\langle r \cdot p \rangle=0, \text { so } 2\langle T\rangle=\langle r \cdot \nabla V\rangle \). QED.

(b)

\( V(r)=-\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \frac{1}{r} \Rightarrow \nabla V=\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \frac{1}{r^{2}} \hat{r} \Rightarrow r \cdot \nabla V=\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}} \frac{1}{r}=-V . \quad \text { So } 2\langle T\rangle=-\langle V\rangle \).

\( \text { But }\langle T\rangle+\langle V\rangle=E_{n}, \text { so }\langle T\rangle-2\langle T\rangle=E_{n}, \text { or }\langle T\rangle=-E_{n} ;\langle V\rangle=2 E_{n} \). QED.

(c)

\( V=\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} r^{2} \Rightarrow \nabla V=m \omega^{2} r \hat{r} \Rightarrow r \cdot \nabla V=m \omega^{2} r^{2}=2 V . \quad \text { So } 2\langle T\rangle=2\langle V\rangle, \text { or }\langle T\rangle=\langle V\rangle \).

\( \text { But }\langle T\rangle+\langle V\rangle=E_{n}, \text { so }\langle T\rangle=\langle V\rangle=\frac{1}{2} E_{n} \). QED.
