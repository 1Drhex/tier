Guest Question.

Compare the temperature distributions in a straight cylindrical rod having a diameter of 2 cm
and a length of 10 cm and exposed to a convection environment with \(h=25W/m^{2}\cdot ^{◦} C\), for three fin materials: copper \([K=385W/m\cdot ^{◦} C]\), stainless steel \([K=17W/m\cdot ^{◦} C]\), and glass\([K=0.8W/m\cdot ^{◦} C]\). Also compare the relative heat flows and fin efficiencies.

Admin
Solution

We have

\(\frac{hp}{KA}=\frac{(25)\pi (0.02)}{K\pi (0.01)^{2} } =\frac{5000}{K}\)

The terms of interest are therefore:

Material
hp/KA
m ml
Copper 12.99 3.604 0.3604
Stainless steel 294.1 17.15 1.715
Glass 6250 79.06 7.906

These values may be inserted into Equation (2-33a)

\(\frac{\theta }{\theta _0}= \frac{e^{-mx}}{1+e^{-2mL}} + \frac{e^{mx}}{1+e^{2mL}}\)

to calculate the temperatures at different
x locations along the rod, and the results are shown in Figure Example 2.8. We notice that the glass behaves as a “very long” fin, and its behavior could be calculated from Equation (2-32).

\(\frac{\theta }{\theta _{0} } =\frac{T-T_{∞} }{T_{0}-T_{∞} } =e^{-mx}\)

The fin efficiencies are calculated from Equation (2-38)

\(η_{f} =\frac{\sqrt{hPKA} \; \theta _{0}tanh \;mL }{hPL\theta _{0} } =\frac{tanh\; mL}{mL}\)

by using the corrected length approximation
of Equation (2-42).

\(L_{c} =L+\frac{\pi d^{2}/4 }{\pi d} =L+d/4\)

 

\(L_{c} =L+\frac{d}{4}=10+\frac{2}{4}=10.5 \enspace cm\)

The parameters of interest for the heat-flow and efficiency comparisons are now tabulated as:

Material h P K A mlc
Copper 0.19 0.3784
Stainless steel 0.0084 1.8008
Glass 3.9×10^-4 8.302

To compare the heat flows we could either calculate the values from Equation (2-36)

\(q=-KA\theta _{0} m(\frac{1}{1+e^{-2ml}}+\frac{1}{1+e^{+2ml}} )=\sqrt{hPKA }\> \theta _{0} \> tanh\> mL\)

for a unit
value of θ0 or observe that the fin efficiency gives a relative heat-flow comparison because the maximum heat transfer is the same for all three cases; i.e., we are dealing with the same fin size, shape, and value of h. We thus calculate the values of ηf from Equation(2-38):

\(n_{f} =\frac{\sqrt{hPKA} \; \theta _{0}tanh \;mL }{hPL\theta _{0} } =\frac{tanh\; mL}{mL}\)

and the above values of mLc.
