Guest Question.

Three-zone mho relays are used for transmission line protection of the power system shown in Figure 10.25. Positive-sequence line impedances are given as follows.

Line Positive-Sequence Impedance, Ω
1–2 6 + j 60
2–3 4 + j 40
2–4 5 + j 50

Rated voltage for the high-voltage buses is 500 kV. Assume a 2500:5 CT ratio and a 4500:1 VT ratio at B12. (a) Determine the settings \(Z_{t 1} \text {, } Z_{t 2} \text {, and } Z_{t 3}\) for the mho relay at B12. (b) Maximum current for line 1–2 under emergency loading conditions is 1400 A at 0.90 power factor lagging. Verify that B12 does not trip during emergency loading conditions.

 

Admin
Solution

(a)

\(\begin{aligned}&Z^{\prime}=\frac{V_{L N}^{\prime}}{I_{L}^{\prime}}=\frac{V_{L N} /(4500 / 1)}{I_{L} /(2500 / 5)}=\left(\frac{V_{L N}}{I_{L}}\right) \frac{1}{9} \\&Z^{\prime}=\frac{Z}{9}\end{aligned}\)

Set the B12 zone 1 relay for 80% reach of line 1–2:

\(Z_{r 1}=0.8(6+j 60) / 9=\underline{\underline{0.53+j 5.33}} \Omega \text { secondary }\)

Set the B12 zone 2 relay for 120% reach of line 1–2:

\(Z_{r 2}=1.2(6+j 60) / 9=\underline{\underline{0.8+ j 0.8}} \Omega \text { secondary }\)

Set the B12 zone 3 relay for 100% reach of line 1–2 and 120% reach of line 2–3:

\(Z_{r 3}=1.0(6+j 60) / 9+1.2(5+j 50) / 9=\underline{\underline{1.3+j 13.33}} \Omega \text { secondary }\)

 

(b) The secondary impedance viewed by B12 during emergency loading is:

\(Z^{\prime}=\left(\frac{\bar{V}_{L N}}{\bar{I}_{L}}\right)\left(\frac{1}{9}\right)=\left(\frac{\frac{500}{\sqrt{3}} \angle 0^{\circ}}{1.4 \angle-\cos ^{-1} 0.9}\right) \frac{1}{9}=22.9 \angle 25.8^{\circ} \Omega\)

\(\bar{Z}^{\prime}\) exceeds the zone 3 setting of \((1.3+j 13.33)=13.399 \angle 84.3^{\circ} \Omega\) for B12. Hence, the impedance during emergency loading lies outside the trip region of this 3-zone mho relay (see Figure 10.29 (b)).
