Guest Question.

Determine the resultant internal loadings acting on the cross section at B of the pipe shown in Fig. 1–7 a . End A is subjected to a vertical force of 50 N, a horizontal force of 30 N, and a couple moment of 70 N # m . Neglect the pipe’s mass.

Admin
Solution

The problem can be solved by considering segment \(AB\) , so we do not need to calculate the support reactions at \(C .\)

Free-Body Diagram. The \(x\) , \(y\), \(z\) axes are established at \( B\) and the free-body diagram of segment \(AB\) is shown in Fig. 1–7 b . The resultant force and moment components at the section are assumed to act in the positive coordinate directions and to pass through the centroid of the cross-sectional area at \(B \).

Equations of Equilibrium. Applying the six scalar equations of equilibrium, we have *

\(\begin{array}{lll} \Sigma F_{x}=0 ; & & \left(F_{B}\right)_{x}=0 \\ \Sigma F_{y}=0 ; & \left(F_{B}\right)_{y}+30 \mathrm{~N}=0 & \left(F_{B}\right)_{y}=-30 \mathrm{~N} \\ \Sigma F_{z}=0 ; & \left(F_{B}\right)_{z}-50 \mathrm{~N}=0 & \left(F_{B}\right)_{z}=50 \mathrm{~N} \end{array}\).

\(\begin{array}{c} \Sigma\left(M_{B}\right)_{x}=0 ; \quad\left(M_{B}\right)_{x}+70 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m}-50 \mathrm{~N}(0.5 \mathrm{~m})=0 \\ \left(M_{B}\right)_{x}=-45 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m} \end{array}\).

\(\begin{array}{c} \Sigma\left(M_{B}\right)_{y}=0 ; \quad\quad\left(M_{B}\right)_{y}+50 \mathrm{~N}(1.25 \mathrm{~m})=0 \\ \left(M_{B}\right)_{y}=-62.5 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m} \end{array}\).

\(\begin{array}{r} \Sigma\left(M_{B}\right)_{z}=0 ; \quad\left(M_{B}\right)_{z}+(30 \mathrm{~N})(1.25)=0 \\ \left(M_{B}\right)_{z}=-37.5 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m} \end{array}\)

 

NOTE: What do the negative signs for \(\left(F_{B}\right)_{y}\), \(\left(M_{B}\right)_{x}\), \(\left(M_{B}\right)_{y}\), and \(\left(M_{B}\right)_{z}\) indicate? The normal force \(N_{B}=\left|\left(F_{B}\right)_{v}\right|=30 \mathrm{~N} \), whereas the shear force is \(V_{B}=\sqrt{(0)^{2}+(50)^{2}}=50 \mathrm{~N}\).

Also, the torsional moment is \(T_{B}=\left|\left(M_{B}\right)_{y}\right|=62.5 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m}\) and the bending moment is \(M_{B} =\) \(\sqrt{(45)^{2}+(37.5)^{2}}=58.6 \mathrm{~N} \cdot \mathrm{m}\)

* The magnitude of each moment about an axis is equal to the magnitude of each force times the perpendicular distance from the axis to the line of action of the force. The direction of each moment is determined using the right-hand rule, with positive moments (thumb) directed along the positive coordinate axes.
