Guest Question.

Finding the performance of a BJT amplifier with shunt-shunt feedback The parameters of the amplifier in Fig. 10.38 are \( R_{ C1 }=5 k \Omega, R_{ E }=2.5 k \Omega, R_{ C 2}=5 k \Omega, R_{ F }=4 k \Omega \text {, and } R_{ s }=200 \Omega \). The DC bias currents of the transistors are \( I_{ C1 }=0.5 mA \text { and } I_{ C 2}=1 mA \). The transistor parameters are \( h_{ fe }=h_{ fe1 }=h_{ fe 2}=150 \text { and } r_{\mu}=r_{ o }=\infty \).

(a) Use the techniques of feedback analysis to calculate the input resistance \( R_{ if } \), the output resistance \( R_{\text {of }} \), and the closed-loop transresistance gain \( A_{ f } \).

(b) Use PSpice/SPICE to check your results.

NOTE: The following analysis can also be applied to MOSFET amplifiers by substituting a very large
value of the B-E resistance \(r_\pi \) tending to infinity, \(1/r_\pi \simeq 0\).

Admin
Solution

For \( I_{ Cl }=0.5 mA,\) Eqs. (8.40) and (8.42) give

\(\frac{1}{r_{\pi}}=\frac{i_{ b }}{v_{ be }}=\left.\frac{d i_{ B }}{d v_{ BE }}\right|_{ at Q \text {-point }}=\frac{I_{ B }}{V_{ T }}=\frac{I_{ B }}{25.8 mV }\) (8.40)

 

\(g_{ m }=\frac{i_{ c }}{v_{ be }}=\left.\frac{\beta_{ F } d i_{ B }}{d v_{ BE }}\right|_{\text {at } Q \text {-point }}=\frac{\beta_{ f } I_{ B }}{V_{ T }}=\frac{I_{ C }}{V_{ T }}=\frac{\beta_{ f }}{r_{\pi}}\) (8.42)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&r_{\pi 1}=\frac{h_{ fe } \times 25.8 mV }{I_{ C 1}}=\frac{150 \times 25.8 mV }{0.5 mA }=7.74 k \Omega \\&g_{ m 1}=\frac{I_{ C 1}}{V_{ T }}=\frac{0.5 mA }{25.8 mV }=19.38 mA / V\end{aligned}\)

 

For \( I_{ C 2}=1 mA ,\)

\(\begin{aligned}&r_{\pi 2}=\frac{h_{ fe } \times 25.8 mV }{I_{ C 2}}=\frac{150 \times 25.8 mV }{1 mA }=3.87 k \Omega \\&g_{ m 2}=\frac{I_{ C 2}}{V_{ T }}=\frac{1 mA }{25.8 mV }=38.76 mA / V\end{aligned}\)

The feedback current \(i_{ f }\)is proportional to the output voltage \( v_{0} \). The effective input current to the amplifier is \( i_{ e }=i_{ i }-i_{ f } \).

(a) The steps in analyzing the feedback network are as follows:

Step 1. \( R_{ F } \) acts as the feedback network. The feedback current \( i_{ f } \) is proportional to the output voltage \( v_{ o } \). The effective input current to the amplifier is \( i_{ e }=i_{ i }-i_{ f } \). The functional block diagram is shown in Fig. 10.39(a).

Step 2. The amplifier uses shunt-shunt feedback, as shown in Fig. 10.39(b). Thus, the units of the gain A must be V/A.

Step 3. The effect of the feedback network can be taken into account at the input side by short-circuiting \( R_{ F } \) to the ground at side 2 in Fig. 10.39(b), and at the output side by short-circuiting \( R_{ F } \) to the ground at side 1. These modifications are shown in the small-signal AC equivalent circuit in Fig. 10.39(c).

Step 4. If we represent the amplifier in Fig. 10.39(c) by an equivalent transresistance amplifier, the resistance at the base of the amplifier is

\( R_{ b }=\frac{v_{ i }}{i_{ b 1}}=r_{\pi 1}+\left(1+g_{ m 1} r_{\pi 1}\right)\left(R_{ E } \| r_{\pi 2}\right)=7.74 k +151(2.5 k \| 3.87 k )=237 k \Omega \)

The input resistance of the amplifier is

\(\begin{aligned}R_{ ie } &=\frac{v_{ i }}{i_{ s }}=R_{ s }\left\|R_{ F }\right\|\left[r_{\pi 1}+\left(1+g_{ m 1} r_{\pi 1}\right)\left(R_{ E } \| r_{\pi 2}\right)\right] \\&=200\|4 k \|[7.74 k +151(2.5 k \| 3.87 k )]=190.3 \Omega\end{aligned}\)

The output resistance of the amplifier is

\( R_{ oe }=R_{ F }\left\|R_{ C 2}=4 k \right\| 5 k =2.22 k \Omega \)

Thus,

\( v_{ b 1}=R_{ ie } i_{ s }=190.3 i_{ s } \)

 

\(\begin{aligned}v_{ be 2} &=\frac{v_{ b 1} \times\left(1+g_{ m 1} r_{\pi 1}\right)\left(R_{ E } \| r_{\pi 2}\right)}{\left[r_{\pi 1}+\left(1+g_{ m 1} r_{\pi 1}\right)\left(R_{ E } \| r_{\pi 2}\right)\right]} \\&=\frac{\left(190.3 \times i_{ s }\right) \times(1+193.8 m \times 7.74 k )(2.5 k \| 3.87 k )}{[7.74 k +(1+193.8 m \times 7.74 k )(2.5 k \| 3.87 k )]}=184.11 i_{ s }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&i_{ o }=g_{ m 2} v_{ be 2}=38.76 m \times 184.11 i_{ s }=7.136 i_{ s } \\&v_{ o }=-\left(R_{ F } \| R_{ C 2}\right) i_{ o }=-(4 k \| 5 k ) \times 7.136 i_{ s }=-15.865 \times 10^{3} \times i_{ s }\end{aligned}\)

 

Therefore, the open-loop transresistance A is given by \( A=v_{ o } / i_{ s }=-15.865 kA / V.\)

Step 5. Using Eq. (10.66), we find that the feedback factor β is

\(\beta=\left.\frac{i_{ f }}{v_{ o }}\right|_{v_{ f }=0}\) (short-circuited input side) (10.66)

 

\( \beta=\left.\frac{i_{ f }}{v_{ o }}\right|_{v_{ f }=0}=-\frac{1}{R_{ F }}=-\frac{1}{4 k }=-0.25 mA / V \)

and

\( \beta A_{ e }=-15.865 kV / A \times\left(-\frac{1}{4 kV / A }\right)=3.966 \)

Step 6. The input resistance seen by the current source is

\( R_{ if }=\frac{v_{ i }}{i_{ s }}=\frac{R_{ ie }}{1+\beta A_{ e }}=\frac{190.3 \Omega}{1+0.25 mA / V \times 15.865 kV / A }=38.3 \Omega \)

Step 7. The output resistance with feedback is

\( R_{ of }=\frac{R_{ oc }}{1+\beta A_{ e }}=\frac{2.22 k \Omega}{1+0.25 mA / V \times 15.865 kV / A }=447 \Omega \)

Step 8. The closed-loop transresistance gain \( A_{ f } \) is

\( A_{ f }=\frac{v_{ o }}{i_{ s }}=\frac{-15.865 kV / A }{1+0.25 mA / V \times 15.865 kV / A }=-3.195 kV / A \)

(b) The shunt-shunt feedback amplifier for PSpice simulation is shown in Fig. 10.40. The results of PSpice/SPICE simulation (.TF analysis) are shown below, with hand calculations to the right:

\(\begin{array}{ll}V (5) / I s =-3.19 E +03=-3.19 kV / A & A_{ f }=-3.195 kV / A \\\text { INPUT RESISTANCE AT IS=3.854E+01=38.54 } \Omega & R_{ in }=38.3 \Omega \\\text { OUTPUT RESISTANCE AT V(5)=4.5E+02=450 } \Omega & R_{ of }=447 \Omega\end{array}\)

The PSpice results are very close to the hand-calculated values.
