Guest Question.

Figure 11.24 shows a load being fed by a voltage source through a transmission line. The impedance of the line is represented by the \((4 + j2) 立\) impedance and a return path. Find the real power and reactive power absorbed by: (a) the source, (b) the line, and (c) the load.

Admin
Solution

The total impedance is

\(Z =(4 + j 2) + (15 – j 10)=19 – j 8=20.62 \angle -22.83^{\circ} \Omega\)

The current through the circuit is

\(I =\frac{ V _{s}}{ Z }=\frac{220 \angle 0^{\circ}}{20.62 \angle-22.83^{\circ}}=10.67 \angle 22.83^{\circ}A rms\)

(a) For the source, the complex power is

\(\begin{aligned}S _{s}= V _{s} I ^{*} &=\left(220 \angle 0^{\circ}\right)\left(10.67 \angle-22.83^{\circ}\right) \\&=2347.4 \angle-22.83^{\circ}=(2163.5 – j 910.8) VA\end{aligned}\)

From this, we obtain the real power as 2163.5 W and the reactive power as 910.8 VAR (leading).
(b) For the line, the voltage is

\(\begin{aligned}V _{\text {line }}=(4 + j 2) I &=\left(4.472 \angle 26.57^{\circ}\right)\left(10.67 \angle22.83^{\circ}\right) \\&=47.72 \angle 49.4^{\circ} V rms\end{aligned}\)

The complex power absorbed by the line is

\(\begin{aligned}S _{\text {line }}= V _{\text {line }} I ^{*} &=\left(47.72 \angle 49.4^{\circ}\right)\left(10.67 \angle-22.83^{\circ}\right) \\&=509.2 \angle 26.57^{\circ}=455.4 + j 227.7 VA\end{aligned}\)

or

\(S _{\text {line }}=| I |^{2} Z _{\text {line }}=(10.67)^{2}(4 + j 2)=455.4 + j 227.7 VA\)

That is, the real power is 455.4 W and the reactive power is 227.76 VAR (lagging).
(c) For the load, the voltage is

\(\begin{aligned}V _{L}=(15-j 10) I &=\left(18.03 \angle-33.7^{\circ}\right)\left(10.67 \angle 22.83^{\circ}\right) \\&=192.38 \angle-10.87^{\circ} Vrms\end{aligned}\)

The complex power absorbed by the load is

\(\begin{aligned}S _{L}= V _{L} I ^{*} &=\left(192.38 \angle-10.87^{\circ}\right)\left(10.67 \angle-22.83^{\circ}\right) \\&=2053 \angle-33.7^{\circ}=(1708-j 1139) VA\end{aligned}\)

The real power is 1708 W and the reactive power is 1139 VAR (leading). Note that \( S _{s}= S _{\text {line }}+ S _{L} \) as expected. We have used the rms values of voltages and currents.
