Guest Question.

Determine the magnitude R of the resultant of the four forces shown in Fig. 2-35a and the angle \(θ_x\) between the x-axis and the line of action of the resultant.

Admin
Solution

The magnitude R of the resultant will be determined by using the rectangular components \(F_x\) and \(F_y\) of each of the forces. Thus

\(F_{1x}=80cos140^\circ =-61.28lb \quad \quad \quad F_{1y}=80sin140^\circ =+51.42lb\)

 

\(F_{2x}=60cos110^\circ =-20.52lb \quad \quad \quad F_{2y}=60sin110^\circ =+56.38lb\)

 

\(F_{3x}=75cos45^\circ =+53.03lb \quad \quad \quad F_{3y}=75sin45^\circ =+59.03lb\)

 

\(F_{4x}=90cos17^\circ =+86.07lb \quad \quad \quad F_{4y}=90sin17^\circ =+26.31lb\)

Once the rectangular components of the forces are known, the components \(R_x\) and \(R_y\) of the resultant are obtained from the expressions

\(R_x=\sum{F_x}=F_{1x}+F_{2x}+F_{3x} +F_{4x}\)

 

\(=-60.28-20.52+53.03+86.07=+57.30lb\)

 

\(R_y=\sum{F_y}=F_{1y}+F_{2y}+F_{3y} +F_{4y}\)

 

\(=+51.42+56.38+53.03+26.31=+187.14lb\)

The magnitude R of the resultant is

\(R=\sqrt{R_x^2+R_y^2}=\sqrt{(57.30)^2+(187.14)^2}=195.7lb \)

The angle \(θ_x\) is obtained from the expression

\(\theta _x=tan^{-1}\frac{R_y}{R_x}=tan^{-1}\frac{+187..14}{+57.30}=73.0^\circ \)
