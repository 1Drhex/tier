Guest Question.

Product distribution for parallel reactions.

We wish to produce R in the following reaction:

\(A + B \rightarrow R , \quad r_{1}=\frac{k_{1} C_{A} C_{B}^{1 / 2}}{1+K_{A} C_{A}+K_{B} C_{B}^{1 / 2}}\) (E5.7.1)

Unfortunately a side reaction occurs, where product P is formed

\(A + B \rightarrow P , \quad r_{2}=\frac{k_{2} C_{B}}{1+K_{A} C_{A}+K_{B} C_{B}^{1 / 2}}\) (E5.7.2)

For 90% conversion of A, find the concentration of R in the product stream as a function of the reaction rate constants. Equal volumetric flow rates of the A and of the B streams are fed to the reactor, and each stream has a concentration of 20 mol/L of the said reactant. The rate parameters are— \( k_{1}=0.1 mol ^{-1 / 2} \cdot L ^{1 / 2} \cdot min ^{-1} ; k_{2}=0.2 min ^{-1} ; K_{ A }=10 mol ^{-1} \cdot l ^{-1} ; \text { and } K_{ B }=5 mol ^{-1 / 2} \cdot L ^{1 / 2}\).The flow in the reactor follows:

a. PFR;
b. CSTR;
c. The best of the three PFR contacting (feeding) schemes in Fig. 5.12.

Solution

As a warning, be careful to get the concentrations and flow rates right when you mix the streams. To begin with, we find the differential selectivity of the desired product

\(S_{R / A}=\frac{\left.d F_{A}\right|_{\text {due to the formation of } R}}{\left.d F_{A}\right|_{\text {Total }}}=\frac{r_{1 A}}{r_{1 A}+r_{2 A}}=\frac{k_{1} C_{A}}{k_{1} C_{A}+k_{2} C_{B}^{1 / 2}}\) (E5.7.3)

One can solve the problem by performing mole balances on each species and solve the differential equations using an automatic integrator. We can also do it more illustrative by using the differential selectivity as defined by Eq. (E5.7.3) \(S_{R / A}=\frac{\left.d F_{A}\right|_{\text {due to the formation of } R}}{\left.d F_{A}\right|_{\text {Total }}}=\frac{r_{1 A}}{r_{1 A}+r_{2 A}}=\frac{k_{1} C_{A}}{k_{1} C_{A}+k_{2} C_{B}^{1 / 2}}\) . This is the approach we will be taking here.

a. PFR. Referring to Fig. 5.12A, noting that the starting concentration of each reactant in the combined feed is \(C_{ A 0}=C_{ B0 } \) = 20 mol/L ÷ 2 = 10 mol/L. Based on the stoichiometry, for every mole of A being consumed, one mole of B is also consumed (both desired and side reactions). This leads to \(C_{ A }=C_{ B } \) everywhere in the reactor. Thus,

\(S_{R / A}=\frac{\left.\Delta F_{A}\right|_{\text {duetotheformationofR }}}{\left.\Delta F_{A}\right|_{\text {Total }}}=\frac{\int_{0}^{f_{\text {Ae }}} S_{R / A} d f_{A}}{f_{\text {Ae }}-0}=\frac{1}{f_{A e}} \int_{0}^{f_{Ae}} \frac{k_{1} C_{A}}{k_{1} C_{A}+k_{2} C_{B}^{1 / 2}} d f_{A}=\frac{1}{f_{A e}} \int_{0}^{f_{Ae}} \frac{k_{1}}{k_{1}+k_{2} C_{A}^{-1 / 2}} d f_{A} \) (E5.7.4)

At this point, we can either work with concentration \(C_{ A }\) or the conversion \(f_{ A }\). The flow rate Q remains constant in the reactor. Let us use the concentration for this case:

\(f_{A}=\frac{Q C_{A 0}-Q C_{A}}{Q C_{A 0}}=\frac{C_{A 0}-C_{A}}{C_{A 0}} \Rightarrow d f_{A}=-\frac{d C_{A}}{C_{A 0}}\) (E5.7.5)

which leads to

\(S_{R / A}=\frac{1}{f_{A e}} \int_{0}^{f_{A e}} \frac{k_{1}}{k_{1}+k_{2} C_{A}^{-\frac{1}{2}}} d f_{A}=\frac{-1}{C_{A 0} f_{A e}} \int_{C_{A 0}}^{C_{A e}} \frac{k_{1}}{k_{1}+k_{2} C_{A}^{-\frac{1}{2}}} d C_{A}\)

 

\(=\frac{-1}{C_{A 0} f_{A e}} \int_{C_{A0}^{\frac{1}{2}}}^{C_{Ae}^{\frac{1}{2}}} \frac{k_{1}}{k_{1}+k_{2} x^{-1}} d x^{2} =\frac{-1}{C_{A 0} f_{A e}} \int_{C_{A0}^{\frac{1}{2}}}^{C_{Ae}^{\frac{1}{2}}} \frac{2 k_{1} x^{2}}{k_{1} x+k_{2}} d x \)

 

\(=\frac{-2}{C_{A 0} f_{A e}} \int_{C_{A 0}^{\frac{1}{2}}}^{C_{Ae}^{\frac{1}{2}}}\frac{x\left\lgroup x+\frac{k_{2}}{k_{1}} \right\rgroup -\frac{k_{2}}{k_{1}}\left\lgroup x+\frac{k_{2}}{k_{1}} \right\rgroup +\left\lgroup \frac{k_{2}}{k_{1}}\right \rgroup ^{2}}{x+\frac{k_{2}}{k_{1}}} d x\) (E5.7.6)

 

\(=\frac{-2}{C_{A 0} f_{A e}}\left[\frac{x^{2}}{2}-\frac{k_{2}}{k_{1}} x+\left\lgroup \frac{k_{2}}{k_{1}} \right\rgroup ^{2} \ln\left\lgroup x+\frac{k_{2}}{k_{1}} \right\rgroup \right]^{ C_{A e}^{\frac{1}{2}}}_{ C_{A 0}^{\frac{1}{2}} } \)

 

\(=\frac{2}{C_{A 0} f_{A e}}\left[\frac{C_{A 0}-C_{A e}}{2}-\frac{k_{2}}{k_{1}}\left\lgroup C_{A 0}^{\frac{1}{2}}-C_{A e}^{\frac{1}{2}} \right\rgroup +\left\lgroup \frac{k_{2}}{k_{1}} \right\rgroup ^{2} \ln \frac{k_{1} C_{A 0}^{\frac{1}{2}}+k_{2}}{k_{1} C_{A e}^{\frac{1}{2}}+k_{2}}\right]\)

Substituting \(f_{\text {Ae }}=0.9, C_{ A 0}=10 mol / L , C_{ Ae }=(1-0.9) \times 10 mol / L =1 mol / L , k_{1}=0.1 mol ^{-1 / 2} \cdot L ^{1 / 2} \cdot min ^{-1} \text { and } k_{2}=0.2 min ^{-1}\) into above, we obtain

\(S_{R / A}=\frac{2}{C_{A 0} f_{A e}}\left[\frac{C_{A 0}-C_{A e}}{2}-\frac{k_{2}}{k_{1}}\left\lgroup C_{A 0}^{\frac{1}{2}}-C_{A e}^{\frac{1}{2}} \right\rgroup +\left\lgroup \frac{k_{2}}{k_{1}} \right\rgroup ^{2} \ln \frac{k_{1} C_{A 0}^{1 / 2}+k_{2}}{k_{1} C_{A e}^{1 / 2}+k_{2}}\right]\)

 

\(=\frac{2}{10 \times 0.9}\left[\frac{10-1}{2}-\frac{0.2}{0.1}\left\lgroup 10^{\frac{1}{2}}-1^{\frac{1}{2}} \right\rgroup +\left\lgroup \frac{0.2}{0.1} \right\rgroup ^{2} \ln \frac{0.1 \times 10^{1 / 2}+0.2}{0.1 \times 1^{1 / 2}+0.2}\right]\) (E5.7.7)

 

\(=\frac{1}{9}\left[9-4 \sqrt{10}+4+8 \ln \frac{2+\sqrt{10}}{3}\right]=0.521445\)

The final concentrations for the products are:

\(C_{R e}=S_{R / A} \frac{v_{1 R}}{-v_{1 A}}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A e}\right)=4.693 mol / L \)

 

\(\text { and } C_{P e }=\left(1-S_{R / A}\right) \frac{v_{2 P}}{-v_{2 A}}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A e}\right)=4.307 mol / L\)

b. CSTR

Fig. E5.7.1 shows a sketch of the CSTR. One can find the parameters already known as \(f_{ A }=0.9, C_{ A0 }=C_{ B0 }=10 mol / L\), \(C_{ A }=C_{ B }=(1-0.9) \times 10 mol / L =1 mol / L\). The selectivity of product R is given by

\(S_{R / A}=S_{R / A}=\frac{k_{1} C_{A}}{k_{1} C_{A}+k_{2} C_{B}^{1 / 2}}=\frac{0.1 \times 1}{0.1 \times 1+0.2 \times \sqrt{1}}=\frac{1}{3}\) (E5.7.8)

The product stream concentration can be computed as

\(C_{R e}=S_{R / A} \frac{v_{1 R}}{-v_{1 A}}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A e}\right)=3 mol / L\)

 

\(\text { and } C_{P e}=\left(1-S_{R / A}\right) \frac{v_{2 P}}{-v_{2 A}}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A e}\right)=6 mol / L\)

c. From Eq. (E5.7.3) \(S_{R / A}=\frac{\left.d F_{A}\right|_{\text {due to the formation of } R}}{\left.d F_{A}\right|_{\text {Total }}}=\frac{r_{1 A}}{r_{1 A}+r_{2 A}}=\frac{k_{1} C_{A}}{k_{1} C_{A}+k_{2} C_{B}^{1 / 2}}\) , we infer that the higher the concentration of A and the lower the concentration of B in the reactor will give the highest selectivity to the desired product R. In this case, feeding A from the beginning will render the highest concentration of A available to participate in the reaction. The lowest concentration of B achievable is the concentration at the end of the reactor. Therefore, scheme Fig. E5.7.1C will give the highest selectivity to R.

In this case, the volumetric flow rate Q and the concentration of A are changing in the reactor, while the concentration of B is desired to be constant that equals to the concentration of B at the end of the reaction (90% conversion), \( C_{ B }=(1-0.9) \times C_{ B 0}^{\prime} / 2=1 mol / L \). We can either solve the problem by direct mole balance and integration (with an automatic integrator), or find out how the concentration is to vary with conversion before we can follow the same steps as we did in part (a).

To aid the solution of the problem, we resketched the PFR feeding scheme diagram as shown in Fig. E5.7.2 and a blowout of the differential section between V and V + dV in Fig. E5.7.3. Both concentration of A and volumetric rate Q are changing in the reactor, but not independently from each other. We thus need to Figure out how these two are related.

1. Mole balance on A:

\(F_{ A \mid V }-F_{ A \mid V + dV }+r_{ A } d V=0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad d F_{ A }=r_{ A } d V\) (E5.7.9)

2. Assuming the density of the B stream is the same as the density of the A stream, as well as to the mixture, mole balance on B:

Flow in + Side Streamin −Out +Gene-ration = Accumulation(0 at steady state)

\(\left.F_{B}\right|_{V}+C_{B 0}^{\prime} d Q-\left.F_{B}\right|_{V+d V}+r_{B} d V=0\) (E5.7.10)

Since \(C_{B}\) = constant and from stoichiometry, \(r_{B}=r_{ A }\), we obtain

\(r_{A} d V+\underset{ \Downarrow }{\left(C_{B 0}^{\prime}-C_{B}\right)} d Q=0\) (E5.7.11)

 

\(\left(C_{B 0}^{\prime}-C_{B}\right) d Q=-r_{A} d V=-d F_{A}=F_{A 0} d f_{A}\) (E5.7.12)

which yields after integration

\(\left(C_{B 0}^{\prime}-C_{B}\right)\left(Q-Q_{0}\right)=F_{A 0} f_{A}\) (E5.7.13)

To find \(Q_{0}\), we refer to Fig. E5.7.2 for clarity. Mass balance of B for the feed stream to the PFR lead to:

\(\left(Q-Q_{0}\right) C_{B 0}^{\prime}\underset{ \Downarrow }{=} Q_{0} C_{B}\) (E5.7.14)

 

\(Q_{0}=\frac{Q_{A} C_{B}}{C_{B 0}^{\prime}-C_{B}}\) (E5.7.15)

Thus,

\(Q=Q_{0}+\frac{F_{A 0}}{C_{B 0}^{\prime}-C_{B}} f_{A}=\frac{F_{A 0}}{C_{B 0}^{\prime}-C_{B}}\left(1+f_{A}\right)\) (E5.7.16)

or

\(C_{A}=\frac{F_{A}}{Q}=\frac{F_{A 0}\left(1-f_{A}\right)}{\frac{F_{A 0}}{C_{B 0}^{\prime}-C_{B}}\left(1+f_{A}\right)}=\left(C_{B 0}^{\prime}-C_{B}\right) \frac{1-f_{A}}{1+f_{A}}\) (E5.7.17)

Having established how the concentration changes with the conversion, we can then employ the selectivity to solve the problem.

\(s_{R / A}=\frac{k_{1} C_{A}}{k_{1} C_{A}+k_{2} C_{B}^{1 / 2}}=\frac{k_{1}\left(C_{B 0}^{\prime}-C_{B}\right) \frac{1-f_{A}}{1+f_{A}}}{k_{1}\left(C_{B 0}^{\prime}-C_{B}\right) \frac{1-f_{A}}{1+f_{A}}+k_{2} C_{B}^{1 / 2}}\)

 

\(=\frac{k_{1}\left(C_{B 0}^{\prime}-C_{B}\right)\left(1-f_{A}\right)}{k_{1}\left(C_{B 0}^{\prime}-C_{B}\right)+k_{2} C_{B}^{1 / 2}-\left[k_{1}\left(C_{B 0}^{\prime}-C_{B}\right)-k_{2} C_{B}^{1 / 2}\right] f_{A}}\) (E5.7.18)

 

\(=\frac{19\left(1-f_{A}\right)}{21-17 f_{A}}\)

which can be integrated to yield the overall selectivity:

\(S_{R / A}=\frac{\left.\Delta F_{A}\right|_{\text {due to the fomation of } R}}{\left.\Delta F_{A}\right|_{\text {Total }}}=\frac{1}{f_{A e}-0} \int_{0}^{f_{\text {Ae }}} s_{R / A} d f_{A}\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{f_{A e}} \int_{0}^{f_{Ae}} \frac{19\left(1-f_{A}\right)}{21-17 f_{A}} d f_{A}=\frac{19}{17 f_{A e}} \int_{0}^{f_{Ae}}\left[1-\frac{4}{21-17 f_{A}}\right] d f_{A}\)

 

\(=\frac{19}{17 f_{A e}}\left\lgroup f_{A e}+\frac{4}{17} \ln \frac{21-17 f_{A}}{21} \right\rgroup =\frac{19}{0.9 \times 17}\left\lgroup 0.9+\frac{4}{17} \ln \frac{21-0.9 \times 17}{21} \right\rgroup\)

 

\(S_{R / A}=0.73661\)

The product stream concentration can be computed as

\(C_{R e}=S_{R / A} \frac{v_{1 R}}{-v_{1 A}}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A e}\right)=6.6295 mol / L \)

 

\(\text { and } C_{P e}=\left(1-S_{R / A}\right) \frac{v_{2 P}}{-v_{2 A}}\left(C_{A 0}-C_{A e}\right)=2.3705 mol / L\)

As a comparison, here is how the three types of reactor arrangement will do to the final product mixture for the particular problem:

 

PFR \(S_{ R / A}=0.19066\), \(C_{ Re }=4.693 mol / L\)
CSTR \(S_{ R / A}=0.33333\), \(C_{ Re }=3 mol / L\)
PFR with optimum feed \(S_{ R / A}=0.73661\), \(C_{ Re }=6.6295 mol / L\)

 

The reactor feed strategy greatly affects the product mixture. PFR with optimum (distributed) feed yields significantly more desired product.
