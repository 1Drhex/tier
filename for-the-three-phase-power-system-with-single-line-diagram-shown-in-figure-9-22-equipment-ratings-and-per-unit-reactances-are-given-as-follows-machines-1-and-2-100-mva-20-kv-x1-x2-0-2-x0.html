Guest Question.

For the three-phase power system with single-line diagram shown in Figure 9.22, equipment ratings and per-unit reactances are given as follows:

Machines 1 and 2: \(\begin{array}{lll}100 MVA & 20 kV & X _{1}= X _{2}=0.2\end{array}\)

\(X _{0}=0.04 \quad X _{n}=0.04\)

Transformers 1 and 2: \(100 MVA \quad 20 \Delta / 345 Y kV\)

\(X _{1}= X _{2}= X _{0}=0.08\)

Select a base of 100 MVA, 345 kV for the transmission line. On that base, the series reactances of the line are \(X _{1}= X _{2}=0.15 \text { and } X _{0}=0.5\) per unit. With a nominal system voltage of 345 kV at bus 3, machine 2 is operating as a motor drawing 50 MVA at 0.8 power factor lagging. Compute the change in voltage at bus 3 when the transmission line undergoes (a) a one-conductor-open fault, (b) a two-conductor-open fault along its span between buses 2 and 3.

\(\bar{Z}_{B U S 1}=\bar{Z}_{B U S 2}= \begin{matrix}(1)\\(2)\\(3)\\(4)\end{matrix}\overset{\begin{matrix}(1) &(2) &(3) & (4)\end{matrix}}{\left[\begin{array}{cccc}j 0.1437 & j 0.1211 & j 0.0789 & j 0.0563 \\j 0.1211 & j 0.1696 & j 0.1104 & j 0789 \\j 0.0789 & j 0.1104 & j 0.1696 & j 0.1211 \\j 0.0563 & j 0.0789 & j 0.1211 & j 0.1437\end{array}\right]} \)

 

\(\bar{Z}_{B U S 0}= \begin{matrix}(1)\\(2)\\(3)\\(4)\end{matrix}\overset{\begin{matrix}(1) & (2) & (3) & (4)\end{matrix}}{\left[\begin{array}{cccc}j 0.16 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\0 & j 0.08 & j 0.08 & 0 \\0 & j 0.08 & j 0.58 & 0 \\0 & 0 & 0 & j 0.16\end{array}\right]} \)

 

Choosing the voltage at bus 3 as \(1 \angle 0^{\circ}\) , the prefault current in line (2) – (3) is

\(\bar{I}_{23}=\frac{P-j Q}{\bar{V}_{3}^{*}}=\frac{0.5(0.8-j 0.6)}{1 \angle 0^{\circ}}=0.4-j 0.3 pu\)

Line (2) – (3) has parameters given by

\(\bar{Z}_{1}=\bar{Z}_{2}=j 0.15 ; \bar{Z}_{0}=j 0.5\)

Denoting the open-circuit points of the line as p and p′ , to simulate opening, we need to develop the Thévenin-equivalent sequence networks looking into the system between points p and p′ .

Before any conductor opens, the current \(\bar{I}_{m n}\) in phase a of the line (m) – (n) is positive sequence, given by

\(\bar{I}_{m n}=\frac{\bar{V}_{m}-\bar{V}_{n}}{\bar{Z}_{1}}\)

 

\(\bar{Z}_{p p^{\prime} 1}=-\frac{\bar{Z}_{1}^{2}}{\bar{Z}_{T h, m n, 1}-\bar{Z}_{1}} ; \bar{Z}_{p p^{\prime} 2}=\frac{-\bar{Z}_{2}^{2}}{\bar{Z}_{T h, m n, 2}-\bar{Z}_{2}} ; \bar{Z}_{p p^{\prime} 0}=\frac{-\bar{Z}_{0}^{2}}{\bar{Z}_{T h, m n, 0}-\bar{Z}_{0}}\)

In this Problem

\(\begin{gathered}\bar{Z}_{p p^{\prime} 1}=\bar{Z}_{p p^{\prime 2}}=\frac{-\bar{Z}_{1}^{2}}{\bar{Z}_{22 1}+\bar{Z}_{33 1}-2 \bar{Z}_{23 1}-\bar{Z}_{1}}=\frac{-j(0.15)^{2}}{j 0.1696+j 0.1696-2(j 0.1104)-j 0.15} \\=j 0.7120\end{gathered}\)

 

\(\begin{gathered}\bar{Z}_{p p^{\prime} 0}=\frac{-\bar{Z}_{0}^{2}}{\bar{Z}_{220}+\bar{Z}_{33 0}-2 \bar{Z}_{23 0}-\bar{Z}_{0}}=\frac{-(j 0.5)^{2}}{j 0.08+j 0.58-2(j 0.08)-j 0.5} \\=\infty\end{gathered}\)

 

Note that an infinite impedance is seen looking into the zero sequence network between points p and p′ of the opening, if the line from bus (2) to bus (3) is opened. Also bus (3) would be isolated from the reference by opening the connection between bus (2) and bus (3).

(a) One open conductor:

\(\begin{gathered}\bar{V}_{a 0}=\bar{V}_{a 1}=\bar{V}_{a 2}=\bar{I}_{23} \frac{\bar{Z}_{p p^{\prime} 1} \bar{Z}_{p p^{\prime} 2}}{\bar{Z}_{p p^{\prime} 1}+\bar{Z}_{p p^{\prime} 2}}=(0.4-j 0.3) \frac{(j 0.712)(j 0.712)}{j(0.712+0.712)} \\=0.1068+j 0.1424\end{gathered}\)

 

\(\begin{gathered}\Delta \bar{V}_{31}=\Delta \bar{V}_{32}=\frac{\bar{Z}_{32 1}-\bar{Z}_{33 1}}{\bar{Z}_{1}} \bar{V}_{a 1}=\frac{j 0.1104-j 0.1696}{j 0.15}(0.1068+j 0.1424) \\=-0.0422-j 0.0562\end{gathered}\)

 

\(\begin{gathered}\Delta \bar{V}_{30}=\frac{\bar{Z}_{320}-\bar{Z}_{330}}{\bar{Z}_{0}} \bar{V}_{a 0}=\frac{j 0.08-j 0.58}{j 0.5}(0.1068+j 0.1424) \\=-0.1068-j 0.1424\end{gathered}\)

 

\(\begin{gathered}\Delta \bar{V}_{3}=\Delta \bar{V}_{30}+\Delta \bar{V}_{31}+\Delta \bar{V}_{32}=-0.1068-j 0.1424-2(0.0422+j 0.0562) \\=-0.1912-j 0.2548\end{gathered}\)

Since the prefault voltage at bus (3) is 1 ∠0 ° , the new voltage at bus (3) is

\(\bar{V_3}+Δ\bar{V_3}=(1+j0)+(-0.1912-j0.2548)\\=0.8088-j0.2548=0.848 \angle -17.5^\circ pu\)

(b) Two open conductors:
Inserting an infinite impedance of the zero sequence network in series between points p and p′ of the positive-sequence network causes an open circuit in the latter. No power transfer can occur in the system. Obviously, power cannot be transferred by only one phase conductor of the transmission line, since the zero sequence network offers no return path for current.
