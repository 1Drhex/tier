Guest Question.

Determine the moments at each joint of the frame shown in Fig. \(11-22 (\mathrm{a})\). \(EI\) is constant for each member.

Admin
Solution

Slope-Deflection Equations. Equation 11–8 applies to each of the three spans. The FEMs are

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=2 E k\left(2 \theta_{N}+\theta_{F}-3 \psi\right)+(\text { FEM })_{N}\\ \text For Internal Span or End Span with Far End Fixed \end{array} \) (11-8)

\(\begin{array}{l} (\mathrm{FEM})_{B C}=-\frac{w L^{2}}{12}=-\frac{2(12)^{2}}{12}=-24 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ (\mathrm{FEM})_{C B}=\frac{w L^{2}}{12}=\frac{2(12)^{2}}{12}=24 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \end{array} \)

The sloping member \(A B\) causes the frame to sidesway to the right as shown in Fig. 11-22a. As a result, joints \(B\) and \(C\) are subjected to both rotational and linear displacements. The linear displacements are shown in Fig. 11-22b, where \(B\) moves \(\Delta_{1}\) to \(B^{\prime}\) and \(C\) moves \(\Delta_{3}\) to \(C^{\prime}\). These displacements cause the members’ cords to rotate \(\psi_{1}, \psi_{3}\) (clockwise) and \(-\psi_{2}\) (counterclockwise) as shown.* Hence,

\( \psi_{1}=\frac{\Delta_{1}}{10} \quad \psi_{2}=-\frac{\Delta_{2}}{12} \quad \psi_{3}=\frac{\Delta_{3}}{20} \)

As shown in Fig. 11-22c, the three displacements can be related. For example, \(\Delta_{2}=0.5 \Delta_{1}\) and \(\Delta_{3}=0.866 \Delta_{1}\). Thus, from the above equations we have

\( \psi_{2}=-0.417 \psi_{1} \quad \psi_{3}=0.433 \psi_{1} \)

Using these results, the slope-deflection equations for the frame are

*Recall that distortions due to axial forces are neglected and the arc displacements \(B B^{\prime}\) and \(C C^{\prime}\) can be considered as straight lines, since \(\psi_{1}\) and \(\psi_{3}\) are actually very small.

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{A B}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{10}\right)\left[2(0)+\theta_{B}-3 \psi_{1}\right]+0 \\ \end{array} \) (1)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{B A}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{10}\right)\left[2 \theta_{B}+0-3 \psi_{1}\right]+0 \\ \end{array} \) (2)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{B C}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{12}\right)\left[2 \theta_{B}+\theta_{C}-3\left(-0.417 \psi_{1}\right)\right]-24 \\ \end{array} \) (3)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{C B}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{12}\right)\left[2 \theta_{C}+\theta_{B}-3\left(-0.417 \psi_{1}\right)\right]+24 \\ \end{array} \) (4)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{C D}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{20}\right)\left[2 \theta_{C}+0-3\left(0.433 \psi_{1}\right)\right]+0 \\ \end{array} \) (5)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{D C}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{20}\right)\left[2(0)+\theta_{C}-3\left(0.433 \psi_{1}\right)\right]+0 \end{array} \) (6)

These six equations contain nine unknowns.

Equations of Equilibrium. Moment equilibrium at joints \(B\) and \(C\) yields

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{B A}+M_{B C}=0 \\ \end{array} \) (7)

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{C D}+M_{C B}=0 \end{array} \) (8)

The necessary third equilibrium equation can be obtained by summing moments about point \(O\) on the entire frame, Fig. 11-22d. This eliminates the unknown normal forces \(\mathbf{N}_{A}\) and \(\mathbf{N}_{D}\), and therefore

\(\curvearrowright +\Sigma M_{O}=0\);

\( \begin{array}{c} M_{A B}+M_{D C}-\left(\frac{M_{A B}+M_{B A}}{10}\right)(34)-\left(\frac{M_{D C}+M_{C D}}{20}\right)(40.78)-24(6)=0 \\ -2.4 M_{A B}-3.4 M_{B A}-2.04 M_{C D}-1.04 M_{D C}-144=0 \end{array} \) (9)

Substituting Eqs. (2) and (3) into Eq. (7), Eqs. (4) and (5) into Eq. (8), and Eqs. (1), (2), (5), and (6) into Eq. (9) yields

\( \begin{aligned} 0.733 \theta_{B}+0.167 \theta_{C}-0.392 \psi_{1} &=\frac{24}{E I} \\ 0.167 \theta_{B}+0.533 \theta_{C}+0.0784 \psi_{1} &=-\frac{24}{E I} \\ -1.840 \theta_{B}-0.512 \theta_{C}+3.880 \psi_{1} &=\frac{144}{E I} \end{aligned} \)

Solving these equations simultaneously yields

\( E I \theta_{B}=87.67 \quad E I \theta_{C}=-82.3 \quad E I \psi_{1}=67.83 \)

Substituting these values into Eqs. (1)-(6), we have

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{A B}=-23.2 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft}\quad M_{B C}=5.63 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \quad M_{C D}=-25.3 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \\ M_{B A}=-5.63 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft}\quad M_{C B}=25.3 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft}\quad M_{D C}=-17.0 \mathrm{k} \cdot \mathrm{ft} \end{array} \)
