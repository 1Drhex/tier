Guest Question.

Again consider the 3 degrees-of-freedom system of and determine the steady state response when a downward force \(\ F=F_{0}sin\omega _{f}t \) is applied to mass 2.

Admin
Solution

For the given conditions, the applied nodal force vector is

\(\ \left\{F(t)\right\}=\left \{ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ F_{0}sin\omega _{f} t \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right \} \)

and the generalized forces are

\(\ \left[A\right] ^{T}\left\{F\right\} =\frac{1}{\sqrt{m} } \left [ \begin{matrix} 0.2956 & 0.4209 & 0.6064 \\ 0.6575 & 0.5618 & -0.3550 \\ 0.6930 & -0.7124 & 0.0782 \end{matrix} \right ] \left \{ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ F_{0}sin\omega _{f} t \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right \}=\left \{ \begin{matrix} 0.4209 \\ 0.5618 \\ −0.7124 \end{matrix} \right \} \frac{F_{0}sin\omega _{f} t}{\sqrt{m} } \)

The equations of motion for the generalized coordinates are then

\(\ \ddot{P} _{1} +\omega ^{2}_{1}P_{1}=\frac{0.4209F_{0}sin\omega _{f} t}{\sqrt{m} } \)

\(\ \ddot{P} _{2} +\omega ^{2}_{2}P_{2}=\frac{0.5618F_{0}sin\omega _{f} t}{\sqrt{m} } \)

\(\ \ddot{P} _{3} +\omega ^{2}_{3}P_{3}=\frac{-0.7124F_{0}sin\omega _{f} t}{\sqrt{m} } \)

for which the solutions are

\(\ P_{1}(t)=\frac{0.4209F_{0}sin\omega _{f} t}{(\omega ^{2}_{1}-\omega ^{2}_{f} )\sqrt{m} } \)

 

\(\ P_{2}(t)=\frac{0.5618_{0}sin\omega _{f} t}{(\omega ^{2}_{2}-\omega ^{2}_{f} )\sqrt{m} } \)

 

\(\ P_{3}(t)=\frac{-0.7124F_{0}sin\omega _{f} t}{(\omega ^{2}_{3}-\omega ^{2}_{f} )\sqrt{m} } \)

The actual displacements, x(t) = q(t) in this case, are obtained by application of Equation 10.112:

\(\ \left\{q\right\} =\left[A\right]\left\{P\right\} \) (10.112)

 

\(\ \left\{x\right\} =\left[A\right] \left\{P\right\} =\frac{1}{\sqrt{m} }\left [ \begin{matrix} 0.2956 & 0.6575 & 0.6930 \\ 0.4209 & 0.5618 & -0.7124 \\ 0.6064 & -0.3550 & 0.0782 \end{matrix} \right ] \left \{ \begin{matrix} \frac{0.4209}{\omega ^{2}_{1}-\omega ^{2}_{f}} \\ \frac{0.5618}{\omega ^{2}_{2}-\omega ^{2}_{f}} \\ \frac{-0.7124}{\omega ^{2}_{3}-\omega ^{2}_{f}} \end{matrix} \right \} \frac{F_{0}sin\omega _{f}t }{\sqrt{m} } \)

Expanding, the steady-state displacements are given by

\(\ x_{1}(t)=\left(\frac{0.1244}{\omega ^{2}_{1}-\omega ^{2}_{f}}+\frac{0.3694}{\omega ^{2}_{2}-\omega ^{2}_{f}}+\frac{-0.4937}{\omega ^{2}_{3}-\omega ^{2}_{f}}\right) \frac{F_{0}sin\omega _{f}t }{m} \)

\(\ x_{2}(t)=\left(\frac{0.1772}{\omega ^{2}_{1}-\omega ^{2}_{f}}+\frac{0.3156}{\omega ^{2}_{2}-\omega ^{2}_{f}}+\frac{0.5075}{\omega ^{2}_{3}-\omega ^{2}_{f}}\right) \frac{F_{0}sin\omega _{f}t }{m} \)

\(\ x_{1}(t)=\left(\frac{0.2552}{\omega ^{2}_{1}-\omega ^{2}_{f}}+\frac{-0.1994}{\omega ^{2}_{2}-\omega ^{2}_{f}}+\frac{-0.0557}{\omega ^{2}_{3}-\omega ^{2}_{f}}\right) \frac{F_{0}sin\omega _{f}t }{m} \)

A few observations need to be made regarding the displacements calculated in this example:

1. The displacement of each mass is a sinusoidal oscillation about the equilibrium position, and the circular frequency of the oscillation is the same as the frequency of the forcing function.

2. The characterstics of the principal modes of vibration are reﬂected in the solutions, owing to the effects of the natural circular frequencies and modal amplitude vectors in determining the forced oscillation amplitudes.

3. The displacement solutions represent only the forced motion of each mass; in addition, free vibration may also exist in superposition with the forced response.

4.Energy dissipation mechanisms are not incorporated into the model.
