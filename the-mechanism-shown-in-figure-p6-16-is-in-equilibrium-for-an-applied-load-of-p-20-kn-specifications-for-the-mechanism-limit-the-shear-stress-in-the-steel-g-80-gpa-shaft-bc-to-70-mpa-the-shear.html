Guest Question.

The mechanism shown in Figure P6.16 is in equilibrium for an applied load of P = 20 kN. Specifications for the mechanism limit the shear stress in the steel [G = 80 GPa] shaft BC to 70 MPa, the shear stress in bolt A to 100 MPa, and the vertical deflection of joint D to a maximum value of 25 mm. Assume that the bearings allow the shaft to rotate freely. Using L = 1,200 mm, a = 110 mm, and b = 210 mm, calculate:
(a) the minimum diameter required for shaft BC.
(b) the minimum diameter required for bolt A.

Admin
Solution

(a) Minimum diameter required for shaft BC. The torque applied to the shaft is

\(T=P b=(20,000 N )(210 mm )=4.200 \times 10^{6} N – mm\)

If the shear stress in the shaft is limited to 70 MPa, the minimum diameter required for the shaft is:

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{\pi}{16} d^{3} & \geq \frac{T}{\tau}=\frac{4.200 \times 10^{6} N – mm }{70 N / mm ^{2}}=60,000 mm ^{3} \\& \therefore d \geq 67.356 mm\end{aligned}\)

If the vertical deflection of joint D is not to exceed 25 mm, then the rotation angle at C must not exceed

\(\sin \phi_{C} \leq \frac{25 mm }{210 mm }=0.119048 \quad \phi_{C} \leq 0.1193306 rad\)

The rotation angle at C is equal to the angle of twist in the shaft; therefore,

\(\phi_{C}=\frac{T L}{J G} \leq 0.1193306 rad\)

The minimum polar moment of inertia required to satisfy this angular limit is

\(J \geq \frac{T L}{\phi G}=\frac{\left(4.200 \times 10^{6} N – mm \right)(1,200 mm )}{(0.1193306 rad )\left(80,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=527,944.944 mm ^{4}\)

Thus, the minimum required diameter to satisfy the deflection limit at D is

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{\pi}{32} d^{4} & \geq 527,944.944 mm ^{4} \\& \therefore d \geq 48.156 mm\end{aligned}\)

The minimum diameter required for the shaft BC is
d = 67.4 mm

 

(b) Minimum diameter required for bolt A. Since the torque in the shaft is \(4.200 \times 10^{6} N – mm\), the force that acts on the bolt at A is

\(V_{A}=\frac{4.200 \times 10^{6} N – mm }{110 mm }=38,181.818 N\)

The bolt acts in single shear. The area required to keep the average shear stress in the bolt to a value less than 100 MPa is

\(A_{ bolt } \geq \frac{38,181.818 N }{100 N / mm ^{2}}=381.818 mm ^{2}\)

Consequently, the bolt must have a minimum diameter of

\(d \geq 22.0 mm\)
