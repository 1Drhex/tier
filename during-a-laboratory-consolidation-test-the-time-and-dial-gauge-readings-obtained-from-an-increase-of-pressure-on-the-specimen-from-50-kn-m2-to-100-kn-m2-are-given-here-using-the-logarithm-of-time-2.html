Guest Question.

During a laboratory consolidation test, the time and dial gauge readings obtained from an increase of pressure on the specimen from 50 kN/\( m^{2}\) to 100 kN/\( m^{2}\)are given here.

Time (min) Dial gauge reading (cm × \( 10^{4}\) ) Time (min) Dial gauge reading (cm × \( 10^{4}\))
0 3975 16 4572
0.1 4082 30 4737
0.25 4102 60 4923
0.5 4128 120 5080
1 4166 240 5207
2 4224 480 5283
4 4298 960 5334
8 4420 1440 5364

Using the logarithm-of-time method, determine \( c_{v}\) . The average height of the specimen during consolidation was 2.24 cm, and it was drained at the top and bottom.

Admin
Solution

The semilogarithmic plot of dial reading versus time is shown in Figure 11.31. For this,\( t_{1}=0.1\) min ,\( t_{2}=0.4\) min to determine \( d_{0}\) . Following the procedure outlined in Figure 11.27, \( t_{50} \approx 19\) min. From Eq. (11.64),

\( c_{v} =\frac{0.197 H^{2}_{dr} }{t_{50} } =\frac{0.197 (\frac{2.24}{2} )^{2} }{19} = 0.013 cm^{2} /min = 2.17 \times 10^{-4} cm^{2} /sec\)
