Guest Question.

The parallel-chord roof truss shown in Figure 6.28 supports purlins at the top chord panel points and midway between the panel points. The factored loads transmitted by the purlins are as shown. Design the top chord. Use A992 steel and select a structural tee cut from a W shape. Use load and resistance factor design.

Admin
Solution

The bending moments and panel point forces caused by the loads acting between the joints will be found by treating each top-chord member as a fixed-end beam. From Table 3-23, “Shears, Moments and Deflections,” in Part 3 of the Manual, the fixed-end moment for each top-chord member is

\(M=M_{nt}=\frac{PL}{8}=\frac{2.4(10)}{8}=3.0\) ft-kips

These end moments and the corresponding reactions are shown in Figure 6.29. When the reactions are added to the loads that are directly applied to the joints, the loading condition shown in Figure 6.29 is obtained. The maximum axial compressive force will occur in member DE (and in the adjacent member, to the right of the
center of the span) and can be found by considering the equilibrium of a free body of the portion of the truss to the left of section a–a:

\(\sum{M_{1}} =(19.2-2.4)(30)=-4.8(10+20)+F_{DE}(4)=0\)

\(F_{DE}=-90\) kips (compression)

Design for an axial load of 90 kips and a bending moment of 3.0 ft-kips.
Table 6-1 in Part 6 of the Manual is for the design of W shapes only, and there are no corresponding tables for structural tees. An examination of the column load tables in Part 4 of the Manual shows that a small shape will be needed, because the axial load is small and the moment is small relative to the axial load. From the column load tables, with \(K_{x}L = 10\) feet and \(K_{y}L = 5\) feet, try a WT6 × 17.5, with an axial compressive design strength of

\(\phi_{c}P_{n }= 149\) kips

A footnote indicates that this shape is slender for compression, but this has been accounted for in the tabulated value of the strength.

Bending is about the x axis, and the member is braced against sidesway, so

\(M_{nt }= 3.0\) ft-kips, \(M_{\ell t} = 0\)

Because there is a transverse load on the member, use \(C_{m} = 1.0\) (the Commentary approach will not be used here). Compute \(B_{1}\):

\(P_{e1}=\frac{π^{2}E I_{x}}{(K_{x}L)^{2}}=\frac{π^{2}(29,000)(16.0)}{(10×12)^{2}}=318.0\) kips

 

\(B_{1}=\frac{C_{m}}{1-\frac{αP_{r}}{P_{e1}}}=\frac{1.0}{1-\frac{1.00(90)}{318.0}}=1.395\)

 

The amplified moment is

\(M_{u }= B_{1}M_{nt }+ B_{2}M_{\ell t} = 1.395(3.0) + 0 = 4.185\) ft-kips

The nominal moment strength of structural tees with compact or noncompact cross sections was introduced in Section 5.16 of this book and is covered in AISC F9. To determine the classification of the cross section, we first check the width-to-thickness ratios for the flange. From the dimensions and properties tables,

 

\(λ=\frac{b_{f}}{2t_{f}}=6.31\)

 

\(λ_{p}=0.38\sqrt{\frac{E} {F{y}}}=0.38\sqrt{\frac{29,000} {50}}=9.152\)

 

\(λ<λ_{p}\) \(∴\) The flange is compact.

 

(This could also be determined by observing that there is no footnote in the dimensions and properties table to indicate flange noncompactness for flexure.)

For the web,

\(λ=\frac{d}{t_{w}}=20.8\)

 

\(λ_{p}=0.84\sqrt{\frac{E}{F{y}}}=0.84\sqrt{\frac{29,000}{50}}=20.23\)

 

\(λ_{r}=1.03\sqrt{\frac{E}{F{y}}}=1.03\sqrt{\frac{29,000}{50}}=24.81\)

Since \(λ_{p} < λ < λ_{r}\), the web is noncompact and \(M_{n} = F_{cr}S_{x}\), where

\(F_{cr}=[2.55-1.84\frac{d}{t_{w}}\sqrt{\frac{F_{y}}{E}}]F_{y}=[2.55-1.84(20.8)\sqrt{\frac{50}{29,000}}]50=48.04\) ksi

 

\(M_{n}=48.04(3.23)=155.2\) in.-kips

 

Next, check the limit state of yielding. Because the maximum moment is a fixed-end moment, the stem will be in compression, assuming that the flange is at the top. For stems in compression,

 

\(M_{n} = M_{p }= F_{y}Z_{x} ≤ M_{y}\) (AISC Equation F9-3)

 

Since \(S_{x }< Z_{x}\), the yield moment \(M_{y}\) will control.

 

\(M_{n} = M_{y }= F_{y}S_{x} = 50(3.23) = 161.5\) in.-kips

 

Check lateral-torsional buckling. From AISC Equation F9-5,

\(B=±2.3(\frac{d}{L_{b}})\sqrt{\frac{I_{y}}{J}}=-2.3(\frac{6.25}{10×12})\sqrt{\frac{12.2}{0.369}}=-0.6888\)

(The minus sign is used when the stem is in compression anywhere along the unbraced length.) From AISC Equation F9-4,

 

\(M_{n}=M_{cr}=\frac{π \sqrt{E I_{y}GJ}}{L_{b}}[B+\sqrt{1+B^{2}}]\)

 

\(=\frac{π\sqrt{29,000(12.2)(11,200)(0.369)}}{10×12}[-0.6888+\sqrt{1+(-0.6888)^{2}}]\)

 

\(=526.0\) in.-kips

 

The limit state of web local buckling controls.

\(\phi_{b}M_{n} = 0.90(155.2) = 139.7\) in.-kips \(= 11.64\) ft-kips

Determine which interaction equation to use:

 

\(\frac{P_{u}}{\phi{c}P_{n}}=\frac{90}{149}=0.6040>0.2\) \(∴\) Use AISC Equation H1-1a.

 

\(\frac{P_{u}}{\phi_{c}P_{n}}=\frac{8}{9}(\frac{M_{ux}}{\phi_{b}M_{nx}}+\frac{M_{uy}}{\phi_{b}M_{ny}})=0.6040+\frac{8}{9}(\frac{4.185}{11.64}+0)\)

\(=0.924<1.0\) (OK)

 

Use a WT6 × 17.5.
