Guest Question.

To determine thermal conductivity of hydrogen, a hollow tube with a heating wire concentric to the tube is often used. Essentially the gas between the wire and the wall is a hollow cylinder and an electric current passing through the wire acts as a heat source. Determine thermal conductivity of the gas, using following data :
\(T_1\) = wire temperature = 200°C
\(T_2\) = tube wall temperature = 150°C,
I = current in the wire = 0.5 A,
V = voltage drop over 0.3 m section of wire = 3.6 V,
\(r_2\) = tube radius = 0.125 cm,
\(r_1 \)= wire radius = 0.0025 cm,
L = length of the wire = 0.3 m.

Admin
Solution

Given : Hollow cylinder of hydrogen gas with
\(T_1 = 200°C, T_2 = 150°C, I = 0.5 A,\)
\(V = 3.6 V r_2 = 0.125 cm,\)
\(r_1 = 0.0025 cm, L = 0.3 m.\)
To find : Thermal conductivity of hydrogen gas. Assumptions :
1. Steady state heat conduction.
2. Heat conduction in radial direction only.
3. Constant properties.
Analysis : The Fourier law of heat conduction for radial system is given as ;
\(\frac{Q}{A} = – k\frac{dT}{dr}\)
where, A = 2πrL
and \(Q = VI = (3.6 V) × (0.5 A) = 1.8 W\)

 

Hence \(\frac{Q}{2\pi L} \int_{r_{1}}^{r_{2}} \frac{dr}{r}= – k \int_{T_1}^{T_2} dT\)

 

\(\frac{Q}{2\pi L} ln\left ( \frac{r_2}{r_1}\right ) = -k(T_2 – T_1)\)

 

\(k =\frac{Q ln (r_2/r_1)}{2 \pi (T_1 – T_2)}\)
Substituting numerical values,

\(\frac{(1.8 W) \times ln(0.125/0.0025)}{2\pi \times (0.3 m)\times (200^{\circ}C – 150^{\circ}C) }\)
= 0.075 W/m.K
