Guest Question.

A roof framing plan consists of 30-ft. by 40-ft. bays, supporting a total dead load of 30 psf and a roof snow load of 50 psf. Determine the layout of the roof deck and the size of the deck. Assume a self-weight of 5 psf for the framing.

Admin
Solution

Dead load on the roof deck alone \(=30 psf – 5 psf = 25 psf\)

Snow load on the roof \(= 50 psf\)

Total load on the roof deck \(= 25 psf + 50 psf = 75 psf\)

For our example, let us try a \(1\) \(1⁄2-in\). by \(20-ga\) galvanized wide-rib deck (the reader should refer to the Vulcraft Steel Roof and Floor Deck Manual [21]) or similar deck manufacturers’ catalogs.

Maximum SDI allowable deck span without shoring during construction \(= 7 ft\). \(9 in.\)

Preferably, the deck should span continuous over at least four beams (i.e., 3-span deck). It should be noted that the deck span selected must be less than or equal to \(7 ft\). \(9 in\). (i.e., the maximum span), and, in addition, the selected deck span must be a multiple of the shorter bay dimension.

Try a \(7-ft\). \(6-in\). span (a multiple of the \(30-ft\) bay dimension) \(< 7 ft. 9 in.\quad\quad OK\)

Allowable load \(= 72 psf < 75 psf \quad\quad\) Not Good.

The next lower multiple of \(30 ft\) is \(6 ft.\) \(0 in.\) Therefore, try a deck span of \(6 ft.\)

\(0 in.\), and \(22-ga.\) deck resulting in five equal spaces per bay.

For this span, the allowable deck load \(= 89 psf > 75 psf \quad\quad OK\)

Therefore, use \(1.5-in\). by \(22-ga\) wide-rib galvanized metal deck.
