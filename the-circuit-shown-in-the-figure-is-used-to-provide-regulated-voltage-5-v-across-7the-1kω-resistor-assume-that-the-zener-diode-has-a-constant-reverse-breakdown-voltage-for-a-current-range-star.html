Guest Question.

The circuit shown in the figure is used to provide regulated voltage (5 V) across 7the 1kΩ resistor. Assume that the Zener diode has a constant reverse breakdown voltage for a current range, starting from a minimum required Zener current, \(I_{Z \min }=2\) mA to its maximum allowable current. The input voltage \(V _{ I }\) may vary by 5% from its normal value of 6 V. The resistance of the diode in the breakdown region is negligible.

The value of R and the minimum required power dissipation rating of the diode, respectively, are
(A) 186 Ω and 10 m W
(B) 100 Ω and 40 mW
(C) 100 Ω and 10 mW
(D) 186 Ω and 40 m W

Admin
Solution

Current \(I_{ S ( min )}=7 mA\)

Current \(I_{ L }=5 mA\)

Current \(I_{ z }=2 mA\)

We know that

\(\begin{aligned}I_{ S ( min )} &=\frac{V_{ I ( min )}-5}{R_{\max }} \\R_{\max } &=\frac{5.7-5}{7}=100 \Omega\end{aligned}\)

Now we have

\(I_{ S (\max )}=\frac{6.3-5}{100}=13 mA\)

And

\(I_{ Z ( max )}=I_{ s ( max )}-I_{ L }=13-5=8 mA\)

minimum power dissipation rating

\(\begin{aligned}P_{ Z (\min )} &=V_{ z } \cdot I_{ z (\max )} \\&=(5 \times 8) mW \\&=40 mW\end{aligned}\)

Hence, the correct option is (B)
