Guest Question.

A Beam Supported by a Cable

A 500.0 kg crate sits 3.00 m from a hinge on a 4.00 m long horizontal beam, as shown in Figure 10-21. The beam is supported at one end with a cable that is attached to a wall; the cable makes an angle of 42.0째 with the horizontal. The beam has a mass of 270.0 kg. Determine the tension in the cable and the force exerted by the hinge on the beam for the system to be in equilibrium.

Admin
Solution

The weight of (or the force of gravity on) the beam, the weight of the crate, and the tension in the cable, as well as the horizontal and vertical components of the support force at the hinge, are shown in the FBD of Figure 10-22. Gravity exerts a downward force of \(m \overrightarrow{ g }\) on the crate and the beam must exert an equal but opposite force on the crate to keep the crate in equilibrium. By the third law, the crate must then exert an equal but opposite force on the beam. We call that force \(\vec{N}\) .

The x- and y-components of the force of the hinge on the beam are unknown quantities. We can find the tension force by summing torques about the hinge at point A. We can then break up the tension into components and sum forces in the horizontal and vertical directions to find \(A_{x} \text { and } A_{y}\). An alternative approach would be to sum torques about the point where the cable is attached to find \(A_{y}\); summing forces in the y-direction will give us \(T_{y}\). Since we know the direction of the tension force, we can find \(T_{x}\), and by summing forces in the x-direction, we can find \(A_{x}\).

We apply the condition for equilibrium for the torques about the hinge at A. The forces that exert torques about A are the weight of the beam \((M \vec{g})\) , the normal force of interaction between the crate and the beam \((\vec{N})\),and the tension in the cable \((\vec{T})\). The forces at the pivot, \(A_{x} \text { and } A_{y}\), do not exert a torque about the pivot because their moment arm is zero. Therefore,

 

\(\sum \vec{\tau}_{ A }=\vec{\tau}_{M g}+\vec{\tau}_{N_{c}}+\vec{\tau}_{T}=0\)

 

Taking clockwise to be positive,

 

\(+\curvearrowright\sum \tau_{ A }=\tau_{M g}+\tau_{N_{c}}-\tau_{T}=0\)

 

From Figure 10-22, using Equation 8-22 for the torque exerted by the tension force, given by the product of the force, the distance to the pivot and the sine of the angle, and moment arm concept for the torque exerted by \(M \vec{g} \text { and } \vec{N}_{c}\) , noting that the magnitude of \(\vec{N}_{c}\) is equal to the magnitude of the weight of the crate \((m \overrightarrow{ g })\), and taking the weight of the beam to be at its centre (2.00 m from the pivot), we get

 

\(\tau_{ O }=r F \sin \theta\) {8-22}

 

\(M g(2.00 m )+m g(3.00 m )-T(4.00 m )\left(\sin 42.0^{\circ}\right)=0\)

 

Solving for T gives

 

\(T=\frac{(270.0 kg )\left(9.81 m / s ^{2}\right)(2.00 m )+(500.0 kg )\left(9.81 m / s ^{2}\right)(3.00 m )}{(4.00 m )\left(\sin 42.0^{\circ}\right)}=7477 N\)

 

\(\Rightarrow T=7.48 kN\)

 

Next, we sum the forces in the y-direction. Here, we have the weight of the beam, the normal force from the crate, the vertical component of the tension, and the vertical component \((\left.A_{y}\right)\) of the support force at the hinge:

 

\(\sum F_{y}=0\)

 

From Figure 10-22, the y-component of the tension force is \(T \sin 42.0^{\circ}\). Choosing up as our positive y-direction, we have

 

\(-M g-N_{ c }+T \sin 42.0^{\circ}+A_{y}=0\)

 

We rearrange to find

 

\(A_{y}=M g+m g-T \sin 42.0^{\circ}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&=(270.0 kg )\left(9.81 m / s ^{2}\right)+(500.0 kg )\left(9.81 m / s ^{2}\right)-(7477 N ) \sin 42.0^{\circ} \\&=2551 N =2.55 kN\end{aligned}\)

 

Now, the only unknown is the horizontal component, \(A_{x}\), of the support force at the hinge. We take the sum of the forces acting on the beam in the x-direction; these are \(A_{x}\) and the horizontal component of the tension, T:

 

\(\sum F_{x}=0\)

 

From Figure 10-22, the x-component of the tension is \(T \cos 42.0^{\circ}\). Choosing right as our positive x-direction,

 

\(-T \cos 42.0^{\circ}+A_{x}=0 \Rightarrow A_{x}=T \cos 42.0^{\circ}\)

 

Therefore,

 

\(A_{x}=(7477 N ) \cos 42.0^{\circ}=5556 N =5.56 kN\)

 

We note briefly that the positive values we obtained for \(A_{y} \text { and } A_{x}\) indicate that our guesses for their directions in the FBD were correct.

 

Making sense of the result

 

The cable supports part of the weight of the beam, but since the cable is on an angle, part of the force it exerts is horizontal (directed toward the wall), which results in the horizontal component of the force at the hinge. A quick check is to sum the torques about the other end of the beam, which gives \(A_{y}=2.55 kN\) as well.
