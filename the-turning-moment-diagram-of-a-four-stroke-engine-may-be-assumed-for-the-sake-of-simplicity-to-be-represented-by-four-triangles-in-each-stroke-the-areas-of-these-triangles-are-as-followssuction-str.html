Guest Question.

The turning moment diagram of a four stroke engine may be assumed for the sake of simplicity to be represented by four triangles in each stroke. The areas of these triangles are as follows:
Suction stroke \(=5 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2}\); Compression stroke \(=21 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2}\); Expansion stroke = \(85 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2}\); Exhaust stroke \(=8 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2}\).
All the areas excepting expression stroke are negative. Each \(m^{2}\) of area represents 14 MN-m of work.
Assuming the resisting torque to be constant, determine the moment of inertia of the flywheel to keep the speed between 98 r.p.m. and 102 r.p.m. Also find the size of a rim-type flywheel based on the minimum material criterion, given that density of flywheel material is \(8150 kg / m ^{3}\) ; the allowable tensile stress of the flywheel material is 7.5 MPa. The rim cross-section is rectangular, one side being four times the length of the other.

Admin
Solution

Given: \(a_{1}=5 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2} ; a_{2}=21 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2} ; a_{3}=85 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2} ; a_{4}=8 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2}\) ; \(N_{2}=98 \text { r.p.m.; } N_{1}=102 \text { r.p.m.; } \rho=8150 kg / m ^{3} ; \sigma=7.5 MPa =7.5 \times 10^{6} N / m ^{2}\)

The turning moment-crank angle diagram for a four stroke engine is shown in Fig. 16.20. The areas below the zero line of pressure are taken as negative while the areas above the zero line of pressure are taken as positive.

\(\therefore \) Net area \(=a_{3}-\left(a_{1}+a_{2}+a_{4}\right)\)

 

\(=85 \times 10^{5}-\left(5 \times 10^{5}+21 \times 10^{-5}+8 \times 10^{-5}\right)=51 \times 10^{-5} m ^{2}\)

 

Since \(1 m ^{2}=14 MN – m =14 \times 10^{6} N – m \text { of work, therefore }\)

 

Net work done per cycle

\(=51 \times 10^{-5} \times 14 \times 10^{6}=7140 N – m\) …(i)

We also know that work done per cycle

\(=T_{\text {mean }} \times 4 \pi N – m\) …(ii)

From equation (i) and (ii),

\(T_{\text {mean }}=F G=7140 / 4 \pi=568 N – m\)

Work done during expansion stroke

\(=a_{3} \times \text { Work scale }=85 \times 10^{-5} \times 14 \times 10^{6}=11900 N – m\) …(iii)

Also, work done during expansion stroke

\(=\frac{1}{2} \times B C \times A G==\frac{1}{2} \times \pi \times A G=1.571 A G\) …(iv)

From equations (iii) and (iv),

\(A G=11900 / 1.571=7575 N – m\)

 

\(\therefore \) Excess torque \(=A F=A G-F G=7575-568=7007 N – m\)

Now from similar triangles ADE and ABC,

\(\frac{D E}{B C}=\frac{A F}{A G} \quad \text { or } \quad D E=\frac{A F}{A G} \times B C=\frac{7007}{7575} \times \pi=2.9 rad\)

We know that maximum fluctuation of energy,

\(\Delta E=\text { Area of } \Delta A D E=\frac{1}{2} \times D E \times A F\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{2} \times 2.9 \times 7007=10160 N – m\)

 

Moment of Inertia of the flywheel

Let I = Moment of inertia of the flywheel in \(kg – m ^{2}\).

We know that mean speed during the cycle

\(N=\frac{N_{1}+N_{2}}{2}=\frac{102+98}{2}=100 r.p.m\)

 

\(\therefore \) Corresponding angular mean speed,

 

\(\omega=2 \pi N / 60=2 \pi \times 100 / 60=10.47 rad / s\)

and coefficient of fluctuation of speed,

\(C_{ S }=\frac{N_{1}-N_{2}}{N}=\frac{102-98}{100}=0.04\)

We know that maximum fluctuation of energy \((\Delta E)\),

\(10160=I . \omega^{2} \cdot C_{ S }=I(10.47)^{2} \times 0.04=4.385 I\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(I=10160 / 4.385=2317 kg – m ^{2}\)

 

Size of flywheel

Let t = Thickness of the flywheel rim in metres,

b = Width of the flywheel rim in metres = 4 t …(Given)

D = Mean diameter of the flywheel in metres, and

v = Peripheral velocity of the flywheel in m/s.

We know that hoop stress \((\sigma )\),

\(7.5 \times 10^{6}=\rho \cdot v^{2}=8150 v^{2}\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(v^{2}=\frac{7.5 \times 10^{6}}{8150}=920 \text { or } v=30.3 m / s\)

 

and \(v=\pi D N / 60 \text { or } D=v \times 60 / \pi N=30.3 \times 60 / \pi \times 100=5.786 m\)

Now let us find the mass (m) of the flywheel rim. We know that maximum fluctuation of energy \((\Delta E)\),

\(10160=m \cdot v^{2} C_{ S }=m \times(30.3)^{2} \times 0.04=36.72 m\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(m=10160 / 36.72=276.7 kg\)

 

Also \(m=\text { Volume } \times \text { density }=\pi D \times A \times \frac{\rho}{g}=\pi D \times b \times t \times \frac{\rho}{g}\)

 

\(276.7=\pi \times 5.786 \times 4 t \times t \times \frac{8 \times 10^{4}}{9.81}=59.3 \times 10^{4} t^{2}\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(t^{2}=276.7 / 59.3 \times 10^{4}=0.0216 m \text { or } 21.6 mm\)

 

and b = 4t = 4 × 21.6 = 86.4 mm .
