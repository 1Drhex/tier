Guest Question.

Finding the frequency response of a two-stage CD–CS MOSFET amplifier A two-stage MOSFET amplifier is shown in Fig. 7.62. The circuit parameters are \( C_{ gd 1}=C_{ gd 2}=2 pF , C_{ gs 1}=C_{ gs 2}=5 pF , R_{ s }=200 \Omega \), \( g_{ m 1}=g_{ m 2}=10 \times 10^{-3} A / V , R_{ s }=200 \Omega, R_{ G 1}=50 k \Omega, R_{ SR 1}=250 \Omega, R_{ D 2}=5 k \Omega, R_{ SR 2}=150 \Omega \), \( R_{ L }=10 k \Omega, C_{1}=1 \mu F , C_{2}=10 \mu F , \text { and } C_{ S 2}=5.3 \mu F \).

(a) Calculate the low 3-dB frequency \( f_{L}\).
(b) Calculate the high 3-dB frequency \( f_{H}\).

Admin
Solution

(a) The low-frequency equivalent circuit is shown in Fig. 7.63(a). There are two coupling capacitors, \( C _{1} \) and \( C _{2} \), and one source bypass capacitor, \( C _{ S 2} \).
The time constant \( \tau_{1} \text { due to } C_{1} \) is

\( \tau_{1}=\left(R_{ s }+R_{ G 1}\right) C_{1} \) (7.136)

 

\( =(200 \Omega+50 k \Omega) \times 1 \mu F =50.2 ms \)

 

The time constant \( \tau_{2} \text { due to } C_{2} \) is

\( \tau_{2}=\left(R_{ D 2}+R_{ L }\right) C_{2} \) (7.137)

 

\( =(5 k \Omega+10 k \Omega) \times 10 \mu F =150 ms \)

 

The time constant \( \tau_{3} \text { due to } C_{ S 2} \) is

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{3} &=\left(R_{ SR 2} \| \frac{1}{g_{ m 2}}\right) C_{ S 2} \\&=[150 \Omega \|(1000 / 10 A / V )] \times 5.3 \mu F =0.32 ms\end{aligned} \) (7.138)

 

From Eq. (2.106), the low 3-dB frequency is

\( f_{ L }=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \sum_{k=1}^{n} \frac{1}{\tau_{ Ck }}=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \sum_{k=1}^{n} \frac{1}{C_{ k } R_{ Ck }} \) (2.106)

 

\( f_{ L }=\frac{1}{2 \pi}\left[\frac{1}{50.2 ms }+\frac{1}{150 ms }+\frac{1}{0.32 ms }\right]=502 Hz \)

(b) The high-frequency equivalent circuit is shown in Fig. 7.63(b). Applying Eq. (7.128) gives Thevenin’s equivalent resistance presented to \( C_{ gs 1} \) as

 

\( R_{ Cgs }=\frac{v_{ x }}{i_{ x }}=\frac{R_{ s }\left\|R_{ G }+R_{ L }\right\| R_{ SR }}{1+g_{ m }\left(R_{ L } \| R_{ SR }\right)} \) (7.128)

 

\( R_{ gs 1}=\frac{R_{ s } \| R_{ G 1}+R_{ SR 1}}{1+g_{ m 1} R_{ SR 1}} \) (7.139)

 

\( =\frac{200 \Omega \| 50 k \Omega+250 \Omega}{1+10 \times 10^{-3} A / V \times 250 \Omega}=128.3 \Omega \)

 

and the time constant \( \tau_{ g s 1} \) is

\( \begin{aligned}\tau_{ gs 1} &=R_{ g s 1} C_{ g 1} \\&=128.3 \Omega \times 5 pF =0.642 ns\end{aligned}\) (7.140)

 

If \( R_{ gs 2} \) is Thevenin’s equivalent resistance faced by \( C_{ gs 2} \text { with } C_{ g s 1}, C_{ gd 1}, \text { and } C_{ gd 2} \) open-circuited, \( R_{ gs 2} \)will be the parallel combination of \( R_{ SR 1} \) and the output resistance of transistor \( M _{1} \). That is,

 

\(\begin{aligned}R_{ gs 2} &=R_{ SRI } \| \frac{1}{g_{ ml }} \\&=250 \Omega \| \frac{1000}{10 A / V }=71.4 \Omega\end{aligned}\) (7.141)

 

and the time constant \( \tau_{ gs 2} \) is

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{ gs 2} &=R_{ gs 2} C_{ gs 2} \\&=71.4 \Omega \times 5 pF =0.36 ns\end{aligned}\) (7.142)

 

If \( R_{ gd 1} \) is Thevenin’s equivalent resistance faced by \( C_{ gd 1} \text { with } C_{ gs 1}, C_{ gs 2}, \text { and } C_{ gd 2} \) open-circuited, the time constant \( \tau_{ gd 1} \) is

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{ gd 1} &=\left(R_{ s } \| R_{ G 1}\right) C_{ gd 1} \\&=(200 \Omega \| 50 k \Omega) \times 2 pF =0.398 ns\end{aligned} \) (7.143)

 

With \( C_{ g s 1}, C_{ gs 2}, \text { and } C_{ gd 1} \) open-circuited, Thevenin’s equivalent resistance faced by \( C_{ gd 2} \) can be found by applying Eq. (2.116):

 

\( R_{ eq }=\frac{v_{ x }}{i_{ x }}=R_{ L }+R_{ i }\left(1+g_{ m } R_{ L }\right) \) (2.116)

 

\(\begin{aligned}R_{ gd 2} &=\left(R_{ D 2} \| R_{ L }\right)+R_{ gs 2}\left[1+g_{ m 2}\left(R_{ D 2} \| R_{ L }\right)\right] \\&=(5 k \Omega \| 10 k \Omega)+71.4 \Omega \times\left[1+10 \times 10^{-3} A / V \times(5 k \Omega \| 10 k \Omega)\right]=5.78 k \Omega\end{aligned}\) (7.144)

 

and the time constant \( \tau_{ gd 2}\) is

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{ gd 2} &=R_{ gd 2} C_{ gd 2} \\&=5.78 k \Omega \times 2 pF =11.57 ns\end{aligned} \) (7.145)

 

Thus, the high 3-dB frequency is

 

\( f_{ H }=\frac{1}{2 \pi \times(0.642 n +0.36 n +0.398 n +11.57 n )}=12.27 MHz\)
