Guest Question.

A curved surface, AB that has the shape of a quarter of a circle with a radius of 4 ft and a width of 5 ft is submerged at a depth of 8 ft in water open to the atmosphere, as illustrated in Figure EP 2.36. (a) Determine the magnitude and location of the horizontal component of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the curved surface, AB. (b) Determine the magnitude and location of the vertical component of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the curved surface, AB. (c) Determine the magnitude and the location of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the curved surface, AB.

 

Admin
Solution

(a) The magnitude of the horizontal component of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the curved surface, AB is determined by applying Equation 2.225 \(F_{H} = pdA= \int_{0}^{L}{(\gamma h(y))(wdy)}= \int_{0}^{L}{(\gamma(y_{o}+ y ))(wdy)}\), integrating over the length of the rectangular plane (vertical projection, CB of the curved surface, AB) using the top of the plane as a reference point for integration as follows:

\( w:= 5 ft \) \(r:= 4 ft \) \( L:= r= 4ft \) \(A:= w.L = 20 ft^{2} \)
\(y_{o} :=8ft \) \(\gamma := 62.417 \frac{lb}{ft^{3} } \) \(h(y):= (y_{o}+y) \)
\(F_{H}:= \int_{0}^{L}{\gamma h(y)w(y)dy} =12483.4 lb \)

Alternatively, the magnitude of the horizontal component of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the curved surface, AB is determined by applying Equation 2.226 \(F_{H} = p_{ca}A = \gamma h_{ca} A = \gamma (y_{o}+ y_{ca})(wL)=\gamma \left(y_{o} + \frac{L}{2} \right) (wL)\) as follows:

\(y_{ca}:= \frac{L}{2}=2 ft \) \(h_{ca}:= y_{o}+ y_{ca} = 10 ft \)
\(F_{H}:= \gamma h_{ca} A = 12483.4 lb \)

The location of the horizontal component of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the curved surface, AB is determined by applying Equation 2.228 \(y_{F_{H} } = \frac{M_{H} }{F_{H}}\) as follows:

\(y_{FH}:= \frac{\int_{0}^{L}{y(\gamma h(y))w(y)dy} }{\int_{0}^{L}{(\gamma h(y))w(y)dy} } = 2.133 ft \) \(h_{FH}:= (y_{o}+ y_{FH}) = 10.133 ft \)

Alternatively, the location of the horizontal component of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the curved surface, AB may be determined by applying Equation 2.229 \(h_{F_{H} } = \frac{I_{x-ca}}{h_{ca}A} + h_{ca} = \frac{WL^{3}/12}{(y_{o}+L/2)(wL)} +(y_{o}+L/2)\) as follows:

\(I_{xca} := \frac{w.L^{3} }{12} = 26.667 ft^{4} \) \( h_{FH}:= \frac{I_{xca} }{ h_{ca}A}+ h_{ca} = 10.133 ft \)

(b) The magnitude of the vertical component of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the curved surface, AB is equal to the weight of the volume of liquid lying above the curved surface, AB to the horizontal projection of the curved surface, DE (located along the free surface) and is determined by applying Equation 2.231 \( F^{V} = F^{G}= \gamma V_{ABCDE} = \gamma w A_{ABCDE}\) as follows:

\( A_{ABC} := \frac{\pi .r^{2} }{4} = 12.566 ft^{2} \) \( A_{ACDE} := ry_{o} = 32 ft^{2} \)
\(F_{G}: = \gamma w( A_{ABC}+ A_{ACDE} )=13908.496 lb \) \( F_{V}:= F_{G} = 13908.496 lb \)

The location (moment arm) of the vertical component of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the curved surface, AB is located at the center of gravity of the liquid, \(V_{ABCDE}\) lying above the curved surface, AB to the horizontal projection of the curved surface, DE. However, because the width, w of the curved surface, AB is a constant, the center gravity of \(V_{ABCDE}\) may be represented by the center of area of A ABCDE. Thus, the location of the vertical component of the resultant hydrostatic force is determined as the weighted average of the center of areas (moment arms) for \(A_{ABC}\) and \(A_{ACDE}\) andiscomputed byapplying Equation 2.233 \( x_{F_{V} }= \frac{(x_{ca} )_{ABC}+ (x_{ca} )_{ACDE}A_{ACDE} }{A_{ABCDE}} \). The center of area of a quarter of a circle and a rectangle is given in Table B.1 in Appendix B.

\( x_{caABC}:= \frac{4.r}{3. \pi } =1.698 ft \) \( x_{caACDE}:= \frac{r}{2} = 2 ft \)
\( A_{ABCDE}:= A_{ABC}+ A_{ACDE}= 44.566 ft^{2} \)
\( x_{FV} := \frac{ x_{caABC}. A_{ABC} + x_{caACDE}. A_{ACDE} }{ A_{ABCDE}} = 1.915 ft \)

(c) The magnitude of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the curved surface, AB is determined by applying Equation 2.234 \(R= \sqrt{F^{2}_{H} + F^{2}_{V}} \) as follows:

\(R:= \sqrt{F^{2}_{H}+ F^{2}_{V} } =18689.075 lb \)

The location of the resultant hydrostatic force acting on the curved surface, AB is determined by applying Equation 2.235 \( \theta = \tan ^{-1} \left(\frac{F_{V} }{H_{V}} \right) \) as follows:

\(\theta := a \tan \left(\frac{F_{V}}{F_{H}} \right) =48.091 deg \)
