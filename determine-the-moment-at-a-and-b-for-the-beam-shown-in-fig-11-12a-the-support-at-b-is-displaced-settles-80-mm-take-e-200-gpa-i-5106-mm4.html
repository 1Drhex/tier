Guest Question.

Determine the moment at \(A\) and \(B\) for the beam shown in Fig. \(11-12 (\mathrm{a})\). The support at \(B\) is displaced (settles) \(80 \mathrm{~mm}\). Take \(E=200 \mathrm{GPa}\), \(I=5\left(10^{6}\right) \mathrm{mm}^{4}\).

Admin
Solution

Slope-Deflection Equations. Only one span \((A B)\) must be considered in this problem since the moment \(\mathbf{M}_{B C}\) due to the overhang can be calculated from statics. Since there is no loading on span \(A B\), the FEMs are zero. As shown in Fig. 11-12b, the downward displacement (settlement) of \(B\) causes the cord for span \(A B\) to rotate clockwise. Thus,

\( \psi_{A B}=\psi_{B A}=\frac{0.08 \mathrm{~m}}{4}=0.02 \mathrm{rad} \)

The stiffness for \(A B\) is

\( k=\frac{I}{L}=\frac{5\left(10^{6}\right) \mathrm{mm}^{4}\left(10^{-12}\right) \mathrm{m}^{4} / \mathrm{mm}^{4}}{4 \mathrm{~m}}=1.25\left(10^{-6}\right) \mathrm{m}^{3} \)

Applying the slope-deflection equation, Eq. 11-8, to span \(A B\), with \(\theta_{A}=0\), we have

\( \begin{array}{l} M_{N}=2 E k\left(2 \theta_{N}+\theta_{F}-3 \psi\right)+(\text { FEM })_{N}\\ \text For Internal Span or End Span with Far End Fixed \end{array} \) (11-8)

\(\begin{array}{c} M_{N}=2 E\left(\frac{I}{L}\right)\left(2 \theta_{N}+\theta_{F}-3 \psi\right)+(\mathrm{FEM})_{N} \\ \end{array}\)

\(\begin{array}{c} M_{A B}=2\left(200\left(10^{9}\right) \mathrm{N} / \mathrm{m}^{2}\right)\left[1.25\left(10^{-6}\right) \mathrm{m}^{3}\right]\left[2(0)+\theta_{B}-3(0.02)\right]+0 \\ \end{array}\) (1)

\(\begin{array}{c}M_{B A}=2\left(200\left(10^{9}\right) \mathrm{N} / \mathrm{m}^{2}\right)\left[1.25\left(10^{-6}\right) \mathrm{m}^{3}\right]\left[2 \theta_{B}+0-3(0.02)\right]+0 \end{array}\) (2)

Equilibrium Equations. The free-body diagram of the beam at support \(B\) is shown in Fig. 11-12c. Moment equilibrium requires

\( \curvearrowleft +\Sigma M_{B}=0 \text {; }\quad M_{B A}-8000 \mathrm{~N}(3 \mathrm{~m})=0 \)

Substituting Eq. (2) into this equation yields

\( \begin{aligned} 1\left(10^{6}\right) \theta_{B}-30\left(10^{3}\right) &=24\left(10^{3}\right) \\ \theta_{B} &=0.054 \mathrm{rad} \end{aligned} \)

Thus, from Eqs. (1) and (2),

\( \begin{aligned} M_{A B} &=-3.00 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \\ M_{B A} &=24.0 \mathrm{kN} \cdot \mathrm{m} \end{aligned} \)
