Guest Question.

Consider a time-independent Hamiltonian for a particle moving in one dimension that has stationary states \( \psi_{n}(x) \)with energies \( E_n \) .

(a) Show that the solution to the time-dependent Schrödinger equation can be written

\( \Psi(x, t)=\hat{U}(t) \Psi(x, 0)=\int K\left(x, x^{\prime}, t\right) \Psi\left(x^{\prime}, 0\right) d x^{\prime} \),

where K (x,x′,t) , known as the propagator, is

\( K\left(x, x^{\prime}, t\right)=\sum_{n} \psi_{n}^{*}\left(x^{\prime}\right) e^{-i E_{n} t / \hbar} \psi_{n}(x) \) (6.79).

Here \( \left|K\left(x, x^{\prime}, t\right)\right|^{2} \) is the probability for a quantum mechanical particle to travel from position x′ to position x in time t.

(b) Find K for a particle of mass m in a simple harmonic oscillator potential of frequency ω. You will need the identity

\( \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-z^{2}}} \exp \left[-\frac{\xi^{2}+\eta^{2}-2 \xi \eta z}{1-z^{2}}\right]=e^{-\xi^{2}} e^{-\eta^{2}} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{z^{n}}{2^{n} n !} H_{n}(\xi) H_{n}(\eta) \).

(c) Find Ψ (x,t) if the particle from part (a) is initially in the state

\( \Psi(x, 0)=\left(\frac{2 a}{\pi}\right)^{1 / 4} e^{-a\left(x-x_{0}\right)^{2}} \).

Compare your answer with . Note: is a special case with \( a=m \omega / 2 \hbar \).

(d) Find K for a free particle of mass m. In this case the stationary states are continuous, not discrete, and one must make the replacement

\( \sum_{n} \rightarrow \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} d p \).

in Equation 6.79.

(e) Find Ψ (x,t) for a free particle that starts out in the state

\( \Psi(x, 0)=\left(\frac{2 a}{\pi}\right)^{1 / 4} e^{-a x^{2}} \).

Compare your answer with .

Solution

(a) We can express Ψ(x, 0) as a linear combination of stationary states

\( \Psi(x, 0)=\sum_{n} c_{n} \psi_{n}(x) \).

where

\( c_{n}=\int \psi_{n}^{*}(x) \Psi(x, 0) d x \).

Then we have

\( \Psi(x, t)=\sum_{n} c_{n} \psi_{n}(x) e^{-i E_{n} t / \hbar} \).

\( =\sum_{n}\left[\int \psi_{n}^{*}\left(x^{\prime}\right) \Psi\left(x^{\prime}, 0\right) d x^{\prime}\right] \psi_{n}(x) e^{-i E_{n} t / \hbar} \).

\( =\int \sum_{n} \psi_{n}^{*}\left(x^{\prime}\right) e^{-i E_{n} t / \hbar} \psi_{n}(x) \Psi\left(x^{\prime}, 0\right) d x^{\prime} \).

\( =\int K\left(x, x^{\prime}, t\right) \Psi\left(x^{\prime}, 0\right) d x^{\prime} \).

which is the desired result.

(b) For the simpler harmonic oscillator, the stationary states are

\( \psi_{n}=A_{n} H_{n}(\xi) e^{-\xi^{2} / 2} \).

\( \text { where } \xi=\sqrt{m \omega / \hbar} x \text {. Then } \)

\( K\left(x, x^{\prime}, t\right)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} A_{n} H_{n}(\eta) e^{-\eta^{2} / 2} e^{-i(n+1 / 2) \omega t} A_{n} H_{n}(\xi) e^{-\xi^{2} / 2} \) \( [ \star ] \).

\( \text { where } \eta=\sqrt{m \omega / \hbar} x \text {. We define } z=e^{-i \omega t} \text { and recalling that } A_{n}=(m \omega / \pi \hbar)^{1 / 4} 1 / \sqrt{2^{n} n !} \). (Equation 2.86) we have

\( \psi_{n}(x)=\left(\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}\right)^{1 / 4} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2^{n} n !}} H_{n}(\xi) e^{-\xi^{2} / 2} \) (2.86).

\( K\left(x, x^{\prime}, t\right)=e^{\left(\eta^{2}+\xi^{2}\right) / 2} \sqrt{\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}} \sqrt{z} e^{-\left(\eta^{2}+\xi^{2}\right)} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{z^{n}}{2^{n} n !} H_{n}(\xi) H_{n}(\eta) \).

\( =\sqrt{\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}} \sqrt{\frac{z}{1-z^{2}}} \exp \left[\frac{\eta^{2}+\xi^{2}}{2}-\frac{\eta^{2}+\xi^{2}-2 \eta \xi z}{1-z^{2}}\right] \).

\( =\sqrt{\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}} \sqrt{\frac{z}{1-z^{2}}} \exp \left[-\frac{1}{2} \frac{\left(1+z^{2}\right)\left(\eta^{2}+\xi^{2}\right)-4 \eta \xi z}{1-z^{2}}\right] \).

Lets clean this up a little bit. We note that \( z z^{*}=1 \) so

\( K\left(x, x^{\prime}, t\right)=\sqrt{\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}} \sqrt{\frac{1}{z^{*}-z}} \exp \left[-\frac{1}{2} \frac{\left(z^{*}+z\right)\left(\eta^{2}+\xi^{2}\right)-4 \eta \xi}{z^{*}-z}\right] \).

We then further note that \( z^{*}-z=2 i \sin (\omega t) \text { and } z+z^{*}=2 \cos (\omega t) \) and we have

\( K\left(x, x^{\prime}, t\right)=\sqrt{\frac{1}{\pi} \frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar}} \sqrt{\frac{1}{i \sin (\omega t)}} \exp \left[-\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar} \frac{\cos (\omega t)\left(x^{2}+x^{\prime 2}\right)-2 x x^{\prime}}{i \sin (\omega t)}\right] \).

(c) For the given initial state we have

\( \Psi(x, t)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} K\left(x, x^{\prime}, t\right) \Psi\left(x^{\prime}, 0\right) d x^{\prime} \).

\( =\sqrt{\frac{1}{\pi} \frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar}} \sqrt{\frac{1}{i \sin (\omega t)}} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \exp \left[-\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar} \frac{\cos (\omega t)\left(x^{2}+x^{\prime 2}\right)-2 x x^{\prime}}{i \sin (\omega t)}\right]\left(\frac{2 a}{\pi}\right)^{1 / 4} e^{-a\left(x^{\prime}-x_{0}\right)^{2}} d x^{\prime} \).

\( =\sqrt{\frac{1}{\pi} \frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar}} \sqrt{\frac{1}{i \sin (\omega t)}}\left(\frac{2 a}{\pi}\right)^{1 / 4} \exp \left[-\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar} \frac{x^{2}}{i \tan (\omega t)}-a x_{0}^{2}\right] \)

\( \times \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \exp \left[-\left(\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar} \frac{1}{i \tan (\omega t)}+a\right) x^{\prime 2}+\left(\frac{m \omega}{\hbar} \frac{x}{i \sin (\omega t)}+2 a x_{0}\right) x^{\prime}\right] d x^{\prime} \).

Even though the constants are complicated, this is nothing more than the Gaussian integral given in the problem statement and we have

\( \Psi(x, t)=\sqrt{\frac{1}{\pi} \frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar}} \sqrt{\frac{1}{i \sin (\omega t)}}\left(\frac{2 a}{\pi}\right)^{1 / 4} \exp \left[-\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar} \frac{x^{2}}{i \tan (\omega t)}-a x_{0}^{2}\right] \)

\( \times \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar} \frac{1}{i \tan (\omega t)}+a}} \exp \left[\frac{\left(\frac{m \omega}{\hbar} \frac{x}{i \sin (\omega t)}+2 a x_{0}\right)^{2}}{4\left(\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar} \frac{1}{i \tan (\omega t)}+a\right)}\right] \).

This simplifies to

\( \Psi(x, t)=\left(\frac{2 a}{\pi}\right)^{1 / 4} \frac{\exp \left[-a \frac{\left(x^{2}+x_{0}^{2}\right) \cos (\omega t)-2 x x_{0}+\frac{i m \omega}{2 \hbar a} \sin (\omega t) x^{2}}{\cos (\omega t)+\frac{2 i a \hbar}{m \omega} \sin (\omega t)}\right]}{\sqrt{\cos (\omega t)+\frac{2 i a \hbar}{m \omega} \sin (\omega t)}} \).

Now, is a special case of this equation. Plugging in t = 0 we see

\( \Psi(x, 0)=\left(\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}\right)^{1 / 4} \exp \left[-\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar}\left(x-x_{0}\right)^{2}\right] \).

so that this corresponds to choosing \( a=m \omega / 2 \hbar . \text { If we plug } a=m \omega / 2 \hbar \) into our general solution we get

\( \Psi(x, t)=\left(\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}\right)^{1 / 4} \frac{\exp \left[-\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar} \frac{\left(x^{2}+x_{0}^{2}\right) \cos (\omega t)-2 x x_{0}+i \sin (\omega t) x^{2}}{e^{i \omega t}}\right]}{\sqrt{e^{i \omega t}}} \).

\( =\left(\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}\right)^{1 / 4} \exp \left[-\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar}\left[\left(x^{2}+x_{0}^{2}\right)\left(1+e^{-2 i \omega t}\right) / 2-2 x x_{0} e^{-i \omega t}\right.\right. \)

\( \left.\left.+\left(1-e^{-2 i \omega t}\right) x^{2} / 2\right]+\frac{i \omega t}{2}\right] \).

\( =\left(\frac{m \omega}{\pi \hbar}\right)^{1 / 4} \exp \left[-\frac{m \omega}{2 \hbar}\left(x^{2}+\frac{x_{0}^{2}}{2}\left(1+e^{-2 i \omega t}\right)+\frac{i \hbar t}{m}-2 e^{-i \omega t} x x_{0}\right)\right] \),

exactly as in .

(d) For the free particle

\( K\left(x, x^{\prime}, t\right)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f_{p}\left(x^{\prime}\right)^{*} e^{-i E_{p} t / \hbar} f_{p}(x) d p \).

\( =\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{e^{-i p x^{\prime} / \hbar}}{\sqrt{2 \pi \hbar}} e^{-i p^{2} t /(2 m \hbar)} \frac{e^{i p x / \hbar}}{\sqrt{2 \pi \hbar}} d p \).

\( =\frac{1}{2 \pi \hbar} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \exp \left[-i \frac{p^{2} t}{2 m \hbar}+i \frac{p}{\hbar}\left(x-x^{\prime}\right)\right] d p \).

\( =\frac{1}{2 \pi \hbar} \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{i t / 2 m \hbar}} \exp \left[-\frac{\left(x-x^{\prime}\right)^{2} / \hbar^{2}}{2 i t /(m \hbar)}\right] \).

where we have used the general expression given for the Gaussian integral. So, for the free particle

\( K\left(x, x^{\prime}, t\right)=\sqrt{\frac{m}{2 \pi i \hbar t}} \exp \left[\frac{i m}{2 \hbar t}\left(x-x^{\prime}\right)^{2}\right] \).

(e) For this initial state,

\( \Psi(x, t)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} K\left(x, x^{\prime}, t\right) \Psi\left(x^{\prime}, 0\right) d x^{\prime} \).

\( =\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \sqrt{\frac{m}{2 \pi i \hbar t}} \exp \left[\frac{i m}{2 \hbar t}\left(x-x^{\prime}\right)^{2}\right]\left(\frac{2 a}{\pi}\right)^{1 / 4} e^{-a x^{\prime 2}} d x^{\prime} \).

\( =\sqrt{\frac{m}{2 \pi i \hbar t}}\left(\frac{2 a}{\pi}\right)^{1 / 4} \exp \left[\frac{i m}{2 \hbar t} x^{2}\right] \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \exp \left[-\left(a-\frac{i m}{2 \hbar t}\right) x^{\prime 2}-\frac{i m}{\hbar t} x x^{\prime}\right] d x^{\prime} \).

\( =\sqrt{\frac{m}{2 \pi i \hbar t}}\left(\frac{2 a}{\pi}\right)^{1 / 4} \exp \left[\frac{i m}{2 \hbar t} x^{2}\right] \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{a-i m /(2 \hbar t)}} \exp \left[-\frac{(m x / \hbar t)^{2}}{4(a-i m /(2 \hbar t))}\right] \).

where we have again used the general expression for the Gaussian integral. This simplifies to

\( \Psi(x, t)=\left(\frac{2 a}{\pi}\right)^{1 / 4} \sqrt{\frac{1}{1+i 2 a \hbar t / m}} \exp \left[-\frac{a}{1+i 2 a \hbar t / m} x^{2}\right] \).

exactly as found in .
