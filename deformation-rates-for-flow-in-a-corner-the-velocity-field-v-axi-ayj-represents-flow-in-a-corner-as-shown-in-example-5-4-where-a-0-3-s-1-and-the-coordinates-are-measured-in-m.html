Guest Question.

DEFORMATION RATES FOR FLOW IN A CORNER

The velocity field \(\vec{V}=A x \hat{i}-A y \hat{j}\) represents flow in a “corner,” as shown in Example 5.4, where \(A=0.3 \mathrm{~s}^{-1}\) and the coordinates are measured in meters. A square is marked in the fluid as shown at \(t=0\). Evaluate the new positions of the four corner points when point \(a\) has moved to \(x=\frac{3}{2} \mathrm{~m}\) after \(\tau\) seconds. Evaluate the rates of linear deformation in the \(x\) and \(y\) directions. Compare area \(a^{\prime} b^{\prime} c^{\prime} d^{\prime}\) at \(t=\tau\) with area \(a b c d\) at \(t=0\). Comment on the significance of this result.

Admin
Solution

Given: \(\vec{V}=A x \hat{i}-A y \hat{j} ; A=0.3 \mathrm{~s}^{-1}, x\) and \(y\) in meters.


Find:

(a) Position of square at \(t=\tau\) when \(a\) is at
\(a^{\prime}\) at \(x=\frac{3}{2} \mathrm{~m}\).
(b) Rates of linear deformation.
(c) Area \(a^{\prime} b^{\prime} c^{\prime} d^{\prime}\) compared with area \(a b c d\).
(d) Significance of the results.
Solution:
First we must find \(\tau\), so we must follow a fluid particle using a
Lagrangian description. Thus

\(\begin{aligned}&u=\frac{d x_{p}}{d t}=A x_{p}, \quad \frac{d x}{x}=A d t, \text { so } \int_{x_{0}}^{x} \frac{d x}{x}=\int_{0}^{\tau} A d t \quad \text { and } \quad \ln \frac{x}{x_{0}}=A \tau \\&\tau=\frac{\ln x / x_{0}}{A}=\frac{\ln \left(\frac{3}{2}\right)}{0.3 \mathrm{~s}^{-1}}=1.35 \mathrm{~s}\end{aligned}\)

 

In the \(y\) direction

\(v=\frac{d y_{p}}{d t}=-A y_{p} \quad \frac{d y}{y}=-A d t \quad \frac{y}{y_{0}}=e^{-A \tau}\)

 

The rates of linear deformation are:
\(\begin{aligned}&\frac{\partial u}{\partial x}=\frac{\partial}{\partial x} A x=A=0.3 \mathrm{~s}^{-1} \quad \text { in the } x \text { direction } \\&\frac{\partial v}{\partial y}=\frac{\partial}{\partial y}(-A y)=-A=-0.3 \mathrm{~s}^{-1} \quad \text { in the } y \text { direction }\end{aligned}\)

The point coordinates at \(\tau\) are: The plot is:
Point t=0 \(t=\tau\)
a (1,1) \((\frac{3}{2},\frac{2}{3})\)
b (1,2) \((\frac{3}{2},\frac{4}{3})\)
c (2,2) \((3,\frac{4}{3})\)
d (2,1) \((3,\frac{2}{3})\)

The rate of volume dilation is

\(\nabla \cdot \vec{V}=\frac{\partial u}{\partial x}+\frac{\partial v}{\partial y}=A-A=0\)

Area \(a b c d=1 \mathrm{~m}^{2}\) and area \(a^{\prime} b^{\prime} c^{\prime} d^{\prime}=\left(3-\frac{3}{2}\right)\left(\frac{4}{3}-\frac{2}{3}\right)=1 \mathrm{~m}^{2}\).

 

Notes:

  • Parallel planes remain parallel; there is linear deformation but no angular deformation.
  • The flow is irrotational (\(\partial v/\partial x – \partial u/\partial y = 0\)).
  • Volume is conserved because the two rates of linear deformation are equal and opposite.
  • The NCFMF video Flow Visualization (see http://web.mit.edu/fluids/www/Shapiro/ncfmf.html for free online viewing of this film) uses hydrogen bubble time-streak markers to demonstrate experimentally that the area of a marked fluid square is conserved in two-dimensional incompressible flow.
