Guest Question.

Evaluate the integrals
\(K_{12}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}(x_{0}+x)\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{1}}{\mathrm{d} x}\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{2}}{\mathrm{d} x}dx\) , \(G_{12}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}(x_{0}+x)\psi _{1}\psi _{2}dx\)
using the Newton—Cotes and Gauss quadratures when \(\psi _{i}\) are the quadratic interpolation functions
\(\psi _{1}=\left ( 1-\frac{x-x_{a}}{x_{b}-x_{a}} \right )\left ( 1-2\frac{x-x_{a}}{x_{b}-x_{a}} \right )=-\frac{1}{2}\zeta (1-\zeta )\)
\(\psi _{2}=4\left ( \frac{x-x_{a}}{x_{b}-x_{a}} \right )\left ( 1-\frac{x-x_{a}}{x_{b}-x_{a}} \right )=(1-\zeta ^{2})\)
\(\psi _{3}=\frac{x-x_{a}}{x_{b}-x_{a}}\left ( 1-2\frac{x-x_{a}}{x_{b}-x_{a}} \right )=\frac{1}{2}\zeta (1+\zeta )\)

Admin
Solution

Note that the integrand F(x) in the integral of \(K_{12}\) is a cubic polynomial (i.e., the degree is r = 3). Hence, we expect the three-point Newtone—Cotes or twopoint Gauss quadrature to yield the exact value. On the other hand, the integrand of \(G_{12}\) is a fifth-order polynomial (i.e., the degree is r = 5). Hence, we expect the five-point Newtone—Cotes or three-point Gauss quadrature to yield the exact value.

The exact values are

\(K_{12}=-\frac{2}{3(x_{b}-x_{a})}(4x_{0}+3x_{a}+x_{b})\) , \(G_{12}=\frac{x_{b}-x_{a}}{15}(x_{0}+x_{a})\)

For convenience of using the Gauss quadrature, we write the given integrals in terms of the normalized coordinate \(\zeta \):

\(K_{12}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}(x_{0}+x)\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{1}}{\mathrm{d} x}\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{2}}{\mathrm{d} x}dx=\frac{2}{x_{b}-x_{a}}\int_{-1}^{1}[x_{0}+x(\zeta )]\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{1}}{\mathrm{d} \zeta }\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{2}}{\mathrm{d} \zeta }d\zeta \)

= \(\frac{2}{x_{b}-x_{a}}\int_{-1}^{1}\left [ x_{0}+x_{a}+\frac{x_{b}-x_{a}}{2}(1+\zeta ) \right ](\zeta -2\zeta ^{2})d\zeta \)

 

\(G_{12}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}(x_{0}+x)\psi _{1}\psi _{2}dx=\frac{x_{b}-x_{a}}{2}\int_{-1}^{1}[x_{0}+x(\zeta )]\psi _{1}(\zeta )\psi _{2}(\zeta )d\zeta \)

= \(-\frac{x_{b}-x_{a}}{4}\int_{-1}^{1}\left [ x_{0}+x_{a}+\frac{x_{b}-x_{a}}{2}(1+\zeta ) \right ]\left ( \zeta -\zeta ^{2} \right )\left ( 1-\zeta ^{2} \right )d\zeta \)

 

(a) Newton—Cotes Quadrature: We evaluate \(K_{12}\) using r = 3; we have \(\zeta_{1} \) = -1, \(\zeta_{2} \) = 0.0 , \(\zeta_{3} \) = 1, \(w_{1}=w_{3} =\frac{1}{6}\) , \(w_{2} =\frac{4}{6}\), and

\(K_{12}=2\cdot \frac{2}{x_{b}-x_{a}}\left [ -3(x_{0}+x_{a})\frac{1}{6}+0\cdot \frac{4}{6}-(x_{0}+x_{b})\frac{1}{6} \right ]\)

= \(-\frac{2}{3(x_{b}-x_{a})}(4x_{0}+3x_{a}+x_{b})\)

We evaluate \(G_{12}\) using r = 5; we have \(\zeta _{1}\) = -1, \(\zeta _{2}\) = -0.5, \(\zeta _{3}\) = 0.0, \(\zeta _{4}\) = 0.5, \(\zeta _{5}\) = 1.0, \(w_{1}=\frac{7}{90}\) , \(w_{2}=w_{4}=\frac{32}{90}\) , \(w_{3}=\frac{12}{90}\) , and

\(G_{12}=-2\cdot \frac{x_{b}-x_{a}}{4}\left [ 0\cdot \frac{7}{90}+(x_{0}+x_{a}+\frac{x_{b}-x_{a}}{2}(0.5)) (-\frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{4})(1-\frac{1}{4})\cdot \frac{32}{90}\right ]\)

+ \(0\cdot \frac{12}{90}+(x_{0}+X_{a}+\frac{x_{b}-x_{a}}{2}(1.5))(\frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{4})\cdot \frac{32}{90}+0\cdot \frac{7}{90}\)

= \(\frac{x_{b}-x_{a}}{15}(x_{0}+x_{a})\)

(b) Gauss—Legendre Quadrature: To evaluate K12, we use r = 2; we have \(\zeta _{1}=-0.57735=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\) , \(\zeta _{2}=0.57735=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\) , \(w_{1}=w_{3}=1\) and

\(K_{12}=\frac{2}{x_{b}-x_{a}} \) \(\left \{ \left [ x_{0}+x_{a}+\frac{x_{b}-x_{a}}{2}(1-\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}) \right ](-\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}-\frac{2}{3})+\left [ x_{0}+x_{a}+\frac{x_{b}-x_{a}}{2}(1+\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}) \right ](\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}-\frac{2}{3}) \right \}\)

= \(-\frac{2}{3(x_{b}-x_{a})}(4x_{0}+3x_{a}+x_{b})\)

To evaluate \(G_{12}\) we use r = 3; we have \(\zeta _{1}=-0.77459=-\sqrt{\frac{3}{5}}\) , \(\zeta _{2}=0.0\) , \(\zeta _{3}=0.77459=\sqrt{\frac{3}{5}}\) , \(w_{1}=w_{3}=0.55555=\frac{5}{9}\) , \(w_{2}=0.88888\) , and

\(G_{12}=-\frac{x_{b}-x_{a}}{4}\) \(\left \{ \left [ x_{0}+x_{a}+\frac{x_{b}-x_{a}}{2})1-a \right ](-a-a^{2})(1-a^{2})\cdot w+0\cdot w_{2} +\left [ x_{0}+x_{a}+\frac{x_{b}-x_{a}}{2} (1+a)\right ](a-a^{2})(1-a^{2})\cdot w\right \}\)

= \(\frac{x_{b}-x_{a}}{2}a^{2}(1-a^{2})(x_{0}+x_{a})w\)

where a = \(\sqrt{\frac{3}{5}}\) and w = \(\frac{5}{9}\) . Substitution of these values gives the result (which is equal to the exact value).
