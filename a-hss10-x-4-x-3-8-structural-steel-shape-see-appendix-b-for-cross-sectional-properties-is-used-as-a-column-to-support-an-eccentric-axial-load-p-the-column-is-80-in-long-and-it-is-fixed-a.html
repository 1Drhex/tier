Guest Question.

A HSS10 × 4 × 3/8 structural steel shape (see Appendix B for cross-sectional properties) is used as a column to support an eccentric axial load P. The column is 80 in. long and it is fixed at its base and free at its upper end. At the upper end of the column (see Figure P16.80), the load P is applied to a bracket at a distance of e = 8 in. from the x axis, creating a bending moment about the weak axis of the HSS shape (i.e., the y axis). Apply the AISC equations given in Section 16.5 and assume that E = 29,000 ksi and \(\sigma_{Y}\) = 46 ksi. Based on the allowable stress method:
(a) Determine whether the column is safe for a load of P = 25 kips. Report the results in the form of the stress ratio \(\sigma_{x} / \sigma_{\text {allow }}\).
(b) Determine the magnitude of the largest load P that may be applied to the column.

Admin
Solution

The following section properties for a standard steel HSS10 × 4 × 3/8 shape are given in Appendix B:

\(A=8.97 in .^{2}, I_{y}=24.3 in .^{4}, r_{y}=1.64 in.\)

Limiting slenderness ratio

\(\begin{aligned}&4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{Y}}}=4.71 \sqrt{\frac{29,000 ksi }{46 ksi }}=118.261 \\&K_{y} L / r_{y}=(2)(80 in .) /(1.64 in .)=97.561\end{aligned}\)

Elastic critical buckling stress

\(\sigma_{e}=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{\left(\frac{K L}{r}\right)^{2}}=\frac{\pi^{2}(29,000 ksi )}{(97.561)^{2}}=30.071 ksi\)

Critical and allowable buckling stresses

Since \(\frac{K L}{r} \leq 4.71 \sqrt{\frac{E}{\sigma_{y}}}\)

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{c r}=\left[0.658^{\left(\frac{\sigma_{y}}{\sigma_{e}}\right)}\right] \sigma_{Y}=\left[0.658^{\left(\frac{46 ksi }{30.07 ksi }\right)}\right](46 ksi )=24.249 ksi \\&\sigma_{\text {allow }}=\frac{\sigma_{c r}}{1.67}=\frac{24.249 ksi }{1.67}=14.520 ksi\end{aligned}\)

(a) Allowable stress method with P = 25 kips:

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{x}=\frac{P}{A}+\frac{M c}{I}=\frac{25 kips }{8.97 in .^{2}}+\frac{(25 kips )(8 in. )(4 in . / 2)}{24.3 in .^{4}} \\&\quad=2.787 ksi +16.461 ksi =19.248 ksi \\&\frac{\sigma_{x}}{\sigma_{\text {allow }}}=\frac{19.248 ksi }{14.520 ksi }=1.326 \quad \therefore \text { Not safe }\end{aligned}\)

(b) Magnitude of the largest load P:

\(\begin{aligned}&P\left[\frac{1}{8.97 in .^{2}}+\frac{(8 in .)(4 in . / 2)}{24.3 in .^{4}}\right] \leq 14.520 ksi \\&\therefore P \leq 18.860 kips =18.86 kips\end{aligned}\)
