Guest Question.

Air exits from a reservoir maintained at 20°C and 500 kPa absolute into a receiver maintained at (a) 300 kPa absolute and (b) 200 kPa absolute. Estimate the mass flux if the exit area is \(10 cm^2\). Use the equations first and then the isentropic flow table, Table D.1. Refer to Fig. 9.5.

TABLE D.1 Isentropic Flow

Admin
Solution

To estimate the mass flux we will assume isentropic flow. For air the receiver pressure that would result in \(\mathrm{M}_{e}=1\) is

\(p_{r}=0.5283 p_{0}=0.5283 \times 500=264.2 \mathrm{kPa}\)

For part (a) \(\mathrm{M}_{e}<1\) since \(p_{r}>264.2 \mathrm{kPa}\), and for part (b) choked flow occurs and \(\mathrm{M}_{e}=1\) since \(p_{r}<264.2 \mathrm{kPa}\)

(a) To find the exit Mach number, Eq. 9.3.13 gives

\(\frac{p_{0}}{p}=\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} \mathrm{M}^{2}\right)^{k /(k-1)}\quad\quad\quad\quad\)

\(\frac{\rho_{0}}{\rho}=\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} \mathrm{M}^{2}\right)^{1 /(k-1)}\quad\quad\quad(9.3.13)\)

\(1+\frac{k-1}{2} \mathrm{M}^{2}=\left(\frac{p_{0}}{p}\right)^{(k-1) / k}\)

Or

\(\begin{aligned}\mathrm{M}_{e}^{2} &=\frac{2}{k-1}\left(\frac{p_{0}}{p}\right)^{(k-1) / k}-\frac{2}{k-1} \\&=\frac{2}{0.4}\left(\frac{500}{300}\right)^{0.2857}-\frac{2}{0.4}=0.7857 \quad \therefore \mathrm{M}_{e}=0.8864\end{aligned}\)

The mass flux is given by Eq. \(9.3 .17\) and is found to be

\(\begin{aligned} \dot{m} &=\frac{p_{0}\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} \mathrm{M}^{2}\right)^{k /(1-k)}}{\sqrt{T_{0}}\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} \mathrm{M}^{2}\right)^{-1 / 2}} \sqrt{\frac{k}{R}} A \mathrm{M} \\ &=p_{0} \sqrt{\frac{k}{R T_{0}}} \mathrm{M} A\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} \mathrm{M}^{2}\right)^{(k+1) / 2(1-k)} \end{aligned}\quad\quad\quad(9.3.17)\)

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{m} &=p_{0} \sqrt{\frac{k}{R T_{0}}} \mathrm{M} A\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} \mathrm{M}^{2}\right)^{(k+1) / 2(1-k)} \\&=500000 \sqrt{\frac{1.4}{287 \times 293}} \times 0.8864 \times 0.001\left(1+\frac{0.4}{2} \times 0.8864^{2}\right)^{-2.4 / 0.8} \\&=1.167 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{s}\end{aligned}\)

(b) Choked flow occurs and thus \(\mathrm{M}_{e}=1\); Eq. \(9.3 .18\) yields

\(\dot{m}=p_{0} A^{*} \sqrt{\frac{k}{R T_{0}}}\left(\frac{k+1}{2}\right)^{(k+1) / 2(1-k)}\quad\quad\quad(9.3.18)\)

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{m} &=p_{0} A^{*} \sqrt{\frac{k}{R T_{0}}}\left(\frac{k+1}{2}\right)^{(k+1) / 2(1-k)} \\&=500000 \times 0.001 \sqrt{\frac{1.4}{287 \times 293}}\left(\frac{2.4}{2}\right)^{-2.4 / 0.8}=1.181 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{s}\end{aligned}\)

Now, let us use the isentropic flow table (Table D.1) and solve parts (a) and (b).

(a) For a pressure ratio of \(p / p_{0}=300 / 500=0.6\), we interpolate to find

\(\begin{array}{l}\mathrm{M}_{e}=\frac{0.6041-0.6}{0.6041-0.5913} \times 0.02+0.88=0.886 \\\\\frac{T_{e}}{T_{0}}=\frac{0.6041-0.6}{0.6041-0.5913}(0.8606-0.8659)+0.8659=0.864 \\\\T_{e}=0.864 \times 293=253 \mathrm{~K}\end{array}\)

The velocity and density are, respectively,

\(\begin{array}{c}V=\mathrm{M} c=0.886 \sqrt{1.4 \times 287 \times 253}=282 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s} \\\rho=\frac{p}{R T}=\frac{300}{0.287 \times 253}=4.13 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\end{array}\)

The mass flux is then

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{m} &=\rho A V \\&=4.13 \times 0.001 \times 282=1.165 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{s}\end{aligned}\)

(b) For choked flow we know that \(\mathrm{M}_{e}=1\). The table gives

\(\frac{T_{e}}{T_{0}}=0.8333 \quad \text { and } \quad \frac{p_{e}}{p_{0}}=0.5283\)

Thus the temperature, velocity, and density are, respectively,

\(\begin{array}{l}T=0.8333 \times 293=244.2 \mathrm{~K} \\\\V=\mathrm{M} c=1 \times \sqrt{1.4 \times 287 \times 244.2}=313.2 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s} \\\\\rho=\frac{p}{R T}=\frac{0.5283 \times 500}{0.287 \times 244.2}=3.769 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\end{array}\)

The mass flux is calculated to be

\(\dot{m}=\rho A V\)\(=3.769 \times 0.001 \times 313.2=1.180 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{s}\)

The results using the equations are essentially the same as those using the tables.
