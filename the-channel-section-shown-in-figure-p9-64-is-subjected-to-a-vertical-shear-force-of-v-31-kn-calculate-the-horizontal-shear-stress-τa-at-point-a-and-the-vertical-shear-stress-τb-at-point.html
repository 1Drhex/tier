Guest Question.

The channel section shown in Figure P9.64 is subjected to a vertical shear force of V = 31 kN. Calculate the horizontal shear stress \(\tau_{A}\) at point A, and the vertical shear stress \(\tau_{B}\) at point B.

Admin
Solution

Centroid location in y direction:

\(\begin{array}{lccccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Width } b \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Height } h \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} \text { (from bottom) } \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { left flange } & 12 & 76 & 912 & 38 & 34,656 \\\text { web } & 226 & 16 & 3,616 & 8 & 28,928 \\\text { right flange } & 12 & 76 & 912 & 38 & 34,656 \\\hline & & & 5,440 & & 98,240\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{98,240 mm ^{3}}{5,440 mm ^{2}} &=18.059 mm \text{(from bottom of shape to centroid)} \\&=57.941 mm \text{(from top of shape to centroid)}\end{aligned}\)

 

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

\(\begin{array}{lcccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { left flange } & 438,976.00 & 19.94 & 362,657.27 & 801,633.27 \\\text { web } & 77,141.33 & -10.06 & 365,866.63 & 443,007.96 \\\text { right flange } & 438,976.00 & 19.94 & 362,657.27 & 801,633.27 \\\hline & & \text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( mm ^{4}\right)= & 2,046,274.51\end{array}\)

 

Shear stress at A:
We will consider Q for the web, starting at A and extending rightward to the y axis.

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{A} &=(125 mm -50 mm )(16 mm )(18.059 mm -8 mm ) \\&=12,070.800 mm ^{3}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\tau_{A}=\frac{(31,000 N )\left(120,070.800 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(2,046,274.51 mm ^{4}\right)(16 mm )}=11.4291 MPa =11.43 MPa\)

 

Shear stress at B:
We will consider Q for the flange, starting at the z axis and extending upward.

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{B} &=(12 mm )(57.941 mm )\left(\frac{57.941 mm }{2}\right) \\&=20,142.957 mm ^{3}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\tau_{B}=\frac{(31,000 N )\left(20,142.957 mm ^{3}\right)}{\left(2,046,274.51 mm ^{4}\right)(12 mm )}=25.4296 MPa =25.4 MPa\)
