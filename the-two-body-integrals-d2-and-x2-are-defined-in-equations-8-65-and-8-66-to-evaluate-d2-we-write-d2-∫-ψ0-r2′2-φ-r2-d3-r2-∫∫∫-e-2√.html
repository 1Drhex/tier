Guest Question.

The two-body integrals \( D_{2} \text { and } X_{2} \) are defined in Equations 8.65 and 8.66. To evaluate \( D_{2} \) we write

\( D_{2}=\iint\left|\psi_{0}\left(r_{1}\right)\right|^{2} \frac{a}{r_{12}}\left|\psi_{0}\left(r_{2}^{\prime}\right)\right|^{2} d^{3} r _{1} d^{3} r _{2} \) (8.65).

\( X_{2}=\iint \psi_{0}\left(r_{1}\right) \psi_{0}\left(r_{1}^{\prime}\right) \frac{a}{r_{12}} \psi_{0}\left(r_{2}\right) \psi_{0}\left(r_{2}^{\prime}\right) d^{3} r _{1} d^{3} r _{2} \) (8.66).

\( D_{2}=\int\left|\psi_{0}\left(r_{2}^{\prime}\right)\right|^{2} \Phi\left(r_{2}\right) d^{3} r _{2} \) \( =\iiint \frac{e^{-2 \sqrt{R^{2}+r_{2}^{2}-2 R r_{2} \cos \theta_{2}} / a}}{\pi a^{3}} \Phi\left(r_{2}\right) r_{2}^{2} d r_{2} \sin \theta_{2} d \theta_{2} d \phi_{2} \)

where \( \theta_{2} \) is the angle between R and \( r_{2} \) (Figure 8.8), and

\( \Phi\left(r_{2}\right) \equiv \int\left|\psi_{0}\left(r_{1}\right)\right|^{2} \frac{a}{\left| r _{1}- r _{2}\right|} d^{3} r _{1} \).

(a) Consider first the integral over \( r _{1} \text {. Align the } z \text { axis with } r _{2} \) (which is a constant vector for the purposes of this first integral) so that

\( \Phi\left(r_{2}\right)=\frac{1}{\pi a^{3}} \iiint \frac{a e^{-2 r_{1} / a}}{\sqrt{r_{1}^{2}+r_{2}^{2}-2 r_{1} r_{2} \cos \theta_{1}}} r_{1}^{2} d r_{1} \sin \theta_{1} d \theta_{1} d \phi_{1} \).

Do the angular integration first and show that

\( \Phi\left(r_{2}\right)=\frac{a}{r_{2}}-\left(1+\frac{a}{r_{2}}\right) e^{-2 r_{2} / a} \).

(b) Plug your result from part (a) back into the relation for \( D_{2} \), and show that

\( D_{2}=\frac{a}{R}-e^{-2 R / a}\left[\frac{1}{6}\left(\frac{R}{a}\right)^{2}+\frac{3}{4}\left(\frac{R}{a}\right)+\frac{11}{8}+\frac{a}{R}\right] \) (8.70).

Again, do the angular integration first.

Comment: The integral \( X_2\) can also be evaluated in closed form, but the procedure is rather involved. We will simply quote the result,

\( X_{2}=e^{-2 R / a}\left[\frac{5}{8}-\frac{23}{20} \frac{R}{a}-\frac{3}{5}\left(\frac{R}{a}\right)^{2}-\frac{1}{15}\left(\frac{R}{a}\right)^{3}\right] \)

\( +\frac{6}{5} \frac{a}{R} I^{2}\left[\gamma+\log \left(\frac{R}{a}\right)+\left(\frac{\tilde{I}}{I}\right)^{2} Ei \left(-\frac{4 R}{a}\right)-2 \frac{\tilde{I}}{I} Ei \left(-\frac{2 R}{a}\right)\right] \). (8.71)

where \( \gamma=0.5772 \ldots \text { is Euler's constant, Ei }(x) \) is the exponential
integral

\( \operatorname{Ei}(x)=-\int_{-x}^{\infty} \frac{e^{-t}}{t} d t \).

\( \text { and } \tilde{I} \text { is obtained from } I \text { by switching the sign of } R \text { : } \)

\( \tilde{I}=e^{R / a}\left[1-\frac{R}{a}+\frac{1}{3}\left(\frac{R}{a}\right)^{2}\right] \) (8.72).

Solution

(a) The \( \phi_{1} \) integral is trivial:

\( \Phi\left(r_{2}\right)=\frac{2 \pi}{\pi a^{2}} \int e^{-2 r_{1} / a} \frac{1}{\sqrt{r_{1}^{2}+r_{2}^{2}-2 r_{1} r_{2} \cos \theta_{1}}} r_{1}^{2} d r_{1} \sin \theta_{1} d \theta_{1} \).

Now do the \( \theta_{1} \) integral:

\( \int_{0}^{\pi} \frac{\sin \theta_{1}}{\sqrt{r_{1}^{2}+r_{2}^{2}-2 r_{1} r_{2} \cos \theta_{1}}} d \theta_{1}=\left.\frac{-1}{r_{1} r_{2}} \sqrt{r_{1}^{2}+r_{2}^{2}-2 r_{1} r_{2} \cos \theta_{1}}\right|_{0} ^{\pi} \).

\( =\frac{1}{r_{1} r_{2}}\left(\sqrt{\left(r_{1}+r_{2}\right)^{2}}-\sqrt{\left(r_{1}-r_{2}\right)^{2}}\right)=\frac{r_{1}+r_{2}-\left|r_{1}-r_{2}\right|}{r_{1} r_{2}} \).

\( = \begin{cases}2 / r_{2}, & \text { if } r_{1}<r_{2} \\ 2 / r_{1} & \text { if } r_{1}>r_{2}\end{cases} \).

Finally, the \( r_{1} \) integral:

\( \Phi\left(r_{2}\right)=\frac{4}{a^{2}}\left\{\frac{1}{r_{2}} \int_{0}^{r_{2}} e^{-2 r_{1} / a} r_{1}^{2} d r_{1}+\int_{r_{2}}^{\infty} e^{-2 r_{1} / a} r_{1} d r_{1}\right\} \).

\( =\frac{4}{a^{2}}\left\{\left.\frac{1}{r_{2}}\left[-\frac{a^{3}}{4} e^{-2 r_{1} / a}\left(2\left(\frac{r_{1}}{a}\right)^{2}+2\left(\frac{r_{1}}{a}\right)+1\right)\right]\right|_{0} ^{r_{2}}+\left.\left[-\frac{a^{2}}{4} e^{-2 r_{1} / a}\left(2 \frac{r_{1}}{a}+1\right)\right]\right|_{r_{2}} ^{\infty}\right\} \).

\( =\frac{a}{r_{2}}-\left(1+\frac{a}{r_{2}}\right) e^{-2 r_{2} / a} \).

(b) Again, the \( \phi_{2} \) integral is trivial:

\( D_{2}=\frac{2 \pi}{\pi a^{3}} \int e^{-2 \sqrt{R^{2}+r_{2}^{2}-2 R r_{2} \cos \theta_{2}} / a}\left[\frac{a}{r_{2}}-\left(1+\frac{a}{r_{2}}\right) e^{-2 r_{2} / a}\right] r_{2}^{2} d r_{2} \sin \theta_{2} d \theta_{2} \).

For the \( \theta_{2} \) integral, let \( u \equiv 2 \sqrt{R^{2}+r_{2}^{2}-2 R r_{2} \cos \theta_{2}} / a, \text { so } d u=\frac{4 R r_{2}}{P} a^{2} u \sin \theta_{2} d \theta_{2} \). Then

\( I_{\theta} \equiv \int_{0}^{\pi} e^{-2 \sqrt{R^{2}+r_{2}^{2}-2 \operatorname{Rr}_{2} \cos \theta_{2}} / a} \sin \theta_{2} d \theta_{2}=\frac{a^{2}}{4 R r_{2}} \int_{u_{1}}^{u_{2}} u e^{-u} d u=-\left.\frac{a^{2}}{4 R r_{2}}(u+1) e^{-u}\right|_{u_{1}} ^{u_{2}} \).

\( \text { Now, letting } \rho \equiv 2 R / a \text { and } x \equiv r_{2} / R \),

\( u_{2}=\frac{2}{a} \sqrt{R^{2}+r_{2}^{2}+2 R r_{2}}=\frac{2}{a}\left(R+r_{2}\right)=\rho(1+x), \quad u_{1}=\frac{2}{a} \sqrt{R^{2}+r_{2}^{2}-2 R r_{2}}=\frac{2}{a}\left|R-r_{2}\right|=\rho|1-x| \).

Then

\( I_{\theta}=\frac{1}{\rho^{2} x}\left\{[\rho|1-x|+1] e^{-\rho|1-x|}-[\rho(1+x)+1] e^{-\rho(1+x)}\right\} \).

Finally, the \( r_2\) integral becomes

\( D_{2}=\frac{1}{4} \int_{0}^{\infty}\left\{[\rho|1-x|+1] e^{-\rho|1-x|}-[\rho(1+x)+1] e^{-\rho(1+x)}\right\}\left[2-(\rho x+2) e^{-\rho x}\right] d x \).

\( =\frac{e^{-\rho}}{4} \int_{0}^{1}\left\{[\rho(1-x)+1] e^{\rho x}\right\}\left[2-(\rho x+2) e^{-\rho x}\right] d x \) \( +\frac{e^{\rho}}{4} \int_{1}^{\infty}\left\{[\rho(x-1)+1] e^{-\rho x}\right\}\left[2-(\rho x+2) e^{-\rho x}\right] d x \)

\( -\frac{e^{-\rho}}{4} \int_{0}^{\infty}\left\{[\rho(1+x)+1] e^{-\rho x}\right\}\left[2-(\rho x+2) e^{-\rho x}\right] d x \).

\( =\frac{e^{-\rho}}{4}\left\{2 \int_{0}^{1}[(\rho+1)-\rho x] e^{\rho x} d x-\int_{0}^{1}\left[2(\rho+1)+(\rho-1) \rho x-(\rho x)^{2}\right] d x\right\} \)

\( +\frac{e^{\rho}}{4}\left\{2 \int_{1}^{\infty}[(1-\rho)+\rho x] e^{-\rho x} d x-\int_{1}^{\infty}\left[2(1-\rho)+\rho x(3-\rho)+(\rho x)^{2}\right] e^{-2 \rho x} d x\right\} \).

\( -\frac{e^{-\rho}}{4}\left\{2 \int_{0}^{\infty}[(1+\rho)+\rho x] e^{-\rho x} d x-\int_{0}^{\infty}\left[2(1+\rho)+(3+\rho) \rho x+(\rho x)^{2}\right] e^{-2 \rho x} d x\right\} \).

\( =\frac{e^{-\rho}}{4}\left\{\frac{2}{\rho}\left(2 e^{\rho}-\rho-2\right)-\left[2+\frac{3}{2} \rho+\frac{1}{6} \rho^{2}\right]\right\}+\frac{e^{\rho}}{4}\left\{\frac{4}{\rho} e^{-\rho}-\left[\frac{3}{4}+\frac{2}{\rho}\right] e^{-2 \rho}\right\} \)

\( -\frac{e^{-\rho}}{4}\left\{2\left[1+\frac{2}{\rho}\right]-\left[\frac{5}{4}+\frac{2}{\rho}\right]\right\}=\frac{2}{\rho}-\frac{1}{8}\left(11+3 \rho+\frac{16}{\rho}+\frac{\rho^{2}}{3}\right) e^{-\rho} \).

Or, puitting back \( \rho \equiv 2 R / a \):

\( D_{2}=\frac{a}{R}-\left(\frac{11}{8}+\frac{3 R}{4 a}+\frac{a}{R}+\frac{R^{2}}{6 a^{2}}\right) e^{-2 R / a} \).
