Guest Question.

Consider the infinitely long (-∞ < y < +∞) wedge-shaped groove as shown in Fig. 4-7. The groove has sides of widths a and b and an opening angle α. Determine the view factor between the narrow strips shown in the figure.

Admin
Solution

After placing the coordinate system as shown in the figure, we find \(Z_{1} =0 , x_{2} =u_{2} \cos \alpha\) and \(Z_{2}=u_{2}\sin \alpha\), leading to

\(S^{2} =(x_{1}-u_{2}\cos \alpha)^{2} +(y_{1}-y_{2} )^{2} +u_{2}^{2} \sin ^{2} \alpha\)

\(=(x_{1}^{2}-2x_{1}u_{2}\cos \alpha +u_{2}^{2} )+ (y_{1}-y_{2} )^{2} =S^{2}_{0} +(y_{1}-y_{2} )^{2}\),

where \(S_{0}\) is the projection of S in the x-z-plane and is constant in the present problem. The two surface normals are readily determined as

\(\widehat{n }_{1} =\widehat{k}\) , or \(l_{1} =m_{1} =0 , n_{1} =1\),

\(\widehat{n }_{2}= \widehat{I}\sin \alpha -\widehat{k} \cos \alpha\) , or \(l_{2} =\sin \alpha , m_{2} =0 , n_{2} =-\cos \alpha\) ,

leading to

\(\cos \theta _{1} = u_{2}\sin \alpha /S\),

\(\cos \theta _{2}=[(x_{1}-u_{2}\cos \alpha )\sin \alpha+u_{2}\sin \alpha \cos \theta]/S = x_{1} \sin \alpha /S\).

For illustrative purposes we will first calculate \(d F_{d1-strip 2}\) from equation (4.8)

\(F_{d A_{i} -A_{j} } =\int_{A_{j}}^{}{\frac{\cos \theta _{i}\cos \theta _{j}}{\pi S^{2} } dA_{j}}\) ,

and then \(d F_{strip 1-strip 2}\) from equation (4.16)

\(F_{di-j} = \int_{A_{j}} dF_{di-jd}\),

\(F_{i-j} =\frac{1}{A_{i} } \int_{A_{i}} F_{di-j} d A_{i}\).

Thus

\(d F_{d1-strip 2} =\int_{dA_{strip 2} }^{}{\frac{\cos \theta _{1}\cos \theta _{2}}{\pi S^{2} } dA_{2}} =\frac{du_{2}}{\pi } \int_{-\infty }^{+\infty }{\frac{x_{1}u_{2}\sin ^{2} \alpha dy_{2} }{\left[S^{2}_{0}+ (y_{1}-y_{2} )^{2} \right] ^{2} } }\)

\(=\frac{x_{1}u_{2}\sin ^{2} \alpha du_{2}}{\pi } \left[\frac{y_{2}-y_{1}}{2S^{2}_{0} \left[S^{2}_{0}+ (y_{1}-y_{2} )^{2}\right] }+\frac{1}{2S^{3}_{0}}\tan ^{-1} \frac{y_{2}-y_{1}}{S_{0} } \right] ^{+\infty }_{-\infty }\)

\(=\frac{x_{1}u_{2}\sin ^{2} \alpha du_{2}}{2S^{3}_{0} }=\frac{1}{2} \frac{u_{2} \sin \alpha }{S_{0} } \frac{x_{1} \sin \alpha }{S_{0} }\frac{du_{2} }{S_{0} } =\frac{1}{2} \cos \theta _{10} \cos \theta _{20}\frac{du_{2} }{S_{0} }\),

where \(\theta _{10}\) and \(\theta _{20}\) are the projections of \(\theta _{1}\) and \(\theta _{2}\) in the x-z-plane. Looking at Fig. 4-8 this may be rewritten as

\(d F_{d1-strip 2}=\frac{1}{2} \cos \phi d\phi\) ,

where \(\phi = \theta _{10}\) is the off-normal angle at which \(dA_{strip2}\) is oriented from \(dA_{strip1}\) . We note that \(d F_{d1-strip 2}\) does not depend on \(y_{1}\) . No matter where on strip 1 an observer is standing, he sees the same strip 2 extending from – ∞ to +∞. It remains to calculate \(d F_{strip 1-strip 2}\) from equation (4.16).

\(F_{di-j} = \int_{A_{j}} dF_{di-jd}\),

\(F_{i-j} =\frac{1}{A_{i} } \int_{A_{i}} F_{di-j} d A_{i}\).

Since equation (4.16) simply takes an average, and since \(d F_{d1-strip 2}\) does not vary along \(dA_{strip1}\) , it follows immediately that

\(d F_{strip 1-strip 2}=\frac{1}{2} \cos \phi d\phi= \frac{x_{1}\sin ^{2} \alpha u_{2} du_{2}}{2S^{3}_{0} }\).
