Guest Question.

A boat having a mass of 80 Mg rests on the bottom or the lake and displaces \(10.25 m^{3}\) of water. Since the lifting capacity of the crane is only 60 kN, two balloons are attached to the sides of the boat and filled with air. Determine the smallest radius of each spherical balloon that is needed to lift the boat. What is the mass of air in each balloon if the water temperature is 12oC? The balloons are at an average depth of 20 m. Neglect the mass of air and of the balloon for the calculation required for the lift. The volume of a sphere is \(V = \frac{4}{3} \pi r^{3}\).

Admin
Solution

The bouyant force acting on the boat and a balloon are

\((F_{b})_{B} = \rho _{w}g(V_{B})_{sub} = (1000 kg/m^{3})(9.81 m/s^{2})(10.25 m^{3}) = 100.55(10^{3}) N = 100.55 kN \)

 

\((F_{b})_{b} = \rho _{w}g(V_{b})_{sub} = (1000 kg/m^{3})(9.81 m/s^{2})[\frac{4}{3}\pi r^{3}] =13.08\pi r^{3}(10^{3}) N = 13.08\pi r^{3} kN \)

Referring to the FBD of the boat, Fig. a,

\(+ \sum F_{y} = 0; 2T + 100.55 kN + 600 kN – [80(9.81) kN] = 0 \)

 

\(T = 42.124 kN \)

Referring to the FBD of the balloon Fig. b

\(+ \sum F_{y} = 0; 13.08\pi r^{3} – 42.125 kN = 0 \)

 

\(r = 1.008 m = 1.01 m. \)

Here,\( p = p_{atm} + \rho _{w}gh = 101(10^{3}) Pa + (1000 kg/m^{3})(9.81 m/s^{2})(20 m) =\)

\(297.2(10^{3}) Pa\) and \(T = 12Â° C + 273 = 285 K.\) From Appendix A,\( R = 286.9 J/kg \cdot K.\)

Applying the ideal gas law,

\(p = \rho RT; \rho = \frac{p}{RT} =\) \(\frac{297.2(10^{3}) N/m^{2}}{(286.9 J/kg \cdot K)(285 K)} = 3.6347 kg/m^{3} \)

Thus,

\(m = \rho V = (3.6347 kg/m^{3})[\frac{4}{3} \pi (1.008 m)^{3}] = 15.61 kg = 15.6 kg \)
