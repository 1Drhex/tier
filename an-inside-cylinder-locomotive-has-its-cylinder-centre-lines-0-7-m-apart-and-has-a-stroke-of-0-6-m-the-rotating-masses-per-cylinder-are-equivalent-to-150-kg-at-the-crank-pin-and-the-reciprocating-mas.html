Guest Question.

An inside cylinder locomotive has its cylinder centre lines 0.7 m apart and has a stroke of 0.6 m. The rotating masses per cylinder are equivalent to 150 kg at the crank pin, and the reciprocating masses per cylinder to 180 kg. The wheel centre lines are 1.5 m apart. The cranks are at right angles.
The whole of the rotating and 2/3 of the reciprocating masses are to be balanced by masses placed at a radius of 0.6 m. Find the magnitude and direction of the balancing masses.
Find the fluctuation in rail pressure under one wheel, variation of tractive effort and the magnitude of swaying couple at a crank speed of 300 r.p.m.

Admin
Solution

Given : a = 0.7 m; \(l_{ B }=l_{ C }=0.6 m \text { or }\) \(r_{ B }=r_{ C }=0.3 m ; m_{1}=150 kg ; m_{2}=180 kg\); \(\omega=2 \pi \times 300 / 60=31.42 rad / s\)

We know that the equivalent mass of the rotating parts to be balanced per cylinder at the crank pin,

\(m=m_{ B }=m_{ C }=m_{1}+c \cdot m_{2}=150+\frac{2}{3} \times 180=270 kg\)

Magnitude and direction of the balancing masses

Let \(m_{ A } \text { and } m_{ D }\) = Magnitude of the balancing masses

\(\theta_{ A } \text { and } \theta_{ D }\) = Angular position of the balancing masses \(m_{ A }\) and \(m_{ D }\) from the first crank B.

The magnitude and direction of the balancing masses may be determined graphically as discussed below :

1. First of all, draw the space diagram to show the positions of the planes of the wheels an the cylinders, as shown in Fig. 22.7 (a). Since the cranks of the cylinders are at right angles, therefore assuming the position of crank of the cylinder B in the horizontal direction, draw OC and OB at right angles to each other as shown in Fig. 22.7 (b).

3. Now, draw the couple polygon from the data given in Table 22.1 (column 6), to some suitable scale, as shown in Fig 22.7 (c). The closing side c′ o′ represents the balancing couple and it is proportional to \(0.9 m_{ D }\). Therefore, by measurement,

\(0.9 m_{D}=\text { vector } c ^{\prime} o ^{\prime}=94.5 kg – m ^{2} \quad \text { or } \quad m_{ D }=105 kg\)

4. To determine the angular position of the balancing mass D, draw OD in Fig. 22.7 (b) parallel to vector c′ o′ . By measurement,

\(\theta_{ D }=250^{\circ}\)

5. In order to find the balancing mass A, draw the force polygon from the data given in Table 22.1 (column 4), to some suitable scale, as shown in Fig. 22.7 (d), The vector do represents the balancing force and it is proportional to \(0.6 m _{ A }\). Therefore by measurement,

\(0.6 m_{ A }=\text { vector } d o=63 kg – m \text { or } m_{ A }=105 kg\)

6. To determine the angular position of the balancing mass A, draw OA in Fig. 22.7 (b) parallel to vector do. By measurement,

\(\theta_{ A }=200^{\circ}\)

 

Fluctuation in rail pressure

We know that each balancing mass

= 105 kg

\(\therefore \) Balancing mass for rotating masses,

\(D=\frac{m_{1}}{m} \times 105=\frac{150}{270} \times 105=58.3 kg\)

and balancing mass for reciprocating masses,

\(B=\frac{c \cdot m_{2}}{m} \times 105=\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{180}{270} \times 105=46.6 kg\)

This balancing mass of 46.6 kg for reciprocating masses gives rise to the centrifugal force.

\(\therefore \) Fluctuation in rail pressure or hammer blow

\(=B \cdot \omega^{2} \cdot b=46.6(31.42)^{2} 0.6=27602 N\) \(\ldots\left(\because b=r_{ A }=r_{ D }\right)\)

 

Variation of tractive effort

We know that maximum variation of tractive effort

\(=\pm \sqrt{2}(1-c) m_{2} \cdot \omega^{2} \cdot r=\pm \sqrt{2}\left(1-\frac{2}{3}\right) 180(31.42)^{2} 0.3 N\)

\(=\pm 25127 N\) \(\ldots\left(\because r=r_{ B }=r_{ C }\right)\)

 

Swaying couple

We know that maximum swaying couple

\(=\frac{a(1-c)}{\sqrt{2}} \times m_{2} \cdot \omega^{2} \cdot r=\frac{0.7\left(1-\frac{2}{3}\right)}{\sqrt{2}} \times 180(31.42)^{2} 0.3 N – m\)

 

= 8797 N-m
