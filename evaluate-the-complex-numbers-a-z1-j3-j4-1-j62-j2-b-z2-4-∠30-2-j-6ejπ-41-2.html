Guest Question.

Evaluate the complex numbers:

a. \(z_{1}=\frac{j(3-j 4)^{*}}{(-1+j 6)(2+j)^{2}}\)

b. \(z_{2}=\left[4 \angle 30^{\circ}+2-j+6 e^{j \pi / 4}\right]^{1 / 2}\).

Admin
Solution

a. This can be solved in two ways: working with \(z\) in rectangular form or polar form.

Method 1 (working in rectangular form):

Let \(z_{1}=\frac{z_{3} z_{4}}{z_{5} z_{6}}\)

where

\( \begin{aligned} z_{3} &=j \\ z_{4} &=(3-j 4)^{*}=\text { the complex conjugate of }(3-j 4) \\ &=(3+j 4) \\ z_{5} &=-1+j 6 \\ z_{6} &=(2+j)^{2}=4+j 4-1=3+j 4 \end{aligned} \)

Hence,

\( \begin{aligned} z_{1} &=\frac{j(3+j 4)}{(-1+j 6)(3+j 4)}=\frac{j 3-4}{-3-j 4+j 18-24} \\ &=\frac{-4+j 3}{-27+j 14} \end{aligned} \)

Multiplying and dividing by \(-27-j 14\) (rationalization), we have:

\( \begin{aligned} z_{1} &=\frac{(-4+j 3)(-27-j 14)}{(-27+j 14)(-27-j 14)}=\frac{150-j 25}{27^{2}+14^{2}} \\ &=0.1622-j 0.027=0.1644 \angle-9.46^{\circ} \end{aligned} \)

Method 2 (working in polar form)

\( \begin{array}{l} z_{3}=j=1 \angle 90^{\circ} \\ z_{4}=(3-j 4)^{*}=\left(5 \angle-53.13^{\circ}\right)^{*}=5 \angle 53.13^{\circ} \\ z_{5}=(-1+j 6)=\sqrt{37} \angle 99.46^{\circ} \\ z_{6}=(2+j)^{2}=\left(\sqrt{5} \angle 26.56^{\circ}\right)^{2}=5 \angle 53.13^{\circ} \end{array} \)

Hence,

\( \begin{aligned} z_{1} &=\frac{z_{3} z_{4}}{z_{5} z_{6}}=\frac{\left(1 \angle 90^{\circ}\right)\left(5 \angle 53.13^{\circ}\right)}{\left(\sqrt{37} \angle 99.46^{\circ}\right)\left(5 \angle 53.13^{\circ}\right)}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{37}} \angle\left(90^{\circ}-99.46^{\circ}\right) \\ &=0.1644 \angle-9.46^{\circ}=0.1622-j 0.027 \end{aligned} \)

b. Let

\( \begin{array}{l} z_{7}=4 \angle 30^{\circ}=4 \cos 30^{\circ}+j 4 \sin 30^{\circ}=3.464+j 2 \\ z_{8}=2-j \\ z_{9}=6 e^{j \pi / 4}=6 \cos 45^{\circ}+j 6 \sin 45^{\circ}=4.243+j 4.243 \end{array} \)

Then,

\( \begin{aligned} z_{2} &=\left[z_{7}+z_{8}+z_{9}\right]^{1 / 2} \\ &=[3.464+j 2+2-j+4.243+j 4.243]^{1 / 2} \\ &=(9.707+j 5.243)^{1 / 2}=\left(11.03 \angle 28.374^{\circ}\right)^{1 / 2} \\ &=3.32 \angle 14.19^{\circ} \end{aligned} \)
