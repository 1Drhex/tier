PHYSICAL PENDULUM

The rigid body illustrated in Figure 2.45 is pivoted about point O at a distance l from the center of mass G. Gravity acts downward. The equilibrium position corresponds to points O and G in a vertical alignment. If the body is pivoted a small angle θ from the equilibrium position and released, show that the motion is harmonic and determine the period of one oscillation

Admin
Solution

Employing Equation (2.12-6)\([M_O=I_G\ddot{\theta } +m(x_G\ddot{y}_G-\ddot{x}_Gy_G ) =I_G\ddot{\theta }+mr\cos\theta (-r\dot{\theta }^ 2\sin\theta +r\ddot{\theta} \cos\theta )-mr\sin\theta (-r\dot{\theta }^2\cos\theta +r\ddot{\theta }\sin\theta )=I_O\ddot{\theta } ]\), we obtain

\(M_O=-mgl\sin\theta =I_O\ddot{\theta }\) (2.13-6)

Since for small angles we have

\(\sin \theta ≈\theta\) (2.13-7)

then Equation (2.13-6) becomes

\(\ddot{\theta }+\omega ^2_n\theta =0\) (2.13-8)

where the natural frequency of the system is defined as

\(\omega _n=\left\lgroup\frac{mgl}{I_O}\right\rgroup ^{1/2}\) (2.13-9)

The solution of Equation (2.13-8) is [4]

\(\theta =\Theta\cos(\omega _nt+\varphi )\) (2.13-10)

where Θ and ϕ are arbitrary constants. Angular motion is thus harmonic in time. Using Equations (2.13-9) and (2.13-10) the period of time, \(\tau\), for one oscillation is found by recognizing that

\(\omega _n\tau =\left\lgroup\frac{mgl}{I_O}\right\rgroup ^{1/2} \tau =2 \pi\) (2.13-11)

from which

\(\tau =2\pi \left\lgroup\frac{I_O}{mgl}\right\rgroup ^{1/2}\) (2.13-12)
