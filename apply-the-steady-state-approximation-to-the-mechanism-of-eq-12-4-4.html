Guest Question.

Apply the steady-state approximation to the mechanism of \(Eq. (12.4-4):(1) O_{3}+M \rightleftharpoons O_{2} + O + M (fast)\)

\((2) O + O_{3} \longrightarrow 2 O_{2} (slow).\)
Admin
Solution

There is one independent equation for each step in any mechanism. One equation must be written for the rate of change of the concentration of a reactant or product. The other equations must be for the rate of change of concentrations of reactive intermediates. We write the two equations

\(\frac{d[O_{2}]}{dt} = k_{1}[O_{3}][M]−k^{′}_{1}[O_{2}][O][M]+ 2k_{2}[O][O_{3}]\) (12.4-8a)

 

\(\frac{d[O]}{dt} = k_{1}[O_{3}][M]−k^{′}_{1}[O_{2}][O][M]-k_{2}[O][O_{3}]\) (12.4-8b)

We invoke the steady-state approximation by setting d[O]/dt approximately equal to zero:

\(k_{1}[O_{3}][M]−k^{′}_{1}[O_{2}][O][M]-k_{2}[O][O_{3}]≈ 0\) (12.4-8c)

We now have one differential equation and one algebraic equation .We subtract \(Eq. (12.4-8c):k_{1}[O_{3}][M]−k^{′}_{1}[O_{2}][O][M]-k_{2}[O][O_{3}]≈ 0 from Eq. (12.4-8a):\frac{d[O_{2}]}{dt} = k_{1}[O_{3}][M]−k^{′}_{1}[O_{2}][O][M]+ 2k_{2}[O][O_{3}]\) to obtain a simpler differential equation:

\(\frac{d[O_{2}]}{dt} = 3k_{2}[O][O_{3}]\) (12.4-8d)

We solve \(Eq. (12.4-8c):k_{1}[O_{3}][M]−k^{′}_{1}[O_{2}][O][M]-k_{2}[O][O_{3}]≈ 0 \) for [O]:

\([O]=\frac{k_{1}[O_{3}][M]}{k^{′}_{1}[O_{2}][M]+ k_{2}[O_{3}]} \) (12.4-9)

We substitute this equation into \(Eq. (12.4-8d):\frac{d[O_{2}]}{dt} = 3k_{2}[O][O_{3}]\) to obtain the rate law

\(\frac{1}{3} \frac{d[O_{2}]}{dt} = k_{2}[O_{3}]\frac{k_{1}k_[O_{3}][M]}{k^{′}_{1}[O_{2}][M]+ k_{2}[O_{3}]} =\frac{k_{1}k_{2}[O_{3}]^{2}[M]}{k^{′}_{1}[O_{2}][M]+ k_{2}[O_{3}]}\) (12.4-10)

Comparison of \(Eq. (12.4-10):\frac{1}{3} \frac{d[O_{2}]}{dt} = k_{2}[O_{3}]\frac{k_{1}k_[O_{3}][M]}{k^{′}_{1}[O_{2}][M]+ k_{2}[O_{3}]} =\frac{k_{1}k_{2}[O_{3}]^{2}[M]}{k^{′}_{1}[O_{2}][M]+ k_{2}[O_{3}]} with Eq. (12.4-7):\Bigl(\frac{1}{3} \Bigr)\Bigl(\frac{3}{2} \Bigr)\frac{d[O_{2}]}{dt} | _{step 2}=\Bigl(\frac{1}{3} \Bigr)\Bigl(\frac{3}{2} \Bigr)2k_{2}\frac{k_{1}[O_{3}]^{2}}{k^{′}_{1}[O_{2}]} =k_{2}K_{1}\frac{[O_{3}]^{2}}{[O_{2}]} \) shows that if

\(k^{′}_{1}[O_{2}][M]\gg k_{2}[O_{3}]\)

then \(Eq. (12.4-10):\frac{1}{3} \frac{d[O_{2}]}{dt} = k_{2}[O_{3}]\frac{k_{1}k_[O_{3}][M]}{k^{′}_{1}[O_{2}][M]+ k_{2}[O_{3}]} =\frac{k_{1}k_{2}[O_{3}]^{2}[M]}{k^{′}_{1}[O_{2}][M]+ k_{2}[O_{3}]}\) becomes the same as \(Eq. (12.4-7):\Bigl(\frac{1}{3} \Bigr)\Bigl(\frac{3}{2} \Bigr)\frac{d[O_{2}]}{dt} | _{step 2}=\Bigl(\frac{1}{3} \Bigr)\Bigl(\frac{3}{2} \Bigr)2k_{2}\frac{k_{1}[O_{3}]^{2}}{k^{′}_{1}[O_{2}]} =k_{2}K_{1}\frac{[O_{3}]^{2}}{[O_{2}]}.\) This corresponds to the assumption on which the rate-limiting step approximation was based, that the forward and reverse rates of the first step are much larger than the rate of the second step.
