Guest Question.

A modified De Sauty bridge is operating at a frequency of 120 Hz and has \(C_{1} = 0.5 \mu F and R_{3} = 12 k\Omega\) . The bridge attains balance when \(R_{1}\) = 130 \(\Omega and R_{4} = 15 k\Omega .\)

Determine:

(a) capacitive impedance

(b) dissipation factor

Admin
Solution

Given that:\( R_{1} = 130 \Omega , C1 = 0.5 \mu F, R_{3} = 12 k\Omega , R_{4} = 15 k\Omega , and f = 120 Hz \)

(a) The capacitance under measurement \(C_{x}\) can be obtained as:

\(C_{x}=\frac{C_{1}R_{3}}{R_{4}}\)

Substituting the required values, we get:

\(C_{x}=\frac{0.5\times 10^{-6}\times 12\times 10^{3}}{15\times 10^{3}}\)

This gives:

\(Cx = 0.4 \mu F\)

The unknown resistance Rs can be found as:

\(R_{3}=\frac{130 \times 15 \times 10^{3}}{12 \times 10^{3}}=162.5 \Omega\)

(b) The dissipation factor D can be calculated as:

\(D=\omega C_{x}R_{s}=2\pi fC_{x}R_{s}\)

On substituting the required values, we get:

\(D = 2\pi × 120 × 0.4 × 10^{-6} × 162.5\)

Or, D = 0.049
