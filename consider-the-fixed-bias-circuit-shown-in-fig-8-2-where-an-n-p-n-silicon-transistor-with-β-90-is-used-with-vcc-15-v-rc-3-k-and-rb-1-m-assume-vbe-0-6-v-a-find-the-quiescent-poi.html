Guest Question.

Consider the fixed bias circuit shown in Fig. 8.2 where an n–p–n silicon transistor with β = 90 is used with VCC = 15 V, Rc = 3 K and Rb = 1 M. Assume VBE = 0.6 V. (a) Find the quiescent point (or Q-point) of the circuit, and (b) Obtain the equation for the ac load line for RL = 1 K.

Admin
Solution

(a) The base current can be obtained by using VCC = 15 V, Rb = 1 M and VBE = 0.6 V in Eq. (8.1):

\(I_{B}=\frac{V_{CC}-V_{BE}}{R_{b}}=\frac{(15-0.6)V}{10^6 \Omega } =14.4 \mu A \)

Since IC ≈ β IB (see Sec. 7.11), the collector current can be given by

\(I_{C}=90\times 14.4\times 10^{-6}A =1.296\) mA

Since the capacitors act as open circuit under dc conditions, the collector-to-emitter voltage VCE can be obtained by applying the KVL at the collector circuit:

VCE= VCC − IC Rc

\(=15-(1.296\times 10^{-3}A)\times (2\times 10^{3}\Omega )=12.408\) V

Thus the operating point, quiescent point, or Q-point in the circuit of Fig. 8.1 is described by a point with IC = 1.296 mA and VCE ≈ 12.41 V on the dc load shown in Fig. 8.2. Note that the dc load line in this case cuts the abscissa and ordinate in Fig. 8.2 at VCE = VCC = 15 V and \(I_{C}=\frac{V_{CC}}{R_{C}}=\frac{15V}{2K}=7.5 \) mA respectively.

(b) The effective collector load \(R^{′}_{L}\) under ac condition is given by

\(R^{′}_{L}=\frac{R_{C}R_{L}}{R_{C}+R_{L}}=\frac{2\times 1}{2+1}=\frac{2}{3} \) K

Since the ac load line must pass through the Q-point (i.e. IC = 1.296 mA and VCE ≈ 12.41 V) and has a slope of \(-\frac{1}{R^{′}_{L}} \) , it can be described by the equation

\(I_{C}-(1.296 mA)=-\frac{V_{CE}-(12.41 V)}{(2/3 K)} \)

Putting IC= 0 in the above equation, we can get

\(V_{CE}=(1.296 mA) \times \left\lgroup\frac{2}{3} K \right\rgroup +12.41 \) V

= 13.27 V

Thus the ac load line cuts the abscissa at VCE = 13.27 V.

Similarly, the value of IC at which the ac load line meets the ordinate is obtained by using VCE = 0 in the load line equation:

\(I_{C}=\frac{12.41 V}{0.67 K}+1.296 mA = 19.91 \) mA

In other words we can say that the ac load line can be obtained by connecting the points (VCE = 0 V, IC = 19.91 mA) and (VCE = 13.27 V, IC = 0 A).
