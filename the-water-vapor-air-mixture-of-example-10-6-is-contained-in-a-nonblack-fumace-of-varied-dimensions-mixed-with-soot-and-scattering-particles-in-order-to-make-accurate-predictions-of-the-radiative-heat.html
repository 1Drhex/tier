Guest Question.

The water vapor-air mixture of is contained in a nonblack fumace of varied dimensions mixed with soot and scattering particles. In order to make accurate predictions of the radiative heat flux possible across the 6.3 μm water vapor band, determine a reordered correlated k-distribution for this mixture.

Admin
Solution

For the water vapor~air mixture of we have \(α = 41.2cm^{-1}/(g/m^{2}), w= 138.15 cm^{-1} , β = 0.211 and ρ_{a} = 54.84 g/m^{3}\). Obtaining a reordered, nondimensional absorption coefficient \(κ^{*} = κ^{*} (g^{*} )\) from equation (10.137) \(\int_{\kappa ^{*}}^{\infty }{}F^{*}(\kappa ^{*}) d\kappa ^{*}=\frac{1}{2} g^{*}=\frac{g}{w}\) [by utilizing the Fortran subroutine wbmkvsg given in Appendix F], we get from equation (10.133) \(A^{*}=\frac{A}{w}=2\int_{0}^{\infty }{}(1-e^{-\kappa ^{*}\tau _{0}}) F^{*}(\kappa ^{*})d\kappa ^{*}=\int_{0}^{\infty }{}(1-e^{-\kappa ^{*}(g^{*})\tau _{0}}) dg^{*}, \tau _{0}=\frac{\alpha }{w}X, \kappa ^{*}=\frac{\kappa w}{\alpha },g^{*}=2\frac{g}{w}.\)

\(\kappa (g)=\kappa (\left|\eta -\eta _{c}\right| )=\frac{\rho _{a}\alpha }{w}\kappa ^{*} \left(\frac{2g}{w} \right)=\frac{\rho _{a}\alpha }{w}\kappa ^{*}\left(\frac{2\left|\eta -\eta _{c}\right| }{w} \right)\) ,

where we have replaced the α in equation (10.133) by \(\rho _{a}\alpha\) in order to obtain a linear, rather than densitybased, absorption coefficient [see equation (10.13) \(\kappa _{\rho v}\equiv \frac{\kappa _{v}}{\rho } , \kappa _{p v}\equiv \frac{\kappa _{v}}{P}\) ], which is generally preferred for spectral calculations.
This equivalent spectral absorption coefficient for the 6.3 μm water vapor band, centered at \(\eta _{c}=1600cm^{-1}\) is shown in Fig. 10-23, and is compared with the spectral narrow band average absorption coefficient, \((S/d)_{\eta}\) for the same conditions. Since, for β= 0.211, there is relatively little line overlap, average values (S/d)_{\eta}[/latex] must come from strongly varying \(\kappa _{\eta }\) with values much larger and much smaller than the average; thus the abundance of large κ (near \(\eta = \eta_{c}\) ) with a quick drop-off away from the band center.
