Guest Question.

The atmospheric pressure is given as \(680 \mathrm{~mm} \mathrm{Hg}\) at a mountain location. Convert this to kilopascals and meters of water. Also, calculate the pressure decrease due to \(500-\mathrm{m}\) elevation increase, starting at \(2000 \mathrm{~m}\) elevation, assuming constant density.

Admin
Solution

Use Eq.2.4.4 and find, using \(S_{\mathrm{Hg}}=13.6\) with Eq.1.5.2,

\( p=\gamma h (2.2.4)\)

\( S=\frac{\rho}{\rho_{\text {water }}}=\frac{\gamma}{\gamma_{\text {water }}} (1.5.2)\)

\(\begin{aligned}p &=\gamma_{\mathrm{Hg}} h \\&=\left(9.81 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{3} \times 13.6\right) \times 0.680 \mathrm{~m}=90.7 \mathrm{kPa}\end{aligned}\)

To convert this to meters of water, we have

\(\begin{aligned}h &=\frac{p}{\gamma_{\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}}} \\&=\frac{90.7}{9.810}=9.25 \mathrm{~m} \text { of water }\end{aligned}\)

To find the pressure decrease, we use Eq. \(2.4 .3\) and find the density in Table B.3:

\(\Delta p=-\gamma \Delta z\) or \( p+\gamma z=\) constant or \( \frac{p}{\gamma}+z=\) constant (2.4.3)

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta p &=-\gamma \Delta z=-\rho g \Delta z \\&=-1.007 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3} \times 9.81 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}^{2} \times 500 \mathrm{~m}=-4940 \mathrm{~Pa}\end{aligned}\)

where we used \(\mathrm{kg}=\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2} / \mathrm{m}\).

Note: Since gravity is known to three significant digits, we express the answer to three significant digits.
