Guest Question.

In general, the angle of incidence, \(\theta_{i}\), and the angle of reflection, \(\theta_{r}\), of an oblique shock reflected from a flat surface are not equal. However, see Refs. 8 and 9, there is an angle \(\theta^{*}\) such that the two angles are equal. Also, if \(\theta_{i}<\theta^{*}, \text { then }\left(\theta_{r}-\delta\right)<\theta_{i}\), and if \(\theta_{i}>\theta^{*}, \text { then }\left(\theta_{r}-\delta\right)>\theta_{i}\). Computationally verify that for \(M_{1}=2,3 \text { and } 4\) at γ = 1.4, the angle of incidence and the angle of reflection of an oblique shock reflected from a flat surface will be equal if

\(\theta_{i}=\theta^{*}=\frac{1}{2} \cos ^{-1}\left(\frac{\gamma-1}{2}\right)\)
Admin

-39.23152 39.23152

γ \(M _{ 1 }\) θ(deg) δ(deg) \(M _{ 2 }\)

-39.23152 39.23152

 

Note the flow in region 3 is just barely supersonic
