Guest Question.

A supply pump for a power plant takes in saturated water at 0.01 MPa and boosts its pressure to 10 MPa. The pump has an adiabatic efficiency of 0.90. Calculate the irreversibility and second-law efficiency.

Admin
Solution

At the inlet and exit states either we are given, or the steam tables in Appendix C provide, the values given in Table 7.3. The actual work is

\( w_{a}=\frac{w_{\text {ideal }}}{\eta}=-\frac{\Delta P}{\eta \rho}=-\frac{10000-10}{(0.9)(1000)}=-11.1 kJ / kg \).

Table 7.3

Exit State 2: Compressed Liquid Phase Inlet State 1: Saturated Liquid Phase
P = 10 MPa T = 45.8°C
P = 0.01 MPa
h = 191.8 kJ/ kg
s = 0.6491 kJ/ kg⋅K

\( \text { Then, by the first law, } h_{2}=-w_{a}+h_{1}=-(-11.1)+191.8=202.9 kJ / k \).

Using this enthalpy, we can interpolate for the entropy from Compressed Liquid Table C.4, and find \( s_{2}=0.651 kJ / kg \cdot K \), As in , the irreversibility is given by

\( i=T_{0}\left(s_{2}-s_{1}\right)=(298)(0.651-0.6491)=0.57 kJ / kg \).

hence

\( w_{ reV }=i+w_{a}=0.57+(-11.1)=-10.5 kJ / kg \quad \eta_{ II }=\frac{w_{ rev }}{w_{a}}=\frac{-10.5}{-11.1}=0.95 \).
