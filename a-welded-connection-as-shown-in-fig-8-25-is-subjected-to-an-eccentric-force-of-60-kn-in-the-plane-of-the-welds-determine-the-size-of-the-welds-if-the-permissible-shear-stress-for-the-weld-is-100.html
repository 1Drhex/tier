Guest Question.

A welded connection, as shown in Fig. 8.25, is subjected to an eccentric force of 60 kN in the plane of the welds. Determine the size of the welds, if the permissible shear stress for the weld is 100 N/mmÂ². Assume static conditions.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } P=60 kN \quad \tau=100 N / mm ^{2} \).

Step I Primary shear stress
There are two horizontal welds \( W_{1} \text { and } W_{2} \) and one vertical weld \( W_{3} \). Refer to Fig. 8.26. By symmetry, the centre of gravity G of the three welds is midway between the two horizontal welds. Therefore,

\( \bar{y}=50 mm \).

Taking moment about the vertical weld and treating the weld as a line,

\( (50+100+50) \bar{x}=50 \times 25+50 \times 25+100 \times 0 \).

\( \bar{x}=12.5 mm \).

The areas of three welds are as follows:

\( A_{1}=(50 t) mm ^{2} \).

\( A_{2}=(50 t) mm ^{2} \).

\( A_{3}=(100 t) mm ^{2} \).

\( A=A_{1}+A_{2}+A_{3}=(200 t) mm ^{2} \).

The primary shear stress in the weld is given by,

\( \tau_{1}=\frac{P}{A}=\frac{60000}{200 t}=\frac{300}{t} N / mm ^{2} \) (i).

Step II Secondary shear stress
As seen in Fig. 8.26, A is the farthest point from the centre of gravity G and its distance r is given by,

\( r=\sqrt{(50-12.5)^{2}+(50)^{2}}=62.5 mm \).

Also,

\( \tan \theta=\frac{50}{(50-12.5)} \quad \text { or } \quad \theta=53.13^{\circ} \).

\( \phi=90-\theta=90-53.13=36.87^{\circ} \).

Therefore, the secondary shear stress is inclined at 36.87Â° with horizontal.

\( e=(50-\bar{x})+150=(50-12.5)+150=187.5 mm \).

\( M=P \times e=\left(60 \times 10^{3}\right)(187.5)=\left(11250 \times 10^{3}\right) N – mm \).

\( G_{1}, G_{2} \text { and } G_{3} \text { are the centres of gravity of the } \) three welds and their distances from the common centre of gravity G are as follows,

\( \overline{G_{1} G}=\overline{G_{2} G}=\sqrt{(25-12.5)^{2}+(50)^{2}}=51.54 mm \).

or

\( r_{1}=r_{2}=51.54 mm \).

\( r_{3}=\overline{G_{3} G}=\bar{x}=12.5 mm \).

From Eq. (8.25),

\( J_{G}=A\left[\frac{l^{2}}{12}+r_{1}^{2}\right] \) (8.25).

\( J_{1}=J_{2}=A_{1}\left[\frac{l_{1}^{2}}{12}+r_{1}^{2}\right] \).

\( =(50 t)\left[\frac{(50)^{2}}{12}+(51.54)^{2}\right] \).

\( =(143235.25 t) mm ^{4} \).

\( J=2 J_{1}+J_{3}=2(143235.25 t)+(98958.33 t) \).

\( =(385428.83 t) mm ^{4} \).

The secondary shear stress at the point A is given by,

\( \tau_{2}=\frac{M r}{J}=\frac{\left(11250 \times 10^{3}\right)(62.5)}{(385428.83 t)} \).

\( =\frac{1824.27}{t} N / mm ^{2} \) (ii).

Step III Resultant shear stress
The secondary shear stress is inclined at 36.87Â° with the horizontal. It is resolved into vertical and horizontal components as shown in Fig. 8.27.

\( \text { Vertical component }=\tau_{2} \sin \phi \).

\( =\frac{1824.27}{t} \sin (36.87)=\frac{1094.56}{t} N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \text { Horizontal component }=\tau_{2} \cos \phi \).

\( =\frac{1824.27}{t} \cos (36.87)=\frac{1459.41}{t} N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \text { The primary shear stress }\left(\frac{300}{t}\right) \text { is vertically } \) upward. Therefore, the total vertical component is given by,

\( \left(\frac{1094.56}{t}+\frac{300}{t}\right) \text { or }\left(\frac{1394.56}{t}\right) N / mm ^{2} \).

The resultant shear stress is given by,

\( \tau=\sqrt{\left(\frac{1394.56}{t}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{1459.41}{t}\right)^{2}} \).

\( =\frac{2018.58}{t} N / mm ^{2} \).

Step IV Size of weld
The permissible shear stress for the weld material is 100 N/mmÂ². Therefore

\( 100=\frac{2018.58}{t} \text { or } t=20.19 mm \).

\( \text { and } \quad h=\frac{t}{0.707}=\frac{20.19}{0.707}=28.56 mm \).
