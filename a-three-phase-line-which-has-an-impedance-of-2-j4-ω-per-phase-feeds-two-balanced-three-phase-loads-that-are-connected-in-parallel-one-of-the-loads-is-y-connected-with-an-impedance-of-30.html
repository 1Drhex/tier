Guest Question.

A three-phase line, which has an impedance of (2 + j4) Ω per phase, feeds two balanced three-phase loads that are connected in parallel. One of the loads is Y-connected with an impedance of (30 + j40) Ω per phase, and the other is Δ-connected with an impedance of (60 – j45) Ω per phase. The line is energized at the sending end from a 60-Hz, three-phase, balanced voltage source of \(120 \sqrt{3} V\) (rms, line-to-line). Determine (a) the current, real power, and reactive power delivered by the sending-end source; (b) the line-to-line voltage at the load; (c) the current per phase in each load; and (d) the total three-phase real and reactive powers absorbed by each load and by the line. Check that the total three-phase complex power delivered by the source equals the total three-phase power absorbed by the line and loads.

Admin
Solution

(a) Transforming the ∆-connected load into an equivalent Y, the impedance per phase of the equivalent Y is

\(\bar{Z}_{2}=\frac{60-j 45}{3}=(20-j 15) \Omega\)

With the phase voltage \(V_{1}=\frac{120 \sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}}=120 V\) taken as a reference, the per-phase equivalent circuit is shown below:

Total impedance viewed from the input terminals is

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{Z}=2+j 4+\frac{(30+j 40)(20-j 15)}{(30+j 40)+(20-j 15)}=2+j 4+22-j 4=24 \Omega \\&\bar{I}=\frac{\bar{V}_{1}}{\bar{Z}}=\frac{120 \angle 0^{\circ}}{24}=5 \angle 0^{\circ} A\end{aligned}\)

The three-phase complex power supplied \(=\bar{S}=3 \bar{V}_{1} \bar{I}^{*}=1800 W\)

P =1800 W and Q = 0 VAR delivered by the sending-end source

 

(b) Phase voltage at load terminals

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{V}_{2} &=120 \angle 0^{\circ}-(2+j 4)\left(5 \angle 0^{\circ}\right) \\&=110-j 20=111.8 \angle-10.3^{\circ} V\end{aligned}\)

The line voltage magnitude at the load terminal is

\(\left(V_{ LOAD }\right)_{L-L}=\sqrt{3} 111.8=193.64 V\)

 

(c) The current per phase in the Y-connected load and in the equiv.Y of the ∆-load:

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{I}_{1}=\frac{\bar{V}_{2}}{\bar{Z}_{1}}=1-j 2=2.236 \angle-63.4^{\circ} A \\&\bar{I}_{2}=\frac{\bar{V}_{2}}{\bar{Z}_{2}}=4+j 2=4.472 \angle 26.56^{\circ} A\end{aligned}\)

The phase current magnitude in the original ∆-connected load

\(\left(I_{p h}\right)_{\Delta}=\frac{I_{2}}{\sqrt{3}}=\frac{4.472}{\sqrt{3}}=2.582 A\)

 

(d) The three-phase complex power absorbed by each load is

\(\begin{aligned}&\bar{S}_{1}=3 \bar{V}_{2} \bar{I}_{1}^{*}=430 W +j 600 VAR \\&\bar{S}_{2}=3 \bar{V}_{2} \bar{I}_{2}^{*}=1200 W -j 900 VAR\end{aligned}\)

The three-phase complex power absorbed by the line is

\(\bar{S}_{L}=3\left(R_{L}+j X_{L}\right) I^{2}=3(2+j 4)(5)^{2}=150 W +j 300 VAR\)

The sum of load powers and line losses is equal to the power delivered from the supply:

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{S}_{1}+\bar{S}_{2}+\bar{S}_{L} &=(450+j 600)+(1200-j 900)+(150+j 300) \\&=1800 W +j 0 VAR\end{aligned}\)
