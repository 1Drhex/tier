Guest Question.

On a disk ① (moment of inertia \(\Theta _{1}\) ), which rotates with an angular velocity \(\omega _{1} \) , a second disk ② (mass \(m_{2}\) , moment of inertia \(\Theta _{2}\) ) rotates with \(\omega _{2}\) about the shaft \(B\) (= center of mass of ②). When ② slides down along \(B\) and touches ①, both disks rub on each other such that ② comes to rest relatively to disk ① after time \(t_{0}\) .

Determine the common angular velocity \(\omega _{0}\) after time \(t_{0}\) .

Admin
Solution

We separate the systen and formulate the principles of angular impulse and momentum for ① (with respect to fixed axis \(A\) ) and ② (with respect to its center of mass \(B\) ):

\(\overset{\curvearrowright }{A}: \left(\Theta _{1}+m_{2}a^{2}\right)\left[\omega _{0}-\omega _{1} \right]=-\widehat{M} \) ,

\(\overset{\curvearrowright }{B}: \Theta _{2}\left[\omega _{0}-\left(-\omega _{2} \right) \right]=+\widehat{M} \) ,

where

\(\widehat{M}=\int\limits_{0}^{t_{0} }{M\left(\bar{t} \right)d\bar{t} } \) .

Notice that the mass m2 of disk ② is considered in the moment of inertia of ①.

Eliminating \(\widehat{M}\) leads to

\(\underline{\underline{\omega _{0}=\frac{\left(\Theta _{1} +m_{2}a^{2} \right)\omega _{1}-\Theta _{2} \omega _{2} }{\Theta _{1}+\Theta _{2} +m_{2}a^{2} } } } \) .

The problem can be solved easier by applying the principle of angular momentum to the total system. Because there acts no \(external moment\) , conservation of angular momentum about the fixed axis \(A\) yields

\(\overset{\curvearrowright }{A} :\left(\Theta _{1} +m_{2}a^{2} \right)\omega _{1}-\Theta _{2} \omega _{2}=\left[\Theta _{1} +\left(\Theta _{2}+m_{2}a^{2} \right) \right]\omega _{0} \) ,

i.e. we find the same result as above.

Remark: For \(\Theta _{2}\omega _{2} =\left(\Theta _{1}+m_{2}a^{2} \right)\omega _{1} \) , the system comes to rest.
