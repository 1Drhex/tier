Guest Question.

The energy equation for heat conduction in a circular disc of radius R is given by (an axisymmetric, one-dimensional problem)
\(-\frac{1}{r}\left ( r\frac{\mathrm{d} \theta }{\mathrm{d} x} \right )=2\), 0 <r<R (1)
and the boundary conditions are
\(r\frac{\mathrm{d} \theta }{\mathrm{d} x}=0\) at r=0 and \(r\frac{\mathrm{d} \theta }{\mathrm{d} x}+\theta =1\) at r=1 (2)
where \(\theta\) is the non-dimensional temperature, r is the radial coordinate, and R = 1 is the radius of the disc. Use two linear finite elements of equal length to determine the unknown temperatures. It is sufficient to give the condensed equations for the unknown nodal temperatures.

Admin
Solution

The finite element model of the equation is given by

\([K^{e}]\left \{ \theta ^{e} \right \}=\left \{ f^{e} \right \}+\left \{ Q^{e} \right \}\) (3)

where

\(K_{ij}^{e}=\int_{r_{a}}^{r_{b}}r\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{i}}{\mathrm{d} r}\frac{\psi _{j}}{\mathrm{d} r}dr\) , \(f_{i}^{e}=\int_{r_{a}}^{r_{b}}2r\psi _{i}dr\) (4)

For the choice of linear interpolation, we note that (see the formula sheet for the interpolation functions)

\(\int_{r_{a}}^{r_{b}}r dr=\frac{r_{b}^{2}-r_{a}^{2}}{2}=\frac{(r_{b}+r_{a})h_{e}}{2}\) , \(\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{1}}{\mathrm{d} r}=-\frac{1}{h_{e}}\) , \(\frac{\mathrm{d} \psi _{2}}{\mathrm{d} r}=\frac{1}{h_{e}}\)

\(f_{1}^{e}=2\int_{0}^{h_{e}}(r_{a}+\bar{r})\left ( 1-\frac{\bar{r}}{h_{e}} \right )d\bar{r}\)

= \(2\left ( r_{a}h_{e}+\frac{h_{e}^{2}}{2}-r_{a}\frac{h_{e}}{2}-\frac{h_{e}^{2}}{3} \right )=\frac{h_{e}}{3}(3r_{a}+h_{e})\)

\(f_{2}^{e}=2\int_{0}^{h_{e}}(r_{a}+\bar{r})\frac{\bar{r}}{h_{e}}d\bar{r}\) = \(2\left ( r_{a}\frac{h_{e}}{2}+\frac{h_{e}^{2}}{3} \right )=\frac{h_{e}}{2}(3r_{a}+2h_{e})\)

Hence, we have (for any element)

\([K^{e}]=\frac{r_{b}+r_{a}}{2h_{e}}\begin{bmatrix}1 &-1 \\ -1 &1 \end{bmatrix}\) , \(\left \{ f^{e} \right \}=\frac{h_{e}}{3}\begin{Bmatrix}3r_{a}+h_{e}\\ 3r_{a}+2h_{e}\end{Bmatrix}\) (5)

Thus element 1 and 2 coefficient matrices are given by (h = 1/2)

\([K^{(1)}]=\begin{bmatrix}0.5 &-0.5 \\ -0.5 &0.5 \end{bmatrix}\) , \(\left \{ f^{(1)} \right \}=\frac{1}{6}\begin{Bmatrix}0.5\\ 1.0\end{Bmatrix}\)

\([K^{(2)}]=\begin{bmatrix}1.5 &-1.5 \\ -1.5 &1.5 \end{bmatrix}\) , \(\left \{ f^{(2)} \right \}=\frac{1}{6}\begin{Bmatrix}2.0\\ 2.5\end{Bmatrix}\) (6)

The assembled equations are given by

\(\begin{bmatrix}0.5 &-0.5 & 0\\ -0.5 &0.5+1.5 &-1.5 \\ 0 &-1.5 &1.5 \end{bmatrix}\begin{Bmatrix}U_{1}\\ U_{2}\\ U_{3}\end{Bmatrix}=\begin{Bmatrix}Q_{1}^{1}\\ Q_{2}^{1}+Q_{1}^{2}\\ Q_{2}^{2}\end{Bmatrix}+\frac{1}{6}\begin{Bmatrix}0.5\\ 1.0+2.0\\ 2.5\end{Bmatrix}\) (7)

The boundary conditions are: \(Q_{1}^{1}\) = 0 , \(Q_{2}^{1}+Q_{1}^{2}\) = 0 , and \(Q_{2}^{2}+U_{3}=1\). The final equations for the unknown temperatures (i.e., the condensed equations) are

\(\begin{bmatrix}0.5 &-0.5 &0 \\ -0.5 &2.0 &-1.5 \\ 0 &-1.5 &2.5 \end{bmatrix}\begin{Bmatrix}U_{1}\\ U_{2}\\ U_{3}\end{Bmatrix}=\frac{1}{6}\begin{Bmatrix}0.5\\ 3.0\\ 8.5\end{Bmatrix}\) (8)

which is to be solved for the three temperatures.
