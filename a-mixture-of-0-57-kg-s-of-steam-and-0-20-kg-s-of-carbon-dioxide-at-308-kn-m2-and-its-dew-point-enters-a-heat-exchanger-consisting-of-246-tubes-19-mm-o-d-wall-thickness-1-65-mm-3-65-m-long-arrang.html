Guest Question.

A mixture of 0.57 kg/s of steam and 0.20 kg/s of carbon dioxide at \(308 kN/m^{2}\) and its dew point enters a heat exchanger consisting of 246 tubes, 19 mm o.d., wall thickness 1.65 mm, 3.65 m long, arranged in four passes on 25 mm square pitch in a 0.54 m diameter shell and leaves at 322 K. Condensation is effected by cooling water entering and leaving the unit at 300 and 319 K respectively. If the diffusivity of steam-carbon dioxide mixtures is \(1.1×10^{-5} m^{2}/s \) and the group \( (μ/ρD)^{0.67}\) may be taken to be constant at 0.62, estimate the overall coefficient of heat transfer and the dirt factor for the condenser.

Admin
Solution

In the steam entering the condenser, there is
\(\frac{0.57}{18} =0.032kmol \space water\)
\(and \space \frac{0.20}{44}=0.0045kmol\space CO_{2} \)
total =0.0365kmol.
Hence the partial pressure of water=\((308\times 0.032/0.0365)=270kN/m^{2}\) and from Table 11A, the dew point=404 K.
Mean molecular weight of the mixture=(0.57 + 0.20)/0.0365=21.1 kg/kmol.
At the inlet:
vapour pressure of water =\( 270kN/m^{2}\)
inert pressure =\((308-270)=38kN/m^{2}\)
total=\(308kN/m^{2}\)
At the outlet:
partial pressure of water at \(322K = 11.7kN/m^{2}\)
inert pressure=\((308-11.7)=296.3kN/m^{2}\)
total=\(308kN/m^{2}\)
\(\therefore steam \space at \space the \space outlet =\frac{0.0045\times 11.7}{296.3}=0.000178kmol \)
and:
steam condensed =( 0.032-0.000178)= 0.03182kmol.
The heat load is now estimated at each interval between the temperatures 404, 401, 397, 380,339, and 322 K.
For the interval 404–401 K
From Table 11A, the partial pressure of steam at 401 K=\(252.2kN/m^{2}\) and
hence the partial pressure of \(CO_{2}=(308-252.2)=55.8kN/m^{2}\).
Steam remaining=\((0.0045×252.2/55.8)=0.0203kmol.\)
\(\therefore\) Steam condensed=(0.032-0.0203 )=0.0117kmol
Heat of condensation= (0.0117×18)(2180+1.93(404-401))=466kW
Heat from uncondensed steam= (0.0203×18×1.93(404-401))=1.9kW
Heat from carbon dioxide= (0.020×0.92(404-401))=0.5kW
and the total for the interval=468.4 kW

Temperature of the gas T=404 K, partial pressure of steam \(P_{g}=270kN/m^{2}\),partial pressure of the inert \(P_{s}=38kN/m^{2}\), water temperature \(T_{w}=319K\), and ΔT=(404-319)=85 K.

If no condensation takes place, the logarithmic mean temperature difference is 46.6 K. In
practice the value is (1407.3/18.98)=74.2 K.
Assuming no scale resistance, the overall coefficient is \( \frac{1407.3}{34.8\times 74.2} = 0.545kW/m^{2}k \)
The available surface area on the outside of the tubes \( = 0.060m^{2} /m \) or
\( (264\times 3.65\times 0.060) = 53.9m^{2} \)
The actual coefficient is therefore \( \frac{1407.3}{53.9\times 74.2} = 0.352kW/m^{2}k \)
And the dirt factor is \( \frac{(0.545-0.352)}{(0.545\times 0.352)} = 1.01m^{2}K/kW \)
As shown in Figure. 1.61, the clean coefficient varies from \(3.64kW/m^{2}K\) at the inlet to \(0.092kW/m^{2}K\) at the outlet.
