we find with \((\eta _{0}/T)_{4.3}=2143 cm^{-1}/800K=2.679 cm^{-1}/K and (\eta _{0}/T)_{2.35}=4260 cm^{-1}/800K = 5.325 cm^{-1}/K\) ,

\(\left(\frac{\alpha E_{b\eta 0}}{T^{3}} \right)_{4.7}/\left(\frac{\alpha E_{b\eta 0}}{T^{3}}\right)_{2.35}=\frac{20.9\times 1.5563}{0.14\times 0.2659} = 874\).

Therefore, since the 4.7 μm band is much stronger (\(\alpha _{4.7}/\alpha _{2.35}\simeq 150\)) and located in a more important part of the spectrum (\(E_{b\eta 4.7}/E_{b\eta 2.35}\simeq 6\)) , the influence of the 2.35 μm band can be neglected. We first need to calculate the band absorptance for the 4.7 μm band. Since values in Table 10.3 are based on the mass absorption coefficient, we need to calculate the density of the CO from the ideal gas law, as we did in :

\(\rho _{a}=\frac{Mp_{a}}{R_{u}T} =\frac{28g/mol\times 1atm}{8.3145J/mol K\times 800K} \frac{1.0132\times 10^{5}J/m^{3}}{1atm}= 426.6 g / m^{3}\)

and \(X= \rho _{a}s= 85.32 g/m^{2}\) . We also find from Table 10.3 n = 0.8 and b = l.l , so that \(P_{e}=1.1^{0.8}=1.079 and \gamma _{0}P_{e}= 0.075\times 1.079=0.081.\) Further we find \(\alpha =20.9 cm^{-1}/(g/m^{2}), w= 25.5\sqrt{800/100}=72.125 cm^{-1} , and \tau _{0}=\alpha X/w = 20.9\times 85.32/72.125=24.72\) . From Fig. 10-20 or subroutine wbmco we obtain \(\gamma /\gamma _{0}= 0.529 and \beta =(\gamma /\gamma _{0})\gamma _{0}P_{e}=0.529\times 0.081=0.043\) . Thus, \(\tau _{0}>1/\beta\) and we are in the logarithmic regime, and

\(A^{*}=\ln (\tau _{0}\beta )+2-\beta =2.018 and A=145.6 cm^{-1}\)

Sticking this into equation (10.138) \(\epsilon \equiv \frac{\int_{0}^{\infty }{}I_{b\eta }\epsilon _{\eta }d\eta }{\int_{0}^{\infty }{}I_{b\eta }d\eta } =\frac{\int_{0}^{\infty }{}I_{b\eta (1-e^{-\kappa _{0}X})d\eta } }{\int_{0}^{\infty }{}I_{b\eta }d\eta } =\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}{}\left(\frac{\pi I_{b\eta 0}}{\sigma T^{4}} \right) _{i}\int_{\triangle \eta b and }^{}{} (1e^{-\kappa _{0}X})d\eta =\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}{} \left(\frac{\pi I_{b\eta 0}}{\sigma T^{4}} \right) _{i} A_{i}\) ,

\(\epsilon _{CO}(800K,1 atm)= \left(\frac{\pi I_{b\eta 0}}{\sigma T^{4}} \right) _{\eta _{0}=2143 cm^{-1}}\times A=\left(\frac{E_{b\eta 0}}{T^{3}} \right)_{\eta _{0}=2143 cm^{-1}}\times \frac{A}{\sigma T}\)

\(=1.5563\times 10^{-8}\frac{W}{m^{2}cm^{-1}K^{3}}\times \frac{145.6 cm^{-1}}{5.670\times 10^{-8}\times 800W/m^{2}K^{3}}\)

=0.0500.
