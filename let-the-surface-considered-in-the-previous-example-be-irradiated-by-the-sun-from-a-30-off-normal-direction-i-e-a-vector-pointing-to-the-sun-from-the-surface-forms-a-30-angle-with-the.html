Guest Question.

Let the surface considered in be irradiated by the sun from a \(30^{\circ }\) off-normal direction (i.e., a vector pointing to the sun from the surface forms a \(30^{\circ }\) angle with the outward surface normal). Determine the relevant surface ahsorptance.

Admin
Solution

Since the sun irradiates the surface from only one direction, but over the entire spectrum, we need to find the total, directional ahsorptance. From , with \(\theta _{i} =30^{\circ }\) we have

\(\alpha ^{\prime }_{\lambda } \left(\lambda ,\theta _{i} =\frac{\pi }{6} \right) =\begin{Bmatrix} 0.45\sqrt{3}, & 0 \lt \lambda \lt 2\mu m \\ 0.3, & 2\mu m\lt \lambda \lt \infty \end{Bmatrix}\)

Since we know that the sun behaves like a blackbody at a temperature of \(T_{sun} =5777K\) , , we also know the spectral behavior of the sunshine falHng onto our surface, or

\(I_{\lambda } (\lambda , \theta _{i})=C I_{b \lambda }( T_{sun},\lambda )\),

where C is a proportionality constant independent of wavelength.’ Substituting this into equation

\(\alpha ^{\prime } (r,\widehat{s_{i} } )\equiv \frac{\int_{0}^{\infty }{\alpha ^{\prime }_{\lambda }(T,\lambda ,\widehat{s_{i} } )I_{\lambda } }(r,\widehat{s_{i} } )d\lambda }{\int_{0}^{\infty }I_{\lambda } (r,\widehat{s_{i} } )d\lambda }\) ,

leads to

\(\alpha ^{\prime } \left(\theta _{i} =\frac{\pi }{6} \right) =\frac{\int_{0}^{\infty }\epsilon ^{\prime }_{\lambda } (\lambda ,\theta _{i})I_{b\lambda } (T_{sun} ,\lambda )d\lambda }{\int_{0}^{\infty } I_{b\lambda } (T_{sun} ,\lambda ) d\lambda }=\frac{1}{n^{2} \sigma T^{4}_{sun} } \left[0.45\sqrt{3} \int_{0}^{2\mu m}{E_{b\lambda } (T_{sun},\lambda ) d\lambda +0.3\int_{2\mu m}^{\infty }{E_{b\lambda } (T_{sun},\lambda ) d\lambda} } \right]\)

\(=0.3+(0.45\sqrt{3} -0.3)f(1\times 2\times 5777)=0.3+(0.779-0.3)\times 0.93962=0.750\) .

In contrast to we find that at a temperature of 5777 K the spectrum above \(2 \mu m\) is of very little importance, and the surface is again essentially gray.

We realize from this example that (i) if a surface is irradiated from a gray source at temperature \(T_{ource}\) and (ii) if the spectral, directional emittance of the surface is independent of temperature (as it is for most surfaces with good degree of accuracy), then the total ahsorptance is equal to its total emittance evaluated at the source temperature, or

\(α=\in (T_{ource)}\)

This relation holds on a directional basis, and also for hemispherical values if the irradiation is diffuse.
