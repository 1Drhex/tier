Guest Question.

Combine the effects introduced in Sections 3.7.3 and 3.7.4.

Admin
Solution

A schematic of the system is given in Figure 3.43. A free-body diagram for the derivation of the equation of motion would be a combination of free-body diagrams from the two component problems. Linearity permits the superposition of the loadings and responses of the system.

The governing equation is then

\(m \ddot{x}+c \dot{x}+k x=m_{0} e \omega_{r}^{2} \sin \omega_{r} t+c \dot{y}+k y\)

As before, assuming the harmonic base motion to be

\(y(t)=Y \sin \omega_{b} t\), and dividing through by \(m\) leads to

\(\begin{aligned}&\ddot{x}+2 \zeta \omega_{n} \dot{x}+\omega_{n}^{2} x \\&=\frac{m_{0}}{m} e \omega_{r}^{2} \sin \omega_{r} t+2 \zeta \omega_{n} \omega_{b} Y \cos \omega_{b} t \\&\quad+\omega_{n}^{2} Y \sin \omega_{b} t\end{aligned}\)

Assuming a response that includes both harmonic components
and then substituting the assumed response into the governing equation allows us to find the coefficients. A simpler way is to solve for the response to each of the inputs on the right-hand side of Equation 3.54 and then add all three components for the complete solution,

\(x_{3}(t)=x_{1}(t)+x_{2}(t)+x_{3}(t)\), where \(x_{1}(t), x_{2}(t)\) and \(x_{3}(t)\) are governed by

\(\begin{aligned}&\ddot{x}_{1}+2 \zeta \omega_{n} \dot{x}_{1}+\omega_{n}^{2} x_{1}=\frac{m_{0}}{m} e \omega_{r}^{2} \sin \omega_{r} t \\&\ddot{x}_{2}+2 \zeta \omega_{n} \dot{x}_{2}+\omega_{n}^{2} x_{2}=2 \zeta \omega_{n} \omega_{b} Y \cos \omega_{b} t \\&\ddot{x}_{3}+2 \zeta \omega_{n} \dot{x}_{3}+\omega_{n}^{2} x_{3}=\omega_{n}^{2} Y \sin \omega_{b} t\end{aligned}\)

Each of these equations is of the form of Equation 3.29
with the solution given by Equation 3.32. Several resonances are possible. If any of the driving frequencies, \(\omega_{r}\) or \(\omega_{b}\), are at or near the natural frequency \(\omega_{n}\), then a significant magnification of the response amplitude may occur. The study of such possible behavior is an important part of any preliminary analysis and design.
