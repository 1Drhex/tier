Guest Question.

Determine the vertical displacement of joint 6 of symmetrical truss shown in Fig. 6.11. Axial rigidity for diagonal and vertical elements is EA and for lower and top chords is 2EA.

Admin
Solution

All elements of the given structure are subjected to axial loads only, so for required displacement the following formula should be applied:

\(y_6 =\sum{\frac{1}{EA}N_p\bar{N}_kl, }\) (a)

where \(N_p\) and \(\bar{N}_k\) are internal forces in actual and unit state, respectively.

Actual state. Reaction of supports \(R_A = R_B = P. As\) usual, the axial forces in the members are taken positive in tension. Equilibrium equations lead to the following internal forces:

\(O_{35} \rightarrow \sum{M_6^{left}}=0 : – R_A.2d +Pd -O_{35}h = 0 \rightarrow O_{35}= -\frac{4}{3}P\)

 

\(U_{24} \rightarrow \sum{M_3^{left}}=0 : – R_A.d +U_{24}h = 0 \rightarrow U_{24}= -\frac{4}{3}P\) (b)

 

\(D_{23} \rightarrow \sum{Y^{left}}=0 : D_{23}sin\alpha +R_{A}= 0 \rightarrow D_{23}= -\frac{5}{3}P\)

 

\(D_{36} \rightarrow \sum{Y^{left}}=0 : R_{A} -P -D_{36}sin\alpha= 0 \rightarrow D_{36}=0\)

Unit state. Unit vertical load P = 1 is applied at the point where vertical displacement is to be determined. Reaction of supports \(\bar{R}_A =\bar{R}_B = 0.5\) . Internal forces in unit state are:

\(\bar{O} _{35} \rightarrow \sum{M_6^{left}}=0 : – \bar{R} _{A}.2d -\bar{O} _{35}h= 0 \rightarrow \bar{O} _{35}=- \frac{4}{3}\)

 

\(\bar{U} _{24} \rightarrow \sum{M_3^{left}}=0 : – \bar{R} _{A}d +\bar{U} _{24}h= 0 \rightarrow \bar{U} _{24}= \frac{2}{3}\)

 

\(\bar{D} _{23} \rightarrow \sum{Y^{left}}=0 : \bar{D} _{23}sin\alpha +\bar{R} _{A}= 0 \rightarrow \bar{D} _{23}= -\frac{5}{6}\) (c)

 

\(\bar{D} _{36} \rightarrow \sum{Y^{left}}=0 : \bar{R} _{A}-\bar{D}_{36} sin\alpha= 0 \rightarrow \bar{D} _{36}= \frac{5}{6} \)

Table 6.3 contains the data which is necessary for computation of displacement according to (a). They are the length of the elements, their axial rigidity, and internal
forces in all members in actual and unit states. The last column contains application of (a) for each member separately.

Summation of the last column of this table leads to the required displacement of joint 6

\(y_6 =\sum{\frac{N_p\bar{N}l }{EA} }=\frac{506P}{18EA},\) (d)

Note. For calculation of displacement of all joints of the bottom (upper) chord of the truss, the different unit states should be considered. For each unit state, the procedure presented in Table 6.3 should be repeated. Thus, the Maxwell–Mohr integral requires calculation of the internal forces in all members of the truss for each unit state. This procedure is cumbersome and will be extremely simplified using the elastic load method.
