Guest Question.

A planetary gear train is illustrated in Figure 10.25. The carrier (link 2) serves as the input to the train. The ring gear (gear 1) is the fixed gear and has 120 teeth. The planet gear (gear 4) has 40 teeth. The sun gear (gear 3) serves as the output from the train and has 30 teeth. Determine the rotational velocity of all members of this gear train when the input shaft rotates at 1200 rpm clockwise. 1.Complete Steps 1–3 2.Compute Velocities of All Links.

Admin
Solution

1: The first step is to temporarily fix the carrier. Then compute the motions of all gears as the previously fixed gear rotates one revolution.

The second step rotates all links –1 revolution. This returns the ring gear to its original position, yielding a net movement of zero.

The two steps are summarized in Table 10.11.

TABLE 10.11 Solution Steps
Link Sun (Gear 3) Planet (Gear4) Ring (Gear1) Carrier (Gear2)
Step 1:

Rotate with fixed carrier

 \((\frac{120}{40} )(\frac{-40}{30} )=-4.0\) \((\frac{120}{40} )=3.0\) +1 0
Step 2:

Rotate all links

 -1 -1 -1 -1
Step 3:

Total rotations

 -5.0 +2.0 0 -1

2: The velocities can be determined by using the ratios of angular displacements.

\(\omega _{ring}=(\frac{\Delta \theta _{ring}}{\Delta \theta _{carrier}} )\omega _{carrier}=(\frac{0}{-1} )(0rpm)=0\ rpm\)

\(\omega _{carrier}=1200\ rpm, clockwise\)

\(\omega _{planet}=(\frac{+2.0}{-1} )\omega _{carrier}=(-2.0)(1200\ rpm)=-2400\ rpm=2400\ rpm,counterclockwise\)

\(\omega _{sun}=(\frac{-5.0}{-1} )\omega _{carrier}=(+5.0)(1200\ rpm)=+6000\ rpm=6000\ rpm,cw\)
