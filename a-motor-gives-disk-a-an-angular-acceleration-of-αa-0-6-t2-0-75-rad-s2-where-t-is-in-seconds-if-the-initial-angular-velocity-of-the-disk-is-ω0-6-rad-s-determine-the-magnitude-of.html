Guest Question.

A motor gives disk A an angular acceleration of \( α_A = (0.6 t^2 + 0.75) \) rad/s², where t is in seconds. If the initial angular velocity of the disk is \( ω_0 = 6 \) rad/s, determine the magnitude of the velocity and acceleration of block B when t = 2 seconds.

Admin
Solution

At t = 2 seconds.

α = 0.6 t² + 0.75 = 0.6 × 2² + 0.75 .

α = 3.15 rad/s².

\( a_B = r_α = 0.15 × 3.15 \).

\( a_B = 0.4725 \) m/s².

α = 0.6 t² + 0.75.

\( \frac{d ω}{d t} = 0.6 t² + 0.75 \).

d ω = (0.6 t² + 0.75 ) d t.

Integrating both sides, we have

\( \int{d \omega } = \int{(0.6 t² + 0.75 )}d t \).

\( ω = \frac{0.6 t^3}{3} + 0.75 t + c_1 \).

At t = 0, ω = 6 r/s ∴ \( c_1 = 6 \).

\( ω = 0.2 t^3 + 0.75 × t + 6 \).

At t = 2 seconds.

\( ω = 0.2 × 2^3 + 0.75 × 2 + 6 = 9.1 \) rad/s Ans.

ν = r ω.

\( ν_B = 0.15 × 9.1 = 1.365 \) m/s Ans.
