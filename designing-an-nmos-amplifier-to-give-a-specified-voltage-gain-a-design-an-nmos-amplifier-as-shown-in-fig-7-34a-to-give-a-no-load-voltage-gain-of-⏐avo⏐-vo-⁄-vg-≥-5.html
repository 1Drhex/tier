Guest Question.

Designing an NMOS amplifier to give a specified voltage gain

(a) Design an NMOS amplifier as shown in Fig. 7.34(a) to give a no-load voltage gain of \( \left|A_{ vo }\right|=v_{ o } / v_{ g } \geq 5 \). The DC supply voltage is \( V_{ DD }=15 V \). The NMOS parameters are \( V_{ t }=1 V , K_{ n }=3.25 mA / V ^{2} \), \( K_{ p }=2 K_{ n }=6.5 mA / V ^{2} \text { for } W=L=1 \mu m , I_{ D (\max )}=10 mA \text {, and }\left|V_{ M }\right|=1 / \lambda=100 V \).

(b) Use PSpice/SPICE to verify your results in part (a).

Admin
Solution

(a) Step 1. Design the biasing circuit. The results of give \( R_{ D }=1.5 k \Omega, R_{ SR }=1.5 k \Omega, R_{1}=650 k \Omega \), and \( R_{2}=100 k \Omega \) .
Step 2. Find the small-signal parameters of the transistor. The results of give \( g_{ m }=6.583 mA / V \) and \( r_{ o }=30 k \Omega \) .
Step 3. Find the values of \( C_{1}, C_{2}, C_{ S }, R_{ SR 1}, \text { and } R_{ SR 2} \). Let us choose \( C_{1}=C_{2}=C_{ S }=10 \mu F \). The worstcase maximum possible gain that we can obtain from the transistor operating at \( i_{ D }=6 mA \) can be found from Eq. (7.71):

 

\( A_{ vo (\max )}=-\frac{-\mu_{ g } R_{ D }}{R_{ D }+r_{ o }}=\frac{-g_{ m } r_{ o } R_{ D }}{R_{ D }+r_{ o }}=\frac{-g_{ m } R_{ D }}{1+R_{ D } / r_{ o }} \) (7.71)

 

\( \left|A_{ vo ( max )}\right|=\frac{g_{ m } R_{ D }}{1+R_{ D } / r_{ o }}=\frac{1.5 k \times 6.583 m }{1+1.5 k / 30 k }=9.404 V / V \)

 

The desired gain is less than the maximum possible value, and we can proceed with the design. Otherwise we would need to choose another transistor with a higher value of \( g_{ m } \). The value of unbypassed emitter resistance \( R_{ SR 1} \) in Fig. 7.34(a) can be found from Eq. (7.70). That is,

 

\( A_{ vo }=\frac{v_{ o }}{v_{ g }}=\frac{-\mu_{ g } R_{ D }}{R_{ D }+r_{ o }+\left(1+\mu_{ g }\right) R_{ SR 1}}=-\frac{g_{ m } r_{0} R_{ D }}{R_{ D }+r_{ o }+\left(1+g_{ m } r_{0}\right) R_{ SR 1}} \) (7.70)

 

\( R_{ D }+r_{ o }+\left(1+\mu_{ g }\right) R_{ SR 1}=\frac{\mu_{ g } R_{ D }}{\left|A_{ vo }\right|} \) (7.72)

 

which, for \( \left|A_{ vo }\right|=5, R_{ D }=1.5 k \Omega, r_{ o }=30 k \Omega, \text { and } \mu_{ g }=r_{ o } g_{ m }=197.48 V / V , \text { gives } R_{ SR1 }=139.79 \Omega \) and \( R_{ SR 1}=R_{ SR }-R_{ SR 1}=1.5 k -130.79=1.3 k \Omega .\)

(b) The PSpice schematic is shown in Fig. 7.37. The plot of the output voltage is shown in Fig. 7.38, which gives a voltage gain of -5.11 V/V. This is close to the calculated value of -5, but it is much too low in comparison to -100 with an NMOS amplifier with current source biasing (in ). Note that there is a phase shift of 180°.
