Guest Question.

A plane homogeneous wave propagates through a perfect dielectric medium (n = 2) in the direction of \(\widehat{S} = 0.8\hat{\imath} +0.6\widehat{k}\) with a wavenumber of \(\eta _{0} =2500 cm^{-1}\) and an electric field amplitude vector of \(E_{0} =E_{0} [(6+3i)\hat{\imath} +(2-5i)\hat{\jmath} -(8+4 i)\widehat{k} ]/\sqrt{154}\) , where \(E_{0} =600 N/C\) , and the \(\hat{\imath} , \hat{\jmath}\) and\(\widehat{k}\) are unit vectors in the x-, y- and z-directions. Determine the magnetic field amplitude vector and the energy contained in the wave, assuming that the medium is nonmagnetic .

Admin
Solution

Since \(W=W^{\prime } \) is colinear with \(\widehat{S} \) , we find from equation

\(W.W=\nu ^{2} \mu \gamma = \nu ^{2}\epsilon _{0} \mu _{0} \left(\frac{\epsilon \mu }{\epsilon _{0} \mu _{0}} -i \frac{\sigma _{e}\mu }{2\pi \nu \epsilon _{0} \mu _{0}} \right) = \eta _{0}^{2} m^{2}\) ,

that \(W=w \widehat{S}=\eta _{0} n \widehat{S}\) and , from equation

\(W\times E_{0} =\nu \mu H_{0}\),

\(H_{0}=\frac{1}{\nu \mu } W \times E_{0} =\frac{1}{\nu \mu _{0}} W \times E_{0}=\frac{n}{c_{0} \mu _{0} } \widehat{S} \times E_{0}\) .

\(=\frac{nE_{0}}{c_{0} \mu _{0} \sqrt{154} } \left|\begin{matrix} \hat{\imath} & \hat{\jmath} & \widehat{k} \\ 0.8 & 0.0 & 0.6 \\ 6+3i & 2-5i & -8-4i \end{matrix} \right| \)

\(=\frac{nE_{0}}{c_{0} \mu _{0} 5 \sqrt{154}} [(-6+15i )\hat{\imath} +(50+25i )\hat{\jmath} +(8-20 i )\widehat{k}]\)

\(=\frac{H_{0}}{\sqrt{3850} } [(-6+15i )\hat{\imath} +(50+25i )\hat{\jmath} +(8-20 i )\widehat{k}]\),

where

\(^{6}\)Note that, since the vector cross-product is a nonlinear operation, the Poynting vector may not be calculated from \(S=R[E_{c}\times E_{c}]\)

\(^{.}\)John Henry Poynting (1852-1914)
British physicist. He served as professor of physics at the University of Birmingham from 1880 until his death. His discovery that electromagnetic energy is proportional to the product of electric and magnetic field strength is known as Poynting’s theorem.

\(H_{0} =\frac{nE_{0}}{c_{0} \mu _{0}} =\frac{2\times 600 N/C}{2.998 \times 10^{8} m/s \times 4\pi \times 10^{-7} Ns^{2}/C^{2} } =3.185 C/ms\) .

and it is assumed that, for a nonmagnetic medium, the magnetic permeability is equal to the one in vacuum, \(\mu =\mu _{0}\) (from Table A. 1).The energy content of the wave is given by the Poynting vector, either equation

\(\overline{S} =\frac{1}{2} \Re \left\{E_{c} \times H^{*}_{c} \right\}\) ,

or equation

\(\overline{S} =\frac{1}{2\nu \mu } \Re \left\{E_{c} \times (W^{*} \times E^{*}_{c} ) \right\} =\frac{1}{2\nu \mu } \Re \left\{W^{*}(E_{c} . E^{*}_{c}) \right\} =\frac{n}{2c_{0}\mu }\left|E_{0} \right| ^{2} e^{-4\pi \eta _{0} kz} \widehat{S}\) .

Choosing the latter, we get

\(\overline{S} =\frac{n}{2c_{0} \mu _{0}} E^{2}_{0} \widehat{s}=\overline{S} \widehat{s} , \overline{S}=\frac{2\times 600^{2} N^{2} /C^{2} }{2\times 2.2998\times 10^{-8} m/s \times 4\pi \times 10^{-7} N s^{2} /C^{2} } =955.6 W/m^{2}\) .
