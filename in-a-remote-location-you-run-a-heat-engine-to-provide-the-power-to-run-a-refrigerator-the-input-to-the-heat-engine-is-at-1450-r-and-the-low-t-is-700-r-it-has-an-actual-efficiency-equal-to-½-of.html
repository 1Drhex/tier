Guest Question.

In a remote location you run a heat engine to provide the power to run a refrigerator. The input to the heat engine is at 1450 R and the low T is 700 R, it has an actual efficiency equal to ½ of the corresponding Carnot unit. The refrigerator has a \(T _{ L }\) = 15 F and \(T _{ H }\) = 95 F with a COP that is 1/3 of the corresponding Carnot unit. Assume a cooling capacity of 7000 Btu/h is needed and find the rate of heat input to the heat engine.

Admin
Solution

Heat engine: \(\eta_{\text {Carnot }}=1-\frac{ T _{ L }}{ T _{ H }}=1-\frac{700}{1450}=0.517 ; \quad \eta_{ ac }=0.259\)

 

Refrigerator: \(\beta_{\text {ref. Carnot }}=\frac{ T _{ L }}{ T _{ H }- T _{ L }}=\frac{459.7+15}{95-15}=5.934 ; \quad \beta_{\text {ref. ac }}=1.978\)

 

Cooling capacity: \(\dot{ Q }_{ L }=7000 Btu / h =\beta_{ ref . ac } \dot{ W }\);

\(\dot{ W }=7000 Btu / h / 1.978=3538.9 Btu / h\)

 

This work must be provided by the heat engine \(\dot{ W }=\eta_{ ac } \dot{ Q }_{ H }\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{ Q }_{ H } &=\dot{ W } / \eta_{ ac }=3538.9( Btu / h ) / 0.259=13664 Btu / h \\&= 3 . 7 9 6 Btu / s\end{aligned}\)
