Guest Question.

Determine the magnitudes and orientations of the forces created by the correcting masses needed to balance the first and second harmonic shaking forces of the three-cylinder engine shown in Fig. 15.30.

Solution

The resultant first harmonic force vector is

\(F _{P}=1.5 P \cos \theta \hat{ i }-1.5 P \sin \theta \hat{ j }\) (1)

where

\(P=m r \omega^{2}\)

Comparing Eq. (1) with Eqs. (15.20), the coefficients are

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{P}^{x}=A \cos \theta+B \sin \theta \\&F_{P}^{y}=C \cos \theta+D \sin \theta\end{aligned}\) (15.20)

A = 1.5P, B = 0, C = 0, and D = âˆ’1.5P.

 

Therefore, from Eqs. (15.25), the forces are

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{1}=\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{(D+A)^{2}+(C-B)^{2}} \\&F_{2}=\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{(D-A)^{2}+(C+B)^{2}}\end{aligned}\) (15.25)

 

\(F_{1}=\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{(D+A)^{2}+(C-B)^{2}}=0\)

and

\(F_{2}=\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{(D-A)^{2}+(C+B)^{2}}=1.5 P\)

Since the first force is zero, there is only one correcting mass, that is, m2. The orientation angle for this mass, from the second of Eqs. (15.26), is

\(\begin{aligned}&\tan \gamma_{1}=(C-B) /(D+A) \\&\tan \gamma_{2}=(C+B) /(D-A)\end{aligned}\) (15.26)

 

\(\tan \gamma_{2}=(C+B) /(D-A)=0 /-3 P=0\)

Therefore, since the denominator (the cosine of the angle) is negative, the result is

\(\gamma_{2}=180^{\circ}\)
