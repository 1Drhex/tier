Guest Question.

A parallel \(RLC \) circuit with \(L = 1 mH\), \(C = 1000 μF\), \(R = 2.5 Ω\), \(V_{o} = 0 V\) and \(I_{o} = 0 A\) is driven by a single pulse of current of amplitude \(100 A\) lasting for \(10 μs\). Obtain and plot all the circuit variables as functions of time.

Admin
Solution

A single rectangular pulse can be expressed as sum of two step functions. Let the pulse height be \(I\), pulse duration be to and let it start at \(t = 0\). Then this pulse can be expressed as \(I[u(t)-u(t-t_{o})]\) where \(u(t-t_{o})\) is a unit step which is delayed by \(t_{o} s\) in the time-axis. Thus, what we have at hand looks like a step-response problem.

Undamped natural frequency \(\omega_{n}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{L C}}=1000 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{sec}\)

Critical resistance \(=\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\frac{L}{C}}=0.5 \Omega \)

\(\therefore \) Damping factor \(\xi=0.5 / 2.5=0.2\)

\(\therefore\) Natural frequencies \(=\left(-\xi \pm j \sqrt{1-\xi^{2}}\right) \omega_{n}=-200+j 979.8 \text { and }-200-j 979.8\)

Hence the circuit is an under-damped one and its natural response terms will be exponentially damped sinusoidal functions of time.

We note that the time constant of the exponential which damps the sinusoid is \(1/200 = 5 ms\). The period of the sinusoid term will be \(2π / 979.8 = 6.413 ms\). Observe that the duration of current pulse applied to the circuit is only \(0.01 ms\). It is very small compared to \(5 ms\) and \(6.413 ms\). An approximate solution to the circuit problem can usually be obtained under such situations by approximating the driving pulse as an impulse with an area content equal to the area content of the pulse being approximated. The pulse can be arbitrary in shape – only that the pulse duration has to be much less than the characteristic times involved in the circuit natural response. Hence this problem can be solved by obtaining the impulse response and scaling it by the magnitude to the impulse used to approximate the rectangular pulse, i.e., \(100 A ×10 μs = 1000 μC.\)

No portion of an impulse current can flow through the resistor in a parallel \(RLC\) circuit since that will result in impulse voltage across the combination. Capacitor will not let that happen. No portion of impulse current can flow through the inductor since inductor does not even permit a finite change in current over infinitesimal time duration. Hence, the entire input impulse current has to flow through the capacitor at \(t = 0\). Therefore, the capacitor voltage changes instantaneously to \(1000 μC/1000 μF = 1 V\) from \(0\). The impulse current source is open (because a current source which is zero-valued is an open-circuit ) for all \(t ≥ 0^{+}\). The only effect of impulse excitation is that it changes the initial condition abruptly.

The subsequent response is the free-response of the circuit.

Now the problem has got reduced to finding the zero-input response (i.e., free-response) of the circuit with \(V_{o} = 1 V\) and \(I_{o} = 0.\)

\(\begin{aligned} i_{L}(t)=e^{-200 t}\left(A_{1} \sin 979.8 t+A_{2} \cos 979.8 t\right) & \mathrm{A} \\ i_{L}\left(0^{+}\right)=0 \text { and }\left.\frac{d i_{L}(t)}{d t}\right|_{\left(0^{+}\right)}=\frac{V_{o}}{L}=1000 \mathrm{~A} / \mathrm{s} \end{aligned}\)

 

Differentiating \(i_{L}(t)\) and applying initial conditions, \(A_{2}=0 \text { and } 979.8 A_{1}-200 A_{2}=1000.\)

Solving for \(A_{1}\) and \(A_{2}\), \(A_{1} = 1.0206\) and \(A_{2} = 0\)

\(\begin{aligned} \therefore i_{L}(t) &=1.0206 e^{-200 t} \sin 979.8 t \mathrm{~A} \text { for } t \geq 0^{+} \\ v(t) &=L\left(i_{L}(t)\right)=e^{-200 t}(-0.204 \sin 979.8 t+\cos 979.8 t) \mathrm{V} \\ &=1.0206 e^{-200 t} \cos (979.8 t+0.2 \mathrm{rad}) \mathrm{V} \text { for } t \geq 0^{+} \\ i_{C}(t)=C(v(t)) &=e^{-200 t}[-0.204 \cos (979.8 t+0.2 \mathrm{rad})-\sin (979.8 t+0.2 \mathrm{rad})] \\ &=-1.0206 e^{-200 t} \sin (979.8 t+0.4 \mathrm{rad}) \mathrm{A} \text { for } t \geq 0^{+} \\ i_{R}(t) &=\frac{v(t)}{R}=0.408 e^{-200 t} \cos (979.8 t+0.2 \mathrm{rad}) \mathrm{A} \text { for } t \geq 0^{+} \end{aligned}\)
