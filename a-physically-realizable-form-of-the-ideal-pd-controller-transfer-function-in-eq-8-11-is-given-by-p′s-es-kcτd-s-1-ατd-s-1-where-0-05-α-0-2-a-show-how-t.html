Guest Question.

A physically realizable form of the ideal PD controller transfer function in Eq. \(8-11\) is given by

\(\frac{P^{\prime}(s)}{E(s)}=K_{c}\left(1+\tau_{D} s\right)\) (8-11)

\(\frac{P^{\prime}(s)}{E(s)}=\frac{K_{c}\left(\tau_{D} s+1\right)}{\alpha \tau_{D} s+1}\)

where \(0.05<\alpha<0.2\).

(a) Show how to obtain this transfer function with a parallel arrangement of two much simpler functions in Fig. E8.2.

(b) Find expressions for \(K_{1}, K_{2}\), and \(\tau_{1}\) that can be used to obtain desired values of \(K_{c}, \tau_{D}\), and \(\alpha .\)

(c) Verify the relations for \(K_{c}=3, \tau_{D}=2, \alpha=0.1\).

Admin
Solution

a)

\(\frac{P^{\prime}(s)}{E(s)}=\frac{K_{1}}{\tau_{1} s+1}+K_{2}=\frac{K_{1}+K_{2} \tau_{1} s+K_{2}}{\tau_{1} s+1}=\left(K_{1}+K_{2}\right)\left[\frac{\frac{K_{2} \tau_{1}}{K_{1}+K_{2}} s+1}{\tau_{1} s+1}\right]\)

b)

\(K_{c}=K_{1}+K_{2} \quad \rightarrow \quad K_{2}=K_{c}-K_{1}\)

\(\tau_{1}=\alpha \tau_{D}\)

\(\tau_{D}=\frac{K_{2} \tau_{1}}{K_{1}+K_{2}}=\frac{K_{2} \alpha \tau_{D}}{K_{1}+K_{2}}\)

or \(\quad 1=\frac{K_{2} \alpha}{K_{1}+K_{2}}\)

\(\begin{aligned}&K_{1}+K_{2}=K_{2} \alpha \\&K_{1}=K_{2} \alpha-K_{2}=K_{2}(\alpha-1)\end{aligned}\)

Substituting,

\(K_{1}=\left(K_{c}-K_{1}\right)(\alpha-1)=(\alpha-1) K_{c}-(\alpha-1) K_{1}\)

Then,

\(K_{1}=\left(\frac{\alpha-1}{\alpha}\right) K_{c}\)

c)

If \(K_{c}=3 \quad, \quad \tau_{D}=2 \quad, \quad \alpha=0.1 \quad\) then,

\(\begin{aligned}&K_{1}=\frac{-0.9}{0.1} \times 3=-27 \\&K_{2}=3-(-27)=30 \\&\tau_{l}=0.1 \times 2=0.2\end{aligned}\)

Hence

\(\begin{gathered}K_{1}+K_{2}=-27+30=3 \\\frac{K_{2} \tau_{1}}{K_{1}+K_{2}}=\frac{30 \times 0.2}{3}=2 \\G_{c}(s)=3\left(\frac{2 s+1}{0.2 s+1}\right)\end{gathered}\)
