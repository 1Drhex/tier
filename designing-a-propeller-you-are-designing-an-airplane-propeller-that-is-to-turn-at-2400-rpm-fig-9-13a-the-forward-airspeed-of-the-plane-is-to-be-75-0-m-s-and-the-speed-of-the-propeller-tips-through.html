Guest Question.

DESIGNING A PROPELLER

You are designing an airplane propeller that is to turn at 2400 rpm (Fig. 9.13a). The forward airspeed of the plane is to be 75.0 m/s, and the speed of the propeller tips through the air must not exceed 270 m/s. (This is about 80% of the speed of sound in air. If the propeller tips moved faster, they would produce a lot of noise.) (a) What is the maximum possible propeller radius? (b) With this radius, what is the acceleration of the propeller tip?

Admin
Solution

IDENTIFY and SET UP:

We consider a particle at the tip of the propeller; our target variables are the particle’s distance from the axis and its acceleration. The speed of this particle through the air, which cannot exceed 270 m/s, is due to both the propeller’s rotation and the forward motion of the airplane. Figure 9.13b shows that the particle’s velocity \(\overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{v}}_{\mathrm{tip}}\) is the vector sum of its tangential velocity due to the propeller’s rotation of magnitude \(v_{tan}\) = wr, given by Eq. (9.13) (v = rw), and the forward velocity of the airplane of magnitude \(v_{plane}\) = 75.0 m/s. The propeller rotates in a plane perpendicular to the direction of flight, so \(\overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{v}}_{\mathrm{tan}} \text { and } \overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{v}}_{\text {plane }}\) are perpendicular to each other, and we can use the Pythagorean theorem to obtain an expression for \(v_{tip}\) from \(v_{tan}\)and \(v_{plane}\) .

We will then set \(v_{tip}\) = 270 m/s and solve for the radius r. The angular speed of the propeller is constant, so the acceleration of the propeller tip has only a radial component; we’ll find it by using Eq. (9.15) (\(a_{\mathrm{rad}}=\frac{v^{2}}{r}=\omega^{2} r\)).

EXECUTE:

We first convert w to rad/s (see Fig. 9.11):

\(\omega=2400 \mathrm{rpm}=\left(2400 \frac{\mathrm{rev}}{\mathrm{min}}\right)\left(\frac{2 \pi \mathrm{rad}}{1 \mathrm{rev}}\right)\left(\frac{1 \mathrm{~min}}{60 \mathrm{~s}}\right)=251 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}\)

(a) From Fig. 9.13b and Eq. (9.13) (v = rw),

\(v_{\text {tip }}^{2}=v_{\text {plane }}^{2}+v_{\text {tan }}^{2}=v_{\text {plane }}^{2}+r^{2} \omega^{2} \quad \text { so }\)

 

\(r^{2}=\frac{v_{\mathrm{tip}}^{2}-v_{\text {plane }}^{2}}{\omega^{2}} \quad \text { and } \quad r=\frac{\sqrt{v_{\text {tip }}^{2}-v_{\text {plane }}^{2}}}{\omega}\)

If \(v_{tip}\) = 270 m/s, the maximum propeller radius is

\(r=\frac{\sqrt{(270 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s})^{2}-(75.0 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s})^{2}}}{251 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}}=1.03 \mathrm{~m}\)

(b) The centripetal acceleration of the particle is, from Eq. (9.15) (\(a_{\mathrm{rad}}=\frac{v^{2}}{r}=\omega^{2} r\)),

\(\begin{aligned}a_{\mathrm{rad}} &=\omega^{2} r=(251 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s})^{2}(1.03\mathrm{~m}) \\&=6.5 \times 10^{4} \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}^{2}=6600 \mathrm{~g}\end{aligned}\)

The tangential acceleration \(a_{tan}\) is zero because w is constant.

 

EVALUATE: From \(\sum \overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{F}}=m \overrightarrow{\boldsymbol{a}}\), the propeller must exert a force of 6.5 × \(10^4\) N on each kilogram of material at its tip! This is why propellers are made out of tough material, usually aluminum alloy.
