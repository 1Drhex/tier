Guest Question.

Apply the techniques of this Section to the \( H ^{-} \text {and } Li ^{+} \) ions (each has two electrons, like helium, but nuclear charges Z = 1 and Z = 3 , respectively). Find the effective (partially shielded) nuclear charge, and determine the best upper bound on \( E_{ gs } \) , for each case. Comment: In the case of \( H ^{-} \) you should find that \( \langle H\rangle>-13.6 eV \), which would appear to indicate that there is no bound state at all, since it would be energetically favorable for one electron to fly off, leaving behind a neutral hydrogen atom. This is not entirely surprising, since the electrons are less strongly attracted to the nucleus than they are in helium, and the electron repulsion tends to break the atom apart. However, it turns out to be incorrect. With a more sophisticated trial wave function (see ) it can be shown that \( E_{ gs }<-13.6 eV \),and hence that a bound state does exist. Itâ€™s only barely bound, however, and there are no excited bound states, so \( H ^{-} \) has no discrete spectrum (all transitions are to and from the continuum). As a result, it is difficult to study in the laboratory, although it exists in great abundance on the surface of the sun.

Solution

I’ll do the general case of a nucleus with \( Z_{0} \) protons. Ignoring electron-electron repulsion altogether gives

\( \psi_{0}=\frac{Z_{0}^{3}}{\pi a^{3}} e^{-Z_{0}\left(r_{1}+r_{2}\right) / a} \), (generalizing Eq. 8.18).

\( \psi_{0}\left( r _{1}, r _{2}\right) \equiv \psi_{100}\left( r _{1}\right) \psi_{100}\left( r _{2}\right)=\frac{8}{\pi a^{3}} e^{-2\left(r_{1}+r_{2}\right) / a} \) (8.18).

and the energy is \( 2 Z_{0}^{2} E_{1} \cdot\left\langle V_{e e}\right\rangle \text { goes like } 1 / a \) (Eqs. 8.21 and 8.26), so the generalization of Eq. 8.26 is \( \left\langle V_{e e}\right\rangle= -\frac{5}{4} Z_{0} E_{1} \) , and the generalization of Eq. 8.27 is \( \langle H\rangle=\left(2 Z_{0}^{2}-\frac{5}{4} Z_{0}\right) E_{1} \).

\( \left\langle V_{e e}\right\rangle=\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)\left(\frac{8}{\pi a^{3}}\right)^{2} \int \frac{e^{-4\left(r_{1}+r_{2}\right) / a}}{\left| r _{1}- r _{2}\right|} d^{3} r _{1} d^{3} r _{2} \) (8.21).

\( \left\langle V_{e e}\right\rangle=\frac{5}{4 a}\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)=-\frac{5}{2} E_{1}=34 eV \) (8.26).

\( \langle H\rangle=-109 eV +34 eV =-75 eV \) (8.27).

If we include shielding, the only change is that (Z – 2) in Eqs. 8.29, 8.30, and 8.33 is replaced by \( \left(Z-Z_{0}\right) \).

\( H=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m}\left(\nabla_{1}^{2}+\nabla_{2}^{2}\right)-\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\left(\frac{Z}{r_{1}}+\frac{Z}{r_{2}}\right) \)

\( +\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\left(\frac{(Z-2)}{r_{1}}+\frac{(Z-2)}{r_{2}}+\frac{1}{\left| r _{1}- r _{2}\right|}\right) \) (8.29).

\( \langle H\rangle=2 Z^{2} E_{1}+2(Z-2)\left(\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0}}\right)\left\langle\frac{1}{r}\right\rangle+\left\langle V_{e e}\right\rangle \).

\( \langle H\rangle=\left[2 Z^{2}-4 Z(Z-2)-(5 / 4) Z\right] E_{1}=\left[-2 Z^{2}+(27 / 4) Z\right] E_{1} \) (8.33).

Thus Eq. 8.33 generalizes to

\( \langle H\rangle=\left[2 Z^{2}-4 Z\left(Z-Z_{0}\right)-\frac{5}{4} Z\right] E_{1}=\left[-2 Z^{2}+4 Z Z_{0}-\frac{5}{4} Z\right] E_{1} \).

\( \frac{\partial\langle H\rangle}{\partial Z}=\left[-4 Z+4 Z_{0}-\frac{5}{4}\right] E_{1}=0 \Longrightarrow Z=Z_{0}-\frac{5}{16} \).

\( \langle H\rangle_{\min }=\left[-2\left(Z_{0}-\frac{5}{16}\right)^{2}+4\left(Z_{0}-\frac{5}{16}\right) Z_{0}-\frac{5}{4}\left(Z_{0}-\frac{5}{16}\right)\right] E_{1} \).

\( =\left(-2 Z_{0}^{2}+\frac{5}{4} Z_{0}-\frac{25}{128}+4 Z_{0}^{2}-\frac{5}{4} Z_{0}-\frac{5}{4} Z_{0}+\frac{25}{64}\right) E_{1} \).

\( \left(2 Z_{0}^{2}-\frac{5}{4} Z_{0}+\frac{25}{128}\right) E_{1} = \frac{\left(16 Z_{0}-5\right)^{2}}{128} E_{1} \).

generalizing Eq. 8.35. The first term is the naive estimate ignoring electron-electron repulsion altogether; the second term is \( \left\langle V_{e e}\right\rangle \) in the unscreened state, and the third term is the effect of screening.

\( \langle H\rangle=\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^{6} E_{1}=-77.5 eV \) (8.35).

\( \underline{Z}_{0}=1\left( H ^{-}\right): Z=1-\frac{5}{16}=\frac{11}{16}=0.688 \). The effective nuclear charge is less than 1, as expected.

\( \langle H\rangle_{\min }=\frac{11^{2}}{128} E_{1}=\frac{121}{128} E_{1}=-12.9 eV \).

\( Z_{0}=2( He ): Z=2-\frac{5}{16}=\frac{27}{16}=1.69(\text { as before }) ;\langle H\rangle_{\min }=\frac{27^{2}}{128} E_{1}=\frac{729}{128} E_{1}=-77.5 eV \).

\( \underline{Z}_{0}=3\left( Li ^{+}\right): Z=3-\frac{5}{16}=\frac{43}{16}=2.69 \text { (somewhat less than 3); }\langle H\rangle_{\min }=\frac{43^{2}}{128} E_{1}=\frac{1849}{128} E_{1}=-196 eV \).
