Guest Question.

Figure 4.35 shows a mechanism that is designed to push parts from one conveyor to another. During the transfer, the parts must be rotated as shown. Analytically determine the position of the pusher rod at several phases of its motion. 1.Draw the Kinematic Diagram 2.Designate the Original Phase 3.Construct the Interval Phases 4.Identify the Limiting Positions.

Admin
Solution

1: The kinematic diagram for this mechanism is shown in Figure 4.36. Notice that it is an offset slider-crank mechanism having one degree of freedom.

2: Arbitrarily, the original phase is selected to be when the crank is horizontal, placing joint B directly left of joint A.

3: Recall that equations \(\theta _{3}=sin^{-1}\left[\frac{L_{1}+L_{2sin}\ \theta _{2}}{L_{3}} \right]\), \(L_{4}=L_{2}\ cos\ \theta _{2}+L_{3}\ cos\ \theta _{3}\), and \(\gamma =180^{\circ }-(\theta _{2}+\theta _{3})\) describe the position of an offset slider-crank mechanism. These can be used in a full-cycle analysis. The equations were used in conjunction with a spreadsheet, yielding the results shown in Figure 4.37. If you are not familiar with using spreadsheets, refer to Chapter 8.
