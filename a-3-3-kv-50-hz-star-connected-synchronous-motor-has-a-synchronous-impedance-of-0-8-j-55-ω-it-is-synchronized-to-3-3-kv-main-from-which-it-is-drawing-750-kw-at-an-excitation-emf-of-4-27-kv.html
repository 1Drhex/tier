Guest Question.

A 3.3 kV, 50 Hz star connected synchronous motor has a synchronous impedance of \( (0.8 + j 55) \Omega \). It is synchronized to 3.3 kV main from which it is drawing 750 kW at an excitation emf of 4.27 kV (line). Determine the armature current, power factor and power angle. Also find the mechanical power developed. If the stray load loss is 30 kW, find the efficiency.

Admin
Solution

Converting to per phase

\( V_{t}=\frac{3.3}{\sqrt{3}}=1905 V , \quad E_{f}=\frac{4.27}{\sqrt{3}}=2465 V\)

Power input to motor, \( P_{i}(\text { in })=\frac{750}{3}=250 kW\)

 

\( \bar{Z}_{s}=0.8+j 5.5=5.56 \angle 81.7^{\circ} \Omega\)

 

\( Z_{s}=5.56 \Omega, \quad \theta=81.7^{\circ}, \quad \alpha=90^{\circ}-81.7^{\circ}=8.3^{\circ}\)

As per Eq. (8.87 a) \( P_{e} \text { (in) }=-P_{e} \text { (out) } =\frac{V_{t}^{2}}{Z_{s}^{2}} R_{a}+\frac{V_{t} E_{f}}{Z_{s}} \sin (\delta-\alpha)\)

 

\( P_{e}(\text { in })=\frac{V_{t}^{2}}{Z_{s}^{2}} R_{a}+\frac{V_{t} E_{f}}{Z_{s}} \sin (\delta-\alpha)\)

 

\( 250 \times 10^{3}=\frac{(1905)^{2}}{(5.56)^{2}} \times 0.8+\frac{1905 \times 2465}{5.56} \sin \left(\delta-8.3^{\circ}\right)\)

 

\( \frac{\left(250 \times 10^{3}\right) \times 5.06}{1905}=\frac{1905}{5.56} \times 0.89+2465 \sin \left(\delta-8.3^{\circ}\right)\)

Power angle, \( \delta=18.95^{\circ}\)

 

\( \bar{I}_{a} \bar{Z}_{s}=V_{t} \angle 0-E_{f} \angle-\delta\)

Or \( \bar{I}_{a} \bar{Z}_{s}=1905-2465 \angle-18.95^{\circ}\)

= – 42.64 + j 800.5

\( =907 \angle 118^{\circ}\)

 

\( \bar{I}_{a}=\frac{907 \angle 118^{\circ}}{5.56 \angle 81.7^{\circ}}=1631 \angle 36.3^{\circ} A\)

 

\( I_{a}=163.1 A , \quad pf =\cos 36.3^{\circ}=0.806\) leading

\( \bar{E}_{f}=2465 \angle-18.95^{\circ}\) or \( 4270 \angle-18.95^{\circ} V\) (line)

\( \bar{I}_{a}\) leads \( \bar{E}_{f}\) by \( \left(18.95^{\circ}+36.3^{\circ}\right)=55.25^{\circ}\) . The phasor diagram is drawn in Fig. 8.69(a).

Mechanical power developed \( P_{m}( dev )=3 E_{f} I_{a} \cos (\delta+\phi)\)

 

\( P_{m}(\text { dev })=\sqrt{3} \times 4270 \times 163.1 \cos \left(55.25^{\circ}\right) \times 10^{-3}=687.5 kW\)

 

\( P_{ st }=30 kW\)

 

\( P_{m}(\text { net })=687.5-30=657.5 kW\)

 

\( \eta=\frac{657.5}{750} \times 100=87.7 \% \)

 

Alternative The phasor method

We want to find out current and pf.

\( \bar{I}_{a} \bar{Z}_{s}=\bar{V}_{t}-\bar{E}_{f}\) (i)

We convert it to current form

\( \bar{I}_{a}=\frac{\bar{V}_{t}}{\bar{Z}_{s}}-\frac{\bar{E}_{f}}{\bar{Z}_{s}}=\bar{I}_{t}-\bar{I}_{e}\)

 

\( \bar{I}_{a}=\left(\frac{1905}{5.56}\right)<-81.7^{\circ}-\left(\frac{2465}{5.56}\right)<? \)

 

\( \bar{I}_{a}=342.6 \angle-81.7^{\circ}-443 \angle ? \) (ii)

As power input is known

\( P_{e}(\text { in })=\sqrt{3} \times 3.3 I_{a} \cos \phi=750 kW\)

Or \( I_{a} \cos \phi=131 A\) (real power component, in phase with \( V_{t}\) )

The phasor diagram is drawn to scale in Fig. 8.69(b) in following steps.

\( O A=I_{a} \cos \phi=131 A\) along \( V_{t}\) (in phase)

Draw the locus of \( I_{a}\) phasor tip at 90° to \( V_{t}\)

Draw \( O B=I_{t}=342.6 A\) lagging \( V_{t}\) by \( \theta=81.7^{\circ}\)

From B draw an arc of radius \( I_{e} = 443 A\) to locate point C on the current locus. We can now measure to scale

\( I_{a}=162 A , \phi=36^{\circ}, \cos \phi=0.809\) as found by the quantitative method.
