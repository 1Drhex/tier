Guest Question.

A 500 V, 6-pole, 3-phase, 50 Hz, star-connected synchronous motor has a resistance and synchronous reactance of 0.3 Ω and 3 Ω per phase, respectively. The open-circuit voltage is 600 V. If the friction and core losses total 1 kW, calculate the line current and power factor when the motor output is 100 hp.

Admin
Solution

Supply voltage per phase, \(V= \frac{500}{\sqrt{3} }= 288.7 V \)

Induced emf per phase, \(E= \frac{600}{\sqrt{3} }= 346.41 V \)

Total power developed, \( 3 P_{mech} = output + mech. loss \\[0.5cm] \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt} \qquad \qquad = 100 \times 0.7355 + 1 \\[0.5cm] \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt} \qquad \qquad =74.55 kW\)

\( Power developed per phase = \frac{74.55}{3} = 24.85 kW \)

or \( = 24850 watt\)

Internal angle, \(\theta =\tan ^{-1}\frac{X_{s}}{R}= \tan ^{-1}\frac{3}{0.3}= 84.3^{\circ }\)

Synchronous impedance/phase, \(Z_{s}= \sqrt{0.3^{2}+ 3^{2}} = 3.015 \Omega \)

Power developed per phase is given as

\(P_{mech}=\frac{E V}{Z_{s}} \cos \left(\theta – \delta \right) – \frac{E^{2}}{Z_{s}} \cos \theta \)

 

or \(24850=\frac{346.41\times 288.7}{3.015} \cos \left(84.3^{\circ } – \delta \right) – \frac{\left(346.41\right)^{2}}{3.015} \cos 84.3^{\circ} \)

or \(24850=33170.3 \cos \left(84.3^{\circ } – \delta \right) = 3953 \)

or \(\cos \left(84.3^{\circ } – \delta \right) = 0.8684 \)

or \(84.3^{\circ } – \delta = \cos ^{-1}0.8684 = 29.73^{\circ }\)

or \(\delta = 84.3^{\circ } – 29.73^{\circ } = 54.57^{\circ }\left(lag\right) \)

From phasor diagram shown in Fig. 8.19.

\(E_{r}= \sqrt{V^{2} + E^{2} – 2 V E \cos \delta} \\[0.5cm] \quad \, =\sqrt{288.7^{2} + 346.41^{2} – 2 \times 288.7 \times 346.41 \cos 54.57^{\circ }} \\[0.5cm] \quad \, =295.6 V\)

 

\(Line current = Phase current , I = \frac{E_{R}}{Z_{s}} =\frac{295.6}{3.015} = \mathbf{98.1 A} \)

 

Again considering phasor diagram

\(\sin \left(\theta +\phi \right)=\frac{E}{E_{r}}\sin \delta = \frac{346.41}{295.6} \sin 54.57^{\circ } = 0.9542\)

 

or \(\phi +\theta =\sin ^{-1}0.9542= 72.7^{\circ }\)

or \(\theta = 72.7^{\circ } – 84.3^{\circ }= -11.6^{\circ } lagging\)

\(\therefore Power factor = \cos 11.6^{\circ }=\mathbf{0.98 lagging} \)
