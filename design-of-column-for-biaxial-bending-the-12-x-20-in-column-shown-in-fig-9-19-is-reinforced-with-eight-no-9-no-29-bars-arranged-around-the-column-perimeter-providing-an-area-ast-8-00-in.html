Guest Question.

Design of column for biaxial bending. The \(12 \times 20\) in. column shown in Fig. 9.19 is reinforced with eight No. 9 (No. 29) bars arranged around the column perimeter, providing an area \(A_{st} = 8.00 in^2\). A factored load \(P_u\) of 255 kips is to be applied with eccentricities \(e_y\) = 3 in. and \(e_x\) = 6 in., as shown. Material strengths are \(f_c^\prime\) = 4 ksi and \(f_y\) = 60 ksi. Check the adequacy of the trial design (a) using the reciprocal load method and (b) using the load contour method.

Admin
Solution

(a) By the reciprocal load method, first considering bending about the Y axis, \(\gamma\) = 15/20 = 0.75 and e/h = 6/20 = 0.30. With the reinforcement ratio of \(A_{st} / bh\) = 8.00/240 = 0.033, using the average of Graphs A.6 (\(\gamma\) = 0.70) and A.7 (\(\gamma\) = 0.80),

\(\frac{P_{ny0}}{f_c^\prime A_g}(avg)=\frac{0.62+0.66}{2}=0.64 P_{ny0}=0.64\times4\times240=614 kips\)

 

\(\frac{P_0}{f_c^\prime A_g}=1.31 P_0=1.31 \times 4 \times 240 = 1258 kips\)

Then for bending about the X axis, \(\gamma =\frac{7}{12}\) = 0.58 (say 0.60) and e/h = \(\frac{3}{12}\) = 0.25. Graph A.5 of Appendix A gives

\(\frac{P_{nx0}}{f_c^\prime A_g}=0.65 P_{nx0}=0.65\times4\times240=624 kips\)

 

\(\frac{P_0}{f_c^\prime A_g}=1.31 P_0=1.31 \times 4 \times 240 = 1258 kips\)

Substituting these values in Eq. (9.19) results in

\(\frac{1}{P_n} =\frac{1}{P_{nxo}}+\frac{1}{P_{ny0}}-\frac{1}{P_0} \)

 

\(\frac{1}{P_n} =\frac{1}{624}+\frac{1}{614}-\frac{1}{1258}= 0.00244 \)

from which \(P_n\) = 410 kips. Thus, according to the Bresler method, the design load of \(P_u = 0.65 \times 410\) = 267 kips can be applied safely.

(b) By the load contour method, for Y axis bending with \(P_u /(ϕ f_c^\prime A_g) = 255/(0.65 \times 4 \times 240)\) = 0.41. The average from Graphs A.6 and A.7 of Appendix A is

\(\frac{M_{ny0}}{f_c^\prime A_g h}(avg)=\frac{0.212+0.235}{2}=0.224 \)

Hence, \(M_{ny0} = 0.224 \times 4 \times 240 \times 20\) = 4300 in-kips. Then for X axis bending, with \(P_u /(ϕ f_c^\prime A_g)\) = 0.41, as before, from Graph A.5,

\(\frac{M_{nx0}}{f_c^\prime A_g h}=0.186\)

So \(M_{nx0} = 0.186 \times 4 \times 240 \times 12\) = 2140 in-kips. The factored load moments about the Y and X axes, respectively, are

\(M_{uy} = 255 \times 6\) = 1530 in-kips
\(M_{ux} = 255 \times 3\) = 765 in-kips

Adequacy of the trial design will now be checked using Eq. (9.18) with an exponent α conservatively taken equal to 1.15. Then with \(M_{nx} = M_{ux} / ϕ and M_{ny} = M_{uy} / ϕ\), that equation indicates

\(\left(\frac{M_{nx}}{M_{nx0}} \right)^{\alpha1}+\left(\frac{M_{ny}}{M_{ny0}} \right)^{\alpha2}=1.0\)

 

\(\left(\frac{765/0.65}{2140} \right)^{1.15}+ \left(\frac{1530/0.65}{4300} \right)^{1.15}= 0.502 + 0.500 = 1.002\)

This is close enough to 1.0 that the design would be considered safe by the load contour method also.
