Guest Question.

The chemical reaction of ammonia oxidation reads,

\( 4 N H_3 + 5 O_2 → 4 NO + 6 H_2O \).

Consider that initially this reaction is taking place with \(N_{NH_3} (0)\) moles of \(NH_3\) and \(N_{O_2} (0)\) moles of \(O_2\). Find the amount of \( NH_3, O_2, NO\) and \( H_2O \) at the end of the reaction.

Numerical Application:

\(N_{NH_3} (0)\) = 2 mol, \(N_{O_2} (0)\) = 2 mol, \(N_{NO} (0)\) = 0 mol and \(N_{H_2O} (0)\) = 0 mol.

Admin
Solution

The stoichiometric coefficients of the chemical reaction,
\( 4 NH_3 + 5 O_2 → 4 NO + 6 H_2O \).
are \(ν_{NH_3} = −4, ν_{O_2} = −5, ν_{NO} = 4\) and \(ν_{H_2O} = 6\). According to relation (8.6), the time evolution of the number of moles of a substance A is given by,
\( N_A (t) = N_A (0) + ν_{aA} \xi _a (t) \). (8.6)
\( N_A (t) = N_A (0) + ν_{A} \xi (t) \).
where \(\xi (t) \) is the extent of the chemical reaction. The molecular oxygen \(O_2\) will run out first. The reaction will stop at time \(t_f\) given by,
\( N_{O_2} (t_f) = N_{O_2} (0) + ν_{O_2} \xi (t_f) = 0 \).
which implies that the final extent of the reaction yields,
\(\xi (t_f) = – \frac{N_{O_2} (0)}{ν_{O_2}} = \frac{2}{5}\) mol.
At the end of the reaction, the amount of \(NH_3\) is,
\( N_{NH_3} (t_f) = N_{NH_3} (0) + ν_{NH_3} \xi (t_f) \)= 0.4 mol.
the amount of NO is,
\( N_{NO} (t_f) = N_{NO} (0) + ν_{NO} \xi (t_f) \) = 1.6 mol.
and the amount of \( H_2O \) is,
\( N_{H_2O} (t_f) = N_{H_2O} (0) + ν_{H_2O} \xi (t_f) \) = 2.4 mol.
