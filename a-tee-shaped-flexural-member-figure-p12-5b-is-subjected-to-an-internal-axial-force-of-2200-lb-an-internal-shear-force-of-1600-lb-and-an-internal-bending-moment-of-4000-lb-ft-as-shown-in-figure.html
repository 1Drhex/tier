Guest Question.

A tee-shaped flexural member (Figure P12.5b) is subjected to an internal axial force of 2,200 lb, an internal shear force of 1,600 lb, and an internal bending moment of 4,000 lb-ft, as shown in Figure P12.5a. Determine the normal and shear stresses at point H, which is located 1.5 in. below the top surface of the tee shape. Show these stresses on a stress element.

Admin
Solution

Centroid location in y direction: (reference axis at bottom of tee shape)

\(\begin{array}{lccccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Width } b \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Height } h \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left( in. ^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} \text { (from bottom) } \\\text { (in.) }\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} A_{i} \\\left( in .{ }^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { top flange } & 6 & 1 & 6.000 & 6.500 & 39.000 \\\text { stem } & 1 & 6 & 6.000 & 3.000 & 18.000 \\\hline & & & 12.000 in. ^{2} & & 57.000 in .{ }^{3}\end{array}\)

 

\(\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{57.0 in .^{3}}{12.0 in .^{2}}=4.750 in .\) (measured upward from bottom edge of stem)

 

Moment of inertia about the z axis: (i.e., horizontal axis)

\(\begin{array}{lcccc}\text { Shape } & I_{C} & d=y_{i}-\bar{y} & d^{2} A & I_{C}+d^{2} A \\& \left( in .{ }^{4}\right) & (\text { in. }) & \left( in. ^{4}\right) & \left( in .{ }^{4}\right) \\\hline \text { top flange } & 0.500 & 1.750 & 18.375 & 18.875 \\\text { stem } & 18.000 & -1.750 & 18.375 & 36.375 \\\hline {\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( in .{ }^{4}\right)=} 55.250\end{array}\)

 

Axial stress at point H:

\(\sigma_{x}=\frac{F}{A}=\frac{2,200 lb }{12.00 in. ^{2}}=183.333 psi\)

Bending stress at point H: \((y=7.00 \text { in. }-4.75 in .-1.50 in .=0.75 in . \text { ) }\)

\(\sigma_{x}=-\frac{M y}{I_{z}}=-\frac{(4,000 lb – ft )(0.75 in .)(12 in. / ft )}{55.250 in. { }^{4}}=-651.584 psi\)

Transverse and horizontal shear stress at point H:

\(Q=(6 \text { in. })(1 \text { in. })(1.75 in .)+(1 in .)(0.50 in .)(1.0 in .)=11.000 in.{ }^{3}\)

\(\tau=\frac{V Q}{I t}=\frac{(1,600 lb )\left(11.000 in .{ }^{3}\right)}{\left(55.250 in. ^{4}\right)(1 in .)}=318.552 psi\) (Note: Sense to be determined by inspection)

 

Summary of stresses at H:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=-468 psi \\\sigma_{y} &=0 psi \\\tau_{x y} &=-319 psi\end{aligned}\)
