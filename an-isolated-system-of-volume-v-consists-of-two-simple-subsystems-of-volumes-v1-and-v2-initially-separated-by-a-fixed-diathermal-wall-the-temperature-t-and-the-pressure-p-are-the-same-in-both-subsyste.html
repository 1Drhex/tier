Guest Question.

An isolated system of volume V consists of two simple subsystems of volumes \(V_1\) and \(V_2\) initially separated by a fixed diathermal wall. The temperature T and the pressure p are the same in both subsystems. The temperature T is constant and the total volume V is fixed.
Initially, there are \(N_1\) moles of an ideal gas of substance 1 in one of the subsystems and \(N_2\) moles of an ideal gas of substance 2 in the other. The wall is removed and the ideal gases diffuse and mix [84]. There is no chemical reaction between the ideal gases.

1. Determine the internal energy variation ΔU of the system during the mixing.
2. Determine the enthalpy variation ΔH of the system during the mixing.
3. Determine the entropy variation ΔS of the system during the mixing in terms of the ideal gas concentrations \(c_1\) and \(c_2\) in the mixture and show that it is positive.
4. Determine the Gibbs free energy variation ΔG of the system during the mixing in terms of the ideal gas concentrations\( c_1\) and \(c_2\) in the mixture and show that it is negative.

Admin
Solution

1. Since the system is isolated, the internal energy variation (1.30) during the mixing vanishes,

\(\dot{U} = 0\) (isolated system)

ΔU = 0

2. According to expression (5.65), the enthalpy variation of an ideal gas during an isothermal process vanishes. Thus, the total enthalpy variation during the mixing vanishes,

H = (c + 1) NR T

ΔH = 0

3. Taking into account the expression (7.17) of the entropy variation for an isothermal process, the entropy variation of the system is written,

\(ΔS_{if} = \int_{S_i}^{S_f}{ dS } = \int_{i}^{f}{ \frac{p dV}{V} } = N R \int_{V_i}^{V_f}{\frac{dV}{V} } = N R \ln \Bigl(\frac{V_f}{V_i} \Bigr)\).

\(ΔS = ΔS_1 +ΔS_2 = N_1 R \ln \Bigl(\frac{V}{V_1} \Bigr) +N_2 R \ln \Bigl(\frac{V}{V_2} \Bigr) \).

Before the mixing, the ideal gases satisfy the equations of state (5.47),

pV = NR T

\(V_1 = \frac{N_1 R T}{p}\) and \(V_2 = \frac{N_2 R T}{p}\)

Summing the two previous equations yields an expression for the total volume V,

\(V = V_1 + V_2 = \frac{(N_1 +N_2) R T}{p}\)

Substituting the two previous equations in the expression of ΔS yields,

\(ΔS = N_1 R \ln \biggl(\frac{N_1 +N_2}{N_1}\biggr) +N_2 R \ln \biggl(\frac{N_1 +N_2}{N_1}\biggr)\).

According to expression (8.35), the ideal gas concentrations are defined as,

\(c_A = \frac{N_A}{\sum\limits_{B=1}^{r}{N_B} }\).

\(c_1 = \frac{N_1 }{N_1 +N_2}\) and \(c_2 = \frac{N_2 }{N_1 +N_2}\)

Substituting the two previous equations in the expression of ΔS yields,

\(ΔS = −N_1 R \ln (c_1) − N_2 R \ln (c_2) = −R \ln (c^{N_1}_1 c^{N_2}_2)\).

Since the concentrations \(0 < c_1 < 1\) and \(0,< c_2 < 1\),

\(\ln (c^{N_1}_1 c^{N_2}_2) <0\) ⇒ ΔS > 0

Thus, the entropy increases during the mixing.

4. According to equations (4.31) and (4.38) for an isothermal process, the Gibbs free energy variation ΔG is related to the enthalpy variation ΔH

dH = dU + pdV + Vdp

dG = dU − TdS − SdT + pdV + Vdp

\(ΔG = ΔH − TΔS − SΔT = −TΔS = R T \ln (c^{N1}_1 c^{N2}_2) < 0\).

Thus, the Gibbs free energy variation during an isothermal mixing is negative.
Moreover, the previous inequality means that the mixture at constant pressure and temperature is a spontaneous process.
