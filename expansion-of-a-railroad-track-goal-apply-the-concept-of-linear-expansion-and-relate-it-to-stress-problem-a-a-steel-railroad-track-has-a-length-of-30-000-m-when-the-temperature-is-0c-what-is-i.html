Expansion of a Railroad

GOAL Apply the concept of linear expansion and relate it to stress.

PROBLEM (a) A steel railroad track has a length of 30.000 m when the temperature is 0°C. What is its length on a hot day when the temperature is 40.0°C? (b) Suppose the track is nailed down so that it can’t expand. What stress results in the track due to the temperature change?

STRATEGY (a) Apply the linear expansion equation, using Table 10.1 and Equation 10.4. (b) A track that cannot expand by \(\Delta L\) due to external constraints is equivalent to compressing the track by \(\Delta L\), creating a stress in the track. Using the equation relating tensile stress to tensile strain together with the linear expansion equation, the amount of (compressional) stress can be calculated using Equation 9.5.

\(\Delta L=\alpha L_{0} \Delta T\) [10.4]

or

\(L-L_{0}=\alpha L_{0}\left(T-T_{0}\right)\) [10.4]

 

\(\frac{F}{A}=Y \frac{\Delta L}{L_{0}}\) [9.5]

Admin
Solution

(a) Find the length of the track at 40.0°C.

Substitute given quantities into Equation 10.4, finding the change in length: \(\begin{aligned}\Delta L &=\alpha L_{0} \Delta T=\left[11 \times 10^{-6}\left({ }^{\circ} C \right)^{-1}\right](30.000 m )\left(40.0^{\circ} C \right) \\&=0.013 m\end{aligned}\)

 

Add the change to the original length to find the final length: \(L=L_{0}+\Delta L=30.013 m\)

 

(b) Find the stress if the track cannot expand.

Substitute into Equation 9.5 to find the stress: \(\begin{aligned}\frac{F}{A} &=Y \frac{\Delta L}{L}=\left(2.00 \times 10^{11} Pa \right)\left(\frac{0.013 m }{30.0 m }\right) \\&=8.7 \times 10^{7} Pa\end{aligned}\)

 

REMARKS Repeated heating and cooling is an important part of the weathering process that gradually wears things out, weakening structures over time.
