\( v_{1}=v_{f}+x\left(v_{g} v_{f}\right)=0.0011+(0.9)(1.164-0.0011)=1.048 m ^{3} / kg \).

The initial volume contained in the cylinder is \( V_{1}=v_{1} m=(1.048)(0.01) \)=
0.01048 m³. The height H can now be calculated as follows:

\( V_{1}=\frac{\pi d^{2}}{4} H \quad 0.01048=\frac{\pi(0.2)^{2}}{4} H \quad \therefore H=0.334 m \).

The final volume is then

\( V_{2}=\frac{\pi d^{2}}{4}(H+0.157)=\frac{\pi(0.2)^{2}}{4}(0.334+0.157)=0.01543 m ^{3} \).

The final specific volume is

\( v_{2}=\frac{V_{2}}{m}=\frac{0.01543}{0.01}=1.543 m ^{3} / kg \).

The final pressure is

\( P_{2}=P_{1}+\frac{K x}{\pi d^{2} / 4}=150000+\frac{(50000)(0.057)}{\pi(0.2)^{2} / 4}=400000 Pa \quad \text { or } \quad 0.40 MPa \).
