Guest Question.

A nichrome wire having a resistivity of 110 µΩ cm is to be used as heating element. The wire diameter is 2 mm and other design features are Current, I = 25 A, Ambient temperature, \(T_∞ = 20°C\) knichrome = 17.5 W/m.K, Convection coefficient,\( h = 46.5 W/m^2.K\) Calculate the heat loss from one metre long heater and also the temperature at the surface and centre line of nichrome wire.

Admin
Solution

Given : A nichrome wire heating element
\(ρ = 110 µΩ cm = 110 × 10^{–8} Ωm,\)
\(d = 2 mm = 2 × 10^{–3} m,\)
or \(r_o = 1 × 10^{–3} m\)
I = 25 A,
\(h = 46.5 W/m^2.K\)
k = 17.5 W/m.K,
\(T_∞ = 20°C,\)
L = 1 m.

To find :
(i) Heat flow rate from 1 m long wire.
(ii) Temperature at the surface of wire.
(iii) Temperature at the centre line of wire.
Assumptions :
(i) Steady state conditions.
(ii) Heat transfer in radial direction only.
(iii) Constant properties.
Analysis : (i) Heat flow rate : The resistance per metre length of wire
\(R_e = \frac{ρL}{A_c}=\frac{(110 × 10^{-8}Ωm) × (1 m)}{(\pi/4) ×(2 × 10^{-3} m)} \)

= 0.35 Ω/m
The heat generated
\(Q_g =I^2R_e\)
\(= (25 A)^2 × (0.35 Ω/m)\)
= 218.83 W/m.
Under steady state conditions, heat generation rate is always equal to heat dissipation rate from the wire
\(Q_g = Q = 218.83 W/m.\)
(ii) Temperature at the surface of wire :
The temperature distribution in the wire (a long solid cylinder) can be expressed as

\(T(r) = – \frac{g_o r^2}{4k} + C_1 ln (r) + C_2\) …(i)
Subjected to boundary conditions At the centre of wire,
\(\left \{ \frac{dT}{dr} \right \}_{r=0}=0\)
and at \(r = r_o\)
\(– k \left ( \frac{dT}{dr} \right )_{r = r_o} = h(T_{r = r_o} – T_{\infty })\)
We get the temperature distribution in the wire as obtained by eqn. (4.32)
\(T(r) – T_∞ = \frac{g_o }{4k}(r_{o}^2 – r^2) + \frac{g_o r_{o}}{2h}\)

where \(g_o = \frac{Q_g}{V}= \frac{218.83}{(\pi/4) ×(2 × 10^{-3})^2 ×1}\)

\(= 69.655 × 106 W/m^3\)
Substituting the values for temperature distribution,
\(T(r) – 20 =\frac{69.655 × 10^6}{4 × 17.5}× [(1 × 10^{–3})^2 – r^2] + \frac{69.655 × 10^6}{ 2 × 46.5}\)

\(T(r) = 995071.42 [(1 × 10^{–3})^2 – r^2] + 769\)
Temperature at the surface \((r = r_o)\)
\(T_s = 769°C.\)
(iii) Temperature at the centre (r = 0)
\(T_c= 995071.42 (1 × 10^{–3})^2 + 769\)
= 770°C.
