Guest Question.

(a) Show that the function

\(f( x )=x_{1}^{2}+2 x_{1} x_{2}+2 x_{2}^{2}+2 x_{1}+x_{2}\)

has a minimum. (b) Find the minimum of the function using Algorithm 1 with \(x _{0}=\left[\begin{array}{ll}0 & 0\end{array}\right]^{T}\) as initial point.

Admin
Solution

(a) From basic calculus, the stationary points of a function are the points at which the gradient is equal to zero. If the Hessian at a specific stationary point is positive definite, negative definite, or indefinite, then the stationary point is a minimum, maximum, or saddle point; alternatively, if the Hessian is positive or negative semidefinite, then the stationary point can be either a maximum or a minimum point.

The partial derivatives of f (x) are given by

 

\(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x_{1}}=2 x_{1}+2 x_{2}+2 \quad \text { and } \quad \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_{2}}=2 x_{1}+4 x_{2}+1\)

 

At a stationary point \(\tilde{ X }\), the gradient g is zero; hence, we obtain \(\tilde{ x }=\left[\begin{array}{ll}-1.5 & 0.5\end{array}\right]^{T}\).

The Hessian can be deduced as

 

\(H =\left[\begin{array}{ll}2 & 2 \\2 & 4\end{array}\right]\)

 

Since the principal minor determinants of H are positive, the Hessian is positive definite (see Sec. 5.3.6), and so Ëœx is a minimum point.

 

(b) The gradient at \(x _{0}^{T}=\left[\begin{array}{ll}0 & 0\end{array}\right] \text { is } g _{0}=\left[\begin{array}{ll}2 & 1\end{array}\right]^{T}\). The inverse of \(H _{0}\) is given by

 

\(H _{0}^{-1}=\left[\begin{array}{cr}1 & -0.5 \\-0.5 & 0.5\end{array}\right]\)

 

and hence the Newton direction can be obtained from step 3 of Algorithm 1 as \(d_{0}=- H _{0}^{-1} g _{0}=\left[\begin{array}{ll}-1.5 & 0.5\end{array}\right]^{T}\). The function under consideration is quadratic and the solution can be obtained with \(\alpha_{0}=1\). From step 5, \(x _{1}=\breve{ x }=\left[\begin{array}{ll}-1.5 & 0.5\end{array}\right]^{T} \text { and } f(\breve{ x })=f_{1}=-1.25\). Note that Algorithm 1 will need two iterations to stop since the termination test in step 6 will not be satisfied until the second iteration.
