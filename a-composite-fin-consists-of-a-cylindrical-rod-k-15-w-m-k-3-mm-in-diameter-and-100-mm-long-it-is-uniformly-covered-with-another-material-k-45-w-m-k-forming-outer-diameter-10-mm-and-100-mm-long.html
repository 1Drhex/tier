Guest Question.

A composite fin consists of a cylindrical rod (k = 15 W/m.K) 3 mm in diameter and 100 mm long. It is uniformly covered with another material (k = 45 W/m.K) forming outer diameter 10 mm and 100 mm long. It is exposed into an ambient with \(h = 12 W/m^2.K.\)
(i) Derive an expression for the efficiency of this fin and its value for given data.
(ii) Calculate effectiveness of the composite fin.
Assume heat coduction in axial direction only and tip of fin as insulated.

 

Admin
Solution

Given : A composite fin as shown in Fig. 5.36.
\(k_1 = 15 W/m.K, k_2 = 45 W/m.K,\)
\(d_1 = 3 mm, d_2 = 10 mm,\)
\(L = 100 mm, h = 12 W/m^2.K.\)

To find :
(i) An expression for fin efficiency and its calculation.
(ii) Fin effectiveness.

Assumptions :
(i) Steady state conduction in axial direction only.
(ii) Even though the fin of composite material, the temperature at any cross-section in the fin is uniform.
(iii) No contact resistance at interface of two materials.
(iv) No heat generation within the fin.
(v) Temperature at free end of fin approaches to \(T_∞.\)
Analysis : (i) Consider a differential element of composite fin of thickness dx at a distance x from the base.
Heat conducted into element by both fins
\(Q_{1x} + Q_{2x}\)
Heat conducted out the element
\(= Q_1(x + dx) + Q_2 (x + dx)\)
Heat dissipation by convection from outer surface element
\(Q_{conv} = h (Pdx) (T – T_∞),\)
where \(P = πd_2 = π × 0.01 = 0.01π m\)
For steady state conditions, the energy balance yields
\(Q_{1x} + Q_{2x} =Q_1(x + dx) + Q_2(x + dx) + h (Pdx) (T – T_∞)\)
or\( Q_{1x} + Q_{2x} =Q_{1x} +\frac{d}{dx} (Q_{1x}) dx + Q_{2x} + \frac{d}{dx}(Q_{2x}) dx + hP dx (T – T_∞)\)
or \(\frac{d}{dx} \left ( -k_1A_1 \frac{dT}{dx} \right )dx + \frac{d}{dx} \left( -k_2 A_2 \frac{dT}{dx} \right )dx + hP dx (T – T_∞) = 0\)
Since the cross-section areas and thermal conductivities are constant. Thus,
\(– k_1A_1 \frac{d^2T}{dx^2} – k_2A_2 \frac{d^2T}{dx^2} + hP (T – T_∞) = 0\)
or \((k_1A_1 + k_2A_2) \frac{d^2T}{dx^2} + hP (T – T_∞) = 0\)
Introducing \(θ = T – T_∞,\) then
\(\frac{d^2θ}{dx^2} – \frac{hP}{k_1A_1 + k_2A_2} θ =0\)

Using \(m^2 = \frac{hP}{k_1A_1 + k_2A_2}\)

Then \(\frac{d^2θ}{dx^2} – m^2θ = 0\)

It is the second order differential equation for composite fin and its solution is
\(θ =C_1e^{–mx} + C_2e^{mx}.\)
Cross-sectional areas :
\(A_1 = \frac{π}{4} d_{1}^{2} = \frac{π}{4}× (0.003)^2\)
\(= 7.0686 × 10^{–6} m^2\)
\(A_2 = \frac{π}{4} (d_2^2 – d_1^2) = \frac{π}{4} × (0.01^2 – 0.003^2)\)
\(= 7.147 × 10^{–5} m^2\)
For insulated tip fin, the efficiency is given by
\(η_{fin} = \frac{tanh mL}{mL}\)
where \(m = \sqrt{\frac{hP}{k_1A_1+k_2A_2}}\)
\(=\sqrt{\frac{12 × 0.01 \pi}{15 × 7.0686 × 10^{-6}+45 ×7.147 ×10^{-5}}}\)
= 10.652
\(η_{fin} = \frac{tanh ( 10.652 × 0.1 )}{10.652 × 0.1 }\)

= 0.7394 = 73.94%.
(ii) Fin effectiveness
\( ε_{fin} =\frac{ Q_{fin}}{Q_{no fin}}\)
\(Q_{fin}= Q_{inner fin} + Q_{outer fin}\)
\(Q_{inner fin} = k_1A_1 \frac{T_0 – T_ ∞}{L}\)

\(= \frac{15 × 7.0686 × 10^{-6}(T_0 – T_ ∞) }{0.1}\)
\(= 0.00106 (T_0 – T_∞)\)
\(Q_{outer fin} = \sqrt{h Pk_2 A_2} (T_0 – T_∞)\) tanh mL
where \(m = \sqrt{\frac{hP}{k_1A_2}}\)
\(=\sqrt{\frac{12 × 0.01 \pi}{45 ×7.147 ×10^{-5}}} = 10.826\)
Then
\(Q_{outer fin} = \sqrt{12 × 0.01\pi × 45 × 7.147 ×10^{-5}} × (T_0 – T_∞) tanh (10.826 × 0.1)\)
\(= 0.0276(T_0 – T_∞)\)
\(Q_{fin} = 0.00106 (T_0 – T_∞) + 0.0276 (T_0 – T_∞)\)
\(= 0.0285 (T_0 – T_∞)\)

The heat transfer rate from corresponding base surface, without any fin.
\(Q_{no fin} = hA_{no fin} (T_0 – T_∞)\)
\(= h × (π/4) × d_2^2× (T_0 – T_∞)\)
\(= 12 × (π/4) × (0.01)^2 × (T_0 – T_∞)\)
\(= 0.0009425 (T_0 – T_∞)\)
\(ε_{fin} = \frac{0.0285}{0.0009425} = 30.24.\)
