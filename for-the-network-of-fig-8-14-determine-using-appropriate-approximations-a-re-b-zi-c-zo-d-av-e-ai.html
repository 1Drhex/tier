Guest Question.

For the network of Fig. 8.14, determine (using appropriate approximations):
(a) \(r_{e}\) .
(b) \(Z_{i}\) .
(c) \(Z_{o}\) .
(d) \(A_{v}\) .
(e) \(A_{i}\).

Admin
Solution

(a) Testing \(\beta R_{E} \gt 10 R_{2}\)

\((210)(0.68 k \Omega) \gt 10 (10 k \Omega)\)

 

\(142.8 k \Omega \gt 100 k \Omega\) (satisfied)

 

\(V_{B} = \frac{R_{2}}{R_{1} + R_{2}}V_{CC} = \frac{10 k \Omega}{90 k \Omega + 10 k \Omega}(16 V) = 1.6 V\)

 

\(V_{E} = V_{B} – V_{BE} = 1.6 V – 0.7 V = 0.9 V\)

 

\(I_{E} = \frac{V_{E}}{R_{E}} = \frac{0.9 V}{0.68 k \Omega} = 1.324 m A\)

 

\(r_{e} = \frac{26 m V}{I_{E}} = \frac {26 m V}{1.324 m A} = 19.64 \Omega\)

(b) The ac equivalent circuit is provided in Fig. 8.15. The resulting configuration is now different from Fig. 8.11 only by the fact that now

\(R_{B} = R^{â€²} = R_{1} \parallel R_{2} = 9 k \Omega\)

The testing conditions of \(r_{o} \geq 10(R_{C} +R_{E})\) and \(r_{o} \geq 10R_{C}\) are both satisfied. Using the appropriate approximations yields

\(Z_{b} \cong \beta R_{E} = 142.8 k \Omega\)

 

\(Z_{i} = R_{B} \parallel Z_{b} = 9 k \Omega \parallel 142.8 k \Omega= 8.47 k \Omega\)

(c) \(Z_{o} = R_{C} = 2.2 k \Omega\)

(d) \(A_{v} = – \frac{R_{C}}{R_{E}} = – \frac{2.2 k \Omega}{0.68 k \Omega} = – 3.24\)

(e) \(A_{i} = – A_{v} \frac{Z_{i}}{R_{C}} = – (- 3.24)\left(\frac{8.47 k \Omega}{2.2 k \Omega} \right) = 12.47\)
