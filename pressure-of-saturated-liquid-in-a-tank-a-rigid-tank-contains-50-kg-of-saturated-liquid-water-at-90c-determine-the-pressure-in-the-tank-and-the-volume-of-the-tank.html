Guest Question.

Pressure of Saturated Liquid in a Tank

A rigid tank contains 50 kg of saturated liquid water at 90°C. Determine the pressure in the tank and the volume of the tank.

Admin
Solution

A rigid tank contains saturated liquid water. The pressure and volume of the tank are to be determined.

Analysis The state of the saturated liquid water is shown on a \(T\) -v diagram in Fig. 3-28. Since saturation conditions exist in the tank, the pressure must be the saturation pressure at \(90^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\) :

\(P=P_{\text {sat } @ 90^{\circ} \mathrm{C}}=70.183 \mathrm{kPa}\) (Table A-4)

The specific volume of the saturated liquid at \(90^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\) is

\(v=v_{f @ 90^{\circ} \mathrm{C}}=0.001036 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{kg}\) (Table A-4)

Then the total volume of the tank becomes

\(V=m v=(50 \mathrm{~kg})\left(0.001036 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{kg}\right)=0.0518 \mathrm{~m}^{3}\)
