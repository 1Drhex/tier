Guest Question.

Kerosene at \( 20^{\circ} C \) C flows from reservoir A to reservoir B through two galvanized iron pipes in series, as illustrated in Figure EP 8.17. The pipe system contains two reservoirs, two single pipes, and one gradual pipe contraction/nozzle with a conical angle, θ = \( 30^{\circ} \) and thus k= 0.02. The pipe entrance from reservoir A is reentrant, and the pipe exit to reservoir B is square-edged. The first pipe is 0.2 m in diameter and 900 m long, and the second pipe is 0.1 m in diameter and 1200m long. The difference in elevation between points A and B is 50 m. Assume the Darcy–Weisbach friction factor is chosen to model the flow resistance. (a) Determine the velocity in each pipe, and the discharge of the flow in the pipes (b) Determine the flow regime. (c) Determine the total head loss due to pipe friction and pipe components between points A and B.

Admin
Solution

(a) Assuming the datum is at point B, the velocity of flow in each pipe is computed by applying the energy equation between points A and B as follows:

\( \frac{P_{A}}{\gamma } + Z_{A} + \frac{V^{2}_{A}}{2g} – h_{f1maj} – h_{f2maj} – h_{fent} – h_{fgpc} – h_{fexit} = \frac{P_{B}}{\gamma } + Z_{B} + \frac{V^{2}_{B}}{2g} \)

The discharge is computed from the continuity equation. The major head
loss due to pipe friction for each pipe is computed from Equation 8.34 \( h_{f} = \frac{\tau _{w}L}{\gamma R_{h}} = \frac{\Delta p}{\gamma } = S_{f}L = \frac{v^{2}L}{C^{2}R_{h}} = \frac{v^{2}L}{\left(\frac{8g}{f} \right) \left(\frac{D}{4} \right) } = f \frac{L}{D} \frac{v^{2}}{2g} \) (Darcy–Weisbach head loss equation) as a Type 1 problem, where the governing flowrate equation, \( Q = Q_{1} = Q_{2} \) establishes the relative velocities and thus the relative head losses in each of the two pipes in series, where \( h_{ftotal} = \sum{h_{fmaj}} + \sum{h_{fmin}} \). The absolute pipe roughness for galvanized iron is given in Table 8.3, and the Colebrook equation for the friction factor is assumed. The minor head loss coefficients and minor head losses are determined for the following pipe components: the reentrant entrance from reservoir A (see Figure 8.10a), the gradual pipe contraction/nozzle with a conical angle, \( \theta = 30^{\circ} \) and thus, k = 0.02, and the square-edged pipe exit to reservoir B (see Figure 8.13b). The density, ρ and the viscosity, μ for kerosene at \( 20^{\circ} C \) are given in Table A.4 in Appendix A. Thus, in total, 12 equations are solved simultaneously for 12 unknowns as follows:

\( D_{1} : = 0.2 m \) \( A_{1} : = \frac{\pi .D^{2}_{1}}{4} = 0.031 m^{2} \) \( L_{1}: = 900 m \)

\( D_{2} : = 0.1 m \) \( A_{2} : = \frac{\pi .D^{2}_{2}}{4} =7.854 \times 10^{-3} m^{2} \) \( L_{2}: = 1200 m \)

\( \varepsilon : = 0.15 mm \) \( \frac{\varepsilon }{D_{1}} = 7.5 \times 10^{-4} \) \( \frac{\varepsilon }{D_{2}} = 1.5 \times 10^{-3} \)

\( \rho : = 808 \frac{kg}{m^{3}} \) \( \mu : = 1.92 \times 10^{-3} N \frac{sec}{m^{2}} \) \( g: = 9.81 \frac{m}{sec^{2}} \)

\( \gamma : = \rho .g = 7.926 \times 10^{3} \frac{kg}{m^{2}.S^{2}} \) \( P_{A} : = 0 \frac{N}{m^{2}} \) \( P_{B} : = 0 \frac{N}{m^{2}} \)

\( Z_{A} : = 50 m \) \( Z_{B} : = 0 m \) \( V_{A}: = 0 \frac{m}{sec} \) \( V_{B}: = 0 \frac{m}{sec} \)

\( k_{ent}: = 1 \) \( k_{gpc}: = 0.02 \) \( k_{exit}: = 1 \)

Guess values: \( f_{1}: = 0.01 \) \( R_{1} = 400.000 \) \( V_{1}: = 1 \frac{m}{sec} \)

\( f_{2}: = 0.01 \) \( R_{2} = 400.000 \) \( V_{2}: = 2 \frac{m}{sec} \)

\( Q: = 1 \frac{m^{3}}{sec} \) \( h_{f1}: = 10 m \) \( h_{f2}: = 10 m \)

\( h_{fent}: = 1 m \) \( h_{fgpc}: = 1 m \) \( h_{fexit}: = 1 m \)

Given

\( \frac{P_{A}}{\gamma } + Z_{A} + \frac{V^{2}_{A}}{2g} – h_{f1} – h_{f2} – h_{fent} – h_{fgpc} – h_{fexit} = \frac{P_{B}}{\gamma } + Z_{B} + \frac{V^{2}_{B}}{2g} \)

\( \frac{1}{\sqrt{f_{1}} } = – 2.log \left(\frac{\varepsilon /D_{1}}{3.7} + \frac{2.51}{R_{1}.\sqrt{f_{1}} } \right) \) \( R_{1} = \frac{\rho .V_{1}.D_{1}}{\mu }\)

\( \frac{1}{\sqrt{f_{2}} } = – 2.log \left(\frac{\varepsilon /D_{2}}{3.7} + \frac{2.51}{R_{2}.\sqrt{f_{2}} } \right) \) \( R_{2} = \frac{\rho .V_{2}.D_{2}}{\mu } \)

\( h_{f1} = f_{1}. \frac{L_{1}}{D_{1}} \frac{V^{2}_{1}}{2.g} \) \( h_{f2} = f_{2}. \frac{L_{2}}{D_{2}} \frac{V^{2}_{2}}{2.g} \) \( h_{fent} = k_{ent} \frac{V^{2}_{1}}{2.g} \)

\( h_{fgpc} = k_{gpc} \frac{V^{2}_{2}}{2.g} \) \( h_{fexit} = k_{exit} \frac{V^{2}_{2}}{2.g} \) \( V_{1} = \frac{Q}{A_{1}} \) \( V_{2} = \frac{Q}{A_{2}} \)
\( \left ( \begin{matrix} F_{1} \\ F_{2} \\ R_{1} \\ R_{2} \\ V_{1} \\ V_{2} \\ Q \\ h_{f_{1}} \\ h_{f_{2}} \\ h_{fent} \\ h_{fgpc} \\ h_{fexit} \end{matrix} \right ) : = Find (F_{1}, F_{2}, R_{1}, R_{2}, V_{1}, V_{2} ,Q, h_{f_{1}}, h_{f_{2}}, h_{fent}, h_{fgpc}, h_{fexit} ) \)

\( F_{1} = 0.024 \) \( F_{2} = 0.024\) \( R_{1} = 3.811 \times 10^{4} \) \( R_{2}= 7.621 \times 10^{4} \)
\( V_{1} = 0.453 \frac{m}{s} \) \( V_{2}= 1.811 \frac{m}{s} \) \( Q = 0.014 \frac{m^{3}}{s} \)
\( h_{f_{1}}= 1.145 m \) \( h_{f_{2}}= 48.674 m\)
\( h_{fent}= 0.01 m \) \( h_{fgpc} = 3.343 \times 10^{-3} m\) \( h_{fexit} = 0.167 m\)

(b) Thus, because the Reynolds number, R is greater than 4000, the flow is turbulent.
(c) The total head loss due to pipe friction and pipe components between points A and B is computed as follows:

\( h_{ftotal}: = h_{f_{1}} + h_{f_{2}} + h_{fent} + h_{fgpc} + h_{fexit} = 50 m \)

where the potential energy, \( z_{A} = 50 \) m is converted into the total head loss, \( h_{ftotal} = 50 m \) .
