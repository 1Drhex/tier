Guest Question.

Complex Power. The circuit shown in Figure 11.5-3 consists of a source driving a load. The current source current is
i(t) = 1.25 cos (5t – 15°) A
(a) What is the value of the complex power delivered by the source to the load when R = 20 Ω and L = 3 H?
(b) What are the values of the resistance R and inductance L when the source delivers 11.72 + j11.72 VA to the load?

Admin
Solution

Represent the circuit in the frequency domain as shown in Figure 11.5-4, where I = 1.25 ∠-15° A. The equivalent impedance of the parallel resistor and inductor is

\(Z = \frac{j\omega LR}{R + j\omega L}\)

(a) When R = 20 Ω and L = 3 H, the equivalent impedance is

\(Z = \frac{j300}{20 + j15} = 12 \angle 53^\circ \Omega\)

The voltage across this impedance is

V = IZ = (1.25 ∠-15°)(12 ∠53°) = 15 ∠38° V

The complex power delivered by the source is

\(S = \frac{VI^*}{2} = \frac{(15 \angle 38^\circ)(1.25 \angle -15^\circ)^*}{2} = \frac{(15 \angle 38^\circ)(1.25 \angle 15^\circ)}{2} = 9.375 \angle 53^\circ VA\)

(b) The voltage across the equivalent impedance can be calculated from the complex power and the current, using

\(S = \frac{VI^*}{2} ⇒ V = \frac{2S}{I^*}\)

When S = 11.72 + j11.72 = 16.57 ∠45° VA

\(V = \frac{2S}{I^*} = \frac{2(16.57 \angle 45^\circ)}{(1.25 \angle -15^\circ)^*} = \frac{2(16.57 \angle 45^\circ)}{1.25 \angle 15^\circ} = 26.52 \angle 30^\circ V\)

The equivalent impedance is

\(Z = \frac{V}{I} = \frac{26.52 \angle 30^\circ}{1.25 \angle -15^\circ} = 21.21 \angle 45^\circ \Omega\)

It’s convenient to take the reciprocal:

\(\frac{1}{R} – j \frac{1}{\omega L} = \frac{1}{21.21 \angle 45^\circ} = 0.033338 – j0.033338\)

Consequently,

\(R = \frac{1}{0.033338} = 30 \Omega and 5L = \frac{1}{0.033338} = 30 ⇒ L = 6 H\)
