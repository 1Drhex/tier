Guest Question.

Let the discrete-time signal:

\(x[n]=\begin{cases} 1, & |n|\leq k \\0,& |n|>k\end{cases}\)

a. Compute the DFT of N length of the signal x[n], where N > 2k + 1.

b. Plot the magnitude and phase of the DFT when k = 2 and N = 12.

c. Estimate the energy of the signal contained between \(f_{1} = 1kHz\) and \(f_{2} = 3kHz\), if the sampling frequency \(f_{s}\) is 8kHz.

Admin
Solution

a. From the signal, it is derived:

\(x[n]=\begin{cases} 1, & |n|\leq k \\0,& |n|>k\end{cases} \Rightarrow x[n-k]=\begin{cases} 1, & 0\leq n\leq 2k \\0,& elsewhere\end{cases}\)

It is true that:

\(x[n-k]\overset{F(DFT)}{\longrightarrow }w^{+km}X[m],w\overset{\wedge }{=}e^{-j\frac{2\pi }{N} }\)
\(\Rightarrow X[m]\leftarrow w^{-km}F_{DFT}\left\{x[n-k]\right\}\Rightarrow DFT\left\{x[n-k]\right\}=\sum\limits_{n=0}^{N-1}{w^{mn}x[n-k]}=\sum\limits_{n=0}^{2k}{w^{mn}}\)
\(\Rightarrow DFT\left\{x[n-k]\right\}=\frac{w^{(2k+1)m}-1}{w^{m}-1}\Rightarrow X[m]=w^{-km}\frac{w^{(k+\frac{1}{2} )m}[w^{(k+\frac{1}{2} )m}-w^{-(k+\frac{1}{2} )m}]}{w^{\frac{m}{2} }[w^{\frac{m}{2} }-w^{-\frac{m}{2} }]}\)
\(\Rightarrow X[m]=\frac{2j.\sin [(2k+1)\frac{\pi m}{N} ]}{2j.\sin (\frac{\pi m}{N} )}=\frac{\sin [(2k+1)\frac{\pi m}{N} ]}{\sin (\frac{\pi m}{N} )},m=0,1,2,..,(N-1)\)

Another way of calculating X[m] is:

\(X[m]=\sum\limits_{n=-k}^{+k}{w^{mn}x[n]}=\sum\limits_{n=-k}^{k}{w^{mn}}=w^{-mk}+..+1+..+w^{mk}\)
\(=w^{-mk}(1+..+w^{2mk})\Rightarrow X[m]=w^{-mk}\frac{w^{(2k+1)m}-1}{w^{m}-1}\)

b. For k = 2 και N = 12, we have:

\(X[m]=\frac{\sin \frac{5\pi m}{12} }{\sin (\frac{\pi m}{12})},m=0,1,2,..,(N-1). \)

It is true that: |X[0] = X[12]|, |X[1] = X[11]|, |X[2] = X[10]| …, etc.
Generally, it is: |X[m] = X[N – m]|.

After some calculations, the following is obtained:

X[0] = 5,X[1] = 3.73,X[2] = 1,X[3] = −1,X[4] = −1,X[5] = 0.268,andX[6] = 1.

c. It is \(F_{1}=\frac{f_{1}}{f_{s}}=\frac{1kHz}{8kHz} =\frac{1}{8},F_{2}=\frac{f_{2}}{f_{s}}=\frac{3kHz}{8kHz}=\frac{3}{8}\).

Thus:\(\Omega _{1}=2\pi F_{1}=2\pi \frac{1}{8}=\frac{\pi }{4}\) and \(\Omega _{2}=2\pi F_{2}=2\pi \frac{3}{8}=\frac{3\pi }{4}\)

From the relations above, it is: \(Ω_{1} ≤ Ω ≤ Ω_{2} ⇒ 2 ≤ m ≤ 4\), hence, the energy of the signal between the frequencies \(f_{1} 1kHz\) and \(f_{2} 3kHz\) is:

\(\Rightarrow E=\frac{1}{N}2\sum\limits_{m=2}^{4}{|X(m)|^{2}}=\frac{1}{6}(1^{2}+1^{2}+1^{2})=\frac{1}{2} \)
