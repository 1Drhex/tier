Guest Question.

An EM wave travels in free space with the electric field component

\(E_{s}=100e^{j(0.866y+0.5z)}a_{x} V/m\)

Determine

(a) \(\omega \) and \(\lambda \)

(b) The magnetic field component

(c) The time average power in the wave

Admin
Solution

(a) Comparing the given E with

\(E_{s}=E_{o}e^{jk\cdot r}=E_{o}e^{j(k_{x}x+k_{y}y+k_{z}z)}a_{x}\)

it is clear that

\(k_{x}=0, k_{y}=0.866, k_{z}=0.5\)

Thus

\(k=\sqrt{k_{x}^{2}+k_{y}^{2}+k_{z}^{2}}=\sqrt{(0.866)^{2}+(0.5)^{2}}=1\)

But in free space

\(k=\beta=\omega\sqrt{\mu_{o}\varepsilon_{o}}=\frac{\omega}{c}=\frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \)

Hence

\(\omega=kc=3\times10^{8} rad/s\)

\(\lambda=\frac{2\pi}{k}=2\pi=6.283 m\)

(b) From eq. (10.106):

\(H=\frac{1}{\omega\mu}k\times E=\frac{a_{k}\times E}{\eta}\)

the corresponding magnetic field is given by

\(H_{s}=\frac{1}{\mu \omega}k\times E_{s}=\frac{(0.866a_{y}+0.5a_{z})}{4\pi\times 10^{-7}\times 3\times 10^{8}}\times 100a_{x}e^{jk\cdot r}\)

or

\(H_{s}=(132.63a_{y}-229.7a_{z})e^{j(0.866y+0.5z)} mA/m\)

(c) Th e time-average power is

\(?_{ave}=\frac{1}{2}Re(E_{s}\times H_{s}^{*})=\frac{E_{o}^{2}}{2\eta}a_{k}\)

\(=\frac{(100)^{2}}{2(120\pi)}(0.866a_{y}+0.5a_{z})=11.49a_{y}+6.631a_{z} W/m^{2}\)
