Guest Question.

In Figure P5.46, the cutaway view shows a solid aluminum alloy [\(L_{2}\) = 600 mm; \(A_{2}=707 mm ^{2}\) ; \(E_{2}\) = 70 GPa] rod (2) within a closed-end bronze [\(L_{1}\) = 610 mm; \(A_{1}=1,206 mm ^{2} ; E_{1}\) = 100 GPa] tube (1). Before the load P is applied, there is a clearance of 2 mm between the rod flange at B and the tube closure at A. After load P is applied, rod (2) stretches enough so that flange B contacts the closed end of the tube at A. If the load applied to the lower end of the aluminum rod is P = 230 kN, calculate
(a) the normal stress in tube (1).
(b) the elongation of tube (1).

Admin
Solution

Equilibrium: Consider a FBD around flange B after the gap has been closed.
Part of the applied load will be carried by the tube and part will be carried by the rod. Sum forces in the vertical direction to obtain:

\(\Sigma F_{y}=F_{1}+F_{2}-P=0\) (a)

Geometry-of-deformation relationship: After the flange contacts the tube end, the rod and the tube will elongate the same amount. However, rod (2) must stretch 2 mm before the rod flange contacts the tube end. Consequently, the total deformation \(\delta_{2}\) of rod (2) is equal to the deformation of the tube (1) plus an additional 2 mm.

\(\delta_{2}=\delta_{1}+2 mm\) (b)

Force-Deformation Relationships:

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}} \quad \delta_{2}=\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}\) (c)

Compatibility Equation
Substitute the force-deformation relationships (c) into the geometry-of-deformation relationship (b) to derive the compatibility equation:

\(\frac{F_{2} L_{2}}{A_{2} E_{2}}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+2 mm\) (d)

Solve the Equations
Solve Eq. (d) for \(F_{2}\):

\(F_{2}=\left[\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}+2 mm \right] \frac{A_{2} E_{2}}{L_{2}}=F_{1}\left(\frac{L_{1}}{L_{2}}\right)\left(\frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}}\right)\left(\frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}\right)+(2 mm ) \frac{A_{2} E_{2}}{L_{2}}\) (e)

and substitute into Eq. (a):

\(\begin{array}{r}F_{1}+F_{1}\left(\frac{L_{1}}{L_{2}}\right)\left(\frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}}\right)\left(\frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}\right)+(2 mm ) \frac{A_{2} E_{2}}{L_{2}}=P \\F_{1}\left[1+\left(\frac{L_{1}}{L_{2}}\right)\left(\frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}}\right)\left(\frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}\right)\right]=P-(2 mm ) \frac{A_{2} E_{2}}{L_{2}} \\F_{1}=\frac{P-(2 mm ) \frac{A_{2} E_{2}}{L_{2}}}{1+\left(\frac{L_{1}}{L_{2}}\right)\left(\frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}}\right)\left(\frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}\right)}\end{array}\)

Substitute values into this equation to obtain:

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{1}=\frac{P-(2 mm ) \frac{A_{2} E_{2}}{L_{2}}}{1+\left(\frac{L_{1}}{L_{2}}\right)\left(\frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}}\right)\left(\frac{E_{2}}{E_{1}}\right)} \\&=\frac{230,000 N -(2 mm ) \frac{\left(707 mm ^{2}\right)\left(70,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}{600 mm }}{1+\left(\frac{610 mm }{600 mm }\right)\left(\frac{707 mm ^{2}}{1,206 mm ^{2}}\right)\left(\frac{70 GPa }{100 GPa }\right)}=\frac{230,000 N -164,966.667 N }{1.417204} \\&=45,888.5 N\end{aligned}\)

(a) Normal stress in tube (1). The normal stress in the tube is thus

\(\sigma_{1}=\frac{45,888.5 N }{1,206 mm ^{2}}=38.1 MPa ( T )\)

(b) Elongation of tube (1). The elongation of the tube is

\(\delta_{1}=\frac{F_{1} L_{1}}{A_{1} E_{1}}=\frac{(45,888.5 N )(610 mm )}{\left(1,206 mm ^{2}\right)\left(100,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)}=0.232 mm \downarrow\)
