Guest Question.

(a) A cable of radius \(r_1\) and resistance\( R_e (Ω/m)\) and carrying a current I(A) is surrounded by an insulator of radius \(r_2\) and thermal conductivity k. The external heat transfer coefficient and air temperature are\( h_o\) and \(T_∞\) , respectively. Derive an expression for temperature distribution in the insulator.
(b) A 1 mm dia. copper wire of resistance 0.02 Ω/m is surrounded by a 2.3 mm dia. plastic coating of k = 0.2 W/m.K. The outside surface of the coating is cooled by air, where the convective heat transfer coefficient is 16 \(W/m^2.K.\) Determine the maximum current, if the surface to air temperature difference is to be limited to 35°C. What is the temperature of the copper wire, if ambient temperature is 25°C ?

Admin
Solution

(a) Given : The insulation system on an electrical cable.
To find : An expression for temperature distribution in the insulation layer.

Assumptions :
(i) Steady state heat conduction.
(ii) Heat conduction in radial direction only.
(iii) 1 m length of the cable and insulation.
Analysis : (a) The temperature distribution in cylinder, eqn. (3.30)
\(T(r) = C_1 log_{e} r + C_2 …(i)\)

With boundary conditions,
\((i) At r = r_1, \frac{Q}{L}= I^2R_e\)
\(= – 2πr_1k \left ( \frac{dT}{dr} \right )_{r=r_1} …(ii)\)
Differentiating eqn. (i) with respect to r
\(\left ( \frac{dT}{dr} \right )_{r=r_1} =\frac{C_1}{r_1}\)
Using in eqn. (ii),
\(I^2R_e = – 2πr k\frac{C_1}{r_1} or C_1= – \frac{ I^2R_e}{2πr k}\)

Then \(T(r) = – \frac{ I^2R_e}{2πr k} ln(r) + C_2\)
(ii) At \(r = r_2, \frac{Q}{L}= I^2R_e\)

\(= h_o(2πr_2)(T_{r=r_2} – T_{\infty })\)

or \(I^2R_e= h_o(2πr_2)\left \{- \frac{ I^2R_e}{2πr k} ln(r_2) +C_2 – T_{\infty } \right \} \)

or \(C_2 =\frac{ I^2 R_e}{2πr_2h_o}+\frac{ I^2R_e}{2πk}ln(r_2)+T_{\infty }\)

Using the values of \(C_1\) and \(C_2\) in eqn. (i), we get
\(T(r) = –\frac{ I^2R_e}{2πk}ln(r) + \frac{ I^2R_e}{2πr_2h_o}+\frac{ I^2R_e}{2πk}ln(r_2)+T_{\infty }\)

\(=\frac{ I^2R_e}{2πk}ln\left ( \frac{r_2}{r} \right ) +\frac{ I^2R_e}{2πr_2h_o}+T_{\infty }\)

or \(T(r) = \frac{ I^2R_e}{2π}\left \{ \frac{ln\left ( \frac{r_2}{r} \right )}{k}+\frac{1}{r_2 h_o} \right \}+T_{\infty }…(iii)\)

(b) Given : \(d_1 = 1 mm, r_1 = 0.5 mm\)
\(d_2 = 2.3 mm, r_2 = 1.15 mm,\)
k = 0.2 W/m.K
\(h = 16 W/m^2.K,\)
\(R_e= 0.02 Ω/m,\)
\(T_s – T_∞ = 35°C,\)
\(T_∞ = 25°C\)
To find :
(i) Maximum current carrying conductor.
(ii) Temperature of copper wire.
Analysis : (i) Rearranging eqn. (iii),
\(I^2 =\frac{2\pi (T – T_{\infty })}{R_e\left \{ \frac{ln\left (r_2/r \right )}{k}+\frac{1}{r_2 h_o} \right \}}\)
At \( r = r_2,\) (surface of insulation)

\(I^2=\frac{2\pi \times 35}{0.02 \times \left \{ \frac{ln\left (1.15/1.15 \right )}{0.2}+\frac{1}{1.15 \times 10^{-3} \times 16} \right \}}\)
\(\frac{70 \pi }{0.02 \times 54.35}=202.32\)
or I = 14.22 A.
(ii) Temperature of copper wire i.e., at \(r = r_1.\)
Using eqn. (iii)
\(T_1 =\frac{(14.22)^{2} \times 0.02 }{2\pi }\times \left \{ \frac{ln\left ( \frac{1.15}{0.5} \right )}{0.2} +\frac{1}{1.15 \times 10^{-3} \times 16} \right \}+25\)
= 0.6436 × (4.164 + 54.35) + 25 = 62.66°C.
