Guest Question.

Determine the inverse of the matrix shown using the Gauss–Jordan method.

\(A=\left[\begin{matrix} 13 & -6 &6 \\ -6 & 12 & -1 \\ 6 & -1 & 9\end{matrix} \right]\)

Admin
Solution

The augmented matrix is given by

\(G= [A \overset{:}{:} I] = G=\left[\begin{matrix} 13 &-6 &6\overset{:}{:} &1 &0&0 \\-6 & 12& -1\overset{:}{:} &0 &1&0 \\ 6 & -1&9\overset{:}{:}&0 &0&1 \end{matrix} \right]\) (1)

We begin the Gauss–Jordan elimination process by dividing row 1 of the augmented matrix by \(G_{11}\) = 13:

\(G=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 &-0.4615 &0.4615\overset{:}{:} & 0.07692 &0&0 \\-6 & 12& -1\overset{:}{:} &0 &1&0 \\ 6 & -1&9\overset{:}{:}&0 &0&1 \end{matrix} \right]\) (2)

Next, we multiply row 1 by \(G_{21}\) = −6 and subtract it from row 2, and then multiply row 1 by \(G_{31}\) = 6 and subtract it from row 3. This yields

\(G=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 &-0.4615 &0.4615\overset{:}{:} & 0.07692 &0&0 \\0 & 9.231& 1.769\overset{:}{:} &0.4615 &1&0 \\0 & 1.769&6.231\overset{:}{:}&-0.4615 &0&1 \end{matrix} \right]\) (3)

Dividing row 2 by \(G_{22}\) = 9.231, we obtain

\(G=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 &-0.4615 &0.4615\overset{:}{:} & 0.07692 &0&0 \\0 & 1& 0.1916\overset{:}{:} &0.04999 &0.1083&0 \\0 &1.769&6.231\overset{:}{:}&-0.4615 &0&1 \end{matrix} \right]\) (4)

Next, we multiply row 2 by \(G_{12}\) =-0.4615 and subtract it from row 1; then multiply row 2 by \(G_{32}\) =1.769 and subtract it from row 3. This yields

\(G=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 &0 &0.5499\overset{:}{:} & 0.09999 &0.04998&0 \\0 & 1& 0.1916\overset{:}{:} &0.04999 &0.1083&0 \\0 & 0&5.892\overset{:}{:}&-0.5499 &-0.1916&1 \end{matrix} \right]\) (5)

Divide row 3 by \(G_{22}\)=5.892

\(G=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 &0 &0.5499\overset{:}{:} & 0.09999 &0.04998&0 \\0 & 1& 0.1916\overset{:}{:} &0.04999 &0.1083&0 \\0 & 0&1\overset{:}{:}&-0.09333 &-0.03252&0.1697 \end{matrix} \right]\) (6)

Multiply row 3 by \(G_{13}\) = 0.5499 and subtract it from row 1; then multiply row 3 by \(G_{23}\) = 0.1916 and subtract it from row 2 to obtain

\(G=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 &0 &0\overset{:}{:} & 0.1513 &0.06787&-0.09333 \\0 & 1& 0\overset{:}{:} &0.06787 &0.1145&-0.03252 \\0 & 0&1\overset{:}{:}&-0.09333 &-0.03252&0.1697 \end{matrix} \right]\) (7)

Thus, the inverse of the given matrix A is

\(A^{-1}=\left[\begin{matrix} 0.1513 & 0.06787 & -0.09333 \\0.06787 & 0.1145 & -0.03252 \\ -0.09333 &-0.03252 & 0.1697 \end{matrix} \right]\)

Finally, we check our computations by using the relationship A\(A^{-1}\) = I:

\(AA^{-1}=\left[\begin{matrix} 13 & -6 & 6 \\ -6 & 12 & -1 \\ 6 & -1 & 9 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} 0.1513 & 0.06787 & -0.09333 \\0.06787 & 0.1145 & -0.03252 \\ -0.09333 &-0.03252 & 0.1697 \end{matrix} \right]\)

\(=\left[\begin{matrix} 0.9997 & 0.0002 & 0 \\ 0 & 0.9993 &0\\ 0 & 0 & 0.9998 \end{matrix} \right]\approx I\)
