Guest Question.

A liquid stripping process is used to transfer hydrogen sulfide ( \( H_{2} S \)) dissolved in water into a contacting air stream. The solvent (water) and carrier gas (air) are assumed to be immiscible, and the solvent is assumed to be nonvolatile. At the present conditions of operation, the composition of \( H_{2} S \) in the bulk gas phase is 1.0 mole %, and in the liquid phase is 0.0006 mole %, or \( 112 mg H_{2} S / L \). The individual mass-transfer coefficients are \( k_{x} = 0.30 kgmole / m ^{2} \cdot s \) for the liquid film, and \( k_{y} = 4.5 \times 10^{-3} kgmole / m ^{2} \cdot s \) for the gas film. The temperature is \( 20^{\circ} C \), and the total system pressure is 1.5 atm. The density of the liquid approaches the density of liquid water at \( 20^{\circ} C \), \( 992.3 kg / m ^{3} \). The equilibrium line for the \( H_{2} S \)-water-air system at \( 20^{\circ} C \) is provided in Figure. The molecular weight of \( H_{2} S \) is 34 g/gmole. The characteristics of this gasâ€“liquid mass-transfer process are developed in parts (a) to (d) below.

 

(a) Plot the operating point, in composition terms of \( p_{A}\) for the gas phase, and \( c_{A L} \) for the liquid phase, directly on Figure.

(b) Determine m, the equilibrium distribution coefficient.

(c) Estimate the overall mass-transfer coefficients \( K_{G} \) and \( K_{L} \).

(d) Determine the flux from the liquid phase to the gas phase, and the interface compositions, \( p_{A, i} \) and \( c_{A L, i}\).

 

Admin
Solution

(a) The following changes on the composition units on the composition units are required. For the bulk liquid phase,

 

\( c_{A L} = x_{A} C_{L} \cong x_{A} \frac{\rho_{B, l i q}}{M_{B}} = \left(6.0 \times 10^{-5}\right)\left(\frac{992.3 kg / m ^{3}}{18 kg / gmole }\right)\left(\frac{1000 gmole }{ kgmole }\right) = 3.31 \frac{ gmole }{ m ^{3}} \)

 

For the bulk gas phase,

\( p_{A} = y_{A} P = 0.01(1.5 atm ) = 0.015 atm \)

 

The operating point \( \left(p_{A}, c_{A L}\right) \) is plotted on Figure as the open circle. Note that since this is a liquid stripping process, the operating point is below the equilibrium line.

 

(b) The equilibrium line in \( p_{A} – c_{A L} \) coordinates is linear, and it is determined that the slope \( H = 8.8 m ^{3} \cdot atm / gmole \). Therefore,

 

\( m = \frac{H \cdot C_{L}}{P} \simeq \frac{H}{P} \frac{\rho_{B, l i q}}{M_{B}} = \left(\frac{8.8 m ^{3} \cdot atm / kgmole }{1.5 atm }\right)\left(\frac{992.3 kg / m ^{3}}{18 kg / kgmole }\right) = 322 \)

 

with \( C_{L} = 55.1 kgmole / m ^{3} \)

 

(c) There are many pathways to obtain \( K_{G} \) and \( K_{L} \). One option is to convert \( K_{y} \) and \( K_{x} \) to \( K_{G} \) and \( K_{L} \):

 

\( k_{G} = \frac{k_{y}}{P} = \frac{4.5 \times 10^{-3} kgmole / m ^{2} \cdot s }{1.5 atm } = 3.0 \times 10^{-3} \frac{ kgmole }{ m ^{2} \cdot s \cdot atm } \)

 

\( k_{L} = \frac{k_{x}}{C_{L}} = \frac{0.30 kgmole / m ^{2} \cdot s }{55.1 kgmole / m ^{3}} = 5.44 \times 10^{-3} m / s \)

 

and then use equation ( \( \frac{1}{K_{G}} = \frac{1}{k_{G}} + \frac{H}{k_{L}}\) ):

 

\( \frac{1}{K_{G}} = \frac{1}{k_{G}} + \frac{H}{k_{L}} = \frac{1}{3.0 \times 10^{-3} kgmole / m ^{2} \cdot s \cdot atm } + \frac{8.8 m ^{3} \cdot atm / kgmole }{5.44 \times 10^{-3} m / s } \)

 

to obtain \( K_{G} = 5.13 \times 10^{-4} kgmole / m ^{2} \cdot s \cdot atm \), and equation ( \( \frac{1}{K_{L}} = \frac{1}{H \cdot k_{G}} + \frac{1}{k_{L}} \) ):

 

\( \frac{1}{K_{L}} = \frac{1}{H \cdot k_{G}} + \frac{1}{k_{L}} = \frac{1}{\left(8.8 m ^{3} \cdot atm / kgmole \right)\left(3.0 \times 10^{-3} kgmole / m ^{2} \cdot s \cdot atm \right)} + \frac{1}{5.44 \times 10^{-3} m / s } \)

 

to obtain \( K_{L}=4.51 \times 10^{-3} m / s, \) Another approach would be to estimate \( K_{y} \) and \( K_{x} \), and then convert via \( K_{G} = K_{y} / P \) and \( K_{L} = K_{x} / C_{L}, \) which will return the same result.

 

(d) Using values for \( K_{G} \) and \( K_{L} \), the flux is

 

\( N_{A} = K_{G}\left(p_{A} – p_{A}^{*}\right) = \left(5.13 \times 10^{-4} \frac{\text { kgmole }}{ m ^{2} \cdot s \cdot atm }\right)(0.015 – 0.029) atm \)

 

\( = – 7.2 \times 10^{-6} \frac{ kgmole }{ m ^{2} \cdot s \cdot atm }\)

 

\( N_{A} = K_{L}\left(c_{A L}^{*} – c_{A L}\right) = \left(4.51 \times 10^{-3} m / s \right)(1.71 – 3.31) \frac{ gmole }{ m ^{3}} \frac{1 kgmole }{1000 gmole } \)

 

\( = – 7.2 \times 10^{-6} \frac{ kgmole }{ m ^{2} \cdot s }\)

 

with

\( p_{A}^{*} = H \cdot c_{A L} = \left(8.8 \frac{ m ^{3} \cdot atm }{ kgmole }\right)\left(3.31 \frac{ gmole }{ m ^{3}}\right)\left(\frac{1 kgmole }{1000 gmole }\right) = 0.029 atm \)

 

and

\( c_{A L}^{*} = \frac{p_{A}}{H} = \left(\frac{0.015 atm }{8.8 m ^{3} \cdot atm / kgmole }\right)\left(\frac{1000 gmole }{1 kgmole }\right) = 1.71 \frac{ gmole }{ m ^{3}} \)

 

Note that the flux calculated based on either the gas phase or the liquid phase returns the same result. Note also that the flux is negative, which indicates that the flux of \( H _{2} S \) is moving from the liquid to the gas phase. Finally, the interface
compositions are backed out from the flux using equations (29-7) and ( \( N_{A} = k_{L}\left(c_{A L, i} -c_{A L}\right) \) ):

 

\( p_{A, i} = p_{A} – \frac{N_{A}}{k_{G}} = 0.015 atm – \frac{- 7.2 \times 10^{-6} kgmole / m ^{2} \cdot s }{3.0 \times 10^{-3} kgmole / m ^{2} \cdot s \cdot atm } = 0.017 atm \)

 

\( c_{A L, i} = c_{A L} + \frac{N_{A}}{k_{L}} = 3.31 \frac{\text { gmole }}{ m ^{3}} + \frac{- 7.2 \times 10^{-6} kgmole / m ^{2} \cdot s }{5.44 \times 10^{-3} m / s } = 1.99 \frac{\text { gmole }}{ m ^{3}} \)

 

All composition points are plotted out on Figure. Since \( C_{A L, i} \) is approaching \( c_{A L}^{*} \) the process is moving toward the liquid phase controlling condition. In comparing this diagram with Figure, we see the differences in mass-transfer driving forces between liquid stripping and gas absorption.
