Guest Question.

A conical pivot supports a load of 20 kN, the cone angle is \(120^{\circ}\) and the intensity of normal pressure is not to exceed \(0.3 N / mm ^{2}\). The external diameter is twice the internal diameter. Find the outer and inner radii of the bearing surface. If the shaft rotates at 200 r.p.m. and the coefficient of friction is 0.1, find the power absorbed in friction. Assume uniform pressure.

Admin
Solution

Given : W = 20 kN \(=20 \times 10^{3} N ; 2 \alpha=120^{\circ} \text { or } \alpha=60^{\circ} ; p_{n}=0.3 N / mm ^{2}\) ; \(N=200 \text { r.p.m. or } \omega=2 \pi \times 200 / 60=20.95 rad / s ; \mu=0.1\)

 

Outer and inner radii of the bearing surface

Let \(r_{1} \text { and } r_{2}\) = Outer and inner radii of the bearing surface, in mm.

Since the external diameter is twice the internal diameter, therefore

\(r_{1}=2 r_{2}\)

We know that intensity of normal pressure \(\left(p_{n}\right)\),

\(0.3=\frac{W}{\pi\left[\left(r_{1}\right)^{2}-\left(r_{2}\right)^{2}\right]}=\frac{20 \times 10^{3}}{\pi\left[\left(2 r_{2}\right)^{2}-\left(r_{2}\right)^{2}\right]}=\frac{2.12 \times 10^{3}}{\left(r_{2}\right)^{2}}\)

 

\(\therefore \) \(\left(r_{2}\right)^{2}=2.12 \times 10^{3} / 0.3=7.07 \times 10^{3} \text { or } r_{2}=84 mm\)

 

and \(r_{1}=2 r_{2}=2 \times 84=168 mm\)

 

Power absorbed in friction

We know that total frictional torque (assuming uniform pressure),

\(T=\frac{2}{3} \times \mu \cdot W \cdot \operatorname{cosec} \alpha\left[\frac{\left(r_{1}\right)^{3}-\left(r_{2}\right)^{3}}{\left(r_{1}\right)^{2}-\left(r_{2}\right)^{2}}\right]\)

 

\(=\frac{2}{3} \times 0.1 \times 20 \times 10^{3} \times \operatorname{cosec} 60^{\circ}=\left[\frac{(168)^{3}-(84)^{3}}{(168)^{2}-(84)^{2}}\right] N – mm\)

 

\(=301760 N – mm =301.76 N – m\)

 

\(\therefore \) Power absorbed in friction,

P = \(T . \omega\) = 301.76 ร 20.95 = 6322 W = 6.322 kW
