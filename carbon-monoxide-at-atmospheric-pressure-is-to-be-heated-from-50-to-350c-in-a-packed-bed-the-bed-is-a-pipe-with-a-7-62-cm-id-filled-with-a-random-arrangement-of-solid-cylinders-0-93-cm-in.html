Guest Question.

Carbon monoxide at atmospheric pressure is to be heated from 50° to 350°C in a packed bed. The bed is a pipe with a 7.62-cm-ID, filled with a random arrangement of solid cylinders 0.93 cm in diameter and 1.17 cm long (see Fig. 7.17). The flow rate of carbon monoxide is 5 kg/h, and the inside surface of the pipe is held at 400°C. Determine the average heat transfer coefficient at the pipe wall.

Admin

at the average of these, or 300°C, we find a kinematic viscosity of \(4.82 × 10^{-5} m^2/s\), a thermal conductivity of 0.042 W/m °C, a density of \(0.60 kg/m^3\), a specific heat of 1081 J/kg °C, and Prandtl number of 0.71. The superficial velocity is

\(U_s=\frac{(5 kg/h)}{(0.6 kg/m^3)(\pi 0.0762^2/4)(m^2)} = 1827 m/h\)

The cylindrical packing volume is \([π (0.93 cm)^2 /4](1.17 cm)= 0.795 cm^3\), and the surface area is \((2)[π (0.93 cm)^2 /4] + π(0.93 cm)(1.17 cm) = 4.78 cm^2\). Therefore, the equivalent packing diameter is

\(D_p =\frac{(6)(0.795 cm^3)}{4.78 cm^2}= 1 cm =0.01 m\)

giving a Reynolds number of

\(Re_{D_{p} }= \frac{(1827 m/h)/(3600 s/h)(0.01 m)}{(4.82 \times 10^{-5}m^2/s)}= 105\)

From Eq. (7.23),

\(\frac{\overline{h} _{c}D_p }{k} = 2.58Re^{1/3}_{D _{p} } Pr^{1/3}+ 0.094Re^{0.8}_{D _{p} }Pr^{0.4}\) (7.23)

we find

\(\frac{\overline{h} _{c}D_p }{k} = 2.58(105)^{1/3}(0.71)^{1/3} + 0.094(105)^{0.8}(0.71)^{0.4}\)

 

\(= 14.3\)

or

\(\overline{h} _{c} =\frac{(14.3)(0.042 W/m K)}{0.01 m} = 60.1 W/m^2 °C\)
