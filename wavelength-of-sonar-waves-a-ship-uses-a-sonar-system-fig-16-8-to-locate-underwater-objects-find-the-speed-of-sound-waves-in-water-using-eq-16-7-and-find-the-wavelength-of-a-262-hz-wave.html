Guest Question.

WAVELENGTH OF SONAR WAVES

A ship uses a sonar system (Fig. 16.8) to locate underwater objects. Find the speed of sound waves in water using Eq. (16.7), and find the wavelength of a 262-Hz wave.

\(v=\sqrt{\frac{B}{\rho}}\) (16.7)

Admin
Solution

IDENTIFY and SET UP:

Our target variables are the speed and wavelength of a sound wave in water. In Eq. (16.7), we use the density of water, \(\rho=1.00 \times 10^{3} \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\), and the bulk modulus of water, which we find from the compressibility (see Table 11.2).

TABLE 11.2 Compressibilities
of Liquids
Compressibility, k
Liquid \(P a^{-1}\) \(atm^{-1}\)
Carbon disulfide \(93 \times 10^{-11}\) \(94 \times 10^{-6}\)
Ethyl alcohol \(110 \times 10^{-11}\) \(111 \times 10^{-6}\)
Glycerine \(21 \times 10^{-11}\) \(21 \times 10^{-6}\)
Mercury \(3.7 \times 10^{-11}\) \(3.8 \times 10^{-6}\)
Water \(45.8 \times 10^{-11}\) \(46.4 \times 10^{-6}\)

Given the speed and the frequency f = 262 Hz, we find the wave length from v = fλ.

EXECUTE:

In , we used Table 11.2 to find B = 2.18 × \(10^9\) Pa. Then

\(v=\sqrt{\frac{B}{\rho}}=\sqrt{\frac{2.18 \times 10^{9} \mathrm{~Pa}}{1.00 \times 10^{3} \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}}}=1480 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\)

and \(\lambda=\frac{v}{f}=\frac{1480 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}}{262 \mathrm{~s}^{-1}}=5.65 \mathrm{~m}\)

 

EVALUATE: The calculated value of v agrees well with the value in Table 16.1. Water is denser than air (ρ is larger) but is also much more incompressible (B is much larger), and so the speed \(v=\sqrt{B / \rho}\) is greater than the 344-m/s speed of sound in air at ordinary temperatures. The relationship λ = v/f then says that a sound wave in water must have a longer wavelength than a wave of the same frequency in air. Indeed, we found in (Section 15.2) that a 262-Hz sound wave in air has a wavelength of only 1.31 m.
