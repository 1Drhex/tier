Guest Question.

In a manufacturing process stainless steel cylinders (AISI 304) initially at 600 K are quenched by submersion in an oil bath maintained at 300 K with \(h=500 W / m ^{2} \cdot K.\) Each cylinder is of length 2L = 60 mm and diameter D = 80 mm. Consider a time 3 min into the cooling process and determine temperatures at the center of the cylinder, at the center of a circular face, and at the midheight of the side.

Admin
Solution

Known: Initial temperature and dimensions of cylinder and temperature and convection conditions of an oil bath.

Find: Temperatures T(r, x, t) after 3 min at the cylinder center, T(0, 0, 3 min), at the center of a circular face, T(0, L, 3 min), and at the midheight of the side, \(T\left(r_{o}, 0,3 min \right) .\)

Schematic:

Assumptions:
1. Two-dimensional conduction in r and x.
2. Constant properties.

Properties: Table A.1, stainless steel, AISI 304 \([T=(600+300) / 2=450 K ]: \rho=7900 kg / m ^{3}, c=526 J / kg \cdot K , k=17.4 W / m \cdot K , \alpha=k / \rho c=4.19 \times 10^{-6} m ^{2} / s.\)

Analysis: The solid steel cylinder corresponds to case (i) of Figure 5S.11, and the temperature at any point in the cylinder may be expressed as the following product of one-dimensional solutions.

\(\frac{T(r, x, t)-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}=P(x, t) C(r, t)\)

 

where P(x, t) and C(r, t) are defined by Equations 5S.2 and 5S.3, respectively. Accordingly, for the center of the cylinder,

\(\left.P(x, t) \equiv \frac{T(x, t)-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\text {Plane wall}}\) (5S.2)

\(\left.C(r, t) \equiv \frac{T(r, t)-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\text {Infinite cylinder}}\) (5S.3)

\(\frac{T(0,0,3 \min )-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}=\left.\left.\frac{T(0,3 \min )-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\text {Plan wall}} \cdot \frac{T(0,3 \min )-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\text {Infinite cylinder }}\)

 

Hence, for the plane wall, with

\(B i^{-1}=\frac{k}{h L}=\frac{17.4 W / m \cdot K }{500 W / m ^{2} \cdot K \times 0.03 m }=1.16\)

 

\(F o=\frac{\alpha t}{L^{2}}=\frac{4.19 \times 10^{-6} m ^{2} / s \times 180 s }{(0.03 m )^{2}}=0.84\)

 

it follows from Equation 5.44 that

\(\theta_{o}^{*}=C_{1} \exp \left(-\zeta_{1}^{2} F o\right)\) (5.44)

\(\theta_{o}^{*}=\frac{\theta_{o}}{\theta_{i}}=C_{1} \exp \left(-\zeta_{1}^{2} F o\right)\)

 

where, with \(B i=0.862, C_{1}=1.109 \text { and } \zeta_{1}=0.814\) rad from Table 5.1. With \(Fo = 0.84\),

\(\frac{\theta_{o}}{\theta_{i}}=\left.\frac{T(0,3 \min )-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\begin{array}{l} _\text { Plane wall } \\ \end{array}}=1.109 \exp \left[-(0.814 rad )^{2} \times 0.84\right]=0.636\)

 

Similarly, for the infinite cylinder, with

\(B i^{-1}=\frac{k}{h r_{o}}=\frac{17.4 W / m \cdot K }{500 W / m ^{2} \cdot K \times 0.04 m }=0.87\)

 

\(F o=\frac{\alpha t}{r_{o}^{2}}=\frac{4.19 \times 10^{-6} m ^{2} / s \times 180 s }{(0.04 m )^{2}}=0.47\)

 

it follows from Equation 5.52c that

\(\theta_{o}^{*}=C_{1} \exp \left(-\zeta_{1}^{2} F o\right)\) (5.52c)

\(\theta_{o}^{*}=\frac{\theta_{o}}{\theta_{i}}=C_{1} \exp \left(-\zeta_{1}^{2} F o\right)\)

 

where, with \(B i=1.15, C_{1}=1.227 \text { and } \zeta_{1}=1.307\) rad from Table 5.1. With Fo = 0.47,

\(\left.\frac{\theta_{o}}{\theta_{i}}\right|_{\begin{array}{l} _\text { Infinite cylinder } \\ \end{array}}=1.109 \exp \left[-(1.307 rad )^{2} \times 0.47\right]=0.550\)

 

Hence, for the center of the cylinder,

\(\frac{T(0,0,3 \min )-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}=0.636 \times 0.550=0.350\)

 

\(T(0,0,3 min )=300 K +0.350(600-300) K =405 K\)

 

The temperature at the center of a circular face may be obtained from the requirement that

\(\frac{T(0, L, 3 \min )-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}=\left.\left.\frac{T(L, 3 \min )-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\text {Plane wall }} \cdot \frac{T(0,3 \min )-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\text {Infinite cylinder }}\)

 

where, from Equation 5.43b,

\(\theta^{*}=\theta_{o}^{*} \cos \left(\zeta_{1} x^{*}\right)\) (5.43b)

\(\frac{\theta^{*}}{\theta_{o}^{*}}=\frac{\theta}{\theta_{o}}=\cos \left(\zeta_{1} x^{*}\right)\)

 

Hence, with \(x^{*}=1,\) we have

\(\frac{\theta(L)}{\theta_{o}}=\left.\frac{T(L, 3 \min )-T_{\infty}}{T(0,3 \min )-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\text {Plane wall}}=\cos (0.814 \operatorname{rad} \times 1)=0.687\)

 

Hence

\(\left.\frac{T(L, 3 \min )-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\text {Plane wall }}=\left.\left.\frac{T(L, 3 \min )-T_{\infty}}{T(0,3 \min )-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\text {Plane wall }} \cdot \frac{T(0,3 \min )-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\text {Plane wall }}\)

 

\(\left.\frac{T(L, 3 \min )-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\text {Plane wall }}=0.687 \times 0.636=0.437\)

 

Hence

\(\frac{T(0, L, 3 \min )-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}=0.437 \times 0.550=0.240\)

 

\(T(0, L, 3 min )=300 K +0.24(600-300) K =372 K\)

 

The temperature at the midheight of the side may be obtained from the requirement that

\(\frac{T\left(r_{o}, 0,3 \min \right)-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}=\left.\frac{T(0,3 \min )-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\text {Plane wall}} \left.\frac{T\left(r_{o}, 3 min \right)-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\text {Infinite cylinder }}\)

 

where, from Equation 5.52b,

\(\theta^{*}=\theta_{o}^{*} J_{0}\left(\zeta_{1} r^{*}\right)\) (5.52b)

 

\(\frac{\theta^{*}}{\theta_{o}^{*}}=\frac{\theta}{\theta_{o}}=J_{0}\left(\zeta_{1} r^{*}\right)\)

\(\frac{\theta\left(r_{o}\right)}{\theta_{o}}=\left.\frac{T\left(r_{o}, 3 min \right)-T_{\infty}}{T(0,3 min )-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\text {Infinite cylinder }}=J_{0}(1.307 rad \times 1)=0.616\)

 

Hence

\(\left.\frac{T\left(r_{o}, 3 \min \right)-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\text {Infinite cylinder }} =\left.\frac{T\left(r_{o}, 3 \min \right)-T_{\infty}}{T(0,3 \min )-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\text {Infinite cylinder }}\cdot \left.\frac{T(0,3 min )-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}\right|_{\text {Infinite cylinder }}\)

 

\(\left.\frac{T\left(r_{o}, 3 \min \right)-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}\right|_ {\text { Infinite cylinder}}=0.616 \times 0.550=0.339\)

 

Hence

\(\frac{T\left(r_{o}, 0,3 min \right)-T_{\infty}}{T_{i}-T_{\infty}}=0.636 \times 0.339=0.216\)

 

\(T\left(r_{o}, 0,3 min \right)=300 K +0.216(600-300) K =365 K\)

 

Comments:
1. Verify that the temperature at the edge of the cylinder is \(T\left(r_{o}, L, 3 min \right)=344 K.\)

2. The Heisler charts of Section 5S.1 could also be used to obtain the desired results. Accessing these charts, one would obtain \(\theta_{o} /\left.\theta_{i}\right|_{\text {Plane wall }} \approx 0.64, \theta_{o} /\left.\theta_{i}\right|_{\text {Infinite cylinder }} \approx 0.55, \theta(L) /\left.\theta_{o}\right|_{\text {Plane wall }} \approx 0.68 \text { , and } \theta\left(r_{o}\right) /\left.\theta_{o}\right|_{\text {Infinite cylinder }} \approx 0.61\) which are in good agreement with results obtained from the one-term approximations.

3. The IHT Models, Transient Conduction option for the Plane Wall and Infinite Cylinder may be used to calculate temperature ratios required for the foregoing product solution.
