Guest Question.

RESISTING MOMENT ON REDUCING BEND

The reducing bend shown in the figure is supported on a horizontal axis through point A. Water flows through the bend at \(m ^{3} / s\). The inlet pressure at cross-section 1 is 150 kPa gage, and the outlet pressure at section 2 is 59.3 kPa gage. A weight of 1420 N acts 20 cm to the right of point A.

Find the moment the support system must resist. The diameters of the inlet and outlet pipes are 30 cm and 10 cm, respectively.

Sketch:

Situation: Reverse bend supported on a horizontal axis.

Find: Resisting moment (in \(N \cdot m\)) by support system.

Assumptions:

1. Water flow is steady.

2. Water temperature is \(20^{\circ} C\).

Properties: From Table A.5, \(\rho=998 kg / m ^{3}\).

Plan

The reducing bend is stationary, so it serves as an inertial reference frame. Flow is steady, so \(\dot{m}_{1}=\dot{m}_{2}=\dot{m}\).

1. Select a control volume surrounding the reducing bend.

2. Sketch the force (and moment) diagram.

3. Sketch the momentum diagram.

4. Apply the moment-of-momentum equation, Eq. (6.27). The bend lies in the x-z plane so the y-direction, represented by unit vector j, goes into the page. A positive moment in the x-z plane is in the clockwise direction.

\(\sum M =\frac{d}{d t} \int_{ cv }( r \times v ) \rho d V +\sum_{ cs } r _{o} \times\left(\dot{m}_{o} v _{o}\right)-\sum_{ cs } r _{i} \times\left(\dot{m}_{i} v _{i}\right)\) (6.27)

5. Evaluate the sum of moments.

6. Evaluate the moments of momentum.

7. Calculate resisting moment.

 

Admin
Solution

1. The control volume selected is shown in diagram.

2. The force diagram shows two pressure forces contributing to the moment and the resisting moment.

3. The momentum diagram shows an influx and outflux of momentum.

4. Apply the moment-of-momentum equation.

 

\(\sum M _{A}=\frac{d}{d t} \int_{ cv }( r \times v ) \rho d V +\sum_{ cs } r _{o} \times\left(\dot{m}_{o} v _{o}\right)-\sum_{ cs } r _{i} \times\left(\dot{m}_{i} v _{i}\right)\)

 

5. Sum of moments about axis A.

 

\(\sum M _{A}=-\left(M_{A}+0.15 p_{1} A_{1}+0.475 p_{2} A_{2}-0.2 W\right) j\)

 

6. Evaluate the moment-of-momentum terms.

• Accumulation term, steady flow, is

 

\(\frac{d}{d t} \int_{c v}(r \times v) \rho d V=0\).

 

• Inflow term is \(\sum_{c s} r_{i} \times\left(\dot{m}_{i} v_{i}\right)=r_{1} \times\left(\dot{m} v_{1}\right)=-r_{1} \dot{m} v_{1} j\).

 

• Outflow term is

 

\(\sum_{c s} r _{o} \times\left(\dot{m}_{o} v _{o}\right)= r _{2} \times\left(\dot{m} v _{2}\right)=r_{2} \dot{m} v_{2} j\).

 

7. Resisting moment at A

 

\(-\left(M_{A}+0.15 p_{1} A_{1}+0.475 p_{2} A_{2}-0.2 W\right) j =\dot{m}\left(r_{2} v_{2}+r_{1} v_{1}\right) j\)

 

\(M_{A}=-0.15 p_{1} A_{1}-0.475 p_{2} A_{2}+0.2 W-\dot{m}\left(r_{2} v_{2}+r_{1} v_{1}\right)\)

 

• Torque due to pressure

 

\(0.15 p_{1} A_{1}=(0.15 m )\left(150 \times 1000 N / m ^{2}\right)\left(\pi \times 0.3^{2} / 4 m ^{2}\right)\)

 

\(=1590 N \cdot m\)

 

\(0.475 p_{2} A_{2}=(0.475 m )\left(59.3 \times 1000 N / m ^{2}\right)\left(\pi \times 0.15^{2} / 4 m ^{2}\right)\)

 

\(=498 N \cdot m\)

 

• Net moment-of-momentum flow

 

\(\dot{m}=\rho Q=\left(998 kg / m ^{3}\right)\left(0.25 m ^{3} / s \right)\)

 

= 250 kg ⁄ s

 

\(v_{1}=\frac{Q}{A_{1}}=\frac{0.25 m ^{3} / s }{\pi \times 0.15^{2} m ^{2}}=3.54\) m/s

 

\(v_{2}=\frac{Q}{A_{2}}=\frac{0.25 m ^{3} / s }{\pi \times 0.075^{2} m ^{2}}=14.15\) m/s

 

\(\dot{m}\left(r_{2} v_{2}+r_{1} v_{1}\right)=(250 kg / s )\)

 

\(\times(0.475 \times 14.15+0.15 \times 3.54)\left( m ^{2} / s \right)\)

 

\(=1813 N \cdot m\)

 

• Moment exerted by support

 

\(M_{A}=-0.15 p_{1} A_{1}-0.475 p_{2} A_{2}+0.2 W-\dot{m}\left(r_{2} v_{2}+r_{1} v_{1}\right)\)

 

\(=-(1590 N \cdot m )-(498 N \cdot m )\)

 

\(+(0.2 m \times 1420 N )-(1813 N \cdot m )\)

 

\(M_{A}=-3.62 kN \cdot m\)

 

Thus, a moment of 3.62 \(kN \cdot m\) acting in the j, or clockwise, direction is needed to hold the bend stationary. Stated differently, the support system must be designed to withstan a counterclockwise moment of 3.62 \(kN \cdot m\).

 

Review

1. Care must be taken in addressing moment-of-momentum problems that the “right-hand-rule” to find the direction of the moment be applied correctly.

2. This type of problem may be encountered when the flanges are flexible and provide no force on the system.
