Guest Question.

The wall of a cold storage consists of three layers: an outer layer of ordinary bricks, 25 cm thick, a middle layer of cork, 10 cm thick and an inner layer of cement, 6 cm thick. The thermal conductivities of the materials are 0.7, 0.043 and 0.72 W/m.K, respectively. The temperature of the outer surface of the wall is 30°C and that of inner is – 15°C. Calculate :
(a) Steady state rate of heat gain per unit area,
(b) Temperature at the interfaces of composite wall,
(c) The percentage of total heat resistance offered by individual layers, and
(d) What additional thickness of cork should be provided to reduce the heat gain 30% less than the present value ?

Admin
Solution

Given : Composite wall of a cold storage :
\(L_1 = 25 cm = 0.25 m, k_1 = 0.7 W/m.K\)
\(L_2 = 10 cm = 0.1 m, k_2 = 0.043 W/m.K\)
\(L_3 = 6 cm = 0.06 m, k_3 = 0.72 W/m.K\)
\(T_1 = 30°C, T_4 = – 15°C.\)
To find :
(a) Heat flux, q,
(b) Temperatures, \(T_2, T_3,\)
(c) Percentage resistance offered by individual layers,

(d) Thickness of cork layer for 30% heat reduction of existing value.
Analysis : (a) Individual thermal resistances per \(m^2 \)in the network
\(R_1 = \frac{L_1}{k_1}= \frac{0.25}{0.7} = 0.357 m^2.K/W\)
\(R_2 =\frac{L_2}{k_2}= \frac{0.1}{0.043} = 2.326 m^2.K/W\)
\(R_3 = \frac{L_3}{k_3}= \frac{0.06}{0.72} = 0.083 m^2.K/W\)
All resistances are in series, thus total thermal resistance
\(Σ_{Rth} =R_1 + R_2 + R_3\)
= 0.357 + 2.326 + 0.083
\(= 2.766 m^2. K/W\)
Rate of heat gain per\( m^2\)
\(q = \frac{∆T}{ΣR_{th}} = \frac{T_1 – T_4}{ΣR_{th}}\)
\(= \frac{30 – (-15)}{2.766}=\frac{45}{2.766}\)
\(= 16.27 W/m^2. \)
(b) Temperature at the interfaces :
(i) Heat flux across brick layer
\(q = \frac{T_1 – T_2}{R_1}\)

or \(T_2 =T_1 – qR_1 = 30 – 16.27 × 0.357\)
= 24.19°C.
It is the temperature at interface between brick and cork layers.

(ii) Heat flux across cork layer
\(q = \frac{T_2 – T_3}{R_2}\)
or\( T_3 =T_2 – qR_2 = 24.19 – 16.27 × 2.326\)
= – 13.65°C.
It is the temperature at interface between cork and cement layers.
(c) Percentage thermal resistance offered by an individual ith layer

\(=\frac{R}{ΣR_{th}} \times 100\)

% resistance offered by brick layer
\(= \frac{0.357}{2.766}\times 100 = 12.9%\)
% resistance offered by cork layer
\(= \frac{2.326}{2.766}\times 100 = 84.1%\)
% resistance offered by cement layer
\(= \frac{0.083}{2.766} × 100 = 3.0%.\)
(d) Desired rate of heat flow = 30% less of present
value = 0.7 × present value
\(q_1 = 0.7 × 16.27 = 11.39 W/m^2\)
\(∴ q_1 = \frac{∆T}{ΣR_{th_{1}}}\)
or \(11.39 = \frac{30 – (-15)}{Σ_{Rth_{1}}}\)

or \(ΣR_{th_{1}} = \frac{45}{1139} = 3.95 m^2.K/W\)
Additional resistance to be offered by cork
\(= ΣR_{th_{1}} – ΣR_{th}\)
\(R_{2}^{'}= 3.95 – 2.766 = 1.184 m^2.K/W\)
Further\( R_{2}^{'} =\frac{L{2}^{'}}{k_2}\)
or \(L_{2}^{'}= R_{2}^{'}k_2 = 1.184 × 0.043\)
= 0.051 m = 5.1 cm.

It is an additional thickness of cork to be provided.
