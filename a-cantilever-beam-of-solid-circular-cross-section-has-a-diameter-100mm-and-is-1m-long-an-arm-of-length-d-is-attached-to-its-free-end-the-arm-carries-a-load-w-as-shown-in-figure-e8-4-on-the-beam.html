Guest Question.

A cantilever beam of solid circular cross-section has a diameter 100mm and is 1m long. An arm of length d is attached to its free end. The arm carries a load W as shown in Figure E8.4. On the beam’s upper surface, halfway along its length and coinciding with its vertical plane of symmetry is a rectangular strain gauge rosette which gave the following readings for particular values of W and d:

 

\(\varepsilon_{ a }=1500 \times 10^{-6}\), \(\varepsilon_{ b }=300 \times 10^{-6}\), \(\varepsilon_{ c }=-450 \times 10^{-6}\)

 

If gauges ‘a’ and ‘c’ are in line with and perpendicular to the axis of the beam calculate the values of W and d. Take E = 200000\(N / mm ^{2}\) and v = 0.3.

Admin
Solution

Again, a direct application of Equations (8.7a) and (8.3b) can provide the principal strains and principal stresses at the point. To determinate the load W and then lever arm d, the bending moment and twist moment due to W and d on the cross-section at the location where the strain gauge rosette is mounted must be calculated. The relationships between the bending and twist moments and the normal and shear stresses on the section are then established, from which the values of W and d are finally determined.

 

\(\sigma_{1}=\frac{E}{1-\nu^{2}}\left[\varepsilon_{1}+\nu \varepsilon_{2}\right]\)

 

\(\sigma_{2}=\frac{E}{1-\nu^{2}}\left[\varepsilon_{2}+\nu \varepsilon_{1}\right]\) (8.3b)

 

(a) Principal strains

Substituting values of \(\varepsilon_{ a }\), \(\varepsilon_{ b }\) and \(\varepsilon_{ c }\) in Equation (8.7a) gives:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{1} &=\frac{1}{2}\left[\left(\varepsilon_{a}+\varepsilon_{c}\right)+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \sqrt{\left(\varepsilon_{a}-\varepsilon_{b}\right)^{2}+\left(\varepsilon_{c}-\varepsilon_{b}\right)^{2}}\right.\\&=\frac{1}{2}\left[(1500-450) \times 10^{-6}+\frac{10^{-6}}{\sqrt{2}} \sqrt{(1500+300)^{2}+(-450+300)^{2}}\right.\end{aligned}\) = \(1802.1 \times 10^{-6}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\varepsilon_{2} &=\frac{1}{2}\left[\left(\varepsilon_{a}+\varepsilon_{c}\right)-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \sqrt{\left(\varepsilon_{a}-\varepsilon_{b}\right)^{2}+\left(\varepsilon_{c}-\varepsilon_{b}\right)^{2}}\right.\\&=\frac{1}{2}\left[(1500-450) \times 10^{-6}-\frac{10^{-6}}{\sqrt{2}} \sqrt{(1500+300)^{2}+(-450+300)^{2}}\right.\end{aligned}\) =\(-752.2 \times 10^{-6}\)

 

(b) Principal stresses

Now from Equation (8.3b):

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{1} &=\frac{E}{1-\nu^{2}}\left(\varepsilon_{1}+\nu \varepsilon_{2}\right) \\&=\frac{200000}{1-0.3^{2}}(1802.2-0.3 \times 752.2) \times 10^{-6}\end{aligned}\) =346.5 \(N / mm ^{2}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{2} &=\frac{E}{1-\nu^{2}}\left(\varepsilon_{2}+\nu \varepsilon_{1}\right) \\&=\frac{200000}{1-0.3^{2}}(-752.2-0.3 \times 1802.2) \times 10^{-6}\end{aligned}\) =-46.5 \(N / mm ^{2}\)

 

(c) Stresses ( \(\sigma_{x}\)
and \(\tau_{x y}\)) on the cross-section

Taking the axial direction as the x direction, the state of stress at the surface point is as follows:

 

where the normal stress, \(\sigma_{x}\) , is related to the bending moment caused by W, and the shear stress, \(\tau_{x y}\) , is related to the twist moment caused by W and d. The hoop stress is zero, that is,

\(\sigma_{y}\) = 0

 

From Equation (7.2), the sum of the two principal stresses yields:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{\max } &=\sigma_{1} \\&=\frac{1}{2}\left[\left(\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}\right)+\sqrt{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right)^{2}+4 \tau_{x y}^{2}}\right]\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{\min } &=\sigma_{2} \\&=\frac{1}{2}\left[\left(\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}\right)-\sqrt{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right)^{2}+4 \tau_{x y}^{2}}\right]\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\sigma_{1}+\sigma_{2}=\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}\)

 

Thus:

 

\(\sigma_{x}=\sigma_{1}+\sigma_{2}=346.5-46.5=300 N / mm ^{2}\)

 

On the cross-section the bending moment is:

 

M =W ×0.5m

 

From Equation (5.1a):

 

\(\sigma=\frac{M y}{l}\) (5.1a)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{x}=\frac{M y}{l}=\frac{W \times 500 mm \times 50 mm }{\pi \times 100^{4} / 64 mm ^{4}}=300 N / mm ^{2} \\&W=58.9 kN\end{aligned}\)

 

Again from Equation (7.2), the difference between the two principal stresses is:

 

\(\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{2}=\sqrt{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right)^{2}+4 \tau_{x y}^{2}}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{x y} &=\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\left(\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{2}\right)^{2}-\sigma_{x}^{2}}=\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{(346.5+46.5)^{2}-300^{2}} \\&=126.9 N / mm ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

On the cross-section, the twist moment is:

 

T =W ×d = 58.9×d

 

From Equation (3.2):

 

\(\tau=\frac{T r}{J}\) (3.2)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tau_{x y} &=\frac{T r}{J}=\frac{W \times d \times r}{J}=\frac{58.9 \times d \times 50}{\pi \times 100^{4} / 32} \\&=126.9 N / mm ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

d = 423mm
