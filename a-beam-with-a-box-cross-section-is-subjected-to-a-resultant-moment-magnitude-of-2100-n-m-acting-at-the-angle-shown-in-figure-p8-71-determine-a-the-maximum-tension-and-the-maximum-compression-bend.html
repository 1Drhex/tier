Guest Question.

A beam with a box cross section is subjected to a resultant moment magnitude of 2,100 N-m acting at the angle shown in Figure P8.71. Determine:
(a) the maximum tension and the maximum compression bending stresses in the beam.
(b) the orientation of the neutral axis relative to the +z axis. Show its location on a sketch of the cross section.

Admin
Solution

Section properties

\(\begin{aligned}&I_{y}=\frac{(90 mm )(55 mm )^{3}}{12}-\frac{(80 mm )(45 mm )^{3}}{12}=640,312.5 mm ^{4} \\&I_{z}=\frac{(55 mm )(90 mm )^{3}}{12}-\frac{(45 mm )(80 mm )^{3}}{12}=1,421,250.0 mm ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Moment components

\(\begin{aligned}&M_{y}=(2,100 N – m ) \sin 30^{\circ}=1,050 N – m \\&M_{z}=-(2,100 N – m ) \cos 30^{\circ}=-1,818.65 N – m\end{aligned}\)

(a) Maximum bending stresses
For a shape having at least one axis of symmetry, Eq. (8-24) can be used to determine bending stresses.
Compute normal stress at y = 45 mm, z = 27.5 mm:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\frac{M_{y} z}{I_{y}}-\frac{M_{z} y}{I_{z}} \\&=\frac{(1,050 N – m )(27.5 mm )(1,000 mm / m )}{640,312.5 mm ^{4}}-\frac{(-1,818.65 N – m )(45 mm )(1,000 mm / m )}{1,421,250.0 mm ^{4}} \\&=45.0952 MPa +57.5827 MPa \\&=102.6779 MPa =102.7 MPa ( T )\end{aligned}\)

Compute normal stress at y = −45 mm, z = −27.5 mm:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\frac{M_{y} z}{I_{y}}-\frac{M_{z} y}{I_{z}} \\&=\frac{(1,050 N – m )(-27.5 mm )(1,000 mm / m )}{640,312.5 mm ^{4}}-\frac{(-1,818.65 N – m )(-45 mm )(1,000 mm / m )}{1,421,250.0 mm ^{4}} \\&=-45.0952 MPa -57.5827 MPa \\&=-102.6779 MPa =102.7 MPa ( C )\end{aligned}\)

(b) Orientation of neutral axis
For a shape having at least one axis of symmetry, Eq. (8-25) can be used to determine the orientation of the neutral axis:

\(\begin{aligned}\tan \beta &=\frac{M_{y} I_{z}}{M_{z} I_{y}}=\frac{(1,050 N – m )\left(1,421,250.0 mm ^{4}\right)}{(-1,818.65 N – m )\left(640,312.5 mm ^{4}\right)}=-1.2815 \\&\left.\therefore \beta=-52.03^{\circ} \quad \text { (i.e., } 52.03^{\circ} CCW \text { from }+z \text { axis }\right)\end{aligned}\)
