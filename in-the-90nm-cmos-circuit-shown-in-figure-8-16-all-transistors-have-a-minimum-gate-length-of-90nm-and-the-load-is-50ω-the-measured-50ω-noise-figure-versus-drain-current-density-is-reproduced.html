Guest Question.

In the \(90 \mathrm{~nm}\) CMOS circuit shown in Figure 8.16, all transistors have a minimum gate length of \(90 \mathrm{~nm}\) and the load is \(50 \Omega\) . The measured \( 50 \Omega\) noise figure versus drain current density is reproduced in Figure 8.17 at three different frequencies:
(a) Determine the total bias current drawn from \(V_{D D}\) and the power consumption if all transistors are biased at \(J_{O P T}\) and \(V_{D D}=1.2 \mathrm{~V}\).
(b) Calculate the value of the input resistance at low frequencies and of the open loop gain of the amplifier forming the TIA. What is the role of \(L_{F}\) ?
(c) What is the role and gain of the second stage formed by\(M_{4}\) and the \(50 \Omega\) resistor?
(d) Calculate the input node capacitance and output node capacitance of the TIA stage.
(e) What is the optimal transistor size at \(36 \mathrm{GHz}\) ?

90nm n-(p)MOSFET parameters:
\(|V T|=0.3 \mathrm{~V}\) ;\( g_{m n}^{\prime}=2 g_{m p}^{\prime}=1.1 \mathrm{mS} / \mu \mathrm{m}\) ;\( g_{d s n}^{\prime}=g_{d s p}=0.15 \mathrm{mS} / \mu \mathrm{m}\); the peak \(f_{T}\) of \(135 \mathrm{GHz}\) occurs at

\(0.3 \mathrm{~mA} / \mu \mathrm{m}\) for \(0.3 \mathrm{~V}<V_{D S}<1 V\).
For both n-channel and p-channel devices: \(C_{g s}=1.0 \mathrm{fF} / \mu \mathrm{m}\) ;\( C_{g d}=0.4 \mathrm{fF} / \mu \mathrm{m}\) ;\( C_{s b}=C_{d b}= 1.1fF/μm\);

\(R_{s}(\mathrm{n}-\mathrm{MOS})=R_{d}=200 \Omega \times \mu \mathrm{m}\) ; \(R_{s}(\mathrm{p}-\mathrm{MOS})=R_{d}=400 \Omega \times \mu \mathrm{m}\)
\(R_{s h g}(\mathrm{n} / \mathrm{p}-\mathrm{MOS})=15 \Omega / \mathrm{sq}\) ; poly contact resistance \(R_{C O N}=10 \Omega\) . Total \(R_{G} / 1 \mu \mathrm{m}\) finger =\(150 \Omega\)
Noise parameters (n/p-MOS): P=1, C=0.4, R=0.25.
\(R=\frac{P}{g_{m}^{\prime}}+R_{s}^{\prime}+R_{g}^{\prime}\left(W_{f}=1\right)=1260 \Omega \cdot \mu \mathrm{m}\)
\(G=\frac{P R_{s}^{\prime}\left(C_{g s}^{\prime}+C_{g d}^{\prime}\right)^{2}}{P+\left(R_{s}^{\prime}+R_{g}^{\prime}\left(W_{f}=1\right)\right) g_{m}^{\prime}}=2.83 \cdot 10^{-28} \frac{\mathrm{F}^{2}}{\mu \mathrm{m}^{2}}\) ;\( G_{C} \approx 0 \\\)
\(B=\frac{P\left(C_{g s}^{\prime}+C_{g d}^{\prime}\right)}{P+\left(R_{s}^{\prime}+R_{g}^{\prime}\left(W_{f}=1\right)\right) g_{m}^{\prime}}=1.01 \cdot 10^{-15} \frac{F}{\mu \mathrm{m}}\)

Solution

(a) From Figure 8.17, we determine that, irrespective of frequency, the optimal bias current density is \(0.15-0.2 \mathrm{~mA} / \mu \mathrm{m}\). Therefore, to save power we select \(J_{O P T}=0.15 \mathrm{~mA} / \mu \mathrm{m}\). The drain currents of each transistor become \(I_{D 1}=I_{D 2}=12 \mathrm{~mA}, I_{D 4}=I_{D 1} / 2=6 \mathrm{~mA}\) and \(I_{D 3}=0.6 \mathrm{~mA}\). The total power consumption of the TIA circuit with output buffer is \(18.6 \mathrm{~mA} \times 1.2 \mathrm{~V}=22.32 \mathrm{~mA}\).
(b) The role of the feedback inductor \(L_{F}\) is to resonate out the capacitance at the TIA node and to improve the bandwidth. It also tunes out the imaginary part of the noise impedance at high frequencies around \(\omega_{f}\). For operation in a \(40 \mathrm{~Gb} / \mathrm{s}\) circuit, \(L_{F}\) must be designed for SRF \(>80 \mathrm{GHz}\). To determine the open loop gain and input impedance, we must first calculate \(g_{m e f f}\) and \(g_{o e f f}\) for each transistor

\(g_{m e f f}^{\prime}=\frac{g_{m}^{\prime}}{1+R_{s}^{\prime} g_{m}^{\prime}}=0.9 \frac{\mathrm{mS}}{\mu \mathrm{m}} \text { and } g_{\text {oeff }}^{\prime}=\frac{g_{o}^{\prime}}{1+R_{s}^{\prime} g_{m}^{\prime}}=0.122 \frac{\mathrm{mS}}{\mu \mathrm{m}}\)
\(A=\frac{-g_{m 1}}{g_{o 1}+g_{o 2}+\frac{1}{R_{F}}}=\frac{-72 \mathrm{mS}}{9.76 \mathrm{mS}+19.52 \mathrm{mS}+5.55 \mathrm{mS}}=-2.07\) .
\(Z_{I N}=\frac{R_{F}}{1-A}=\frac{180}{3.07}=58.6 \Omega\) .

(c) The role of the second stage is to provide matching to the \(50 \Omega\) external load without allowing the load to affect the performance of the TIA stage.

The voltage gain of this stage is \(A_{V 2}=\frac{-g_{\operatorname{meff} / 4}}{g_{\text {oeff } 4}+\frac{2}{z_{0}}}=\frac{-36 \mathrm{mS}}{4.88 \mathrm{mS}+40 \mathrm{mS}}=-0.8\). Here we have accounted for the on-chip \(Z_{0} \) load, as well as for the off-chip one.

(d)
\(C_{I N} =C_{g s 1}+(1-A) C_{g d 1}=(1+3.07 \cdot 0.4) \frac{\mathrm{fF}}{\mu \mathrm{m}} 0 \cdot 80 \mu \mathrm{m}=178 \mathrm{fF}\)
\(C_{o u t} =C_{d b 1}+C_{g d 1}+C_{g d 2}+C_{d b 2}+C_{g s 4}+\left(1-A_{v 2}\right) C_{g d 4}\)
=88+32+64+176+40+28.8=429 fF .
(e) At \(36 \mathrm{GHz}\), \(Ω_{f}\)=0.628.
From (8.48), we obtain

\(W_{O P T}=\frac{1}{\omega} \sqrt{\left(\frac{1}{Z_{0}}+\frac{1}{R_{F}} \frac{1}{1+\omega_{f}^{2}}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{1}{R_{F}} \frac{\omega_{f}}{1+\omega_{f}^{2}}\right)^{2}}\sqrt{\frac{1}{\frac{G_{F E T}}{R_{F E T}}+G_{C, F E T}^{2}+B_{F E T}^{2}}}\) \(=\frac{0.0241 \cdot 5.38 \cdot 10^{14}}{2.26 \cdot 10^{11}}=57.4 \mu \mathrm{m}\)

which is smaller than the gate width of the input MOSFETs in the schematic of Figure 8.16 .
The \(80 \mu \mathrm{m}\) width of the input device is optimal for a \(3 \mathrm{~dB}\) bandwidth of \(25.8 \mathrm{GHz}\), reflecting the measurements in [8].
