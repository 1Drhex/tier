Guest Question.

Use the variational method to evaluate the energy of the ground state for a particle bound on the segment \([-a, a] .\) Use, as a trial function,

\(\psi(x ; \alpha)=|x|^{\alpha}-a^{\alpha}\)

where \(\alpha\) is the variational parameter. Compare this approximation with the exact value and determine the relative error.

 

Admin
Solution

The energy expectation value in the state \(\psi\) is

\(\begin{aligned}E(\alpha) &=\frac{\langle\psi|\mathcal{H}| \psi\rangle}{\langle\psi \mid \psi\rangle}=\frac{2 \int_{0}^{+a} d x\left(x^{\alpha}-a^{\alpha}\right)\left[-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \frac{d^{2}}{d x^{2}}\right]\left(x^{\alpha}-a^{\alpha}\right)}{2 \int_{0}^{+a} d x\left(x^{\alpha}-a^{\alpha}\right)^{2}}=\\&=-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{4 m a^{2}} \frac{1+3 \alpha+2 \alpha^{2}}{1-2 \alpha} .\end{aligned}\)

By requiring that the derivative of \(E(\alpha)\) vanishes, there are two solutions

\(\alpha_{\pm}=\frac{1}{2}(1 \pm \sqrt{6})\)

The solution relative to the \(+\) sign gives us the minimum point sought (the other solution corresponds to a maximum point). For this value of \(\alpha,\) we obtain the following approximate value for the ground state energy:

11.2 Ground State of a Potential Well

\(E_{1}^{v a r}=\frac{1.23737 \hbar^{2}}{m a^{2}}\)

to be compared with the exact value

\(E_{1}=\frac{\pi^{2} \hbar^{2}}{8 m a^{2}}\)

The relative error is equal to

\(\frac{E_{1}^{v a r}-E_{1}}{E_{1}}=0.002976\)

less, therefore, than \(0.3 \%\).
