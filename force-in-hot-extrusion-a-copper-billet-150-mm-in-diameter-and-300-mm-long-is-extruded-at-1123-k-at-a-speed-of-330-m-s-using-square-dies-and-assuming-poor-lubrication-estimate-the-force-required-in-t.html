Guest Question.

Force in hot extrusion

A copper billet 150 mm in diameter and 300 mm long is extruded at 1123 K at a speed of 330 m/s. Using square dies and assuming poor lubrication, estimate the force required in this operation if the extruded diameter is 75 mm.

Admin
Solution

The extrusion ratio in this operation is

\(R=\frac{150^2}{75^2} =2, \)

and the average true-strain rate, from Eq. (6.59),

\(\dot{\overline{\epsilon } }=\frac{6V_{\omicron }D^{2}_{\omicron } \tan \alpha }{D^{3}_{\omicron }-D^{3}_{f} } \ln R,\) (6.59)

is

\(\dot{\overline{\epsilon } } =\frac{6\times 0.3}{0.150} \ln 2=0 .3177.\)

From Table 2.5,

Approximate Range of Values for C and m in Eq. (2.16)(\(\sigma =C\dot{\epsilon }^m \) ) for Various Annealed Metals at True Strains Ranging from 0.2 to 1.0

Aluminum alloys 475–775 310–35 0–0.20
Copper 575–1175 240–20 0.06–0.17
Copper alloys (brasses) 475–1075 415–14 0.02–0.3

*At a strain rate of \(2 × 10^{- 4} s ^{-1}\).
Note: As temperature increases, C decreases and m increases. As strain increases, C increases and m may increase or decrease, or it may become negative within certain ranges of temperature and strain.

let’s assume that an average value for C = 240 MPa and that m = 0.06. Hence,

\(\sigma =C\dot{\epsilon }^m =\left(240\times 10^6\right)\left(0. 3177\right)^{0.06} = 272 \) MPa

Assuming that \( \overline{Y} =\sigma \) we have, from Eq. (6.55),

\( p=Y\left\lgroup1.7\ln R+\frac{2L}{D_{\omicron }} \right\rgroup. \) (6.55)

\(p=\overline{Y}\left(a+b\ln R\right) =272\times 10^6 (0.8+1.5\ln 2)=500\) MPa.

Therefore,

\(F=\left(p\right) \left(A_{0}\right)=\left(500\times 10^6\right) \frac{\pi \left\lgroup\frac{150}{1000} \right\rgroup ^2}{4} =8.8\times 10^6 \) N.
