Guest Question.

Use Equation 9.23 to calculate the approximate transmission probability for a particle of energy E that encounters a finite square barrier of height \( V_{0}>E \) and width . Compare your answer with the exact result (), to which it should reduce in the WKB regime \( T \ll 1 \).

\( T \approx e^{-2 \gamma}, \quad \text { with } \quad \gamma \equiv \frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{0}^{a}|p(x)| d x . \) (9.23).

Admin
Solution

\( \gamma=\frac{1}{\hbar} \int|p(x)| d x=\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{0}^{2 a} \sqrt{2 m\left(V_{0}-E\right)} d x=\frac{2 a}{\hbar} \sqrt{2 m\left(V_{0}-E\right)} . \quad T \approx e^{-4 a \sqrt{2 m\left(V_{0}-E\right)} / \hbar} . \).

From , the exact answer is

\( T=\frac{1}{1+\frac{V_{0}^{2}}{4 E\left(V_{0}-E\right)} \sinh ^{2} \gamma} \).

Now, the WKB approximation assumes the tunneling probability is small (p. 359)|which is to say that Î³ is large. In this case \( \sinh \gamma=\frac{1}{2}\left(e^{\gamma}-e^{-\gamma}\right) \approx \frac{1}{2} e^{\gamma}, \text { and } \sinh ^{2} \gamma \approx \frac{1}{4} e^{2 \gamma} \) , and the exact result reduces to

\( T \approx \frac{1}{1+\frac{V_{0}^{2}}{16 E\left(V_{0}-E\right)} e^{2 \gamma}} \approx\left\{\frac{16 E\left(V_{0}-E\right)}{V_{0}^{2}}\right\} e^{-2 \gamma} \).

The coefficient in \( \{\} \) is of order 1; the dominant dependence on E is in the exponential factor. In this sense \( T \approx e^{-2 \gamma} \) (the WKB result).
