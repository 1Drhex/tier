KINETIC ENERGY CORRECTION FACTOR FOR LAMINAR FLOW

The velocity distribution for laminar flow in a pipe is given by the equation

\(V=V_{\max }\left[1-\left(\frac{r}{r_{0}}\right)^{2}\right]\)

Here \(r_{0}\) is the radius of the pipe and r is the radial distance from the center. Find the kinetic-energy correction factor \(\alpha\).

Problem Definition

Situation: Laminar flow in a round pipe. Velocity profile is given.

Find: Kinetic-energy correction factor \(\alpha\) (no units).

Sketch:

Plan

1. Find dA using the above sketch.

2. Find the mean velocity \(\overline{V}\) using the flow rate equation (5.8).

\(Q=\bar{V} A=\frac{\dot{m}}{\rho}=\int_{A} V d A=\int_{A} V d A\) (5.8)

3. Find \(\alpha\) using Eq. (7.21).

\(\alpha=\frac{1}{A} \int_{A}\left(\frac{V}{\bar{V}}\right)^{3} d A\) (7.21)

Admin
Solution

1. Differential area

• From the sketch, dA can be visualized as a rectangular
strip of length \(2 \pi r\) and width dr. Thus, \(d A=2 \pi r d r\).

 

2. Mean velocity

 

\(\bar{V}=\frac{1}{A}\left[\int_{A} V d A\right]=\frac{1}{\pi r_{0}^{2}}\left[\int_{0}^{r_{0}} V_{\max }\left(1-\frac{r^{2}}{r_{0}^{2}}\right) 2 \pi r \, d r\right]\)

 

\(=\frac{2 V_{\max }}{r_{0}^{2}}\left[\int_{0}^{r_{0}}\left(1-\frac{r^{2}}{r_{0}^{2}}\right) r d r\right]=\frac{2 V_{\max }}{r_{0}^{2}}\left[\int_{0}^{r_{0}}\left(r-\frac{r^{3}}{r_{0}^{2}}\right) d r\right]\)

 

\(=\frac{2 V_{\max }}{r_{0}^{2}}\left[\left.\left(\frac{r^{2}}{2}-\frac{r^{4}}{4 r_{0}^{2}}\right)\right|_{0} ^{r_{0}}\right]=\frac{2 V_{\max }}{r_{0}^{2}}\left[\frac{r_{0}^{2}}{2}-\frac{r_{0}^{2}}{4}\right]=V_{\max } / 2\)

 

Interpretation: For laminar flow in a round pipe, the mean velocity is one-half of the maximum (centerline) velocity.

 

3. Kinetic-energy correction factor \((\alpha)\).

 

\(\alpha=\frac{1}{A}\left[\int_{A}\left(\frac{V}{\bar{V}}\right)^{3} d A\right]=\frac{1}{\pi r_{0}^{2} \bar{V}^{3}}\left[\int_{0}^{r_{0}} V^{3} 2 \pi r d r\right]\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{\pi r_{0}^{2}\left(V_{\max } / 2\right)^{3}}\left[\int_{0}^{r_{0}}\left[V_{\max }\left(1-\frac{r^{2}}{r_{0}^{2}}\right)\right]^{3} 2 \pi r d r\right]\)

 

\(=\frac{16}{r_{0}^{2}}\left[\int_{0}^{r_{0}}\left(1-\frac{r^{2}}{r_{0}^{2}}\right)^{3} r d r\right]\)

 

To evaluate the integral, make a change of variable by letting \(u=\left(1-r^{2} / r_{0}^{2}\right)\). The integral becomes

 

\(\alpha=\left(\frac{16}{r_{0}^{2}}\right)\left(-\frac{r_{0}^{2}}{2}\right)\left(\int_{1}^{0} u^{3} d u\right)=8\left(\int_{0}^{1} u^{3} du \right)\)

 

\(=8\left(\left.\frac{u^{4}}{4}\right|_{0} ^{1}\right)=8\left(\frac{1}{4}\right)\)

 

\(\alpha=2\)
