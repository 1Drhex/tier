Guest Question.

The power winch A hoists the 800-lb log up the 30° incline at a constant speed of 4 ft /sec. If the power output of the winch is 6 hp, compute the coefficient of kinetic friction \(\mu_{k}\) between the log and the incline. If the power is suddenly increased to 8 hp, what is the corresponding instantaneous acceleration a of the log?

Admin
Solution

From the free-body diagram of the log, we get \(N=800 \cos 30^{\circ}=693\) lb, and the kinetic friction force becomes \(693 \mu_{k}\). For constant speed, the forces are in equilibrium so that

 

\(\left[\Sigma F_{x}=0\right] \quad T-693 \mu_{k}-800 \sin 30^{\circ}=0 \quad T=693 \mu_{k}+400\)

 

The power output of the winch gives the tension in the cable

 

1 \([P=T v] \quad T=P / v=6(550) / 4=825 lb\)

 

Substituting T gives

 

\(825=693 \mu_{k}+400 \quad \mu_{k}=0.613\) Ans.

 

When the power is increased, the tension momentarily becomes

 

\([P=T v] \quad T=P / v=8(550) / 4=1100 lb\)

 

and the corresponding acceleration is given by

 

\(\left[\Sigma F_{x}=m a_{x}\right] \quad 1100-693(0.613)-800 \sin 30^{\circ}=\frac{800}{32.2} a\)

 

2 \(a=11.07 ft / sec ^{2}\) Ans.

 

Helpful Hints

1 Note the conversion from horsepower to ft-lb/sec.

2 As the speed increases, the acceleration will drop until the speed stabilizes at a value higher than 4 ft/sec.
