Guest Question.

An experimental furnace is supported on a brick column (k=0.056 W/m K) of square cross-section (100 mm×100 mm). The installation is such that the three sides of the column are exposed to a constant temperature of 300°C while the fourth side is exposed to atmosphere (at 25°C) and loses heat by convection (\(h=7W/m^{2}K\)). Using a square grid of Δx=Δy=20 mm, calculate the nodal temperatures.

Admin
Solution

The grid is shown:
In this case, nodes 1–16 are interior nodes and one can apply Equation
\(T_{m+1,n}+T_{m-1,n}+T_{m,n+1}+T_{m,n-1}-4T_{m,n}=0\).
Nodes 17–20 are exposed to convective boundary condition and one can use Equation
\(-T_{m,n}(2+\frac{h\Delta x }{k} )+\frac{h\Delta x }{k} T_{\infty }+\frac{1}{2}(2T_{m-1,n}+T_{m,n-1}+T_{m,n+1})=0 \) (1)
for these nodes. Symmetry considerations further suggest that
\(T_{1}=T_{4} ,T_{2}=T_{3}, T_{8}=T_{5}, T_{6}=T_{7}, T_{9}=T_{12},T_{10}=T_{11} ,T_{14}=T_{15} \),
\(T_{13}=T_{16}, T_{18}=T_{19}, \space and \space T_{17}=T_{20}\)
thereby reducing the number of unknowns significantly. Introducing these ideas, the grid now looks as follows:
One can now set up the nodal equations for the 10 nodes as follows:
\(300+300+T_{3}+T_{2}-4T_{1}=0 \)
\(T_{1} +T_{2} +T_{4}+300-4T_{2}=0 \)
\(T_{1} +T_{5} +300+T_{4}-4T_{3}=0 \)
\(T_{3} +T_{6} +T_{2} +T_{4}-4T_{4}=0\)
\(T_{7} +T_{3} +T_{6}+300-4T_{5}=0 \)
\(T_{5} +T_{8} +T_{4}+T_{6}-4T_{6}=0 \)
\(300 +T_{5} +T_{8}+T_{9}-4T_{7}=0 \)
\(T_{7} +T_{10} +T_{6}+T_{8}-4T_{8}=0\)
Using hΔx/k =7×0.02/0.056 = 2.5, we can use Equation (1) for the exterior nodes 9 and 10:
\(-T_{9}(2+2.5)+2.5T_{\infty }+(1/2)(2T_{7}+T_{10}+300 )=0\)
\(-T_{10}(2+2.5)+2.5T_{\infty }+(1/2)(2T_{8}+T_{9}+T_{10} )=0\)
Solving these equations, we obtain the following results:
\(T_{1}=283.54^{\circ }C, T_{2}=281.32^{\circ }C,T_{3}=273.75^{\circ }C, T_{4}=260.41^{\circ }C,T_{5}=251.14^{\circ }C,\)
\(T_{6}=226.15^{\circ }C,T_{7}=204.62^{\circ }C, T_{8}=166.89^{\circ }C,T_{9}=100.46^{\circ }C, T_{10}=69.91^{\circ }C\)
